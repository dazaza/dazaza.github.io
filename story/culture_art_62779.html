**Appreciation of "The Night Revels of Han Xizai", One of China's Ten Most Famous Paintings: Who Created "The Night Revels of Han Xizai"?**

**[Basic Information]**

- **Name**: The Night Revels of Han Xizai

- **Category**: Color Painting

- **Era**: Five Dynasties (the original is lost; the existing version is a Song dynasty copy)

- **Artist**: Gu Hongzhong

- **Current Status**: Silk painting, 28.7 cm wide, 335.5 cm long, currently housed in the Palace Museum, Beijing.

**Description of the Artifact:**

"The Night Revels of Han Xizai" is a renowned masterpiece in the history of Chinese painting and one of China's ten most famous paintings. It depicts the scene of a grand banquet and revelry at the home of Han Xizai, a prominent official during the Southern Tang dynasty. To avoid suspicion from Emperor Li Yu of the Southern Tang, Han Xizai used music, dance, and indulgence as a way to conceal his true intentions. He frequently hosted lavish night banquets, mingling freely with guests. This long scroll captures the entire process of one such night banquet at Han’s residence.

The scroll features precise, fluid, and meticulously detailed lines that are full of expressive power. The coloring is elegant, refined, and layered, bringing out the unique charm and spirit of the work.
