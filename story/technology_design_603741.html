L'Oréal "Young Women Scientists of China Award"

by juyaqufa003 on 2011-12-05 09:46:44

As the only award in today's world that rewards and sponsors all women engaged in life sciences and basic sciences on a global scale, the "For Women in Science" program was born in 1998 and was jointly established by L'Oréal, the world's largest cosmetics group, and UNESCO. The aim is to highlight the key roles played by women in science around the world, thereby giving full recognition to their unique contributions in this field. The program has two main objectives:

**Recognition of Excellence:**

The L'Oréal-UNESCO "For Women in Science Awards" is specifically awarded to the most outstanding female scientists who have already achieved remarkable success worldwide. Every year, five of the most distinguished female scientists representing the five continents receive this honor, setting examples to inspire the younger generation to pursue careers in scientific research.

**Encouraging Talent:**

The UNESCO-L'Oréal "International Rising Talents" fellowships were created in 2000. Each year, 15 excellent young women at the doctoral or postdoctoral stage are awarded scholarships to promote international scientific exchanges and cooperation.

The L'Oréal-UNESCO "National Fellowships for Young Women in Science" are established according to the actual situation of each country, jointly by L'Oréal subsidiaries in various countries and the national UNESCO committees, to encourage local young female scientists to conduct their scientific research in their home countries.

Between 1998 and 2012, a total of 72 women received the "L'Oréal-UNESCO For Women in Science Awards," and more than 1,200 women from 103 countries and regions around the world received the Young Talents Fellowships. (http://www.forwomeninscience.com)

**Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China**

In light of the tremendous success and growing influence of the "For Women in Science" program, UNESCO headquarters and L'Oréal Group actively promoted the development and extension of the program in various countries to encourage more women to engage in scientific careers on a larger scale. With the support of the All-China Women's Federation, the Chinese Association for Science and Technology, the National Commission of the People's Republic of China for UNESCO, and L'Oréal China, the "Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China" was officially established on July 21, 2004.

Highlighting Achievements and Contributions of Women in Scientific Research

The "Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China" aims to recognize and reward young female scientists who have made significant scientific achievements, encouraging them to continue their scientific careers and achieve even richer research results in the field of science and technology. This award is evaluated annually. From 2004 to 2011, a total of 67 women from 20 provinces (municipalities, autonomous regions) and the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region received this honor, with L'Oréal's total donation amounting to 5.1 million RMB.

Encouraging the Younger Generation, Unveiling a New Face of Chinese Science

In 2009 and 2013, it marked the second five-year plan of the "Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China." The All-China Women's Federation, the Chinese Association for Science and Technology, the National Commission of the People's Republic of China for UNESCO, and L'Oréal China carried forward the excellent spirit of the "For Women in Science" program, dedicated to further expanding the influence of the award nationwide and inspiring more young people to engage in scientific research. A series of new initiatives have been launched: In 2010, the "Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China" was opened for the first time to the Hong Kong and Macao Special Administrative Regions, and one female scientist from Hong Kong has been recorded in the history of the "Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China." In 2011, the "Award for Outstanding Young Female Scientists in China" campus tour was launched at Peking University, and more award-winning female scientists will enter campuses in the future.
