The scale of car beauty shops has always been a topic of discussion. Some people say that large shops make money, they have momentum, and can intimidate people. Others say small shops also make money. Who is reasonable? We don't know. Only those who have truly worked in the industry, earned money, and suffered losses are qualified to speak. I think I am qualified to say this because I have seen others earn money and others lose money, so I myself feel it is a sin. Therefore, when answering questions, I write down some opinions, which are completely personal opinions and do not involve anyone or anything.

China Auto Beauty Talent Network shares the following about the investment scale and service philosophy of auto beauty shops:

1. What kind of scale?

The scale is mainly determined by your investment. If you have 100,000 yuan, then start a 100,000 yuan shop. If you have 1 million yuan, then start a 1 million yuan shop. Don't think that this industry is so profitable that you end up borrowing money to enter this industry. A large shop is not necessarily good or profitable. We know that cost and profit are proportional. No matter how good the profit is, as long as the hardware cost is too high, it will not be profitable. Just having a gilded shop is useless. If a 200 square meter shop is well managed, detailed, and refined, it won't lose money. It mainly depends on how you manage it. Don't listen to people saying that it's not profitable. First, you need to understand this industry clearly. Do you understand my meaning?

2. What kind of service philosophy?

I have guided many shops, and the only flaw among these shops, which is also a common point, is that they can't even wash cars well. This seems simple and easy to do, but it directly relates to the survival of your shop. Some headquarters don't care about washing cars, but I tell you, relying on car washing to attract customers and other projects will allow your shop to survive and truly succeed. Some people still think that car beauty is just renting a small storefront, hiring two car washers, and that's it. However, they don't know that this industry is developing, they don't know what kind of shop is needed, and they don't know how the concept of car owners has changed. Only those who come from the masses and go back to the masses understand the market. The general manager of the franchise headquarters drinks alcohol and plays cards every day. Can he understand the problems each franchise store encounters? No, so the people at the bottom won't voice their problems. For this kind of beauty shop, catering to car owners is the most important thing. Car washing is not just an ordinary task; it's a concept, a service. It's a service that treats customers as gods. Think about KFC. Or think about the beauty and hairdressing industry. It's actually the same principle. After years of experience, I have come to the following conclusions:

One: Refined products

Everyone says that car beauty is an industry with a low threshold, but for investors just entering the industry, no matter how much capital you have, it's ultimately not a small amount. Entering a strange industry, you may not have a clear idea of how much investment is appropriate, or how your later operations will be. These are all issues.

My suggestion is to start small, achieving the greatest profit with the lowest cost, which will reduce risks significantly. (This advice is for investors with less capital. Wealthy investors can ignore it, but this service philosophy applies to large shops as well.)

Two: Specialized shop image

For investors with small investments and newcomers to this industry, they don't know how big their shop needs to be. Too big means expensive rent and costly decoration. For new entrants, a 100 square meter shop is the time for you to try out, to begin understanding and getting to know this industry, and it's also your stepping stone into the car beauty industry. Since you don't know how deep the water is in this industry, why not throw a stone first to test it? Maybe it's not as deep as you think. Of course, a 100 square meter shop is relatively suitable for car beauty investors.

Basic layout of a 100 square meter shop:

The total length of the shop is 12 meters, with a width of about 8.5 meters. There are four rooms in total, each 3 meters long. One beauty room, one Japanese-style car wash room, another sales and customer rest room, plus an office area.

Three: Simplified equipment

There are many types of car beauty equipment available on the market, but for a new shop, there may be very few that you'll use within half a year. Many investors want to purchase lift machines and tire changers right away, but such equipment can be added later. In the beginning, you'll rarely use them, almost not at all. One, it ties up your operating funds; two, it occupies your limited space. So, a new 100 square meter shop requires very little equipment.

Four: Precise service

Know what services the customers need, know what the car owners want. People need respect and agreement the most. Give them respect, give them agreement, satisfy the car owners with precise service, and there will be no project that cannot be negotiated.

Five: Professionalism achieves everything (car washing starts to form complete procedures and standard processes)

Car washing is key, car washing is also the hardest part. Actually, car beauty isn't difficult, but car washing is the hardest. Some shops just can't wash cars well, but they don't know the specific problem. They've always thought that as long as the car is washed well, it's fine, but that was a few years ago. Now, car owners pursue perfection and professionalism. Once, I heard a car owner say that they couldn't find a professional car wash shop. All were some big shops, but the car washing wasn't good. Car washing isn't good? Where is the problem? It's professionalism. Professional process, professional products, professional service. Only when car owners are satisfied can you talk about car beauty projects. Otherwise, no matter how big your shop is, you're just selling products...
