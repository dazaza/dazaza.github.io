In this lonely day

by mimi456 on 2012-01-08 22:29:36

In these lonely days,

I once thought that I would always prefer tranquility to noise!

When reality sets in, I realized that I had quietly changed. Change involves indulgence in facing the past or calmly embracing the new life about to begin; it also involves the strengthening of self-awareness: wanting to climb, wanting to chase, perhaps these only belong to dreams, but they have ultimately changed; there is also a serene and peaceful attitude towards life, away from the clamor and ostentation of the past...

There is nothing wrong with being alone.

I am changing the consistent timing of the past, even though I doubt whether I have deviated from the center, but I like this visible and tangible change. Without nagging, only self-reliance; without counting, only self-righteousness. Under the sunset, I lazily move my body, following my heart without any constraints, leaving behind those beautiful times. Even if I am alone, I still change myself in this gentle evening breeze, enjoying this rare solitude and seclusion.

Facing loneliness alone, I used to be afraid.

I always felt that life without noise meant less playful banter and heartfelt conversations with friends, which made me feel sorrowful. But now, my life does not have the sentimental sadness, instead, it adds a few real comforts and personal leisure in these mundane days. Originally, people can change! Loneliness changes life and sways emotions, making one become the true master of life without needing others' trivial words, and slowly liking solitude.

I don't know why I have changed? Yet, I cannot explain the reason!

A pool of lotus flowers blooming together, how could they resist the alluring fragrance? Saying fragrance might be inappropriate, after all, besides her pure aroma, there is also the beauty of color. People are just like this, when detached from the past, they gain deep loneliness and continuous silence. Loneliness may be temporary or eternal, but no matter what, I have to learn to change myself and gradually proceed through life...

Sitting on a bus with no familiar faces, one can freely and slowly open a natural door of imagination, even if there is no peace in this door that I desire, I will still be grateful because I have learned to face loneliness alone. My eyes watching the receding trees or pedestrians, I will merge my heart with them. Because in those moments afterward, I can look forward to the sweetness and the faint smiles that belong solely to me!

Loneliness or solitude will only appear in those who truly need noise.
