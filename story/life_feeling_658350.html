Loved, Faded, Scattered

If you, like me, also love this kind of green, this kind of sunshine. At this angle, looking up at the sky. Then, go to Gulangyu.

On a sunny afternoon, we rode our bikes flying like the wind, and finally, I wobbled left and right and landed on the ground...

Nowadays, we like to write these numb and muddled sentences, then carry a thorn in our hearts, ready to mislead many good people to stop and watch, to be picked up with sympathy and pity, and then use the thorn to hurt those who sympathize and pity you, satisfying the inner emptiness. I have always thought that men are crazier than women, women are crazy on the surface, but men are crazy in their hearts.

We loved, faded, scattered.

Whose waiting can always coincide with the blooming of flowers?

I often look at the silent sky from the balcony outside the operating room. The sound of car horns on the road is very loud, mixed with Sichuan accents selling tofu pudding. The sky is often dazzlingly dark blue, when birds fly by, because they are too far away, I often don't see whether they are pigeons or sparrows. A floating cloud drifts by, making one's heart warm and melancholy. Time is the fleeting moment of passing each other, the delicate whisper, is the dust of time falling off.

I cannot let go of this feeling, as if it is the end of life. When sending off the parturient women, I observe the expressions of the men, they all laugh because they have become fathers. But there is a subtle detail, most of them seem relieved. Only occasionally do some hold their wives' hands and whisper tender words. I think, precisely because the world is so silent and indifferent, we like to deeply love, because of deep love, the darkness will be filled with flower-like memories.

So many people, ebbing and flowing in the world, so I am often addicted to traveling. The time of travel, day by day, stands behind you, sharing the beauty and silence of the world with you. Stretching happiness long.

When summer comes, children can run barefoot on the cool muddy ground, screaming, being unrestrained like a little fish. I like to walk under the sunlit leaves, the streets full of willow branches make people feel refreshed. Smooth and simple paths, with a warm and happy mood.

Love comes and goes, and eventually, every petal will fall like rain. But it can be like food, caressing your stomach, fragrant, then bringing joy to the brain, spreading to every cell, making the soul richer.

Women like to expose their emotions. In the coldest summer in Shangri-La, the whole family sat together in a restaurant drinking butter tea. There was no sunshine, the sky was somewhat dim, I sat on a slightly worn wooden chair, with dark red checkered windows. On a stone wall outside the window, I found several big characters, "Love, turning the corner." Because my phone was out of power, so I didn't take a picture. I like such bold and passionate expression, yes, we should wake up in the morning, kiss the face of the lover beside us, pull open the curtain, and see the quiet sun on the mountaintop. Seeing you are still there, this is the life we want.

There used to be a simple and plain ideal, to open a flower shop after retirement. Preferably in some quiet corner, allowing passers-by to smell the fresh fragrance of jasmine and orchids. I don't know if I want to gain the silence and self-awareness of life in this way. I like those high mountains that are hard to touch, I like their heavy loneliness.

We loved, then later faded, then later, we will scatter because life ends. Those we loved, or have already faded, before we scatter, let's carefully reminisce once more. At dusk, the streets gradually empty, I am in the dimness, following the clockwise direction, bit by bit watching. In the unfathomable life, time covers everything, smells, sounds, and yearning...
