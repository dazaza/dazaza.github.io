Yesterday, a entrepreneurship project franchise meeting held by Zhejiang Industry & Trade Vocational and Technical College sparked a wave of entrepreneurial enthusiasm among on-campus college students.

Under the impact of the financial crisis, more private enterprises in Wenzhou have shifted their focus to the establishment and improvement of domestic online marketing terminals. Yesterday, Zhejiang Industry & Trade Vocational and Technical College jointly held an entrepreneurship project franchise meeting with 11 private enterprises in Wenzhou, targeting on-campus college students to open online stores. The participating private enterprises in Wenzhou are mainly shoe, clothing, glasses foreign trade production enterprises and upstream suppliers of online stores.

The person in charge of Wenzhou Yijia Optics Co., Ltd. introduced that the company is an outward-oriented glasses production enterprise. The financial crisis has led to a reduction in foreign trade orders, and they hope to support college students in opening online stores to directly sell their products, open up the domestic market, and establish an online marketing team. College students familiar with computer networks and e-commerce are the best targets, and they can also become the company's reserve talents in the future.

"The advantages of college students in online sales are obvious." Cai Fuxin, the recruitment supervisor of Wenzhou Pinggoubao Network Technology Co., Ltd., introduced that the company currently provides sources of goods and logistics services to more than 30 online stores. On-campus college students who open online stores and join their company can enjoy free shipment of goods worth a cumulative total of 2000 yuan.

Li Qian, a first-year student at the school, is determined to join the entrepreneurship and open an online store. She said that opening an online store is one of the best carriers for on-campus college students to start their own businesses.
