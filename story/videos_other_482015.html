Corn oil prices may soar after the Mid-Autumn Festival

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-26 16:23:35

Following a wave of price increases in bean oil, rape oil and blended oil in early August, corn oil prices have also recently been rumored to be on the rise. Yesterday, a supermarket in Guangzhou confirmed to the Yangcheng Evening News reporter that they had received news of a price increase from the manufacturer, but the specific increase and time are still unknown.

An industry analysis report also pointed out that it is expected that around the Mid-Autumn Festival, corn oil prices will enter an upward trend under the influence of bean oil prices.

Pre-holiday promotional prices slightly decreased

Recently, it was reported that Fulinmen corn oil was having a low-price promotion in Beijing supermarkets, with the highest discount reaching 20 yuan. Yesterday, the reporter visited various large supermarkets in Guangzhou and found that edible oils were currently undergoing pre-Mid-Autumn Festival promotional activities, but the share of corn oil was not significant.

At Xin Danxin Department Store, the reporter saw only two brands of corn oil products on promotion. The price of the 5-liter Chuyi corn oil dropped from the original 87.6 yuan to 77.8 yuan, while Dao Ma brand reduced its price from 106.2 yuan to 102 yuan. Other brands maintained their original prices. At the Hao youtuo supermarket, the price of the 1.8-liter Fulinmen corn oil dropped from the original 35 yuan to 31.5 yuan, and the 5-liter Chuyi corn oil fell from 69.9 yuan to 64.9 yuan.

In response, some analysts believe that as a small variety of oil, the low-price promotion of corn oil is a strategic retreat, and there may be a rebound after the Mid-Autumn Festival. A person from a certain supermarket in Guangzhou who did not wish to disclose their name also confirmed to the reporter that they had indeed received news about possible price increases for corn oil from edible oil companies, but no official notification has yet been received.

Corn oil prices expected to rise in the future

Although there is no confirmation from specific enterprises yet, the industry generally expects corn oil prices to rise in the future. A retail industry person analyzed that looking at historical data, this year's edible oil price increases mainly involved soybean oil, rapeseed oil, and blended oil. If edible oil prices continue to rise in the future, the main players will likely be corn oil and peanut oil.

On September 19, Shenwan Hongyuan issued a research report on Xiwang Food (000639.SZ), whose main business is corn oil. It believes that after entering the peak sales season of the fourth quarter this year, the price of small-packaged corn oil is expected to rise. It is predicted that around the Mid-Autumn Festival, corn oil prices will enter an upward channel under the influence of soybean oil prices, driving up gross margins.

In addition, the fully taxed price of American corn arriving at Chinese ports has also seen a slight increase recently. Data shows that the new contract December corn futures CZ1 reached a new high of $7.44. The FOB price of US No. 2 yellow corn for delivery in September was $316.2 per ton, equivalent to RMB 2024 per ton; after arriving at Chinese ports and being taxed, the total cost was approximately RMB 2782 per ton, up by RMB 12 per ton from the previous day, and up by RMB 633 per ton compared to the same period last year.
