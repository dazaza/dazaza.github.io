Strategies and Countermeasures for Continuous Development of China's Bag Enterprises: A Systematic Introduction to Domestic Bag Production Enterprises

Currently, the bag production enterprises in China can be roughly divided into the following types: one type is formed by foreign investment; the second type is invested and built by domestic investors such as Zhejiang merchants; the third type is formed by domestic bag merchants moving up the industrial chain; the fourth type is the diversified extension of clothing enterprises; the fifth type is established by workers engaged in the production management of the bag industry. Among them, more than half are not registered with the industrial and commercial authorities. In bag production enterprises, less than 1% of the factories operate their own brands, some have brands but have not formed a sales synergy.

The vast majority of production enterprises are mainly OME (Original Manufacturer Export). This is because: (1) their own brand has no advantage in terms of fame and promotion, (2) customers have their own brands and selling power, (3) customers do not need branded products.

The sources of customers for bag enterprises are mainly: 1. A very small part sells domestically and internationally, 2. A very small part directly collaborates with terminal retailers or wholesale companies through exhibitions and the internet, 3. 50% do orders for foreign trade companies, that is, through intermediaries, 4. More than 30% do processing orders for large factories.

The profit source for bag enterprises: processing fees. It can be said that there is little profit. There is a saying in the industry: workers within eight hours are doing their own wages, and overtime is making profits for the boss. It is normal for workers in the bag industry to work more than ten hours a day.

Challenges Faced by Domestic Bag Enterprises

Therefore, domestic bag enterprises have a strong desire to directly contact customers and directly take orders. Only in this way can they get rid of the dilemma of low profit and the inability to develop their own brands, and embark on a path of continuous development. But they also face the following challenges:

I. Product-related issues

Due to information and internal problems, most bag production enterprises do not have the time, energy, or funds to develop products that meet the needs of the domestic and international markets, and even lack information on what products are needed in the market.

II. Talent-related issues

One, there is a serious shortage of talent in product development. There is a saying in the industry: China has no designers in the bag industry, they only know how to lose money for the factory. Two, due to budget, office environment, and scale issues, it is difficult for enterprises to introduce marketing talents. Because of many enterprises' intentional or unintentional short-sighted actions, it is impossible to invest funds and time in long-term investments.

III. System and Thinking-related Issues

Domestic bag production enterprises often have a strong family flavor, low sensitivity to the market, and rarely have clear development plans. Many factories have everything done by family members, making it difficult to attract and retain outside talents.

Issues That Bag Production Enterprises Should Pay Attention To

Based on the current situation of the domestic and international bag industries, facing the continuously increasing trade barriers internationally and increasingly fierce competition domestically; facing the adjustment of the RMB exchange rate and the rise of domestic labor costs, I believe that production enterprises should focus on the following aspects:

I. Avoid blindly expanding production scale, it’s better to strengthen than just enlarge.

In the production enterprise sector, many people believe that bigger is better. In fact, the expansion of capacity must be related to the stability of customers and the strength of self-owned funds. The international bag market is becoming increasingly complex due to environmental protection and price factors. It is possible that just after borrowing money to build factories and buy equipment, orders will decrease. Expanding capacity through horizontal integration obviously has stronger risk resistance than expanding capacity on your own.

II. Formulate a Long-term Product Development Plan

Good products are not something that can be created today by hiring people tomorrow. I suggest starting small in design and pattern-making, beginning from looking at pictures (collected from the market and fashion shows), and allowing designers to interact more with the market and customers. If funds permit, you can cooperate with overseas design institutions to gradually cultivate your own design personnel. In product development, taking the path from copying patterns to following diagrams to independent development should be the lower-risk option.

III. Build a Marketing Team Gradually

A good marketing team not only sells the company's products but also provides significant assistance in product development. Building a marketing team is also a necessary measure to shorten distribution channels, increase profits, and reduce risks during corporate development. The size of the investment depends on the company's strength. I recommend keeping the investment within an affordable range. Initially, the scale should not be too large. You can choose online marketing (starting from B2B platforms, starting from suitable platforms for yourself, don't blindly participate in competition, it will definitely have no effect, just using your hard-earned money to support others), website promotion, exhibition participation, etc., but avoid being comprehensive and all-encompassing at the start. This requires a process of growing from small to large, exploring experience, and accumulating talent.

IV. Solve the Problem of Having the Right Mindset

Many companies want to see returns immediately after investing in product development and marketing platform construction. Many companies stop operations once they see no short-term income. This is actually detrimental to their own development. I believe: according to its own strength to construct product development and marketing platforms, initially do not invest too much, gradually increase investment, as long as there are initial achievements, even if there are no results, it does not matter, this will certainly develop well. Initially, do not rely on product development and marketing platforms to bring orders to the company. Treat them as hopes for the future of the company. With the right mindset, given time, it will surely become a competitive company.
