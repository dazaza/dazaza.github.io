How to Distinguish the Quality of Baijiu

When selecting baijiu, you can use the following methods to distinguish its quality:

If the packaging is a colorless transparent glass bottle, hold the bottle in your hand, slowly turn it upside down, and observe the bottom of the bottle against the light. If there are any substances sinking or cloud-like phenomena, it indicates that the alcohol contains more impurities. If the alcohol does not lose luster, is not turbid, and has no suspended matter, it suggests that the quality of the alcohol is better. Because in terms of color, except for soy sauce-flavored alcohol, most baijiu should be colorless and transparent. If the alcohol is packaged in a porcelain bottle or colored glass bottle, slightly shake it before opening, and similarly observe its color and sediment.

Pour the alcohol into a colorless transparent glass, and observe it under natural light. The baijiu should be clear and transparent, with no suspended matter or sediment; then smell its aroma by bringing your nose close to the rim of the glass to identify the intensity and characteristics of the aroma.

Taste its flavor by drinking a small amount of alcohol and spreading it over your tongue, distinguishing the thickness, smoothness, harmony, roughness, as well as the balance of sour, sweet, bitter, and spicy flavors, and whether there is a lingering aftertaste and how long it lasts. Low-grade inferior baijiu is usually made from poor-quality or moldy grains, with crude processes, often impersonating famous brand alcohol or popular alcohol. Alcohol that causes throat irritation and headaches when consumed must be of inferior quality.

Place a drop of baijiu on your palm, then rub your palms together forcefully for a few moments. If the alcohol emits a fragrant smell when heated, it is of high quality; if the smell is sweet, it is mid-range alcohol; if the smell is bitter or foul, it is of inferior quality.

Drop a single drop of cooking oil into the alcohol. If the oil spreads irregularly and sinks at a noticeably changing speed, it indicates that the alcohol is of inferior quality.

Baijiu has a high alcohol content and is not prone to spoilage, but it has strong permeability. Generally, bottled baijiu can remain clear and free of sediment for up to 1 year under conditions ranging from 0°C to 30°C.

Baijiu is not "the older, the better." After being stored for a long time, low-alcohol baijiu may experience hydrolysis of acetic acid substances, leading to a bland taste. Therefore, when purchasing low-alcohol baijiu, it's best to choose products with a production date within two years.
