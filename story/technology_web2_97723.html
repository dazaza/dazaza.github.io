Viewing the Six Degrees of Space Theory Through "Friend Buying and Selling" - Longer Version

by longersing on 2008-10-21 02:18:28

By Longer

What's the most popular game on the Internet today? What game is the most relaxing and carefree? What game has the most interactive users? "Friends Buying & Selling," "Stealing Cars," and many other web-based games centered around online interaction have begun to infiltrate every corner of the internet. SEO Tribe will use the "Friends Buying & Selling" game as an example to tell you what the Six Degrees of Separation theory is, and how to make your own website stand out in a fiercely competitive environment.

With the rise of SNS (Social Networking Services) websites, more creative social interaction ideas have emerged, gradually showcasing the advantages of social networking services in portal competition. In recent years, a wave of SNS-focused online trends has swept through domestic websites in China, with representative examples including Xiaonei (Renren), Kaixin001, MySpace, and portal sites like Sina. These websites can all be considered exemplary models of SNS in China, worthy of study. We'll take Sina Spaces as an example. After Sina launched its Spaces service, one of the biggest changes over the past year or so has been the addition of some seemingly simple yet intriguing mini-games. These games integrate our real-life experiences into the online world, making them especially suitable for office workers to play. Some people say: it’s these games that bring people closer together, and this statement is absolutely true.

In recent years, the applications of SNS in China have mainly been reflected in the interactive gaming segments modeled after Facebook. "Friends Buying & Selling" is a game that not only entertains but also helps players make more friends.
