The early technical characteristics of dark horse stocks are mainly reflected in three aspects.

by gpgp6486 on 2009-11-22 14:54:11

The early technical characteristics of dark horse stocks are mainly reflected in three aspects. The early technical features of dark horse stocks are mainly reflected in the following areas: First, the pattern. The vast majority of dark horse stocks have a relatively long accumulation period before they start moving, lasting from 1 to 4 months, or even longer. In terms of candlestick patterns, this is represented by a prolonged consolidation phase. For such consolidations, several key points should be noted: one is the position. On the ORSI overlay chart of Jinzhi software, the low-level consolidation should be near the 20-degree line, preferably hovering around one grid below the 20-degree line. According to statistics, among more than a dozen dark horse stocks that soared 2-5 times in recent years, 70% consolidated for many days around one grid below the 20-degree line. This indicates that the forces driving the price down have been largely exhausted, and the stock price has basically stabilized in the oversold area. For example, Yian Technology (0008) from the second half of 1998 to January 1999 exhibited such a situation.

Secondly, the distribution of chips. A low-level consolidation does not necessarily mean that the market maker has completed the accumulation process. If you get involved too early, your funds will be locked up for quite a while, which is extremely inefficient in terms of overall capital scheduling. Therefore, you can closely monitor the floating chip distribution index of Hairong software and the CYQ index of Compass. Generally speaking, if the chips at the low level appear scattered, it indicates that the entire situation is in the initial stage of horizontal accumulation, and there is still a long way to go, so there is no need to rush in. In the later stages of consolidation, the chips at the low level will form an extremely compressed situation on the indicators. The more flattened and elongated the compression, or even forming fine lines, the better the selection, because such appearances not only indicate the basic completion of horizontal accumulation but also show the market maker's strong strength and determination. According to statistics, the compression degree of chips at the low level for major dark horse stocks in recent years has been quite tight. For example, Acheng Steel (600799) had such a shape in its chip distribution on December 4, 1998.

Second, the shape of the candlesticks. The candlestick patterns of dark horse stocks during their bottom consolidation often have distinct characteristics, not only being very tightly and neatly arranged, but also appearing as granular shapes, also known as "small bean arrangements." In fact, the "small bean arrangement" is quite common. The difference lies in: the small bean arrangement of dark horse stocks is lower in position compared to other stocks' mid-to-high level consolidations; and the duration is longer. The small bean arrangement of dark horse stocks often runs through the entire bottom consolidation period. If the stock adopts a "slow bull" method to move upwards when starting, this small bean arrangement situation will continue for a long time, and may even accompany the stock price to an unimaginable height. The small bean arrangement indicates a small amplitude of stock price fluctuation, showing that the market maker not only holds a large number of chips but also has a strong ability to control the market. Therefore, experienced stock traders often say: "No need to panic, no need to rush, follow the small beans when they rise." For example, Oriental Pearl (600832) had a typical small bean arrangement before its start.

Third, volume. The trading volume of the vast majority of dark horse stocks during their bottom consolidation periods will significantly shrink, forming a long-term flat average volume line on the volume indicators, like pearls strung together with fine threads. However, it should be noted that although direct observation has straightforward effects, it is not as precise and reliable as using technical indicators, because some individual stocks may experience a significant shrinkage in trading volume, but the volume energy indicators are still not adjusted properly. I believe that using the OBV overlay chart of Jinzhi software in actual combat can accurately observe the situation where the volume energy of dark horse stocks is adjusted properly before they start moving. For example, at the beginning of 1999, many stock analysts favored quality stocks, believing that since quality stocks had fallen for a long time, they would definitely have a wave of momentum soon. But the market played a big joke - the quality stocks at the time showed no signs of improvement, instead, a batch of junk stocks rose significantly. The reason was that the quality stocks at the time were not adjusted properly, while a batch of junk stocks not only had their trading volume shrunk, but their OBV volume energy indicators had already been lying flat at the bottom for many days, just as the saying goes: "The longer it is stored, the faster it will burst." The reason is: any market maker who wants to create a dark horse must hold a considerable amount of chips at the bottom. And the fact that OBV has been lying flat at the bottom for a long time is precisely the direct reflection of its accumulation. Careful observers can find that: in the OBV overlay chart below, the OBV indicator basically forms a straight line, while the stock price fluctuates more than OBV.

Generally speaking, when the stock price touches the OBV indicator lying flat below for the second time (sometimes the third time), this standby dark horse will gallop within a short period. The situation of Founder Technology (600601) in January 1998 was a typical example.
