In 2010, we experienced a year of rapid development for e-commerce. At the beginning of 2011, Premier Wen Jiabao emphasized in the "Government Work Report": "Actively develop new service formats such as e-commerce, online shopping, and geographic information." Thus, e-commerce will enter an era of fierce competition.

Competition drives development. Currently, the competition among e-commerce businesses mainly focuses on quantity rather than quality. Most offline enterprises have yet to realize the opportunities in e-commerce. Compared to the vast brand competition in the offline market, the real competitive era of the online economy is about to arrive, elevating the competition in e-commerce to a new level. This presents both opportunities and challenges for many Chinese e-commerce companies.

Currently, many e-commerce businesses face issues primarily revolving around the core of "non-professionalism." Non-proficiency in products, branding, marketing, management, finance, etc., are the main factors leading to the lack of growth momentum in Chinese e-commerce and represent the most disadvantageous aspect in future competitions.

Solving these inadequacies is crucial for the development of Chinese e-commerce. Not being professional isn't necessarily bad; economic giants like Jack Ma and Zong Qinghou did not start with specialized expertise. The most frightening thing for an e-commerce business is lacking a clear understanding and self-reflection. To become successful, one must first consider the thresholds and determine what can be resolved and what cannot, and how external forces can help address those issues, especially in the emerging professional field of e-commerce. A clearer understanding of this point is essential.

To tackle these "non-professional" issues, the upcoming "First China E-Commerce and Media Marketing Summit Forum" will focus on them. With the theme of "Integrating Media Planning into E-commerce," the forum will center on the development of e-commerce and media marketing. Through the analysis and interpretation of actual operational cases, it will address hot topics such as "how to introduce professional management through talent acquisition, how offline traditional enterprises can open online channels, how online e-commerce can complement traditional enterprises, how e-commerce can utilize media resources, how traditional media can scientifically and effectively engage in e-commerce and increase advertising, how media and enterprises on e-commerce platforms can achieve breakthroughs in win-win cooperation, and how e-commerce enterprises can obtain investment and financing." The aim is to make participating enterprises and media more "professional" or closer to "professionalism" in the field of e-commerce, thereby helping enterprises enhance their core competitiveness, extend their life cycle, and gain broader development space.

Success may not be replicated but can be transplanted. The forum will invite a group of internet economy giants to share their successful experiences and stories. Their sharing is expected to provide unique insights and experiences for the participants, achieving the forum's ultimate goal: guiding success.

Guided by the China Advertising Association's Newspaper Division and Television Division, and organized by Guanglian Times (Beijing) Information Technology Co., Ltd. and Beijing Zhongheng Helili Management Consulting Co., Ltd., the forum content will cover multiple fields such as e-commerce operations, planning, media marketing, advertising placement, traditional enterprise e-commerce, venture capital, and capital operations.
