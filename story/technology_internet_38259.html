Loosening of travel domain name policy may trigger a rush registration wave

by now on 2007-12-07 10:36:13

The latest news shows that starting December 21, the .travel registry will make significant adjustments to the policies regarding the travel domain names. The registration conditions will be further relaxed, making it more convenient and faster to register a travel domain name at that time. However, experts have also expressed concerns that before the relaxation of .travel domain registration, the strict approval process had, to some extent, protected the rights of certain enterprises. Now, with the relaxation of conditions, there might be a rush among domain investors.

It is reported that the .travel domain is a new global top-level domain exclusively for the tourism industry, just like the .com/.net domains, which are globally available. On December 31, 2006, the international .travel domain registry announced the global open registration of .travel domain names, and Era Interconnect became China's first .travel domain service provider.

In the 2007 National Tourism Informatization Work Conference, Vice Minister of National Tourism Administration Du Jiang proposed that "the tourism information website recommends using the global tourism-specific .travel suffix domain." Previously, the registration and approval of .travel domain names were always very strict, allowing only 18 tourism-related industries such as amusement parks, accommodation and catering, tourism associations, hotel entertainment, etc., to register, and usually requiring relevant materials to be provided. Although the procedures were relatively complicated, statistics show that nearly 1,000 well-known tourism organizations successfully registered .Travel through Era Interconnect (www.now.cn). These registrations mainly concentrated on tourism information portals, tourism bureaus, and tourist attractions, such as the General Agency of China International Travel Service cits.travel, Ctrip ctrip.travel, Yunnan yunan.travel, Huangshan huangshan.travel, etc.

Due to the overly complicated approval process for .travel domain names in the past, many related domestic enterprises hesitated. Recently, the registration conditions for .travel domain names will be relaxed. It was reported that the new policy would be implemented starting December 21. At that time, local names will be allowed to register; special names will no longer be restricted; and the information submitted will no longer require certification from relevant departments. The confirmation authorization deadline will also be extended, allowing certification to be provided within three days before or after successful registration.

As the top-level domain for the tourism industry, .travel is a valuable asset for the tourism industry, playing an incomparable role in enhancing corporate image, promoting tourism resources, and increasing visibility. Experts remind that the relaxation of the .travel policy provides convenience for Chinese tourism-related enterprises to protect their .travel brands but also offers opportunities for domain investors. Therefore, it is suggested that enterprises should protect their tourism network trademarks as soon as possible.
