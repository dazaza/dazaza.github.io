Law enforcement sparks controversy, police car surrounded for two hours, villagers beaten by law enforcement officers for violating traffic rules and village insulted

by qiyouchax02 on 2011-05-20 09:57:00

Kangjiazhuang Village, where the police car was surrounded.

Villagers: Traffic police beat the driver

At 7:10 PM on July 27th, Li Qiang (a pseudonym), a villager from Kangjiazhuang Village of Chan Kou Town in Dingxi City's An Ding District, was sitting at his courtyard gate playing cards with neighbors and friends when suddenly, an agricultural motorized tricycle sped by on the street in front of his house. A police car was closely chasing behind it, with only about 10 meters between the two vehicles. According to Li Qiang, after the tricycle and the police car entered the village, the police car weaved back and forth four or five times before finally stopping the tricycle next to a garbage pile at a corner of a wall. One traffic policeman pulled the tricycle driver out of the cab, while two other policemen proceeded to punch the driver’s face and chest, also constantly cursing at him with very offensive language.

"At that time, dinner had just finished, and there were many people in the village. The incident drew quite a crowd. When they saw the traffic police beating someone, some villagers shouted 'The police are beating someone.' After hearing the shouts from the villagers, the three traffic policemen stopped," said a villager from Kangjiazhuang who was present at the scene on the morning of July 28th.

Police: Drunken Villagers Causing Trouble

On the afternoon of the 28th, Deputy Director of An Ding District Traffic Police Brigade and Captain of Chan Kou Team He Biao said in an interview that on the evening of the 27th, he led five traffic policemen on duty on the road. During this time, he left the duty point due to some matters. At 7:56 PM, he received a call from the duty traffic police, informing him that they had detained an agricultural motorized tricycle being driven by a drunk driver.
