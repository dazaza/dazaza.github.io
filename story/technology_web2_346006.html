4.15 Global economy is doing well, A-share market is ready to break out after a long accumulation

by huaxun880 on 2010-04-15 13:19:50

Recently, we have noticed that the global economy is rising strongly and there are signs of overheating. For example, data released by Singapore's Ministry of Trade and Industry shows that after seasonal adjustments, the country's GDP grew at an annual rate of 32.1% in the first quarter. The Monetary Authority of Singapore announced a tightening of monetary policy, meaning that the central bank would shift its policy focus from stimulating economic recovery to curbing consumer prices. In addition, Eurostat announced that the eurozone’s industrial production rose 0.9% month-on-month in February, and increased 4.1% compared to the same period last year, indicating that the eurozone economy is also growing while following the global economic recovery, which is beneficial for the stability and rebound of the euro. From the recently released data, strong growth has been observed in U.S. consumers, non-farm employment, and manufacturing. Under the global expectation wave of "exit plans," the Federal Reserve might consider raising interest rates and curbing inflation in the future, but not currently. Because Chairman of the Federal Reserve Ben Bernanke indicated in his testimony to Congress on the 14th that he would continue to maintain the current low interest rate. However, he also stated that the trend of U.S. economic recovery was evident. After his speech, the dollar fell, and U.S. stocks rose. Moreover, the earnings reports of U.S. listed companies were better than expected. Intel's net profit in the first quarter was $2.4 billion, up 288% year-on-year. Banking giant JPMorgan Chase announced that its net profit in the first financial quarter increased by 55% year-on-year, exceeding market expectations. A report from the U.S. Department of Commerce showed that retail sales in March increased by 1.6% month-on-month, the largest increase since November last year. Good earnings reports from listed companies and economic data, plus Bernanke's statement that the current interest rate would be maintained, greatly boosted investors' confidence in future earnings reports of other companies, stimulating the rise in U.S. stocks. The S&P 500 Index broke through the 1200 point mark for the first time since late September 2008, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average rose by more than one hundred points. The U.S. dollar will wait for the final clarification of the Federal Reserve's monetary policy in a volatile adjustment manner, while commodity prices will remain high for a period of time.

In the context of the overall good performance of the global economy, China's economy is not faring too badly either. After all, China is one of the countries that emerged from the financial crisis first. Today, the National Bureau of Statistics will release the first quarter data, and many experts and industry insiders believe that the GDP growth rate will exceed 11%, triggering concerns in the market about overheating of the economy. Therefore, it can be said that the strength of China's economic recovery does not fall short of the global economy. But looking back, the domestic stock market, which reflects the barometer of the economy, including stock software, is one of the worst-performing markets globally in the first quarter. Why such a contradictory situation occurs?

The author believes that the contradiction between the A-share market and economic development reflects the contradiction between policies and the economy. The focus of China's economy this year is to find a balance point between preventing inflation and maintaining economic growth. We must ensure that the economy does not experience a second bottom, while also worrying about overheating. In the game between policy and the economy, what is most affected is precisely the A-share market. The author believes that in the balance between recovery and overheating, national policies prioritize the former, which can also be seen from the current state of China's economy: ensuring growth in the first half of the year and preventing inflation in the second half. Therefore, if there is any market activity this year, it can only occur in the first half, specifically in the second quarter. The A-share market will accumulate strength before bursting forth; the bearish factors of policies to be implemented in the second quarter, such as interest rate hikes, have already been digested in the first quarter’s trends. The second quarter will advance amidst volatility. Considering the current status of market liquidity, the author previously mentioned that the second quarter will see a market scenario where blue chips provide the stage and individual stocks take center stage. Blue chip stocks cannot lead the market in a single upward trend; investors should focus their energy on selecting individual stocks. At the end of the first quarter, the author repeatedly emphasized that the aviation sector has already taken off. Next, the defense industry will undoubtedly take over. Let's wait and see together.
