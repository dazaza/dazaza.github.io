On February 22, a female doctor at a hospital in Shantou complained about collecting bodies late at night via Weibo, hoping the patient would wait for her to finish work before dying, which sparked public outrage. However, before this incident had even settled, netizens from Jinshan Jintan exposed that a nurse at a certain hospital in Jintan had insulted patients on Weibo, once again drawing the attention of netizens. The hospital responded online that after seeing this post, our medical department is investigating, and if true, we will deal with it seriously and announce the results. Yesterday afternoon, reporters came to the hospital where the nurse worked to investigate and understand the situation. Quick reporter Zhou Qing Chao Jing.

Netizens expose nurses posting Weibo to insult patients

On the morning of February 27, the post titled "A female nurse in a certain hospital in Jintan insults patients on Weibo" became the focus of discussion among netizens on the Jinshan Jintan forum. This morning, while browsing Sina Weibo casually, I found a nurse wrote the following sentences. After flipping through, it turned out to be from Jintan, but it was unclear which hospital. A few days ago, a netizen posted that a female doctor somewhere complained about tidying up late at night, hoping the patient would wait until she finished work before dying. Now another one has emerged, all full of complaints. Poster Mai Zi took screenshots of the owner's photos and texts from Sina Weibo and attached the link to the Weibo.

The text screenshot shows: (swear words), what's wrong with you old patient, (swear words) ah, wait until I become an old nurse, I'll kill you. The time of the post was 10 PM on October 30, 2010. The girl in the photo wearing a nurse's uniform is smiling, looking very young.

Yesterday afternoon, when reporters tried to open the link to the Weibo, they found that the Weibo account had been canceled, and there was no content on the Weibo page.

Netizens searched that the nurse started working in December last year

After the post about this female nurse was exposed online, it immediately drew a large number of netizens' attention. Netizen Jintan Real Talk also searched for the identity of this female nurse. He continuously took screenshots of Sina Weibo posts from October 30 and 31, 2010, proving that the content of the post by netizen Mai Zi was completely true. From the numerous screenshots of Sina Weibo uploaded by this netizen, it can be seen that the nurse comes from a certain hospital in Jintan City, was born in 1989, entered the hospital in 2010, and obtained her nursing license on December 15, 2010.

From the Weibo post on February 22, 2011, it can be seen that she seems dissatisfied with her current job, starting to dislike night shifts. What a load of crap, sister devoted the most precious sleep, or even youth, to you, and didn't get a single moment of peace throughout the night, which made me so angry, (swear words), I'll just leave, regardless of whether it's a career-based position or not, life is short, I want to be carefree, go home and be my little princess.

Netizen Jintan Real Talk also deduced from the red motorcycle and iPad posted on the nurse's Weibo that she might be a rich second generation.

Netizens debate, majority condemn, some show understanding

After the post spread, most netizens felt angry, while a small part expressed sympathy. She doesn't deserve to do this job, lacking even basic professional ethics! Netizen Master Tang Sanzang said. Netizen Dingdingding also believed that it was inappropriate for the nurse to write such things, too outrageous, your job is to save lives! We spend so much money on medical treatment, only to get this kind of service attitude? Netizen Blizzard Rain clearly felt very angry.

Prominent doctor-patient conflicts, young people venting emotions are understandable. Disapprove of being punished for speech. Netizen Lang Taoshajin showed sympathy for the nurse. Netizen Shh, Quiet also thought, now the pressure on nurses is not ordinary, doctors are hard to talk to, patients are hard to serve, exams are endless, mental and physical torture, occasionally venting how? Who cares about the nurses? Give some freedom space, people, why make it hard for others?

Netizen New Words believed: Nurses are indeed very busy, under great pressure! We have all been sick, and have all been to hospitals! We should recall those scenes! Sometimes nurses endure委屈without anyone comforting them! Not easy! They really need to vent, but absolutely cannot take it out on patients, whether or not they become old nurses! Must remember the saying "A doctor's heart is like a parent's."

Hospital response: from common sense, it shouldn't be her who posted it

Yesterday afternoon, reporters went to the hospital where the nurse works in Jintan City, confirmed by the hospital that the exposed female nurse is Ji Ping (a pseudonym) from the neurology department of the hospital, but both the head of the nursing department and the head nurse of the neurology department stated that this Weibo post should not have been made by Ji Ping.

The head nurse told reporters that on the morning of February 27, after learning about this matter, she immediately called Ji Ping to the office. Ji Ping then flipped through her Weibo posts one by one for the head nurse to see. I saw one from October 28, 2010, and another from October 31, but I did not see the one from October 30. The head nurse said that she did not tell Ji Ping beforehand about checking her Weibo, so Ji Ping probably did not know about the post on Jinshan Jintan. The head nurse said: After Ji Ping knew about this matter, she was stunned, crouching next to the bed in the office, not saying a word.

Although her family background is good, she works very actively, and there has never been any negative information reported to me here. The head nurse said. The director of the nursing department also felt: from common sense, these words should not come from Ji Ping's mouth. Last year, she learned early about her acceptance into the hospital, came to familiarize herself with the environment first, arriving on October 25th, the time of this Weibo post was October 30th, having been at the hospital for only five days, still in an excited phase, there should not be such complaints. In fact, she officially reported to the hospital around November 20th, 2010, and as a new nurse, she only followed experienced nurses to collect medicine and add medicine, and did not work alone on shifts, so basically did not directly interact with patients, let alone have disputes.

Yesterday, the hospital replied online: after seeing this post, our medical department is investigating, and if true, we will handle it seriously and announce the results.
