What are the benefits of eating durian?

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-24 15:00:44

Durian

Benefits: It is hot in nature, especially beneficial for replenishing the cold deficiency of postpartum women.

Hazards: Overeating can lead to breathing difficulties, red face, and bloating.

The King of Fruits - Durian

The durian is a famous high-quality fruit. Produced in various Southeast Asian countries, Thailand has developed this variety in recent years, with production increasing year by year. The durian belongs to the Bombacaceae family of evergreen trees, reaching heights of 15-20 meters. Its leaves are long and oval, with pointed tips. It has cyme inflorescences, with flowers of a pale yellow color. The fruits are about the size of a football, with a hard shell densely covered in triangular spines. The flesh is composed of the meaty covering of the pseudo-seed coat, with a light yellow color, sticky and juicy, soft and sweet, giving it an ice cream-like texture when eaten. The durian fruit contains 11% starch, 13% sugar, 3% protein, as well as multiple vitamins, making its nutrition quite rich. Thai people consume durians after illness or childbirth to nourish their bodies. Locals consider it the "King of Tropical Fruits."

Every part of the durian is valuable. Its shell cooked with bone soup makes for an excellent tonic. It's said that the nutritional and medicinal value of its seeds is extraordinary, considered a treasure by locals. Some high-ranking officials even bring durians as prestigious gifts during foreign visits. There was once a Thai saying: "When durians appear, sarongs are discarded." This means that young women would rather sell their skirts to enjoy a full meal of durians.

According to legend, during the Ming Dynasty, Admiral Zheng He led his fleet on three voyages to the South Seas. Due to the long time spent at sea, many sailors were eager to return home. One day, Zheng He discovered a pile of strange fruits on land. He picked up several and shared them with everyone. Unexpectedly, most of the sailors praised them, temporarily forgetting their homesickness. Someone asked Zheng He what the fruit was called, and he casually replied, "Liu Lian." Later, it was transformed into "Liu Lian" (Durian). Nowadays, people in the Guangdong region have also become fond of it, from large supermarkets to small fruit stalls, all are filled with durians. Truly, no fruit stall is complete without durians, and sales continue to rise.

However, opinions on the taste of durians vary. Some praise it as smooth as cream, leaving a lingering aroma, drooling, and loving it dearly. Others find it as foul-smelling as cat feces, unbearable to the nose, and choose to avoid it.

There are various types of durians, such as Jin Yin, Jia Lun, and Qing Ni, with Jin Yin being the best quality and most precious. When purchasing durians, be sure to ask about the variety first, don't buy Jia Lun or Qing Ni at the price of Jin Yin. Durians that naturally split open after ripening should not be left for too long; if they smell of alcohol when sniffed, they have certainly gone bad and should not be purchased.

Unopened durians, unripe ones have a grassy smell, while ripe ones emit the characteristic aroma of durians. If you purchase an unripe durian, wrap it in newspaper at home, light the newspaper, and after it burns out, wrap it again in newspaper and place it in a warm area. After one or two days, if you can smell the fragrance, it indicates that it has ripened. To eat, gently drop it on the ground to create a crack, then pry it open from the crack and enjoy. First-time eaters can plug their noses with soft objects, focusing only on the taste in their mouths, and avoid smelling its odor. After three or four times, you can bravely smell it, and you will find only intoxicating fragrance. There is an indescribable charm that only you will know.

Although durians are good, they should not be overeaten at once because of their rich nutrition, which your intestines and stomach may not fully absorb, leading to heatiness. If you eat too much, you can eat a few mangosteens, as only the Queen of Fruits can subdue the King of Fruits.
