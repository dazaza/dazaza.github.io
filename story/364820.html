Speaking of a series of changes that have taken place in the teaching staff of the district,

by baleta on 2010-09-17 09:55:33

A complex case of identity substitution among teachers in a certain district is discussed, and Yuan Lei openly talks about the complexity of this case. His ID card shows a name, birth month and year - 0.

Yuan Lei reported another substitute, Yuan Yongtian, to the disciplinary inspection office of the Yingquan District Education Bureau in Fuyang City. Photo by reporter Ye Tieliao. "It's a bit messy, a bit messy," said Li Yang, head of the disciplinary inspection office of the Education Bureau of Yingquan District, Fuyang City, Anhui Province, when discussing the complexity of a chain substitution case that occurred among the teaching staff in the district. In this chain substitution case, A, who did not meet the conditions, obtained a primary school teaching position under the name of B, who did meet the conditions. A year later, B reported A, A was laid off, and B took over the position; but A also reported B, saying that B had also substituted for someone else's academic record in secondary school and engaged in numerous falsifications of his resume and qualifications during the process of becoming a teacher. In January 2008, the People's Procuratorate of Yingquan District, Fuyang City, investigated this chain substitution case, concluding that the situation was true, and formed a "Procuratorial Opinion Letter," demanding necessary party and administrative disciplinary actions against the responsible individuals and handling of B's teacher status. However, two years have passed, and neither the responsible individuals have been dealt with, nor has B lost their teacher status. This substitution case also exposed issues such as management chaos and uneven levels within the grassroots teaching staff in some regions. It has a warning significance for strengthening grassroots teacher team building. Substituting a younger brother as a teacher, then being laid off after being reported. When first meeting Yuan Lei, it is hard to believe the birth year shown on his ID: 1979. "I was born in 1973," Yuan Lei openly admitted. On his ID card, the surname, date of birth, and ID number all belong to someone else. The name on the ID is "Yuan Shutian." Since he turned 26, whether it was during the two years he worked as a teacher or the subsequent years he went out to work, he has been called "Yuan Shutian." He has long since become accustomed to this identity. During the two years he was called "Teacher Yuan Shutian," he felt a sense of identity and pride. Today, in the materials he uses to report others, he signs both names: Yuan Lei and Yuan Shutian. In 1999, Yuan Lei became a primary school teacher under the name of his uncle's son. At that time, his cousin was named "Yuan Shutian," who graduated from the Huainan Finance School in Anhui Province (now renamed as the Vocational and Technical College Department of Huainan) in 1998. But after graduation, his cousin failed to secure formal employment and chose to go out to work. In 1998, according to the district committee's No. 13 meeting minutes document, Yingquan District in Fuyang City issued a notice stating that graduates from previous years who had not been assigned jobs would undergo pre-service teacher training at Ninglaozhuang Vocational High School. Those who passed the exams could be transferred to become formal state teachers. After graduating from junior high school, Yuan Lei soon went out to serve in the army, serving for five years from 1991 to 1996. Upon returning, he had no formal job. One day, while visiting his uncle, Yuan Lei learned that his uncle had used his cousin "Yuan Shutian's" name and managed to secure a training quota. But the uncle was over 40 years old and had two children, so even if he became a teacher, he probably wouldn't be able to support his family. After Yuan Lei took up the position as a primary school teacher, he discovered that his monthly salary was only 130 yuan, paid by the township government, and not on a monthly basis, but rather distributed twice a year during the summer and autumn harvests. Yuan Lei said that his cousin "Yuan Shutian" didn't return for the training because he thought the salary was too low. "He himself didn't want this qualification anymore." "I said to my second uncle, 'If you don't go, I'll go,' and he said, 'Go ahead.'" In July 1999, Yuan Lei attended the Ninglaozhuang pre-service teacher training program. In August, after passing the exam, he obtained the "Fuyang City Yingquan District Teacher Training Completion Certificate" and was subsequently assigned to teach mathematics to the second grade at Shangying Primary School in Wenji Town, Yingquan District. Thereafter, Yuan Lei became "Teacher Yuan Shutian." Afterwards, Yuan Lei also used the name "Yuan Shutian" to apply for an ID card and complete the procedures for transferring to civil service. However, the glory brought by the identity of "Teacher Yuan Shutian" came to an abrupt end on a day in March 2000. That day, Shang Congzhong, then director of the Wenji Town Education Office, notified him to stop teaching and wait for further instructions. The reason was that his teaching qualification was reported by his cousin "Yuan Shutian," accusing him of impersonation. Yuan Lei then learned that his cousin returned from working outside and also wanted to teach. Yuan Lei thought that if his identity was uncovered and he couldn't continue teaching, that was fine, but his cousin hadn't participated in the pre-service teacher training and didn't meet the spirit of Document No. 13 of the meeting minutes, which stated that non-teacher graduates should be arranged to teach in remote towns and must undergo relevant subject training at Ninglaozhuang Vocational High School. However, in 2003, he learned that someone was teaching under the name "Yuan Shutian" at another primary school in Wenji Town, and that person was his cousin. He believed that his cousin saw the benefits of being a teacher and thus exerted effort. "After 2000, being a teacher became increasingly attractive. Paid by the finance department, a teacher earns more than 800 yuan a month, and the salary is paid monthly, which is quite good in rural areas. Now it has increased to over 1000 yuan." Yuan Lei stopped tolerating it. He began reporting his cousin and claimed that his cousin's real name wasn't "Yuan Shutian" either; he had also substituted for someone else's name.
