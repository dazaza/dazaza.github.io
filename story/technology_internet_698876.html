Three Ministries Release First Batch of Tax Exemption Vehicle Models Catalogue

by abcdfdhehe on 2012-03-09 11:37:29

The Ministry of Finance, the State Administration, and the MIIT jointly issued a notice yesterday stipulating that starting from January 1 this year, vehicle and vessel tax for energy-saving cars and ships will be halved; while for new-energy vehicles and vessels, the tax will be exempted. Meanwhile, the three commissions also released the first list of vehicle models eligible for reduced or exempted vehicle and vessel tax, making explicit indications for specific models for the first time.

The first batch of models is mainly small-displacement gasoline cars.

The first directory announced nearly 50 energy-saving vehicle models with halved vehicle and vessel tax, and over 170 electric vehicle models exempt from vehicle and vessel tax.

The "Notice" proposed specific criteria for recognizing new-energy vehicles fully exempt from vehicle and vessel tax. In addition, the new-energy vehicle models are consistent with those defined in the draft of the "Energy Saving and New Energy Automobile Industry Plan" released by the MIIT in 2010. However, in the specific published list of exempted models, currently only three plug-in hybrid models from FAW, Geely, and BYD have been included.

The first model directory includes nearly 50 passenger car products from ten joint venture and domestic brands, all with engine displacements below 1.6L, including popular models such as Volkswagen Golf, Magotan, Lavida; Chevrolet Aveo, Buick Excelle; Nissan Sunny, Qida, Chery QQ, etc., and these are basically all models that previously enjoyed a 3000 yuan energy-saving people's welfare subsidy.

Industry insiders believe that this reduction in vehicle and vessel tax shifts subsidies from the purchasing stage to the consumption stage, but the subsidy amount is somewhat lower compared to the original one-time 3000 yuan subsidy.

Hybrid still lacks significant policy support

Notably, the first directory did not include oil-electric hybrid models newly incorporated into the scope of national policy subsidies. According to relevant persons, relevant departments may still be formulating detailed policies for specific products.

Domestic automobile commentator Zhong Shi said that the "Notice" only vaguely included the concept of hybrids within the scope of reduced or exempted vehicle and vessel tax, without formulating policies for specific models. Non-plug-in hybrids on the domestic market are divided into mild hybrids and full hybrids based on the proportion of electric motor auxiliary drive participation, [the two hybrid technologies differ greatly in fuel-saving effects, if the policy applies uniformly, it would be difficult to ensure fairness].

Moreover, hybrid models enjoy exactly the same half-exempt vehicle and vessel tax discount as traditional small-displacement models, which does not highlight the state's policy inclination towards hybrids.
