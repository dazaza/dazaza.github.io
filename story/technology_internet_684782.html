ChinaHR's new observation research report on campus recruitment shows that recruiting and managing [post-90s] will face five major challenges:

Challenge 1: Characteristics and diversity of values.

[Post-90s] are more self-centered, and compared with [post-70s, post-80s], their values are more diversified, and finding their individuality and value will be a major issue for recruitment and employment.

Challenge 2: Stress resistance ability.

The first job for [post-90s] can give them a sense of security because they show good career prospects. They do not want any pressure, and the work is easy to use, without risk, below their stress resistance strength and resilience.

Challenge 3: Job satisfaction.

[Post-90s] do not want to fall behind, nor do they want to lose to others. They want their work to be recognized and respected.

Challenge 4: Unstable mentality.

Compared with [post-1970s] who prefer "nine-to-five" and step-by-step progression, they pursue a flexible work rhythm. This unstable mentality will not only reflect in attendance details but also reflect in [post-90s]' lack of long-term career planning, and they can change jobs at any time. Frequent job-hopping by [post-90s] will be a big problem.

Challenge 5: Self-centeredness.

[Post-90s] are more self-centered, and many things are done from their own interests and self-centeredness. In modern enterprises, team spirit is emphasized, and an appropriate balance between comprehensive development and personality development is emphasized. How [post-90s] adapt to the real workplace is a challenge for enterprises.
