Here are some good ways to maintain this "magic broom":

1. Regularly add lubricating oil to the gears.

2. Apply butter to the dehydration shaft.

3. Apply butter to the spring under the rack, which can effectively prevent rust and greatly increase the service life of the spring.

Problem identification and repair methods:

1. For imitations, if the following problems occur:

A. After stepping on the pedal, the bucket spins, but the pedal does not return to its original position. This problem is most common, possibly due to insufficient elasticity of the spring or excessive friction in the gear set, causing jamming. You can replace the corresponding spring. The replacement steps are as follows:

Step 1: First, use a common screwdriver to remove the pedal, then use a hex key to remove the red large gear.

Step 2: Remove the rack, take off the damaged spring, and install the new spring into the card slot of the rack.

Step 3: Install the rack, and use needle-nose pliers or other tools to install the other end of the new spring into the other card slot on the pedal.

Step 4: Install the large gear, and finally install all the pedals.

If it's not a spring problem, you can replace the corresponding gear or grind down the part with excessive friction.

B. After stepping on the pedal, the bucket rotates and then reverses, and the pedal returns to its original position. This problem often occurs when the one-way small bearing is damaged and needs to be replaced with a matching bearing to solve it.

C. After stepping on the pedal, the bucket rotates and then reverses, but the pedal does not return to its original position. This is because the one-way small bearing is completely jammed and rusted, requiring replacement with a matching bearing to solve it.

2. For genuine products, Pai Ning Magic Mop, a small part of the problems that appear in genuine products are similar to those in imitations, but springs and bearings rarely get damaged. The most common problem is that after using it for a while, when stepping on the pedal, the bucket rotates, but the pedal doesn't return smoothly or powerfully enough. This is because after using the rack for a while, it generates friction with the sides. The solution is to remove the rack, use a knife to shave off the back six or seven teeth slightly, increasing the space between it and the sides, which solves the problem. Alternatively, you can replace it with a new rack.
