Twitter to be listed on NYSE tonight

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:04:49

This morning, Twitter confirmed the IPO price at $26 per share, significantly higher than the initially proposed range of $17 to $20. With a sale volume of 70 million shares, it will raise approximately $1.82 billion in funds, valuing the company at about $18.3 billion. According to sources close to the NYSE, stock trading is expected to commence around midnight Beijing time on November 8th. Tencent Technology will live-stream the key performances before and after Twitter's listing once the NYSE opens.

Apple iOS Targets Automotive Market

A research report indicates that Apple's strategy to integrate its iOS system with in-car infotainment systems will yield significant results in the coming years. By 2018, nearly half of the global in-car infotainment systems equipped with smartphone integration technology are expected to adopt Apple's "iOS in the car" system.

Square Seeks 2014 IPO

Mobile payment company Square’s growth rate is comparable to Twitter. Informed sources reveal that this year's sales are projected to reach approximately $550 million. It is predicted that sales will approach $1 billion in 2014. Additionally, informed sources disclose that Square has consulted with multiple banks, including Goldman Sachs Group and Morgan Stanley, regarding an IPO in 2014.

BlackBerry Seeks $1 Billion Tax Refund from Government

As the former leader in the global smartphone market, this company paid substantial taxes to the Canadian government in previous years. BlackBerry hopes to receive a $1 billion tax refund from the Canadian government by the end of this year to support its financial position.

First 4K TV Certification Standards Released

Products from five companies—Sony, Samsung, TCL, LG, and Sharp—are among the first to pass inspection and certification for 4K. The certification standards measure the level of 4K ultra-high-definition TVs based on five aspects: clarity, color gamut coverage, refresh rate, contrast, and native resolution. The physical resolution of 4K TVs reaches 3840*2160, which is four times that of full HD TVs (FHD.1920*1080) and eight times that of HD TVs (HD.1280*720).

Samsung Plans to Develop Its Own 64-bit Chip

In September this year, Apple launched the world's first 64-bit chip designed specifically for smartphones. Recently, Samsung announced that it would follow Apple's lead and develop its own 64-bit chips tailored for mobile devices. Samsung hinted that future mobile devices might be equipped with memory similar to PCs, necessitating 64-bit chips.

US Website Exposing 'Homewreckers' Gains Popularity

According to reports from The Paper, since its official launch in 2012, the US-based website “She's a Homewrecker” (Shesahomewrecker.com) has received nearly 250,000 positive reviews. The site encourages women who have been hurt to expose the identities of third parties and provide detailed accounts of their experiences.

By 2018, LTE-TDD Networks Will Cover More Than Half of Asia's Population

Market research firm ABI Research predicts that by 2018, LTE-TDD networks will cover 53% of the population in the Asia-Pacific region, representing a compound annual growth rate of 41.1% from 2012 to 2018. The increase in covered population is mainly attributed to the promotion of these networks in several major Asian countries with large populations, such as China, India, and Japan.
