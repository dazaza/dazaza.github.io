Maokelin Transforms Business Model to Gear up for 2012 China E-commerce

by maikaolin0111 on 2012-03-09 16:55:46

China's largest online clothing company Maekolin is committed to providing customers with fashionable, value-for-money products and a pleasant shopping experience. In addition to its own brands Oumengda (Euromoda) and Lan Peiqi (Rampage), Maekolin also offers carefully selected third-party brands, including well-known international brands and some distinctive local brands. China's e-commerce industry is still booming, with women's fashion accessories being popular. Investors from all over the world are flocking in, and the purchasing enthusiasm of Chinese consumers is rising. Maekolin is one of the witnesses to this wave. Among Maekolin's three retail channels, the online business is showing a trend of replacing the other two. This can be seen from the company's third-quarter financial report: the number of Maekolin's offline direct-operated stores (excluding franchise stores) has decreased from 143 last year to 115 this year. Although the total net revenue was $53.13 million, down 4% year-on-year, the average monthly visitors to Maekolin's website increased by 23% year-on-year. "This trend also verifies our strategy," said Gu Beichun, CEO of Maekolin, in a public statement. As a seasoned e-commerce company, how to reposition itself in the fierce competition and shape its core competitiveness? Gu Beichun, CEO of Maekolin, said that some companies adopt a multi-product sales platform strategy. Regarding the issue of doing a brand or a platform, after considering and balancing the market competition landscape, economic development trends, Maekolin's own strengths, style, and scale, etc., we have adjusted our strategy: returning to a brand-focused positioning. We will go deep and thorough in the vertical field of clothing, providing unique style fashion products for a specific demand group, distinguishing ourselves from platform-based e-commerce with our own style. Platforms and brands each have their advantages and disadvantages. Platform companies can achieve considerable scale. However, the current competitive landscape of domestic platform e-commerce is already relatively clear, with Taobao owning more than 80% of the market share. To succeed in this market, a large amount of capital investment is required, and one must be prepared to make no profit for 5-10 years. Secondly, there is a winner-takes-all phenomenon in the platform e-commerce market, where only 3-4 platforms can succeed in a certain category. Therefore, to position oneself as a platform e-commerce, one must face significant risks. At the same time, in such a competitive landscape, the requirements for operational capabilities on the platform and capital investment are high, including technical platforms and logistics platforms, etc. As a brand e-commerce, one can have more advantages. The internet can become one of your channels. You can use online channels, such as Taobao's Singles' Day, or other platforms to expand brand influence. Meanwhile, we can also focus more on enhancing our style and products to attract consumers. Furthermore, the strengths and capabilities required for being a brand merchant and a platform operator are very different, and the talent reserves are also different. Teams doing platforms focus on operational aspects, while doing brands requires having a strong creative and design team. Therefore, the current strategic positioning is a decision made after considering the company's style and strengths, as well as combining with the market competition situation. After having the brand positioning and style, a fixed customer base will be formed. We hope to provide better fashion products for Maekolin's customers, allowing more customers to choose the products they like.
