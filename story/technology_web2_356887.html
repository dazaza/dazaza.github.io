How to Optimize a Squidoo Lens

by vodo36061 on 2010-05-29 13:49:31

Sometimes, making money online doesn't require you to spend a single cent on advertising fees. All major search engines give good natural rankings to websites that have quality content and are frequently updated with fresh material. So, if you want your website to have a good ranking on GOOGLE, YAHOO, and MSN, learning how to use squidoo.com can make you a more efficient article marketer.

First, register for a Squidoo account: How to register for a Squidoo account? Learn how to set up Squidoo's Lens: How to create Squidoo lenses?

Next, learn how to optimize your Lens. A Lens needs at least three modules when you first create it, and some components are pre-added for you. You can keep or delete some of these components. It is recommended to only keep the following components:

Introduction Text / Write Modules

RSS Module

Introduction Module [Introduction Component]: This component appears in all your lenses and is very important. The introduction component allows you to add images, which you can simply upload from your computer. Placing a screenshot of a product here is a good choice!

Text / Write Module [Text Component]: This is also the most versatile component, allowing you to add headings, text, images, HTML links, etc.

Optimization Tip: It is recommended to use multiple Text/Write components to build your lens. Because large headings and introductions allow readers to quickly understand the content of your article without reading every word, this helps you attract readers' attention through optimized headings. Another reason is that Squidoo requires publishers to have at least 4 components in a Lens, aiming to prevent spammers. You can publish a Lens with few components, but Squidoo will assign a very low Squidoo Rank to you.

RSS Module [RSS Component]: Adding an RSS FEED to the component makes search engine crawlers visit your article more frequently because RSS feeds are dynamically updated. Therefore, adding an RSS component to the bottom of your lens greatly helps improve your search engine ranking on Squidoo. How to add an RSS feed: You can use an RSS feed from any blog or website, but if you want your lens to run with flash, refer to blogsearch: http://blogsearch.google.com/

Perform relevant keyword or phrase searches on Google Blog Search. In the search results, you will see an ATOM link to the left of the search results. Copy this link. Use this link as the RSS source for your Squidoo RSS component.

Tip: If you have your own blog, you can add your blog's RSS feed to the Squidoo Lens.

Lens Optimization Summary:

Set relevant headings for each Text/Write component.

Include some "Call to action" links using your website or affiliate links.

Add some images to break up the text.

Avoid long paragraphs without breaks, links, or images.

Maximize the focus of the lens on your promotional page.

Add fresh content to the page via the RSS feed component.
