IT giant IBM begins to enter the Chinese cloud computing market

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-24 16:28:49

Perhaps sensing the impact of HP and Dell in China's IT cloud computing market, Qian Daqun, Chairman and Chief Executive President of IBM Greater China Region announced yesterday in Shanghai the establishment of a Greater China Cloud Computing Division to strengthen its layout in the Chinese market.

This is the first time since 2008 that IBM has set up a dedicated cloud computing business department in China after promoting the concept of a smarter planet.

In the past two years, IBM has been competing for digital city orders under the concept of a smarter planet and has established numerous cloud computing data centers in Wuxi, Jiangsu; Dongying, Shandong; and Chengdu, Sichuan. These are the infrastructure for the cloud computing service model. Currently, this trend continues. On August 4, IBM announced that it will build the Chifeng Mengdong Cloud Computing Center in the Hongshan Logistics Park in Chifeng City, Inner Mongolia.

To accelerate its layout speed, IBM also collaborates with partners to jointly explore regional markets. On August 8, it announced a cloud computing partner special program to attract partners with incentives such as funds. It is said that partners can receive rebates as high as 15%.

So far, IBM has secured orders from many key industries in China.

Qian Daqun stated that IBM alone implemented 2,000 private cloud projects globally in 2010, and IBM’s cloud platform already has 19 million users. In addition, IBM has invested globally to establish 10 cloud labs, 5 cloud centers, and 12 backup clouds. So far, 80% of Fortune Global 500 companies have used IBM’s cloud computing services.

Of course, those big players prefer to build their own type of cloud computing service. "IBM Development Center CTO and General Manager of New Technology Research and Development Center Mao Xinsheng told the 'First Financial Daily'.

Rivals HP and Dell are unwilling to let business opportunities fall into IBM's hands. Over the past year, they have aggressively acquired cloud computing companies and frequently competed for the Chinese market. Not long ago, HP released its China cloud computing strategy, announcing an investment of $2 billion and has already planned cloud computing bases in Hainan and Chongqing. Michael Yang, President of Dell Greater China Region, previously told our newspaper that Dell will build two large solution centers in China and construct a new cloud computing data center. Previously, Dell's Chengdu base had already broken ground.

Microsoft and Intel are also not willing to lag behind. Microsoft will establish a Cloud Innovation Center next month in Shanghai. Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer said that in the next ten years, the revenue from cloud services of just the server and development tools division aims to reach $10 billion. Last year, Intel launched its cloud strategy and pulled in Alibaba, Baidu, PetroChina, Tencent, and others.

And local Chinese enterprises touting the concept of cloud computing resemble more like dealers of products and solutions from overseas giants like IBM. Previously, IT industry observer Liu Xingliang (Weibo) had told our newspaper that all the clouds floating in the sky come from the West.
