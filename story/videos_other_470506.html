Fourth Guest: Chen Ou, Founder of Cosmetics Group-buying Website Jumei Youpin

Regarding internet entrepreneurs from the post-80s generation, it seems that in recent years, they have been locked onto three individuals - Mao Kankan, Dai Zhikang, and Li Xiang. As their experience gradually makes them "seniors" in the internet world, a new batch of post-80s internet entrepreneurs is beginning to emerge, more focused on emerging fields like group-buying and mobile internet, including Google optimization. Among these is Chen Ou, founder of the cosmetics group-buying platform Jumei Youpin.

Born in '83 and graduated from Stanford University, as an 80s-born overseas returnee, Chen Ou's entrepreneurial story has been exposed by the media multiple times: before going to Stanford, he was favored by the famous angel investor Xu Xiaoping, who invited him to stay in China and start a business, but Chen Ou resolutely chose Stanford. During his studies at Stanford, he founded the gaming platform Garena, and in 2009, after returning to China, he joined the group-buying battle, with Xu Xiaoping becoming his angel investor.

Choosing the group-buying market for entrepreneurship was because Chen Ou saw an unchangeable reality: the threshold for starting a business in the internet industry has increased, and the golden days of opportunities everywhere in 2000 are gone. Directional fields have been monopolized by large companies, leaving only vertical niche markets. It was early 2010, just as the chaos of thousands of group-buying sites was brewing in China's internet scene.

Initially, Chen Ou had little confidence, due to many issues needing resolution in China's commercial environment, integrity, and transportation. After briefly attempting group-buying for lifestyle services, Jumei Youpin transitioned to focus exclusively on cosmetics group-buying. The increase in user numbers after the transition gave Chen Ou confidence. Facing the vast female market demand, he positioned Jumei Youpin as a lightweight cosmetics B2C platform.

Key figures about Jumei Youpin include: monthly sales exceeding 30 million yuan, over 300 employees, around one million registered users, average order value around 100 yuan, gross margin between 20-30%. They mainly group-buy international second-tier or domestic distinctive cosmetic brands, sourcing products through general distributors and authorized dealers.

Internet entrepreneurs usually have a dream of changing the world, but Chen Ou believes this difficulty is increasing. "For our new batch of post-80s entrepreneurs, there’s a greater chance of success in vertical opportunities and directions rather than starting off wanting to do something too grand," said Chen Ou. Besides vertical group-buying, mobile internet and game applications will also be particularly promising.

Overseas returnees are often controversial groups in internet entrepreneurship, especially young returnees. Chen Ou's experience is to "definitely land." While at Stanford, Chen Ou set up 30 projects, but upon returning to China, none of them seemed feasible. "The internet is very grassroots; coming in with high-flying American ways may lead to failure. The key in China is the right market plus a suitable team to achieve success."

For this entrepreneurial venture, Chen Ou hopes to scale Jumei Youpin to IPO size before he turns 30. Currently, Jumei Youpin has received tens of millions of dollars in investment, with investors including Xu Xiaoping, attracting the attention of Sequoia Capital China, and the second round of financing will soon be announced. (By Cui Xi)

Below is the interview transcript:

Host Cui Xi: Good morning, everyone. Welcome to Sina Tech's New Forces in E-commerce interview. Today we are delighted to have our guest, the founder of Jumei.com, Chen Ou, who runs a B2C group-buying cosmetics website. First, please let Chen Ou say hello to everyone.

Chen Ou: Hello everyone, I am Chen Ou. It's great to be here at Sina for this interview, thank you!

Host: There might still be many people who don't know much about Jumei Youpin. Could you give us a brief introduction?

Chen Ou: Jumei Youpin was established in March last year, and it's been about a year now. The growth rate has been quite good. In the first month, we made 99,000 yuan in sales, and now we exceed 30 million yuan per month, growing rapidly with many users.

Host: Can you share some specific figures, such as the number of employees?

Chen Ou: Now we have around 300 people.

Host: Approximately how many registered users do we have?

Chen Ou: Around one million.

Host: This interview focuses heavily on figures. Can you share the average order value and gross margin?

Chen Ou: The average order value is over 100 yuan, and the gross margin is pretty good, significantly higher than regular group-buying prices.

Host: What is the overall gross margin level for cosmetics? What should it reach?

Chen Ou: About 20-30% is relatively average.

Host: Many people wonder why group-buying websites offer services like massages or good restaurants, but you chose a cosmetics group-buying site. How did you come up with this idea?

Chen Ou: When group-buying first emerged, to be honest, I didn’t have strong confidence in this industry. China’s commercial environment, trust, and traffic problems need to be resolved. Spending time traveling to enjoy a 50-yuan service for 30 yuan isn’t ideal, and the final service may not meet expectations. However, the speed of group-buying development exceeded my imagination. Why choose cosmetics group-buying? Lifestyle service group-buying has fatal flaws, but the group-buying market has opportunities. With the rise of cosmetics, as user volume increases, the user experience and service quality enjoyed by your users will decline. That's why I chose cosmetics.

Moreover, cosmetics group-buying has existed for a long time. This model was organized on forums, where someone would propose buying a cosmetic product, and interested people could follow up. This is not a new concept. I simply applied the group-buying model to cosmetics e-commerce, making the definition simpler.

Host: Not only are we doing it, but JD.com and Redbaby already have cosmetics sections. Jumei Youpin offers not only well-known cosmetics brands but also sample sales. People are curious about the procurement channels. On Taobao, there are mixed genuine and counterfeit products, including those bought on behalf of others.

Chen Ou: Samples are getting smaller and are sold as sets, originally disassembled and sold separately, now sold as a whole. We sell unique items, international second-tier or domestically distinctive products. Regarding channels, most are from manufacturers' main distributors. For example, L'Oreal has official cooperation, providing goods via its official website. We also work with authorized dealers and main distributors like Kosé, which collaborates with manufacturers. We collaborate with large suppliers and major dealers through authorized contracts.
