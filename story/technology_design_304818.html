Authoritative report measures who is the fastest player in the world: Ronaldo ranks first surpassing Robben

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-13 12:50:18

According to authoritative reports, who is the fastest player in the world? Cristiano Ronaldo ranks first, surpassing Arjen Robben. Sina Sports News - Who is the fastest player in the world? There are various opinions. However, a recent scientific report by Germany's Der Spiegel magazine shows that Cristiano Ronaldo's speed during high-speed sprints reaches an astonishing 33.6 kilometers per hour, making him undoubtedly the fastest player in today's football world.

The decisive factor for an athlete's speed mainly depends on their muscle type. Generally speaking, sprinters have more white muscle fibers, which provide greater explosive power and stronger short-distance acceleration ability. Long-distance runners, on the other hand, have more red muscle fibers. The report by Der Spiegel points out that Ronaldo's muscle type is naturally white fiber, which is the fundamental reason for his outstanding speed. Although Ronaldo's sprinting speed is still far from the human limit of 44.72 kilometers per hour set by the 100-meter world champion Usain Bolt, Bolt also pointed out: "If Ronaldo pays attention to the center of gravity while running, he can definitely run faster."

Der Spiegel tested the players' sprinting speed without the ball, not with the ball. Interestingly, Ronaldo's dribbling speed is even faster than his flat running speed. Previously, Nike conducted a speed test experiment on its players, and the results showed that Ronaldo's dribbling speed was 0.1 kilometers per hour faster than his flat running speed! Regarding this, Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson also expressed surprise: "Ronaldo's dribbling speed is no slower than his flat running speed, which is incredible."

In this research report, the player with the second-highest speed after Ronaldo is Arjen Robben of Bayern Munich, whose speed also reached 32.9 kilometers per hour, followed by Theo Walcott of Arsenal (32.7 kilometers), Wayne Rooney of Manchester United (32.6 kilometers), and Robin van Persie of Arsenal (32.1 kilometers).

However, there is controversy among academia and the media regarding who is the fastest, as it is related to the player's physical condition, field conditions, and even mental state. For example, at the 2008 European Cup, according to the official data of the tournament, the fastest player should be Robin van Persie of the Netherlands team, whose maximum sprinting speed in that competition reached 31.5 kilometers per hour, while Ronaldo only had 29.4 kilometers per hour. The professional data ranking by OPTA is different again, with Ronaldo still ranked first, but his speed is only 20.9 kilometers per hour, followed by Andrew Johnson of Fulham (20.6 kilometers per hour) and Fernando Torres of Liverpool (20.5 kilometers per hour).

Interestingly, regardless of the ranking or testing method used, Lionel Messi, the forward of Barcelona, does not appear in the top five fastest rankings. This is understandable, as Messi's speed is more reflected in his stride frequency and quick turns, stops, and direction changes while dribbling, so in terms of absolute speed, he falls behind Ronaldo, who has better physical fitness.
