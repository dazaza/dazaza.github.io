by now on 2010-03-11 14:39:36

In recent years, with the rapid popularization and development of China's Internet, the application of the Internet has become more widespread. It is no longer limited to chat, email, and information services but also includes the mature application of many enterprises in computer conferencing and other aspects. Together with the four pillars of the online gaming industry, increased enterprise applications, extensive personal applications, and e-commerce, IDC business maintains stability while promoting its rapid growth.

In 2008, the global Internet Data Center (IDC) market size reached $11.86 billion, a year-on-year increase of 31.5%. The domestic IDC market size reached RMB 4.87 billion, a year-on-year increase of 40.8%. According to data from CCID Consulting at the beginning of 2009, it is expected that by 2011, China's IDC market size will reach RMB 13.16 billion.

The huge market brings about enormous business opportunities, attracting an increasing number of service providers into this field, making competition increasingly fierce. However, the Internet is not a tranquil harbor; it is a stormy ocean. Only those service providers who can withstand the storms can sail far on this ocean.

The year 2009, which just passed, was a great challenge for the Internet industry, while the already arrived 2010 is a year of great opportunity for the IDC industry. In 2010, in the domestic market,细分 fields such as gaming, e-commerce, and hosting will maintain double-digit high-speed growth. Competition among service providers has escalated from product competition to service competition and continues to upgrade.

The author learned from the top-tier service provider, Era Interconnect (www.todaynic.com), that in the new round of competition, price wars will no longer be the first choice. Greater emphasis will be placed on customer experience and brand competition. Additionally, agent competition will also become one of the highlights.

Since 2002, China's IDC industry has entered a vibrant development stage, attracting many agents to join this industry. The participation of numerous agents has further accelerated the already rapid development speed of this industry. Agents have unique advantages in capturing market share. Relatively speaking, while agents bring many benefits to the IDC industry, they also gain substantial returns for themselves. The substantial returns are the most direct reason for more agents joining.

A relevant person in charge from Era Interconnect told the author that to attract more agents to join and secure greater advantages in the new round of competition, service providers will launch a series of preferential activities targeted at agents. For instance, right after the New Year, Era Interconnect launched an activity where pre-deposits would be rewarded with cash or postal remittances. Using preferential interactions to bring more benefits to agents can retain existing agents and attract more people to join, ultimately achieving a win-win situation. This way, they can take a more proactive position in the new competition.

Overall, in 2010, the Internet will enter a new phase of development and inevitably reach another level of competition. To achieve greater gains in 2010, peripheral industries related to the Internet must seize more initiative when facing such situations.
