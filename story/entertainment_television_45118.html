Chow Sing Chi's latest masterpiece, "Changjiang No. 7," which took him three years to create, will be officially released during the 2008 New Year season. Recently, reporters learned that "Changjiang No. 7" is not the familiar flying saucer next to Star Sir, but rather refers to something else. The true identity of "Changjiang No. 7" has been revealed with the exposure of the official stills.

"Changjiang No. 7" focuses on warmth and special effects. The movie "Changjiang No. 7" will continue to follow Chow Sing Chi's signature comedy style. In addition to rich comedic elements, this time, Star Sir emphasizes "warmth" and "special effects." The film tells the story of a father, played by Chow Sing Chi, who is down on his luck and lives a life of dependence with his son Xiao Di, portrayed by Xu Jiao. Because he cannot afford toys for his son, the father feels very guilty. By chance, he finds a toy in the garbage heap and plans to take it home as a gift for his son. Unexpectedly, this toy turns out to be an alien creature, starting a wondrous journey. Through this experience, father and son come to understand the warm truths that money cannot replace...

The film is full of innovation, with a strong sci-fi flavor, and the computer special effects are meticulously crafted. The space pet design in the film is unprecedented. To achieve the best special effects, the renowned Hong Kong Mankan Company, which has worked on major films such as "Kung Fu," "Infernal Affairs," "Initial D," and "Crazy Over You," was specially invited to create the visual effects.

"Changjiang No. 7" Turns Out to Be a Dog: Chow Sing Chi Pays Tribute to His Beloved Pet

From the film title or from the filming and production process, including the official trailers, many people would assume that the name "Changjiang No. 7" refers to the large flying saucer behind Chow Sing Chi. Recently, with the release of the latest official stills, the true identity of "Changjiang No. 7" has been revealed. A pale green little body topped with a fluffy golden head, with a small antenna-like touch on its head, and flashing a pair of lively big eyes, "Changjiang No. 7" turns out to be an alien dog that can fit in one hand. This might just be the cutest image of an extraterrestrial visitor in cinematic history.

The concept of "Changjiang No. 7" as an alien dog originates from Star Sir's nostalgia for his former beloved pet "Bull Bull." Chow Sing Chi expressed that he has always liked dogs. When his beloved pet was still alive, they were inseparable, often running and shopping together, forming a very close bond.
