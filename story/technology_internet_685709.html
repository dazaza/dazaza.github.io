Train a docile dog: Five-step training

by doisndcb263 on 2012-03-01 16:25:47

Fierce and scary dogs can be somewhat disliked. How can owners train their pet dogs to become gentle and lovable? To make your dog gentle and endearing, the editor of Nantong TV has five simple tricks! Let's learn these five magical methods together! (1) Making friends: As the owner, you must first befriend the dog. Otherwise, you cannot gain its respect and trust, nor can you become its true owner. In order for the dog to accept education, the love of the guide for the dog as well as their own qualities are very important issues. If you want to teach the dog to do things, you need to carefully observe and analyze its mental state, proceed step by step, work tirelessly, and patiently and repeatedly teach it. Therefore, the guide must be patient, have strong self-control, and must have more love for the dog in order to better educate it. (2) Obedience: The pet network editor points out that when educating a dog, it is not just about adopting a simple reward system, which means making it perform actions and then giving material rewards upon completion. Instead, use human willpower to let it earn rewards. The real reward is the owner's love. For the dog, nothing makes it happier than the owner's love. Food and toys cannot replace love. If through education the dog can reach the level of obeying commands, it will also understand the intentions of humans. Therefore, dogs that are basically obedient to humans can understand and complete even more difficult actions. It can also improve its obedience. (3) Cultivate "labor" desire: Cultivate the "labor" desire that arises from the dog's instincts, such as its fondness for biting shoes during play. Its act of biting shoes indicates a willingness to actively work with you. To enhance this desire, you can give it things to bite and retrieve, and play with it together. Additionally, for the dog's spontaneous behavior, you should understand what it is doing, determine the matter, and then decide whether to praise or scold it. Otherwise, the dog may feel it is unfair, which would disrupt the hard-earned "obedience" heart and also affect its future "labor" desire. (4) No spoiling: Dogs living in families must be taught to follow "house rules," but all of this requires strict education to achieve. Therefore, during the educational process, dogs should not be overly spoiled, otherwise they will not learn patience and will cause many troubles in future coexistence. However, at home, you can conduct strict education in ways you prefer. (5) Talent differences: During education, attention should be paid to the fact that different breeds, personalities, and intelligence levels exist among dogs. Therefore, not all dogs can reach the level of "acrobats" through education, so dogs should not be held to "excessively harsh" standards. That is, it's not just about making your beloved pet obey human will, but also accepting the realistic results it strives for. This way, feelings will become more harmonious.
