China News Service, Ningbo, March 2 (Xu Xiaoyong, He Jiangyong, Yu Yang) - At 1:11 AM on the morning of March 2, a car accident occurred in Beilun District, Ningbo City, Zhejiang Province, where a car flipped into a ditch, resulting in the deaths of five people in the vehicle.

At 1:11 AM on the morning of March 2, the Ningbo Fire Department received an emergency call reporting that a car had fallen into a ditch in front of China Beifa Pen Manufacturing City on Jiangnan Road, with multiple people trapped inside. The Xiaogang Fire Station, which was responsible for this area, immediately dispatched two fire trucks and 12 firefighters to rush to the scene.

At 1:22 AM, the firefighters arrived at the scene. After questioning the people present, the rescuers learned that during the incident, a car had crashed through the greenbelt and fell into a deep pit in front of Beifa Pen Manufacturing City. The deep pit had been dug for sewage pipe repairs, and due to the rain over the past few days, it had already filled with water.

The on-site commander immediately issued orders for two combatants to quickly cordon off the area. The commander and a soldier who was a good swimmer tied safety ropes around themselves and dived into the water to carry out the rescue. After diving into the water, the rescuers found that due to water pressure, the car doors could not be opened, making it impossible to confirm the situation inside the vehicle. Subsequently, the rescuers secured the rear wheels of the car with ropes and then used the fire truck to pull it upwards until the car doors surfaced, allowing the five people inside to be rescued. The five were then rushed to the nearest hospital.

It was learned that all five people in the car have died.

The cause of the accident is still under further investigation.
