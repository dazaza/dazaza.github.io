Aquilani is expected to return to the pitch in mid-March. According to the terms specified in the loan contract, once Aquilani represents Milan in 5 more matches, the Rossoneri will be obligated to buy him out at the end of the season. This weekend, Palermo, Milan's Serie A opponent, is the team that has been scored against by Ibrahimovic the most times in the league. The Swedish star has scored 9 goals in 14 encounters against them.

On December 20, 2011, during Milan's away match against Cagliari which they won, Aquilani started and played the full 90 minutes. However, after that game, the Italian international midfielder suffered an ankle injury and hasn't appeared on the pitch since, leaving his appearance count for the Rossoneri this season at 20 matches. Recently, his agent, Savaglia, spoke about Aquilani's recovery progress and his prospects of staying with Milan in an interview with "TuttoMercatoWeb."

Due to a left ankle injury, Aquilani has missed Milan's last 13 games since the start of the new year. Nevertheless, the Italian international still leads the team alongside Cassano, Ibrahimovic, and Robinho with 7 assists this season. Regarding his estimated return time, Aquilani’s agent Franco Savaglia revealed: "His recovery is going well, and he should be back on the field in about two weeks. We estimate that he will return to the squad around mid-March."

As a player loaned from Liverpool, Aquilani has already made 15 appearances in Serie A for Milan this season, plus 5 matches in the Champions League group stage, bringing his total number of appearances for the Rossoneri to 20, just 5 short of triggering the buyout clause in his loan agreement. This information was confirmed by Savaglia: "Once Aquilani reaches 25 appearances for Milan this season, he will automatically become a permanent member of the Rossoneri." At the end of the season, if Milan decides to buy Aquilani outright, they will pay Liverpool 6 million euros.

Although Aquilani will not be able to participate in the match against Palermo, Ibrahimovic's return is excellent news. The Sicilian side Palermo, which Milan will face this Saturday away from home, happens to be the favorite opponent of the Swedish striker. Since the 2004-2005 season, Ibrahimovic has faced Palermo a total of 14 times across Juventus, Inter Milan, and AC Milan, scoring 9 goals and providing 4 assists, helping his teams achieve a record of 7 wins, 5 draws, and 2 losses. Alongside Parma, Palermo is the team in Serie A whose goal their net has been breached by Ibrahimovic the most.

The top eight teams in Serie A that have been scored against the most by Ibrahimovic are as follows:

Palermo - 9 goals in 14 matches

Parma - 9 goals in 10 matches

Roma - 8 goals in 17 matches

Lazio - 7 goals in 13 matches

Fiorentina - 7 goals in 12 matches

Sampdoria - 6 goals in 9 matches

Lecce - 6 goals in 8 matches

Livorno - 6 goals in 7 matches
