Yahoo's net profit in the third quarter was 297 million US dollars, down 91% year-on-year

by anonymous on 2013-10-19 11:56:12

Yahoo! released its third quarter financial results on Tuesday, covering the period ended September 30, 2013. The report showed that Yahoo!'s revenue excluding traffic acquisition costs (TAC) was $1.081 billion, a 1% decrease year-over-year; operating profit was $93 million, down 39%; net income was $297 million, down 91%. Last year's Q3 report included a one-time gain of $2.8 billion from the sale of Alibaba Group shares.

Exceeding Market Expectations and Stock Performance

Yahoo!'s Q3 performance exceeded market expectations. According to a Thomson Reuters survey, analysts had on average expected Yahoo! to generate $1.08 billion in revenue excluding TAC and earnings per share of $0.33 excluding certain items.

Following the release of the earnings report, Yahoo!'s stock surged $1.48, or 4.43%, in after-hours trading. In regular trading hours that day, Yahoo! closed at $33.38, down $1.82 from the previous day.

Key Q3 Results:

--Revenue for the third quarter was $1.139 billion, down 5% from $1.202 billion in the same period last year.

--Display advertising revenue was $470 million, down 7% from $506 million in the same period last year.

--Display advertising revenue excluding TAC was $421 million, down 7% from $452 million in the same period last year.

--Search advertising revenue was $435 million, down 8% from $473 million in the same period last year.

--Search advertising revenue excluding TAC was $426 million, up 3% from $414 million in the same period last year.

--Operating profit for the third quarter was $93 million, down 39% from $152 million in the same period last year.

--Net income for the third quarter was $297 million, down 91% from the same period last year. Diluted earnings per share were $0.28, compared to $2.64 in the same period last year.

Maria Mayer, CEO of Yahoo!, commented, "I am very pleased with our execution, especially as we continue to invest in and strengthen our core business. In Q3, we launched many new user experiences around daily digital habits such as the new Yahoo Screen video site, personalized home page product My Yahoo, and Fantasy Sports. Now, Yahoo! has over 800 million monthly visitors, an increase of 20% over the past 15 months. We have achieved meaningful growth in user engagement and traffic."

Financial Analysis for Q3:

Revenue for the third quarter was $1.139 billion, down 5% from $1.202 billion in the same period last year. By segment, revenue from the U.S. department was $851 million, higher than $844 million in the same period last year. Revenue from the EMEA (Europe, Middle East, Africa) department was $89 million, lower than $96 million in the same period last year. Revenue from the Asia-Pacific department was $199 million, less than $262 million in the same period last year.

Operating profit for the third quarter was $93 million, down 39% from $152 million in the same period last year. Excluding certain items under U.S. GAAP, operating profit was $173 million, down 27% from $238 million in the same period last year.

Adjusted EBITDA for the third quarter was $331 million, down 19% from the same period last year.

Net income for the third quarter was $297 million, down 91% from the same period last year. Diluted earnings per share were $0.28, compared to $2.64 in the same period last year. Excluding certain items under U.S. GAAP, net income was $358 million, down 24% from the same period last year. Diluted earnings per share excluding certain items were $0.34, down from $0.39 in the same period last year.

Cash Flow Information:

As of September 30, 2013, Yahoo! held a total of $3.2 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities investments, a reduction of $2.8 billion from $6.0 billion as of December 31, 2012. In Q3 2013, Yahoo! repurchased 59 million shares for $1.685 billion and used $163 million in cash for acquisitions.
