Women, remember: never marry a man who divorces his wife for you.

by da2j4g9z5o9 on 2012-02-26 09:21:39

A woman bets her whole life on a man, which speaks of wisdom, but in the end still relies on luck. Women are fickle, changing their faces. Men are fickle too, but they change their hearts. Then, is a man who divorces for another woman considered a good man? Is he worth a woman's bet? This is indeed something worth considering.

Women have countless charms and changes. But men are all the same, only divided into two kinds: good men and bad men. Since ancient times, there are two types of good men worthy of a woman's dedication, even to the point of no regrets. One type is the man who dare to sacrifice his life for a woman. Such men value sentiment and righteousness, hard to meet but not to seek. Unfortunately, in the materialistic era, such men are fewer than giant pandas. The other type is Yang Guo from Jin Yong's novels, loved by Xiaolongnü - the number one lover in the world. This kind of man advocates love above all, which aligns very well with women's values in love. It is regrettable that in today's fleeting love, men who remain faithful for 16 years are almost extinct.

In many literary works and films, these two types of men are often the protagonists of great love stories. I always sincerely praise this kind of men living in women's fantasies, although sometimes this praise seems abstract or even boring.

In fact, in real life, these two types of good men worth a woman's lifelong admiration are few and far between. Most men are somewhere between good and bad men. These men are slick and difficult to understand. A small step left may turn them into bad men, while a small step right may make them good men.

And once a woman unfortunately suffers from such a bad man, her happiness in the second half of her life is almost buried in tears. Women are like seeds. If sown on fertile soil, they will bloom and bear fruit, enjoying great happiness. But if sown on barren land, they will wither into mud, leading a dim and bleak life.

In reality, many beautiful flowers are often planted on cow dung. And men like cow dung are often more favored by women than those who are empty inside but have a handsome appearance. Interestingly, many men can't stand seeing beautiful flowers planted on cow dung, blaming women for being selfish and too realistic. From a woman's perspective, cow dung smells bad, but it is nutritious. At least, it makes the flower bloom more beautifully than on barren land. Women are like flowers, and flowers have their blooming seasons. Once missed, even if they bloom again, it will be lonely. In the materialistic era, women choosing fertile soil need not feel guilty, and others have no reason to criticize. Who would willingly let themselves wither in the night without applause?

A woman bets her whole life on a man, which speaks of wisdom, but in the end still relies on luck. Choosing a man is like buying stocks. If this man is a potential stock or a high-performance stock, the woman will have rich returns for her whole life. If this man is a junk stock, she will lose everything and get stuck deeply. But in reality, most men are good at pretending. It's not easy for a woman to fully understand a man before marriage. Women are fickle, changing their faces. Men are fickle too, but they change their hearts. Men who have changed their hearts are beyond redemption. Regrettably, many women encountering such men always want to use tenderness to appeal to them, or foolishly hope that one day they will repent. In fact, not many men who have changed their hearts will return to their senses. Smart women, once encountering unfeeling men, do not need to beg pitifully. The only thing they can do is to maintain the last bit of dignity and give the unfeeling man a back that he might regret one day.

Then, is a man who divorces for another woman considered a good man? Is he worth a woman's bet? This is indeed something worth considering.

The talented Zhang Ailing once reflected on her scarred emotional journey during a night and exclaimed: Perhaps every man has two such women, at least two.

Marrying the red rose, over time, the red becomes a streak of mosquito blood on the wall, while the white remains the bright moonlight before the bed; marrying the white rose, the white becomes a grain of rice stuck on the clothes, while the red becomes the cinnabar mole on the heart.

Even a smart woman like Zhang Ailing, whose dissection of the essence of men is so true and cruel, could not escape the gentle trap set by men. In my view, my female classmate who has been divorced for two years and has an affair with a married man is destined not to have a good result. Countless facts prove that men who divorce for another woman are mostly not good men. So I always think that women should not marry men who divorce for them. After all, what the previous woman couldn't handle, the next ten women basically can't handle either. Smart women should realize that the best woman in a man's heart is always the unknown next one. Don't mistakenly think that a man who dares to divorce for you is the one worth entrusting your life to. Zhang Ailing's life experiences tell us: No woman is the only one for a man. He can divorce for you, and he can also divorce you for the next woman.

Men's vows are unreliable, just like women's ages, always full of mystery. When young, we always think vows are important. Love is forever, loving someone means never changing. In fact, what changes fastest in the world are often those sweet vows, and love is the same. Not many long-lasting loves can withstand the test of time. And those great loves that make us cry are often tragedies that start well but end badly. In real life, how many women can withstand the ups and downs of great love?

The Wenchuan earthquake made many people re-examine the fragile love and marriage like life. The harsh reality tells us: Life is unpredictable, cherishing the person in front of you is the most important thing.
