**Exhibition Background**

Since its inception in 1984, FISPAL has successfully held 27 editions. In 2011, the exhibition attracted 61,434 domestic visitors from Brazil, along with nearly 3,000 international visitors from other countries, with 1,370 companies participating as exhibitors.

For the global market, FISPAL is a dynamic and vibrant exhibition. It plays a significant role in organizing the industry and contributing to the entire agricultural production chain in Brazil and the Americas. It is the largest food fair in Latin America. For customers and company operators, FISPAL is an innovative exhibition that provides ample opportunities for increased communication between companies and offers market services such as sales channel support. Additionally, by staying attuned to the pulse of the international food production chain, it frequently improves corporate communication methods and strengthens research into technology and market behavior.

Brazil borders the Atlantic Ocean to the east and shares boundaries with all South American countries except Chile and Ecuador. To the north, it neighbors French Guiana, Suriname, Guyana, Venezuela, and Colombia; to the west, Peru and Bolivia; and to the south, Paraguay, Argentina, and Uruguay. With a land area of 8.547 million square kilometers, Brazil accounts for approximately 46% of South America's total area, making it the fifth-largest country in the world after Russia, Canada, China, and the United States.

Our association has organized groups to participate multiple times, and the participating enterprises have achieved significant results, expressing their intention to continue participating in the next year's exhibition.
