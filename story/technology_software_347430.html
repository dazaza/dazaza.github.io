5000 square meters of illegal construction faces forced demolition after being rented out

by mjzd6713 on 2010-04-17 23:12:06

Our reporter Sun Qian - "We signed a five-year contract, paid one year's rent, and were just preparing to start business when they told us this was illegal construction!" Yesterday, 12 merchants from the restaurant street of Xibeiwang Village in Haidian District's Xibeiwang Town gathered together to discuss countermeasures. Recently, the Haidian Urban Management Team issued a notice stating that the mall where the merchants are located is an illegal construction and needs to be vacated. The merchants questioned whether the village committee's issuance of proof as the property owner misled them and concealed the fact that the building was illegal, resulting in losses of nearly 2 million yuan for the merchants.

The new shops have been identified as illegal constructions and will be demolished. The Xibeiwang Restaurant Street is a two-story building, but many of the 12 new shops have closed down. I saw the word "demolish" written on the walls of the shops, along with the Haidian Urban Management Team's "Vacate Notice" dated July 7th.

The notice stated that this building, with a floor area of more than 5000 square meters, is an illegal construction. The Urban Management Team had legally made a decision to demolish it within a specified period on March 17, 2009. However, the building was not demolished within the specified time. Now, after approval by the Haidian District Government, it will be forcibly demolished, and the merchants are required to vacate the premises by the 18th.

Mr. Mi, one of the merchants, said that the market began renting out last June, and he rented a shop from landlord Xu Xinggao. When signing the contract, he also worried about "getting into trouble due to unclear ownership," but Xu Xinggao presented a certificate stamped by the Xibeiwang Village Committee on July 1, 2009, stating that the property rights of these houses belong to the Xibeiwang Village Committee. "We all signed contracts based on this official certificate." Mr. Chen, another merchant, said that the merchants signed five-year contracts with Xu Xinggao and prepaid rent and deposits totaling at least 2 million yuan.

The merchants questioned whether the village committee and Xu Xinggao deliberately concealed this matter. "A demolition notice was issued in March last year, so how could they still rent it out?" Xu Xinggao, however, claimed that he rented the house from Guohua Real Estate Brokerage Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Guohua Company). "I signed a 14-year contract and didn't know it was an illegal construction. I am also a victim."

Mr. Liu, another merchant, believed that the Xibeiwang Village Committee issued a property ownership certificate knowing that this place was an illegal construction, intentionally deceiving the merchants. Xu Xinggao said that during the contract period, the village committee charged him electricity fees according to commercial standards. "The village committee concealed the truth about the illegal construction, sought profits, and has inescapable responsibilities."

The village committee claims it is unrelated. A staff member of the Xibeiwang Village Committee said that the land belongs to Xibeiwang Village and was rented to Guohua Company several years ago. The company then built the building, but due to lack of approval procedures, the Urban Management Team ordered its demolition. However, Guohua Company did not demolish it but instead rented it out to Xu Xinggao. "The house was rented out by Guohua Company, and it has nothing to do with us."

When reporters called Mr. Wang, the person in charge of Guohua Company, he stated that he would not accept any form of interview. No relevant information about the company was found on the website of the Municipal Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Currently, the merchants refuse to evacuate as a sign of protest.
