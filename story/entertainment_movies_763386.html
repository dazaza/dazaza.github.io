Introduction:

This October, Discovery Communications launches the epic series "Discovery Atlas." As a global media event, we will take viewers on a journey around thirty countries worldwide, capturing their landscapes like never before with exceptional cinematography, high production values, and advanced HD technology. Rich in scientific knowledge and visually stunning, the series "Discovery Atlas" is Discovery Communications' latest global offering, helping audiences explore their world and satisfy their innate curiosity.

Over the next five years, this remarkable series of narrative documentaries will travel to some of the oldest and most diverse nations across the globe, focusing on their rich cultures, geographies, and natural phenomena. With the global premiere of the series "Discovery Atlas: China" on Discovery Channel, our journey officially begins on October 14th. Following China, the series "Discovery Atlas: Italy," "Discovery Atlas: Brazil," and "Discovery Atlas: Australia" will also delve into the unique customs and traditions of these regions.

The aim of the series "Discovery Atlas" is to create the most comprehensive visual record, documenting the history, civilizations, geography, industries, technologies, and peoples of the world, said John Hendricks, Founder and Chairman of Discovery Communications. The series "Discovery Atlas" brings unprecedented depth and scale to the most fascinating countries, capturing the core content of Discovery: creative storytelling and the endless joy of learning new things. We hope that as we broadcast the series "Discovery Atlas" through our global media and educational platforms, this project will inspire greater understanding and discovery of the world.

The series "Discovery Atlas" is Discovery Communications' most ambitious global project to date, involving all departments within the company. Discovery Channel is responsible for the global premiere of the series "Discovery Atlas" in over 170 countries and regions, while Discovery HD Theater will also air each episode, using the latest HD technology with stunning picture quality to present each nation.

The production companies for the Discovery Channel series "Discovery Atlas" are as follows: Lion Television, with Cassian Harrison as producer (China); Atlantic Productions, with Anthony Geffen as producer (Italy); Tigress Productions, with Hona Peters as producer (Brazil); and Beyond Productions, with Jeff Fitzpatrick as producer (Australia). Maureen Lemire serves as executive producer for Discovery Channel.

"Discovery Atlas: China"

Today's China has 1.3 billion stories. Viewers will join farmers growing crops on land tilled by their ancestors for eighteen centuries; Shaolin monks teaching martial arts that have been practiced for five hundred years. Viewers will also share dreams: an eleven-year-old gymnastics prodigy yearning for an Olympic gold medal; a real estate tycoon in Shanghai exploring life at the foot of the Great Wall, facing the clash between tradition and modernity.

On October 1, 2006, the 57th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China, at 9 PM, Canada's Discovery Channel will broadcast the grand documentary "A Glimpse of China" (Discovery Atlas: China Revealed). From today until October 8, Canada's Discovery Channel will air at least one hour of exciting China-themed programs daily, forming a "China Theme Week." Yesterday, the Discovery Channel displayed a set of stunning photos taken during the production process at Taikoo Plaza, hoping more people will understand China through the programs.

Paul Lowis, General Manager of Canada's Discovery Channel, stated that to produce this special program on China, the Discovery Channel spent 18 months collecting materials and selecting topics, formulating detailed plans before shooting in China. Subsequently, four filming crews were dispatched, spending over ten weeks shooting in more than fifty locations across China. Among these, they were granted permission to film reports on China's aerospace industry and astronaut training for the first time, setting historical precedents.
