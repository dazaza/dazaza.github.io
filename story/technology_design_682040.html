Understand the role of collagen, and easily become a beautiful woman.

Currently, the most popular skincare product is none other than collagen. As a beauty-conscious woman who loves collagen, the more you understand about collagen, the closer you are to health and beauty. Below, I will take you through some relevant knowledge about collagen:

1. Collagen and Skin: Collagen is the main component of skin collagen, accounting for 70-80% of the total protein in the skin. The collagen molecule has a triple helix structure that other proteins do not possess, and it is linked with a large number of anionic side chains. The triple helix structure and anionic side chains can firmly lock water molecules, thus giving the skin high elasticity and tension. It can stretch outward and quickly retract. When the content and quality of collagen decrease, elasticity decreases. For example, the loose skin on an elderly person's neck cannot quickly retract after being stretched.

2. Functions of Collagen:

1) Locks water molecules to maintain skin moisture and elasticity, blocks the formation of brown pigmentation, and keeps the skin healthy (prevents spots, peeling, red blood vessels, etc.)

2) Nourishes hair to make it black and shiny

3) Nourishes capillary walls: Capillary walls are also composed of collagen. When the collagen content in the capillary walls decreases, the vessels become thin and transparent, leading to the appearance of red blood vessels on the face. Supplementing enough collagen can prevent the formation of red blood vessels.

4) Cell proliferation, growth, metabolism, etc.: Collagen is the nutrition and environment for cell growth, promoting cell proliferation, growth, and metabolism, thereby enhancing the body's immunity and energy.

5) Promotes bone formation and prevents osteoporosis.

3. Collagen is the young molecule that maintains the beauty of the skin:

As age increases, the content and quality of collagen in the skin gradually decrease. Collagen fibers become dry, coarse, and stiff, resulting in water loss in the skin. The face then shows signs of aging. The process of collagen content decreasing and collagen fiber aging begins at the age of 20.

4. Taking collagen has a significant tightening effect on the skin, making loose skin firm and strong:

This tightening effect includes correcting facial skin sagging, leg muscle relaxation, and abdominal swelling and drooping.

5. Oral collagen can prevent and treat UV radiation damage:

UV radiation severely damages collagen fibers, causing them to age and reduce in content. Only by taking collagen orally and promoting the synthesis of new collagen within the body can UV damage to the skin be prevented.

6. Collagen fibers are the functional molecules that keep the breasts firm and beautiful:

1) Collagen has a unique triple helix structure, forming the suspensory ligament composed of collagen fibers, acting as "steel cables" that firmly fix the breasts on the skin and body wall.

2) Collagen fibers are widely distributed in the breasts. Young collagen fibers have a dense structure like elastic nets, pulling and supporting breast tissue.

3) The collagen fibers in the surface skin of the breasts must be youthful and strong to tighten the skin of the breasts, making them stand upright and rejuvenating their charm.

7. Weight Loss and Collagen:

Weight loss involves removing excess fat accumulated under the skin through various methods. The relationship between fat and skin collagen is similar to the relationship between "grain" and "bags." Removing part of the "grain" makes the "bag" shrink. After weight loss, wrinkles appear on the face and abdominal skin sags due to the removal of fat. Taking oral collagen can timely deliver highly elastic triple helix collagen fibers to the skin, tightening the skin, increasing its tension, and preventing the skin from sagging or developing wrinkles after weight loss.

8. Taking collagen has a regulatory effect on many gynecological and obstetric diseases:

Because the endometrium is composed of collagen fibers. In a normal menstrual cycle, the endometrium matures (rich in collagen fibers) and sheds (loss of collagen fibers) according to hormonal changes. After the endometrium sheds, the uterus needs to quickly synthesize new collagen to repair the endometrium. However, due to the lack of hydroxyproline, a raw material for synthesizing collagen in food, or excessive stress from study or work, the endometrium may not reach sufficient thickness, leading to irregular bleeding and menstrual disorders. Many girls and women with irregular menstruation who take collagen for 1-2 months find that their menstruation becomes regular. Similarly, collagen has a nourishing effect on postpartum recovery and uterine recovery after induced abortion. Taking collagen can quickly repair the injured uterine lining caused by childbirth or induced abortion, allowing women to recover promptly.
