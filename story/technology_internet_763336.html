  Some of the latest MacBook Air users have started reporting flickering issues when running Adobe Photoshop, which Adobe blames on Apple.

Adobe's support forum has seen posts about "severe flickering in Photoshop," and similar reports have also appeared on Apple's support forum.

The first related discussion on Adobe's forum was posted on June 26th, titled: "Photoshop CS6 - Screen flickers when using brushes on the latest MacAir 2013." The author wrote: A large number of users encounter severe screen flickering when using slightly larger brush tools (clone, eraser, brush, spot healing, dodge, burn) in Photoshop CS6. Given that many users on the forum are experiencing this issue, it seems strange and unusual! The flickering appears as black boxes across the width of the screen, and sometimes the entire screen turns black. This problem seems to occur only when we move the cursor over the image to be edited.

Another user reported that the same issue occurs with Photoshop CC.

Chris Cox from Adobe responded by saying that the root cause could be graphics card drivers or GPU problems, or possibly LCD driver issues. He pointed out that the primary responsibility lies with Apple, suggesting "perhaps there’s an issue with this batch of hardware."

Cox stated that Adobe currently cannot reproduce the error, writing: "I suspect that under these circumstances, it may be a specific version of the GPU or driver causing the problem, and it hasn't affected all machines (otherwise we would have discovered it officially)." He added: "We can't resolve such graphics driver issues because we don’t have access to the source code of Apple's graphics drivers."

"Adobe isn’t Apple, and Adobe can't solve all of Apple's problems," he defended, adding, "Adobe relies on the proper functioning of Apple's system code, just like your car needs a decent road. You can't ask Honda to fix potholes in the road."

However, Cox later managed to reproduce the error on his machine and contacted Apple.

Another post mentioned that Apple has already become aware of the issue.

A post on the Apple forum, titled "My 2013 MacBook Air experiences screen flickering while running Photoshop," describes the same problem. One post reads: "When I use the brush tool in Photoshop CS6 and adjust it above 100px, the screen goes crazy and starts flashing." This post dates back to June 18th.

A reply suggests: "Increasing the screen resolution by one level can temporarily resolve the issue."

Another suggests that upgrading the Apple operating system can fix the problem, but obviously, most users won't do that.

Currently, the new MacBook Air models are also being reported to have WiFi issues. Apple has sent invitations to some users, hoping they will join the AppleSeed program to get a preview of an upcoming MacBook Air WiFi update.
