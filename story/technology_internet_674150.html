The story goes like this: someone conducted an experiment. They placed a flea in a wide-mouthed bottle and covered it with a transparent glass lid. The flea jumped inside, colliding with the glass lid several times. It continued jumping inside but no longer reached the height where it hit the glass lid. Later, when the glass lid was removed, the flea did not jump out. It had already adjusted its jump height to avoid hitting the glass, so it didn't jump out. After hearing this story, don't think my brother is romantic enough to tell fairy tales. He didn't explain its metaphorical meaning to me but instead posed a question. Do you understand its meaning? With a thoughtful expression and a bit of confusion, he said, "You don't have to answer right away. Think about the meaning of this story yourself." And indeed, like a student back then, after hearing the teacher's fable, I didn't feel its humor or wit but rather pondered what social reality it symbolized. I knew I was that flea.
