Index weight stocks surge, dragging the market to a roller-coaster ride - Stocks

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:25:40

In terms of news, industry insiders said that the stock index futures may be launched by next June. Shenzhen Stock Exchange has set limits on accounts suspected of manipulating Jifeng Nongji Co., Ltd. The People's Bank of China net withdrew 687 billion yuan in eight weeks. Banco Bilbao Vizcaya Argentaria (BBVA) increased its shares in CITIC Bank by 10.9 billion yuan. China Shipbuilding Industry Corporation will be subscribed on December 7th.

In terms of industries, the insurance sector surged by 6.37%, leading all sectors higher. The securities sector followed closely with a gain of 4.93%. Banks and coal-petroleum sectors both rose by more than 3.50%, ranking third and fourth respectively. Besides, large-cap stocks like futures and H-shares also performed well. Small-cap stocks fell the most by 4.81%, while online games, agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry and fishery, electronic information, low-carbon, gold, brewing and food, smart grid, chemical fiber and other small and medium-sized sectors retreated significantly. In total, 49 sectors fell by over 1.0%, and 33 sectors fell by over 2.0%. The non-ferrous metals and real estate sectors declined by 1.30% and 0.56% respectively.

In general, there were fewer rising stocks than falling ones today, and the market was severely divided between large-caps and small-caps. The theme stocks which soared previously suffered greatly, while large-cap blue-chip stocks which had been quiet for a long time pulled up strongly. There are a large number of trapped chips above 3300 points. Under the background of the upcoming Central Economic Work Conference and the differentiation of economic policies among major global economies, it is worth paying attention to how the government maintains the continuity and stability of policies. Currently, there are differences in the market's expectations of the next policy direction, and the volatility of the index may increase next week. It is suggested that investors pay attention to industries and stocks that benefit from inflation.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows:

When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The contents of the order include the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you want to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. Meanwhile, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being traded: the quantity of stocks being bought must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks being sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place orders: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
