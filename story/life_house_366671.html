Property Tax Reform Pilots to Expand Nationwide, Only Housing "Half" Levy Deed Tax

by dwzhanghao on 2010-09-30 10:06:04

Pilot property tax reform to be expanded nationwide, 50% reduction in deed tax for sole residential property

Families owning sole residential property enjoy a 50% discount on deed tax

Original text: Third, adjust the preferential policies of deed tax and personal income tax at the housing transaction stage. Strengthen supervision and inspection of land value-added tax collection, focusing on real estate development projects with prices significantly exceeding surrounding property levels for land value-added tax settlement and auditing. Accelerate the pilot work of property tax reform and gradually expand it nationwide.

Interpretation:

Regarding the above-mentioned new preferential policies for deed tax and personal income tax, the Ministry of Finance, the State Administration, and the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development quickly issued detailed rules last night, namely from October 1, 2010, if a family purchases ordinary housing and this housing is their sole residential property, the deed tax will be reduced by half. For taxpayers selling their own housing and re-purchasing within one year, the personal income tax reduction will no longer apply.

Deed tax "reduced by half" for sole residential property

The document clearly states that in terms of deed tax policy for real estate transactions, for individuals purchasing ordinary housing and this housing being the family's (including the buyer, spouse, and minor children) sole residential property, the deed tax will be reduced by half. For individuals purchasing ordinary housing of 90 square meters or less, and this housing being the family's sole residential property, the deed tax will be levied at a rate of 1%. For ordinary housing purchased by individuals not meeting the above conditions, they cannot enjoy the above preferential policies.

According to previous Beijing's current deed tax policy regulations, the deed tax collection rate is 3%, but for first-time housing purchases under 90 square meters, the deed tax is levied at 1%, and for those meeting the standard of ordinary commercial housing, it is halved, i.e., levied at 1.5%. According to the new policy, starting from tomorrow, October 1st, only first-time homebuyers whose ordinary housing is their family's sole residential property can enjoy the 50% discount on deed tax; similarly, for ordinary housing under 90 square meters enjoying a 1% deed tax rate discount, it must also be the family's sole residential property to qualify for the discount. Those who purchase ordinary commercial housing but own multiple commercial housing units or public housing, housing reform houses, etc., will no longer be eligible for the deed tax preferential policy. Similarly, the preferential policy for personal income tax has also been "reduced", the new policy clearly states that for taxpayers selling their own housing and re-purchasing within one year, the personal income tax reduction will no longer apply.

The official first mention of "property tax pilot work"

The statement in the new policy regarding "accelerating the pilot work of property tax reform and gradually expanding it nationwide" is not only the official's first mention of "property tax pilot work" but also the first mention of "gradual expansion of the pilot to the whole country."

Industry insiders judge that as a long-term tax policy that can stabilize the real estate market, its proposal to accelerate the pilot work and gradually expand it nationwide will once again make "property tax" a sword hanging over the real estate market, affecting price expectations.

Cities with rapidly rising housing prices will increase land supply

Original text: Fourth, effectively increase the effective supply of housing. All regions should increase supervision and assessment of the actual completion of the 2010 housing construction plan and land supply plan, effectively implementing the construction plans and land supply plans for medium-sized ordinary commercial housing and affordable housing. Cities with rapidly rising housing prices should increase the total supply of residential land. Fully implement the tax preferential policies supporting the construction of public rental housing. Encourage financial institutions to support the construction of affordable housing projects, and quickly formulate medium- and long-term loan policies to support the construction of public rental housing.

Interpretation:

Industry insiders believe that "increasing supply" is a problem mentioned in multiple real estate control policies since the introduction of the "Ten Measures." Currently, including Beijing, the newly added housing supply has indeed started to increase, and Beijing's housing inventory recently returned from the "supply and demand imbalance" warning line of 100,000 units back to over 100,000 units. "If a series of measures to increase supply can be implemented, next year's stable real estate market will have an additional ace."

Illegal real estate companies will be suspended from purchasing land and obtaining loans

Original text: Fifth, increase the inspection intensity of the housing trading market, and investigate real estate brokerage agencies for speculative buying and selling, inflating housing prices, and inciting clients to sign 'yin-yang contracts.' For illegal behaviors such as idle land, changing land use and nature, delaying commencement and completion times, withholding properties from the market, continue to increase exposure and penalties. For real estate development enterprises with such illegal records, suspend their issuance of stocks, corporate bonds, and acquisition of new land, and all commercial banks stop issuing new project loans and loan extensions.

Interpretation:

Regarding the provision of "investigating brokerage agencies for speculative buying and selling, inflating housing prices, and inciting clients to sign 'yin-yang contracts,'" industry insiders believe that this is the first time in joint regulatory policies issued by several ministries that mentions investigating brokerage agencies for speculative buying and selling and "yin-yang contracts." Compared to the new policies in April, these provisions address the main issues existing in the market with targeted detailed rules. For example, in Beijing, most current second-hand home sales pay only 50% of the actual taxable amount. Once the new policy is fully implemented, speculators in the second-hand housing market will face high costs of speculation.

Penalties for developers engaging in illegal activities such as idle land, changing land use and nature, delaying commencement and completion times, withholding properties from the market, have been specifically outlined from vague previous regulations to "suspend their issuance of stocks, corporate bonds, and acquisition of new land, and all commercial banks stop issuing new project loans and loan extensions," making them highly operable. A relevant person from Yingcan Real Estate believes that since the resumption of IPOs, unlisted real estate companies raising funds through domestic listings, shell listings, or listing in Hong Kong, and listed real estate companies raising funds through bond issuance have become important channels for developer funding. In the future, if intentional delays in construction or withholding properties from the market are discovered, stock trading will be prohibited, which will certainly be effective for listed companies.

New Policy Analysis

Second-tier cities may see both price and volume declines

The newly introduced policy yesterday will have what kind of impact on the real estate market is the most watched issue. Regarding this, last night, including developers like Vanke, Chindex, Gemdale, Shoukai, SOHO China, and real estate brokerages like Centaline Property and Lianjia Real Estate, along with homebuyers who saw the policy announcement through the news broadcast and online, were discussing it. Summarizing their views, there is relatively consistent agreement that the newly introduced policy yesterday may have a greater impact on Beijing's real estate market in terms of suppressing housing price expectations rather than substantial effects, leading the market to observe again; while for other places, especially some second-tier cities that did not strictly enforce the "Ten Measures" in the past, it will have substantial impacts, and price and volume declines may soon appear.
