First Love Reunites 54 Years After Being Torn Apart (Picture)

by svykn6n3hur on 2012-03-01 09:36:45

When they met again, the once handsome young man had a slightly hunched back, and the once charming young girl's temples were as white as frost. (At the request of the individuals involved, all names in this article are pseudonyms.)

In 1994, Zhao Fang retired from her job. She was fond of handicrafts since childhood and became obsessed with making crafts out of waste materials.

Meeting again after 64 years, their long-sealed romance was rekindled.

Zhao Fang didn't like cooking; she only loved handiwork. Li Yi took charge of all the housework, his motto being: "You make flowers, I'll cook!"

An unexpected turn of events abruptly ended their budding romance.

After being separated for 54 years, this pair of childhood sweethearts could finally reunite, supporting each other through life’s difficulties.

Sweetness and shyness brought a youthful glow to this 73-year-old grandmother. A warm spring-like atmosphere filled their simple Wuhan university staff dormitory.

Tossing and turning at night, Li Yi handed Zhao Fang a letter titled "Unforgettable." In it, he asked her, "Do you remember the first time I came to find you at nursing school? Do you remember walking from school to Yuemar Chang, through Gulaodong up to Sheshan Mountain? At that time, there was always one word on my mind, but I could only hide it inside - this was our pure first love." This was Li Yi's confession that was delayed by 54 years.

After graduating from high school, Li Yi returned to Wuhan and worked at a provincial government office. Coincidentally, he lived less than 2000 meters away from the hospital where Zhao Fang worked.

The lovers reunited in twilight years.

At the time, Li Yi's wife suffered from breast cancer and endured pain for many years. Thinking of Zhao Fang's background in the medical system before retirement, Li Yi sought her help over the phone. Zhao Fang immediately helped by contacting hospitals and arranging radiation therapy. However, disease is merciless, and Li Yi's wife eventually passed away. "While my wife was alive, she was so busy helping that she never had a chance to thank Zhao Fang," said Li Yi. After calming down last August, at the age of 75, Li Yi managed to find out Zhao Fang's address. By then, he learned that Zhao Fang's husband had passed away two years ago, and her two daughters had already started their own families. She was living alone.

Zhao Fang originally came from Shanghai and lived with her grandmother in Huangpi Chaku when she was young.

Reported by Hubei Daily Metropolis News, journalist Li Lil.

After finishing the household chores, Li Yi would watch Zhao Fang make handicrafts beside her. "My greatest wish is to hold her hand and spend every day of the rest of our lives together."

The two resumed their relationship, leaving traces of their affection-filled companionship in parks and cinemas. Their feelings remained unspoken, but their intentions towards each other were clear. Li Yi even thought that perhaps soon they could get married.

Facing Li Yi's sincere confession, Zhao Fang had many concerns because she had been diagnosed with colon cancer. Although the surgery was successful, her condition could change at any time. "Li Yi had already seen his wife pass away from cancer, and now I'm in the same situation. No one wants to go through this again," Zhao Fang said. "No matter how many changes your condition may have, I will face them with unwavering determination!" Li Yi's response was resolute. He did not want to miss her again.

Holding a bunch of handmade "lilies," the slightly hunched Li Yi slowly walked towards Zhao Fang, whose hair was as white as frost.

Unexpectedly, in this year (1957), Li Yi was sent to a school in Macheng to be a teacher. Thinking of the unknown future and unwilling to delay Zhao Fang's youth, he cut off contact with her.

In 1963, Li Yi also got married. Over the following years, he occasionally heard news about Zhao Fang moving back to Wuhan, having two daughters, etc., but none of these things were relevant to him anymore.

For the reuniting of the two elderly people, Zhao Fang's two daughters and Li Yi's children all expressed support.

"Valentine's Day is coming up, let's us oldies also join the trend and celebrate!"

Before the 2008 Beijing Olympics, Zhao Fang, who had gained some fame, appeared on TV. On the day the program aired, Li Yi was watching TV with his wife when he heard a familiar name. He froze instantly. The face on the screen was no longer the same as before, but still radiant. Pointing at the screen, he told his wife, "Look, she is my elementary school classmate..."

Later, Li Yi went to Xiaogan High School, while due to limited family economic conditions, Zhao Fang entered nursing school early. They lost touch from then on.

Li Yi gradually developed feelings for the younger sister next door, but he dared not confess, keeping it hidden in his heart, filling his notebook with Zhao Fang's name. Meanwhile, Zhao Fang also developed feelings for Li Yi, but neither broke the silence on their budding romance.

Living side by side, the two grew up together as close childhood friends.

Nowadays, mentioning these past events, Li Yi no longer has the shyness of his youth: "If only I had said it earlier, one mistake cost more than fifty years..."
