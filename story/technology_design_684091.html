A wonderful voice comes from a healthy life.

by ciaifuke on 2012-02-29 17:41:45

As we age, our ears gradually become less effective, and hearing loss or impairment has become, like gray hair and age spots, a matter-of-fact symptom of aging in people's minds. However, a survey from the United States shows that about 75% of hearing loss is not entirely caused by aging but rather by damage inflicted on the ears throughout one’s life. Research results indicate that excessive exposure to noise is one of the primary reasons for hearing damage. Noise can harm human ears in two ways. First, noise can instantaneously impact the ears, leading to what is known as ear trauma. Second, noise can also cause gradual hearing loss over decades without one even realizing it.

If we consider decibels as the temperature gauge for our ears, then 85 decibels is the maximum noise level that ears can tolerate without protection. The sounds of washing machines and vacuum cleaners (both below 85 decibels) are unlikely to harm the ears, even if one hears them constantly while awake.

However, if the decibel level exceeds 85, the ears will be damaged to varying degrees depending on the duration of exposure to the noise. Ears can safely endure the sound of a power drill (100 decibels) for two hours, but in a noisy video arcade (110 decibels), one can only endure it for no more than 30 minutes. For every 10-decibel increase in sound, the harmful noise affecting the ears increases tenfold. Be cautious of potential noise damage to children; the sound of toy collisions can reach 110 decibels, and the sound from children's electric guitars can be even higher.

Regardless of your living environment, there are practical measures you can take to protect your ears. You might consider purchasing a pair of earplugs from the pharmacy. The style of these small gadgets (made of sponge, silicon, or wax) is not important; what matters is that they can reduce noise by 20 to 30 decibels. Please note that cotton should not be used as a substitute for earplugs because it does not stop wave impacts on the ears.

If you're unsure whether you need to wear earplugs, you can do a simple test: If you must raise your voice to be heard by someone who is three feet away, you should wear earplugs. Also, try giving your ears a rest. After all, the ears are relatively fragile body organs, yet everyone relies on them to listen to the most beautiful sounds in the world until the very last moment of life.
