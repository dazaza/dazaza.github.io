Six questions about the Amazon tablet

by diglog on 2011-09-28 13:22:08

1. If Amazon's tablet is launched, what will be the price? So far, there has been no official news from Amazon, but it is possible that the tablet could appear at a lower price with the main goal of expanding market share. The outside world speculates that the price of the Amazon tablet could be $250, which is half the price of the iPad 2. It is worth noting that Amazon is skilled at using discount strategies to attract consumers.

2. If the Amazon tablet is priced at $250, how can it ensure profit? Amazon has a well-established content platform covering books, music, and video. It is itself the world's largest online book publisher and is actively introducing music and video content. Currently, it is collaborating with multiple manufacturers such as CSB, NBC, and FOX, expanding its video library to 11,000 titles and securing the rights to broadcast popular TV series like FOX's "24" and "The X-Files."

Currently, Amazon is actively promoting subscription services. Whether it is e-book content or music and video content, users only need to pay an annual fee to enjoy unlimited access to Amazon's digital content. Users can already purchase Amazon Prime service for just $79 to enjoy video content. As for e-book services, Amazon is currently in negotiations with various publishers.

3. Is it too late for Amazon to join the tablet market now? It should not be too late. Amazon is a content provider, and like the Kindle, the tablet is another channel for distributing digital content. This differs from Apple, which relies on hardware sales to generate high profits.

Amazon’s focus is not on competing directly with the iPad; instead, it needs to perform better than most Android tablets to gain more market share.
