Hydrophilic fillers such as hyaluronic acid will gradually be absorbed by the human body over time, usually between 4 to 6 months. This is considered extremely safe by many plastic surgeons. "If you are not satisfied after injection, it can be restored after a period of time, with no damage to the body, because the material itself is a component of the skin, and will be discharged through metabolism," Liu Feng pointed out. However, for this reason, hyaluronic acid filling has a "time limit". According to Luo Shengkang's introduction, the validity period of the currently approved Restylane is about 4-6 months, and then it will return to its original state. According to relevant experts, although the national approval is for nasolabial groove filling, clinical practice has extended to other facial wrinkles such as forehead and eyebrow wrinkles (川字纹) due to the same principle.

As for the use of hyaluronic acid in shaping (such as nose augmentation, lip enhancement), Luo Shengkang said that there are many types of hyaluronic acid, and their "adhesion" degrees are different. The viscosity of Restylane is relatively low, mainly used for wrinkle smoothing; there are also other products (such as Perlane), which have been processed through some refining techniques, increasing their density and purity, with higher viscosity, mainly used for shaping, and its effectiveness will last longer, but such products are not yet approved at present.

It is understood that there are currently more than ten types of injectable hyaluronic acid, such as Restylane, Fineline, Perlane, SUB-Q, etc., mainly produced by European and American companies, including about four or five hyaluronic acid products including Restylane that have passed FDA certification in the United States.

Experts

Aesthetic seekers must be cautious in choosing products and hospitals. Although hyaluronic acid is favored by plastic surgeons because it can be absorbed by the human body, there are still certain risks in its use. According to experts, the hospital recently treated an aesthetic seeker who suffered from skin necrosis due to hyaluronic acid injection. "This is mainly due to the injection technique problem, because hyaluronic acid is a dermal filler, if the doctor injects too shallowly, it will lead to skin necrosis," he pointed out that the operation threshold for injection cosmetic procedures is low, and many unqualified personnel with low skills are operating illegally, so aesthetic seekers should choose carefully.

On the other hand, on the database of the National Food and Drug Administration, the use of each product must be transparent. If serious safety problems occur, they should be reported to relevant departments in accordance with the relevant adverse reaction monitoring regulations. When the product is re-registered, it should submit an evaluation report on all adverse events caused by the use of the product. For this, many plastic surgery experts believe that these regulations are conducive to safeguarding the interests of aesthetic seekers. Luo Shengkang introduced that currently only one product has been approved by SFDA, but there are no less than 10 kinds of hyaluronic acid filling products on the market, among which there may be counterfeit products, even more worrying is that there may be products diluted with Okinawa, or products made with hyaluronic acid used in industry.
