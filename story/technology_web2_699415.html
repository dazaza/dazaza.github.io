US Judge Rejects Wi-Lan's Patent Infringement Lawsuit Against LG Electronics TVs

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-09 14:58:07

Sina Technology News, early morning on March 8th Beijing time, U.S. Manhattan District Judge Lewis Kaplan rejected the patent infringement lawsuit brought by Canadian company Wi-Lan against LG Electronics on Wednesday. Wi-Lan previously stated that LG Electronics had infringed upon its V-chip patent, which provides parental filtering functions in televisions.

Kaplan also dismissed LG Electronics' counterclaim against Wi-Lan and declared an end to the litigation. Wi-Lan filed this lawsuit against LG Electronics in January 2010. Kaplan's decision followed the recommendation of U.S. Federal Magistrate Judge Andrew Peck, who suggested dismissing the case last August. Peck believed there was no reason to indicate any infringement, a view with which Kaplan agreed.

Neither party's legal representatives responded to the court's ruling. Wi-Lan previously indicated that the V-Chip technology was invented by Tim Collings in 1991 to help users filter programs through digital TV receivers.

Starting from the year 2000, all TVs of 13 inches or larger sold in the U.S. market were required to be equipped with this technology. In December 2010, Wi-Lan reached a settlement agreement with LG Electronics, ending their patent litigation in a federal court in Texas.

Wi-Lan focuses on the licensing and enforcement of patents. The company said that more than 250 companies, including Cisco, Nokia (Weibo), and RIM, have used its licensed patents. (Waijin)
