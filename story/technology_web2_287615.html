The story of the cup and water

by yadono05qd on 2009-12-01 14:45:03

Cup girl: "When people are lonely, they need water. Give them fire on their face." Servant: "Well, if you have the water you desire, will you no longer be lonely?" Cup: "It should be so." The servant poured fire into the cup. The water was warm, and the cup felt like it was about to melt. The cup thought, this must be the power of love. As the water cooled, the cup felt comfortable. The cup thought, this must be the feeling of life. When the fire became cold, the cup was afraid, not knowing what it feared. The cup thought, this must be the taste of loss. When the water turned completely cold, the cup felt disappointed. The cup thought, this must be the "masterpiece" of fate. Cup: "Person, quickly pour the water out, I no longer need it." But the person was no longer there. The cup felt like it was being crushed to death, with its beloved water now cold, lying in its heart, but still feeling good to pass through. The cup made a great effort and finally expelled the water from its heart. Feeling relieved, suddenly, the cup fell to the ground. The cup shattered. In its dying moments, it saw that every place in its heart bore traces of water. Only then did it realize, it hated the water, it hated it so much. But, it could never completely expel the water from its heart again. The cup cried, its tears mingling with the water, hoping to use its last strength to love the water one more time. The master picked up the fragments of the cup, and one cut his finger. Blood dripped. The cup cried, hating love, what is it after all? Is it only when we experience pain that we learn to cherish? The cup cried, love, what is it after all? Must we only abandon it when everything is irretrievable? The cup cried, love, what is it after all...
