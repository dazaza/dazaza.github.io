General manager was caught drunk driving and showed the CPC membership card to refuse getting out of the car (Photo)

by mingdaolin on 2009-11-24 11:04:27

The traffic police had no choice but to call a tow truck to take the BMW car away. On the evening of the previous day, Lin, the driver of the BMW, was stopped by Jinshan traffic police on Tangyuan Road in Zhujin Town for suspected drunk driving. However, in the following hour, Lin staged a farce: he first attempted to turn around and flee but failed due to the engine stalling. When the police asked him to show his documents, he actually pulled out a "Party Member Certificate." Because he refused to get out of the car, the police called a tow truck. During the process, he swapped seats with someone else in an attempt to find a scapegoat. Eventually, unable to hold his bladder any longer, he had to get out of the car.

In a phone call, Lin revealed his identity as the general manager of a company in the ZhuJin Industrial Zone in Jinshan. The on-site alcohol test showed that Lin's blood alcohol content reached 1.14 milligrams per milliliter, exceeding the standard for drunk driving. He was then administratively detained for 15 days. Due to this "outstanding performance," Lin was dubbed the "toughest boss" by the onlookers.

When caught for drunk driving, Lin tried to use his "Party Member Certificate" to evade responsibility. At 7:25 PM on the previous evening, the Jinshan traffic police set up a checkpoint at the intersection of Tangyuan Road and Tingfeng Highway in Zhujin Town to check passing vehicles. At this time, a BMW traveling north along Tangyuan Road suddenly stopped about ten meters from the checkpoint, preparing to turn around. During the turning process, the car suddenly stalled, its lights went out, and it came to a stop in the middle of the road. The duty traffic police immediately sensed that "this car must have something wrong" and proceeded to check it.

Despite it being nighttime, with the help of streetlights, one could still see through the car window glass that there were five people inside, including the driver whose face was red, indicating suspicion of drunk driving. Since the car doors and windows were tightly closed, after saluting, the traffic police indicated for the driver to show his driver's license and vehicle registration certificate. Unexpectedly, not only did the driver refuse to cooperate, but he also started the car and attempted to flee. At this point, two traffic police officers blocked the BMW's way. After multiple unsuccessful attempts, the driver finally gave up trying to escape.

When the traffic police again indicated for him to show his documents, the driver actually opened a small gap in the window and shoved out a red "Party Member Certificate." Subsequently, the window was quickly closed. Regardless of how the traffic police saluted or signaled, the five people inside completely ignored them. This move by the BMW driver left the traffic police both laughing and crying. "You should show your driver's license and vehicle registration certificate, not other documents." After looking at the cover of the "Party Member Certificate," the duty traffic police returned the document to the BMW driver.

During the towing process, the driver switched places with another person to bring in reinforcements. Due to the incident occurring in a bustling section, the BMW with the license plate number沪FJ8852 soon attracted dozens of onlookers. "No matter how the police persuaded, that driver just wouldn't get out of the car and was even making phone calls inside." Spectator Mr. Shi told reporters that he suspected the "BMW" driver was seeking connections and bringing in reinforcements. "That driver looked to be forty or fifty years old, quite authoritative, and clearly appeared to have been drinking." Since the driver and others refused to get out of the car, the duty traffic police called in a videographer to record evidence on site.

Regardless of the videographer filming outside the car window, the five people inside the BMW remained steadfastly unmoved. As the car had already affected the normal passage of Tangyuan Road, the traffic police had no choice but to call a tow truck. Even after the tow truck arrived on the scene, these five people still refused to get out of the car.

After more than ten minutes, the BMW was towed to the courtyard of the Fourth Detachment. At this point, the traffic police were surprised to find that the driver had actually swapped seats and moved to the back seat during the journey. Fortunately, the traffic police had previously recorded video evidence, otherwise, they would have been deceived. At this time, the reinforcements brought by the BMW driver also arrived at the Fourth Detachment.

It turned out that this BMW driver was the general manager of a company in the ZhuJin Industrial Zone in Jinshan, and the "reinforcement" was a staff member of the industrial zone. Faced with the illegal facts, the "reinforcement" had a clear attitude, demanding that he immediately get out of the car and accept handling. However, the BMW driver simply didn't appreciate the gesture.

Finally got out of the car due to urgent need to urinate. Around 9 PM, nearly two hours after the incident occurred, the BMW driver finally opened the car door, the reason being that he could no longer endure the strong urge to urinate that he had been suppressing for a long time. The police immediately conducted a breathalyzer test on Lin. The results showed that his blood alcohol content was 1.14 milligrams per milliliter, exceeding the standard of 0.8 milligrams per milliliter for drunk driving.

During the police investigation, the 43-year-old Lin claimed to be from Fujian Province, stating that this BMW 7 Series car belonged to him; when asked about his occupation, he repeatedly emphasized that he was just working for someone else.

Traffic Police: Handling is Irrelevant to Identity

For Lin pulling out the "Party Member Certificate" to evade responsibility for drunk driving, many onlookers believed this action was absurd.

Regarding this, a Jinshan traffic police officer who was on duty that day stated that regardless of identity or other titles, the traffic police will handle cases according to law. According to relevant provisions of the "Road Traffic Safety Law," Lin was ultimately administratively detained for 15 days, and his motor vehicle driver's license was temporarily confiscated.

According to the Jinshan police, from 7 PM to 9:30 PM on the previous evening, a total of 36 cases of drunk driving violations were detected in Zhujin Town, among which there were 10 cases of drunk driving. [Related Links]

Investigating "Drunk Driving" Cannot Be Downgraded

In September this year, Shanghai police published the latest law enforcement principles regarding drunk driving on their official website in the form of "Government Information Disclosure." For those caught for "drunk driving," penalties will be imposed strictly and swiftly without any excuse for downgrading the handling.

In specific penalty standards, those who drive under the influence of alcohol will have their motor vehicle driver's licenses suspended for three months and fined 500 yuan. Those who drive while intoxicated will be detained for ten to fifteen days, have their motor vehicle driver's licenses suspended for six months, and be fined 2000 yuan. If there are aggravating circumstances, they will be detained for fifteen days, have their motor vehicle driver's licenses suspended for six months, and be fined 2000 yuan.
