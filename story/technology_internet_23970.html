10 Most Common Blog Time Management Tips

by cacard on 2007-07-03 22:17:35

When a blogger finds their writing time getting tighter and more difficult, they should analyze where they are wasting their time. Some common habits can unconsciously consume one's time. After analysis, here are ten time management tips for bloggers that address common time-wasting areas.

1. **Unsubscribe from Certain Subscriptions**

This is a major time-waster. If you have over 200 feeds subscribed to in Google Reader, you often end up with more than 100 articles to read daily. At this point, it’s best to unsubscribe from some feeds and keep your reading volume under 100 articles. You can unsubscribe from feeds that update frequently, only show summaries, or duplicate content from other feeds. Move these high-update feeds to Live or iGoogle for easier management. This way, you save a lot of time while still receiving accurate updates.

2. **Avoid Tweaking the Template**

Is the link color #0033cc better than #0066cc? Maybe the sidebar could be wider, or perhaps the footer should be gray. Beautifying your blog and optimizing SEO is endless, so don't waste time on it every day. Instead, set aside an entire weekend for such tasks.

3. **Test Ad Units Longer**

Optimizing ad unit sizes, colors, and positions can increase your earnings, but changes in click-through rates and revenue aren't always immediate. Therefore, wait a week before checking the effectiveness of your ads.

4. **Check Stats Less Often**

Website traffic is an important metric, but is it necessary to constantly check the site statistics and focus on referral sources? Checking website stats once a day is sufficient. Knowing the daily total visits is enough to analyze your SEO performance.

5. **Turn Off Instant Messaging**

Is it necessary for others to see that you're online? If you want to focus on writing, avoid distractions by closing all instant messaging apps (MSN/Gtalk/QQ). Concentrate on writing. If you need to check offline messages, go incognito and connect only when necessary.

6. **Handle Emails Twice a Day**

Do you need to reply to emails immediately? You're not doing customer support, so there's no need to respond within four hours. Handle emails twice a day and spend your time on more meaningful activities. When replying, don’t delay—respond to today’s emails today; don’t leave them for tomorrow.

7. **Ignore Spam Comments**

Don't waste time searching through thousands of spam comments filtered by Akismet for rare "non-spam" ones. If a reader's comment is important, they will follow up. So, don’t bother looking at spam comments or spam emails.

8. **Turn Off Adsense Notifications**

There’s a cool Firefox extension that checks your Adsense income every 15 minutes. While good income can boost morale, it’s better to turn off this feature and check your income once a day. This will help you stay focused on writing posts. Turn off all real-time statistical report tools.

9. **Reduce Keyword Alerts**

Using Google Alerts can help track breaking news or topics you care about, but these notifications can pile up, wasting a lot of time. Delete alerts unrelated to your blog's theme.

10. **Stop Overthinking**

Write down your ideas on paper or in an electronic file. Your sudden inspirations may fade quickly, so record your thoughts and plans in a notebook. Don’t just imagine things—write them down. These notes might become your next article.

Are you still wasting your time? Try implementing the solutions above. You'll soon escape the trap of wasting time and manage your time correctly.
