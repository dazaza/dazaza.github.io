"Sweet Emperor" is back again, as high as 8000 yuan per ton

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-15 14:25:24

With the arrival of the annual sugar consumption peak, the "sugar high index" has once again made a comeback. Data shows that currently, the spot market quotes for white sugar in East China and Central Plains regions have reached as high as 8,000 yuan per ton, almost doubling compared to two years ago. The futures market price, after being impacted by the US debt crisis, has basically returned to its previous high.

Every third quarter, the white sugar market is in the transition phase where the sugar from the previous crushing season is almost depleted and the new season's sugar has yet to come out. Meanwhile, beverage production and festival celebrations increase market consumption, making it the peak season. Even without a significant decrease in production, the market price tends to be strong during this period every year. "The overlap of the peak season consumption and the dual holiday preparation period for National Day and Mid-Autumn Festival makes the white sugar inventory even tighter," said an industry insider.

According to Guangxi Sugar Network data, by August 12, the spot quotes for most white sugar sales regions, including Northwest China, East China, and Central Plains, have reached over 8,000 yuan per ton. The quote in Lanzhou, which has seen a larger increase, is as high as 8,100 yuan. "Despite the price basically doubling since 2009, the prices in both production and sales areas are almost rising daily. Especially with the upcoming Mid-Autumn Festival, the market expects to experience a peak in demand," said an industry insider.

Many analysts pointed out that the return of the "sugar high index" is mainly due to the continuous reduction in production leading to supply-demand imbalance and the overlapping of the peak consumption season. Speculation in international markets about the possible reduction in Chinese demand and Brazilian supply has also further pushed up sugar prices.

Statistical data shows that during the two weeks from August 1 to 12 when financial markets were most significantly impacted, the maximum decline of the white sugar index, which tracks the performance of white sugar futures prices, did not exceed 1.5%. In contrast, the domestic commodity market fell nearly 5% during the same period. If we include the strong market performance in July, the cumulative increase in white sugar futures prices on the Zhengzhou Commodity Exchange since the second half of the year has exceeded 8.5%, far stronger than other commodities like copper and aluminum.

"Although high sugar prices have curbed some consumption, the market expects the full-year demand to reach around 13 million tons, leaving a market gap of over 2 million tons," said Zhang Sang, analyst at Huatai Changcheng. The main producing areas have experienced three consecutive years of reduced production, resulting in a persistent supply-demand gap in the domestic market. Although the market supply-demand should be balanced with reserve releases and imports, expectations of declining market inventories and replenishment needs after reserve releases also support the rise in sugar prices.

"It's worth noting that the influence of the international market on the domestic market is increasing," said Zhang Sang. Actually, from the perspective of the supply gap, the volume of over 2 million tons has not significantly increased compared to last year, but this year's market continues to speculate on Brazil's possible reduced production and the expected increase in China's imports. The rise in international market prices also provides strong support for the domestic market.
