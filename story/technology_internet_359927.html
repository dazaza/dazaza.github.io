Natto cured my high blood pressure (case)

by yy1s21 on 2010-07-05 10:10:36

 

Mr. Zang is a retired worker from Baosteel in Shanghai. He is 61 this year. He enjoys drinking alcohol and smoking, and no matter how his children try to persuade him, he doesn't listen, always believing there’s nothing wrong with it. Last year, he suffered from thrombosis caused by high blood pressure, leading to blurred vision and double images. After receiving two weeks of thrombus treatment at the hospital, he basically recovered. However, his blood pressure still fluctuated frequently, often rising to around 190, sometimes even reaching 200-210/120-130 mmHg. This made him constantly worried about whether something might happen while sleeping. Although he said he didn’t care, seeing his adorable grandson made him wish to watch him grow up…

 

One day, his daughter brought back a product called GMC Japanese imported natto. Mr. Zang complained to his child about why they had bought this, but since it was already purchased, he started taking it. Starting from February 19th of this year, he took two capsules daily, one in the morning and one in the evening. By the beginning of the third week, one night while sitting at home watching TV, Mr. Zang felt as if mosquitoes were biting his right calf, but no matter how he looked, there were neither ants nor mosquitoes. His spouse checked carefully, but found nothing either. Suddenly, he realized it was the place on his leg where varicose veins occurred. This situation lasted for two or three days and then disappeared.

 

Mr. Zang has osteophytes on both legs. The doctor advised him to exercise less, avoid climbing mountains and going upstairs, because Mr. Zang often experiences stiffness and swelling in his lower limbs, feeling like they weigh a thousand catties. He couldn’t take his grandson out to play, and every day was painful. Since taking the GMC Japanese natto, his blood pressure has been basically controlled within the normal range, and the varicose veins that have plagued him for over twenty years have also improved. Now, Mr. Zang takes his grandson to play on the small hill in the community every day, and he can also help his spouse buy groceries and cook meals, looking completely renewed and full of energy.

 

Small Suggestion: How to choose better nattokinase?

    With so many products on the market, how should consumers choose? Generally speaking, there are two things to look at: First, check the origin. Products that are originally imported from Japan are considered superior. Here, "original import" means the entire product is originally imported, not just the raw materials being imported and repackaged domestically. Due to Japan's strict supervision and mature market, the quality and content of original Japanese natto products can generally be guaranteed. Secondly, look at the manufacturer. Large domestic enterprises are also trustworthy, such as Nogink. Many small factories are also producing natto, but they merely package natto powder directly, and the actual content is far lower than what is indicated.
