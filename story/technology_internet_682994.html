Less experienced investors tend to immediately think about cashing out once they see profits after buying or selling a commodity. Gold trading differs from stock trading. People often focus solely on prices while ignoring the overall upward or downward trends of gold prices. When gold prices rise, they become increasingly expensive, and the higher they go, the more hesitant people are to buy. Conversely, when gold prices fall, they seem cheaper, leading people to hold off on purchases. In practice, this often leads to going against market trends rather than following them. In an uptrend, the only wrong buying point is when the price reaches its peak—like hitting the ceiling where it cannot rise further. Apart from this, any other point is a correct buying opportunity. Similarly, in a downtrend, the only wrong selling point is when the price hits its lowest point—like reaching the floor where it cannot fall further. Beyond this, any other point is a correct selling opportunity.
