The Love of Blue Cheongsam

Blue is my preference, blue is my fantasy, and the blue cheongsam is my lifelong cherished treasure... —— Preface

I don't know why, but I have a particular fondness for the color blue, especially that alluring, gem-like blue. This blue seems to have seeped into my very marrow, transformed into blood, and ceaselessly circulates within me; this blue sometimes feels like a dream-filled enchantment, swirling in my mind. Because of my love for blue, I even made the icon for my collection of kind-hearted essays and the header image for my personal anthology in shades of blue. Yet, my blue dream remains deeply, cautiously pressed at the bottom of my camphorwood chest, for fear it might shatter at the slightest touch. Oh, it's a heart-dazzling blue cheongsam...

Speaking of purchasing this cheongsam, it was both fate and coincidence. On the day I returned from a business trip, I discovered that a new cheongsam shop had moved in downstairs. Out of curiosity, I observed at the door, and the shop owner enthusiastically invited me to take a look inside. I hesitated for a moment, but out of politeness, I went in.

What first caught my eye was its color—a crystalline, translucent blue. Seeing it felt like falling into a dreamlike fairyland. This blue, akin to the gentle freshness after a rainstorm, brims with joy and grandeur; this blue, similar to the rosy glow breaking through clouds, is filled with mystery and profundity. Its design features a single lotus flower, several lotus leaves, encircling a blue water surface adorned with gold and silver. A beautiful dragonfly rests leisurely on the edge of a lotus leaf, attentively staring at the few dewdrops on the leaf surface, as if they would slip off at any moment. From afar, this cheongsam looks like an exquisite yet fragile blue-and-white porcelain vase sitting there; even when you approach, you dare not easily pick it up to admire in your hands. It's so beautiful that it instantly triggered my desire to possess it.

The shop owner took it down and held it against my chest for comparison, exclaiming how harmonious it looked with my skin tone, making me unwilling to let it go. Strangely, once I tried it on, I didn't want to take it off. The size fit perfectly, the length was just right, and wearing it, my reflection in the mirror appeared bewitchingly charming. It was as if it had been tailored specifically for my body: the waistline fit snugly, perfectly showcasing the curves of my figure; the length reached just above my ankle, fully displaying my slim physique; the sleeveless design allowed my skin to glow beautifully against the blue. Its patterns were fresh and vivid, the layers distinct, and the silk surface shimmered with a pearl-like elegance. Touching it gave a feeling of softness and etherealness, the fabric pure without any impurities. The impeccable traditional Suzhou embroidery refined it to perfection. Wearing it, as the smooth, delicate silk touched my skin, I was enveloped by a sense of ethereality, emptiness, self-admiration, and completeness—exceedingly pleasant and irresistibly tempting.

The shop owner said this cheongsam was custom-made for a customer a year ago, but after completion, the customer disappeared and never came back to collect it. She wanted to recover her costs, so she hung it for sale for a long time but couldn't find anyone who could wear it. We know that it's the cheongsam that picks the woman's posture, and it's the cheongsam that makes women beautiful enough to topple cities. For graceful women, meeting their ideal cheongsam is destiny. In the mirror, I playfully acted coquettish—turning around, glancing back; stepping and twisting; raising my hand to mimic willow branches; sighing sweetly; provoking the shop owner's continuous approval, saying I looked more elegant and graceful in the cheongsam. Under her guidance, I appeared exceptionally classical and charming in the mirror, arousing an irresistible desire. Of course, I knew her words were heavily exaggerated. But, hearing them made me feel good, compelling me to reluctantly pull out my wallet and make the purchase.

Honestly speaking, not all women can wear cheongsams, and not all women can wear them with grace. But this does not affect my love for cheongsams, nor does it affect my admiration for them. Tragically, after buying this cheongsam, I haven't worn it outside many times. I only wore it when going to the cinema or theater, and most often, I wore it in front of the mirror at home. Because, women who wear cheongsams must possess stunning beauty and extraordinary posture. I lack these qualities, I am aware, but I do not tarnish the dignity of the cheongsam. Therefore, I could only store it deep in the bottom of the chest, carefully preserving it.

Since acquiring this blue cheongsam, I often gaze at the rainy sky, hoping one day to traverse Dai Wangshu's "Rain Alley." That is a clear and beautiful scene, constantly imprinted in my mind: a young lady from Jiangnan, dressed in a light blue cheongsam, holding an oiled paper umbrella, her face full of charm and tenderness, treading hesitantly in small steps, lonely passing through a long, winding rain alley, standing on a misty stone bridge. The woman's elegant demeanor evokes boundless imagination, her refined and composed aura inspires melancholic dreams; the fresh air carries a faint lilac fragrance, wafting by with the wind; under the rain threads, it feels as if one is floating in a dreamlike landscape. Especially that enchanting blue cheongsam, which highlights the woman's clarity, transparency, depth, serenity, warmth, tranquility, and gracefulness, making her appear extremely bewitching, described as peerlessly beautiful without exaggeration.

The renowned Russian painter Wassily Kandinsky, when discussing colors, said that blue is a color that stimulates brain thinking, a color that can be heard and smelled. It is more seductive than red, more ethereal than green, more mysterious than yellow, more beautiful than purple, and more elegant than black. In his book "The Relationship Between Spirituality and Art," he profoundly analyzed the subtle relationship between psychological effects and colors... Therefore, I was destined to fall into the trap of buying this blue cheongsam. However, I still genuinely adore it from the bottom of my heart, having a special affection for it.

The famous impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh, when discussing colors, also said that blue is a color full of life tension, and excessive attachment to it can cost one's life! No wonder, under Van Gogh's starry sky, the night is so mesmerizingly blue, and the background of his self-portrait is incomprehensibly blue, with its beauty known only to the master himself. Immersed in the despair of blue, the master was lonely and lost, painting the blue so suffocating and sorrowful that it almost makes one cry uncontrollably. Eventually, he tragically ended his own life under the allure of blue... Therefore, I hold blue with reverence and respect, fearing to desecrate it. After bringing this cheongsam home, every time I wear it, I must bathe and burn incense before daring to do so.

Since acquiring this blue cheongsam, my passion for blue has become even stronger, even more devoted! I love blue cocktails, captivating and intoxicating; I enjoy blue-tinted soft music, relaxing and sophisticated; I like blue-covered books, melancholic and profound; I even like blue advertising slogans: Sky Blue, Sea Blue, Dream Blue, evoking endless imagination...

Perhaps every woman loves cheongsams, perhaps every woman wants to wear one. But, the cheongsam is a magical garment: being able to wear it is a kind of fate; wearing it well is a kind of taste; appreciating it is a kind of comfort; collecting it is a kind of寄托寄托. No woman can resist the beauty of a cheongsam, and no man can withstand its allure. A blue cheongsam fills women with ambiguity and entanglement towards cheongsams, and even if they have to borrow money or go into debt, they would buy it without hesitation!

Blue is my preference, blue is my fantasy, and the blue cheongsam is my lifelong cherished treasure... This beloved blue cheongsam belongs solely to me, belonging to my collection of dreams.
