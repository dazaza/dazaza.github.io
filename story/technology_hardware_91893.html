The girl who accompanied me to one hundred blind dates became my wife.

by nmgjggz on 2008-09-14 22:44:52

We were colleagues several years ago.

She graduated from a prestigious university, and everyone who met her for the first time was amazed by her beauty - an extraordinary kind of beauty.

I am a self-effacing person, almost possessing nothing, so I only knew that I had to keep moving forward with relentless effort.

Since I am not a successful person, in this big city, I possess nothing, which makes it hard for me to find a partner. Many people I met didn't think highly of me. I just wanted a home, and the thought of someone waiting for me after work moved me deeply. Since I got to know her, she has been very concerned about me, actively introducing potential partners to me. She often accompanied me on these meetings, though she stayed out of sight. After each meeting, she would critique my behavior and give feedback on the other party, offering reasonable advice and suggestions for improvement. What she was dissatisfied with was that I wasn't romantic enough. Indeed, I don't know how to be romantic. In fact, as someone who possesses nothing, I have no right to be romantic. Perhaps many people think romance has nothing to do with money, but I truly know how inferior I am.

As time went by and family pressure increased, finding a partner became the top priority at home. The frequency of meetings increased, and in total, there were over a hundred times. She and I got busier, frequently attending matchmaking events held by various websites. Because she also didn't have a partner, she was also looking. Due to her beauty, many small-time successes with houses and cars wanted her. Every time I saw a good man, she disagreed, thinking them unworthy. In my heart, I felt that her standards were too high. I even envied her a bit because many girls with poor conditions wouldn't look at me, yet she refused men with good conditions, making me sigh at the unfairness of life and feel that she was too proud, not giving others a chance.

Perhaps the more anxious one gets, the harder it is to find the right person. Over the years, I haven't found a suitable partner, and surprisingly, neither has she, as she hasn't found anyone she likes. I often get angry that she misses out on good opportunities, and I often ask her angrily, "What exactly are you looking for?" She tells me not to worry, saying that if she doesn't like anyone, there's nothing she can do.

One day, an old colleague saw us together frequently and said we should get married. I was dumbfounded; I had never thought about dating her. The colleague suggested I propose and reminded me that since I don't drink alcohol, I should try Red Bull, as some say it boosts courage.

One day after work, I invited her to meet at the park, and I bought a bouquet of roses. It was the first time I had ever bought roses for someone, and I was extremely nervous, worried about what would happen if she refused. I would be utterly embarrassed. Fortunately, I drank two cans of Red Bull and ate some chocolate.

When she arrived and saw the flowers I had bought, she said, "You're going to another meeting today, and you actually bought flowers? How romantic! You've finally learned a little bit of romance." I looked down, stammering, "They're for you." At first, she didn't believe me, but after I repeated it a few times shakily, she understood clearly. When she took the flowers, she burst into tears and cried for a long time. I had always thought she was a strong, proud person, and it was the first time I saw her like this.

In the end, the girl who accompanied me on a hundred blind dates married me.

This is a story, and I hope to share it with you.
