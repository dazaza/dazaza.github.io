An American movie just released, called "He's Just Not That Into You," which essentially means he doesn't like you that much. "All unmarried older women should watch this movie."

The beginning of the movie is very interesting.

From a native in a tribe in Africa, to white-collar workers in high-end restaurants in New York,

from middle-aged women with prosperous figures, to slender girls with devilish figures,

almost every corner of the world has girls asking:

Why hasn't he called me? Why doesn't he come find me? Why did he suddenly lose contact?

And by such girls' side, there are always a group of close friends trying to console her.

Friends always say, "He does this just because he loves you too much," "Maybe he's shy," "Maybe he's insecure," "Maybe he doesn't know how to contact you," "Trust me, he definitely likes you"...

Women only want their sisters to laugh quickly and rarely think about how to make them clear-headed.

The truth is, maybe he just doesn't want to find you.

The movie says, if a man really likes you, he will use all his strength to find you, mobile phone, email, msn, google...

This isn't the Stone Age anymore, truly liking you, even after tsunamis and floods, even if you disappear in the crowd, finding you would be like finding a needle in the sea.

If he promised something but didn't do it, even if it was just a phone call.

Don't make excuses for him, "He's so busy that he forgot", "At least he really apologized to me"...

He's busy, but you're not at ease either, who in this city isn't busy?

Is it busy enough to become the President of the United States, or are there billions of dollars worth of business to discuss every hour?

With a mobile phone, quick dialing, or even voice dialing, sometimes when you don't even think about making a call, the phone dials itself from your pocket.

If he really liked you, he wouldn't forget, if he forgot, it means he doesn't care about your disappointment.

Men never say they're "too busy" for what they want.

If he's being ambiguous.

Don't explain for him: "He's been hurt before," "He just broke up/divorced, he wants to take it slow," "He's used to freedom"...

If a man really likes you, he won't be ambiguous, he'll declare his ownership of you to the world.

If he likes you but due to personal reasons wants to take it slow, he will immediately clarify this point to you.

He won't let you guess, because he doesn't want you to feel disappointed and leave.

If he doesn't want you to meet his friends and family, if he doesn't want to bring you into his circle,

saying because it's just between two people, then please automatically translate it as "I just want to use you to pass the time," "I don't like you that much."

If he betrays you.

Don't think: "He was drunk," "That was just an occasional accident," "He did it unintentionally"... Betrayal has no excuse.

Betrayal is not something that "happens unintentionally," he can't say "oh, I accidentally fell and ended up on someone else's bed."

If he's so drunk to find you but unwilling to change for you while sober, then you should leave,

because life requires sobriety.

If the timing is right but he still doesn't want to get married, it might just mean "he doesn't want to marry you,"

those who say "they don't want to get married" will eventually get married, just not to you.

If he keeps breaking up with you and then comes back to reconcile.

If he suddenly disappears mysteriously.

If he's already married.

So many ifs, actually have only one answer.

Sometimes we'd rather believe that a man is too scared, too nervous, too insecure, too attached to his ex-girlfriend, too sensitive, too busy, too affected by childhood shadows, under too much family pressure, too tired...

than to see the simple truth.

Yes, he's not too busy, not hurt,

not having childhood shadows, not encountering accidents,

not about to become president, not suffering from amnesia,

not losing his phone in hotpot, not having memory loss,

moreover, it's not that you've become strong enough to make him worry-free, he just doesn't like you that much.

Then someone asks, how can you believe that he likes you.

In this world where all rules can be broken and all morals are gradually disappearing, how can you firmly believe?

Please, forget all the rules I said more than half an hour ago.

Never trust the rules, trust your own feelings.

I think this era needs a bit of self-deception, telling ourselves that he actually loves us,

encouraging us to fully commit ourselves to someone without doubt, question, speculation, or testing.

Do you remember what we said about "running hard and falling gracefully"?

In love, please bravely, firmly, run hard,

and then, even if you fall, you can say, you were graceful.

My favorite line from "He's Just Not That Into You".

It's a girl named Gigi, who thought a boy liked her,

after confessing and realizing it was a misunderstanding, she got mocked by the boy, and said a line.

Gigi said:

Maybe I'm too sensitive and make mountains out of molehills, but at least it means I care.

You think using all these rules that can see through women makes you win?

Maybe you won't get hurt, won't embarrass yourself, but you'll also never experience such love.

You're not winning, you're lonely.

Maybe, I've done a lot of silly things, but I know, this kind of me will find the right person faster than you.

Believe in your own feelings, like your own life.

Maybe the so-called happy ending doesn't include giving you a perfect guy,

maybe the so-called happy ending is holding onto the hope of never giving up and continuing forward.

Run hard, fall gracefully.

In a sea of people, keep walking and loving.
