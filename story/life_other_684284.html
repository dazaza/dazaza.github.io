Without Commitment, Does Love Still Exist?

She once told him that after a breakup, they couldn't be friends because they had hurt each other. He was sad and asked repeatedly: "After a breakup, can we really not be friends? Why?"

She didn't tell him the truth —— she still loved him and let him go to give him the freedom he wanted. He didn't know that every day she would stand by a window on the fourth floor watching him silently walk by below, then keep her eyes on him until he disappeared from view. When she saw him walking with many friends, she would smile faintly, feeling happy for him. When he looked disheveled, she would silently pray for him in her heart.

"You must live better than me. Don't fall into ruin for anything. Don't let me see you living poorly; it would break my heart." Every time, her eyes blurred as she watched him disappear. Whenever he accidentally looked up, she would quickly avoid his gaze and enter the broadcasting room just to watch him from afar and avoid the awkwardness of direct eye contact. She didn't want him to see how much she cared about him, nor did she want him to know that she came to the window before he passed by, even if it meant missing her broadcasting time. She would still repeat a song until he was out of sight before returning to find some materials to deal with the program.

Once, she jokingly said to a friend, "I used to be in the broadcasting station too," but he casually responded with "trash." At that moment, tears almost welled up in her eyes, and the noodles she ate got stuck in her throat due to choking. She excused herself by saying the noodles were unpalatable and could no longer eat. An inexplicable sadness arose in her heart.

In fact, she was a sensitive and timid girl who feared everything. Because she had been neglected by her family since childhood, her self-protection awareness was very strong. She was also a fragile child at heart. She thought, "Many times, being strong is just an appearance of strength."

Her so-called real strength was hiding in a corner where no one could see her and letting the tears she had held back in front of others flow freely. Her dream since childhood was to have her own home when she grew up, regardless of its size. She would do her best to make everyone in her family feel warmth.

Therefore, she dared not easily invest her emotions. She feared getting hurt because she couldn't afford more pain. However, she wouldn't let those around her get hurt by her either. Until she met him, she asked him, "Why did you ask me to be your girlfriend?" He replied, "I want to have a home with you." She sat by the riverbank, and the neon lights across the river gradually blurred into a patch of orange. He often told her about his lost childhood, about how he kicked pebbles on the roadside alone, watching leaves fall...

She thought, "Our lives are all gray. Can I make up for what your mother owes you?"

That night, while he was drinking with friends, she sat outside and sent him a text message: "When everyone leaves you, I will not leave. Don't drive me away. I will mend all the scattered pieces of your childhood!"

He was moved and pulled her into his arms, letting her sit on his lap. She felt very happy.

For the first time, when he cried for her, she saw his helplessness and loneliness. She taught him to see things from another's perspective. She hugged him and cried. She was numb and didn't know what to do because what he wanted, she couldn't give. She hated her timidity and cowardice. It turned out that falling in love is easy, but staying together is hard. She thought, let him go. In the future, he could find a better girl. The happiness he wanted, she couldn't give him, but someone else could, which would also be a kind of happiness. As long as he lived well, she would be happy.

However, when she really saw his tears, she understood the ocean in his heart. She quietly told herself, "Innocent and kind boy, you've given me red sorrow and transparent happiness. Girl, don't let a boy cry for you. A boy's tears are dignity. Since he has shed tears for you, treat him well. Don't let him regret giving up his dignity for you. Accompany him through life and death, no matter what difficulties you encounter. Even if he tells you to leave when he's under pressure or annoyed, don't go. Men are more fragile. Take good care of him"...

After going through so many ups and downs, can they walk hand in hand forever?

Never thinking about stopping to pick flowers and smile, does that mean waiting for your return? Jingwei once said, "Once love disappears, commitment is just blank paper."

Without commitment, does love still exist?
