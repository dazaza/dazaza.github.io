The Realm of the Giants in Lightweight Blogging: WordPress vs. Tumblr

by xwkj0983 on 2012-02-21 15:05:22

Tumblogging: Short posts, multimedia support, and have received widespread acclaim in recent years. Tumblr is a well-known tumblogging platform, but it doesn't necessarily have to be purebred; nowadays, there are also many WordPress themes available that can transform WordPress into a tumblog.

For bloggers who don't want to write lengthy articles but wish to share photos, music, videos, and other content, tumblogging is an excellent choice. Compared to maintaining a regular blog, managing a tumblelog has less pressure and is more casual. The question is which tumblog platform to choose.

Should you choose Tumblr or a self-hosted WordPress-style tumblog, or perhaps another platform?

WordPress Tumblog Transformation

Since the release of WordPress 3.0, bloggers have been able to add custom post types, which opens up infinite possibilities for creating tumblogs. Bloggers can set custom formats for various types of content such as videos, photos, audio, quotes, text, and others. Now, it's easier for developers to create tumblog themes similar to Tumblr, eliminating the previous disadvantage of using WordPress for tumblogging.

WooTumblog is a plugin that transforms WordPress into a tumblog. By installing this plugin, WooTumblog provides WP with a fully functional tumblog publishing interface: articles, images, links, audio, videos, and more forms of content.

The biggest advantage of WordPress is self-hosting, where the blogger retains full control over the content and can personalize their website however they like, satisfying their need for control.

Drawbacks of WordPress

Of course, having autonomy also means taking full responsibility for management. It's not just about updating posts; you also need to ensure website security, update plugins, check for malicious code, etc. These tasks sound simple, but virtual hosting requires significant effort in practice.

Another drawback of WordPress is the higher cost compared to free platforms like Tumblr. First, you'll need at least a basic virtual host, and even the most ordinary service providers charge several dollars per month. Especially when considering the basic media traffic for each tumblog site, the higher the traffic, the higher your costs will be.

Tumblr-based "Intimate" Blogging

Today, Tumblr has formed a massive user community: nearly 12 billion published posts, over 32 million tumblogs, with a frequency of 39 million posts per day.

Tumblr's greatest advantage lies in its combination of hosting services and being free, allowing bloggers to express themselves freely. There are plenty of free themes available, or bloggers can create their own CSS and HTML code to DIY themes.

Additionally, Tumblr boasts an active user community, making it particularly easy to find information on Tumblr. Bloggers can instantly bookmark, comment, or directly repost relevant information. This is something that self-hosted WordPress cannot handle (and may never be able to). If you want to join the tumblog community, then embrace Tumblr-based "intimate" blogging.

Tumblr's Sharing Hub

Tumblr has a Goodies page: a sharing hub that connects to mobile apps (iPhone, BlackBerry, Android, etc.), Mac backup tools, Facebook, Twitter, and bookmarklets, enabling instant sharing.

Drawbacks of Tumblr

The pain point of Tumblr is that the fate of users' published content depends on Tumblr's stability. Although Tumblr has been experiencing frequent outages, these issues seem to have been cleared up in recent months. If you use a self-hosted server, as long as you have a good virtual host, you generally won't encounter similar problems.

Thus, hosting services are a double-edged sword. Hosting means users do not have ultimate control over their content. If Tumblr were to shut down one day, there's no guarantee that all user-published content could be recovered. If their official policies conflict with user behavior, they can close accounts without warning. This highlights the importance of backing up Tumblr content to avoid future troubles.

Posterous - The Wild Card

Among various tumblog platforms, one often overlooked option is Posterous.

Posterous is a unique yet simple and easy-to-use blogging platform. You don't need to publish content through the backend or a similar interface; you can simply post via your personal email (though they do offer a backend and mobile apps supporting iOS and Android).

In September 2011, Posterous launched a new tumblog platform called Posterous Spaces. Now, Posterous has reformed privacy controls, allowing users to choose whether to publish content publicly or privately. Posterous Spaces includes all the original features of Posterous, along with several new themes and corresponding functions, effectively integrating tumblogging with social networking, potentially capturing a large number of users.

Moreover, Posterous Spaces allows users to create multiple Spaces (public or private) and set access permissions (similar to MSN Spaces). In a sense, it combines the functionalities of Tumblr and Google+. Like Tumblr, Posterous's advantages include being free, while its drawback is the hosted service.

If you're new to tumblogging, Posterous is a great choice.

Conclusion

If you don't have enough time or don't want to write long articles all day, choose tumblogging: quick publishing, short and concise, fast-paced.

Compared to regular blogs, tumblogs are more casual, more private, and more detailed. Precisely because of this, tumblogs enjoy a unique status on the internet and in people's online lives.

Each platform has its pros and cons, so the best choice is the one that suits you best.
