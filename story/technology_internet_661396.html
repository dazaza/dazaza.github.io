Lost every year, the weather is coldest at dusk.

by baiyi0092 on 2012-02-09 02:09:29

Forgotten is the coldest time of year, when many hospitals put up banners advocating for "treating winter diseases in summer." But what exactly does treating winter diseases in summer mean? Treating winter diseases in summer takes advantage of the natural tendency during summer when yang energy is abundant and rising, along with the human body's yang energy which also tends to rise and become more robust. During this time, the condensation of heat within the body can easily occur. For those with yang deficiency, tonifying herbs that assist yang energy are used, or for those with severe internal cold, warming herbs that dispel cold from the surface are used. This helps to better facilitate the treatment principle of reinforcing yang and expelling cold. Simultaneously, it stores yang energy for the spring and winter seasons, ensuring that the body has sufficient yang energy so that it will not be easily injured by the cold during winter.

Treating winter diseases in summer is a distinctive therapy in traditional Chinese medicine. Based on the principle of "nourishing yang in summer" as stated in the *Huangdi Neijing*, it utilizes the favorable opportunity of warm weather in summer when the body’s yang energy is full. Combined with acupuncture and moxibustion therapies, drugs are applied to specific acupoints to adjust the yin-yang balance of the body, support righteous qi, enhance immunity, and achieve the purpose of either directly or indirectly treating and preventing disease.

Here, winter diseases refer to certain conditions that are prone to occur or worsen during winter. These primarily include respiratory chronic diseases such as allergic asthma, bronchitis, allergic rhinitis, etc. Besides these, they also include rheumatic arthritis, diarrhea, chilblains, acute diarrhea, joint pain caused by certain gynecological diseases due to cold deficiency, kidney deficiency-induced back pain, young people with cold intolerance, and diseases belonging to the category of spleen-stomach cold deficiency in Traditional Chinese Medicine. These diseases generally have characteristics of yang energy deficiency and onset triggered by cold. Summer treatment refers to the fact that these conditions tend to alleviate during summer. Therefore, during the hottest period of the year, the three dog days of summer (三伏天), differential diagnosis and treatment are applied. Appropriate internal and external medications are administered to improve constitution, enhance immunity, prevent recurrence of chronic diseases in winter, or reduce their symptoms.

In terms of treatment methods, treating winter diseases in summer includes various therapies such as acupuncture, bathing, cupping, massage, physical therapy, dietary therapy, acupoint application, and external herbal applications, among which acupoint application is the most commonly used. Below are several recommended acupoint application prescriptions:

1. **Asthma patients**

Use 20 grams each of white mustard seed, Perilla seed, and Corydalis tuber; 10 grams each of bitter Kansui root and Asarum. Grind them into fine powder. Each time, use one-third of the powder, add ginger juice to form a paste, and spread it on six pieces of plastic cloth with a diameter of 5 cm. Apply it to the lung shu, heart shu, and diaphragm shu points (i.e., 1.5 cun lateral to the spinous processes of the 3rd, 5th, and 7th thoracic vertebrae). Secure with adhesive tape and leave for about 3-6 hours. Repeat this during the first, second, and third dog days of summer, for a total of three applications.

2. **"Cold leg" patients**

Use 50 grams of Sichuan Rhizoma, 30 grams of Evodia, 9 grams each of mugwort leaf and thoroughwort, and 6 grams of Asarum. Grind them into fine powder. Wrap the powder in paper, cover with gauze, sew it tightly, and place it under the footpad. Start using it on the first dog day of summer, change the medicine on the second dog day, and again on the third dog day.

3. **Severe dry arthritis patients**

Use 50 grams each of cinnamon and dried ginger, 50 grams each of black pepper and Asarum, 20 grams of clove, 30 grams of myrrh, and 50 grams of Aconite. Grind them into coarse powder. Boil 200 grams of honey into a paste, mix the powder evenly into the honey paste, spread it on cloth, and apply it to the affected area starting on the 10th night of the first dog day. Apply for 6-8 hours daily until the end of the third dog day.

4. **Shoulder periarthritis patients**

Boil 10 grams of Cinnamon twig, 20 grams of Thoroughwort, 30 grams each of Windproof vine and fermented soybean ginger, 15 grams each of Tendon grass, Ginger rhizome, Szechuan Lovage rhizome, and Herbaceous Peucedanum, and 12 grams of Chinese Senega root. Take 300-400 grams of wheat bran, stir-fry it until yellow, add the decoction and a spoonful of old vinegar while still hot, mix well, and place in a cloth bag for hot compresses on the shoulder joint. Each bag can be used for a week. From the first dog day, apply once daily for 6-8 hours continuously until the end of the third dog day.

5. **Patients prone to chilblains**

Boil 25 grams of Cinnamon twig, 10 grams each of White flower, Perilla leaf, Aconite, and Schizonepeta, and 30 grams of dried ginger in an appropriate amount of water. Use the decoction to fumigate and wash the affected areas of chilblains daily. Use one dose per day, and continue for 10 days as one course of treatment.

Many people believe that everyone can undergo "treating winter diseases in summer," but this is not true. The concept of "treating winter diseases in summer" does not cure all diseases and has strict indications and contraindications. The following groups of people are not suitable for patch treatments:

1. Pregnant women and infants over two years old.

2. Patients with severe congenital heart disease, diabetes, and other organic diseases.

3. Individuals who are allergic to the components of the patch.

4. People who develop large blisters after short-term application.

5. Individuals with skin conditions such as blisters, boils, or damaged skin.

6. Patients in the acute stage of a disease (such as fever, coughing, wheezing, etc.).
