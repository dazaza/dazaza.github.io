The royal family declined, and the young emperor ascended the throne. Prince Regent Xiao Qi took control of the government alone. The survival of her family and her husband's ambition inspired a woman's ambition, pushing Wang Xuan (Little Wu) from the boudoir to the court, standing side by side with Xiao Qi at the pinnacle of power. Moving forward meant facing dangerous situations, while retreating meant falling into an abyss. Zidan, who grew up with them, was summoned back to the capital and forced to lead troops in a southern campaign, resulting in fratricidal conflict; palace conspiracies were everywhere, leading to estrangement between masters and servants, and life-and-death struggles; clan members watched hungrily as family members gradually abandoned them; the Tujue requested the princess's hand in marriage, scattering the romantic affairs of the noble families in the desert sands... After weathering numerous storms, who ultimately stood by them unwaveringly, and who passed by them?

The iron-blooded son aims for the world, his sword pointing toward light-cold armor, defending the land and expanding territory, constantly waging war; the beautiful woman is no less capable than men, cutting through thorns and brambles, demonstrating grace and dignity in all directions. The delicate hands that once painted eyebrows in the chamber can also wield swords and drink blood, unafraid of becoming stained with crimson...
