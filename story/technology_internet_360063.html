Why More Than 80% of People Fail to Lose Weight

by yy1s21 on 2010-07-06 22:33:49

According to surveys, over 80% of people who attempt weight loss and body slimming end up disappointed. Why exactly? The reasons are actually quite simple. More than 60% of individuals pursue weight loss and slimming for the sake of beauty, while only 10% focus on health; over 90% aim to reduce fat in their abdomen, waist, hips, or legs, judging their progress solely by weighing themselves or looking in the mirror; more than 60% adopt dieting (including direct fasting and through weight loss pills), with less than 25% engaging in moderate exercise.

---

Dieting is doomed to fail from the very beginning.

No one would doubt that you can lose weight through dieting (by fasting or taking medication). Just look at the emaciated, vacant-eyed children in impoverished areas of Africa. The question is how long you can sustain it. Even if you could, the result often is a decline in bodily functions faster than the rate of weight loss, eventually leading to an inability to continue due to decreased resistance, malnutrition, hormonal imbalances, and other issues. This leads to overeating and eventual weight rebound, possibly even heavier than before.
