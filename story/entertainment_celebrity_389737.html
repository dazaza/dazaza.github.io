Global stars join disaster relief concert; Sina Weibo statistics show that celebrity Weibos account for 30% of earthquake-related Weibos.

by edivista on 2011-03-25 11:45:38

More than 40,000 earthquake-related Weibo posts, with celebrities accounting for a third of them

Benefit performances for disaster relief: the global entertainment industry in action

Weibo's great love

Sina Weibo conducted a statistic that after the Great East Japan Earthquake, the number of Weibo posts related to the earthquake reached an astonishing total of more than 40,000. As the main users of Weibo, statements from celebrities made up one-third of these posts. As the currently most fashionable and hottest communication tool, Weibo became for the first time a forefront position for mutual safety reporting, surrounding assistance, and even emotional release during a major disaster. In the face of natural disasters and man-made calamities, fragmented information once again demonstrated its irreplaceable value.

Besides spreading the word on Weibo, the entertainment circle has released warm and surprising energy in response to this great earthquake. Yesterday, the Hong Kong Artistes Guild held a press conference announcing a series of disaster relief activities to support those whose homes and hearts have been hurt through practical actions.

At the same time, global benefit performances for disaster relief are heating up continuously. Artists like Lady Gaga and U2 are already auctioning off their personal items on a large scale. If you're my fan, you must give it your all this time. The eccentrically dressed Lady Gaga even issued a global decree.

Celebrities' Weibo no longer pretentious

"Believe in Hope" Charity Gala

Reporting safety, blessing, searching for people, promoting scientific knowledge

In the public's impression, celebrities playing Weibo is nothing more than chatting, showing off, or venting. However, this Great East Japan Earthquake has completely overturned this fixed notion. Weibo is like an ancient beacon tower, fully playing the role of finding people, reporting safety, and exchanging information at any time. This time, Weibo has gained enough image points. Renowned sociologist Li Xuyuan commented as such.

Due to filming or rushing notices, many domestic artists were exactly in Japan when the earthquake happened. Reporting safety became the top priority mission for Weibo. He Run Dong told our reporter that he was holding a concert in Tokyo that day. In the afternoon, I was just being interviewed by Japanese media in the hotel when I suddenly felt nauseous. At first, He Run Dong thought his stomach ailment had flared up, but just as he was about to call his assistant, he realized he couldn't stand up, and then the chandelier above started shaking.

A few seconds later, the hotel staff shouted "Earthquake!" and He Run Dong ran with his junior AK to the hotel lobby: some people were squatting, others lying down, but we just couldn't stand steady; the ground felt like it was going to split open. After the quake sensation passed, He Run Dong's first thought was to report safety to his company back home. I brought my juniors out, so I must be responsible for them. Subsequently, He Run Dong took a photo of AK with his phone and posted it on Weibo, mainly to show that we were still alive, which could also let the company and fans rest assured.

If during the Wenchuan earthquake, Weibo's transmission of disaster conditions was still in its infancy, then this time with the Great East Japan Earthquake, its simplicity, flexibility, and speed undoubtedly showed itself perfectly: compared to traditional media, whether for communication, stress relief, or transmitting rescue information, Weibo's advantages are very evident. Li Xuyuan said.

Prayers and appeals are another function that Weibo plays in the hands of celebrities after the earthquake. According to Sina Weibo's statistical data, after March 11th, almost 90% of celebrity users expressed close attention to the disaster situation on Weibo.

Singer Wang Feng said: When disaster strikes, all historical grievances and entanglements can be put aside. As a person, out of respect and cherishing life, let us pray for Japan and the Japanese who suffered the disaster.

Wang Fei usually jokes around on Weibo, but after learning about the Japanese earthquake, she not only played an extended version of "Heart Sutra" during her concert but also hung the "Heart Sutra" video on her Weibo: dedicating this song to the people in the earthquake zone, hoping their hearts can calm down soon.

Zhou Bichang called for protecting the earth on Weibo: Disasters keep happening everywhere. Can we start now, from ourselves, to protect our home? Tonight, although it is my concert in Shanghai, I sincerely ask every friend who attends to protect the environment. Civilization relies on self-awareness. Thanks everyone.

And there are practical types like Zhang Guoli, who stated that his foundation is ready to go at any moment, closely monitoring the disaster situation in Japan. If relevant departments need unified donation actions, we will definitely follow orders, do what needs to be done, and donate if needed.

Eliminating the psychological impact brought by the earthquake has also become an important topic on celebrity Weibo. For example, the recent salt rush, artists explained their views in different ways. Wang Fei belongs to the teasing type, first talking about "difficult-to-find salt," then joking that she would surely marry someone with salt in the future; the outspoken Yao Chen appeared with a cold joke: On TV, aunts were queuing up to buy salt, while an aunt nearby was watching coldly. A reporter asked why she didn't participate, and the aunt calmly replied: The salt I bought during the SARS outbreak hasn't been finished yet.

Many responsible celebrities have persisted in forwarding correct radiation protection scientific knowledge daily and debunking rumors using their influence. For instance, Yao Chen often forwards the latest news from Sina's debunking special section on her Weibo: the more people see it, the lower the blind panic can be reduced.

Global charity concerts

Artists' awareness of great love is gradually increasing

If calling for help on Weibo is still theoretical, then the charity events held around the world showcase the entertainment industry's powerful spirit in the face of disasters. In addition to the upcoming series of activities by the Hong Kong Artistes Guild, Taipei's stars have already held two large-scale charity fundraising galas.

Sun Yanzi told reporters that on March 17th, she was shooting the MV for her new album "Foolish Nation" in Taipei, waking up at 7 am and shooting until midnight. Sun Yanzi said that when she woke up the next day, her waist was already so sore she couldn't straighten up. She originally planned to fly back to Singapore, but a friend called to say there was a charity program that night, so she changed her itinerary temporarily.

Subsequently, Sun Yanzi immediately called several good friends, all of whom were Singaporean singer friends: I asked them if they wanted to come to the event that night, and each one agreed right away and immediately bought tickets. Sun Yanzi said that everyone cares deeply about the earthquake in Japan, and they are just worried about not finding a way to contribute.

That afternoon, Sun Yanzi called her father and asked him to donate money through a Singapore charity organization, then rushed to the scene of the "Believe in Hope" charity gala because she had another task — to serve as a telephone operator. Besides performing, Sun Yanzi answered donation calls for half an hour that night: everyone who called was very excited.

That evening's gala included dozens of artists such as Sun Yanzi, A-Mei, Jolin Tsai, Wu Bai, Chou Hsiao-Tien, Wu Jianhao, and Jiang Hui. Sun Yanzi exclaimed that she hadn't seen such a powerful lineup in years. Ultimately, the entire gala raised a total of 750 million New Taiwan dollars, which should be considered quite an impressive result.

The day before the "Believe in Hope" charity gala, more than a hundred artists including Liu Qian, David Tao, Fan Fan, Wu Jianhao, Zheng Yuan Chang, and Ruby Lin had already held a charity performance titled "Sending Love to Japan," raising a total of 115 million New Taiwan dollars. Having such large-scale charity performances with a massive artist lineup for two consecutive days seems to have been missing for a long time, according to Peng Kangyu, Deputy Chairman of the Taipei Performing Arts Association.

In Peng Kangyu's view, as the entertainment landscape becomes increasingly pan-Asian, artists' sense of great love is gradually strengthening: To aid Africa, big names in Europe and America collectively participate in the Live8 music festival every year, breaking geographical boundaries early on. This time, for the charity concert for the Japan earthquake, our artists also demonstrated a very united front.

Murakami Haruki

Identity: Writer

Works: "Norwegian Wood," "1Q84," etc.

Experience: When the earthquake occurred, Murakami Haruki and his wife were on vacation in the United States, thus avoiding disaster. Later, Murakami Haruki reported safety to Chinese readers through his regular translator Lin Shaohua: I want to express my sincerest gratitude to Chinese readers. It's understood that Murakami has now returned to Japan and plans to begin working on a new book.

Yui Hatano

Identity: Actress

Work: "Bikini"

Experience: Due to her sweet appearance, Yui Hatano is hailed as the "Lin Zhi Ling" of the Japanese actress circle. After the earthquake, the internet wildly spread rumors that Yui Hatano was swept away by a giant wave while shooting at the beach, causing countless fans immense grief, with Japanese media failing to provide definitive news for a long time. However, a few days ago, the news of Yui Hatano releasing a new film appeared on various websites, promptly clarifying the rumor of her demise.

Ayumi Hamasaki

Identity: Singer

Works: "Moments," "Talk to Myself"

Experience: In this earthquake, Ayumi Hamasaki was initially rumored to have fled to the United States to escape danger, but it was later confirmed that the diva stayed in Japan.

An hour after the earthquake, Ayumi Hamasaki sent out her first Twitter post, sending out 143 tweets in 14 hours, averaging one every 10 minutes, even including multiple languages such as Chinese, English, and French. The content covered everything from encouraging disaster victims to providing evacuation information. Currently, Ayumi Hamasaki has postponed the release of her new album, and her April concert has also been indefinitely delayed.

Yoshida Kyoko

Identity: Actress

Works: "Wonderful Life," "Thunder Cop"

Experience: After the earthquake, there were reports online that Yoshida Kyoko was missing, but later Yoshida Kyoko herself issued a safety statement via Twitter. Currently, Yoshida Kyoko is actively raising funds to support disaster area reconstruction, and the foundation she personally established has received generous support from fellow professionals such as Otsuka Makoto, Shizuka Arakawa, and Ai Fukuoka.

Takehiko Inoue

Identity: Manga artist

Works: "Slam Dunk"

Experience: Once rumored to have fainted during the earthquake, Takehiko Inoue began publishing a series of comic sketches titled "Smile" on his Twitter a week ago. Besides reassuring fans, he hopes to encourage Japanese citizens with this comic and pray for the victims.

Akira Toriyama

Identity: Manga artist

Works: "Dragon Ball"

Experience: Akira Toriyama, who was rumored to have died in the earthquake, published a drawing encouraging the affected Japanese population on the "Weekly Shonen" magazine on March 15th, dispelling the false rumor of his death. Beside the drawing, Toriyama wrote: "Victims, you've suffered a lot. But you must persevere, don't lose, please keep going!" Currently, Toriyama is collaborating with Japanese manga artists to prepare for a large-scale work charity sale.

Yuko Shimizu

Identity: Manga artist

Works: Hello Kitty

Experience: Because she couldn't be contacted by phone, Yuko Shimizu was rumored to have died in a tsunami while drawing by the sea. However, three days after the earthquake, Yuko Shimizu reported safety through her official blog, saying that when the earthquake occurred, she was on the train. After the emergency evacuation, she rested in a coffee shop, and her mood gradually calmed down. Currently, Yuko Shimizu plans to auction her original Hello Kitty drawings and donate all proceeds to the brave souls remaining at the Fukushima nuclear power plant.

"We Are the World"

Performers: Michael Jackson and Various Artists

Popularity: ★★★★★

Review: In 1985, Michael and Richie jointly composed this piece, followed by Michael Jackson completing the lyrics alone. Initially, this song was Jackson's solo version, but at the end of 1985, 45 popular American singers recorded the chorus version. This charity album for rescuing African famine victims eventually raised over 50 million US dollars in donations, making many politicians who talk the talk but don't walk the walk feel ashamed.

In 1999, "We Are the World" became popular again when Pavarotti called on Ricky Martin, Mariah Carey, Boyzone, and other superstars in the music industry to sing for children in Guatemala and Kosovo, with blues master B.B. King and rock veteran Joe Cocker also joining, making the performance lineup even more star-studded.

"Hand in Hand"

Performers: Leehom Wang, Taso Tao, and Various Artists

Popularity: ★★★★

Review: Due to SARS, two talented musicians, Leehom Wang and Taso Tao, finally had the opportunity to collaborate. Whether from the lyrics or melody, "Hand in Hand" is a rare gem among domestic disaster relief songs. It's said that the two spent half an hour in the recording studio to complete the entire song's composition, clearly demonstrating the power of their collaboration.

The entire song invited nearly 30 singers, including Xintuo Band, Jeff Chang, Dick Cowboy, Elva萧亚轩, Stefanie Sun, Coco Lee, Power Station, etc., to join in the chorus. All proceeds from the single's release were donated to the anti-SARS charity organizations at the time.

"Tomorrow Will Be Better"

Performers: Lo Ta-yu and Various Artists

Popularity: ★★★★

Review: In 1984, Ethiopia experienced a large-scale famine. To help the victims and advocate for world peace, Lo Ta-yu created this song "Tomorrow Will Be Better."

The recording gathered almost all the famous record companies of the Mandarin music industry at the time, including Rock Records, PolyGram, and Fugue, with participating singers including Teresa Teng, Su Rui, Sandy Lam, Chyi Yu, Chyi Chin, and Fei Yu-ching, totaling 60 people. After the album was sold, it raised approximately 6 million New Taiwan dollars, all used for public welfare purposes.

"Live For Love United"

Performers: Zinedine Zidane, Francesco Totti, Juan Sebastian Veron, and other football stars

Popularity: ★★★

Review: To assist AIDS patients, during the 2002 FIFA World Cup, these big-name football players sang the anti-AIDS theme song "Live For Love United," which was even more popular than that year's World Cup theme song. However, the football players may excel at playing soccer, but singing is more amateurish. It's said that the audio engineer spent an entire week on post-production tuning.
