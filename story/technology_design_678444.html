Ling Tian Legend: the state encourages media groups to run newspapers abroad and supports cooperation with large e-commerce platforms

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-02-27 14:26:49

By Cao Hong, Early Edition Reporter - Under the backdrop of a large-scale cultural system reform, the State Administration of Press, Publication, Radio, Film and Television (SAPPRFT) has issued new regulations to push for deeper reforms within publishing media groups.

Xinhua News Agency reported yesterday that SAPPRFT had recently released "Guidelines on Accelerating the Reform and Development of Publishing Media Groups" (hereinafter referred to as the "Guidelines"), which consists of 32 articles in eight sections. The Guidelines clearly state that publishing media groups are encouraged to merge with or restructure central and local publishing enterprises with similar businesses and resources to achieve cross-regional development. They are also encouraged to merge with or restructure other state-owned enterprises outside the news publishing sector to achieve cross-industry development.

A senior publishing industry insider told the Early Edition yesterday that this time around, the administration is likely encouraging mergers and acquisitions within the scope of the "capital market." As the transformation of publishing and media enterprises into enterprises continues, many publishing and media enterprises have been recognized as market entities, meaning there will be more possibilities for buying and selling in the capital market.

Additionally, the Guidelines have, for the first time, explicitly proposed "encouraging and supporting news publishing units that have completed their enterprise transformation to voluntarily join various publishing media groups." The aforementioned publishing person said this is akin to loosening restrictions on the industry, encouraging mergers and acquisitions in the publishing media market through the capital market. Voluntary joining implies that cross-media operations in the news publishing sector are being encouraged.

Regarding how to carry out mergers and acquisitions, the Guidelines further specify the targets, encouraging publishing media groups to integrate newspapers, journals, books, audio-visual products, electronic publications, digital publishing businesses, as well as publishing, printing replication, and distribution resources to achieve multimedia and full industrial chain development. They support publishing media groups in achieving cross-regional operations by setting up branches in different locations, engaging in chain operations, and cooperating with similar enterprises on products, projects, and capital. They also support publishing media groups in collaborating with large enterprises in the broadcasting, television, and telecommunications industries.

Moreover, the Guidelines mention encouraging publishing media groups to develop new forms of publishing represented by online publishing, mobile publishing, and cloud publishing; supporting publishing media groups and large e-commerce enterprises in strategic cooperation and resource integration to build a modern publishing logistics system combining online and offline circulation.

However, a securities analyst told the Early Edition yesterday that the policy is "encouraged," not mandatory, and no specific measures on how to "encourage" have been provided, thus making it less operational.

Allowing control or participation in non-public enterprises

The Guidelines mention that by the end of the "12th Five-Year Plan," the national-level humanistic, educational, and scientific and technological publishing media groups will be further strengthened and optimized, cultivating several large backbone publishing media groups with annual sales exceeding 20 billion yuan. They will promote cross-regional mergers and acquisitions of Xinhua Bookstores and the establishment of nationwide large state-owned distribution groups.

According to data from the SAPPRFT website in February, so far, China has established more than 120 news publishing enterprise groups including newspapers, publishing, distribution, and printing. Among them, 48 enterprise groups involve news publishing business and have gone public. In 2010, these more than 120 groups owned total assets of 323.42 billion yuan and achieved main business revenue of 178.58 billion yuan.

In the path to strengthening publishing media groups, there is also a signal of further opening up to non-public cultural enterprises.

The Guidelines point out that it is necessary to guide and standardize the product cooperation, project cooperation, and capital cooperation between state-owned publishing media groups and non-public cultural enterprises; allow state-owned publishing media groups to introduce qualified non-public cultural enterprises as one of their departments to participate in publishing activities; allow publishing media groups to hold or participate in non-public cultural enterprises with good growth potential to achieve cross-ownership development; support publishing media groups to achieve overall listing of their main business; recommend high-tech R&D publishing media enterprises to list on the Growth Enterprise Market.
