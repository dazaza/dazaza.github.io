**Hong Kong Company Tax Filing**

Every year, Hong Kong Limited Companies are required to file taxes with the Inland Revenue Department (IRD). This responsibility lies with the directors of the company. If a company fails to submit an auditor's report, tax return form, and employees' salaries declaration within the specified time, the IRD will conduct a tax assessment on the company. To avoid paying unnecessary taxes, directors should proactively handle the company's accounts and submit them to the auditor for review as soon as possible. The first audit deadline is within 18 months of the company's establishment (generally, the cut-off date is set as March 31 or December 31, and about 80% of Hong Kong companies choose March 31 as the closing date for their accounts). When the company receives relevant letters or tax forms from the IRD, it should promptly arrange for the auditor to conduct the audit.

**Hong Kong Company Tax Filing can be divided into two types:**

1. **DORMANT A/C (Inactive Business)** - Applicable if all the following conditions are met:

- No purchase of any property or assets;

- No bank transaction records (bank statements);

- No business operations.

2. **AUDITOR'S REPORT (Auditor's Report)**

According to the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance, every Hong Kong limited company must submit an auditor's report annually, detailing all business activities conducted during the year. Regardless of whether the company has made a profit or not, a report must be submitted.

Since Hong Kong adopts the principle of territorial taxation, if the company's profits were not generated in Hong Kong, the IRD does not have the authority to require the company to pay profits tax. In such cases, the company can apply to the Hong Kong government for overseas profits exemption. After approval, the corresponding tax can be exempted. However, an appropriate auditor's report must still be prepared.

Why does the idea of "no need for an auditor's report" spread among many companies? Because an auditor's report cannot be prepared by just anyone. Only licensed Certified Public Accountants (CPAs) are qualified to issue auditor's reports. Many commercial consulting companies in mainland China do not have qualified CPAs to prepare these reports. Therefore, to avoid trouble and doubts, they claim that "there is no need for an auditor's report" or "only an inactive business auditor's report is needed." Such behavior could result in significant legal risks for clients. If you have any questions, you can contact the Hong Kong IRD for clarification. Their telephone number is: 00852-1878088, or visit their website at http://sc.info.gov.hk/.

Of course, some customers who consistently file inactive business auditor's reports and do not conduct business in Hong Kong may find it difficult for the IRD to take legal action against them. However, if these customers travel to Hong Kong, they may receive a court summons or even be detained. These experiences can be very painful.

According to Hong Kong law, a limited company must audit its financial statements annually and prepare at least one auditor's report each year. The number of times the auditor's report is submitted to the IRD is not fixed. Generally, the IRD requires limited companies to submit at least once a year, but there are also cases where it is submitted every six months or every two years, depending on different circumstances. However, in most cases, it is submitted once a year.

When a Hong Kong limited company receives a tax return form from the IRD, it is legally required to submit an auditor's report within one month. Otherwise, if the submission is delayed without applying for an extension, the company may face fines from the government. In severe cases, the directors may also bear administrative responsibilities.
