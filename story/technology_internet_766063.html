Back-to-school season gives Windows 8 a big boost in market share? It's not that simple.

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:42:53

As the school season in September approaches, in August before the start of the school year, both domestically and internationally, there will be a significant increase in the sales of computers, especially laptops. It's easy to associate this period as a golden time for the market share growth of the Windows 8 operating system.

The facts somewhat align with these thoughts. Meanwhile, the old Windows XP system continues to lose ground, while Windows 7 remains strong, further expanding its market share amidst this ebb and flow.

According to data from the market research company Net Applications, in August this year, the market share of Windows 8 increased by 2.01% within one month, growing from 5.4% in July to 7.41%. On the other hand, Windows 7 regained 1.14% of its lost territory, rising from a market share of 44.49% to 45.63%, still firmly holding the top position.

From an overall perspective, the market share of the Windows system in computer operating systems has declined. From July to August, the market share of Windows dropped by 0.37%, falling from 91.56% to 91.19%. In contrast, Apple's OS X slightly increased by 0.09%, reaching a market share of 7.28%.

Although there are small changes in the market share of operating systems each month, the fluctuations in August were somewhat excessive. A one or two percent change may seem small, but considering that there are approximately 1.5 billion computers worldwide, a 1% change means that 15 million computers globally have either replaced or newly installed their operating systems. Therefore, the 2.01% gain in market share for Windows 8 in August implies that over 30 million active devices installed Windows 8 in just one month.

Another unreasonable point is that Windows XP has been experiencing a steady decline, but in August, its market share plummeted sharply from 37.19% to 33.66%.

According to IDC data, it is estimated that the total PC shipments this year will be around 140 million units, so it is difficult to explain the surge in the market share of Windows 8 solely based on the purchasing spree during the back-to-school season suspected by TNW.

Net Applications collects its data monthly by monitoring 160 million unique visitors across about 40,000 websites to reach these conclusions. According to ZDnet, the significant fluctuations in the market shares of Windows operating system versions in August might be due to a change in Net Applications' algorithm.

In July this year, a document titled "Major Algorithm Change" was added to the company’s website, but without specifying when it would take effect. The new algorithm aims to measure the real behavior of PC users rather than background activities executed by browser code, hence hidden pages were removed from the statistics.

The proportion of hidden pages in Firefox, Opera, and Chrome browsers is much higher than in IE, and the latest version of IE does not support the older Windows XP, which could explain why the market share of Windows XP plummeted after the algorithm change.

Another strange phenomenon is that according to Net Application's data from June this year, the proportions of hidden pages in Firefox and Opera were 6.38% and 6.33%, respectively, higher than Chrome's 5.60%. However, in August, the usage rates of Firefox and Opera increased while Chrome's decreased, a change that contradicts the logical results of adopting the new algorithm.

Thus, setting aside the statistical data from Net Applications, let’s look at StatCounter's data: the market share of Windows 8 rose from 6.61% in July to 7.04% in August. This gradual change might reflect the true situation.
