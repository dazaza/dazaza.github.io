Attention! Eight times when you should avoid taking a shower the most.

by xiaoxiao67 on 2011-12-22 16:59:59

Taking a shower is something we do every day, but ladies, have you paid attention to whether your showering method is correct? Many people believe that taking cold showers is beneficial to health, and many female friends have joined this trend, sticking to it all year round. However, what they don't know is that taking cold showers can make women more susceptible to certain gynecological diseases. An incorrect showering method can bring unexpected troubles to women.

1. It's not advisable to take a shower right before sleeping.

Many people are accustomed to taking a hot shower and then going to bed immediately, which is actually an incorrect habit. It’s better to bathe two hours before sleeping. Sleep often comes when the body temperature drops, and a hot shower will raise the body temperature, delaying the brain's release of "sleep hormones." If one can only shower before sleeping on a certain day, they can use a wet towel to cold compress the forehead for five minutes after bathing, allowing the body temperature to return to normal levels and fall asleep faster.

Showering after waking up to wake up

In the standards of sleep quality, an internationally recognized indicator is "morning feeling". If one feels energetic, even with a short sleep duration, it is considered good sleep quality. After getting up in the morning, a slightly cooler shower can invigorate us, improving satisfaction with nighttime sleep.

2. It's not advisable to take a shower after drinking alcohol.

Alcohol inhibits liver function and hinders the release of glycogen. When showering, the body consumes more glucose. Taking a shower after drinking alcohol can prevent timely replenishment of blood sugar, easily causing dizziness, blurred vision, and general weakness, and in severe cases, even hypoglycemic coma.

Additionally, because the body sweats during a shower, the alcohol concentration in the blood increases. Hot water promotes blood circulation, expands blood vessels, and accelerates the pulse rate, often leading to a drop in blood pressure and increased blood viscosity, making the body difficult to adapt, possibly triggering heart disease or stroke.

Supplementary Reading: It's also not good to wash your hair immediately after drinking.

If you wash your hair not long after drinking, it may cause abnormal cranial vessel function, resulting in symptoms such as dizziness, blackouts, and vomiting. Especially elderly people with cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases should avoid using overly cold water to wash their hair, and pay more attention after drinking.

3. It's not advisable to take a shower after a full meal.

After a full meal, the skin surface vessels expand due to the stimulation of hot water, increasing blood flow to the surface and relatively reducing the blood supply to the abdomen, affecting digestion and absorption, and causing hypoglycemia, or even fainting.

Supplementary Reading:

It's also not good to shower when hungry.

When one is hungry, the blood sugar level is at its lowest, unable to ensure the heat consumption required for showering. Therefore, showering when hungry can easily lead to symptoms such as dizziness and fainting.

It's better to shower one to two hours after a meal.

It's best to shower one to two hours after a meal. One can choose the shower time according to their physical condition. For example, frail elderly people can choose to shower at noon when the yang energy is strongest, achieving complementary effects and preventing illness.

4. It's not advisable to take a shower when running a fever.

When a person's body temperature rises to 38°C, the body's heat consumption can increase by 20%, making the body relatively weak, and showering at this time can easily lead to accidents.

Additionally, even without a high fever, it's best not to shower during the acute phase of an illness. Patients with severe heart disease, severe anemia, uremia, hypertension with blood pressure reaching above 180 mmHg, myocardial infarction, acute nephritis, acute hepatitis, trauma, etc., should not shower.

Patients with cerebrovascular diseases should not bathe frequently. The thermal stimulation produced during bathing can accelerate blood flow within the patient's body, thereby increasing the pressure of the blood on the blood vessels. When the blood flows through certain locally diseased areas, it is easy for the blood vessels to rupture.

Epileptic patients should not bathe frequently. During bathing, a large amount of water vapor is inhaled into the body, causing the oxygen content in the body to decrease. Additionally, the sweat excreted by the body increases, disrupting the electrolyte balance and easily causing temporary functional disorders, inducing epilepsy.

5. It's not advisable to take a shower immediately after labor.

Whether after physical labor or mental labor, one should rest for a while before taking a shower; otherwise, it can easily cause insufficient blood supply to the heart and brain, and even cause fainting.

During bathing, you can combine massage, such as back rubbing. The best tool for back rubbing is silk gourd network, which itself is a kind of Chinese medicine and has the effect of promoting blood circulation and unblocking meridians.

There is also sea floatstone in Chinese medicine, which is like a coarse sandwheel and has the effect of removing phlegm. Especially for the elderly, on the one hand, it is relatively hard and can scrub off thick skin on the heels, and on the other hand, it removes phlegm. These natural bathing tools are better than today's synthetic fiber bath towels.

6. It's not advisable to take a shower when blood pressure is too low.

Because the water temperature is higher during bathing, it can cause vasodilation, and people with low blood pressure are prone to cerebral ischemia, leading to fainting.

Supplementary Reading: Be cautious when soaking feet if you have low blood pressure.

Many people are used to soaking their feet in hot water or soaking in hot springs. Experts remind that people with heart disease and low blood pressure should not soak their feet or soak in hot springs at too high temperatures, otherwise, it may cause discomfort or even danger of fainting.

Healthy people have no problem soaking their feet or soaking in hot springs, but special groups need to be careful. For example, patients with heart disease, heart failure, low blood pressure, frequent dizziness, should not soak their feet in too hot water or soak in hot springs for a long time.

Because soaking feet or soaking in hot springs will cause vasodilation in the human body, and the blood from important organs will flow to the surface of the body, which will inevitably cause ischemia and hypoxia in important organs such as the heart and brain, increasing the risk of illness for people with heart disease and low blood pressure.

7. Don't shower within one hour before or after a massage.

Massage can eliminate fatigue, relieve tension, and also promote blood circulation and refresh the spirit. But many people who often get massages overlook a question: should they shower before or after the massage?

People who prefer to shower before the massage think that they can rest immediately after the massage, which is convenient and simple; those who shower immediately after the massage think it is safer and more hygienic. In fact, neither of these practices is recommended, because whether you are doing an oil massage or an aromatherapy SPA, whether the masseur uses tonifying or purging techniques, you should not shower within one hour before or after the massage.

When people shower, hot water stimulates the skin and accelerates the blood circulation on the body surface, causing congestion. Massage is a kind of "work" acting on the body surface, which can also cause skin congestion and accelerate blood circulation. Therefore, if you take a shower and then immediately get a massage, the double effect of showering and massage will cause relative oxygen deficiency in the stomach and brain, easily leading to fainting or collapse.

Similarly, during a massage, the blood and qi temporarily fill the body surface, causing relative ischemia and hypoxia in various internal organs, leading to discomforts such as dizziness and nausea, or even fainting. Moreover, during a massage, the blood and qi gather on the body surface, opening the pores, and taking off clothes to shower immediately can easily cause a chill, leading to catching a cold. Therefore, after a massage, one should rest in a warm and comfortable environment (for 1-2 hours) to let the body return to its normal state before engaging in other activities.

8. Do not shower immediately after cupping.

In addition to massage, you should not shower immediately after cupping, otherwise, it can harm the skin and even cause secondary chilling.

After cupping, the skin is in a damaged state, being sensitive and fragile. Showering at this time can easily lead to skin damage and inflammation.

If the water temperature is too low during the shower, it will cause the skin with open pores to catch a chill. Therefore, you must not shower immediately after cupping to avoid inviting illness.
