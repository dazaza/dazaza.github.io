The Medicinal Value of Burdock Tea

Burdock tea, made from the natural Chinese herbal ingredient burdock root, is a purely natural tea product. The "Compendium of Materia Medica" details that burdock has a warm nature, a sweet taste, and is non-toxic; it can unblock the twelve meridians, eliminate evil qi from the five zang organs, and long-term consumption leads to a lighter body and resistance against aging. Burdock tea possesses the characteristic of simultaneously eliminating toxins from the human body while nourishing and regulating with its nutritional components, aligning with the traditional Chinese medical practice of "for every elimination, there must be a replenishment." Additionally, burdock tea contains large amounts of arctiin and lignans, among which arctigenin is an anti-cancer active substance. Burdock tea has a golden hue and a pleasant aroma, making it a health drink suitable for all ages throughout the year. Burdock tea can also be prepared as a dish, using methods such as stewing, boiling, hotpot, or making soups, to be consumed with meals.

Medicinal Value

The "Compendium of Materia Medica" details that burdock has a warm nature, a sweet taste, and is non-toxic; it can unblock the twelve meridians, eliminate evil qi from the five zang organs, and long-term consumption leads to a lighter body and resistance against aging. It has functions of clearing heat, detoxifying, removing dampness, strengthening the spleen, invigorating the stomach, promoting bowel movements, nourishing yin, tonifying the kidneys, benefiting qi, lowering blood pressure, and preventing strokes. Nutritionists believe that Dongyang Shen S (burdock) is both a medicinal herb and a vegetable, with very high nutritional value. In 100 grams of fresh Dongyang Shen, it contains 87.00 grams of water, 0.80 grams of protein, 3.00 grams of carbohydrates, 38.00 kilocalories of energy, 2.40 grams of crude fiber, 2.40 grams of ash, 242.00 mg of calcium, 61.00 mg of phosphorus, 7.60 mg of iron, 390.00 mg of carotene, 0.02 mg of vitamin B1, 0.29 mg of vitamin B2, 25.00 mg of vitamin C, 1.10 mg of niacin, as well as zinc, magnesium, and copper components. Its carotene content is 287 times that of carrots, and its calcium content is the highest among root and stem vegetables. Excerpted from: "Vegetable Cultivation Science" edited by the Vegetable Research Institute of the Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences, "Compendium of Materia Medica" (Herb Section, Volume Fifteen, Evil Realities), "The Anti-Aging Bible" and "The Herbal Health Bible" written by renowned American health expert Dr. Earl Mindell.

In the notification issued by the National Health Department in March 2002 titled "Further Standardization of the Health Product Market," it clearly stipulates that burdock seeds and roots are two of the 82 ingredients approved by the state for use in health products. China's "Modern Chinese Pharmacopoeia," "Chinese Pharmacopoeia," and other authoritative national pharmacopeias summarize the pharmacological effects of burdock into three aspects: first, it promotes growth; second, it contains substances that inhibit tumor growth; third, it has antibacterial and antifungal properties. The main function of burdock root is to disperse wind-heat, detoxify, and reduce swelling, used for wind-heat colds, coughs, sore throats, constipation, dizziness caused by wind-fire rising, tinnitus and deafness, blurred vision, externally treats red and swollen head and face due to wind poison, toothache caused by heat poison, gum pain, carbuncles, and malignant sores. In fact, burdock root, as a Chinese herb, is detailed in modern medical literature such as the "Compendium of Materia Medica," "Famous Physicians' Biographies," "Pharmacological Properties," "Newly Revised Materia Medica," "Supplement to the Compendium of Materia Medica," "Classified Herbal Properties," and "China Herbal Cuisine Encyclopedia."
