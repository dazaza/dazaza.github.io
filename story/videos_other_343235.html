Side A features "Love life", Pet Shop Boys’ previously unreleased 2001 recording of a song which became a hit for the Swedish group Alcazar. Side B has "A powerful friend", recorded in 2002 and never released before, featuring Release tour guitarists Bic Hayes and Mark Refoy. (A proof of the single artwork is reproduced here.) The record is one of a series of limited seven-inch vinyl records Parlophone is releasing to support this year’s UK Record Store Day. They include The Beatles’ "Paperback Writer"/"Rain" and Tinie Tempah’s number one single "Pass Out". Each release is limited to 1,000 copies and exclusive to the independent record shops taking part in the event. Record stores in Belgium, Denmark, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Sweden, Portugal, the Netherlands, Ireland, the USA and Brazil are also participating. Neil Tennant comments: "Independent record stores play a vital role in selling music from the fringe as well as the mainstream. We’re delighted to be releasing a one-off vinyl single only available in independent shops as part of Record Store Day."
