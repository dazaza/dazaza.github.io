Several Points to Note for Enterprise SEO in Building Good Internal Links

by qiyeseo5774 on 2012-03-02 20:14:20

Nowadays, the optimization of a website basically consists of several parts: external links + internal links + quality content + promotion. Internal links, as an important factor, play a significant role. Many webmasters are troubled by the layout of internal links and do not know how to proceed. Some webmasters also fall into many pitfalls in internal link layouts, adding internal links recklessly for keyword rankings or even adding numerous keyword backlinks at the bottom of the website. This is very harmful to our site. Today, I will share some steps for optimizing internal link layouts, hoping to help webmasters who are confused.

1. The Idea Behind Internal Link Layout

Before doing internal link layouts, we need to correct our approach. If we don't aim to enhance user experience when creating internal links, that's wrong. We would inevitably use cheating methods. On the contrary, if we focus on helping users find content or improving page conversion rates and fulfilling user needs while optimizing internal link layouts, our ultimate achievement will be keyword rankings.

2. Internal Link Operations

- Control the number of links: We know that page and site authority are passed through links, and anchor text links are one of the voting factors for keyword rankings. Based on this principle, many webmasters believe that placing many links on the homepage helps with indexing and ranking. While this is true for high-authority large sites, it can lead to slow indexing for low-authority new or small sites.

- In-text link operations: Not every article requires in-text links. Those that do include technical articles and humanities/history articles, which contain technical terms that we should link to explain to users. For reference, see Google Webmaster Help; its in-text internal links are well done. News articles, however, do not require in-text internal links, as forcing keywords can be dangerous.

- List link operations: First, control the quantity; recommended lists should ideally be limited to 10 items. When recommending, prioritize primary needs first, then secondary needs. For example, if selling headphones, recommend four products—three headphones and one earphone organizer. For the earphone organizer, recommend the best variety that is cost-effective. Meeting primary needs involves recommending the most relevant item, while satisfying secondary needs involves recommending the best and most cost-effective option.

3. Common Pitfalls in Internal Links

- Avoid backlink issues: Do not create keyword backlinks at the bottom or top of your site, as such internal links are meaningless.

- Breadcrumb paths should not have intermediate (hidden) layers. For instance, the normal path might be Longfeng Crane > Crane Technology > Diesel Engine Repair, but an abnormal path would be Longfeng Crane > Crane Technology > Crane Repair > Diesel Engine Repair. If you can directly access the Diesel Engine Repair article from the Crane Technology page, then Crane Repair is considered a hidden layer.

- Control the number of links on the homepage, especially for new sites where product categories should not be too fine-grained.
