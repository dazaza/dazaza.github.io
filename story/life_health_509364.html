Health certificate only required to run a small workshop - an urgent need to address the vacuum in food safety management - Hexun News Channel

Beijing, October 15 (reporter Wenjia Ma) According to the report by China National Radio News, what permits are required for operating a small workshop that both produces and sells its own products, such as a bakery? This question has been troubling Ms. Liu (a pseudonym), the owner of a cake shop in Jiyuan, Henan Province. She reported through the news hotline of China National Radio that the health bureau no longer issues health permits, while the local administration for industry and commerce stated that having a health certificate would suffice for obtaining a business license.

Why have health permits, which were previously approved and issued by the health department, stopped being processed? Is this phenomenon occurring in other places as well? Who should be responsible for managing small food workshops like bakeries and cake shops?

Ms. Liu from Jiyuan, Henan, is a cake shop owner who, as an insider, shared her confusion about renewing her business license.

In May this year, her health permit expired, so she went to the health bureau to apply for a new one. However, the local health bureau informed her that her cake shop was no longer under their jurisdiction. This left Ms. Liu very puzzled. In the past, the health bureau had strict regulations on them, requiring inspections of the store’s hygiene conditions and facilities before issuing the health permit, and conducting regular checks afterward. A few months later, she learned from the industrial and commercial department that now all that was needed to open a cake shop was a health certificate to obtain a business license. Ms. Liu pointed out that not only her cake shop but also nearby noodle shops, steamed bun shops, and similar small food workshops followed this procedure for obtaining a business license. This made her somewhat concerned: if there was no supervision in the future, would the food produced still be safe? To verify this, the reporter contacted both the Jiyuan City Administration for Industry and Commerce and the Health Bureau. Both sides confirmed that it was indeed no longer necessary to apply for a health permit when obtaining a business license.

The reason cake shop operators no longer need to apply for a health permit lies in the Food Safety Law, enacted in June 2009. This law stipulated that the original health permits issued by the health department have now been replaced by three other licenses: food producers must obtain a food production license from the quality and technical supervision department; those engaged in food sales must get a food circulation license from the industrial and commercial department; and those providing catering services must acquire a catering service license from the food and drug supervision department. Therefore, according to the Food Safety Law, the health bureau indeed no longer issues health permits.

However, a new problem arises: small workshops that engage in both food production and sales have become "orphans." Ms. Liu's confusion stems from this issue. In fact, Article 29 of the Food Safety Law states that specific methods for supervising and managing small workshops should be formulated by the standing committees of the people's congresses of provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the central government. However, according to the reporter's investigation, not all provinces have yet issued relevant regulations or detailed rules. As a result, many places across the country have encountered management vacuums regarding small workshops.
