Car decoders are expected to be included in the special management

by anonymous on 2012-03-06 14:18:44

In the deep night, a brand-new Mercedes-Benz car drove into the parking lot of a high-end community. Several thieves lurking around the parking lot suddenly became alert. One of them skillfully took out a black electronic device. A string of digital "0" was displayed on the red electronic screen. The car owner got off the Mercedes-Benz, locked the door by pressing the lock button on the remote key, and walked straight towards the community. At this moment, six digits suddenly jumped out on the screen of the electronic device held by the thief. His accomplice inputted the data into another electronic device - a car decoder, car jammer, car unlocking tool, car remote decoder. Under the cover of the night, they approached the Mercedes-Benz. About 2 minutes later, the car door password was cracked. The thief opened the car door, started the engine, and drove away in a cloud of dust...

Currently, in many car theft cases that have been solved across the country, criminals often use the originally intended vehicle tuning and inspection electronic control system inspection equipment (commonly known as "decoder") to steal the car door password, thereby implementing car theft activities. Recently, industry insiders have called for relevant departments to include the production, sales, use, and training of decoders within the scope of special industry management to combat increasingly rampant car theft.

Repair tools become theft tools

With the increasing number of vehicle electronic control systems, the use of car decoders has become more common in car repairs. According to Jiang Yanshan, a senior technician at Beijing Benyu Fu Automobile Repair Shop, although fuel-injected cars have dedicated data interfaces, they cannot be directly connected to the detection computer. They must go through the decoder for translation to achieve smooth "conversation" between the two, thereby checking whether there are any fault codes in the driving computer, whether the data is normal, and viewing the working status of various sensors distributed throughout the car. In addition, after replacing some electronic control equipment on the vehicle, it is also necessary to use the decoder to check whether these devices match the whole car.

"Decoders produced by regular manufacturers can only be used for car inspections and are not theft tools," said Jiang Yanshan. Car decoders do not originally possess the function of stealing car door anti-theft passwords. However, some illegal enterprises modify the decoders produced by regular manufacturers, giving them this function. Some auto repair shops help car owners reconfigure their car door keys after losing their anti-theft passwords, precisely using illegal decoders. Once this method is mastered by criminals, it can easily lead to car theft.

Jiang Yanshan told reporters that although regular car repair shops generally would not buy illegal decoders, due to the existence of some unscrupulous auto repair shops, the market demand for illegal decoders is still relatively strong. He believes that since buying illegal decoders is not difficult and the operation process is not complicated, it is not surprising that decoders have become criminal tools.

Industry calls for decoders to be included in special industry management

When I entered the keyword "decoder" in Baidu, I found that there were numerous merchants selling car decoders online. On many web pages, various styles and models of products were accompanied by pictures, with prices ranging from several thousand yuan to tens of thousands of yuan. Customers just need to select the product and can purchase it via mail order. Merchants do not verify the identity or purpose of the customers. Ironically, one merchant specifically noted on the webpage: "If the customer uses the product for illegal behavior, the merchant assumes no responsibility."

It is understood that according to Chinese law, industries such as lock opening, hotels, and pawnshops have been included in the national special industry management category. Relevant operators must register with the local public security organ's special industry management department and accept supervision from relevant departments. However, since car decoders are currently a new thing, their production, sales, use, and training have not yet been included in the scope of special industry management. Combined with inadequate self-discipline within the industry, crimes using decoders for car theft have become increasingly rampant.

A person who has long worked in the industrial and commercial department told reporters that as a specialized tool for the automotive repair industry, the car "decoder" belongs to a common commodity circulating in the market. Simply purchasing a "decoder" does not violate relevant management regulations. If lawbreakers buy a regular "decoder" and then illegally modify it to make it a theft tool and conduct transactions in the circulation field, the industrial and commercial department has the authority to investigate. However, since criminals usually conduct cross-regional transactions through the network, it is difficult for the industrial and commercial department to discover them in time alone.

Industry insiders believe that the state should quickly introduce regulations specifying that only regular car repair units can purchase "decoders", which can only be used for car repairs. If it is necessary to use the "decoder" to unlock, procedures must be handled at designated departments. In addition, the threshold for units engaged in "decoder" training business should be raised, and a complete student file system should be established.

The police remind car owners to enhance their awareness of theft prevention. Statistics show that the risk of theft for cars less than two years old and older models with poor anti-theft capabilities is significantly higher than other models. The police remind car owners to enhance their awareness of theft prevention, try to choose better quality anti-theft tools, do not leave valuable items in the car, and park the car in places with someone watching or surveillance whenever possible. In addition, after locking the car door with the remote key, manually check whether the car door and trunk are closed before leaving.

A car owner told reporters that thieves who are accustomed to stealing cars through high-tech means usually do not use violent theft methods. Therefore, car owners can consider adding a steering wheel lock or gear stick lock to their car to prevent the success of the thief. "Although the method is primitive, it is often more effective," he said.
