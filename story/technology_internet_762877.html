Taobao Draws a National 'Insomnia Map': Shanghai has the most sleepless people

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:48:26

"Every day, I go to bed around 11 o'clock, then watch videos and browse online stores on my phone. I don't feel sleepy until around 1 o'clock," complained Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker who works in a commercial building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou. In fact, more and more people are beginning to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep are all similar, but those who can't sleep have their own reasons," the Taobao shopkeepers asked humorously, "Dear, why can't you sleep?" They took the opportunity to sell "sleep aid" products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. The group of people who can't sleep has very obvious regional characteristics. In the top five cities with the highest per capita purchase of two sleep aids, eye masks and earplugs, over the past five months, all four first-tier cities—Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen—are on the list collectively. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city among the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people surf online stores at midnight

"At this point, is there anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such late-night insomnia-related Weibo posts have become increasingly common. "Insomniacs" instantly transform into "night shoppers." Lying in bed, they use their mobile Taobao app, and their passion for shopping does not diminish due to the quietness of the night; instead, late at night becomes one of the high-frequency times for mobile online shopping.

"In such hot weather, I definitely won't go out shopping during the day. When I can't sleep at night, I use that time to shop," said Miss Li from Hangzhou, a typical night owl. She said she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her mobile Taobao app to browse. "Yesterday, I bought two pairs of sandals and one dress within an hour." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she fell asleep. However, her money flowed out daily.

There are many such "night shoppers." According to the data released by Taobao, in 2012, a total of 22.832 million people browsed Taobao between 11 PM and 5 AM. Among them, a significant number of men stayed up late browsing Taobao, accounting for 48% of the total, while women accounted for 52%.

The most insomniac provinces:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What to do if you really can't sleep? An increasing number of people are starting to rely on external tools like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Clever online store owners have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. A search for earplugs on Taobao revealed that there were over 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks alone includes various types such as 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, and bamboo charcoal eye masks. Since the start of summer, ice pack eye masks have been highly popular. On one Taobao store, the monthly transaction volume of a single item exceeded ten thousand pieces.

Some sellers have also made great efforts in designing pillows, offering lavender pillows and buckwheat pillows for calming the mind, and even foods like millet and jujubes have been labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to the transaction data from Taobao over the last five months, from the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks out of the total city population, all the first-tier cities—Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen—are on the list of the top five. Hangzhou, which ranks third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai can be considered the city with the most severe insomnia. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep quality.

"Can't sleep at night, can't get up in the morning" forms a vicious cycle. The cities where people have the hardest time sleeping at night are also the ones where people find it hardest to get up in the morning. In the last five months, the top five cities with the largest population purchasing alarm clocks are also Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou.

"I have to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear the alarm on my phone in the morning. After setting my phone alarm to vibrate, I'm afraid it won't wake me up, so I bought another vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleep induction" and "wake-up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So, who are the people suffering from insomnia? Online store owners know best. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, with more customers consulting, and the age range is getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of the total sales," said an online store owner selling earplugs.

Indeed, the store's signature product—a 19-yuan pair of earplugs—sold 19,000 units in just one month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It is understood that students who buy eye masks and earplugs may live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are very popular among students, and after bedtime, some classmates may still be playing games, so to avoid being disturbed, they might as well "seal themselves off."

White-collar workers, on the other hand, experience greater work pressure during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. Some buyers even amusingly stated that they bought earplugs because "my husband snores too loudly."

Taobao data shows that most buyers of earplugs and eye masks are from the post-90s and post-80s generations. For example, in the last five months of eye mask sales, the post-90s generation is the largest demand group, with 36% of eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, the 25-29 age group accounts for more than 30% of the purchases.

From the purchase ratio of various products, men are more likely to suffer from insomnia than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than females.
