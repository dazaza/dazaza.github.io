Champions League draw conspiracy comes to light: Platini fooled the whole world

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-20 10:30:03

The shocking conspiracy behind the UEFA Champions League draw has come to light - Platini fooled the entire world. "This is my UEFA," Platini declared. Kenyon retorted, "This is all your doing, Mr President." Platini remained silent.

According to Sina Sports, in last season's UEFA Champions League round of 16 draw, Manchester United faced Inter Milan, Chelsea faced Juventus, and Arsenal faced Roma; this season it changed to Manchester United facing AC Milan, and Chelsea facing Inter Milan. What changed were Manchester United and Chelsea's opponents, but what remained constant was the main theme of elimination matches between the Premier League and Serie A teams. Meanwhile, the three Spanish powerhouses enjoyed good fortune in the draw. If Real Madrid drawing Lyon could be considered an average draw, then Barcelona and Sevilla undoubtedly received excellent draws. This was not the first time Spanish teams had received good results in the draw. Last season, while the Premier League and Serie A teams were fiercely competing against each other, Barcelona, Villarreal, and Atletico Madrid's opponents were Lyon, Panathinaikos, and Porto respectively. Apart from Atletico being eliminated by Porto, both Barcelona and the Yellow Submarine advanced very easily. The only exception was Real Madrid, who were brutally defeated 5-0 by Liverpool, which somewhat tarnished the reputation of the Spanish teams.

Upon closer examination, this is the third consecutive season that Premier League and Serie A teams have clashed in the UEFA Champions League, and also the third consecutive season that Spanish teams have received favorable draws.

In the 2007-2008 season, during the UEFA Champions League round of 16 draw, Arsenal faced AC Milan, Liverpool faced Inter Milan, Manchester United faced their old rival Lyon, and only Chelsea faced the relatively weaker Olympiacos. On the contrary, the Spanish teams' draws were much better: Real Madrid faced Roma, a slightly weaker Italian team, while Sevilla and Barcelona faced Fenerbahce and Celtic respectively, almost guaranteeing their advancement to the quarterfinals. In the quarterfinal draw, UEFA made Arsenal and Liverpool fight against each other, and had Manchester United face Roma, the Italian team that had eliminated Real Madrid. But who was Barcelona's opponent? Schalke 04!

In other words, in the past three seasons of UEFA Champions League knockout stages, Premier League and Serie A teams have met six times previously, with Premier League teams always emerging victorious. This season, Chelsea vs Inter Milan and Milan vs Manchester United are the seventh and eighth encounters between Premier League and Serie A teams in the past three seasons. Surprisingly, Spanish and Serie A teams have only met once in the UEFA Champions League knockout stages in the past three seasons, and the Serie A team Roma emerged victorious. In other words, in the past three seasons, Spanish teams have never defeated Serie A teams in the UEFA Champions League knockout stages to advance. In the past three seasons, Premier League and Spanish teams have met five times in the UEFA Champions League knockout stages, with Premier League teams winning three times and Spanish teams winning twice, both victories achieved by Barcelona, who eliminated Chelsea in last season's semifinal and defeated Manchester United in the final.

Objectively speaking, it will be difficult for the Premier League to replicate the scene of the past two seasons where they encountered Serie A teams six times and eliminated all of them. It will also be difficult to repeat the glory of occupying three out of four spots in the UEFA Champions League semifinals for three consecutive seasons. After all, Liverpool has already been eliminated in the group stage, Manchester United and Arsenal did not make significant signings in the summer transfer window, resulting in no increase or even a decrease in strength; and Chelsea, after changing managers, did not receive adequate reinforcements.

After Cristiano Ronaldo left the club this summer, Manchester United now lacks a stable second scoring option. The 30.75 million pounds spent on Berbatov has proven to be a poor investment. This season, Berbatov has not scored a single goal in the UEFA Champions League, and more worryingly, even when facing weak opponents like Wolves Reserves, he has lost his desire to shoot. A forward without shooting ability cannot score goals. With Berbatov failing to perform, a hidden concern for Manchester United is that if their top scorer Wayne Rooney gets injured, their attacking power will become extremely weak. Unless Sir Alex Ferguson can sign strong reinforcements in the winter transfer market, finding such a capable striker who hasn't participated in the UEFA Champions League is not easy in today's transfer market.

Similar to Manchester United's reliance on Rooney, Chelsea heavily depends on Didier Drogba. Although Kalou and Borini work hard, objectively speaking, their abilities are far inferior to the Ivorian beast. If new signings cannot be brought in next month, it will be difficult for anyone to hold up Chelsea's frontline if Drogba gets injured. The rumored Aguero, even if he joins, will not be eligible to play for Chelsea in this season's UEFA Champions League. Of course, Chelsea's biggest advantage is Carlo Ancelotti. The renowned Italian coach excels in UEFA Champions League matches, and he has already stated that Chelsea's target this season prioritizes the UEFA Champions League over the Premier League.

Arsenal is, relatively speaking, the least reliable among these three Premier League teams and the one that experienced the biggest decline in strength this summer. Despite this, the Gunners' path to the quarterfinals is not particularly difficult. Whether they can progress further depends on the upcoming draw. Wenger's team did not face any significant challenges in this season's UEFA Champions League group stage. Their round of 16 opponent Porto, after losing their key players Lisandro Lopez and Lucho Gonzalez, is no longer as formidable. However, Arsenal's problem lies in losing key players like Adebayor and Toure this summer, followed by the loss of their top scorer Robin van Persie at the start of the season. Their strength has clearly declined. If Arsenal hopes to continue advancing in the UEFA Champions League, Wenger must act in the January transfer window.

Remember, this was just Platini's sleight of hand.
