A new borrowing model to solve troubles

by bfukaitongm on 2012-03-08 09:53:00

The development of the Internet has produced a great impact on many traditional concepts. Together with the continuous improvement of several domestic credit systems, the traditional lending model is increasingly showing its disadvantages. For some small loans, not only are the bank approval procedures cumbersome, but they also often fail to get approved. For some Internet users who have idle money but lack good investment channels, finding a platform that can "absorb deposits", lend money to others, and collect interest has become an urgent need.

On the one hand, banks do not favor "small amounts" of money, while on the other hand, Internet users with "small amounts" of money have spending needs. This has provided a market opportunity for the emergence of online lending [http://www.fydai.com], a new type of lending model. Online lending originated in Europe and America, where users can achieve direct borrowing and lending on C2C (business-to-business) financial service websites without bank intervention. In August 2009, the P2P (person-to-person) credit online lending platform Fu Yidai [http://www.fydai.com] was established in Shenzhen. The company is well-funded, and its business scope is continuously expanding. It has integrated resources from the government, enterprises, and banks, particularly increasing its services for micro-enterprises, trading companies, civil servants, and white-collar workers. So how do online lending platforms work? Compared to traditional lending methods, online lending platforms provide "one-stop" services for both lenders and borrowers, from information release and data review to transfer borrowing, interest rate calculation, and timely repayment. While providing a convenient trading platform for both parties, online lending platforms also extract a certain percentage of commission from each loan amount, similar to how stock exchanges extract commissions from each securities transaction.

If Internet users only need a small loan, why must they choose an online lending platform instead of going to a small loan company? According to pilot policies issued by the China Banking Regulatory Commission and the People's Bank of China, small loan companies can only lend money and cannot accept deposits. If you have a few thousand or a few hundred yuan in idle funds and want to entrust a small loan company platform to lend it out for you and collect interest, it is impossible. However, on online lending platforms, "absorbing deposits" becomes possible. After registering as a member on Fu Yidai and depositing 3,000 yuan, an Internet user lent out the money half a month later in increments of no less than 50 yuan per transaction. After a borrower posted their borrowing needs, investors would pool their contributions like a "joint subscription," with some lending hundreds of yuan and others lending dozens of yuan. Judging from the IP addresses, these Internet users were from all over the country.

Various borrowing needs [http://www.fydai.com] are diverse, including business capital turnover, renting, renovation, buying computers, getting married, traveling, and so on.

In traditional thinking, if you want to borrow money, "large sums" go to the bank, and "small sums" go to acquaintances. But in reality, borrowing money is a difficult thing for anyone. Borrowing from banks requires multiple approvals, takes a long time, and if the loan amount is small, the bank may be unwilling to approve it. Borrowing from friends risks "hurting feelings when talking about money," and asking family members for money can be embarrassing. Online lending makes this difficult issue easier.
