Ancelotti doesn't want to draw the Milan teams, Arsenal = weakest top team, Inter Milan = strongest second team

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-15 12:11:01

Anchelotti does not want to draw the Milan duo; Arsenal = weakest group winner, Inter Milan = strongest second-place team. According to Sina Sports, Chelsea drew 3-3 with Everton in the last round of the Premier League, and all three goals conceded seemed bizarre. Many fans at Stamford Bridge could not accept it and booed the players on the spot. Czech goalkeeper Petr Cech was partly responsible for the three conceded goals, but in an interview with Chelsea's official website, he expressed strong dissatisfaction with the booing from home fans.

"I was very surprised to hear boos from the fans. This is the first time we've drawn at home this season. Look at our previous performances in the league, Champions League, and League Cup - we have won all our home games and scored so many goals. How can the fans react like this? I don't understand," said Cech. He emphasized that the blame for conceding set-piece goals should not be solely placed on him: "We missed many first balls, but in set-pieces, it's not the responsibility of one person; it's a collective issue."

In contrast to the poor performance of Chelsea's defense, Drogba scored twice. However, as the Africa Cup approaches, The Daily Mail revisited the topic of whether Chelsea suffers from "Drogba dependency." But head coach Carlo Ancelotti refuses to admit it. The Italian insists that Chelsea will still score goals without Drogba during the Africa Cup period. "At this stage, I trust Drogba; he is very important to us, but I believe we can win even without him in January," said Ancelotti.

A report by Sportlife may bring some relief to Ancelotti: Drogba is trying to achieve "loyalty to both club and country." According to Ivory Coast's national team preparation plan, head coach Vahid Halilhodzic hopes that Drogba will return to the team two weeks before the Africa Cup starts, i.e., at the end of December. However, Drogba plans to persuade Halilhodzic to give him a few more days to help Chelsea get through the Christmas fixture congestion before returning to his country. "I will talk to the national team coach and Ancelotti to find the best solution. It's difficult to leave the club because obviously, Chelsea pays my salary, and the difficulty of leaving for a month is unimaginable. But I think the club also understands; players have no choice when representing their country because we must play for our national teams. I want to stay at Chelsea, but I have to go to the national team, and making a decision is not easy. Fortunately, the club respects us; they are very respectful towards African players, and I hope the team handles this matter well."

Regardless of how Drogba's schedule for the Africa Cup is arranged, Ancelotti's biggest wish is to see a healthy Drogba return, preferably without injury, which could delay his participation in the Champions League knockout stages. The Champions League round-of-16 draw will take place this Friday, and according to The Daily Telegraph citing La Gazzetta dello Sport, Ancelotti has openly stated, "I'd rather not face either of the Milan teams."

"In February, I don't want to face Leonardo at Stamford Bridge. If it happens, there's nothing we can do. Such things happen on the pitch; for example, AC Milan facing Kaka in the group stage. If Chelsea draws AC Milan, it would have a very special meaning. From a technical perspective, if we draw Inter Milan, the only problem is that they are very strong. I consider Inter Milan to be the strongest second-place team," Ancelotti, who knows Italian teams well, added, "It's very difficult to eliminate an Italian team."

Among the group winners, Ancelotti specifically mentioned Arsenal: "The only team lacking experience is Arsenal, a very young team. Compared to the lineup that eliminated AC Milan two years ago, Arsenal now lacks several key players. In midfield, there's only Fabregas left, and the losses of Flamini and Hleb are significant. Among the second-place teams, I believe three teams are on par with the group winners: AC Milan, Inter Milan, and Bayern Munich. The other five teams are slightly weaker."
