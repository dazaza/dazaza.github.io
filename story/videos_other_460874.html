Golf swing - increasing power swing

by hacnno66 on 2011-07-12 10:14:58

Flexibility is the original exercise key to improving the ability of the golf swing. If the golfer is incapable of developing a full backswing with a torso rotation of 90 degrees and the ability to get the golf club parallel to the ground at the top of the swing, then flexibility will be a key factor in generating more power in the golf swing.

Increasing the speed of your golf swing requires some patience. The golfer first needs to address golf swing issues. If a golfer has an inefficient swing and wants to improve golf wrap technology, then targeted practice is necessary because increasing swing speed through exercise training alone won't necessarily increase distance.

When you examine many of the routines that PGA tour players are using these days, many of them focus on building core strength through the use of medicine balls, balance training, and resistance bands. However, additional abdominal exercises and other workouts will also do a good job of developing your core muscles.

If the golfer has the ability to achieve a full backswing but is still not generating sufficient club speed for better control of the golf ball head, then strength training should become the focus. Strength training should start with the legs since they are the foundation of the golf swing. Poor balance as a result of weak legs will often hold a golfer back.

Once we set the golfer on the right path with the golf swing, we can address some of the physical factors that may be holding back the swing speed. Keep in mind, in some cases, it may be a physical limitation that makes accelerating the swing more difficult. That's when a trainer with experience in exercise training may be valuable.

After addressing leg strength, the next most important area is the core. In fact, core strength is probably just as important as leg strength when it comes to increasing swing speed. I have a friend who is a long drive champion and now trains potential long drive champions, and he mentions that "I am all about the core." And if you watch him work out, you know that he truly believes that. Incredibly, he is capable of 300-yard drives off his knees, and that is only possible with amazing core strength.

So what exactly is the core? It is the area between the pectoral muscles and the upper thighs. In other words, it mainly includes the muscles in the abdomen and rib cage, the obliques, the middle and lower back muscles, the glutes, and the hip flexors. These muscle groups are important for stability and balance in playing golf correctly, and for providing rotational force in the golf swing.
