Ectopic pregnancy (extrauterine pregnancy) is very dangerous for women of childbearing age. Women must choose a good treatment method. How to terminate an ectopic pregnancy effectively is a topic that many women consult about. Below, the experts from Jingtan Hospital will introduce some methods for treating ectopic pregnancy: Since any type of ectopic pregnancy occurs in areas that are not structurally suited for fetal development, there are various treatment options available, but the principle is mainly surgical treatment. There are two types of surgical treatments: one is the removal of the affected fallopian tube; the other is conservative surgery which preserves the affected fallopian tube.

So, what is the best way to terminate an ectopic pregnancy? Upon discovering an ectopic pregnancy, immediate hospitalization is required to avoid life-threatening internal bleeding due to rupture. The choice of surgery depends on the patient's fertility requirements (conservative surgery is suitable for young women who wish to have children), the size and location of the ectopic pregnancy, and the patient's overall health condition. However, it is crucial to seek treatment at a reputable hospital. Hysteroscopy surgery is currently the most effective method for treating ectopic pregnancies. This method causes minimal surgical trauma. Doctors use cold light sources to illuminate and analyze the patient's condition based on images displayed on a monitor from different angles. Special laparoscopic instruments are then used to perform the surgery.

What is the best way to terminate an ectopic pregnancy? Treating ectopic pregnancy through hysteroscopy is considered the best method, favored by many women as it maximally ensures their physical health and fertility. Laparoscopic surgery for ectopic pregnancy only requires a few small incisions near the navel and lower abdomen. The laparoscope lens (with a diameter of 3-10mm) is inserted into the abdominal cavity, and the operation process is similar to open surgery, allowing for a one-time cure. Its greatest advantage lies in its minimal invasiveness, quick recovery, reduced risk of pelvic organ adhesion, and minimal impact on future fertility.

Currently, our hospital primarily uses laparoscopic surgery, where patients can typically be discharged within 3-5 days post-surgery. This minimally invasive technique has largely replaced traditional open surgeries. It causes less disturbance to the pelvic cavity, avoids direct contact between gauze and hands with tissues, and thoroughly rinses the pelvic cavity during surgery. Therefore, patients experience significantly fewer pelvic adhesions after laparoscopic surgery compared to open surgery. In most cases, the affected fallopian tube can be preserved, with little or no need for sutures. The scars are minimal, and the surgery is clear and reliable.
