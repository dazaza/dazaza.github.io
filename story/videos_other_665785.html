2012 Cultural Heritage Art Auction Market: Passion Returns to Rationality

All art auctions in 2011 have ended, presenting a situation of first rising then declining throughout the year. The curtain for the spring auction of 2012 is about to be lifted; how will the cultural heritage art market perform?

On a High Note

Since being affected by the financial crisis from the second half of 2008 to the first half of 2009, Chinese cultural heritage art auctions have been on an upward trend since the autumn auction of 2009. Up until the spring auction of 2011, having gone through four seasons, each season has seen growth of more than one fold. The spring auction of 2011 was another historical peak after the autumn auction of 2007. The performance of major auction houses all set new historical records, with a total turnover of 20.509 billion yuan from ten companies.

In 2011, Chinese calligraphy and painting still occupied an absolute share of the entire market. Among the 348 pieces of Chinese calligraphy and painting works exceeding 10 million yuan from ten companies, there were 207 pieces of modern and contemporary calligraphy and painting works, with a total transaction amount reaching 5.749 billion yuan. Zhang Daqian, Qi Baishi, Xu Beihong, Li Keran, Fu Baoshi, Lu YanShao, and Huang Zhou stood out particularly, occupying the vast majority.

In 2012, the absolute market position and high growth rate of Chinese calligraphy and painting will not change, mainly due to the following reasons: First, Chinese calligraphy and painting are the most important traditional art forms in our country, with a large quantity available, wide recognition of famous artists across generations, and high artistic value; Second, compared to artifacts, the trading market for Chinese calligraphy and painting is concentrated domestically; Third, compared to porcelain or miscellaneous art items, Chinese calligraphy and painting are easier to appreciate, with a broad audience base, and new buyers usually start with Chinese calligraphy and painting.

In 2011, ten companies held 52 special auctions for ceramics, jade, and rare treasures, successfully selling 4,396 pieces (sets), accounting for 7.71% of the total number of items, with a transaction amount of 4.371 billion yuan, accounting for 11.75% of the total market. This includes special auctions like "Cautious Virtue Bright Dao Wutai Mountain Person's Collection of Qing Dynasty Dao Guang Imperial Porcelain," "Dongbozhai Collection of Porcelain," "Imperial Court Porcelain Elite of Prosperous Dynasties" from China Guardian Auctions; "Overseas Collections of Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing Jade" and "Tianhan Yaoqiong Overseas Collections of Chinese Jade Carving Art Masterpieces" from Beijing Poly Auction; and "Contemporary Chinese Jade Masterworks" from Xiling Seal Engravers' Society. It is estimated that in 2012, bronze wares, porcelains, and other fine cultural relics will remain the main focus pursued by collectors besides calligraphy and painting.

Moreover, at various auction venues, categories such as furniture, guqin (ancient Chinese zither), classical furniture, philatelic numismatic items, purple sand tea utensils, etc., have all made breakthrough developments. Categories that were previously only included in certain special sessions now shine as highlight special sessions, and some items that were rarely or never listed before frequently appear in the auctions of various companies.

Steady Transactions

In recent years' cultural heritage art auctions, masterpieces and special sessions of fine works have always been "hard currency," with strong anti-decline capabilities. In the spring auction of 2011, the ten auction companies had as many as 5,726 individual items sold for over one million yuan, an increase of 2,260 items compared to 2010. Among these 5,726 items, there were 533 items over ten million yuan, an increase of 188 items compared to 2010; 45 items over fifty million yuan, an increase of 11 items compared to 2010; and 13 items over one hundred million yuan, an increase of 2 items compared to 2010. This was the year with the largest number of high-end artworks in the history of our country's cultural heritage art auction market spanning 19 years.

Special sessions of renowned collections are highly sought after. For example, China Guardian Auctions' "Zhongxing Hall Collection Special Session," "Qiu Hall Collection Special Session," "Singapore Collection of Five Pieces of Lu YanShao's Fine Works," "True Aspiration Studio Collection Special Session," "Purple Mud Community Collection of Modern and Contemporary Purple Sand Masterpieces Special Session," "Luming Room Collection of Ming Style Furniture Special Session," "Wutai Mountain Person's Collection of Qing Dynasty Dao Guang Imperial Porcelain (Part I & II) Special Session," and "Eating Bamboo Hall Collection of Modern and Contemporary Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Session;" Beijing Poly Auction's "Good Collector Guo Ruiteng's Doctorate Night Session of Traditional Chinese Calligraphy and Painting," "Collecting Essence Tower Night Session of Modern and Contemporary Chinese Calligraphy and Painting," "True Aspiration Studio Collection of Modern and Contemporary Calligraphy," and "Guo Xiuyi's Centenary Anniversary Rare Collection of Calligraphy and Painting Night Session;" Beijing Hanhai Auction's "Sun Peicang Family Collection of Xu Beihong Oil Painting Treasures Special Session" and "Zhao Qingwei Collection of Illustration Series Hand Manuscripts Special Session;" Beijing Kuangshi Auction's "Xuhuai Collection of Modern and Contemporary Calligraphy Special Session;" Beijing Huacheng Auction's "Zhu Yunxuan Collection of Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Session" and "Ran Family Book Hall Collection of Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Session;" Shanghai Duoyunxuan Auction's "Liaotian Building Calligraphy and Painting Special Session;" and Xiling Seal Engravers' Society's "Leisure Residence Collection of Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Special Session," among others.

In 2012, the reliability effect and brand effect of special sessions of renowned collections, historical masterpieces, and collections of fine works will become increasingly prominent in the cultural heritage art auction market. In the art market, there are more and more art investors and investment institutions. Before them, risk control is the primary task. Given the current immature appraisal technology and chaotic appraisal system, they will inevitably choose works with reliable sources as their purchase objects. Moreover, all purchasing behaviors aimed at investment hope that the purchased items and the purchasing behavior itself possess a certain brand effect and news effect, which is beneficial to the further enhancement of the item's value and the resale of the work.

Strong Rise?

In recent years, whether in terms of transaction volume or the level of high-end auction items, the international dominance of Chinese cultural heritage art auctions has shifted to China. Beijing has also become one of the four major global art auction centers after New York, London, and Hong Kong. The "Global Art Market Report" released by The European Fine Art Fair in 2011 stated that the Chinese art market is strongly rising and has already ranked second globally. With the growth of the Chinese economy and the arrival of our country's cultural economic era, more and more international collectors, investment experts, and international auction houses will focus on Chinese art transactions. It can be affirmed that our country's cultural heritage art auctions will play an increasingly important role in the international market.

Although the Chinese cultural heritage art auction market has maintained high-speed growth each season in recent years, in fact, the growth rate has shown a downward trend. In 2012, there are many uncertain factors in the Chinese economy, and the GDP growth rate is expected to be adjusted downward. M1 growth may also decrease correspondingly as monetary policy tends to stabilize. Faced with the unclear economic situation and funding situation at this stage, it is unlikely that there will be significant growth in the transaction amount of our country's cultural heritage art auction market in 2012. We believe that with the deepening understanding of the value and credit system of art pieces by the whole society, the cultural heritage art auction market of our country will enter a period of market adjustment characterized by passion returning to rationality, unification of investment and collection, and further improvement of the integrity system construction.

Under the background of national policies promoting the great development and prosperity of culture industries, and with the gradual standardization trends in all aspects of the art market, including Shanghai calligraphy and painting transactions, the medium- and long-term trends of our country's cultural heritage art auction market will remain optimistic.
