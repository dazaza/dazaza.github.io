The 6 Dizzying Scenes of Fierce Female Drivers I've Witnessed

by gogole111 on 2007-05-30 23:24:00

The Six Absolutely Dizzying Scenes of the "Triple-No" Female Drivers

Scene One: No Awareness of Car Models:

Scene 1: On the Capital Airport Expressway, a white BMW was traveling at a speed of 60 kilometers per hour, with thick blue smoke trailing from its rear as if it were ascending to the clouds. Initially, it was thought that the vehicle had caught fire, but upon closer inspection, the blue smoke was emanating from the rear wheels. It turned out that the handbrake hadn't been fully released, causing the brake disc to overheat and produce smoke. Quickly accelerating alongside the BMW on its left side, I lowered my window and waved to get the driver's attention. By then, two taxis had already honked and flashed their lights at it from behind and to the right, yet the female BMW driver remained seated upright inside, her eyes fixed straight ahead without any awareness. The group did not give up; one taxi accelerated past the BMW, then merged in front of it and braked to slow down. Unexpectedly, the BMW responded by slowing down, turning the wheel, changing lanes, accelerating, overtaking, and speeding away, leaving behind even thicker trails of blue smoke.

We all looked at each other speechlessly, marveling at the excellent soundproofing quality of the BMW and its powerful acceleration performance while also being amazed by the female driver's focus and persistence.

Scene 2: On the single-lane bridge leading from South to West at Zizhu Bridge on the West Third Ring Road, about one kilometer long, overtaking is impossible. A Yaseon (Hyundai Accent) was steadily driving at 30 kilometers per hour in front, "leading the way." With an open road ahead, the long line of vehicles could only follow closely behind. Despite the flashing lights and honking from the vehicles behind, the Yaseon continued as if saying, "It's my territory, and I'm in charge." It took five whole minutes to cross the one-kilometer-long bridge. Once the road opened up, and we managed to overtake by changing lanes, glancing back revealed the female driver inside gracefully conversing with a friend using elegant gestures...

Scene Two: No Clue in Hand Type:

Scene 3: On the auxiliary road of Jing-Tong Expressway, there was one lane for motor vehicles and one for non-motorized vehicles. The traffic flowed smoothly, though slightly dense, with occasional vehicles overtaking from the non-motorized vehicle lane, quickly merging back into the motor vehicle lane when necessary. The female driver of a red Audi A4 seemed quite dissatisfied with the frequent "cuts" from the BMW ahead. Seizing the opportunity, she turned the wheel, accelerated, overtook the BMW on the right, turned left, cut in, braked suddenly, and collided with a bang... The entire process was seamless, smooth as flowing clouds, accomplished in one breath... Was she really that talented?

Scene 4: On the South Fourth Ring Road, traffic flowed smoothly at a speed of 70 kilometers per hour. Suddenly, red lights flashed frequently ahead, and all vehicles began to split off to the left and right. Upon reaching the current point, a yellow QQ car was seen slowly crossing horizontally from the innermost lane to the outermost lane almost at a standstill, heading towards the ring road exit. All normal...
