[Exhibition Overview]

The Italian Verona Solar Expo (SOLAR EXPO) and Green Building Expo (GREEN BUILDING) are the largest international solar exhibitions in Italy. SOLAR EXPO is dedicated to promoting cooperation and exchange between Italian solar companies and international professional enterprises. The exhibition also organized 5,320 attendees for 54 professional meetings of various scales and themes, focusing on the development and exchange of solar technology and international market trends. Meanwhile, heads of solar associations from nearly 30 countries, key technical experts from research institutions, and representatives of exhibiting companies totaling 415 gave special forum speeches during the event.

The Italian government has introduced corresponding policies to encourage the use and popularization of solar technology, supporting the development of solar companies, such as analyzing and evaluating the potential for solar power generation and heating nationwide; specifically analyzing various factors affecting the use of solar energy and proposing administrative and legal measures to eliminate obstacles to the widespread use of solar energy; suggesting the implementation of solar utilization and energy-saving measures in the construction industry; and formulating national plans for the development of the solar technology industry. A portion of electricity tax revenue (a total of 2 billion euros) was used to invest in solar power generation and heating equipment, aiming to achieve the EU's goal of reducing electricity consumption by 20% through various energy-saving measures and increasing the proportion of renewable energy to 25% by 2012. With the introduction of Italian government policies supporting the use of solar energy and economic support, its solar market has rapidly developed in Europe, achieving a growth rate of 46.4% in 2006. Currently, Italy has become the fourth-largest solar market in Europe.
