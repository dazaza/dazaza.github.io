Matters needing attention in the diet for diabetic nephropathy:

1. Control blood glucose stability: Persistent elevation of blood glucose will induce fat cholesterol metabolic disorders, promote thickening, hardening, and degeneration of the glomerulus and renal capillary intima, causing them to lose normal function. The key to controlling blood glucose is strict restriction of calorie intake and adherence to taking antidiabetic drugs, avoiding various factors such as emotional excitement and infection that can cause fluctuations in blood glucose.

2. Salt intake should be restricted: To protect the kidneys and reduce their workload, dishes for diabetics should be as bland as possible. Salt intake should be less than 7 grams per day, and water intake should also be restricted during severe renal failure.

3. Appropriately restrict potassium and protein intake: Diabetic nephropathy is prone to acidosis and hyperkalemia, which can trigger arrhythmias and hepatic coma once they occur.

4. Adequate intake of vitamins and trace elements: Especially vitamin B, vitamin C, zinc, calcium, iron, etc., which can protect the kidneys.
