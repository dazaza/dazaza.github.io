Discussion on the Replacement Timing of Anti-static Cleanroom Suits

by bys1688 on 2010-06-30 08:27:08

Anti-static cleanroom garments are specialized clothing designed for dust-free and static-free environments, thus some of their special functions make their use quite different from ordinary clothing. However, in many small ways, we can find familiar methods for handling regular clothing, but the irreplaceability of major aspects forces us to master the techniques for dealing with anti-static cleanroom garments.

Some dust-free factories replace anti-static cleanroom garments based on appearance evaluation standards or cleaning frequency. Most anti-static cleanroom garment cleaning service providers certify that their workwear can generally be cleaned up to 100 times. So, what criteria should be used to determine the replacement time of anti-static cleanroom garments? This article provides some reference standards. The percentage change based on original test data can serve as an objective standard for determining when to replace anti-static cleanroom garments. Applying this standard to IEST-RP-CC003.3, the recommended replacement standard for anti-static cleanroom garments is as follows:

Helmke Drum Test: According to the Helmke drum test, the cumulative number of particles greater than 0.3um per cubic foot of air drawn in within one minute for Class I garments should not exceed 2000, or the cumulative number of particles greater than 0.5um should not exceed 1200.

ASTM F-51 Test: For Class A garments, the total number of particles greater than 5um should be less than 1000, and there should be fewer than 10 filaments.

Helmke Drum Test EOS/ESD STD 2.1 Electrostatic Test Standard: 1.0 x 10^5 meters ~ 1.0 x 10^11 Ω/square.

Human Box or Particle Control Test Limits depend on the cleanliness level of the cleanroom where the garments are worn.

Subjective standards relate to customer specifications regarding stitching, contamination, and the number of patches on the garments:

Patches should not exceed one-quarter of a 3-inch area. Heat-sealed materials must match the garment material.

Anti-static threads must be straight, and patches must be fully adhered to the fabric.

Stitches should not exceed 3 inches and should comply with the Efb-2 class specifications in ASTM/FED-STD-751A. Anti-static thread edges must be straight.

Stains should not exceed the size of a credit card. Adhesive substances that scatter dust are not allowed.

Snaps and buttons should be intact. Fabric reinforcement around snap areas is required.

To meet the above standards, baseline data such as ICP/MS ion extraction tests, ESD, SEM/EDX, HELMKE drum, and ASTM F-51 tests must be obtained during the initial program for anti-static cleanroom garments. In daily use, routine tests like ICP/MS ion extraction, ESD, SEM/EDX, HELMKE drum, and ASTM F-51 should be conducted and trend charts made. In summary, most anti-static cleanroom garments, according to the operational guidelines of IEST-RP-CC003.3, can still be used effectively after 100 washes and continue to meet the particle testing standards of IEST-RP-CC003.3 and ASTM F-51 in real-time applications. Typically, there are 17 process cycles in a year. Therefore, if employees wearing anti-static cleanroom garments strictly adhere to cleanroom regulations, these garments should last at least six years under typical cleanroom conditions. Repairs to anti-static cleanroom garments must be handled by designated fabric or garment manufacturers to ensure repair effectiveness and durability. If the anti-static cleanroom garments meet the above standards for active and inactive particle containment/shedding, ion contamination, and ESD, and their appearance is well-maintained according to the aforementioned criteria, then there will be no risk of contamination to the cleanroom, products, and processes inside the cleanroom. Therefore, if any part of the above standards is not met, it is recommended to replace the anti-static cleanroom garments.

Anti-static cleanroom garments demonstrate their value in industrial production through our work usage. During this process, we strive for maximum production efficiency, so reasonable and scientific management methods have become essential skills that all enterprises and individuals need to master.
