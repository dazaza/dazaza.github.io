Lei Jun: Smart TV is another screen for smartphones

On September 10th, news came out that Lei Jun, CEO of Xiaomi, stated at the Great Wall Club's Smartphone 2.0 Summit that the most important feature of the next generation of smartphones would be "using a phone to connect with the world." The original intention behind Xiaomi making TVs also stems from the central position of the smartphone: "If we consider smart TVs as an extension of the smartphone screen, then the TV market will become a huge market in the next few years."

On September 5th, Xiaomi released its first smart TV at a new product launch event, attracting industry attention and heated discussions. Prior to this, internet companies such as Letv and iQIYI had already ventured into the smart TV market. However, Lei Jun believed that Xiaomi TVs are still very different from regular smart TVs.

"Regular smart TVs simply attach a smart system to a TV, resulting in a low activation rate. Xiaomi, on the other hand, integrates the smart system directly as a module within the TV, or you can view Xiaomi's TV as a large-screen computer with TV functionality," Lei Jun said.

He also revealed that he started working on smart TVs six months after the release of Xiaomi phones, with only one goal in mind—making the TV another screen for the smartphone.

"The phone is the remote control for the TV, and the TV is the display screen for the phone," Lei Jun believes that the phone will be the center of the entire world, replacing computers as the most common intelligent tool, and phone screens will be ubiquitous.

In Lei Jun's view, the current computing and storage capabilities of smartphones no longer need to be a concern; the key lies in data input and output. Enlarging the phone screen to 6 inches or 7 inches cannot solve this problem, but the TV screen can easily become the display screen for the phone. The TV screen is the best solution to the limitation of the phone screen. "If we consider the smart TV as the screen for the smartphone, then the TV market will become a massive market in the next few years."
