Painful, yet precious memories. Horror movies.

by hoouk260 on 2012-03-03 20:07:23

Before I knew it, Ttu has been four and a half months old. Watching the round sleeping face of the little guy, all the tiredness in my heart and body disappeared at once. At this moment, I must be smiling with kind eyes. The three women around me who are both family members and close friends have entered the ranks of would-be moms. Looking at them, I tried hard to recall my past, but it was not so clear. It seemed that the ten months passed without much feeling. Only the memory of entering the delivery room was still vivid, but I could not remember how it hurt at that time. It's strange that time gave mothers special painkillers, so that mothers who had given birth before could gather in groups of three or five, otherwise how could they choose to endure such inhuman pain again. The moment I saw Ttu, I had just woken up from a coma and my consciousness had not yet become clear. It is said that I cried, saying that my son's head was squeezed into a long one. Then, I saw my little baby on the adjacent bed. He was also tired and slept soundly. That little face and little mouth made me cry with emotion. The mood at that time could not be described by words. I just felt that it was worth it, everything was worth it, no matter how painful or difficult it was. Now, I look forward to Ttu growing up and having more skills, being able to call mom, and being able to coquettish. But I am afraid that Ttu will grow up too fast, I am afraid that I did not enjoy every moment of my child's growth, I am afraid to miss every progress of my son, it is really contradictory. It's late and I am not nervous anymore. For those who have not found true love, quickly. For those who do not want children, quickly. For those who are already pregnant, stay calm, rational, and feel the happiness of every day carefully. Those are the wonderful moments that you will try your best to recall in the future.
