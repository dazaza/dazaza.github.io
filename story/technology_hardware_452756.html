Reflections on Accounting Investigation and English Education - Essay

by hylw113k8l5r on 2011-06-28 09:10:20

The basic situation of the questionnaire survey mainly involves the following aspects: impact, materials, teaching methods, and instruction. During the survey conducted in May 2010, a total of 80 questionnaires were distributed, with all 80 returned, including 80 valid responses, achieving a 100% return rate. The surveyed group consisted of junior high school students studying accounting. By understanding the respondents' learning experiences and needs, we aim to promote positive and effective teaching reforms in these areas. Based on the survey results and analysis, we designed a questionnaire covering students' English proficiency levels, six English teachers, and other options.

The survey results showed that the primary factors ranked in importance were: teaching method, English language ability, textbook, teaching content, teaching in English, teaching environment, and conditions. The findings indicate that from the students' perspective, the teaching method has a significant influence. This aligns with financial management as it relates closely to the application of accounting information and accounting knowledge, making it necessary to appropriately increase the transmission of financial management knowledge. Accounting itself is highly technical and practical knowledge; thus, enhancing the basic operation of accounting software in English teaching can help familiarize students with an English language environment for accounting processing. Simultaneously, applying English accounting knowledge to practice benefits in raising students' interest and enthusiasm for learning accounting in English.

The investigation and analysis of teaching materials often lead to the following results: 51.25% prefer English-Chinese translation textbooks, 30% choose English textbooks supplemented with Chinese notes and key materials based on their adaptability to technical terms and definitions, while only 18.75% opt for original English textbooks. For students with minimal English proficiency, learning from original English textbooks presents a steep learning curve, making bilingual translation materials the most challenging among the three types of study materials. Students favor Chinese-English translated textbooks due to their lower difficulty level, reflecting their struggles with English and psychological fear. The exploration of scientific and reasonable teaching methods through survey and analysis of teaching approaches, combined with experiences from other institutions and our own, summarized six applicable levels. The survey revealed that case studies are popular among students, with 71.25%, followed by theoretical teaching at 61.25%, then situational teaching and cooperative learning group discussions at 47.5%. Question-based teaching methods rank fifth and sixth at 25% and 22.5%, respectively. These findings provide crucial references for improving our teaching methods.

Through surveys and analyses to understand the gap between students' expectations and their learning outcomes in English accounting, we aim to enhance students' learning interest. The survey shows that with a large number of foreign companies entering China and more domestic enterprises going global, there is an increased demand for accounting talent proficient in both local accounting practices and international English standards. To achieve reasonable and effective teaching reform and improve teaching outcomes, this paper focuses on students' "accounting English, teaching content, materials, teaching methods, and instruction."
