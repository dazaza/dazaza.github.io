Synopsis:

The story takes place in 1947 when banker Andy Dufresne's wife has an extramarital affair. She and her lover are found dead at their home, leading to Andy being accused of shooting his wife and her lover. Andy is sentenced to life imprisonment, meaning he will spend the rest of his life in Shawshank Prison.

Red was sentenced to life imprisonment for murder in 1927 and has been denied parole multiple times. He has become an "authority figure" in Shawshank Prison, capable of obtaining almost anything you want as long as you can pay for it—cigarettes, candy, alcohol, and even marijuana. Whenever new prisoners arrive, everyone bets on who will cry on the first night. Red believed that the frail, bookish Andy would certainly cry, but Andy's silence made him lose four packs of cigarettes. However, this also caused Red to view Andy differently.

For a long time, Andy did not interact with anyone. While others complained, he walked leisurely in the yard, as if he were in a park. A month later, Andy asked Red to help him get his first item—a small rock hammer. His explanation was that he wanted to carve small things to pass the time and said he would find a way to avoid routine inspections by the prison authorities. Soon, Red started playing with the chess pieces carved by Andy. Later, Andy also put up a large poster of Rita Hayworth on the wall of his cell.

Once, Andy and a few other inmates went out for labor. He accidentally overheard the warden talking about tax issues. Andy said he had a way to legally exempt the warden from paying a large amount of taxes. In return, he secured two bottles of Tiger beer for each of his dozen inmate friends. Drinking the beer, Red said that for many years, he felt free for the first time again.

Due to Andy's expertise in financial systems, he quickly escaped from the heavy physical labor in the prison and harassment from other deviant inmates. Soon, the increasingly famous Andy began handling tax issues for more and more prison guards, and even consulted on children's education. Meanwhile, Andy gradually became an important tool for Shawshank Warden Norton to launder money. Because Andy continuously wrote letters to the governor, he finally managed to secure a small sum of money for the construction of a prison library. Prison life was very monotonous, so one always had to find something to do. Andy heard that Red used to like playing the harmonica, so he bought one for him. On quiet nights, the melodious and faint harmonica music could be heard echoing through the prison.

The arrival of a young inmate disrupted Andy's peaceful prison life: this inmate had previously served time in another prison and had heard about Andy's case. He knew who the real killer was! But when Andy requested a retrial from the warden, it was firmly rejected, and he received a severe punishment of two months' solitary confinement. To prevent Andy's release, the prison even orchestrated the death of the informant!

Faced with the harsh reality, Andy became very depressed... One day, he told Red, "If one day you can be paroled, please go to a certain place for me and fulfill a wish. It's where I had my first date with my wife. Dig up a box under a big oak tree there. You'll know what it is then." That night, amidst thunderstorms and lightning, Andy, who had achieved spiritual redemption, successfully escaped from prison.

It turned out that for twenty years, Andy had been digging a tunnel every day with that small rock hammer, then covering the opening with the poster. After escaping from prison, Andy took part of the warden's illicit funds and exposed the truth about the warden's embezzlement and bribery. In the safe where the warden kept his secret accounts, he found a Bible left by Andy, inside which was a rock hammer almost worn into a round head.

Red was released, and under the oak tree, he found a box of cash. The two old friends finally reunited on the sunny beaches of Mexico.
