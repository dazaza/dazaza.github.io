Rules of Zha Jin Hua (Poker Game)

“[Zha Jin Hua]” Introduction

Three-card poker (Zha Jin Hua) is a folk card game widely spread throughout China. It has unique rules for comparing hands. Players compare their three cards to determine the winner, and the game tests the player's courage and wisdom.

[Game Rules]

Number of Participants and Cards

The number of participants is between 2 - 6 people, using a deck of cards with the big and small jokers removed, totaling 52 cards.

Game Terminology Explanation

Dealer: The winner of the previous round becomes the dealer for the next round; for the first round or when the winner leaves, a dealer is randomly selected.

Ante: Before the game starts, each player must first invest game coins. The amount varies depending on the room settings.

Cap: There are two situations where a cap can occur

A) Each venue has an upper limit on the total amount of game coins that can be bet. Once this limit is reached or exceeded, the system automatically reveals the cards.

B) When a player does not have enough game coins for the next bet, the system automatically reveals the cards.

Fujian-Taiwan Chess and Card Games

Check Cards: View the suits and numbers of your three cards. Bets after checking the cards are considered open bets.

Blind Bet: Betting without looking at the cards.

Open Bet: After checking the cards and betting, the amount wagered doubles.

If additional bets are made, they are doubled after being added.

Call: Match the amount wagered by the previous player.

If your hand is open, you call openly; if it's blind, you call blindly.

Raise: Add more game coins based on the previous player’s bet.

For example, if the previous player bet 100 and you choose to raise by 100, then the total bet becomes 200.

You can choose specific amounts to raise, which vary depending on the venue settings.

Reveal Cards: When only two players remain who can place bets, either player can choose to reveal the cards.

The player choosing to reveal must pay double the current required bet.

If the hands are of equal strength, the player who revealed first loses.

Fold: Forfeit all game coins wagered so far and lose the round.

Basic Game Flow Description

Pay Ante: The game coins paid before dealing the cards.

Deal Cards: Cards are dealt starting from the dealer; for the first round, a user is randomly chosen to deal first.

Each player receives three cards, face down as hidden cards.

Gameplay: The player clockwise after the dealer starts betting, followed by other players in turn.

When it's a player's turn, they can choose to raise, call, check cards, fold, or reveal cards based on conditions and judgment.

Reveal Cards: When only two players remain, either can choose to reveal the cards;

Or when more than two users remain but the total bet reaches the cap, the system reveals the cards.

Determine Winner: Determine the winner based on hand comparison rules.

Show all non-folded players' hands. If only one player remains capable of betting, that player is declared the winner.

Hand Rankings

Triple: Three cards of the same rank.

Straight Flush: Three consecutive cards of the same suit.

Flush: Three cards of the same suit.

Straight: Three consecutive cards of different suits.

Pair: Two cards of the same rank among the three.

Special: Different suited 235.

Single: Any hand not fitting the above categories.

Hand Comparisons

Triple > Straight Flush > Flush > Straight > Pair > Single

Triples, flushes, pairs, and singles are compared according to point order.

Straight Flushes and straights are compared sequentially. AKQ>A23>KQJ>QJ10...

Special hands are treated as single hands under normal circumstances.

When a Triple exists, special hands > Triples.

Special Rewards

Winning with a special hand grants corresponding special rewards,

which are deducted equally from all losing players and given to the winning player.

If a losing player cannot afford the special reward, they forfeit all remaining game coins.

Triple: Special reward = Total ante * 10

Straight Flush: Special reward = Total ante * 5

Game Coin Calculation Rules

Game coins are virtual game data used in QQ games.

In game coin scoring venues, game coins serve as the scoring unit.

After completing a round, a player's game coins change,

and after each game ends, the system automatically recovers a certain number of game coins from each player.

Specific calculation method:

Victory player's game coin reward: X×N + Special Reward - A

Defeat player's game coin consumption: X×N + Deducted Special Reward + A

Where:

N is the room coefficient for game coins, A is the number of game coins automatically recovered per game by the system,

which varies by room, see specific room details.

X is the base value for the game, calculated as follows:

Victory player's X = Total amount paid by all other players

Defeat player's X = Amount paid by the individual player

Example:

N (room coefficient) = 1, no special reward, victory player's base value = 5000,

defeat player's base value = 5000, A is 100

Then the victory player's rewarded game coins = 5000×1 + 0 = 5000 game coins

Defeat player's consumed game coins = 5000×1 + 0 = 5000 game coins
