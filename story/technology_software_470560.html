LED wide viewing angle price drop, ten recommended cost-effective LCD monitors

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 10:24:22

Before the launch of Apple smartphones, only a few people would believe that a phone costing around 5000 yuan would sell out. However, it did happen. Seeing Apple products everywhere, we can only say everything is possible. The same applies to the monitor field. Although monitor prices are getting lower and lower, price wars no longer stimulate purchasing desires. Most consumers have developed visual fatigue towards constant price drops. Their demand for monitors is no longer just low prices; the need for better image quality and more humanized functions is becoming increasingly strong.

To meet the purchasing needs of different consumers, the author has specially selected the 10 most cost-effective monitors in August. There are high-cost-performance products designed for entry-level users, LED+ wide-view products with significant recent price drops, and also high-end models for those who pursue high image quality and multiple functions. There will surely be one suitable for you.

Dell U2412M with 24-inch IPS + LED

Reference Price: 2290 yuan

Review: Dell monitors have always been favored by many consumers for their excellent image quality, practical and easy-to-use design, and comprehensive after-sales service system. Many classic models have been launched. However, these classic models generally use traditional CCFL backlighting, which inevitably has the disadvantages of high power consumption and heat generation. Recently, a new model, U2412M, featuring an LED backlight with a 16:10 ratio and a 24-inch wide-view large screen, has begun sales. The dealer's quote is 2290 yuan, recommended for users who have higher requirements for image quality.

Product Advantages: Wide-view panel, full range of interfaces

Product Disadvantages: Not a high-end IPS panel

Suitable Audience: Users with high requirements for image quality

Front Appearance

In terms of appearance, as a high-end monitor aimed at quasi-professional users, Dell U2412M features ultra-narrow bezels, matte material body, and adjustable rotating base, presenting an overall dignified and rigorous look.

Back Design

Interface Section

In terms of performance, it uses an LED-backlit e-IPS panel with a 16:10 aspect ratio, a recommended resolution of 1920*1200, and a dot pitch of 0.27 mm. The surface adopts a 3H hardened anti-glare coating. The color display capability is 6bit or 16.7 million colors, with a color gamut of 82% (CIE 1976). The viewing angle reaches 178 degrees both vertically and horizontally. The default contrast ratio is 1000:1, and dynamically it can reach up to 2 million:1. The brightness is 300 nits. Interface options include one DVI-D supporting HDCP, one DisplayPort, one 15-pin D-Sub, and a four-port USB 2.0 Hub.
