At the crossroads, should we secure profits or build positions at lower levels?

by xxywing on 2009-02-19 09:52:54

Wednesday, February 18. The two markets continued yesterday's decline with the Shanghai Composite Index sharply falling by 109 points and the Shenzhen Component Index dropping by 377 points. There were very few rising individual stocks. Among the participating contestants, there were more losers than winners. Contestants who performed well today mostly ranked at the top of the leaderboard. For example, contestant "Laozhe" had a daily return rate of 5.1%. The Yuen Changxing that he held rose against the market trend by 5.66%, and the return on this single stock reached 60%. Contestant "Tuoniao" also achieved a return rate of 6.01%. The rise limit of Antai Technology that he held made up for the fall of another stock, Baoxin Energy. Contestant "Ci Ke" had an even higher return rate of 10.03%, becoming the highest earner of the day.

Several high-yield contestants who ranked at the top of the leaderboard conducted large transactions today. High-yield contestant "Shanghai Leilei" sold all of his holdings in Haitong Securities, worth more than 800,000 yuan. He sold them at the opening price of 13.38 yuan, and Haitong Securities has already earned him more than 100,000 yuan. Contestant "Shanghai Leilei" also sold 4,500 shares of ST Pingneng today, which brought him a 50% return. Currently, contestant "Shanghai Leilei" only holds stocks worth 50,000 yuan, basically leaving him with an empty position and a holding ratio of only 5%. This series of actions gives everyone insight into selling high for profit and conveys his intention to other contestants and spectators of being bearish on the future market. Contestants "Chengzhang Dongli" and "Jiazhi Youxuan" are different; they are clearly going against the trend. The two contestants respectively took out 1.5 million yuan and 1.6 million yuan to simultaneously buy Chengtou Holdings and Dazhong Utilities. Contestants ranking lower on the leaderboard have less funds but can be roughly divided into two categories with equal strength: those who are aggressively selling and those who are buying heavily. Is it about securing earlier gains or building positions at low prices and waiting for a second rise? Which operation is correct? Only time will tell.

Contestant "Ci Ke" had a return of 10.03% today. The Antai Technology that he held rose against the market trend by 10.03%, allowing his ranking to rise nine places and return to the top ten of the leaderboard. Everyone is relatively familiar with contestant "Ci Ke". This contestant fully invested in 200,000 shares of a single stock, currently valued at 3.09 million yuan. A single Antai Technology brought him a doubled return of 100%, earning him 1.55 million yuan. This is quite an impressive number in just two months of competition. As of the close of trading on Wednesday, the average holding ratio of contestants was 74.79%, which did not drop significantly compared to yesterday, only decreasing by 0.7 percentage points. Bearish contestants and bullish contestants are basically evenly matched. Today, 44 contestants traded, one less than the previous trading day. The average return rate of contestants is 19.16%, which has fallen below 20. As of the close of trading on Wednesday, the top ten contestants are: Shanlang, Dundun, Laozhe, Ci Ke, Hao Xinqing, Shanghai Leilei, Tuoniao, Chengzhang Dongli, Jiazhi Youxuan, and Qicaihong.

So far, this round of market conditions has determined a phase high point, and a significant adjustment has already begun. It is expected that after quickly probing the support of the lower level in a single needle-like manner, the market may still bravely turn upward and carry out a strong rebound. However, it is necessary to closely monitor the energy and intensity of the rebound to decide whether a new high can be created in the future and whether this fall is just an adjustment or the end of the market trend. Now objectively speaking, I dare not make a hasty conclusion. After all, the overall fall and breakdown of surrounding markets have cast the shadow of a possible second outbreak of the financial crisis over us. And the excess liquidity - the released tiger - after taking a temporary rest, whether it can again stir up irrational waves and whether the management layer can decisively and effectively curb it, will have a decisive impact on the future of A-shares. In terms of operations, 1. If the contestants' holdings are not heavy, they can continue to hold and observe in the medium term, and the chips added today can be sold after gaining profits from the subsequent rebound; 2. As the adjustment continues, successful short-term risk-avoidance traders can continue to buy low and sell high.
