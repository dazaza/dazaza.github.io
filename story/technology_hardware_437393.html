Mr. Xia introduced that the ideal weight of a newborn is 6 to 7 jin, and over 8 jin is defined as macrosomia. In recent years, the number of macrosomic babies born in hospitals has obviously increased, but those over 10 jin are not very common. This is closely related to excessive nutrition intake of pregnant women. Overweight in pregnant women will increase the chance of getting high blood pressure and gestational diabetes, which is not good for fetal growth; adults who were macrosomic babies will also have an increased chance of getting diabetes and cardiovascular disease. Experts reminded that pregnancy is a normal physiological process. In terms of diet, it is not appropriate to take too many tonics or eat greedily. Intaking 1 bottle of milk, 2 eggs, 3 liang of meat, 400 grams of staple food, and 500 grams of vegetables and fruits every day can satisfy the need for nutrition. In addition, pregnant women can choose appropriate exercises such as walking and doing calisthenics to maintain physical strength.

Reported by Chu Tian Jin Bao reporter Zhenzhen Gao, correspondents Jianyue Zhou and Honglei Wen: The birth of a chubby son should be a happy event. However, new mother Ms. Yang was extremely worried about her baby's health. Her chubby son, weighing 11.3 jin at birth due to being overweight, triggered a series of diseases and was sent to the neonatal intensive care unit on the day he was born.

March 28th, Ms. Yang from Xishui had been pregnant for 40 weeks. Because the child was estimated to be a macrosomic baby, she underwent a cesarean section at the local hospital, and the doctors had a hard time delivering the child. Upon weighing, this chubby boy turned out to be 11.3 jin. Before Ms. Yang's family could celebrate, the baby immediately showed symptoms of oxygen deficiency and severe asphyxia, with a very low heart rate. He was transferred to the neonatal department of Hubei Maternal and Child Health Care Hospital for treatment that night. "When we received the patient, Mao Mao's entire skin was blue-purple, his breathing was shallow, his heart rate slowed down, and the tarpaulin. Due to oxygen deficiency, it also caused damage to his heart-lung and nervous systems," introduced Director Physician Xia Shiwen. The experts immediately started the rescue, and after nearly 40 hours of high-frequency ventilator, the baby's breathing condition improved. His life was saved, but subsequent treatments encountered difficulties. Because the subcutaneous fat of the baby was too thick, the blood vessels could not be seen at all, making injections difficult. Even experienced nurses arranged by the hospital had to exert great effort to successfully give him injections for treatment.

The chubby baby (on the left) is clearly much bigger than the little friend next to him.

Director Xia introduced that most babies in the neonatal intensive care unit weigh around 1 kilogram and are premature low-weight infants, making the chubby baby appear particularly large among them. Due to his chubbiness, the baby sweated a lot, often causing red rashes on his skin. Nurses had to provide special care: slightly lowering the temperature of his incubator, frequently cleaning and wiping his sweat, especially keeping the dryness of the skin folds, and continuously soothing his emotions. After several days of treatment, the chubby baby's condition basically stabilized, but doctors still need to improve his breathing problems and treat the damage to his heart, lung, liver, and kidney functions caused by oxygen deficiency asphyxia. "After I got pregnant, my appetite was always particularly good, and everything tasted delicious. Thinking that the baby needed nutrition, I basically ate without any restrictions," recalled Ms. Yang. Due to excessive nutritional intake during pregnancy, she knew that the fetus was heavy in the late stages of pregnancy, but she didn't expect it to grow to over 11 jin.
