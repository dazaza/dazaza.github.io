Huijin may increase holdings in three major banks via dividends to ease capital raising pressure. After this year's rapid increase in credit assets scale, supplementing capital for sustainable development has become an important issue for next year. The key is what method to adopt and when to implement it. At this point, the major shareholder - Central Huijin Company, with a model of reinvesting dividends, has become the most popular solution in the market. Therefore, we analyze that the possibility of Huijin using dividends to replenish bank capital should be more than 50%. In fact, it is equivalent to Huijin increasing its holdings in listed banks again. This plan has less impact on the secondary market, is highly operable, and should be one of the preferred plans for listed banks.

Let's make a bold assumption and calculation: according to the prediction that Bank of China will achieve a net profit of 78.9 billion yuan in 2009, assuming a dividend payout ratio of 50%, Huijin holds 67.5% of Bank of China's shares, the dividend will reach 26.6 billion yuan. If this fund is directly used to replenish capital, it will increase Bank of China's core and overall capital adequacy ratio by about 0.5 percentage points, alleviating some of the capital raising pressure.

Former CSRC Chairman Zhou Daoyong: Stock Index Futures will soon be launched

Zhou Daoyong, Chairman of the PECC China Financial Committee and former Chairman of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC), said on Thursday that China should focus on risk control and management, and actively and steadily develop the domestic financial derivatives market, while quickly launching stock index futures. Zhou Daoyong said at the "Fifth China (Shenzhen) International Futures Conference" that due to the underdevelopment of the domestic derivatives market, some large enterprises, especially central enterprises, have to rely more on the international financial derivatives market for hedging. However, transactions with foreign investment banks in over-the-counter markets have low transparency and poor liquidity, thus implying significant risks. He believed that transparent market environments have advantages in reducing risks and preventing default risks. Therefore, China's financial derivatives market should particularly develop exchange-traded derivatives, starting with futures products related to the real economy. Zhou Daoyong called for the acceleration of the introduction of the Futures Law and said, "Stock index futures will soon be out."

In the secondary market, stocks related to the futures concept have recently performed well. Their trends and today's relevant speeches by regulators imply the significance of these developments.
