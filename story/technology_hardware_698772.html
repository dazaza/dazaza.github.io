Eat your way to health.

by yangliu435sh on 2012-03-09 11:08:42

Today I'm going to talk to you about a revolutionary product called "raw food." When I mention raw food to 100 people, all 100 ask me what raw food is because the concept is so new. To explain raw food, I need 30 minutes of your time to fully clarify this product. Before discussing raw food, let's address the first question: why do people get sick?

There are two reasons people get sick. Twenty percent of cases are due to congenital issues, which are genetic problems that science can't yet solve. The other 80% are due to postnatal factors, which include five elements: mindset, diet, sleep, exercise, and environment—meaning the air we breathe, the water we drink, and the regions where we live. These five factors are extremely important for health, but among them, I believe diet is the most crucial. Now, I want to share with you an important piece of information: "Health comes from what we eat."

### One: Health Comes from What We Eat

Our bodies don't lie; the food we consume determines our health status. Since eating is so important, let’s examine what humans consume throughout their lives. People consume three categories of things: medicine, supplements, and daily meals.

#### Medicine

Who consumes medicine? Patients. Why do patients take medicine? To cure diseases. Here's the second key point: if someone who isn’t sick takes medicine, they have a real problem—they might be mentally ill. However, many of us, to some extent, fall into this trap. Why does this happen? There are three reasons:

1. China's medical system has issues, turning many doctors into businessmen. Doctors now have two roles: healing and selling drugs. They often ask you two questions: Is it covered by insurance or self-paid? Are you rich or poor? This determines the quantity and price of the medication prescribed.

2. Medicines have side effects, and usually, the more expensive the drug, the greater its toxicity. Many wealthy individuals today aren't dying from a lack of money for treatment but from being poisoned by excessive medication. Studies show that one-third of Chinese patients die from drug poisoning.

3. Each family has a small box filled with various medicines, and we often misuse them. Additionally, the amount of antibiotics used in China is 30-40 times higher than in developed Western countries because the animal products we consume contain large amounts of antibiotics and other chemicals due to the feed given to animals.

#### Supplements

Who should take supplements? Those who are sub-healthy. Why? To improve their condition. However, supplement companies and their salespeople often claim: “Our supplements can cure diseases if you're sick and prevent them if you're healthy.” If this logic were true, we should give supplements to 12-year-olds to prevent cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and diabetes from childhood. Clearly, this is not feasible. Furthermore, Chinese consumers' psychology when purchasing supplements is immature. We often ask three questions: Can your supplement cure diseases? Is it effective quickly? Is it cheap? If a supplement is more effective, faster acting, and cheaper than medicine, then it's essentially a drug, and likely an inferior one. Recently, a supplement was exposed that made people sleep after just two tablets—it contained banned substances no longer allowed in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia since 20 years ago. Supplements produced by unknown small companies often work faster because they contain banned substances, making them dangerous to consume.

#### Daily Meals

Who consumes daily meals? Everyone. No one can survive without eating; it's the foundation, root, essence, and source of life. Food builds our blood and flesh. So, what am I trying to convey? The pharmaceutical industry is chaotic, and the supplement industry even more so, but combined, they don't pose as big a problem as our daily meals. On our dining tables, it's almost impossible to find safe food. These delicious foods are slowly poisoning us. Let's examine the "three major problems of food."

### Two: Three Major Problems of Food

#### Problem One: Unsafe

The first issue is safety—pesticides, fertilizers, additives, and chemical residues. Today, even those who demand high-quality living consume hundreds of toxins daily. Someone who eats carelessly may consume thousands of toxins daily. Thus, there's a popular online joke: "City dwellers produce fakes, and rural residents poison them." City dwellers counterfeit goods to deceive rural residents, and rural residents retaliate by poisoning city dwellers through unsafe food.

On February 6, 2010, three vice-premiers and 14 ministers jointly established a Food Safety Committee, indicating that food safety had reached a critical point requiring urgent resolution.

#### Problem Two: Imbalance

The second issue is imbalance, divided into comprehensive imbalance and single imbalance.

First, comprehensive imbalance: Humans are omnivores, and we should consume over ten varieties of fruits, vegetables, grains, fungi, and algae daily. But we cannot achieve this. Even if we could, we shouldn't just eat the fruits but also the roots, stems, leaves, skins, and seeds. The most nutritious part of a grape isn't the pulp but the skin, and the most nutritious part is the seed, which we rarely consume. We shouldn't only eat white foods but also red, yellow, green, and black ones. We shouldn't only eat sweet foods but also sour, bitter, spicy, and salty ones. This aligns with China's 2,000-year-old health preservation culture, emphasizing that five colors and five tastes nourish the internal organs. Despite knowing this for centuries, we still fail to implement it, leading to comprehensive imbalance.

Now, let's look at single imbalance. Our staple foods today are mainly refined rice and flour. Rice and flour come from seeds (draw a seed). This part is the sprout, accounting for 3%, and this part is the bran, accounting for 5%. Inside is the endosperm, making up 92%. Modern science proves that the most meaningful aspect of "life" lies in the "birth" of active substances in food, including sprouts, chlorophyll, biological enzymes, dietary fiber, vitamins, and plant-based active substances. Sixty-six percent of these active substances are in the sprout, 29% in the bran, and only 5% in the endosperm we consume. For example, if you bury a whole seed in the soil and water it, it will sprout and regenerate. However, if you bury a seed stripped of its bran and sprout—the refined flour or rice—it will rot and not regenerate because it lacks active substances. What is in the 92% endosperm? Carbohydrates, simply put, sugar. Sugar is one of the seven essential nutrients for the human body, providing energy to sustain life when food is scarce. However, in today's China, few people die from insufficient calories but rather from excessive calories. Therefore, I suggest eating until you're only 80% full. In Japan and some developed countries, a lifestyle known as "0.8" is becoming popular—you don't need to worry about starving, but you must always remind yourself not to overeat. Consuming excess calories that you cannot burn off will turn into fat, leading to obesity and laying the groundwork for cardiovascular diseases. Eating only refined grains instead of whole grains causes single imbalance.

#### Problem Three: Low Nutrition

The third issue is low nutrition, primarily caused by the use of fire. Fire is a great invention of humanity, but modern people, especially Chinese, excessively use fire, creating a serious problem. Aside from fruit, almost everything we eat is thermally processed. The valuable active substances I mentioned earlier are destroyed by high temperatures. When the temperature reaches 20 degrees Celsius, marine algae lose their active substances; at 40 degrees, plants lose their vitamins and enzymes; at 60 degrees, biological enzymes are destroyed; at 100 degrees, folic acid is destroyed; and at 200 degrees, proteins become distorted, forming free radicals that directly cause cancer. Barbecued and fried foods we consume today are all above 200 degrees, making them carcinogenic. Additionally, highly processed packaged foods like instant noodles, sausages, and fast food are essentially junk food.

Because we consume too much unsafe, imbalanced, and low-nutrient food without a good solution, the direct consequence is disease. Let's examine the relationship between diet and disease.

### Three: Relationship Between Diet and Disease

I previously stated that health comes from what we eat, and similarly, diseases also come from what we eat. A common saying is, "Disease enters through the mouth." Let's look at two sets of data. Before 1949, the top cause of death was malnutrition—starvation. The second was inflammation due to lack of medicine and antibiotics, where pneumonia and wound infections were fatal. The third was infectious diseases due to poor public health and medical conditions. After 1990, shortly after the reform and opening-up when Chinese people just solved the problem of having enough food, they quickly caught up with Western modern civilization diseases. The order of mortality changed: cancer ranked first, followed by cardiovascular diseases, and thirdly, diabetes and its complications. The data shows that before 1949, these diseases were caused by poverty and hunger—in short, starvation. After 1990, these diseases are called "rich diseases," essentially overeating. All these overeating-related diseases are actually one type of disease: cell metabolic disorder diseases. What are cell metabolic disorder diseases? It means good cells cannot grow as fast as bad cells. Taking cancer as an example, when good cells cannot outgrow cancer cells, a person gets cancer. Similarly, diabetes and cardiovascular diseases follow the same principle.

Modern medicine proves that if cells malfunction, tissues malfunction, organs malfunction, systems malfunction, and eventually, the entire body malfunctions. Cell malfunction is 70% food-related. Comparing the mortality rates before 1949 and after 1990, you'll notice changes in food quality and lifestyle lead to changes in mortality rankings. Still taking cancer as an example, ancient Chinese discovered cancer when medical knowledge couldn't explain its causes. Through extensive clinical observation, they found that within the character for "disease," there are three mouths—one for breathing, one for eating and drinking, and one for excretion. When someone inhales too many toxins, consumes too many toxins, and fails to detoxify through the lower mouth, they develop cancer. This ancient discovery is correct. Modern medicine proves that the primary causes of cell metabolic disorder diseases are two: malnutrition—not the kind from lack of food before 1949—but from not consuming the right foods, such as not eating germ and bran or coarse grains. Instead, we feed these to pigs and chickens, which grow fast and fat but rarely develop diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, or cancer. Nowadays, treating diabetes involves feeding patients what was once fed to pigs. The second reason is excessive toxins—humans shouldn't consume fertilizers, pesticides, and chemicals, but we do, and in large quantities. This is the main cause of high-mortality diseases. Minister of Health Chen Zhu stated several months ago that diabetes, cancer, and cardiovascular diseases will surge dramatically in the future, primarily due to food quality, lifestyle, environmental pollution, and mental stress. Therefore, food quality and grade are crucial for health.
