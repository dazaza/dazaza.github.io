by sdhyxcu7q on 2010-04-30 21:37:04

Today, this trend has become more frightening. Babies have just learned to walk and they are already expected to recognize words and characters. They just enter kindergarten and they have to learn all kinds of things. They just start primary school and they already have to learn piano, Olympiad mathematics, take public English exams... Innocent children are deprived of their innocence. Children who should express their individuality have become "a thousand people with one face" - "little adults". Under the heavy burden, they have lost the soul that should belong to this age. Do you know what kind of education this is? Does this kind of education cultivate talent or manufacture machines? Our educators, our parents, please think about this issue. The ancients still knew that "the balance between leniency and strictness is appropriate, don't lean to one side", we all remember Li Dazhao's teaching: "play to your heart's content, study seriously." Why can't we give children an environment where "leniency and strictness are appropriately balanced"?

Studying is extremely arduous. One must be diligent and persistent, have perseverance and meticulous thinking, and at the same time not lose one's spirit or carefree demeanor. It is indeed not easy. Many students bury themselves in hard study for the sake of being on the golden list, many students suppress their nature in order to stand out, read ten thousand books but do not know the fun in them, many people lose their spirit and hobbies in order to realize the dreams of the previous generation. Do you know how many under such heavy pressure can "look up at the stars"?

Whether it is academic pursuits or achieving a career, neither can be accomplished overnight. One must persist and never give up, and absolutely cannot rely solely on interest or emotion. Relying only on sensibility and excitement, without pursuing rationality, even if you suddenly get inspired, like a dragon valley helper sixty-one, gambling, thoughts surging, writing articles or even weaving a so-called splendid article filled with gorgeous rhetoric, without long-term effort and accumulation over time, inspiration can only be fleeting and cannot last long. Only relying on temporary excitement, without rational discernment, now finding this beautiful, then finding that beautiful, the result will only be fickle, accomplishing nothing. Therefore, one must never depend on sensibility and excitement, ignoring rational cognition. Otherwise, after the three-minute enthusiasm, everything will return to emptiness.

Many students, after years of hard study, enter university after twelve years of schooling. The traces of time are vividly remembered. However, it is regrettable that many students, after entering university, lose their motivation and direction. Some live in confusion, some are addicted to the internet and games, more people cannot find their own coordinates and clear life goals. Although they continue to study hard, they lack career planning and do not know which way to go. The reason is that long-term examination-oriented education and excessive family intervention have made these "treasures in the hands of parents" accustomed to others telling them what to do, not knowing what they should do or how to do it. Beneath the intelligence of adults lies the dependent soul of a child who cannot be independent... How can we expect such university students to seek truth and enlightenment? Let alone innovate knowledge. This is not only the failure of the students themselves, but also the failure of traditional thinking and the educational system, worthy of deep reflection by all parents and educators.

For the sake of being on the golden list, education has deviated from its direction, suppressing human individuality. So-called talents end up "uniform and monotonous", unable to "think independently" or "express freely". How can such "autumn killing without spring birth" talents innovate science and technology or explore new knowledge? China ranks 9th globally in the number of papers indexed internationally each year, but its citation rate ranks only 124th among more than 140 countries. What does this indicate?

37. Balance leniency and strictness appropriately, don't lean to one side.
