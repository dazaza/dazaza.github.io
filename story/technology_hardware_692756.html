cadres in the meeting room fell asleep in different postures, and the county Party secretary asked the reporter to take pictures and play them on the spot

by yanv505nxue on 2012-03-06 11:24:14

In a conference held by the county Party committee and government of Binhai County with about 800 participants, a few cadres actually fell asleep, with their awkward postures making them appear very ungraceful. The party secretary sitting on stage did not react immediately but quietly arranged for the TV station reporter to take video of these sleeping individuals one by one and broadcast it on the spot, making many of those "caught" feel extremely embarrassed. This unexpected scene occurred during a work meeting in Binhai County on the morning of August 9th.

The rumor of "the party secretary rectifying meeting conduct, making sleepers lose face" spread throughout the city of Binhai yesterday. Subsequently, the reporter confirmed this matter through a vice bureau chief who attended the meeting from a certain bureau in Binhai. This attendee also said: "I didn't expect that falling asleep would really be filmed; under everyone's watchful eyes, it was truly embarrassing."

It turns out that on the morning of August 9th, Binhai County held a mobilization meeting for the "Hundred Projects Hundred Days Competition", with the main leaders of the county Party committee and government sitting on the rostrum. Attending personnel came from all deputy department-level cadres and above across the county, reaching a total of 800 people. Halfway through the meeting, the good order of the venue was disrupted. According to a vice town mayor who attended the meeting, there were people sleeping at the meeting site, with various sleeping postures. Some were dozing off while holding materials, others had fallen asleep resting their heads on the back of the front row seat.

This scene was clearly noticed by Wang Bin, the party secretary of Binhai County sitting on the rostrum. Taking advantage of the time someone was speaking, Wang Bin immediately contacted the person in charge of the Party Committee Office and instructed the TV station reporter covering the meeting to film the sleepers. Soon, the various postures of the sleepers were captured by the TV camera. At 10:30 am, Wang Bin suddenly invited everyone to watch the recording, saying, "It's a great movie, to refresh everyone's spirit." Subsequently, the screen above the rostrum began playing the footage of the sleepers, and instantly there was silence in the hall.

"These comrades who fell asleep might have been working overtime at night and are really too tired," Wang Bin said, "but it is unacceptable for leading cadres not to have a good fighting state. These people are so slack and scattered even in meetings, how can they get work done?"

At the meeting, Wang Bin required that if the sleepers caught on camera were the top leaders of their units, the county government’s divisional leader should have a warning talk with them; if they were deputy leaders, the top leader of their unit should deal with them. "If you 'show a sweet smile again in the waves,' then 'farewell my friend,'" Wang Bin humorously warned, with no lack of stern warning.

During the interview, the reporter contacted a staff member of the Binhai County Party Committee Office. He said that the number of sleepers caught on camera was not as rumored, being less than 10 people. Regarding the handling of the sleepers, the staff member stated that relevant departments were already implementing the secretary's opinion, but due to the short time frame, no results had yet come out. (Muzi Xiaochuan)
