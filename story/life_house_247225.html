Acceptance phase causes regrettable engineering

by longersing on 2009-10-13 22:56:07

Shanghai decoration companies warn everyone that many mistakes are unforeseen in advance and only discovered during the acceptance phase. As a result, it leads to regretful projects. We often encounter various kinds of regrets. Below are a few representative examples.

1. Due to limited time, a full-package decoration method was chosen, and only went to the site for acceptance. At that time, no problems were found. However, when the appliances were moved in, an issue arose. The large refrigerator in the kitchen was placed on a slanting surface. Upon moving the fridge, it was discovered that the floor was uneven. Now, cardboard has to be placed under one side of the fridge, and we can only wait for an opportunity to redo the work.

2. In pursuit of effect, large tiles were selected during the renovation and cut into smaller pieces for paving. It was agreed that the tile manufacturer would handle the work. On the delivery day, due to time constraints, only a few tiles were checked before payment was made. During the acceptance after laying, it was found that many tiles had small dents on their edges. Now, it is unclear whether it's the manufacturer's fault or the decorator's fault.

3. Due to not being thorough during the inspection, a flaw was discovered in the balcony window used for sealing the balcony upon installation and acceptance. The glass frame was peeling. Although the manufacturer agreed to replace it, a lot of time and labor costs were wasted, leaving a feeling of discomfort.

4. A bathtub purchased in advance was placed in the new house. One day, while checking the site, it was found that materials and garbage were piled inside and outside the bathtub. The toilet and other accessories were also placed inside, covered with wood shavings. The entire bathtub lacked proper protection.

5. During the demolition of the old cabinets, workers blindly knocked on the pipes, breaking the drainage pipe at its base. Water from upstairs seeped in, accumulating on the ground, increasing the construction workload, and additional fees were required for repairing the pipes.

6. When purchasing this set of cabinets initially, the overall effect seemed very good. During on-site installation, satisfaction with the overall effect was expressed. However, upon moving in and using them, it was discovered that the cabinet doors couldn't be closed tightly due to inconsistent gaps between the door panels. Therefore, attention must be paid to every detail during acceptance.

7. The sink cabinet and toilet purchased at a large mall were inspected by the girlfriend and father upon delivery due to work commitments. Concerns about the toilet led to a careful inspection, but the sink cabinet was only unpacked without detailed examination before signing off on it. Upon installation and disassembly, it was discovered that the bottom side panel of the sink cabinet was broken. Since the signature confirmed the goods, the other party refused to replace it.
