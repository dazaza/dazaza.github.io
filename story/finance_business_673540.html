by xiao1845 on 2012-02-23 16:55:42

Report on May 16: In the eyes of mainstream Italian media, the core project for Juventus' team building in the summer is the reform of the midfield, and Pirlo is the key figure in this renovation plan. Despite the fact that the Milan brain recently clearly stated that he still hopes to serve the Red and Black Army, Turin's local media "The Messenger" has gathered an important piece of intelligence: Pirlo has started looking for accommodation in Turin, which makes the future of this midfield master increasingly ambiguous.

"The Messenger": Pirlo searches for accommodation in Turin, Marotta dreams of Cassano.

Although frequently linked with news of a transfer to Juventus in recent times, and also pursued by Inter Milan and Chelsea, etc., Pirlo, whose contract with Milan will soon expire, expressed his hope for renewal through the club's official website just a few days ago. However, considering his post-tax annual salary of up to 7 million euros (including bonuses) and his tactical role in the team being far less significant than before, many Italian media outlets believe that Pirlo is merely making a final, albeit futile, effort to stay at the Rossoneri.

Moreover, a revelation from "The Messenger" further substantiates the prediction that the Milan veteran will leave in the summer. The newspaper reported that although he has said he is ready to discuss his future with Galliani, Pirlo apparently has little confidence in the outcome of these discussions. Therefore, he has already begun planning his life after leaving Milan. It was learned that last week, Pirlo had a phone call with a German friend working at Juventus headquarters, where he detailed asked about the environment of Turin and listened to some suggestions from his friend, even entrusting his close friend to look out for suitable accommodation near Juventus' training base in Vinovo. "The Messenger" indicated that this already shows that Pirlo joining Juventus has progressed from "very possible" to "almost becoming a reality."

This Turin-based newspaper further analyzed that, unlike transferring to the city rival Milan, if Pirlo joins Juventus, he would not be considered a true "traitor" by the fans of the Rossoneri, like Inter Milan's coach Leonardo. Moreover, there are Buffon, Del Piero, Chiellini, Marchisio, and other close friends of Pirlo from the Italian national team within Juventus, and these core players of Juventus have publicly welcomed Milan midfielder's participation multiple times, which undoubtedly would help this player better integrate into the new team. Of course, the generous contract offer from Juventus management is also a major reason why this transfer is getting closer to completion. "The Messenger" stated that after multiple negotiations with Juventus general manager Marotta, Pirlo's agent D'Introno has actually accepted a three-year deal with a post-tax annual salary of 4 million euros, and they have reached agreements on several key details of the contract.

As for whether Pirlo can regain his former glory after joining Juventus, "The Messenger" seems quite confident. This newspaper pointed out that due to participating in various competitions including the league, Coppa Italia, Champions League, and national team events, Pirlo often plays at least 50 matches in a season. A crucial reason why his position at Milan became marginalized this season is due to overexertion and injuries. Now, this midfield master has fully recovered his health, and for a technical player, 32 years old is not necessarily the start of decline. Therefore, Pirlo can completely handle the critical position as the midfield core of the new Juventus.

"The Messenger" also believes that Cassano, who joined Milan in the winter, might "accompany" Pirlo to join the Turin giant, although this possibility currently seems low. Although his contract with the Rossoneri extends until 2014, due to the presence of Robinho and Pato, etc., Cassano is not an iron-clad starter, which is certainly an unfavorable factor for the Barri bad boy who desires more playing time to establish himself as a core player in the new Italian national team. Moreover, the news that Kaka might return to Milan casts a shadow over the future of the former Sampdoria player, thus leading to multiple rumors in recent times that Cassano might be sold by the Rossoneri.

Despite missing out on next season's Champions League or Europa League, compared to Milan, Juventus, which is rebuilding from scratch, can better satisfy Cassano's desire for a starting position. Moreover, there are familiar faces from his Sampdoria era in the club's management, such as Marotta and Paratici. Therefore, "The Messenger" believes that the prospects of this deal are not merely speculative.
