by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 19:48:20

Looking Forward - Barcelona's Determination to Avoid Falling into the Same Trap as Three Years Ago; Messi Misses Out on Semi-Finals

Sina Sports News: Barcelona is set to embark on their FIFA Club World Cup journey today, with their opening match against Atlas widely regarded as a straightforward contest. As Guardiola put it, "In which competition isn't Barcelona the favorite?" Besides the similarity in kits (both teams wear red and blue), there are also parallels in football philosophy between the two sides. Atlas coach José Cruz is a staunch follower of former Mexican national team coach Ricardo La Volpe, who is also a coach that Guardiola greatly admires.

In the predictions, Barcelona is so heavily favored that the organizers of the Club World Cup have decided that Barcelona will wear their traditional red and blue kit, while Atlas will have to wear an indigo kit. Mexican media noted that this color scheme is not bad, evoking memories of the Caribbean Sea. In fact, both teams had initially agreed to decide on the kit colors by drawing lots.

Atlas does not seem overly concerned about this, as they only brought three fans and one journalist with them. Founded in 1916, Atlas has long been known for representing the working class, but since the 1980s, the club gradually fell into the hands of big capitalists, moving further away from ordinary people. Currently, the club is in financial difficulty, with the team even lacking a dressing room, forcing them to train on a golf course.

However, Atlas' players are relatively young and play good football. If Barcelona underestimates them, it would be a grave mistake. Barcelona's Mexican center-back Rafael Márquez stated, "Atlas has a very decent squad, especially along the spine of the team. I am familiar with their goalkeeper and striker." Interestingly, the striker for Atlas is also named Rafael Márquez, sharing the same name as Barcelona's defender. "The game will definitely not be easy," Márquez warned his teammates.

The best compliment for Atlas comes from their opponents themselves. Guardiola never takes any opponent lightly or undervalues any match. The Barcelona boss remarked, "If we don't get past the semi-final, then there won't be a final for us. You have to earn your place in the final on the pitch; everything else is meaningless."

Barcelona's trophy cabinet is filled with various cups, yet it lacks a 'Club World Cup' trophy. Historically, Barcelona has participated twice in the Club World Cup (including its predecessor, the Toyota Cup). In 1993, they lost 1-2 to São Paulo of Brazil, and in 2007, they were defeated 0-1 by Internacional of Brazil. If Barcelona can lift the trophy this time, it would fill a significant void in the club's history. Additionally, Barcelona could achieve the unprecedented feat of becoming the sextuple champions. In 2009, Barcelona already secured the Spanish League, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League, Spanish Super Cup, and UEFA Super Cup. Winning the Club World Cup would create a historic milestone.

However, Guardiola is currently uninterested in discussing the possibility of winning six titles. "I'm more focused on figuring out how to beat Atlas on the football field. If we don't concentrate on the football itself, we'll make mistakes. If we're not prepared for the semi-final, it's entirely possible we won't reach the final," he said.

Márquez also emphasized that Barcelona must avoid repeating the mistake of arrogance. In 2006, Barcelona crushed América of Mexico 4-0 in the semi-final, leading the entire team to become overconfident. As a result, they were defeated by Internacional of Brazil in the final. Guardiola also referenced the 1992 match where Barcelona lost to São Paulo, saying, "At the time, no one in the team, including the head coach, knew how to win this trophy." Psychological preparation began on Tuesday, with Guardiola looking serious, and Barcelona players understanding the importance of preparing seriously for matches against weaker opponents.

It is worth noting that Messi's absence opens the door for Pedro to make history. Although the Argentinean participated in normal training yesterday, Guardiola still considers it unlikely that he will start in the semi-final. Pedro commented, "I hope to participate in important Club World Cup matches. If I can score and be written into history, it would be perfect. I eagerly await scoring a goal, but most importantly, the team must win." Currently, Pedro has scored in five competitions: La Liga, Copa del Rey, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and UEFA Champions League. Scoring in the Club World Cup would make him the first player to score in all six competitions in a single season.

Marca predicts Barcelona's starting lineup:

Valdés / Alves, Puyol, Piqué, Abidal / Xavi, Touré, Iniesta / Pedro, Ibrahimović, Henry
