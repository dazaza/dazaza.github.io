[1] Don't wait for me to give orders before you act

After Premier Wen's previous statement "It's the people who support you, so you should look after them!", another sentence during a seminar on the government work report has once again gained popularity online: "Don't wait for me to give orders before you act."

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the government work report was 4.6%, while the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences (CASS) Institute of Sociology published a social blue book stating that the urban surveyed unemployment rate was 9.4%. Why is there a 5.2% difference between the statistics from the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security (MHRSS) and CASS? The public has become very curious about this. In response to this kind of "fighting" behavior, Premier Wen stated:

"You, the Ministry of Social Labor Security, should quickly go online, or hold a press conference quickly, and explain the differences between the 4.6% and the CASS' 9.4%. Don't wait for me to give orders before you act."

Why are these two data points different? Li Peilin, Director of the Institute of Sociology at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, explained it this way:

"For nearly two decades, there has been a discrepancy between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, with the latter usually higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, the two figures are close, but when the employment situation is poor, there will be a large gap between the two. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, such as migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, they are difficult to include in the statistics after becoming unemployed."

However, the MHRSS has a different explanation:

"The survey unemployment rate of 9.4% is the result of a sample survey of 7,000 people, among whom there are 2,288 urban economically active population, and 200 of them are unemployed. This figure is derived by comparing these two numbers."

In fact, we ordinary people have our own views on these two sets of data:

"Like me, graduates who have worked in the city for only a few years, our hukou (household registration) is still left in school, where can we register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, we can only quickly find a new job ourselves, how dare we tarnish the country's unemployment rate?"

For this calculation method that does not include people who neither work nor register, it has long been questioned by scholars both domestically and internationally. Premier Wen should make them explain not just the severely underestimated urban registered unemployment rate, but also provide us with a number that can withstand scrutiny.

[2] Is it because of "May Day"?

Some departments indeed take action without waiting for the premier's instructions. After the Guangdong provincial government officially announced the "May Day" holiday plan, Hangzhou, Suzhou, and Nanjing proposed to jointly restore the May Day Golden Week, making whether to restore the May Day Golden Week a focal point of discussion.

In response to the current heated debate, the General Office of the State Council officially responded: no localities may arbitrarily restore the May Day Golden Week.

Should the total number of working days in a year be unified across the country? Although some comments refer to Germany's central government only stipulating some unified statutory holidays, with the rest determined by each state, suggesting that the May Day holiday could be decided locally, but have they considered whether this suits China's national conditions under different political systems?

In fact, I would like to ask, why do various places make statements without reporting to the General Office of the State Council for approval?

[3] Every province can save

The Yunnan police officer shooting case, which has already attracted much attention, has caused controversy again due to a signed agreement. Mengzi County Public Security Bureau reached a reconciliation agreement with the victim's family, paying a huge compensation of 550,000 yuan to the victim's family.

Regarding the case of police officer Ji Zhongchun shooting someone, the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau originally defined it as a personal act, unrelated to his identity and position as a police officer. Since it was a personal act, why did the Public Security Bureau pay the compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it this way:

"The reason the County Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances. Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked at the County Public Security Bureau for more than 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home. Out of humanitarianism, the County Public Security Bureau gave prior humanitarian compensation to the victim's family. And this 550,000 yuan is saved office expenses from the County Public Security Bureau."

It is well known that the incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun had a dispute with someone while reversing his car. How can a police officer who owns a private car be defined as having poor economic conditions at home? This makes one weakly ask: what exactly constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the "Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police", if a police officer illegally uses weapons, causing unnecessary casualties or property damage, the relevant agency to which the police officer belongs shall compensate according to the relevant provisions of the "State Compensation Law". Why does the Mengzi Public Security Bureau refuse to admit that this compensation is state compensation? If it indeed is not, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money for "private gain"?

[6] Overhyped internet entrepreneurship

Suddenly, as if a spring breeze came overnight, millions of online shops sprouted everywhere. Amidst the current economic crisis, the internet seems to have become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only are media outlets repeatedly promoting online employment as a great path for students, but even during the Two Sessions, some representatives suggested that the state should subsidize college students starting businesses on Taobao.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, internet entrepreneurship suddenly became a darling. Many domestic colleges have set up online store training classes to help students start businesses. Yes, if everyone now follows the trend to open online stores, the employment problem can be solved immediately, but what about a year later, ten years later? How many people can truly succeed? Let alone issues like medical insurance and social security. Network security and legal standardization have always been the most neglected challenges in the information economy tide. Are our experts and professors considering this point while advocating that college students choose internet entrepreneurship?
