Professors of economics from various universities expressed that stimulating consumption through a loose credit system no longer meets the needs of future economic development. The economic crisis merely revealed the tip of the iceberg, and unplanned overproduction of goods will lead to more serious global consequences such as the greenhouse effect and energy crisis.

Churchman University and other universities pledged to use their influence in each state to promote American financial institutions to treat the issuance of credit more rigorously. At the same time, Churchman University and other universities also promised to rely on old alumni currently in the political arena to push this plan forward, taking on the responsibility that a century-old prestigious school should bear in solving the American financial crisis.
