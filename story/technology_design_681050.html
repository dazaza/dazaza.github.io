BH sponsorship achieves good results in the competition

by anonymous on 2012-02-28 10:42:50

The Qinghai Tianyoude Cycling Team, sponsored by BH, made its official debut at the 10th Tour of Qinghai Lake in 2011. CCTV broadcast the opening ceremony live on July 2nd. Apart from the Tianyoude team, no fewer than three other teams used BH bicycles, highlighting the recognition and fame of the BH brand among professional sports teams.

The Qinghai Tianyoude Cycling Team is one of China's continental cycling teams registered with the UCI, named after the Qianghai Mutual Barley Wine Factory that sponsors it. The team members are mostly native to Qinghai, including outstanding road cyclist Ma Haijun, as well as rising stars Dong Xiaoyong, Wu Shengjun, and Liu Biao. They have trained and competed multiple times in Europe, significantly improving their technical skills, making them competitive in various Asian events.

Cycling in Qinghai as a competitive sport began after the founding of the People’s Republic of China. On September 25, 1952, during the first provincial games, a cycling performance team consisting of more than ten workers showcased various cycling stunts on the streets, drawing cheers from the crowd. Since then, cycling has been included as a competition event in every provincial games. In August 1958, a professional cycling team was officially established. By October 1993, many excellent athletes such as Man Jintang, Li Fei, Li Yanmei, and Tan Zhengshun had emerged, with ten becoming national athletes and one becoming a senior coach. They achieved top-eight finishes 98 times in various national competitions, including nine first-place finishes, fourteen second-place finishes, eighteen third-place finishes, and fifty-seven fourth to eighth-place finishes. In international competitions, they won first place once and second place once. Six people visited twelve countries including the United States, Japan, France, and the Philippines. The entire team represented China in the "Marlboro Cup" invitational tournament held in Hong Kong, contributing to friendship between the Chinese people and those of other countries. During the project adjustment in the Seventh National Games in 1993, the cycling team was disbanded.

In January 2002, the Qinghai Provincial Sports School re-established the cycling team, initially comprising one coach and six athletes. In January 2006, the team moved to the Second Provincial Sports Brigade. After two years of effort, in December 2008, it grew into one of the professional teams registered with the International Cycling Union, known as the "Qinghai Tianyoude Cycling Team." Currently, the team consists of one head coach, two coaches, one mechanic, one team doctor, seven registered professional team members, and twelve second-line members, including two very promising female athletes.

To catch up with world-class teams, the Provincial Sports Bureau has sent several locally-born athletes like Ma Haijun, Liu Biao, Dong Xiaoyong, and Wu Peilun abroad for training and participation in various European competitions, significantly enhancing their competitive level, physical fitness, and match experience, achieving excellent results in numerous competitions. Ma Haijun, an outstanding athlete, has won four first places and one fifth place in international competitions since 2006; five first places, four second places, one third place, and six fourth to eighth places in domestic competitions. On June 29, 2008, he won the individual time trial championship at the World Road Cycling Championship B-level held in South Africa, marking the first world champion in cycling. In October 2009, he won the silver medal in the individual event at the 11th National Games, securing the only silver medal for the Qinghai delegation. Young athlete Wu Shengjun won the championship in the first stage of the Tour of Qinghai Lake International Road Cycling Race in July 2009, achieving the best result since the inception of the race. He has also placed second once and fourth to eighth twice in domestic competitions. Liu Biao placed eighth overall in Asia at the Tour of Qinghai Lake International Road Cycling Race in July 2008, winning third place once and fourth to sixth place four times in domestic competitions. Together with Dong Xiaoyong, they placed sixth in the Madison race. Wu Peilun placed fifth once and seventh once in domestic competitions and has since been transferred to the PLA team for further training, showing significant improvement in his technical level. Wei Yuan placed seventh and eighth once each in domestic competitions. In March 2009, the Provincial Sports Bureau sent young athletes Liu Biao and Wu Peilun to France for a three-month training session.

The Qinghai Tianyoude Cycling Team is still a sapling among the world's professional cycling teams and relatively young in the Asian professional teams, requiring further cultivation and growth. However, its establishment marked the beginning of a domestically-based team becoming a UCI professional cycling team, a source of pride for Qinghai people. As a remote province, Qinghai took advantage of the Tour of Qinghai Lake to establish a professional cycling team domestically, promoting the development of cycling in our country, publicizing Qinghai, and advancing the social, economic, and cultural sports development of our province. Now, our cycling athletes wearing professional uniforms are confident in achieving excellent results in future major competitions, bringing new glory to the cycling project on the Qinghai Plateau.
