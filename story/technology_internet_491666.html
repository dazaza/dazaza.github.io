How are you?

by dpwtaoiux on 2011-09-19 18:33:45

Deep in my memory, when I was a child, every time someone bullied me and hurt me, you always went to the school to confront them, and had conflicts with others! Watching your figure, listening to your teachings, made me learn to be strong, not to care about others' cold stares, to prove myself with one certificate after another, to walk my own path with pride! To live a life that makes you proud!

Grandpa, are you doing well? My longing for you is like an overwhelming flood. I really miss you so much. Every time I think of you, my throat gets choked up, and my heart starts to ache. Longing, self-reproach, and guilt overwhelm me and don't dissipate easily. Although I know my clumsy writing can't express everything, and words seem so powerless in front of the most true and sincere feelings. But I can no longer tell you in person. At this moment, my heart and hands act involuntarily. Please allow me to use my inferior words to express my longing and regret for you....

Every time my mom did something harmful to me, although you couldn't be there to solve it in person, you called her countless times and scolded her severely, strongly demanding that I leave and abandon them! I know that apart from you, no one else cares about my feelings and future. When I was forced to self-harm, you received the call thinking it was my mom committing suicide, and all you could hear on the phone was cursing, saying it would be better if she died sooner. But when you found out it was me who attempted suicide, your tone instantly turned gentle and sorrowful, even causing your blood pressure to rise and making you dizzy. It's my disrespect, once again, I apologize!

It has been more than half a year since you left, but your kindness and love for me have become clearer in my mind. Especially in recent days, missing you has become an indispensable part of my life. The longing is no longer limited to each festival related to honoring you; it can be triggered by a plot in a TV drama, or even some text, making me think of you, my heart surging like waves, filled with regret, tears streaming down my face.

As the saying goes: "The tree wants to be still but the wind won't stop; the child wants to support but the parents are no longer around!" Death is so cruel, leaving no room for assumptions or chances to correct mistakes! But if there is another life, I will still be your granddaughter, living happily with you. Sitting by the bed hearing your stories, walking with you on the streets, playing chess together during our leisure time, laughing happily. Cherishing every moment we spend together, I won't be reckless and make you sad, upset, or angry as I used to! Beloved grandpa, I will always remember and miss you. Rest in peace!

In my childhood, no one cared for me. Everyone thought that without a father or mother, I was nothing and unlikely to amount to anything in the future. Only you never gave up on me, loving me! Whatever other children had, you tried hard to give me too. When I craved snacks and grandma wouldn't give them to me, you secretly bought them and put them in my schoolbag halfway. You often took me around, I followed you eagerly wearing shorts. You never looked down on me, as long as I wasn't a burden in your eyes!

After that time when I failed in business and the shop closed down, my mood was hard to recover. Upon knowing the truth of my failure, my heart felt dead. Under your guidance, I moved back to live with you. Looking at your white hair and how you struggled to move after having a stroke, I felt so guilty, realizing suddenly how many years I had spent away from home, leaving you lonely for so long! Seeing you drag out a pile of things you saved for me from under the bed, my heart warmed up so much. In order to take care of you in the future and repay your love, at that moment, I vowed to start anew.

Until now, I believe that the last time I saw you, you knew your life was nearing its end. You urged me to go back and work hard, to cherish myself and live well. But I didn't expect that this farewell would be eternal, how foolish I was! Hearing the news of your passing, I collapsed! I was in such pain, feeling like I was dying. Rolling and crawling, crying and shouting, but I couldn't see you one last time because doctors said any movement might endanger my life. During those days, I ate furiously and took good care of myself, wanting to recover quickly to see you, but it was too late! Only then did I realize how painful it is to lose someone you love, just like what Mr. Yu Minhong said in his motivational speech!

Later, I went to Hubei and Beijing. You were so supportive yet worried about me. No matter how unwell you were, you always said everything was fine over the phone! By the time I returned from my journey in Beijing, you had added many more white hairs! I was too selfish, not cherishing the time we had together enough, not spending enough time with you, it was all my fault!

Every time I made a mistake, you didn't scold me but taught me patiently. Even when you knew I was wrong, you stood by my side. You said you were my shelter from the storm, a place to rest when I felt wronged or tired outside, where I always had a place as long as you were there! Whether I was disheartened or arrogant, you cared for and protected me differently!

The following days were so peaceful and comfortable! I was lazy, even skipping meals sometimes to satisfy my laziness, but you went to the market every day to buy fresh vegetables for me and sent them over. You often cooked them and delivered them to me. Whatever you thought was delicious, you kept a portion for me. Even when you stewed soup, you would pack it in a thermal container and send it to me! Whatever I asked for, you bought it for me! If you heard of something beneficial for my health, no matter how far, you would find it and ask about it! Anything I wanted, whether it was tables or cabinets or anything else at home, you let me take it! Back then, we quietly hid in the room eating egg tarts. Every time grandma wasn't paying attention, I stuffed your pocket with goodies, only regretting that I couldn't give you anything meaningful back then!

When I returned to the familiar yet strange home and looked at your portrait, I could no longer hear your voice or see your figure. I could only sit on the chair you usually sat on and quietly recall your kindness. My heart aches so much, but I must endure it. I cannot show it in front of grandma. I know that after you passed away, the person you were most concerned about was grandma, so it's my responsibility to take care of her. Although I can't replace you in taking care of her, showing concern and cherishing her, as long as I am capable, I will definitely honor grandma! Please forgive me, I truly can't stay there longer because I can't bear the pain of missing you so deeply! Therefore, I avoided visiting your grave during the Qingming Festival, only burning paper money for you from afar. Even on the 14th day of the seventh lunar month, I avoided facing it! Until now, I cannot accept that you are forever gone, until now, I feel like you are still here! Every time I want to call you, when I open the phone, I realize there is no line that can connect me to you anymore!

That year, when I was less than a year old, I lived with you. Your serious preference for boys over girls never affected me, and I believe this is fate! Since I was a child, I've been unhealthy, cried a lot, was capricious and disobedient, causing you a lot of trouble!
