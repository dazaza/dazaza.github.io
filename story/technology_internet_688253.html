Teach you to master the basic knowledge of home safety and fire prevention 18671079388

by hbjndfwang on 2012-03-03 09:12:07

1. Be familiar with the geographical environment of where you live, and ensure that the passage is unobstructed at any time;

2. Understand the fire-fighting equipment and usage methods in your living place;

3. Do not overload the circuit. If you need to add indoor appliances, they must be installed by a qualified electrical technician;

4. Do not store any flammable or hazardous materials indoors;

5. Indicate the location of the main power switch to family members. In case of a fire, cut off the power supply promptly;

6. When using gas, open the windows as much as possible to prevent accidents in case of gas leakage. When stopping the gas, close the main valve;

7. Regularly check electrical appliances, wires, and rubber hoses connected to gas. If any damage is found, replace them immediately;

8. In case of a fire, remain calm, report it to the fire department immediately, and correctly indicate the source of the fire;

9. Different types of combustibles should be extinguished with different types of fire extinguishers: wood, paper, etc., should be extinguished with pressurized water extinguishers; oil, fuel, gasoline, and other flammable liquids should be extinguished with dry powder or foam extinguishers; electric wires, electrical appliances, motors should be extinguished with dry powder or carbon dioxide extinguishers.
