Eight good eating habits are essential for a long life.

by zhouzhou56 on 2011-12-23 12:09:53

1. Drink two glasses of water before each meal. This way, you can keep your body in a "well-hydrated" state and also control your food intake. A Dutch study showed that drinking two glasses of water before meals can reduce hunger and food consumption, thus helping with weight loss.

2. After eating fast food, drink a large glass of water. Fast food usually contains excessive calories and salt. While we can't do much about the fat we've already consumed, a large glass of water can help dilute the concentration of sodium in your body, keeping you further away from high blood pressure.

3. Don't miss any opportunity to eat onions. Many people carefully pick out onions from their dishes, trying to avoid them as much as possible. This is a big mistake. Onions contain large amounts of flavonoids that protect the heart, so eating onions should be our responsibility. Especially when eating less healthy foods like grilled meat, the onion inside can be your "lifesaving herb."

4. If possible, steep black tea in cool boiled water. Recent research by the U.S. Department of Agriculture found that compared to greens or carrots, a serving of black tea contains more antioxidants, which can effectively help you fight against wrinkles or cancer. Cool boiled water allows beneficial substances in tea to slowly dissolve without being destroyed, all you need to do is wait a little longer.

5. Have a timely snack at 3 PM. Perhaps sleeping in can be an excuse for skipping breakfast, but the snack at 3 PM cannot be postponed for any reason. Supplementing nutrients between lunch and dinner can help you get through the most tiring part of the day. Yogurt, fruit, and cookies are all good choices.

6. Set a daily water-drinking goal. Busy work may make you only remember to drink water when your mouth feels dry, realizing you haven't had any water all morning. Prepare a 1.5-liter bottle on your desk, pour the water you need to drink during the day into it, and set a rule that you can't leave work until you finish drinking it.

7. Eat more "dipped" vegetables. It's not just grilled meat that has high calories; sauce can also add many calories to otherwise healthy cold dishes. Therefore, place the prepared sauce in a small bowl and dip the cut vegetables into it to eat. This way, you only need 1/6 of the original amount of sauce.

8. Some "vegetarian" dishes should be eaten "meaty." Eating too much oil is bad, but eating none at all is even worse. Pumpkins and carrots contain large amounts of beta-carotene, so they shouldn't be cooked too plainly. They can be stir-fried with oil or made into a cold dish. If you use pumpkin to cook porridge, then ensure other dishes have oil so they can meet in the gastrointestinal tract.
