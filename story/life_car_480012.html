Car Beauty: Tire Cooling Should Avoid Watering

In the scorching summer, not only do people suffer from the heat, but cars also face difficulties. How can we help our beloved cars survive the hot summer and avoid mechanical failures? In terms of daily maintenance, the following points should be prevented:

(1) Prevent over-rich air-fuel mixture. Due to high temperatures, gasoline flows easily, and the orifice expands, increasing the gasoline flow and making it more likely to evaporate, which can lead to an overly rich air-fuel mixture. Therefore, the orifice should be adjusted smaller, the acceleration device and throttle arm connection position should be adjusted, and the float chamber oil level height should be appropriately reduced to decrease the fuel supply.

(2) Prevent vapor lock. In the hot summer, gasoline engines are prone to vapor lock due to rising temperatures, causing insufficient fuel supply or even interrupted fuel supply. To prevent vapor lock, asbestos pads can be used to separate the gasoline pump from the exhaust pipe, or wet cloths can be used to wrap around the gasoline pump to cool it down.

(3) Prevent evaporation. Oil and water evaporate faster at high temperatures, so the gas cap should be tightly closed, and oil pipes should be checked for leaks. Regularly check the water tank water level, crankcase oil level, brake master cylinder brake fluid level, and battery electrolyte density and liquid level height. If they do not meet the specifications, they should be added or adjusted promptly.

Fully Equipped: Cool Driving Solutions for Summer

Even before May, the sweltering summer has already arrived. Just thinking about the long lines of cars on the scorching asphalt makes one feel restless. Have you prepared your summer clothing? Your car needs it too! Quickly remove the furry steering wheel cover, give your car a cool summer outfit, and put on sunglasses. Be fully equipped to welcome the blazing sun!

Window Tinting

To block out the scorching ultraviolet rays, many car owners consider tinting their windows first. However, if you drive eastward in the morning and westward in the evening, there’s nowhere to hide from the enthusiastic sun! Some women fear sun exposure so much that they tint even the front windshield. The front windshield requires high light transmittance and excellent UV and infrared blocking rates, so front windshield film holds an irreplaceable position in the world of anti-explosion films, unmatched by curtains. Additionally, placing a reflective board on the front windshield after parking can maintain "shade."

Warm Reminder: The installation of anti-explosion film requires specialized tools and dust-free workshops. If the installation environment is dusty, it will inevitably cause dust to adhere between the anti-explosion film and the car window glass, leading to poor adhesion and partial shadows.

Cushions

In summer, sweating is common, and to prevent wrinkles on the back of clothes, some girls like to add a cushion behind them. Last year, a popular type of cushion made of mesh fabric was favored because it allowed smooth airflow and provided a cool sensation. However, it posed safety hazards. The cushion creates a gap of about 10 centimeters between the shoulders and the seat, increasing the distance between the head and the headrest to over 20 centimeters. In case of a traffic accident, especially a rear-end collision, the head may undergo rapid whip-like motion, and the vulnerable neck can easily get injured.

Air Conditioning

The key to staying cool while driving in summer is the air conditioning. When restarting the air conditioning after a six-month hiatus, it's best to perform a sterilization and odor removal process. Long-term disuse of the air conditioner can lead to the growth of fungi and mold, which not only causes unpleasant moldy smells but also harms the health of the occupants inside the car. This can be handled at a repair shop or with self-purchased sterilization and odor removal spray.

When using the air conditioning, the replacement of air inside the car exceeds the normal rate by two to five times. Most people will experience dizziness and fatigue after being in such an environment for a few hours, and memory may also decline. When the air conditioning is on, passengers should not smoke inside the car. If smoking is necessary, adjust the air conditioning ventilation control to the "external circulation" position. If the car has been exposed to direct sunlight in a parking lot, open all the windows first, activate the external circulation mode to expel the hot air, and wait until the cabin temperature drops before closing the windows and turning on the air conditioning.

If the vehicle is stationary while using the air conditioner, the danger increases significantly. This is because the air conditioner draws carbon monoxide gas emitted by the engine into the car, and the oxygen inside the car decreases rapidly as the occupants exhale large amounts of carbon dioxide. After a few hours, the people inside the car may experience nausea, unconsciousness, and in severe cases, even suffocation and death.
