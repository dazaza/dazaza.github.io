Singapore Universal Studios Guide

On March 18, 2010, Universal Studios finally opened in Singapore. Wow, my favorite Shrek has his own theme area called "Far Far Away"~ My husband kindly agreed to take me to play in Singapore.

Before going, I couldn't find any information online. Since it was just opening, you could say that my trip to Universal Studios was like being a guinea pig for Chinese tourists, haha.

Enough chit-chat, here's the information.

One, Special Tips:

Language. Although 70% of Singaporeans are Chinese, it's best if you can speak and understand a bit of English. All performances and announcements in Universal Studios are in English. Also, they refer to Mandarin as 'Huayu'.

Questions. Wherever you go or whatever you do, don't hesitate to ask questions if there's anything unclear. The people in Singapore are very friendly and will be happy to help you.

Exchange Money. The exchange rate at the airport isn't much worse than in the city. If you're heading straight from the airport to Sentosa, you can exchange money right there. When exchanging money in the city, compare rates and avoid those Indians who only have a small counter instead of a proper shop. I admit I made a stupid mistake once and regretted it.

Universal Studios Operating Hours. From 9 am to 6 pm. It's better to go early to avoid the heat.

Size. Compared to Hong Kong Disneyland, Universal Studios is even smaller. If you just walk around without visiting stores or playing games, I think you can finish the whole park in twenty minutes. So don't worry about not having enough time from nine to six. Of course, this doesn't apply if you're crazy about souvenir shops.

Tax Refund. In Singapore, all purchases include a 7% sales tax, and dining may incur additional fees based on the restaurant's level (similar to a surcharge), many prices quoted don't include taxes, so always check before buying. Tourists can claim tax refunds, but only for shopping - food and accommodation aren't eligible. When purchasing items in Universal Studios, inform the cashier that you're a tourist and want a tax refund, asking them to print a tax refund slip for you (remember this). Keep your receipts and refund slips together with those from purchases in the city to process your refund at the airport. I might write another post specifically about tax refunds because it can be quite troublesome.

Casino. Sentosa has a new casino near Universal Studios (about a 5-minute walk). I feel like Universal Studios closes early to encourage everyone to go to the casino~ There are dress codes for the casino; you can't enter wearing shorts or slippers (I think short skirts and sandals should be fine). You also need to show your travel documents to enter - no documents, no entry! (Local Singaporeans have to buy a $100 SGD tax ticket to enter, sweat!)

Hygiene. I don't need to elaborate on how strict Singapore's laws are. Please give the Chinese people some credit and absolutely do not spit anywhere, smoke in non-smoking areas, or litter!

Two, Transportation.

Universal Studios Singapore is located in Sentosa, which is an island-like resort area. There are various ways to get there from the city center, as many travel guides would suggest, so I won't go into too much detail. However, if you're coming from the airport to Sentosa with a lot of luggage, the most convenient method is to take a taxi. Taxis come in different types with varying starting fares, usually around $2.80 SGD, plus an additional airport fee of about $3 SGD at the airport. In total, it costs approximately $30 SGD to reach Sentosa, taking about twenty minutes. It's a bit expensive, but saves you about 1.5 hours compared to taking the MRT and bus. Everyone can decide according to their budget.

*There's an uncle at the airport's taxi stand directing traffic. Before getting in, you can tell him you want the cheaper type of taxi, so make sure you don't accidentally get into the more expensive one and blame me later.

*Tourists entering Sentosa have to pay an entrance fee, similar to our island fee. But if you're staying in a hotel inside, you can inform the driver in advance, who will then let the toll booth know, and you won't have to pay. If you're using other transportation methods, I'm not sure. Haha.

Besides Universal Studios, Sentosa has many other attractions such as casinos, the giant Merlion statue, cable cars, and beaches. If you plan to stay on Sentosa for two days, these places are worth visiting. You can take the free monorail on the island, which is circular, each station representing an attraction (except the beach). You can buy individual tickets or package deals for discounts.

Three, Hotels.

Singapore's Universal Studios Singapore is part of the Resorts World Sentosa project costing $4.32 billion USD, officially opening on March 18, 2010. It's Southeast Asia's first and only Universal Studios theme park. Singapore's Universal Studios will feature a total of 24 rides and attractions, 18 of which are specifically designed or modified for Singapore.

In conjunction with Universal Studios, four themed hotels also opened: Crockford Hotel; Hotel Michael; Singapore Hard Rock Hotel; Festive Hotel. All hotels are close to Universal Studios, about a five-minute walk away. Among them, the Festive Hotel is centrally located between Universal Studios, the casino, and the Festive Walk, making it easy to find wherever you want to go.

Hotels can be booked online through the official website of Resorts World Sentosa: http://www.rwsentosa.com

Of course, there are other hotels on Sentosa Island. Staying at other hotels will be further from Universal Studios, requiring the use of the monorail to reach the park. The monorail within the island is free.

Four, Universal Studios.

*Tickets must be purchased in advance, bookable online (official website of Resorts World Sentosa: http://www.rwsentosa.com)

*At any store within the park, you can request that your souvenirs be delivered to the main store at the exit, where you can collect everything at once upon leaving, free of charge.

*Remember to ask for a tax refund form when shopping.

Inside the park, there are seven themed areas: (in visiting order)

Hollywood

Upon entering the gate, you'll see Hollywood Boulevard. First, head to the customer service center on the left with your admission ticket to exchange for food and gift vouchers (each ticket gets you a $5 SGD gift voucher and a $10 SGD food voucher). These vouchers can be used directly without needing to spend a certain amount. Make sure to grab them!

Hollywood Boulevard resembles Disney's Main Street USA, with many souvenir shops and restaurants. You can save exploring it for your way back.

Madagascar

Alex the lion and his friends, the penguins from Madagascar, are featured here. The main ride is the Crate Escape, but it wasn't open on the day I went, which was disappointing.

King Julien's Dance Party is a live performance where the Madagascar characters sing and dance. It's very entertaining. Check with the staff for exact show times.

Far Far Away

This is my favorite.

Shrek 4D Adventure. Everyone knows what 3D is, but 4D means you move along with the action. It's highly recommended despite the wait.

Donkey's TALK Show is an interactive talk show in English, which might be challenging for some.

Dragon Training Academy is a ride.

Magic Menagerie is a small Ferris wheel inside Granny's Magic Shop (a souvenir shop).

Granny's Magic Shop sells many exclusive Shrek-themed souvenirs, so if you see something you like, grab it quickly. I missed out on a super cute mini Shrek nodding doll, and by the time I went back, it was sold out. Regretful.

The Lost World

Jurassic Park, no introduction needed~

Jurassic Rapids. Before entering, there's an Indian uncle selling raincoats for $1.50 SGD each at the entrance, quite expensive, but those who don't buy might regret it since most people come out soaking wet. If you don't mind getting wet, go ahead. It's fun, definitely worth it.

Skyline Flight. A ride where you fly over Jurassic Park with your feet off the ground.

DinoSoar is a ride for kids.

Amber Rock Climbing. The only game in the park that requires extra payment. It's rock climbing on a fake fossil wall.

WaterWorld. An English high-difficulty water stunt show with explosive scenes, very impressive. The seating area is divided into fully wet, slightly wet, and dry zones. Choose wisely before sitting down~

Check with the staff for the exact WaterWorld show times and arrive early to secure your spot (if you want to sit in the fully wet zone, haha).

Ancient Egypt

Mummy movie set, spectacular!

Revenge of the Mummy. An indoor roller coaster, fantastic. Before entering, you must store all your bags in lockers. Free lockers are available on the left at the entrance for the first 30 minutes. Why? Because it's dark and scary inside, wow!

Treasure Hunts. Kids can drive their own jeeps to explore the Egyptian desert. There were too many people waiting, so I didn't get to try it. Those who did, please let me know if it's fun, thanks.

Carter's Treasure Trove. A souvenir shop selling replicas of ancient Egyptian artifacts, beautifully crafted!

Sci-Fi City

Seems like this area is sponsored by Panasonic, its logo is everywhere. When I visited, nothing was open except the gift shop, disappointing.

This area features a creatively designed and world's tallest dueling roller coaster (which unfortunately was closed due to a malfunction a few days before my visit on April 1st, so I didn't get to try it~). One rider sits while the other hangs, passing each other in mid-air. This thrilling roller coaster includes sudden twists, unexpected drops, and close encounters, guaranteed to make you scream. If anyone has tried it, please let me know how it was, thank you.

New York

Replicates some New York street scenes. Great for photos.

"Spielberg's Movie Effects Theater", incredibly exciting and worth it. Try to stand in the front row near the water for the best experience.

Stage 28. Go behind the scenes to experience the charm of the eighth art.

Hollywood

Last stop for shopping and photos. Don't miss any of the shops.

Hollywood Theatre. Live show. Check with the staff for specific showtimes.

That's it, back to the entrance.

To summarize, although Singapore's Universal Studios is not large, the entire park is meticulously crafted, and the staff is very friendly, far surpassing Hong Kong Disneyland. Kudos! I plan to visit again once all the attractions are fully operational.

Also, if you happen to encounter your favorite cartoon character on the way, be sure to take a photo with them.

First-time guide writer, any suggestions are welcome.

I hope this guide can provide some help. Thank you~

Have fun~
