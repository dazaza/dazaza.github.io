Here are some car maintenance tips.

A. Glass

When it comes to glass maintenance, you might find it strange: what maintenance could there be for glass besides washing it with water? In fact, glass maintenance is quite scientific, and not all issues can be solved by simply rinsing with water. For example, oil stains on the glass may not be visible to the naked eye, but they can greatly reduce the efficiency of the wiper blades. At this point, you need to use degreasing cleaning agents to remove these oils. There are also permanent scratches on the windshield caused by sand particles trapped in the wipers, and injuries caused by pebbles hitting the glass. Advanced materials and technologies can polish and repair these damages.

B. Paintwork

Body maintenance mainly refers to paintwork care. Dust and wind and rain during trips cause significant damage to your beloved car's paintwork, so thorough cleaning is essential. Then, professional asphalt removal water should be used to clean asphalt spots on your car. If there are minor scratches, use polishing sandpaper for local polishing and polishing. After all this is done, you can proceed with the whole car waxing and polishing process to fully restore the youth of your car's paintwork.

C. Interior

Self-driving trips are unforgettable, especially when traveling in a completely unfamiliar environment, lowering the window to observe pedestrians and streets outside the car feels very novel. But since the windows are open for a long time in the wild, a lot of dust accumulates inside the car, especially on the carpet, roof, and door decorative panels wrapped in velvet, where accumulated dust is hard to handle. This requires professional steam cleaners to perform dry cleaning on these parts. Other parts of the cabin, such as the dashboard, although less likely to accumulate dust, corners and crevices should still be cleaned with a cloth or cotton swab to avoid dust accumulation.

D. Seals

There is one thing that is easily overlooked, namely the maintenance of various rubber edges and strips on the car. Don't underestimate these rubber edges and strips; they not only decorate the body but also play a vital role in sealing the cabin. Therefore, once these sealing strips age or crack, they will severely affect the waterproofing and soundproofing performance of your beloved car. We should use special strip cleaning agents to clean and maintain these strips. Pay special attention to important areas like the rubber strips on both sides of the car window glass, as neglecting their maintenance can lead to aging affecting the raising and lowering of the glass or causing unusual noises.

E. Air Conditioning

Air conditioning maintenance is divided into two aspects. One is carefully checking the air conditioning refrigerant tank located at the front of the car after returning from a self-driving trip to see if it has leaked due to bumps or collisions. If it has leaked, it must be repaired and refrigerant added; the second situation is due to harsh environments during the trip, causing a large amount of dust to accumulate in the air conditioning radiator and pipes. For some minivans and station wagons, the air conditioning radiator does not share with the engine radiator, so after cleaning the engine radiator, don't forget to clean the air conditioning radiator. As for the dust accumulated in the air conditioning pipes, although it cannot be directly touched, a dedicated cleaning agent can be injected directly into the air conditioning intake port, then the air conditioning inner circulation turned on to remove the dust in the pipes. This cleaning agent is non-corrosive, so there's no need to worry about damaging plastic components in the pipes.

Rain Wipers: You may not have noticed, but the originally fine wipers suddenly fail to clean water properly after the trip. The culprit is the intense sunlight and a large amount of sand and gravel encountered during the journey. First, you should determine if the angle of the wiper blade is correct, then clean the wiper blade to remove fine sand and gravel. If none of this works, it means the blade has aged and needs replacement.
