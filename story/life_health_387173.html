What are the advantages of dental implants over dentures?

by 454kq on 2011-03-23 10:27:30

Dental implants do not have the hooks or caps of traditional denture methods. They are like new grown teeth, very comfortable without denture base, so there is no foreign body sensation and they do not affect pronunciation. They do not affect healthy adjacent teeth. They exist independently by their own tooth roots and do not grind the healthy teeth on both sides. There are no side effects in the oral cavity.

Artificial dental implants are not about planting teeth, but inserting metal tooth roots into the jawbone. After the metal tooth root is closely combined with the alveolar bone, false teeth are installed on it. Just like building a house, a deep foundation must be laid before the house is solid and stable. Artificial dental implants are more stable than traditional false teeth and are less likely to move, which makes them more popular. Dental implants have three major advantages over traditional false teeth:

1. When one or a few teeth are missing, traditional fixed dentures need to grind down the two teeth next to the missing tooth area as pillars for stabilizing the crown so that the denture will be firm. While removable dentures need to make a large baseplate as a base, making people feel uncomfortable and have a sense of foreign objects. Artificial dental implants can directly implant artificial dental implants in the missing tooth area to fix the denture without damaging other teeth, and without foreign objects or discomfort.

2. In the case of complete loss of molars in the posterior part, only removable dentures could be installed in the past. However, the chewing function of removable dentures is poor, and the metal hook of the denture is easy to trap dirt, difficult to clean, and the metal hook is also easy to expose, affecting the appearance. Artificial dental implants do not have the above disadvantages.

3. When all teeth are missing, full-mouth dentures must be made. Traditional dentures rely only on the support of the mucosa soft tissue of the gums, so the fixing force is weak. Dentures are prone to looseness, and the chewing function is not good, only restoring three to four tenths of the normal function. Moreover, if the gums are improperly stressed, the alveolar bone will gradually atrophy, and the effect of dentures will become worse and worse. Artificial dental implants will not move or have poor chewing function, and the functional stimulation of dental implants on the alveolar bone in the implanted area delays the absorption of the alveolar bone. Therefore, some people in the medical community call artificial dental implants the third set of human teeth.

The biggest advantage of dental implants is that their chewing efficiency is higher than traditional dentures, making you feel very real, just like real teeth.
