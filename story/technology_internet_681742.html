Approaching the Olympiad, Hua Competition, and Hope Cup: Mastering problem-solving patterns for early preparation

by edurqusr on 2012-02-28 15:12:37

The Math Olympiad, Hua Cup, and Hope Cup are approaching. What are the must-know test points for these three major math competitions held every year? How can one prepare for the math competition to achieve high scores? At 7 PM on February 16th, on the sixth floor of Huada Mall in Gaoshengqiao, Chengdu, Professor Zhong Bo, an authority on math Olympiads in Sichuan Province, and his team of teachers imparted the secrets to succeeding in math Olympiads to nearly 200 parents and students on site.

Grasping the patterns of exam questions and breaking them down one by one

At the scene, Professor Zhong Bo summarized the types of competition questions over the years, including calculation, application, analysis and reasoning, typical questions, unconventional questions, and more. He also outlined common and likely test points such as concentration problems, profit problems, graphic problems, travel problems, and meeting problems.

Professor Zhong Bo said that mathematics has its own knowledge system. With nearly three months left until the competition, it is necessary to focus on training and establish a complete knowledge structure system, breaking down topics one by one. Additionally, students should cultivate their mathematical thinking and make full use of methods such as translation, folding, assumption, reasoning, restoration, reverse inference, and proportion to simplify problems.

Professor Zhong reminded candidates not to think that only Chinese language requires reading; mathematics also involves reading, which means learning how to understand questions, identifying known conditions, listing unknown conditions, and finally seeking solutions. When entering the competition examination room, besides being thoroughly familiar with textbook knowledge, it's important to maintain a good mindset and not get anxious when encountering difficult problems. [If you cannot solve a competition question, others may not be able to either!]

[Grade 4] [Grade 5] Benefits of Preparing Early

To participate in the three major math competitions, many [Grade 4], [Grade 5] students enroll each year. For these students, Teacher Zuo Kai from Professor Zhong Bo's math Olympiad team suggested that students can engage in self-study and fully grasp the knowledge of Grade 6 within the next three months.

Teacher Zuo Kai said that much of the knowledge between [Grade 4], [Grade 5], and [Grade 6] is interconnected. Under proper guidance, this knowledge can be transformed into familiar computational methods for students.

Some parents worry whether participating in competitions will affect regular studies. Teacher Zuo Kai said that parents need not worry. Preparing for the math Olympiad in advance serves as a preview of Grade 6 content, which is beneficial for future studies. It also helps cultivate students' ability to learn independently, enhancing their adaptability and resilience. If students can reasonably balance their study of mathematics with other subjects, it will greatly benefit their academic advancement.
