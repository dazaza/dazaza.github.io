Whose crimson maple leaves are dyed red?

"Knock, knock, knock," a boy stood in front of the door, his slender fingers lightly tapping on it, with a faint smile on his face.

The door opened, and a middle-aged beautiful woman pushed it open to see the boy. She smiled: "Xiao Feng, are you here to find Qian again?" The boy scratched the back of his head, slightly embarrassed, and said: "Auntie, hello!" The middle-aged beautiful woman saw the boy's shy expression and teased him: "When will you call me mother-in-law?" The boy quickly pretended not to hear and rushed upstairs in three quick steps. Every time he came, he was teased, and no matter how thick-skinned he was, he couldn't handle it.

At the corner of the staircase, a girl was about to go downstairs when the hurried boy almost collided with her full-on. "Qian!" The boy whispered softly. The girl lowered her voice, revealing a faint warning: "We're just pretending for our parents; don't call me so affectionately!" A trace of disappointment flashed in the boy's eyes but was covered up instantly: "Let's go out!"

The girl pouted slightly and turned around angrily, ignoring the boy behind her. The boy quickly caught up: "Be careful that Auntie doesn't notice something!" The girl, hearing this, reluctantly hooked her arm around the boy's. "Are you going out?" The middle-aged beautiful woman saw them and casually asked. "Yeah! Qian said she's bored, so I'll take her out for a walk!" The boy casually responded and quickly pulled the girl away. Staying any longer, who knows what ambiguous words she would say.

Just at the door, the girl shook off the boy's hand: "You can go play now. I'll call you later to send me home." The boy revealed a mischievous look: "Every time you eat your fill and then abandon the monk! You should give me some benefits!" The girl squinted her eyes: "Want to take advantage of me, huh? What benefits do you want?"

The boy, seeing the girl's expression, hastily shook his head and dryly laughed: "Hehe~ I didn't say anything! You go play fast! If you don't go soon, he'll be waiting impatiently!" The boy knew that whenever the girl squinted her eyes, she was about to explode, and he certainly wouldn't be foolish enough to provoke her anger. The girl gave him a look of 'you know better,' and turned to leave. The boy watched the girl's retreating back, the smile on his lips disappearing, his face filled with deep loneliness.

Feng and Qian have been childhood sweethearts, and they still attend the same school now. Feng has always liked Qian, and both sets of parents have always thought they were quite suitable for each other, arranging their marriage as children and often trying to bring them together. However, Qian has never really liked Feng, saying he's too lazy and carefree, and she has fallen for a boy from their class.

Qian's parents watch her closely, so every time Feng brings Qian out, she goes to meet the boy she likes. At these times, Feng feels somewhat superfluous, unable to join them, afraid to go home lest he gets caught, and thus waits until Qian finishes her date before sending her home. Feng has always felt dejected, but he never shows it in front of Qian, always acting mischievously and maintaining a carefree demeanor.

As the sky gradually darkened, Feng took out his phone to check the time, muttering to himself: "It's about time, she should be coming back!" He stubbed out the cigarette butt, leapt down lightly from the short wall, dusted off the dirt on his pants, and walked towards the crossroads that the girl must pass through, leaving the cigarette butts scattered on the ground. Arriving at the crossroads, he looked in the direction Qian left, but did not see her. Feng leaned against the lamppost, took out another cigarette, lit it, and idly watched as the smoke slowly diminished between his fingers.

"You big smoker, all you do is smoke, smoke yourself to death!" The sound reached his ears even before the person arrived. Feng threw the cigarette butt, stepped on it to extinguish it, and put on a mischievous look: "They say smoking keeps you healthy, gambling sharpens your mind, and dancing eliminates troubles!" Qian rolled her eyes, irked: "Then why don't you go gamble and dance more? I bet you won't even know how you die!"

Feng grinned without a care in the world and went over to grab Qian's hand: "Hey! Are you worried about me, feeling sorry for me?" Qian turned her head aside, huffed, and said: "Don't flatter yourself, this girl will never feel sorry for you!" After saying that, she shook off Feng's hand, but as soon as she did, her body tilted and she fell toward the ground.

Feng, quick as lightning, caught Qian, furrowing his brows and asking: "What's wrong with your foot?" There was actually a hint of blame in his tone. It was the first time Qian had seen Feng angry, and she weakly said: "I walked too much today and accidentally sprained my ankle." Feng scolded her: "I've told you not to wear heels that are too high. You never listen, now you know what it feels like, right? Let me see your foot!"

After speaking, he squatted down and helped her take off her shoe. Qian looked at Feng's domineering manner and secretly thought: "Who would have thought that he usually looks so indifferent, but when he's angry, he's actually quite cool!" Feng, looking at her slightly swollen little foot, furrowed his eyebrows even more and turned around, gently shouting at the girl: "Get on!"

Qian, with a stubborn look on her face, said: "I can walk myself, I don't need you to carry me." Feng glanced at her, said nothing, and before Qian could react, he lifted her up and carried her away. Qian struggled slightly, but Feng held her even tighter: "Don't move, if you do, I'll tell everything to Auntie! Be a good girl and let me carry you home!"

There was an unyielding tone in his voice. Qian, seeing that Feng was truly angry, shrank her neck and obediently said "okay." After a while, Qian secretly stretched her head from Feng's shoulder and looked at his side face, suddenly realizing that Feng was actually kind of handsome, something she hadn't noticed before. And his back was pretty wide too, leaning on his shoulders felt so comfortable, it would be nice if it could stay like this forever.

"Ah! How could I think like this? I don't like this carefree and reckless guy! Hmph! That was all an illusion!" Qian shook her head to drive away those thoughts. After sending Qian home, Feng reminded her a few more times before returning home. Lying in bed, Qian thought about Feng's domineering tenderness earlier, wrinkled her cute little nose and huffed, "Dead rascal!" Then she quietly covered her mouth and giggled softly, slowly drifting into dreams.

The crisp bell rang for class, and Qian absentmindedly drew circles on her desk. For some reason, she couldn't concentrate today, unable to hear a word the teacher on the podium was saying, her mind replaying that scene over and over. Shaking her head, she muttered to herself: "All because of that dead rascal! I like him, not Feng!" Then she secretly glanced sideways at the sunny and handsome boy sitting not far away, lost in thought: "I like him! My boyfriend is so handsome! Hehe!"

Suddenly, there was a chaotic commotion from the teaching building below: "Fire! Run!" "Wow! The fire is too big, it's uncontrollable, call 119!" "Don't push me, you idiot!" "Wow! You stepped on my heel!" The teacher's calm voice sounded: "Students, don't panic, go down the stairs on the right side, the fire isn't too bad there."

Hearing the noise downstairs, the classroom erupted into chaos, everyone rushing madly toward the door. Qian was among the crowd, her frail body already knocked down by the crowd, her injured foot trampled multiple times, painful to the point where she couldn't stand up anymore. In the chaotic crowd, Qian looked everywhere for his figure. Suddenly, her eyes brightened as she spotted the familiar silhouette, and she waved her hands, using all her strength to shout: "Ting, I'm here! Wait for me! Don't leave me behind!"

That boy glanced at the girl with the corner of his eye, hesitated for a moment, then looked back at the raging fire that was beyond control. Biting his lip, he made a decision and ran away. Behind him, Qian's voice, tinged with crying, echoed: "Ting, don't leave me! Didn't you say we'd be together forever?" But Ting seemed not to hear and ran away without looking back. Qian slumped onto the stairs, desperately shouting: "Ting! I'm scared! Take me with you!"

By now, the flames had spread, thick smoke billowing upwards. Qian hugged her legs and sobbed quietly, fear, pain, helplessness, and despair overwhelming her. Being abandoned by the one she loved most, she became completely disoriented.

In Qian's despair, she suddenly saw a figure rushing through the thick smoke and roaring fire. Thinking it was Ting, she joyfully called out: "Ting, I'm here! Come save me!" However, when that figure reached her, Qian was taken aback! It was him, the dead rascal! Feng!! Without hesitation, Feng took off his wet coat and wrapped it around the girl's body. The fire had already engulfed the entire building. Feng looked at the fire, bent down, and said to Qian: "Qian! Close your eyes! Bury your head in my chest!" Qian looked at Feng's resolute face, nodded slightly, closed her eyes, and buried her head in Feng's chest. Feng gave the girl in his arms a gentle smile, braving the fire and dashing towards the stairs downstairs...

Hospital

When Qian woke up, she saw her frantic parents pacing anxiously by her bedside and weakly asked: "Where's Feng? Is Feng alright???" Her parents only looked at her and remained silent. "What's wrong with Feng!" Qian's voice was eerily calm, almost frighteningly so. In that moment at the fire scene, she suddenly realized that the person she loved the most had always been by her side, yet she had constantly lost her temper with him, hitting and yelling at him, and he had silently endured it all, protecting her and staying by her side.

Finally, Qian's parents spoke: "Feng, at that time, he covered you with his coat, he had no protection himself, and suffered severe burns all over his body. He's currently in the intensive care unit." "I want to see him, take me there quickly!" As Qian said this, she struggled to get out of bed. Her mother helped her up and led her step by step towards Feng's ward.

As soon as they entered the ward, Qian saw Feng, whose whole body was bandaged up like a mummy, and her heart felt like it was bleeding. "Rascal!" The girl opened her mouth and called out softly. Who knew that as soon as Feng saw her, he began yelling fiercely at the girl: "Get out! I don't want to see you! Get out!" The girl's face was full of question marks: "Feng! What's wrong with you!" Feng's face was full of resentment: "It's all because of you! If it weren't for you, I wouldn't have ended up like this! I don't want to see you ever again! Get out! Get out!!!"

Feng yelled viciously. Qian stood dumbfounded, blaming herself: "It's all my fault, if it weren't for me, you wouldn't have become like this! Let me take care of you, okay? I'll be your girlfriend! Haven't you always liked me?" Feng's face was full of disdain: "Someone like you, stop being self-centered. Your face is decent, but your figure is like a washboard! How could I possibly like you!" Tears slid quietly down Qian's face as she turned around, heartbroken, and left. She didn't see that after she left, Feng couldn't hold back his tears, which streamed down his face.

The next day, Qian's parents rushed into the ward in a panic and said to Qian: "Something's wrong! Feng..." Qian suddenly had a bad premonition: "What happened to Feng!"

"Feng... he jumped off the building and committed suicide!!!" This sentence was like a bolt of lightning, striking Qian's heart hard! "How could this be! How could that rascal commit suicide! Impossible! Absolutely impossible!" Before she finished speaking, tears streamed down her face... "Here's the letter he left you." Qian's mother took out an envelope.

The girl's hands trembled as she opened the letter, and after reading it, she burst into tears... "Qian, by the time you read this letter, I will no longer be here. I cannot burden you. The doctor said that my whole body has been severely burned, and I am disfigured, unworthy of you. I know if I stayed, you would never leave me, so I chose to leave. Actually, I am so reluctant to part with you, I wish I could laugh mischievously at you and tease you again. Since we were young, you've always said I'm too carefree and lack seriousness. I don't like showing my melancholy side in front of you because I love seeing your smiling face, so cute. Take care of yourself! Find someone who loves you more than me, someone who will protect you for me!

If you miss me, look at my photos or the red maple leaves in autumn, they burn with passion just like my love for you! In the next life, don't fall for anyone else, okay? Do you know? Every time I learned you were going on a date with him, it felt like my heart was bleeding, so painful! So painful! And in the next life, don't change your name! That way, I can find you more easily! I won't drink the forgetfulness potion, because I want to remember your name, your appearance! Will you be my wife in the next life?"

As Qian read the letter, tears streamed uncontrollably down her face... Under the maple tree, the girl stared at the fiery red maple leaves, and it seemed as if the boy appeared before her eyes, a mischievous smile on his lips. The girl softly smiled and said: "Feng! Don't cross the bridge to the underworld too quickly, wait for me! I can't walk anymore! I still need you to carry me! In the next life, I will definitely not fall for anyone else! I will be your wife! We will be together forever!" With that, the girl took out a small blade and slashed her wrist. Bright red blood flowed out, just like the falling maple leaves, fiery red...
