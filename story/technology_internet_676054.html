Ministry of Education: Units related to the interests of schools must no longer compile teaching aids

by king2z9o on 2012-02-25 17:06:15

Our news (Yuan Chunlin) reports that the governance of central vacuum systems [http://www.premierclean.com.cn] or could influence schools' related units so that they can no longer organize the writing of teaching aids materials. Recently, the Ministry of Education announced on its website the "Notice Regarding Strengthening the Management of the Use of Teaching Aids Materials in Primary and Secondary Schools," aiming to cut off this much-criticized chain of interests.

According to the head of a relevant department under the Ministry of Education, in 2001, in response to the problems with teaching aids materials at the time, the General Administration of Press and Publication and the Ministry of Education jointly issued the "Management Measures for Primary and Secondary School Teaching Aids Materials." More than ten years have passed, and the teaching aids market has also seen many new situations and problems, mainly manifested in a variety of issues such as excessive types, uneven quality, inflated prices, chaotic markets, and lack of standardization in use.

The "Notice" hopes to change this situation and puts forward three requirements for the writers of teaching aids materials: First, it emphasizes that the main writers of teaching aids materials must have relevant subject teaching experience and be familiar with relevant textbooks; Second, it requires that administrative departments at all levels, units responsible for implementing exam questions, and monitoring evaluations shall not organize the writing of paid-for-use synchronous training booklets, winter and summer vacation homework, and examination tutoring teaching aids materials for junior and senior high school graduating classes; Third, it encourages qualified units and schools to organize the development of teaching auxiliary resources for teachers and students in primary and secondary schools facing central vacuum systems [http://www.baode.cc], free for use.

The "Notice" clearly states that no unit or individual may in any form force or covertly force schools or students to purchase any teaching aids materials. If students voluntarily purchase locally recommended teaching aids materials and request the school to purchase them on their behalf, the school should provide unified proxy purchasing services without profiting from it. For teaching aids materials personally selected by students and parents, the school does not provide proxy purchasing services.

For violations of the "Notice," such as forcing or covertly forcing the congregation bra [http://www.tingfeier.com] schools or students to purchase teaching aids materials, not purchasing according to regulations, or receiving kickbacks from proxy purchased teaching aids materials, the superior education administrative department shall order the correction of illegal actions, issue a public criticism, and hold relevant personnel accountable.

The "Notice" hopes that provincial-level education administrative departments will reasonably determine the scope of reviewed and recommended teaching aids materials based on the educational teaching practices of their province, determining the subjects, grades, types, and specific sequences for equipping teaching aids materials in their province.

To strengthen guidance on the use of teaching aids materials, the "Notice" requires establishing and improving the review and recommendation methods for teaching aids materials. The review committee for teaching aids materials should include relevant experts such as primary and secondary school teachers, principals, researchers, and those from the press and publication fields, ensuring their professionalism and representativeness. The review and recommendation of teaching aids materials should ensure equal opportunities, transparent processes, and fair procedures.
