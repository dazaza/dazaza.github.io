Taxi driver reads "book" while driving

"On October 28, a taxi driver was seen reading a book while driving through the underpass from Huaxin Street to Jiangbei. This happened during a traffic jam and was captured by a nearby netizen. Regarding the taxi driver's ability to multitask, netizens jokingly asked if the driver had some kind of 'cheat code.' Despite strong criticism, many netizens defended the driver, believing that reading a book during a traffic jam posed no issue.

Netizens: Is this driver using a cheat code?

On October 28, a post titled "This Taxi Driver is Extraordinary, Amazing!" appeared on Dayu Community. The poster, a netizen named "Tiantian," recounted how she and her husband were driving back from Daping to Huangnihe when they encountered a traffic jam near the underpass from Huaxin Street to Jiangbei. At that moment, she noticed a taxi driver next to them reading a book. "Tiantian" also mentioned that there were two female passengers sitting in the backseat of the taxi who were chatting and completely unaware of the driver’s actions.

The post quickly drew attention from numerous netizens. Although it was unclear what the driver was reading or why, a heated debate ensued over whether it was acceptable for a driver to read a book. Opinions were divided, with one side arguing that reading while driving disregards the safety of both the driver and the passengers, while the other side believed that reading a novel during a traffic jam was harmless.

"Purple Butterfly": Mocking the lives of passengers and himself, does this driver even deserve to drive? Just because no accident has happened yet doesn't mean it's not serious; once an accident happens, it will be disastrous. There's no regret pill to sell.

"Zhengdong": The driver might have been studying traffic laws.

"Jazz": I'm a driver, and flipping through a newspaper during a traffic jam is something I often do; it helps relieve fatigue.

Driver: It was a contact book

Yesterday afternoon, according to the taxi's number, the license plate was found to be YB3T2XX, and the involved driver Yang Qiang was located through Ruitor Taxi Company. He told reporters that he is 27 years old, and around 10:50 AM on October 28, a service staff at the Hilton Hotel in Lianglukou hailed his cab. Two foreign women got in, and the service staff instructed him to head towards Jiangbei. When the car reached a fork in the road at Huaxin Street, Yang Qiang asked the passengers, “Which way?” Since he didn’t speak English, but the two foreigners seemed to understand some Chinese, one of them took out a notebook similar to a contact book, flipped to a page written in Chinese, and handed it to Yang Qiang from the back seat. "It said Songpai Road Hanting Hotel," Yang Qiang explained. From the time he picked up the notebook until he put it down, it lasted about two minutes. However, he admitted that such behavior could pose certain safety risks, and he would definitely be more careful in the future.

Poster: The underpass wasn't completely blocked

Yesterday, the poster Miss Tian told reporters that at around 11 AM on October 28, due to heavy traffic, the underpass from Huaxin Street to Jiangbei was moving very slowly. "At the time, the road wasn't completely blocked, cars were stop-and-go, and it took about two minutes to pass through the underpass." During these two minutes, the taxi driver kept holding the book with his left hand, reading it while still keeping up with the flow of traffic ahead.

Yang Qiang also confirmed that the road wasn't completely blocked. Due to the process of passing the contact book, the pages got mixed up accidentally. He had to hold the contact book with his left hand, keep his right hand on the steering wheel, and use his peripheral vision to find the page with the Chinese address while watching the road conditions and following the vehicles ahead.

Response: The taxi driver may undergo off-the-job training

Yesterday, Mr. Wang, the general manager of Ruitor Taxi Company, stated in an interview with reporters that regardless of the circumstances, reading while driving poses safety hazards, which is irresponsible not only to the safety of the passengers but also to the driver himself. Yang Qiang has been in the profession for two years without receiving any complaints and is considered a driver with good professional ethics. An initial investigation into the incident exposed by the netizen has been conducted. If, as Yang Qiang claims, the passengers were foreigners and the need to read the Chinese address on the notebook was due to a language barrier, this can be understood. Therefore, the company decided that if Yang Qiang can provide evidence proving that he was reading the address on the notebook rather than a novel, the company will not penalize him. However, if he cannot provide evidence, he will undergo off-the-job training and education for 3~7 days.

All manuscripts in this group were written by reporter Luoyao Wang.
