A Parody of "The Thirty-Six Stratagems": The War of Ten Thousand Kings (Part II)

Ladies and gentlemen, welcome to the second part of my parody on "The Thirty-Six Stratagems." This article is purely for entertainment purposes. Please do not associate it with the original work or consider it an academic discussion. Do not take it too seriously. If any big brother or sister has used these strategies before, that's purely coincidental. Now let's continue with the twelve strategies in the middle section.

Thirteenth Strategy: Plucking the Goat

This strategy refers to seizing opportunities when they arise, similar to obtaining a VIP activation code for "Zhu Xian 2." It is a plan outside the main strategy aimed at increasing income and acquiring wealth. In one realm's mission in another realm, Mark Dempsey participates in the film by displaying his profession. After completing the mission, he suddenly sees a BOSS spawn. Naturally, he attacks it (avoid attacking weak realms as they don't drop much loot). Use this strategy cautiously as it may offend allies!

Fourteenth Strategy: Luring the Tiger from the Mountain

"The tiger" represents a strong enemy. Attacking directly would lead to defeat. A certain realm wants to advance deep into enemy territory but is blocked by a powerful group guarding the border. Unable to breakthrough, the entire group shifts to other elemental realms and splits into two teams to launch strong assaults on the enemy's allies. The enemy, noticing no elements in our realm, leads a large army to support their allies. Our group takes the opportunity to break through.

Fifteenth Strategy: Substituting One for Another

In warfare, achieving complete victory is rare. The key lies in comparing strengths and weaknesses. Sacrificing small parts for the greater good can be a winning strategy. For example, sacrificing weaker units to win the overall battle. (Oh! All these stars and moons are so painful to fight!)

Sixteenth Strategy: Beating the Bushes to Catch the Rabbit

When a realm wants to declare war without a valid reason, find an excuse. For instance, "Oh no! Your realm killed such a beautiful girl from our realm!" Or "Heaven forbid! Your realm came to ours to perform drunken tasks without cleaning your shoes, bringing so much mud inside! We must kill you to preserve the beauty of our realm!" The intention to kill was there all along; we just needed a reason.

Seventeenth Strategy: Watching the Fire from Across the River

One realm is particularly strong and often oppresses others, leaving them with no confidence to form alliances. When a realm is under attack, the king's messenger comes to our realm seeking help via YY. We immediately agree but hold back our troops at the elemental boundary. They won't unite unless they feel the pain of defeat. Once they're sufficiently beaten, we send reinforcements. The fire has already burned out, and we only put in minimal effort. (Personally, I dislike this approach. Friends should always help each other in times of need!)

Eighteenth Strategy: Feigning Activity

Even a weak realm or group can create a formidable appearance. A weak realm is often bullied by a large group. One day, while trying to destroy vehicles and earn battle merits, they appear with a strong lineup, chasing the enemy until they suffer heavy casualties. In reality, they bought several high-level accounts to strengthen themselves. This strategy is versatile, aiming only for self-preservation.

Nineteenth Strategy: Diverting Attention

If you don't pretend to repair the栈道(stack road), you cannot secretly cross Chen Cang. Publicly show weakness to gather strength. There will always be an opportunity for revenge. Let them seize crystals and destroy grain carts. I won't move until I reach the history city. Upgrade and prepare equipment for future retaliation.

Twentieth Strategy: Retreat as the Best Option

This "retreat" means frequent movement. Whether winning or losing, retreat is necessary. In the War of Ten Thousand Kings, flexibility is key. Staying in one place makes you vulnerable to being wiped out. Hit and run tactics leave no trace. Today! Have you retreated yet?

Twenty-First Strategy: Feigning Madness

Others laugh at me for being crazy, but I laugh at them for not seeing through my disguise! As the saying goes, "Appear foolish but have a clear mind!" I'll act dumb while you show off your strength! The first to show strength becomes the target, while we, the quiet dogs, bite!

Twenty-Second Strategy: Capturing by Letting Go

To catch a fish, you must give up the bear's paw. Active abandonment leads to more active acquisition. To break a realm, you must set aside personal interests, form many alliances, and be willing to compromise on small gains and petty grievances. The more you want something, the less you should appear to care about it. "Oh? Are we becoming friendly realms now? Oh, let me discuss it with everyone!" In reality, the decision has already been made, but maintaining composure is always beneficial.

Twenty-Third Strategy: Removing the Fuel from Under the Cauldron

Make a firm decision to resolve fundamental issues once and for all. Hesitation is unattractive, and chronic problems require drastic measures. A certain realm has been continuously disrupting our missions. Though I originally had no intention to counterattack, I've been plagued by their constant harassment. Without further ado, the entire group gathers to attack anyone and everything they encounter, committing to a daily massacre. (Fly: Why do you hit me every day? Swatter: You keep buzzing around!)

Twenty-Fourth Strategy: The Empty City Ruse

Temporary abnormal behavior can confuse enemies, creating uncertainty between truth and falsehood. However, ask yourself, does anyone in the seven realms have the audacity to stand alone in the element and boldly proclaim, "Today, my city is empty, with not a single soldier. I play music, drink tea, and fan myself leisurely." Such bravado would likely result in the king being killed and resurrected countless times.
