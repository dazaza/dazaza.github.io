Related thematic articles: Untitled Document In March, under the fireworks, this city renowned for its beautiful scenery is at its most stunning time of the year. Everywhere, there's lush greenery, chirping orioles and swaying willows by the lake. Peach blossoms on the roadside are in full bloom. As the spring breeze passes, petals of peach flowers dance with the wind. Before long, the stone ground is covered with a rosy red hue, scattered remnants that still hold their charm, making one reluctant to step on them. Following the green stone path, passing through a green bamboo forest and turning left, you can see a two-story Chinese-style building with black tiles and red walls, and carved railings. In the center of the plaque, there are several elegant words written: "Past Life Present Life." Past Life Present Life, this is the name of a teahouse and also the place where I, Ye Yin, a 19-year-old girl, work. In this city known for its tea-drinking culture, such teahouses are countless. Of course, teahouses are places for business, but besides tea, we do other businesses - a special kind of business related to past lives and present destinies. Have you ever heard of this saying: To know the events of your previous life, look at what you endure in this life; the cause from the previous life leads to the result in the next. If you have planted this cause, then no matter how many reincarnations pass, you cannot escape this outcome. So how can it be resolved? Perhaps this is an unavoidable and unresolvable fate, but our business precisely aims to return to the life when you planted the cause, find the root of destiny, and change it. This business does not take money; it only takes - a single tear from you. Some might say isn't that time travel? How could it be possible? Indeed, time travel is impossible for ordinary people, but for him, it's not a problem. He - my master, the owner of this teahouse. My master has a beautiful name - Si Yin, just as his name suggests. Since I was adopted by Si Yin as a child, I naturally consider Si Yin to be my closest person. Since I understood things, I rarely saw Si Yin smile or get angry or upset. He always remained calm, as if nothing in this world could move him. In my memory, he seemed to show some warmth only when teaching me spiritual techniques and exorcism. Besides Si Yin's name and spiritual techniques, I know nothing about him, including his age and nationality. Normally, there are three people in the teahouse: Si Yin, Ming Zhuo, me, and my senior apprentice, Fei Niao. Although Fei Niao's name sounds very oriental, he has a head of dazzling golden hair and eyes as blue as the sea. I don't know where he comes from, but he was already there when Si Yin adopted me. Four years older than me, he is a warm and outgoing boy. Perhaps because of his personality, his girlfriends come and go like a carousel. However, we who grew up together have a deep bond, a relationship better than siblings. He is highly intelligent and became a master much earlier than me. All along, he has been constantly traveling through different times and spaces, searching for the past-life roots of clients. The past lives of different clients vary widely and are not limited to ancient China; occasionally, they include ancient Europe and ancient Africa. Language is not a big issue as Si Yin always gives him a language-understanding pill before each mission, allowing him to communicate freely in any time-space and country. However, the language-understanding pill is only effective in other dimensions and has no effect in the current space-time. After learning this, I had to reluctantly pick up my English textbook again, originally thinking that with just one pill, I could also excel in the real world. Today, however, is a special day for me because I've officially become a master. From today onwards, I can also roam through five thousand years of history like my senior apprentice. Every time I listened to my senior talk about the details of other dimensions, I felt itchy inside. Now, I can finally stand on my own. Thinking about this, I couldn't help but laugh out loud. I hope the past life of my first client is in an era that interests me. No, I shouldn't think like that; this is work, not play.
