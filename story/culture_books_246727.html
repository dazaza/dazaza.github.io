"Mayor's Backyard" - A tale of emotional turmoil amidst official disputes

by jqrxass on 2009-10-12 16:20:48

Lizi Da Zhouxuan in the political struggle, he stepped onto the political stage as an expert and then quickly submerged into the undercurrents of officialdom. He calmly accepted gifts from real estate developers and used his position to give them a green light; to stabilize the rear, he indulged in his wife's greedy actions; to protect his subordinates, he went against his conscience and tacitly approved the excessive sentencing of an unknown soldier. At the same time, he also walked on the edge of morality. He is not a saint and will not adhere to propriety for his marriage full of holes; he is also not a promiscuous person...

In the conflict between love and political future, he even considered giving up his lover; but he is by no means heartless either. In life-and-death situations, he protected his lover Lu Wen with his own life, defending the dignity of a man in love. The author does not merely replicate the world, but rather focuses on explaining it. No one can be sure that Lizi Da did not consider killing his wife when he pushed her down, and it is precisely under this humanitarian concern that the author confronts the ugliness of human nature...

Mayor's Backyard Part One

Being a lover, whether a paramour or a mistress, has always been a bitter and tiring thing. No one who becomes a lover only reaps happiness and pleasure without "enjoying" its negative effects. Those who fall into the role of a lover often experience a sense of heaviness, sadness, and sorrow. But the number of lovers does not decrease because of this. This is probably because the role of a lover contains many tempting factors, which may be stronger than its negative effects. Lizi Da stepped into the role of a lover at the age of thirty-eight, just three months after being appointed as the factory manager of the steelmaking division of Zhongnan Province Steel Company...

Mayor's Backyard Part Two

Lizi Da stepped into the place where Lu Wen had agreed to meet. The two didn't speak, just looked at each other intently, and almost simultaneously their bodies moved, embracing tightly, followed by a deep kiss. They had never embraced so passionately or kissed so deeply before. This should be the eruption of a volcano of emotions suppressed for too long, this should be the fervent celebration of instinctual desires overcoming subjective restraint. When they rolled together, their souls merged into one body, and they tasted the essence of humanity, the satisfaction of desire, the joy that took their breath away. As they slowly came down from the peak of ecstasy along rugged paths, Lizi Da realized that he had failed again.

Mayor's Backyard Part Three

Lizi Da knew too well how much Lu Wen needed love. A girl like her should have a happy family; at her age, she should enjoy pleasant sexual relations. But she had none of that, if there was anything, it was what little he gave her, indeed very little, out of 365 days in a year, how many times could they see each other? He carried her to a small bed against the back wall, both were tired, unable to stand. They lay down, undressing each other, both having strong desires. He didn't want to press her down, he loved her too much, he was so gentle and tender in caressing her, hugging her...

Mayor's Backyard Part Four

Lizi Da didn't pursue the matter further, pursuing it further would likely result in another "war." He didn't want to argue with her at this moment, also because he harbored guilt in his heart, after all, he had secretly met his lover behind his wife's back, these past few days he indeed lived a carefree life. But what about his wife? She seemed absent-minded on the outside, but inside she was very sad. Lizi Da wasn't confused, how could he not know Luo Hong's distress? But what could be done?

Mayor's Backyard Part Five

The younger brother asked the elder sister, now that things have come to this point, what do you think should be done? Even if I, your younger brother, go through fire and boiling oil, I don't care, as long as I can help you get revenge. Luo Hong said, these days she had thought of some ways, thinking over and over, and finally decided on one way. Luo Liang asked, what way? Luo Hong said, disfigurement, that is, destroy the face of that little wretch. Men like women because of their pretty faces, if her face is destroyed, making her neither human nor ghost, which man would still be afraid to see her, and continue to flirt with her, losing himself? Luo Liang drew a cold breath, he didn't expect that his sister could come up with such a cruel plan...

Mayor's Backyard Part Six

Luo Hong didn't force her younger brother anymore, she instead thought that this matter shouldn't be entrusted to family members at all. Moreover, she didn't want to take on such a trembling-inducing risk! If it weren't for that young woman interfering in her family, making her the Mayor's wife in name only, a living widow with a husband, where would she want to harm her? All her actions were forced by that woman! After the incident happened, Brother Li Ming seemed to notice their marital discord and tried to mediate. She already saw that Brother Li wanted her husband to change his mind and pay more attention to the family.

Mayor's Backyard Part Seven

"I want to be your wife, you should be my husband, we should have our own home, a legitimate home, Zhi Da, I want - I'm crazy with wanting! My brain feels like it's going to explode, my body and mind feel like they're falling apart, I really can't take it anymore..." Then, Lu Wen cried about the harm caused by the anonymous letter, cried about the talk with the unit's party secretary, cried about the cruelty of her rival, who wanted to ruin her reputation and make her lose everything. She was almost collapsing, falling apart! With each word she poured out, her crying became more intense, more painful, louder...

Mayor's Backyard Part Eight

Lizi Da's words shocked Luo Hong so much that she broke out in a cold sweat for a while. She stared at her husband's angry and somewhat distorted dark face, noticing the fierce light in his sunken eyes, a look she had never seen before. She drew a cold breath, pondering whether to confront or retreat, reconcile or divorce, this unfaithful husband...

Mayor's Backyard Part Nine

Lu Wen stopped talking, just letting Lizi Da embrace her. Her eyes slightly closed, she fell into a state of intoxication. Lizi Da pressed their faces tightly together, their lips firmly imprinted, kissing deeply and lingeringly. At this moment, all confusion and disorder disappeared without a trace, only a floating dream remained. And only during this time did Lu Wen and Lizi Da have their own happy world, despite its smallness, its brevity, lovers were willing to cherish it as a lifelong aspiration and dream. After their passionate kisses, they leaned together upstairs. Although the living room downstairs was quiet and comfortable, they still felt it wasn't private enough. Upstairs there was a study, a bedroom, also a living room and bathroom. They first entered the study, sitting on the double sofa, recounting the bitterness and sweetness of these days.

Mayor's Backyard Part Ten

That night, Xiao Bai drove the minivan with Luo Hong's body inside, until he threw the body into the Yellow River, he never looked at the thing wrapped in the sheet, he didn't know who it was. But he knew it must be a person, though he didn't know if it was a man or a woman, old or young. He only knew that there was a rule that must be followed, which was not to know what the thing was, because he had received a lot of money from someone, so he should keep their secret. Of course, receiving money should also be kept secret. After finishing the task that day, he hurried home with the money and told his sister to keep it safe, saying that it was the money for their mother's livelihood, no one knew its origin, and no one would ask about it. If anyone did ask, they must not mention the money.

Mayor's Backyard Part Eleven

Since the day of Lizi Da's trial, Dawn's mood became heavy. When he faced his former mayor and current prisoner as the presiding judge, the inner sorrow and embarrassment were indescribable. He couldn't bear to see his fellow townsman, childhood classmate, and once ambitious Lizi Da suddenly become a prisoner. It had been a long time since he had sat on the bench as a presiding judge to handle cases directly. That day, when he saw the former mayor and his neighbor, handcuffed and shackled, walk into the defendant's seat in the courtroom under the escort of court police, his mind instantly went blank, and the thousands of words originally active in his mind were squeezed out by the figure appearing before him.
