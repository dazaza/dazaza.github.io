Turns out, rookies can also change the shell of an IPOD VIDEO...

by hongfa on 2008-12-18 09:50:45

It's been a long time since I've written anything, but I never thought I'd actually have the chance to write a technical article like this. Recently, my bundled system was finally up and running. However, the first choice RH1 was forcibly taken by my wife, who claimed she would use it for listening to music while going to and from work. She also took an E5C... The world is so cold-hearted. Since the quality of the IPC's LINE OUT is unbearable for me, I had to consider buying another RH1. I was negotiating prices with a Hong Kong proxy on Taobao when I casually browsed through the second-hand section of the forum and found a 30G iPod Video modified by Feng for a relatively cheap price. It even came with four blue DOCK heads, all for just 800 yuan. Feng alone charges around 400 yuan for modifying the machine and adding four blue DOCK heads, meaning the 30G IPV cost only 400 yuan, and the battery was replaced just half a year ago by the seller and was in good condition. The only downside was its poor condition because the seller was a carefree guy from Northeast China. He didn't even use a cover or screen protector on this IPV, resulting in countless scratches on the host after prolonged use. However, since IMOD modifies the LINE OUT interface of the iPod Video, which is rumored on forums to have excellent quality and a style different from RH1's LINE OUT, I decided to buy it immediately after some consideration. The Northeastern guy was very straightforward; we barely negotiated the price, and within half an hour, the deal was done. I paid via Taobao, he sent it by SF Express, and the next evening, the item was already in my hands. After unpacking and testing it, the quality of the modified iPod Video's LINE OUT interface indeed improved by one level, surpassing the unmodified version comprehensively. However, the condition was indeed as bad as that Northeastern guy described, as can be seen in the pictures below, especially the scratched-up back cover. There's nothing I can do; after all, it was such a bargain price, so I have to accept its poor condition.
