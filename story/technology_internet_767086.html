How "intimate" are mobile phones and toilets?

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:54:10

The story of the relationship between mobile phones and toilets continues to unfold. The reason is quite simple: you are becoming increasingly dependent on your phone. Even during private activities such as using the toilet, having sex, or taking a shower, you are constantly in close contact with your phone.

How "intimate" is the relationship between your phone and the toilet? This can be seen through three different infographics.

One in five people have experienced dropping their phone in the toilet

According to statistics from Splick.it, 12% of adults use their smartphones while showering, and 75% use them in the bathroom. Tragically, 19% of people have had the experience of dropping their phone into the toilet.

Since Splickit is an O2O service company, they also analyzed how people place orders using mobile devices. According to their data, 95% of smartphone users search for restaurants, and 90% of these searches convert into actual purchases within the same day, with 64% deciding to purchase within an hour. The reasons why people choose to order via their phones, in order of importance, are: speed, not wanting to wait in line, and the desire to obtain coupons.

Men are twice as likely as women to drop their phone in the toilet

According to a survey by American insurance company Protect Your Bubble, men are 70% more likely than women to lose their phones; men are also 57% more likely to drop their phones in the toilet. On the other hand, women's phones are 42% more likely to be stolen compared to men's.

In another survey, the most common places where phones get damaged, in descending order, are: the garage, the bathroom, the restaurant, the bedroom, and the living room. Among these, 59% of men damage their phones in the garage, while 41% of women damage theirs in the bathroom. A report from 2012 stated that since the launch of the iPhone, Americans have spent $5.9 billion on iPhone repairs.

What do you do with your phone in the toilet?

The third survey comes from location services company Telenav. Their survey shows that 70% of men use their phones while on the toilet, while this proportion is 59% for women. Additionally, 65% of respondents use their phones in the bathroom.

More importantly, Telenav also investigated what people do with their phones in the restroom. Ranked in order, the activities are: Sending/receiving messages (47%), browsing the internet (46%), playing games (42%), sending/receiving emails (34%), using social networks (23%).

Since Telenav is a location services company, their survey also provided a different perspective: what do people do with their phones when stuck in traffic? Ranked by frequency, the activities are: making calls (66%), sending texts (30%), and using social networks (11%). Given the high frequency of texting and calling among foreigners, it's no wonder New York has introduced "texting layover zones" to allow drivers to safely stop and send texts or make calls.
