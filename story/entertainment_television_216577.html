During college, most female students have had the experience of wondering what the guys do in their dormitories. In fact, the topics and pranks of the boys always go beyond your expectations. At seven-thirty tomorrow evening, Hunan Economic TV's specially produced youth comedy drama for the summer vacation, "Campus Cha Cha Cha," will uncover all your curiosities about university male dormitories.

"Campus Cha Cha Cha" is a campus-themed indoor situational comedy specially produced by Hunan Economic Television. The first season consists of 32 episodes, each 25 minutes long. The entire series blends elements of youth idols, motivation, and humor, focusing on the lives of six freshmen boys. Six first-year students from all over the country, speaking different dialects, come together in Dormitory 306. From the very first day scrambling for bed positions, they create numerous jokes. And as they continue through military training, big and small exams, conflicts between males and females, and romance, they deliver laugh-out-loud stories.

Although these storylines revolve around the campus dormitory setting, they reflect more deeply on societal, international, and ethical issues that people care about today. The six main characters may have wildly imaginative ideas, with language mixing internet slang, colloquial expressions, dialects, and trendy vocabulary, completely absurd, but they leave a lasting impression after the audience understands and laughs.

Moreover, "Campus Cha Cha Cha" borrows from the European and American situational comedy model. The six lead actors are all played by actual college students, each with distinct personalities yet imbued with an entertainment spirit. Many hosts and artists from Hunan television, such as Chen Yingjun, Mei Dong, Qiu Xiao, Wang Han, KK, YOYO, Yang Shanshan, Liu Ao, Du Haitao, Xiaodouzi, Tian Yuan, and others, will appear in important supporting roles you've never seen before, giving it their all.

"Campus Cha Cha Cha" makes those who have been to university reminisce endlessly, fills those who haven't been with longing, and in short, keeps everyone laughing non-stop. Starting July 4th, every Saturday at 7:30 PM, it will premiere on Hunan Economic TV—don’t miss out!
