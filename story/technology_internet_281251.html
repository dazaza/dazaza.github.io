Spanish media expose that the deputy of the Royal Madrid team will leave in winter. Will the Golden Wolf join Manchester United in January?

by djbgufgire on 2009-11-28 14:02:40

Spanish media have exposed that Guti, the vice-captain of Real Madrid, will leave in winter. Will the Golden Wolf join Manchester United in January? At 2:00 AM Beijing time on November 30, the 236th El Clásico will be staged at Camp Nou, with Real Madrid visiting to challenge the reigning La Liga champion Barcelona. All Spanish media are focusing on this match, but Guti, the vice-captain of Real Madrid, is destined to be a spectator and will not appear on the pitch. After the previous locker room incident, Guti has long become a marginal figure for Real Madrid. Recently, "As" revealed that the vice-captain of Real Madrid might leave Bernabeu during the January winter transfer window, and the two most likely teams he could join are Manchester's clubs - Manchester United and Manchester City.

Due to the well-known locker room shouting match incident, Guti has become the focal point of internal power struggles within Real Madrid. Florentino very much hopes for the emergence of a new leader to replace the "Raul-Guti Group," and the Casillas faction also rapidly rose after this dispute. In such a situation, Guti's position at Bernabeu has been precarious, and he has also begun searching for a new landing spot. Although Spanish media had previously whitewashed the peace, claiming that the conflict between Guti and Pellegrini had passed, "As" believes that things are far from simple. Guti's current situation within Real Madrid is very awkward, and he has reached a point where he "has to leave."

Although already 33 years old, Guti still does not want his career to end just like this. "As" analyzed that there are currently two main destinations available for Guti to choose from: one is to seek fortune in the Middle East leagues, and the other is to join the Premier League. From the current situation, the latter seems more likely, and both Manchester clubs are very interested in Guti. After the entry of Middle Eastern owners, Manchester City has always adhered to the principle of "not necessarily the best, but certainly the most expensive," and players like Guti with a certain level of influence fit right into the Emirati owner's taste. Meanwhile, Manchester United manager Ferguson also highly values Guti's organizational ability in midfield and hopes he can come to Old Trafford to contribute his remaining skills. "As" believes that although Manchester City may be more likely to meet Guti's salary requirement of over 4.5 million euros, Manchester United’s strength and heritage give it more appeal than its city rival.

During last weekend's training, Guti was injured after colliding with Higuain and will definitely miss the game of the century on Sunday. He won't be able to return until the match against Almeria on December 6. However, in Pellegrini's plans, Guti may never get another chance to appear on the field again. It is precisely because of this that "As" determined that Guti will leave the team after Christmas. Perhaps by next January, we will see Guti in a red Manchester United jersey galloping across the Premier League field.
