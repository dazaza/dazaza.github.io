Where are you at after leaving the company?

by qingian007 on 2012-02-28 17:13:38

According to the view of psychologist Abraham Maslow, human needs form a hierarchical pyramid. At the base of the pyramid are basic needs such as safety, food, and freedom from fear. At the top of the pyramid are knowledge and spiritual needs, such as self-esteem, status, recognition, growth, achievement, and personal development. After the lower-level needs are met, people seek to fulfill the higher-level needs in the pyramid.

For instance, for a low-level worker who must earn a living through work, threatening him with "you're nothing without the company" is effective because he has a strong sense of insecurity and relies on this job to avoid hunger. However, for more senior employees, such threats are not only despicable but also harmful. It makes them feel that they are not recognized, hurting their self-esteem, and they may even consider leaving - this is exactly how my friend felt. This fully demonstrates that sincere respect from the upper management is very effective in enhancing employees' loyalty and work enthusiasm. Unfortunately, this respect is actually the most scarce resource in all workplaces!

The friend said that his boss bought a book in bulk and distributed it to the employees for learning. The book is called "You're Nothing Without the Company". I must admit that I haven't read this book yet, so I can't comment on its value, but personally, I am quite repulsed by the title. The author might just be playing with clickbait, or perhaps the book does contain some useful advice about the workplace that benefits the 9-to-5 crowd. But I do suspect whether the boss who bought this book in bulk actually read it carefully?

Perhaps the reason he bought this book for the employees is simply because of the title? When he handed out the book, did he think: "You guys are thinking of quitting? You're nothing without the company! Stop daydreaming and work hard for me!"

Of course, the above is all baseless speculation, but if I were an employee at this company, I would definitely feel insulted upon receiving this book from the boss. According to common sense, I work for the company, and the salary the company pays me is absolutely less than the value I create. The boss isn't stupid; he wants to profit from me, which means I am useful to him. On one hand, he exploits my surplus value; on the other hand, he threatens me by saying I'm nothing without the company - what kind of logic is that?

From another perspective, the fact that this friend wants to quit and dares to quit shows that he has confidence in himself. He doesn't believe that he will end up destitute and starve to death without the company. He has something to rely on, and after leaving this position, there will be better positions and prospects waiting for him. As Zheng Yuanjie once said: An iron rice bowl is not an unbreakable bowl, but rather having a bowl wherever you go. Working to this level can be considered success.

As for myself, my long-term happy goal has always been to survive without relying on any specific organization and live well. I have not completely achieved this point yet, so I need to continue striving.
