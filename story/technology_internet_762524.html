Agent revelation: how does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:56:13

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. Therefore, establishing a search marketing system has become one of the main tasks for 360 this year. Under the pressure of Baidu's extensive nationwide channels, how is 360, as a latecomer, positioning itself?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search to reveal the expansion status of its search marketing system. We learned that 360's rapidly growing market share made agents smell an opportunity to make money, and Baidu's tightened agency rights over the years indirectly contributed to agent defections. The number of 360’s agents and orders have increased significantly since the beginning of the year.

Specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by agents. So far, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and cities, totaling 27 agencies, compared to 13 four months ago. Moreover, many local agents have started developing sub-agents. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular in local areas. "Just like in the early days of Baidu's development, many agents back then gained substantial benefits, so when 360 entered the search market, they were eager to secure the agency as soon as possible," said an agent from Henan province who told Sohu IT that they obtained the 360 agency rights last month.

2. The development of 360 agents also benefited from the gap formed by Baidu reclaiming its agency rights. The aforementioned Henan agent stated, "After many years of Baidu's development, many places have reclaimed their agency rights and set up branches or provincial general agents." 360 does not have branches in various regions and mainly relies on agents. Among these agents, some have previously represented Baidu ads, some have represented Sogou or Sou Ads, and others have even represented 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, with most agents establishing independent companies specifically for representing 360 ads.

3. Low price is an important advantage of 360 Search ads locally. An agent from Wuhan believes that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: less competition, low price, and high conversion rate. "Some keyword ad prices for 360 Search are less than one-fifth of Baidu's," he said. Leveraging the title of "the second-largest search engine" and the market education provided by Baidu earlier, the agent claims promotion isn't too difficult. However, many customers remain on the fence because 360 just entered the market, and they "need to see if 360 is truly willing to develop its search engine seriously."

4. In half a year, the order volume of 360 Search ads has grown rapidly. We reported in March that 360 Search completed about 1,000 orders in Beijing within three months. A salesperson from Tian Di Online, one of 360's Beijing area agents, told us that now they can exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing: Tian Di Online and Ju Tao Culture. The latter entered later and is relatively smaller. Henan San Bai Liu, which just secured the agency rights last month, has already completed more than 1,000 orders.

5. The average order value is around ten thousand yuan, which is not low. The minimum account opening fee for 360 Search ads is 4,600 yuan, including 600 yuan for service fees and 4,000 yuan for pre-deposited advertising funds. A Wuhan agent revealed that the current average order value is approximately ten thousand yuan. For comparison, Baidu's average customer revenue last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads have covered all industries. Currently, 360 Search ads have covered all industries except healthcare. Recently, they also opened up the previously restricted health supplement industry and adult products industry. It is worth noting that 360 only accepts corporate clients and does not accept individual clients, and each company can only advertise for one site (Baidu can accept individual clients). Many agents are developing offline clients such as education and consulting.

However, can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360 Search's agency system has initially taken shape, it is still at a very basic stage of competing with Baidu. One Baidu agent frankly stated: "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

Based on feedback from various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet intense. "360 Search is now more about giving customers a new market and new choices," said one agent, adding that many customers place ads on both platforms.

But this is a temporary situation. The scale of 360 Search is still small and requires more subsequent investment. However, the monetization of search ads will undoubtedly be its next most imaginative income growth point. When 360's client base truly grows and prices rise, another inevitable battle between 360 and Baidu may ensue.
