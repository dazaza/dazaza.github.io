China's Largest Toy Factory Goes Out of Business, Leaving Over 6000 Workers Unemployed

by hophi on 2008-10-18 00:29:41

The day before yesterday was a black day for those who had connections with Hong Kong’s He俊 Group, whether they were workers, shareholders, or suppliers. This Hong Kong stock market listed company of great importance in the toy industry saw its He俊 Toy Factory and Jun领 Toy Factory in Zhangmutou, Dongguan close down completely. More than 6000 workers from the two factories suddenly found themselves unemployed. Yesterday morning, more than 200 suppliers gathered in Dongguan to seek help from the government.

The government took over and issued an "announcement to calm the people".

"Yesterday we were still at work, today we are closed." The morning of the day before yesterday, after the He俊 Toy Factory in Zhangmutou overnight "died", the boss and mid to high level personnel all disappeared together, thousands of workers surrounded the side of the road in the Baoshan Industrial Zone of Zhangmutou, pulling banners demanding their wages. Some workers told reporters that several thousand workers in the entire factory have not received their wages for more than two months.

In order to calm the emotions of the workers, the Zhangmutou Town Government quickly posted notices at the entrance of the factory stating that the government has already established a special working group to try to solve the problem of the employees' wages. The Zhangmutou Town Government also stated that they will definitely handle the demands of the workers within three days according to relevant laws and regulations, reasonably and legally protecting the rights of the workers.

According to informed individuals, He俊 factories in other places also simultaneously closed down.

Owes hundreds of millions in goods payments to suppliers in various places

The closure of "He俊" made some suppliers become like ants on a hot pot. Yesterday morning, more than 200 suppliers from the Pearl River Delta gathered at the municipal government's petition office hoping to receive help. A Mr. Zhang, a supplier, stated that he is the boss of a hardware factory in Xiegang, Dongguan. Since the beginning of this year, "He俊" has continuously been in arrears for goods payments, currently owing him more than 8 million yuan. "This past half year, every time I came to collect payment, they said it would soon arrive," Mr. Zhang said, because they had cooperated for many years without any issues in the past, he also felt that the market was not doing well, so he decided to get through the difficulties with "He俊". Unexpectedly, the boss of "He俊" has now "disappeared".

During the reporter's investigation, it was learned that among the suppliers owed money, there are those who deal in hardware, plastic and circuit boards, as well as stationery and purified water. According to the suppliers on site, the total amount owed reached more than 60 million yuan. If one adds the other 200 or so suppliers who did not make it, the debt owed by "He俊" must certainly exceed 100 million yuan.

The closure of Dongguan's toy factory is not the first.

According to the mobile phone numbers provided by the suppliers, the reporter called the mobile phones of four senior executives of the He俊 Group including chairman Hu Jinbin, but either they were turned off, or they had activated the "incoming call reminder" service.

It is understood that Hong Kong's He俊 Holdings was founded in 1996. At its peak, it had a total of four production factories in Dongguan City and Qingyuan City, employing about 10,000 people, mainly producing high-end electronic toys, plush stuffed animal toys, etc., which were exported far to Europe, North America, Japan and other regions. It is claimed that He俊 Group's annual sales exceeded 700 million Hong Kong dollars.

Why did this giant in the toy industry collapse?

The notice from the Zhangmutou Town Government indicated that the rise in raw material costs and poor business management were the main reasons for the closure of the enterprise. In fact, the reason for the closure of toy factories due to such reasons, "He俊" is not the first.
