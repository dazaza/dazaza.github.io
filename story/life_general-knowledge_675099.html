--Methods for keeping cool in summer

by gege on 2012-02-24 17:48:29

1. Eat small meals frequently

The more food you eat in one meal, the more metabolic heat your body will produce to digest it.

Pay special attention to eating less high-protein foods, as they generate particularly more metabolic heat.

2. Eat spicy foods

Although it might be hard to imagine eating these on a hot day, spicy foods can stimulate the heat receptors in your mouth and improve blood circulation.

3. Take a warm water shower

Use slightly lower than body temperature warm water for showers or baths, especially before bed. Although a cold water shower may sound tempting, your body will generate more heat after a cold shower to compensate for the lost heat during the shower.

4. Stay in the basement

If you have a basement, it's best to hide there during the hottest part of the day. Generally, the temperature in the basement is 10 to 15 degrees lower than higher floors.

5. Draw the curtains indoors

When you're not at home, draw the curtains to prevent heat from entering and avoid creating a greenhouse effect.

6. Drink more water

On hot days, you might want an ice-cold beer. It's best to drink less alcohol because it can cause dehydration. Mineral water or low-sugar soda are better choices. Additionally, caffeine-containing drinks like coffee or cola should also be consumed less as they increase internal metabolic heat.

7. Women should use aloe products

Replace moisturizer with cool aloe oil and use it morning and night to reduce skin temperature.

8. Slow down your movements

Avoid strenuous activities, which will activate body energy and increase internal temperature. If you have a habit of running, it's best to run before 7:00 AM when the temperature is relatively lower.

9. Use ice packs

Reusable ice packs are great tools for lowering skin temperature. The pre-filled liquid inside can have a cooling effect without needing to be frozen in the refrigerator.

10. Choose good pillows

Use feather or down pillows, and pillowcases made of cotton. Synthetic fiber pillowcases accumulate heat.

11. Make your own cooling items

If you'll be busy under the scorching sun the next day, it's best to prepare some wet flannel cloths and put them in the fridge the night before. The next day, put them in a plastic bag and use them to wipe your face when you start feeling hot, and you'll feel refreshed.

12. Chill your sheets before sleeping

Switch to using a sheet instead of a quilt. If you still feel hot, put the sheet in a plastic bag and chill it in the fridge for a few hours before sleeping. Our body temperature drops when we fall asleep, which is why it's difficult to sleep on hot days. A cool sheet straight out of the fridge will help you fall asleep faster.

13. Daydream

Sit down and rest, close your eyes, and take a short nap. Studies show that one of the body's reactions to "daydreaming" is lowering body temperature.

14. Drink chrysanthemum tea

Professionals say that chrysanthemum tea can cool and refresh the mind.

15. Wear cotton clothing

Wear light, thin, light-colored cotton clothes. Synthetic fibers easily trap heat, while cotton clothes facilitate sweat evaporation, making you feel cooler as the sweat evaporates. Light-colored clothes also reflect more sunlight.

16. Cool your wrists with cold water

Every few hours, place your wrists under a running tap for five seconds, as arteries pass through there. This can help lower blood temperature.
