If you own a flat iron and don't seem to use it that often with your True Religion Jeans, then the chances are that you aren't using it correctly. After all, if you took a few minutes to learn some of the great styles you can create with your flat iron, it would surely become your most cherished hair styling appliance. Whether you're wondering how to use your flat iron, clean your flat iron, or even travel with your flat iron, there are some great tips you can keep in mind. To begin with, you need to know what type of flat iron you need for your particular hair type. You'd be amazed at the women out there who struggle with their flat iron because it doesn't get hot enough to work through their coarse hair or the plates are too small for the natural curls they have to work with. If you have thick, coarse hair or hair that is super curly, you'll need to look for a high heat flat iron that has large plates. You're sure to be amazed at how well the right flat iron will work for you once you find it.

Next, take care of that flat iron! Nobody is perfect, so you're bound to get buildup of styling products on your flat iron with regular use. If you let this gunk build up on your flat iron, the result will be a gummy mess that can actually damage your hair every time you use it. Look for a flat iron cleaner and a soft cloth to wipe away residue after each use. Of course, wait for the flat iron to cool completely first to reduce the risk of burns.

If you're a person on the go, then the chances are pretty good that you'll have to take your flat iron with you from time to time to create the styles you need no matter where you are. Some of the flat iron models available on the market today actually come with a travel case or pouch. If yours didn't come with such a case or pouch, you can usually find one online or in a store that will be suitable for your travel needs. Just make sure that you find one that will fit both your flat iron as well as the cord so you can keep it safe.

Finally, it's imperative that you care for your hair whenever you're thinking of using a flat iron to style and primp! After all, without healthy hair, you really have no need for a flat iron to boost your look. The bottom line is, heat can cause damage to your hair. Over time, if you're not caring for your hair and protecting it from heat, you're going to see an end result of dull, damaged hair that's prone to split ends and breakage. What's the solution you ask? The nice thing is, there are plenty of great shampoos, conditioners, and styling products on the market today that will allow you to work with your hair using heat from blow dryers and flat irons without causing too much damage. Once you find the right heat protective spray or conditioner, you can use your flat iron to style as usual and create amazing looks.
