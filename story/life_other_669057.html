Who has torn whose sky?

In the river of time, I drift passively with the waves. I am dizzy from being tossed by the wind and waves. I feel that I have lost myself and many beautiful things. I have been thrown into a forgotten corner, left to mold, rot, and disappear.

I am very sad. I find that I have really lost a lot. I often indulge in my own fantasies, drowning myself in old memories. I want to give myself a smile, I want to point at the mirror and say: "You, must live on!" But I don't have the courage. Sometimes I wonder if I am living in a dream, who am I? I feel like I am just a moving puppet, but who is the manipulator?

I heard that Hai Zi was a poet who committed suicide. Although he has been dead for a long time, although I have just met him, I am in pain, crying inside. Human beings are fragile. I wonder when I will die? Will the sky be blue when I die? Will I commit suicide? I am fragile too, I think.

Lying on the railway tracks, how many pieces will Hai Zi be crushed into? Red, will it dye the whole world? I like the sound of windbells, clear and crisp. But now, I am trembling at the sound of the train whistle. The long curve, the rolling circle, roaring past. Accompanied by the rumbling sound, as if crushing the whole world, suffocating! Vomiting! Bright red!

I see that the leaves of the poplar tree have fallen again, bare and deathly silent. I understand what desolation and bleakness mean. The branches stab my skin like swords. I only feel that everything before my eyes is blurred. No matter how wide I open my eyes, I can't see anything.

Winter is coming, I mutter, wrapping my coat tighter. It's getting cold, really cold. I look up at the sky, trying to find a hint of blue. I won't be able to see the stars tonight. I love stars. Walking on the street, standing still, letting the leaves be swept up by the wind, spinning around, sliding past my feet.
