Keyword research and text optimization

by soft0604 on 2009-02-12 16:53:30

Last time we said that in SEO optimization, "king of kings" relies on website content, and the key to website content is the use of keywords. Proper use of keywords can bring us quite a bit of traffic. Therefore, website content should be organized based on keywords.

Generally, it is recommended that the number of key keywords used on each page does not exceed three to four. Using too many keywords can disrupt the theme. Do you remember when you were in middle school, your Chinese teacher would usually give some key aspects or even specific words to use after assigning an essay topic? This writing method can be applied to web text writing by organizing language around keywords.

We use three to four keyword phrases in the text, but if a text is filled with keywords throughout, even to the extent that it damages the readability of sentences and exercises, this will not only anger the webpage readers but also draw the displeasure of search engines. Keyword density refers to the ratio of keyword usage in all the readable text on a webpage that search engines can read. Each search engine has different views on keyword density, but generally, 2%—8% is ideal. Simply put, out of every hundred words, having the keyword appear two to eight times is sufficient.

Compiling webpage text is very important for the quality of the webpage and the website. With the results of keyword analysis, we need to know that the webpage needs to be written around the keywords. However, to write unique content, creativity is indispensable. Specifically, how to write creative content is something you need to think about.

Keyword research is ongoing. In the process of building and promoting a website, keyword research is a continuous task. This requires periodically studying whether the original keywords are still as important and if there are any new keywords that need to be added. Generally, many people who do search engine optimization only conduct keyword research once at the beginning and then never revisit it. This lacks a long-term concept of search engine marketing and sensitivity to search trends.
