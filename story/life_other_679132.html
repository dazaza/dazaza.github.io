The Art of Drinking Beer - The Science Behind Enjoying Beer

Beer has long swept across the globe, becoming a world-famous beverage. In our country, beer is loved by almost everyone. However, how can we drink beer in a way that allows us to better appreciate its rich flavors and charm? King Edward VII of Britain once said, "Drinking beer should not be just about drinking for the sake of it. When drinking beer, one should smell its aroma, observe its color, savor its taste, and then drink it slowly, using it as a topic of conversation." Today, as we raise our glasses and drink heartily, let's delve into all things related to beer.

Beer Requires an Appropriate Temperature

Firstly, it's important to understand the relationship between beer and temperature. Here, temperature has two meanings: one is the temperature of the beer itself; the other is the environmental temperature. The temperature of the beer directly affects its foam and taste. If the beer is too warm, there will be excessive foam which quickly dissipates, leading to insufficient carbonation, making the beer bland and less refreshing. If the beer is too cold, there will be less foam, with a more pronounced bitterness and reduced aroma. Therefore, an inappropriate temperature can significantly diminish the flavor of the beer. So, what is the ideal temperature for beer? At around 10℃, the beer's foam is richest, fine and long-lasting, with a concentrated aroma and a comfortable mouthfeel. However, the temperature of beer is often constrained by the environment. As the environmental temperature changes throughout the four seasons, the temperature of the beer will inevitably be affected. Thus, people are required to adjust the beer's temperature to an appropriate level in different environments, allowing you to experience the best color, aroma, and taste of the beer.

The Role of Beer Glasses

When beer is poured into a transparent, colorless glass, the upper third consists of white, fine foam that spills over the rim, forming a half-spherical white flower. The lower two-thirds of the liquid is pale yellow and transparent, with a streak of light passing through it, causing golden stars to shimmer in the glass. To give beer its visual appeal in terms of color and appearance, nowadays most people use large, thick-walled glass cups with handles. When using these large handled cups, the thumb remains relaxed and nearly free. When the cup is brought to the lips, the elbow does not extend outward, the elbow remains closed, the back muscles stretch, and the mouth opens wide, allowing the beer to flow smoothly through the mouth. Therefore, using a large handled cup makes drinking and swallowing convenient. When using a wine glass, the middle finger and ring finger hold the stem, keeping the thumb free, thus achieving a similar effect as using a large cup. For couples enjoying a drink together, a tulip-shaped wine glass is recommended. At this time, the pale yellow liquid and soft tones create a dreamlike charm, making the couple feel intoxicated even without getting drunk on alcohol.

The Way to Drink Beer

To maintain the taste and flavor of beer, drinking beer also has requirements different from other types of alcohol. In ancient China, poets and scholars would sip wine while composing poetry and painting, taking their time with each sip. However, to fully appreciate the flavor of beer and feel its refreshing sensation, one cannot sip it slowly. In beer industry terminology, drinking beer should be done by "whale swallowing," meaning taking large gulps. This way, the beer stays in the mouth for a short period, preventing it from warming up, ensuring minimal changes in flavor and mouthfeel. Therefore, a glass of beer should be finished as quickly as possible. If using a regular glass, the beer need not be filled to the brim but rather enough for one gulp.

How to Pour Beer

Bartenders in bars and beer houses have mastered a set of proficient pouring techniques. The beer they pour contains two-thirds or half liquid and one-third or half foam, with the foam towering high without spilling a drop. If you follow the procedures and key points below, you can also pour a beautiful glass of beer.

One, wash the beer glass thoroughly and rinse it again with cold water before pouring. If possible, place the cleaned glass in the refrigerator to cool down first.

Two, when pouring, hold the beer bottle at a right angle to the glass and pour directly into the center of the glass until the foam rises to the rim.

Three, wait for a moment, allowing the foam to subside slightly, then pour again into the center of the glass until the foam forms a crown shape, overflowing the rim.
