Chinese domain names are ready to go, how much do you know about registration common sense?

by now on 2008-10-30 17:28:16

Recently, ICANN launched a global deployment survey for non-English domain suffixes such as Chinese, with the first stop chosen in China. Domain names in the format of "xx.China" will be widely popularized next year, and a large number of enterprises will open their own "China domains."

A domain name is an important address resource on the Internet, characterized by uniqueness and scarcity. However, most people are still unfamiliar with domain registration. How can one successfully register a Chinese domain name? The person in charge of Times Network introduced this issue:

1. Choose a larger and reputable domain registrar. A domain name is your private property. Although its price is not high, it may have significant potential for appreciation. Nowadays, many English domains are gradually moving towards Sinicization, such as "Baidu Youa" and "Kaixin Network," where these domains are gradually aligning with Chinese pinyin. Such domains conform to the writing and memory habits of Chinese people, making them simple and easy to remember. This not only promotes the widespread adoption of domains but also advances the development of the Internet.

2. Understand the domain registration fee, renewal price, registration period; how long after payment it takes to successfully register; regarding domain security and ownership: make sure to correctly fill out the domain owner, registration contact, and email address. If the registration information changes, it should be updated promptly.

3. Have management permissions for the domain: whether you have the domain management password, if you can modify the basic domain information, and if free transfers in and out are allowed.

After successfully registering a domain, don't assume that pointing it to a website means everything is fine. Issues related to domain usage are often overlooked in domain management.

1. Domain DNS resolution

Domain resolution requires specialized DNS servers, and at this point, the stability of the DNS server plays a very important role. It is recommended that you choose the VDNS domain resolution technology provided by Times Network (www.now.cn). It can intelligently resolve to the optimal IP address based on the client's source IP address. It offers full domain management rights and a powerful domain control panel, allowing users to modify domain information themselves.

2. Domain pointing settings

According to current netizens' habits, many directly input domain URLs without www in the IE address bar. To ensure that now.cn can also directly access Times Network, binding both with and without www to one website has almost become an industry standard. A small setting not only facilitates visitors but also increases website traffic.

In summary, domain registration is the first step for individuals and enterprises to embark on e-commerce. Whoever seizes the resources first means gaining a network advantage. CNNIC predicts that the public will be able to apply for Chinese domain names with the ".中国" suffix around May or June next year. Major domain registrars are actively preparing promotional work, and the domain market next year is bound to be extremely lively.
