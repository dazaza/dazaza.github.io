Couples who have lived together for more than 1 year without taking contraceptive measures but fail to conceive are considered to have infertility. Couples who have never conceived within 2 years of marriage are referred to as primary infertility; those who have had childbirth or abortion and have been unable to conceive for another consecutive 2 years are called secondary infertility. Sex is the process of conceiving a baby. If the couple does not handle their sex life well, it will not only affect the quality of life, but may also lead to infertility. Therefore, some unhealthy sexual activities can cause infertility. So which unhealthy sexual activities can lead to infertility?

The occurrence of infertility is generally related to several factors such as the frequency of sexual activity, sexual habits, and timing of sexual activity.

1. Sexual Habits

In some rural areas with poor knowledge of sex, many infertile women have had intercourse during menstruation, mistakenly believing that having sex during menstruation can increase the pregnancy rate. In fact, having sex during menstruation stimulates the body to produce anti-sperm antibodies, varicocele, and can cause immune infertility. It can also lead to bacterial infections moving upward, causing inflammation of the fallopian tubes or leading to tubal blockage and infertility.

2. Frequency of Sexual Activity

If couples have intercourse too frequently, there won't be enough sperm supply, and the quality will also be poor, affecting fertilization. Additionally, as sperm is an antigenic substance, frequent stimulation in women can continuously produce anti-sperm antibodies, causing sperm to aggregate or lose vitality, directly affecting fertilization. Therefore, excessive sexual activity often leads to counterproductive results.

3. Timing of Sexual Activity

Choosing to have intercourse during ovulation can increase the chances of conception. For example, if the cycle is 28 days, from the first day of menstruation to the 14th day is the ovulation day. For cycles shorter than 28 days, the calculation method can be adjusted accordingly. Generally, only one egg is released per menstrual cycle, and the lifespan of the egg is 18-30 hours, so it must meet the sperm within 24 hours to be fertilized. Sperm can survive in the cervical canal for 1-2 weeks, but its fertilization ability is generally believed to not exceed 48 hours. Based on this, having intercourse two days before the expected ovulation day, on the expected ovulation day, and one day after the expected ovulation day can increase the chances of conception.

4. Sexual Climax

Achieving sexual climax indeed increases the chances of conception. There are three reasons: 1) During sexual climax, the uterus experiences positive pressure, which sharply decreases afterward, creating negative pressure, making it easier for sperm to enter the uterine cavity. 2) Due to sexual excitement, the position of the uterus rises, making the distance between the cervix and the semen pool closer, facilitating the inward movement of sperm. 3) The vagina is normally acidic with a pH of 4-5, which is not conducive to sperm survival and activity. During sexual excitement, vaginal fluid increases and the pH rises, making it more suitable for sperm activity. Therefore, both partners learning some knowledge about sexual psychology and physiology, promoting the wife's sexual climax, can not only improve the quality of sexual life but also potentially increase the chances of fertility.
