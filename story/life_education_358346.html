The term "public welfare" has never really been related to me until the Yushu earthquake not long ago, during which I was deeply touched by what a friend of mine did. What did my friend do? Please read more>>

Every June 14th is World Blood Donor Day. Our city held a promotional event for Blood Donor Day on the 13th. I met a friend recently who is part of the local rare blood type alliance and has been planning the promotion activities for the 13th. I introduced him to another friend of mine who does performance art, and they immediately hit it off, hoping to use performance art to help promote the Rare Blood Type Alliance.

I took leave in advance and participated in the promotional event on the 13th as a volunteer. I also donated 300cc of blood无偿. It's funny, when I entered the blood donation room, my ring finger got pricked first for testing, and I was identified as Type B. After rolling up my sleeve, I said, "Let's do 400!" The beautiful doctor said, "You're so thin, let's do 300." Then I insisted on 400. Unexpectedly, the doctor was equally persistent, "No, 300!" Reluctantly, I agreed to 300. After tying an elastic band around my arm, a needle was inserted into my vein. It didn't hurt much, but my hand felt a little numb from the elastic band. After doing dozens of fist-clenching exercises, 300cc was collected. That was it. No discomfort at all.

Then I continued distributing flyers... and took a group photo with the human statues. These human statues were all volunteers made up of RH- rare blood type holders.

DSC00370

In fact, many people are not unfamiliar with the term "public welfare". But it always seems far away from oneself. In reality, it's right beside us. So what can you do for public welfare? Perhaps your small actions may seem insignificant. But when millions of caring individuals come together, their power is great enough to save many lives. Spare just a little of your time to contribute to public welfare. The flowing love will eventually converge into a surging river, continuously flowing into lakes and seas!
