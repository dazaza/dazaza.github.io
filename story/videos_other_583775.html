Experts said that the group-buying market in China could only support the survival of five or six group-buying websites.

by beetgk2958 on 2011-11-24 15:39:48

A few days ago, I suddenly received a call from a friend who works as a media specialist at a well-known group-buying website telling me that he was leaving. The reason I say "suddenly" is because in the morning he had just asked me if I had any group-buying related topics recently, but by afternoon he had already completed his resignation procedures. The speed was indeed so fast that it left people speechless. According to him, the reason for his departure was that this industry really has no future, and it would be wise to start looking for another way out early.

This makes sense. The current development of the group-buying industry in Beijing has an astonishing resemblance to the changing seasons. After experiencing the ups and downs of the first three quarters, China's nearly 5000 group-buying websites are also facing the severe test of winter this year.

In fact, since August and September this year, the competitive pressure on domestic group-buying websites has begun to show. On the Baidu Group Buying Navigation page, there are nearly a hundred online shopping websites of various sizes, but only about ten group-buying websites can be listed on the navigation homepage, including well-known ones such as Lashou.com, Meituan, and JuHuaSuan etc. This seems to indicate the current survival status of domestic group-buying websites. Data from Yitao.com, China's largest shopping search engine, shows that more than 89% of the market share is monopolized by large group-buying websites, which include Juhuasuan, Wowotuan, Lashou.com, Meituan.com etc., as shown on the group-buying navigation website. Only a little over one-tenth of the market share is left to be divided among more than 5000 group-buying websites, showing how harsh the survival environment is.

Still according to Yitao’s data, by September this year, at least more than 400 group-buying websites have made major adjustments, stopping updates, undergoing redesigns, or simply shutting down altogether. From September until now, at least more than 700 group-buying websites have disappeared or transformed. Of course, even well-known group-buying websites are not having a good time either. First came reports of large-scale layoffs at Gaopeng.com and Kaixin Group Buy, followed by Taobao's cutting its staff by half, Wowotuan laying off up to 70% of its workforce, and so on. For a while, complaints and integrity issues such as "service reduction," "absconding with funds," and "salary cuts and layoffs" became internet buzzwords. Under multiple pressures, it is reasonable that group-buying websites are entering their "winter."

The arrival of the group-buying winter was actually expected, and it doesn't come as a surprise that it arrived so early. The current domestic group-buying market is not very large. In this still underdeveloped market, group-buying websites have wildly increased to more than 5000 in a short period of time, so it's no wonder that the words "bubble" and "reshuffle" have been accompanying them all along.

The arrival of winter is not the problem; the problem is how to get through this harsh and cold winter. In fact, most domestic group-buying websites have no choice when facing the winter—disappearance is the final road. Only those relatively well-known websites in the leading cluster of the industry have some room for choice. Of course, their options are not many either—"salary cuts and layoffs" seem to be the last and most effective adjustment measures. After all, only by getting through the difficulties will they have the possibility of making a comeback.

Some experts say that the domestic group-buying market can only accommodate the survival of five or six group-buying websites, and the rest are bound to fail, according to a comprehensive list of group-buying websites. Now it seems that this statement makes sense—how could such a small market withstand the severe homogenized competition of 5000 enterprises?

From 5000 to 5, such an elimination rate will inevitably be a "bloody and cruel" big battle. The entire group-buying industry will face a life-and-death test. Only by persisting is there a possibility of becoming the "survivor king" to embrace a beautiful new world of domestic group-buying in a healthy state.
