Basic concepts and scope:

IT = Information Technology. In fact, information technology has three levels: the first level is hardware, mainly referring to host computers and network communication equipment for data storage, processing, and transmission; the second level refers to software, including various types of software that can be used to collect, store, retrieve, analyze, apply, and evaluate information. This includes commercial management software such as ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning), CRM (Customer Relationship Management), SCM (Supply Chain Management), as well as WF (workflow) management software for enhancing process management, DW/DM (data warehouse and data mining) software for auxiliary analysis, etc.; the third level refers to application, which involves collecting, storing, retrieving, analyzing, applying, and evaluating various types of information, including direct decision-making assistance using ERP, CRM, SCM software, as well as utilizing other decision analysis models or with the help of DW/DM technologies to further improve the quality of analysis and assist decision-makers in making decisions (emphasizing one point, just assisting rather than replacing human decision-making). Some people understand IT by combining the first two layers into one, collectively referring to the storage, processing, and transmission of information, while others combine the latter two layers into one, dividing them into front-end hardware and back-end software. Typically, the third layer hasn't received enough attention, but in fact, only when information is effectively applied can the value of IT be fully realized, truly achieving the goal of informatization. Informatization itself is not the goal; it's just a better means to achieve goals under the current era background.

Information technology itself is just a tool, like a sharp sword or a good pen. Buying it cannot necessarily guarantee how much your martial arts skills or handwriting will improve; you need to constantly practice how to wield the sword or write. Informatization construction also requires you to continuously enhance your ability to use information, which is truly the core and hardest part. All the efforts lie outside the question! The purpose of informatization (digitization) is not to implement systems to get a few numbers; it is just the foundation. The essence lies in the character "transformation," integrating relevant information about various resources and configuring them reasonably and optimally. For example, using historical information to assist in sales forecasting, procurement planning, production planning, delivery planning, inventory planning, issuing instructions according to these plans, and rolling revising plans based on actual operating conditions. Dr. Joseph Harrington proposed computer-integrated manufacturing (CIM) containing two basic viewpoints: one is that all aspects of enterprise production, including market analysis, product design, processing and manufacturing, business management, and after-sales service, are an inseparable whole that must be closely connected and uniformly considered; the second is that the entire operation process is essentially a process of data collection, transmission, and processing, and the final product can be seen as the physical manifestation of data. If the information system is implemented but not used to assist decision-making, and operations continue in the original way, then the role of the information system may be limited to liberating certain tasks of certain positions, such as statistical reports, while simultaneously increasing the workload of other positions. A large amount of experience shows that if the data collected by the information system is not used, it is impossible to achieve accurate, timely, and complete information for decision-making assistance, and only by using the information system can its accuracy, timeliness, and completeness be gradually improved. According to the evaluation standards of core competitiveness, constructing core competitiveness essentially comes down to integration capabilities, and the more overt the capability, the easier it is to imitate. The tip of the iceberg, the less exposed on the outside, the harder it is to imitate, and the more likely it is to constitute core competitiveness.

Thomas H. "Converting data into something more useful requires significant human effort and intelligence, but most organizations only view this issue from a technical perspective. Therefore, we need to clarify some frequently confused basic concepts here.

Data = records of facts, such as the sales of Series A products in East China last quarter were 1.2 million yuan.

Information = Data + Meaning, such as the sales of Series A products in East China last quarter decreased by 25% compared to the same period last year.

Intelligence = Information + Understanding (understanding) and Reasoning (reasoning), such as analyzing whether the reason is due to poor performance of sales units in East China, or Series A products entering the decline phase, or the company's overall marketing activities lagging behind, strong promotions by competitors leading to this? Or other reasons.

Knowledge = Problem-solving skills (skills), what strategies should the company adopt in response to this issue?

Wisdom = Selection of Knowledge (Selection) There may be multiple action plans, but choosing which one (strategy) * wisdom. Action will then produce new transaction data.

There are multiple cyclic relationships between data, information, intelligence, knowledge, wisdom, actions, and management activities.

IT is the abbreviation of Information Technology, meaning "information technology", including modern computer, network, communication and other information field technologies. The widespread application of IT marks the entry into the information society. Different people and different books have different interpretations of this. But one view that almost everyone agrees with is that IT consists of the following three parts:

----- Sensing technology This is the extension and expansion of human sensory organs, the most obvious example being barcode readers;

----- Communication technology This is the extension and expansion of the human nervous system, responsible for transmitting information functions;

----- Computer technology This is the extension and expansion of human brain functions, responsible for processing information.

Informatization refers to using information technology to transform other industries and sectors, thereby improving corporate efficiency. In this process, information technology plays the role of a powerful tool.
