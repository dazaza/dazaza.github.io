Symptom One: Dehydration and Dryness

nbsp；

UV rays can rapidly dehydrate epidermal cells, leading to dryness, redness, peeling, etc. The skin loses its luster, becoming tight and even flaky, with a rough texture. Makeup application may result in powder sticking, and skincare products may cause stinging and discomfort.

nbsp；

Jiayun Beauty School Countermeasure: Hydration and Moisturization

nbsp；

Just like us under the sun, the skin feels thirsty and needs cool drinks to relieve it. Apply a post-sun repair mask to soothe swelling and pain while quickly replenishing lost moisture; or soak cotton pads with soothing toner and apply them to uncomfortable areas to alleviate skin stress. After bathing, take advantage of fully opened pores and select repair creams containing aloe gel and seaweed essence to maximize skin comfort. To reduce skin burden, it is recommended to choose oil-free products. Use a hydrating essence before makeup in the morning to make foundation and sunscreen easier to apply, achieving two goals at once.

Symptom Two: Redness and Allergy

nbsp；

Allergies caused by sun exposure may have two reasons: one is UV allergy, where people sensitive to UV rays need extra protection during summer; another is acute allergies due to decreased skin resistance after excessive sun exposure.

nbsp；

Nanjing Beauty School Countermeasure: Calming Relief

nbsp；

When facing sun-induced sensitivity issues, first calm the skin and lower its surface temperature to physically relieve redness and heat. Be sure not to wash your face with hot or warm water. Many post-sun repair products contain cooling and calming ingredients, providing a refreshing sensation for the skin. If you don't have specific post-sun repair products, placing daily skincare items in the refrigerator for a while before use can produce similar effects. At this time, preventing inflammation and further stimulation is crucial. Avoid using rough items to wipe your face, minimize unnecessary hand contact with the skin, and pause exfoliation until the skin is fully recovered.

Symptom Three: Aging and Slackening

nbsp；

The greatest harm of UV rays to the skin isn't just tanning but also accelerating aging, making it hard to guard against. Post-sun facial skin becomes loose and thin, with enlarged pores. Fragile parts such as around the eyes and mouth easily develop fine lines with expressions. UV rays increase cell aging and free radicals, causing skin slackness and loss of youth. Without timely remedy, irreversible wrinkles may develop.

nbsp；

Jiayun Beauty School Countermeasure: Combat and Repair

nbsp；

Generally, post-sun fine lines can be partially relieved through hydration, but this often addresses surface phenomena. For fine lines that have penetrated beneath the stratum corneum, to prevent and alleviate more serious conditions, the first step is to solve the skin's antioxidant problem. UV exposure generates a large number of free radicals, accelerating cellular oxidation. Using products containing antioxidants such as vitamin C and derivatives can effectively prevent free radical generation and simultaneously brighten the skin. Repairing skin cells damaged by sunlight and free radicals, supplementing collagen and peptides through cosmetics to restore skin firmness and elasticity is the second step in handling post-sun fine lines, restoring skin vitality and rejuvenation.
