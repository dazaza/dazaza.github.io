Has a connection with Heibao through the eyes

by stdxiaoyank on 2011-09-13 21:59:28

It was a fateful meeting.

Passing by the dog market, I saw a more than two feet long puppy, nesting in a wooden basin, with four little heads under its belly, looking lazy and motionless. I couldn't help but be curious, so I went over to take a look. The other three just glanced at me as if they were already used to such prying eyes. But it, upon seeing me, jumped up and started licking the hand I extended.

This is what you call fate.

When the children of the neighborhood see Heibao, they point and laugh: "Haha, that dog doesn't have eyes!" My Heibao does have eyes; it's just that his whole body is pitch black and shiny. There's only a bit of white fur on his belly and four little paws, so if you don't look carefully, you can't see them. In such case, how could you tell where the eyes are? Their eyes aren't as clearly defined as humans'. Heibao has a small sore on his belly, which he was born with. The doctor said it was nothing serious, so we left it alone. Later, when we sent Heibao back to Luojiang, he became one of our family members. As he grew bigger, he developed good living habits and never pooped or peed inside the house. If he really had to go badly, he would run to the door and knock hard. Mom loves him so much that sometimes I feel jealous. She always keeps cakes and sausages as his snacks. His main food is usually bone soup with rice or boiled eggs, and calcium tablets are also no problem for him. However, Heibao's favorite food is fried dough sticks. On her way back from sending her son to school in the morning, mom would pass by the fried dough stick stall, where the vendor recognized Heibao. When he sees Heibao coming, he gives him some food and waits for mom to pay later. When Heibao wants water, he will look at the faucet while looking at people. Moreover, unless absolutely necessary, Heibao does not drink stagnant water.

Animals all have insight. Before you know it, they have already made you worry about them. Just because of their simple gratitude...

We needed some shrubs and branches for the vegetable garden. It followed mom to the back mountain, with its black eyes fixed on mom without turning. When mom accidentally moved her body, it barked loudly. Mom told it there was nothing wrong and she didn't fall, then it stopped. I think it understands human language.

Heibao can find all the unusual things in the house, such as rats occasionally under the bed, geckos behind the door corner, and of course, many toys dropped by the son. But unfortunately, Heibao himself often can't find his own toys... Perhaps it's like humans being confused when they're involved.

My friend Hanshan and I only return to Luojiang on weekends. Once it sees us, it must jump onto us to show affection, otherwise, it won't be peaceful all day. One night, I played computer games at my aunt's house until nine o'clock. The son went home alone (about fifty meters away). Seeing that I wasn't leaving, Heibao refused to follow. I said, "Bao, if you don't go, Xiao will walk alone in the dark?" It looked at me, then at Xiao, and happily went to accompany the son.

The son and Heibao are very good friends. When the son does homework, Heibao squats at his feet and sleeps. When the son goes out, it races with the son. When the son eats, it shares with the son. A bag of chips is often split equally between them. Thinking about it, I feel guilty because I spend less time with my son than Heibao does. In the blink of an eye, half a year has passed. Heibao has grown into an adult dog, about a foot long, weighing around ten pounds, plump and shiny. The neighbors in the village all say that our Heibao is a handsome man. Isn't that true? In our family's mind, Heibao has already transcended the concept of a dog.

A while ago, Heibao got sick, and mom took him to the pet hospital for intravenous therapy. One day, when I went to that hospital to buy dog food, it hid somewhere far away once it heard the doctor's voice, refusing to come out no matter how much we called. Only after I gave in and said I wouldn't buy anything did it follow me home. Smart like a dog, it still remembers past pain, whereas humans often forget pain once the wound heals. It seems that in certain moments, humans are fundamentally inferior to dogs. Then why do we despise and trample on their lives?

However, our Heibao is now going through the biggest calamity of his life. A motorcycle ran over his hip. At that moment, it stood up and ran far away to hide. Mom searched everywhere but couldn't find it. Later, she found it hiding behind an abandoned factory and brought it home. They say that dogs won't die at home. They will hide when they are dying. I imagine that our Heibao was enduring the test of life and death at that time, and it didn't want us to see its painful appearance. But I wonder if Heibao knows that this nature of his is actually cruel to us humans. Or, perhaps humans are the same. When we lick our wounds alone, aren't we also cruel to our loved ones and friends?

After going to the hospital, the doctor gave it painkillers and anti-inflammatory injections, examined its body, and didn't give any IVs. He said that if the medicine works and shows some improvement, then it will be fine. If there is no improvement, we should mentally prepare ourselves, as it might result in paralysis of the hindquarters.

I just want Heibao to live healthily. Is that too much to ask?
