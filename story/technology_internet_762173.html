Agent Revelation: How does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 14:07:56

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. Thus, establishing a search marketing system has become one of the main tasks for 360 this year. Under the immense pressure from Baidu’s extensive nationwide channels, how does 360, as a latecomer, manage to enter the market?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search, revealing the expansion status of 360's search marketing system. We learned that 360's rapidly growing market share made the agents smell an opportunity for profit, and Baidu's tightening of agency rights over the years indirectly contributed to the agents switching sides. The number of 360's agents and orders have significantly increased compared to the beginning of the year.

Specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by agents. So far, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and cities, totaling 27 agencies. Four months ago, this number was only 13. Moreover, many local agents have already started developing sub-agencies. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular locally. "Just like during Baidu's early development phase, many agents at that time gained considerable benefits, so now that 360 is entering the search market, they are all eager to secure this agency as soon as possible," said an agent from Henan to Sohu IT. They obtained the agency rights last month.

2. The development of 360 agents also benefits from the gap formed by Baidu reclaiming its agency rights. The above-mentioned Henan agent stated, "After many years of development, Baidu has reclaimed many local agency rights and set up branch companies or provincial chief agents." 360 has not established branch companies in various places yet, still mainly relying on agents. Some of these agents used to represent Baidu ads, some represented Sogou or Sou ads, and some even represented 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, with most agents setting up independent companies specifically for representing 360 ads.

3. Low price is an important advantage of 360 Search ads locally. An agent from Wuhan believed that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: less competition, low prices, and high conversion rates. "Some keyword ad prices for 360 Search are less than one-fifth of Baidu's," he said. Leveraging its reputation as the "second largest search engine" and the groundwork laid by Baidu in educating the market earlier, this agent claimed that promotion is not too difficult. However, many clients remain on the fence because 360 has just entered the market, and they "also need to see if 360 is really willing to fully develop its search engine."

4. In the past six months, the order volume for 360 Search ads has grown rapidly. In March, we reported that 360 Search had about 1,000 orders in Beijing over three months. A salesperson from Tian Di Online, one of 360's Beijing region agents, told us that now they can exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing: Tian Di Online and Ju Tao Culture. The latter entered later and is relatively smaller in scale. Henan San Bai Liu, which just secured the agency rights last month, has already completed more than 1,000 orders.

5. The average order value is around 10,000 yuan, which is not low. The minimum account opening fee for 360 Search ads is 4,600 yuan, including a 600 yuan service fee and 4,000 yuan prepaid deposit for advertising. An agent from Wuhan revealed that the current average order value is around 10,000 yuan. For comparison, Baidu's average customer revenue last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads have covered all industries. Currently, 360 Search ads have covered all industries except healthcare. Recently, it has also opened previously restricted industries such as health supplements and adult products. It is worth noting that 360 currently only accepts enterprise customers and does not accept individual customers (Baidu accepts individual customers). Moreover, one company can only advertise for one site (Baidu allows multiple sites). Multiple agents mentioned they are developing offline customers in education and consulting sectors.

But can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360's agency system has initially taken shape, its competition with Baidu is still in a very early stage. When talking to a Baidu agent, he frankly stated, "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

From the feedback of various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet fierce. "360 Search is now more about giving customers a new market and new choice," said an agent. Many customers will place ads on both platforms.

However, this is definitely a temporary situation. The scale of 360 Search is still small and requires more subsequent investment. Nonetheless, the monetization of search ads will undoubtedly be the next most imaginative income growth point for 360. When 360's client base truly grows and prices rise, another inevitable battle may ensue between 360 and Baidu.
