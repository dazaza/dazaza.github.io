This is closely related to the friendly policies that Tanzania provides for foreign investors and foreign products. In Tanzania, tariffs are relatively low, and the tariffs for re-exporting to neighboring countries are also very low, or even exempt from tariffs. Moreover, its neighboring countries such as Ethiopia, Somalia, Mozambique, Uganda, etc., are all on the path to development. Speaking of the automobile industry, with the development of local and neighboring countries, the demand for new cars and auto parts in East Africa is increasing year by year. At the same time, because most areas in Africa are emerging markets, and the automobile industry in African countries is extremely backward, almost zero, so automobiles and auto parts largely depend on imports, and there is a very large market gap. In addition, due to the relatively low consumption level and purchasing power in the local area, high-quality and affordable Chinese products are very favored. Currently, the number of Chinese enterprises developing this region is not many, but their market share is indeed quite considerable. Therefore, the business opportunities of the East Africa International Auto Show cannot be ignored.\r\n\r\n\r\n \r\nMarket Introduction\r\n\r\nDar es Salaam (Dar-res-Salaam) is the capital of Tanzania, as well as the largest city in Tanzania, the national political, economic, and cultural center. It is located on the coastal plain of the middle segment of the Indian Ocean shore in Africa, controlling the key chokepoint of the West Indian Ocean shipping route. "Dar es Salaam" means "Harbor of Peace". It is an excellent natural seaport, with deep waters in the port area. There are six deep-water berths in the port, which can accommodate thirteen ocean-going vessels simultaneously. It is an important international trade port in East Africa. Some materials from neighboring countries Zambia, Zaire, Burundi are also transshipped here. There is an oil pipeline connecting to Zambia's Ndola. Dar es Salaam is an important industrial city in Tanzania, with industrial output accounting for more than half of the country, mainly focusing on light textile industries. Dar es Salaam is also the starting point of the central railway and the TAZARA Railway, with highways connecting to major towns across the country. It is the largest port in the country, with a naturally excellent harbor bay, complete warehouses, ship repair, and loading/unloading facilities.
