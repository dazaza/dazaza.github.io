Dig a well network - Come and have a drink!

by wajingcom on 2008-04-21 21:28:45

Building a network of personal relationships is like digging a well.

First, you need the motivation to dig the well. "One day I might get thirsty, and then I will need a well to draw water from. In order to have water when I'm thirsty, I need to start digging now."

Secondly, you must make preparations for digging the well.

Next, you must take action. This isn't actually a difficult thing, because you've already unknowingly started. You already have friends, family, colleagues, neighbors, and other social connections. All you need to do now is make this network bigger.

As you start digging the well, you might be shoveling dirt with great effort, sweating profusely. But don't forget, we can't just work hard without looking up at the road ahead. Only by mastering the appropriate techniques can you dig more easily and deeper. Careful thought, creativity, and even a bit of style in your design are also essential. Only in this way can your interpersonal well be dug deeper.

While digging the well, you cannot forget to protect what you've already dug. Maintaining the integrity of the well walls is as important as the progress of digging the well. Networks of personal relationships are the same way; maintaining contact with others is as important as establishing contact with them.

Also, you must be careful not to become trapped in the well and unable to escape. The well-digger cannot fall into the well themselves. How can you avoid taking detours while building your network of personal relationships? Mistakes that you might make have already been made by those who came before you. Let's learn from the experiences of our predecessors.

The more proficient you are in every step of digging the well, the deeper your well will be. You may even want to pass on these experiences and insights to others in your network so they can benefit as well. As your friends feel the same happiness as you, your well will be dug even deeper.

Now it's up to you. Start digging!
