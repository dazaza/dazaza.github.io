China will encounter the largest range of rainy and snowy weather after autumn, affecting 30 provinces and cities.

by kevine76dm on 2009-11-17 14:35:34

, Sina homepage; According to China National Radio Beijing news on November 9, after causing a sharp drop in temperature in the northeast and other areas, the cold air continued to move southward. In the next three to four days, China will experience the largest scale of autumn temperature drop and rain-snow weather since this fall. The temperature drop and rain-snow weather will affect thirty provinces and municipalities except Tibet and Yunnan.

This cold air started affecting China from two regions, Xinjiang and Inner Mongolia and Northeast China, on the seventh day. On the eighth day, it had already brought strong cooling weather to the northeastern part of Inner Mongolia and the Northeast region. Within one night, some areas in Inner Mongolia, Heilongjiang, Jilin and other places experienced a temperature drop of more than sixteen degrees Celsius.

According to forecasts by the Central Meteorological Observatory, affected by both cold and warm air, China will experience a large-scale rain-snow weather process in the next three days. Xinjiang, central and eastern parts of northwest China, central and western parts of Inner Mongolia, North China and other places will have light to moderate rain or snow or sleet, among which some areas in Inner Mongolia, Shaanxi, Shanxi, Hebei and other places will have heavy snow or blizzards. The Yellow River-Huai River area, Jianghuai, Jianghan, Jiangnan, most parts of South China, as well as the eastern part of southwest China and other places will have light to moderate rain or showers, among which some areas in Shaanxi, Henan, Hubei, Jiangxi, Anhui, Jiangsu, Shanghai, Zhejiang, Fujian and other places will have heavy rain.

Expert Li Xiaoquan from the China Meteorological Administration said that from the ninth to the twelfth, China will experience the largest scale of autumn precipitation since this fall. The scope of the temperature drop and rain-snow weather will affect most parts of China except Tibet and Yunnan.

Chief forecaster He Lifu of the Central Meteorological Observatory said that the current cold air invading China is moving relatively slowly, causing prolonged precipitation and a cumulative large temperature drop, with a wide impact range. Currently, not only is there cold air affecting central and eastern China, but also a strong warm and humid airflow. The interaction between the two mainly brings rain and snow weather.
