[1] Don't wait for me to tell you before you act

Following his previous statement "It's the people who support you, so deal with it yourselves!", Premier Wen's recent comment at a government work report consultation meeting - "Don't wait for me to tell you before you act" - has once again been warmly embraced by netizens.

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the government work report was 4.6%, while the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences' Institute of Sociology published a social blue book that announced an urban surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4%. Why is there a 5.2% difference between the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security (MHRSS) and the CASS unemployment rate statistics? The public has developed a strong interest in this question. In light of this "quarrel", Premier Wen made the following statement:

"You, the Ministry of Social Labor Security, should quickly go online, or hold a press conference quickly, explain the differences between the 4.6% and the CASS's 9.4%, don't wait for me to tell you before you act."

As for why these two figures are different, Li Peilin, Director of the Institute of Sociology of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, explained it this way:

"For more than a decade, there has been a difference between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, usually the latter is higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, the two numbers are close, when the employment situation is poor, there will be a large gap between the two numbers. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, such as migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, they are difficult to be counted after becoming unemployed."

However, the MHRSS had another explanation:

"The 9.4% survey unemployment rate is the result of a sample survey of seven thousand people, among which there are 2288 urban economically active population, and among them, there are more than 200 unemployed people, this figure is derived from the comparison of these two numbers."

In fact, we ordinary people all have our own views on these two sets of data:

"As a graduate who has worked in the city for only a few years, my hukou (household registration) is still at school, where can I register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, I can only quickly find a new job myself, how dare I tarnish the national unemployment rate."

For this calculation method that does not include people who are neither employed nor registered, it has long been questioned by domestic and foreign scholars. Premier Wen should make them explain not only the seriously underestimated urban registered unemployment rate but also give us a number that can withstand scrutiny.

[2] Is it because of "May Day"?

Some departments indeed take action without waiting for the premier to speak. After the Guangdong provincial government officially announced the "May Day" holiday plan, Hangzhou, Suzhou, and Nanjing proposed to jointly restore the May Day Golden Week, making the discussion on whether the May Day Golden Week should be restored a focal point.

In response to the current heated discussion, the General Office of the State Council officially responded: Do not arbitrarily restore the May Day Golden Week.

Should the number of working days in a year for a country be unified? Although some comments refer to Germany's central government only stipulating some unified statutory holidays, and the rest of the holidays can be determined by each state, proposing that the "May Day" holiday could be decided by local governments, but have they considered whether it suits China's national conditions under different political systems?

In fact, I just want to ask, why do various places shout at each other without reporting to the General Office of the State Council for approval?

[3] Savings that every province can make

The case of a police officer in Yunnan Province shooting and killing someone, which has already attracted much attention, has recently sparked controversy among the public due to a signed agreement. Mengzi County Public Security Bureau reached a reconciliation agreement with the deceased's family, paying the victim's family a huge compensation of 550,000 yuan.

Regarding the case of police officer Ji Zhongchun shooting and killing someone, Mengzi County Public Security Bureau originally classified it as a personal act, having no relation to his identity and position as a police officer. If it was a personal act, why did the Public Security Bureau pay the compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it this way:

"The reason the County Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances. Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked in the County Public Security Bureau for more than 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home. Out of humanitarianism, the County Public Security Bureau first gave the deceased's family humanitarian compensation. And this 550,000 yuan is office expenses saved by the County Public Security Bureau."

It is well known that the incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun had a dispute with someone while reversing his car. How can a police officer who owns a private car be defined as having poor economic conditions? This can't help but raise the question: what exactly constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the "Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police": If a police officer violates regulations in using weapons, causing unnecessary casualties or property damage, the agency to which the police officer belongs shall compensate according to relevant provisions of the "State Compensation Law." Why doesn't Mengzi Public Security Bureau admit that this compensation is state compensation? If it is not, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money to benefit privately?

[6] Overhyped network entrepreneurship

Suddenly, as if overnight, millions of online stores sprouted everywhere. Amidst the current economic crisis, the internet seems to have become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only are media outlets bombarding students with the message that finding jobs online is a great path, but even during the two sessions, representatives suggested that the state should subsidize college students starting businesses on Taobao.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, network entrepreneurship became an instant favorite overnight, and many domestic colleges and universities have opened online store training classes to help students start businesses. Yes, if everyone rushes to open online stores now, the employment problem can be solved immediately, but what about a year later, ten years later? How many people will truly succeed? Not to mention issues like medical insurance and social security. Network security and legal standardization have always been the most neglected difficulties in the information economy tide. Are our experts and professors considering this point while enthusiastically advocating that college students choose network entrepreneurship?
