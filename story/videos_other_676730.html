In clinical practice, there are many methods to measure the customer's natural breast volume, then selecting implants based on this volume. Methods such as X-rays and MRI can be used, but I believe that measuring the actual breast volume has limited clinical significance and serves only as an auxiliary role. Ultimately, the decision on the implant size should be based on the individual's body shape, breast characteristics, and aesthetic parameters, combined with the surgeon's clinical experience and the patient's desires. Selecting implants according to individual differences is more practically significant. For example, if Zhou Chan and Alain have the same existing breast volume and ultimately receive the same size and shape of breast implants, will they both achieve satisfactory results?
