ZhiSheng Education - SSAT Exam Questions Are Harder Than TOEFL

Young students aspiring to study at private high schools abroad must face a critical hurdle: passing the SSAT or ISEE exams. In the United States, almost all prestigious private secondary schools require applicants to provide SSAT scores or ISEE results (short-term exchange students are exempted). Moreover, SSAT or ISEE scores serve as very important reference indicators; the quality of the applicant's test performance directly determines whether they will be admitted to their desired school. Only by successfully passing the exam can one enter renowned private high schools.

The SSAT and ISEE exams are two nationwide private middle school entrance exams in the United States, chosen independently by private schools in the U.S., Canada, and other countries. The SSAT stands for Secondary School Admission Test. The ISEE is known as the Independent School Entrance Examination. Both the SSAT and ISEE consist of English, mathematics, and essay components. Outside of the U.S. and Canada, the SSAT exam is held annually in January, March, April, November, and December, with registration closing four weeks before the exam date. In the U.S., the exam is held seven times a year, while in regions outside North America, it is held five times annually. These exams primarily measure students' proficiency in mathematics, English, and comprehension. Although the SSAT essay is not scored, it is sent to the admissions departments of the schools applied to as part of the admission consideration. The SSAT exam is divided into two levels: lower level (for candidates currently in grades 5-7) and upper level (for candidates currently in grades 8-11). The ISEE is hosted by the Independent School Entrance Examination Office and the Educational Records Bureau located in New York, mainly assessing students' verbal and numerical reasoning abilities.

Although many parents hope that their children can pass the SSAT exam and successfully enroll in American high schools, experts caution that the exam is not easy to pass. Experts point out, "Some questions are even harder than those on the TOEFL, which imposes higher requirements on the examinees' English proficiency."

The SSAT exam primarily measures students' mathematical, English, and comprehension skills. It consists of four sections: mathematics, verbal, reading, and essay, with a total testing time of 145 minutes. The mathematics section is divided into two parts, each containing 25 problems. Candidates need to solve math calculation problems related to their studies or real-life situations. The reading section includes 40 questions based on 7 passages, focusing on testing reading speed and comprehension ability. Its style resembles that of the GRE, so candidates should read more American novels and literary reviews. The essay question has a relatively high difficulty level, covering topics such as personal experiences, current events, history, and culture. While SSAT does not grade this section, it sends the report form to the students.

Experts suggest that to pass the SSAT exam, students should regularly memorize vocabulary, broaden their reading scope, and practice with real exam questions. Regarding vocabulary, candidates should aim to master at least 3,000 to 4,000 words.
