Sales-oriented talents are the most sought-after in the market.

by bhhgsyfk4143 on 2012-02-08 12:52:48

Sales talents are the most sought-after in the market. The monthly salary of ordinary workers is similar to that of coastal areas, and migrant workers are constantly flowing back. During the interview, the reporter also noticed a change. In the past, companies usually recruited younger workers, and "35 years old" was often a hurdle for job seekers. Recently, many enterprises have adjusted their age requirements to "under 40", and this year, one company even relaxed the age limit for后勤 personnel to "under 55".

Ms. Liu Yina, the general manager of a building materials company in Nanchang, told reporters that after the spring work started, their company needed to recruit more than 100 new welders, polishers, painters, and loaders. However, after attending two large job fairs after the Spring Festival, the actual results were not ideal. Out of desperation, Ms. Liu Yina, after discussing with the human resources manager, made a decision to adjust the age requirement on the recruitment notice from "20-35 years old" to "20-55 years old". This adjustment was expected to yield better results since it expanded the scope. For employees aged 55, the company might arrange them in positions such as后勤 management with a base salary of around 1200 yuan.

However, due to the sluggish real estate market, there has been a cooling in the recruitment of talents in this industry. "In the top 10 industries in the job seeker's career intention ranking, real estate development/sales ranks at the bottom." A staff member of Jiangxi Talent Personnel Network said that this trend is more evident in the real estate industry.

A staff member of Jiangxi Talent Market believed that influenced by labor shortages and social aging, the use of older workers by enterprises will continue.

"Due to purchase restrictions and banks being reluctant to lend, our current situation is tough." At the marketing store of a well-known real estate project in Nanchang's High-Tech Zone, Miss Yang, a salesperson with four years of experience in commercial property sales, told reporters that during the entire Spring Festival, only six new homes were sold in Nanchang city. As the sound of the real estate market entering a turning point grows louder, many home buyers are holding cash and waiting to see what happens. Miss Yang said that her income mainly comes from a basic salary, commissions from selling houses, and bonuses from the company. Since houses are hard to sell now, they, as real estate consultants, no longer receive commissions or bonuses, and currently can only rely on a basic salary of over 1000 yuan to sustain their living.

To alleviate the labor shortage, many companies in economically developed coastal regions like Zhejiang and Fujian have adopted a "worker referral" model, where existing employees bringing in new ones receive certain economic rewards. Now, this model has been replicated by many companies in Jiangxi.

Just after the Lantern Festival, recruitment and job hunting entered its peak period. On the 7th day, Jiangxi Talent Personnel Network released the first post-festival employment survey ranking. Both in the employer demand ranking and the job seeker intention ranking, sales/advisor categories ranked first.

However, when the reporter interviewed, it was found that some sales industries did not perform as well as indicated in the rankings.

The age threshold increased from 35 to 55 years old, and the use of older workers by enterprises became more prominent.

"Our company stipulates that any old employee who brings in a new employee will be given a material reward, especially internal employees who bring in skilled technicians, with a reward of 1000 yuan," said Mr. Zhang Bin, the director of the recruitment department of Nanchang Kohler Electroplating Workshop. Besides "worker referral", they also plan to organize recruitment teams to go to rural areas to "grab people."

"A worker in labor-intensive industries outside the province earns a monthly salary that is not much different from those within Jiangxi province. The biggest difference lies in the wages of skilled workers, with a monthly wage gap of 300-400 yuan for the same level of technician," a staff member of the Labor Resources Development Department of the Jiangxi Labor Employment Service Administration Bureau told reporters. The similarity in monthly salaries between ordinary workers and coastal regions has led many migrant workers to flow back within the province, instead of blindly going out, and start considering working nearby.

A staff member of Jiangxi Talent Personnel Network told reporters that the first post-festival employment survey ranking was derived from an analysis of over ten thousand positions provided by 220 employers after the festival, ultimately discovering that sales talents were the most sought-after.

The reporter learned that currently, many manufacturing enterprises in Jiangxi are recruiting large numbers of ordinary workers, and the wages of ordinary workers have become almost the same as those in coastal developed regions. According to relevant department monitoring, the monthly salary of migrant workers working in economically developed coastal regions such as Zhejiang is 1800 yuan. Ms. Yu Shiyun, a staff member of the Human Resources Department of "Aux Air Conditioning", stated that they are short of over 500 workers, mostly frontline operators, and are currently recruiting everywhere, offering monthly salaries above 2000 yuan. Similarly, Mr. Zhang Bin, the director of the Nanchang Kohler Electroplating Workshop, also indicated that the monthly salary of the ordinary workers they are recruiting is around 2000 yuan.

Compared to first-hand property consultants, second-hand property brokers are having an even tougher time. A branded real estate intermediary on Fuzhou Road in Nanchang even posted a "transfer" notice. Liu Jia, who has been in the second-hand property business for eight years, told reporters that media reports about comprehensive losses in first-tier city real estate intermediaries indicate that the second-hand property business is indeed becoming difficult. For example, the volume of second-hand property transactions in Nanchang was zero during the Spring Festival holiday. Therefore, many real estate intermediaries have taken measures such as closing stores and laying off employees to maintain operations, resulting in a large number of unemployed or re-employed second-hand property brokers.

"The popularity of marketing talents reflects the characteristics of Jiangxi employers, which is that production-oriented enterprises are not yet numerous, and most are trade-oriented enterprises." Mr. Liao Mingbo, the director of the Recruitment Services Department of Jiangxi Talent Market, expressed that given this situation, they would hold a specialized marketing talent job fair this Saturday.

Mr. Feng Jie, the general manager of a machinery manufacturing company in Nanchang, also told reporters that the company's biggest concern is that products cannot be sold, especially for trade-oriented enterprises. Therefore, they also planned to hire 30 salespeople after the Spring Festival.

Coincidentally, Ms. Rao Li, a staff member responsible for recruitment at "Taifeng Tires", also told reporters that they have an "internal recommendation award" and also plan to go to rural areas to "recruit soldiers."

Real estate salespersons are relying on a basic salary of over 1000 yuan to live.
