Tell you a story in Nanjing dialect.. Mr. Wang

by jiajiao400aktfs on 2012-02-29 17:34:07

Today I stayed in all day, and while washing clothes, [Enya] sister's soft and sticky Nanjing accent kept flashing in my mind. Sister said she wanted to hear my voice, so a few nights ago she called and we chatted for a long time. I can't quite remember what I told her, but it seems like the strong stimulation after "Thirteen Flowers" made Qin Xian unsure if she had fallen in love with the Nanjing accent.

Qin Xian, who likes music, has never been particularly interested in local operas. It's not that she doesn't like them; more accurately, it's because of ignorance there is no "intoxication". That day, the alluring and ethereal Qinhuai accent in "Thirteen Flowers" seemed to linger on endlessly. In a daze, it was always her accent that got stuck in my thoughts, making bubbles ferment one by one from my brain and blow out the beginning of 2012, somewhat crookedly.

Jin praised me for liking to misuse idioms in my blog posts. Actually, I think if I manipulate them well, they are quite relieving. Today I ate a lot of longan, which I didn't used to eat much due to its high sugar content. Since Jin instructed her husband to buy back a box, I have developed the habit of eating them every day at her place. When she pulled those fruits out of the cardboard box, there were many longan branches. I joked that her husband bought back a longan tree. Jin wouldn't give in. After I became addicted, she instructed her husband to bring back another box for me, specifying that no vaccinations should be given, and we would compare whose husband bought better longans. Later, I truthfully reported, "[My family also bought a longan tree]," it's evened out.

Actually, I didn't use to like Nanjing dialect very much, because of a Nanjing man who relentlessly pursued Qin Xian. It was quite a story. He met Qin Xian by chance on a train, remembered her conversation with others, and after a few rounds of letter-writing tactics, their friendship turned into love letters. The relatively late-maturing Qin Xian, who had no feelings for him at all, couldn't bear being bombarded with at least 30 pages of letters. Eventually, this made the strictly disciplined Qin Xian student get scolded by her dad quite a bit. Actually, that man was still pretty "tall and successful." To explain, I don't actually know what "tall and successful" means, but I often heard Sister Xinling say that her ideal prince of love at that time was a "tall and successful" man. My understanding is a tall, thick-browed, big-eyed man. Anyway, apart from being annoyed, Qin Xian felt no chemistry, so she ended up disliking that delicate and soft Nanjing accent.

It appears that preferences and tastes can change. We shouldn't speak too definitively about anything. Qin Xian student's language ability is pretty strong. Today, I've started calculating in my mind how to entertain her next phone call with some light Qinhuai tunes.

Today is the first day of 2012, and Qin Xian likes to stir things up. Although I haven't stepped out of the door, I still decided to take action on my own hair, dyeing it brown. Actually, messing around with red and green hair was something I did a few years ago when I was a "angry youth." In recent years, Qin Xian student has firmly fallen in love with the "authentic black" given to me by our ancestors. Actually, my hair is a bit naturally chocolate-colored. The reason I'm messing with it today stems from my last attempt, a golden tone I did not long ago. Now that the black hair has grown out, I can't stand this "hybrid color," so I had to do a golden brown tone to make this New Year have a complete color.

This is the first time I've used this brand of hair dye. While typing now, I'm wearing a shower cap on my head. After applying the "foam" all over my head just now, because I have less hair, I found half a bottle left over, which seemed such a waste, so I shouted for my husband to come over and let me mess with his hair for a "partial highlight." Unexpectedly, he, who is considered somewhat "fashionable," dodged as if avoiding something, making me feel quite frustrated.

"Eh, then find me a shower cap!" I ordered. Knowing he had offended me, he became overly attentive,

"Here," handing one over, he asked, "Do you need more?"

I immediately glared at him, "Huh! How many heads do I have? If I had two heads, would you still be here!!"

This time, because the color was purchased online and turned out a bit lighter, I've hesitated to start, worried it might look a bit "vulgar." But, come closer, I'll tell you a secret. I've already thought of a countermeasure. If the effect isn't good, especially if I don't get praise from Jin, I'll just say it's because I ate too many longans. Think about it, standing there, am I not just like a fragrant longan tree? [Teacher Jin, did I use the idiom correctly this time?]

Well, the longan tree is almost ripe, and the "Nanjingwa" is getting hot. I wonder if Enya sister will laugh uncontrollably after hearing my self-created Nanjing accent?! Never mind!
