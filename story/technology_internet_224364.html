4. Lotus Root and Yellow Bean Spare Ribs Soup

Ingredients:

Pork ribs, lotus root, yellow beans, jujubes, chopped coriander, salt, cooking wine, chicken essence, broth, scallions, ginger slices, soy sauce, Sichuan peppercorns, cooking oil

Directions:

1. Clean the pork ribs and cut them into sections. Peel the lotus root, wash it, and cut it into chunks.

2. Heat a pot with oil over medium heat. Once the oil is heated to 50% hot, add the ribs and stir-fry. Add cooking wine, soy sauce, broth, Sichuan peppercorns, scallion segments, ginger slices, yellow beans, salt, and lotus root chunks. Transfer everything to a clay pot after boiling, and simmer until the meat falls off the bone. Garnish with chopped coriander before serving.

Characteristics: Delicate flavor, rich aroma, highly nutritious.

Daily Tip: Lotus root has nourishing effects for the heart and spirit, strengthens the spleen, and helps stop diarrhea.

5. Astragalus Spare Ribs Soup

[Ingredients]

500 grams of pork ribs, 50 grams of yellow beans, 10 red dates, 20 grams of astragalus, 20 grams of通关藤 (Tongcao), ginger slices, and an appropriate amount of salt.

[Preparation]

(1) Wash the pork ribs and chop them into pieces. Clean the yellow beans, red dates, and ginger. Wash the astragalus and Tongcao, and wrap them in cheesecloth to form a medicinal bag.

(2) Add water to a pot, bring it to a boil over medium heat, then add the ribs, yellow beans, red dates, ginger, and the medicinal bag. Simmer on low heat for 2 hours, remove the medicinal bag, and season with salt.

[Features]

Aromatic meat, fresh soup. Can be enjoyed by drinking the soup, eating the meat, yellow beans, and red dates.

14. "Nutritious Recipes of Various Vegetables" - Winter Melon, Wax Gourd, and Their Recipes

Winter Melon

Winter melon is available all year round, coming in varieties such as oily green, green, and white. Most sold in markets are green.

Most gourds bear fruit in spring, summer, and autumn, but winter melon grows even in winter, hence its name.

Winter melon is the best vegetable for cooling and reducing heat during summer. It can treat swelling and promote urination. It is suitable for people with high blood pressure and obesity.

Modern medical experts claim that the pulp contains plant fiber, which is beneficial to the intestines and stomach.

Winter melon contains large amounts of Vitamin B and plant fiber. It can cool and reduce heat, quench thirst, and generate body fluids, helping prevent heatstroke.

Wax Gourd

Wax gourd, also known as hairy gourd, is a southern vegetable and one of the most nutritious gourds. Eating more wax gourd in summer can help prevent colds or aid quick recovery.

Fresh wax gourds have dense fuzz on their surface, and their stems still carry yellow flowers. For making stuffed wax gourd rings, choose uniformly sized and smaller wax gourds, as they are particularly tender. Larger ones are suitable for soups and stews.

Wax gourd contains large amounts of Vitamins B, C, and fiber. It can prevent colds, cleanse the intestines, and promote urination.

1. Winter Melon and Tangerine Peel Duck Steamed Rice

Ingredients: 1000 grams of winter melon, half of a deboned duck (about 750 grams), 100 grams of ham threads, 3 dried shiitake mushrooms, 1/4 tangerine peel, 3/4 teaspoon of salt, 1/2 teaspoon of sugar, a little cornstarch.

Directions: Remove the skin and seeds from the winter melon, cut it into large pieces, and place the rind facing up in a deep dish. Soak the mushrooms until soft, remove the stems, and shred them. Soak the tangerine peel, scrape off the inside, and shred it. Clean and dry the duck, fry it in oil until golden brown, then place it on top of the winter melon. Add the ham threads, tangerine peel, and mushrooms. Steam slowly for 1 hour, then pour out the original juice, mix it with the sauce ingredients, thicken it, and drizzle it over the duck before serving.

2. Dried Scallop Braised Wax Gourd

Ingredients: 750 grams of wax gourd, 2 dried scallops, 100 grams of shredded lean pork, 3 dried shiitake mushrooms, a little ginger thread.

Sauce Ingredients: 1.5 teaspoons of soy sauce, 1/2 teaspoon of sugar, an appropriate amount of cornstarch.

Directions:

1. Scrape off the skin of the wax gourd, clean it, slice it in half, and use a thick needle to pierce through the wax gourd. Fry it in hot oil until fragrant, then set aside to drain the oil.

2. Soak the dried scallops and shred them. Soak the mushrooms until soft, remove the stems, and shred them. Heat a pan, add oil, and stir-fry the ginger threads. Add the pork threads and stir-fry, then add the scallops and mushroom threads. Pour in an appropriate amount of water, add the wax gourd, cover, and braise gently for about 15 minutes. Thicken with the original juice and sauce ingredients, then serve.

3. Ham Winter Melon Sandwiches

Ingredients: 960 grams of winter melon, 40 grams of ham, 3/4 teaspoon of cornstarch.

Directions:

1. Peel the winter melon, remove the seeds, clean it, and cut it into inch-long rectangular blocks. Cut a slit into one side of the melon rind.

2. Slice the ham thinly.

3. Take one piece of winter melon, insert a slice of ham into the slit, arrange them on a plate, and steam until the melon is soft (about 1 hour).

4. Pour out the steaming liquid, boil it in a pot, thicken it with cornstarch water until glassy, and drizzle it over the winter melon.

4. Sweet Bamboo Wax Gourd with Yellow Beans

Ingredients: 640 grams of wax gourd, 2 slices of ginger, 150 grams of yellow beans, 1 green onion, 80 grams of sweet bamboo, a little salt.

Directions:

1. Peel the wax gourd, clean it, and cut it in half. Cut diagonal knife marks on the surface of the wax gourd but do not cut through. Dry roast the yellow beans in a wok, rinse them with cold water, and set aside.

2. Soak the sweet bamboo until soft, drain. Clean the green onion and shred it. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil, fry the ginger slices, then add the wax gourd and fry until slightly golden on both sides. Add the yellow beans and half a cup of water, braise until the wax gourd and yellow beans are tender, then add the sweet bamboo and cook for 5 minutes. Season with salt, sprinkle with green onion threads, and serve.
