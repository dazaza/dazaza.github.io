Solutions when UG mold separation fails - Quanzhou Mold Network

by gotook2012 on 2012-02-24 14:28:40

Regarding the splitting problem of parting surfaces, I have encountered a few issues:

1. After completing the parting surface and performing the split, red dots, green lines, or cyan lines appear. At this point, the system is actually telling us where the issue lies. This usually happens because the completed parting surface has not fully combined with the part, leaving small gaps or excessive overlaps. Sometimes, it could even be due to an unfilled hole.

Solution: Locate the red dot and zoom in to check if there's an issue. Adjust the absolute accuracy consistently; I typically set the absolute accuracy between 0.006-0.02 beforehand. This significantly reduces the chances of failure.

2. Even after confirming that the parting surface has no problems, the split still fails.

Solution: Examine the part. Use various detection methods such as copying surfaces to see if they can all be copied; perform geometric checks for any self-intersecting phenomena; check for extremely thin entities within the part (which may not be visible without thorough inspection). Identify problematic areas and if the issue isn't severe, create a surface or entity to cover it, which can be subtracted after the split.

3. In areas with many holes, if making surfaces to fill the holes seems too troublesome, simply create an entity inside the part to cover it, then add it back after the split. This reduces potential selection errors during the split process.
