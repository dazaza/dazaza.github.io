Earthworms used as feed additives, earthworm powder serves as a source of animal protein nutrition. In the past two to three decades, it has been increasingly widely applied in human nutrition and animal husbandry. Earthworms are rich in nutrients, with an average protein content of 56.5%, up to 70%. They also contain 11 kinds of amino acids, among which the arginine content is 2-3 times higher than fishmeal, tryptophan content is seven times that of beef liver, and lysine content reaches 4.3% [2]. They also contain abundant vitamin A, B complex, E, and various trace elements, hormones, enzymes, and carbohydrate substances, with each kilogram of worm body containing 12.5 mg of VB12 and 23 mg of VB2. Moreover, the iron, copper, zinc, and manganese content in earthworm bodies is higher than in fishmeal, and the utilization rate of phosphorus is as high as over 90%.

Earthworm powder, as a feed additive in livestock and poultry breeding, can not only improve the production performance of livestock and poultry and enhance the quality of livestock and poultry products but also boost the immunity of livestock and poultry. Additionally, using earthworms can treat various diseases in livestock and poultry. Therefore, utilizing livestock manure, bedding, and other solid waste from the breeding industry to breed earthworms can not only reduce the pollution of these wastes to the environment but also provide high-quality animal protein feed for the livestock breeding industry.

Using livestock manure, bedding, and other solid waste from the breeding industry to breed earthworms, then using earthworms to feed livestock, this scientific ecological balance system can not only reduce the pollution of these wastes to the environment but also produce highly efficient organic fertilizer and provide high-quality animal protein feed for the livestock breeding industry. Meanwhile, earthworms themselves are also a traditional Chinese medicine with extremely high medicinal value and can be used to treat various human and animal diseases. Therefore, they play an important role in the entire breeding industry. This article reviews the application of earthworms in the livestock ecosystem from three aspects: feed additives, treatment of livestock diseases, and handling of solid waste in the livestock breeding industry.

1. Application of earthworms and earthworm dung in the poultry industry

(1) Application of earthworm powder in laying hens Feeding chickens with earthworm powder results in faster weight gain, better meat quality, more egg production, and effects superior to fishmeal. Zhang Guiying (1995) reported that compared with laying hens fed fishmeal, adding 5% earthworm powder increased the average egg weight of laying hens by 1.99g (P0.05). Therefore, adding 5% earthworm powder to the daily diet of laying hens can not only replace the same amount of fishmeal but also reduce the feed consumption of laying hens, increase egg production and egg weight, making earthworms a high-quality protein feed for laying hens. Ma Xueyun et al. (2002) reported that adding 2% earthworm powder to the basic daily diet of laying hens could increase the egg-laying rate by 3.41% (P0.05). Calculating the feed cost at 1.30 yuan/kg for the control group and 1.00 yuan/kg for the experimental group, the feed cost required for weight gain was reduced by 0.42 yuan in the experimental group. Therefore, using earthworms as feed additives in broiler feed can not only reduce the feed-to-meat ratio and production costs but also lower the mortality rate of broilers.
