Taiwan Strait Network, September 16th (Strait Herald reporter Pengbo Wu / article, Qiaosi Chen / photo) - How long can a person's nails grow? Have you ever seen nails that are 30 centimeters long? Mr. Wang from Xiamen has been growing the nail on his left little finger for 15 years, and it has reached an impressive length of 30 centimeters! Someone once offered 60,000 yuan to collect his nail, but he didn't part with it.

The left little finger nail is 30 centimeters long

This man surnamed Wang is 40 years old. At around 10:30 in the morning yesterday, the reporter met Mr. Wang near the Hubli bus station. It was clear that his left little finger had a long, off-yellow nail, slightly curled.

How long is this nail exactly? The reporter bought a ruler at a nearby supermarket and measured the length of the nail on-site. Since the nail was curled, the reporter did not include the curved part in the measurement, which was exactly 30 centimeters long. Mr. Wang said that if the nail were washed with warm water, the curled part would straighten out, making the actual length of the nail even longer.

Growing nails for self-cultivation

Mr. Wang said that he started growing this long nail in 1994, and it has been 15 years since then. When asked about the reason for growing his nails, Mr. Wang said it was mainly for self-cultivation.

Mr. Wang said that 15 years ago, he was still a young and hotheaded guy who often got into trouble due to his explosive temper. After getting married, in order to control his fiery temperament, he decided to grow his nails. Growing nails requires great patience; if accidentally broken, all previous efforts would be wasted. Since starting to grow his nails, his mind became more peaceful, and he no longer acted impulsively.

Sleeping without covering the left hand with a blanket

Mr. Wang said that having such long nails requires extra care in daily life. "Actually, growing nails doesn't require special care; as long as you clean them with warm water, otherwise people might think they're unhygienic," Mr. Wang explained.

In fact, the family also opposed him keeping such long nails. They initially strongly urged Mr. Wang to cut his nails, but he did not waver in his determination. "Later, as the nails grew longer, they stopped caring and got used to it," Mr. Wang said.

However, such long nails have brought some inconvenience to daily life. Mr. Wang told reporters that first, he needs to be particularly careful to avoid damaging the nails when handling objects. Additionally, winter becomes troublesome; he has to roll up his sleeves before putting on clothes and then slowly insert his nails into the sleeves. When covering himself with a blanket at night, his left hand must remain outside the blanket, so his left hand often gets cold during winters.

Offered 60,000 yuan, but he didn't sell

With such long nails, Mr. Wang has almost become a "celebrity" in his neighborhood. Every time he goes out, people curiously talk about his nails, and he patiently explains to them.

Mr. Wang also told reporters that two years ago, someone offered 60,000 yuan to collect his nails, but he refused to sell.

"After keeping it for so many years, I've developed feelings for this nail, and I really couldn't bear to give it away; besides, I started growing nails for self-cultivation, not for making money," Mr. Wang said.

However, because he recently plans to do business elsewhere, keeping such long nails is indeed inconvenient, so Mr. Wang is preparing to cut off the nails he has grown for 15 years.

Mr. Wang said that accepting an interview from the Herald is to have the reporter witness his nails, as he has kept them for so long and has feelings for them; additionally, if anyone is willing to collect his nails, he could consider selling them.
