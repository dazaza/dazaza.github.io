Forgotten, you've already walked far away

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-01-06 22:23:51

I forgot that you have already gone far.

The four seasons, flowers still bloom.

I walk with ancient charm, smell the fragrance of flowers along the way, and recite as I go. Sunlight penetrates through the leaves, and speckled shadows dance in the green shade. Birds sing on the treetops, and sunlight flashes like neon lights.

Nature's symphony, I approach you because I am attached to you.

The summer breeze gently caresses the green earth, provoking the colorful summer flowers to quietly bloom on the branches, just like beauties in a Miss World competition, charming and seductive. The sky is deep blue and high above, like seawater hanging upside down. White clouds drift slowly, like silk woven from mulberry silkworms in Jiangnan. It's smooth, soft, gentle, light, and thin, making me want to touch it. My feet step on the green grass, bathed in the wind and sunshine. My heart resembles clouds at the horizon, drifting freely. My feelings are like summer flowers, splendid like a painting.

The season renders its beauty to the extreme, a land of prosperity and noise, all screened out by nature. Romantic breezes gently blow past my ears, like sweet whispers from a lover. On the lawn, streaks of golden yellow and pale red blushingly enter this grassland full of red and green flowers, looking like a fluttering butterfly playing in the ocean of flowers, wandering among the green notes, letting the flower fragrances kiss me, grass tips touch me, sunlight penetrate me, and thoughts fly.

In the distance, the peaks rise high into the clouds, connecting with the sky. They seem like a natural barrier, protecting the people here from outside interference. They live peaceful, wealthy, happy, and harmonious lives.

My arrival did not disturb them. Instead, they welcomed me with their warm hospitality, making me feel at home. Interacting with them is so harmonious and friendly that I am also deeply attached to them, as if I will always be the unchanging protagonist here.

Dealing with worldly people makes me feel weary, but being with them makes my heart light, like a cloud, a carefree cloud, slowly floating in their world.

Sunlight pierces through loneliness and solitude, revealing the appearance of the heart.

This beautiful corner unfolds a natural painting for me, a natural performance. There is no need for disguise or concealment; the truest, most beautiful, and purest aspects are all shown to me.

I quietly sit in the sea of flowers, forgetting everything when I came. I clear my mind, set it to zero, and accept the language of every leaf and every flower here. To communicate with them, I must be pure and cannot carry worldly hearts or prejudices, because they are lively, pure lives, freely jumping and shining with wisdom under the sunlight, blooming with confidence. Gazing at them, I seem to sense a kind of strength, a confident strength, a kind of brilliance, a kind of wise brilliance that they pass on to me.

I am attached to every plant, flower, leaf, peak, and valley here, forgetting the change of seasons, the passage of time, and that you have already gone far. I hang my worries on the moon, quietly listening to the murmurs of time, and listen to the sound of flowers blooming.

A pure heart falls into the mortal world, witnessing vicissitudes. At night, I gently play the jade zither under the moon, playing a tune of deep longing, waiting for you under the wind-swept longing tree, presenting fragrant osmanthus wine, sharing with me the joys and sorrows of reunion and separation, letting our promise be engraved as a heartfelt vow, letting time flow into fresh poetic lines.
