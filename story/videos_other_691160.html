"The Estate" features an intense inheritance battle and domestic violence scenes that are so dramatic they resemble an action movie - www.d1kk.cc

by mkomko8f1h on 2012-03-05 14:35:53

Recently, family ethics dramas have started to become popular on the screen again. The first emotional drama to explore the issue of inheritance, "Family Property", will premiere on Jiangsu Satellite TV's Happiness Theater starting November 2nd. In this drama, the four children of the Gao family stage a fierce battle over the inheritance left by their late father. Veteran actors Liu Lili, Sun Song, and Zhao Jun, as well as young actors Jing Nailiang and Lin Jiachuan, deliver outstanding performances in the series. Yesterday, the drama held a press conference in Beijing where the creators shared insights into the creation of the drama and behind-the-scenes anecdotes, revealing that the cast got so deeply involved in their roles that they still referred to each other as family members in real life. Moreover, due to the disputes over inheritance, this family ethics drama is filled with scenes of physical altercations.

Emotional dramas have recently clustered around similar themes, but "Family Property" stands out for its unique focus on the subject of inheritance, which is both distinctive and universally relatable. “The story in this drama echoes aspects of every family,” explained Ma Guangyuan, the young scriptwriter who created "Family Property." Furthermore, Ma Guangyuan noted that the drama doesn't just focus on the older generation but also examines the issue of inheritance from the perspective of the younger generation. Many Chinese families experience strained relationships among siblings due to inheritance issues. Ma Guangyuan summarized the plot’s principle: “First, the four families meet, then they argue, and soon after, they come to blows.”

Ma Guangyuan expressed that choosing this topic was aimed at breaking through the current limitations in emotional drama themes: “Many family ethics dramas revolve around mother-in-law and daughter-in-law relationships or marital crises, leading to thematic saturation. Therefore, we chose the theme of inheritance, which is an issue that both our generation and the previous one need to confront together. This drama isn't just for one generation; it hopes that parents, children, brothers, and sisters will watch it together and gain some insights.”

Due to the intense emotional conflicts depicted in "Family Property," many situations involving disputes over inheritance are presented through domestic violence, especially among the three sons of the Gao family, who resort to physical altercations whenever they meet. Ma Guangyuan joked, “Though it's a family ethics drama, there are quite a few fight scenes, making it almost like an action movie.” To capture these action scenes effectively, Ma Guangyuan thanked the camera crew for using flexible shoulder-mounted shooting techniques without tripods, which contributed significantly to capturing compelling action sequences.

Regarding the domestic violence scenes, actor Sun Song, who plays Gao Daguo, remarked that although he often fights with his brothers, he always maintains restraint. Interestingly, the harshest character in these scenes turns out to be Liu Lili, who plays the eldest sister. In one scene, upon hearing about the commotion among her brothers right after their father's passing, she jumps up and delivers a powerful slap to their foreheads.

The son of renowned screenwriter Lin Heping, young actor Lin Jiachuan, plays Gao Fei, the son of Gao Daguo, who is portrayed as unscrupulous and often gets beaten in the drama. Most of the beatings weren’t planned by the director but were improvised by Sun Song during filming. During one particular scene, Sun Song suggested Lin Jiachuan lean against the sofa while delivering his lines. However, during filming, Sun Song lost his temper and slapped Lin Jiachuan hard enough to knock him to the ground. Despite the pain, Lin Jiachuan insisted on re-shooting the scene, deeply impressing everyone on set.

Despite the heated disputes over inheritance in "Family Property," the actors who portray the family members felt a strong sense of camaraderie both on and off-screen. At the press conference, Sun Song and Lin Jiachuan directly addressed each other as "Dad" and "Son," unable to break out of their characters. Liu Lili, who plays the matriarch Gao Daxiu, recalled feeling an immediate connection with Jia Nailing, who portrays her son in the drama. She emphasized how easy it was to believe in each other's performances, noting that sometimes reality blurred with fiction as they truly felt like the characters they played. Liu Lili humorously mentioned overhearing Yang Qing call Zhao Jun "husband" backstage. She also found it amusing that in another recent drama, Li Xiaolu, who plays her daughter-in-law, visited the set to support Jia Nailing, making Liu Lili feel like their real-life mother.
