Precautions for carpet cleaning and exterior wall cleaning in Qingdao

by lxbj2009 on 2009-11-29 09:14:23

Qingdao exterior wall cleaning generally refers to the cleaning of the facade of high-rise buildings in cities. This is also within the scope of high-altitude work, along with high-rise exterior painting and installation/removal of high-altitude exterior advertisements. It is a common service provided by cleaning companies, such as carpet cleaning in Qingdao. Currently, most cleaning companies adopt the seated suspended board method for cleaning. This method is relatively simple and cost-effective. A good working environment is crucial for everyone's health. As long as the workers are physically fit and the ropes are securely connected, the cleaning can proceed. Most buildings can be cleaned using this method. However, safety measures must be fully implemented; otherwise, it could be very dangerous, and casualties often occur due to inadequate safety measures. Due to the danger and complexity of high-rise exterior wall cleaning, this job has a considerable degree of scientific and professional requirements. First, a detailed construction plan should be formulated based on the site conditions and environment, following the prerequisites for exterior wall cleaning, the waterproof grade and requirements of the building, and strictly adhering to the safety operation procedures for exterior wall cleaning to ensure the project's safety without any mistakes. Below are the conditions and safety operation procedures for exterior wall cleaning:

Conditions for Exterior Wall Cleaning:

Weather Conditions: Exterior wall cleaning must be carried out under favorable weather conditions, with wind force less than level 4. Work stops when the wind exceeds level 4. Therefore, wind speed should be measured before work, especially at high altitudes. Additionally, rain, snow, fog, poor visibility, high temperatures (above 35℃), and low temperatures (below 0℃) are not suitable for exterior wall cleaning.

Personnel Conditions: To implement national safety regulations, ensure worker safety, and keep equipment running normally, it is stipulated that high-altitude operators must be male citizens over 18 years old, pass physical examinations and safety technology training, pass exams, and obtain high-altitude work certificates before starting work. Workers must not drink alcohol before going on duty, and if they have colds or other physical discomforts, they should temporarily suspend high-altitude operations.

Equipment Conditions: The equipment for exterior wall cleaning must be in good working condition. If there are signs of wear on the hanging boards or partial rope strands crack, new ropes must be replaced immediately.

Safety Operation Procedures:

1. Before work, carefully check the machinery, tools, ropes, seat boards, and safety belts for any damage. Ensure that the machinery performs well and all tools are normal before starting the operation.

2. The operating rope and safety rope must be separately anchored and tightly tied. Padding soft objects near the edge prevents rope wear and breakage. The bottom end of the rope must touch the ground, and the person releasing the rope must also tie a temporary safety rope.

3. Before going on duty, construction personnel must wear work uniforms and safety helmets. When going on duty, first fasten the safety belt, then the insurance lock (on the safety rope), followed by securing the unhook (on the operating rope). Also, ensure the seat board buckle is tightened and secured.

4. When lowering the rope, the construction supervisor and the upstairs monitor should give guidance and assistance.

5. During the operation, auxiliary tools must be securely fastened to prevent injury from falling objects. Joking around and carrying irrelevant items are strictly prohibited.

6. Monitors upstairs and on the ground must stay on-site, fulfilling their duties, observing the tension and entanglement of the operating rope and safety rope, and reporting and resolving issues promptly.

7. Upstairs monitors must not walk back and forth along the roof edge carelessly. If necessary, they must first secure their own safety rope before assisting. Ground monitors must not read books or newspapers on-site and should not casually observe other scenes. They must also prevent pedestrians from entering hazardous areas and stop behaviors like pulling or swinging ropes.

8. When moving or adjusting the operating rope and safety rope up or down, monitors and auxiliary workers must coordinate properly. When not in use, the ropes should be bundled and secured.

9. Before landing, construction personnel should first inspect the facilities on the ground and walls, the positioning of the operating rope and safety rope, and the pedestrian flow situation. After the ground monitor handles and adjusts the situation and agrees, the descent can begin slowly until reaching the ground.

10. Safety always comes first, and civilized construction must be maintained to ensure the quality of the engineering service. Do not take or damage any items or facilities belonging to the client.

11. High-altitude workers and on-site monitors must follow the unified command and management of the construction supervisor.
