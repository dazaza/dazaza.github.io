Love movies or love food? "Crazy Dinner Party" brings food and movies into one world.

by anonymous on 2012-03-05 17:25:29

Love movies or love food? "Crazy Dinner Party" unites food and film in one world. Perhaps a question-style topic would leave everyone at a loss for an answer, but the release of the movie "Crazy Dinner Party" has made us realize that film and dinner parties are inseparable, forming a unified whole. Just like how many restaurants nowadays choose unique and memorable names by simply using movie titles to name their establishments. Next, we will take stock of several restaurants with cinematic characteristics...

"Trainspotting," a 1996 movie directed by Danny Boyle, is a youth punk film that has influenced many people.

Guessing Trains is located in a renovated large factory with a tall loft structure added to about half of the space. There are two skylights on the ceiling, front and back, while the white transparent glass room on the west side is an additional sunroom. Various cultural activities often take place here; the boss is called Lao He, a fat man who, like me, suffers from gout. He is a cultured elder and a lover of baijiu (Chinese liquor). Almost every week, there are independent film screenings here, and Guessing Trains might be the longest-running independent film screening venue in Beijing, starting from a semi-basement in Wangjing. Here, you can also enjoy fiery southwestern cuisine based on Guizhou dishes. When the "Salvation Bone" is served, you can feel the intensity of southwestern cuisine. The selected pork chop "Undercover Agent" comes with a thick layer of salted vegetables, crushed peanuts, and small green and red peppers, perfect for accompanying rice. The adorable side dish "Lovers' Fruit" consists of six tofu balls made by the Buyi ethnic group. After peeling back a piece of the skin, scoop a few spoons of the sauce into it. The outer skin is chewy, while the inside is soft and full of juice. This is a common snack eaten by young Buyi couples holding hands on the street, hence the name "Lovers' Fruit." Another Buyi salted sour meat offers a fresh spring taste. Despite being made from fatty pork, it is not greasy due to the inclusion of special Buyi pickled vegetables. Sliced fatty pork is eaten together with pickled cabbage circles, giving a fashionable twist to the ethnic minority's cuisine.

Address: No. 46 Fangjia Hutong, Dongcheng District

Seagull Canteen - Only three tables

"Seagull Canteen" is a simple and clean Japanese movie, akin to a haiku, telling stories about the mood of simple food, which can be paired with the Japanese TV drama "Midnight Diner."

The real Seagull Canteen is even smaller, with only three tables, but it is warm and serene. It specializes in creative light meals, mainly featuring potato-based pizzas and pasta. The standout feature of Seagull Canteen is its delicious potato-based pizza. Traditional pizzas use flour as the base, resulting in hard and flavorless pizza dough. However, Seagull Canteen’s potato-based pizza uses fresh potatoes carefully selected and processed through seven steps to create a flavorful potato pizza base that is soft, rich, and filled with abundant toppings covered with imported cheese. A spoonful placed in your mouth brings immense satisfaction, fresh, rich, and creamy. Additionally, they offer delicious Italian pastas, soups, salads, appetizers, various homemade beverages, and freshly ground coffee. All foods are guaranteed to contain no additives, all handmade by the two beautiful ladies who own the shop.

Address: No. 70 Dongsi Fourth Alley, Dongcheng District

Swallowtail Butterfly - Excellent Tom Yum Goong

"Swallowtail Butterfly" is a masterpiece by Japanese director Shunji Iwai, a comprehensive and shocking work beloved by many.

This is a Thai restaurant offering excellent Tom Yum Goong. I like their mint lemongrass wine beef, which turns a dish of beef into a Thai-flavored specialty due to the mint and lemongrass. Mint and lemongrass dominate the flavor. The finely chopped beef highlights the powerful aroma of the lemongrass, which smells like a man, while the mint leaves have a girl-like fragrance. Although cardamom seems to describe cardamom, this term better describes mint here. They are crowded on one plate, like a concert playing for our mouths.

Address: No. 58 Nanliutun South Road, Chaoyang District

Jasmine Restaurant - "If You Are the One" was filmed here

Around Gongti Stadium, concentrated are Beijing's fashion elements, including Jing Ge Pavilion and Zi Yun Xuan, examples of architecture and style. Jasmine Restaurant appears more frequently in the entertainment section than in the food section, as movies and celebrities have been filmed here. No wonder, it boasts Beijing's tallest and most magnificent wine cellar and a three-story dining space with complete functions. Jasmine calls its cuisine Innovative Chinese Cuisine (New Style Chinese Cuisine). Chinese ingredients are plated and cooked in Western styles, accompanied by nearly a hundred wines and champagnes from around the world stored in Jasmine's wine cellar, delighting many wine enthusiasts and tasters.

Address: Inside the East Gate of Workers' Stadium, Chaoyang District

Yue Shi Yue Xiang - "Du La La" had meals here

Yue Shi Yue Xiang is located under Yintai Center, serving as a canteen for surrounding office workers. Many dining scenes from the movie "Du La La's Promotion Diary" were filmed here.

Previously, Yue Shi Yue Xiang specialized in "Borderless Fashion Food Market." In the spring of 2011, it transformed from "borderless" to purely Chinese cuisine but remained "province-less," encompassing dishes from south to north and east to west in its menu. From Northeast Great Harvest to Beijing Leek and Mutton Cake, from Yunnan Wild Mushroom Old Chicken Soup to Taro Sausage Claypot Rice... If you can eat dishes from all over the country in one restaurant, it is usually suspicious. However, at Yue Shi Yue Xiang, you don't need to worry. All dishes are designed by the head chef of Park Hyatt Beijing, Mr. Ou Yang Qing Long. If I were to find a chef I trust the most, it would be this chubby bespectacled Chef Ou Yang.

Address: B1 Floor, Yintai Center, No. 2 Jianguomenwai Avenue, Chaoyang District

Yin Quan - Filming location for "33 Days of Lost Love"

"33 Days of Lost Love" features many outdoor shooting locations at restaurants, and some people specifically search for places where characters in the movie have been. If you're looking, Yin Quan could be a good choice. Yin Quan specializes in sushi, and I recommend Yin Quan Zhi Yu, a store themed around teppanyaki. The interior decoration is very Japanese, with different decor styles in each room. The teppanyaki here is highly recognizable due to the high-quality ingredients and skills. I recommend the foie gras, perfectly grilled, tender without being greasy. Paired with a slice of kiwi, a refreshing taste instantly permeates through the smooth foie gras.

Address: No. 8 Villa within the West Gate of Chaoyang Park, Chaoyang District
