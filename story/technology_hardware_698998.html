Many people choose to renovate their new homes in the summer, but due to the weather, there are also many quality and safety hazards in summer renovations. Today we will look at the summer renovation tips summarized by experts, which will surely be of great help to you.

Precaution One:

Summer renovation safety first

The temperature in summer is relatively high, and the climate is dry. There are more materials piled up at the construction site. From a safety perspective, materials should be placed in a cool place as much as possible to avoid fires caused by poor air circulation and high temperatures. In summer, materials should also be reduced on-site to reduce potential accidents. Additionally, electrical appliances and tools used during work should maintain a certain distance from the materials. When working with electricity, try to stay away from the materials, and it's best to make a reasonable division between the work area and the material area.

Precaution Two:

Ventilate frequently to prevent pollution from renovation

To avoid being harmed during the "high incidence period" of summer renovation pollution, in addition to choosing non-toxic or less toxic decoration materials and hiring reputable home renovation companies, it's also important to ensure good ventilation and air purification in the renovated rooms. Whenever possible, let the room ventilate as much as possible; if not, indoor ventilation devices and air purifiers that can reduce harmful gases indoors can be selected.

Precaution Three:

Moisture-proofing and preventing exposure to direct sunlight

When purchasing wood products in the summer, including major materials like plywood and auxiliary materials such as white wooden strips, corner lines, door edges, window frames, and baseboards along the wall, it's essential to test the moisture content of the wood products. At building material markets, most wood product specialty stores should have moisture content testers for wood. According to national regulations, the moisture content of standard boards should not exceed 12%. Experts suggest that during the rainy season, when buying materials, you can choose slightly drier ones. Once the materials arrive on-site, let them sit for one or two days so they adjust to the surrounding humidity, making them more suitable for use.

Additionally, wooden panels should be protected from strong sunlight. If the humidity is high on rainy days, storage measures should be taken to prevent dampness and deformation. Do not place panels and wood in rooms directly exposed to sunlight, as the intense sun can accelerate the aging of paint and glue on wooden decorative items. Also, rainwater can cause wood products to swell and deform, or even mold. Therefore, extra attention should be paid in daily life.

Precaution Four:

Wallpaper and coatings should naturally air-dry

Coatings and wallpapers are commonly used wall decoration materials. In spring, due to the humid air, windows and doors are often opened wide to quickly dry the walls. However, in summer, because of the dryness, wallpapers are generally soaked in water before pasting, and then glued and laid down. Opening windows and doors at this time would allow the newly laid wallpaper to be blown by crosswinds, causing it to rapidly lose moisture and result in shrinkage and deformation.

Precaution Five:

Remove mildew odors

In home renovations, latex paint may dry slowly and develop moldy odors in hot and humid weather. The common solution used by renovation companies is to turn on air conditioners to dehumidify and remove moisture from the air. Cabinet doors should remain open to keep the area ventilated. After renovations, various odors may linger, affecting health. Renovation companies suggest placing more green plants after renovations, or putting two or three lemons, oranges, or bananas in the room to achieve rapid odor removal effects.

Precaution Six:

Do not paint on rainy days

Experts recommend that for wood products, whether applying clear varnish or oil-based paint, remember not to paint on rainy days. On rainy days, let the construction team do other tasks or temporarily halt work for a couple of days rather than doing painting jobs in the rain.

Precaution Seven:

Don't rush to repair seasonal issues

In spring, the air humidity is high, and the moisture content in walls, floors, and wooden furniture is also relatively high. Entering summer, the air gradually becomes drier, and some problems may arise due to seasonal changes, such as wooden floorboards shrinking and gaps increasing between them; walls and door frames developing cracks due to different contraction rates of different materials; and walls cracking. These are all normal phenomena during seasonal transitions. Repairing them at this time may still lead to continued moisture evaporation, and walls could continue to crack. It's better to wait until the wall moisture aligns with the external climate conditions for a final repair, which will yield better results.

Precaution Eight:

Consider radiators in your plans

Although you won't need radiators in the sweltering heat of summer, you'll soon realize their importance in future living situations. Moreover, home renovation projects have an "irreversibility," so no matter when you renovate, always consider replacing radiators. In current home renovations, traditional cast-iron radiators are being replaced by modern steel pipe radiators that provide better heating and aesthetic appeal, becoming a popular trend. However, it's necessary to inform relevant departments.
