On Friday last week, Yu Ke-Wei, who has the best singing skills on the "Super Girl" stage, was eliminated.

Chengdu Daily reported on September 2nd that in the afternoon of September 1st, Yu Ke-Wei, who ranked fourth nationwide in the "2009 Happy Female Voice", returned to her hometown and met with local media at the Lotus Mansion - where her dream began. With regret for stopping here, controversies arose, but she hardly responded directly. However, when it came to "family ties," facing the local media, she opened up her heart and announced her prediction for the top three: "Jiang Yingrong will be the champion, Huang Ying will be the runner-up, and Li Xiaoyun will be third place. Jiang Yingrong is a versatile singer and dancer. She stands out on stage and has the charisma of a superstar. Huang Ying is simple, serious, and unconventional; I really like her."

Yu Ke-Wei frankly admitted that her current mindset is very calm. Regarding the rumors online about the public judges being forced to vote for Huang Ying, Yu Ke-Wei also stated that these are all baseless rumors, "There are even rumors saying that the public judges' airfare won't be reimbursed, which is really laughable. Because the public judges all take trains, how could their plane tickets be reimbursed?" In the semi-final match, Pan Hongyue's "unexpected vote" put Yu Ke-Wei into the ultimate PK, leading to her eventual elimination. Regarding Pan's "betrayal," Yu Ke-Wei was very magnanimous: "My relationship with her is still good. Yesterday, we went back to Chengdu together, then we all had dinner, there was no 'zero communication' as rumored by the outside world! At the dinner table the night before last, Pan Hongyue said she felt guilty towards me, and I consoled her, telling her it was okay!" Yu Ke-Wei reiterated again that she respects Pan Hongyue's choice, because this is just a game, a competition. The reporter observed that although her answers were flawless, Yu Ke-Wei's expression was still somewhat unnatural.

When talking about her own development, she also expressed some uncertainty about her future, "I haven't thought about it yet, I'll have to see the company's arrangements!" "Participating in the competition has been a change for me, I will cherish everything now. Perhaps in three or five years, I will return to my starting point, but even if I go back to where I was before the competition, back to the Lotus Mansion, as long as I can sing, I will be equally happy."
