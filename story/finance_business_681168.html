2012 Huangshan Trip - Part 1

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-28 11:47:56

The next day, we continued our journey. While taking a break by the road, the village nearby should have had a banquet arranged, with temporary tents set up and a large number of chairs, very much like the scene at Y's home. Finally, I set off with my friends to drive to Mount Huang. Before departure, we studied the route for a long time. The shortest distance was originally via the Yuegan Expressway, but a tunnel collapse in the middle only allowed one-way traffic, and it was said that the traffic jam was very severe, so we had to find another way. After consulting a friend from Fujian, we learned that the expressway was fully accessible, so we decided to detour through Fujian to Mount Huang. Even so, the road from Shenzhen to Huizhou was smooth, but there were traffic jams all the way from Heyuan to Meizhou via G105, moving as slowly as snails. With children in the car, driving at night was unsafe, so we stopped for the night when it got dark. On the first day, we only covered 400 kilometers and checked into the Zijin Hotel in Shanghang County, Fujian Province. Luckily, taking photos took quite a bit of time, otherwise we would have been ambushed. All these ammunitions were prepared for us.

Upon reaching the mountain, I accidentally dropped my camera on the ground while not paying attention, separating the lens from the body, which broke my heart. Upon picking it up and checking, the lens mount was already broken and unusable, leaving me extremely frustrated. With such beautiful scenery, I could only capture it with prime lenses and telephoto lenses.

Who is the mother of flowers? It is Mengbi. Why? Because "Mengbi Shenghua". From a distance of 30 meters, the dream-pen-in-bloom was faintly visible, like an ethereal fairy in a fairyland!

The hotel was located by the river, with ancient bridges spanning over it.

Huge icicles hung under the eaves of the Beihai Hotel.

There were few cars on the expressways in Fujian. The first refueling stop was at the Xincun Service Area, where the vast parking lot had only about ten cars, including many with Jiangsu license plates starting with "Su".

The entire Mount Huang was covered in thick snow, a world of silver white. Looking back at the photos at night, I thought I had taken black-and-white photos. My experience in snow photography was insufficient; even though I had increased the exposure, it still appeared dim, and manual white balance made it look bluish, causing much frustration.

Even before reaching the mountain, this pedestrian path leading to the cable car was already covered in snowflakes!

As we approached the highway exit near Mount Huang, it started to snow lightly, then heavier and heavier, with the mountain peak shrouded in clouds and snow...

After two days of hard travel, we reached Huangshan City, still 50 kilometers away from Mount Huang, and checked into the Huashan Hotel. It was quite amusing; the hotel at the foot of Mount Huang was called Huashan Hotel. Was it challenging Mount Hua?

Shixin Peak. According to legend, during the Ming Dynasty, Huang Xiyuan traveled from Yun Valley Temple to this peak, feeling as if he entered a painting, surreal yet real, thus believing in the extraordinary beauty of Mount Huang, naming it "Shixin" (Start to Believe).

Hotel staff hung festive red lanterns by the roadside. The bright red lanterns illuminated by the white snow immediately brought festive joy to life!

The nearby snowman didn't lag behind, holding a lantern to greet passersby.

We happened to meet a passerby whose warm ear muffs caught our eyes—so cute!
