Two confirmed absentees for Arsenal vs Liverpool as Wenger refuses Cruyff's call for Fabregas

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 19:55:33

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two confirmed absentees in starting lineup, Wenger rejects Cruyff's call for Fabregas

Sina Sports - Arsenal's away match against Burnley on Wednesday is not expected to be too challenging. Securing three points and overtaking Villa again is their sole objective. However, Wenger's team has seen some casualties emerge as key player Denilson suffered a back injury during Sunday's match against Liverpool, while Traore sustained a groin injury. Arsenal's official website reported that both players have been confirmed unavailable for the match against Burnley.

However, there is good news as well. Eboue, who missed the Liverpool game due to a groin strain, has returned to the squad and can strengthen the right flank options. Wenger said: "There was too little recovery time after Sunday's match, so we've added some injured players. Denilson will definitely not be able to go, he might be available for Hull City on Saturday, but we must check thoroughly, ensuring his back is fully recovered before playing. However, Eboue has returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits. His team had completely shaken off the shadow of the heavy defeat against Chelsea. When discussing the championship prospects this season, Wenger stated that the 2009-10 Premier League title race is the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. Wenger said in an interview with the official website: "No team has been able to maintain a leading position early in the season. Instead, four teams are entangled, and I believe a total of six, seven, or even eight teams can surprise anyone on any given weekend. Clearly, this is the most open Premier League top spot battle in my 13 years of coaching. I feel many teams have the potential to win the league title."

Respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own team. Wenger always has special confidence in Arsenal. He said: "I deeply believed from the start of the season that this would be our year. I said this after the Chelsea match as well. The reason I say this is because: look at how well we have improved as a whole, how strong our team spirit and quality are, and how prepared we are for every match like a war. From September to next May, all of this will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain their championship contention, Fabregas' importance is undeniable. In the absence of Van Persie, Fabregas is almost the only guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger naturally wants to ensure that Fabregas plays as many crucial matches as possible. Some external requests that can be rejected will be rejected, such as the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for next month on the 22nd. Catalonia coach Cruyff requested Fabregas to play in this event at the Nou Camp, but Wenger firmly refused.

Fabregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyff, but Wenger disregards this, as he has the right to reject such requests since Catalonia is not an officially recognized FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fabregas feels physically fine, and since it’s not a double-header week, he believes he has time to visit Spain. He said: "I'll play against Burnley on Wednesday and then Hull City three days later in the league, but after that, there will be close to a week without Premier League games, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

But Wenger's mind is solely focused on his team. There's a key match against Villa next weekend. The other three big clubs have lost to Villa, and whether Arsenal can perform well is worth questioning. Van Persie got injured playing for Holland, and Liverpool clearly refused Mascherano's participation in the match against Catalonia for Argentina. Wenger, who has always disliked international game troubles, certainly wouldn't agree for Fabregas to play in a non-A-level match. Wenger said: "We must respect the concept of priority, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain has another noteworthy friendly match next week, which is the Spanish La Liga Stars versus African All-Stars. This match is called the second Africa Charity Match, jointly initiated by UNICEF and the Kanoute Foundation, and will take place at the Bernabeu. Originally, the African team's list included five Arsenal players: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboue. Obviously, besides native-born players, those with African heritage were also counted. Wenger wouldn't agree with such arrangements. Therefore, in the final list, there isn't a single Arsenal player, in fact, not even a current Premier League player. The closest connection to the Premier League is probably Mali's Frederic Kanoute, founder of the Kanoute Foundation, former West Ham and Tottenham forward, now playing for Sevilla FC, and Manchester United's former forward Manucho, who recently joined Valladolid.
