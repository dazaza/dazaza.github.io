Port coal stock declines, price rebound is imminent

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-26 16:21:49

Since August, the coal shipping volumes of major coal shipping ports - Qinhuangdao Port and Guotou Jingtang Port - have remained at high levels with stable rail transport, causing a downward trend in the inventories of the two ports. According to statistics, from the 1st to the 23rd of this month, Qinhuangdao Port shipped an average of 701,000 tons of coal per day. The coal inventory decreased from 8.16 million tons on August 6th to 7.24 million tons on August 23rd, reducing by 920,000 tons over 17 days, averaging a daily decrease of 54,000 tons, a relatively large decline. This month, Guotou Jingtang Port shipped an average of 130,000 tons of coal per day, with its coal inventory decreasing from 1.83 million tons on August 6th to the current 1.59 million tons, averaging a daily decrease of 14,000 tons. Currently, the transportation momentum of the two ports is optimistic. If the weather remains clear in the latter part of the month, according to this progress, the coal shipment volume of the two ports this month will reach a high production level.

In August, temperatures soared sharply, increasing civilian electricity use. The daily coal consumption of coastal power plants in East China and South China increased, raising the demand for coal; plus, the fog sealing at the end of last month affected the berthing of ships, causing a large number of ships to wait in the anchorage areas of northern ports. In the first ten days of this month, there were a relatively large number of anchored ships in the anchorage areas of northern ports. On average, there were 143 waiting ships at Qinhuangdao Port, including 83 ships that had completed procedures, all at high levels. Qinhuangdao and other main ports urgently responded to user needs, seizing the favorable opportunity of improved weather conducive to loading and unloading operations. They scientifically organized production, maintained heavy-load handovers, expedited coal transport, and frequently set new highs in daily coal shipments. Since entering August, Qinhuangdao Port has shipped an average of 701,000 tons of coal per day. The increase in coal shipments and efficiency has reduced the number of waiting ships.

As of August 23rd, there were only 120 anchored ships in Qinhuangdao Port's anchorage area, among which 37 ships had completed procedures, showing a declining trend in both categories. In terms of resource areas, due to the continuous drop in coal prices, the enthusiasm of coal mining stations and dealers for shipping was dampened, affecting market coal shipments. Major coal-producing provinces such as Shanxi and Inner Mongolia have successively conducted safety inspections of coal mines, which may affect the release of coal production capacity to a certain extent.

Regarding railway transport, after the particularly serious railway traffic accident on the Yongwen Line on July 23rd, to ensure safe production, the Ministry of Railways increased the safety inspection intensity of high-speed railways and EMUs, slightly affecting railway operation efficiency. At the same time, considering various factors including actual downstream demand, the Ministry of Railways decided not to guarantee the 450 million-ton transport target for the Daqin Line but instead adjusted the plan target to 430-440 million tons. Therefore, the overall traffic flow and coal car arrivals at the port on the Daqin Railway slightly decreased, but the transport volume basically remained at a medium-to-high level. In the middle of this month, when the field stocks of Qinhuangdao Port and Guotou Jingtang Port in the north fell somewhat, the railway department expedited transportation, and the number of arriving trains in the mid-to-late part of the month was slightly higher than in the early part.

Currently, it is the peak summer period, especially since August, some southern regions have experienced persistent high temperatures, with temperatures in some areas exceeding 35 degrees Celsius. National power generation has repeatedly set new records, and daily coal consumption for power generation has significantly increased. Currently, both electricity and coal usage are at peak periods, with national daily power generation continuing to rise and repeatedly setting historical records. The mid-to-late August is a critical period for the peak summer demand, and cooling electricity load continues to increase. The Ministry of Railways prioritizes coal transport, increasing transport capacity inclination; each railway bureau further strengthens dispatching command, accelerates vehicle turnover, improves vehicle utilization efficiency, ensuring coal loading and downstream user demands. Meanwhile, the Ministry of Transport requires strengthening transport capacity guarantees, ensuring transport safety, fully ensuring safe, smooth, and orderly coal transport. Northern ports fully organize, expedite transport, and improve handling efficiency.

In the early part of this month, the north-south shipping routes were continuously interrupted due to typhoons, resulting in some loss of vessel capacity. Additionally, due to sustained high temperatures in the southern regions in the mid-to-late part of the month, the demand for coastal coal transport increased, causing a rebound in coal shipping prices. As of August 22nd, the freight rate for the Qinhuangdao to Guangzhou route was 70 yuan/ton, and the freight rate for the Qinhuangdao to Shanghai route was 42 yuan/ton, both having increased compared to the beginning of the month.

As of now, the total coal inventory of the six major coastal power plants reached 11.07 million tons, with a daily consumption of 701,800 tons, equivalent to 15.7 days of available coal supply, still at a medium-to-high level. Among them, the coal supply duration for Zhejiang Energy and Guodian coastal power plants was 23.4 and 19.1 days respectively, unchanged compared to the same period last month. For others, such as Shangdian, Yuelectric, Datang, and Huanneng coastal power plants, the coal supply durations were 15.8, 11.2, 11.7, and 12.4 days respectively, showing a decrease of 2-4 days compared to the same period last month.
