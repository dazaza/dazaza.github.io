EA Founder: Apple Has Peaked and Will Decline in a Year or Two

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:32:23

Apple CEO Jobs

Sina Technology News, August 4th early morning news, Trip Hawkins, the founder of EA, said today that despite Apple's current status as the Roman Empire of the tech world, it will soon decline.

Hawkins said: "Looking back at the peak periods of all historical powers - like the Roman Empire - you will see that Apple is now at the top. Their CEO is still alive; they invented the tablet and made it grow; they successfully reinvented the phone; they gave Mac a new life; their marketing is astonishing. All this is unparalleled - but how much more can they improve?"

He continued: "It may take another year or two before Apple begins to decline, but this is inevitable - everything is like this. Everything about Apple centers around Jobs; no matter how excellent his subordinates are, they are not Jobs. Although I hope he lives long, no one can live forever."

Hawkins questioned Apple's closed model and pointed out one of its major flaws: lack of support for Flash.

He said: "If Apple decides to support all web standards, it would be a great joy for the entire industry because then Apple could be the best in every way. Currently, Apple is very cautious about this; they want you to stay in the App Store rather than switch to the browser."

Hawkins pointed out that the reason Apple does not want to support Flash is not due to a preference for HTML5, which is just an excuse. He said: "Flash can provide many good games, something HTML5 cannot do yet. But as HTML5 matures, this issue is expected to be resolved within a few years. Apple could be more generous in supporting Flash and other industry standards, or at least let things take their natural course." (Yanfei)
