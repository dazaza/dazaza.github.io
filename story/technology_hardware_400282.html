How can we consume fairly, save money, reduce waste, and not affect our normal quality of life? In fact, a few small tricks can save us a lot of money and also help us understand the principles of saving and managing a household.

1. Develop the habit of online shopping

For the same item, if it is available online, it is usually cheaper than offline because the operating costs of online stores are lower, product prices are easier to compare (with the help of search engines for price comparison), and customers can easily switch purchasing targets (just click the mouse), so price competition is more intense. Think about it, if your family's various consumption needs for clothing, food, housing, and transportation can be conveniently purchased online, how much money could you save in a year? Of course, online sellers vary greatly in quality, so buyers need to be careful in distinguishing and seek advice from experienced buyers.

3. Go to wholesale markets

Every city, especially large cities, has various wholesale markets. Although it is called wholesale, it does not mean that the stall owners do not retail. If you figure out the distribution and characteristics of the wholesale markets in your city, accumulate some non-urgent purchasing needs, and when you have enough, take some time to visit the wholesale market, you will likely return home fully loaded. Although it may be tiring, the inner satisfaction of "I am smart, saving money and getting benefits" will make you feel happy and reminisce for several days, haha.

4. Keep a financial account

Humans are creatures that fear trouble, and keeping an account of one's financial income and expenses, and daily consumption, is not easy to persist in the long term. However, if you really do it (even roughly), it can not only improve your financial management skills but also help you regularly review your unreasonable consumption, optimize and improve it, gradually forming good financial consumption habits. After persisting for half a year or a year, when your financial situation significantly improves and you can spend your money more appropriately, the sense of achievement and satisfaction will make you feel that all your previous efforts were completely worth it.

5. Choose a suitable mobile phone package

Study various mobile phone packages together, carefully analyze your consumption needs for making calls, sending text messages, and mobile internet use, and then choose a package suitable for yourself. If your mobile phone consumption characteristics change, or if the operator introduces new mobile phone packages, pay attention and update your package in time. If you have any questions, call your telecommunications operator's free service hotline and consult the customer service representative. There is a crazy guy who often calls those sweet-voiced and gentle mobile telecom customer service representatives, not only fantasizing about them as his wife but also claiming that his eloquence was trained this way, amazing.

6. Make network calls or free video/audio calls

Generally, everyone has their own computer to access the internet, right? Install SKYPE, buy a discount package online at a low cost, and you can make very cheap network calls. Through instant messaging software like QQ, you can have video/audio calls with others (and even group calls). Apart from paying for internet fees, electricity fees, and computer depreciation fees, nothing else is required, of course, the call quality depends on our broadband operators, they need to be powerful!

7. Buy fewer printed publications

Well-known print media have their own websites and electronic versions, and the publication time is not much slower, why spend money to buy them, they are cumbersome and not environmentally friendly (forests are crying... paper media friends don't hit me). To keep up with news, politics, social hotspots, and entertainment gossip? With QQ pop-up news + Sina Weibo + Tianya, you won't miss any information. Besides, there are TV and radio, many companies also have public newspaper and magazine racks. Except for passing time while traveling by car, I rarely spend money buying newspapers and magazines.

8. Supermarket money-saving strategies

Many supermarkets' fruit platters, salads, pastries, and cooked foods start to be discounted at 9 PM, and the price may be less than half of what is marked on the label. After dinner, go for a walk and casually visit the supermarket, pick up some high-quality (spoiled items are definitely not acceptable) "cheap goods" to take home, why wouldn't that be fun? Additionally, many supermarkets and their resident merchants have numerous promotional activities during holidays, weekends, store anniversaries, and various celebrations, with promotional product prices much lower than usual, and often with free gifts.

9. Science of buying groceries

Avoid the early morning peak for grocery shopping, and choose to go to the market after 5 PM; prices will be about 30% cheaper than in the morning. If it encounters continuous rainy weather, vegetable prices will significantly rise, so be sure to check the weather forecast and try to stock up on vegetables before sudden weather changes. Also, instead of aimlessly wandering around the market each time, it might be better to focus on a stall with a wide variety of vegetables, consistently consuming there. After becoming familiar with the vendor, the vegetable prices will be cheaper, and sometimes you can take a couple of green onions along.

10. Bring your own lunch, healthy and economical

Ordering takeout every day costs a lot of money, and the options are limited. Working until noon, but often worrying about what to eat for lunch, gradually turning the meal that should be the best into a perfunctory task. Moreover, the quality of today's takeout is questionable, and eating it is not reassuring. Want to eat what you like, cook it yourself, eat with peace of mind, economically and practically.

11. Smart ways to save on beauty and makeup

Beauty products do not necessarily have to be purchased at large malls; online purchases are both convenient and economical. If you know a trusted beautician, you can ask if the beauty salon sells large packages of your trusted makeup brands because salons have professional channels and are much cheaper than outside retail stores. When new cosmetic products are launched, merchants usually have some activities, such as spending a certain amount and getting a refund, or receiving small samples and discount gift sets. Among these discount plans, merchants offer the greatest discounts in the form of gift sets. If you can find a few good friends to buy the set together, you want toner, I want cream, a few people split it, this is the most cost-effective.

12. Choose an easy-to-maintain hairstyle

Not taking care of your hair is definitely not okay, but you can still look beautiful without spending much money. If you don't need regular expensive highlights, tell the stylist to just trim your ends, which will cost much less. Moreover, frequent dyeing is not good for your health.

13. Little tricks to save on clothes

Merchants often have clearance clothing, which is cheap but may fit you perfectly, worth noting. Take advantage of seasonal changes to buy a few versatile, timeless classic styles of clothing, which can greatly reduce the cost of buying clothes. If you find a piece of clothing you really want to buy, it’s best to check other places first, cool down for a few minutes, and then decide whether to buy it.

14. Clever home decoration

Buying a house is expensive, but you still need to decorate it well before moving in, which costs a lot of money. Spending more time thinking about decoration and furniture can help you save a lot of expenses. Try not to do complex, hard-to-change decorations, and do more soft decorations, which can be changed every year and a half.

15. Don't waste your fitness card

Is the fitness card you impulsively bought now becoming useless? Do you go to the gym less than once a week? Don't waste it anymore, quickly transfer the card to someone who needs it more.

16. Gifts you don't like can be given to others

Among the gifts you receive, there are inevitably some that don't suit your taste. At this time, don't rush to remove the tag, just keep it! Next time there is an appropriate occasion, repackage it and give it to someone else. This is not impolite to others, but rather maximizing the use of limited resources.

17. Co-branded credit cards let you spend and earn at the same time

Co-branded credit cards refer to credit cards jointly issued by issuing banks and profit-making institutions (such as airlines or department stores). In addition to enjoying the convenience of credit cards, holders of co-branded cards can also get a certain percentage of discounts, rebates, or other value-added services provided by profit-making institutions.

18. Morning is the cheapest time to buy tickets

Because ticket discounts are usually adjusted overnight. Additionally, try to avoid traveling on Monday mornings and Thursday evenings, as these are the times with the most passengers. The ideal flight time is: noon every day and the middle of the week. Taking connecting flights to your destination will be much cheaper than direct flights; choosing relatively smaller airline flights is usually cheaper than super-large airline flights.

19. Am I the team leader? Who am I afraid of?

The popularity of group buying is one of the major trends in online consumption this year. Group buying in large quantities makes merchants willing to offer very low discounts, and even to promote themselves by offering incredible cost prices or loss-making prices. Therefore, frequently visiting various group-buying websites and searching for group-buying products suitable for yourself will definitely surprise you, saving money and being fun. Not only online shopping, but also going to physical stores and supermarkets to buy things, you can participate in group buying.

20. Definitely don't waste, wasting is shameful!

This is purely a bad habit issue: ordering too much when dining out, leaving half unpaid, some dishes untouched, the person paying the bill "generously" not packing leftovers, and the guests feeling embarrassed to pack them; buying a bunch of things at the supermarket, using the urgent ones and ignoring the rest, which not only takes up space but also molds and spoils over time; impulse buying, buying clothes, shoes, and promotional items easily influenced by salespeople, regretting it after bringing it home, but soon forgetting the pain and repeating the mistake; etc. There are too many examples like this. Earth's resources are limited, personal needs are limited, wasting is shameful, wasting is shameful, definitely don't waste, definitely don't waste!

I believe there are many more money-saving tips in life, and everyone must have their own unique tricks. The key issue is: do we truly remember and take them seriously? Are we really following these practices? If we can develop good habits and achieve half of the above tips, I believe that by the end of the year, we can certainly save ourselves a large sum of money!
