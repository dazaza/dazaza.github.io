The Thorny Emotional Journey of a Widowed Single Woman: Marry Me, Written by Xiyang Rongruo

by jqrxass on 2009-10-03 16:44:57

A single woman who has lost her husband quietly waits in loneliness and solitude; the men in her life take turns to appear. The desperate abandoned women struggle with life in different ways, whether looking back or moving forward, they are unwilling to escape from their original mental journey. Young sisters who are caught in the whirlpool of human relationships resolutely throw themselves into an unknown field with all their spirit and life. That is the man's family, where the wings of love carry the heavy flesh, unable to bear the weight.

Fate connects their lives and the men in them in incredible ways, and so everything begins: honesty and deception, conscience and morality, love and family bonds, entanglement and rupture...

When life appears in the form of trivial complexity, when indifference and obedience become the main way to numb the nerves, when the wandering soul becomes the norm of life, when the persistence of love becomes a legend, when unrepentant giving becomes a joke, who still believes that love is the only eternal thing in this world?

However, those passions and dreams that were once impacted, overturned, and destroyed still echo in the depths of the heart, making one restless. Those hidden pains, despairs, helplessnesses, and those deep-seated fears about the future that cannot be grasped, we must face them all one by one, not just pass by.

Marry Me - Part One

Xiao Lin in the office was authoritative, naturally commanding respect without anger. Most of her hair was combed backward, gathered into a beautiful bun at the back, with no decoration on the bun, elegantly tied with a black ribbon without any visible traces. But at her forehead, there was a very neat and black fringe, neatly sticking above her eyebrows, which had a slight upward curve, thus making her eyes seem sharp and cold. She liked to use the same cold light purple lipstick, lightly applying a layer, as if she wanted that calm color.

Marry Me - Part Two

In fact, An Qingqing felt very hopeless. During this period, she bought a lot of magazines, women's magazines, of course, and some women's literature, guides to the emotional lives of urban women. These were all about the living conditions and strategies of abandoned women. An Qingqing spent all her free time exploring these texts. Later, she sorrowfully discovered that the magazines had collectively run out of ideas. She even once suspected that the editors were all dumb, could they not come up with something new?

Marry Me - Part Three

The sky grew darker, the air warm and humid, and the first few snowflakes began to fall from the sky. People outside the window hurried home or rushed to the hospital ward. Xiao Lin placed the narcissus flowers by the bedside, hung two lanterns in front of the window, and instantly, the ward was filled with the New Year atmosphere. Mao Mao held Wang Fan's hand by the bed, recounting stories he had once told, repeating jokes he had once said.

After arranging everything, Xiao Lin resumed her normal care for Wang Fan.

Marry Me - Part Four

She recalled every detail of events from start to finish, from her meeting Zheng Wei, chatting with him, his inexplicable disappearance for a period, re-establishing contact, then coming to Hangzhou, and meeting Xiao Lin. Everything, yes, just as Mao Mao said, that Zheng Wei was actually Xiao Lin. This Zheng Wei, who left messages for her every now and then, was not the original Zheng Wei but his wife, Xiao Lin. According to An Qingqing's account, the time of Jiang Zhengwei's death matched the period when he disappeared.
