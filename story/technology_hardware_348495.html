Zhejiang Jinhua distributors push back against manufacturers: LCD TV screens warranty for 3 years

by tfseo8888 on 2010-04-19 20:37:21

Legal Daily News: Reporter Yao Zhe - The Administration for Industry and Commerce (AIC) and the Consumer Protection Committee of Jinhua City in Zhejiang Province, together with home appliance dealers, have joined hands to use a "reverse pressure mechanism" to force manufacturers to extend the warranty period for flat-screen TVs to three years.

In China's current "three guarantees" regulations, although the warranty period for major components of televisions is three years, since these regulations were issued before plasma and LCD flat-screen TVs existed, the description of "major components" refers to cathode-ray tubes, motherboards, and chips. However, in the total cost of an LCD TV, the display screen takes up a large portion, yet there is no relevant text in the list of major parts. This has led to the warranty period for flat-screen TV displays not being clearly defined, leaving it up to the manufacturers to decide. Some manufacturers have shortened the warranty period for displays to two years.

To break the unreasonable practice of warranty periods in the flat-screen TV industry, the AIC and the Consumer Protection Committee of Jinhua City actively visited home appliance dealers and initiated the proposal that "the free quality warranty period for flat-screen TV displays should be extended to three years equally, regardless of domestic or international brands," and eventually reached a consensus. Key home appliance retailers in Zhejiang Jinhua submitted written commitments to the Jinhua Consumer Protection Committee while publicly committing to society: all domestically and internationally branded flat-screen color TVs sold by them will enjoy "a one-year (12 months) whole machine free quality warranty period, and the display screen free quality warranty period will be extended to three years (36 months)" after-sales service, eliminating the concerns of many consumers when purchasing flat-screen TVs. This reverse pressure mechanism forces flat-screen TV manufacturers to extend the warranty period to three years.

Industry insiders believe that this "reverse pressure mechanism" fills the gap in the "three guarantees" for flat-screen TVs, setting a precedent nationwide, and provides reference for other places.
