Wang is the head of a non-ferrous metal recycling company. Liang, pretending to be "Liu Xinyan", claimed that she could contact the department head and sell all the X-ray films from the Peking Union Medical College Hospital to Wang. This would be a big deal for Wang. Eventually, in order to make the deal happen, Wang gave Liang 80,000 yuan as a kickback, but in the end, the matter was not resolved, and Wang's 80,000 yuan went down the drain. After the police arrested Liang, the Peking Union Medical College Hospital issued a statement: all X-ray films are kept by the hospital itself, and what Liang said was not true.

After investigation by the police, it was found out that the house where Mr. Cui lived was rented by Liang at a monthly rent of 5,000 yuan, claiming that it was a house for sale. Over more than two years, the rent paid amounted to several ten thousand yuan. The apartment where Liang later moved in the same residential area was also rented by her, even her boyfriend who was in a relationship with her was deceived, truly believing that it was a property under Liang's name.

Liang (pictured on the right), who appeared in court, had a ponytail, her hair already gray. According to the prosecution, Liang previously impersonated a Peking Union Medical College Hospital doctor studying in the United States, promising to help a couple working in a medical association buy a house. Under the pretense of paying for the house, transfer fees, and parking space fees, she defrauded the couple of a total of 860,000 yuan. Later, Liang again claimed that she could help Wang purchase used X-ray films from the Peking Union Medical College Hospital, defrauding Wang of 80,000 yuan.

However, the house did not smoothly fall into Mr. Cui's hands. After repeated urging, Mr. Cui paid several times more money and finally moved into this large house at the end of 2007. But when Liang was handling the property certificate, she pulled another trick, saying it could only be processed after the Olympics. Liang then also found a place in the same residential area with her boyfriend, which made Mr. Cui and his wife feel reassured. In the following six months, they continuously handed over more house payments to Liang.

Liang is 50 years old. Due to impersonating a Peking Union Medical College Hospital doctor and defrauding others of house payments, Dongcheng Court legally sentenced her to 11 years in prison. The fraud method adopted by Liang was quite "ingenious," renting a house for the victims to live in and lying that the house could only be transferred later. Over two years, she defrauded a total of 860,000 yuan from the victims. Behind Liang's fraudulent acts lies a story that is difficult to categorize as right or wrong: Liang committed fraud in order to "trick marriage." Yesterday, it was learned that Liang, who had served an eight-year sentence for fraud before, made the same choice as 20 years ago - appealing.

Mr. Cui and his wife were trapped in Liang's scam for two years. In 2006, Liang met Mr. Cui, impersonating the identity of Dr. Liu Xinyan from the Peking Union Medical College Hospital, quickly gaining Mr. Cui's trust. Liang introduced cheap houses to Mr. Cui, first mentioning a flat in the former Xuanwu District priced at one million yuan, then saying it was unavailable and recommending a building in Fengtai District, 140 square meters in size, still priced at one million yuan. Mr. Cui admitted that he and his wife originally did not plan to buy a house, but unable to resist Liang's persuasion and the low price, they paid a deposit to Liang.

The attitude during the trial was just as bad as the previous time. When the judge verified Liang's basic information, Liang spoke weakly, claiming she had heart problems and felt unwell. But when asked if she confessed to the crime, Liang denied it outright, "I haven't committed fraud." Liang continued to resist, ultimately, Dongcheng Court sentenced Liang to 11 years in prison for fraud based on the facts recognized. When the judge asked for her opinion, Liang still refused to cooperate, "I didn't hear clearly." The judge read the verdict again, and Liang nodded to indicate she heard clearly.

Liang's bad attitude during the trial wasn't the first time. According to the original Xuanwu District Court's judgment, in 1991, Liang defrauded over 17,000 yuan under the pretext of purchasing imported household appliances, furniture, and plane tickets, resulting in an eight-year prison sentence. The description "refusing to admit guilt, bad attitude" appeared in the judgment and subsequent final verdict. Liang served her full eight-year sentence and was released in 1999.

After the trial, Su, the agent representing Liang's boyfriend who had been interacting with her before the case broke, stated that Liang's case was not simple. She committed fraud in order to trick marriage. Recently, the reporter contacted Su. Su said he was a retired official, after Liang started interacting with him, he also thought they could get along, but had not considered marriage yet. "She really wanted to be with me."

The prosecutor handling the case explained that in this case, Liang spared no expense in renting a house for Mr. Cui to live in while also renting one for herself, winning Mr. Cui's trust. This was her cunning and clever way of deceiving. Different from the "getting something for nothing" method used by many criminals in the past, in some recent fraud cases, in order to make the scam more realistic, there is a new phenomenon where criminals invest small costs to gain big profits. However, no matter how real the criminals act, there will always be loopholes. Citizens should verify more carefully to avoid being scammed.

Su recalled that shortly after meeting, Liang stopped going to work. When he asked why, Liang said she had a blood disease and needed rest. It was not until the case broke that Su learned that Liang was not "Liu Xinyan" and had defrauded Mr. Cui of over 860,000 yuan. Regarding this, Su expressed deep regret, "Her act of fraud is undeniable, but it was because she wanted to marry me and was harmed by others."

Su said that Liang got involved in an economic dispute between him and his old classmate Mr. Ding, without her knowledge. Mr. Ding kept demanding tens of thousands of yuan from Su, which Su believed was unreasonable and refused to pay. One day when Mr. Ding came to Su's home, he met Liang, who said she would handle the matter for Su. Liang first repaid some money, but later, because she didn't repay for a period of time, Mr. Ding went to the Peking Union Medical College Hospital looking for "Liu Xinyan" and discovered it was not Liang herself. Through friends, Mr. Ding learned about Liang's criminal record. Her unsavory past was exactly what Liang didn't want Su to know, so she agreed to continue repaying the money, constantly handing over the money she defrauded from Mr. Cui to Mr. Ding.
