Is the early pregnancy test accurate?

by betty2012 on 2012-02-12 13:14:03

Most female friends who have sex life will use the early pregnancy test paper to detect whether they are pregnant after their menstruation is delayed. Then, is the early pregnancy test paper accurate? Simply put, the early pregnancy test paper is a product designed for women to conveniently check if they are pregnant. Not long after fertilization, placental trophoblast cells will secrete a glycoprotein hormone called human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG), which is produced by the placenta in pregnant women and exists in large quantities in the urine of pregnant women. However, HCG is almost non-existent in the urine of non-pregnant women. The AiKang HCG colloidal gold method test kit uses the double antibody sandwich one-step method technology with colloidal gold as an indicator mark to detect the concentration of HCG in urine, thereby confirming whether a woman is pregnant. The test paper is a reliable indicator to assist in clinical determination of pregnancy. Although many early pregnancy test papers indicate that women can conduct a self-test for pregnancy one day after missing their normal period, in reality, not all pregnant women excrete HCG hormones at the same speed and quantity; it varies from person to person.

>> Is the early pregnancy test paper accurate?

The general test results can only achieve an accuracy rate of 50% to 75%. If tested too early, the early pregnancy test paper may be inaccurate. HCG begins to be secreted 10-14 days after conception and reaches its peak at 60-70 days. If tested within 10 days of conception, even if pregnant, the HCG content is extremely low, insufficient to make the early pregnancy test paper show a positive result.

>> Does a positive result on an early pregnancy test paper always mean a normal pregnancy?

A positive result does not necessarily mean a normal pregnancy. In cases of ectopic pregnancy, the pregnancy test has a 65% positive rate, but a positive result cannot reveal whether it is a normal intrauterine pregnancy or an ectopic pregnancy. Similarly, performing medical or surgical abortion based solely on a positive early pregnancy test paper is unsafe.

>> Does a positive result on an early pregnancy test paper always mean pregnancy?

A positive pregnancy test does not necessarily correlate with conception. Positive results can also occur in cases of pituitary disease, hyperthyroidism, ovarian cysts, endometrial hyperplasia, and uterine cancer. A small number of women in early menopause or undergoing gynecological treatment may experience false positives due to hormonal imbalances or medication effects.
