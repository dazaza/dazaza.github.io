"I feel a bit tired, a bit sore, but I hope to adapt to the rhythm as soon as possible. In the second leg, Barcelona still needs to win," said Busquets in the first interview after Barcelona's 3-1 victory over Bayer Leverkusen. Sanchez, who scored twice during the match, was also caught by reporters. The Chilean player expressed, "I think my goals were crucial, and then Leo scored the third one. My condition is good. Barcelona will not slack off; we will persevere all the way through the elimination matches."

Ten minutes after Sanchez finished speaking, Guardiola appeared at the press conference. He began by saying, "In the first half, it was difficult for us to find chances to score, but we are satisfied with the final result because scoring three goals in Germany is very challenging. The players know that their performance in the second half was good. We competed well, just like in other matches, such as against Valencia."

Regarding Xavi's absence once again, Guardiola explained, "Xavi hasn't been in good shape for many days. I wanted him to play, but he couldn't." Pique's absence had nothing to do with injury, Guardiola admitted this was his decision: "Gerard (who did not play) was indeed... for technical reasons. I selected 18 players, and I couldn't afford to lose Gerard... But Tello and Xavi were also left out. Gerard has no reason to be upset. He knows he is irreplaceable for us and is a very important player, but I have to make choices." Guardiola denied punishing Pique: "Next week, Mascherano and Alves won't be able to play, so I let Gerard rest for this match."

Soon, Guardiola returned to the topic of the match itself. He analyzed, "The situation in the second half made me feel better, even though the first half was more suitable for our style of play because our players ran faster and better. The Germans could easily create difficulties for us. The first half was more of a back-and-forth battle, but that's normal because you're on the European stage. They were relaxed and created chances, and so did we. From the second half of the game, we learned lessons for the second leg. We cannot come here thinking that they won't create goal-scoring opportunities. Leverkusen players are very strong, they are a German team with many advantages, whether playing well or not, the players work hard."

Guardiola praised Sanchez: "Sanchez has scored a lot of goals. I really wish he hadn't been absent for so long because he brings us great contributions. His runs were excellent, making the field seem longer. I am happy for him, and you already know my evaluation of him." Previously, Guardiola had specifically praised Sanchez's spirit of sacrifice for playing with an injection while enduring pain. Guardiola also highly praised Messi: "Messi is an extraordinary player, and having him is a kind of luck for us. I know strikers need to score goals, and Messi has surpassed that point. He completed a very beautiful goal and initiated many attacks."

Guardiola also countered the media: "I know that only when we win 4-0 will people praise us. My feeling is that Barcelona's current performance is not mediocre; it's just that we previously played at an extremely high level, which makes our current performance look bad. I believe our current state is good. Barcelona players are artists in the art of competition, and they have proven that, today is no exception."
