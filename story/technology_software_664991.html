A Brief Analysis of the Case of Shanxi Forest Remote Video Monitoring and Fire Prevention System

by jiaojiao880206 on 2012-02-14 10:47:03

Forest fires are one of the world's major forestry disasters. With the continuous development of China's reforestation industry, fire prevention has become a top priority task. Forest fire prevention must adhere to the principle of "prevention first and active firefighting" in order to truly discover and solve problems early. I. Project Introduction

Shanxi Province is located at the edge of the Loess Plateau with a forest coverage rate of 14.12%, totaling 32.59 million mu (about 5.37 million acres). The province's forest areas, mining areas, and agricultural areas are interwoven with many scattered points across a vast area. Meanwhile, the narrow tube effect of the Taihang Mountains and Luliang Mountains parallel distribution accelerates the formation of windy weather. With the rapid growth of forest resources, the pressure on forest resource fire prevention and control has increased day by day.

It is estimated that during the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period, Shanxi Province experienced a total of 127 forest fires. Among them, there were 2 major forest fires covering an area of 11,563 hectares, affecting 2,808 hectares of forest land, destroying 81,269 cubic meters of timber and 1.64 million young trees. Eighteen people were injured or killed while fighting the fires, including five fatalities. The firefighting costs and direct economic losses totaled 31.82 million yuan. Frequent fires caused significant loss of forest resources and exposed prominent issues such as outdated equipment, insufficient firefighting methods, and lagging monitoring and warning systems. This resulted in four difficulties: timely warnings and forecasts for the whole province's forest fire prevention, difficulty in maintaining forest patrols, difficulty in organizing timely firefighting, and difficulty in commanding and coordinating on-site operations.

To fundamentally enhance the comprehensive capability of preventing and combating forest fires throughout the province, the forestry department of Shanxi Province requires the construction of a three-level "province-city-county" forest remote video surveillance system. The system uses a pure digital network video surveillance system to conduct 24-hour real-time warning and monitoring for more than 70% of the province’s forest resources area. Through remote video surveillance images, it can promptly detect fire hazards in forest areas, improving the accuracy and timeliness of forest fire prevention warnings. The annual forest fire occurrence rate will be less than 2 times per 100,000 hectares, the annual victimization rate less than 0.1‰, and the control rate less than 10 hectares per incident.

II. System Architecture

Forest fire prevention is a complex multi-disciplinary system engineering project and a major challenge faced by every country. The United States uses unmanned aerial vehicles for 24-hour forest fire warning; Canada combines satellite patrols with helicopter rotations for fire prevention; Australia adopts a combination of public reporting, high mountain observation, aircraft patrols, and satellite remote sensing for monitoring; domestically, most countries use a combination of high mountain observation, ground patrols, and satellite remote sensing. Although each method has its own advantages, they all suffer from high costs, incomplete supervision, and delayed detection. How to improve the efficiency of forest fire prevention with limited funds has always been a major problem troubling forest protection personnel.

In response to the immense pressure faced by forest fire prevention work, the Forestry Department of Shanxi Province has adopted modern communication and video surveillance technologies to explore a new method suitable for the provincial conditions of Shanxi - the "Forest Remote Video Surveillance Fire Prevention System". The first phase of the project covers 11 cities and 104 counties (cities, districts) in the province. The main construction content includes 400 remote monitoring points, 1 provincial forest fire monitoring command center, 11 municipal monitoring command centers, 12 main video conference venues, and 104 sub-video conference venues. The system adopts a three-level networking solution, namely monitoring points + county level, city level, and provincial level three-level networking, to predict fires and provide emergency command.

Monitoring Points + County Forestry Departments

Monitoring points + county forestry departments belong to the first level of the three-level networking system. In the forested mountains and highest points within the jurisdiction of various counties and regions in Shanxi Province, video monitoring points are established. These monitoring points consist of the following equipment: towers providing installation support for the main camera, anti-theft camera, microwave transmission equipment; indoor equipment such as batteries, charge/discharge controllers, inverters, encoders placed in the machine room; solar power supply providing uninterrupted power supply for 72 hours to all front-end devices; the main camera conducts video scanning for remote forest monitoring with a scanning radius reaching 1 to 5 kilometers; the anti-theft camera provides security monitoring and alarm prompts for front-end equipment; the remote voice system provides two-way remote intercom between the front end and the monitoring center and voice commands from the center to the front end; microwave equipment provides transparent transmission channels to transmit processed video and audio signals to various command centers and simultaneously receive control signals from the center.

All signals from the front-end monitoring points are encoded and compressed via network video servers for remote transmission to municipal forestry departments (central management centers). County forestry departments are the most direct users of the forest remote video surveillance fire prevention system, responsible for the front-end monitoring points of the forest remote video surveillance fire prevention system within their county jurisdictions and fulfilling the function of forest fire prevention; using management permissions distributed by municipal forestry departments, they can freely check data and information from any monitoring point within their county but cannot manage monitoring points in other counties or cities.

Municipal Forestry Departments

Municipal forestry departments belong to the second level of the three-level networking system. They are the core part of the entire forest remote video surveillance fire prevention system and also the centralized management center for this system, responsible for managing various monitoring points. Front-end monitoring points transmit data via a wireless network to the nearest wired network access point and then connect to the wired dedicated network nearby; real-time data and images from front-end monitoring points are transmitted to municipal forestry departments; data is centrally stored; corresponding management and usage permissions are allocated to county-level, municipal, and provincial forestry departments and various levels of users; data forwarding services are provided to county forestry departments and provincial forestry departments. Users of municipal forestry departments use their own permissions to freely check data and information from any monitoring point within their city but cannot manage monitoring points in other municipal forestry departments.

Provincial Forestry Departments

Provincial forestry departments belong to the third level of the three-level networking system. They can inspect the status of each monitoring point at the first level, remotely command and coordinate the resources under the jurisdiction of municipal forestry departments and county forestry departments, remotely command on-site fire prevention and firefighting work, and implement emergency command functions. Provincial forestry departments can use their own permissions to freely check data and information from any monitoring point within the province at various municipal and county forestry departments.

III. System Features

The system comprehensively employs digital video technology, modern communication technology, computer technology, and solar energy technology for implementation. Based on the video surveillance system, it integrates GPS positioning systems, meteorological sensor systems, geographic information systems, remote sensing systems, and video conferencing systems to form a digitized forest information management system that electronically manages forest fires, forest logging, wetland protection, afforestation situations, forest pests and diseases, farmland restoration to forest situations, and the species and numbers of wild animals in forest areas.

The system is a comprehensive monitoring system that features automatic fire prevention monitoring, remote voice communication, anti-theft alarms, solar power supply, large screen graphic display, and massive video data storage. Each functional module is no longer an independently operating module but rather an important component of the system, integrated into a complete and mature forest remote video surveillance fire prevention system. IV. Actual Benefits

After nearly two years of operation, the Shanxi forest fire remote monitoring system has achieved relatively obvious results. During the operation period, 28 fire incidents were detected within the monitored range, including 4 forest fire incidents, 7 fire incidents at the edges of newly reforested lands, and 17 incidents of burning straw and field edges in farmland. All 28 fire incidents were extinguished within 3 hours without causing a single forest fire. For this reason, the Forest Public Security Bureau of Shanxi Province proposed to continue promoting and implementing the construction of the forest remote video surveillance fire prevention system throughout the province, initially forming a three-level network video surveillance system at the provincial, municipal, and county levels. It will cover more than 26 million mu (about 4.27 million acres) of forests in the province to ensure the safety of forest resources. The Shanxi forest remote video surveillance fire prevention system provides a very good example for national forest fire prevention. Remote video surveillance technology can serve forest fire prevention, allowing forestry resources to receive more proactive and effective technical protection.
