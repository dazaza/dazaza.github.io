The Bittersweet Experience of Starting a Business on Taobao: Partners Leave, Health Declines

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 13:16:14

Editor's note: The author of this article, Mr. Chai, is a Taobao entrepreneur from Guangdong who gave up a stable high-paying job to start his own business, which is truly commendable. His experiences over the past three months are worth reading for anyone preparing to enter the e-commerce field or currently in the process of starting a business.

Opening remarks

Unconsciously, I have been doing Taobao for more than three months. For me, these three months can be said to be the lowest point of my life and also the period when I felt the most pressure, as if I had fallen into the lowest valley of my life. These days, I have been thinking whether I should write down this part of my life experience. If I don't write it down, I'm afraid I will forget it; if I do write it down, I'm afraid it will be too sad. After much deliberation, today I finally gathered the courage to write down some insights and experiences from these few months, to share with everyone. I hope to convey a positive energy that can encourage everyone to continue moving forward.

I officially started doing Taobao on May 20th. Why did I choose this day? First, this day is very memorable, 520 means "I love you"; Second, it was just the first Monday of the first week after I quit my job, officially starting my Taobao life. On such a romantic day, my tough Taobao life began.

In fact, doing Taobao has always been my dream. In the previous article "My Story with Taobao", I also mentioned the beginning and end of my relationship with Taobao. After several years of missed opportunities, this time I finally gathered the courage, gave up my job, gave up my stable life, even gave up my dignity, and under various pressures, I decided to do Taobao, so as not to regret again. I want to prove to everyone that I must make a name for myself on Taobao.

Starting the Taobao entrepreneurial journey

If we want to grow big and do well on Taobao, I alone am not enough; I can only play around. So I teamed up with a few friends who wanted to start a business. One of them is good at procurement, has a wide network of contacts, and can handle procurement and external work; another one was the creative director of my advertising company last year, mainly responsible for design; while I am mainly responsible for Taobao operations and promotion, as well as the overall planning and coordination of the business. It seems like the three of us form a perfect team. We also went through a period of adjustment before, and we all got along very well. The key is that everyone is very enthusiastic and wants to start a business, wants to do well in this matter, and success seems very close to us.

During the time when the three of us decided to start a business, we were all very excited. At that time, the movie "Chinese Partners" was released, and the three of us went to the cinema together to experience the success and hardships of Chinese partners. At that time, we also hoped to become partners like Cheng Dongqing, Wang Yang, and Meng Xiaojun.

Each time we would fantasize about our future, perhaps becoming the top in a certain Taobao category, or even winning the Top Ten Online Merchants Award in 2013, etc. In short, everything beautiful seemed to be waiting for us. Of course, we also considered failure, but the good outweighed the bad. After more than a month of communication and plotting, after repeated discussions, we finally decided to focus on the product of 3C digital accessories - phone cases. Why did we choose this product?

First, we have a friend's cousin who runs a factory that makes phone cases, with first-hand resources and does quite a large scale, and the products are also good;

Second, the initial investment is not very large, and the capital occupation is not significant;

Third, this market should still be in the development stage, temporarily not obsolete, as long as there are phones, they need phone cases, just changing styles;

Fourth, this operation is relatively simple, easier than clothing, shoes, and other products. Whether taking photos or writing detailed descriptions, it is relatively easy to get started. Therefore, we unanimously decided to do phone cases. Of course, we discussed many products in the early stage, including clothes, electronic products, shoes, etc., and even talked about adult products, but after repeated discussions, we still chose this product.

After deciding on the product, the next step was to finalize the office location. Due to my previous entrepreneurial experiences, I suggested not to invest too much in the early stage, minimizing risks. Whether it's purchasing goods or office space, everything should be kept simple, saving wherever possible. Therefore, we set up our office at our designer partner's home, which is spacious and where only he and his father live, making it seem suitable.

Feeling the timing was right, it was time to start our business. The designer and I resigned first, while the other partner, because his boss had helped him, it wasn't convenient for him to resign yet. Since we didn't need too many people initially, the designer and I started working first, and he continued part-time. That's how it began! As mentioned earlier, on May 20th, the day our Taobao entrepreneurial journey started.

The first week of Taobao life

The first week was very exciting. Actually, no matter what you do, it's always like this at the beginning, whether buying a new car, moving into a new house, or getting married. It's like newlyweds going on their honeymoon, the honeymoon period. Starting a business is the same. Because all the beautiful things are waiting for you, so whatever you do, you feel happy.

Many things look simple, but once you actually do them, you realize they're not so easy. Take something simple like familiarizing yourself with the phone case product, which took us a lot of effort. We weren't very knowledgeable about phones before, and the patterns on the phone cases were all the same, so receiving and checking the goods took a long time. We felt like every phone case looked the same (different models but the same pattern, each model had about 10 different patterns). Although we only had 7-8 products, it took us a whole week to get familiar with them. To understand these phones, we researched online, visited phone stores, and even bought phone models, trying each phone case to know which was 7100, i9100, 9300, etc.

To save money, we initially asked a photographer friend to take pictures for us. Even though he offered the lowest price, taking pictures of these phone cases still cost several thousand yuan. We thought it wasn't worthwhile, so we decided to take the pictures ourselves. Unfortunately, we hadn't learned photography before and didn't have any props, so we had to learn photography.

In the following days, the designer and I spent our time taking pictures, preparing product listings, designing logos, decorating the store, and learning Taobao rules. Whenever we didn't understand something, we would go to forums to learn or watch training materials. Every day, from 9 AM until 10 PM, we worked tirelessly without feeling tired or hardworking because this was our own business. Despite having only a few products, it took us a lot of effort and time, but we still felt a great sense of accomplishment.

Summary: The first week was mainly focused on foundational work, decorating the store, listing products, learning photography and decoration, and moving forward by trial and error.

Finding people to place fake orders, slowly seeing a small increase in orders

After a week, the products were listed, and the store was decorated, but we found that no one was paying attention, not even one person consulted. Occasionally, the Wangwang chat would sound, but it turned out to be someone advertising. The three of us discussed what methods could be used to quickly get some orders. A new store, even if customers come in, seeing no sales, they wouldn't dare to buy, as everyone has this mindset. The more people buy, the easier it is to make a sale; this is herd mentality. Chinese people like this, preferring crowded places. This is why restaurants with more customers tend to have better business, and customers are willing to queue for numbers. The neighboring restaurant doesn't need queuing, and no one goes there for this reason.

Therefore, after discussing for three months, we decided that each of us would develop five friends to buy items from our store. On Monday, the three of us actively promoted to our close friends, asking them to shop in our store. It didn't matter if they needed the items; as long as they placed an order, we would ship the goods and refund their money. Surprisingly, this method proved effective. Through our promotions, our customers were quite supportive and bought dozens of items. Once a few items had a certain sales base, on the Wednesday of the second week, indeed, an unfamiliar customer came to purchase. This was our first order, and we were thrilled. Following that, there were the second, third, and gradually more and more customers coming to buy.

Summarizing the second week's life, through our promotion to friends and placing fake orders, we finally made our first sales and achieved a good start.

Here, I also advise everyone not to place fake orders, especially using professional companies to do so, as some may be scammers. This has also been shared in Brother Seven's WeChat article.

A month to upgrade to a diamond level, but the pressure also came

Since making the first sale, we had continuous orders every day, from one to two, then to ten. Within a month, we upgraded to a diamond level. On the day we upgraded, the three of us celebrated a little in the evening, as our efforts finally paid off with a small reward.

After a month, although we upgraded to a diamond level, we didn't make much money. We were doing a low-price free shipping route, 28 yuan nationwide free shipping, with a cost of 16 yuan. Due to the small volume, the average shipping fee was 9 yuan, leaving a profit of only 3 yuan per order. For customers in remote areas like Inner Mongolia, Xinjiang, Gansu, Qinghai, etc., we would even incur losses. Thus, at the end of the month, we didn't make any money.

During this period, every weekend, we went to Huaqiangbei to check the market. Since it was our first time visiting Shenzhen Huaqiangbei, it took us some time to find the real wholesale phone case locations. On the second visit, we found the place but weren't sure what products to take. On the third visit, we finally picked up some new items. Each trip was costly, costing hundreds each time. Not making any profit and continuously investing added a lot of pressure on us. You might say, this amount isn't much, but considering I lost a lot last year running a restaurant and didn't have much money left, it was significant for us.

Actually, none of this mattered much. Since I started Taobao, the first thing my wife asked me every day was, "How many orders did you receive today? How much money did you earn?" At the beginning, I would tell her that I received two orders if I got one, and five orders if I got two. Fortunately, after our efforts, we managed to receive up to 20 orders at one point, but those were loss-making. Besides asking daily about profitability, she would also ask, "When can we start making a profit? If there aren't many orders, will you keep persisting?" Every day, she would remind me, "Look at how much you earn now. You need to support the car, buy milk powder, pay rent, send your son to school, etc." She would often ask, "When will you start making money?" Honestly speaking, I earned at least 8,000 yuan a month working, but now I don't even make 800 yuan. These invisible pressures were suffocating, and I felt exhausted.

I still remember one day when my uncle called me. He said, "I heard you quit your job to do Taobao," and I replied affirmatively. Then he added, "I also heard that you haven't even earned enough for food." When I heard this, I felt a lump in my throat, a mix of emotions surged in my heart. But I calmly and smilingly answered my uncle, "It's not going well right now, but it's normal during the early stages of entrepreneurship. I believe I will improve through my efforts." My uncle continued, "I heard your salary was pretty high before. Doing this full-time is a waste. You can do this part-time while working. Young people having dreams is good, but earning money is important too." I reassured my uncle, "Rest assured, Uncle, I will definitely do well and make money." After hanging up, I really felt upset because I had always been an excellent person in my family. How could I say I couldn't even earn enough for food? I earned my tuition fees in college, made tens of thousands during summer breaks, got promoted quickly after a few years of work, had a stable income, opened a chain convenience store during my employment, was the first person in the village to buy a car, and last year opened a Hunanese restaurant covering over 400 square meters. All these achievements made my family proud. Now, I can't even earn enough for food, and it's filled with mixed feelings.

Actually, from the very beginning, my wife didn't fully support me doing Taobao, and my family opposed it too. But I couldn't give up my dream because of this. I wanted to prove myself, that Taobao can also lead to a good life.

Even though I listened to my wife's nagging every day and faced increasing living pressures, it didn't shake my determination. The three of us were still very confident. During the day, the designer and I repeated the routine tasks: listing products, improving the store, learning marketing, studying promotion strategies, and shipping goods. In the evenings, the three of us would discuss issues and ways to improve. After a while, we realized that phone cases were already a saturated market on Taobao, with fierce competition and low profit margins. As small sellers, it was difficult for us to make money. Selling just a dozen orders a day meant we couldn't even talk about making a profit; feeding ourselves was a problem. Our store mainly sold just two items, and the newer items we brought in didn't sell at all. We began to doubt if we had chosen the wrong product or the wrong category.

We started discussing choosing a new category. What should we do? Without funds or resources, our discussions led to no results. Some things are not as simple as you think. Finding a good product is rare, and finding a good industry requires a certain level of expertise in a particular field, which we lacked in the past two years. This issue troubled us for a long time and was discussed extensively. We also fantasized about finding a good category and then seeking investors, but these ideas were not practical.

Unexpected event: one of the partners decided to leave

Days passed, but the number of orders didn't increase significantly. Just when we were confused, an incident occurred involving one of our partners whose family had an emergency. Actually, when he sent us a text message that night, I knew things would change. This incident might affect some of our cooperative relationships, and indeed, he eventually decided to leave. Due to financial pressures, he couldn't quit his current job; he had to support his family without any other sources of income. He had no choice but to make this decision. We respected his choice, as it might be the best decision, since even I didn't know how to proceed with our current path. Regardless, he and I remained friends and good partners.

The departure of one of our partners had a significant impact on us, though we knew it was inevitable, it still made us a bit sad. By this point, it had been more than two months, and we still hadn't seen much change. At this time, we rented a simple apartment outside to use as an office. The place was rudimentary and located in a remote area with poor mobile signal. To save money, we had to find such a place. At this point, both the designer and I didn't even have money for food. We ate the cheapest fast food every day and dared not spend a single penny recklessly. The me who used to enjoy dining out and singing karaoke with friends hasn't done so since starting Taobao. There was no personal life left, only a simple and monotonous Taobao life, staring at the computer all day, thinking about how to do Taobao, while enduring various pressures. Sometimes it was really hard to persist. Luckily, during this Taobao journey, I met a few friends who also did Taobao. They gave me a lot of encouragement and support and shared many valuable Taobao experiences. I am deeply grateful to them.

Under the pressure of life, my other partner also left

After a few months, we still didn't make much profit, and my other partner had to temporarily leave as well. He also faced significant life pressures, being in his thirties, needing to get married and buy a house. He couldn't continue like this, and I fully understood and supported his decision to return to work, which might alleviate some of my own pressure (omitted details).

After he left, only I remained, persisting alone. Eating alone outside, I felt particularly tasteless, possibly due to getting accustomed to the two-person lifestyle. Though it was tough, there were still many joyful moments. Once, reflecting on my current situation, I suddenly felt a wave of sadness and had an impulse to cry, with tears welling up in my eyes. I had never felt this way, not even during a breakup. Amidst conflicting emotions, I restrained myself, telling myself, "I cannot cry. I haven't lost yet. Even if the whole world abandons you, you must not give up on yourself. As long as you persevere a little longer, things will surely get better. This is the hardest time, and you're already at the lowest point. What do you have to fear?"
