Wang Xiujuan, CEO of Dajie.com: Sister is going to create a Chinese version of LinkedIn!

by anonymous on 2013-10-15 21:20:16

Beijing in September has a touch of autumn, and through the glass on the 5th floor of Building D of Tsinghua Tongfang Science & Technology Plaza, the green still strongly shows its presence.

Wang Xiujuan's face was filled with excitement. She had just returned from Hong Kong, bringing back the Red Herring Asia Top 100 Technology Enterprises Award for 2013.

Trips like this have almost become a microcosm of Wang Xiujuan's year, attending various technology industry conferences, giving speeches everywhere, telling people about what she is doing.

In 2013, Wang Xiujuan was more high-profile than ever before. She and the street network she founded are accelerating towards the dream of becoming the Chinese version of LinkedIn, and this dream may have taken shape when she truly entered the workplace.

Social DNA

Wang Xiujuan, who studied finance, started her career in a familiar field, choosing a foreign securities company as her first stop.

In 1999, after working at the securities company for two years, Wang Xiujuan felt exhausted, frequently encountering setbacks in business progress, and finding it difficult to adapt to the internal culture of the company.

That year, she was 22 years old, slightly disappointed with work, no longer holding the aspirations she had when she started; slightly confused, where is my future? What should I do? No longer seeing youthful enthusiasm ahead, nor a bright future behind, drawing her sword and looking around, her heart was filled with confusion, like the seven-year itch in marriage, Wang Xiujuan's career development encountered the "three-year itch."

An accidental opportunity led Wang Xiujuan into two fields: the Internet and social networking, and since then she has been moving forward on this path, "Street Network may be the endpoint of this journey," Wang Xiujuan smiled, her expression serious, her gaze resolute.

"I remember seeing a recruitment notice for ChinaRen on the Beijing Youth Daily at the time, and I went immediately," said Wang Xiujuan, 36 years old, leaning against the sofa, her deep gaze looking out the window, seeing the shadow of herself at 22.

According to Wang Xiujuan's recollection, in a dilapidated bungalow near the south gate of Tsinghua University, she met the three founders of ChinaRen: Chen Yizhou, Zhou Yunfan, and Yang Ning. Her impression of meeting these three founders was described with words like "spirited," "young talents," and "new era."

On one side was the sea water, and on the other side was fire. Looking around at the shabby office, facing the spirited internet people, like falling in love, Wang Xiujuan fell in love with this "strange industry." Once entering the gates of the Hou family, the ocean was deeper than expected, and when she looked back, Wang Xiujuan had already become an "elder" of the second wave of internet joiners, many years gone by.

Indeed, in 1999, China was right at the beginning of the rapid development of the internet, and Chen Yizhou, Zhou Yunfan, and Yang Ning, who brought the latest technology and ideas from overseas to found ChinaRen, left a deep impression on Wang Xiujuan.

Without any hesitation, Wang Xiujuan moved from the best office building in Beijing, Hengjin Center, to a dilapidated bungalow. "There was actually a bit of a gap, not only the change in the workplace, but also the salary was halved. But none of that mattered, the impact of the internet was indescribable, and I joined without looking back," she lit a Su cigarette in front of her, and her lips revealed a reserved smile when talking about her choice at the time.

Fast Lane

If the previous shock was due to the longing for unknown things, then after joining ChinaRen, Wang Xiujuan was shocked again and again.

"The company was developing very quickly at the time, the entire company was quite aggressive, with a feeling of unstoppable momentum," gesturing with her hands, Wang Xiujuan seemed to return to that excited state at the time, "Before being acquired, the alumni directory product alone saw growth of tens of thousands of users per day, making it the fourth most visited website in China, and the internet completely touched and influenced my professional outlook."

Wang Xiujuan believed that this rapid development stemmed from ChinaRen's initial positioning, accurately targeting the student market, successfully solving the problem of internet speed, and launching a series of products such as the alumni directory, increasing user stickiness.

Even today, in the development process of Street Network, there are traces of ChinaRen, such as the initial positioning of Street Network which also chose college students as its breakthrough point.

In addition, many products launched by Street Network this year, such as circles and online education, ultimately aim to increase user stickiness, breaking the vicious cycle of low user stickiness in professional social networking. In Wang Xiujuan's words, "The core of all this is still professional social networking, serving professional social networking."

A person's development depends on learning and experience, and familiar places are always easier to handle. The experience at ChinaRen influenced Wang Xiujuan's later actions.

Just as ChinaRen was rapidly developing, the first internet bubble arrived unexpectedly, and ChinaRen faced its biggest crisis, with a much-needed round of financing failing to materialize, leaving the entire team in difficulty.

Sohu acted at the right moment.

Zhang Chaoyang brought the entire ChinaRen under his wing, but soon after, the three founders of ChinaRen left successively, with Chen Yizhou going abroad, and Zhou Yunfan and Yang Ning founding Kongzhong Corporation.

After staying at Sohu for 11 months, Wang Xiujuan eventually chose to leave, saying, "At Sohu, I didn't feel a sense of belonging, not part of the core group."

Following the footsteps of two of ChinaRen's founders, Zhou Yunfan and Yang Ning, she joined Kongzhong Corporation.

This was another rapidly developing company, as the 2.5G business was just emerging, and Kongzhong Corporation, through a series of moves and cooperation with operators, advanced swiftly, "It was simply a money-making pace, with revenue doubling every month," Wang Xiujuan described, but at the same time, she also saw that the SP/CP model could not last long.

At this time, Chen Yizhou returned to China, with the support of Son Masayoshi, Chen Yizhou founded Oak Pacific Interactive (OPI), and Wang Xiujuan chose to collaborate with Chen Yizhou once again, taking over Mop, achieving triple-digit growth in performance, incubating Xiaonei.com, securing a $430 million investment from SoftBank, and putting Oak Pacific Interactive on track within just a few years.

Slow Lane

At OPI, Wang Xiujuan's career reached its peak, as a professional manager, she achieved excellence, rising to the ranks of OPI's senior management team and overseeing almost all aspects of OPI's operations.

But those with unyielding ideals are never people who can stay idle, and nearly ten years of experience in the internet industry further intensified her characteristic of "loving to stir things up."

Thus, she chose to fly solo, bringing with her a decade of accumulation in the internet field, and founded Street Network, dreaming from the start of creating a professional social platform that would become the Chinese version of LinkedIn.

"Starting Street Network was due to multiple reasons. Investment institutions have always wanted me to start something, to invest in me, and as you mentioned, the 'glass ceiling,'" but she believed the most important reason was, "I've been in the internet industry for so many years, and I always wanted to have a business that I participated in from start to finish, that belongs to me."

In 2008, Street Network made its debut.

Unlike her previous experiences at ChinaRen and OPI, from its establishment in 2008 to the present, Street Network has accumulated only about 25 million users, which seems contrary to the rapid advancement mode Wang Xiujuan experienced at ChinaRen and OPI.

And these 25 million users were developed mainly over the past two years, between 2008 and 2011, Street Network evolved from an idea plus Wang Xiujuan's confidence into a preliminarily planned platform, "In those early years, the BSNS model was rather undervalued, even my early angel investors frankly stated that they were investing in me personally, and were not too optimistic about the BSNS model."

"But I firmly believed in this model because it is a model with actual demand, despite being slow, I persisted," Wang Xiujuan lit another cigarette, perhaps even now that Street Network has entered a fast lane of development, she still feels lonely, as professional social networking continues to face many doubts.

But she has continued to persist, perhaps waiting for Street Network to have the same explosive growth as LinkedIn after five years of accumulation, and this persistence is closely tied to her own experiences.

There is a story circulating within Street Network: during a marathon race last year, Wang Xiujuan competed alongside a physically outstanding colleague, and she managed to persevere while the colleague failed to finish.

"When I was young, I wasn't in good health, so I often practiced long-distance running, circling the mountains of our small county," people who practice long-distance running almost always possess a quality of never giving up.

Over the past five years, Street Network has invested a great deal of effort in accumulating a vast database of personal and corporate professional information, enduring solitude and persisting unwaveringly, leading to its current explosive growth.

"In the early years, Street Network walked a lonely but stable path, and now, we need to start running," Wang Xiujuan firmly believes, "The era of professional social networking in China has arrived, this is an irresistible era, and also a revolution in business networks."

To wait for this opportunity, she has remained steadfast for five years, or perhaps she has been waiting since she first entered ChinaRen, over these fifteen years, Wang Xiujuan has gained much, and sacrificed much. During the interview, she repeatedly tapped her left arm, whether out of habit or because there was once pain there.

A simple reflection, the greater sacrifices made for Street Network might only be clear to her, but her eyes remain resolute, her spirit remains high.

Centering on professional social networking, Street Network's products are being launched one after another, and Street Network has entered a phase of accelerated development.

However, whether Street Network can break through amidst giants like Weibo, WeChat, and Renren, similar to how LinkedIn broke out amidst Facebook and Twitter, remains doubted by outsiders.

In the field of professional social networking, cultural differences between China and foreign countries are significant. For example, overseas corporate leaders can engage in equal communication with many industry professionals, but in China, there is a strong sense of hierarchy; furthermore, overseas people can easily become good friends online, whereas in China, without having met in person or shared meals, it is difficult to become true friends.

In China, close relationships among acquaintances have always been a widespread subculture, and this reality determines that despite professional social networking being slow, it is a rigid demand.

Wang Xiujuan believes that Street Network will surely succeed, "Originally, everyone in China said users wouldn't register on websites using their real names, and wouldn't transfer offline relationships online, but now it has succeeded. So nothing is absolute, as long as Street Network is excellent enough, localized professional social networking in China will surely succeed."

Not only can it succeed. She also quoted Jack Ma, "eBay is like a crocodile in the ocean, and I am a crocodile in the Yangtze River. If in the ocean, I may not win, but in the Yangtze River, I am sure to win!"

"In China, we can create the first professional social platform with Chinese characteristics, and we can surpass LinkedIn!" Wang Xiujuan shifted her tone, "Look, after some time, I can take you to see the red leaves of Fragrant Hills, they are indeed breathtakingly beautiful, though it's a bit early now."

Yes, the mountain covered with red leaves is a season of harvest.
