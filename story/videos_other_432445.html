The owner posted online to sell his house, saying that a BMW and ten thousand yuan of educational funds would be given as gifts.

by iface001 on 2011-05-17 16:40:31

Yesterday, after a close-range contact by the reporter, it was discovered that he was actually a handsome guy born in the 80s.

Is this Fuzhou's most powerful owner in history real? Why does he want to sell his house and give away a BMW car? Is this just a harmless gimmick by the developer?

Since Mr. Xue learned that Ms. He was from Fuqing, he also conversed in the Fuqing dialect and mentioned which village he was from. Through various channels, the reporter learned that this most powerful owner, although young, started his own business in 2003 and is also the general manager of a certain digital company in Fuzhou.

Netizens questioned: giving away a BMW doesn't make this owner lose out

After checking the property procedures, Mr. Xue introduced that the house was purchased in 2007 and handed over in 2008. The original intention of buying the house was to let his parents move in, but later his parents didn't want to.

"Buy a villa and get a BMW car plus ten thousand yuan scholarship," on April 6, an owner from Jinshan in Fuzhou posted a selling post online, which was recently reprinted by forums such as Sina and was dubbed "Fuzhou's most powerful owner in history" by netizens.

Quoting a comment from a netizen, this is just a creative house-selling post in the internet era. This reporter, Guo Liang, Luo Yumin.

"Fuzhou Park No.1, 160 square meters ground-level villa, fixed price 1.5 million yuan for sale. Buyers who decide on the same day after viewing the house will be gifted a brand new 3-series BMW; if they decide the next day, then a NISSAN Teana will be given; if they decide the day after, then a Buick LaCrosse will be gifted." This post, initially only published on a community website in Fuzhou, was reprinted by Tianya and Sina forums the next day due to its catchy gimmick. In addition, the poster, Mr. Xue, also mentioned in the post that only three house viewers are arranged each day. If the buyer is the first viewer of the day, then an additional 10,000 yuan scholarship will be given; the second viewer gets 8,000 yuan scholarship; the third viewer gets 5,000 yuan scholarship. It also notes that intermediaries, reporters, and other uninterested parties should not disturb.

A house-selling post with enough gimmicks

For this most powerful owner, most netizens expressed doubts in their comments, believing it to be just a gimmick. Some think that the owner's selling price is too high, and even with a BMW 3 series given away, it's not a loss. "A house worth 1.1 million yuan can be done, also with panoramic views of Jinshan Park, saving more than 400,000 yuan can buy a well-configured 3 series."

Additionally, Mr. Xue's property was also listed for sale on Fuzhou websites in March, with the same house priced at 1.2 million yuan at that time. The difference was that the earlier post did not include giving away cars, and a mobile phone number was left. However, compared to the later post which only left a QQ number and strongly rejected reporters and intermediaries, the earlier post received significantly fewer replies and was never reprinted.

The poster is from Fuqing, in the digital industry, and is also a general manager

At 3 PM yesterday, Mr. Xue, known as the most powerful owner, appeared at the entrance of the sports club in Zone B of Park No.1 in Jinshan, Fuzhou, with his girlfriend. During the conversation, the reporter also learned that he was actually born in the 80s.

"Today you are the first batch, many people contacted me yesterday," Mr. Xue asked Ms. He, who came to view the house, if she was an intermediary right away because he didn't want to sell the house through intermediaries, so he posted online.

I learned that Park No.1 is located in the Jinshan Park area, known as the synonym for luxury housing in Fuzhou. From the sales office of the complex, I learned that the current phase three of Park No.1 mainly consists of duplex units, with areas ranging from 130 to 170 square meters, and prices per square meter ranging from 6,000 to 10,000 yuan. The house Mr. Xue plans to sell belongs to phase two of Park No.1, which opened for sale in 2007, "It cost several hundred thousand yuan when I bought it then."

As long as someone buys the house, he will immediately go buy a new BMW and give it to them, treating it as a discount on the house price. This 80s-born most powerful owner said that if the house were sold through intermediaries, they would make a profit of five or six ten thousand yuan, and he felt that it wouldn't be worthwhile.

Although the mysterious Mr. Xue only left a QQ number as a contact method, influenced by the post, Ms. He from Fuzhou still managed to get in touch with him and made an appointment to view the house.
