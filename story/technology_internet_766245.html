IBM says it will no longer directly subsidize retiree health care plan

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 20:05:17

On the morning of September 8, according to Beijing time, IBM announced this week that it would cancel the medical plan for 110,000 retired employees subsidized by the company and instead pay these employees to help them purchase insurance through a medical insurance exchange. This move shows that even the most financially strong employers can no longer afford the once very common medical benefits in the face of rising medical costs in the United States.

This initiative by IBM will affect all retired employees eligible for Medicare. Medicare is a system managed by the US federal government that provides medical insurance for people over the age of 65 and disabled individuals. IBM will thus be rid of the responsibility of providing retiree medical benefits. The company said that without significant new investment, the growth in medical costs would make the current plan unsustainable.

In President Obama's healthcare reform proposal, the medical insurance exchange is similar to today's stock exchanges. As more and more companies transfer retiree benefits to pension institutions and uniformly pay fees to both current and retired employees, allowing them to bear their own medical expenses, the role of the medical insurance exchange will become even more important.

In recent weeks, in a notice signed by Chief Medical Officer Kyu Rhee, IBM has informed retired employees that if they wish to continue receiving medical benefits, they must choose one of the health insurance plans provided by Extend Health. Extend Health is a large private health insurance exchange incorporated into Medicare and operated by Towers Watson & Co in New York.

IBM stated that the current retiree insurance plan for those eligible for Medicare will terminate on December 31, 2013. IBM mentioned in the notice: "The cost growth of the current retiree medical plan will be unsustainable. For employees eligible for Medicare, the medical costs associated with IBM’s current plan will nearly triple by 2020, which will significantly impact premiums and cash payment costs."

Exchanges like Extend Health typically offer multiple insurance plans from various insurance companies, allowing insured individuals to choose the plan that best suits their needs and budget. This creates competition, ensuring lower prices.

In the future, IBM will no longer directly subsidize retirees' premiums but will instead pay retirees an annual fee through a health retirement account, enabling retirees to purchase Medicare Advantage plans and supplemental insurance via the exchange and cover other medical expenses. If retirees do not participate in this plan through Extend Health, they will not receive any subsidies. (By Weijin)
