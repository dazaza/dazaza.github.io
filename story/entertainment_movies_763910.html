Synopsis:

Even the most insignificant person can rise to become a true hero in times of crisis... Paul Blart (Kevin James) is a single father living in the suburbs of New Jersey, raising his young daughter Maya (Raini Rodriguez). As a security guard at a nearby shopping mall, Paul strives hard to maintain his modest yet cozy home with his meager salary. However, it seems that no one but Paul himself finds his job important, often mocking him for being overly serious about trivial matters. Despite this, Paul never pays attention to others' disdain and firmly believes he is safeguarding the lives and property of mall shoppers.

Unexpectedly, a group of highly skilled robbers invades the mall, driving out all the people and sealing every exit, clearly targeting the bank inside the mall... Paul, accidentally caught up in the situation, transforms from an overlooked mall security guard in New Jersey into a courageous vigilante when he discovers that Amy (Jayma Mays), the object of his secret affection, has been taken hostage by the robbers. Not only does he act for the one he loves, but also for the mall he cherishes – don't underestimate me just because I haven't shown my strength!

Behind-the-scenes Production:

The Director's Expertise in Comedy:

Steve Carr, the director of "Paul Blart: Mall Cop," is a seasoned hand in comedy genres, having directed successful films like "Dr. Dolittle 2" and "Daddy Day Care." Speaking about this new work, Steve Carr said: "This is a relatively warm-hearted comedy. My goal was to make it 'clean' and 'healthy.' I hope both children and adults can enjoy watching it, especially families who can watch it together in theaters without feeling awkward, unlike many comedies that might embarrass them in front of friends or family. After setting this tone, I had to adjust some parts of the original script, mainly focusing on the protagonist's actions and words. You know, sometimes during comedy shoots, actors improvise, and these improvisations can easily cross boundaries, especially profanity. If these reach MPAA, they would definitely prevent this movie from receiving an all-age rating. So my requirement for the actors was to stay within appropriate limits and always remember that this is a movie suitable for 7 or 8-year-old children."

Speaking about the story of "Paul Blart: Mall Cop," Steve Carr said: "The script was written by the lead actor, Kevin James, and the reason I took on this project was due to his invitation. I am very grateful for his trust in me. Personally, I'm somewhat dissatisfied with the plot of this movie, mainly because of the running time restriction. The company required me to control the runtime within 90 minutes, while Kevin James' script could have easily extended beyond two hours. Due to this limitation, I had to cut many interesting segments. I believe this resulted in a reduction of the film's coherence, making it seem less convincing. I wanted to show more of the protagonist's inner journey, but there wasn't much opportunity for that."

Mentioning Kevin James' performance in the movie, Steve Carr said: "He is a very funny person. There are quite a few action scenes in this movie, which were indeed challenging given his physique. But he endured through it all, always smiling and telling me how much he enjoyed shooting those heroic scenes. Sometimes, when he couldn't take it anymore, he would secretly ask me if we could shoot more dialogue or life-related scenes next. I'd reply, 'No, you must jump down from the stairs right now.' During the filming process, Kevin James was extremely professional. He spent his breaks living and patrolling with real mall security officers, understanding their professional habits and work processes, making his role in the movie very convincing. I must say, Kevin James put a lot of effort into this movie, and I hope the audience will like his performance."

Kevin James Tackling Comedy Alone for the First Time:

When mentioning Kevin James, many people associate him with his previous works such as "My Big Fat Gay Wedding" and "Don't Mess with Zohan," where he partnered with Adam Sandler. Kevin James doesn't deny this: "Adam Sandler and I are a great team. We became good friends while shooting 'My Big Fat Gay Wedding.' To be honest, while writing the script for 'Paul Blart: Mall Cop,' I did consider whether to give Adam Sandler a role, but I thought he wouldn't want to play a mall security officer or shopkeeper. I even imagined what it would be like if he played a villain, but I knew he wouldn't do it because the villain in this movie is much more serious than his other comedic roles. The other actors in 'Paul Blart: Mall Cop' performed exceptionally well, such as Jayma Mays, who plays my dream lover, and Raini Rodriguez, who plays my daughter. Working with them was very pleasant."

Regarding the conception of the story for "Paul Blart: Mall Cop," Kevin James, as the screenwriter, said: "I came up with this idea a few years ago. I wanted to create a contrast; how would an ordinary person, who considers themselves extraordinary, react in the face of danger? Initially, the script I wrote was an action movie where an average person proved their abilities with fists, but later I realized that kind of story was uninteresting, as there are already too many similar ones on the screen. So I made some changes, turning it into its current form, making the whole story appear more absurd and funnier. Some people think this movie resembles 'Die Hard,' and I am also a fan of Bruce Willis. I must admit I was influenced by him, but this movie is not simply a copy of 'Die Hard.' It's more about the story I wanted to tell. The character I play, Paul Blart, is a typical middle-aged American blue-collar worker. He has many things he wants to do but hasn't done, his career is not going well, but he remains optimistic and faces everything with a positive attitude. Some call it the 'American spirit,' but I think it's a universal spirit or a spirit of struggle found worldwide. There's a beautiful love story in the movie where he falls for a woman named Amy, played by Jayma Mays. In the movie, Paul creates quite a few laughs for her. I think this is something men often do when they're infatuated with someone, hoping the audience will genuinely laugh while watching. Amy is also the motivation for Paul to become a hero. For her, Paul does things he would never normally think about or do, ultimately winning her heart. If I were to summarize 'Paul Blart: Mall Cop' in one sentence, it's the story of an ordinary person becoming a hero. Although it sounds cliché, I believe everyone will like this kind of story."

Fun Facts:

- This film is considered a tribute to Bruce Willis' 1988 film "Die Hard."

- Sony initially planned to release this film directly on DVD. After persuasion by the producers, they agreed to a wide release in theaters across the U.S.

- Filming for "Paul Blart: Mall Cop" began in late March 2008, originally titled as "Kevin James' New Film."

- The first few weeks of filming took place in Boston, but the main scenes were shot at the Burlington Mall in Massachusetts.

- Filming lasted from April to August 2008. Since the story takes place in winter, the Burlington Mall was decorated with Christmas ornaments.

- During the production of this film, Kevin James didn't just play the lead role; he also served as the screenwriter and producer.
