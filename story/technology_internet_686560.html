What are the harms of flat warts?

by shenqiang0 on 2012-03-02 10:07:35

What are the harms of flat warts? The appearance of flat warts has a relatively serious impact on patients, and the influence is multifaceted. In daily life, people's understanding of the harm caused by flat warts to patients is not very clear. Below, experts from Shanxi Huimin Hospital will give a brief introduction to this issue.

The occurrence of flat warts seriously affects aesthetics. The flat wart rash is a flat, smooth papule ranging in size from a hat pinhead to a mung bean. It is round, oval or polygonal in shape, hard in texture, and normally skin-colored or light brown. It can occur on various parts of the body such as the face, back of the hand, armpit, etc.

Flat warts also have the characteristic of being easily contagious. The contagiousness of flat warts can be transmitted indirectly, such as through contaminated objects, wounds, etc., and scratching oneself can also cause接种transmission, leading to flat warts!

There is also a relatively high probability of flat warts becoming cancerous. A greater harm of flat warts is that HPV virus can cause cervical cancer in women. Modern medical research has confirmed that all diseases caused by [human papillomavirus] infection can lead to canceration, among which [wart-like epidermal dysplasia] has the highest rate of canceration, followed by [genital warts], and [flat warts] rank third in terms of canceration rate.

According to statistics from the World Health Organization, the occurrence of cervical cancer is closely related to human papillomavirus HPV infection, with a carcinogenic rate of 4.7%-10.2%. Cervical cancer ranks second among female malignant tumors, only after breast cancer, and the age group of patients is increasingly getting younger.

The harmfulness of the occurrence of flat warts is still quite obvious, so in daily life, it is necessary to pay attention to preventive work!
