52 Special Wine Red Luck 460ml (3-bottle set): Special Wine adheres to the ancient brewing techniques, using top-grade tribute rice essence from a national-level reserve as its raw material. It absorbs the charm of wine brewed in ancient Danxia pits and is aged for several years before finally achieving the quality of an excellent premium liquor body. The exquisite porcelain used to contain it reflects traditional Chinese aesthetics, with an elegant and rich fragrance that allows the charm of the East to flow between cups. In each sip, there's a world uniquely mine.

Special Wine has a long history, originating from whole grain rice as its main ingredient, using Daqu as the saccharifying fermenting agent. Through traditional residue-continuing mixed distillation, "three entries and four exits" operation, solid-state fermentation in old pits, gentle steam distillation, quality-based alcohol extraction, graded storage, and multiple processes including aging and blending, it forms a unique aroma type known as "Special Aroma". Its process features are: "whole grain rice as the raw material, Daqu made from wheat bran and lees, red褚stone bricks forming the pit walls, three types present but not reliant on them" (the three aromas being soy sauce aroma, strong aroma, and clear aroma), making it a unique variety among China's liquors.

The former National Light Industry Ministry's Food Fermentation Research Institute spent over a year analyzing the aroma components of Special Wine, confirming that its aroma component content and quantity ratio relationship are clearly different from other types of liquor, possessing independent characteristics to form its own aroma type. In March 1997, after review and recommendation by the National Light Industry General Corporation, and approval by the National Standardization Committee, Special Wine's aroma type (Special Aroma) became one of the five major aroma types under the "Other Aroma Types" category of national liquors.

As the "Leader of Special Aroma": Special Wine is unique, using whole grain rice as its raw material, rich in odd-numbered complex fragrances, harmonious in aroma and flavor, with a lingering aftertaste, no headache-inducing effects, and no post-drinking headaches, making it a rare treasure among wines.

Special Wine was consecutively rated as Jiangxi Province's Famous Wine in 1963, 1980, and 1983. It received the title of Jiangxi Province's Quality Product twice. In 1984, it won the Silver Cup Award at the Light Industry Ministry's Liquor Quality Competition.
