What are the causes of toothache? What are the reasons for toothache?

Toothache is not a big problem, but it feels really bad. You can't eat well or sleep well, your body's resistance decreases, and the disease takes longer to heal.

There are several reasons for toothache:

1. Dental hypersensitivity. It is often caused by gum recession, exposure of dentin at the neck of the tooth, and dental defects. At this time, cold, heat, sweetness, sourness and other stimuli can cause pain, but the pain will disappear after the stimulation stops.

2. Food impaction pain. The space between teeth can be impacted by food, causing toothache, which is called food impaction pain.

3. Dry socket syndrome. It usually occurs 2 to 4 days after tooth extraction and can cause spontaneous continuous severe pain. During examination, it can be found that the blood clot in the tooth extraction wound smells bad.

4. Acute periapical periodontitis. It is caused by acute pulpitis or trauma. The affected tooth shows continuous pain, a floating sensation, and inability to chew. Patients can accurately identify the affected tooth, and tapping on the affected tooth causes pain. At this point, since the nerve of the affected tooth has died, there is no provoked pain.

5. Periodontal abscess. Further development of periodontal tissue inflammation can lead to suppurative inflammation. When an abscess forms, the pain is severe, and when the abscess forms, a fluctuation sensation appears locally. After the formation of a periodontal abscess, the pain can significantly decrease or relieve.

6. Acute pulpitis. It is common in patients with deep cavities. Bacteria enter the pulp cavity through the cavity, causing congestion and inflammation of the tooth nerve. Toothache is often spontaneous, worsens at night, and becomes more severe after cold or hot stimulation. The pain can also radiate to the face and ear. In cases of suppurative pulpitis, patients experience increased pain when exposed to heat, while cold stimulation reduces or eliminates the pain.
