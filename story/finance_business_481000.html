How Master Kang became Master Kuang - Import Water Purifiers, Inlet - Focus Blog

by shinian60 on 2011-08-24 13:35:47

In recent days, the fact that Master Kang's mineral water is mixed with tap water was exposed by the "Daily Economic News". This revelation caused a huge stir, and the "Water King" which ranked first in market share of mineral water in 2007 became the target of public criticism. People began to discuss Master Kang's false advertising. An article published on August 11th in our newspaper, titled "Some merchants in Jinan jokingly refer to it as 'Deceptive Master'", caused strong reactions among consumers. The scandal of Master Kang turning into "Deceptive Master" spread widely, plunging Master Kang into the "water source gate".

It should be noted that as early as June, some media had questioned the claim on the packaging of Master Kang's mineral water that it "contains free potassium". On June 12th, the Shanghai "Daily Economic News" published an article titled "Master Kang's Free Potassium Drinks for Health: Chemistry Professor Calls It Nonsense", which first uncovered Master Kang's false advertising. On June 17th, the Qianjiang Evening News published two advertisements titled "Why is Mineral Water More Acidic Than Purified Water?" and "What is the pH Value of Your Drinking Water?", which made people doubt the health benefits of Master Kang's mineral water.

Finally, on August 6th, an article in the "Daily Economic News" titled "Source of Master Kang's Mineral Water Turns Out to Be Tap Water" provided the answer. The report pointed out that Master Kang's so-called mineral water was actually mixed with tap water, and calling it mineral water was just Master Kang's own claim. Immediately, the press was flooded with reports accusing Master Kang of suspected false advertising.

Facing public pressure, Master Kang admitted on August 5th to the "Daily Economic News" that its Hangzhou production base indeed used urban tap water to produce mineral water. However, online doubts continued to grow, targeting the issue of "selecting high-quality water sources" in the advertisement, asking "If it comes from tap water, how can it be a high-quality water source?" Master Kang also admitted that apart from Changbai Mountain spring water, using tap water to make mineral water was an unwritten rule in the industry.

On August 6th, Master Kang Holdings Limited publicly issued an explanation titled "Explanation of 'Master Kang Drinking Mineral Water'" on its official website, arguing that "whether using tap water, groundwater, or other natural water, all meet the national standard GB5749 'Hygienic Standard for Drinking Water'". However, relevant experts stated that there are no strict regulations for mineral water, and its standards are entirely Master Kang's "own claim". As a result, Master Kang got deeper involved in the "water source gate" incident.

Just as one scandal was not settled, another emerged. On the 14th of this month, the "Daily Economic News" revealed again that the label on Master Kang's mineral water clearly marked the address of the Hangzhou factory, but the actual production site was in Shanghai. The Songjiang District Industry and Commerce Enforcement Department received consumer reports yesterday that Master Kang's mineral water was suspected of falsifying the place of origin, deceiving and misleading consumers. The enforcement department immediately inspected the Kangshifu factory located in the Songjiang Industrial Park, discovering that the labels on Master Kang's mineral water did not have a "place of origin," but only an "address." Regarding whether this constitutes misleading consumers, the industry and commerce department said further investigation is needed, "if it is found to indeed mislead consumers, we will certainly deal with it according to law."

Why did we fall for Master Kang's deception? Consumers of Master Kang's mineral water naturally resent its false advertising, but the question is why it was so easy to fall for Master Kang's trick, drinking tap water for so long thinking it was premium mineral water? The fundamental reason lies in the long-term insecurity people have about drinking water: tap water quality is barely acceptable, consumer health awareness has increased, at this time if businesses slightly "plan carefully," catering to consumer psychology by creating various concepts of healthy water, it is naturally very easy to lure consumers into the dazzling "era of certain types of water." And unconventional, expensive bottled water and barrelled water merely reinforce one concept: tap water is unreliable, lacks nutrition. Master Kang is simply adept at this too. Common people who drink expensive water for a long time forget about tap water, naturally easily accept without question the so-called "premium water" created by businesses using so-called high technology.

Master Kang's bottled water factory is located in heavily polluted large cities; where is there any premium water source? If it were transported from elsewhere, how could it be so cheap? Based on common knowledge alone, one should question their so-called "premium water source." Under the constant bombardment of businesses and public opinion, ordinary consumers' judgment on drinking water has weakened to what extent! Since Master Kang uses tap water, and it has become an "open secret" within the industry, why not directly sell premium tap water? Businessmen prioritize profit, we shouldn't overestimate their moral integrity. What is puzzling is how such an obvious act of consumer fraud, operated by a leading company in the industry for many years, managed to stay safe under everyone's scrutiny? Where are the guidance and regulations from authoritative departments? Is it because the businessmen's deceptive tactics are too sophisticated, or is it due to negligence by relevant departments? ("Daily Economic News")

How many "Master Kangs" are deceiving consumers with "premium"?

"Selecting premium water sources" is a vague yet clever advertising slogan. Even after being exposed as made from tap water, it's still hard to find major flaws because you can't prove that Hangzhou's tap water isn't a "premium water source." We can only say we've been deceived by shrewd businessmen. In the scientific community, there is controversy over artificially adding sulfates, chlorides, and other minerals to contaminated water to create "mineral water;" there is also insufficient scientific evidence regarding whether drinking this water poses health risks, making it essentially a commercial hype.

Looking at the domestic drinking water market, problems abound. Tap water failing to meet standards goes without saying, and the quality of bottled water is no better, with bacteria and heavy metal levels exceeding limits unprecedentedly. Bottled water is also in chaos. Besides traditional "mineral water," "mineral water," "space water," "active water," "ion water"... the market is a mix of good and bad, uneven quality, and the public can only follow along—serving as guinea pigs for drinking water manufacturers!

It can be said that the chaos in the market is the result of profit-driven motives and regulatory failure, compounded by poor public service quality. The pollution of drinking water sources has caused serious water quality shortages in China, making the public reluctant to drink tap water, thus giving rise to a massive drinking water market, providing a stage for profiteering businessmen. Coupled with rough standards related to drinking water, weak regulatory awareness, insufficient strength, and outdated methods, it has led to the disorder in the drinking water market. To rectify the chaos in the drinking water market, besides conventional measures such as correcting standards, regulating industry competition, and strengthening supervision, more importantly, efforts should be made to vigorously manage pollution, protect water sources, provide qualified tap water to the public, and ensure the most basic drinking needs of the public. ("Guangzhou Daily")

Experts question whether mineral water exceeds heavy metal standards

Zhao Feihong, technical director of the Health Drinking Water Professional Committee of the Chinese Medical Promotion Association, pointed out that the key to the quality of mineral water products lies in the types and quantities of added minerals, and currently, there is no unified national standard. Moreover, this type of water has some safety hazards, such as: different manufacturers and different products contain different types and quantities of minerals, causing the quality of these products to vary greatly; mineralizing solutions are all strongly acidic, generally with a pH below 3, so mineral water is also slightly acidic; most products have not undergone biological safety testing; the types of mineral additives that can be used for mineral water are limited, mainly concentrated in sulfates and chlorides. If the amount added is excessive, it will cause the content of chlorides and sulfates in the water to exceed national standards; some mineral solutions are extracted from minerals, which often contain some heavy metals. For example, some McFarland stones contain lead, arsenic, and other harmful heavy metals. If improperly selected, it is easy to cause heavy metals to exceed national standards. ("Beijing Science and Technology Newspaper")

Artificially synthesized mineral water not explained, misleading consumers

Lawyer Qiu Baochang from Beijing Huia Law Firm believes that ordinary consumers cannot distinguish between "mineral water for drinking" and "natural mineral water," meaning the former is artificially synthesized while the latter is naturally formed.

From the perspective of product benefits to the human body, "natural mineral water" is obviously superior to "mineral water for drinking."

"Master Kang Drinking Mineral Water" does not specify that its product is artificially synthesized mineral water. Consumers would then find it difficult to understand the nature and composition of the product from its packaging, leading to confusion, believing that this mineral water is natural and beneficial to the human body. Therefore, the product's promotion is suspected of infringing on consumers' right to know and misleading consumers. ("Legal Evening News")

How many Master Kangs are implicated in the tap water scandal?

Had it not been for netizens posting online, perhaps Master Kang's trick of passing off tap water as mineral water would not have been disclosed to the public, but if it weren't for insiders exposing the industry's unwritten rule of passing off tap water as mineral water, it would be even more shocking. After the truth came to light, condemnation was endless.

To be fair, in the capital era, companies occasionally resort to unethical practices to gain higher profits, which doesn't surprise people. What truly surprises consumers is why counterfeiting has become an unwritten rule in the drinking water industry? How long has this unwritten rule existed? After the relevant regulatory authorities neglected their duties, shouldn't they come up with some truly effective punitive measures?

As one of the consumers of drinking water, I look forward to the relevant departments conducting a thorough industry-wide investigation, revealing exactly how many companies are trapped in the "tap water scandal" and cannot extricate themselves. Simultaneously, a thorough investigation of the relevant regulatory authorities should also be carried out to uncover the deep-seated reasons why the "tap water scandal" has spread. The "tap water scandal" requires a clear and transparent thorough investigation result, and more importantly, relevant departments need to come up with a convincing punishment result. www.51h2o.com
