Zhejiang University, not under the direct administration of the Ministry of Education, is an ordinary key university that was one of the first to enter the national "211 Project" and "985 Project". The predecessor of Zhejiang University was established in 1897 as a college, making it one of the earliest modern higher learning institutions founded by Chinese people. In late 1952, during the nationwide adjustment of colleges and universities, it was split into several specialized schools, with some departments being merged into other universities. In 1998, Zhejiang University reunited with Hangzhou University, Zhejiang Industrial University, and Zhejiang Medical University to form the new Zhejiang University. After more than a hundred years of construction and development, the university has become a research-oriented, comprehensive university with solid foundations, strong capabilities, distinct characteristics, domestic first-class standards, and significant international influence.

The university is located in Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province, a famous historical and cultural city and world-renowned tourist destination. To the south lies the Suzhou-Shanghai area, to the west Ningbo port, and to the north links to Fujian-Guangdong, making it an important city in the Yangtze River Delta region of China's southeastern coast. The university has five campuses: Zijingang, Yuhang, Xixi, Huajiachi, and Zhijiang, covering an area of 5.18 square kilometers, distributed across different locations in Hangzhou. Surrounded by mountains and water, the campus environment is serene, lush with flowers and trees, green grass like a carpet, and picturesque scenery complementing the beauty of West Lake, making it an ideal place for reading and studying.

During its long history of operation, Zhejiang University has cultivated a large number of outstanding talents with rigorous academic discipline and created abundant results with a persistent spirit of innovation, gaining fame both domestically and internationally. It was praised by the renowned British scholar Joseph Needham as the "Eastern Cambridge." Among the alumni elected as academicians of the Chinese Academy of Sciences and the Chinese Academy of Engineering, there are nearly 160 individuals, including 90 who studied at Zhejiang University.

Zhejiang University's disciplines cover eleven major categories: philosophy, economics, law, education, literature, history, science, engineering, agriculture, medicine, and management. The university currently offers 112 undergraduate majors, 43 doctoral programs, and has authorization for doctoral degrees in 41 primary disciplines, master's degrees in 242 secondary disciplines, and master's degrees in 317 secondary disciplines. Additionally, there are professional master’s degree authorization points in clinical medicine, law (JM), business administration (MBA, EMBA), public administration (MPA), education, sports, industrial design, landscape architecture, veterinary medicine, public health, engineering, architecture, clinical medicine, stomatology, and Chinese international education. The university has 14 primary discipline national key disciplines and 21 secondary discipline national key disciplines. There are 14 national key laboratories (specialty), 5 national engineering (technology) research centers, and 3 national humanities and social sciences key research bases. There are also 7 national basic scientific research and teaching talent cultivation bases, 4 national teaching bases for fundamental courses in engineering, 3 national and local industry talent cultivation bases, 1 national cultural quality education base for undergraduates, and 1 national drawing teaching and research base.

【Characteristics of Running the School and Employment Situation】

1. Scientific Educational Model: Through long-term practical research on society and enterprises, using societal demand as guidance, determine teaching methods and content, specifically establish industry-specific majors with good employment prospects, achieving complementary advantages between higher education and vocational education. Not only does it emphasize students' ideological quality and theoretical education but also highlights vocational skills education, helping students obtain authoritative national qualification certificates, providing double insurance for student employment, and increasing employment weight.

2. Strong Faculty Strength: All courses are taught by backbone teachers of the subject, strictly implementing the system of class mentors responsible for the entire process, subject teachers focusing on students, and divisional leaders taking overall responsibility, with full-time counselors managing and guiding.

3. Excellent Learning and Living Environment: All freshmen study, live, use computers, conduct experiments, and read on-campus, allowing students to fully enjoy the campus life environment and learning atmosphere of a key university.

4. Scientific Academic Management: Academic management is personally handled by experienced educational administrators, ensuring a first-class living environment and good learning atmosphere for students.

5. Employment: A special employment guidance office is established to handle employment training, guidance, recommendation, and placement for students, as well as employment tracking services. Students can sign employment intention agreements upon enrollment, and after obtaining their undergraduate diploma and advanced vocational certificate, they can be uniformly placed in jobs by the school. Freshmen of the 2009 employment skills class sign a "Recommended Employment Agreement" upon entering school, following the school's unified arrangements for study and training. In the fourth year, 100% employment recommendation is guaranteed, and students receive paid internships.
