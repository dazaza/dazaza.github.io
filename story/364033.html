How men keep mind and body in shape

by rfwerferwf on 2010-09-08 17:14:26

Maintaining physical and mental health is a beautiful aspiration for everyone. As we age, both our physical and mental states undergo constant changes and are frequently affected by various diseases. So, how can men maintain their physical and mental health and find their own "health manual"?

Emphasize Mental Health

Regarding men's health issues, physical and mental health are closely related. If men want to maintain their overall well-being, the first step is overcoming psychological problems. It is an undeniable fact that men often live under great stress, especially middle-aged men who must shoulder heavy responsibilities at work as well as the burdens of supporting elderly family members and raising and educating their children, making them one of the groups with the heaviest psychological loads. Psychologists state that men who are constantly under high tension and immense mental pressure almost always exhibit varying degrees of psychological disorder symptoms such as lethargy, irritability, unwillingness to trust others, and reluctance to communicate. Therefore, psychologists remind men who experience these symptoms to actively adjust their mindset, relieve stress in a timely manner, and escape from various psychological crises.

Escape Common Health Misconceptions

Traditionally, when men fall ill, they often tough it out without informing their families or going to the hospital. Especially when dealing with reproductive system diseases, due to concerns about "saving face," they dare not visit professional and legitimate hospitals but instead secretly seek treatment at small clinics or self-prescribe medications, thinking that taking some medicine will get them through it. This approach often leads to delayed treatment, wasting time and money, and causing greater harm to their health. Doctors warn that if there is indeed an issue detected, one should go to a specialized hospital for corresponding professional examinations, and doctors will guide you on taking proper medications based on your condition. Ignoring these signs can turn minor ailments into major ones, missing the best early treatment window.

Annual Physical Examination

Men should never "tough it out" when feeling unwell. Experts remind that during annual check-ups, men should not overlook key areas and tests: blood pressure and cholesterol checks, hepatitis B surface antigen/antibody tests or hepatitis C antibody tests, protein metabolism assessments, abdominal ultrasounds, prostate exams, testicular exams, colon exams, etc.

Finally, experts emphasize that men experiencing psychological issues should not suppress them internally, as accumulated negative emotions may lead to mental disorders. For men under heavy work pressure and significant mental strain, it is appropriate to seek psychological counseling and assistance from psychiatrists to maintain psychological balance and preserve overall health.
