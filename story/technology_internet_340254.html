[Military Pictures] Members of the "Snow Leopard" Commando Unit implemented a strict blockade at the incident site. On June 17, 2009, the last subject of China’s "Great Wall No. 6" national anti-terrorism exercise - "Handling Chemical Plant Explosion Terrorist Attack" was held in Zhuozhou, Hebei Province. The exercise simulated a chemical plant being attacked by "terrorists", with factory workers trapped and highly toxic chemical substance storage tanks damaged by explosions. Hebei Province activated all levels of anti-terrorism command posts to quickly eliminate the "terrorists", extinguish fires, defuse bombs, treat and handle casualties, and properly deal with toxic chemicals at the scene. At Hebei's request, the state urgently coordinated national anti-terrorism emergency professional response forces and members of the national anti-terrorism expert advisory group to go to the scene for support and handling. Through the exercise, it effectively tested the on-site handling and rescue work of chemical explosion attacks, the cross-regional mobilization mechanism of national anti-terrorism emergency response forces, emergency mobility and actual handling capabilities, as well as the organization of public emergency evacuation and protection capabilities and levels. Since June 9, the "Great Wall No. 6" national anti-terrorism exercise has been held successively in regions around Beijing such as Inner Mongolia, Shanxi, and Hebei.

The naming ceremony for the "Snow Leopard" Commando Unit and the swearing-in rally for the Sino-Russian "Cooperation-2007" joint anti-terrorism exercise were held on August 29, 2007. Wu Shuangzhan, commander of the Armed Police Force, presented the flag and emblem of the "Snow Leopard Commando Unit" to a special operations battalion of the Beijing Corps. Snow leopards have sharp senses, are alert, agile, good climbers and jumpers, and primarily use ambush-style hunting techniques. They are known as the "God of the Snow Mountains." Naming the special operations battalion after the "Snow Leopard" symbolizes loyalty, agility, tenacity, fearlessness, and determination to win, summarizing the noble qualities, combat capabilities, fighting spirit, and firm belief that special operations soldiers should possess. The establishment of the "Snow Leopard Commando Unit," a counter-terrorism special force based in Beijing and serving nationwide, marks a new phase in the construction of the Armed Police Force's counter-terrorism units.

Selection Criteria for "Snow Leopard Commando Unit" Members: The selection criteria for "Snow Leopard Commando Unit" members are very strict: height must be over 1.75 meters, but can be reduced to 1.72 meters if they possess special skills; education level must be high school or above; must have served in the Armed Police Force for more than one year. After candidates meet the basic conditions, they qualify to enter the nationwide "preliminary selection." Those who stand out in the "preliminary selection" are formed into a reserve team and participate in a one-year intensive training session. During the training, various assessments lead to successive eliminations, and the winners form the "combat team." However, the combat team needs to undergo a three-month probation period before officially becoming members of the "Snow Leopard Commando Unit." During this process, the elimination rate is as high as 40%-50%.

A Day in the Life of "Snow Leopard Commando Unit" Members: Rising at 5:30 am for morning exercises, breakfast at 7:00, training starts at 8:00, lunch at 12:00, continuing training after a 1 to 2-hour nap, dinner at 7:00 pm, study at 8:00 pm, night training at 9:00 pm, and sleep at 11:00 pm. Every Saturday, bedtime is at 11:30 pm—this is the daily schedule of the "Snow Leopards." The commandos never have weekends off. All 365 days of the year, they remain in a state of readiness. What left the deepest impression on the "Snow Leopard" members was undoubtedly the "Devil Week" training. During this period, each member carried a standard load of 35 kilograms and marched 241 kilometers on foot; they rested for only three and a half hours per day, ate 2 ounces of rice and a bag of pickled vegetables, while also completing 14 training subjects including climbing, shooting, and obstacle courses.

Equipment for the "Snow Leopard Commando Unit": The "Snow Leopard Commando Unit" is equipped with vehicles, weapons, non-lethal weapons and police equipment, engineers, chemical defense, delta-wing aircraft, etc., mainly used for rapid deployment of troops, reconnaissance, blockades, assaults, rescue, and medical aid missions. The weapons and equipment provided to the "Snow Leopard Commando Unit" are at a relatively advanced level among domestic security forces worldwide, especially with highly digitized reconnaissance equipment. (Source: People's Armed Police Newspaper, Ding Zuojun, Kang Jian)
