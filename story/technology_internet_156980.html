Online marketing - who are you? - Original by Liu Yuhan

by yuhanwangluo on 2009-06-06 16:13:51

From the perspective of online marketing, how valuable is your corporate brand?

Some companies, because they have been involved in the internet for a long time, think they understand online marketing very well and even ignore some good suggestions. Some even consider themselves experts. Of course, this refers to those who are not too humble. They may have read a lot of theoretical articles about online marketing, but there are few practical ones that they can use effectively. Perhaps some people really understand some basic internet knowledge, and naturally, they will know more after seeing a lot. However, when it comes to application, they are still in a state of confusion because no one has corrected them, and they don't realize that they are in a misunderstanding. Today, I will simply talk about my understanding of online marketing and common problems in general enterprises. Everyone should take a careful look and see if you belong to this group. All the views below are based on Yu Han's personal experience in enterprise promotion work in the past, shared with everyone, hoping to contribute a little to the development of online marketing. Please do not throw bricks if there are any offensive words.

1. The company was very fashionable and built a website N years ago, costing over ten thousand yuan, which was quite proud at that time. It thought it could make great achievements in the field of e-commerce from then on, but after a period of time, the effect was not ideal. In fact, maybe there are many problems with your website. Let me tell you:

1) Unclear website positioning: Is it for sales or for branding? The purpose of building a website is unclear.

2) Small information capacity of the website, static pages, unable to update information independently.

3) The homepage of the website is showy but lacks substance. Flash entry not only affects speed but also is unfriendly to search engines.

4) Unreasonable planning of website columns, messy and complicated structure, blindly pursuing personality uniqueness "stimulation".

5) Not much information on the website, lots of pictures, big and small, colorful, just "good-looking"!

6) Website information has not been updated for N years, always an "old face", making people not want to look again.

7) Unclear functions of the website, not knowing whether it serves the leadership or sales.

8) Lack of interactivity on the website, unable to communicate online, leave messages, or even lack a corporate email address.

9) No central idea on the website, no selling points for products, customers cannot see characteristics, website content is hollow.

10) Never considering promotion for the website, just printing it on business cards, isn't it just for looking good?

Summary: A website should have simple page structures, clear logic, convenient browsing, comprehensive and rich information, highlight corporate characteristics and product selling points, avoid overly luxurious pictures, fit reality, provide abundant information, focus on the center, be flexible, reflect service awareness, and be like your office. Just as you would treat guests in person, you should do the same online, thoroughly and meticulously.

2. Lack of understanding of search engine marketing leads to insufficient attention to search engines. This problem mainly manifests in three aspects:

1) Issues with the design of the corporate website, such as Flash imports or excessive pictures on the homepage without text updates, preventing search engines from logging.

2) Corporate websites do not pay attention to soft promotions on search engines, only knowing to spend money on technical promotions.

3) Corporate websites do not actively spread information, failing to effectively promote and release information.

To put it simply, if your company built a website but never did Baidu or Google promotions and encounters the above situation, then when users log into the search engine and enter the name of your company or product, you will have very few chances to appear in the search results. If you do appear, it will be several pages later. Search engines like Baidu mainly collect information from websites through intelligent capture after website information updates. When users search for information, the results are fed back to the search results and displayed before the users. If users do not know the name of your company, I think you will not have any chance to appear before them.

For customers who have done Baidu or Google promotions, when users search for the name of your company or product, even if they do not know what you do, they can still find you as long as you submit relevant keywords. If the range is wide enough, you can display your corporate website in front of users. However, you should know that all the information displayed is extracted by technology in a fixed pattern framework. True customers need more than just the information you provide; they need vivid displays rather than lifeless advertisements. Do you understand?

3. Not knowing how to use network resources for information dissemination, attraction, guidance, and stimulation of customer needs

Today's customers focus on convenience, utility, health, safety, and cost-effectiveness in their information lives. Let me give you an example by telling you a marketing story. Liu Yuhan is a pretty girl, but she is a bit overweight, 1.68 meters tall, 118 pounds, and has always wanted to lose weight. But she doesn't know which weight loss medicine is the best. She saw a lot of advertisements from various companies online, got confused, and didn't know which one to choose. So she started searching for information online and accidentally came across an article, a girl's weight loss diary, written on Sina Blog. The content was: "Recently, I lost 20 pounds of fat, and I finally succeeded. I have to thank 'a certain weight loss fruit.' Although taking weight loss pills is harmful to the body, for beauty, I can only do this. Besides, a friend introduced me to this weight loss pill, which is purely plant-based, with minimal side effects, made from aloe vera. I didn't feel any side effects, and instead, my skin became whiter, tenderer, and tighter. Hehe, summer is coming, and I can wear beautiful skirts. I'm so happy!... After reading this, I quickly acted and bought this weight loss pill.

The story is over. Can you see the implication? What does this girl's short diary tell us, and why did I decide to buy this weight loss pill so quickly? I won't keep you guessing anymore; I'll tell you directly.

Through this little story, we can analyze two points:

1) A girl wants to lose weight but doesn't know which medicine to choose. She wants to find the most suitable one through the internet but has many choices and doesn't know which one to pick. We can interpret the previous text as the possible state of potential customers in many companies. So, what is your ace when facing such customers? Will you let customers encounter a blog diary by coincidence? Will it bring you opportunities? Have you prepared such opportunities for yourself?

2) The blog diary mentions a few key points: 20 pounds, harm from taking weight loss pills, pure plant-based, made from aloe vera, white tender skin. These few words are full of temptation. They include the selling points of the product and the sales strategy.

20 pounds is a report on the effectiveness of the product. Saying that taking weight loss pills is harmful is called being honest, not exaggerating or deceiving consumers. The foundation of a company's survival lies in the fact that anyone knows that taking weight loss pills must have side effects and are bad for the body. By proactively telling customers, the company lowers its own position, making customers feel closer and not hostile, presenting the correct attitude, and bridging the gap with customers. Then it reveals its first ace: pure plant-based, aloe vera. Then it adds another point: not only does it have no obvious side effects, but it also beautifies the skin because it is made from pure plant-based aloe vera. For some companies, this may seem simple, and some people might think the principle is easy and that they can do it well... I won't say more here; I'll leave some room for reflection.

I personally think this little story is not simple. It contains techniques of online marketing, principles of corporate survival, sales strategies, market analysis, and more. Of course, everyone's level of understanding is different. Some people may see more, while others may see nothing. What do you think???

Let me introduce you to a few free resources mentioned earlier: blogs, podcasts, RSS, forums, and bookmarks are all instant interactive communication tools. In the eyes of most people, they don't understand and even look down on them. Alas! Thinking about these issues with companies, I really feel sorry for them. They would rather spend a high price to buy an advertisement board at a prominent location at the intersection than utilize simpler to produce, easier to maintain, and wider-ranging "online billboards." Nowadays, there are actually many free online promotion resources, it's just up to you to apply them appropriately. Can you master them all well? Can you integrate with netizens? Are you familiar with netizens' search habits? Do you understand their interests? Can you ensure the most effective communication?

4. Not considering cost-effectiveness, blindly investing, foolishly throwing large sums of money at various media, fantasizing that enough money will bring results, being overly eager for success, and not knowing how to gradually build a good reputation online.

1) Actually, if you look at the physical market, this principle is not difficult to understand. Which famous brand suddenly appeared overnight? Coca-Cola, Adidas, including China's Haier, none of them gained today's influence without years of accumulation. On the Internet, the convenience and speed of information communication are indeed unmatched by other channels. Although it can create overnight celebrities like Sister Furong and Fairy Sister Tian in almost no time, for corporate promotion, we should not have such fantasies. China's business philosophy, recognized worldwide, is based on integrity. Relying on false and exaggerated hype may bring a bit of short-term profit, but for companies seeking long-term stable development, it is undoubtedly drinking poison to quench thirst, absolutely unacceptable.

2) Many companies pay a lot of attention to traditional media advertising, but they overlook one point: nowadays, more people are concentrated on the Internet. The number of newspaper, TV, and magazine readers is far less than that of the Internet, especially among young people and middle-aged groups. These two groups have the highest consumption ability. More and more people browse, search, compare, and refer through Internet search engines to get familiar with a product, a company, a piece of news, an event, etc. A company's information coverage on the Internet directly affects them. Whether the public opinion of the company is good or bad plays a leading role in their consumption because they need a good direction and a good guide. There is no actual commander on the Internet, and what is referred to here is an attractive spread of information, leading and guiding, which can be understood as word-of-mouth. Doing it well is called word-of-mouth marketing. It is the same principle as offline salespeople visiting door-to-door to promote their products and establish the image of the company.

3) Online marketing is important, and how to flexibly apply it on the Internet requires you to carefully plan and operate, just like developing customers, following up with customers, and closing deals in traditional markets. Both online and offline should be fully utilized, telling your customers about your strengths and advantages, forming a chain of customers, and then providing good services, making your customers become your promoters. You should know that consumers' word-of-mouth is the most effective advertisement!

After saying so much, I don't know if it has been helpful to everyone. Yuhan has been engaged in online promotion work since 2003 and is now in the stage of self-entrepreneurship. From cultivating others' fields to opening one's own fertile soil, I deeply understand the principles of corporate survival and development. Yuhan always does one thing: first, be a shopping guide for my customers, then cultivate friendship, and finally become a partner. I respect them, and they will respect me and recognize me. How do you expect your customers to treat you? Treat them the same way first. People are mirrors; how you treat them, they will treat you. Survival in life is actually a sales process with skills and strategies. Getting others' recognition, liking, help, and praise means you have successfully sold yourself. If you make people dislike, fear, alienate, or feel hostility, it means your failure in selling yourself because you don't know how to win others' trust and liking and cannot gain everyone's recognition. Doing business is the same principle. The winners in the market are those who seriously analyze market needs, identify opportunities, and create various products of value beyond competitors for target customers. Make your company more humane and your service more intimate for customers. First, let us present the best products and services to our customers, sincerely care about their feelings, and wholeheartedly serve them. Then, customers will definitely buy your products, give you money, and even say thank you!
