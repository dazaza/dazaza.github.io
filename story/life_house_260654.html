Re-discussion on Four Major Issues of Winter Renovation

by longersing on 2009-11-17 15:32:22

Winter decoration inevitably encounters some headache-inducing problems, such as wooden products being prone to dryness and cracking easily. When modifying the radiator, it may still be functioning properly. Replacing exterior windows and the final acceptance process are also easy to go wrong. These issues are all related to our mood during the New Year, so it is necessary to have a good discussion.

1. Wooden Products Drying Easily and Cracking

Many people don't know that when using artificial boards in winter decoration, especially veneer plywood, they are at their driest time. Therefore, its surface is very prone to cracking after production. This problem may not be noticeable at first, but if the painter's paint sealing work does not keep up, it can easily cause the surface veneer of the plywood to crack, which is neither aesthetically pleasing nor affects its lifespan. Actually, this issue is quite easy to solve: Purchased materials should not be stored outdoors; after materials are transported to the construction site, they should not be placed near vents or radiators to avoid excessive drying speed; after the carpentry work is completed, workers should quickly follow up with paint sealing work to prevent moisture evaporation. This will play a good role in protecting the wooden products later on.

2. Precautions for Radiator Modification

People are increasingly paying attention to indoor heating facilities, especially the selection of radiators (heating). Cast-iron radiators are unsatisfactory in both design and heating effect. Therefore, many people replace radiators during renovation.

Currently, the water pressure inside residential radiators is very high. If the hot water bursts through the radiator or pipe fittings, the water pressure is strong enough to knock down an adult standing in front of it. Moreover, the construction companies or manufacturers replacing the radiators do not have equipment to test the radiator's pressure resistance. Replacing radiators relies entirely on the experience of the construction personnel, but this experience is not completely reliable. Therefore, I warn consumers who plan to replace radiators not to do so arbitrarily. If replacement is necessary, it is best to find workers from the property management company, as they are more experienced and can help resolve any issues that arise promptly.

3. Replacing Exterior Windows Requires Caution

Replacing ordinary steel windows or aluminum alloy windows with plastic-steel windows also requires attention to some issues. First, replacing exterior windows must be firmly fixed. The wind and sand in northern winters are very strong. Some workers installing plastic-steel windows to save effort do not securely fix the base of the plastic-steel window. This can cause the originally square window to deform due to the wind (relatively speaking) or the vertical window to tilt. It might become increasingly difficult to open and close the window, or rainwater might flow into the room during the rainy season. Second, the cement mortar around the exterior window must be sealed tightly. There is an old saying: "A pinhole-sized hole can let in a lot of wind." We should block any leaks tightly, otherwise, during the coldest part of winter, we may find cold air blowing indoors even though the heating is working well. Third, the sealant strips on plastic-steel windows should not be sealed too tightly. Many substances experience thermal expansion and contraction, and the rubber sealant strips used on plastic-steel windows are no exception. They contract to a certain extent during installation in autumn and winter, and expand when heated during the hot summer.

4. Acceptance Time Should Be During the Day

When winter decoration reaches the acceptance stage, we encounter a practical problem: the indoor lighting starts to dim, and the natural lighting in the afternoon near dusk becomes very poor. It is best to conduct the acceptance during the most naturally lit time of day, at noon. For example, the wall tiles used in the kitchen, if paired with regular tiles and decorative tiles or patterned tiles, under natural light, we can see that the construction quality is excellent. However, under non-natural light, due to the different reflective properties of the wall tiles, good construction quality might be misinterpreted by the inspector as having quality issues.
