Chapter One: The Birth of the True Dragon I will absolutely not say that I am the best in the world, but I also will absolutely not admit that I am the second. ——Bruce Lee This chapter: Jun was born, different from ordinary people since young, his father was very happy; he liked to think deeply, had an extreme color, could not speak at the age of two, which made his father very anxious and seek help. ——from "The Bruce Lee Story" On November 27, 1940, in Peace Hospital in San Francisco, USA, with a thunderous cry, a male infant who would surely make a great impact in the future came into the world. "Congratulations Mr. Li, it's a boy!" A young and pretty nurse informed Li Haiquan who had been waiting outside all night. Strangely enough, since early this morning, the little guy wanted to come out, but wouldn't, almost causing a difficult delivery, making Li Haiquan worry all night. This little fellow cried so loudly, he will definitely make a great impact in the future, Li Haiquan thought happily as he went inside after entering the room, holding his still wailing baby son, and gently said to his wife beside him: "Madam, you've worked hard!" She had been giving birth all night, almost losing her life, Mrs. Li was pale but still couldn't hide her natural beauty, she smiled faintly at her husband and said: "Let me see our son!" Although Li Haiquan loved his son dearly, he handed him over to his wife as instructed, being extremely careful, afraid that one slip would cause the child to fall on the ground. Mrs. Li extended her white and beautiful jade-like hand, gently patting her son, her eyes full of tenderness, she asked her husband: "Have you decided on a name for our son?" Li Haiquan felt somewhat jealous. You little rascal are truly fortunate, Ai Yu isn't as gentle with me as she is with you. He chuckled and said: "Our son made a great impact right after his birth, he will surely shock San Francisco in the future, let's give him the name Zhenfan!" How could Li father have imagined that the baby in his hands would not only shake San Francisco in the future, but also become famous worldwide, becoming the most familiar Asian person to Europeans, one of the seven top martial artists in the world, etc. "What are you laughing about?" Mrs. Li gave her husband a scolding look, his eyebrows twitched, she was sure he was thinking up some mischief. "Nothing, I finally have a son, I'm happy!" Li Haiquan said as he looked towards his son who was still crying loudly, and said: "Dear son, you now have a name, from now on you will be called Li Zhenfan, how does that sound? Do you like it?" Mrs. Li giggled: "A newborn child, how can they understand what you're saying." Li Haiquan said: "That's not necessarily true, Zhenfan must be just as smart as his father, his name will spread far and wide..." Before he finished speaking, Li Zhenfan's cries abruptly stopped. The couple looked at their son, only to see this little guy open his eyes, squinting through a slit, looking at Mrs. Li and Li Haiquan, glancing at one then the other, and his face showing disbelief, then he started crying loudly again. Mrs. Li tenderly kissed her son's forehead, couldn't help but laugh again. "You're absolutely right, our son is different from others even at birth, he has great spirit." After returning home, Li Haiquan still couldn't bear to put down his precious son. Hearing that Mrs. Li had given birth to a son, the maids and servants also curiously gathered around, the housekeeper arranged for firecrackers to celebrate, even though they were away from home, Chinese customs were still indispensable. Everyone saw Li Zhenfan opening his little eyes, looking around, his eyebrows as beautiful as his mother's, not yet fully grown, but slightly furrowed with a hint of melancholy, and they all laughed: "Young master knows how to frown, how adorable." At this moment, the door was pushed open a crack, and a small head peeked in. Li Haiquan turned back and laughed: "Ying'er, come in and take a look at your younger brother." As soon as the door opened, a girl of about five or six years old darted in. She was exquisitely carved, resembling her mother's features, a perfect beauty in the making. This girl was Li Haiquan's eldest daughter, but she wasn't his biological child, she was the child of Mrs. Li's sister, who had died during childbirth. Her husband became mentally unstable due to grief, so he entrusted the child to his sister-in-law to raise. Mrs. Li loved her greatly, treating her as her own. Upon hearing this, Ying'er approached eagerly, watching Li Zhenfan in Mrs. Li's arms with great interest. "Dad, my younger brother is so small!" Ying'er said, opening her beautiful eyes wide, looking at Li Zhenfan who was smaller than a kitten. The nearby nanny laughed and said: "When you were born, you weren't even as big as your younger brother now."
