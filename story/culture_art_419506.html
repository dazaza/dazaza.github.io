Learn from the Harvard Spirit and Achieve a Successful Life.

by dianxin8 on 2011-04-21 21:23:28

Harvard is the symbol of the highest intelligence and the best academic institution. The name itself is a gift to humanity, an ideal represented by what Harvard offers. ks5u College Entrance Examination Resource Network tells us that learning the Harvard spirit will help you break through the barriers of exams and successfully enter the school of your dreams.

When we went to Harvard University at night, we were surprised to see that the entire campus was lit up, making it a city that never sleeps. The dining hall, library, lecture halls, and many students were all studying. A strong atmosphere of learning permeated everything. At Harvard, whether day or night, students are always busy. At that time, I realized that in America, schools like Harvard place immense pressure on their students. At Harvard, it's common to see people sleeping anywhere, even on the benches in the cafeteria. People coming and going for meals don't find this strange because they know these individuals are very tired. They sleep because they understand the exhaustion of those around them. What we see most at Harvard are students eating bread laced with stimulants while studying. The Harvard interview process is one of the deepest pains experienced by Harvard students during their studies, but evidently, they find it quite amusing. What drives Harvard students to endure such hardship? My experience suggests that it's their intense interest in their respective fields. This is the burning heart of Harvard students, preparing to shoulder significant responsibilities in the future. From these students, you can sense that living here provides them with inspiration.

ks5u College Entrance Examination Resource Network writer says: At Harvard, a girl told me that each student at Harvard chooses 4 to 8 courses per semester, needing to complete at least 32 courses over four years and pass exams to graduate. Normally, the school requires students to finish core courses within the first two years before starting specialized training in the third year. Only the best talents among students can read more than 32 books, and regular students often feel dizzy trying to keep up with the workload. In class, teachers forbid speaking if you don't understand the material and assign heavy reading loads for the next lesson, which makes completing assignments challenging.

Doctoral students at Harvard can devour thick books, finishing one every three days, each hundreds of pages long, and then write detailed reports about them. Boston’s Harvard Bridge was once overseen by Dr. Zhang, the former head of Harvard University's Anthropology Department, who spent years without stepping onto the Boston deck.

Whether it be Harvard students or professors, glory isn’t achieved overnight; it’s a testament to hard work. How does one make others willing to follow, and who ultimately might have potential?

Do you know what Harvard lacks in China?

As a world-class university, Harvard has cultivated many celebrities, including 33 Nobel laureates, U.S. Presidents, and elite professionals in various industries. What makes Harvard the cradle of elites? What kind of spirit and philosophy do Harvard students uphold? These questions attract ks5u College Entrance Examination Resource Network to determine the answers.

Harvard students and teachers often say that if you want to enter any company at any time, or be in any situation where you're valued and worth evaluating, then you are a Harvard student, adhering to the widespread Harvard motto: Time is precious, so use it wisely amidst the busyness of autumn harvests, study, study, and study again.

A person's time and energy are limited, so time must be carefully utilized, not wasted entirely on napping.

At Harvard, despite the high-pressure environment and scholarships, students aren’t encouraged to study all the time. They believe that science should be approached methodically, and competitions shouldn’t be taken lightly. Harvard students also mention that life at Harvard is better than formal studies. Moreover, Harvard realizes that extracurricular activities won’t undermine educational duties but support them. Therefore, they advocate playing like a gentleman.

At Harvard, students study urgently but also participate in various artistic activities such as concerts, theatrical performances, dance shows, and art exhibitions. Every year, Harvard holds an arts festival to provide students with active leisure activities. With a strong cultural foundation, these activities not only allow students to receive artistic education and inspiration but also enhance their cultural and aesthetic abilities.

Harvard's philosophy demands that in times of urgency in study and work, one can completely forget temporary matters to focus and relax. In reality, dedicating oneself during free time to recover mentally and physically will be crucial for the next phase of infinite strength. Thus, on the path forward, one not only enjoys work but also learns to relax.

Drooling now will become tears tomorrow

There cannot be success and stability when choices at either end are abandoned. As Harvard reminds us: drooling now will become tears tomorrow. Things not done happily today will affect tomorrow.

Investing in the future aligns with reality

As a world-class university, Harvard emphasizes the necessity of long-term investment for the future. Investing in the future means establishing good directions early in life by setting early life goals. Without goals, there can be no talk of growth, let alone success. Some people succeed because most already know what they want, while others may be unclear or uncertain. Quickly observing and understanding life goals is essential for achieving success in life.
