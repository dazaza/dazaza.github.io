Lao Liu was my middle school classmate. After graduation, we were far apart and rarely in touch. A class reunion in 2009 made us meet again. Apart from introducing our situations of recent years, we unexpectedly found out that we were both "futures people". Due to our passion for futures, we were fully engaged in our conversation.

I learned from our conversation that he opened an account at a local futures company branch for trading. Because of the high handling fee, it was difficult to make money from intra-day trading. Over several months, his principal kept shrinking. However, after deducting the high handling fee, his overall intra-day trading was still profitable.

Although I later invited him to open an account with our company, due to his small capital amount and the company's strict control over handling fees, his trading cost didn't decrease much eventually. On the other hand, the introduction of futures intermediaries clearly moved him more. After thorough understanding and preparation, he decided to engage in intermediary business and encouraged his newly-wed wife Xiaoyun to develop customers with him.

After confirming the matters, they started registering a company, selecting office locations. Meanwhile, I also actively cooperated with their business development, preparing opening account contracts, company introductions, product manuals, and necessary risk warnings. In the following two months, they successfully developed more than 20 customers, with 8 effective trading customers. However, as time went by, various problems kept emerging. Due to the lack of systematic futures knowledge, insufficient manpower, and regional limitations, the work of developing and maintaining customers could not continue. Reality told him that futures is an industry with a very high degree of professionalism. After careful consideration, the couple decided to return to their old trade and resume the spring processing business.

Lao Liu's family has always been in the spring business. After their division in 2008, he and his younger brother respectively opened a spring processing enterprise. Due to insufficient orders, his own spring factory was basically semi-closed, which gave him time to engage in futures trading. After one failed attempt at futures development business, he became more determined to engage in areas where he excelled. Thus, using some methods from his previous futures development business, such as making phone calls, personal visits, and sending materials, he soon found a breakthrough in the development of spring processing business. Similarly, all these customer development works fell on Xiaoyun, while Lao Liu mainly focused on production. After 2010, their processing plant began to operate normally. During this period, I also visited them. Although conditions were tough, they felt that this approach was more solid, and at least they had a clear mind. After two years of hard work in 2010 and 2011, their spring processing plant initially took shape. The number of machines increased from a few to more than ten, and they plan to increase it to around 20 in the next two years, reaching the scale of a medium-sized processing enterprise.

Although Lao Liu's current work has little connection with futures, he still occasionally pays attention to futures prices, especially rebar, because the raw material for springs is wire rod, which has a large price linkage with rebar. As Lao Liu put it: "After venturing into futures, I realized that controlling raw material prices is an indispensable part of the company's future development. Although we currently do not participate in futures trading, using the price discovery function of futures to guide the daily operation of the enterprise is also very necessary."
