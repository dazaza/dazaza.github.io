Rapid Development: 9 Revolutions of 3D Printing for Enterprises

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 15:31:59

July 22nd, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, the development of 3D printing technology has surpassed people's imagination. Recently, the American technology website The NextWeb summarized nine possible technological revolutions that the 3D technology revolution may bring to enterprises by interviewing the founders of nine American companies.

1. No concept of manufacturing products

With 3D technology, companies will no longer produce products and ship them to consumers. Consumers will buy a design, from glasses to houses, including all things. The cost of printing these products on the website will be much smaller than the current production processing. Once 3D printing becomes the most effective way to make things, you will have a "physical" app store where you can download and print what you want at any time, which may be the biggest revolution in the history of the Internet.

2. Print small gadgets

3D printers can print out small gadgets in people's daily lives, such as screws on coffee machines and headphones on treadmills. 3D printers will become a personal "Lowe's website" (Lowe's is the second largest indoor decoration material retailer in the United States) and "Home Depot Store" (the world's largest home building materials retailer).

3. Print cheaper samples

One of the reasons why the fashion industry is expensive is that it needs to make samples before product production, and the cost of making a sample is generally between $200-400. 3D printers can help young designers make samples more easily, especially those wear-resistant materials that can be printed with 3D technology.

4. Reduce production costs

The most exciting thing about 3D printing technology is that it can greatly reduce the production costs of start-up companies. 3D printing will replace the production sector business of start-up companies.

5. Test ideas

"Production needs" has always been one of the constraints for the development of small businesses. Due to the higher "minimum production quantity" requirements, the enterprise production preparation costs are generally limited. With the emergence of lean start-ups, tech startups need to consider testing the minimum viable product before producing an "A+" product.

Physical product manufacturing companies can use 3D printing technology to test their ideas before investing time and money into full-scale production. The more ideas tested, the better the company's start will be.

6. Print necessities

The potential of 3D printing seems endless. 3D printing technology can help poor areas build small houses, manufacture machines, and even food. The power of this technology lies in its speed in making things.

Anything you see or feel seems to be printable with 3D technology. In the near future, you will see 3D-printed mobile phone cases and jewelry accessories.

7. Start a business at home

For entrepreneurs, the technology of scanning and printing 3D objects is exciting. This scanning technology was unveiled at the SXSW Music Festival held in Austin, Texas in 2013, and MakerBot Industries showcased its desktop "digital conversion" device. Before the advent of 3D printing technology, there was no clear boundary between small-scale trials and large-scale mass production. Now, entrepreneurs can make models at home, scan them, and then start production.

8. Increase efficiency

What can 3D printing bring to design and production? People can increase the speed of object production by 10 times through optimizing the use of space and materials, thereby improving their work efficiency.

9. Print machine parts

For a manufacturer, the most direct impact of 3D printing technology is that it can print machine parts. Sometimes, the cost of replacing machine parts is even more expensive than the original equipment.
