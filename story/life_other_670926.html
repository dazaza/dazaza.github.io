The 11 Most "Mysterious" Coincidences in the World

by xbjrsy on 2012-02-21 13:39:55

1. Key Coincidence - Once, William, an American single woman, forgot to bring her keys when she went out. When she returned home, she was locked out. As she was at a loss, the mailman delivered a letter from her brother, which happened to have a key to her house inside! In the letter, her brother mentioned that during his last visit, William had given him an extra key to the front door. He forgot to return it when he went back to Washington, so he mailed it to her.

2. Two Lauras - In June 2001, ten-year-old Laura Boxton from Stafford, UK, wrote her name and address on a small label, tied it to a hydrogen balloon, and released it. The hydrogen balloon flew over 140 miles and landed at a resident's home. Unbelievably, this family also had a daughter named Laura Boxton, who was also exactly ten years old!

3. A Severe Mistake - Doris and Shela, sisters living in Alabama, USA, both wished to visit each other and give each other a surprise. Thus, they bade farewell to their families, got into their cars, and drove along Highway 25 towards each other's homes. However, at some section in the middle of the road, the two sisters' cars suddenly collided with each other, resulting in both sisters losing their lives simultaneously.

4. The Cursed Sports Car - In 1955, American movie star James Dean died in a car crash while driving his branded sports car for a joyride. His wrecked sports car was later towed to a repair shop. During the dismantling process, the car, which was supported by a jack, suddenly fell down and broke a mechanic's leg. Later, the car's engine was sold to a doctor who installed it in his own racing car. This doctor subsequently died in a car crash during a race. Another racer who bought the steering shaft of Dean's scrapped car also died in a car accident. The shell of Dean's car was used for exhibition, but then the exhibition hall caught fire unexpectedly. On another occasion, it fell off the display platform and crushed a visitor's hip bone.

5. A Suicidal Leap - After Vera Zdemak, a housewife from the capital of the Czech Republic, Prague, found out about her husband's infidelity, she decided to commit suicide. She jumped from the third floor and landed directly on a man who had just walked out from underneath the apartment building. The impact killed this man, while Vera only suffered minor injuries. This man turned out to be her faithless husband!

6. "Death" Takes a Vacation - In 1946, during the week West, the obituary editor of New York's 'Alton Evening News', took a vacation, the 'Alton Evening News' astonishingly did not receive a single obituary advertisement! Normally, the newspaper received no less than ten obituary advertisements every week!

7. Bridge of Death - In 1957, Bisinger from California, USA, was run over by a car while crossing a bridge near his home; two years later, his son Hiram was run over by a truck while crossing the same bridge; six years later, Bisinger's fourteen-year-old grandson David Wisler was playing on the bridge when a car sped by and crushed him into a pulp.

8. The Reunited Bracelet - Barbara Hutton from the UK accidentally flushed her antique bracelet down the bathroom drain once. A few days later, when she went to a jewelry store to buy a new one, a man came in with the bracelet she had lost, asking the store owner to appraise it. According to this man, he was a cleaner who found the bracelet while cleaning the sewer.

9. Fatal Experiment - In August 1995, Lisa Potter from Essex, UK, crossed the Essex Mott Line railway crossing with her mother. Lisa's father was run over by a train at this crossing eleven years ago, so Lisa's mother refused to cross this railway. Lisa thought that to eliminate her mother's fear, she should cross the railway first. So she walked towards the railway, but at that moment, a train suddenly passed by and hit Lisa, killing her.

10. The Drifting Phone Number - In 1990, Australian man Tod was watching the football final in the stadium when an excited audience member tore a yellow pages phone book printed by the post office into pieces and scattered them in the air. A piece drifted onto Tod's knee. He picked it up and found that it had his name, address, and phone number written on it.
