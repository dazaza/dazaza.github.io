Q: Is it good to drink tea after drinking alcohol?

A: After drinking, alcohol is converted into ethanol in the liver and quickly enters the kidneys along with the caffeine in tea, putting excessive strain on the kidneys and potentially damaging kidney function.

Q: Why should one not drink alcohol in the morning?

A: From 6 am onwards, the ether in the body gradually rises, peaking at 8 am. Drinking alcohol at this time combines alcohol with ether, making one feel tired throughout the day.

Q: How to sober up after getting drunk?

A: Repeatedly touch the throat with fingers or chopsticks to induce vomiting, then drink several bowls of water and vomit again to wash the stomach; vinegar water or baking soda water can also be consumed.

Q: Can tea sober one up?

A: Scientific research shows that tea does not contain any components that break down alcohol. Moreover, drinking tea with alcohol can damage kidney function, so one should avoid drinking tea, especially strong tea, after drinking.
