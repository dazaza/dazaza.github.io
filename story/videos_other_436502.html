Liu Yishou, a college student donated bone marrow twice for a girl suffering from leukemia.

by anonymous on 2011-05-24 17:40:49

We are extremely grateful to you for saving my daughter's life and giving her a new hope for life and a beautiful future.

Yang Ziwei, lying in bed while his father read this special letter of gratitude, had just made a second donation of bone marrow to a female student suffering from leukemia. The letter was entrusted by the girl's mother to medical staff to be delivered to Yang Ziwei.

Born on April 3, 1990, Yang Ziwei is a senior at Wuhan University of Bioengineering in the Architecture Department. Within a year, he donated bone marrow twice and also donated the 10,000 yuan Red Cross condolence money received after each donation to a female postgraduate student with leukemia in Sichuan.

In 2008, when Yang Ziwei turned 18, on his birthday he did two significant things: one was borrowing a classmate's razor to shave for the first time; the other was donating blood. Given his previous experiences of being rejected twice for blood donation due to being underage, this blood donation, which symbolized coming of age, made him feel very relaxed.

Discovering that his blood type was O-type, the universal donor, half a year later, he donated blood again voluntarily. On the blood donation bus, the doctor gave him a flyer about donating hematopoietic stem cells. He filled out the application form, underwent blood sampling, joined the ranks of volunteers in the Chinese Marrow Donor Program (CMDP), and additionally drew 5 milliliters of blood as a preliminary matching sample. Afterward, he didn't give it much thought since the probability of successful matching is less than one in ten thousand.

At the end of 2009, Yang Ziwei's email inbox received an email stating that the blood sample he had retained matched successfully with a female student with leukemia in Beijing, asking if he would consider donating. His first reaction was: am I being scammed? He called the contact number provided in the email to inquire and only believed it was true after carefully verifying various pieces of information.

Without hesitation, Yang Ziwei began following the procedures for donating hematopoietic stem cells, starting with preliminary matching, then high-resolution HLA typing, followed by a physical examination. Only a few days before the collection of hematopoietic stem cells did Yang Ziwei call home. To avoid making his parents anxious, he consistently used the professional term "hematopoietic stem cell donation" rather than the more common term "bone marrow donation." However, his mother immediately disagreed upon hearing it and told him to ask his father. When he asked his father, his father also strongly opposed it.

Their concerns were understandable. Yang Ziwei is not only the only child in the family but also the eldest grandson of the clan, regarded as a treasure by his parents and the entire family.

January 3, 2010, was the day determined for the collection of hematopoietic stem cells. Yang Ziwei called home, asking his parents to come accompany him. Despite their reluctance, his parents still came to Wuhan and accompanied their resolute son into the operating room. The surgery lasted nearly four hours, during which the blood drawn was separated through centrifugation to extract hematopoietic stem cells, then returned to the body. With needles inserted in both arms, Yang Ziwei couldn't move during the surgery, and his parents remained anxiously by his side throughout.

The day after the surgery, his parents gave detailed instructions before boarding the train back home. Yang Ziwei returned to the hotel where he had rested previously to pack up his belongings, waiting for his classmates to pick him up and return to school. He casually turned on the TV and happened to see a group of students donating to Li Ruifang, a 2008 master's degree graduate student at Sichuan Normal University's School of Economics and Management who suffers from leukemia. At the end of the program, there was a phone number left for Li Ruifang. He quickly noted down the number.

Before leaving, his parents gave him the 5000 yuan recovery condolence money given by the CMDP staff. Back at school, he called Li Ruifang and, after confirming her bank account details, transferred the 5000 yuan to her.

After returning home after the exams, Yang Ziwei saw various materials collected by his parents regarding the donation of hematopoietic stem cells on his mother's dressing table, feeling a lump in his throat. When he received the second call from the CMDP staff, Yang Ziwei directly refused.

A few days later, he received another call, learning that the little girl was critically ill. His heart suddenly felt heavy, finding it particularly hard to bear, feeling as if a close relative was about to leave.

This time, his answer changed to yes. He prepared to persuade his parents and step onto the operating table for bone marrow donation for the second time.

Facing the prospect of a second donation, Yang Ziwei's parents were extremely upset. To ease the family's worries and to help more people understand the knowledge of bone marrow donation, Yang Ziwei asked his overseas-studying classmates to look up information locally, comparing the Chinese and foreign data. In the end, he confirmed that donating bone marrow is harmless to the human body and can even have a certain positive effect of purifying the blood to some extent.

He told his parents: If we don't try this time, wouldn't all the efforts made before be wasted? Convinced by his words, his parents accompanied Yang Ziwei to Beijing to collect hematopoietic stem cells again, rescuing the girl once more.

After the donation, he transferred the 5000 yuan recovery condolence money to Li Ruifang.

According to CMDP staff, nationwide, there are only dozens of cases where someone has consecutively donated bone marrow twice. The choice of Yang Ziwei, a post-90s university student, moved many people. Some called him the most handsome university student. Yang Ziwei said: It was just that I happened to encounter this low-probability event of matching. Contributing a little bit can help others overcome difficulties, which makes me feel happy.

Yang Ziwei hopes more people will join the Chinese Marrow Donor Program to bring hope of life to more leukemia patients. His wish has been responded to. On May 4, the Yang Ziwei Blood Donation and Bone Marrow Donation Volunteer Team was established at Wuhan University of Bioengineering, with over 1,200 teachers and students joining the volunteer team, becoming the first blood donation and bone marrow donation volunteer team named after an individual in Wuhan.

On May 5, the Student Union of the Architecture Department of Wuhan University of Bioengineering initiated a donation drive for Li Ruifang at Sichuan Normal University, helping the student Li Ruifang facing economic difficulties due to medical treatment, passing on Yang Ziwei's love.
