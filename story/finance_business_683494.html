Japanese Superstition: Scientists Use Genetic Techniques to Create Singing Mice (Picture)

by aiipii12987 on 2012-02-29 14:41:53

Singing Mice: Japanese researchers announced on the 21st that they used genetic engineering techniques to induce gene mutations in mice, allowing them to "sing" like birds.

This research project, named the "Evolution Mouse Project," was conducted by researchers from Osaka University in Japan, focusing on genetically modified mice. These mice have deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) that is easily "rewritten," leading to mutations.

"We allowed these genetically modified mice to breed for several generations... observing each newborn mouse," team leader Ariki Yutaka (phonetic translation) told the AFP. "One day, we discovered one of the mice could 'sing' like a bird."

Ariki believes that the appearance of the "singing mouse" was purely accidental, but this mutated trait will be passed down to the next generation. Researchers found that when these mutant mice are placed in unfamiliar environments or cohabitate with the opposite sex, their "singing" volume increases. This means their singing behavior is "an expression of emotion and physical condition."

The study has so far "evolved" more than a hundred "singing mice." Ariki mentioned that during the research, a physically unique mouse was also produced, "with shorter limbs and a tail resembling a dachshund."

Researchers hope that this study can help "unveil" the process of human language evolution. Some other country researchers previously used songbirds such as finches as research subjects, attempting to "reveal" the origin of human language. Research results show that these birds can compose different sound components into structures similar to human languages, linking them together according to certain grammatical rules to form "music."

Ariki believes that compared to birds, mice are better research subjects, "because they are mammals, with brain structures and other physiological characteristics closer to humans."

According to Xinhua News Agency.
