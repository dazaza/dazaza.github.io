Twitter's acquisition of MoPub may trigger a merger and acquisition war in the industry

by anonymous on 2013-09-12 12:12:01

Introduction: A foreign media outlet published an article on Tuesday titled "The Impact of Twitter's $350 Million Acquisition of MoPub and Which Ad Tech Firm Is Next," suggesting that Twitter's acquisition of MoPub could spark a wave of mergers and acquisitions in the industry, with Microsoft, Yahoo, and Amazon among the potential buyers. Mobclix, inMobi, and Millennial Media are likely to be the next targets for acquisition.

Here is a summary of the article:

In preparation for its upcoming IPO (Initial Public Offering), Twitter has taken several steps to enhance its revenue potential, and the $350 million acquisition of MoPub, a startup that helps mobile publishers manage ad space, is the latest move. Meanwhile, there are some questions hidden behind this deal: Will Twitter be able to coexist peacefully with other publishers after acquiring MoPub? Which tech company will be stimulated by Twitter's move to make acquisitions in this field?

Twitter already provides promoted tweets services to advertisers, but if it can expand its monetization model, its revenue from brand advertising will greatly increase. The acquisition of MoPub gives Twitter such an opportunity because MoPub will provide Twitter with an industry-leading mobile ad exchange platform.

To create a real-time, automated ad market, MoPub has been working with multiple publishers. However, whether MoPub can continue to maintain its position as an open market after being acquired by Twitter remains uncertain.

Although Twitter stated that it would continue to maintain MoPub's core business and work with publishers, some industry insiders believe that maintaining MoPub's independent market status long-term wouldn't make much sense for Twitter. One insider said: "If you're on Twitter's board, what value is there in spending $350 million to acquire MoPub and then continuing to keep MoPub as an independent market?"

Moreover, Twitter can use MoPub's ad inventory to promote other apps, such as Vine video app. Video ads are currently in their early stages, and this part of the business will become increasingly important for Twitter. The competition in this area is evident; Tremor Video and YuMe recently went public, and AOL spent over $400 million to turn Adap.tv into AOL Video.

So, who will be next? Potential buyers include publishers like Yahoo and Microsoft, whose many businesses rely on ad revenue but have performed poorly in recent years. Other potential buyers include Amazon or Adobe, both of which are actively defending themselves in the ad market to protect their own businesses. Additionally, Facebook and Twitter cannot be ruled out.

Which companies might these tech giants acquire?

1. Mobclix

Mobclix is also a mobile ad exchange waiting to be acquired and could be the next target. Due to its parent company Velti's poor condition, potential buyers may get a good price. Velti recently announced that it has hired an investment bank to handle the sale of Mobclix. In the last fiscal quarter, Velti's mobile ad revenue was $89 billion.

2. Nexage

Nexage is also a good choice. It is a mobile ad exchange with 300 publishers and 200 demand sources. Nexage launched in 2011 and has worked with apps like Angry Birds. Nexage announced in January that its revenue grew 171% in the previous fiscal year. Last year, the company raised $15 million.

3. Smaato

Smaato is a mobile ad exchange focused on international markets, with annual transactions reaching $110 million. The second-largest market for Smaato's real-time bidding business is India, and it also performs strongly in Europe.

4. inMobi

inMobi is an Indian ad tech company with 25 offices worldwide and strong performance in the Chinese market. inMobi has raised over $200 million from SoftBank and KPCB, so the possibility of it being acquired is slightly lower.

5. Millennial Media

Acquiring Millennial Media could be the most dramatic mobile acquisition transaction, as the most likely scenario is that a private equity firm privatizes this public company. Recently, Millennial Media's stock performance has been sluggish. After announcing last month that it would acquire competitor Jumptap for $200 million, the company's stock didn't improve much. Millennial Media's current market capitalization is slightly above $500 million. (By Li Ming)
