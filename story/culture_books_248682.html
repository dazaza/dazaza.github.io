"You've Always Been in the Distant Place That Makes Me Cry" - A Touching Love Story That Heals Emotional Wounds

by jqrxass on 2009-10-17 14:23:07

Every woman will meet her knight and prince, one who guards her unconditionally, and the other who admires her without reason.

That day, I wasn't ready to wait for you, my knight. You were like a fresh and fragrant beam of sunlight, quietly falling on the lonely first half of my life, leaving an eternal mark in many fleeting moments of my existence.

At that time, I wasn't ready to fall in love with you, my prince. You were like an unfamiliar ordinary person, entering my heart automatically, warming every moment afterward, until life and death parted us.

She thought she had waited for her knight and prince, and happiness was smiling not far away.

Young as she was, she found herself trapped in a black hole of emotion and ethics. When her love met another's family head-on, should she turn left or right?

Crying, struggling, defending, turning... none of these could set her free.

Life, was it destined to be filled with only loneliness? How many more near misses would it take to bring her the right person?

You have always stayed in the distance that makes me cry - Chapter One

Unfortunately, this journey lasted only ten minutes before it ended. Lin Sensen stopped at the entrance of the neighborhood, firmly refusing to let him see her off any further. It was evident that deep down, she had a deeply rooted defense against men trying to get close to her.

Tong Xulang couldn't force the issue, so he stood there watching her figure gradually disappear into the distance. The night wind was strong, blowing her jet-black long hair, casting her shadow long under the streetlights, making her look even more delicate. He always felt that a woman as delicate and refined as her should be protected in the strong arms of a man.

However, getting her to lower her guard wouldn't be an easy task. He sighed lowly, shook his head, and turned to stride back home.

You have always stayed in the distance that makes me cry - Chapter Two

Lin Sensen liked his smile; it gave a feeling of warmth and calmness, unlike a certain man who was always carefree and disregardful of his image, yet sharp enough to make one unable to open their eyes. Recently, not seeing that person for a while made her think of him often. That unpredictable man always appeared suddenly in front of her when she least expected it.

Lu Junhui stared at Lin Sensen with some discomfort in his heart. She was lost in thought again, just like several times over the phone. He knew it wasn't because of him. She was different from when they first met; the then her was calm and wise, never losing focus. What changed her? He refused to investigate the cause, deceiving himself.

Could it be that she finally accepted me? He secretly rejoiced, his confidence greatly boosted. Finally managing to successfully put his arm around her shoulder, he felt elated. After some time, the pose slightly stiffened, and he tried to slowly tighten his arm around her, eventually hugging her entirely. For a long time, his empty embrace finally felt delightfully full. He felt for the first time that besides his career, he had another sweet value — he could help her bear all the burdens of life, he could protect her from all external intrusions, his strength and robustness were meant to protect her happiness and peace forever... The sudden tenderness made him want to lower his head and whisper a few words to her, but he was shocked to find her eyes brimming with tears at some point. He felt a sharp pain in his heart, inexplicably hating the fabricated movie that made her sad.

The rain became denser, but his coat was large and warm, creating a safe little world for her. Suddenly, she felt her heart soften and no longer insisted on maintaining the so-called dignity and resistance, obediently leaning on his back as he helped her stand up steadily and hurriedly moved forward. In this warm bumping, she vaguely remembered that elderly people used to say "carrying a wife," perhaps because a man's back is the warmest and safest place on his body? In this cozy turbulence, she felt a corner of her heart soften too...
