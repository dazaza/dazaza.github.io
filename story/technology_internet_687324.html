[Qingdao Cuisine] Creative Delicious Ribs [Jelly Sweet and Sour Ribs]

by qssqwe215 on 2012-03-02 15:03:33

A few days ago, I received a box of New Zealand kiwifruit. I used two of them to make kiwi jam. However, as there was no bread at home, I wasn't sure how to eat it. I also wanted to try the new Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce we had bought for stewed meat, to see if it would make a noticeable difference. It was also a good time to improve my husband's diet. So, I decided to combine the kiwi jam with the double tea dark soy sauce.

Considering that kiwifruit is nutritionally rich and the jam contains some sugar, I made ribs with kiwi jam, and the result was fantastic! I highly recommend it.

The biggest feature of this sweet and sour spare ribs is that no sugar was added, yet the taste is absolutely sweet and sour. The method introduced here can be applied in various ways. Any leftover jam can be used instead of sugar when stewing meat. Not only does it color the dish, but it also adds a fruity aroma, enhancing the complex fragrance of the stewed meat, achieving multiple benefits. The recipe for making kiwi jam will be updated in a later blog post.

For this recipe, I used crispy ribs rather than regular ones. These ribs contain crunchy bones which are great for calcium supplementation, allowing you to enjoy the meat while getting extra calcium. Previously, a friend asked me about the two types of tea in the new Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce. To clarify, the Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce contains Pu'er tea and green tea, mainly functioning to provide double freshness and reduce greasiness.

Ingredients: An appropriate amount of ribs, bay leaves, cloves, fennel, star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon bark, scallion segments, ginger slices.

Seasonings: Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce, starch, salt, cooking wine, white vinegar, kiwi jam.

Steps:

1. Place the ribs in cold water, add a little Sichuan peppercorn, star anise, and cooking wine, then bring to a boil and blanch.

2. Once boiling, remove the ribs, drain, and set aside.

3. Drizzle with 2 ceramic spoonfuls of Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce.

4. Add an appropriate amount of starch, mix well, and marinate for about 10 minutes.

5. Heat an appropriate amount of oil in a pan, and when it reaches about 70% heat, add the ribs.

6. Quickly stir-fry the ribs. When the oil temperature reaches about 90% heat and starts smoking, stir evenly, remove, and set aside after draining.

7. In a pot, add an appropriate amount of water, along with half a cube of concentrated old hen soup (or one cube if using more ribs).

8. Add 2 ceramic spoonfuls of Lee Kum Kee Double Tea Dark Soy Sauce, a small amount of white vinegar to adjust the broth, and an appropriate amount of salt for seasoning.

9. Add about half a small milk bottle's worth of kiwi jam.

10. Other flavors of jam can also be used as substitutes, and the amount of jam can be appropriately increased or decreased.

11. Place the ribs into the seasoned broth, and add star anise, cinnamon bark, cloves, fennel, and bay leaves.

12. Bring to a boil on high heat, then simmer for about 40 minutes to an hour. Finally, thicken the sauce over high heat.
