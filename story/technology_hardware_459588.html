Every individual will experience several career changes during their professional lives: transferring from one company to another, switching from one industry to another, or even choosing to start your own business instead of joining any company. Whether it's job hopping, starting a business, or changing industries, when such career transitions occur, we can't help but ask ourselves: "What kind of job should I look for now?" Or: "What kind of undertaking can I accomplish?"

When we focus most on our next step in terms of prospects and future, we often inadvertently neglect something far more important than the immediate future itself: the accumulation of professional energy.

What is "professional energy"?

Like all objects, our careers accumulate unique, intangible energy. This energy cannot be simply summarized by what is commonly referred to as "work experience." It also includes the spirit, temperament, vision, breadth of mind, intuition, and other things accumulated in the workplace that cannot be replaced by "experience." Every time you play a role in the workplace, a specific type of "professional energy" quickly gathers around you and integrates with you to form a whole. In other words, you, along with that "professional energy," create a complete and genuine "you" in the workplace. You and your friends around you may notice that after experiencing a new career, some aspects of you seem to have changed, making you different from before. What's the reason? Actually, it's not that you yourself have undergone any change. Scientifically speaking, it's precisely your current unique "professional energy" that unconsciously and imperceptibly causes you to change.

The reset of professional energy isn't merely about work experience — work experience can be quickly gained, but restoring the overall professional energy that has already been reset requires much effort. Moreover, some people frequently and blindly switch careers due to various reasons, resetting their professional energy multiple times.

It must be realized that every time you reset your professional energy, it's like dropping a devastating atomic bomb on your own "career continent," causing your life to suffer a major trauma. Repeated resets of professional energy ultimately result in only one outcome: your career will lose energy due to multiple "earthquakes," with both impact and resilience significantly weakened. Coupled with age, life, environment, and other objective factors, you will increasingly fall behind others in the workplace while being caught in negative emotions such as self-doubt, resentment, and discouragement.

Therefore, rational professionals will, from the very beginning of entering their professional lives or at the onset of facing career transitions, include "accumulation of professional energy" as the most critical factor in their overall career planning. They absolutely do not allow their careers to become disconnected, nor do they permit their professional energy to be inexplicably released, solidified, or reset. They always use every possible method to link each career transition to the track of the previous station they reached. Thus, for them, career transitions are upgrades rather than formats.

Continuous accumulation of professional energy leads to changes in "professional energy levels." The enhancement of professional energy levels does not represent a promotion in rank, nor does it directly signify an increase in salary. However, it fully demonstrates that you possess the foundation for higher ranks and higher salaries. As long as the opportunity arises, these will not be issues. You will be more appreciated, have more opportunities, greater freedom of choice, and gain more.
