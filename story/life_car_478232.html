Five Bottlenecks Restricting the Development of the Automotive Decoration and Beauty Industry

With the development of the automotive beauty and decoration industry, situations have emerged that restrict its growth. The China Automotive Beauty Recruitment Network has its own insights and suggestions regarding the five major bottlenecks restricting the development of the automotive decoration and beauty industry:

1. Uneven Product Quality

The profit from automotive beauty and decoration mainly comes from the price difference of materials and labor costs. Some merchants, in pursuit of greater profits, often pass off inferior products as quality ones, making it impossible for ordinary consumers to identify them. Some consumers who use substandard sun protection films may even experience difficulty seeing outside at night. Others, after replacing their rearview mirrors with so-called "wide-view" mirrors, experience dizziness and blurred vision while driving, leading to misjudgment of distances and creating significant safety hazards. These issues lead to a lack of trust among consumers towards automotive beauty products and shops.

2. Lack of Standardization

Due to the absence of unified service standards, there is a huge disparity in pricing for car beauty and decoration services between different merchants. The profit margin in the automotive decoration market generally ranges from 40% to 50%, but some individual merchants can achieve margins of 120% to 200%. For example, leather seats sold for 4,000 yuan might cost less than 1,500 yuan; fabric seat covers costing less than 100 yuan are sometimes sold for over 500 yuan. Industry insiders believe that the lack of unified service standards in the beauty and decoration industry leads to wide variations in service prices, turning the automotive beauty market into one of excessive profits where service quality is difficult to measure. If this continues, consumers will lose confidence in the beauty and decoration market.

3. Inconsistent Personnel Quality

Since it is hard to immediately distinguish the quality and effect of car beauty treatments within a short period, many "four-nothing" beauty shops have emerged on the market: no formal training, no professional brand products, no professional machinery, and no service quality guarantee. It is reported that there are approximately 9,000 companies specializing or partially engaged in automotive beauty and decoration in China, employing around 150,000 people, many of whom operate without proper licenses.

4. Severe Talent Shortage

It is well known that the threshold for entering the automotive beauty and decoration industry is low. With just 3 to 5 people, a high-pressure water gun, a few bottles of car wax, several rolls of sun protection film, and a few simple tools, a small-scale beauty shop can open for business. Taking car decoration as an example, the current reality is a shortage of professional car decorators, and there is currently no standard for evaluating professionals in this industry. Even in 4S stores of various car brands, which are considered to have relatively high technical and service standards, although they all have their own decoration departments and related staff undergo strict training from manufacturers, their work typically only includes polishing, waxing, interior cleaning, and installing reverse parking radars. Tasks such as applying sun protection films and glazing are usually outsourced or handled by professional technicians.

5. Lack of Regulatory Laws

It is understood that the main reason for the disorder in China's automotive beauty and decoration industry is the absence of nationwide management regulations and industry standards. The rapid development of the automobile industry and the increasing popularity of family cars attract more investors to join this sector. Issuing corresponding management methods and industry standards to regulate the market is necessary. This can reduce and, to a certain extent, prevent counterfeit and substandard products from entering the market, standardize the behavior of producers and distributors, and allow the market to develop towards professionalism and standardization, thereby protecting the legitimate rights and interests of car consumers. People generally reflect that in order to do a good job in car beauty, the car beauty industry itself needs beautification first. Recently, the Professional Committee of Automotive Beauty Decoration and Accessories of the China Automobile Circulation Association has announced that the formulation of the "Management Norms for Automotive Beauty Decoration Services" has entered the discussion agenda and is expected to be implemented within the year. The introduction and implementation of the "Norms" will reorganize the industry order of the post-car market and punish non-compliant behaviors. Relevant officials from the Market System Construction Department of the Ministry of Commerce expressed that during the formulation of the "Norms," opinions from enterprises within the industry should be widely solicited to ensure that the service management norms play the maximum guiding role.
