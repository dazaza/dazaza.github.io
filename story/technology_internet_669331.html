Share to: Welcome to post comments I want to comment ■ Pregnant woman suddenly felt unwell and had a miscarriage followed by heavy bleeding ■ Netizens helped spread the Weibo post, the main party expressed gratitude Netizen "Jingcheng Xiaocui'er V" is Mr. Wang's friend, who had already returned to their hometown for the New Year when they received Mr. Wang's call. "We all work in Beijing, so we don't know much about his wife's specific situation, only that she was carrying twins, and seemed to have been hospitalized before the Spring Festival," said "Jingcheng Xiaocui'er V." "When we received the call, we were also very worried." "Jingcheng Xiaocui'er V" explained that since none of them were in Beijing, even if they wanted to donate blood, it would be difficult to make it happen, especially given the urgent situation. Weibo recommendation | Today's Weibo hot topics (Editor: SN017) "At around 3 a.m. on the second day of the New Year, my wife suddenly had a stomachache, so we rushed to the hospital immediately," Mr. Wang, the husband of the pregnant woman, told reporters. His wife was carrying twins and was already more than six months pregnant. She had shown signs of a possible miscarriage before the Spring Festival and had been hospitalized for three days at Haidian Maternal and Child Healthcare Hospital. On New Year's Eve, the hospital confirmed that the pregnant woman's condition was stable, so she was discharged. "The first two days at home were fine, but unexpectedly, an issue arose on the second day. Upon arriving at the hospital, the situation was very critical. Not only did we lose the babies, but my wife also experienced heavy bleeding. Her breathing became difficult." "Heavy bleeding, serious stuff, life-threatening! Praying for this sister, hoping for her stability and peace of mind for her parents. Unfortunately, I am not the required blood type! If you see this, please spread it quickly!" Netizen "Wu Qing" sent blessings while forwarding the Weibo post. Other netizens commented, "This world has love, this is love, hoping everyone can lend a helping hand. Please forward this message! Let this world be filled with warmth! This winter will no longer be cold!" Netizen "Han Dahi V" responded, "Alright... I can only help spread it... Every drop of blood is warm and continues life!" Some netizens mentioned they had forwarded the request to the China Rare Blood Type Alliance. Yesterday afternoon, the main party, Mr. Wang, informed reporters that his wife had been transferred to Peking University Third Hospital, underwent surgery, and was now under observation in the ICU. Her condition had stabilized. Throughout the day, he and his friends received numerous calls from kind-hearted people offering assistance and words of comfort. They expressed deep gratitude. Although the hospital had resolved the blood supply needs, the kindness from many still warmed his heart. "In this time of seasonal transition, although the weather remains cold, our hearts are filled with warmth. Thank you all," said Mr. Wang and his friends in their expression of gratitude. ■ Friends Already Left Beijing, Heard the News and Sought Help via Weibo According to reports, "A friend had a miscarriage and is experiencing heavy bleeding. She is currently being treated at the Haidian Maternal and Child Healthcare Hospital and urgently needs B-type RH positive blood. Please help spread this rescue information in Beijing. Thanks. I beg each of you." Last night at around 9 p.m., netizen "Jingcheng Xiaocui'er V" posted a plea for help on Weibo, which was quickly forwarded and by noon yesterday had been retweeted over 6,000 times. Some netizens not only forwarded the Weibo post but also indicated their willingness to go to the hospital to donate blood. Mr. Wang said that doctors mentioned during the rescue that the mother needed platelets, which the hospital might lack. "Although it was just a mention at the time, seeing my wife's condition made me anxious, so I quickly called my friends for help, asking them to find blood for me." "Friends are all trying different methods; some are finding ways through connections, others are contacting blood banks. I feel that Weibo spreads information quickly, so I hurriedly went online to post a plea for help, which then drew the attention of many netizens." "Jingcheng Xiaocui'er V" told reporters that her plea for help on Weibo had been retweeted over 6,000 times, and both she and the main party, Mr. Wang, had received many calls from netizens, with several indicating their willingness to donate blood.
