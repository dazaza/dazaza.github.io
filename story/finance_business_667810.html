Lotus L5 Sportback Actual Shooting

by juyaqufa004 on 2012-02-17 10:29:00

Since the launch of the Lotus L5 sedan at the Shanghai Auto Show in April 2011, within a short span of less than half a year, the two-door hatchback model followed closely and was launched at the Guangzhou Auto Show in September. This shows that Youngman Lotus is determined to succeed with the two-door version of the L5. However, the actual market sales performance of the L5 (including both two-door and four-door versions) has not been very optimistic. Perhaps it's because Youngman Lotus lacks a long-standing automotive culture domestically, or perhaps the sales channels have yet to be fully opened, or maybe... But does poor sales mean that the L5 lacks quality and competitiveness? The reality is quite the opposite.

Design Background: The Lotus L5 was designed by the British Lotus car design team for Youngman Lotus over a period of five years. Its design elements originate from the currently popular hatchback style. Therefore, in terms of appearance, besides the fashionable hatchback styling, the L5 Sportback also exhibits its British style. The market positioning of the L5 Sportback (two-door) is as an "urban fashion coupe." Further segmentation of the model provides more choices for young people who like coupes.

Appearance: Although the design of the Lotus L5 comes from British Lotus, and we can indeed see some of its British style on the L5, whether this British style can be accepted by domestic consumers remains uncertain. If the answer is yes, then at least so far, it hasn't been achieved yet, and it still needs some time. On the exterior of the L5, we can see many willow-leaf shaped designs, such as the headlights, grille, and taillights. Just this willow-leaf style somewhat differs from the aesthetic preferences of domestic consumers.

The metal grille with a willow-leaf shape is well-made and deserves high praise. Such a metal grille can also be seen on some handcrafted sports cars. However, the willow-leaf shaped grille paired with willow-leaf shaped headlights seems somewhat contrary to domestic design styles, but it is indeed unique. However, the configuration is generous, with LED daytime running lights and blackened headlights with lenses standard across all models.

Compared to the front face, I believe the hatchback styling of the L5 two-door side is easier for consumers to accept. The transition of the rear hatchback styling is very natural, and the rear design is quite successful. Let's take a look at the rims and tires. The tires used are Dunlop SP3 sport tires, with dimensions of 205/50 R16. The spare tire is also a full-size one and is standard across all models! Even among domestically produced cars touting high configurations, it's hard to find another car as generous as this one.

Besides making the "rear end" appear fuller and more stylish, the hatchback design also significantly enhances the trunk storage space. In daily use, when the rear seats are folded down, you can put whatever you want in there, as long as you dare to imagine! The designer considered the inconvenience caused by the hatchback design when opening and closing the trunk and thoughtfully designed a handle. If you want an electric tailgate, then you can check out Audi or BMW, as you won't find an electric tailgate on a car priced around 100,000 yuan.
