Arsenal won a thrilling victory, and the professor exploded for one person. A ticking bomb forced the Gunners to make another move.

by yinding999 on 2009-12-14 20:13:15

Arsenal Narrow Victory, Professor Explodes for One Player;定时炸弹逼枪手再出手

By reporter Ke Han: In the match at Anfield, Arsenal came from behind to win. Beneath the joy, there was also a sense of relief: Without Andrey Arshavin, would Arsenal have endured another embarrassing loss? Especially chilling is the fact that while Liverpool dominated in the first half, their goal was not a sure thing but rather a gift from goalkeeper Manuel Almunia's mistake.

Before this game, Arsenal legend Ian Wright had expressed concerns in his Sun column: "Almunia is a good second-choice goalkeeper, but he lacks the demeanor of a number one keeper for a championship-contending team. Occasionally, he looks as if he's just not up to the level. Arsenal needs a top-class goalkeeper, but the problem is that such a keeper would be hard to attract to the Emirates Stadium."

Almunia's deputy, Lukasz Fabianski, who participated in last season's 4-4 draw and has recently recovered from injury, said: "I hope to put as much pressure on Almunia as possible. What else can I do? Only train hard every time and show my abilities when given the chance. I also hope Almunia will enjoy this competition."

But Almunia insisted: "It has been difficult for me recently (sickness, injury, unexpected death of his mother-in-law in a car accident). I didn't feel great at the start of the season, and everything else made things more challenging. Psychologically, I wasn't fully recovered, but now my confidence is back. The old Manuel is back!" However, in the 9th minute, he kicked a goal kick directly out of play, drawing boos from the crowd.

In the 12th minute, Almunia did indeed catch Fernando Torres' one-on-one shot cleanly. This was not surprising, as Torres, after returning from injury, clearly hadn't regained his match fitness. Upon receiving Steven Gerrard's brilliant pass, instead of rushing into the penalty area to try and outmaneuver his compatriot, Torres attempted a long-range shot from outside the box which lacked power, making it easy for Almunia to save.

But Almunia's weakness lies in: When the opponent uses set-pieces to loft balls into the box, he often makes wrong decisions. During the first half of this game, there were several examples of this: In the 23rd minute, Gerrard's corner kick entered the penalty area, leaving Almunia somewhat flustered; two minutes later, Gerrard sent another free-kick into the penalty area, and Almunia could only punch the ball away; in the 32nd minute, Gerrard slotted the ball diagonally into the penalty area, and although Almunia caught it, he immediately gave the ball away during the subsequent counterattack!

In the 41st minute, Aurelio's free-kick posed no real threat, but Almunia, coming off his line, failed to secure the ball, instead hitting it straight to Dirk Kuyt on the edge of the box. The Dutchman naturally said thank you very much and immediately scored with a long-range shot! In a tough away game, the most important thing is to avoid conceding first, but Almunia's amateurish error once again brought shame to the team.

Fairly speaking, Lucas may have been offside on this goal, and Arsène Wenger on the sideline showed his anger about it. But as the BBC website said: "Almunia might have been impeded by Lucas, but he still should have done better. This is a goal that Almunia will want to forget. Those fans still chanting 'Almunia for England' can take a break."

At halftime, the professor erupted in fury in the dressing room. Vermaelen later said: "I've never seen the boss so angry, so in the second half, we all started working harder." The thoughtful Belgian didn't say whether Wenger's main target of anger was Almunia, but many believe the Spanish goalkeeper must have received the full force of Wenger's rant.

The second half saw a dramatic shift. Arsenal scored two goals within ten minutes to level the score, and an unexpectedly low-scoring Liverpool couldn't properly test Almunia again, allowing the ticking time bomb to temporarily go silent. The lesson for Arsenal from this game is: With Arshavin, they may not lack for forwards, but sooner or later, they will need to buy a goalkeeper better than Almunia.
