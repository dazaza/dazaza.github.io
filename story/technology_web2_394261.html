What is an online store, the development and promotion of online stores, and the process of opening a store.

by anonymous on 2011-03-28 09:48:58

Definition of an Online Store:

An online store, as the name suggests, is a shop opened on the internet. As a form of e-commerce, it's a website that allows people to browse and make actual purchases, completing the entire transaction process through various payment methods. Currently, most online stores are opened using third-party platforms such as Taobao, EachNet, Youa, Paipai, and Goupu Mall. Creating your own e-commerce site involves significant technical work and requires a large initial investment.

Development of Online Stores:

Online stores have evolved from initially just showcasing products online to becoming websites where visitors can not only view products but also make actual purchases.

Characteristics of Online Stores:

1. Convenient and fast - no need for renovations or procurement processes required by traditional shops; with just a few clicks on the keyboard, you can open an online store.

2. Quick transactions - after both parties reach an agreement, payments can be made immediately, and goods can be delivered to buyers via logistics or courier services.

3. No risk of overstocking - you don't need a physical store and can operate solely online without needing to stockpile inventory, which is one of the attractive features of online stores.

4. Easy to manage - no need to hire staff or travel long distances to restock shelves; everything is done online, and restocking is as simple as clicking a mouse.

5. Diverse formats - regardless of what you sell, there are suitable formats available. If you have substantial funds, you can choose to use general online store programs or register with reputable online store service providers to conduct transactions.

6. Trust is key - online transactions cannot provide the tangible experience of in-person shopping, leading buyers to prefer transacting with merchants they trust more. Therefore, if the first transaction goes smoothly, the likelihood of repeat business is higher.

Preparations for Starting an Online Store:

Starting an online store means having a website with online shopping functionality. First, you must have basic website infrastructure: web space (which can be a virtual host, independent host, or VPS host), domain name, database (for static websites, databases are not necessary, but online stores require them to store product, customer, and order information). An online store system is a set of software programs that run on the website server. To manage the website, you need some software on your computer, the most important being FTP software. FTP is a network tool that allows you to manage files on your website space. Common FTP software includes FlashFXP and CuteFTP. Most importantly, you need your source of goods.

Online Store Systems:

Online store systems: these arose because some platforms could no longer fully meet the needs of online merchants, resulting in third-party independent online store systems that are easy to use, flexible, and independent.

Development Languages for Online Store Systems:

.NET - Microsoft's main platform, currently gaining popularity. It has the potential to replace JSP and has a promising future.

JSP - Primarily used for large web sites like portal sites, enterprise government website management systems, etc. Various frameworks and technologies are mature. It's currently the mainstream for building websites.

PHP - Similar to ASP but performs better. It has good security and occupies a certain share of the market. For example, the domestic market-leading online store system ShopEx is based on PHP technology.

Here, we recommend .NET systems; following Microsoft is definitely a safe choice. Many well-known domestic companies use .NET, such as JD.com, Newegg, Vancl, and PPG.

Operation of Online Stores:

A good online store operator should pay attention to selecting the right online store service provider and service format: choosing between an online store service provider or starting your own online store; selecting good sources of goods and handling the processing and placement of product images after stocking up; then decorating the online store; communicating with customers; ensuring secure transaction processes, etc.

Three Steps of Online Store Operation:

1. Selecting the Business Direction:

Operating an online store is completely different from operating a physical store offline. Offline, as long as your store location isn't too bad, even small businesses can do quite well, selling common items can still be profitable. However, doing business online requires finding unique paths. Generally, it's best to sell items that are hard to find offline (such as special crafts, limited edition items, branded clothing, electronics, etc.). This way, dedicated enthusiasts will find your store, and if you cooperate well with them, the business will flow steadily with repeat customers. Choosing unique items that others find hard to get is a good start, ensuring quality at reasonable prices will keep your customers.

Step 2: Stocking Goods and Pricing:

In online sales, there's no pressure from rent or worries about industrial and commercial taxes, nor any harassment from organized crime. So, as long as we have good sources of goods, every profit counts easily. Therefore, prices must be cheaper than offline. Don't be greedy; refer to other prices and offer as much discount as possible. This will attract cost-conscious customers who might become long-term clients with good service. After selecting products, create detailed product descriptions. Some sellers' descriptions are overly brief, leaving potential customers confused. A good description not only informs but also shows respect for the buyer and the product itself. Good descriptions can attract knowledgeable buyers and help beginners interested in the product understand it better, thus developing interest in your store. Detailed descriptions also make customers perceive the seller as an expert, increasing trust in both the seller and the product.

Step 3: Opening for Business, Integrity as the Foundation:

Answer messages promptly and honestly to resolve buyers' doubts and increase their trust. Regularly update your offerings, even if there's no immediate business, frequently add new items to the shelf and resolve any backlog issues. Only by keeping funds flowing quickly will profits roll in. Some stores offer discounts, gifts, or lotteries to attract customers. Online business must emphasize credibility; only with good credit can you gain more stable customers. Some stores build communities by introducing star-rated customers, sharing life experiences, and publishing essays, turning their stores into social hubs and customers into friends. Always cherish your credit; doing business is like living life—only with integrity can you stand firm.

Two, Sources of Goods:

How to find low-cost sources of goods?

1. Be a Market Hunter:

Closely monitor market changes and take advantage of product discounts to find low-cost sources of goods. For example, sellers often find high-quality brand-name clothes during seasonal changes or clearance sales, reselling them online and earning sufficient profits through regional or time differences.

2. Focus on Foreign Trade Products:

If familiar with foreign trade manufacturers, you can directly source from the factory. In leftover foreign trade orders, there are many good items, mostly only 1-3 pieces, often next year's or current trends, priced at 40-70% off mall prices, making them very marketable.

3. Purchase Brand Surplus Inventory:

Some brands have a lot of surplus inventory, and some merchants sell all their inventory to dedicated online sellers. If you have strong bargaining skills and can buy their inventory at low prices, you can earn substantial profits.

4. Obtain Overseas Discounted Goods:

World-renowned brands overseas offer very cheap prices before seasons change or holidays begin. If sellers have relatives or friends abroad, they can ask them for help to obtain attractive discounts for online sales, even at 40-70% off traditional mall prices, still leaving 10-40% profit margins.

5. Wholesale Goods:

Visit regional wholesale markets frequently, such as Xizhimen, Xiushui Street, Hongqiao in Beijing, Xiangyang Road, and Chenghuang Temple in Shanghai. Not only will you familiarize yourself with the market, but you'll also get very cheap wholesale prices.

After finding sources, you can test-sell a small amount of goods. If sales are good, consider increasing your order volume. Some sellers have good relationships with suppliers and only purchase goods after they've been sold, avoiding capital tie-ups and overstocking.

In short, regardless of how you find sources, low prices are the critical factor. Finding high-quality, low-priced goods lays the foundation for your online store's success.

Three, Product Naming and After-Sales Service:

1. Give a Good Name:

Product names should concisely summarize the product's characteristics, aiming for standardization so that people can roughly understand the basic information at a glance, and they can be easily found via search engines.

The general format is: Brand + Product Name + Specification + Description

For example, "15ml of Lancome Oil-Free Radiance Nutrient Eye Cream" should minimally be named: Lancome (Brand) + Radiance Nutrient Eye Cream (Product Name) + 15ml (Specification) + (Oil-Free Type) Description, i.e., Lancome Radiance Nutrient Eye Cream 15ml (Oil-Free Type).

Avoid using various so-called "personalized" symbols in product names, such as 【】●★▲■. First, it appears unprofessional, like a street vendor, significantly reducing trust. Second, some symbols may prevent the product name from displaying correctly.

2. Pictures Decide First Impressions:

Product pictures are your customers' first impression. Blurry, unclear pictures give a very poor impression, like a dirty face that doesn't attract attention. Pictures can be sourced from online searches; most manufacturers have their own websites where you can select product images from their introductions. Alternatively, you can scan product manuals for clear images. If neither works, it's best to hire someone with good photography skills to take pictures, then edit them with image processing software for good results. If you spend a few weekends learning tools like Photoshop to beautify images before posting, it would be even better.

3. Smile and Act Quickly:

Finally, someone likes your item and paid for it despite the difficulty of opening their wallet. Without question, you should handle the order as quickly and effectively as possible while providing excellent customer service. Moreover, they might bring unexpected benefits: word-of-mouth promotion is powerful and doesn't require persuading others. A single sincere effort can yield long-term returns.

4. Build Relationships:

For customers who have purchased from you, you can regularly follow up. For example, soon after shipping, ask if they received the item; a month later, ask if they're satisfied; two months later, ask for suggestions or if they need other items... Let customers feel valued and cultivate their purchasing habits. Once they get used to buying from you, you've gained a free advertiser.
