Plot Introduction:

The towering Yimeng Mountains stand as a monument to selfless dedication, and the rushing Yi River tells endless stories of the Yimeng children's unwavering loyalty to the Party.

In 1947, on the eve of the Menglianggu Campaign, Yan Village in Yimeng Mountain held a special wedding where Sister Chunying held a rooster in place of her soldier brother-in-law to complete the marriage ceremony with Yuefen. President Lanhu of the Women's Rescue Association led the only women and elders left in the village on a difficult task ahead: making five thousand catties of baked pancakes within two or three days, collecting five thousand catties of horse fodder, and sewing two hundred pairs of military shoes. Chunying’s family stayed up two whole nights baking pancakes; the child bride Heyan was beaten by her mother-in-law for making military shoes; Xiuxiu's brother Dashuang from the Sixth Column took leave to visit his sick mother but ended up being locked in the cellar by his father's scheme. Heyan gathered Xiaohè, Xiùxiù, Yuèfēn, and they devised a "lure-the-tiger-away-from-the-mountain" plan to rescue Dashuang by setting fire to their own barns...

As war intensified, new front-line support tasks were assigned, but public grain could not be delivered immediately. So, the villagers began gathering food from all around. Yuefen traveled dozens of miles over mountain roads to borrow grain from her parents' home, just narrowly missing her husband who was heading to the front line—a regret she would carry for life...

The Menglianggu Campaign began, and Lanhu led the sisters' front-line support team to the battlefield. They braved gunfire to rescue wounded soldiers; they risked enemy artillery fire to build a "frontline bridge" with their frail shoulders in the icy river water, enabling the troops to promptly reach the battlefield...

The Red Sisters of Yimeng demonstrated their bravery, resilience, and selfless dedication, interpreting the moving Yimeng Spirit of China.
