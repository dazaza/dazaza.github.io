Today, at the IT Teahouse, I saw a diagram called "The Big Picture of SEO." In my response, I simply wrote five words: "Very good, very powerful." Afterward, I started to worry a little. I was concerned that many friends who see this diagram might treat it as a formula or rule to learn and apply. Lonmen thinks that the author of this diagram probably wouldn't want to see such a result either! SEO techniques are definitely the product of development combined with practice, not something rigid and unchanging. When learning from the experiences of predecessors, one must not blindly inherit but instead think more and innovate based on practical experience.

The above statement may seem like empty talk, so let's discuss a specific case. If I were to say that search engines are not very friendly towards dynamic URLs and that URL staticization is very important for SEO, probably few friends would object. Looking at the current trend of URL staticization on the internet, it can be described as becoming increasingly intense. Some webmasters even go through great trouble (though I'm not saying all URL staticization is done for search engines). But what is the actual situation?
