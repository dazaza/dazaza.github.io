The Initial Encounter, the Final Sigh

My initial yearning for love came from a song, "Approximately in Winter." In this song, Qi Qin abandoned his carefree ways, sorrowfully and delicately narrating his love and melancholy. I knew he was singing this song for that person, and that woman was Joey Wong. I was amazed by his devotion, and I envied her happiness. That was my initial yearning for love; I never expected that years later, it would only result in a sigh!

I always thought that after enduring so many storms, they would surely hold each other's hands and grow old together. But as time passed, appearances changed, and the unpredictability of love was beyond our imagination. They eventually parted ways. Although he said they were still friends, I couldn't believe it. When the initial love turns into the final wound, how many people can remain calm? And how many people know about the scars on one’s body? There is no right or wrong in love, but only oneself knows the pain.

When the excitement of the first encounter is worn down by the trials of life, and when the final outcome is separation, even the most beautiful things become broken. Saying one doesn’t care is false; only the heart knows what it truly feels. I once thought Joey Wong didn’t deserve such an ending. She held onto that love with all her might, yet she never expected that time would be her greatest enemy. The most devoted person let go of her hand with indifference, and the fairy tale of love became like Cinderella's glass slipper—worthless after the clock strikes midnight.

Every time I watch Joey Wong's "A Chinese Ghost Story" on my computer over and over again, I always feel an indescribable sadness. Like Xiaoxian, she gave everything, risking her life for love, but ultimately did not receive a perfect ending. Sometimes, having expectations isn't always a good thing. Courage is one aspect, but whether there is luck to reach the end is the key! In matters of love, Joey Wong lost, completely and utterly.

Now, times have changed, and without Joey Wong, Qi Qin's life remains colorful. But every time I see news about Qi Qin, I always think of Joey Wong first, and remember that song "Approximately in Winter."

Do not assume that you can bet on the future with love, because doing so will leave you battered and bruised. Time flows like a song, and the romantic moments in movies eventually become illusory like flowers in a mirror or moonlight on water in real life. One can always yearn for love, but must also constantly remind oneself: no one belongs to anyone forever, and only time can give you the best answer.

At the initial encounter, the excitement and thrill you possess may, in the end, only bring you a sigh. Time is the best teacher, teaching the deepest lessons. Exchanging expectations for the future is always a losing proposition!
