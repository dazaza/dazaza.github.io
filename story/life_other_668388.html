Recalling the Wasted Years

Many years ago, I attended the Second High School adjacent to the Confucius Temple.

Back then, I didn't have any mature ideals to imagine or look forward to. I just went to school and came home mechanically, chatting and laughing with my good friends along the way, enjoying the sound of the wind as I rode my bike with my eyes closed.

On a night when there was a power outage, we were turning a corner happily when we were drawn by a group of onlookers shouting in tension. We saw a pool of red blood, white brain matter, and a twisted bicycle. The lights from passing vehicles shone on the ground and reflected onto our faces, making each of us look terrifyingly scared. On the way home, we were all silent, heads drooping.

Later, I don't know how remorseful the driver who caused the accident was. But anyway, whenever we students saw those big trucks dragging long trailers, a sense of fear arose within us. Every day when we passed that street where the bloody scene occurred that night, we always felt uneasy and our hearts beat faster.

Even after many years, I still strongly dislike the darkness of the night.

That momentary memory was etched into my mind at the age of fifteen, leaving me very dispirited. As a result, I became crazily obsessed with Zhao Chuan's song "I Am a Little Bird."

"Sometimes I feel like I am a little bird, wanting to fly but never able to soar high. Maybe one day when I perch on a branch, I become the target of hunters. When I fly up to the blue sky, I realize I have no support. Every time it gets quiet at night, I can't fall asleep. I wonder if only my tomorrow won't get better. Who knows what the future will be like? Is happiness merely a legend, something I can never find..."

The little bird carried away my prayers and stole my dreams. My heart was in turmoil, feeling lost and confused.

Because of Qiong Yao's verbosity and clamor, compared to other classes, the literature class students had an extra touch of inappropriateness, pretending to be tender and overly sentimental, stopping themselves from speaking out their true feelings. Thus, amidst the smell of sweaty feet and body odor that filled the classroom, there was now some unseen ambiguity and perfume scents. Occasionally, in a corner, the melancholy and cry of "I am ugly, but gentle" could be heard.

Under the distant sea breeze, the young men and women under the night sky gradually fell into decadence, continuing their ways even as the college entrance exam drew near, making life unbearably suffocating. They remained stubbornly stuck in their lazy habits.

The three years passed in despair and emptiness. Unable to distinguish the saltiness of tears, unable to judge the level of being carefree, and unable to face the fullness of youth, all they gained was a cold, drifting sense of melancholy.

To conclude this period of memories, I'll borrow Dai Wangshu's words: "Flipped open a blank page, Closed a blank page."
