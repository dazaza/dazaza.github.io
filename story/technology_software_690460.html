Henan Business Daily reported (reporter Tong Cunrui / writer Wang Chunsheng / photographer) that you may have heard of cases where license plates are stolen and the car owner is asked to pay money to buy it back. Yesterday, a similar situation occurred in Zhengzhou, but what was stolen were not the license plates, but the rearview mirror lenses.

Yesterday morning, when Mr. Zhang prepared to go to work, he found that the two rearview mirror lenses on his BMW parked near the intersection of Shunhe Road and Gongyi Street were missing. There was also a note left asking the car owner to redeem the lenses. On the other side of the intersection, another BMW sedan met the same fate.

At around 10 a.m. yesterday, I rushed to the scene, where the mirror lenses in front of these two cars' frames had disappeared, with two threads exposed outside. In Mr. Zhang's hand, there was a note saying "redeem lenses," along with a phone number. While I was interviewing, patrol members of the Renmin Road patrol team discovered that at the intersection of Gong'er Street and Gongyi Street heading west, the rearview mirror lenses of a Mercedes-Benz car were also stolen. There was also a note left on the car asking for money to redeem the lenses.

After the rearview mirror lenses were stolen, several car owners reported the incident to the police and attempted to communicate with the number on the note. The driver of Mr. Zhang's BMW received a message from this number: "Your lenses are useless to us. After you transfer the money, we'll tell you where the stuff is hidden, and you can take it and install it yourself." They demanded 1500 yuan for the two lenses, no bargaining allowed.

The Mercedes-Benz car owner said that after contacting the number on the note, they were told that the two lenses would cost 2000 yuan. Several car owners whose mirrors were stolen claimed that the price quote for two lenses from the BMW's 4S store was 2400 yuan, while the lenses for the Mercedes-Benz would cost 7300 yuan.

Regarding the stolen rearview mirror lenses, Mr. Zhang stated that he hasn't paid the thief yet. The Mercedes-Benz car owner said that if he pays this time, he might be extorted again in the future, but without the lenses, driving is very inconvenient.
