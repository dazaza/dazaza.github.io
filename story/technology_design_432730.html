Longwen School and the World Cup: Letting Children Experience the Beauty of Striving

Passion-filled June brings with it the college entrance exam (Gaokao) and high school entrance exam (Zhongkao), events that captivate the hearts of millions. The World Cup ignites passion in June, as a sport that stirs the emotions of people worldwide begins. This year in our country, nearly 100 million people are taking part in the Gaokao. Even if you're not a candidate, you should know that the fervor of the Gaokao is enough to set alight men and women, young and old across the nation. After the Gaokao, comes the heart-pounding FIFA World Cup, intensifying the heat of June and capturing the attention of football enthusiasts around the globe.

As the South Africa World Cup kicks off, many parents exhibit mixed feelings — joy at being able to fully enjoy the thrilling matches of the World Cup, and worry about whether their children, who may also be "football fans," should watch the games together. How can parents guide their children in watching these matches? Will watching football games affect their academic performance? These questions indeed present a very real and urgent issue for many parents. The World Cup happens only once every four years; it's a global event that excites and even drives people to madness. Personally, I believe that as future citizens, it is necessary for children to care about this global event. Moreover, if parents guide them appropriately, the once-in-four-years World Cup can become a rare and extremely valuable educational resource in family education.

The World Cup allows children to experience firsthand the beauty of striving. The matches on the World Cup stage are contests between world football powerhouses. While watching football games, children not only get to admire their favorite football stars' impressive ball control, passing, and goal-scoring moments but also feel the spirit of hard work and perseverance embodied by the players.

The World Cup enables children to learn abundant extracurricular knowledge. Since the start of the South Africa World Cup, children have been more curious about various aspects, such as the situation in South Africa, what the mascot of the South Africa World Cup is, the specifications of the official match ball for this World Cup, and the theme song of the South Africa World Cup, among others. Some of this information might come from conversations with parents, some from TV news, and some from online searches. Every four years, the World Cup, due to its interest to children, prompts them to use all the information-gathering methods they already know. In practice, they enhance their ability to collect and process information, acquiring knowledge that cannot be learned from textbooks while enjoying the process.

However, from a parent’s perspective, a rare holiday is still the best time for children to catch up on their studies. To address this, Longwen Experimental School has introduced its 2010 vibrant summer "6+1" efficient learning method with great emphasis.

**Longwen Experimental School 2010 Vibrant Summer "6+1" Efficient Learning Method Teaching Content**

**"6+1" Red List Retake Training Camp Class Settings**

1. **Summer Preparatory Classes (Optimization Classes):** Admissions are based on students scoring below 400 points in the 2010 Zhongkao or Gaokao exams or those without Zhongkao or Gaokao results. Students will take an exam after participating in the preparatory class, and classes will be divided according to their final exam scores and Zhongkao/Gaokao results. Comprehensive arrangements and systematic teaching plans will be made accordingly.

2. **High Scoring Elite Class:** Admission is for candidates scoring 30-50 points below the key high school or university admission line in 2010. The 2011 Zhongkao/Gaokao goal is to enter renowned key high schools or universities domestically, or top-tier universities like Peking University or Tsinghua University.

3. **Arts and Sports Class:** Starting from foundational knowledge, intensive training is provided, and personalized one-on-one teaching plans are developed based on each art student’s individual circumstances. **Teaching Plan:** An innovative "6+1" efficient learning method tailored to precise learning diagnostics, enabling targeted measures to promote student growth. Based on the actual conditions of retake students for Zhongkao and Gaokao, expert advisors and mentors participate in classroom listening, teaching research activities, offering scientific evaluations, guidance, and suggestions regarding teaching goals, content selection and arrangement, teaching strategies, and the implementation of new curriculum reforms.

**Teaching Methods:**

1. **Layered Teaching:** Classes are divided based on Zhongkao and Gaokao scores, with comprehensive, systematic, and highly targeted teaching solutions ensuring true individualized "one-on-one" instruction.

2. **Full-Time Classes:** Divided into boarding and day classes. Classes are held five days a week with alternating schedules, including morning and evening study sessions to ensure ample study time.

**Head Teacher's Enhanced Services:**

1. Each class has a dedicated head teacher focusing on comprehensive student management. Orientation education and quality analysis of each phase are regularly conducted, along with student-parent meetings.

2. A student record file is established for each student, documenting various pieces of information, including tracking records of the entire learning and living process.
