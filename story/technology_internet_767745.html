Jack Ma will close his WeChat account, and said thank you uncles and aunts for using Laiwang instead

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:19:33

A friend of Jack Ma revealed that Ma posted a message on his account today. The text was drafted in 15 minutes on the plane, and it is still related to "Laiwang", the interactive platform for friends that Alibaba has been promoting recently.

"Dear sisters, brothers, uncles, aunts, and all the people related to WeChat: My connection with WeChat has come to an end. In three days, I will close my account. Thank you, WeChat, for giving me so much joy and inspiration. But for the sake of our child 'Laiwang', I have to feed him. I know many people have told me that this child has no characteristics, was born at the wrong time, and cannot grow up big. Even if he grows up, he won't be as successful as WeChat. (We know, we hear. We don't expect this child to be as successful as WeChat, but we expect him to be more ambitious, individualistic, and studious. He may have a gap compared to his elder brother today, but he doesn't need to surpass his brother. Instead, he needs to create his own future. We believe that the world needs more than one child because we all know the troubles brought by single-child families."

"Dear friends, I also need to strive for the year-end red envelope. I need at least the help of 200 uncles, aunts, and other relatives. I originally thought having WeChat as a child would be enough, but now I realize how beautiful and interesting it is to have two children. By the way, this child likes making friends and can build a group of 500 people, which is like me. Hahaha"

"Brothers and sisters-in-law, after three days, if you have any matters, find me on 'Laiwang' and take a look at the child. Your care will make him understand the meaning of persistence better, especially the confidence to challenge the successful older brother. Tao thanks the uncles, aunts, and everyone else on behalf of the owner of the 'Laiwang' child. Hahaha... By the way, you can add me on Laiwang by adding my phone number. Hehe."

It is reported that on September 23rd, Alibaba officially launched its mobile interactive platform "Laiwang". Besides basic communication functions such as voice and text, Laiwang aims to establish a social platform for familiar people to communicate, exchange, and share. It supports chat groups of up to 500 people, sound pictures, and a "disappearing messages" function that allows users to erase private conversations after reading them.

Two days ago, Jack Ma publicly expressed his stance on Laiwang in the company's internal network, calling on Alibaba employees to become promoters of the Laiwang product. He compared Laiwang's challenge to WeChat as a "war," stating that the greatest pleasure lies in helping it grow and improve, demonstrating his determination to expand Laiwang. Ma also stated that Laiwang's "biggest feature is the spirit of over ten thousand employees who refuse to lose," and encouraged Ali people to "use the spirit of Yu Gong to challenge X-signal." He also issued a strict order - by the end of November, if an Ali person does not have at least 100 external users on Laiwang, it will be considered as giving up the red envelope. "Of course, having more than 100 does not guarantee a red envelope, but without 100, there is definitely no red envelope."

The Ali Group internally places unprecedented emphasis on the mobile social platform "Laiwang," with rarely seen senior executives like Ma, Lu Zhoushi, and Peng Lei becoming active and approachable on Laiwang. Last Friday, Lu Zhoushi, CEO of Alibaba Group, personally mobilized all managers across the company, requiring the entire group to "ALLIN Wireless." Any product or application must prioritize wireless applications. He emphasized that Laiwang is a "battle that must be fought."

Since two days ago, Laiwang's activity level has increased by at least 300%, with a significant increase in the number of voice communications and dynamic information posted daily by users. Decoration teams, taxi fleets, old classmates not seen for years have all started using Laiwang. Yesterday, the famous Hangzhou V-taxi fleet also opened a Laiwang group, reaching 500 members within three to four hours. Some smart catering stores, headhunters, car rental companies, Taobao merchants, and other external users have also joined. Under this offensive, Laiwang once again topped the Apple APPSTORE list of free social app downloads yesterday.
