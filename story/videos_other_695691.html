"The Difference Between Likability and Love"

by atijvnzd04 on 2012-03-07 16:54:22

Bin: Wen Fang -- Senior High School Teacher, Director of the Psychology Research Office at Beijing's Xu Beihong High School; Member of the Municipal Education Association's Educational Psychology Research Committee; District Psychological Education Professional Committee Member and Secretary; Director of Youth Health Center.

Host: Hello, netizens. Welcome to our reproductive health live broadcast as scheduled. Sitting next to me is Mr. Wen Fang, Senior High School Teacher and Director of the Psychology Research Office at Beijing's Xu Beihong High School. Today, we will be discussing the difference between affection and love. What are your initial thoughts about today's topic?

Wen Fang: I often hear young friends say in consultations, "I love this one, I love that one," whether it's unrequited infatuation or persistent pursuit. In reality, what these friends call 'love' is actually liking, not yet reaching the level of love. Liking and loving are two different things. You can't just say you love something because you like it. Young people, in their social interactions, encounter many individuals. It’s not only the young but also those older or younger, male or female. During these interactions, emotions naturally arise, and feelings inevitably influence emotional changes. Everyone has the ability to discover beauty.

Host: GuestA VGSiA: Can there be true friendship between men and women? How can we maintain friendship without turning it into love?

Wen Fang: The first question is definitely yes, and such friendships are common in society. While interacting with both same-sex and opposite-sex individuals, feelings naturally arise – a willingness or unwillingness to interact. This leads to some people you prefer to chat with, while others you may avoid entirely. These preferences determine who you want to be friends with. At the liking stage, the connection is shallow and lacks responsibility. However, when entering a romantic relationship, responsibility becomes crucial since love is exclusive and reciprocal. If one party acts irresponsibly, it could lead to moral condemnation from society. Therefore, mutual responsibility is necessary once a romantic relationship is established.

Host: How should girls handle such situations?

Wen Fang: Friendships form the foundation for romantic relationships. Similar to choosing an apple in a store, you wouldn’t immediately pick one upon entering. Instead, you observe, compare, and select the best one. During social interactions, you encounter various individuals with strengths and weaknesses. Through appreciation and selection, you learn whom to befriend and whom to avoid. Once you’ve developed the ability to choose wisely, you can then consider selecting your one true partner. Unlike casual friendships, romantic relationships involve deeper commitment and responsibility. Prematurely ending a romantic relationship requires explanation, whereas ending a friendship does not. Therefore, it’s important to distinguish between friendship and love.

Host: How can a girl prevent pregnancy if she wants to have sexual relations with her boyfriend?

Wen Fang: When in a romantic relationship, it’s best to ensure mutual commitment before considering physical intimacy. Emotional impulsiveness should not rush physical decisions.

Host: Guesta71hxW: Why do many boys pride themselves on having sexual relations with multiple women?

Wen Fang: Most boys don’t act this way. Having multiple sexual partners increases the risk of contracting diseases like AIDS or sexually transmitted infections (STIs). Such behavior is not only irresponsible towards others but also harmful to oneself.

Host: Guesta71hxW: If two people find sexual compatibility after having intercourse, could this develop into love?

Wen Fang: Compatibility should ideally be tested after marriage. As long as communication is strong, most couples can achieve compatibility.

Host: GuestY64nru: By sophomore year, most students seem to have paired up, sometimes with sex as a support point. Is this kind of relationship reliable?

Wen Fang: By sophomore year, students have gained some understanding of each other, leading to selective friendships. However, tying oneself too early to one person might hinder future growth. Emotional impulsiveness leading to premature sexual relations may indicate a lack of trustworthiness.

Host: Guesta71hxW: If a married couple loves each other but struggles with communication, what should they do?

Wen Fang: Legal marriage doesn't equate to automatic satisfaction. Emotional connections require continuous nurturing through communication. Expressing feelings correctly allows for better interaction, whether in household chores or intimate relations.

Host: GuestBHvqJX: Can love last a lifetime?

Wen Fang: Like eating, initially enjoyable experiences can become monotonous over time. To sustain happiness, add humor and harmony to family life. Create excitement and maintain understanding during misunderstandings.

Host: Guest4QKtDX: Your examples don't work in real life. After three years apart, even married couples lose touch. How can we expect teenagers to maintain feelings?

Wen Fang: Love tests rationality. Many couples remain together despite long separations due to work. For teenagers, maintaining feelings is rare.

Host: Shangxin Xiaocao: Is a menstrual cycle of 35-38 days normal? How do I measure basal body temperature, especially with night shifts? Does daily intercourse affect pregnancy?

Wen Fang: Measure basal body temperature every morning on an empty stomach. After several months, identify patterns to pinpoint ovulation day, which correlates with temperature changes.

Host: Guest4QKtDX: Many teenagers already have sexual experience. Your approach seems overly rationalized, assuming all love leads to commitment. Lack of communication is the biggest challenge. Without dialogue, how can couples face difficulties together?

Wen Fang: Love and sex are separate. Sexual activity doesn't necessarily equate to love. Adding rationality to emotional decisions enhances life's completeness. Purely emotional actions lead to confusion. We encourage young people to balance emotion with reason.

Wen Fang: Excessive rationality also isn't ideal. A balance between emotion and reason ensures a responsible and fulfilling life.

Host: Due to time constraints, please conclude today's discussion on the differences between affection and love in a few sentences.

Wen Fang: I hope our young friends learn to handle life's events rationally, leading to happy and fulfilling lives.
