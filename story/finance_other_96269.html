Xu Lu is a college student at a university in Beijing. In June 2003, the school organized a cultural performance, and Xu Lu, along with several other female students, choreographed a dance to participate in the show. Just before the curtain call, someone played a prank by soaking one of the girls' white chiffon skirts. This made the girl extremely embarrassed because it was summer, and her thin clothing revealed her private parts entirely when wet. Especially visible were the transparent areas around her chest. Later, this girl cried all day back in the dormitory.

After this incident, Xu Lu noticed a problem: with the growing popularity of strapless dresses and backless outfits, more and more female students were choosing lightweight chiffon and lace bras—not only fashionable but also breathable. Moreover, many of them had intricate lace patterns that added a sense of mystery. However, these bras had a defect: they easily became see-through when wet.

In early July, while Xu Lu was looking through gifts her sister (who worked as a flight attendant) brought back from abroad, she discovered something resembling a bra but without straps or clasps. Curious, Xu Lu asked her sister what it was. Her sister explained that it was a decorative nipple cover specifically designed to prevent exposure and was very popular overseas. If one needed to wear a backless outfit or a strapless dress, this "equipment" was essential. Then her sister demonstrated how to use it—just peel off the adhesive layer, gently rotate it, and stick it on; it was very convenient and could avoid many embarrassing situations.

Back at school, Xu Lu's roommates were also interested in her new discovery. After a frenzy of inquiries and "purchases," all the nipple covers Xu Lu had brought were sold out. Some female students from other departments even came to her dormitory to place orders. From this enthusiastic demand, Xu Lu saw a business opportunity and began planning to make money from this small accessory.

Subsequently, she asked her sister to contact suppliers abroad and found a lingerie store near the school where she requested a shared rental space. Since nipple covers are disposable products, after a few uses, the adhesive layer loses its stickiness, so there were many repeat customers. These included university students from other schools and even some male students purchasing for their girlfriends. Xu Lu's business outside the campus was thriving.

When Xu Lu happily counted her pure income of over 4,000 yuan in August and September, her boyfriend reminded her that the weather would soon turn cold, and this seasonal product might lose its market. As Xu Lu hesitated about whether to give up her shared rental space, she received a call from the business manager of a five-star hotel. The manager proactively contacted Xu Lu, wanting to resell her product. The hotel's clientele mostly consisted of female white-collar workers who were very interested in this type of decorative nipple cover. The business manager offered favorable conditions—giving Xu Lu a 10% rebate, which could increase to 15% if sales increased. All Xu Lu had to do was supply the goods, and payments would be settled monthly.

Not affecting her studies, yet having a steady income every month, this was truly a great opportunity. Xu Lu asked her sister to continue importing goods and requested to select varieties from several countries. She then supplied these goods to the hotel. As expected by the business manager, the sales were excellent—many white-collar women purchased dozens of pairs at once. Xu Lu found that products from South Korea, the United States, and France were very popular due to their good texture, wide range of colors, realistic appearance, good breathability, and high-end packaging. The demand far exceeded that of the initial Thai products, making them perfect for the main white-collar market. As for the price of forty to fifty yuan, white-collar women could easily afford it.

Through casual conversations with customers, Xu Lu gradually identified market hotspots: white-collar workers preferred dignified flesh tones, light gray, and black, liking realistic, thick, and sensual nipple covers; whereas fashion-conscious young women liked colorful yellow, pink, blue-purple, and lace-patterned ones, emphasizing invisibility and preferring no-trace nipple covers. Therefore, after receiving the goods, Xu Lu placed the cheaper, colorful, and patterned items for sale at stores near the school, while putting higher-end, more refined items with better packaging at the hotel, complete with English instructions [originally published in the Science Investment magazine, Issue 7, 2004, the best practical guidance magazine for personal entrepreneurship, investment, finance, and small-medium enterprise operations].

Just as Xu Lu rejoiced over her accidentally discovered business opportunity, complaints started coming in. It turned out that some women developed small red rashes on their breasts after wearing the nipple covers, possibly due to skin sensitivity caused by the adhesive, which affected sales to some extent. This problem troubled Xu Lu for a while. Eventually, she decided to self-fund a trip to South Korea to investigate the local women's underwear market. She discovered that foreign underwear markets were very comprehensive, and the most prominent feature was their universal health massage function. She brought back several types of health and medicinal nipple covers and also bought many promotional materials about nipple covers from abroad.

Upon returning, the store reported that some forward-thinking female customers even proposed customizing patterns on-site. Xu Lu realized that in today's era of body painting and body art, this customer request indicated a huge market potential. Thus, she asked her design-major boyfriend to hand-draw some abstract patterns.

Indeed, her boyfriend’s artistic designs received great praise from customers. As the weather gradually warmed up again, Xu Lu added health, massage, and nipple-cover painting services to her shop, making it busier than ever. While others were busy printing resumes and contacting potential employers, Xu Lu set her sights on the lingerie counters of major shopping malls. She tirelessly negotiated with mall managers. After nearly two months of “flying solo,” most of the lingerie counters in the malls stocked her nipple cover products. In just nine months, she earned 120,000 yuan. With this money, Xu Lu felt reassured: “Rather than blindly seeking job opportunities like my classmates, I’d rather focus on developing the marketable products I have.”

Xu Lu enthusiastically shuttled between various malls, hotels, and foreign enterprises, then officially opened a bustling store in a busy area to operate her "Charming Nipple Covers," naming it "E Tribe Girl." During these months, the busy Xu Lu gained more than just the enviable cash admired by her classmates; more importantly, she interacted with people from all walks of life, accumulating much social experience. One day in September 2003, while explaining in English to a white-collar customer how to maintain the adhesion of the nipple covers (do not wash with water, but wipe lightly with alcohol), the lady stared at Xu Lu for a long time before inviting her: “Your English is really good. Would you like to work part-time as an English editor on our website?” Thus, Xu Lu not only found an enviable job but also utilized the internet advantage to look up trends and prospects of nipple covers on foreign websites.

Some data predicts that in the coming years, nipple covers and bras will equally divide the market and each lead the trend. With this, Xu Lu’s confidence grew stronger. She not only hired two assistants but also involved her boyfriend, assigning him to develop personalized nipple cover accessories, focusing on enhancing their aesthetic appeal. She believed that future products should emphasize the combination of practicality and beauty.

Interestingly, Xu Lu never ran any advertisements, yet her business remained prosperous. Xu Lu analyzed, "This is still the fault of fashion." Apparently, avant-garde products always hold allure for those chasing fashion trends.

Xu Lu really likes the name "E Tribe Girl" for her store. She said she wanted to make her jewelry store akin to the internet—limitless communication, limitless expansion, and boundless imagination. She also mentioned her next step would be to research a non-harmful adhesive nipple cover to ensure women can use them more safely and confidently. Additionally, she plans to develop zodiac-themed nipple covers, meaning the shapes could be plum blossoms, peonies, or cute little dogs and rabbits... In late April 2004, Xu Lu began preparing to open branch stores and planned to expand her business to major cities nationwide. Isn't it interesting and thought-provoking that this female college student, following the steps of fashion, has dug her first pot of gold?
