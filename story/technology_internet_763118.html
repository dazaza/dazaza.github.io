Google Chairman Schmidt Reported to Have Multiple Mistresses

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 15:32:50

On July 22nd, according to Beijing time, the UK's The Sunday Mail reported that Eric Schmidt, one of Google's triumvirate and its Executive Chairman, supports so-called "open marriage" and has multiple mistresses.

Although his hair is increasingly thinning and his face gradually showing signs of aging, this internet tycoon, who seemingly would have no scandals, has in recent years been romantically involved with several young women, including a cheerful television host, a long-legged blonde PR executive, and a Vietnamese pianist. Schmidt has also signed confidentiality agreements with at least one of his mistresses.

In the meantime, despite American media having reported multiple times about their "open marriage," Wendy, Schmidt's 57-year-old wife, remains in her seaside mansion on Nantucket Island, Massachusetts, focusing on her charitable endeavors.

The UK's another newspaper, The Daily Mail, commented: "He is the boss of Google and possesses a billion-dollar fortune, but he has been criticized for encouraging his company to aggressively avoid taxes in the UK and for not doing enough to combat child pornography. But today, The Sunday Mail has exposed another thing that the 58-year-old Google Executive Chairman Eric Schmidt has been trying hard to conceal: his private life filled with complicated romantic escapades, which could even rival Google's dazzling doodles."

Schmidt is 58 years old. In 2001, Google founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin hired Schmidt as the company’s CEO. During his decade-long tenure, Schmidt propelled Google from a star startup to become the world's most powerful internet giant. In January 2011, Schmidt stepped down as CEO, succeeded by Larry Page. Currently, Schmidt serves as Google's Executive Chairman. His net worth amounts to £5.4 billion.

It should be noted that The Sunday Mail is a tabloid in the UK, although it has a large circulation, its content mainly focuses on gossip news, and whether it is reliable is worth discerning.

Promising Divorce to Female Host

Television host Kate Bohner, aged 46, maintained a three-year affair with the married billionaire between 2007 and 2010.

Kate Bohner's friend, journalist Jason Parsley, revealed to The Sunday Mail that Bohner deeply loved Schmidt, and they were "true love." He said: "They were true love. She adored Eric. This wasn't a one-night stand; they were in love for three years."

He added: "I am disclosing this because someone who talks casually about others' privacy uses enormous wealth to protect his own image. That is extremely ironic."

To live closer to Schmidt, Bohner moved to Los Angeles. Schmidt owns two houses nearby, one close to Google's Silicon Valley headquarters, and the other a £25 million (approximately $38 million) mansion in Montecito, California.

Bohner had already publicly expressed her feelings towards Schmidt, referring to him humorously as "Dr. Strangelove" in an anonymous blog under the pseudonym Recoverygirl007. Schmidt once gave her an iPhone prototype (the late co-founder of Apple, Steve Jobs, was Schmidt's friend), spent romantic holidays with her, and lavishly purchased expensive jewelry for her.

She also expressed her pain after their breakup, saying: "I kept my BlackBerry by my pillow all night, thinking 'Dr. Strangelove' might send me an email."

At the time of their affair, Schmidt was at the peak of his power. In 2001, he was hired by Google co-founders Sergey Brin and Larry Page as the company’s CEO, transforming the search engine into a global media powerhouse.

However, Google has faced repeated criticism for inadequately protecting users, especially in combating child pornography online. Additionally, he has been heavily criticized for helping Google evade taxes in the UK.

This week, Bohner did not respond to interview calls or emails, possibly due to signing a confidentiality agreement with Schmidt. However, Parsley insisted: "This was true love, at least she thought so."

"To my knowledge, Schmidt paid for her to see him. He repeatedly assured her he would divorce, and she cried countless times on my shoulder. She believed without reservation that he would divorce for her." Schmidt and Bohner once took a photo together at the 2007 'Burning Man Festival.'

To cope with sandstorms, the couple wore goggles and scarves. Schmidt loved his mistress dearly, but he refused to stay in the desert camp, instead driving five hours to the nearest hotel.

Parsley continued: "When they were apart, they often called and emailed each other. But since Schmidt always spent holidays with his family, it exhausted Kate. It was always like this—Thanksgiving, Christmas, all the holidays were the same."

"I am telling the truth because online comments are unfair to Kate. In any extramarital affair, women are always the ones blamed. People say: 'Oh, she knew he was married.'"

"But it's not that simple. Yes, Eric is married and still hasn't divorced. But he needs to reflect on himself and return to reality. Eric wants to philander; everyone knows how many affairs he has had." Parsley said.

Long-Legged Blonde PR Director

Like Bohner, Schmidt's other rumored lovers are middle-aged but possess charming looks and successful careers.

Marcey Simon is one of them. This long-legged blonde PR director is over 40 and established a relationship with Schmidt in Viéras, France, in 2006, remaining very close to him until now. He gifted her a large yellow diamond ring.

Gossip website Garker.com reported their breakup in December 2007 and claimed they reconciled in 2009. Allegedly, they engaged in a sexual relationship during conferences in Colorado and Aspen Ideas in California.

Schmidt also had an affair with Lisa Shields, a 47-year-old divorced single mother and producer of the top-rated morning show Good Morning America.

According to The New York Post, in 2011, Schmidt took Shields on vacations to the Caribbean and the French Riviera aboard his £47.3 million ($72.3 million) Oasis luxury yacht.

They had dinner together in Southampton, upstate New York, which is the billionaire's backyard. According to The New York Post, they also frequently spent summer nights together in the Hamptons.

An insider told The New York Post: "She told her friends they were dating, openly appearing together in New York state and the Hamptons."

Lisa Shields currently works in public relations at the Council on Foreign Relations in New York. When interviewed at her home in the Hamptons, she did not deny her relationship with Schmidt but added: "I never comment on my private life."

According to The New York Post, Schmidt's latest romance is with the wild Vietnamese pianist Chau-Giang Nguyen. They have been seen dining together in upscale restaurants in New York many times.

An insider said: "He has greatly helped her music career. Eric is becoming more interested in art."

Chau-Giang Nguyen declined to comment on her relationship with Schmidt. However, she was previously engaged to Hollywood producer Brian Grazer. Schmidt's wife Wendy did not comment on her marriage with Schmidt. They met in the late 1970s at UC Berkeley and got married in 1980.

In her only response, Wendy told The New York Times: "Some couples are together all the time, but we are both busy. I think these reports are nonsense. We know what our lives are like, and we are both happy. These scandals are part of being a public figure." Their two daughters, Sophie and Alison, remain close to their father.

She also added that if she had to follow her husband around the globe all day, she would get bored. "I would feel like luggage, and he doesn't want me to feel that way," she said.

When discussing the issue of "open marriage," she told The New York Times: "I think this question is inappropriate, and I will not comment on these rumors."

When critics argue that Google should pay more attention to protecting user privacy, Schmidt responded: "If you have something you don't want anyone to know, perhaps you shouldn't have done it in the first place." This statement seems particularly fitting when applied to himself.
