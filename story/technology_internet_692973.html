Revisions to the copyright law should be based on national conditions and keep pace with the times

by keyizhang1 on 2012-03-06 13:43:57

In the intellectual property legal system, copyright law is the most complex in terms of legal relationships, the richest in content, and the most frequently changing. It is also one of the laws that requires the strictest legislative techniques. Therefore, the third revision of the Copyright Law is particularly important and challenging.

Looking back at the history of revisions to China's Copyright Law, two features stand out: one is long-term inactivity. Since its establishment in 1990, it has only been revised twice, with each interval lasting a full decade; the other is relative passivity, as the first revision was mainly due to pressure from joining the WTO, and the second revision was based on the final ruling of the World Trade Organization. From the perspective of international reform trends and the overall development of our country, the third revision must adapt to new situations, making its significance especially crucial. The author believes that this revision should be grounded in the current national conditions of our country and guided by the following ideas, focusing on the following aspects.

Keeping pace with the times and establishing guiding principles

Based on the current national conditions of our country, the author believes that the revision of the Copyright Law should follow these guiding principles:

Firstly, balancing localization and internationalization. The third revision of the Copyright Law is taking place amidst the trend of intellectual property integration and internationalization. As a signatory to the Berne Convention and the WIPO Internet Treaties, we must adhere to the international obligations stipulated by international conventions. At the same time, the revision of our country's Copyright Law should also consider the development levels of our economy, science, technology, and culture.

Therefore, we propose balancing localization and internationalization: Emphasizing compliance with the minimum standards of international conventions at the international level, while also drawing on legislative experiences from developed countries; at the domestic level, grounding ourselves in reality to solve practical problems and promote economic and social development.

Secondly, the revision should keep up with the times. Copyright law is both a technical law and an industrial law. Therefore, the revision of our country's Copyright Law should promptly respond to the development trends of network technology, maintaining the timeliness and advancement of the law; it should also carefully consider the development needs of the copyright industry, promoting the great development and prosperity of socialist culture.

The author believes that the above principles can enable our copyright legislation to transition from being "forced to use" to "using for our benefit," stimulating the leapfrog development of our copyright industry, thereby achieving the strategic goal of building an innovative country.

Balancing modification and absorption, improving relevant regulations

This revision of the Copyright Law is a summary and enhancement of 20 years of China's copyright legislation and judicial experience, as well as a reflection of our copyright teaching and research achievements. The author believes that the revision can follow the following思路:

Modification and absorption should coexist. Based on the existing copyright system in our country, there are several options for revising the Copyright Law: one is the "one-to-three" plan, which involves fully incorporating several regulations related to the Copyright Law into the Copyright Law during this revision to unify the sources of the Copyright Law; another is the "one-plus-three" plan, which involves only modifying the articles within the Copyright Law while completely retaining the integrity of the related regulations to avoid excessive revision costs; the third is the "one-drag-three" plan, which involves absorbing relatively mature contents from related regulations that have been accepted by judicial practice into the Copyright Law, and then gradually revising and improving the related copyright regulations after the Copyright Law revision case is passed.

The author believes that this revision can adopt the "one-drag-three" revision plan, which includes a large number of modifications to the content of the Copyright Law itself, as well as absorbing relatively mature parts from related regulations. This plan balances the "revolutionary" and "prudent" aspects of the revision.

Adding work definitions, reconstructing right types

The purpose of legislating the Copyright Law in our country is to protect the legitimate rights and interests of copyright owners and promote the extensive dissemination of knowledge information. Based on this, the author believes that the revision can focus on the following aspects:

Firstly, scientifically defining the scope of works and adding work definitions. With the widespread application of new transmission technologies, new types of works continue to emerge. Relying solely on an enumerative legislative model is no longer sufficient to address the challenges brought by technological development. Clearly defining works will help clearly define the objects protected by copyright law, facilitate the extensive dissemination of cultural knowledge, and achieve the flourishing of culture and the development of industries.

Therefore, this revision should first add the concept of works, elevating the provisions regarding the definition of works in the Implementation Regulations of the Copyright Law, namely "[Works referred to in this law mean intellectual creative results in the fields of literature, art, and science, which are original and can be reproduced in some tangible form]," to legal articles. At the same time, add elements not protected by works, such as abstract thoughts, non-protectable practical factors like operational methods, practical solutions, and practical functions. This clause, combined with the aforementioned suggestions for revising the definition of works, provides a more accurate definition of works under copyright law from both positive and negative perspectives.

Secondly, adapting to the development of transmission technology and restructuring right types. On the one hand, based on the different attributes of personal rights and property rights in copyrights, a separate legislative model should be adopted, setting two parallel clauses to separately specify personal rights and property rights in copyrights. Personal rights and property rights in copyrights are two different types of rights, each with different legal attributes. From the perspective of right attributes, personal rights emphasize the protection of authors' spiritual interests, generally having no term limit (except for the right of publication), indivisibility, and non-deprivation. Property rights, on the other hand, focus on the economic value of the author, allowing transfer and inheritance, leading to significant differences in protection periods and utilization methods. Separate specifications are more appropriate.

On the other hand, the author believes that various types of rights in copyrights should be revised. This includes expanding the scope of reproduction rights to include the reproduction of works from two-dimensional to three-dimensional forms; introducing the principle of exhaustion of distribution rights to coordinate the conflict between copyright and ownership of the work carrier; expanding the scope of rental rights and introducing the rental right of works embodied in sound recordings; revising the definition of performance rights to include live performances (public performance of works) and mechanical performances (rights to publicly perform works using various means), aligning with international convention regulations and eliminating confusion in the rights system; introducing public communication rights to directly cover wired, wireless, interactive, and non-interactive communications, providing necessary legal support for information dissemination in the network era.

Improving right restrictions and increasing protection efforts

In addition to the key contents of the revision mentioned above, the author believes that the following areas should also be revised:

Firstly, reasonably drawing on international experience to improve the system of right restrictions. On the one hand, the determination of the scope of application of China's reasonable use system of copyright should include abstract judgment criteria on the basis of an enumerative legislative model, enabling the judiciary to have a unified standard for behaviors listed in the Copyright Law. Therefore, in this revision, the "three-step test" originating from the Berne Convention can be included as a consideration standard for determining fair use, and it is necessary to adjust and merge the restriction systems of traditional property rights in copyright and information network dissemination rights, so that the fair use clause applies to all property rights in copyright.

On the other hand, expand the scope of statutory licenses and add the statutory license system for "preparing courseware to implement nine-year compulsory education and national education plans." Combining the legislative purpose and principles of the Copyright Law, as well as the development of the statutory licensing system of copyright in the network environment, based on the public welfare nature of distance education and the characteristics of the network, including the preparation of courseware in the statutory license allows low-cost realization of network-based teaching methods such as distance education, thereby achieving a balance between the interests of authors and the public.

Secondly, responding to the needs of industrial development and constructing a related rights system. On the one hand, directly naming Chapter Four of the Copyright Law as "Related Rights," elevating Article 26 of the Implementation Regulations to the content of the Copyright Law can echo Chapter Two "Copyright" of the Copyright Law and unify the contents of each section of Chapter Four, making the Copyright Law system more logically coherent and identifiable.

On the other hand, improve the types of rights in related rights. Add performers' secondary performance rights. Many countries' copyright laws stipulate that radio and television stations should pay performers when broadcasting recordings of their performances. In our country's Copyright Law, only copyright holders enjoy broadcasting rights and mechanical performance rights, but performers do not have these rights, resulting in unbalanced protection of rights; improve broadcast organization rights. The main revisions should include clarifying the object of broadcast organization rights as program signals, adding the public communication rights of recorded radio and television programs, and adding public communication rights in commercial venues.

Furthermore, intensify the fight against infringement and enhance the level of rights protection. Given the characteristics of low cost and wide range of copyright infringement, it is necessary to increase the statutory compensation standards for infringement and introduce punitive damages. This revision can increase the maximum statutory compensation for infringing copyright behavior to 1 million yuan. Theoretically speaking, our country amended the Patent Law in 2008, raising the maximum statutory compensation for patent infringement to 1 million RMB, providing a realistic reference for adjusting the maximum statutory compensation for copyright infringement; analyzing from administrative law enforcement practices, copyright infringement phenomena remain very prominent at this stage, and it is necessary to adopt stricter legal responsibility mechanisms to curb the high incidence of infringement.

This revision can introduce a punitive damages system for copyright infringement. From foreign experiences, South Africa, Brazil, Russia, and the United States adopt punitive damages beyond losses in determining civil compensation amounts for copyright infringement; domestically, strengthening civil liability for copyright infringement aligns with the judicial policy of building an innovative country, increasing infringement costs, and intensifying the crackdown on infringement, as well as with the industrial policy of promoting the healthy development of the copyright industry and enhancing the contribution rate of the copyright industry to economic development.
