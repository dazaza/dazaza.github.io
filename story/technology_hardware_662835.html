Common Faults and Troubleshooting Methods for Insertion-type Electromagnetic Flow Meters

by zhihao2033 on 2012-02-10 16:41:18

Common faults and troubleshooting methods of insert-type electromagnetic flowmeter Fault one: No output current after power on. Cause: Power not connected. Troubleshooting method 1. Check if the fuse is blown 2. Check the power circuit wiring. Fault two: Zero output current (4mA) after power on Cause: 1. The sensor marking direction is opposite to the fluid flow direction. 2. The excitation power line of the converter is connected incorrectly. 3. The input signal lines E1 and E2 are reversed. Troubleshooting method: 1. Adjust the sensor direction to be consistent with the fluid direction. 2. Rewire according to the correct method. 3. Swap the signal lines E1 and E2. Fault three: Output signal exceeds 20mA after power on or during operation Cause: The flowmeter range is less than the actual measurement range Troubleshooting method: Reset the flowmeter range to make it reasonable Fault four: Excessive fluctuation of output signal Cause: 1. There is gas at the sensor electrode, causing poor contact between the electrode and the measured medium. 2. Accumulation on the electrode surface causes poor contact between the electrode and the medium. 3. Large actual fluctuations in the medium flow. 4. Poor grounding. Troubleshooting method: 1. Install correctly and eliminate the influence of gas in the pipeline. 2. Clean the electrode surface. 3. Increase the damping time of the instrument. Fault five: Output signal gradually decreases or becomes zero during operation Cause: 1. Water ingress in the sensor. 2. Poor insulation of the signal line. 3. Accumulation on the electrode surface reduces the signal acquisition capability of the electrode. Troubleshooting method: 1. Eliminate water ingress. 2. Replace the signal line. 3. Clean the electrode. 4. Improve the grounding method.
