Visit to Microsoft's Home of the Future: Touchscreens are everywhere

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 16:31:39

Not far from the office where executives work at Microsoft's headquarters, there is a special room that represents Microsoft's vision for the future of homes and offices. Harald Becker, Microsoft's senior product planner, took us on a tour of this special room. Usually, this room is not open to the public; only corporate clients can make appointments to visit. This is the gateway to Microsoft's future home.

Becker told us that this room is similar to a concept car, created by the research and development personnel of Microsoft's technology team, representing Microsoft's investment in future technology. Although it has not yet been realized, this room indeed demonstrates how interconnected and synchronized devices will change our lives and work. Microsoft's vision for the future includes family, work, entertainment, and more.

Becker briefly introduced Microsoft's future vision.

An office full of futuristic feel, with large open and leisure spaces.

A modern working environment equipped with a modern whiteboard.

Users can transfer the content on the whiteboard to a Surface tablet.

Giant touch screens are everywhere, which are products produced by Perceptive Pixel, a company acquired by Microsoft.

With just a flick, users can transfer the ongoing work on the Surface to a large screen.

On this large screen, users can drag and drop components to complete projects, look up information, and it is very easy to operate.

The giant screen in the modern meeting room.

Users can draw projects on the touch screen.

If users need to order a component required for a project, they can immediately bring up a video introduction of that component.

A simulated retail store of the future.

Choose a toy from the retail store and place it on the touch screen.

The touch screen will display information such as the price of the toy. If you want to buy it, you can use an NFC (Near Field Communication) enabled phone to complete the payment, then enter the mailing address on the screen, and the store will send it.

A motorcycle is placed in Microsoft's future home, but it is just for decoration; the company did not showcase future motorcycle technology here.

This showcases how retail stores will evolve.

A restaurant for receiving customers. We didn't dine here, so we don't know what technological elements were integrated into the restaurant.

Move forward.

Now we arrive at the home area, where Becker demonstrated scanning a pot using a Kinect camera, planning to print it out with a 3D printer at home.

Before printing, he reshaped the pot using motion control technology to make it slimmer.

As you can see, touch screens and projections play an important role in Microsoft's room of the future.

Now entering the kitchen, touch screens are still indispensable.

On this screen, users can search for recipes and cooking guides.

Following the cooking guide, start cooking.

Users can also complete cooking under the guidance of a virtual chef.

Digital picture frames are widely used to display works and photos.

Now we come to the living room, a place where Microsoft ambitiously wants to "occupy."

Video chatting, telling stories to children through the screen.

Children can choose small animals that appear in the story, then align them with the camera, and Microsoft's technology will recognize and add them to the story.

Story illustrations are synchronously displayed on the screen.

Children can use the Surface to take pictures of scenes in the story, then explore more information.

Projectors project appropriate leaves that can move around.

The visit to Microsoft's room of the future ends here.
