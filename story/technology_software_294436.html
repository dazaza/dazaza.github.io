The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you're buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. This order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to trade, the quantity, and the price at which you wish to buy or sell. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. When placing an order, the stock code and abbreviation must match. Additionally, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity for purchasing stocks must be a multiple of 100, whereas the quantity for selling stocks does not need to be a multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place orders: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
