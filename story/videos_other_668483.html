With the revamp of the CCTV News Channel, Hu Die, originally from the International Channel, has once again become a focal point of attention! Hu Die joined CCTV in 2007 after participating in its fifth Host Competition. At that time, Hu Die was already one of the top female anchors at Beijing Television, and many people were extremely optimistic about her future prospects, believing she would become the leading lady of BTV. However, just as her hosting career was flourishing, in 2007, CCTV's once-every-four-years host competition began. She didn't want to miss this opportunity to exercise and improve her overall quality, so she signed up for the competition. Unexpectedly, during the competition, she faced the toughest decision of her life. After winning the preliminary round, BTV told her to either withdraw from the competition or resign—she had to choose one. Some people encountering such a situation would have chosen to quit while they were ahead, opting to leave the competition. After all, there were still several more grueling ultimate showdowns ahead, and who would ultimately win the championship was still unknown! Moreover, in today's severe employment situation, finding an ideal job is no easy feat! However, Hu Die chose to continue with the competition to avoid leaving any regrets in her life. Despite her deep reluctance to lose her position, she did not drop out halfway but instead persisted until the end with strong perseverance and extraordinary courage, eventually winning the championship of the CCTV Host Competition final!

Previously, Hu Die hosted the programs "Today Asia" and "China News" on CCTV-4. Many viewers, after watching her broadcasts, greatly appreciated her hosting style, saying that her appearance on the CCTV screen was like a refreshing breeze in the hot summer, very invigorating! This shows how much her intimate, natural, grand, and steady broadcasting style is loved by the audience.

Now, Hu Die hosts "Morning News" on the news channel and has truly become famous worldwide!
