Attached: The correct method of brushing your teeth:

Talk about the correct method of brushing your teeth (in detail) - how many times a day should you brush? What is the appropriate duration?

Brushing your teeth is the main means of removing plaque and preventing periodontal disease.

Choose a well-designed toothbrush that is easy to turn inside the mouth and can remove food fragments from all parts of the teeth, fitting the shape of the dental arch and the size of the teeth. Generally, select nylon filament toothbrushes with elasticity, smoothness, and ease of cleaning such as rounded or ball-ended bristles. For adult toothbrushes, the brush head length is 25~35mm, width 8~12mm, bristle height 10~12mm, diameter 0.2~0.3mm, and no more than 4 rows of bristles. For children's toothbrushes, the brush head length does not exceed 28mm, width does not exceed 11mm, bristle height 9~10mm, diameter does not exceed 0.25mm, and no more than 3 rows of bristles.

Persisting with the correct brushing method can remove dental plaque and massage the gums. Healthy individuals generally brush their teeth 1~2 times per day, but those with gum or periodontal diseases should emphasize brushing in the morning and evening, and add another session after lunch, each lasting 3 minutes. The correct brushing methods are short horizontal brushing and vertical rotation brushing.

Short Horizontal Brushing Method (also known as the horizontal vibration method): People usually use a long sawing motion when brushing horizontally. This kind of horizontal brushing, combined with hard-bristled toothbrushes, can harm the gums (bristle damage to the gum edges, gum recession, wedge-shaped defects at the root neck, etc.). Short horizontal brushing with small vibrations most effectively cleans plaque, so it is also called the sulcus brushing method. Place the tip of the bristles at a 45° angle to the tooth-gum surface with slight pressure, allowing the tips of the bristles to enter the gingival sulcus, with some outside the sulcus, then make 6~8 forward and backward vibrations, with the bristles moving only 1mm. When brushing the lingual-palatal surfaces of the upper and lower front teeth, if the dental arch is narrow, the toothbrush head can be placed vertically for short horizontal brushing vibrations. Additionally, pull the brush across the posterior teeth occlusal surfaces to clean the pits and fissures of the occlusal surfaces.

Vertical Rotation Method: This effectively removes plaque and soft deposits and stimulates the keratinization of the gums, maintaining the normal shape of the gums. A wide range of toothbrushes can be selected, including hard and medium-hard bristle brushes, but elderly people, children, or those who have undergone periodontal surgery should choose medium-hard or soft bristle brushes. The action for brushing the labial-buccal surfaces and the posterior tooth lingual-palatal surfaces is to align the bristles parallel to the long axis of the teeth, pressing them against the tooth surface, directing the bristles toward the gumline, applying pressure, and rotating the toothbrush, making the bristles form a 45° angle with the long axis. Rotate the toothbrush, brushing the upper teeth by following the interdental spaces downward and brushing the lower teeth upward. Note that the movements should be slightly slower with a bit of vibration, repeating 5~6 times in the same area. Use the same rotational action to brush the lingual-palatal surfaces of the anterior teeth; for narrow dental arches, place the toothbrush vertically, partially pressing the bristles onto the gums, pulling along the interdental spaces toward the crown while also pulling across the occlusal surfaces of the posterior teeth.

Toothpaste contains abrasives and cleansing agents that can prevent cavities, reduce inflammation, stop bleeding, prevent acid, relieve pain, inhibit tartar, and reduce bad breath. Therefore, using toothpaste while brushing can enhance the effectiveness of cleaning the teeth.
