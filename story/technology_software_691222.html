More than 20 elders driving 12 motor homes arrived in China from Italy

by aiipii12987 on 2012-03-05 14:54:54

Oriental Net June 2nd news: According to the Oriental Morning Post report, yesterday evening in a certain parking lot in Longhua, Xuhui District, twelve neatly parked RVs could be seen. More than twenty Italian seniors who had just returned from their travels outside opened their car doors and either took out chairs and tables to sit and chat or went inside to start fires and cook meals. This scene made the passers-by envious.

Twelve couples, with an average age of 70 years old, the youngest being 64 and the oldest 82. On March 31st, the group set off from Italy, driving RVs through Lithuania and Russia; on April 20th they entered China from Mongolia. On May 29th, they arrived in Shanghai.

From strangers to forming a team

The captain of the fleet is 64-year-old Mario. He told reporters that he saw an advertisement in a local Italian newspaper where a travel agency was organizing an RV self-driving tour of China, so he signed up with his wife.

"We, these 12 couples, come from different cities, and we didn't know each other before," said Mario. "We joined this tour group and began a five-month long journey." Along the way, they were careful with expenses, and each person's total expenditure for the entire trip would be 60,000 RMB, lasting five months and covering nearly 30,000 kilometers.

In every country they visited, there was a local cooperating travel agency to receive them and help handle entry, parking and other procedures. These "husband-and-wife teams" took turns driving, traveling across Xinjiang, Gansu, Shaanxi, Sichuan, Hubei, Jiangxi, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, and finally arriving in Shanghai on May 29th.

The 12 RVs included Mercedes-Benz, Renault, and Iveco models. The vehicles are slightly larger than the regular Iveco found domestically. However, the interior is quite unique: there are mini bathrooms with showers, carefully designed kitchens fully equipped with refrigerators, pots, and stoves; three-person sofas and tea tables as well as foldable mattresses. Even the car door has built-in makeup cabinets and trash bins.

According to the guide who accompanied them throughout, it wasn't easy for the seniors to find a parking spot that could accommodate 12 RVs, conveniently allow for water refilling and charging, and have nearby supermarkets for shopping in every city.

Shanghai Bund "wonderful"

Before coming to China, the seniors had done their homework in Italy, going online, buying books, researching materials, and studying Chinese tourist attractions thoroughly.

The full-time guide introduced that in China, only in a few cities like Beijing, Xi'an, and Shanghai did they hire local guides. In other cities, the seniors relied entirely on the research they had done, holding maps and taking subways or taxis to explore independently. The seniors said that only in this way could they truly "feel China."

On May 29th after arriving in Shanghai, on the first day they visited the Shanghai Museum and the Oriental Pearl Tower, and in the evening, they cruised along the Huangpu River. This day left a deep impression on the seniors. With little knowledge of English, the seniors used the word "wonderful" repeatedly to express their satisfaction. On the second day, the seniors visited the Shanghai Urban Planning Exhibition Hall and Zhujiajiao. Yesterday, the seniors split up for free activities.

Citizens admire "living truly freely"

During these three days, every evening when the seniors returned to the parking lot to rest, there would always be passers-by stopping to enviously observe these contented foreign seniors.

Yesterday evening, a private car passed by, the window rolled down, and a Shanghai auntie stuck her head out curiously asking: "Where are these people from? What are they doing?" Upon learning that they were tourists who had driven RVs from Italy to Shanghai, the auntie sighed: "They really live freely!"

Today, the Italian RV group will leave Shanghai for Nanjing and Beijing, then cross Mongolia, Siberia, Poland, Romania, Switzerland, and return to Rome on August 31st.
