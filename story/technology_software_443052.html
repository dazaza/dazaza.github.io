The metaphors used are 3210 and 8800.

by ywaljz41 on 2011-06-08 17:07:15

Keywords: Nokia, design, crux, brand, brand building

In the interview at Nokia's headquarters, Chief Designer Alastair Curtis of Nokia should be the most popular. Curtis is quite young, with a specialization in industrial design, and manages a design team of over 200 people.

Curtis has participated in designing dozens of models, and he is particularly proud of the 3210 and 8800, but the one he is most proud of is the 1100, as it has surpassed a global volume of over 100 million units. Through Curtis, we can catch a glimpse into the secrets of Nokia's design, which include some key points:

1. The main rule is simplicity and usability.

2. Emphasizes overall design, even requiring designers from different places to participate in three-day brainstorming sessions, stressing collective design.

3. Observe before designing, observing different groups of people.

4. Keep learning, challenging and questioning the present.

5. Dare to create extraordinary things.

6. Dare to take risks, the most important thing is to allow failure and learn from it.

7. Personalized experience, as well as the trend of mobile interconnectivity.

I asked Curtis how to make Nokia cooler? Curtis replied that under the premise of maintaining inner beauty, it needs to be unified with outer beauty. Personally, I believe that in terms of design strategy, while Nokia maintains its original design genes, it needs to be further updated. The starting point of the design strategy is the user experience, and Nokia has always maintained this straightforward feeling. From the perspective of user experience, it lacks fresh experiences, and from the designer's point of view, it is not cool enough. Nokia has redefined the standard for user interfaces, now it needs to redefine the standard for what is considered cool.
