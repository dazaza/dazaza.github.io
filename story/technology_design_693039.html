College student's house burned down on New Year's Eve, sought help via Weibo and received donations

by yanu742yxue on 2012-03-06 14:06:05

Our press (reporter HuanYu Li) A big fire on the Chinese New Year's Eve burned three rooms of HaiQing Li, a student in Communication University of China into ruins. Haiqing Li is a third year student in Art Design Department of Drama and Film Academy of Communication University of China, originally from Dongxiaozhai Village, Jianming Town, Zunhua City, Hebei Province. On the New Year's Eve, she watched the Spring Festival Gala with her family in the house."The first to be fired was the neighbor's simple shed used for storing firewood, then the fire burned over along the roof," said Haiqing Li. When the incident happened, as all four of them were watching TV in the room with loud sound, they did not find it in time. It was not until some villagers who passed by found it and knocked on the door desperately that Haiqing Li's family still failed to notice."Later, they jumped over the wall into our yard," at this moment, Haiqing Li discovered that the roofs of the three rooms had already been surrounded by fire."If we found it later, our family might have perished in the fire." With the help of neighbors, Haiqing Li's family only managed to take out several home appliances and a cabinet of clothes. By this time, the fire had already burned inside the house, and the fierce fire and thick smoke prevented people from getting close."The electric switch was burned out, and there was a power failure." Without electricity to start the water pump, Haiqing Li's father could only fetch water from the neighbor's house to save the fire. When the fire truck arrived, the fire was burning fiercely. The firefighters used exactly three trucks of water, until 2:00 am on the early morning of the first day of the lunar new year, to put out the fire. At this point, the house was burned into ruins, and relevant certificates of Haiqing Li were also turned into ashes because they were not taken out in time. Later, Haiqing Li and her family were arranged separately. At 3:00 am, more and more afraid, Haiqing Li sent a microblog with her mobile phone:"On the New Year's Eve, a big fire took away my home, the house was destroyed, and our family of four became homeless!" Subsequently, this microblog was forwarded more than 3000 times, and many netizens sent words of comfort and encouragement online to Haiqing Li. But some netizens raised questions and criticisms. What made Haiqing Li moved was that, on the night of the incident, a classmate knew about her family situation and sent some money first, then got in touch with the monitor. Early on the first day of the lunar new year, the monitor reflected the situation of Haiqing Li's family to the teacher in the department. Then the teachers in the college and department organized donations, and currently, more than 6000 yuan of donation has arrived. Haiqing Li said that, because of supporting herself and her younger sister to go to school, the family basically had no savings. Now that the house was burned like this, it would be so difficult to build a house again. Currently, her younger sister lived in a classmate's house, and her parents temporarily lived in an office provided by the village committee, but it was not a long-term solution."I've inquired, building a house needs 100 thousand yuan, but now I can't raise this sum of money at all."
