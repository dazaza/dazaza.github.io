Since I joined the workforce, I have always been devoted to the Party's educational cause and loved my job. I earnestly studied new educational concepts, extensively read various knowledge, formed a relatively complete knowledge structure, respected students, implemented democratic teaching, and ensured that students learned effectively. This continuously improved my teaching level and ideological awareness, allowing my work to reach new heights each year. Below is a summary: Improving teaching quality is the most important aspect of all work, and enhancing classroom teaching efficiency guarantees this improvement. The classroom is the main platform for students to acquire knowledge and develop skills. Before class, I carefully study the textbook, thoroughly understand its content, and prepare every lesson well. I know that interest is the best teacher, so to give a good class, I must adopt flexible and diverse teaching methods based on the students' characteristics to stimulate their interest in learning and achieve the expected results. In class, I strive to implement new educational concepts, create a relaxed and democratic teaching atmosphere, and encourage students to speak up actively. I often tell students: Chinese is different from other subjects; there is no right or wrong in answering questions, only whether the answer is complete or not. This greatly increases students' enthusiasm for answering questions, even those who usually don't like to talk raise their hands to speak, livening up the classroom atmosphere and improving students' verbal expression abilities. In class, I consistently implement the principle of student-centered autonomous cooperative learning, enhancing students' ability for independent exploration.

Improving teaching quality also depends significantly on cultivating students' abilities and habits. I have long been responsible for teaching Chinese and being a head teacher for the graduating class of elementary school. Conducting ideological education for students is particularly important. Based on the characteristic of my class enjoying reading, I use various methods to cultivate students' good habit of reading extracurricular books. First, I collected a large amount of knowledge from the computer: essential ancient poems for primary school students, famous sayings, proverbs, riddles, couplets, a large number of words, and word chains, printed them into booklets, and let the students read them every day. Even so, it still could not satisfy the students' extracurricular reading needs. I discussed with the principal to regularly distribute books from the school library to the students and required them to write reading notes and reflections, then exchange ideas with each other. Through extensive reading, students accumulated many good words and sentences, improved their speaking ability, and their writing level has also significantly improved.

As a Chinese teacher, teaching students to write Chinese characters well is one of the main themes in my education work. Nowadays, most children do not write well, and many students mistakenly believe that in the technological era, everything can be done by computers, so handwriting does not affect grades, and thus there is no need to practice writing. Regarding these misconceptions among students, through examples, I patiently educated them that as Chinese people, we start learning to write Chinese characters from kindergarten, and if by sixth grade we still cannot write well, it would be a very embarrassing thing. I also educated the students that writing a good hand is not difficult, but it requires persistence. As a result, the students gradually realized the importance of writing well and became more interested in calligraphy. Every week, I set aside a class to guide the students in writing, providing hand-by-hand guidance, and requiring the students to practice writing one sheet of large imitation every day. We regularly hold calligraphy competitions, and the excellent works are posted on the classroom walls for mutual observation and learning from each other's strengths. Over the years, the handwriting of the students in the classes I taught has been at the forefront of the entire street.

Strengthening the transformation of underachievers is also a key to improving teaching quality. Underachievers often do not receive affirmation from teachers, yet their performance directly affects the overall performance of the whole class. This spring, after listening to Professor Qian Zhiliang's lecture in Fuhua, I benefited immensely. I deeply realized that as teachers, we should not "teach tirelessly," but rather go with the flow, patiently instruct, give them more care and affection, discover their strengths, leverage their advantages, overcome shortcomings, and avoid scolding and insulting, which would only lead to counterproductive results. We should truly reason with them, move them emotionally, understand their various psychological manifestations and opinions during the learning process, trust them, and encourage them. Any slight improvement should be acknowledged.

Actively participate in the school's various teaching and scientific research activities to promote the improvement of one's professional level. Over the years, I have persisted in listening to the excellent teachers' classes in our school, the excellent teachers' classes in the street, the district's excellent teachers' classes, and the city's excellent teachers' classes. I carefully took notes, repeatedly reflected on them, broadened my horizons, and improved my professional level. I also actively participated in various teaching and scientific research activities organized by the school. Although there were no significant scientific research achievements, it did further improve my professional level. In short, I spare no effort to do my teaching work, always strictly require myself, learn from others' strengths, continuously update my teaching concepts, and apply them to my actual teaching, ensuring that every class achieves excellent teaching results and enabling every student to truly improve their Chinese literacy. The subject I teach has stood out in multiple quality inspections. My various works have made students satisfied, parents reassured, and society recognized. I was consecutively named an excellent teacher in the street in 2004 and 2005; received the second prize for information technology papers in the district in XX year; was named an excellent counselor in the district in XX year; was awarded the title of "Excellent Tutor" in the Chaoyang Reading Activity in 2010; and was granted the honorary title of "Outstanding Young Pioneer Counselor for Red Scarf Newspaper Reading and Usage" in Weifang City in November 2010. The achievements obtained are inseparable from the support of leaders at all levels, and in the future, I will work even harder.
