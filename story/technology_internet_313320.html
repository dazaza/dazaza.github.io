Looking ahead - Barcelona vows to avoid the trap it fell into 3 years ago; Messi rests and will not participate in the semi-final.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-18 08:15:34

Preview - Barcelona vows not to repeat mistakes from 3 years ago; Messi rests and misses semifinal

Sina Sports News - Barcelona will begin their FIFA Club World Cup journey today, with their first match against Atlante widely regarded as an easy contest. As Guardiola said: "In which competition is Barcelona not the favorite?" Besides similar uniforms (both red and blue), the two teams also share similar football philosophies. Atlante's coach, José Cruz, is a loyal follower of former Mexican national team coach Ricardo La Volpe, who is also a coach that Guardiola greatly admires.

In predictions, Barcelona is such a favorite that the organizers of the Club World Cup have decided that Barcelona should wear their traditional red and blue uniforms, while Atlante must wear indigo. The Mexican media says this color is also nice, reminding one of the Caribbean Sea. In fact, the two teams originally agreed to decide on the uniform colors by drawing lots.

Atlante does not seem too concerned about this, as they only brought three fans and one journalist. Atlante was founded in 1916, and for a long time has been known for representing the lower classes. However, since entering the 1980s, this club has gradually fallen into the hands of big capitalists, moving further away from ordinary people. Currently, the club is even in difficulty, with the team lacking a changing room and having to train on a golf course.

However, Atlante's players are relatively young and play good football. If Barcelona underestimates them, it would be a huge mistake. Barcelona's Mexican center-back Márquez stated: "Atlante's lineup is quite good, especially the team's spine is very perfect." He is very familiar with Atlante's goalkeeper and forward. Interestingly, Atlante's forward is also named Rafael Márquez, sharing the same name as him. "The game will definitely not be easy," Márquez reminded his teammates.

The best compliment for Atlante comes from the mouth of their opponents. Guardiola never takes any opponent lightly nor looks down on any match. The Barcelona coach said: "If we cannot get past the semifinal, then there will be no final. You have to win the opportunity to participate in the final on the pitch; everything else is empty talk."

Barcelona's trophy room is filled with various trophies, but the only one missing is the "Club World Cup" trophy. Historically, Barcelona has participated in the Club World Cup twice (including its predecessor, the Toyota Cup). In 1993, they lost 1-2 to Brazil's São Paulo, and in 2007, Barcelona lost 0-1 to Brazil's Internacional. If Barcelona can lift the trophy this time, the club's significant void could be filled. Moreover, Barcelona could achieve the miraculous feat of winning six titles. In 2009, Barcelona has already won five major trophies: La Liga, Copa del Rey, Champions League, Spanish Super Cup, and UEFA Super Cup. If they can win the Club World Cup, this team will create a miracle unprecedented.

However, Guardiola is not interested in talking about the sextuple at the moment: "I am more interested in studying how to defeat Atlante on the football field. If we do not focus our attention on the game itself, we will make a mistake. If we cannot prepare well for the semifinal match, it is entirely possible that we will not reach the final."

Márquez also mentioned that Barcelona cannot afford to make the mistake of arrogance again. In 2006, Barcelona defeated Mexico's América 4-0 in the semifinal, making the whole team overly confident. As a result, they were defeated by Brazil's Internacional in the final. Guardiola also referenced Barcelona's loss to Brazil's São Paulo in 1992: "At that time, the entire team, including the head coach, did not know how to win this trophy." Psychological counseling began on Tuesday, with Guardiola looking serious and the Barcelona players feeling nothing wrong with preparing seriously for the match against a weaker opponent.

Finally, it is worth noting that Messi's absence will open the door to history for Pedro. Although the Argentine participated in the team's normal training yesterday, Guardiola's possibility of starting him in the semifinal remains low. Pedro expressed: "I hope to participate in an important Club World Cup match, and if I can score and be written into history, it would be even better. I look forward to scoring a goal, but the most important thing is for the team to win." Currently, Pedro has scored goals in five competitions: La Liga, Copa del Rey, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and the Champions League. Scoring in the Club World Cup will create the legend of scoring in all six competitions in one season.
