Near the spring sign, a couple from out of town held their daughter suffering from leukemia seeking help here. "What's going on? Give away daughter and pay money?" Some people were curious and squatted down to look at the medical bills; "It's another scam, this kind of thing is too common!" Some people glanced and left indifferently. "I really have no other way, who would give their own child to others?" Wang Qihai from Zoucheng countryside said helplessly. His wife beside him was already sobbing uncontrollably, tightly holding her 5-year-old daughter Qingqing... Giving away daughter and returning hundred thousand yuan, this couple's approach has been questioned. At noon on the 28th, the reporter rushed to the spring sign near Quancheng Square. On a step, the 5-year-old girl Wang Ziqing was next to her parents, with fair skin, big eyes, slightly curly hair, occasionally showing a pure smile on her face, constantly playing with the little tiger toy in her arms. For her, death might be too inhumane: severe aplastic anemia, this serious illness, has already fallen upon her. "Give away daughter and return hundred thousand, joint venture to save life." The crumpled old newspaper, two crooked lines of writing with a writing brush. Wang Qihai from Zoucheng countryside said that the high treatment cost made the poor family unable to bear it, so he and his wife Li Qiwen had to resort to this desperate measure. Next to the old newspaper, there was also a pile of medical bills for Qingqing. Wang Qihai told the reporter that these are Qingqing's medical bills from the end of January to now, including hospitals in Zoucheng, Beijing, and Tianjin. "Giving away daughter and returning hundred thousand yuan, it's really strange!" After understanding the situation by looking for a few minutes, citizen Xiao Liu felt a bit sympathetic towards the little girl, but also questioned the actions of Wang Qihai and his wife: anyway, never heard of giving away children and paying money. The 5-year-old daughter wouldn't stop bleeding from her nose and was diagnosed with leukemia What's going on? The reporter talked with Wang Qihai and learned the ins and outs of the matter. On January 27, 2010, the 5-year-old Qingqing suddenly fell ill, with a high fever and unstoppable nosebleed. Wang Qihai hurriedly sent his daughter to a local hospital in Zoucheng, where her blood condition was checked and her platelet index was found to be below normal levels. At 2 p.m. that afternoon, Wang Qihai transferred his daughter to the Rizhao People's Hospital. Based on the examination results, the doctor suspected that Qingqing had leukemia and suggested seeking treatment at a larger hospital. On the afternoon of January 28, after emergency treatment at the Beijing Children's Hospital, Qingqing's condition was basically stable. After examination, the doctor suspected aplastic anemia, but further examination was needed for confirmation. "It's like being struck by lightning on a clear day, hard to believe this result." Subsequently, Wang Qihai took Qingqing to Tianjin, where she was finally diagnosed with severe aplastic anemia (commonly known as leukemia). The doctor recommended hospitalization for treatment, requiring allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation, otherwise there would always be a risk to life. Spent 80,000 yuan front and back, the condition is like a time bomb Wang Qihai told the reporter that after Qingqing was diagnosed and hospitalized, she needed daily blood transfusions and large amounts of drug treatment. From January 27 when Qingqing fell ill until she was discharged from the hospital, about 80,000 yuan was spent, nearly 50,000 of which was borrowed from relatives and friends. The current Qingqing looks like any other child on the surface, but in reality, she could be in danger at any time. "Must wear a mask when going out, can't have any mistakes." Wang Qihai worriedly said. A doctor from the hematology department of a hospital in the provincial capital told the reporter that severe aplastic anemia is characterized by a large reduction in white blood cells, platelets, etc., easily causing symptoms such as anemia, infection, and bleeding in patients; the main cause is problems with the patient's own immune system, making treatment very difficult. Successful bone marrow matching with sister, but huge costs difficult to gather In mid-March, Qingqing successfully matched bone marrow with her older sister Mengmeng, theoretically ready for hematopoietic stem cell transplantation. But facing this news, Wang Qihai couldn't be happy at all, because the doctor informed him that the transplant surgery plus subsequent medication would cost 300,000-400,000 yuan. This is an astronomical figure, beyond the means of this ordinary rural family. Helpless, Wang Qihai and his wife came to Jinan on March 27 and staged a "daughter-giving" event. "Don't hate your parents, we really have no other way." On the afternoon of March 28, in Quancheng Square, Li Qiwen hugged her daughter Qingqing and cried incessantly. The sensible Qingqing just helped wipe her mother's tears, perhaps not yet fully aware of how serious her illness was. The reporter learned that Wang Qihai is a temporary miner at a local mine, earning 2,000-3,000 yuan per month, and his wife Li Qiwen has no job. Li Qiwen told the reporter that after learning about their situation, teachers and classmates of her elder daughter Mengmeng reached out to help, donating money and goods. But these, for this family, are almost like drops in the bucket. "Can't borrow anymore, the child is sick like this, I really have no other way, who would willingly give their own child to others?" Wang Qihai helplessly said that he had borrowed money from all his relatives and friends, and now he was almost completely broke; he hoped that some good-hearted people could come and save his daughter. At this point, his wife beside him was already sobbing uncontrollably.
