Self-reflection on March 31, 2011

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 11:55:02

Went out for tea tonight, so I guess when I lie down I'll just be tossing and turning. It's been quite a while since I've been online - quitting the internet basically means quitting online shopping, and I've found it really saves a lot of money~!

Sometimes I really don't know who I am, fluctuating between highs and lows. No matter how I try to define myself, it always ends up being neither one thing nor the other. This in-between state is truly awkward. Sometimes I think I'm almost there, then suddenly I think it's better to just accept my fate. At this stage in life, what more can I hope for? Even standing still would be a pleasant surprise. After being in a certain state for too long, you want to change it, but whether good or bad, it's hard to plan. Just go with the flow, I guess. People who couldn't accept me before might eventually find me okay after some time, and those who thought I was okay might eventually decide not to stay in touch. I think this is true for people, and also for life in general. Wastefulness can turn into frugality, and how much plainer can plainness get? In short, maybe being busy leaves no time for shopping, being busy leaves no time to compare yourself to others, being busy stops you from overthinking, and when you're busy, time flies by. Who knows, a lifetime could pass in the blink of an eye.

But can I really be busy? Maybe I can't handle the hustle and bustle, and if I can't handle it, I escape. If I can't escape, I argue. If I can't argue, I give the cold shoulder. If I can't give the cold shoulder, all I can do is reflect. Reflection leads to self-examination, and the result of self-examination is that I can't live anymore~

Is it like this? Not really, right? Maybe it is.

Right now, I'm in the middle of self-reflection.

I admire, I envy, I like, I hate, I believe, I doubt... My question marks are always there when it comes to Guangzhou VIP Shop Information Technology Co., Ltd.

Don't let me get everything I want, because then life would become even more monotonous and unremarkable.

I have to go to work tomorrow, so I'll stop here.
