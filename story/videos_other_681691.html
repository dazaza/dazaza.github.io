What makes people curious is why many students who felt they performed well in the interview were not admitted, while Xiao Zhou, who made mistakes in the interview, was selected by the professor? During the interview, the reporter found a clear trend of younger students participating in "A-LEVEL" foreign college entrance exam training. In the past, many second-year high school students gave up taking the domestic college entrance exam and switched to the UK A-Level exam. Now, students are giving up the junior high school graduation exam and directly studying "A-LEVEL" courses right after finishing junior high. Among the 200 graduates of the Shanghai Normal University Cambridge International Center this year, 75% of the students started studying "A-LEVEL" courses right after finishing junior high, making them the main force in taking the "A-LEVEL" exam. The reporter learned that due to the increasing international awareness of parents and the trend of younger students going abroad for study, the "A-LEVEL" education market is growing annually. In 2006, there were only two schools in Shanghai offering A-Level education: Shanghai Normal University Cambridge International Center and Yew Wah International School. Over the past five years, the number of schools across the country offering A-Level education has reached over a hundred. As a new phenomenon, international high school curricula currently lack relevant management documents issued by the Municipal Education Commission. Many private institutions see the low market entry threshold and rush into the field. Therefore, students choosing international curriculum schools to study "A-LEVEL" courses at a younger age should be cautious.

The "A-LEVEL" course in the UK is a selective exam specifically aimed at students applying to prestigious universities. If a student performs poorly academically in regular schools domestically, they may not be suitable for this course.
