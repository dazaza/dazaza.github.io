On April 16, the China Film Copyright Association was officially established in Beijing. They announced that they would represent the copyright of all domestic films and decided to start charging internet cafes and long-distance buses for copyright fees from this year onwards. To collect these copyright fees, they will use a "Domestic Film Internet Cafe Digital Distribution Platform" for management. With this platform installed, internet cafes will no longer face the risk of infringing on domestic film copyrights. It is precisely this platform that has sparked doubts among netizens. Some netizens have analyzed that this platform is not very reliable, and some even believe that it is using administrative power for market promotion.

The China Film Copyright Association is the first and only copyright collective management organization in the Chinese film industry. The association has discussed and passed the "China Film Copyright Association Charter," "Collective Management Fee Collection Standards for Film Works Copyright," and "Methods for Transferring Collective Management Fees for Film Works Copyright," thereby obtaining legal authorization for all domestic films.

This year, charges will be levied on internet cafes and long-distance buses.

On April 16, the "China Film Copyright Association," the first and only copyright collective management organization in the Chinese film industry, was officially established in Beijing. The predecessor of this association was the China Film Copyright Protection Association, which was founded on August 29, 2005, with the aim of "protecting the copyright and related rights of members' films and works created by methods similar to film production." The association has transformed from an industry rights protection organization into a collective management organization, becoming the fifth copyright collective management association after music, audiovisual, literary, and photographic fields.

The association has discussed and passed the "China Film Copyright Association Charter," "Collective Management Fee Collection Standards for Film Works Copyright," and "Methods for Transferring Collective Management Fees for Film Works Copyright," as well as signed the "Authorization Letter for Collective Management of Film Works Copyright." According to the introduction, the "Fee Collection Standards" and "Fee Transfer Methods" were formulated based on relevant laws and regulations such as the "Copyright Law of the People's Republic of China" and the "Regulations on Copyright Collective Management." After being announced by the National Copyright Administration, the association can issue movie usage licenses, collect and transfer usage fees, and protect rights against infringement and piracy under its own name. Although the National Copyright Administration has not yet announced these documents, the association has already decided to charge copyright usage fees for domestic films to internet cafes and long-distance buses in 2010. The "collective management" in networks and other fields will also follow suit.

Netizen Doubts: Is Zhonglu Wangshang Reliable?

It is reported that the Film Copyright Association will uniformly charge internet cafes for domestic films through the "Domestic Film Internet Cafe Digital Distribution Platform," then distribute according to the allocation standards discussed and approved by all members. The "Domestic Film Internet Cafe Digital Distribution Platform" was launched by Beijing Zhonglu Wangshang Company after "two years of market research and technical development." The company's general manager, Xu Gang, once introduced to the media that installing this platform is equivalent to paying all domestic film copyright usage fees since the founding of the country, as well as new domestic film copyright usage fees during the service period.

According to the official press release, Zhonglu Wangshang Company is described as: "Zhonglu Wangshang Company is a joint venture between the Ministry of Culture's direct subordinate enterprise, China Recording and Videotape Publishing Corporation, and a leading provider of film and entertainment content services —— Wangshang Culture, specializing in the promotion and service of the internet cafe digital distribution platform."

After Southern Metropolis Daily reporters checked the introduction on the Ministry of Culture's website, they found that there is no "Beijing Zhonglu Wangshang Company" among the subordinate units of China Recording and Videotape Publishing Corporation, and no other relevant information about the company could be found online. When South Metropolis Daily reporters tried to log onto the website of China Recording and Videotape Publishing Corporation for inquiries, they discovered that the link provided by the Ministry of Culture's website had been redirected to "Oral Health Network."

A widely circulated online post titled "Zhonglu Wangshang Domestic Film Internet Cafe Digital Distribution Platform Unreliable" raised questions about this platform: Copyright is primarily a private right, and whether rights holders join or exit copyright collective management organizations is voluntary. What is the basis for "unified collection and settlement by the China Film Copyright Association"? In fact, some film companies have already granted the copyright of certain films to specific video websites. Moreover, Chinese netizens do not only like watching domestic films. "How should copyright disputes caused by non-domestic films such as Hollywood blockbusters and Korean dramas in internet cafes be resolved?" Promoting this platform under the guise of administrative power, "besides making profits, can internet cafe owners really摆脱infringement disputes forever by purchasing this platform?"
