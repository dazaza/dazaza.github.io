Preschool age is the best time to learn English.

by gege on 2012-02-27 20:08:18

English is an international common language, and not knowing English will impose great restrictions in information exchange. Meanwhile, research also shows that studying foreign languages can promote the development of intelligence.

A psychologist from Yale University in the United States believes through analysis and research that toddlers who speak two languages have advantages over those who only speak one language in terms of brain flexibility and problem-solving abilities. Language stimulation is a factor that promotes the growth and development of brain cells. Early childhood is the critical period for language development. Providing bilingual stimulation during this period can promote coordination between the two hemispheres of the toddler's brain and strengthen the connection between neurons. Therefore, English training during early childhood greatly benefits children.

So, when is the best time for toddlers to learn English? Many experts believe that early childhood, especially before the age of six, is the optimal period for acquiring a foreign language.

Research in child psychology shows that the first to mature in the perception development of toddlers is their image thinking ability. Whether toddlers recognize Chinese characters or English letters, it is actually an activity of information stimulation. They treat the shape of words as a specific image, like remembering candies, toys, or people's appearances. After repeated exposure, this special "image" is stored in the brain. Therefore, learning foreign languages as a toddler is very different from adults learning foreign languages. At the same time, before the age of six, toddlers are more sensitive to sounds, and the right brain has stronger control over the vocal organs. The regulation of speech nerves such as the vocal cords, tongue, and lips also has great plasticity. Practicing speaking at this time makes it easier to master pronunciation skills. As age increases, this ability will gradually decline, making it harder to learn foreign languages.

Toddlers learning English also have many advantages that adults do not possess. For example, they don't worry about exams and can learn in a relaxed, pleasant environment without pressure, "playing" while learning. Even if they make mistakes, they won't be ridiculed by others. Another example is that toddlers have a long future ahead, and there is no rush to increase vocabulary during lessons. Therefore, they have more opportunities to practice speaking. Moreover, children are all born linguists, and once their enthusiasm for learning English is stimulated, their potential will quickly manifest.

To make toddlers' English learning effective, the key lies in having the correct teaching method. In toddler English learning, we oppose "adult-style" English teaching and advocate "horizontal" teaching focused on environmental immersion and unconscious memory. If the adult English learning model of "reading texts, explaining word meanings and sentences, and reinforcing exercises" is moved into the toddler classroom, it will not only fail to improve teaching effectiveness but may also make toddlers dislike English, creating obstacles for future English learning. Teaching toddlers English should create a light-hearted and pleasant atmosphere, allowing them to master English just like they would their native language (the language of their own ethnicity). Toddler English learning mainly focuses on repetitive speaking and letter recognition. Since their hand muscles are not yet fully developed, they are not required to write. The choice of methods should be based on the physical and mental development laws of toddlers. For example, during early childhood, children's attention is difficult to focus and easily distracted. Teachers should skillfully use various methods to attract children's attention, such as games, songs, stories, etc., allowing toddlers to integrate themselves and then inspire their interest in learning.

In addition, there are two issues to note when toddlers learn English.

1. Choose a good textbook.

The textbooks we refer to include books, newspapers, picture books, recordings, videos, and TV programs. Textbook formats and editions can vary widely. Choosing textbooks should be done carefully, with not too many types. It's best to choose video or TV programs and systematically study according to this textbook.

2. Pay attention to pronunciation.

When toddlers learn English, parents play a very important role. If parents are highly concerned about their child's learning and create a positive English conversation atmosphere for the child, it is very beneficial to their learning. However, some parents' English is "Chinese-style." If they teach the child without correcting it, it is easy for the child to pronounce incorrectly, which becomes harder to correct when they grow up. Therefore, parents teaching their children should pay attention to pronunciation. If they cannot say it correctly themselves, they should let the child learn from the teacher or TV as much as possible.
