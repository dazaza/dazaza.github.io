In 1998, the two stock market commentary hotline lines hosted by the author, numbers 16883139 and 16883015, were very popular among a large number of stock investors. The author remembers one noon when he received a fax. Its general content was: "We are a group of more than 20 laid-off workers who are small individual stock investors. I listen to your broadcasts three times daily on behalf of us all. We hope you can shorten your broadcast time to the minimum because the phone fee is too expensive for us. We have even used the money meant for our children's milk powder. We are truly sorry! Believe it or not, our children haven't eaten meat for over half a year. One colleague, due to their child's poor nutrition, stole a piece of meat from the market and after being caught, both parents committed suicide, leaving behind an elementary school-aged child for us to take turns raising. After being laid off, we originally wanted to make some small profits in the stock market, but now we realize how difficult that is. We don’t know how long we can keep going. Writing to you has no other meaning; though poor, we still have pride. We believe everything will get better gradually."

That day, all the colleagues in the office couldn't eat lunch. Everyone hoped to find this unfortunate orphan and provide some living assistance, but unfortunately, we never received another fax from them. We deeply understand the risks of the stock market and sincerely wish this group of laid-off workers could survive through it. During the broadcasts, we often called out to them, hoping they would soon contact us. After 16883139 stopped broadcasting, our only hope was to publish a book, clearly stating the contact address. As long as they were willing to contact us, they should be able to find us. If they want to fight with their own strength in the stock market, we hope this book serves as a small boat accompanying them through the storms of the stock sea. Thus, within 20 days, the book "Silver Short Line" was written. Despite lacking elaborate rhetoric and complex formulas, it indeed contains the author's nine years of stock trading experience and lessons.

The book finally hit the shelves, reportedly ranking high on the book sales chart for five consecutive weeks. In the months since, tens of thousands of reader letters were received, yet there was still no news about this group of laid-off workers. Reading these letters, we realized many more people need our help! Most readers hoped we would write a more detailed, practical, and understandable securities book for laid-off workers. Therefore, we again put pen to paper, writing this "Short Line Practical Combat" with our sincerity, experience, and lessons.

All examples provided in the book come from real combat experiences: real-time dialogue records, real-time printed charts. When making decisions, there were moments of hesitation and decisive actions; situations of seeming dead-ends and unexpected opportunities. The author has been hired by securities institutions as a senior advisor for a long time. Many major investors also vie to request the author serve as their private advisor. To help a broad range of retail investors, the author consistently provides free services to them. A distinctive feature of this book compared to other securities books is:

Establishing a spatial framework in the stock market. When you buy low with the hope of selling high, sometimes lower prices appear after the low point; after selling at a high price, the stock may soar continuously, making you regret missing the opportunity! This is because you do not know the exact position of the stock price at the time. Here’s an example of a spatial framework: the top of a skyscraper has lightning rods and rooftop gardens, the bottom has underground garages. Seeing these signs, people know their location. The stock market is similar; as long as we observe carefully, each low, medium, and high point of the market trend has its corresponding sign: the bottom has signs like "Lotus Rising" and "Oriental Red Great Yang Rising"; the middle has signs like "Old Duck Head" and "Green Dragon Fetching Water"; the top has signs like "Three Death Crosses Top", "Guillotine Blade", and "Nine Yin White Bone Claw". These signs are simple, clear, easy to understand, and remember. Even those with junior high school education in previous short-term training courses could skillfully master and apply them.

Establishing a timetable in the stock market. Buying and selling stocks requires pinpointing the right time. When should you buy? When should you sell? This is indeed a perplexing matter. Human beings have managed time for thousands of years, from centuries down to seasons, and even seconds. Because of seasonal divisions, people won't sow seeds in autumn or harvest in spring. So can we establish such time divisions in the stock market, dividing it into four seasons? We divide each institutional stock into three stages: stealth accumulation and strong accumulation, pushing up and lifting, stealth distribution and strong distribution, each stage having specific signs. This way, we can accurately buy during the accumulation phase, hold confidently during the lifting phase, and sell calmly during the distribution phase. Practice shows that mastering the stock market timetable equals mastering the timing of buying and selling.

Estimating the power of stock rises. Although we have established the spatial framework and the timetable for buying and selling, how long will a stock rise in the future? How high will it go? This is something investors are eager to know. One person's stock rises from 10 yuan to 20 yuan, while another person's stock rises from 10 yuan to 11 yuan. Although both have risen, the profits differ greatly. How do we find a method to pre-estimate the stock price's rising space? This book specifically discusses methods to estimate rising space and the "Ultrasound" stock selection method. Through them, we can select black horses with larger rising spaces in actual combat.

How important is this book to your future? Spend just five minutes flipping through the contents, and you'll naturally understand its value!
