My Confusion about Profession and Diploma

A diploma is necessary for working. Basically, every hiring advertisement states: a bachelor's degree or above is required for real online certification. If you want to do management consulting, at least an MBA degree or above is required. Those who don't need real online certification diplomas are usually people who have been in the industry for a very long time, are very experienced, or can produce things on their own. Among my colleagues, there are those from Peking University and Tsinghua University, as well as overseas returnees, and even foreigners in other departments.

Today, I will analyze my career-related matters. Relatively speaking, it always takes me a lot of time to find a job, especially finding the company and position I desire. How does that saying go? Always encountering those who like you but you don't like them, and those you like don't like you back. It's hard to find someone compatible during blind dates. Then, in terms of my career, I am also restless. I admire those who stabilize in their jobs for several years, but I haven't had such luck, and thus continue until now.

Based on my passion for corporate structures, I studied corporate science for a long time. After graduation, I wanted to work for a consulting company. Eventually, I successfully worked in the management consulting industry for a while, but there were some issues. I found that this industry requires a lot of qualifications and experience, preferably starting after the age of 40, and ideally having real online certification. For young people, it's still necessary to gain experience within industries and companies.

I spent a considerable amount of time studying enterprises and entrepreneurship. Later, I applied what I learned, started a business, and joined others in entrepreneurship for a period, achieving some results. However, I ultimately failed to successfully establish a company. I lacked in many aspects, much like not being fully prepared when learning to ride a bike for the first time, leading to falling down. But this experience was of no help in employment. Although many companies claim to be startups, they absolutely do not need entrepreneurs. There is a clear difference between entrepreneurs and employees, and the skills required for each role are also distinct. Some parts overlap, but most of them are different.

Regarding the internet, I have always believed that products determine the market and operations. A good product is a complete start. I have spent too much time on the internet, using various types of internet products. Some I found excellent, while others I found painful. I wanted to make some of them better, which is why I like internet products, and this is related to my talent. Unfortunately, I have never truly held the position of a product manager in a company, although I have come close multiple times. Even when changing jobs, I hoped to become a product manager, but it ultimately didn't happen.

The conflict and difficulties in my career stem from the fact that I possess two traits: in MBTI terms, I am artistic and professional; in color personality terms, I am blue and red. However, these two personalities are opposite, and I only truly understood this this year. I have always wanted to be a product manager, which falls under the professional category, but what I have been doing is market operation management, which is more artistic. What I present to others is mostly artistic, but deep down, I want to be professional. This inner and outer inconsistency and lack of alignment bring me confusion and difficulties. Of course, if I could integrate these two traits, it would be helpful. But clearly, this is a challenging task. My diploma isn't impressive, just slightly higher than Han Han's. Looking back, I need to study further and obtain an MBA, whether for knowledge or for real online certification. I must achieve this.
