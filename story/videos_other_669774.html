Mourners pay respects to wrong ashes after funeral home burns wrong body

by anonymous on 2012-02-20 10:58:09

Funeral Home "Burns the Wrong Person" -- One Family Mourns the Wrong Relative, Another Loses a Body. After negotiation, both bereaved families received over 120,000 yuan in compensation.

According to Jin Qian Online News, due to a mistake by the funeral home staff, two bodies were mixed up: one that should have been cremated was not, and another that shouldn't have been cremated was, with its ashes mistakenly given to another family for mourning during a video conference.

After this incident occurred, it caused great dissatisfaction among the relatives of the deceased from both sides. On December 14, the funeral home took the initiative to accept responsibility. In addition to apologizing to the two bereaved families, they also compensated each family with 80,000 yuan and 48,000 yuan respectively for emotional distress and other expenses.

An informed source revealed that this incident happened at a relatively large-scale funeral home in our province. Last week, a bereaved family came to this funeral home to prepare for the cremation of their relative's body when they opened the freezer where the body had been stored and discovered that the person inside was not their own relative.

"Could the body just disappear?" The funeral home staff also found this very strange. After verification and searching, it was confirmed that the body had already been cremated on the day it was brought in, and the ashes had already been taken away by another family.

Upon learning of this situation, the funeral home staff urgently went to the bereaved family who had taken the ashes. At that time, the relatives of the deceased were gathered around the urn in mourning. "We sincerely apologize," said the staff member, "we mistakenly cremated the wrong body; the ashes inside may not be those of your relative." Before the staff member finished speaking, the bereaved family was greatly shocked. Subsequently, the family accompanied the staff back to the funeral home for verification, only to find out that their relative was still lying peacefully in the freezer.

The funeral home staff recalled that on the day of the incident, two bodies were almost simultaneously delivered and placed side by side. One was requested to be immediately cremated, while the other was to be stored. It was precisely at this moment that the staff mixed up the bodies.

It is understood that according to normal procedures, before cremating a body, the staff should ask the family to further verify. Only after confirmation can the cremation proceed. "If this step hadn't been skipped, there wouldn't have been any mistakes," said relevant staff members at the funeral home. Verification of the body requires the deceased’s relatives to actually check the face of the deceased and make an affirmative response after being asked.

After the incident of the wrong body being cremated, it caused great dissatisfaction among the relatives of the deceased from both sides. "Losing a loved one is painful enough, and now we've suffered this additional blow." Both bereaved families strongly demanded an explanation from the funeral home and insisted on compensation for emotional distress and other damages.

Yesterday, a staff member of the funeral home revealed that such a case of mistaken cremation was unprecedented at this funeral home and also rare nationwide. After the incident, the funeral home proactively apologized to both bereaved families. After negotiations, the funeral home compensated the relatives of both families with 80,000 yuan and 48,000 yuan respectively for emotional damages and other expenses, and waived the cremation fees for both families' deceased, ultimately gaining the understanding of the bereaved families. Guizhou Metropolis Daily.
