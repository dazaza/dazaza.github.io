The Story Underneath the Maple Tree

As the autumn wind blows, a wisp of nostalgia ripples in my heart. There, under that maple tree, are traces of you everywhere. As I walk by gently, maple leaves sway leisurely with the autumn breeze before me. I lift my hand lightly to catch the maple leaf just about to drift past my eyes, as if trying to hold onto the fleeting memories fading away from my heart...

The story underneath the maple tree.

A faint sorrow wells up within me. The autumn wind wildly sweeps across my fragile heart, and my thin clothing cannot conceal the bleeding wound. How could those gently falling leaves possibly represent the drops of my burning blood? The scorching wound slowly cools in the autumn wind. I close my eyes, letting your figure mercilessly strike my mind. The cooling wound grows hot again; I open my eyes, only to see a watery haze. Tears quietly slide down my face while the autumn wind continues to blow ruthlessly...

Recalling silently. When the spring breeze turned that maple tree green, our encounter was so wonderfully fated. Your smile is etched into my heart like that maple tree rooted firmly in the earth. Leaning lightly against the trunk, we narrated stories of spring. The blossoms beside the tree were our best accompaniment, and the green leaves grew alongside our tale.

Slipping away quietly. That maple tree over there provided us with a shaded haven. I played the guitar while you sang, and birds often perched on the branches to accompany your singing. Butterflies lingered around the tree, dancing for you. But how could the blazing sun that year have been so intense, testing that maple tree so severely? And how could our story withstand such a test?

When the autumn wind dyes the entire tree red, under the setting sun where the first maple leaf falls between us in that autumn breeze, you and I stood facing each other silently for a long time. Just as the sun was about to disappear, you finally resolutely turned around. I watched your retreating figure silently until it vanished under the sunset at the horizon. Only your faint silhouette and the long shadow of the setting sun accompanied me, alone under the tree. Autumn leaves began to fall one by one near my face, and amidst the mournful cries of crows, I finally realized - where the maple leaf had brushed against my cheek, there were faint tear marks. I couldn't help but burst into tears.

When snowflakes fall on the bare tree trunk, I play that familiar melody in the cold wind, but your song is no longer there. Every time this happens, I can't help but shed tears...

When the autumn wind once again colors that maple tree red, I stand weathered in the position where I once stood. The autumn wind remains desolate, and maple leaves drift leisurely before my eyes. As I raise my hand waiting for another maple leaf to descend, I find that these leaves carry all our stories. Is it because our story is too light and insignificant, or because the autumn wind blows too fiercely? Otherwise, why does the maple leaf in my palm drift away with the wind?

I stare blankly at the departing maple leaf, just as I once stared blankly at you leaving me behind. I laugh, yet I cry...

Oh! The story underneath the maple tree...
