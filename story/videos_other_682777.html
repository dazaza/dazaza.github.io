Owner's littering cigarette ignite miscellaneous articles and smoke suffocated 3 people to death

by zsh0827404 on 2012-02-29 09:08:03

"It's not illegal to put things in the hallway!" said a 55-year-old Ms. Zhang angrily, while another neighbor who was also sued for piling up clutter in the corridor echoed her sentiment. An owner carelessly discarded a cigarette butt which ignited sofas and other clutter stacked in the hallway, resulting in the death by smoke inhalation of Mr. Yang from a certain residential complex as he was evacuating with his three-year-old daughter and a friend of his wife. Consequently, the deceased’s family filed a lawsuit against the perpetrator, the property management company, and two neighbors who had piled up clutter, demanding joint compensation of over 2.5 million yuan. Yesterday morning, the Shuangqiao Court of Chaoyang District opened the trial for this case.

**Incident: Fire in the Hallway Claims Three Lives**

On the night of January 18 last year, a fire broke out on the first floor hallway of a building in Lianxinyuan Community, Changying Township, Chaoyang District. Mr. Yang, aged 28, along with his three-year-old daughter and Ms. Gao, a friend of his wife, rushed out of their sixth-floor apartment to escape but were suffocated to death by the thick smoke in the hallway.

The Chaoyang District Fire Brigade determined that the fire was caused by a lingering fire source, with the cause being the ignition of items piled up in the hallway. Police investigations revealed that Mr. Liu Shaobo, a resident on the third floor, was the last person to enter the unit door. Surveillance footage showed that when Liu Shaobo entered the west gate of the neighborhood late at night, he was chatting and smoking with a security guard. The police concluded that after entering, Liu Shaobo threw an unextinguished cigarette butt which led to the fire. At the end of last year, Liu Shaobo was sentenced to five years in prison by the Chaoyang Court for arson.

The family of the deceased believed that the property management company failed to clear the clutter in the hallway in time; the two neighboring households who piled up fabric sofas, quilts, and other clutter in the hallway also bore some responsibility for the fire.

**Trial: Emotional Outburst Among Defendants**

Yesterday morning, both the plaintiff and defendant benches were filled with people, and the faces of the two defendant households were sullen. "We don’t want to talk about it, we’re too angry! What does this have to do with us?" several people responded indignantly to the reporter's inquiries.

"Liu Shaobo and the owner of the burning sofa should bear the responsibility!" The defendant Beijing Changying Property Management Company stated that they had assisted the residents' committee in posting notices prohibiting residents from piling up clutter in the hallway, but the residents did not heed the warnings. The company had never signed a property management agreement with the owners nor collected property fees, so they should not bear responsibility.

"I just placed a long sofa and a bed in the hallway!" A 55-year-old Ms. Yao turned her face away and said angrily. "Why is what you told the police different?" The judge then read out a large list of clutter from the file, at which point the two admitted that what they originally said was true.

Ms. Yao said that the fire was caused by Liu Shaobo and he should bear full responsibility. Moreover, several neighbors at the time had piled up old furniture in the hallway, not just them. She explained that a week before the incident, due to the inability to use the kitchen and toilet inside the house, they sought repairs from the property management and moved the furniture into the hallway. The property management did not stop them, only posting notices after the fire occurred. "The way the victims escaped was also problematic!" Ms. Yao believed that only these three people rashly came out leading to their deaths, while others stayed inside and remained safe.

"I am not breaking any laws by putting things in the hallway, and these items are not flammable or explosive!" A woman living on the second floor, Ms. Zhang, said she also thought there were issues with the victims' escape methods. It was learned that due to the intense fire, residents on the lower floors could not open their doors and ended up being saved by staying inside. However, the residents on the sixth floor were able to open their doors smoothly, which instead led to the tragedy.

Due to the lawyer of Liu Shaobo requesting a suspension of the trial, the court will deliberate further before making a decision.

By Yan Fei, Beijing Morning Post

**Post-Trial Follow-Up: Woman Staying Over at Friend's House Meets Tragic End**

In one night, Zhuang Chunxia lost two close family members—her husband and daughter—and sat silently in the plaintiff's seat, occasionally wiping away silent tears. The day before the incident, she had just returned to her hometown in Hebei to obtain a birth permit due to her pregnancy, thus escaping this calamity. On that evening, Ms. Gao, a guest staying at her home, was also present. Ms. Gao, aged 34, had a five-year-old daughter and was involved in divorce proceedings at the time, temporarily residing at Zhuang Chunxia's place. "Who would have thought that borrowing someone's house would lead to such a disaster..." lamented the attorney representing Ms. Gao's family.

**Perpetrator Appeals, Criminal Case Sent Back for Retrial**

Liu Shaobo, aged 40, was a driver for a company. That evening, he had dinner and drank alcohol with friends, and was already walking unsteadily when he returned. According to Liu Shaobo's confession, he still had a cigarette in his mouth while opening the building door, and after finishing, he casually threw the cigarette butt. At that moment, he was right next to the position where the sofa was piled. Due to fear, he did not tell the truth after the fire started.

During the trial, Liu Shaobo denied throwing the cigarette butt, but the court believed that the evidence in the case formed a complete chain of evidence, excluding the possibility of the fire being caused by others or other reasons. After the first-instance judgment, Liu Shaobo appealed to the Beijing No. 2 Intermediate People's Court. Recently, the No. 2 Intermediate Court has ruled to send the case back for retrial.
