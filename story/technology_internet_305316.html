Pellegrini Hails Real Madrid's Best Performance of the Season; Core Player Says Team Will Be Stronger in the Future

According to Sina Sports, Real Madrid's 3-2 victory over Valencia, a strong team this season, showcased a high level of play in Pellegrini's eyes. He believed that this match was Real Madrid's best performance since the start of the season and also the best overall performance since he had taken charge of the team.

Pellegrini was particularly satisfied with the players' attitude on the field because "the players moved a lot in every line," and he emphasized that what made him happy was that "whether at home or away, the players were ambitious and hoped to attack and win."

"The opponent has high-level players, and this away game was difficult, but our players played at a high level for 90 minutes," Pellegrini said. "I disagree with Emery's (Valencia's head coach) statement. A draw would be unfair to Real Madrid because, apart from the three goals we scored, we also had three or four good chances to score."

"In the first 15 minutes after the start, we encountered some difficulties, but we quickly turned the situation around. The second half was very exciting, as Valencia equalized twice," Pellegrini continued to analyze.

Pellegrini praised Benzema's performance in this game: "The overall cooperation of Real Madrid has improved, and I am not surprised by his contribution to the team because he has shown this level and potential since he joined the team. Today, his coordination with Higuaín was excellent."

Regarding Pepe, who suffered a serious knee injury in this match, Pellegrini revealed: "He twisted his knee when landing. We don't know the specific injury yet; we are waiting for the medical department's report."

Higuaín, who scored two goals, said: "I have always tried to do the right things, and I am glad I could achieve it today. My current form is good. In the first half, I missed two good chances, but I made up for it in the second half, so I am very happy. My personal achievements are built on the strength of the team. Benzema gave me great support today, and his passes to me were excellent."

Real Madrid's midfielder Xabi Alonso, who performed well in this match, believed that the team deserved to be happy about the victory but should also recognize that there is still considerable room for improvement: "Obviously, we still have a lot of room for improvement. In the past few matches, the team has shown increasing strength. The better we solve our defensive problems, the sharper our offense will become, and we must continue to adhere to this approach."
