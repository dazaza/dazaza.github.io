Love is something you have to take seriously.

by meimei234 on 2012-03-05 12:57:42

Love, we must face it seriously.

It's been three years. Yesterday when you came to see our son, you reminded me of you again!

Once the door of memory is opened, it cannot be closed immediately:

That year, I had just graduated from university and started working. For various reasons, I hadn't found a girlfriend yet. And you, my middle school classmate, one day I went online just to receive a file, and you saw me and started chatting. You said you hadn't found a boyfriend yet and asked if I had a girlfriend. I told you about my situation. Coincidentally, my family and friends were also urging me to find a girlfriend, so I began to pay attention to you. A week later, I got sick, and you came to see me. I thought you were coming to take care of me, which moved me for a long time. Later, I found out the truth and could only sigh. A few days later, you said you were going home, and then I couldn't reach you. One day, you suddenly appeared on QQ, saying that you were very sad, but you never told me why. After that, you stopped replying to my messages.

A week later, you suddenly found me again, looking exhausted and tired. But you didn't say anything to me. You stayed with me for a week but didn't talk much, only getting angry. Later, you said you wanted to find a job, so I helped you find one through someone else. After you started working, I lost contact with you again. I don't know how long it was, but now thinking back, it was probably more than ten days. You sent me a text message saying you had no money and asked me to send some to you. You knew how much I earned. I wanted to call you to ask about the situation, but you didn't answer, saying you were busy at work. Then you sent me an account number.

After the money was sent, I wanted to call you to confirm if you received it, but you disappeared again. I thought maybe you lost your phone or someone was trying to scam me. Just as I didn't know what to do, I met you on QQ. I asked if you received the money, and you simply said yes and thanked me before logging off. But I heard nothing from you for a long time.

While I was relieved that the money hadn't gone wrong, I still felt uneasy but couldn't figure out what was wrong.

Half a year later, you reappeared, saying you wanted to marry me. But I said I had nothing to offer and couldn't give you happiness. However, you said you didn't care...

On the wedding day, you seemed unhappy, as if something was troubling you. Now looking back, it was all because you were too naive. But I knew even if I asked, you wouldn't have told me anything.

After marriage, I don't know how we lived. You often played hide-and-seek with me. Every time I came home, I saw how tired you looked. Sometimes you were happy, but most of the time, you looked upset as if I had offended you in some way. This continued until our son was born. When you were sitting confinement, I took a month off to take care of you. Every day, I washed the baby's diapers. You were so indifferent to the child, as if he had nothing to do with you. Sometimes you would just sit there spacing out, ignoring the baby when he cried.

When our son was three months old, you said you wanted to go back to work. No matter how I tried to persuade you, you wouldn't listen. Once you started working, you played hide-and-seek again. Sometimes when the baby was hungry, I had to feed him formula milk. Do you know that the child is innocent! When the child was over half a year old, you said your breast milk wasn't enough, so you stopped breastfeeding. I thought you were tired from work, so I bought a lot of supplements for you, but you never appreciated it and even scolded me for being boring. When our son was one and a half years old, you had disappeared for almost a month. Looking back now, from the time we got married to when our son was one and a half years old, did you spend even three months with me and our son? Every time you disappeared, no matter how hard I tried, I couldn't find you. Sometimes I went to your company, and they said they hadn't seen you. Your family said you hadn't come home either. All I could do was wait for you to come back. Other than that, I had no choice. I trusted you and never forced you to do anything. Perhaps it was this trust that led to what happened:

One day, I took our sick son to the hospital. A friend of yours at the hospital saw me taking the child to see a doctor alone. Maybe she had a change of heart, so she finally told me the truth about your disappearances: In fact, you had been seeing your ex-boyfriend all along. The first time we met on QQ, when I was sick and you came to see me, it was entirely because you had run away after arguing with your ex-boyfriend. When you said you were going home to handle some matters, you were actually with your ex-boyfriend. After another big argument, you came back to me out of spite and asked me to help you find a job. Even the decision to marry me was made after another argument with your ex-boyfriend. But every time, you would return to your ex-boyfriend, playing your disappearing act on me. Your friend at the hospital looked at me and at the child in my arms who was barely over a year old and whispered quietly, "This child might not even be yours."

When I woke up, I saw my parents in front of me. I asked where the child was, and they said his fever had improved a little. But I fainted again, exhausted. I asked where my wife had gone, and I could only lie to them, saying she was busy with work. But at that moment, my heart was in great pain.

When she stood in front of me again, humming a little tune, she didn't notice my exhaustion or that our son was sick. When I placed the prepared divorce agreement in her hands, I trembled slightly, saying nothing. I only requested that I get custody of our son, and she could take whatever else she wanted. She silently agreed and moved out, taking with her a large amount of stuff. Actually, those things were originally mine, and in the end, she also took several tens of thousands of yuan from me.

I won't talk about how I raised our son afterward; the bitterness can only be understood by those who have experienced it. But when our son called me "Dad" for the first time, I, who hadn't shed tears for a long time, burst into tears uncontrollably. The little guy looked at me in confusion. I decided then that regardless of whether he was biologically mine, he was my son, and I would raise him well, teach him how to be a good person, and let him truly be himself.

When I was almost able to forget the past, our son started kindergarten. That day, she came to see him again. I didn't want her to see him, but the son should call her "Mom," and he has the right to know his mother. I couldn't deprive them of that right. When she came to our house, the son saw her, ran to me, and refused to let her hold him. I could see how awkward she felt, and I helplessly shook my head. I told her, "It's all over now. Live your own life." She wanted to take the son to her place, but the son refused to go.

When she left, she gave 500 yuan to our son, who playfully said, "Dad said we shouldn't take things from strangers. It's best if everything belongs to us. In the future, I will support myself and Dad." I felt both heartbroken and comforted. Our son was less than four years old, and at the same time, I saw hope.

Actually, I really want to say sorry to our son, but how can I bring it up? Today, your visit reminded me of you again. Perhaps I will never forget you, but every time I think of you, I treat the people around me even better. Tomorrow is the weekend. Since I saw you today, I will spend the whole day tomorrow with our son to make up for it! That's probably the end of the story. Life must go on. Our son is growing up, and hopes are gradually being realized. It might be tough, but I believe it's worth it. Moreover, I don't blame you. On the contrary, I thank you. It's because of you that I understand life and people like you!

Finally, I want to warn young men and women who haven't started families yet: Love, marriage, and family are not games. We must face them seriously. Also, children are never wrong. We cannot impose our mistakes on them. At the same time, everyone has the right to make their own choices, but the one thing you cannot choose is who your parents are. So, no matter what, they will always be your parents!
