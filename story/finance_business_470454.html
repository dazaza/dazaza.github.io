SFDA talks with online pharmacy: e-commerce violation triggers debate again

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:24:52

CFDA Summons Online Pharmacies, Aiming to Send a Warning Signal

E-commerce drug sales face numerous obstacles, sparking renewed debate over legality.

An announcement from the Beijing Food and Drug Administration (BFDA) recently confirmed earlier industry rumors that relevant authorities intended to warn B2C online pharmacies.

The announcement revealed that the BFDA had recently summoned the heads of nine websites in Beijing that have obtained qualifications for internet pharmaceutical transactions. This was in response to recent media exposure of "a few websites outside Beijing selling drugs to individuals without obtaining the necessary pharmaceutical transaction service qualifications." The BFDA demanded that these websites not sell drugs in violation of regulations.

This round of summonses by the BFDA targeted online pharmacies that e-commerce platforms like Taobao were aggressively courting. Since online pharmacies are directly supervised by the drug administration, the deeper implications of this move should not be underestimated.

Taobao-Contracted Pharmacies Summoned

Among those summoned by the BFDA were nine internet transaction websites that have already obtained qualifications for internet pharmaceutical transactions. However, the BFDA emphasized that B2C online pharmacies must not conduct transactions through unapproved third-party trading websites. Additionally, B2C online pharmacies must not use their own websites to provide internet pharmaceutical transaction services for enterprises outside their membership.

The BFDA also specifically conveyed the spirit of the National Food and Drug Administration's (NFDA) "Notice on Further Intensifying Crackdowns on False Pharmaceutical Information and Illegal Sales of Drugs via the Internet," and informed about the investigation results regarding suspected unauthorized operation of pharmaceutical websites by the NFDA and relevant provinces.

Previously, the pharmaceutical section of Taobao Mall, headquartered in Hangzhou, Zhejiang, was shut down by the Zhejiang Food and Drug Administration for engaging in B2C pharmaceutical business without proper certification.

However, Taobao Mall has so far refused to acknowledge its handling by the Zhejiang Food and Drug Administration. Nearly a month later, links with five contracted B2C online pharmacies remain disconnected. The official reason given is still: "Due to the independent operation of Taobao Mall and system adjustments, we are upgrading the external redirection system for product links to merchants' official websites. Users who wish to purchase should directly log into the merchants' official websites. We will restore the system as soon as possible after the upgrade."

Upon checking the Jinxiang Pharmacy Online Mall, which cooperates with Taobao, it was found that on the page of Taobao Mall's pharmaceutical section, all traces of previously sold drugs and medical devices from Jinxiang Pharmacy Online Mall have disappeared. Currently, only health food and cosmetics can still be found.

"Facilitating" Transactions Deemed Non-compliant

According to the "Interim Provisions on the Approval of Internet Pharmaceutical Transaction Services" issued and enforced by the National Food and Drug Administration in 2005, only enterprises that simultaneously possess the "Internet Pharmaceutical Transaction Service Qualification Certificate" and the "Internet Pharmaceutical Information Service Qualification Certificate" (referred to as the "two certificates") may engage in online drug sales. Enterprises with only the "Internet Pharmaceutical Information Service Qualification Certificate" may only display drug information and may not conduct transactions. However, these interim provisions do not specify detailed rules regarding cooperation between B2C online pharmacies that have obtained the "two certificates" and other third-party e-commerce platforms.

Industry experts believe that this summons by the BFDA to the responsible persons of nine websites in the city with internet pharmaceutical transaction service qualifications will make B2C online pharmacies more cautious when cooperating with e-commerce platforms in the future. This poses a new challenge for e-commerce platforms trying to attract B2C online pharmacies.

Upon reviewing the "Measures for the Administration of Internet Pharmaceutical Information Services," it was found that Article 24 stipulates that even if an enterprise has obtained the "Internet Pharmaceutical Information Service Qualification Certificate," providing pharmaceutical information that directly facilitates online drug transactions is considered non-compliant. Once determined as non-compliant, penalties may include warnings, fines, or even criminal prosecution by judicial authorities.

It is rumored within the industry that last month, the Zhejiang Food and Drug Administration required Taobao Mall, which already possesses the "Internet Pharmaceutical Information Service Qualification Certificate," to rectify its operations based precisely on this management measure.

Currently, without any revision schedule for the "Interim Provisions on the Approval of Internet Pharmaceutical Transaction Services" and the "Measures for the Administration of Internet Pharmaceutical Information Services," Taobao Mall, which has been forced to disconnect links with five B2C online pharmacies, appears to have only two paths forward:

Firstly, adopt the approach used by JD.com in partnering with Hao Yao Shi online pharmacy—directly invest in a B2C online pharmacy while abandoning links with B2C online pharmacies, expanding business solely on the existing website of the B2C online pharmacy;

Secondly, first establish physical retail chain drugstores, apply for a pharmaceutical operation license, then apply to the National Food and Drug Administration for the "Internet Pharmaceutical Transaction Service Qualification Certificate," since according to the "Interim Provisions on the Approval of Internet Pharmaceutical Transaction Services," only legally established retail chain drugstores may apply for this qualification.

When contacted by Southern Metropolis Daily regarding the BFDA's summoning of online pharmacies, Yan Qiao, the public relations director of Taobao Mall, stated that Taobao was unable to comment. However, he emphasized that Taobao ventured into the B2C pharmaceutical business on the basis of obtaining qualifications for displaying drugs on the internet, and has always operated in compliance with laws and regulations. Regarding the issue of prolonged disconnection of links, he explained that Taobao is currently undergoing internal restructuring, and after the restructuring, the Taobao Mall responsible for the pharmaceutical section will operate independently.

B2C online pharmacy representatives interviewed by Southern Metropolis Daily generally believed that the actions taken by the drug administration were still mainly out of consideration for safe medication use, though there are differing opinions within the industry.

Unwavering Determination to Enter B2C E-commerce Pharmacies

However, some domestic individuals dedicated to expanding B2C pharmaceutical businesses see this merely as a setback that inevitably occurs during the development of new business models. They believe that B2C pharmaceuticals represent a trend.

In Su Zhan's view, CEO of Jianke.com, "The operation of B2C online pharmacies and the proliferation of counterfeit drugs online are two different issues. Strengthening supervision by relevant departments is necessary to combat counterfeit drugs, but this has no direct connection to the operation of legitimate online pharmacies. Currently, B2C pharmacies selling drugs online all have legal qualifications and operate relatively standardly."

The self-rectifying Taobao Mall pharmaceutical section is actively taking measures. It launched a rewarded survey on July 25 targeting individual consumers purchasing pharmaceutical products to formulate a more reasonable market expansion plan.

A relevant person in charge of Guangdong Dachanlin Chain Pharmacy even believed that strengthening supervision would, in the long term, increase consumer confidence in buying drugs online.

"Our company plans to officially launch an e-commerce platform by the end of this year. Our engagement in e-commerce is not just for profit; it also provides a direct channel to communicate with suppliers and consumers. Of course, higher sales volumes will also enhance our bargaining power with suppliers," said the person from Dachanlin Chain Pharmacy.

A researcher from Sinopharm Holdings, which was rumored to potentially partner with dangdang.com, also agreed with the "inevitable trend" theory. However, he also believed that since official attitudes indicate that third-party platforms, including e-commerce ones, must obtain relevant documents before selling drugs, parties entering the B2C pharmaceutical business cannot rush into it.

Data shows that among the 38 B2C enterprises in China that can "provide drugs to individual consumers," none have yet formed a significant presence, and most are in a loss-making state.

Positive signals are also emerging. At the end of last month, the National Food and Drug Administration initiated the pilot project "Research, Development, and Application Demonstration of Key Technologies for Monitoring the Internet Pharmaceutical Market and Public Services." It aims to standardize and monitor internet pharmaceutical market entry, information, and transaction activities throughout the process, establishing a credible environment for internet pharmaceutical transactions and services. To ensure the smooth implementation of this research outcome, the National Food and Drug Administration has decided to conduct pilot projects in Zhejiang and Guangdong provinces.

Viewpoint Comparison

Drug Supervision Department:

Only enterprises possessing both the "Internet Pharmaceutical Transaction Service Qualification Certificate" and the "Internet Pharmaceutical Information Service Qualification Certificate" can conduct online drug sales. Enterprises with only the "Information Certificate" can only display drug information and cannot conduct transactions.

If enterprises with the "Information Certificate" provide drug information that directly facilitates online drug transactions, it is also considered non-compliant behavior.

Representatives of B2C Pharmacies:

Regulatory departments should clarify how the concept of "direct facilitation" is defined.

Internet pharmaceutical transactions and the proliferation of counterfeit drugs are not directly related; the solution lies in strengthening supervision.

Why is it considered non-compliant for a third-party platform established by e-commerce companies, similar to a department store, to allow qualified enterprises to sell drugs in the provided space?

Reporters Ma Jianzhong, Interns Sheng Jiawan and Jiang Chenyue
