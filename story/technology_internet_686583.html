Self-driving Tour Plan for Jiangxi Wuyuan Rapeseed Flowers during the 2012 Qingming Festival Holiday

With a three-day holiday scheduled for the Qingming Festival in 2012, we can take advantage of this short break to embark on a self-driving tour to Wuyuan, Jiangxi. Known as "China's Most Beautiful Village," Wuyuan in Jiangxi Province offers an experience of simplicity and natural charm through its ancient town architecture and rural landscapes. Walking along the stone-paved roads of the countryside and crossing the arched stone bridges over streams, you will find rapeseed flowers blooming amidst the Huizhou-style farmhouses.

Every March and April, Wuyuan becomes filled with the fragrance of rapeseed flowers. When they bloom all over the mountains and fields, it marks the most beautiful time of the year there.

The rapeseed flowers in Wuyuan differ from those in many other places. In Wuyuan, these flowers possess a unique charm: surrounded by mountains, blue skies, and clear waters, the Huizhou-style dwellings with their white walls and black tiles are nestled among lush greenery. Ancient villages dot the vast fields of rapeseed flowers, creating a picturesque scene reminiscent of Chinese ink paintings. Pink peach blossoms and white pear flowers further embellish the golden sea of rapeseed flowers, complementing the white walls and gray tiles of Huizhou architecture. This serene environment provides solace for those escaping the hustle and bustle of urban life. Spending the night at a local farmhouse, enjoying a pot of fragrant rice wine and a couple of simple dishes, costs only a few dozen yuan but elevates one to the pinnacle of life's pleasures.

I. How to Reach Wuyuan

To enter Wuyuan by car, you can generally come from Quzhou, Huangshan, Jingdezhen, or Shangrao.

1. From Quzhou to Wuyuan, it is recommended that tourists from Shanghai and Zhejiang enter Wuyuan via this route. There are highways within Zhejiang up to Quzhou, and from Quzhou to Wuyuan, it's National Highway 320 turning into a secondary provincial road, with good traffic conditions. The distance from Quzhou to Wuyuan is about 150 kilometers.

2. From Huangshan (Tunxi) to Wuyuan, it is suggested that tourists from Jiangsu and Anhui enter Wuyuan via this route. Additionally, self-driving tourists who have visited southern Anhui and Huangshan can enter and exit Wuyuan through this path. From Huangshan to Wuyuan, it’s a secondary asphalt provincial road, approximately 110 kilometers long. Note that many sections pass through mountainous areas with numerous bends.

3. From Jingdezhen to Wuyuan, visitors coming from Hubei or Nanchang can enter Wuyuan through this route. From Jiujiang to Jingdezhen, it's a highway, about 140 kilometers long, and from Jingdezhen to Wuyuan, it's an 83-kilometer secondary asphalt provincial road.

4. From Shangrao to Wuyuan, tourists from Fujian can enter Wuyuan through this route. The entire journey is about 180 kilometers on a secondary asphalt provincial road.

5. For self-driving tourists coming from Mount Sanqing to Wuyuan, detailed information about this route hasn't been fully clarified yet, and current materials suggest some detours. Further updates will be provided once new data becomes available.

II. Road Conditions within Wuyuan

Wuyuan has many sandy soil roads, but asphalt roads have also been constructed extensively.

Detailed situations are as follows:

1. Asphalt Roads in Wuyuan:

① Jingbai Highway from Jingdezhen through Wuyuan to Zhejiang, directly connecting to Quzhou, passing through Fuchun, Zhongyun, Gaoshan, Wuyuan County, and Meilin. The distance from Jingdezhen to Wuyuan County is more than 80 kilometers. Due to the aged condition of the road, there are some potholes, so it is advised to drive below 60 km/h.

② Xilu Road from Wuyuan County to Anhui, leading directly to Huangshan, passing through Likeng, Wangkou, Jiangwan, and Daban. The distance from Wuyuan to Huangshan is approximately 110 kilometers. It is recommended to drive below 80 km/h.

③ Wuyuan-Wuqing Road from the county seat to Qinghua, a newly paved secondary asphalt road, passing through Siku to Qinghua. Approximately 25 kilometers, it is advised to drive below 80 km/h.

④ Jiangwan-Xiaoqi Road: A newly paved tertiary asphalt road, about 8 kilometers, it is suggested to drive below 60 km/h.

⑤ Siku-Yancun-Sixi Road: A newly paved tertiary asphalt road, about 2 kilometers, it is recommended to drive below 60 km/h.

2. Sandy Soil Roads in Wuyuan:

① Xiaoqi-Jiangling-Gaoshanpinghu-Qingyuan: This section includes about 10 kilometers from Xiaoqi to Jiangling, with a flat surface, evenly distributed sand, but narrow roadways, suggesting a speed around 40 km/h. From Wuyuan Jiangling to Gaoshanpinghu (first station), it's about 8 kilometers, and from Gaoshanpinghu to Qingyuan, it's about 10 kilometers. This segment has many pebbles and potholes, mostly winding mountain roads with sharp turns, suggesting a speed of 30 km/h, slowing down and honking at bends.

② Qinghua-Likeng Road: Approximately 25 kilometers, with relatively smooth surfaces but slight potholes, it is recommended to drive at 40 km/h.

③ Qinghua-Zheyuan-Hongguan Road: About 15 kilometers from Qinghua to Zheyuan, and about 10 kilometers from Zheyuan to Hongguan, with many pebble-filled potholes, it is advised to drive below 40 km/h.

④ Qinghua-Zhangshan-Dazhangshan Road: About 10 kilometers from Qinghua to Zhangshan Township, currently under construction for asphalt paving, making the road muddy and difficult to navigate due to large potholes. Speed is not recommended; simply getting through is the priority. Estimated improvements by May with gravel laid down. About 10 kilometers from Zhangshan Township to the foot of Dazhangshan Mountain, a general sandy soil road, it is suggested to drive below 40 km/h. From Shangcun to Dazhangshan Village, a winding mountain road with many turns and rocky surfaces, approximately 15 kilometers, it is generally not recommended for self-driving tours. Instead, taking a local minibus is advised. If insisting on driving, remember to proceed slowly as the roadside cliffs are hazardous.

3. Three-Day Tour

Based on a two-day tour, you can add Hongcun in Qinghua and the Western Line's Yuanyang Lake.

In summary, when driving on sandy soil roads in Wuyuan, be mindful of narrow roads, numerous bends, and cautious driving practices.
