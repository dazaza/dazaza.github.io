Yesterday, American media reported that the attacks on Google originated from Shanghai Jiao Tong University. Today, Shanghai Jiao Tong University responded to this accusation, and experts have analyzed similarly to what I wrote in my article yesterday - it is likely that their computers were used as "bots" (compromised computers). (I learned all this from novels; does that mean the novel author is a hacker?)

Foreign media claims Google was attacked by Jiao Tong University and Lanxiang Technical School.

Shanghai Jiaotong University stated they never support hacking activities and are currently investigating. Experts believe the university's computers may have been remotely controlled by hackers.

By Morning Post reporters Mia Xiaoli, Dong Chuanfeng, and Zhou Kai,

Yesterday, multiple media outlets quoted The New York Times reporting that investigators had traced the IP addresses involved in the cyberattacks on Google back to Shanghai Jiao Tong University and Shandong Lanxiang Senior Technical School. Shanghai Jiaotong University stated that they do not support any hacking behavior, and they only became aware of the situation through media reports. They are currently investigating and verifying the matter; representatives from Lanxiang Technical School said they were “unaware” of the incident. Industry insiders pointed out that stealing third-party IP addresses for attacks is a common tactic used by hackers, making it unreliable to judge the true perpetrator based solely on this information.

Foreign Media: Schools Could Also Be Victims

According to foreign media citing an unnamed source, positioning investigations revealed that the source of the hacker attack could be traced back to Shanghai Jiao Tong University and a vocational training school - Shandong Lanxiang Senior Technical School. According to the source, the attacks on Google might have started as early as April 2009, earlier than previously anticipated. They also discovered that hackers exploited vulnerabilities in Microsoft’s Internet Explorer browser. The report further "revealed" that Lanxiang Technical School has a foreign teacher who specifically teaches special computer skills.

However, foreign media also admitted that determining the origin of the attack is actually quite difficult. In many cases, computer equipment located in different countries is used as intermediary steps in the attack. Hackers use virus programs or other technologies to issue commands to targets located elsewhere. Bruce Schneier, Chief Security Technology Officer of BTGroup, quoted in The Wall Street Journal, said that what we know now is that this computer participated in the attack, but this does not necessarily mean the attack originated here. We don't know if someone was sitting in front of this computer acting as the mastermind.

Lanxiang: High School Level Students Can’t Be Hackers

"We also heard about this news one-sidedly," Shanghai Jiao Tong University said yesterday to the Morning Post. Currently, the school is still investigating and verifying this information. It can be confirmed that Shanghai Jiao Tong University has never supported any hacking behavior.

Subsequently, when reporters called Lanxiang Technical School, two staff members who answered the phone both claimed they were "unaware" of the matter. Previously, the head of Lanxiang's Computer Department told the media that their students would not be capable of attacking Google or other American companies because they are merely high school-level students with limited skills. Additionally, the school implements strict management measures, making it difficult for outsiders to easily enter.

On Lanxiang Technical School's official website, you can see that their computer science curriculum is similar to that of ordinary technical schools, including advanced technician classes, graphic advertising design classes, environmental art design classes, network technology classes, computer repair classes, and advanced technician computer classes. It is indeed hard to associate them with "attacking Google."

Relevant personnel from Google China were very cautious about this news and did not provide comments.

Netizens Exclaim at the "Too Shocking" News

The news that "a technical school in Shandong attacked Google" spread domestically, and netizens generally found it extremely "shocking," exclaiming, "Lanxiang Technical School has become famous this time."

Some netizens humorously joked, "To learn hacking, go to Lanxiang," suggesting that this was a carefully designed embedded advertisement by Lanxiang Technical School. Over the past decade, their continuous advertising efforts probably haven't achieved results as good as this incident.

A netizen named "Drunken Flower Splendor Soul Dance" posted on Sina Weibo, saying, "If IT companies ranked among the top in the world couldn't withstand so-called student hacker attacks, then shouldn't these companies fire their employees and hire students instead?"

Possible Culprits: Eastern European Crime Organizations

Industry insiders disclosed that hackers often use various technical means to control computers thousands of kilometers away (commonly referred to as "bots") to launch attacks on specific targets. Even if investigators trace the attack back to a certain school's computer, the students or faculty of that school often have no idea what happened.

It is worth noting that in a report disclosed on the same day, NetWitness, a well-known U.S. computer security company, stated that hackers successfully infiltrated nearly 2500 companies and government agencies worldwide during a coordinated global attack, stealing a massive amount of personal and corporate secrets. "Evidence suggests that Eastern European criminal organizations are behind this operation, possibly using some Chinese computers since Chinese computers are easier to manipulate without being detected," said Joseph, the CEO of NetWitness.
