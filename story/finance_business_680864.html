Fair internal linking promotes the ranking of inner pages. For many new websites, it takes time for search engines to index the site and give it a good weight, which is not something that can be achieved overnight. Therefore, the most important thing for a new website in its early stages is to have the right mindset, do the right things, and not always worry about indexing and snapshots or PR. Since a website is at different stages, it will have different positioning. Thus, new websites should focus more on content, maintain the right mindset, do the correct things, and accumulate weight over time. The desired results will naturally come.

The core lies in having the right mindset.

New websites without external links to enhance weight, and unfair internal linking to boost the ranking of inner pages, often have incomplete data. Sometimes, they are constantly being revised and improved. So for new websites, it is more about adapting and adjusting. Additionally, the core for new websites is not about looking at data but maintaining stability while maximizing content expansion, reasonably arranging various copywriting, and digging out long-tail keywords to fill content. This is the focus. Checking rankings and indexing every day not only affects time but also slows down content construction, which is overall detrimental to the development of the website. In summary, the core for new websites lies in having the right mindset and following a predetermined strategy to enrich and improve the content of the website as the foundation.

When we have the right mindset and focus on our key tasks, the website will gradually become more standardized. Indexing, snapshots, PR, weight, and even keyword rankings will move toward our predetermined goals. But from another perspective, how can we make the website gradually become more standardized? While having the right mindset, the following few tasks should also be done well, summarized below.

First, improve structure and details.

Although new websites undergo testing before going live, this testing is not with large amounts of data and lacks user interaction, so there are certain limitations. After our website goes live and operates for some time, adjustments will be made based on actual usage and user feedback. These adjustments are based on reasonable user experiences and sometimes even come from customer requests.

Second, determine an updating content method.

There are many ways to update website content. Different types of websites require different methods. For general small websites, it is sufficient to progressively update original content ourselves. However, for forum-type websites, users can post updates themselves, and we just need to guide them. For large websites, sometimes batch updates are needed, requiring the use of certain programs, and necessary times may involve collecting and pseudo-original content.

Third, be good at promotion and continuously increase external links.

In terms of promotion, a skilled method needs to be found. Paid promotion, of course, shows results the fastest, but when the promotion budget is limited, a lot of free promotion is still needed. In fact, promotion methods are like the eighteen martial arts; you don't need to be proficient in all of them, just being good at one is enough. Many webmasters like to use soft articles for promotion, so write more soft articles. They can make our website appear in multiple places on the network and also increase many external links. Don't doubt the power of one method. When you practice it long-term, become more adept at it, and master its essence, you will find that the results it brings are often beyond imagination.
