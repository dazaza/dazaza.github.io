Mr. Wang: Techniques for QQ Group Promotion in Online Marketing

by anonymous on 2010-11-20 17:57:34

QQ is a household name for Chinese internet users. It’s not an exaggeration to say that almost everyone has an account, and many have several. This has created a vast user base, making QQ groups an indispensable channel for online promotion. Here, Wang Dajun will share with you some insights and experiences on promoting through QQ groups.

To promote via QQ groups, the first requirement is obviously having a group, which can be achieved in two ways:

1. Joining someone else's QQ group

When venturing into a particular industry, it's crucial to understand it. Joining industry-specific groups is the best way to do this, as it allows you to interact with industry veterans and seek cooperation. It's quite normal for online marketers to join more than 30 industry groups (this is also a basic requirement set by teacher Jiang Likun when recruiting core team members for his program, prompting me to aggressively add groups. I learned that each QQ number can add up to 20 groups per day). Choosing the right target groups is also a key point in QQ group promotion.

When joining groups, there are a few points to keep in mind:

1. Groups with too few people are generally not worth joining, as they lack activity or may be internal groups not open to the public, which would be meaningless for you.

2. Inactive groups should also be avoided. Even if there are many members, if they don’t communicate or engage in activities, they’re not useful to you.

3. Don't join homogenized groups. Even if you join multiple groups, the same people might be active in all of them, so adding more wouldn't make much difference.

4. Most groups usually aren't open to the public and require verification. The better the group, the higher the threshold. Before filling out the verification information, learn about the group and fill it out accordingly to show sincerity to the group owner.

5. After joining a group, get into the habit of reading the group rules and updating your profile promptly. You need to follow the rules when you're on someone else's turf. As a promoter, seize every opportunity to subtly incorporate your promotional message into your profile. Remember to add symbols beneficial for ranking (usually just adding a space is enough) so that others see your presence every time they log in, knowing the potential promotional effect.

6. Don’t immediately post ads after joining a group; it's the most annoying thing to do. Nowadays, users are immune to such scrolling ads, and they're ineffective. All you'll get is a kick from the admin and disdain from other members. The correct approach is to maintain a certain level of activity within the group, frequently interacting with other members. Once you've built rapport, the group members will trust your promotions. When you become an active member of the group, occasionally posting an ad won't result in being kicked out. If you really don't have time to interact, at least try to stay visible, like greeting the group members every morning before work.

2. Building your own QQ group

As the popular saying goes: "My territory, my rules." Promoting in someone else's group is never as effective as doing so in your own. This stems from the authority that group owners and administrators hold. As teacher Mou Changqing once said: "When the group owner posts links, it's considered sharing; when regular members do, it's advertising. So everyone should build more groups." Building QQ groups was the second assignment given by teacher Jiang Likun, and I became particularly excited when my first article on network marketing, "How to Judge the Quality of a Domain Name and Its Relationship with SEO Website Optimization," was marked as an excellent post on his forum. This greatly boosted my confidence as a beginner to write more and practice more. Again, I thank teacher Jiang Likun.

Excitement aside, let's get back to the topic. Some newbies might say their QQ level isn't high enough to create a group, or that their QQ can only create one group. Wang Dajun will tell you a well-known secret: become a VIP member. A regular account needs to be level 15 (one sun) to create a 100-person regular group, while a level 4 (one moon) VIP member can create four 200-person advanced groups. If you upgrade to VIP level 6, you can create one 500-person super group and four 200-person advanced groups. Here, Wang Dajun reminds everyone that paying annually for membership is more cost-effective. If I'm not mistaken, payments through online banking receive a 10% discount, while payments through Tenpay receive an 8.8% discount.

When creating a group, keep the following points in mind:

1. If conditions allow, create more advanced groups. More people mean more scale, giving the impression of strength and authority, which many people believe.

2. Naming the group should be targeted. For example, my group is named "Network Marketing Elite Tribe," and from this name, you can understand its positioning. Everyone is welcome to join my QQ group: 120973316, to exchange ideas on network marketing. However, I hope members who join can have a good user experience, so those who only post scrolling ads, dislike communication, and refuse mutual benefits are kindly asked to bypass.

3. Pay attention to the gender ratio of group members. As the saying goes: "Men and women working together makes the job lighter," this truth is as real as gravity. Female members in QQ groups have sufficient appeal.

4. Recruit responsible administrators. One person's power is limited, and to keep the group healthy and active, this is necessary. On this point, Wang Dajun has an idea of implementing an elimination system for administrators through group voting, hoping to increase member activity. The results will be shared after validation. Those interested can follow Wang Dajun's Network Marketing Blog (www.wangdajun.net) or try it themselves.

5. Maintain group activity so that members feel a sense of belonging and trust, laying the foundation for future promotions. This highlights the importance of points 3 and 4 above. Organize more activities within the group to enhance interaction among members.

QQ group promotion is also a long-term process that requires building long-term relationships within the group to achieve good promotional effects.

1. Whether joining or creating a group, establish emotional connections before promoting. As mentioned earlier, regularly appearing and communicating with group members is essential.

2. Post the same promotional information only once a day. Repeatedly posting the same unnecessary information can be annoying and easily cause resentment, especially in others' groups where you might be forced out.

3. Use default fonts and colors for comfort. I remember seeing a brother post an advertisement with not many words, but it didn't fit on one screen and had various colors, causing dizziness. Such promotions can't help but be disliked.

4. Avoid hard-sell advertisements and instead integrate marketing naturally into daily conversations. Natural integration is the easiest way for others to accept.

5. Make full use of the group name and bulletin board features, though these can only be operated by the group owner or administrator. Therefore, building your own groups or serving as an administrator is recommended.

6. Make full use of group emails, which I find to be a very effective method of QQ group promotion. The reason is that even when members are offline, they'll still receive email notifications upon logging back in, and these emails will remain in their QQ mailboxes for a long time. However, the downside is that the group must have activated the group email function. Additionally, Wang Dajun reminds group owners that the setting for group emails is located in QQ Mail, not in the QQ group itself. Also, sending group emails should be done in moderation, otherwise, it could be flagged as spam by Tencent officials, locking your mass-sending function, including complaints from group members, which again tests your interpersonal skills.

7. Make full use of group space functions such as group forums, group sharing, and group voting. These are excellent promotional channels. In the era when QQ users are highly immune to hard-sell rolling instant advertisements, we must adapt our promotional methods to achieve any effect.
