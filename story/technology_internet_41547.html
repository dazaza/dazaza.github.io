yo2.cn: A New Direction for BSP

by hsia on 2008-01-05 11:27:05

Starting today, NetEnter will recommend a featured site every day. Ranking first is yo2.cn, and I would like to thank it for providing me with such comprehensive blog services.

Even though yo2 is already very popular now, it still belongs to a small website in its start-up phase: the entire site was officially established in 2007, and basically from hardware to software, it is maintained by oneoo alone. The number of paying users is less than a hundred... However, it has already occupied the forefront of the new generation of BSPs (blog service providers), possessing a complete model for growth. If it can continue to persist, "the path may be winding, but the future is bright."

As shown in the first image, yo2's positioning features three characteristics: blogs, community, and services. Hidden behind these is the maximization of user permissions and the social services of the main yo2 site as the connecting link for all users. Unlike Sina and other currently mainstream BSP providers in China, yo2 has gained a more professional foundation through the popular WordPress service and relevant Chinese localization plugins right from its debut. Blogs that users create for free on yo2 have extensive openness and autonomy, no longer being constrained by the rules of traditional BSPs. yo2 has abandoned traditional practices and remains behind the scenes of user blogs (if you wish, you can completely eliminate yo2 from your page, which is a hallmark feature of maximum user authority), focusing on providing faster and more perfect services.
