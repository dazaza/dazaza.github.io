International Women's Day celebration plans

by oecsvrcg55 on 2012-03-03 15:50:03

Activity objectives:

1. To know that March 8th is Women's Day, a holiday for mothers, grandmothers, and other women.

2. Through parent-child activities, to cultivate care for the elders and enhance emotional bonds with them.

Preparations:

1. Music tape "The World Only Has a Good Mother"

2. Six hula hoops and some candies

Process:

1. Play the music "The World Only Has a Good Mother" - The world only has a good mother, having a mother makes one feel like a treasure...

(Teacher) Do you little friends know what this song is called? (Children) The world only has a good mother.

2. Today, we've invited our mothers and grandmothers to join us in our activities. Do you know which festival we are celebrating? Whose festival is it?

(Children) It's the mothers' festival.

(Teacher) Today is not only the mothers' festival but also the festival for grandmothers, maternal grandmothers, aunts, and all women.

3. What do mothers do at home? What does grandmother do at home? (Children respond)

With mothers and grandmothers working so hard, what should we do for them? (Children respond)

4. Mothers do many things for us every day, besides work, they also take care of us. Mothers work very hard! Today, let's spend a happy "March 8th" festival with our mothers, okay?

Interactive Segment:

"Inside Out"

Game Preparation: Six hula hoops

Game Rules: Children jump into the hoop first, then the mother jumps in. After both jump into the hoop, the child takes out the hula hoop from down to up and places it in front, continuing to jump inside. Until reaching the end point, the child and mother stand in the hoop and run back to the starting point. Whoever reaches the starting point first wins. (Winners can receive prizes)

Three, Feeding Candy to Mom

(Teacher) Usually, mothers work hard. In the past at home, mothers always gave us candy. Now we have grown up and can do many things. Let mom rest well today, and we will feed candy to our moms ourselves, alright?

Today, our babies and moms gather together to celebrate the festival of moms. Are you happy? We hope our babies will make moms happy every day in the future. Finally, let's send a song called "The World Only Has a Good Mother" to all moms, wishing all moms eternal youth and happiness!

Objectives of the activity:

1. To know that March 8th is Women's Day, a festival for mothers.

2. To understand the work and interests of mothers and love mothers.

3. Through performances and games with mothers, express celebrations for mothers.

Material Preparations:

1. Some song tapes such as "My Good Mother," "The World Only Has a Good Mother," "Little Crows Love Their Mothers," etc.

2. Gifts prepared by children for their mothers. Flowers, small prizes.

3. Inform mothers to participate in the activity.

Process:

Introduction

(Teacher) "Today, there are so many mothers in our kindergarten. Do you know why?"

(Children respond.) Teacher: "Yes, the International Women's Day has arrived. Today is the festival for all mothers, aunts, and grandmothers around the world. We are gathered here to celebrate the festival of mothers. Are you excited?" Let's clap our hands to welcome our mothers. (Kindergarten claps) Let's sing a song together called 'The World Only Has a Good Mother' or 'My Good Mother.' (Children sing collectively) Please introduce your mother's name and her job. "Little friends, what is your mother's name, and where does she work?" (Individual children introduce) Guess the fruits that mothers like to eat. "Do you love your mother? Then do you know what fruit your mother likes the most? Think about it first, don't say it out loud. I'll ask the mothers to write down the fruits they like on paper, then I'll ask the children to guess." Correct guesses win prizes.

Performances: "Shoemaker Dance," "Clapping Flower Ropes," Chair Grabbing Games, Parent-Child Cooperation Tossing Paper Ball Competitions, Bean Clamping Competitions, Peeling Lychees to Feed Moms Competitions. Flowers Presented to Good Moms, each child holds a red carnation and presents it to their mom. Encourage children to express their feelings to their mothers generously.

Teacher Summary: Our mothers are really capable and work so hard. Let's thank our mothers together: "Mom, you've worked hard. Mom, I love you! Mom, you're the best!" The teacher asks the children to whisper sweet words to their mothers. After the children finish whispering sweet words, the teacher says: "Our whispers are our hearts. Mom, thank you. Mom, wish you a happy festival." All children: "Wish mom a happy festival." Applause rises. Activity ends.
