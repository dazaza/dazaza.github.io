Collagen is an extracellular protein, a fibrous protein formed by three peptide chains twisted into a helical shape. It is at the forefront of biomedical beauty, discovered by SINOMOS/A29. Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body, accounting for more than 30% of the total proteins in the body. It is rich in amino acids such as glycine, proline, and hydroxyproline that the body needs, and is the most important component of the extracellular matrix.

Collagen is called "the bone of bones, the skin of skins, the flesh of flesh". It can be said to be a strong support for the dermis layer, and its effect on the skin is self-evident. Don't underestimate it; your smooth, hydrated, and elastic skin all depend on the collagen contained within.

As a new concept in beauty products, there are many types of collagen formulations available on the market, including collagen powder, collagen tablets, and collagen oral liquids. Among these, the most debated question is whether collagen powder or liquid collagen is better.

In fact, each type of collagen has its own advantages and disadvantages.

Let's first understand collagen liquid. Liquid collagen products are now a very common formulation. The best absorption is the biggest advantage of collagen liquid. This is something no other collagen formulation can match. However, precisely because it is in liquid form, to ensure it does not spoil during production and transportation, some flavoring agents and preservatives need to be added. This significantly reduces the purity of the collagen, which is a major bottleneck for liquid collagen products. Although drinking it feels like crystal grapes, the actual effects on our skin are minimal. In summary, the advantages of collagen oral liquid are: easy for the human body to absorb. The disadvantage is: low content. Therefore, claims by some companies about high-content liquid collagen have exaggerated components and cannot be highly recommended.

Next is collagen powder. The biggest advantage of collagen powder is that it can reach a very high purity, so powdered collagen products account for a large portion of the market. However, collagen powder has a fatal flaw: it is easily destroyed by stomach acid, preventing it from reaching the intestines smoothly, with only a portion being absorbed and utilized by the skin. Unless the molecular weight is around 1000 daltons, allowing direct absorption. In summary, the advantage of collagen powder is: the content is much higher than oral liquid. Disadvantage: you need to choose the right product and use appropriate preparation methods.

Therefore, the quality of different types of collagen products cannot be judged universally. The key is to choose branded products, as regular brand products have certain quality guarantees.
