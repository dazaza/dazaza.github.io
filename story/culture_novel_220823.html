"If nothing goes wrong, Gong Ming will be a great bride..." Tang Ze thought while absentmindedly staring at his computer screen with a cigarette in his mouth. On the screen was the portrait of an eerily beautiful woman gradually taking form under his brushstrokes, her snow-white gown fluttering and evoking in Tang Ze memories like flowing water... A tremor passed through him as he murmured softly: "Ming'er..."

Suddenly, the electricity in the town of Sizhu was cut off, plunging the room into darkness as both the computer and lights went out. The bedroom, which had been well-lit just moments ago, now left only the dimly flickering red ember of the cigarette on Tang Ze's lips. But it soon faded too, much like the shock and disgust in Tang Ze's eyes that gradually subsided. Cursing under his breath, Tang Ze crushed out the cigarette, reluctantly interrupting his thoughts about the pitiful marriage between himself and Gong Ming.

This dilapidated little town was truly absurd, always experiencing power outages when people needed light the most. Since returning to live in this small town, Tang Ze had already grown extremely irritated by these meaningless blackouts. His Toshiba computer could never be used with peace of mind. Many times, the drawings he was halfway through creating were lost due to unexpected power failures, forcing him to start over. Although he majored in chemistry in university, he had a deep love for art design.

Tang Ze didn't have time to worry if the portrait of Gong Ming he had just drawn would be lost again. In the pitch-black darkness, he struggled to find the lighter. He needed even a tiny bit of light to dispel the unsettling darkness that made his heart race. With a sigh, he cursed again at the hateful blackout. And just as his fingers touched the lighter, a sudden chill ran down his spine: Why did the power go out tonight? He had already arranged with the substation to ensure normal power supply no matter what tonight. How could it still—oh no, the Buddha's Eye!

This rare treasure caused his mind to suddenly become disordered. Quickly striking the lighter, he began searching for the candle in the faint light. Suddenly, a flash of intense light outside the window pierced his eye painfully. "Who's there!" Tang Ze shouted in alarm, knocking over a half-burnt candle standing on the corner of the desk with his hand. Splashing sound followed as the red candle rolled onto the floor.

Tang Ze quickly extinguished the lighter; the oppressive darkness that had made his chest tight earlier now became the protection he desired. Struggling to calm his nerves, he slowly got up from his chair and found the cool iron rod behind the door, which calmed him significantly.
