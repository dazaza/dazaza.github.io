Six factors hinder the development of domestic smart home enterprises

by longersing on 2009-08-27 17:49:50

Intelligence has always been the direction of development of the electronics industry, and at the same time, it is also a sign of progress in other industries. In the future, people's lives should be fully intelligent, which is a reflection of human technological development. This manifestation of intelligent applications is not only reflected in computers, smartphones, and other 3C products but will also be embodied in home appliances and general living furniture, such as smart sofas, smart beds, and smart bathrooms. These are the future trends of various categories of furniture.

However, these products have become increasingly realistic abroad, while in China, they lag far behind. People are still popularizing smartphones, and as for smart furniture, not to mention popularization, our furniture industry has stagnated in terms of intelligence. What exactly is stopping the furniture industry from advancing in intelligence?

Firstly, smart home enterprises are still in the stage of isolated product production.

Currently, domestic smart home enterprises are still in the isolated product production phase and have not developed to the point where each focuses on a specific area. For example, some companies can specialize in developing smart home software, some can focus on product research and production, and others can specialize in providing system solutions, etc. If each enterprise only focuses on its own strengths, then the development of the entire industry will accelerate.

Secondly, hype is rampant, especially among some home appliance manufacturers.

Regarding the hype phenomenon in the smart home industry, we have discussed it many times before, and it is particularly severe among home appliance manufacturers. Due to their substantial capital, these manufacturers often make grandiose promotional efforts with significant investment. Typically, home appliance manufacturers emphasize the smart control of networked appliances in their promotions. However, for true smart home control systems, controlling appliances is just a small part. Such promotion can mislead consumers. Whether it is smart home manufacturers or home appliance manufacturers using the smart home banner, we hope they can be "truthful" and avoid excessive hype.

Thirdly, genuine R&D companies lack strength and cannot expand the market.

In the industry, companies genuinely focused on product research and development are usually weaker, with limited funds and no ability to conduct large-scale market expansion. These companies may have achieved good results in product development, but without effective market expansion, it is impossible for them to grow.

Fourthly, waiting passively for market opportunities.

After years of market development, smart home enterprises have not achieved ideal market returns, and cultivating this market has been very arduous. Now, most companies no longer have the ability to invest heavily in the market and can only maintain basic promotional activities, or even just "minor efforts." They no longer have the ability to actively create opportunities and can only passively wait for market opportunities.

Fifthly, manufacturers lack an innovative spirit.

Domestic smart home manufacturers generally lack an innovative spirit. Whether in product research and process design or in smart control solutions, companies lack innovation, which is a fatal key in business development. Hopefully, in future development, companies can pay more attention to the spirit of innovation so that businesses can develop sustainably.

Sixthly, foreign manufacturers lack understanding of the domestic market application and are unwilling to enter.

Smart homes have developed in China for nearly ten years, but foreign smart home manufacturers have been "waiting outside" and have not entered the market. The reason is that foreign manufacturers lack understanding of the application of smart home markets in China and are unwilling to enter the domestic market. However, without competition from foreign manufacturers, the development of the domestic smart home market still needs further refinement. We should promote the development of the domestic smart home industry more, allowing foreign companies to understand it. Once they enter, the domestic smart home market will immediately become active, and competition brings progress.
