1. Home air conditioners can be cleaned and disinfected once a year by professionals, especially the evaporator of the indoor unit. Qualified disinfectants and correct mixing methods must be used, and operated by professionals. After disinfection, rinse off any remaining disinfectant to prevent it from volatilizing and being harmful to health. During the use of the air conditioner, the filter should be washed frequently (it can be rinsed directly with water), preferably once a week.

2. Before turning on the air conditioner, open the window for ventilation for 10 minutes to allow as much fresh outdoor air as possible to enter the room. After the air conditioner has been running for a while, turn it off and open the windows again for ventilation for 20-30 minutes, repeating this process to create airflow between the indoor and outdoor air, allowing harmful gases to be expelled outdoors.

3. The indoor temperature is best controlled around 25°C, with the temperature difference between indoors and outdoors not exceeding 7°C; the cold air outlet should not be directed straight at people or desks.

4. Being in an air-conditioned room with a low temperature for a long time can easily cause joint pain. Entering and exiting an air-conditioned room, due to the large temperature difference between indoors and outdoors, sudden cold and heat can easily lead to symptoms such as coughing, headache, and runny nose associated with a cold. In addition, since the air conditioner's filter can adsorb negative ions in the air, increasing positive ions in the room, which can have adverse effects on the human body.

5. If the air conditioner is not regularly cleaned, it will accumulate dust, fibers, and breed large amounts of bacteria, viruses, mold, mites, etc. When the air conditioner is turned on, these dust particles and pathogenic microorganisms are blown out by the air conditioner, causing indoor air pollution and spreading diseases. Among them, the most harmful is the Legionella hidden in the centralized air conditioning ventilation system, which can cause Legionnaires' disease. Moreover, mites are strong allergens that can cause allergic asthma, dermatitis, rhinitis, etc.
