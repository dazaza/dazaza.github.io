Shanghai women "too thin"

By Liu Qiaobin, Health Times

48% of white-collar women in Beijing cook at home every day, whereas in Shanghai it is less than 5%. In our impression, women in Guangdong are supposed to be more petite. However, due to a larger number of foreign enterprises, the pursuit of slimness by European and American cultures has now influenced women in Shanghai more. These reasons have led to women in Shanghai being crowned as the "slimmest" among the three cities - Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou - in a recent survey.

"We oppose obesity, but we also do not advocate excessive thinness. What we need is a normal and healthy weight," said Professor Cai Meiqin from the Nutrition Department of Shanghai Jiao Tong University School of Medicine, who was responsible for this survey: "Nowadays, many young white-collar women have an extremely unreasonable diet, eating too little staple food. Most people only eat small amounts of vegetables and fruits per meal, and do not eat rice, steamed buns, or fatty foods at all. This is the main reason why their bodies are so thin," said Cai Meiqin.

The survey also showed that nearly 10% of white-collar women in Shanghai never cook. Most people find the cooking process too complicated and the smoke and oil unbearable, so the vast majority of white-collar women dine out at least seven times a week.

Cai Meiqin pointed out that giving up home-cooked meals is not scientific, and this is one of the main reasons why white-collar women's bodies are too thin. When eating at home, people generally cook according to their own taste preferences and also have enough time to ensure they consume a certain amount of staple food. Nowadays, restaurant food is generally very greasy and high in monosodium glutamate content. White-collar women usually eat less oily food and focus on vegetarian options, feeling full quickly, so they generally don't eat staple food.

Rice, steamed buns, and noodles are the main sources of carbohydrates for the human body and also the main way to intake various trace elements. If staple food is not consumed for a long time, the body will be in a state of insufficient carbohydrate intake, which can easily lead to sub-health conditions such as thinness, anemia, and hypoglycemia. Severe cases may even cause gynecological diseases due to low immunity.

Cai Meiqin said that mastering one's own healthy weight index is not difficult. According to the international standard Body Mass Index (BMI = weight in kg / height in m²), a female's BMI between 18.5 and 24 is considered normal. Anything less or more than this range is considered unhealthy.

Professor Cai Meiqin also provided a daily nutritional menu for white-collar women: rice (300~500 grams), vegetables (400~500 grams), fruit (100~200 grams), meat (50~100 grams), soy products (about 50 grams), milk (200~250 grams).
