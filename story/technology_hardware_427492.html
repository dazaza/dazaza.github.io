Company posts a notice of holiday to facilitate employees to watch the World Cup

by dehua900 on 2011-05-08 17:08:31

The Online Leave Application Guide is Out

As the semifinals approach, the World Cup matches are becoming more intense and captivating. With the flames of competition growing fiercer, how can football fans possibly miss out? But how can one watch the games without risking their "rice bowl" (job)? Thus begins the "World Cup" between employees and bosses, with leave application becoming a challenge for employees.

Yesterday afternoon, a reporter visited Ningbo Qilu Cultural Living Supplies Co., Ltd., where a notice was posted on the company gate - "To facilitate everyone's enjoyment of the World Cup, approved by the chairman, it has been decided to grant half-day leaves on the mornings of the semifinals and finals, specifically July 7th, 2010, July 8th, and July 12th. Please enjoy the passion and joy of the World Cup!"

Ma told the reporter that during the last Germany World Cup, the company even purchased a table football machine, which eventually broke due to overuse.

Let Rest and Watching Matches Go Hand in Hand

According to statistics, every time "World Cup month" arrives, among the over 9 million employees in the UK, each person on average "skips work" for 3.5 days, and there are as many as 31.85 million different reasons and excuses. Ten percent of hardcore fans have said that if they get a World Cup ticket and their boss doesn't approve their leave, they would consider resigning. However, most people choose to use various excuses to ask for leave from their bosses. A certain sponsor of the World Cup conducted a survey of 4000 British people, and found that stomachaches, gastric pain, vomiting, headaches, and migraines topped the list of excuses.

Watching Matches and Asking for Leave Becomes a Challenge

In today's rapidly developing internet era, netizens adhere to the principle of "when one side is in trouble, support comes from all sides." Whenever there's an issue, people worldwide come up with solutions, leading to the emergence of numerous "World Cup Leave Application Guides" and "Leave Application Strategies" on various websites. Examples include: "My wife suddenly gave birth to a chubby baby boy yesterday, please approve my leave;" "A police car parked under our dormitory building this morning and directly took him away, he hasn't returned yet;" "My wife is sick, I can't let her go to the hospital alone;" "I've contracted chickenpox and scarlet fever;" "The toilet at home is broken, the radiator is broken, there's a gas leak;" "I'm allergic to renovation materials, these days we're moving..."
