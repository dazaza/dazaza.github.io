1980s: Students reported to school with quilts on their backs; Post-95s: Students come with the Apple trilogy.

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 13:28:13

In recent days, universities have gradually welcomed freshmen born after 1995. Reviewing their enrollment equipment, items such as laptops, smartphones, DSLR cameras, and even the Apple trilogy are all within reach. Among parents sending their children to school, there are also college students from different generations who, when discussing their own standard "equipment" at the time of enrollment, many parents lament that they hadn't realized until now how significantly the times have changed, as reflected by the changing gear of new university students!

Before the 1980s, going to university with bedding on your back

Basic equipment: Snow cream, plastic shell thermos, bedding, eggs, fountain pen

Luxury equipment: Watch, radio, bicycle

With the start of the new term for this year's freshmen, discussions around the topic of enrollment have been ongoing. Recently, a post on Weibo showcasing "college student enrollment equipment through the decades" has sparked fond memories among university students of various eras about their past university lives.

Before the 1980s, getting into university was akin to "crossing a narrow bridge," so when a family had a child accepted into university, it was considered a great honor. Although materials were relatively scarce at the time, families would always try their best to prepare some "decent" items for their children starting university.

Bedding, plastic shell thermoses, fountain pens, enamel basins, enamel cups, etc., were all part of the standard equipment for new students. Li Yuehong, who enrolled in university in 1986, was the eldest child in her family and set a good example for her two younger brothers. When she reported for her first day, the bedding she brought was a new quilt from her aunt's wedding. The thermos, enamel basin, enamel cup, and other toiletries were all bundled in a net bag, and she arrived at school with several large and small packages. Li Yuehong told reporters that the most luxurious item she owned was a men's watch her father had worn for a long time.

"Actually, during that time, everyone's family conditions were similar, and the basic equipment was pretty much just these few things. However, since I managed to get into university, my mother bought some fabric before school started and made me a shirt."

In the 1990s, getting a Walkman to learn English before entering university

Basic equipment: Small fan, electric kettle, cassette recorder (Walkman)

Luxury equipment: Guitar, mobile phone, pager

Hao Lin entered university in 1999, and the only electrical appliance in her freshman enrollment kit was a cassette recorder (Walkman). "My financial situation wasn't particularly good. At the time of reporting, several girls in our dormitory had CD players, and one girl even had a mobile phone. However, she didn't use it often because there weren't many people with mobile phones back then, and she didn't know who to call or text." Hao Lin laughed as she recounted this.

"Boys carrying guitars looked especially handsome in our eyes back then. Students with an artistic flair seemed mysterious and a bit rebellious in the eyes of those of us who just studied hard." Hao Lin said she once had a crush on a boy in white shirt who played and sang his own songs during the welcome party in her freshman year.

"In fact, after entering the 1990s, people's lives gradually improved. The gadgets used by university students also updated quickly." Hao Lin said that pagers were still popular in her freshman year, but by sophomore and junior years, mobile phones began to gain popularity; by sophomore and junior years, desktop computers were still being used, but by senior year, classmates with better financial conditions had already switched to laptops.

From the 2000s to now, mobile phones have become common items

Standard equipment: Desktop computer, MP3 player, CD player, smartphone, laptop

Luxury equipment: DSLR camera, Apple trilogy

A classmate named Lin, who entered university in 2008, remembers that at the time, MP3 players and mobile phones were the most common enrollment equipment, while laptops were still considered luxury items. By 2013, laptops had become standard equipment.

Li Yuehong's daughter was admitted to Tongji University this year, and her excellent grades made Li Yuehong very proud. "We only have one daughter, and our family conditions are decent now. Originally, her father proposed buying her an iPhone and an Apple computer, but our daughter is very sensible and said she only needed a regular notebook computer and mobile phone." Li Yuehong said that the enrollment equipment she provided for her daughter was a laptop and a smartphone.

Li Yuehong's daughter Xiaoxia mentioned that she had heard from seniors that having a good smartphone could solve many issues. "Modern smartphones are fully functional; they can be used as MP3 players for listening to music, making calls, watching videos, surfing the internet, taking photos, acting as a voice recorder, and even capturing the teacher's PPT or recording key points during lectures." Xiaoxia said that having a laptop is convenient for portability and looking up information online. She isn't actually very interested in electronic devices, as long as they work fine.

However, Li Yuehong noticed upon the start of classes that many new classmates owned the Apple trilogy. "University students born after 1995 are actually quite fashionable," Xiaoxia summarized her peers in this way.
