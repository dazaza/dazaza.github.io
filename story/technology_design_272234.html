Huangmei Picking Flowers • Blooming in Inheritance and Innovation

by f20091023 on 2009-11-23 10:30:21

As China's economy has taken off, the amount of money in ordinary people's pockets has increased, and all kinds of novel and fashionable products can be found in every corner of the country. You can buy an iPod in Ali, Tibet, or choose Huangmei embroidered flowers in Mohe, Heilongjiang. The affluence of the masses has brought about a higher pursuit of both material wealth and spiritual fulfillment. This is especially evident in the realm of art, where Huangmei embroidered flowers — a nearly lost ethnic embroidery cultural heritage — has been revitalized thanks to the rescue efforts of Mr. Chen Mince. The inheritance and innovation of traditional art is essentially about interpreting and re-disseminating it using the popular language of the new era.

"Only Chu produces talents, and the best are from Eastern Hubei." Since ancient times, Huangmei has been renowned for its abundance of talent, which has laid the foundation for the accumulation and development of regional arts and culture.

The ancient embroidery, after undergoing a phoenix-like rebirth through rescue efforts, now radiates with new vitality. With over 1000 years of history, Huangmei embroidered flowers have been rejuvenated today.

Mr. Chen Mince, a knight wandering in the circle of ethnic arts, has a particular fondness for Huangmei embroidered flowers and calligraphy. His dedication and attachment to art have led to the birth and development of an ethnic embroidery brand.

In order to protect more treasures of Huangmei embroidered flowers, in 1999, Mr. Chen Mince established the "Huangmei Embroidered Flowers Arts & Crafts Co., Ltd.", organizing and excavating techniques and patterns scattered among the folk. At the same time, the CMC brand was created, transforming these unearthed traditional embroidery art resources into commercial products and successfully promoting them in the market.

In 2006, "Huangmei embroidered flowers" was included in the first batch of national intangible cultural heritage lists, further proving the cultural strength and status of Huangmei embroidered flowers.

During his visit to Thailand, Chairman Chen Mince was warmly received by Prime Minister Abhisit at the July 28th Thai-Chinese Business Forum and the World Outstanding Chinese Entrepreneurs Association Wealth Thailand Thank-you Banquet. He presented two scrolls, "Huangmei Embroidered Flowers • Qingming Riverside Scene" and "Qinyuan Spring • The Great Wall," as "national gifts" to Prime Minister Abhisit. Huangmei embroidered flowers instantly became the focus of reports by mainstream media from both countries. Currently, being selected as "national gifts" within the cross-stitch industry is a first for Huangmei embroidered flowers.

During the Thailand trip, mainstream media from both China and Thailand vied to report on the amazement and charm brought by Huangmei embroidered flowers.

CCTV International Channel CCTV4 also made a special report on this matter.
