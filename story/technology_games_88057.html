Jiong! White-collar workers embezzle 200,000 of public funds to play "The Journey" - Age1983

by hexiaoyao on 2008-08-29 16:21:06

Jiong! Shi Yuzhu is about to be criticized again…… The online game "The Journey" is extremely tempting.

Xiao Yongle was born in a farmer's family in Anhui Province. After graduating from high school, he was admitted to a junior college in his hometown, majoring in accounting. After graduating from the junior college, with the help of relevant departments of the local government, he started working at a food factory in this city. In May 2007, due to his outstanding work performance, Xiao Yongle was sent to Lanzhou as the principal accountant. "After starting work in Lanzhou, I suddenly had a lot more free time. Because the computer at my workplace was too slow, I spent my own money to buy one, specifically for playing online games. At first, I played "The Legend", but later I found it wasn't exciting enough, so I started playing "The Journey"." In the detention center of the Beichen Branch of the Public Security Bureau, Xiao Yongle immediately became animated when talking about online games.

Xiao Yongle said that to play "The Journey", you just need to fill out a form to register an account. "According to the story background, players will be amazed to find themselves transformed into royalty, abandoned in the wilderness due to war, and growing up to fight for dignity and honor... Although everything is virtual, I once firmly believed that I had found a golden path leading to glory and dreams."

The evil hand reached out to public funds

"Because of the nature of my accounting work, except for the end of the month which is busier, I spent most of my time playing online games. The longest time I spent was 14 hours without eating or drinking," Xiao Yongle said. "The plot design of 'The Journey' is tragic and majestic, the only thing that troubles people is the need for a lot of equipment to make oneself more powerful."

To acquire equipment, one needs to purchase point cards. For Xiao Yongle, who earns only over 2000 yuan per month, wanting to become a king in the virtual world would be far beyond his income, so he reached out to public funds. Xiao Yongle said helplessly: "A few months later, I was shocked to discover that I had already embezzled over ten thousand yuan from the company. During that period, I was constantly worried. Later, I quit online games for about half a year, depositing my monthly salary into the company's account without withdrawing it, gradually filling the hole."

After things settled down, Xiao Yongle finally felt relieved. But with no friends and no other hobbies, he quickly got bored with all the spare time. The thrilling battle scenes in the "The Journey" game were like invisible hands scratching his heart. At the end of last year, Xiao Yongle returned to "The Journey" and once again reached out to public funds.

Unable to repay, he ended up in jail

In early June this year, Xiao Yongle discovered that he had embezzled over 200,000 yuan of the company's accounts receivable, this astronomical figure made him tremble with fear.

On June 19th, realizing the gravity of his mistake, he decided to flee with the company's 65,000 yuan accounts receivable vouchers and 20,000 yuan in cash.

A few days later, the leaders of Xiao Yongle's company discovered a huge deficit in the Lanzhou office, and reported the case to the Economic Investigation Detachment of the Beichen Branch of the Public Security Bureau. The investigating officers found that the financial supervisor, Xiao Yongle, was a major suspect.

In July, the Beichen police lawfully arrested Xiao Yongle. Recently, the criminal suspect Xiao Yongle was legally approved for arrest by the procuratorial organs on suspicion of embezzlement.
