Plot Summary:

This series is created by the Emmy-winning duo Joe Russo and Anthony Russo (Arrested Development). While most sitcoms focus on "love", "family", "life", or "work", this show takes a different approach by focusing on higher education. CBS's "The Big Bang Theory" is one of the successful examples of this genre in recent years. How does America address the issue of making education accessible? How do adults who are already in the workforce but want to further their education pursue higher learning? This sitcom provides some answers to these questions.

The students at Greendale Community College are somewhat "unique". It's not that they have physical disabilities, but rather the diversity of their backgrounds - there are high school "underachievers", recently divorced housewives, and elderly individuals committed to lifelong learning. Without the pretense of an ivory tower, community colleges help these individuals fulfill their dreams of higher education.

The protagonist is a fast-talking lawyer (played by Joel McHale) who originally had a college degree, but due to unforeseen circumstances, it became invalid. Unable to return to his previous university, he chooses to enroll in a community college. His active mind quickly helps him make a group of friends, and he organizes a study group among them. In fact, their understanding of each other may be deeper and more valuable than their understanding of textbooks and assignments.

Life is short, don't waste your prime years - that's the message this show conveys.
