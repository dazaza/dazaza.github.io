ZTE, Huawei Capture 10% of Global Smartphone Shipments in Second Quarter

by anonymous on 2013-07-30 11:48:41

On July 29, Beijing time, according to the latest report from Strategy Analytics, global smartphone shipments grew by 47% in the second quarter, reaching a record high of 230 million units. Samsung's sales accounted for one-third of the global market, while Apple's market share fell to its lowest point in three years. Meanwhile, Huawei and ZTE's sales cannot be overlooked, as their combined sales accounted for nearly 10% of global smartphone sales.

In the second quarter of 2013, ZTE Corporation sold 11.5 million smartphones globally, achieving a record-high market share of 5%. ZTE became the world's fourth-largest smartphone manufacturer for the first time, with growth mainly driven by the massive Chinese market.

In the same period, Huawei sold 11.1 million smartphones globally, also capturing a 5% market share. The report suggests that, based on the data, the global smartphone market is currently dominated by Asian brands.

Senior analyst at Strategy Analytics, Neil Shah, stated: "Global smartphone shipments increased by 47% year-on-year in the second quarter of 2013, rising from 156.5 million units in the second quarter of 2012 to 229.6 million units. This marks the largest single-quarter shipment volume for smartphones. Smartphones now account for 59% of total global mobile phone shipments."

Neil Mawston, executive director of Strategy Analytics, said: "Samsung achieved a year-on-year growth rate of 56% in the second quarter, with global shipments hitting a record high of 76 million units and a market share of 33%. Samsung shipped twice as many smartphones as Apple, with stable demand for its flagship Galaxy S4 both in China and globally. Apple shipped 31.2 million iPhones globally, marking a 20% year-on-year increase, which is less than half of the overall smartphone industry growth rate of 47%. Apple's share in the global smartphone market stood at 14%, the lowest level since the second quarter of 2010."

Analyst Linda Sui from Strategy Analytics commented: "LG performed exceptionally well, doubling its global shipments year-on-year to 12.1 million units. LG's market share reached 5%, maintaining its position as the world's third-largest smartphone manufacturer for the second consecutive quarter. Its popular Optimus and Nexus models were the main drivers of LG's success. If LG can expand its retail presence and marketing in major countries like the U.S. or China, it has the potential to challenge Apple's second place." (Xiao E)
