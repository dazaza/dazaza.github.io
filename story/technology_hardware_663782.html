Preventing Scratches When Parking by the Side of the Road in Residential Areas: 10 Protective Tips

February 10, 2012, 9:21 AM, China Auto Consumption Network, I want to comment (0)

Font size: T|T

1. Visiting relatives and friends, parking in mid-to-high-end residential areas is not too bad, but be careful in low-end areas. There are more people with unbalanced mindsets who resent the rich and envy the poor, making it easier to fall victim to malicious acts. In mid-to-high-end areas, cars don't stand out as much, and the property management is stricter, so there's less risk of scratches. In low-end areas, it's best to park within the sightlines of security guards at the gate. Give them a cigarette, say some nice words, ask for their extra attention, which can reduce the risk. Another method is to park next to a better car, transferring the risk to others.

2. For outdoor parking, no matter where, be cautious about parking near high-rise balconies and windows. Some people have bad habits, such as throwing objects from heights, spitting, or tossing cigarette butts. Not to mention various fruit pits and used batteries. The car glass of an Accord parked downstairs was shattered by a rotten peach thrown from the 11th floor. A friend’s black Qida had its hood dented by a used battery thrown from the 15th floor. Worse yet, during typhoon days, flower pots on balconies that aren’t secured well may blow down, with unimaginable consequences.

3. Do not randomly occupy someone else's fixed parking space or block their passage. This is especially prone to retaliation—scratching paint, puncturing tires, deflating, or smashing windows could all happen.

4. Do not park vehicles haphazardly on the side of the road. Random roadside parking will get you a ticket from the traffic police—not only does it cost 200 yuan each time, but the adhesive marks left by the ticket take half a day to clean off and might even damage your glass (it has very strong adhesion). Your car might also be forcibly towed away. Automatic transmission vehicles have rules when being towed—for example, they must be set to neutral gear, travel at a speed under 50 km/h, and can only be towed up to 50 kilometers (to prevent planetary gears from breaking). Alternatively, all four wheels should be lifted during towing. If you obstruct traffic and your car is forcibly towed, it might cause damage to your automatic transmission if not handled carefully.

5. Be careful of your car getting damaged by someone else opening their door! Choosing an empty parking spot is crucial. Many underground spots are between two pillars with several positions available. The worst position is the middle one because both sides of your car will have other cars, increasing the probability of someone carelessly opening their door and damaging yours. The leftmost and rightmost spots are better, with the leftmost being the best. Because there won’t be any car on the left side of your car, and the right side of your car is the driver’s side. Drivers generally understand the importance of being careful when opening doors to avoid mutual damage. However, passengers who don’t drive tend to lack this awareness.

6. When parking side-by-side, the horizontal distance matters. One way is to stay as far apart as possible, ensuring that even when the doors are fully opened, they do not touch. This distance should be at least 1.2 meters. If you cannot stay far apart, then stay as close as possible, keeping it under 60 centimeters. When parked closely, everyone’s movements for opening doors and getting in and out of the car are smaller, reducing the risk of collision. The most dangerous distance is around 1 meter. At 1 meter, the doors can reach each other, and when they do, it is almost at the maximum opening angle, resulting in the highest linear speed and impact force, which will almost certainly dent or scratch the paint.

7. In summer, be cautious about parking near the water outlet of air conditioning units. Air conditioner water stains on the car paint leave marks that are hard to wash off, and you might need to polish or use sanding wax to remove them.

8. Be mindful when parking under trees. Some trees drop fruits during certain seasons. These fruits break upon hitting the ground or your car, leaving juice that resembles phlegm. Trees also easily attract bird droppings, which are highly corrosive and can leave scars on your car paint if not promptly cleaned.

9. During New Year's Eve, Lantern Festival, and welcoming the God of Wealth when fireworks are commonly set off nationwide, try to park in underground garages if possible, or at least find a relatively safe spot. Fireworks can definitely damage paint and shatter glass. Moreover, the yellow sulfur stains are quite difficult to clean.

10. Avoid storing cash or valuable items on the dashboard to prevent someone from smashing your window just for a few dollars.
