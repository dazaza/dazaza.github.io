Wow! Apple's cash assets exceed those of the US government.

by lewis062223 on 2012-02-07 16:33:11

According to national observers, the creator of the iPhone has quickly become a financial giant in the US. Apple's current cash assets have reached $98 billion and are expected to reach $150 billion by the end of the year, surpassing the US government. Wall Street predicts that Apple will embark on the largest gamble in its corporate history by entering the Internet TV (flat-panel TV) industry. Its operating model is similar to that of the iPod and iPhone; the iTV will ignite new battles in the movie and television industries just as it did in the music and mobile phone industries. It will also launch a series of acquisition actions. The American financial giant, Apple, seems more interested in advancing into the pay-TV business with its substantial investment in the flat-panel TV industry. Apple CEO Tim Cook can be considered one of the highest-paid bosses in the US. A recent compensation package included cash and stocks totaling 240 million pounds, but he is not extravagant in his consumption. Even after selling $100 million worth of stocks, he still rents a modest house, finally purchasing a four-bedroom house worth $1.9 million in 2010.

This prediction by observers stems from Disney CEO Bob Iger joining Apple's board of directors. Following successful collaborations with Apple to release movies like "Toy Story" and "Finding Nemo," Jobs became the largest shareholder in Iger's company in 2006 when Disney paid $7.4 billion for Pixar, Jobs' computer animation design studio. Disney's dividend last December was 50%, at $0.6 per share, allowing Jobs' fund to earn $82.8 million from its 138 million Disney shares.

Apple has nearly two-thirds of its assets overseas, which limits the share of dividends available to domestic investors since, according to US government regulations, merchants can defer paying taxes on overseas profits.
