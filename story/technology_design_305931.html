AC Milan's Nemesis: A Milan Discard Scores 3 Goals in 3 Years, and Selling Him Feels Like Blood from the Heart for the Rossoneri

Sina Sports - AC Milan lost 0-2 to Palermo at home, and without a doubt, the best player of the match was Palermo forward Michele Micoletti. With an exquisite curved world-class shot into the far corner and a beautiful sprinting run that tore open the opponent’s defense to create a decisive opportunity for his teammate, Micolletti was the determining factor in the outcome of this game. Strangely enough, this small forward who originally came from AC Milan's football school and left AC Milan due to being deemed "not making it," always puts on extraordinary performances when facing his old team.

When Micolletti was initially loaned to Perugia by Juventus, he was already AC Milan's "nemesis." In the 02/03 season, he scored with a stunning long-range strike, leaving the Rossoneri heartbroken. After being loaned to Fiorentina and returning to Juventus, he was relatively quieter during matches against AC Milan. However, since joining Palermo in 2007, Micolletti has never let AC Milan feel comfortable. In the fifth round of the 07/08 season, Micolletti's stoppage-time free kick helped his team beat AC Milan 2-1. During the second half of the season, Micolletti was absent due to injury, allowing AC Milan to win 2-1. In last season's clash, AC Milan lost 1-3 away, with Micolletti scoring the opening goal. This match's goal marked Micolletti's consecutive scoring against AC Milan across three seasons. From a career perspective, this short, technically gifted forward hasn't been particularly successful, with his national team career limited to two "grand experiments" under Trapattoni where he performed well. At Juventus, he indeed didn't become a starter, but with Micolletti's abilities, even in a top club, he would definitely be a useful backup.

It was understandable that Micolletti showed nothing remarkable when he first left AC Milan, but examples like Mattio are harder to forgive. This forward, sold by AC Milan to Cagliari last summer, has scored eight goals so far this season—more than any single player at AC Milan. If they had been more patient and kept Mattio, could the €15 million spent on acquiring Huntelaar have instead been used to strengthen the midfield or full-back positions?

Fortunately, AC Milan's approach is now showing signs of adjustment. Antonini and Abate, both products of Milan's youth academy, reappeared in the starting lineup together. Despite AC Milan's poor performance in this match, the two players performed fairly decently. Although both were first sold before being repurchased, AC Milan has consistently monitored their progress even during their loan periods, which is fundamentally different from selling Odo and then buying him back at a high price later.

In a somewhat ironic twist, former AC Milan star Thomas Hitzlsperger mentioned names like Paroschi, Mattio, and Aquafresca when discussing the young Italian forwards he sees today. Two of these come from Milan's youth academy. Mattio has already been sold, and Paroschi's co-ownership currently lies between AC Milan and Parma. In a recent poll conducted by "TuttoMercatoWeb," the majority of Milan fans urged bringing Paroschi back. Indeed, no one wants to see another Micolletti...
