USB flash drive specifically for Vista

by xt_cliff on 2007-09-08 11:15:33

I thought USB drives had reached a point where there was nothing new to offer, but unexpectedly, there's still something like this.

Friends who are familiar with Vista should know that this system (we won't talk about the fact that it's garbage) has an interesting technology called ReadyBoost. It's quite simple in theory; it allows you to use a USB drive as RAM (a pretty cool technology, so why haven't we heard more about it?).

The USB drive I'm introducing today is specifically designed for this purpose. It's actually very simple - after plugging it in, it can rotate 180 degrees and stick closely to your laptop, making it less of a bother (extremely cold joke...).
