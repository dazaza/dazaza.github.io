What are the harmful effects of having osteoarthritis?

by wang790 on 2012-03-04 14:23:50

Osteoarthritis is a common orthopedic disease, which falls within the scope of rheumatic diseases. It often affects many joints and poses certain harm to people's physical and mental health. So, what are the hazards of having osteoarthritis?

High incidence: Theoretically speaking, osteoarthritis is one of the more common joint conditions in daily life. Moreover, the incidence of osteoarthritis increases with age, being relatively higher among middle-aged women. With the aging population, this disease will continue to increase, which requires everyone's attention.

Joint pain, stiffness, deformity, and functional impairment: Osteoarthritis has a significant impact on both the physical and mental health of patients. The most severely affected joints by osteoarthritis are those that are used frequently and bear heavy loads, such as fingers, hips, cervical vertebrae, lumbar vertebrae, and ankles. For example, hand osteoarthritis can cause hand pain or redness, swelling, pain, and tenderness in the soft tissues near the hand; this makes it difficult for people to lift heavy objects or bear too much weight. Additionally, knee osteoarthritis may lead to symptoms like joint soreness and stiffness, causing obvious pain when walking or squatting. Intense exercise, exposure to cold, or damp weather can exacerbate the condition. Some patients also experience soreness, swelling, stiffness, and fatigue in the lower back and its soft tissues, with restricted bending motions.
