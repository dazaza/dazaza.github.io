When adding water, it should be poured slowly along the wall of the tank, making the inner wall around the hatching nest slightly moist is enough. Do not pour water directly into the nest or make the humidity inside the nest too high. After hatching, although the young centipedes temporarily gather in groups, they can also move and forage independently. At this time, the female will gradually leave the hatching nest and act independently. Because centipedes have the habit of fighting for food and eating smaller ones, the female should be moved out in time or the young should be separated for breeding.

The breeding technology of centipedes by Nongke Humin is first-class, and the indoor hatching tanks should be properly placed in advance. Once the eggs are laid, do not move them easily. Observers should be careful and gentle, do not move the covering glass (tiles) casually, and do not shine flashlights on them, at the Shifang centipede breeding base. During the hatching period, no feeding is required, but as it is the height of summer, if the humidity in the hatching nest is too low, it will affect the normal development of the embryo and individual growth patterns. Under artificial feeding conditions, the body length of centipedes can reach the medicinal small standard (8 cm) within two years, and after two years, they will approach the large standard. Therefore, it can be inferred that from capturing adult centipedes, laying eggs, hatching until providing medicinal use, it takes 2-3 years. The processing technology of medicinal centipedes uses dried whole bodies. After catching, they are scalded to death with boiling water, then a pointed fine bamboo stick is inserted into the head and tail parts, using the elasticity of the bamboo strip to straighten them, and dried under the sun. After processing and preparation, the centipedes present a flat long shape, about 14-16 cm long, about 0.6-1.0 cm wide, with a total of 22 segments, the last segment being small and called the tail; the head is reddish-brown, with one pair of tactile organs and venomous hooks; the back is dark brown and shiny, with two prominent ridges; the abdomen is yellowish-brown and shriveled; each segment has one pair of legs, yellowish-red, bending backward, the last segment like a thorn. It has a fishy smell and a special pungent odor, with a spicy and slightly salty taste. Inside the centipedes are two toxic components similar to bee venom, namely histamine-like substances and hemolytic proteins; in addition, they contain tyrosine, leucine, formic acid, fatty oils, cholesterol, etc. Medicinal centipedes have the functions of calming wind, relieving spasms, reducing swelling, and detoxifying. They are mainly used to treat children's convulsions, tetanus, seizures, facial paralysis, lymph node tuberculosis, abscesses, ulcers, etc., and recently it has been found that centipedes have anti-tumor effects.
