[Reprint] Methods for Resolving Conflicts Among Colleagues (Part II)

by koucai4472 on 2012-03-05 15:05:00

Although, the eloquence training network cannot say that a person with good moral character will definitely have good interpersonal relationships, there is one thing that can be certain: a person with low moral quality and inferior character will never have good popularity. As the saying goes: "birds of a feather flock together". A normal person, who would want to associate with someone of low character? Therefore, whether one's character is good or bad is the decisive factor in determining whether one has good popularity. Of course, one must also master some social skills. Besides being polite to others, your eloquence is also an indispensable part. You need to be able to speak, and you need to know how to speak, otherwise, how would you converse with others? Therefore, you must understand some "art of public speaking" to deal with these situations.

Therefore, whether one can establish good relations with colleagues is the touchstone for testing an employee's character. So, how can one maintain good relations with colleagues? The eloquence training network will share with everyone the general methods of handling colleague relations, so that everyone can also learn some basic methods of interpersonal relationship management here. In fact, many methods have already been mentioned in the article "Methods for Resolving Conflicts Between Colleagues." Next, the author will mention a few more methods.

First, solve problems privately. When there are issues, if they do not involve others, do not resolve them in front of those who like to meddle. Do not discuss such matters when there are people around in the office and every word can be heard clearly. Chinese people value face; offending someone by not giving them face causes even more resentment. Moreover, if it is a private matter, it is best not to discuss it publicly as it involves personal privacy.

Second, avoid sarcasm. If you have any complaints, make sure to express them to the person directly involved. Do not damage their reputation. Remember, you need to maintain a working relationship rather than a personal one. There is no need to criticize a colleague's character. You can say, "He did not complete his work according to the rules last week," but you should not say, "He is just a blockhead."

Third, self-criticism. Conflicts in the workplace mostly occur due to work-related issues, and resolving them is not too difficult. However, this has one prerequisite: if it is proven that you are indeed responsible for the conflict, you should say "I'm sorry." As the saying goes, "a person needs a breath of air, and a Buddha needs a stick of incense." Sometimes, a simple "I'm sorry" can resolve a significant conflict.

Fourth, figure out the details; the problem may have nothing to do with you. Perhaps you think your colleague is targeting you, but in reality, that day their mood was just very bad. Take some time to think carefully before responding. Sometimes, saying nothing might be the best response.

Fifth, let bygones be bygones. Everyone is here for work, and it is hard to be perfect in words and deeds. It is not scary when things happen; clearing up and solving them is enough. Forget about them as soon as possible so that you can move forward. This is an important step in avoiding more conflicts.

Of course, some might say this is related to personality, but I believe personality can also be cultivated. Haven't the ancients often talked about cultivating oneself and nurturing one's nature? Learning to communicate will bring you more rewards in your career than just a salary.
