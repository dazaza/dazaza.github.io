Dancing and singing, performing joyfully, when it was almost time to say "goodbye", all the noble guests from across Asia gathered together, bidding each other "take care". On the evening of November 27th, the Guangzhou Asian Games were about to come to an end. Chen Weiya, the chief director of the opening and closing ceremonies said that if one word was used to describe the characteristics of the closing ceremony, that word would be "joyful". "The audience on site will feel as if they are in the Guangzhou Opera House; we will try our best to create a joyful atmosphere for all of Asia."

From grandeur to warmth and romance, famous CCTV host Zhang Bin described the grandeur of the Guangzhou Asian Games: At the Beijing Olympics, IOC President Jacques Rogge gave the evaluation "unparalleled", and the Guangzhou Asian Games may very well receive the evaluation "unprecedented and unlikely to be repeated". Indeed, this is an unprecedented edition of the Asian Games. Whether it's the number of competition events set by the organizing committee, the number of participating athletes, or the investment scale of the host city, all have greatly exceeded any previous edition of the Asian Games. Moreover, starting from the next edition, the Asian Games will be scaled down, and it will be difficult for any future edition to match the Guangzhou Asian Games.

Such a scale, unprecedented and unlikely to be repeated, naturally requires an opening ceremony that matches it. On the evening of November 12th, Guangzhou presented a grand opening ceremony to all of Asia, using the Pearl River as a stage and the city as a backdrop. The entire process was grand and magnificent, leaving people in awe. Now, it's time to say "goodbye". In the moment of reluctant farewell, the more appropriate atmosphere is one of warmth and romance.

Please leave your song behind

In response to the theme of the opening ceremony "set sail", the theme of the closing ceremony "please leave your song behind" conveys warmth through singing and dancing by people from all over Asia, expressing joy and reluctance to part. At that time, the audience will enjoy various performances with distinct Asian charm, from the Cantonese nursery rhyme "Moonlight" favored by the locals, to representative folk songs from Indonesia, Kazakhstan, Japan, Mongolia, Lebanon, and other countries. The entire "evening party" showcases Asian characteristics. The final song is named "Triumph", used to express the victorious joy of the athletes.

The 4 giant sails used as screens during the opening ceremony will take on the important task of creating a warm atmosphere. The blue sky is filled with twinkling little stars, cartoon calves and fawns wander on lush green grasslands, the scene is grand, warm, and tender like water. Even the final segment where the sacred flame is extinguished will be completed under the melody of "Warmth". After 16 days of thrilling competitions and fierce rivalries, now, from the athletes to ordinary spectators, everyone needs to completely relax and enjoy this beautiful moment.

Incheon brings an 8-minute cultural performance

The next Asian Games will be held in Incheon, South Korea. On the 27th day of the closing ceremony, besides receiving the flag of the Olympic Council of Asia, the Incheon delegation will also mark the start of the "Incheon Time" for the Asian Games. Subsequently, the Incheon delegation will bring an 8-minute cultural performance, with the biggest highlight undoubtedly being the superstar RAIN. The performance mainly consists of two parts: one is a "drumming" program brought by young actors from Incheon, expressing their welcome to all of Asia, and the second part is mainly carried out by RAIN, who will perform live, lasting approximately 4 minutes. This is after the Korean singer Kim Hyun-jung sang "Rising Sun" alongside four other famous Asian singers Wei Wei, Tan Jing, Joey Yung, and Tao Zhan at the opening ceremony of the Guangzhou Asian Games, another famous Korean pop star performs at the Guangzhou Asian Games.

It is understood that the 8 minutes of Incheon are closely related to water, continuing the tradition of the Guangzhou Asian Games opening and closing ceremonies. See you again in Incheon in 4 years!
