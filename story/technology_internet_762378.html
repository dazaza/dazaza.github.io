"The Inevitable 'Death of Yougou': Belle's E-commerce Has Many Hard Injuries"

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:09:14

Recently, Yougou.com, which has been suffering from a severe loss of senior executives, was hit again. On July 17th, it was confirmed by an insider from Yougou that COO Zhang Xiaojun will be leaving and is currently processing his resignation. As one of the founding members of Yougou, Zhang Xiaojun's departure holds significant implications, indicating that Yougou is indeed facing serious crises.

Let’s review the e-commerce journey of Belle International, the parent company of Yougou:

Belle cautiously experimented with e-commerce several years ago, steadily developing through three channels: Taobao distribution, Tmall, and B2C distribution (B2C distribution completed via Taoxiu.com). Led by veteran group member Xie Yunli, a not-so-lavish e-commerce team initially focused on selling products under Belle's own brands. Later, they introduced exclusive online models and new network brands. This development path was considered a more solid case of traditional brand enterprises successfully entering e-commerce before 2011, surpassing Li-Ning.

Following the over $100 million financing and rapid growth of Letao and Haolemai in 2010, Belle Group felt pressured. On one hand, they were concerned about being constrained by distribution channels; on the other hand, they assessed their significant advantages in the e-commerce field, including:

One, numerous brands: In addition to its own major women's shoe brands, the agency for internationally renowned sports brands like Adidas and Nike saw astonishing growth in online sales;

Two, easier category expansion: For Belle Group, which specializes in women's shoes and well-known sports brands, how to achieve absolute market advantage and status through multi-brand and multi-category strategies became the most thought-provoking issue. Expanding brands and categories through physical store openings was obviously too slow, while the ongoing e-commerce business could easily expand brands but had difficulty quickly expanding categories;

Three, financial advantage: During collaborations with e-commerce companies, Belle discovered that e-commerce is capital-intensive. However, Belle Group's financial strength allowed them to develop without relying on external capital, enabling them to outlast many vertical e-commerce companies dependent on capital, becoming the "survivor" in the end;

Four, talent advantage: After years of e-commerce practice, they have developed a highly professional and execution-strong e-commerce team, with talent cultivation capable of handling the scale development of e-commerce or even more;

Five, supply chain advantage: This made Belle internally excited, also attracting top executives from brand e-commerce or platform e-commerce. After all, for e-commerce, the supply chain is always fascinating, as it seems to determine life and death aside from capital. The supply chain includes product research and development, production, warehousing management, delivery after-sales services, and a series of "business" contents. In traditional enterprises emphasizing "commerce" over "e-commerce," this is considered the competitive advantage superior to e-commerce enterprises.

With these advantages, Belle's e-commerce decided to go all-in, first purchasing the double-character domain name yougou.com,

abandoning the slightly imitation "topshoes.cn" Taoxiu website for redirection, stopping supplies to all distribution websites, while retaining direct third-party online stores such as Taobao and Tmall. Although still operating, the investment and attention decreased. They recruited JD.com VP and Vancl VP at high salaries for CMO and COO positions respectively, dispatched veteran key members as CEO, forming a golden triangle. They aimed to create a full-category vertical B2C mall, implementing multi-brand strategies on one hand, and expanding into apparel and other categories on the other, realizing the dream of a department store empire impossible offline!

Everything sounded perfect, but every dream needs to face the hard reality, and the situation was:

One, higher technical requirements for independent B2C: Located in Shenzhen headquarters, Belle couldn't recruit more excellent technical talents, and technical outsourcing service companies were scarce in Shenzhen. But technology determines user experience, backend management efficiency, and reliable data. Independent B2C involves dynamic technology, different from relatively static technologies like ERP, continuous changes remain a hurdle for many traditional enterprises;

Two, increased management difficulty with category expansion: Someone once said doing well in women's shoes doesn’t necessarily mean success in men's shoes, something Belle probably deeply experienced when expanding into men's shoes. Expanding into women's clothing and accessories within a year shows what kind of difficulties the backend might encounter. For traditional brand enterprises accustomed to meticulous calculations, this rapid approach worked during Vancl's special period and could be understood within Vancl and JD.com's internal systems. However, although Belle employed Vancl's COO and JD.com's CMO, whether they could be understood and executed with "exaggerated costs" remains questionable;

Three, system: Many people don’t understand the conflict between e-commerce and traditional enterprises unless they've crossed both. Simply put, department settings (organizational structure), approvals, finance and budget, reporting methods, corporate culture, communication styles, execution methods, risk control, process regulations, etc., constitute a company's so-called "mechanism." The result of this conflict is that e-commerce attempts to convince the acceptance of its "system," while the traditional team tries to understand but can never fully grasp the e-commerce "system," leading to inefficiency and instability in the execution team. Eventually, the chairman or board may say, "E-commerce and traditional enterprises each have their advantages and disadvantages, the future must be a fusion, so you must also fuse in these aspects..." If this so-called "fusion without harmony" appears, it becomes so-called management innovation, and thus "CEO rotation system" appeared even in a company like Belle!

It was originally believed that Yougou, Belle's e-commerce, was far ahead, with senior executives awaiting the glory of spin-off listings, enjoying success and wealth, setting examples for countless traditional enterprises succeeding in the e-commerce era, rather than being entangled and disheartened by these critical issues.

After all, whether it be the veterans who grew with Belle or the poached e-commerce VPs, it's clear they didn't leave due to lack of ability. When the VP from JD.com returns, they can continue achieving remarkable results; when the VP from Vancl has the courage to start their own venture. Are these hard injuries limited to the three points above? Or are these not just Belle's hard injuries but also the hard injuries of many traditional enterprises attempting to enter e-commerce?
