Case: OEM Private Labeling

1. [Apparel Industry] Miss Lin from Hangzhou: Engaged in the apparel wholesale business at Hangzhou Sijiqing and Beijing Dahongmen markets, she had been purchasing goods from other factories due to the lack of her own brand. In 2001, through Ruifeng Accounting Firm, she registered [XX International Apparel Co., Ltd.] in Hong Kong [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/] and simultaneously registered trademarks in both Hong Kong and mainland China. Under the name of the Hong Kong company [http://www.rf.hk/company/hongkong/], she commissioned relevant factories for private-label processing. The packaging and labels of the clothing were marked with the name of the international apparel company, giving the clothing its own brand and moving towards a high-end direction. Now, Miss Lin's family has invested in advanced equipment in Hangzhou and established a large-scale assembly line clothing factory.

The furniture’s instruction manual and labels are marked with the name of the Italian company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/ltaly.html] and the trademark registration TM [http://www.rf.hk/trademark/]. Now, Mr. Wang's home products have truly become popular in the Chinese market.

Case: International Trade

1. [Tax Optimization] Mr. Wang from Fujian: Mainly engaged in project engineering investment bidding, due to business expansion needs, he wanted to establish a reputable group company in mainland China. However, tens of millions of registered capital were unavailable, and the desired company name was already occupied by others. Later, through a friend's introduction, he established the group company in the Cayman Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/cayman/introduction.html], and also set up holding subsidiaries in Hong Kong, Samoa [http://www.rf.hk/company/samoa/13235.html], Bahamas, and mainland China, becoming a true multinational group. After careful planning by accountants, the company's taxes were reasonably arranged.

2. [Import/Export] Ms. Du from Hefei: Long-term engagement in international import trade. Once, a French chemical raw material supplier refused to sign a contract with her, insisting on having an overseas intermediary company sign the contract and issue a letter of credit [http://www.rf.hk/exp/xinyongzheng/], citing the reason that overseas intermediary companies had higher credibility and timely reliable payments. Since this was a long-term business relationship, Ms. Du had no choice but to establish a company in Delaware, USA [http://www.rf.hk/company/usa/], and open an offshore account for the Delaware company at a foreign bank in Shanghai (this account had all kinds of settlement functions in international trade). This way, she established a long-term good cooperative relationship with the client.

3. [Trade Settlement] Mr. Jin from Tianjin: Engaged in international textile product export trade, he always had to export through intermediaries, but was concerned about business secrets and the safety of settlements. For long-term stability and to avoid exchange rate differences, he wanted to register an offshore company [http://www.rf.hk/company/offshore/], independently export, and directly settle transactions. However, the offshore company should not require complicated financial reporting procedures. With the help of Ruifeng professionals, Mr. Jin registered a BVI offshore company in the British Virgin Islands [http://www.rf.hk/company/bvi/bvijr.html] (which does not need to provide annual financial reports to the government), and simultaneously opened an offshore account at a foreign bank in Beijing, fully complying with Mr. Jin's offshore trade planning requirements and allowing for greater international development.

4. [Export Tax Rebate [http://www.rf.hk/exp/ckts/]] Mr. Lin from Xiamen: Long-term engagement in stone import and export trade. Since Xiamen had canceled the stone export tax rebate, trade profits dropped sharply. Through online research, he successfully registered [XXXX Stone Import and Export Co., Ltd.], not only eliminating the export tax rebate issue but also maximizing profit retention, and significantly speeding up the receipt of overseas payments compared to before.

5. [Payment Settlement] Mr. Chen from Shenzhen: Consistently supplied electronic components to Hong Kong manufacturers, many of which had factories in Shenzhen, Dongguan, and Zhuhai. Sometimes, Hong Kong manufacturers paid with Hong Kong bank [http://www.rf.hk/bank/hongkongbank.html] checks. Mr. Chen consistently faced issues with safe and direct payment collection. Later, after registering a company in Hong Kong and opening a bank account there, he solved the long-standing payment difficulties and gained the trust of long-term cooperation from clients.
