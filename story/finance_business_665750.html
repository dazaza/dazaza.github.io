Tetraplegic Woman Circumnavigates Britain by Controlling a Sailboat with Her Breathing (Photo Gallery)

by anonymous on 2012-02-15 09:51:09

Hilary Lister, a 37-year-old British woman, completed a 40-day circumnavigation of Britain on August 30th and returned to her hometown. For an able-bodied person, completing this journey would not be remarkable, but Lister is a quadriplegic with only her head, eyes, and mouth capable of movement.

After 40 days at sea, Lister's specially designed sailboat arrived at her hometown of Dover Port on the local time of August 30th, receiving a warm welcome. Lister expressed that the most exciting part of the entire trip was being able to see birds in the sky and fish in the sea from such close distances.

However, there were setbacks during the voyage. The most challenging moment for Lister was when the engine of the inflatable lifeboat equipped on the sailboat broke down, requiring her to spend 9,000 pounds to purchase a new one. "It was a tough decision to continue the voyage with such a high expense since it drained much of my savings," she said. "But life is like this; money is just money. You can't take it with you when you go."

"Breathing" to control the sailboat

At the age of 11, Lister was diagnosed with "reflex sympathetic dystrophy," leaving her paralyzed from the neck down with only her head, eyes, and mouth able to move.

However, sailing changed her life.

Lister piloted a specially designed 6-meter-long sailboat, controlling its direction and sails through her "breath."

On this specially modified sailboat, three straws are connected to the boat's sensor system. One straw controls the rudder: inhaling turns the boat right, exhaling turns it left. Another straw controls the winch that adjusts two sails, allowing Lister to raise or lower them freely. The third straw enables Lister to control the autopilot on the sailboat.

(Han Yang)

Next Goal: Circumnavigating the World

In a report dated August 23rd, 2005, Lister set off from Dover Port in the UK and successfully crossed the English Channel in 6 hours and 13 minutes, arriving at Calais Port in France. She became the first quadriplegic to conquer the English Channel and also set a world record for the farthest distance traveled alone by a quadriplegic.

In July 2008, Lister independently sailed around the Isle of Wight in the English Channel, becoming the first quadriplegic woman in the world to do so.

After successfully navigating around Britain, Lister's next dream is to one day sail around the world. (Han Yang)
