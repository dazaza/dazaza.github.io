Zhang Ziyi: The issue of money isn't necessarily solved by money!

by jackyshen on 2010-04-06 22:06:28

After evading the issue for two months, Zhang Ziyi has finally made a direct response to the "donation scandal." A message from the 311 Ticketing Network (www.311piao.com) claims that on the morning of the 17th, the American law firm hired by Zhang Ziyi released a statement regarding the memorandum of the Zhang Ziyi Foundation. The statement confirmed the legal and financial status of the Zhang Ziyi Foundation, particularly addressing questions that netizens were concerned about, such as "how much money has the foundation raised and in what form? Where is the money currently located?" The complaints that netizens have been lodging for several months have finally received a response. Moreover, this statement has the nature of international notarization and certification. However, some netizens who are determined to discredit Zhang Ziyi still refuse to accept it, claiming that Miss Zhang's clarification is unclear. They have raised "four major doubts" and decided to "continue discrediting Zhang Ziyi."

Zhang Ziyi, as the most accomplished Chinese film star, was once the darling of the film industry and a guarantee for box office success. However, the former谋女郎who once made movie tickets (www.311piao.com) hard to come by has now become like a rat crossing the street, everyone shouting to hit her.

Focus One: At the donation site, who was counting the money?

The lawyer's statement pointed out that, "Approximately 15 to 20 media outlets and two or three dozen other guests attended the fundraising event in Cannes on May 21, 2008. At the fundraising event, every cash donation was placed into two transparent boxes, which were passed around the venue. Subsequently, different currencies' cash donations were placed inside an envelope. These cash donations were counted and recorded on the outer layer of the envelope by two event volunteers. According to informed netizens, the staff has already confirmed the above facts in published interviews."

Ms. Zheng, the leader of the Cannes interview team, stated that at the donation site, "it wasn't entirely us; Zhang Ziyi also had a staff member count the money with us, seemingly an assistant of some sort." Hai Xia, the text journalist of the Cannes interview team, said, "Counting the money occurred after the donation event ended. Ji Lingling and one of her assistants counted the money in a relatively independent VIP area, with our other staff members nearby, including Ms. Zheng." It seems that the Zhang Ziyi side also sent people to count the money, not just "two event volunteers."

Focus Two: Why weren't the 1300+ USD deposited into the foundation?

The lawyer's statement pointed out that the total amount of cash donations collected at the scene by the fund was 145 pounds, 860 euros, and 60 Hong Kong dollars, totaling 1392.36 USD.

At the same time, according to the lawyer's statement, the deposits of the Zhang Ziyi Foundation, from May 30, 2008, to August 4, 2008, consisted of nine transactions totaling 39715.5 USD, but did not include the aforementioned 1392.36 USD. The reason why this 1392.36 USD was not deposited into the foundation was not addressed in the lawyer's statement.

Regarding these 1392.36 USD, netizens questioned, "Even if there was only 1300+ USD in cash, where was it used and which account did it enter? This should be clarified."

Focus Three: Can the 450,000 USD promised donation be recovered?

The lawyer's statement pointed out that there were eight promised donations in total, with 15050 USD from three promises already received, while 450,000 USD from five other donors has yet to be received. After contacting these promisees, one promised donor indicated they would fulfill their commitment and fully donate 50,000 USD; another person who promised to donate 100,000 USD stated that due to recent economic conditions, they could not make a full donation; two others who each promised to donate 100,000 USD stated that they had already donated to other charitable organizations set up for Sichuan earthquake relief. One of them expressed that they might consider donating to the Zhang Ziyi Foundation, while the other has not yet decided.

Wendi Deng is one of the last two "promised donors." She had already donated 100,000 USD after Zhang Ziyi's Cannes fundraising, and there is a record of this in her blog. However, according to this lawyer's statement, the amount that Zhang Ziyi can confirm to recover should only be 50,000 USD.

Focus Four: Where is the 2700 Euros?

At the beginning of the "donation scandal," a report from Sohu on May 22, 2008, used as "evidence" by many netizens, wrote: "These 3.5 million RMB destined for disaster area reconstruction consist of a 465,050 USD check, a 2700 Euro check, as well as 145 pounds, 860 Euros, and 60 Hong Kong dollars in cash." Among these, according to the lawyer's statement, the 465,050 USD was a promised donation, "which was also counted and recorded by two Sohu event volunteers on the same envelope (together with the cash donations). But the volunteer made a mistake when recording, writing the promised donation card as 'check.'"

Now, the 465,050 USD promised donation has been explained, but where is the "2700 Euro check?" Thus, some netizens questioned, "Who is lying? Is it Zhang Ziyi or Sohu?" Some netizens lamented, "When it comes to money issues, no matter how much you explain, it remains unclear."

Additionally, the lawyer's statement only disclosed the situation of the Cannes fundraising. Netizens have found Zhang Ziyi's donation advertisement in The Hollywood Reporter, and how much money that advertisement raised is also something netizens are paying attention to. In response, Zhang Ziyi mentioned in an exclusive interview with China Daily, "Unfortunately, we did not receive any donations." It seems that even celebrities find fundraising difficult.

It is uncertain when we will see Miss Zhang's next film (www.311piao.com) after this ordeal, and it is also unknown how many people will still be willing to buy tickets for her movies.
