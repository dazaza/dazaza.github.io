The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) will gradually phase out the bundled sales of mobile phones and chargers.

by diglog on 2011-11-23 12:10:19

November 23 news: According to informed sources, relevant institutions under the MIIT have introduced new regulations. Mobile phones submitted for new inspections will no longer need to be tested alongside their chargers. The underlying implication is that in the future, the MIIT will promote the sale of mobile phones themselves without bundling them with chargers. In this case, as long as the interface standards are consistent, users can use chargers from different brands to charge their phones. The era where a phone charger costs over a hundred yuan will become history. It is reported that this regulation will be implemented on December 1st. Its most significant implications include three points: First, the simultaneous testing of phones and chargers has been abolished. In the future, mobile phone manufacturers will not need to procure chargers, nor bundle them with phones for sale. Second, for users, it will greatly reduce the cost of purchasing a phone. In the past, chargers with the same interface only needed to cost 10-30 yuan in the market, but many mobile phone manufacturers would significantly increase the price of the included charger when selling their phones. The more prestigious the brand, the higher the markup, even though for all phones, the chargers could be the same. No big-name mobile phone manufacturer produces its own phone chargers; chargers and phone brands are entirely separate matters.
