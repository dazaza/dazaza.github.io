Developers hold back properties for future price hikes

by oulalala94 on 2010-05-28 18:31:46

In the middle of last year, Yang Xin (pseudonym) became interested in a residential property at Tian Shan Huating in Changning District, Shanghai. He heard that this project would open for sales at the beginning of 2008. After New Year's Day, he made more than ten phone calls to the sales office to inquire about the opening date. However, the sales staff kept postponing the opening time from January to February and then from February to March. Yesterday, when he called the sales office again, he was told that the opening date would be at the end of March or early April. [Related reading: New tactics in withholding properties: forcing employees to borrow money to buy houses and wait for higher prices before selling.] Many homebuyers are encountering the same issue. According to incomplete statistics collected by the Early Post from Youwei Real Estate Research Center, Focus Real Estate Network, and the market, at least 16 new residential projects have delayed their launches since the beginning of this year. The number of postponed launches even exceeds the number of new residential projects launched in February.

According to investigations by the Early Post, including Shanghai ONE City (InTown), Kai Xin Haoyuan Phase II, Waterfront Blue Bridge Phase III (Yupin Waterfront), and Deerport Lane, these new residential projects were originally expected to open at the beginning of this year but have not yet been launched.

Under normal circumstances, real estate projects will start promoting themselves three months before the opening and announce the sales time through advertisements. Youwei Real Estate and Focus Real Estate Network found that at least 16 projects have postponed their opening times from the beginning of the year to March, April, or even later.

In normal situations, Shanghai launches around 150 to 200 new projects per month, with about 50 being residential projects. However, in February this year, the number of new housing projects launched in Shanghai hit the lowest level since 2005, with only 31 new projects launched, including just 13 residential projects, according to Youwei Real Estate Research Center Director Jianxiong Xue.

Based on these figures, the number of residential projects launched in February is even less than the number of residential projects that have postponed their openings since the beginning of the year.

Regarding the recent phenomenon of developers delaying their openings, an analyst from Focus Real Estate Network who wished to remain anonymous believed that the main reason is the sluggish sales and观望market sentiment that began last year, causing developers to worry about poor sales after launching and facing pricing pressures later on.

He stated that the promotional activities held by Shanghai Vanke after the Lantern Festival was also one of the reasons for some developers to delay their openings. Many developers were concerned that Vanke's discounts would create a psychological expectation among homebuyers that housing prices would decrease, thereby affecting the sales speed of new homes.

However, Director Jianxiong Xue of Youwei Real Estate Research Center believed that Vanke only offered discounts for one day during the Lantern Festival and managed to sell seventy percent of its properties, indicating that market demand remains very strong. This has led most developers to still be optimistic about the future market. It's possible that many developers believe that as long as they withhold their properties through this period of observation, prices will rise again.

Jianxiong Xue believes that the main purpose for most developers to delay their openings is actually to wait for the market to warm up again and for housing prices to continue rising.
