■ Types of Sunrooms

Differences in raw materials: aluminum alloy vs steel http://www.sanshuaimc.com

Aluminum alloys are highly valuable as they can be precisely extruded and have excellent load-bearing capabilities.

An aluminum alloy-based sunroom offers limitless possibilities for personalized design. It allows for various shapes and architectural styles to reveal individual characteristics, providing extensive color choices. Aluminum alloy sunrooms possess time-tested safety, exceptional durability, and optimal thermal insulation properties.

Steel-structured sunrooms have no limitations in terms of creativity, size, and shape. All structures, from simple balcony installations to multi-story glass installations, can be realized due to the excellent compressive strength of steel. Its high stability makes it suitable for large-scale buildings. With its narrow visible surface, it enhances indoor brightness; under extreme climatic conditions, it still achieves good thermal insulation and soundproofing performance. http://www.sanshuaimc.com

Environmental significance of choosing aluminum alloy

★ Aluminum originates from bauxite, which is almost inexhaustible on Earth.

★ 60% of the energy used to smelt aluminum comes from completely pollution-free hydroelectric power worldwide.

★ Recycled aluminum alloy retains its original quality level after being reused.

★ The energy required to melt recycled aluminum alloy is equivalent to only 5% of that needed to smelt aluminum.

★ Today, 35% of aluminum alloys in Germany are recycled, and this proportion continues to increase.

The difference in performance with or without heating facilities http://www.sanshuaimc.com

A sunroom without heating facilities is an expansion based on the original building and is independent of the original structure. The sunroom itself does not require heating, acting as a heat buffer to reduce heat loss in the building. Therefore, this type of sunroom is extremely beneficial for balancing the overall heat gain and loss of the building. A sunroom with heating facilities may or may not be connected to the original building. It can increase floor area, serve as part of the curtain wall, or act as an external annex. It can also balance heat, where the heat loss in the room and the solar heat absorbed by the sunroom offset each other.
