Dads of different age groups have their own financial management priorities and insurance skills due to the differences in work and life focus. Taobao.com quoted financial planners' advice and specially prepared three financial plans for dads aged 30, 40, and 50, hoping that all fathers can become "shrewd dads."

**30-Year-Old Dads: Invest in Stocks Without Forgetting Accident Insurance**

Men around the age of 30 are usually new fathers. However, the joy of a new family member cannot stop the arrival of life pressures. Nowadays, raising a baby from birth until school age costs each family at least 200,000 yuan in child-rearing expenses.

Families of men in this age group are generally in the growth phase, with medium risk tolerance. Family financial goals should not only include the accumulation of children's educational funds but also consider financial security and asset appreciation under inflation. The suggested proportion of financial asset investment portfolios is: 10% in protection-oriented life insurance, 30% in combined deposits and bonds, and 60% in stock funds and wealth management products.

Additionally, young dads who have just become fathers are often the economic pillars of their families. To effectively avoid family financial risks, they should first consider the coverage of accident insurance and critical illness health insurance in terms of insurance. Secondly, to prevent accidents, they can also choose term life insurance with low premiums and consumption type.

**40-Year-Old Dads: Reserve Funds for Education and Pensions**

Entering the age of 40, dads will suddenly find many burdens added to their lives: hiring tutors for their children, increasing medical expenses for their parents... Most 40-year-old dads see their family responsibilities gradually increase, and their ability to bear risks decreases accordingly. Investments should be relatively conservative.

When managing finances daily, dads of this age group should preferably reserve current emergency funds and fixed-term deposits for children's education expenses in their bank deposits; additionally, they can choose government bonds or funds as pension preparations, and trust products for parents' support fees. It is important to note that considering risk factors, the proportion of high-risk investments should not exceed 15% of the total family assets.

For 40-year-old dads, who are in the stage of "respecting elders above and educating children below," social competition pressure increases. When choosing health insurance products, they should consider lifelong protection-type insurance products that offer "treatment when sick and old-age care when healthy." These types of insurance products not only provide protection but also have long-term investment returns and flexible allocation characteristics.

**50-Year-Old Dads: Stable Investments, Critical Illness Insurance**

After decades of life experiences and accumulated property arrangements, as children grow into adulthood, the age range of 50 becomes the post-peak earning period for dads. With the increased awareness of risks, they begin to seek ways to reduce investment risks, accumulate retirement funds, and start planning for a quality "retirement" life.

When investing for retirement goals at the age of 50, dads must first ensure sufficient liquid funds as basic reserves. They should also focus on savings in future salary income and investment returns, maintaining sufficient cash flow to deal with possible urgent funding needs. Additionally, if dads hope to achieve an ideal living state after retirement, they can appropriately broaden their investment fields, such as growth-oriented investment funds, low-risk bank wealth management products, etc., ensuring insurance.

It is important to note that the age of 50 has entered the high-incidence age range for major diseases. At this time, it is necessary to have a commercial insurance policy to hedge against risks. Experts suggest that dads can purchase a combination insurance policy with additional critical illness coverage. Moreover, they must buy it early because they are usually rejected for insurance after the age of 60.
