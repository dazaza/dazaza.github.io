How to use the marketing activities of Telecom Tianyi mobile phones to make a profit in online marketing

by herohu on 2009-04-13 09:55:42

I frequently visit the webmaster site, mainly because I like computers. However, I rarely get involved in website building. At first, I just played around with free forums and bound domains. It was only in my senior year that I could start building websites, as I had a lot of free time then. However, I didn't delve deeply into it, and my skill level was limited to understanding DEDECMS and making modifications according to need. Even now, I think my website-building skills are far inferior to those of the various experts hidden away on the webmaster site.

However, I believe there is one thing we can be sure of: why do we build websites? For the vast majority, without a doubt, it's for profit, to make money. Whether full-time or part-time, how do websites generate profit? Many friends on the webmaster site have given answers. I'm not an expert, so I can't provide a comprehensive answer, but most webmasters still rely on advertisements like GOOGLE ADSENSE for profit.

Let me clarify something: I am discussing the survival state of mainstream webmasters with average skill levels, like myself. If you're doing it part-time, then it's a different story. As long as website income doesn't account for a high proportion of your overall income, you can approach website building with a relaxed mindset. If you're a full-time webmaster or if website income accounts for a high proportion of your overall income, then you will definitely encounter confusion and troubles. How to make money? The income generated daily by online ads may not even compare to doing manual labor.

I think more friends need to consider how to convert website traffic into business opportunities. Many friends build spam sites, but first, spam sites have passed their golden period, and personal energy is limited. Second, no matter how many of these sites you build, they won't bring you a sense of accomplishment. Third, if webmaster friends have some real industry experience and can break away from spending over ten hours a day in front of a computer, it will definitely positively change your life. Some friends manage one or two websites, and I admire these friends, but due to limited skills, the issue of profit models often worries them to death.

Let me briefly explain my situation. I am a sales manager at a mobile phone company. Because I am very familiar with the mobile phone market in Shenzhen, and Shenzhen is the mobile phone capital of China and even the world, I want to utilize this resource to take my first step in personal entrepreneurship. Regardless of whether or not I build a website, I can use this resource without any issues right now. However, if I want to expand the business and increase the scale, having a website is still necessary. So my plan is to first build a website to attract target customers to my site. Once the website has gained some recognition in terms of traffic, I will then start a real business. It will consist of two parts: one part is wholesale business, which I don't worry about since I have channel clients, and this is the core of the business profit. The other part is retail, which will require slowly gathering people through the website. Since the real business involves an investment of hundreds of thousands, it's more important for the website to gather popularity rather than focus on business initially.

Building a website also requires favorable conditions. Currently, the telecom "Tianyi 189" campaign is in full swing. This is the lifeline of telecom, and telecom has a strict task this year, which is to develop tens of millions of users. Therefore, my website can seize this opportunity and achieve a good position in network keyword rankings, combining my resource advantages and industry advantages, maximizing the value of the Tianyi 189 Mobile Info website.

Just everyday work itself is already very busy. I originally wanted to try SEO and such things slowly, but my energy is indeed limited. In the initial stage, I also didn't want to hire someone to do this, and running this project is very meaningful for me personally. I am persisting, persistence is like holding on stubbornly, and many friends who build websites probably have this kind of survival state, haha.

I have always admired webmaster friends with technical skills, and I hope to reach that level. However, there is one point: I hope it can serve as a warning to all webmaster friends. After mixing in the Shenzhen business circle for a long time, I feel that many people with business acumen are willing to take risks, they are willing to do practical things, their websites are very simple, maybe just a blog-like QQ space, but they are very young and have done big businesses. So I really hope that if you're not someone who wants to build websites for a lifetime, you should get more involved in real industries, combining them with the internet is our biggest advantage.
