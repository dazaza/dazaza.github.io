Jay Chou goes to Shangri-La Snow Mountain: "I will never dare to open the refrigerator again"

Beijing Entertainment Xinbao, August 7, 2006, 10:29 AM

Recently, Jay Chou went to the distant and mysterious Shangri-La to shoot a new advertisement for a clothing brand he endorses. The oxygen-deficient and cold plateau made Mr. Chou suffer a lot.

Shooting while injured and receiving oxygen

The theme of this advertisement is: Life is a crazy symphony. Jay Chou and his partners traveled through the forest. In their minds, there was a world of wild imagination where deer could play hide-and-seek with them, leaves attacked them, and there were abnormally bright flowers and suddenly extended rainbow roads. All impossible things became possible in this "crazy symphony," and "wild imagination" was their way of life that "doesn't follow the usual path." To realize such an exciting creative idea was not an easy task.

Entering the mountainous area covered by primeval forests, everyone had to hike up the mountains step by step, which was extremely difficult. No one could escape the invisible claws of "altitude sickness." Although breathing heavily while walking, Mr. Chou looked quite excited because he had never seen such a primeval forest before. When he couldn't hold on anymore, he would go into the medical vehicle to inhale oxygen. He only regretted that he couldn't carry an oxygen tank while shooting the advertisement. So, it was a cycle of inhaling oxygen for a while, shooting for a while, and then inhaling again.

Simple running and jumping on the plain became "high-difficulty" actions on such a high plateau. A running action from a lower place up to the hill finally made Mr. Chou sprain his ankle and fall to the ground. The staff all got a cold sweat. Fortunately, the injury was not serious. After the doctor who accompanied him applied some medicine, Mr. Chou continued to shoot despite his injury. His professional spirit moved all the staff.

Then, they moved to the snowy mountain to continue the outdoor shooting. After experiencing the difficulty of stepping deep and shallow in the snow, this king of pop couldn't help but sigh, "I will never dare to open the refrigerator again."
