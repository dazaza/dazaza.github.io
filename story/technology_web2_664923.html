Individual mortgage quotas are tight, but banks deny any change in preferential policies for second homes.

by qqfgskkgfec1 on 2012-02-14 09:35:35

Tightening of personal housing loan quotas: Banks deny changes to second-home preferential policies - Building Materials Knowledge - Industry News - Building Materials Network. As the cyclical characteristics of the industry become apparent, the market game between banks and real estate has triggered an annual "loose in the beginning, tight at the end" change pattern in home loans.

On November 3rd, a customer service representative from Guangzhou Panhua Financial Services Group stated during a business consultation that all cooperating banks had canceled preferential interest rates for second homes, with an increase of 10%. Additionally, the universal "70% interest rate discount" at the start of the year is now only available to first-time homebuyers.

The individual further explained that starting from mid-August, some banks began tightening their interest rate preferential policies, which have now extended to all banks strictly enforcing second-home regulations.

Although the second-home policy isn't new, a securities analyst from Shenzhen noted that from the persistent emphasis by regulatory authorities on strict enforcement, many banks were not fully implementing these rules, especially at the start of the year. Given the significant proportion of mortgage loans in banks' portfolios, the impact of interest rate discounts and down payment ratios on bank profits is evident. Therefore, banks are now adopting a "doing without saying" approach.

Moreover, foreign banks that increased their lending in the second half of the year have also joined this trend. According to media reports, the second-home mortgage interest rates of Bank of East Asia and Citibank have already been set at the benchmark rate plus 10%, while HSBC and Standard Chartered's second-home mortgage rates still offer a minimum 7.1% discount.

Policy Red Lines Unchanged

The aforementioned financial intermediary personnel also noted that since 2007, banks' attitudes toward personal home loans have been loose at the beginning of the year and very tight by the end. This adjustment in interest rate preferences may continue until early next year before any opportunity for reversal.

Since the beginning of the year, driven by market competition, commercial banks have broken through the red line of second-home policies. Although there are policies in place, their execution has been lenient.

On June 22nd, the China Banking Regulatory Commission (CBRC) issued a notice titled "Further Strengthening Mortgage Loan Risk Management," reiterating the policy baseline of a 40% down payment and a 10% interest rate increase for second homes. Subsequently, at various regulatory meetings and occasions, CBRC officials repeatedly emphasized individual loan risks and the standards for second-home policies. Recently, at the fourth quarterly economic and financial situation analysis briefing (teleconference) in 2009, Chairman Liu Mingkang of the CBRC stressed the need to strictly enforce the "second-home loan" policy without wavering, comprehensively implementing face-to-face interviews and signatures with borrowers, and consolidating the credit limits of developers, project companies, and parent companies under unified regulation.

Additionally, the excessive lending by banks in the first half of the year has made them appear "uninterested" in the second half due to depleted quotas.

Industry insiders revealed that as of the end of August this year, the increment in personal consumption loans reached 1.04 trillion yuan, whereas last year's increment was only 320 billion yuan during the same period. Personal home purchase loans account for about 80% of personal consumption loans, meaning they increased by nearly 800 billion yuan in the first eight months of this year.

This far exceeded the banks' expectations. A person involved in individual loans at a shareholding bank mentioned that at the beginning of the year, the outlook for personal loan businesses was pessimistic, so task assignments were cautious, with smaller increments, possibly only one-third or half of the average increment in previous years.

However, the recovery of the real estate market surpassed market expectations. Thus, some banks increased their personal loan targets mid-year, but others did not adjust their plans. Therefore, "By September and October, we had already completed our tasks, something unimaginable in previous years because the 'Golden September and Silver October' period usually accounted for the majority of sales, and it wasn't until October that we knew how much work remained," said the aforementioned person from the shareholding bank’s individual loan department.

He further pointed out that the significant increase in mortgages this year is related to policy support encouraging the development of the real estate market and the recovery of the housing market. Overall, the growth remains relatively rational.

As of the end of August, the balance of personal consumption loans was 4.8 trillion yuan, while corporate loans increased more than the total amount of personal loans. Therefore, "China's consumer credit development lags behind, accounting for less than 10% of the overall credit disbursement. Chinese consumers' debt-based consumption cannot compare with foreign countries," said the aforementioned person from the shareholding bank’s individual loan department.

The aforementioned securities analyst from Shenzhen indicated that by the third quarter, most banks, especially shareholding banks, had seen their loan-to-deposit ratios approach the high level of 75%, even though they continuously reduced the proportion of bills to increase quotas, their lending capacity was still restricted. More importantly, many banks have started shifting their focus towards attracting deposits and storing customers for next year.

Additionally, from the perspective of banks, this year marks the narrowest period of interest rate spreads. From the profit performance in the first three quarters, compensating for price with volume clearly hasn't worked, thus most banks lack motivation to make a final push at the end of the year and instead show more interest in reserving customers for next year.

Future Speculations on Housing Loan Policies

Despite continuous information suggesting tighter second-home policies at the operational level, at the management level, banks consistently deny changes in policy enforcement.

On November 3rd, a responsible person for individual loan business at a shareholding bank pointed out that they had not received any notification regarding an increase in second-home loan interest rates, nor had the headquarters issued similar documents.

He stated that their bank could still offer discounted interest rates; "If the down payment for a second home reaches 40%, discounts of 15%, 20%, or even 25% can be offered"; however, some banks have adopted the benchmark interest rate or increased it by 10%.

He believed that raising the interest rate on second-home loans should be a practice of branch offices, a choice made by market-end operating institutions. "The reasons are quite similar; many banks have performed well in completing their individual loan targets. After finishing their individual loan tasks, these operating institutions do not want to handle too many cases, and the 70% discount does not yield profits. At this point, if they want to conduct individual loan businesses, they will raise the interest rate. Some commissions on second-hand houses are no longer returned."

On November 3rd, a relevant official from the Bank of China also denied claims of tightened housing loan policies. The individual stated that from a quantitative perspective, the growth in individual loans has been relatively stable since the beginning of the year. However, due to lagging factors, the transactions of the third quarter might only be reflected in the fourth quarter. Nevertheless, in early September, the Guangdong branch of the Bank of China, which led in credit growth, even suspended housing and car loans.

On the same day, a relevant person from the individual loan department of Ping An Bank claimed that the head office had not restricted the expansion of individual loans. In terms of quota, although the current tasks have been completed, due to uncertainties about next year's policy direction, quality customers have not been refused.

However, although banks have begun to raise the threshold for home loans, speculations about housing loan policies have caused reverse effects on real estate sales.

Industry insiders pointed out that actually, before the State Council Document No. 131 ("Several Opinions of the General Office of the State Council on Promoting the Healthy Development of the Real Estate Market"), regulators had specified the down payment ratio and interest rates for second homes, namely a 40% down payment and an interest rate 1.1 times the benchmark, increasing significantly with additional properties.

After the issuance of State Council Document No. 131, "to encourage housing consumption, improvements were proposed, which blurred the requirements for second homes, as purchasing a second home often involves improvement elements; combined with the commercial banks' own capital situations, autonomous pricing essentially blurred the requirement of a 1.1 times interest rate," said the aforementioned insider.

It is worth noting that the policy in State Council Document No. 131 promoting ordinary commodity housing consumption (including credit support and tax reductions) is temporarily set to expire on December 31, 2009.

A responsible person from a large financial services group in Shenzhen stated that banks actually need to consider the stance of regulatory authorities regarding policies such as the 70% discount. The real estate sector and some potential homebuyers holding cash even expect adjustments to policies like the 70% discount at the beginning of next year.

The responsible person continued, "Unlike interest rate fluctuations, policies like interest rate discounts and business taxes are not adjusted annually, hence causing the recent rush to complete transactions before the deadline."

Will the competition among banks for second-hand home loans lead to non-compliance and false transactions, resulting in an increase in defaulting customers? The aforementioned person from the shareholding bank stated that the default situation this year has been relatively stable, while the number of defaults last year at this time was higher.

Industry insiders mentioned that in previous years, the number of defaulting customers was lower at the end of the year, mainly due to annual report factors. It gradually increases after the first quarter of the following year, peaking in May. With the release of the semi-annual report in June, the number of defaulting customers decreases; then rises again in July and August, reaching another peak in September.

However, the increase in defaulting customers is not strongly correlated with excessive competition. "Our business processes and risk policies have not changed for the sake of the market. Loan reviews are separate, with independent review and marketing personnel managed along two lines," said the aforementioned person from the shareholding bank.
