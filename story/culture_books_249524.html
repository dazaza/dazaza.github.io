Is studying really that difficult? Part One

In 1992, Shandong Province held a bottom-up speech contest. At the time, I was at Yishui No.1 Middle School, where the school required each teaching research group to present a program, and as a result, the math department selected me. I thought about this speech contest, and realized I had absolutely no advantage: first, I specialized in mathematics and didn't understand literature at all; second, my Mandarin wasn't very standard either. If compared with teachers from the Chinese, history, or political science departments, I would just be an accompaniment. But since the math department gave me the task, I took it as a mission to complete, hastily scribbling down a few pages.

Is studying really that difficult? Part Two

After a person finishes eating, their stomach requires a large amount of blood to digest and absorb the food they have just eaten. However, the volume of blood in the human body is limited. With a large amount of blood being used for digestion in the stomach, the brain will lack blood supply and enter a state of inhibition. Most people feel sleepy and confused after lunch, and this is the reason why. Additionally, sitting down to study right after eating, regardless of study efficiency, is also detrimental to health.

Is studying really that difficult? Part Three

Students believe that as the college entrance examination approaches, everything has become set in stone, making further study unnecessary. The type of student mentioned earlier who missed entering Tsinghua University by two points falls into this category. Even worse, some teachers have mistaken notions, telling students that with only a month left until the college entrance examination, looking at your current situation, you're basically set in your ways, and should mainly focus on adjustments, checking for omissions, and don't need systematic learning anymore. Parents also believe that at this point, students should mainly focus on conserving their strength, resulting in students being unable to study properly.
