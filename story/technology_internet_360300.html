The magical web game "Dungeon Guardians" receives industry praise, Dongyou Network is acknowledged

by apple5678 on 2010-07-09 10:51:49

The game "Dungeon Guardians," produced by Eastgame Network, has received numerous positive reviews from various media outlets in the gaming industry since its debut. As a fantasy web-based game, "Dungeon Guardians" offers an experience comparable to traditional online games. It is sure to bring surprises to players.

[Game Overview]

In the game, a player's underground kingdom can be divided into two main parts: one being the player’s underground city — which features various types of underground structures and different kinds of underground creatures, representing the primary area for player development; the other part is the maze — also known as the "dungeon," which serves as the only route connecting the underground city to the surface. It is also where players dig and extract resources, mainly consisting of tunnels dug by the player.

The dungeon is a crucial concept in the game — not only because it randomly generates essential resources needed for city development, but more importantly, it is the only passage connecting the underground city to the surface. Therefore, the two main interactive processes for players — "adventure" and "battle" — both take place within the dungeon.

For players seeking adventure, when visiting another player's underground city, you will appear at the entrance of their dungeon. You then need to control your character in an RPG-like manner, navigating through complex maze tunnels to reach the exit — which is the entrance to the underground city. What are the benefits of doing this? Once you reach the entrance of the underground city, you can plunder a large amount of resources from the owner of that underground city.

On the other hand, as a dungeon master, you cannot allow other players to freely loot your underground city. Thus, the dungeon becomes the battlefield to repel invading players. Generally, there are three methods to block invaders: 1) The dungeon master can construct a very complex dungeon maze, with many branching tunnels that make it difficult for adventurers to find the correct path to the exit; 2) The dungeon master can place various traps on non-essential paths, dealing fatal blows once triggered by invaders; 3) The dungeon master can recruit powerful underground monsters within their city and position them inside the castle. When another player invades, they will randomly encounter these guards and trigger battles. Using guards to drive away invaders is often the most common and primary defensive method used by players.

[Maze System]

Regardless of how one wants to develop their underground city, creating a dungeon is always the first thing a player must do. In this game, the basic process of creating a maze is completed instantly without requiring any time. Therefore, players can conveniently and arbitrarily change their underground city tunnels, switch layouts, or even completely redesign their mazes. The construction of mazes mainly includes functions such as digging tunnels, setting entrances and exits, filling tunnels, resetting dungeons, and submitting settings. Within the maze, players can also establish mines, dig for minerals, gold, and other resources which are the main sources for developing the underground city. However, as a dungeon master, one's primary responsibility is to defend against other players entering their underground city. One of the main methods of defense is to set up various traps within the dungeon, delivering fatal blows when triggered by other players.

[Development System]

Continuously expanding and developing one's underground city and monster army is the core content of this game. So how exactly does a player develop? This section primarily introduces this content.

The development of the dungeon can mainly be divided into three systems: the building system, the monster system, and the engineering system. The building system introduces the buildings in the player's underground city and their functions; the monster system introduces the concept of dungeon monsters and their composition; the engineering system explains how the development process is realized.

[Character System]

The character is the main object controlled by the player when adventuring in another player's underground city and represents the faction during battle. Additionally, when the game expands to include systems like the dungeon master expulsion system and the arena system, which enable direct interaction between players, the character will become the main target of interaction. Moreover, the level of the character determines the maximum level of the dungeon. Therefore, the character is actually another key measure of a player's gaming strength, aside from the dungeon.

[Battle System]

The battle portion of the game is a simple turn-based combat process. In the battle interface, each side has three positions where monsters can be placed, called "battle positions." Initially, each "battle position" will randomly generate a number between 0 and 9, referred to as the current "priority" of that battle position. The player needs to select appropriate monsters to assign to these three positions and confirm readiness. When both sides are ready, the player's monsters will compete with the corresponding "battle position" monsters of the enemy. The higher number initiates the attack, while the lower number defends, calculating the damage taken by both sides. Next, if both sides can continue fighting, the "priority" of the three "battle positions" will change according to certain rules, entering the next round of combat and repeating the above steps. Besides this, during the battle preparation process, players can also use various spells and special items to strengthen themselves and weaken opponents.
