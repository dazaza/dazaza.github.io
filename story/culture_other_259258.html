### Slimming Face Strategy: Buccal Fat Pad Removal

An important structure causing facial fullness is the buccal fat pad located in the cheek area. The buccal fat pad is located behind the jaw and in front of the masseter muscle, enclosed in an independent capsule. In infancy, it helps maintain negative pressure during sucking and serves as a major support structure for the face. Enlargement of the buccal fat pad is one of the main causes of lower facial obesity and large faces. Removing the buccal fat pad is an effective solution for a large face.

From a cosmetic perspective, a woman's cheeks should not be overly full. A slightly flat appearance with a beautiful contour line is more desirable. The contours around the cheeks can be clearly identified, with the anterior edge of the parotid gland and masseter muscle serving as the posterior boundary, the nasolabial groove as the anterior boundary, the protruding zygomatic bone as the upper boundary, and the clear edge of the mandible as the lower boundary of the cheek. People who feel their face shape is too large are suitable for buccal fat pad removal surgery.
