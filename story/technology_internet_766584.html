Silicon Valley peers view Microsoft: Ballmer was fired by Gates

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 22:08:07

On September 17, an editor from the tech news website BusinessInsider conducted interviews in the San Francisco Bay Area. He visited Silicon Valley and spoke with employees there about a company that has been under intense scrutiny these past few days—Microsoft. So, what do insiders in the high-tech industry think of Microsoft? Here's what people had to say:

"Microsoft is in total disarray. No one there knows what to do next."

"Just days before the company's restructuring plan was implemented, Ballmer informed the board of this major decision."

"Ballmer was completely unaware he was going to be fired until it happened; it was a forced termination." "Moreover, Microsoft's major shareholder, ValueAct, was also kept in the dark."

"In short, Ballmer didn't leave on his own terms or gracefully. His departure was due to a sudden executive order."

"The decision to fire Ballmer came from Bill Gates."

"Similarly, Gates has been deeply involved in the search for the new CEO of Microsoft."

"Gates is planning to return to Microsoft and personally take charge of the company’s operations."

"Gates is looking for a CEO who can adapt to his active management style, someone who can develop long-term strategies for the company's growth. More importantly, Gates wants a CEO with experience managing a publicly traded company and who has the ability to oversee more than 125,000 Microsoft employees."

"Therefore, Nokia CEO Stephen Elop is considered a suitable candidate for the position."

"If Gates returns to Microsoft, it would make many in Silicon Valley very happy. Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg once expressed in an interview how eager he is for Gates to take back control of Microsoft."

"Advice to Microsoft: Spin off the consumer business. Optimize Windows and Office versions' privileges, invest in creating an AWS platform dedicated to enterprises. Hire someone like Marten Mickos (founder of MySQL) as CEO. Yahoo CEO Marissa Mayer believes Microsoft should hire a CEO that fits the company's needs."

"Additionally, many industry insiders believe Gates is the only person who can turn Microsoft's situation around. Microsoft has essentially become a casualty of Gates' successful career. The problem lies in the massive installed base of Windows. For a company in decline, maintaining this requires far too much capital."
