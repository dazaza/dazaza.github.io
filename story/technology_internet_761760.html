Alibaba is moving towards "loss of control"

by geekzhang on 2013-05-09 11:09:31

Behind Ma Yun's resignation as CEO of Ali, it was his self-choice that pushed Ali towards a state of uncontrollability.Taobao.com, one of the biggest victims of counterfeits and substandard products, feels the pain deeply and must adopt more means to completely eliminate this tumor. On the afternoon of April 23rd, Ma Yun, Chairman of the Board of Directors of Alibaba Group (hereinafter referred to as Ali), said at a press conference.The protection of intellectual property rights and anti-counterfeiting are the last challenges that Ali needs to overcome before its overall listing after solving the ownership issue of Yahoo. Ma Yun chose this as the theme for his last appearance before resigning as CEO, which is profound in meaning.Since the beginning of 2013, changes in Ali seem to have never stopped. Ma Yun announced that he would step down as CEO of the Ali Group on May 10th, and with an email, he split the seven business groups that were just reorganized six months ago into 25 divisions, each supervised by the Group Strategic Management Executive Committee. In March, four new divisions were also created under the Ali Financial Group. Accompanying these changes were the appointments of Lu Zhouxi as the new CEO of the Ali Group and Peng Lei as the CEO of the Micro Financial Services Group.Although several months have passed, why did Ma Yun leave? Why does he want to further divide and refine the organization? What will Ali be like after the division? Where does Ali want to go? The reasons behind all this have been debated by outsiders, leaving many questions unanswered.In fact, these doubts also exist within Ali's internal system. Since the beginning of the year, all available meeting rooms inside Ali have been fully booked around the clock. It's still not enough; it's rumored that all coffee shops, restaurants, and any place suitable for meetings around the Ali Group are thriving with business.A large amount of time and space is being used by Ali employees for intense communication. The reason is obvious: there are too many internal and external changes happening too fast, seemingly no one knows how to proceed next, nor does anyone know what the future will look like. This giant internet company, which generated a trillion yuan in transactions in 2012, suddenly seems to be heading towards a state of uncontrollability amidst high-speed operation and frequent changes.However, this might just be the result Ma Yun wants.Scattering and ReconstructingIn the eyes of insiders, Ali indeed had problems over the past two years. First, Ma Yun found that Taobao was becoming more like an empire. His intuition told him something wasn't right, so the solution then was to break up Taobao; secondly, the anti-corruption campaign in 2012. When the market grows extremely fast and management can't keep up, it's easy to hit a wall, so the group was divided into the 'Seven Swords'.And why is there another breakup this time? Let's first take a look at the newly formed 25 divisions, which can be categorized into two types according to Zeng Ming, Chief Strategy Officer of Ali Group:Firstly, vertical category businesses. From the early Taobao, to Tmall and Juhuasuan later, and independent divisions such as aviation and travel, music, etc., all have specific user groups and market values.Secondly, infrastructure platform businesses. For example, cloud computing, payment, logistics, these are basic platform businesses. This year, they will attempt to integrate them into what is called a shared business platform.We hope the boundaries between vertical businesses become clearer, and the relationship between platform businesses and vertical businesses becomes clearer as well. And when this problem becomes complex to a certain extent, traditional methods really cannot solve it anymore. Zeng Ming explained the motivation.Looking at Ali's internal structure from above, it's not hard to see that this adjustment not only overlays vertical and horizontal businesses together but also emphasizes the significance of horizontal shared platforms even more. When some commonalities emerge between different vertical businesses, they should be systematized and deposited onto the shared platform. For instance, if you have a marketing process, other Business Units (BUs) can also share and use it.The purest method is to make the shared platform an internal open-source system, using common standards to achieve advantage sharing. Regardless of how varied the front-end businesses may be, the underlying system remains stable. This fundamentally resolves the perpetual conflicts between vertical businesses and platform businesses. Zeng Ming said, "We are very excited because we can finally operate a company using Internet methods."Just like many people say about the disappearing Taobao, is Taobao gone? In fact, it has evolved into a huge underlying layer, the most critical foundation of the entire ecosystem. When basic rules and forms are settled down, organisms will adapt to this environment, and the freer the ecosystem grows, the deeper Taobao will sink.What Ali hopes for is the evolution of a true shared system, including membership systems, transaction systems, payment systems, product management systems, credit systems, a series of underlying technologies, and product systems, etc. The purpose of the entire set of underlying systems is that any business, once the direction is clear, can grow by plugging into this underlying platform. This precisely demonstrates Ali's greatest defense instinct against uncertainty about itself and the external environment.You will find that there are actually many information nodes within Ali, forming a web-like nodal matrix. As the composition of an organization becomes increasingly fine-grained, inevitably more and faster combinations will emerge, much like a kind of savage growth. Zeng Ming added that the role of the nine management responsible persons above the 25 divisions is to make judgments and coordinate during various wild clashes and extremely internal consumption.As for Ali's thinking behind the division, the number of divisions doesn't matter. From Taobao being split into three parts, to the seven business groups being subdivided into 25 divisions, these BUs aren't the key adjustments. They can increase anytime tomorrow. The key lies in this action of splitting, Zeng Ming said, ultimately aiming to form a more unified e-commerce ecosystem, OneEcosystem, through dispersion and division.We hope the organization eventually transforms into a network structure, allowing us to let go of control with confidence. Because everyone's goals are consistent. The previous Top-Down management style and organizational model are greatly challenged, and I am also adapting in the process, tolerating my own ignorance of many things, allowing more talents to grow. Zhang Yu, Vice President of Ali, said.However, behind much of the uncontrollable situations and need for adaptation, relatively clear is that this adjustment by Ali is a difficult scattering and reconstruction—not changing for the sake of problems, not changing for water to flow smoothly, but to create a completely new ecological situation without precedent, just as Ma Yun wrote in his letter.A self-organizing high-frequency mutation will inevitably cause discomfort due to tremendous inertia. Zeng Ming said that gradually exploring a path for this new organizational form, matching culture, and the new qualities required for people in such an environment, will be the most fundamental and important work in the next two to three years.Using Collaboration to Weaken KPIsWhat exactly does Ali hope to become?At the internal strategic meeting at the end of 2012, Ma Yun spent only half an hour proposing a blueprint of four transformations: marketization, platformization, ecologization (species diversification), and dataization. Specifically, the understanding is as follows:One of the major values of the Internet is serving a massive number of users through a simple platform-level product. Taobao has achieved the value of platformization with a scale where 100 million people log in daily, 6 million sellers sell 800 million items.If this platform is managed, designed, and resource-allocated using a planned economy method, not only would the efficiency be low, but it would also generate derivative issues like corruption. The significance of marketization lies in not actively distributing resources but promoting system alignment through market-based methods.Ali, as an ecosystem, naturally evolves characteristics pursuing diversity, i.e., species diversification. There’s survival of the fittest, food chains upstream, midstream, and downstream, complementarity, and coordination, requiring the ecosystem to explore and evolve on its own.The core running through all this is data—maximizing data value, optimizing, mining, and operating data. Ali's essence in the future will certainly be a data operation company.Just like the smooth sailing Double Eleven event in 2012. Many people remember the 19.1 billion yuan figure. In fact, for the internal team, the greater significance lies in the complete integration of the four transformation concepts throughout the event. Taobao operators can decide the rules but not the goods or merchants. Merchant recruitment, from registration to ranking, to placing products, assigning positions, page layouts, including rotation, all follow the rules, replacing allocation with systematic and management-matching methods. Data's value is the foundation—it's the base layer, and we're the application layer, said Zhang Yong from Tmall.From a strategic perspective, looking at Ali, from the September 2007 group proposal to build an open, collaborative, prosperous e-commerce ecosystem, to the proposed four transformations, Ali has gradually established a methodology for both internal and external ecosystems. And this round of adjustments essentially sees Ali exploring a new management method and system—whether there's a method that can better adapt to the ever-changing environment of the Internet.While driving the external ecosystem, Ali hopes the entire company internally also becomes ecological. That is, decision-making mechanisms, resource allocation, communication and coordination mechanisms can simulate a self-circulating ecosystem.In the entire process of scattering and reconstructing, it means everyone must accept continuous breakthroughs and collisions, accepting unpredictable variables. Of course, the question arises: Ali is too big, how exactly do you evaluate this system?This time, we decoupled GMV and KPIs, not evaluating these metrics, at least not at the group level for divisions. How teams below decide themselves is their choice, said Shao Xiaofeng, Ali's Chief Risk Officer.He frankly stated that the evaluation this year after the adjustment is actually quite abstract. It's entirely based on case evaluations, simply covering three aspects:First is innovation. Mainly evaluating what innovations exist, which innovations are recognized by the market, and accepted by users. These are obviously not a bunch of specific numbers.Second is collaboration. After the split, the relationships between the 25 BUs will become very tight. And the ecosystem that Alibaba has always wanted to build requires better collaboration and cooperation among BUs, with closely related interests. Evaluation dimensions include which collaborations are good, which works are done for other BUs, achieving mutual promotion. People often joke now, "I'm in your hands, and you're in mine."The third dimension is today and tomorrow. This is hard to understand. Shao Xiaofeng explained: beyond the expected development of today's business, what preparations have been made for tomorrow on this basis? This might not produce direct numerical changes in this year's BU business development, but it lays a good foundation for future development.Tracing back to the corruption issues within the group previously, Ali once only looked at GMV, which was the prettiest number, making everyone think it was great. The easiest way to do data is to operate blockbuster products; sellers all hope to rank higher and get traffic resource support, leading to corruption among operators, and the ecosystem gets stuck.From another angle, the issue of KPIs is also the most common consideration for current Internet companies. Taking Baidu as an example, according to the Commercial Value journalist's understanding, its internal reflection recently involves Li Yanhong's near-perfect requirements for KPI evaluations, pursuing highly efficient KPI management culture, which is fine in a traditional, maturely operating company and business model.But when innovation is needed, all complete structures become the biggest obstacles. Especially now, in the mobile Internet era. When your KPI targets a specific direction, it's easy to chase KPIs and find it hard to pivot.This point is acknowledged across the entire capital market. Google explicitly stated upon going public that it wouldn't provide quarterly guidance targets anymore, unwilling to manipulate quarterly certainty; Amazon also frequently experiences significant losses and profit fluctuations. Amazon incurred a massive loss in 2012 because it was investing heavily for the future again.The Internet needs a high tolerance for uncertainties and must dare to lose control.When you don't have a common foundation and can't collaborate, you can only resort to KPIs as a relatively simple incentive method to get a simple output. Facing such a complex environment and needing something entirely new, it's impossible to know what kind of KPIs are most reasonable. Zeng Ming indicated.Moving Towards UncontrollabilityFrom breaking down the organizational structure, diluting KPIs, to every department facing increasing collisions, collaborations, and cooperations with others, from this drastic change not being top-down or pre-planned to our decentralization, few truly know what Ma Yun is thinking, but at this point, we can instead look back at why Ma Yun resigned.In many organizations, the departure of the founder usually has two motives: one is doing poorly, and the other is doing exceptionally well. According to Ali's current evolutionary state, within the large e-commerce ecosystem, Ali as a species or subsystem is undoubtedly the latter. But when everyone idolizes Ma Yun infinitely, certain things are bound to be changing.And in such an organization with absolute authority, giving up his position and direct control, Ma Yun seems to be laying the groundwork for losing control.Losing control is a relatively exaggerated and somewhat philosophical description. So-called losing control means following natural laws on a large scale without presetting, even encouraging mutations rather than evolving slowly according to rules. You need to know that unexpected events can become key factors for a company's outward development. Just like WeChat, born outside the wireless department and relatively late to enter the company's strategic direction, this product of Ma Huateng's favored state of losing control has changed Tencent's status in the mobile Internet era.Viewed from a ten-year perspective, Ali resembles an industrial age, extremely pursuing efficiency and scale: starting from a C2C marketplace, gradually entering a comfortable phase with the B2C approach of Tmall. In 2012, the 1 trillion yuan in transactions accounted for 5% of GDP. Meanwhile, renowned within the industry, besides Ma Yun, Ali also has many strategists like Zeng Ming and Lu Zhouxi.However, when the changes in e-commerce over the years have grown exponentially, and the environmental changes in the Internet and mobile Internet are unpredictable, Ma Yun must have seen that even under his tightly structured strategy, many new forces have quickly risen, even challenging Ali.For example, despite Ali generating a trillion yuan in annual transactions, from the perspective of revenue watched by the capital market, JD.com, mainly self-operated, isn't without competitiveness. At least, who will IPO first is accelerating competition; the traditional home appliance giant Suning mobilized all group resources in two years, making a desperate self-revolution, with no one failing to marvel at its courage and determination; furthermore, Ma Yun spoke early about SNS-ifying, but Taobao has repeatedly failed in attempts, while two websites, Meilishuo and Mogujie, used a decade's worth of accumulated hundreds-of-million entry-point traffic to capture 1/10 in just two years; and in the highly imaginative O2O direction, local websites like Meituan are aggressively declaring war with a goal of reaching 100 billion yuan by 2015 in scale and magnitude.Of course, not just e-commerce, with WeChat, users' attention to Weibo has rapidly shifted, and many are eagerly watching how to quickly develop a commercial path on it; who will be the entry point for future e-commerce, and who will dominate the wireless desktop—all these intangible stimuli and heavy pressures make it impossible for Ali to remain indifferent.In such a landscape, can Ali become the leading player in Ma Yun's bet, maintaining 50% of GDP for the next decade? How will the next stage develop? Following the original step-by-step strategic planning, or even under Ma Yun's strong leadership, can it still face this constantly accelerating industrial transformation?Ali internally needs to respond through a state of losing control—the larger the company, the more necessary it is to break free from existing constraints and patterns, and seek balance through a state of losing control with all vested interests and accumulated difficulties.Ali's chosen state of losing control is a difficult-to-predict defensive move both internally and externally. For example, the upcoming micro-Tao, personalized, interactive, and with much room for extension; for example, the wireless strategy, as one of the group's top priorities, according to Zeng Ming, to increase product investment, all technical personnel across the group are allocated 5% proportionally, equivalent to direct job transfers, all reassigned to the wireless department.The best way to not know where the next new species will grow is not to bet on anything. Ali Wireless strategies have three approaches: each BU's business can go wireless, such as Tmall and Juhuasuan; independent wireless BU, based on wireless features to expand the direction of e-commerce development; and Ali OS will have millions of units shipped out.For the development of wireless in the next two to three years, what habits users will have regarding applications on mobile phones, tablets, and other wireless devices are still undecided today. Shao Xiaofeng said. Just like the O2O direction alone, the new product Yitao Fire Eyes recently launched by the Wireless BU aggregates information and services from many local merchants. Meanwhile, Juhuasuan will leverage wireless to deliver a combination punch towards localized life services.Some say that Ali's long-standing rotational assignment system has prepared sufficiently for this state of losing control, and only Ali can ensure that this state of losing control won't truly spiral out of control. Others argue that managing 25 divisions incurs enormous costs, with contradictions arising from inconsistent horizontal and vertical standards, leading to significant internal consumption. After the transition, inevitable reintegration will occur, and the sharp rise in management costs will be the biggest short-term challenge.Regardless of how these judgments turn out, one undeniable point is that the good and bad will continuously collide, emerge, and even be eliminated under the atmosphere of losing control. The term "losing control," though sounding negative, is a necessary advanced transformation process for enterprises reaching a certain level. Sometimes, without undergoing a thorough disassembly and reconstruction, it's impossible to leap to an entirely new form. Ma Yun must know that the cost of evolution lies precisely here.
