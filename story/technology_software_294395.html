The "shell preservation movement" is in full swing as ST stocks play out the role of treasure hunters towards the end of the year.

With the ebb and flow of the market, ST stocks always manage to realize their own value according to their own logic. Every year, around the time of annual reports, the traditional "shell preservation movement" of ST stocks always resurfaces dramatically. Each year, different ST stocks transform from "ugly ducklings" into "swans". This year is no exception. Currently, many ST companies have successfully transformed through asset restructurings. *ST Haixing, ST Tianxiang, *ST Jiarui, and other companies have transformed into real estate companies through asset restructurings; *ST Changhe has changed from automobile manufacturing to aviation lighting and control system manufacturing. Not only that, but recently, *ST Guonong, *ST Tangtao, ST Chenggong, ST Yaohua, *ST Qionghua, S*ST Xinta, and other companies have also successively announced asset restructuring information.

What has stunned the market is that following *ST Jiufa's legendary performance of consecutive limit-ups in the secondary market, another restructured stock has made a dazzling appearance. With S*ST Xinta's resumption of trading, it has consecutively hit seven 'one-word' limit-ups, making the myth of huge profits brought by the transformation of the ST sector the focus of attention for mainline funds. Clearly, compared to business adjustments within listed companies themselves, asset restructurings are the most effective way to quickly and drastically improve the fundamentals and performance of poorly performing companies. The enhancement in intrinsic value brought by asset restructurings constitutes the new connotation of value investment under the fully circulated shares situation.

It is highly noteworthy that as the year-end approaches, ST-class companies that have been continuously losing money and are on the verge of delisting must undergo substantial asset restructurings to avoid being delisted. This presents a significant opportunity for industrial capital eager to use shell companies for listing. Meanwhile, many ST stocks often engage in concentrated asset sales or acquisitions of quality assets at the end of the year to embellish their performances and preserve valuable shell resources, thereby obtaining the right to survive in the stock market. This provides fertile ground for the creation of profit legends involving ST stocks. It can be anticipated that with the vigorous development of the "shell preservation movement" in the A-share market at year-end, the grand drama of ST stocks playing the role of treasure hunters will continue uninterrupted!

Accelerated restructuring process: ST Shixian harbors significant investment opportunities

According to relevant data, ST Shixian (600462), formerly known as Shixian Paper Mill, was established in 1936 and is a large-scale integrated enterprise for pulp and paper production, serving as an important pillar enterprise for economic construction in Yanbian. The company's main business involves the production and sale of newsprint, offset paper, commercial pulp, and by-products. Due to the impact of the global financial crisis, which led to a decline in the prosperity of the paper industry, the company's product sales volume and prices dropped, accounts receivable increased, and it had to digest the inventory of high-priced raw materials from earlier periods and planned to dispose of some assets. As a result, the company incurred continuous losses in its operating performance in 2008 and the first half of 2009. Relying solely on its main business operations is currently unsustainable.

However, like most ST stocks that eventually achieve a miraculous transformation from an ordinary bird to a phoenix, as a key industrial enterprise and listed company resource in the Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture, the Yanbian government has given considerable attention and importance to ST Shixian. According to ST Shixian's (600462) annual report data, Jilin Shixian Paper Industry Co., Ltd.'s largest shareholder is the Yanbian State-owned Assets Operation Corporation, holding a 60.89% stake, and it is also the actual controller of the listed company. It is worth noting that historically, the Yanbian State-owned Assets Operation Corporation has announced intentions to restructure the major shareholder Shixian Paper Industry. Although this intention has not been implemented so far due to various factors, the strong restructuring expectations for ST Shixian (600462) are evident.

As the year-end approaches, driven by factors such as preserving the shell, the restructuring process of ST Shixian (600462) has clearly accelerated. On September 18, 2009, the company held its tenth board meeting of the fourth session, where it reviewed and agreed to transfer the 51% equity held in its controlling subsidiary Mudanjiang Hongshi Chemical Co., Ltd. to Honglin Chemical. It is clear that the company is accelerating its pace in cleaning up non-performing assets. Moreover, at the end of September, ST Shixian (600462) received summonses and complaints from the Intermediate People's Court of Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture due to overdue debts. Under the increasing difficulty of obtaining loans again through normal channels, the asset restructuring of ST Shixian (600462) is likely to restart. Therefore, we believe that under the market context where the myth of huge profits brought by the transformation of the ST sector has once again become the focus of attention for mainline funds, as a low-priced ST stock with a significantly accelerated restructuring process, a major investment opportunity for ST Shixian (600462) is imminent. In the secondary market, after adequate preparation, the stock has shown increased volume and strengthened momentum. Its ample rebounding power is expected to stage a powerful main upwave rally!

The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Furthermore, such an order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you wish to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits long. When placing a commission, the code and abbreviation of the stock must match exactly. Additionally, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the number of stocks purchased must be a whole multiple of 100, but the number of stocks sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.
