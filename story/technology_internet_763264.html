Facebook in the palm of your hand, growing mobile ads

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:54:37

Throughout the history of media and communication development, many content formats have emerged and disappeared, but only advertising has remained evergreen, evolving alongside the growth of media. This has led to an increasingly broad range of advertising platforms, including television, radio, outdoor, internet, newspapers, magazines, and films.

According to Nielsen data, among these advertising platforms, television and outdoor advertising are perennial stalwarts, while radio, newspapers, magazines, and film have gradually become less effective. However, the fastest-growing and most promising category is online advertising, with mobile advertising becoming increasingly important as part of the mobile wave.

Mobile advertising is a market that no one can afford to ignore and an opportunity that must be seized. As the master of online advertising, Google will inevitably enter this field, but currently, Facebook is leading the charge.

Compared to the disappointing quarterly earnings reports from Microsoft, Google, and Apple, Facebook's financial performance stands out. Thanks to its best quarterly report since going public, Facebook's stock surged by about 30% after releasing its earnings. The biggest contributor to this success was the strong performance of mobile advertising.

In Q2 2013, Facebook reported revenue of $1.81 billion, up 53% from $1.184 billion in the same period last year. Net profit reached $333 million compared to a loss of $157 million during the same time last year. Mobile advertising generated $656 million, accounting for 41% of the company's total advertising revenue. The significant increase in mobile advertising revenue is primarily due to steady growth in Facebook's mobile user base. Monthly active users increased from 1.11 billion in the previous quarter to 1.15 billion, while daily active users rose from 665 million to 669 million. Growth on the mobile platform was even more pronounced, with daily active users reaching 469 million and monthly active users increasing from 751 million to 819 million. The faster growth rate on mobile indicates that more and more users are shifting their activity to mobile devices.

On another note, users spend more time on mobile than on desktops. For instance, in March of last year, Facebook users spent 441 minutes per month on mobile versus 391 minutes on desktops. A year later, the time spent on mobile has likely increased further.

In terms of mobile advertising content, the core remains advertisements integrated into the news feed. Zuckerberg explained that currently, for every twenty items in a Facebook news feed, one is an advertisement. Mobile users typically see personalized interest-based ads as the second item after refreshing the page. Although these ads may seem intrusive and lacking in subtlety, they undeniably deliver good results.

Facebook's CFO, David Ebersman, stated that the promotion of news feed ads has been highly successful. Despite adding more ad placements and raising ad prices, user engagement continues to grow along with the active user base on mobile, delivering significant returns to advertisers.

Unlike the random and annoying ads seen on platforms like Sina Weibo (e.g., promoting depilatory creams or wedding dresses to straight men), Facebook's ads are more targeted. Leveraging user-provided data, Facebook delivers relevant ads that are more likely to achieve high conversion rates.

Looking at Google, whose financial report fell short of expectations, its performance in mobile advertising is less impressive. To date, Google’s Cost-Per-Click (CPC) has fallen for seven consecutive quarters, constrained by screen size limitations and low ad conversion rates. Users find it difficult to digest excessive ad content, which highlights Facebook's advantage in integrating ads within its social network.

Despite the positive trend in mobile advertising, Facebook still lags behind Google in desktop advertising, where Google maintains a dominant lead.

According to market research firm eMarketer, Facebook is projected to capture 25.2% of the mobile advertising market share in 2013, surpassing Google's 19.6% and Pandora's 10.3%. Based on current trends, Facebook is likely to exceed this forecast.
