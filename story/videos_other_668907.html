The current scope of acceptance for this SMS emergency platform includes: emergency assistance requests from deaf and mute disabled individuals; assistance requests from foreigners who encounter language barriers when reporting emergencies; situations where it is inconvenient to use voice calls due to ongoing criminal activities threatening personal safety and property security, such as pickpocketing, gambling, or robbery on buses or long-distance vehicles, kidnappings, illegal detentions, or being in complex environments like gambling dens, prostitution rings, and drug trafficking locations. Additionally, the platform can be used for reporting other cases and crime leads when making a phone call is not feasible, as well as other situations where voice alarms are inappropriate.

According to reports, due to technical reasons from various telecommunications operators, Hebei Province temporarily cannot implement a unified province-wide 12110 emergency SMS number. Currently, the SMS emergency numbers for each city are "12110 + local area code." The police are actively coordinating with all operators to resolve technical issues and achieve a unified 12110 SMS emergency number across the province. The charge for this SMS emergency service will follow the standard SMS rate set by each telecommunications operator.

On January 9th, China News Service in Shijiazhuang reported (by Huang Fang) that starting from January 10th, Hebei Province will launch the 12110 SMS emergency service platform, which will be handled by the command centers of public security bureaus at all levels to receive SMS emergency messages and requests from the public.

The police stated that since SMS messages transmitted through telecommunications networks may experience delays or loss, people encountering urgent situations can resend the SMS message and are advised to send the SMS to relatives or friends who can then report the emergency via a 110 phone call. Additionally, the content of the emergency SMS should be as comprehensive as possible, clearly stating the location, time, and nature of the incident to enable the police to accurately and quickly respond. If the exact location cannot be determined, notable landmarks along the way, such as tunnels, entrance/exit names, or road signs, can be referenced. In special environments, before sending an emergency SMS, the mobile phone should be set to silent mode to avoid detection by criminals when receiving replies from the police. The sender can also indicate "Do Not Reply" in the SMS based on the situation on-site.

It was disclosed that SMS emergency reporting is an auxiliary, non-urgent method of emergency assistance, serving as a supplement to the 110 telephone emergency system. Its primary service targets are the hearing-impaired and mute disabled individuals who cannot use voice calls and the general public who find it inconvenient to use voice calls under specific circumstances.
