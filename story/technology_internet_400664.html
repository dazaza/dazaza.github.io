The online shopping crowd sounds the charge to the traditional business management model.

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:21:39

I don't know if you have ever had this experience: in a specialty store, seeing the clothes you like, immediately go to the fitting room and secretly note down the model and size while trying on the clothes, then buy them online.

The price difference between the two is at least 30% to 35%, some even more than 50%. Such a price difference has attracted countless shopping enthusiasts to shift their attention from physical stores to online shopping.

Not long ago, during the "11・11" Singles' Day promotion of a shopping website, there was an event offering half-price discounts on all products for the day, which immediately triggered a frenzy among online shopping enthusiasts. It is said that some clothes were sold dozens of times per minute, setting a historical record.

Nowadays, from small accessories, books, and clothes to large furniture and appliances, almost everything needed in life can be bought online. With the prevalence of online shopping, both shopping mall counters and individual shops have felt the impact of online shopping on their business.

Change in Shopping Views

"Now we all shop online. As long as we choose sellers with high credibility, things are cheaper and of good quality. Who still buys in physical stores?" Wang Lan, a "post-80s" who works in a community in Yucheng District, told reporters about her first online shopping experience.

One weekend, Wang Lan went shopping with friends, planning to buy a pair of boots. In a brand shoe specialty store in the city center, Wang Lan immediately liked a brown mid-calf boot priced at 899 yuan. Since it was in-season, the store offered no discount.

"Originally, I was ready to pay for these shoes, but my friend stopped me." Wang Lan said her friend whispered to her, "Maybe they are also sold online, and they might be cheaper."

After trying on the right size, Wang Lan quietly noted down the style and item number, went home and searched online, and indeed found the same shoes. A seller from Hangzhou quoted the lowest price at only 379 yuan. This seller specialized in online out-of-town proxy purchases and clearly promised on their homepage that the shoes were all genuine products from major shopping mall counters. The seller also promised unconditional returns within seven days. The reason for such a favorable price was that the price of these short boots in Hangzhou malls was 759 yuan, plus the mall's promotional 50% off, so the final price was 379 yuan.

Although Wang Lan still maintains the habit of going to the mall once a week, she rarely consumes in physical stores now. Online prices are generally equivalent to 50% to 60% of those in physical stores. In some big cities, due to the gathering of many brands and fierce competition, the promotional efforts are very large, so purchasing through online proxy purchases from other places is still very cost-effective.

Online shopping is indeed cheap and of good quality, and now there are more and more people like Wang Lan who belong to the online shopping group. On some shopping websites, some sellers even directly note: "Dear ladies, if you see something you like in a physical store, you can report the model to me, and I will check for you and sell it to you at the most favorable price."

Another factor why consumers turn their attention to online stores is that seasonal clothing in malls or specialty stores often has no discount, and when these clothes are discounted, most of them are already out of season. However, online shopping allows you to buy seasonal clothes at a discounted price, naturally becoming the first choice for consumers.

"Seeing my daughter love to shop online, now I've also learned how to shop online." Ms. Li, 48 years old, living in Ping'an Neighborhood, told reporters that online shopping can compare prices from different sellers, just like shopping around.

Nowadays, being able to select high-quality and affordable goods without leaving home, the consumption concept of many consumers is changing.

Impact on Physical Stores

Data from the internet shows that in 2009, the national online shopping scale reached 267 billion yuan, with 130 million online shoppers.

The popularity of online shopping brings more choices and conveniences to consumers, but it also exerts pressure on physical stores. Many consumers choose to pick styles and models in malls and purchase them online. The price advantage of online shopping currently becomes an insurmountable "gap" for some physical stores.

Mr. Chen, the owner of a brand specialty store at Holiday Plaza in the city center, told reporters: "There is no denying that online stores indeed increase sales for brand clothing manufacturers." Mr. Chen pointed out that while online stores help boost sales for producers, physical stores are facing unprecedented impacts. "Apart from selling inventory goods, it is difficult to find balance between online stores and physical stores. Therefore, many brand clothing online stores mainly sell out-of-season and discontinued products."

Even so, some manufacturers still believe that such distribution methods would affect physical stores. After all, online sales are basically equivalent to zero-cost or low-cost operations, while physical stores need to rent storefronts, renovate, hire employees, and pay taxes, spending money on every move.

In addition, consumers have become accustomed to various promotional activities conducted by malls on weekends and holidays. Some consumers even stated that except for first-tier brands, they feel that buying at original prices in malls is a loss. The idea of "not consuming unless there's a discount" has been recognized by some consumers.

Especially in Ya'an, where there are fewer large clothing malls, consumers prefer to take a bus to Chengdu for procurement rather than bother going back and forth. They'd rather just shop online.

In response to the current situation where online stores impact physical stores, some brand merchants have started taking action. They prohibit agents from selling new fashion items online, allowing them to sell only discontinued out-of-season items. Violators will be severely punished.

Moreover, some manufacturers simply prohibit agents from opening online stores to sell products, even prohibiting the sale of out-of-season and discontinued clothing online. These products can only be sold through physical store discounts. Even so, the convenience and popularity of opening online stores still allow some distributors to "profit significantly."

"Online shopping has indeed made us lose quite a few customers, especially for those busy with daily work who don't have time to shop around. Why not just sit in front of the computer and click the mouse to shop?" Miss Yu, who runs a Korean-style clothing store on Dazhong Road in the city center, said. Her clothing store used to make two or three thousand yuan a month, but in the past year, her monthly income has dropped below two thousand yuan. Miss Yu believes that as a new shopping method, she should also take advantage of online shopping. She plans to open an online store as well. Since she already has a physical store, opening an online store is more convenient, with ready-made resources for procurement and shipping.

Opening an Online Store is Simple and Practical

Selling one mobile phone every minute, a piece of jewelry every 30 seconds, and a T-shirt every 10 seconds... Accompanying the rise of online shopping, online stores have gradually evolved into a simple and fast third industry. Such an attractive market has prompted many people to decide to "dive" into online business, registering, uploading goods, and operating their own stores.

The reporter knows several friends who opened their own online stores this year, selling food, cosmetics, books, clothes, etc., covering all types of goods. According to Xiao Chen, who has two years of online store operation experience, online stores mainly come in two forms. One is to set up a dedicated website as a sales platform, handling everything from website maintenance, updates to publicity and sales. The advantage of such an online store is that the website design can reflect your personality to attract customers, and you can also set up a website forum to promptly receive feedback from buyers.

The other form is much simpler and more practical, using platforms provided by other websites to sell your goods. For example, on eBay or Taobao, you can save time designing a website, and the reputation of large websites helps increase clicks on your store, saving on publicity costs. Most ordinary online store sellers choose this method because it is simple and convenient. The steps are: register as a member on large portal sites like eBay, Paipai, or Taobao. Once you pass real-name verification, you can start operating. For instance, on Taobao, if you post more than ten items, you can open a free store. According to Taobao's relevant regulations, before selling, you must first understand the market conditions, take photos of your goods, upload the pictures, set reasonable prices, and provide detailed descriptions of each item. After the goods are listed, you should timely check the sales status of the items, answer buyer questions, and proactively contact buyers. Finally, confirm the transaction method. A deal is thus completed.

Hearing from friends who run online stores, the prices of online stores are generally divided into fixed prices and auction prices. Fixed prices allow buyers to purchase immediately at a set price, while auction prices allow buyers to bid for purchases. Besides offline procurement, you can also procure goods directly online. Some of these shopping websites are free, some require paying a certain monthly rental fee (i.e., virtual space rental fee), and others charge a commission fee after each successful transaction.

The simplicity and feasibility of opening an online store has allowed online stores to grow rapidly like bamboo shoots after rain. With more online stores, competition has increased, which also raises higher requirements for the quality of online goods and the service attitude of sellers. Fan Zhaoquan, the director of the Social Work Department at Sichuan Agricultural University, believes that online stores will cause a huge impact on the traditional clothing industry's sales model, "which cannot be avoided, but must be faced head-on. How to transform and adapt to market trends is what the traditional clothing industry needs to think about."

Management Measures

Are Being Issued Sequentially

On June 1st of this year, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce officially issued the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Trading and Relevant Service Behaviors," making clear provisions for the real-name authentication of individuals engaged in online transactions and putting forward a series of requirements for operators providing online trading platform services.

The issuance of the new measures elevates the previously "unspoken agreements" in transactions to explicit regulations, undoubtedly increasing the credibility of online shopping goods for consumers. In the past, if buyers believed the purchased goods did not match the online description, they could return the goods or seek after-sales service from the seller. But since there were no clear stipulations, the constraints were not strong enough.

Therefore, online shopping commodities always ranked on the annual consumer complaint list.

"Only when consumers' rights are protected will the credibility of online shopping improve, and more people will try online shopping." A staff member of the Consumer Association said that according to the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Trading and Relevant Service Behaviors," websites must establish inspection and monitoring systems for the goods information of online stores. If any violations are discovered, they must report to the local industry and commerce department, take measures to stop them, and may terminate the provision of online trading platform services. Thus, websites are obligated to "inspect and monitor," and once a transaction issue arises, corrective actions gain legal authority. This person reminded consumers that items on the internet vary greatly in quality, and counterfeit branded goods cannot be ruled out. Online shoppers should carefully choose when purchasing.

However, regarding the rapid development of online shopping currently, this person believes that the impact varies across different industries. "Clothing, books, and daily necessities have lower quality requirements, so citizens are more willing to shop online; whereas for more expensive items, citizens still trust seeing is believing, and the services and after-sales guarantees are better ensured."

Mr. Li, who runs an automobile decoration store on Shawan Road in the city center, sells his items online to expand sales. The online prices are cheaper than in physical stores, but physical stores offer free installation services that online shopping cannot provide.

With the continuous segmentation of the market, it is believed that more diversified marketing models will emerge.
