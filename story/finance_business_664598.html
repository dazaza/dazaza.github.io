Chongqing's Investigation on the Chaos in the Designated Driving Market: Urgent Need for Policy Regulation in Industry Governance

by gangguo77sz on 2012-02-13 16:49:58

No entry threshold for the "illegal chauffeur" to run rampant. Chongqing Cheng Automobile Driving Service Co., Ltd. is the first driving company registered in the industrial and commercial department in Chongqing. General Manager Gao Tianjun introduced that the regular chauffeur service process is generally as follows: guests make reservations with the chauffeur company, set the location and time; after meeting, guests need to fill out a "Driving Service Agreement", confirm the car condition, mileage, car insurance, etc., and clarify relevant responsibilities; after reaching the destination, according to the mileage, charge the corresponding chauffeur fee according to the company's specified charging standard. Mr. Li who lives near Longtousi in Chongqing has suffered from "illegal chauffeur". Before the Spring Festival, after gathering with several friends, he found a "chauffeur" outside the restaurant. Since he was drunk at the time, he fell asleep on the car. When he woke up, he found himself still in the car, but the car body had been scratched. He then contacted the chauffeur, but the other party's phone could not be connected. "If the unqualified and uncontracted illegal chauffeurs have a car accident or steal your property, once they run away, where can you find them?" said Zuo Cheng, chauffeur of Chongqing Cheng Automobile Driving Service Co., Ltd. No competent authority, no uniform standard. "Our company has been established for almost a year, and we still don't know who the competent authority is." said Gao Tianjun. When the company was just established, they consulted many departments such as the city transport administration bureau, the city industry and commerce bureau, and traffic police patrol, and the reply was that the chauffeur industry is not under their jurisdiction. Industry insiders pointed out that due to the lack of competent authorities, the chauffeur industry does not even have a formal status, and a "unified industry standard" is like a castle in the air. In fact, there are indeed chaotic situations in various aspects such as charging and services among different chauffeur companies, without a unified standard. Moreover, in terms of service standards, some companies require chauffeurs to wear uniforms and standardize the service process, while others do not have any requirements; in terms of personnel requirements, some companies require chauffeurs to be local residents of Chongqing, familiar with urban routes, and have more than 5 years of driving experience, while others only require more than 1 year of driving experience and have no other requirements. In addition, the distribution ratio between the company and the chauffeur is also different, ranging from "three seven split" to "five five split". The industry needs urgent policy regulation. In November 2011, led by Chongqing Cheng Automobile Driving Service Co., Ltd., five chauffeur companies registered in the industry and commerce department spontaneously formed the "Chongqing Chauffeur Alliance". Chairman Gao Tianjun of the chauffeur alliance said that the establishment of the alliance is to standardize the market and integrate resources. The chauffeur price of the alliance companies is jointly formulated through sufficient market research. If customers encounter random charges or theft of property, they can also complain to the alliance, which will properly resolve the issues. At the same time, they are preparing to establish the "Chongqing Chauffeur Association". "But we still hope that the chauffeur industry has a competent authority, so that the entire industry has a unified entry threshold, charging standard, etc., and can effectively crack down on illegal chauffeurs," he said. Professor Wang Anbai of Southwest University of Political Science and Law believes that currently, whether it is the qualification of driving personnel in the chauffeur market in Chongqing or even nationwide, or the charging standard and operation process, all present a chaotic situation, seriously hindering the "chauffeur" from becoming a mature industry; at the same time, without policy basis, the qualification of driving personnel, charging standard, and service process can only be self-formulated and supervised by each chauffeur company, and the quality of the companies themselves is uneven, greatly increasing the risk of chauffeur services. Many industry insiders mentioned that currently, South Korea has more than ten thousand regular chauffeur companies, and its chauffeur business is operated by the South Korean Chauffeur Association, which is supervised by the Social Committee of the South Korean National Assembly. Eight years ago, South Korea's chauffeur market was also very chaotic. Later, an "illegal chauffeur" driver caused a serious car accident, resulting in vehicle destruction and loss of life. The driver escaped the scene after surviving, and the relevant departments were unable to investigate. From then on, South Korea began to regulate the chauffeur market. Wang Anbai appealed that relevant departments should soon introduce relevant policies to regulate the chauffeur industry. For example, how much registered capital the chauffeur company should have, how many driving personnel should be employed, how many years of driving experience the driving personnel should have, what clauses the "Driving Service Agreement" should include, and which department is responsible for supervising the chauffeur company, etc., should all be clearly defined in the relevant policies.
