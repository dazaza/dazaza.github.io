Editor | Delete "Happy Feet 2": Penguin sound is no less than dolphin sound

by dy5200 on 2012-02-25 07:05:37

If the human sea dance makes you sleepy, then the tap dance of the penguin sea will surely make you cheer. The film begins with a grand scene of penguin dancing, accompanied by penguin sounds throughout. Classic songs like "Kingdom of Rhythm", "Tonight the Stars Shine Brightly", and "We Are the Champions" are effortlessly performed by the penguins. If there's trouble, just sing a song; if it's overcome, then dance. This grand dance needs no stage design, as the white snow already highlights the graceful movements of the penguins.

Of course, in the animal world, there are also unconventional penguins and krill. This indirectly tells children not to be someone who follows the crowd. Although children may not understand the deeper meaning of the food chain theory of the two krill, Will and Bill, when millions of krill form a swarm to find the next larger swarm, Will and Bill can make their own judgments. Even though their wishes might sound primitive—climbing to the top of the food chain to eat meat—they refuse to be bound by their place in the food chain, proving Darwin's conclusion of survival of the fittest.

In this icy landscape, there is indeed its own chain. Krill are at the bottom; they are herbivores and planktonic organisms, often unable to control their own future. Penguins are numerous, feeding on fish and shrimp from the sea. Seagulls eye trapped penguins hungrily but may not necessarily win if they attack. Walruses are large in size, but even they need penguins' help when stuck in the ice. Humans have an awkward position; they seem like invaders, yet retreat in disarray. In the face of harsh snowy conditions, they are more fragile than penguins and do not truly belong here.

The animation also endows animals with warmth. Penguins also know how to reason and touch emotionally. The father-son relationship between Eric and Mumble sees Eric grow from a naturally timid penguin into one who dares to be a spokesperson before walruses, while Mumble constantly teaches his son through words and actions. The friendship between puffins and penguins shows that penguins initially thought puffins were their kindred spirits. Because puffins could fly, they naturally became idols of the penguins. Despite not being the same species, the puffin gained great recognition. Even when penguins learned the truth about the puffins' inability to fly and realized they weren't the same kind, they didn’t abandon the puffin. The relationship between walruses and penguins is one of showing true friendship in adversity. The relationship between krill Will and Bill is somewhat unclear—"passion"? Let’s call it comradery for now, as they share the same ambition: moving in the opposite direction of the swarm.

Creatures in the icy wilderness can all find themselves, whereas humans are rather awkward. When rescuing penguins, they give up due to weather reasons. Their panic may not be expressed through words or actions, but the turbulence of that krill reflects the unease of humans themselves. They think they are omnipotent, strumming electric guitars to accompany penguins' dances. They take pride in their musical talent, and the collapse of icebergs and tectonic drifts are also their "masterpieces." Most of the time, however, they turn a blind eye and deaf ear. When facing snowstorms, let alone saving the seemingly insignificant penguins in their eyes, self-preservation becomes difficult.
