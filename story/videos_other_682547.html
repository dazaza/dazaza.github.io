On the evening of February 28th, Beijing time, in the first round of the 2012 Xingpai Cup Snooker Haikou World Open, Jin Long gave an excellent performance and surprisingly defeated Ding Junhui, who had just won the Welsh Open, with a score of 5-1. This was Jin Long's first time advancing to the round of 16 in a world professional tournament. It was also the first time in seven years that Jin Long had defeated Ding Junhui in a major competition.

Jin Long, who is six years older than Ding Junhui, hails from Jinzhou, Liaoning Province. He has won the Asian Championship twice, which allowed him to enter the World Professional Circuit twice. He is one of China's earliest snooker players based in the UK. During his time in professional competitions, he made it to the last round of several ranking events' qualifiers, including the 2008 London Watch Grand Prix and the 2009 Chinese Open. His highest world ranking was within the top 64. However, after the end of the 2009-2010 season, he fell out of the professional circuit due to insufficient points.

Jin Long is a very talented player, but unfortunately, his best period coincided with the rise of Ding Junhui, who became a long-term nightmare for him. Jin Long's last victory over Ding Junhui was seven years ago, in the final of the June 2005 National Snooker Tournament in Jinan, where he defeated Ding Junhui 6-4 shortly after Ding had won the Chinese Open. That victory became Jin Long's only unbeaten record against Ding Junhui in the following seven years. In those years, they met multiple times in the Chinese Touring Series, including two finals, but Jin Long never managed to win against Ding Junhui. As a result, for several years, Ding Junhui ranked first in the Chinese Touring Series, while Jin Long remained second, unable to surpass him.

However, since falling out of the professional circuit two years ago, Jin Long has devoted more energy to domestic competitions and won again at the Chengdu Open last year. This was his first national championship in six years, which restored much of his confidence.

Due to his outstanding performance in the Chinese Touring Series, Jin Long received a wildcard to continue participating in world professional tournaments held in China. In the 2010 Shanghai Masters wildcard round, he defeated Milkins 5-3, setting up a meeting with Ding Junhui in the first round. However, that Chinese derby match was undoubtedly very frustrating for Jin Long. He started well, leading 4-1 and holding match points, but as victory seemed within reach, Ding Junhui adjusted and ultimately won four consecutive frames to reverse the situation with a 5-4 victory. Ding Junhui not only maintained his high win rate against Jin Long but also shattered Jin Long's dream of making it to the round of 16 in a professional tournament for the first time.

Whether by coincidence or fate, at this Haikou World Open, Jin Long once again faced Ding Junhui. From the wildcard round, Jin Long was well-prepared, defeating Baird 5-3, who had won four consecutive matches in the qualifying rounds. However, given that Ding Junhui had just won the Welsh Open, Jin Long was not favored to win this derby match.

But the match began dramatically, with Jin Long consecutively scoring over 60 points in the first two frames. Although he didn't finish off either frame in one go, Ding Junhui's attempts to catch up were halted when he missed on the final black ball. Jin Long eventually clinched these two frames by sinking the final black balls, indicating that luck was clearly on Jin Long's side.

In the end, after taking a 4-1 lead, Jin Long did not allow Ding Junhui to repeat the dramatic reversal from the 2010 Shanghai Masters first round. He triumphed 5-1, defeating Ding Junhui for the first time in seven years and historically reaching the round of 16 in a professional tournament. Through this victory, Jin Long achieved a long-awaited breakthrough.
