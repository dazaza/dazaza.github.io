Drivers on the expressway borrow lanes to evade tolls before the opening. The Transportation Bureau expressed that it will strictly investigate this issue.

by bi1r8063ha on 2012-02-16 11:11:37

On October 15, Mr. Chen, a resident of Shangrao, reported to our newspaper that a highway is currently under construction between Shangrao and Wuyi Mountain (hereinafter referred to as the "Shang-Wu Expressway"). During this period, many drivers have taken advantage of the lack of supervision to evade toll payments by using the unopened YONGPING lead mountain entrance and exit of the Shang-Wu Expressway. Additionally, some villagers have been drying rice on the highway, posing a serious danger. However, relevant departments have failed to provide adequate oversight.

Vehicles evading tolls

At around 9:00 AM on October 15, I accompanied Mr. Chen who was reporting the situation, entering the Shangrao West Station of the Liwen Expressway, collecting an IC card for highway tolls and driving towards the Nanchang direction.

When the car reached the intersection of the Shang-Wu Expressway and the Liwen Expressway, I noticed that the highway supervisory department had not set up barriers for vehicles heading to the Shang-Wu Expressway at this point. At the unopened YONGPING lead mountain entrance and exit of the Shang-Wu Expressway, when the car was preparing to leave the highway, I saw a staff member stationed at the exit, moving the barrier to allow passage.

When I asked the staff member about the IC card for toll collection and the amount of the vehicle toll, the staff member only gestured for the driver to throw the card on the ground.

A Fujian-born man leading a construction team on the Shang-Wu Expressway told me that after getting on the expressway from the Shangrao West Station of the Liwen Expressway and driving to the construction site, he collects a card every day without paying the fee and does not need to return the IC card. He also mentioned, "Since the start of construction, hundreds of IC cards have already been lost. Moreover, many long-distance transport trucks and cars frequently use the YONGPING lead mountain entrance and exit of the Shang-Wu Expressway to evade fees."

Villagers drying rice on the highway

At 3:40 PM on October 15, I accompanied the complaining driver to the intersection of the Shang-Wu Expressway and the Liwen Expressway, where there was an expanded section of the highway. I met a villager surnamed Zheng from Yangjiaban Village, Liansheng Village, Dongtuan Township, Shangrao County, who was drying rice on the highway. The two daughters of Mr. Zheng, both around ten years old, told me that their father had just returned home with dried rice down the highway when his electric tricycle carrying the rice flipped over, causing him to be injured all over his body. His family had already sent him to the hospital for treatment.

One of the little girls told me that her family had asked her to watch over the dried rice. When I left, I repeatedly warned the two sisters not to walk on the highway and to go home early.

Shang-Wu Expressway Construction Management Office◎ Already assigned personnel for supervision

In the morning of October 16, a responsible person surnamed Liu from the Safety Labor Department of the Shangrao to Wuyi Mountain Expressway Construction Management Office informed me that the office dispatches staff members daily to patrol the construction site and also assigns local police officers to guard the unopened toll stations.

When I questioned why villagers were drying rice on the highway and non-construction vehicles were passing through, Mr. Liu claimed that recently they had discovered a large number of non-construction vehicles and personnel entering the Shang-Wu Expressway construction site. Since July 16, the management office has collaborated with the Qianshan County Government, the Shangrao Economic Development Zone, and the towns along the route to implement traffic control and has distributed a notice titled "Announcement of Closed Construction for the Shang-Wu Expressway" to the local villagers. He immediately showed me a document numbered [2011]1 from the Shang-Wu Jian An Labor Office.

Shangrao Transportation Bureau◎ Will assign personnel to strictly investigate this matter

In the morning of October 16, Hu Ming, the head of the Revenue Supervision Department of the Yangmeiling Management Office of the Liwen Expressway Company, told me that since the roadbed of the Shang-Wu Expressway was completed and opened to traffic, the computer records of vehicle passage at the Liwen Expressway Shangrao West Station have shown that many vehicles have only records of entering the highway but no records of exiting and paying tolls. Furthermore, there are significant losses in the number of vehicle passage IC cards. To find out the reason, on October 8, he conducted a field investigation. On that day, he randomly checked 200 local vehicles entering the highway from the Liwen Expressway Shangrao West Station and found that 40 to 60 vehicles had records of entering the highway but no records of exiting and paying tolls.

Hu Ming also informed me that afterward, the management office coordinated multiple times with the Shang-Wu Expressway Construction Management Office. Since the management office lacks enforcement authority, it can only collaborate with strict inspections to prevent the loss of state assets.

Finally, Hu Ming stated that he has already reported this matter to higher authorities, and this situation will soon be brought under control.

Subsequently, I also contacted Wu Minghan, Director of the Shangrao Transportation Bureau and Director of the Shangrao to Wuyi Mountain Expressway Construction Management Office via telephone. Wu Minghan informed me in the phone call that he would immediately organize personnel to strictly investigate this matter and hold the responsible parties accountable.
