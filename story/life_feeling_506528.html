Behavioral art originates from the West and is a peculiar phenomenon in Western society. In essence, it can be defined as a kind of free life activity, which is an art form that artists use thinking and behavior process to create. Some behavioral arts indeed enlighten people's intelligence, enrich people's imagination world and cognitive field, and have been passed down for a long time. Since the 1980s, performance art has become increasingly popular in China. Some creators often use extreme behaviors such as nudity, self-abuse, injury, and blood as the main means of expression. This mal-digested import often collides with the aesthetic view, moral view, and social customs of Chinese people, and becomes the focus of people's debate. In Xi'an, some people also carry out various creations under the banner of performance art. The other day, the reporter walked into this circle and tried to show their inner secrets to readers.

In mid-September, a photo titled "Why do post-90s play bloody human suspension" appeared on Weibo. Once released, it quickly received a large number of netizens' forwarding and heated discussions. Some netizens guessed whether the protagonist of this bloody performance was a post-90s girl who mutilated herself for fame or purely performance art? The reporter originally wanted to contact the post-90s girl in the photo, but because this post was a forwarded post, it was difficult to trace back.

Subsequently, after the reporter entered "post-90s girl human suspension" on Baidu, he finally found the original post that caused heated discussions among netizens on Weibo. It turns out that this photo was taken at the end of April. The two hangers in the photo were both post-90s girls from Chongqing. They and several friends agreed that this human suspension was indeed performance art rather than self-mutilation for eyeballs.

According to the description of the original post, the theme of this human suspension art performance is the meaning of the existence of the soul. On that day, except for the two girls participating in the suspension, only a few people in the circle who like performance art participated. Before the performance, the suspenders all underwent strict skin disinfection treatment and tetanus injection. The whole suspension process lasted nearly an hour. From the expressions of the suspender, it was easy and natural, without any sign of pain.

The reporter found through online search that the main venue of human suspension disappeared in 2008. Such performances, which are bloody and cruel in the eyes of ordinary people, the participants said it was a kind of performance art. Scholars pointed out: if this is also art, then swallowing glass is also art, it is at most challenging the physiological limit of human beings. Regardless of whether it is art, the real intention and psychological activities of the participants themselves are worth exploring.

The brutal competition of suspension makes art lose its authenticity

Interview with Art Creator Wang Shuiyi

In October 2007, several young people carried out the last human suspension in Northwest China near Han Yao in Xi'an. The most eye-catching thing about this suspension was the so-called knee suspension gimmick as the first in China. On October 2, the reporter contacted Wang Shuiyi, the creator of the knee suspension performance, and interviewed him.

Wang Shuiyi, an 80s-born man from Xianyang, left a deep impression with his medium-length hair, a little long beard on his chin, and a totem tattoo on his neck. He even expanded the hole in his right ear. I think everyone is performance art, just talking about this concept is too big. This is his explanation of performance art.

Everyone can be performance art

Sanqin Evening News: What do you think is performance art? When did you start to contact this kind of art?

Wang Shuiyi: This question is too big! Performance art is to express the self-emotion of the creator through body language. Performance art is an abstract expressionism, it is the mutation and extension of action painting and informal art. In this sense, everyone can be performance art. For example, a person drinking tea, as long as the background of him drinking tea is simply artistically processed, then it also counts as performance art.

In fact, the human suspension, tattoos, piercing, wearing, and nail embedding we usually see all belong to BME (BodyEzine human modification). Personally, I think BME is an art creation with strong individual color rather than performance art.

I was born in an ordinary working-class family. Because my father liked to paint, I grew up liking it. My earliest contact with BME was in 2002, when I saw an introduction to tattoos, piercings, and various BMEs on a foreign website, and I became obsessed with it ever since.

Performance art itself is a conceptual art

Sanqin Evening News: What is the origin of human suspension and how is the development of BME now? In the eyes of the public, most performance arts are bloody and self-abusive, and it is difficult to accept now. What do you think about it?

Wang Shuiyi: The earliest human suspension in human history should have appeared in the slave society. At that time, the slave owners punished the runaway slaves by hanging them up with hooks on their collarbones or pectoral bones, also known as hanging punishment. For thousands of years after that, suspension has always existed as a cruel punishment in human society. In the late Qing Dynasty and the early Republic of China, in Hainan and Fujian, human suspension became a performance of folk strange skills, similar to the Tianqiao style in old Beijing.

Human suspension officially became popular in China in 2005. At that time, the human suspension in Beijing, Guizhou, Yunnan, and Northeast China was the most outstanding. Each suspension generally determined the theme by the hanger, and the main purpose was still to vent personal emotions. Until 2007, human suspension gradually faded out of people's sight. Because later the development of human suspension gradually changed flavor, the creators competed to see who hung harder and who hung more strangely, thus causing the human suspension art performance to lose its authenticity and greatly damaging the environment. BME has many branches, and the most popular in the circle now are nail embedding and subcutaneous needle placement, while expansion and tattooing are more common. There are no less than 2 million technicians in China who can perform expansion and tattooing, but the technical quality varies greatly. There are less than 200 masters with professional standards and regular operations, and those who are good at nail embedding and subcutaneous needle placement are rare.

The emergence of any matter to be accepted by the masses needs a long process. Performance art itself is a kind of conceptual art. Some people like it and some people reject it, just like painting, everyone can draw casually, but there are very few people who can really understand the masterpieces. Of course, some insiders choose radical forms of expression due to certain emotional releases, I don't agree with it, but it cannot be one-sidedly concluded that performance art is bloody and self-abusive.

Most citizens are repulsed by performance art and traditional art rejects performance art

Xi'an performance art investigation

In today's era of pluralistic culture, as a form of art, performance art enters our lives beyond reproach. The key is not to violate artistic principles, not to exceed moral boundaries, not to distort the understanding of normal life, and not to make performance art further away from civilization, making it hard for us to bear sensory and spiritually.

Obviously, some so-called performance arts at present are actually a hype by performance artists. In order to prove that their actions are art, the performers often disguise themselves with profound theories, claiming that their actions are art, and others can't understand because they don't understand art.

Mentioning the special art exhibition held in Shuyuanmen, Xi'an in November 2007 - the third Sino-Japanese Performance Art Exchange Plan Xi'an Exhibition, I believe many people still remember it vividly. That time, 13 foreign performance artists from Japan, South Korea, the Philippines, Indonesia, etc., and 12 local performance artists in Xi'an gathered in Shuyuanmen to let the audience appreciate a series of exquisite performance art works.

At that time, an Indonesian young man swimming naked in Shuyuanmen angered the old scholars who were writing calligraphy on the street next to Shuyuanmen. An old scholar wrote the three big words "mental illness" on his back with a brush.

Another old man beside looked disdainful. This Indonesian young man then lay on the writing desk of this old man. The old man wrote six words "traveling in Xi'an, traveling in the ancient city" on his body, and then threw away the brush in his hand. Shuyuanmen hides many talented people who can write well. Is this called art? It's simply ruining art. Performance art was rejected by traditional art here.

What attitude do ordinary people have towards performance art? On October 9, the reporter randomly interviewed several citizens.

Wang Xiaolin, 27 years old, dance teacher. She said, "I don't know much about performance art, but I really like Su Zizi's works. It is a kind of stubborn and somewhat radical ideology of modern girls, but she is a promising stock. She dares to use this shocking way to express her thoughts and art, and she dares to look straight at the lens, free and calm. When her ideas reach a higher level, learn more things, and express her thoughts better, she herself will become a beautiful medium for spreading art."

Zhang Qi, 49 years old, civil servant. Regarding the complex performance art performances nowadays, he thinks: "I don't know what happened to the young people now. There are so many forms of artistic expression, why must they choose such bloody piercing and suspension? It looks too terrifying. As the saying goes, the body and skin are given by parents. This extreme performance under the guise of art, I don't support it!"

During the interview, the reporter found that eighty percent of the citizens expressed rejection and opposition to human suspension and naked swimming performances. Some citizens also said that the general public still misunderstands performance art and should take a tolerant attitude.
