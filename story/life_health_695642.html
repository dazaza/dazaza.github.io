The relationship between reproductive infection and sexual life

The spread of reproductive infectious diseases is closely related to an individual's lifestyle. Therefore, to effectively prevent reproductive infections, one needs to establish a correct living concept, starting from daily life, and effectively control possible disease transmission routes. However, due to some people lacking basic knowledge about reproductive infections, they mistakenly believe that they can prevent reproductive infections by using drugs for treating such infections, thus often engaging in unclean sexual behaviors after using these drugs. In fact, this practice is very dangerous because drugs for treating reproductive infections cannot serve as preventive measures. Moreover, males with frequent sexual activity should learn more about the basic knowledge, prevention methods, and treatment approaches for various reproductive infections, which will help them better maintain their own health.

Doubtful and Excessive Fear

Many people exhibit certain characteristics during the diagnosis of reproductive infections. For example, poor psychological endurance, easy tension, and often attributing normal physiological reactions or slight discomfort to unclean sexual behavior or past reproductive infections. Even after examination and testing have confirmed their health, they still remain skeptical and hope for medical treatment. The fear of reproductive infections has made them "fear everything," frequently confirming their health through repeated tests but still not feeling reassured. In fact, different reproductive infections have different sexual transmission probabilities, and not every unclean sexual behavior will lead to reproductive infection. Scientific research shows that in a single sexual contact, the transmission probability of gonorrhea is approximately 50%, chlamydia infection around 20%, soft chancre infection about 80%, and HIV only 0.1%. Due to the different characteristics of male and female urinary reproductive tracts, there are also differences in the transmission probabilities of reproductive infections between genders. Taking gonorrhea as an example, the probability of a male patient infecting a healthy female is 60%, while the probability of a female patient infecting a healthy male is 30%. Therefore, the transmission probability of reproductive infections through sexual behavior cannot be generalized.

Misleading Information Delays Treatment

Insufficient understanding of reproductive infections can easily lead many patients to choose incorrect treatment methods. The most common misconceptions mainly include the following two:

Some patients opt for convenience or fear privacy exposure, thus choosing doctors without professional licenses. Due to the lack of knowledge and professional equipment, these doctors often use medications indiscriminately without even determining which type of reproductive infection the patient has, completely disregarding the serious consequences that may result. This "blind" treatment not only fails to alleviate the condition but may also cause severe complications and sequelae. For instance, early symptoms of syphilis can be fully cured with professional hospital treatment; however, if antibiotics are used arbitrarily, the disease might turn into latent and hidden conditions, accompanied by the possibility of transmission and recurrence. Thus, irregular treatment can lead to the hidden development of the condition, easily prolonging the disease course to the late stage and losing the opportunity for complete cure.

Moreover, some patients easily believe in folk remedies, ultimately suffering losses. Folk remedies may temporarily improve the condition in some periods, leading patients to believe they are already cured, which can unintentionally cause the spread of reproductive infections. Due to the lack of professional guidance, many patients who self-treat do not disinfect and isolate items like foot basins, towels, and toilet utensils, thereby affecting family members.

These situations indicate that non-standard treatments not only harm personal health but can also lead to further spread of reproductive infections within families and society, with very serious consequences. Therefore, patients with reproductive infections should not blindly treat themselves but should quickly go to regular hospitals for systematic examinations, actively receive treatment after confirmation, and regularly follow up. Reproductive infections are not scary; as long as the doctor's advice is followed and confidence is established, the majority of reproductive infections can be cured.
