Focus on the tone of next year's economic policy _ Stock Introduction

by ww0982993 on 2009-12-05 03:35:03

I. Policy Orientation May Be More Flexible

The current situation is that the domestic economy has stabilized through investment-driven growth, but the internal driving force and structural issues of the economy remain less than optimistic. From this perspective, the overall orientation of economic policy must necessarily be adjusted appropriately according to time and place. However, given the management's traditionally steady style, to ensure a smooth transition from economic recovery to structural transformation, the adjustment of policies is likely to be gradual and leave room for maneuver.

On the other hand, due to the current macroeconomic fundamentals still facing many uncertainties, policy adjustments could be difficult to make. A flexible stance would help reserve more space for governance next year.

Therefore, we still hold the view that tougher wording on preventing asset price bubbles may not appear at this meeting. The general tone of the meeting should be mild yet elastic.

II. Shift in Work Priorities

Although this is the case, among the several main goals of the policy, the positions and importance of maintaining growth, adjusting structure, and preventing inflation may change.

Adjusting the structure may be elevated to the top priority, reserving policy space for preventing inflation, maintaining reasonable investment growth (but not rapid growth), and temporarily no longer overly focusing on maintaining growth to gradually de-emphasize the goal of maintaining growth, which should be a relatively rational choice by the management.

At the same time, this meeting may pay more attention to how to stimulate consumption, but it is unlikely to have specific measures. This is mainly because the nature of the Central Economic Work Conference has always been mainly about setting the tone, and it is unlikely to involve too substantive measures. Moreover, it is currently very difficult to propose specific plans. However, Premier Wen Jiabao's recent remarks during his inspection in Shanghai, "to curb speculative home purchases and promote the healthy development of the real estate industry," are clearly different from the previous attitude of prioritizing everything for maintaining growth, and actually hint at the possible direction of future policies.

Although the shift in policy priorities is gradual, we should still pay attention to the early manifestation of expectations.

III. Mid-term Market Likely to Remain Volatile

Since this Central Economic Work Conference may not provide a relatively clear directional tone, this also determines that the market expectations influenced by it will be vague. On one hand, the expectation of economic recovery has already been largely exhausted, and the prospects of medium- to long-term economic growth are actually unclear; but on the other hand, after the Dubai incident, the management began to consider whether an early exit would lead to a second recession in the market, thus the willingness to suppress asset prices and tighten credit has significantly weakened, and the market liquidity remains loose.

In this situation, we emphasize that it is difficult for a one-sided trend to appear, and the market will mostly wait for clarity in expectations in a volatile form. In this process, mainstream institutions in the market will continuously explore relevant structural theme sectors for speculation, which is worth our attention. For stock beginners.

Therefore, from a strategic point of view, we still emphasize being light on indices and heavy on individual stocks, deeply analyzing the basis of the future landscape to discover corresponding themes, stock.
