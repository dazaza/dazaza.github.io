According to higher-level arrangements, the back pay for January to August was distributed according to the old method, i.e., based on the "Lecong Middle School Teacher Performance Evaluation Plan." The school's finance personnel prepared the tables according to this plan on September 20th for the teachers to sign, preparing for distribution. However, at 8:08 PM on September 21st, the school received the forwarded "Supplementary Opinions on the 2011 Teacher Salary Adjustment Work" from the town education bureau. According to Article 3, Point 1 of the "Supplementary Opinions": "The portion exceeding 1.0 proportion for school administrative personnel, i.e., the additional performance wage portion (principal 0.4 times, vice-principal 0.2 times, director 0.13 times, deputy director 0.1 times) is not included in the total school performance wage, and is separately arranged by the fiscal authorities, not participating in the school's distribution."

Lecong Education Bureau reported to Southern Metropolis Daily yesterday that during the back pay distribution for teachers from January to August 2011, Lecong Middle School strictly followed the spirit of the higher-level documents.

Zeng Shenghua said yesterday that the distribution plan formulated on September 20th was based on the school's previous scoring system. "At 8:08 PM on September 21st, I received the notification, and according to the document, I should receive '1' point worth of the average amount, with the rest distributed separately. I immediately notified the finance personnel to redo the tables, and corrections were made the next day." Zeng Shenghua said that his OA system still had records of when he received the documents, so he could accurately state the time of receipt as 8:08 PM on the 21st.
