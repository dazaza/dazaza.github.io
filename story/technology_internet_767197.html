Court rules Amazon wins CIA cloud services order

by anonymous on 2013-10-10 18:37:39

Thomas Wheeler, a judge at the US Federal Court of Claims, ruled today that Amazon Web Services (AWS) legitimately and fairly won the order from the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA), dismissing IBM's accusations as invalid.

Cloud computing is a business that Amazon has been focusing on in recent years. Winning the CIA order means that its cloud computing services have developed significantly, gaining recognition for security and reliability, enough to rival industry giant IBM.

In a bidding process earlier this year, Amazon Web Services beat industry giant Amazon to win a $600 million cloud equipment order from the CIA. IBM had doubts about this because a report issued by the US Government Accountability Office (GAO) showed that Amazon Web Services did not meet all the conditions required for the bid. IBM subsequently filed a protest, causing this deal to be shelved.

Subsequently, Amazon Web Services appealed to the US Federal Court of Claims, hoping to restart the previously suspended contract. The court ruled today that Amazon Web Services had won the order through legitimate means.

IBM expressed disappointment with the result and plans to continue appealing. In a statement, IBM said that its offer had a higher cost-performance ratio and that the company already has decades of cooperation experience with the US government.

This failure is a significant blow to IBM. The company has invested considerable research efforts into cloud equipment, and it is simply not on the same level as Amazon Web Services as a competitor.
