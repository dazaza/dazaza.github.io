Beijing's commercial housing inventory stops falling and rebounds, while the price of luxury houses remains firm.

by dbfgskkgfen6 on 2012-02-14 09:25:50

Beijing's commercial housing inventory reverses decline and begins to rise, luxury property prices remain strong - Market Observation - Industry News - Building Materials Network. According to data from Beijing Real Estate Transaction Management Network, in the first half of May, 2,127 pre-sale residential units were sold in Beijing, a reduction of 58.3% compared to the first half of April; only 284 existing residential units were sold, a decrease of 68.8% compared to the first half of April. Affected by the slowing sales volume, Beijing's commercial housing inventory has started to reverse its decline and begin to rise. Currently, Beijing's available pre-sale housing stock is maintained at around 88,500 units.

Industry insiders pointed out that last year, the land supply involving residential properties in Beijing reached 12 million square meters, and this part of the land will gradually be converted into housing supply after the second half of this year. At that time, the supply-demand tension in the commercial housing market will significantly ease.

Both volume and price of second-hand houses have fallen. According to statistics from the Beijing Zhongyuan Tertiary Market Research Department, in the first half of May, the online signing transaction volume of second-hand residential units in Beijing was 7,430 units, a drop of 51.6% compared to the same period in April. Excluding economic适用housing, the transaction volume of second-hand commercial housing in the first half of May was only 2,930 units, a fall of more than 81.7% compared to the transaction volume in the first half of April.

In terms of price, since the implementation of Beijing's new policy, the transaction price of second-hand commercial housing in Beijing was 18,635 yuan per square meter, a drop of 6.2% compared to the historical high point in the first half of April.

The listing average price of second-hand houses has also been adjusted down from 22,580 yuan per square meter in the first half of April to 20,885 yuan per square meter, with a fall of 7.6%. In Tongzhou area where prices rose too quickly earlier, the second-hand house asking prices have fallen by more than 15%. Industry insiders expect that currently, Beijing house prices have stopped rising, and within three months, 50% of speculators will gradually sell off their properties, and the overall house prices may likely adjust downwards by 20%-30% within this year.

Data shows that in the first half of May, the new demand for Beijing second-hand houses fell by 89%, while the supply of housing remained relatively high, increasing by more than 20% compared to the first half of April.

Currently, due to the increase in housing supply, Beijing's second-hand housing market has begun to shift towards a buyer's market, with the number of new listings exceeding the number of new customers. Data shows that at the beginning of April, the supply-to-demand ratio for second-hand houses was 1:4.5, it was 1:2.3 in the second half of April, and currently, this ratio is 1.12:1.

As for new properties, except for ordinary residential projects in suburban areas, the prices of most projects, especially luxury projects, remain strong. Monitoring data shows that the average price per square meter of Yuan Dajiu No.7, which opened in the first half of May, increased from 28,500 yuan to 31,000 yuan.

Promotions in the new property market are still individual actions by some developers, while more developers are observing and delaying their openings. Data shows that since May, the average transaction price of new properties has slightly dropped by 3.3% compared to the first half of April, mainly due to the concentrated transactions of low-priced units in some far suburban areas.

The transaction volume of Beijing villa projects has declined but prices have remained stable. According to SouFun's statistics, in the second week of May, Beijing added a supply of 208 villas, covering an area of 51,869.5 square meters. A total of 27 villas were sold, with a transaction area of 10,897.14 square meters, a drop of 10% compared to the previous period. The average transaction price was 21,340 yuan per square meter, with little change compared to the previous period.

Industry insiders pointed out that villa and other luxury projects generally have special positioning, with distinct circle marketing characteristics, and the sensitivity of sales volume to price changes is not strong. Taking Su Garden under Wanda Real Estate as an example, the project is located on Longshan Mountain in Huairou District of Beijing, covering a total area of 103,000 square meters, including a sample area of 24,000 square meters and a large western area of 79,000 square meters, with a building density of 0.24. It plans to construct 119 Suzhou-style detached villas. Since its opening, the sales pace has remained good, and prices have steadily risen. Relevant personnel from Wanda Real Estate stated that due to the limited remaining inventory and all being customer-customized designs, they would not consider discount promotions or similar promotional methods.
