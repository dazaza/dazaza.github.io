The controversial factors that cause differences in the IT industries between China and India

by hua71981e on 2010-03-30 15:28:51

Recently, a post by a Chinese national who has lived in India for five years has once again sparked heated discussions among Chinese netizens about "Chinese innovation." This user posted on Maopu questioning the weakness of Chinese innovation and claimed that "Chinese IT 'elites' all deserve to be criticized!" This immediately triggered online condemnation. So, which country has stronger creativity, China or India?

It is worth noting that the more independent and individualistic post-80s and post-90s generations have shown an unexpectedly high level of interest in the topic of "Chinese innovation." In a short period of time, this post has garnered over a million clicks and more than 6,000 replies.

In fact, if we look at the representative of the IT field - internet applications, there are at least ten controversial factors that cause differences between China's and India's IT industries.

1. Whether students are divided into liberal arts and sciences. Some people say that due to the division of liberal arts and sciences in China's education system, those studying liberal arts have weaker logical thinking, while those studying sciences have less imagination. Since those engaged in IT are mostly science students with insufficient imagination, their creativity is also limited. However, the issue is that India also divides students into liberal arts and sciences, and even commerce. Science is the hardest, and there have been calls to reduce the proportion and difficulty of mathematics. Therefore, this is not the main factor. Moreover, China may abolish the division of liberal arts and sciences within the next decade, and it remains to be seen what the situation will be then.

2. Whether it is exam-oriented education. Many believe that exam-oriented education has turned many Chinese students into exam machines, making them strong in theory but weak in practical skills, and greatly stifling their creativity. This sounds reasonable and is indeed a problem in China's education system. However, according to reports, India also practices exam-oriented education and faces the same "thousands crossing a narrow bridge" scenario. Therefore, this cannot be considered the main factor behind the difference between China's and India's IT industries.

3. Whether there is a culture of imitation. Many have noticed that in China, the "shanzhai" phenomenon is widespread, especially in the internet industry. Whatever new thing foreigners come up with, China quickly follows with imitative websites; whoever succeeds, a large number of similar websites soon appear like mushrooms after rain. This is undoubtedly one of the manifestations of lack of creativity, but some websites have succeeded through imitation, such as Tencent, Baidu, Kaixinwang, Renrenwang, etc., which further encourages imitation.

4. Whether foreign language proficiency is high. In the past, it was often believed that one of the main reasons why China's IT industry lags behind India's is that Indians have much better foreign language skills than Chinese. Even though their pronunciation may not be accurate, English is one of their official languages, and IT innovations or programming languages developed in Europe and America are designed based on English grammar, so India's IT industry develops better. This may be an important reason, but it cannot be considered decisive. After all, in some programming, once you get started, the main focus is still on system architecture, algorithms, and other aspects.

5. Whether personality is extroverted. The aforementioned post mentioned, "Indian tech professionals are not as dull and uninteresting as we imagine. Most of them are particularly extroverted and full of vitality, which might be the reason for their creativity." This seems to be insufficient reasoning, as in any group, there are various personalities. Moreover, even extroverted individuals may be more suited to entrepreneurship and management, while introverts may be more suited to technical design. Therefore, the "personality determines" theory lacks solid grounding. Besides, good ideas often require people who can sit still and think frequently to generate inspiration, and such individuals are likely not very extroverted.

6. Whether there is a tendency towards quick success and instant benefits. Many people claim that in China's current development stage, there is a widespread mentality of seeking quick success and instant benefits, restlessness, and the desire for overnight wealth. This affects the IT industry, which requires calm contemplation and continuous creativity. This reminds me of the recent discussion on Weibo about the post: "If you can spend ten hours a day doing one thing for several consecutive years, the probability of succeeding in that thing is almost 100%." From this, it can be seen that many people no longer fully believe in the concept of "grinding an iron rod into a needle," and instead have a tendency towards the psychology of overnight wealth.

7. Whether piracy is rampant. Some say that piracy in China is too severe, making IT products worthless and lowering the income of practitioners, so high-quality things cannot be produced, and thus the IT industry lags behind India. However, others argue that China's piracy has promoted the popularization of many new software applications. In fact, this cannot be considered a decisive factor. Given China's current income level, spending thousands of yuan on a software program is beyond the reach of most people. Of course, rampant piracy does hinder the creative enthusiasm of IT personnel, so it is an important factor.

8. Whether demands are clear. Sometimes, IT developers find that many project clients do not know what kind of functions they need, and when asked, they cannot explain clearly. Once samples are developed, there are numerous issues. Another meaning is that many aspiring entrepreneurs cannot find market demand or grasp the direction of market development, so they cannot create, are incapable of creating, or are afraid to create. This is indeed an important factor. Creation involves finding blue oceans or different operational models and accurately grasping customer needs. If these cannot be achieved, creativity becomes a major issue.

9. Whether management is standardized. Most people consider this to be the main reason for the difference between China's and India's IT industries, and I agree with this view. After all, modern software development can no longer be chaotic but must be systematic, like building a house. It requires understanding requirements, system design, detailed design, testing, and operation in the most efficient and reasonable way possible, step by step, with clear time nodes, which is the concept of software engineering. However, in many domestic IT companies, requirements are often unclear, timelines are delayed, and testing is incomplete, reflecting the lack of standardization in IT development management. This should be one of the main reasons.

10. Whether design standards are high. This is another important factor affecting creativity. On one hand, you may have an idea, but due to low design standards, the choice of system framework may be inappropriate, and the resulting product may be riddled with problems. On the other hand, due to an imperfect design process, weak system requirements, system security, and system testing links, the product may reveal its flaws as soon as it goes online, as seen in issues with the Olympic ticketing system and Shanghai's license plate auction system. Additionally, whether a product can achieve excellence and provide quality service is also a reflection of design standards. There is still a gap here.

Finally, as a Chinese person, I sincerely hope that in the next few years, China's IT industry will make significant improvements in the six factors I recognize. With the intelligence of the Chinese people, including the internet, China's IT industry will surely succeed and unleash tremendous creativity. I hope the sound of "Chinese creation" will echo through the skies in the future.
