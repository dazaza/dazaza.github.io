Microsoft CEO internal candidate roundup: Six have a shot at the top job

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:07:08

On Friday, Microsoft announced that CEO Steve Ballmer had decided to retire within the next 12 months. Simultaneously, the company stated it plans to search for a successor both internally and externally. If Microsoft chooses its next CEO from within, the following six executives are considered the top contenders.

First: Microsoft Chief Operating Officer Kevin Turner

Turner currently oversees Microsoft's sales team and is known for his straightforwardness. However, as a company that values engineering and technical expertise, insiders are skeptical about Turner becoming CEO. Turner is currently the highest-paid executive at Microsoft. His power was somewhat diminished in the recent reorganization.

Second: Executive Vice President of Operating Systems Terry Myerson

In Microsoft's recent reorganization, Myerson emerged as one of the big winners. From being responsible for the WP smartphone operating system, Myerson now oversees the core business of PC, server, smartphone, and Xbox operating systems.

Third: Executive Vice President of Devices and Studios Julie Larson-Green

After less than a year in charge of Windows technology, Larson-Green was moved from the software division to the hardware division during the recent reorganization. Ballmer has indicated that Microsoft will transition from an operating system and application software company to a service and device company, which could elevate Larson-Green's position. She currently oversees businesses such as Surface and Xbox, which together account for less than 10% of Microsoft's total revenue. However, with the growth of Microsoft's hardware business, Larson-Green's influence is expected to increase accordingly.

Fourth: Executive Vice President of Cloud and Enterprise Satya Nadella

Nadella has worked in multiple departments at Microsoft and is currently the president of the Server and Tools Business Division. Previously, he served as the vice president of research and development for Online Services. He also led Microsoft's Business Solutions Group and the Cloud and Enterprise Engineering Group. Currently, Nadella is responsible for backend server software, databases, and highly profitable enterprise technologies. There have been rumors that Nadella is Ballmer's preferred choice as successor. In the recent reorganization, Nadella's responsibilities were further expanded.

Fifth: Executive Vice President of Business Development Tony Bates

A former Cisco executive and Skype CEO, Bates joined Microsoft in 2011 after the acquisition of Skype. Since then, he has been entrusted with increasing responsibilities, including overseeing Microsoft's Lync video conferencing product. In the recent reorganization, Bates was given a key role, managing corporate development, developer relations, mergers and acquisitions, and product promotion for Microsoft.

Sixth: Executive Vice President of Applications and Services Qi Lu

Lu has been managing Microsoft's loss-making Bing search engine. In the recent reorganization, Lu gained responsibility for some of Microsoft's highly profitable software products, including the Office suite, which is considered Microsoft's "cash cow."
