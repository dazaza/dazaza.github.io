The upward movement of the market encountered pressure, with securities firms and steel sectors taking turns to support the market＿stocks

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:35:34

The Shanghai Composite Index opened lower this morning at 3268.00 points, and then experienced several dips due to the drag from the petrochemical giants and banking sectors, touching today's morning low of 3243.75 points during trading. Subsequently, the steel and broker sectors jointly supported the market, causing the index to continue rising after some fluctuations, and reaching a high of 3270.37 points in mid-morning trading. However, under the pressure of yesterday’s closing price, the index fell back again. In terms of support and resistance levels, the index found potential support at the 10-day and 20-day moving averages, and faced potential pressure from yesterday’s closing price. From the perspective of gaps on the chart, there are still two short-term gaps below at 2635.31 points and 2803.86 points that need to be filled, and the lowest gap below the index at 1762 points remains unfilled, suggesting that the SSE Composite may still have long-term adjustment needs technically. The next long-term target above should be the gap left by the sharp opening decline on January 22 last year, ranging from 4818.00 points to 4891.28 points. Therefore, the result of the confrontation between bulls and bears will determine the directional choice of the index. As of the noon break today, the Shanghai Composite Index was reported at 3255.61 points, down 14.14 points or -0.43% from the previous trading day's closing price, with a turnover of 97.529 billion yuan.

The Shenzhen Component Index also opened lower this morning, starting at 13789.08 points. The subsequent weakness in the real estate, brewing, and electrical appliance sectors dragged the Shenzhen Component Index down slightly, soon touching today's morning low of 13675.07 points. After continuous oscillations and shakeouts, the steel and broker sectors strengthened significantly, driving the Shenzhen Component Index to turn red intraday and reach today's morning high of 13822.91 points. In terms of support and resistance levels, yesterday’s closing price nearby constituted significant pressure for the Shenzhen Component Index this morning, while the 10-day moving average provided potential support for the index. From the perspective of chart gaps, there were previously two gaps below the Shenzhen Component Index at 11874.90 points and 11300.03 points that needed to be filled, and the earlier gap at 7266.41 points in the Shenzhen Component Index still implied the necessity of a long-term correction for the index. As of the noon break today, the Shenzhen Component Index was reported at 13734.68 points, down 65.82 points or -0.48% from the previous trading day's closing price, with a turnover of 66.191 billion yuan.

From a sector perspective, the initial determination of Chinese shareholders for the Shanghai Disneyland project has been made, with Lujiazui holding a 60% stake. Lujiazui opened with a limit up, and under the strong opening stimulus of Lujiazui, Disney concept stocks performed relatively strongly after the opening, with individual stocks like Jielong Industry, SMIK, and Pudong Golden Bridge leading the gains. Song Liping, General Manager of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, stated that currently, margin trading is ready, and funds will become both the financing targets for margin trading and financial targets for hedging risks of stock index futures. Affected by this news, the stock index futures concept returned strongly, with individual stock surges. Stimulated by rumors about margin trading, the securities sector as a whole showed strength in early trading. The Ministry of Finance announced on November 1st that after the new corn harvest in Northeast China in 2009, central government finances would provide one-time fixed-amount cost subsidies to southern feed-consuming provinces purchasing the new corn from Northeast China and transporting it locally. This news stimulated agricultural stocks to become the largest hot spot in the current intra-day market. Additionally, the transportation logistics sector index also performed well. With the global economy warming up, the recent increase in raw material demand has been noticeable, and the recent coal demand peak season has led to slight price increases for some freight companies. However, most growth enterprise market (GEM) stocks fell, but Huayi Brothers and Dayu Water Conservation, which closed limit-up yesterday, rose more than 4%. In summary, the computer application services, trust, GEM, agriculture, telecommunications equipment, and securities sectors are among the top gainers this morning, while the banking, aerospace, insurance, nonferrous metals, and real estate sectors are performing relatively poorly.

In terms of news, 1) 68 centrally-administered enterprises involved in financial derivatives business face floating losses of 11.4 billion yuan; 2) Central leadership reiterates curbing real estate speculation; 3) The State Council will strengthen incentive and constraint mechanisms for capacity elimination; 4) The Chinese company for Shanghai Disneyland established, with Lujiazui holding a 60% stake; 5) WTO ministerial meeting fails to make significant progress on Doha Round; 6) Excessive inventory causes New York oil prices to fall by 2.3%; 7) New York gold prices close above $1200, setting a new record; 8) Financial and energy stocks decline, US markets close mixed: Dow falls 18 points, Nasdaq rises 9 points.

By the end of today's session, a total of 711 stocks in the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets rose, with 16 hitting the daily limit up, including 6 S, ST, *ST class stocks. The three newly listed stocks, Santai Electronics, Rihai Communications, and Yaze Shares, all opened with gains exceeding 80%, and by the noon break, their respective gains were: Santai Electronics up 109.16%, Rihai Communications up 99.96%, and Yaze Shares up 78.12%. A total of 850 stocks fell across the two markets, with two limit-down stocks being part of the SEG Group - SEG Samsung and Shenzhen SEG. Among the top 50 weight stocks in the Shanghai market today, except for Shanghai Automotive which was suspended from trading, 11 blue chips rose, 34 weight stocks fell, and 4 large-cap stocks remained flat. It can be seen that the short side clearly had an advantage among the weight stocks this morning, and taking advantage of the low point to position for a rebound, adjusting positions ahead of time could help enjoy the rise in individual stocks.

Procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you're buying or selling, the name (or code), quantity, and price of the stock. And this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. Stock abbreviations are usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. The stock code and abbreviation must be consistent when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks ordered to buy must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks ordered to sell does not have to be a multiple of 100. There are four ways to place an order: counter submission order, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
