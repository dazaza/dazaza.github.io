Every day, go to clean the house after the carpenter finishes the decoration.

by ruydyir02 on 2012-02-09 16:31:28

I started working in 2006. Construction work is very tiring. I was only 17 that year, such a good age! I was crawling around in the mud and bricks. After a few months, I stopped working, but I still appreciate that job because construction work was hard and exhausting with low pay, which made me always want to change jobs. But in that small county, how many suitable jobs were there for me? At that time, my self-esteem was particularly strong, and I had a great deal of arrogance. I didn't want to do the job that people looked down upon. When families in better conditions were learning skills, I couldn't find what I should learn. A few months later, my father found someone from the village and let me apprentice with him to learn aluminum alloy windows and doors. As an apprentice, I only earned 150 yuan per month, which was relatively better than construction work. In construction, I was sore all over every day, and it was somewhat more decent.

Thus, I worked in one store for half a year and then moved to another store. This new store wasn't as good as the previous one, and the things I learned before were useless here. I had to start over again. At first, I wasn't learning technical skills but doing unrelated tasks, such as cleaning up houses after carpenters finished their decoration work. I carried those heavy garbage bags down from upstairs, which was exhausting. At 17, without much strength, I endured for half a year. Through family introductions, I embarked on a journey back home in glory.

On July 12, 2007, I left home for Shanxi. Before leaving, I had never seen big mountains, so when I first saw them, I was extremely excited. However, when I reached Hebei, the excitement turned into a sense of being farther away from home. The continuous mountains seemed like barriers separating me from home. By the time I arrived there, it was already evening. After eating dinner, I went to sleep. After several days of traveling by train and changing stations multiple times, I felt exhausted. On the first day of work, I really missed home. Surrounded by mountains, I wished I could see home in the direction I was looking.

During this year in Jincheng, I experienced many things. Initially, I worked very hard, but later I relaxed. I have a habit of reading books every day, and I am interested in enterprise management. My supervisor at the time was a fresh graduate from Petroleum University, who highly appreciated my learning spirit and treated me well. He often took me out to handle affairs. After a few months, he was transferred to the headquarters, and a new supervisor from Shandong University of Science and Technology came. We got along even better. Gradually, I became familiar with the place, starting from being completely unfamiliar. Another turning point began.

On October 6, 2008, I was transferred to Lüliang, in the Shanxi branch, marking a new beginning. I went to a new power station where the equipment was not yet installed. Every day, I was busy installing and debugging, and it took half a month to get it running normally. When I arrived, I brought an apprentice, and the supervisor was from the headquarters. I was just in charge of the shift. I knew the General Manager at the time, and he had a good impression of me, which led to direct conflicts with my supervisor. It was completely unnecessary. People should be more generous. If you only use people who are worse than you, such a leader has no future.

In April 2009, I started stock trading. The market index was at 2400 points, which was a good opportunity. The market trend was good, and I made some money. Actually, buying any stock would make money when the market was good. Don't feel overly competent during good market trends. In my work, I received a transfer order to Hubei, starting again in a new place. Going to a new place is not a pleasant experience; everything is unfamiliar, and you have to re-establish connections. Now thinking about that taste, my heart still feels quite bitter. It's different from traveling; going to a nice place doesn't give you the same feeling, especially in those mountainous areas.

After several months of crazy growth in the stock market in 2009, it began to plummet in August. In this crash, I couldn't escape, and I still haven't calculated how much I lost. I don't want to think about it; thinking makes me feel better, and I understood many truths. If I hadn't failed in the stock market, it wouldn't have led to my prolonged confusion lasting nearly two years, unwilling to do anything, unhappy every day, and thus disliking work. In 2010, I resigned and stayed at home for over a month before coming to Qingdao. Here, I was still unhappy, but after playing around for half a year, I started working again. Gradually, I moved forward in my work. In March 2011, I started learning to drive, and in this process, I realized many truths.

In June, I obtained my driver's license and slowly returned to reality. I've been striving for several years but still haven't achieved much. In October, I returned home, becoming very grounded, without that strong sense of self-esteem, viewing things lightly, and understanding much more. After taking some money from home, I returned to register my own company. This path has been long and full of setbacks, including stock market failures and difficulties in selling jewelry. Moreover, using money that wasn't earned by myself to start a company might mean that heaven will help fulfill your wishes, but not perfectly, because success is built upon failure. Now, it's a new journey again, and I thank all the hardships because your presence has made my life today better than before.
