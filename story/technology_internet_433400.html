At around 1 p.m. yesterday, a No. 506 bus was traveling towards the Lianhu Park station in Xi'an when the driver, Wang Wei, suddenly collapsed due to a heart attack. In his final moments of life, he used all his strength to slow down the vehicle and pull over to the side of the road. After Wang Wei fainted, a passenger quickly stopped the bus. Nearly 20 passengers on the bus, pedestrians on the roadside, and other vehicles were all safe and sound, but Wang Wei passed away after unsuccessful rescue efforts.

The speed of the vehicle suddenly slowed down and kept veering towards the roadside...

At 1:30 p.m. yesterday, near the Lianhu Park station, I saw the red No. 506 bus with the license plate number Shaanxi AF6174 parked on the fast lane of the Lianhu Road heading west from east. There was no one on the bus, both doors were open, there was a deep dent below the right front headlight, and the front door was slightly deformed.

On the north side of Lianhu Road facing east, 100 meters away from the bus at the subway station construction barrier wall's bottom, there were two obvious collision marks, and the isolation fence beside Lianhu Road on the south side of the barrier wall had scrape marks.

Witness Mr. Chen said that around 1 p.m., this No. 506 bus was driving westward along Lianhu Road just past the intersection of Lianhu Road and Boxin Street when the speed suddenly slowed down, and the body of the vehicle kept veering towards the roadside. The right side of the car hit the subway station construction barrier wall twice, scraping over the isolation fence, and instead of turning into the slow lane, it swayed unsteadily for sixty or seventy meters along the fast lane before stopping.

Before the driver fainted, he slowed down the vehicle and drove it towards the roadside...

If the driver didn't reduce the speed and pull over to the roadside in time, colliding with an oncoming car or pedestrians on the roadside would have been dangerous! Passenger Ms. Qin sat panic-stricken by the roadside. She said there were nearly 20 passengers on the bus at that time, and she was standing at the front of the compartment, closest to the driver.

Ms. Qin recalled that just after passing the intersection, the speed of the vehicle suddenly slowed down, getting closer and closer to the roadside barrier. After the first collision with the barrier wall, a screeching sound was heard. At that time, the driver still looked out of the window, his hands gripping the steering wheel, constantly turning to the right.

Ms. Qin said that the driver didn't say anything, but his movements were clearly slower than before, looking very strained. After the second collision with the barrier, his body suddenly fell towards the left window. Ms. Qin said that the driver's left hand and head rested on the window, his right hand still on the steering wheel, and then he completely lost consciousness. Ms. Qin expressed that thanks to the driver's final emergency avoidance, otherwise the nearly 20 passengers on the bus would have been in great danger.

After the driver fainted, a passenger stopped the bus...

After the driver fainted, the bus was still slowly moving forward. "The driver fainted, who can control the vehicle!" Ms. Qin shouted loudly. A man in a black shirt rushed to the driver's seat, steadied the out-of-control steering wheel, and firmly pressed the brake, finally stopping the bus on the side of the fast lane.

Passengers got off the bus one after another, and three or four young people directly jumped out of the windows. Ms. Qin said that several male passengers first lifted the unconscious driver and laid him flat on the floor of the compartment. Worried about the heat inside the compartment, they then carried him off the bus and placed him on the steps by the roadside. Some passengers untied his belt to let him breathe, while others hastily dialed 120. Subsequently, the 120 emergency ambulance and traffic police arrived, taking the still unconscious driver to the hospital.

At 2:30 p.m., the bus driver Wang Wei, who was sent to the emergency department of Xi'an Central Hospital, unfortunately died after more than an hour of rescue. Doctor Wang Lei from the emergency department said that when the patient was brought in, his pupils had already dilated, and he had no heartbeat or breathing. A preliminary judgment suggests that the condition might be triggered by hot weather leading to heart-related diseases.

Police officers from the Accident Team of the Lianhu Brigade of the Traffic Police Corps of the Xi'an Public Security Bureau promptly arrived at the scene to understand the situation. The traffic police said that from the current situation, because the bus driver suddenly fell ill, the vehicle could not be immediately stopped, nor did it cause any consequences. A preliminary determination is that this incident does not yet constitute a traffic accident.

Wife sobbing uncontrollably

Always thought he was healthy

Ms. Zhang, Wang Wei's wife, rushed to the hospital but failed to see her husband for the last time. Sobbing uncontrollably, Ms. Zhang said, "My husband is only 39 years old. I've never heard him say that he felt unwell, and he has never taken any medicine. We always thought he was very healthy, but... "

The bus company is preparing for aftermath handling

Currently in contact with the deceased's family

A staff member of Xi'an Wanli Industry Company, which owns the No. 506 bus, said that Wang Wei had been driving the No. 506 bus for just over a month. He was diligent in his work, skilled, and very responsible. Wang Wei's monthly salary was nearly 3000 yuan. Each No. 506 bus is equipped with two drivers, and Wang Wei was just starting his shift when the accident occurred. "We are currently in contact with the deceased's family to discuss aftermath matters as soon as possible," the staff member said. The family members were extremely emotional and unwilling to talk about Wang Wei's family situation.

It is understood that the No. 506 minibuses have been contracted to individuals by unit of vehicle. Whether Wang Wei himself purchased personal accident insurance is unclear, according to the staff member.

Investigation

80% of bus drivers are in sub-health conditions

According to surveys on the living conditions of bus drivers in other provinces and cities, 80% of bus drivers are in sub-health conditions. Some drivers have developed occupational diseases such as cervical spondylosis, lumbar disc herniation, gastric ptosis, and cardiovascular diseases.

Deputy Chief Physician Wang Gengli of the Xi'an Red Cross Hospital said that sub-health can induce latent pathological changes. Long-term continuous driving by bus drivers, irregular eating habits, and other factors may trigger potential pathological changes within the body.

Minibus drivers have high labor intensity

Mr. Du, who used to be a minibus driver, said that driving a minibus involves high labor intensity. The company sets quotas, requiring a certain number of round trips (a certain mileage) each day to avoid being penalized financially. Drivers work more than 10 hours a day.

Relevant personnel from Bus Company No. 5 said that their company currently requires each driver to work 8 hours a day. Requirements differ among various bus companies and minibus companies in Xi'an, whether in terms of income or labor intensity, resulting in significant differences.

Summer is the high incidence period for sudden death

Summer is the high incidence period for myocardial infarction and sudden death. With high temperatures, low air pressure, and excessive sweating, people are easily prone to sudden death due to the sultry heat. It is important to replenish water and electrolytes in a timely manner during summer. Drinking plain water alone is insufficient; it is also necessary to appropriately supplement with diluted salt water and vegetables and fruits to prevent sudden death caused by electrolyte imbalance.

If you encounter any of the following precursors, it is essential to seek medical treatment nearby to eliminate potential hazards.

Precursor one: Feeling brief angina pectoris, or experiencing difficulty swallowing, accompanied by sweating, with chest tightness lasting 3 to 5 minutes.

Precursor two: Occasional chest tightness or pressure, palpitations, fatigue, and dizziness.

Precursor three: Sudden hypotension, chest pain, cold sweats, and breathing difficulties.

What is sudden death?

It refers to the abrupt death of a person who appears healthy, caused by the sudden onset or worsening of a latent natural disease. Unlike organ necrosis, sudden death is caused by excessive strain, similar to how a computer crashes when used excessively.
