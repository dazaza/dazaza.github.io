Henan cemetery price is higher than housing price, selling cemetery plots is better than selling buildings

by bodamn2 on 2009-07-23 09:21:36

Mang Mountain, also known as North Mang, is located to the north of Luoyang City in Henan Province, on the south bank of the Yellow River. It is a branch of the Xiaoshan Mountains, which are the afterglow of the Qinling Mountains. Broadly speaking, Mang Mountain starts from the north of Luoyang City, stretches along the south bank of the Yellow River to the Guangwu Mountain in the north of Zhengzhou City, with a length of more than 100 kilometers. Since ancient times, Mang Mountain has been home to many tombs of ancient emperors and noble families, thus becoming a traditional feng shui treasure land for burial in Central Plains. The Tang Dynasty poet Wang Jian once wrote in his poem "North Mang Walk," "There is little idle soil on the top of North Mang Mountain, all covered by old graves of people from Luoyang. There are so many old graves that even gold offerings have no place to be put."

A thousand years later, the "idle soil" on the top of North Mang Mountain has not increased with time. Instead, the revival of traditional culture combined with market-oriented operations has made Mang Mountain a "gold mountain" once again. Even nearby areas not originally part of Mang Mountain, such as Dengfeng, have been drawn into this round of "grave real estate investment fever" due to their geographical advantages.

Grave prices exceed housing prices

On April 4th, Qingming Festival, Mr. Zhang's family of three from Lianyungang came to Xingyang under Mang Mountain in Zhengzhou once again.

"I'm here to choose a good resting place for the elderly." Mr. Zhang said that his father was nearly ninety years old, and since the elder's ancestral home was in Luoyang, he firmly believed in the saying "Born in Suzhou and Hangzhou, buried in Mang Mountain," and had decided to return to Mang Mountain after death to find his roots. "We've come here several times already, looking at cemeteries in Dengfeng, Xingyang, and Huaiji District (formerly Zhengzhou Mangshan District), but none of them were very satisfactory. I didn't expect the grave market to be so hot, there are really too few options available."

People like Mr. Zhang who are troubled by choosing a good grave site aren't just those from outside Henan Province; even local residents in Zhengzhou feel the pressure of limited grave spaces. According to statistics from the Zhengzhou Civil Affairs Bureau, in 2007, the city cremated 35,297 bodies, with a cremation rate of 88.7%. The city has seven funeral homes and 16 approved commercial cemeteries by the provincial Civil Affairs Department,

but the current design capacity of these 16 cemeteries far fails to meet the increasing demand. For example, when Wanfu Jinxiang Cemetery in Zhengzhou was planned before 1999, it was estimated that the first phase could meet the needs for twenty to thirty years. Unexpectedly, by this year, the first phase of the cemetery has basically reached saturation. A responsible person from the cemetery management told reporters, "The speed of increase in graves is more than twice as fast as originally expected, and no one anticipated it would become so popular."

"Many people from other places come here to select graves, including some overseas Chinese. To better handle the reception work, we have set up an office on Kangfuhou Street in the city," a staff member of the Shaolin Liantian Cemetery in Dengfeng told China Economic Weekly. "Resources are already scarce, and with the demand from other places, of course, it will be tight."

With strong demand and a fascination with feng shui, the price of cemeteries around Zhengzhou has continued to rise. From multiple cemetery management offices, including Songshan Cemetery, Wanfu Jinxiang Cemetery, and Mangshan Cemetery, I learned that the current cemetery prices are calculated based on the specifications of the graves. The cheapest ones are around 7,000 yuan, occupying about 1 square meter (according to statistics from the Zhengzhou Housing Administration Bureau on April 7th, the average price of commercial housing in Zhengzhou was 5,179 yuan per square meter), while in 2006, the lowest price for the same cemetery was 4,900 yuan. Based on the quality of materials used in the graves and the size of the occupied area, the most expensive graves in the Xingyang Mangshan Cemetery are priced at 200,000 yuan, which are family-type luxury graves, occupying nearly 10 square meters including pathways and greenery, equivalent to approximately 20,000 yuan per square meter.
