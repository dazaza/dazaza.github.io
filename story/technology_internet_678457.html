[Qingdao Auto] Get rid of bad driving habits

by qssqwe215 on 2012-02-27 14:32:02

Driving methods such as quick gear shifting and coasting in neutral seem totally normal to most drivers, but some people think that these driving techniques are not only unsafe, but can also cause damage to the vehicle. Recently, reporters consulted professional technicians from automobile 4S stores regarding these controversies. They generally believe that some driving habits are no longer suitable for today's more technologically advanced vehicles, and some outdated habits should be changed.

1. Quick Gear Shifting

Chen Hua, a repairman at a 4S store, said that there is nothing wrong with quick gear shifting, but the key issue is that many drivers start shifting gears as soon as they begin moving, and the more experienced drivers tend to have this bad habit. They believe it saves fuel and is good for the engine, but this is actually a mistaken habit. Each car has a recommended gear-shifting RPM. Drivers should press the accelerator to bring the vehicle up to a certain RPM before shifting gears. Nowadays, engine designs generally use high RPMs as reference values. If you shift gears at low RPMs without proper coordination, the vehicle is prone to stalling and shaking.

2. Neutral Coasting

In today’s era of high oil prices, using neutral coasting to save fuel is a common technique among drivers. However, whether for automatic or manual transmission vehicles, neutral coasting does not save fuel and can even lead to serious consequences. According to Master Chen, most modern cars use electronic fuel injection systems. For safety reasons, manufacturers do not account for neutral coasting during design. Therefore, when the vehicle is in neutral, it is in an abnormal operating state. This will increase wear on the clutch and gears. Not only does it fail to save fuel, but it also creates potential safety hazards while driving.

3. Partial Clutch Usage

When you first learn to drive, your instructor probably told you to press the clutch all the way down and then release it slowly. However, some experienced drivers find this time-consuming and often prefer to start or stop the vehicle with the clutch partially engaged. Over time, this habit accelerates the aging of the clutch. When the clutch is partially engaged, its driving plate and driven plate remain in a state of slight friction for extended periods, causing the clutch to overheat. High temperatures can damage the clutch and also accelerate the wear of components like the pressure plate and separation bearing. Experts suggest that drivers avoid taking shortcuts and make sure to fully depress the clutch pedal.

4. Braking During Turns

Slowing down while turning is absolutely essential for safe driving, but many drivers prefer to brake during the turn rather than before entering it. Ideally, braking should occur before entering the turn, and then accelerating once inside the turn. Master Chen explained that braking while turning causes uneven wear on the front, rear, left, and right brakes. Over time, this can cause the vehicle to vibrate during braking, affecting its balance. If the steering and braking force are not properly controlled, it could even lead to accidents.
