Sleeping at the beginning of Hai hour to ensure sleep quality

Sleep is the first great supplement for life. If cancer patients want to maintain health and longevity, they must follow natural laws by achieving "I wake when the sky wakes, I sleep when the sky sleeps." One should sleep at the beginning of Hai hour (Hai time 9-11 PM), which ensures entering a deep sleep state by 11 PM, allowing all the meridians to rest and recuperate. This helps boost human immunity and aids in recovery. During Hai time (9-11 PM), the triple burner meridian is most active, and the triple burner connects all the meridians. From 9 PM to 3 AM is the best time to nurture the liver and gallbladder. The liver stores blood, and when one lies down, blood returns to the liver. When blood returns to the liver, it can eliminate waste and generate new energy, allowing the flow of qi and blood to all meridians; and after all meridians are nourished, there will be enough energy generated to produce sufficient new cells, replacing aging ones, thus leading to health and longevity. Centenarians mostly go to bed at the beginning of Hai hour.

If one goes to bed past Zi hour (11 PM) or even midnight, new cells in the body will have difficulty regenerating, making it impossible to replace already deceased cells. Over time, the body will become weak. If one does not sleep early at night and relies on sleeping late the next day to make up for it, it often proves ineffective. Because from 5-7 AM is Mao hour, during which the large intestine meridian is active, and the body has already entered the excretion period, the division of new cells becomes weaker. In traditional Chinese medicine, those who seek medical help due to staying up too late and going to bed too late usually show very little improvement. Therefore, to recover, one must go to bed early. Maintaining an early bedtime ensures that upon waking in the morning, one feels mentally clear and refreshed, full of vitality throughout the day. As long as we replenish our qi and blood and ensure good sleep quality, we will recover.

Another important point is that I first drink a glass of water, waiting for the water to reach the dantian before going to the bathroom. Doing so raises the qi without depleting the original qi. Many elderly people suddenly suffer from cardiovascular diseases, often during their morning trips to the bathroom. Because as one ages, yang qi becomes deficient, and once excretion occurs, the original qi becomes even weaker. We, as cancer patients, also fall ill due to qi deficiency, unable to keep our meridians unblocked, compounded by high doses of radiation and chemotherapy, further weakening us. Due to work commitments, I rarely slept before midnight. Long-term violation of natural laws caused my liver qi to become severely deficient, often resulting in cramps. This was one of the reasons for my illness, even leading to liver metastasis. Now, I go to bed at the beginning of Hai hour and have very high sleep quality. Appropriate exercise, balance between work and rest

We cancer patients need to exercise appropriately and in moderation. However, attention must be paid to exercising at different times. Achieve changes with the sky and earth, adapting accordingly. Nurture yang qi in spring and summer by getting up early for outdoor activities; nurture yin qi in autumn and winter by going to bed early and rising early in autumn, while sleeping early and rising late in winter. In winter, you must wait until the sun comes out before going out to exercise. As the saying goes, "winter hibernation," meaning preserving yang qi and preventing its leakage. If you don't feel like going out to exercise, don't force yourself.

From Start of Spring to Winter Solstice, get up between 4:30-5:30 AM, walk outdoors for 20 minutes, then rest at home. Also, bask in the sun when it's abundant, warming your back until your whole body feels warm. This excites the central nervous system, feeling cool air moving down to your feet, penetrating three feet into the ground, causing yang qi to rise, forming strong immunity. Since the abdomen is yin and the back is yang, where yang qi resides, "yang" mainly manifests in qi, and when qi flows smoothly, the back feels refreshed; otherwise, the back feels tight, indicating yang deficiency. Only by frequently maintaining ample yang qi, allowing it to reach places it hasn't before, can it dissolve abnormal elements.
