In series with the upper resistor, connect a resistor with a resistance value of 200K.

by zxd39tp32 on 2011-12-04 14:33:18

Fault Phenomenon: Insufficient field amplitude appears, with the lower half of the image normal and the upper half compressed. Additionally, there is a very narrow horizontal bright band in the middle. Other than this, the audio, brightness, chroma, and contrast are all normal. In Philips TVs, the lower half of the image is normal while the upper half is compressed.

Analysis and Handling: This fault phenomenon indicates that there is an issue with the field scanning circuit of this color TV. The initial judgment was poor field linearity. Carefully checking the circuit components around the field output thick-film integrated block 7400 (TDA3651AQ), especially the field linearity adjustment circuit components such as 3401, 3408, 3409, 3421, and 2407, all appeared normal. Adjusting the field linearity potentiometer 3401 slightly expanded the top field amplitude. Then, by connecting a 200k resistor in series with resistor 3401 (value 82k), and readjusting 3401, the horizontal bright band disappeared, and the field amplitude could be expanded. However, the field linearity remained poor, with the image having a rolled-up edge on top. It seemed the problem wasn't here. Checking the external circuit elements connected to pins ①, ②, and ③ of the line and field oscillation and synchronization integrated block 7875 (TDA2577A), including 2410, 3810, and 3813, revealed no issues. At this point, unsure of how to proceed, the DC voltage at each pin of the field output and the line and field oscillation and synchronization integrated block 7875 was tested under power. It was found that +25V, +12V, +115V, +29V, and +185V were respectively +20V, +10V, +90V, +24V, and +130V, all being lower than expected, suggesting a possible short circuit in the line output load. Further testing of the line output tube and high-voltage forming circuit along with its surrounding load circuits showed no problems. Finally, upon testing the switch-mode power supply, it was discovered that the voltage on resistor 3337 (value 1Ω) in the protection circuit was only -0.17V, and other detected voltages were also low. After disconnecting the power, it was found that the switching diode 6317 (model IN4148) between the protection circuit and the switch-mode power supply oscillation pulse output circuit had degraded forward and reverse characteristics. Replacing it with a good IN4148 diode restored everything to normal.

This case demonstrates that faults encountered during repairs should not be addressed superficially but require thorough detection and careful judgment based on comprehensive testing for efficient results.

Based on our many years of experience repairing Philips color TVs, if there's a fault where the power supply voltage is too low, we can roughly judge using the DC voltage on resistor 3337 (value 1Ω) in the power protection circuit. If the voltage across this resistor is less than 0.2V, the fault generally lies within the power supply circuit. If the voltage is higher than 0.2V, it usually suggests an open circuit fault in the line output circuit.
