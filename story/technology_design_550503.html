Six Steps to Eliminate Bad Breath for Pregnant Women

by fuweiwo17 on 2011-11-11 20:55:30

Due to hormonal changes in pregnant women, frequent vomiting occurs, leading to bacterial residue; furthermore, the decrease in taste sensitivity causes some pregnant women to prefer foods with strong flavors, keeping their taste sensation focused on the food they consume, which may result in severe bad breath.

1. Clean the tongue coating: When a strange taste appears in the mouth, you can clean the tongue after brushing your teeth and thoroughly remove food residues on the tongue, which helps eliminate oral odor and restores the accurate taste sensation of the tongue's taste buds, preventing increasingly heavy food preferences.

2. Rinse mouth and drink water frequently: Pregnant women can rinse their mouths often to get rid of bad breath and prepare some cooling drinks, or tea and fruit juice, to remove oral odor. They should also pay attention to oral hygiene before and after meals, ensuring that unpleasant breath has nowhere to hide.

3. Avoid spicy and raw cold foods: To cater to the changing tastes and interests of pregnant women, various sour, sweet, bitter, and spicy foods can be consumed in moderation during pregnancy. However, overly spicy foods should be avoided to prevent overloading the gastrointestinal tract. Eating too much spicy or excessively raw and cold, or not fresh enough food, may lead to severe diarrhea, and in serious cases, it could even trigger premature birth.

4. Change ingredients and cooking methods: Pregnant women can choose visually appealing and delicious foods to stimulate appetite, avoid bitter flavors while enhancing sour or sweet flavors, adopt the principle of eating small amounts frequently, and always pay attention to oral health care.

5. Regular dental check-ups: It is very necessary to have a dental check-up before becoming pregnant because, for the comfort of pregnant women and the safety of dental treatment, dental treatments should be avoided as much as possible during the early and late stages of pregnancy. During the middle stage of pregnancy, if the pregnant woman's condition is stable, some dental treatments can be performed to prevent more serious lesions from occurring later due to cavities or periodontal disease, which could adversely affect the health of both mother and fetus.

6. Track special medical history: Many diseases can cause changes in taste or bad breath, such as infections in the upper respiratory tract, throat, nasal cavity, bronchi, and lungs. Diabetics, or those with liver or kidney problems, may also experience changes in taste. Therefore, if pregnant women have a special medical history or experience significant changes in breath and taste, they should seek medical advice for differential diagnosis.
