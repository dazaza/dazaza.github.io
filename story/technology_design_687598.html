Internet of Things shows "prospects for money", experts warn against thematic speculation. The introduction and implementation of the 12th Five-Year Plan for the Internet of Things has pointed out the direction of development for the emerging domestic IoT industry, which has been heating up in recent years, and is believed to have the potential to nurture a trillion-level market. However, at the same time, experts remind that the domestic IoT industry is still in its infancy, facing bottlenecks such as missing standards and unclear business models. Therefore, it is still necessary to be rational when investing, guarding against blind concept speculation and the market risks behind it.

The Internet of Things showcases "money prospects". According to the plan, during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, China will break through a series of core key technologies for the Internet of Things, achieving more than 500 important research results in technology fields such as perception, transmission, processing, and application; researching and formulating more than 200 national and industry standards; promoting the construction of a number of demonstration enterprises, key laboratories, engineering centers, and other innovation carriers, laying a foundation for forming continuous innovation capabilities.

In the next few years, a relatively complete IoT industrial chain will be formed, cultivating and developing 10 industrial clusters and more than 100 backbone enterprises, initially forming an IoT industrial system that is comprehensive, reasonably laid out, and structurally optimized. In addition, a series of application demonstration projects will be completed in nine key areas, striving for scaled applications.

In the eyes of the industry, with the introduction of the 12th Five-Year Plan for the Internet of Things, policy support will promote the vigorous development of the Internet of Things and related industries in the next few years. The plan is expected to accelerate the pace of IoT application.

As the first "five-year plan" for the Internet of Things issued domestically, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology pointed out in the plan that it will vigorously overcome core technologies, accelerate the construction of a standard system, coordinate the promotion of industrial development, focus on cultivating backbone enterprises, actively carry out application demonstrations, reasonably plan regional layouts, strengthen information security guarantees, and enhance public service capabilities.

To achieve scaled applications, the competent authorities will also carry out application demonstration projects in key areas. These key areas are intelligent industry, intelligent agriculture, intelligent logistics, intelligent transportation, intelligent power grid, intelligent environmental protection, intelligent security, intelligent medical care, and intelligent home.

Analyst Xiao Jia Yan from Analysys Mason believes that as a pioneer in the development of the Internet of Things, vehicle networking will strengthen the links between dynamic traffic information, automobiles, and in-vehicle terminals in the industrial chain, surrounding the intelligent development of in-vehicle systems and road infrastructure construction, attracting more non-traditional companies to enter.

It is understood that Shanghai currently has more than 300 enterprises involved in the Internet of Things, providing strong informatization support for urban transportation and safe operation.

Experts said that since the Internet of Things may take a long time to truly enter the residential market of thousands of households, its applications will inevitably start from industries or enterprises.

Smart Home: Beware of Thematic Speculation

According to experts, in the past two years, the Internet of Things has become an investment hotspot in various places in China. The "2011 China Internet of Things Industry Prosperity and Entrepreneurs' Confidence Index Report" released by Beijing Guomai Interconnection Information Consulting Co., Ltd. once pointed out that the number of IoT projects in China significantly increased in 2011, with most entrepreneurs in the industry showing high investment enthusiasm and optimistic about the development prospects of the Internet of Things.

However, it cannot be ignored that the Chinese IoT industry is still in its infancy, facing many challenges. Currently, there is a large gap between China's core technologies and high-end products in the Internet of Things compared to foreign countries, weak high-end comprehensive integration service capabilities, lack of backbone leading enterprises, low application levels, fewer scaled applications, and potential information security hazards.

According to Xiang Bianbin, Senior Research Manager of IDC China Telecom Research Department, the main bottlenecks for the scale application of the Internet of Things domestically include: missing standards, unclear business models, unclear user needs and fragmentation, and urgent need for integration of upstream and downstream resources in the industrial chain.

"The Internet of Things is a new industry, and its current main driving force comes from policy-driven rather than market demand-pulled, and the market acceptance is still unknown. Investors should pay attention to policy risks. Besides, the establishment and improvement of standards also require a long time. Before the standards are basically perfected, there may be situations of disorderly competition," said Xu Yifei, analyst at TrendForce Shanghai Institute.

Therefore, experts remind that since our country's Internet of Things technology, market, and products are still in their early stages, whether the current investment timing for the Internet of Things is mature remains inconclusive, so the market risk is also relatively high. Investors need to be cautious!
