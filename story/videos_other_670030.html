Replacement Therapy: Traditional medicine treats diseases primarily through either chemotherapeutic drugs or surgical treatment. However, with continuous updates in medical technology, especially in developed countries, people are gradually rejecting chemical drugs and surgical treatments, instead opting for more natural healing therapies. Alternative Medicine, "replacement medicine," has emerged to meet this demand. As its advantages based on human physiology and natural therapies become increasingly evident, developed countries have begun adopting it widely.

As early as 1992, the U.S. Congress passed legislation establishing the OAM (Office of Alternative Medicine) within the NIH (National Institutes of Health), one of the world's most advanced medical research institutions, with an annual investment of $40 million. After the establishment of OAM, scientific research in this field rapidly developed, leading to the creation of research centers at internationally renowned universities such as Harvard, Columbia, and Stanford. Subsequently, many countries established similar institutions, making alternative medicine an undeniable new medical trend worldwide.

In China, alternative medicine has yet to be widely promoted. However, encouragingly, some sprouts of alternative medicine have gradually gained popularity and recognition. Replacement Therapy for Arthritis Treatment: The term arthritis is familiar to most people. Arthritis refers to a group of diseases characterized by inflammation of connective tissues and degeneration of articular cartilage. Common types of arthritis include osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and rheumatic arthritis. Osteoarthritis, also known as degenerative arthritis, presents symptoms such as joint pain, stiffness, and swelling. Rheumatic arthritis involves inflammation of the synovial membrane, damaging the cartilage, bones, and surrounding structures, potentially affecting blood vessels, skin, nerves, and muscles, causing joint pain. Rheumatoid arthritis results from immune system disorders, leading to the disintegration and deformation of articular cartilage.

Currently, medical treatments for arthritis mainly involve two methods: symptomatic treatments such as pain relief or traction; and joint replacement surgery. Joint replacement surgery involves replacing damaged joints with artificial ones, commonly used for hips and knees. However, aside from being expensive, joint replacement surgeries can result in complications such as loosening, wear, misalignment, deep infections, foreign body reactions, soft tissue calcification, and restricted movement. How to effectively treat joints has become an issue we cannot ignore.
