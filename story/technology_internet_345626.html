Characteristics and Introduction of Underfloor Heating

by bjhxcyhj on 2010-04-14 17:49:37

Radiant floor heating, also known as floor radiation heating or gas radiation heating, has the English name "Radiant Floor Heating." It uses the entire floor as a radiator. Through the heat medium in the floor's radiation layer, it evenly heats the entire floor. By utilizing the floor's own heat storage and the upward radiation of heat, it conducts heat from bottom to top to achieve the purpose of heating.

I. What is Radiant Floor Heating?

Due to the formation of a temperature gradient indoors that decreases gradually from foot to head, it gives people a comfortable feeling of warm feet and cool heads. Ground radiation heating conforms to the Traditional Chinese Medicine theory of "warm feet and cool head" for fitness purposes. It is currently the most comfortable heating method and a symbol of modern living quality.

II. The History of Radiant Floor Heating

Ground radiation heating (shortened to "floor heating") is a technique that is both ancient and innovative. In China, floor heating can be traced back to the late Ming Dynasty, when it was a heating method only available to the royal family. For example, in the existing Forbidden City, smoke channels were built under the blue brick floors. During winter, smoke would pass through these channels and exit through properly configured smoke windows, warming the blue bricks and then transferring the heat indoors to produce warmth. Later, fire walls and fire beds appeared in northern rural areas of China, and Korea and Japan developed floor heating systems. From ancient times to today, humanity has continuously inherited civilization, innovated, and progressed. Now, with the advent of the technology era, ground heating technology has evolved from the original flue-based heating to modern material-based ground radiation heating. This technology began to be applied in developed countries in the 1930s, and China started applying this technology in projects such as the Great Hall of the People and the Overseas Chinese Hotel in the 1950s.

III. Classification of Radiant Floor Heating

According to different heating methods, ground radiation heating is mainly divided into water heating and electric heating. Electric heating is further divided into heating cable heating and electric heating film heating.

Water heating refers to low-temperature hot water floor radiation heating, which uses water at a temperature not exceeding 60°C as the heat medium. The water circulates within the heating pipes, heating the floor and supplying heat indoors via radiation and convection.

Electric cable floor radiation heating uses low-temperature heating cables as the heat source. It heats the floor and supplies heat indoors via radiation and convection. Common heating cables are divided into single-core and double-core cables.

Low-temperature radiation electric heating films are semi-transparent polyester films that generate heat when powered. They are made by processing and thermally pressing conductive special ink and metal current-carrying strips between insulating polyester films. When working, the carbon-based ink serves as the heat source, radiating heat into the space to keep the body warm.

IV. Advantages of Radiant Floor Heating

① Comfortable, hygienic, and health-promoting: Ground radiation heating is the most comfortable heating method. The indoor floor surface temperature is uniform, and the room temperature decreases gradually from bottom to top, giving a pleasant feeling of warm feet and cool heads; it prevents the circulation of dirty air, keeping the indoor air clean; it improves blood circulation and promotes metabolism.

② Saves space, beautifies living spaces: Indoor radiators and their branch pipes are eliminated, increasing usable area, facilitating decoration and furniture arrangement, and reducing sanitary dead angles.

③ Highly energy-efficient: Radiation heating is more efficient than convection heating, concentrating heat within the height where the human body benefits; there is minimal heat loss during transmission; low-temperature ground radiation heating can be controlled on a floor-by-floor, household-by-household, and room-by-room basis, allowing users to adjust according to their situation, effectively conserving energy.

④ Good thermal stability: Ground heating layers and concrete layers have large heat storage capacities, ensuring good thermal stability. In intermittent heating conditions, the indoor temperature changes slowly.

⑤ Low operating costs: Compared to other heating equipment, it saves about 20% energy, fully utilizing low-temperature hot water resources or taking advantage of electricity price policies to reduce operating costs.

⑥ Long service life: In low-temperature ground heating, plastic pipes or heating cables are buried underground, providing good stability, no corrosion, and no human damage, with a service life synchronized with buildings. Compared to convection heating, it reduces maintenance and replacement costs.

⑦ Strong adaptability: Equipment is unaffected by outdoor temperatures, greatly extending the life of the heating system.

V. Precautions for Installing Radiant Floor Heating

Floor heating relies on hidden plastic pipes or heating cables beneath the floor to provide heating. Therefore, the quality of the hidden heat transfer materials and insulation materials beneath the floor significantly affects the floor's service life. Whether the design of the floor heating system is reasonable, whether the installation position and loop spacing of the floor heating pipes or heating cables are appropriate, and the construction quality all primarily affect the effectiveness of the floor heating usage. Neither should be overlooked to ensure peace of mind.
