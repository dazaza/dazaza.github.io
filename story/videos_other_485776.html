Market fertilizer prices were mixed, urea prices rose and fell

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-09-06 09:50:10

In the second half of August, international market crude oil futures prices fluctuated at around $85 per barrel. The overall trend of international fertilizer market prices was divergent, with urea, diammonium phosphate (DAP), and compound fertilizer prices showing mixed movements. Sulfur, ammonia, and potassium chloride prices continued to rise slightly.

Urea prices saw both increases and decreases. On the 22nd, the Yuzhny bulk granular urea free on board (FOB) price rose from $358.5 to $362 per ton (ton price, same below), then further increased to $369 on the 26th, accumulating a total increase of 3.1%. On the 22nd, the Middle East bulk prilled urea FOB price rose from $387.25 to $393, and further climbed to $410.25 on the 26th, cumulatively increasing by 5.9%. Additionally, the US Gulf large granule bagged urea FOB price was $480, falling by $15; the Baltic Sea region small granule bulk urea FOB price was $465, falling by $10; the Arab Gulf small granule urea FOB price was $515, remaining unchanged; the Southeast Asia large granule bulk cost and freight (C&F) price was $505, stable; the Brazil small granule bulk C&F price was $510 per ton, falling by $5.

In the second half of the month, the Rouen, France urea ammonium nitrate solution open wagon delivery price remained steady at $248; the New Orleans urea ammonium nitrate solution 32% bulk FOB price stabilized at $273.13.

Ammonia prices rose. On the 22nd, the Yuzhny bulk ammonia FOB price rose from $507.5 to $517.5, then further increased to $520 on the 26th, rising by 2.5%; the US ammonia (Tampa CFR) price stabilized at $560. Additionally, the US Gulf FOB price was $560, up by $10; the Arab Gulf FOB price was $520, up by $5; the Far East C&F price was $626, up by $21; the India C&F price was $545, up by $10; the Northwest Europe C&F price was $575, up by $10.

Diammonium phosphate (DAP) prices showed mixed movements. On the 22nd, the US Tampa bulk DAP FOB price fell from $658.5 to $657.5; on the 12th, the New Orleans DAP FOB price dropped from $663 to $658.6, decreasing by 0.6%. Additionally, the Baltic Sea FOB price was $705, up by $10; the Belgium C&F price was $735, up by $10; the Tunisia FOB price was $690, up by $20; the Brazil C&F price was $710, stable; the Uruguay C&F price was $710, steady.

Sulfur prices rose. The Saudi solid sulfur FOB price was $215, up by $20; the Iran FOB price was $200, up by $5; the US Gulf area was $215, stable; the Vancouver area was $215, up by $5; the Black Sea area was $205, up by $5.

Potassium chloride prices were stable with slight increases. The Vancouver FOB price was $505; the Baltic Sea FOB price was $480, up by $5; the Malaysia C&F price was $510, Thailand C&F price was $510, stable; the Brazil C&F price was $560, stable; the Jordan FOB price was $490, up by $5.

Additionally, the German 45% high concentration compound fertilizer FOB price was €400, down by €5; the UK 40% bagged compound fertilizer FOB price was £320, down by £10; the Southeast Asia 48% bagged compound fertilizer C&F price was $500, stable; the Black Sea 48% FOB price was $496, up by $10; the French 51% high concentration compound fertilizer C&F price was €460, stable.
