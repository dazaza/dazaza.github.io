How to prevent neurosis? Nowadays, neurosis is a common disease and one of the most prevalent neurological diseases. Therefore, we must be prepared to prevent neurosis in our daily lives. So, how should we prevent neurosis? Below are some suggestions given by Professor Gao, the director of the Department of Psychiatry at Shenyang Air Force Organ Hospital (705 Hospital), on how to prevent neurosis. Let's take a look!

Firstly, maintain an optimistic and positive mood. Being cheerful can prolong life and help prevent the occurrence of neurosis. A heavy psychological burden and feelings of distress and frustration can harm health and lead to the development of neurosis. The legend of Wu Zixu passing through a checkpoint and becoming completely gray-haired overnight due to worry is a very typical example. Major events can have such effects, but so can daily trivial matters.

Secondly, cultivate a stable mindset and avoid overreacting. In a moderately stable environment, a person generally can live, work, and study normally while maintaining good mental health. However, in special, sudden, or rapidly changing environments, whether one can remain consistent and function well reflects an individual's psychological quality. Therefore, paying attention to cultivating a correct understanding of various environmental events, not avoiding difficulties, not being stuck in old ways, changing a fragile personality, and strengthening one’s willpower to form a positive and stable mental state is an important aspect of preventing neurosis.

Next, developing a strong and resilient character and accepting the impact of various stressful events is crucial in preventing neurosis. Everyone has their own personality; some are complete and healthy, while others may lack certain qualities, some are strong, and some are fragile, some are sensitive, and some are steady. The mental health of a person, whether it is good or bad, complete or incomplete, and whether they can accept the impact of various stressful events while continuing to maintain good physical and mental health, is related to the cultivation of one’s character. Even if someone has the best innate conditions, it does not necessarily mean that they will develop a good character later in life. Conversely, even with innate deficiencies, with persistent hard work and learning, a strong and resilient character can still be developed.

Finally, strengthen moral cultivation and avoid negative stimuli. A person with good moral cultivation is bound to be open-minded and sincere, able to treat others and themselves kindly, warmly, and tolerantly. In speech and behavior, they will not hurt others and can actively avoid conflicts and disputes in daily life, thus maintaining a good mental state, which is the best way to prevent neurosis.

By achieving the above points, you can be confident that neurosis will not find you. For patients who are introverted and sensitive, actively doing preventive work against neurosis is essential. If you want to learn more about preventing neurosis, you can click to consult with an online doctor.
