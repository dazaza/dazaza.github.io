The pain of love..

by yang5230 on 2009-12-04 17:27:18

Outside the window, the heavy rain poured down - a must-read memo on marriage for the post-80s generation. Opening the window, a cold gust of wind rushed in. At that time, she would always worry about someone standing quietly behind the window, gazing at a desolate land outside, without the noise of the countryside, without the chirping of birds, and without the shouts of colleagues.

Recalling the happy moments of the past, a picture flashed through her mind, and sorrow involuntarily crept onto her lips. Every time she thought back to this new incident, tears would well up in her eyes. She said:

"In the season when youth blossoms, they loved each other deeply, relying on each other, indulging in thoughts of a beautiful future and today. This longing for a better life was not an illusion to him but a romantic relationship bestowed by fate. But if it was fate, then why did they have to part ways? He no longer believed in the elusive concept of destiny; he only believed that she had left his world forever, never to return, leaving him in a rainy season of endless nights and days, drowning in loneliness."

Their love ultimately ended in failure. Such an unexpected conclusion struck him like a thunderclap on a cloudy day, shaking his heart profoundly. Everything happened so suddenly. His trembling body was like a lonely leaf adrift in a vast sea, seeming so insignificant. The dizzying sensation of falling made him experience suffocating anguish. This love that once drove him mad now felt like a red-hot sword silently piercing his chest without warning. A heart already fragmented felt as though it were being torn apart again. It seemed as if his heart had stopped beating, and his breathing ceased. Everything before him vanished instantly, and the world turned dark. He could no longer sense the existence of life. The words "It's over" constantly tormented his mind, forcing him to confront the pain. Silent tears streamed down his face, allowing him to taste both the sweetness and bitterness of love. "Do you know, I fear losing you, just like fearing the sand I hold in my hands slipping away unnoticed. And now, you've left me unprepared, quietly departing. Do you know what it means to be tearless in grief or overwhelmed with joy? Only those who truly love can understand this feeling, a taste that lasts a lifetime."

Before their separation, she might have sought a close friend to confide in, shedding a few tears, and then continued to profess her love after turning around. But he, he could only weep silently in a solitary corner, gazing at the starry sky, reminiscing about the past beauty. Her former words, "We will keep moving forward," had always spurred him on, yet now she chose to let go, abandoning his lifelong devotion to her. He had hoped so earnestly that his efforts would elevate her to a higher social status, but she couldn't wait for the day he fought for her, leaving him with only the harsh words, "It's over." A broken heart cannot be mended easily. It was she—his beloved one—who hurt him so deeply, yet he loved her without regret.

The cruel twists of fate deprived him of the feelings of love and pain, leaving him unsure of what happiness or bittersweet emotions were. Now, she has moved on, loving someone else. That heart which once loved her is now shattered completely, proving how painful love can truly be.
