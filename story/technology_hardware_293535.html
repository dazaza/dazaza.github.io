The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. And this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being bought or sold: the quantity of stocks being ordered to buy must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks being ordered to sell does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
