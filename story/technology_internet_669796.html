Senior three student played computer all night and then got facial paralysis, with symptoms of facial rigidity and drooling.

by anonymous on 2012-02-20 11:14:52

Early reports from Qingdao tell the story of a third-year high school student who, after spending an entire night on his computer in a video conference, suddenly felt facial stiffness, loss of control over his mouth corners, and involuntary drooling. Upon visiting the hospital, he learned that he had developed facial paralysis. The hospital disclosed to reporters that recently there has been a steady stream of patients seeking treatment for facial paralysis. With the weather turning hotter, it has now entered the high incidence season for facial paralysis, with young people being the primary patients.

With the conclusion of the college entrance examination, many third-year high school students have suddenly been freed from their intense study schedules and are passing the time by sitting in front of computers daily. Xiao Li, a candidate residing on Siliu South Road, became engrossed in online games, spending all his time except for eating and sleeping in front of the computer playing obsessively. Two days ago, after a full day of gaming, he suddenly felt discomfort in his cervical vertebrae accompanied by dizziness. By 9 p.m., he noticed facial stiffness and uncontrollable drooling, and his head was difficult to control or turn. After checking at the hospital, the doctor informed him that he had developed facial paralysis.

Yesterday, reporters learned from various hospitals across the city that summer is the peak season for facial paralysis. "In recent times, every day in the outpatient clinic sees more than 30 patients with facial paralysis," said Director Gao Xia of the Acupuncture Outpatient Department at the Rehabilitation Center of the City Central Hospital. As the weather gets warmer, air conditioning has been turned on in many places, and "catching a chill" is one of the main causes of facial paralysis. Gao Xia stated that most people suffering from facial paralysis these days are young individuals. In many people's minds, facial paralysis is a condition that only affects the elderly, but this isn't true. With the end of the college entrance exams, many students spend long hours on their computers, making them prone to facial paralysis. Gao Xia explained that this is actually due to insufficient blood supply caused by cervical spondylosis, leading to nerve dysfunction which impacts the facial nerves. The occurrence of cervical spondylosis is increasingly affecting younger people, often associated with prolonged sitting, poor posture, and sleep habits. (Reported by Xu Zhuang)
