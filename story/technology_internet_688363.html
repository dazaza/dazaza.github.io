Nine Tripod Records: An 80-year-old man homemade soybean milk and delivered it for free to the old men in the village (picture)

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-03 10:02:08

Recently, in the Lingtian Legend, Yang Wanjun delivered soy milk to an elderly person in the village. He has been delivering soy milk to the lonely and disabled elders in the village for six years.

【Touching Deeds】

Name: Yang Wanjun

Age: 71 years old

Community: Shuiyu Village, Nanjiao Township, Fangshan District

【Virtuous Conduct Record】

In the city, drinking soy milk for breakfast is not uncommon. However, before 2006, in the mountainous village of Shuiyu, fresh soy milk was a rare item for elderly villagers. Villager Yang Wanjun gave himself a "job." He buys soybeans at his own expense, uses a soy milk machine to make fresh soy milk, and every early morning walks seven or eight li (about three miles) of mountain roads to deliver the soy milk free of charge to the homes of the elderly in the village. Over the past six years, he has already broken five soy milk machines. Including the expenses for buying soybeans and electricity, he has spent more than 10,000 yuan.

At 7:40 every morning, the sunlight outlines the contour of the mountains in Shuiyu Village. 85-year-old Zheng Shuzhen is still in her kang (a traditional Chinese bed), and her husband Yang Tianbo is collecting firewood outside. At this time, someone has prepared hot soy milk for them. This person is Yang Wanjun. After walking seven or eight li of mountain roads, sweat is dripping from his back, and he is holding two large thermoses. He enters Zheng Shuzhen's home, unscrews the bottle cap, pours a bowl of hot soy milk, and after the old couple finishes drinking it, they wipe their lips. Yang Wanjun then rushes off to the next elderly person’s house, saying goodbye and leaving in a hurry.

For six years, just for the sake of delivering soy milk to the elderly, Yang Wanjun has broken five soy milk machines. This meticulous old accountant, whose salary is only 800 yuan, has spent over ten thousand yuan on soy milk alone over the past six years.

Winning a soy milk machine inspired his enthusiasm for respecting the elderly.

Yang Wanjun's home has four "retired" soy milk machines. In late 2005, due to his outstanding performance in the economic census work, the township government awarded him a soy milk machine. "Making soy milk just for myself is wasteful and troublesome. Why not make more and give it to others?"

Yang Wanjun said that he first thought of a few old Party members who had participated in the anti-Japanese war in the village. Most of their children were not around. Thus, on a snowy day on January 20, 2006, Yang Wanjun carried a thermos of hot soy milk to Zheng Shuzhen's house. The old lady, seeing Yang Wanjun covered in snow, was stunned. "Soy milk, made from soybeans, nutritious, I'm giving it to you," Yang Wanjun said.

Soon, Yang Wanjun's act of delivering soy milk spread throughout the village. Many villagers determined that he would only be enthusiastic for "three minutes." His daughter Yang Li also opposed it. Yang Wanjun did not listen, "Do my own thing, let others say what they want."

Delivering soy milk and working as an accountant simultaneously, he once got injured on a rainy day.

The mountain roads are difficult to navigate. On rainy days when Yang Wanjun needs to go downhill, he has to squat down and carry the nine-pound thermos, step by step descending the steep slope. This makes Yang Li very worried. Yang Wanjun once got injured. Yang Li said that one day, while delivering soy milk on the mountain road, her father slipped, fell down, and his knee bled, breaking one of the thermoses. But he still managed to deliver the other bottle of soy milk.

Although Yang Wanjun is already 71 years old, he still assists with accounting work at the village committee. Every day at 7:30 after delivering soy milk, he has to rush home for breakfast, go to work, soak soybeans after lunch, use the soy milk machine to grind soy milk in the evening, and cook it the next morning.

Seeing Yang Wanjun bustling about every day, Elder Yang Tianbo felt uneasy, "He still has to deal with village matters every day, how can I let him deliver soy milk to me for free?" Yang Tianbo always invites Yang Wanjun into his home, offering him cucumbers, pancakes, and such. The daughter of Elder Jin Guangling said, unwilling to trouble Old Yang, she bought a soy milk machine for her father to make soy milk.

During these six years, 19 "old customers" have passed away.

Yang Wanjun said that among the 19 "old customers" who have passed away in the last six years, Shi Junhua was one of them. Previously, she always came out to meet Yang Wanjun. That day, lying in bed, she was unconscious. Yang Wanjun realized the old lady had a fever and quickly called her children. Shi Junhua's son took her home to recuperate, but Yang Wanjun continued to deliver soy milk to the old lady daily. Half a year later, Shi Junhua passed away.

In addition, a pair of elderly couples were found by Yang Wanjun to have carbon monoxide poisoning while delivering soy milk. They were rescued in time and escaped danger. Encountering similar situations is rare, but Old Yang feels the impermanence of life, "The day before delivering soy milk, the elder was fine, but the next day, they were gone."

Nowadays, Yang Wanjun has 21 "customers," among whom the youngest, Yang Yueqin, is 49 years old. She suffers from cancer and cannot afford hospitalization, so she stays home to recuperate. Wanting to drink soy milk to supplement nutrition, she asked her husband to call Old Yang. Due to the cancer cells spreading to her neck and compressing her breathing, she can only sustain her life by drinking a few sips of the soy milk delivered by Old Yang.

【Journalist's Question】

This reporter: Don't you want to enjoy your retirement?

Yang Wanjun: Isn't this enjoying retirement? Happy every day. The commune secretary said, Old Yang, role model, bringing glory to Nanjiao Township, and I said, don't just talk about whether it brings glory; I care about living well in my later years. Others have told me, although you've spent a lot of money, you haven't fallen ill, saving your children some money, and it's also a way to exercise.

【Family's Question】

Daughter Yang Li: How long do you plan to keep delivering soy milk? You're already so old.

Yang Wanjun: I haven't thought about giving up. As long as I can move, I will continue delivering soy milk. It has become a habit now. If I don't have money to buy soybeans, I'll ask you for money. When I can no longer deliver soy milk in the future, perhaps someone will take over inspired by my deeds.

【Words of Virtue】

Doing good deeds, exercising the body, respecting the elderly, educating the children. Villagers all say, "You're still doing this, aren't you foolish?" I say I am foolish.

It's not easy to do good deeds; you must persevere, or you would have stopped long ago.

—Yang Wanjun
