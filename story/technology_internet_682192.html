Mrs. Li, who lives in the Wanshou Road area of Haidian, is a relatively new internet user. Initially, upon hearing that one could purchase excellent goods at extremely low prices online, she, known for her thriftiness, began to explore the web!

At the start of the year, her child, who was in junior high school, was struggling with math. After a parents' meeting, the teacher suggested to Mrs. Li that it might be a good idea to hire a private one-on-one tutor for additional lessons. Mrs. Li had already been considering this option. Given the high costs associated with large tutoring institutions, she decided to look online. A friend had previously recommended a site called Ivy Beijing Tutoring Network [http://www.jiajiao400.com/] (http://bj.jiajiao400.com).

On this site, Mrs. Li found a tutor named Mr. Zhang. The page showed that he had successfully connected with many parents through the website. There were also thank-you letters from previous parents, and his personal introduction was very detailed. He was a recently retired frontline teacher. Mrs. Li felt that he was exactly the person she was looking for. According to the website's rules, users seeking tutors can register for free and contact the tutors. After a simple registration process, Mrs. Li was able to connect with Mr. Zhang. After negotiations, Mr. Zhang charged 80 yuan per hour for tutoring services. Mrs. Li noticed that this was more than half the price of tutoring centers like Xueda Education! After several months of tutoring, her child's grades indeed improved!

This experience deepened Mrs. Li's belief in the magic of the internet. She remarked that due to limited social connections, finding a good tutor is not an easy task. However, searching for tutors online makes everything clear and straightforward! The one-on-one tutoring fee standards are clearly listed on the webpage. Additionally, if you're looking for Beijing university student tutors [http://bj.jiajiao400.com/], this site is also a great option! Many students from prestigious universities like Peking University, Tsinghua University, Renmin University, Beihang University, and Capital Normal University register on this site for part-time tutoring jobs.

According to our reporter, there are currently a large number of people in Beijing offering tutoring services and seeking tutors online! Ivy Beijing Tutoring Network [http://bj.jiajiao400.com/] stands out among tutoring websites. From the beginning of the year to now, nearly 20,000 people have used this website for tutoring services, and over 9,000 parents have hired tutors through it. On the website’s homepage, you can see a lot of information such as [Ms. Liu looking for a middle school science tutor] or [Ms. Chen from Television Tower looking for a middle school math tutor].

This website officially launched in 2011, founded by former Baidu employees. It seems to embody Baidu's philosophy of "making it convenient for people to obtain information and find what they need."

The website has achieved the following innovations:

1. Parents can directly obtain the tutor's phone number for free without waiting. This is fundamentally different from other tutoring websites.

2. Multiple verifications of tutor information:

- Automated verification of all user phone numbers to ensure connectivity.

- Review of various certificates such as work permits, teaching qualifications, student IDs, work credentials, degree certificates, CET-4/CET-6 certificates, and English proficiency certificates.

- Tracking and collecting feedback on the teaching situation and parental reviews, marking outstanding tutors, and ceasing cooperation with low-quality tutors.

3. Provides multi-disciplinary simultaneous search and regional map-based search functions to help users find the most suitable tutor in the shortest time possible.

4. Like many other internet services, it is completely free! This website charges no fees for users seeking tutors.

The founder of the website stated that the purpose of establishing Ivy is to efficiently match the supply and demand sides of the tutoring market and reduce intermediary costs to the lowest level possible!
