Green. Old Ma

When writing this article, I repeatedly listened to the interlude of Old Ma's final salute. It is the only music that I think matches this plot perfectly, with its ups and downs, twists and turns. Except for Xu Sanduo who was laughing foolishly, almost everyone had tears in their eyes, but no one let those tears fall. I like such farewells, I like men's farewells like this, standing tall in a row, resembling poplar trees under the sunlight. The collar flowers and cap badges reflected the sun's light. Realistic, yet not sentimental. Among all these comings and goings, only Old Ma left in civilian clothes, so, this color that best represents soldiers, I dedicate it to him.

Old Ma's appearance was quite comedic. No one could have imagined that this soldier wearing an apron, looking disheveled, was once the most exemplary squad leader of Red Third Company. Watching him argue indifferently with the political instructor, then carefully handing over the apron to the instructor, I felt some relief for Xu Sanduo. Grassland Squad Five might not be the best, but it was indeed the most suitable for Xu Sanduo because there would be plenty of time here for someone as slow to warm up as Xu Sanduo, who failed after being pushed too hard in the new recruit company, to truly start over again from the beginning.

The drama does not explain why Old Ma came to Squad Five, but the contradictions left on Old Ma are obvious. In the vast grasslands, it seems that only by sinking deeply into the long passage of time can one find a glimmer of hope for the current way of life. Xu Sanduo's arrival stopped Old Ma from sinking further, but instead pushed him to the brink of choosing between unity or himself.

Old Ma should genuinely recognize Xu Sanduo. Although they were in the same squad, they lived in different worlds. After that fruitless squad meeting that went in circles without success, Old Ma began to truly consider turning back. So, it’s unclear who saved whom, or who changed whom; hence, Old Ma has a particular fondness for the last soldier he trained.

Xu Sanduo, diligently repairing the road, was unexpectedly transferred to Steel Seventh Company. Many people said that this opportunity should have been Old Ma's. Indeed, emotionally, we more readily accept Old Ma's transfer. After all, this was his last chance, even though this opportunity merely extended his time in the military by one or two years, but that was already the final comfort for this veteran.

The decision to retire was within my expectations. One can imagine that the Old Ma praised by the political instructor would not be inferior to Shi Jin or Wu Liu Yi. Even such an excellent soldier, after Xu Sanduo pulled out all the withered grass in his heart, could he still remain in the overlap of the past and reality? He took out the last bit of sharpness that hadn't been eroded by loneliness, drawing a perfect conclusion to his time in the army.

The soldiers he trained came, all of them came; none cried, none of them cried. But he himself, in the moment of turning away, wiped away the unseen tears with the corner of his clothes.

An iron campsite, a flowing stream of soldiers, Old Ma might be the truest embodiment of this saying. What he gave to Xu Sanduo was merely simple recognition, yet in Xu Sanduo's heart, it blossomed into the first flower, laying the final foundation for Xu Sanduo's growth. And himself, in the gradually lengthening sunset, bade farewell to former comrades without a word, filled with reluctance and expectations, all expressed through the slightly trembling lips, that straight turn, in that last familiar yet solemn military salute.

If time is a painting, I wish you could become this vibrant green, guarding the dreams that endure forever.

Red. Gao Cheng

Gao Cheng's first appearance gave me this impression: he should be red, because of his bloodiness and arrogance, like the Dan Dan flowers blooming all over the mountain, dazzling and unrestrained.

When the new recruits arrived, Gao Cheng waved his arms and shouted loudly: "Tank company, don't bother me!" I was somewhat puzzled, how could this company commander be so rude? The more I watched, the more I understood, if he wasn’t like this, he wouldn’t be the Gao Cheng who jumps with joy when receiving new equipment.

In fact, Xu Sanduo has always lived according to others' standards. Before enlistment, he was his father's useless son; after enlistment, he was Gao Cheng's 'surrendering soldier'. A friend told me they didn't like such a Gao Cheng, feeling he was somewhat arrogant. But I think this is the most real Gao Cheng. The seventh company has already merged into his blood. His requirements for the soldiers in the seventh company apply equally to these new recruits.

Sometimes, Gao Cheng seems like a child, with his love and hate written all over his face. Even elite soldiers like Shi Jin and Wu Liu Yi can play around with him freely. But later, Xu Sanduo, who became the thorn in the seventh company's eye, couldn't even get a glance from him. Excellence, for us, is a somewhat difficult goal to achieve, but for Gao Cheng, it is his only standard. In this sense, the origin of his character may come from the prominent family background of a general's son, but more importantly, it comes from the superiority of being in the seventh company. Here, superiority is a positive term; it is the fighting spirit and arrogance that a soldier, even a man, should possess.

Men who are superior are often concerned about their face, and naturally, Gao Cheng is no exception. Accepting Xu Sanduo but never acknowledging him, or rather, never verbally acknowledging him, but what about inside? When Bai Tie Jun and Gan Xiao Ning helped Xu Sanduo practice reaction time, his lips clearly curved into a smile. For whom was he smiling? When Xu Sanduo did 333 abdominal rotations, he stared intently at the window, so much so that his cigarette flew out of his hand. This anger and hatred towards Xu Sanduo was not unfounded. When Gan Xiao Ning handed over the CD to Shi Jin, how much pride and praise was hidden in that seemingly impatient gaze?

Excellent men are often emotional, and Gao Cheng is no exception when it comes to his favorite soldiers—Shi Jin and Wu Liu Yi. He openly talks about them, which might be a source of his pride, and definitely also a reason why he kept pushing Xu Sanduo away. He said he didn't want to ruin the best squad leader for a soldier who couldn't stand up, but faced with Shi Jin's determination, what else could he do except wait for that day to come?

The drama doesn't specify how much effort Gao Cheng put into keeping Shi Jin, but I believe he did as much as he did to keep Wu Liu Yi. However, when we later learned that the exercise that ended Shi Jin's military career also became the watershed for the seventh company's reorganization, everything he could do, even everything his father as a general could do, was just like trying to stop a car with bare hands. No one could keep his beloved third squad leader from the past.

So, at the end of the exercise, Gao Cheng asked Shi Jin drunkenly, "What will you do from now on..." It was the first time I saw his sadness, the first time I heard him call "Jin." When Xu Sanduo stood behind Shi Jin smiling innocently, the intertwined love and hate made him revert to his previous disdain and coldness. Similarly, when Wu Liu Yi left, it was the same. The deeper his love for Wu Liu Yi, the deeper his hatred for Wu Liu Yi. That slap, was it on Wu Liu Yi's face or on his own heart? Holding the sobbing Shi Jin in his arms, hugging the stubborn Wu Liu Yi and crying together, weren't the white rabbits at that moment as bitter as the hot tears rolling down?

Unable to keep Shi Jin, unable to keep the seventh company, they all left, gone.

At Ma Xiaoshuai's induction ceremony, only Gao Cheng solemnly saluted the company flag. How much love? How much pain? Everyone knows, those who have left, those who stayed, all know.

In the empty barracks, only he and his hell remained head to tail, "I've cried, two hours ago." A very calm sentence, yet it made me think of all the shattered glass on the ground and that majestic Soviet military song. What is blood? Burying tears in the heart, blending them into the blood, and then seeping deeply into the bones.

I think Gao Cheng feels guilty towards every soldier in the seventh company. It's clearly not his responsibility, but the seventh company, under his leadership, was torn apart. He could only blame himself, intentionally or unintentionally. He forgave Cheng Cai, resolved Xu Sanduo's heart knot. I believe that in the headquarters, he performed as outstandingly as he did in the 702 regiment, but that hunting flag has already merged into his blood, becoming one with his life.

If time is a painting, I wish you could transform into the rosy dawn, guarding that vibrant green.

Written beforehand: Shi Jin is the hardest person for me to write about. Although his appearance is less than half of the episodes, the shock and emotion he brought me cannot be described in words. This kind of shock is not the grand visual effect, but a smile, an action, a silhouette, even a tear. I try my best to describe my thoughts, and any inadequacies, please bear with me.

Blue. Shi Jin

Have you seen the sky above the Potala Palace? A clean sky without a speck of dust, that color belongs to Shi Jin.

They say Shi Jin is the mother of the army, but I think it's a much deeper kind of emotion. Except for Shi Jin, probably no one would use "not bad" to describe Xu Sanduo before. Placed on anyone, would they judge a person through a brief conversation? Shi Jin could, but he couldn't bring such a "not bad" Xu Sanduo into the army. The seventh company is a high-speed operating machine, and before Xu Sanduo could catch up with its speed, he might already be thrown out.

During the home visit, I noticed a subtle change in Shi Jin's eyes. Was he reminiscing about his own past? Annie said that some people seem close from the moment they appear, as if recognizing them again after losing them. The memory in the brain remains clear, without error. That closeness has a warm and real texture, allowing one to embrace upon first meeting. For Xu Sanduo and Shi Jin, and vice versa, it is mostly this feeling. In the train carriage, Shi Jin loudly ordered Cheng Cai to sit down but gently embraced Xu Sanduo whose emotions were almost out of control. Such an action might make many people see maternal warmth and deep affection in Shi Jin.

After the new recruit training, Shi Jin ultimately couldn't keep Xu Sanduo in the seventh company, but this wasn't his fault. He still felt guilty, guilty for betraying Xu Sanduo's expectations and deviating from his own promise.

Yes, that was not just a promise, but a hurdle in Shi Jin's heart. The first time, he couldn't overcome it. He looked at Xu Sanduo with a guilty expression, speaking insincerely about the importance of being in Squad Five, then drank the beer Xu Sanduo handed him in one gulp, and turned away without looking back.

Xu Sanduo stood once again at the door of the seventh company. This time, Shi Jin overcame that hurdle. He wanted to keep Xu Sanduo, even willing to exchange his military uniform for it. Don't abandon, don't give up. If Gao Cheng and Wu Liu Yi taught Xu Sanduo these words, then Shi Jin's actions made Xu Sanduo fully understand them. With his sea-like profound chest, he accepted Xu Sanduo, tying the two tightly together from then on.

I watched the scene where Xu Sanduo wielded the hammer many times. The usually gentle and humble Shi Jin, smiling like a flower, exploded in anger at the terrified Xu Sanduo, "Do you want to drag me to death, Xu Sanduo!" Rather than anger at Xu Sanduo's inability to improve, it was more of a release for his countless arguments with the company commander, the brothers' patient persuasion outside the door, his long-standing persistence, and most importantly, for the "tortoise son" he brought out but realized belatedly or even unconsciously. Dignity and blood awakened in Xu Sanduo's body, and with the resounding sound of the hammer, Shi Jin smiled, his eyes filled with tears.

"You are the last soldier Old Ma trained." When Xu Sanduo said this, he was still laughing carelessly. Shi Jin lay quietly on the upper bunk, and in the darkness, I couldn't see his expression. Did he want to say, "You are also the last soldier I trained?" Or was he thinking, when he leaves, would the person below burst into tears?

Shi Jin smiled in the rain as he bid farewell to Xu Sanduo, still maintaining his upright posture and standard military salute. Suddenly, he turned around gracefully, as if transformed into another person. During the exercise, when Shi Jin got hit, he knew his departure was only a matter of time. For Xu Sanduo, leaving him with a perpetually smiling face and a perpetually graceful back was the best kind of farewell.

Shi Jin didn't expect the moment of parting to be so coincidentally wrong. Xu Sanduo returned and even chased him to the garage. The classmate full of warmth in his bones, the classmate he thought he could keep with all his efforts, removed his collar flowers and shoulder insignia, and in a month, became the most familiar stranger in front of his eyes.

He knelt in front of Xu Sanduo, each word making me cry. The person he couldn't let go of was still this child. Yes, the child, at this moment, Xu Sanduo became his child. This child grew up, but he had to leave him, painful? How could it not be painful? This was a hurdle in his heart, now forcibly leveled, just like the last blade of grass in Xu Sanduo's heart, pulled out not with mud but with blood! We can see Xu Sanduo's sorrow, but we can only feel Shi Jin's pain, in the two rows of tears he shed, in the moment he pushed Xu Sanduo's hands away.

Shi Jin is not flawless, just like occasionally dark clouds gather in a clear sky, and the vast night sky becomes incomprehensible. But at least we can calmly wait, waiting for the sun to rise again, waiting for the promise of seeing the moon after the clouds clear. Importantly, he is not far from us, as the article says, we understand love and growth while being hurt, and understand the meaning of life when others help us in our time of need. Such a person is around you, around all of us.

If time is a painting, I wish you could transform into a blue sky, embracing the earth and accompanied by clouds.

Brown. Wu Liu Yi

A face with distinct angles, sharp eyes, plain and silent Wu Liu Yi, resembles the land and endless mountains of Shangrong Tree.

Wu Liu Yi's appearance gave me a false impression, crouching at Shi Jin's feet like a tamed little beast seeking its master's favor. "Your deputy squad leader, me, promoted half a level, squad leader." Not just "me," but "your deputy squad leader," showing the weight Shi Jin held in Wu Liu Yi's heart, which is imaginable. Precisely because of this reliance, he particularly rejected Xu Sanduo, rejecting the Xu Sanduo Shi Jin favored so much, both in the new recruit company and Squad Three.

Wu Liu Yi and Xu Sanduo were in the same squad, but their hearts belonged to two different worlds. However, these two worlds shared a common intersection: Shi Jin. For these two people, Shi Jin was the most important person, the most trustworthy and reliable person. Xu Sanduo tied himself firmly behind Shi Jin with his smile, while Wu Liu Yi, "When the squad leader leaves, I won't have any friends anymore."

Wu Liu Yi didn't talk much, but his feelings were extremely heavy. When Shi Jin loudly scolded Xu Sanduo in the garage and pointed outside, there stood Wu Liu Yi who had been standing there for a long time, "My best brother, the soldier I trained, I broke with him too!" "Brother," I like this word. Compared to "friend" or "confidant," it contains more gallantry and blood. Before this scene, Wu Liu Yi persuaded Shi Jin to give up on Xu Sanduo, casually pulling off his gloves but with emotion in his eyes, "Don't make yourself leave." He understood Shi Jin was gambling, but the stakes were too heavy, heavy enough for him to watch helplessly as one side of the scale gradually tilted, powerless and without choice. When Shi Jin retired, Wu Liu Yi held his breath and called out the first "good!" but Xu Sanduo's "bad!" disrupted all his parting emotions. Everyone in the dormitory was stirred up by Xu Sanduo except Wu Liu Yi. He stood by the window, straight and motionless, looking at what? The stone bench he sat on countless times talking with the squad leader? The training field he walked alongside the squad leader countless times? Tears flowed, eventually tears flowed. At this moment, Wu Liu Yi wished the person the squad leader hugged was himself, wished the person facing the squad leader teary-eyed was himself, wished the person the squad leader touched last was himself. He didn't look, didn't look, didn't want to look, didn't dare to look. Afterwards, he quietly played with the last pack of cigarettes given to him by the squad leader. From Tian Nan to Hai Bei, it was just a step away, a phrase Shi Jin told Xu Sanduo, perhaps at some point in the past, he also told it to Wu Liu Yi who always followed him.

In the seventh company, Wu Liu Yi was one of the most thoroughly conquered by Xu Sanduo. Compared to Gao Cheng, his initial dislike for Xu Sanduo wasn't limited to Xu Sanduo's consistently poor performance, but more importantly, "You are more like a lump of unraisable mud than I am!" He finally said this to Xu Sanduo, along with his thoughts of Shi Jin, his eyes red despite soaking in water, covering the tears but unable to cover the desire to cry. Never would he have expected that this Xu Sanduo he despised the most would make him willingly pay the price of a punishment to support the last piece of sky for the seventh company. He wouldn't talk big truths, nor tell Xu Sanduo what was meaningful, just silently walk, carrying mountains with his strong arms.

For the old A selection, Xu Sanduo insisted on bringing the injured Wu Liu Yi to sprint together, striving for the last spot. Wu Liu Yi knew Xu Sanduo's mind was as clean as his eyes, without a single impure thought. He also knew that even if he lost this opportunity, Xu Sanduo would drag him to the finish line. But he was Wu Liu Yi, and having accepted this friend meant he was destined to exchange his dream. The moment he bit open the rescue bullet, Xu Sanduo was stunned, Wu Liu Yi laughed, it was a laugh that moved Yuan Lang as well. His dream was destroyed in the instant it was within reach, with no chance of revival.

"Xu Sanduo, in the future, no one will spoil you anymore." In the hospital room, the two looked at each other, and Wu Liu Yi left Xu Sanduo with these last words. He lay sideways on the bed, crying like a child, finally able to vent freely, but in the loneliest and most helpless moments. Did Gao Cheng's slap hurt? How could it not hurt? He couldn't stay, he was a mountain, even if he fell, he would fall like a mountain. Stubbornly refusing Gao Cheng's embrace, he turned his face away again and again, his reddened eyes filled with tears, finally collapsing onto Gao Cheng's shiny officer shoulder badge, crying, everyone was crying.

Wu Liu Yi is a man with shoulders. He knows how painful it is to be gradually deprived of all support, but he doesn't say much. He runs ten thousand meters with Xu Sanduo circle after circle. His feelings for Xu Sanduo are complex, so every time he appears, it is in a stubborn and condescending manner. Seeing the note he gave Xu Sanduo, "If you can't hold on, write to the squad leader." What Xu Sanduo needed the most, he couldn't provide, so he handed it over to the brother he once relied on so much. Such a person is a mountain, broad-minded and towering. He used his continuous echoes to let Xu Sanduo, Gao Cheng, and even Yuan Lang know what it means to not abandon or give up.

If time is a painting, I wish you could transform into the silent earth beneath your feet and the continuous mountains, neighboring the sea and connecting to the sky.

Gray. Cheng Cai

What is gray? Confusion? Or maturity? Both, yet neither.

If Cheng Cai had stayed in the seventh company, I wouldn't doubt that he would have become the next Wu Liu Yi, and years later, be called "the strongest soldier of Steel Seventh Company," but he didn't. He was very talented, ambitious, and smart, but always seemed to be in a fog, walking steadily but without direction, only knowing to be strong, only wanting everyone to see his back, but what is strength? He didn't understand, or rather, didn't understand for a long time.
