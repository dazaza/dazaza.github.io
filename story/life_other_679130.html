The Art of Drinking Beer - The Science Behind Enjoying Beer

Beer has long swept the globe, becoming a world-famous beverage. In our country, almost everyone loves to drink beer. However, how can we drink beer in a way that allows us to better appreciate its delicious taste and charm? King Edward VII of Britain once said: "Drinking beer is not just about drinking; when drinking beer, one should smell its aroma, observe its color, savor its taste, then drink it slowly, using it as a topic of conversation." Today, while we raise our glasses and enjoy the drink, let's delve into all things related to beer.

Beer Needs an Appropriate Temperature

Firstly, it's important to understand the relationship between beer and temperature. Here, temperature has two meanings: one is the temperature of the beer itself; the other is the environmental temperature affecting the beer. The temperature directly impacts the foam and taste of the beer. If the beer is too warm, there will be more foam, but it will quickly disappear, leading to insufficient carbon dioxide in the beer, lacking the refreshing sensation, and tasting bland and unrefreshing. If the beer is too cold, there will be less foam, with a prominent bitterness and reduced fragrance. Therefore, an inappropriate temperature can greatly diminish the flavor of the beer. So, what is the ideal temperature? Beer at around 10°C produces the richest foam, which is both fine and long-lasting, with a strong aroma and a comfortable taste. However, beer temperature is often constrained by the environment. As the environmental temperature changes throughout the year, the temperature of the beer will inevitably be affected. Thus, people are required to adjust the beer's temperature to a suitable level. At the appropriate temperature, you can experience the best color, aroma, and taste of beer.

The Role of Beer Glasses

Pouring beer into a transparent, colorless glass creates a beautiful scene: about one-third of the top is filled with white, fine foam that overflows from the rim, forming a half-spherical white flower; the lower two-thirds of the liquid is pale yellow and transparent, with golden stars shimmering through the liquid. To give beer its visual appeal, thick-walled large glass mugs with handles are commonly used today. When using these large handled glasses, the thumb remains relaxed, nearly free. When raising the glass to the lips, the elbow remains close to the body, back muscles stretch, and the mouth opens wide, allowing the beer to flow smoothly. Therefore, using a large handled glass makes drinking and swallowing easier. When using a wine glass, the middle finger and ring finger hold the stem while the thumb remains free, achieving a similar effect as drinking from a large mug. For romantic couples, tulip-shaped wine glasses are more suitable. At such moments, the light yellow liquid and soft tones create a dreamlike allure, making lovers feel intoxicated even without excessive alcohol consumption.

The Way to Drink Beer

To preserve the taste and flavor of beer, drinking beer requires different methods compared to other alcoholic beverages. In ancient China, poets and scholars would sip wine slowly while composing poetry or painting. However, to truly appreciate the flavor of beer and experience its refreshing quality, one cannot sip it slowly. In beer industry terminology, drinking beer should be done by "whale swallowing," meaning taking large gulps. This way, the beer spends less time in the mouth, preventing it from warming up, and ensuring minimal changes in flavor and taste. Therefore, a glass of beer should be finished as quickly as possible. If using a regular glass, the beer need not be poured to the brim; instead, pour an amount that can be finished in one sip.

How to Pour Beer

Bartenders in bars and beer halls have mastered the art of pouring beer. They can pour a glass where the liquid takes up two-thirds or half of the cup, and the foam takes up one-third or half, creating a tall foam crown that doesn't spill over. If you follow the steps below, you can also pour a beautifully presented glass of beer.

1. Wash the beer glass thoroughly. Before pouring, rinse the glass with cold water again. If possible, place the clean glass in the refrigerator to cool it down first.

2. While pouring, hold the bottle at a right angle to the glass, pouring the beer straight into the center until the foam rises to the rim.

3. Wait a moment for the foam to subside slightly, then pour again into the center of the glass until the foam forms a crown shape, spilling slightly over the rim.
