**Russian (INTERAUTO) Auto Parts Exhibition**

- **Exhibition Date**: August 28-31, 2012

- **Exhibition Venue**: CROCUS International Exhibition Center, Moscow

- **Exhibition Cycle**: Annually

- **Exhibition Scope**: Various types of vehicles, automobiles, accessories, components and parts, auto repair equipment, maintenance products, wheels and tires, speaker systems and equipment, in-car entertainment systems, automotive processing products, gas station equipment, batteries and power supplies, air conditioning and refrigeration systems, automotive decoration products, electronic mechanical systems, engines, various tools, coatings, safety systems, etc.

**Exhibition Introduction**:

As early as April 2005, the organizer of this exhibition reached agreements with well-known companies such as FORD, CITROEN, AVTOTOR (Avtodor Company), AVTOFRAMOS (a joint venture between the Moscow municipal government and French Renault), OBUKHOV AVTOCENTRE, Russian energy giant SLAVNEFT, and others regarding collaborative participation. InterAuto 2005 successfully attracted 400 prominent international enterprises, involving sectors such as complete vehicles, automotive components and accessories, professional car audio systems, etc., which were displayed in themed sections. In addition to audiences from other cities in Russia, a significant portion also came from nearby countries (such as Ukraine, Belarus, Kazakhstan, etc.). Currently, INTERAUTO has become the largest and most influential automobile industry exhibition in Russia and the CIS region, supported by the World Exhibition Alliance Organization (UFI) and officially authorized by the International Organization of Motor Vehicle Manufacturers (OICA).

**Market Introduction**:

The automotive components market in Russia is expected to grow steadily over the next few years. According to calculations by analysts at Russia's "Automobile Statistics" company, the average growth rate of the primary automotive components market will reach approximately 20%-25% annually, with a total value reaching $14.8 billion by 2010. Currently, Russian producers' share in the primary parts market is less than one-third (31.8%). Most of the components in the Russian automotive parts market are imported assembly joints and combinations. The relatively large market for vehicle maintenance materials (automotive chemicals, automotive beauty products, paint coatings, etc.), safety systems, accessories, and other additional equipment is not included in the statistics. Considering all these factors, experts believe that the scale of the automotive components market can fully compete with the automotive market, which currently has an annual sales volume of $50 billion.
