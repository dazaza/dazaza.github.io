What are Yellow Pages:

The "Yellow Pages" originated in North America. In 1880, the world's first Yellow Pages telephone directory was introduced in the United States and has a history of over 100 years. The Yellow Pages is an internationally recognized business and industrial telephone directory arranged by enterprise type and product category, essentially functioning as a register for businesses in a city or region. By convention, it is printed on yellow paper, hence the name "Yellow Pages." Today, what we commonly refer to as Yellow Pages is the telephone directory, and almost every city in the world has this directory (Yellow Pages) printed on paper.

What are Online Yellow Pages:

So-called "Online Yellow Pages" refers to moving traditional Yellow Pages onto the internet, using the internet as a medium for online distribution, dissemination, and application of the telephone directory. However, "Online Yellow Pages" is not merely a carbon copy of traditional Yellow Pages; its content is broader and its service functions more diversified. It has advantages that traditional Yellow Pages cannot match. Currently, along with the 114 telephone inquiry service and traditional Yellow Pages, it forms one of the three main methods for querying city telephone numbers. Looking ahead at its development direction, it will likely stand out from these three methods and become the most ideal tool for people to look up telephone numbers and obtain customer information.

What are Video Yellow Pages:

Video Yellow Pages, also known as video navigation services, represent a new type of Yellow Pages that integrates video technology into traditional Yellow Pages, based on the foundation of interconnectivity.
