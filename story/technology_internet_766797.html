OPPO N1 Launch Event: More Than Just a Phone

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:35:49

September could be called "the month of product launches," as domestic and international smartphone manufacturers launched new products one after another from the beginning to the end of the month. On September 23, the situation became even more intense, with OPPO, Coolpad, and Samsung holding separate launch events in different locations and at different times in Beijing on the same day.

After years of development, Chinese players in the smartphone industry have become increasingly mature. With China now being the world's largest smartphone market, their rise is reminiscent of the Warring States period, evoking a sense of fierce competition.

Among domestic smartphone manufacturers, OPPO’s movements are particularly noteworthy. In contrast to Xiaomi's attention-grabbing nature and Meizu's "Anger of Huang Zhang," this Shenzhen-based phone company appears low-key and steady. Its products are very popular in second- and third-tier cities, and this year the company started operating its own specialty stores, hoping to enter first-tier cities and change consumer perceptions of its brand. All of this needs to be proven through its products. That evening, OPPO CEO Chen Mingyong unveiled the OPPO N1 and the mobile operating system Color OS, representing OPPO's transformation.

Color OS

To everyone's surprise, the first thing introduced at the launch event was not the phone, but Color OS. It was claimed that the company made great efforts for this product: "10 months, 243 R&D personnel, 200,000 public beta testers, nearly 1 million user feedbacks, 4775 experience improvements, and 43 patents."

OPPO Chief User Experience Designer Danlels focused his introduction on the interaction experience of Color OS. First, he introduced Quick Reach, which aims to make operations faster and includes support for multiple operation methods: black screen gestures, multi-finger operations, global gesture boards, etc.

In terms of icon design, OPPO removed the borders, freeing up the icons. The current color matching scheme for Color OS is based on a single-page base color with bright accent colors. This design style also runs through the slide design of the launch event.

The O+ desktop has some minor innovations, emphasizing "connecting the virtual world with reality" and "emotional experience." For example, when you open your phone, you might see snowflakes falling on the screen while it is actually snowing outside. The call interface will display real-time weather information about the other party's location, allowing you to send a greeting and close the distance.

OPPO also emphasized the robustness of its third-party application ecosystem: "The total download volume of the OPPO Software Store has exceeded 1 billion, including over 400 million game downloads, with user activity leading the industry."

Color OS will collaborate with developers such as Shua Ji Jing Ling and Jifeng.com to offer compatibility services for multiple models, including the Galaxy S4 and HTC One.

OPPO N1

Finally, the OPPO N1 was unveiled. According to the event materials, the specifications of the device are as follows:

5.9-inch 1080p HD screen; Qualcomm quad-core processor; O-Touch touch-sensitive area; 16GB or 32GB storage; 13-megapixel F2.0 aperture camera; Battery capacity of 3610 mAh.

Chen Mingyong summarized the design philosophy of OPPO's three models last year with the word "perfection" and proposed the slogan "Perfection, Unmatched Quality," which is also OPPO's product positioning for the next generation of flagship models. Starting with the N1, OPPO launched the new N-Lens series. OPPO Brand Manager William stated, "N represents infinite possibilities, Lens represents the lens, and the N-lens series aims to provide users with unexpected top-notch and fun photography experiences under the concept of 'Perfection, Unmatched Quality'."

The N1 features an O-Touch touch-sensitive area on its back, allowing various operations with just a finger movement. Undoubtedly, this is also a solution to the problem of single-handed operation of large-screen phones.

The camera uses a rotatable design, capable of rotating 206 degrees. In other words, the entire phone has only one camera, and when you need to take a selfie, simply rotate it, making it effectively a 13-megapixel front-facing camera. The N1 includes a soft light, preventing overexposure when using the flash. The specially customized QQ app for Tencent allows real-time video beautification. The camera starts in just 0.6 seconds.

The N1 also features NFC mobile payment functionality. Additionally, in Chengdu, consumers can use the N1 to ride buses, subways, and pay utility bills. University students can use the N1 to make purchases in canteens, libraries, and around campus. The N1 is also a smartphone equipped with a dashcam.

The OPPO N1 starts at 3,498 yuan and will be available in October. Pre-orders for the N1 trial version can be made via WeChat.

From Quick Reach, back-touch functionality, the appearance design of the phone, and the heavily promoted photography capabilities, the OPPO N1 showcases an "aggressive OPPO." In the upcoming battles among domestic smartphone manufacturers, can OPPO stand out and become an international smartphone brand? We will wait and see.
