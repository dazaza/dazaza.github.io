Baobao Chest: The Legend of Condor Heroes 2 Spring Outing to Orange Isle

by 7iu4568214 on 2012-02-22 02:35:00

During the Spring Festival, my nephew gave me a well-matched new Canon digital camera. Before school started, I went to the Orange Isle with my two nieces who were in university.

The Orange Isle, also known as the Tangerine Isle Park, named for its abundant production of oranges, is a small island located in the middle of the Xiang River within the Yuelu District of Changsha City, Hunan Province. It is about five kilometers long and has a history of over 1700 years. The isle is a famous tourist attraction in Hunan Province and an important scenic spot in Changsha. Adjacent to Mount Yuelu and accompanied by the Xiang River, the scenery here is breathtakingly beautiful. From west to east, the mountain, water, isle, and city form a harmonious whole, like a magnificent painting, presenting the most beautiful scenery.

Walking across the bridge, we had a panoramic view of the Xiang River, where the water met the sky, resembling a moving painting. Looking into the distance, the city and the Xiang River were shrouded in dense fog, creating a mysterious atmosphere. The towering skyscrapers were barely visible through the translucent mist, giving this beautiful city a sense of mystery. The three of us recited Chairman Mao's poem spontaneously: "Standing alone in the cold autumn, watching the Xiang River flow northward, at the head of the Orange Isle..."

After getting off the bridge and entering the park gate, we saw a round flower bed full of spring vitality. On the super-large screen, the scenery of the Orange Isle was being played. The beautiful melody, blending with the warm spring breeze, entered our ears. Two welcoming cypresses that seemed ready to shake hands caught our eyes.

"Auntie, I like these trees shaking hands, let's take a picture here."

"Aunt, me too!"

Next to the welcoming cypresses, we left our graceful figures. As we continued walking, we were greeted by the sight of beautiful camellias in full bloom, like a group of lying maidens laughing so hard that they scattered petals all over the ground.

"Children, take more pictures here, these flowers are really beautiful."

"Click, click," the vibrant flowers entered our cameras, and we laughed together with the flowers. As we walked and took pictures, another tree of flowers came into our sight.

"Aunt, are these peach blossoms?"

"No, peach blossoms are much more gorgeous than these, they might be cherry blossoms."

There were some tourists nearby taking pictures just like us, but none of them knew what kind of flowers they were. Moving forward, the words "Plum Garden" appeared before our eyes.

"Haha, it turns out to be plum blossoms!"

Row upon row of plum blossoms bloomed proudly, their sparse shadows crossing diagonally, their cold stamens and thin branches decorated with light pink on the bare branches, a quiet beauty without any makeup.

"Picking sparse maids fills my sleeves, secretly happy that the red spring remains." Approaching, a refreshing fragrance filled our hearts.

"Seeing spring coming towards you and me." We savored this brilliance and enjoyed the fragrant scent, letting our souls wander in purity...

Passing through the bamboo forest, we saw the bamboo leaves had turned yellow. Perhaps the severe winter snow froze their beauty. We admire the silence and calmness of bamboo, no matter the season, always quietly nurturing themselves, not competing with other plants for beauty. When spring comes, amidst the myriad colors, bamboo retains a unique green. We believe that in the warm spring breeze, bamboo will soon reveal its new posture.

Continuing along the flat and spacious path, tall camphor trees, the city tree of Changsha, lined both sides of the road, with lush green grass beneath them. Climbing onto the tree branches and frolicking on the lawn, our strong figures and radiant smiles were captured in our cameras.

The Mitsubishi cone-shaped monument of the "Xiangjiang Declaration" appeared before us. It resembled a wind-blown leaf or a sailboat. On April 18, 2009, world's top 500 companies gathered on the Orange Isle for the opening of the international forum on "capital saving and environmental friendliness". This monument inscription in both Chinese and English recorded the event, symbolizing the three main bodies of the forum action: government, public, and enterprises. The cone shape signified the concerted efforts of the three parties to promote the global environmental protection cause upward. The "Xiangjiang Declaration" monument is a permanent mark of the Orange Isle, recording the fervent hope of the descendants of the three Xiangs to pursue harmony between humans and nature and jointly build a better homeland.

From afar, we could see the huge white marble stele. Walking quickly up front, we saw Chairman Mao's handwritten "Orange Isle Head" in bold red letters on a white background, shining brightly. On the back, the full text of Mao Zedong's "Qinyuan Chun - Changsha" was engraved, with powerful strokes, flowing like clouds and water, unrestrained and free, every word and sentence exuding great energy. The three of us recited the poem together: "Ask the vast earth, who controls its rise and fall?"

"Up ahead is Chairman Mao's statue."

At the top of the Orange Isle Park, there stands a milky white statue of Mao Zedong in his youth. Chairman Mao's expression is resolute, his eyes shining, his presence majestic. His flowing hair and slightly upturned mouth depict the style of a young and vigorous great man.

"Classmates in their prime, flourishing with talent; poets full of spirit, commanding the vast land... " Chairman Mao was the great leader of the Chinese people, leaving behind precious legacies in many aspects during his lifetime. Chairman Mao devoted his entire life to fighting for the people's interests without any selfish desires. In his words and actions, apart from the people and their interests, we see nothing else. Although the giant has passed away, his great achievements will last forever. He stands on the Orange Isle Head, looking far away with the people towards one spring after another in Changsha...

We took many photos in front of Chairman Mao's statue. As we left the statue, we silently bowed three times to express our respect for the great leader. I also made a sincere wish in front of Chairman Mao's statue...

The five-kilometer-long Orange Isle stretches from south to north. The isle head bears the glory and dreams of this city, standing firm against the surging southern waters, resonating with stirring notes. The isle tail, on the other hand, presents a vast and tranquil river sky, thousands of years unchanged, just like the enduring memory of this city.

"Can you recall, swimming midstream, stopping flying boats with waves?" Reciting Chairman Mao's poetry again, we were filled with joy. We made an appointment, in autumn, to come back and see the orange garden scenery, when the mountains are covered with red leaves.
