Domestic mobile phone coupon service recommendations

by ybcz0519 on 2011-08-15 09:42:03

Before introducing domestic mobile coupon services, I would like to share some data about mobile phones with everyone: 77% of people in the world own a mobile phone, which is approximately 5.3 billion, five times the number of PCs; 90% of text messages are read within three minutes of being sent; by 2013, the number of text messages per second will reach 200,000. Mobile coupons will have a promising market. With the popularity of smartphones, besides the text message method, coupon applications based on mobile apps will also have great development potential. There are already quite a few mobile coupon applications domestically, and I will make a simple summary and share them with you.

1. Dianping

Dianping is the mobile app of Dianping.com, where you can find various merchants such as restaurants, leisure and entertainment venues, hotels, etc.; GPS positioning automatically searches for nearby merchants; additionally, it offers free download of coupons for seven major cities including Beijing and Shanghai.

Company: Dianping.com

Link: http://dianping.com/

2. Aizheke

Aizheke is a location-based mobile discount and promotion service. Users can view nearby merchants offering discounts, including dining, stores, and movie information. You can follow your favorite merchants to learn about their latest promotions. Merchants can also post their promotional activities themselves to attract consumers and directly communicate with consumers, striving to build an interactive platform between merchants and users. Currently, iPhone and Android clients are available.

Company: Shanghai Renjian Network Technology Co., Ltd.

Link: http://www.aizheke.com/

3. Yuele

Yuele Coupon is a location-based lifestyle consumption platform that automatically recommends nearby discount information based on your location, allowing you to enjoy the most favorable services in the shortest time and closest distance. Currently, iPhone and Android clients are provided.

Company: Beijing Yuele Network Technology Co., Ltd.

Link: http://yuele.com/

4. Zhaoyoudian

Zhaoyoudian allows you to interact with real-world merchants through your mobile phone. It not only provides various merchant information around you but also lets you earn "advantages" by visiting Zhaoyoudian's partner merchants, or even just by following merchants.

Company: Beijing Jiuhexiyoupin Technology Co., Ltd.

Link: http://www.zhaoyoudian.com/

5. Dingding Discount

Dingding Discount is a mobile app under Dingding.com that specifically provides services for viewing nearby discounts. Its feature is supporting QR codes: taking a photo of the merchant's QR code with your mobile phone allows you to enjoy discounts. Personally, I believe QR code technology will have significant application development potential in the future.

Dingding.com has currently launched three mobile apps, including Dingding Discount, Dingding Life, and Dingding Check-in.

Company: Dingding.com

Link: http://mobile.ddmap.com/index.jsp

6. Discount Line

"Discount Line" provides users with popular discount coupons downloads, including McDonald's, DQ, Burger King, True Kung Fu, etc. No need to pay or print, just download and use instantly, enjoying the convenience of mobile discounts. Additionally, we provide reminders for available discounts (various bank cards, reputation cards, etc.), discount comparisons, and friend reviews sharing services. Currently, it is open in cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Chongqing, etc.

Company: Zhangkuo Technology

Link: http://m.zheq.cn/

7. National Chain Restaurant Coupons

This software includes a comprehensive collection of discount coupons for most fast-food chains such as McDonald's and KFC, as well as electronic discount coupons for McDonald's, KFC, Yonghe King, Pizza Hut, Xibuxibu, Oriental Dawn, True Kung Fu, Burger King, Yoshinoya, Big, Bang! John, Bean Pot, Mr. Pizza, etc.

Company: Beijing Buding Fangzhou Technology Co., Ltd.

Link: http://www.buding.cn/

8. Qianku Electronic Coupons

You can use its coupons in three ways: directly show them to the merchant, or report the coupon number to use; download mobile coupons, use the mobile SMS at the merchant to consume and enjoy discounts; print paper coupons from the Qianku self-service terminal machine, and then use the coupon at the merchant.

Supported merchants include: McDonald's, KFC, True Kung Fu, 72 Streets, Little Sheep, Yoshinoya, Burger King, Papa John's, Backflash Ice, Dengliushan, Baidu BBQ, Ajisen Ramen, Daiyu Japanese Cuisine, Naturalist, Island Glasses, Changlong Tickets, Golden Eagle Cinema, 7 Days Inn, Guangzhou Home Inn Express Hotels, etc.

Company: Guangzhou Yimeng Digital Media Technology Co., Ltd. (Qianku Media)

Link: http://gz.checkoo.com/

9. IQ-Mobile Coupons

IQ Mobile Coupons allow you to enjoy various life consumption discounts and special merchant coupons anytime and anywhere, gathering surrounding discount information through LBS positioning, allowing you to enjoy the most favorable services in the shortest time and shortest distance. McDonald's, True Kung Fu, Haoke Lai, Burger King, and other chain restaurant discount coupons can be used without printing.

Company: Guangzhou Lehui Network Technology Co., Ltd.

Link: http://www.iqpon.cn/

Watsonxu’s Remarks: The above summarizes some of the mobile discount applications I know. In fact, from the App Store and Android Market, there are many similar mobile coupon applications. From a model perspective, mobile coupon applications are a typical O2O application, and these applications have very specific application scenarios. Combined with geographical location, they can well meet this type of demand. Of course, from the perspective of mobile products, the implementation methods vary: regarding coupon content, some focus on food while others emphasize life entertainment; regarding merchants, some obtain merchants' discount information through cooperation, while others provide merchants with an autonomous publishing platform; regarding coupons, some implement them via text messages, others directly use mobile electronic coupons, and some use QR codes or offline devices to print. Of course, in terms of user experience, I personally feel that there is still room for improvement in this type of application. Additionally, coupon services often need to be grounded locally, which is likely why many applications currently support only a few cities. Platforms like Foursquare may also focus heavily on this coupon field in the future.
