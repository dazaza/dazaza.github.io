Facial red blood threads are facial phenomena caused by poor dilation performance of facial capillaries, damaged stratum corneum or superficial location of some capillaries. They are distributed like spider webs with crisscrossing threads, and in severe cases, they will be connected into patches, turning into red face. This kind of skin is thin and sensitive, and turns even redder when exposed to extreme cold, heat, emotional excitement or sudden temperature changes.

For patients with facial red blood threads, their faces appear redder than normal complexion. In some cases, only the cheeks are red with a round boundary. This kind of skin is thin and sensitive, and in severe cases, it may form deposited pigmentation spots that are difficult to cure, affecting not only the beauty of appearance but also causing psychological shadows and bringing great inconvenience to normal life. Traditional treatment for facial red blood threads:

1. Instrumental treatment: It often violates the natural laws of skin generation and metabolism, forcibly removing red blood threads at one time. This can cause stiffness or even clotting of capillaries, pigmentation deposition, and recurrence.

2. Chinese medicine treatment: All drugs have toxicity. The efficacy of Chinese medicine has diversity and cannot accurately define effective active ingredients. It lacks modern biotechnology engineering, and some Chinese medicine components can also cause allergies.

3. External cosmetics: They can only improve the symptoms of red blood threads from the surface layer of the skin, with very single targeting, treating the symptoms but not the root cause. Some external products may contain stimulating ingredients, hormones, harmful chemical components, which directly harm the skin upon contact.

4. External hormone ointments: They can quickly relieve symptoms in a short period of time, but these drugs have significant side effects and tend to produce dependency. Symptoms will recur and the skin will become more sensitive.
