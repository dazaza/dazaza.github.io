Taobao and Sina announce details of business integration, Taobao wins profits, and Sina gets closer to making money

by anonymous on 2013-08-06 18:45:59

Three months after the announcement of Alibaba's investment in Sina Weibo, both parties have now revealed the progress of their business-level cooperation. The cooperation is centered around Taobao and Sina Weibo, with these two leading platforms in their respective niches beginning a deep integration at the underlying system level.

This collaboration includes many details that can be summarized into three main areas:

- **Account Interoperability and Data Exchange**: After users link their accounts, Weibo users will be able to directly log into the Taobao platform to complete purchases and payments. Simultaneously, users can access both platforms with one account, reducing login costs.

- **Taobao Product Information Presented in CARD Format on Weibo**: Weibo will specially parse Taobao product links, so instead of dull short URLs, users will see information-rich cards that include price, store reputation, and popularity levels.

- **Weibo Taobao Edition Launch**: The Weibo Taobao edition simplifies the post creation box, retaining only the options for images and emoticons besides text input. In the backend of the Weibo Taobao edition, sellers can upload products by clicking the "item" button below the post box, with each post capable of showcasing up to 9 items, all presented in CARD format. The Weibo Taobao edition also sets up a data center providing services such as market condition monitoring, influence analysis, fan analysis, Weibo page analysis, and conversion rate analysis.

This new system will go live on August 5th, marking a significant change for both users and sellers. At that time, users will be able to log in with one account on both Weibo and Taobao, seeing an entirely new product display effect in the timeline, while sellers' management will become more efficient.

This change has a positive promoting effect for both sides. Overall, however, it benefits Taobao more since Taobao gains tangible transactions.

For users, eliminating the need for separate account logins on both platforms indeed brings convenience, but this does not necessarily affect the final purchase decision. On the contrary, the CARD display effect has a greater impact; users can view products and seller information without switching pages, saving the search step. If Weibo's big data can achieve sufficient accuracy, it could significantly promote transactions.

On the other hand, this function still needs improvement. Taobao users have a strong habit of comparing prices, and without the ability to compare, single-item information may find it hard to prompt immediate purchases, leading users back to Taobao for multiple searches and comparisons.

This collaboration holds great significance for sellers. As stated by Wang Gaofei, Vice President of Sina, sellers can achieve more precise advertising, better recommendations, and better ad effects on Weibo in the future.

Previously, promoting products on Weibo was cumbersome for sellers, requiring content to be posted across two platforms with no distinction from ordinary users, making it impossible to highlight seller or product characteristics. The Weibo Taobao edition reduces the cost of information release for sellers, improving efficiency and display effects.

At the same time, sellers can analyze promotion effects similar to the backend of Weibo's enterprise version. Sellers can obtain more accurate marketing data to enhance the precision of their sales pitches.

Interestingly, yesterday Taobao blocked the third-party marketing interface of WeChat, avoiding excessive influence of WeChat on Taobao. This indicates that Taobao and Sina Weibo will engage in tight and exclusive cooperation, advancing together in big data marketing. Taobao will therefore grow stronger, while Weibo will get closer to revenue generation. However, the latter risks being labeled as a Taobao service and marketing platform.
