Considering the potential hazards left by the original floor mats, some whole vehicle manufacturers even carried out large-scale recalls not long ago, citing the reason as preventing possible detachment of the front row floor mats which could obstruct the accelerator operation and cause accidents.

With changes in temperature, personalized floor mats from the products market have recently started to gain favor among car owners again. So, how should people correctly choose high-quality floor mats?

How to measure the "safety factor" of floor mats

Because more and more cases of trouble caused by floor mats have been reported in the media, people have also begun to care about the safety factor of these usually overlooked car accessories. Industry experts point out that there are mainly several aspects where floor mats can affect normal driving safety, and when choosing, one should focus on these aspects to evaluate the quality of the product.

1. Whether they are prone to slipping

Danger index: ★★★★

Floor mats that are either too large or too small can lead to shifting during braking. If your pedals get caught or hooked by the floor mat, it may lead to traffic accidents. It is not difficult to imagine that in a high-speed moving vehicle, if an emergency situation occurs, under normal circumstances, the driver can avoid accidents by timely braking. However, at this critical moment, if the floor mat shifts, leading to the driver's inability to brake in time, the probability of an accident undoubtedly increases significantly.

Technical experts explained to reporters that the general reasons for car floor mats slipping are twofold: one is that the size of the floor mat is unsuitable, so it easily shifts; another reason is that ordinary car floor mats usually have flat bottoms, making them easy to slip when stepped on. Some car floor mats will add "anti-slip nails" to the bottom, but due to manufacturing processes, these "anti-slip nails" are hard objects that do not provide good grip, thus potentially causing slipping during use. Therefore, when purchasing, it is recommended to choose anti-slip floor mats whose entire bottom design consists of specially designed soft anti-slip nails (the nails have good grip) that won't slip even under heavy pressure. Floor mats with buckle-type anti-slip features undoubtedly offer dual anti-slip effects. You can observe the adhesive on the back of the floor mat; ordinary footpad products often have foam-based adhesives that are prone to tearing, cracking, and slipping. In such cases, besides damaging the product easily, it also poses significant safety hazards while driving. High-quality floor mats have special eco-friendly rubber made into anti-slip soft nails on their backs, making them safer and more durable.
