Love has been suspended due to unpaid fees.

Looking back, I always feel overwhelmed with emotion. Why did you appear in my world and stay so carelessly?

In that season when the summer flowers were in full bloom, it was my most innocent time. As the sun set, you said I was a flower in the school's back garden. To others, I might be ordinary, and among countless flowers, I might be common, but you thought I was the most special. No one knew that at that moment, I was flattered and my heart was blooming with joy.

Every evening, I would watch you shoot basket after basket on that lawn. Each shot was thrilling, each one accompanied by cheers and applause. I handed you a bottle of water and wiped the sweat from your face with a tissue. Pride and confidence filled your face and eyes. I smiled, and so did you. I said you were great, and you said it was because I was there.

Do you still remember - watching fireworks together?

On that cold night with drizzling winter rain, you held my hand by the quiet Longtan River. You casually told me about what you had seen and heard at another school, and I quietly listened. You said you liked my silence. My hands were icy cold, and you tenderly held them. I liked your warm palms. The light drizzle was enough to soak us, but I held an umbrella for you as we stood watching fireworks on the opposite bank. My heart was warm, as if the fireworks were burning and shining in my heart.

You leaned down and told me, that wasn't fireworks, it was my smile - warm and bright. The cold wind blew, but your embrace was warm. Together, we counted the days we had spent together - three hundred and sixty-five days plus seventy-seven days.

You remembered more clearly than me, and I felt that you cared about me. Thinking back to when we were still at the same school, we went to class together, took exams together, ate together, shopped together on weekends, and did many other things...

But we were separated in two different cities because of our dreams. In the first year of separation, over the phone, I said I missed you, and you said you missed me even more.

You said that no matter what, we had to learn to be alone. When it rained, we had to hold our own umbrellas; when we were sad, we had to wipe our own tears. During the National Day holiday, you came to see me, and I was happy and touched for a long time. You gave me a watch, and I said how could you give me something that symbolizes "the end"? You said you just wanted to express the time we had spent together, and I said okay. Later, you started your internship while I was still studying hard for my college entrance exam.

You called me and told me how strict your boss was, and you could only answer my calls at night, but you promised to keep going. When you were tired, you could feel my comfort far away. Honestly, I was moved. Before 10 p.m., your phone was always off.

After 10 p.m., you said you felt so tired from work, and you wished the summer vacation would come soon so we could play together, crazily. So you believed that distance could create beauty. But I thought that although distance could create beauty, it couldn't provide a fortress for love.

When we were close, there was no room in your heart for anyone else except me, and I was your only one. Then you said you would protect me for a lifetime, and I believed you without hesitation.

I made a mistake on my college entrance exam and didn't get into a key university, which made me very sad. You consoled me, and I thought about retaking the exam, but you said you would wait for me. However, at this time, I received an acceptance letter from a second-tier university in the north, and you congratulated me. You also said four years would pass quickly, and we would still be together then. So I went to university with peace of mind. On the day I left, you said you couldn't come to see me off.

I was extremely disappointed. There was only an hour left before check-in, and I couldn't find you in the crowded crowd. It started raining, and seeing others holding umbrellas, I wanted to cry.

Suddenly, someone hugged me tightly from behind, scaring me. Haha, you scared me by lying that you couldn't come to see me off. This time, I really cried, and you could say I was foolish. When entering security, you said you would wait for me to come back.

The long-awaited winter vacation finally came, and I returned. I thought you would be waiting for me at the train station early, but who knew that I ended up waiting for you at the train station for over an hour. You said the road was blocked, and I got angry. You said I should understand you.

Haha, okay then. We surfed the internet together, and you logged into QQ and then went to the bathroom. I casually opened your QQ homepage and was shocked... The exclusive group for me no longer had my name. I also felt guilty because I liked a senior in university who was good to me, but I didn't know why I should feel guilty towards you.

Without saying a word, I went home alone. When you called me and asked why I left, I only thought you were hypocritical. I asked who Ting was, and you were silent. Maybe you were also shocked on the other end of the phone. You faintly said you originally wanted to tell me, but you were afraid it would affect my college entrance exam, and later you were afraid I would enter university with sadness. Also, since I was a girl going so far away, you continued to hide it from me. You explained, apologized, hesitated, and were lost. You also said you didn't want us to become strangers and hoped we could remain friends after breaking up.

I just thought you were deceiving me, and you had too many excuses. I wanted to forgive you, but why was I so heartbroken? I can't accept loving two people at the same time, so I don't like Chiung Yao's novels, where there is always betrayal of body and soul. All along, I had already decided on you, treating your matters as mine. When you said your monthly living expenses were overdrawn, I saved money and sent part of my living expenses to you. You said you were moved, and said I was the one who truly cared about you, and I didn't deny it.

In the busy high school life, I squeezed out time to learn knitting gloves. I wanted to knit them for you before winter came and lied that I bought them. In university, I learned to knit scarves and originally planned to give you one as a birthday gift during the winter break. Even if there was no love anymore, there could still be friendship. But now, I just can't give it to you. The hurt and deception you gave me were so deep, so painful.

You said you would return the money I sent you, and I asked why not all at once. You said you didn't want to stop contacting me. I really thought you were being unreasonable and too unfair to me.

All along, I thought as long as I loved you, you would definitely feel it, and you would definitely love me. Later, I realized that love isn't determined by one person, and it isn't about one person's persistence or contribution. The watch you gave me stopped at 23:43, maybe at that moment, you stopped focusing on me and started with that girl. I have been worrying about you for four years, and you should have been cheap a long time ago.

Now, what I see is real sunshine, full of warmth and floral fragrance. Actually, without you, my world can still be beautiful.

In the past, I was just used to having you in my heart, but now, new feelings are like cells in my body, truly alive. What reason do I have not to cherish them? I just want to tell you that I am very happy now, and you have long been a passing cloud in my past. Our love has been suspended due to unpaid fees.
