Arsenal suffered five more injuries in Sunday's game; Wenger's 13-year management hit by worst injuries this season.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-09 08:23:22

Arsenal suffered five more injuries in the weekend match; Wenger's 13-year tenure sees the worst season of injuries so far.

According to Sina Sports, Arsenal beat Stoke City 2-0 in the Premier League last weekend, finally stopping their losing streak. However, during the game, the already injury-riddled Gunners lost five more players. Wenger complained that their injuries have put their competitors ahead in the race for the top spot.

After 15 rounds of league matches, although the young team coached by "The Professor" has been playing attractively, they are now 8 points behind the leader, Chelsea. In Saturday's home game against mid-table Stoke City, Arsenal won but seemed to be out of luck. Fabregas missed a penalty, and Eboue saved a sure goal on the opponent's goal line. After the game, the professor counted and found there were five more injured players! According to the Daily Mail, Gallas and Traore injured their thigh muscles, Eboue strained his muscles, Arshavin sprained his ankle, and the fragile Rosicky hurt his groin.

Half of Arsenal's squad was already sidelined before the game. According to Arsenal's official website, there were eight first-team casualties before Saturday's match: Eduardo (thigh), Diaby (calf), Bendtner (groin), Walcott (hamstring), Clichy (back), Gibbs (foot), Jorquera (knee). And main striker Van Persie is out for the season due to an ankle injury.

Coach Wenger could not help but express his frustration, as other teams haven't been so unlucky! He said, “Compared with other big clubs, we have many more injuries. There may be reasons for this, but it's hard to find a common answer. For example, Rosicky and Eduardo got injured because they had long-term injuries previously, Van Persie got injured while playing for the national team, and Walcott hasn't played since he was fouled in Birmingham. This is the most severe season of injuries since I've taken over the team (13 years), and what makes it worse is that the injured players play similar positions. Last year, we had many defensive injuries, and this year almost all our offensive players have been affected.”

Rosicky's injury made people sigh at his bad luck. Before the start of the season, Rosicky had rested for a year and a half due to knee and thigh injuries. After relentless rehabilitation training, the 29-year-old Czech midfielder returned to Emirates Stadium, but good times didn't last long. The fragile player, who had just come back, now faces another month of rest. According to the Daily Mail, Wenger revealed, “He is heartbroken, it's a vicious cycle. Eduardo also didn't play for a year and a half after fracturing his leg, recovery is difficult. Your first and second games back require extreme physical effort, and no one can guarantee everything will go smoothly.”

When discussing the January transfer window, Wenger denied rumors of Ruud van Nistelrooy joining and also stated that former captain Patrick Vieira's return to North London was impossible: “Vieira is not a forward, we don't lack midfielders, Song's suspension will be over next week, I believe our midfield can cope, what we lack are forwards.” The professor also unusually admitted that reinforcements were needed up front: “I don't know if our situation in January will be the same as now. But don't think I'll buy a world superstar to solve all problems. We need a player who can play in the Champions League (i.e., someone who hasn't already played in the Champions League this season for another club) and preferably one who is free to sign. It looks like we'll have to count on Santa Claus to bring a miracle.”

The meticulous professor likes inexpensive yet high-quality players, but this time he doesn't need to search the world to find a solution to the forward crisis. According to Arsenal's official website, new defender Vermaelen, after the match against Stoke, believed that teammate Arshavin could fill the void left by Van Persie. The Tsar, playing as a makeshift center-forward against Stoke City due to a lack of forwards, performed exceptionally well. What started as a reluctant move by the professor turned into Arshavin scoring the opening goal for the home team, creating a penalty in the game, and receiving the highest score in the post-match evaluation.

After the game, Vermaelen admitted, “Van Persie is an excellent forward, and his injury has made things difficult for us, but we have an equally excellent Arshavin. He is incredibly fast and scored a beautiful goal today. Our style of play relies on passing, and with him, we can pass the ball behind the opponent's defenders and let him attack their defense.”

With the league matches over, European competition begins again. Tonight, the Gunners will travel to Athens for the final group stage match in the Champions League. Since Arsenal have already secured their place in the knockout stages and locked down the top spot in the group, the professor will treat this Olympiacos match as a League Cup game, using it as an opportunity to give younger players some experience. According to Arsenal's official website, 32-year-old veteran Sylvain Wiltord will become the 'silent leader' of the young squad.

Coach Wenger believes this compatriot should not be underestimated: “He doesn't often receive praise for his attitude and quality, but his commitment to the game last week against Manchester City in the League Cup was absolutely commendable. He is a respected player in the dressing room. He talks little but is a positive leader, always helping the young players. Of course, the best help is winning games.”

Wednesday's Champions League match isn't the first for the former Manchester United player, but for Arsenal's youngsters, it might be their first time. Wenger revealed, “You will see Wilshere. Last week, we used our youth team against Manchester City's first team, and for the first hour, we weren't at a disadvantage, although our level dropped afterward. This time against Olympiacos, I will still field a young team. It will certainly be thrilling for the young players, as the opponents will fight hard to secure second place. I hope to use this opportunity to prove that our young players can perform better than they did against Manchester City.”
