The "One Yuan" Marketing Strategy of Beggars

by bogcwkdj on 2012-03-09 10:37:46

Before discussing the issue, let me tell a story first: "Boss, my wife has run away! Would you sell me a cardboard box for one dollar?" A beggar in his forties pleaded to the owner of a small supermarket. "Take all these boxes and go find your wife. Keep the money," replied the kind-hearted boss's wife. The story is simple, but it contains a profound principle that can be considered as the third ultimate skill of the Beggar Sect: marketing (besides the 18 Dragon Subduing Palms and the Beggar Staff Technique)!

First, who am I? Undoubtedly, this "marketing master" is indeed a beggar, but unlike other beggars, he does not ask for food but for things, specifically cardboard boxes, to sell for money. It seems that he has surpassed the stage of basic sustenance and is transitioning towards wealth accumulation through investment. Moreover, he clearly understands his own SWOT analysis. Being in the prime of life with no disabilities, he cannot evoke deep sympathy from others, which is his disadvantage. Therefore, he avoids direct confrontation and uses a clever strategy. The cardboard box is not free; he offers one dollar to buy it. He knows very well that no one would accept money from a beggar, so he doesn't have to worry about the investment risk of this one-dollar transaction, and the profit is clearly visible. It's essentially a business without capital.

For brand promotion, one must learn to leverage strengths and mitigate weaknesses. In the real estate industry, properties located in bustling areas often promote their convenient transportation, being only ten minutes away from the train station, etc. Properties in suburban areas usually emphasize how fresh the air is and how far they are from the noise of the city. Even more ingeniously, this beggar uses the guise of purchasing with one dollar to actually beg for cardboard boxes. This tactic of misdirection is used extensively in the health supplement industry. For example, Brain White Gold doesn't advertise itself as a sleep improvement health supplement but as a healthy gift; Haoji Star is just an ordinary Chinese-English electronic dictionary but is marketed as a powerful tool to improve English scores. Therefore, before promoting a brand, one must first find its positioning and understand who they are and which path is more beneficial.

Secondly, how do I promote myself? "My wife has run away" is the core slogan of this beggar. For a normal man, being without a spouse in middle age is undoubtedly the greatest sorrow. He uses this phrase to directly appeal to the most tender part of people's hearts, gaining sympathy and recognition, thereby achieving his goal. Let's search our memories: "Mom, I can help you with chores now" —— Diao Brand detergent; "Every bottle of water you drink contributes a penny to afforestation in our country" —— Nongfu Spring mineral water; Health wine for fathers —— Daolukuigui wine.

No matter what others say, as long as it doesn't violate national laws, social ethics, or harm others' interests, any means beneficial to brand promotion can be adopted. All methods that achieve the purpose of dissemination are worthy of affirmation. From a market behavior perspective, whether the beggar's wife really ran away is not important; it might just be a gimmick or a promotional slogan. Perhaps his wife is at home sorting out the cardboard boxes her husband "bought"!

Thirdly, what is the essence of marketing behavior? The beggar sells his pitiful appearance to gain sympathy, along with the one dollar that he doesn't actually need to spend, obtaining the cardboard box and achieving a feeling of success with minimal effort. The small supermarket's bossess also completed a form of event marketing. Her cost was a few cardboard boxes, but her act of sympathizing with the weak earned her recognition and respect from the surrounding crowd.

Therefore, the essence of marketing, in a certain sense, is to obtain a feeling. A $10 electronic watch and a $300,000 Rolex show the same time, so wearing a Rolex isn't for more accurate timekeeping but for the feeling of being a successful person; buying Contac isn't because it tastes good but for eliminating cold symptoms and restoring health; drinking white wine with friends isn't for the throat burn but for seeking the feeling of "thinking of the vast universe, alone I weep with sorrow."

Similarly, as marketing planners, we are not here to spread confusing concepts for fame and fortune, but to sell our ideas and wisdom, helping clients succeed through practical actions, thereby achieving a feeling of shared success. I believe that this is the essence of life's pursuit!
