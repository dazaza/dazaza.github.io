It's just that when I was editing posts in the past, I often needed to use PS, but my computer wasn't very good and I had a lot of software open, so it would frequently freeze. So later on, I started using the web-based version of "PS". Today, a friend needed to edit some pictures. We couldn't communicate clearly over QQ, but it was actually a very simple editing task. I had no choice but to teach her how to use PS. Unfortunately, she didn't have it installed on her computer and downloading it would be troublesome. So, I decided to teach her how to use the online version. However, it had to be as close to PS as possible. Therefore, I taught her how to operate from my two favorite online versions of "PS". In fact, there are many online image editing tools. Without going into how many, take a look at this post: More than 130 online photography tools and peripherals. However, the ones I truly like are Splashup and pixlr. Why? Because their interfaces are most similar to PS, and the operations are also very similar. Personally, I don't pursue too many functions, which is why I like these two websites the most. But this article isn't targeted at everyone, so I'm introducing 10 websites instead of just two. The other websites have many characteristics and each has its own advantages. For example, Adobe Photoshop Express provides 2G of space, and its features are also very powerful. flauntR's functions are even more powerful, as it can even customize phone wallpapers for you. There's also Pixenate, which offers magical special effects production, etc. Everyone can try them out. The one that suits you best is the best. For people who don't use PS much but occasionally need to edit pictures, I personally still recommend Splashup and pixlr, or domestic options like IEPhotoshop and Picture Warehouse.
