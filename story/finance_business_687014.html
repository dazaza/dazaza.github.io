HDPE Steel Strip Enhanced Helical Corrugated Pipe is also known as steel strip enhanced polyethylene (PE) helical corrugated pipe or PE steel strip enhanced pipe. The steel strip enhanced spiral corrugated pipe (MRP) uses U-shaped steel strips combined with PE materials, allowing the pipe to significantly increase its ring stiffness without additional costs. Under equal weight conditions, it offers much higher strength compared to wound pipes and other types of pipes. The advantages of large-diameter pipes are even more pronounced, maintaining a ring stiffness of over 8KN/m2, making them lighter and stronger than concrete pipes and other plastic pipes.

The layers are compounded while the plastic is in a molten state; this is completely different from the secondary melting molding process used in coiled pipes (hollow wall pipes), ensuring that each layer is fully bonded with extremely high strength. The surface of the steel strip undergoes special pre-treatment to enhance the corrosion resistance of the steel and improve the bonding between the steel and the plastic, increasing the peel strength. Formed by winding under full melting conditions of plastic raw materials, the overall structure of the pipe is stable and reliable. Due to the use of special materials and processes, the problem of steel plate corrosion has been solved, as well as the adhesion between the steel plate and PE material, making the service life of the pipeline the same as pure plastic pipelines, ensuring a lifespan of over 50 years.

It can be connected using electric heat fusion, heat shrink tape, internal/external extrusion welding, or a combination of multiple connection methods, providing stable and reliable connections that can make the pipeline achieve zero leakage.

National standards implemented:

CJ/T255-2006 "Buried Drainage Steel Strip Enhanced Polyethylene (PE) Helical Corrugated Pipe"
