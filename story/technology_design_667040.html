Daily care tips for female infertility: Authoritative experts from Sperm and Egg Network introduce the daily care tips for female infertility. The experts of Sperm and Egg Network pointed out that many men and women do not pay attention to the daily maintenance of their bodies, resulting in infertility and disharmony in families. So, how should patients with infertility receive better treatment?

Experts from Sperm and Egg Network point out the daily care for infertility:

1. Female infertility patients should seek treatment as soon as possible, and also pay attention to proper protection in their lives to achieve better treatment results.

2. Mentally, one should be calm, avoid excessive anxiety and worry, maintain a happy mood, and cultivate broad interests. Couples hope to have children after marriage, but often due to too much eagerness, they cannot conceive. Especially for older people or those who have been married for several years without conceiving, their mood is more tense, which affects the neuroendocrine function.

3. Pay attention to self-protection and reduce the occurrence of infertility. For some jobs that may affect fertility, protective measures should be taken, such as avoiding contact with radiation and harmful substances like certain chemicals and heavy metals, and avoiding high-temperature work. Infertile patients should try to avoid smoking and drinking.

4. Increase nutrition and exercise. Eating more animal organs such as liver and brain is beneficial for the synthesis of sex hormones, and vitamins are also essential nutrients. It is advisable to eat more protein-rich and vitamin-rich foods, such as lean meat, eggs, fresh vegetables, and fruits.

5. In daily life, it is advisable to practice abstinence and avoid excessive sexual activity. Controlling sexual life is beneficial for conception and preventing infertility. However, it should not be too long, otherwise retained semen can affect sperm motility. It is recommended to have intercourse during the female ovulation period to increase the chances of conception.
