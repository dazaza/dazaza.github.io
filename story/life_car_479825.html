Top Ten Hardware Maintenance Methods for Cars under High Temperature

1. Engine

Under high temperature, the engine's heat dissipation becomes worse, affecting its power performance. Especially when driving on mountain roads, climbing slopes under high temperature or idling for a long time, due to the slow speed, although the fan plays a certain role in heat dissipation, it cannot use the wind brought by high-speed driving to cool the engine. This leads to poor cooling effect and higher temperature of the engine. A high-temperature engine makes the motor oil viscosity thinner, reducing lubrication performance. If there is a situation where fuel mixes with motor oil, causing even greater viscosity reduction, and the engine load remains high, serious accidents such as engine seizure or cylinder scoring may occur. Moreover, high temperature can cause rapid oxidation and deterioration of motor oil, producing oil sludge and carbon deposits over time. Oil sludge and carbon deposits can block the filter core, further affecting the engine's lubrication. In addition, gasoline evaporates easily under high temperatures, and the engine fuel supply system is prone to "vapor lock," which greatly affects the normal operation of the engine. Under high-temperature working conditions, wear, scratches, and aging fatigue of components are more severe than at normal temperatures. The car should pay attention to the cooling system's cooling measures. Coolant, battery water, and motor oil evaporate faster in summer and need to be checked and replenished in time. Furthermore, overloaded operation, with the engine frequently driving air conditioning refrigeration, consumes 25%-30% more power and 15% more gasoline than usual. Therefore, during the summer, attention should be paid to engine maintenance work, switching to higher viscosity grade lubricants and appropriately shortening the oil change cycle. Additionally, after the engine runs for a period of time, some lubricating oil will be consumed, and it needs to be added according to the actual situation. Remember, adding lubricating oil should not exceed the marked upper limit; otherwise, too much lubricating oil increases the rotational resistance of the crankshaft, reducing the engine's output power. Moreover, it causes the engine to burn lubricating oil, emit blue smoke, increase fuel consumption, accelerate the wear of cylinders and pistons, and shorten the engine's service life.

2. Radiator

The temperature of the engine is generally higher in the summer season. To ensure the engine operates at a normal temperature, the cooling system must not be overlooked. Car owners should pay special attention to whether the radiator has enough water because the water in the radiator evaporates faster in hot weather. When water shortage is detected, stop immediately but do not add water right away; instead, wait until the idle speed reduces the temperature before replenishing the water and be careful not to open the radiator cap immediately to avoid scalding. Also, check if the radiator is blocked by dust or other dirty things and if the electronic fan is operating normally. In short, once the engine overheats, stop and inspect the vehicle; continuing to drive could result in serious problems like cylinder explosion.

3. Electronic Components

In summer, vehicles often catch fire spontaneously. Therefore, cars need to do a better job of "fire prevention" in summer, paying attention to the condition of the fuel system and checking if there is any oil leakage. Moreover, short circuits in electronic components are also an important cause of vehicle spontaneous combustion. During driving, if any issues with the vehicle's electronic components are discovered, they should be inspected promptly without being ignored.

4. Air Conditioning

Poor air conditioning cooling effects in summer are the most unbearable. Generally, it is caused by insufficient refrigerant quantity. Many people believe that adding more refrigerant improves cooling efficiency, but this is not true. The amount of refrigerant should be moderate; too little or too much will affect the cooling effect. Car owners must strictly follow the specifications and technical parameters in the repair manual when adding refrigerant. Especially if too much refrigerant is added, the expansion space for the refrigerant becomes limited, reducing the heat conversion ability and affecting the cooling effect. Additionally, ensure the air conditioning ducts remain unobstructed, and regularly check, clean, and replace the air filter.

5. Tires

Blowouts are what drivers fear most when driving in hot summers. Therefore, checking tire pressure before each day's trip is particularly important. Drivers know that inappropriate tire pressure can easily lead to blowouts and danger. Having a "pressure gauge" ready in the car is very convenient. It's best to measure pressure when the car is cold and according to specified values, usually around 2.2 bar, which applies to almost all models. Measuring hot tires may show an increase of 0.8 bar - 1.0 bar, which is normal.

6. Battery

Battery maintenance is also very important. Pay attention to whether the positive and negative terminals are loose, check them periodically, and remove any oxidation. Prevent water or other liquids from existing between the terminals and keep them clean.

7. Antifreeze

Car owners need to break a misconception: adding too much water to antifreeze. We previously thought adding water was economical and cost-effective, but this often raises water temperature, preventing the vehicle from functioning properly. What's the reason? Antifreeze and water have different boiling points and freezing points. Water boils at 100°C, so when the water temperature is too high, the water in the radiator easily boils over. However, antifreeze has a relatively higher boiling point, making it less likely to boil over. Like how water freezes at 0°C in winter, antifreeze freezes at -40°C. Moreover, water contains many alkalis that easily form scale in the cylinder head, engine body, cooling tank, water pump, and heat exchanger cooling tubes. The addition of antifreeze should not fall below the warning line, but adding too much is also bad as it easily overflows, leading to waste. However, in hot summers, the radiator boiling over is inevitable. When this happens, remember not to open the radiator cap immediately, as the water spraying out can injure people.

8. Fuel Tank

Fuel expands in hot environments. If the tank is filled too full, it can become dangerous easily. In summer, the ground temperature is also high, which we should be mindful of. If the hood catches fire, don't rush to open it to prevent oxygen from entering and accelerating combustion. Have a fire extinguisher ready and slowly open the hood to spray inside.

9. Gasoline Pipes

Before driving daily, checking the gasoline pipes is crucial. There are many oil pipes under the car hood. Pay attention to whether they have leaks because these gasoline pipes in the car can produce sparks wherever a circuit forms, making them highly flammable if damaged. Additionally, check if there are any leftover items under the car, such as oil or water. If so, check quickly to prevent accidents.

10. Audio System

Cassette Tapes: In summer, when cars are parked outdoors, cassette tapes' shells, made of chemical materials, deform under high temperatures. Once inserted into the player, this causes tape jamming. High temperatures also make the tape core softer and stickier, resulting in reel-up malfunctions. First, check the tightness of the cassette tapes. If loose, tighten them with your fingers or a pencil. If too tight, relax them by fast-forwarding/reversing. If the catalog tag on the cassette protrudes, either reattach it or tear it off to avoid tape malfunction. It's best to eject the cassette when not using it or turning off the machine for extended periods.

CD: Due to high sun exposure in parking situations, CD players sometimes fail to work, which is normal as the high-temperature protection circuit activates. With the arrival of the rainy season and increased humidity, CD surfaces often develop a layer of fog, causing reading difficulties. It's best to wipe them down every so often. When installing a CD drive, avoid placing it under the floorboard or seats to prevent moisture. Install it higher up on the car body, but not near the rear windshield where high temperatures can accelerate the aging of electronic components and laser heads. Factors affecting the normal operation of car audio systems in summer include high temperatures and humidity.
