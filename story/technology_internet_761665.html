A year after Microsoft and Barnes & Noble joined hands, a third party has emerged, and the 3 billion US dollars investment might go down the drain.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-06 11:08:15

Tencent Technology (Larry) - On May 5, according to foreign media reports, a year ago, Microsoft and Barnes & Noble, the largest physical bookstore retailer in the United States, announced that they would establish a strategic partnership. For this purpose, Microsoft invested $300 million in Barnes & Noble's Nook business department and at the same time held 17.6% of the joint company's shares. The deal was completed in October last year, which finally brought together the software giant and the e-book giant, making Microsoft have enough confidence and confidence to compete with Amazon Kindle.Barnes & Noble flirts with Google to revitalize the Nook businessYes, last Friday, Barnes & Noble announced that it will add the Google Play app store to the Nook series of tablets, allowing users to access more than 700,000 Android apps and games, thereby enhancing the competitiveness of the Nook series of tablets. Barnes & Noble hopes that this move will curb the continuous decline of the Nook business.However, considering the sensitive competitive relationship between Microsoft and Google, Barnes & Noble's upgrade plan will undoubtedly pose a serious challenge to its cooperation with Microsoft. To put it bluntly, where does Barnes & Noble, which is stepping on two boats, leave Microsoft, who has spent money? How can it bear?Did Microsoft become the biggest loser?So far, embedding the Nook app into the Windows 8 operating system is the biggest achievement of the alliance between Microsoft and Barnes & Noble, and that was back in November last year. Embedding the Nook app into the Windows 8 operating system is not as profound and complex as it sounds. It simply uses some built-in features of Windows 8 to achieve the combination of the two, such as integrating the Nook app into the startup menu of the Microsoft operating system. But in fact, any third-party developer can do this, and Microsoft doesn't need to spend $300 million for it.Moreover, the cooperative relationship between Microsoft and Barnes & Noble has lasted for a year, and the Windows Phone has long been on sale, but there are still no signs of Windows Phone applications. It is understood that according to the cooperation agreement between the two parties, Barnes & Noble has the obligation to develop Nook applications for Microsoft's smartphone platform. And developing Nook applications for Windows Phone is time-limited, but we don't know if Barnes & Noble has missed the deadline required by the agreement.Famous IT reporter Todd Bishop once sent letters to relevant representatives of Microsoft and Barnes & Noble to inquire about detailed progress and whether Barnes & Noble's cooperation with the Google Play app store has obtained the consent of the investor Microsoft, but has not received any response so far.Microsoft also has its own plansIt is understood that in order to make up for shortcomings in a certain aspect, Microsoft usually conducts targeted cooperation and investment with other companies. The cooperation agreement with Barnes & Noble was personally signed by Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer, but it is one of Microsoft's series of external cooperation and investments. Last year, Microsoft wrote down $6.2 billion in non-cash assets due to the acquisition of aQuantive. However, Microsoft's investment in Facebook was very successful. Regardless of cash returns, it at least made its backbone much stronger in competing with Google. Although Microsoft and Facebook are not real partners either.Barnes & Noble's unilateral cooperation with Google may make Microsoft dissatisfied, but Microsoft also has its own plans. It is understood that Microsoft is currently developing its own e-book device, which may fully incorporate Nook's e-book content and platform. Microsoft will soon announce its small Windows 8 tablet, and this possibility is becoming increasingly high.The partnership between Microsoft and Barnes & Noble was a mistakeWe must admit that the cooperative relationship between Microsoft and Barnes & Noble was a mistake from the beginning, and they even went to court. Microsoft believes that Android devices infringe on their patents. Barnes & Noble accuses Microsoft of trying to achieve a monopoly in the mobile device market by charging patent licensing fees for Android devices, which is an anti-free competition illegal act.Up to now, perhaps for these two companies, these are all past events, but is this what is called a strategic partnership? It is really sigh-worthy.
