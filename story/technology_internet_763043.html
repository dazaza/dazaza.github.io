Profit triples, valuation at 380 billion yuan, Alibaba paves the way for IPO

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 12:41:18

Author: Zhou Heng

"Despite numerous reports, as of now, we have yet to confirm which market we will definitely list in," a senior executive at Alibaba cautiously told the Shanghai Securities News last night. However, many signs indicate that Alibaba is preparing for its IPO on various fronts, especially in enhancing the company's valuation: taking a stake in Sina Weibo, acquiring AutoNavi, constructing the Cainiao Network, releasing impressive performance figures, and most notably, the launch next week of the "Alibaba Box", which could disrupt traditional shopping and lifestyle entertainment ecosystems, significantly boosting Alibaba's valuation. In response, the executive humorously remarked, "Valuation isn't about being high or low, but whether it's reasonable."

Profit Doubling

And Ali IPO Valuation

Data indirectly disclosed during Yahoo!'s earnings call on Tuesday showed that Alibaba's net profit in the first quarter of this year tripled year-on-year to $669 million. Particularly noteworthy was Alibaba's 48.4% profit margin in the first quarter, making it the most profitable internet company among China's Big Three. This marked the second consecutive fiscal quarter that Alibaba's net profit exceeded Tencent's after last quarter. Public data shows that Tencent's profit in the first quarter was RMB 4.0711 billion (approximately $649.4 million), an increase of 17.3% over the previous quarter. Meanwhile, another domestic internet giant, Baidu, reported a net profit of only $330 million in the first quarter.

This data might bolster Ma Yun's recent commentary on the "Big Three" of the internet industry: "In terms of comprehensive processing and management, we are leading. Tencent excels a bit more in products and engineering, while Baidu leads slightly in search technology."

A market analyst believes that "raising funds through the most profitable business to support the development of new businesses" may be the underlying logic behind Ali's listing. With its five-year-old dream of going public nearing fruition, the ecosystem projects of "Ali Finance" and "Cainiao Network" may still need funding from what is expected to be the world's largest financing exercise. Bloomberg recently aggregated data from eight investment banks and research institutions, estimating a median reasonable valuation of Alibaba at $62.5 billion. If Alibaba issues 20% new shares, based on this calculation, the estimated financing amount would be around $12.5 billion, though the actual amount may far exceed this figure.

A person familiar with the China Development Bank (CDB) based in Hong Kong told the Shanghai Securities News that CDB Group has great confidence in Alibaba, hence actively participating in last year's financing arrangement aimed at repurchasing Yahoo!'s equity, viewing this project as the "last major deal" opportunity in China's internet industry. At that time, Alibaba Group's valuation was approximately $40 billion.

An Alibaba senior official stated that valuation isn't about being high or low, but rather whether it's reasonable. He informed reporters that Alibaba continues to maintain rapid growth. Currently, express deliveries originating from Alibaba account for 70% nationwide. In 2012, the total sales of Taobao and Tmall reached 1 trillion yuan. Alibaba is currently accelerating the construction of various new types of ecosystems integrating "data flow", "logistics", and "capital flow". The hallmark of these systems is "sustainable development". By 2020, the scale of China's "e-commerce economy" is expected to approach the GDP total of China in 2012, forming a powerful new "economy".

The senior official further stated that when evaluating the valuation of this round of Ali's IPO, one should also consider the future growth space of the "e-commerce economy". He opined that ten years ago, Alibaba was just a seed in China's internet landscape; today, this seed has grown into a leading force driving economic transformation in China.

"The e-commerce economy is the engine driving China's economic development," said a senior official at Alibaba Group to the Shanghai Securities News. The process of self-development within the e-commerce economy is also a continuous evolution of the business ecosystem. Taking online retail as an example, from the initial emergence of online merchants and their co-evolution with consumers, to the co-evolution between online merchants, consumers, and e-commerce platforms (such as Taobao and Tmall), then to the co-evolution between online merchants, consumers, platforms, and e-commerce service providers, and finally to the co-evolution between the entire e-commerce application and services and society, what ultimately emerges is a vibrant and dynamic life process of continuous growth and evolution of the business ecosystem.

Alibaba expects that by 2020, the scale of China's e-commerce economy will reach 47.8 trillion yuan, approximately 5.8 times that of 2012, where the scale of e-commerce applications and the scale of e-commerce services will reach 43.8 trillion yuan and 4 trillion yuan respectively. According to National Bureau of Statistics data, China's GDP in 2012 was 52 trillion yuan, meaning that by 2020, the concept of China's e-commerce economy will closely approximate the GDP of China in 2012.

Small Box, Big Dreams

Another factor that could boost Alibaba's valuation is the upcoming launch of the "Ali Box".

In accordance with the scheduled timeline, the "Ali Box" will unveil its mysterious veil at a press conference in Beijing in five days. In the invitation sent by Alibaba Group to the Shanghai Securities News, Ali deliberately downplayed the term "box", preferring to refer to it as a "Smart Life Alliance Strategy Launch" involving numerous brands.

Even before the event, capital markets have already seen a wave of "Ali Concept Stocks". Huasuan Media, which has a partnership with Ali's "Box", welcomed its second trading limit yesterday, while Teda and Tianyao, potentially involved in the first batch of direct sales fund websites, were forcefully sealed at their trading limits near the end of the day due to substantial capital inflows. Even Boreyan Technology, tangentially related to Taobao outsourcing services, surged significantly yesterday.

"The 'boxes' already on the market essentially aim to disrupt street vendors, whereas the Ali Box may disrupt the entire lifestyle ecosystem," commented a person close to Ali Cloud on July 18th. Through the release of the "Box", Ali could impact the consumption habits of the world's largest consumer group regarding entertainment, shopping, and wealth management, inevitably altering almost all aspects of Chinese people's lives.

An experienced internet professional analyzed that the "Box" is not merely an entertainment product or an internet TV application product. Accurately speaking, it is an "interface" between individual users and the external world. Through numerous highly sticky product applications, users can easily achieve personalized applications such as entertainment, socializing, calling taxis, shopping, payment, and wealth management within the box. It could even serve as an entry point for the interactive platform of the C2B (customers post what they want, specify prices, and then merchants decide whether to accept customer offers) business model in the future.

The person indicated that he personally believed that many internet application products invested in and acquired by Ali in recent years, such as AutoNavi, Momo, Kuaidi Dache, Qyer, etc., have the potential to be embedded in the box. Embedding Taobao, Tmall, and Yu'e Bao in the box is equivalent to bringing a large supermarket and convenience store without cement walls and steel structures into your home, allowing you to complete the shopping process effortlessly with a remote control, disrupting the traditional television shopping business model. Even in unfamiliar social networking, the Ali Box holds significant imaginative space. For instance, "Momo on the box might instantly display who else is watching the same TV series as you, unlike the past where appearance and distance were used as the first dimension for social networking."

He believed that the "Box" might just be an anchor point for the interaction of "three flows". Through the efforts of "Cainiao Network" over 5-8 years, once a logistics system capable of delivering within 24 hours to any region across the country is established, the innovations in payment methods by Ali Finance will provide logistical and payment guarantees for the aforementioned data interactions within the "Box". Given that Ali launched the "Box" ahead of its listing, it is foreseeable that this product will greatly enhance Alibaba's valuation.

The Interest Chain of the "E-commerce Economy"

An experienced internet industry analyst told reporters that as long as Ali develops normally and builds a healthy ecological chain, maintaining high-speed growth within the "e-commerce economy" is not a myth.

In his view, Ali is a decentralized and extremely elastic organism. Departments can be split and structures changed without harming the core vitality, which is rare in both the internet and traditional industries. This type of organization, like "mercury spilling," is very adaptable to the rhythm changes described by Jack Ma in the "e-commerce economy" era as "three years here, three years there." Although it may bring shocks to many industries, it also brings change and new benefits to those industries.

Recently, a person close to the upper management of China Asset Management expressed the psychological impact of "Yu'e Bao" to the Shanghai Securities News. As the leader in China's fund industry, China Asset Management has accumulated approximately 1 million proprietary customers (non-bank channel customers) over fifteen years. However, just six days after the launch of Yu'e Bao, it brought over 1 million customers to Tianhong Asset Management, a mid-to-lower ranked fund company, and currently, this number has approached 3 million. The person believed that although individually, Tianhong Asset Management's customer capital volume and customer loyalty far lag behind China Asset Management's proprietary customers, the speed of customer growth is indeed astonishing.

The person believed that with the launch of "Yu'e Bao," Tianhong Asset Management, a small fund company ranked in the middle to lower tier, successfully disrupted the original development rhythm of the fund industry, achieving a "rags-to-riches" story. This is just the beginning of the financial shock waves brought by Ali Finance.

Both Alibaba and Cainiao Logistics have brought new impacts and interest reordering to related industries, also providing new development opportunities for related industries and companies. Recently, apart from Jinzheng Stock gaining widespread attention from the capital market for building the embedded system between Tianhong Asset Management and Yu'e Bao, Boreyan Technology also posted job advertisements on recruitment websites seeking personnel such as "Taobao Seller Certification Review Project Leader." The company had previously provided operational maintenance outsourcing services for Taobao. Additionally, Huace Testing had publicly revealed that it had moved into the Taobao Seller Service Platform. The aforementioned seasoned internet industry analyst stated that the "e-commerce economy" drives the transformation of commercial infrastructure, and increasingly more listed and non-listed companies will participate in the construction and operation of commercial infrastructure. Since 2008, the e-commerce service industry has experienced a massive explosion, preliminarily forming a richly diverse e-commerce service system, with the market size increasing from over 100 billion yuan in 2011 to 246.3 billion yuan in 2012.

From the perspective of China's current development, in the process of integration between the internet and physical entities, e-commerce plays the role of a "connector," gradually infiltrating from front-end marketing and retail into the supply chain links of physical industries, forming an increasingly complex business ecosystem including consumers, retailers, distributors, and service providers. What Ali is building is precisely this "ecosystem."

In Jack Ma's view, "empires" will perish, but ecosystems are "endless." He believes that the key is not how much money we can make from this "ecosystem," but how much money the enterprises within this "ecosystem" can earn here, because "only when they earn more do we have the opportunity to make money."
