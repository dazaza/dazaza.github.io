Should You Cover the Wok When Stir-frying?

When cooking at home, many people tend to cover the wok with a lid, believing that doing so helps the food cook faster while saving time and fuel. In reality, this practice should vary depending on the type of dish being prepared, as some dishes are not suitable for being cooked with the lid on.

When stir-frying vegetables, it is best not to cover the wok. This is because most vegetables contain organic acids, which differ in kind based on the vegetable variety. Common types of organic acids include oxalic acid, acetic acid, and amino acids. Some of these acids are beneficial to the human body, while others can be harmful. During cooking, it's important to remove the harmful organic acids. When preparing vegetables, keeping the pot uncovered and stirring occasionally allows the organic acids to easily evaporate. Additionally, when stir-frying without the lid, green vegetables are better able to retain their original color. When the lid is on, green vegetables tend to turn yellow due to the fact that organic acids in the vegetables will volatilize into the air when the lid is off. However, if the lid is on, these volatile acids return to the vegetable juices, causing magnesium in chlorophyll molecules to be replaced by hydrogen, leading to the de-magnesiation and yellowing of chlorophyll. Therefore, when stir-frying green vegetables, it is advisable not to cover the wok to preserve their vibrant green color.

Most vegetables are best cooked using high heat for a short period, as this method can maximally preserve the nutrients within the vegetables. If the wok is covered during stir-frying, not only will the organic acids find it difficult to evaporate, but the taste of the dish will also suffer. Covering the wok makes it harder to control the level of heat; if the heating time is too short, the vegetables won't be fully cooked, and if the time is too long, they become overly soft, making it difficult to achieve the desired crispness without being raw.

People in Northern China often use radishes to make soup. It's worth noting that after putting the radish into the pot, it's best not to immediately cover it with a lid. Doing so helps release the spiciness, enhancing the flavor of the radish soup.

Of course, whether or not to cover the wok depends on the specific dish being prepared. For some dishes that require longer cooking times, covering the wok is necessary, as it prevents vitamins dissolved in water from escaping with steam.
