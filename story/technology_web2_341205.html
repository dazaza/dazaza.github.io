Young man deceived by high salary recruitment advertisement sells kidney for 100,000 yuan (Photos)

by ztt0288868 on 2010-04-07 14:19:13

A suspect who forced people to sell their kidneys was arrested. When the police found the den of the kidney-selling gang, it was already deserted. The police arrested two suspects who denied forcing people to sell their kidneys. Two outsiders said that this man in black lured them to Zhengzhou with a high salary to sell their kidneys.

An intern named Zhang Yu took the pictures. "Somebody restricted our personal freedom, took us to the hospital for a physical examination, and then offered 100,000 yuan to buy our kidneys. We were very afraid, and just now we ran out while throwing garbage and reported the incident." At 15:05 yesterday, a man ran to the intersection of Yihe Road and Funiu Road, his voice trembling as he reported the incident to 110.

The man reporting the incident was so scared that his whole body was shaking. Ten minutes later, I arrived at the scene. At the southwest corner of the intersection of Funiu Road and Yihe Road, Officer Wu Anchun from the Second Special Patrol Police Brigade was searching a young man dressed in Li Ning clothing and temporarily confiscated his mobile phone. Another group of police officers, together with several patrol team members from the Lvdong Village Office, caught up with another suspect who had fled at the intersection of Funiu Road and Yinghe Road.

At the traffic light at the intersection of Funiu Road and Yihe Road, a trembling young man was being questioned by the police officers from the Lvdong Village Police Station. He was the person who reported the incident, Liu Ping, who registered a company in Shanghai. According to Liu Ping's introduction, he came from Xiangfan, Hubei Province. He was 22 years old and worked as a decorator at home, earning about 2000 yuan a month. A week ago, while chatting online, a netizen with the name "Northern Wolf" told him that there was a company in Henan where workers earned more than 5000 yuan a month and they were recruiting people. "When I heard that, I came," Liu Ping said, looking around fearfully as if someone was going to grab him. Patrol team member Wang Fengsheng told him, "Don't worry, we and the police will protect your safety."

Liu Ping was immediately controlled upon arriving in Zhengzhou. Liu Ping said that after confirming with "Northern Wolf" online that he would come to Zhengzhou, on March 31st at noon, after arriving at the Zhengzhou bus station, someone met him there, which made him feel warm and think that this company was indeed good. The person who met him brought him to a family courtyard behind the Yue Du Hotel at the southeast corner of the intersection of Funiu Road and Yinghe Road, and he was taken to a three-bedroom apartment on the seventh floor.

"At that time, there were seven people in the room, one of whom claimed to be Manager Li. Here, I met Cao Tingtao from Shanxi who was also applying for a job and was about my age. Both Cao Tingtao and I wanted to start working immediately, and they said we could. This morning, he took us to Zhengzhou Renhe Hospital in the north for a physical examination, saying that after the inspection, we could start work. After the blood test in the morning, at noon, they surrounded us and advised us to sell our kidneys, offering 100,000 yuan per kidney, saying that selling one wouldn't harm our health much. We refused, and they locked the door, not letting us leave, and even took away our mobile phones," Liu Ping said.

Taking advantage of taking out the trash, the two managed to escape and report the incident. Liu Ping and Cao Tingtao were very afraid when they heard they were going to have their kidneys removed. Cao Tingtao was relatively smart and kept hinting to Liu Ping not to panic. "It was Cao Tingtao's cleverness that saved me. He told Manager Li that we needed some time to think about it, saying that 100,000 yuan was not a small amount and could do many great things. His words confused those few people, and they relaxed their supervision of us. They even persuaded us, saying the surgery was simple and would only take more than ten minutes. I thought about how painful it would be if someone dug out my kidney, scaring me so much that I wanted to jump out of the window. In the afternoon, after drinking beer, some of them went to rest. Manager Li asked us to eat, and Cao Tingtao kept giving me eye signals. He said to Li first to throw the garbage outside and then come back to eat. While Cao Tingtao was holding the trash can, he seized the opportunity to grab his mobile phone from the table, opened the door, and we ran downstairs," Liu Ping said. They ran along Funiu Road to Yihe Road before daring to report the incident.

The police discovered the suspects while lying in wait. After receiving the order, the officers from the Second Special Patrol Police Brigade immediately rushed to the scene, met the person who reported the incident, and led Cao Tingtao to the vicinity of the Yue Du Hotel at the intersection of Funiu Road and Yinghe Road, waiting in hiding.

Five minutes later, a man hurriedly reached the intersection and looked around. Cao Tingtao identified him as Manager Li. Wu Anchun immediately chased after him, and Manager Li ran eastward. Wu Anchun caught up with him and controlled him. Officer Zhang Jian subsequently controlled another man who came out. The young man dressed in Li Ning clothing, who claimed his surname was Li, said that his home was in Jiyuan and that he had started a small company here but hadn't yet obtained a business license.

Facing the journalist's questioning, Manager Li expressed that he didn't understand what Liu Ping and Cao Tingtao accused him of restricting their personal freedom and forcing them to sell their kidneys. The journalist rushed to the adjacent Zhengzhou Renhe Hospital for inquiries. Staff at the hospital introduced that indeed two youths and their friends came for a physical examination in the morning, but the hospital was unaware of any kidney-selling activities.

The state strictly prohibits the buying and selling of human organs. After questioning the suspects and the person who reported the incident, the police took them away for investigation. The police introduced that a week ago, they also discovered similar individuals on Gangpo Road who used high salaries as bait to lure young people eager for jobs and then forced them to sell their kidneys. Someone reported the incident, and the police dealt with it.

Attorney Li Fenghua from Tianji Law Firm introduced that according to China's "Regulations on Human Organ Transplantation," no organization or individual may engage in any form of buying or selling of human organs, nor may they engage in activities related to the buying and selling of human organs. The recipient of a living donor organ is limited to the spouse, direct lineal relatives, or collateral relatives within three generations of the living organ donor. Or persons who have evidence proving that they have formed a familial relationship due to support or other reasons with the living organ donor. Since there was no fact of trading human organs in this case, the police should handle the suspects on suspicion of illegal detention.
