Messi announced on Weibo that he will sit out the Champions League match to fully prepare for El Clásico.

by edivista on 2011-12-06 16:08:16

Click to enter Messi's Tencent Weibo

Barcelona (Weibo) will face Real Madrid in the El Clásico this weekend, and then they will set off on their FIFA Club World Cup campaign. On the evening of December 5th, Beijing time, Barcelona superstar Lionel Messi updated his Tencent Weibo and QQ Zone. The Ballon d'Or winner stated that before a series of crucial matches, don't forget there is also the UEFA Champions League event. However, in order to prepare for the match against Real Madrid, Guardiola has arranged for him to rest.

As the top football star of today, Messi has become the player under Guardiola with the most playing time this season among non-goalkeeper players. In the weekend game against Levante, Barcelona achieved a big 5-0 victory, with Messi contributing both an assist and a goal. Currently, the Ballon d'Or winner has played in all 15 La Liga games this season, starting 14 of them and playing the full 90 minutes. He played 28 minutes in the match against Real Sociedad, accumulating a total playing time of 1288 minutes. Adding the first five rounds of the Champions League group stage where he played a total of 450 minutes, Messi’s combined playing time across two competitions has reached 1738 minutes, placing him just behind goalkeeper Victor Valdes as the player with the highest attendance rate for Barcelona among non-goalkeepers. Including the UEFA Super Cup, the Spanish Super Cup, and two World Cup qualifiers with the Argentina national team, Messi's total playing time this season has reached 2188 minutes, making his fatigue level quite evident.

Through Tencent Weibo and QQ Zone, Messi informed fans that Guardiola has decided to let him rest during the midweek Champions League match. Messi said: "There is only one day left until the last Champions League game of 2011. I won’t be playing in this game because Pep (referring to Guardiola) prefers to keep me on the bench so we can get some rest before our El Clásico match against Real Madrid this Saturday." Besides Messi, many other Barcelona key players including Xavi, Iniesta, and Villa will also miss the final round of the Champions League group stage. Clearly, Guardiola has shifted his focus to preparing for the match against Real Madrid.

This weekend, the Spanish El Clásico is about to kick off, but Messi reminded fans on Tencent Weibo not to forget the Champions League match against BATE Borisov before the match against Real Madrid. Messi said: "SquétodosquierenquehablemosdeesepartidoperohayquerespetarelcalendarioybiensabemosqueprimeroestaelBorisovyluegosvendrelpartidodelBernabeu./ I know everyone wants us to talk about the Clásico, but we must respect the schedule. We all know that we have to face Borisov first, and then comes the Bernabéu match."

On the evening of December 5th, Beijing time, FIFA announced the list of three candidates for the FIFA Ballon d'Or award this year, with Messi, Xavi, and Cristiano Ronaldo all included. If Messi can lead Barcelona to win the FIFA Club World Cup title, he is very likely to achieve a three-year consecutive win of the Ballon d'Or award. Whether or not the Argentine football king can realize his dream, let's wait and see.
