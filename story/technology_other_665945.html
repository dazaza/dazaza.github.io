Methods for dealing with coke brick situations in rotary kilns

by hx515317 on 2012-02-15 11:38:57

The rotary kiln (rotarykiln), as one of the main industrial drying equipment, has a wide range of applications: in many production industries such as building materials, metallurgy, chemical industry, environmental protection, etc., rotary kilns are widely used to mechanically, physically or chemically treat solid materials. Rotary kilns are extensively utilized in the metallurgical, chemical, and construction industries. Besides firing cement clinker, rotary kilns are also used for firing clay, limestone, and performing slag drying, among other processes. In refractory material production, raw materials are fired in a rotary kiln to achieve dimensional stability and increased strength before being processed into shape. In non-ferrous and ferrous metallurgy, metals such as iron, aluminum, copper, zinc, tin, nickel, tungsten, chromium, and cobalt use rotary kilns as smelting equipment to perform sintering and roasting on ores, concentrates, intermediates, and more.

In a rotary kiln (rotarykiln), the raw materials rise slowly with the kiln wall under the action of frictional force, behaving as a single unit until they reach a certain height where the angle formed between the surface of the material layer and the horizontal plane equals the material's angle of repose. At this point, the particles slide down the material layer under the influence of gravity. Since the rotary kiln (rotary kiln) has a certain degree of inclination, when the raw material particles roll, they descend along the maximum incline direction, thereby moving forward a certain distance. When the raw materials move inside the kiln, the motion pattern of the raw material particles undergoes periodic changes—they either ascend with the material layer or fall from the surface of the material layer. However, it is only during the process of descending along the surface layer that the particles advance along the length of the kiln. The movement of raw materials within the kiln affects their retention time (i.e., heating time) inside the kiln; the filling coefficient of the raw materials inside the kiln (i.e., the heated area); and the situation of particle size turnover, which also impacts the uniformity of the raw materials (i.e., affects the surface temperature of combustion products and raw materials).

For situations where coke bricks appear inside the rotary kiln furnace, the following operational methods can be adopted:

Prevention is key, closely monitoring the fire conditions in the preheating zone. When there is a trend of the fire developing into a large blaze, high-temperature gases should be extracted in advance from the rear of the preheating zone. If there are clear signs of a large fire, the residual heat gate in the firing zone can also be raised to release the fire and reduce the temperature early. Controlling the speed of the fire progression. Regardless of whether there is a residual heat recovery system, reducing the height of each air gate and slowing down the advancement speed of the flame head is beneficial for controlling the intensity of the fire. When there is a large fire in the firing zone, several residual heat gates before and after the large fire can be raised to disperse the fire power. If the residual heat gate at the location of the large fire is raised, it will not only fail to disperse the fire but may instead concentrate the fire, causing over-firing. When there is a large fire at the bottom of the firing zone, first lower the distant and nearby gates. In addition to raising the residual heat gate, increase the air intake to accelerate cooling. Furthermore, cautiously and gradually raise the air gate in the insulation zone to extract the high-temperature air at the bottom. Note that the air gate at the location of the large fire should not be raised because doing so will not only fail to disperse the fire but will instead concentrate the fire, definitely resulting in coke bricks, or even lump formation or kiln collapse accidents.
