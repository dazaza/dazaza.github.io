Is the LED display industry facing a reshuffle?

What problems is the LED display industry encountering?

There are various opinions: Some say that the external environment is poor, citing the deepening of the European debt crisis, the sluggish economies of the U.S. and Japan, and instability in the Middle East, all of which have affected the foreign trade market expansion for LED displays and the prices of LED daylight lamps. Others argue that the industry threshold is too low, lacking standards, resulting in a mix of good and bad companies within the industry. Some new entrants盲目cut prices, reducing industry profit margins and damaging the market. There are also those who claim that domestic LED display companies lack core technologies, leading to product homogenization and a general lack of core competitiveness, making them vulnerable to risks.

All these points are valid, but I believe the fundamental issue lies in the small size of the LED display market.

In recent years, major events such as the Beijing Olympics, the 60th anniversary National Day military parade, the Shanghai Expo, the Guangzhou Asian Games, and the Shenzhen Universiade have seen extensive use of LED displays. These displays not only provided dazzling visual feasts for audiences but also brought the LED display industry into public view.

The applications in government-sponsored important activities have served as excellent demonstrations for market development. In recent years, during urban renovations and infrastructure construction across the country, LED displays have been widely used, driving the prosperity of the LED display industry. Within just a few years, nearly ten thousand LED display-related companies have emerged nationwide. Currently, the main business of these LED display-related companies comes from various projects led by the government.

Under the market economy system, the role of the government in economic development is to promote growth through demonstration and guidance. However, for the LED display industry to truly grow strong, it cannot solely rely on government orders. Instead, it needs to expand market applications based on the characteristics and advantages of LED displays.

The current situation is that the government's guiding work has been effective, but many companies have become dependent on large government-led projects. With limited resources and an increasing number of players, the competition intensifies naturally, leaving some struggling or even failing.

In stark contrast to companies like Jun Duoli and Boluntex, some firms that bravely explore new markets have carved out new territories during the overall downturn of the industry.
