The back-and-forth farce: WeChat laughs, YiXin falls into the pit.

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:54:03

One month after YiXin was launched, LaiWang also made a noisy comeback. Compared with YiXin, which is a collaboration between NetEase and China Telecom and benefits from the operator resources of China Telecom, LaiWang is a more pure OTT product, completely identical to WeChat, only slightly richer in functions. Overall, LaiWang is a high-quality mobile IM product that combines many mobile social functions but lacks its own characteristics. Seeing the new version of LaiWang, WeChat smiled knowingly, while YiXin felt betrayed.

Function Mega Collection: A High-Quality IM with No Distinctive Features

Industry insiders all know that LaiWang is not a new product from Alibaba; it has been quietly developed within Alibaba for a long time. When YiXin appeared and received media praise, Alibaba saw an opportunity in the mobile IM market and offered a 100,000 yuan reward for the first user of LaiWang. More than a month has passed, and it's unclear who won the 100,000 yuan reward, but under the incentive, the new version of LaiWang made a high-profile appearance. The new version of LaiWang has many features, but these features are mostly not original and have been copied from different applications.

View Once and Burn: This feature has recently become popular, copied from the popular foreign photo-sharing application Snapchat; Group chats of 500 people: There is no great technical content in the upper limit of the number of people in mobile IM groups, and WeChat can also achieve this; handQ's group chat function is there; Sound pictures: This is the main feature of domestic mobile social applications like PAPA; Doodle: This is not a fresh feature either, as many photo-taking software, image processing software, and other mobile IMs also have similar functions; Shake to add friends: This is essentially a variant of "Shake"; Group gathering: Similar to the micro-meters function previously launched by Sina; Other free stickers, free network calls, prohibition of forwarding and comments are also not distinctive features.

To put it bluntly, LaiWang is now a high-quality mobile IM product that is a collection of various mobile functions and lacks its own characteristics. Most of the product functions are imitation improvements pieced together from various sources, without its own unique tone. From the perspective of UI interaction and user experience, the product quality is still relatively high. But given the current characteristics of LaiWang, it still has a long way to go to compete with WeChat. Functions can be imitated, user experience can be actively improved, and even if LaiWang is positioned on pure friend interaction, it will be of little use. This is just a concept; there is no such thing as pure friends in social relationships. Many people start playing WeChat planning to only add acquaintances, but eventually end up adding all kinds of people.

Spending 10 million Yuan to promote LaiWang, or even 1 billion Yuan, it's impossible to create another WeChat. If it were before WeChat or at the beginning of WeChat's launch, and Alibaba had the determination to develop a mobile IM, the investment of 1 billion Yuan might have produced some scale. But now it's too late. Users have already formed relationships on WeChat, and it's almost impossible for them to abandon WeChat and switch to LaiWang. The only thing that can be done is to attract users to use LaiWang while continuing to use WeChat. Momo achieved this through positioning, so despite the pressure from WeChat, Momo can continue to move forward; YiXin attracts users with the two distinctive features of free SMS and telephone messages provided by China Telecom. Although LaiWang now has comprehensive functions, it lacks a clear entry point.

Alibaba's continuous efforts in social products deserve praise. Micro TaoBao, Ai TaoBao, Wang Xin, and now LaiWang. Alibaba wants to flourish in multiple areas, but the cost is internal product competition and internal consumption. Even though Alibaba is huge, the resources for user promotion are always limited. Without a doubt, Micro TaoBao is the main product of TaoBao Wireless. Ai TaoBao shoulders the heavy responsibility of SNS transformation for PC TaoBao. Wang Xin is a failed mobile IM with millions of users. Now LaiWang has been pushed to the forefront by Alibaba. Is it given enough support to compete with Micro TaoBao for resources, or left alone to ultimately suffer the same fate as Wang Xin? Micro TaoBao is a rare product from TaoBao that found the right direction in the SNS field. Promoting Micro TaoBao now is a wise and correct decision. LaiWang has missed the best development period, and temporarily giving some financial support to let it entertain itself is more appropriate. If it makes some achievements, it's the ability of the LaiWang team; if money is spent with little effect, that's normal.

Dispersing Users and Media Attention to Slow Down YiXin

Ali letting LaiWang make a fuss pleases WeChat the most. Previously, when YiXin was launched, the media swarmed to compare YiXin with WeChat, instantly elevating YiXin's market status in public opinion, making it a comparison app with WeChat. This is a result YiXin is very happy to see, which greatly helps YiXin's development. Unfortunately, today LaiWang made a high-profile appearance, which will greatly disperse the media's attention on YiXin. This is like LaiWang accidentally grabbing YiXin's leg, making YiXin very uncomfortable.

YiXin's current market performance cannot yet escape the competitive influence brought by LaiWang's appearance. Although YiXin has reached a user base of tens of millions, LaiWang, also backed by a giant, keeps the media impression and user perception of both at the same level. YiXin urgently needs to break out of this situation to prove that it is at the same level as WeChat, rather than being categorized as a newly launched mobile IM product by giants like LaiWang. If it cannot do so, when China Mobile, China Unicom, Baidu, Sina, Sohu, and others also launch similar products, YiXin's market position will rapidly decline, eventually becoming one of the many unsuccessful mobile IM products by giants, ranking alongside FeiXin, FeiLiao, WoYou, YiLiao.

The appearance of LaiWang not only disperses the media's attention on YiXin but also disperses YiXin's potential users. The more similar and equally scaled products there are, the higher the cost for YiXin to acquire users, and the harder it becomes to gather users. The reason is simple: from a product function perspective, LaiWang is no worse than YiXin, so why should users choose YiXin? This principle applies mutually, and vice versa for LaiWang and other giants' IM products. Therefore, the more giants launch mobile IMs, the more advantageous it is for WeChat.

WeChat is pleased to see Alibaba's high-profile promotion of LaiWang, which allows it to escape YiXin's entanglement. The more giants launch similar applications, the happier WeChat becomes. Every industry has a second-place holder, and the mobile IM market needs one. The more giants enter the mobile IM field, the more intense the struggle for the second-place market position becomes, and the more stable WeChat's top market position becomes, allowing it to focus on developing more businesses.

Conclusion

LaiWang's high-profile appearance pits YiXin, delighting WeChat. In fact, the resources and efforts Alibaba invests in LaiWang are far less than what NetEase and China Telecom invest in YiXin, but the media is willing to take LaiWang as a topic. That's how public opinion works, liking to stir up trouble and putting unrelated or indirectly related things together for comparison. YiXin now needs to strengthen its PR promotion, highlight its equal status with WeChat, and shape its image as the perennial runner-up in the market.
