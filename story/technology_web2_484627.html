15 Feng Shui Taboos That Are Most Likely to Be Encountered in New House Renovation

by dd26huiuygui on 2011-09-02 11:21:15

Randomly Altering Rooms

If you're not satisfied with a house you've already purchased, it's best not to make random changes. For example, all doors should not be directly aligned. If the main door is directly aligned with another door, it can cause trouble and create obstacles. In old Beijing courtyards, there were always screens based on this principle. Generally speaking, there should be some form of obstruction at the entrance. The position of this obstruction should align with the clockwise movement of people. If you're really not satisfied, you can compensate for any shortcomings in the room design through spatial design.

Main Door Facing the Stove

The stove is where food is cooked at home. Clothes and food fall under the category of material possessions, which can be exchanged for money or bought with money, so food also falls under the category of wealth, and the stove will affect one's financial luck.

If the food gets burnt and cannot be eaten, naturally the wealth has been burnt away.

In ancient times, kitchens were generally not adjacent to bedrooms; they had a separate room for cooking, hence the term "kitchen." Nowadays, it might be more appropriate to call it "cooking area" since today's kitchens are often in the same room as the bedroom.

It was said that the door should not face the stove, meaning if the main door faces the stove, strong winds could destabilize the flame, affecting the food and thereby the financial luck. It could also cause sparks from the stove to fly around, potentially causing fires and disasters.

For modern homes, as long as the main door does not directly face the stove, it's fine. If someone standing outside the main door can see the stove, it may cause financial issues. If the stove is only visible through the kitchen door, there’s no need to worry. Avoid thinking every corner of your home is unlucky.

Wrong Placement of Protective Objects

The Longquan sword is a good object for warding off evil, but it should only be placed in the study. Do not place it in two places: the bedroom and the living room. A Bagua mirror should not be placed randomly either. If you want to neutralize bad energy, choose a convex mirror, which bulges outward. If your house faces a bank and you wish to absorb its wealth, then place a concave Bagua mirror to attract wealth.

Stove Height Exceeding Window Level

Some say that having a stove behind a window is not ideal, meaning facing the window while cooking. However, many families do cook facing the window, especially those with exhaust fans installed. As long as the stove height does not exceed the window height, it's fine.

If the stove height exceeds the window level, the flame may easily be blown out by wind, affecting financial luck. Even if the flame flickers due to wind, it's problematic. Therefore, the stove height should be lower than the windowsill height, but there's no need to worry about facing the window.

Refrigerator Facing the Stove

The sink should also not face the stove.

If your bedroom is next to the kitchen, be aware that your head or feet should not face the stove. If adjustments are impossible, you can hang a compass on the wall facing the stove inside the bedroom to resolve the issue.

There should be a person sleeping at the height of the bed or the stove height. The compass should be attached to the wall, facing the stove.

Incorrect Placement of Landscapes

When hanging large landscape paintings at home, pay attention to the content of the painting. Most landscapes have waterfalls. There must be a pool below the waterfall to store wealth; otherwise, the waterfall will flush away your wealth, making it better not to hang such paintings.

If the painting depicts a river with boats, remember the boat's bow should point inward, not outward, as an outward-facing boat drains wealth.

Surrounded by Bamboo Groves

A house surrounded by bamboo groves is good if there's support behind and arms embracing on both sides, but not in front. The front should not be blocked by bamboo, obstructing the door and path. The view should open forward.

Trees should not block the main door; blocking the door's orientation is called Dingxin Sha (a type of Feng Shui affliction).

Trees are best planted on the left side of the house, known as the Green Dragon direction.

Tree roots should not enter the house, as they may attract negative energy. Tree branches should not enter the window, as they may cause illness. Windows are like eyes, and tree branches entering the window resemble poking the eye, leading to eye diseases.

Trees should not be too close to the house or too numerous, as they may drain the house's energy.

Generally, avoid planting overly tall trees that block sunlight.

Confusing Main and Secondary Rooms

Some mistakenly believe that larger rooms are always for the master, and smaller ones are for children or elders. This isn't necessarily true. Good Feng Shui requires distinguishing between main and secondary rooms. Choose the best-positioned, well-ventilated, and sunlit room as the master room.

Room size isn't everything; function and orientation are crucial. Don't blindly follow room sizes or developer layouts, labeling rooms as master or secondary without consideration.

Fish Tank Misplacement

Do not place the fish tank incorrectly or put it in the wealth sector of your home.

Various fish, like parrotfish, dragon fish, and angel fish, can be used for aesthetic purposes and to enhance wealth. However, do not place the fish tank in the wealth sector of your home. Placing it there would result in losing wealth rather than gaining it, as the wealth sector is a water outlet.

Where is the wealth sector? A simple way to identify it is the diagonal opposite of the main door when entering your home, or the diagonal opposite of the master bedroom door. Avoid keeping completely black fish for wealth attraction, though they can ward off misfortune. If your black fish dies, it means it protected you from a disaster. Replace it immediately with a similar one.

Choosing Incorrect Plants

Do not choose plants incorrectly, as it can lead to undesirable outcomes.

To enhance wealth and Feng Shui, avoid flowering plants because some do not attract wealth and may cause allergies.

Choose robust, lush plants with large leaves that thrive in sunlight. These plants absorb ample sunlight and release more positive energy indoors, significantly aiding financial luck.

Misplacing Buddha Niches and Deity Positions

Buddhas and deities represent different concepts, though both stem from ancient religions. Buddhas include Sakyamuni, Amitabha Buddha, and various Bodhisattvas like Manjushri, Avalokiteshvara, and Ksitigarbha. Deities are mainly from Taoism, like the Jade Emperor, Sun Wukong, and the Victory Buddha.

Buddhas require niches, which are closed spaces opened only during worship. Deity positions are open. Do not confuse these concepts! If you believe, understand them thoroughly. Do not waver in your beliefs or belief patterns.

Nailing Doors

Many people like nailing things onto the back of doors or walls for convenience, but this is not advisable. Especially avoid nailing things behind doors. If you must hang items, consider using non-damaging hooks.

Poor Air Circulation

To maximize space utilization, we often divide, combine, or even renovate spaces. But never forget to leave windows and ventilation openings.

Remember, Feng Shui requires wind and water for energy flow. Wind enters through windows and doors with light. Do not seal off these areas unnecessarily, confining yourself in a small box. Ensure proper airflow.

Disorganized Clutter

People seeking good Feng Shui often desire wealth and blessings but neglect cleanliness, leading to messy homes. Such individuals cannot achieve wealth or blessings. Someone who doesn't love life or respect their living environment likely lacks good fortune.

Do not overlook clutter at home or ignore corners and hard-to-see areas. Accumulated dust brings stagnant and harmful energies, worsening your home's luck.

Including leaks in the home, some men may be lazy when their wives report leaks, saying they're too busy to fix it and suggesting placing a basin underneath. In reality, this allows wealth to leak out.

Incorrect Door Alignment

Toilet doors should not face bedroom doors, and kitchen doors should not face the main door... Too many misaligned doors can be frustrating. Sometimes, this happens due to structural limitations, and designers suggest slightly misaligning doors, which is extremely wrong!

Either fully realign the doors or keep them perfectly aligned; do not slightly misalign them. If doors face each other, simply make the doors identical in color and size. There's no need for alterations.

Special cases like toilet doors facing bedroom doors can be resolved by converting the toilet door into a sliding door and installing bead curtains inside the sliding door.
