I. Don't Miss the Rapid Growth Period

During the physical development of children and adolescents, when does height increase the fastest? Studies have confirmed that for most Han Chinese children, the peak height growth spurt occurs around age 12 for girls and around age 14 for boys. Over 90% of girls experience their fastest height growth between ages 11 to 13, while for boys, it happens between ages 13 to 15. To help children grow taller, parents should pay special attention to nutrition and exercise during this rapid growth period.

II. Emphasize Nutritional Supplementation

Nutrition is key to the physical growth of children. The energy, protein, and amino acids required for normal physical growth must be supplied by food, mainly from meat, eggs, beans, and bean products. Bone formation also requires sufficient calcium, phosphorus, as well as trace amounts of manganese and iron. Insufficient calcium intake and lack of vitamin D can lead to insufficient bone mineralization. A deficiency in vitamin A causes bones to become shorter and thicker, while a deficiency in vitamin C leads to defects in the formation of bone interstitial substances, making them brittle. All these factors affect bone growth.

Currently, in households with balanced diets that include both meat and vegetables, nutrition should be comprehensive and adequate. Parents should ensure that children do not develop picky eating habits and avoid excessive snacking that could interfere with the intake of essential nutrients.

III. Do Not Neglect Physical Exercise

Physical activity enhances metabolic processes, accelerates blood circulation, promotes the secretion of growth hormones, and stimulates bone tissue growth, which benefits overall height increase. Below are several exercises that may help with height growth:

1. Hanging Swings: Using a bar or doorframe at a height where your feet barely touch the ground, grip the bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, keep feet together, and swing the body back and forth without excessive amplitude or duration. It's best to practice this every morning, keeping the body relaxed and hanging for 20 seconds. Young men should do 10-15 repetitions, while young women should do 2-6 repetitions.

2. Jumping Touches: Jump up to touch pre-set objects such as tree branches, basketball hoops, or ceilings. Perform 30 double-foot jumps, rest briefly, then perform single-foot jumps on each side using the same method.

3. Ball Activities: While playing basketball, aggressively grab rebounds and jump to intercept balls; while playing volleyball, jump frequently and focus on spikes and blocks; in soccer, practice jumping headers.

4. Jump Training: Include one-legged hops, frog jumps, triple jumps, multi-jumps, and vertical jumps in place.

IV. Do Not Forget to Create a Good Living Environment

Abnormalities in the nervous system can also affect the physical growth of children. For example, in cases of parental divorce, abnormal relationships between children and guardians, frequent abuse, or other severe stressors, growth rates may gradually slow down, resulting in heights below the normal range for peers of the same age and gender. Short stature may exacerbate a child's sense of inferiority due to psychological and social stimuli affecting the transmission of neural impulses from the cerebral cortex to the hypothalamus, thus inhibiting the secretion of growth hormone by the pituitary gland. When the child’s environment improves, growth rates may return to normal.

V. Do Not Forget Active Prevention and Treatment of Chronic Diseases

Chronic diseases during childhood and adolescence should be actively treated. Long-term conditions such as chronic infections, hepatitis, nephritis, asthma, heart disease, and anemia can all impact growth and development. Genetic skeletal diseases like chondrodysplasia can also limit bone growth.

Is a person's height predetermined by genetics, or does it depend on postnatal efforts?

What can developing children do to potentially stand taller?

Assessing Growth Rate through Bone Age

The gradual increase in height during human growth is the result of bone growth and development, primarily reflected in the growth of lower limb bones and vertebrae.

The growth of lower limb bones results from the growth plates of the femur and tibia. Located at both ends of the bones, the growth plates are stimulated by growth hormones secreted by the pituitary gland, continuously producing cartilage. This new cartilage calcifies to form hard bones, causing the bones to lengthen and widen.

From birth to age one is the fastest-growing stage of a child's life. Then the speed slows down until puberty, when sex hormones and growth hormones interact, leading to rapid increases in height and weight.

Dr. Wang Zhihong of the Department of Orthopedics at National Taiwan University Hospital pointed out that a person's height progress should be assessed alongside "bone age" (the age of skeletal growth and development), not just chronological age. Some early-maturing children have faster bone age progression than their actual age, initially appearing taller than peers of the same age, but this doesn't necessarily mean they will be taller as adults.

Our height depends on when the growth plates close. Once closed, it indicates skeletal maturity, bones stop growing, and height no longer increases.

What Factors Influence a Person's Height?

Genetics Determines Your Height

The main determinant of a child's height is genetics.

"The correlation between parents' height and their children's height is as high as 80%, with each parent contributing equally," said Dr. Wang Zhihong of the Department of Orthopedics at National Taiwan University Hospital. Therefore, if neither parent is tall, the child's height generally won't be particularly outstanding.

However, there are exceptions. Mr. Yang, who works in construction, is only about 160 cm tall, and his wife is also short, yet their two sons broke genetic limits, one exceeding 180 cm and the other over 170 cm, humorously referred to by friends as the "genetic mutation" masterpiece of the Yang couple.

For such special cases, Dr. Li Junxian, the attending physician in pediatrics at Shin Kong Memorial Hospital, pointed out that human height is not determined by a single specific gene. Most people simultaneously possess genes for both tallness and shortness. Whether a person grows tall or short mainly depends on which gene is expressed, "So sometimes within a family, one child grows tall while another remains short," explained Dr. Li Junxian.

However, doctors believe that cases where the height difference between parents and children exceeds ten centimeters are rare. Most children still resemble their parents in build after development.

After reading this, many parents of average height might be greatly disappointed. If genes determine everything, what can postnatal efforts achieve?

Actually, besides genetic influences, other related factors cannot be overlooked. "If genes determine you can grow to 160 cm, but due to malnutrition and insufficient exercise, you may not reach this height," believes Dr. Li Junxian. Postnatal efforts are equally important.

Nutrition and Exercise Help You Grow Taller

Human growth is a comprehensive manifestation. Besides the inherent limitations of genes, the following factors also influence a child's future height:

Nutrient Intake

Adequate and balanced nutrition is absolutely crucial for helping children grow taller.

According to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)'s recommendations, essential nutrients for children to grow taller and stronger include proteins, calcium, Vitamin A, Vitamin C, Vitamin D, magnesium, and zinc.

Proteins are fundamental substances for constructing and repairing human muscles, bones, and various tissues. Protein deficiency can lead to developmental delays, causing muscle and bone atrophy. Animal-based foods like meats, seafood, and milk provide complete proteins, while plant-based sources can be obtained from beans, grains, and nuts.

Calcium serves as the raw material for making bones and can promote growth while increasing bone density. Drinking two glasses of milk daily is a good way for children to accumulate bone mass.

Vitamin D is another nutrient that strengthens bones. It can be obtained from milk, salmon, tuna, and other fish. Additionally, spending 10-15 minutes in the sun daily allows the body to produce Vitamin D naturally.

Zinc is a particularly important nutrient for infant development. Dutch research found that insufficient zinc intake during infant development can lead to poor development outcomes. Moreover, according to multiple foreign studies, adequate zinc intake in children can reduce diarrhea occurrences. Foods rich in zinc include meats, liver, seafood (especially oysters), eggs, and wheat germ.

"Iron is also crucial for growth and development," added Ms. Weng Huiling, a nutritionist at National Taiwan University Hospital. However, according to the Ministry of Health's national nutritional status survey, females aged four and above generally have iron deficiencies, while males have higher iron deficiency rates during puberty and after age 65. Thus, growing children should consume lean meats, animal livers, egg yolks, or dark green vegetables to obtain sufficient iron.

Movement

Many believe that rope skipping and basketball contribute to height growth, and indeed, medical perspectives support this view.

Studies show that excessive pressure on the growth plates can cause delayed growth, whereas moderate and intermittent compression or stretching stimulates growth.

Therefore, children and teenagers wanting to grow taller should engage in physical activities, especially those involving jumping. However, excessive weight training like gymnastics or weightlifting hinders growth, so it's advised to avoid these during the growth phase.

Hormone Secretion

Various hormones secreted in the human body dominate or influence our growth and development, such as growth hormones, sex hormones, thyroid hormones, parathyroid hormones, and insulin.

Sleep

As mentioned earlier, human growth is primarily influenced by growth hormones, which are secreted abundantly during nighttime sleep. Therefore, to grow taller, it's essential not to sacrifice sleep time by staying up late and to go to bed before 11 PM whenever possible.

Disease

Congenital heart, kidney, lung, and liver diseases, bone diseases, endocrine and metabolic disorders, chromosomal abnormalities such as Turner syndrome, etc., all affect normal growth and development.

Besides a few individuals being shorter due to familial genetic factors, malnutrition-induced stunted growth can be improved by nutritional supplementation. However, if growth abnormalities are caused by diseases, early medical treatment is necessary.

Many parents struggle to determine whether their child's growth is normal, sometimes worrying excessively and forcing their children to eat more or seeking out "height-increasing" secrets.

Doctors suggest parents can assess their child's growth situation in two ways:

Measure height every six months.

Under normal circumstances, children grow at least 5-6 cm per year before puberty. If a child grows less than 4 cm in a year, parents should take them to a pediatrician for evaluation to understand the reasons for growth retardation.

Refer to the growth curve chart in the "Child Health Handbook."

If a child's height is below the 3rd percentile for their age group, it is considered pathological short stature requiring examination and treatment. Conversely, if the growth curve exceeds the 97th percentile for the age group, it may also be abnormal and requires confirmation through examination.

Psychological Factors

Excessive stress can disrupt endocrine functions, leading to insufficient secretion of growth hormones and limiting growth. Additionally, too much stress can cause gastrointestinal dysfunction, reducing appetite and absorption ability. Over time, this leads to malnutrition, making it difficult to grow taller.

Secrets to Eating for Height and Strength

Although genetic limitations necessitate "hearing the heavenly mandate," doctors and nutritionists believe that focusing on diet combined with regular exercise is the most effective "human effort" method.

Parents simply need to grasp a few dietary secrets to make it easy for children to grow taller and stronger.

Adhere to the principle of balanced diet.

Teenagers grow quickly and increase their activity levels. To provide the energy and nutrients needed for growth and development, their caloric needs are relatively high. Boys need to consume 2150-2650 calories per day, while girls need 2100-2200 calories.

Parents should set an example by not being picky eaters.

Research shows that children's eating habits are most significantly influenced by their parents. Ms. Weng Huiling, a nutritionist at National Taiwan University Hospital, believes that not being picky eaters is the basic principle for children to grow well. Parents should vary the dishes in three meals to let children try various foods from a young age. With a rich variety of foods consumed, there's no need to worry about lacking the nutrients needed for growth.

Avoid negative evaluations of food in front of children.

For example, saying "Bitter melon is so bitter, would you dare to eat it?" can leave a lasting impression on a child, becoming a reason for them to refuse certain foods. Of course, food should never be used as a reward or punishment for children.

Teach children the concept of balanced diet.

In modern dual-income families, most meals rely on dining out. Many parents give their children money to buy lunch themselves. Nutritionist Ms. Weng Huiling suggests that parents pay more attention to their children's self-service eating situations and instill healthy eating concepts in them, such as eating less fried foods, choosing a balanced and varied selection of dishes, and consuming all six major food groups. "If parents cannot control their children's eating habits, it can easily lead to poor nutrition in the long term," she emphasized.

Avoid letting children become overweight or lose weight through dieting.

Dr. Li Junxian of Shin Kong Memorial Hospital pointed out that obese children, due to excessive calorie, fat, and sugar intake, experience accelerated bone age progression compared to their actual age. Although they may initially appear taller than other children, their growth period is shortened and compressed, so they may not end up as tall in the future.

Preventing children from becoming overweight is simple: limit their consumption of high-calorie foods like hamburgers and French fries, reduce sugary drinks, and combine with regular exercise to avoid obesity problems.

Additionally, Ms. Weng Huiling believes that modern teenage girls, influenced by the slim figures of idol stars, continue to diet even if their weight is normal, missing out on opportunities to grow taller and develop fully, severely impacting their health in adulthood.

Which exercises are beneficial?

To grow taller, what kind of exercise should be performed? Often, we hear about children joining basketball clubs at school to grow taller. Does playing basketball really make you taller?

Simply put, any exercise that enhances appetite, promotes sleep, and applies a certain degree of longitudinal pressure to the bones is beneficial for height growth. Specifically, jogging, skipping rope, dancing, playing basketball, and playing volleyball are examples. However, many basketball and volleyball players are originally tall. If you merely want to grow taller and force a child who dislikes these sports to participate, you are mistaken. Emotional stability is also important for growth, so let your child choose sports they enjoy. However, intense exercises that excessively deplete physical strength are better suited for high school students whose bodies have stopped growing. Bone development benefits from longitudinal pressure, but excessive pressure (like weightlifting) can hinder longitudinal bone growth.

How can short-statured individuals grow taller?

For short-statured teenagers, except for those whose short stature is caused by diseases and must be handled seriously by doctors, most need to rely on their own efforts to tap into their potential for height growth. How to tap into this potential? In short, under the guidance of doctors and sports professionals, adjust diet and lifestyle, and persist in specialized sports training.

Regarding adjusting diet and lifestyle, the following suggestions can be considered:

1. Adjust diet reasonably, avoid picky eating and binge eating, ensuring adequate nutrition while practicing moderation. No smoking or drinking;

2. Have a regular lifestyle, get sufficient and timely sleep, preferably sleep on a hard mattress with a pillow no higher than 5cm;

3. Pay attention to personal health, prevent illness when healthy, and treat illnesses early. Read books about short stature research and height growth and development. If unclear, consult a doctor to increase knowledge and use science to guide actions;

4. Maintain mental and physical health, enrich cultural and entertainment life, maintain emotional stability, and remain carefree for optimal growth and development.

Recently, authors have reported that continuous 1-2 hours of moderate sports can significantly increase growth hormone levels in the body within a certain period. As blood growth hormone levels increase, it activates the growth zone of tubular bones, thereby increasing height.

It should be considered that regardless of whether the exercise involves smaller movements (such as jogging, slow swimming, etc.) or larger movements (such as marathon running, long-distance skiing, etc.), neither will increase height. The former lacks stimulation, while the latter weakens the body (for example, performing a thousand jumps in a day and excessive heavy barbell lifting exercises can even hinder the growth of long bones). Success depends on the correct combination of different exercises.

Some scholars believe that long-term activities such as jumping, hanging, and swimming can indeed promote height growth. We believe that short-statured children and teenagers whose epiphyses have not fused, if not diagnosed with short stature diseases by doctors and without other medical contraindications, should bravely begin specialized sports training to increase height. However, it's worth mentioning that entering training should be gradual, especially avoiding overdoing it in the initial stages. Pay attention to how the child feels, and consult doctors and physical education teachers if discomfort arises.

Grip the horizontal bar tightly with both hands, allowing the body to hang freely. The ideal suspension height is when the toes lightly touch the ground. Then perform pull-ups. Boys can do 10-15 repetitions daily, while girls can do 2-5 repetitions. The key to practice is exhaling during pull-ups and inhaling while slowly descending. After finishing the practice, walk around to relax the muscles. Once the hands are relaxed, clench the fists tightly, place the hands on the chest, then release the fingers, followed by closing the eyes, opening the mouth, smoothing the eyebrows, and relaxing the facial muscles. Lie down afterward to relax the back and arm muscles. In summary, learn to exert maximum force and achieve maximum relaxation.

After practicing the suspension method for some time, you can increase the difficulty by first suspending for 20 seconds, then attaching 5-kilogram sandbags to each leg and suspending again for 20 seconds. Next, secure yourself with a belt on the bar and suspend for 15 seconds, then wear a 10-kilogram iron sand vest and suspend again for 15 seconds. However, the time and weight are not absolute and can vary depending on the individual.
