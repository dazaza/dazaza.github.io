A good residence that can hide wind and gather energy must have three basic conditions: a good environment location, a good construction layout, and an imposing presence. The Yellow Emperor's Classic of Architecture says: "A house is the pivot of yin and yang, and the model of human ethics. Without the broad knowledge of sages, no one can understand this way." Therefore, when choosing a residence, one should have keen eyesight, carefully investigate, make meticulous judgments, and pay special attention to the following issues.

1. Environment and Location

The environment and location of a residence are very important. Some locations make people feel comfortable and relaxed as soon as they arrive because they have a bright hall in front for wealth, a cultural star behind for official luck, and auspicious harmony. This is because a good residence has a good orientation, smooth and convenient traffic, a good environmental location, and a proper layout with reasonable combinations, wide views, good lighting and ventilation, full of vitality. However, the following eleven locations should not be used to build or purchase residential properties:

- Beside an overpass, because the traffic around the overpass is inconvenient, and the noise and vortex airflow generated by high-speed vehicles may harm the residents, which will also greatly disrupt the feng shui and fortune of the building.

- Beside a major intersection, because the influence generated by the intersection is similar to that of an overpass, and it also causes dust and makes living there uncomfortable.

- Near high-voltage power towers or radio/TV towers, because these places generate strong electromagnetic waves. If the human body is exposed to such strong electromagnetic waves for a long time, the nervous system and immune system may be damaged, potentially causing various serious diseases.

- Beside a gas station, where fire hazards exist and vehicle noise is loud, making residents irritable.

- Opposite glass curtain walls, living in the reflection of a glass curtain wall creates a sense of oppression, and sunlight reflection forms light pollution, which is very detrimental to health.

- A balcony or master bedroom directly facing a road in a straight line, otherwise the house would be affected by the road's sha qi (negative energy), which is harmful to health.

- A balcony or master bedroom directly facing the corner or sharp angle of nearby buildings, like a wedge driven into the center of the residence, called a "sharp blade sha" in Feng Shui, making residents feel unbearable, affecting their mood and health.

- Residences built near railways, trains travel at high speeds, generating strong air flow vortices and whistles, disturbing peace and being detrimental to health.

- Surrounding buildings are all very tall while one’s own residence is small, blocking outward views, making residents feel confined, severely impacting development.

- Balconies and windows cannot face cemeteries or factory chimneys, otherwise, the psychological state of residents would be significantly affected, possibly causing frequent nightmares and being detrimental to health.

- Residences should not be too close to loose mountainsides, because heavy rain could cause landslides, leading to serious consequences.

To choose a good residential site, one must follow four principles: understanding nature, utilizing nature, transforming nature, and adapting to nature. One must not be careless but carefully examine the surrounding environment to have a clear understanding and confidence.

2. Layout Coordination

It says: "A house is considered its form as the body, springs as blood vessels, land as skin, grass and trees as hair, houses as clothing, and doors and windows as belts. If so, everything appears dignified and elegant, thus highly auspicious."

This is ancient people personifying houses, indicating that appropriate layout coordination is very important for both houses and people. Why do some houses feel refreshing and invigorating upon entering, while others feel oppressive and stuffy? This lies in the difference between superior and inferior layouts. A spacious and well-lit layout with coordinated arrangements is the best choice for a home. However, the following types of residences should be avoided:

- The main door directly faces an elevator or staircase, which is considered inauspicious. Originally, a residence is a place to gather energy and nurture life, but now the vital energy inside the house is entirely absorbed by the elevator or staircase, which is a great taboo. A remedy is to use a screen or entrance partition to separate the area.

- The main door and balcony are in a straight line, requiring separation by a screen or entrance partition. Because of the transparency from front to back, a proverb says: "Front and back being transparent leads to loss of both people and wealth." Additionally, through-drafts can easily cause illness.

- If the main door directly faces a long corridor, this is also considered inauspicious. The longer the corridor, the more unfavorable it is for the home. This is called the "penetrating sword" layout. If there is no screen to block inside the door, it is unsuitable for living.

- If the house windows open towards a corridor, it belongs to a leaking energy layout, losing privacy and being unfavorable for the home. Moreover, too many windows leak energy, and too few windows lack energy, both being inauspicious and needing adjustment.

- The main door should not directly face a toilet door. Imagine entering and immediately seeing a toilet; what function does the residence serve? Additionally, the bedroom door should not directly face the toilet door, nor should one bedroom door directly face another. These all violate the principle of door alignment.

- If the toilet door connects to the kitchen door, the toilet door should always be kept closed to avoid contamination from dirty air.

- In bedrooms, living rooms, or dining rooms, beams should never press down on beds or seats. The ceiling height should preferably be higher rather than lower.

- Inside the residence, there should not be too many sharp corners. Many modern high-rise apartments have diamond-shaped living rooms with sharp corners appearing, not only bringing sha qi (negative energy) but also disrupting the harmonious unity of the living room. If such a situation occurs, it is advisable to fill the empty corners with wooden cabinets or low cabinets. Alternatively, placing a large and lush evergreen plant in the sharp corner can reduce the impact of the sharp corner on the feng shui of the living room.

3. Bedroom Layout

The bedroom is the place where one spends a third of their lifetime, gathering energy and nurturing spirit. There is no specific size requirement for a bedroom, as the saying goes: "An elegant room need not be large, and flowers need not be numerous if they are fragrant." However, the layout must be carefully considered to create a stable, peaceful, warm, and harmonious environment, which is the ideal living condition. Several points need attention:

- The bed is the most important furniture in the bedroom. Generally, the preferred orientation for the bed is north-south, aligning with geomagnetic gravity, but for some houses and individuals, east-west orientation may be better.

- There should be no beams pressing down on the bed in the bedroom, as this can cause a sense of oppression and harm physical and mental health.

- The head of the bed should not face the door. Additionally, placing the bed next to the entrance is also a taboo.

- Mirrors in the room should not reflect the pillow position, as this can disturb sleep and lead to lethargy.

- The bedside table should be slightly higher than the bed, enhancing sleep quality and wisdom, improving sleep quality.

4. Internal Decoration and Placement

The Yellow Emperor's Classic of Architecture states: "A house is the foundation of people. People take houses as homes, living comfortably leads to family prosperity. If not comfortable, then the family declines." Therefore, indoor decoration should pay attention to the following points:

1. If the living room is dark, wall hangings or paintings can be added to compensate for defects. For example, peony flowers or sunflowers can collect positive energy, and hanging paintings should have bright and positive content, avoiding isolated objects. If there are landscape paintings hung in the living room, observe the water flow towards the house, not outwards. Mountains represent population and water represents wealth; water flowing inward brings treasure, water flowing out loses wealth. Boat paintings should have the bow facing the house, not outside. Facing outward damages wealth and population, while facing inward brings wealth and treasure, meaning a full return.

2. Wall clocks are necessary in some entryways and rooms, especially in comprehensive offices, as hanging clocks in these places can have five functions: attracting wealth, warding off evil, assisting the host's fortune, managing subordinates, and timing. However, if the clock placement is not correct, it can be ineffective. The front of the clock should not face inward but should face the door or balcony direction.

3. Keeping goldfish at home. Goldfish are often called feng shui fish, compensating for deficiencies in home feng shui and filling the house with vitality, stimulating life force, but the size of the fish tank must be moderate, with no other clutter around, not directly facing the stove position because the stove position belongs to fire, conflicting with water. Dead fish should not be present, and no wealth god statues should be placed above the fish tank.

4. Green plants should be placed indoors. The living room can have lucky bamboo, money trees, etc., symbolizing vigorous life, producing oxygen, beneficial to the dwelling. Fresh flowers year-round are not necessary, but the plants must remain green and vibrant. Withered plants should be replaced promptly to maintain vitality in the home.

5. Regardless of whether the room is occupied, the windows and doors should be regularly opened and closed to allow energy flow and fresh air circulation, achieving "flowing water does not rot, and a moving hinge does not rust." Additionally, the door gaps should not be too large to prevent leakage of wealth energy.

6. If the house is small, the furniture in the room should not be too large, and the hanging mirrors should not be too large, considering balance.

7. Some families like using Eight Trigram mirrors, wind chimes, etc., without understanding the rules, so these items should not be used randomly to avoid harm. If there are flaws in the layout, it is best to resolve them through renovation or plants.

8. Room colors: White, yellow, blue, and green are excellent choices for room colors, wood tones are also acceptable, black should be avoided, and gray should be used sparingly. Ceiling colors should be light rather than heavy, as the primordial universe was originally chaotic. Later, it differentiated into two energies, with the clear ones rising to form heaven and the turbid ones sinking to form earth, hence the distinction between heaven and earth. The living room ceiling symbolizes "heaven," and its color should be light and clear. Light and clear refer to lighter and softer colors; generally, white, pale yellow, and light blue are the main colors, symbolizing a bright blue sky, with white symbolizing drifting clouds.

Five, Ten New Elements for Choosing a Residence

In recent years, the design and supporting facilities of new-type residences have been changing every year. Based on my observations and research on new-type residences in recent years, the following aspects should be noted when choosing a residence and interior decoration:

1. Residences must have eight functions. Current designs cannot have fewer than eight functions: living room, dining room, kitchen, bathroom, sleeping area, storage, work/study area, and balcony. For developers, if certain functions are missing, the house will be hard to sell. For buyers, if any function is missing, the house will be difficult to retain value.

2. Apartments should become more apartment-like. Small high-rise and multi-story residences are increasingly developing towards apartment-like structures, meaning fewer households per floor. Currently, most have two households per floor, up to three. More refined designs have only one household. Such layouts often use unit combination methods, satisfying each family's privacy while ensuring sufficient lighting and ventilation.

3. Facades should follow a three-part design. Building facades should adopt a three-part design: bottom, middle, and top. The bottom walls use deep brown stripes. Most three-part facades have vertical and horizontal lines decorations, and more refined ones use materials like glass, cast iron, tiles, stainless steel, as well as air conditioning panels and recessed windows, giving a strong modern texture and rich layers.

4. Duplexes should become miniaturized. Duplexes have been popular for many years, and this year there is a trend toward smaller duplexes. In the past, duplexes were at least 200 square meters, but now the total area is controlled, resulting in "mini duplexes" of 150 to 180 square meters. Many high-rise residences have duplex structures on the ground and top floors, and some entire buildings are duplex.

5. South-facing balconies are mostly used for activities or flower pots, while north-facing balconies have become increasingly multifunctional in recent years. They not only serve as auxiliary spaces for kitchens but also start to consider placing washing machines. Some even design the north-facing balcony as semi-closed, giving the washing machine an "indoor" space.

6. Storage rooms have been widely adopted but were previously mostly simple semi-closed layouts. Now, storage rooms are designed as fully enclosed independent entry spaces, akin to regular small rooms. Some even consider the internal design of the storage room, making it more aesthetically pleasing and practical.

7. Walls have concave and convex separations. Indoor walls have always been flat, but now concave and convex separation walls appear. Buyers can embed furniture or bookshelves, and some cleverly decorate them with rich natural displays using the concave and convex separations.

8. Living rooms currently divide the hall into "guest" and "dining" functions. The living room and dining room are staggered separately, not in a straight line. The pistol handle design of the living room has been widely used. The living room faces south with a sliding door balcony, the dining room faces north with a workstation, and some elevate the living room by 20 to 30 centimeters, creating a "difference", making the hall layout richer and more lively, distinguishing the two functions of the hall.

9. Compact small units with three bedrooms and one living room are still in high demand recently, but the total area is trending downward. Many houses control the area of three-bedroom-one-living-room units within 110 square meters, known as "compact three-bedroom". On one hand, it avoids raising the total price due to excessively large rooms, and on the other hand, it considers the trend of children growing up and living separately. Currently, there is also a growing demand for one-bedroom-one-living-room units due to the specific living needs of the urban youth generation.

10. Neighborhood artification. The environment of neighborhoods is increasingly becoming "artistic". Many developers deliberately pursue integration of architecture and scenery, natural terrain and artificial attractions. At the same time, they try to expand green spaces and ensure mutual response and identifiability between different buildings. Additionally, with the increase in income, the number of people buying cars is rapidly increasing, so parking spaces within residential areas must be sufficient, otherwise it will cause a lot of trouble for residents. This has attracted much attention from many developers and home buyers.

Ten Principles of Modern Feng Shui

What is Feng Shui theory? In fact, it is a natural science that integrates geography, geology, astrology, meteorology, landscape studies, architecture, ecology, and human life information studies. Its purpose is to carefully and comprehensively examine and understand the natural environment, adapt to nature, moderately utilize and transform nature, create a good living and survival environment, gain the best timing, location, and harmony, and achieve the ultimate realm of harmony between heaven and humanity. Based on this pursuit, during the long-term development of Feng Shui theory and practice, a wealth of practical experience has been accumulated, and through theoretical thinking, it has absorbed and integrated the wisdom of various sciences, philosophies, aesthetics, ethics, religions, customs, and many other aspects, ultimately forming a unique theoretical system with rich connotations, strong comprehensiveness, and systematicness—modern Feng Shui.

Summarized, there are ten principles.

1. Holistic System Principle

Holistic system theory, as a complete science, emerged in this century; as a primitive method, China's early philosophers began applying it. Feng Shui thought regards the environment as a holistic system, centered around humans, including heaven and earth, all things. Each subsystem in the environment is interconnected, interdependent, mutually restrictive, opposing, and convertible elements. The function of Feng Shui is to macroscopically grasp and coordinate the relationship between systems, optimize structure, and seek the best combination. Feng Shui fully considers the overall environment. The Yellow Emperor's Classic of Architecture advocates "using form and momentum as the body, spring water as the veins, land as the skin, grass and trees as the hair, houses as clothes, and doors and windows as belts. If so, things are strict and elegant, thus extremely auspicious." In the Qing Dynasty, Yao Yanluan emphasized overall functionality in "Yangzhai Jicheng," advocating "Yangzhai must choose terrain, backed by mountains and facing water, suitable for the heart, mountain bones coming from the dragon, beautiful and flourishing, water must surround and embrace in a circular shape, a wide open hall is blessed, water mouth collecting accumulates thousands of gold. No obstacles in the two directions of Guan Sha, brightness is right in the prosperous courtyard." The overall principle is the general principle of Feng Shui, and other principles belong to the overall principle. Handling the relationship between humans and the environment according to the overall principle is the basic point of modern Feng Shui.

2. Adaptation to Local Conditions Principle

Adapting to local conditions means adopting a lifestyle suitable for nature based on the objectivity of the environment. The Book of Changes proposes: "Suitable shape and stop." During the pre-Qin period, Jiang Taigong advocated adapting to local conditions. The Records of the Grand Historian records: "Taigong Wang was enfeoffed in Yingqiu, where the land was salty and barren, and the population was sparse. Thus, Taigong encouraged female labor, developed skills, and promoted fishing and salt production." China's vast territory has significant climatic differences, varying soil types, and different architectural forms. In the northwest, where it is dry and rainy, people live in cave dwellings. Cave dwellings mostly face south, are easy to construct, save land, conserve materials, are fireproof and cold-resistant, keep warm in winter and cool in summer, promote longevity, and increase egg production in chickens. In the southwest, where it is humid and rainy, with many insects and animals, people live in stilt houses made of bamboo. The Old Book of Tang records: "There are poisonous herbs, leeches, and snakes in the mountains; people live in elevated houses, climbing ladders to enter, known as 'gan lan.'" Underneath the houses, animals are kept, and people live upstairs. Bamboo houses provide good ventilation, stay cool, and prevent dampness, often constructed near mountains and water. Additionally, nomadic herders in the grasslands use Mongolian yurts as their homes, allowing them to move with the water and grass. People in Guizhou's mountains and Dali use stones to build houses. These architectural forms are established based on specific local conditions at the time. Many existing Chinese buildings are exemplary models of adapting to local conditions. Hubei Province's Wudang Mountain is a famous Taoist scenic spot. Initially, Ming Chengzu Zhu Di sent 300,000 people to build temples on the mountain, commanding that the mountain not be split or rebuilt, but only walled and constructed according to the terrain. China is a pragmatic country, and adapting to local conditions reflects this pragmatic thought. Taking effective measures based on actual circumstances allows people and buildings to suit nature, return to nature, revert to simplicity, and achieve harmony between heaven and humanity, which is the essence of Feng Shui.

Without water, humans cannot survive. Archaeological discoveries show that almost all primitive tribes lived on river terraces, which suited their hunting and fishing economy.
