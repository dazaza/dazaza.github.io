Forward A little misunderstanding destroyed everything (Lovers should take a look)

by sakq9015h on 2010-04-11 13:04:05

When the girl and boy were in love, the girl didn't use the Internet. The boy helped her apply for a QQ number and taught her how to chat. The password was set by the girl herself and she didn't tell the boy, even though the boy had asked her countless times. She always said that it was her own thing and no one else should know. The boy tried every method but still couldn't find out the password.

Gradually, their relationship seemed to grow distant. The girl thought the boy didn't care about her as much as before, while the boy thought that after the girl learned to go online, she wasn't as innocent as before. He believed that there must be many boys pursuing her on QQ.

In this way, the relationship between the boy and the girl changed subtly every day. The boy no longer showed as much concern for the girl as he used to. He wouldn't wait outside for more than an hour just to see her, wouldn't call her countless times every day, longing to hear her voice every minute and know her situation, wouldn't tolerate her tantrums with infinite patience, and so on. Because the boy thought that on the Internet, there must be more boys giving her these things.

Finally, the girl could no longer bear the changes and proposed a breakup. She thought the boy would try to keep her, but unexpectedly, the boy only said, "Ai Lu Wei (a Chinese phrase expressing regret), then let's break up." The girl felt her heart completely frozen. She turned around without shedding tears, but the boy also turned around and shed tears. He silently said, "Maybe she has found her happiness. I hope she is happy."

Afterwards, apart from no longer being a couple, nothing else changed between them. They could still chat happily on QQ.

An accident happened, and the girl died in a car crash. The boy didn't know this news at the first time. No one informed the boy of the girl's funeral. When the boy found out, the girl's friends were very angry and confronted him, asking why he didn't show up. The boy was dumbfounded upon hearing the news. The girl's friends said that the boy didn't understand the girl well enough because the girl had told them that the boy had never read her blog posts, yet she wrote many things in her diary.

The boy rushed to the computer and entered the girl's space. There were indeed many things written about him: "Husband, thank you for helping me apply for this number"; "Husband, I really miss you. I will love you forever"; "Husband, why do I feel that you don't care about me as much as before? Don't you love me anymore?"; "Husband, our distance seems to be getting further and further"; "Husband, I can't take it anymore, let's break up"; "Husband, today we officially broke up. I wish you could have held me back, all it would have taken was a word from you and we wouldn't have broken up, but you didn't. Don't you love me anymore?"; "Husband, today is the last time I call you this. Why have you never read my diary? Why don't you understand me so much? Husband, do you know that my password is the date we first met? You could log in anytime. Husband, I want to hear you say 'Baby, I love you' once more. Since we don't have the fate to be lovers, let's be the best friends. Remember to be happy in the future, haha!"...

The boy logged into the girl's QQ, and the password was indeed the date they first met. But after logging in, the boy was stunned because the girl only had one friend on her list, with the note name "Husband". The boy finally noticed that the girl's signature was actually "I go online just to wait for you".

The boy felt unbearable pain. Later, every time the boy went online, he would hang both his and the girl's numbers together and talk to her a lot. He also liked to write blogs. He hoped that one day he could get a reply from the girl...

This story is just to tell all couples that if you love each other, you should cherish each other and pay attention to everything about each other...
