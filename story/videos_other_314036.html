David Beckham: It's Not Impossible for Milan to Surpass Inter, Willing to Accept Any Position in the New Formation

Sina Sports - At seven o'clock in the evening, the bustling streets of Beverly Hills have become deserted. After sunset, neither the star-chasing tourists nor the enthusiastic vendors selling star maps can be seen. On these smart maps indicating the residences of movie, TV stars, and sports celebrities, Beckham's residence on San Isidro Avenue is one of the most clicked addresses. The taxi driven by Olivero, the special envoy of "Milan Sports Daily," was immediately questioned by the security system as soon as it approached the intercom at the entrance to the villa. Originally worried about not being able to enter without an appointment, but after confirming the identity of the visitor, the gates of the Beckham family unexpectedly opened slowly. Near the entrance path is a garage where a motorcycle and two Lexuses are parked. There’s also a basketball hoop hanging on the wall of the garage. In addition, the luxurious cars parked on both sides of the path with short shrubs often attract attention: a black and a white Porsche, a Cadillac, a Jeep, and a Land Rover. Walking along the path for about 150 meters, Beckham's house finally comes into view. Beckham and his two eldest sons, Brooklyn and Romeo, were waiting in the hall. Through the glass window of the main entrance door, their figures are still clearly visible.

Walking into the hall, Beckham, wearing a white t-shirt, black casual pants, and red non-slip socks, greets with a smile. The entrance hall of the villa is paved with mosaic marble flooring, and a large wooden table is full of various decorations, candlesticks, and a double-eared large vase. The door on the left side of the hall leads to Beckham's huge kitchen, where three servants are busy preparing dinner under the interested gaze of Beckham's eldest son Brooklyn and third son Cruz. Victoria, however, is nowhere to be seen. Romeo has gone to the living room alone to watch a DVD; on the large LCD TV hanging on the wall, an old Tom Hanks movie is playing.

Since it was a sudden visit, the special envoy of "Milan Sports Daily" obviously couldn't stay too long, especially since it was almost dinner time. However, looking at Beckham's smiling face, the reporter still asked if he could chat a bit about AC Milan.

- David, how are you?

- I feel much better now. My ankle is healed, and there’s nothing wrong with my body.

- Do you want to restart your journey with AC Milan?

- I'm very happy to return to Italy: I can't wait to return to Milan. I'm counting down the days, and now there are only two weeks left.

- Have you talked to Leonardo yet?

- We often send text messages to each other. I think he congratulated me, and he is doing very well now, despite the team losing their last game to Palermo. We'll be able to talk face-to-face soon.

- Did you follow AC Milan's matches in the first half of this season?

- Of course, I watched all of AC Milan's games. I am fully aware of what happened in Italy.

- So you know that AC Milan is currently second in Serie A, five points behind Inter Milan at the top. Do you think AC Milan can win the Serie A title?

- There is still a long way to go to win the Serie A title. The team needs to catch up with and surpass Inter Milan. But anything is possible. Of course, it won’t be easy. But all the players of AC Milan are very strong, and they are performing excellently now, which makes me believe that AC Milan can win the Serie A title.

- But you always wanted the Champions League trophy more.

- That's normal. The teams competing in the Champions League are the biggest and strongest in Europe, such as Real Madrid, Barcelona, Manchester United, and Chelsea. I hope to win the UEFA Champions League trophy because it's a special event. But participating in Serie A across different cities in Italy is also a beautiful thing: I enjoy Serie A.

- AC Milan might face Manchester United in the round of 16. Would you feel happy or sad if that happens?

- I would be very happy if we had a chance to play against my former team. Since I left Manchester United in 2003, I haven't played at Old Trafford again: it has been seven years, and I really want to step onto Old Trafford again. If I can play against Manchester United, it will be a wonderful thing, right?

- Wonderful but challenging.

- It will definitely be very challenging, but also very exciting. I like big challenges, and I like playing such games.

- Since you are paying attention to Serie A and AC Milan, then you must know that Leonardo has changed the formation. Can Beckham find a position in the current 4-2-1-3 formation?

- Who knows... I can only say one thing: it doesn't matter whether I understand the position I will play or what Leonardo thinks about me. I want to be part of AC Milan, and I will accept any position Leonardo assigns me. Whether it's midfield, attacking midfielder, right wing, or even the bench, it doesn't matter. What matters is how I perform in that position. And I want to reiterate: what I want most is to be part of this magical team.

- When this season ends in June next year, the South Africa World Cup will begin: do you think England is one of the teams with a real chance to win the championship?

- Of course, there is no doubt about that. England is ambitious, but it is not the only one. Many national teams are as competitive as us. It will be an exciting World Cup.

- Which team would you like to meet in the final?

- Of course, the Italian team.

So, David, see you in Milan, or even, Johannesburg.

(Garibaldi)
