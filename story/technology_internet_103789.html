Three major network giants release financial reports, with mixed feelings of worry and joy - Age1983

by hexiaoyao on 2008-11-15 20:52:49

Sina Enters the $100 Million Quarterly Revenue Club; Giant's Net Profit Declines 53.8% Year-over-Year

Chinese internet concept stocks have entered a busy period for Q3 earnings releases. On the 13th, Sina, NetEase, and Giant Network all released their Q3 financial reports, leading to a mixed reaction of joy and concern among the companies.

Sina's Q3 report showed advertising revenue of $76.2 million, an increase of 66% year-over-year, aligning with the company's previous guidance of $75 million to $77 million. Total net revenues reached $105.4 million, up 64% compared to $64.3 million in the same period last year, surpassing Reuters' expectation of $103.3 million. Sina’s diluted earnings per share for the quarter were 44 cents, higher than Reuters' forecast of 42 cents.

Sina CEO Charles Chao stated: "Sina has once again achieved excellent results in Q3, with total quarterly revenue breaking through $100 million for the first time in our history. Our unprecedentedly successful online media coverage of the Beijing Olympics, as well as the strong performance of our online advertising business amid fierce market competition, further solidify Sina's leading position in both domestic online media and online brand advertising. We believe that, as internet applications continue to expand and corporate advertising budgets shift from offline to online, Sina will maintain its market competitiveness."

In the same day's release, NetEase's Q3 report indicated total revenue of RMB 806.6 million (approximately $118.8 million), compared to RMB 571.1 million (approximately $84.1 million) in the same period last year; net profit was RMB 313.3 million (approximately $46.1 million), compared to RMB 260.2 million (approximately $38.3 million) in the same period last year, marking a year-over-year increase of 20.4%. NetEase's net profit saw a decline quarter-over-quarter, with the previous quarter's net profit being RMB 438.2 million (approximately $64.5 million). NetEase's Q3 net profit did not meet analysts' expectations, but its revenue exceeded analyst forecasts.

Nonetheless, NetEase Chairman William Ding expressed confidence in the future: "We believe that the prospects for the Chinese internet market remain positive, and we expect healthy growth momentum in 2009 due to the rapid growth of internet users, the increasing popularity of e-commerce, and the continued shift of advertising revenue from traditional media to the internet."

In contrast to the first two companies, Giant Network's financial report appeared quite awkward. Giant's Q3 report showed net revenue of RMB 265.2 million (approximately $39.1 million), down 34.6% year-over-year; net profit was RMB 134.1 million (approximately $19.7 million), down 53.8% year-over-year. Regarding the poor performance of the financials, Giant Network's Chairman and CEO Shi Yuzhu explained: "In July of Q3, we adjusted the game model of 'Zhen Tu' to encourage players to spend more on daily in-game consumption rather than overly rely on promotional activities. The adjustment involved reducing fee-based items, which led to a reduction in this quarter's revenue." Shi Yuzhu added: "Overall, although our fee adjustments caused some short-term fluctuations in financial and operational performance, the long-term prospects remain unchanged. The company's fundamentals remain sound, and our solid financial statements indicate that we have sufficient resources to continue executing our growth plans."

The capital market responded accordingly to the performances of these three companies' earnings reports. In after-hours trading on Wednesday, Sina's stock price rose 3.8% to $27, Giant's stock price plummeted 18.06% in after-hours trading, and NetEase's stock price fell 3.79% in after-hours trading.
