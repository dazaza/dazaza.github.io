Fudan University students skip class en masse to see Tony Leung, professor angrily issues "fall of the country" statement

by zzsem on 2011-11-22 10:33:10

Students skipped class to see a celebrity instead, which was a strange incident that happened at Fudan University recently. Zhang Qingxiong, a professor in the Department of Philosophy, was extremely angry about this and lamented it as "the sound of a nation's doom."

On the afternoon of November 10, 2011, Professor Zhang Qingxiong walked into the classroom as usual and was shocked to find it empty. "A student came up and told me that because Hong Kong artist Tony Leung Chiu-Wai was visiting, 35 cadets from the Armed Police were called by the Youth League Committee to maintain order, so they asked for leave." Zhang Qingxiong was very angry, saying, "Today is the first time I've been so angry in many years," "The purpose of writing this article is not to pursue individual responsibility, but to reverse this deviation from righteousness," "Confucius once said: 'The sounds of Sangjian and Pusang are the sounds of a nation's doom!' (which implies that if a nation indulges in soft music all day without striving for progress, gradually losing its fighting spirit, it will eventually perish)." Zhang Qingxiong expressed in the article.

On the 20th, the official Weibo account of Fudan University Student Union released an explanation and admitted the mistake, apologizing repeatedly to Zhang Qingxiong. The Fudan University Student Union also stated: "This incident was mainly caused by incomplete communication and our insufficiently thorough work. We have been reflecting and summarizing these past few days, and we will certainly learn from this experience in the future."

Regarding this, many netizens expressed that Zhang Qingxiong made sense; however, many others believed that the professor was making a mountain out of a molehill: "There was nothing wrong with the students in this incident. They were sent there, not went on their own." Regardless, instead of lamenting the state of the world, one should pay attention to people's hearts. The fact that the event site was packed contrasted sharply with the emptiness of Zhang Professor's classroom in the afternoon, clearly indicating the positions of attending classes and entertainment in the hearts of many at Fudan. This was why the professor was "so angry for the first time in many years."

Teachers should be inclusive and understanding of modern students

When Tony Leung visited Fudan University, students skipped class to see him, resulting in 35 absences in a philosophy class taught by Professor Zhang Qingxiong.

Because of this matter, the professor was extremely upset and even wrote an article titled "'The sounds of Sangjian and Pusang are the sounds of a nation's doom!' - From a movie star's visit and 35 absences in a class" to express his feelings. This article was published on Fudan's internal website and after being reported by the media, it was widely reprinted.

Was this incident serious enough to lead to the "doom of a nation"? In response to this matter, this reporter interviewed Wang Libo, a teacher at Shenyang Normal University.

Wang Libo said: "Tradition and popularity are often intertwined in modern society. First of all, this professor's dedication is worthy of respect, but at the same time, I don't think that students skipping class to see Tony Leung rises to the level of 'The sounds of Sangjian and Pusang are the sounds of a nation's doom!'. The charm and power of modern popular culture are irresistible, and every student cannot be separated from social culture. It can be said that this generation of students has grown up surrounded by fashion elements such as American TV dramas, idols, and microblogs. Not to mention a philosophy class, for students, none of these compare to the allure of seeing Tony Leung. We cannot judge students as uneducated or restless fans based on this incident alone. Tony Leung doesn't come every day, but philosophy professors can be seen every week. Clearly, the former is more scarce. From a sociological perspective, the students' choice isn't off the mark, it's too normal! As teachers, we should have an inclusive attitude, understand, and accept modern students."
