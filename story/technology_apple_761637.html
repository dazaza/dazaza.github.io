Apple Accounts for Nearly 40% of U.S. Smartphones, Extending Lead

by geekzhang on 2013-05-04 11:38:09

American Smartphone Manufacturer Ranking in the First Quarter of This YearMay 4, Beijing Time - According to foreign media reports, the latest data released by market research company comScore shows that as of March this year, Apple's share of the U.S. smartphone market has grown to 39%, not only widening its lead over Samsung, but also growing at a rate four times that of Samsung.Data from comScore shows that in the first quarter of this year, the Apple iPhone remained the most popular smartphone in the U.S. market, with a total user base of 136.7 million, accounting for 39%, an increase of 2.7% compared to 36.3% in the fourth quarter of last year. This was also the largest increase among the top five smartphone manufacturers in the U.S. market.As of March, Samsung's market share was 21.7%, an increase of 0.7% compared to 21% in the fourth quarter of last year. Samsung ranked second after Apple, but its lead over third-placed HTC was more than doubled. HTC's market share was 9%, down from 10.2% in the fourth quarter of last year.The decline in HTC's market share gave Motorola, ranked fourth, and LG, ranked fifth, a chance to narrow the gap. As of the first quarter of this year, Motorola's share of the U.S. smartphone market was 8.5%, down 0.6% from the fourth quarter of last year; LG's market share was 6.8%, down from 7.1% in the fourth quarter of last year.American Smartphone Operating System Market Ranking in the First Quarter of This YearIn terms of operating systems, because all iPhones are equipped with the iOS system, the share of iOS in the U.S. smartphone market also increased by 2.7% in the first quarter. The Google Android platform still occupies half of the U.S. smartphone market, but its overall market share fell by 1.4%, dropping from 53.4% in the fourth quarter of last year to 52%.BlackBerry ranked third with its market share declining from 6.4% to 5.2%, while Microsoft's Windows Phone market share slightly increased by 0.1% to reach 3%. Symbian barely remained in the top five, with its market share falling from 0.6% to 0.5%.According to data from comScore, in the first quarter of this year, smartphone users accounted for 58% of the entire mobile market in the U.S., up 9% from the fourth quarter of last year. Due to high hardware subsidies and attractive package prices provided by carriers, the proportion of smartphone users is expected to continue to rise in the foreseeable future and will soon replace so-called feature phones.
