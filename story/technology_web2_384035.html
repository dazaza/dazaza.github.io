Weibo Marketing: How to Spark Fans' Curiosity

by edivista on 2011-03-09 09:04:12

Weibo News Network reposted from Hexun Network on November 16, 2010, Sina announced at its first Developer Conference that the number of registered users on Sina Weibo (click to log in) has exceeded 50 million. This figure indicates: the young Weibo is already the fastest-growing and most popular Internet application platform in China today. Faced with unlimited growth potential, Weibo marketing is increasingly attracting the attention of many companies. However, in marketing practice, companies have found that although their Weibo followers have reached a certain quantity level, there are still very few responders to marketing activities. Where exactly does the problem lie?

Analysis of Weibo Followers' Participation

The participation of Weibo followers determines the extent of their experience with the brand, and it also directly affects the effectiveness of brand activities. The participation of followers is reflected in their enthusiasm for brand activities, as well as the appeal of brand activities to followers. According to the degree of participation of Weibo followers and their behavioral performance and motivation, the types of Weibo activity followers can be divided into four levels: onlookers, commentators, activists, and endorsers. Specifically:

American online expert Jakob Nielsen once proposed a 90-9-1 rule for online communities, which means: 90% of users only visit websites without contributing content, 9% occasionally participate, and only 1% produce most of the content. Combined with the above analysis of Weibo followers' participation, it is not difficult to find that from onlookers to endorsers, the number of participants in Weibo activities gradually decreases, but the quality of participation becomes higher and higher. Compared with the highly participative activists and endorsers, low-participation onlookers and commentators are equally important because they can spread the information about the activity, create momentum for the brand's Weibo activity, and generate a sensational effect.

In addition, it should be noted that the level of follower participation is closely related to whether the incentive policy of the activity is attractive, whether the motivation for follower participation is sufficient, and whether the design of the activity is reasonable. This provides ideas for companies to increase the participation of Weibo followers.

Lowering the Participation Threshold to Increase Fan Trial Participation

Many fans actually have an interest and motivation to participate in the activity, but due to the complicated setup of the activity process, they can only give up, which is quite detrimental to increasing fan participation. Weibo is considered a communication tool with a human touch, so Weibo activities should be as simple and easy to implement as possible while ensuring marketing results.

Lowering the participation threshold means reducing the cost of fan participation, which will trigger low-cost trial participation. For example, the Super New Move activity initiated by Sina abandoned cumbersome collection and upload formats, trying not to change user behavior patterns, thus achieving the maximum scope of participation.

Using Suspense Settings to Stimulate Fans' Curiosity

As a psychological technique to cover up central information, suspense can stimulate fans' curiosity, making them deeply involved in the activity and creating long-term stickiness. When designing suspense, it is necessary to first clarify what information fans are most interested in, then set it through certain covering techniques.

Kogi BBQ claims to be the world's best mobile barbecue stand, and its case has become a model of Weibo marketing. One of its features is setting its location as a suspense, every three hours it tells fans where it will appear via Twitter (Twitter), making many fans obsessed.

In fact, as long as it is information that fans are interested in, suspense can be set, such as Daily Discount which sets suspense through product information. In addition, price suspense can be set to attract fan participation. The domestic diamond e-commerce website BLOVES (Wedding Ring Network) launched a brand interaction activity on its official Weibo account called Guess Price Win Diamond Ring, which attracted tens of thousands of netizens to forward and comment. This Weibo activity mainly uses the form of guessing prices to stimulate fan participation enthusiasm. Since October 18, 2010, this activity has been held once a week, each time giving away a diamond ring worth more than ten thousand yuan, and the person who guesses closest to the answer will win the ring. To further enhance participation, the activity designed many clues to help Weibo fans explore this price. Since the start of the activity, apart from BLOVES' fans growing at a rate of around 1500 people per day, the forwarding volume of its activity Weibo has also exceeded ten thousand, showing the fans' participation enthusiasm.

Prizes + Celebrities Get Fans Excited

In addition to driving with people's curiosity, prize promotions can also be used to drive fan participation enthusiasm with benefits. Vancl is considered one of the excellent practitioners of Weibo marketing, it is very good at using prize promotions to mobilize Weibo fans' participation enthusiasm, its Grab Floor Giveaway Anniversary T-shirt, 1 Yuan Flash Sale Original Price 888 Yuan Clothing activities once triggered a fan participation craze on Weibo.

Vancl's Weibo promotion is very good at borrowing the power of celebrities to stimulate fans' excitement points. On November 9, 2009, when Vancl only had 4,000 fans, it sent a Weibo message to Yao Chen's top-followed fan group: "We plan to give some benefits to Yao Chen's 210,000 fans, but how should we operate? I hope Yao Chen's fans will speak out." This Weibo immediately sparked heated discussions among the fan group, almost all Weibos were followed, commented, and forwarded. After signing Han Han as a spokesperson, Vancl took advantage of the popularity of Han Han's "Solo Choir" launch, using Weibo as a platform, initiated an activity themed "PS Vancl, Send Solo Choir", calling participants to use Han Han's official advertisement content as a template for creative graphic designs, then allowing netizens to vote for their favorite works, the ones with the highest votes would receive Han Han's "Solo Choir". This was a significant temptation for Weibo fans and Han Han fans alike. In just a few days, Vancl's Weibo activity was forwarded nearly 4,000 times, and the number of new fans increased by more than 2,000.

Empower Your Fans

In the past, there was a clear division of labor between enterprises and users, where enterprises were responsible for the entire process from product design, production to marketing, and users were merely informed and persuaded. However, in the era of consumer sovereignty, companies can try empowering fans, perhaps reaping unexpected results.

Recently, fast-fashion brand Uniqlo held a "More Tweets, Lower Price" event, handing over the pricing power of some products to Weibo fans. Uniqlo displayed 10 pieces of clothing on the event website, when a fan tweeted (here it can be interpreted as a verb click) on a piece of clothing, the price of that item would decrease. The website would inform participants how many tweets (here it can be interpreted as a noun click) the item currently has, the selling price, the discount, and how far it is from the lowest price. At the same time, the system would also write a paragraph of text, of course, participants could also customize the text, then hit Tweet, the system would prompt that under your efforts, the price has decreased again. By the end of the event, many items were reduced to the lowest price of 33% off.

Empowering fans, companies can do a lot more on the Weibo platform: Can product promotional discounts be decided by fan voting? Can the next round of advertising creativity be subject to fan evaluation or even rejection? Can product packaging design accept fan submissions? These authorizations can maximize the participation of fans, thereby gradually turning fans into brand endorsers.

In fact, besides using methods such as stimulating curiosity, benefit-driven, celebrity guidance, and fan empowerment, there are many ways to increase the participation in Weibo marketing activities, but no matter how they change, they ultimately must be based on a deep understanding of Weibo fans to formulate participation strategies and achieve the purpose of Weibo marketing.
