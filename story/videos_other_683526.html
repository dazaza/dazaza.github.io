Two men imitate TV drama scenes to steal from hotels

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-29 14:48:36

Our newspaper news (reported by Zhou Xin, correspondent Huang Yan): Learning theft from TV, partnering to commit crimes in Beijing; a kindergarten principal is put on trial for embezzling children's meal fees. Using the "switcheroo" method, they specialized in stealing branded cigarettes and practiced yoga for breast enhancement. Within two months, they stole goods worth over 14,000 yuan from various hotels and restaurants in Beijing. Yesterday, a 48-year-old woman was criminally detained for defrauding an 82-year-old man into marriage. The reporter learned from Xicheng District Procuratorate that Pi Mou and Zhao Mou were sentenced to four years in prison for theft. A driver ran multiple red lights to take a sick child to the hospital, and the Traffic Management Bureau will remove the exposure of his violations.

In June 2011, Pi Mou and Zhao Mou, while watching TV programs at home, accidentally saw someone using the switcheroo method to steal from hotels. Thus, they decided to use the same method to make money in Beijing.

After arriving in Beijing, Pi Mou and Zhao Mou called themselves managers, hired a black car, and started deceiving people in various hotels. Usually, they would drive around looking for targets. When they chose a hotel, both Pi Mou and Zhao Mou would enter the hotel's merchandise department together. If the server was a young woman in her twenties, the two would start their act. "Give me four cartons of soft Zhonghua!" At the same time, Pi Mou would also request some good tea. When the server brought the items, Pi Mou would excuse himself by saying he didn't want anyone to see the gifts, asking the server to find a box for packaging. Meanwhile, Zhao Mou would ask the server to write a detailed receipt. While the server was busy writing the receipt, Pi Mou would quickly hide the cigarettes in his clothes and immediately seal the box with tape. Then, Zhao Mou would leave under the pretext of checking other places, and Pi Mou would follow suit by claiming he needed to call a car. Eventually, the two were caught by the police.

It is understood that between June and July, Pi Mou and Zhao Mou stole cigarettes worth over 14,000 yuan using the same method. Currently, both have been sentenced to four years in prison for theft and fined 4,000 yuan each.
