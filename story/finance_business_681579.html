Da Vinci Furniture Counterfeiting: Behind the Exorbitant Price of Foreign Brand Furniture

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-28 14:18:22

Da Vinci Furniture can be said to be the most influential high-end furniture brand domestically, renowned for its exorbitant prices. A single bed can sell for more than 100,000 yuan, and a set of sofas can reach over 300,000 yuan. The reason these pieces of furniture can be sold at such astronomical prices, according to Da Vinci's salespeople, is because their furniture is 100% "internationally super-branded" and produced in Italy, using unpolluted "natural high-quality materials." However, after more than half a year of investigation, reporters discovered that a significant portion of the expensive furniture sold by Da Vinci was not actually made in Italy, nor were the materials used as claimed by Da Vinci—precious solid wood. Upon testing, the purchased Da Vinci furniture was even judged as substandard.

[Weekly Quality Report] Da Vinci's "Code"

Source: CCTV Network, from the program Weekly Quality Report

Consumers spent nearly 3 million yuan on furniture with numerous issues.

Ms. Tang from Beijing spent over 2.8 million yuan purchasing more than forty pieces of furniture from a Da Vinci specialty store. Among them, a set of sofas cost over 300,000 yuan, and a single bed was priced at more than 100,000 yuan. As soon as these expensive pieces of furniture entered her home, Ms. Tang noticed problems; she told reporters that these costly furnishings emitted a strong, unpleasant odor. Additionally, the single bed she bought, valued at over 100,000 yuan, was supposed to be 1.5 meters according to the contract but only measured 1.2 meters when delivered. When communicating with the staff at the Da Vinci specialty store, Ms. Tang was informed that “domestic sizes differ from international sizes.”

Ms. Tang explained to reporters that the reason she was willing to spend so much money on Da Vinci furniture was due to the salespeople's promise that their furniture was "internationally super-branded" and produced in Italy, using materials that were "natural high-quality raw materials without pollution." So how could such expensive furniture have so many problems? Although Beijing Da Vinci Furniture Company provided Ms. Tang with import procedures for the furniture, she still had doubts about the Italian origin of the furniture she purchased from Da Vinci.

Da Vinci Specialty Stores Claim High-Priced Furniture is Indeed Made in Italy

According to the official website of Da Vinci Company, Da Vinci Home Co., Ltd. was established in Shanghai in 2000, founded by the company's general manager, Pan Zhuangxiuhua. After eleven years of development, it has become the largest and highest-class furniture agency in Asia, having won multiple awards for being the "most favored furniture brand by the wealthy," as awarded by the Hurun Rich List magazine. Currently, the headquarters in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, Chongqing, Hangzhou, Hong Kong, and other places all have branches and specialty stores, representing over 100 brands including Capellini, Tesoro, Hollywood, Armani, Versace, Fendi, and Lamborghini. These brands are all considered "international super-brands" in the furniture industry.

Reporters then conducted an investigation at the Beijing Da Vinci Furniture specialty store. This store is located in the Friendship Store near Jianguomen in Beijing, spanning four floors with an operating area of over 8,000 square meters. Inside the store, there are various styles of furniture, including Spanish, French, German, and American designs, all shimmering with luxurious and extravagant vibes.

Yang Liping, the manager of Beijing Da Vinci Furniture Co., Ltd., told reporters that their main business is the agency sale of original imported furniture, and they are currently preparing for an IPO.

On the second floor of the Beijing Da Vinci Furniture specialty store, reporters found the type of Capellini furniture purchased by Ms. Tang. The prices were indeed astonishing; a wine cabinet was priced at 170,000 yuan, a TV cabinet at 180,000 yuan, and this double bed was priced at over 300,000 yuan. Zhao Ying, the store manager of the Beijing Da Vinci specialty store, repeatedly assured reporters that the Capellini furniture sold here is 100% produced in Italy. She also informed us that the beautiful carvings on these pieces of furniture are made from a precious wood called poplar thorn root, which is only found in a remote town in Italy.

Subsequently, reporters visited Da Vinci specialty stores in Shanghai, Chongqing, Chengdu, and Shenzhen. Similar to the Beijing store, the salespeople in these stores also claimed that the materials used in the Capellini furniture they sold are "natural high-quality raw materials" and are originally imported.

Further investigation revealed that the customs clearance company for Da Vinci's furniture imports is Shanghai Jiusheng Customs Clearance Co., Ltd., and the operating entity is Shanghai Dijie Trading Co., Ltd. All import procedures were complete, and no flaws were detected.

Unveiling the Origins of Da Vinci’s Expensive Furniture

Salespeople at the Beijing Da Vinci specialty store stated that all Capellini furniture they sell is produced by Capellini in Cantu, Italy. Following the address provided by the salespeople, reporters found Capellini in Cantu, Italy. The company's responsible person confirmed that they do have a cooperative relationship with Da Vinci, but the carvings on their furniture are not made from solid wood carvings but rather from a special resin material.

In previous interviews, salespeople from Da Vinci Furniture in Beijing consistently claimed that the carved portions of the Capellini furniture they sell are made from a unique Italian wood—poplar thorn root. This clearly differs from what Capellini's factory in Italy stated.

With this question in mind, reporters further investigated Da Vinci's origins.

After much effort, an insider provided a crucial clue: A furniture company named Jin Fenghuang in Shenzhen once produced furniture for Da Vinci.

Reporters immediately went to Jin Fenghuang Furniture Company in Shenzhen to verify this information. The responsible person of Jin Fenghuang told reporters that in 2004 and 2005, Da Vinci indeed purchased large quantities of furniture from them. However, starting in 2006, due to price and other reasons, Da Vinci stopped cooperating with them and sought new partners in Dongguan. The specific partner remains undisclosed.

Based on the statement from this company's responsible person, it appears that the furniture sold by Da Vinci may not be entirely 100% produced in Italy as they claim. What is the truth? Reporters continued their investigation in Dongguan.

Dongguan City in Guangdong Province is an important furniture production base in China, with over 3,000 furniture manufacturing enterprises. To find the mysterious manufacturer producing furniture for Da Vinci, reporters spent six months visiting over 100 furniture factories.

Finally, at Changfeng Furniture Company's showroom in Dongguan, reporters discovered a bed almost identical to the Capellini double bed in the Beijing Da Vinci Furniture specialty store. Peng Jie, General Manager of Changfeng Furniture Company, said that this was unsurprising because all Capellini furniture sold by Da Vinci is produced by their company. To substantiate his claim, he showed reporters electronic payment receipts from Shenzhen Da Vinci Company to their company. This responsible person also mentioned that the double bed sold in Da Vinci specialty stores for over 300,000 yuan costs only around 30,000 yuan at their place.

It turns out that the Capellini furniture, supposedly 100% produced in Italy, is actually made by the relatively unknown Changfeng Furniture Company located in Dongguan, Guangdong.

During further investigations at Changfeng Company, reporters found that the materials used for the furniture produced for Da Vinci are not Italian precious woods but rather high-molecular resin materials, plywood, and particleboard. Just stepping into the workshop, one can smell the pungent odor of these chemical substances. Moreover, the decorative carvings on the furniture are not hand-carved but instead formed using molds, differing from what Da Vinci's staff claims.

Changfeng's responsible person told reporters that their production strictly follows Da Vinci's requirements. To support his claim, he showed reporters an email from Da Vinci Company instructing Changfeng to use non-solid wood wherever possible, avoid manual carving, and adopt mold-forming methods.

Through processes like sanding, baking, and drying, these pieces of furniture made from high-molecular resin materials, plywood, and particleboard transform into so-called premium timber processed luxury-priced furniture.

Peng Jie, General Manager of Changfeng Company, said that since 2006, they have been producing furniture for Da Vinci. Initially, they produced one series, which has now expanded to three series under the brands Capellini, Hollywood, and Riva. Their annual transaction volume with Da Vinci is approximately 50 million yuan.

The reporter's further in-depth investigation into Changfeng Company revealed that Da Vinci took great pains to cover up the fact that they purchase furniture from Changfeng. They designed a complete set of procedures, keeping their transactions strictly confidential, setting up dedicated telephone lines, faxes, and assigning specific personnel for communication and liaison.

Another detail caught the reporter's attention. In the "advance payment details account" provided by Da Vinci to Changfeng, Da Vinci claimed to purchase small components like fabric panels and hangers from Changfeng, not furniture. The responsible person from Changfeng said that what Da Vinci actually buys from them is furniture; they just don't dare to specify it in the accounts to prevent exposing the truth that the so-called foreign brand luxury-priced furniture they sell is domestically produced.

General Manager Peng Jie of Dongguan Changfeng Furniture Company told reporters that after delivering the furniture to Da Vinci, Da Vinci ships the furniture out of the port in Shenzhen, transports it to Italy, and then returns it to Shanghai, where it clears customs back into the country. With this process, the furniture gains full import documentation and becomes what Da Vinci claims: 100% Italian-made, "internationally super-branded" furniture.

Not long ago, Ms. Tang sent the beds and TV cabinets she purchased from Da Vinci to the National Furniture and Indoor Environment Quality Supervision and Inspection Center for testing. The results showed three items did not meet national standards. Specifically, the TV cabinet was made from particleboard, not solid wood, and was judged as a substandard product.

To this point, the truth is clear. The high-priced furniture sold by Da Vinci is not 100% produced in Italy as they claim, but rather processed in some furniture companies in Dongguan. The materials used are not the Italian precious wood Poplar Thorn Root, but rather high-molecular resin materials, plywood, and particleboard.

From Xiang Wushi audio equipment to Oudian flooring, and now Da Vinci's high-priced furniture, counterfeit foreign brands continue to emerge and escalate. We often say, "Quality matches price." However, the furniture sold by Da Vinci is priced at hundreds of thousands or even millions of yuan, certainly not cheap, yet both its identity and quality are fake. Such gilded exterior but internally rotten high-priced furniture gives consumers another blow. We cannot help but ask, in today's era of such a lack of corporate integrity, what can consumers still trust?
