1. Are there better ways to protect your eyes besides eye exercises?

Answer: For young students, you also need to pay attention to writing and reading distance, which should preferably not be less than 30 centimeters; desk lamps should not be too bright, a regular 15-watt bulb is appropriate, and a three-band energy-saving lamp of 3 watts is appropriate. When using eyes for a long time up close, special myopia prevention glasses should be worn; the distance for looking at a computer should not be less than 60 centimeters, and the distance for watching TV should not be less than 3 meters. Eyes that are already myopic or have other refractive defects should always wear glasses with accurate degrees. Sleep time should not be less than 8 hours.

2 Can doing eye exercises prevent myopia? If already myopic, can vision be restored?

Answer: Vision loss caused by myopia cannot be restored. Eye exercises can only restore part of the vision loss caused by eye fatigue.

3 What kind of glasses can protect your eyes?

Answer: Wearing glasses adapted to the environment can protect your eyes. For example: wearing sunglasses under strong light; wearing distance glasses when mainly using distance vision; wearing nearsighted glasses when mainly using near vision; wearing computer glasses when mainly using computers; wearing dedicated electrical welding glasses during electric or gas welding...

4 What machines can treat myopia?

Answer: No machine can effectively prevent or cure myopia. The best way to treat myopia is to wear glasses.

5 Can true myopia be restored?

Answer: It cannot be restored. True myopia means the eyeball axis has become longer and cannot become shorter, thus true myopia cannot be restored.

6 Is wearing glasses harmful to the eyes?

Answer: As long as the method is appropriate, it is beneficial and harmless to the eyes. But currently most people use them improperly, resulting in deeper glasses over time and worse eyesight.

7 Should glasses be worn all the time without removing them?

Answer: Glasses should be worn all the time if they are suitable. However, different people have different refractive conditions and different uses of their eyes, so what kind of glasses should be worn and when to remove them varies from person to person. Glasses correcting hyperopia and treating amblyopia should be worn all the time. Other situations are more complex, and when to remove them varies from person to person and cannot be generalized. You must follow the advice of optometric experts (do not follow the advice of non-experts).

Answer: There are multiple causes of amblyopia, the most common being refractive defects and visual fatigue. If amblyopia is caused by prolonged use of computers up close, moving further away from the computer screen, eating some vitamin A, ensuring adequate sleep time, and taking a break for a period can help return to normal.

28 Why do eyes deform after wearing glasses?

Answer: There are two reasons: One, myopia, especially moderate to high myopia, causes the eyeball axis to lengthen and the eyeball to enlarge. Regardless of whether glasses are worn, the eyeball protrudes more compared to when not myopic. For every 3.00 degrees of myopia, the eyeball protrudes by one millimeter. Two, glasses have a beautifying effect. Myopia of 3.00 degrees forms gradually over more than two years, with the eyeball axis lengthening by one millimeter and the eyeball protruding by one millimeter, happening in 3.00 stages. If glasses are not worn, no one would notice this subtle change. After wearing myopia glasses, the concave lens reduces the image of the eyeball, making it appear smaller. Getting used to it creates a visual standard. Removing the glasses reveals the actual eyeball, showing significant changes, making it seem larger. The higher the degree of the glasses, the more noticeable the change.
