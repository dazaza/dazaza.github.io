Centipedes contain histamine-like substances, hemolytic proteins, multiple amino acids, formic acid, fatty oils, cholesterol, as well as orange pigments, yellow pigments, and light blue-green pigments. The venom secreted by their poison glands comes in two types: one is cutting off the chelicerae and immediately collecting the venom with a glass capillary tube. The other involves electrical stimulation using a multi-purpose instrument for pharmacological and physiological purposes, continuously stimulating with an induction current at a frequency of 128Hz to 20V to 4m. When stimulating to extract venom, the last leg of an adult centipede is connected to the output end of the stimulator with an alligator clip, the left antenna on its head is grounded, and the contact between the electrode and the centipede is moistened with physiological saline; when the circuit is connected and stimulated, centipedes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for calming wind, stopping convulsions, attacking toxins, and dispersing nodules. They are mainly used to treat infantile convulsions, tetanus, seizures, facial paralysis, lymph node tuberculosis, abscesses, etc. Centipedes were first recorded in the "Shennong Bencao Jing" (The Classic of Herbal Medicine), a traditional Chinese medicine with a long history of medicinal use, and the "Golden Head Centipede," known for its unique efficacy, complete shape, fresh color, and luster, enjoys a prestigious reputation both domestically and internationally. Modern research has proven that venom can be seen discharging from the opening of the centipede's venom hook, and modern medical testing has proven that centipedes contain two effective components similar to bee venom, namely histamine-like substances and hemolytic proteins. In addition, they also contain tyrosine, leucine, formic acid, fatty oil, and cholesterol. It has different levels of inhibitory effects on convulsions caused by cardiazol, pure alkaloids, and strychnine alkaloids, varying degrees of inhibitory effects on various skin fungi, and inhibitory and killing capabilities against Mycobacterium tuberculosis. The venom is collected in small beakers, then vacuum dried, and freeze-dried for storage. The former method can be combined with capture and processing for commercial purposes. The latter method of extracting venom does not harm the centipedes, allowing continuous venom extraction every two weeks. Centipede venom is a highly active biological toxin that has been actively developed and researched in recent years, especially regarding live venom extraction and activity research and application, significantly enhancing the social and economic benefits of centipede farming and promoting the vigorous development of artificial centipede breeding.

Specialized Farming - How to raise centipedes?

In animal taxonomy, centipedes belong to the phylum Arthropoda, class Arachnida, order Scolopendromorpha, family Scolopendridae, genus Scolopendra, species Scolopendra. This species is further divided into two similar local subspecies: the lesser spiny centipede and the greater spiny centipede.

With the widespread use of pesticides and fertilizers, wild centipedes in rural plains have become rare, and those in mountainous areas are decreasing due to extensive capturing by farmers. Additionally, with the spread of traditional Chinese medicine culture abroad, the export volume of centipedes has increased annually, making it impossible to meet the increasing demand solely through hunting wild centipedes. Medicinal centipedes have become one of the scarce herbs in the market, with stable sales and steadily rising prices.
