Experts appeal for due attention to be paid to the health problems of epileptic patients

by king0r8k on 2010-07-04 09:23:02

Epilepsy is a chronic disease characterized by sudden abnormal discharges of neurons in the brain, which leads to transient dysfunction of the brain. In traditional Chinese medicine, it is called "xian zheng" and commonly referred to as "yang jiao feng." An epileptic seizure refers to the clinical phenomenon caused by abnormal and excessive hypersynchronous discharges of neurons in the brain. Due to the different locations of the abnormally discharging neurons in the brain, there are various manifestations, including brief motor, sensory, consciousness, behavioral, and neurological dysfunctions.

Epilepsy deserves societal attention.

Epilepsy has become a common disease that seriously threatens the health of our people. Currently, about 400,000 new cases of epilepsy occur annually in mainland China, with an incidence rate increasing by 5% each year, making it the fastest-growing disease in terms of incidence over the past decade. To reduce the incidence of epilepsy, this year, major and medium-sized cities in our country have implemented the "Project for the Census of One Million Epilepsy Patients." According to international standards, each of the one million epilepsy patients will undergo four examinations to help them detect and treat their condition as early as possible.

Excessive protection can also harm epilepsy patients.

Director Ding Ziwei from Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Hospital (www.dx110.com) believes that due to the lack of proper understanding of epilepsy by most people, there is a certain degree of social discrimination, causing patients to face difficulties in marriage and employment. This places a significant economic and psychological burden on patients' families and society and has become a serious public health issue.

When a family has a child with epilepsy, it brings a considerable psychological impact to the parents, who generally go through stages of shock, denial, sorrow, resentment, and adaptation. Some parents adapt relatively quickly, while others may remain stuck at a particular stage for a long time. At this point, parents often overly exaggerate the adverse factors affecting the child and excessively protect them, such as indulging their behavior or overly managing their daily life details. This makes children with epilepsy more prone to learning difficulties, emotional disorders, and other issues.

Eliminate social discrimination and let epilepsy patients relax.

Director Ding Ziwei points out that currently, society still widely holds attitudes of fear and rejection towards epilepsy, exacerbating patients' feelings of inferiority, negativity, isolation, or loss of confidence in their future. People with epilepsy are prone to develop socio-psychological problems, often manifesting as depression, anxiety, epileptic personality, schizophrenia-like psychosis, neurosis, etc., among which depression is the most common and can lead to suicidal tendencies in severe cases. Epileptic personality is characterized by stubbornness, irritability, emotional outbursts, slow movements, and self-centeredness. How to help epilepsy patients relax has gradually become a social issue. Besides active anti-epileptic treatment, measures should be taken to improve the psychological problems of patients.

First, strengthen education and publicity throughout society. Let all members of society have a correct understanding of epilepsy, enhance the correct understanding of epilepsy among the masses, especially leaders in education and labor departments and teachers, eliminate prejudice and discrimination, improve the living space for people with epilepsy, and enhance their quality of life.

Second, guide standardized medical care for patients and their families. Educate patients and their families not to blindly trust irresponsible advertisements, such as "guaranteed cure," "root removal," "ancestral secret formulas," etc., to avoid being deceived. Seek medical care at regular hospital neurology departments, adhere to scientific treatment, take medication as prescribed, do not arbitrarily stop or change medication, and scientifically address the disease while overcoming the sense of shame associated with it.

Finally, Director Ding Ziwei emphasized that when treating epilepsy, one should choose efficient, scientific, formal, and professional hospitals as early as possible.
