Pirate bus stop lures waiting passengers - only to see advertisements but no buses (Picture)

by umanhua1 on 2012-03-07 10:31:05

Pirate Bus Stops

Have bus stops also been pirated? Yes, you haven't read it wrong! Recently, the Chengdu Evening News received complaints from some readers, who said that there are quite a few "pirate bus stops" on bus stop lines in the urban area. These pirate bus stops not only mislead passengers but also occupy sidewalks, causing safety hazards. The reporter investigated according to clues provided by the readers and indeed found some "pirate bus stops". Their biggest feature is: even if you wait until dark, no buses will arrive. However, such "pirate bus stops" do exist in the city center for a long time and in large numbers.

Passenger Complaints

Encountering Fake Stops, Waiting Half a Day for No Buses

"It's normal to wait more than ten minutes without seeing a bus, but waiting over half an hour without one coming is really annoying!" Recently, Mr. Zhan complained to the Chengdu Evening News reporter about encountering a "bus stop where buses would never come." What exactly happened?

Originally, Mr. Zhan, who lives near the West Gate, took bus No. 35 from Guanghuacun several days ago to attend a wedding banquet near Guojiqiao. After finishing the banquet that afternoon, Mr. Zhan went to the opposite bus stop of where he got off to prepare to return home, but encountered trouble. Mr. Zhan waited on the stop for more than half an hour, watching many buses pass by, but none stopped there, including the No. 35 bus he had taken earlier.

Later, under the reminder of a passerby, Mr. Zhan discovered that the "bus stop" behind him only had large advertisements and no bus information at all, not even the name of the stop. "Is this a deserted stop? If it's useless, it should be dismantled, misleading passengers into waiting uselessly!" Mr. Zhan repeatedly complained to the reporter.

In fact, before Mr. Zhan's complaint, our newspaper had already received similar complaints, stating that some bus stops displayed seven or eight advertisement boards, while the bus information was only a small section, making it inconvenient for passengers to check. Moreover, standing on the back part of the stop wouldn't result in any buses stopping either.

What kind of "bus stops where no buses stop" are these? How many are there in the urban area? The Chengdu Evening News reporter immediately conducted an investigation.

Reporter Investigation

Counting with Public Transport Group Personnel: Over 100 Pirate Stops on the Second Ring Road

Pirate Stops Right Before Our Eyes

Public Transport Group Personnel Were Shocked

"There won't be such situations, right?!" Deputy Manager He Zhouxing of the Advertising Subsidiary of Chengdu Public Transport Group Corporation looked puzzled and shocked after listening to the reporter's description. Is the readers' reflection true? Subsequently, Manager He brought along engineering personnel to accompany the reporter to conduct an on-site inspection.

At the "Shuangnan Road South Station" bus stop, which is less than hundreds of meters away from the Chengdu Public Transport Group Corporation, passengers were orderly waiting to board the bus. "This is our stop, everything is normal here," Manager He pointed to two large advertisement boards and a slightly smaller public transport information display board on the stop. However, just less than 2 meters away from these three advertisement boards on the same line, there stood another three identical advertisement boards, looking like two adjacent bus stops. Several passengers waited there for a long time, but no buses stopped nearby.

"These are fake ones, no wonder passengers have complained!" Manager He carefully examined the "bus stop" formed by the three advertisement boards behind and suddenly understood.

Are there real and fake bus stops?

Occupying Sidewalks

Misleading Passengers and Endangering Safety

Under Manager He's guidance, the reporter found the difference between the two adjacent bus stops: the former stop had prominent names and route information, and the few advertisement boards on the stop were specifically marked with explanations, even bearing the outdoor advertisement release number approved by the industrial and commercial department; the latter stop's three advertisement boards only had advertisements, with absolutely no route information, not even the stop name, and no approval number from any relevant department on the advertisement boards.

Apart from the above subtle differences, the two stops were almost identical, no wonder some passengers couldn't tell them apart. Within the hour the reporter observed here, quite a few passengers stood on the latter "pirate stop" to wait for the bus, realizing something was wrong only after missing the bus and wasting time.

Worryingly, especially during crowded hours, because the "pirate stops" occupied quite a bit of sidewalk space, it was difficult for alighting passengers to disperse timely, forcing them to crowd near the road edge, forming a safety hazard. Similarly, because the sidewalk was occupied, pedestrians had to walk through the remaining less-than-1-meter-wide sidewalk outside the "pirate stops", causing inconvenience.

Profit from Pirate Stops Publishing Ads

This kind of "pirate bus stop," how many are there in the urban area? The reporter conducted a survey around the Second Ring Road. After going around once, apart from a few stops that even Manager He couldn't distinguish as genuine or fake, more than 100 stops were clearly identified as "pirate bus stops." Most "pirate stops" were built adjacent to regular stops, making it hard for the vast majority of passengers to differentiate. Another type of "pirate stop" simply set up independently on the roadside sidewalks.

"The main purpose of their existence is to publish advertisements," Manager He introduced to the reporter. The annual fee for a large advertisement on a regular bus stop is about 50,000 yuan. According to the reporter's discovery of more than a hundred "pirate stops," the advertisers could make profits reaching millions of yuan per year!

"They take advantage of the pirate appearance to exploit loopholes," Manager He said, possibly because it's hard to distinguish the authenticity of bus stops, thus avoiding supervision from relevant departments.

The reporter counted the types of advertisements published by "pirate stops," mainly including training and educational institutions, plastic surgery hospitals, developers' housing projects, etc.

Root Cause

Response from the Builders of Pirate Stops:

Cost Already Invested, Currently Can't Dismantle

The reporter found the company behind the "pirate stops" through the contact numbers on some advertisement boards - Chengdu Jiubang Public Transport Facilities Co., Ltd.

"We are also helpless about this issue," frankly admitted General Manager Wang Huiqi of the company, acknowledging the impact of these nominally existing bus stops on passengers, but he had something to say about why they still existed.

According to his introduction, Jiubang Company was founded in 2006, at that time commissioned by nine private bus enterprises in the city, gradually building these bus stops according to routes, which was approved by the transportation department. But with the gradual integration of private buses, these stops gradually lost their original functions. Especially after routes were canceled and merged, the only function left for bus stops was to publish advertisements.

Meanwhile, Manager Wang also admitted that the construction of some stops and the publication of advertisement contents were not approved by the urban management and industrial and commercial departments. But because costs were invested in construction, currently, they can only wait for adjustments in the bus industry structure, merging all these "pirate bus stops" into state-owned bus enterprises, "at that time, dismantle what should be dismantled, merge what should be merged."

During the interview, Manager Wang revealed another piece of information: apart from Jiubang Company, many of the "pirate bus stops" mentioned by the reporter were built by other advertisers. Manager Wang said, if Jiubang Company's stops belong to historical legacies, then the remaining pirate bus stops are openly illegal. According to his introduction, building a bus stop costs more than 10,000 yuan, while the annual advertisement fee collected is over 30,000 yuan, which is the greatest motivation for other advertisers to illegally build stops.

Solution Path

City Transportation Committee Quickly Launches Special Rectification

How to solve the problems existing on the city's bus stops? Director Zhang Conggui of the Office of the Chengdu Transportation Committee and responsible persons of relevant departments accepted interviews from our newspaper reporters. According to data provided by the Transportation Committee, there were 174 complaints from citizens about bus stops and signs in 2011.

Director Zhang first introduced the approval process for setting up legal bus stops: First, they should be jointly surveyed and selected by the Transportation Committee, traffic management departments, and bus companies; Secondly, if they occupy sidewalks or green belts, they need to be reported to the urban management and gardening departments for approval; Finally, the signboard advertisements should be approved by the industrial and commercial department.

Regarding the hundreds of bus stops set up without approval within the city, Director Zhang stated that the Transportation Committee will join forces with other relevant departments to quickly carry out a special clean-up operation on bus stops. At the same time, regarding the legacy issues involving Jiubang Company's stops, the Transportation Committee will actively promote consultations among related units to resolve the problem. For illegally set up or unused bus signs after route adjustments, orders will be issued to the construction units to dismantle them within a limited period; for those refusing to dismantle, the Transportation Committee will join forces with relevant departments to dismantle them according to law; for bus stops with incomplete approvals, they will be urged to follow procedures, and illegal land use for setting up bus stops will be investigated. Finally, illegal publishing of advertisements on bus stops will be investigated.

Extension

Municipal People's Congress Representative Submits Suggestions

Standardizing Stop Construction

The current situation and impact of "pirate bus stops" have also attracted the attention of radio host Sun Jing. As a municipal people's congress representative, she submitted "Suggestions on Standardized Setting and Management of Bus Stops and Signs in the Main Urban Area of Chengdu and Removing Illegal Settings" at the recently concluded Fifth Session of the Fifteenth Chengdu Municipal People's Congress, hoping to legislate to clarify the functions of various government departments and standardize bus stop construction.
