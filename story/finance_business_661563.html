1. Why should people have a sense of responsibility?

"If a person has no passion, he will accomplish nothing, and the starting point of passion is precisely responsibility." -- Russian writer Tolstoy

It is said that many qualities in life can be cultivated later on, but one of the hardest qualities to cultivate is a sense of responsibility. A responsible person can achieve: not being overwhelmed by big tasks, not neglecting small tasks, not forgetting numerous tasks, and not making mistakes with complicated tasks. Responsibility is gold; compared with self-respect, self-confidence, ambition, charity, compassion, sympathy, ambition, pride, perseverance, filial piety, care, kindness, etc., among these "various hearts", responsibility is the most brilliant heart. Even if other "hearts" are weak, having a sense of responsibility will compensate for many weaknesses. Those who implement responsibility in their actions, always and everywhere fulfilling responsibilities for society, others, and themselves, will ultimately achieve something. Only when you possess a high sense of responsibility will you find your personal achievements and happiness index continuously rising. Responsibility is an invisible burden on each of our shoulders. Even if today you do not feel its weight, one day it will fall upon you. If you are unwilling to pay for it, then one day it will make you pay even more. For those who dare to take responsibility, the greater the sense of responsibility, the larger the stage. Responsibility achieves your competitiveness without a doubt. Responsibility is a crucial personality trait for anyone to stand in society, gain career success, and obtain family happiness.

2. Why should people cherish life?

"The most valuable thing in life is life itself, and life only happens once for each individual. A person should live so that when they look back at the past, they do not regret wasting time or feel ashamed for doing nothing." -- Soviet writer Ostrovsky's protagonist in *How the Steel Was Tempered*: Paul Korchagin.

Life: means living, it refers to the body; Destiny: refers to thought and soul. Life is more important than anything else, living is the most beautiful. This not only shows responsibility to oneself but also to others. The body does not belong to you; only the spirit of life belongs to you, it is wealth that others cannot take away. Life does not equal the body. The atoms of the body and natural matter do not solely belong to you. Life = Body + Thought. God treats everyone differently, as if depositing a million dollars for each person coming to earth. However, God also has a little bias: some have a few thousand more, while others may only have a few more dollars or cents. This tail number represents the wealth gap between people, but compared to the million-dollar account, shouldn't this tail number be negligible? So, please don't be hard on yourself because of God's slight bias, and do not cherish life!

3. Why should people value the present?

"On the great clock of time, there are only two words: Now." -- British playwright Shakespeare

Life is meaningful precisely because of its uncertainty. If a fortune-teller revealed everything about your future, you would immediately lose interest. And "now" is an absolute concept. There is only the past, only the future, no now. What is now? The last second has already passed, the next second has yet to arrive, where is now? Now is in your current mood, which might last a minute, maybe a day, perhaps a week, a month, a year... Because there is no "now," we must cherish it even more. Our lives are composed of countless unfindable, absolute "nows." The longest "now" is "I am still alive now" -- do not abandon, do not give up! Living is about cherishing the present!

4. Why do people feel negative?

"An optimist sees an opportunity in every misfortune, while a pessimist sees a misfortune in every opportunity. Always remember, the angle from which one thinks can dominate your attitude towards events." -- American poet Sylvia Plath

Personality determines destiny, mindset determines height. There is a good phrase describing negativity well: it is like the default option that appears when installing software. If we do not take positive action, we unconsciously choose negativity. No one is born lucky, even if they are born into wealthy or noble families, the decline of such families is not uncommon. On the contrary, stories of a chicken coop producing a phoenix or an ugly duckling turning into a swan are more inspiring. The founder of success studies, Li Jian, summarized eight symptoms of negativity: lack of goals, fear of failure, fear of rejection, complaints and blame, denial of the present, giving up halfway, pessimism about the future, aiming too high. Negativity also produces six consequences: losing opportunities, shattering hope, limiting potential development, consuming 90% of energy, losing support, and failing to fully enjoy life. When life requires you to endure pain, besides being strong, there is no choice. And this strength is positivity and optimism, avoiding negativity!

5. Why should people learn to be forgiving?

"The moral impact of forgiveness is much stronger than that of punishment." -- Soviet educator Sukhomlinsky

The cultural history of the world is actually a history of progress from intolerance to tolerance, from slave societies to feudal societies, and then to capitalist societies, where tolerance continues to develop. Forgiveness is a power, forgiveness is an enjoyment. To love others and forgive others, the prerequisite is first to love yourself and forgive yourself. Forgiveness is not only a virtue, a mindset, a realm, but also a wisdom. Life is just a game of calling and being called, and people are happiest when they receive and give love.

6. Why should people learn to think (study)?

"A person who does not make up his mind to cultivate the habit of thinking loses the greatest pleasure in life." -- American inventor Edison

Thinking is a kind of enjoyment, I think therefore I am happy. Eating well is merely a qualitative change, not a quantitative change (changes in the heart and brain), and a person will not feel true happiness simply by eating well. Happiness can only be obtained by the heart and brain. Many people work hard all their lives just to satisfy the mouth, satisfying the eyes, ears, nose, and throat, but never think about satisfying the two most important organs: the heart and brain. We should live for our hearts (mindset and character) and brains (thought and wisdom). A person's life is a process of going from knowing to not knowing, and then from not knowing to knowing again, which is actually a process of learning and thinking. Thinking is the laser focused on the concept of things, forming the structure of things in the background. Thinking is the argumentative dialogue of the soul with itself. Thoughts and spirit are the true wealth of a person. Material and money are not, they are temporarily registered under your name, and one day you will have to return them to others, to society. Cultivate your thoughts, even if you are not a thinker, you should have your own thoughts, thoughts are the source of your happiness.

7. Why should people learn to experience?

"The whole meaning of life lies in endlessly exploring the unknown, and continuously increasing knowledge." -- French writer Zola

Life is like a glass of wine. The same glass of wine, if you cry while drinking it, you will find it bitter; if you smile while drinking it, you will find it sweet. This is experience. From experiences, we can gain insights, attitudes, and wisdom. The key is what you experience and what you realize. There is nothing absolutely right or wrong in this world, right and wrong are relative. If a person keeps experiencing success, he may not necessarily really improve or progresses slowly, conversely, if a person often experiences failure, but he is tirelessly pursuing and not repeating the same mistakes, he grows in "pain and joy" and will surely become a successful person.

8. Why should people learn to choose?

"Choose what you like, love what you choose." -- Russian writer Leo Tolstoy

The best choice is not necessarily choosing the best. Life offers no best choices, only taking responsibility for your own choices! Therefore, learning how to choose is very important. Women fear marrying the wrong man, men fear entering the wrong profession. Whether carrying gold or bricks, you must let go of them to travel light, to be relaxed, composed, and calm. You will be energetic, spirited, and full of vitality. People need to learn to choose, first, they must learn to let go. Giving up means gaining, without giving up, there is no gain. People need to let go of everything they think belongs to them, including letting go of the experience of success and the lessons of failure, to face new challenges in the best posture.

9. Why do people want to succeed?

"Setting goals, working, succeeding, are the three major elements of human activity." -- French microbiologist Pasteur

Simply put, success is: unity of knowledge and action. China speaks of harmony between heaven and humanity. Success is a human instinct, the best way to gain praise and respect, though sometimes it brings jealousy. Everyone wants to have influence, and success is the best way to gain influence. So-called success is actually one's own evaluation and opinion of oneself, while failure is others' evaluation and opinion of you. Another definition of success: success is doing what you want to do, doing what you want to do, going where you want to go, saying what you want to say. Just like asking why people work, those who work certainly want to succeed, those with ambition, working is beautiful. Tell everyone a good way to not hate work and to live well: treat work as life, treat life as work. Success is always relative, just like happiness is always relative. Do you think having money equals happiness? Wrong, regardless of whether the spirit is fulfilled, when you regard money as the only source of happiness, when you meet someone richer than you, the sense of happiness quickly disappears. Success is getting what you want; happiness is liking what you have. Successful people are not necessarily happy, but happy people are definitely successful, because we come into this life to seek happiness.

10. Why do many successful people not feel happy?

"Success has two elements: external success and internal success. Material success is measurable external success, which is a low level of freedom, while mental maturity and freedom are higher-level internal successes, and happiness often comes from the latter." -- Brand expert Xu Haoran

Happiness does not lie in possessing success, but in the joy of achieving success and the passion of creativity. Many people succeed, but they are not necessarily happy. Success has two factors: external success and internal success. Material success is measurable external success, while mental maturity and freedom are higher-level internal successes, and happiness often stems from the latter. Many people in high positions or extremely wealthy, or people considered successful, often lack freedom in action and thought, especially in thought. One believes they are successful, that is success; one believes they are not successful, that is not success. But failure is different; even if you do not believe you failed, others may consider you a failure. However, many people use others' judgment as the standard for their own success or failure, so they fail. Therefore: success is relative, failure is absolute. Some people have a lot of money, material freedom, but lack spiritual freedom, having no leisure time to appreciate flowers, trees, and the beauty of life, which only experiencers can truly feel and enjoy.

Relatively speaking, I admire happy people more than successful ones. So I would rather wish everyone happiness than success. Learn to seek happiness, feel happiness, and share happiness.
