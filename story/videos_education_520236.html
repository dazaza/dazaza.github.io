Key Points for On-the-job Postgraduate Entrance Examination in 2012

by shuoshi on 2011-10-25 16:44:04

First trick: Avoid rote memorization

At the beginning of preparing for the postgraduate review, avoid rote memorization of textbooks. After so many years of exams, everyone should understand that in the review, we must be able to "thin" the book and also make it "thicker". Avoid rote memorization. Candidates only need to start from the exam outline, with the guidance of improving ability, comprehensively review relevant knowledge and skills, encounter problems, break through the boundaries of thinking to think more, and grasp it more from the macro perspective of psychology.

Second trick: Confidence

Confidence is one of the most critical factors in determining whether a person succeeds or not. Whether in the postgraduate examination or in the interview after the preliminary test, candidates must be confident. The most effective way to improve and strengthen the confidence of candidates is to continuously improve their abilities and research literacy.

Third trick: Utilize every available opportunity

There are many factors that determine the success or failure of the postgraduate examination. Li Ning also said, "Everything is possible," so before the final result is settled, candidates cannot give up any opportunity for learning, improving their own abilities, or other potential opportunities. They must have the right attitude, actively prepare and review, and the key is to learn knowledge and improve their own abilities.

Fourth trick: Change of concept

In the preparation and review, the examinee should try to think about problems from the perspective and vision of a researcher, changing the original cramming-style learning to problem-centered thinking and understanding, constantly seeking the best solutions and inferring results.
