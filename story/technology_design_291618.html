My hometown is in Chizhou, Anhui, where the four major Buddhist holy sites, "Jiuhua Mountain," belongs to the region where my hometown is located. Jiuhua Mountain naturally became the place we traveled far to visit when we were young. I still remember clearly that when I was young, I always stood outside the temple unwilling to enter because I was carrying the Young Pioneers flag and the Youth League flag.

When I grew up a bit, I learned that the reason why Jiuhua Mountain has such a thriving incense offering is because of the statement by Ksitigarbha Bodhisattva: "If hell is not empty, I cannot become a Buddha." What kind of realm is this... And among many Buddhist temples and mountains, only Jiuhua Mountain has many uncorrupted bodies. Interestingly, those who can become uncorrupted bodies are mostly ordinary low-ranking monks or nuns similar to servants, which seems like a kind of satire.

Later, in order to learn English, I dealt with Christian missionaries in Haikou and saw how Western believers conflicted with each other, leading to various wars and massacres. Comparing the war history of the East and West, the East mostly fought for "beauty," while the West had more wars among believers to maintain their faith.

Afterward, following Li Shutong's footsteps, I stayed in Hangzhou, known as the "Southeastern Buddhist Country." Over the years, I visited the top temple Lingyin Temple, the top nunnery Qiuxue Nunnery; originally the Lingyin Sutra Hall, now a place for nuns to study Buddhist scriptures, Fajing Temple, where I often went to listen to them chant scriptures and preach Buddhism when I was sick in 2003, standing there for two or three hours; further up, Faying Temple, known as Zhong Tianzhu, now a Buddhist academy; and finally, the lecture hall at the end of Tiandu Road, Shang Tianzhu; Jingci Temple under Nanping Mountain, where I often stopped between Zhang Cangshui's and Zhang Taiyan's graves to listen to the evening bells of Nanping and the rustling leaves; Jingshan is truly a place beyond the mundane; Zhenji Temple on Wuyun Mountain; and the place I visited the most naturally was Hupao, where Li Shutong fasted and became a monk...
