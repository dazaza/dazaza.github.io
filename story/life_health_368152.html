Source: Sina, October 16, 2010, 04:44 AM. Nandu News reporter Yan Hua reported that yesterday morning, the Dongguan municipal government once again held a city-wide prevention and control meeting regarding the Chikungunya epidemic. Mayor Li Yuquan of Dongguan clearly stated at the meeting that the reason for holding this meeting again was because some towns and streets did not take it seriously after the last prevention and control meeting, "and some towns and streets basically did not carry out any work." It is understood that as of 16:00 on October 11, Dongguan had reported a total of 270 Chikungunya cases, all of which were mild cases with no critical or fatal cases.

"Currently, all the cases in Dongguan are concentrated in a relatively small area. We must take this opportunity to quickly and thoroughly eliminate the disease, otherwise, this disease will spread every year," said Liu Qi Yong, a researcher at the Institute of Infectious Disease Prevention and Control of the Chinese Center for Disease Control and Prevention at the meeting. "Currently, Dongguan's prevention and control mechanisms and measures are relatively complete."

Deputy Mayor Wu Daowen of Dongguan required that all towns in the city should concentrate on mosquito eradication actions within the next three days, while simultaneously launching a nationwide patriotic public health campaign mainly focused on preventing and controlling Chikungunya fever. A relevant official from the Municipal Health Bureau said that at that time, each town would conduct cross-checks, and disease control personnel from hospitals in each town and street would go to each community to monitor the Breteau index.

"In the face of such infectious diseases, our thinking cannot be麻痹 (numb), measures cannot be sloppy, and work cannot be irresponsible! And with the upcoming Asian Games, everyone knows how urgent this work is," emphasized Li Yuquan, stressing that we must prevent Chikungunya from becoming a common disease in Dongguan or even nationwide. While doing a good job in mosquito prevention and eradication, relevant departments must track the currently appearing cases, "we need to understand where they have been, which families they have come into contact with, and those places need to be checked more carefully."
