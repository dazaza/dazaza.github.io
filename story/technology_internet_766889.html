Twitter Equity Structure Revealed: Co-founder Becomes Largest Shareholder

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 22:35:41

Twitter co-founder Evan Williams

According to a report published by Bloomberg on the morning of September 26 Beijing time, citing unnamed sources, Twitter co-founder Evan Williams (Evan Williams) is currently the largest shareholder of the company, owning approximately 15% of its shares. After Twitter's IPO (Initial Public Offering), Williams will become a billionaire with assets exceeding $1 billion.

Sources said that Williams' shareholding percentage surpasses that of another Twitter co-founder, Jack Dorsey (Jack Dorsey). Earlier this month, Twitter announced that it had confidentially submitted IPO documents to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

If Twitter's market capitalization after the IPO exceeds its current valuation in the private market, which is $10.5 billion, then Williams' stake would be worth more than $1.5 billion. If Twitter's IPO price is higher or if there is a significant increase in stock price on the first day of listing, Williams could join the ranks of ultra-rich individuals like Bill Gates, Mark Zuckerberg, Larry Ellison, and Jeff Bezos.

Maha Ibrahim, a partner at California venture capital firm Canaan Partners, said: "This demonstrates his long-standing belief in Twitter. He had many opportunities to sell his shares on the secondary market." Neither Williams nor a Twitter spokesperson commented on the news.

Other Twitter shareholders whose holdings are close to $1 billion include Jack Dorsey and venture capital firm Union Square Ventures. In 2007, Fred Wilson, a partner at Union Square Ventures, made the first round of investment in Twitter when the company was valued at only $20 million.

In 2003, Williams sold his previous website, Blogger, to Google and invested the proceeds into the development of Twitter. In 2005, Williams founded a podcasting company called Odeo, but it ultimately failed. Subsequently, Williams collaborated with Dorsey and Biz Stone (Biz Stone) to establish Twitter, which now has over 200 million users.

From 2008 to 2010, Williams served as CEO of Twitter, during which time the user base grew rapidly. In October 2010, Dick Costolo (Dick Costolo), then Twitter's head of operations, replaced Williams as CEO. In 2011, Williams left Twitter and established a new startup called Medium, offering media publishing services.

Facebook's IPO last year created at least four billionaires with wealth exceeding $1 billion, including CEO Mark Zuckerberg, co-founders Dustin Moskovitz and Eduardo Saverin, and former president Sean Parker. During LinkedIn's IPO in 2011, co-founder Reid Hoffman also surpassed $1 billion in assets.
