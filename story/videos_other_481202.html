No pet cats

by xlockf93 on 2011-08-24 16:46:14

In the workplace, there are no pet cats. When the master (the company) feeds the cat fish (high salary and good position), the cat must not forget to catch mice (do the work that should be done). Otherwise, it will be difficult for him to achieve long-term steady growth in the workplace!

Two managers with different attitudes and luck

Remy and Cui Jing entered a company at the same time. Remy was in the planning department, and Cui Jing was in the finance department. After years of hard work, they finally became the managers of their respective departments.

After becoming department managers, Cui Jing quickly felt the "benefits" of being a leader: not only higher wages, but also the ability to command subordinates to complete tasks that should belong to her. Every day, Cui Jing surfed the internet for news or chatted, living a very comfortable life. Remy, on the other hand, seemed destined to be busy. Even after becoming a department manager, she still worked hard every day. Every day, watching Remy frown while thinking about marketing strategies, Cui Jing would laugh, thinking why didn't she just delegate these tasks to her subordinates? Why does she have to do it herself?

The planning department directly handled some external activities of the company. From planning to the end of the activity, Remy was involved throughout, getting exhausted every day. The boss would always show concern when seeing Remy: "Remy, you need to rest! Working so hard is not good. Let me tell you something, can you plan an event soon to promote our latest product with minimal cost?" Remy laughed inwardly, thinking how interesting the boss was, advising her to rest but then assigning her a new task immediately. The boss realized this awkwardness and smiled: "There's no way around it, capable people have more responsibilities. You'll have to work hard, but after this period, I'll give you a vacation." Remy didn't expect the boss to actually give her a vacation; all she wanted was for the boss to know how hard she worked every day.

Another two years passed, and the company underwent a major personnel adjustment. Remy was promoted by the boss to become the vice president. But what everyone didn't expect was that Cui Jing was dismissed, replaced by a very diligent subordinate from the finance department. Cui Jing now became a subordinate to her former subordinate, which was a blow she couldn't bear. What made it worse was that the seemingly foolish Remy got promoted. How could this happen?

When in a high position, one must still do their own job well.

Thus, when Cui Jing met Remy again, she deliberately looked up with her face raised to express her dissatisfaction! Remy responded with a bitter smile.

One day, Remy found an opportunity to talk to Cui Jing. She told a story: "When I was little, we had a yellow cat and a spotted cat at home. My mom, fearing the cats would starve, bought catfish from the market every day. Over time, the two cats showed significant differences: the yellow cat only liked eating catfish, sleeping or playing in the yard after eating, never catching mice; the spotted cat caught mice while eating catfish, often hiding in corners to catch mice even after being full. A year later, the whole family disliked the yellow cat that didn't catch mice and drove it out of the house; the spotted cat, on the other hand, was always treated kindly by the family until it naturally grew old and died... Of course, nowadays many people keep cats not for catching mice, but purely as pets. I'm telling you this because in the workplace, there are no pet cats. When given fish (high salary and good position), one must not forget to catch mice (doing the work one should do). When you were a department manager, you enjoyed the 'fish' (high salary and good position) while avoiding your own responsibilities, delegating your work to your subordinates. The boss naturally got angry with your behavior. Therefore, removing your 'fish' (high salary and position) was normal..."

After hearing Remy's words, Cui Jing suddenly saw things clearly. Her resentment towards the company disappeared, and she deeply regretted not taking her work seriously back then...

In the workplace, one must always remember "even if you eat fish, you still need to catch mice." Only in this way will you not lose your 'fish', and be able to achieve long-term steady growth in the workplace!
