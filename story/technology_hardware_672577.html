Maintenance of Transparent White Light Glasses:

1. Follow the instructions on the solution for maintenance. Each bottle of maintenance solution emphasizes different points and does not necessarily differ in terms of good or bad. "Actually, proper maintenance can achieve the same cleaning effect." Additionally, pay attention to the production date and expiration date of the maintenance solution, do not use expired solution. Discard the maintenance solution three months after opening even if it is not finished, and do not continue to use it.

2. Regularly replace transparent white light glasses. Even long-wear types, including hard transparent white light glasses, should be updated every 1-2 years. Do not treat daily wear as bi-weekly wear or bi-weekly wear as monthly wear, extending the usage period will harm the cornea.

3. Fixed removal of proteins. If using hydrogen peroxide solution, remove proteins daily before soaking in the solution; those using multi-functional maintenance solutions are advised to remove proteins weekly to reduce infection opportunities.

4. Lenses cannot be stored in physiological saline. Physiological saline has no bactericidal effect, storing lenses in it can easily grow bacteria.

5. Clean the storage box at least once a week and replace it every three months to avoid becoming a breeding ground for bacteria. The cleaning method can involve wiping the inside of the small box with cotton balls dipped in cleaning solution, then disinfecting in hot water above 79℃, then drying, and try not to place it in the bathroom because dampness can easily cause mold.

6. Do not soak lenses in solution for long periods, as the solution will gradually lose its disinfectant function, instead becoming a breeding ground for bacteria. For those who do not often wear transparent white light glasses, it is best to change the soaking solution every two days, and remember to re-disinfect and clean before wearing.

7. Do not wear transparent white light glasses when swimming. Chlorine, bacteria, impurities, etc., in the swimming pool can adhere to contact lenses, harming corneal tissue or causing infections, and even corneal ulcers.

8. If you want to change the solution due to seeing new products on the market or being introduced by friends, you must pay special attention to the condition after using the new solution. If there are symptoms such as redness, swelling, itching, foreign body sensation, dryness, tearing, secretions, etc., stop wearing immediately and confirm whether it is an eye or solution problem. If caused by the solution, it is suggested to stop using it and return to the original solution.

Protection of Glasses:

1. Single-handed removal and wearing will destroy the left-right balance of the frame, leading to deformation. It is recommended to hold both sides of the temple along the cheeks parallel direction for removal and wearing.

2. When taking off and putting on, generally fold the left temple first to prevent frame deformation.

3. It is recommended to rinse glasses with clear water and dry with a paper towel, then use a dedicated eyeglass cloth to wipe. Hold the edge of the frame on the side being wiped lightly and gently wipe the lens to avoid excessive force that could damage the frame or lens.

4. When not wearing glasses, please wrap them in a cloth and put them in the case. If temporarily placed, please place the convex side up, otherwise it is easy to scratch the lens. At the same time, glasses should avoid contact with insect repellents, toilet cleaning supplies, cosmetics, hair gel, medicines, etc., corrosive substances, and avoid prolonged direct sunlight and high temperature (above 60℃) placement, otherwise it can lead to degradation, deterioration, discoloration, etc., of the lens and frame.

5. Regularly visit professional stores for reshaping adjustments. Frame deformation can burden the nose and ears, and the lens can easily loosen.

6. Do not use glasses during intense sports to avoid strong impact causing the lens to break, causing injury to the eyes and face; do not use worn lenses to prevent light scattering causing vision decline; do not look directly at the sun or strong glaring light to avoid injuring the eyes.
