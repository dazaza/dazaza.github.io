Henan Zhengzhou Strong Brick Making Machine Turns Construction Waste into Treasure

In recent years, Henan Strong has found that construction waste generated from urban new buildings, house renovations, expansions, reconstructions, and re-planning of old buildings has reached 2.3 billion tons per year. If such a huge amount of construction waste is not dealt with, it will undoubtedly bring serious consequences to our entire society and future generations. After expert verification, the unfired brick machine, cement brick machine, hollow brick machine, block brick machine, and spacer machine produced by Henan Strong Road & Bridge Engineering Co., Ltd. are the most suitable for processing construction waste!

Currently, there are mainly two methods for handling construction waste in China. The first method is collection and landfilling, which not only incurs high transportation costs but also occupies a large amount of land, polluting land resources; the second method is comprehensive recycling and utilization, which generally involves using construction waste and various inorganic chemical materials to undergo reactions at normal or high temperatures, solidification, and pressing into building wall materials. Although this method is currently one of the better ways for recycling and utilizing waste, it still has shortcomings, mainly manifested in: the cost of firing bricks is relatively high, and there are issues of secondary pollution.

To improve the utilization rate of construction waste in China and enable construction waste to be better recycled on a large scale through the production of building wall materials, it is imperative to study new technologies for making building wall materials. Henan Strong Machinery Co., Ltd., in order to fully utilize construction waste as a renewable resource, has developed brick-making equipment specifically for processing construction waste based on its components and application fields after processing. Construction waste, using construction debris as raw material, is crushed and screened to be used as aggregate, mixed with cement, additives, and water to form a mixture, which can be turned into various new types of building materials such as concrete solid bricks, paving bricks, wall materials, and recycled pre-mixed concrete according to need.

Construction waste is not actually "waste." After reasonable resource recovery and utilization, it can once again become a valuable resource for people's use. Construction waste is a persistent problem in urban construction, and the issue of resource recovery and utilization has garnered attention from all sectors of society. It is believed that the widespread application of construction waste processing equipment will usher in new horizons for urban development.

Nature itself does not have absolute waste or useless things. What is waste or useless now may become a treasure or useful item elsewhere or at another time. It is clear that comprehensively utilizing construction waste is an effective way to save resources and protect the ecosystem.
