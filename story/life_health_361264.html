Zizaiyuan | Froggy Rice

by dragonsoft on 2010-07-27 17:03:39

"In the fragrance of the rice flowers, we speak of a bountiful year, listening to the chorus of frogs" – Zizaishan "Frog Rice" uses high-quality Japanese rice varieties such as "Hitomebore" and renowned Jiangnan varieties like "Laolaiqing" and "Dongfan" for its seeds, planted purely through natural green methods. Throughout the process, no chemical fertilizers or pesticides are applied. An organic biological chain circulates benignly in the paddy fields: tiger frogs are raised in the paddies to prey on pests, their droppings serve as organic fertilizer; the rice nurtures the frogs, and the frogs protect the rice. Scientific cultivation leads to abundant rice harvests with fragrant grains.

The tiger frog is large in size, measuring 8-13.5 cm in length, with adult frogs weighing between 125-175 grams. It is a natural enemy of over 30 species of crop pests, including stem borers, rice leaf rollers, rice locusts, mole crickets, crickets, and leafhoppers, earning it the title of "guardian of crops." Statistics show that one tiger frog can consume ten thousand insects in a year, devouring up to 260 insects in a single day. Tiger frog meat is delicious and highly nutritious, containing 25% more protein than beef, making it a naturally exquisite delicacy.

The paddy fields of Zizaishan "Frog Rice" have remained pure and unpolluted for decades. Within the Qingpu Modern Agricultural Park, a high-tech "integrated artificial wetland water treatment system" has been implemented, elevating the water quality to Class II standards. According to measurements, all indicators of river water quality improve significantly after passing through this artificial wetland. Under the action of pumps, the purified water flows towards vegetable greenhouses, rice paddies, and fish ponds, providing clean water sources for the agricultural park.

The "Hitomebore" rice variety originates from Japan and is considered one of the world's top-tier rice varieties. Before its introduction, it was only sold in limited quantities at high-end department stores. Zizaishan Frog Rice maintains the original product quality, appearing as clear and white as jade, exuding a fresh aroma, and tasting soft with a hint of sweetness.

The Qingjiao Thin Rice was documented as early as the Ming Dynasty in *Tiangong Kaiwu*: "One type of fragrant rice is chosen for its aromatic scent to serve the nobility, but its yield is very low." Qingjiao Thin Rice was an imperial tribute during the Ming and Qing Dynasties. As the supreme variety of fragrant rice, it features moderate stickiness, a crystal-clear and translucent appearance, and a fragrant, palatable taste.

Zizaishan Frog Rice is rich in nutritional value, with an elliptical shape, moderate length and width, and plump grains.
