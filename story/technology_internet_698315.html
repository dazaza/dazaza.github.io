We analyze the power consumption situation using this LED small module! As shown in Figure 2, this is an LED small module driven by the CYT62726 chip introduced by Changyuntong Optoelectronics. Its supply voltage is 5V. First, we do not calculate the power consumption of peripheral components since they account for a very small proportion of the entire screen. Thus, all the power consumed by the screen is on the lights. We first calculate the light point power as Pled = n * Uvf * Iled (n is the number of channels, Uvf is the voltage drop of the LED light point, and Iled is the set current value). The pin voltage drop of the CYT62726 drive IC is generally around 0.6V. The voltage drops of the red, green, and blue light points are 1.8V, 3.0V, and 3.0V respectively. Therefore, each channel only needs 4V (3.0 + 0.6V) to work normally. Conservatively, it can be set at 2.8V for the red channel and 3.8V for the blue and green channels. However, our actual supply voltage is 5V, which is equivalent to increasing the power consumption of 1V * Iled inside the IC. Therefore, as imagined above, if we reduce the supply voltage to 2.8V for red, 3.8V for green, and 3.8V for blue, we can save the power consumption of 1V * Iled on the IC channels. Under unchanged conditions of other components, we can achieve at least 15% energy saving on the LED display. Moreover, reducing the heat dissipation requirement of the LED screen can also realize energy saving to a certain extent. For large screens, this is already a significant number, and it is believed that customers will be happy to accept it!
