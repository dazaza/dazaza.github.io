Respect, the power that most can conquer hearts.

by dunk4666 on 2010-04-12 19:13:25

To his surprise, this general turned out to be an ill-tempered and rude person. Maybe because of the military affairs on his shoulders and the inability to travel, he went out every day to inquire about the situation. Upon returning, he would curse heaven and earth in the house. Seeing Mazu Daoyi meditating there day by day, the general always had a look of contempt on his face, often mocking: "Isn't your Buddha omnipotent and compassionate? Why doesn't he have mercy on the people and let floods ravage the world? You sit here practicing Zen every day, aren't you also stuck here and unable to move forward?" Mazu Daoyi did not pay attention to the general's rudeness and mockery, continuing to practice Zen as usual. When Mazu Daoyi was young, he once traveled all over the world, seeking teachers and learning. Once, while on the road seeking knowledge, he encountered heavy rain, and the flood blocked the way. He could only stay at an inn, waiting for the flood to recede. Because there were many people staying at the inn, he was arranged to share a room with a general.

Mazu Daoyi continued with his own studies. After finishing, he got up and carefully wiped the general's dirty boots clean, then placed them in front of the general's bed.

One evening, the general returned from outside, and Mazu Daoyi sat there as if he had entered the realm of "no self." The general saw that he seemed like a statue made of clay or wood, showing no reaction when he came in. Feeling angry without any outlet, when going to bed, he casually threw the two mud-covered boots at Mazu Daoyi. The boots hit him, giving him quite a fright. Seeing Mazu Daoyi's slightly startled eyes, the general even showed a hint of smugness, then lay down on the bed and fell asleep.

It was this pair of polished boots that deeply taught the general a lesson: the most powerful force to conquer hearts comes precisely from respect for others. Later, when this general was stationed in the north, although he commanded a large army, he never glorified in conquests and killings. Instead, he took it upon himself to pacify and respect the nomadic tribes in the north. During his tenure, all ethnic groups along the border lived in peace.

The next morning, when the general got up, he found his boots in front of the bed, spotlessly clean. A thought stirred within him. Recalling his behavior from the previous night, he felt extremely ashamed and apologized to Mazu Daoyi. Mazu Daoyi simply said calmly: "I just polished the general's boots." The general, moved, said: "No, you've polished my heart. Although I am a rough man, I have understood the meaning of being moved from you."
