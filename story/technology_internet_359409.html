What is natto?

by yy1s21 on 2010-06-29 11:06:18

       Natto (NATTO) originated in China. Natto (NATTO) is similar to fermented beans and strange-flavored beans in China. Ancient books (He Han San Cai Tu Hui) record: "Natto (NATTO) started to be made since the Qin and Han dynasties in China." Natto (NATTO) initially stemmed from Chinese fermented black beans. According to the book "Food Culture • Fresh Market" (authored by Ishige Naomichi, page 101), it introduces that both types of natto (NATTO) have ties with China. Especially salty natto (NATTO), which was approximately introduced to Japan during the Nara and Heian periods by Zen monks. Natto (NATTO) was also called "chi" in Japan, and the character "chi" was found on wooden slips excavated from Heijō-kyō. It is the same as the fermented black beans consumed by modern Chinese. Since the fermented black beans were produced at the temples' storage areas and then stored in jars or barrels, Japanese people called them "Tang Natto (NATTO)" or "Salty Natto (NATTO)." The Japanese used it as a nutritional food and seasoning, while the Chinese cooked or steamed fermented black beans for use as a seasoning.

       After natto (NATTO) was introduced to Japan, it developed according to Japan's climate and geography. For instance, instead of using fermented black beans, they used miso or soy sauce instead of bean juice. Moreover, since it was spread from China to Japanese temples by Zen monks, natto (NATTO) first developed within temples. For example, natto from Dairi Temple, Daitokuji Temple natto, Ikkyu natto, Hamana natto from Daifukuji Temple, and Yatsuhashi natto from Goshin Temple all became famous local specialties of temples.

       Japanese people like to eat natto (NATTO). They mainly consume salty natto (NATTO) and stringy natto (NATTO); people from Kansai prefer the former, while those from Kanto favor the latter. Stringy natto (NATTO) produces sticky threads due to a different fermentation method and contains no salt.

       In recent years, research by Japanese medical scientists and physiologists has shown that soy protein is insoluble, but when made into natto (NATTO), it becomes soluble and generates amino acids. Additionally, various enzymes that do not exist in raw materials are produced due to the natto (NATTO) bacteria and related bacteria, aiding in digestion and absorption in the gastrointestinal tract. The components of natto (NATTO) are: 61.8% water, 19.26% crude protein, 8.17% crude fat, 6.09% carbohydrates, 2.2% crude fiber, and 1.86% ash. As a plant-based food, it is rich in crude protein and fat. Natto (NATTO) is a high-protein nourishing food. The alcohol enzyme contained in natto (NATTO), when consumed, can eliminate some cholesterol in the body, decompose oxidized lipids in the body, and normalize abnormal blood pressure (Body Science magazine, January 1994 issue, published by Nihon Hyoronsha, page 4).

       Natto (NATTO) suits Japan's climate and geography. The staple food of Japanese people is rice, generally eaten in granular form. Natto (NATTO) is also in granular form, making it feel familiar to the Japanese. Eating natto (NATTO) helps digest rice and makes Japanese people who love polished white rice less prone to cerebrovascular diseases. There are many ways to eat natto (NATTO). In the purchased natto (NATTO), mustard and seasonings are usually included; after stirring, it can be eaten directly, often paired with rice. You can also add green onions or seaweed based on personal preference. Due to the revelation of its nutritional value, more and more people are consuming it. Nowadays, with increasingly advanced food processing technology, natto (NATTO) has been made into many different flavors, allowing individuals to choose based on taste and needs.

       Studies show that the health benefits of natto (NATTO) are mainly related to functional factors such as nattokinase, natto isoflavones, saponins, vitamin K2, etc., contained in natto (NATTO). Natto (NATTO) is rich in saponins, which can improve constipation, lower blood lipids, prevent colon cancer, reduce cholesterol, soften blood vessels, prevent hypertension and arteriosclerosis, inhibit the AIDS virus, etc.; natto (NATTO) contains free isoflavones and various beneficial enzymes for the human body, such as superoxide dismutase, catalase, protease, amylase, lipase, etc. These can clear carcinogenic substances in the body, significantly improve memory, liver protection and beauty, delay aging, and increase the digestibility of food; ingesting live natto (NATTO) bacteria can regulate the balance of intestinal flora, preventing dysentery, enteritis, and constipation, with effects superior to commonly used lactic acid bacteria probiotics in some aspects; the sticky substance produced by natto (NATTO) fermentation coats the mucous membranes of the gastrointestinal tract, thus protecting the stomach and intestines, and alleviating drunkenness when drinking alcohol.
