So what exactly is acrylic?

by bjpmmac on 2009-11-22 10:49:11

1. The term "acrylic" may sound unfamiliar because it is a relatively new word that has only appeared in mainland China in the past two years. It wasn't until 2002 that it began to be gradually understood by a few people in the advertising, furniture, and handicraft industries. "Acrylic" is a transliteration of a foreign word; its English name is ACRYLIC. It is a chemical material with the chemical name "PMMA," belonging to the propylene alcohol class, commonly known as "specially treated organic glass." In the application industry, its raw materials generally appear in the form of granules, sheets, and tubes.

2. Acrylic Sheets and Organic Glass: "Organic glass" originates from the English term Organic Glass. In recent years, in some areas, all transparent plastic-made sheets have been collectively referred to as organic glass, which is incorrect. Acrylic specifically refers to pure polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA) material, and PMMA sheets are called acrylic sheets.

3. Characteristics and Advantages of Acrylic Sheets: Acrylic has high transparency, with a light transmittance of 92%, earning it the reputation of "plastic crystal." It also has excellent weather resistance, especially when used outdoors, surpassing other plastics. It has good surface hardness and luster, and can be processed into various required shapes and products. Furthermore, there are many types of sheets with rich colors (including semi-transparent color sheets). Another feature is that even thick sheets can maintain high transparency.

4. History of Acrylic: Acrylic (ACRYLIC), commonly known as specially treated organic glass, has over a hundred years of research history. In 1872, the polymerization of acrylic acid was first discovered; in 1880, the polymerization of methyl methacrylate became known; in 1901, the synthesis method of acrylic polypropionate was completed; in 1927, industrial manufacturing was attempted using the aforementioned synthesis method; and in 1937, the industrial manufacturing of methyl methacrylate ester was successfully developed, entering large-scale production of acrylic. During World War II, due to its excellent toughness and transparency, acrylic was first applied to aircraft windshields and tank drivers' vision mirrors. In 1948, the world's first acrylic bathtub was born, marking a new milestone in acrylic applications.

5. Applications of Acrylic: PMMA has the advantages of being lightweight and cost-effective, making it easy to mold. Its molding methods include casting, injection molding, mechanical processing, and heat forming. Especially injection molding allows for mass production with simple processes and low costs. Therefore, its applications have become increasingly widespread. Currently, it is widely used in instrument parts, car headlights, optical lenses, transparent pipes, etc.

Introduction to Acrylic: Acrylic comes from the transliteration of the English word Acrylics, which is the general term for acrylic and methacrylic chemicals. This includes monomers, sheets, pellets, resins, and composite materials. Acrylic sheets are made by polymerizing methyl methacrylate monomer (MMA), i.e., polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA) sheets, commonly known as "organic glass." "Organic glass" originates from the brand name "Oroglas" (a type of PMMA sheet), derived from "Organic Glass." However, in recent years, all transparent plastics such as PS and PC have been collectively referred to as organic glass.

Acrylic Production Technology: Acrylic sheets can be divided into cast and extruded types based on production technology. Cast sheets have better performance than extruded ones but are more expensive. Cast sheets are mainly used for engraving, decoration, and craft production, while extruded sheets are typically used for advertising signs and light boxes.

Characteristics of Acrylic:

1. Excellent Transparency: Colorless transparent acrylic sheets have a light transmittance of over 92%.

2. Outstanding Weather Resistance: Strong adaptability to natural environments ensures no change in performance even after prolonged exposure to sunlight, wind, and rain. Good aging resistance makes it suitable for outdoor use.

3. Good Processability: Suitable for both mechanical and thermal forming processes. Acrylic sheets can be dyed, painted, silk-screened, or vacuum-coated.

4. Superior Comprehensive Performance: A wide variety of acrylic sheets come in rich colors and possess excellent comprehensive properties, offering diverse choices for designers. Acrylic sheets can be dyed, painted, silk-screened, or vacuum-coated.

5. Non-Toxic: Long-term contact with humans is harmless, and burning does not produce toxic gases.

Product Advantages:

1. Hardness: Hardness is one of the key parameters reflecting the production technology and quality control of cast acrylic sheets. It reflects the purity of the MMA raw material, the weather resistance of the sheets, and their high-temperature performance. Hardness directly affects whether the sheets will contract, bend, deform, or develop cracks during processing. Hardness is one of the critical indicators for evaluating the quality of acrylic sheets. Our company's imported-material cast acrylic sheets have the highest hardness index among domestic equivalents, averaging around 89 degrees on the Rockwell scale.

2. Thickness Tolerance: Control of sheet thickness tolerance is an important reflection of quality management and production technology. The thickness tolerance of imported-material cast acrylic sheets is controlled within +0.2mm.

3. Transparency/Whiteness: Strict raw material selection, advanced formula updates, and modern production processes ensure the excellent transparency and pure whiteness of the sheets. They become crystal clear after flame polishing.

Precautions:

1. Acrylic sheets should not be stored together with other organic solvents and must not come into contact with organic solvents.

2. During transportation, the surface protective film or paper should not be damaged.

3. Should not be used in environments exceeding 85℃.

4. When cleaning acrylic sheets, use 1% soapy water with a soft cloth. Do not use hard objects or dry wiping, as this can easily scratch the surface.

5. Acrylic sheets have a large thermal expansion coefficient, so temperature changes should be considered, and a stretch gap should be reserved.
