Plan One: Planning insurance purchases early

For Ms. Huang, who has just started working, at the beginning stage of her life planning, protection must be "targeted." From being a happy single person to eventually starting a family, young singles experience the biggest changes in living environment and lifestyle. This is an important phase where insurance should be used for precautionary measures, and arrangements should be made early.

1. Clarify the focus of protection. Most life insurance products are long-term, so before planning to purchase, a long-term financial plan must be made, such as dividend insurance. Taking protection insurance as an example, it mainly reflects responsibility to the family. The concept of family is different before and after marriage, and thus the responsibility to the family is also different, with a significant difference in the required amount. Since Ms. Huang is currently single and will face major life events like marriage, buying a house, and having children in the next few years, pension insurance is not the most urgent need at this stage.

As a newcomer to society with relatively low income and no significant family burden, priority should be given to personal accident and accidental medical protection; secondly, a certain amount of life insurance can be considered to fully prepare for repaying the kindness of her parents; meanwhile, taking advantage of her youth, she should prepare critical illness insurance as early as possible.

2. Place the demand for accidental insurance first. According to statistical data, young people have the highest rate of accidents. A major feature of accidental insurance is its low premium, making it the most economical and practical choice for newly employed young people. By spending about 200 yuan annually, one can obtain a general accidental insurance with a coverage of approximately 100,000 yuan. Regarding the selection of accidental insurance amounts, it is generally advisable to choose 5-10 times the annual income.

3. Match accidental medical insurance and hospitalization allowance insurance well. Although Ms. Huang participates in medical insurance, due to her short working period, her personal medical account reserve fund is insufficient, and the proportion of self-funded outpatient expenses is high. Therefore, it would be best if she could obtain accidental outpatient medical expense insurance. Additionally, some supplementary hospitalization allowance insurance can be added to make up for the shortcomings of medical insurance and provide compensation for lost wages.

4. Life insurance is a "moral policy" for parents. Although young people mostly die from accidents, there is still concern that if death occurs due to illness and only accidental insurance is purchased, parents would not receive compensation. Therefore, to prevent any mishaps, a certain amount of life insurance can be purchased. Making such an insurance plan at this stage can be considered preparing a "moral policy" for parents.

In health insurance, it is especially important to purchase critical illness insurance as early as possible. It is one of the indispensable insurances in life, and the earlier you subscribe, the more cost-effective it is.

From the perspective of amount arrangement, the author gives the following suggestions to Ms. Huang: accidental insurance coverage of 150,000 yuan, accidental medical coverage of 5,000 yuan, life insurance coverage of 100,000 yuan, critical illness insurance coverage of 100,000 yuan, and additional hospitalization allowance insurance. It is important to remind Ms. Huang: do not buy too much insurance because, as a young person, on one hand, economic payment ability is limited, and on the other hand, there are always other expenses. At this point, the purpose of purchasing insurance is to form a basic living protection. After economic conditions and other situations change, adjustments and modifications to the insurance plan can still be made.

Plan Two: Choose annual payment method

A popular saying nowadays is "If you don't manage your finances, your finances won't manage you," regardless of current income levels. Strong financial management desires combined with reasonable financial measures can certainly help young people achieve their wealth dreams, including old-age insurance.

Insurance, as an indispensable survival stabilizer for modern people, can make our lives more solid and comfortable. In life, we will go through many risk tests, such as unexpected illnesses, unforeseen accidents, property disasters, and liability risks caused by negligence, etc. Therefore, the author suggests that young people who have just entered society and begun independent lives start with the most basic protections and gradually increase protection items and amounts as income increases. Moreover, since the monthly payment rate for insurance is slightly higher, it is recommended to use the annual payment method.

Plan Analysis:

1. As Ms. Huang mentioned that her health is not good, the author suggests choosing critical illness insurance. This type of insurance allows Ms. Huang to be insured until the age of 65 (after the age of 55, human body functions gradually decline, and the risk of critical illness increases), maximizing protection. Moreover, under this clause, when she reaches the age of 60-69, she can receive an annual physical examination subsidy of 2,000 yuan, and at the age of 70, if no critical illness has occurred, she will receive a longevity bonus of 100,000 yuan.

2. Considering that Ms. Huang may have a higher chance of hospitalization than the average person, the author specially chose AIA Life's IPA plan for her. This plan has a reimbursement ratio that can reach 100%, with no deductible, unlimited number of claims per year, and includes coverage for accidents, illnesses, hospitalization subsidies, and surgical costs.

Insurance Purchase Tips:

Ms. Huang mentioned that her health is not good, but it is unclear whether it is due to weak constitution, a history of serious illness, or genetic factors. If it is due to the latter two reasons, insurance companies may require a physical examination or even reject the application when determining whether to underwrite, so prior consultation is necessary.

Plan Three: Best to purchase universal insurance

For people in their twenties who have just graduated and started working, they are exactly at the beginning of their career. People at this stage often exhibit the following characteristics: 1. Due to lack of work experience after graduation, income is less and unstable. 2. After receiving salary, they give part of it to their parents for household expenses. 3. Monthly expenses are large, and many people unfortunately become "moonlight clan" by the end of the month. 4. Must save money for marriage and housing. 5. Due to fierce job competition, they are often physically and mentally exhausted. Developing good financial habits and customizing a reasonable and suitable protection plan is very important.

Ms. Huang is a typical example. Her monthly income is only 2,000 yuan, and not only does she need to support her family every month, but she also needs to make regular investments in funds. Therefore, there is not much money left for her own disposal in her salary, so we must make big achievements with small money. The author believes that according to Ms. Huang's current financial situation, choosing to subscribe to a universal insurance policy is the most appropriate. First, the characteristic of universal insurance lies in its lower periodic premium and very flexible payment period, and the coverage can also be adjusted appropriately according to different stages of life. Secondly, the personal account income of universal insurance is relatively stable, which can reduce the investment risk brought by Ms. Huang's regular investment in funds, and the personal account value can also be partially withdrawn at any time, so as to prepare for emergencies. With such planning, Ms. Huang can have a high level of protection while also having a stable investment return.

The author recommends the following insurance plan for Ms. Huang: 1. Universal life insurance with a coverage amount of 100,000 yuan, with an annual periodic premium of 2,000 yuan, or 500 yuan per quarter; additionally, 1,200 yuan can be added annually as extra premium. In this way, both a certain coverage amount and a stable investment can be achieved. After 5 years, this universal insurance will no longer deduct initial fees, and as long as several hundred yuan of risk premiums and account management fees are paid, the policy will remain valid, giving Ms. Huang a lot of room for choices. In five or six years, Ms. Huang will be at marriage age, and there may be some major expenses in her finances, so it is suggested that Ms. Huang can choose to temporarily stop paying premiums at that time, and continue to pay the corresponding premiums when her finances stabilize. 2. Anxin term life insurance with a coverage amount of 150,000 yuan and a protection period of 25 years. Additionally, the author also recommends adding critical illness insurance with a coverage amount of 100,000 yuan and an insurance period of 30 years. 3. Wuyoubao accidental insurance with a coverage amount of 100,000 yuan, so if Ms. Huang unfortunately dies due to an accident, her family can receive up to 300,000 yuan in insurance benefits. 4. Although Ms. Huang's company has social security, she still has to bear some medical expenses when hospitalized, so it is appropriate to add some accidental injury medical insurance and daily hospitalization subsidies and hospitalization expense medical insurance.

Such a set of protection plans has an annual premium expenditure of 4,752 yuan, and Ms. Huang only needs to save 396 yuan per month to have the first-stage protection plan of many items. We control her annual premium expenditure to around 20% of her annual income, which can meet Ms. Huang's various financial arrangements while not bringing her any payment risks.

Ms. Huang is 23 years old this year, just graduated and started working, with a current monthly income of about 2,000 yuan. Ms. Huang lives with her parents, and her average monthly consumption expenditure is 500 yuan. In addition to basic expenses, she gives her parents 300 yuan for living expenses each month and invests 300 yuan in funds regularly. She has already enrolled in medical insurance at her workplace.

Ms. Huang wants to save some money for the future by purchasing insurance. At the same time, she hopes that the product has strong flexibility and can be withdrawn at any time for emergency needs. It would be best to pay premiums monthly or quarterly to force herself to save money. Additionally, due to her poor health, she wants to add some critical illness insurance and hospitalization subsidies.
