Lung literature to Beckham - Happy 34th Birthday!

by bpnongmin on 2010-05-26 00:34:11

You can choose to love him, love his awe-inspiring crescent moon kicks and his charming smile that has captivated millions.

You can choose to hate him, hate his reckless kick against Simeone in '98, hate the constant showboating under the influence of his celebrity wife.

But you cannot help but respect him, especially when you saw him in tears after being injured and substituted in the 2006 World Cup quarter-final match against Portugal... Yes, his stepping up in critical moments, his sense of responsibility, his dedication and perseverance, his true passion for football, is truly touching - including me, a die-hard England fan for 13 years who has always been rather unimpressed with him. After all, if he merely intended to use football as a tool for making money, he could have chosen the path of Kournikova, but he didn't, and there's only one reason - football will always be the most important thing in his life!

It's another May 2nd, and the man born in '75 may or may not have the chance to appear on the pitch again at next year's World Cup or the European Championships in 2012... Perhaps now we shouldn't discuss his uncertain future, but instead sincerely wish him well - Beckham, happy 34th birthday!

Here, I present 34 pure football photos as a birthday tribute to him, allowing us together to retrieve those memories almost forgotten amidst the glaring flashlights and noisy entertainment news...

Below this poem wasn't written by me, it was probably penned by one of his loyal fans, yet its simplicity moved even me, someone who once greatly disliked him. Yes, the real Beckham lies within these fluid words:

When he won the treble with Manchester United, people said it was just because of his handsome face.

When he became captain of England, people said it was just because of his vanity.

When he arrived at Real Madrid, people said it was just because of his shrewd wife.

When he crossed the Atlantic, people said it was just because of his lingering past.

Under the Old Trafford night sky, unseen were the countless free-kick practices of the young player.

Under the dome of Wembley Stadium, unseen were the repeated selfless efforts of the captain.

Amidst the countless flashes at Bernabeu, unseen were the many assists provided by number 23.

In the solitude and ridicule of America, unseen were the solitary performances showcasing football artistry.

When faced with doubt and criticism, he left his mentor helplessly.

When cursed and abused, he mustered the courage to reach his peak.

Amidst endless rumors, he remained steadfast in protecting his family.

As new talent emerged, he quietly demonstrated his worth.

Without Maldini's loyalty, he is the unforgettable number 7.

Without Zidane's brilliance, he is the indispensable team captain.

Without Raul's universal adoration, he is the most resilient foreigner.

Without Eusebio's history, he is the best global football ambassador.

David Beckham,

He is not a god, but he is a god forever in the hearts of billions.

The glory of Manchester is the mark of your passionate youth.

The ups and downs of the Three Lions are proof of your maturity.

The pain in Spain is the resolute persistence that you've honed.

The taste of being overlooked is the deeper patience you've endured.

You,

Representing Manchester United,

Representing England,

Representing the world of football.

Worthy to inherit the No. 7 shirt from Cantona.

Worthy to take the armband from Shearer.

Worthy to shine among the stars at Bernabeu.

Worthy to be loved by countless people across borders.

The footprints behind you, the footprints walked for football, the footprints chasing glory,

At ten, twenty, thirty

At thirty-four,

Now,

Still shining brightly at San Siro...
