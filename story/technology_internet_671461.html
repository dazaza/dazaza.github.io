· Beijing Road Pedestrian Street extended to Yanjiang Road, Huishi East to build a super business circle

by wgyuvs8a on 2012-02-22 08:12:53

In fact, the "pedestrianization" of Shamian Island is not an isolated case. Under the large background of advocating environmental protection, fewer cars, industrial transformation, and creating business district popularity, Yuexiu, Tianhe, and Haizhu Districts either previously or currently have plans to develop new pedestrian streets. The current status of these "quasi-members" has important reference value for Shamian South Street and even the entire city. The Yangcheng Evening News recently visited each location one by one to present these pedestrian streets that you might be unfamiliar with.

Since the 1980s, a Jiangnan West Commercial Street gradually formed in the western part of Haizhu District in Guangzhou, which further drove surrounding commercial properties and developed into a "National Shopping Assurance Street." Nowadays, centered around the original Jiangnan West Commercial Street and extending outward, a business district has initially formed, covering a radius of 2-3 kilometers. This Jiangnan West Business District mainly includes sections such as Jiangnan West Road, Boye Road, Baogang Avenue, Jiangnan Avenue, Changgang Road, and Gongye Avenue. The total population within the radiation area is nearly 500,000 people, making it one of the most densely populated areas in Haizhu District.

However, the old saying among Cantonese residents, "Henan also needs its own commercial pedestrian street," has been on their minds for three years. Now, although the Jiangnan West Business District has integrated shopping, dining, leisure and entertainment, cultural tourism, and other functions and is called the "Little Beijing Road," the roads originally designated for the pedestrian street are still the main thoroughfares for vehicle traffic in the Jiangnan West Business District.

Moreover, beneath Jiangnan West Road lies another "commercial street" known as Jiangnan New Land. It was reported that Phase I of Jiangnan New Land opened in 2009, gathering fashion, premium, leisure, and dining businesses, expanding the capacity of the Jiangnan West Business District, and driving the three-dimensional development of the business district. Following Beijing Road and Shangxiajiu, it became the third commercial pedestrian street in Guangzhou. In August this year, relevant departments in Haizhu District indicated that they would initiate Phase II of the Jiangnan New Land project, extending the underground commercial street from its original length of 500 meters by an additional 330 meters to Baogang Avenue, connecting Metro Jiangnan West Station and Guangbai Xinyicheng.

Furthermore, there are bus stops on both sides of the dual lanes of Jiangnan West Road, with each stop having about a dozen bus routes, directly reaching places like Zhongshan Eighth Road, Lijiao, Gexin Road, Panyu Dashi, and Yangtao Park. If Jiangnan West becomes a pedestrian street, the bus stops will need to be relocated. However, the side roads around this section are relatively narrow and unsuitable for bus stops, which would create a headache for the travel arrangements of the 500,000 people in the radiation area.

An employee selling a certain chain brand of ready-to-eat food on Jiangnan West Road said, "I take the bus to work every day, walking back to the store from the Jiangnan West bus stop in less than a minute. If the road becomes a street and the bus stop is removed, it would be 'very inconvenient'."

Mr. Chen, who lives on Ziyu Street, said that his granddaughter attends the first primary school of Jiangnan New Village next to Jiangnan West Road. Every time he picks up his granddaughter, he sees many vehicles at the entrance of the school. "If it becomes a pedestrian street, I don't know how these vehicles will go."

As a "veteran shopper" in Jiangnan West, Ms. Wu also believed that there was no need to convert the surface of Jiangnan West into a pedestrian street. "Besides affecting traffic, the actual effect is not significant because Jiangnan New Land is already a pedestrian street. Converting the surface road into a pedestrian street doesn't make much sense."
