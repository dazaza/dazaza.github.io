Pirate bus stops deceive the waiting passengers - only see advertisements but no buses (picture)

by umanhua1 on 2012-03-07 10:34:10

Counterfeit Bus Stops

Can bus stops be counterfeited? Yes, you haven't read it wrong! Recently, the Chengdu Evening News received complaints from some readers who said that there were quite a few "counterfeit bus stops" on the city's bus routes. These not only misled passengers but also occupied sidewalks causing safety hazards. According to the clues provided by the readers, the reporter conducted an investigation and indeed found some "counterfeit bus stops". Their biggest feature is: even if you wait until nightfall, absolutely no bus will arrive. However, such "counterfeit bus stops" do exist in the downtown area for quite a long time and in significant numbers.

Passenger Complaints

Encountered a fake stop, waited half a day for a bus that never came

"It's normal to wait over ten minutes without seeing a bus, but waiting for more than half an hour without any buses coming is just too annoying!" Recently, Mr. Zhan told the reporter of the Chengdu Evening News that he encountered a "bus stop where buses would never come." What exactly happened?

It turns out that several days ago, Mr. Zhan, who lives near the West Gate, took the No. 35 bus from Guanghuacun to Guojiabridge to attend a wedding banquet. After finishing the banquet that afternoon, Mr. Zhan went to the opposite bus stop from where he had gotten off to prepare to return home, but ran into trouble. Mr. Zhan waited for more than half an hour at this stop and saw many buses pass by, but none stopped here, including the No. 35 bus he had taken earlier.

Later, under the reminder of passers-by, Mr. Zhan realized that there was no bus information, not even a station name, on the "bus stop" behind him, except for large advertisements. "Is this a deserted stop? If it's abandoned, it should be dismantled because it misleads passengers into waiting in vain!" Mr. Zhan complained repeatedly to the reporter.

In fact, even before Mr. Zhan's complaint, our newspaper had received similar complaints, saying that some bus stops displayed seven or eight advertisement boards while the bus information was only a small part, making it very inconvenient for passengers to check, and also one couldn't wait for a bus to stop at the back stops.

What are these "bus stops where no buses come"? How many are there in the downtown area? The reporter of the Chengdu Evening News immediately conducted an investigation.

Reporter Investigation

Counted with bus company personnel; over a hundred counterfeit bus stops around the second ring road

The counterfeit bus stop right in front of eyes

Bus company personnel were greatly shocked

"There won't be such a situation, right?!" Mr. He Zhouxing, deputy manager of the Advertising Branch Company of Chengdu Bus Group Corporation, was full of doubt and shock after listening to the reporter's description. Is the reader's feedback true? Subsequently, Manager He brought along engineering staff to accompany the reporter to conduct an on-site inspection on the street.

On the "South Nan Road Station" bus stop less than hundreds of meters away from the Chengdu Bus Group Corporation, passengers were orderly waiting to board the bus. "This is our stop, and everything is normal," Manager He pointed at two large advertisement boards and a slightly smaller bus information release board on the stop and said. However, within less than 2 meters away from these three advertisement boards on the same line, there were another three identical advertisement boards standing side by side like two adjacent bus stops. Several passengers waited for a long time there but no bus stopped beside them.

"These are fake ones, no wonder passengers have complained!" Manager He suddenly understood after carefully examining the "bus stop" formed by the three advertisement boards behind.

Are there real and fake bus stops?

Occupying sidewalks

Misleading passengers and endangering safety

Under Manager He's guidance, the reporter found the differences between the two adjacent bus stops: the first stop had prominent stop names and route information, and the several advertisement boards on the stop were marked with special plaques to explain, with the outdoor advertisement release number approved by the industrial and commercial department printed on the advertisement boards; the three advertisement boards on the latter stop had no route information at all, not even the stop name, and there was no approval number from any relevant department on the advertisement boards.

Apart from the aforementioned subtle differences, the two stops were almost identical, no wonder some passengers couldn't tell the difference. Within the hour the reporter observed here, quite a few passengers stood on the latter "counterfeit stop" to wait for the bus, realizing something was amiss only after missing the bus and wasting time.

What's worrying is that especially during crowded hours, due to the "counterfeit bus stop" occupying a lot of sidewalk space, it was difficult for passengers getting off the bus to disperse in time, forcing them to crowd onto the roadside, forming a safety hazard. Similarly, because the sidewalk was occupied, pedestrians had to pass through the narrow pedestrian path outside the "counterfeit bus stop", which was less than 1 meter wide, causing inconvenience.

Profit from releasing advertisements on counterfeit bus stops

Surprising profits

How many of these "counterfeit bus stops" are there in the downtown area? The reporter conducted an investigation around the second ring road. After going around the entire circle, apart from a few stops that even Manager He couldn't distinguish as genuine or fake, more than a hundred stops were clearly identified as "counterfeit bus stops". Most of these "counterfeit stops" were built adjacent to regular stops, making it difficult for the vast majority of passengers to differentiate. Another type of "counterfeit stop" simply set up independently on the sidewalk by the street.

"The main purpose of their existence is to release advertisements," Manager He introduced to the reporter. The annual fee for a large advertisement on a regular bus stop is about 50,000 yuan. According to the reporter's estimation based on the more than a hundred "counterfeit bus stops" already discovered, the advertisers could make profits reaching millions of yuan per year!

"They take advantage of the counterfeit appearance to exploit loopholes," Manager He said, possibly because it's hard to distinguish the authenticity of bus stops, thus evading supervision from relevant departments.

The reporter counted the types of advertisements released on "counterfeit bus stops", mainly including training and educational institutions, plastic surgery hospitals, developers' properties for sale, etc., strong armor.

Root Cause

Response from the builders of counterfeit bus stops:

Already invested costs, currently can't dismantle

Through the contact numbers on some "counterfeit bus stop" advertisement boards, the reporter found the Chengdu Jiubang Public Transport Facilities Co., Ltd. behind them.

"We are also helpless about this issue," frankly admitted General Manager Wang Huiqi of the company regarding the impact of these bus stops with names but no substance on passengers, but he has something to say about why they exist.

According to his introduction, Jiubang Company was founded in 2006, at that time it was commissioned by nine private bus enterprises in the city to gradually build these bus stops according to routes, which was approved by the transportation department. But with the gradual integration of private buses, these stops gradually lost their original functions. Especially after the routes were canceled and merged, the only function left for the bus stops was to release advertisements.

At the same time, Manager Wang also admitted that the construction of some stops and the release of advertisement content were not approved by the urban management and industrial and commercial departments. But because the company invested costs when constructing, currently they can only wait for the adjustment of the bus industry structure, merging all these "counterfeit bus stops" into state-owned bus companies, "when the time comes, those that should be dismantled will be dismantled, and those that should be merged will be merged."

During the interview, Manager Wang also revealed a piece of information: apart from Jiubang Company, many of the "counterfeit bus stops" mentioned by the reporter were built by other advertisers. Manager Wang said that if Jiubang Company's stops belong to historical legacies, then the remaining counterfeit bus stops are openly illegal. According to his introduction, building a bus stop costs more than 10,000 yuan, while the annual advertising revenue is over 30,000 yuan, which is the greatest motivation for other advertisers to illegally construct.

Solution Path

Chengdu Transportation Commission quickly launches special rectification

How to solve the problems existing on our city's bus stops? Director Zhang Conggui of the Office of the Chengdu Transportation Commission and responsible persons from relevant departments accepted an interview with our newspaper. According to the data provided by the Transportation Commission, in 2011, a total of 174 complaints from citizens regarding bus stops and signboards were received.

Director Zhang first introduced the legal process of approving and setting up a bus stop: First, it should be jointly investigated and selected by the Transportation Commission, traffic management department, and the bus company; Second, if it occupies a sidewalk or green belt, it should be reported to the urban management and garden departments for approval; Finally, the advertisement release on the signboard should be approved by the industrial and commercial department.

Regarding the more than a hundred bus stops established without approval within the city, Director Zhang stated that the Transportation Commission will join forces with other relevant departments to quickly carry out a special cleanup campaign for bus stops. At the same time, regarding the legacy issues of stops related to Jiubang Company, the Transportation Commission will actively promote consultations among relevant units to resolve the problem. For illegally set up or discontinued bus signboards after route adjustments, the construction units will be ordered to dismantle them within a specified period; for those refusing to dismantle, the Transportation Commission will cooperate with relevant departments to legally dismantle them; for bus stops with incomplete approvals, they will be urged to fulfill the procedures and the illegal use of land for setting up bus stops will be investigated. Finally, the illegal release of advertisements on bus stops will be investigated.

Extension

City People's Congress representative submits suggestions

Standardize stop construction

The current status and impact of "counterfeit bus stops" have also caught the attention of radio host Sun Jing. As a city People's Congress representative, she submitted "Suggestions on the Standardized Setting and Management of Bus Stops and Signboards in the Main Urban Area of Chengdu City and the Removal of Illegally Set Up Ones" at the Fifth Session of the Fifteenth Chengdu Municipal People's Congress, hoping to achieve standardization of bus stop construction through legislation and clarify the functions of various government departments.
