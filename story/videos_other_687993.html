Introduction to the Process of Making Stinky Tofu: Authentic Shaoxing Stinky Tofu Production Technology

I'm a bit hesitant to eat stinky tofu bought outside, worrying about the use of poor-quality oil in its production. Therefore, it's better to make and eat it ourselves. I found this good thing elsewhere and would like to share it with you all. Even if you don't like eating it, you can still take a look while pinching your nose.

1. Cut the tofu into small blocks and place them in the middle of a white cloth.

2. Wrap the tofu tightly, and tidy up the edges.

3. Place the wrapped tofu on a wooden board and neatly arrange them.

4. Press with a wooden board, and place heavy objects on top for an entire night. Afterward, most of the water content in the tofu has been squeezed out. Take it out and open it to see; the tofu has already been pressed firmly. The texture of the stinky tofu made this way will be very delicate.

5. Prepare a cardboard box and lay clean straw inside. Since I couldn't find straw, I used clean corn leaves as a substitute.

6. Neatly stack the tofu on the leaves, following the order of one layer of leaves and one layer of tofu until all are stacked. Then, place it in the storage room and wait for the tofu to grow mold.

7. Pick them out, remove the white cloth, and fry them in a small amount of oil over low heat in a frying pan until both sides turn golden brown. This is the last picture, the finished product! Pair the fried stinky tofu with salt and pepper or chili powder—how delicious!
