Two confirmed absentees for Arsenal vs Liverpool as Wenger rules out Cruyff call for Fabregas

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 22:52:21

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absent from Starting Lineup, Wenger Again Rejects Cruyff's Call for Fabregas

Sina Sports - Arsenal's away game against Burnley this Wednesday is not expected to be too difficult, with the sole aim being to secure three points and reclaim the lead over Villa once again. However, more injuries have emerged in Wenger's squad. In Sunday's match against Liverpool, mainstay Denilson suffered a back injury, while Traore sustained a groin injury. Arsenal's official website reported that both players have been confirmed unavailable for the game against Burnley.

But there is some good news as well. Eboue, who was sidelined due to a groin strain in the Liverpool game, has returned to the squad, strengthening the right-sided options. Wenger said, "There was too little recovery time after Sunday's game, so we've added some more injured players. Denilson is definitely out of the question, possibly available for Hull on Saturday, but we must check thoroughly to ensure his back is fully recovered before he can play. But Eboue has already returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits, with his team completely shaking off the shadow of their heavy defeat by Chelsea. When discussing the championship prospects for this season, Wenger stated that the 09-10 Premier League title race was the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. In an interview with the official website, Wenger said, "No team has been able to maintain a dominant lead early in the season. Instead, four teams are entangled, and I believe a total of six, seven or even eight teams can surprise anyone on any given weekend. Obviously, this is the most open top-of-the-table competition in my 13 years of coaching. I feel that many teams have a chance to win the league title."

Respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own team. Wenger always has a special confidence in Arsenal. He said, "From the start of the season, I have deeply believed that this will be our year. I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: look at us as a whole, how well we have improved, how strong our team spirit and quality are, and how fully prepared we are for each match like a battle. From September to May next year, all this will continue to improve."

To maintain their championship aspirations, Fabregas' importance is undeniable. With Van Persie injured, Fabregas is almost the only guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger naturally wants to ensure that Fabregas plays as well as possible in every key match. Some external requests can be rejected if possible, such as the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for next month on the 22nd. Catalonia coach Cruyff requested Fabregas to play in this game at Camp Nou, but Wenger clearly refused.

Fabregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyff, but Wenger doesn't care about these connections; he has the right to reject such requests because Catalonia is not an officially recognized FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fabregas feels his physical condition is fine, and since it's not a double-header week, he believes he has time to go to Spain. He said, "I'll play against Burnley on Wednesday and Hull three days later, but after that, there will be nearly a week without Premier League games, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

However, Wenger's mind is solely focused on his own team. There is a crucial game against Villa next weekend. The other three big clubs have all lost to Villa, raising questions about whether Arsenal can perform well. Van Persie got injured playing for Holland, and Liverpool explicitly refused Mascherano's participation in the Argentina vs Catalonia game. Wenger, who has always disliked international game complications, would certainly not agree for Fabregas to play in a non-A-level match. Wenger said, "We must respect priorities, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain also has another highly anticipated friendly next week, which is the La Liga All-Stars vs African Stars Team. This match is called the Second African Charity Match, jointly initiated by UNICEF and the Kanoute Foundation. It will take place at the Bernabeu Stadium. Originally, the African team's list included five Arsenal players: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboue. Clearly, apart from locally born players, those with African heritage were also counted. Wenger wouldn't agree with such arrangements. Therefore, no Arsenal players made the final list, and in fact, there weren't any active Premier League players either. The closest connection to the Premier League is probably Kanoute, the founder of the Kanoute Foundation, former West Ham and Tottenham forward, and current Sevilla player from Mali, and perhaps only former Manchester United forward Manucho, who recently joined Valladolid.
