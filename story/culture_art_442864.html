**Issue Background:**

In 1911, the Xinhai Revolution overthrew the feudal imperial dynasty that had lasted for over 2,000 years. As the Expo Year of 2010 was coming to an end and the 100th anniversary of the Xinhai Revolution approached in 2011, China released its first collection commemorating the centennial of the Xinhai Revolution — *The Complete Collection of China's Century-long Gold and Silver Coins*.

The National Cultural Relics Administration and the Chinese Cultural Relics Society have unveiled 100 long-sealed precious cultural relics of gold and silver coins from their collections, including rare items such as "China's Double Rare Gold Coins," "China's Top Ten Silver Coin Treasures," and "China's Three Rare Misprint Coins." Approved by the National Cultural Relics Administration, these coins have been meticulously replicated at a 1:1 scale, preserving the original color, weight, and material. A total of 100 high-fidelity replicas have been produced, with an exclusive limited edition release of *The Complete Collection of China's Century-long Gold and Silver Coins*. Only 3,000 sets will be cast globally, after which all casting molds will be destroyed, ensuring no further production.

---

**Collection Introduction:**

*The Complete Collection of China's Century-long Gold and Silver Coins* is entirely crafted from 999 pure gold and 999 pure silver. It includes all gold and silver coins issued between the sixteenth year of Emperor Guangxu's reign (1890) and the thirty-sixth year of the Republic of China (1947). The collection comprises 100 varieties of coins totaling 100 pieces, all replicated at a 1:1 scale with the original color, weight, and design. After casting, all molds were destroyed, and no further production will occur. Each set includes a bonus "Yuan Datou" coin from the third year of the Republic of China (1914). Every set also comes with a certificate of authenticity from the Chinese Gold and Silver Quality Testing Center.
