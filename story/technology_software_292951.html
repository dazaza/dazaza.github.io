Two trees in waiting

by yang5230 on 2009-12-04 17:12:21

In his twilight years, the boy and girl were studying at a small school in the south. They were an enviable couple. He wrote beautiful poems, and she painted wonderful pictures. People called them "the golden boy and jade girl."

The girl was from a large town north of the river, while the boy hailed from Nanjing. When they first met, it was like when Bao Yu first saw Dai Yu: "Sister, I have seen you before."

After four years of love, graduation came, and the girl brought the boy home to meet her family. Her mother asked about his background, and the girl recounted everything she knew. The girl noticed that her father's expression changed, and then he turned away abruptly, issuing an order for them to leave.

"What happened?" the girl asked her mother anxiously.

Her mother said, during the "Cultural Revolution," the boy's father had been responsible for her father's death. At that time, the girl was too young to understand. The mother continued, "Can you marry him? If you do, I'd rather die."

The boy couldn't believe it. Upon returning to his northern hometown, he questioned his father frantically. His father remained silent for a long time before saying, "It was a chaotic time during the 'Cultural Revolution,' some things can't be explained clearly..." followed by a prolonged silence.

Thus, a river seemed to suddenly divide two lovers who were meant to be together due to the grudges of the previous generation.

How could they willingly give up? The girl knelt behind her mother, pleading for her to let them have a chance. Her mother replied, "Unless I die, this will never happen." After nurturing her for over twenty years, how could she abandon this blood bond?

The girl was disheartened and tearfully told the boy of their separation: "Other than you, I will never marry anyone. I'll wait for you, even if my hair turns gray." The boy, tears streaming down his face, hugged her tightly, saying, "Other than you, I will not marry anyone else, even if I have to wait until the next life." This was in the 1980s, a pledge of true love. They vowed to stay apart but faithful to each other forever.

Five years after graduation, they remained steadfastly loyal, ignoring all parental pressure. Whenever someone proposed marriage, they firmly refused. Secretly, they would meet, evading both sets of parents, for distance could never separate their love.

During these five years, the girl was in the south, and the boy in the north. Every two months, she would take the train to see him, traveling from Nanjing to his city, always buying the cheapest seat to save money to buy him gifts. He was too thin, and she worried about him.

This journey lasted five years. Five years of traveling from Beijing to a small town, filled with the girl's love and joy, secretly doing all this for her father, telling him it was business, but in reality, just wanting to catch a glimpse of her distant lover.

At the age of 28, the girl found him again: "Let's elope, or perhaps, let's die together!" It turned out there was trouble at his home; his mother had passed away. As an only child, his father knelt before him, pleading, "Son, please get married. Our family line cannot end with me!" His father knelt for hours to convince him! The boy took a long train ride to find her, planning to elope with her.

The girl hesitated. The cost of their love was too high; she couldn't let her hatred ruin his father's happiness. Though stubborn, such loyalty was selfish!

"No!" the girl said, "I won't elope with you; you don't have that freedom! I won't die with you either; you must take care of your elderly father. Go, find a good girl to marry. I don't blame you. Because, your happiness is my happiness."

The boy embraced her and wept bitterly, as if crying blood like a cuckoo bird. He hadn't expected that his beloved girl would be so magnanimous, sacrificing her own happiness for his family's well-being. He consoled her, "You should also get married. Don't wait for me. In another life, I will definitely marry you."

The girl shook her head, "In this life, it will be hard to meet and know anyone else. I will be the tree waiting faithfully for you, sitting in solitude, waiting for you!"

For their last meeting, the boy gave the girl a pair of jade cicadas, precious emeralds, heirlooms of his family. The two cicadas sat side by side, gazing at each other with unwavering affection. The boy said, "Though not priceless, when you are old and unable to work, sell it. It can support you. Seeing it is like seeing me."

The girl sobbed in his arms, moved that he had thought of her future, giving her the family heirloom in case she couldn't make it alone. In this life, loving once was worth it!

The gift the girl gave to the boy was a painting, the best she had ever done - two kapok trees, covered with blossoms, one after another. She said tenderly, "That's my hope, hoping in another life, I am one of those flowers, and you pluck me."

On his wedding day, the boy hung the painting in his new home, tears streaming down his face. Those two kapok trees, one was him, the other her. She hadn't left, remaining in his heart, in his soul.

The two lovers promised never to see each other again, never to contact each other, because the kind-hearted girl wanted him to focus on his family.

For the next 20 years, they had no contact, one in the north, the other in the south, truly separated by thousands of miles. During these 20 years, the girl became a famous art dealer in the north, opening a large gallery in Beijing and frequently traveling abroad to buy and sell paintings. However, she remained alone, despite many suitors, she simply shook her head.

By then, the girl's mother had passed away, holding her hand in her final moments and saying, "Child, I've wronged you, delaying your life. You should go find him." The girl cried, knowing these words were 20 years too late. He already had a wife and children. Could she still go find him?

Twenty years later, the girl was nearly fifty, with silver streaks in her hair and wrinkles on her forehead. She was no longer young, but her heart was still in her twenties. Her heart still belonged to him, entirely to him.

One day, while heading to Russia on business, she received a call from a strange woman. "I'm his wife," the woman said, "He's gone, constantly calling your name. I know about you, because he often calls your name in his dreams."

In an instant, the girl collapsed, trembling all over. She got off the train midway and rushed to the airport. She had to see him, regardless of what others might say. The person she had missed so much in her dreams, you must wait for me!

When they saw each other, they were stunned: years had passed, and both were now grey-haired.

In the hospital bed, the boy was emaciated, his face pale - he had liver cancer, in its late stages. If not for waiting for her to come, he would have already passed away elsewhere.

"How could you let yourself become like this? Who allowed you to become this way?..." The girl rushed forward, full of grievances, "You promised to live to be eighty, and you must be the tree beside me!"

The boy was unable to speak, only weakly extending his hand to touch her face. She buried her face in his palm, and tears streamed down her cheeks.

His wife and daughter sat nearby, tears flowing like rain.

A few hours later, the boy passed away. The girl was heartbroken, attending his funeral. She personally dressed him in his burial clothes. While putting on his undershirt, she froze. On his chest was a tattoo, a lotus flower, delicately beautiful. She burst into tears; her real name was Qinglian (Green Lotus).

Qinglian, it was a tattoo of a lotus flower.

And her tattoo was in her heart, his person, his name, his face, all etched into her heart, a scar that would never fade.

After the funeral, visiting the boy's home, the girl learned that he had lived a life of poverty, working as a teacher all his life, yet his home was empty. His wife was unemployed, his daughter couldn't afford university, and if he had money, his illness wouldn't have been neglected until this point. He clearly knew she was wealthy; her news was everywhere online, at so many auctions, every move costing millions, yet he never reached out. That was him! Just a simple tree, watching her from afar, never entangling her.

The girl did something no one expected. She bought his family the best villa in the area, sent his daughter abroad for education, left a small sum of money, and quietly left.

The girl understood, if she loved this man, she would love everything about him - his wife, his children. Love, in the end, was all about tenderness, all about compassion, all those intricate threads of genuine emotion that couldn't be cut or untangled.

The boy was gone, and the world seemed vast and empty, originally intertwined for so many years! But now, he was gone, alone in another world. From then on, the girl never appeared at any auction, nor did she appear in fine clothing again. Soon, her funeral was held in Nanjing. She passed away in the same year as him, less than half a year apart.

The girl died of melancholy, childless. Relatives said that when she passed, she was holding a piece of jade, a jade called double jade cicadas.

It was the boy's wife who buried the girl, placing her beside him in the small town north of the river. Wasn't this the place she had yearned for all these years?

"Let them be together forever," the boy's wife said, "Plant longing trees behind their graves, plant unity flowers behind their graves, let them love each other in heaven."
