Contraceptive techniques for "weekend couples" when they are intimately close

by qingaige0 on 2010-04-18 23:45:31

What are "weekend couples"? As the name suggests, it refers to couples who live apart due to work or study and can only meet once a week or every half month. With the acceleration of modern life rhythm and the increase of work pressure, this kind of marriage pattern is becoming increasingly common. "Short separation is better than honeymoon", after meeting, it's natural to be passionate. However, for lovers who have no immediate plans to have children, while enjoying the intimacy, contraception also becomes an issue that cannot be ignored.

Love is more important than pleasure

My wife is a nurse in a large hospital. Her job is very hard, and she often has to work night shifts; I am the chief consultant of a consulting company, and it is common for me to be out on projects for ten days to half a month without returning home. Therefore, although we are married, we see each other rarely. Every time we meet, we inevitably get intimate. We don't plan to have children right now, so I use the method of coitus interruptus and safe period contraception. The reason we don't use condoms is because I feel it greatly affects the pleasure, and it feels strange to be completely exposed yet still have something in between. My wife calculates her safe period every month and tells me, and my coitus interruptus often turns into "intra-coital ejaculation" because I don't want to stop. There's nothing my wife can do about my antics in bed, but I am indeed grateful for her trust in me. When I'm traveling on business and entertaining clients, I sometimes accompany them to explore the "cultural market," but I am just playing around. However, my wife doesn't fear that I will bring any diseases from outside, which is enough to make me loyal to her.

I know that both coitus interruptus and safe period contraception have high failure rates. Moreover, many times I take chances and use no method at all, just for those few seconds of pleasure of being one with her. After a long time, I began to wonder why my wife could always "withdraw unscathed." Until one day, I saw contraceptive pills in her drawer. It turned out that she was ensuring my "undiscounted pleasure" with these pills! And most of the time, for one monthly encounter with me, she had to continuously take the pills for 30 days.

Looking at these pills, I remained silent for a long time. A few days later, I found an opportunity to have an honest conversation with my wife. I first admitted that my pursuit of pleasure was too selfish, then took out a big box of condoms and said that contraception is a matter for both people, and as a man, I cannot let the person I love suffer. My wife listened to my words and smiled happily, and I understood for the first time how important it is to truly think for the other person. Yes, steadfast love is far more important than fleeting pleasure.

We need "rational" passion

At the turn of the century, I became Wei's bride. In the early days of our marriage, Wei was very attached to my body, and his passion and skills often made me lose myself. Since we both wanted to enjoy a few years of "two-person world" life, he would always wear a condom during sex to prevent pregnancy. This thin layer did not hinder our deep affection for each other but instead increased our desire to explore each other. For over a year, we lived like celestial lovers until Wei suddenly received a new appointment notice from his company, requiring him to take full responsibility for the local market development in another city. Although the city wasn't far away, it meant that we could only meet on weekends.

Living apart like the Cowherd and Weaver Girl ignited new passions in our love. Every weekend, Wei would take the train to reunite with me. Each time, we would make love until we were exhausted, then contentedly hugged each other and fell sound asleep. For a while, Wei was extremely busy and couldn't find time to return home for three consecutive weeks. By the fourth week, I couldn't bear it anymore and traveled to his residence in that city. When Wei saw my tired face standing at the door, he was so surprised and happy that he lifted me up and spun me around several times. We passionately kissed and nipped each other, expressing our longing, and in the moment when Wei entered me, I realized that he didn't wear a condom for the first time. But at that moment, I didn't care. The novel sensation made us experience wave after wave of climax, until Wei ejaculated entirely inside me...

After the passion subsided, I couldn't help but worry about getting pregnant. One month later, the test strip confirmed my worries - I was pregnant. Since it was discovered early, I followed the doctor's advice and underwent medical abortion, refusing our little treasure's visit to this world. My weakened body and complex emotions kept me in bed for two whole days. Wei couldn't take time off to come home and could only apologize repeatedly over the phone. I didn't blame him at all, really, but I realized that appropriate "rationality" during passionate moments can save a lot of unnecessary trouble. (Editor: Yuan Guoji)

Contraception (Contraception) is the use of scientific methods to temporarily prevent women from becoming pregnant. It mainly controls three aspects of the reproductive process: 1. Suppressing the production of sperm and eggs; 2. Preventing the combination of sperm and egg; 3. Making the uterine environment unfavorable for sperm capacitation, survival, or implantation and development of the fertilized egg. Common contraception methods include taking contraceptive pills, using condoms, contraceptive films, safe period contraception, coitus interruptus, urethral compression contraception, surgical contraception, etc.
