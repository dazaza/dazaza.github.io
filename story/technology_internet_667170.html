Primary school student helps mother, a sanitation worker, to clean streets every weekend without interruption in the past 5 years (Photo)

by bmnxsnfz on 2012-02-16 15:31:45

One morning two months after she started her job, before dawn, Li Shuiying got up to go to work with great enthusiasm. However, it was clear that she had not yet recovered from the previous day's exhaustion. At this moment, Yang Cheng woke up and hearing his mother sighing, he sat up and said: "Mom, today is Sunday, no school. Or I can go help you."

According to reports, during non-holiday periods, Yang Cheng accompanies his mother to work twice a week; during holidays, he almost goes every day. As for how many times he has worked in the past five years or how much garbage he has transported, neither he nor his mother could remember.

His mother is very satisfied with his performance. Yang Cheng happily dances around saying: "Mom, in the future, every weekend and holiday, I'll come help you."

The legal professional Mr. Ran believes that it is necessary for children to do appropriate labor, but doing the job of a cleaner is too early for Yang Cheng. "That is to say, his age is not suitable for such work. His place should be on campus, not on the streets or roads," Mr. Ran says. In addition, some people believe that cleaners work long hours into the night, and allowing a child to participate may affect his sleep, thereby affecting his health.

Mother and son soon set off. Li Shuiying was responsible for sweeping the floor and cleaning up the garbage, while Yang Cheng pushed the garbage truck behind his mother. After the sweeping was done, he accompanied his mother to dispose of the garbage and clean the tools.

Yang Cheng's filial piety is commendable, but his "filial piety" also raises concerns and doubts.

On November 27th, at noon, when our reporter interviewed in Zhenyuan, Li Shuiying was working on the road. That day was Sunday, and Yang Cheng and Li Shuiying's youngest daughter Yang Xiqin were also helping by her side.

"The street cleaners are working on the road where there are many cars. If something happens to the child, no one will be able to take responsibility," said Director Long.

Perhaps due to their similar fates, they have more common language. With everyone's matchmaking, Li Shuiying remarried with her son Yang Cheng to Yang Zuobin.

"Mom has a disability, so I want to help her," said Yang Cheng.

Some worry that Yang Cheng's studies might be affected, but since he started school, he has maintained good academic performance. According to his current homeroom teacher, Mr. Zhang, Yang Cheng performs well in school, with excellent grades, and is considered an all-around excellent student. In the recent midterm exam, his Chinese, math, and other subjects all scored over 80 points.

Director Long of the Zhenyuan Sanitation Station said that he currently does not know about the situation of the "young sanitation worker," but according to relevant regulations, sanitation workers are not allowed to bring their children along to work.

This year, 12-year-old Yang Cheng is sweeping the leaves on the road with a broom, while his younger sister Yang Xiqin stands by holding a dustpan, gathering the scattered leaves together. Yang Cheng then takes the dustpan from his sister and sweeps the leaves into it.

Li Shuiying thought about it and agreed.

"Although the children are young, deep down I don't want them to do this. Who would willingly let their own children suffer? Later, someone told me that as long as it doesn't affect their studies, allowing children to do appropriate labor is also acceptable," said Li Shuiying. Therefore, she agreed to let the children help out, but only during holidays and weekends. And for the safety of the children, she arranged for them to "work" on the sidewalk.

Yang Cheng developed a love for labor through long-term accompaniment of his mother. During his free time, he even secretly went to construction sites to carry bricks to earn pocket money. He is a role model for his younger sister, who under his influence also developed a love for labor. Starting this year, she also began accompanying their mother to work.

After his son was born, Yang Guangguo came to work at a brick factory in Baiyangping, Zhenyuan, where he became close friends with Yang Zuobin from Jianggu, Zhenyuan. During this time, Yang Guangguo brought his wife and child along.

In 2004, Yang Guangguo suffered from lung cancer and other diseases, and died after ineffective treatment on the thirteenth day of the tenth lunar month. It was also that year that someone introduced Yang Zuobin to a girlfriend from Shidong, Taijiang, but she was also a critically ill patient, bedridden for a long time. On the day after Yang Guangguo's death, she also passed away.

In 2007, Yang Zuobin brought his family to make a living in the Zhenyuan county town. With the help of kind-hearted people, both became street cleaners. Of course, this job was easy for Yang Zuobin, but for Li Shuiying, it was not so easy because she had a disability in her right foot and moved inconveniently.

When this started, Yang Cheng was only eight years old.

Some of Yang Cheng's neighbors also share similar concerns.
