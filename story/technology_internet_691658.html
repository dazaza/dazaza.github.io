1. Short-term opportunities after a V-shaped reversal

When operating in a V-shaped reversal pattern, there are two points worth noting: One is the extent of the stock price increase. A V-shaped reversal occurs after a sharp drop in stock prices and is usually a sign of exhaustion after the concentrated release of selling pressure. However, the weakening of selling pressure does not equate to a rapid increase in buying pressure; the conversion between the two forces happens gradually. It’s difficult to predict how much improvement will follow after a V-shaped reversal. The second point is the extent of volume expansion. The reason short-term opportunities arise after a V-shaped reversal is due to active accumulation by major players during the reversal. As the stock price rises, the trading volume increases due to the accumulation of these major players. If the trading volume before and after the V-shaped reversal is clearly at two different levels (minimal volume before the V-shape and maximal volume during), it becomes challenging to determine whether this increase in volume is due to accumulation or manipulation by the main players. Generally, the effect of manipulation-induced volume increase is more significant than that caused by accumulation. If the stock price rises too quickly and sharply, it's likely due to manipulation with the purpose of selling high.

2. Short-term opportunities in an ascending pennant [http://www.zljjs.com]

An ascending pennant refers to the process where, during a stock price increase, a series of positive lines push up the price, forming a flagpole. Subsequently, the stock price begins to slowly adjust downward, and the K-line during the adjustment forms a triangular flag. This is a pattern indicating a stock price increase, and the short-term buy point is when the stock price adjusts back to the flag area.

3. Short-term opportunities under technical support levels

Technical support levels not only align with our graphical aesthetic perspective but also have deep market formation mechanisms. Many investors make correct decisions when buying and selling stocks based solely on intuition, and they may not even be clear about the origin of this feeling. Such intuition is both an accumulation of market experience and an innate ability to appreciate graphical aesthetics. Using technical support levels for short-term trading undoubtedly combines both elements effectively. Generally, when this support level initially forms, there might be some accidental factors involved, such as a stable market or individual stock benefits. Regardless, this is a relatively active market trading position with small fluctuations, reflecting the short-term market holding cost. As the stock price fluctuates (whether rising or falling), it deviates from this support level. When the stock price returns to this position for some reason, due to the involvement of previous holders, technical analysts, and the main players maintaining the position, this location becomes a true "support level". In practical application, we refer to it as a technical support level.

4. Short-term opportunities under an ascending intermediate platform

During the stock price's upward trend in a channel, there will always be some consolidation patterns. The appearance of these consolidation patterns is not accidental; they are actions by the main players to increase the market holding cost in preparation for further lifting of the stock price later. Only after sufficient turnover will the main players lift the stock price; otherwise, when the stock price is truly at a high level, the floating chips will rush to sell along with the main players, making it difficult for the main players to successfully control the stock. A consolidation phase during the ascent presents a good short-term entry opportunity. Generally, we should choose stocks that have been consolidating horizontally for over a month at relatively high positions to intervene, achieving the best combination of time and returns. Entering too early wastes time and erodes confidence, while entering too late might increase the holding cost. If the stock price can continuously fluctuate slightly at a high horizontal position, this trend itself proves that the main players have no intention of selling large amounts; instead, it is often a signal for them to continue lifting the stock price.
