The best spring travel destination - Wuyuan, Jiangxi's beautiful countryside

by youyouwanglongt on 2012-03-07 11:19:44

[The most beautiful country in China], [a green pearl set at the border of Anhui, Zhejiang and Gan provinces] - this is Wuyuan.

Wuyuan is located in Northeastern Gan province, and is one of the six counties of ancient Huizhou, as well as being one of the birthplaces of Huizhou culture. Wuyuan is known as the "Land of Scholars" and the "Tea Country", and is a nationally famous county for its culture and ecological tourism. The beauty of the countryside here is praised by outsiders as naturally harmonious. "The green hills look splendid towards evening, while the springtime water flows alongside the railings". Every village is like a painting, with scenery at every step. The misty haze that drifts between the mountains and rivers, the ancient houses, stone paths, and roofed bridges surrounded by green mountains and clear waters, all exude an artistic charm akin to ink-wash paintings.

Wuyuan has a history of more than 1200 years. In the 28th year of the Tang Dynasty's Kaiyuan era (740 AD), it was established from parts of Xiuning County in Shizhou Prefecture and Leping County in Raozhou Prefecture, under the jurisdiction of Shizhou Prefecture (now She County). Throughout the Tang, Song, Yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties, it belonged to Shizhou, Huizhou, Huizhou Road, and Huizhou Prefecture respectively. In the first year of the Republic of China, the prefecture was abolished but the county remained, directly subordinate to Anhui Province. In the 23rd year of the Republic of China (1934), it was transferred to Jiangxi Province. In the 36th year (1947), it returned to Anhui, and in the 38th year (May 1949), it was again transferred back to Jiangxi Province.
