Han Songlin

Like pearls hidden deep in the fields of mulberry,

In fact, you don't know any of your own interests,

Only I can clearly understand God's design beforehand,

And find the cruel pearl among the sands.

— From a song lyric

In this era that highlights individuality, "you choose yours, I'll choose mine" has become a philosophy embraced by many high school students. Everyone is trying to live life their own way. But unfortunately, this uniqueness and distinctiveness disappear when it comes to filling out college entrance exam (Gaokao) applications. In fact, there's even an astonishing amount of similarity. Simply put, everyone wants to get into the popular majors at universities located in economically developed areas such as Tianjin, Nanjing, Shanghai, and Beijing. Universities in less economically developed regions are largely ignored. This tendency of candidates also creates another landscape in the university admissions process: popular universities in "Tian (Tianjin), Nan (Nanjing), Hai (Shanghai), Bei (Beijing)" have crowded applications and fierce competition. On the other hand, universities in "old, young, border, poor" regions are left with significant vacancies, and many schools even go unnoticed, the situation quite dire.

Of course, there are many reasons for the lack of applicants to some universities, but without exception, they were not favored by candidates during the admissions process. Some cases are occasional, while others follow long-term rules. These universities, like pearls scattered among the sands, if noticed, are often forgotten by candidates in the corners of Gaokao applications, which is truly regrettable. Let us now approach these institutions and carefully listen to their heartfelt confessions!

Confession One: The Endless Loneliness of Regional Disadvantaged Universities

(No one wants their child to suffer in underdeveloped areas, especially since it would be harder for them to come back and find jobs afterward. Conditions are better in the eastern regions, with more opportunities and a better employment environment. — A parent’s voice)

In recent years' university admissions, a clear trend has emerged: regardless of the tier, the distribution of student sources remains consistent. Large cities are preferred over medium and small cities; economically developed regions are favored over relatively less developed ones; coastal areas are chosen over inland areas; nearby regions are selected over remote areas. Universities in advantageous regions like Beijing, Guangdong, Shanghai, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, etc., often see overwhelming application numbers, resulting in overcrowded situations where the first-choice applications are usually enough. However, universities in disadvantaged areas such as Northeast, Northwest, Southwest, and other remote regions, like Guizhou University, Yanbian University, Shaanxi Normal University, etc., face difficulties, needing not only supplementary recruitment but also multiple re-advertisements, yet still struggling significantly. Some western universities even experience a "zero submission" phenomenon for their first-choice applications.

Guizhou University, listed among the universities with insufficient applications, has its own restless heart. It is a key construction university under the national "211 Project", established in 1902, having gone through stages as Guizhou College, Provincial Guizhou University, National Guizhou Agricultural College, and National Guizhou University, making it a century-old institution. After the founding of New China, it was renamed Guizhou University, with Mao Zedong personally inscribing its name. In 1997, the original Guizhou University, Guizhou Agricultural College, Guizhou Arts Higher Specialized School, and Guizhou Agricultural Management Cadre College merged for the first time. In 2004, a new Guizhou University was formed based on the original Guizhou University and Guizhou Institute of Technology. Its main specialties include civil engineering, mechanical design and manufacturing in engineering, power systems and automation; Chinese language and literature, English, Japanese, law in humanities; international economics and trade, public administration, e-commerce in economics; engineering management, financial management in management science. An international scientific and technological cooperation project led by Professor Tao Zhu of Guizhou University and his Australian counterparts, titled "Research on Self-assembly Systems and Applications of Molecular Capsules Based on Cucurbiturils," demonstrates Guizhou University's research strength. It has already built the first domestic R&D center and production base for cucurbituril macrocycle compounds, being the only group domestically capable of providing research-grade cucurbiturils. This project finds applications in organic toxicant collection, drug sustained release, control, detection, and management of water pollution. Boao Forum Secretary General Long Yongtu graduated from here.

Shaanxi Normal University also faces similar issues of insufficient applications during enrollment. Founded in 1944, the predecessor of Shaanxi Normal University was the Shaanxi Provincial Teachers College. In 1960, it merged with Shaanxi Teachers College to establish Shaanxi Normal University. It is a Ministry of Education-affiliated normal university and a key construction university under the national "211 Project". It is a crucial base for cultivating teachers and educational administrators for higher education institutions and secondary schools, known as the "cradle of teachers" in the northwest region. Despite this, it is not favored by candidates, mainly due to its location in the economically less developed northwest region. Shaanxi Normal University has formed a "doctorate cluster" in subjects such as chemistry, mathematics, biology, history, geography, Chinese, food, and economics. Among these, historical geography is the only national-level key discipline in humanities in Shaanxi, and also a national first-level discipline doctoral authorization point. The Chinese language and literature discipline is also strong, with ancient Chinese literature being the third batch to obtain doctoral degree authorization. Disciplines such as literary studies, Chinese language and literature have been built into doctoral degree authorization points, becoming a prominent teaching and research stronghold in the field nationwide. Additionally, analytical chemistry, applied chemistry, basic mathematics, zoology, botany, materials science, acoustics, Marxist theory and ideological and political education, Chinese philosophy, and tourism management have also seen good development. Shaanxi Normal University deserves attention from liberal arts candidates in all aspects.

Speaking of Shanxi, Shanxi has a glorious past. Even today, people from Shanxi feel that their economy may lag behind, but their culture does not. Despite this, local universities such as Taiyuan Normal University, Xinzhou Normal University, Jinzhong University, Yanbei Normal University, Datong University, etc., have never become focal points in candidate applications. Recruitment shortfalls are commonplace. Taiyuan Normal University was reorganized in March 1999 from the merger of the Shanxi University Teachers College, Taiyuan Teachers College, and the Shanxi Provincial Education Institute, forming a full-time higher normal college primarily focused on undergraduate teacher education. Since its merger, it has finally shed the century-old title of Shanxi University Teachers College and started a new chapter, though its core strength still originates from the previous Shanxi University. Although it has had a short development period after the merger, it still ranks among the top in Shanxi's normal universities, stronger than those mentioned later. Being located in the provincial capital Taiyuan, it also benefits from the foundation of the original Shanxi University.

(A warm reminder: Due to information gaps, many underdeveloped region institutions unable to normally recruit sufficient students might offer reduced score requirements, offering low-scoring students a rare opportunity to enroll.)

Confession Two: Deep Watchfulness of Unfamous Heroes

(An unfamous university, attending it wouldn’t be useful! – A candidate expresses dissatisfaction)

From past admission situations, apart from "picking regions", candidates also use "going for fame" as an important criterion for choosing schools. Any school associated with prestigious universities, even if it's just an independent college affiliated with a famous university, will receive candidates' favor, sometimes with admission scores exceeding the second-tier threshold. For example, Nankai University's Binhai College, Beijing Technology and Business University's Jia Hua College, Capital Normal University's Ke De College, etc., are among the outstanding examples. If the university lacks renown or isn't widely recognized, candidates will maintain a certain distance, after all, it's a "stranger." Under the influence of this psychological effect, many decent institutions are overlooked by candidates.

The former Southwest Normal University and Southwest Agricultural University, once beloved by candidates, faced challenges after merging to form Southwest University. The reason lies in the unfamiliarity brought by the new name, naturally making it less trustworthy compared to familiar institutions, leading to poor source of students, which was expected. In reality, both Southwest Normal University and Southwest Agricultural University originated from the Sichuan Eastern Normal School founded in 1906, sharing the same roots, making their merger natural. The former Southwest Normal University was also a key comprehensive normal university directly under the Ministry of Education, formed by the union of the National Women's Normal College and Sichuan Provincial Education College in 1950. The former Southwest Agricultural University was formed by the merger of Sichuan Provincial Education College, Huaxi University Department of Agriculture, and Xianghui College Department of Agriculture, becoming a national key university in 1979, where renowned agricultural experts like Yuan Longping were cultivated, indeed impressive. Clearly, Southwest University has substantial internal strengths beyond its new packaging, so candidates preparing to fill out applications should pay close attention and not miss out.

There are also some colleges that have been upgraded from junior colleges to undergraduate institutions, overshadowed because candidates do not recognize the new faces. Such as Hechi College, Hexi College, Hezhou College, Guiyang College, Binzhou College, Yulin College, etc., rarely attracting applicants, and even their locations are unclear. They harbor much bitterness, with no one to express it to.

Hezhou College is the only ordinary higher education institution in eastern Guangxi, upgraded to Hezhou College in 2005. Just from the school name, or lack of understanding about its characteristics, its predecessor was Babu Normal School, Wuzhou Region Teacher Training College, Wuzhou Region Education College, Wuzhou Normal Higher College, thus teacher education is a strong specialty of the school. It offers 13 departments (sections) including Chinese, foreign languages, humanities and management, preschool education, primary education, art, social sciences, etc., setting up 16 teacher education majors including Chinese language and literature, politics and history, English, primary education, preschool education, etc. The courses of mathematical analysis and middle school mathematics textbook teaching methods are listed as regional key construction courses. The school also employs part-time professors or guest professors from well-known universities such as Tsinghua University, Beijing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics, Guangxi Normal University, etc.

Binzhou University is located in the new urban area of Binzhou City, the hometown of "the Sage of War" Sunzi, a vibrant and energetic emerging city in the Yellow River Delta, approved by the Ministry of Education as a comprehensive general undergraduate institution co-managed by the province and city, mainly cultivating high-level talents for regional economic and social development. Despite its modest reputation, its strength is noteworthy. Here, research institutes such as the Sunzi Studies Research Institute, Educational Science Research Institute, Sports Science Research Institute, Control Science and Engineering Research Institute, etc., have been established. Moreover, the university has established intercollegiate exchanges and cooperation relationships with foreign higher education institutions such as Australia's BRIT, France's Rennes Higher School of Business Management, signing joint education agreements: Computer Information Management, Electrical Engineering and Automation, Business Management, Marketing are joint education majors, providing convenience for students in these majors, enabling them to "connect internationally" and access the latest foreign information.

Regardless, candidates have significant information gaps when choosing schools. With the expansion of higher education, there are countless招生institutions, making it impossible to learn about each one, especially those outside the province, leaving candidates clueless and relying solely on factors like school reputation. These non-rational factors can sway candidates' choices, so it's best to review relevant information before the exam to uphold one's preferences effectively.

(A warm reminder: For universities entering local admissions for the first time, due to insufficient renown, their admission scores won't be too high. Many "first-time recruiting" universities accept candidates just above the admission line, making the chance of enrollment relatively greater.)

Confession Three: Lonely Lives of Cold Door Institutions and Majors

(Originally, I wanted to check out which schools and majors were available for supplementary recruitment, but the remaining schools were mostly agriculture, forestry, geology, and normal universities. These schools are ones I don't want to attend. Thinking about it, I decided to give up rather than retake the exam. – A Hangzhou candidate scoring over 600 points)

Agriculture, forestry, and mining-related universities have consistently been placed in the "cold palace" in university admissions, failing to attract satisfactory sources of students even in the first round of applications. After supplementary recruitment, many vacancies remain. Each time looking at the shortage information table, the most common entries are these types of schools, a pain that schools with "agriculture," "forestry," "mining" in their names cannot easily express. Such as Shandong Agricultural University, Tianjin Agricultural College, Sichuan Agricultural University, Northeast Forestry University, Zhejiang Forestry University, etc. Candidates are relatively "stingy" when applying to these types of institutions, with many willing to retake the exam rather than attend these universities, leading to more and more "high four" phenomena.

The predecessor of Shandong Agricultural University was the Shandong Advanced Agricultural and Forestry School established in 1906. In 1999, the original Shandong Agricultural University and Shandong Water Conservancy Junior College merged to form Shandong Agricultural University, a multi-disciplinary university with agricultural science and life science as its advantages and characteristics, integrating agriculture, science, engineering, management, economics, literature, law, etc., and is one of the strongest provincial universities in Shandong Province. It has self-produced academicians, national key disciplines, and ranks among the top 100 universities in the country every year. However, due to its association with agriculture and its location in Tai'an, it has never been very attractive to high-scoring students. Shandong Agricultural University has officially submitted an application to the provincial government to remove the word "agricultural" from its name, believing that the two words "agricultural" severely restrict the development of the university, causing disadvantages in recruitment and employment, and should be removed, renaming it "Qilu University," although results have yet to materialize.

Non-key non-provincial capital normal universities also face the dilemma of being sandwiched between two fronts. On one hand, primary and secondary schools in developed areas require increasingly higher academic qualifications for teachers, while remote areas, due to limited fiscal funds, prefer substitute teachers. As a result, the employment rate of normal graduates has decreased in recent years, reducing candidates' enthusiasm for application. A few provincial universities have decent sources of students, but institutions like Jilin Normal University, Tianjin Vocational and Technical Normal University, Jiangsu Vocational and Technical Normal University, etc., are coldly received. Schools like Chuxiong Normal University, Tianshui Normal University, Zhoukou Normal University, etc., are rarely noticed. Furthermore, some normal universities are eager to upgrade, requiring further examination of their educational quality, faculty strength, etc., lowering societal recognition.

Jilin Normal University is a key university in Jilin Province, located in Siping, Jilin Province, classified as a D-class institution in the region, unable to compare with Northeast Normal University in Changchun, which is a comprehensive normal university directly under the Ministry of Education. Without geographical advantage or a strong backing, Jilin Normal University's obscurity is understandable. Tianjin Vocational and Technical Normal University is different, being a multi-disciplinary university mainly focusing on engineering and education, one of the first vocational technical normal colleges established in China, primarily cultivating and training teachers, management personnel, and highly skilled application talents for higher and secondary vocational technical education across the country. Due to its designation as "engineering + normal," many mistakenly believe it to be a vocational college, causing it great distress. In reality, employment prospects for graduates from such institutions are often smoother than those from general comprehensive universities, as these graduates not only understand technology but also teach it, making them practical.

Simultaneously, relatively unpopular majors or academically strong majors within these institutions, such as agriculture, forestry, mineral resources, oil, mechanical engineering, chemical engineering, etc., often face insufficient sources of students and rely on adjustments during annual admissions. With some students unwilling, the school must conduct ideological work after enrollment, making the recruitment process difficult. However, upon graduation, these students become "hot items" for many units. Graduates from geology-related majors at China University of Geosciences are very popular in the job market, with many recruiters visiting student dormitories to hire talent. Traditional majors at Huazhong Agricultural University are also deeply favored by employers, enjoying scholarships from employers. Ironically, these easily employable schools and majors spend their time desperately seeking students.

Due to a lack of communication of information, candidates tend to make direct associations upon seeing the names of these schools, assuming they are only related to fundamental majors like agriculture, forestry, and mining. In reality, many schools have become comprehensive institutions, with non-agricultural, non-forestry, non-mining majors accounting for half of the school's offerings. For example, more than 60% of Shandong Agricultural University's majors are non-agricultural, developing well, but its admission score lines remain low, likely due to its cold-toned name deterring candidates.

Another type of unpopular major worth mentioning is the humanities majors in agriculture, forestry, and engineering universities, generally unaccepted by humanities candidates, considered unsuitable. In the 2006 humanities undergraduate first batch admissions, relatively popular majors like Tourism Management and Advertising at Northeast Forestry University, Law and Chinese Language and Literature at South China Agricultural University, Social Work and Advertising at Changchun University of Science and Technology, etc., also experienced shortages.
