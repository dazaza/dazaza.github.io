Investment banks are satisfied with Apple's Q4 performance, claiming that the sales of new products will be extremely popular.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 17:48:43

According to the U.S. tech blog AppleInsider, Apple's financial results for the fourth quarter of the 2013 fiscal year, announced yesterday, exceeded Wall Street expectations and generally satisfied analysts. They also expressed optimism about the new iPhone and iPad models, believing they would achieve huge sales volumes.

Apple generated $37.5 billion in revenue during the fourth quarter, with a net profit of $7.5 billion, selling a record-breaking 33.8 million iPhones.

**Morgan Stanley**

Analyst Katy Huberty noted that Apple's revenue growth was surprising. She also raised her forecast for the company's revenue growth in 2014 from 6% to 7.5%.

Huberty was encouraged by the "upbeat" tone displayed by Apple management during the earnings call. She said that Tim Cook, Apple's CEO, had previously admitted in the last two quarters that the company's performance did not meet expectations, but this time he emphasized his confidence in the future. "Apple executives continue to emphasize innovation in existing and new product categories, which may include the iWatch and iTV."

**JPMorgan Chase**

Analyst Mark Moskowitz advised investors to seize the opportunity to buy Apple stock on dips in the near term. Given the strong performance in the fourth quarter and the expected robust results during the holiday season, he anticipates that Apple's stock will become more stable in the future.

Moskowitz pointed out that Apple's guidance for the quarter ending in December is slightly higher than the average analyst expectation.

In his view, if Apple can avoid the supply chain issues that occurred during last year’s holiday season, it will undoubtedly achieve very strong results.

**Deutsche Bank**

Analyst Chris Whitmore expects that demand for Apple's new products during the holiday season will be very strong.

He anticipates that Apple will have several growth catalysts over the next 12 to 18 months, including the sixth-generation iPhone, a partnership with China Mobile, and further geographic expansion.

Regarding Apple's highly anticipated new product category, Whitmore expects the company to launch either a television or wearable device, "which should alleviate concerns about innovation."

**Bank of Canada Capital Markets**

In the view of analyst Amit Daryanani, Apple's current execution is impressive. He expects the company to have multiple short-term growth catalysts, including cooperation with China Mobile, the upcoming release of the iPad Air and Retina-display iPad mini, and potential cash return growth.

He believes that the average selling price of the iPhone and iPad (which were $577 and $439 respectively in the previous quarter) shows signs of stabilization. He anticipates that under the impetus of strong sales of the iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c, Apple's profit margin will begin to grow in the 2014 fiscal year.

**Piper Jaffray**

Analyst Gene Munster pointed out that Apple's guidance for the current fiscal quarter is encouraging, exceeding his own revenue projections. He expects Apple's profits during the holiday season to remain roughly flat compared to the previous year, and to grow by 8% in the first quarter of 2014.

Munster also predicts that Apple will launch a larger-screened iPhone and smartwatch next year, and he continues to expect the company to introduce a television.

**Cantor Fitzgerald**

Analyst Brian White believes Apple has upward potential, setting a target price of $777 per share, nearly $250 higher than its current price.

He also feels that Apple's guidance for the current fiscal quarter is somewhat conservative. He had previously estimated the company would generate $58.9 billion in revenue for the quarter, higher than Apple's high-end estimate of $58 billion.

**ISI Group**

Brian Marshall believes that Apple is protecting its powerful ecosystem and countering threats from competitors like Google Android by offering software such as OS X desktop system, iWork, and iLife for free.

He increased his revenue and net income forecasts for Apple this calendar year to $174.1 billion and $39.96 billion respectively. His global sales forecasts for iPads and iPhones are 156.6 million units and 73.2 million units respectively, with a product gross margin of 37.2%.

**Canaccord Genuity**

Analyst Michael Walkley stated that a "more favorable" mix of iPhone and iPad sales should help drive average selling prices and profit margins above market expectations during the holiday season.

He also expects the company to launch a TD-LTE version of the iPhone for China Mobile, which will boost Apple's performance in the first quarter of 2014 and partially offset the seasonal impact after the holiday season in Western markets.

**J.P. Morgan Securities**

Alex Gauna, who has long been bearish on Apple, found the better-than-expected iPhone sales and gross margins stabilized at 37% to be encouraging. However, he maintained the company's "neutral" rating, noting that domestic cash decreased by $5 billion, and neither the iPhone 5s nor the new iPad had "enough innovation" to attract consumers.
