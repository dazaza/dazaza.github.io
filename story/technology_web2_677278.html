Follow-up on the incident where citizens lifted a car to rescue a mother and daughter involved in a traffic accident: the injured are temporarily out of danger.

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-26 22:34:58

It is understood that the specific cause of the accident will be further investigated by the traffic police department. After a discussion, many kind-hearted people decided to lift the bus together to rescue the trapped mother and daughter. However, when dozens of people stood on the side of the bus and used all their strength to lift it, they found that the vehicle did not move at all.

The injured are currently fine. In the hospital, the obedient Lingling received much care from others. When it was learned that 3-year-old Lingling was injured, there were nurse aunts telling her stories, kind patients' families sending her fruits, and uncles and aunts in the same ward preparing her a small bed and bringing hot water.

Subsequently, Master Ou crawled under the car and fixed the jack on the axle and used a crowbar to slowly lift the body.

According to the eyewitness Mr. Wu, after the incident, the bus driver immediately stopped the car, jumped out of the car. When he discovered two people lying under the wheels, his face turned pale. The bus driver originally wanted to take the little girl out of her mother's arms and then pull the mother out. However, both bodies were tightly stuck in one corner of the wheel. Helplessly, the bus driver hurriedly sought help from passers-by, and soon a crowd gathered around.

In the hospital, the doctors introduced that Ms. Lin had an external fracture on the left knee, which was not serious. Lingling had soft tissue contusions and was somewhat drowsy. The specific situation needs further observation.

"How is my Lingling?" "Is she okay?" In the hospital, Ms. Lin was most concerned about her cute little daughter, asking repeatedly to everyone she met. When she learned that her daughter was fine, Ms. Lin sighed with relief and insisted on going through the discharge procedures and requested the doctor to let her personally take care of Lingling in her daughter's ward.

According to Lingling's grandfather, at that time, Lingling was carrying a red small schoolbag, skipping happily ahead. Her mother and grandfather, worried about her, held Lingling's hand. Thus, the three generations walked hand in hand to the bus stop near Ge Dadian vegetable market. No one noticed that a No. 116 bus was slowly approaching behind them.

When the bus made a slight right turn, Ms. Lin seemed to have noticed the danger nearby and tightly hugged her daughter next to her. Subsequently, both were scraped down and got stuck behind the front wheel. After being frightened, Lingling started crying loudly.

Hearing Lingling's crisp cries, the surrounding citizens were all worried. Since the bus was parked on a slope, the kind-hearted citizens worried that the bus might slide down and injure the mother and daughter under the bus, so they found bricks and wedged them under the other three wheels.

"Let me try with the jack!" Master Ou, who lived opposite the bus stop, volunteered. Master Ou is a truck driver. That morning, Master Ou upstairs heard the child's cry and immediately went downstairs to check.

"Out! Out!" Everyone cheered. Subsequently, Ms. Lin and Lingling were pulled out by everyone and were sent to the Third People's Hospital for treatment by the subsequent 120 emergency services.

At around 9:30 yesterday morning, Ms. Lin, living in Dawei Town, brought her 3-year-old daughter Lingling (a pseudonym) and Lingling's grandfather to the city hospital to visit her elder sister-in-law who was about to give birth. Unexpectedly, when passing through the Ge Dadian bus hub station, they were hit by a bus, and the mother and daughter were trapped under the bus for tens of minutes. Fortunately, they were rescued by kind-hearted citizens and are now fine.
