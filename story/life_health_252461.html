Experts from Shanghai Nanpu Gynecology Hospital: Optimal timing choice for abortion during the first pregnancy

Within 10 weeks of pregnancy, the uterus is not too large; the fetus and placenta have not formed yet, generally there's no need to dilate the cervix, and it's easy to remove the fetal tissue. The reaction during surgery is mild, with less bleeding, shorter operation time, and after the abortion, one can go home after resting for 1-2 hours, recovering quickly with minimal impact on the body. Between 10-14 weeks of pregnancy, as the embryo gradually grows, the placenta has already formed, and the uterus also enlarges accordingly. Obstetricians remind that at this stage, simple suction curettage cannot be used for abortion, and forceps evacuation must be adopted instead. This procedure is more difficult, involves more bleeding, slower recovery, and has some impact on the body.

Experts from Shanghai Nanpu Gynecology Hospital: Pain-free abortion is the best choice for women experiencing their first pregnancy

The advantage of pain-free abortion lies in reducing postoperative complications. The surgery is performed under anesthesia, with the patient in a sleep state, feeling comfortable, and not hearing the painful moans of the patient. The doctor can perform the surgery without any interference. This ensures the quality of the surgery and avoids symptoms such as abdominal pain, arrhythmia, blood pressure drop, pallor, dizziness, nausea, and sweating associated with abortion syndrome. Pain-free abortion is highly safe because the patient can fully cooperate with the surgery, significantly reducing surgical complications such as uterine perforation, missed aspiration, and abnormal bleeding. Additionally, the new anesthetic drugs used in pain-free abortions not only relieve physical pain but also greatly reduce the psychological fear of the patient. Therefore, experts from Nanpu Gynecology recommend that women experiencing their first pregnancy choose pain-free abortion.
