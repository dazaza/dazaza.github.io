11 Lesser-Known New Ways to Make Money Online

by hophi on 2008-11-05 09:10:04

Community websites and network applications have quickly become increasingly popular. Young entrepreneurs or businessmen have made a lot of money through simple ideas connecting people and places. It’s no secret that great ideas can change the web.

Are you interested in becoming one of them? The dream is simple: with careful planning and some spare time, people like you can create a wise idea, develop it further, and then get rich. But where are these great ideas?

Why do we keep seeing so much nonsense instead of great new ideas? Where are the sapphires in the rust?

In fact, they're right under your feet. You just need to look more carefully. Here are 11 unpublished ideas. Learn them today, and tomorrow you'll be wealthy. Hurry up! The alarm has already gone off.

1. Inverted Job Board

Mini-market: "Buying and selling jobs online."

Detailed Meaning: Have you heard about bounty job hunting? On Berle Zhike website, it's not employers posting recruitment ads but job seekers posting application ads.

Transaction Principle: Designers, developers, writers, and anyone looking for work can post their requirements along with how much commission they would give to those who help connect them.

Feasible Reason: More and more people are doing freelance work, and sometimes it's hard and time-consuming to find jobs. If freelancers can help you find a job, wouldn't that be great? Especially if you happen to know someone who needs a website or logo design, and someone else is looking for design work.

Income Source: Collecting bounty commissions or charging monthly fees for demand information or contact data.

2. Community Consultation

Mini-market: "Yahoo answers for site owners."

Specific Meaning: A website where site owners pay for advice and receive opinions from experts across various fields.

Transaction Principle: Site owners pay coins (or other currencies - Jingbo note), then post articles with screenshots of their sites along with their goals. Community consultants provide advice and collect coins. Coins can be cashed out or used to ask questions.

Feasible Reason: Views and suggestions are important for improving your site. This method allows you to avoid paying huge consulting fees by only paying small amounts to a team. It will also increase your value over time.

Income Source: Site owners buy coins. For example, if community consultation services for submitted websites cost 25 coins, you could charge 5 coins and pay the remaining 20 coins to those providing the consultation.

3. Online Debate

Mini-market: "Debate Wikipedia."

Specific Meaning: Debates fill every forum and website. They are almost uncontrollable, lack structure, depth, and evidence. A well-managed debate site would definitely have a market. Recently, I saw a similar site called Hey Monkey Brain on Squidoo, but I think there's still room for improvement.

Transaction Principle: Propose a debate topic, choose a side, state your reasons, and encourage others to debate.

Feasible Reason: Everyone likes to express their opinions. Your job is to let them express their opinions and control the debate process so you can make some money.

Income Source: People might pay a little fee to debate, but your main income source should be affiliate advertising or other matching ads.

4. Online Butler Service

Mini-market: "Your online butler."

Specific Meaning: Have you ever thought about online shopping but don't want to spend too much time searching or filling out long forms for plane tickets and movie tickets?

Transaction Principle: You send requests via email, SMS, or voice system to your online butler, such as "Two tickets for Die Hard at 8 PM." Your online butler faithfully follows your instructions. There are automated services now, but an effective human butler service would make you stand out.

Feasible Reason: Currently, outsourcing and personal virtual assistants are hot topics. Leading online butler services would have a big market. There are many busy people in the world. You can offer one standard of service for very wealthy people and another standard for less wealthy people.

Income Source: Monthly or per-service charges.

5. Live Auction Website

Mini-market: "Buy now."

Specific Meaning: Online auction sites are good, but few are as good as live on-site auctions. I think there's still room for improvement in live auction websites.

Transaction Principle: For example, when selling an iPhone, you can only bid on the top item first, which is only valid for a few minutes. Bidding is live and guaranteed, and once the top item is auctioned, the next item begins its auction.

Feasible Reason: As a salesperson, it's getting harder to list items, resonate with buyers, and sell them quickly. A simple live online auction site would solve these problems.

Income Source: Just collect commissions from successful transactions. You must ensure all auctions are genuine. You might require users to deposit some funds before bidding or use other methods.

6. Skill Exchange

Mini-market: "Exchange skills, not cash."

Specific Meaning: A network hub for exchanging skills with others. No need to pay for services; you simply exchange your skills.

Transaction Principle: What needs to be done? Post a "help wanted" ad along with your skills and examples of how you've applied them.

Feasible Reason: There are thousands of top-notch designers, developers, writers, illustrators, musicians, etc., on the internet. But currently, there's no good way to connect them. Mediating disputes and ensuring fair and guaranteed exchanges, a skill exchange website could become a big business.

Income Source: Monthly charges, per-exchange charges, or charging for contact data.

7. Sale of Bestsellers, DVDs, Games, and CDs

Mini-market: "Quickly sell any item with a barcode."

Specific Meaning: Do you have some books, games, DVDs, or CDs that you no longer use? This is a bit embarrassing, isn't it? A webpage or software can scan barcodes via a webcam to quickly list items, saving time and making money.

Transaction Principle: Log in, scan your books, and set prices. That's it! It can operate as a website or as a standalone app linked to Amazon Marketplace.

Feasible Reason: Thousands of people have piles of unused books, games, or other things. But they're too lazy to sort them out! This is your opportunity.

Income Source: Earn commissions from book sales or charge fixed software fees if you go the standalone app route.

8. Read Later Website

Mini-market: "Web reading list."

Specific Meaning: A community reading list on the web. RSS browsers are great, but they aren't social enough, and some people may not adapt well. We need something everyone can use and understand—a web reading list.

A website that combines reading, archiving, and sharing never goes out of style, especially for non-technical people.

Transaction Principle: Have you encountered this situation—seeing a good article but not having time to read it? You can bookmark it, but you might forget soon. Wouldn't it be better to label the page "read later" and save it on a web reading list?

Then, each week you can set aside an hour to read all the articles on the list and categorize them, checking which content is viewed the most.

Feasible Reason: RSS browsers are popular, but socially shared links are even more popular. Combining the two would be great. A Firefox plugin does this, but it needs to be expanded.

Income Source: Make money through advertising, which is a great fundamental business model.

9. Real-time Real Estate Price Index

Mini-market: "How much is your house worth now?"

Specific Meaning: A real-time house price website requires a powerful data model to display house prices on a map. To some extent, someone has already done this, and the market is crowded. But if you can provide the real-time correct price of houses, you will make a fortune.

Transaction Principle: The data model may be very complex. You need to rely on social factors to adjust your prices and build a reliable valuation system based on previous house prices, local trends, market impacts, and customer perspectives.

Feasible Reason: Every landlord wants to know how much his or her house is worth. For large real estate investors, tracking and promptly understanding house prices is crucial. Additionally, a human market index may be useful for real estate agents' sales and valuations.

Income Source: The potential for making money with this idea is unlimited, including house sale commissions, affiliate sales, advertisements for real estate agents and real estate investors, community advertisements, etc.

10. Real-time Public Billboards

Mini-market: "Your ad can appear anywhere instantly."

Specific Meaning: A network of billboards, projector booths at newsstands linked to a website, allowing ordinary people like you to place ads in public places.

Transaction Principle: You register and upload your ad, choose a location, and after confirmation, you can see live webcams showing your ad in any public place around the world.

Feasible Reason: Booth advertising costs are high for many small businesses and individual entrepreneurs. A public, global, mini billboard accessible to ordinary people and small businesses, updated with just a mouse click, could potentially earn millions. If successful, Google might partner with you.

Income Source: Charge fixed prices or monthly costs for placing ads. You can divide electronic billboards/projectors into many small units or sell the entire space or network to the same advertiser.

11. Website Sale

Mini-market: "A place to buy and sell websites."

Specific Meaning: If you have a built blog you want to sell, where would you take it? There is no dominant player in the website sale market yet. What's stopping you from exploring this market?

Transaction Principle: List the features of the website you want to sell, including screenshots, traffic, PageRank values, and revenue status, either at a fixed price or minimum auction price, and then wait for buyers!

Feasible Reason: Domain name sales are a big deal. However, selling websites at a reasonable price or purchasing web properties through a trustworthy channel is still more profitable.

Income Source: Collect commissions from successfully sold websites or charge per service.
