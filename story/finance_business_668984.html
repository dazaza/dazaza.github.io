It can only be the support of other applications

by fanli9um9w554 on 2012-02-18 17:38:24

Started ERP yesterday, was prepared psychologically. Still chaotic today. Lots of noise and the air quality is not so good, so I take the time to go out for a few cigarettes. Classmate Zhengyi is still very active, making an effort to cooperate actively. Little Lu and Ahui also cooperate actively. Youth is great; no matter what, full of hope, strong and healthy, can stay up all night, can eat lots of meat and drink lots of water. At least they can put in more effort on things they don't understand, without much thought to figuring out the reasons. On the contrary, I find myself thinking too much. Maybe this is called sophistication. A group of colleagues went out to eat with everyone paying their own way at noon. It's been too long since I've worked with people from traditional industries, so I find it quite fresh. In my original circle, every word out of our mouths was about IT stuff, but now listening to Zhou Zheng talk about various industries, I'm gaining knowledge. For topics I can't contribute to, I usually smile silently, not for pretense, but for listening. E-commerce is just an online extension of traditional industry operations. Whether it belongs to the internet or traditional industries is no longer important. In another ten years, the internet will also be classified as a traditional industry, and at that time, which merchant won't engage in e-commerce? E-commerce and games are probably the only projects now where you can see tangible results by throwing money at them. Social networking services (SNS) are too hard to survive in such a cold winter. Pure web communities are no longer reliable; they can only support other applications. I wonder if Tianya also needs to lay off some people? Online advertising, no matter how it changes, can't come up with any new tricks anymore. Platforms are kings; who can become the next big player in personal application platforms? The threshold is getting higher and higher. Back in 2002, PONY asked me if I wanted to do games. Naively, I said yes, but I didn't understand. Now, if someone asks me if I want to do games, I would quietly say, yes, because this is one of the few profitable industries left on the internet now. When I was young, I only knew it was fun and interesting. Now, I know it's not just fun, but also know how to make money. This process took six years.
