Han Han's blog links to Japanese adult websites, netizens criticize it as "very yellow and bold"

by pc144 on 2008-04-13 00:16:07

Han Han, who has always been known for his rebellious and unconventional image, has repeatedly challenged traditional societal concepts. He writes books, releases albums, participates in car racing, and previously made bold statements such as "I will never join the Writers' Association; it is a laughable existence" and "I indeed want to set an example by punishing one to warn a hundred; I have long been dissatisfied with these literary critics." Currently, Han Han's personal blog has received 125,696,124 visits, surpassing 100 million, and ranks second in total views on Sina Blog. This blog also created gossip between Han Han and Xu Jinglei, as well as controversies like the so-called "Han Bai dispute." Visitors include loyal fans and casual netizens, resulting in a very large daily traffic.

Sichuan News - The controversial young writer Han Han has once again sparked discussion. Among the only three blog links on his personal blog, there is one named "Matsushima Kaede Japan." Upon opening this link, one can see a lot of restricted content with bold and revealing pictures. "Matsushima Kaede" is a famous Japanese AV actress, and the content of the "Matsushima Kaede Japan" link is her personal blog.

By "recommending" the Japanese AV actress Matsushima Kaede on his public blog, what consequences might arise if minors click or view it? How does Han Han consider this?

Netizens Demand Deletion and Apology

Regarding this link incident, netizens have already posted on the NetEase forum, with over 50,000 clicks within a day, criticizing Han Han with strong words. Initially, when the news broke, many netizens expressed disbelief, and some even thought it was a prank or an automatically added system link. However, on March 17, Han Han published a post titled "Matsushima Kaede," explaining in detail why he added this link and his views on Japanese AV actresses, confirming the incident. After clicking, the website that pops up is Matsushima Kaede's personal Japanese blog, whose main site is an adult AV actress website. A single accidental click could display a series of restricted images on the screen.

For such actions by a public figure, netizens feel very angry: "Since the space is publicly shared, one should be responsible for oneself and others. Moreover, as someone with a special identity, acting so casually is unbecoming and inappropriate!" Netizens demand that Han Han apologize and delete the link. Interestingly, some netizens left humorous comments: "This way, I don't have to waste time looking for the site; it saves me a lot of trouble."

Han Han Admires Matsushima Kaede

When interviewed by phone about this event, Han Han casually stated: "I admire Matsushima Kaede because her boyfriend is a director, and she earns money to help fulfill her boyfriend's dream of making movies. Her spirit deeply moved me." Han Han does not think there is anything wrong with linking to this website and firmly said: "With today's advanced network, how many teenagers haven't browsed these sites or watched 'pornographic films'? I don't think this will alter their life trajectory. Just because they've seen these websites or images doesn't mean they'll necessarily go astray. Bringing this up is merely an excuse for some people, like if people dislike 'Lust, Caution,' they can use this reason as a weapon." In response to online criticism and accusations of "self-promotion," he helplessly said: "Then I can only say 'I laugh at those who cannot see through.'"

"It's Actually Normal and Healthy"

When discussing adult websites and sex culture, Han Han began talking enthusiastically: "In some countries and regions, these things are legal, while others find them illegal, so it's not an absolute thing. Our non-recognition shows we're not healthy yet, and we're not strong enough because these are actually normal and healthy things."

Han Han made a startling statement: "Many foreign countries have established and regulated sex culture markets, such as America's red-light districts. Can any Chinese adult male guarantee they haven't watched pornographic films? And definitely not with a critical attitude?" Regarding this "market," Han Han also proposed his own suggestion, which is to legalize it or semi-legalize it, making it more orderly and beneficial to the mental health of teenagers.

"This Is My Role Model"

As a public figure, every action inevitably attracts much attention and becomes an object of imitation and pursuit. The previous "sex photo scandal" proved that the influence and guidance power of celebrities cannot be underestimated.

Han Han laughed without worry: "I am completely not worried about affecting myself; this is my image, my role model. I want to tell everyone that China's current situation is abnormal and unhealthy. In fact, this also involves a matter of degree. In Japan, there is a mature related market, and they have established a morality committee because some overly extreme and immoral situations may occur, and the morality committee is meant to regulate such behaviors."
