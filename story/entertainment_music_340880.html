2010 Guangzhou Asian Games: Will the ticketing website be reshuffled?

by hipposhen on 2010-04-06 21:50:00

Is Technology King or Service King?

The official ticketing website of the Asian Games, which was originally scheduled to start pre-selling tickets on February 28, 2010, delayed the pre-sale time due to adjustments in the competition schedule of some events. The ticketing website once again drew widespread attention. In terms of the Internet industry, is technology more important or service? This has always been a topic of heated debate. The relatively objective view at present is: for network companies with technology at their core, such as the search engine industry, technology takes the lead. As for ticketing websites, service plays a decisive role. After all, although the technology of ticketing websites is equally complex, it does not need to handle vast amounts of data at will. Is this really the case?

Currently, popular ticketing websites in the market, such as 311 Ticket Network (www.311piao.com), China Ticketing Online (www.piao.com.cn), China Ticketing Pass (www.piaowutong.com), Yongle Ticketing Network (www.228.com.cn), etc., have different focuses in their business models due to differences in market positioning and development directions. China Ticketing Online, Ticketing Pass, and Yongle Ticketing Network all originated from channels and have accumulated deep advantages in ticket sources and services. The 311 Ticket Network is a ticketing platform under City Online, the ticketing operator of the Guangzhou Asian Games. It uses the same system as the official Asian Games ticket booking website, making it a typical emerging technology-oriented website. Judging from the current market performance of both sides, service-oriented websites have the upper hand. However, as a dark horse that has emerged in recent years, 311 Ticket Network has a solid foundation. It is said that it is currently promoting a series of distinctive services. The battle between old and new players makes it difficult to predict who will come out on top.

Understanding the taste of consumers means understanding their wallets.

The large number of tourists attracted by the Guangzhou Asian Games will undoubtedly inject a strong stimulus into Guangzhou's tourism and cultural markets. Various tours, commercial performances, cinemas, and sports events will generate a large number of tickets, making this a "decisive battle" for major ticketing websites. It is said that it is hard to please everyone, and tourists from all over the country will certainly have diverse demands. Because ticket operations rely solely on a website platform is far from enough; it can only capture a niche market of people accustomed to online shopping.

Major ticketing websites are formulating marketing policies for the Asian Games. 311 Ticket Network has already begun setting up booths along the streets, placing terminal ticket machines in chain stores such as 7-11 convenience stores, OK convenience stores, Cheers convenience stores, TianTian Laundry, and Ri Yue Tang, to snatch people who face internet barriers and those who urgently need to pick up tickets.

Enhancing the diversity of ticket purchasing terminals, the convenience of picking up tickets, and the security of payment is an inevitable trend for ticketing websites. The Guangzhou Asian Games has brought unprecedented development opportunities for ticketing websites, but also posed huge challenges. In these opportunities and challenges, the landscape will inevitably change. Who will become the big boss of the ticketing world? Is it the veteran China Ticketing Online, China Ticketing Pass, Yongle Ticketing Network, or the newcomer 311 Ticket Network? We will wait and see.
