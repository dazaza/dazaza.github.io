Upper Gastrointestinal Bleeding

Source: Category: Medicinal Diet Therapy Published: 08-25

Upper gastrointestinal bleeding can manifest as vomiting blood, black stool or bloody stool, which falls under the category of Traditional Chinese Medicine's "vomiting blood" and "bloody stool." After minor bleeding or major bleeding has been controlled with emergency treatment, appropriate medicinal diet therapy can be used for regulation.

1. Stomach Heat causing vomiting of bright red or dark blood, often mixed with food residue, halitosis, fullness and distension in the epigastrium, intermittent pain, black stool or constipation, red tongue with yellow greasy coating, slippery and rapid pulse.

2. Liver Fire causing vomiting of bright red or purple-dark blood, irritability, bitter taste and flank pain, insomnia and vivid dreams, red and purplish tongue, wiry and rapid pulse.

3. Qi Deficiency causing continuous bleeding that does not stop easily, varying in severity, dull and pale blood, fatigue, shortness of breath, palpitations, pale complexion, pale tongue, fine and weak pulse.

Medicinal Diet Therapy Prescriptions:

1. Trumpet Lily (also known as Golden Needle Vegetable): When consumed cooked, it has the effect of clearing liver heat, cooling blood, and stopping bleeding. It can be used as an auxiliary treatment for upper gastrointestinal bleeding caused by stomach heat or liver fire.

2. Water Spinach (also known as Empty Heart Vegetable): Consuming this (raw or cooked) clears the heat from the gastrointestinal tract. It is suitable for the same conditions mentioned above.

3. Lotus Root: Fresh lotus root cools the blood and promotes circulation. For upper gastrointestinal bleeding due to stomach heat or liver fire, fresh lotus root juice can be used as an auxiliary treatment.

4. Black Fungus (also known as Wood Ear): Has a blood-cooling and hemostatic effect. This product can be stir-fried until charred, ground into powder, and taken orally or decocted as medicine for its hemostatic action. Suitable for the same conditions mentioned above.

5. Shepherd’s Purse: Has a blood-cooling and hemostatic effect. It can be eaten raw or cooked and serves as an auxiliary dietary therapy for upper gastrointestinal bleeding associated with liver fire.

6. Sophora Flower: Has the effects of clearing heat, cooling blood, and stopping bleeding. Drinking a decoction of this herb as tea can serve as an auxiliary treatment for bloody stools.

7. Trumpet Lily Drink: Use 60 grams each of trumpet lily and fresh lotus root (sliced), and 30 grams of white茅root. Decoct together and take as a drink. It can serve as an auxiliary treatment for upper gastrointestinal bleeding due to stomach heat or liver fire.

8. Sanqi Steamed Egg: Refer to the section on chronic gastritis and peptic ulcers. Suitable for the same conditions mentioned above.

9. Sanqi Three Juice Milk: Use 30 grams each of fresh white茅root, fresh lotus root, and fresh lesser clematis. Wash, extract the juice, mix with 100 milliliters of milk, add 3 grams of powdered sanqi, and drink. Suitable for the same conditions mentioned above.

10. Modified Gardenia Seed Porridge: Decoct 30 grams of white茅root, filter the juice, remove the residue, add 60 grams of fresh lotus slices and 100 grams of glutinous rice to cook into porridge. When the porridge is almost ready, stir in 6 grams of finely ground gardenia seeds. Cook slightly more until ready, divide into two servings within one day. People with upper gastrointestinal bleeding due to liver fire can use this porridge as an auxiliary treatment.

11. Compound Lotus Porridge: Use 30 grams each of lotus seeds (peeled and without core), yam, and 100 grams of glutinous rice, add an appropriate amount of water, and cook into porridge. Divide into two servings within one day, each time mixing in 3 grams of baiji powder. People with upper gastrointestinal bleeding due to qi deficiency should use this prescription as an auxiliary treatment.

12. Ginseng and Astragalus Sanqi Stewed Chicken: Use one young hen, slaughter it, remove feathers, open the cavity, remove internal organs, wash clean, place 15 grams each of astragalus and ginseng, 9 grams of white atractylodes, 6 grams each of sanqi and tangerine peel in a cheesecloth bag, put inside the chicken cavity; place the chicken in a casserole, add an appropriate amount of water, add appropriate amounts of scallion, ginger, and salt, stew slowly until the chicken is thoroughly cooked, remove the medicine bag, eat the meat and drink the soup. Suitable for the same conditions mentioned above.
