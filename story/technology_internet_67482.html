Don't say I didn't tell you: regular QQ numbers can also clone QQ friends

by e_wuqing on 2008-05-08 10:59:32

NO.1 - Regular QQ numbers can also clone QQ friends

I believe everyone has a few spare QQ numbers, usually obtained during the initial free registration period for QQ. However, sometimes due to certain reasons, you might suddenly need to completely clone all the friends from one QQ number to another. This "massive project" is something most people don't have the time or energy to complete. You don't need to rush to envy the friend cloning feature available to QQ members; we can do it ourselves and still accomplish this "impossible task"!

First, we need to log in again with our original QQ number and the new number. Then, in the folder named after the old QQ number, find the user.db database file, copy it, then go to the new number's folder and paste it over the original file. After logging into the new number, you will find that the friends are there, but they will never come online. Don't worry yet; the next step is the most critical part. Open the Friend Manager, select the people you want to add from the friend list. If selecting multiple, hold down the Ctrl key. After selecting them, drag the selected friends to the strangers section. Here, you might ask, why delete them? Because this is the key step to bring them back to life. In the dialog box that pops up, select confirm for all, then go to the strangers section, select your friends as before, drag them back to the friend list, and they will successfully be revived. However, the next thing will keep you busy for a while. Many friend confirmation dialog boxes will pop up, so just keep clicking. Although this method is a bit clumsy, the goal is still achieved.

NO.2 - Send free SMS via QQ

Under normal circumstances, you can bind your QQ number with your mobile phone number, allowing you to send SMS through QQ, with the cost deducted from your mobile phone bill. However, if your mobile phone is suspended due to non-payment, leading to the cancellation of your phone number (or you cancel the number yourself), since Tencent's data is not synchronized with the telecommunications department's data, QQ remains unaware that your phone number has been canceled. At this point, you can still use the previously bound QQ number to send SMS to other phones without having to pay any fees. Therefore, if someone binds their mobile phone number with their QQ number and then cancels the phone number, they can use that QQ number to send SMS for free! Moreover, the sending speed is incredibly fast!

NO.3 - Hide my QQ, making it invisible!

When at work, due to boredom, you may want to surf the internet and chat with friends, but you're afraid of being discovered by your boss. What should you do? Although QQ's main interface can automatically hide under default settings, what about the QQ icon in the taskbar? Hiding it isn't difficult either. The method is: right-click on the QQ icon in the system tray, choose "System Parameters" from the popup menu, and remove the checkmark before "Show Icon in Taskbar" in the "Parameter Settings." Isn't the icon in the taskbar already gone? If you simultaneously uncheck "Always on Top," the concealment becomes even stronger. How do you open it when you want to? Use the shortcut key "Ctrl+Alt+Z," or set your own shortcut key.

NO.4 - Make the other party's QQ blacklist ineffective

Sometimes, because of an accidental mistake or a wrong word, the other party deletes you or drags you to the blacklist out of "temporary dissatisfaction," preventing us from talking to them anymore. Here, I'll tell you a way to make the blacklist ineffective. With this method, even if they add us to the "blacklist," it won't matter; we can still re-add them to our friend list and continue sending messages to them. The principle is quite simple, actually exploiting a bug in QQ. This bug occurs during the system authentication process.

Now let me explain in detail. If the other party drags you into the "blacklist," we cannot chat with our friend anymore. However, if they originally sent you a join request, we just need to find that command in the "System Messages," click the "Pass Verification" button, and we can be added back to their QQ friend list. Even if they delete you from their friend list or add you to the "blacklist," it doesn't matter; we can use this method to return to their friend list. It's impossible for them to keep us out.
