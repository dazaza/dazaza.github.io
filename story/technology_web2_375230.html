Kuantu: China's Tumblr?

by ybcz0519 on 2010-12-19 12:12:55

This article is simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media please do not reprint! Kuantu is a social sharing site that allows users to post text, images, articles, and videos. In fact, the first time I saw this site, I thought of Tumblr.com. Tumblr's current valuation has reached $135 million, and I have been wondering why there hasn't been a similar site domestically. After seeing Kuantu, it can be said to be good news for domestic users who like Tumblr but cannot use it.

Main functions:

- Allows users to create pages similar to watsonxu.kuantu.com;

- Users can customize the style of their own pages or choose existing templates;

- Users can conveniently bookmark content through the provided browser plugin, or directly post content on the website;

- Provides a follow mechanism, allowing you to directly follow interesting users, and follow information will appear on the user's homepage;

- Provides a showcase wall to display various interesting collections;

- Currently testing the event release function, and released events can be seen by users who follow you;

Watsonxu says: Since Kuantu is quite similar to Tumblr, let me talk about Tumblr first. Actually, Tumblr is a type of lightweight blog platform. I used to wonder about the difference between Tumblr and Twitter. In fact, Tumblr focuses more on content dissemination of all kinds of content. In this sense, Sina Weibo is more like an enhanced version of Tumblr rather than Twitter. However, the relationships on Tumblr are actually weaker than those on Weibo. A weaker relationship isn't necessarily a disadvantage; personally, I think it represents another aspect of demand, which is the need for weak content sharing, limited to the spread within attention relationships. This may also be the value of Tumblr and Kuantu!

Link: http://www.kuantu.com/

In order to better understand Kuantu, I conducted an interview via email with its founder, Sun Chen (Jerry):

1. Please briefly introduce your product.

Kuantu is a simple light-blog that makes it easy for you to share everything in life. For more specific introductions, see here: http://help.kuantu.com/kite/542689

2. Briefly introduce yourself and your team.

Our team is in Beijing, relatively small, with fewer than 10 people. The common point is that we all love Kuantu and believe in its success.

3. What was the original intention behind creating this product?

We hope to have a place for ordinary people to create and share.

4. When I first saw Kuantu, I immediately thought of Tumblr. Can you tell us the differences between Kuantu and Tumblr? What are Kuantu's features and advantages?

We believe that websites of the Tumblr type are the direction of future social media development. We hope to combine the actual situation of China's Internet and draw on and absorb good elements suitable for Chinese netizens from traditional blogs, Twitter, Plurk, and Tumblr.

Currently, the differences with Tumblr are:

1) Emphasize interaction: such as replies and instant communication attempts we're trying, we believe these conform to the habits of Chinese users.

2) Emphasize creativity and extensibility: each user can have multiple pages, we encourage users to express different interests or personalities through different themed pages, and even some users customize their pages into mini personal navigation websites, like about.kuantu.com.

3) Emphasize cooperation: we encourage a page to be composed of multiple users, forming small-scale groups to jointly create and share.

4) Emphasize simplicity and convenience: compared to the main functional processes and main pages, we are simpler and more convenient than Tumblr.

Features and advantages:

Simplicity: we highly value simple design, simple usage, simple interface, and powerful functions, which will always be our goal. Many users' positive reviews come from the first impression of simplicity.

Creativity: create in various ways at any time (RSS import, bookmark tools, future platform clients), encourage active forwarding and replies, spread excellent resources of creative users.

Besides these two that I can immediately think of, many advantages of microblogs and traditional blogs, as well as Douban groups and traditional forums, can be integrated into Kuantu to meet needs, which I won't detail here.

5. What kind of website does Kuantu hope to become in the future (vision)?

Vision? That would be One Piece, ^_^

6. Has Kuantu considered business model issues so far?

Yes, but currently, we mainly focus on improving user experience.
