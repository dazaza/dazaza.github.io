Hai Yong's commentary: Can't wait to teach girls how to contracept

by qaswr673 on 2011-06-10 15:11:59

Original article from Xinmin Network: After the summer vacation, the number of post-90s girls getting "abortion" has greatly increased.

"In these days after many schools have started classes again, I found that a lot of young girls come to consult about abortion. Some come by themselves, some are accompanied by their boyfriends. Looking at their naive faces, I feel not only shocked but also deeply saddened." Recently, an unnamed gynecologist told reporters that after the summer vacation, in the gynecology departments of some hospitals in Dongguan, the number of post-90s girls getting abortions has increased significantly compared to normal times.

A girl just turned 16 after the summer vacation and came for pregnancy testing.

"A few days ago, I received a young couple. The girl had long hair down her back, fair skin, and appeared nervous and shy. The boy tightly held the girl's hand, his sparse beard could not hide his youthful innocence. They came to ask me how to determine if she was pregnant. I felt very sad at the time," a gynecologist told reporters.

After the girl was brought into the examination room, she told the doctor that she and the boy waiting outside were classmates and had been dating for a while. During the summer vacation, they lived together. Unexpectedly, more than a month passed, and the girl realized her period had not come. She bought a pregnancy test strip from a small pharmacy, which showed that she was pregnant. She was worried because she had just turned 16 after the summer vacation, so she came to the hospital for further confirmation.

"On the age column of the medical record, I saw her carefully write down '16'. It was clear that she was also very upset. So I asked her if she had taken any contraceptive measures when she started having sex so early, and if she had considered the consequences? She just shook her head regretfully and did not answer."

Abortion is like treating a cold

"Generally, it is during the last week of summer vacation and the first few weeks before school starts on Fridays and Saturdays that we see the most students here," the doctor told reporters. Most students coming for abortions are between 16 and 22 years old, with 50% being high school students, some being freshmen or sophomores in college, and even some junior high school students. "Sometimes in one week, there are 5-6 girls under 20 years old in the medical records. In the past, our hospital performed an average of one or two abortions every two days, now there are two or three cases every day. Our medical guides can tell when students come in that they are here for an abortion."

What made this doctor even more heartbroken was that unintended teenage pregnancies led to bitter consequences, but some teenagers didn't take abortions seriously at all. "I am also a mother, so I feel very concerned. Once, I received a pair of middle school-aged youths who came holding hands and laughing. As soon as they walked in, they directly asked if she could get a free abortion," the doctor told reporters. Before the doctor could speak, the girl confidently took out her student ID to prove that she was eligible for a free operation. "Nowadays, children are not like our generation. They treat abortion as casually as catching a common cold, which is astonishing and heartbreaking!"

Experts warn of the great harm of abortion

Regarding this phenomenon, experts analyzed that with the development of information technology, teenagers have many channels to obtain sexual information, and negative sexual information can easily mislead them. Although many parents recognize the importance of sex education, in actual practice, they are often passive, especially among parents of high school students. When asked questions such as "Have you provided reproductive education?", "Have you discussed sexual organs?", "Have you talked about how to interact with the opposite sex?", less than 20% of high school parents answered "yes", and some options were even lower than those of elementary and middle school parents.

In response, gynecological experts pointed out that teenage abortions can be extremely harmful. Girls should not take abortion lightly, or they will pay a heavy price. It is understood that abortion surgery may result in complications such as infection, bleeding, and uterine perforation. Some women who undergo abortions may experience menstrual disorders, uterine cavity adhesions, and endometriosis, and in severe cases, lifelong infertility. If multiple abortions are performed, the consequences will be even more serious.

Comment: The sexual concepts of doctors and parents shocked and saddened me

Teenage pregnancy is no longer a novelty. What shocked and saddened me from this report was not teenage pregnancy itself, but the obstetrician and the parents who always treat children as little kids. As an obstetrician, one should know the age of sexual maturity and what teenagers want to do after reaching sexual maturity. I was shocked that the doctor ignored professional knowledge and instead adhered rigidly and conservatively to social norms, suppressing physiological knowledge.

We have seen a previous report indicating that in Guangzhou, the onset of puberty for teenagers has advanced from 13 years old to 11 years old. So, let's think about it: The 16 to 22-year-olds mentioned in the report have been through puberty for 5 to 10 years. Their sexual development is mature, and they have engaged in sexual activity. Where is the shock? And where is the sadness? Think about our grandparents' generation; boys and girls getting married at 16 was quite common. Who has heard of 16 to 22-year-olds engaging in sexual activity and being harmed?

Some people seem unable to understand the difference between natural and social sexuality. Whether in school or working, whether appearing innocent or mature, none of these factors necessarily correlate with their own sexual maturity. When sexually mature, wanting to engage in sexual activity is completely normal.

I find the last paragraph of this report particularly noteworthy: Less than 20% of high school parents pay attention to their children's sex education. On one hand, sexual maturity occurs earlier, sexual awareness awakens earlier, sexual morality becomes weaker, and sexual behavior becomes more common. On the other hand, the sexual concepts of both parents and even doctors remain stuck in the past. Continuing like this, more teenagers will be ruined by outdated societal sexual consciousness.

Original Article Two: In Guangdong, annual income of 200,000 yuan results in a maximum fine of 480,000 yuan for exceeding birth limits.

http://news.xmnext.com/domestic/shehui/2007/09/18/892180.html

Comment: Exceeding birth limits fines must not break the bottom line of legal equality

The overpopulation issue mentioned in this report is something we often hear about. On one hand, the rich have money to raise children and don't fear fines for exceeding birth limits, wanting to have more children; on the other hand, in vast rural areas due to traditional views of having more children, without considering what kind of life they can provide for their children, they blindly have more children anyway.

Family planning is very necessary for our country, which has a large population. Local areas implementing some local practices based on their actual conditions can be understood. However, the method in Guangzhou of determining fines based on individuals' incomes, I believe, is very inappropriate. On one hand, it is difficult to accurately calculate each family's actual income; on the other hand, doing so severely violates the fundamental legal principle of equality before the law. The rationale is similar to how compensation amounts cannot be determined based on the economic situation of the person causing harm.

Everyone should equally bear the amount of the overpopulation fine. Otherwise, it would give the impression that relevant departments are taking advantage of the situation to make money. Just as righteous people value wealth obtained through proper means, this applies to individuals, units, and departments alike. Otherwise, while managing violations of laws and policies, it also conveys a message to society - the entrenched idea in China of robbing the rich to help the poor; this is contrary to the state's guiding ideology of letting a part of the population get rich first.
