The Easter egg is absolutely classic.

The Sorbs, as the only minority in Germany, celebrate Jesus' resurrection in the form of a hundred-ride grand chorus. Sorb people, dressed in black shirts and wearing black hats, ride on horses adorned with ribbons, flowers, and white shells, marching in great numbers along forest paths. As they walk, they sing hymns with their rough and powerful voices, making for an incredibly spectacular scene.

In Western Easter celebrations, typical Easter gifts are associated with spring and rebirth: eggs, chicks, bunnies, flowers, especially lilies which are symbolic of the season. On the eve of Easter, children decorate eggs for their friends and families. These eggs are either hard-boiled or just empty shells. On Easter morning, children will find Easter baskets by their beds filled with chocolate eggs, Easter bunnies, fluffy chicks, and doll toys. It is said that the Easter Bunny hides colored eggs indoors or in the grass for children to find. The annual White House Easter Egg Roll event is often broadcast live by television stations.

In most Western countries, grand religious parades are held during Easter. Parade participants wear long robes, hold crosses, and proceed barefoot. They dress up as characters from Christian history and sing hymns to celebrate Jesus' resurrection. Nowadays, these parades have lost much of their former religious intensity. The festive atmosphere is filled with vibrant folk characteristics and regional features. In the U.S., the parade includes clowns dressed as cowboys on stilts and beloved cartoon characters like Mickey Mouse. In Britain, parades focus more on introducing local history and customs, with participants dressed as Scottish bagpipe bands and palace guards, attracting numerous tourists. The arrival of Easter also prompts people to change into new clothes. In the past, Christian believers would go to church for baptism before the festival and then put on their new robes to celebrate Christ's new life. The custom of dressing anew has been preserved to this day because people believe it’s bad luck not to wear new clothes during the holiday. During Easter, people also enjoy thoroughly cleaning their homes, symbolizing the start of a new life.

The German government stipulates two days off for Easter. During the festival, families gather together to taste various traditional foods, and relatives and friends exchange congratulations upon meeting.

Eggs, fire, water, rabbits, and other symbols of life have become auspicious signs for Easter. Eggs and rabbits in the West represent new life and prosperity. The natural color of eggs symbolizes the sun, while dyeing eggs red symbolizes happiness in life. During Easter, parents specially prepare chocolates shaped like eggs and rabbits for their children. Friends and family exchange colored eggs. In some towns in the Middle Rhine region and eastern Hesse, the ancient custom of "colored egg trees" still exists today. People paint hundreds of eggshells and string them into chains, hanging them on pine trees on Easter to create colorful egg trees. Adults and children sing and dance around the egg tree to celebrate Easter. Meanwhile, young women in the Alps express their love through the gift of red eggs. If a girl gives three red eggs to a young man on Easter, it signifies the girl's expression of love towards him.

There is a legend about why rabbits have become auspicious symbols of Easter. In ancient times, when Jesus was resurrected, it coincided with the "Spring Sun Festival" celebrated by the Scandinavian residents. As one of the most prolific animals in the grasslands and forests, rabbits symbolized the revival of spring and the birth of new life. Additionally, they were the pet of the goddess of love Aphrodite and the candle-bearer for the Germanic earth goddess Holle. Therefore, now rabbits serve as messengers who deliver Easter eggs to children, making them deeply loved by kids. Children are sure to receive rabbit-shaped gifts on Easter.

Fire not only brought light to humanity but also gave the earth new life. As a symbol of Jesus' rebirth, many Easter activities are related to fire.

On Easter, people light candles outside the church to signify sanctification and bring holy candles into every household. On this day, the happiest thing for children is delivering the holy fire to each home. They light branches with the holy fire outside the church and then run to deliver it to every household, filled with joyous festival atmosphere. In Bavaria, Germany, residents hold torch races every Easter to celebrate Jesus' rebirth. The famous Easter firewheel rolling in Luetzhoehe, North Rhine-Westphalia is well-known. Six giant wooden wheels are set on fire and roll down the valley, resembling six fiery balls descending from the sky, illuminating the dark valley brightly. The firewheels and colorful fireworks complement each other, once again showing how fire brings new life to mankind.
