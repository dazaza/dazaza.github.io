Indonesia guarantees ethylene product supply and accelerates the development of petrochemical industry

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-29 16:47:46

It was reported that Indonesia Industry Department planned to speed up the expansion of naphtha cracking and refinery processing capacity, better providing raw materials for petrochemical production. It aimed at realizing the self-sufficiency of petrochemicals by 2025, thereby摆脱摆脱shedding dependence on imports. With the continuous increase in the population of middle-class people in Indonesia, Indonesia's economy maintained an annual average growth rate of more than 6% in the past few years, which drove the rapid growth in demand for automobiles, diapers, bottled water and other packaging materials.

Accordingly, it was expected that the demand for main petrochemical raw materials such as ethylene and propylene in Indonesia would grow rapidly. The ethylene demand would increase from the current 1.12 million tons/year to 2.1 million tons/year, the propylene demand would increase from 700,000 tons/year to 1.9 million tons/year, and the polyethylene (PE) resin demand would increase from 600,000 tons/year to 1.55 million tons/year. The market trend of chemical industry showed that the polypropylene (PP) demand would increase from the current 1 million tons/year to 1.77 million tons/year.

Currently, the main raw materials of Indonesia's petrochemical industry all relied on imports, including naphtha. Its oil processing and petrochemical industries developed relatively lagged behind. Pertamina, Indonesia National Oil Company, was an integrated company for petroleum exploitation and petroleum processing with a crude oil processing capacity of about 50 million tons/year. Chandra Asri Petrochemical Company was the only ethylene plant in Indonesia with an ethylene production capacity of 600,000 tons/year, while the domestic ethylene demand exceeded 1 million tons/year. There was a huge gap. With the continued rapid economic growth, the future supply gap would further expand.

For this reason, in recent years, Indonesia National Oil Company had increased its cooperation with foreign investors, planning to cooperate with Kuwait Petroleum Corporation and Saudi Aramco to build two refineries with a processing capacity of 15 million tons/year so as to end the dependence on imported fuel as soon as possible. Chandra Asri Petrochemical Company also planned to expand its naphtha ethylene cracking production capacity. In addition, Hunan Petrochemical Company of Korea said that it was considering investing $4.49 billion to build a petrochemical complex in Indonesia. Currently, the company had a petrochemical comprehensive production capacity of 2.5 million tons/year in Korea and Malaysia.
