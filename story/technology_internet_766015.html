Samsung Smartwatch Galaxy Gear Leaked In Real-Life Pictures

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:09:18

After the hardware specifications and application screenshots were exposed, Samsung's smartwatch, Galaxy Gear, has finally been revealed today. The pictures obtained by VentureBeat come from a promotional video provided by a third-party team collaborating with Samsung; the informant did not allow the shooting of the actual device.

The Galaxy Gear features a 3-inch OLED display, built-in health and fitness tracking tools. Samsung aims to make the Galaxy Gear a device similar to Nike FuelBand — a smartphone accessory rather than a replacement.

The device is large in size, with a thick frame surrounding the square screen. The color options are somewhat monotonous, only black and gray are available. It is estimated that Samsung will gradually introduce more color options after the launch, similar to how they handle their Galaxy phones.

The wide strap gives off a masculine vibe, which might not appeal to women. Christina Farr, author at VentureBeat, said, "After wearing it, my small female arm looks even tinier." It remains unknown whether Samsung will release a Galaxy Gear mini specifically designed for women.

A 4-megapixel camera is embedded in the strap, with a tiny microphone hole located at the buckle where the strap connects to the body. You can use the camera to take pictures of food and tag them, such as "milk" or "chocolate." Samsung plans to assess the health attributes of food through images captured by the camera, although this discreet lens might be used for other purposes by many people.

The Galaxy Gear connects to phones and tablets via Bluetooth and may also support devices from other manufacturers. It also comes with Wi-Fi for easy access to the internet and checking emails.

Other information includes:

1. A dual-core processor, 1GB RAM, 6GB or 8GB internal storage

2. Samsung S Voice voice control

3. Pre-installed Android social networking and health tracking applications

4. Swipe to quickly access applications, launch the camera, and browse pictures

5. Call Logs function, which can transfer calls to the watch

6. Over 10 hours of battery life

Health-related uses:

- Track personal health data such as pace, heart rate, and calorie consumption

- Monitor heart rate at any time using the built-in heart rate sensor

- Assist in creating exercise plans

- Track food intake through photos taken, with alerts for overeating
