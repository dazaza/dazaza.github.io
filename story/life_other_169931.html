Nanjing vicious traffic accident caused by an intoxicated driver

by kaxiya on 2009-07-03 09:59:39

Nanjing car accident driver receives highest penalty: death sentence plus 2.2 million yuan in compensation

Can the maximum sentence only be 15 years? Yesterday, a media outlet speculated on the tragic case that occurred in Jiangning the previous evening, which left many netizens and citizens feeling incomprehensible. Where is the fairness and justice of the law if such a cruel person is given a light sentence? Yesterday, the reporter consulted several senior lawyers and legal experts about this case, all of whom believed that Zhang Mingbao's actions were not just a case of traffic accident (escape), but could also involve charges of intentional homicide or endangering public safety by dangerous means. The maximum punishment for these two charges can reach the death penalty. In terms of civil compensation, senior traffic police believe that Zhang Mingbao and the car owner Wang Yun will face a huge claim of 1.7 to 2.2 million yuan.

The tragedy has also sparked reflection on the bad habit of drunk driving, which is extremely irresponsible to society and oneself. Law enforcement agencies in Jiangsu Province have launched special crackdown operations across the province. It seems that the tragedy did not awaken the awareness of drivers who like to drink. Last night, during the crackdown operation, Nanjing police caught a large number of drunk drivers, including a government official.

What crime did he commit?

Traffic Accident + Endangering Public Safety + Intentional Homicide

"It's too shocking!" Jia Zhenghe, the director of Jiangsu Shengdian Law Firm, who has been engaged in criminal defense work for many years, has seen countless tragedies, but when he read the report on the Jiangning car accident in the Express, he couldn't help but hit the table with his fist, expressing his anger at Zhang Mingbao's drunk driving behavior.

"People who commit crimes while drunk should bear criminal responsibility," Jia Zhenghe said. Whether a person drinks or gets drunk has nothing to do with the case itself, and the perpetrator must bear full responsibility. According to the current investigation by the police, Zhang Mingbao is obviously fully responsible for this accident, and his actions have already undoubtedly constituted the crime of causing a traffic accident resulting in five deaths and four injuries. But whether it involves other charges still needs analysis.

Jia Zhenghe stated that Zhang Mingbao's drunk driving and collision with the first person can be identified as the crime of causing a traffic accident. However, after that, knowing that his behavior was out of control, he still fled at high speed, resulting in multiple casualties. The accident occurred at around 8:20 PM, when there were still many pedestrians and vendors on the road. The perpetrator should have been able to foresee that his behavior might cause harm to society.

"In a situation where consequences can be foreseen, continuing to drive at high speed after hitting someone without stopping, thus allowing the danger to persist, indicates an indirect intention, which meets the legal elements of endangering public safety by dangerous means." Therefore, Lawyer Jia believes that Zhang Mingbao's behavior should be prosecuted for suspected crimes of causing a traffic accident and endangering public safety by dangerous means, and punished for multiple crimes.

In this regard, Dr. Wu Dawei, a criminal law expert, believes that according to reports, the perpetrator's behavior is at least related to three charges: one is causing a traffic accident, one is intentional homicide, and another is endangering public safety by dangerous means.

"The first collision should be considered negligence, establishing the crime of causing a traffic accident (escape)," Wu Dawei said. Subsequent behaviors theoretically may result in two interpretations. One leans towards intentional homicide, which here should be indirect intentional homicide; the other leans towards endangering public safety by dangerous means.

Wu Dawei stated that the perpetrator's behavior actually constitutes "imaginary competition," meaning the same act violates two charges: endangering public safety by dangerous means and intentional homicide. Taking the heavier punishment would suffice.

But among the two charges, which is the more severe crime? Depending on the specific circumstances of the case, different views exist, forming two possibilities. Regardless of the possibility, they will definitely be combined with the charge of causing a traffic accident for multiple punishments.

Yesterday, many netizens expressed various opinions online. Although everyone agreed that Zhang Mingbao has aroused great public resentment, considering his behavior as a traffic accident, he may not have had the subjective intent to kill. Therefore, netizens believe that he may just end up serving a few years in prison. However, both Jia Zhenghe and Wu Dawei expressed differing views.

Jia Zhenghe introduced that if it was merely a case of causing a traffic accident, the maximum sentence would clearly be no more than seven years of fixed-term imprisonment. However, according to Article 115 of the Criminal Law, endangering public safety by dangerous means, "a sentence of more than ten years of fixed-term imprisonment, life imprisonment, or the death penalty" can be imposed. If Zhang Mingbao's behavior constitutes both causing a traffic accident and endangering public safety by dangerous means and is sentenced for multiple crimes, the maximum sentence can certainly be life imprisonment or even the death penalty.

Wu Dawei said that according to Article 232 of the Criminal Law: "For those who intentionally kill others, the punishment shall be death, life imprisonment, or fixed-term imprisonment of more than ten years; if the circumstances are relatively minor, the punishment shall be fixed-term imprisonment of three to ten years."

From the situation of the Jiangning accident, Zhang Mingbao's behavior does not fall under the category of relatively minor circumstances. Therefore, if sentenced for multiple crimes based on causing a traffic accident and intentional homicide, the punishment would not be light, with the maximum reaching life imprisonment or the death penalty.

However, Wu Dawei said that these discussions are theoretical. In practice, judges must make comprehensive judgments based on the specific circumstances of the case.

The highest possible sentence could be the death penalty.

How will it be sentenced?

Jia Zhenghe stated that according to the current police investigation, the owner of the Buick LaCrosse with license plate number Su ATH900 should be Wang Yungang.

However, Wang Yungang claimed that he actually sold the car to Zhang Mingbao last year. That is, the perpetrator and the car owner are not the same person, but neither party has registered the change of ownership. According to relevant laws, changes in registration for properties like houses and cars should be processed with the relevant department. Without registering the change, it cannot counteract the claims of a good faith third party.

In this case, if Wang Yungang's statement is true, his status as the car owner is merely nominal, and the actual owner and controller of the vehicle is the driver involved in the accident.

Therefore, Jia Zhenghe believes that civil liability should be borne by the driver involved in the accident for compensation, while the nominal car owner Wang Yungang should bear supplementary compensation liability.

However, some legal professionals indicated that currently, Wang Yungang's statement lacks supporting evidence.

In such a situation, as the car owner and actual driver, Wang Yungang and Zhang Mingbao should bear joint compensation liability, meaning the victims can file a civil compensation lawsuit against either or both of them.

With five deaths and four injuries, how much civil compensation is required? According to a seasoned police officer from the Traffic Management Bureau's Accident Division, the compensation standards for traffic accident victims are not uniform, and the compensation amounts vary for different victims.

He said that according to the current standard, for fatal accidents, if the driver is fully responsible, the typical compensation amount is between 300,000 to 400,000 yuan.

"The situation for injured people is more complicated, depending on their treatment costs and whether any sequelae remain, with significant differences in amounts."

He explained that for the five deceased individuals, adding the fact that one was pregnant, the compensation amount would be relatively higher. A rough estimate puts the total compensation amount at 1.7 to 2.2 million yuan.

Legal professionals stated that victims can directly seek compensation from Zhang Mingbao and Wang Yungang. Wang Yungang's losses can be recovered from Zhang Mingbao through another procedure.

Death compensation alone amounts to around 2 million yuan, and the car owner must bear responsibility.

How much compensation is required?

"After drinking, driving is an insurance company's exemption clause, this is a common practice in the insurance industry, and it is explicitly written in the insurance contract!" Yesterday, Mr. Wang, an insurance claims expert from People's Insurance Company, told reporters that for the major accident caused by Zhang Mingbao's drunk driving, the insurance company will not compensate.

It is understood that according to current insurance company practices, there are mainly two types of insurance contracts. One is a general insurance contract, which does not limit the driver. After an incident, regardless of who drove the car, the insurance company will pay the compensation to the insured. The other type limits the driver. This kind of insurance contract usually specifies that if the designated driver causes an accident, normal procedures apply for compensation; if a non-designated driver causes an accident, a certain discount ratio applies for compensation. However, regardless of the type of insurance contract, insurance companies clearly specify in the contract that drunk driving, overdue vehicle inspections, and unlicensed driving are exempted from liability. Therefore, in the accident caused by Zhang Mingbao, regardless of who the insured is, as long as the actual driver was drunk, the insurance company can refuse compensation.
