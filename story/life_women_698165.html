1. Pole Dance: Toning and Body Sculpting

Most Suitable For: Sexy girls who want to create toned curves

Is pole dancing just a sexy dance? Of course not! In fact, it is also a strength exercise that shapes perfect curves.

Pole dancing consists of two parts: tricks on the pole and dance moves off the pole. The pole tricks effectively exercise every muscle in your body, especially the arms, thighs, buttocks, and waist, providing excellent body sculpting effects. The dance moves off the pole incorporate elements from various dances such as sexy jazz, belly dancing, and ballet. These moves combine flexibility with passionate, strong movements, fully expressing the allure of a woman. Women who practice pole dancing will naturally exude an alluring charm even when they are away from the pole in their everyday gestures.

Coach's Private Tips: "Why do some girls who aren't fat still look less than perfectly shaped, appearing soft and loose? Because being thin doesn't necessarily mean being toned. Pole dancing actually trains the muscles, but unlike weightlifting equipment, it won't easily make the muscles bulky. It's similar to yoga in that it elongates and firms the muscles through various stretching exercises, making the entire body more toned, making it particularly suitable for women to practice. However, pole dancing differs from yoga in that it's not as quiet as yoga, yet it has more feminine charm. The self-absorbed feeling you experience while dancing pole dance is something yoga cannot offer. Moreover, it can exercise areas that are usually hard to work out, such as the back and lower back—these are places where women really want to tighten up."

2. Hip-Hop: Full-body Weight Loss

Hip-Hop Full-body Weight Loss

Most Suitable For: Young, energetic girls who want full-body weight loss

You can call it hip-hop, HIP-HOP, or street dance—whatever you prefer.

If you want to lose weight, this is undoubtedly the best choice. Why? Because it is inherently a great aerobic exercise. While dancing, you burn a large amount of calories, engaging muscles throughout your body. At the same time, it improves cardiovascular function and achieves the goal of weight loss.

The steps in hip-hop are simple, have a large range of motion, and are powerful, yet when combined, they express complex dance sensations. It’s relatively easy to learn. The accompanying music is always very dynamic, without any fixed style or pattern. You can freely express yourself to the music, sweating profusely while also relieving stress. The exaggerated movements and strong bursts of energy allow you to release all your emotions while dancing—it truly feels great. Its movements also involve a lot of small joints and small muscles, reaching areas that are usually difficult to exercise. If you want full-body weight loss, this is the best option!"
