 Facebook Challenges LinkedIn with Skills Pages

　　According to reports on September 9th, Beijing time, social network Facebook is currently testing the addition of "skills" content to user profiles in a limited capacity, aiming to challenge LinkedIn.

　　The "skills" feature that Facebook is testing is located under the work and education section in the "about" section of a user's personal homepage. Users simply need to fill in relevant professional information.

　　After users have filled in their skills information, they will be associated with certain interests, making it easier for other users who are "non-friends" but share similar interests and skills to find them. Users can search for others with similar interests through "Graph Search." Clearly, this move by Facebook is an imitation of professional networking site LinkedIn, hoping to help users find jobs online using social network relationships.

　　In fact, LinkedIn launched this function much earlier. However, currently, on LinkedIn user profile summaries, these interests are "static," meaning they do not allow users to browse other users with similar interests. If users use search, they can only look for people, jobs, companies, organizations, and universities, none of which are based on interests.

　　Facebook has always been "half a step behind" in the online recruitment and job hunting market, and the launch of the "skills" feature is a sign that the company wants to accelerate its pace. A year ago, Facebook announced a partnership with the U.S. Department of Labor and other institutions to launch the "Social Jobs Partnership" application, which made over 1.7 million job opportunities available to users.

　　Currently, it is unclear whether Facebook's "skills" feature will be promoted globally or is only targeted at a small market.
