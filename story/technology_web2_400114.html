Analysis Report on the Demand for Express Service Satisfaction

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 11:34:11

In recent years, with the rapid rise of e-commerce, the express delivery industry has entered a period of high-speed development and achieved remarkable results. However, during this rapid development, issues such as random unloading of goods, contract traps, signing before inspection, and difficulties in compensation have been frequently exposed. Express delivery services have become one of the hotspots for consumer complaints. It is not difficult to see that there is still a significant gap between the quality of service in the express delivery industry and user expectations.

To objectively evaluate the role of China's express delivery industry at all levels of society and promote the improvement of service standards among various express companies, CCTV.com's logistics channel has launched a "China Express Industry Service Satisfaction Survey" activity for netizens nationwide. According to the voting results, netizens' satisfaction with express delivery services is only 70%, with the speed of collection and delivery, cargo safety, service attitude, and claims insurance becoming the focal points of attention for netizens.

### I. Analysis of Express Delivery Service Satisfaction

During the survey process, we statistically summarized the factors that make netizens satisfied or dissatisfied with express delivery services. The specific situation is as follows:

#### Figure 1: Satisfactory Factors of Express Delivery Services

#### Figure 2: Unsatisfactory Factors of Express Delivery Services

From the above two figures:

(1) In the collection phase, netizens are more satisfied with express delivery services that deliver quickly and have skilled staff; they are dissatisfied with companies that take too long to collect items, have slow delivery speeds, and experience delays.

(2) In terms of transportation range, netizens are more satisfied with express companies that have wide transportation ranges and comprehensive network coverage. Many netizens suggested in their feedback that “the scope of express delivery should be expanded” and “hope that rural areas can also benefit from online shopping.”

(3) Regarding cargo safety, netizens are more satisfied with express deliveries where the outer packaging is intact and there is no damage or loss. They are extremely dissatisfied with situations where goods are tampered with, damaged, or lost. Netizens have urged express companies to avoid rough handling and sorting errors, suggesting that “goods should not ‘fly around’ anymore.”

(4) In terms of transportation price, netizens are more satisfied with express companies that offer transparent and reasonable freight charges. They hope for standardization of prices across different companies, avoiding cutthroat competition based on low prices, and focusing instead on improving service.

(5) In terms of service attitude, netizens are more satisfied with express personnel who wear uniforms, are polite, and allow inspection before signing. They also have high requirements for telephone voice services.

(6) In terms of after-sales service, netizens are satisfied with express companies that provide online tracking and inquiry services for packages but are dissatisfied with aspects such as failure to honor insurance, difficulty in making complaints, and non-standard compensation.

### II. Analysis of Attention to Express Delivery Services

So, what factors do netizens care about most when using express delivery services?

#### Figure 3: Factors Most Concerned by Netizens in Express Delivery Services

From the statistical results of the above figure, it can be seen that netizens generally pay high attention to the following aspects:

(1) Speed of sending and receiving packages. The speed of package receipt and dispatch is the most important factor for netizens when using express delivery services, accounting for 21%. According to statistics from the 315 Consumer Electronics Complaint Network, delays remain the main issue in complaints about the express delivery industry. In 2010, there were a total of 15,194 complaints related to delays, accounting for 50.20% of the total number of complaints, an increase of 6 percentage points compared to 2009. The demand for "speed" is paramount.

(2) Cargo safety. Cargo safety is a longstanding and relatively prominent issue in the express delivery industry. 19% of netizens are most concerned about cargo safety (loss and damage rate), which ranks second.

(3) Insurance and after-sales service. Besides service attitude and transportation range, which remain key focuses for netizens, it is worth noting that 17% of netizens chose "failure to honor insurance and difficulty in making complaints" as reasons for dissatisfaction. As the express delivery industry develops, the issue of item insurance becomes increasingly prominent.

(4) Express delivery price. Compared to speed and safety, the concern for express delivery price is slightly lower, at 14%. Some netizens frankly stated that "speed and safety" are the primary considerations, and only under the premise of ensuring these two factors would the price of express delivery be considered as a comparison factor.

(5) Personalized value-added services. The demand for express value-added services is also increasing. Nearly 10% of netizens hope that express companies can provide better and more complete personalized services (such as online package inquiries).

### III. Expectations of Netizens for Express Companies

1. Keywords

What netizens expect most from express companies are: professional competence of employees, industry regulation, entry threshold, delivery range, informatization service, price transparency, sending and receiving speed, cargo safety, customer service attitude, online inquiry system, industry legislation, compensation system, and inspection before signing.

2. Expectation Levels of Netizens

We summarized 13 keywords of "netizen suggestions" and their proportions from the feedback comments, as shown in Figure 4.

#### Figure 4: Netizen Suggestions

From the chart, it can be seen that apart from the most focused factors mentioned earlier, netizens also have many suggestions regarding the professional competence of employees, informatization services, industry regulatory mechanisms, entry thresholds for express companies, price transparency, and even the service image of express personnel.

(1) Regulatory strength and establishment of compensation mechanisms. It is worth noting that many netizens have raised demands and suggestions regarding the regulatory strength of the industry and the establishment of loss and damage compensation mechanisms, and also made requests to the government. Netizens proposed: “The government should further regulate the express delivery industry, establish a certain supervisory mechanism to ensure the safe and timely delivery of goods,” “Industry associations should fulfill their responsibilities for industry monitoring, control malicious competition, and enhance social credibility,” “Government supervision needs to be in place, always without recourse, and express companies have many reasons to make you feel at fault,” “Raise the entry threshold for the express delivery industry,” etc.

(2) Informatization services. Currently, informatization networks are developing rapidly, and netizens are particularly concerned about informatization services. Good express query and tracking services are urgently needed by netizens. Most netizens suggest that the network query system synchronization needs further upgrades.

(3) Professional competence of employees and service image. Many netizens mentioned that they hope express companies can overall improve the professional competence of employees and have certain requirements for uniform clothing. We understand that companies like EMS and larger private express companies such as SF Express, Yunda, HHTT, Shentong, and ZTO Express perform well in terms of uniform clothing and unified service image.

(4) Price transparency. Price transparency is a term repeatedly mentioned by netizens. How to do well in price transparency is an area where express companies need to strive. It is understood that EMS and SF Express perform better in this regard. On the websites of EMS and SF Express, you can directly check the price between the two locations, with a higher level of transparency.
