Synopsis:

The story of the legendary outlaw Robin Hood, who became famous in England six hundred years ago, has been filmed countless times - from silent to sound, from black and white to color, from Errol Flynn to Kevin Costner. In terms of production value and entertainment, this film is considered top-notch, combining history, intrigue, action, and romance into a non-stop thrilling experience.

Shot in authentic locations, including ancient forests in England and medieval sites in both England and France, the movie also delves deeper into the personalities and traits of Robin Hood’s key companions, enriching the timeless legend of "Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves."

During the Crusades, an Englishman named Robin was imprisoned in the East. After five years of living in a dark and terrifying dungeon, he seizes an opportunity to kill a guard, freeing himself and two fellow prisoners. One of them dies en route, asking Robin to deliver his belongings to his sister, Maria. The other, a Moor named Azeem, follows Robin loyally to repay him for saving his life.

Upon returning to England, Robin is thrilled but soon gets into trouble when he kills one of Sheriff Nottingham’s men while rescuing a poor child being pursued by soldiers. This act earns him the wrath of the sheriff's cousin. Before Robin could return home, the cruel Sheriff had already executed Robin's father, Lord Locksley, under the accusation of devil worship. Unable to reclaim his family estate, Robin vows revenge. When he seeks refuge with Maria, a relative of King Richard, she initially views him as the same reckless youth he once was.

While evading the sheriff's men, Robin and Azeem stumble upon Sherwood Forest, where they encounter a group of impoverished bandits. Through his bravery and leadership, Robin wins their respect and becomes friends with them. As a wanted criminal by the sheriff, Robin renounces his noble status and begins organizing these disenfranchised farmers to fight against the tyrannical rule of the sheriff. Soon, Sherwood Forest becomes a nightmare for the rich and nobles, with the rebel peasants stealing countless treasures, including a large chest of money the sheriff intended to use to buy loyalty and seize power. The hungry peasants share in the spoils of victory and grow more devoted to their young leader, Robin Hood. One day, Maria accidentally stumbles upon the rebels in Sherwood Forest, learning from Robin about the sheriff's plot to usurp the throne. During this brief encounter, she sees a new side of Robin and begins to develop feelings for him.

Frustrated by repeated losses and unable to capture Robin, the sheriff seeks help from a witch. She advises him to hire barbarians to surround and annihilate the peasant army in Sherwood Forest and to kidnap Maria, forcing her into marriage to legitimize his claim to the throne.

The ruthless sheriff surrounds Sherwood Forest with fire, overwhelming the outnumbered peasant army. Many are captured, and back at the castle, the sheriff tortures the peasants while searching for Robin. He uses Robin's necklace to deceive the captured Maria, claiming Robin is dead, coercing her into agreeing to marry him. The wicked sheriff plans to execute the captured peasants at his wedding ceremony, which Robin and his remaining allies learn about and begin plotting a rescue.

Infiltrating the heavily guarded castle, Robin and his team launch a surprise attack during the execution, catching the enemy off guard. Maria, seeing that Robin is alive, fights desperately not to marry the sheriff. The crazed sheriff attempts to hastily perform the wedding ceremony and then rapes Maria. In a critical moment, Robin arrives, engaging in a fierce battle and ultimately killing the brutal sheriff, leading the common people to storm and capture the entire castle.

Robin Hood and his band of rebels return to Sherwood Forest. As a monk performs the wedding ceremony between Robin and Maria, King Richard unexpectedly arrives and acts as the officiant. Amid cheers from the crowd, he blesses the couple for eternal happiness...
