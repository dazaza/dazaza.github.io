Paris L'Oreal's 40th anniversary of the "I'm worth it" slogan

by juyaqufa003 on 2011-11-25 14:53:40

In the past forty years, this familiar slogan has given modern women positive and confident strength, inspiring them to be independent and keep up with the times. The L'Oréal Paris "Dream Team" spokespersons Fan Bingbing, Jane Fonda, Emmy Muirns, Inès de la Fressange, and Freida Pinto gathered in Paris to celebrate this extraordinary journey together with the guests present. The five words of "I'm Worth It" are closely related to the era in which they were born. In 1971, a social revolution was just beginning, and a new feminist spirit was about to emerge. A revolutionary hair dye called Preference from L'Oréal Paris was much more expensive than its main competitor, and how to make consumers accept this product became a big challenge. To promote the reasonableness of the product price and allow more consumers to accept it, the slogan "I'm Worth It" was born accordingly. For 40 years, this slogan has gone beyond being just an advertising line; it awakened modern women's awareness of their own value and gave them confidence and strength.

Cyril Chapuy, Global President of L'Oréal Paris, said: "This celebration event reviews the legendary history of this slogan. Nowadays, this slogan inspires more global women than ever before. It interprets beauty through self-respect and independence, which are at the core of our brand."

International Milestone of the Brand - The Evolution of "I'm Worth It"

In 1997, "I'm Worth It" became the international declaration of the L'Oréal Paris brand and was promoted in the cosmetics and skincare sectors during the brand's internationalization process. This slogan resonated with women worldwide—not only in Western countries but also influenced Asia, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East. In the advertisements of the 1990s and the early years of the 21st century, this slogan appeared in various forms using "you" and "we" as subjects, reflecting the brand's new communication methods with consumers and society. Now, this slogan has been translated into 40 languages, conveying L'Oréal Paris' diverse concept of beauty.

Bao Yan Yue, General Manager of L'Oréal Paris China, said: "L'Oréal Paris entered the market in 1997, and with the phrase 'You're Worth It,' it touched the hearts of millions of consumers and opened a window to international beauty. Over the past 15 years, this well-known slogan has led Chinese women to bravely pursue their dreams, face life positively, and fully realize their own value."

The endorsers of the L'Oréal Paris "Dream Team" all possess extraordinary personalities and interpret the unique essence of the brand with exceptional charm. From international film star Gong Li and rock superstar Gwen Stefani to supermodel Claudia Schiffer, fashion icon Laia Castellet, or French rising star Leila Bettach, each L'Oréal Paris endorser conveys the meaning of beauty in modern society and interprets their understanding of this slogan. Today, "You're Worth It" encourages women worldwide to display and feel beauty, symbolizing charm, strength, and confidence.

L'Oréal Paris has over 25 international image endorsers, including Gong Li, Fan Bingbing, Li Bingbing, Zhang Zilin, Wu彦祖, Pan Wei Bo, Beyoncé Knowles, Gwen Stefani, Claudia Schiffer, Jennifer Lopez, Laia Castellet, Letizia Coste, Inès de la Fressange, Mila Jovovich, Eva Longoria, Andie MacDowell, Emmy Muirns, Freida Pinto, and Rachel Weisz.
