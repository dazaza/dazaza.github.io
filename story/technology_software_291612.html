Chongqing Restaurants Replicate the 'Pirate Edition' of the State Banquet

According to reports (by reporter Chen Ke), less than a week after Obama's visit to China ended, his influence on domestic consumption trends has already begun to show. The four dishes and one soup he enjoyed at the Great Hall of the People during a banquet hosted by General Secretary Hu Jintao received much praise online and in the media. This meal is now highly favored by restaurants in Chongqing. Some restaurants have quickly replicated their own version of the state banquet, promoting it to corporate and government clients. Among these dishes, the green juice chicken bean curd soup has become particularly popular, with daily sales increasing by over 30%.

The Chongqing Version of "Four Dishes and One Soup" is Priced at 688 Yuan

Green juice chicken bean curd soup, Chinese-style steak, stir-fried bamboo shoots and asparagus, roasted Red Star grouper fish, plus a cold dish... Total price 688 yuan. Immediately after President Obama left China, the varieties of the state banquet he enjoyed at the Great Hall of the People were quickly bundled into special set meals by restaurants, entering the business dining market. In Chongqing, some restaurants even advertised with slogans like "Obama ate this dish, XXX also sells it," fully utilizing celebrity effects. Pirate versions of the state banquet set meals can be found in Sichuan cuisine restaurants such as Jiaquanju and Hezhiji. Yan Zhihong from Jiaquanju stated, "Apart from the roasted grouper fish which is relatively expensive, the other dishes are common everyday dishes for ordinary people," so the difficulty of promotion is not high. However, due to the scarcity of grouper fish, general customers need to reserve a day in advance.

Green Juice Chicken Bean Curd Soup Becomes Best Seller

Some restaurants are more cautious, only selecting elements with strong Chinese characteristics from these four dishes and one soup, such as green juice chicken bean curd soup and stir-fried bamboo shoots and asparagus for individual orders. At Tao Ran Ju's Chongqing Guild Hall and Tao Ran Guild Hall, the green juice chicken bean curd soup is sold at 68 yuan per serving or 18 yuan per person. Hezhiji, Qinghuayuan, and other restaurants also list it. Wang Hesheng, the owner of Hezhiji Sichuan Cuisine Tian Gong, said, "After the media exposed the 'four dishes and one soup,' the restaurant immediately recommended the existing green juice chicken bean curd as a specially promoted dish, selling over a hundred servings daily, with sales growth exceeding 30%, and a very high click rate."

Restaurants Seize the Opportunity to Promote High-End Customization

Using Obama's name for single dish advertising indeed cannot be underestimated, but how long the Obama craze around the pirate edition of the state banquet will last remains questionable. However, restaurants are quite open-minded about this. "Promoting the Obama set meal is more about taking advantage of Obama's popularity to compete in the high-end reception market," admitted Wang Hesheng, the owner of Hezhiji. Chen Xiaobin, the executive chef of Tao Ran Ju, who once customized menus for guests like Wu Pohsiung, said that Tao Ran Ju also seized this opportunity to launch a high-end customization of the "four dishes and one soup," moving beyond the 'Hu-Obama Meeting' menu.

He explained that although the dishes in this type of high-end reception customization are simple, they often require the selection of relatively refined ingredients, thus being slightly higher than mass consumption. The average consumption per person is approximately 200 yuan, with more emphasis on etiquette and formality. Chen Xiaobin revealed that in such high-end receptions, "satisfying hunger" should not be emphasized; therefore, after the banquet, it is usually more considerate to offer a bowl of noodles to everyone.

Fo Xiang Yuan

Wuhan Fo Xiang Yuan

Perhaps you might think I am a Buddhist believer because of the title "Fo Xiang Yuan," but actually, I'm not. For more than thirty years, I have always followed the principle of "entering every temple, but not worshiping any Buddha." But when I face Buddha, I am certainly devout, with respect in my heart. For this unknown world, all I can do is respect it. What we call "Buddha's Edges" also has its reasons:

My hometown is in Chizhou, Anhui Province, where one of the four great Buddhist sites, Jiuhua Mountain, belongs to the region. Naturally, Jiuhua Mountain became our childhood distant travel destination. I still clearly remember as a child, because I carried the Young Pioneer flag and the Youth League flag, I often stood outside the temple unwilling to go inside.

As I grew up, I learned that Jiuhua Mountain's incense offerings are prosperous because of the Bodhisattva Ksitigarbha's saying: "If hell is not empty, I will not become a Buddha." What kind of realm is this... And among numerous Buddhist temples and mountains, Jiuhua Mountain is unique for having many uncorrupted bodies. Ironically, those who achieve uncorrupted bodies are mostly low-ranking monks or nuns similar to servants, seemingly a satire.

Later, in order to learn English, I dealt with Christian missionaries in Haikou, seeing Western believers conflicting with each other and initiating various wars and massacres. Comparing the war history of the East and West, the East mostly fought for "a beauty," while the West had more wars among believers to maintain their faith.

Then, following Li Shutong's footsteps, I stayed in Hangzhou, known as the "Southeastern Buddhist Country." Over the years, I visited Lingyin Temple, the first among many temples, Qiuxue Nunnery, the first among many nunneries; Fah Jing Temple, originally the scripture hall of Lingyin Temple, now a place for nuns to study Buddhist scriptures, where I often went to listen to them chant sutras and talk about Buddhism when I was sick in 2003, standing there for two or three hours; further up is Fah Jing Temple, known as Zhongtianzhu, now a Buddhist academy; and finally, Shangtianzhu at the end of Tiantu Road; the Jing Temple under Nanping Mountain, where I often lingered between Zhang Cangshui and Zhang Taiyan's tombs, listening to the evening bell of Nanping and the sound of falling leaves; Jing Mountain is truly a place outside the world; Zhenji Temple on Wuyun Mountain top; and naturally, Hu Bao, where Li Shutong practiced fasting and became a monk...

Later, for business trips, I visited the Big Wild Goose Pagoda and Small Wild Goose Pagoda in Xi'an, Ta'er Temple in Xining, Shuangta Temple in Taiyuan, Guiyuan Temple in Wuhan, the Buddhist temple with a thousand hands and eyes near Cuilake in Kunming (I forgot the name), Shaolin Temple in Mount Song, Dajuesi and Badachu in Beijing, Qixia Temple and Changlong Temple in Nanjing...

It seems that a habit has formed: in each city, the relatively ancient and well-preserved places are naturally temples or nunneries, and these are naturally the places to find the old memories of the city.

Do I believe in Buddhism? I believe in the Fragrance of Buddhism! Although with age, I increasingly like these things. The Heart Sutra brought back by Sanzang, then sung by Chi Yu in a completely different way, is truly "beautiful because of this." When Tibetan brothers and fellow townspeople bring me stories and classics related to Tibetan Buddhism, the tolerance and generosity of Buddha, as well as the self-help teaching of Buddhism, are similar to how we modern people use personal charm to influence others, thereby establishing a personal brand.
