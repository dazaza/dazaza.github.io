Liu Chuanzhi: It's absolutely right not to let children into Legend

by anonymous on 2013-10-22 19:19:28

Nowadays, people are talking about the social responsibility of entrepreneurs. Overall, I think it is about leading by example, starting with oneself, and influencing more people through actions to impact the entire society, rather than mobilizing everyone to talk or shout slogans. Because talking and shouting slogans are the responsibilities of scholars and the media. Why study this? Because different perspectives lead to different conclusions. My perspective is from my own enterprise's point of view, doing one's own job well. Running a business, try to do your best, influence others. Lenovo has a relatively small "tonnage" compared to the vastness of the nation, so we can only do our best.

I believe there are a few important things within an enterprise: first, if rules are not established, they should be applied equally to all. Not just my children but also the children of executives can come in. Many old colleagues who started the company with me have children studying computers, and many have already joined the company. If someone makes a big mistake, will we consider face during the handling process? This is hard to say. If we do this, the company's rules will no longer be rules.

Secondly, the entry of a certain child may affect the enthusiasm of others. For example, after my son comes in, his performance might make others think whether there are other intentions for the future. If I strictly require my son or treat him fairly, he might feel that I am too harsh on him, while others might think I actually favor my son because people always tend to feel that their efforts are not adequately rewarded, which is a human habit.

Why did Lenovo diversify?

Lenovo accomplished something foreigners thought was impossible—acquiring IBM's PC business—and we succeeded, which was very meaningful. If at that time, we couldn't compete with foreign companies in China, China's informatization would have been delayed. This was very evident then; when developed countries were selling 486 machines, China was still using 386. When we had our own enterprises competing with them, they immediately introduced newer machines. We stood ahead, and the overall price of machines came down.

The computer industry is actually a high-risk, high-tech industry. From the shareholder's standpoint, new industries need to be opened up to stabilize steps. The acquisition of IBM was successful, but if it failed, the company would have disappeared. However, without the acquisition, Lenovo would have become a very mediocre company. The current Lenovo Group's computer business revenue is approximately $34 billion, and before the acquisition, it was $2.99 billion. But if there were another business segment, such as investment businesses or real estate businesses, it would make the person in charge of the computer business more willing to move forward boldly and take risks because shareholders wouldn't constantly hold them back. Therefore, diversification is necessary for companies like ours.

Lenovo Holding's main goal is to list its own industries. In Lenovo Holding's vision, it hopes to have leading enterprises in multiple industries. Currently, Lenovo Group has successfully acquired companies in Brazil, Japan (NEC), Germany, etc. However, Lenovo Holding also hopes to have leading enterprises in agriculture, consumer goods, and other industries.

To become a century-old store, you must have a cornerstone.

To become leading enterprises in multiple industries and aim to be a century-old store is not easy. For instance, Nokia, such a massive entity, collapsed suddenly. If we have leading enterprises in multiple industries, we will have room to adjust, advancing towards becoming a century-old store.

Our ultimate goal is to hope that Lenovo becomes a century-old store without being a family-owned enterprise. Because work that truly lasts has deep meaning—it’s not just one generation's effort but requires several generations' continuous efforts. What kind of company is easiest to achieve this? A family business, treating it as a family cause. As for companies like ours, which are not family businesses, we want to make it like a family business with a sense of mission—a family business without a family. This mainly relies on mechanisms and culture to form, and I'm not sure if it can be done.

To become a long-term century-old store, the management team should have shares and incentive mechanisms, but these alone are not enough. Several American companies, including HP, suffered from this—completely lacking major shareholders, letting them drift. Therefore, there must be a cornerstone. Lenovo happens to have this.

After Lenovo Holding achieves overall listing, the shares of old entrepreneurs will become the cornerstone of the company, making it easier for Lenovo to build a century-old store.

The shares of old entrepreneurs cannot be sold; they are the cornerstone of the company. However, there will be a 35% dividend each year. Among the dividends, 11% of the shares originally belonging to employees are suggested to be transferred to others. Following Panhai's purchase price of the Academy of Sciences, 11% will be purchased, thus allowing 20% of the shares to go to Lenovo's management and employees. This company will then become a company with owners, and unlike other listed companies, it will have a cornerstone.

What is the cornerstone? Suppose the company grows larger, and later both the Academy of Sciences and Panhai sell their shares, then public shares will increase significantly, similar to HP. After public shares increase, what situation arises? There is no true large shareholder, making it easy to drift. For example, small shareholders, from a short-term perspective, who really takes responsibility for the company? These managers want to be owners, but their shareholding ratio is low. Some shares are permanently unsold, owned by descendants of the original entrepreneurs, etc., turning these into the cornerstone. With this shareholding ratio, the company can stabilize.

Many entrepreneurs and some groups in society hope that China's commercial society can become more standardized and gain status in the world. Most entrepreneurs also hope for social stability and economic development, which is our common wish. However, for entrepreneurs themselves, it's probably not about shouting or writing profound articles and comments. The most important thing is to do one's own job well and treat one's employees kindly.

I believe that businessmen should have an important status in China. In the past 30 years of reform and opening up, entrepreneurs or businessmen have indeed represented the "three represents." The first represent is the requirement for the development of advanced productive forces in China, which we have indeed achieved. This should be the status of society. However, in terms of social status, sometimes we feel honored, sometimes we doubt whether we have this status. Entrepreneurs first hope to gain social recognition, especially acknowledgment from the Party and government, which is guaranteed and completed by legal means.

To succeed in entrepreneurship, talk less about grievances.

For young entrepreneurs today, is it harder to start a business now or during your time? What’s your feeling? I think many of the difficulties young people encounter in entrepreneurship today stem from the perception that the playing field isn’t level, giving a sense of unfairness. However, in the first thirty years, you weren’t even allowed to start a business, and any attempt would be quickly shut down. Which situation is better? Clearly, the current situation is better than before.

Starting a business involves a lot of uncertain factors. The only certainty is that starting a business is extremely difficult. He suggests that if you really want to start a business, you need to think carefully and be prepared to endure hardships. For example, if the capital used for starting a business comes from parents' pensions, which are hard-earned, the first attempt at entrepreneurship is unlikely to succeed immediately. Lenovo Holdings has a specialized investment company that invests in growing enterprises, all of which have gone through ten to eight years of ups and downs, with the first two years being particularly challenging. Once conditions improve, such as having previous work experience, gaining more experience, and accumulating some money, falling and returning to work won't have a significant impact.

Before starting, set clear goals and remain steadfast. Don't dwell on feelings of injustice. Entrepreneurship is a personal choice, and maintaining this mindset will naturally guide you toward this path.

For entrepreneurs, the road is definitely tough. If you really want to start a business, think clearly and remain resolute. Being resolute doesn't guarantee success, but hesitation will surely prevent it.

In the company, he is a subordinate, so these topics can't be discussed. My daughter works in investment, and under confidentiality, we also exchange methodological ideas, studying investment issues and how to evaluate companies, which are frequent topics of discussion at home.
