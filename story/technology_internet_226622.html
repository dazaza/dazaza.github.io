Are you referring to gout? Or is it rheumatic arthritis? If it's the former, here’s an article for your reference:

**Gout**

Gout, gout—pain comes and goes suddenly, like the wind. Hence, it is named "gout." Gout is an ancient disease, often described as a "wine and meat disease," "a rich man's disease," or even "an emperor's disease." The more privileged one's living conditions are, the more likely they are to suffer from gout. Throughout history, many leaders and celebrities around the world have endured the torment of gout, including Alexander the Great, King Louis VII and XIV of France, U.S. President Benjamin Franklin, Emperor Kublai Khan of China, and renowned scientists Isaac Newton and William Harvey. Moreover, men are more prone to this condition than women. Why does gout favor these people?

**[Cause]**

The cause of gout is already well understood: due to the imbalance in purine metabolism, its metabolic product—uric acid—increases in concentration within the body, or the kidneys fail to excrete uric acid in time. Excess uric acid easily forms sodium urate crystals, which deposit in joints and nearby soft tissues, causing redness, swelling, and pain in the joints, especially in the fingers, toes, wrists, ankles, and knees.

Various meats, animal organs, shrimp, shellfish, and other seafood contain high levels of purine. Alcohol consumption is believed to disrupt purine metabolism; thus, those who continuously indulge in banquets with alcohol and meat have a 30% higher chance of developing gout. Additionally, medical research has found that for alcohol consumers, drinking beer poses a greater risk of developing gout compared to hard liquor, while moderate wine consumption does not increase the risk.

Clinical data also shows that obesity, diabetes, atherosclerosis, coronary heart disease, and primary hypertension often coexist with gout.

**[Symptoms]**

Most gout patients are over 40 years old, with 95% being male. Some patients may have elevated uric acid levels in their blood but never experience any symptoms throughout their lives, while others develop gout as they age.

Acute gout attacks occur rapidly, like sudden lightning. Patients often wake up at midnight due to pain. Everything seems normal before bedtime, but during the night or early morning, they are awakened by excruciating pain in their big toe, as if cut by a knife. The pain gradually worsens until it becomes unbearable. The affected toe becomes red, swollen, warm, and painful.

At the beginning, the affected joint is usually a single joint, asymmetrically. The most commonly affected area is the big toe joint, followed by the top of the foot, ankle, knee, fingers, wrist, and elbow. Some patients may experience very mild symptoms during the acute phase, such as "bumping the big toe" or "spraining the ankle," which often go unnoticed.
