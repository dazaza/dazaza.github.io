Pre-treatment precautions for kidney stone treatment at Guangzhou Renai Hospital

by miaok132 on 2011-05-27 09:16:50

What are the precautions before kidney stone treatment? Let's consult the experts at Guangzhou Ren'ai Hospital.

Experts at Guangzhou Ren'ai Hospital said:

Patients with kidney stones should limit excessive nutrition according to their caloric needs, keeping the daily intake of protein to 75-90g to maintain energy balance and reduce the risk of urinary stones. For patients with a family history of hyperuricemia or gout, the intake of protein should be limited to 1g per kg of body weight. The intake of refined sugar should be controlled. Foods such as spinach and animal organs should be avoided.

Drink plenty of water. Experts from Guangzhou Ren'ai Hospital explained that water has a diuretic effect, and drinking large amounts of water can increase urine output, dilute the concentration of solid salts in the urine, prevent further deposition of urinary salts on the stones, and prevent the stones from growing larger; at the same time, the secretion of large amounts of urine stimulates the smooth muscle peristalsis of the renal calyx, renal pelvis, and ureter wall, promoting the excretion of stones. Clinical research data shows that if patients with urinary stones drink (tea) enough to produce more than 2500 milliliters of urine per day, it can significantly improve the efficacy of stone expulsion.

Experts from Guangzhou Ren'ai Hospital explained that for phosphate stones, taking 1.5-2.0 grams of oral orthophosphate per day can inhibit intestinal calcium absorption, reduce urinary calcium concentration, dissolve calcium in already formed stones, and make kidney stones smaller and easier to expel.

Experts from Guangzhou Ren'ai Hospital explained that when kidney stones cause renal colic, using 600 mg of crystalline hydrochloride procaine, adding it to 500 ml of 5% glucose saline, and administering intravenous drip injections, along with intramuscular injections of atropine or Demerol, have good analgesic and diuretic effects; while undergoing the above treatment, antipyretic drugs for urinary tract infections should also be added until three days after the pain disappears to prevent secondary renal infection; after each episode, an ultrasound review should be conducted to observe changes in stone position, whether there is obstruction, and whether there is hydronephrosis, so as to decide whether a change in treatment plan is needed based on new situations.
