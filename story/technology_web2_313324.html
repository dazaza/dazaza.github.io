Manchester United's flying wing is more stylish than 'Showboat' and has become the Devil's second-in-command; Real Madrid's 20 million can hardly buy him. Since entering November, a new firepower point for Manchester United has begun to emerge - it is Antonio Valencia. The Ecuadorian has scored 3 goals in 6 league matches, averaging 1 goal every 2 games. The dilemma that once plagued him of only providing assists without scoring goals has now been resolved. Currently, he has played in 16 league matches for Manchester United, scoring 4 goals. Although 1 goal every 4 games may not seem like much, considering his recent performance, Valencia has become Manchester United's second biggest attacking threat after Rooney.

It is somewhat embarrassing to note that despite having 14 players score goals in this season's league and cup competitions, Rooney's 13 goals stand far above the rest. The second highest scorer, Owen, has only scored 7 goals, and only two of those were in important league matches. Berbatov's four league goals were spread out over a longer period. Only Valencia, like a swift blade cutting through chaos, has consistently reaped rewards recently, and each goal has been more beautiful than the last. Although he ranks second on the team's league goalscorer list alongside Berbatov, fans clearly recognize Valencia's recent form as far superior to Berbatov's.

Today's goal thoroughly embodies the principle upheld by strong teams in counterattacks: maintaining high speed and efficiency while emphasizing the combination of individual creativity and opportunity-grasping ability. Any team can launch quick attacks, but if they only pursue speed, with inaccurate passes in between and off-target final passes leading to confusion at the end, any miscalculation in any one link will render scoring points through quick attacks nothing more than empty talk. In this goal, Scholes' chip pass was perfectly executed; Berbatov's overhead kick after juggling the ball combined technique and imagination flawlessly; finally, Berbatov's right-footed volley after adjusting his center of gravity completed the goal. Such classic finishing touches after brilliant team combinations, often seen when Sheringham and Solskjaer played as forwards, have been completely replicated onto today's Valencia.

Ferguson praised Valencia lavishly after the match: "This finishing touch was very spectacular. Antonio can increase his goal tally significantly because we all know during training that he is actually an excellent marksman. Today's goal is a great example. Antonio's progress has been tremendous; he performs particularly expansively in matches and interprets his role very well. He keeps improving himself, and we are very satisfied." What does 'expansive' mean? It refers to Valencia's style of play; his range of activity in the midfield and forward positions has expanded, allowing him to move and switch positions with ease, unlike when he first joined the team and was more of a dedicated right winger. Multiple one-touch passes throughout the match had a high accuracy rate. One-touch passing requires a high level of默契 (teamwork) between the player and teammates running along other routes while maintaining overall high-speed advancement. Valencia's willingness to execute such plays indicates that Valencia has now fully integrated into Manchester United. In that case, the future number of goals and assists from this 17 million star should, as Ferguson emphasized, increase substantially.

Manchester United should feel fortunate to have secured Valencia. This summer, Bayern Munich offered 18 million pounds and Real Madrid went up to 20 million, prices slightly higher than Manchester United's. Fortunately, Wigan Athletic didn't care about these few million pound differences and emphasized that Valencia's destination would depend solely on himself. Valencia, who was already accustomed to playing in the Premier League, ultimately chose Manchester United. Looking back after half a year, this deal has started to yield significant returns for Manchester United. Although Valencia hasn't yet reached the destructive power of Cristiano Ronaldo, his record of 4 goals and 3 assists in 16 games, especially his heroic performances since entering November, make people believe that despite his somewhat fixed position, the 24-year-old Valencia is advancing towards the great goal of becoming an absolute right-wing mainstay spanning an era, leaving an important chapter in Manchester United's glorious history.
