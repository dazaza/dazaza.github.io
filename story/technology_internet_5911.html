Angry youth or ignorant people

by interjc7 on 2006-09-08 22:01:12

http://interjc.net/2006/09/blog-post_08.html

Today, I read an article by KESO. KESO said:

The common people were originally not violent, and the youth were originally not angry, but it's hard to resist sensationalist media. After being misled by the media, the common people become "rioters," and the youth become "angry youth." This is especially evident on the Internet; whenever themes involving nationalism or moral judgment are involved, they often easily develop into "Internet mob" incidents.

I believe that people's understanding of "angry youth" is unfair, even Wikipedia's answer seems a bit biased. The prosperity of a country or a nation needs "angry youth." From Lu Xun to Chen Danqing, and even KESO himself in the IT field, they are all paragons of "angry youth." Of course, this kind of "angry youth" is not the same as that kind. In my opinion, true "angry youth" have discernment, a sense of justice, dare to face the truth, and can propose practical solutions. As for those people we consider "angry youth"? I think they can be called ignorant masses. They belong to the ignorant masses but cannot represent them. They are an angry branch of the ignorant masses. This is somewhat like the concept of hackers; many people think hackers are those who steal QQ numbers or World of Warcraft passwords, but these people are actually called crackers.

True "angry youth" look at the world with a cold eye, objectively analyze social ills, and propose solutions when appropriate. They detest the various ugliness in reality so much that they are labeled with the word "angry."

And those who issue online wanted notices, every day...
