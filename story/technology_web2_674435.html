Liposuction for weight loss is a very effective body contouring procedure. For individuals who have fat accumulation in certain areas of the body but have been unable to effectively lose weight, this is the best way to lose weight. However, many people seeking liposuction often fall into common misconceptions about the procedure. So, what should one be aware of when considering liposuction for weight loss? Experts from Beauty168.com will now explain.

1. Liposuction is for reshaping the body, not necessarily for reducing weight. Some patients question their doctor after surgery because their weight hasn’t changed. What they don’t realize is that fat cells are like sponges and are very light — as the saying goes, oil is lighter than water. Removing 1-2 liters of fat locally doesn't significantly affect overall weight. If it’s full-body liposuction, the quantity removed is larger, and there may be a noticeable drop in weight.

2. Pay attention to diet and exercise to prevent rebounding. Although liposuction reduces the number of fat cells, making it less likely to rebound compared to general weight loss methods, fat cells are the most elastic cells in the human body, with the ability to expand and shrink by up to 9000 times. Therefore, post-surgery attention to diet and increased exercise is essential.

3. Wear compression garments well. After liposuction, pressure bandaging is required to stop bleeding. Then, you'll need to wear an elastic compression garment prepared specifically for you. Its purpose is not only to continue stopping any residual bleeding but also to fix the remaining fat in place and help the skin contract with elasticity. After 3–6 months, your beautiful curves will be basically set.

4. Be mindful of temporary skin numbness, which usually recovers within 3–5 weeks. Liposuction causes trauma to fat tissues, and during the healing process, fibrous tissue forms, similar to scabbing, which can feel a bit hard but will soften over time.

5. Avoid sun exposure to prevent pigmentation and ensure good skin appearance. Those who had liposuction on their face, arms, or legs should especially be careful and protect themselves from the sun for 4–6 weeks.

6. The amount of fat removed in one session shouldn’t be excessive, nor should too many areas be targeted at once. Experts warn against those who seek full-body liposuction in one go due to impatience or fear of secondary surgery. This approach increases risks and is detrimental to health.

7. For autologous fat transfer to the breast or buttocks, only negative-pressure liposuction can achieve this. Experts remind that liposuction techniques using ultrasound, electronic, energy, resonance, or focused energy technologies dissolve fat cells into fragments, destroying their vitality and rendering them unsuitable for breast or buttock augmentation. Only negative-pressure liposuction can extract intact fat cells, though it carries higher risks and greater trauma, making it suitable only for highly experienced doctors performing it cautiously.

8. Early activity and physical therapy can enhance recovery.
