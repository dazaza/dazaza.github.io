Elementalists

Chpt. 1

"It's done. My masterwork is now complete." The doctor stepped back from the babies lying in front of him.

"How long before we know if it worked?"

"It will take a couple of years before we know for sure. If it works, we can isolate the gene at an older state and still be able to sell it to the highest bidder. Armies would crawl over each other to have super-powered soldiers. I want all these babies dispersed among the streets. Only the strong will survive; those will be the ones I take the gene from."

"What about the parents? They are going to wonder what happened to their kids."

"Kill them." The doctor stared impassively at his aide. "I really don’t care how it’s done, just do it and do it quickly." With that, the doctor walked from the lab room, leaving his aide to deal with the dirty work.

Skie sat up quickly, sweat glistening on his brow. His eyes adjusted to the light quickly enough. It helped that the sun was now setting. He brushed his hand through his brown hair. It fell quickly into its place. Jumping from his bed, he headed up the stairs to the rooftop. The sun was setting over the horizon, melting into the mountain side.

"Hey, you up there," A voice came from far below.

"Hey Ember, how are you?"

"I’m just fine, how about you? What are you doing up on the roof?"

"Just relaxing, why don’t you come join me?"

"Alright, I’ll be there in a sec." Ember quickly ran into the house and up the stairs. He appeared in the doorway moments later. "I got us some food Skie." Ember threw a cold pop to his friend while he opened one for himself. Together they sat on the roof, watching the stars appear one by one.

"This sure is the life, don’t you agree Ember."

"I couldn’t think of a better one." Ember’s mind began to drift as he recalled his youth. With his parents dead long before he could remember, Ember had made a life for himself on the streets. He had had to steal to survive, but only stole as much as he needed. It was among those years that he had met Skie. Skie had lived almost the same life as he had, except maybe worse. Skie had grown up in an especially bad area. Not many survived from there. It had happened when both of them had tried to steal the same piece of food at the same time. They had fought over the piece for some time, till neither had the strength to move one muscle. The piece of food lying just at their feet. Just then, a black cat happened to stroll by the two, and picked up the piece of food and ran off with it. They could do nothing but watch helplessly as their dinner ran off into the shadows. Later, they both laughed over the incident, and soon began to help each other. They became terrors of the street, the infamous night duo that could never be caught.

"Do you feel that?" Ember said as he quickly sat up, his eyes peering into the darkness.

Skie remained on his back, never taking his eyes off the stars. "Ya, I feel it too. Although I doubt it’s 'him'."

"Still, don’t you think we should check it out?"

"Fine, if you really think so." Skie sat up and finished off his pop before setting it down. "This better not ruin my night, I mean it started off so well."

Building after building crumbled to the ground. The earth beneath it tore apart, swallowing the buildings until nothing but a smooth clearing remained. The cloud of dust and debris skirted around Skie and Ember, blocked by Skie’s shield. "Who could have done something like this?" Ember said as he stared around the now vacant lot.

"Him." Skie pointed toward a boy standing on the edge of the clearing. The boy, Rocky, just smiled a small smile.

"So there are others like me, how interesting. Well let’s just have a little fun shall we?" Before either boy could blink, a mountain of gravel collapsed on top of them both. "Is that all you’ve got? How pathetic."

The gravel slowly began to move, revealing an invisible wall of air. "Oh come now. You didn’t think it would be that easy did you?" Skie moved the gravel away from them before he began twirling his hands. They spun faster and faster before a long swirling spike began to form. Skie drew out the spike until it came to a point. Holding the end like a sword, he began to charge the rock-wielding foe.

Rocky just laughed as Skie charged in on him. Slamming his fist into the ground, he began to pull a sword of his own. Before Skie could strike the first blow, Rocky deflected it with his sword. Skie calmed himself down as he studied his opponent. He reminded himself that hastily rushing against an unknown foe would just get him and Ember killed. As he circled Rocky, he studied his movements, waiting for his opening. Finding it, he lunged, a solid wall shot up before his sword, deflecting the blow. Skie felt a sharp pain in his side. The sight of blood made his head spin. While his balance was off, Rocky took the advantage and struck a piercing blow to Skie. Another blow came in from above, but this time Skie was ready. The air condensed around the sword, causing the sword to fell like it was cutting through a diamond. While his opponent was focused on his sword, Skie returned the favor with a quick thrust through his opponent’s side. The wind ripped through the flesh harder than any steel sword ever could.

Rocky screamed in rage, causing a wave of rock to plow into Skie, sending him through the nearest building. Rocky just laughed as Skie lay there, close to unconsciousness. A sudden flame erupted next to Rocky. Ember stood, pushing the remaining debris off his leg. "You hadn’t forgotten about me had you?" Ember held no restraint as he erupted forward, sending wave after wave of heat balls at Rocky. Rocky simply sliced through each one. Ember decided to switch his tactics. He sent one more ball and quickly followed after it. As Rocky sliced through the last ball, he was met by a pair of flaming fists which struck left and right across his face.

Rocky squatted and held onto the ground to keep from falling over. A small chuckle began to form that grew into a roaring laugh. "You really think you can defeat me? I’m Rocky, the strongest fighter in the world." He brushed away the blood from his lip and spit the rest on the ground. Rocky stood and closed his eyes, focusing all of his strength. Two giant rock hands grew from out of the ground. Rocky took his fighting pose once again and the giant rock followed suit. The hands hit left and right, never seeming to slow as they ripped apart Ember’s body. With a quick uppercut and forward strike, Ember flew across the empty lot, skidding to a stop face down in the dirt.

Ember slowly began to rise, coughing up blood as he went. Everything in his sight had gone blurry and red as he stared at the boy striding toward him. Ember bit his lip just to keep focus. He needed to fight. He needed to win. He wasn’t going to die at the hands of some punk like him. The heat grew from within him and flowed down to his fingertips. He added everything he had into one last fireball, the biggest he had ever made. As the edges of unconsciousness crept into his brain, he let go of all he had, and the darkness engulfed him.

Rocky’s eyes bulged at the sight of this new fireball, but it mattered little to him. He had destroyed the others and would destroy this one just the same. He raised his sword, preparing to strike it down when he noticed a figure off to his side. Skie’s hand was stretched out in front of him, yet it wasn’t aiming at Rocky, but at the fireball instead. All Skie could say before he toppled over was one word. "Boom."

The air inside the fireball increased tenfold, causing the fireball to explode inches before Rocky had a chance to strike it down. The ensuing explosion caused even Rocky’s desperate attempt at a defensive wall to crumble. Rocky rolled across the ground before slamming into a lamppost twenty feet away. Luckily for him, the blast had caused him to lose consciousness long before he had reached the pole.

Sirens were the first sound that Skie heard as he clung to consciousness. He rubbed the blur from his eyes and began to look around. It was dark everywhere except for the police lights off in the distance. Skie tried to stand but found his legs too rubbery to hold his weight. He sat a moment longer before trying again.

"You really must become stronger if you’re going to survive." A voice came from the shadows next to Skie. A dark-haired figure stepped out, yet his face remained hidden behind a long hood. "This victory was nothing but a fluke, a stroke of luck. And if you ever want to survive in the ensuing battles, you’ll have to be stronger."

"Who ever said we were going to fight any more battles. We didn’t want to fight in this one!"

"I’m sorry, but you really have no choice in the matter. More battles will come, so you better be prepared." With his final warning, the hooded person stepped back into the shadows and disappeared.

"Wait, who are you?" Skie scanned the shadows but could find no sign of the hooded stranger. Skie mulled over what the man had said before the sirens interrupted his thoughts. He quickly stood up and limped over to Ember. His friend was unconscious but still breathing. Skie lifted his friend, and positioned him as best he could. They wobbled off into the alleyways as the cops arrived on the scene.

Ember awoke with a haze still clouding his head. "What happened?"

"We won. That final blast of yours defeated him Ember. Good job."

Skie smiled, yet never took his eyes off the colors in the distance. "I wonder how many there are?"

He turned to look at Ember, his troubled nature shown across his face. "I mean we just saw another like us. So how many do you think there could be?"

"Not too many I suppose, otherwise they would be all over the news."

"So then why were we chosen to have these powers? Who gave them to us? I just don’t know." Skie forced a weak smile for his friend. "I’m sorry; I guess I just have a lot of questions."

"It’s okay, Skie. I was thinking the same thing, but even if we do know, it won’t really help us much anyway. What does it matter if there are only ten or one hundred? It doesn’t change the fact that we were given powers. The important question is, are you going to use your powers to help or destroy?"

Skie never answered, he just sat and watched the lights.
