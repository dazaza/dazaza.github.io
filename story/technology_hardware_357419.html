In the first five months of this year, the Shanghai Composite Index fell by more than 20%, and among the 350 equity-oriented funds, 54 saw their net asset value (NAV) drop by over 20%. Only 12 achieved positive returns. What are the winning strategies for these standout funds amid the crash?

Notably, some funds that had mediocre performance earlier in the year emerged strongly during the sharp declines in April and May, demonstrating the saying "heroes emerge in chaotic times." For instance, Dongwu Value Growth, Dongwu Industry Rotation (580003), and Dongwu Progressive Strategy saw NAV growth rates of 4.02%, 3.53%, and 1.17% respectively in the first five months. This growth was primarily driven by their operations in April and May. Additionally, CICC Shengshi Blue Chip (550003), CICC Four Seasons Red (550001), and TEDA Macroeconomic Efficiency Selection have also achieved positive returns this year, thanks to their strong performance against the market trend in April and May.

Operational Secrets: Position Control and Structural Adjustment

The most typical example of a significant reduction in position is Jiasih Theme Selection, which had already reduced its stock position to 60% by the end of the first quarter. This downward trend continued in the following two months.

Jiasih Theme Selection (070010) and Huashang Shengshi Growth maintained their strength, with NAV increases of 11.03% and 7.58% respectively in the first five months, ranking first and second. Remarkably, during the severe declines in April and May, Huashang Shengshi Growth's NAV increased by 4.43% over these two months, achieving the highest NAV growth rate among all equity-oriented funds.

The key to success for these funds lies mainly in appropriate industry allocation. Some funds reacted quickly in terms of industry allocation and stock selection, with Jiasih Theme Selection (070010) and Huashang Shengshi Growth being the most prominent.

Similarly outstanding funds like Morgan Stanley Leading Advantage and ChinaAMC Strategy Selection (002031) achieved positive returns in the first five months, with NAV growth rates of 3.39% and 0.89% respectively. However, they were severely affected in April and May, with Morgan Stanley Leading Advantage seeing a NAV drop of 5.52% over these two months, and ChinaAMC Strategy Selection (002031) falling by 6.99%.

A fund manager in Shanghai told reporters that compared to the end of the first quarter, there hasn't been much change in stock positions. The ability to withstand the sharp decline is mainly due to structural advantages, having adjusted structures in the first quarter to favor consumer goods and pharmaceuticals.

12 Funds Achieve Positive Returns

Statistics show that as of May 31, the average NAV growth rate of 350 equity-oriented funds (including stock funds and equity-biased hybrid funds) was -12.72%, with only 12 achieving positive returns. Index funds performed poorly overall, and among the 54 funds that fell by over 20% in the first five months, passive index funds were predominant.

In contrast, maintaining high-position operations makes operational challenges greater. CICC Shengshi Blue Chip and CICC Four Seasons Red had stock positions exceeding 80% at the end of the first quarter. Dongwu Value Growth and Dongwu Industry Rotation had stock positions of 78.57% and 89.89% respectively at the end of the first quarter, both showing an increase from the end of last year.
