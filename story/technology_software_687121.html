How to be a good real estate agent

by nbkfpnz8m11 on 2012-03-02 13:47:31

If you want to be a qualified real estate broker, you not only need to be proficient in real estate professional knowledge, but also need to be familiar with legal knowledge, financial common sense, and negotiation skills. Only then can you build a bridge between real estate developers and consumers, buyers and sellers, and stand invincible in the intermediary industry.

Real estate professional knowledge (including real estate information). Real estate brokerage refers to providing real estate information and intermediary agency services for clients. Simply put, it means being a middleman. Intermediary connects two parties: the upper party and the lower party, and plays a role of communication, promotion, and stability between them. For a real estate broker, mastering a large amount of market information is a prerequisite for doing this business well. This information should include: laws, regulations, and related policies regarding real estate, changes in purchasing objects, etc. To a large extent, brokers make profits by using information, which is their most important capital. If intermediaries focus on information, then agents must possess professional knowledge. Agency represents one party to find another, acting as a full-process agent for transactions and leasing, covering all aspects of real estate production. Therefore, brokers are required to be familiar with real estate development and operation, architecture, real estate transactions, real estate price appraisal, and other professional knowledge based on relevant real estate laws, regulations, and ordinances. Specifically, one needs to master real estate development investment, market research, development procedures, project feasibility studies, engineering construction and completion acceptance, capital financing, market promotion, etc. Secondly, one needs to be familiar with basic construction knowledge and understand the entire construction process. Thirdly, one needs to be familiar with the conditions, procedures, and specific regulations for transfer, mortgage, and leasing, as well as some basic knowledge about property rights. Fourthly, one needs to master the theory and practice of real estate price appraisal.

Marketing knowledge. The development of real estate today is no longer a simple product competition like it was a few years ago. For real estate developers, what they need is not just simple intermediary services, but intermediary services that can provide comprehensive marketing plans and sophisticated market planning solutions. The characteristic of separation between production and consumption in the real estate market determines the importance of real estate marketing. The separation of time, quantity, type, house type, floor ownership, information, and valuation in real estate requires marketing to fill the gap. Real estate marketing includes theories, market prediction and research methods, even product strategies, pricing strategies, distribution and promotion strategies, all of which are indispensable parts of the core of real estate marketing. To master marketing knowledge, one must grasp skills such as market research, target market selection, product development, product pricing, channel selection, product promotion, after-sales service, etc.

Legal knowledge. Although real estate brokers are not lawyers and do not necessarily have to reach a professional level, they must know the laws and regulations related to real estate. The development of China's real estate industry benefits from the adjustment and improvement of real estate laws and regulations. That is to say, every business in real estate cannot be separated from law and specific regulations. The three laws of real estate, namely the Urban Real Estate Management Law, the Land Management Law, and the Urban Planning Law, as well as the rules and local regulations formulated accordingly, almost cover all real estate affairs. As brokers, not only do they need to master the laws, regulations, and policies of various levels of government regarding real estate, but they also need to be familiar with civil law, economic law, administrative law, administrative litigation law, and civil litigation law. Take the simplest example: signing a contract involves economic contract law, general principles of civil law, and other legal knowledge. For lease agency, one needs to be familiar with the "Urban House Lease Management Measures" and the "Urban Private House Management Conditions," understanding which houses can be rented, which houses cannot be rented, and how lease contracts take effect. For sales agency, one needs to be familiar with the "Urban House Transfer Management Regulations" and the "Urban Property Registration Management Measures," including conditions for transfer, transaction procedures, property rights knowledge, and registration procedures. If representing developers, one needs to master even more legal knowledge. In recent years, the popular real estate lawyer certification business sufficiently illustrates the importance of legal knowledge. Real estate brokers need to learn the law, understand the law, use the law, handle affairs according to the law, and protect the legitimate rights and interests of their clients with the law.

Financial knowledge. As an important part of the financial industry, real estate finance is closely linked to real estate development, operation, and management. It is the product of the marriage between real estate and finance. The development of the real estate industry requires the backing of the financial industry, and the financial industry needs to expand, with the real estate industry being the top choice. Especially after the implementation of the new housing reform system and the abolition of physical housing allocation, residents buy houses in the market, making financial knowledge particularly important for brokers. Whether it is full-process agency for developers or mortgage loans, even real estate consulting services, financial knowledge is indispensable. Generally speaking, brokers should be familiar with international financial conventions, current national financial policies, financing forms, bank interest rates, cost calculations, loan types and procedures, repayment methods, and housing provident funds.

Negotiation skills. Negotiation is a skill, an art, and a science. It achieves mutual satisfaction through communication of views and emotions between negotiating parties. Real estate brokers' negotiations are no exception. Through negotiations, they accomplish the tasks assigned by the client, obtain commissions while satisfying market demands. Common negotiation strategies such as opportunity utilization strategy, benefit compromise strategy, and winning by sincerity strategy are all essential for real estate brokers to refer to. Specifically, for intermediary or agency businesses, at least three or more strategies—upper, middle, and lower strategies—should be proposed. For real estate brokers, there is also a very practical issue: how to close deals for both parties. This requires brokers to "strike when the iron is hot" and employ some wisdom, techniques, and methods to prompt the other party to make decisions early. However, fraudulent means should not be used.

Real estate brokers appear glamorous on the surface but are actually a hardworking profession. Only those who personally engage in this career can truly understand its nuances. Working in this industry is extremely arduous, with long hours and almost 24/7 readiness, followed by incessant phone calls to others. This is a job that best demonstrates personal ability, charm, and value. Because each transaction involves two completely different clients (the homeowner and the buyer), every deal is a process of outsmarting many people. Each day is exciting. Almost every seasoned broker has a belly full of grievances. They often complain, "It's not that we don't want to be transparent; it's just that everyone lacks trust." Those in the intermediary industry admire Hong Kong's model, where every property transaction is clear and the responsibilities of lawyers are defined, thereby increasing trust in brokers. In general, this society is very fair. The lack of integrity in the industry leads to a lack of trust in practitioners, so only by continuously becoming more "transparent" is it possible to gradually build trust. Who doesn't want to perform well? Who doesn't want to smoothly close deals every month and gain good commission returns? But in reality, the sales process involves more setbacks, lost deals, and unrewarded efforts. Returning home every day with a weary body and mind, only to face greater performance pressure the next day. The average closing cycle for a broker is 20 days to a month, sometimes tracking clients for a year, with the service cycle following through until the client signs the property handover form. Both workload and psychological pressure are immense, posing a great challenge to physical and mental health.

How to become a successful and outstanding real estate broker?

I believe the following conditions should be met:

1. Stress tolerance. Real estate brokers shoulder enormous sales targets. Newcomers face the dilemma of not being able to secure deals. Watching others successfully close deals and reap rich rewards, while those working away from home face life pressures, with supervisors constantly urging them, yet facing client indifference and rejection. Faced with difficulties, quite a number of people feel lost and frustrated, either giving up or working passively, thus moving further away from success. Therefore, adjusting one's position, maintaining the right attitude, facing pressure, and accepting challenges are abilities that every real estate broker, especially young people just stepping into the workplace, should possess. Where in the world does anything come easily? Which industry's success comes effortlessly? If every deal went smoothly without rejections, then everyone would become a real estate broker. One can imagine excellent achievements, past happy events, family and friends' expectations. I am a real estate broker, providing services with my expertise. Those who reject me may lose a great opportunity. They lose much more. One can also imagine worse scenarios or tragic experiences of acquaintances. Comparatively, one is still successful. Gradually building confidence.

2. Analytical ability. Real estate brokers should possess keen analytical skills for market opportunities, learning to identify market opportunities. For example, under the impact of the six national policies, market transactions begin to shrink. At this point, one can explore new markets, such as commercial real estate. Standing out in competitive battles, analyzing market demand, and the success factors of top brokers. What should I learn from and analyze? Continuously finding market gaps or new performance growth points keeps oneself invincible.

3. Communication ability. A successful real estate broker must be a good communicator. Real estate intermediaries primarily deal with people. How to achieve good communication with clients, homeowners, and colleagues is the key to success. Communicating one's views, beliefs, plans, and methods to superiors, subordinates, and clients/homeowners is one of the most important abilities of a real estate broker. Good communication skills are the best way to win others' support. Practice tells us that many problems in sales arise due to poor communication. Communication mainly involves expressing one's ideas to others while listening to theirs. Everyone desires respect, so you should value others, carefully listen to every word they say, clearly express your own thoughts, clearly understand others' thoughts and inner feelings, smile, show enthusiasm and sincerity, and make others want to confide in you.

Learning: Never being satisfied with already achieved results, continuously learning new knowledge, absorbing nutrients, learning from high performers, adopting their strengths, and applying them to actual work ensures continuous success for real estate brokers. For real estate brokers, sales life is like a battle, an uninterrupted pursuit that leaves no room for rest. Successes are mixed with failures, joys, expectations, pride, and happiness, often intertwined with fear, rejection, and disappointment. Despite facing numerous obstacles and pressures, excellent real estate brokers always rely on their strong learning abilities to maximize their understanding of societal and client changes, using knowledge as a backup, preparing fully for progress, advancing steadily, persistently, and ultimately reaching the peak of success.

Many real estate brokers suffer from a lack of marketing knowledge and low marketing skills, with blanks in areas such as planning and design, market research methods, marketing promotion techniques, contract formulation, laws and regulations, real estate professional knowledge, finance, and architecture. Their operations are messy, and they may even be unable to perform these tasks, relying solely on gifts, banquets, reducing commissions, secret operations, and offering "kickbacks" to sell products. Their lack of professional knowledge is astonishing. Some real estate brokers, although capable of calculating purchase, sales, gross profit, and departmental variable costs, lack basic knowledge of expense levels, capital turnover, sales and purchase costs, capital profit-tax rate analysis, and investment value. They may even be unable to understand ordinary financial statements. Without striving to learn, how can such brokers conduct marketing decisions according to market economy rules? It would be impossible for them to adjust sales behavior through accounting calculations. The professional knowledge of brokers is mainly reflected in four aspects: having a comprehensive understanding of the company, processes and quality management of real estate buying and selling, content of after-sales service, and the company's development direction. Mastering real estate industries and commonly used terms. Brokers should have some understanding of the local real estate development direction. They should accurately grasp local real estate dynamics and the advantages and disadvantages of competitors and reliable selling points. Additionally, they must master real estate marketing knowledge, bank mortgage knowledge, property management knowledge, engineering and construction knowledge, real estate legal knowledge, and some professional terms such as green space ratio, building density, usable area, etc. Understanding customer purchasing psychology and characteristics. Understanding customers' pragmatic, innovative, aesthetic, prestigious, profitable psychology, preferences, self-esteem, imitation, secrecy, doubts, security, etc. Understanding marketing-related content. Brokers should learn about real estate product strategies, marketing price strategies, marketing channel strategies, promotional mix strategies, etc., to understand real estate marketing knowledge. Psychological qualities require strong adaptability, sincerity, confidence, optimism, generosity, perseverance, the ability to withstand various hardships, strong sense of responsibility, and strong self-control.

Knowledge: The main task of brokers is sales. Without sales, there is no hope for performance, and the enterprise has no future. Simultaneously, the work of sales representatives also aims at expansion. Only sales alone offer no hope because what you sell is houses and services. Only by continuously expanding the market and treating customers with integrity can you establish a long-term market position, win a long-term market share, build crucial intangible assets for the company's sales channels, and secure stable performance for yourself. Excellent real estate brokers not only show clients around properties, discuss prices, sign contracts, and collect commissions but should also master more real estate-related knowledge: finance, insurance, investment management, law, property management, decoration, etc., providing clients with comprehensive investment management knowledge and becoming trusted financial experts.

Details: Considering from the client's perspective, most clients won't decide immediately. If you can offer more options at once, especially during bad weather, they will chat endlessly with you and thank you profusely. After all, you've considered their next step. Sometimes, when I know the situation of the next property they plan to visit, I directly suggest whether they should go or not. Since some properties have flaws or don't meet their requirements, visiting them would waste their time. By telling them directly, you save their effort, improving your service invisibly. Out of a hundred clients viewing properties, can any give a deal on the spot?

Yes, sometimes it happens, but in most cases, it's impossible. If the client and the broker don't know each other beforehand, there is no trust relationship, and the client will hesitate, reconsider, and think again. However, as a broker, if a hundred clients ask you to show them properties and none gives you a deal, will you feel disheartened or neglect them? If so, you'd better quit this industry. Someone said: "Your mindset is really good, never annoyed. If it were me, I'd have stopped caring long ago. Who has that patience!" Actually, if you think carefully, you'll realize: taking a hundred clients as an example, if you carefully attend to them, any one of them might become your loyal client in the future. If you neglect them because they didn't give you any deals now, you'll gain nothing. Conversely, if you treat them differently, you might win not only these hundred clients but also their friends, relatives, friends of friends, and relatives of relatives.
