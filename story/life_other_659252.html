A two-and-a-half-month relationship

There is one less person in my heart, and I don't have to worry about anyone anymore. Suddenly, I feel empty. Six months ago at the graduation ceremony, that feeling of never seeing someone again came back.

The first day we separated, my heart suddenly felt hollow. The feeling of not having to worry about someone anymore didn't bring relief, only longing. Today you were on duty, and every time you were on duty, I would call you: What are you doing? Did you eat dinner well? Now I don't have to do that anymore, no more long phone calls, no more worrying about radiation.

The second day after we separated, I still thought about you all the time, trying to act like I've let go, not wanting my parents to worry. I'm already this old, it's really not right for them to worry about my love life. My mom said a lot, but I understand the truth: it's not his fault. Even though I say I understand, how can I let go so quickly because of something like this?

The third day after we separated, I obediently stayed home for dinner, thinking about whether you might also be at home, whether you're not used to living alone yet. Every day I habitually dialed your number, but I don't have to do that anymore. Everything has ended.

I struggled for so long in a relationship that wasn't seen favorably from the start, and ultimately decided to give up. It turns out I'm not as strong as I thought. Every time I think about it, tears keep falling uncontrollably. But even with all the reluctance to let go, when it's time to let go, you must let go. I don't want to make everyone worry.

Finally, I'm free. I should feel very relaxed. In the future, I won't have to feel pressured because of my own uncertainties, I won't have to wait for your calls everyday, I won't have to complain about you being on duty and unable to accompany me, I won't have to worry about you coming home late or even unsafe after police duties, and I won't have to rack my brains thinking about what gift to give you for your upcoming birthday. Everything will pass. Go back two months, we didn't know each other, I'm still alone, life will be the same as before, just missing your care. No matter how cold the winter gets, there'll be no one to warm my hands anymore. Deleting text messages one by one, no matter how reluctant to let go, I keep telling myself, it's just about getting used to it. Suddenly, an idea that shouldn't have occurred to me: maybe not too long from now, these messages will appear on another girl's phone. Life seems to be like that, one relationship ends, and the next one can happen anytime. Still, reality prevails.

In this relationship, I was always passive. Although I didn't say much, I remember all the good things you did for me. I always feel that any expressed sense of gratitude isn't real gratitude. Including when you went to Jinan to pick me up and take me home, including a two-word text message (keep going), all the pressure in my heart disappeared suddenly at that moment. I don't know why you said those two words then, but to me, it wasn't just moving, it was more encouragement and understanding. After the passion fades, life goes back to normal, and we can only face the reality.

On the last day of 2010, looking at countless people's year-end summaries, I realized that this year was extremely unlucky, which made me calmly wait in the end. However, the most fortunate thing was meeting you at the end, who gave me a lot of care. Thank you for being good to me. This relationship can be considered the biggest gain of the year.

As time passes, slowly all that remains are memories. Regardless of how you view this relationship, regardless of how different our starting points were, I sincerely treated it, and my memories are beautiful.
