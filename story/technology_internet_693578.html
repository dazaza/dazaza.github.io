For the comprehensive training in the first phase, I recommend that both students and parents clearly understand the current state of the child’s English level and conduct targeted training. For students whose English scores are consistently above 115, after thoroughly understanding the first and second mock exam papers, appropriately doing some college entrance exam cloze tests and reading comprehensions can help boost their scores to perfection. For students whose current English scores are between 108-115, they must focus on understanding every knowledge point in the first and second mock exams until they are completely familiar with similar knowledge points. For students whose scores are between 100-108, we must ensure "shallow water areas" (including multiple-choice, listening, reading comprehension A/B sections, and the first four questions of task-based reading) achieve full marks, while "deep water areas" (cloze tests, reading comprehension C/D sections, question five of task-based reading, and essays) aim for high scores. If the current score is below 100, it is necessary to find past exam questions and first mock exam questions and train repeatedly until ensuring no similar mistakes are made. Once this standard is reached, we can then shift our focus to the "deep water areas."
