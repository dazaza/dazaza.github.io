A Look at the Equipment Powered by Lenovo Technology for Yu Zhu's Summit Attempt

by zxcdm331dl on 2011-08-27 11:04:04

As the only global Olympic partner in China, the global partner of the Beijing 2008 Olympic Torch Relay, and the designer of the Olympic torch "Xiangyun", Lenovo will do its utmost to assist the Olympic torch in reaching the summit of Mount Everest. Since signing as a strategic partner of the Chinese Mountaineering Team on April 27 this year, Lenovo has been committed to supporting and promoting the development of Chinese mountaineering with high-quality products, technology, and services.

Providing safe and reliable information technology support for the Olympic torch's ascent to the summit of Mount Everest in May 2008 is an important manifestation of Lenovo's technological assistance to Chinese mountaineering. Lenovo plans to jointly build the "Torch Summit Everest Information Command System" with the Chinese Mountaineering Team by the end of February next year. This system will establish a local area network at the Everest base camp and set up a remote monitoring management platform in Beijing, providing comprehensive information services to the mountaineering team. Lenovo's technological assistance in the successful summit of Yuzhu Peak this time is precisely to accumulate experience for the successful summit of Mount Everest next year.

The torch's ascent to the summit of Mount Everest is a massive project involving command and control, athletic training, meteorological analysis, logistics support, news dissemination, and more. It also faces severe tests from the harsh environment of Mount Everest’s high altitude, high humidity, high wind speed, low temperature, and low pressure. The information system must ensure zero-failure operation to guarantee the successful ascent of the torch to the summit of Mount Everest. This poses strict requirements on technology and product performance.

After the completion of the "Torch Summit Everest Information Command System," it will play a direct role in formulating the torch summit plan, designing the summit route, determining the summit timing, establishing contact solutions, and more.

Lenovo will provide computing equipment for this information system, including all-in-one servers, ThinkPad laptops, Tianyi laptops, printers, mobile storage, and corresponding technical support. These products will be applied in the command and service systems for the torch's ascent to the summit of Mount Everest, the Everest base camp internet cafe at an altitude of 5154 meters, the advanced command contact point at an altitude of 6500 meters, and the media center and internet cafe at an altitude of 4000 meters.

Lenovo Tianyi F41 Snow Mountain Edition, conquering peaks, challenging limits

The Lenovo Tianyi F41 Snow Mountain Edition laptop is the world's only notebook computer product designed with snow mountains as its theme. The hand-drawn images of the world's five major peaks on the A side of the body, paired with a snow-white appearance, integrate modern technology with the spirit of challenging limits and being resilient and brave, offering valuable collections for people with courage who desire to enjoy peak experiences. Upon opening the F41 Snow Mountain Edition, the image of a determined and ever-advancing mountaineer on the left side of the touchpad vividly conveys the user's attitude of challenging dreams and showcasing oneself.

The majestic mountains harmonize perfectly with the robust qualities of the Lenovo Tianyi F41 Snow Mountain Edition. Performing exceptionally well in environments ranging from 40 degrees Celsius high temperatures to super-low temperatures, as well as in dry, humid, and dusty conditions, it can withstand rigorous tests. Echoing the interlocking Olympic rings characteristic of Lenovo laptops, it embodies the Olympic spirit of "faster, higher, stronger," granting the F41 even more commemorative significance in terms of surpassing oneself and climbing higher peaks.

The new high-end rack server products from Lenovo, the R515/R525, will serve as the core platform for the network construction of the "Torch Summit Everest Information Command System" in 2008. The R515 achieved top global performance in the SPECjbb energy efficiency test, while the R525 ranked among the top three in the SPECjbb overall performance test. Both the R515/R525 feature superior performance, energy conservation, environmental protection, and high stability, successfully addressing the challenges of extreme cold, low air pressure, and poor power supply conditions at the Everest base camp. They passed the Yuzhu Peak test smoothly and ultimately assisted the Olympic flame in reaching the summit of Mount Everest.
