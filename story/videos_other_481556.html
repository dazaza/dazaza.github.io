Internet giants' next decade "blueprint"

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-25 16:21:40

Ten years ago, no one could have imagined that a mini-blog of 140 characters could change the lifestyle habits of all Chinese netizens, online shopping could quickly replace physical consumption, and the once expensive and high-tech representative PC would soon be replaced by smartphones. Even the entire internet industry underwent a tremendous transformation.

But today, these have become our daily routine of internet life.

At the 2011 China Internet Conference, four major figures in the Chinese internet industry—Pony Ma (Tencent), Charles Chao (Sina), William Ding (NetEase), and Kai-Fu Lee—gave a consistent answer: SNS, mobile internet, and e-commerce are the next 10 years for the Chinese internet!

Let's see how these industry leaders depict the blueprint of the next ten years for the Chinese internet.

Trend 1: Weibo, Weibo, and still Weibo

Sina, 200 million; Tencent, 233 million. These are the latest registered user numbers announced by China’s two largest Weibo operators. Behind the numbers, the full-scale explosion of Weibo is just beginning.

As the internet leaders spoke confidently on the stage at the 10th China Internet Conference, across the ocean, in the heart of New York's Times Square, a huge advertisement for Sina Weibo was displayed on the Nasdaq Tower. The giant "Hello" represented 200 million Sina Weibo users greeting the world. Sina's CEO and President, Charles Chao, described Sina Weibo as a phenomenon-level internet application. He pointed out that over the past few months, Sina has continuously launched new Weibo applications and services. The new version of Weibo, originally scheduled for public beta testing in June, is expected to begin beta testing in September. The new version will bring significant changes to user experience and system architecture, aiming to provide better scalability, a more friendly user interface, and offer superior API development connections to third-party developers. The new version of Weibo will enable users to form stronger social relationships on the Weibo platform.

Meanwhile, Tencent, which leads in registered user numbers, isn’t being modest either. Tencent Chairman and CEO Pony Ma stated at the conference: "I think this field is a battleground for all companies, including Tencent." He pointed out that for portal sites, Web 1.0-style network media is outdated, Web 2.0 is the direction. However, the online community represented by Web 2.0 currently holds the strongest appeal for Chinese netizens. Weibo lies between Web 1.0 and Web 2.0, and the path for portal site content to transition to Web 2.0 is actually the road of Weibo.

The newcomer in the Weibo space, NetEase, is also not falling behind. The latest data shows that NetEase Weibo has a total of 56.77 million users. NetEase CEO William Ding candidly admitted at the conference that he "steals skills underwater" on Sina Weibo. Recently, NetEase made a strategic adjustment in its Weibo strategy, integrating Weibo with NetEase Passports. Users registering for a NetEase Passport (including email) will automatically receive a NetEase Weibo account. This means that NetEase email users will be converted into NetEase Weibo users, and the email service is considered the single business with the most registered users at NetEase.

Can different Weibos interconnect? No way!

At the conference, the internet leaders were asked a user-focused question: Is there any possibility of interoperability between different Weibo platforms? The answer was resolute: No way!
