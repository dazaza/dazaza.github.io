Interior decoration. Why not go find a punching bag to hang up?

by hartyqekb on 2009-12-02 17:22:22

1. Do not log in to websites unrelated to work on the office computer. If you think no one is paying attention to what you're doing or consider yourself a computer expert who can erase all traces of your browsing history, you are clearly overestimating your abilities. Your boss can easily and quickly check what you've been doing on your computer if he wants to, especially in well-equipped large companies where this is even easier. So if you don't want to give your boss any leverage, just wait until you get home before indulging in whatever it is you're viewing—preferably with your wife's support.

2. Do not dress in a way that makes others feel you're too tempting. Even if your work environment doesn't require formal suits and ties, opt for simple casual wear. Besides wearing a wedding ring, avoid wearing excessive jewelry, as it might make you appear more feminine than your female colleagues. Try to avoid pink tones in your clothing. If you don’t want to spend time matching outfits, choose darker colors. Be sure to note that when wearing black leather shoes, never pair them with white socks. Regularly clean out your wardrobe and throw away any socks with holes.

3. Do not gossip about your boss behind their back. If you really feel the urge, why not hang up a punching bag, write your boss's name on it, and then beat it up. At least, compared to telling your coworkers bad things about your boss, this is less dangerous. Generally speaking, the things you say about your boss behind their back will quickly reach their ears, often sounding much worse than what you actually said. Be careful; at some point, your boss might confront you—perhaps not physically, but maybe by firing you.

4. Do not overtly flatter your boss. If they're not an idiot, they'll realize that you're just playing with them.

5. Do not tell dirty jokes. Understand that this habit has nothing to do with humor. Although you may make your female colleagues laugh, they might turn around and think: "Goodness, how shameless! He actually says such things!"

6. Do not befriend your coworkers. While imagining your coworkers as "imaginary enemies" might seem extreme, it can at least help prevent certain unpleasant office disputes. Think of them as a group of strangers whose names you know. Never confide your privacy to your coworkers. It's like planting a landmine beside you. When it doesn't explode, everything seems calm, but if it does, you're completely done for. A coworker’s damage potential far exceeds that of a close friend—at the very least, your close friend doesn't know who your boss is.

7. Do not date your coworkers. Since you're just a normal man, it's hard for you to ignore your significant other, which directly leads to: even if you work diligently like an ox, your boss will still suspect that you spend your working hours dating. Don't complain about your boss's baseless suspicions; if you were in their position, you'd think the same way. If you really fall in love with one of your colleagues, it seems there are only two ways out: either you leave the company or your lover leaves the company.

8. Do not donate more money than your superior. In some social activities, such as government or union calls for donations to disaster areas, if your superior donates 100 yuan, you'd better donate no more than 85 yuan. If you want to donate more, you can do so in other ways! The reason is obvious once you think about it.
