Summary of the Development of International B2B Websites!

Today, I met an old friend in the B2B industry on MSN. I heard that he has now moved to www.madeinchina.com under慧聪 (Hc360). Naturally, I congratulated him politely on his new position, but I also worried about him because I had browsed the madeinchina.com website and felt it was a mishmash. Initially, I kept my opinions to myself and didn't tell my friend, but as soon as he saw me, he showered me with compliments, saying that since I've been in the B2B industry for a long time, I must understand it well, and he asked me to give some suggestions and opinions about his company's website.

Unable to resist his repeated requests, I could only share my views based on my past experience, though I have already transitioned to developing online accounting software. To be honest, my English isn't very good, so visiting www.madeinchina.com was particularly challenging for me. Here are my summarized thoughts:

First: Madeinchina.com misplaces priorities, lacks focus

Because of my poor English, I was fortunate that there were some Chinese characters on the homepage of madeinchina.com, which helped me understand it more quickly. However, after looking at the homepage, I believe that madeinchina.com misplaces its priorities and lacks focus. As an international B2B website, the main audience should be foreigners, as they are the buyers, while Chinese companies are the suppliers. However, madeinchina.com does not seem to consider this. Based on my own experience and browsing through the site, I think madeinchina.com is designed more for Chinese suppliers. Foreigners who visit the site may not even know what kind of website it is. If the priorities are misplaced like this, how can the website help Chinese companies find buyers? The homepage leads Chinese companies to register and use the site, rather than guiding foreign buyers to browse and make purchases. Moreover, the focus is unclear, and after a quick look at the site, I don’t know what the main point or theme is. It seems like registering Chinese companies will lead to hopes for foreign trade, but even if all Chinese companies register, without foreign buyers coming to purchase, it’s still equal to zero.

Second: No tempting or guiding features for foreign buyers

Imagine you're a foreign buyer. What would your reaction be upon seeing madeinchina.com? I estimate you wouldn’t have much of a reaction at all, and after a few minutes, you might not even know what the site is for. Can such a site truly be effective? Definitely not. Therefore, I believe the primary audience of the site should be foreign buyers, and the homepage should guide them to use and browse the site. If foreigners want to buy products, what do they hope to see? They likely hope to see product images, manufacturers, production information, market conditions in China, and product prices. However, none of these are prominently displayed on the homepage; there are just a few simple product pictures. Foreigners are unfamiliar with Chinese manufacturing enterprises, so they urgently need to understand the Chinese enterprise market. For example, where in China are sock manufacturing companies most concentrated? Which sock manufacturer is the most reputable and trustworthy in China? Where are the major wholesale markets located, their scales, and annual production volumes? These are things the website needs to provide, rather than letting foreigners search for themselves. If the website offers such services, foreigners will surely like it and trust the site.

Third: No confidence assurance provided to Chinese companies

After viewing the madeinchina.com website, I kept thinking about one question: how does the site help Chinese companies promote their products, let foreigners know about the site, and recognize the registered companies on the site? After reviewing the site, it’s hard to find any answers to these questions. As a Chinese company, one would naturally want to join the site, but why should they? It's because they hope to receive foreign trade orders through the madeinchina.com platform. But what does madeinchina.com do to attract foreigners to seek out the companies listed on the site? Without providing such confidence assurances, how can the site persuade companies to join? Currently, the homepage provides only rough content to attract Chinese companies to join. I believe the homepage should list the promotional services the site offers overseas, the efforts made, participation in international exhibitions, the results achieved, the number of foreigners paying attention and supporting the site, media coverage from foreign outlets, and other related details. If the site doesn't offer these or fails to display them, what charm or appeal does madeinchina.com have?

After a brief review of the homepage, these are my general impressions. I dare not delve deeper into each page because I'm afraid that if I say too much, my friend won’t be able to handle such criticisms. After all, expressing my true opinions amounts to negating my friend's work! Sincerely, I hope that under my friend's leadership, madeinchina.com can improve and excel.
