Were you not the one who shouted that the reward is one hundred gold?

by cffg1188c on 2012-03-09 17:11:24

Chapter Four: Always Keep Your Word When Doing Things. There was an old saying in the gambling dens of yore at the card tables: "You can be cunning and even explosive in gambling, but never a deadbeat." Its meaning is that you can use any means at your disposal to defeat your opponent, as long as you do it cleverly and subtly enough not to be caught, even if it's crafty or deceitful, it will be allowed. However, one must always honor their bets and pay off all stakes made, no refunds accepted.

There was a story about a wealthy man who fell into the river during a boat ride across the river. The rich man shouted loudly, "Whoever saves me will get a hundred gold coins!" Two fishermen happened to be passing by on their way home from fishing. They saw him and jumped into the river to save the rich man. But when the rich man realized it was just a fisherman heading home who had saved him, he didn't want to keep his promise of giving a hundred gold coins. He only gave the fisherman ten gold coins. The fisherman said, "Didn't you shout that you would give a hundred gold coins?" The rich man became extremely angry and said, "You are just a small fisherman, you earn only a few gold coins a year. Giving you ten gold coins is already quite generous." The fisherman returned home disgruntled. Later, another time, this rich man capsized again while crossing the river and once again shouted, "Whoever saves me will get a hundred gold coins!" There weren't many people passing by this place, mostly some fishermen. Coincidentally, the same fisherman who had saved him last time passed by again. He told the other fishermen, "We cannot save this person, he doesn't keep his word." As a result, the fishermen didn't believe what the rich man said and trusted the fisherman's words instead, so they did not go to save him, and thus the rich man drowned.

Not keeping his word cost him his life, a very profound lesson indeed.

Being untrustworthy not only shows a person's low character and improper behavior but also reflects a short-sighted and foolish action that disregards the future and long-term consequences, making it impossible to accomplish anything.

An article titled "Without Credit, There Is Nothing" states: "In a mature society, a powerful society, we not only consider each individual but also establish necessary files for them. These necessary files are not blacklists, but rather documents that officially confirm your credibility to relevant parties. This way, banks can lend money to you, merchants dare to do business with you, others can cooperate with you, companies can hire you, and of course, you can buy houses or goods on installment payments... As long as there is evidence showing that you are a person of good credit, credit becomes your passport, allowing you to be respected and travel freely in this civilized society. If you don't keep your word? If you dare not repay your debts, or if you dare to evade tickets on public transport, tear up contracts, evade taxes, convert public property into private gain, lie and deceive people, in short, if you dare to break your word even once, you will end up on the blacklist of those without credit. You will lose many opportunities, banks will naturally not lend you money anymore, no one will be willing to cooperate with you, neighbors will avoid you, no company will want to hire you, and naturally, no one will want to be friends with you. It will be hard for you to stand firm in this civilized society."

A man of integrity does not act dishonorably; wise men do not act dishonorably. Gaining the trust of many and building your own creditworthiness, regardless of the method you choose, sincerity, honesty, and diligence remain the most fundamental secrets to success.

How does one test their own level of creditworthiness? There is a simple way: borrow 1000 yuan from every friend and relative you know. The number of people who agree to lend you 1000 yuan can roughly estimate your creditworthiness. If you can borrow 20,000 yuan this way, then it proves that you have the credit of 20 people. If you can borrow 50,000 yuan, probably you have the credit of 50 people. For you, the better your repayment ability, the more relatives and friends will support you. Of course, credit is not entirely based on debt repayment ability. For example, some relatives and friends may be willing to lend you money because they have confidence in your abilities. They might think beforehand that this money could be lost, but they still lend it to you. If you're fortunate enough to be like this, why worry about anything you can't accomplish?
