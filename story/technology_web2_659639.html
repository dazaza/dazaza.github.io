In 2011, global smartphone shipments exceeded those of PCs + tablets for the first time.

by ous09032ds on 2012-02-06 14:53:32

Sina Technology News, early morning of February 6th Beijing time - According to the latest report released by market research company Canalys, in 2011, global smartphone shipments exceeded PC shipments, including tablets for the first time.

Globally, smartphone shipments from all manufacturers in the fourth quarter reached 158.5 million units, an increase of 57% year-on-year. This brought the total smartphone shipments for 2011 to 487.7 million units, a two-thirds increase year-on-year. On the other hand, the total global PC shipments in 2011 were 414.6 million units.

Canalys Vice President and Chief Analyst Chris Jones said: "Smartphone shipments surpassing those of PCs is an important milestone. In just a few years, smartphones have evolved from niche products in the high-end mobile phone market into true mass-market products."

He stated that lower prices have driven the development of smartphones, while also increasing consumer demand for internet browsing, content consumption, and the use of mobile applications and services. However, some industry insiders pointed out that shipment volumes do not equate to sales, and counting tablets as PCs is also incorrect.

Canalys data also showed that in the fourth quarter of 2011, Apple became the leading manufacturer of both smartphones and PC clients. The shipments of iPhones, iPads, and Mac computers were 37 million units, 15.4 million units, and 5.2 million units respectively. Apple also set a record for the highest quarterly smartphone shipment volume by a single manufacturer, surpassing Nokia's (Weibo) shipment volume of 28.3 million units in the fourth quarter of 2010. Additionally, Apple surpassed Nokia for the first time to become the top-ranked smartphone manufacturer in terms of annual shipment volume. Data shows that Apple shipped 93.1 million iPhones in 2011, an increase of 96% year-on-year.

However, the situation in 2012 may not be as optimistic as in 2011. Canalys believes that this year's smartphone shipment growth will slow down, and manufacturers will implement more cost controls to enhance profitability. Canalys noted: "It should be pointed out that some manufacturers attracting the low-end market with low prices, such as Huawei (Weibo), ZTE, and LG, are also focusing more on the high-end market. Flagship-level products that can boost selling prices and profit margins will receive more attention this year."
