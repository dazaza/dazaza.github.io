Plan One: Purchasing insurance requires early planning

For Miss Huang, who has just started working, at the beginning stage of life planning, protection must be done with clear objectives. From being a happy single person to later forming a family and starting a career, young singles experience the most changes in living environment and lifestyle. It is an important stage where insurance should be used for precautionary measures, such as travel insurance, which should be arranged early.

1. Clarify the focus of protection. Most life insurance products are long-term, so before purchasing, a long-term financial plan must be made. Taking protection insurance as an example, it mainly reflects the responsibility to the family. The concept of family is different for a person before and after marriage, and thus the responsibility to the family is also different. The demand amount can vary greatly. Since Miss Huang is currently single and will soon face major life events like marriage, buying a house, and having children, pension insurance is not the most urgent need at this stage.

As a newcomer to society with relatively low income and little family burden, priority should be given to personal accident and medical accident protection; then consider a certain amount of life insurance and health insurance to adequately prepare for repaying the upbringing kindness of her parents; meanwhile, take advantage of her youth to prepare critical illness insurance as early as possible.

2. Place the demand for accident insurance first. According to statistical data, young people have the highest rate of accidents. A major feature of accident insurance is its low premium, making this type of insurance the most economical and practical for newly employed young people. By spending about 200 yuan per year, one can obtain approximately 100,000 yuan worth of ordinary accident insurance. Regarding the choice of accident insurance amount, it is generally advisable to choose 5-10 times the annual income.

3. Match accident medical insurance and hospital allowance insurance well. Although Miss Huang has medical insurance, due to her short work tenure, her personal medical account reserve fund is insufficient, and the proportion of outpatient self-payment is high. Therefore, it would be best to get accident outpatient emergency medical expense insurance. In addition, some supplementary hospital allowance insurance can be added to make up for the shortfall of medical insurance and compensate for lost wages.

4. Life insurance is a "conscience policy" for parents. Although young people mostly die from accidents, there is still concern that if death occurs due to disease, if only accident insurance is purchased, the parents will not receive compensation. Therefore, to prevent any possibility, a certain amount of life insurance can be purchased. Planning such an insurance scheme at this stage is considered preparing a "conscience policy" for parents.

In health insurance, it is especially important to purchase critical illness insurance as early as possible. This type of insurance is one of the indispensable insurances in life and the earlier you subscribe, the more cost-effective it becomes.

From the perspective of quota arrangement, the author gives Miss Huang the following suggestions: 150,000 yuan for accident insurance, 5,000 yuan for accidental medical insurance, 100,000 yuan for life insurance, 100,000 yuan for critical illness insurance, and supplementary hospital allowance insurance. A reminder for Miss Huang is: don't buy too much insurance because as a young person, on one hand, economic payment ability is limited, and on the other hand, there are always other expenses. At this time, the purpose of buying insurance is to form a basic living security. When economic conditions and other situations change, the insurance plan can still be adjusted and modified.

Plan Two: Choose an annual payment method

A popular saying nowadays is "if you don't manage your finances, your finances won't manage you." Regardless of current income levels, strong financial management desires combined with reasonable financial management measures will certainly help young people achieve their wealth dreams.

Insurance, as an indispensable survival stabilizer for modern people, can make our lives more solid and comfortable. A person's life will go through many risk tests, such as unexpected diseases, unforeseen accidents, unwarranted property disasters, and negligence-induced liability risks, etc. Therefore, the author suggests that young friends who have just entered society and started independent living begin with the most basic protections, gradually increasing protection items and amounts as income increases. Moreover, since monthly insurance premiums are slightly higher, it is recommended to adopt an annual payment method.

Plan Analysis:

1. Since Miss Huang mentioned that her physical condition is not good, the author suggests choosing critical illness insurance. This type of insurance allows Miss Huang to insure until the age of 65 (after the age of 55, human body functions gradually decline, and the risk of critical illness increases), maximizing protection. Moreover, according to this clause, when she reaches the age of 60-69 years old, she can receive 2,000 yuan annually as a physical examination subsidy, and if no critical illness occurs by the age of 70, she will be given a 100,000 yuan longevity bonus.

2. Considering that Miss Huang may have a higher chance of hospitalization than the average person, the IPA plan from AIA Life Insurance is specially chosen for her. The reimbursement ratio of this plan can reach 100%, with no deductible, and unlimited times per year, including coverage for accidents, diseases, hospital subsidies, and surgical costs.

Subscription Prompt:

Miss Huang mentioned that her physical condition is not good, but it is unclear whether it is due to weak constitution, a history of serious illness, or genetic factors. If the latter two reasons cause poor physical condition, insurance companies may request physical examinations or even deny coverage when determining whether to underwrite, so prior consultation is necessary.

Plan Three: Best to purchase universal insurance

For people in their twenties who have just graduated and started working, they are exactly at the beginning of their careers. People at this stage often exhibit the following characteristics: 1. Due to lack of work experience after graduation, income is less and unstable. 2. After receiving salary, they give some money to support their parents' household expenses. 3. Monthly expenses are large, and many people unfortunately become "moonlight clan" by the end of the month. 4. Must save for marriage and house purchase. 5. Due to fierce job competition, they are often physically and mentally exhausted. Developing a good financial management habit and customizing a reasonable and suitable protection plan is particularly important.

Miss Huang is a very typical example. Her monthly income is only 2,000 yuan, not only does she have to support her family monthly, but she also has to invest in funds regularly. Therefore, the remaining money available for her own disposal is not much, so we must make big plans with small money. The author believes that based on Miss Huang's current financial situation, choosing to subscribe to a universal insurance policy is the most appropriate. First, the features of universal insurance include lower installment premiums and very flexible payment periods, and the insurance amount can be appropriately adjusted according to different stages of life. Secondly, the personal account returns of universal insurance are relatively stable, which can reduce the investment risks brought by Miss Huang's regular fund investment. Meanwhile, the value of the personal account can be partially withdrawn at any time, so as to prepare for unexpected needs. With such planning, Miss Huang can have a high level of protection while obtaining a stable investment return.

The author recommends the following insurance plan for Miss Huang: 1. Universal life insurance with an insurance amount of 100,000 yuan, with an annual installment premium of 2,000 yuan, or 500 yuan per quarter; additionally, an extra premium of 1,200 yuan can be added annually. In this way, both a certain amount of insurance and a stable investment can be achieved. After 5 years, this universal insurance does not need to deduct initial fees anymore, and as long as hundreds of yuan of risk premium and account management fee are paid, the policy remains valid, so Miss Huang will have a lot of choices at that time. Plus, in five or six years, Miss Huang will be at the age of marriage, and there may be some big expenses financially, so it is suggested that Miss Huang can choose to temporarily stop paying premiums at that time, and continue to pay the corresponding premiums after the finances stabilize. 2. Anxin term life insurance with an insurance amount of 150,000 yuan and a protection period of 25 years. Additionally, the author also recommends adding critical illness insurance with an insurance amount of 100,000 yuan and an insurance period of 30 years. 3. Wuyoubao accident insurance with an insurance amount of 100,000 yuan, in this way, if Miss Huang unfortunately dies due to an accident, her family can receive up to 300,000 yuan of insurance. 4. Although Miss Huang's company has social security, she still has to bear a portion of medical expenses when hospitalized due to illness, so some additional accidental injury medical insurance and daily hospitalization subsidies and hospitalization cost medical insurance can be appropriately added.

Such a set of protection plans has an annual premium expenditure of 4,752 yuan, and Miss Huang deposits 396 yuan per month, allowing her to have the first phase of protection planning with many projects. We control her annual premium expenditure within about 20% of her annual income, which can satisfy various financial arrangements for Miss Huang without bringing her any payment risks.

Miss Huang lives with her parents, with an average monthly consumption expenditure of 500 yuan. Apart from basic expenses, she gives her parents 300 yuan as living expenses each month, and invests 300 yuan in funds every month. She has already processed medical insurance at her workplace.

Miss Huang wants to save some money for the future by purchasing insurance. At the same time, she hopes that the product has strong flexibility and can be withdrawn at any time for unexpected needs. It would be better to pay premiums monthly or quarterly to force herself to save money. In addition, due to her poor health, she wants to add some critical illness insurance and hospital allowances, etc.
