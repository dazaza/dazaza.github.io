The mundane world is like water.

by wpnxiaoyans on 2011-09-19 18:28:45

Tying my black hair for you, ties three thousand reminiscences, ties a lifetime of entanglement. Black hair as a ring of love, encircling, is a lifetime without regret...

Who smiles at the beginning of time, who sighs at its end. Even if I sing palace music tune after tune, in the deep loneliness and affection, who hesitates and moves forward.

In your eyes, coiled into the peach of spring. Peach blossoms bloom, in the furrowed brows where your worries are compressed, blooming in the bottom of my bright autumn water eyes. In the glance, peach blossoms are like jasper, red fallen petals cover the sky. The most beautiful face in the world cannot withstand the flow of time, flowers bloom for one season, fall for one day. Consideration, consideration, how can one not consider...

That touch of warmth, who in the moonlit night, stirs my heart like a spring, gurgling out. Once upon a time, past. Those who have passed away are thus. It's better to go back, it's better to go back. Some love has been buried deep in soil that will not sprout. Some memories have been treasured in a gold-painted box with a rusted lock.

A passionate encounter is the most beautiful, joyfully smiling from head to toe. Two intertwined hearts, my black hair like clouds, gently wrapping around your delicate fingers.

Even if I get drunk three thousand times, wasting my whole life in sorrow, who would know. Who knows me, with such intelligence, yet with so many twists in my heart. Who knows me, with a gentle smile, yet with tears wetting my handkerchief.

Faraway departure, white moonlight, clear wind, accompanying me with a smile, riding a horse and galloping through the flowers. Treading on Yangzhou, crossing Xie Bridge.

In your eyes, coiled into the plum of winter, in the snowfall, blooming that touch of pink, a faint fragrance permeating the heart...

My tenderness cannot erase the deep red mole on your heart. Your pain, my wound. As if destined in a cycle, a calamity, a difficulty. Who owes whom tender feelings, who betrays whose longing. When plain years come without brilliance, who will still remember fragments, who will still yearn and be hurt?

The world is like water, a cup of emotions, morning after morning. Years pass by in the blink of an eye, in my focused gaze, day by day, hour by hour, flowing away.

Spring remains the same, people grow thin, tear stains linger as they guard the window, burying thoughts of longing with the deepening spring.

At the place where dust settles, worldly prosperity begins to shine brightly. Forgetting, beginning. Starting point, endpoint. Originally following cause and effect. Originally one thought of longing, two places of silent contemplation. Under the Bodhi tree, the heart is as clear as glass. Transparent. All hatred and infatuation fade away, leaving no trace...

In Yi An's small order, who accompanies me, watching green leaves grow thick, lamenting red flowers fading, saying the hibiscus remains unchanged. That sorrowful melody, saddening whose mood, making whose face pale.

In your eyes, coiled into the chrysanthemum of autumn, layer upon layer of heart affairs, knotted, budding. On the day the flowers bloom, proudly displaying intricate solitude. When the heart affairs bloom layer by layer, who will listen quietly. That flower event, colorful...

Gentle black hair, lingering affection, is your expectation or your evasion. The hesitation in the eyes, hurts whose tender feelings.

Morning rises, tides ebb, morning breeze, evening rain. Day by day, year by year. Only the east wind still carries fragrance, but the traces of old friends have vanished. How deep is this deep affection, how much madness. Curtains layer upon layer, pondering how much. Who can shadow my solitary figure wiping wine, who can sympathize with my restless sleepless nights.

That season of beautiful encounter, blooms in the jade gate brushed by the spring breeze. At the willow bank, breaking willows as gifts, is it earnest retention or the quietness of parting. Who waits for me year after year on the shore of time, waiting for me to pick morning dew lightly, waiting for me to be filled with floral fragrance.

If love departs, like spring fades, there is no way to hold it back. Even if I sing "Yangguan San Die" over and over again, I cannot wait for the graceful returning figure.

Worldly affairs, lingering, loving those crystal-clear eyes, loving the warmth of holding hands, loving the murmured sweet nothings, filled with warmth and fragrance.

In your eyes, coiled into the lotus of summer, swaying gracefully in the green pond. From afar, the lotus leaves stretch endlessly green, the enchanting flowers bloom red. Pure lotuses stand tall, listening to the sound of falling rain...

Enough, enough, untie this knot of affection, untie this buckle of longing...
