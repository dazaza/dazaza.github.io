The suspect fled for 20 years and was sentenced according to the criminal law 30 years ago, without a fine.

by lovei0c1 on 2012-03-06 13:56:46

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing (by Xu Gang, Qiao Xu, and reporter Sun Ying) - After fleeing for 20 years, the robber Yang was finally arrested in Shenzhen. Twenty years ago, Yang, along with two accomplices, used a knife to rob a password case. Time has passed, and so has the criminal law been revised. The question now is whether the new or the old criminal law should be applied. We have learned today that the Xicheng District Procuratorate approved the arrest of Yang and believed that his criminal responsibility should be pursued according to the old criminal law.

On June 11, 1991, Mr. Xu, the victim, was looking at stamps in the Yuetan philatelic market. He had a brown password case with him, which contained more than 8400 RMB, 200 USD, Tibetan liberation 40th anniversary commemorative stamps, and other stamps and albums. At about 2 PM, Mr. Xu went to the park restroom to relieve himself and placed the password case on the ground. As he was fastening his belt, Yang and his two accomplices, Wang and Li, burst in. Yang and Wang held down Mr. Xu and beat him while Li picked up the password case and ran away.

Li was caught by the crowd just fifty to sixty meters away from the restroom and handed over to the police station. Seeing Li being caught and the password case safe, Mr. Xu began chasing and beating Wang, who was also captured by the crowd not too far away. However, at the time of the incident, everyone's attention was on Li and Wang, allowing Yang to escape amidst the chaos. After returning home, Yang took the money and fled, evading capture for twenty years.

In October 1991, the Xicheng Court sentenced Wang and Li to six years in prison for robbery. Yang, as an absconding co-conspirator, was wanted nationwide.

At the end of last year, Yang was captured in Shenzhen. With twenty years having passed, would Yang still face prosecution? Prosecutors explained that since Yang fled after the crime and has been continuously wanted, this does not fall under the category of exceeding the statute of limitations as defined by the criminal law, and therefore, criminal responsibility should still be pursued.

During these 20 years, the Criminal Law was revised in 1997. Should Yang's act of robbery from 20 years ago be prosecuted under the new or the old criminal law? According to the 1979 Criminal Law, robbery is defined as "using violence, coercion, or other methods to rob public or private property, punishable by three to ten years of imprisonment." In the 1997 revision, the law added a fine: "using violence, coercion, or other methods to rob public or private property, punishable by three to ten years of imprisonment, and a fine." The new law includes a fine, whereas the old one did not. The prosecutor stated that based on the principle of lex mitior, Yang should be subject to the provisions of the 1979 Criminal Law, sentencing him to three to ten years of imprisonment without imposing a fine.
