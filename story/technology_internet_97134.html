Little-known stories about Jack Ma's entrepreneurship when he founded Alibaba

by hophi on 2008-10-18 09:41:07

Collective Southern Return, Farewell to Beijing

In 1999, Jack Ma led his entrepreneurial team in Beijing to build a website for the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade. In an awkward situation, Ma, who was unwilling to be constrained by others, pondered daily about one question: what should be the next step? Ma believed that Beijing's internet scene was too restless and difficult to achieve anything. Moreover, Ma had already endured enough restrictions and obstacles while working in government enterprises, as well as hesitancy. After much inner struggle and questioning, Ma once again made a significant milestone decision in his life: return south.

On the night Ma made the decision, he gathered all members of the team, who had consistently followed him (previously in Hangzhou, then in Beijing) and worked outstandingly. However, when Ma announced his decision, almost everyone was stunned. Ma told them: "I give you three choices: first, go to Yahoo, I will recommend you, Yahoo will definitely hire you, and your salary will be high; second, go to Sina or Sohu, I will recommend you, and your salary will also be high; third, come home with me, you will only get 800 yuan, your place must be within five minutes of me, you rent your own house, no taxis allowed, and you must work at my house. You make your own decision." Ma gave them three days to decide.

As these people gradually walked out, Ma did not regret his decision. He still firmly believed in his choice. Three minutes passed, and all members of the team returned, telling Ma: "Ma, let's go home together."

At that moment, Ma, who had always been strong, shed tears. During the 14 months of working in Beijing, Ma had never taken these people to play. Ma decided to take the team to visit the Great Wall. The next day, Ma and his team visited the Great Wall. It had been a year since they came to Beijing, always working, never having played freely, except for Ma accompanying Yang Zhiyuan to visit the Great Wall once, none of them had been there.

Walking on the Great Wall, everyone felt particularly heavy-hearted. No one could understand why, after doing so much and contributing so much to the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade, they hadn't earned a penny and didn't even have a foothold in Beijing! It was unclear who among them suddenly burst into tears, shouting loudly towards the Great Wall: "Why, why..."

On the last night before leaving Beijing, Ma and more than a dozen young people gathered in a small tavern in Beijing. That night, it snowed heavily. The group drank large bowls of wine, ate big chunks of meat, cried together, and finally sang "True Hero." After singing "True Hero," they sang old songs, one after another. This group of men traveling south and north avoided the word "farewell," which was too heavy for them. Many don't remember exactly what Ma said that night, nor do they know what kind of life awaited them starting the next day, but that night, the wine was warm, the hearts were warm, and the songs were warm.

The "Conspiracy" in the Neighborhood

In February 1999, in Hupan Garden, Hangzhou City, in a completely unmarked, unassuming residential area where no one would notice, Ma's journey with Alibaba quietly began.

Ma chose Hangzhou for a very simple reason: "Due to its distance from IT centers like Beijing and Shenzhen, human resources are relatively cheap." In the early stages of entrepreneurship, a Hong Kong IT expert named Tonny wanted to join Alibaba. Ma said, "500 yuan per month." Tonny replied, "That's not even enough for me to call my girlfriend in Canada."

In his home in Hupan Garden, Ma held the first full meeting and, as usual, recorded the entire "major event," firmly believing it would have great historical value.

In this documentary, in the dim light of the screen, the 18 founding members either sat or stood solemnly around the passionate Ma. Ma stood behind a table, quickly and fervently delivering his impassioned speech.

Ma excitedly told the founding members, "Together we grope in the dark, together we shout. When I shout and charge forward, you won't panic. You wield your broadswords and charge forward. With more than ten of us charging forward, what is there to panic about?"

At this "initiation" meeting, "the start-up funds must be idle money, and borrowing from family and friends is strictly forbidden because the possibility of failure is extremely high. We must prepare to accept the 'worst possible scenario.' But even if Tyson punches me, as long as I'm alive, I'll jump up and continue fighting."

Ma and his partners pooled their money, raising 500,000 yuan in capital. At the time, Ma cut off all retreat options for himself and the team, leaving only one path ahead: succeed or perish. Ten years later, after the Internet industry experienced a winter and Alibaba faced the setback of moving back from overseas to Hangzhou, the 18 founding veterans remained steadfast in various positions within Alibaba. Over the years, most of them have become core pillars of Alibaba.

Naming Alibaba

To achieve the three goals proposed by Ma, the team seemed to vanish from all public places for a long time. Due to limited funds, they did not rent office space but instead worked from Ma's home, with as many as 35 people in the room at one point.

Ma constantly encouraged employees, saying, "Once the starting gun sounds, you won't have time to see how your competitors are running. You just have to sprint all the way." He also warned employees, "The biggest failure is giving up, the biggest enemy is yourself, and the biggest opponent is time." They worked crazily for 16-18 hours a day at Ma's house like wild beasts. They designed web pages day and night, discussed ideas and concepts, and rested on the floor when tired. Neighbors often found it strange that this group left Ma's house at six or seven in the morning and returned in the afternoon at three or four.

Effort pays off. In March 1999, Hangzhou was filled with springtime vitality, and Ma's Alibaba website was officially launched.

An ancient saying goes: "A proper name ensures legitimacy" and "Actions must be justified." People who write books and news articles also know that "a good title makes half the article."

Why name it "Alibaba"? Ma's thoughts at the time were quite visionary.

Although there was only 500,000 yuan in start-up capital, Ma still believed that the future company should have a global perspective and spirit, so the name should also be resonant and international. It is said that Ma thought hard about the name for a long time until one day while eating in the U.S., he suddenly asked a waiter if he knew the name Alibaba. The waiter replied that he did and even told Ma that Alibaba's magic phrase to open treasures was "Open Sesame." Afterwards, Ma repeatedly asked others in various places, and through this test, Ma discovered that this story was familiar worldwide, and regardless of language, the pronunciation was nearly identical. Thus, the decision was made, and Ma named the company Alibaba.

When Ma happily went to register the domain name, he was informed that "Alibaba" was already taken. It turned out that the domain was purchased by a Canadian.

Despite holding only 500,000 yuan in start-up capital, Ma was willing to spend $10,000 to buy back the alibaba.com domain from the Canadian. At the time, many people couldn't understand this move, but perhaps this was the idealism that ran throughout Ma's life! Compared to Google spending millions to redeem two CN domains, Ma's move may not have been a loss.

In April 2005, global Internet search giant Google spent millions of dollars to buy back the CN domains google.com.cn and google.cn that were registered by others years ago, setting the highest price record in CN domain history. This astronomical transaction stirred waves in the domain investment world.

It seems that Ma's initial expenditure was worthwhile because not only did he save millions of dollars in redemption fees, but also the precious time wasted in endless arbitration.

What was Alibaba doing in 1999? Ma's answer was: "We were developing our product in isolation. After returning to Hangzhou in 1999, we decided that within 12 months, we would not actively promote ourselves externally but focus solely on building the best website."

A journalist from Business Week recalled finding Alibaba in a residential area in Hangzhou ten years ago. In the journalist's astonished eyes, Alibaba's working environment was described as "a not-so-large apartment crowded with over 20 employees, bed sheets spread everywhere, and the air filled with the smell of shoes." The journalist was utterly amazed!(Berle Vocational Network)
