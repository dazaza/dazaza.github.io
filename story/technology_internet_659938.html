When will steel prices stop falling and stabilize?

by fdkrkkgfem0 on 2012-02-07 09:17:30

When will steel prices stabilize after the fall - Analysis and Prediction - Industry News - Building Materials Network

Where are steel prices heading? In the nearly one month since the holiday, steel prices in various regions have surged before falling back. I warned beforehand that a rally without volume would not last long. Once the price trend turns, profit-taking resources will flood the market, increasing the risk of a price decline. Recent trends in steel prices largely confirm my earlier judgment about the market. With current significant price drops, it is now essential to determine when steel prices will stabilize.

The rally without volume eventually fizzled out. Improved profitability and the pursuit of scale and profit by steel mills are the main reasons for the recent sustained increase in steel production. Since November last year, domestic steel prices experienced a strong upward trend. Steel mill ex-factory prices increased significantly while raw material prices lagged behind. Mills took advantage of the time difference between finished product price increases and cost increases to gain substantial profits, leading to a significant improvement in profitability. Preliminary estimates show that hot-rolled steel gross profit once exceeded 500 yuan/ton and has recently dropped to around 400 yuan. The improved profitability clearly stimulated production enthusiasm among mills. Additionally, many mills' pursuit of output scale, market share, and profit similarly supported further release of steel production capacity.

According to the latest report from the China Iron and Steel Association (CISA), member enterprises produced 15.949 million tons of crude steel in mid-February, with a national estimate of 18.14 million tons, averaging daily production of 159,500 tons and 181,400 tons respectively. The report also shows that the national daily crude steel production in early and mid-February reached 1.8004 million tons, a significant rebound from January's 1.703 million tons, an increase of nearly 6%. From the report data, the daily crude steel production in mid-February has reached a high level in recent years, only 60,000 tons away from the historical peak of April last year (1.873 million tons).

With the recent acceleration of capacity release, supply and inventory pressures in the entire steel market will continue to increase. It is understood that currently, the finished steel inventory of key enterprises has reached 9.46 million tons, significantly higher than the 7.57 million tons at the end of January, indicating that some mills are beginning to face sales pressure. Meanwhile, social steel inventories are approaching historical peaks, reaching 18.5 million tons, only 50,000 tons away from last year's March high point. From the current mill production schedule, there is a clear trend of increased capacity utilization in March, with daily crude steel production breaking through 1.8 million tons being inevitable. Export prospects in the short term are not ideal, suggesting that mills will increase domestic resource deployment, causing social inventories to continue rising. At the same time, due to exacerbated inversion between wholesale and retail prices, agents' and end-users' ordering enthusiasm has declined, and mills' own finished goods inventories will also increase.

The CISA's report shows that the steel inventory within domestic key enterprises has increased to 9.46 million tons, significantly higher than the 7.57 million tons at the end of January, with a monthly increase of nearly 2 million tons. However, mills' February order ratios were only 50-80%, indicating that during the January steel price increase, mills clearly reduced the amount of resources released to the market. Nevertheless, these resources inside mills will eventually be released to the market. Currently, rebar inventories in society have reached a two-year high of 7.61 million tons, an increase of 82.9% from the low point last year; total social steel inventories in key markets have increased by 41.7% from last year’s low point. After mill inventories are transferred to the market, the supply of steel resources in society will be even more overwhelming.

In contrast, this rally was mainly driven by mills using the energy-saving and emission-reduction concept to manipulate expectations of reduced market inventories, controlling shipment quantities, stimulating hoarding psychology among traders, combined with traditional "winter storage" factors, resulting in a perfect manipulation. After prices reached high levels, mills used the futures market to transfer risks and cash out at high positions. However, there are two points that need careful analysis and summary: 1) Market participants did not expect steel prices to rise so high, 2) Market participants did not expect steel prices to fall so quickly. Regarding these two points, I analyze as follows: Due to the joint efforts of mills and traders, plus extraordinary energy-saving and emission-reduction measures, raw materials remained at high levels, forming a de facto interest community between mills and traders. However, after steel prices set new highs, mills engaged in large-scale hedging at high futures positions, and traders subsequently sold off their spot inventories. These major factors led to the sharp drop in steel prices. As prices plummeted, the attitudes of mills and traders diverged. After a rapid price drop in the short term, mills will likely compensate traders for their previous high-cost resources. Around the 5000 yuan mark, some mills switched from short to long positions, attempting to stabilize prices. If successful, mills will significantly reduce compensation to traders. However, traders, aiming for maximum compensation from mills, will only suppress steel futures and spot prices at all costs, accelerating the adjustment cycle of steel prices, allowing them to enter again at lower levels. This is the true reason for the short-term violent fluctuations in prices.

Cost support will gradually strengthen. With the rapid adjustment of steel prices, raw material price increases started slower than steel price increases. Although some raw material prices have shown signs of reversal, overall they are still slower than the extent of steel price declines. Once near the production cost line, mills and traders will lose the motivation and reason to sell, forming a new balance.

1) Significant increase in funding costs

On February 24, China raised the reserve requirement ratio. Within five months from October 2010, China raised the reserve requirement ratio four times, from 17% in October to the current 19%, and increased interest rates three times, totaling 75 basis points. In January 2011, RMB loans increased by 1.04 trillion yuan, down by 318.2 billion yuan year-on-year. This scale is far below the 1.6 trillion yuan and 1.39 trillion yuan in the same period of 2009 and 2010, and also below the market-expected 1.2 trillion yuan.

In a relatively tight monetary environment compared to previous conditions, financing costs have risen significantly. The trend of acceptance bill discount rates and bank overnight lending rates can prove this. As of February 17, the discount rate for large bank acceptance bills reached 6.52‰ per month, slightly down from 6.9‰ per month on February 11, but up 30.9% from 4.98‰ per month at the beginning of January. Currently, some domestic mills have raised the discount rate for acceptance bills to as high as 9‰ per month, significantly increasing dealers' capital usage costs. Monetary factors will become an important factor affecting the steel market trend in the future.

2) Raw material costs remain at high levels

Iron ore: On February 28, the Indian government announced that export tariffs on iron ore fines and lumps would be uniformly increased to 20%. Previously, the export tariff on Indian fines was 5%, and the lump ore export tariff was 15%. This increase in fines tariffs is particularly noteworthy. According to current price levels, the export tariff on 63.5% Indian fines will increase by $24-$25 per ton. Although under the influence of a significant drop in downstream steel prices, iron ore prices have also fallen slightly, the extent is still relatively limited.

Coke: Currently, the ex-factory price of second-grade coke in Tangshan is 2100 yuan, and the ex-factory price of quasi-first-grade coke is 2180 yuan. Adding 10 yuan for transportation costs, the delivery price to Tangshan Guofeng Steel is 2110-2190 yuan/ton. The ex-factory price of second-grade coke in Hebei including tax is 2060-2090 yuan/ton, and the delivery price to Tangshan area steel mills is around 2200 yuan/ton. The main bearish factors affecting coke prices in Hebei include poor recent downstream steel sales, with price declines of nearly 200 yuan/ton across various regions; high coke inventories at steel mills, with no intention of large-scale purchases in the short term; and some southern steel mills already leading the way in reducing coke prices, impacting the mindset of northern steel mills. The coke market in March will exhibit relatively strong resistance to falls, but local areas may see slight declines.

Billet: Recently, due to a small rebound in the futures market, traders' resistance mentality remains strong. However, due to the inability of the rebar futures to sustain the rebound, several price drops occurred, along with continuous sluggishness in the spot market, making billet procurement difficult. After several adjustments, the 4000 yuan level could not be maintained, continuing a weak downward adjustment. Currently, the Anfeng delivered price for 150mm square billets in Tangshan is 4190 yuan/ton, local mills are mostly quoting 4170 yuan/ton, and traders' naked prices are around 3950 yuan. Market transactions remain sluggish, and trading volumes cannot pick up. Many traders have a selling-off mentality. Given the rapid adjustment of steel market prices recently, mills’ lack of enthusiasm for billet procurement is unavoidable, and a weak oscillation is expected in the near future, with little possibility of another sharp decline.

The stark contrast between the sharp rise in funding costs and the slow fall in raw material prices against the sharp fall in steel market prices suggests that once steel prices touch the bottom of costs, the mindset of mills and traders will change. Buyers entering at low levels will create a bottoming effect on steel prices, stabilizing the trend of steel prices.

Recent steel price trend predictions:

There are irrational factors in the recent sharp fall in steel prices. In a weakly balanced market supply and demand situation, once the steel price trend turns, there will often be sharp rises or falls. Influenced by futures, this year's steel price trend has shown quick rises and falls. From the post-holiday period to now, the steel market seems to have never seen a proper rebound during its sharp decline, which is abnormal.

Observing the futures market, mills attempted to switch from short to long positions around the 5000 yuan mark, trying to forcibly stabilize steel prices. However, under the influence of tightening policies and market forces, the futures market continued to fall, forcing mills to cut losses or get deeply trapped. Once the market stabilizes, mills will inevitably rescue themselves, and with longs covering their positions, a technical rebound is very possible. Currently, the tug-of-war between bulls and bears around the 4800 yuan mark has begun.

Driven by Libyan factors, international oil prices have been continuously rising, breaking through the $100 mark. The logistics cost of producing one ton of steel includes the logistics cost of six tons of material, and the significant increase in steel logistics costs partially supports steel market prices. Another important cost increase this year is the substantial rise in labor costs. With the convening of the National People's Congress and Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, the minimum wage line across the country will increase by 10-20% before May, leading to a noticeable increase in the average labor cost in the steel industry, especially for private steel companies operating at low costs. As steel prices fall close to cost lines, mills will inevitably control production to maintain prices, stabilizing steel prices. Based on current production costs and position costs, the theoretical holding cost for futures steel contract 1110 is around 4800 yuan. On this basis, oversold conditions will lead to rebounds.
