IFM Proximity Switches:

Currently, inductive sensors are very widely used in the industrial field. Compared with mechanical switches, inductive sensors have perfect prerequisites: contactless and wear-free operation, high switching frequency, and high switching accuracy. In addition, they are anti-vibration, dust-proof, and moisture-proof. Inductive sensors can detect all metals without contact.

IFM Magnetic Sensors:

In control technology, magnetic sensors can be used for position detection in a contactless and wear-free manner. They can be used in all application fields where the application of inductive proximity switches is limited. Since the magnetic field can penetrate all non-magnetized materials, magnetic sensors can identify objects such as non-ferromagnetic materials, stainless steel, aluminum, plastic, or wooden walls.

IFM Capacitive Sensors:

Capacitive proximity switches can detect any object in a contactless manner. Compared to inductive sensors that can only detect metal objects, capacitive sensors can also detect non-metallic materials. Typical application areas include the wood industry, paper industry, glass manufacturing, plastic manufacturing, food industry, and chemical industry.

IFM Inductive Safety Switches:

Here you can learn about the first electronic safety switch verified by TÜV up to category 4. This product does not require special components and can directly connect doors, VA or ST37 metal components. Therefore, they are wear-free and unaffected by installation tolerances after long-term use of the door.

IFM Infrared/Red Light Sensors:

In all fields where the precise position of an object needs to be identified safely and without contact, photoelectric sensors should be used. Regardless of the material of the detected object, the instrument can operate normally. Compared to proximity switches, photoelectric sensors have a larger detection range.

IFM Laser Sensors:

In all areas where small objects need to be detected or precise position detection is particularly required, laser systems must be used. Through a small divergence angle, a range length of up to 60 meters can be achieved.

IFM Fiber Optic Sensors, IFM Amplifiers:

In all areas where the installation space for standard photoelectric sensors is too small, fiber optic sensors should be used. The advantage of this system: the display processor and photoelectric components are installed separately from the light entry and exit holes, so the sensing head of the fiber optic can be installed in hard-to-reach positions.
