Operation Ajax: A Book for the iPad Era

by diglog on 2011-11-20 12:30:11

Graphic novels are generally considered as comic books in booklet form with longer content. CIA: Operation Ajax, which was just launched by Cognito Comics on the Apple App Store, is one such work. The presentation format of Operation Ajax has been optimized for the iPad and has thus received very high praise. Its self-introduction states: (It) "is one of the coolest media experiences I've had on an iPad," according to The New York Times. Operation Ajax completely reinvents the book for the digital age.

This statement is not mere self-aggrandizement. In the app store, readers have given it all five-star reviews. Based on my own experience, this narrated motion comic indeed offers a better experience than before. Operation Ajax extends the concept of a "page" by dynamically sliding each panel into view. For example, the four images below are from the scenes that appear on a single "page":

Clearly, if laid out traditionally, these four panels would not be able to appear at the same size on the iPad; users would have to flip pages. Highlighting the current panel's image also better aligns with the expressive form of comics. Additionally, the narration is also an important element. These designs make Operation Ajax look cooler than traditional works. I eagerly anticipate classic works like Kingdom Come and Watchmen being reborn through new forms of expression.

Chikodi Chima of VentureBeat, after experiencing this app, said, "To say nothing of exaggeration, Operation Ajax shows us how tablets can transform the reading experience. It's like watching a movie where you can pause the action at any time and marvel at the scene."

The angel investors of Cognito Comics clearly have a long-term vision and great patience. The entire creation process of the work lasted four years. The company's original plan was to create a traditional book, but the 2010 release of the iPad brought new possibilities to books. This caused the company to change its original course.
