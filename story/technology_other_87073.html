Care for your breasts, care for yourself.

Step 1: What does the organization need? You should know. There are hundreds of rumors circulating about breast health and beauty. Which ones are credible and which ones are suspicious? Why not start by understanding the four types of tissue that make up the breast, and learn what breasts really need to differentiate between truth and falsehood.

● Glandular Tissue → Maintain Breast Health. The glandular tissue is responsible for lactation and is controlled by hormones. It gradually enlarges and then returns to its original state with each menstrual cycle.

Why can't breasts handle too much stress? Staying up late, binge eating, and emotional fluctuations directly affect hormone levels. Fluctuations in hormone levels within the body stimulate the glandular tissue, making it prone to disease.

● Connective Tissue → Prevent Breast Sagging. Connective tissue combines with chest muscles and is the tissue that suspends the breasts. It has no elasticity at all; once overstretched and torn, it is difficult to recover, leading to breast sagging.

Why wear a well-fitting bra? After breast development, wearing a bra that provides good support is very important.

● Fat Tissue → Control Breast Size. The most abundant component in the breast is fat, with glandular and connective tissues floating within the fat. The amount of fat determines the size of the breast.

Why can't you diet excessively? One reason women shouldn't diet excessively is this: dieting results in overall fat loss, including in the breasts.

● Pectoral Muscles → Determine Breast Shape. Breasts hang externally on the pectoral muscles via connective tissue. The support of the pectoral muscles determines the direction of the breasts.

Why can exercise enhance bust size? Through exercise, pectoral muscles can grow, lifting the chest. Exercising ligaments can make the chest more upright, and an increase in pectoral muscle size makes the breasts protrude, making the chest appear fuller.

Step 2: Find the Ultimate Bra, the kind of leadership your breast tissue needs. The ultimate bra has: a custom fit, tailored specifically for you or fitting as if it were made just for you; comfortable material, giving the peaks the softest embrace, feeling like you're not wearing anything or even more comfortable than not wearing anything; a dreamlike design, seemingly effortless but leading the peaks to a better position and shape than when left alone, achieving a perfect front view with it rather than without it. Yes, a bra is like a manager for the chest, just with vastly different styles - some are brutal, others laissez-faire, some too tight, others too loose, none of these are good leaders. Is the one you're wearing now a good leader?

Just ask yourself four questions: When raising your hands, does the lower band of the bra also rise? When standing, does the center of the bra not fit snugly? Is any part of the breast outside the cup? Is the back band of the bra higher than the front? If you answered yes to any of these questions, go change it fast, it's not your Right Bra. Of course, your Right Bra isn't limited to just one type; besides the most common, you may also need some special bras, such as:

Menstrual Bra - Seven to ten days before menstruation, breasts swell and become more sensitive due to hormonal changes. At this time, some bras might feel constricting. Experts suggest considering fabric and size, perhaps switching to a bra that feels more comfortable for this period. For example, changing from full coverage to 3/4 cups allows the breasts to breathe freely.

Sports Bra - Exercise causes the female chest to vibrate, excessive amplitude can damage the connective tissue of the breast, affecting support and causing sagging. Always wear a professional sports bra with excellent anti-shock and sweat-absorbing functions during exercise to protect the chest from injury. Choose different styles and elasticities based on the type of exercise you do regularly. Additionally, sports bras come in sizes S, M, L, so always try them on when purchasing.

Shaping Bra - Do not wear shaping bras for more than 4 hours a day. These bras usually have double layers of soft gel tape under the armpits to push all fat and muscle into the cups to create the illusion of "breast surge," which equals added pressure on the breasts, and prolonged use is detrimental to health. If you want to shape your chest, consider using safe methods such as the BRAVA shaping system.

Bra Should Be Timely Obsolete - The breast is truly an organ that changes daily. Therefore, bras should also be timely obsolete. Before buying new bras, measure again and try them on. Even if cleaned, used, and stored properly, the average lifespan of a bra should not exceed 10 months because both the wear and tear over 10 months and the changes in the breasts will gradually distance their relationship.

Step 3: Monthly, Weekly, Daily, Give Your Breast Ideal Perfect Life

● Daily Must-Do - Diet is the health advisor for the breasts. Proper nutrition ensures that breast growth truly meets its needs.

Glandular Tissue hopes you eat soybeans - Research shows that soybeans balance the secretion of hormones in the body. When estrogen is too low, soybeans increase it, and when estrogen is too high, soybeans decrease it. This dual-balancing effect of soybeans is hard to find in other foods.

Fat Tissue hopes you eat fish - Moderate amounts of fish and dairy products increase a small amount of fat, maintaining breast fullness.

Connective Tissue hopes you eat collagen - Collagen is the main component of connective tissue, and sufficient connective tissue is a prerequisite for preventing breast sagging. This can be found in pig trotters, ox hooves, chicken wings.

● Weekly Must-Do - Whether in bed or out of bed, exercise takes on the task of enriching and protecting the breasts. Regulating emotions and mindset directly and powerfully affects hormones, don't let hormone levels swing wildly like a rubber band.

Back expansion and chest opening: Women's chests and backs are closely related to breast aesthetics. Expanding the back and chest muscles, strengthening the pectoral muscles, makes the breasts stand taller.

Swimming 2-3 times: Exercising in water has dual effects of massaging and exercising the breasts, making the pectoral muscles evenly developed and healthier.

At least one sexual activity: Sexual intercourse can balance hormone secretion and protect the mammary tissue.

1-2 sessions of stress relief and venting activities: Negative emotions such as depression, tension, anger, etc., cause hormone levels to rise, increasing the risk of breast diseases, and should be released promptly.

● Monthly Must-Do - Perform a self-examination of the breasts a week after menstruation and write down a self-examination record.

Self-examination includes observing whether the breasts naturally droop, whether there are any abnormalities in the color of the breasts, and whether the direction of the nipples is normal while standing. Then lie flat or on your side and check if there are any nodules in all parts of the chest tissue. Bend your right arm at a right angle and place it flat above, use your left hand to check the right breast, starting from the armpit and extending to below the breast ribs, horizontally to the sternum, upward through the clavicle, across the shoulder, and back to the armpit. Switch sides.

● Every One and a Half Years Must-Do - Receive professional examination.

Doctors recommend that after the age of 35, undergo a professional breast examination every 18 months. For busy, high-pressure, irregular-lifestyle career women, we suggest starting examinations from the age of 30.

Cancer cell growth requires a certain amount of time. If screening can be done within the growth cycle of cancerous changes, early breast problems can be detected. Professional breast examination results are superior to the manual skills of surgeons. Almost all palpable breast cancers can be detected by mammography, with the minimum sensitivity of fingers being 1.5 cm, and mammography being 0.5 cm.

Calm down the self-examination "storms" After the age of 30, the breasts enter the active phase of life, and various breast issues are most common at this time. Especially when doing self-examinations, you may unexpectedly discover: small lumps, pain, nipple discharge... But most situations are not serious illnesses. 70-80% of adult women have breast hyperplasia, and the incidence of breast fibroadenoma is around 10%.

Suspicious Symptom 1: Lump - Although this is a common early symptom of breast cancer, you might as well touch it again. If the lump has clear boundaries, is soft, and moves well, it is likely a benign adenoma; if the lump changes with the menstrual cycle, it is the most common breast hyperplasia.

This could be the lump symptom of breast cancer: harder texture, adhered to surrounding tissues, painless, accompanied by swelling of lymph nodes under the armpit, collarbone, and neck.

Suspicious Symptom 2: Pain - If cyclical pain is breast hyperplasia; if the pain persists and is close to the sternum, it might be rib hyperplasia of the chest wall.

This could be the pain symptom of breast cancer: pain accompanied by symptoms such as lumps, inverted nipples, skin orange peel-like changes.

Suspicious Symptom 3: Nipple Discharge - If the nipple discharge is only serous or milky, it is likely breast duct dilation or a benign pituitary tumor in the brain.

This could be the nipple discharge symptom of breast cancer: bloody nipple discharge.
