Beijing police crack 3 major cases of online pornography dissemination - Nanjing SEO Bar

by maomaochaoren on 2010-02-21 09:09:38

Beijing police have cracked down on three major cases of online pornography dissemination, primarily utilizing blogs as the main method.

Since the launch of the special operation by nine ministries to combat and rectify obscene and vulgar information on the Internet and mobile media, due to intensified police crackdowns, new trends have emerged in online obscene activities. The majority of pornographic websites have relocated their servers overseas, while domestic pornographic activities have gradually shifted towards secret operations via QQ groups, blogs, and other network services. The Ministry of Public Security disclosed that the police recently concentrated efforts to crack a series of significant obscene cases with new characteristics.

**Using Blogs to Spread Pornographic Images**

According to reports, Beijing police successfully solved three cases involving the use of blogs for disseminating obscene information, arresting three suspects.

"Recently, we received an online tip-off about a 'NetEase' blog containing obscene content." After verifying the report, the Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau's Cybersecurity Division confirmed that the blog indeed contained obscene materials. They legally ordered NetEase to shut down the blog. Further investigation revealed that this blog frequently referenced content from other blogs, including three named "xiaozhangzuo's Blog," "sexyw's Blog," and "caoyuemei12345's Blog," all of which contained a large amount of obscene content. Police quickly conducted remote evidence collection using technical means, confirming that these blogs met the criteria for criminal prosecution.

Based on the login activity analysis of the three involved blogs, the police identified the locations of the suspects. Collaboratively, the Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau’s Cybersecurity Division worked with the relevant departments of Haidian, Fengtai, and Chaoyang districts to form a special task force. They arrested Zuo (a 33-year-old from Gansu), Zhang (a 26-year-old from Henan), and Shan (a 40-year-old from Shandong).

"I collected over 400 obscene images during my study period in Beijing and posted them on my personal blog," confessed Zuo, who has since been criminally detained by the Haidian branch. Zhang, criminally detained by the Fengtai branch, admitted that between May and December last year, he collected more than 20 obscene images online and posted them on his personal blog, which was clicked over 20,000 times by netizens. Shan confessed that between November and December last year, he posted over 400 obscene images on his blog, which were clicked over 70,000 times by netizens. Shan has now been criminally detained by the Chaoyang branch.

**Profit through VIP Membership on the "Sex Bar" Website**

According to the Ministry of Public Security, over a month ago, cyber-police discovered someone managing the Chinese-language obscene website "Sex8" based overseas. This website is suspected of spreading a large amount of obscene information domestically through recruiting moderators and developing members, profiting illegally through bank transfers and online payments. Preliminary investigations found that the website had 8 main sections, 22 sub-sections, 124 detailed sections, 3.32 million registered members, 2,400 VIP members, 800,000 main posts, and 6.4 million replies, making it one of the largest Chinese-language obscene websites abroad.

Further investigation uncovered the internal organizational structure and system of the website, identifying over 30 main domestic managers across 23 provinces (regions, municipalities). It was determined that there were over 50,000 obscene images, more than 1,500 articles, and over 1,300 films. With sufficient evidence, clear identities of the involved individuals, and a clear understanding of the group's organizational structure, the Ministry of Public Security coordinated a unified deployment. More than 300 police officers from over 50 arrest teams across 23 provinces (regions, municipalities) carried out successive arrest actions, capturing 81 illegal suspects, including the domestic chief moderator Li.

The overseas "Sex8" obscene website profits through VIP membership fees. With 2,400 VIP members, the charges are: quarterly VIP RMB 238, semi-annual VIP RMB 398, annual VIP RMB 796, all transferred via Industrial and Commercial Bank accounts. Additionally, the website provides monthly work subsidies ranging from RMB 300 to RMB 1,500 to 37 managers above the super-moderator level. To date, 62 suspects have been criminally detained, and the police arrest operation is still ongoing.

**Organizing Prostitution via QQ Groups**

In addition, public security authorities also successfully dismantled a major prostitution ring organized online. Recently, cyber-police discovered that someone was using an Internet-based QQ group called "Nightlife" to post information about organizing and introducing women for prostitution. Immediately, the cyber-police organized forces for a comprehensive investigation, discovering that the suspects posted prostitution-related information online and conducted transactions offline. Thus, they adopted a "online-offline" combined approach to gather evidence of the suspects' online prostitution organization behavior, while simultaneously conducting on-site investigations, evidence collection, and surveillance offline to confirm the suspects' real identities and activity trajectories.

After several weeks of continuous work, investigators ultimately determined the location of the QQ group administrator and the prostitutes by combining network technology advantages with traditional investigative methods. Over a month ago, under close cooperation among various police units, they successfully dismantled the crime syndicate using the "Nightlife" QQ group for online solicitation and organizing women for prostitution, arresting the main suspect QQ group administrator Meng, along with assistants Zhang and Liu who helped facilitate and organize prostitution. Currently, Meng has been criminally detained, while Zhang and Liu have been administratively detained and sent for re-education respectively.
