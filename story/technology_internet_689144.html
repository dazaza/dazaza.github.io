The agony of publishing

by dipper3379 on 2012-03-03 18:45:44

It's been a long time since we pulled an all-nighter to release an assignment. Originally, the plan was to release the new website before midnight on the 15th. That morning, everyone worked frantically to finalize the last details. Around 8:00 PM on the 15th, we hoped to finish by 10:00 PM. LY said with great effort, "Let's go home before ten o'clock; it'll be dangerous if we drive later."

Around 10:00 PM on the 15th, we expected to complete everything before midnight on the 16th. I said, "I'm not afraid, I'll just drive home anyway."

Around 1:00 AM on the 16th, we hoped to finish by 3:00 AM. LY said, "It looks like we'll have to spend the night at the company." Around 3:00 AM on the 16th, we aimed to finish by 6:00 AM. LY had collapsed on the sofa, and I couldn't take it anymore so I went downstairs to grab a hat from my car. QT was still energetic sitting in his seat, ZH was impressive as he remained focused despite the fatigue, while poor LU was under immense pressure and couldn't stop working. LH quietly modified static links, and we later found out that he had changed over four hundred in three hours, which earned him some ridicule from ZH because it could have been done in a second using the replace function. It seemed like everyone was dizzy.

Around 6:00 AM on the 16th, we aimed to finish by 9:00 AM before everyone started their normal workday. We no longer anticipated a midnight snack but looked forward to breakfast instead.

Around 9:00 AM on the 16th, we aimed to finish by 10:00 AM. The hope grew stronger as we passed through the drowsy phase, and everyone became energized again, proving how powerful our biological clocks were.

Around 10:00 AM on the 16th, we aimed to finish by noon. DAI sister showed concern and asked if we wanted to eat first. However, we were twelve hours behind schedule, and at the final moment, there was simply no mood for eating.

Around 1:00 PM on the 16th, we finally completed the release. DAI sister told us to go eat, but after a release, there are usually many unstable factors, so we continued to fix small details and bugs without letting our guard down. Small SONG ordered us some milk tea, which was wonderful! We persisted and sent a message to Ah Niu, emphasizing that we had fought for nearly thirty hours. We asked for her help in preparing a good meal, assuring that we would definitely be home by around 3:00 PM.

Around 3:00 PM on the 16th, we expected to finish the page detail adjustments post-release by 4:00 PM. Finally, our stomachs couldn't take it anymore, so we grabbed a quick meal first. We messaged Ah Niu, confirming that dinner wouldn't be a problem.

Around 5:00 PM on the 16th, we expected to finish the page detail adjustments post-release by 6:00 PM. Everyone joked that we had gone from anticipating a midnight snack to breakfast, then lunch, and now could only look forward to dinner. LH's modifications to the right-side static pages drove everyone crazy, and tempers began to rise.

Around 7:00 PM on the 16th, we finally completed the basic adjustments and went home to sleep. QT left first, followed by ZH. Then LU and LH left, leaving only LY and me. I called Ah Niu, guaranteeing that I would be home by 8:00 PM and emphasized that dinner could start being prepared.

Finally, everyone left. On the way, I strongly urged myself to stay alert while driving, fearing any accidents.

At 8:00 PM, I finally returned home, immediately fell into bed, and fell asleep.

At 8:30 PM, my sister violently dragged me up, and I managed to finish two bowls of rice at the table. By 9:00 PM, I returned to bed, deeply asleep. Finally, I rested zzZZZ.
