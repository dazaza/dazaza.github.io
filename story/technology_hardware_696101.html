Industrial growth slows in first four months, adjusting structure under greater pressure

by s3e4k1o8d5 on 2012-03-07 20:35:33

The Slowing Down of Industrial Growth is Internally Reasonable

In the first quarter of this year, the year-on-year growth rate of industrial added value above designated size in our country fell by 5.2 percentage points. In April, the industrial added value above designated size fell by another 1.4 percentage points compared to March. Analyzing from all aspects, the slowing down of industrial growth is internally reasonable. It is not only a normal phenomenon where industrial economy transitions from a significant rebound after the implementation of stimulus plans to steady growth, but also an inevitable result of intensified structural adjustment pressures.

Firstly, the year-on-year decline in industrial added value in the first quarter of this year is inseparable from the relatively high base last year. Secondly, although the global economy continues to recover slowly, uncertain factors have increased. Especially, the earthquake, tsunami, and nuclear radiation disaster in Japan has directly and indirectly affected the supply of key auto and electronic components, which has had a certain impact on our country's industrial production. Thirdly, the pressure of overcapacity has increased. Domestically, with the intensification of real estate regulation, the growth of consumption for appliances, furniture, and building materials related to housing has slowed down. Moreover, with the expiration of car consumption subsidy policies, these have directly and indirectly affected the market demand for some industrial products, increasing the pressure of overcapacity in industries such as steel, building materials, and automobiles, thus affecting the industrial growth rate to a certain extent. Fourthly, the cost of industrial production has risen, and constraints on production factors have intensified. The international market prices of energy and raw materials have soared sharply, and domestic labor costs have continued to rise, creating significant pressure on industrial production and leading to a slowdown in the growth rate of some industries. Due to strong imported inflationary pressures and inflation expectations, monetary authorities have tightened credit and controlled loan scales, leading to rising capital costs and tight funds for some enterprises, especially small and medium-sized enterprises. Meanwhile, due to the rapid development of high-energy-consuming industries, energy supply and demand contradictions have become prominent.

The Pressure of Industrial Structural Adjustment is Increasing

This year marks the beginning of the "12th Five-Year Plan," and the industrial economy faces the dual tasks of maintaining steady and relatively fast growth and accelerating structural adjustments. Judging from the current trend of industrial economic operations, the overall growth of industrial production remains stable, but the growth rate on the demand side has slowed down, the inventory of industrial products has grown faster, and the pressure of overcapacity has increased. Therefore, it is necessary to increase the intensity of structural adjustments and promote the transformation and upgrading of the industrial economy.

To advance industrial structural adjustment, it is necessary to accelerate the elimination of backward production capacity, strengthen enterprise technological transformation, expedite the cultivation and development of strategic emerging industries, reinforce efforts in energy conservation and emissions reduction, and actively expand international markets.

Industrial Growth Will Still Be Steady and Relatively Fast Throughout the Year

Rapid industrial growth plays a very important role in ensuring and increasing supply, expanding employment, and maintaining steady and relatively fast development of the national economy. Looking ahead to the whole year, the steady and relatively fast development of our country's industrial economy faces many favorable conditions.

In terms of investment, the active and steady advancement of urbanization, the expansion of the scale of affordable housing construction, and the encouragement of expanded private investment will maintain a relatively fast growth trend in investment. On the consumption side, the country implements the strategy of expanding domestic demand, making the expansion of resident consumer demand a strategic priority, intensifying income distribution reform, and achieving relatively fast growth in residents' income, which will create conditions for expanding consumer demand. Internationally, as the influence of international sudden events gradually diminishes and the global economy continues to recover, it will drive faster export growth. At the same time, it is expected that the price carry-over factor in the second half of the year will gradually weaken, which will help alleviate inflationary pressure. These factors are conducive to improving the industrial development environment and promoting the continued relatively fast development of the industrial economy, thereby creating more favorable conditions for the steady and relatively fast development of the entire economy throughout the year.

By Wang Yiming (Columnist), Deputy Director of the Macroeconomic Research Institute of the National Development and Reform Commission
