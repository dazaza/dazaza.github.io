I. What causes fungal vaginitis?

Fungal vaginitis (monilial or mycotic vaginitis) is caused by fungal infections. Its incidence has surpassed that of trichomonal vaginitis. In medical terms, fungal infections are referred to as Candida infections; thus, fungal vaginitis is also known as candidal vaginitis. It is commonly seen in young girls, pregnant women, diabetic patients, and postmenopausal women who have been treated with high doses of estrogen.

The main causes are often due to: sexual contact transmission, contaminated clothing and utensils, unqualified sanitary napkins, toilet paper, and panty liners.

There are many types of fungi (fungus), but the most common in the human body belongs to the Candida albicans genus. About 80-90% of vaginal infections are caused by Candida albicans, while the rest are caused by other types of Candida and Tomlopsis. Therefore, fungal vaginitis is essentially candidal vaginitis or vaginal candidiasis. Candida albicans is oval-shaped, forming pseudohyphae from budding spores and elongated cells. Pseudohyphae connect with spores to form branched or chain-like structures. Candida is usually a saprophytic parasite that can live on normal human skin, mucous membranes, digestive tract, or other organs, often existing in the vagina without symptoms. Among non-pregnant women with increased vaginal discharge, about 10% carry this fungus, while about 30% of pregnant women do so. When the amount of glycogen in the vagina increases and acidity rises, or when the body's resistance decreases, it can become a cause of disease. Long-term use of broad-spectrum antibiotics and adrenal corticosteroids can significantly increase the likelihood of fungal infections because these two drugs can lead to microbial imbalance within the body, altering the mutual constraints among microorganisms in the vagina and reducing the ability to fight infections. Additionally, vitamin deficiencies (complex vitamin B), severe infectious diseases, and other consumptive diseases can all become favorable conditions for the proliferation of Candida albicans. During pregnancy, the content of glycogen in vaginal epithelial cells increases, enhancing the acidity of the vagina. Moreover, pregnant women have a lower renal glucose threshold, often resulting in nutritional glycosuria, which increases sugar content in urine and promotes the growth and reproduction of Candida albicans.

II. How is fungal vaginitis treated?

Basic treatment

1. Altering the pH of the vagina, such as using alkaline medications to rinse the vagina. Sodium bicarbonate solution (2-4%) can be used to rinse the vagina, thereby changing the living environment of fungi.

2. Medication treatment

(1) Locally applying compound nystatin cold cream twice daily.

(2) Oral ketoconazole (ketoconazole) 400mg, twice daily for five days.

(3) Trichomycin (trichomycin), see treatment for trichomonal vaginitis. Alternatively, oral clotrimazole (clotrimazole) or chlorobenzyl imidazole 0.5-1g, three times daily, or local use of 1-5% ointment or cream agent or lotion, three to four times daily, also shows good efficacy.

(4) Wantong Xiaomi suppository, vaginal medication, once daily, one tablet per dose, three boxes per course, no side effects, no recurrence.

(5) Local application of 1%-2% gentian violet solution is an age-old therapy, highly effective and widely used, but it may stain underwear. (Currently, the state has banned the use of gentian violet drugs due to their strong carcinogenic properties, so caution is advised.)

If a pregnant woman suffers from fungal vaginitis, there is a possibility of spontaneous recovery after childbirth, but there is a risk of infection in newborns, so timely treatment is still necessary, preferably with local medication as described above.

3. Male carriers must also undergo routine treatment, which is one of the important measures to prevent recurrence in female patients.

Microwave treatment

Microwave treatment for fungal vaginitis can improve local blood circulation. Through the benign stimulation of warmth, local nutrition and metabolism are significantly improved, promoting the absorption and dissipation of pathological products, promoting tissue regeneration and repair, and strengthening the compensatory function of undamaged vessels, improving tissue hypoxia, facilitating the absorption of inflammation, and quickly eliminating inflammation.
