Someone often uses the word "magnificent" to describe the luxury and splendor of the host's house. In fact, the pomp described by the word "luxury" does not count as true grandeur! Home decoration all in pure gold, steps in marble, etc.; is the extravagance provided by money considered as grandeur? The answer is definitely no. Then, in what aspects does the grandeur of the homeowner's house usually manifest?

According to traditional sayings, grandeur generally manifests in the facade of the house. In ancient society, some high-ranking officials and nobles would place two mighty stone lions at the entrance of their courtyards to indicate their prominent status. The doors were painted red with a certain number of door nails, and this was considered grandeur. This was done to showcase their social status and a certain amount of power, thus achieving an intimidating effect on the facade!

The grandeur that the ancients focused on was just the facade of a house. But they ignored the truly effective staircase! The main door outside was seen by most people, while the staircase inside did not receive much attention or importance from most people. In fact, in highlighting the level of grandeur; the staircase plays its due role second only to the facade. But grandeur does not equate to luxury. These two concepts absolutely cannot be confused together! For example, the imperial palaces of the Ming and Qing dynasties, the Beijing Forbidden City. Apart from showcasing grandeur on the palace entrance facade, the most important aspect was inside the palace. Some staircases, columns, and railings vividly displayed the grandeur of the deep palace courtyard. Speaking of the construction materials used to build this palace, most of them were made of stone and wood, at most some high-quality stone and wood! However, this project mostly owes its success to the designers and builders, who created the miracle of the Forbidden City lasting hundreds of years with their masterful stone carving and wood carving arts.

Therefore, through such a case, it can be sufficiently proven that indoor layout far surpasses the grandeur of the facade. The staircase, in a sense, is the soul of a family. Where to install the staircase? How to design it? How to install it? These are all big questions, with great considerations! Generally speaking, can installing the staircase indoors highlight grandeur? It ultimately depends on whether the designer's plan is successfully implemented! Not just design. For example, selecting materials, choosing materials, etc. Moreover, these materials cannot be blindly adopted; there are multiple factors involved. Before formulating the renovation plan, we must also consider whether the combination and use of building materials are appropriate. For example, what kind of house suits what style! What type of house fits what kind of staircase! This not only requires judgment from the designer but also suitability to the homeowner's personal style.

Finally, let's talk about how the staircase can display its grandeur. In fact, to highlight the grandeur of the staircase, one must start from the selection of materials. The modern fashion trend is increasingly prevalent, and after the reform and opening up, European and American cultures have gradually influenced China. Western-style clothes, western-style tableware, etc. Staircases are no exception. In recent years, due to most people pursuing European elegance, they naturally ignore Chinese architecture and culture. Materials like stainless steel, glass, crystal have replaced wood! Most of these homeowners pursue fashion, elegance, distinctiveness, and other factors. Due to the prevalence of these building materials, they naturally lack grandeur and magnificence. Actually, the material that truly shows the grandeur of a staircase is still wood. This is not only a traditional building material but also a superior living realm and quality pursued by urban people.

Of course, choosing wooden materials as components for staircase construction cannot completely release the grandeur. Still, the key lies in the performance of the designer and builder! Generally, the grand and magnificent solid wood staircase styles are mostly carved with great care. As the saying goes, "Jade unpolished is not a fine object!" Even the best and most perfect materials need the ingenuity of artists. Generally speaking, to highlight the grandeur of the house; the staircase should always be located right in the center of the house. Only then can guests feel the imposing momentum of the house when they enter! Thereby displaying the homeowner's prestige!
