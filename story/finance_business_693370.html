What should be done if there is bleeding after cervical polyp surgery?

Experts say that judging from the symptoms of postoperative bleeding after cervical polyps surgery, postoperative vaginal discharge is normal. However, if there is bleeding, it may be due to postoperative bleeding, infectious bleeding or bleeding caused by scab shedding. Do you have any discomfort symptoms? If the bleeding does not stop, it is recommended that you go to the hospital for examination as soon as possible to clarify the cause of the disease. Pay attention to cleanliness and hygiene within one month after treatment, and abstain from sexual life. One month later, go to the hospital for a review of the recovery situation and confirm the symptoms of postoperative bleeding after cervical polyps surgery. Try to avoid eating raw cold, spicy, pungent and other stimulating foods in daily life.

The main symptoms of cervical polyps are increased leukorrhea and yellowing of leukorrhea. However, some women may experience bleeding. Generally, bleeding is mainly divided into two situations: contact bleeding in the vagina and bloody leukorrhea. Many women who have cervical polyps and still engage in sexual intercourse may experience symptoms of contact bleeding in the vagina, or blood streaks in the leukorrhea may appear as bloody leukorrhea. There will be a certain amount of bleeding after cervical polyps surgery, so infection is likely to occur. Therefore, it is necessary to do a good job in nursing after cervical polyps surgery:

1. After cervical polyps surgery, patients should take medicine on time and have regular check-ups. Patients need to take anti-infection drugs according to the doctor's requirements, and must not stop taking or adjust the dosage arbitrarily. Given the high recurrence rate of this disease, it is best for patients to have a follow-up examination every 2 months to reduce subsequent effects.

2. Pay attention to rest and avoid overwork after surgery. Although cervical polyps surgery is very simple, with short operation time and minimal trauma, patients still need to pay attention to let the body recover to normal levels as much as possible, so as to prevent pathogens from invading when the patient's immunity is low.

3. After cervical polyps surgery, the patient's diet should be light and nutritious. Generally, postoperative patients can choose some easily digestible foods rich in vitamins, such as tomatoes and apples. Spicy, stimulating, greasy, and high-sugar foods are not conducive to wound healing, so patients are advised to eat less or not at all.

4. After surgery, patients should pay attention to hygiene and suspend sexual life. After the cervical polyps are removed, the cervical wound will gradually be covered by new cell tissue and then return to normal. During this period, patients should absolutely abstain from sex to avoid stimulation of the cervical wound by semen and sexual intercourse, which may affect its healing. To prevent infection, it is best for patients to rinse the vulva with clear water daily, change underwear frequently, and avoid bacteria entering the vagina.

There will be a certain amount of bleeding after cervical surgery, which may lead to bleeding. If the bleeding gradually decreases and the blood color is normal without accompanying discomfort, there is no need to worry too much, considering it as a normal phenomenon. If the bleeding is heavy and continuous, the color turns black, and there is abdominal pain and other discomfort, it may be caused by cervical or uterine damage, infection, etc. It is recommended to seek medical attention as soon as possible. Wound bleeding may continue, or it may be intermittent. There is no need to worry too much. First, observe for a while. If the bleeding is heavy, it is recommended to go to the hospital for examination. Pay attention to personal hygiene and prevent infection.
