The first leader of Manchester United's defense has seen his value surge by four times, while a Barcelona mainstay was merely his substitute last year.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 17:07:18

The leader of Manchester United's defense has seen his value skyrocket fourfold, while a key player for Barcelona was merely his substitute last year. According to Sina Sports, in his 110th league start, Vidic finally scored his 9th league goal for Manchester United. Back in January, Vidic had consecutively scored in the league, and after such a long drought of league goals, he finally ended the year with a strong finish. This goal may be symbolic, as it affirms that the Serbian is undoubtedly the first among equals in Manchester United’s backline, unmatched by anyone.

Including cup matches, Vidic's last goal was in the Champions League round of 16 against Inter Milan, where he powerfully headed the ball past Vieira's defense. Today, against Wolves, a similar move was showcased - a commanding header from the front post, despite an average angle but with immense power, leaving Hannemann powerless even though he touched the ball. This was Vidic's fifth attempt at scoring this season, and he finally succeeded. This goal once again proves the absolute advantage the Serbian possesses in aerial duels. In fact, before Rooney scored from the penalty spot, it was due to Vidic's powerful challenge that forced the opposing defender into a handball foul. Thus, in this match, Vidic created two goals. Ferguson praised Vidic as the most dangerous player on set pieces for the entire team.

Due to minor injuries throughout this season, Vidic has only played 16 games for Manchester United, missing 10. With other defenders also suffering from various injuries, Ferguson has been forced to make significant changes in the central defensive line. After Rio Ferdinand's long absence due to back injury, any issue with Vidic would severely affect the confidence of Manchester United's defensive line. Therefore, whenever Vidic plays, the defensive confidence increases significantly. We can see in the game that because of Vidic's presence, young Darron Gibson dared to break forward on the right flank, knowing someone was covering behind him, and Carrick also played effortlessly. Of course, this was not a particularly difficult game, and Vidic's leadership was more prominently displayed. Don't forget, he was the captain of the starting lineup, with Ferguson's trust fully expressed through that small armband.

Recently, there have been rumors of some friction between Vidic and the club, with his agent demanding a salary increase to 100,000 pounds per week. However, with two and a half years remaining on his contract, the club will not act so early, and even by summer when there are still two years left, the club might still take no action. Since the upper management insists on starting renewal negotiations only when the contract enters its final year, there is a risk of being poached by other major clubs. Despite Vidic showing no outward dissatisfaction, it is hard for him to control his freely acting agent. If Barcelona offers a lucrative contract, it is hard to say Vidic wouldn't be tempted. Pique, who became an absolute starter for Barcelona's central defense, was Vidic's first substitute until last summer. Barcelona would certainly be willing to pursue a deal for the top defender.

However, it seems that external speculation aside, Vidic indeed values everything currently happening. Recently, he stated that he only focuses on Manchester United and does not think about the World Cup, which is still over half a year away. He said: "I don't want to think too far ahead; sometimes the only thing you care about is your injury. The best way is to focus on the next game and keep going, that's all. There is still plenty of time before the World Cup, so I am fully focused on playing for Manchester United." Such statements are what every Manchester United fan wants to hear.

Before the game against Wolves, Vidic urged his teammates to secure three points. In an interview with the official website, he said: "This is our best opportunity to play beautifully, score a few goals, and bring back confidence. When you lose a game, it's important to win the next one immediately. So you need to play better and make up for the mistakes made in the previous game." Clearly, he and his teammates achieved their goal. A player like him, who maintains a strong aerial presence at both ends of the box, as long as he is healthy and fully committed without distractions, could easily overpower teams like Wolves with the prowess of last season's Premier League MVP.

When Vidic joined Manchester United, his transfer fee was 7 million pounds, already quite high for a central defender. But after these few years of development, according to Transfermarkt's valuation, Vidic's current worth is around 35 million euros, or 31.15 million pounds, a more than fourfold increase in just four years. These numbers speak for themselves. It can be foreseen that Manchester United will eventually meet his salary demands, placing him in the top income bracket of the Premier League, determined by Vidic's superior performance and dominance on the field.
