Reverberating among the treetops

by wpnxiaoyans on 2011-09-19 18:28:58

The rainwater on the road wiggled slightly, as if the tranquility highlighted the slenderness of the drizzle. I looked up and already my shoes were wet and my clothes cold. The leftover rumors brought chill, and the icy sensation around me awakened the sorrow in my blood. Suddenly, I began to detest the nakedness of this early spring which revealed too many bitter memories. I tightly wrapped myself in my thin clothes and gazed at the dampened sky, standing foolishly in the rain.

Strands of rain slid quietly along the eaves into the mundane world, rippling with heartbroken waves. The rain sounds cut by the wind, shattered delicately, embracing the rhythm of my heartbeat, leaving lingering echoes. The dark green tiles dreamt aimlessly without a tune. Who could uncover this icy rhythm? At this moment, the sky seemed to have lost its color.

Perhaps, living alone for too long makes one accustomed to the solitary reflection on the water's surface. Drinking cup after cup of solitude, letting time bloom with cold indifference. Fireworks easily turn cold, gazing brings sorrow. Listening to the elegant sadness in light music, tears uncontrollably leave the past behind. With every trembling step taken, it felt like stepping towards an end. Occasionally looking back, seeing those footprints swallowed by the rain, the heavens betrayed my departure, bringing me sorrow as if I were two different people.

A cool breeze sways through the tree tips, like flying fluff, wandering under the slanted moonlight at the willow ends. Quietly standing under the midnight moonlight, alone hugging this bone-chilling cold, holding hands with loneliness, I lower my head and gently kiss the melancholy in this drizzle.

Through the soaking of a gentle rain, I was completely unaware that my life had been shortened in each season, those cold greens shedding from the branches overnight, fading away all my passion.

It’s been a long time since I last saw the moonlight I loved, indifferently watching day after day that didn’t belong to me. The thin blanket on the bed couldn't contain the emptiness of my soul. Accustomed to waking up in the middle of the night, touching those forgotten old songs, seeking memories in their lingering and entwining rhythms. Looking at familiar figures in photos, I enjoy immersing myself in this insomnia-induced seclusion, mending the desolation left by the passing of youthful years. Those gray memories or plots seem to be trying to hurt my temperature thoroughly. I missed every beautiful moment, perhaps only one sentence can make you inexplicably excited; also experiencing a very long lifetime, making you remember and suddenly realize might only be a fleeting moment. However, this moment stretched out for a very, very long time...

If I leave, there may be no reunion, just because there is no waiting on this shore, leaving a seemingly carefree silhouette, continuing to pursue that dream without an ending...

The wheel of fate still rolls on my fading youth, ancient worries accumulate in my chest. I feel myself beginning to fall apart and grow old, just like these ancient words I cannot settle, while the wandering footprints are stranded in this repetitive brilliance, tracing a splendid poem spanning past and future lives. This night, I still savor that penetrating sadness, unrelated to time and space, just like enjoying chasing those disheveled yet rational words. But sometimes, after looking for too long, why do I cry? Perhaps, as others say, living alone for too long really becomes addictive...

In the fleeting time, how many silhouettes, how many memories pass by like sightseeing on horseback, brushing over my confused eyes. Those fragmented images shrink faintly in my pupils, that distant warmth still narrates regret. It seems like I am lying in winter snow, singing that endless song in every quiet night.

In my world, there is no goodbye, just like the initial departure, only a graceful turn, so elegant, so straightforward. What remains are just those faint beauties and severed strings of affection.

It seems like it has been a long time since I last experienced such delicate feelings, the coolness enveloped by the warm spring, swirling through dim eyes onto a blurred heart tip. Forgotten by the roadside, loneliness stubbornly bursts in the rain, as if also listening to my soaked heart. Watching the flowers freely shed tears, oxygen-deficient memories blossom faintly, those heart-piercing stories still wander in the air.
