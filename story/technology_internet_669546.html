Hebei Provincial Development and Reform Commission learned that in 2011, Hebei Province's economy maintained a steady and relatively fast development momentum. The trend of excessively rapid price increase was curbed. Steps to improve people's livelihood were accelerated, and the harmony of social and economic development was continuously strengthened.

In 2011, Hebei Province’s GDP growth rate was 11.3%, and investment growth rate showed signs of decline. In 2011, the province's economic growth rate slightly decreased but still remained at a relatively fast pace. Last year, the national total demand growth rate moderately declined, mainly due to the active macro-control of national policies, which is beneficial for achieving the policy balance of controlling excessively rapid price increases and maintaining stable economic operation. From the perspective of Hebei Province, the GDP growth rate in 2011 increased by 11.3% on the basis of a high base of 12.2% in 2010, maintaining a steady and relatively fast growth momentum. Although it was lower than the average annual growth rate of 11.7% during the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" period, it was still within a stable and relatively fast growth range. From the perspective of demand, in 2011, investment growth rate showed signs of decline, except for slight increases in May and October, the monthly cumulative growth rate of fixed asset investment dropped from 27.8% in January to 24.1% for the whole year. Social consumption was relatively active, with the cumulative growth rate of the total retail sales of consumer goods reaching 16.4%, 17%, 17.5%, and 17.8% in the first quarter, first half, first three quarters, and the whole year respectively. Export volume continued to expand, with export value reaching $28.58 billion, increasing by 26.7%.

In 2011, Hebei Province's tourism revenue exceeded 200 billion yuan. In 2011, structural adjustments in Hebei Province were actively promoted, and operational quality continued to improve. From an industrial perspective, in 2011, the added value of large-scale industries in the province reached 1,050.94 billion yuan, increasing by 16.1% year-on-year. Among them, the added value of equipment manufacturing increased by 24.9%, 8.8 percentage points faster than the province's industry, contributing 26.1% to economic growth. The added value of high-tech industries increased by about 25%. Modern services developed well, with financial insurance, modern logistics, and other high-end service sectors developing rapidly, and tourism revenue breaking through 200 billion yuan. From a benefit perspective, profit growth rate of large-scale industries began to accelerate monthly starting from July, growing by 34.8% for the whole year. Total fiscal revenue and local general budget revenue grew by 25.4% and 30.4% respectively, increasing by 6.1 and 5.7 percentage points year-on-year. From a regional perspective, construction of the Green Economic Circle around the capital city accelerated, with fixed asset investment growth rate being 5 percentage points higher than the provincial average; the quality of industrial development in coastal areas improved, with profit growth rate of large-scale industries being 13.5 percentage points higher than the provincial average.

The excessively rapid price increase trend in Hebei Province was curbed. In 2011, initial results of price control in Hebei Province were seen, and the excessively rapid price increase trend was curbed. The monthly growth rate of the overall level of consumer prices reached its peak of 7.4% in July. Affected by factors such as reduced base effect, improved monetary conditions, and declining commodity prices, the growth rate showed a downward trend, reaching 4.7% in December. On one hand, the monetary conditions for controlling prices continued to improve. In 2011, the national broad money supply (M2) grew by 17.3%, narrow money supply (M1) grew only by 8.7%, and RMB credit balance grew by 14%. Compared with 18.9%, 20.4%, and 19.9% in 2010, the liquidity compression trend was quite noticeable, and domestic monetary and credit control targets were achieved as expected. On the other hand, imported inflationary pressure somewhat weakened. Due to the impact of the European and American debt crises, global economic growth significantly slowed down. Coupled with market risk aversion effects, the dollar rebounded in the short term, and crude oil, iron ore, and other commodity prices fell to some extent, leading to a downward trend in the national import trade index year-on-year. Another aspect was that food price growth stabilized and declined. In 2011, Hebei Province's grain production achieved an "eight consecutive increase", reaching 63.45 billion catties, which helped stabilize grain and food prices; pork prices fell from a high of 16 yuan per jin in September 2011 to around 14 yuan per jin by the end of the year. Plus, other consumption category prices fell to varying degrees, resulting in a 5.7% increase in the province's residents' consumer prices compared to the previous year.

In 2011, Hebei Province solved drinking water safety issues for 37.74 million rural residents. In 2011, steps to improve people's livelihood in Hebei Province accelerated, and the harmony of social and economic development continuously strengthened. In 2011, the disposable income per urban resident was 18,292.2 yuan, and the net income per rural resident was 7,120 yuan, increasing by 12.5% and 19.5% respectively, with the growth rate accelerating by 2 and 3.8 percentage points year-on-year. Medical and health system reforms progressed smoothly, with all government-run grassroots medical institutions implementing zero markup sales of essential drugs, and township health centers initially establishing new management systems and operating mechanisms. Urban employment increased by 700,000 people, achieving 104.5% of the annual target task; the urban registered unemployment rate was 3.8%, controlled within the target range of 4.6%. The number of people participating in basic old-age insurance in urban areas was 10.6 million, exceeding the target by 300,000 people, and the pilot scale of the new rural social old-age insurance continued to expand. Construction started on 385,000 sets of affordable housing and shantytown renovation housing, successfully completing the tasks assigned by the state. Drinking water safety issues for 37.74 million rural residents were resolved. Meanwhile, education, science and technology, culture, and other social undertakings also made significant progress.
