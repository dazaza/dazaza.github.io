Why did Baidu Have Ah fail?

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 12:02:44

Why did Baidu Ya close down? I'm sure many online marketing professionals are asking this question today. Let's talk about why Baidu Ya closed down!

1. First, let's analyze Baidu Ya's competitors. Taobao from Alibaba, Paipai from Tencent, Joyo, Dangdang, and JD.com can all be considered as Baidu Ya's competitors. Of course, we can separate Taobao, Paipai, Joyo, and Dangdang from JD.com as vertical e-commerce platforms. However, there is no doubt that when Baidu officially announced its entry into the e-commerce platform and entered the B2C market in 2007, the domestic e-commerce market was already very hot. Whether it was Taobao, which implemented a ban on crawling by Baidu spiders, or Tencent, which was both a friend and an enemy of Baidu, they really wanted this new "kid" to die before he grew up. Or maybe saying it this way is a bit inappropriate, but we can confirm one thing: Taobao and Paipai did not want Baidu's own e-commerce platform to appear on the domestic e-commerce stage. They would also think that in the future, it might be a tripartite division of the world among them, and Baidu's search advantage cannot be underestimated!

2. Now let's take a look at what actions Baidu itself took for its new child. Which ones promoted the development of Baidu Ya, and which ones hindered the development of Baidu Ya?

Baidu completed a series of improvements to products that imitated Taobao, including Youa Shop, Youa Mall, Youa Shop Assistant, Youa Consumer Protection Service, and relatively more important, Baifubao. For those familiar with Taobao, these may sound very familiar. Similarly, the Paipai Assistant and Tenpay also seem familiar. Regardless of how you look at it, this series of actions by Baidu played an undeniable role in the development of Baidu Ya and were necessary steps to improve Baidu's e-commerce strategy platform. Of course, from the perspective of search, under equal conditions, Baidu gave a significant weight to Youa products in the search results. At that time, many SEO enthusiasts conducted tests and believed that Baidu gave a lot of support to Youa. I think that Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., should have many friends who tested this and deeply felt it, and many people took advantage of this and got a lot of benefits.

Now let's look at some problems encountered in the development of Baidu Ya:

(1) The promotion of Baifubao became a major obstacle to the development of Baidu Ya. Since Taobao and Alipay had already occupied an absolute leading position domestically, and Paipai and Tenpay from Tencent also tightly utilized the huge user base of QQ to occupy a place, if Baidu Ya wanted to occupy a place in the e-commerce platform, it must first solve the issues related to payment for its users. It's impossible to support Alipay, Tenpay, or online banking. But until yesterday, Baifubao was not truly pushed towards the vast network shopping population.

(2) Baidu Ya was "born at the wrong time"!

Abroad, the e-commerce market is basically dominated by two players, eBay and Amazon. One is C2C, the other is B2C.

Baidu only began to formally enter the C2C e-commerce market in 2008, compared to Taobao which was founded in 2003, Baidu has obviously missed the best opportunity to enter the C2C market. A five-year lead advantage, without obvious technological and resource advantages, makes the probability of Baidu defeating Taobao or even breaking Taobao's monopoly almost negligible. There is Taobao, which firmly occupies more than 80% of the market share, and nearby, there are Paipai and EachNet dividing the remaining more than 10% of the market share. The C2C path chosen by Baidu is undoubtedly a dangerous one.

(3) Baidu Ya should focus on capturing a large number of mature online shopping users.

Baidu Ya should focus on capturing a large number of mature online shopping users, but personally, I think Baidu did not remember this point. As mentioned in (2), Baidu Ya was "born at the wrong time," so it should optimize based on its strengths and weaknesses. Because Baidu Ya is a new platform, if many people familiar with online shopping dare not use it due to unfamiliarity, then how can we talk about the great strategic development of Youa.

3. What should Baidu Ya do next?

According to the latest news seen on the official website of Baidu Ya, adjustments will begin to be made to the Baidu Ya business. The mall business of Youa will be transferred to partners such as Rakuten and Yaodian100 in a planned manner. Subsequent work will also be quickly carried out. Whether for sellers or buyers, I believe Baidu will provide an explanation.

Personally, I think domestic local enterprises should aim for global expansion rather than dominating the entire country. Any enterprise will go through a period of expansion. The B2C leader Amazon, Taobao with both C2C and B2C, and the continuously growing shopping search + B2C model might be a very good choice in the future. We can see this from Google's continuous support and increased investment in shopping search!
