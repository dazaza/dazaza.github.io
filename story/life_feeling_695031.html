Love for Old Bookstores

by gege on 2012-03-07 13:56:19

The Romance of the Old Bookstore

One

A place so old that it can forget time, a place so quiet that only the heartbeats of two people can be heard, a space hiding all romance. The old bookstore standing quietly at the street corner, time passed by lightly, forgetting its essence, silence. Along the way, the yellowing leaves of French plane trees fell and twirled with the wind, landing on the palm, still carrying the heat of summer. The sky wasn't very blue, but it didn't affect the mood, Anruo thought so.

The emptiness and boredom brought by the end of high school filled Anruo's life these days. She wanted to go out for a walk, to a faraway place, turn off all communication methods, then quietly appreciate the scenery that only she understood, which might not look beautiful to others. Wandering on the street, there was no noisy traffic here, few pedestrians, only a few pigeons flying lightly over the plane trees in the sky. Just by looking up, you could see them.

Old-fashioned wooden windows, yellowed signs, wind chimes hanging on the door, and the blue mountain bike parked at the entrance, it was somewhat like an oil painting, impressionistic. This was the old bookstore, so old that no one remembered its name, nor did anyone care about such forgetting. Anruo looked up at the lamp on the lintel at the entrance, covered with dust, yet seemed to give a warm feeling. Gently pushing the wooden door, it creaked, no one responded. Three tall bookshelves reaching the ceiling divided the space, there was a smell, from the books, Anruo took a deep breath, a very intoxicating feeling, a historical feeling.

The books stood quietly, waiting, for someone who could read them, someone who could release them. Anruo's fingers gently traced the titles of the books, various fonts either upright or lying down, exaggeratedly attracting the eyes. A very simple book, yellowed paper seemingly with handwritten fonts, "The Romance of the Old Bookstore", the paper was light, flipping page by page, very comfortable. This was a book of unknown age, the content was roughly about the love between two protagonists in the old bookstore, the story was not long, but the words were very elegant. Anruo seemed to feel that this story was not fictional, time might make it come true.

Anruo leaned against the bookshelf, reading page by page. The autumn sunlight poured through the glass onto Anruo's fingers, dancing lightly with the book. At this moment, the wind chime rang, Anruo instinctively pulled her hand back, hiding the book in her chest. The man who walked in wearing a baseball cap, holding a stack of thick old books, couldn't be seen clearly in the sunlight. Anruo turned around, hiding behind a pillar next to the bookshelf. The man walked in with heavy steps, placed the books on a small wooden table, took off his hat, wiped the sweat with his arm, then shook his hair. At this moment, the sunlight entering from the crack just shone on his face, a very delicate face, profound eyes carrying an indescribable sadness, and some curly hair, sweat was still evaporating. Judging from his clothing, a loose white shirt covering a black T-shirt, wearing faded jeans, estimated his age was about the same as hers.

Anruo put the book neatly in place, preparing to leave. The man said, "Don't you want to stay a while longer? I also like that book, from its content to its binding." Anruo paused, a little hesitant, but still turned back, took out the book, sat on a rattan chair in the corner of the wall. The man was cutting pages, page by page seriously. Under the light, he wore black-rimmed glasses, playing freely with a pair of scissors in his hands. This old bookstore couldn't say it had been decorated, using books as decorations was too extravagant, a world map hung on the wall, with many red dots marked on the Mediterranean coast. It must be a tourist attraction, Anruo thought so.

"This is the bookstore my grandfather left me, a building from the 60s, I didn't even know where I was back then," the man smiled involuntarily, "the books here are all sold here when others think they have no place to put them, I like organizing books, owning books. What about you?" Anruo suddenly felt a bit at a loss when asked, "I just like this old feeling, forgetting time, forgetting everything, being able to communicate with books." Anruo looked up at him, her eyes drifted elsewhere. "Haha, I feel the same way, oh, forgot to introduce myself, I'm Wuteng, a freshman in college, temporarily helping out here." "I'm Anruo, I just happened to pass by here." Anruo still dared not take a good look at Wuteng, because this was the senior she had secretly loved for three years, meeting him so coincidentally in such a big world was enough for Anruo.

That day's sunset was very beautiful, lingering at the edge of the distant mountains as if unwilling to set, Anruo stepped on the fiery red sunlight scattered on the ground, her heart was also fiery red.

At night, there was a silent breeze blowing on the balcony, carrying the scent of autumn, stirring the curtains, also stirring the slightly budding emotions in Anruo's heart at this moment. The moon is how many people's lover, under this lonely night, those with heart look at the moon with tears in their eyes, those without heart step on the moonlight walking briskly, taking away a bit of attachment, regardless.

Two

The next day, Anruo passed by the street corner, the blue mountain bike lay quietly on the ground, she thought Wuteng should be there. A little hesitant, but driven by something unknown, she still pushed the wooden door open, the falling paint covered her hands, Anruo lightly patted her hands. Passed by the bookshelf. Wuteng turned his head, could easily find her, moved a chair next to the bookshelf, said sit down and read. Anruo politely replied thank you. Thus, the time in the old bookstore could stop everything, regardless of the noise of the outside world. Unconsciously it was evening again, Anruo got up, closed "The Romance of the Old Bookstore" and put it back in its original place, preparing to leave. Wuteng waved at her, his head still low. Pointed to a photo on the corner of the wooden table for her to see. This photo looked somewhat old, there was a man and a woman in the picture, the man was pushing a bicycle, the woman was walking beside him, the sunset was right in front of them, a very warm feeling. "This is my grandfather and grandmother, that was a 27-inch bicycle, now it's still lying in the storage room downstairs, grandfather liked books and bicycles, which was exactly passed on to me. Since the summer vacation started, I liked to ride far during the day, and indulge in the bookstore at night." Anruo listened intently, for a moment didn't realize Wuteng had finished speaking, Wuteng patted her, "Oh, originally it was like this." Anruo pretended to understand and nodded, Wuteng wanted to laugh but held it back.

It was very late, the lamp at the entrance covered with dust emitted a dim yellow light, with a few moths flying around. The autumn night was a bit cool, the short sleeves worn during the day couldn't resist the slight cold now, Anruo unconsciously hugged her arms. Wuteng could be considered a thoughtful person, quickly took off his white shirt and handed it to Anruo, Anruo kept saying no need no need, but Wuteng insisted on giving it to her. In the end, she had no choice but to accept it.

White shirt, dim yellow light, romantic, do they necessarily connect? Anruo was thinking.

Night always makes people think a lot, letting you have nowhere to hide in the darkness, exposing your feelings. Wuteng finished cutting and pasting the last page, sighed deeply, stretched, stood up, stood in front of the world map, "Today should be here, Rome, there are only a few more stops before coming back." Wuteng pointed at Rome on the map and muttered to himself.

Three

His, also doesn't count as a girlfriend, he confessed, but she didn't react much, so they continued as friends. Back then, they agreed to travel around the Mediterranean together, but it ended up being Wuteng's fault, studying was too intense, leaving him no time. So the girl went with Wuteng's best friend instead, Wuteng knew, maybe she liked his friend. They first flew directly to the UK, then traveled throughout the UK, to France, and then, station by station according to the map markings. Initially, the girl would send back some photos, of landscapes, of their group photos, as the journey progressed, the distance between the man and woman in the photos became closer, the intimacy grew, Wuteng thought, this was the end. Thereafter, no more photos were sent. Wuteng began to peacefully cut and paste books in the bookstore.

All of these Anruo knew nothing about, she saw a photo posted on the map at the UK location, it was a group photo of Wuteng and a girl. They seemed to have a good relationship. Anruo didn't want to meddle. She had already gotten used to secret love, wouldn't express herself actively.

Days passed by like this, the time spent in the bookstore seemed very short, from turning the pages to closing the book, it was always so fast. Books might be a black hole, here time seemed insignificant, ancient writings are still so clear now. Let it flow, change perspectives, it's still the same. But what remains unchanged is just the books, people, because of being human possess characteristics that matter doesn't have, after being together for a long time, a strange feeling arises in the heart, pulling each other closer, henceforth inseparable.

Anruo went to the old bookstore every day, then went out with Wuteng. Wuteng added a rack at the back of the mountain bike, carrying Anruo to watch the sunset on the hill at dusk, then walking back together. From the back view, it was as romantic as the photo.

Unconsciously, snow began to fall, whitening the hills behind, filling the streets. Even the lamp previously covered with dust was newly decorated. Anruo liked snow, enthusiastically built a snowman at the door, then used a bucket as a hat, a broom as hands, and even wrapped her own scarf around it. Wuteng stood inside, watching through the glass, smiling sincerely.

A couple walked towards them, hand in hand, laughing and talking. Anruo watched them and suddenly felt like she forgot something, so she ran into the bookstore, forcibly pulling Wuteng to take a group photo. Randomly finding a passerby, Anruo leaned on Wuteng, holding his left hand. Click, one shot, Anruo insisted on taking a few more, Wuteng had no choice but to comply, actually willingly. Some leaning, some playing around. When taking the last one, Wuteng's smile was somewhat unnatural. Anruo didn't notice.

They came back, Wuteng didn't expect them to visit here again. Holding hands made Wuteng feel a little heartache, but it wasn't as excruciating as before. Anruo noticed that Wuteng was a bit off, so she came over and held Wuteng's hand, Wuteng didn't pay attention, still standing there dazedly. They came to give some souvenirs, Wuteng could only pretend to be completely unbothered. After exchanging a few words with them, the girl pointed at Anruo and asked Wuteng, "Is she your girlfriend? She's so cute!" "Yeah, I love her very much." Saying this sentence, Wuteng felt his heart warmed, perhaps because Anruo was standing next to him. After a brief exchange, they bid farewell, Anruo didn't know what was going on, insistently nagged Wuteng to ask, Wuteng went into the bookstore, found a letter mailed from Greece last autumn, handed it to Anruo, Anruo gently opened it, reading line by line, Wuteng rubbed his eyes. Anruo calmly handed the letter back to Wuteng, didn't say a word, just came over, tightly hugging Wuteng, very firmly.

"It was her who missed you, not you who missed her, now it's my turn, I don't want to lose you, forget her." Wuteng didn't respond, just held Anruo tighter.

Snowflakes started to fall again outside the window, pure white like angels, Anruo made a wish to these angels.

The old bookstore thus quietly stood at the street corner, time couldn't take it away, nor could it take away this quiet love. Quietly sprouting.
