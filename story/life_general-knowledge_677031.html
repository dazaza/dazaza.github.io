Good living habits -- four essential good habits to do after getting up!

A. Taking a morning shower is a good habit

Taking a morning shower is generally not a Chinese habit, but it indeed helps promote better blood circulation. The feeling of cleanliness and the fragrance of shower gel can also adjust one's mood and make one more energetic.

B. Having a bowel movement in the morning is a good habit

Once this good habit is formed, you will benefit from it for life. If you don't have this habit yet, don't worry. You should try to cultivate it. For example, eat more high-fiber foods like cabbage, sweet potatoes and other coarse grains. Also, go to the bathroom every morning whether you feel the need or not. Over time, this effort will become a natural habit.

C. Waking up slowly in the morning is a good habit

Sudden rising is usually only done when necessary. Try not to get up too quickly as it can cause the blood to rush upwards, resulting in sudden changes in blood pressure that may lead to dizziness and other symptoms. A better approach is, after opening your eyes, stay in bed for a moment and move your limbs and head around for about five minutes before getting up. If you have trouble waking up, you can close your eyes and stretch your limbs which will greatly help with becoming alert. For example: bend your knees one by one and push them towards the opposite side of your body; use both hands to hug one knee to your chest for 10 seconds; lie on your back and stretch your hands as far back as possible, inhale through your nose and exhale through your mouth, keeping your body still for 5 seconds.

D. Drinking a glass of cool water after waking up is a good habit

After a night’s sleep without any water intake, people can easily become dehydrated. A glass of cool boiled water (not iced water) is a beneficial supplement and also helps clean the gastrointestinal tract.
