FeedBurner to Make Some Services Free After Google Deal

by officefurniture on 2007-07-04 16:22:35

July 3rd, Beijing Time -- According to foreign media reports, after RSS hosting service provider FeedBurner was acquired by Google, it finally took an important step by making its MyBrand service free.

Through the MyBrand service provided by FeedBurner, website publishers can keep their domain brand on the feed instead of displaying the usual FeedBurner domain. Previously, MyBrand was a paid service, but it will now be offered for free to users. Renowned tech blogger Ionut Alex Chitu welcomed this move by FeedBurner. He stated that according to the number of feeds, users previously had to pay between $3 and $14 per month for the MyBrand service.

FeedBurner stated in its service FAQ: "Through the MyBrand service, website publishers can benefit from all of FeedBurner's services. They can operate through their domain name, thereby gaining a more transparent experience." Website publishers who value their own brand and marketing may need to adopt the MyBrand service. As an RSS hosting service provider, FeedBurner has already secured a place, and MyBrand will make its feeds more valuable.

In June this year, Google officially acquired FeedBurner. Analysts believe that this transaction is of great significance to both Google and FeedBurner. For Google, as more and more Internet users access blogs or other websites through RSS readers, the value of Google AdSense and DoubleClick is being inadvertently diminished. The reason is simple: users no longer need to directly visit websites, which significantly affects website publishers who rely on ad clicks for revenue. From this perspective, Google's acquisition of FeedBurner is a wise move because the company can incorporate its ads into the increasingly popular RSS.
