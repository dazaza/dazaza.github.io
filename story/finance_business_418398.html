Defeated by music, so what

by musicshow5 on 2011-04-19 09:35:35

In 2006, with the recording studio going bankrupt, I felt utterly disheartened. As the year was drawing to a close, I found myself in solitude and resolutely headed south to welcome a lonely Spring Festival that was uniquely mine. During this time, I constantly reflected on my own shortcomings. The ringtone business was booming, yet it couldn't withstand the policy pressure from the mobile service provider. My high-profile entry into the industry had me indulging in the entanglements of high-end corporate clients, only to be unexpectedly eliminated. My downfall was worse than that of a wild mountain chicken losing its feathers. Without a consumer base among the common people or small-scale ground activities for support, these were perhaps the reasons for my failure.

Upon arriving in Guangzhou, I stayed there. Through a friend's recommendation, I met Mr. Shao from the Guangdong Popular Music Association. We discussed music enthusiastically, talking about its glorious development history, piracy issues, gaining official recognition, original compositions, and establishing an official brand. I sighed with relief, feeling as if I had encountered a kindred spirit once again. Without hesitation, I joined his team as an advisor for music-related projects.

The Guangdong Popular Music Association has its own music website, more precisely, an original music website. Although it is officially hosted by the association, in reality, it is mainly executed by Mr. Shao, who has invested a great deal. With the help of the ringtone business, it managed to operate quite freely. This led to the first plan for Hunan Music Network, though it was merely a thought at that time, not yet put into action. Since I had just suffered a setback, the pain had not subsided, and the scars had not healed. Not having formally engaged with the internet, I decided to start by dipping my toes in the water to study the field. How to recruit and operate artists, how to promote music, and how to turn music into wealth are all long-term endeavors. Managing the website before me was already filled with numerous trivial tasks.

After a year of struggling and learning, one day, I saw a company sponsor Mr. Shao’s music event with 2 million yuan, which left me astonished. I deeply realized the charm of music, its deep-rooted influence on the hearts of the people, and its ability to sway public sentiment. If Guangdong could build a brand, why couldn't Hunan?

For the sake of Hunan music, I once again took a bold step. I gave up the relatively generous salary at the time and returned to my hometown, planning the stories to come...
