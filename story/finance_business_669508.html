2. Why Make a Forum?

Forums are known to everyone. They don't collect ad fees as well as regular web pages and don't make as much money as webpage sites. However, I think forums are lively, which is why I like them.

Because they gather a circle of people. It's easy to retain users. Interaction is strong. But this isn't absolute; not all sites should be turned into forums. This article only provides forum operation experience, so if you don't like forums, you don't need to read further.

3. What Kind of Forum to Make

I suggest you base it on your preferences and resources. Every industry's forum can succeed. Personally, I think excellent examples include Tianya, Houxin, and Xake Webmaster Forums.

4. What Kind of Space Do You Need for a Forum?

If your forum attachments are small or few, you need less space. The forum program itself is usually only a few MBs, but the database will gradually increase. This specific issue requires specific analysis.

5. How Many "Hardworking Moderators" Are Needed?

When running a forum, it's best not to rely on one person. Quickly recruit moderators, and at the beginning, you can adopt measures such as QB rewards. Moderators need to work hard initially. At least content updates need to keep up. I don't recommend collecting content; forums that collect content tend to be of poor quality (personal opinion). Attachments should be localized as much as possible because some resources may become outdated. Besides updating content, moderators need to establish board rules and register some minor accounts to reply to posts, creating an atmosphere where there are many replies.

6. New Forums Need Some "Tricks"

Since new forums are obscure and rarely attract people to stay, your promotion won't retain many users. It's suggested to appropriately use some "tricks." Currently, most forums have virtual user number systems, and you can also register a batch of members yourself. Don't overdo it; the number of members and post counts should be proportional. For example, at the beginning, you can show 20 online members. And it's essential to display detailed online lists (of course, once it gets big, this isn't necessary).

7. Forums Need Reward Systems

Besides moderators, you need to retain members. At least the content needs to attract these members. To create an atmosphere, new forums can hide posts until replied to. However, images and attachments must be visible to guests. Clear reward systems are needed. For active members, promote their permissions, elevate their ranks, issue forum coin rewards, QBs, or physical rewards. These are all good methods.

8. You Need to Establish a "Consumption System"

Forum virtual coins are the motivation for users to post and also represent status. Many members like being the "rich people" of the forum. Therefore, downloading a post attachment costs a certain amount of money, while posting content adds a certain amount of money. This needs to be adjusted based on your forum.
