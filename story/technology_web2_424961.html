Woman falsely accuses boyfriend, leading to his imprisonment; Boyfriend does not abandon her and they become engaged

Date: 2006-01-20 10:13:50 Source: Chengdu Business Daily

Haikou Evening News report on January 20 according to Chengdu Business Daily: To give her boyfriend a birthday gift, she, lacking funds, took a dangerous risk and stole someone else’s digital camera.

After the truth came out, she pushed all responsibility onto her unsuspecting boyfriend. Eventually, she was sentenced to eight months in prison.

She thought that love was over, feeling unbearable pain. When all family members were ashamed of her, her boyfriend wrote her a diary every day, wrote a letter every two days, earned money with difficulty and sent her money once a week...

The boyfriend's tolerance saved a lost soul. Yesterday, after being released from prison, she became engaged to her boyfriend.

At their worst times, they owed four months' rent, and all the money they had together did not exceed 10 yuan. But their feelings did not fade because of the lack of money.

Yang Yu is petite, and at the age of 20, she looks somewhat weathered. She said that having gone through such painful lessons, she would mature somehow. Although it has been almost half a year since she returned to normal life, as soon as she mentioned those past events, her eyes reddened. She said she was afraid others would look down on her.

In 2004, Yang Yu met her cousin's colleague Li Ya while playing with her cousin. As they got to know each other, they developed feelings for one another. Li Ya's hometown is in Nanchong, where they still live in a mud wall house and lead a hard life. This was the biggest reason why Yang Yu's parents opposed their relationship. Under strong parental demands, Yang Yu had no choice but to break up with Li Ya. Li Ya, angry, ran back to his hometown.

After Li Ya left, Yang Yu felt an emptiness inside. She quit her job and went to Nanchong to find Li Ya. They reconciled and returned to Chengdu to live together. At this time, Li Ya worked at a large printing factory in Chengdu, earning more than 2000 yuan a month during peak seasons, but only received 300 yuan living allowance during off-seasons. Yang Yu, without a diploma, faced difficulties finding work everywhere. While learning to ride a bicycle, Yang Yu accidentally injured herself, which added to their already strained financial situation. Yang Yu said that at their worst, they owed four months' rent, and all the money they had together did not exceed 10 yuan. But their feelings did not fade because of the lack of money.

Slip into hatred

Boyfriend's birthday - She steals a gift due to lack of money

She knew she had done wrong, but she wanted to tell her boyfriend that she stole something because she didn't have money to buy him a birthday gift, but there was no chance to explain.

On November 9, 2004, it was almost Li Ya's birthday, and Yang Yu did not have enough money to prepare a decent birthday gift. In her conflict, she stole her boss's camera!

After the boss reported to the police, Yang Yu realized the seriousness of the situation and stammered her confession. She took the boss home to retrieve the camera. On the way upstairs, they encountered Li Ya coming downstairs. "Why are you going home?" Li Ya was very curious.

By the time she came downstairs, the police car was already waiting below. Yang Yu was taken to the Jitou Town Police Station. Naively, she thought returning the camera would be enough to resolve the issue.

Mistake after mistake - In a panic, she falsely accused her boyfriend

She didn't expect that the opposition from her parents couldn't separate them, but they would be separated in such a way.

But unexpectedly, when she arrived at the police station, the police began severe questioning. Upon seeing this, Yang Yu's mind became chaotic: "Will I go to jail?" "How will my parents view me?" She dared not think further. Under repeated questioning by the police, she just wanted to quickly escape responsibility, and in her confusion, she lied: "My boyfriend stole it!" But immediately after saying it, she regretted it.

At this moment, Li Ya came to the police station, and through the glass window, Yang Yu saw Li Ya's worried face. Because of her testimony, Li Ya was taken to another room for questioning. "At that moment, I really despaired! Our relationship is over!" Yang Yu said.

The police quickly investigated and found that the camera had nothing to do with Li Ya! That day, Yang Yu was sent to the detention center on suspicion of burglary.

When boarding the vehicle, she saw Li Ya far away. His anger and worry made her feel utterly humiliated. She wanted to explain clearly, she wanted to say that she stole the camera because she didn't have money to buy him a birthday gift, she wanted to say that she regretted framing him, but there was no opportunity to explain.

That night, when she was locked up in the detention center, she received a package of clothes. On the clothing list's signature, "Li Ya" was clearly written. Yang Yu hugged the clothes and cried uncontrollably. She didn't expect that the opposition from her parents couldn't separate them, but they would be separated in such a way.

Love's Tolerance

Beyond the high walls, her loving boyfriend waits patiently

"Food can be skipped, but if I don't see his letters, I'll go crazy!" Yang Yu said that people who haven't experienced life behind high walls wouldn't understand what a letter means in a place completely cut off from the outside world.

On February 2, 2005, the day of the court's verdict arrived. In the vast audience seats, there was only Li Ya! Her parents, whom she missed day and night, didn't show up, yet Li Ya, who almost took the blame for her, actually came. Ultimately, she was sentenced to eight months in prison!

On April 4, 2005, it was the day Yang Yu was allowed to meet her relatives. On the visitor list, she filled in her parents' names. When she arrived at the meeting room, she discovered that Li Ya was the only one who came. Through the glass, they stared at each other for a long time before picking up the phone. Yet she had so much to say but couldn't open her mouth. All she remembered was Li Ya's haggard appearance.

From then on, Yang Yu would receive a thick stack of letters from Li Ya every so often. Li Ya's words became her motivation to keep living. Sometimes, Li Ya would include a couple of beautiful rose petals in the letters. "Food can be skipped, but if I don't see his letters, I'll go crazy!" Yang Yu said that people who haven't experienced life behind high walls wouldn't understand what a letter means in a place completely cut off from the outside world. Every week, Yang Yu would also receive money sent by Li Ya, sometimes 100 yuan, sometimes 50 yuan. Yang Yu said she knew that Li Ya wasn't doing well outside either.

As her release date approached, Yang Yu's heart became heavy. She felt she didn't deserve Li Ya. Just as she was preparing to give up, Li Ya sent her the thickest letter he had ever sent, and this letter would be enough for her to savor for a lifetime: Inside were two thick diaries. On the cover of one, neatly written were the words "I'm waiting for you," dated November 11, 2004, the day Yang Yu entered the detention center. Originally, after Yang Yu's incident, Li Ya had been writing a diary every day. Yang Yu was moved to tears again.

On July 13, 2005, Yang Yu finally walked out of the big iron gates, breathing the air of freedom. Far away, Li Ya smiled as he walked towards her and made a gesture of embracing. Yang Yu cried again, telling Li Ya that she would not make such a mistake again and she wanted to start anew. (Yuan Liya)
