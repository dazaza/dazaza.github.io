"Asian Games let us grow up, Asian Games let us have fun" (in pictures)

by jonkkk861 on 2011-05-07 13:16:41

It is often said that a seed can only have unlimited vitality when it is deeply rooted in fertile soil; and as an athlete, especially as a blind football athlete, one can only thrive in an atmosphere of hard work and struggle. Once, I was a little boy from a mountain village in Fujian, now, with 700 million funds flowing out, who is the main force, I am the "blind Messi" in the mouths of football fans; once, I was a masseur at a street-side massage shop, now, I am a champion of the Asian Para Games. I will work even harder and believe that my tomorrow will have more brilliant colors!

The first element in competitive sports is to cultivate and select excellent players who can fight big battles and crucial battles. When we cultivate people, we emphasize lofty goals, all-round development, competition training, and strict management; when we use people, we emphasize adapting to individual needs, prescribing remedies accordingly, and not mixing personal emotions; when choosing the starting lineup, we emphasize putting fighting spirit first, having a tough mental attitude, and maintaining a balance between stability and aggression.

Training volunteers is another part of my job. Do you remember "Smiling Sister"? During the 40-day training period, they wore 5 cm high heels every day, practiced balancing by placing books on their heads, and trained smiles by biting chopsticks; do you remember the "security officers"? They squatted 2600 times a day, ran 5 kilometers, and during the Asian Games, they wore out dozens of pairs of sports shoes; do you remember the "diplomats"? They read extensively and spread the history and culture, social development situation of Yangcheng to friends from various countries... In order to make volunteers showcase their best and most professional side, we formed a team of 736 volunteer lecturers and wrote more than 50 training textbooks. Personally, I have given 253 lectures totaling over 1000 hours, training 18,000 core members.

Firstly, innovation combined with practical combat realizes the safe and smooth opening and closing ceremonies. We focus on innovating venue security concepts and implement comprehensive planning of the main venue. We emphasize innovative building control concepts and promote humanized management. We focus on innovative prevention and control concepts, constructing a three-dimensional prevention and control system integrating land, water, high points, and air. Secondly, strictness combined with harmony builds a solid security defense line. We carefully study and formulate various explosion-proof security inspection principles and policies, comprehensively coordinate more than 12,000 explosion-proof demolition and security inspection forces including security police and security volunteers, equip them with "high, precise, sharp" equipment, and ensure detailed security inspection measures to overcome difficulties in emergency response at explosive sites. Thirdly, prevention combined with control ensures the safety and order of all activities. Whether in torch relay security or event security, whether in venue security or accommodation security, we adhere to early warning analysis, determine security levels according to local conditions, take preventive measures, ensuring the smooth and safe conduct of each activity. Accommodation security achieved the goal of "zero violations, zero cases, zero disputes, zero complaints." Fourthly, management combined with crackdown lays the foundation for "Safe Asian Games." We strictly control items involving guns, explosives, and dangerous materials, ensuring no loss, no theft, no firing, no explosion. We strictly manage entertainment service venues, ensuring no formation of "pornography, gambling, drugs," and fencing dens. We severely rectify prominent public security issues, ensuring no hotspots are formed and no impact is caused.

The Asian Games made us grow, the Asian Games made us happy. Although the Guangzhou Asian Games have come to an end, the spirit of the "happy responsibility casting brilliance" of the Asian Games volunteers will be passed down forever. We will continue to dedicate our youth to the civilization of Guangzhou. Let's join together, for a more wonderful future!

I am known as the "blind Messi" among football fans.

Li Sen, customer engineer of Guangzhou Branch of China Telecom Corporation Limited:

During the Guangzhou Asian Games, we sang the slogan "everyone is the host, everyone is a volunteer," initiating the arduous yet joyful task of promoting the concept of volunteering. I will never forget A Hao, the 15-year-old boy with leukemia whose greatest wish was to perform Asian Games volunteer services once. That day, I came to his ward, dressed him in an Asian Games volunteer uniform, pinned on an Asian Games volunteer badge, and together we conducted volunteer services at a bus stop. On the way back to the hospital, his face radiated with satisfaction and happiness. This scene made me feel incredibly proud and honored to be an Asian Games volunteer.

I am responsible for the operation allocation and supervision of volunteers at 12 competition venues, leaving home every morning at 7 and returning after 9 PM, changing buses at least 20 times a day, walking nearly 5 kilometers. At the venues I supervised, there were no complaints about volunteer services. Honestly speaking, volunteers work hard, but when we received flowers at the closing ceremony, when citizens spontaneously organized the "Asian Games Volunteer Care Team" to give us ginger soup and throat candies, I felt that all the hard work was worth it, and we were happy and content.

Ensuring "no major incidents, no minor ones either"

The Asian Para Games gathered us in the beautiful city of Guangzhou, igniting Asia with passion, embodying the concepts of gathering, integration, and sharing deeply into our minds.

The successful hosting of the 16th Asian Games and the first Asian Para Games in Guangzhou fully realized the goal of "two equally splendid Asian Games." Behind the brilliance, success, and unforgettable memories lies the extraordinary effort of people from all walks of life, the selfless dedication of the citizens of Guangzhou, and the strong support of the entire nation. At yesterday's summary and commendation conference of the Guangzhou Asian Games and Asian Para Games, representatives of four advanced collectives and individuals expressed touching and passionate sentiments with their simple words.

At the Guangzhou Asian Games, through the tenacious efforts of the entire team, the national table tennis team won all seven gold medals again after 32 years, bringing honor to the motherland and laying a solid foundation for preparing for the London Olympics.

Becoming a volunteer originated from an accidental opportunity. In the year 2000, I read a comment in the newspaper: "The level of civilization of a city does not lie in how many skyscrapers it has, but in how many volunteers it possesses." Thus began my path of volunteer service. Over the past 11 years, the volunteer service team I lead has grown from an initial few dozen people to more than 30,000, conducting fixed weekly volunteer services across more than 800 positions.

Under the banner of national honor above everything, building team spirit. During preparation, we seriously summarized the lessons from the women's team's setback at the 2010 Moscow World Championships, unified thoughts with "reshaping team spirit, coalescing collective strength, fighting for the country!" Vowed "must win the Asian Games!" and established smooth emotional exchange channels, enhancing communication, discussing issues freely, trusting each other in preparatory training, creating a harmonious and positive atmosphere, significantly increasing the overall combat effectiveness of the team.

Captain Huang Biao of the National Table Tennis Team:

Achievements belong to the past, a beautiful future is waving towards us.
