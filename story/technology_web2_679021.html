### Discussion:

The ultimate goal of hypertension treatment is to reduce cardiovascular complications and mortality rates, as well as alleviate existing studies indicating prognosis. Left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH) is an independent predictor of cardiovascular events, strong even when blood pressure and other cardiovascular risk factors are controlled. Therefore, reversing LVH has become one of the main goals of hypertensive therapy. LVM index (LWNI) is a new predictor of vascular events. This study shows that cilazapril effectively lowers blood pressure while simultaneously reducing LVM index, primarily through reductions in interventricular septum and left ventricular posterior wall thickness. With the reversal of LVH, left ventricular diastolic function improved significantly, as evidenced by an increased E/A ratio. Since left ventricular diastolic dysfunction in hypertensive patients with LVH is an early change, even without LVH or during the period of preserved ventricular systolic function, left ventricular compliance may be weakened. Thus, improving the prognosis should first involve enhancing diastolic function. The description suggests that cilazapril can be applied for the early treatment of hypertension, improving the prognosis of hypertensive patients.
