Microsoft Announces CEO Ballmer to Retire within the Next 12 Months

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 13:58:57

On the evening of August 23rd, Beijing time, Microsoft announced that its CEO, Steve Ballmer, had decided to retire within the next 12 months.

Microsoft stated that Ballmer will officially retire after a successor is chosen and the transition process is completed. For now, he will continue as CEO, leading the company's shift towards becoming a device and service-oriented company.

Ballmer said: "There’s never a perfect time for this kind of transition, but it feels like the right time now. We have laid out a new strategy, restructured as an institution, and have an excellent senior management team. My initial thought was to retire during the company's transformation into a device and service-focused direction. We need a CEO who can execute the new strategy over the long term."

The Microsoft board has established a special committee to handle Ballmer's succession. The members of this special committee include independent director John Thompson, company chairman Bill Gates, audit committee chair Chuck Noski, and compensation committee chair Steve Luczo.

This special committee is collaborating with the executive search firm Heidrick & Struggles International, planning to simultaneously search both internally and externally for a suitable successor for the CEO position.

Thompson commented: "The board is committed to ensuring Microsoft transitions effectively into a successful device and service company. As we continue this mission, we will focus on finding a new CEO."

Gates remarked: "As a member of the succession planning committee, I will work closely with other members to find an outstanding new CEO. Before the new CEO takes over, we are fortunate to have Steve continue performing his duties."
