How to Make People Around You Like You More

by gege on 2011-12-22 18:56:34

1. Always remember: looks will never disgust people, only stupidity, hypocrisy, impoliteness, arrogance, bragging, and not getting things done are the most disgusting.

2. Charm is the key. If you can't master fashion, prefer simplicity.

3. When shaking hands with someone, hold on a little longer. He will feel your sincerity.

4. When talking to others, smiles are the strongest secret weapon. Your smile will make the other person lower their guard and quickly become good friends who talk about everything.

5. Don't lose your temper for no reason, life is not a TV drama.

6. Complain less in front of others saying "how boring," the more you say it's boring, the more others think you're boring.

7. When taking a taxi with others, sit next to the driver first.

8. When someone says bad things about another person in front of you, just smile.

9. If you drive a small car, don't stop especially to greet a colleague riding a bicycle. They might think you're showing off.

10. When a colleague is sick, visit them. Sit naturally on their hospital bed, then wash your hands carefully when you get home.

11. Don't let everyone know all about your past; making yourself seem like a blank sheet doesn't benefit you much.

12. Respect those who don't like you.

13. When others criticize you, let them finish speaking, even if you're right. If you choose to argue, you'll have a lot of trouble.

14. Respect those who criticize you, because they can truly help you improve.

15. Self-criticism always makes people believe, but self-praise does not.

16. Nothing can improve your bowling score better than bystanders. So, don't hesitate to cheer for others.

17. Don't take others' kindness for granted; learn to be grateful. No one was born to serve you, even if they're a maid.

18. The "mynah" on the banyan tree talks but doesn't listen, resulting in chaos. Learn to listen. Understand before speaking, and focus on the important points. Then, you'll be an excellent negotiator.

19. Respect the masters in the delivery room and the cleaning aunties. Throw garbage into the bin, give up your seat to the elderly on the bus. These small actions greatly enhance your charm.

20. When speaking, often start with "we." It will bring you closer to others.

21. Applaud for every person who sings on stage; they will be deeply touched.

22. Sometimes ask knowingly: "Your diamond ring must be expensive!" Sometimes, even if you want to ask, don't ask, such as: "How much is your salary?"

23. Talking too much leads to mistakes; speak less in crowded places.

24. Change the unspoken "no" to: "This takes time," "I'll try my best," "I'm not sure," "I'll call you after I decide... This is a sign of maturity. (It's also a common tactic used by employers. Most of us graduates encounter employers telling us to wait for a call when job hunting, at which point you can consider changing to another company.)

25. Don't expect everyone to like you; that's impossible. Making the majority like you is a sign of success.

26. Of course, you should like yourself.

27. If you're performing or giving a speech, even if only one person is listening, continue wholeheartedly. Even without applause, keep going because this is your path to success, your cradle of success. You're not there to make the audience succeed, but to make yourself succeed.

28. Keep saying good things about others behind their backs, don't worry whether these good words reach the ears of the concerned party. Take the easiest-to-understand example: For instance, if you and your girlfriend have a big argument, you may say a thousand things, ten thousand things, but still can't reconcile. At this moment, the best way is to find your girlfriend's best friend. One word from her equals a thousand from you. Do you believe?

Just let her convey three sentences, guaranteed to solve the problem:

(1) Hasn't your husband always been good to you?

(2) He buys you things, helps you out, how could you break up just because of a few words?

(3) Make up, can you really split?

Your girlfriend will definitely say: Oh, yeah, let's just muddle through, but this guy has made me so angry, later you help me teach this guy a lesson.
