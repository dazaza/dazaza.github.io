Mobile QQ vs WeChat: Internal Competition or Sibling Rivalry?

by geekzhang on 2013-05-11 13:02:22

The mobile QQ has enabled the classic Tencent mode. Tencent is using all its efforts to imitate itself. According to an article written by a member of the lifestyle e-commerce team recommended on Tencent's internal network, handQ (mobile QQ) is on the right and bright path.It can be frankly said that this path is the WeChat way.WeChat's COPYOn May 8th, the Android version of QQ Mobile entered the 4.1 era. From some summaries of version 4.1, we can see:1. The exit button disappeared, changed to log out of the current account. That is, only account logout is possible; the program runs in the background by default and cannot be exited (unless through the Android system method). This is a major change noticed by almost all users. Netizens who are not accustomed to it are probably not regular WeChat users. Because WeChat has long adopted a similar method: log out. Emphasizing 24-hour real-time online. This is the first big change in handQ.2. All friend list avatars are in color. Unable to distinguish whether friends are online. Continuing to align with WeChat, weakening the online/offline status. This is the second major change in handQ.3. The conversation window has undergone a major makeover. It actually became more similar to WeChat. For example, the expressions and more options at the bottom left were moved to the right, while the voice icon was placed on the left. Even more exaggeratedly, the word "image" from the previous version was updated to "photo." None of these changes fail to show alignment with WeChat.The above practices address handQ's shortcomings: due to the distinction between being online or offline, messages cannot be delivered promptly. The new version alleviates this issue using WeChat’s methods, allowing people to communicate via handQ or WeChat anytime and anywhere.Of course, there are also some minor innovations, such as changing the message count reminder box from round to square (different from WeChat), along with a few major updates mentioned by the official team.Recalling what Pony Ma said a few days ago at GMIC, the competition between mobile QQ and WeChat is not about mutual replacement but self-updating.But from the user's perspective, this statement feels exactly the opposite.Users express strong dissatisfactionThis version of the product could be launched in such a form due to Tencent's positioning, but this is absolutely not a good version. If one were to rate the worst QQ version from Tencent, this version would definitely take the crown. Tencent's internal experts and product managers will surely feel indignant. For instance, they might say this version follows the trend of mobile internet, maintains consistent experience with WeChat, enabling users to communicate anytime and anywhere. However, these are all expert opinions. I believe whether a product is good or not is not determined by experts or product managers, but by users.A search for mobile QQ on Weibo reveals almost entirely requests for help, how to restore the old version, angry curses, and complaints. It is unprecedented to see such overwhelming dissatisfaction towards a product. What? Weibo users are not representative? Then go check various app markets, Baidu, or forums. It is difficult to find a single approving message, with over 90% of ratings being 1-star (the highest score is 5 stars).Just the day before this version was released, on May 7th at GMIC, Pony Ma said the reason for mobile QQ's slow actions lies in organizational structure issues, and later commercialization will accelerate, increasing differentiation from WeChat. But the version released the next day raises a question mark on Mr. Ma's words: where's the promised differentiation?To be honest, I have been eagerly awaiting the launch of this version of handQ. This is the first major version after mobile QQ merged into the Social Networking Group following the departure of veteran Liu Chengmin. It is the version refined over a quarter after resolving the organizational structure issues mentioned by Mr. Ma. I wonder what feelings Liu Chengmin would have upon seeing this new version at his winery? I am extremely disappointed with this new version: I don't need another WeChat.The intense backlash from users and the significant gap below expectations are closely related to the differences in user groups and usage scenarios between WeChat and handQ.handQ is used for chatting when needed, while WeChat provides 7*24 hour communication;handQ is entertainment-oriented, while WeChat is life-oriented;handQ represents weak relationships, while WeChat represents strong relationships;handQ's social dependence on space makes it a black hole of excessive social content, whereas WeChat's social circle of friends is a high-quality social website for sharing photos and texts in daily life;handQ has weak media attributes, while WeChat has public accounts;handQ explicitly tells everyone my status: I'm playing with my phone, I'm online; WeChat does not display whether users are online or offline.In light of these differences, mobile QQ needs to differentiate itself from WeChat rather than crudely copying it. If users are dual users of both mobile QQ and WeChat, the smaller the difference between the two, the more reason they have to uninstall one.The thin brother is getting fatter, and the fat brother wants to lose weight. WeChat is becoming simpler by adding features, while handQ is reducing features due to its bloat. Losing weight is a painful process, but gaining weight, though not easy, is not painful.With ample room for differentiation, handQ removed its core characteristics and made significant differentiations in less important areas, such as changing rounded corners to rectangles, altering the megaphone graphic, changing color styles, and changing from 'log out' to 'log out of current account.' This is akin to a woman losing chest size while gaining belly fat, ultimately needing to find ways to recover.Why did handQ take this path?Why did handQ follow the path of WeChat? A former Tencent employee, Chris Chen, replied on Zhihu:If the road to success is already laid out, why go through thorny paths filled with danger and no reward? Standing on the shoulders of giants, you gain not just speed but also time to improve your own matters better.Perhaps this statement somewhat represents Tencent's classic logic, which seems correct on the surface. But the problem is, success theories are unreliable. Others' paths to success may not necessarily be ones you can follow. This older brother still hasn't figured out: what exactly is his job? Seeing the younger brother doing his job, he decides to redo what the younger brother has done, trying to surpass him?I analyze that the reasons behind this version taking this path might be as follows:1. handQ had a predetermined route before integrating into the new business group at the beginning of the year. This 4.1 version is merely a continuation of version 4.0. In other words, the truly revolutionary and differentiated version of handQ has yet to arrive. Although Tencent is known for its speed-first approach, adjusting the organizational structure, especially for its super-core business, won't hastily change the course;2. handQ hasn't yet figured out how to differentiate itself or has figured it out but can't let go. handQ and WeChat share many overlapping user groups, scenes, and functions. But they can each encroach on the other's territory. The commonality between WeChat and QQ is communication based on relationships. However, WeChat's life and media attributes, and handQ's entertainment and social attributes, are points of differentiation. The issue is that WeChat can next do games and communities, and handQ can add real-time walkie-talkies in the next version. WeChat's current focus is on commercialization, while handQ must think about how to avoid direct competition with its younger brother, meaning differentiation. On mobile devices, it is likely to become a secondary role. Given that its activity level is still twice that of WeChat, it naturally refuses to give up.3. handQ and WeChat tend to have similar functionalities, but their operations and commercialization will differ. Tencent might let handQ and WeChat play a game of self-combat. This will be cruel, but for Tencent, whoever wins, it's Tencent's victory. This means a lot of resource wastage. Tencent will inevitably let the two sons try and err in commercialization to quickly find the profit model for mobile internet. Games, music, advertising, value-added services, e-commerce, O2O all need to be attempted. By doing so, Tencent can sooner monetize mobile internet, upgrade stand tickets to sit tickets, or even possess two tickets simultaneously.4. handQ lacks a god-level product manager like Zhang Xiaolong. Personal reasons may have an impact, but they shouldn't be decisive factors.5. To deal with disputes over operator charges, intentionally create a masquerade WeChat? This is the least reliable. Heard that the operator charge issue was just a farce. Just treat it as a joke.Perhaps, there are other unresolved reasons. But the WeChat-version mobile QQ appears before us, before Pony Ma, before Zhang Xiaolong, and before the departed Liu Chengmin.Mobile QQ has initiated Tencent's classic combat mode, treating younger brother WeChat as a competitor, aiming to stage a typical Tencent imitation and disruption drama. A friend told me that handQ is planning and about to launch a public platform. I speculate that if Mr. Ma doesn't soon appoint WeChat as the crown prince and find a place for handQ to be king, the final outcome will be fratricide!
