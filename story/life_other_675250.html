The Enlightenment in Leifeng Pagoda: One Thought Leads to Death, One Thought Leads to Life

[Summary] That beautiful love story passed down for so long is actually this cruel: a man put poison in the hands of a woman who loves him...

[Main Text] He gave his vacation to another woman.

Lin Qiu returned at midnight, his steps as quiet as when he left. "May Day", a long holiday, and he had gone missing. His shirts hung on the balcony, his razor was in the bathroom, and even the pillow smelled like him, as if he had slept soundly by my side last night, offering me his arms. But in fact, he had gone missing. He said the company organized a group trip to Hainan, but I checked all the airline passenger records and there was no name of his. During these 6 days, I guessed many possibilities, although I clearly knew that he would definitely return the day before work, just as I clearly understood that he gave this vacation to another woman.

I loved Lin Qiu, for 7 years, knowing him as well as myself. Seven years ago, in my youth, my love was like a rose blooming at its most beautiful time, regardless and unrestrained. I spent two years growing my hair long because he liked it; I remembered not to show all my teeth when smiling, pretending to be a lady; I remembered to hold my breath and pull in my stomach while walking, elegant and noble. For him, I gradually changed myself. In the third year after graduation, my father was already helping me with my visa, but I ultimately gave it up. At that time, several TV stations were repeatedly broadcasting "The Legend of White Snake". Seeing the previews where Xu Xian becomes a monk, losing hope in the secular world, feeling utterly disheartened, I sighed, thinking about my separation from Lin Qiu, as if there would be no more reunion. I clung to Lin Qiu, asking him to promise that we would never separate as long as we lived, not even for a day. Back then, Lin Qiu loved me, he solemnly nodded, promising me a lifetime.

Time is truly a strange thing. After 7 years, the initial tenderness and obsession have long been returned to the past. I stayed at home alone, guessing countless versions of the destination of the person who once promised me a lifetime, and the woman beside him. On the evening of the 7th, Lin Qiu returned, sitting on the sofa in the living room. The lights were dim, and I could imagine him smoking, those yellow Camel cigarettes with their pungent smell. These 7 days, I repeatedly edited on my phone: Xia Linqiu, let's break up. I wrote it again and again, but I never pressed send. Now, he sat outside the house, and I lay in bed looking at dozens of identical messages in my drafts box, my face wet with tears. Ultimately, I decided to go out and talk to him. I needed an explanation.

I went barefoot, and as soon as I stepped out of the bedroom door, I met his embrace filled with intense longing and nostalgia. I let him unbutton my underwear one by one, and when my skin was exposed, his tongue coolly wrapped around it. I could feel his slight trembling. The night was long, and every inch of skin he kissed was engraved with our past vows. Lin Qiu's fingers blossomed over my body, now having a scorching heat. We tossed and turned on the bed in the bedroom, his whispers still so gentle. My anger melted away completely in this intimacy. I had forgotten that a second ago, I wanted to tell him that I would leave him; now, the next second, I only remember that I can't leave him.

There was still a faint fragrance of perfume on his body. The wind was strong tonight, but it did not blow away the traces of betrayal. The scent was light, yet it stung my eyes. Lin Qiu said, "Baby, I missed you." He said it so familiarly and straightforwardly, as if he really thought of me all the time while lingering in someone else's body. I stiffened my body, staring straight at him, seeing the avoidance in his eyes. I helplessly withdrew my body, turned around, and the air suddenly became cold. Lin Qiu buried his head in the soft pillow, silent, without any hint of flattery. After a moment of deadlock, it was I who eventually turned around, throwing myself into his arms, pushing back all the questions and pain of the past few days. Only I knew that the excitement carried a taste of despair.

My good friend Xiaobei has been disappointed in me more than once. She said that my goodness to Lin Qiu was to the point of losing myself, which wasn't called love, purely degrading. But Lin Qiu told me that he would be with me for a lifetime, Xiaobei didn't know how important the three words "a lifetime" were to me.

In fact, women are not smart to begin with, especially when caught in the web of love. I knew I was the same, so I simply stopped thinking.

I've already gotten used to Lin Qiu saying "I'm sorry."

In the morning, after preparing breakfast, I suddenly wanted to go to Langmu Temple. Someone told me that the gods reside in Langmu Temple. If you devoutly come here to turn the prayer wheels, the turning wheels will take away the hardships of this life and bring happiness in the next life.

After a whole afternoon of travel, the tranquil Langmu Temple was shrouded in the thin rain of dusk. Everywhere you could see rows of simple corridors under the hills, with prayer wheels of various sizes, polished bright by devout hands. I touched them along the way until the end of the small path, not knowing whether the happiness of the next life would really come.

Yesterday, Xiaobei asked me if I was happy. For a long time, I didn't know how to answer. Happiness in this life seemed very distant to me. In recent years, I often yearned for a peaceful and serene marriage, yearning to stand by Lin Qiu in a pure white dress, sincerely saying "I do" in a holy church. When I told Lin Qiu about these aspirations, he always laughed absentmindedly. He has never mentioned marriage to me, from the beginning to now. He often says when he's drunk or needs to flirt, "How do I deserve your kindness?" The kindness he speaks of is my tolerance of his betrayals.

Xia Lin Qiu's first betrayal was six years ago. Back then, I was still the woman Lin Qiu cherished, young, charming, full of sunshine. After knowing him for one year and three months, I began to crave sour foods and sleep excessively. I went to the hospital alone, and then happily took a bus to Lin Qiu's company, wanting to give him a big surprise. The 27-floor elevator made me dizzy with happiness when it reached the top. I quietly pushed open the door of his office: pale bodies, piercing joy, and in an instant, the world changed. I gently closed the door behind me, inside the man and woman's bodies were still passionately entangled. I trembled all over, sitting on the window of the 27th floor, getting drenched in the rain outside the window, silently counting the raindrops falling into my palm, imagining what kind of exquisite posture it would be if I jumped down from the 27th floor.

An hour later, Lin Qiu came out. I stood up and stared at him. This man's expression instantly turned panicked. I turned and walked downstairs, circling around until I collapsed, and Lin Qiu sent me to the hospital. Afterward, my abdomen returned to its previous emptiness.

After being discharged from the hospital, I mentioned breaking up. Just like all clichéd TV dramas, from the initial noisy despair to finally crying softly in his arms, I made a compromising gesture. It wasn't because I heard him say that for those women, it was just desire, and desire comes with no responsibility, so he acted without restraint. It was because I knew I couldn't live without him, even though after this, his infidelity became a habit.

It's rare to describe a man as "beautiful," but for Lin Qiu, this adjective fits perfectly. Over the years, I've faced his betrayals more than once, almost becoming a routine. I fought wits and strength with those women who fantasized and lusted after him, watching them leave defeated time and again. I could identify each of his lies, inadvertently uncovering evidence of his betrayals, each time feeling like the end of the world. My heart went from tearing apart to hurting deeply, and I've grown accustomed to hearing Lin Qiu say "I'm sorry."

Lin Qiu always liked to boast to his friends about my generosity, as if it was a sign of virtue. As for myself, I naturally knew about his promiscuous affairs, but what mattered to me was that I loved him now and would continue to love him in the future. Although I knew marriage was far away, Lin Qiu was close to me. I only demanded endless physical satisfaction from him, indulging in his warmth, faking happiness and orgasms, pretending there were no other women and no betrayals. I consoled myself, thinking he was like a mischievous child who wouldn't stray too far, and after getting lost, would cry and find his way home. Everything seemed bearable, except for the increasing insomnia I experienced late at night.

Once again in the TV series, I saw Xu Xian during the Dragon Boat Festival. The woman who loved him had already hung mugwort and planted wormwood for him. She devoutly prayed for peace and blessings in this earthly life. Fate decreed that the Dragon Boat Festival was her calamity, yet she didn't retreat to the mountains or hide in the lake bottom. She cherished every moment of this worldly life with him, even if it meant going through fire and brimstone. He betrayed her feelings, believing slander, and persuaded her three times to drink the realgar wine. Watching this woman who lost herself in love being forced into drinking the cup, leaving her with no retreat, I suddenly felt much hatred towards Xu Xian. To him, a cup of realgar wine distinguished human from demon; to her, it sealed her fate between life and death. Thinking that this man could forget the shared umbrella experience in the West Lake rain, even if he no longer loved her, he didn't need to reveal her true snake form on the Dragon Boat Festival day.

A lifetime can be casually said.

On November 11, nearing winter, the rain was extremely cold, and I wandered aimlessly on the street, letting the rain soak my body. They call this day Singles' Day, and if one could be a carefree bachelor, it would be a beautiful thing, unlike me. On the eighth anniversary of our meeting, I saw a shopping receipt among the clutter on the table, buying a bottle of perfume. I knew this perfume, not a rich and sweet floral fragrance, but one with bergamot as the top note, subtly carrying a Zen flavor. Looking at the invoice, it seemed these so-called "evidence" he didn't even bother to destroy. Over the years, tears seemed to be cut off from me. When in pain, I usually bit my lip and remained silent, but at this moment, I stood dumbfounded in the middle of the living room, sobbing like a child. Facing the betrayals and pains Lin Qiu gave me, I preferred to escape. I thought that if I didn't talk about it or think about it, the images of those women with snow-white skin and chest-like peaks would never appear.

Finally, I got tired of walking and stopped in front of a mall. Among the crowd seeking shelter from the rain was a couple. The woman chattered about today's vegetable prices, and the man said that his relatives' children from the hometown were going to join the army, and they planned to visit together when free. Such trivial happiness gnawed at my heart bite by bite. A woman approached me to chat, promoting a brand's vitamins and cosmetics. She said that women around 30 can be without love but cannot be without vitamins. At 30, I suddenly realized how bleak this number was. Subconsciously, I looked at the bright display window next to me, noticing that my eyes were no longer as clear as before. A woman's days should not be counted too precisely, as doing so brings wrinkles to the forehead.

That night, Lin Qiu didn't come home. He sent a text message, dear, I'm working overtime. At 10 o'clock, I passed by his office and walked home. The entire building was pitch black, and I didn't see his shadow working overtime. However, the phone at home rang restlessly near midnight. Each time I picked it up, there was only slight breathing on the other end. After repeated attempts, I said, "Are you looking for Xia Lin Qiu?" In this bustling world, people often get lost in emotions. Early in the morning, I met this woman. Her expressions of longing, expectation, and despair were immediately comprehensible to me. She didn't tell me the version of her story with Lin Qiu, she only said, "How could this happen? He said he would be good to me for a lifetime." The word "lifetime" she spoke struck my heart loudly. Suddenly, I realized that I had given this phrase heavy significance, but originally, it was merely a tool for flirtation during passionate moments.

At night, I asked Lin Qiu if we would break up. Lin Qiu firmly said no, saying, "Girl, we've become accustomed to each other." Sitting on the sofa, I bitterly smiled, thinking that being accustomed to each other might be the highest level of love. I stared at Lin Qiu's lips; that sexy little pucker could clearly say "I love you" effortlessly, even genuinely after blatant betrayal. For the first time, I inexplicably lost my temper, smashing the thick glass on the coffee table with a large ashtray, leaving it shattered and scarred. I turned and left, and as I exited the door, some channel was still playing "The Legend of the White Snake." At that moment, Xu Xian was hiding fearfully in Jinshan Temple, refusing to come out. Behind me was desolation.

The moment I stepped out of the hallway, I suddenly felt no hatred at all. Isn't love always fragmented? That's why the process looks so beautiful.

Eight years ago, I met Xia Lin Qiu at a job fair and was captivated by his beauty and charm. Step by step, I finally became his girlfriend. I quit my job because I didn't want to affect his image, and I rejected my father's painstakingly arranged study abroad plans because I couldn't imagine a life without him. I went from hating ginger to liking chicken diced with ginger, from being initially arrogant and capricious to now being "tolerant and generous." Life seemed to change a lot, happiness decreased, loneliness increased, and the city became abnormally large, large enough that no one could stop to accompany me.

I've never reflected on my life like I did tonight. When everything was sorted out and laid bare before me, I finally saw that my years-long love was already riddled with holes. In fact, my love had become a habit, obstinately sliding down like a gambler who lost sight of reality, the more chips placed, the harder it was to leave the table. But later, it became a betrayal and abandonment of myself. My eyes betrayed my heart, my body abandoned my desires, leaving only self-deception and futile persistence. Just as I always expected each "I'm sorry" to be the last, what I received was only the chains I imposed on myself.

"The Legend of the White Snake" has evolved to this point, and I suddenly understood. Previously, I always resented Xu Xian's conspiracy during the Dragon Boat Festival, thinking that without that cup of yellow wine, she wouldn't have been eternally confined to the tower. I thought all her sufferings stemmed from that man's plot with the realgar wine, thinking all her tears were due to Fa Hai's relentless pursuit. Actually, it wasn't. All of this happened because she lost herself in love, knowing it was unbearable yet drank the wine, knowing his cowardice and retreat yet still desired eternal life in the mortal world, stubbornly clinging to her love, which ultimately led her to a dead end —— she trapped herself in the cold dark Leifeng Pagoda.

The truth of the legendary story

In the morning, I went home, back to the house where I cohabited with Lin Qiu. Lin Qiu was slumped on the couch in the living room, looking haggard, just one night since my departure. He said, "You didn't come home all night, and I was worried." I remembered someone saying that love is like this — when you're high, he's low, and when you're low in the dust, he's high in the clouds. Just as I was about to leave in sudden enlightenment, Xia Lin Qiu started to care about me. I thought leaving this room where I had lived for so long would hurt a lot, but the inner tug was just shallow. Eight years, like clouds rolling and dispersing, became the past in an instant.

That beautiful love story passed down for so long is actually this cruel: a man poisoned the hand of a woman who loved him. It was May in the human world, the gardenia flowers in front of the courtyard bloomed beautifully and cleanly, the color of the umbrella on the Broken Bridge hadn't faded yet, and his woman, knowing the outcome, still drank this bitter wine. In fact, even without the gossipy Fa Hai, Xu Xian and Bai Suzhen's love would not have ended happily. People say their love is a legend, but in the end, it's just a story of a devoted woman drinking a bitter cup of wine and struggling desperately with herself.

And what ultimately caused the Leifeng Pagoda to collapse thunderously was not Fa Hai's cultivation, not Xu Xian's repentance, not the shift of heaven and earth over time, but Bai Suzhen herself finally realizing, no longer deceiving herself.

Buddha said, one thought leads to death, one thought leads to life.
