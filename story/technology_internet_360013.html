Wang Sheping: From Selling "Coal" to Selling "Airplane Tickets"

by ditan360 on 2010-07-06 14:34:53

Wang Sheping, Chairman of the Jizhong Energy Group and Hebei Airlines Group (photographed by Zhang Ke)

Exclusive report from Lowcarbon.org - Can a coal mine operator make medicine? Yes. Can it also operate an airline? Perhaps yes.

On Wang Sheping's new business card, in addition to his title as Chairman of Jizhong Energy Group, there is now another new title: Chairman of Hebei Aviation Investment Group Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "Hebei Airlines Group").

At around 11 a.m. on June 29, standing on the south apron of Shijiazhuang Zhengding International Airport, Zhang Yunquan, Secretary of the Hebei Provincial Committee of the CPC, loudly announced, "The first flight of Hebei Airlines has taken off!" This was the first time in the history of Hebei Province to have its own airline, its own aircraft, and see its own plane soar in the sky.

On the body of this Airbus A320, there were five large characters written: "Jizhong Energy No." Hebei Airlines is the core enterprise of Hebei Airlines Group, which was announced to be established just the day before.

The registered capital of Hebei Airlines Group is RMB 5 billion, all held by Jizhong Energy Group.

Why Jizhong Energy?

"Promoting the development of Hebei's civil aviation industry and filling the gap in Hebei's aviation operation entities, at the beginning of this year, the provincial government specially convened a provincial civil aviation work conference and made a strategic decision to accelerate the establishment of Hebei Airlines Group and implement integrated development of Hebei's civil aviation industry, airport economic zones, and related industries," Zhou Jie, Director of the Hebei Provincial SASAC, told reporters. But who could shoulder such a heavy responsibility? Many people didn't expect Jizhong Energy at that time.

In this world, every effect has its cause. Choosing Jizhong Energy Group to establish Hebei Airlines Group was not accidental but rather a series of seemingly accidental yet inevitable results.

Wang Sheping told reporters that although Jizhong Energy Group is mainly engaged in the coal industry, it has accumulated rich experience in diversified development.

In 2003, Handan Mining Group, the predecessor of Jizhong Energy, was facing the dilemma of coal resource depletion, with over 20,000 miners about to face unemployment.

Coal is as important to mines as grain is to farmers. "If there's no grain nearby, we will look for it further away!" Wang Sheping, then chairman of Handan Mining Group, said to everyone.

Wang Sheping turned his attention to Shengyuan Company in Zhangjiakou. On one hand, Shengyuan Company had abundant coal resources; on the other hand, Shengyuan Company was also hoping for new progress in management and operations.

Soon, Handan Mining Group completed the joint restructuring with Shengyuan Company and used this as a base to expand to surrounding areas and further into the Bashang region and Inner Mongolia. In one fell swoop, they acquired Kangbao Mine and Zhuolu Mine, establishing the Zhangjiakou Mining Area, increasing coal reserves from 6.69 billion tons in 2003 to 24.34 billion tons.

In 2005, Handan Mining Group merged with Xing Mining Group to form Jineng Group. Leveraging advantages in funds, projects, technology, and talent, the total assets of Jineng Group increased from RMB 6.2 billion before the merger to RMB 19 billion, annual sales revenue increased from RMB 1.5 billion to RMB 7.3 billion, and profits turned from an annual loss of RMB 95.5 million to a profit of over RMB 90 million.

In 2008, the strong alliance between Jineng Group and Fengfeng Group led to their renaming as "Jizhong Energy Group Co., Ltd.", with total assets amounting to RMB 28.298 billion and net assets totaling RMB 11.65 billion. In the first half of 2009, Jizhong Energy ranked first among Hebei's state-owned enterprises in terms of economic benefits.

On June 26, 2009, Jizhong Energy wrote a significant chapter in its pursuit of diversified development — restructuring Huayao to enter the biopharmaceutical industry.

Biopharmaceuticals are considered a capital-intensive, technology-intensive, and talent-intensive sector. Because the tablets produced are white, they are vividly called the "white industry." The "black and white" industries are vastly different and usually unrelated. However, with Jizhong Energy's restructuring of Huayao Group, these two industries began to have "intimate contact."

Wang Sheping told reporters that at the time, Huayao faced red lights in multiple aspects such as management, sales, and funding, with a funding gap of RMB 2.8 billion and encountering multiple difficulties in production and operation.

After taking control of Huayao, Jizhong Energy Group first launched a battle to turn losses into profits, accelerating the launch of new drugs. In the same year of restructuring, Huayao achieved historical highs in production and sales over the past three years, reaching 105 products and 89 products respectively. In the first quarter of this year, Huayao Group achieved sales revenue of RMB 2 billion, a year-on-year increase of 60.3%, and realized profits of RMB 150 million, a year-on-year increase of 18.27 times.

"Currently, Jizhong Energy already owns nine subsidiaries and three listed companies, forming a super-large enterprise with comprehensive development across multiple industrial sectors including coal as the mainstay, pharmaceuticals, power, chemicals, machinery, building materials, and logistics," Wang Sheping said.

Precisely because of Jizhong Energy Group's capability in restructuring, "After repeated research and careful deliberation, the provincial party committee and government decided that Jizhong Energy Group would take the lead in piloting the establishment of Hebei Airlines Group," Zhou Jie told reporters.

Filling the Gap in Large Airlines

However, the aviation industry is a completely different field.

Wang Sheping told reporters that the aviation industry has the characteristics of "high investment, high risk, low returns," with long growth periods, substantial construction investments, high operating costs, and enormous capital requirements. "There are many uncertain factors from establishment to normal operation and profitability."

Previously, although some local airlines were established, most of them did not operate well.

On the 28th, at the press conference announcing the establishment of Hebei Airlines Group, I noticed Wang Sheping's solemn expression and silence. However, during the next day's launch ceremony, he was radiant again.

He believed that people would come to sit on his planes.

According to data provided by the Hebei provincial government, in 2009, Hebei's GDP of RMB 1.70266 trillion ranked sixth nationwide, entering the national "trillion GDP club." Nearly 750,000 passengers flow annually to the four major airports of Beijing, Tianjin, Henan, and Shanxi, sufficient to support the normal development of an airline and airport. Currently, Hebei's per capita GDP exceeds USD 3,000, entering the stage of accelerated growth in aviation demand.

A previous responsible person from Shijiazhuang Airport frankly admitted, "The biggest problem currently facing the airport is insufficient aircraft capacity."

Hebei Airlines' fleet consists of three aircraft. Besides the Airbus A320 flying to Shanghai, there are two EMB-145s. According to long-term planning, by the end of the "12th Five-Year Plan," Hebei Airlines' fleet size will reach 20 aircraft, achieving an annual passenger transport capacity exceeding 3 million.

A plan from the Hebei provincial government regarding the "establishment of Hebei Aviation Investment Group Co., Ltd." proposes that by 2015, a preliminary framework of a reasonably configured and structurally complete aviation passenger service system, aviation logistics distribution system, and land-air associated industry system will be formed, generating revenue exceeding RMB 10 billion and entering the top ten domestic airlines;

By 2020, a modern civil aviation industry chain and advanced aviation industry productivity cluster will be basically formed, completing the historical transformation from a regional hub airline to a domestic backbone airline, with revenue reaching RMB 30 billion and entering the top five domestic airlines.

Zhang Yunquan is Wang Sheping's strongest backing. Before serving as the Secretary of the Hebei Provincial Committee, Zhang Yunquan was the Director of the State Commission of Science, Technology, and Industry for National Defense, having deep ties and extensive connections in the aviation industry.

On the 28th, a speech by Sun Ruibin, Vice Governor of Hebei Province, dispelled Wang Sheping's concerns: "During the cultivation and growth period of Hebei Aviation Investment Group and Hebei Airlines, the Hebei provincial government and relevant departments will provide strong support in terms of land, finance, taxation, route subsidies, and talent introduction."

Wang Sheping's vision is to create a quick and affordable "rapid air shuttle" system centered in Shijiazhuang, directly connecting nearby cities. This might be a niche market that domestic large airlines are unwilling to venture into.

Wang Sheping is a representative of the 11th National People's Congress and was named one of the "most influential model workers in Hebei since the founding of New China." There were rumors that Wang Sheping was aiming for the position of Governor of Hebei Province in the future. However, there is no record of political experience on his resume.

This did not affect his efforts to integrate market resources through government forces. The integration of Hebei's coal industry went through several twists and turns. But when Wang Sheping took action, it only took 20 days from submitting the request report to receiving approval from the provincial government. The restructuring of Huayao was also carried out swiftly. Similarly, the establishment of Hebei Airlines Group took only over 100 days.

"We are filling the blank space where Hebei does not have an airline," Wang Sheping said.

Relevant Information:

Hebei Aviation Investment Group Co., Ltd.

Hebei Aviation Group Co., Ltd. (English full name Hebei Aviation Investment Group Co., Ltd., hereinafter referred to as Hebei Airlines Group) was officially inaugurated on June 29, 2010. It was solely funded by Jizhong Energy Group and falls under the supervision of the Hebei Provincial SASAC as a large state-owned enterprise group. Its headquarters is located in Shijiazhuang, with a registered capital of RMB 5 billion.

Hebei Airlines Co., Ltd.

Hebei Airlines Co., Ltd. (Chinese abbreviation Hebei Airlines, English abbreviation HEBEI AIRLINES, hereinafter referred to as Hebei Airlines) is the core enterprise of Hebei Airlines Group Co., Ltd. It is a modern airline approved by the Civil Aviation Administration of China, controlled by Hebei Airlines Group Co., Ltd., with Sichuan Airlines Group Corporation and Shenyang Zhongrui Company participating as shareholders. Its main operational base is Shijiazhuang Zhengding International Airport, specializing in air passenger, cargo, and mail transportation services, while also engaging in general aviation services and businesses related to air transport.

Jizhong Energy Group Co., Ltd.

Jizhong Energy Group Co., Ltd. (abbreviation: Jizhong Energy) is a provincially owned sole proprietorship company in Hebei Province. It oversees nine subsidiaries: Fengfeng Group Co., Ltd., Jizhong Energy Co., Ltd., North China Pharmaceutical Group Co., Ltd., Handan Mining Group Co., Ltd., Zhangjiakou Mining Group Co., Ltd., Jingxing Mining Group Co., Ltd., Xingtai Mining (Group) Co., Ltd., Mechanical Equipment Co., Ltd., and Shanxi Jizhong Energy Mining Co., Ltd. It also owns three listed companies: Jizhong Energy, North China Pharmaceutical, and Jin Niu Chemical.

Jizhong Energy takes coal as its main business while developing multiple industrial sectors such as power, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, machinery, building materials, and logistics. The group has 130,000 employees on its roster, with total assets of RMB 73 billion and coal resource reserves of 26 billion tons. In 2009, it produced 42.37 million tons of coal, generated RMB 55 billion in sales revenue, and achieved a profit of RMB 2.01 billion.

In 2009, Jizhong Energy ranked 149th among the top 500 Chinese enterprises (excluding North China Pharmaceutical), 117th among the top 500 large industrial enterprises in China, 88th among the top 200 large enterprises with excellent economic efficiency in China, and was among the top 10 in the coal industry, making it the second-largest coking coal base in China.
