In wedding house装修, some mistakes are very foolish. The editor has collected the following eight "silly" mistakes, which must be avoided in your wedding house. 1. Walls with "aprons". Speaking of walls with "aprons", it was back in elementary school in the 80s. Whether at school or at home, "wall skirts" were a very popular kind of decoration at that time. Schools generally used blue paint, while homes used wallpaper or wooden boards to "decorate" the part of the wall below one meter. Times have changed, and after twenty years, if your home still has walls with "aprons", that's too "outdated"! 2. Ironwork everywhere. Purely a European product, a popular item among royalty in the 16th and 17th centuries, but now, what era are we in? Is the trend from hundreds of years ago still not over? There's no need for ironwork all over the house; your home is not an ironwork store. 3. Grids everywhere. The grid style on walls probably originated from Chinese palaces! However, there were many treasures in the palace, so they made many grids to hold these treasures. If you have many treasures at home, there's no need to make a "treasure cabinet" like in the palace. First, it's not practical, second, it's very inconvenient to clean. The palace had many people to clean up, but how many people do you have at home? 4. Walls painted in bright colors. Just in recent years, good white walls suddenly changed. They became colorful, full of all kinds of colors you can think of. But don't forget, strong colors appearing every day will cause visual fatigue over time and may also lead to nervous suppression. Therefore, try to make the walls in your home lighter in color, which is beneficial to health. 5. Looks like a hotel. It's unclear whether homes are becoming more like hotels or hotels feel more like homes. Such decoration is only suitable for hotels; don't decorate your own home this way. 6. Tiles look like puzzles. Tiles have become an indispensable part of the home, but be reminded not to use them excessively. Too many joints will make it visually messy and difficult to clean. So, go big, choose large tiles for aesthetics and functionality. 7. Plaster ceilings. It's unclear which country in the 80s introduced this to China, but ceiling decorations became quite popular for a long time. At that time, the concept of home decoration design was to solve the wiring problem for pendant lights by hiding irregular wiring in the ceiling. Nowadays, this method doesn't seem wise. Current wiring might be more user-friendly, considering the direction of internal wires and other common lines during the building stage. Therefore, there's no need to "hide" those wires now, nor to compromise by reducing the height between the ceiling and the floor by tens of centimeters. 8. Entrance hall looks like a gallery. You should know what an art exhibition looks like. Making your entrance hall resemble a gallery looks neither comfortable nor warm like a home.
