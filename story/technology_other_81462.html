Shang Zan: A Love Legend of Shangri-La

by ken119 on 2008-08-03 14:12:20

Now, what I'm about to tell you is not a story.

That legend, which once vividly existed in my life, is just a legend for you. Please allow me to use the way of storytelling to commemorate a tribe forgotten by the world, and a man who I deeply loved all my life.

Now, I have already turned white-haired, with wrinkles on my face that would almost scare any man away. But back then, let me say this without modesty, I was indeed a gentle and adorable woman. Otherwise, how could he - my Kenu, have fallen in love with me? Even now, when I look back, I still feel an overwhelming sense of happiness.

This cold house makes me feel at peace. Leaving the Na'vi was an unfortunate necessity for me, but it was also the only path I could choose. Don't lament or pity for me because I left my lifelong love there.

In my life, I've had the fortune to go there, that's enough.

I once read James Hilton's book, and his words were indeed somewhat biased. No matter what, I did manage to reach that place. It is a dreamlike utopia favored by the gods, and "beautiful" cannot even begin to describe its essence.

The bronze-colored skin, every pore exuding a primitive charm. His arms are so robust, his eyes so sharp, they see right through to my innermost desires and expectations. He was born to be the king of the Na'vi. Without his tribe, I couldn't stay a moment longer. Even if thorns tore my arms and landslides almost blocked my escape route, I still had to run, run, run... At that moment, only running blindly could let me momentarily forget this pain.

Sixty-four long years have passed, and I am still here waiting for a miracle. Every day and night, I pray here, hoping that heaven will let me see him again, my Kenu. Although such waiting offers little hope, apart from this, I really don't know how I can survive. With just this one belief, I am still sitting here, still telling you about my love legend.

Yes, you have obtained this fragrant hairpin, you are my destined person.

I know my time is limited, please listen quietly as I finish this story, do not interrupt me. I will try my best to detail it, you must know, my time is truly running out.

Then, dear you, could you help me fulfill my last wish, as a reward for listening to this story?

Touching this broken silver hairpin, tears stream down my face. The blood inside the silver hairpin is almost drained, and I haven't received any information about you yet. Now, apart from a story, I have no other belongings. So, I can only use my story for one last effort.

If heaven could show some mercy on this dying old woman, if heaven could remember my painstaking sixty-four-year wait. Please let me finish this story, if my hazy memory can still clearly recreate those picturesque landscapes, may heaven grant me more beautiful words!

I pry open my already inflexible fingers and calculate - it's almost there, almost sixty-four years. After tomorrow, our meeting will have gone through four cycles of tests. Just one day away from perfection, tonight, I come to tell you this story.

As the sky darkened, Zhu Ling twisted on the bedside lamp near the bed, pulling up the quilt for Old Ajiao, adjusting the height of the pillow, allowing Old Ajiao to lie in a more comfortable position to reminisce.

Her journalistic instincts quickly prompted her to pull out paper and pen from her bag to prepare for recording.
