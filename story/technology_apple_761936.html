BlackBerry Denies BBM Open to iOS, Android Will Lose Users, No WP Support for Now

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:18:49

On May 14th, BlackBerry announced that BBM would be freely available on iOS and Android platforms, which sparked concerns that existing BlackBerry users might switch to iOS or Android phones. Andrew Bocking, BlackBerry's Executive Vice President for Software and Ecosystem Development, stated in a conversation with Phoenix Technology that after adopting the BlackBerry 10 platform, BlackBerry not only attracts users through BBM but also offers competitive enterprise-level security services.Currently, BBM supports group chats, sharable pictures and videos, and groups of up to 30 people. It has 60 million BBM users globally, with 51 million active users who send 10 billion messages daily. Starting this summer, iOS 6 users and Android 4.0 users and above will be able to download the BBM app for free.If using the old BlackBerry operating platform, this is indeed possible, Andrew Bocking said, as many BlackBerry users choose BlackBerry phones mainly because of its unique BBM service. However, since the launch of the BlackBerry 10 platform, things have changed.Andrew Bocking believes that the BlackBerry 10 platform has enough appeal to maintain user retention, as besides the BBM application, the BES enterprise service based on the BlackBerry 10 platform is equally attractive.Yesterday, BlackBerry announced an upgrade of BES to version 10.1, supporting iOS, Android clients, BlackBerry operating systems, and the BlackBerry 10 platform simultaneously.In March, Microsoft's WP platform surpassed BlackBerry globally for the first time, ranking fifth with a share of 1.5%. Apple's dominant position remains unshaken, occupying 61.41% of the entire market share; Android ranks second with a share of 24.58%, while JavaME holds third place with a share of 8.16%.Regarding whether BBM will support the WP platform, Andrew Bocking revealed that currently, BBM user demand is primarily focused on BlackBerry's own operating system, iOS, and Android. The demand from WP platform users for BBM is limited. In the future, if necessary, BlackBerry will consider opening BBM to the WP platform.
