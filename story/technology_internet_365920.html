United Games original shareholders wildly reduce holdings; Tang Jun encounters a $1 crisis

by guxu on 2010-09-25 00:22:59

Uh, when you have bad luck, there's really nothing you can do about it.

Shanghai United Gaming Network Technology Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as United Gaming Network), after going public through a reverse merger with Dahua Construction, was not well-received by the public, including the previous long-term shareholders of Dahua Construction.

According to publicly available information, after United Gaming Network went public through the reverse merger, the original shareholders of Dahua Construction began a frenzy of selling off their shares. By September 14th, they had reduced their holdings by a total of 6,391,883 shares, at an average cost of $0.8 per share, for a total market value of approximately $5.12 million. In the eyes of industry insiders, the heavy sell-off by the original major shareholders of United Gaming Network may indicate that these shareholders are not optimistic about the future prospects of the listed company.
