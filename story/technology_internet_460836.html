Startup Lessons: Small Webmasters Can't Afford the Pain of Quick Website Filing

by nuo01 on 2011-07-12 09:43:27

Xiao Wu, who has been thinking about starting a business online, has recently become very upset. A website that he worked hard on was submitted for recordation more than three months ago, but it still hasn't been approved. In the meantime, he was even scammed out of 200 yuan. As all webmasters know, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) clearly stipulates that without a filing number, domestic hosting will not be bound under any circumstances, so Xiao Wu's website was naturally shut down. In a hurry, Xiao Wu quickly sought help online and fell for the scam of quick filing.

Similarly, there have always been countless posts online offering so-called rapid filing services for a fee, claiming that no documents need to be provided and the filing number can be obtained within a day. These are illegal activities conducted by lawbreakers online, either in violation of regulations or exploiting the lax verification processes of some access providers. Do not believe them.

Xiao Wu is one such victim. After transferring money to the scammer, his site remained unregistered and his money was gone. Additionally, the website might be blacklisted due to inaccurate filing information. If the filing information is untrue or inaccurate, even if it passes the review, it will be immediately canceled once the false information is discovered.

Moreover, according to relevant provisions of the "Domestic Internet Website Blacklist Management System," websites with false filing information, especially those intentionally falsified, may have their domain names placed on the blacklist by the Communications Administration Bureau. Once a domain name is added to the blacklist, getting it removed and onto the whitelist will be extremely difficult. Thirdly, the official filing process isn't complicated. As long as the information is filled out truthfully and conforms to standard formats, it generally gets approved. China Nuowang (www.nuo.cn), which specializes in information services for small and medium-sized enterprises, advises everyone to follow the official filing procedures and not to blindly trust online claims of "rapid filing."

Recently, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology issued a notice stating that some individuals have been impersonating "website filing agencies" offering "rapid filing" services and charging fees, infringing on the legitimate rights and interests of website owners. The MIIT also reminds everyone that there is no such thing as "rapid filing" in the website filing process, and the filing process does not involve any charges. If you find cases of unfiled websites, false filings, or scams under the guise of website filing, please report them to the MIIT at phone number 010-66418012 or email beian@miit.gov.cn. The MIIT will investigate and handle these issues.

Building a website isn't easy, so all webmasters should prepare in advance and file their websites early to legally provide internet information services. China Nuowang launched a special filing guide back in 2008 (http://www.nuo.cn/beian/beian.htm). It addresses various difficulties and questions during the filing process and provides detailed instructions on how to complete the process quickly. Webmasters currently facing filing issues are encouraged to check it out. Entrepreneurship cannot afford to fail at the starting line.
