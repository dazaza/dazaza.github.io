What do you fear most when you get old?

by shunyuan on 2012-02-25 10:52:10

Old age, still thinking about holding grandchildren. But... In old age, can't eat anymore! With the development of the economy, the function of families economically supporting the elderly might be entirely replaced by an increasingly perfect social security system. Modern society gives the elderly more choices in lifestyle.

As one gets older, memory tends to fade.

Zhang Huifang has spent her entire life doing housework. Now, even as she sits in a wheelchair, she still insists on helping others with chores and taking care of children, which makes people laugh at her for being senile.

The only activity Old Han does every day is leaning against the corridor alone and finishing two packs of cigarettes.

When sick, there's no place or money to see a doctor, so they just sleep it off to ease the pain.

Medal of Honor. Once, they were also proud.

The issue of elder care is something each of us will inevitably face. With the implementation of the family planning policy, most families in our country will gradually evolve into a "124" structure: one couple, two families, four elders. This family structure means we will face even stricter elder care issues.

In simple terms, providing for the elderly is "giving time to life," while offering them spiritual comfort at a higher level is "giving life to time." In a certain sense, every elder is a hero. After enduring the passage of youth, experiencing all the flavors of life, societal changes, turmoil, and even war, their still-beating pulses are nothing short of a final triumph of life.

After sitting for a while at her daughter’s home, she has to return to the senior apartment.

Seeing a doctor requires someone's help.

When feeling lonely, he lights up a cigarette.

Lonely and desolate, the elderly reminisce about their past struggles with great enthusiasm.

Leftover steamed buns from the previous meal.

Daughter sending her father back to the senior apartment, sending away one's birth parents from home is often out of necessity. Even though everyone may have their own families now, the elders still have to be reluctantly abandoned, which cannot help but make one heartbroken.

87-year-old Mr. Liu has been paralyzed in bed for over two years due to cerebral embolism. He cannot speak or walk.

Various forms of social elder care institutions have emerged. Alongside these come varying levels of fees, and the seniors living there have no worries about food and clothing. When we think that the elderly can enjoy their twilight years, we overlook a very important issue: the elderly not only have basic survival needs, but they also have psychological needs. They need emotional comfort to overcome the feelings of loss and loneliness after leaving work and their families.

The loneliness of the elderly cannot be dispelled by expensive entertainment and fitness facilities. For those who are physically challenged or suffering from various severe illnesses and geriatric diseases, lying in bed, their entire world becomes the bed beneath them and the ceiling in front of them.

Old Sun called the nurse into his room, moments ago he was thinking of asking for some help. But then he forgot what he wanted to say. Forgetfulness is a significant sign of brain aging.

Editor's postscript: As the saying goes: having an elder at home is like having a treasure. However, currently in many families, this "treasure" has been forgotten or even abandoned. Everyone will grow old. If you treat your elders this way now, when you grow old, it's not impossible that the same could happen to you. The elderly contributed greatly to society and their families during their younger years; enjoying their golden years is their right and also the responsibility of their children to respect and care for their elders. Are you making sure that your elders are happily enjoying their twilight years? Finally, ask yourself, what do you fear most when you grow old?

We are all growing continuously; after youth comes adulthood, after adulthood comes middle age. But time waits for no one, and old age eventually comes. Imagine, when old age arrives, what do you fear most? My answer is loneliness. Enduring the torment of illness in solitude until dying alone is undoubtedly the most terrifying thing in life. Look at the pictures below; you will understand what happiness in old age looks like, and you will realize why I fear loneliness in old age!
