The Legend of Lai Buyi

Chapter One: Lai Chengshan Searches for a Auspicious Grave Site, Bringing Fortune to Future Generations

During the Huizong period of the Song Dynasty, in Fenggang, Dingnan County, Jiangxi Province, there was a geography master well-versed in classical texts named Lai Buyi. From a young age, he was intelligent and had thoroughly studied the Four Books and Five Classics, passing the title of Xiucai at the age of nine. His father, Lai Chengshan, was also a renowned geography master at the time, known for his kindness and extensive knowledge.

Lai Buyi's original name was Lai Fenggang, with the courtesy name Wenjun. The nickname "Buyi" was given to him by later generations. At the age of eleven, after the death of his grandfather, Lai Chengshan called him over and said, "My child, your intelligence surpasses others, so I do not intend to teach you the art of Feng Shui. I hope that in the future, you can achieve fame and fortune, bringing honor to the Lai family. Now I must leave home to find a resting place for your grandfather, and perhaps with the help of Feng Shui, bring you success and prosperity."

Thus, Lai Chengshan left home in search of the dragon's lair. Following the Nine Peaks Mountain, he arrived in LePing County, Guangdong, where the mountains were clear and the water beautiful, exuding a powerful aura. He guessed that somewhere nearby, an auspicious grave site might be hidden. As he continued climbing, the sky grew dark. Suddenly, a strong wind blew down from the mountain top, causing Lai Chengshan to stumble. Looking up, he saw a black kite as large as an eagle flying from the north and disappearing on the opposite cliff.

Lai Chengshan felt puzzled. The kite was about two zhang long and nine zhang wide. Could it be a spirit transformed into a kite? To investigate further, Lai Chengshan immediately got up and walked for about four to five hours, finally reaching the opposite cliff. After looking around and finding no sign of any kite, he suddenly noticed a strangely shaped boulder standing by the cliff edge. It turned out that the kite he had seen earlier was actually a rock formation, which behind it revealed what geomancers call a dragon's lair. Lai Chengshan immediately calculated that the descendants of the family buried here would produce a prime minister and a national teacher, with their lineage continuing gloriously for generations. Overjoyed, he then noticed the moonlight shining directly on the mountain tomb and sighed, “It’s a 'teacher-violating land,' what a pity!" In Feng Shui theory, a 'teacher-violating land' refers to a place that most easily absorbs the essence of the sun and moon; if ancestors are buried here, the descendants will enjoy endless blessings, but the person who arranges the burial will encounter misfortune within three years, either becoming disabled or even dying.

Lai Chengshan became worried, thinking that although he knew it was a 'teacher-violating land', for the sake of his descendants and his hardworking father, sacrificing his life would be worth it. The next morning, Lai Chengshan hurriedly returned home. After more than thirty days of travel, he finally made it back to Jiangxi. Upon entering the door, he told his son Lai Buyi, "My child! Father has found a blessed place that will greatly benefit your future. In the future, you should take good care of this family." Lai Buyi nodded in agreement but did not believe in the mysteries of Feng Shui at all. He asked Lai Chengshan when they would bury their grandfather, but Lai Chengshan told him it would be in five years. Although Lai Buyi found it strange, he did not ask further, thinking his father must have his reasons.

Time flew by, and five years passed quickly. By now, Lai Buyi had been studying diligently for several years, growing physically stronger and more mature in dealing with people and affairs. Lai Chengshan thought it was time for his father to be laid to rest, so he chose an auspicious day, ordering the servants to prepare incense and paper money for their journey to the mountain in LePing County, Guangdong. As soon as dawn broke, Lai Chengshan set off with Lai Buyi and several servants. Upon arrival, Lai Chengshan adjusted the meridian line and instructed the servants to lower the coffin. Just as they were about to cover the soil, one servant, needing to relieve himself, urinated near the boulder before Lai Chengshan could stop him. Lai Chengshan could only sigh, saying, "Everything is predestined, not a bit under human control."

Seeing his father's troubled expression, Lai Buyi approached and asked, "Father, what's wrong? Why are you so worried?"

Lai Chengshan replied, "This blessed land could have helped you achieve official success and lifelong glory, but now that a servant has urinated here, dispersing the mountain's spiritual energy, at most you will only become a national teacher."

Lai Buyi consoled his father, saying, "Father, even being a national teacher isn't bad! Moreover, it continues your legacy, and if I become a world-renowned geography master, it would still bring honor to our family, wouldn't it?"

From that day on, Lai Chengshan began to impart all his Feng Shui knowledge to Lai Buyi. While Lai Buyi studied diligently, he also learned the art of Feng Shui. He believed that learning another skill would only be beneficial. However, he still hoped to succeed in the upcoming exams.

Time passed swiftly, and two more years went by. On this day, Lai Buyi was packing his luggage, preparing to go to the capital for the imperial examination. Before leaving, his father earnestly advised him not to value fame too much and to use the art of Feng Shui to benefit the countryside, which was the most practical path. Lai Buyi said, "Father, please rest assured. These past few years, I've been studying late into the night, and the private tutor values me highly. You just wait and see me return in glory."

On the way to the capital, he secretly inquired and found that among the scholars from various provinces, none matched his academic prowess. Secretly pleased, he thought that this year's top scholar would surely be him.

On the day of the exam, Lai Buyi entered the examination hall and wrote quickly. Surprisingly, he completed the three-day test in just one day. As he reviewed his work, he heard painful groans from the neighboring room. Lai Buyi stood up to check. He saw a fellow examinee rolling on the ground, clutching his abdomen. Lai Buyi immediately helped him up. With clenched teeth and pale face, Lai Buyi guessed it was probably due to discomfort with the local water and food, causing cramps and fever. So, he gave him some medicine pills. Half an hour later, the man woke up. "I'm Liu Zhongda, from Xiuyong, Jiangxi. My family is very poor, and on the way to the capital, I barely ate anything. Normally, I eat leftovers given by others, so today..." Liu Zhongda began to cry, sighing, "If I fail this year's exam, I might as well die." Lai Buyi comforted him and instructed him to rest well to continue the exam over the next two days. Unexpectedly, by noon on the third day, Liu Zhongda's condition had not improved. But since this exam was his only hope, he wanted to try writing despite his weakness. Seeing his pale face and burning body, Lai Buyi felt compassion and said, "Brother Liu, as long as the green hills remain, there will always be firewood. Why push yourself so hard? If you trust my writing skills, let me write for you!" Liu Zhongda had no other choice but to agree. After finishing the exam, Lai Buyi quickly returned to his own testing room to wait for the proctor to collect the papers.

After submitting the exam, Lai Buyi urgently took Liu Zhongda to see a doctor. After a month of recuperation, Liu Zhongda gradually recovered and repeatedly thanked Lai Buyi for saving his life.

In the blink of an eye, the day of the announcement arrived. Lai Buyi eagerly awaited his name on the list but was surprised to find that Liu Zhongda was the top scorer. At this moment, he suddenly remembered his father's words before he left, and could only bitterly smile. After bidding farewell to Liu Zhongda, Lai Buyi packed his belongings and left the capital.
