[Reprint] Important Methods for Handling Interpersonal Relationships in the Office (Part One)

by koucai7346 on 2012-02-21 14:22:23

In the workplace, each one of us is an important member of this team, and this is also the content we will discuss today—how to conduct effective team communication and team building. Since we are all independent individuals, some of our behaviors are carried out independently. This means that everyone exists independently. However, in the workplace, it is necessary for every member to act like an individual so as to bring out the important role and energy of the team. Thus, the question arises: how can we make the team united and have strong combat effectiveness? This is what the Elocution Training Network will share with you today—how to be a good team player and how to manage interpersonal relationships and team communication.

Being in the workplace, we must be prepared for everything, and it is possible that we may have to give up some of our own personalities because only in this way can we build good relations with everyone in the team, which will help with team building. For example, if you are very loving and understand workplace etiquette, these will help you survive better in the team and enhance the cohesion of the team. Therefore, let's take a look together at how exactly we should do to have excellent networking in the team.

1. Moderate Praise, Smooth Sailing

Each person has their own inner desire for "evaluation," hoping others can understand and give praise. This is a kind of subconsciousness we have as human beings, all hoping to become the center of attention and hoping the efforts we make can be recognized and affirmed by others. As a leader, timely encouragement and commendation should be given to subordinates' certain abilities, guiding them to work harder. In fact, being a leader requires eloquence, and there are many things that need to be understood, such as the art of speech, psychology, and motivation, etc., which are essential for a leader.

2. Empathy, Sympathy

This phrase means perspective-taking. When we think about problems from another's point of view, you will find that when you change your angle, the way you look at problems will also change accordingly. Especially for leaders, if you can consider problems from employees' perspectives, then the employees will feel that you are a humane leader, increasing your popularity and leadership credibility from another side. As a member of the team, if you can consider problems from others' perspectives, you will also get a very good effect, and colleagues will think you are a nice person who they would like to be friends with.

3. Seek Common Ground While Reserving Differences, Shorten Gaps

Between peers or between superiors and subordinates, there more or less exist "common awareness." As a leader, in order to effectively persuade colleagues or subordinates, one should keenly grasp this common awareness, so as to seek common ground while reserving differences, shortening the psychological gap with the persuaded object, thereby achieving the purpose of persuasion. Leaders need to try to shorten the psychological distance with others. The proposal of common awareness can make even those who strongly oppose the leader no longer contrary to the leader's opinions, and calmly listen to the leader's persuasion. Thus, the leader gets the opportunity to explain his/her views and further penetrate into others' hearts.

4. Open-hearted Communication, Emotional Appeal

An ancient saying goes: "Nothing moves the heart more than emotion." To a large extent, the leader's persuasion work is the conquest of emotions. Only by skillfully using emotional techniques, appealing to emotions, and moving people with feelings, can one touch the heart. Emotion is the bridge of communication; to persuade others, one must cross this bridge to reach the other party's psychological fortress and conquer them. When the leader persuades others, he/she should open-heartedly communicate, appeal to emotions, explain the pros and cons, making the other party feel that the leader's advice does not carry any personal purpose or bad intention, but sincerely helps the advised person, considering his/her immediate interests. Bai Juyi once wrote these two lines of poetry: "How swift the success comes, it lies in putting heart into others' stomachs." Although times have changed, the principle remains the same.
