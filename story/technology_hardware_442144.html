Personal Gourmet Plan

Key target groups: Pregnant women, teenagers, white-collar workers, businessmen, and the elderly.

Plan:

Pregnant Women

1) Preparing for pregnancy

Time: Two to three months before pregnancy

Dosage: 3-5 grams of dried bird's nest per serving, once daily. Can be consumed on an empty stomach in the morning or evening.

Purpose: Not only strengthens the mother, but also makes the future newborn stronger, whiter, more beautiful, and less prone to illness.

2) The first three months of pregnancy (0-12 weeks), known as early pregnancy. There may be symptoms such as dizziness, weakness, fatigue, sleepiness, and reduced appetite. Some may experience unusual cravings, picky eating, preference for sour tastes, and aversion to greasy foods. It is not recommended to consume during this period; start after the pregnancy reaction subsides.

3) Pregnancy weeks 13-27 are considered mid-pregnancy, a stable period for both mother and fetus.

Time: Weeks 13-27 of pregnancy.

Dosage: 3-5 grams of dried bird's nest per serving, once daily. Can be consumed on an empty stomach in the morning or evening.

Purpose: Supplements nutrition for both mother and baby, providing quick replenishment for the baby.

4) Late pregnancy begins at week 28.

Time: After week 28.

Dosage: 3-5 grams of dried bird's nest per serving, once daily or every other day. After hospitalization, it is recommended to use ready-to-eat bird's nest, 20-30 grams per serving, saving family stewing time.

Purpose: Prevent excessive fatigue.

5) Postpartum women

Time: Postpartum women.

Dosage: 3-5 grams of dried bird's nest per serving, once daily. Can be consumed on an empty stomach in the morning or evening.

Purpose: Relieves the fatigue of raising a child, helps restore pre-pregnancy figure; regulates physical condition, removes chronic ailments, and lays a solid health foundation for the future.

The simplest way to consume bird's nest during pregnancy is to place 3-5 grams of soaked and stewed bird's nest or 20-30 grams of ready-to-eat bird's nest into milk, soy milk, porridge, honey, or lotus root powder. Start consumption three months before pregnancy, once daily or every other day.
