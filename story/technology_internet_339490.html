Hotel owner lowers flag to half-mast due to mother's passing away

by xia2oo007 on 2010-04-03 15:36:08

Fengmiao Town is a small town in the northern part of Lingbi County, Suzhou City. Recently, an event occurred in this small town: since March 28th, the three flags in front of a hotel in the town were lowered to half-mast, one of which was the national flag —— the Five-Star Red Flag.

How could the sacred national flag be lowered to half-mast without reason? Yesterday, the reporter went to Fengmiao Town, Lingbi County for investigation. Local residents told the reporter that the hotel was opened by a somewhat famous person surnamed Yan in the locality. On March 28th, Yan's mother passed away, and from that day on, the national flag was lowered to half-mast.

Investigation

The national flag at the hotel entrance was lowered

After receiving feedback from readers, the reporter immediately drove to Fengmiao Town, Lingbi County.

Following a tarmac road, the reporter entered Fengmiao Town. Not more than two or three hundred meters later, the reporter saw a two-story hotel with the words "Garden Hotel" written large on its roof. Approaching the hotel, the reporter noticed a tent erected outside the main door, surrounded by wreaths, indicating it was a mourning hall.

What astonished the reporter was that the three flags on three flagpoles outside the hotel had all been lowered to half-mast. The flags on the side poles were light green, while the one in the middle was the national flag —— the Five-Star Red Flag. Behind the flags were wreaths, and beside the flagpoles was a paper ox used for sacrificial purposes.

"A national flag can't just be lowered to half-mast like this! It's sheer nonsense!" Since this hotel was located in a busy area of Fengmiao Town, many vehicles and pedestrians passed by, and the lowering of the national flag drew much attention, sparking discussions among people. Some residents told the reporter that this incident had caused a very bad influence in the town.

Female Employee

The national flag was lowered to half-mast on March 28th

The reporter learned during interviews that the "Garden Hotel" was opened by a local person surnamed Yan. Besides this hotel, Yan also operated two agricultural supply stores in the town, both doing fairly well. "He is capable and quite approachable, quite famous in our town," local residents reported.

Under the guidance of local residents, the reporter found one of Yan's agricultural supply stores, but no one was inside. Nearby residents told the reporter that Mr. Yan had gone home to handle his mother's funeral arrangements. Subsequently, the reporter came to another agricultural supply store opened by Yan and saw a female employee there.

The female employee told the reporter that she was Mr. Yan's daughter. She said her grandmother passed away on March 28th, and her father had been handling the funeral arrangements at home.

When asked about the issue of the national flag being lowered to half-mast, the female employee said that the national flag was lowered to half-mast on the day her grandmother passed away. "Who came up with the idea of lowering the flag?" Faced with this question, the female employee smiled but did not answer.

Town Government

Did not notice the national flag being lowered

During the interview, the reporter found that the "Garden Hotel" was right next to the Fengmiao Town government office, only about 50 meters away.

As of yesterday, the sacred national flag had been lowered to half-mast for six days. Why had no one intervened during such a long period of time? At the Fengmiao Town Party and Government Office, when asked about the issue of the national flag being lowered to half-mast, staff member Cao Jindragon appeared surprised, claiming he did not know about the matter.

Cao Jindragon told the reporter that Yan's hotel was excellent locally, and the person himself was very decent. "He loves the country, he wouldn't do something like this, would he?"

"Since the town government is so close to the hotel, hasn't anyone noticed the half-mast flag?" Facing such questions, Cao Jindragon answered: "We go to work and leave every day, and no one has noticed this."

Party Concerned

I don't know who did it

At around 4:30 PM yesterday, when the reporter passed by the "Garden Hotel" again, the flags on the three flagpoles had disappeared.

According to Cao Jindragon, after they received the reporter's feedback, they immediately went to check the hotel and indeed found that the national flag had been lowered to half-mast.

"Private residences cannot arbitrarily raise the national flag, let alone lower it to half-mast," Cao Jindragon said. They immediately instructed Yan to take down the national flag and criticized and educated him. "He said that when he lowered the other two flags, he also lowered the national flag, probably because his awareness of the national flag was relatively weak," Cao Jindragon added.

At around 7:20 PM last night, the reporter got in touch with Yan. Yan said he did not know about the flag-lowering incident.

Under the reporter's persistent questioning, Yan changed his statement, saying that there were no flags on the flagpoles now, and claimed "I don't know who did it." He then hung up the phone.

Subsequently, the reporter dialed his number again, but the phone remained busy.

Lawyer

This act violates the National Flag Law

Does lowering the national flag to half-mast constitute a violation of the law? The reporter consulted lawyer Guo Ai from Anhui Wang Liangqi Law Firm about this matter. Guo Ai stated that if Yan lowered the flag to mourn his deceased mother, although it shows his filial piety, his action has violated China's National Flag Law.

According to Guo Ai, according to the provisions of the National Flag Law, private residences are not allowed to hoist the national flag. For lowering the flag to mourn, Article 14 of the National Flag Law clearly stipulates that the dates and places for lowering the flag are decided by the state-established funeral organization or the State Council. Moreover, Article 18 of the National Flag Law explicitly states that the national flag shall not be used for private funerals.

Regarding the unauthorized lowering of the flag to mourn, Guo Ai pointed out that current national laws do not have clear penalty provisions for this behavior, but the illegality of the act itself is beyond doubt.

Guo Ai emphasized that when private individuals lower the flag to mourn, the government department should immediately intervene to stop it and make appropriate handling. (Chang Guoshui)

National Flag Law of the People's Republic of China

Passed at the 14th meeting of the Standing Committee of the 7th National People's Congress on June 28, 1990

Implemented from October 1, 1990

Article 3: The national flag of the People's Republic of China is the symbol and sign of the People's Republic of China.

Every citizen and organization shall respect and cherish the national flag.

Article 4: Local governments at all levels supervise and manage the hoisting and use of the national flag within their administrative regions.

Article 14: In case of the death of the following persons, the national flag shall be flown at half-mast:

(1) The President of the People's Republic of China, the Chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the Premier of the State Council, the Chairman of the Central Military Commission;

(2) The Chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference;

(3) Persons who have made outstanding contributions to the People's Republic of China;

(4) Persons who have made outstanding contributions to world peace or the progress of humanity.

In the event of an exceptionally serious accident or severe natural disaster causing major casualties, the national flag may be flown at half-mast.

Decisions regarding lowering the flag under items (3) and (4) of paragraph 1 and paragraph 2 of this article shall be made by the State Council.

The dates and places for lowering the flag under this article shall be decided by the state-established funeral organization or the State Council.

Article 18: The national flag and its patterns shall not be used as trademarks or advertisements, nor shall they be used for private funerals.

Article 19: Whoever intentionally insults the national flag of the People's Republic of China in public by burning, damaging, drawing, defiling, or trampling it shall be held criminally liable; if the circumstances are relatively minor, the public security organ shall impose detention of no more than fifteen days according to the provisions of the Public Security Administration Punishment Regulations.
