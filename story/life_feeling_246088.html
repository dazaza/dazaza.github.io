Question One: Do you love me?

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'Yes.'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Do I even need to answer that?'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'Are you annoying on purpose?'

>> > Standard Answer: Gaze lovingly at her for three seconds, then nod solemnly while making an 'Mmm' sound, then pull her into your arms...

>> > Analysis: Answer A will make her feel like you're not taking it seriously and just fooling around with her; Answer B will make her feel like your love for her isn't firm enough, and she will definitely keep asking until you give her a clear answer, which will annoy you; Answer C will surely lead to a big fight or a cold war. Actually, women have a pretty good idea whether men love them or not. Asking this question is purely coquetry. Hugging her and kissing her will make her happier than any verbal response.

>> > Question Two: Do you think this dress looks good on me?

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'It looks fine.'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Not bad.'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'It looks great, everything looks good on my wife.'

>> > Standard Answer: Come here, turn around so I can see... After she shyly turns around, pull her close, take her hand, smile at her and say: 'It really does look good.'

>> > Analysis: Answer A will make her feel like you're brushing her off; Answer B will make her lose confidence because in her heart, whatever she wears, you should think it's good; but if you really say it looks good, like Answer C, she might think you're being insincere. So, you need to show seriousness about the question she asked, you must take a careful look before answering.

>> > Question Three: After introducing her best friend to you, when she gets home, she asks: 'What do you think of my friend?'

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'She's quite pretty.'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Not really, she's nowhere near as good as you.'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'I didn't really notice her.'

>> > Standard Answer: I think she's very sincere towards you, you should cherish such a friend.

>> > Analysis: Answer A is pure trouble-seeking. Women cannot tolerate hearing you say anything positive about other women in front of them (of course, except for mothers); Answer B sounds too fake; Answer C might seem clever to you, but she won't believe it for a second, and she will start imagining why you don't want to evaluate her friend. Therefore, you should avoid the question and answer indirectly, making her feel that everything you do is for her, giving her a sense of being cared for.

>> > Question Four: Do you think I've gained weight?

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'No, you haven't.'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Seems like you have.'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'Oh, aren't you tired recently? How come you've become so thin?'

>> > Standard Answer: Let me hug you, after hugging her, say: I like you this way, a little plump.

>> > Analysis: Answer A is too straightforward, it doesn't satisfy her. Answer B is simply asking for trouble, how could girls like hearing others say they are fat? Even if you repeatedly emphasize that you like chubby girls, she will still be unhappy; Answer C sounds so insincere, modern women aren't that easy to fool. In fact, in a woman's heart, she hopes men don't care about whether she is fat or thin. Although she says she wants to lose weight for you, it's actually for herself to be more beautiful and wear more nice clothes. So showing your preference for her having a bit of meat through body language is enough.

>> > Question Five: Compared to my ex-girlfriend, who do you like better?

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'Of course it's you, otherwise why would I be with you?'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Hmm, well, if I were to tell the truth, each has their own merits.'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'She's much worse than you.'

>> > Standard Answer: Bite her hard, kiss her, or pinch her cheeks. When she starts cooing cutely, say: 'Next time you ask such a boring question, you'll get punished again.'

>> > Analysis: Answer A is too blunt and direct, she will follow up by asking: 'If you meet someone better than me in the future, would you dump me?' Answer B means you're heading towards a breakup, too honest. Answer C sounds okay, but aren't you slapping yourself in the face? Saying something negative about your ex-girlfriend makes the woman feel smug but also lowers your value. Actually, in a woman's heart, she hopes your ex-girlfriend was an exceptionally excellent woman, but you chose not to stay with her, this gives her a sense of accomplishment. But you can't directly say how good your ex-girlfriend was, so use a method she likes to avoid the question (whether she likes you pinching her cheeks or biting her is another matter).

>> > Question Six: If both me and your mom fall into the water, who will you save?

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'Are you serious?'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Both of you.'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'Honey, don't put me in such a dilemma, besides, this situation won't happen.'

>> > Standard Answer: Oh, I forgot to tell you, my mom is a swimming champion.

>> > Analysis: Although asking this question is indeed very silly, you can't respond like Answer A, which will lead to endless arguments; Answer B will make her persistently ask further questions, do you want some peace? Answer C sounds decent, but still feels forced. Why not be humorous once, stop her from worrying unnecessarily and finding problems where there are none all day long.

>> > Question Seven: If I grow old and become unattractive, will you still love me?

>> > Wrong Answer A: 'Impossible, you will still look good when you're old.'

>> > Wrong Answer B: 'Women have different charms at different ages...'

>> > Wrong Answer C: 'Don't worry, when you're old, I won't look good either, neither of us will dislike the other.'

>> > Standard Answer: I hope you become less attractive and older, so I can feel more secure keeping you at home.

>> > Analysis: Answer A will make her happy, but she knows deep down it's absolutely impossible; Answer B stop right there, you sound like Tang僧 (a reference to the monk from Journey to the West known for his verbosity); Answer C is essentially saying she will definitely be unattractive when she's old, how can she accept that? Therefore, the smartest answer is one that dispels her worries and makes her know how much you care about her and how afraid you are of losing her.
