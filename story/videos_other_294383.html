Style conversion signal appears, weight highlights the future market trend＿stocks

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:43:35

From this week's market performance, on the one hand, we see a very active market in small and medium-sized stocks with theme-related topics. They not only stimulate the activity of their related sectors but also warm up the market sentiment which became depressed after last week's sharp decline. They even contribute significantly to the stabilization of the index and the repair of short-term technical patterns. Examples include Sinoma Automobile, United Chemicals, Feida Environmental Protection, and Yong'an Forestry. However, at the same time, the demand for adjustment from the continuous rise of small and medium-sized stocks over the past quarter and the accumulation of profits from them further manifested during this week's market rally. This bubble-like risk will intensify the differentiation and volatility of individual stocks and future market trends, as demonstrated by the performance of individual stocks on Friday. On the other hand, we also notice that blue-chip stocks have started to show signs of upward movement this week. For instance, the strengthening of the steel sector led by Baosteel and the sustained force of the coal sector led by Jinniu Energy have provided the market with momentum for gradual growth and strong fluctuations. In general, the market situation this week should be in a painful transition period where the earlier style dominated by small and medium-sized stock themes gradually shifts to blue-chip weight stocks. Before this style transformation is completely successful, the market will experience an upward trend with fluctuations, and the differentiation of individual stocks will continue to increase in future market trends. Analyzing the market movements of blue-chip stocks on Friday, although the start of banks and brokers might be influenced by news, looking at the market situation entering the last trading month of the year, these blue-chip stocks with relatively smaller increases inherently have a need to catch up and improve their annual reports. Coupled with the arrival of the year-end performance wave and the market's expectations of future policy directions, they will attract more capital attention in the future market. That is to say, the pace of the market style shift will continue to unfold. Technically, after breaking above the 120-week moving average line this week, the market sentiment will become more optimistic next week. With the upward 60-week moving average line forming a golden cross signal with the 250-week moving average line, their support effect on the index's upward movement will further manifest in the future. Finally, the red histogram of the MACD indicator on the daily K-line chart has reappeared. Although currently small in length, we have reasons to believe that a spark can still start a prairie fire. With the 5-day moving average line forming a golden cross with the 10-day and 20-day moving average lines on Friday, and the breakout of the pressure level at 3300 points with increased volume, the upward space for the index in the future has been opened.

Given the relatively optimistic fundamental and technical aspects of the current market, investors need to guard against risks brought by the differentiation of future themes. Under the premise that the overall mid-term upward trend remains unchanged, focusing on varieties with consistently excellent performance, grasping the market rhythm and trend, steady profits following the index upward movement are highly anticipated.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: You must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. Stock abbreviations usually consist of three to four Chinese characters, while the stock code is six digits. When placing an order, the stock code and abbreviation must match. Additionally, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the number of shares for a buy order must be a multiple of 100, but the number of shares for a sell order does not have to be a multiple of 100.

There are four types of orders: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
