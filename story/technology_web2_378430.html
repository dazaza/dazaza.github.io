Roadshow Network: An Online Roadshow Production Website

by ybcz0519 on 2011-01-16 15:46:32

Luyan.net is an online roadshow production website. In my understanding, it's a service that fully records the process of a presentation through video. Simply put, it can turn the content you upload into a presentation document. You can then add voice explanations while demonstrating this document, and the entire demonstration process will be completely recorded, generating an online roadshow video.

Main features:

- Supports direct login with Google or Yahoo accounts;

- Single roadshow creation time is 8 minutes;

- Uploaded content can be images, PPTs, MP3s, or docs;

- During recording, in addition to voice recording, you can also make various text markings on the content, and these marking processes will also be recorded;

- You can choose the cover image for the recorded video;

- Professional users can directly insert webcam videos inside;

- You can set permissions for the recorded videos, such as public, private, or password-protected;

- You can share your created public content directly via a link;

Above is the page for making online roadshows. Users can upload various types of content to create presentation documents. After the presentation document is ready, you can start recording. During the recording process, you can annotate the presentation document with a marker pen, and all operations during the entire presentation process will be recorded. Once you click "recording complete," the video recording is finished. You can share the produced video content via email or directly through a link.

Watsonxu's take: Personally, I think it's very meaningful to record the entire process of a presentation, including voice and demonstrations, in video form. Perhaps based on this, a more three-dimensional SlideShare could be created, offering a lot of imaginative space. It also holds great value for businesses and education. Of course, regarding Luyan.net at present, I personally feel there are still some things that can be improved, such as the website name, which may need to allow users to understand the site's functions more directly; another aspect is functionality—there’s room for improvement in how uploaded PPTs are displayed. However, I really like this model. If the product is improved well, it should have very good development prospects.
