Can an abortion be performed at 2 months of pregnancy? Experts from the Shanxi Huimin Hospital Gynecology Department introduce that if an unintended pregnancy does not involve wanting to keep the child, it cannot be terminated at any time; there is a best timeframe for the procedure. Based on menstrual history, a positive pregnancy test generally indicates pregnancy, but conditions such as molar pregnancies and other trophoblastic diseases must be ruled out. So, can an abortion be performed at 2 months of pregnancy?

**Can an abortion be performed at 2 months of pregnancy?**

Experts from the Shanxi Huimin Hospital Gynecology Department point out that the optimal time for an abortion after becoming pregnant is between 6 to 10 weeks of gestation. Within 10 weeks of pregnancy, the uterus is not too large, the fetus and placenta have not fully formed, and in most cases, the cervix does not need to be dilated extensively. It is relatively easy to remove the embryonic tissue, with minimal reaction during the procedure, less bleeding, shorter operation time, and patients can go home after resting for 1-2 hours post-surgery. Recovery is quick, and the impact on the body is minimal. Therefore, 2 months of pregnancy is considered the best time for an abortion.

Between 10 to 14 weeks of pregnancy, as the embryo grows larger, the placenta forms, and the uterus enlarges accordingly. At this stage, a simple suction curettage is no longer appropriate, and a more complex dilation and evacuation (D&E) procedure may be required. This type of surgery is more challenging, involves more bleeding, slower recovery, and has a greater impact on the body. If pregnancy exceeds 14 weeks, neither of these two types of abortions can be performed, and instead, induction of labor in a hospital setting becomes necessary, increasing both the discomfort for the patient and the risks associated with the procedure.
