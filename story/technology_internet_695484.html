Four Major Techniques for Guidance in Filling out Voluntary Applications for the Adult College Entrance Examination

by qindingdangx on 2012-03-07 15:47:44

I. Choosing a Learning Format

The teaching methods for the adult college entrance examination are divided into three formats: full-time, part-time, and correspondence study. Candidates should choose a suitable learning format based on their own actual situation.

Part-time: Part-time teaching methods generally recruit students at the school's location, with classes mostly held at night or on weekends. This is suitable for employed candidates residing near the school.

Full-time: Younger candidates or those who wish to experience university life can choose the full-time format. Full-time study involves attending school in a full-time manner, similar to regular universities, with normal and relatively fixed classrooms, management requirements, and stable winter and summer vacation arrangements. (Note: From 2008, regular colleges have ceased recruiting full-time adult classes.)

Correspondence: This learning format suits working professionals with limited spare time. Correspondence teaching mainly focuses on planned, organized, and guided self-study, supplemented by systematic centralized face-to-face instruction. The main components of correspondence teaching include: tutoring, answering questions, assignments, experiments, internships, exams, course design, graduation design, and defense. Each academic year has around 3 sessions of concentrated face-to-face instruction lasting 10 days or half a month. For schools recruiting teachers, the face-to-face instruction time is usually during winter and summer vacations.

II. How to Choose a School

Choosing a school for the adult college entrance exam differs from the regular college entrance exam. Adult candidates should fully consider the following factors when selecting a school:

1. If you are an employed candidate aiming to use your spare time for further study, it is best to choose a school that offers part-time courses. To avoid affecting work, it is advisable to choose a school closer to home or your workplace. Of course, if you choose correspondence, this factor does not need to be considered.

2. In recent years, the value of adult college entrance exam diplomas has been questioned. However, if you attend prestigious institutions like Peking University or Tsinghua University, the value of your diploma will not be inferior to that of a regular university. Therefore, besides considering distance, you should also consider the reputation of the school.

3. For younger candidates or those hoping to experience university life, you can choose full-time study. However, many adult education colleges of universities do not offer classes on their main campuses, so the environment of the school and its surroundings should be your primary consideration.

III. How to Choose a Major

In general adult college entrance exam registrations, science majors concentrate on communication, electronics, and computer-related fields, while liberal arts majors focus on foreign languages, accounting, economics, and law. There is no category distinction among adult colleges, and all admission score lines are the same. Popular schools may adjust recruitment plans based on the popularity of different majors. Key institutions and popular majors carry higher risks for candidates just above the admission line, so careful consideration is needed when choosing. When registering, candidates should prioritize practicality and reality, focusing on acquiring useful knowledge rather than following trends blindly. Candidates pursuing a bachelor’s degree should preferably choose majors related to their previous field.

For high school graduates applying for a bachelor’s degree or associate degree, they should select majors based on personal interest and foundation. Among many students hoping to receive higher education through adult education, most aspire to enter prestigious universities. Taking well-known Beijing universities as examples, their adult education recruitment quotas have the following characteristics: all evening school recruitment quotas are in Beijing. All correspondence student recruitment quotas are outside the province. Most full-time (daytime) class recruitment quotas are in Beijing. When choosing a major, candidates must fill out a second choice and agree to be assigned, avoiding "dead files."

IV. How to Fill Out Application Forms

The adult college entrance exam adopts the method of filling out application forms before taking the exam. When filling out the application form, candidates cannot simultaneously apply for both liberal arts and science majors at the high school level; candidates applying for a high school starting point for a bachelor’s degree can also apply for a high school starting point for an associate degree (vocational college), adhering to the principle of choosing majors with the same cultural subject exams; police, medical, and traditional Chinese medicine majors cannot be mutually applied for; police majors can be combined with liberal arts associate degrees; traditional Chinese medicine and Western medicine majors can be combined with science associate degrees; art and sports majors must be filled in the "[Art, Sports Major Volunteer Column]"; candidates applying for art majors can also apply for liberal arts majors; candidates applying for sports majors can also apply for science majors; candidates applying for a bachelor’s degree can only combine within majors with the same unified exam subjects.

High Starting Point: In the Art and Sports Majors column (first batch of admissions), two volunteer schools can be selected, each with one major; In the High School Starting Point Bachelor Non-Art and Sports Majors column (second batch of admissions), two volunteer schools can be selected, each with one major; In the Vocational College (Associate Degree) Non-Art and Sports Majors column (third batch of admissions), two volunteer schools can be selected, each with two majors.

Bachelor’s Degree Upgrade: Two volunteer schools can be selected, each with two majors. In the application information, candidates must indicate whether they agree to adjustment. If agreed, options such as "[Similar Learning Formats, Similar Majors, Nearby Institutions]" can be selected. For vocational colleges (associate degrees) and bachelor’s degree upgrades, combinations can only occur within majors with the same unified exam subjects. High school starting point for a bachelor’s degree can be combined with vocational colleges (associate degrees). Candidates applying for art majors can also apply for liberal arts majors; candidates applying for sports majors can also apply for science majors.
