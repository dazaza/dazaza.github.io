At 1:30 PM on December 2nd, a man held his wife hostage with a knife in Qinghu Village, Junhe Street, Baiyun District, Guangzhou, causing a cut to the index finger of the woman's right hand. After receiving the report, the Baiyun police immediately organized manpower to go to the scene for handling. Upon arrival, the police quickly dispersed the surrounding onlookers and sent officers to negotiate with the suspect to stabilize his emotions. During this time, the police took advantage of his distraction, decisively moved forward, and seized the knife from the suspect, successfully rescuing the female hostage safely. Subsequently, the rescued woman was sent to the hospital for treatment and was out of life danger.

Mr. Lin said that at this point, everyone felt the matter was serious, so they immediately called the police. Not long after, several security officers arrived at the scene first. Seeing this, the tall man immediately pressed the woman down into the car, then sat on the seat himself, using a fruit knife to press against the woman's throat, telling the security officers not to come closer. The security officers stabilized the tall man's emotions while calling for backup.

According to reports from the Yangcheng Evening News by reporter Wang Pu and correspondent Bai Gongxuan, on the afternoon of December 2nd, the Baiyun police successfully handled a case where a person held their spouse hostage with a knife due to emotional disputes. The criminal suspect, Liao Mou (male, 31 years old, from Guangxi), caused a conflict with his wife over emotional issues, which led him to hold his wife hostage with a knife.

The woman was not in life danger.

At around 4:10 PM, a man dressed in a white coat, resembling a doctor, was allowed to enter the minivan, followed by two plainclothes men entering the vehicle. Soon after, the injured woman was escorted into the health center by the police waiting outside the car door, while at the same time, a loud shout came from inside the van: "He’s been subdued!" Immediately, more than twenty policemen and security officers rushed towards the minivan, opening both side doors and the rear door, dragging the resisting tall man out of the back door and pinning him to the ground. After being handcuffed, the tall man was taken away in a police car.

Currently, the suspect has been detained by the police, and the case is still under further review.

At around 4 PM, when the reporter arrived at the scene, the minivan had already been moved from the left side of the health station to the entrance. The police had closed off the road in front of the health station, with numerous police forces surrounding the minivan for handling the situation. Due to the tinted windows of the minivan, it was impossible to clearly see the situation inside.

Mr. Liang said that the minivan was moved to the entrance of the health station because the tall man had previously agreed with the police to move it for treating the woman's injury on her right hand. However, after the minivan was moved, the tall man refused to let the woman get out of the car for treatment and also refused to put down the fruit knife.

Witness Mr. Lin stated that at around 1:30 PM, he saw the tall man holding a fruit knife more than ten centimeters long against a woman's neck, while continuously cursing in Cantonese. Mr. Lin said that suddenly the tall man pulled open the door of the microbus parked on the left side of the Qinghu Health Station and demanded that the woman get in. "The woman refused to get in no matter what, so he forcibly dragged her, eventually hugging her onto the back seat of the minivan."

Afterward, according to informed sources, the man dressed in a white coat who first entered the minivan was not a doctor but an officer from the Baiyun District Public Security Bureau, who cleverly disguised as a doctor to rescue the hostage. According to the chief doctor of the Qinghu Health Station, after the injured woman was rescued, it was found that her right-hand tendon was severed. After initial treatment and bandaging, she was transferred to another hospital for further treatment, with no life-threatening injuries.

"Subsequently, it seemed like there was an argument among the three. Suddenly, the tall man pulled out a knife and without saying a word stabbed towards the woman's stomach direction," witness Miss Wang said. "The woman quickly stepped back and blocked it with her right hand, and the blade sliced her right index finger, fortunately not hitting any vital areas." Miss Wang said that seeing the woman's right hand bleeding, the tall man put away the fruit knife and repeatedly ordered the woman to go home with him, but the woman remained silent, sitting on the steps crying uncontrollably.

"For hours, the three of them were seen pulling and pushing each other on the steps." Ms. Zhang said. During this time, some security officers and neighbors approached to inquire about the situation, but the tall man claimed it was just a marital dispute and that the woman's finger was caught in something, asking everyone not to meddle. Until around 1 PM, under the repeated requests of the shorter man, the woman finally got up and followed the shorter man to the Qinghu Community Health Service Station about a hundred meters away on foot, with the tall man following closely behind. "Seeing the tall man following behind, the woman walked a bit and stopped, not daring to go to the hospital."

On the morning of December 2nd, in the pedestrian street of Qinghu Village, Baiyun District, Guangzhou, a hostage incident occurred where a man suspected of having an emotional dispute held his wife hostage in a minivan, holding out against the police for three and a half hours. After negotiations failed, the police took action; a policeman disguised as a doctor entered the minivan and seized the opportunity to snatch the knife and subdue the suspect, successfully rescuing the hostage. Currently, the suspect has been detained by the police, and the woman who was held hostage is out of life danger.

According to witness Miss Wang, at around 9 AM on the day of the incident, the woman who was held hostage and a man about 1.6 meters tall in his thirties were preparing to go home after buying groceries near the supermarket on Qinghu Pedestrian Street when a man wearing white pants and a black coat, about 1.7 meters tall, suddenly appeared in front of them.
