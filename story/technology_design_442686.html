"Assembly Call" · Details

The film "Assembly Call" deliberately avoided using big-name stars and instead cast relatively lesser-known male actors at the time, such as Zhang Hanyu, Liao Fan, and Yuan Wenkang. This strategy proved highly effective, with the final box office exceeding 260 million yuan and catapulting lead actor Zhang Hanyu into the ranks of top-tier celebrities. "Die Xue Gu Cheng" (Bloodbath in an Isolated City) follows a similar path, starring Yuan Wenkang, who played a supporting role named Wang Jincun in "Assembly Call." In terms of other actors, aside from more famous names like Louis Koo and Annie Yi, the rest are newcomers. In war films, what matters most is texture, scenes, and plot—stars aren't the main focus, says Shen Dong.

Emotional Value

Shen Dong frankly stated that unappealing war films can't just rely on intense battle scenes; they must also have unique character portrayals that delve into inner emotions and feelings. He claims that "Die Xue Gu Cheng" is the only domestic commercial war epic released during the summer season. The film showcases the brutality and intensity of war while also featuring romantic subplots, making it a well-rounded war movie. "I don't dare say my movie is better than 'Assembly Call,' but it's certainly not worse," he said.

Comparable to an Encyclopedia of the Eight-Year War of Resistance

What constitutes a war film? Can you call it a war film if there isn’t at least an hour of fighting? Shen Dong pointed out that if a 90-minute film only has 10 minutes of war scenes, calling it a war film might be a stretch. There should be at least 20-30 minutes for it to be considered conventional. Films like "Nanjing! Nanjing!" and "John Rabe" aren’t purely war films either. "Assembly Call" is a war film, but its battlefield is only a prelude, not the main story. I almost fell asleep after watching just 10 minutes.

Intensity of Battles

Regarding whether too many war scenes might be overly bloody or disorienting for the audience, Shen Dong said: These battles actually happened in history. Our ancestors sacrificed their blood in this way, and we need to face this history squarely. He further explained that the main storyline of "Assembly Call" involves 47 brothers of Valley Guided’s Ninth Company, who were reduced to just one survivor by the end. In contrast, "Die Xue Gu Cheng" features 8000 soldiers, with only 83 remaining by the end. You can imagine how brutal this war was!

Yuan Wenkang, who starred in both "Die Xue Gu Cheng" and "Assembly Call," revealed that Feng Xiaogang swore never to touch war films again after shooting "Assembly Call" due to the immense hardship of the process. Although he acted in both films, Yuan Wenkang shares Shen Dong's view: "Assembly Call" is essentially about a person's lifelong journey, with war being just a part of it. What sets "Die Xue Gu Cheng" apart is that it fully portrays the entire process of a war from start to finish, making it a true war film.

"Assembly Call" · Focus

On August 15th, during the summer release season, the war film "Die Xue Gu Cheng" (originally titled "Changde Great Blood Battle"), starring Louis Koo, Annie Yi, and Yuan Wenkang, will hit theaters. Recently, the film preview was announced in Beijing, with director Shen Dong and leading actor Yuan Wenkang making appearances. Shen Dong criticized "Assembly Call" on-site, saying it wasn't a real war film, adding, "I fell asleep within ten minutes." Yuan Wenkang, who had acted in "Assembly Call," disclosed that Feng Xiaogang vowed never to touch war films again after filming it due to the immense difficulty of the process.
