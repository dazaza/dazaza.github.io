Besides focusing on exploring traditional culture, Longtang also emphasizes continuing nearly lost Chinese manual skills, making them unique brand traits and aesthetics of Longtang. Through years of practice, they not only became familiar with various techniques and expressions of the "Four Famous Embroideries," but also conducted field investigations and research on various folk manual skills in Southwest China, Northwest China, Southern Hunan, and other regions. During this process, they discovered some nearly extinct folk hand embroidery, such as a folk three-dimensional double-sided embroidery where the reverse side feels like a detailed painting while the front side is a three-dimensional velvet surface. This technique has never been applied to modern clothing, and there is almost no record of it in the history of embroidery in China. It has been interrupted in folk practices for decades. Through Longtang's efforts, it has been successfully replicated and will be gradually introduced when the opportunity matures.

Additionally, Longtang's designers delved into Tujia, Miao, Dong, Yao, and other ethnic minority areas, earnestly exploring folk embroidery and handwoven brocade art, and voluntarily designing ethnic tourism products for some folk brocade artisans. They felt the ancient culture passed down for thousands of years, laying the groundwork for future product design at Longtang, striving to continuously cultivate their own unique product monopoly advantages, hoping that various traditional, folk, excellent manual skills (embroidery, batik, tie-dye, brocade, etc.) will be fully embodied through Longtang's products in the future; conveying the deepest spiritual resonance of this era through artistic forms!

Based on Longtang's many years of research findings, Longtang's designer, He Xiangpin, was honored to become one of the main authors of "Hunan Minority Costumes," one of the series of books in "Lake Xiang Library," which was published in November 2010. "Hunan Minority Costumes" mainly includes the traditional national costumes and newly made costumes retaining traditional styles of the Tujia, Miao, Dong, and Yao ethnic groups in Hunan, including men's, women's, and children's costumes and their accessories, and provides descriptions of the historical origins, development, types, styles, colors, and patterns of these national costumes. It also briefly describes the traditional costumes of Bai and Hui ethnic groups with relatively smaller populations. To comprehensively reflect the characteristics of materials, decoration, and coordination of the above minority costumes, "Hunan Minority Costumes" also adds four sections closely related to clothing: textile printing and dyeing, embroidery and cross-stitch, brocade, and silver ornaments, filling the gap in the study of Hunan minority costumes.

("Lake Xiang Library" is a major publishing project personally led by the governor, implemented by the provincial government. It is a large series of books mainly focusing on the collation and publication of Lake Xiang literature, extensively excavating and deeply researching Lake Xiang figures, history, and local conditions, providing a comprehensive understanding of Lake Xiang culture and a deeper understanding of Hunan. The significance of "Lake Xiang Library" lies in accumulation and inheritance, in governance and education, in leaving a lasting spiritual wealth for future generations. Compiling and publishing "Lake Xiang Library" is a large cultural project that "does not disappoint ancestors and benefits future generations.")

Embroidery Art Advisor

Longtang people believe: Only by bringing traditional clothing crafts to their fullest potential can a Chinese clothing brand achieve its most enduring value. Therefore, Longtang specially hired the outstanding contemporary embroidery artist, renowned Hunan embroidery fine arts master - Zuo Jichun as the manual skills director.

Master Zuo Jichun started learning embroidery from her mother at a young age, engaging in embroidery art for over 40 years. In 1965, she became a "Hunan embroidery technical expert" in Changsha County. In 1966, she was transferred to work at the Hunan Provincial Arts and Crafts Research Institute, participating in the creation of the giant Hunan embroidery "Contemporary Heroes" displayed in the Hunan Hall of the Beijing Great Hall of the People, measuring 9 meters wide.

In 1973, under the direction of the Hunan embroidery research institute leadership, she became an apprentice of the famous old Hunan embroidery artist Yu Zhenhui, learning the special "Pony Hair Stitch" technique of Hunan embroidery, becoming one of the inheritors of this technique at the time. In 1988, she was one of the first to be rated as a fine arts master. In 1992, she was selected to perform Hunan embroidery skills in Singapore and Malaysia. In 2010, she was named the "Hunan Intangible Cultural Heritage Project (Hunan embroidery) inheritor."

Through nearly half a century of experience, she has accumulated very rich embroidery experience and quite comprehensive technical expertise. Whether in the subjects depicted such as flowers, landscapes, figures, animals, or in the forms of expression such as Chinese paintings, oil paintings, watercolors, and photographs, she can bring out the best artistic effects of different objects through her embroidery skills. She excels not only in single-sided embroidery and double-sided embroidery but also fully masters the highest skills of "double-sided completely different embroidery" and "pony hair stitch" embroidery. The embroidery works she participated in won the first prize of the "China Arts and Crafts Hundred Flowers Gold Cup Award." Many embroidery works have been exhibited domestically and internationally, becoming classic works of Hunan embroidery, cherished by various professional museums and people at home and abroad.

For decades, due to her "moral and artistic excellence" social influence, solid and comprehensive embroidery skills, understanding of painting knowledge, and corresponding expressive ability, she once presided over the "Double-Sided Embroidery Skills Training Class" at the Hunan Provincial Embroidery Research Institute. In recent years, she has devoted herself to teaching her skills selflessly to apprentices, making active contributions to the inheritance and development of the highest skills of "double-sided completely different embroidery" and "pony hair stitch" embroidery of Hunan embroidery, and was named the "Hunan Intangible Cultural Heritage Project (Hunan embroidery) inheritor."

Mr. Xu Ruigeng, Master Zuo Jichun's husband, was born in Ya'an, Sichuan Province in 1940. In 1955, he was sent to the attached middle school of Southwest Academy of Fine Arts, and in 1959, he continued his studies at the Department of Industrial Art of Sichuan Academy of Fine Arts. In 1963, after graduation, he was assigned to work in Hunan. He worked for more than 40 years in provincial direct arts and crafts research units. He was once a senior fine arts master and deputy general fine arts master at the Hunan Provincial Embroidery Research Institute. From 1986 to 1989, he was selected to work in Zimbabwe, Africa, engaged in embroidery skills assistance. He created a batch of Hunan embroidery fine draft works with African themes. His works have distinct personalities and characteristics, based on watercolor painting, absorbing the precision of Chinese detailed painting and the richness of oil painting colors. After embroidery, the works become even more graceful and elegant, unique, appreciated by both refined and common tastes.

Master Zuo Jichun and Mr. Xu Ruigeng have achieved remarkable success in nearly 20 years of "husband painting and wife embroidering," being one of the few living examples in the history of Hunan embroidery who have dedicated their lives to the inheritance and development of Hunan embroidery skills.
