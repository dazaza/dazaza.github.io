Facebook is a large American social networking service site, a leader among the new generation of Internet companies. Its founder, Mark Zuckerberg, is called [The Next Gates] and [The Successor to Jobs].

As one of the most creative IT companies, Facebook introduced [standing desks] for its employees as early as 2011 (there is a theory that standing desks originated in Japan). These desks are slightly higher than waist level, with computer screens elevated to face height so that the eyes remain parallel to the screen, effectively reducing neck strain.

Considering the fatigue caused by standing for long periods, each standing desk is equipped with a high stool. It is said that Facebook is also experimenting with [treadmill desks], installing treadmills under the desks so that employees can operate computers while walking...

Netizens Spread Standing Benefits

[Standing while working can reduce the incidence of diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, and cancer. It helps with weight loss, prevents lower back pain, improves concentration, promotes blood circulation, increases blood supply to the brain, leading to more creative ideas. Many Alibaba employees responded: [I hope we can use them soon], [Such a powerful desk, I've been dreaming of it], [Very sharp. Envious!], [If it could be popularized, standing could help lose weight.]

[Huang Xinzi] is an e-commerce representative for a lubricant company on Alibaba. [When I was a child, I injured my waist and underwent surgery. Sitting for too long makes my legs go numb. Recently, the situation has worsened, and my lumbar vertebrae hurt all day, forcing me to undergo physical therapy.

[Doctors advised me to stand more and avoid sitting if possible. However, doing e-commerce requires being in front of a computer all day. During busy times, there isn't even time to stand for a moment. I saw standing desks on Weibo and really want to try them.]

A staff member of Alibaba's media publicity department said: [To my knowledge, the company does not have plans to introduce standing desks. However, this idea is pretty good. Alibaba employees can collectively post on the forum and ask Mr. Ma to approve the purchase of standing desks to try out the experience of working while standing.]

In Beijing and Shanghai, some multinational corporations have already followed the headquarters' trend.

Are There Companies in Hangzhou That Work Standing Up?

Standing desks available online come in two types: manual and electric. The manual ones raise the desktop by rotating a handle and are relatively cheaper. Electric ones are motor-driven, allowing precise adjustment of the table height and resemble the standing desks used by Facebook.

A staff member of Alibaba's media publicity department said that basically, there are no standing desks in Hangzhou's IT enterprises. However, there is a lift table company in Ningbo that manufactures electric height-adjustable desks.

"Our products are mainly exported to the United States and Europe, where many customers are located in Silicon Valley," said Mr. Zhang, the sales manager of the lift table company. Many companies in Silicon Valley follow Facebook's [standing office] model, providing employees with more opportunities to move around and prevent lumbar and cervical spine diseases.

"We also custom make adjustable meeting tables and dining tables. Some large European companies have trends of holding meetings while standing and even eating while standing."

The domestic sales of standing desks have just started, with customers mainly concentrated in high-tech companies in Beijing and Shanghai. Mr. Zhang said: "Most domestic customers are Fortune 500 multinational companies whose headquarters have already equipped standing desks, and the Chinese branches follow the Silicon Valley trend. Currently, there are no customers in Hangzhou."

Electric height-adjustable desks cost approximately $800 in Europe and America, and around RMB 5000 domestically. "Because they are expensive, customers mostly provide them only for senior executives and middle managers. Regular employees use regular chairs or manual height-adjustable desks. One search engine website has an adoption rate of 30%." Manager Zhang said.

Google Executives Stand While Working

Even Hemingway Wrote Standing

Sina Weibo user [Eight Week Bird], a programmer at Google, shared a photo of his hand-cranked adjustable computer desk: a seemingly ordinary white table with a monitor in the center and a black handle on the right side.

"Holding the handle and cranking it 40 times raises the table, which is rather troublesome," Eight Week Bird said: "Employees at Google can apply for adjustable desks. There are electric versions too; pressing a button suffices, but getting one is quite difficult."

In November last year, Xie Xiaohu, Deputy Director of the Google China Engineering Research Institute and Global Technology Director of Google, also shared a picture of his adjustable computer desk on Weibo: a black desk placed in front of a floor-to-ceiling glass window with skyscrapers visible outside, and the Oriental Pearl Tower in the distance. There’s a button on the desk—it’s electric. Good equipment indeed always goes to the leaders first...

Some netizens researched and found that working while standing has been popular in Europe and America for hundreds of years, and many celebrities were fans of standing desks.

U.S. President Thomas Jefferson had a "[six-legged high table]" that could adjust the angle of the tabletop, allowing him to read and draw while standing. He once sketched the preliminary design of the Virginia State Capitol on this table.

British Prime Minister Winston Churchill always stood while reviewing the galley proofs of his next book, picking out parts that needed correction.

British novelist Charles Dickens also used a standing desk. A visitor once recalled: [The room was full of books piled up to the ceiling; Dickens stood at a desk writing, surrounded by comfortable chairs.]

American novelist Ernest Hemingway wrote at a homemade standing desk. His book "My Father, Ernest Hemingway - Private Memories" describes: [He converted a bedside bookshelf into a standing workstation, placing a typewriter on top along with some papers. He had a special reading board when he wrote.]
