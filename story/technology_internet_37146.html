"China Entrepreneur": Where is the moral bottom line of online games?

by lengpengcheng on 2007-11-29 18:44:46

On December 1, 2006, an advertisement that appeared during the prime time on both Channel One and Channel Five of CCTV caught the attention of hundreds of millions of viewers: A red-clothed girl with long hair suddenly burst into uncontrollable laughter in front of a white notebook computer. Then came a strange shout imitating Peking Opera, followed by a palm-shaped icon pulling out four characters "ZT Network", while a website address www.ztgame.com was shown at the bottom of the screen throughout the ad. This seven-second advertisement was repeated three times just during the prime time between the News Broadcast and Weather Forecast, and aired continuously for a whole month.

This was the first time that a domestic online game operator had made its appearance on CCTV in the form of corporate image advertising after the State Administration of Radio, Film and Television required to ban TV advertisements of computer online games in 2004. The industry and public opinion were amazed. It is well known that the chairman of ZT Network is Shi Yuzhu, who is called as "marketing madman".

Following the "Giant" Han Card, "Brain White Gold" and "Golden Partner" health products, the "Journey" online game has become the latest money tree for this controversial entrepreneur. In November 2004, Shanghai ZT Network Technology Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "ZT Network") was officially established. The subsequent performance of ZT left the industry speechless: In April 2006, Shi Yuzhu announced in Shanghai with great fanfare that the "Journey" had started making money during the internal testing period; In November 2006, Shi Yuzhu disclosed that the monthly profit of "Journey" reached 8.5 million US dollars, ranking second only to NetEase (NASDAQ:NETS) among domestic game companies; As of May 20, 2007, according to the data shown on the official website of ZT Company, the number of concurrent online users of "Journey" has exceeded one million. "Journey" has also become the third Chinese online game with more than one million concurrent online users globally, following NetEase's "Dream of Mirror West" and The9's "World of Warcraft".

According to the survey conducted by iResearch, in the first quarter of 2007, ZT Network surpassed The9 with a revenue of 4.8 billion RMB yuan and a market share of 15.6%, entering the top three for the first time, ranking only behind Shanda (NASDAQ:SNDA) and NetEase. There are reports that ZT Company, which has never introduced venture capital and is absolutely controlled by Shi Yuzhu personally, is about to enter the quiet period procedure for listing and is expected to land on NASDAQ in the second half of this year. However, our magazine reporter's multiple phone calls and emails to ZT Network for interviews with Shi Yuzhu himself were all rejected.

It is worth noting that under the environment where the prosperity of online games is not as good as before, as the only online game operator in China currently operating a single online game product, "Journey", which has created a commercial miracle, has not won the respect of its peers. On August 15, 2006, the Green Game Evaluation Activity initiated by the China Youth Internet Association of the Communist Youth League Central Committee even designated "Journey" as a dangerous game and suggested it to suspend operation.

In fact, Shi Yuzhu - this bald, thin, heavy-smoking, topic entrepreneur who likes to wear a white tracksuit or a bright red T-shirt when appearing on TV programs, has long been accustomed to various controversies surrounding him. If ZT successfully goes public, it is estimated that it will easily exceed the current market value of about 1.2 billion US dollars of The9 (NASDAQ:NCTY), thus adding several billion US dollars to Shi's wealth.

However, "Journey" is not the Giant Building, Brain White Gold or Golden Partner health products. It is a cultural product that directly affects the spiritual life of millions of young people.
