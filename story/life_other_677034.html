How to Treat Burns -- Four Effective Self-help Methods for Burns

Skin damage caused by splashes of boiling water, hot oil, hot porridge, or steam in daily life should be treated at a hospital promptly if severe. For minor burns, some simple methods can be used for self-help and self-treatment.

There are records in ancient Chinese medical texts such as "Qian Jin Fang" and "Wai Tai Mi Yao" about treating burns with honey and egg whites. Modern medical journals have reported that "honey-soaked gauze strips can accelerate wound healing." The author has also successfully treated multiple cases of burns with fresh egg whites mixed evenly with honey for external application. Here are a few prescriptions recommended for your selection.

Ginger Juice Wet Dressing

Drug Preparation: Take fresh ginger, remove impurities, wash and dry it, then crush it in a container. Squeeze the juice out through a gauze bag.

Usage: Use a sterilized cotton swab to dip in the ginger juice and apply externally, or use ginger juice-soaked gauze to wet-dress the affected area.

Miraculous Angelica膏

Drug Preparation: 1 part Angelica, 1 part beeswax, 4 parts sesame oil. First, put the Angelica in the sesame oil and fry until dry, then remove the residue. Add the beeswax and stir quickly to mix evenly. Soak sterilized gauze or silk in the ointment, place it in a sterile porcelain box, and cool it for storage.

Usage: Apply the gauze or silk to the wound and bandage it with dressing.

Dandelion Paste

Drug Preparation: Take an appropriate amount of fresh dandelion roots (without stems and leaves), wash them clean, then crush them to extract the juice. Place the juice in a sterilized porcelain container, and after 2 hours, the juice will naturally solidify into a paste.

Usage: Wash the affected area first, then apply the medicine paste thickly on the affected area. The pain will reduce within one hour. Apply the medicine twice a day. When changing the medicine, wash off the previous medicine with cold boiled water.

Enhanced Purple-yellow Oil

Drug Preparation: 15 grams each of purple root and raw rhubarb, place them in 100 grams of linseed oil and boil, then remove the residue. Add 10 grams of boric acid powder and stir evenly, then bottle it for storage.

Usage: Clean and dry the affected area first with a 1% saline cotton ball, then use a cotton ball to dip in the oil and apply externally, or cover with oil-soaked gauze. Apply three to four times a day until the skin dries and crusts over, then stop the medication without bandaging. Suitable for first and second-degree burns.

The author once treated a 13-year-old boy who accidentally spilled boiling water from a thermos onto the inner side of his left arm during summer, causing redness, heat, and pain on the skin, along with two small blisters. After applying the enhanced purple-yellow oil externally for a short while, the burning pain was significantly reduced, and he healed after three days of continuous application.
