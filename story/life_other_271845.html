The naming of some place names in Fujian Province is largely related to mountains, water, era names, blessings and auspiciousness, which is closely related to the geographical environment of Fujian, indicating that the geographical environment has a great influence on the existence and development of society. Here are some examples:

Sixteen place names named after mountains:

Fuzhou: named after Mount Fu.

Quanzhou: named after Mount Quan (Beishan, Mount Qingyuan).

Changle: named after Mount Changle.

Xianyou: named after Mount Xianyou.

Longyan: named after Mount Longyan.

Fuding: named after Mount Fuding.

Dongshan: named after Mount Dongshan (Mount Sufeng).

Yunxiao: named after Mount Yunxiao.

Liancheng: originally called Liancheng, named after Mount Lianfeng.

Pingnan: named for being south of ancient Mount Ping.

Hua'an: named for its location at Huafeng Ridge where people live peacefully and prosperously.

Zherong: named after "Zheyang Dongshan, eastward to the sea for hundreds of miles."

Pingtan: named for the rocks on Hai坛Island resembling an altar.

Xiapu: named after Mount Xiapu; also said to be named after Xiapu River.

Fuqing: renamed because "mountains from Yongfu Li, water from Qingyuan Li, converge at the seat of government."

Wuyi Mountain: originally called Chong'an County, renamed after the famous southeastern mountain "Wuyi Mountain."

Twenty-four place names named after water:

Lianjiang: named after Lianjiang.

Luoyuan: named after Luojiang (Luoyuan Creek).

Minqing: named for the confluence of Meixi and Minjiang rivers here, "the river water is turbid, the creek water is clear", so it was named.

Xiamen: named Xia Men due to its location below the maritime route and resembling a gate, with a similar pronunciation to Xiamen.

Qingliu: named for having the Longjin River and clear flowing water.

Youxi: named after Youxi.

Shaxian: named after Shaxi, a tributary of Minjiang River.

Datian: named after Datian Creek.

Jinjiang: named after Jinjiang River.

Anxi: originally called Qingxi, named for its clear stream water; later changed to the current name due to "thieves rising." Also referred to as "clear and smooth streams, good for navigation, hence called Anxi."

Zhangpu: named after Zhangjiang (Zhangxi) River.

Pucheng: named after Pucheng being located at Nanpu Creek.

Nanan: "named after having Nanan River."

Gutian: "there was a Xie Neng, who lived by cultivating fields in ancient times, hence named Gutian." But also due to the Gutian Creek, a tributary of Minjiang River.

Songxi: named after Songxi River, with tall pines on both banks, "hundreds of miles of pine shade."

Wuping: named after Wuping River within its territory, a tributary of Tingjiang River.

Mingxi: "Mingxi is to the east of the county, also called Mingjiang... flows northeast, there are two large and small hills on the side of the rock, resembling the character 'ming', thus called Mingxi."

Zhangzhou: named after Zhangjiang (Zhangxi) River.

Zhangping: "located at the upper reaches of Zhangjiang River, among ten thousand mountains, this area is relatively flat."

Jianyang: named for being north of Jianxi River.

Changting: named after Ting River. "Ting water flows southward, south corresponds to Ding position."

Shunchang: named after Shunyang Creek to the northwest of the county.

Qingliu: named for having Qingxi to the north, the upstream of Shaxi River, with many forests along the creek and clear water like silk, hence named Qingliu.

Shanghang: named for having fish reefs downstream of its river, making navigation impossible, but navigable upstream, "hang" and "navigation" have the same meaning, hence named Shanghang.

Two place names named after era names:

Yongtai: established in the second year of Yongtai during the Tang Dynasty (766).

Zhenghe: changed from Guanli County in the fifth year of Zhenghe during the Song Dynasty (1115).

Eighteen place names expressing blessings and auspiciousness:

Changle, Ninghua, Taining, Hui'an, Yong'an, Dehua, Zhao'an, Fu'an, Nanjing, Jianning, Nanping, Shouning, Changtai, Shunchang, Yongding, Pinghe, Nan'an, Tong'an, etc.

Seven composite place names:

Minhou (Min County and Houguan County).

Longhai (Longxi County and Haicheng County).

Sanming (Sanyuan County and Mingxi County).

Jianou (Jian'an County and Ouning County).

Ningde (Ningchuan and Gande).

Zhouning (Zhou Dun and Ningde).

Jiangle (Jiangjian and Changle).

Four place names related to geographical landscapes:

Putian: grows a lot of bulrushes.

Yongchun: originally called Taoyuan, renamed for lush vegetation within its territory, remaining spring-like all four seasons.

Songxi: named for growing many pine trees on both banks of the river.

Zherong: named for having many Zhe trees within its territory.
