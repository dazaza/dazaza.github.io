8-Year-Old Child Cares for Vegetative Father, Mother Unable to Work Due to Illness

Don't want dolls or watermelons, I want dad to get better quickly

A In the past, dad cared for her, now she cares for dad

Chunlei girl Jiang Tong is in the first grade at Qixin Primary School in Jiaodong Office of Jiaozhou City. Originally, she had a very happy family. Although her mother has been suffering from Meniere's syndrome and cannot go out to work, the income of her father as a technician in the factory allowed Jiang Tong and her sister to live carefree lives. However, the happiness of this family stopped forever on the afternoon of April 25, 2009, when Dad was involved in a car accident.

"After the accident involving her dad, our family of three could only survive on a quarterly low-income allowance of one thousand yuan. I even stopped taking medicine for my headaches, but the children were still too young back then. Even though they knew the family's financial situation had worsened, it was hard not to desire things when they saw them. I once beat her and told her that it wasn't because we didn't want to buy it, but because our financial situation wouldn't allow it. After that, she never asked me for anything again," said Tongtong's mom. The happiest thing for the child every week was sitting on the backseat of the bicycle and looking at the Barbie dolls on the counter in the supermarket. "She told me: 'Mom, I won't buy it, I just want to take a look,'" said Tongtong's mom, crying again as she recounted this.

When Tongtong was little, her dad once bought her a Barbie doll. Seeing how much his daughter loved it, he promised to buy her another one during their May Day holiday outing. But as little Tongtong counted down the days eagerly waiting for the holiday to come, on the afternoon of April 25, 2009, her dad was involved in a car accident on his way home from work.

Chunlei Wish

Every day after school, Tongtong would give her dad a shoulder massage. The Barbie doll her dad bought before the accident is her favorite toy.

Tongtong said she likes going to school because she can learn a lot of knowledge there, "I like both Chinese and math." During the final exams, Tongtong scored 100 in Chinese and 98 in math. The teachers all liked this obedient child. Despite not getting a perfect score, an exception was made and she was given a big award certificate. When she got home, Tongtong leaned over her dad's bed and whispered the good news into his ear. She gave the certificate to her mom, who looked at her youngest daughter with surprise despite her daily toils, her eyes filled with joy. Although she is dark and thin like a little radish head, Tongtong has good relationships in her class. She said, "There are 39 classmates in the class, more than 20 of them are my good friends."

Seeing her report with grandma, Cheng Xinyi cried again

With her dad bedridden and her mom in poor health, eight-year-old Tongtong is very understanding, not causing much worry for her mom in terms of study and life.

On May 30, at the commendation meeting of "Spring Bud Stars" organized by the Women's Federation, Cheng Xinyi from Yifeng Middle School in Jimo delivered a speech as a representative. Speaking about her life without parents since childhood and living with her grandmother, Xinyi sobbed uncontrollably on the podium, and many listeners in the audience couldn't help but shed tears. "I believe, all my dreams will bloom!" At the end of her speech, Xinyi shouted out her wish loudly.
