We who sit at the same table, we who sleep in the same dormitory

by qq15480628 on 2011-11-18 00:35:55

Do you still remember on August 30, 2010, the first time we pulled an all-nighter together? You took me to a ghost server and leveled up to thirteen. That was my first warrior. We were both level nine when we went down into the Luya Ravine and died how many times at the hands of the first little monster? Do you remember that because of my shield, Brother Chao got OT (off tank) and yelled loudly in the Internet cafe? You guys used to belittle me, saying I was a T who couldn't possibly not get OT, and I argued with you for a long time. We smoked, drank, played cards, and ate peanuts brought by my mom on the balcony together. We never woke up before the third class in the morning. When you got blocked, I helped you block back. Do you remember that window on the third floor? Do you remember us asking the security guard to catch us when we flipped over the wall? And that song "Old Boy" we sang in the dormitory? That night, we cheered Brother Chao on and sang that out-of-breath "Red Bean" for Wang Jun. We skipped classes together, slept together, played Warcraft together, and chatted together. On the day we hit level eighty, we said we would develop our own guild and carve out our own territory. You kept saying that the warrior-mage-priest combination wouldn't work and that my operations were poor, making me sit there sulking every day. Do you still remember the name of our three-three team? Stupid Chao, Stupid Ke, Stupid Yangguo. The name of our guild, that English I couldn't memorize, ended up with only a few 80-level members, making it quite embarrassing. And that poem we wrote: "Senior three, go ahead, live or die, shield wall break cauldron, disappear after hard practice, college entrance exam is destiny, immortal forever." That night, we pulled an all-nighter and opened our first gold group, 25-person ToC. Not only was our command bad, but our luck was also black as hell. We only made 400 gold because of the lack of talent, and thus our first gold group failed. At level 80, you all said I was a good warrior T. I didn't let you down. I bravely stood up when the warrior T was weakest. Thank you for your support. That day I was in a bad mood, and you accompanied me in skipping the exam. I often think about how we overslept frequently and got scolded by Brother Jun, collectively called in parents, and even overslept through the English monthly exam. We often sat on the bed late at night and reflected on ourselves. Back then, we were inseparable shadows, whether it was calling in parents, playing basketball, fighting, flipping over walls, ICC, ToC, sleeping, smoking, and those ghost games and murder games. Our laughter and joy gave the life teacher a headache. And that Texas Hold'em game, we don't know how much water we drank and rushed to the restroom during breaks. And that LeBron James picture on Brother Nai's head, how many boys and girls fell for it. Every night, the songs from that Q4 chat room would sound. Maybe there are still many things between us, but this doesn't matter because we are brothers. The thing I regret most in high school was letting you jump down from the second floor because I came back. You wanted to accompany me, and I regret it, I deeply regret it. You should be with us in the guild now. You said you would give me the vice president position. Is your foot okay? Our agreement, desk partners talking endlessly, dorm mates blowing everything out of proportion. We are forever brothers. I hope you recover soon.
