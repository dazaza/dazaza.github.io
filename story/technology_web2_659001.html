Guangdong acid rain change

by eawdqrxbh on 2012-02-04 22:12:27

Guangdong Acid Rain Change Reported in Xinhua, Guangzhou, August 19: The Guangdong Provincial Environmental Protection Department announced on the 19th the environmental quality of the province for the first half of 2011. The announcement indicates that the overall environmental quality of the province remained stable. In particular, the mean pH of urban rainfall increased compared to last year, and the frequency of acid rain also dropped from 45.9% last year to 37%.

The long-standing issue of acid rain in Guangdong has shown improvement. The announcement states that among Guangdong's 21 prefecture-level cities and Shunde District, air quality reached the national secondary standard, with an increase in the number of excellent air quality days compared to the same period last year. Compared to the same period last year, the mean pH of urban precipitation rose by 0.2 pH units, and the frequency of acid rain fell by 9.5 percentage points. The area of heavy acid rain reduced from five to four regions, with Shenzhen and Huizhou exiting the ranks of heavy acid rain areas. Meanwhile, the four cities of Shaoguan, Qingyuan, Foshan, and Zhaoqing were included in the list of heavy acid rain areas.

The announcement further mentioned that the mainstream of large rivers and some tributaries, as well as the river areas of the Pearl River Delta in Guangdong province, generally had good water quality. Among the 117 provincial-controlled sections monitored for water quality in the province, 6.8% of the water quality was worse than class V, indicating severe pollution. Specifically, eight sections of the Longgang River, Ping Shan, Foshan Waterway, Shiqiao waterways, the Shenzhen River, Meixi, Lianjiang, and a small section of the Dongjiang River near Zhanjiang were heavily polluted. The main pollutants were ammonia nitrogen, total phosphorus, and some oxygen-demanding organic matter.

The announcement also stated that among the three provincially controlled lakes in Guangdong, Huguangyan Lake's water quality was class II, indicating excellent water quality. Xinghu and West Lake had class IV water quality, showing slight pollution, but all met the water environment functional area water quality requirements. All provincial-controlled lakes and reservoirs within the city limits, including centralized drinking water reservoirs, showed no occurrence of algal blooms during the cyanobacteria bloom monitoring conducted in the first half of the year.
