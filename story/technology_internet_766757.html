Eight Outstanding Designers Review iOS 7: Fresh but Lacking in Highlights

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 20:52:04

Although consumers have waited three months for Apple's latest mobile operating system, the much-anticipated system was finally released to all users on September 18th local time. Even Apple CEO Tim Cook called it "the biggest change iOS has seen since the introduction of the iPhone."

The design of iOS 7 was led by Apple Senior Vice President Jony Ive. Since Apple reorganized its executive team in 2012, Ive replaced Scott Forstall and began overseeing the user interface design of the iOS system. As a result, iOS 7 transitioned from the familiar "skeuomorphic" design that iPhone users were accustomed to into a "flat" design style.

So, how does the world perceive this significant stylistic shift in the system? Recently, U.S. media interviewed eight industry design experts and asked them to share their opinions on the new user interface of iOS 7. Here are their views:

1. Senior Designer Demelza Rafferty at Sid Lee Amsterdam

Sid Lee Amsterdam Senior Designer Demelza Rafferty

The graphic design style in iOS 7 is very colorful and flat, which is quite good. However, many navigation icons in the system are designed too small for aesthetic reasons, and the default font is also too tiny, making it easy for users' eyes to become fatigued, especially when using high-contrast black-and-white interfaces.

In addition, I am not a fan of the multi-page switching interface in Safari. I think this card-style interface is outdated and doesn't match the overall system style. Moreover, to emphasize its 3D effect, the default background image moves slightly with the angle (but the icons themselves do not), and the pull-up and pull-down effects in the text message conversation interface are similar. However, I believe such practices go too far. Perhaps, like many other Apple products, we will eventually adapt to this new design style, just as the company always upholds the philosophy that "seeing more makes users feel more comfortable." However, I still feel that this system prioritizes design over usability.

2. Co-founder Simon Manchipp at SomeOne

SomeOne Co-founder Simon Manchipp

It goes without saying that I love this system.

We should not overlook the immense power of a simple, clean user interface. At SomeOne, we have always been strongly against excessive use of Helvetica (a commonly used typeface in publishing), so we have grown tired of frequently seeing it in input interfaces.

I think the graphical user interface of iOS 7 is adorable and features some excellent designs. For example, the clock application in iOS 7 can display the current time in real-time, which is a pleasant design.

3. Creative Partner Darren Scott at Truth

Truth Creative Partner Darren Scott

For a long time, Apple has been a leading tech brand known for its many design-focused enthusiasts. However, Apple seems to have overlooked some important elements in iOS 7. The company put too much effort into "change" but failed to set a new standard for graphic design in the industry, which is my biggest dissatisfaction with iOS 7.

I believe Apple didn’t need to obsess over using Helvetica or painstakingly applying bright colors throughout the system while neglecting the importance of input interfaces and their corresponding icons. Because if you claim to completely "change" an existing operating system, then please keep your promise. Of course, no matter what, this system is still the best mobile operating system on the market.

4. Senior Human-Machine Interaction Designer Lee Carroll at Seymourpowell

Seymourpowell Senior Human-Machine Interaction Designer Lee Carroll

Apple's latest system features many stunning details, such as the default background image moving slightly with different angles, which explains why Apple has so many fans. Whether it's the breathing light on the previous MacBook Pro, the exquisite app icon designs, or the slight bounce effect in the iOS 7 messaging interface, all these demonstrate Apple's relentless pursuit of detail.

In short, iOS 7 can be described as another "repainting" of the original system's interface details and improvements in practical aspects (such as pulling down the screen to bring up the Spotlight search box). Moreover, the smartest aspect of iOS is that its designers clearly understand the principle of "letting apps take center stage." However, this design philosophy also limits iOS 7 system designers when redesigning the system because they can only redesign it at a basic level and then encourage third-party developers to create quality applications for this platform.

Of course, Apple has completely overturned people's impressions of previous operating systems in iOS 7 and provided developers with a more powerful system platform. The inclusion of sensor control, biometric technology, and more advanced processor chips allows developers to boldly change the way people use iPhones, and as ordinary consumers, we need to wait patiently for future innovations in this area.

5. Design Director Richard Bassett at Thompson Brand Partners

Thompson Brand Partners Design Director Richard Bassett

During installation, the system prompted me to clear about 3GB of available space and encountered many errors and busy server issues until I finally managed to complete the setup at 1:30 AM today. So, it’s certain that I have very high expectations for this system.

Overall, this system did not disappoint me. I like its flat, colorful design style, although I had already seen pictures of the system before, actually using it was still breathtaking. In terms of functionality, I haven't found anything unsatisfactory yet. Being able to operate commonly used applications like alarms and calculators by swiping your fingers is very convenient, and Apple seems to have fully considered how users use their devices in this system update.

Now, we need to wait for other third-party applications to quickly adapt to Apple's latest design style, and the current version of Instagram feels somewhat outdated.

6. Creative Director Dave Palmer at Love

Love Creative Director Dave Palmer

For me, Apple's excellent product design and system interface style always seemed somewhat incompatible, and I was never a big fan of the previous "skeuomorphic" design. But now under Ive's leadership, Apple can finally unify hardware and software in a way never seen before, and I can't wait to upgrade to this system.

7. Interactive Director Alasdair Scott at Images & Co

Images & Co Interactive Director Alasdair Scott

As third-party app developers for Apple, we've been working with iOS 7 for several months, so we're very excited to see the system officially launched. On the streets of London today, you can see many people smiling or frowning as they look down at their newly updated iPhones.

I've been using this system since June, and my thoughts are: 1. iOS 7 is significantly different from the previous six generations of iOS. 2. Apple's ability to seamlessly integrate hardware, software, and services has reached unprecedented heights, and the new system provides users with an unprecedented experience, especially on the iPhone 5s with the new 64-bit A7 processor. 3. I have a feeling that this iPhone release incorporates many excellent elements from the iPad. 4. If Apple can find better ways to utilize network services like Siri and iCloud, it would be equivalent to finding the golden key to future success.

8. Art Director Gijs van den Berg at KesselsKramer

KesselsKramer Art Director Gijs van den Berg

First, I'm still waiting for the day when Apple announces the removal of some iOS features instead of continuing to add services. Do we really need our phones to have so many complex functions? Indeed, the new design style of iOS 7 is very fresh, but the continuous addition of complex animations and blur effects makes it seem like Apple can no longer add any practical features to the iPhone.

Second, I think Apple is already developing a newer system.

Third, I am surprised that people talk more about Justin Bieber than about Apple's new iOS 7 system.
