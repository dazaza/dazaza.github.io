The magnetic drill, also known as magnet drill, iron magnet drill, or suction iron drill, is a type of hand-held electric drill. Compared to regular hand-held electric drills, magnetic drills have advantages such as high precision, good perpendicularity, high safety, and no reactive force. They are mainly used for drilling metal structures, such as stainless steel, aluminum plates, steel plates, H-beams, angle steels, and other types of materials. They are suitable for large structural components that require large holes, making them indispensable specialized equipment in fields such as shipbuilding, bridge engineering, oil platforms, power plant maintenance, power company construction, and railway construction.

Magnetic Drill Working Principle [http://www.hanyue-sh.com]

The main structure of a magnetic drill consists of four parts: motor, magnetic base, gear box, and control circuit. In terms of speed adjustment, magnetic drills can be divided into stepwise speed regulation and stepless speed regulation. The advantage of stepless speed regulation is that it allows for speed changes without stopping the machine.

When powered on, by turning on the magnetic base switch, electromagnetic effects generate thousands of kilograms of magnetic force, allowing it to adhere to steel plates and structural components, serving as a mechanism to fix the machine. The stronger the magnetic base's adsorption ability, the more stable the body of the magnetic drill will be during operation, resulting in higher precision in drilling. When the machine switch is turned on, the rotor part rotates at high speed and through the transmission gear set drives the transmission shaft to rotate, which in turn drives the drill bit movement. Drilling occurs at the desired location via the drill bit. Compared to flame cutting, the holes drilled by the magnetic drill [http://www.hanyue-sh.com] have higher precision, belong to physical cutting, do not produce chemical reactions, and do not cause material deformation. Besides the traditional twist drills available on the market, there are also hollow drill bits specifically designed for magnetic drills. These drill bits use the principle of milling cutters, achieving high efficiency and good precision.
