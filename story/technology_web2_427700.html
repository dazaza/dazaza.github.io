Guangzhou releases draft of hotel safety management during Asian Games

According to the draft, from September 1 to December 31, Guangzhou police will take special security measures at hotels, inns, hostels, restaurants, hotels, guesthouses, buildings, reception centers, resorts, villas, sanatoriums, clubs, reception stations and bathing-massage-foot bath places that provide overnight services.

The draft requires that lodging business units register guests' valid ID information. Guests who do not carry valid ID should go to the police station within the jurisdiction of the lodging business unit to issue relevant identity certificates. For guests and visitors who refuse to register for accommodation, lodging business units shall reject their entry. The lodging business units should install video surveillance facilities in important parts such as the lobby, passage, entrance and exit, and the video surveillance data should be kept for 30 days for investigation.

The police also require that if a person needs to deposit items, the lodging business unit should request the depositor to open the bag (box) for inspection. Luggage items of guests who are not staying at the unit are not allowed to be deposited. For those who refuse to accept security checks or bag (box) inspections, the lodging business unit should prevent them from entering or depositing items.

Restricted fruits and eggs in venues

Prohibited items in Asian Games venues:

◆ Guns, ammunition, explosives

◆ Replica guns and crossbows, daggers and other controlled weapons

◆ Fireworks, gasoline, alcohol and other flammable and explosive hazardous materials

◆ Highly toxic, corrosive and other dangerous chemicals and radioactive materials

◆ Harmful biological agents, pathogens of infectious diseases and other dangerous substances

◆ Heroin, cocaine, marijuana, methamphetamine and other types of drugs

◆ Other items explicitly prohibited by Chinese laws and regulations

Restricted items in Asian Games venues (these items do not violate the law, but violate the Asian Games conventions or may affect safety and order, and are not allowed to be brought into the Asian Games activity venues):

◆ Fragile items and containers such as glass cups, thermos cups, baby bottles

◆ Self-brought soft and hard packaged drinks and easily thrown food exceeding personal use quantity (such as large quantities of fruits or eggs)

◆ Banners, slogans and other promotional items with political, racial, religious, commercial content and violating the "OCA Charter", Chinese laws

◆ Any means of transportation except baby strollers and wheelchairs

◆ Animals (except service animals such as guide dogs)

◆ Instruments, including musical instruments, whistles, horns, drums, etc.

◆ Baseball bats, long sticks, sharp objects, long-handled umbrellas, pointed umbrellas and umbrellas longer than 50 centimeters

◆ Balls, radios, flying discs and similar items

◆ Large-sized luggage and handbags unsuitable for bringing into seating areas

◆ Flags of countries or regions not participating in the Asian Games and Para-Asian Games; flags with an expanded area exceeding 2 meters x 1 meter; flagpoles longer than 1 meter

◆ Unauthorized professional camera equipment (except family-used, small handheld cameras) and tripods

◆ Items that distract athletes, technical officials, coaches, interfere with the electronic signals of the Asian Games, cluster signals, affect the progress of events or hinder others from observing, including but not limited to radios, laser devices, amplification equipment, walkie-talkies, radio equipment

◆ Other items that may affect safety or violate the "OCA Charter", Chinese laws or ticket agreements, such as lighters, matches and other ignition tools, syringes, medicines, etc.

Details of anti-explosion security for the Asian Games drafted; blasting suspended during the Asian Games

According to the draft, from 24:00 on October 20, 2010 to 24:00 on December 31, production, sales, and transportation of explosives, highly toxic, and radioactive hazardous materials will be stopped and restricted in Guangzhou City. Fireworks sales will be stopped and all blasting operations will cease. Non-designated highly toxic chemical transport vehicles are prohibited from driving on roads within the administrative region of the city.
