Longing, touching my pain

Gazing out the window at the azure sky, dotted with drifting white clouds; a pristine full moon hangs high in the firmament. Tonight, the stars are sparse, for they have been washed away by a storm, leaving fewer of them visible.

A woman who has always loved lively company has become much quieter these days, constantly feeling the loneliness that haunts her heart. Alone on an enormous bed, all she feels is its vastness, enveloping her as if she were a small ant on this large bed, insignificant like a shadow seen from the sky. Quietly sitting there, her hands are busy working on the endless cross-stitch patterns. One unfinished piece leads to another beautiful picture that captivates her. Perhaps the busyness of her hands and eyes with these patterns distracts her, or perhaps being alone recently has made her accustomed to this quietness. She's gotten used to being alone, to keeping her hands busy, to looking up at the ceiling when her eyes feel sore, and to the longing that comes with solitude. At this moment, only needles and threads are her closest companions. She knows that only they understand her inner loneliness, for during these past one and a half years, they have accompanied her day and night. She uses them to numb her yearning, weaving her daily thoughts into their patterns.

Tonight, after another storm's tumultuous excitement, the stillness seems almost eerie; the thunder and lightning have scorched the power lines, making golden sparks fly. In the pitch-black night, only faint moonlight seeps through the gaps in the curtains. The numbing fatigue of a day's work with her hands makes her realize how colorful her patterns have become. Stretching her back, which has been bent all day, she now feels the weariness.

Tonight there is no electricity, tonight it is silent. The children are bored, jumping rope in the yard, while I am filled with longing. I miss all my family and friends. Picking up my ever-present companion, my "cell phone," I press buttons incessantly, browsing through familiar phone numbers stored in my phone. Their familiar faces flicker before my eyes, but what I feel is distant longing.

Longing often makes people alternately happy and heartbroken. These feelings of joy and sorrow are memories of time spent with loved ones or friends. Each receding figure, each familiar smile, each wave goodbye becomes a memory after parting, so familiar yet so far away.

In silence, one often wonders: is it only women who long more deeply? Is it only women who cry over longing? I answer myself: actually, men and women are the same, both carrying longing within. Women just shed a few more tears outwardly, while men often keep their tears inside. This self-questioning and answering is a way I console myself.

A profound recollection moistens my eyes again. Taking a deep breath to ease my suppressed mood is one of my tricks for adjusting my state of mind. In the darkness, I first enter my friends' online spaces to see their updates and writings; I browse the news, hoping to release some of my inner melancholy and relieve my heavy heart.

Longing always causes a hidden pain in one's heart, a pain that cannot be described in words. It makes you weep uncontrollably, pierces your heart, feels like being cut by a knife, and leaves you unable to bear it.

No matter how much I try to relieve myself, it can never replace the longing and heartache; holding my lonely phone, I first call my father to inquire about his safety and busyness these days. Then I call my sister to ask about her family's well-being and health, and my nephew's college entrance exam results. Finally, I call my best friend, asking if her loneliness has lessened and comforting her grieving heart. Actually, I have many friends, and each friend represents a longing, but I don't want to disturb everyone individually, so I let her represent them all.

After each inquiry, my heart finds some solace and relief. At this moment, I sing a song, showing the sentimental nature of silly women; this is the way silly women relieve themselves; this is the easily satisfied mood of silly women.
