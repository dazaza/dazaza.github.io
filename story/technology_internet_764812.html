More than a year later, Facebook's share price returned to its $38 IPO price.

by anonymous on 2013-08-07 18:42:52

After Facebook's IPO in May last year, it seemed like a nightmare. Starting from the second day, the stock price began to continue to fall. By August 31st of last year, it reached a record low of $17.55, less than half of the initial public offering price of $38.

At that time, Facebook was widely questioned, with many believing that social networks could not support a billion-dollar valuation and that there was a bubble in the market.

Now, after more than half a year, Facebook has step by step proven its value. Just yesterday, Facebook's stock price once again broke through $38 during trading hours, marking the first time since the second day of the stock issuance that it has risen above $38, and it has been over a year since then. From the bottom to recovery, Facebook has also experienced a roller coaster ride.

The sharp rise in stock prices fully demonstrates investors' confidence in Facebook. However, from $17.55 to $38, how did investors gradually come to trust Facebook? The answer can be found in mobile development. The vigorous growth of Facebook's mobile business in recent years has brought great surprises, and the progress of diversified businesses is also an important factor attracting investors.

In the past year or so, Facebook's emphasis on the mobile market has shown significant results. Although this shift came a bit late, it was not too late. Facebook made a very firm decision in attitude, and after determining the "mobile-first" strategy, they formed a team of senior engineers to solve advertising issues. At the same time, they actively communicated with advertisers to help them improve their ad performance.

They also collaborated with data company Datalogix to monitor the effectiveness of advertisers' placements, boosting their revenue. Currently, on mobile devices, every twenty pieces of information in Facebook's news feed will include one advertisement. Although somewhat dense, due to high precision, it hasn't caused significant user backlash, and ad click rates have been continuously rising, which has significantly increased Facebook's mobile income.

Good placement effects and thoughtful technical services have attracted more and more advertisers, allowing Facebook's mobile advertising business to operate at high speed. As a result, in this fiscal quarter, they earned $656 million in mobile advertising revenue, accounting for 40% of total advertising revenue, increasing by 11% compared to the previous quarter, which is four times the growth rate of PC-end advertising revenue.

While strengthening mobile advertising business, reducing the proportion of advertising in overall revenue is also a direction Facebook strives for. In the process of diversifying business, games become the preferred choice for generating revenue. Since parting ways with Zynga, Facebook has finally announced its own gaming platform recently.

With as many as 260 million game users among its users and 800 million potential users, once Facebook's gaming platform is promoted, its power cannot be underestimated. Currently, the main mobile gaming platforms are concentrated in Apple and Google's app stores, where they take a 30% cut of in-app purchases. Additionally, Japan's Line communication software has also earned substantial income through mobile games. After Facebook launched its gaming platform, besides co-operating with game developers, it can also develop and release its own games. It has already attracted numerous game developers, and given the booming mobile gaming market, the launch of this platform will help Facebook further tap into users' revenue potential.

Besides, Facebook also has a killer app: Instagram. Recently, this app added a short video function, directly competing with Vine. Instagram currently has over 100 million users, a high-quality community for premium images, and for brands with distinct personalities, it is an excellent advertising venue. However, Facebook has not yet initiated its commercialization.

As Facebook's stock price continues to rise, it also drives Mark Zuckerberg's net worth to soar. In the past week, his wealth surged by $5 billion, reaching $15.9 billion. His ranking in terms of wealth reached 42nd place, surpassing Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer and Dell founder Michael Dell. The rise in its stock price indicates the enormous monetization potential of social networks and also sets an example for other similar products.
