My life-and-death tribulation: putting down arms

by wwxxq8408 on 2009-12-07 01:30:29

A hero is indeed a hero; their attacks are all insane. One hit doesn't show less than 1000+ HP. As for me, I was touched by that ES's "salty hand" and only had 1000+ HP left, with 300 MP! Since we were unprepared, our gears were all junk. Not to mention, I only had 5300+ HP, losing over 1000 in one hit. Poor Qi Gui couldn’t even come into play, so there was nothing to do but take some potions. The main monster was killed off, immediately transforming into a BY Ghost General with 16000 HP, making us feel like smashing our heads against tofu #36.

Fortunately, the sword soul was smart, reversing and hitting #89. A main monster is a main monster; with just a light touch on its subordinates, it deals over 2000 HP, ruthless! The Sword Soul rushes up again, wildly sweeping around. The monsters on the other side aren't dumb either; they specifically target him. Poor thing!~ He gets swept and dies, resurrected by my level 9 skill, while level 142’s 5Z Huangxuan gives him potions and restores his health. Then he sweeps again, dies again.

After being revived from dying three times, the main monster finally disappears, and the smaller ones get sealed. 5Z is truly great; when sealed, it saves time for even moving. That's how, unexpectedly, Fire Dragon managed to pass the first level. When the second level came, we all fled. At that time, we thought that as long as we fought once, the whole team would have passed. Unexpectedly, it only counted for the team leader! Huangxuan was dissatisfied, saying he wanted to pass too. There was no choice but to form a team and try again. This time wasn't as smooth as last time. From the start, Huangxuan got sealed, and in the second round, Ice Dragon also got sealed. As for me, I was swept by both the main monster and the smaller ones and died...

In the second round, Huangxuan moved quickly, escaping, while the others all teleported back to the CA rooftop for free. Outraged, I went home and brought some big gold incense, five dragons, changed equipment, and went again! This time, a few people were swapped out. Still Ice Dragon, Fire Dragon, me, then a 140-level non-flying PS Crystal, and my clumsy husband at 135 non-flying DT. Reorganizing and going back on stage, we were still a little scared inside. After all, those monsters were BT (insane). We took out ghost-repelling pets. After my husband's sweep, the pet rushed up and with one hit, the main monster disappeared! Everyone was delighted #50.

Ice Dragon's seal didn't seem to work well due to some kind of counter-effect or something. Even though it was 20 levels higher, it didn't have the same hit rate as Crystal who was 18 levels lower. My poor husband, forgetting to transform into a vampire, rushed up and swept three times, getting stunned and dying right at the entrance. I used my level 9 resurrection skill, and he rushed up again, only to be stunned and die under someone else's pants. He was so mad he lost his shape, but it made me laugh until my stomach hurt #25.

Because we lacked a 5Z, many monsters needed to be killed. Luckily, most were sealed. Those smaller ones were like pigs with their hands and feet tied, left for us to deal with as we pleased. And we? We just had to decide whether to cut this one first or that one. This time went very smoothly, and we barely used any potions except for my husband getting stunned twice #17.

I consider myself an old hand in Happy Valley, with a six-digit ID number starting with six, yet I've only reached level 135, sigh. And my husband, with a seven-digit ID number starting with two, has already reached the same level as me... He has both master and apprentice titles, so he has one more title than me, which often makes me angry!~ But this time, I gained a new title for passing the life-and-death trial, so I can finally brag to him, hehe!~

We did several more attempts after this, but I won't go into details here #17.

Summary:

The farthest left and right in the back row are both ES, using ascension skills at the beginning. Close to the ES are STLs, either transforming or attacking normally. (These are the ones I remember most clearly.) The front row mostly uses normal attacks. The farthest left and right in the front row are DTs. Near the DT on the left is another ES.

First round: The main monster usually starts by sweeping, if not sweeping, it will use a normal attack.
