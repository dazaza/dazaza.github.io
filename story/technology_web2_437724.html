How is Shenzhen Fuhua Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital for treating liver fibrosis? Director Mo Yuzhen, an expert in liver fibrosis at Shenzhen Fuhua Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital, pointed out that complications of liver fibrosis only occur at the end stage of the disease. Therefore, the goal of treatment is to weaken rather than eliminate the liver's response to chronic damage. Anti-fibrosis treatment can partially reverse or slow down the progression of liver fibrosis.

Director Mo said that it is generally believed that anti-liver fibrosis drugs are only effective for those with liver fibrosis below stage 3. If it reaches stage 4, it is difficult to achieve therapeutic effects because anti-liver fibrosis drugs have no effect on aged collagen fibers. Therefore, early treatment must be given. If effective treatment is carried out early, complete reversal of liver fibrosis can be achieved.

How is Shenzhen Fuhua Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital for treating liver fibrosis? It is unclear how long liver fibrosis treatment needs to last. Since liver fibrosis is a chronic process, treatment may need to continue for several years, which can be intermittent. Current studies show that significant reversal of liver fibrosis can occur within five years of treatment.

How is Shenzhen Fuhua Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital for treating liver fibrosis? Director Mo introduced that ideal anti-liver fibrosis treatment should have the following characteristics: targeting hepatic stellate cells with high specificity; minimal toxicity to hepatocytes and other non-parenchymal cells; able to effectively reverse progressive liver fibrosis instead of merely preventing new collagen deposition.

Director Mo said that liver fibrosis can lead to compression, distortion, closure of intrahepatic vessels, or the formation of short-circuit anastomoses between arteries and veins, resulting in portal hypertension. On the other hand, liver fibrosis can cause circulatory disorders, affecting blood supply to liver cells, making it difficult for damaged liver cells to repair or aggravating the damage, and eventually possibly leading to liver failure. As seen above, if liver fibrosis is left untreated and allowed to progress, it can result in very serious consequences, gradually developing into cirrhosis or liver cancer.

Liver fibrosis advocates early detection and early treatment. If liver fibrosis can be treated promptly in its early stages, it can interrupt its pathological progression and hopefully prevent the occurrence of cirrhosis. The treatment for liver fibrosis mainly focuses on two aspects: one is to address the underlying disease by removing causative factors, and the other is to treat liver fibrosis itself to prevent further progression of the condition and the appearance of complications.
