Analysis: Why Alexa data is still favored by many webmasters

by longersing on 2009-07-10 22:15:38

Alexa has been highly anticipated since its inception, and at the very least, all webmasters are familiar with it. Moreover, some webmasters pay close attention to Alexa's data, such as Alexa ranking data, traffic data, etc. Why do webmasters still care about Alexa even when they understand the limited utility of its data? We will carefully analyze this. SEO Tribe will take you deep into the minds of Chinese webmasters to explore the reasons for Alexa's existence.

Written by Nangong Weiguo

Veteran webmasters will not forget some of Alexa's earliest features (What is Alexa). From its establishment to today's new functionalities, we have always been paying attention to the data Alexa provides us. It's not because these data are particularly special, but rather because they are too ordinary, just a partial statistic. Many people ask: why don't I care about universal URLs but still focus on Alexa data? Why can I ignore my website conversion rate yet still care about Alexa data? The answers lie in the psychology of webmasters. By breaking through psychological barriers, we uncover them, and indeed, one word "illusory" sufficiently explains the reason for Alexa's existence.

Feeling Insecure - I have no other advantages

After operating a website for several years without receiving any tangible returns, I believe most webmasters are losing money while running their sites, albeit only the costs of domain names and hosting. Everyone wants to find a buyer to sell their site, but how to convince the buyer? If your site lacks selling points or uniqueness, what can you show the buyer? Data? What kind of data? Alexa (the role of Alexa rankings). Yes, a large part of Chinese webmasters are immersed in the game of numbers. With these data and Alexa rankings, they naturally think: I'm awesome. And we also know that tools for boosting Alexa rankings are everywhere.

Appearances - The website lacks fame, but Alexa can be used to justify it

Some webmasters use their SEO ranking success to prove their real capabilities, and I believe they leverage these search ranking techniques to make their websites generate infinite traffic, high conversion rates, and broad profit points. Other webmasters use the fame of their websites to deceive advertisers or "net lags" (people who lack awareness of the internet) to invest in them, thereby solving their financial difficulties in project initiation. Alexa rankings then become the object of their deceptive speeches.

Character - Poor character, using Alexa to scam investors' money

I once saw a network company in Guilin hire a so-called SEO expert (in 2006, domestic SEO awareness was immature) as the general manager of the company's subsidiary, and eventually let him manage the entire project. His true abilities left me astonished: someone who doesn't even understand the internet could become the general manager, spending all his efforts on manufacturing Alexa data, boasting about it to the boss to get money, saying invest this much, invest that much, and finally coming up with many foreign certifications costing thousands here and there (all thanks to the early stages of SEO entering China, where SEO was perceived as a cutting-edge technology, but in reality, it's just an ordinary network method).

Related information: On the http://www.alexa.com/edit page, you can submit and modify your website's information.

Actually, what I've said above is just individual phenomena. I believe that most webmasters today rationally view Alexa.
