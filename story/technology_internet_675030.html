How do teachers cultivate outstanding daughters? Get closer to the excellent student and her mother.

by lmxbbwudi23 on 2012-02-24 17:19:44

Student Daughter: Never miss any extracurricular activity opportunities

Teacher Mom: Exercise memory from a young age, give full choice rights

Lhasa Road Elementary School has earned many good reputations for its students' excellent performance in Olympiad competitions and the large number of students admitted to Nanjing Foreign Language School. So, what learning experiences do the students of Lhasa Road Elementary School have? And what secrets do the teachers at Lhasa Road Elementary School [grab students] have? The reporter takes you closer to the [outstanding child] — Guo Naimeng and her teacher mom. □ Reporter He Ye

What kind of [outstanding child] is the daughter?

Great at table tennis, talented in English

The [outstanding child] is named Guo Naimeng, a sixth-grade student at the Yincheng Branch of Lhasa Road Elementary School. She achieved an [excellent] score in last year's [National New Curriculum Standard English Level 5 Exam]. There were only 22 elementary school students who achieved this score.

Seizing every opportunity to learn English

Guo Naimeng has a cute nickname [outstanding child], which represents the diligence, love for learning, and excellence of the sixth-grade students at the Yincheng Branch of Lhasa Road Elementary School.

The reporter learned that the National New Curriculum Standard English Levels 4 and 5 are equivalent to the English proficiency of junior high school students, and [Level 5 Excellent] has already reached the graduation level of ordinary junior high schools. How did Guo Naimeng, still in the sixth grade, achieve this?

"I like watching original animated films most; they are all great contributors!" Guo Naimeng said happily while holding a stack of Disney original animated films. When asked if she could understand them, the little girl said that during the first viewing, she mostly focuses on the storyline and listens to the Chinese dubbing. On the second viewing, she forces herself to listen to the original English dubbing. Subsequently, Guo Naimeng started talking about the character dialogues and music from the animated films while speaking with the reporter. Guo Naimeng's mother said that this interesting training not only enhances interest in learning English but also exercises listening and reading skills, producing very good results.

In the eyes of both students and teachers, Guo Naimeng is a lively child who particularly enjoys making friends. During the summer vacation of first grade, Guo Naimeng attended the English summer camp at Nanjing Foreign Language School. For two weeks, she stayed at Nanjing Foreign Language School with other campers, immersed in a fully English environment. Her mother said that this activity was the first step towards Guo Naimeng's independence and also the first step in learning English. Now, Guo Naimeng often talks about how she missed her mother then and how she overcame difficulties.

After finding joy in learning English, Guo Naimeng actively signs up for extracurricular English interest classes every year and attends classes every weekend. Whenever there is an opportunity to meet foreign students, Guo Naimeng seizes it. She once hosted a visiting American university student staying in Nanjing University (Weibo). Although Guo’s father understands English, he only handles logistics and does not interfere with Guo Naimeng's communication with her foreign friend. Guo Naimeng took the American sister shopping, introducing her to Nanjing and showcasing the warmth of the children of Nanjing.

Now, because she wants to study at Nanjing Foreign Language School, Guo Naimeng has never relaxed her English studies. On her bookshelf stands a set of seven Harry Potter books in their original full English version, specially brought back from the United States by her father. She is attempting to finish reading them. This semester, she voluntarily requested to enroll in the interest class. On the first day of class, she got up early, saying she wanted to go early to occupy a seat, "I want to sit in the middle of the third row." Her mother asked why? She replied, "The first two rows are given to classmates with poor vision who haven't yet gotten glasses."

The Student Principal Who Loves Sports

Guo Naimeng always helps her classmates in subtle ways. She usually carries an extra set of stationery in her pencil case and an extra dictionary in her bag, ready to lend to careless classmates. Initially, it was her mother who required her to do so, but gradually, it became a habit, and she began to consider her classmates in other aspects as well.

Because of her good relationships, she was nominated by her classmates to participate in the evaluation for the "Four Good Youth of Jiangsu Province" and successfully elected. At the same time, she also ran for the position of "Student Principal."

To run for "Student Principal," one must create a campaign poster and deliver a campaign speech to the entire school. Guo Naimeng had previously served as a leader for physical exercises in grades one and two. In her speech, she interacted with the children who followed her daily exercises, mobilizing their enthusiasm and gaining high support rates.

One of the tasks of the "Student Principal" is to provide the school with a "golden idea." Guo Naimeng observed during recess that younger students liked to chase and play on the playground, while older students were unwilling to leave the classroom for activities, neither being beneficial rest methods. Therefore, she proposed a "one-to-one" plan, allowing older students to play with younger students during breaks. Her idea was "partially adopted" and modified by the school into a new system.

Frequent public speaking has exercised Guo Naimeng's language expression ability and self-confidence. Chinese is also her forte. Her teachers praised her writing skills especially highly, commenting on her: "Many things described in your writings make many classmates feel outclassed. You can use very fluent language to express unique viewpoints. Listening to your speeches not only benefits classmates but also enlightens teachers." She also has multiple essays published in newspapers.

What pleases her mother most is: despite the heavier workload in the sixth grade, Guo Naimeng maintains a good learning state, efficiently completing learning tasks, "[She doesn’t forget to feed and tease goldfish and turtles, and occasionally jokes with her parents, which is really good!]"

Guo Naimeng generally finishes her homework at school, using her time at home to study English, read extracurricular books, and sometimes watch TV dramas. Her efficient state is not unrelated to her continuous practice of playing table tennis. She sweats profusely in the gym every day after school and has become a registered athlete for table tennis in Nanjing City.

How did the mother cultivate her?

Personality and habits are more important than grades

Among the various award certificates listed in the self-recommendation materials made by her mother for Guo Naimeng's primary school entrance exam—English, composition, mathematics, as well as various "three-good students" and "four-good youths"—which certificate does she value the most? This "teacher-mother" said: none of them are important; the habits and character formed from childhood are the most important.

The mother does not do the child's tasks for her

As a primary school teacher and head teacher, Guo Naimeng's mother is also busy in her daily life. "In the first and second grades, I focused on cultivating her habits, but after the third grade, I basically don't need to manage her anymore." Now, any recitations or dictations assigned by the teacher are completed independently by Guo Naimeng, and her mother only signs to confirm. "She dares not to complete them, otherwise, she would feel guilty," her mother trusts her completely.

This habit was cultivated since kindergarten. The kindergarten teacher would assign some homework for parents and children to complete together, such as going out to play and then having the child narrate the experience while the mother writes down the details. Since kindergarten children cannot write, some parents take shortcuts and complete the assignments alone.

Guo Naimeng's mother absolutely refuses to do so. She tells her child: "Completing homework is your own responsibility. If you don't move your hands or think, I won't help either." The same applies to other assignments, such as paper cutting and handicrafts.

Raising children according to textbooks

After starting primary school, her mother required Guo Naimeng to be organized, not procrastinate, and learn to control her emotions. Only when Guo Naimeng loses control of her emotions will her parents shout at her. If her test scores are unsatisfactory, they pay no attention and let her analyze the reasons herself. They have always believed that as long as the child's habits and methods are correct, her grades will not be bad.

Although Guo Naimeng's mother is a teacher at the Yincheng Branch of Lhasa Road Elementary School, she also raises her own child "by the book." "Our generation of mothers actually raise children according to textbooks." Guo Naimeng's mother said that she is also raising a child for the first time, without much experience, feeling her way forward. The key is to be good at learning and read more educational books.

Parent-child games to exercise memory

In the early stages of childhood, exercising memory capacity is crucial. However, simply making children memorize can be quite painful. Guo Naimeng's mother's method is to play parent-child games.

For example: there are many items on the table, let the baby observe the table and remember the positions of the items, then turn around. The mother quietly moves the items on the table and then lets the baby look back at the table and guess how the mother moved the items. This game can start easy and gradually increase in difficulty, moving one item first, then slowly increasing the number.

The reporter asked Guo Naimeng to try it out, moving two items on a table filled with fruits, cups, books, and various other clutter. She almost didn't need to think and clearly pointed out the movement trajectory.

Another game is jigsaw puzzles. Her mother bought several sets of puzzles for Guo Naimeng, and she still likes to play them now. "Playing puzzles not only exercises the baby's observation skills but also trains her small fingers," her mother explained. The more agile a baby's fingers are when they are young, the smarter their brain becomes as they grow older. Training in playing the piano and table tennis serves a similar function.

Giving children the right to choose

Every child inevitably has flaws and weak points. Her mother clearly knows Guo Naimeng; she is good in other areas except for "Olympiad Mathematics," which requires higher abstract thinking abilities.

Guo Naimeng's math within the curriculum isn't bad. But when faced with difficult problems in "Olympiad Mathematics," she inevitably gets frustrated. Sometimes, if her exam scores aren't ideal, she comes home frowning, venting her dissatisfaction with "Olympiad Mathematics" to her mother.

In such situations, her mother patiently chats with her and helps her sort out the troubles. Knowing that her daughter has always aspired to attend Nanjing Foreign Language School, she told her: "If you want to go to Nanjing Foreign Language School, you have to learn a bit more than other children, and you need to train in 'Olympiad Mathematics.' Learning 'Olympiad Mathematics' will encounter difficulties. What should you do when you encounter difficulties? If crying and shouting can solve the problem, I support you crying. But if it can't solve the problem, complaining won't solve the problem either, and the difficulty still needs to be overcome by yourself."

Whether to study "Olympiad Mathematics" or not, she gives her daughter the choice, telling her: "You have three choices: one, not studying Olympiad Mathematics reduces your chances of getting into Nanjing Foreign Language School; two, deciding to work hard and excel in Olympiad Mathematics; three, surpassing Olympiad Mathematics mentally, following along without dropping it, but without strict requirements, whether you perform well or not doesn't matter." Finally, Guo Naimeng chose the third option, and she returned to her previous happy learning state.
