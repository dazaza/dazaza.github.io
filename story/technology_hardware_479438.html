From the domestic market perspective, in recent years, with the rapid development of the traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) industry, the TCM herbs market has become increasingly prosperous. Due to renewed trust in TCM, there has been significant growth in the research and development of Chinese patent medicines, leading to a supply shortage of precious TCM herbs, with their market prices continuously rising. In the early 1980s, the market purchase price for centipedes was only about 0.4 yuan per piece, but now it has risen to 2 yuan, and there is still a severe shortage of supply. The market supply of centipede venom is even more insufficient.

Centipede breeding relies on rural resource advantages as a good method. Breeding animal-based medicinal materials such as centipedes can be a good choice if excellent techniques are mastered.

As one of China’s traditional valuable medicinal herbs, centipedes have a long history of medicinal use, being recognized and utilized by people over 2,000 years ago, with detailed records found in many medical compendiums like *Compendium of Materia Medica* (*Bencao Gangmu*).

The medicinal value of centipedes bred by Nongke Humin No. 2 Centipede Farming Project is very high; there are over 100 traditional Chinese prescriptions that include centipedes. Centipedes are an essential ingredient in more than 30 types of Chinese patent medicines, such as Ginseng Regeneration Pills, Great Holistic Pill, and Qianzheng Powder, showcasing notable therapeutic effects. For example, centipedes can treat vascular inflammation, blood clot blockages, trigeminal neuralgia, anti-cancer detoxification, tumor dissipation, wind-removal and shock-relief, facial paralysis, tetanus, and more. These applications have sharply increased the demand for centipedes. Besides medicinal uses, centipedes can also be made into nourishing foods.

With the advancement of social material civilization, centipedes bred by Nongke Humin No. 2 Centipede Farming Project, as treatment and health-care products, have gained much attention. Centipede wine and centipede Fen health products have successively appeared on the market. Additionally, with the development of medicine, the role of centipede venom has been widely recognized—centipede venom is more valuable than gold, priced at approximately $150,000 per kilogram. About 500 grams of venom can be extracted annually from 10,000 centipedes. Therefore, the medicinal value of centipede venom far exceeds that of the centipedes themselves.

Currently, the market supply of centipedes mainly depends on wild capture. However, with the extensive use of fertilizers and pesticides, the natural habitat of wild centipedes has been damaged, causing a sharp decline in the natural population, resulting in market supply shortages. Currently, the national production can only meet around 30% of the demand, leading to a sharp rise in market prices. Artificial centipede farming has broad development prospects, and a wave of getting rich through centipede farming will appear nationwide.

The economic benefits of centipede farming are significant. While providing society with valuable traditional Chinese medicinal herbs to benefit humanity, those who farm centipedes can also achieve high economic returns. As a valuable herb, centipedes have a large market demand. Artificial centipede farming is gradually becoming a promising undertaking.
