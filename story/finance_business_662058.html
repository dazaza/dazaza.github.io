by yixan2012 on 2012-02-09 16:56:08

□ Reporter Xinping Liu, Intern Chaowen Dong, Xinxiang News: At around 9 a.m. on February 4th, Ms. Wang (29 years old) residing in Xinxiang City gave birth to a male infant weighing 14.08 pounds via cesarean section at the Maternal and Child Health Hospital of Xinxiang City. Currently, both mother and child are healthy. According to information obtained by the reporter, this big baby boy is very likely to have broken the national record for the heaviest newborn.

Yesterday afternoon, in the ward of the hospital's maternity department, the reporter saw this large baby boy measuring 60 centimeters in length. He was lying in his grandmother's arms, sucking on a pacifier. His chubby little face was extremely adorable. This one-day-old infant looked bigger than a full-term baby.

"I've been a doctor for 20 years, and it's my first time seeing such a big baby!" Deputy Director of Obstetrics at the hospital, Dr. Ren Xijie, smiled as she told reporters that when she examined Ms. Wang earlier, she already knew the baby would be heavy. Initially, she estimated it would be about 10 pounds, but when she placed the baby on the electronic scale, the number kept climbing until it finally settled at 7040 grams, which surprised her greatly. "Newborns' weights are measured in grams; over 4000 grams is considered a large baby; over 4500 grams is an extra-large baby. This child weighs 7040 grams, truly a big baby boy!"

It is understood that Ms. Wang has another daughter who is six years old, and the daughter weighed more than 8 pounds at birth. Both Ms. Wang and her husband Mr. Han have normal builds. The surgery went very smoothly, and it only took about 20 minutes for this "chubby boy" to be born. Ms. Wang said that during her pregnancy, she was just like any other pregnant woman, except her belly was a bit larger. Her diet was also similar to other pregnant women, with nothing particularly unusual. According to the doctor, the reason the baby was so heavy is mainly because "he absorbed nutrients well in the womb."

Ms. Wang's mother-in-law happily said, "The whole family is brainstorming names for our grandson. Since he was born on Start of Spring day, I gave him a nickname 'Chun Chun!'"

According to information obtained from online research, the Guinness World Record for the heaviest baby is held by an Italian-born infant in 1955, weighing 20 pounds 4 ounces. On February 3rd, 2006, a girl weighing 12 pounds 3 ounces was delivered via cesarean section at the Third Affiliated Hospital of Xuzhou, Jiangsu Province, making her the third heaviest baby in the world at the time. On March 28th, 2008, a 14-pound male infant was born at a hospital in Haicheng City, Liaoning Province, setting the national record for the heaviest newborn. In 2009, a Shandong province woman gave birth to a 14-pound male infant; on May 8th, 2010, a 39-year-old woman named Tang Xinxiang in Zhumadian City, Henan Province, gave birth to a 14-pound male infant, tying for the heaviest baby in the nation at the time.
