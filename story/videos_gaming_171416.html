"Devil May Cry 4" Personal Research Insights

by lk0gamea on 2009-07-06 08:28:46

In everyone's mind, a master of Devil May Cry has always been someone who performs flashy moves and gets an S rank in every level. Actually, performing flashy moves is something we can all achieve with enough playtime; each of us has our own combo strategy. The key lies in how to connect these combos. Whether it's fighting bosses or lesser enemies, connecting the moves is simple, but getting a high evaluation isn't easy. Devil May Cry 4's evaluation system depends on three points: time, stylishness, and red orb gain. I won't talk much about red orbs since I also struggle with them. As long as you get an S rank, your time will definitely be an A. I usually get an S rank from level 1 to 20. Time management comes down to your familiarity with the route and speed of defeating enemies. Of course, there are also bosses. I'll mainly focus on stylishness.

The way to achieve stylishness is through combos. Devil May Cry 4 is simpler in this regard compared to 3. In 3, if you lose the combo chain, the stylish bar disappears and you have to start over. So, 4 can be seen as more beginner-friendly.

First, you need to master combos. During the combo process, if you use the same move twice or more, your stylishness won't increase and may even decrease. Therefore, you need to have at least two sets of combo moves.

Next, after you've mastered two sets of combos, what you need to do is learn how to dodge. This is where the skill of the experts shines. To keep the combo chain going, you need to dodge. It's like wanting to hit others but first learning how to take a hit. Here, I'll share a relatively quick method. If you've completed the game once, Blood Palace mode will naturally unlock. You can go there to practice dodging. Before that, download a trainer (sorry, moderator, but the countdown can be tough, so please bear with me), set your health and time to be infinite, then dodge the enemies. When you can avoid being hit for 2 to 3 minutes, defeat them and move to the next floor. Try one enemy type at a time, and finally, try mixed types. Two minutes is enough for mixed types.

Practice has proved that "Happy Farm" is full of fun.
