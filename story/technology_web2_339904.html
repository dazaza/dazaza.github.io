Fitting is key for formal wear. The length of the jacket should extend past the hips, the standard size is half the distance from the neck to the ground; the sleeve length should end about 11 cm above the thumb; the shirt collar should be slightly higher than the suit collar; the pants should not show the socks, reaching the heel; the front waist should be lower and the back higher, the pant style can be chosen according to the trend, and the edges cannot be rolled up; these are all basic pairings for wearing suits, showing the normativity of formal wear.

Suit core coordination Brightly colored suits should be paired with gray, black, white, or high-brightness shirts of the same color family; dark suits should be lined with light-colored shirts; single-color suits should be paired with patterned or striped shirts according to the atmosphere of formal occasions. Solid-colored suits are suitable for striped shirts + solid or small-pattern ties; striped suits are suitable for solid shirts + small-pattern ties; dark suits should be paired with light shirts and dark ties; light suits should be paired with dark shirts and light ties.
