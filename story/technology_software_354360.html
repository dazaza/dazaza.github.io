China UnionPay and telecom operators make efforts on mobile payment and stir up capital pool

by pcboyecbec on 2010-05-04 14:45:27

Mobile payment can integrate all relevant cards for daily life payment and settlement into one. (Reference photo) □ Written by Qiao Qianqian of Information Times. Without carrying a wallet or public transportation card, you can realize the small amount debit card and credit card payment functions just by "brushing" your phone. As the opening of the Shanghai World Expo is approaching, China Mobile is taking the opportunity to promote its mobile payment business, which is interpreted by the industry as a kind of "business breakthrough". And China Mobile's intention to invest in the Shanghai Pudong Development Bank for developing mobile payment is self-evident. While China Mobile is conducting mobile payment, UnionPay is also in the race. It is understood that in mid-March, the concept of mobile payment, which has been uneventful, became a hot topic in the capital market.

China Mobile's investment in Pudong Bank aims at breaking through. It is understood that China Mobile's mobile payment business has been launched for 6 years, but its development has not been smooth sailing. Although this business has a tortuous development path in China, China Mobile's investment in Pudong Bank seems to be a landmark event for the further development of mobile payment business this year.

An analyst said that the promotion of mobile payment business cooperated by UnionPay, various telecom operators and banks was not successful before, because there were conflicts of interest with the existing credit card business of banks, so the banks' support was not strong. "Mobile payment involves small amount payment, which has little relation with banks and does not violate financial policies. But if it is large amount payment, then it needs to rely on the bank's platform to carry out." Li Zhizhong, an electronic industry researcher of Rixin Securities, said.

China Mobile's investment in Pudong Bank can solve this problem. The share subscription agreement issued by China Mobile shows that the shares of Shanghai Pudong Bank subscribed by China Mobile will be paid in cash by Guangdong Mobile at the closing. Guangdong Mobile will pay the consideration with its internal resources. The announcement of China Mobile also shows that China Mobile and Guangdong Mobile signed an exhibition cooperation memorandum with Pudong Bank on the same day. According to the strategic cooperation memorandum signed, both parties intend to carry out close cooperation in jointly developing mobile finance and mobile e-commerce, including but not limited to cooperating in carrying out mobile payment business, mobile bank card business, mobile transfer business, and other forms of mobile finance and mobile e-commerce business.

"In Japan, where mobile payment business is relatively mature, monopolistic operators acquired banking business early, and then completed resource integration well by acquiring merchant shares for network construction." Li Zhizhong said, and China is actually developing according to Japan's model.

Zhou Jun, a senior analyst of communication industry of Orient Securities, believed that in 2010, China Mobile would include mobile payment business in the group KPI assessment system and focus on promoting it. In recent years, businesses included in the group KPI have all achieved extraordinary development. Referring to this development model, it is believed that mobile payment business users will achieve explosive growth this year, and the number of users will reach millions.

The replacement cost of POS machines tests operators. Although the market believes that China Mobile is ambitious, the development direction of mobile payment is still unclear. An analyst pointed out that at first, China Mobile wanted to break through with the help of the Expo, that is, launch the business of brushing phones to buy tickets and take public transportation during the Expo period, and conduct POS machine consumption in cooperative merchants, becoming one of the highlights of the Expo. But recently, it was reported that there are still controversies within China Mobile about the development direction of mobile payment. Due to the high cost of near-field payment, opinions favoring remote payment are more advantageous.

"Although the RF-SIM solution has certain advantages, since its frequency band is unique, once mobile payment develops, all hardware devices such as POS machines need to be replaced, and the cost issue becomes a factor that operators must consider." Li Zhizhong said, currently, it is understood that no provincial company has received a notice to stop the business, it can only be said that the upper management of China Mobile is also discussing.

Operators and UnionPay are racing in mobile payment. While China Mobile is conducting mobile payment, UnionPay is also in the race. On March 16th, UnionPay announced that the pilot area of the new generation of mobile payment business would be expanded to six provinces and cities: Shanghai, Shandong, Ningbo, Hunan, Sichuan, and Shenzhen.

On April 16th, China UnionPay just launched the financial standard Changsha Citizen Card with five commercial banks including ICBC in Changsha. At the same time, with the Changsha Citizen Card as the carrier, China UnionPay and China Unicom will jointly promote UnionPay mobile payment business in Hunan. Users do not need to change their phone numbers when applying to open this service, they just need to implant the customized financial smart card into their phones through the issuing bank. It is expected that the card issuance volume will reach 1 million within the year. It is known that this is the first substantial progress made in the field of mobile payment since China UnionPay and China Unicom signed a comprehensive strategic cooperation agreement in November 2009. The willingness of China UnionPay and China Unicom to promote mobile payment business is self-evident.

Li Zhizhong said that no matter which solution mobile payment adopts, it will face the problem of cross-industry collaboration. Due to the funding strength issue of UnionPay, it is difficult to develop mobile payment business solely relying on UnionPay, but the advantage of shop POS machines lies in UnionPay, and China Mobile has a large scale of user volume and capital strength. The best way is for China Mobile, UnionPay, and banks to negotiate on this issue, ensure mutual interests on the basis of predetermined interests, and promote industrial development. This is the most important thing.

Current situation: The capital market has been stirred up. In the secondary market, the concept of mobile payment has experienced a round of speculation. According to WIND information classification statistics, companies in the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets that fit the concept of mobile payment include Sunluo Electronics, Zhengtong Electronics, Kangqiang Electronics, Dongxin Peace, and Changdian Technology, etc., and these stocks all rose against the market trend in the first quarter. On March 17th, related stocks such as Dongxin Peace, Xin大陆, Xiamen Xinda, Yuanwang Valley, and Gaohong Shares collectively hit the limit-up, while Changdian Technology hit the limit-up for two consecutive days.

An analyst believed that after a round of speculation, the valuation level of the mobile payment concept has become high, and chasing the rise should be cautious, but once the mobile payment industry develops, its industrial investment opportunities will still be great.

In his report, Li Zhizhong, an electronic industry researcher of Rixin Securities, pointed out that the development of the mobile payment market will drive domestic operators to strengthen cooperation with banks, UnionPay, equipment providers, system integrators, merchants, etc., to preempt group users, merchants, and terminal users in advance. The commercial experiment of mobile payment will drive the upstream and downstream of the entire industrial chain, including chip manufacturers, card manufacturers, mobile operators, service providers, and mobile phone manufacturers in the mobile payment value chain.

"As a SIM card supplier, Dongxin Peace occupies nearly 70% of the current market share of China Mobile's RF-SIM cards, and the benefits are obvious; as a communication card supplier for China Unicom and China Telecom, although Hengbao Share has no RF-SIM card orders at present, it has become a SIM card supplier for China Mobile by acquiring Oriental Yingka, and with the subsequent development of mobile payment business by China Unicom and China Telecom, Hengbao Share may also become a direct beneficiary." Li Zhizhong pointed out in his report.

"In the next 2~3 years, China Mobile plans to install 1 million RF-POS machines in merchants, schools, subways, buses and other systems. The initial network construction will be initially formed. Calculated at a market price of 2000 yuan per machine, the market size is 2 billion yuan." Li Zhizhong also said.

Huatai United Securities also pointed out that Nantian Information, as a preferred IT service provider in the financial field, has certain advantages in the competition of mobile payment platform solutions. Currently, Nantian has cooperated with mobile operators to build platforms in pilot provinces. It is expected that the company will maintain a 20% market share this year, and the POS machine business will become a new growth point for the company. Maintain its "Buy" rating.

Relevant background: The development road of mobile payment has been full of twists and turns. It is understood that mobile payment was originally carried out by sending text messages and logging into websites to use bank accounts for transactions, just by binding the mobile phone number with the bank account. Later, on-site payment gradually appeared. The "mobile wallet" business launched by China Mobile includes on-site consumption payment. It is understood that China Mobile's mobile payment business has been launched for 6 years, but its development has not been smooth sailing.

Currently, the RF-SIM solution adopted by China Mobile's "mobile wallet" business is to replace the RF-SIM card without changing the mobile phone number, and realize "brushing" consumption on POS machines, buses, subways and other service places. "Nowadays, the RF-SIM solution is generally perfect. This card is the same size as the mobile phone SIM card, and ultra-high frequency microwaves can easily penetrate the mobile phone lithium battery and metal plate, which is the one currently used by China Mobile's "mobile wallet" business." Li Zhizhong, an electronic industry researcher of Rixin Securities, told reporters that the business is currently being tested in more than ten provinces and cities such as Hunan, Chongqing, Guangdong, and Shanghai, and it is estimated that the number of users is at least over 500,000.

In addition to the on-site payment form of the RF-SIM solution, there are also NFC and SIM-pass. Li Zhizhong said that NFC is mainly promoted by Philips, Nokia, Sony and other manufacturers. Its application is to embed an NFC chip in the mobile phone, and then interconnect it with the SIM card. "This mode requires users to change their mobile phones, and due to the high cost, it is difficult to promote; the SIM-pass solution does not require changing the mobile phone, but due to the low frequency, the card is placed behind the battery, and the weak signal cannot penetrate the lithium battery or metal plate, so the stability is poor. This solution was tested internally by operators in 2006, and currently has less than 50,000 users. These two modes are basically stagnant now."
