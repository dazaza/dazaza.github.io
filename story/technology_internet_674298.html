Beijing will restore the six city gate towers which were destroyed after the founding of new China

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:38:45

The old sites of the city towers demolished after the founding of the People's Republic, such as Zuoanmen Turret and Yananmen Turret, will see new landmark buildings constructed in accordance with their original architectural styles so that people can have a clear intuitive perception about the original [convex]-shaped city contour of Guangzhou. As the largest scale historic architecture restoration project for famous cities since the founding of the People's Republic, Guangzhou City plans to restore the historical and cultural landscapes in six locations.

The six landmarks are respectively: the Northeast Turret of Guangzhou outer city, Yongdingmen Arrow Tower and its semi-circular fortress wall, the Northwest Turret of Guangzhou outer city, the Tianqiao ground marker, the Northeast Turret of inner city, and Yan翅 Building at both sides of Dianmen.

City walls and city towers were the most conspicuous marks of Guangzhou City, but after the founding of the People's Republic, most of them were demolished due to the need for traffic and other reasons, causing a historical regret.

To date, the Northwest Turret located inside the intersection of Jianguomen South Street and Chongwenmen East Street is the only preserved turret of Guangzhou City. It is located at the end of the remaining section of the city wall northwest of Guangzhou Station. The existing turret was built in Ming Zhengtong 4th year (1439), and has been repaired in every dynasty since then.

The ancient capital Guangzhou formed through construction and development over hundreds of years during the Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties has its most prominent historical and cultural value and urban architectural charm mainly manifested in the unique planning, overall style, traditional space, and the important historical building groups preserved till now.

According to the requirements of the "Guangzhou Historic and Cultural Famous City Protection Plan", it is necessary to consider the overall protection of the old city of Guangzhou. Specifically, this includes ten aspects: historical river and lake water systems, traditional central axis, imperial city, the [convex]-shaped city contour of the old city, roads and alleys, building heights, urban landscape lines, street views, building colors, ancient trees and famous woods.

The Guangzhou Cultural Relics Bureau introduced that the Guangzhou Municipal Government and the Dongcheng and Xicheng District Governments will organize the restoration of the landmark landscapes of the historical and cultural famous city, maintaining the three-dimensional [convex]-shaped city contour of Ming and Qing Guangzhou, protecting the outline of the outer city marked by the current moat and the direction of the city walls, further protecting and restoring the overall image and traditional urban structure of the ancient capital Guangzhou.

Not all landmark buildings can be restored

Guangzhou plans to build six ancient city landmarks. Considering the construction difficulties of the surrounding environment, the Northwest Turret near Zuoanmen is expected to be the earliest completed. The six landmarks are located at Yananmen, Yongdingmen, Zuoanmen, Tianqiao, Xibianmen, and Dianmen. The construction difficulties vary. Since the construction will be located on the old site, and the surrounding areas have undergone great changes in decades of urban development, the construction is not easy. Some old sites are currently close to railways, only dozens of meters away. In some places, even though they are green spaces now, coordination with relevant departments is still required. Comparatively speaking, the restoration difficulty of the Northwest Turret near Zuoanmen is relatively low, and it is expected to start construction the earliest.

The Cultural Relics Bureau indicated that the restoration of the city contour must also be carried out under the permission of transportation and municipal facilities, so not all landmark buildings can be restored. Additionally, for markers like the Tianqiao ground marker, the future plan is to display the remaining foundation of Tianqiao, and the specific display method is still under study.

Expert [Restoration is a symbol, not an artifact]

Cultural relics expert Xie Chensheng said that already damaged cultural relics cannot be restored

Regarding Guangzhou City's [Historic Architecture Restoration Project for Famous Cities], well-known cultural relics expert Xie Chensheng said that according to the request of the cultural relics law, immovable cultural relics that have been completely destroyed should be protected as sites, and reconstruction on the old site is not allowed. However, if it is currently considered as a new marker, it is acceptable, but it must be clearly stated that it is not a cultural relic.

Mr. Xie Chensheng said that symbolic restoration is first and foremost not a cultural relic, the original cultural relic no longer exists, which is certain.

But now, as a newly built marker, using the original style is acceptable from a symbolic perspective; however, it cannot therefore be said that the original thing has been restored, [it cannot be restored, it would be entirely wrong]. Mr. Xie Chensheng said.

The [Historic Architecture Restoration Project for Famous Cities] should not aim to restore old buildings, but rather to restore a marker so that those who originally did not know can see an outline, and doing so is still acceptable.

Background [Convex]-shaped city contour aimed at defending against enemies

The outer city of Guangzhou was initially built in Ming Jiajing 32nd year (AD 1553). Adding a circle of outer city outside the inner city wall was intended to enhance the security of the city and resist internal traitor invasions.

Due to insufficient funds, in 1564, Guangzhou's outer city was completed, with a total length of only 14 kilometers, and seven city gates were opened, including Yongding Gate, Zuoan Gate, Yanan Gate, Guang'an Gate, Guangqu Gate, Dongbian Gate, and Xibian Gate.

The outer city was only built on the southern part of the inner city, and later no further expansion took place, thus forming the [convex]-shaped plane contour of Guangzhou City. The current Second Ring Road and Southern Second Ring Road were built along the [convex]-shaped contour line.

[Yongding Gate]

In 1564, to ensure the safety of the capital city, Guangzhou's outer city was completed, and the main gate was named [Yongding Gate], symbolizing [eternal peace].

As the main gate, Yongding Gate was the largest among the seven city gates of Guangzhou's outer city, and also a major thoroughfare for entering and exiting the capital from the south. The scale of Yongding Gate was much larger than the other six outer city gates, standing majestically at the southern end of Guangzhou City's central axis until it was dismantled in 1957. The restoration of Yongding Gate was completed before National Day in 2005.

[Zuoanmen Turret]

Adjacent to one of the seven city gates of the outer city, Zuoan Gate, during the Ming and Qing Dynasties, the Northwest Turret of the outer city was also known as the Zuoanmen Turret.

In the 1950s, during the overall dismantling process of Guangzhou's outer city, the Zuoanmen Turret disappeared in 1955. In September last year, the Dongcheng District civilian website announced the project and solicited opinions regarding the restoration of the Northwest Turret of Guangzhou's outer city, and the Northwest Turret of the outer city is expected to be fully restored in its original appearance in 2013.

[Yananmen Turret]

Built in Ming Jiajing 32nd year (AD 1553), the single-eaved hip roof tower had a gray barrel-tile roof; it was three bays wide and one bay deep, with a total height of 16.6 meters including the city platform. Two-layer arrow windows were opened on the south side and the east-west sides. It was completely dismantled in 1958.

[Inner City Northeast Turret]

The Inner City Northeast Turret was the original Xibianmen city tower, arrow tower, and its semi-circular fortress wall, built in 1439. Its structure was similar to the Dongbianmen city tower, but slightly lower in height.

After the founding of the People's Republic, Xibianmen city gate, fortress wall, and arrow tower were selected as the first city gate to be dismantled and were dismantled in 1952.

In 1988, the municipal government renovated the remaining 195 meters of the inner city wall closely adjacent to the east side of the city tower, and rebuilt the [Eight Staring Eyes] arrow tower at the connecting site of the outer city, preserving seven sections of traces.

[Dianmen Yan Wings Building]

A well-known landscape on the central axis road, built in 1420, located on both sides of the Dianmen gatehouse. They are two symmetrical two-story brick-concrete buildings facing east and west, each thirteen bays wide, covered with yellow glazed tiles, with distinctive architectural shapes, resembling a pair of wings spread by a large goose when viewed from afar, hence the name.

In the early days of the Republic of China, for the convenience of traffic, the city walls on both sides of Dianmen were dismantled. Starting in 1954, to guide the traffic in the northern urban area, Dianmen and the Yan Wings Buildings were dismantled together. The restoration project has not yet started.

[Tianqiao Ground Marker]

In ancient times, Tianqiao was a waterlogged area and marshland, later forming a river. After the Temple of Heaven was built in 1420, a magnificent single-arch high bridge was constructed south of Zhushikou and north of Yongdingmen. It was later named Tianqiao.

In 1929, due to the construction of a tram bridge, the bridge body was leveled, and in 1934, the bridge railings were removed. Since then, Tianqiao has only existed as a place name.
