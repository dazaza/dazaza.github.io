It seems almost certain that the successor to Nokia N8 will be announced at the MWC 2012, but there is still little information about what makes this photography flagship unique. Not long ago, overseas sources revealed that this phone would feature the largest sensor ever, and recently, Feng Guangsun, the Director of Information for Nokia China, also hinted on Weibo that the upcoming imaging flagship, the Nokia 808 PureView, will have a camera with far more than 16 million pixels.

Clearly, this successor to the Nokia N8 will bring even greater surprises.

Speculation on Camera Pixel Specifications

The speculation regarding the pixel specifications of the built-in camera in the successor to the Nokia N8 stems from a video Nokia released on its official YouTube channel titled "Get ready to capture a pure view," which explicitly referenced the 27th of this month, naturally bringing to mind the rumored Nokia 808 PureView.

Regarding this official video, Feng Guangsun stated on Weibo: "Here's another clue for you all. Can you guess how high Nokia will raise the mobile imaging capabilities in the upcoming release? Certainly much higher than the rumored 16 million pixels." In other words, the Nokia 808 PureView will break the record for the highest pixel count in a smartphone camera, held by the HTC Titan II, becoming the phone with the highest resolution camera in the future.

Perhaps Equipped with a 30 Million Pixel Lens

Notably, as people on Weibo kept increasing their guesses for the pixel count—moving from 24 million to 36 million—Feng Guangsun remarked: "Finally, someone has the guts to go big." Thus, we might speculate that the upcoming Nokia 808 PureView could be equipped with a camera around 30 million pixels.

Uniquely, such ultra-high pixel speculations are not new. DSMobile, known for leaking insider information about Nokia devices, described the built-in camera of this phone as "crazy" on Twitter. As a result, mynokiablog, an unofficial source, made a startling prediction: the Nokia photography flagship might be equipped with either a 30 or 40 million pixel camera.

Largest Sensor Ever

Interestingly, if past rumors are true and the Nokia 803 is indeed the current Nokia 808 PureView, then according to previous claims, this phone will be equipped with the largest sensor size available in any smartphone camera today. Everyone knows that the larger the sensor area of a camera, the better the image quality. Therefore, based on various signs, the Nokia 808 PureView may create a sensation when it is officially released next week.

In terms of other features of the Nokia 808 PureView, the device will run on a newer version of the operating system, featuring a 4.0-inch touchscreen and supporting NFC near-field communication functionality. It will also have an HDMI interface, and the SIM card slot will switch to micro-SIM. Additionally, while no specific processor details were disclosed, if this device truly is the successor to the Nokia N8, then according to past rumors, it will likely feature a more powerful processor, though whether it will upgrade to dual-core remains unclear.

Nokia is set to host a new product launch event on February 27 under the theme "Adventure in Barcelona," where six new models, including the Nokia 808 PureView, will be officially introduced. As for exactly how many megapixels the Nokia 808 PureView camera will have, let's wait and see.
