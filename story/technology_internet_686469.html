Beer, white wine or red wine? Which is your choice?

by chjdcj on 2012-03-02 09:47:42

Born in China, grew up in China. Family dinners, couples' dates, friends' gatherings - none of these are complete without everyone having a good drink. The most common phrase is: "Today, we don't go home until we're drunk!"

To be honest, drinking can be a pleasure, an emotion, even a release. "Drowning sorrows in alcohol" is the best example.

Beer is generally used for casual parties or in bars because of its low alcohol content and refreshing taste, with people often holding bottles to toast. Even girls who can't drink much can sip a little to join in the fun. It's very suitable for young people's gatherings and relatively inexpensive, making it a popular choice. If not consumed in large quantities, it's hard for those with a good tolerance to get drunk.

Wine, on the other hand, brings to mind classic scenes of flickering candlelight, soft violin music, and couples raising their glasses to share their hearts. Indeed, wine symbolizes romance and sophistication. High-end wineries produce wines with excellent taste, but they come at astonishing prices. There aren't many physical wine stores, at least I rarely see them in Nanjing. However, there are quite a few online flagship stores. As a commoner, I can't afford too high-end wines, so I usually buy red wines under 200 yuan. I once purchased from Taobao's "You Red Wine" and found it quite satisfactory. The price is also suitable for ordinary people. If you're interested, you can search for it. Additionally, drinking red wine has many benefits for women, such as beautifying and nourishing the skin.

Finally, let's talk about baijiu (Chinese white liquor). Generally, our parents' generation loves baijiu. My dad especially likes drinking baijiu; he enjoys a small glass every noon and feels like a happy god. Those who love baijiu pay attention to various types. Which ones are premium, and which ones are just cheap enough to get by. During the Spring Festival, my boyfriend brought a bottle of Tianzhi Lan to my house, knowing that my dad loves alcohol. He specially bought it to please him. Naturally, my dad was very happy. After the first sip, my dad said, "This wine is very smooth." I remembered that the blue classic's selling point is its softness. Although I rarely drink, I don't know what smoothness means, but my dad could tell right away. They finished a whole bottle over lunch. (Speechless)

Baijiu is more commonly used in formal dinners, such as business negotiations or showing respect to elders at the table. When there's someone older present at the dinner, baijiu is definitely required. Baijiu seems to encompass a lot of Chinese culture, from ancient times with the wine immortal Li Bai, continuing the culture of baijiu to today. There are many types of baijiu, each with its own preference. Anyway, after my dad tried Tianzhi Lan, he liked it. As children, how could we not be filial? When we have some savings, we buy a few bottles to show respect. Originally, I bought them from the supermarket, but they were slightly more expensive than direct sales. After searching online for a while, I found a trustworthy store called Da Chong Jiu. They sell Hai Zhi Lan, Meng Zhi Lan, and Tian Zhi Lan. I don't buy much, others buy by cases. I guess it's for hotels or something. Buying a case can come with free gifts. =_= But I can't buy that much. Why did I choose this store? Mainly because they have a physical store. Although I initially worried about potential quality issues, I thought that since they have a physical store, if there's a problem, I have somewhere to go ask. I live near Wutaishan, although it's a bit far from the physical store, I mentioned the distance to the staff, and they said the Qiaohuai Road store will also open soon, specifically the Nanjing Yanghe Qiaohuai Road store. Huh. Hopeful things happen =_= In the future, I can go directly to the store to buy.

Of course, everyone has different preferences. Whether you like beer, red wine, or baijiu, remember to drink in moderation. Overdrinking isn't good!
