China is the world's largest aquaculture country, accounting for more than 70% of global farming. The freshwater farming area is about 10 million mu, and the production of freshwater products has been ranked first in the country for 16 consecutive years, enjoying unique advantages in the development of the aquatic industry. With the rapid development of the provincial economy, modern agriculture also shows vitality. Greenhouse shrimp farming can avoid and reduce the impact of climate on shrimp farming, further ensuring high and stable yields. The company takes survival through quality and development through technology as its growth concept. While doing a good job in the breeding base and providing healthy and safe products, it stands on the current situation of the enterprise and continuously expands the development path of the enterprise to achieve multi-polar comprehensive development. The experience lesson that those aquaculture enterprises that want to go public draw from this story is: enterprises should do a good strategic planning before going public, launch products with relatively high profits, and at the same time beware of the trend of profit averaging. Specialized farming is a sunrise industry that the state vigorously advocates and supports. Due to the development of agriculture and environmental changes, many wild animal resources have sharply decreased, and the demand is increasing day by day (not including large-scale exports). Therefore, artificial breeding is inevitable, and the prospect of breeding is very broad with remarkable benefits. Deputy Minister of Agriculture Niu Dun proposed requirements for the work in 2011 and during the [12th Five-Year Plan] period at the liaison meeting held at the end of 2010 for administrative, public institutions, enterprises, and social organizations in the fishery system in Beijing: In the next five years, we will adhere to ecological healthy farming as the direction, promote professional, standardized, scaled, and intensive development, and ensure the safe and effective supply of aquatic products. Healthy farming has become a hot topic in aquaculture and a word with high frequency of use. Stable food intake, wide range of food. High degree of artificial domestication, can be bred on a large scale in factories. Strong disease resistance and high survival rate. As long as the temperature is well controlled, the water quality is kept fresh and non-toxic, free of industrial pollution, the feed is fresh and sufficient, the micro-strong American bullfrog rarely gets sick, with a survival rate of over 90%. Simple farming equipment, low investment. About 20,000 adult frogs can be farmed per acre, and after 5-6 months of feeding, each frog can weigh 500 grams, making the economic benefits quite considerable. If an original intensive farming method is adopted, the economic benefits will be even higher.
