The northernmost part of Northeast China is Mohe, located at approximately 53°N. On the day of the summer solstice in Mohe, night begins at 10 PM, and after four hours, it transitions back to daylight. In winter, the opposite occurs; around the time of the winter solstice, the night lasts for 17 hours, and even at noon, the sky isn't very bright. For this reason, Mohe is known as "China's Arctic." Additionally, Fuyuan Delta in Heilongjiang Province, situated at 135°E, starts its daily activities while people in Beijing are still asleep. This inspired poet Hu Shizong to write a song called "I Welcome the Sun into Our Motherland."

The Northeast region is located in the cold temperate zone and has a distinct temperate continental monsoon climate. It features concurrent rainfall and heat, as well as concurrent snowfall and cold. The entire Northeast experiences cold and long winters, with warm and humid summers. The coldest month is January, with average temperatures ranging from -30°C in the north to -5°C in the south. The hottest month is July, with average temperatures ranging from 16°C to 20°C. Average precipitation increases from 300 mm in the north to 1200 mm in the south, with most of the rain concentrated in July, August, and September. The coldest place is Mohe, where on February 13, 1969, the temperature dropped to -52.3°C.

The frost-free period in the Greater Khingan Range is only 100 to 140 days. The average number of snowy days in Heilongjiang Province can reach up to five and a half months, with snow accumulation between 0.8 and 1.5 meters. The soil freezes to a depth of 1 to 2 meters, and the permafrost layer remains unfrozen all year round. The rivers of Heilongjiang, Songhua, Wusuli, and Nen freeze over in winter, with ice layers reaching up to 1.5 meters thick. Navigation resumes only after the ice floes (also known as ice jams) have passed in May of the following year. Therefore, even with scientific farming methods, the Greater Khingan Range cannot produce peanuts, sweet potatoes, apples, or pears like Liaoning's southern regions. Instead, potatoes, radishes, and cabbage remain the main staples (except for small-scale experimental farming).

Thus, Northeast China, especially Heilongjiang and Jilin provinces, has become famous for two major phenomena: snow and ice, and rime ice.

Although icy and snowy conditions prevent agricultural production, they bring joy to people—

Winter sports: skiing, skating, sledding, playing with ice tops...

Ice and snow art: ice lanterns, ice sculptures, snow castles, snow sculptures...

Playing with ice and snow has become an industry, allowing people to earn money happily through these activities.

Rime ice, also known as tree hanging, is the result of collisions between cold and warm air in nature. Rime ice forms on tree branches and grass stems, freezing into beautiful, fluffy frost flowers that are stunningly exquisite but fleeting. With its unique charm, rime ice is regarded as one of China's four natural wonders alongside Guilin's landscape, Yunnan's Stone Forest, and the Three Gorges of the Yangtze River.

No one loves snow and ice more than compatriots from Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan. When they come to the icy cities and snowy villages, they roll in the snow and sleep on the ice. Eventually, they transform themselves into "Snow White" and "Santa Claus."

Northeast China is not just about cold; it also has warmth. Along the Bohai Sea and the Yellow Sea, the climate incorporates oceanic characteristics. Cities like Dandong, Dalian, Yingkou, Jinzhou, and Huludao differ significantly from other parts of Northeast China. The annual average temperature ranges from 8°C to 11°C, with annual rainfall between 550 mm and 1000 mm. Winters are not severe, and summers are not scorching. The coldest month is January at -24°C, and the hottest month is August at 35°C. The mild climate keeps many people rooted in their coastal homes, even if they venture out to seek livelihoods, they return to this land by the sea when the leaves fall.
