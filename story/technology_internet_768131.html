Inspection of Microsoft Research Asia's Frontier Technology: real-time speech translation, Kinect sign language translation

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 18:21:15

At the 15th anniversary celebration of Microsoft Research Asia, Director of Microsoft Research Asia Xiao-Wen Hong told the story of the institute's past fifteen years through a nine-grid digital image. As Microsoft's largest basic research institution outside of the United States, Microsoft Research Asia promotes technology transfer through some forward-looking fundamental research and products, integrating into Microsoft products and supporting the company's strategy.

At yesterday's event site, we personally experienced some forward-looking technologies and products, including big data and machine learning, cloud computing and mobile technology, natural user interfaces, and technology-driven product innovation. Although they have not yet been converted into commercial technologies, from them we can glimpse the future landscape of technological life.

Real-time Speech Translation System

The event showcased Microsoft's real-time speech translation system. The system can translate what users say into another language and play it back through voice, while preserving the speaker's accent, tone, and timbre to restore the speaker's voice as much as possible. Currently, this method can achieve mutual translation between all 26 languages supported by the Microsoft Voice Platform.

Although there were some translation errors during the live demonstration, it basically achieved the purpose of simple communication. We learned that using this speech translation software, users need about an hour of training to establish a model for their own voice and integrate it with Microsoft's standard text-to-speech model, which is used for translation into the target language.

Kinect-Based Sign Language Translation System: Free Dialogue with Hearing-Impaired Individuals

Xiao-Wen Hong highlighted the "Kinect-Based Sign Language Translation System" at the scene. This system uses Kinect motion sensing devices to capture color and depth image information, recognizing and translating sign language. It can simultaneously recognize and translate both Chinese and English sign languages, providing voice feedback.

In addition, the system can translate textual information back into sign language, thus enabling normal communication with hearing-impaired individuals.

Inferring Air Quality Using Big Data

Modern urban populations are increasingly concerned about their living environment, but especially in large cities, air quality varies greatly across different locations. Currently, people can only rely on limited monitoring stations scattered across the city layout to make judgments, making it almost impossible to know the air quality around them at any time.

In the exhibition area, we saw a system that can infer the air quality at any location in the city in real time. It uses data mining and machine learning technologies to fully utilize big data, establishing an implicit mapping between monitoring information and corresponding results, thus inferring air quality data for any location in real time.

According to the staff, the data sources for this system include weather conditions, traffic volume, personnel movement trends, road network structures, and population concentration points, among others.

Developers also created a Windows Phone app for this system. In addition to displaying the air quality index of the current location, users can freely select points on the mobile map and present them visually.

Power Map: Bringing Excel Data to Life

In Excel, users can use the 2D chart function to convert data into visual forms such as pie charts and bar charts, facilitating PowerPoint presentations. In the exhibition area, we saw a tool that converts Excel data into 3D visualizations.

Using the example of the medal information statistics table from the 2008 Olympics in China, the number of medals and types of medals from different provinces can be mapped onto a map using a 3D bar chart.

Xiao-Wen Hong stated that this technology has entered the beta stage, and it is believed that it will soon be integrated into Excel as a 3D data visualization tool.
