Tip rhinoplasty

The nose tip, as commonly referred to in everyday language, is medically termed as the nasal tip. The cosmetic surgery of the external nose is collectively known as rhinoplasty. For individuals with a low nose bridge and an unsatisfactory nasal tip shape, the postoperative sensation from a simple nose augmentation often fails to reach perfection. In such cases, performing nasal tip beautification surgery can be likened to the final "dotting of the eyes" in a painting.

The attractiveness of the nasal tip is determined by the supporting framework structure, with the main contributors being the medial and lateral crura and the dome of the alar cartilage; followed by the upper lateral cartilage and the septal cartilage. An ideal nasal tip possesses appropriate projection, angles, and some refined points and surfaces.

Often during rhinoplasty, there's consideration given to simultaneously augmenting both the bridge and the tip of the nose. However, these days, there’s also a trend towards just correcting the nasal tip through tip rhinoplasty. Tip rhinoplasty constitutes a significant portion of overall rhinoplasty procedures, and its methods vary just as much as the diversity in nasal tip shapes.

The most desired nasal tip among Chinese women is one that is upright and slightly upturned. Conversely, the nasal tips of most East Asians tend to be short and composed of more soft tissue. In such cases, for a more upright and aesthetically pleasing nasal tip, methods such as suturing between the alar cartilages, cartilage grafting, or columella lift surgery can be considered.

What is nasal tip rhinoplasty?

When there is a certain height to the nasal bridge or when it has been augmented via implant insertion, achieving a corresponding height and firmness in the nasal tip results in an ideal nose shape. To reflect this, the procedure includes the insertion of a columella support. For enhanced nasal tip projection, intercartilaginous sutures can be performed, along with cartilage grafting or the insertion of artificial dermis on the sutured cartilage to increase the nose's projection and height. To ensure proper structural support and maintenance, the process of inserting the columella is crucial.

Columella insertion plays a role in adjusting the angle formed between the lips and the nasal tip, creating a more ideal and mature appearance. This maximizes the effects of rhinoplasty. Of course, not everyone requires columella insertion. It becomes an important step when existing rhinoplasty is insufficient or when only nasal tip correction is requested.

Who needs nasal tip rhinoplasty?

This applies to those with round, flat, overly high, cleft, or hidden cleft nasal tips, as well as eagle-hooked noses, which are mostly congenital deformities.

Methods of nasal tip rhinoplasty:

1. Correction of Low Nasal Tip

A. Cartilage Graft Method: Make an incision along the nasal margin inside the nostrils or within the cartilage to expose the alar cartilage and the upper lateral cartilage. Depending on the situation, remove part of the head side of the alar cartilage or the tail side of the upper lateral cartilage. The excised cartilage is then reshaped and sutured onto the top of the dome to elevate the nasal tip. Alternatively, auricular cartilage can be harvested for transplantation.

B. Bilateral Alar Cartilage Medial Crural Approximation Method: Make bilateral incisions on the sides of the columella to expose the medial crura and the dome of the alar cartilage. Remove the soft tissue between the two medial crura, then perform mattress sutures at the upper and lower ends to bring the two medial crura closer together. This method can elevate the nasal tip by 2-3mm.

C. Wedge Resection of the Nasal Wing Base: If the nasal tip shape is good but slightly low, a full-thickness wedge-shaped piece of tissue can be removed from the base of both nasal wings, causing the nostrils to converge inward and the dome to protrude upward, thereby elevating the nasal tip.

2. Correction of Super High Nasal Tip

An appropriately projected nasal tip refers to a smooth transition from the nasal dorsum to the tip. A significantly arched elevation is called a super high nasal tip. After surgical correction, the results are excellent. Perform an intercartilaginous incision, dissecting the dome of the alar cartilage in a retrograde manner towards the tail side. Appropriately remove a section of the most prominent part of the dome, then suture the ends together. Make shallow incisions on either side of the cartilage to weaken it, resulting in a sharp but not pointed nasal tip. The excised portion can also be located at the base of the medial crura of the alar cartilage, though its effect is not as good as removing from the dome.

3. Correction of Blunt Flat Nasal Tip

From a frontal view, the aesthetic standard for a beautiful nasal tip is conical. A blunt flat nasal tip is clearly unattractive. Some people seeking cosmetic surgery say: "Please change my 'pig nose'." This statement indicates that this type of nose has reached a point where surgery is necessary. The surgery involves making an incision along the nasal wing margin inside the nostrils to fully expose the domes of both alar cartilages. Then make 3-6 incisions on their surface without cutting through. Remove the soft tissue between the medial crura of the alar cartilage and perform mattress sutures to bring the two medial crura closer together. If the nasal tip is low, cartilage grafts can be used to elevate it.

4. Correction of Thick Nasal Tip

People commonly refer to this type of nose as a "garlic nose." The reason is due to thicker skin, more subcutaneous tissue, and thickened cartilage framework with a tendency to bulge outward. Corrective surgery for thick nasal tips is known as nasal reduction surgery, which is the most challenging type of surgery for this condition. The surgery involves making marginal incisions or intercartilaginous incisions. Fully expose and free the alar cartilage and the upper lateral cartilage, uniformly removing fibro-fatty tissue from all areas. Remove 1/2 to 2/3 of the head side of the alar cartilage, rotating the lateral crura medially and cephalad, at which point the alar retraction becomes visible. Make intermittent incisions on the dome of the alar cartilage without cutting through. Strengthen the nasal opening or prevent it from becoming longitudinal by performing a wedge resection at the base of the alar if necessary. If the nasal tip lacks height, the excised cartilage can be placed under the dome to elevate the nasal tip.

Nasal tip beautification surgery is complex, subtle, and intricate, requiring cosmetic surgeons to have a deeper understanding of nasal aesthetics.

Characteristics after nasal tip rhinoplasty:

1. A nose with a raised and slightly upturned tip appears more confident and beautiful than one with a drooping and low tip.

2. Nasal tip surgery is performed to raise and turn up the tip, correct a short and thick nose.

Contraindications for nasal tip rhinoplasty:

1. Women should avoid their menstrual period.

2. Ensure good health before surgery, free of infectious diseases or other bodily inflammations.

3. Patients with hypertension and diabetes should fully inform the doctor of their conditions during the initial consultation so that the attending physician can confirm the surgical plan.
