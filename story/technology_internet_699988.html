Fruit prices rose year-on-year by 10% to 30%.

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 17:20:05

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, October 28th (reporter Xiaoling Wang) According to the Xinhua News Agency's national agricultural by-products and production materials price monitoring system, compared with the previous day, on October 28th, the prices of pork and eggs continued to decline; the price of pure milk in plastic bags increased; the prices of edible oil, vegetables, fruits, aquatic products fluctuated slightly; the finished grain prices remained stable. The price of pure milk in plastic bags rose slightly by 0.6%. Since September, the monitored national average price of pure milk in plastic bags has risen slightly, and on October 28th, it was 1.7% higher than on September 1st. The prices of both pork tenderloin and pork belly dropped by 0.5%; the prices of beef tendon and beef brisket remained unchanged, while the prices of boneless fresh mutton and bone-in fresh mutton increased by 0.1% and 0.3%, respectively. The prices of oranges and Fuji apples fell by 0.4% and 0.2%, respectively, while the prices of tangerines and bananas rose by 1.6% and 0.3%, respectively. The prices of duck pears and snow pear remained unchanged. Since the beginning of this year, the national average prices of most monitored fruits have been at relatively high levels. After the price of bananas hit the bottom in July, it also continued to rebound. Currently, the prices of duck pears, Fuji apples, bananas, oranges, tangerines, and snow pears are all higher than the same period last year, with year-on-year increases of 23.1%, 9.9%, 33.8%, 10.6%, 16.5%, and 19.1%, respectively.
