How to develop an online software market?

In fact, online software has been around for a long time; the common professional term is SAAS. However, China's online software market has never really developed, while there are some relatively mature developments abroad. Since last year when I first came into contact with online software, I have been paying attention to this field. Although I haven't made much progress, I do have some insights. I am currently operating an online financial management software called CaiKe Online: www.coko365.com. This can be considered one of the early explorers and users of China's online software market.

I have always been at the forefront of China's online software market, but one question has consistently puzzled me: how can we develop China's online software market? Online software has certain advantages and benefits, but in terms of market development, promotion, and operation, it differs from standalone software and websites. Therefore, we need to gradually explore and innovate. There are definitely many friends who develop online software like me, and they are also constantly thinking about how to develop the online software market. In response to such questions, I roughly thought of the following points:

First: Focus on making the product better

The core of the online software market lies in the product. Developing online software is more complex than developing a website, but it is different from the development of regular software, as it involves issues such as upgrades. Therefore, the essence of online software lies in the product. Without a good product, even if you promote it heavily, the feedback in the market will not be significant, and it may even harm the market. Thus, my technical partner and I have been striving for breakthroughs and innovations in technology, continuously improving the online software products of CaiKe Online (www.coko365.com), making them more in line with member needs, more suitable for member requirements, and more competitive in the market.

Second: Need more competitors to quickly stir up the market

The internet world requires a large number of competitors to cultivate the market. The concept of online software needs to be promoted and instilled in the current market stage. As there is no dominant market leader yet, this task of promoting and instilling the concept falls on the shoulders of various online software websites. Therefore, the more competitors there are, the easier it is to expand the online software market. With this consideration, CaiKe Online (www.coko365.com) once launched a promotional advertisement, which roughly meant that it would sell the source code of its CaiKe Online accounting software at a low price. If a company were to develop it themselves, it would cost over 100,000 yuan and still not achieve the functionality and requirements of CaiKe Online's accounting software, as the development cost of online software is higher than that of website development. However, in order to quickly stir up the online finance and online accounting market, CaiKe Online once sold the source code of its accounting software and website at a low price.

Third: Collaborate with users of standalone software versions

Currently, the online OFFICE software abroad is very successful, so China's online software should also learn from its promotional and marketing methods. First, targeted promotions and advertisements should be directed towards users of standalone software versions. For example, the CaiKe Online accounting software developed by CaiKe Online (www.coko365.com) could target users and potential customers of similar standalone accounting and financial management software, such as CaiZhi Software. To my knowledge, many online software programs in the current market lack focus in their promotions, including CaiKe Online itself, whose promotions are rather blind and lack targeting, leading to slower development.

I believe that online software will definitely be a future trend. I have been closely monitoring Google's activities, as Google has been dedicated to the development and application of online software products and services. I believe that in the near future in China, a batch of online software websites will definitely emerge, creating another blue ocean in the internet industry.
