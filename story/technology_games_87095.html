P4P technology increases online game downloading speed by 60% - Age1983

by hexiaoyao on 2008-08-25 01:45:48

Introduction: While web-based games are on the rise, 3D online games remain the favorite of hardcore online gamers. The stunning game scenes and character designs enhance user experience but also bring operational challenges — how to quickly distribute clients and upgrade files that can be as large as hundreds of megabytes? Self-built download stations, rented CDNs, and bundled P2P are the three common strategies used by game product managers. However, compared to the newer P4P technology, they all seem somewhat inferior — P4P can increase user download speeds by 60% and the proportion of intra-city data exchanges has improved nearly tenfold.

As online games become more visually stunning, their appeal to internet users grows, but so does the barrier to entry. On one hand, the hardware requirements for gaming have increased; without a configuration of at least 256MB of video memory, a P4 2.0GHz CPU, and 1GB DDR2 RAM, users might hesitate to join online games, fearing ridicule from peers due to low-end equipment. On the other hand, network bandwidth requirements have also risen. Client software and upgrade files often reach hundreds of megabytes in size. Even with broadband access, the download nodes of game operators must provide good acceleration to ensure smooth performance.

The shortcomings of the three common strategies for game distribution

Self-built download stations, rented CDNs, and bundled P2P are the usual strategies employed by online game operators. Self-built download stations still use traditional HTTP protocol download methods, which place high demands on server bandwidth and concurrent threads but have obvious disadvantages — slow speed when there are too many users, and poor interconnectivity. Building a CDN requires high standards for the number of nodes, distribution technology, and traffic management technology provided by the CDN service provider, along with relatively high backbone network bandwidth costs. In P2P mode, data often cannot be verified, leading to wasted downloads for users. HASH verification was introduced, but it creates operational barriers for ordinary users.

In comparison, P2P remains one of the most cost-effective distribution methods for online game operators. Is there a way to "use its strengths and tolerate its weaknesses"? This is where P4P comes in. P2P (Peer-to-Peer) overly emphasizes "equality," with completely unordered exchanges between each node. A user in Beijing could exchange file fragments with a user in Guangzhou or even with someone far away in the United States. Clearly, unordered exchanges lead to unnecessary cross-regional or even international "data travel," not only consuming valuable domestic and international bandwidth resources at great cost but also increasing the likelihood of data packet errors during transmission.

Let P2P play locally: Enter P4P

If all users happen to be in the same region, then the cost of localized exchanges would significantly decrease, while quality and speed could greatly improve. This is the simple principle behind P4P — let P2P also play "locally." P4P stands for "Proactive Network Provider Participation for P2P (Telecom providers actively participating in P2P networks)." Unlike P2P's random selection of peers, the P4P protocol coordinates network topology data, effectively selecting nodes to improve network routing efficiency. For example, a user in Beijing would prioritize exchanging file fragments with other users in Beijing before expanding to more distant regions, and only resort to international exchanges if absolutely necessary. Of course, the operation mechanism of P4P goes far beyond the concept of "local exchanges." It comprehensively evaluates the user's upload and download bandwidth to make the most effective choices, maximizing overall exchange efficiency.

It is worth noting that the pioneer of P4P is a Chinese man working at Yale University, Dr. Xie Haiyong. Dr. Xie is the chief researcher of the American Distributed Computing Industry Alliance (DCIA) and the P4P Working Group. He proposed and completed the P4P theory and system design. Since late February this year, Dr. Xie and other researchers have conducted large-scale field tests on the P4P system design.

Impressive commercial performance: 60% faster downloads

This invention led by Chinese researchers has shown excellent performance in commercial testing. According to Verizon's feedback, using P4P technology, P2P users' average download speed increased by 60%, with fiber-to-the-home users experiencing an increase of 205%~665%. Additionally, internal data transmission distances within the operator were reduced by 84%. 58% of the data came from the same city, a nearly tenfold improvement over the traditional P2P ratio of 6.3%.

In China, some telecom value-added service providers have already ventured into this field. For instance, Century互联, which leads in IDC and content acceleration, quietly launched P4P services recently. Century互联 owns the largest scale of IDC data centers in China’s internet and the best BGP resources for interconnection, showing outstanding performance in both scale and technology. They successfully provided content acceleration services for CCTV's global HD live broadcast of the 08 Spring Festival Gala and NBA Chinese official website video on-demand.

It is understood that Century互联's P4P network platform consists of distributed edge servers across the country and millions of user computers equipped with P4P acceleration engines. The server nodes serve as basic service resources, ensuring service quality during off-peak hours, while reasonably utilizing client-side resources accelerates peak-hour and hot-content scenarios. This also effectively addresses sudden traffic spikes causing server congestion and declining service quality. Currently, Century互联's content distribution service based on the P4P network can be applied in areas such as software downloads, video downloads, and streaming media on-demand/live broadcasts.

Accelerating game distribution: Client size down to 1M

Through the P4P system, Century互联 provides download client services for several online game companies, including complete client downloads and upgrade downloads. The complete client download service solves the problem of online game version management entirely. By distributing an online download client of less than 1M, it ensures that users can always download the latest version of the online game client regardless of the time.

During testing, distributing online game clients and upgrade files via P4P resulted in download speeds that were 60%~200% faster than traditional methods, with better transmission quality compared to traditional P2P downloads.
