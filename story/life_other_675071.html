How to Prevent Mosquitoes -- How to Eliminate Mosquitoes -- Tricks to Deal with Mosquitoes! -- Life Tips

Do you dislike the smell of mosquito coils and prefer to use mosquito repellent or insecticide to deal with those pesky mosquitoes? The latest research shows that some mosquito repellents and insecticides can indeed keep mosquitoes away within half an hour to an hour after application. However, if you don't reapply the repellent or insecticide, your skin may actually become more attractive to mosquitoes at this time.

Researchers from the University of Florida point out that mosquitoes are actually quite picky eaters because they understand the importance of "balanced diet and nutrient absorption." Therefore, for mosquitoes, the preference for human flesh and blood can vary by up to a thousand times.

To understand how mosquitoes select their victims, researchers first attached silicone films to the arms of ten people for four hours. They then used electro-olfactogram devices to compare the number of bites on these ten patches by 150 mosquitoes.

The results showed that within eight hours, one person's patch was bitten a total of 8000 seconds (bite seconds). The least popular patch was only bitten 8 seconds (bite seconds).

Who is more likely to be bitten by mosquitoes? This requires more research. However, there are some small tricks that can help you successfully avoid mosquito bites:

1. Keep your home environment clean. If you like planting flowers or raising small animals, pay extra attention to cleanliness and hygiene.

2. If you plan to use mosquito coils or electric mosquito repellents at home, ensure good air circulation to avoid discomfort.

3. Try using different brands of mosquito repellents or insecticides regularly and choose the ones with the least allergic side effects but effective insect repellency. When traveling, you won't ruin your trip due to product side effects.

4. When using mosquito repellents or insecticides, it’s best to wear long sleeves and pants, avoid using perfume, and reapply the repellent or insecticide every so often.

5. If you experience side effects from mosquito coils, electric mosquito repellents, mosquito repellents, or insecticides, seek treatment from your family doctor or dermatologist as soon as possible.
