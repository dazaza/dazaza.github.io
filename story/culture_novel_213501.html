1. **Accelerating the Transformation of Thinking**

After my position adjustment in Year ××, I was appointed as a "Level × Execution Accountant" in the new post. I repeatedly reminded myself to quickly find my place and enter my role because the business hall does not allow idlers. After unloading some managerial responsibilities, I focused my energy on frontline business and product marketing, especially on the marketing of savings deposits and related new products, which I tightly grasped without any slack.

Entering Year ××, through carefully studying and understanding the spirit of a series of staff meetings, middle-level cadres meetings, and our bank’s first “Business Analysis Meeting,” the brand-new thinking of the new leadership team, characterized by unconventional and leapfrog development, not only stimulated the work enthusiasm of all personnel in the business hall but also broadened my own work perspective. The "survival" pressure turned into motivation. In the labor competition themed around survival and development, we continuously increased our efforts based on fully tapping all potential resources. In view of the actual situation, standing at the height of rapid development, I, along with the director of the business hall, objectively analyzed the gap between the increment share of deposits and other banks. Under the guidance of the Accounting Supervisor, we meticulously formulated work plans and schemes, truly following the work concept established by the General Branch Committee: "Great Development, Great Market, Great Marketing."

The business hall, as the main accounting branch, is the hub of all the bank's work and the center of its accounting calculations. To improve our bank's accounting management level and accounting calculation quality, we must start with the basic accounting work of the business hall. According to the requirements of the internal control system, through carefully analyzing different positions and types of businesses in the business hall, we redefined and detailed the job responsibilities, adjusted the rotation system, strictly regulated the operation process, and formulated a series of management regulations such as cash box cash management methods, cash box document management methods, securities business management methods, voucher organization binding methods, and accounting error assessment methods.

A dedicated person was assigned to monitor in real-time the cash in the cash box, documents, key accounting subjects, and accounts, ensuring that any issues are promptly discovered and resolved. At the same time, by formulating a secondary distribution method, the performance wages of the business hall personnel were linked to business volume, deposit task completion, calculation quality, service quality, labor discipline, etc., breaking the "small pot meal" and "small average" in distribution, promoting the completion of various task goals and improving service quality and calculation quality.

Additionally, I personally devoted myself to the wave of attracting deposits and increasing reserves. During work hours, I talked about deposits; after work, I ran around seeking deposits. Through my actions, I inspired and guided the front-counter clerks to enhance their awareness of deposits and indicators. Together with my colleagues, we summarized the experience: "Attract new customers with service, stabilize old customers with feelings." In Month ××, an elderly customer who deposited U.S. dollars in the business hall forgot his password. I visited him twice to handle the loss and cancellation procedures for him. The customer was deeply moved and transferred all kinds of deposits from other banks to the business hall.
