Studying in Canada: Accounting and Finance are Popular Majors

The cost of studying in Canada is relatively cheaper compared to major study-abroad countries such as the US and UK. However, Canada's strict education system ensures the high value of master's degrees and diplomas. There are many popular majors for studying in Canada, but the choice for international students still depends on the development prospects of the major.

Popular Majors Accounting and Finance are Preferred

With the Canadian master's study market gaining widespread attention, the number of applicants for popular majors such as accounting and finance is also increasing. Application Requirements: GMAT and GRE Scores Can Increase Application Success Rate

It is understood that Canadian master's courses are divided into two types: taught and research. Taught master's programs are credit-based; as long as you complete the required credits and finish your thesis, you can graduate smoothly. Research master's programs involve a large amount of topic research and project discussions, with extremely high language requirements, usually admitting local students and international students who graduated from Canadian undergraduate programs. It is relatively difficult for Chinese international students to apply for research master's programs.

Canadian master's programs start in September each year. The basic requirements for direct application include the following aspects: a four-year bachelor's degree from a domestic university; a GPA of 3.0 or above (average score of at least 70%); typically English scores are required to be IELTS 7.0 or TOEFL 90 or above (having GMAT or GRE scores can increase the success rate of applications). Additionally, certain majors like MBA and education require relevant work experience.

Study Expenses: Average of 12,000 Canadian Dollars per Year

The tuition fees for Canadian universities are generally 50% lower than those in the US, averaging around 12,000 Canadian dollars per year. Besides providing partial scholarships and teaching/research assistant opportunities to international students, Canada also offers unique Co-op courses (short for Cooperative Education, commonly known as "paid internship courses"), where students can earn corresponding remuneration during internships.
