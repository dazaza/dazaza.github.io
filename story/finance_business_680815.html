Home network marketing is gaining popularity, and consumers are trying new ways of purchasing.

by 2b027wowo on 2012-02-28 09:45:15

The furniture production and distribution enterprises are mostly small and medium-sized family businesses. Compared with other mature industries, the overall management level of the furniture industry is relatively low, with poor modern marketing management awareness, lacking systematic market competition strategies and management methods. Most enterprises adopt relatively single competitive tactics, reflecting a low-level competition.

It is understood that the traditional furniture retail model is mainly based on booth rental operations. Furniture producers do not directly contact consumers but sell products to dealers in wholesale form, making it difficult to control the market price of furniture. Dealers invest a lot in renting venues and decorating stores, considering their own interests, they are unwilling to minimize profits, which makes it hard for traditional home malls to carry out product promotions. Meanwhile, due to their own qualities and operating capabilities, dealers cannot provide the best after-sales service to consumers, leaving both consumers and manufacturers in an awkward situation. Facts have proven that this business model finds it difficult to protect consumer rights and meet market demands.

It is understood that in order to achieve optimal shopping prices, many consumers have begun to try new purchasing forms, such as online group buying and direct sales from manufacturers. It is evident that the traditional dealer-centered sales model is facing challenges, which has become an undeniable fact.

To make up for the shortcomings of traditional sales channels and also to avoid paying high rents to malls, some manufacturers have started to attempt chain operations, quickly drawing unanimous praise from the industry. Many enterprise leaders have expressed that online marketing operations will be the inevitable path for furniture marketing in the coming few years. This trend, apart from being influenced by intensified market competition and merchants' urgent need to break through, has a deeper reason: the consumption concepts and methods of consumers are changing.

A survey shows that currently, 81% of home merchants made profits from online sales last year, and 76% of merchants made profits from online sales the year before. Regarding these survey results, mall managers all express approval. A well-known brand furniture's Chengdu general distributor stated that as early as a few years ago, some strong enterprises began to pay attention to online marketing, but at that time, websites mainly played a role in promoting corporate visibility. Now the market is different; everyone is starting to focus on truly exploring the substantive value brought by online marketing, hoping to get better new channels with less investment.
