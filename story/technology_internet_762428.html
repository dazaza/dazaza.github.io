Agent reveals: how does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:26:43

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. Therefore, establishing a search marketing system has become one of 360's main tasks this year. Under the pressure of Baidu's vast nationwide channel network, how is 360, as a latecomer, entering the market?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search, uncovering the expansion status of 360's search marketing system. We learned that 360's rapidly growing market share made the agents smell an opportunity to make money, and Baidu's tightened agency rights over the years indirectly contributed to the agents switching sides. The number of 360's current agents and order volume have both grown significantly compared to the beginning of the year.

The specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by agents. So far, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and municipalities, totaling 27 agents, while four months ago, this number was 13. Moreover, many local agents have already started developing sub-agents. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular in local areas, "just like during Baidu's early development phase, many agents back then gained considerable benefits, so now with 360 entering the search market, they are all eager to secure the agency as soon as possible." An agent from Henan told Sina IT that they obtained the agency rights for 360 last month.

2. The development of 360 agents also benefits from the gap formed by Baidu reclaiming its agency rights. The aforementioned Henan agent stated, "After so many years of Baidu's development, many places have had their agency rights reclaimed, and branch companies or provincial chief agents have been established." 360 does not have branch offices in various locations; it still mainly relies on agents. Among these agents, some used to represent Baidu ads, some represented Sogou or Soso ads, and some even represented 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, and most agents have established independent companies specifically for representing 360 ads.

3. Low prices are an important advantage of 360 Search ads in local areas. A Wuhan-based agent believes that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: less competition, lower prices, and higher conversion rates. "Some keyword ad prices for 360 Search are less than one-fifth of Baidu's," he said. Riding on the reputation of being the "second-largest search engine" and benefiting from the market education provided by Baidu earlier, this agent claims promotion isn't too difficult. However, many clients are still observing, as 360 just entered the market, and they "also need to see if 360 is truly willing to do a good job with its search engine."

4. Over the past six months, the transaction volume of 360 Search ad orders has grown rapidly. In March, we reported that 360 Search completed about 1,000 orders in Beijing within three months. A salesperson from Tiandizai Online, a Beijing-based agent for 360, told us that now they can exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing, Tiandizai Online and Jutao Culture, the latter entered later and is still relatively small. Henan SanbaiLiu, which just secured the agency rights last month, has already completed more than 1,000 orders.

5. The average price per customer for completed transactions is around ten thousand yuan, which is not low. The minimum account opening fee for 360 Search ads is 4,600 yuan, including a 600 yuan service fee and 4,000 yuan pre-deposit for advertising. A Wuhan-based agent revealed that the current average transaction price per customer is approximately ten thousand yuan. For comparison, Baidu's average revenue per customer last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads have covered all industries except healthcare. 360 Search ads have now covered all industries except healthcare, and recently opened up previously restricted industries such as health supplements and adult products. It’s worth noting that 360 only accepts enterprise customers and does not accept individual customers, and each company can only advertise for one site (Baidu can accept individual customers). Many agents are developing offline customers in education and consulting.

But can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360's agent system has initially taken shape, its competition with Baidu is still at a very early stage. When talking to a Baidu agent, he frankly said, "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

From the feedback received from various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet intense. "360 Search is currently providing customers with a new market and new choices," said one agent, with many customers placing ads on both platforms.

However, this is definitely a temporary situation. 360's search volume is still small, requiring more subsequent investment, but the monetization of search ads will undoubtedly be its next most imaginative income growth point. When 360's customer base truly grows and prices rise, there may inevitably be another fierce battle between 360 and Baidu.
