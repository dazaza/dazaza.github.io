The engineer—Dr. Alan Hocknell, VP of Innovation and Advanced Design for Titleist MB 710 Irons at Callaway Golf: "Our new 'Edge' technology helps players close the face at impact, which transfers maximum power to the ball."

The new golf clubs include the Callaway Big Bertha Diablo fairway woods, where the Diablo Edge Fairway Wood builds on the foundation laid by the Big Bertha Diablo and develops it into an even more impressive model, adding between 10 and 12 yards to your shots. The Big Bertha Diablo is equipped with Callaway’s Edge Technology, which uses a unique weight placement within the stainless-steel clubhead to help golfers hit longer, more accurate shots whether off the tee or from the fairway.

The aforementioned "Edge" technology moves mass further to the perimeter, or edge, of the clubhead. This results in a higher MOI (Moment of Inertia) for increased stability through the hitting zone. The technology culminates in strategically Neutral (smaller size, neutral weight placement) or Draw (added mass toward the heel) options to match your shot-shape requirements, giving golfers the opportunity to select a club that will most benefit their individual swing types and promote their desired shot shape.

The Callaway Big Bertha Diablo Fairway Woods also incorporate VFT or Variable Face Thickness Technology, allowing for different levels of thickness across the clubface to ensure much higher ball speeds from a larger area of the face. The Big Bertha Diablo Fairway wood is available in 3, 5, 7, and 9 wood options with a draw bias and also in 13, 15, and 18-degree neutral options. The head has a slightly lower profile than some other woods, enabling it to really get under the ball and launch it. It's very lightweight but substantial enough to feel the force of the impact.
