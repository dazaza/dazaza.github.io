44.2 Million Euro Spanish Golden Boot Seeks a New Home: Besides Real Madrid and Barcelona, Also Open to Chinese Super League

At 11:00 PM Beijing time on November 25th, news from Spain reported that David Villa, the top scorer of the Spanish national team who almost transferred to Real Madrid or Barcelona this summer, is rumored to be considering another transfer. Valencia has reportedly set his price at 44.2 million euros, and for the first time, Villa has expressed willingness to consider leaving La Liga for other top leagues.

According to *Marca*, the financial crisis has hit many medium and small clubs in Spain, including Valencia. With debts amounting to 550 million euros, the club's operations are severely strained. Next summer, Valencia will likely have to sell its star players to fill the financial gap. Consequently, after failed negotiations with Real Madrid and Barcelona this summer, the departure of two key national players—Villa and David Silva—is once again on the agenda.

Valencia is about to celebrate its 90th anniversary, and President Miguel Ángel López stated that player sales are not currently under consideration, but everything will be decided next summer.

However, Villa’s attitude has changed. This summer, he firmly rejected an invitation from Chelsea, saying he didn’t want to leave Spain. But before the season has even reached the halfway mark, Villa has now indicated openness to any league abroad.

"Whether it’s in Europe, Africa, America, or even North Korea or Asia, I won't refuse to consider it," Villa said. "Of course, I can't go just anywhere, but I no longer directly reject any club because my time at Valencia is limited. My career is getting shorter, and enjoying football wherever I am becomes more important."

This suggests that if the conditions are right, Villa might even consider playing in the Chinese Super League (CSL).

Since being affected by debt issues, Valencia has made every effort to keep Villa in the team for the past two years. However, June of next year may be the final time he leaves. Last season, Villa scored 44 goals across all competitions for Valencia and the Spanish national team. So far this season, he has already scored 15 goals in both domestic and international matches, demonstrating his ever-improving goal-scoring ability.

Currently, Villa still leads the scoring charts in La Liga, but he remains humble. "It's only November, and there are still many rounds left in the league. The current top position doesn't necessarily mean the final result. What I hope for is that this trend continues." He also mentioned that scoring isn't his sole objective; helping Valencia or the Spanish national team win is what matters most to him.

Real Madrid? Barcelona? Villa doesn't want to be forced to choose between them right now. Regarding the upcoming El Clásico, he hopes for a draw, which would help Valencia improve its standings in La Liga.
