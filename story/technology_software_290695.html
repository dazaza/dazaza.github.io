Tourism: Signs of industry recovery are obvious, focus on individual stock trends, recommend 7 stocks＿Stock Knowledge

In a rapidly growing market, the dependence of tourism demand on the macroeconomic environment is very strong. When the economy grows, the disposable income of residents generally increases as well, and a considerable portion of this disposable income is spent on tourism expenses. This situation is particularly prominent in emerging economies. Overall, the growth rate of international tourist numbers significantly exceeds the GDP growth rate. Between 1975 and 2000, the global GDP growth rate was approximately 3.5%, while the growth rate of international tourist numbers was about 4.6%, making the growth rate of international tourist numbers 1.3 times that of GDP.

Currently, China's tourism industry is developing very quickly. In 2007, the total revenue of China's tourism industry reached 109.57 billion yuan, breaking through the 100 billion yuan mark for the first time, increasing by 22.6% compared to the previous year. Tourism consumption is becoming the most dynamic sector in China's consumption structure, driven by the following factors: (1) Upgrading of resident consumption structure in China; (2) Improvement of infrastructure making tourism activities more convenient and comfortable; (3) Strong government policy support for the development of the tourism industry.

China's tourism industry still has a large growth space. The National Tourism Administration believes that the medium-term (2006-2010) development speed of China's tourism industry is an annual growth of 10%. Based on past experience, the prediction data from the National Tourism Administration is generally conservative. Currently, the tourism industry has become a highlight in China's national economic life, with the proportion of total industry output in GDP reaching 3.8%, but the proportion of total world tourism industry output in global GDP is around 10%. Compared to the development level of the world tourism industry, China's tourism industry still has great potential for development. Moreover, the World Tourism Organization predicts that by 2015, China will become the world's top-ranked tourism destination country and also the world's fourth-largest source country.

Signs of recovery in the tourism industry are evident, focusing investment on individual stock trends. In the first half of 2009, China's tourism market showed a "total volume growth, two up one down" development trend. Tourism listed companies mainly focused on the domestic tourism consumption market enjoyed significant benefits from the recovery of industry prosperity. In the first half of 2009, the tourism sector achieved a total operating income of 6.835 billion yuan, increasing by 2.46% year-on-year. For the tourism sector, signs of moving out of the shadow of the second half of 2008 have already become very clear. Due to the characteristics of the tourism industry such as low entry barriers, fierce industry competition, and low social average profit margins, not all tourism enterprises can share in the "feast" of industry growth. Therefore, investment in the tourism sector should focus on individual stock trends, investing in companies that can share in the industry's growth. We believe the following stocks are worth investors' attention, and we believe these companies can share in the growth of the tourism industry. They are: Guilin Tourism, Jinjiang Shares, CYTS, Capital Tourism, Emei Mountain, Huangshan Tourism, Lijiang Tourism.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Furthermore, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you want to buy or sell the stock. Stock abbreviations usually consist of three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code consists of six digits. When placing an order, the stock code and abbreviation must match. Additionally, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks being bought or sold: the quantity of stocks ordered for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, whereas the quantity for selling stocks does not need to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
