Is this the last snow?

Facing the snowflakes falling profusely outside the window, I suddenly thought of these words and was startled by myself.

I like snow as much as I like cigarettes. The snow sways lightly and white in the air, slowly drifting down, sometimes spinning in front of your eyes, or rushing to your face, a bit cool, a bit drunk, perhaps with a bit of happy redness from being kissed. What a beautiful moment, so beautiful that it's a bit dazzling, a bit dazzling, a bit extravagant. I like this extravagance, I like the intoxication brought by this extravagance.

Once regarded snow as a lover, delicate and gentle, becoming tears when entering the eyes, full of softness when kissed on the lips, and melting into the scorching skin when held in the palm. Once fantasized about holding a snowflake under the dim street lamp and dancing to my heart's content, letting the white tenderness and the dim brilliance seep into every pore, even if it drives me crazy. Once wandered alone in a short forest in the snow, enjoying the boundless reverie to my heart's content.

When I wander in the short forest in the snow, I always remember an essay I once read. That article describes a man sitting in a wheelchair who every day watches the wind rise and leaves fall, the clouds roll and relax, the passers-by rush, and time passes by in a desolate garden. Through those words, you can also see a simple and kind mother leaning on the door and looking in the direction her son returns; a sick but strong-hearted man; a compassionate mother who cares for all beings; a calm and profound soul flying out of the wheelchair; and an old garden always covered with fallen leaves.

I wonder if it snowed in that garden tonight? If it snows, is this snowfall the last one in that garden? Perhaps, with the fall of the last snowflake, the garden will become eternal?

The year 2010 is coming to an end, tomorrow will be called 2011, a "new" year has already stood firmly in front of us. Will there be snow in 2011? Will the snowy sky be cold? I don't know, and I believe you don't know either. There are many things in this world that we don't know, since we don't know, then don't guess. Live quietly!
