In this life, are you willing to practice cultivation with me until the end?

When people were happily preparing for Christmas, the man was no exception. He deceived his boss by saying there was an emergency at home, but he rushed to another city overnight just to catch a glimpse of the young woman. Or in his heart, he thought his appearance would bring her a pleasant surprise. He even imagined that when he stood in front of her, perhaps she would run directly into his arms.

The man had fantasies like those of a young woman.

However, when they met again, their laughter of the past was gone. The man, facing this young woman, saw his gift trembling in his hands, his face stiffened by the cold wind, and he felt more nervous than ever before because from afar, he already read the indifference on her face.

The young woman took the gift indifferently and simply asked: "How did you get here?"

The man said: "I came to give you a gift."

The young woman said: "Oh, thank you. I'm going back to work now."

The man hurriedly asked: "Can we have dinner together tonight? It's Christmas Eve today."

The young woman said: "No, I have something tonight."

The man asked: "Can I still see you tonight?"

The young woman replied: "It depends. I'll call you between seven and eight o'clock."

The man wanted to say something more or confirm if what she said was true. But the young woman had already walked away, leaving only the man sighing and softly calling out "Wait a moment." The young woman either didn't hear it or pretended not to hear it, otherwise why didn't she turn back?

His mood was colder than expected, like the weather. The man was not prepared, and could only let the cold air invade his soul, as if he could hear the sound of freezing. He thought, after rushing here overnight last night, it started like this. If so, maybe this journey should be ended early.

Waiting is a kind of torment. Without relatives around, even the most prosperous city feels desolate and broken; an empty heart makes even the busiest crowd feel strange and cold.

At seven, eight, and half past eight, the young woman's phone never answered. The man confirmed again that he would end this journey tomorrow. However, just as he thought that, his phone rang. Seeing the familiar name flashing on the screen, how could one describe how the man grabbed the phone? Like a drowning person grabbing a weak straw in the final moment before death.

Almost running all the way to the young woman, the man couldn't bear her waiting too long in the wind, and was also afraid of her saying: "If you don't come within five minutes, I'll leave!"

The young woman was no longer cold as in the afternoon, or maybe the man thought she was cold in the afternoon, but she herself wasn't. She first said she hadn't eaten yet, so they went to a nearby small restaurant. While she ordered food, the man secretly watched her every move. Whenever she wanted to talk to him, the man always pretended not to hear and deliberately changed the subject.

People are very strange creatures. Even though they want to see each other, now sitting beside each other, they still look secretly; even though they want to talk, now when she starts speaking, he pretends not to hear; even though they like each other, now wanting to hold hands, he just quietly rubs his hands together in front of his chest.

The man silently watched the young woman eat, occasionally responding with a few quiet words. After the meal, they went to a KTV, which was where they sang for the first time. Now they were back again. The man thought, the starting point is here, maybe the endpoint is here too. This is what's called a cycle. Thinking about this, the man's mood relaxed a bit, so he picked a song at the beginning: "Who Does the Moon Represent?"

He didn't sing, just listened, because he was also asking, who does the moon represent? If it represents me, does this close-by young woman understand? Since she doesn't understand, then whatever heart it represents is fleeting...

The man picked another song: "Lying." Yes, lying! The man isn't stupid, but he has always let the young woman be smart in front of him. You say your phone is out of power, the man accepts it; you say you didn't hear the phone ring, the man accepts it; you say you're busy with work, the man accepts it; all these various big and small things are kind lies, and the man will accept them all.

By one o'clock in the morning, the man knew he really couldn't keep her any longer, so he silently sent her home. After getting out of the car, the man finally held onto the young woman's hand unwilling to let go. He had so much to say, but didn't know how to start. He could only say under the lamplight: "I just want us to be the same as before, simple friends. You can still treat me like we used to. If you have worries or anything to say, tell me. I will tell you everything that happens to me."

The young woman just said lightly: "We'll see."

The man's heart trembled. He remembered the young woman's old catchphrase: "Well..." The man always laughed and said to her: "You always give such ambiguous answers, making others feel like they're being scratched inside." Now "well..." has become "we'll see," what else can the man say?

The man said, "Let me hug you one more time." After saying it, he didn't care if the young woman agreed or not, and hugged her tightly. At that moment, the man's heart shouted: "Listen, my heart is frozen right now. Can you help melt it?"

In less than a second, the young woman struggled and said: "Okay, okay, it's fine." The man bitterly smiled in his heart and could only hold her little hand, sending her to the building downstairs. How could he bear to let go? But all the things he wanted to say were stuck in his chest. All he could do was to hardy hold her hand, hoping this moment would last forever. The man couldn't resist wanting to embrace her again, but the young woman shook him off and left.

The man shouted to the instantly disappearing back: "Will you play with me tomorrow?"

There was nothing but the cold air.

Just a moment ago, the man wanted eternity; now, the man just wanted to escape quickly. Who could understand his steps? After coming such a long way, except for repeated buckets of cold water poured over him, what else did he receive?

Under the dim light, a lonely figure, a sobbing heart...

Another night almost without sleep, in the morning, the man bought the train ticket as quickly as possible. The originally planned four-day trip, which he had envisioned so beautifully, now had to be hastily ended in less than a day and a half. He dialed her phone again, but no one answered, so he simply wrote a message: "I'm rushing to buy a train ticket. Can you come to see me off?"

No response.

After eating lunch simply, the man came to the station alone. Coincidentally, the place where he was taking the bus this time was exactly where he gave the young woman the gift yesterday. He remembered the young woman saying: "I never wear scarves." The man's heart hurt. He wanted to say, do you know? To buy this scarf, I walked through the entire city square, carefully selecting, a kind of meticulousness I've never had before. I can't imagine how your small body withstands this cold wind. I want to embrace you, using my not-so-robust chest to shield you from the wind and rain. Are you willing? If not, then let this scarf tightly surround you, shielding you from a bit of the cold.

But could the man say this?

In half an hour, he could escape, but fate still brought him to this place. The man dialed the young woman's phone again. Her sleepy voice came: "Hello."

The man choked up but acted as if nothing happened and said: "Not up yet? I'm about to board."

The young woman was surprised: "What? So fast?"

The man bitterly smiled and said: "What else can I do? You won't play with me, and I don't know anyone in this city except you."

The young woman said: "Oh, I didn't say I wouldn't play with you. I went to bed late and just saw your call."

The man immediately said: "Will you play with me now?"

The young woman: "Didn't you already buy the ticket?"

The man: "No problem, I'll ask if it can be changed."

The young woman: "Oh, okay, if it can be changed, I'll play with you. If not..."

The man: "It can definitely be changed."

The man knew clearly that even if it couldn't be changed, he wouldn't care anyway. He wanted to see her, and nothing could stop him, anything, nothing...

When they saw each other again, the man was nervous, nervous. The torture of this day and night finally brought a ray of bright hope. The man gently asked: "Are you still mad about last night?"

The young woman said: "Mad about what? I'm not mad, haha."

The man loved her bell-like laughter, which could wash away all worldly troubles. The man's heart warmed up again. He suggested they go shopping on Zhongshan Road. This was the itinerary he had planned before coming this time. He wanted to pick out clothes for the young woman because winter was coming, and she would return home for the New Year, where it was very cold unlike here. He couldn't let her freeze.

Sitting on the unique bus of the city, the man always hoped this ride had no terminal station; or as people say, the next station - happiness.

When they arrived at Zhongshan Road, it started drizzling, and it got colder.

Shopping with the young woman was a pleasure. The man wished he could buy the whole street for her; but it was also a torture. The man always enthusiastically recommended items to her, asking: "How about this?" The young woman no longer gave ambiguous answers but simply and straightforwardly said: "No."

One piece after another, they walked through the entire mall. The young woman only tried on two pieces of clothing briefly, and everything else was "No" or "Not good." The street seemed short, and they had finished half of it in the blink of an eye. They came to a leisurewear store, and the man immediately liked the white down jacket. Excitedly, he asked: "This one, how about this one?"

The young woman also thought it was good. When she put it on, the man saw her in the mirror like a little swan, pure white, beautiful and charming. At that moment, the man decided this was the one. The young woman was a kind and beautiful girl who didn't want to spend the man's money. So she pulled the man out of the store and continued walking forward. The man said, "Let's go buy a pendant, as a birthday gift for me."

The young woman said: "Good, good."

What a lovable young woman! She didn't want the man to buy her a simple piece of clothing, but was willing to buy him a delicate pendant.

When the man heard this answer, he wanted to hug her tightly and not let go, but he didn't dare. He could only tightly hold her little hand and silently weave through the crowded sea of people.

This time the young woman was especially thoughtful, not missing any store. The man could see that although she didn't have much money, she was willing to spend all she had to buy a pendant that the man would like. However, how could the man allow that? He looked at one shop after another and quickly pulled her away.

After finishing the remaining half of the street, the man said: "Let's go back and buy the clothes we saw earlier. You can buy me a pendant online next time."

The young woman understood the man's meaning and said "Mm."

They returned to the leisurewear store.

The man said, "Come, try it on again."

The young woman said: "Huh? Why?"

The man said, "Anything, the more you decide at the last moment, the more careful you need to be, look repeatedly."

The young woman said: "Oh." Squinting her cute eyes.

When she put on the down jacket again, a beautiful little swan reflected in the mirror. The young woman asked: "How's this?"

The man smiled and said: "Someone claims to be 159 cm tall, but actually a size 155 fits perfectly, this size 165 seems a bit big."

The young woman's face suddenly turned red like two highland roses: "Ah, I am indeed 159 cm."

The man chuckled: "Yes, yes, 159, 159, okay, then this one works. Winter requires wearing more clothes underneath, so it's fine."

The man said: "Two pieces here will get a discount. Pick one for me, and we'll make them a couple set."

The young woman said: "Good, good, then I'll give it to you, you choose."

The man said: "Mm."

At this moment, you would notice a strange yet normal phenomenon. The man always carefully examined and compared the things he was giving to the young woman, but for himself, he only casually browsed some styles, tried on two pieces, and then said, "That's enough, nothing suits me." The young woman said, "Ah, that's not okay, then let's go, I don't want mine either."

The man's heart was once again moved by the young woman in front of him. She was still thinking of him. So the man picked another one and said, "This one."

When they left the store, the young woman was already wearing the new clothes. Under the dim sky, the young woman shone brightly in white, her face filled with joy. And the man's heart surged with happiness. For him, seeing her smile was the greatest enjoyment. He just wanted to make her happy and watch her enjoy that happiness. That was enough for the man.

The young woman kept insisting on buying the man something to eat for the road. They circled Walmart several times. The man picked out two pairs of fluffy slippers for the young woman, saying: "One for you, one for your sister. This way, you'll stay warm and comfortable at home in the winter."

As he spoke, the man was secretly laughing in his heart. He remembered last time when his friend came to visit and said: "Your slippers are so worn out, the soles are broken in the middle. You should change them. Too stingy!" The man said, "So what? I only wear them occasionally at home. Who sees the broken sole? It's called heaven knows earth knows, you don't know I know."

The young woman nodded happily. She repeatedly asked the man what he wanted to eat, and the man said he didn't need anything for the road. The young woman said, "You'll get hungry on the road." The man said, "I won't, I'll fall asleep on the road at night." The young woman said, "No, definitely not." The man stopped arguing and just smiled.

When the young woman reached the chocolates, the man said, "I'll buy you chocolates, pick two of each type, double the luck." The young woman squinted her eyes and smiled happily. The young woman said, "I'll pick for you too." The man smiled and said, "Good, I'll take one of each type." The young woman giggled.

The man also accompanied the young woman in picking out two bowls and two spoons, both very exquisite. The young woman said, "Wow, so cute. In the future, I want my whole family to use this kind of tableware."

The man smiled and said, "In the future, we'll pick them together, okay?"

The young woman said: "Good, good!"

Wonderful, wonderful happiness! Everything in the future could become reality, how beautiful would that be? A picture formed in the man's mind. A small table, under dim lighting, the two of them using exquisite and cute tableware, serving each other the most delicious dishes...

When it came to paying, the man only let the young woman buy the pack of chocolates and jelly beans, while he pushed half a cart of items. The cashier curiously asked them: "Aren't the ones behind you together?"

The man said: "We'll pay separately, hehe."

The young woman blushed coyly, and the man smiled foolishly.

However, happiness is always short-lived, which is why people call it happiness.

The two came to the station. The man always held the young woman's hands, not wanting to let go, repeatedly advising her: "Eat more, grow to 80 pounds, take care of yourself, wear more clothes..." The young woman also repeatedly asked: "What if it gets cold on the road? What if you get hungry...?"

Suddenly, the young woman asked: "What's so good about me that you like me?"

The man smiled, holding the young woman's hands against his face, and softly said: "I don't know what's so good about you, I haven't thought about it carefully. I just fell in love, regardless of your good or bad points. I just want to share the happiness I bring you, I just want to see you happy every day. When I see you happy, I forget everything else, that's enough, I'm satisfied. As for whether you're good or bad, it doesn't matter to me. Whether you're a murderer or however bad, I don't care..."

For the man, indulging her was a talent, without reason!

The young woman was silent, two clear tears rolled out of her eyes, sliding down her cheeks. She quickly wiped the one on the right, and the man quickly wiped the one on the left, gently asking: "What's wrong? Did I say something wrong?"

The young woman cried and said: "No, I'm not as good as you said."

The man's heart warmed up, and he laughed: "Did I say you were good? Haha."

The young woman paused, yes, he didn't say she was good, but it was far warmer and more touching than if he had...

The man said: "You, you're such a little girl."

The young woman said: "What? I'm not a little girl, I'm a woman."

The man chuckled: "Yes, yes, a young woman."

The young woman wiped away her tears and said: "Hehe, young woman then, young woman. And you? Big man."

The man said: "Me? Old man."

The young woman: "Hmmph, pfft..."

The man slowly said: "You should grow up quickly, grow up quickly, so that we can marry you."

The young woman: "I'm already twenty according to the Chinese age system, okay?"

The man said: "Good, but still grow up quickly, understand..."

The young woman understood what the man meant and nodded gently.

The man said: "I don't want to leave, I won't leave today, let's stay and play some more, okay?"

The young woman said: "No, I have to work tomorrow."

The man didn't say anything. He was crystal clear in his heart. The man gently kissed the young woman's cheek and said: "Kiss me back." He tilted his face, and the young woman said no, then lightly kissed the man's cheek and said, "Okay, that's enough." The man just chuckled foolishly.

The departing bus arrived on time. Watching the people boarding one by one, the man tightly embraced the young woman from behind, resting his head on her neck. Seeing the amethyst hanging there shining, tears flowed from the man's eyes. He quietly wiped them on her clothes. He just wanted to hold her, to keep holding her...

Unwilling to let go, the man wanted to send the young woman to a taxi before returning to board the bus. The young woman refused, so the man stood there watching her figure walk away, thinking in his heart: "Turn around, look at me once. I know if you love me, you will!"

The young woman walked a few steps, then turned around slowly to look back. The man stood dumbfounded, watching the young woman leave the station and disappear around the corner. With a heavy heart, the man boarded the bus back home.

Happiness was so short-lived, it disappeared in a flash...
