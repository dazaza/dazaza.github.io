Wenzhou Oujiang College pioneers Taobao training class

by 304885068 on 2009-03-27 09:06:27

Oujiang College of Wenzhou University officially opened a Taobao.com entrepreneurship training class, with 30 students becoming the first batch of trainees for this class. Taobao University also formally established a training program with Oujiang College to specifically teach these students how to start businesses online.

Cultivating students to master the skills required for online entrepreneurship

According to Bao Changxin, the teacher in charge of the entrepreneurship park at Oujiang College, they have been striving to create conditions for student entrepreneurship. Relatively speaking, starting a business on Taobao is easier to operate, so the idea of opening a Taobao class came about.

Currently, the college has specially set up a practical training base in the entrepreneurship park and invited staff from Taobao University to provide guidance on campus. The school's dedicated guidance teachers will also go out to learn. Courses such as "Opening Online Stores and Daily Management," "Store Positioning and Goods Procurement," and "Online Store Promotion and Sales Techniques" will all become part of the curriculum for the Taobao entrepreneurship training class. Additionally, the school has contacted external enterprises to serve as supply points for students who are temporarily unable to find sources of goods, providing convenience for them.

According to the introduction, the first batch of 30 students come from different majors and mostly have some experience in online sales. Based on each student's professional strengths, they are divided into eight departments: marketing, administration, technology, sales, sourcing, finance, logistics, and projects. These students will also undergo rotational training to ultimately master the skills required for online entrepreneurship.

Under the premise of employment difficulties, more and more students are inclined towards independent entrepreneurship. However, entrepreneurship is not such an easy matter.

"The advantage of Taobao is that it has low costs, simple operations, and relatively flexible timing, making it suitable for college students." Bao Changxin said that among the students he has interacted with, over 80% have had experiences with online shopping, and at least one-third are currently engaged in or planning to start Taobao businesses.

"Attempting online entrepreneurship first also lays the foundation for future employment and entrepreneurship." Xie Anjun, the director of the Information Department at Oujiang College, analyzed the current situation. Many students come from families that run their own businesses and have procurement channels, and their family businesses themselves need online sales channels. By leveraging these relationships, students opening online stores hold more significance. With the rapid development of the internet today, the development of online stores is undoubtedly a major trend. Currently, there are 19,514 online stores opened in Wenzhou on Taobao.

Low cost, simple operation, suitable for college students

Zou Wei is a third-year student at Oujiang College of Wenzhou University. Her Taobao store has been open for seven months now, and her credit rating has risen to two diamonds. She sells shoes.

Calling Zou Wei is a long waiting process because her phone is always busy. "They're all buyers calling me," Zou Wei said. Initially, she opened a Taobao store because she found her second-year summer vacation a bit boring. "I wanted to do something to pass the time and earn some pocket money along the way." After considering various possibilities, Zou Wei decided to sell shoes because her home was near Wenzhou Shoe City.

At the start of her entrepreneurship, Zou Wei purchased nearly 2000 yuan worth of goods and placed them at home, taking and processing photos herself, working hard and under pressure. "I was afraid the inventory wouldn't sell, and every item unsold was money." Gradually, her business improved, and Zou Wei found a new path: after two months of contact and coordination, she established connections with some shops. She could first take pictures of shoe styles and list them, then only purchase the goods from the shoe city when customers placed orders. This way, she achieved zero inventory.

Now, Zou Wei can handle thirty to forty orders per day, earning a profit of over 5000 yuan per month.

Zou Wei - A third-year student earning 5000 yuan per month

Smart students take over the campus courier business

Gao Peng has been graduated for more than half a year, but he is still active on campus. Because he saw frequent Taobao transactions within the campus, Gao Peng directly took over the courier business within the campus, earning money from both Taobao buyers and sellers.

"During my time at school, I helped in the school's entrepreneurship department and saw many entrepreneurial students doing Taobao businesses, with a lot of packages coming and going." Thus, the smart Gao Peng started the courier business and has been doing it for over a year.

Gao Peng estimated that he receives around sixty to seventy orders daily from university Taobao sellers, mostly cosmetics, clothes, jewelry, etc. The items are generally stored in dormitories, with most operating alone and some collaborating with two classmates.

For the future, Gao Peng plans to continue doing this business. "I know that the courier business will only increase, and Taobao is a big trend."

Yiwu Industrial and Commercial College: Over a thousand students open online stores, one diamond equals two credits

Yiwu Industrial and Commercial Vocational and Technical College—this school, which has not yet upgraded to undergraduate level, with only third-year students being the highest grade—60% of the students can manage their living expenses independently, and the total annual income from student work and entrepreneurship exceeds 25 million yuan.

Jia Shaohua prepared a free "entrepreneurship park" for students just starting their online stores—each department was equipped with a classroom for internet access and a warehouse for storing goods, while teachers were responsible for teaching students how to use Photoshop (PS) to edit product photos, negotiate with customers, and find appropriate sources of goods. Students of the entrepreneurship academy can replace academic credits with their Taobao levels, with one diamond equivalent to two credits.

Jia Shaohua's "grand" plan is to establish four "Taobao" classes by 2012, with each student earning over ten thousand yuan per month upon graduation.

Last year, Taobao created

570,000 job opportunities

The report "Development Report of the Online Shopping Market in 2008" released by iResearch Consulting Group and Taobao shows that in 2008, Taobao created 570,000 direct and full-time job opportunities, meaning that in 2008, 570,000 people achieved employment through opening stores on Taobao, which is equivalent to the total number of new job positions added in Shanghai in 2008.
