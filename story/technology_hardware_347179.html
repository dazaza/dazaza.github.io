Assuming your monthly income is about 4,000 yuan, you live in a company dormitory, and after all expenses, you can still save about 2,500 yuan. After two or three years of work, you also have savings of tens of thousands of yuan. What kind of insurance do you want to buy to ensure that you and your family are worry-free?

(1) When choosing an insurance plan, it's important to prioritize. In terms of insurance, there is no perfect solution; only the one most suitable for oneself. For new employees just entering society, it’s essential to clearly prioritize their needs when selecting insurance plans. First, these individuals just started working, with limited economic income, so they cannot let paying premiums put too much financial pressure on them. Additionally, considering factors like supporting parents and starting a family, health insurance must be considered to guard against potential risks. Therefore, the first consideration should be purchasing relatively inexpensive accident insurance and term life insurance. If illness or accidents occur, comprehensive insurance ensures that family members will not lose their economic security. While purchasing accident and term life insurance, you may consider adding hospital subsidies and buying some social insurance, allowing you to enjoy medical benefits. Hospital subsidy insurance can complement social medical insurance, thereby compensating for economic losses caused by hospitalization. After securing accident and medical coverage, you can consider using part of your monthly surplus to purchase insurance that combines protection and investment, with dividend capabilities, preparing for future emergencies. I believe this type of insurance sacrifices some "interest" to gain security, making it suitable for young people known as the "striving generation." As for pension-related insurance, it can be postponed until later when income increases and economic conditions gradually improve.

Nowadays, many students prefer to stay in the cities where they study after graduation, leaving their hometowns and becoming so-called "first-generation entrepreneurs." The characteristics of these white-collar workers include short working time, high education levels, strong working abilities, being diligent and hardworking, often becoming the backbone or key cultivation targets of their companies, with decent incomes. Living alone in another place, economically independent, they need to fulfill the duty of supporting their parents and prepare for future marriage and children's education. They might currently have their own dormitory or rent a place, being single or unmarried friends. So how should such friends manage their own insurance?

(3) It is advisable to choose long-term payment periods. Regarding insurance types that allow annual payment terms, it is recommended to opt for long-term payment methods. Because the "striving generation" who have just entered the workforce usually don't have strong economic power yet. With the same level of coverage, the longer the term, the less the annual premium paid, resulting in smaller economic pressure, which is easier to bear. According to what we've discussed, if a person has nearly 2,500 yuan left over each month and about 20,000 yuan in bank deposits, choosing a shorter-term installment payment method would result in greater payment pressure, which does not align with the original purpose of purchasing insurance. Therefore, if you're buying installment-payment insurance products, choosing a payment period of 20 years or more is more appropriate.

Generally, companies only purchase basic social insurance for their employees. For new employees, purchasing additional commercial insurance is an important way to increase the safety factor of life.

(2) When choosing an insurance company, consider its strength. Insurance contracts have legal effect once they take effect, and insurance companies must honor the insurance benefits according to the contract provisions. There are many insurance companies in the current market. To occupy more market share, various insurance companies try their best to ensure their uniqueness in service and claims settlement. Customers require their services to be professional, thorough, and detailed; claims settlement to be swift, dedicated, and not playing "borderline games." While considering these two aspects, you should also pay attention to analyzing the background and strength of insurance companies, focusing on their profitability and development prospects. Especially when your career is just beginning, you need to fully invest yourself and don't have the time to keep track of the numerous financial products on the market. In general, if you choose dividend-paying insurance or pension insurance with savings and investment properties, joint venture insurance companies can be the first choice for purchase.
