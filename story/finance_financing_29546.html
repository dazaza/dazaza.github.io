Has the real Web 2.0 not truly taken off yet?

The year 2006 was the peak era of blogs in the internet world. Since blogs became popular, the concept of Web 2.0 has been hyped by people in various sectors of the internet industry. Many venture capitalists were initially enthusiastic about blogs but have not seen any significant returns or true profits from them. As a result, many pessimistic internet professionals have started to claim that the Web 2.0 concept is the end of the internet.

I am just an unknown individual in the internet world, currently working on an online accounting and financial management software targeted at personal and family use (Caike Online: www.coko365.com). My understanding of Web 2.0 is not very deep, but I do not agree that Web 2.0 is the end of the internet. I believe that Web 2.0 will be the true driving force of the future internet, and we are currently only in the early stages of Web 2.0, not yet ready for takeoff.

Why do I say this?

For example, in the Internet Web 2.0 era, online work has become fashionable and trendy. Even Adobe Systems' Photoshop is quickly catching up with the times, as they have already launched an online version of Photoshop. In the future, users will just need to access the internet, log into their website, and be able to use Photoshop without needing to download it.

Currently, many SAAS (Software as a Service) applications are developing rapidly. Giants such as Microsoft, Google, and domestic companies like Yonyou are entering the SAAS field. They clearly know that online software is the only way forward for the future of software and an important area to dominate the internet.

Since the development and maturity of CRM and ERP software, there are now many online CEM (Customer Experience Management) and online ERP software applications. Online software is gaining increasing recognition and support from customers. Even Alibaba sees potential in the online software sector and entered this field in 2006. Alibaba Software specializes in developing online software, mainly targeting enterprises, which shows its market prospects and future development trends.

We can fully believe that one day in the future, Microsoft's operating system and OFFICE software will all be online software, no downloads required, and no need to occupy customer computer resources.

Blogs are also a form of online application. People used to write diaries in notebooks, but since the advent of the internet, people have started writing diaries online. Similarly, with CRM software, companies used to download it and use it on their own computers. With the development of SAAS, many internet companies have introduced online CRMs. Customers do not need to download anything; they just need to access the internet to use it, making it simple and convenient.
