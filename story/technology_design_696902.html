After a terrifying first half and an exhilarating second half, AC Milan managed to successfully advance to the Champions League quarterfinals thanks to their 4-goal home advantage, despite conceding 3 goals to Arsenal at the Emirates Stadium. According to national television, in the first 45 minutes, Allegri's team failed to enter the game and couldn't organize effective attacks, already trailing by three goals, while Arsenal had a crushing advantage. The situation improved somewhat in the second half, with Abbiati making two breathtaking saves, and Nocerino missing a great opportunity. Ultimately, AC Milan advanced on the strength of their 4-0 home victory, with Abbiati being the chief architect of AC Milan's successful qualification.

In the match, Wenger opted for a 4-3-2-1 formation, but the attacking force was not limited to Van Persie alone, with particularly active wing play. AC Milan stuck to a 4-3-1-2 formation, with Robinho as the attacking midfielder, and Ibrahimovic and Shaqiri forming the forward line, marking Shaqiri's debut in European competition. In midfield, Emmanuelson gritted his teeth to persevere, forming the midfield line with Van Bommel and Nocerino. In defense, Allegri chose Abate, Mexes, Silva, and Mesiaba. However, national television emphasized that Allegri's choices were all "forced," such as Zambrotta's poor form at left back and Antonini's injury, both unable to complete the 90-minute game. Besides the conflict with Allegri, Nesta also had injuries. In midfield, Ambrosini was suspended, Aquilani barely made it to the substitute bench, and Seedorf was injured. Up front, Boateng and Pato were also injured. In fact, not only did Shaqiri need to start, Allegri had no one else to replace in the midfield and attack, with only Bonera available as backup in defense. Therefore, national television believed that San Siro's 4-0 was AC Milan's greatest reliance, but Damico warned that English fans rarely become discouraged voluntarily, especially at home, so Allegri's AC Milan must be careful.

Soon enough, Damico's warning became reality. Allegri fielded Ibrahimovic, El Shaarawy, and Robinho simultaneously to score quickly, and Wenger sent out four attacking players for the same purpose. From the start, Arsenal launched fierce attacks. In the sixth minute of the first half, Arsenal scored a goal on their second corner kick, during which Abate and his teammates completely forgot about Koscielny's presence. For the Frenchman, scoring was effortless. This was a disastrous start for Milan.

Allegri's problems didn't stop there. In midfield, Arsenal's pressing put Milan in trouble, clearly showing that Arsenal was not Cesena or Palermo. Allegri ordered his team to push forward a few meters, but this gave their opponents more offensive space. Moreover, Arsenal's quick ball circulation between their players meant the two teams were practically playing at two different speeds. With Emmanuelson easily losing possession to Rosicky near his own penalty area, Milan's troubles were evident. From the 10th to the 19th minute, Abbiati made two decisive saves against Van Persie's shots. Sky TV commented that Galliani liked Van Persie very much, hoping the Dutchman's performance would stimulate Berlusconi, who said before the game, "We patiently wait for Pato's return." At this moment, Milan entered its most difficult phase. Ibrahimovic was like a fish out of water, occasionally flailing helplessly. Robinho returned to the Milan derby, detached from the whole team's system, and Shaqiri? He seemed frightened. Nocerino couldn't find his rhythm, and only Van Bommel supported the team with experience and tenacity. In the 25th minute, after a brief panic in Milan's penalty area, Silva's clearance led to the ball falling to Rosicky's feet, making it 2-0, completing half of Arsenal's reversal mission. That wasn't enough; Arsenal's fast counterattack on the right flank saw Nocerino and Mesiaba tackling their opponent, leading to Van Persie converting a penalty to give Arsenal a 3-0 lead. On Sky TV, commentators were already discussing precedents like Istanbul and Deportivo La Coruna. Just before halftime, AC Milan finally got their first chance when Emmanuelson delivered a perfect pass to Shaqiri, but unfortunately, the young pharaoh missed wide under pressure from the opposing goalkeeper. Thus, Allegri and his players returned to the locker room with grim faces.

It was easy to imagine that Allegri must have taught his players a lesson during the halftime break. Afterward, it was reported that Allegri told the players that their performance was terrible, but he believed it wasn't the real Milan, so he would continue to trust the starting eleven. Encouraging the players was one of the objectives, but the lack of substitutes was an awkward situation. Nonetheless, Milan was more active in organizing attacks in the second half, with Ibrahimovic supporting the frontline alone. Additionally, Milan's pressing was slightly more aggressive. But Arsenal still created the main opportunities. Especially in the 13th minute, Abbiati first blocked with his foot and then caught the ball with his hand, successfully preventing Arsenal's goal. However, Milan realized in the second half that offense was the only way out, and they created several genuine chances. In the last 20 minutes, Allegri decided to strengthen defense, substituting Shaqiri with Aquilani, while Wenger chose to strengthen the attack. In the final 10 minutes, Mexes, Silva, Mesiaba, and Abate were "long" stationed in Milan's penalty area. By this point, both sides were exhausted, and the best chance belonged to Nocerino, who unfortunately hit the ball onto the opposing goalkeeper. Slow-motion showed that Allegri had already prepared to celebrate. Unlucky Nocerino even received an unjust yellow card for protesting Sagna's alleged handball.
