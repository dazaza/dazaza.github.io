Know Your Enemy - Which Type of Acne Do You Have?

1. Pseudo-scars - Red Spots

When acne becomes inflamed, blood vessels expand. However, before they subside, the blood vessels don't immediately shrink back, forming flat, red temporary spots. Generally speaking, these red spots usually take about half a year to fade on their own.

2. Pseudo-scars - Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation

Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation causes areas where red acne has healed to leave behind dark, dirty-looking colors. These colors will gradually fade over time.

3. Real Scars - Depressed Scars

When acne inflammation is too severe and damages too much collagen in the dermis, there is a possibility of leaving depressed scars due to the collapse of the skin structure. Depressed scars won't fade on their own.

4. Real Scars - Hypertrophic Scars (Keloids)

For those with particularly sensitive constitutions, the fibroblasts in the dermis are overly active. As a result, after the dermis is injured by inflammation, instead of flattening out, it thickens into hypertrophic scars resembling crab legs.
