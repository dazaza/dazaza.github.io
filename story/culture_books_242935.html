"Eight Thousand Hunan Daughters Ascend the Tianshan", the first generation of mothers on the wilderness.

by jqrxass on 2009-09-26 11:38:47

Fifty-five years ago, the grand historical feat of "8000 Hunan women going to Tianshan" closely linked the people of Hunan and Xinjiang. History and the people will not forget that for half a century, these Hunan women have made indelible contributions to the reclamation and defense of Xinjiang.

The Legend of "8000 Hunan Women Going to Tianshan" is Actually History

"During the war, you are not afraid to charge forward; in reclaiming land, no matter how great the difficulties, you can overcome them, I believe all of this. But not wanting to find a wife and have children, I don't believe it. Even if you want to stay bachelors for life, I still disagree. Our reclamation cause needs successors. If all of you remain bachelors, who will inherit our cause? I've talked with Wang Beihu (a nickname), he will recruit some female soldiers from inland areas..."

Chapter One of "8000 Hunan Women Going to Tianshan" - Hunan Women Entering Xinjiang

I secretly registered without telling my family. Soon after, I saw my name on the list, at that moment, I was truly excited. But when it came time to depart, I didn't want to go anymore. Indeed, it was a place I had never even dreamed of. I repeatedly looked at the map, seeing the vast desolation between the Altai, Tianshan, and Kunlun mountain ranges, looking at that distant and winding road, thinking about leaving my hometown, leaving friends and family. My tears couldn't stop flowing down. Most importantly, my father had lost his official position, thus losing his salary, making the family's living conditions increasingly strained. I wanted to stay and become an elementary school teacher to alleviate the difficulties faced by my family.

Chapter Two of "8000 Hunan Women Going to Tianshan" - Love on the Wilderness

When I arrived in Yanqi, I was assigned to the propaganda team of the 17th Regiment of the 6th Division of the Second Army as a deputy squad leader, while others continued moving forward. At that time, I heard that Kashi produced rice, so I thought of going to Kashi. What we cared most about back then was whether there was rice to eat. In some places, the scenery was too desolate, Hunan female soldiers wouldn't get off the vehicle unless someone brought a basin of rice over, at which point they would disembark, thinking that since there was rice to eat here, it wasn't bad. As for Shandong female soldiers who refused to get off, they were given bundles of green onions instead.

Chapter Three of "8000 Hunan Women Going to Tianshan" - The Greatest Entrepreneurship

In October 1953, I noticed that my allowance had increased to eighteen yuan, so I asked why I had more money, and they told me I had been promoted. Not long after, I joined the recruitment team to Shandong to recruit Shandong women into the army, aiming to solve the marriage problems of many veterans who had defected. The 25th Division recruited more than five hundred people. However, upon arriving in Xinjiang, the Shandong women found no houses, no tiles, only harsh conditions, causing them to cry and make a fuss. Eventually, the division headquarters ordered the comrades leading the women to work alongside them until they settled down. Thus, I ended up in the company.

Chapter Four of "8000 Hunan Women Going to Tianshan" - In the Storms

It was actually a Gobi desert, apart from the Gobi stones darkened by the sun year-round and the occasional cluster of camel thorns, there was nothing else. It was already evening, most of us were officers coming down from the military region authorities, seeing such a situation, no man remained unchilled at heart, and no woman refrained from shedding tears. With our emotions unsettled, facing this situation, we felt helpless. We pulled out the camel thorns, opened our backpacks, each family claiming a spot, lying down under the sky, spending our first night on the wilderness.

Chapter Five of "8000 Hunan Women Going to Tianshan" - Descendants of Hunan Spread Across Tianshan

Since I was mobilized as a dependent, since I could no longer work, even if I had to beg, I must raise my five children well, allowing them to grow up and succeed. In this way, I traveled thousands of miles to Xinjiang, also contributing my own efforts to it, I encouraged myself. My husband earned only dozens of yuan per month, making ends meet difficult. When meat and fish were cheap, I bought them and cured them using Hunan methods into preserved pork and preserved fish. This way, the whole family could avoid buying fresh meat in summer, and we could sell some to supplement our household expenses. Until now, I still do this, of course, now mainly for our own consumption, because after decades, the whole family has gotten used to eating preserved pork. We also sell some, because the preserved pork and fish I make have become a local delicacy, with some regular customers enjoying them.
