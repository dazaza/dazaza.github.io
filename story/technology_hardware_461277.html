Summary: Under the circumstances of the Computer Science Institute, application-oriented courses are provided, and a profound analysis and discussion of the current situation of teaching and learning, as well as the reasons for its formation have been carried out. On the basis of thought, constructive reform opinions are proposed, and preliminary attempts have been made by two students, resulting in a summary of data obtained from follow-up reviews of this case, with significant overall results of the reform.

Keywords: Application-oriented, Computer Science, Learning, University-Industry Interaction, Strengthening today's applied undergraduate institutions, construction of higher education institutions and regular colleges and universities to cultivate talent for the purpose of application. Graduates of computer science majors work in specialized fields such as system research and development, website design and maintenance, automation, and e-commerce. Considering factors such as school operating costs and simplifying entry, many universities focus on developing professional talents in the direction of computer application software. However, as graduates of computer science, achieving a true understanding of computers and effectively implementing applications, along with an understanding of how computers work, is essential, making hardware courses still necessary. Courses such as professional computer hardware and software integration should be carefully considered because, regardless of their capabilities, computers internally process digital calculations that ultimately remain a series of 0s and 1s, which is machine language code.

Due to its ease of memorization and modification, people discovered mnemonic instruction commands, also known as assembly language. Currently used programming languages, assembly language directly controls computer hardware functions and is the most effective language for doing so. Assembly language programming for computer science students, rather than high-level language courses, requires a deeper understanding of computer hardware architecture, and memory instructions are relatively complex. Its advantage is that the target program is short and fast in many real-time control applications, microcontroller systems, and is either entirely or partially written in assembly language. The plan aims to cultivate students' system-level programming skills, code debugging, and analytical abilities, suitable for the training objectives of applied undergraduate institutions. Combining assembly language with hardware and software foundational courses creates a teaching context for the course.

Thus, it becomes overly complex and restrictive for many teaching plans; secondly, many non-computer professionals do not fully understand the structure and related computer hardware theories, making it easier to master high-level language programming; lastly, assembly language has poor portability, leading to many computer science graduates focusing on high-level languages as common development tools in institutional developments. All these factors lead to many students finding assembly language programming monotonous, difficult to configure, and useless, resulting in low interest in learning, teaching, and hindering work development. 2.1 2.2.1 The rapid development of computer hardware and software poses theoretical issues for teachers and textbooks, with a significant lag between computer hardware and teaching materials. Win32 assembly has been widely used in protection modes, but most schools' textbooks still focus on 8086 assembly language instructions. Due to limited teaching outlines and class constraints, most teachers only emphasize key points and difficulties in the course without touching on other aspects.
