Introduction to the Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) Diabetes Department of China-Japan Friendship Hospital

The TCM Diabetes Department of China-Japan Friendship Hospital is one of the hospital's key departments, with a total of 32 beds, 7 doctors (including 2 chief physicians, 2 associate chief physicians, and 3 attending physicians), and 11 nurses. The department focuses on diabetes and its complications, metabolic syndrome, hypertension, lipid abnormalities, obesity, etc., as important research directions. Department head Dr. Tong Xiaolin serves as the chairman of the Diabetes Professional Committee of the Chinese Association of Traditional Chinese Medicine, the chairman of the Doctoral Academic Research Society of the Chinese Association of Traditional Chinese Medicine, and professor and doctoral supervisor at Peking University Health Science Center and Beijing University of Chinese Medicine. The department has undertaken more than ten national-level and ministerial-level scientific research projects, winning multiple awards, and has published over 150 academic papers in professional journals.

Characteristics of the TCM Diabetes Department of China-Japan Friendship Hospital

The TCM Diabetes Department of China-Japan Friendship Hospital has the following characteristics in the prevention and treatment of diabetes and its complications:

1. In blood glucose regulation, both traditional Chinese medicine and Western medicine are used in combination, applying syndrome differentiation and treatment to improve patients' insulin sensitivity, eliminate uncontrollable factors of blood glucose, control blood glucose within an ideal range, while reducing the dosage of Western medicine.

2. Using TCM to improve symptoms and physical constitution of diabetic patients.

3. For diabetic patients without complications, adopting a preventive approach using TCM intervention.

4. For diabetic complications, adopting a comprehensive treatment method mainly based on TCM combined with Western medicine in various forms, focusing on delaying the occurrence and development of complications and improving patients' quality of life.

5. Conducting personalized education on diabetes knowledge for diabetic patients so that they not only improve their condition during hospitalization but also benefit from changing their understanding of diabetes for life.
