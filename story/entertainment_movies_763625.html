Synopsis:

This is a supernatural story about the tattoo culture of the Samoans. For the Samoans, tattoos are a gift from God, and those who abuse tattoos can bring shame to themselves and their families. Living in disgrace is even more tormenting than death.

Jack Soya is a tattoo artist from the United States who frequently travels around the world to exchange experiences with different tattoo masters.

At a tattoo exposition in Singapore, Jack meets a beautiful woman in white named Sina. As if destined, Jack follows Sina into a tent where he is amazed by the traditional Samoan tattoo method. However, Sina's cousin clearly does not welcome this intruder. When leaving, Jack secretly takes a small chisel, a traditional Samoan tattoo tool. Jack’s palm is accidentally cut by the tool, but he doesn't realize that Pandora's box has been opened, and an evil spirit is about to unleash terrifying retribution...

Jack arrives in New Zealand and, with the help of an old friend, finds work as a tattoo artist. Unexpectedly, Jack reunites with Sina, and the two quickly fall in love. In a moment of passion, Jack tattoos a stunning red flower on Sina's lower back.

Tragedy begins to unfold as Jack's customers one after another meet with misfortune. Their tattoos spread all over their bodies, blood turns into ink flowing continuously, and their deaths are horrifying. Jack watches helplessly as one of the young girls struggles on a hospital bed. In a daze, Jack sees an evil spirit repeatedly tattooing the girl. Despite the doctors' best efforts, the girl still dies a terrible death. Terrified, Jack realizes that Sina's life is also in danger...
