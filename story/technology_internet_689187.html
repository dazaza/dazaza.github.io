Today, at the Academy Awards, the 84th Oscar for Best Animated Feature was announced: "Rango" (Chinese title: Lan Ge, 2011). The film was produced by Nickelodeon (American Children's Channel), and its director, Gore Verbinski, graduated from the UCLA Film School (University of California, Los Angeles Film School). UCLA's film and television program ranks first in the United States, but in the 21st century, only one of its students has won the Oscar for Best Director in animation.

Currently, students interested in animation majors can be roughly divided into two categories: those who like Japanese-style comics and those who prefer American-style animated films. The former has a greater influence among students, while the latter can be said to appeal to all ages. I often encounter students who love animation and hope to study abroad. Below, I will introduce a few Oscar-winning animation directors and two top U.S. schools offering animation programs.

Pixar Animation Studios

Many students studying animation dream of entering Pixar Animation Studios. The former CEO of Apple, Steve Jobs, was the founder of this company and once served as its CEO. From 2001 to 2010, all eight films produced by Pixar (Apple Jobs) were nominated for Best Animated Feature, with six winning the Oscar for Best Animated Feature. Below, I will provide a statistical overview of the educational backgrounds of the directors of these eight nominated films.

The director of "Finding Nemo" (Chinese title: Underwater Mobilization, 2003) and "WALL-E" (2008) is Andrew Stanton, who graduated from the California Institute of the Arts, specializing in character animation.

The director of "Monsters, Inc." (Chinese title: Monster Company, 2001) and "Up" (Chinese title: Fly House Travelogue, 2009) is Pete Docter, who started college at the University of Minnesota before transferring to and graduating from the California Institute of the Arts.

The director of "The Incredibles" (Chinese title: Super Family Mobilization, 2004) and "Ratatouille" (Chinese title: Chef Rat King, 2007) is Brad Bird, who studied at the California Institute of the Arts under Disney sponsorship.

The director of "Cars" (Chinese title: Car Mobilization, 2006, which lost to Happy Feet) is John Lasseter. He initially attended Pepperdine University before hearing that the California Institute of the Arts offered an animation major, becoming the second student to enter the program.

The director of "Toy Story 3" (Chinese title: Toy Mobilization 3, 2009) is Lee Unkrich, who graduated from the University of Southern California School of Cinematic Arts.

From the above list of directors, it is not difficult to see that the majority of Oscar-winning animation directors in the 21st century graduated from the California Institute of the Arts. Below, I will introduce in detail the situation of the program and admission requirements for interested students.

California Institute of the Arts

CalArts offers two animation directions: Program in Character Animation & Programs in Experimental Animation. The director of Cars, John Lasseter, studied the Character Animation direction. This direction only provides an undergraduate program, i.e., BA only. This program is designed for students who wish to understand animation role performance and storytelling through comprehensive artistic and technical training, helping students become capable animators proficient in both traditional and CG techniques.

The other animation program offered by the school is the Experimental Animation direction, which provides BFA and MFA programs. Compared to Character Animation, Experimental Animation is more closely related to art - such as teaching students painting, using characters, and telling stories. This program encourages students to explore different, novel, or unconventional ways to express themselves.

Due to the different directions, there are also significant differences in the curriculum settings between the two directions.

Character Animation Direction: In the first year of undergraduate studies, the courses mainly focus on figure drawing, color and design, storytelling, and character animation - traditional techniques and CG techniques. In the second year, students delve deeper into techniques such as combining dialogue, sound effects, and music. In the third year, the focus shifts to professional training, including short-term traditional animation and 3D computer animation story classes, as well as specialized work in drawing, chatu, novels, and visual design. In the final year, students enter core course studies and develop courses based on their research interests.

Experimental Animation Direction: The main courses offered include 2D drawing, single frame production, 2D and 3D computer animation, practical knowledge of creative simulation, and courses on animation history.

Application Tips:

[Deadline & Application Fee]

Early admission deadline is December 1st, application fee $50 (U.S. students) $65 (international students).

Regular admission deadline is January 5th of the following year, application fee $70 (U.S.) $85 (international).

[Application Documents]

Transcripts, recommendation letters (with forms, 2 letters), Artist’s Statement (i.e., SOP or PS).

[Admission Requirements]

A. Character Animation Admission Requirements: (paper push)

(1) Provide Observational drawings (minimum 15 drawings)

(2) Provide Sketchbook/Visual Diary works

(3) Provide other works, such as Sequential image storytelling, Digital creative work, photography, optimization, sculpture, murals, character design, cartoons, comics.

B. Experimental Animation Admission Requirements:

(1) Provide a project outline.

(2) Provide film reviews, book reviews, etc., art-related critiques.

(3) Project plan, explaining which type of film production attracts you most.

[Score Requirements]

The California College of the Arts does not offer language programs, so undergraduate applicants must have a TOEFL score of over 80, and graduate applicants must have a TOEFL score of over 100. Without providing TOEFL scores, the school will absolutely not admit international students.

[Tuition Fees]

Total tuition for 2011-2012 is $37,684. Health insurance is $1,572, energy $100, activity fees $76, technology fee $400, and experimental fees are calculated separately.

[Scholarships]

The school offers financial aid including grants, loans, work-study, scholarships, and (graduate) teaching assistantships.

Among them, more than eighty percent of California Institute of the Arts students receive some form of financial assistance, but it is very difficult for most students to obtain full-tuition financial assistance, especially in the first year.

International students can only receive limited institutional funds financial assistance. Most scholarships are only available to U.S. students.

University of Southern California

Although USC does not produce many Oscar-winning animation directors in the 21st century, its animation program is on par with UCLA and should not be underestimated. Below, I will also introduce in detail the Animation & Digital Arts (Animation and Digital Arts Major) of the university.

This major creates a unique animation learning environment for students. As an internationally renowned diversified project, the John C. Hench Animation & Digital Arts Department incorporates the development of digital art, as well as the history courses of traditional media, painting, sculpture, video, and decoration into the curriculum, cultivating students' animation production skills while teaching theoretical knowledge and film criticism knowledge. The department persistently pursues the combination of tradition and new technology. Through studying human appearance, organic media, and posture, it explores the true and complex thoughts and emotions behind the media, believing that the process of making animations reflects the evolution of human physiology and psychology. The major offers three teaching programs: Bachelor's degree, Master's degree, and minor. It usually takes four years to complete a Bachelor's degree and three years to complete a Master's degree. Detailed major directions include: Character Animation, Experimental Animation, Visual Effects, 3D Computer Animation Production, Scientific Visualization, and Interactive Animation.
