★[Practical Tips] Winter Management of Angora Rabbits

[Oral Introduction] With the rise in rabbit hair purchase prices, the Angora rabbit breeding industry is starting to recover, and many farmers are shifting their breeding focus to Angora rabbits. However, in terms of feeding and management, we find that some breeders spend a lot of money buying good breeds, but still use old methods in feeding and management, especially in winter, unscientific management leads to a significant decrease in the hair production of good breeds. After some time, these good breeds become ordinary rabbits. Therefore, in today's "Practical Tips" section, we will teach you how to raise Angora rabbits well in winter.

[Voiceover] The most prominent feature of winter is low temperature, strong wind, and short daylight hours. For Angora rabbits, all kinds of green fodder are scarce, so winter is the slowest growing season for Angora rabbits. However, winter is often also the peak breeding season for Angora rabbits. Doing a good job in winter management lays a good foundation for the coming year. First, the winter feeding and management of Angora rabbits need to pay attention to keeping warm (Subtitle: Keeping Warm). In cold weather and snowy days, the doors and windows of the rabbit house should be closed, especially for rabbits that have just had their hair cut or plucked.

[Contemporary Sound] Chairman of Dafeng City Snow Rabbit Hair Production Professional Cooperative Xiao Zhengwang: Winter is also the breeding season, especially for newborn baby rabbits, more attention should be paid to keeping warm. Use birthing boxes, add more grass, and add some rabbit hair, inferior rabbit hair, which helps keep warm.

[Voiceover] Secondly, pay attention to timely hair cutting (Subtitle: Timely Hair Cutting). After entering winter, the temperature gradually drops. This change may not be beneficial for keeping warm, but the relatively low temperature is very advantageous for rabbit hair growth. When the rabbit hair reaches the standard, choose a sunny noon to cut or pluck the hair. When cutting the hair, leave the abdominal hair longer to protect the internal organs from wind and cold invasion. Thirdly, feed reasonably (Subtitle: Reasonable Feeding). In winter, besides increasing the supply of concentrated feed, households with conditions should try to add some high-energy feed in the feed mix, such as corn. Baby rabbits not only need high-energy feed, but also more protein supplementation than adult rabbits, mainly from cakes such as soybean cake and rapeseed cake, which can fully provide protein content. Green fodder is relatively scarce in winter. To ensure normal reproduction of Angora rabbits and rabbit hair growth, households with conditions should grow some suitable multi-season forage grasses to ensure daily access to green fodder.

[Contemporary Sound] Chairman of Dafeng City Snow Rabbit Hair Production Professional Cooperative Xiao Zhengwang: To raise rabbits well, green fodder is indispensable. Farmers raising rabbits usually have land themselves, where they can grow forage grasses, gramineous feed, carrots, black forage grass, and chicory, all of which are excellent green fodder.

[Voiceover] Note that baby rabbits that have just been weaned should not be fed excessive green fodder to prevent diarrhea caused by intestinal discomfort. The nursing time for winter-born baby rabbits can be appropriately extended to enhance their physical condition. The fourth point to note is strengthening winter management (Subtitle: Winter Management). Rabbit droppings in the rabbit house should be cleaned frequently to keep the rabbit house clean and ensure fresh air. To prevent bacterial and viral infections, disinfection should be carried out every 7-10 days, and it should be done on a clear and windless day. Although the temperature is low in winter and bacteria reproduce less actively than in summer, the epidemic prevention work for Angora rabbits cannot be relaxed (Subtitle: Epidemic Prevention and Treatment).

[Contemporary Sound] Chairman of Dafeng City Snow Rabbit Hair Production Professional Cooperative Xiao Zhengwang: Even in winter, rabbit fever prevention, Pasteurella, E. coli, and Clostridium should all be vaccinated according to the immunization schedule, which will allow better development in the spring of the following year.

[Postscript] Raising Angora rabbits well in winter lays a good foundation for the spring breeding and feeding of Angora rabbits in the coming year.
