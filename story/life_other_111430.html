Couple forces teenage girls into prostitution; each girl must receive 20-30 customers per day

by xiaoshiyiluokuang on 2008-12-15 08:44:52

A married couple in Bozhou acted as pimps, forcing young girls into prostitution while also making them call them "mom" and "dad". Recently, the intermediate people's court of Bozhou announced its verdict on this case involving organized prostitution. The court sentenced Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an to death with a two-year reprieve and life imprisonment respectively for the crime of organizing prostitution.

Forcing prostitution and requiring to be called "parents"

Between 2002 and 2007, under the pretense of recruiting workers for a local liquor company in Bozhou, Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an lured 17 young girls from the Qiaocheng District of Bozhou and from Bo'ai County in Henan Province to their residence at the Zhanqiao Inn and Chaoling New Village in the Qiaocheng District of Bozhou.

Wang Mei instructed defendant Li Baoyang to beat victims such as Li Juan and forced them into prostitution. She also organized Mei Zi, Wang Qiao, and Wang Ping to engage in prostitution near the Mache Inn in the Qiaocheng District of Bozhou. It was found that Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an used methods of bribery, threats, and violence to organize and force these women to engage daily in prostitution activities in inns and hotels within the alleys around the Mache Inn in the Qiaocheng District of Bozhou. They also stipulated that all victims must address them as "dad" and "mom", and must complete a daily quota of 20 to 30 prostitution acts, charging between 30 to 100 yuan per act. Victims who failed to meet the quota were punished by being kept awake at night and verbally abused.

"Parents" kept a gang of thugs

According to investigations by the prosecution, all proceeds from prostitution belonged to Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an, who paid the victims monthly wages ranging from 300 to 1000 yuan. Additionally, defendants Li Baoyang, Li Baopeng, Guo Lei, Cao Hui, Sun Peng, and others, under the employment, instruction, and arrangement of Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an, were responsible for taking the victims daily to engage in prostitution around the Mache Inn area in the Qiaocheng District of Bozhou, supervising the victims, providing lookout, and collecting prostitution fees, actively assisting in the organization of prostitution. Meanwhile, they used violence and threats against victims who refused or were reluctant to engage in prostitution or tried to escape, directly forcing them into prostitution under the instruction of Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an. Among them, Li Baoyang was the main enforcer.

Defendant Wang Yanting, knowing that Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an were organizing prostitution, followed Wang Mei's instructions to conduct virginity checks on some new victims who arrived at Wang Mei's home, allowing Wang Mei to charge higher fees.

Severe punishment for organizing prostitution

The court ruled that defendants Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an, with the aim of profit, took measures such as recruitment, inducement, coercion, and harboring to control multiple individuals engaging in prostitution. Their actions have constituted the crime of organizing prostitution, and there were aggravating circumstances such as forcing underage girls under 14 years old into prostitution and raping before forcing into prostitution, which made the crimes especially severe.

Defendants Li Baoyang, Li Baopeng, Guo Lei, Cao Hui, Sun Peng, and Wang Yanting assisted others in organizing prostitution. Their actions have constituted the crime of assisting in organizing prostitution, and the circumstances were serious. Based on relevant laws, the above-mentioned sentences were handed down to Wang Mei and Wang Ping'an. Simultaneously, guilty verdicts were made against Li Baoyang, Li Baopeng, and others, sentencing them to fixed-term imprisonment ranging from three to nine years, and fines ranging from 30,000 to 50,000 yuan. (All victim names in the article are pseudonyms.)
