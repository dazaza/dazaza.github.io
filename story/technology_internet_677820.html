What Causes Vitiligo?

1. Self-medicating, leading to serious consequences.

Generally, vitiligo patients treat their condition without proper diagnosis, without distinguishing types or identifying causes, and blindly undergo treatment. They only know they have vitiligo and panic, so they use medication blindly. This results in many patients using medication long-term without cure, some experiencing ineffectiveness, drug resistance, large-scale expansion, allergic reactions, and other adverse effects, losing the best treatment opportunity.

2. Impatience for a quick cure.

Many vitiligo patients do not seek systematic treatment from experts but instead wander around seeking treatments, even consulting quacks, using folk remedies, or non-standard medications. When one drug is ineffective, they switch to another, continuously treating without resolution, resulting in misdiagnosis and mistreatment. Some patients who don't have localized fixed-type vitiligo still undergo skin grafting surgery, which leads to large-scale expansion post-surgery due to injuries. Patients with vitiligo caused by injuries also undergo skin grafts, as do those with scarred skin, causing extensive scarring on their faces after surgery. Some allergy-prone vitiligo patients misuse drugs, leading to systemic allergies.

3. Blind use of medication.

Many patients do not follow expert guidance when using medication but instead rely solely on the instructions provided, using any medication meant for treating vitiligo regardless of type or cause. Some patients seek random medical advice with a "try and see" attitude, leading to misdiagnosis and mistreatment.
