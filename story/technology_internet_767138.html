Samsung denies acquiring shares of Best Buy, or discusses expanding the scale of experience stores

by anonymous on 2013-10-08 18:21:24

Samsung Electronics released preliminary Q3 financial data last Friday showing strong earnings growth, but market analysts do not believe that the profit growth driven by its smartphone business will continue in the coming years. To address the growing saturation of the smartphone market, Samsung might consider acquiring a stake in Best Buy, the largest electronics retailer in the U.S. However, a spokesperson for Samsung Electronics has issued a statement denying this rumor.

Vice Chairman of Samsung Electronics, Lee Jae-yong, recently told *The Korea Daily* that the company is in talks with Best Buy to strengthen its presence in the U.S. Lee Jae-yong stated that the U.S. is the world's largest consumer electronics market, and Samsung plans to increase its market share there, with collaboration with Best Buy being a crucial step. Lee Jae-yong said, "I recently met with some executives from Best Buy to discuss certain business issues."

Lee Jae-yong met with Best Buy executives at Samsung Electronics' headquarters in Seoul, joined by co-CEO Yoo Boo-keun. Lee Jae-yong declined to disclose the details of the discussions. There were earlier reports suggesting that Samsung was planning to acquire a stake in Best Buy. Previously, Samsung had signed an agreement with Best Buy to open experience stores within 1400 of its shops.

However, regarding rumors about Samsung acquiring a stake in Best Buy, a spokesperson for Samsung Electronics issued a statement saying, "In the past few days, there have been reports that Samsung Electronics is considering acquiring a partial stake in Best Buy. Please do not believe these rumors. The two companies have not engaged in any negotiations regarding equity acquisition."

Industry insiders noted that Samsung established a partnership with Best Buy in April this year, starting Samsung Experience Stores within Best Buy locations. At these Samsung Experience Stores, consumers can purchase smartphones, tablets, laptops, cameras, and various accessories. Although Samsung's statement indicates that it does not plan to acquire a stake in Best Buy, it does not rule out the possibility of further cooperation between the two companies in the future. Given that Samsung also manufactures home appliances and televisions, similar collaborations could occur in these product areas, possibly leading to designated sections within Best Buy stores focused on selling such products.

Samsung may have recognized the value of retail outlets and aims to enhance its presence in Best Buy's mobile product offerings. Apple has achieved great success through its Apple Retail Stores, so in the future, Samsung Experience Stores could not only sell products but also train customers and provide repair and other services.
