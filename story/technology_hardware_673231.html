The most crucial aspect of winter skincare is hydration, and using certain ingredients can effectively provide moisture to the skin. Today, let's take a look at how mung beans and coix seeds can hydrate the skin!

Firstly, prepare the ingredients: mung beans, coix seeds, and yam. These ingredients not only can be used to make porridge but also utilized for skincare. Coix seeds are renowned as the "Queen of Beauty," with many cosmetics containing coix seed components. Crush these ingredients.

After mixing and crushing the ingredients, prepare some cucumbers and loofahs. Everyone knows that cucumbers have moisturizing properties, and loofah water has also become popular in recent years. Therefore, these two can be blended into juice and used together with the crushed powder.

Blend cucumber and loofah separately and mix them with the crushed powder. These two can be used separately. Cucumber water not only has moisturizing properties but also excellent cleaning effects, so it can be used as a cleansing and moisturizing mask. Loofah can be used purely for hydration.

Before use, add some dry ingredient powder appropriately to ensure the mixed mask adheres evenly to the face.

Apply the blended cucumber or loofah mask on your face and wait for 15 minutes. Since water loss accelerates in winter, do not leave the mask on for too long. If you're worried about dryness, you can apply another layer of a water-based mask to enhance hydration.

After 15 minutes, rinse off the mask with warm water, and your hydrated, tender face will appear. However, don't forget to continue with your regular skincare routine afterward.
