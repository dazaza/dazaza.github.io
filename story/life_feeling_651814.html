By Ru'er

There was once a group of women, their talent compared to Yi'an (Li Qingzhao), their art approached Cai Yan, and their beauty surpassed Luo Fu. However, they were despised by the world; they toiled for half their lives and were still mocked as beauties who brought calamity.

They had no control over their own fates, yet they were branded as morally corrupt. Because of their lowly status, they sold themselves to brothels, where their dreams were shattered along with the fading spring. They were unfortunate, having accompanied those days long gone.

-- Written in the foreword

Among the chirping birds and bustling crowds, I saw you sitting gracefully in the hall, your face beaming with the warmth of spring. The stringed instruments and flutes could not do without your smooth hands to play them, nor could they do without your melodious voice to harmonize. Your eyes resembled a pool of autumn water, intoxicating the guests, who cheered at every sight of you, adding more charm to your already beautiful smile.

In the midst of cosmetics, you stood out like a single flower among many. Everyone saw your beauty, but no one understood your heart or saw through your eyes. Amidst the pervasive aroma of wine, you remained the only sober one in the hall; amidst the swirling smoke, you remained the only clear-headed one in the room.

Among the fragrant flowers, your graceful figure occasionally appeared. Under the moonlight, I met you, dressed in moonlight, accompanying an indifferent gentleman. You recited a few lines of poetry fitting the scene, hummed a few segments of amorous tunes, glanced a few times at the moon on the horizon, and looked a few times at the people before you... I imagine you have long been accustomed to such pretense, performing it flawlessly. Wearing the same mask for different people, playing the same act. The people before you only thought that your beauty and artistry were unparalleled, but they were too lazy to care about your tears or sighs.

Drinking under the moon together, we only wished to remain drunk and never wake...

I saw you leaning half against the window, without a trace of a smile. A look of desolation chilled through the fragrant chamber, with yellow flowers scattered all over the ground behind you.

A cup of fragrant tea, steeped with endless sorrow.

Night after night of "The Rear Palace" song, how can you sing your own songs, play your own music? Day after day of dancing and singing, which tune is sung for yourself?

Even if you were unmatched in beauty and artistry, what does it matter? Your brilliant strategies in chess were still someone else's pawn, your proficiency in calligraphy and painting was merely writing someone else's life. Have you ever drawn a single stroke for your own future? Even if you are well-versed in ancient and modern knowledge, once you entered this door, you became a stain on history, impossible to erase...

Dust and dirt swirl around you, how can you calmly see your own flowers, admire your own moon? As the stars flow westward and the night is not yet over, which star is your very own Cowherd?

Not watching the mandarin ducks play in the water, not flying side by side, your entire life without children by your side, only facing the bronze mirror in sorrow. Reciting an old poem, guarding a half-hearted longing. Remembering ceaselessly, that faithless lover. The dream of the pleasure house may be pleasant, but it's hard to express deep affection...

The person you wait for never appears, and those around you are all fleeting passers-by. Coming and going in haste.

"Clothes becoming looser, I do not regret, for her sake, I waste away!"

Alone in the room, watching the flowers wither, when the mist clears, you've forgotten when you were born, where you are now.

A lifetime of splendor, never enjoying its pleasures. Coming like a spring dream so brief, leaving like morning clouds lost forever.

What remains is not your voice or color, not your jade-like bones. Behind the tales of amorous affairs, your soul remains pure and untainted...

Not loving the dust of the world,

It seems destiny has led me astray

Flowers bloom and fall in their own time

Always relying on the master of spring

Leaving eventually must leave

Staying, how can one stay

If mountain flowers cover the head

Do not ask where I shall return

Life blooms as splendid as summer flowers, death, also as serene as autumn leaves. Clinging to this, letting sorrow linger endlessly.

Trapped in the cacophony of flutes and songs, with nowhere to go. Gazing into the abyss of sorrow...
