The ability to inspire others to achieve excellence is called leadership. Kouzes and Posner's co-authored book "Leadership" is one of the most cherished and resonant books on this subject. The authors focus on "ordinary people" rather than "big stars" and how they achieve their best leadership outcomes. The authors believe that when these "ordinary people" exhibit leadership, they display certain specific behaviors, which may vary slightly due to differences in industry, ethnicity, nationality, or even position, but these behaviors or patterns can all be learned through study.

This makes it clear that leadership is something everyone can learn and achieve. Through long-term research, Kouzes and Posner found that those who can lead others have followed a similar path. Although each person’s achievements are different, they share common behavioral patterns. These include setting an example, sharing a vision, challenging the status quo, enabling others to act, and inspiring the heart and mind.

### Setting an Example

Leaders must first clarify and frequently discuss their own beliefs and guiding principles. To demonstrate what is important, the best approach is to lead by example and set a standard.

To become a trustworthy leader, one must first explore within oneself and find one's own voice. If you cannot find your own voice, you will only echo others' words, reciting speeches written by secretaries or parroting others’ statements. If what you say isn’t your own and you don't even believe it, over time, your words and actions won't align, making it impossible to lead by example. Beliefs are guiding lights, and beliefs are action guides.

Simultaneously, a shared organizational belief must be formed through mutual understanding. Long-lasting organizations all have their core beliefs and values.

Leaders are ambassadors of these beliefs. Attention should be paid to using appropriate language, such as rarely using terms like employees, bosses, or subordinates, and instead using partners, citizens, and teams—modern organizational language. Real stories from within the company should be used to reinforce core beliefs.

### Sharing a Vision

Every organization begins with a dream. Leaders must describe an attractive opportunity that they and their followers can achieve in the near future, envisioning an exciting and achievable future. Leadership is a process of dialogue, not a monologue. Leaders use vivid language and highly engaging methods to paint the team's vision, igniting enthusiasm for pursuing the vision.

Leaders must discover or inspire a shared vision through deep listening rather than promoting their personal worldview. In a sense, leaders hold up a mirror reflecting the aspirations of their followers. Leaders must find reasons for which people are willing to dedicate themselves. Reading, listening, or watching Martin Luther King Jr.'s speech at the Lincoln Memorial in 1963 before 250,000 people clarifies this point.

### Challenging the Status Quo

In every remarkable leadership story collected by the authors, there is a challenge. No one achieves greatness by maintaining the status quo. Leaders are not inventors but guardians of change. It is crucial for leaders to understand that reform and change involve experimentation and carry risks. Taking small steps can mitigate potential risks and possible failures, while accumulating small successes can build confidence to face greater challenges.

Regularly ask yourself: "Why do we do this? Why do we do that?" This helps identify necessary improvements. Questioning the status quo is not just the leader's responsibility; outstanding leaders create an atmosphere where others also question the status quo voluntarily.

Encourage everyone to seek ideas. Let idea collection become part of everyone's daily, weekly, and monthly work. Keep your antennas up wherever you are because new ideas can emerge at any time.

### Enabling Others to Act

Great dreams cannot be realized by one person alone. Achieving goals requires teamwork, sincere unity, and solid relationships. There must be group cooperation and individual responsibility.

Leaders should advocate reciprocal behavior principles.

Through studying thousands of leaders' achievements, the authors found that leaders use "we" three times more often than "I." Outstanding leaders cultivate collaboration, which should not be limited to a small group of loyalists but must also include colleagues, clients, suppliers, and anyone relevant to the vision.

Leaders must learn to trust and empower others. Leaders get others to act not by exercising authority but by relinquishing it. Leaders are coaches.

### Inspiring the Heart and Mind

The journey to climb the peak is difficult and long. People may feel exhausted, frustrated, and want to give up. Leaders must inspire their followers to muster the energy and strive forward.

What leaders need to do is recognize people's contributions and create a culture of celebration within the organization. If people improve performance, enhance quality, or achieve other outstanding results, recognition should make it clear that these align with the organization's values.

Personalized and creative encouragement is essential. For instance, a few well-timed compliments or a note with a smiley face can go a long way. Celebration events should be carefully organized. Suitable encouragement methods should be found. Stories should be passed down forever.

As individuals, we tend to fulfill the expectations of our leaders (teachers, coaches, or parents). Here, expectations are not commands. Outstanding leaders set higher expectations because they know: high expectations lead to high performance. Social psychologists call this the "Pygmalion effect." In Greek mythology, Pygmalion was a sculptor who carved a beautiful woman and fell deeply in love with the statue. Through his earnest desire, the statue came to life.

Kouzes and Posner's research on leadership provides us with paths and methods to develop leadership skills that everyone possesses.

Ultimately, whether we can become people with leadership qualities, and whether we can become people with many willing followers striving together to achieve great dreams, depends on each of our insights and actions.
