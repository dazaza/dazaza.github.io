www.ra9988.tv China Women's Table Tennis Team Sweeps South Korea to Advance to the Finals

by qyoz8899 on 2012-02-25 01:54:54

February 25th, Beijing time, the 2012 Table Tennis Asian Championships continued in Macau. The Chinese team defeated the Indian and South Korean teams with two 3-0 victories to advance to the finals of the women's team competition. They will compete against their old rivals, the Singaporean team, for the championship. The Taipei and Japanese teams occupied the last two semi-final spots in the men's team competition, with the Taipei team set to face the Chinese team in the semi-finals.

The women's team championship group underwent two rounds of competition. In the quarter-finals, the Chinese team sent out Ding Ning, Guo Yan, and Guo Yue, who easily defeated their Indian opponents with three 3-0 victories, allowing the Chinese team to advance to the semi-finals with a 3-0 sweep over India.

In the quarter-finals, the world champion Singaporean team faced off against the North Korean team. Wang Yuegu defeated Kim Jong 3-0, Feng Tianwei then beat Kim Hoesung 3-0 in the second match. Li Jiawei secured the third victory by defeating Ri Myong Sun 3-1, allowing the Singaporean team to advance with a 3-0 sweep.

The South Korean team encountered some difficulties. In their match against the Taipei team, Tang Na secured the first point for the South Korean team, but Shi Lei and Liang Xiayin lost to Lee Yizhen and Chen Siyu respectively, putting the South Korean team behind 1-2. In a must-win situation, Shi Lei and Tang Na won the last two matches, allowing the South Korean team to reverse the game and eliminate the Taipei team 3-2.

In the semi-final that followed, the Chinese team faced the South Korean team. The Chinese team adjusted its lineup, sending out Guo Yan, Ding Ning, and Liu Shiwen. Guo Yan easily defeated Shi Lei in straight sets (11-3, 11-4, 11-9), followed by Ding Ning also winning 3-0 (11-3, 11-9, 11-8) against Tang Na. Liu Shiwen then defeated Liang Xiayin 3-0 in the third match, allowing the Chinese team to win 3-0 and advance to the finals.

In the other semi-final, the Singaporean team faced the Japanese team. Feng Tianwei secured the opening victory for her team, defeating Kasumi Ishikawa 3-1 (9-11, 11-8, 11-5, 11-7). In the second match, Ai Fukuhara avenged her loss to Mengyu Yu at the Kuwait Open, narrowly winning 3-2 (8-11, 11-4, 9-11, 11-2, 11-7), leveling the score for the Japanese team at 1-1. In the third match, Saori Hirano and Li Jiawei battled it out for five games, with Hirano eventually winning 3-2 (13-11, 7-11, 11-9, 4-11, 11-3), allowing the Japanese team to take the lead at 2-1.

Feng Tianwei stabilized the situation in the fourth match, defeating Ai Fukuhara 3-1 (11-9, 7-11, 11-6, 11-9). In the final match, Mengyu Yu narrowly defeated Kasumi Ishikawa 3-2, with the scores being 11-7, 4-11, 8-11, 11-2, 11-9, allowing the Singaporean team to reverse the game and win 3-2 to advance to the finals. They will compete against the Chinese team for the championship of the women's team at the Asian Championships.

In the last two quarter-finals of the men's team championship group, Huang Shengsheng, Wu Zhiji, and Chen Jian'an each contributed a victory, allowing the Taipei team to narrowly defeat the Indian team 3-2. Japan's Dan羽Xiaoxi, Mizutani Jun, and Matsudaira Kentaro each secured a victory, helping their team to sweep the Singaporean team 3-0. In the semi-finals, the Chinese team will face the Taipei team, while the Japanese team will face the South Korean team.
