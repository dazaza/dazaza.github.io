It's normal to encounter various problems when dealing with people.

by hartyqekb on 2009-11-25 17:45:32

Almost all people who have been through the renovation process share one viewpoint: renovation is a project filled with regrets. No matter how beautifully or perfectly a home is renovated, there will always be some minor regrets. While it's nearly impossible to completely eliminate these regrets, minimizing them is certainly achievable. Therefore, before starting any renovation, it's crucial to make adequate preparations so that we can transform our small nests into happy, warm, and complete homes.

Most people who have experienced home renovations often talk endlessly about their experiences. Observant individuals will notice that among the endless topics, there are not only proud accomplishments in their design but also plenty of "bitter complaints" from the renovation process. For many homeowners who have just purchased new homes and received their keys, the idea of renovating brings more excitement and novelty, while they overlook the difficulty and complexity of the renovation project. Thus, before beginning the renovation, one must not take it lightly, assuming it's an easy task. It's important to have sufficient mental preparation to face the various difficulties and problems that may arise during the renovation. Only then can one effectively deal with issues as they come up. Homeowners about to renovate might ask themselves the following questions:

1. How much do I know about the renovation process?

2. Is my knowledge of purchasing and installing building materials and furniture sufficient?

3. In the process of buying building materials and furniture, I may encounter price traps, quality traps, and promotional traps set by merchants. Do I have enough strategies to handle these situations?

4. If I'm dissatisfied with the renovation design, can I start over once construction has already begun?

5. What should I do if the renovation company delays the schedule?

6. Will rainy or extremely cold weather affect the quality of construction?

7. Which parts of the construction process on-site require special attention to ensure workers don't cut corners?

8. What should I do if workers slack off or try to cheat? How should I interact with them?

9. How much do I understand about hidden projects in the renovation process?

Such questions are countless. After reading these questions, perhaps those eager to jump into the renovation won’t feel so relaxed anymore. However, recognizing the problems and challenges doesn't mean we should fear them and hesitate to move forward. In short, while we should view the overall strategy with contempt, mentally we must take it seriously. With sufficient psychological preparation, let’s begin to renovate our own homes. "Highly value, fully prepare, correct attitude, cautiously prevent" are my sixteen-word guidelines for renovation.

★ Highly Value: Chairman Mao once said, “Strategically despise the enemy, tactically take the enemy seriously.” This saying also applies to home renovation. Only by giving enough importance to the renovation can you seriously study and recognize the various issues you might encounter in detail.

★ Fully Prepare: Having sufficient respect is not enough; you need to prepare thoroughly for the renovation from all aspects. If your mind is preoccupied with renovation details whether you're eating, walking, or sleeping, then congratulations—you've entered the initial state. Of course, don't go too far—excess is as bad as deficiency. Losing sleep over renovation would be counterproductive.

★ Correct Attitude: Everyone who has gone through a renovation understands this: no matter how well-planned or thought out your renovation is, problems will still arise, and you will still encounter headaches and troubles. On this point, it’s essential to maintain the right mindset. Don't assume that everything you planned beforehand will work out perfectly. During the renovation process, there will always be things you haven't considered because you're dealing not only with materials but also with people who directly influence your renovation outcome. Issues arising from human interactions are normal, so keep the right attitude—face each challenge as it comes. Complaining is useless; solving problems is the best way to handle renovation troubles!

★ Cautiously Prevent: During the renovation process, pitfalls can appear at any moment and in any place. These pitfalls start even in the preparation phase. For instance, relying solely on one opinion found online could lead you into a misconception because what suits others may not suit you. The design phase also has its pitfalls; free designs may not be as tasty as expected and could even be distasteful. Buying materials, dealing with unlicensed contractors, or having construction teams cut corners on-site—all these areas are fraught with potential traps. Therefore, caution is necessary. You must not harbor harmful intentions toward others, but neither should you neglect vigilance!
