According to the Ministry of Commerce's forecast, the total transaction volume of e-commerce in 2010 will exceed 4 trillion yuan. Especially in online shopping, the market transaction scale reached 350 billion yuan in the first three quarters of this year, increasing more than double compared to the same period last year. According to the "Business Blue Paper" released by the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, the annual transaction volume of online shopping in 2010 is expected to reach 50 billion yuan, accounting for approximately 3% of the total retail sales of social goods for the whole year.

The scale of online shoppers continued to expand in 2010, with 38% of people completing at least one online purchase per month; the proportion of people who have never shopped online has dropped from 20% in 2007 to 12%; high-income groups are the main force of online shopping, and among those earning over 100,000 USD annually, 34% complete at least three online purchases per month. It is predicted that the total scale of the global e-commerce market will reach 680 billion USD in 2011, an increase of 18.9% year-on-year; this figure will reach 963 billion USD in 2013.

While online shopping malls continue to grow vigorously, comparison shopping websites, as the entry point for online shopping searches, are quietly emerging. Some forward-thinking entrepreneurs have begun to choose the concept of "comparison shopping" and entered the hot Chinese online shopping market.

Comparison Shopping

Comparison shopping is a subcategory of search engines, specifically a professional vertical search engine in the field of online shopping. The original idea of comparison shopping was to provide consumers with comparison services for product prices, merchant credibility, product performance, and shopping convenience from various online retailers across different stores.

Comparison shopping websites can bring convenience to consumers shopping in the complex online world, allowing them to understand the cheapest product information in the shortest time possible. They list the selling prices of the same product on different websites and inform you which website offers the lowest price. They also notify you promptly about discount information on certain websites. You can download discount coupons from these shopping comparison websites and use them when shopping on corresponding websites, greatly facilitating the lives of online shoppers. For those good at financial management, it saves both time and money.

Analysis of the Foreign Comparison Shopping Market

The foreign comparison shopping industry is currently in a relatively mature stage, developing rapidly. Comparison shopping has become a necessary tool for foreign online shoppers and an indispensable partner for B2C merchants. According to surveys of internet users in the United States, 68% of users habitually check comparison shopping websites before shopping on B2C sites. Searching for products and comparing prices and merchants on comparison shopping websites, and then purchasing from the satisfactory merchant in the search results, has become a mandatory process for many buyers when shopping online.

Development of Comparison Shopping in China

Domestic comparison shopping websites first appeared in 1996, but due to the winter of the internet economy at the time, comparison shopping was shelved. It wasn't until 2004 that comparison shopping websites began to emerge with the popularization of the internet. Due to technical difficulties, complex product classification, and low user awareness, the comparison shopping market has not shown remarkable performance.

The domestic comparison shopping sector is still in its infancy, exploring and trying out. The domestic market has not yet formed a scale, but with the rapid development of e-commerce, more and more online shoppers have started to realize the convenience and practicality brought by comparison shopping. After adapting to the environment and combining with the characteristics of the Chinese market, Chinese comparison shopping is beginning to be accepted by more and more users.

According to iResearch data analysis in 2010, there are nearly 30 specialized comparison shopping websites domestically, and the number of users using comparison shopping websites has exceeded 800,000. Xungou.com, a leading domestic comparison shopping rebate website, has developed rapidly, capturing nearly 50% of the comparison shopping market share. It is reported that Xungou.com officially launched in September 2009, with monthly sales exceeding 10 million yuan and over 100,000 new users added each month.

The success of Xungou.com can be attributed to its sensitivity to the domestic e-commerce market and its accurate grasp of Chinese netizens' purchasing habits. By combining the single comparison shopping model, which was not suitable for the domestic market, with shopping rebate models and group-buying guide models, it quickly adapted to the Chinese market, continuously grew, and attracted more and more users.

Outlook for 2011

It is reported that in 2011, Baidu will make a major move into the e-commerce market, strongly supporting its comparison shopping community. The launch of Google's Chinese version of shopping search and its efforts to return to the Chinese market all indicate that giants have noticed the huge potential and broad prospects of comparison shopping.

The broad market prospects and unpredictable user benefits have prompted domestic pioneers to begin learning from successful comparison shopping websites in Europe and America, focusing on improving search technology, perfecting product and price information, and enhancing user experience. Striving to establish a firm position in the domestic comparison shopping search field is key to the initial success of Xungou.com, the largest domestic comparison shopping website, ahead of the giants. These are essential tasks for domestic comparison shopping to succeed.

Although issues such as the practical experience of comparison shopping in China and technical difficulties currently hinder its development, with the future maturity of e-commerce, the online sales of large offline malls like Carrefour and Suning, and the close integration of online and offline shopping, we look forward to and are optimistic about the infinite potential of comparison shopping and the emerging types of products derived from it, including Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd. We also hope to see outstanding performances from comparison shopping in 2011. (Editor-in-Charge: Zhang Chong)
