**Hong Kong Company Establishment Set (Including Company Secretary and Registered Address)**

The Hong Kong company establishment set is designed for non-Hong Kong residents or investors who are unable or unwilling to provide a company secretary and registered address. It suits investors who cannot appoint a local Hong Kong resident as the company secretary or provide a Hong Kong address as the registered office.

**One, New Hong Kong Company Establishment**

1. **Pre and Post-Establishment Procedures**

1. Confirm that the company name is not already registered.

2. Prepare the company's articles of association and other relevant documents.

3. Prepare the forms required for registration and submission.

4. Our registration service fee and government registration fees (Hong Kong Companies Registry and Business Registration Office).

5. Certificate of Incorporation.

6. One-year valid Business Registration Certificate (Renewal request will be mailed before expiration).

7. Create a company kit including the company seal, share certificates, register of members, register of directors, and minutes book.

8. Minutes of the first board meeting (or written resolution of the board).

9. Assistance in opening a company bank account and preparing the necessary meeting records (or resolutions).

2. **Provision of Company Secretary Services**

According to the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance, every company established in Hong Kong must appoint a company secretary to assume legal responsibilities. Our company can provide secretary services, which include:

* Compliance with the provisions of the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance and the Companies Registry.

* Serving as the company secretary for one year.

* Updating the statutory registers and handling shareholder and board meetings.

3. **Provision of Company Registered Address**

Pre-establishment procedures are included in this package.

**Two, Structure for Establishing a Hong Kong Private Limited Liability Company**

* A minimum of one shareholder, one director, and one company secretary is required.

* Shareholders can be either a legal entity or an individual, regardless of nationality.

* Shareholders can also serve as directors.

* The company secretary must be either a Hong Kong resident or a company established in Hong Kong (Our company can act as the company secretary).

* If the company has only one director and one shareholder, the director or shareholder cannot simultaneously serve as the company secretary.

* The minimum issued capital is HKD 1.

* The registered address must be in Hong Kong (Our company can provide this service).

**Three, Procedure for Establishing a New Hong Kong Company**

1. Clients provide their desired company name, and we conduct a search for similar names before proceeding with the establishment process, which typically takes about six business days from the date we receive the client’s application documents. For urgent cases, we can arrange the purchase of a shelf company.

2. Unless otherwise specified, all Hong Kong companies are private limited liability companies (similar to stock corporations) based on the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance.

**Step One**

Clients provide their preferred company name, and we check with the Hong Kong Companies Registry to confirm if the name is available. Names can be registered in both Chinese and English or a combination of both. However, the desired name cannot be identical or similar to any existing registered company names. Simultaneously, clients should send the following incorporation documents via email, fax, or mail (download the Hong Kong company registration form, complete it, and return it via email or fax). Payment for the service fee is required.

**Step Two**

Once the availability of the desired company name is confirmed, we prepare the company's articles of association and the following documents:

1. Appointment letter for the initial director.

2. Notification of appointment for the initial director and secretary.

3. Consent to act as director.

4. Notification of the registered office address.

5. Other files.

A company’s articles of association must be prepared in both Chinese and English, signed by the incorporators in the presence of a notary public. The articles must include basic details such as the company name, registered office address (which must be in Hong Kong), the company's purpose, the limited liability of members, and the proposed registered share capital and细分 number of shares.

**Step Three**

We send the documents prepared in Step Two to the client. Upon receipt, the client signs the designated sections and returns them to us. Alternatively, the client can sign the documents at our respective offices.

**Step Four**

We submit the intended articles of association to the Hong Kong Companies Registry and pay the registration fee. After reviewing the documents, the registry usually issues the Certificate of Incorporation within about four business days if there are no issues with the application.

**Step Five**

The Hong Kong Companies Registry issues the Certificate of Incorporation. Simultaneously, the incorporation documents prepared in Step Two are submitted.

**Step Six**

After receiving the Certificate of Incorporation, the following steps are carried out:

1. Preparation of the company's articles of association.

2. Ordering a seal with the company name for signing contracts.

3. Ordering a rubber stamp with the company name and "Representing the Company" for use when the company top management signs contracts on behalf of the company.

4. Ordering a statutory register for recording directors, secretaries, members, and meeting minutes.

5. Issuing share certificates to shareholders.

6. Holding the first board meeting to handle various matters related to the establishment of the company, such as leasing office premises and opening bank accounts.

**Step Seven**

We apply for and receive the Business Registration Certificate from the Hong Kong Business Registration Office and pay the business registration fee (HKD 2600.00 for the current year).

**Step Eight**

Upon completion of all procedures, we mail the incorporation documents to the client or the client can collect them from our office.

**Four, Required Documents and Information for Establishing a Hong Kong Company**

1. Desired company name: Provide 2-3 options. The company name can be registered in both Chinese and English or a combination of both.

2. Copy of the shareholder's identity card (for Hong Kong residents) or passport (for non-Hong Kong residents) and address. If the shareholder is a legal entity, provide the company registration certificate and address.

3. Copy of the director's identity card (for Hong Kong residents) or passport (for non-Hong Kong residents) and address. If the director is a legal entity, provide the company registration certificate and address.

4. Copy of the company secretary's identity card and address (if not using our company secretary service). If the secretary is a legal entity, provide the company registration certificate and registered address.

5. Registered office address (if not using our registered address service).

6. Statutory capital, issued capital, and share allocation ratio. If no special instructions are given, the registered capital is HKD 10,000.

7. One notary public for the company's articles of association. When shareholders sign the articles, a third party notary (aged 18 or above) is required.

**Five, Documents Received After Establishing a Hong Kong Company**

Upon completion of the incorporation procedures, we mail the following documents to the client (to be submitted to the bank for opening a bank account):

1. Certificate of Incorporation.

2. Business Registration Certificate.

3. 20 copies of the company's articles of association.

4. Seal with the company name for signing contracts.

5. Rubber stamp with the company name and "Representing the Company" for use by the company top management when signing contracts.

6. Statutory register containing records of directors, secretaries, members, and meeting minutes.

7. 20 blank share certificates.

8. Appointment letters for the first directors, secretary, and notification of the registered office address.

**Six, Important Notes**

1. The desired company name cannot be identical or similar to any existing registered names.

2. A minimum of one shareholder, one director, and one company secretary is required.

3. Shareholders or directors can be of any nationality or from any country. However, the company secretary must be either a Hong Kong resident or a limited company established in Hong Kong. Our company provides company secretary services and other related services.

4. The registered office address must be in Hong Kong. If no office is rented or another address is unavailable, our company's address can be used as the registered office address. Similarly, our registration set can be selected.

5. The registered capital of shelf companies sold by our company is generally HKD 10,000 with issued capital of HKD 1 (assuming one shareholder). Additional capital increase and issuance can be provided by our company.

6. If the registered capital exceeds HKD 10,000, an additional registration fee of 0.1% is required.

7. Free courier fees for documents within Hong Kong; additional charges apply for areas outside Hong Kong.
