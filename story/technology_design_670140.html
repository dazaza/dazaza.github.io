In 1887, Yamaha was founded by Torakusu Yamaha, who made the first reed organ.

In 1954, they established a music education system and produced the first Hi-Fi player.

In 1986, the Yamaha Digital Sound Field Processor DSP-1 was born.

In December 2010, the R&D personnel of Shenzhen ETON (Yitong) Technology adopted the globally top-class Yamaha D-class music amplifier to develop the P256 grey-market phone with mellow and full-bodied tone. Its internal circuit design uses the "Pure Pulse Direct Speaker Driver Circuit" to directly drive the speaker, minimizing noise introduced during the amplification of music signals, achieving the highest standards of distortion rate and low-noise characteristics.

Considering consumers' strict pursuit of sound quality, the P256 uses Yamaha's patented NonClip anti-clipping technology, reducing pulse signal distortion rates, lowering noise, ensuring that no clipping or distortion occurs when the music player is used for external playback, providing excellent volume and sound quality during external playback.

At the same time, the A-Box super-large speaker design with an independent sound chamber ensures loud and high-quality external sound quality, guaranteeing a full sound field in the low-frequency region and clear, bright results in the high-frequency region; while the low-frequency unit uses a high-quality composite membrane speaker, allowing users to fully enjoy the vibration effects of heavy bass.

To make the concert hall effect more realistic and achieve unprecedented presence, the P256 newly added Yamaha amplifier sound effects, achieving twice the output effect of the original audio, amplifying the audio output to twice its original size without affecting sound quality, while retaining surround sound effects and various environmental effects. The exclusive YPAO (Yamaha Parametric Acoustic Optimizer) brings the texture and rich layers of heavy bass.

The P256 considers the user comfort of consumers, carefully selecting soft, high-end materials and ergonomic shell structures, fitting more closely to your eardrum, reproducing a stereo sound field effect, giving you an immersive listening experience.
