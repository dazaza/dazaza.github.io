Whisky (Whisky) comes from the ancient Scottish language, meaning "water of life." Therefore, Scotland is generally regarded as the birthplace of all whiskies. The earliest written record of Scottish whisky was in 1494 when John·Cor purchased eight sieves of malt and produced 35 boxes of whisky. Of course, it can be confirmed that the birth of whisky far predates 1494. Between 1534-1535, Protestant reformers from England destroyed almost all monasteries. They quickly developed this skill, and at that time, whisky was mainly used as a potion to ward off severe cold.
