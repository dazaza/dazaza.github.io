The defense stated that after the incident, Song Aning and his family could actively sell their assets to compensate for the losses, hoping that the court would impose a lighter punishment on him, including debt recovery.

At 9:30 yesterday morning, Song Aning, a 28-year-old man from Liaoning, was brought into the courtroom.

Song Aning said that although his lowest authority was 6.2% off, in reality, he never gave such discounts to other customers like Xinhua Bookstore. Customers like Zhang found him online themselves without any introductions. Moreover, they had particularly high sales volume and were especially convenient to take advantage of cheap prices.

According to reports (by reporter Sun Siya), yesterday morning, Song Aning, the distribution manager of Social Sciences Academic Press, appeared in court at the First Intermediate People's Court of the city for trial due to selling books worth over 8.6 million yuan at 3.1 million yuan and embezzling the proceeds from book sales.

Song Aning expressed no objection to the charges and said that his main responsibility was to distribute books to the sales department. His jurisdiction mainly covered Beijing, Hebei, and Tianjin areas. In Beijing, the main business partners with Song Aning were Xinhua Bookstore and some wholesalers. He distributed the publisher's books to the bookstore at a certain price, and the publisher granted him a 6.2% discount.

The prosecutor suggested in court that Song Aning be sentenced to more than 10 years in prison or life imprisonment. A verdict will be announced on another day.

Song Aning said that Zhang and others were all his clients. To make a profit, he arbitrarily increased the discount rate and sold them at about 30% off. When Zhang and others took the receipts to pick up the books, Song Aning told the warehouse staff that these people were from Xinhua Bookstore. Song claimed that he used Xinhua Bookstore's name because it was a big customer with a good reputation, making it less likely to be investigated, which facilitated his crime. After Zhang and others picked up the books, they transferred the book purchase money into Song Aning's account. Subsequently, Song used other methods to balance the accounts. These illegally obtained book purchase funds were all used by Song Aning for personal consumption.

The prosecutor read the indictment, stating that between July 2007 and August 2010, Song Aning, using his position as the issuing principal of the distribution department of China Social Sciences Press, created some false "issued but not paid statistics tables,"擅自 sold books worth over 8.6 million yuan to Zhang, Liu, and others at prices significantly lower than the discount specified by the press. The illicit proceeds amounting to 3.1 million yuan were kept by Song Aning.
