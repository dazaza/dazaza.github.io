At the 2012 National Two Sessions, food safety once again became one of the top ten issues most concerned about by the public. How can we ensure that ordinary people eat safely and with peace of mind? For this reason, our newspaper conducted exclusive interviews with national political advisory committee members Wen Sumei and Cheng Ping, as well as academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering Chen Junshi, to interpret the root causes of food safety issues and response measures.

Key Issue: Numerous Gaps in the Industry Chain

Food safety has long been a key focus of government efforts. But why do food safety incidents keep happening? Wen Sumei believes this is determined by the food industry chain. He said that with economic development and deepening, the food industry chain has extended, correspondingly creating more vulnerabilities. Problems can occur at any stage—production, transportation, processing, or sales. Additionally, he thinks that an imperfect market leading to "bad money driving out good" is also one of the reasons.

Chen Junshi believes that China's agricultural production has not yet reached a scale of standardized production; scientific knowledge levels are insufficient, and the non-scientific use of fertilizers is objectively present. Driven by market economy interests, some people incite farmers to produce illegally. We have over 500,000 small and medium-sized food production enterprises, many of which are small producers. The hygiene conditions and microbial contamination indicators of these enterprises always have a part that does not meet standards.

Contradiction: The More Effective the Supervision, the More Issues?

The state’s supervision and rectification of food safety has been increasing year by year, yet the public feels that food safety issues have become more frequent. What is the reason for this? Chen Junshi explained that the main reason why food safety issues appear numerous in China is because many issues not related to food safety are equated with food safety issues, such as the "colored steamed bun" issue, which is actually a counterfeit and substandard product problem.

He added that another reason is the heightened awareness of food safety among consumers and the media, along with the exposure of problems that existed for a long time but were not discovered before, such as the Shuanghui "clenbuterol" incident, which had a focusing effect within a short period of time.

Solution: Adjusting Agricultural Production Structure

Chen Junshi believes that the fundamental solution to food safety lies in adjusting the agricultural production structure. Agricultural production needs to be on a larger scale, with standardized production and rules. The production of edible agricultural products should have a certain standardization, with corresponding norms for pesticide and fertilizer usage. However, it will take some time to regulate scattered farmer production. He emphasized that safe food is produced, not regulated or inspected into existence. Therefore, solving potential hazards at the production source is the key to addressing the problem.

Wen Sumei emphasized that solving food safety requires focusing on the management of the entire process, all links, and multiple nodes, establishing corresponding standards and traceability systems. If there is a problem in any link during pre-production, mid-production, or post-production, penalties should be applied to that specific link, leaving no place for illegal behavior to hide.

Proposal: Be a Rational Consumer

While the state and industry are making efforts, ordinary people can also start from small steps to prevent unsafe food. To this end, Chen Junshi proposed first, diversifying food consumption. Second, buying branded food is more reassuring. Third, learning a bit of scientific knowledge to enhance self-protection.

Wen Sumei stressed that one should be a rational consumer, learn more about food safety knowledge, avoid food without a "birth certificate," and buy food from large supermarkets. Their cost of violating laws is higher, so the probability of selling unsafe food is smaller. In addition, he suggested that people further increase their rights protection awareness, regardless of the cost, dare to report, crack down on counterfeit and substandard goods, and show no tolerance for illegal merchants.

Dialogue with Political Advisory Committee Member Cheng Ping:

99% Safety Rate for Livestock Products in Guangdong Province

National Political Advisory Committee member and Deputy Director of the Guangdong Provincial Department of Agriculture, Cheng Ping, stated in an interview with our newspaper that the department has made a series of efforts and innovations in controlling the source of food production and the testing system, ensuring 99% safety for livestock products and 90%-95% safety for vegetable products. The quality and safety of agricultural products are gradually improving.

Reporter: What measures has Guangdong Province taken to address food safety issues?

Cheng Ping: Safe food is produced, and ensuring the safety of the source of agricultural products is crucial. Regarding the inputs used in producing agricultural products, such as fertilizers, pesticides, and feed, we have carried out relevant registration and testing. We also provide training to farmers, guiding them to scientifically plant, thereby strengthening the safety of the food source. Secondly, we strengthen control at each link, optimize the production environment, promote production bases and farmers' cooperatives, encouraging scaled and standardized production. Thirdly, we further improve the regulatory system, standardize market entry mechanisms, implement labeling management, and conduct regular or random inspections of enterprises and production bases from the provincial level down to the county level. There are also special agricultural product tests.

Reporter: What innovative points has Guangdong Province implemented in solving food safety issues?

Cheng Ping: We were pioneers in establishing labeling management, focusing on "three products and one mark + brand name products." The three products refer to green food, organic food, and pollution-free food, while the one mark refers to geographical indications. We carry out special rectifications for these items, starting from these brand-name products, reinforcing labeling management, establishing a linkage mechanism, and once the leaders in the food industry are handled well, other problems will naturally be resolved.

Additionally, we have early on achieved informatization management of the testing system, effectively monitoring food safety information across the province. At the same time, we attach great importance to the training of testing teams, establishing two provincial expert groups for emergency response and experts on agricultural products, providing scientific advice to solve agricultural product safety hazards. This also includes training for expert teams below the provincial level.

Reporters: Li Hua, Li Dong, Ren Chaoliang
