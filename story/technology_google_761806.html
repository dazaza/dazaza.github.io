Google Glass was bad, and it will continue to be bad.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 19:02:32

The craze for Google Glass has already begun, and it is unlikely to subside until the mass production version is officially launched. From its debut, developer edition pre-orders to media reviews, praise and anticipation for Google Glass far outweigh concerns, which currently seem limited to functional aspects or privacy issues. Recently, VentureBeat published an article that poured cold water on the heated Google Glass phenomenon.As a Google Glass owner, I quickly realized that this device could be very useful for many people, such as doctors, mechanical engineers, or others who need to work in the field. However, the author also states that for ordinary people, Google Glass only makes one look more like a geek and further estranges us from real-world environments and social relationships.The author was among the first to preorder Google Glass at the 2012 Google I/O Conference and gave it as a gift to her tech-enthusiast husband. The problem was that even he found Google Glass difficult to use. When he excitedly wore the glasses and stepped out of the house, he immediately stepped on dog poop—Google Glass made him too distracted from the world outside the screen. Subsequently, he refused to wear it on public transportation: he feared being robbed.Currently, there are not many applications available for Google Glass, the operations are unintuitive and full of bugs. Additionally, it can do very little, such as taking low-quality photos or shouting keywords to search on Google. However, perhaps after some time, it might be able to upload videos, dictate long texts, or play social games, but by then, it will have become a gateway to an endless labyrinth of digital distraction.Indeed, in tech news, we will focus on each day when new apps and features for Google Glass emerge. We will report on novel use cases, like who writes the first novel or screenplay using Google Glass, or which young woman uses it to make a movie.Sometimes, we may also write about someone crashing their car because they were wearing Google Glass, or which school bans students from wearing it during class, or someone accidentally harming their baby while engrossed in playing with the glasses. In fact, some articles have exaggerated how these digital devices cause so-called alienation in human relationships, yet your smartphone or headphones can just as easily make you ignore everything around you.In reality, Google Glass won't change the habits of ordinary people in consuming information or managing their digital lives; its effects are similar to those of smartphones, possibly slightly better or worse. However, for specialized workers, such as airplane engine mechanics or deep-sea divers, a hands-free manual floating in front of their eyes could be extremely helpful.Technology is merely a tool, not an end goal. We have become immersed in the enjoyment brought by technology rather than using technology as a tool with caution. In the author's view, Google Glass will only interfere with our lives without providing any substantial help. Take Google Maps, for example, having navigation right in front of your eyes isn't more convenient than on your phone, nor is it easier when you post on Weibo, update social networks, or upload photos.The author concludes that Google Glass indeed changes the game, but perhaps in the worst possible way. If you simply play with it, it is interesting but offers no substantial help, while occupying your real life. It could even be the reason you step on dog poop, irritate your girlfriend, or lose your baby for the first time. If it's not needed for work, forget about Google Glass.After reading the author's complaints, what do you think? I haven't used Google Glass yet, but I must admit I am deeply attracted to it because it's cool. What is cool? Google Glass can record my life from a first-person perspective, I can summon a digital assistant in front of my eyes like in sci-fi movies, and it makes me look super geeky with the glasses on.I remain curious about Google Glass, and I will definitely buy one once it goes into mass production. But now I have slight doubts about its usability, doubting whether it serves any purpose other than being a first-person camera. What do you think?
