The Fate Disparity of Google's First Batch of 21 Employees

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 20:05:28

Apart from Larry Page and Sergey Brin, only seven of the 21 initial employees at Google remain with the company. Among these early 21 employees, some have become entrepreneurs or professional managers, others are now engaged in angel investing, while a few have faded into obscurity or even retired.

Now that Google has celebrated its 15th anniversary, let's take a look at where the early employees of Google stand today.

1. Larry Page

Page has worked at Google since 1998. He is one of the co-founders and currently serves as the CEO of Google.

2. Sergey Brin

Brin joined Google in 1998 and continues to work there as one of the co-founders.

3. Craig Silverstein

Craig was among the first employees hired by Larry and Sergey, joining Google in 1998 as the human resources manager. He left in 2012 and is now working at a startup called Khan Academy.

4. Heather Cairns

Cairns joined Google in 1998 as the human resources manager and left in 2004. She is now retired and engages in angel investing.

5. Ray Sidney

Sidney joined Google in 1999 as a software engineer and left in 2003, just 13 months before Google's IPO. He is now a real estate developer.

6. Harry Cheung

Cheung joined Google in 1999 as an engineer and is now an angel investor, having invested in companies like Qwiki, Badgeville, and PrePay. He was still an engineer when he left Google.

7. Amit Patel

Patel joined Google in 1999 and later became part of Sunfire. While at Google, he co-developed the company motto "Don't be evil" with Paul Buchheit. His departure date is unknown.

8. Urs Holzle

Holzle joined Google in 1999 and continues to work there as the Senior Vice President of Technical Infrastructure.

9. Georges Harik

Harik joined Google in 1999 as a software engineer and resigned in 2005. He is now an angel investor.

10. Salar Kamangar

Kamangar joined Google in 1999 and remains there as the CEO of YouTube and Senior Vice President overseeing video services.

11. Omid Kordestani

Omid joined Google in January 1999 and continues to serve as a Senior Advisor to the CEO and Founders. He also made it onto the Forbes list of billionaires.

12. Steve Schimmel

Schimmel joined Google in 1999 and left in 2004 after working in business development. He is now an angel investor.

13. Rachael Chambers

Chambers joined Google in May 1999 as an executive assistant and left in October 2000 as an ad sales coordinator. She is now a project manager at Cisco.

14. Chris Skarakis

Skarakis joined Google in May 1999 and left in May 2005 as the manager of business development. He is now the founder of Blip.fm and a board member of the Kanye West Foundation.

15. Joan Braddi

Braddi joined Google in 1999 and continues to work there as the Vice President responsible for partnerships. She previously served as the Vice President of search services.

16. Susan Wojcicki

Wojcicki joined Google in April 1999 and continues to serve as the Senior Vice President overseeing product management.

17. Gerald Aigner

Aigner joined Google in 1999 and managed supply costs. He left in 2006 and is now an "Internet professional" based in London. Before leaving, he was one of Google's executives.

18. Jim Reese

Reese joined Google in 1999 and served as the Chief Operating Engineer before leaving. His current status is unknown.

19. Larry Schwimmer

Schwimmer initially joined Google as a software engineer and helped develop the Snippets system used to manage employees during the company’s rapid growth phase. He joined Google in 1999 but his exact departure date is unknown. Before leaving, he mainly worked as a software engineer. His current status is unknown.

20. Kendra DiGirolamo

Kendra initially joined Google as an ad sales coordinator, left the company, but returned three years later when Google went public. She worked at Google from June 1999 to May 2001 and is now a business analyst at Driscoll.

21. Marissa Mayer

Mayer initially joined Google as a software engineer and is now the CEO of Yahoo! She worked at Google from June 1999 to July 2012, serving as Vice President responsible for localization, maps, and location services before her departure.
