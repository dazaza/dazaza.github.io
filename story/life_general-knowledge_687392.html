Who can bear the mood of whom?

She and she met on the Internet, which is almost five years so far. In many days, they just chat when they have nothing to do, joke around when they have no business, and talk about their moods. As the days go by, her mood is sometimes good and sometimes bad. Unconsciously, she confides in him, and he comforts her like a next-door older brother, accompanying her through one琐碎and messy day after another. Gradually, she got used to having him around, and he probably got used to listening to her vent.

After a very long time, one day, he suddenly told her that he liked her. After hearing this, she laughed happily. But she thought it was just a joke between them, and didn't take it to heart at all. For a long time afterward, he still did not reduce his enthusiasm, joking around but not losing sincerity. Occasionally, she felt that if she lost him, her life seemed to be missing something. Maybe she liked him too. She wasn't sure. He still joked with her, laughing at her for being timid and not daring to admit some facts.

Over the years, she learned a little about his life, and he also learned a little about hers. He was happy, with a lovely son at home, and also adopted a homeless daughter. For this, his family appeared on TV programs. He had no power or influence, and was easygoing. His broad-mindedness made her feel so vast. As the days went by, their online friendship continued. He often wanted to meet her, but she only gave him a photo. Although she often went to his city, she did not see him. Better not to meet than to meet; what would meeting accomplish?

She still met him online, and occasionally called him. But if she didn't see him for a long time, he said he started feeling uncomfortable. She just smiled. She knew that sometimes she felt the same way. She couldn't explain it, thinking he should be her good older brother. But he didn't want things to stay that way, yet he couldn't do anything about her.

Her mood still fluctuated between good and bad. Her琐碎days were never peaceful. Her home was still filled with love, but often carried a sense of conflict. Some things might be out of habit. Habits become unchangeable, slowly turning into nature.

One day, she encountered some trouble and became extremely sad. When she returned home, her family was full of hostility, offering no comfort whatsoever, but instead adding fuel to the fire. She thought of him, went online, and found him there. She didn't say anything, just sent an expression of extreme sadness. He replied, "What's wrong?" She remained silent, then told him she was extremely upset. He asked what happened? She paused for two minutes, wanting to tell him what happened. But as she sent the message, she saw his avatar go dark. She thought he must have gone invisible, thinking she had left. She sent a text message back, the content just a "?" Soon, he replied, "The boss lady has returned!"

Yes, she suddenly realized, what was she? A sister who couldn't be seen in the sunlight. Although there had been nothing inappropriate between them, in her extremely low moment, he couldn't come to accompany her and comfort her. The ones who consoled her were those strangers online, occasional chatters, or online friends she had known for a long time but interacted with lightly. In her heart, she thanked them for giving her all the help they could during her low moments. Although these verbal expressions couldn't solve practical problems, they were enough to soothe her lonely mood.

She knew she was creating troubles for herself. There were some things she couldn't change, and the only thing she could change was her own mood. She should open up more, see more brightness in life, and let herself bear her own mood!
