The most popular and humble online -- Miss Accord

by zhaoyue0438 on 2008-02-06 11:36:32

In 2006, the first appearance of "Accord Girl" was on August 9, when she uploaded a video titled "Arrogant White-Collar Beauty's Self-Narration: I Drive a Honda and Smash Your Crappy Car." In the video, she claimed to be a "noble, tasteful senior white-collar worker," and she drove a "luxurious, noble Honda Accord car." One day, a QQ car overtook her, so she stopped the QQ car and smashed it.

This incident caused a huge backlash from many netizens who quickly criticized this woman. Netizens gave her a nickname - "Accord Girl." Although she had already angered the public, "Accord Girl" later released four more videos. In the videos, "Accord Girl" continued to speak in an arrogant tone, claiming how noble and wealthy she was, while looking down on the poor. On September 19, "Accord Girl" again released a video titled "People with a Salary of Less than Three Thousand Yuan Are Low-Class People."

This video subsequently triggered almost the entire internet's "condemnation" of "Accord Girl." On the "Qing Entertainment" website, the views of posts about "Accord Girl" exceeded 5.8 million, with tens of thousands of netizens posting comments criticizing her. On the busiest day, more than ten thousand netizen comments were made. Additionally, quite a few netizens also used video as a way to refute "Accord Girl."

On the afternoon of the 25th, Jin Yinan, editor of the "Qing Entertainment" website, stated that after "Accord Girl" appeared, every day many netizens called them to express their anger towards "Accord Girl." On various website forums, the "Accord Girl Incident" also became a focal point for netizens. At this time, in the mouths of netizens, "Accord Girl" was labeled as a "public enemy of the Internet."

The behavior of Accord Girl is very similar to that of a female netizen who claimed to be an aristocrat on a virtual community called Tianya. Later, the so-called aristocrat was strongly criticized by someone who claimed to be a true aristocrat, the "Runan Zhou family." The Runan Zhou family aristocracy was mentioned in a video advertisement that Feng Xiaogang directed for Yahoo Star Search.
