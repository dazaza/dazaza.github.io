White Cloud Ancient Cave Memory

by lk0gamek on 2009-07-20 00:41:27

Today, this essay has finally been completed. When I was mulling over the first two or three paragraphs, it happened to be the time of the 5.12 Wenchuan Earthquake. The immense national sorrow and pain made my heart heavy and restless for a long time. Every day, I anxiously followed the news reports about the disaster relief efforts in the affected areas. As a member of the post-80s generation, I felt for the first time that Chinese people are actually much stronger than we imagined... Praying, hoping everything will get better tomorrow, and that tomorrow will be brighter...

Switching back to the topic of games, this essay is about our beloved guild - Fengzi Wuyu (Elegance Story). Our guild isn't one of the larger ones at Baiyun Gudong, as we have never exceeded a hundred members (I don't know what level we've reached now). The members' levels aren't particularly high, and more than half are middle-ranked. However, the affection I have for it goes beyond my own imagination... I can say that my growth in Xian You (Leisure) has been closely tied with the development of the guild. Fengzi Wuyu has witnessed every step of my difficult journey in leveling up. Bang Zhu (Guild Master), Honey, Xiao Ai, Shuai Guo, Sheng Lang, apprentice, PP, Xiao Ai's apprentice... and so on. Each friend I've met in the guild is the most precious wealth I've gained from playing Tian Long Ba Bu (The Legend of Condor Heroes). Every day spent in Fengzi Wuyu is an indispensable part of my happy memories.

Our wise and handsome Guild Master, called Bie Shi Zunzhe, is not someone I boast about lightly, but his golden long hair is one of the best colors I've seen in the game (even though he's a guy, it still deserves such description). The Guild Master belongs to Ming Sect and was reportedly leveled by a proxy before reaching level 65. I've always forgotten to ask him why he founded the guild, but I guess it might be related to his wish to interact more with his younger brother (our vice-guild master). When we just returned to Baiyun, the guild had only been established for a short period of time. Despite having its own territory, it was still in its primitive state, exuding a simple rural charm. The Guild Master is an IT elite (and let me share a bit of gossip: our Guild Master graduated from Tsinghua University's computer science department!). His online time is not fixed, but whenever he's online, he will surely work hard to run businesses and expand the guild's funds.

One major feature of the guild at the time was a bunch of small accounts between level ten and twenty, all named starting with "Longtao" (Chorus), like "Yuyong Longtao No.1", "Yuyong Longtao No.2", etc., etc. These were all small accounts that the Guild Master and vice-guild master worked hard to level up for the benefit of the guild ^0^. Not long after, the Guild Master discovered that I was online longer than both him and the vice-guild master. He then decisively decided to promote me from elite to vice-guild master to manage the guild. At the time, I was extremely reluctant. First, I didn't have a sense of responsibility and hated all kinds of troublesome matters. Most importantly, I was only around level forty at the time, and wearing the title of vice-guild master made me feel like I was embarrassing the guild, fearing that other guilds would laugh at us, and even more afraid that those who wanted to join would change their minds upon seeing my level. A sentence said by the Guild Master at the time had a decisive impact on how I viewed gaming. Although I don't remember his exact words, the gist was: if a guild's unity and development depend on whether the guild master's level is high or low, then such a guild lacks value... I think, when playing games, the most important mindset is not to place too much emphasis on levels and gains and losses. And as a small group in the game, the most valuable aspect of a guild is whether it is united or not...

After being reluctantly promoted to vice-guild master, the first thing I needed to do was advertise to expand the talent pool of the guild. Since I never spend RMB, luxury items like big announcements were unaffordable. All I could do was broadcast free ads in the world chat every three minutes. Although it was far less efficient compared to buying announcements, some handsome guys and beautiful girls joined out of curiosity. The most practical gain from these free ads was the guild's top contributor, Dianfeng Piaomiao~ Dianfeng has always been respectfully called Shuai Guo (Handsome Guy) by the Guild Master, and we followed suit~ The real development of the guild began after Shuai Guo arrived... A little gossip aside, Shuai Guo owns a gun that can be considered the best in Baiyun - its attributes are amazing, and its star rating even more so - six stars, identified by himself, admiration, admiration, admiration... Before, the guild only knew how to run businesses to maintain the guild's fund consumption. After Shuai Guo came, he taught everyone how to start building for the guild. Gradually, the guild's houses became sturdier, the roads smoother, and we slowly embarked on the path to prosperity~ Later, I found out that Shuai Guo was simultaneously playing four or five accounts, averaging eighty to ninety levels each. The one he played the most was his Emei account, Ye Mei, the leader of Yin Yue, which he inherited from his sister who stopped playing... Shuai Guo is very cute, but also quite fond of fighting. It's not the kind of unnecessary trouble-seeking, but if his small accounts are bullied, Shuai Guo will open a few high-level accounts to take revenge. The funniest classic case I know is when Shuai Guo impulsively decided to train a small Xian You account, happily solo grinding. Two players around level sixty came and rudely asked Shuai Guo to move. Shuai Guo refused, and they arrogantly killed his small account. Then Shuai Guo opened two high-level accounts to chase and kill them. From then on, whenever he saw them, he would definitely give them a lesson. Such people indeed deserve a lesson, hehe~ It's said that Shuai Guo has left Fengzi and returned to Yin Yue, missing Shuai Guo and Ye Mei...
