The $199 Challenger: Hands-on with the Kindle Fire

by diglog on 2011-11-20 12:30:11

After nearly two months of waiting, the annual product Kindle Fire from Amazon was finally received on the 16th. This tablet, which has been a hot topic since its launch, successfully attracted a large number of consumers with its low price of $199, solid hardware, and the great appeal of the Amazon brand. In the first month of pre-orders on Amazon (9/28-10/28), the pre-order quantity reached 500,000 units. Although the Kindle Fire is not positioned as a direct competitor to the iPad 2, such an excellent market performance is enough to make it a long-awaited strong competitor to the iPad. So, what kind of tablet has Amazon brought us? Let me open the package for you and lead you through a comprehensive look at this heavyweight product of the year.

Packaging/Appearance: As the most renowned B2C website, Amazon is known for its complete logistics and after-sales service. The packaging of the Kindle Fire naturally draws on Amazon's rich experience in logistics delivery. The package adopts a Frustration-free packaging method, where the corrugated outer packaging box is also the Kindle Fire's packaging box, and the inner core is a recyclable paper-molded protective box. The first time holding the Kindle Fire in your hands, you'll find its size quite compact. Generally, men can easily hold the Fire in one hand while using the other to operate it.

The appearance of the Kindle Fire has always been a point of contention since its release because it looks too similar to the BlackBerry PlayBook. We have reported that Quanta, the production design company for the Kindle Fire, also handled the hardware design and production of the PlayBook. Although the appearance design is not particularly outstanding, the overall manufacturing process of the machine is commendable.
