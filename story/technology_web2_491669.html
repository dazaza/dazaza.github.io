And you never really showed up at all

by thhtaoqry on 2011-09-19 18:40:41

There was just such a day, when his grandchildren sat with him before the fireplace and asked about the vast and wondrous world, that he mentioned this extraordinary period of his life. Yes, he said, I traveled around when I was young, writing in my diary about love, searching for something, what now I can no longer remember. Some things I found, others I did not. But the important thing is that I have traveled.

Suddenly, he paused, as a fleeting memory flashed through his mind. To have traveled when one is young. Time travel! Was that what the old man's words meant? Discovery lies in the search? His mouth opened several times, half-spitting, half-swallowing.

From Tuesday to today, time has been fully occupied, tired, it's not fake.

As for where the future will lead, regarding love or life and all that? Once again, I've fallen into contemplation over the passage of time.

He thought: I want to take a short rest there and think about my future and my love, even though my future is uncertain, but surely it is ahead of me. Although my love is far away, it will surely come.

Thinking of this, the boy happily walked towards the tree, but only when he got close did he discover that the shade had already been occupied by an elderly man fast asleep. The boy was a well-mannered child, so he quietly sat aside waiting for the old man to wake up and share some of the shade.

And you really never appeared, essay.

The boy sat down and thought for a while. Elderly sir, he said again, do you know a place I can go? A place where I can find my love?

Then, Grandpa. The boy continued, perhaps you can hear which road leads to a wonderful world? The old man tilted his head to one side and listened, then tilted it to the other side and listened again, finally shaking his head and saying: My hearing isn't very good anymore, I used to be able to hear whispering grass.

The old man finally opened his eyes and gestured kindly for him to move closer under the shade of the tree, although by this time it was sunset, the night was deepening, but the boy didn't complain, because he knew his love was ahead, while the old man's path had already passed behind him.

I am looking for my love, sir. The boy said, could you tell me which road ahead is the best? Where should I go to find love? The old man scrutinized him from top to bottom, then looked at the roads leading far away, finally shaking his head and telling him: My eyesight isn't very good anymore, I used to be able to see walking winds.

A few days later, the old man was leaning against the old tree when suddenly a young man covered in dust approached.

I can never go back. None of us can. Now we must continue resting, then complete the remaining two-thirds of the shooting, at least two months of shooting, for life, for art, haha.

When I was young, I also went out to travel, searching for something, what now I can no longer remember. Some things I found, others I did not. But the important thing is that I have traveled when I was young.

To do the best, then beautifully participate in the film exhibition.

Some days later, the elder was sitting by the old tree when suddenly a youth came along covered in dust.

Even if he remembered time travel, it was merely like a myth read during childhood, never once disturbing his peaceful mood.

And you still haven't appeared.

Of course, time travel is the best place I know. He replied. He knew that this young man might take many years to understand this answer, experiencing many arduous yet wonderful days.

I think time travel is the best place. Saying this, the elder creakily stood up, stretched his waist, and disappeared into the shadow of the tree. The boy didn't follow after him incessantly, but instead spent the night under the branches. When a red sun slowly rose from the eastern sky towards him, he seemed to hear a distant call and immediately stood up. He chose a road at the crossroads that he hoped would lead to time travel.

Young man, the elder said, I am searching for my love, could you tell me which road I should take? The elder leaned against the big tree and gazed at the sky, clouds swiftly passing over his head.

However, he finally left time travel behind. He planted food around his house, creating a world. He met a good girl, married her, they helped each other and kept moving forward.

He journeyed for many days, experienced many events. He mined gold on mountains, dived for pearls in the sea, climbed mountains, drilled caves, lived outdoors, traveled day and night. He experienced the great world, tasted all kinds of bitterness and sweetness, but he still stubbornly searched for time travel.

For life, having experienced is enough.

Then he closed his eyes and peacefully fell asleep.

Then it was time to look for new topics again. New works, new shooting techniques.
