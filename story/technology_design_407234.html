Matters needing attention in diabetic life

by lkts1ji2kod on 2011-04-07 12:09:36

Diabetes, known in Traditional Chinese Medicine as "Xiaoke," is a disease caused by impaired insulin secretion and sugar metabolism. In recent years, due to changes in dietary structure, there has been an increasing trend in the incidence of diabetes. According to surveys, it is estimated that China has over one million people with diabetes, more than 90% of whom are middle-aged or elderly. The incidence of erectile dysfunction (ED) among diabetic patients can be as high as 40-60%, and for those over 50 years old, the rate of ED is even higher. Elderly diabetic patients often also experience significant arteriosclerosis.

The primary cause of sexual dysfunction in diabetic patients is metabolic disorders and degenerative changes caused by diabetes, which continuously damage peripheral nerves, autonomic nerves, and small blood vessels. Evidence shows that 95% of diabetic patients have signs of penile artery obstruction. Diabetic vascular lesions are progressive, and when they affect the small vessels of the corpus cavernosum, they can lead to erectile dysfunction. Diabetes is a chronic condition, and patients may experience physical decline due to long-term medication and dietary restrictions. Factors such as work stress, marital conflicts, economic difficulties, and emotional distress can all increase the likelihood of developing ED. Retrograde ejaculation is also a common form of sexual dysfunction in diabetic patients.

Sexual dysfunction in female diabetic patients is less pronounced than in males. This is because women are generally in a more passive position during sexual activity and experience less psychological pressure compared to men. Some female diabetic patients mistakenly believe that diabetes can be transmitted through sexual contact, which is unscientific. Diabetes is a genetic disease and cannot be spread through sexual contact. This misconception can lead to some individuals experiencing sexual dysfunction and reduced sexual desire. Female diabetic patients often suffer from vaginal infections, urinary tract infections, abnormal odors, and painful intercourse. Additionally, decreased vaginal lubrication and autonomic nerve damage reduce vaginal sensitivity, hindering the achievement of sexual climax.

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, diabetes is considered to result from spleen and kidney deficiency, leading to qi and yin damage, which inevitably affects sexual function. It is recommended that diabetic patients control their sexual activity to avoid further depletion of kidney essence and worsening of their condition. On the other hand, they should consume kidney-nourishing and yin-nourishing foods and supplements, such as Yi Hong capsules, to treat diabetes, enhance libido, and improve sexual function.

For diabetic patients, maintaining a healthy sexual life involves controlling diet, using appropriate medications to normalize blood sugar levels, and engaging in self-care to eliminate psychological barriers. By adopting these wellness methods, early-stage diabetic patients often achieve good results. Bai Hemai Yi Hong capsules can help control blood sugar and improve sexual function.
