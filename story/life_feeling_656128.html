If, there is moonlight tonight...

I have just returned from a dinner gathering outside. Tonight, I drank a bit too much. I don't know since when I became the type who can drink heavily in their eyes. Actually, I cannot drink that much, really! In the joyful atmosphere, I did not refuse any of their gentle blessings, but this does not mean I can drink endlessly. Therefore, at the dining table, I never dare to talk big, especially with Zhao, the handsome guy from pure Northwest China! If I were easily defeated then, I would be confused and not know which way is west or east!

However, I truly, genuinely love the atmosphere of clinking glasses. That feeling is great! Great! The sensation of being tipsy yet not completely drunk is also great! Great! Many worldly disturbances are forgotten, leaving only the tranquil joy of solitude for myself.

Such gatherings are really great! Fate connects us with one thread, we are all family. In the deepest realms of life, we have collided into each other's lives, and since then, many beautiful stories have unfolded. In the season where autumn is particularly strong, flowers bloom everywhere!

Thinking about it, thinking about it, a faint smile always appears at the corners of my mouth. In the days of wandering, there are so many moments of bitterness, confusion, happiness, and helplessness, known only to myself. However, in the city where I roam, there are always souls like mine, making it easy for our hearts to get close, for emotions to grow. Life and work inevitably bring disturbances, but with a smile in response, we remain friends!

Tomorrow marks the fourth year of my wandering life! Should we celebrate? The bits and pieces of growth along the way pass through my mind like a movie, suddenly bringing forth an urge to cry. Four years of time, if they can change something, it's enough. If they can make me experience something, it's deeply memorable. It seems I have grown accustomed to people coming and going around me, numb to the point of showing no expression, but deep down, I am sad! Truly sad!

In early summer, Girl left. She found another kind of happiness and joy for herself. The roses in Pengcheng (Shenzhen) in May were just shedding their petals, covering the ground with vibrant colors, pricking my heart slightly! Perhaps it was a coincidence that Girl left Shenzhen on the same day I chose to start my trip to Beijing. There was no overly emotional farewell scene. I thought about it countless times in my heart, but I harshly told Girl: I won't see you off! I can't bear the loneliness after you leave! Girl understood and said: Don't see me off! Darling, I will miss you!

In mid-summer, Maoqing also left Shenzhen. With different choices in life, I might have been able to give any reason to hold her back, but her lifelong happiness together was something I couldn't find the words to stop. In the large KTV private room, during her birthday banquet, what should have been a happy day turned into almost two hours of tears due to just a few words from me. She hugged me tightly, biting each of our shoulders hard, saying "I want to use this way to make you remember me forever." It hurt, really hurting for nearly a week. What a silly girl! After nearly three years together, we wouldn't need such a method to remember each other, deeply remember, the profound memories etched by time. Who could easily forget? MaoMao said: I will come to see you guys again in Shenzhen! I look forward to seeing her again when spring returns, green mountains and clear waters, she returning with her beloved companion. Still the same old look, happiness overflowing...

As summer ends, autumn comes again! I deeply cherish every person who has entered my life! After performing many stories, quietly departing, I still count the memories, smiling and telling you: cherish the present. After turning around, often remember, stay in touch when you have time!

On the eve of the Mid-Autumn Festival, in the misty night. If, there is moonlight tonight, I'll send you a tune of longing, a basket of blessings!

To, every single one of you, in my life...
