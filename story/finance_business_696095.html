Financial assets repel contraction, making interest rate hike before the festival possible

by w9pzjf58625 on 2012-03-07 20:34:09

By reporter Cui Ye and intern Wu Yu

In the first half of the year, due to relatively high policy uncertainty, HSBC China's head of securities strategy, Sun Yu, described the situation with an old song lyric: "stay sober, stay drunk." However, from the perspective of investors, concerns about high inflation, economic growth, declining profit indices, and massive corporate financing may alternately trouble the market, causing fluctuations, so one needs to be more sober in the first half. In the second half, GDP growth is expected to slow down to 9.2%, while CPI will rise to 4.6%, a soft landing can be expected, the market may perform better in the second half than in the first half, so one can "indulge a little more" in the second half.

Do not have unrealistic expectations for the first quarter market

"In terms of tightening, I think the tightening may come early. The interbank central bank bill trading rate has increased by 140 basis points since last November, the central bank has already raised it twice by 25 basis points, there may be 2-3 more interest rate hikes this year. If there are rate hikes, they might occur in the first half, around the Spring Festival," Sun Yu said that the risk of tightening would be released in the first quarter, so it is impossible to have unrealistic expectations for the market trend in the first quarter.

Regarding the real estate bubble, Sun Yu expressed pessimism about solving the problem of excessively high real estate prices. In the short term, the central bank would need to drastically tighten money supply and significantly raise interest rates to control the momentum of rising housing prices in the short term; in the medium to long term, on the one hand, social security housing, because it has just begun large-scale construction, it will take time to meet the housing requirements of middle and lower income groups, and the land fiscal issue requires significant reform of fiscal policy, it will be difficult to see results within three to five years or even longer.

"For corporate profits, we know that the profits of Chinese companies mainly depend on two factors, monopoly and liquidity. The monthly year-on-year growth of sales revenue and profits of national industrial enterprises. Corporate profit growth is affected by tightening, the important thing is income distribution system reform, including two major aspects, one is corporate income, the other is property income, creating jobs leads to corporate income growth," Sun Yu said that if economic transformation is needed during the '12th Five-Year Plan' period, resident income needs to be increased, interest rate levels must be liberalized, that is, 'interest rate marketization'.

Focus on industries related to people's livelihood

Overall, Sun Yu's judgment on this year's investment market is still relatively cautious, mainly recommending industries such as retailers, energy conservation and emission reduction, advanced manufacturing, services, and also bullish on insurance stocks.

In addition, reporters noticed that in 2010, pharmaceutical stocks performed well under the influence of policies, events, and other factors. WIND data shows that the full-year increase in the Shenwan Pharmaceutical & Biological Index reached 29.69%, ranking second among all 23 Shenwan primary industry indices. Meanwhile, the Shanghai Composite Index fell 14.31% for the whole year, and the excess return of the pharmaceutical & biological index over the market was 44%.

However, Li Wenjian, the fund manager of E Fund Healthcare Industry Fund, pointed out that there were significant differences in quality among individual stocks in the pharmaceutical sector. Out of 156 stocks, 44 had negative annual gains. Based on the numerous domestic pharmaceutical companies, unbalanced development, and accelerated industry mergers and acquisitions, investing in pharmaceutical stocks is relatively challenging. To enhance investment stability, ordinary investors might consider shifting their focus to funds that target the healthcare industry, relying on expert stock selection to overcome the high threshold of selecting stocks in the pharmaceutical industry. It was learned that the E Fund Healthcare Industry Fund, which launched at the beginning of the new year, received enthusiastic subscriptions within a week.

Meanwhile, as a key supported industry in the '12th Five-Year Plan' and emerging industry planning, the sustained good growth of the pharmaceutical industry has attracted widespread market attention. From its long-term past performance, the pharmaceutical industry exhibits characteristics of long-term stable growth. Driven by medical consumption upgrades, starting from around 2000, the pharmaceutical industry experienced its first decade of vigorous development, during which many "bullish stocks" emerged.

Citigroup's head of investment research and analysis in China, Shen Minggao, believed that the Chinese stock market in 2011 might show a trend of upward volatility. Investment opportunities in the market come from three aspects: first is inflation, when CPI rises faster than PPI, the possibility of corporate profits rising is greater, thereby benefiting the stock market; second is liquidity, M1 growth is closely related to the Chinese stock market, if this year's new loans could maintain growth above 15%, M1 growth in the first half might be around 20%, although it has dropped compared to last year, it is still at a historical high before the crisis, abundant liquidity will support the upward trend of the stock market; third is the dollar value, Citigroup's research shows that the Chinese stock market is the second most sensitive to the US dollar index among emerging market economies, only after Brazil. This year, the possibility of the US economy recovering is greater, but the pressure of structural depreciation of the dollar still exists, if the dollar index continues to fall, it means that investor risk preferences are increasing, more funds flow into emerging markets, which is also a great advantage for the Chinese stock market.

Anti-inflation products from banks emerge

In response to the overall recovery of the economy and the warming up of domestic and international market demands, as well as the need for anti-inflation investments from investors, banks have launched various anti-inflation wealth management products. Reporters learned from the Bank of Communications that the yield of the bank's "Half-Year Profit" RMB wealth management product has been adjusted higher along with interest rate changes. Taking a principal of 200,000 yuan as an example, a half-year term could achieve an annualized yield of 4.21%. The East Asia Bank launched the "Step-by-Step Profit" investment strategy, ensuring 100% principal protection for investors while capturing opportunities in the rising commodity market. Notably, with the recent market warming, several commodity-linked structured products previously launched by the East Asia Bank have performed excellently. For instance, the "Profit Booster Series 24" launched at the end of August last year, linked to gold and agricultural ETFs, triggered early expiration in September 2009, achieving an annual yield of approximately 7%. Furthermore, leveraging the rise in crude oil prices, the "Step-by-Step Profit 2" selected oil stocks RMB principal-guaranteed investment product achieved early triggering within 3 months last October, successfully realizing an annualized high yield of 9% for investors.

Reporters learned that the East Asia Bank recently launched the new "Step-by-Step Profit 9 Anti-Inflation Selected Commodities Theme II" principal-guaranteed investment product, with subscription open until January 25. This product is linked to gold, oil, and soybeans. Besides providing 100% principal guarantee upon maturity, if on any quarterly observation day or settlement day all linked assets' closing prices are higher or equal to 106% of their starting price, investors could obtain a potential annual yield of 9%. The investment term could be shortened from 2.5 years to as short as 3 months, enhancing the liquidity of funds. Otherwise, investors would receive a minimum guaranteed annualized yield of 0.36% upon maturity.
