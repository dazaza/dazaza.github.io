Don't treat the game as a game, treat the game as a star.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-08 18:09:39

Currently, 100 new games are launched online each month, with a large number of game companies entering the market. However, 90% of these companies earn less than 1 million yuan annually.This is the data presented by Xiao Jian, CEO of China Mobile Game Group at today's GGS Global Mobile Game Summit. Although some domestic game companies have reached an operational revenue in the millions, issues still inevitably arise as they transition from simply creating game products to commercial operations.Game developers face six hurdlesIn summary, the issues can be categorized into the following reasons: product initiation, product core, technical experience, market interference and competition, operational challenges, and talent bottlenecks.One of the more fatal yet common problems is having a product without a core. Simply copying will not produce a good game, or even a relatively complete one. Xiao Jian believes that to develop a game with a successful concept, it is necessary to clarify where the fun points of the game lie, whether the game logic is closed-loop, if the system is reasonable, and if the billing point design aligns with the player’s immersion logic. Each of these questions represents a hurdle.The market itself also has disturbances, with bubbles starting to appear. Under these disturbances, developers become anxious, and the consequence of this anxiety is various piecemeal and forced plagiarism.Developers need optimized operational platforms and hope that investors or operators adjust their operational methods; on the other hand, operational platforms believe that game developers should possess certain operational capabilities so that their products meet current market demands. Thus, a conflict arises, with neither side willing to give way. In fact, neither side is wrong. The market precisely needs both parties to coexist and play their roles simultaneously to push the market forward.Don't make games too cheapThis is a problem for domestic game developers, which can easily create an illusion that the moon abroad is rounder, meaning there are more high-quality games overseas with no issues.Gao Lianbing, co-founder of Lexiang Games, gave a speech mainly discussing strategies for foreign high-quality games entering the Chinese market, telling us that everything is not as simple as it seems and requires two types of understanding: team understanding and conceptual understanding.Foreign developers looking to enter the Chinese market have only two choices: set up their own company or entrust a Chinese company. Relatively speaking, the former is much more difficult and complex than the latter, so most companies choose to talk to Chinese agents. Unfortunately, many Chinese distributors are only concerned about how much money they can make. In Gao Lianbing's eyes, this is disrespectful to others' achievements.He stated that the reason many games similar to Temple Run are willing to let them act as agents is because they have sufficient understanding of the product and the team. They don't need to consider technical aspects too much but genuinely think about how to localize foreign games well. To achieve localization, you must understand comprehensively.Games are like the children of developers. No parent would want you to control what hairstyle or clothes their child should have. If you don't understand his (the developer) thoughts and he doesn't understand the Chinese market, then how do you proceed?He said that abroad, users will try to pay for good games, but it's different in China. If changes are made just for profit in a rigid or even brutal manner, developers will think you've made their game very cheap.Treat games like starsFinally, there's the issue of brand operation.In his view, many games, especially star-level games, are stars rather than just games.If you see it as a game, it remains just a game; if you see it as a star, it can do many things, like making movies or appearing in advertisements.To operate a star-level game, the key is to extend its lifecycle as much as possible, which requires comprehensive development. There are also many relevant cases: Fruit Ninja released storybooks targeting younger audiences because parents monitor their children playing iPads but not reading books; Tiny Blaster released many peripheral products, such as subway cards, because it's cute.
