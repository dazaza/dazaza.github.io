Chongqing girl sells Chongqing specialties via online store, breaking three million yuan in monthly sales.

by longyi on 2008-08-31 14:14:25

A 22-year-old girl from Chongqing relies on selling various local specialties and famous snacks online. Now, her monthly online sales have exceeded 30,000 yuan, with customers spread across major cities in China. Currently, she is too busy to handle everything alone, so her online store team has grown from one person to three.

Chongqing Girl Sells Local Specialties Online

The online merchant "Xintian" is originally from Bishiyi. In 2006, she operated a store in Beijing selling famous local snacks from both Beijing and Chongqing. However, due to high operating costs, she returned to Chongqing in September and began selling Chongqing specialties on Taobao.

In September of last year, Xintian's online store "Chongqing Chanmao Party" officially opened. The initial customer base came from the old clients she accumulated while running her physical store in Beijing. Her goods were sourced from local food manufacturers in Chongqing, and she relied on posting threads online for promotion. Products like the famous磁qi Kou twisted dough sticks, Deying beef, Bishiyi board duck, and Hechuan peach slices became her main merchandise.

"Compared to other online merchants, I think my advantage lies in lower prices. I only make a few yuan profit per box of food. The key to selling food is volume," said Xintian. She also emphasized her ability to ensure the freshness of the food.

Customers Spread Across the Country

Looking at Xintian's online store, it can be seen that her customers are now spread across major cities in China, with the majority of buyers coming from out-of-town. Many customers purchase through group-buying organized by colleagues from their companies. "Actually, Chongqing's famous local snacks are quite well-known nationwide. Employees from many companies outside Chongqing voluntarily organize bulk purchases. This customer group accounts for 70% of our current online store sales, with monthly sales reaching about 30,000 yuan," Xintian said.

Currently, Xintian has become a full-time online merchant, renting a house in Shiqiaopu to run her online store. When discussing her experience in online business, Xintian summarized two points: first, price wars online are fiercer than in physical stores. The best way to face price wars is to build a brand and retain old customers; second, when opening an online store, one should focus on what they are good at. "I wouldn't sell clothing because I know nothing about that industry."
