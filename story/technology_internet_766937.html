Opportunity or Pitfall? How Domestic Smartphone Tycoons View 4G

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 10:39:36

Minister of Industry and Information Technology, Miao Wei, reiterated that "4G licenses will be issued within the year," pushing the domestic mobile phone market into the "first year of 4G terminals." Apple, Samsung, Sony, and other international manufacturers have already launched TD-LTE phones.

However, domestic smartphone manufacturers that suffered from overstocking due to "rushing too early" in the early days of the 3G era are now questioning whether they should actively respond to China Mobile's call. The author recently interviewed various leaders in the domestic 4G smartphone industry on this issue.

In general:

1. Domestic large brands such as ZTE, Huawei, Coolpad, and TCL generally see this as a "big opportunity," finally free from numerous small brands waging "price wars," allowing them to make money at a more sustainable pace.

2. Compared to the revolutionary innovation of 3G data over 2G voice, 4G is not a revolution. Except for chips and basebands, most components of 4G and 3G phones are interchangeable, which will greatly accelerate product release speeds.

3. Due to Qualcomm's dominance in chip solutions, it will be difficult to see sub-1000 RMB 4G phones before the third quarter of next year. Lei Jun, will you launch another Redmi this year?

4. Domestic companies Huawei and ZTE will both introduce their own 4G chip solutions. MediaTek's TD-LTE chip MT6290 is optimistically expected to enter mass production in the first quarter of next year. Lenovo and other major MediaTek customers need to act fast this time.

5. Apple, Samsung, Sony, and other domestic smartphone manufacturers have announced that their flagship models will support TD-LTE standards. It will still be challenging for domestic smartphones to use 4G to transition to premium products.

6. In front of domestic smartphone manufacturers, price wars will eventually come. Who will initiate this war: Lenovo, Coolpad, or Huawei?

7. With operators' aggressive promotion of 4G, high-end smartphones sold after the second quarter of next year will generally support 4G. No user will want to spend big money on outdated phones.

8. From the fourth quarter of 2014 to the first quarter of 2015, this is the industry-wide recognized "boom period" for domestic 4G smartphones.

9. Medium-sized domestic smartphone manufacturers currently have mixed attitudes. They fear investing heavily only for China Mobile not to procure their products, but without investment, they worry about being left behind in the reshuffle. The pressure is immense.

10. 4G will deliver another heavy blow to already declining counterfeit smartphones. Even the solution providers don't see much hope; counterfeit smartphones will basically disappear in the 4G era.

The following are the main views of the industry leaders:

HTC Chairman Wang Xuehong: 4G will become an important opportunity for HTC in the domestic market, especially in cooperation with China Mobile. HTC will release its first TD-LTE phone in mainland China mid-October, with several more expected by the end of the year.

Wang Xuehong emphasized: unlike Samsung and others who upgrade old models, HTC's releases are all new products tailored to China Mobile's needs, covering different price segments including future flagship models.

ZTE Communications Executive Vice President He Shiyu: 4G represents an opportunity for large domestic brand smartphone manufacturers like ZTE. ZTE is one of the first four vendors to receive TD-LTE phone approval from the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology. Currently, the supply chain limits 4G phones, making entry relatively high, which can improve the profit margin of domestic brands.

He Shiyu revealed that ZTE plans to roll out its own 4G chip solution in the first half of 2014, initially not supporting China Mobile’s 5-mode 10-band standard but ensuring basic TDD/FDD/WCDMA/GSM 2G to 4G network user experience.

He Shiyu also believes that because 4G networks require time for widespread coverage and the supply chain needs further maturity, the domestic market in 2014 will still be primarily 3G with 4G as a supplement, with 2015 marking the true explosion of the 4G smartphone market.

Coolpad Vice President Zhang Guangqiang: In the initial phase of 4G license issuance, the threshold is relatively high, which benefits domestic brands like Coolpad during this "window period." Compared to 3G terminals, 4G phone design involves more complex antennas, supports more frequency bands, and uses chips and components that cost 1-2 times higher than 3G terminals.

Coolpad has already released two TD-LTE phones, the 8920 and 8735, with plans to release 5-6 more 4G phones by the end of the year. Mid-range products will gradually reach prices between 1500-2000 RMB. Zhang Guangqiang predicts that if China Mobile and other operators build their 4G networks smoothly, by the end of next year, the number of domestic 4G users could approach 100 million, with user adoption of 4G significantly faster than the initial phase of 3G.

Lenovo Group Vice President Feng Xing: Lenovo has not yet officially launched a 4G phone, but is fully prepared. After the 4G license is issued, Lenovo will immediately launch its first TD-LTE phone using Qualcomm's solution. Feng Xing believes that the real volume increase in domestic smartphones will occur in 2014, and domestic phone manufacturers need to manage their product timelines well. Lenovo's 4G phones will cover the full range of products in the third and fourth quarters of 2013, including high-end flagship models and sub-1000 RMB smartphones.

TCL Communication China Region President Wang Jiyang: TCL's first FDD phone is ready for the domestic market, with TDD phones planned for shipment in the first quarter of 2014. By the end of 2014, TCL plans to launch 6 TDD products, priced mainly between 1000-2000 RMB, with 1-2 flagship products priced around 2500 RMB.

Wang Jiyang believes that domestically, TD-LTE phones will mainly use Qualcomm chip solutions until the third quarter of next year, so truly sub-1000 RMB 4G phones will not appear on a large scale until the end of 2014. However, operator marketing efforts will directly affect user expectations for mid-to-high-end phones, as no one wants to buy an outdated product. The popularization of domestic 4G terminals may happen earlier than current operator forecasts.

Huawei Terminal China Region President Wang Weijun: Huawei's first domestic TD-LTE phone, the Ascend D2-6070, has already passed the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology's network access certification, and next year's high-end models will all support TD-LTE networks.

Notably, according to Wang Weijun, since Huawei's 4G phones use their own HiSilicon chips and solutions, they are not constrained by Qualcomm or MediaTek chip supplies. Huawei's TD-LTE sub-1000 RMB smartphones will be widely available in large quantities by July-August 2013, leading other domestic manufacturers by six months. The domestic 4G smartphone market is expected to explode in 2015.

Large domestic brands view 4G as a key opportunity to escape domestic smartphone price wars. How do medium-sized brands feel?

Shenzhen Bai Lifeng Mobile Phone Chairman Huang Mingquan: Currently, TD-LTE phones in the country largely use Qualcomm's 8926 chip solution, so the threshold for medium-sized domestic phone manufacturers is not particularly high. Bai Lifeng's first TD-LTE phone will be released in January 2014, priced just above 1000 RMB.

Huang Mingquan believes that the initial price of 4G phones will be high, and users' demand for phone brands will be stronger. This will bring market difficulties for small and medium-sized domestic brands. However, for medium-sized brands, this is also an important time window for brand transformation, with both significant risks and opportunities.

How do counterfeit phone enterprises view 4G?

Huaqiangbei mobile phone market veteran observer Yang Xianzhong: Counterfeit phone enterprises transitioning to 4G products requires several factors: operator network construction speed, supply chain maturity, solution provider pricing, and user acceptance. Currently, MediaTek and Spreadtrum lack the capacity for large-scale shipments in the short term. Yang Xianzhong lamented that counterfeit phone manufacturers are disappearing rapidly, and their most urgent task is survival and brand transformation. For them, 4G remains a "distant topic."

Small and medium-sized phone brands and counterfeit enterprises are all looking to MediaTek and solution providers. What is the progress of these companies?

Chip Companies

MediaTek China General Manager Zhang Weili: MediaTek plans to launch the TD-LTE chip MT6290 by the end of 2013, using 28nm process technology. Due to China Mobile's stringent requirements for 5-mode 10-band, MediaTek is communicating with some terminal manufacturers to resolve these issues, with mass production expected in the first quarter of next year.

Phone Solution Companies

YouSi Company General Manager Gu Xinhui: 4G represents an opportunity for domestic smartphone brands: 1. TD-LTE standards are led by domestic enterprises; 2. Supply chains are more mature than in the early days of 3G; 3. Domestic enterprises and personnel are more experienced after the 2G/3G exercises; 4. Consumers adopt new technologies quickly.

Gu Xinhui stated that YouSi's first TD-LTE phone will be released by the end of the year, but actual mass production will depend on operator network deployment and domestic user acceptance rates. In terms of solutions, Gu Xinhui believes that compared to the "public version" era of 3G, 4G phones require higher finished product standards, requiring the determination of molds, appearance, and baseband antenna deployment from the start. Solution companies must closely collaborate with branded companies. The "public version" era of smartphones will be hard to reappear in the 4G age, and counterfeit manufacturers will have no future without transformation.
