Hengdian Two-day Trip -- Gossip on the Bus Before Departure

On March 24, 2010, Jayce said she wanted to visit Hengdian for some relaxation and then continue her happy life. I agreed to join her, taking this opportunity to have a good chat and improve our interaction pattern.

"Out there, you should spend where it's necessary..." Two hours later, the tour bus arrived at Jiaxing station. The guide let everyone use the restroom and then began persuading us to participate in the "Dreamlike Tai Chi" show that evening. In his persuasion, words like "heart-stirring" and "120 yuan" frequently appeared. Jayce and I looked at each other for two seconds and said in unison: No way!

Jayce's purpose was clear; she just wanted to see the place where her idol, Jay Chou, filmed "The Golden Armor" and the restaurant he often ate at. As for me, I didn't want to spend more money. The guide, seeing our firm refusal, could only look at us with disdain, muttering "stingy." He repeatedly stated that not going would be our biggest regret of the trip...

Jayce then called her hometown sister who had been to Hengdian before for consultation. The sister bluntly said: There's no need to go! It's just a mountain top with fake volcanic eruptions, nothing much to see. So, on the way, we skipped what most people considered the must-see attraction: Dreamlike Tai Chi.

Here's a bit of gossip about the hometown sister. She is a long-time fan of Xiaoqiang, having reached the highest level as the president of the fan club and the moderator of the bar. Her relationship with Xiaoqiang is very close. Whenever Xiaoqiang comes to Cool Colors, she always takes leave to visit the set. Once, while playing at Jayce's house, she suddenly received a call from Xiaoqiang's agent, informing her that Xiaoqiang had arrived at a certain hotel. Without hesitation, she rushed downstairs, bought a bucket of strawberries and a bucket of zongzi (rice dumplings) from the fruit supermarket, and prepared for a meeting. But just after buying them, she received another call from the agent saying Xiaoqiang had caught a cold and postponed the meeting to the next day... The hometown sister could only bring the two buckets back to Jayce's house and let her eat them all. Jayce complained, "What kind of gifts are these? They can't even be eaten, and they went bad later."

According to the hometown sister, Xiaoqiang treats her fans well, always treating them to meals whenever she comes and chatting together in the hotel. The hometown sister contentedly sighed: Being a fan to this extent is already satisfying.

Jayce said she had a colleague who spent over 5000 yuan to buy a front-row seat for an Eastern Shinhwa concert just to get a close-up view of the idols for two hours, which was almost two months' salary for that colleague... Besides saying "wow," I really didn't know how to express my feelings. Thinking back, when Snow was still the agent for Eastern Shinhwa, I disdainfully turned down so many opportunities to get close to them. If Jayce's colleague knew this, I wonder what they would think.

Jayce said: Compared to my colleague, my support for Jay Chou doesn't amount to much. Next time at Jay Chou's concert, I will try to sit in the front row...

After another two hours, passing through countless tunnels, we finally arrived at Hengdian Film City. The car stopped at the resort where celebrities often eat and stay. Here, we had a simple lunch. In the afternoon, the first stop of our two-day Hengdian trip began - Pingyan Cave Palace, the hotel inside the resort where celebrities often stay during filming.
