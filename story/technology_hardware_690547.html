The Development of DIY in the Field of Smart Home in China is Restricted

Currently, domestic manufacturers in the smart home field are mainly divided into three categories: The first category consists primarily of traditional intercom manufacturers for buildings such as Fujian Guanlin, Videosecurity, Legrand, and Anjubao. These manufacturers mainly provide an integrated intelligent platform that consolidates numerous subsystems including security alarms, appliance control, multimedia audio-visual control management, etc.; The second category consists mainly of traditional home appliance manufacturers like Haier, TCL, Changhong, etc., who focus on providing networked information appliances; The third category includes companies specializing in modules and interfaces for lighting and curtain control such as Suobo and Bocreate. These manufacturers mainly cooperate with the first two categories by providing various types of smart switches and module interfaces.

With more and more product suppliers joining the smart home industry, such as access control, alarm systems, intercoms, and home appliances, the markets targeted by these manufacturers vary greatly, leading to a situation where almost all manufacturers in the smart home market operate independently.

In contrast, the development of the smart home industry abroad started earlier, with more mature brands, generally based on wired systems combining strong and weak electricity. Additionally, foreign users can, to a certain extent, do it themselves (DIY), performing some product debugging and installation according to their actual needs for personalized design. The advantage of this operation lies in reducing user costs while increasing the number of optional solutions. In reality, domestic users rarely purchase and install products themselves, whereas in foreign countries, such as the United States, users can directly buy smart home products like light control devices and smart alarm devices at supermarkets and perform DIY installations.

Few domestic manufacturers directly set up specialty stores or sell products through supermarkets and department stores to end-users. Typically, in smart home project applications, real estate developers and system integrators provide residents with automated comprehensive control platforms. After the platform is built, residents can purchase smart home systems according to their own needs, expanding or further perfecting the functions of smart homes on the already established platform. The design, installation, and other operational steps are usually handled by system integrators. Currently, domestic smart home products have not fully reached the "plug-and-play" mode of operation, meaning they cannot be used directly upon purchase like traditional household appliances. They involve debugging and installation operations which have a certain level of professionalism and complexity, often requiring specialized companies such as strong and weak electric system integrators to design, debug, and install based on the actual needs of residents!

However, due to manufacturers not directly participating in the design and installation operations of projects, if relying on system integrators for installation, their technical level may not be sufficient to complete the entire project, possibly failing to meet user expectations. Alternatively, when procured and installed uniformly as part of the refined housing packages provided by real estate developers, the average cost is reduced, but residents cannot choose product styles, function types, etc., according to their own needs.

There are many reasons why domestic residents are restricted from doing it themselves. One is cost-related; hiring decoration companies domestically is relatively cheaper than abroad, and the cost for residents to do it themselves is not necessarily cheaper. Another is the living environment issue; the population density in China is higher, and most residences are apartment buildings rather than single-family homes or villas common in foreign countries. Each household in foreign countries typically has a basement that can accommodate tools and construction work. Therefore, compared to foreign countries, Chinese residents are less inclined towards DIY in smart home applications, with most installations still being done through real estate projects. The main application of domestic smart home products currently lies in communication with real estate developers, depending on how they position their own properties, with the key being whether the real estate developer is willing to invest funds in smart homes.

Real estate developers often install complete smart home systems in houses to enhance sales volume and add value to the property. Abroad, smart homes are considered an "individual concept" and can be referred to as home equipment control or lighting control, not as an overall concept. However, in China, smart home manufacturers or real estate developers promote them as an "overall concept."
