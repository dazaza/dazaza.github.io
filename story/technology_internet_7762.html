Internet copyright respect should start with Blogger

by xuelan1985 on 2006-10-21 14:48:09

Recently, I've read a lot of discussions about copyright issues, and I can't help but want to write something down to express my own thoughts. Nowadays, people on the Internet are becoming more aware of protecting their copyrights. I am just a small figure in the world of the Internet, so my scope of attention is quite limited. There's no need for me to pretend to be knowledgeable by gathering a bunch of information just to write this article. I'll only talk about what I know and understand.

The troubles with YouTube video copyrights and Google's experience in Belgium - these things happened abroad, and I only know the general gist of them. A while ago, the Moonlight Blog was accused of plagiarizing GSeeker's article, there were debates on the official PJBLOG forum regarding style copyrights, and Mars Lizard was upset about his blog articles being plagiarized - these incidents happened domestically, and I consider myself to have personally experienced them. And today, I just learned about the infringement controversy surrounding PostShow, though I only know a little bit about it.

I won't comment on the copyright issues mentioned above. To be honest, it's really hard to protect copyrights. In reality, if I were asked to stop using pirated materials, I admit it would be difficult for me because legitimate versions are simply too expensive, and I don't have the financial means. However, respecting copyrights on the Internet is something I think I can do as a blogger. Recently, I've been reading the blogs of Huànmiè (幻灭) and Hóng (虹), and under their influence, I feel that indicating the author and source is a virtue, even though I didn't always do it in the past. Moving forward, I will definitely make an effort to do so.

I don't know much about copyright issues on the Internet. My understanding is limited to the idea that other people's work belongs to them, and even if you take it, it still belongs to them; you cannot forcibly claim it as your own. Actually, we are just small figures among bloggers. Even if we infringe upon someone's rights, it's unlikely anyone would notice or come after us. But we shouldn't abandon our dignity just because we are small figures in the blogging world. We should not engage in thievery. Small figures need dignity even more, and we need to respect others' copyrights so that others may recognize our existence. This applies especially to you well-known bloggers.

Today, I just saw the controversy surrounding PostShow due to a watermark on an image, and I realized that I, as a blogger, might also be infringing upon someone's rights. I used to like putting watermarks on all the images in my blog, but I never removed others' watermarks.
