Xidi Village in Huangshan -- a water town with ancient style

by jj0505 on 2012-02-20 17:09:39

Xidi is located in the southeastern part of Yixian County, Anhui Province, with a village area of 12.96 hectares. Xidi Village is surrounded by mountains on all sides, and two streams flow from the north and east of the village, converging at Huayuan Bridge in the south of the village. The village is structured mainly around a longitudinal street and two roads along the streams, forming an eastward-oriented village street and alley system that extends to the north and south.

All streets and alleys are paved with black stones from Yixian County. The ancient buildings have wooden structures with brick walls for maintenance, featuring rich wood carvings, stone carvings, and brick carvings. The layout of the alleys, streams, and buildings is harmonious. The spatial changes in the village are tasteful, and the architectural tones are simple and elegant, demonstrating the outstanding talent and achievements of the ancient villages in southern Anhui in creating living environments, with high historical, artistic, and scientific value. Xidi Village is known as a museum of Ming and Qing ancient architecture and is considered a "classic of Huizhou".

Xidi Village is an ancient village where the Hu family gathered to live based on clan blood ties. The village originated in the 11th century and reached its peak development between the 14th and 19th centuries. In the early 20th century, with the disintegration of China's feudal clan system, the development of Xidi Village also gradually slowed down. Due to historically experiencing fewer wars and not being impacted by economic development, the original form of the village has been well-preserved, maintaining the authenticity and integrity of its historical development. Currently, there are 124 preserved ancient residences from the Ming and Qing dynasties, and three ancestral halls, all of which have been listed as key cultural relics protection units in Anhui Province. On November 30, 2000, it was also included in the World Heritage List by UNESCO.
