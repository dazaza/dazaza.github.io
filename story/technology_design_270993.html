Built-in deduction in knock-off mobile phones becomes an unspoken rule, and it is urgent to cut off the interest chain

Obviously, maintaining consumer rights and cutting off the profit chain is the key.

■ By reporter Hongxiang Liao

The term "knock-off" has never been given a positive explanation. Recently, the scandal of pre-installed money-making SP services in knock-off phones has made these already unpopular phones even more despised. The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) has quickly issued an "Urgent Notice on Further Strengthening the Management of Mobile Websites" to operators, requiring them to conduct self-inspections. But without the participation of the entire interest group, how could knock-off manufacturers be so active and smooth in embedding "traps" into the phone terminals? Cutting off the profit chain will be the key for knock-offs to get rid of the "money-sucking gate".

Knock-off phones exposed again in "money-sucking gate"

Knock-off phones, which had been quiet for a while, have recently become a topic of public and media attention again, but not for good reasons. Due to being exposed for harboring consumer traps, all related information about knock-off phones has been brought to light under the scrutiny of the media.

"Watching TV on my phone for about 30 minutes resulted in a debt of 581.5 yuan! It clearly says 4 yuan for 30 days on the phone, isn't this deceiving people?" Consumer Mr. Zhang's experience unveiled the long-hidden "money-sucking trap" in knock-off phones.

The incident started when Mr. Zhang bought a new knock-off phone for less than 500 yuan. The manual indicated that the mobile TV fee standard was "4 yuan for 30 days." However, when Mr. Zhang later checked his bill, he found that just half an hour of watching mobile TV caused a debt of 581.5 yuan. There were also a large number of complaints about the same phone model on the 315 Consumer Electronics Complaints website, with almost a dozen every day.

Similarly, consumer Ms. Pang sent several text messages accidentally by pressing a button, each costing her two yuan in information fees.

These exposed infringement cases are just the tip of the iceberg regarding the "money-sucking traps" pre-installed in knock-off phones. As the media got involved, the ins and outs of the incident began to gradually come to light.

Unspoken rules under the transactions of interest groups

Subsequently, the other party involved in the incident, namely relevant individuals from the knock-off phone industry, explained the "unspoken rules" of the knock-off phone industry to the media: the pre-installed SP services are actually a collaboration between SP service providers, knock-off phone manufacturers, and solution providers. Relevant services such as games and music are pre-installed in the phones before they leave the factory to provide value-added services and meet various entertainment needs of users. With the development of the mobile phone industry, the built-in value-added services have gradually gone astray. Some SPs, tempted by high profits, ignore national regulations and disregard consumer interests, starting to "carefully operate" consumption traps.

In fact, this kind of irregular phenomenon has formed so-called "industry rules" within the industry, and even many large SP companies are engaged in the business of pre-installing fee-deducting services. Accompanying the rapid development of China's mobile phone manufacturing industry, especially knock-off phones, the number of participants in the "unspoken rules" has increased, making more and more consumers victims. Subsequently, a large number of rights protection incidents occurred, exposing these "unspoken rules" to the public.

Looking at the development of this incident, blaming only the knock-off phone manufacturers cannot solve all problems. Collusion is the keyword for these frequent infringement incidents. If it weren't for the collusion among these "behind-the-scenes black hands," how could the "money-sucking traps" succeed so easily? First, SP manufacturers collude with knock-off phone manufacturers to make huge profits through pre-installed services; secondly, operators' tolerance or even shielding of SPs during the supervision process makes this bad trend worse.

At the root of it, it is due to the temptation of high profits that all links in the upstream and downstream of the entire industrial chain have formed a common interest group. To gain more profits, all parties begin to "create and practice" so-called industry unspoken rules.

Cutting off the profit chain is fundamental

Without the cohesion of the profit chain, none of this would go so smoothly; without the collaboration of the profit chain, none of this would be so coordinated. It is the profit chain that leads to numerous consumer traps, and it is the profit chain that causes repeated harm to consumers. Therefore, cutting off the profit chain is the key to rectifying the "money-sucking trap" of knock-off phones.

The MIIT has stated that malicious SP enterprises that severely infringe on consumer interests will be severely punished. Once verified, they should be legally ordered to suspend operations and prohibited from accessing any national telecom operator. On the 15th, the MIIT further issued an "Urgent Notice on Further Strengthening the Management of Mobile Websites" to operators, requiring them to strengthen management according to relevant regulations issued by the MIIT and conduct a comprehensive inspection in the near future. At the same time, they should enhance the management of business promotion channels and the proxy charge环节.

But relying solely on this passive regulation may not yield satisfactory results. Handling each case as it occurs is more like settling accounts after the fact and cannot truly prevent consumers from falling into consumption traps again. Removing the possibility of setting traps at the source allows consumers to consume with peace of mind, and 3G can develop smoothly free from the shackles of consumption traps.

Returning to the key link in the industrial chain, the knock-off phone manufacturers. Since knock-off phones rely on low prices and multiple functions to stand their ground in the market, low cost is a principle that enterprises must adhere to. With the occurrence of the economic crisis and setbacks in overseas markets, knock-off phones have not had it easy in recent years. Survival is difficult for companies without money, let alone continued development. Therefore, to survive, many manufacturers have resorted to "deceitful ideas" and taken risks by engaging in the "consumption trap" business.

Therefore, the key is how to enable knock-off manufacturers to break away from the low-value-added cloning and imitation. Once these manufacturers find other ways to make money, they will naturally not be so enthusiastic about illegal activities.

Furthermore, regulation should still be strengthened. Any non-compliant enterprise should be strictly investigated, especially for operators. Supervision efforts should be increased. Without the platform provided by operators, the money that shouldn't have been spent would not end up in the pockets of unscrupulous businessmen.
