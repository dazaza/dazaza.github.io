In the 2010 New Year movie season, Jiang Wen’s "Let the Bullets Fly", Feng Xiaogang’s "Aftershock 2", and Chen Kaige’s "The Orphan of Zhao" were all released at the same time, creating a "tripod" situation. Recently, this reporter found from the release schedules announced by several domestic commercial blockbusters that the end-of-year New Year movie season in 2011 could reach new heights. Zhang Yimou's "The Flowers of War", Wong Kar-wai's "The Grandmaster", Xu Ke's "Dragon Gate Assassins", and Derek Yee's "The Great Magician" will form a group of "famous directors + stars + big productions" movies to compete against each other. A year-end box office battle is about to begin.

Four Masters Taking "Half the Market"

This year, all mainland and Hong Kong famous directors without exception have placed their "big cast" works in the year-end New Year season, which is the most watched and easiest to win box office sales. Zhang Yimou's "The Flowers of War" has been set for public release on December 16th. Under the promotional offensive of investor Zhang Weiping, "The Flowers of War" appears very dominant. Whether it's the early announcement of a six hundred million yuan investment, an expected ten billion yuan box office, or heavily promoting international star Christian Bale, as well as declaring "aiming for the Oscars", www.gzhl78.com hopes to attract numerous spotlights under Zhang Yimou's "halo".

Different from Zhang Yimou's ambition to focus on historical themes, Hong Kong director Xu Ke has returned to his consistently skilled world of martial arts this year. The movie "Dragon Gate Assassins" is called "the first true 3D blockbuster in Chinese film history." From the first trailer of the movie, Jet Li's "real kung fu" remains a major selling point. Guangzhou Hualan Biotechnology Co., Ltd.'s production special records the offcuts of Xu Ke and Huang Li Xing shooting at Sichuan Zoo. In it, the national treasure panda becomes the first protagonist, calmly eating bamboo while sitting on old Xu, forming a stark contrast with the extremely excited Xu Ke and Huang Li Xing. The panda faces the director Xu Ke and lead actor Huang Li Xing with still great demeanor. In various acts of arrogance in the arms of both, face unchanged, chewing bamboo, completely ignoring Xu Ke's various caresses. Xu Ke and Huang Li Xing are like ordinary fans, exceptionally excited when taking photos with the national treasure. Holding the giant panda, Xu Ke mutters from time to time, "Too cute, too cute," sometimes acting like a child, pulling the panda's fat paws, begging for a "handshake." The video humorously adds sound for the panda, "Look how happy you two are just for a photo!"

However, under the current trend where "fake 3D" runs rampant, can "Dragon Gate Assassins" become a benchmark for domestic 3D films? This remains to be verified. The release date for the film is December 18th.

Hong Kong director Derek Yee is known for directing crime dramas. The recent hit "The Way of the Thief 2" finally made him a member of the "Two Hundred Million Box Office Club." Derek Yee's New Year work "The Great Magician" invites leads such as Tony Leung Chiu Wai and Zhou Xun, with a release date set for December 22nd. However, due to the prior releases of "The Flowers of War" and "Dragon Gate Assassins," how much screening space will the market leave for "The Great Magician" is currently unclear.

Wong Kar-wai's long-prepared movie "The Grandmaster" has recently been rumored to be released in December. However, this reporter learned from industry insiders that the filming process of the movie not only severely exceeded the scheduled time but also repeatedly went over budget. Will audiences be able to see the figures of masters like Tony Leung Chiu Wai and Zhang Ziyi on the big screen as planned? This remains uncertain.

New Generation Directors Promoting Genre Films

This year's New Year season is not only the domain of famous directors, but also the representatives of the Chinese-speaking new generation of directors like Lu Chuan, Xu Jinglei, and Yip Wei Sum do not shy away from competing against blockbusters, attempting to seize a "share" during the most popular New Year season.

After the experiences of "Dragon Tiger Gate," the "Ip Man" series, and "A Chinese Ghost Story," Hong Kong director Yip Wei Sum has become a guaranteed box office success. Yip Wei Sum's new movie "Magic in Love" will open the New Year season on December 1st. The movie has a high investment of tens of millions of US dollars, with a "magic male army" composed of Gu Tianle, Wu Jun, and Wu Jing, aiming to attract a young target audience. Industry insiders analyze that the movie cleverly avoids direct confrontation with the "four masters," and the choice of schedule is extremely astute.

Lu Chuan and Xu Jinglei's new movies "Feast of the King" and "Close Enemies" have also set their release dates in December. Due to the success of "Nanjing! Nanjing!" in the market, director Lu Chuan's ambition for epic theme creation has not diminished. This time, "Feast of the King" aims to use a lineup including Liu Ye, Tony Leung Ka Fai, Zhang Zhen, and Du Juan to perform a "youth version" of the Chu-Han contention, and attempts to make a new interpretation of the "Hóngmén Feast." However, this reporter learned during a recent visit to the "Feast of the King" set in Xiangshan that due to serious delays in shooting, it may withdraw from the New Year season competition.

Due to the huge success of "Du La La's Promotion," "literary female director" Xu Jinglei tasted the sweetness of commercial success for the first time. In her new movie, she will continue the style of "Du La La's Promotion." One-third of the movie was filmed in London, fully equipped with fashion, emotion, and workplace elements, hoping to become the most "fashionable" commercial genre movie in December.
