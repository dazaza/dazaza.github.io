Roger Federer, world No. 3, believes he can return to the top of the rankings despite Novak Djokovic's dominance on the ATP World Tour. The Swiss was dethroned from the South African Airways ATP Rankings top spot in June 2010, one week shy of Pete Sampras' all-time record of 286 weeks at No. 1.

"I strongly believe if I do the right things that it will come back. If I keep working hard there is a chance to get back to No. 1 and break the record," Federer said in a Sunday press conference at the Burj Al Arab hotel in Dubai, where he is competing this week at the Dubai Duty Free Tennis Championships.

"I feel like if I have a great run from here until the US Open that I obviously have a big chance to get back to No. 1," continued the Basel native. "But then again so do maybe ten guys. But I feel like I'm playing well. I've won a lot of tournaments in a short space of time, which obviously gives me a lot of confidence."

"But the focus now is just to try to play well every day and make sure that I have good runs from Dubai until Miami. And then who knows, maybe between Roland Garros and the US Open, during these big events, that's when the No. 1 ranking could be decided amongst the main contenders."

The 30-year-old holds the record for most major titles with 16 but hasn't added to his Grand Slam haul since triumphing at the 2010 Australian Open, where he beat Andy Murray in the final. He was beaten by Rafael Nadal in last year's Roland Garros final and suffered heartbreak in September when he lost a US Open semifinal after holding two match points against Djokovic.

"I've been close," Federer said. "I've worked hard, made the right decisions, and hopefully I can pick up a couple more along the way. Have I missed out on some great opportunities? I think that's probably more accurate than saying I haven't played as well as I have done in previous years; sometimes I even feel like I've played better. I've had some tough losses over the past few years that have cost me a couple of Slams."

Federer is one of eight players in the Top 10 competing at this ATP World Tour 500 hard-court event beginning Monday in Dubai. The four-time champion arrives buoyed by his 71st career title at the ABN AMRO World Tennis Tournament in Rotterdam last week and will open his campaign against Marseille runner-up Michael Llodra.

"I feel good. The week off between Rotterdam and Dubai helped because I've already played a lot of matches. I'm recovered physically, I'm playing well, and I'm excited to be back in Dubai," Federer said.

"The draw is difficult; Llodra was impressive in Marseille so he'll be full of confidence, which always makes matches tough. It's a really strong tournament. The conditions are tough; the courts are always fast here. In the past I've always struggled early on. Hopefully I can start well this time."

"I've won here four times so obviously I'd love to win again. Five would be incredible, but it's going to be tough."
