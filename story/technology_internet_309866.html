Arsenal Wins the Declining Power Match, Reigniting Championship Hopes

By Xiang Tong

If we compare the four major powers to The Beatles, then the Liverpool vs Arsenal match could be considered a contest between George and Ringo (the two less famous members of The Beatles). However, the battle between declining powers is equally significant. Liverpool needs to reclaim their top-four position, while Arsenal naturally hopes to take advantage of Chelsea and Manchester United's struggles in this round to narrow the gap with the league leaders. Arsenal had previously lost every time they faced strong opponents this season, but last Sunday at Anfield, it was Arsenal who came out on top.

Arsenal's 2-1 comeback victory marked their first league win at Anfield since 2003. Now, with one fewer game played, Arsenal trails Manchester United by only three points and Chelsea by six points, making anything possible. It can be said that both teams each played a good half. Arsenal was dominated in the first half, but their second-half resurgence benefited from Wenger's fiery halftime speech.

Steven Gerrard's Penalty Appeal Unsuccessful

Liverpool now has no excuse regarding injuries, as there are no first-team players injured except Babel. Torres, having overcome his hernia issues, started for the first time in six weeks. On the contrary, Arsenal had more than half a dozen key players injured, and after using youth players in the midweek Champions League match, Wenger fielded his available starting lineup, with Walcott making his first Premier League start in two months.

Liverpool took control early, not only showcasing beautiful moments like Aurelio's clever feint to bypass Nasri but also quickly creating scoring opportunities. In the 12th minute, Liverpool launched a stunning counterattack through their front two stars. Torres set up Gerrard with a wall pass from midfield, and the Liverpool captain advanced rapidly along the right flank into the penalty area before delivering a through ball. Torres pushed the ball towards goal, but his shot lacked the necessary angle and power, allowing Almunia to make the save. Just a minute later, Gerrard burst into the center of the box and tried to nutmeg Gallas, who collided with him. Despite Gerrard's appeal for a penalty, referee Howard Webb did not award one.

In the 21st minute, Liverpool displayed smooth cooperation on the right wing, with Kuyt providing a precise pass. Glen Johnson entered the penalty area, skillfully dribbling past Denilson and Nasri, but his final cross was disappointing. Encouraged by his performance, Johnson soon unleashed two consecutive long-range shots, and Liverpool's set-piece deliveries frequently caused chaos in the opposition's penalty area. In the 34th minute, Torres was brought down by Gallas just outside the penalty area, but Webb still chose not to intervene.

Arsenal paid the price for their set-piece defense problems in the 41st minute. From 40 yards out, Aurelio's free-kick was headed into the penalty area, where Almunia misjudged the ball and let it slip through his grasp. Kuyt controlled the ball with his chest and poked it into the net, making it 1-0. This marked Liverpool's club-record 19th consecutive home Premier League match with a goal. Wenger was extremely dissatisfied with this goal, as Lucas appeared to be offside when he jumped to challenge Almunia for the ball.

Two Key Transfers, One Villain

An unexpected familiar face in the stands was former Liverpool player Xabi Alonso, who returned to Anfield after joining Real Madrid this summer. Some British media outlets paired photos with sarcastic captions: "We're not going to joke about this picture." However, the real dark humor lay in the fact that Alonso's 'replacement' Aquilani still only had substitute appearances, while another significant summer signing, Glen Johnson, became the villain for Liverpool by contributing to two goals against his own team within eight minutes of the second half.

In the 50th minute, after a series of well-coordinated passes in Arsenal's forward line, Nasri received a through ball from Fabregas and broke into the penalty area on the right side before sending a low cross. The ball deflected slightly off Carragher, and in a panic, Johnson inadvertently blocked it with his left foot into his own net, leveling the score at 1-1. In the 58th minute, Fabregas broke through from the right and delivered a cross. Arshavin received the ball and easily dribbled past a disoriented Johnson before unleashing a powerful shot that hit the inside of the post and bounced into the net, putting Arsenal ahead 2-1.

Just after being equalized, Torres had a chance. He cut inside from the left flank, weaving along the baseline into the penalty area, but neither shooting nor passing options were available, so his attempt was ultimately ineffective. Witnessing Arsenal's quick two-goal turnaround, Gerrard furiously charged down the right wing, kicking Nasri and knocking over Kuyt in the process. Ultimately, Johnson delivered the cross, but Torres' header glanced wide.

Neither side could create many genuine chances thereafter. After receiving a pass from Nasri, Arshavin's shot from the right side of the penalty area posed no threat to Reina. A helpless Liverpool made continuous substitutions, with applause for Alonso as Aquilani replaced Mascherano, but it had little effect. Arsenal ended their six-match winless streak against the top four.

On the other hand, Liverpool has only won three of their last 15 matches across all competitions. Since beating Manchester United at the end of October, they've had two draws and two losses at home across both competition lines, raising concerns. The recurring issue of being overturned in the Champions League has now spread to the league, which is equally worrying. Benitez admitted post-match that the team's confidence was fragile: "Our first-half performance was excellent, and we were satisfied. Gallas' foul on Gerrard should have been a penalty. But the first goal changed everything. Our confidence disappeared, and we began to make mistakes. As we conceded the second goal, our opponents grew increasingly confident, while we did the opposite. Now, finishing in the top four remains our goal. It's a long road, but we must continue. Even though Aston Villa is already five points ahead of us, there's still a long way to go until the end of the season, and we will catch up."
