New Perspectives for Art Students at the University of Quebec in 2012

by abgjlp615 on 2012-02-20 17:01:58

For art students in 2012 who wish to study abroad and immigrate to Quebec, they can choose to study at the University of Quebec in Canada, which offers a low cost of studying abroad with high cost-effectiveness. The two-year postgraduate tuition fee for studying in Quebec is generally only 150,000 RMB, and students can enjoy a policy benefit of up to 90% scholarship exemption, with a two-year master's degree worth up to 100,000 RMB.

Traditional design and art majors will gradually increase, especially industrial design. With the increasing demand for creative talents in China's employment market, creative and art-related majors will become a new hotspot for Chinese students studying abroad in 2012. Due to this emerging demand, foreign universities have launched relevant courses for Chinese students to choose from. These countries' creative arts programs each have their strengths, differing in admission thresholds, educational philosophies, and training directions. In terms of detailed art majors, multimedia art, image styling, industrial styling, architecture, computer art, advertising, and other majors are beginning to gain prominence. Graduates from these majors will fill the significant gap in China's high-end art talent market upon returning home.

Canadian postgraduate study experts introduce five aspects:

1. "Exemption Scholarship" guarantees a 90% scholarship. Canada is known for its difficulty in obtaining scholarships, but among nearly 100 public universities in Canada, there is one unique Canadian postgraduate program that guarantees a 90% scholarship. This is a bilateral project between the National Scholarship Committee and the Quebec Provincial Government, saving up to 90%. The annual tuition fee at the University of Quebec is 11,000 CAD, and the "Exemption Scholarship" can save 10,000 CAD per year, equivalent to 60,000 RMB. Due to the limited number of "Exemption Scholarships" available each year, based on experience analysis, the following types of students have a higher chance of winning: first, those pursuing higher degrees, such as graduate studies over undergraduate studies. Second, those who receive direct admissions, specifically speaking, direct admissions are preferred over conditional admissions. Third, those studying French, meaning those studying French have a better chance than English. Fourth, those who submit applications earlier, since the "Exemption Scholarship" adopts a policy of applying anytime, reviewing anytime, reviewing first-come-first-served, and stopping once all spots are filled, submitting applications earlier naturally increases the chances of winning.

2. Quebec study deposit guarantee requires only 300,000 RMB, much lower than other provinces in Canada (other provinces require 400,000 to 500,000 RMB).

3. Quebec's immigration policy is more advantageous than other provinces in Canada. Technical immigration compared to the points system is more flexible. Quebec technical immigration requires 60 points, while other provinces require 67 points. Quebec immigration, international student experience-based immigration, according to Canadian law, has priority in immigration approval.

4. No TOEFL or IELTS score requirement threshold, no need to worry about achieving an IELTS score of 6.5. After four months and 500 hours of French language training in domestic French language training institutions, you can apply for a master's degree at the University of Quebec in Canada (search for the Canadian Quebec Province university on the Ministry of Education's overseas study supervision website).

5. Employment rate as high as 95% with "paid internship courses". Generally working 40 hours per week, recommended universities include the organizational management major at the University of Quebec, which provides students with a six-month paid internship opportunity. According to official information from the University of Quebec, for five consecutive years, the employment rate of the school has been as high as 95%. You can gain knowledge, obtain Canadian work experience, earn tuition fees (15 CAD per hour), and some students may even be directly hired by companies after graduation, achieving four goals in one move: graduation = employment. Upon graduation, you immediately obtain a three-year work visa, and after working for one year, you qualify for immigration application, with the fastest application cycle completed within two months. Canada's immigration doors are now open to you, allowing you to achieve both study and immigration in one step.
