How Small and Medium-sized Enterprises Choose a Call Center System

by 865512 on 2008-11-03 14:03:13

Speech recognition, IP call center solutions, call recording and analysis, customer self-service solutions—these high technologies that were applicable only to large enterprises years ago have now begun to slowly enter the realm of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

The SME market has now become the sought-after treasure for call center solution and service providers. The business goals of SMEs are generally similar to those of large enterprises, but their individual needs vary significantly, leading to differences in the procurement, implementation, and operation of technical solutions.

Currently, many SMEs have yet to fully grasp their own requirements and thus are easily attracted by the dazzling array of technologies available on the market. Only after use do they realize they have spent money on a host of features that their users don't need, resulting in a waste of financial and time resources.

To help SMEs' call centers make wise choices, we interviewed some industry companies with rich experience in this area and gathered plenty of valuable advice and suggestions to assist SMEs in correctly choosing the required call center technology and services.

Stephen R. Kowarsky, Executive Vice President and Co-founder of CosmoCom:

When selecting a call center platform, enterprises should ask themselves and potential vendors some basic questions to ensure long-term returns on their investment. For example:

- Does the vendor's product comprehensively meet the needs of the call center, saving the cost and time of purchasing other solutions and integrating them?

- Does the vendor support all channels, including voice, video, instant messaging, and email, while using common business rules to integrate them together?

- Can the solution be easily integrated into third-party business applications such as CRM?

- How scalable is the technology? Can it steadily upgrade as the enterprise grows?

- Is the technology sufficiently independent and flexible?

- Can the solution generate independent reports and manage agents based on different locations?

- Can the solution be compatible with the company's existing standard hardware, or does it require the purchase of expensive proprietary equipment?

- Does the solution offer both hosted and on-premise operational modes?

Tracy Tufillaro, Vice President of Marketing at Five9:

Choosing a technology vendor is a complex and often headache-inducing task. Many SMEs currently operate their businesses using informal methods and low-cost tools, while others have installed systems that fail to keep up with the constantly changing business environment. Regardless, SMEs should seek a solution provider that aligns with their philosophy, business goals, and pace of change.

To find this provider, SMEs need to establish selection criteria to balance customer needs, business practices, and operational goals. Based on this framework, they can identify potential vendors for further evaluation and discussion.

Vendors that provide quick, effective, reasonably priced, and easy-to-use solutions are more attractive. Through subscription models, these vendors can provide access to the latest technology and maintain regular updates. As SMEs grow and change, the associated call center solutions will evolve to a new level, ensuring that SMEs consistently deliver positive experiences to their customers.

Ralph Manno, Vice President of the North American SME Market at Interactive Intelligence:

SMEs share several similarities with large enterprises in terms of technological needs, but they must also accommodate the characteristics of smaller budgets and fewer resources.

Therefore, SMEs should look for solutions that are easy to deploy, manage, and customize. These types of solutions are typically "all-in-one" package applications. Such applications can significantly reduce deployment costs and complexity, with lower requirements for equipment and integration. Additionally, since these solutions are user-friendly, they can provide SMEs with faster return on investment.

Moreover, SMEs should also consider the scalability and upgrade potential of the solutions when making their selection. Solutions that are software-based and provided in modular form can be conveniently upgraded by adding licenses, allowing SMEs to avoid significant future expenses.

Given the higher price sensitivity of SMEs, they may consider choosing a hosted (SaaS) model.

Finally, SMEs should select vendors who can promptly respond to issues. The chosen vendor should have a good reputation and not neglect customers simply because of their smaller scale.
