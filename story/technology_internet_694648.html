Problems with static electricity during offset printing

by yudanr256 on 2012-03-07 10:51:32

Let's first understand what static electricity is? As we all know, any substance is made up of atoms, and the basic structure of atoms is protons, neutrons, and electrons. Scientists define protons as positively charged, neutrons as neutral, and electrons as negatively charged. Under normal circumstances, the number of protons in an atom is equal to the number of electrons, so positive and negative charges are relatively balanced, and therefore it appears not to be charged externally. However, due to external influences, such as friction or various forms of energy like kinetic energy, potential energy, thermal energy, chemical energy, etc., this can lead to an imbalance of positive and negative charges in atoms. In daily life, friction essentially refers to a process of continuous contact and rapid separation. Sometimes, static electricity can also be generated without friction, for example: induction electrostatic charging, thermoelectricity, piezoelectricity, Helmholtz layer, and jet electrostatic charging, etc. Any two objects made of different materials will produce static electricity when they come into contact and then separate. Moving air, of course, can also generate static electricity. Why does moving air produce static electricity? Because air is also made up of atoms, it can be said that at any time and any place in people's lives, static electricity may be generated. It is almost impossible to completely eliminate static electricity, but some measures can be taken to control static electricity so that it does not cause harm. Regarding static electricity in offset printing [http://www.sxywgs.com], during the offset printing process, static electricity is generated due to the high-speed rubbing and quick separation of the paper. When static electricity accumulates to a certain extent, it is very easy for discharge phenomena to occur, leading to serious consequences such as electric shocks or fires. When the static voltage is relatively high, static-charged ink can cause fire hazards, directly threatening the safety of printing operators and affecting the normal production of the printing factory. Paper surface being charged will attract paper scraps or dust and impurities floating in the air, seriously affecting the speed and quality of printing, causing problems such as poor impression and mottling on printed materials, resulting in defective printed products. There is also the phenomenon of ink carrying static electricity, which discharges during the printing process, causing large ink stains on the printed material. This phenomenon often occurs when using paper plate printing. When performing large-area solid bottom printing, the charged ink discharges at the edge of the printed material, causing ink whiskers to appear on the edge of the paper.

The main reasons for static electricity generation in offset printing: Material properties - including the intrinsic chemical composition of the material, internal structure of the substance, stress-strain mechanical characteristics, material shape, and conductivity, etc. The conductivity of the material has a dual effect on static electricity. If the material is a conductor, charges flow freely on the surface, and the distribution of these charges on the surface causes a lower voltage. Conductive materials immediately transfer charges to the ground upon contact with it. Insulating materials differ from conductors, and quite a few printing materials such as paper and plastic films, when charged, form higher voltages, and these voltages cannot be eliminated by grounding. Surrounding environmental conditions include: contact objects, surrounding gas composition, temperature, humidity, pressure, etc. Mechanical action situations include the type of contact between two materials, contact time, contact area, separation speed, and the nature of the material force, etc. The tighter the contact between two materials or the faster the separation, the greater the static electricity generated. Different types of material combinations result in different polarities of static electricity. Different materials have different intensities of static electricity. Regarding static electricity in printing paper, it is mainly due to the low humidity in the printing workshop and the long storage time of the paper, leading to low moisture content in the paper.
