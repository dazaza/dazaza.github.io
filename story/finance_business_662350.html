What are the specific clinical symptoms of cervical spondylosis? They are caused by degeneration of the cervical disc and osteophyte formation in the cervical vertebrae. The main manifestations include neck and shoulder pain, which radiates to the occipital region or upper limbs. In severe cases, spasticity of both lower limbs, difficulty walking, and even quadriplegia may occur. A few patients experience dizziness. Today, we will further explore the specific clinical symptoms of cervical spondylosis.

Many people want to know what the symptoms of cervical spondylosis are. Understanding its specific clinical symptoms can help you detect it in a timely manner. Based on clinical manifestations, cervical spondylosis can be classified into several types: cervical type, myelopathic type, vertebral artery type, radiculopathy type, sympathetic type, and other types. Therefore, the specific clinical symptoms of cervical spondylosis can vary widely.

Among these, cervical-type cervical spondylosis is more common and represents the earliest stage of the disease. It primarily affects the neck, with more young adults being affected. The main symptoms include localized pain sensations, neck discomfort, or restricted movement. Radiculopathy-type cervical spondylosis has the highest incidence rate and is more common in people over 40 years old. Initially, there is neck pain and stiffness; followed by shoulder and back pain or upper limb pain sensations. There may also be a feeling of heaviness in the upper limbs, reduced grip strength, sometimes dropping objects, and numbness in the fingers.
