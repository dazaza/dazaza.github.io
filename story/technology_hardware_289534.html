Zhang Fengxiang: A Skilled Hand "Embroidering" Spring's Return (Travel Companion)

by fengxiang on 2009-12-02 21:06:26

The interview with Zhang Fengxiang began with a discussion of his name. The name "Fengxiang" was given to him by his grandfather, who was an old traditional Chinese medicine practitioner. When he named his newborn grandson, perhaps he didn't think too much about it. Feng represents the king of birds, and xiang symbolizes spreading wings and flying high. However, it seems as if fate had destined this profound name to correspond with Zhang Fengxiang's life - he became a phoenix in the field of medical aesthetics in China, bringing beauty to millions of patients.

Establishing the first medical aesthetics department in China

Zhang Fengxiang is 56 years old this year. Since becoming a real doctor in 1976, he has been on the path of medicine for exactly 30 years. There are countless memories from these 30 years, and many stories to tell, like a dramatic TV series where the protagonist's destiny rises and falls with the plot. Zhang Fengxiang's story began with the establishment of China's first medical aesthetics department.

In 1968, after graduating from the Wuhan Military Region Medical College, Zhang Fengxiang, who came from a family of doctors, was assigned to the No. 193 Army Hospital of the People's Liberation Army as a military doctor. But good times didn't last long; after only half a year, he was transferred to the party committee office, beginning a career of working with words that lasted for eight years. It wasn't until 1976 that he returned to his position as a doctor. Surprisingly, Zhang Fengxiang, who was not usually talkative, did not simply stick to his duties but instead conceived an astonishing plan - to establish the first medical aesthetics department in the country.

What prompted Zhang Fengxiang to make this decision were the patients he encountered. In 1979, a large number of injured soldiers from the self-defense counterattack against Vietnam were sent to the No. 193 Army Hospital for treatment. These wounded officers and soldiers had scars all over their faces and bodies, and some were left permanently disabled. As a doctor, Zhang Fengxiang knew that if they couldn't be treated effectively and promptly, these young soldiers would spend their lives enduring the trauma of war. If that were the case, wouldn't they have to endure a lifetime of pain? At that moment, Zhang Fengxiang made up his mind to heal these disabled soldiers.

As Zhang Fengxiang began treating these soldiers, another patient came to see him. This patient suffered from post-acne facial scarring, known medically as atrophic scars, which led to repeated breakups. When he came full of hope to the No. 193 hospital, Zhang Fengxiang could only reply helplessly. The look of disappointment and pain on the patient's face shook Zhang Fengxiang again. He decided to become a "pioneer," overcome the difficulties of medical aesthetics, and restore beauty and hope to these tormented and suffering patients.

No one knows how determined Zhang Fengxiang was when he made this decision, nor does anyone know how much hardship and effort he put into realizing this ideal. To master the professional knowledge of medical aesthetics, Zhang Fengxiang worked normally during the day and studied textbooks at night. He finished a thick medical aesthetics textbook in just two months. Then he sought out books on domestic and international skin rejuvenation and beauty treatments through various channels and studied them carefully. Zhang Fengxiang knew that only by mastering solid theoretical knowledge could the next step of the plan proceed smoothly.

At the end of 1983, Zhang Fengxiang went to Shanghai and other places to learn basic skin rejuvenation knowledge. Zhang Fengxiang was like a sponge, continuously absorbing knowledge and nutrients in the field of medical aesthetics, especially focusing on learning the emerging beautification technique of dermabrasion. After returning from his studies, to make his techniques more proficient and ensure the academic success, Zhang Fengxiang began experimenting on animals. He himself lost count of how many times he experimented, but as long as it improved his clinical beauty skills, even if it took a thousand or ten thousand times, Zhang Fengxiang was determined to persevere.

On New Year's Day 1984, Zhang Fengxiang successfully performed his first facial dermabrasion surgery on a middle school teacher in Wuhan. The success of this surgery also led the hospital leadership to finally approve Zhang Fengxiang's suggestion to establish a medical aesthetics department. From then on, China's first medical aesthetics department was established at the No. 193 hospital, and Zhang Fengxiang thus embarked on the most glorious journey of his life.

Skilled Hands "Embroidering" Faces

In March 1990, CCTV interviewed Zhang Fengxiang, calling him a "sculptor under the shadowless lamp." Under the shadowless lamp, Zhang Fengxiang's hands were flexible, deep when necessary and shallow when appropriate, like an artist creating a work of art, skillfully achieving the desired results effortlessly.

Zhang Fengxiang deeply understood that cosmetic surgery was akin to embroidery on the patient's face. Done well, it could help patients regain confidence and happiness; done poorly, it could push them into an abyss of despair. Due to extensive experimentation on animals, Zhang Fengxiang's skills became increasingly proficient, reaching a level of perfection. Patients with mild facial aftereffects could return to normal after a single dermabrasion, while those with severe cases needed only two to three sessions to achieve satisfactory results. His creation of dermabrasion combined with release surgery brought good news to patients with large facial scars and facial dust staining.

Among the patients cured by Zhang Fengxiang was a coal miner surnamed Yang. During a blast, his face was injured, covered with soot, making him appear like a black pockmarked face. For a young man in the prime of his life, this was undoubtedly a catastrophic disaster. Xiao Yang sought treatment in big cities like Beijing and Shanghai, but when doctors saw his disfigured face, they all shook their heads and sighed, indicating they were powerless to help. Just as Xiao Yang fell into despair, Zhang Fengxiang brought him hope and restored his confidence in life. After two dermabrasions, the black pockmarked face disappeared, and the Xiao Yang in the mirror was still the handsome Xiao Yang he used to be, unchanged in any way.

There was also a young woman with acne on her face, leaving serious scars, causing her immense psychological and physiological pressure. Not only did she lack a boyfriend, but her normal work was also affected because of this. After Zhang Fengxiang learned about her situation, he told her: "You are a person too, and you have the right to pursue beauty." After two surgeries, the young woman told everyone: "Doctor Zhang not only cured my physical defects, but more importantly, he taught me how to love life."

His superb medical skills made Zhang Fengxiang famous, and patients from all over the country flocked to him. The aesthetic department expanded from originally having 8 beds to 30, and then from 30 to over 100 beds, yet it still couldn't meet the demand. To facilitate patient access, three medical aesthetic departments were opened in Hankou and Wuchang respectively.

Many people thought that with everything he has today, Zhang Fengxiang could finally take a breath and enjoy the fruits of his success. But Zhang Fengxiang was not satisfied with this; he had already begun his next research. In scar dermabrasion, carbide sand or silicon carbide grinding heads were traditionally used, but the脱落of sand and silicon during grinding affected the healing of the patient's wound. After repeated clinical practice, Zhang Fengxiang developed an olive-shaped stainless steel head, avoiding the problem of脱落and achieving high-quality grinding results.

Replacing bandages after surgery was also a problem Zhang Fengxiang wanted to solve. Typically, after surgery, patients need to change bandages once daily for five consecutive days. Each time the bandage is changed, the patient experiences excruciating pain. To fundamentally address this issue, after numerous trials and research, Zhang Fengxiang adopted non-layered sterile gauze that naturally peeled off. This method eliminated the pain of changing medication, prevented infection, and saved both the time of medical personnel and medical materials. Through Zhang Fengxiang's improved dermabrasion beauty procedure, it can be widely applied to more than 30 types of diseases, including acne, chickenpox, smallpox, eczema, traumatic scars, tattoos, etc. After surgical practice on over 20,000 people, excellent results were achieved. With his own hands, Zhang Fengxiang "wove" and "embroidered" on the faces of patients, helping them recover the beautiful appearances they had once lost.

Declaring War on Spots

During the process of treating patients, Zhang Fengxiang discovered a new problem - pigmentation on the patient's face after surgery. Treating facial scars shouldn't leave new spots for the patient. Almost simultaneously with his rigorous practice of dermabrasion, Zhang Fengxiang delved deeper into the research of spot removal.

Since pigmentary skin diseases have a relatively high incidence rate in China, such as melasma and freckles, which are common diseases, Zhang Fengxiang read a large amount of literature and gained new insights into the mechanism of pigment formation. He developed a Meifu Kang tablet (extracted from traditional Chinese medicine) as an oral medication, combined with the whitening cream he formulated for external use, integrating internal and external treatments to address both symptoms and root causes, achieving significant effects.

Initially using the drugs he formulated for patients, Zhang Fengxiang used toothpicks to apply the medicine on the affected area, which was time-consuming and inconvenient and unsafe. Therefore, Zhang Fengxiang invented a spot-removal beauty pen based on fluid mechanics principles. The beauty pen resembles a ballpoint pen used for writing, being convenient for carrying and storage, and safe to use, fundamentally solving the problem. As a medical aesthetics expert, Zhang Fengxiang expressed concern about some beauty salons currently on the market that exploit customers' urgent and blind psychology, using non-standard methods or even substances harmful to patients' health for spot removal.

Currently, spot-removal products generally use mercury to corrode the skin to achieve the effect of removing spots. However, to achieve rapid spot removal, the content of mercury in the spot-removal products used by most beauty salons often severely exceeds standards. Zhang Fengxiang said that mercury is a very "inert" substance, and many substances will oxidize in the air, but mercury will not change in the air for 100 years. If mercury enters the human body through the skin, the liver cannot "disinfect" it, nor will it be expelled from the body. Instead, it accumulates in the blood, hair, and cells of the human body, forming a highly toxic substance that harms the human body.

Zhang Fengxiang once encountered a patient with mercury poisoning. This patient received spot-removal beauty treatment at a certain beauty salon. However, this patient did not know she was allergic to mercury, resulting in mercury allergy symptoms during the use of the beauty salon's product, accompanied by red skin syndrome and high fever, posing a risk of death at any time. Although Zhang Fengxiang ultimately pulled this patient back from the brink of death, the severe harm caused by mercury was difficult to eliminate. Zhang Fengxiang warned consumers preparing to remove spots and those undergoing spot removal to adopt a scientific attitude and not rush for quick results, otherwise, they might achieve the opposite effect.

Peaches and plums do not speak, but paths form beneath them

Although Zhang Fengxiang has walked the path of medical aesthetics for nearly thirty years, and although he is already renowned in the industry, Zhang Fengxiang remains low-key. He is not good at expressing himself, but when talking about patients and his career, he seems to have endless things to say.

One year during the Spring Festival, on New Year's Eve, his family didn't see him come home. Originally, he was performing surgery on a university teacher from Tianjin. Zhang Fengxiang said that if I waited until after the holiday to perform the surgery, it would also be fine, but why let someone waste their time here unnecessarily? If I perform the surgery before the holiday, when I return to work, the patient can have their stitches removed and go home. My extra effort doesn't matter... Zhang Fengxiang did these things without expecting anything in return. He believed it was something a doctor should do for a patient, nothing worth boasting about. Zhang Fengxiang said he also accepted red envelopes from patients. He said: "If you don't accept it, the patient will feel uncertain and think you won't put your heart into the surgery, which will create mental pressure." However, the red envelopes he accepted were immediately handed over to the nurse manager to be deposited in the finance department. Many patients found extra money in their prepayment when settling their bills upon discharge, realizing that Zhang Fengxiang had returned the red envelope money to them in this way.

It is precisely because of Zhang Fengxiang's meticulous care for patients that his reputation has grown larger and larger. From 1984 to now, nearly 100,000 patients have come to him for plastic and beauty treatments. Some patients traveled thousands of miles to find Zhang Fengxiang, seeking "safety and peace of mind." Doctors coming to intern and study with him also come from all over the country. To date, Zhang Fengxiang has trained nearly 6,000 students, and wherever he goes, he finds his students. His students not only learned superior medical skills and a rigorous scientific attitude from him but also his compassionate spirit.

Sometimes Zhang Fengxiang jokes with his wife, saying: "For the sake of living, we run around everywhere." But his wife says: "You're not doing it for living; you're clearly doing it for your medical aesthetics career." Zhang Fengxiang truly loves this career from the bottom of his heart, and he plans to dedicate the rest of his life to it.
