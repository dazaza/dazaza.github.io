Love encountered for the first time staying in the girls' dormitory

by jj0505 on 2012-03-01 13:42:42

I remember meeting you on a very hot noon (maybe you don't remember), and I unintentionally caught your gaze. There was no spark, but I had an impulse to chat with you. So, I got to know you in such a simple way. At first, I had this indescribable feeling that I couldn't grasp myself, and later I realized that this thing is called love. I'm a veteran in matters of the heart, but I've never felt this way about a girl in such a short time, and this emotion is very different from before. I concluded that I must be crazy. We played around together, and I started to like being with you, always finding opportunities to talk to you. Even when you sometimes hit me, I still felt comfortable. Gradually, I wanted to see you more, and even saying a few more words to you made me happy. Seeing you cry, seeing you smile! But as books say, happy days are always few, and on the night we parted, we spent some time alone. I told you about my past relationships, and you confided in me about your past. When you were tired, I gently kissed you, and you didn't refuse. I didn't confess all night. The next day, I left without saying goodbye to you, with too much reluctance and helplessness in my heart, not allowing myself to love you.

After two days apart, I stayed in my dormitory for two days without going out. My friends said I was lost, and I didn't oppose it; I just smiled. You contacted me twice during those two days, but in front of you, I always acted like a rogue, a playboy. When you asked what I was doing, I always said I was at the bar. I wanted you to think I was a bad boy so that you wouldn't contact me anymore. Although I contacted you often, I hoped you would be cold towards me, not wanting you to care about me. I didn't dare to love you. What if you hurt me? I'd rather lock my heart in darkness; although it's cold, it doesn't hurt!

But people can't always go against themselves. Your one phone call made my heart feel warm again. Although it was just a few simple sentences, I let go of my love. Tomorrow is Qixi Festival, and I bought you chocolates and jelly beans. I want to confess my feelings to you on Qixi Festival. In the KFC at Guogou, I mustered up the courage to confess to you, just like my first love. I was actually shy and nervous, very afraid, and I tried hard to hide my embarrassment. When I asked you to be my girlfriend, you neither refused nor agreed. You said you were worried about some things, and I also knew that. The first time I wanted to hold your hand was at the entrance of Guogou. It was raining, and I instinctively reached for your hand. I don't know if it was intentional, but you pulled away. I knew that even though you didn't agree with me, I managed to hold your hand on the way back to your school. Your hand was so small and soft that I had an impulse to kiss it. I knew at that moment that I completely fell in love with you. After returning to school, I treated your roommate to dinner. I thought the test was over, but unexpectedly, your dorm mates questioned why you would love me. I couldn't answer, so I drank. I got drunk and said that I truly loved you. I took you to another room, held you in my arms, and told you that I loved you, hoping you would believe me. You believed me without any reason, just by your intuition. I was deeply moved. All the memories after we walked out of the hotel that night became blurred. I knew I was drunk. The next morning, I woke up to find myself sleeping outside the dormitory door on the ground. Once you start loving, you love fiercely. A few days later, I went to see you again. Our first date, I've never been willing to take a bus and walk to meet a girl. Friends know I'm lazy, but for you, I didn't mind the effort. When I arrived at your apartment, you were still asleep because you went to the KTV with friends last night and a group of men I didn't know. I was upset, but I didn't show it. I called you and you asked me to wait for you. For the first time waiting for a girl, I waited for fifty minutes, yet I didn't feel irritated at all. For the first time seeing you as lovers, your face turned red, so red. We both found it awkward to hold hands on the street. Finally, we just did it casually as you said, whichever way feels comfortable. For the first time, I sang to you, and for the first time, you said my singing sounded good.
