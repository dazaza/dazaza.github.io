I. Background of the Investigation

Electricity, a familiar term, has been deeply engraved in our hearts. Life cannot go on without electricity at any moment. However, as electrical appliances bring convenience with their rapid development, people's frequency of using electricity has sharply increased, leading to tight electricity supply in many places. In real life, how efficiently do people utilize electric energy? How strong is their energy-saving awareness? With these questions, we conducted a special investigation into household electricity use and energy-saving awareness.

II. Our investigation was divided into four steps:

1. A rough understanding of the overall situation of household electricity use

2. Designing the survey questionnaire

3. Conducting the survey

4. Summarizing the results

III. Data collection from the investigation

(1) Visiting the Old Town Power Supply Office to understand the local residents' electricity usage situation and learning some simple energy-saving knowledge from them.

(a) Electricity usage by Old Town residents in recent years (unit: ten thousand kWh)

The survey found: In recent years, electricity usage by Old Town residents has shown an upward trend.

(b) Distribution of electricity usage by Old Town residents in 2008

The survey showed that residential living electricity accounted for 46.6%; industrial electricity accounted for 47.4%; non-residential lighting electricity accounted for 2.4%;

commercial electricity accounted for 3.4%; agricultural production electricity accounted for 0.2%.

(c) Electricity distribution for home appliances of a certain household

Refrigerator: 49%; Air conditioner: 11%; TV: 15%; Computer: 16%; Light: 9%

(2) Field investigation and visits to Old Town Street:

Walking into Old Town Street, we also discovered...

Along the street, we saw some shops keeping their lights on during the day!

Televisions in the appliance stores were always on! Beijing Haier Refrigerator Repair Center!

Conclusion ①: It can be seen that the energy-saving awareness of residents is too weak!

(3) Investigation and visits to residential buildings:

We then entered nearby residential buildings...

Conclusion ②: During the investigation, some residents have realized the importance of saving electricity, but they just don't have the habit of doing so. They often act only when they remember (to save electricity), and forget about it when they don't. For example, most residents know that standby mode consumes electricity, but for convenience, few people turn off their home appliances from standby mode.

The role of 1 unit of electricity (1kWh) in the home:

1. It allows a 25W bulb to light continuously for 40 hours;

2. A TV can play for 10 hours;

3. A home refrigerator can run for 1 day;

4. A regular fan can run continuously for 15 hours;

5. It can boil 8kg of water;

Conclusion ③: There is an awareness and habit of saving electricity, but lack of methods

They know the price of electricity, many have solar-powered appliances, and can pay attention to choosing energy-efficient appliances when purchasing, but still fall short in some details. For example, they don't know that regularly cleaning appliances helps save electricity, they don't know what wattage light bulbs are both practical and energy-saving, and they don't know that regularly checking plugs and sockets also helps save electricity.

IV. Some energy-saving methods we've learned:

1. Sources of our learned energy-saving methods:

(1) Learned some basic energy-saving knowledge at the power supply office

Learning promotional materials, interviewing staff

(2) The boss of the appliance store enthusiastically introduced us to customers' habits when purchasing appliances and some energy-saving methods

(3) Through online resources

2. Sharing the energy-saving methods we've learned:

(1) Reasonably selecting high-efficiency energy-saving lamps (an 11-watt energy-saving lamp has the same brightness as a 60-watt incandescent bulb, with a lifespan 8-10 times longer than an incandescent bulb)

Basically all lamps in stores are energy-saving lamps

(2) Removing scale from the heating element in electric kettles can improve heating efficiency, saving electricity and extending its lifespan.

(3) The brighter and louder the television, the more electricity it consumes. By turning on a 5-watt energy-saving lamp indoors and appropriately adjusting the brightness and volume of the TV, you can get good viewing results while avoiding eye fatigue.

(4) When cooking rice, soaking the washed rice for 10 minutes before cooking can save electricity; when cooking the same amount of rice, a 700-watt rice cooker is more time and energy efficient than a 500-watt one.

(5) In summer, combining an air conditioner with a fan running at low speed can appropriately raise the set temperature of the air conditioner, providing comfort and saving electricity. Using curtains for shading can also save electricity used for air conditioning cooling.

(6) It is advisable to keep the refrigerator's storage capacity around 80%. Storing too little food reduces heat capacity, while storing too densely hinders cold air circulation, increasing the compressor's operating time.

V. Our Suggestions:

1. Each student should develop good energy-saving habits, master correct energy-saving methods, save more electricity, and conserve frequently.

2. Schools should strengthen students' education on energy-saving, cultivate their awareness of saving electricity, carry out thematic educational activities such as "Energy Conservation Publicity Week," and encourage everyone to save electricity together.

3. The Old Town government can also conduct "Energy-Saving Day" educational activities across the town, enhance publicity efforts, mobilize all citizens to save electricity, and actively build a conservation-oriented society.
