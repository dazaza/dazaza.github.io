Nike Air Max 90 Dutch Prince Johan Friso in Coma After Avala

by angleproxz on 2012-02-27 10:38:00

Prince Friso, who was on a skiing holiday in the Austrian resort of Lech, remained buried under snow for about 15 minutes before being rescued. Following the accident, in which no one else was injured, he was taken to a hospital in Innsbruck. The prince, 43, is the second son of Queen Beatrix of the Netherlands. However, he is not in line for the throne because he married in 2004 without the government’s approval. Queen Beatrix and the prince’s wife, Mabel, have visited him at Innsbruck’s University Hospital, as have his brothers, Crown Prince Willem-Alexander and Prince Constantijn. A friend of the prince who was skiing with him during the avalanche has been questioned by police.

'Avalanche Details'

Resort officials stated that the prince had been skiing off the marked pistes with between one and three other individuals when the avalanche struck shortly after midday local time last Friday. The prince is seen here at Lech in the Austrian Alps last year. He was reportedly buried by an avalanche measuring about 30m (yards) wide by 40m long. A beeper he was wearing allowed rescuers to locate him quickly.

Speaking to reporters in Innsbruck, Dr Wolfgang Koller said it took nearly 50 minutes to revive the prince. MRI scans have shown that his brain suffered "massive damage" in the avalanche. The prince will be moved at a later date to a private clinic for further treatment, but it may take years before he awakens, if ever, according to the doctor.

'Public Reaction'

The Dutch royal family is highly regarded by most people in the Netherlands. Queen's Day is one of the most widely celebrated national holidays. The news of Prince Friso’s condition has been met with outpourings of grief and sympathy. Prime Minister Mark Rutte’s office stated that he called Queen Beatrix on Friday morning to express that the "country sympathises deeply with the royal family in this time of concern and grief." As soon as the news broke, Prince Friso became a trending topic on the social networking site Twitter, with users expressing their love and support for his wife, Mabel, and their two young daughters, Luana and Zaria. Queen Beatrix has said that the family has been moved by the "countless" messages of condolence and encouragement they have received.

"We cannot say today with certainty whether Prince Friso will one day regain consciousness," added Dr Koller, who heads the trauma unit at the hospital. "In any case, a neurological rehabilitation will be required that will take months, if not years." The doctor explained that the prince’s brain had been deprived of oxygen due to the amount of time spent under the snow. "This resulted in a heart attack that lasted about 50 minutes.... Fifty minutes of reanimation is very, very long, one might even say too long. Our hope was that the patient’s mild hypothermia would provide some protection for the brain. This hope was not realized."

'The Incident'

The Dutch royal family regularly spends skiing holidays in Lech, located in Austria's western Vorarlberg province. Florian Moosbrugger, owner of the hotel where the royal family stays, had been skiing with the prince, a childhood friend. He survived the incident unscathed, having worn an avalanche airbag, and reportedly dug his friend out with his own hands and called the emergency services, according to the Austrian Times. The Dutch royal family has been visiting the prince in the hospital. He has been questioned by police seeking to establish which of the skiers went down the slope first and how the avalanche began. Mr Moosbrugger, who could reportedly face charges of "unintentional grievous bodily harm in particularly dangerous circumstances," says he is totally innocent. According to his mother Kristl, Queen Beatrix herself comforted him after he was questioned.
