Only Mind

No ocean view house? Luckily there's Nansha

"I have a house, facing the sea, with spring flowers blooming."

This line of poetry by Haizi always brings me warmth like a gentle breeze. I don't have an ocean view house, but I can go to Nansha.

On the map, Nansha looks like a large banana leaf spread out at the mouth of the Pearl River Estuary, witnessing the ebb and flow of time, from ancient times to modern days. It is because of Nansha's expansion on the map that Guangzhou has opened its door to the ocean. The city's layout becomes broader because of Nansha, and people's moods become more relaxed because of Nansha.

When you come to Nansha, you'll always have a feeling that the sky here is deep blue, and time seems to move slower by half a beat. There seems to never be traffic jams here, no need to queue up, no worries about smoggy skies. Nansha provides a serene ecological retreat for those who have been busy working all week in the city.

Urban development is full of contradictions. Skyscrapers along the main roads devour the greenery of mountains and fields, and people are eager to embrace the prosperity of the city while also longing to escape the noise within it. But they can't escape too far, preferably a short weekend trip, allowing them to leisurely wander between nature and the city. Therefore, places like Nansha, which are close to the urban core but not yet fully conquered by concrete and steel, have become convenient gateways for urbanites to connect with nature.

In Nansha, you can watch the mighty Pearl River rush into the sea, admire wetland birds soaring high, enjoy delicious fruits and vegetables from the waterlogged lands of Wanchingsha, and listen to the songs of fishermen in Hengli. Here, the scent of rain and soil often wafts towards you, straight into your heart.

Nights in Nansha can feel a bit lonely, with almost no light pollution, making the starry sky appear vast and profound. Members of the Astronomy Society often come here to observe stars, as finding a place to gaze at the stars is indeed not easy these days.

But after escaping, one must eventually return. As the car travels on the highway and sees the distant lights of the bustling city gradually becoming clearer, I know that the busy new week is about to begin again. Looking through the camera lens, seeing the waves, sunset, and reeds captured, there's a sense of ease worth savoring.

Ultimately, the purpose of escaping is still to return.

Main Story

Not Lonely on Lingdingyang

The wise find joy in mountains, the intelligent in water.

Guangzhou, backed by Baiyun Mountain to the north and overlooking Lingdingyang to the south, fulfills people's desire to get close to nature and enjoy leisure time. In fact, Nansha used to seem distant to Guangzhou residents until the subway lines were laid and road networks connected, making people realize that this is Guangzhou's own Shangri-La, where the tense nerves of urban life can slowly relax.

Nansha was once humorously referred to as Panyu's "Siberia," with desolate fishing villages and fierce typhoons making it appear overly wild. Not until the late 1980s, when business magnate Ho Yin-tung docked his boat here and immediately recognized this feng shui treasure land, did he start building roads and bridges, opening mountains and filling seas, laying the groundwork for infrastructure projects and officially kicking off the development of Nansha.

Under Ho Yin-tung's investment lead, Nansha gradually became a hotspot for investment, revealing its economic value unprecedentedly in the early 21st century. In 2005, Guangzhou implemented administrative district adjustments, establishing the Nansha administrative district, marking a new historical phase in Nansha's large-scale development. According to statistics, from 2002 to 2007, Nansha's GDP increased from 6.17 billion yuan to 30.568 billion yuan, forming seven major industrial bases for automobiles, shipbuilding, steel, machinery, electronics, chemicals, and port logistics, becoming the "engine" of economic development in the Guangzhou area.

Do not assume that Nansha shows only a stern industrial face after its grand development. In fact, the charm of Nansha lies in its coastal scenery and local customs. In recent years, Guangzhou's largest waterside park has been built here, acquiring Guangzhou's only beach, and in the future, Guangzhou's largest yacht club will also be constructed here. Because of Nansha, Guangzhou is one step closer to the sea.

In recent years, the controversial petrochemical project in Nansha has settled, with the integrated refining and chemical project, expected to invest over $5 billion and produce 15 million tons, deciding to relocate to Zhanjiang. Now, Nansha has become a short-distance travel choice for people from Guangzhou and many other cities in the Pearl River Delta. During the three-day "May Day" mini-golden week this year, Nansha received over 400,000 tourists. Some people simply love the tranquility and freedom of Nansha so much that they buy a property here, using it as their second home on weekends and holidays.

Field Report

It's best to take the subway to Nansha, not only because it's convenient and fast, but more importantly, you can get a preview of Nansha's landscape. After leaving Xinzao station on Line 4, the train soon emerges from underground and races on an elevated bridge. You can enjoy the views along the way in the bright carriage, experiencing the smooth sensation of wide-open spaces. On a lazy Sunday afternoon, let us pack our bags lightly and embark on a leisurely journey to Nansha.

Wetlands: Beautiful Water Views and Lotus Colors, Joyful Birds and People

The first stop of the trip is at Wanqingsha, the southernmost tip of Nansha. At the tail of the eighteenth river channel in the Sha Wei Reclamation Area, there is Guangzhou's largest wetland park. We boarded an electric small boat and headed deep into the wetland park.

Like a maze on the water, the small boat weaves through low and lush mangroves, surrounded by lotus ponds, reeds, and flocks of wild ducks. There's no sound of urban clamor, only the chirping of birds singing softly. If it's winter, you'll see tens of thousands of migratory birds dancing gracefully here.

Nansha Wetlands are known as "Guangzhou's Kidneys." Located at the confluence of three rivers at the estuary, sediment has formed extensive mudflats here over the years. People planted large amounts of mangroves and reeds during the process of reclaiming and cultivating land. One day, people were surprised to discover that tens of thousands of red-billed gulls had gathered here, indicating that the Nansha Wetlands had become a breeding and resting ground for East Asian international migratory birds, forming a rare bird paradise in the Pearl River Delta. The Nansha Wetlands are now listed alongside Hong Kong's Mai Po, Shenzhen's Futian, and Zhuhai's Qi'ao Island wetlands as four major important wetlands at the Pearl River Estuary.

According to monitoring by the South China Endangered Animal Research Institute, the Nansha Wetlands have recorded 121 species of birds, accounting for 9.1% of the total number of bird species nationwide. Among them, one species of national first-class protected animal (Oriental White Stork) and seven species of national second-class protected animals (Black-faced Spoonbill, White Spoonbill, Common Buzzard, Black Crowned Falcon Eagle, Peregrine Falcon, Lesser Coucal, and Brown Coucal) have been recorded. The Black-faced Spoonbill currently has only about 700 left in the world, making it even rarer than the giant panda.

The Nansha Forestry Department has decided to expand the size of the wetland park by 2-3 times this year, planning to build a national-level wetland park within 3-5 years. By then, the existing 3,400 mu wetland plus another 6,000 mu wetland on the west side will be connected by bridges, forming Guangzhou's only ten-thousand-mu wetland park.

Visiting Ancient Sites: Fortresses Still Stand, Copper Gates Hard to Find

As an administrative district, Nansha may be young, but its history is far from thin. It bears a tragic history of resisting foreign invasions, showcasing a heavy national spirit. For many people, they might have learned about the name "Humen Fortress" in their history textbooks before hearing about Nansha. Yes, the Humen Fortress is in Nansha, where the first shot of the Opium War was fired.

Nowadays, if you want to explore these ancient sites, you should first take a speedboat from the foot of the Humen Bridge to Hengdang Island. Hengdang Island is divided into upper and lower sections, geographically controlling the throat of the Pearl River Estuary, linking with Dajiao Mountain on the opposite bank of the Pearl River to form the first line of defense for the Pearl River and Guangzhou. The Qing Dynasty deployed the densest artillery array of modern South China maritime defense here. It's said that during the Opium War, Qing soldiers placed iron chains across the river here to prevent British warships from entering the Pearl River, forming the famous "Golden Lock and Copper Gate."

However, there are no traces of the iron chains on the island now, and several Krupp cannons purchased from Germany were destroyed during the Anti-Japanese War, leaving only empty cannon platforms facing the vast sky. The island has restored its former tranquility, with the old barracks and ammunition depots still well-preserved. Walking into the moss-covered trenches and tunnels feels like stepping into a time tunnel, returning to the era of gunfire and warfare, evoking a sense of the passage of time.

In fact, Nansha's history is much older than people imagine. In 2001, during an archaeological excavation in Lujing Village, Nansha, a Shang Dynasty tomb was discovered, unearthing a complete skeleton of a Shang Dynasty person, dating back over 3,000 years. Later, anthropologists reconstructed his head image and named him "Nansha Man," representing the earliest image of a Guangzhou resident. Today, at the Tianhou Palace Park in Nansha, you can see the sculpture of this reconstructed head image.

Beach: Reed Waves, Sea Breeze Brings Coolness

Beaches are the most typical and romantic symbols of coastal cities. As the only area near the sea in Guangzhou, Nansha's only regret might be the lack of natural beaches. A few years ago, visitors to the eastern scenic area of Nansha, Tianhou Palace, would remember that the shore at the mouth of the Pearl River was still a marshland covered with reeds, appearing somewhat desolate.

Last year, Nansha specially transported 6,000 cubic meters of high-quality fine sand from Guangxi, spraying it onto the coastal marshes to create an artificial beach nearly 400 meters long and 60 centimeters thick, thus rewriting the face of Guangzhou without beaches. Nan Yue Garden has created a romantic "blue sea silver beach" for visitors coming to Nansha.

The best time to visit the beach at Tianhou Palace is in the evening, as the tide rises, merging the sea and sky, dissipating the sweltering heat of the day, and bringing a refreshing sea breeze. Take off your shoes and stroll on the beach, feeling the gentle and free rhythm of the fine sand and the lapping of the tide against your feet. Couples from the university town often take the Line 4 subway to the Nansha seaside, leaving romantic images witnessed by the river and sea on this relatively small beach.

If you're lucky, you might catch a small concert on the Nansha beach. As night falls and the river flows ceaselessly, the beautiful guitar strings subtly stir emotions deep within your heart, perhaps bringing a touch of mild melancholy.

At such moments, drinking away sorrows seems like a good option. Just next to Tianhou Palace at Pu Zhou Garden, you can find a well-known beer house. The beer here is brewed entirely using German technology, available in varieties like wheat beer, barley beer, and black beer, with a crisp taste that doesn't easily intoxicate. Gather a few friends, sit around a table outdoors, drink and chat, and life doesn't get better than this.

Verification

Scale: The total area of the district is 527.65 square kilometers, covering one street and three towns: Nansha Street, Wanqingsha Town, Huangge Town, and Hengli Town.

Active hours for human activity: From 10 am to 9 pm.

Main active population: Civil servants, industrial workers, local fishermen, family travelers.

Leisure theme: Ocean, water town, ecology.

Nansha boasts a unique geographical environment, located at the geometric center of the Pearl River Delta Economic Zone. To the east, it overlooks the sea across from Dongguan's Humen, and to the west, it connects with Zhongshan City. With Nansha as the center, within a radius of 60 kilometers, there are 14 large and medium-sized cities in the Pearl River Delta, making it the confluence of the Xijiang, Beijiang, and Dongjiang rivers. Nansha is 38 nautical miles from Hong Kong by sea and 41 nautical miles from Macau. In aviation, there are international airports nearby in Guangzhou, Hong Kong, and Macau.

In terms of development planning, Nansha's spatial structure and layout are divided into one center and five clusters: with areas along both sides of the Jiaomen River, along the port access road, and including the Humen Bridge-Dajiao Mountain waterside park as the central area of the coastal new city, Huangge comprehensive industrial cluster, southern Nansha Island high-tech industry cluster, regional comprehensive service cluster, Wanqingsha port-industry cluster, and Longxue Island modern logistics comprehensive cluster.

The popularity of the water town is increasing

Mr. Luo, relevant person in charge of the Nansha District Tourism Bureau

In recent years, the number of tourists visiting Nansha has been growing at a rate of 20% annually. In 2008, 2.7 million people visited Nansha, generating a total tourism income of 540 million yuan. The popularity here is getting stronger, and this year the number of tourists is expected to exceed 3 million.

The theme of Nansha tourism is the coastal water town, and the ocean is Nansha's most precious resource. For short-distance tourism, the easiest way for Guangzhou residents to see the sea is to come to Nansha. Therefore, in the next step, we will upgrade and transform Nansha's tourism projects around the marine theme in two parts.

Firstly, the eastern scenic spots of Tianhou Palace and the coastal park have expanded the beach from less than 500 meters last year to 1 kilometer this year. In the future, we will introduce recreational facilities such as ATVs and beach volleyball. The adjacent Pu Zhou Garden is also adding recreational facilities. The highly anticipated Upper and Lower Hengdang Islands are not yet officially open, and we are advancing the construction of the docks, aiming to allow tourists to visit the islands next year.

Another point of upgrade for Nansha tourism is in the south, where the nineteenth channel of Wanqingsha is currently undergoing renovations and upgrades to its catering industry. In fact, Nansha's cuisine is very distinctive. Seafood from the brackish water boundary is unique to Nansha. In the future, we will turn this place into a fishing port, where tourists can not only buy specialties but also bring seafood to restaurants for processing.

Notably, Nansha is preparing to launch port industrial tourism. In the future, people can visit ports and the shipyard on Longxue Island in Nansha, allowing tourists to experience the charm of Nansha's industries up close.

The Former Glory of the Southern Gateway Remains

Director of the Humen Fortress Management Office of Nansha District, Mr. Huang Liping

Nansha's history and culture are significant in the history of modern Chinese maritime defense. Dense fortifications like those on Hengdang Island are not common along China's coastlines. The Humen Fortress has 17 gun emplacements from the Qing Guangxu period, as well as official offices, powder magazines, connecting tunnels, etc. From the Qing Dynasty to the Republic of China and up to the early liberation period, this was a military restricted area, so there are not many descriptions of Hengdang Island, maintaining a mysterious veil for tourists.

Yong'an Fortress on the Upper Hengdang Island was personally designed by Guan Tianpei. During the war, the fortress was bombed, leaving only half standing, evoking deep feelings upon sight. There are too many historical anecdotes about the Lingdingyang in Nansha. The Southern Song poet Wen Tianxiang once wrote the famous poem here: "Since ancient times, who among men has escaped death? Let my loyal heart shine in history."

What we are doing now is to restore the original appearance of the fortresses at the entrance of the Humen as soon as possible, recreating the former glory of China's southern gateway.

Analects

"Coastal" Is Not Nansha's Greatest Advantage

Director of the Provincial Tourism Development Research Center, Chen Nanjiang

Reporter: As Guangzhou's only coastal area, what do you think is Nansha's greatest tourism advantage?

Chen Nanjiang: Nansha's coastal advantages are not significant, mainly because it is in an inland river near the estuary, where sedimentation forms mudflats rather than sandy beaches, and the river water quality is not good. Moreover, Zhongshan to the south has ocean hot springs and Lingdingyang historical relics, and Dongguan across the river has Weiyuan Island and Humen Fortress. I believe Nansha's greatest tourism advantages lie in its natural ecology, industrial tourism, and Mazu religious tourism.

Reporter: In which aspects can Nansha's tourism resources be further explored in the future?

Chen Nanjiang: Nansha can develop more rural resort, educational tourism, religious meditation tourism products. For example, enhancing the influence of Tianhou Palace, utilizing the Humen Bridge and country parks, developing industrial tourism and rural tourism, etc. The wetland park should be well developed, drawing on the experience of Hong Kong's wetland park to form its own local cultural characteristics.
