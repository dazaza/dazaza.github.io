A Nightmare Experience: The Only Princess in History Sold as a Slave.

by xtyzry on 2007-06-05 17:05:30

In history, when the country was in ruins and families were destroyed, the unfortunate experiences of princesses who insisted on living on were various and strange beyond our imagination and beyond the scope of historians' records. We know very little about it. Pain is like a meteor thousands of years ago. It quietly slipped away before we paid attention to it, forever silent. But history did record an example of a princess selling herself into slavery. That was the nightmare experience of Princess Linhai, the second daughter of Emperor Hui of the Western Jin Dynasty.

Empress Jia of Emperor Hui of Jin had four daughters: the eldest daughter Princess Hedong, the second daughter Princess Linhai, the third daughter Princess Shiping, and the youngest daughter Princess Aixian. Among them, the second daughter Princess Linhai had the worst luck.

Princess Linhai was originally titled Princess Qinghe. After the fall of the Western Jin Dynasty, Luoyang fell into chaos, and members of the royal family fled for their lives. She got separated from her family and sisters on the way. A villager found the destitute Princess Qinghe. He didn't know her true identity and sold her to Qian Wen of Wuxing County for a few coins.

Qian Wen had a daughter who was spoiled and selfish since childhood, especially towards the servants around her. She gave orders imperiously and beat or scolded them at will. After Qian Wen bought the princess, he gave her to his beloved daughter as a maid. Thus began the most difficult and humble period of the princess's life.

Princess Qinghe was dignified in appearance. Because she carried the bloodline of the imperial family, her every step was elegant, a kind of demeanor that no common lady could match. This made Qian's daughter jealous. She tried to find out the princess's identity before she was sold, but the princess avoided talking about it.

Qian's daughter was quite cruel to the princess. She ordered her to do all sorts of things at will, and if anything went wrong, she would whip her, lock her in a dark room, and deprive her of food and water for days and nights. The princess was lonely and friendless, with nowhere to turn. She could only swallow her pain silently. She thought that no matter how prosperous her past life as a princess was, it was all just a fleeting cloud. She accepted her fate.

Emperor Yuan of Jin restored the Jin Dynasty in Jiangnan, historically known as the Eastern Jin Dynasty.

When Princess Qinghe heard this news, it was like grabbing a glimmer of hope in the darkness. When Qian's daughter sent her out to buy cosmetics, she took the opportunity to escape from the fire pit. After going through countless hardships, she reached the capital and sought an audience with the current emperor. However, the guards at the gate saw her ragged clothes and servant-like appearance and didn't believe she was the daughter of the former Emperor Hui. After she swore repeatedly, the guards were half-convinced and finally agreed to report it upward.

In front of Emperor Yuan, the princess wept and recounted the turmoil and hardships she had endured over the years. Emperor Yuan lamented the misfortune of the princess and was furious at Qian Wen and his daughter for abusing her so cruelly. He ordered Qian Wen and his daughter to be brought in and locked up in a death cell.

Not long after, Emperor Yuan changed Princess Qinghe's title to Princess Linhai and married her to Cao Tong of the Zongzheng family. The princess's life finally cleared up. Her experience as a princess being sold into slavery was also considered unique in history.
