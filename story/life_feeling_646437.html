A ten-yuan ring, omelet rice love

by meimei234 on 2011-12-28 23:54:52

That was a happy time, when the two of us only had one hundred yuan in our pockets.

I suddenly showed up in his city with nothing but the clothes on my back. I said to him, "Let's just be together." He hugged me tightly.

One of his friends borrowed his savings for a lawsuit. So, he didn't have much money left.

We rented an apartment in a very quiet neighborhood and bought necessities. After opening our wallet and counting, we only had one hundred yuan left.

He said, "It doesn't matter, we can borrow some from friends. It will be fine once we get paid."

I said, "No borrowing. If you borrow once, there will be a second time. We need to endure any hardship if we want to be together forever."

I lay on the bed and started dividing the money.

Thirty yuan was for his bus fare in the morning. Occasionally, when the weather was bad, it could be used for a taxi ride home.

Fifteen days, we just needed to last fifteen days until his paycheck came.

Another thirty yuan was for his breakfast.

The remaining thirty yuan was for our dinner.

Ten yuan was reserved as emergency funds.

He lay on top of me, calling me wife. He buried his face in my chest, silent. I knew he was deeply moved inside.

So we embraced each other, I hummed a little tune, and he kept his face buried in my chest listening to my humming.

He said, "This is how much I love you."

I said, "We will be together forever."

On the first morning, I got up with him, watched him wash his face and brush his teeth, then held hands and saw him off to work. I watched him get on the bus. There were many people on the bus, and I saw him squeezed among them, happiness written all over his eyes. We waved goodbye, and I watched the bus drive away before returning home to do laundry. I cleaned the room, using a clean cloth to wipe the floor, kitchenware, and doors.

The wind chimes on the balcony made a crisp sound, ding, ding, dang... dang... dong... dong... I looked at them and smiled. That was the applause for our happiness tomorrow.

At noon, he asked me if I had eaten lunch.

I said, "Yes."

Actually, I hadn't. I wanted us to get through this month without borrowing money.

I was so stubborn, keeping his pain in my heart, refusing to let him borrow money.

So, when he returned and placed a thousand yuan in my hand, I cried.

I said, "I'm not someone who fears hardship. But, I want us to be able to persevere through anything, just like how I came to your city, persevered by leaving my parents behind, giving up my job, leaving my friends far away. I came to this unfamiliar city just to be with you."

I accompanied him, taking the bus to his friend's house to return the money.

That night, the sky was beautiful, filled with stars. On the grass, I lay on his lap, counting the stars. I said, "How beautiful the sky is, how beautiful our love is."

Let me recite some poetry for you. Whose? Hmm, Qin Guan's 'Magpie Bridge', Su Shi's 'Divination Son'. Unconsciously, I recited 'River Town'. He leaned down and kissed me. He said, "We will never separate."

Thus, we said, "Only when the mountains lose their peaks, heaven and earth unite, would I dare to part from you."

I was afraid he wouldn't eat breakfast, so I often bought it for him. Once, at 11:30 PM, we were both asleep, and I dreamed of many bakeries. When I woke up, I realized I forgot to buy him breakfast. Wearing my pajamas, I ran downstairs. He stopped me, asking where I was going, worried I might fall.

I said, "Sorry, I forgot to buy you breakfast."

I saw him cry. At the door, he kissed me without reservation, making me unable to breathe.

That night, I bought sweet pies. What a familiar name.

In the evening, it was interesting, eggs cost 2.4 yuan per catty. I knew how to pick fresh ones. The market was dirty, I squeezed among a group of middle-aged women, picking eggs. I could bargain.

Dinner was made by me, buying a little bit of mushrooms. Placed in boiling water, then beat the eggs into it, watching it boil. Added a little scallion and chicken essence. Very fragrant. A bowl of soup cost us about eighty cents.

He liked spinach, which was very cheap. One yuan for a bunch. I could make various styles of spinach dishes. For example, boiled or steamed. He ate very happily. Watching him, I laughed.

Sometimes, we would only have fried rice with egg. This was my specialty, he often praised me. I learned this from an experienced chef.

First, put oil in the pan, add the rice, stir a bit, add a little salt.

Then, place it in a big bowl.

Take a small bowl, beat two or three eggs, stir. Add monosodium glutamate, salt, and other seasonings. Stir evenly, then add oil to the pan. Put the eggs in, use chopsticks to stir. Let the eggs scatter, don't overcook them.

Then, mix the rice and eggs in the pan. Cover for a few minutes.

Then wait for him to come home to eat.

We ate from a large bowl, two spoons, heads facing each other. Laughing happily. A bowl of light mushroom soup, a large bowl of fried rice. He told me some happy stories.

During that time, I didn't go out, I didn't want to spend money. There was no TV at home, nothing, but I had plenty to do. I had to air our quilts. I loved smelling the scent of the sun when sleeping at night. Like our love, warm and pleasant.

He also liked the smell of the sun. But, he preferred holding me, smelling my scent.

He said, "That's the scent of a good wife."

In those fifteen days, we spent eighty-five yuan.

He spent twenty-six yuan on bus fare. Breakfast, thirty yuan. I didn't eat lunch. Dinner, twenty-nine yuan.

After he received his paycheck, he took me shopping and said, "What do you want? Let me buy it for you."

I took him to a small bustling area I passed by every time I bought groceries. There were many small stalls. I pointed to a ring in a small box and said, "I want that."

That was a ten-yuan ring.
