This questionnaire was sent out to 17,636 people and received 417 valid replies regarding online shopping on Taobao. Among the respondents, while half of the netizens believed that giving around 200 yuan as New Year’s money (lucky money) to children was appropriate, those choosing 500 yuan and 1000 yuan or more accounted for 22.8% and 18.9%, respectively.

### Analysis:

#### Lucky Money Starting Point Raised to a Thousand Yuan

Ms. Song mentioned that lucky money is originally a material form of blessing given to children, but she personally believes that giving gifts can better express the affection of elders.

"During the Spring Festival, seeing the joy on children's faces when they receive their lucky money makes us feel happy too. However, now both elders and children compare the amount of lucky money, and the meaning of red envelopes seems to have changed," said Ms. Song. Originally, giving a few candies during the New Year could make children happy for many days, but now issues such as how much lucky money to give and how to handle children's lucky money have arisen.

#### Case Study:

Almost 20% of netizens plan to increase the lucky money to a thousand yuan.

From the survey results, it can be seen that some netizens are considering increasing the lucky money to a thousand yuan. In contrast, only 3.6% of netizens plan to raise it to 800 yuan this year. Therefore, some netizens expressed that there are currently two standards for lucky money: one recognized by salaried workers at 200 yuan, and another being a thousand yuan or more. "Personally, I think if you plan to give 800 yuan as lucky money, it would be better to give 1000 yuan instead," said one netizen.

#### Each Child Receives 500 Yuan

"I clearly remember last year giving my eldest nephew a 300-yuan red envelope, but others gave 500 yuan, which made me feel very embarrassed," said netizen 'Windflower Snow Moon' while participating in the discussion about lucky money. This year, he plans to give his eldest nephew 800 yuan to reclaim the lost face. Another netizen shared the same view. "If everyone else raises the amount of lucky money, even if I am tight on cash, I still wouldn't feel right not following."

Yesterday, Ms. Wang mentioned that just the lucky money for children during the Spring Festival will cost her 2000 yuan. She told reporters that although she works in Changchun, most of her relatives live in Shenyang, so every Spring Festival she has to prepare lucky money for younger generations. She introduced that including nephews and nieces, there are four children in her family. Giving each child 500 yuan means spending a total of 2000 yuan. "With the improvement in living standards, giving 200-300 yuan as a red envelope feels a bit embarrassing now."

It can be said that giving lucky money to younger generations in recent years has become a hot topic. For this reason, I conducted a questionnaire through our QQ platform asking, "How much do you plan to increase your lucky money this year?"

#### "Face-Saving" Leading to Annual Increase in Lucky Money

From the original 50 yuan, to 100-200 yuan, then 300-600 yuan, now to 800 yuan or more than a thousand yuan. With the rise in prices and the improvement in living standards, lucky money has also been increasing annually. Some netizens frankly admitted that the increase in lucky money is related to "face-saving".

Ms. Zhang also said that under the trend of increasingly larger red envelopes, she prefers giving children gifts. "Giving children gifts they like, such as clothes, toys, school supplies, etc., can express affection on one hand, and save part of the expenses on the other, making it a win-win situation," she said.

Ms. Wang is not an isolated case; many citizens expressed that giving lucky money to younger generations during the Spring Festival indeed adds pressure. "Fortunately, each family only has one child; otherwise, we really couldn't afford it," Ms. Liu said. Many of her friends have raised the amount of lucky money they give to children to a thousand yuan.

#### Improvement in Living Standards

#### Perspective:

The Urban Evening News reported that with the Spring Festival approaching, many citizens are planning to give red envelopes to younger generations. However, in recent years, with the rise in prices and living standards, the amount of lucky money given to children has also increased significantly. Through our QQ platform, I conducted a relevant questionnaire survey involving 17,636 participants, and nearly 20% of netizens indicated that the starting point for giving lucky money to children has been raised to a thousand yuan.
