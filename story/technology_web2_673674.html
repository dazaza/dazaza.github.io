Time leaves no trace, life just passes by day by day, and I always think of Zhu Ziqing's "Haste". Actually, in my early years, during university or even until after starting work, I loved literature more than anything else, favoring experiencing sensibility over rationality. But at some point, sensibility left me, and when it occasionally surfaces in my heart, all I can say is: why do our days never return? When eating, the days flow past the edge of the bowl; when drinking water, the days flow past the water. Darling, why do our days never return?

My favorite was actually a non-main character from Gu Long's novels named "Flower Full Building". He was blind but a cultivated and elegant man, with a faint smile always on his face. I most wanted to be a man like him, but times change, and I became the protagonist in my world. I also loved reading about the love between Bu Jingyun and Xue Yuan in "Stormy Clouds", their love moving heaven and earth, making me shed tears while reading.

I also loved writing letters to my family on rainy autumn evenings when the wind and rain came suddenly. Talking about how trees wish to be still but the wind won't stop, and how children wish to take care of their parents but they are no longer around. Nowadays, I can only enjoy such beautiful moments alone in the quiet depths of the night, undisturbed.

Time leaves its mark on people, but I feel the marks on me are heavier. Why do I say that?

I have seen people who become more radical due to time, fighting against it. I have seen frustrated people who cannot adapt deeply enough to time. I am not either of these types, but rather someone who adapts to time and forgets themselves in the process. Time has changed me, but it's not visible. It's not visible because we have strangely unified in this moment. Therefore, what it gives me is the deepest and hardest to express.

It is in such times that I follow it in the most flamboyant or the most silent way, trying to make small changes to it. Adapting itself is a kind of silence!
