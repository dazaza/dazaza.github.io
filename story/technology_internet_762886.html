Zhou Hongyi Exposes Himself as the Spoiler in the Baidu's 91 Transaction Case: Participated in Price Hiking

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:49:57

July 18 news, Chairman and CEO of 360 Company, Hongyi Zhou was recently invited by PingWest to give a semi-formal speech at Stanford University. During the event, he revealed that he had once disrupted Baidu's acquisition of 91 Wireless for $1.9 billion. He claimed that if it weren't for the presence of 360, some companies (note: referring to Baidu) would not have felt enough pressure to offer such a price.

Hongyi Zhou stated that 360 was also one of the early investors in 91 Wireless. In the early stages, he had advised the people from 91 Wireless not to sell their company, or at least try not to sell it unless absolutely necessary. Even as bids started coming in, Zhou still suggested they avoid selling the company and instead develop independently unless someone offered an irresistible price.

"They asked me what this irresistible price would be? I said it should be at least $2 billion," Zhou mentioned, believing at the time that no one would pay such a high price for 91 Wireless. "Unexpectedly, after some negotiation, they managed to settle at $1.9 billion."

360 and 91 Wireless indeed share a deep history, but their relationship might not be as good as Zhou described. Two years ago, when 360 had yet to enter the mobile assistant market, their strategy was to collaborate with NetDragon's 91 Mobile Assistant to further layout in the mobile internet sector. 360 partnered with NetDragon to establish a research and development center, sending R&D personnel to assist in developing the new version of the 91 Mobile Assistant.

Nowadays, the 91 Mobile Assistant under 91 Wireless and 360 Mobile Assistant have become each other's biggest competitors. NetDragon may feel somewhat regretful about fostering a competitor. It is unlikely that Hongyi Zhou and the senior management of 91 Wireless are still on good terms. However, 360 was indeed a disruptor in the sale of 91 Wireless. Tech Tencent learned that just before the finalization of Baidu's acquisition of 91 Wireless, Zhou flew to Fujian to discuss with NetDragon founder Dejian Liu, attempting to prevent the deal from going through.

Moreover, Zhou's attempt to stop Baidu's acquisition of 91 Wireless had another purpose: After Baidu acquired 91 Wireless, the combination of Baidu Mobile Assistant, 91 Mobile Assistant, and Android Market could quickly take the lead in the wireless distribution field, posing a threat to 360 Mobile Assistant. This acquisition also provided an important balance against 360's offensive moves in Baidu's search domain.
