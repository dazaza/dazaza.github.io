Iranian plane crash kills 168, including 10 judo nationals

by kaxiya on 2009-07-16 14:21:25

Comprehensive message: An Iranian passenger plane crashed in the northwest of Tehran on the 15th. None of the 168 people onboard survived. The authorities of Tehran airport said that there were no Chinese passengers onboard. This is Iran's most serious air disaster in recent years.

According to the website of the Iranian national television, this aircraft was flight 7908 of Caspian Airlines, which was originally scheduled to fly from Tehran to Yerevan, the capital of Armenia. It fell into a farm in the western province of Qazvin 16 minutes after takeoff.

The passenger plane exploded violently after falling to the ground

According to reports by the French News Agency, there were 153 passengers and 15 crew members on board. In addition, the head of the Iranian national judo youth team, eight athletes and two trainers were also on board.

The head of the emergency rescue department of Qazvin said that the plane caught fire and was seriously damaged after the crash, with debris scattered everywhere. All the people on board "died".

An eyewitness said that the plane was already on fire in the air and a violent explosion occurred when it hit the ground, leaving a huge hole on the ground. Armenian TV reported that the crash was caused by an engine fire.

A semi-official Fars news agency quoted an eyewitness as saying: "I saw the plane... It was about to hit the ground. The landing gear was out and the bottom of the plane was on fire. It looked like the pilot was trying to land. Then, the plane hit the ground, broke into pieces, and scattered around." TV footage showed a large crater at the crash site with scattered aircraft debris.

It is suspected that mechanical failure caused the engine to catch fire

A rescuer told a French News Agency reporter that the scene was full of "broken bones and flesh." He said: "There are no recognizable complete remains at the scene, not even a complete finger." There was a body bag filled with human flesh next to him.

Mohemani, the general manager of the Iranian Airport Administration, said that the content of the last communication between the pilot and the ground personnel was "normal and did not indicate any technical failures." However, senior provincial official Saberi said that the aircraft had experienced technical problems and attempted an emergency landing but failed.

Iranian President Ahmadinejad has ordered the Ministry of Transport to investigate the cause of the air disaster.

The aging aircraft lacked maintenance and repair

Iranian civil and military aircraft generally have aged fleets, and lack maintenance and repair, resulting in poor condition. Due to sanctions, Iran cannot purchase American Boeing or European Airbus planes.

Caspian Airlines, a joint venture between Iran and Russia established in 1992, uses Russian-made aircraft exclusively. According to the company's website, Caspian Airlines has more than fifty weekly flights and many chartered flights to Iranian cities and several destinations in the Middle East and Eastern Europe.

Since its debut in 1968, the Tu-154 passenger aircraft has been involved in at least 30 air disasters, with an accident rate of 3%. These aircraft were comprehensively discontinued in China seven years ago, but they remain the main civilian transportation for Russia, former Soviet Union member states, Eastern Europe, and Iran. Half of Russia's passengers travel daily on this type of aircraft to inland destinations.

China once owned 31 Tu-154 passenger aircraft, which were completely phased out in October 2002 after 15 years of service. The reason for abandoning the Tu-154 aircraft reportedly lies in their safety and performance being lower than those of European and American aircraft, along with higher fuel consumption and maintenance costs, and the lack of guaranteed spare parts supply.

This is a three-engine medium-range passenger aircraft developed by the Tupolev Design Bureau of the former Soviet Union, which was claimed to be on par with the American Boeing 727 and the European Trident aircraft at the time. The Tu-154 series aircraft can carry between 167 to 180 passengers. To date, more than 900 Tu-154 aircraft have been produced, with approximately 250 still in service.
