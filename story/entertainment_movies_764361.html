Synopsis:

Ten years ago, in the remote mountains of Colorado, USA, there was a helpless little girl who could only watch as her family was killed by a group of crazed hunters, all to erase the secrets hidden in the bloodline of her family. Under the cover of dense forests, she managed to escape, transforming into a wolf and disappearing into the night.

Ten years later, she lives on the other side of the world but is still haunted by nightmares every night due to her constant fleeing. Her name is Vivian Grandylene. She resides in Bucharest, the capital of Romania, which is relatively safe. Vivian works at a chocolate factory during the day and, at night, roams through the city's underground clubs to prevent her troublemaking cousin, Raf, from committing any irreversible crimes. When it is quiet at night, Vivian enjoys running through the woods surrounding the city, as if only then can she truly feel free. Nothing else matters at that moment, whether being chased or evading, nothing can match her speed. Aidan Gallivan is an American artist who has come to Bucharest to study the ancient culture of "werewolves" for inspiration and material for his next portfolio. Aidan is fascinated by these creatures that can change shape at will, wanting to find out what caused this once revered race of humans to be completely wiped out.

Of course, Aidan's pursuit of werewolves also has a secret he keeps to himself. Even though they have not been seen for centuries after being hunted down en masse by humans and labeled as "devils" and "murderers," Aidan firmly believes that they still exist somewhere in the world. These people with special abilities are not so easy to exterminate. Aidan does not know that most records about werewolves are fabricated, but his guess is correct - a clan of werewolves still exists, right in Bucharest.

Aidan came to an abandoned church based on historical speculation, where it was said that werewolves used to hold ceremonies. There, he met Vivian unexpectedly... Although everyone has secrets, no one's is as extraordinary as Vivian's; she is a member of the last surviving clan of werewolves. Werewolves now have a very strong leader, Gabriel, under whose mysterious protection and control they live humbly in the shadows.

The encounter was brief, but Aidan was drawn to Vivian's cold demeanor. He followed her, gradually softening her hard attitude, and their relationship began to warm up. However, Vivian dared not tell Aidan her background because she feared he would leave her upon learning the truth. But secrets always come to light eventually, as Vivian's eyes change color when she smells blood, revealing her identity.

To ensure the survival of the last remaining werewolves, Gabriel controls them strictly with laws. Every seven years, he marries a new bride, and the next one is Vivian. Gabriel also stipulates that werewolves can only kill humans on moonlit nights and must hunt in groups... Although humans outnumber werewolves, each hunting operation targets only one person, and if that person reaches the other side of the river before being caught, they will be spared - so far, no prey has achieved this. Although Vivian participates in the hunts, she has never killed a human because the conflict between her humanity and wildness torments her. What Vivian did not expect is that her love for Aidan threatens the survival of the werewolves and puts herself in danger - Aidan has been designated as the next hunting target. Should she betray her kind or protect her lover? Vivian's choice will change the fate of the werewolves.

Behind-the-scenes production:

[About "Werewolves"]

There are many words used to describe "werewolves," such as shapeshifters, demons, monsters, etc., all carrying derogatory connotations. The history of these mythical creatures is as old as human civilization. Their names and stories are often associated with disappearances, murders, violence, and death in human history. In director Katja von Garnier's mind, "werewolves" are just a way for humans to express unknown fears: "It is an unknown within the unknown, carnivorous beasts hiding seamlessly among people, like wolves in sheep's clothing living in a flock."

Just the word "werewolf" is enough to captivate anyone, let alone "Blood and Chocolate" exploring the survival and evolution of werewolves' history - no one can resist such a fantastical movie nowadays. A race that can only live under the moonlight, lonely and proud, can this shape-shifting creature really be summed up with just the word "cruel"?

Hugh Dancy, who plays the human Aidan Gallivan in the film, strongly agrees with Katja von Garnier's statement: "If you've read most mythological stories, the fantastical elements include werewolves, vampires - these are just forms of reality, expressions of our inner world. Because there is a demon lurking in everyone's heart, these psychological aspects that do not conform to modern civilized society can only be buried deep within our thoughts."

"Blood and Chocolate" focuses on the werewolves, a species with an ancient historical background, using an unfortunate love story as a carrier. Agnes Bruckner, who plays Vivian, speaks of the helplessness of this species' survival: "In fact, these 'outsiders' have been trying their best to quickly adapt to the changing times and continue to thrive in highly civilized societies. They hope that humans will not judge 'werewolves' unfairly based on unrealistic legends. Through this film, we will gain a new understanding of werewolves - they should coexist with humans in this world."

Vivian is the nobility of the wolf clan because she comes from a 'leader's family.' Although her clan has already arranged her future, Vivian does not appreciate it; she does not want to repeat the paths others have walked. She lives with her clan because they provide protection for her life. After her family was cruelly slaughtered, it was these people who adopted her... However, Vivian is fundamentally different from them, somewhat aloof and somewhat cold. The werewolves are led by Gabriel, who appears young but whose capabilities and age are equally ancient and powerful. Due to the decreasing number of werewolves, they must avoid human persecution through strict organizational methods to survive. Gabriel's only concern is the survival and unity of the werewolves because he has seen too many tragic ends of those who live solitary lives. Vivian's family is a vivid example - they lived alone in Colorado and were ultimately massacred after exposing their identities.

[Hatred and Love Between Different Races]

The wolf clan has a custom: every seven years, their leader chooses a female werewolf to become his new spouse. This time, Gabriel chose Vivian because she represents a new perspective on rules and traditions, which goes against the general direction of the wolf clan's development but is the only way for their survival. In Gabriel's view, Vivian will eventually betray him because she has always been so out of place.

Vivian lives with her aunt Astrid, who was once Gabriel's spouse. Being abandoned mercilessly made Astrid still feel sad today, but the code of conduct of werewolves is not always fair. Rafe is the son of Gabriel and Astrid. Even though Vivian was forced to become a member of the wolf clan, she cannot bring herself to like this cousin. Rafe and his "Five-Man Gang" succumb to their primitive desires, using the excuse of protecting themselves from human invasion to constantly murder humans. Rafe is a typical representative of the new generation of werewolves, a rebellious generation that despises rules. His appearance appropriately mocks the rules that the wolf clan has followed for so long, which once protected them from extinction but have now become unfeasible for them.

Once a month, Gabriel approves the killing of one human, as if conducting a blessing ceremony. But Vivian never participates, just standing aside coldly watching everything happen - it is a real murder. Vivian has always deeply resented and resisted her wild side. She dislikes the system that the current werewolves rely on to survive because it is built on hatred and revenge.

However, Vivian accidentally meets the young American artist Aidan before falling in love. She didn't realize that Aidan was a pawn placed by Gabriel. Despite their vastly different backgrounds, they share a commonality: both burdened by family expectations and both fleeing... As Hugh Dancy describes: "Aidan, like Vivian, is desperately running away from his family reputation and mission. Although she lost her family, she feels uncomfortable and out of place in her current environment. What is Aidan running away from? He cleverly conceals his past and does not reveal his true self."
