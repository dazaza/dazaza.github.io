For a long time, the state has annually encouraged the large-scale capture of centipedes. In some main centipede-producing areas, what was originally seasonal capturing and seasonal purchasing has now become cross-seasonal capturing and year-round purchasing. The purchase price of each centipede in major Chinese herbal medicine markets has soared from a few cents to over 1.5 yuan, with even higher export prices, and demand exceeds supply, with prices rising year by year. However, due to frequent deforestation, pesticide and fertilizer pollution, and other ecological damages, wild resources are becoming increasingly scarce, leading to a severe shortage of centipede resources and rising prices.
