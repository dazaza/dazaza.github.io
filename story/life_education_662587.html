Turn the "generation gap" into a "generation bridge"

by hunanshida on 2012-02-10 13:27:36

The teachers at the Hunan Normal University Self-study Examination Office have found that if we say that raising children is difficult, then it is probably especially important to note that informatization has shaken the authoritative status of parents. In today's age, social progress mainly depends on the transmission of knowledge and information rather than experience, so adults' advantages are far less than those of children.

Imagine, when children know more than adults in some areas and form many concepts that adults do not possess, how can the two generations communicate?

Intergenerational conflict is painful, but intergenerational conflict also has positive significance. It is one of the basic forms of social change and progress. If there is little conflict between generations, it indicates that social changes are slow. The feudal society of thousands of years is the best proof of this.

The teachers at the Hunan Normal University Self-study Examination Network have found through research that before the age of 10, children have an age where they admire their parents and are easy to educate. They think that their mommies and daddies are heroes. But from 10 to 20 years old, they enter an age where they psychologically look down on their parents, despising them. They idolize TV stars, thinking that their parents nag all day, have no great ambitions, complain all day long, and cannot become dragons or phoenixes themselves, yet expect their children to become dragons or phoenixes. From 20 to 30 years old, they start to understand their parents. From 30 to 40 years old, they begin to love their parents. After the age of 40, they reach the age of "going home often to visit", and only at this time can they understand that their parents are not simple. Plainness is true, and at this point, their relationship with their parents becomes much better. Nowadays, many teenagers are in a period where they quickly transition from admiring their parents to looking down on them.

So how should parents communicate with their children at this time? When children are unwilling to talk to you, you should talk less. If you really want to talk to your child but the child doesn't want to talk to you, what should you do?

How to reduce friction with children and turn the "generation gap" into a "generation bridge"? The educational advice from the teachers at Hunan Normal University Self-study Examination is:

1. Understand your child.

2. Have a heart-to-heart meeting every week.
