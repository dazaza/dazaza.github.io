The Prosperous Prospect of Autumn Auctions for Artworks is Worth Expecting

by xyxandy8 on 2012-03-05 09:13:41

The Prosperous Scene of Art Autumn Auctions Can Be Expected In 2011, the domestic art auction continued the revelry from last year. The spring auction total sales reached a new record of 42.8 billion yuan. Some high-quality artworks have appreciated several times, even tens of times in just a few years, demonstrating wealth effects that have received widespread attention. Meanwhile, art as an alternative investment to real estate and stocks has increasingly formed a consensus as the third pole of wealth. Of course, the contradiction between the profit-seeking nature of art investment and its intrinsic value has become more prominent, which seems to predict that the art market in 2011 will become increasingly turbulent. However, reporters found in interviews that this year's autumn art auction market will continue the prosperous scene, and the overall price will further increase.

Funds are crazily entering the art market. From a series of important economic data recently published, it can be seen that the domestic macro-economy was generally on a downward trend in the first half of this year. It is understood that since the beginning of this year, domestic inflationary pressure has continued to rise. The central bank has repeatedly raised the deposit reserve ratio and benchmark interest rates. At the same time, the year-on-year growth rate of money supply has also declined. Affected by this, domestic property, automobiles, and other terminal consumption have continued to decline, producing obvious negative impacts on the economy. However, under the monetary tightening policy, the total sales of domestic art auctions in the first half of 2011 reached 42.8 billion yuan, an increase of 15% compared to the second half of last year, far below the 85% growth ratio of the autumn auction in 2010 compared to the spring auction of the same year. Although the growth rate has decreased, the total transaction amount is still on an upward trend, and various market indicators show good trends.

Art market commentator Wang Chunyuan stated that despite the rapid development of the art market in recent years, it still belongs to a niche market. However, the mild decline in the domestic macro-economy has caused a considerable portion of liquidity funds to exit from other investment markets, thus seeking other outlets. As one of the three major wealth categories alongside real estate and the stock market, the art market has attracted these funds exiting from other investment fields due to its continuous explosive growth over the past few years. "Although it is currently impossible to accurately calculate the amount of investment funds entering the art market, it can be confirmed that the continuous rise of the art market is closely related to the crazy involvement of investment funds," said Wang Chunyuan.

Wang Chunyuan indicated that from the current state of the art market, cultural property exchanges have been established across the country. Moreover, institutional collectors and some entrepreneurs have become new forces in the auction market in recent years, and even some banks have launched art-related financial products. This shows that after many years of development, a large amount of capital is rapidly entering the art market. "Art resources are limited, while investment demand is growing geometrically. This will inevitably exacerbate the imbalance of supply and demand in the art market. According to economic principles, when demand exceeds supply, only price can balance the market, and the most direct manifestation is that art prices will be further driven up."

Strong Expectation for Rising Art Prices Without a doubt, people's high confidence in the trend of art prices in the first half of 2011 was an important reason supporting the entire spring auction market. According to the survey results of an art investment institution on market confidence in the first and second quarters of this year, participants were generally optimistic about the trend of art prices. In the first quarter, 45.16% of respondents were quite optimistic about the future trend of art prices, and 61.29% believed that it was not appropriate to buy art at present. By the second quarter, the proportion of respondents optimistic about the future of the art market surged to 82.93%, and those who believed it was inappropriate to buy art quickly dropped to 48.78%. Clearly, with the successive hammering down of this year's spring auction and the good performance of the entire art market, investors' confidence in China's art market is increasing day by day.

In terms of art categories, people's focus gradually shifted from Chinese painting to ceramics. According to survey data, people paid more attention to ancient Chinese paintings, modern and contemporary paintings, etc., in the first quarter. However, starting from the second quarter, the attention to these types of paintings showed a significant downward trend compared to the first quarter, while the attention to ceramics significantly increased. Additionally, collectors' and investors' interest in jade, watches, and jewelry also showed a noticeable recovery in the second quarter. Therefore, with the steady rise of the porcelain miscellaneous items sector in this year's spring auction market, porcelain miscellaneous items have also become the focus of collectors and investors.

Of course, while most people are optimistic about the art market, some are worried about the art auction market in the second half of the year. After all, along with the rapid development of China's art auction industry and the emergence of sky-high auction prices, phenomena such as fake auctions, passing off inferior goods as genuine ones, fake bidding, and black-box operations have raised questions about the auction market, challenging the credibility of auction houses like never before. Moreover, issues of buyer integrity have become increasingly prominent, and the phenomenon of buyers not paying in the auction market is common, adding certain resistance to the healthy development of China's auction industry. Therefore, in the current situation where the art auction market is developing rapidly, facing various problems in the auction market, both buyers and sellers need to actively address them and implement effective solutions. Only then can China's auction industry gain international recognition, attract international buyers, and achieve long-term prosperity and development. Otherwise, it will inevitably lead to chaos and disorder in the domestic auction market, causing ineffective investments for many people and becoming a stumbling block on the path of development for the art market.
