Chongwu fish rolls are a famous dish in the Minnan region of Fujian, and one of the ten most renowned snacks in Quanzhou. They have won numerous awards at competitions held in Minnan and are highly praised by the local people and tourists visiting Huian. Chongwu, with its superior geographical location and rich cultural heritage, has developed a long tradition of producing this delicacy - fish rolls. The "Old Fisherman's Family" brand of fish rolls adheres to traditional production techniques, utilizing modern technology for meticulous ingredient selection and strict production processes, resulting in their own unique flavor. They are tender and crunchy when eaten, leaving a lingering aroma in the mouth. They are an essential dish for entertaining guests and during festivals, as well as a healthy food gift for friends and family. They can be cooked in various ways, whether boiled, stir-fried, deep-fried, or grilled, and are suitable for hotpot all year round. For many years, they have been loved by a wide range of consumers. Chongwu fish rolls have a high reputation, and for those familiar with Chongwu, it is definitely Chongwu fish rolls that they must order. Not eating fish rolls when visiting Chongwu is considered a regrettable experience, while buying a few pounds of fish rolls to take home after eating them is very common. It's also quite common for people from other places to ask others to buy fish rolls in Chongwu.

Product Name: Old Fisherman's Family Fish Rolls

Main Ingredients:

Fresh mackerel, premium starch, egg whites, fresh meat shreds, green onions, etc.

Product Specifications: 500 grams per pack

Storage Method: Below -18℃

Shelf Life: 3 months

Cooking Methods:

① Boil: No need to thaw the fish rolls. Cut directly into pieces about 2 cm in size and boil in boiling clear soup for 3-5 minutes. Bone broth or fish broth can be used as the base according to personal preference.

② Stir-fry: Slightly thawed fish rolls can be cut into various shapes and first stir-fried in oil. Then add garlic chives, carrots, green peppers, or cucumbers.

③ Fry: Slightly thawed fish rolls should be diagonally sliced into thin slices about half a centimeter thick and directly fried in oil until golden brown. They can also be dipped in egg whites and crispy powder before frying.

④ Steam: Slightly thawed fish rolls can be cut in half and steamed for about 5 minutes before eating (microwave oven can also be used). According to personal preference, they can be eaten with soy sauce, sweet and spicy sauce, etc.

⑤ Can also be used as hot pot ingredients
