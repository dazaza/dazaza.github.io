Be a happy fisherman

by musicshow5 on 2012-02-09 14:38:21

A thousand layers of lotus flowers, ten thousand miles of green waves, I am here, fishing for your smile! Staying at the shop all day, I feel like a fisherman trying to scoop up happiness. If I can't catch what I want today, I don't want to return. I prefer to take it straight rather than seek it through crooked ways - this is a reflection of my stubbornness! One day after another, some lightly sketched, some deeply etched in memory, all are undoubtedly real. After the Beginning of Spring, I feel that the seasons are like me, suddenly awakening into spring, as beautiful as before, gradually forgetting the cruel bloodshed, no need to worry about holding all the bits and pieces of life... Not worrying myself, not salivating over others' gains, I've already opened my heart and adjusted my attitude, hoping to quickly surpass this neglected field. I've squatted for a long time during the day, but only made a few transactions, so I can only hope for better luck at night. However, tonight, the mist obscures the bright moon, and a bitter sorrow wells up inside me. I thought I had become numb to fishing, but then I blamed others for not understanding me.

Past thirty, I still act on impulse, doing things my own way. Even if fate gives me a foot, I'll seek a yard. I can't bear to accept empty hands, and this might be the most helpless persistence of my life! Sometimes I wonder, which shopkeeper can live as genuinely as I do? Regarding the introduction of lotus seeds in the store, there's no need for pretentious exaggeration. The quality, good or bad, dares to withstand the test of time, layer by layer filtering out the sand. My words, though sparse, express my thoughts and loves. My heart entrusts a bit of purity and simplicity to the lotus seeds in the store, believing they can bloom the most beautiful lotus flower amidst the worldly dust.

Alas, you don't understand me, but I don't blame you. Suddenly, there's a ding dong sound, and I sit upright on the lotus platform. I should have known long ago that everyone harbors greed in their hearts. Even with a 70% discount, I'm still asked to provide free shipping. Knowing that the goods must be sent from Hunan to Shenzhou, Hebei, there's no escape, even if it's farther away, I'll still take the order with gritted teeth. But at the moment of the transaction, I still feel genuine pain, cold sweat pouring down like rain. This is me, you don't know how much effort I put in, you don't know how cost-effective my lotus seeds from Hunan are. I don't blame you, because even a smile can make me feel warm.

You seem conflicted, and your words are cold and emotionless, cold enough to chill my heart. Doing this business, am I worth it or not? You're already my god. In a gloomy mood, a faint sentence comes from a corner: "Qingxia blesses Grandpa Ma." I'm surprised, I start to understand. This isn't just a transaction; it's her effort spent, her trust in our tribute lotus shop, her spiritual comfort for the distant old man. Looking around at the many Taobao friends, how many are like her? In my eyes, blessings are a magical force, able to carry one forward, crossing mountains, ridges, mudflats, and tidal flats.

I can't sit still anymore, overwhelmed with emotion, having forgotten the previous pain. I'd rather feel deeper pain, grabbing an extra handful of lotus seed tea for the old man to brew and nourish his heart. A few words fall onto the card, carried by this blessing, drifting far away...

Alas, I finally understand you, please don't blame me.

I often sit alone in silence, discovering many beautiful things in the world. Sacrificing oneself for true love, seeking transcendence in happiness, I sit here like this, no longer needing any other reason to find joy.

Today, I am happy, and tonight the fisherman closes his shop with joy...
