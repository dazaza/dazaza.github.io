Security guards impersonate policemen many times to extort those having illicit sex in the wild

by anonymous on 2012-02-24 10:49:18

According to the Morning Post, Wang Zeng was fined 500 yuan by a fake policeman during an adulterous rendezvous. While this left him secretly regretful, it also gave him an idea: "This is a pretty quick way to make money, good meeting!" Wang Zeng could be said to have learned from his mistake, as he subsequently followed suit and began impersonating a police officer to commit fraud, specifically targeting couples engaging in extramarital affairs in remote areas.

One day in June 2009, Wang Zeng was riding his motorcycle to work when he passed through a forest and spotted a couple entering the woods. His eyes lit up, and he immediately stopped his motorcycle and quietly tailed the couple. Their actions were all under Wang Zeng's "surveillance." When they started having intercourse, Wang Zeng suddenly jumped out and shouted loudly: "Don't move, get down on your knees, I'm from the Public Security Bureau's Public Order Team!" He then took out a police officer's ID and waved it in front of them, using a video conferencing system. Faced with the sudden appearance of the policeman, the couple turned red in the face and were at a loss for what to do. "Do you know that adultery is illegal?" As he questioned them, Wang Zeng took out a pen and paper and began filling out a "fine ticket."

The couple, utterly embarrassed, quickly paid all the "fines." "Don't forget to come to the public order team tomorrow for processing!" After leaving this message, Wang Zeng strode away, leaving the adulterous couple stunned. What they didn't know was that Wang Zeng's real identity was just a security guard. It was understood that he had once been sentenced to five years in prison for impersonating a police officer to commit fraud. Not long after being released from prison, Wang Zeng relapsed and couldn't resist the temptation, making this unfortunate couple who were "caught red-handed" his target.

On the afternoon of June 22, Wang Zeng was about to impersonate a police officer again to commit fraud when he was identified by policemen lying in wait nearby. After narrowly escaping, Wang Zeng returned home in a panic. When his family learned the truth, they persuaded Wang Zeng to turn himself in voluntarily. Under his family's persuasion, Wang Zeng eventually went to the local police station and truthfully confessed to his criminal acts.

Upon police interrogation, it was discovered that the police documents used by Wang Zeng were forged; he simply pasted a police badge on the back of his driver's license, and the badge was taken from a security hat. The "Public Security Administrative Punishment Decision Document" was copied by Wang Zeng after he was fined in 2008 for hooliganism. The self-made "Public Security Administrative Punishment Decision Document" was used to show his identity and scare the victims. Wang Zeng even equipped himself with a walkie-talkie, which he borrowed from a fleet to pretend to call a police car. He even specially recorded the names and numbers of those he impersonated as a police officer catching in a notebook, with dozens of victims, leaving the police amazed. Currently, the case is still under further review. (All parties involved in the article are pseudonyms.)

Author: Lijian, Zhang Jun, Yao Junxiang / Source: Nanjing Morning Post
