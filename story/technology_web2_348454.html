Stricker Sticks to Four-Year Old Irons, New Orleans May See New Weapons

by ztt0288868 on 2010-04-19 18:23:58

Stricker considers new irons for New Orleans event

Sina Sports News - April 16, Beijing time - World number three Steve Stricker has used the same model of Titleist irons for four years because he felt that after changing clubs, the new ones might not perform as well. However, after a disappointing week at the Masters, Steve Stricker changed his mind. When he comes back to play in the New Orleans Elite Tournament, he may switch his irons.

Steve Stricker did not perform ideally at Augusta, only breaking par in the final round. His biggest issue was with his irons. And at Augusta, irons play a very important role.

"I might try some different clubs," said Steve Stricker, "Over the past one or two years, I have tried some different clubs, and now might be the time to make a change. I want to try a different feel."

If Steve Stricker really decides to make a change, he might switch to AP2 irons. During the Chevron World Challenge in December last year, he used those clubs and shot a 65 in the final round. However, when the new season began, he put his old 755s irons back into his bag.

"I like the feel of hitting with them, but I still can't fully unleash their potential," said Steve Stricker, "Maybe this time I can."
