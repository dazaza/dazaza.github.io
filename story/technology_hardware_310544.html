Arsenal Triumphs in Clash of Declining Powers; Title Hopes Reignited as Gunners Stay Focused

By Xiang Tong

If the top four clubs were compared to The Beatles, then the Liverpool vs Arsenal match could be seen as a contest between George and Ringo (the less prominent members of the band). However, the battle between declining powerhouses was just as crucial. Liverpool needed to reclaim a top-four spot, while Arsenal naturally hoped to capitalize on Chelsea and Manchester United's struggles this round to close the gap with the league leaders. Arsenal had previously suffered defeats against all strong opponents this season, but last Sunday at Anfield, it was the Gunners who emerged victorious.

Arsenal's 2-1 comeback win marked their first league victory at Anfield since 2003. Now, Arsenal are only three points behind Manchester United with one game in hand, trailing league leaders Chelsea by six points—anything is possible. It could be said that both teams played well for half each. Arsenal were dominated in the first half but rallied after halftime thanks to Wenger’s fiery halftime pep talk.

Steven Gerrard's Penalty Appeal Falls Flat

Liverpool started with significant control, not only showcasing brilliant moments like Aurelio's skillful move to deceive Nasri, but also quickly creating scoring opportunities. In the 12th minute, Liverpool launched a beautiful counterattack through their twin stars. Torres provided a wall pass at midfield, sending Gerrard racing down the right flank before delivering a through ball into the box. Torres attempted a powerful shot, but it lacked precision and was saved by Almunia. Just a minute later, Gerrard burst into the penalty area from the center, trying to dribble past Gael Clichy but was brought down. Despite Gerrard's appeal for a penalty, referee Howard Webb did not award one.

In the 21st minute, Liverpool produced smooth play on the right flank. Kuyt delivered an excellent pass, and Glen Johnson maneuvered past Denilson and Nasri inside the box, but his final cross was disappointing. Fired up, Johnson soon unleashed two powerful long-range shots, and Liverpool's set-piece crosses repeatedly caused chaos in the opposition's penalty area. In the 34th minute, Torres was tackled by Clichy near the edge of the box, but again, Webb showed no interest.

Arsenal paid the price for their poor set-piece defense in the 41st minute. Aurelio's free-kick from 40 yards out found its way into the box, where Almunia failed to cleanly punch it away. Kuyt capitalized, chesting the ball down and poking it home for a 1-0 lead. This goal extended Liverpool's club-record streak of scoring in 19 consecutive Premier League home games. Wenger was highly dissatisfied with this goal, as Lucas appeared to be offside when he jumped to challenge Almunia for the ball.

Two Summer Signings, One Scapegoat

A familiar surprise guest sat among the spectators: former Liverpool player Xabi Alonso, who joined Real Madrid this summer, returned to Anfield to watch the match. Accompanying British media photos, some unkindly wrote: “We won’t make any jokes about this picture.” Ironically, reality provided its own dark humor as Alonso’s "replacement," Alberto Aquilani, remained on the bench, while another key summer signing, Glen Johnson, became the culprit behind Liverpool's two goals conceded within eight minutes in the second half.

In the 50th minute, Arsenal executed a series of precise passes in the attacking third. Nasri received a through ball from Fabregas, cutting into the penalty area from the right flank and delivering a low cross. The ball deflected slightly off Carragher and, in confusion, Johnson mistakenly blocked it with his left foot, deflecting it into his own net, leveling the score at 1-1. In the 58th minute, Fabregas broke through on the right wing and crossed the ball. At the far post, Arshavin collected it, easily bypassing the disoriented Johnson before smashing a fierce shot that ricocheted off the inside of the near post and into the net, giving Arsenal a 2-1 lead.

After being equalized, Torres had a chance. Cutting inside from the left flank, he danced along the baseline into the box but found neither a shooting angle nor a passing option, resulting in an ineffective effort. Seeing Arsenal score twice consecutively, Gerrard angrily charged down the right wing, kicking Nasri and even knocking over Kuyt, eventually forcing Johnson to deliver a cross. Unfortunately, Torres' header glanced wide.

Neither side managed to create many more genuine chances. After receiving a pass from Nasri, Arshavin's shot from the right side of the penalty area posed no difficulty for Reina. With no clear path forward, Liverpool made multiple substitutions, bringing on Aquilani for Mascherano amid applause from Alonso, but to little effect. Arsenal ended their six-game winless streak against the top-four rivals.

Liverpool, meanwhile, have won only three of their last 15 matches across all competitions. Since beating Manchester United at the end of October, they've drawn two and lost two at home across both competitions, raising concerns. Their recurring problem of conceding leads in Champions League matches has now spread to the league, which is equally troubling. Post-match, Benitez admitted the team lacked confidence: “Our performance in the first half was very good, we were satisfied. The foul by Clichy on Gerrard should have been a penalty. But the first goal changed everything. Our confidence disappeared, and we began making mistakes. As we conceded the second goal, our opponents grew more confident while we did the opposite. Now, securing a top-four finish remains our target. It's a long road ahead, but we must keep moving forward. Although Aston Villa already leads us by five points, there’s still plenty of time left in the season for us to catch up.”

Liverpool Lineup (4-4-1-1): Reina / Glen Johnson (Degen 82'), Carragher, Agger, Aurelio / Kuyt, Mascherano (Aquilani 64'), Lucas, Benayoun (N’Gog 79') / Gerrard / Torres

Arsenal Lineup (4-2-3-1): Almunia / Sagna, Gallas, Vermaelen, Traore (Silvestre 87') / Denilson, Song / Walcott (Diaby 70'), Fabregas, Nasri / Arshavin (Ramsey 90')
