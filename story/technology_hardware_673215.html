What should pregnant women eat for the good of the fetus? What kinds of fruits are good for the fetus?

by uefbp54187 on 2012-02-23 14:41:57

The most important nutrients during this period include vitamin D and the omega-3 fatty acid DHA, which are crucial for the development of the fetus's brain and eyes. Fish and seafood are the best sources of these nutrients. If you don't like eating fish, your doctor will also advise you. Eating more fruit is beneficial for the fetus, but even if you eat more, it can't replace meals; staple foods cannot be lacking. Nuts and similar items are all acceptable, mainly to keep a cheerful mood. If possible, start prenatal education in the fifth or sixth month. Pregnant women should consume a variety of high-nutritional-value foods, which is beneficial for fetal development and maternal health. Generally speaking, the following twelve categories of food should be reasonably combined and continuously supplemented:

1. Protein-rich foods: lean pork, beef, chicken, duck, fish, shrimp, eggs, dairy products, and legumes.

2. Carbohydrate-rich foods: rice, noodles, and other grain-based foods, as well as sweet potatoes and potatoes.

3. Fat-rich foods: animal and plant oils, eggs, dairy, soybeans, peanuts, sesame seeds, sunflower seeds, etc.

4. Calcium-rich foods: milk, fish, egg yolks, etc.

5. Iron-rich foods: pig liver, lean meat, eggs, black fungus, sweet peaches, red dates, longan, spinach, carrots, etc.

6. Zinc-rich foods: oysters, kelp, cuttlefish, beef, chicken liver, pork, eggs, peanuts, etc.

7. Magnesium-rich foods: grains, beans, walnuts, etc.

8. Iodine-rich foods: seaweed, sea fish, and other seafood.

9. Vitamin A-rich foods: carrots, spinach, rapeseed, animal livers, eggs, loach, eel, oysters, etc.

10. Vitamin B1-rich foods: soybeans, grains, flour, peanuts, lean meat, animal livers, etc.

11. Vitamin B2-rich foods: animal livers, eggs, dairy products, beans, green leafy vegetables, etc.

12. Vitamin C-rich foods: fresh vegetables and fruits.

Fruits:

In early pregnancy, the fetus is not yet firmly implanted. If you consume overly cold, slippery, or blood-activating fruits such as watermelon, cantaloupe, hawthorn, etc., it can easily cause diarrhea in pregnant women, induce uterine contractions, and lead to miscarriage, especially in women with weak constitutions or a history of miscarriage. In the middle and late stages of pregnancy, pregnant women often have a hot constitution and insufficient yin blood. If they consume too much warm-natured fruits like lychee, mango, and longan, it can easily cause constipation, sore throat, eczema, and other "heat" symptoms, and may even cause fetal restlessness.

Apple: Apples are typical fruits with mild taste and nature, rich in iron, vitamins, and fine fibers. They have a good regulatory effect on the gastrointestinal tract, relieving both diarrhea and constipation. Anemia and constipation are very common symptoms during pregnancy, and apples can play an excellent role in dietary therapy.

Grape: Grapes have high nutritional value, with relatively high calcium, phosphorus, and iron content, and contain many vitamins and amino acids. The "Compendium of Materia Medica" considers grapes to be sweet, mild, and non-toxic, with auxiliary treatment effects on "fetal upsurge to the heart" and "edema", making them an excellent nourishing food for pregnant women. However, note that fresh grapes should be consumed, not stored in the refrigerator for too long, and excessive consumption should be avoided.

Daily diet should basically cover 1-2 varieties from each of the above categories to achieve diversified nutrition.

During early pregnancy, avoid bananas, pitaya, and rambutan, as they are cold in nature and can easily cause miscarriage. After four months when the placenta stabilizes, you can eat some pitaya in moderation. Rambutan should only be consumed after five months and not excessively. Pineapple, mango, and lychee should ideally be avoided throughout pregnancy, as consuming them might result in babies with skin problems, such as rashes or ulcers on their heads. Peaches are rich in vitamins and are the best fruit for pregnant women. Durians are currently in season and can be eaten in moderation, as elders say that one durian equals seven chickens in terms of nourishment. However, overeating should be avoided. Over-supplementing in early pregnancy can backfire, so it's better to wait until mid-pregnancy. Apples are also good, though I personally don't like them. Pears can be eaten. I ate a lot of pears during my pregnancy. During mid-pregnancy, I was prone to constipation, so I usually ate one pitaya per day. Grapes are good for eyesight, but they shouldn't be eaten regularly as blood sugar levels could rise. Grape consumption should be appropriately controlled during mid-pregnancy due to its high sugar content. Cakes should also be eaten sparingly because of their high sugar content.
