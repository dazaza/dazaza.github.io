Causes of scars may be due to some external injuries (such as car accidents, burns, etc.) that cause skin damage. Scars can roughly be divided into five types:

1. Good scars, which are linear and flat, running parallel to the skin wrinkles, with colors similar to normal skin.

2. Common scars, slightly raised, with colors slightly darker than normal skin.

3. Hypertrophic scars, which are raised, darker in color, and accompanied by itching and pain, but the degree of elevation does not exceed the range of the wound, and they will flatten over time.

4. Keloids, whose shapes resemble crab legs and are brown, invading surrounding normal skin. This is due to the continuous division of fibroblasts inside, expanding like tumors. They commonly occur on the shoulders and chest.
