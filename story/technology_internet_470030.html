QQ sues 360 for RMB 4 million in damages; court hearing to be held tomorrow in Beijing

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-03 13:24:05

This log comes from QQ space Q8 12348, welcome to reprint.

Abstract: The court has accepted Tencent's lawsuit against 360 for unfair competition and will hold a hearing on November 25 in Chaoyang District Court of Beijing. Meanwhile, 360's countersuit against Tencent for "defamation" has also been filed in Xicheng District Court of Beijing.

The life-and-death battle between 360 and Tencent came to a temporary end after the intervention of relevant departments, with both parties temporarily "harmoniously coexisting." However, recently, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) stated that it would investigate and deal with the suspected illegal acts of the two companies. It was learned yesterday that the case of QQ suing 360 for unfair competition would be heard in Beijing on November 25. Lawyi.com and Rongke Law Firm stated that they had received complaints from nearly 3,000 paid QQ users and obtained authorization from some users to sue Tencent for compensation. They have submitted complaints to courts in Shanghai and Shenzhen, and the litigation war is imminent.

On the evening of November 21, under the criticism and orders of MIIT, Beijing Qihoo Technology Co., Ltd. and Tencent Computer System Co., Ltd. in Shenzhen apologized again to netizens.

Two Apology Letters, Dust Not Yet Settled

Tencent Network published an apology letter to QQ users titled "With You," which said, "Initially, we focused all our attention on issues like 'who is right or wrong' and 'righteousness.' At the same time, we ignored the feelings of users. We believe that after the end of this incident, under the impetus of the government, the era of 'competition in the sunshine' that we have long desired will eventually come. Tencent will embrace this era with a more open mind along with users and the industry."

Qihoo 360 Company also pointed out in its apology statement published on its official website, "We reflect here: while focusing on innovation, we must also take into account the harmony of the industry; while respecting users, we must also take into account orderly competition. We will look to the future, win respect through innovation, and win trust through responsibility."

Even so, the litigation war between QQ and 360 is still imminent.

QQ vs. 360 Case to Be Heard Tomorrow

It was learned yesterday afternoon that the court has accepted Tencent's lawsuit against 360 for unfair competition and will hold a hearing on November 25 in Chaoyang District Court of Beijing. Meanwhile, 360's countersuit against Tencent for "defamation" has also been filed in Xicheng District Court of Beijing, but the specific hearing date has not yet been determined.

The case to be heard this Thursday stems from Tencent's belief that 360 was "smearing" it. The 360 Privacy Protector directly targeted QQ, listing the routine security checks by QQ's security check module and normal operations by QQ users as "QQ peeping at user privacy," which Tencent considered a defamation.

According to insiders, apart from demanding that the defendant apologize continuously for three months, Tencent also seeks 4 million yuan in compensation.

Paid QQ Users Sign Up to Sue Tencent

In fact, as early as November 5, Yao Kefeng, secretary-general of the Internet Anti-monopoly Alliance, and Wang Fengchang, CEO of Lawyi.com, submitted an "Anti-monopoly Investigation Application" to the State Administration for Industry and Commerce in their capacity as internet users, requesting an anti-monopoly investigation of Tencent. For the first time, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce sought opinions regarding "Tencent's suspected monopoly" and did not make a clear statement after preliminarily understanding the situation.

More than two years after the implementation of the Anti-monopoly Law, people can't help but ask: Will this become China's first anti-monopoly case in the internet sector?

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter interviewed Yao Kefeng, secretary-general of the Internet Anti-monopoly Alliance. According to him, they have already received complaints from nearly 3,000 paid QQ users via Lawyi.com and have obtained authorization from some users to prepare to sue Tencent for compensation in the near future.

The lawsuits are divided into two parts: one part accuses Tencent of infringing on user privacy, and the other accuses Tencent of forcing users to choose between QQ and 360. In fact, more people signed up, with tens of thousands registering on microblogs. Due to high screening standards, only 3,000 people passed, and the number may continue to increase.

Currently, they have separately submitted some complaints to courts in Shanghai and Shenzhen. Due to the lack of cooperating lawyers in Guangzhou, no lawsuit has been filed there yet, but there are also many users in Guangzhou who request to file a lawsuit.

Lawyers Say They Will Choose Typical Cases to Sue

"We held a seminar and invited some QQ users to participate. Among the users suing, one paid QQ user claimed she suffered losses due to QQ's sudden suspension. She always used QQ to negotiate business with clients online. Before November 3, everything was almost settled, and she sent some files via QQ. During the negotiation, QQ suddenly popped up saying it could not be compatible with 360. Since the client was unwilling to disclose other contact information except QQ, the forced disconnection caused the business to be interrupted. When trying to resume, the client no longer wanted to talk. When talking about this, the user was very emotional, saying that an opportunity for a turnaround was lost. Some users also had their QQ numbers stolen after uninstalling 360, suffering varying degrees of loss."

"The evidence and claims for each individual case are different, and we sue them one by one. But we don't want all 3,000 people to sue, as that would also be a burden on the national judiciary. We just want to exert influence through some typical cases," said Yao Kefeng.

Who Is Right or Wrong?

Yesterday, the Law School and Intellectual Property School of South China University of Technology, together with internet industry insiders, held an academic seminar on the legal issues and internet industry competition triggered by the dispute between 360 and QQ. The debate at the meeting was intense and views were mixed.

Did QQ's Startup Scan Invade User Privacy?

Whether 360's announcement to the public that "QQ spies on user privacy during operation" constitutes damage to Tencent's commercial credibility depends on whether Tencent QQ's startup scan invades user privacy.

Users Signed Privacy Agreements When Installing QQ

Professor Wang Yan of SCUT Law School pointed out that this is related to Tencent QQ's user privacy agreement. Tencent requires users to agree to allow Tencent to access user information when installing software. However, almost all users do not read the agreement when installing QQ software and directly choose to install it; even if users read it, without professional internet and legal knowledge, such a large user privacy agreement is impossible to understand. So, from the perspective of the user privacy agreement, users chose to agree to let Tencent scan their computer data when installing.

User Privacy Agreement Suspected of Being an Unfair Contract Term

Dr. Zhang Chengji of SCUT Law School believed that under the condition of serious information asymmetry, and since the user agreement terms can only be explained by the software company, such a user privacy agreement actually belongs to the standard terms in e-commerce contracts and should be accompanied by relevant reminders and explanations; otherwise, the clause is invalid. Dr. Li Qiucheng pointed out that as a company providing users with virus scanning services, even if users agree with the privacy agreement of the software, issuing warnings about potential risks of the software is its duty and does not constitute infringement of the commercial credibility of the software company.

Does 360 "QQ Guard" Hijack QQ?

Tencent accused "QQ Guard" of being an external plug-in product. 360 once explained that "QQ Guard" has nothing to do with external plug-ins, it is a security software developed for QQ users, which can detect the security of QQ and repair vulnerabilities in QQ software, eliminate QQ password-stealing Trojan horses, and prevent QQ password theft, etc.

Intercepting Software Can Be Terrifying

Dr. Li Qiucheng of SCUT Law School believed that if users agree to accept the behavior of the software publishing advertisements while using it for free, then the interception behavior of "QQ Guard" actually destroys the integrity of the products and services provided by Tencent to users and constitutes an infringement. Some experts also indicated that once this practice is abused by other companies, 360 or other companies in the future can hijack any software and service, and there might be MSN Guard, Baidu Guard, and various other guards appearing. This practice makes people feel terrified because hackers can obtain a large amount of user privacy from it, and ultimately, it is the users who get hurt.

Essentially Different from Coral Bug QQ

Famous intellectual property lawyer, senior lawyer of Beijing Zhenbang Law Firm, Liu Hongwei, believed that "QQ Guard" did not mention the name of QQ, thus did not infringe on trademark rights and reputation rights, and "QQ Guard", in his opinion, is also not an external plug-in. Taking Coral Bug QQ as an example, this external plug-in program deletes Tencent's advertisements and replaces them with its own advertisements, illegally profiting and涉嫌unfair competition. 360's "QQ Guard" is just auxiliary software. This software is chosen autonomously by users, effectively preventing QQ advertisement pop-ups without making profits through advertisements.

Is QQ Suspected of Abusing Its Monopoly Position?

"Anti-monopoly" has been repeatedly mentioned in the "3Q War". Tencent expressed that it cannot simply judge whether Tencent has a dominant market position based on the number of QQ users. Tencent is not the largest in search and e-commerce fields, it just has a larger share in instant messaging. As for whether it has a dominant market position, it needs to be judged by open and credible third-party data, and it also requires a judicial argumentation process.

Market Share Does Not Represent Everything

Legal personnel in the internet industry attending the meeting stated that in the fields of search, security, and e-commerce, there are companies with very large market shares. The key is still to see

whether there is abuse of market share and power under normal competition. Doctor Xie Huijia of SCUT Law School believes that Tencent made users choose between the two options, which although users find emotionally hard to accept, considering the measures taken by Tencent, reviewing the relevant articles of the Anti-monopoly Law, none of them clearly regulate such behavior. Whether it is an abuse of market dominance is also a very complex issue in the content of the Anti-monopoly Law. Therefore, Tencent's actions do not belong to the abuse of monopoly status.

Cannot Only Consider Self-interest

Doctor Zhang Chengji believes that in this dispute, 360 and Tencent can no longer only consider their own interests. Whether it is virus detection or instant messaging, both have very large user bases and have become public platforms. In the social public domain, every move of the two companies involves the interests of numerous users and should bear public responsibility.
