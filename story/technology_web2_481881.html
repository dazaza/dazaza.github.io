Stop paying bribes to driving instructors unjustly when learning to drive

by akou065wgw on 2011-08-26 13:23:47

Learning to drive at a driving school can cost upward of three to four thousand yuan. On top of the basic tuition fees, the "biggest expense" is actually charged by instructors under various pretexts such as "showing respect to examiners". Worried about being deliberately failed during exams, students have no choice but to obediently offer bribes.

In recent years, the "unspoken rules" of driver's license exams have taken root and spread in Hechi like a cult. Many students detest this practice but feel powerless against it, leading to increasing calls to crack down on these unspoken rules. "If any examiner or instructor is found to be engaging in bribery, after investigation and verification, they must be severely punished." During interviews, traffic police departments strongly condemned the act of collecting "black money" during driving exams.

The suspension of driving tests has its reasons.

Recently, a netizen named "Buzhengze Ming66" posted on the Hechi Net forum questioning whether it was true that "Hechi driving exams have been urgently suspended by higher authorities due to excessive bribery of examiners and suspected cheating." This post drew widespread attention from netizens, making the unspoken rules of driving exams a target of public criticism.

According to information from the traffic police department, there is a reason for the suspension of driving exams in Hechi. It was explained that the reason for the suspension of some test items in the second subject (nine indoor items) is based on the "Regulations on the Application and Use of Motor Vehicle Driving Licenses" (Ministry of Public Security Order No. 111) and Article 43 of the "Regulations on the Administration of Motor Vehicle Driving License Business Work". Starting from April 1, 2011, for those applying for large buses, urban buses, medium-sized buses, large trucks, small cars, small automatic cars, and disabled-specific small automatic passenger cars, the second subject examination must be conducted using a site driving skills examination system. After the exam, the results are automatically uploaded to the computer management system.

According to the requirements of the Ministry of Public Security and work standards, the Autonomous Region Department of Public Security closed the manual entry function for site exam scores on July 1. Six municipal detachments, including Hechi, suspended some test items in the second subject due to non-compliance with hardware standards. Starting from July 1, our city's traffic police detachment suspended the second subject site examination. The expected completion date for our city's second subject driving skills examination site is September, after which normal exams will resume and exam results will be automatically uploaded to the computer management system.

"Pay-to-pass" is a lie.

It is introduced that the driving test consists of three subjects. Subject one mainly tests road traffic safety laws, regulations, and related knowledge; subject two is the site examination; and subject three is the road test. Currently, since subject one exams have all adopted computerized paperless exams and subject two pile tests have achieved intelligent evaluation, any possibility of human manipulation has been completely eliminated. Next, other site driving skill exams in subject two will also achieve computerized intelligent evaluation by September this year, and subject three will be monitored through video and audio to ensure the openness, fairness, and justice of the entire exam process.

All along, the city's traffic police department strictly adheres to the examination fee standards set by the price control department, while requiring examiners to give students a "pre-exam lesson" for each subject, announcing exam discipline, publishing exam fee standards, and emphasizing that the traffic police department does not charge any fees other than the exam fee during the exam process. Therefore, the traffic police department absolutely does not allow instructors to collect money in the name of examiners and reminds candidates not to easily believe the lies told by some instructors, claiming that "as long as you pay, you can pass the exam."

"Since the current driving test has been computerized, even if students want to bribe their way through, it's impossible. Why waste that money?" A responsible person from the vehicle management office pointed out that under high transparency, the driving test scores are announced immediately after the exam, leaving very little room for examiner misconduct. Moreover, paying money to influence the selection of exam questions is entirely unfounded, as the selection takes place on the morning of the exam day before coaches and students enter the exam room when examiners gather to select the questions. "During internal question selection, coaches are not allowed to be present, so there is no possibility of human manipulation."

"The exam is essentially testing psychology, knowledge, and skills," the responsible person pointed out. As of July 20 this year, 13,438 people in our city have taken the driving test, with a total of 10,965 passing, accounting for 81.59%. Therefore, as long as students study diligently and hard, they can pass the exam and obtain their driver's license smoothly.

Strictly cracking down on "black money" behavior.

It is reported that currently, there are 25 automobile driving schools in the city, and competition is fierce. For a long time, in order to attract more students, various schools have spared no effort to attract students by lowering training fees. Relevant persons pointed out that on one hand, the current driver's license examination system and social environment both have varying degrees of deficiencies, leading to candidates taking exams with a侥幸psychology of bribery; on the other hand, it cannot be ruled out that some instructors increase their income by collecting "black money" through "unspoken rules", causing the unspoken rules of driving exams to persist despite repeated bans.

It is understood that the traffic police department is the main body responsible for examinations, only handling the application, examination, and issuance of driver's licenses for students. Instructors' qualifications are issued by the transport management department, but their management and constraints belong to the driving schools they work for.

"A school's management has always been strict, absolutely not allowing instructors to solicit bribes from students or require students to 'fundraise' to bribe examiners," emphasized a person in charge of a driving school in Hechi city during an interview. The school has always severely cracked down on so-called "unspoken rules". When instructors come to teach, they first sign a commitment letter with the school, ensuring self-discipline. If students encounter arbitrary charges, they can report them to the school. Once verified, the school will immediately dismiss the instructor.

"Some instructors hint to students to pay 'pass fees', stimulating students'侥幸psychology, which is detrimental to improving training quality and fosters social malpractices," said a responsible person from the vehicle management office. Collecting "black money" from students by instructors is an illegal act. Any bribery behavior discovered can be reported by calling the hotline number. When reporting, detailed time, location, examiner badge number, name, or other forms of evidence should be provided to facilitate investigation by the public security disciplinary inspection department.

In April and May this year, two driving schools in Yizhou and Jinchengjiang were reported for instructors collecting "black money". After investigation by the traffic police department, they were given two months of suspension for rectification. The traffic police department pointed out that driving schools where instructors are found to be collecting "black money" will be suspended from exams. Only after rectification and successful inspection will the school's exam applications be accepted. If it constitutes a violation of law, the relevant departments will hold the responsible parties legally accountable.
