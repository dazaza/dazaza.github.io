Daily life precautions Below is the self-defense manual given to each student by the school when someone first arrived in the UK to study. These short words, if thought carefully, really make sense. And now it seems that the security problems encountered are the same globally. 1. Prepare your keys before you reach your door, don't stand at the door looking for them. 2. If driving a friend home, wait until your friend enters their house before leaving. 3. Always carry various denominations of coins on your person. 4. In case of misfortune, stay calm and remember the features of the other party. 5. Female friends should always have condoms ready; in case of misfortune, require the other party to use one. 6. When driving, always lock the doors. After turning off the car, lights, and engine, stay inside the car for a moment to observe before getting out, and place valuable items where they cannot be seen. 7. Keep a small card with your name and contact details of relatives or friends in your wallet. 8. Open more than two bank accounts, and only carry the ATM card for petty cash (just enough for daily use). Hopefully, this can minimize unexpected incidents, and if an accident does happen, reduce harm as much as possible. 9. When entering or exiting an elevator, pay attention to whether the co-passenger appears malicious or doesn't press a floor button. Try to stand near the control buttons, and if attacked, immediately press all the floor buttons so the elevator stops at every floor. Shout "fire" loudly to draw attention. Don't fight the attacker bare-handed. 10. If sharing rented accommodation, establish clear rules not to casually give keys to other friends. 11. When going out at night, take a jacket and cover your bag to avoid robbery. 12. If followed, kick or hit nearby parked cars with your bag to trigger the alarm and attract others' attention. 13. When taking a taxi, check if anyone is hiding in the front seat. 14. Before getting into your car, check the surroundings. The first action after getting in is to lock the doors to prevent sudden intrusion. When parking, do not write your home phone number on the car window to avoid being intentionally called to move your car for bad intentions. 15. To prevent fake accidents leading to real robberies, upon encountering an accident, do not get out immediately. Calmly observe if there are multiple people or if they seem unfriendly. Drive away quickly while honking loudly to attract others' attention. 16. When parking, if another vehicle follows, after stopping, do not get out immediately. Wait until the other vehicle has driven far away to avoid abduction. 17. When drinking alone in public places, if you leave to go to the restroom or make a call without finishing your drink, it's best not to continue drinking to avoid being drugged. 18. When returning late at night, walk on well-lit streets or walk against traffic to better understand road conditions. Press the doorbell to ask family members to come down and meet you to avoid ambushes in stairwells. Travel safety and what to do in case of robbery Now, let's start talking about the safety of "traveling." Everyone, listen up. I hope those who see this will spread the word so more friends can see it. Of course, don't "like" it because the term I'm most familiar with is "gouzi," and liking it would feel very unpleasant. Let's start with something serious now. First, travel safety: An old mentor told me, often walking at night, sooner or later you'll encounter ghosts, applicable to everyone here regardless of age. Left and right, everyone here is cultured, so I won't need to explain this sentence. Now, I want to clarify that in today's world, everywhere is brightly lit, with more cars than ghosts. So, I've revised it to: often walking at night, you will inevitably encounter vehicles. In the late 1980s, those driving at night were mostly people who had been drinking. Now, in the new century, besides drunk drivers, there are also people out for drinks, picking up women, smugglers, or those desperately trying to support their families. In short, these individuals are either mentally impaired or extremely fatigued, reducing their driving safety. As someone walking, you obviously cannot compete with steel frames, so remember: either go out less at night, or when you see headlights, stay far away. As long as you don't want your wife and children to cry for you, the farther you stay away, the better. Second, tailing: Walking alone in a dark and dimly lit place, when you feel scared, it's indeed a dangerous time. Human intuition is often correct. If you don't believe me, in a dark alley under a starless sky, bravely follow someone ten meters ahead, neither too fast nor too slow, and see if they can last three minutes. If they keep looking back at you, it means they're already scared. If they slow down, they're preparing to fight. If they run quickly, it shows they're smart. I hope everyone hides their pride in their pockets and values survival above all else. Be a smart person. If someone is following you, the best method is to quickly run towards crowded, well-lit areas. If you can't escape, immediately give up your valuables. Remember, giving up your valuables to save your life, do you know why? Why do people keep jumping off buildings to demand wages? It's because it's the only way out. When fighting for your life is ineffective, meaning when someone doesn't care about life and death, it's a terrifying state. If you're willing to trade decades of hard-earned wealth for a reputation of fearlessness and unknown trouble, I think it's incredibly foolish. Therefore, I once again advise everyone, especially ladies, to avoid walking alone at night. Ghosts walk at night. Third, what to do if you encounter a robbery: What to do if you encounter a robbery? Robbing others? Being robbed? This isn't something to joke about. Do you think you're a hero? Have you ever seen someone's face carved up? Seen someone's ear cut off? Haven't seen it, right? Not seeing it is good, so listen carefully to what I have to say. I won't elaborate on the face carving but will mention the ear cutting incident briefly. One day during my schooling (probably early 90s), I saw a few people pull something out of a newspaper roll. Then a flash of cold light appeared, and soon after, someone was holding their bleeding ear. There were only onlookers around, and a solitary ear-like object lay on the ground. The result is unknown. Now, let's talk seriously. What to do if you see someone being robbed? First, you're not Wu Song, second, you're not Schwarzenegger, third, you don't have a machine gun. So, I don't recommend rushing forward to speak justice. Based on my analysis, if you don't listen to me, the consequence is generally lying on the ground like a shrimp, groaning. If your luck is bad, your blood will splash three feet high, not onto the thief but onto the road. If your conscience still exists, I suggest calling the police at 110 and succinctly saying, "Someone is about to die somewhere." This is the best language to prevent deaths. Whether you use your mobile phone or a public phone depends on your judgment. If you use a mobile phone, make sure the thief doesn't see it, otherwise, you'll become the second target. If you yourself encounter a robbery or theft, note that these are two different concepts. Lawyers will tell you, this one-character difference is a matter of life and death. Regardless of which situation you encounter: 1. You must remember, you're not Guo Jing, you don't have martial arts skills. (It's helpful to frequently sing these two lines.) I've seen in movies where heroes take two hits and can still gesture around, but that's not you or me hitting them. You must remember, you're just a mortal body. A brick to your head will make you kiss the ground instantly. If your luck is bad, you might not even recognize your parents. If your luck is worse, like encountering a wolf-tooth club or a big hammer, you'll be chatting with Mr. Impermanence on the Bridge of Sighs. I've heard a phrase: "You have a wolf-tooth club, I have a skull." The person who said this has reportedly been crazy for many years, so you educated people shouldn't believe it. 2. Knowing you're just a mortal body isn't enough; you also need to have the right attitude. Attitude determines everything, so you should silently recite dozens of times daily: "I am a mortal body, I can't withstand a brick hit. I have an 80-year-old mother and a child who needs milk, I can't withstand a brick hit." If you think these words are too vulgar, no problem. I know there are many wise people here who allegedly can achieve instant results with words. They will surely summarize more elegant phrases. Believe that after more than a year of practice, you will definitely engrave this concept deeply in your heart. 3. Remembering you're just a mortal body isn't enough; you also need to address a technical issue—what to do if you foresee a thief? Generally, people here aren't rich, so the possibility of encountering organized robbers is almost zero because the cost of organizing such an operation far exceeds all your assets, including selling blood or shorts. So, the most likely scenario is encountering aimless wanderers seeking prey. With this understanding, things become easier. Most of these people don't value life and death. If you try to fight them, it's a matter of two lives. Therefore, I don't recommend fighting. The basic principle is: money can be taken, but not life. If you encounter such people, immediately hand over the money in your pocket. Then both parties go their separate ways. However, beware—if they tell you they want to tie you up or take both your money and life, that's unacceptable. At this point, I suggest fighting back with anything hard you have, including your teeth, for your final struggle. Here's the truth: rascals are rascals, devils are devils because they lack moral bottom lines. Money is their closest ancestor. So, never try to persuade them with words. Although you may be skilled at verbally incapacitating others here, this trick won't work on criminals—you've never seen someone command a donkey with words, right? 4. If you think doing this is too cowardly, I suggest you reflect in solitude for three days. Think about it—the robbers are homeless drifters. The best-case scenario is that after years of wandering, they finally get caught on some unpredictable date. By then, you wouldn't even be able to access Cat's Eye anymore. Additionally, practice punching stacked paper. To enhance your practice, draw your supervisor's face on the paper or, if possible, obtain their photo and birth details and punch them together. While punching, imagine them begging for mercy. Avoid spitting, as it will wet the paper and cost more money. When you've practiced diligently for years and mastered the skills, you can test them. The goal is: unarmed single assailants. If the opponent has a knife, abandon your plan. If there are three or more opponents, abandon it immediately. If the opponent wields a machete, use your 5000-meter specialty to run toward crowded areas. Choosing this path leaves you with two options: escape or fight to the death. Given this, careful consideration is advised. 5. If you can't run, your teeth are weapons, your pen is a weapon, and your watch, removed and wrapped around your knuckles, is also a weapon. If you have a phone, it's a great weapon, but never throw it like a brick. Hold it tightly and use it fully. If none of these work, take off your coat, wrap it around your arm, and use it as a shield, primarily defending against the opponent's wrist. Blocking knives is possible, but only as a tool to protect your head from iron rods. Subsequently, you'll hear a crisp sound—that's the sound of bones breaking. 6. If doing all this still doesn't work, immediately lie down and pretend to be dead. Understand? Pretend to be dead, and after a while, you'll come back to life.
