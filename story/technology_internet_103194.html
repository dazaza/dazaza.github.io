The Fog in the Internet Industry: Choose Virtual Hosts with Caution

by now on 2008-11-14 10:25:31

The financial crisis has triggered a wave of salary cuts and layoffs. On October 15th, two factories under the Hoppus Group, one of the largest toy manufacturers in the world, went bankrupt. This was called the first case of the U.S. financial crisis affecting the collapse of China's real economy enterprises, with IT companies related to the financial investment industry laying off employees en masse. Since mid-September, IT and technology companies across China have announced layoffs totaling 19,683 people; domestic PC manufacturer Tongfang has also rumored plans for layoffs. Alibaba, the e-commerce site that boasts the world's largest electronic online trading market, also rumored large-scale layoffs in late September. The IDC (Internet Data Center) industry is experiencing another wave of closures. The impact of the "Shanghai Net Housing Incident" has not yet subsided when news of the closure of a Beijing-based IDC company broke out. Ten years of vision in the video conferencing industry came to an abrupt end. Several IDC operators went bankrupt successively, leaving video websites struggling, leading to widespread layoffs in various companies. The global financial tsunami sweeping across the world is gradually revealing its harm to China's real economy.

2008 was a year of internal and external troubles for the IDC industry. Events such as the telecommunications restructuring, the Olympic network lockdown, and the "Internet winter theory" made small and medium-sized IDC enterprises more vulnerable than ever. Currently, all industries are cautiously seeking development. In website construction, many small and medium-sized enterprises are starting to cut costs and no longer favor expensive server hosting services. Virtual hosting products, which offer higher stability and security at affordable prices, better meet the needs of SMEs' website building and personal websites. However, the low threshold for virtual hosting in China has led to market chaos, making it crucial for businesses to select a virtual host that suits their actual situation from the mixed host market. Choosing a capable, skilled, and service-oriented IDC provider is even more critical.

It makes network technology more popular, and people's pursuit of hosting will also increase accordingly. Virtual hosting is gradually developing more and more functions. Virtual hosting is a long-term service, and major providers should comprehensively enhance their enterprise R&D capabilities, strengthen services, and deepen business integration with Internet companies to achieve more sustainable development."
