Keywords: Magnetic rudder, disc-shaped spacecraft, cosmic magnetic field, gyroscope, mini nuclear power

1 Introduction

A disc-shaped spacecraft is a new type of flight apparatus. Research into disc-shaped spacecraft not only broadens the study area of aircraft but also continuously meets civil and commercial needs, as well as international anti-terrorism and national defense requirements. Furthermore, it can be used to develop and utilize space resources, conduct space operations, and experiments.

Compared with other types of aircraft, disc-shaped spacecraft have a more compact structure, can generate tremendous lift, and can balance through gyroscopes. Studying them holds significant practical importance and has gradually become a major research focus both domestically and internationally.

2 Current Research Status

From the current domestic and international research situation, studies on disc-shaped spacecraft (excluding those used as props in films and TV) are mainly divided into two categories based on overall structural form: non-coaxial disc-shaped spacecraft and coaxial disc-shaped spacecraft; if classified by power drive form, they include propeller engines and jet engines.

In my opinion, the drawbacks of currently researched disc-shaped spacecraft are obvious. They resemble true flying saucers only superficially. Although they possess certain flight capabilities, all of them rely on aerodynamic propulsion designs and cannot operate independently outside the atmosphere.

3 Magnetic Flight Mechanism of Flying Saucers

3.1 Definition of Magnetic Rudder

The so-called magnetic rudder refers to a propulsion device that uses electromagnetic principles to produce a powerful, omnidirectional rotating magnetic field using electrical energy, driving itself and its payload through the magnetic field.

Magnets exhibit the property of like poles repelling and opposite poles attracting each other. Magnetic forces can levitate objects as well as propel them. The invention of the electric motor was inspired by this principle, provided there is control over the direction of the magnetic poles of the object being propelled. We can deepen our understanding of magnetic propulsion through an interesting experiment with a floating gyroscope: prepare a toy gyroscope, install a cylindrical magnet in the middle (the rotation axis), with the N pole facing down. Then prepare several large-diameter electromagnet coils (large enough to allow the gyroscope to rotate inside the coil), and connect the ends of the coil to a DC power source. After preparation, set the gyroscope spinning at high speed within the coil and energize the coil, ensuring that the current direction produces a magnetic field with the N pole facing upward. As you increase the current, an interesting phenomenon occurs: the gyroscope rises into the air. If you maintain a constant current, the gyroscope will hover after fluctuating up and down a few times. This phenomenon is not hard to explain: like magnetic poles repel each other, and the magnetic force generated by the electromagnet exerts an upward thrust on the gyroscope. When the thrust exceeds the weight of the gyroscope, it will rise off the ground. As it rises, the magnetic force decreases, reaching equilibrium with gravity at a certain height. Additionally, the rotating gyroscope possesses inherent stability due to its gyroscopic effect, resulting in the fascinating phenomenon of hovering in mid-air. If you further increase the current, the gyroscope will ascend higher until it eventually stops spinning due to air resistance and falls when the magnetic polarity becomes unstable.

3.2 Cosmic Magnetic Field

Earth’s internal liquid conductor iron in turbulent flow creates Earth's magnetic field. Similarly, the universe contains magnetic fields due to the high-speed operation of galaxies, which we can call etheric magnetic fields.

The universe contains many galaxies, which in turn contain numerous planets. There is no gas between these planets, typically referred to as vacuum, yet there exists a substance called a magnetic field. According to reports in "Scientists Daily", scientists recently discovered a mysterious magnetic field in the Milky Way that is constantly changing, namely the large cosmic magnetic field M51 existing in the galaxy.

It is precisely the quasars in the early Milky Way that shine with abnormal brilliance, providing clues for scientists searching for the origin of this large magnetic field: when the universe was only one-third of its current age, these magnetic fields were slowly strengthening. Astronauts found that the light radiating from quasars has an angularity and exhibits polarization phenomena, caused by the distortion of light by cosmic magnetic fields. The degree of polarization increases as the distance of the quasar increases.

Therefore, a research team composed of scientists from the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology used large telescopes at the European Southern Observatory in Chile to observe nearly seventy quasars to find hidden galaxies and mysterious cosmic magnetic fields between the quasars and Earth. During the observation process, researchers found that the degree of polarization of magnesium II spectra in the light emitted by the currently observed quasars far exceeded the polarization conditions of the same spectra obtained from other quasars. Researchers understood this phenomenon as definitive proof of the existence of galaxies and cosmic magnetic fields, with polarization occurring as light passes through galaxies and magnetic fields.

Through spectral redshift, the age of the galactic magnetic field can be inferred. Analysis shows that this magnetic field formed approximately five hundred twenty million years ago. Scientists also found that initially, there was no mysterious magnetic field in this galaxy, but the intense motion of quasars generated a large, weak-intensity cosmic magnetic field. Cosmic storms gradually expanded the magnetic field. Researchers said, "Observations show that the expansion speed of the cosmic magnetic field is quite astonishing." Placing these magnetic field data into computer models related to stars allows us to understand why it formed five hundred million years ago and also understand the impact of the magnetic field on the size of star formation. Currently, scientists continue to observe this mysterious, expanding cosmic magnetic field.

a) Takeoff

b) Ascending Flight

The attitude of the flying saucer makes its horizontal neutral plane form a certain angle with the flight direction, simultaneously utilizing the aerodynamic shape to generate tremendous lift, ascending into space over the strongest magnetic poles of the planet.

During high-altitude or space flight, all magnetic rudders adjust their directions opposite to the cosmic magnetic field lines. The vertical neutral plane of the flying saucer is parallel to the magnetic field lines, eliminating gas resistance, enabling flight at extremely high speeds and accelerations.
