How to correctly distinguish between a ball mill and an autogenous mill

by hx515317 on 2012-02-29 10:29:30

How to distinguish between a ball mill and an autogenous mill is probably a very headache-inducing issue for many people. However, from the perspective of Hongxing, it's actually quite simple. By analyzing their respective performance characteristics, one can easily discern the differences between them. Below, Hongxing will lead everyone in analyzing their individual features:

The performance characteristics of autogenous mills include the following points:

1. Large grinding ratio - up to 3000 or more, simplifying the crushing process by combining coarse, medium, and fine crushing into one step. Typically, a single-stage autogenous mill process can replace medium crushing, fine crushing, and coarse grinding operations; the process is short, reducing the number of operators and maintenance personnel as well as costs, with significant economic benefits.

2. Strong selective grinding effect - this has been proven effective for diamonds. There are no domestic studies on asbestos yet, but foreign data suggests that it can be used.

3. Reduces steel consumption.

4. Minimizes product sludging and iron contamination.

5. Relationship between the rotational speed of the sphere, the material inside the mill, and product quality.

As for ball mills, there are various types, but we'll analyze just two:

1. Performance characteristics of grid-type ball mills:

1. Fast ore discharge, high productivity (20-30% higher than overflow type, saving 20-30% electricity).

2. Minimal overgrinding - low-level ore discharge, less stored pulp, easy timely discharge of finely ground particles.

3. More balls can be loaded - not only large balls but also small balls due to the presence of grid plates, providing good grinding conditions.

4. The structure is more complex compared to the overflow type - multiple grid plates occupy space, reducing effective volume.

2. Performance characteristics of overflow-type ball mills:

1. The main advantage of overflow-type ball mills is their simple structure, ease of management and maintenance, and fine grinding product size (generally less than 0.2mm).

2. The disadvantages are high liquid level during ore discharge, long retention time of pulp inside the mill, low unit volume processing capacity, uneven discharge particle size, and tendency to produce overgrinding.

3. Overflow-type ball mills are suitable for the second stage of grinding in a two-stage grinding process and for regrinding intermediate products.

From this, it's easy to see the differences between autogenous mills and ball mills. It's worth emphasizing that the most significant feature of autogenous mills is their ability to directly feed raw ore from the mine site or ore that has undergone coarse crushing into the mill. Usually, minerals are fed into the mill according to a specific particle size distribution. Autogenous mills can grind materials down to -0.074mm in one pass, with its content accounting for more than 20％～50％ of the total product. The crushing ratio can reach 4000～5000, several times higher than that of ball mills or rod mills. An autogenous mill is a new type of grinding equipment that combines both crushing and grinding functions. It uses the material itself as the medium, achieving pulverization through mutual impact and abrasion, hence the name autogenous mill. Autogenous mills are also referred to as media-less mills.
