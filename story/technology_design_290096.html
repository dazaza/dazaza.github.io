Inner Mongolia's female prosecutor, who drives a luxury car worth a million, is again exposed for building a luxurious office building

by djbgufgire on 2009-12-03 11:25:32

The female procurator of Inner Mongolia who drives a luxury car worth a million yuan has been exposed again for building a luxurious office building. The People's Procuratorate Office of Arong Banner, Inner Mongolia was provided by netizens. The under-construction People's Procuratorate of Arong Banner and its effect picture were also provided by netizens. Rednet Changsha, December 2nd (reporter Tianming Fu from Hunan Morning Post) - the female procurator of the People's Procuratorate of Arong Banner, Hulunbuir City, Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, was recently exposed to "use public funds to purchase a luxury car worth more than one million yuan for her own use" and pictures were uploaded as evidence. In response to the overwhelming claims, she replied that she was defamed. Just when the "luxury car" controversy had not yet subsided, another "luxury building" controversy arose.

According to posts on Tianya Community and Rednet Forum, the old office building of the Procuratorate located on Alun Street was built in 2005 and put into use in 2006, covering an area of more than 3,000 square meters. Alun Street runs north-south, with the Public Security Bureau, Procuratorate, and Court built sequentially from north to south on the right side of the street.

One claim is that Liu did not want to be sandwiched between the Public Security Bureau and the Court, so she decided to construct an independent building. Another popular claim is that the original Procuratorate building had no windows on the protruding corner, which was considered bad feng shui, so Liu redecorated the building's corner and added windows.

In the photos provided by netizens, two black lions are placed on either side of the Procuratorate entrance, and these black lions have been identified by netizens as being used to "ward off evil spirits and protect the residence."

The post pointed out that Liu used the construction of case-handling and technical business rooms as a reason to plan the construction of a new office building on the left side of Alun Street (about 500 meters away from the original Arong Banner Procuratorate building, diagonally opposite the County Court) and received approval. About half a year later, a six-story new office building rose. The tender document shows that the building covers an area of 6,379.29 square meters. Adding the area of the old office building, the total area of the Arong Banner People's Procuratorate Office Building is about 10,000 square meters.

The post finally calculated the cost of the Arong Banner Procuratorate's office building. There are approximately 45 employees, temporary workers, and drivers at the Procuratorate, meaning the average office space per person will exceed 220 square meters. Therefore, Liu was criticized again: "There is no quality issue with the old office building of Arong Banner. Liu Lijie's construction of a new office building is a complete waste of national fiscal revenue."

Previously, Liu had been caught up in the "luxury car" controversy. She once defended herself to the media, claiming that the car was borrowed from a friend, but this statement not only failed to clear her name but also drew more rebuttals from netizens.

In a poverty-stricken county, the luxury car

According to relevant data, in November 2007, Liu Lijie became the procurator of the Arong Banner Procuratorate. Previously, she served as the deputy procurator of Yakeshi City Procuratorate in Hulunbuir.

Not long after she took office as the procurator of Arong Banner, she was embroiled in a network storm in November this year.

The trigger was a post titled "A Female Procurator in a Poor County and Her Famous Car," which then sparked heated replies from netizens.

Netizens directly pointed out that Arong Banner, Hulunbuir City, Inner Mongolia is an old revolutionary base, a typical agricultural banner, and a provincial-level poor county with a weak economic foundation. In 2006, the county's fiscal revenue barely exceeded ten million yuan, doubling since the year 2000. The capital accumulation in the county is difficult, making it a typical "food-eating" finance. According to the 2004 population census, this provincial-level poor county only had 320,000 people. However, in just two years since taking office, the female procurator of the county's Procuratorate, Liu Lijie, purchased five new cars for her new unit, the Arong Banner People's Procuratorate, each more advanced and expensive than the last.

Netizens also mentioned that when Liu Lijie first arrived at the Arong Banner Procuratorate, she bought two Hyundai Sonata cars for two of her "trusted subordinates," with license plate numbers Meng E69929 and Meng E69939. She herself bought a Buick LaCrosse worth about 400,000 yuan, with a license plate number Meng OE0729. Later, she also purchased a Buick business car worth over 200,000 yuan. After driving the Buick for more than a year, due to some minor accidents on the road, she changed her official car to a Volkswagen Touareg worth nearly one million yuan.

Another detail in the post mentioned that previously, Liu Lijie had fallen into controversy. On June 15, 2006, three thieves broke into her home in Yakeshi and stole 260,000 yuan in cash, jewelry, watches, and other items. The total value disclosed in the indictment was over 320,000 yuan, and there were rumors of shockingly valuable property deeds.

Relevant departments are investigating, and the results will be announced soon.

According to the national standards for official vehicles, only officials at or above the level of full provincial ministerial rank can be equipped with official cars worth more than 450,000 yuan. The official vehicles used by Liu Lijie far exceed the national standard. As a procurator at the deputy department level, she showed no integrity or frugality, instead extravagantly spending millions to buy famous car models in just two years.

Questions arise: where did the money come from for the Arong Banner Procuratorate to buy luxury cars and build a new office building in a poor county?

On December 1st, reporters from Hunan Morning Post repeatedly dialed Liu Lijie's phone, but it was never answered.

The Secretary of the Flag Committee answered the call, suggesting finding the spokesperson but did not provide contact information for the spokesperson.

On December 1st, the reporter again called the Secretary of the Political and Legal Affairs Committee of the flag, the flag governor, and the flag secretary, but none of them answered the calls.

"This matter, there are inaccurate places on the internet." A responsible person from the Publicity Department of the flag told Hunan Morning Post that relevant local departments are currently investigating the matter. Regarding the specific details of the investigation, the responsible person said it is temporarily inconvenient to disclose.

He said that the investigation results would be announced soon. When asked by the reporter for the specific date of the announcement, the responsible person still said, "It will be very soon," and would be announced at an appropriate time in the near future.
