Bottleneck of patent protection

by keyizhang1 on 2012-02-28 14:26:53

Enterprises, especially small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), are the main players in technological innovation. However, due to the restriction of patent maintenance costs, SMEs are unable to effectively protect their intellectual property rights, leading to insufficient motivation for innovation.

Patent annual fee costs:

Currently, the situation of patent application and patent annual fees in our country is as follows: the application fee for an invention patent is 950 yuan (including a printing fee of 50 yuan); the application fee for a utility model patent is 500 yuan; the application fee for a design patent is 500 yuan; and the examination fee for an invention application is 2500 yuan. To obtain and maintain a patent, the applicant must also pay patent annual fees and other charges to the Patent Office after the application.

The current national standard for patent annual fees increases gradually according to the patent year, which is intended to prompt patent holders to automatically abandon patents with no value. The annual fee for an invention patent is: years 1-3, 900 yuan; years 4-6, 1200 yuan; years 7-9, 2000 yuan; years 10-12, 4000 yuan; years 13-15, 6000 yuan; and years 16-20, 8000 yuan. For a utility model patent: years 1-3, 600 yuan; years 4-5, 900 yuan; years 6-8, 1200 yuan; and years 9-10, 2000 yuan.

For enterprises with a large number of authorized patents, this cost is not a small figure. For example, Huawei spends tens of millions of yuan annually on patent annual fees; a medium-sized enterprise with hundreds of patents needs to pay over 100,000 yuan in patent annual fees each year. For some SMEs, the patent annual fee has become a headache. Due to concerns about the inability to afford excessive patent costs, some SMEs will abandon patent applications; and for patents already obtained, if they fail to generate income through product sales or technology transfer, they will also abandon these patents. Although the state or local governments offer preferential policies for patent fees, their effects are limited.

Infringement litigation costs:

Infringement behaviors in the market continue despite repeated bans. In order to safeguard their legitimate rights and interests, enterprises must seek protection under intellectual property laws, thereby generating judicial-level patent maintenance costs.

To protect their patent rights, enterprises must necessarily invest corresponding human resources, material resources, and finances in investigation and evidence collection. A single patent lawsuit can incur many expenses: advance payment for litigation fees, lawyer agency fees, pre-litigation preservation fees, infringement evidence collection and notarization fees, judicial appraisal fees, travel expenses, etc. Moreover, since infringement behaviors are often distributed across various regions nationwide, it leads to increased costs.

For instance, Apple incurred significant costs in patent litigation. According to industry estimates, Apple spent more than $100 million on just one lawsuit against HTC. In fact, Apple could have used this money for research and development or product development, actions that would certainly have been worthwhile for Apple.

The issue of excessively high litigation costs is common both domestically and internationally. Small business groups in the UK strongly urged the government to protect the patents of small businesses, arguing that high court litigation costs prevent illegal acts from being punished, and urging the government to provide more protection for small business innovation.

The cost of patent maintenance significantly affects the innovative drive of China's SMEs at this stage, especially emerging private science and technology enterprises, making it an urgent problem that needs to be solved.
