"Chinese People Can Eat Safer" Chapter 2: Purchasing Safe Food - A Housewife's Essential Skills

As a housewife, after work, the thing I think about the most is what to cook for dinner to satisfy the whole family's appetite. To make delicious dishes, one must have high-quality ingredients. Personally, I prefer shopping at large supermarkets because they can provide a certain level of quality control during the purchasing process. Moreover, if any quality issues arise, one can directly file a complaint at the supermarket. The situation is quite different at the market where some unscrupulous vendors sell substandard goods, and if problems occur, they will not take responsibility. If food causes health issues, no one would be able to bear the consequences.

Of course, shopping at supermarkets is my personal preference, and I'm not saying one should absolutely avoid buying groceries at markets. As long as you can "identify quality goods," regardless of where you choose to buy your food, you won't end up with substandard products!

"Chinese People Can Eat Safer" Chapter 4: The "Ultimate Path" of Kitchen Operations

Chinese people place great emphasis on taste and color when cooking, striving for a complete combination of color, aroma, and flavor. Sometimes, even nutritional value is sacrificed for taste. I believe this approach is somewhat counterproductive. Household diets should especially avoid preparing meals that disregard nutrition. Additionally, improper cooking methods or carelessness during details can easily produce substances harmful to health. Although I am not an expert in cooking, combining food safety with my own cooking experiences has allowed me to summarize many useful tips. I hope my experience can add flavor to your healthy kitchen.

"Chinese People Can Eat Safer" Chapter 5: The Key to a Healthy Diet in Daily Life

Chinese people have always emphasized dietary health preservation. However, in ancient times, common people struggled to secure a meal, making dietary health preservation a privilege of the few noble classes. Now, with improved living standards, health and well-being have become universally attainable goals, no longer mere dreams. Improved living standards focus on eating better and more thoughtfully, not merely indulging in meat but also enjoying the nutritional benefits of a healthy diet. However, most people only have a superficial understanding of dietary health preservation, often eating haphazardly without much thought. This easily leads to mistakes, often resulting in harm rather than benefit. In reality, knowledge of dietary health preservation doesn't need to be comprehensive or extensive; mastering some basic common sense is enough for the entire family.
