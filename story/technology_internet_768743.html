Xiaomi Router Price Exposed: Forget About 99 Yuan

by anonymous on 2013-11-21 14:02:32

This morning, Xiaomi officially announced its "new toy," the smart router, and simultaneously launched a new webpage for "Xiaomi Router" with the new domain miwifi.com. However, apart from a few pictures of the appearance, no detailed information or pricing was released by the official side.

This afternoon, there were further reports that the Xiaomi router would be equipped with a 1.7GHz Qualcomm Snapdragon 600 quad-core CPU, 2GB of RAM, and 8GB of flash storage, supporting dual-band Wi-Fi and Bluetooth 4.0. It would come pre-installed with a deeply customized MIUI V5, support remote control via smartphone, and even include a built-in robotic vacuum dock. Its price was rumored to be just 99 yuan.

If the specifications are accurate, many tech-savvy netizens would agree that the 99-yuan price is completely unrealistic; a single Snapdragon 600 chip alone should cost more than this price. Now, the true price of the Xiaomi router has been revealed, and it's quite surprising.

This afternoon, an insider released a screenshot. Based on the information in the screenshot, the final price of the Xiaomi router starts at 799 yuan.

Specifically speaking, the Xiaomi router should be able to achieve functions similar to a NAS (Network-Attached Storage), serving as a home data center with an internal hard drive for data storage (indicating that an offline download function will likely be included). From the screenshot, we can see that the version with a 1TB internal hard drive is priced at 799 yuan, and there may also be options with larger capacities, which would naturally be more expensive.

Additionally, there are reports that the Xiaomi router will have two versions: a lower-end model using Broadcom's BCM5358U solution, supporting 2.4/5GHz dual-band Wi-Fi, with an undetermined price, and a higher-end model mentioned earlier, which includes the internal hard drive and is priced starting at 799 yuan.

Notably, the official Weibo account @Kuaipan hinted today that there might be "some intersections" with the Xiaomi router, suggesting that one of the key features of the Xiaomi router could be "cloud storage." However, there are currently no clear details available.
