Wuxi is actually not far from me, but it has been a long time since I last visited. The last time I went was when I was very young, about ten or twenty years ago. I have almost no impression of it in my mind, except for the Wuxi spareribs which I still remember clearly.

I boarded the EMU train at 7:15 on time and headed to Wuxi. After 54 minutes, I arrived at Wuxi on time. As everyone said, there were indeed a lot of aunties and uncles at the train station asking if you wanted to go to tourist attractions or take their small cars. Sigh~ I really hope that the relevant departments in Wuxi can rectify this situation, at least consolidate it. There was no other way but to patiently refuse the aunties and uncles one by one. Then I boarded the No. 82 bus and headed straight to the Three Kingdoms City. The Shanghai transportation card can be used in Wuxi, which is very cost-effective. A 2-yuan bus ticket only costs 1.2 yuan when using the card, reducing travel expenses. After 40 minutes, I arrived at the Three Kingdoms City. I had already contacted the seller I found on Taobao beforehand, and the tour guide was already waiting at the entrance. After smoothly picking up the tickets, we entered the Three Kingdoms City. It's a nice place. The guides all say that it takes one and a half hours to finish, but we spent two and a half hours. The "Three Heroes Battle Lu Bu" performance at 10:30 was still worth watching, but there were quite a few people. After the 20-minute play ended, we slowly walked towards the arena where there was a "Peach Garden Oath" performance at 11 o'clock. This one wasn't as good as the previous one. After watching Liu Bei, Guan Yu, and Zhang Fei swear allegiance, we left. There were many amusement facilities nearby with interesting names like "Naval Training Camp". Further ahead were Zhou Yu's general platform and the Seven Stars Altar, which we visited on our way back later. We first went to the Wu camp water fort and took an ancient warship, which wasn't particularly interesting, so it's recommended to skip it. After getting off, we walked straight ahead to the Wu King Palace. This place was pretty good, feeling like a hidden world inside, with some grandeur. Then we wandered around the Ganlu Temple. Wuxi has quite a few places for burning incense. We then returned to the general platform mentioned earlier and then left. Overall, the Three Kingdoms City is still worth a visit. Passing by the Water Margin City on the bus, it seemed less popular than the Three Kingdoms City. As for Tang City, there was basically no one at the entrance. If you want to explore thoroughly, it's better to focus on one place. Also, there aren't many dining spots inside, so it's suggested to bring your own snacks.

In addition to the Three Kingdoms City, I took the No. 82 bus to Baojie Village and saw the signpost for Yuantouzhu, so I followed it in. Hey, this stop was quite far and difficult to walk. If going from the Three Kingdoms City to Yuantouzhu, it's recommended to take the No. 1 bus for one stop, which stops right at the entrance of Yuantouzhu. After contacting the online seller again, I picked up the group tickets and entered. Mistakenly believing the online rumor that the distance from the entrance to the ferry point wasn't far, I walked in leisurely, but it turned out to be quite a long road, taking about 20 minutes. So, it's better to take the bus at the entrance~ At this time, quite a few cherry blossoms had fallen, but they were still worth seeing~ Upon arriving at the Yuantouzhu scenic area, there were many people waiting for the ferry. Just wait, it won't take long as each boat can carry a lot of people. Then follow the signs and visit each attraction one by one. Due to the large number of people, I actually found Yuantouzhu less fun than the Three Kingdoms City. Moreover, the weather wasn't the clearest, making the entire lake surface look misty, not lively~ When returning, there was another long queue, but the waiting time wasn't long as the line kept moving, allowing us to board the ship quickly. After leaving, we headed straight to the No. 1 bus stop. There were still a lot of people, so we waited~

After getting off at Shengli Gate, I strolled along Zhongshan Road. Due to construction, it wasn't easy to walk. I saw the famous Sanfeng Bridge but didn't buy anything. I continued walking to Wangxingji on Zhongshan Road, where many varieties had already sold out. I ordered crab roe soup dumplings and shepherds purse wontons. Wuxi's soup dumplings are sweeter, with soy sauce meat, and the skin is thicker, so I didn't have much liking for them~ Then I took the bus back to the hotel, staying at Baijin Hangjue Grand Hotel.

This restaurant is a private one, and on the day we went, there were two newlywed couples holding banquets. This hotel is relatively convenient for travel, with a car able to reach places like the train station, and the road wasn't very congested. However, the location is a bit remote. But the rooms are quite large, the facilities are excellent, with both a bathtub and a shower room. Thoughtfully, they also provide disposable cups, password boxes, and disposable slippers that feel more like home slippers, which are quite good. They even offer computer rentals for 50 yuan a day, considering everything rather carefully. However, it was a bit disappointing that breakfast wasn't included in the room rate.

The next morning, I took the No. 115 bus to the train station, bought breakfast at KFC, and then boarded the No. 88 bus. During the ride, I was approached countless times by aunties and uncles. There are many No. 88 buses, and after a one and a half hour ride, I reached the destination. After contacting the Taobao seller, I picked up the tickets and entered for a visit. Due to equipment maintenance, I didn't see the Nine-Dragon Bathing, but Ling Mountain Scenic Area was still grandiose. After rubbing Buddha's feet, I arrived at the Brahma Palace. It is said that 500 million yuan was spent on decoration, and it was indeed magnificent, with dazzling ceilings. Originally planning to watch the "Jixiang Song", there were no tickets left. Anyway, after visiting the replicas of the twelve animal heads, I went to eat the buffet. For 38 yuan per person, the cost-effectiveness was still acceptable. The variety was rich, and the dining environment was good, except for a slightly dirty tablecloth, everything else was fine. The vegetarian dishes imitating meat dishes were done decently. After eating enough, I continued touring the Brahma Palace. On the lower floor, there was a souvenir shop. Some Q-version Buddhas were quite nice, so I bought one to take home~ Then I took the No. 88 bus back to the train station. Because it rained, I didn't use the remaining time and went straight to McDonald's beside the train station. I saw plainclothes policemen in Wuxi catching people with style.

Looking back on these two days of itinerary, Wuxi is a place worth visiting several times. The attractions are quite far apart, but the public buses are relatively convenient. There are many choices for accommodation, but the attraction tickets are quite expensive. I haven't bought any full-price tickets. If I have another chance to come here, I will visit Xihui Park, Liyuan, Donglin Academy, and Meiyuan to make up for missed visits. These places all have some cultural depth.
