Troublemaker claims he was falsely accused of hitting people, saying that he was helping an old man who fell down. Monitoring proves he was lying.

by abcearwj on 2012-03-09 10:06:52

China News Service, Nanning, March 8 (Lin Zengchong, Sun Jie) - The much-publicized Guangxi version of the "Xu Yunhe" incident has finally come to light: the woman from Yulin, Guangxi, who claimed she was wrongfully accused of causing an accident while helping an elderly person as part of a "Learn from Lei Feng" initiative, finally admitted her guilt after the traffic police department revealed surveillance footage showing her hitting the pedestrian.

On March 2, posts appeared on Tianya Community and Guangxi Red Bean Community forums titled "[Xu Yunhe Case Reappears in Guangxi??]." The author, identifying herself as "Zhang Du," claimed to be from Bobai County in Yulin City, Guangxi. She posted about being wrongfully accused by the traffic police and the victim's family of causing the accident, seeking online public opinion support.

"At around 11:40 AM on March 1, I was driving my own minivan out of the West City Parking Lot on Dabeilu Street in Yulin. I saw an elderly person trembling beside the road divider at the gate. I drove extra cautiously and slowly. The elderly person was on the right side of my car when I passed. Unintentionally looking at the left rearview mirror, I noticed that the elderly person suddenly sat down. Seeing this, I immediately stopped the car, ran over, and helped the elderly person up, carrying them to the roadside."

"I thought, isn't there a lot of talk in newspapers and magazines about acts of bravery? Everyone can see that I didn't knock him down, so there shouldn't be any problem, right?"

In the post, "Zhang Du" compared herself to the Guangxi version of "Xu Yunhe," repeatedly regretting that she should not have helped the elderly person and calling for society to restore her innocence and fairness. On March 5, a local Yulin newspaper also published an article on Red Bean Community and its personal blog titled "Collision or Assistance? Farmer Zhang Du from Bobai is Recently Troubled."

The incident drew significant attention from netizens, with some demanding that the traffic police "return the car to the owner," causing considerable interference for the authorities handling the case during the investigation phase. Vice Mayor of Yulin City and Director of the Public Security Bureau, Xu Jianzhong, also requested that the traffic police quickly uncover the truth, enforce the law impartially, and handle the matter seriously and according to the law.

On March 7, in the accident resolution inquiry room of the First Detachment of the Traffic Police Brigade of the Yulin Municipal Public Security Bureau, Zhang Du continued to insist that she was not the perpetrator: "I have no regrets about my conscience!" To completely break her "defense," the police officers played the electronic surveillance video from near the accident scene. The footage showed that the involved elderly person was struck by the front-left corner of a minivan turning at high speed while crossing the road, and the elderly person fell immediately. This vehicle was precisely the one driven by Zhang Du.

Faced with the video evidence, Zhang Du's expression changed dramatically, and she ultimately confessed awkwardly that she was indeed the perpetrator.
