The ticket only shows a decline in the 3-year and 5-day growth performance.

by hartyqekb on 2009-12-07 17:47:13

The Central Economic Work Conference is set to conclude today. The market expects that next year will continue to see the implementation of an active fiscal policy and a moderately loose monetary policy. Investors are eagerly awaiting the release of fiscal, monetary, and industry policies from the conference. Based on past experiences, policies announced at the meeting that exceed expectations may prompt related sectors to benefit and rise.

The three-day Central Economic Work Conference is expected to end today with the release of the meeting content announcement. There might be some changes in the market's suitable investment direction. Personal household consumption may receive subsidies, and resource price system reform should continue, leading to potential increases in resource prices.

Macroeconomic Trend: Unchanged Stance but Significant Fine-tuning

Fang Si Hai, Chief Economist of Hongyuan Securities, stated that it is estimated the central government’s fiscal budget will continue the "high deficit" route next year and may even surpass the 2009 record of 950 million yuan, hovering around the "1 trillion yuan mark." Joan Hong, China economist at Goldman Sachs, believes the absolute amount of the central fiscal budget deficit may increase, but its proportion of GDP will stabilize at the 2009 level (less than 3%).

Fang Si Hai is confident that under the traction of continued relatively high investment, improved consumption, and external demand, the national economic growth rate will definitely exceed the growth of 2009. It is predicted that the GDP growth level in 2010 will be between 9.0% to 10.0%, where resident consumption growth should be over 10%; fixed asset investment growth rate may relatively decrease by several percentage points compared to 2009 but will still remain at a relatively high level.

Joan Hong believes that the economy will continue steady growth in 2010 and 2011. This could be an excellent opportunity for China to improve people's livelihoods, reform social security, healthcare, and education systems, as well as strengthen environmental protection before the full recovery of external demand. The Central Economic Work Conference is also expected to make more efforts in these directions. Besides promoting consumption, there is significant room for policy adjustments in solving regional development imbalances and issues related to energy and resource efficiency. Goldman Sachs also anticipates that the government will introduce specific fiscal measures to stimulate private investment and resident consumption, such as providing loan interest subsidies and consumption subsidies.

Monetary Policy: Increased Efforts in Central Bank Liquidity Drainage

Professor Li Shi Kai from Guangdong University of Finance analyzed that the phrasing of a loose monetary policy won't change, and the possibility of interest rate hikes has decreased. Monetary policy fine-tuning might be reflected in the strength of liquidity drainage through open market operations.

According to statistics compiled by the reporter, starting from October, the central bank has continuously reduced the money supply for eight weeks through open market operations, achieving a net drainage of 687 billion yuan.

However, due to the current situation where first and second-tier market interest rates are inverted, this restricts the demand for central bank bills, leaving the central bank in a dilemma of needing to raise central bank bill interest rates to continue substantial drainage. Senior economist Lu Zheng Wei from Industrial Bank's Fund Operation Department believes that the central bank will undoubtedly continue to increase drainage efforts but does not want to raise central bank bill interest rates because any increase would create pressure on benchmark bank interest rates. Therefore, the central bank might restart 3-year central bank bills to address this.

Nevertheless, Guo Tian Yong, Director of the China Banking Center at Central University of Finance and Economics, expressed that if next year sees rapid inflation or asset price increases, there remains a possibility of mid-term adjustment in policy stance, such as interest rate hikes or reserve requirement ratio increases.

Li Shi Kai further opined that monetary policy fine-tuning might also manifest in structural adjustments in credit issuance. Real estate credit policies may undergo changes, potentially leading to regulatory measures being introduced for the real estate sector.

Market Outlook: High Probability of Stock Market Rise After the Meeting

Experts believe that the possibility of major unexpected policy adjustments from this Central Economic Work Conference is relatively low. However, the meeting might lay the groundwork for future policy fine-tunings.

In the past 11 years, only three years saw declines in the stock market within five days after the meeting, while seven years witnessed overall rises. Policies exceeding expectations announced during the meeting may prompt relevant sectors to benefit and rise. Among them, specific policies related to low-carbon and new energy, domestic demand consumption, agriculture, and regional economic development are worth looking forward to.

Industry analysts believe that the release of relevant policies from the Central Economic Work Conference, akin to the "falling shoe," is positive for the stock market and beneficial for long-term bullish trends. In the short term, however, factors like stock index futures, margin trading, and refinancing may have a greater impact on the market.

Recently, the stock market has seen significant fluctuations, yet emerging industries have been unusually active. It is anticipated that policies exceeding expectations announced during the meeting may prompt relevant sectors to benefit and rise. Specific policies related to low-carbon and new energy, domestic demand consumption, agriculture, and regional economic development are worth looking forward to.
