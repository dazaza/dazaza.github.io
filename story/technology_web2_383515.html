Online personal card: about.me

by gadgetoflife on 2011-03-05 12:37:18

It's already not easy to keep track of how many Social Network links and profiles you have. To then tell your friends your personal online information, there are just too many links to send. So the emergence of online personal cards has indeed made things more convenient. Let me introduce to you a beautiful, free online personal card: About.me.

It is a very powerful tool, yet very simple to use. On About.me, you can create a page with your own name, for example, which is not only extremely easy to remember, but also conveys the purpose of this page through its URL. Yes, this webpage is for introducing yourself.

Users can freely insert their own photo, name, title, and personal introduction, as well as the network services they use on the page, such as Facebook, Blogger, Twitter, Foursquare, Linkedin, Flickr or Posterous, making it very convenient and easy to handle. All editing actions will be previewed and saved instantly, so you don't have to worry about losing data due to forgetting to save changes after modifications. Some well-known figures have already started using the About.me service, and you can check out their personal pages, such as the founder, WordPress founder, AOL CEO, Engadget and Gizmodo founders, GigaOM founder and writer, etc.

You can take a look at mine for reference. What are you waiting for? Create your online personal card now! [via]
