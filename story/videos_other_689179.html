Minimalism and the Creation of Ultimate Swordsmanship:

Black robes, black headscarves, black boots... After four drafts, the final appearance of Jet Li’s character has no superfluous colors or decorative components, retaining only the simplest and most practical parts.

Despite consistently maintaining a simple look on screen, this "Swordsman" image in "Flying Swords of Dragon Gate" is considered true "minimalism," including his ever-faithful longsword, which appears unremarkable. Director Fl克, familiar with Jet Li's performance characteristics, believes: "What makes Jet Li most attractive is the fluid beauty of his martial arts. I don't want costumes and decorations to restrict his movements; all the audience's attention should be focused on his unique figure and swordsmanship. Therefore, we need to highlight his sword skills through the simplicity of his character design."

Back in the 1992 version of "The Smiling, Proud Wanderer," Fl克, Jet Li, and action director Yuen Bun formed an iron triangle, transforming Jin Yong's famous "Nine Swords of Dugu" into tangible forms. The creative action designs like "Breaking Sword Style" and "Swinging Sword Style" are still regarded as classics today. Now, the three have reunited, aiming to create even more visually stunning "ultimate swordsmanship." Action director Yuen Bun stated: "The highest level of swordplay has always existed only in legends because it is difficult to master, and those who can wield a sword perfectly are few. Because the one wielding the sword this time is Jet Li, we can confidently design more challenging and innovative moves; with his experience and reputation, he will not disappoint us."

One Literary Scene, Ten Martial Scenes - 3D Promoting Kung Fu:

Twenty years ago, Jet Li's swordplay on screen was already impeccable, but now, executing beautiful sword techniques in the 3D visual world is more challenging than expected. Director Fl克 introduced: "The three key elements of martial arts design are the difficulty of the moves, overall layout, and how to use imagery to present the characteristics and principles of Chinese martial arts. 3D brings a completely new visual experience, ultimately rendering the effects previously seen on 2D screens less impactful. Therefore, these three points cannot simply follow traditional rules but must be redesigned to suit the best 3D presentation."

Halfway through filming, Jet Li admitted that he had gained a deeper understanding and appreciation of the difficulties involved in shooting a 3D wuxia film. For example, 3D cameras are much larger and more complex, so actors must consider their positioning relative to the camera while fighting, sometimes necessitating multiple retakes even when a move is executed perfectly due to the special requirements of 3D positioning. Additionally, to highlight the advantages of 3D in depth and stereoscopic perception, complex action combinations must be broken down. Jet Li joked that now he often gets through literary scenes in one take, but martial scenes require repeated filming over a dozen times, "but after finishing this movie, my kung fu probably improved again because now I can accurately position myself mid-air."

Due to the long shooting schedule and harsh winter conditions, Jet Li deeply felt the hardships of the creative process and hoped to fulfill his mission: "Director Fl克's habit is to complete the editing of a set of actions on-site, and wearing 3D glasses, the force and speed of wuxia actions appear greatly amplified, making you feel like everything is happening just one meter away from you, causing involuntary tension and pressure."
