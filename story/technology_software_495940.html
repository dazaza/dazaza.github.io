Jinshan KuaiPan New Version Preview: Will Launch with the New "Cloud Bridge" Interface Technology

by xdash on 2011-09-27 20:41:51

On September 19th, Kingsoft's cloud synchronization software—Kingsoft KuaiPan (www.kuaipan.cn)—announced a new technology with "global pioneering significance" at a new product communication event and partner conference for Kingsoft Office Software in Beijing. This technology is called the Cloud Bridge Technology, or embedded integrated operation interface technology.

(Above: Preview of the new version of KuaiPan’s “Cloud Bridge” interface; click to view larger image.)

According to reports, in the upcoming new version of KuaiPan, both local and server files will be managed under a unified visible interface. This interface seamlessly integrates with the file explorer through an embedded method. When users perform regular operations on a file within KuaiPan, they can see the operation progress and effects on the backend server through this interface. Such integration is unprecedented in domestic and international cloud storage services. This technology will greatly improve the current situation where using cloud storage is not intuitive and users struggle to understand server-side operations, further promoting the adoption of sync cloud drives among general users.

Yang Gang, head of KuaiPan, stated: "Kingsoft KuaiPan has always focused on refining product technology and mining user needs. After analyzing the reasons for Dropbox's high valuation, it becomes clear that the number of users, profitability, user experience, and innovation are key factors for cloud storage products to grasp the market pulse. Innovation provides the driving force for the other three aspects. The launch of Cloud Bridge Technology today marks a milestone in KuaiPan's development, and we have full confidence in winning the long-term competition."

Background information: In 2007, Kingsoft established a cloud storage laboratory. At the time, KuaiPan was only intended to provide personal document storage functionality for tens of millions of active WPS Office users. Later, the acceptance and popularity of Dropbox abroad demonstrated the vast potential of personal cloud storage services, leading to KuaiPan being spun off as an independent entity. The release of the internal beta version in March 2010 made KuaiPan China's first multi-terminal synchronized cloud drive. Within over a year of its launch, KuaiPan's user base has approached 5 million, with users uploading over 2 billion files, totaling more than 1 PB in volume, processing nearly 50 million files daily.

It is also reported that the KuaiPan team has already been split out to operate independently within Kingsoft. Yang Gang mentioned that the primary goal of current business development is to grow the user base, and the company will begin offering online storage services in the European, American, and Japanese markets after the National Day holiday.
