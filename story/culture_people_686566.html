【Qing Dynasty Culture】Yongzheng and Nian Gengyao

Emperor Yongzheng had a close friend named Nian Gengyao. Yongzheng's private relationship with Nian was very good, and he gave Nian special favor. Yongzheng believed that having a frontier governor like Nian Gengyao was his good fortune; if there were ten or so people like him, the country would not worry about being well governed. After quelling the rebellion in Qinghai, Yongzheng was extremely excited and, disregarding the dignity of his supreme position, called Nian Gengyao his "benefactor." Yongzheng also required that future generations remember Nian Gengyao's great achievements forever, otherwise they would not be his descendants or subjects.

Yongzheng once said to Nian Gengyao: "If I do not become an outstanding emperor, I will feel unworthy of your loyalty to me; if you do not become an extraordinary minister, you cannot repay my trust in you. I hope that we two can set a timeless example for future generations."

However, at this time, Nian Gengyao was full of pride and completely intoxicated by the flattery and favor shown to him, leading him to commit many acts beyond his proper role, which eventually alerted and aroused Yongzheng's suspicion and hatred, resulting in the destruction of his family.

In October of the second year of Yongzheng's reign, Nian Gengyao paid his second visit to Emperor Yongzheng in the capital. On the way to Beijing, he ordered General Fan Shijie and Governor Li Weijun of Zhili to kneel on the side of the road to welcome and bid farewell. Upon arriving in Beijing, all officials below the rank of prince who came to greet him also knelt by the roadside, while Nian Gengyao sat calmly on his horse without even looking at them. When princes and ministers dismounted to greet him, he merely nodded. Even before Emperor Yongzheng, his attitude became extremely arrogant, demanding rewards for military merit and requesting punishment for Alingga and others. This greatly hurt Yongzheng's sense of self-respect.

After returning to his post, Nian Gengyao received an imperial edict from Yongzheng, which included a discussion about how meritorious subjects should preserve their honor: "As a minister, it is easy to establish achievements, but hard to maintain these achievements; maintaining achievements is relatively easy, but remaining steadfast throughout one's life is difficult. There are many historical examples where meritorious subjects relied on their achievements to overstep boundaries, causing emperors to turn gratitude into enmity, which is common in human nature." In this red edict, Yongzheng changed his previous tone of praise and commendation, warning Nian to be cautious and self-restrained. From then on, Nian Gengyao's situation took a sharp downturn.

Two years later, under Yongzheng's hint, court deliberative ministers submitted ninety-two major crimes of Nian Gengyao to Yongzheng, requesting immediate execution according to law. Yongzheng said that among these crimes, more than thirty deserved the death penalty or immediate beheading. However, considering Nian Gengyao's remarkable contributions and widespread fame as the "General Nian" known across the nation, executing him might cause unrest among the people, and Yongzheng himself might be criticized as cruel and ruthless, killing meritorious ministers. Thus, he showed mercy by granting Nian the privilege of committing suicide in prison. All members of Nian’s father and brother's families who held official positions were dismissed, his direct descendants were sent to border regions for military service, and his properties were confiscated by the government. The once-renowned General Nian ended up disgraced, his family ruined and name destroyed.
