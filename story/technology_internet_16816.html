Celebrity footprint Bill · Review of Gates' visit to China

by diglog on 2007-04-19 02:46:03

Bill, chairman of Microsoft Corporation Mr. Gates will visit China from April 18 to April 21. This is also Bill. Gates' 10th trip to China may also be Bill. Gates made his last trip to China in 2008 before stepping down from day-to-day management of Microsoft.

In the past nine visits to China, Bill · Gates has had his joys, his gains and his failures. However, Bill Gates' footprint has always made us remember, because he is not only the richest man in the world's wealth, but also the absolute pride of the IT software industry.

Once again, let's take a look back at Bill. Gates' visit to China to bring us the shock.

The first visit to Beijing on March 18, 1994, laid the foundation for the final release of the Chinese version of Windows 95, and was called a historic one.

In 1995, in addition to meeting with government officials, I also visited the Terracotta Warriors, Dunhuang and the Three Gorges in central and western China, which was the most relaxing tour.

In late 1995, less than six months after his last visit to China, Bill Gates visited Guangdong again in China. With the end of this visit, Microsoft China has also completed its first step in China: opening up its own market and establishing sales channels.

1996 Bill · Gates visited Shanghai for the first time.

1997 Bill · Gates visited Beijing and Shanghai to give a speech at Tsinghua University. Gates once said, "I am moved by you." Microsoft Research Asia will be based in Beijing instead of India.

1999 Bill · Gates visited Shenzhen from Hong Kong, only stayed for a short six hours, but the "Venus Plan" he launched is still fresh in the memory of the industry. Project Venus was the initial expansion of the Windows platform from home computers to other end devices. Soon after, Project Venus put them in an awkward position, and Microsoft adjusted accordingly.

2001 Bill · Gates was invited to the APEC meeting, where he gave three speeches in 48 hours, signed a memorandum with the Shanghai government, and promoted Windows XP.

2003 Bill · Gates visited Beijing, Microsoft and China officially signed a government source code filing program agreement.

2004 Bill · Gates visited China for the 9th time, and after meeting with Premier Wen Jiabao of The State Council, he attended the first China International Service Industry Conference, CEO Summit, "2004 Microsoft New Technology Outlook Conference" and other activities within two days.
