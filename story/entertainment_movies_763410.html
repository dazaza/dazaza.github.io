Derek is a successful asset manager who has recently been promoted. Just as he feels content with his bright future and happy family, a temporary employee named Lisa tries every means to seduce him. As time goes on, Derek becomes increasingly entangled in an extramarital affair and finds himself unable to break free. He realizes that everything he worked hard for is now under the threat of destruction...

Derek (Idris Elba) is a successful asset manager who has just been promoted. While he feels satisfied with his promising career and harmonious family, a temporary employee named Lisa (Ali Larter) uses all sorts of tactics to seduce him. Unable to resist her fiery advances, Derek gets more and more deeply involved... However, the story soon takes a thrilling turn as Derek attempts to stop the affair. Lisa's behavior becomes increasingly erratic—she lies naked in Derek's apartment attempting suicide, breaks into his home and destroys everything... Meanwhile, Derek's wife (Beyoncé Knowles) must fight fiercely to protect her family.

In his thirties, Derek Charles (Idris Elba) has been busy lately. After working in asset management for several years, he has always been a low-level employee. Recently, he finally got a chance and successfully completed an asset management case. Derek was appreciated by his boss and received the long-awaited promotion and raise. Newly promoted, Derek quickly became the envy of both male and female colleagues in the office. His gentle and virtuous wife, Sharon (Beyoncé Knowles), also became Derek’s supportive partner. With a happy and harmonious family and a bright future ahead, Derek's life seemed perfect.

However, all of this changed because of an encounter in the elevator. Derek's company hired some new interns, and a blonde beauty, Lisa (Ali Larter), came to work temporarily in Derek's office. Derek's colleagues joked that this man-dominated place finally had someone pleasant to look at. Lisa's first contact with her new boss Derek was filled with flirtation. In the days that followed, the alluring Lisa used every method possible to seduce Derek. Unable to withstand her continuous advances, Derek's defenses crumbled, passion overcame reason, and the image of his beautiful wife disappeared instantly from his mind.

But Derek's wife Sharon (Beyoncé Knowles) wasn't so naive. Suspicious of her husband's suspicious behavior, Sharon discovered Derek's affair with Lisa. Once exposed, Derek desperately tried to save his marriage and family, but Lisa, as the third party, didn’t care about anything. She screamed, threatened suicide, and tried to make the whole incident public. Sharon, however, used her own methods to resolve the situation. Desperate and out of options, Lisa decided to go after Derek and Sharon's daughter. At this point, Sharon realized her husband couldn’t be relied upon and that yielding wouldn't make the third party back off. Instead, it only made her more aggressive. Thus, Sharon decided to confront Lisa head-on to protect her entire family...

Behind the Scenes:

From Television to the Big Screen

Director Steve Hill, who has directed TV shows for over twenty years, including popular series like "ER," "The Sopranos," and "Rome," talks about his transition to the big screen with "Obsessed." He says, “During my time shooting TV shows, I had many opportunities to direct movies, and I even acted in small roles in films. I was actively looking for suitable movie projects, but they were put on hold for various reasons. The inspiration for 'Obsessed' came from 'Knight Rider,' which was produced following a cinematic model. I gained a lot of experience from it, and after finishing 'Knight Rider,' I felt fully capable of directing a movie. So I started searching for scripts, and that's when I read David Lahari's 'Obsessed.' I thought the story was simple yet challenging for both actors and directors, so I decided to make it.”

David Lahari, the screenwriter, mentioned the origin of the story: “It started during a gathering of friends. One of my friends told us something that was troubling him—a story about being harassed by a female subordinate at his workplace. He was married and had always been honest, so we found it strange and asked how he planned to handle it. He said it was difficult because the woman was very persistent. The outcome wasn't good; my friend ended up getting divorced, though he hadn't done anything wrong with the subordinate. His wife simply couldn't accept the situation, and eventually, he resigned from the company. This story sparked my interest in the mysterious third party. What kind of power did she have to torment those around her? That's what inspired me to write a story about a cunning third party determined to achieve her goal, and that's how 'Obsessed' began.”

For director Steve Hill, making his feature film debut, the team behind 'Obsessed' felt familiar: “I know David Lahari and Ali Larter well. We often met during our previous work, so when David said he had a great script idea, I read it and immediately reached out to Ali Larter. I told her, 'You're perfect for this role, check out the script I sent you.' Not long after, she called back and said, 'Sure, when do we start filming?' Having familiar actors and a familiar production team made the movie shoot smoothly.”

Actress Ali Larter, known for her leading roles in the popular TV series 'Heroes' and the sci-fi blockbuster 'Resident Evil 3,' commented on 'Obsessed,' a movie the director believed only she could play: “It's indeed a fascinating story. I love my character in the movie, and I think she's very challenging. I'm grateful the director thought of me first when making this movie, otherwise, I would have missed out on such a great movie and role. Compared to the director's expectations, he gave me little pressure, but I put immense pressure on myself. Steve Hill hoped I could freely express myself within certain limits, but I had to constantly immerse myself in the mindset of a 'third party.' Goodness, my life is normal, so I had to hypnotize myself every night before sleeping, telling myself that I was a woman who steals other men's husbands, just to get into character."

Pop Diva Challenges Her Acting Skills

For Beyoncé Knowles, who plays the protagonist's original spouse in the movie, audiences are most familiar with her singing career. Known as a pop diva, she ventured into acting with 'Dreamgirls' in 2006. Regarding her 2009 film 'Obsessed,' Beyoncé Knowles said: “The screenplay completely captivated me. My character is a very ordinary wife who loves her husband and children. Due to the appearance of a third party, her life changes, and she must fight against the third party to protect her family. I admire such women, so I took on this movie.”

Since 'Dreamgirls' and 'The Pink Panther,' Beyoncé has shown a preference for music-themed films. This time, 'Obsessed' is a movie entirely unrelated to music. Beyoncé frankly stated: “From the beginning, the director told me that in this movie, I am just an actress, not a singer. Therefore, there won't be any of my songs in 'Obsessed.' My appearance is quite plain and realistic, looking no different from other housewives. I hope 'Obsessed' will allow viewers to see me anew on the silver screen. The story scope is small, and the budget isn't high, but it's very interesting. I hope every woman can watch this movie. The characters played by me and Ali Larter represent the two sides of a woman, serving as a warning to many women. This movie has taught me to value family more, and I hope it brings the same feeling to the audience.”

Trivia:

- This is director Steve Hill's first officially released movie. Before this, his works were mainly TV series, except for 'Knight Rider,' which was a TV movie sequel.

- Initially, 'Obsessed' was planned to be an R-rated movie, but due to market considerations, the studio rated it PG-13, resulting in significant adjustments to many erotic scenes.
