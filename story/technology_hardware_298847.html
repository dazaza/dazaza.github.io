The overall market offensive is imminent.

by 51hicsb on 2009-12-08 12:03:55

The news over the weekend was relatively quiet, causing a slightly lower opening for the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets in the morning. The index fluctuated narrowly throughout the day. At closing, the Shanghai Composite Index stood at 3,331.90 points, up 0.45%; while the Shenzhen Component Index closed at 14,051.5 points, up 1.20%. Among A-shares listed on both markets, 1,355 stocks rose and 222 fell. Trading volume shrank dramatically, with the Shanghai market dropping from RMB 263.2 billion in the previous trading day to RMB 175.7 billion; while the Shenzhen market decreased from RMB 152.2 billion to RMB 101.6 billion.

In terms of sectors and individual stocks, papermaking, food, and textile sectors led the gains, while non-ferrous metals, coal, and finance sectors were among the biggest losers. Large-cap blue-chip stocks generally underperformed small- and medium-sized cap stocks. Sinopec fell 0.16%, PetroChina dropped 0.43%, and Ping An Insurance declined 0.30%. The strengthening of the dollar dragged down commodity prices, leading to a noticeable retreat in steel and gold-related stocks. Benefiting from the economic structural adjustment, most stocks in the electronic information sector surged, with Nantian Information hitting the daily limit up. Following the conclusion of the Central Economic Work Conference, it was proposed to focus on expanding domestic demand, especially increasing resident consumption, which boosted the consumer sector. Beijing Tourism rose 5.85% and Suning Appliances climbed 3.29%. A 13% increase in Moutai's ex-factory price next year could stimulate other liquor companies to raise prices, prompting the brewing sector to perform well for a period. Kweichow Maotai gained 3.29%. In total, 21 stocks across the two markets hit the daily limit up, including notable ones like TeFa Information, Shenzhen Textile A, YAZHI Shares, Guoxing Real Estate, Zhongyuan Huadian, Orient Yuhong, Ningbo Hua Xiang. Including ST stocks, there were no limit-down stocks in the entire market.

From a news perspective, several points are worth noting: First, the Central Economic Work Conference concluded in the afternoon, proposing to maintain the continuity and stability of macroeconomic policies, continuing to implement an active fiscal policy and moderately loose monetary policy. Next year, China’s economy needs to focus on promoting the transformation of development patterns, with emphasis on expanding domestic demand to make significant progress in economic restructuring. Second, according to the latest issue of Outlook News Weekly, measures such as open market operations, raising the reserve requirement ratio or capital adequacy ratio will be used to prevent excessive bank lending; the preferential loan policy for second-home buyers will be canceled to prevent real estate bubbles; speeding up IPOs and new share issuance or raising the stamp tax will curb the formation of stock price bubbles.

Regarding future market trends, the Shanghai Composite Index today showed a small bullish line after a narrow-range consolidation, staying above the key level of 3,300 points for two consecutive days. With continued positive fundamentals, optimistic November economic data was released last weekend, alleviating concerns about liquidity. The inflow of hot money from overseas has become a direct driving force for the upward movement of the stock market. Overall, the current valuation of the stock market is reasonable. After a brief correction, market hotspots may break away from the "20-80" phenomenon, forming a good profit effect. A new upward trend in the market has formed, and the Shanghai Composite Index has the potential to break through the 3,050-3,350 point range box, challenging the high of 3,478 points seen on August 4th. According to data from the SouFun Capital Monitoring Platform, net inflows of funds into the Shanghai market today amounted to RMB 3.134 billion, with RMB 2.117 billion net inflows into the Shenzhen market. In the short term, after rebounding for two consecutive days, the Shanghai Composite Index has stabilized above 3,300 points, and the stock index is expected to rise sharply in the future. Operationally, holding stocks should be the main strategy.
