A touching story - True Love

"Lazy girl, the sun is already shining on your butt, get up quick, let me help you put on your clothes. It's a bit cold today, wear a thick sweater..."

"Girl, do you still remember? When we got married, our family was so poor that all we had was one room, there wasn't even space to put down a foot. Your parents were strongly against it, but you insisted and followed me despite their objections. Over these years, you've suffered a lot with me..."

Every day, the man wakes up at 6 o'clock. He first massages the woman, helps her move her limbs, washes her body with hot water, then dresses her. Afterward, he hastily washes his face and rushes to the market to buy fresh vegetables and fruits. Once home, he juices the vegetables and stews them with bones. Every two hours, he feeds her; every hour, he turns her over.

Every night, the man drinks a lot of water so that he wakes up frequently due to needing to urinate. Once awake, he turns her over, attends to her needs, pats her back, and lulls her to sleep... The man is very meticulous in what he does. As he works, he sings some old songs or whispers sweet nothings. During his free time, the man sits by the bed, sometimes reading news from the newspaper, sometimes playing a piece on the erhu (a traditional Chinese string instrument). His erhu music is very tender, full of passion. If you listen carefully, it feels as if you can see butterflies dancing gracefully among flowers.

Ten years ago, the man was rough and fiery, often shouting at the woman for no reason, refusing to wash even a single sock. When the woman served him dinner, he would be out in the alleyway playing chess with a group of old men, completely absorbed in the game. If the food was bland, he'd taste it, grab a handful of salt and toss it into the pot, ruining the entire meal and forcing the woman to start again. Occasionally, when the woman visited neighbors, the man would return and shout her name loudly across the street with his deep, coarse voice.

The woman suddenly fell ill, suffering from high blood pressure and cerebral infarction. Although she was saved after emergency treatment, she became a vegetative state patient. Lying in bed, she didn't speak, her eyes vacant.

The man's eyes also looked vacant. He felt out of place without her; when he couldn't find his socks, he instinctively called out her name, only to see her lying in bed, staring blankly at him. Taking a bite of food, it tasted unbearably salty, and when he slammed his chopsticks on the table, he saw her staring blankly at the ceiling, expressionless. Not knowing which button to press on the washing machine, he lost focus, causing water to overflow everywhere...

The man's heart shattered bit by bit. The doctor said: "Your wife in this condition may last a month or two, or half a year to a year. She doesn't have much time left, take good care of her."

The man stared at the haggard woman with disheveled hair lying before him. This was the person he loved most, yet he had never once pampered her or spoken a single warm word of love... He held her head in his arms, gently rubbing her cheek with his chin, tears streaming down her face in large drops.

He went to his workplace and arranged for an early retirement, dedicating himself entirely to caring for her. This rough man suddenly became meticulous, feeding her water and food, washing and massaging her, handling her waste, and even whispering cheesy love words. Even though usually he was talking to himself, he believed that the woman in the bed could understand.

This kind of life continued for ten years. The man spoke those tender love words for ten years. During these ten years, the man once fell seriously ill due to overwork. Sitting by the woman's bedside, he repeatedly asked: "Girl, what will you do if I'm not alright?" As he spoke, tears streamed down his face.

After recovering, the man still sat by the woman's bedside, combing her hair while saying: "I knew I would go after you." The man's tone was somewhat proud. His face smiled like a chrysanthemum, but tears flowed from the chrysanthemum, each drop clear and bright.

That day was Valentine's Day, and in the small house of the man's home, I saw this couple. The woman lay in bed, her complexion rosy, her eyes gazing at the gray-haired man sitting by her bedside. The gray-haired man was feeding her with a syringe. Their gazes intertwined, filled with deep affection. Only then did I realize, this is true love.
