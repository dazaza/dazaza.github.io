Tencent: The court is very fair, 360 has already "lost ten times in a row"

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 13:38:02

This afternoon, the Guangdong Provincial Higher People’s Court publicly announced its verdict in the case of Tencent suing Qihu 360 for unfair competition. The court's first-instance ruling determined that Qihu 360 was guilty of unfair competition. The verdict ordered 360 to compensate Tencent with 5 million yuan and to publish an apology on its homepage and other media in a prominent position continuously for 15 days.

In response to this ruling, 360 expressed dissatisfaction, stating that the Guangdong Higher People's Court had issues with unclear fact-finding and erroneous application of law while separately hearing the anti-monopoly case and the "Kou Kou Baojia" case involving 360. They claimed there was a clear tendency towards local protectionism. As a result, 360 has decided to appeal to the Supreme People's Court.

Tencent subsequently issued a public statement, considering the court's ruling to be fair. They pointed out that so far, 360 has suffered ten consecutive defeats, not only in Guangdong but also in other places like Beijing where they ended up losing lawsuits. Therefore, 360's claim that the Guangdong Higher People's Court exhibited local protectionism is completely baseless.

The full text of Tencent's statement is as follows:

We thank the court for its fair judgment. We have always believed that the law would give us a just outcome.

We note that the court emphasized in its ruling that 360 violated the principles of honesty, integrity, and equal competition, engaging in obvious malicious unfair competition, which was the fundamental cause of the 3Q War, severely disrupting the normal order of internet operations.

We believe the significance of this ruling lies in two aspects:

Firstly, the court made a relatively detailed delineation of the rules of competition in the internet industry and the functional authority of security software, defining the boundaries of competition in the internet industry and establishing standards for fair and unfair competition.

Secondly, this ruling conveys a belief, encouraging and protecting enterprises' legal operations, encouraging enterprises to gain user and market recognition through their own accumulation and efforts, and imposing severe punishment on acts of unfair competition.

The court's announcement and punishment of these acts of unfair competition clearly define the commercial rules and bottom lines of the internet industry, promoting the development of China's internet towards fair, transparent, healthy, and orderly business and industry rules.

We note that 360 has suffered ten consecutive defeats so far, not only in Guangdong but also in various places like Beijing, where they lost lawsuits against multiple companies in local courts. We believe the court's judgment is fair and impartial. If the opposing party chooses to appeal, we will respect their legal rights, and we firmly believe that the law will make a fair judgment.
