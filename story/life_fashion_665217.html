1. Eye Sunscreen Prevents Melanin Formation

The skin around the eyes is the thinnest and most fragile part of the body. Its thickness is only about 1/2 of the facial skin thickness, and it lacks sweat glands and sebaceous glands, so its moisture and self-protection ability are very low. External factors that induce aging of the skin around the eyes, 90% come from ultraviolet rays. Ultraviolet rays can make our skin around the eyes become dull and lifeless in a short time, and also accelerate the formation of melanin, forming permanent dark circles.

In the general person's concept of maintenance, eye maintenance is no more than the prevention and repair of dark circles, eye bags, fine lines, and wrinkles. However, if you ignore the fact that ultraviolet rays are the real culprit of aging and do not do a good job of sun protection for the skin around the eyes, then it can only be considered as doing half of the eye maintenance. Even in autumn and winter, you need to pay attention to sun protection for the eyes. It is recommended to have a sunscreen eye cream on hand. Try to consistently do sun protection for your eyes for a week, and your dark circles will significantly improve.

2. Thorough Eye Makeup Removal

Cosmetics usually contain pigment particles. Long-term use of makeup may lead to deep pigment particles penetrating into the eyelids, eventually forming dark circles over time. The correct way to remove makeup is the first step in maintenance. First, use an eye makeup-specific remover to clean off the makeup. When removing eye makeup, gently use your middle and ring fingers to start from the upper eye socket, then slide down along the upper eyelid to the lower eyelid, and finally return to the upper eye socket.

Even if you've only applied a layer of concealer, you should use a dedicated eye makeup remover to clean the area around the eyes. Dust in the air, computer radiation, sunscreen isolation creams, etc., left on the eyes can cause significant harm to the skin. Incomplete cleaning and incomplete absorption of eye care products can possibly form fat granules and dark circles.
