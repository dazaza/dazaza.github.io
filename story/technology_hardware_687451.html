F.S & PAISTE Suits' Perspective: "Where Does the Charm Come From in Men's Clothing?"

I once asked an expert, if someone's financial condition only allows them to buy one suit and one tie, what color should they choose? He said the first suit should be navy blue, and the tie should be red; if they buy another set, dark gray or black-gray would be good.

He does not recommend buying olive green or light coffee brown, as these colors make Chinese people's faces look more yellow. However, a very deep coffee brown suit is acceptable. In general, darker suits are better, as they do not get dirty easily. If it's summer, you can wear lighter-colored suits, such as off-white suits, but they get dirty easily, so you must always pay attention to keeping them clean. Custom-made suits should have relatively better fabric, which can maintain their color for a longer period of time. If your suit generally has a subtle stripe pattern, it looks quite generous and often stays in fashion; or if it has patterns or large checks, follow the rule of two solid colors and one pattern, making your shirt and tie solid colors the best option, or with very subtle patterns. This is the simplest principle, remember it well, and it will make it easy for you to match appropriately.

Suits are generally divided into three types:

The first type is a matching suit, where the material of the suit jacket and pants is uniform, looking relatively formal.

The second type is a sports jacket suit, where the material of the sports jacket and pants does not need to be uniform. A sports jacket can also be paired with jeans, although it is less formal, it is more casual.

The third type is a three-piece matching suit, which is also relatively formal.

Suits come in many styles, but there are only two types of shoulder styles: those that naturally slope down according to a person's shoulders or straight shoulders, the latter being very stiff, similar to military uniforms. These two shoulder styles do not have a comparison of which is better than the other, but rather should be chosen based on each individual's body shape. Most Asians have relatively sloped shoulders, so if your shoulders are sloped, I suggest you go for a slightly straighter shoulder style, which can make you appear taller and straighter, but not excessively large.

Suit lapels vary in width, some are narrower, some are wider. Different designers have their own styles, and trends will continue to change, but if you buy lapels of moderate width, they will never go out of style.
