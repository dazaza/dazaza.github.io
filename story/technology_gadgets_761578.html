Surface sales not meeting expectations: Only 900,000 units sold in Q1

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:06:25

Recently, Microsoft has not released the actual sales data of the Surface tablet, but several market research agencies have already published their statistical results, which vary. Among them, Strategy Analytics claims that in the first quarter of 2013, Microsoft sold a total of 3 million Windows tablets, while Bloomberg states that since its release in October 2012, Microsoft has sold a total of 1.5 million Surface tablets: 1.1 million Surface RTs and 400,000 Surface Pros.IDC has just released its latest report, showing that the total sales volume of Microsoft's Surface tablets in Q1 was 900,000 units (most of which were Surface Pros), allowing Microsoft to rank among the top five global tablet manufacturers, following Apple, Samsung, Asus, and Amazon. However, the sales volume of Microsoft's Surface tablets is not optimistic, accounting for only 1.8% of total sales, compared to Apple and Samsung's combined 58%. In comparison, Microsoft's Surface sales are negligible.The IDC data shows that Apple's iPad sales totaled 19.5 million units this quarter, reclaiming the title of sales champion for the first quarter, with a year-over-year increase of 63%; Samsung made great strides, with shipments exceeding 8.8 million units, growing by 283% compared to last year's 2.3 million units. The data provided by IDC implies that Microsoft sells about 300,000 tablets per month (10,000 per day), while Apple sells approximately 6.5 million tablets per month (216,000 per day). It should be noted, however, that the iPad is already an established brand, while Surface is still in its development phase.From the perspective of operating systems, Windows RT accounts for only 0.4% of total sales, but the overall sales volume of Windows tablets reached 3%, increasing by 700% compared to the same period last year. Android has surpassed iOS for the first time this quarter to become the sales champion (sales figures being: 27.8 million Android tablets, 19.5 million iOS tablets).
