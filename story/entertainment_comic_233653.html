Annual Review of Hong Kong Companies

After the registration of a Hong Kong company is completed, its subsequent management is relatively simple. A company established in Hong Kong must file an annual return with the Companies Registry and the Inland Revenue Department within one year from the day after its anniversary date every year, and renew its Business Registration Certificate. This fee fluctuates according to adjustments made by the government (equivalent to the annual inspection of domestic companies in China). Specifically:

I. The following information is required for the annual review of Hong Kong companies:

Existing clients:

(1) Personally signed confirmation letter

(2) Personally signed annual return documents

New clients:

(1) Copy of the Business Registration Certificate;

(2) Copy of the Certificate of Incorporation;

(3) One copy of the company's articles of association;

(4) Complete set of statutory documents for the establishment of the company (including forms D1, D3, R1);

(5) Copies of identity proof documents of shareholders or directors (as submitted to the government for record-keeping purposes)

(6) Copies of relevant documents regarding share changes, capital increase, or name changes (if no such matters are involved, this item can be ignored)

(7) Personally signed confirmation letter

(8) Personally signed annual return documents

II. Specific matters involved in the annual review of Hong Kong companies:

1. Annual review by the Inland Revenue Department: Renewal of the Business Registration Certificate. Every year starting from the day after the company’s anniversary date, a new Business Registration Certificate must be obtained from the Inland Revenue Department. Expired certificates do not need to be returned; please keep them.

2. Annual return filing with the Companies Registry: Renewal of the Certificate of Incorporation. Based on the company's previous year's situation, a set of annual return documents must be signed by the director personally and then submitted to the Companies Registry for the annual return, once a year.

3. Statutory Secretary: A necessary condition for establishing a company in Hong Kong. If the client cannot provide it, our company can offer it on their behalf.

4. Registered Address: A necessary condition for establishing a company in Hong Kong. If the client cannot provide it, our company can offer it on their behalf.

5. Dennt provides professional services: Full cooperation and assistance in handling.

III. Content of the finished product of the Hong Kong company's annual review:

1. Original Business Registration Certificate issued by the Inland Revenue Department;

2. Original statutory documents of the annual return form;

IV. Penalty details for overdue annual review of Hong Kong companies:

Companies Registry:

1. If paid within 42 days from the day after the anniversary of incorporation, the payable fee is: ¥105

If more than 42 days overdue, the penalties are as follows:

1. If more than 42 days but not exceeding 3 months from the day after the anniversary of incorporation……… …¥870

2. If more than 3 months but not exceeding 6 months from the day after the anniversary of incorporation………¥1,740

3. If more than 6 months but not exceeding 9 months from the day after the anniversary of incorporation………¥2,610

4. If more than 9 months from the day after the anniversary of incorporation……………………………………¥3,480

Inland Revenue Department

Penalty for overdue payment throughout the year……………………………………………………¥300

Overseas Company Annual Review

1. BVI Company Annual Review

The renewal period after registering a company is divided into two phases each year, specifically:

First phase: Every May, companies registered from January to June must submit renewal before May 1st each year;

Second phase: Every November, companies registered from July to December must submit renewal before November 1st each year.

For example:

(1) Company A was registered on April 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before May 1, 2006;

(2) Company B was registered on December 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before November 1, 2006;

2. SAMOA Company Annual Review

Renewal time after registering a company is every November.

For example:

(1) Company C was registered on January 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before November 1, 2006;

(2) Company D was registered on December 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before November 1, 2006;

3. American Company Annual Review

Renewal time after registering a company is every February.

For example:

(1) Company E was registered on January 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before March 1, 2006;

(2) Company F was registered on December 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before March 1, 2006;

4. British Company Annual Review

Renewal time after registering a company is one month before the anniversary of the registration date.

For example:

(1) Company H was registered on January 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before December 31, 2005;

(2) Company I was registered on December 1, 2005, and needs to submit renewal before November 30, 2006;
