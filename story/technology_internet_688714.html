10 Theorems for Surviving in the Workplace

by liwuwang2012 on 2012-03-03 13:49:39

1. Opportunities never [get lost]. If you lose them, others will get them. Don't always rely on fate and wait idly for opportunities like a rabbit waiting under a tree. Opportunities are meant for those who are well-prepared and good at creating them. Perhaps, when you are regretting and complaining about losing an opportunity, the opportunity is being seized by that "unlucky guy" right in front of you. If there are no opportunities, create them; if there are opportunities, seize them skillfully.

2. If you don't like your current job, either quit or keep quiet. When you're just starting out, you often aim high but lack the skills to match. You can't handle big tasks and refuse to do small ones. Don't develop the habit of being picky and showing dissatisfaction everywhere. Remember, if you're going to do something, do it well.

3. Everyone has times of loneliness. Learn to endure solitude so that you can mature. Don't be restless, learn to calm down, and don't do meaningless things because of loneliness, wasting precious time.

4. When things are going well, prepare for bad luck. Retreat routes are also important. Carry dry rations even when full, and bring an umbrella even on a sunny day. Accumulate bit by bit, and success will come naturally. Some things may seem worthless today, but one day they might become invaluable.

5. Don't be as fragile as glass. Some people always focus on themselves, so they cannot grow tall or see far, always blaming heaven and earth. Without hardship, how can you have sweetness? Since you've opened your eyes to enjoy the coolness of the wind, don't complain about the tiny grains of sand in the wind.

6. Control your mouth. Don't talk about yourself, and don't gossip about others. Talking about yourself often leads to arrogance and hypocrisy, losing yourself in unrealistic claims. Gossiping about others often gets you entangled in trivial disputes. Criticizing others behind their backs lowers your character.

7. Write down what you want to do in your life, put the list in your wallet, and take it out frequently to review. Life should have goals, plans, reminders, and a sense of urgency. A series of small goals strung together becomes your lifelong big goal.

8. If your phone doesn't ring much, you should make calls. Often, a phone call can bring unexpected rewards. One key to socializing is being proactive. Good relationships and reputations often help your career reach new heights.

9. Never marry hastily just because you've reached marriage age. If you want to get married, find a partner who can resonate with you and support each other. Don't fall in love due to indulgence or games, and don't let romance affect your work and career. A hasty and failed marriage can hinder your life. Acting on emotions often leads to losing sight of the bigger picture.

10. Prepare an alarm clock to wake you up on time. Sleeping in and being late will become stumbling blocks in your work and career. Not only should you learn to be punctual, but you should also learn to be ahead of schedule. The [alarm clock] is just a simple sign and reminder. Truly flexible and practical time management lies in each person's heart.
