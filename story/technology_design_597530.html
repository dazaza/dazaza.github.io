Distributing options to employees does not mean the company will go public immediately.

by beetgk2958 on 2011-12-01 11:04:25

After the first battle of domestic group buying website Lashou.com's listing in the United States failed, the fate of other domestic group buying websites has become a focus of attention. According to an insider from Meituan.com who spoke with NetEase Technology, Meituan.com recently began implementing an employee stock ownership plan.

An internal email sent by Meituan.com stated that the company had started implementing the previously announced stock ownership plan for all employees. The internal email from Meituan.com stipulated that formal employees who signed labor contracts before November 1, 2011, and have been employed for at least six months are eligible for the first phase of stock options distribution. It is reported that this round of stock options distribution amounts to 500 shares per person, with each share having an exercise price of 0.96 US dollars.

Meituan.com introduced that the distributed stock options will be exercised over four years, with one quarter being exercised after completing one year of service. Starting from the second year, one sixteenth will be exercised every quarter until fully exercised after four years.

In this email, Meituan.com particularly pointed out that distributing stock options to employees does not mean the company will immediately go public. Last month, when interviewed by NetEase Technology, Meituan CEO Wang Xing expressed that going public is not Meituan's ultimate goal but did not disclose whether there were any preparations for going public.

Meituan.com received its first round of 20 million US dollars in financing from Sequoia Capital last October and obtained 50 million US dollars led by Alibaba in July this year. The B-round investment also saw participation from Northern Light Venture Capital, Walden International, and the initial round investor Sequoia Capital.

According to the latest data from group buying aggregator site Tuan800, Meituan.com ranked first in October sales with 173.8 million yuan, closely followed by WoWo Group and Lashou.com with 157 million yuan and 152 million yuan respectively. Lashou.com submitted a prospectus last month intending to list on NASDAQ, but due to allegations of financial fraud and other issues, the listing plan was eventually shelved.
