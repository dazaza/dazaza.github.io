I. Daily maintenance: 1. Turn the doormat over and shake off the sand and dirt. For a through-bottom type of doormat, there is no need to turn it over; simply shake off the sand. 2. Use a vacuum cleaner to suck away dust from the surface and fiber gaps of the doormat. 3. Clean and wipe the floor clean. 4. Rearrange the doormat.

II. Cleaning and renewal: 1. Shake off the dust on the doormat as in daily maintenance. 2. Rinse with a high-pressure water hose, preferably using water below 40°C for rinsing. You can appropriately use some neutral detergents, but avoid acidic or alkaline cleaning agents because they will accelerate the aging of the doormat. 3. Dry in the sun and keep dry. 4. Rearrange the doormat.

III. Special stain treatment methods for carpet cleaning:

1. Edible oil stains: Remove with gasoline or chlorinated carbon volatile solvents, then clean the remaining part with alcohol.

2. Soy sauce stains: Brush first with cold water, then use detergent to remove. For old stains, soak in warm water with detergent and ammonia, then wash clean.

3. Shoe polish stains: Wipe with gasoline or alcohol, then wash clean with soap.

4. Urine stains: New stains can be brushed off with warm water or a 10% ammonia solution. For old stains, wash with detergent, then clean with ammonia. For pure wool carpets, use citric acid for washing.

5. Juice stains: First clean with a 5% ammonia solution, then wash again with a neutral detergent. Ammonia has a damaging effect on pure wool carpet fibers, so its use should be minimized. Generally, lemon acid or soap can be used for cleaning, or alcohol can also be used.

6. Ice cream stains: Wipe with gasoline.

7. Wine stains: New stains can be cleaned with water. Old stains require an ammonia and borax water solution to clear. For wool silk material carpets, oxalic acid can be used for cleaning.

8. Coffee and tea stains: Can be removed with ammonia. Silk and wool carpets can be soaked in a neutral detergent for 10-20 minutes before washing, or cleaned with a 10% glycerin solution.

9. Vomit stains: One method is to wipe with gasoline, then brush with a 5% ammonia solution, and finally wash with warm water. Another method is to wet the vomit with a 10% ammonia solution, then wipe with alcohol-soap solution, and finally wash clean with a neutral detergent.

Carpet cleaning standards and operations:

Including various leather, pure wool, synthetic fiber carpets, wall hangings, bed blankets, decorative blankets, etc. Carpet treatment uses steam saunas, multi-functional cleaning machines, vacuum cleaners, combined with dedicated disinfection and stain removal agents to steam kill mites, bacteria, foam clean and remove stains, vacuum and water machine absorb sewage, air dry, comb, and finally handle with anti-stain spray agent to form a waterproof film on the carpet surface, achieving a waterproof and dustproof effect.

Tools and materials used:

I. Water extraction carpet cleaning (suitable for synthetic fiber carpets)

Equipment used: Carpet brush, sprayer, water extractor, carpet washer.

Agent used: Carpet shampoo.

Operation method:

1. Use a vacuum cleaner for comprehensive dust removal

2. Dilute the cleaning agent, which can also be injected into the water tank

3. Spray the cleaning agent all over the carpet

4. After 10-15 minutes, the stains detach from the fibers

5. Extract and wash with a carpet washer. Move backward while operating so that each operation row overlaps partially, requiring at least two extractions.

6. While cleaning the carpet, use a water extractor to extract the already washed carpet

7. Let the carpet completely dry. To speed up drying, you can turn on the carpet dryer.

II. Dry foam carpet cleaning (suitable for pure wool carpets)

Equipment and tools used: Single-disc scrubber with carpet brush and foamer, carpet comb or rake, vacuum cleaner.

Agent used: High-foam carpet cleaner.

Operation method:

1. Use a vacuum cleaner for comprehensive dust removal

2. Local treatment means using dedicated cleaning agents to separately treat oil stains, fruit stains, coffee stains on the carpet

3. Dilute the carpet foam cleaner and inject it into the foaming box

4. Handle the edges, corners, and areas where the machine cannot reach with a hand brush

5. Use a single-disc sweeper equipped with a foamer and carpet brush to brush the carpet with dry foam

6. Act for a while, then repeat

7. Comb the carpet fibers with a carpet comb or rake. This is very important for the appearance of the carpet, especially for longer-fibered cotton velvet carpets, and also accelerates the drying of the carpet

8. Let the carpet fibers completely dry

9. Use a vacuum cleaner to remove dirt and dry foam crystals.
