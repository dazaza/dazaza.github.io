Fertilizer Tubes:

High-pressure seamless tubes used in fertilizer equipment (GB6479-2006 fertilizer tube) are suitable for chemical equipment and pipelines operating at temperatures from -40~400℃ and pressures from 10~30Ma. These are made of high-quality carbon structural steel and alloy steel seamless tubes.

1. High-quality carbon structural steel (GB/T699-1999)

Steel contains carbon (C) elements and silicon (Si) (usually no more than 0.40%) and manganese (Mn) (usually no more than 0.80%, up to 1.20% maximum) alloy elements for deoxidation, without other alloy elements (excluding residual elements).

Such steels must ensure both chemical composition and mechanical properties. Their sulfur (S) and phosphorus (P) impurity element content is generally controlled below 0.035%. If controlled below 0.030%, it's called advanced quality steel, and its grade should be followed by "A", such as 20A; if P is controlled below 0.025% and S is controlled below 0.020%, it's called super-grade quality steel, and its grade should be followed by "E". For other residual alloy elements brought into the steel by raw materials, such as chromium (Cr), nickel (Ni), copper (Cu), etc., their content is generally controlled within Cr≤0.25%, Ni≤0.30%, Cu≤0.25%. Some grades have manganese (Mn) content reaching 1.40%, known as manganese steel.
