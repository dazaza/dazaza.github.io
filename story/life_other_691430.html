Many friends who rent cars often end up paying not just the rent but also some extra money. So, how can one rent a car without losing money?

OrderingCar.com reminds: renting a car must involve choosing a legal merchant. Check if they have a business license and a fixed place of operation. Details of the car rental contract cannot be overlooked; it should specify commitments regarding the condition of the vehicle, claims for vehicle damage, and how over-time rentals are charged. Additionally, don't forget to test drive the car before picking it up to determine its general condition. Consumers should also check for scratches on the body of the car, whether the lights are intact, and if the locks are functioning properly.

Moreover, car insurance needs to be thoroughly understood. In general, rental companies will purchase corresponding insurance products based on their own risk requirements. However, some rental companies lie about having purchased insurance in order to increase profits, when in reality they haven't.
