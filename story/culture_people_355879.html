A water pipe broke north of Jingguang Bridge on the east third ring road of Beijing, and water flooded the auxiliary road of the third ring road.

by jnlagua on 2010-05-26 10:31:41

At around 4:08 PM yesterday afternoon, on the auxiliary road of Jingguang Bridge on the third ring road in Beijing, a main water supply pipe burst and started leaking water, with a water jet reaching over 4 meters high. According to informed sources, the pipe rupture is suspected to be related to the previous construction of the Line 10 subway, where the constructed vertical shaft exerted pressure on the pipeline.

A main water pipe with a diameter of 600 millimeters burst, with the water jet reaching over 4 meters high. Due to the high water pressure, it took the staff nearly two hours to stop the water flow. As a result, about 500 households in the surrounding area were left without water. In addition, after the accident occurred, relevant sections of the road were closed. Because it was during the evening rush hour, parts of the eastern third ring road experienced traffic congestion.

At around 5:40 PM, rescue personnel shut off the valve, and the water stopped flowing out. However, the accumulated water on Chaowai Street was gradually receding but still remained about 10 centimeters deep. The incident affected water supply for residents on two streets. Around 6 PM, the main road of the eastern third ring road resumed smooth traffic, and the east-west direction of Chaoyangmenwai Street gradually returned to normal, although vehicles were moving very slowly. The cordon line for the north-to-south direction had already been prepared for removal. The station manager of a section of Subway Line 10 stated that the subway was currently unaffected.
