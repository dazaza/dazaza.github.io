1. In my opinion, the most crucial part of opening a Taobao store is having good sources of goods. Good sources include many factors: price advantages, timely updates of styles, distribution of product categories, brand agency rights, cash turnover, etc. My family has been doing children's shoes business in Northern Jiangsu for more than ten years, and our physical store is the largest in Northern Jiangsu. I believe that the combination of physical and online stores is the best model. Because physical stores themselves have fast style updates, and the purchase prices are directly from the manufacturers. I have first-level agency rights for dozens of well-known brands. Online stores don't have cash turnover issues, styles are updated in time, and quality can be controlled. Therefore, in the competition among Taobao sellers, we can highlight our advantages and allow buyers to enjoy more benefits. Then many people will ask if you can still do as well without this physical store, and I answer yes. If I didn't have this physical store, I could still do well. Why? Because I have a good strategy. I suggest new sellers who want to open a store find a relatively large retailer locally and negotiate with them. This is more reliable than sourcing goods online. You can take good pictures of special products in the physical store, then negotiate with the store to take goods at cost price, and split the online sales profit 50-50. On this point, I think all merchants would accept it. After all, every extra piece of goods sold means more profit. And in the early stages of opening an online store, it can avoid many risks, there's no pressure of capital, styles can be updated in time, and there's no inventory issue. Whatever type of goods you sell can be operated this way. Of course, if you can source goods directly from the manufacturer or get foreign orders, that's another matter. I believe most people, like me, don't have such connections in this aspect. So quality and style are the top priorities.

2. With good sources of goods, the next step is taking pictures. Taking pictures is very important. We know that the internet presents things in a "what you see is what you get" manner. Pictures are the most direct way to express items and directly affect the buyer's psychology. I've spent quite a bit of thought on this. Previously, I saw some sellers often posting threads about their shooting experiences or photography techniques, including softboxes and other stuff, but actually none of that is necessary. The child shoe pictures I take are pretty decent. We're not photographers, so there's no need to buy expensive photography equipment, nor to master advanced photography skills and techniques. I think taking good pictures boils down to a few points.

Firstly, consider the use of light. This is a crucial point. No one wants their pictures to come out dim or overexposed and completely white. Moreover, they should truly reflect the actual color of the item. I suggest buyers make a small box themselves using wooden boards. Install two high-brightness energy-saving lamps on both sides of the box, preferably long ones. Then install two 40W spiral energy-saving bulbs on the top of the box. This is to avoid bright and dark spots caused by crossed light beams. Cover the inside of the box with white paper. Since paper reflects light, it's best to cover it with a layer of white silk cloth. Silk absorbs light the most, making the light inside the box soft and non-reflective. Due to its whiteness, the bottom of the item doesn't need any processing when photographed, as the background color is almost transparent under the light. This saves a lot of time for post-production PS work. I also suggest using a flash when taking pictures. Inside such a lightbox, the flash is neutralized by the light, resulting in minimal reflection points. Instead, it can truly restore the colors of the item. You can try it.

Secondly, pay attention to details when shooting items. Handle places with fingerprints before shooting. Make sure everything is clean. If it's clothing, trim the loose threads first. Iron clothes with wrinkles before shooting. Show the best side of the item. Be meticulous and patient. For jewelry items, place a piece of glass before shooting, then put a piece of dark velvet cloth underneath the glass. This makes the item appear more luxurious. The placement of items is also important. Try to give it a sense of movement; too static pictures may feel lifeless. Sometimes random placements can highlight effects better.

Thirdly, post-production. Many people like to use Photoshop (PS), which is certainly good, but most people don't know how to use PS. Neither do I. PS can make pictures look better, but it requires too many skills and is too complicated. Generally, online store owners don't have much time to process pictures because products should meet buyers as soon as possible. I use a small tool called Photo Wizard, which you can search for. Even if the preparation is done well, the photos may still have problems because different colored items reflect light differently. So we need to use this software to handle it. It's simple: first check if the item is too white or too dark, then adjust the brightness or darkness accordingly. Then choose either true color or vibrant color in the reversal film option. If these two options don't satisfy you, adjust the curve and brightness/contrast in the adjustment options. The effect will be surprisingly good. You can check out my child shoe pictures. Finally, crop and resize the pictures. Since the bottom is white, there's no need for PS editing. Resize to around 500x500 pixels. This is the area, and you can adjust according to your needs. The processed pictures generally weigh around 60K. If you have an online photo album, it saves space as well.

3. Now it seems we should talk about service aspects. Actually, running a store inevitably involves various issues, and this can't be blamed on buyers because they buy based on the pictures and cannot see the actual items, so discrepancies are normal. Or sometimes you encounter picky buyers with bad attitudes, which is also normal. So how do we solve these issues? Well, this is a kind of art, usually called crisis public relations. Service determines details, and details determine the sustainable development of an online store. Yeah, it sounds like big talk, but big talk is still talk. Some people say, just being polite is enough? Actually, that's far from enough. A few days ago, I bought something online and encountered a seller who kept saying "dear" and sending kisses and flowers, repeatedly. It made me uncomfortable, feeling like she was relying on looks to sell. I reminded her, but she said her boss required this, and she was already a three-diamond seller. But is this good? The most important thing about service is sincerity, realism, helping the buyer's mindset, treating them as friends, and being patient like family. Although making money is the top priority, online transactions are more about friendship, trust, and support. Right? So grasp the balance; too much or too little is not good. Let me tell you two small stories that happened to me.

The first incident involved selling a pair of children's shoes to Wuxi for 28 yuan. The next day, the buyer asked for a refund of 5 yuan. At the time, I was just starting my store, and I was momentarily stunned. I immediately called to ask why, and she said there were two extra stitches in one place on the shoe. Actually, 28-yuan shoes aren't branded shoes, and the quality is average with variations in stitching. She had to rush to a meeting and hung up. An hour and forty minutes later, I called back and sincerely explained the reason to her, telling her that extra stitches wouldn't affect the quality, and shoes at this price level have less detailed handling compared to branded shoes. I told her I could exchange the same pair, with each party covering half the shipping cost, or I could refund 5 yuan. She laughed and said it wasn't necessary, that 5 yuan wasn't worth mentioning, and she didn't request a refund. I promised her free shipping for her next purchase. This conversation cost me over ten yuan in phone bills.

The second incident involved a younger sister from Hunan who bought six pairs of shoes from me. At the time, I was just starting out and didn't understand much, so I thought 20 yuan courier fee would suffice. I charged her 10 yuan. The next day, when I went to the courier company to send the package, they said her location couldn't be reached by regular courier, only EMS. I was stunned for a minute, then made a decision. I used EMS, and I paid the 50 yuan courier fee myself. I lost 30 yuan. And I didn't mention the shipping fee to the buyer. My seller friends knew and said I was a bit foolish because I could have refunded her or asked her to add shipping fees, there was no need to lose money. But I didn't do that. When the buyer received the goods, she asked me why I used EMS. I told her the truth, and she was deeply moved. She added a few more purchases without haggling.

From these two small incidents, I can tell everyone that in doing business, I always pursue "big" profits, but this "big" doesn't mean excessive profits; it means broad. Doing business isn't a short-term thing, it's a lifelong endeavor. Each online buyer represents a group that can bring countless others. We need to grab every buyer, let them help us promote, become friends with them, and gain their trust through sincerity. Online transactions are based on trust. Although I lost money temporarily, I gained reputation, trust, and market share. The internet is boundless, so our business is boundless too. Isn't there a saying that business should be as vast as the world? Sellers should have this mindset: earning one person's money is not as good as earning ten, a hundred, or a thousand people's money.

4. Regarding shipping, this can show the dedication of a shop owner. When shipping, be sure to check carefully, fill out the courier form accurately, and write neatly to give the buyer a good impression. Isn't it said that handwriting reflects a person? Mine is pretty good, haha. Before shipping, check thoroughly, even more meticulously than buying things for yourself. Handle loose threads and such, avoiding potential future issues. Placing a small card or putting effort into packaging is better, though generally well-performing stores don't have time for that since they're too busy. Just keep it simple and clean.

5. The four points mentioned above basically summarize the most important factors of a good store. Each point can extend into many more. I haven't elaborated much because it's unnecessary. If you're serious about doing well in online stores, you must learn to think and apply knowledge broadly. Regarding store names, categories, window recommendations, and fixed-price settings, I suggest sellers check out posts on Experience Zone, which are all well-written.

How to Face Competition

The internet is shared, and Taobao has established a series of rules for sellers. These rules constrain certain behaviors but also reveal loopholes that some people exploit. Large sellers' suppression of small sellers, the influence of positive reviews and credibility, price wars, all these determine a bumpy road for new sellers. So what to do? I sell children's shoes, and since there are no costs online, I set low prices. When I have time, I search for similar children's shoe stores, learning from their experiences and applying them to my own. This is a good method because those experiences are proven effective. Once, I encountered a seller whose same style of shoes was priced one yuan less than mine. The next day, I adjusted my prices, lowering all prices by two yuan across the board. My shipping fees are based on actual costs, with no added profit. This way, new stores can be more attractive and avoid direct confrontation. Competition is multifaceted. We don't have to compete on the same level; we can compete on price, service, quality, or credibility. Any one of these well-applied can bring popularity. Remember Mr. Ho once said, "Business must first have popularity to have prosperity." This is especially important for online stores. Facing large sellers' popularity, credibility, and price pressures, what to do? The only way is to find your own advantages, persist, and keep persisting. Persistence is a kind of waiting, waiting for opportunities brought by the opponent's negligence. Large sellers, due to high volume of sales, are prone to errors and cannot be as sincere as new sellers, giving you opportunities. In the same situation, the same price, service determines the choice. So sometimes when there's no solution, persistence is key—play to your strengths and let opponents give you opportunities. Pay attention to forming your store's unique style. Having a style creates differences and defines the customer base range. I won't elaborate further, as those who think critically will succeed.

The rapid development of the internet has brought online entrepreneurship and network sales into a golden era. Sales formats on the internet are constantly emerging and innovating. One day, a business friend saw my blog and contacted me, asking if I could act as an agent for my products, without handling shipments. That moment, the concept of "zero-risk online store agency" formed in my mind. If you lack capital and good projects but have ample time for online entrepreneurship, recognize the true value of a product, and have suitable marketing methods, you can fully adopt the "zero-risk online store agency" business model. Such a way can make your life more fulfilling and lead you to carve out your own niche.

Through a period of study and practice in forums and my own insights, I have compiled some basic marketing strategies for online store agencies. I hope friends will participate and share their views to form a more complete marketing strategy. Everyone learns and improves together.

1. Investigate the Goods Source and Recognize the Product

The goods source is already available, provided by suppliers. The key is to investigate whether the source of the goods is reliable. Usually, suppliers have company websites where you can carefully observe and research the website construction and reliability. This generally allows you to judge the situation. Conduct market research to assess the demand for similar products and have a clear understanding. Once the product is determined, the most critical factor is whether you recognize it. Fully recognizing and approving the chosen product gives you the confidence and courage to introduce it to the market and enables long-term development possibilities.

2. Agency Fee (or Franchise Fee) Issues

1) "Zero-Risk" Online Store Agency: No capital is required. You directly use an online store or operate without a store to sell the supplier's products (online stores, Taobao, and eBay are all free platforms). You sell products, the supplier ships them, and you earn the middle margin.

If you're new to this field, unfamiliar with various aspects, uncertain about success, or already have an online store but with limited product variety and more time to manage, start with a zero-risk online store agency to test the waters. This is also a process of accumulating experience.

2) "Risk-Involving" Online Store or Physical Store Agency: Some products require an agency fee or franchise fee, or you need a physical store. Some follow the company's unified storefront and decoration mode requirements. In such cases, find reputable companies, such as those certified by Alipay Trust. Carefully review their business license, industrial and commercial tax permit, authorization letter, and the amount of the agency fee to ensure legitimacy, which requires experience to judge.

3. Transaction Issues

There are many ways to conduct transactions, and the simplest way is direct account transfers or using Alipay.

The key is the Alipay operation process:

After listing products on your online store, if customers come to purchase, contact the supplier to confirm inventory availability. Customers place orders for your products in your store and pay through your Alipay. Then, you place orders for each customer's product at the supplier's store. The supplier verifies and calculates the wholesale price. After verification and calculation, the supplier sends you the wholesale price, and you transfer the payment to the supplier's Alipay account. After the payment, the supplier ships the products to the customers according to the addresses you placed orders for in their store and fills in the tracking number, confirming shipment. Subsequently, you provide the shipping information to your customers. Once the customers receive the goods and leave positive reviews or after a certain period without issues, Alipay transfers the customer's payment to your account. After receiving the goods, you evaluate the supplier. Only after your positive evaluation can the supplier receive the payment transferred by Alipay.

Precautions:

1. The relationship between suppliers and online store agents is akin to that between suppliers and distributors. Suppliers should regularly provide detailed product information, explain the products, and conduct training to help distributors quickly familiarize themselves with the products.

2. Newcomers opening online stores may not be familiar with the network or sales processes. Simply turning on the computer to wait for customers won't work. Taobao provides a free online school with practical tutorials. The sales domain teaches you how to proceed, emphasizing the importance of this tutorial-based online sales university. The more you learn, the better your sales performance will be.

3. A major drawback of online store consignment is that consignors only have product images and basic descriptions for customers. Often, they can't answer customers' various questions because they haven't seen the physical products and don't fully understand each product's specific performance details. Therefore, agents should familiarize themselves with the products well or purchase some simple products to deepen their understanding and recognition. It's suggested not to stock too many products; focus on quality rather than quantity.

4. Negotiate relevant matters, such as returns, exchanges, timely shipping, delivery schedules, etc.

Many people plan to open online shops, but most remain hesitant. Starting from their personal situations, they have numerous concerns and confusions.

1- Should it be full-time or part-time?

For those who already have a physical shop, adding an online shop to expand sales channels is a natural extension and doesn't require much consideration. However, for working professionals or students, careful deliberation is needed. Unless you have significant confidence, a good supply channel, familiarity with the related industry, and sufficient funds, a prudent suggestion is to start part-time. Once the business grows steadily, transitioning to full-time is not too late. Calmly thinking more, reducing entrepreneurial risks, and leaving oneself a fallback option is the wise choice!

2- What exactly should I sell?

Every friend who wants to open an online shop initially faces this question. Take a look at Taobao; it’s diverse, and almost everything is sold, showing the varied ideas of individuals. Due to differing resources and interests, people engage in different industries. Securing stable and reliable sources of goods is the prerequisite for successfully running an online shop.
