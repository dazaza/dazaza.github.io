To start a business, I sold my favorite song "Mom Don't Cry"

by fenqu on 2008-04-24 21:07:35

In order to start a business, I sold my most beloved song "Mom Don't Cry".

Not long after dinner, I received a call from a stranger. It was from Beijing. At first, I didn't know who it was. Later, I found out that it was a music friend who added me on QQ last week. He is a teacher at the Central Conservatory of Music and currently works as a music director in a music record company. After listening to several of my songs, he wanted to buy one of them, which also happens to be my most beloved original song - "Mom Don't Cry". He plans to repackage and produce it to create a song with promotional value, and let his students participate in competitions. "Mom Don't Cry" was created for my mother when I was a sophomore. I have always loved it very much, and every time I go home, I would play the song for my mother. My mother always feels particularly happy after listening to it.

Since partnering with a friend to start an online accounting website - Caike Online (www.coko365.com), there are many places where money is needed. The website has not been profitable, so we encountered many difficulties. We plan to buy a new server in May, which will require a large amount of money. In order to start a business and make Caike Online bigger, better, and stronger, I have to sell my most beloved song "Mom Don't Cry". From now on, "Mom Don't Cry" no longer belongs to me. I only have the right to be named as the creator. I originally planned to wait until I had money to hire more famous musicians to help me remake it, but now I can only sell it to another company. In order to start a business and for Caike Online, I have paid too much and endured too much hardship. Never mind, I won't talk about it. When I have money in the future, I will write some better songs for my mother.

Starting a business is tough. I cannot properly repay my mother, and often think of her. Since my grandmother passed away, I feel even more guilty towards my parents for not being able to take care of them properly. I studied physics education and am a physics teacher. My mother has always hoped that I could teach in my hometown so that I could take care of them, and they would feel reassured. But I have always wanted to venture out.
