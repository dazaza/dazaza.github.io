Love movies or love food? "Crazy Dinner Party" brings food and movies into one world. Perhaps a question-style topic would leave everyone at a loss for an answer, but the release of the movie "Crazy Dinner Party" has let us know that movies and dinner parties are inseparable, forming a unified whole. Just like how many restaurants nowadays, in order to get a unique, easily memorable name, simply use a movie title to name their restaurant. Next, we will review several restaurants with cinematic characteristics...

"Trainspotting," director Danny Boyle's 1996 film, is a youth punk movie that influenced many people.

Guess Train was converted from a large factory building, featuring high ceilings with a loft structure added to about half of the space on the second floor. There are two skylights on the ceiling, and the white transparent glass house on the west side is an additional sunspace. Here, various cultural activities are often held; the boss, Old He, is a chubby man, also a gout patient like me, a literary elder, and a baijiu lover. Almost every week, there are independent film screenings here, and probably the longest-running independent film screening in Beijing is Guess Train, which started in a semi-basement in Wangjing. They also serve fiery southwest cuisine, based on Guizhou dishes. When the Miao King Bone arrives at the table, you can feel the intensity of the southwest cuisine. The selected pork chop "Undercover" comes topped with a thick layer of salted vegetables, crushed peanuts, green and red chili peppers, making it a perfect dish to eat with rice. The adorable small dish called Love Fruit consists of six tofu balls made by the Buyi people. A piece of the outer skin is peeled back, and several spoons of seasoning juice are poured inside. The outer skin is chewy, while the inside is soft and full of juice. This is a common snack eaten by young Buyi men and women holding hands on the street, hence the name Love Fruit. Another Buyi Salt Acid Meat has a spring taste. Although it's made from five-layer pork belly, it's not greasy at all because it's mixed with special Buyi pickled vegetables. The thin slices of pork belly are eaten together with pickled cabbage rings, giving this ethnic cuisine a fashionable deliciousness.

Address: No. 46 Fangjia Hutong, Dongcheng District

Seagull Canteen Only Three Tables

"Seagull Canteen," a simple and clean Japanese movie like haiku, tells about the mood of simple food, which can be paired with the Japanese TV series "Midnight Diner."

The real Seagull Canteen is even smaller, with only three tables, but it's warm and peaceful. It mainly features creative light meals, focusing on potato-based pizzas and pastas. The main feature of Seagull Canteen is the delicious potato-based pizza. Traditional pizza uses flour as its base, resulting in a very hard pizza dough that doesn't absorb flavor well. However, Seagull Canteen's potato-based pizza uses fresh potatoes, carefully selected and processed through seven steps to make a flavorful potato pizza base. It's soft, rich, and covered with abundant toppings and imported cheese. Each bite fills your mouth with satisfaction, freshness, and richness. Additionally, they offer delicious pasta, soups, salads, snacks, homemade drinks, and freshly ground coffee. The food is guaranteed to have no additives and is entirely handmade by the two beautiful ladies who run the shop.

Address: No. 70 Dongsi Fourth Street, Dongcheng District

Swallowtail Butterfly Offers Excellent Tom Yum

"Swallowtail Butterfly" is a masterpiece by Japanese director Shunji Iwai, a comprehensive and shocking work beloved by many.

This is a Thai restaurant offering excellent tom yum. I like their mint lemongrass wine beef, where the mint and lemongrass transform a simple beef dish into a Thai-flavored specialty. Mint and lemongrass take center stage here. The beef is finely chopped, highlighting the strong aroma of lemongrass. Lemongrass tastes like a man, while mint leaves taste a bit like a girl, evoking the phrase "bean-capsule years." Though referring to cardamom, the term fits the mint better here. They are squeezed onto one plate, like a concert performing for our mouths.

Address: No. 58 Sanlitun South Road, Chaoyang District

Jasmine Restaurant "Do Not Disturb" Here

In the vicinity of Gongti, concentrated are Beijing's fashion elements, such as Jing Ge and Zi Yun Xuan, examples of efforts in architecture and style. Jasmine Restaurant appears more frequently in the entertainment section than in the food section, having been used for filming movies and celebrities. This is understandable, as it boasts Beijing's tallest and most magnificent wine cellar, with three floors of fully functional dining spaces. Jasmine refers to its cuisine as Innovative Chinese Cuisine (new-style Chinese cuisine). Chinese ingredients, Western plating and cooking techniques, accompanied by nearly a hundred wines and champagnes from around the world stored in Jasmine's wine cellar, delight many wine enthusiasts and connoisseurs.

Address: Inside the East Gate of Workers' Stadium, Chaoyang District

Yueshi Yueshang "Du Lala" Has Dined Here

Yueshi Yueshang is located under the Yintai Building, serving as a canteen for nearby office workers. Many dining scenes in the movie "Du Lala Promotion Diary" were filmed here.

Previously, Yueshi Yueshang promoted "Borderless Fashion Food Market," but in the spring of 2011, it transitioned from "borderless" to purely Chinese cuisine but remained "province-less," representing dishes from all over the country from south to north, east to west, reflected in the menu. There are Northeast Big Harvest dishes, as well as Beijing scallion lamb cakes, Yunnan wild mushroom old chicken soup, and fragrant taro preserved sausage clay pot rice... Eating dishes from all over the country in one restaurant is often suspicious, but at Yueshi Yueshang, there's no need to worry. All dishes are designed by the head chef of the Park Hyatt Hotel, Qinglong Ouyang. If I had to choose a chef I trust the most, it would be this chubby bespectacled Chef Ouyang.

Address: B1 Floor, Yintai Center, No. 2 Jianguomenwai Avenue, Chaoyang District

Yinquan "33 Days of Heartbreak" Filming Location

"33 Days of Heartbreak" features many restaurant exterior shooting locations, and some fans have specifically searched for places visited by characters in the movie. If you're looking, Yinquan could be a good choice. Yinquan specializes in sushi, and I recommend Yinquan's Words, a teppanyaki-themed store. The interior decoration here is very Japanese, with different decorative styles in each room. The teppanyaki here stands out due to the high-quality ingredients and skilled craftsmanship. I recommend the foie gras here, perfectly grilled, tender without being greasy, paired with a slice of kiwi, bringing a refreshing taste through the smooth foie gras.

Address: Inside the No. 8 Manor, West Gate of Chaoyang Park, Chaoyang District
