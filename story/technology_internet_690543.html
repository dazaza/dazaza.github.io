Research on the Practical Teaching of Accounting Computerization

by cvxzty on 2012-03-05 10:47:00

Abstract: With the popularization of computerized accounting in enterprises and institutions, there is an increasing demand for computerized accounting talents in society. However, graduates majoring in accounting-related fields often find it difficult to directly handle the practical work of computerized accounting as required by employers, and their operational skills are far from ideal. This is mainly due to various problems existing in the teaching of computerized accounting, such as curriculum design, course content, teaching methods, textbooks and teaching software, faculty strength, practical training formats, and teaching evaluation methods. This article analyzes these issues and proposes corresponding improvement measures.

Keywords: Computerized Accounting Practical Teaching Hands-on Ability Classification Number: As the application of computer information technology in the field of accounting becomes increasingly extensive and profound, the computerized accounting course has become a core professional course that must be offered by all schools in financial and economic majors. As an interdisciplinary subject, computerized accounting closely integrates theory with practice, especially as the level of computerized accounting popularization continues to increase, its practical nature becomes increasingly important. With the continuous improvement of the performance-to-price ratio of computers, the application of computers in the accounting field will continue to expand, and the application of computerized accounting will become more widespread in economic life. When dealing with increasingly frequent and complex accounting tasks, adopting computerized accounting has become a consensus among people. However, how to better develop computerized accounting to meet the needs of China's rapid economic growth is a problem that we face. The teaching of computerized accounting plays a crucial role in solving this problem and poses higher requirements for the curriculum design, teaching methods, and organizational implementation of the computerized accounting specialty.

1. Accounting computerization teachers should change traditional teaching concepts and research more effective teaching methods.

- In course setting and teaching content arrangement, emphasis should be placed on combining theory with practice. In traditional college entrance examination education concepts, the principle of "the master leads you to the door, but cultivation depends on oneself" is often followed, where the teacher's "teaching" is emphasized in class while the student's "learning" is neglected, causing separation between "teaching" and "learning." Therefore, in traditional computerized accounting teaching, teachers overly focus on theoretical research while neglecting the practicality of the discipline and the cultivation of students' practical operation abilities, resulting in a severe lack of independent hands-on capabilities among students.

This teaching method can cause disconnection between students' theory and practice, making it hard for them to face actual work after graduation. Thus, during teaching, the theoretical content to be taught should first be clearly explained with a strong sense of hierarchy to establish a rigorous and clear logical relationship in students' minds. Then, necessary and substantial practical teaching should be carried out so that the theoretical framework in students' minds becomes more vivid and concrete. Especially noteworthy is that in teaching, theory should be promptly combined with practice, meaning that theory lectures and practice should alternate, complementing each other through learning and practicing, aiming to achieve better teaching results.

- Regarding learning methods, attention should be paid to the principles of progression, starting simple and gradually becoming more complex. In teaching, the instruction of computerized accounting practical courses should be progressive. Initially, teaching content that is relatively simple, with a straightforward handling process and strong representativeness should be chosen, avoiding excessive complexity which beginners may find difficult to accept, leading to loss of interest. Learning should progress from shallow to deep, from simple to complex, ensuring students maintain high enthusiasm throughout their studies and establishing good interactive relationships. This is more conducive to enhancing students' learning enthusiasm, stimulating their initiative, and achieving ideal teaching effects.

- In selecting textbooks and practical exercise software, the principle of high social recognition and strong practicality should be followed.
