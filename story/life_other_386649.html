Gynecological Experts Introduce Symptoms of Pelvic Inflammatory Disease

Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) is a common gynecological inflammation among many women. Despite its prevalence in gynecological conditions, according to experts from the Changchun Gynecology Hospital, many women still lack understanding of the symptoms of PID. This often leads to delayed treatment and worsening of the condition. Below, we will look at the relevant introduction by gynecological experts regarding the symptoms of PID.

Gynecological experts explain that most cases of PID are chronic PID. Typically, patients exhibit noticeable symptoms when the disease occurs; however, the specific manifestations differ depending on the type of PID.

The specific manifestations of PID include: early symptoms may not be obvious, sometimes accompanied by low-grade fever and fatigue. With prolonged illness, some patients may experience symptoms of neurasthenia, such as lethargy, general discomfort, and insomnia. When the patient's immune system is compromised, there is a higher likelihood of acute or subacute flare-ups.

Chronic inflammation leading to scar adhesions and pelvic congestion can cause sensations of heaviness, pain, and soreness in the lower abdomen and lumbar-sacral region. These symptoms tend to worsen after physical exertion, sexual activity, and during menstruation. Due to pelvic congestion, patients may experience increased menstrual flow; ovarian dysfunction can lead to irregular menstruation; and tubal adhesion or blockage can result in infertility.
