After the Copenhagen Climate Conference, "[low-carbon]" quickly became a global buzzword. Climate change is no longer just a concern for environmentalists, government officials, and experts but is now closely related to each of us. In response, LED lights have also gradually caught everyone's attention.

Why can LED lights [http://www.led99114.com/] respond to "[low-carbon, environmental protection]"? Let's first introduce the advantages of LED lights [http://www.led99114.com/]:

1. Small Size

LEDs are essentially small chips encapsulated in epoxy resin, making them extremely small and lightweight.

2. Low Power Consumption

LEDs [http://www.led99114.com/] consume very little power. They are DC-driven with ultra-low power consumption (single tube 0.03-0.06 watts), and the electro-optical power conversion efficiency approaches 30%. Generally, the working voltage of LEDs is 2-3.6V, and the working current is 0.02-0.03A. This means that its power consumption does not exceed 0.1W, achieving the same lighting effect while saving nearly 80% energy compared to traditional light sources.

3. Long Lifespan

Under normal voltage conditions, the lifespan can reach 60,000 to 100,000 hours, which is more than 10 times longer than traditional light sources. The LED light source [http://baike.baidu.com/view/10954.htm] is known as a long-life bulb. It is a solid cold light source encapsulated in epoxy resin, with no loose parts inside the lamp body, avoiding issues such as filament burnout, heat accumulation, and light decay [http://baike.baidu.com/view/1211719.htm]. Some people claim that under appropriate current and voltage, LEDs do not have these disadvantages.

4. High Brightness, Low Heat

LEDs use cold-light technology, generating much less heat compared to regular lighting fixtures.

5. Environmental Protection

Green lighting source without infrared rays, neither producing heat nor radiation. Fluorescent lamps contain mercury, which can cause pollution, whereas LEDs are recyclable. There is no ultraviolet light in the spectrum. LEDs are made from non-toxic materials, unlike [http://baike.baidu.com/view/30779.htm], with minimal glare, cool light sources, and safe to touch. They are typical examples of green products.

6. Sturdy and Durable

LEDs [http://www.led99114.com/] are fully encapsulated in epoxy resin, making them stronger than bulbs and fluorescent tubes. There are no loose parts inside the lamp body, reducing the likelihood of damage.

Experts have tested that: "A 5W LED tube has brightness equivalent to a 14W energy-saving lamp but saves 60% to 70% more electricity than an energy-saving lamp and over 90% more than an incandescent bulb. If used for 5 hours a day, one LED bulb can last for 20 years."

With the passage of time and encouragement from the government, more and more LED lights are entering factories and homes. We look forward to the moment when LED lights completely replace incandescent bulbs. Together, let’s respond to "[low-carbon, environmental protection]"!
