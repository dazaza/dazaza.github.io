Relevant group buying information of door enterprises can be found from time to time on large home building materials websites.

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-20 13:25:26

Door enterprises use home building materials websites to release group-buying information. Brand door enterprises prioritize the establishment and improvement of internal networks. The group-buying information on home building materials websites targets individual door enterprises, which can alleviate the competitive pressure among door enterprises but narrows the choices for consumers, making it difficult to meet customers' diversified needs. The author found that relevant group-buying information from door enterprises could be seen from time to time on large-scale home building materials websites. Careful observation of this kind of group-buying information reveals that most of it comes from wood door enterprises that have cooperative relationships with these websites. Unlike group-buying in home malls, this mainly targets single enterprises. Home websites utilize their accumulated popularity to call on netizens in forums to participate in group-buying activities, thereby achieving marketing for wood door products. Industry insiders stated that competition in the wood door market is increasingly accumulating, and the market is like a battlefield where standing still means falling behind. Nowadays, wood door group-buying activities are just getting started, so door enterprises seize consumers' shopping psychology and initiate group-buying activities at the right time.

Brand door enterprises prioritize the establishment and improvement of internal networks while establishing cooperative relationships with wood door websites to jointly promote group-buying interactions. Door enterprises use the accumulated popularity of home building materials website forums to attract netizens' participation, becoming the first choice for many door enterprises.

In the era of rapid network development, some wood door enterprises have long focused their attention on the Internet, exerting force towards it. Many wood door enterprises have begun to deeply explore and develop the use of the Internet. After customers select styles and place orders in stores, they can go online to understand the production progress and care instructions for doors, making consumers feel more secure.

For the vast majority of consumers, home decoration may only happen once, so choosing the highest cost-performance product is the primary consideration for many consumers. Currently, the 80s generation is gradually becoming the mainstream consumer group for wood doors. This consumer group has a significant characteristic: they enjoy purchasing goods online after comparing prices from multiple sources. The prevalence of group-buying conveniently combines both preferences, so it's no surprise that group-buying has swept across the Internet in a short period.

The essence of wood door group-buying activities in home malls is not much different from promotional activities. However, because group-buying information is primarily released through the Internet, it can attract more consumers' attention, making it a preferred group-buying method for many door enterprises. Conducting group-buying activities requires offline cooperation with furniture malls. Due to the overall nature of home malls, group-buying items often not only include wood doors but also cover related fields such as sanitary ware, flooring, and wardrobes. Industry insiders pointed out that the scale of group-buying activities in home malls is generally large, basically covering the entire mall simultaneously. To expand influence, malls will conduct activities with websites, and some home malls, such as Red Star Macalline and Jiajun Home, will even promote group-buying activities on their internal networks.
