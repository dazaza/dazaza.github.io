3 Effluent Quality

④ During debugging and operation, it was found that the surface of the DAT-IAT process is prone to forming floating scum compared to intermittent SBR processes. The reasons are as follows: the DAT-IAT process involves continuous influent and semi-static sedimentation, requiring extremely low flow velocities through the distribution holes (velocity < 2.50 m/h). The water flows laminarly through the sludge layer without disturbing the water layer, preventing mixing between upper and lower water layers. However, slight changes in velocity during actual operation may cause sludge to float. Additionally, gas-containing sludge can also lead to sludge floating.

③ Since the IAT tank stops aeration while the DAT tank continues to receive influent without sludge recirculation, the thickness of the sludge layer in the IAT tank must exceed the height of the distribution holes. Only then can the active sludge in the DAT tank avoid loss, ensuring that wastewater entering the IAT tank passes through the sludge layer to retain and adsorb suspended solids and organics, guaranteeing qualified effluent from the decanter.

② Grates are essential to prevent clogging of the lift pump and aerator throats.

① Septic tanks must be regularly cleaned to avoid overloading the integrated equipment with organic matter.

2 Commissioning Operation and Analysis

④ Automatic Control System: Time and water level dual control, fully automatic operation. The equalization tank is equipped with high-water and low-water ball switches, and the IAT tank has high-water, mid-water, and low-water ball switches. Under normal circumstances, when the equalization tank reaches the high-water level, the lift pump starts to transfer water to the DAT tank, simultaneously starting the aerators in the IAT and DAT tanks. When the IAT tank reaches the mid-water level, aeration stops, beginning the sedimentation phase while the lift pump and DAT tank aerator continue to operate. When the IAT tank reaches the high-water level, the decanter starts to discharge the supernatant. When the IAT tank water level drops to the mid-water level, decanting stops, the IAT tank aerator resumes aeration, and the next cycle begins. In this design, the lift pump and DAT tank aerator are interlinked.

The decanter used in this device employs a pump-type pressure drainage system, primarily consisting of an upper cylinder, submersible pump, float ball, lower cylinder, drainage pipe, rollers, and tracks. The upper cylinder regulates buoyancy and prevents scum discharge, while the lower cylinder and submersible pump remain submerged. Their depth can be adjusted. To prevent active sludge from entering the lower cylinder during aeration, a float ball blocks the inlet. During drainage, the submersible pump starts, causing the float ball to sink and automatically open the inlet of the lower cylinder, ensuring continuous discharge of the supernatant.

The most critical part of the DAT-IAT process is the supernatant discharge device, the decanter.

③ DAT-IAT Integrated Equipment: Underground steel equipment, dimensions LBH = 2.8m x 2.5m x 3.3m. The volume of the DAT tank accounts for about 20% of the total volume. The DAT tank is equipped with a 1.0kW underwater jet aerator; the IAT tank is equipped with a decanter with a drainage capacity of 30m³/h, a 1.5kW underwater jet aerator. The operating cycle is 4 hours, including 2 hours of aeration, 0.7 hours of sedimentation, and 1.3 hours of decanting and idling. The discharge ratio is 1/2, and excess sludge is discharged into the septic tank. Detailed structure is shown in Figure 1.

② Equalization Tank: Due to uneven discharge of water volume and quality, regulation is necessary. Since the process involves continuous influent, the equalization tank is designed with the minimum required volume. Underground reinforced concrete structure, dimensions: 4.0m x 1.5m x 2.0m. Equipped with two WQ15101.0 type submersible pumps (one in use, one standby).

① Grate: To prevent large impurities (such as plastic bags) from blocking the pump, a stainless steel basket grate with 10mm mesh is designed, placed at the inlet of the equalization tank for regular cleaning.
