Small and medium capitalization stocks perform actively, market bounces back after bottom exploration＿Stock Introduction

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:30:27

The report exclusively released by the National Information Center in China Securities on the 3rd believed that next year, the GDP growth rate of our country would hopefully reach around 8.5%, basically equaling to that in 2009. The economic growth and prices were expected to realize "double stability" operation situation, the possibility of obvious inflation in short term was not big, but it was inappropriate to further increase the intensity of expansion type policies in the future. Analysts believed that there was no too much innovation in the whole report, basically within everyone's expectation. The tendency of policy would not change, would make fine adjustments for anti-inflation, the main focus was whether the growth rate of M2 could be controlled at around 18%. This year, the growth of M2 is expected to reach 30%. Based on such a high basis, if continue with so much money supply, the liquidity in society would still be loose, which was good for stock market and real estate market. Due to the factor of suspension of new share issuance in the first half of this year, it was expected that the scale of enterprise financing from the securities market next year would be greater than this year. Judging from the recent rapid rise of issuance speed, the new share financing speed tomorrow would not be slow either. The capital pressure on the market would be greater than this year. Offsetting each other, the difficulty of 2010 market to achieve such a high rise as this year would increase. Large-cap blue chips performed weaker than small and medium-sized stocks. Sinopec fell by 1.14%, PetroChina fell by 0.37%, and Ping An of China fell by 1.51%. The shareholders' blueprint of Shanghai Disneyland has initially taken shape, Lujiazui Group held 60% shares, and the Disney concept was activated again, Lujiazui rose by 6.57%. The general manager of Shenzhen Stock Exchange indicated that margin trading was ready, and broker stocks performed well in the morning session, Haitong Securities rose by 3.36%. GEM continued to extend yesterday's sharp rise, Dayu Water Conservation hit the upper limit. Agriculture stocks extended the recent strong momentum, and rose against the trend today, Denghai Seed hit the upper limit. Automobile stocks with new energy concept continued to rise, and Sinoma Automobile hit the upper limit again. In addition, shipbuilding, medical devices and instruments and meters sectors ranked among the top gainers, while aircraft manufacturing, real estate and non-ferrous metals sectors were relatively weak. Institutions believed that the market recently welcomed the real small-cap stock rally. The main reason might be the high transfer theme. This was because the high transfer theme was often one of the main themes at the end and beginning of the year. Small and medium-sized companies with higher per-share capital surplus, higher undistributed profits per share and smaller share capital often had higher expectations for high transfer. Moreover, those newly listed secondary market stocks this year, due to high-price issuance and high capital surplus, laid the groundwork for next year's high transfer. Therefore, finding some individual stocks with transfer potential was a direction for small and medium-sized board investment in December.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. And this commission is only valid on the day the commission is issued. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock you want to buy or sell. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the stock code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when entrusting to buy or sell. At the same time, there are certain regulations on the quantity of stocks bought and sold: That is, the quantity of stocks commissioned to buy must be an integral multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned to sell does not have to be an integral multiple of 100. There are four ways to commission: counter submission commission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
