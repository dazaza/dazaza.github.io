Definition of CTI Concept in the Era of Multi-technology Convergence

by 865512 on 2008-11-03 13:59:18

The general discussion about the development of the CTI industry can be traced back to about 6 years ago. At the "China CTI Engineer Conference" in October 2002, CTI industry experts, CTI enterprise managers, and CTI R&D engineers discussed the theme of "Where is the CTI industry heading?" and it caught the attention and sparked discussions within the industry media. At that conference, Professor Song Junde, an expert in China's CTI industry, pointed out: "The development of the Internet has caused the communications network (telephone network) to retreat from its position as the world's largest network. Not only will the Internet become the world's largest network, but it will also transform into the core of various networks. Therefore, CTI technology will become a technology centered around the Internet." He also said: "Mobile value-added applications," "CRM data mining," and others will be important future directions for the development of CTI technology. Dongjin Technology also pointed out at the conference: "CTI has shifted from the traditional concept of computer-telephone integration (Computer Telephone Integration) towards the new domain of the computer telecommunications industry (Computer Telecommunication Industry), encompassing increasingly broad content. Any product spanning the fields of computers and telecommunications can be regarded as CTI, representing a massive emerging market." This grand event, organized by Dongjin Technology with participation from all sectors of the industry, provided guidance for the prosperous development of this emerging CTI industry in the coming years.

It can be said that during these past few years, CTI has played a significant role in the development of application areas such as telecommunications value-added services and call centers. However, more often than not, CTI was the unsung hero behind the scenes, and when telecommunications value-added services became a hot topic and widely recognized, CTI remained largely unknown to people. Even when various applications took center stage and garnered much attention, including from the industry media, there was still very little understanding of CTI. As a branch spanning both computer technology and communication technology, yet being marginalized by both fields for a long time, CTI has always played a silent supporting role.

With the technological advancements in various fields of the information industry, the CTI industry, which itself connects different fields, continues to evolve. In 2007, Dongjin Technology first proposed at the "2007 China Multimedia Information Communication Forum" that after technological developments, today's CTI is no longer the same as the CTI of the past. After evolution, today Dongjin redefines the concept of New CTI. The New CTI integrates C (Computation), representing the data processing capabilities of computer systems, T (Telecommunication), representing the reliability and systematic nature of telecommunications equipment, and I (Internet), representing the advantages of IP network/Internet distributed processing and interconnectivity. Dongjin believes that with the "convergence" of technology and industries, the intersection between various fields is increasing. Spanning three major fields, the New CTI will gradually move from the periphery of intersections to become the core of intersections, meaning that CTI applications will have a significant impact on the three major industrial fields.
