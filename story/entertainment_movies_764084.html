Synopsis:

A young woman engages in compensated dating, a submissive boyfriend, and an array of merchants and politicians. Can this escort girl have her own life? This film focuses on an escort girl who charges up to $2000 per hour, and the actress playing the lead role, Sasha Grey, is also a real-life adult star. This is Soderbergh's first movie dealing with sexual relationships since his breakout hit "Sex, Lies, and Videotape."

The English title "The Girlfriend Experience" refers to a social phenomenon where wealthy men pay high fees for sex workers to provide sexual services while pretending to be their girlfriends. To make the script more authentic, Soderbergh and the writing team interviewed several high-end escorts who earn around a million dollars annually. The film's screenwriters Brian Koppelman and David Levien previously collaborated on the script for "Ocean's Thirteen," and the story was developed together with Soderbergh.

At age 20, Sasha Grey entered the industry shortly after turning 18 and has starred in many adult films. In January of last year, she won the Best Actress award at the 2008 Adult Oscars. Her favorite filmmakers include Jean-Luc Godard, Bernardo Bertolucci, and Catherine Breillat. "The Girlfriend Experience" is Sasha Grey's third official film, having previously appeared briefly in the independent film "Quit" and the Canadian horror film "Smash Cut."

Behind-the-scenes production:

Expressing genuine emotions:

In 1989, Steven Soderbergh won the Palme d'Or at the Cannes Film Festival with his debut "Sex, Lies, and Videotape" when he was just 26 years old. Twenty years later, he returned to the field of "sexual topics." "I'm not making a documentary," Soderbergh said: "Although I used an adult star, and there's no real script, I'm still making a feature film. Like when I made "Sex, Lies, and Videotape," I'm purely interested in this topic. And the screenwriter I worked with also wanted to write about this subject, so we were very compatible."

Perhaps 20 years later, Soderbergh found a new theme, or perhaps after making Che Guevara's biopic, he wanted to express himself through a new way. Regardless, "The Girlfriend Experience" is quite special and premiered at the Sundance Film Festival before editing was completed, adding to the intrigue surrounding Soderbergh's return. Soderbergh said: "I think I've captured a small fragment of life. I don't have the ambition to deconstruct and reconstruct life; I just want to showcase a little slice of it. In the movie, I use five working days of a high-class escort to show her lifestyle. Who she interacts with, what she thinks, what she does - all of that is in my movie. I want to do everything I can to make a truthful film. Maybe the story is fictional, the characters are fabricated, but I ensure that their emotions are real, which is my goal in making this movie."

The Soderbergh who shot "Sex, Lies, and Videotape" gradually disappeared into the world of Hollywood blockbusters. Is this a misfortune for filmmakers or an opportunity for Soderbergh, who loves indie films? He said: "The quality of a film cannot be measured by its budget or box office. My movies rarely make big profits at the box office. The master I admire, Ingmar Bergman, is also like this, but who can say that Ingmar Bergman's films are of low quality? The inspiration for shooting this movie comes from his famous work "Cries and Whispers," and I want to shoot such restrained and calm yet emotionally charged films."

Adult stars and movie actors:

The biggest buzz about the film, aside from Soderbergh revisiting sexual topics, is the leading actress Sasha Grey. In the film, she plays a high-class prostitute - or rather, a "call girl." In real life, she herself is a porn star. Just turned 20 this year, she entered the adult film industry at 18. Though she is a porn star, she has a deep love for cinema, and her list of favorite directors includes Godard and others. In January, Sasha Grey won the Best Actress award at the Adult Oscars. In an interview with AVN, she said: "I really enjoy watching movies and am willing to collaborate with directors to make movies. For me, there are no taboo subjects that I can't shoot. I've known Soderbergh for a long time and deeply admire his works. It's an honor to work with him. I've won an adult Oscar, but Soderbergh has won a real Oscar." This isn't Sasha Grey's first film; she previously starred in the independent film "Quit" and a low-budget Canadian horror film "Smash Cut." However, in those works, she was merely a "cameo appearance," with only brief appearances.

Regarding casting an adult star as the lead in his movie, many critics have complaints, labeling Sasha Grey as a "non-professional actor." Facing these criticisms, Steven Soderbergh said: "I don't care if the actors are professional - or rather, what they mean by 'professional.' But I think no female actor could be more 'professional' than Sasha Grey. What I value is her potential and the feeling she gives me in the movie. Besides, I think no one is more suitable for this role."
