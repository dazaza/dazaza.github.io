Price list for coin and paper money collection

by zhenjie616 on 2011-03-23 14:10:12

Our country has already issued four sets of coins, the first set being the 1955 edition of circulating metal fen coins issued by New China. The denominations include 1 fen, 2 fen, and 5 fen. From 1955 to 1992, our country issued a total of 75 coins in these three denominations. The material for the 1955 edition coins was all aluminum-magnesium alloy. With the rise of coin collecting fever in our country, the fen coins have gradually become the darlings of collectors, with their prices steadily increasing. Currently, the price of a complete set of new fen coins has risen from several hundred yuan in 1990 to over 3000 yuan. Some years' editions of fen coins were issued in very limited quantities, sold only as souvenirs (in deluxe packaging) to foreign tourists, and rarely circulated domestically, making them now extremely hard to find. Their prices are naturally high, such as the 1979 5-fen, 1980 2-fen and 5-fen, and 1981 1-fen and 5-fen coins, known as the "Five Great Kings" of fen coins, each with a collection value of over 200 yuan, thus becoming highly sought-after "rarities" by collectors. Due to their rarity, there are now counterfeit coins on the market, so collectors should be cautious.

From 1955 to 1992, New China issued 75 circulating metal fen coins, including 28 types of 1-fen coins, none of which were issued in 1960, 1965-1970, 1988-1990. There were 27 types of 2-fen coins, none of which were issued in 1955, 1958, 1965-1973. And there were 20 types of 5-fen coins, none of which were issued in 1958-1973, 1975, and 1977. Due to different issue quantities and circulation times, some year's coins are now very hard to find, and their collection values and potential for appreciation cannot be underestimated. Relevant experts believe that when specifically operating, one does not need to deliberately pursue "rarities", but rather choose those varieties with smaller issue quantities, not too common in normal circulation channels, yet relatively easy to collect. For example, the 1-fen coins of 1955, 1957, and 1992, and the 2-fen coins of 1959 and 1992, which are relatively rare, could bring unexpected returns. For those varieties with larger issue quantities, if collected in perfect condition, they can also yield gains.

Additionally, it should be noted that from 1993 to 2000, the state issued eight sets of deluxe coins, each containing 1, 2, and 5 fen, and 1 jiao, 5 jiao, and 1 yuan coins. Among them, the 1, 2, and 5 fen coins do not circulate in the market, and are exclusively for collection purposes, with significant potential for appreciation and possibly becoming rarities among coins.

The second set is the 1-jiao, 2-jiao, 5-jiao, and 1-yuan denomination metal coins issued by the People's Bank of China in April 1980, to accompany the third series of paper currency. The materials used for the jiao coins were copper-zinc alloys, and for the yuan coins copper-nickel alloys. This set of coins includes seven different editions from 1980 to 1986, with four coins per year, totaling 28 coins. Among them, the coins of 1982, 1984, and 1986 are the rarest, with higher collection values. Other year's coins are less commonly seen on the market, with relatively lower prices, but still possess certain collection and investment values.

The third set is the 1-jiao, 5-jiao, and 1-yuan denomination circulating metal coins of the 1991 edition issued by the People's Bank of China in June 1992, to accompany the fourth series of paper currency. Their materials are respectively aluminum-magnesium alloy, copper-zinc alloy, and nickel-plated steel core. Among them, the 1994 1-yuan coin is relatively scarce, and the "Cast Coin Catalogue of the People's Republic of China" published by the China Financial Publishing House in 1995 lists this 1-yuan coin as two-star level.

The fourth set is the 1-jiao, 5-jiao, and 1-yuan coins designed by the People's Bank of China in 1999, as subsidiary currency accompanying the fifth series of paper currency. Their materials are respectively aluminum-magnesium alloy, copper-zinc alloy, and nickel-plated steel core.

In summary, the ones with relatively high collection values are: 1. The Five Great Kings of fen coins, namely the 1979 5-fen, 1980 2-fen and 5-fen, and 1981 1-fen and 5-fen. 2. The non-circulating 1, 2, and 5 fen coins from 1993 to 2000, totaling 24 coins. 3. The 1-jiao, 2-jiao, 5-jiao, and 1-yuan coins from 1980 to 1986 (commonly known as the Great Wall coins), totaling 28 coins. 4. The 1994 1-yuan coin.
