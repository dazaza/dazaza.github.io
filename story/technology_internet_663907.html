The National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information recently issued a notice titled "Further Strengthening the Management of Internet Map Service Qualifications", emphasizing that starting from February 1, 2012, units that have not applied for internet map service qualifications shall not engage in internet map services.

Mr. Tang introduced that the company made great efforts to obtain the qualification. In addition to establishing a joint venture company, they also arranged personnel and equipment according to the relevant regulations of the National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information. However, it is still unclear whether this application method will ultimately obtain the license, so while striving, they also made other preparations: "The joint venture company mainly operates 'Micro Territory'. Another product 'Tuding' which does not belong to the joint venture company gradually reduces the content of map services, but its main business should not be affected."

Google also applied for internet map service qualifications by establishing a joint venture with a local partner. However, upon checking the list of qualified units published on the official website of the National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information, its joint venture Nanjing Guxiang Information Technology Co., Ltd. did not appear on this list. Analyst Mr. Yan Laohao of Domestic (Weibo) stated that according to reports, Google's application materials were returned due to certain issues, "Currently, the prospects for Google obtaining a license are not optimistic, possibly related to its less proactive attitude."

Some websites avoid discussing whether they have obtained or applied for a license. Beijing Qingxin Unlimited does not directly provide map services on its website, only introducing itself as providing GIS and LBS software development and technical services on the website. Its responsible person Mr. Di Hou said, "The company only provides GPS satellite positioning services: when customers come and make requests, we provide corresponding services, I am not clear about the matter of internet map qualifications."

The person in charge of Jiabusi Company, which owns "Micro Territory" and "Tuding," Mr. Tang, informed reporters that since last June, the company has been in further contact with the National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information regarding the application process. "But because we have a foreign capital background, according to regulations, we do not possess the qualifications to apply. Therefore, we established a joint venture with Sina and applied under the guise of Sina, which possesses Class A qualifications." Mr. Tang indicated.

The National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information has issued "National Class A Internet Map Service Surveying Qualifications" to 147 units and issued Class B qualifications to 107 units. Units that have obtained Class A internet map service surveying qualifications can engage in four types of services: map search, location services, geographic information annotation services, and map download, print services. Units that have obtained Class B internet map service surveying qualifications can only engage in the first two types of services and cannot engage in the latter two types of services.

There are still some websites without qualifications that are still operational.

Local companies aim to "indirectly enter"

Previously exposed unqualified websites now seem to have disappeared, such as Capital Donkey Hut Home Network, Big Town Map, Land Network, Journey Travel Network, Shanghai Love Network, Damage Housing Network, Taihu Pearl Network Map websites, and a large number of websites are no longer searchable.

However, some licensed map websites are still accessible, such as the People's Republic of China Map Network.

It was confirmed by Mr. Lu, the person in charge of Taihu Pearl Network: "A few months ago, we closed the website with the prefix 'map', mainly due to internet map service qualifications."

In recent years, the National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information has begun to rectify the internet map industry. It requires units providing map search, location services, etc., on the internet and wireless internet to conduct normal business operations and should obtain an internet map license. Currently, more than 200 enterprises have obtained corresponding licenses, but companies with foreign capital backgrounds have never been listed, and whether they can obtain licenses before the deadline remains questionable.

It is reported that in response to such situations, the National Administration of Surveying, Mapping and Geo-information will urge local regulatory authorities to strengthen the investigation and punishment of such "problematic websites." For those continuing to engage in internet map services without qualifications, the administrative department of surveying, mapping, and geo-information will take measures such as interviews, exposure, and legally imposing time-limited rectifications or even halting internet map services.

Luxury housing websites were still providing map services until shortly before being announced as unlicensed sites last year. However, recently, it was discovered that their websites no longer allow searching for luxury housing locations via maps. The relevant responsible person of the company, Mr. Li, stated that the website abandoned map services several months ago and now only introduces luxury housing through text and images.
