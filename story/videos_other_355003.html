Nanjing's real estate market sees a price drop trend with the maximum decrease approaching 13% (Part I)

by splinta24 on 2010-05-12 15:55:28

Nanjing's Jiangbei real estate market suddenly hit by price cuts Yesterday, the large-scale property market in Nanjing's Jiangbei area, which has always been at the forefront of development and sales volume in Nanjing, suddenly made a bold move! Following the Xuri Shangcheng project offering a fixed price of 4500 yuan/square meter for over 300 units, Suning Properties then launched a fixed-price strategy for its three projects in Qiaobei: the Tianrun Cheng project set a fixed price of 4088 yuan/square meter for 350 units, which is about 600 yuan/square meter cheaper than before.

The continuous discount promotions of the Qiaobei projects have raised questions: Does their collective price drop mean that the Jiangbei property prices can no longer hold up? Is this a sign of an overall decline in Nanjing's property prices?

[Price Drop] Three more projects offer fixed-price units

The super-large scale Tianrun Cheng project, which threw out an astonishing fixed price of 4088 yuan/square meter, offers a total of 350 special-priced units spread across the project. Coincidentally, two other projects in the Qiaobei area of the Jiangbei block also adopted a fixed price: the Venice Water City offered a uniform price of 4480 yuan/square meter for 150 units, and the Tianhua Green Valley offered a uniform price of 4188 yuan/square meter for 50 units. However, it was learned that these two projects imposed strict restrictions on the floors of the units, such as Project B targeting the first floor and top floor units, and Project C targeting the first to sixth floors of high-rise buildings, the first floor, and the sixth floor of multi-story buildings.

A total of 550 special-priced units account for 16% of the total available units for sale. According to data from Nanjing Online Real Estate, these three projects currently have a total of 3360 available units for sale, meaning the 550 special-priced units account for more than 16% of the total available units.

An industry insider stated that for a major player like Suning in Qiaobei, 550 units distributed across three projects are just a drop in the bucket, but considering the current total number of available units, the 16% proportion is still relatively significant.

Sold to specific groups? The sales office says anyone can buy

Interestingly, Suning Properties' unified statement yesterday was: These 550 units from the three projects were specially selected to give back to society and show social and cultural care, targeting provincial and municipal model workers, advanced workers, military personnel, and other groups who have contributed to society.

However, when reporters separately inquired with these three projects, the sales staff all indicated there were no such restrictions, and buyers could purchase normally.

Why does this developer act so covertly? An industry insider speculated that the developer might be concerned that previous homebuyers might feel they bought at a loss and demand refunds.

[Citizen Reaction] Both those who want to buy and those who don't have their reasons

Citizen Mr. Shi expressed that if the fixed-price discount only applied to the first floor and top floor units, it wouldn't make much sense, but he noticed that some fixed-price discounts weren't solely targeted at the first floor and top floor units, like Project C offering fixed-price discounts for the first to sixth floors of high-rise buildings. Units on the fourth and fifth floors selling for 4480 yuan/square meter are worth considering.

However, Mr. Shi also believed that generally speaking, these fixed-price projects were hundreds of yuan per square meter cheaper than the prices he had been following, and with five or six hundred units involved, the scope of the price reduction was quite broad. He decided to go see the houses over the weekend.

But some citizens were not impressed by the price drops in Jiangbei projects. Citizen Ms. Lu said that she recently took a bus to Jiangbei to check out properties but got stuck in traffic for over two hours on the bridge. By the time she reached the project site to view the house, she had lost all enthusiasm. With such poor transportation conditions, living there in the future would be too troublesome. Even though the prices have dropped significantly, she remains cautious and will not consider buying a house in Jiangbei for now.

[Industry Analysis] Qiaobei's attempt to reach 5000 yuan/square meter shattered

Last year, the Jiangbei projects all aimed to break through the 5000 yuan/square meter mark, but now it seems they've been brought back to reality. Vice President Shi Dong of Nanjing Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution analyzed that from a transaction average price of less than 3500 yuan/square meter in February last year to struggling to maintain at 4500 yuan/square meter now, breaking through 5000 yuan/square meter in Jiangbei has clearly failed.

This can also be seen from the comparison of sales volumes in the same period: In February this year, the sales volume of Pukou's projects decreased by 74% month-on-month, putting pressure on developers.

According to predictions from Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, this year, Jiangbei will launch 3.313 million square meters of new commercial housing (slightly higher than 3.21 million square meters in 2007), including 2.865 million square meters of newly approved projects, ranking first among all blocks in Nanjing. This year, the additional supply of residential units from the 45 ongoing projects in the Jiangbei block will reach 2.8 million square meters. The concentrated and large-scale supply of commercial housing will inevitably intensify competition in the Qiaobei real estate market in Jiangbei.
