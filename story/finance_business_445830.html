Attitude Towards Money

Frequently, on mainstream TV shows, magazines, and newspapers, there are articles educating people on how to treat money. Essentially, they rank it and encourage everyone to elevate their mindset to a level of indifference or even disdain towards wealth.

In society, however, you won't find such discussions. Society doesn't debate whether money is important or not; instead, it uses countless examples to tell you: money is your second most loyal friend in this world, with your dog being the first.

As long as you're still breathing, your bank card will never abandon you for someone else. This is the attitude your money has towards you.

So, how should you treat money?

From the moment you step into society, you need to maintain a sense of greed towards money! This is one of your strongest driving forces. It's hard to imagine someone who is indifferent to money becoming a controller of vast wealth. Being perpetually hungry for money can make your thinking sharper, your actions quicker, and your spending more scientific, thereby allowing you to accumulate more wealth.

Under any circumstances, do not lose your interest in money. Under any circumstances.

Don't Make Unnecessary Sacrifices

There is only one rule: if you encounter an armed robber, and the money on you isn't worth risking your life, then hand over the money without resistance.

Many passionate young people might not understand this. They might say that by doing this, aren't we just emboldening criminals? What will happen to our society then?

When I look at this issue, I don't elevate it to such a grand level. I simply tell you: if you wish to live comfortably for a long time, fighting these people isn't worth it. As for whether it emboldens them or not, consider this: if there were better opportunities, how many people would willingly resort to armed robbery?

Death only comes once, and I hope everyone can eventually pass away peacefully in bed, surrounded by their loved ones.
