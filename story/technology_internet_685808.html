Considering the leggy girls—and there’s nowhere they’re more leggy than in Isabel’s—the crowd, wearing Isabel Marant sneakers, escaping the unseasonably warm European weather, might only think about staying fresh and free enough to run swiftly with their rolling suitcase full of the designer's oversized tie-dyed denim jackets, football tees, sneakers, a few pairs of slim trousers, and some dirt bike pants folded up in a baseball cap. It becomes traveling light because her body-con clothes are almost as snug and modest as swimsuits are small. The same goes for eveningwear: revealing, silver lamé-covered pieces or folkloric tapestry ribbon sections to layer over whatever.

In many ways, Isabel Marant is one of fashion's most authentic voices in relaxed dressing—the French equivalent of, say, Alexander Wang in New York. However, she has her own feminine glamour appeal. It's always about the heel (this season a sandal with silver snowchains at the ankle, secured as a fetish object next season) and she's genuinely into a vaguely hippie but captivating way of doing things. She has her staples: the trousers, which fit her perfectly in this season's denim revival, and the quilted jacket she often wears, now collaged with tie-dye denim and mirror embellishments. Every piece is believable, accessible ready-to-wear that women everywhere need to get through the day. While the economy may be heading in an uncertain direction, Isabel Marant has every right to keep a confident spirit. This blog will continue promoting.
