People grow bigger and start feeling more troubles. You all chat, I'll leave first! Starting to miss childhood, beginning to feel how happy childhood was. But when we reflect on our youth, we will reminisce about how much we wanted to grow up then. So, we are always envying what we can't reach.

In our childhood, we think "I want to grow up." When I graduate, I will have more of my own space and time. I won’t always hear adults saying to be a good child. But when we go to school, we find out that our free time has shortened, we must get up on time every day, finish homework on schedule. So, we start fantasizing about adulthood because adults can choose when to sleep and when to do housework. No one tells them when to do what.

But when we step out of school and into society, we can freely control our time and space, yet we begin to face this real society and its complex interpersonal relationships. We start learning to shoulder responsibilities, understand what responsibility is, and learn how to face difficulties and keep moving forward. Sometimes, when feeling tired, we think, "When I grow up, I will be successful."

The daughter has grown up, employment has succeeded, there's no longer a need to rush around, and one can enjoy their later years!! But I believe that by then, I will surely miss my younger days, yearning for the time when I was healthy, full of energy, and had children around me.

Therefore, today's me, rather than reminiscing about the past or longing for the future, should cherish every day now.
