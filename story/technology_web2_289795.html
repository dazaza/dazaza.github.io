Market Storm

by v12393455 on 2009-12-03 09:46:55

In the early years, when Chi Fung was young, Fu Chi Jin played Jia Wei, Xin Wei Lian played with Yan Bin, and Huang Zhi Qiang played as the three pests in the market. On the surface, apart from running their street vendor stalls, they secretly operated underground industries, indulging in eating, drinking, prostitution, and gambling. At that time, the police devised a plan to divide the three in order to eliminate them. The police lured Jia Wei and asked him to cooperate with them. Tempted by money, Jia Wei colluded with a high-ranking police officer to frame Chi Fung. During a gambling event, Jia Wei pretended to be held hostage by the police. Unaware of this, Chi Fung tried to rescue him but accidentally injured a police officer. Under Jia Wei's false arrangements, Chi Fung planned to smuggle himself out of the country to avoid trouble. Jia Wei secretly informed the police, who came to the dock to arrest Chi Fung. Although Chi Fung escaped smoothly, Yan Bin became his scapegoat and was imprisoned instead.

Chi Fung fled to Holland, where he met an old man from the same street. Before passing away, the old man entrusted his grandson, Yao Zu (played by He Zhi Jian), to Wei Feng. Out of sympathy, Chi Fung adopted Yao Zu. Years passed, and the young Yao Zu had grown into a handsome teenager. Chi Fung learned that the arrest warrant against him had expired and decided to return to his hometown with Yao Zu.

Returning to familiar places, Chi Fung found that much had changed. The once notorious "three pests" of the market were no longer. As a child, Yao Zu had often heard his adoptive father boast about his past glory and couldn't help but mock Chi Fung for being overrated. Back at the market, Chi Fung inquired about the whereabouts of his wife, daughter, Jia Wei, and Yan Bin. He discovered that Yan Bin had been released from prison but was now dispirited, selling vegetables in the market. Upon meeting, Yan Bin blamed Chi Fung for not being loyal and causing him injustice. Due to Yan Bin's wife falling ill while he was in prison, she eventually passed away, and his son was given away, leaving Yan Bin with resentment towards Chi Fung.

Chi Fung's stall was mostly managed by his daughter Su Fang (played by Xiao Pei Ying). The neighborhood speculated about Chi Fung's return, and Da Fu worried about Chi Fung holding a grudge over the transfer of the stall, whereas Sheng Li didn't take it seriously. Over the years, Sheng Li harbored love for Su Fang and quietly waited for her, helping her whenever possible.

At the market, Chi Fung unexpectedly encountered his wife, Pei Shan (played by Huang Xuan Xian), only to find out that she had remarried with his daughter Yi Qian (played by Chen Mei Jun) to none other than his former brother-in-arms, Jia Wei. Chi Fung was deeply shocked. Jia Wei had become the chairman of a listed company, Xinglong Group, and secretly engaged in illegal activities. Jia Wei felt uneasy about Chi Fung's appearance, fearing that his past deeds would be exposed and his current operations jeopardized. Jia Wei plotted to kill him to silence him.

On the other hand, when Jia Wei took custody of Yan Bin's son upon his imprisonment, he raised Zhi Xiong (played by Zhuang Zhong Wei), who grew up to become Jia Wei's right-hand man, committing many immoral acts on his behalf. However, Zhi Xiong was unaware that he was Yan Bin's son or that he had a sister named Su Fang. Zhi Xiong developed feelings for Yi Qian after growing up together. Meanwhile, Yi Qian fell in love with Yao Zu at first sight, causing jealousy in Zhi Xiong.

Yao Zu worked at Jia Wei's company and accidentally discovered that the murderer of his parents, Qing Biao, was Jia Wei's personal assistant and also witnessed Jia Wei's illegal activities. Zhi Xiong, having a liking for Yao Zu, attempted to lure him into operating underground businesses. Chi Fung decided to turn the tables, and Yao Zu reported Qing Biao and Jia Wei to the police, leading to mutual suspicion between them. Qing Biao coerced Pei Shan, who, unable to resist temptation, agreed to collaborate with him. To gain Jia Wei's trust, Yao Zu proposed to Yi Qian and revealed the truth, hoping for her cooperation in completing the scheme. Enraged upon seeing Yao Zu's intimate actions with Su Fang, Zhi Xiong ordered his men to destroy the stall and injure Yan Bin. Learning that Yao Zu knew Chi Fung, Jia Wei confronted Chi Fung and realized that Qing Biao was a murderer. Jia Wei blamed all illegal activities on Qing Biao and informed Pei Shan of her relationship with him. Zhi Xiong lured Yao Zu to a bar to harm him and demanded Su Fang's submission. Recognizing her brother through the jade pendant on Zhi Xiong's chest, Su Fang uncovered the truth. Qing Biao threatened Jia Wei with the ledger but realized it was fake. Under Jia Wei's pursuit, Chi Fung was timely saved by Yao Zu and Su Fang. Yao Zu's feelings for Su Fang deepened day by day.

How will these young people untangle their emotional conflicts? Severely injured by the triad, there wasn't enough blood at the hospital, but Yan Bin stepped forward. Moved by selfless familial affection yet ashamed to face everyone, Zhi Xiong received forgiveness from others. Through Yao Zu's relentless investigation, Chi Fung discovered that it was Jia Wei who tipped off the police to arrest Chi Fung, and the so-called warrant was merely a deception by Jia Wei. Facing the wealthy and powerful Jia Wei, how will Chi Fung expose his tricks? How will Chi Fung resolve Yan Bin's misunderstanding of him? Lastly, looking at his daughter Yi Qian, how will Chi Fung recognize her?
