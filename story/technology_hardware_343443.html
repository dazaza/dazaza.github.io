The Cool Nest of the SOHU Generation

This unique case was designed for a young SOHO couple: the male owner is an expert in film and television production who likes to collect Transformers, limited edition Coca-Cola bottles, various film and television posters, and is also a fan and DJ of electronic music; the female owner works in animation design, and her entire wall is filled with dolls that she loves.

When considering space for such owners, the designer faced a building from 1897, not much younger than the owners themselves. Therefore, it was necessary to re-divide the space:

First, the designer focused on transforming the leisure and work areas, which must meet the needs of both owners working from home. On this basis, the dining area can be weakened because the owners do not cook or eat at home often. Finally, the designer also helped the owners expand the comfort and convenience of the bathroom.

In terms of interior decoration, the designer used more black and white as the overall tone of the house: the black metal glazed tiles in the dining room, the black imitation steel plate PVC stone plastic floor in the living room, and the white real stone paint used on all walls, created rich visual changes between the originally strongly contrasting black and white colors.

Considering the practicality of this case, the designer used red as an accent color in the overall color matching, bringing vitality to the otherwise cold space. The white furniture blends with the white walls, making the originally small space appear spacious due to its neatness.

The choice of classical window pattern wallpaper for the bedroom "softened" the resting space while maintaining a unified overall style. The revolving door between the living room and the bedroom added interest to the entire space.

In terms of furniture selection, the designer chose simple Nordic-style designs that better meet the owners' needs for fashion and modernity.

Exclusive Space for a Jewelry Designer

This small apartment of only 45 square meters belongs to a girl named Dandan who works as a jewelry designer. After thorough communication with the designer, she decided to set the theme of the space as bright and comfortable Mediterranean style. Due to her busy work schedule, Dandan "claimed" that as long as the house looks good, she could accept it without having to worry too much about the construction process.

Even though she said that, the designer did not dare to slack off. After fully understanding Dandan's needs, the following overall design guidelines were established for this small space:

1. Since the house itself is not large, all functional partitions are indispensable. The designer did not make a hard partition of the space but let all partitions coexist harmoniously in a large space;

2. The designer chose the white-based Mediterranean style. A pure white space gives a visual feeling of wider space, and local bright color treatment makes the function partition clearer and the visual feeling richer and more delicate;

3. To make the space softer and more comfortable, the designer adopted curve design in many parts. These curves everywhere fit the identity and usage needs of the female owner.

Under the three-point overall planning by the designer, Dandan's home is like a delicate drama sketch, each corner carefully matched and arranged by the designer and the owner. Although Dandan's home space is not large, there are quite a few scenic spots. The setting of changing scenes step by step makes people not feel the space is small.

The core of the entire space is a platform that combines sofa, workshop, bookcase, and entertainment functions. The designer specially made a higher-than-ground design to make this partition more important.

In addition, the designer separated the functional partitions to retain a certain privacy by setting up many curtains and gauze curtains in the space. The floating fabrics not only separate the various functional partitions but also add a lot of feminine gentleness to the space.

Finally, worth mentioning is that since Dandan does not like to waste free time watching TV, the designer did not specifically design common TV background walls and simply hung a small LCD TV in one corner of the space.

43 Square Meters - Unique and Wonderful Two-Person World

Elegant Room Profile -

Usable Area: 43 square meters

Apartment Features: Studio apartment

Decoration Style: Modernism

Resident Population: Married financial workers

Project Cost: 50,000 RMB (excluding appliances)

Duration: 2 months

Main Decorative Materials:

Wall: Terracotta tiles

Floor: Composite flooring, tiles

Two-Person World: Unique and Wonderful

Mimi and Xiaochou completely subverted our understanding of home. Their small home is purely set up for their own lives. They did not consider entertaining guests or accommodating overnight stays. When the door is closed, it becomes a purely self-centered space. As they put it themselves: two-person world, only for two people to consider. Under this demand, the designer created a space exclusively for these two owners.

Private Bath - Concealable and Visible

Mimi, the female owner, loves taking baths to the extent of changing bath salts according to her mood every day. If she doesn't take a bath for a day, it feels like the day hasn't ended yet, the fatigue cannot be relieved, and the mood cannot become light-hearted. The original apartment layout had only a tiny 3-square-meter bathroom, just enough for a shower. When creating this home, the first priority was to meet Mimi's daily bathing requirements.

The bathroom should be large and comfortable, allowing one person to soak in the bathtub without affecting the other person's showering. The existing bathroom space definitely needed to be expanded. Together with the designer, the couple separated the toilet, shower, and washing functions into three different spaces. The small shower room became a separate toilet and bathroom; the sink took the fashionable external placement method so that mirrors and skincare products would not be dampened by water vapor; the shower and bathtub were combined into one space, fully ensuring the comfort and purity of each function.

In a two-person world, there was no need to consider blocking views. Why didn't they choose an open bathroom? On this point, the couple was unanimous: it looked good, it was avant-garde enough, but it wasn't convenient or hygienic, especially for someone who loved soaking in the bathtub. So they sacrificed a small space and used glass barriers. The semi-transparent Roman curtain at the bathtub could be opened or closed, more for adding ambiance rather than privacy considerations.

Private Storage - Plenty of Storage Space

To achieve the functions of bedroom, study, bathroom, kitchen, studio, audio-visual relaxation, etc., in a small house, and not make it look messy, how to maximize the use of space for storage was indeed something to think about. In addition to using the storage cabinets in the TV theme wall, Mimi also opened up two good places.

One was in the middle of the bathroom and sink, utilizing the corridor-like space, sealed and added doors, with a width of one meter used to store seasonal clothes and shoes, and the opposite side of the wardrobe was a full-length mirror. The small dressing room and bathroom were separated by a glass door, preventing water vapor from passing through. The second was to use the place where the mattress was placed. To save space, they did not use a bed but instead placed the mattress directly on a 40-centimeter-high platform similar to tatami. The platform was in the shape of a concave character, with the recessed part used for placing sofas, and the protruding wings were large drawers that could be pulled out, storing books, miscellaneous items, DVDs, or snacks, making them easy to access.

Besides storage, the couple also opened up five display niches above the bed to showcase their collected seal stones, each one being a private treasure.

A piece of gauze curtain separated the entertainment and rest areas, and the TV theme wall was changed into a theme cabinet, becoming the largest storage space in the house. There was also storage space next to the sofa armrest.

Personal Details - Humanizing the Home

Mimi's bed platform was 40 centimeters high, and a long desk was set beside the bed near the window. Placing a soft cushion on the desk made it very comfortable to surf the internet. On the other side of the desk, under the bed platform, sitting on a regular-height chair allowed comfortable writing on the desk. This design was truly impressive when seen for the first time, saving space while allowing the owner to use it in their preferred posture.

Such details were visible throughout the small house. On the left-hand side upon entering, there was a hanging shelf on the wall that could be folded away when not in use and lowered when needed, such as signing for packages or placing umbrellas on rainy days. The female owner also liked to prepare vegetables on it as a temporary preparation station. Mimi placed a high-legged bar stool next to it, making it a good place to sip a drink alone late at night.

After using glass barriers in the bathroom, the sharp corners appeared prominent. Mimi used a wooden board along the edge to create a round-cornered small table, just the right size for two people to eat. Whenever this design was mentioned, the female owner always felt very proud.

The range hood was another one of her favorite choices. Mimi's home did not have separately laid easy-to-clean tiles in the kitchen but instead laid a carpet on the wooden floor to block dirt. Occasionally cooking, her carefully selected powerful suction range hood came in handy, basically achieving the effect of no seepage. Although it was a bit expensive, for those who love cooking but don't want to sleep in the smell of oil, this was indeed a simple and effective choice method.

Private Privacy and Ambiance

Mimi's small house was rectangular with only one large window for lighting. Upon entering, one would directly see the window opposite, and the rest and leisure areas were clearly visible. Although two people might not care about privacy, seeing everything at once would lack some beauty.

Adding a pillar next to the platform at the sofa position became their secret weapon to create a winding atmosphere inside the house. It not only separated the rest and leisure areas, but its flat shape with shelves became another display space; with this pillar, the square small house gained layers, and the extending reliance for the gauze curtain separating the rest and leisure areas was ensured.

Old Small Space Gets New Tricks

Currently, many young people preparing to get married, due to the high price of commercial properties, have shifted their attention to relatively cheaper second-hand houses. However, because the buildings are relatively old, many second-hand houses are not only small but also have very unreasonable layouts.

This case is a very typical example: the total area is only 29 square meters, and except for the bathroom partition wall, all are load-bearing walls that cannot change the layout.

The new owners of this house are a young couple about to get married, and this house will serve as their wedding home. In addition to meeting basic usage functions, the owners hope to reflect a "youthful yet stable, simple yet warm" style through the decoration.

Due to the small usable area, after sufficient communication with the owners, the designer first considered how to arrange the space layout. The overall idea of the designer is to try to make each functional area able to borrow space from each other and not to make any physical functional divisions. For this purpose, the designer employed three clever tricks:

First, the designer demolished the original entrance door and the adjacent window and wall below it and used the partial load-bearing wall beside the window and the concrete beam above it to create a curved opening, keeping the dining room and living room transparent while increasing the dining room lighting and the overall spatial transparency;

Second, the designer removed the door and window connecting the balcony and effectively utilized the balcony area to become an organic part of the indoor space, using outdoor corrosion-resistant wood to create a small leisure area distinguished by a platform;

Third, between the living room and the bedroom, the designer arranged a semi-transparent partition to distinguish the functional areas of the living room and the bedroom while ensuring a certain degree of privacy in the bedroom;

In the interior design of small residential spaces, storage space is essential and the hardest to arrange. To fully utilize the space and make the small space appear tidier, the designer planned a huge storage cabinet on one side of the bedroom, living room, and dining room to meet the owner's storage needs. The designer also created a small workspace inside the wardrobe using panel furniture, with sliding doors identical to the wardrobe doors. When the owner is not using the desk, the sliding doors can be closed to hide the workspace and keep the room tidy.

Many people think that small spaces don't need designers to focus on style: there isn't enough space to show any style.

Actually, this notion isn't entirely correct. In handling this layout, the designer combined the owner's requirements and skillfully incorporated some Southeast Asian decorative elements perfectly into this small unit: the universally used teak color makes the space feel very comfortable and distinctive, and the local plant wallpapers also add charm to the space.

The Splendor of 37 Square Meters

In a space of only 37 square meters, it is necessary to meet all the owner's usage requirements, accommodate the accumulation from her life in Thailand: bed, wooden horse, Buddha statue... Most importantly, it must have a very outstanding decorative effect. These three requirements indeed gave the designer considerable pressure. After thorough communication, the designer made the following changes to this small space:

Balancing the multifunctionality of the 37 square meter small space with the owner's (Ms. Huang) personality and dreams for home is indeed a thought-provoking design concept, from the early spatial arrangement transformation to the later color and furniture style matching.

Ms. Huang's design differs from previous design processes. It can be described as a combination of convention and reverse thinking. The reverse aspect involves the owner's accumulated items from her life in Thailand, such as beds, wooden horses, Buddha statues... These are items the owner hopes to fully utilize and appropriately match in this design, presenting a challenge for the designer to think in reverse and determine what kind of space and style would best suit these items.

Thus, the major tones and styles have been roughly determined.

Next, it is about detailing, combining functions, and considering how the handling of space affects psychological feelings, which the designer fully grasps.

At the entrance foyer, the space is narrow, so a hidden wardrobe mirror is provided, satisfying functionality while alleviating the crowded feeling in a confined space.

An external sink and a custom-made bar stool enhance the space's style while effectively utilizing the area.

An open kitchen combines the heavy coffee color, clean white, and vibrant red-purple with Thai-style beds, akin to a gust of Southeast Asian exotic wind...

Designer's Exclusive Space

This is the designer's own home, so naturally, the design is particularly exquisite. When the designer created their own living space, they had only one thought: "As long as I want it, it's right," allowing their unrestricted creative ideas to finally find a space free from any constraints.

Due to the limited area, the designer also exerted considerable effort in arranging the functional zones:

Firstly, to save space and facilitate use, the designer reduced the size of the bathroom and placed the sink outside.

To make cooking easier, the designer also made some adjustments to the kitchen: the open kitchen is divided into two parts, mainly using the open Western-style kitchen section for simple meals when busy with work, and the enclosed Chinese-style kitchen for showing off culinary skills when there is time.

Finally, the designer utilized the balcony area, opening it up to create a leisure area.

In terms of style control, the designer has a penchant for the strong Southeast Asian flair and traditional ethnic charm, blending these seemingly unrelated styles together to form a unique decorative effect.

In terms of materials, the designer seems to prefer natural textures, such as raw wood, red bricks, and stones, which are visible throughout the space.

The heavy colors used in this plan are the most noteworthy feature. Typically, using heavy colors in a small space can easily make it feel cramped and oppressive. However, through the designer's clever arrangement, proper lighting and illumination design break this rule, making even a small space beautifully vivid and colorful.
