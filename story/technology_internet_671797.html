How to make pig stomach soup delicious

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-22 13:46:32

Pig stomach soup belongs to the Cantonese cuisine, with the main ingredient being pig stomach. The cooking method is stewing, and the difficulty level of preparation is medium. If you have tried this recipe, whether successful or temporarily failed, China Food Network Recipe Encyclopedia welcomes your opinions on this recipe, and we also welcome you to share your experience.

Ingredients for Pig Stomach Soup: One fresh pig stomach

Accompaniments for Pig Stomach Soup: 10~20 dried longan, lotus seeds, danggui (Chinese angelica), and some dried lilies

Method for Pig Stomach Soup:

1. Wash the pig stomach and blanch it in boiling water.

2. Slice the blanched pig stomach into strips.

3. Stew together the lotus seeds, dried longan, danggui, and dried lilies. If using an electric rice cooker with a stew function, set it for 2 hours, then wait for it to be ready to eat.

Two methods for cleaning the pig stomach:

1. First rinse clean with clear water, then rub with peanut oil (of course, use a small amount) and knead repeatedly for about ten minutes.

2. The most cost-effective method is to rub with salt.

Eating for Health:

The taste of pig stomach is sweet and slightly warm. According to "Bencao Jing Shu" (Commentary on the Classic of Materia Medica): “Pig stomach is an important supplement for the spleen. When the spleen and stomach are supplemented, the middle qi benefits, and diarrhea stops... Supplementing and benefiting the spleen and stomach promotes the generation of essence and blood, and heals deficiency and fatigue.” Therefore, many food therapy recipes that replenish the middle and benefit qi often use it. It is used for weakness and weight loss due to deficiency, spleen-stomach deficiency diarrhea, frequent urination or bedwetting, and pediatric malnutrition.

Culinary Taboos:

Pig Stomach: Incompatible with water chestnuts. Avoid eating together as it may cause abdominal pain.

Pig stomach + lotus seeds, if cooked using white eggplant branches (dry eggplant tree leaves) for fire, becomes highly toxic, and eating it will be fatal.

One pig stomach (whether there is a small stomach doesn't matter). A few grains of pepper. Salt. MSG. And the most important thing, a few slices of Hakka pickled vegetables.

Production:

1. (Do not buy frozen pig stomach. You must buy fresh ones. Although they are harder to wash, after today, you'll find it's actually very easy.) Cut off the fat from the pig stomach if possible; otherwise, use a little peanut oil. Yes, peanut oil. Pour some down, and if necessary, add some starch. Then grab both sides evenly. Rinse with clear water while grabbing, and it's done. Believe me, you can smell it. There is no fishy smell, only the fragrance of peanut oil. And your hands without gloves won't feel greasy. Strange, right? Try it. It's really easy.

2. Crush the peppercorns with a knife and then rub them with salt inside the pig stomach.

3. For the pickled vegetables, press with your hand and slice them thinly. Not much is needed, just one or two ounces suffice. Of course, you can add more if you like. This is the most distinctive feature of this dish. Without it, it would just be ordinary pepper pig stomach soup.

4. Now you can cook. Remember to boil first, then take it out and cut it. After cutting, roll it again and season it.

If it's springtime, this soup can also relieve spring fatigue and has the effect of sobering up.

Peppercorn Pig Stomach and Pig Intestine Soup (This soup is very delicious)

Ingredients: 100 grams of white peppercorns, one pig stomach, 300 grams of pig intestines, 200 grams of lean meat

Method:

1. Remove the fat from the pig stomach, wash and clean thoroughly to remove any odor (you can use flour and salt for cleaning), and clean the intestines.

2. Boil the pig stomach and intestines in boiling water for 10 minutes, then take them out, wash them clean, dry the moisture, put the peppercorns inside the pig stomach, and tie the mouth of the pig stomach with grass string (like those used for binding zongzi) to prevent the peppercorns from falling out.

3. Blanch the lean meat and wash it clean.

4. Bring 9 cups or an appropriate amount of water to a boil, add the peppercorns, pig stomach, pig intestines, and lean meat, bring to a boil again, then simmer over low heat for 3 hours, and finally season with salt.

Corncob White Nut Pig Stomach Soup

Ingredients: One pig stomach, 160 grams of lean meat, 80 grams of bean curd sticks, 80 grams of white nut kernels, half a bowl each of raw coix seed and cooked coix seed, four pieces of horse hoof meat, a little salt, and an appropriate amount of clear water.

Method: (1) First, remove the fat from the pig stomach, turn it inside out, and rub with salt and flour evenly, then wash clean; repeat the process once, wash clean, then place in boiling water for a moment, take it out, and wash again.

(2) Break the bean curd sticks into segments,

(3) Remove the heart of the white nuts,

(4) Slice the horse hooves,

(5) Use a vertical earthen pot to bring about eight-tenths full of clear water to a boil,

(6) Add the pig stomach and simmer for about an hour,

(7) Add the other ingredients and simmer for another hour,

(8) Take out the pig stomach and slice it,

(9) Combine with the other ingredients,

(10) Serve together on a plate.

Note: (1) The soup ingredients can be dipped in soy sauce for consumption.

(2) Bean curd sticks are made by processing tofu skin into flat round long sticks, commonly used in vegetarian dishes.
