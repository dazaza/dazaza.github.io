Nanjing real estate market迎来witnesses price-cutting trend, with the maximum reduction approaching 13% (Part II)

by oulalala94 on 2010-05-13 14:56:29

Under the premise that the problem of transportation across the river in Jiangbei is not solved, there is a trend for property prices in Jiangbei to decline. Song Jian, director of Nanjing Su Ding Real Estate Research Institute, believed that with the traffic bottleneck of the Yangtze River Bridge still existing, real estate projects in Jiangbei will definitely see substantial price cuts, but even with price cuts, they may not necessarily sell well. Why? Song Jian explained that this was related to the excessively large volume of properties listed in Jiangbei. After favorable news such as the cross-river tunnel was released last year, property prices in Jiangbei rose too quickly. Therefore, it was reasonable for developers to appropriately reduce prices.

How can we understand the idea that price cuts don't necessarily mean selling more? Because the housing sources offered by Jiangbei developers come with conditions. In this case, three real estate projects have made restrictive regulations on the floors. Some units with good floors and good views will definitely not be sold at a cheap price. At the same time, after the financial pressure on developers eases, a gradual adjustment of prices is inevitable.

[Competitors' Response] Low land prices in Jiangbei leave room for price cuts. How can developers withstand such price reductions and maintain their profits? A developer from Jiangbei who wished to remain anonymous said: We shouldn't interpret developers' profits in this way because the price reduction space for real estate projects in Jiangbei is much larger than that for projects in the main urban area. He said that some super-large real estate projects in Qiaobei had relatively low land prices when they were acquired. For example, one project had a minimum land price of only 90,000 yuan per mu. Considering an average plot ratio of 1.5, the floor land price (the cost of land price in housing prices) is less than 100 yuan per square meter. With such low land prices, the cost of selling houses can almost be ignored, leaving developers with ample room for price reductions.

Mingfa Binjiang New Town plans to moderately increase housing prices. Will the flat-price sales method of several real estate projects in Qiaobei spread to other nearby developers? How do they view this price reduction? Ouyang Yan Yu, the marketing manager of Mingfa Binjiang New Town, surprisingly said: Our real estate project will not take any price reduction measures. Instead, the prices of a batch of housing units to be launched at the end of the month will be moderately increased.

Why is Mingfa Binjiang increasing prices while others are reducing them? Ouyang Yan Yu laughed and said, Mingfa Binjiang has a construction area of 2.2 million square meters, and so far, 1.6 million square meters have been sold. The remaining part can be considered profit. If we were to offer discounts, they would certainly be more aggressive than other projects. However, the 17 buildings to be launched later will all be riverside apartments, where every household can enjoy a view of the Yangtze River. Moreover, this is also responsible to the owners who purchased the earlier phase of the project: the value of the house needs to appreciate.

In the short term, the price reduction in Jiangbei will have some impact on property prices in Jiangnan. Now, with the price reduction in Jiangbei, will it affect the commercial properties and second-hand homes in Chengbei and Gulou, Xiaguan on the opposite bank? Wang Wei Wei, manager of Nanjing Zhongyuan Real Estate Trading Center, said that if Jiangbei maintains a price around 4,500 yuan/square meter, then in the short term, it might have some impact on the property prices in Chengbei and Xiaguan areas. After all, there's only a river separating them spatially. There's also an industry saying that the ratio of property prices between Jiangbei and Chengbei is 1:2. If Jiangbei is around 4,500 yuan/square meter, then Chengbei will stay around 9,000 yuan/square meter. As for second-hand homes, there should be no significant impact because the comparability between the two is relatively poor. For instance, the property prices near Gulou by the river are mainly supported by their prime location.
