Public Security Department: Not conducive to public security

According to reports (reporter Xu Lijia, Wang Qing), ink thrown on someone will fade after a period of time, biting a capsule will create the appearance of spitting blood, and sprinkling white powder on someone will cause itching... Yesterday, a newly opened magic supplies store on Jianghan Road attracted many buyers. Many young people bought these "magic props" to seek novelty and try pranks. For this, the Wuhan Public Security Department reminded that pranks can easily have negative effects on the psychology of teenagers, and at the same time, professional doctors also reminded that these props can harm the human body.

Upon entering a newly opened magic supplies store on Jianghan Road, the best-selling item "bloody sugar" was placed on top. On the front cover of the product, there was a picture of a red-faced person with a large amount of blood coming out of their mouth. The product description stated "put the capsule in your mouth, bite it at the appropriate time, and let disgusting and terrifying blood flow out of your mouth", while the product was marked in English not to provide it to children under 14 years old.

In addition to harm to the human body, the Wuhan Public Security Department also expressed that such prank products might have negative impacts on the psychology of teenagers, making them think that teasing others is fun. Once they gain benefits from tricking and deceiving others, they might further develop other malicious intentions.

Furthermore, "bloody sugar" can also affect the psychology of some adults. Relevant personnel from the public security department introduced that currently, the common "monkey collision" phenomenon in society uses fake injuries to extort money from drivers. If they buy this "bloody sugar", it would further deceive the victims, making it hard to distinguish between truth and falsehood, thus harming public security.
