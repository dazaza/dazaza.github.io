Analysis of iBeacon Technology: Apple's Sharp Tool Against NFC

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 21:41:44

At this year's WWDC in June, as one of the most important new features in iOS 7, Apple officially released iBeacon to the public. Although Apple’s Senior Vice President of Software Engineering, Craig Federighi, did not mention iBeacon during the keynote, and Apple also did not disclose any details about the technology, the name "iBeacon" still appeared on the WWDC Keynote.

Even though Apple didn’t mention iBeacon at the new iPhone launch event on Tuesday, foreign media Gigaom believes that iBeacon will be one of Apple's key development focuses in the future. Meanwhile, a company called Estimote has announced that it will release beacons compatible with iBeacon technology and has released a demonstration video.

Why would companies like Estimote support iBeacon technology? First, iBeacon provides a platform for emerging applications such as indoor mapping and can make the internet more easily integrated into our daily lives. It might even have the potential to surpass NFC, which is currently considered the most promising wireless payment method.

What is iBeacon?

By using Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE), also known as Bluetooth 4.0 or Bluetooth Smart, an iBeacon beacon can create a signal area, and when a device enters this area, the corresponding application will prompt the user whether they want to connect to this network. Through small wireless sensors placed in any object and BLE, users can transmit data via their iPhones.

For example, if you walk into a large mall store with an iPhone 5s (running iOS 7 and supporting iBeacon), it means you have entered the store's iBeacon signal area. Then, the iBeacon beacon can send various information to your iPhone, such as coupons or in-store navigation information. Even when you approach certain counters, iBeacon may provide personalized product recommendations. This means that within the iBeacon beacon's information area, users can obtain personalized micro-location information and notifications through their smartphones.

It is easy to see from the above example that iBeacon can provide users with the information they need, and like NFC technology, users can also complete payments through iBeacon. Moreover, each iBeacon beacon contains an accelerometer, flash memory, ARM architecture processor, and Bluetooth module, and a small button battery can power an iBeacon beacon for up to two years.

What is Bluetooth Low Energy?

As its name suggests, the biggest feature of Bluetooth Low Energy is low power consumption, thus enabling devices to have longer battery life. However, Bluetooth Low Energy only supports low file transfer rates, so it can be used for information transmission between wearable smart devices but cannot accomplish tasks like audio transmission. Currently, only Android 4.3 supports Bluetooth Low Energy, which is why older Android models do not support some navigation apps.

Why could iBeacon become an NFC killer?

From a technical perspective, a wider information transmission range is iBeacon's biggest advantage over NFC. Although NFC tags are much cheaper than NFC chips, the theoretical effective distance of NFC tags is only 20cm, and the ideal usage distance is only 4cm, making the range very small. Also, mobile devices such as phones must be equipped with NFC chips to support NFC communication. Additionally, although iBeacon beacons are slightly more expensive than NFC chips, the information transmission distance of iBeacon can reach about 50m. Nowadays, almost every phone supports Bluetooth technology, but not necessarily equipped with NFC chips.

Why is the cost of using iBeacon not high?

Let's go back to the previous example. Suppose a store's area is around 16,000 square meters, and if the farthest transmission distance of each iBeacon beacon is 50m, then the coverage area is approximately 2,500 square meters. Therefore, the store only needs to purchase 7 iBeacon beacons to meet the requirements.

According to the price provided by Estimote, the preorder price for 3 iBeacon beacons is $99 (approximately RMB 610). Note that although Estimote recommends that the optimal usage effect is within 10m, if following Estimote's suggestion, the coverage area of each iBeacon beacon is about 100 square meters, then the total cost would be approximately $5,000. If using NFC tags, calculated at 10 cents (approximately RMB 0.61) per tag, 100,000 items would require $10,000, which is twice the cost of using iBeacon beacons.

Google's Approach: Focus on NFC Technology

Compared to Apple, Google seems more focused on NFC technology. In terms of using Bluetooth technology, since Android systems before Android 4.3 did not support Bluetooth Low Energy, many applications requiring this technology could not release Android versions. Under the suggestions of some OEM manufacturers, Google finally realized this issue and ultimately added support for Bluetooth Low Energy in Android 4.3. In contrast, NFC technology was supported as early as Android 4.0.

Apple's Approach: Focus on Bluetooth Technology

Before each new iPhone launch, there are always predictions that the new iPhone will be equipped with NFC technology, but facts prove that these are just outside speculations. In iOS 7, Apple added the AirDrop wireless sharing function, and Craig Federighi believed that "there is no need to walk up to someone just to touch phones for file sharing," which is Apple's view on NFC technology.

A Series of New Applications May Be Born?

From the characteristics of iBeacon, this technology is likely to promote the birth of a series of indoor map applications. Since GPS signals are very weak indoors due to various obstacles, navigation cannot be achieved. This is why, although Google has already established indoor maps for some areas, indoor navigation is still not possible. This is precisely where iBeacon technology has an advantage.

With the help of smartphones, users can connect to the nearest iBeacon beacon, thereby obtaining the GPS location information of that beacon and knowing their current location. When users enter or leave the communication range of a certain iBeacon beacon, they will receive corresponding notification messages, thus achieving the purpose of navigation.

Bluetooth Low Energy is the Best Way to Bring the Internet Into Reality

To integrate the internet with the real world, various sensors play a very important role. For a sensor, size, price, and network connectivity are all critical indicators. If sensors can be remotely controlled, their uses could be infinite. For example, turning on the power at home while on the way back, controlling the temperature of the refrigerator according to the weather, and controlling the lights in the room via a phone, etc. Estimote says they are working hard to improve iBeacon beacons to further reduce costs and size, making iBeacon more practical.

Since Apple can solve short-distance data transmission problems through iBeacon using Bluetooth Low Energy, why would people need to physically touch their phones? Moreover, compared to NFC technology, Bluetooth can offer functions that NFC cannot achieve.
