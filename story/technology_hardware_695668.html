The company rules stipulate that male employees must wear perfume during working hours.

by g3qfxz18053 on 2012-03-07 16:43:48

Times News: Little Xie, who works at a hotel management company on Qingchun East Road in Hangzhou, has recently been troubled by a bottle of perfume. "It feels weird for a man to use perfume," said Xie.

Recently, Xie transferred to a new company where there is an unwritten rule that male employees must wear perfume during work hours and smoking is not allowed in the office.

Xie has always wondered: "I can understand why smoking isn't allowed in the office, but requiring male employees to use perfume seems strange."

The boss says: "My company, my rules"

Yesterday, the reporter interviewed the general manager of the company, Wei Keduo.

Wei Keduo, a seasoned professional in hotel management in his early 40s, was neatly dressed. During the conversation, the reporter could faintly smell a delicate fragrance.

"I think personal taste and inner quality are crucial in the hotel industry. Using perfume can enhance one's sophistication; the pleasant aroma can make people feel good. In service industries, you need to be in a good mood first so you can pass your happiness on to customers," said Wei Keduo.

Wei Keduo continued: "In my company, I have the final say, and this is part of our corporate culture. Using perfume is not a bad thing. I hope employees choose different perfumes based on their moods."

An old employee says: "The boss's approach works somewhat."

Liu Hao has worked with his boss for many years and has been influenced to use perfume.

"I believe a man of taste should know how to select a suitable perfume," Liu Hao said, "Who wouldn't like something elegant and beautiful?"

Liu Hao told the reporter that once he closed a deal thanks to his perfume.

"Previously, I had a difficult female customer who was dissatisfied with all our plans. One day, I accidentally changed my perfume, and she really liked the scent. We had much more to talk about, which made it easier to get along. Eventually, we closed the deal, and even became friends." Liu Hao smiled, saying, "The boss's method works somewhat."

Should men use perfume during work hours?

With this question, the reporter interviewed ten white-collar workers to hear their opinions.

Pro: "No longer be called a smelly man"

Little Chen, male, 22 years old, employee at Hangzhou Tencent Technology Co., Ltd.

"Great, no longer be called a smelly man," said Little Chen, supporting men using perfume. "Using perfume in the office is becoming increasingly common. It shows respect for oneself and others."

Little Chen added: "Nowadays, many perfume brands offer male fragrances, increasing men's choices. Men using perfume is a positive trend, just like women applying makeup—it pleases both oneself and others."

Pro: Perfume bottles can be cleaned and used to store alcohol

Wang Kan, male, 35 years old, employee at Hangzhou East China Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd.

Wang Kan said: "I don't oppose using perfume in the office. For men, wearing perfume symbolizes identity. It's important in daily interactions as it leaves a good impression on others and boosts one's enthusiasm for work, achieving twice the result with half the effort."

"And after cleaning perfume bottles, they can be used to store alcohol. You can even perform tricks of drinking alcohol from perfume bottles in front of friends, which is pretty cool," joked Wang Kan.

Con: The office is a formal setting, unsuitable for perfume

Hu Wen, male, 29 years old, employee at a natural gas company in Zhejiang Province

Most of Hu Wen's office colleagues are male, but only Hu Wen occasionally uses perfume, and even then, it’s a very light scent.

"It might leave a bad impression. Although we're not a government agency, we're still considered a semi-state-owned enterprise, and using perfume doesn't seem appropriate," Hu Wen believes it depends on the nature of the company. In workplaces with strict requirements, using perfume may not be suitable. "At least not during working hours."

Con: Men using perfume appear frivolous

Zhou Wei, male, 46 years old, unwilling to disclose company information

Old Zhou strongly opposes the use of perfume in the office, especially by some interns who pass by leaving a strong whiff of perfume, making it unbearable.

"Men using perfume appear frivolous. Some young people wear too much perfume, attracting unnecessary attention and creating a poor image," Old Zhou thinks it's inappropriate to use perfume in formal settings, especially when conducting business negotiations.

"Spraying perfume for leisure activities or parties is fine, but work and entertainment should be separated. Applying perfume indicates a lack of seriousness," (Intern Reporter Kang Lünye, Zhang Yiwen, Times Reporter Zhang Qin)
