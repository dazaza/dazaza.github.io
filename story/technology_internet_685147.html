The shape of the staircase

In home interiors, staircases mainly come in the following types:

1. Straight staircase. These are rarely seen now due to their excessive space consumption.

2. Curved staircase. Some duplexes still use this design, but it suits only large duplexes.

3. Folded staircase. Commonly used in most duplex designs.

4. Spiral staircase. A variant of folded staircases. While there are many forms, they are hard to enumerate thoroughly. However, in terms of materials, they are relatively easy to categorize.

Stairs can be decorated using any material found in home decoration, such as solid wood, stone slabs, tiles, steel plates, carpets, etc.

The quality of handrail design is crucial in evaluating the overall design of a staircase. The worst choice for indoor handrails is mirror-polished stainless steel or other shiny metallic surfaces. If you insist on using stainless steel, I recommend using matte-finished stainless steel. Based on my experience, the ideal material for staircase handrails is forged steel, followed by cast iron, then wood, and finally ceramics.

For staircases, the most ideal handrail material is wood, followed by stone. The design of the staircase steps is often predetermined in building architecture. However, many homeowners choose to dismantle and reinstall them. Regardless of the style, if you wish to redesign it, the ideal material is cement concrete, followed by steel structures, and then wood. Considering this, it is essential that the staircase makes as little noise as possible when walked upon.
