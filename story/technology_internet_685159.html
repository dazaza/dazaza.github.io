When we visit building material supermarkets in various cities, it is not difficult to find that although the number of stalls occupied by stair brands is not many, the display arrangements in each shop are very meticulous, showcasing as many miniature versions of various stairs as possible in front of customers. So, what kind of material stairs are favored by consumers?

Wooden stairs. In the future development of furniture design, the furniture-like transformation of stairs will be an inevitable trend. This is the type with the largest market share. Consumers like them mainly because wood itself gives a warm feeling, and it is easy to match with floor materials and colors, making construction relatively convenient. Consumers choosing wooden products for stairs should pay attention to matching the size of the floor with the stairs. Currently, the most common floor size in the market is 90cm long and 10cm wide. However, stair floors can be paired with 120cm long and 15cm wide floors, meaning one step of the stairs only requires two regular floors. This reduces the number of joints, making construction and maintenance easier. The selection of columns and handrails should ensure that the wood and style are as matching as possible. Although wooden stairs may seem to lack a changing mood, they have their own advantages—texture and purity.

Iron stairs. Compared to pure wooden stairs, this type of stairs seems to add a touch of lively fun. It is actually a composite stair made of wood and iron products. Some stair handrails and railings are iron products while the stair treads are still wooden products. Others have iron railings with wooden handrails and treads. There are also quite a few customers who choose such stairs. Nowadays, there are many choices for forged patterns in stair railings, including column-style and various pattern compositions; colors range from antique to copper and iron's natural hues. Such stair handrails are custom-made, complex to process, and relatively expensive. Cast-iron stairs, on the other hand, have fewer styles. Manufacturers usually have fixed designs for customers to choose from, and colors can be processed according to customer requirements. People who pursue varied forms tend to choose this type of stairs.

Marble stairs. Customers who choose this type of stairs generally already have marble laid on the ground floor. To maintain the uniformity of indoor colors and materials, marble continues to be used for the stairs. However, most retain wooden handrails to add some warmth to the cold space. The price of such decorations mainly depends on the quality of the marble. It gives a very high-class feel.

Glass stairs. This is a newer style suitable for modern young people. The glass is mostly frosted, not fully transparent, with a thickness of over 10mm. Some of these stairs also use wooden handrails, costing slightly less than imported marble.

Steel stairs. This is a rather unconventional choice, but it has become trendy. Generally, paint is sprayed on the surface of the material. Since this paint is matte, it does not have a glaring, dazzling feel. Both the material and processing costs for this type of stair are relatively high. Additionally, there are those that use steel wire and hemp rope as railings, paired with wooden treads and handrails, which look quite nice. This type of trendy and somewhat nature-reverting decorative stair is inexpensive and remains fashionable.
