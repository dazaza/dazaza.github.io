"Longing for Each Other" - A Modern Woman Travels Through the Chaotic Tang Dynasty

by jqrxass on 2009-09-06 12:40:37

She is an ordinary woman named Lu Wanwan in this life, and a talented tune composer named Lu Wanyin in her past life in the Tang Dynasty.

She is the song fairy of the Tang Dynasty, Xu Hezi, whose songs on the tower could captivate the clouds with her enchanting voice.

How could they have foreseen the coldness of future events? Everything was just beyond their control.

Suddenly one day, they realized that no matter whether it's this life or the past life, what they sought was nothing more than a place to rest...

Chapter One: Dreaming of Jiangnan

Yes, it was this pipa (Chinese lute) that brought me to my past life - as Lu Wanyin at the age of fourteen, merely a newly arrived palace attendant in the Yunshao Institute. I hadn't yet had the chance to see that rosewood mother-of-pearl inlay five-string pipa.

At that time, I roughly combed my hair into two buns, wore a thin blue shirt, tightly hugged the bundle in my arms, and knelt together with many girls of similar age before the steps of the Imperial Music Bureau. First, the female officials from the Shangyi Bureau checked our appearances one by one: if there were any flaws on our skin, any problems with our facial features, or if our fingers were too short or thick. Then the female officials from the Shanggong Bureau led us behind the curtain to inspect our bodies, checking if we were as pure as jade.

Afterward, the music instructors from the Teaching Institute assessed our talents and selected those who could be molded. I chose the pipa, a white wooden practice instrument.

Chapter Two: Washing the Silk Gauze

***I remember that day when I seemed to have combed my hair into a cloud-like bun, adorned with a gold butterfly step-shaking ornament with mandarin ducks and lotus patterns, with a green jade mark on my forehead and flower patches on both cheeks. Probably reflecting the shimmering light in the room, I might have been somewhat charming, so when Prince Yong saw me fall to the ground, he pulled me into his arms without waiting for me to apologize.

Just a pair of hands sliding down my neck to my chest. The musicians' clothes are mostly made of light silk, so it was very convenient for him to lift them up. I didn't want to imagine what was about to happen next, so I just closed my eyes, bit my lips, and used the chill spreading from my heart to my skin as a form of resistance. Soon, a hand slipped inside the embroidered corset, probing left and right.

That hand was indeed much hotter than my body.

Chapter Three: Bodhisattva Barbarian

Zhao Ling suddenly grabbed both of my hands. I was startled and looked straight into his gaunt face, his eyes deep like a pool, his features sharp as if carved with a knife, and between his beard already showed signs of hidden melancholy, such a hardworking face. But I clearly remembered what the maids in the mansion said, that the master had just passed his thirties and had no children yet. In these few weeks living peacefully in the Zhan Affairs Office, although Zhao Ling treated me extremely coldly, he never showed any disrespect or insult, and naturally, I felt some gratitude and respect for him.

So when he made such a move at this moment, I didn't resist much, only slightly surprised, why wasn't there even a hint of joy for the New Year in his eyes?

Chapter Four: Pound the Practice

I don't know whether it was the rain or tears that soaked my eyes, and I desperately called out: "Your servant has sinned! Sir, please go back..." While saying this, I dodged his whip, hitting my forehead against the ground, once, then again, hard, so hard.

He angrily threw the whip and kicked me with his foot. For a moment, our gazes met, and we both froze. His eyes quickly flickered, and he turned away. Leaving me lying in the rain with the blood-stained whip.

"Help her inside." He left me with a lonely back figure.

Then the maids came forward to support me, and I barely managed a few steps, but my body couldn't bear the weight, and I fell heavily to the ground again.

Chapter Five: Small Mountain Repeated

But finally, I smiled faintly: "Sir, may you seek much happiness on your journey, and may the Buddha protect you. Your servant... will not go."

There was still a glimmer of hope in his eyes, and suddenly he whispered: "Don't you want to be Wanyin again and follow me to admire the moon over Kunchi?"

My whole body couldn't help but tremble lightly, my lips stammered, but I still replied with four words: "Sir, take care."

I never expected that such a plain person would suddenly burst out.

He suddenly grabbed both of my hands tightly, almost crushing every bone in my body —— my whole body couldn't suppress violent trembling, pain in my eyes, and I kept muttering: "Please don't do this..."
