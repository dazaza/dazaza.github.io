Exclusive disclosure by brokers in the circle: Zhao Wei's wedding to Long Ge is fixed

by sea0gate6 on 2009-12-03 10:52:31

There has never been an actress in China like Zhao Wei, who became a superstar for ten years based solely on a Qiong Yao drama; nor has there been an actress who, when you thought she would always be Xiao Yanzi, gave a stunning transformation performance in "Painted Skin." She always brings us unexpected surprises that are hard to fathom, just as you couldn't imagine her openly discussing "thong underwear," "wet dreams," and "courtesy sex" in the media. This year, Zhao Wei is set to bring two highly anticipated period blockbusters, "Mulan" and "Brocade Warrior," and amidst this whirlwind of attention, her wedding date has also been revealed. Despite three rumored engagements within the year, it's said that she will officially marry mainland tycoon Huang Youlong next year. During our exclusive interview, although Zhao Wei refused to comment on her marriage, she did not deny being in a relationship. You never know what kind of surprise she might bring in the next moment.

Interview and writing by reporter Qiuzhilǐ, photography by reporter Shaoyin

Revealing the identity of Long Ge

Name: Huang Youlong

Net worth: As high as 400 million yuan, owns multiple properties in mainland China, reportedly has a private jet.

Company name: Shenzhen Dongrunda Investment Development Co., Ltd.

Address: Room 2901, International Culture Building, No. 3039 Shenan Zhong Road, Shenzhen

Registered capital: 10 million yuan

Enterprise: Guangzhou Golden Leaf Resort Hotel (now renamed Sanzheng Half Mountain Hot Spring Resort Hotel)

Romantic history: Dated Miss Hong Kong contestant Ye Cuicui, rumored to have a 9-year-old son.

In September last year, after the end of the movie promotion, Huang Youlong drove a Mercedes-Benz to pick up and drop off Zhao Wei. A rather tipsy Zhao Wei crossed the road with Huang Youlong closely following behind.

After dinner, they went to sing at KTV all night. The already slightly drunk Zhao Wei kept affectionately holding Long Ge's hand.

In June this year, Zhao Wei attended the Shanghai International Film Festival carrying a Hermes bag, which was criticized as being mismatched with her outfit.

Since her breakup with Wang Yu, the son of Wang Daohan, apart from a somewhat mysterious relationship with national table tennis player Wang Liqin, Zhao Wei’s emotional life in front of the media was almost blank. However, this void has now been filled. Hong Kong media reported that Zhao Wei secretly married mainland real estate tycoon Huang Youlong in Singapore as early as August this year. Moreover, Ye Cuicui, Huang Youlong's previous partner and a former Miss Hong Kong contestant, came out last week to talk about their past romance, further corroborating Zhao Wei and Huang Youlong's relationship. A friend of a top-tier celebrity's agent privately told the reporter that according to their understanding, Zhao Wei and Huang Youlong are not yet married but have a stable relationship and have already set a wedding date.

Huang Youlong is over thirty, unmarried, rumored to have a 9-year-old son, and is known as "Long Ge." Starting with real estate, his business spans widely, including operating resorts in Shenzhen and Guangzhou. It's also known that he runs a cigarette factory in Macau and owns a private jet worth hundreds of millions. His businesses involve real estate development and management, financial investment management, building materials marketing, automobile trade, and tourism, among others. He is currently vigorously developing the hotel industry. Friends of Huang Youlong revealed that to pursue Zhao Wei, Long Ge gifted more than ten Hermes bags, Vertu diamond mobile phones, and Richard Mille diamond watches to his beloved, totaling over tens of millions of yuan.

Although many people in the circle knew about Zhao Wei and Huang Youlong's romance, the two were rarely seen together. The only time was in September last year, when Zhao Wei was photographed having dinner with a man in a restaurant after the promotional event for the movie "Painted Skin." Later, the two went to a KTV to sing all night. Due to her intoxication, she walked while holding the man's hand closely, and it turned out to be Huang Youlong. At the end of last year, cabin crew members witnessed Zhao Wei holding hands with a gentleman at the airport VIP lounge, heading to Dubai in the UAE. The gentleman's initials were HYL, and he held a 200,000-yuan Vertu phone. In May this year, another fan spotted them traveling discreetly at Beijing Airport, heading to Paris, France, where Zhao Wei was going to promote "Mulan" at Cannes.

Recently, Hong Kong media not only revealed Zhao Wei and Huang Youlong's secret marriage but also brought out Huang's ex-girlfriend Ye Cuicui to recount old feelings, even claiming that Zhao Wei and Huang Youlong met during the introduction by Ye Cuicui last year during the Olympics. Ye Cuicui, in an interview with Ming Pao Weekly, admitted for the first time that she indeed had a brief relationship with Huang Youlong between April and May last year, which ended shortly, leaving her in tears daily. However, Ye Cuicui did not reveal whether Zhao Wei was the third party, saying, "My relationship with him is over. These are Zhao Wei's private matters, I hope everyone gives her some space."

With Huang Youlong's business acumen, Ye Cuicui once jointly invested in a mainland real estate project with him, making a profit of millions. Now, Zhao Wei has surprisingly become very open about investments, with reports indicating that Zhao Wei and Huang Youlong jointly invested in opening several restaurants. This interview for "Mulan" was arranged in a restaurant in Sanlitun, Beijing, where although Zhao Wei publicly claimed to be only a nominal shareholder, the restaurant manager confirmed that Zhao Wei is one of the three shareholders with actual shares. Since the opening, Zhao Wei has been actively managing the business every day, or bringing family and friends to dine, repeatedly recommending the restaurant on Weibo, even saying, "My current ideal is to do business, hoping to become a billionaire in the second half of my life."

During our exclusive interview, Zhao Wei candidly discussed her wet dreams and called herself innocent.

Zhao Wei: I don't know what my bottom line is.

Zhao Wei stated that the character "Mulan" is an expression and release of various emotions accumulated over her 12-year acting career.

At the press conference for "Mulan," Zhao Wei wielded swords and fought alongside Fang Zuming.

"Mulan" dressed as a man

"I'm too 'manly,' I'm afraid no one will marry me"

Without a doubt, "Mulan" is Zhao Wei's most anticipated work in recent years. Apart from the well-known subject matter, Zhao Wei's portrayal of Mulan fighting on the battlefield in male attire is also a major highlight. In recent years, Zhao Wei has successfully transitioned from a flower maiden to a mature actress. Once again, she dons military garb, fully immersing herself in the role of a man, even shedding tears while watching the film.

Nandu Entertainment: Did you cry watching "Mulan"?

Zhao Wei: I rarely cry when watching my own films, it's been a long time since I last cried because I feel it's quite embarrassing to cry over my own performances. But I cried several times watching "Mulan" because the plot was extremely touching. For a woman to go to war is a tragic and cruel experience. Myself, Chen Kun, and the entire team often faced dire situations during filming. People struggling for survival are always pitiful, so many scenes in the movie deeply moved me.

Nandu Entertainment: Was the toughest part of shooting "Mulan" the harsh environment or the action scenes?

Zhao Wei: Both the environment and the action scenes were tough. In my scenes, I was constantly riding horses and fighting. Although only a third or a quarter of these fight scenes appear in the film, it often took two weeks to shoot a scene lasting just two or three minutes.

Nandu Entertainment: Were your fight scenes on horseback or dismounted?

Zhao Wei: All my scenes were on horseback. I controlled the horse with one hand and fought with weapons in the other. This was a basic skill required for the character. After practicing for about half a month, I developed great harmony with the horse. Fang Zuming and Chen Kun, riding horses, couldn't keep up with me.

The media's questions remain sensitive and gossipy, but Zhao Wei's responses have become increasingly relaxed, even adding humor and self-deprecation. Turning thirty without a marriage prospect is truly terrifying. Recently, Zhao Wei's image has received much praise, and her mood seems increasingly cheerful, seemingly due to the happiness brought by love.

Nandu Entertainment: Have you personally chosen your outfits recently, which have been well-received?

Zhao Wei: Yes, I select and buy my clothes myself. In the past, I was too lazy, and everyone kept criticizing me, which made me angry. After getting angry, I started shopping myself.

Nandu Entertainment: Do you pay attention to those criticisms?

Zhao Wei: Not only criticism, fans also remind me to take care of my skin, suggesting eyelid surgery, giving me many kind pieces of advice. Of course, I know people criticize my clothing choices. In my own words, it's "ignoring the dignity of the red carpet," but I really didn't take it seriously. According to my thinking, wearing jeans and a white shirt on the red carpet is perfectly fine. Why must actresses be turned into little dolls on top of cakes? It's unbearable. But later, I couldn't escape convention either and joined everyone else in becoming a doll.

Nandu Entertainment: Are you becoming more concerned about your appearance?

Zhao Wei: Yes, I've become more interested in looking good compared to before. After being reminded by everyone, I realized I should start paying attention to this issue. After focusing on it, I found that my aesthetic isn't wrong, I was just too lazy before. From childhood, I believed that substance is the most important thing, and external packaging and interpersonal relationships didn't matter. Now, I also realize that an artist's image does need attention. We should present a good, beautiful image to others and strive to give the audience visual enjoyment.

Nandu Entertainment: This year, there have been three rumors of engagement, all involving the same person, nicknamed Long Ge?

Zhao Wei: I don't want to participate in any hype, it's none of my business.

Nandu Entertainment: So, are you married?

Zhao Wei: I won't answer such questions. I've answered them many times already. If I don't respond, it reduces the number of news stories.

Nandu Entertainment: Didn't you recently publicly mention someone special, saying you'd get married within two years?

Zhao Wei: Maybe not. Who knows? If I were a spokesperson for the news, society would be pretty dull. Don't take my words too seriously.

Nandu Entertainment: Are you in a relationship?

Zhao Wei: I won't answer that.

The Zhao Wei you don't know

(Excerpt from Glamour magazine, December 2009 issue)

M.C.: There's a term now called "courtesy sex." For example, if there's only a man and a woman in a room, it seems like not having sex implies finding the other person unattractive. What do you think about this?

Zhao Wei: That's true gender equality then, at least there's no longer the notion of taking advantage of each other. Traditionally, sexual relations between men and women meant the woman, regardless of whether she enjoyed it or not, was "taken advantage of." Nowadays, having sex with someone is a compliment to their charm.

M.C.: Do you remember a vivid wet dream?

Zhao Wei: Believe it or not, I've had several recently! I never used to have such dreams. Suddenly, I dream about someone I don't particularly care about, someone completely impossible in reality, and something happens between us. The feeling in the dream is very strange and interesting, constantly asking myself, "Why shouldn't it happen?" In the dream, I pondered and worried a lot, unable to let loose.

M.C.: What specifically happened?

Zhao Wei: It was about staying in the same room with a member of the opposite sex, two single beds, nothing happened for three days, still very polite and proper gentlemen. On the third day, I desired something to happen, but when it did, I felt it shouldn't. That person eventually broke down and asked me, "How can you do this?" Ultimately, morality killed all interest (laughs).

M.C.: Have you worn thong underwear?

Zhao Wei: Of course! Don't treat me like an antique! But it was all for clothing, to avoid showing panty lines, not affecting the look of the clothes, and not for any man.
