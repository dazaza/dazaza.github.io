Life in this world is nourished by the five grains and mixed with joys and sorrows.

Publication Time: 2011-2-28 13:18:05 Source of the Article: Internet Author of the Article: admin Number of Views: 4

Life in this world is nourished by the five grains and mixed with joys and sorrows on the Qing network, experiencing sadness and empathy. Truly, one endures the mixture of joy and sorrow in human life, thus it is impossible not to fall ill. There cannot be a person without illness.

Since most people have illnesses, how should we view "illness"? I believe that illness should be rationally approached. Taoism is China's national religion, and one of its principles is to seek balance between yin and yang. If yin and yang energies are balanced within the human body, one will not fall ill. Once this balance is lost, one becomes sick. This means imbalance between yin and yang leads to illness. The result may be an excess of yang over yin or vice versa. Regardless of the disease, it is caused by an imbalance of yin and yang leading to blocked qi and blood. Conversely, illness helps maintain the balance of yin and yang because they are also forms of energy. Since they are forms of energy, changes occur, which cause illness. When yin and yang are imbalanced, the body must release the excess energy. How does the body release this energy? It releases it through illness. We might notice that some very robust individuals, glowing with health and vitality, suddenly pass away or succumb to illness very quickly. On the other hand, there are those who are often sick for years, some even bedridden but do not die. Why is this? Answer: Because unhealthy people, who are chronically ill, release energy through their illnesses and achieve yin-yang balance through treatment, promoting recovery. Those seemingly healthy individuals who suddenly die do so because the sudden imbalance of yin and yang catches them unprepared. Thus, illness can help balance the energy of yin and yang, which is a good thing. Those who are physically strong should not be overly complacent because they are always at risk of getting sick. Therefore, being free of illness itself can be considered a kind of sickness.

Having illness is absolute, being free of illness is relative. Once someone falls ill, there is no need to fear. What should be feared are those who have never been sick, for once they fall ill, it could be incurable.
