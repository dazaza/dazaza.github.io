Still selling for 610 yuan? Nokia Asha 501 official website appearance

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 17:42:17

Yesterday, Nokia launched a low-end smartphone with a strong Lumia style in India - the Asha 501. Its price of only 610 yuan and its attractive appearance gave many fans new hope. Now, this affordable marvel has appeared on Nokia's official Chinese website.The domestic version of the Nokia Asha 501 supports dual SIM cards and Wi-Fi network connectivity, and it boasts an ultra-long standby time (still 48 days?). Consistent with the international version, the device comes with a variety of colorful back covers and features a smarter, faster new UI.The Asha 501 will be equipped with a 3-inch capacitive touchscreen with a resolution of 320x240 covered by scratch-resistant glass. It offers 4GB of storage space and supports up to 32GB expansion, featuring a 3.2-megapixel main camera (no front camera).The exact price of the domestic version of the Asha 501 is still unclear. However, based on previous Asha releases, the domestic version of the 501 should not differ much from the international version, and is expected to be around 650 yuan.Below is a summary of the interview:Q: Why use a capacitive screen on such an affordable phone?A: To provide more people with a high-end experience. On the Asha 501, the capacitive screen can offer a better user experience. We considered many factors and made adjustments to both software and hardware to ensure that performance is not compromised.Q: What are the differences between the Asha 501 and cheap Android phones? Does it support multitasking?A: The Asha 501 does not use a high-end operating system, nor is it designed for high-end devices, so it has been streamlined to run on low-spec hardware. The key lies in the experience. The Asha 501's experience is optimized for its target market, hardware, and price point.The Asha 501 supports multitasking. Multiple tasks such as Twitter notifications and Fastlane updates can run in the background. The focus of the Asha 501 is visual multitasking, which is reflected in the Fastlane feature where you can access recently used apps and control your preferences. Just swipe to switch to Fastlane.Q: If the Asha 501 is meant to blur the lines between feature phones and smartphones as a transition, would users face a completely different experience when they upgrade to the Nokia Lumia? Would using the N9 for this transition be better?A: Nokia plans to introduce the Asha 501 to the market to unify service experiences across various devices and bring some of Nokia's qualities to budget products.The market for the Asha 501 is clearly different from that of the Lumia, so the features of this product reflect the needs of its target users. Durability is an important factor, which is what Nokia is most famous for. Lastly, imaging. Nokia has always been working on developing imaging technology, and even at this price point, the Asha 501 features a 3.2-megapixel camera.Someone asked: Why not just make the Lumia cheaper? The answer is simple: we designed the Asha 501 to deliver an excellent experience on low-spec hardware at an extremely low price. The Lumia represents Nokia's future. If we were to bring this experience to low-end devices, similar to low-end Android phones, the experience would be very poor, which could harm the brand image. Nokia absolutely does not want that.The core experience of the Asha 501 is the same as that of the Lumia. There is a home screen, and you can view a list of installed apps. Simply swipe to see an updated list, from app information to notifications and schedules. On the Asha, all of this is achieved through Fastlane, while on the Lumia, it is realized through live tiles. The same concept, presented differently.Q: What role do functions borrowed from smartphones play in Asha updates?A: Smartphones are the core. The entire platform aims to bring high-end experiences to low-cost devices, and functions borrowed from smartphones provide a framework for this. Nokia can take elements and concepts first seen in the N9 and reintroduce them to the market efficiently. The Asha 501 is highly efficient and can last up to 48 days on standby.Q: Why choose a fixed-focus lens?A: When consumers buy a phone at this price point, they are not picky about the camera. Therefore, choosing a fixed-focus lens over an auto-focus lens is to achieve a balance between quality and price.
