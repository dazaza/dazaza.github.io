Cherish life

That summer, she, Xiao Si, 16 years old, had just graduated from junior high school. Due to her disappointment in the high school entrance exam and her parents' lack of understanding, she began to give up on herself, started to play with life, started to be tired of living, and started to become decadent. Every day, she hung out with kids outside. With them, she learned to smoke, drink, fight, take drugs, and develop all sorts of bad habits. Even her originally black long hair changed along with her; it became uneven and colorful. She also got her nose and mouth pierced. In her view, there was nothing left in this world worth her attachment, and no one would still yearn for her. Thus, she continued to become more decadent.

One night in July, when she and a few so-called good buddies came out of a bar drunk, they met Xiao Yue, the class monitor and the person she loved. Xiao Yue recognized her from afar: that girl holding a wine bottle, dressed strangely, walking unsteadily and barely standing was her. Seeing her with a group of hoodlums made Xiao Yue very worried, heartbroken, and guilty. He wanted to try to bring her back on the right path, to let her return to her original life. He quickly walked to Xiao Si's side and softly said, "Xiao Si, you're drunk. Do you need me to send you home?" Xiao Si laughed and coldly replied, "Who are you? What qualifications do you have to meddle with your sister! Little brother, go play somewhere else!" Later, those hoodlums around her also joined in and drove him away, not allowing him to approach Xiao Si. However, he persisted and said, "Xiao Si, you said you were afraid of the dark. Let me send you home!" Upon hearing these words, Xiao Si inexplicably agreed.

They walked silently on a quiet road, with no passersby on the roadside. The dark night only had a few weak beams of light coming from others' rooms. It was very quiet, very quiet. On the way, neither of them spoke first. The boy just silently followed behind the girl, looking at her weary and exhausted silhouette, feeling very heartbroken. The road wasn't long, but they walked for a very long time. Finally, the girl arrived home. When she was about to go in, the boy finally spoke. He said to the girl, "Xiao Si, can you stop being like this? Even if it's for me, please don't continue down this path, alright?" The girl paused for a moment, then smiled and replied, "Do you know who you are? For you? That's laughable! Who is there for me? Isn't it what you said, we have no relationship?"

That happened a month ago, on June 1st, which was the girl's birthday. The girl was very happy because she received many gifts and felt very satisfied. She thought this 16th birthday was meaningful. But she felt something was missing because she liked their class monitor, Xiao Yue. However, Xiao Yue seemed indifferent to all girls. She wasn't sure how Xiao Yue felt about her and hoped Xiao Yue also liked her.

She really wanted to know how Xiao Yue felt about her, so after school, she slipped him a note that read: "Xiao Yue, come to the playground after evening study session. I have something to tell you, and I'll wait for you." Xiao Yue went to the playground as scheduled, but when Xiao Si confessed her feelings, Xiao Yue rejected her and said some harsh words: "You say you like me, but can you tell me why or what you like about me? What kind of relationship do we have?"

This was an unexpected response for Xiao Si. She was stunned and didn't know how to answer, so she chose silence. Xiao Yue continued, "So, you don't know, right? I know. We have no relationship beyond being classmates. There's nothing between us." These words completely shattered Xiao Si's heart. She cried and quietly left the playground alone. Her departing figure looked so lonely and sad in the darkness. But she didn't know that after she left, Xiao Yue also cried.

After crying, Xiao Yue prepared to leave and go home. But in the dark corner of the playground, amidst the pitch-black night, he heard a girl crying. He knew that the girl hiding in the corner crying was Xiao Si. He walked over and said to her, "Are you okay? It's not safe for a girl to go home so late. Let me send you home!" Xiao Si didn't speak, but that night, he still sent her home. That night's scene was the same as tonight's, and so was their silence on the way. Along the way, Xiao Si only said one sentence: "Do you know? I'm very afraid of the dark. I wish... I could always..."

Before finishing her sentence, Xiao Si cried again. Xiao Yue understood her meaning: she wanted him to always send her home. Xiao Yue deeply regretted saying such harsh words, regretted speaking those insincere words. Actually, he had always loved Xiao Si too, and his feelings had never changed. He said those harsh words because he hoped Xiao Si would focus all her energy on studying. The high school entrance exam was coming up, and he hoped Xiao Si could get into a good school.

But unexpectedly, things went contrary to his wishes. Starting from that day, Xiao Si never focused on studying again. During class, she often daydreamed. During this period, Xiao Yue wanted to explain to Xiao Si, but whenever Xiao Yue approached, Xiao Si would sneak away like a mouse seeing a cat, not giving him a chance to explain or even talk. Later, the scene described at the beginning occurred: Xiao Si failed the high school entrance exam and began her decadent life.

That night, he couldn't sleep, tossing and turning all night. He thought for a long time and about many things. He loved her and didn't want to see his beloved live a decadent life forever. He was thinking of a way to bring Xiao Si back to where she started, back to her original life. He stared at the crescent moon in the dark night, unable to see its glow, only feeling its chill.

The next morning, Xiao Yue was already waiting at the crossroads Xiao Si passed every day, holding roses. After waiting for a long time, the person he loved finally arrived. Blushing, Xiao Yue walked towards her, handed her the roses in his hands, and confessed his love. Xiao Si couldn't believe what he was saying. In her eyes, he only liked her now because she was hanging out with hoodlums and being decadent. He was only sympathizing with her and pitying her.

She didn't want anyone's sympathy and forcefully threw the bright red rose to the ground, then raised her head and walked away. At that crossroads, apart from the flowing vehicles, only he was left standing alone by the roadside. He also experienced the taste of rejection, which was truly unbearable. His heart hurt, indescribably painful. He began to understand her bitterness and therefore didn't want to give up. He silently told himself, "I must keep trying."

Every day since then, he gave her roses. Every day, he kept following her. When she went to the bar to drink, he drank for her. When she smoked, he snatched the cigarette from her hand and threw it away. When she fought, he silently watched beside her because he knew he couldn't persuade her. But if someone hit her, he would immediately rush forward to protect her, using his body to shield her so she wouldn't be hurt. Wherever she was and whatever she did, there was always his shadow. Time passed slowly, unchanged.

One day in August, the girl went out to fight again. The boy still followed her. This fight was different from usual. Before, when they went out to fight, only a few people carried knives. This time, everyone had one, and he was given one too. The blade was very sharp, and the knife was heavy, but his heart was heavier. He tried to stop the girl from going, but she wouldn't listen to him.

The fight began. He stood dumbfounded in the corner, extremely scared because this time was really different from before. This time, they were risking their lives. One by one, they charged with real weapons. Soon, blood appeared on the ground. Many people were injured. He saw blood streaming from many people's wrists, faces, foreheads, arms, abdomens, and legs. But they wouldn't stop fighting, determined to either kill or be killed. The girl was also injured, with fresh red blood dripping from her wrist, yet she continued to fight alongside the hoodlums. He couldn't bear it anymore. He, who had never fought before, lifted the heavy knife in his hand and rushed to the girl's side to help her fight. Later, he noticed someone behind the girl preparing to attack her. The knife gleamed with white light. He tried to shout to warn her to look back, but she didn't listen. He had no choice as the attacker moved closer. In a flash, the boy rushed forward and pushed the girl aside. The girl fell to the ground, her arm bruised and bleeding. But when she got up and turned to scold the boy, she was speechless. She was shocked by what she saw - the boy lying in a pool of blood, a red knife piercing his chest, the tip still dripping blood. The girl screamed "Help!" hysterically, but everyone ran away upon seeing the situation.

The girl cried again, sobbing uncontrollably. The boy weakly raised his right hand to wipe away the tears on the girl's face and said to her, "Xiao Si, please don't cry, okay? I really love you, and I always have. My love for you has never changed. If I'm not here anymore, take good care of yourself. Pay attention every single day. Everyone loves you. Don't cry. I like your smile..."

Before he could finish speaking, Xiao Yue passed away. The girl didn't cry; she kept smiling, kept smiling. Because the boy said he liked her smile.

On the day of the boy's funeral, the girl didn't attend. She dressed up beautifully and came to the crossroads where he first gave her flowers. Facing the passing vehicles, she said a long passage: "Dear, I've come. I'm used to you always walking behind me. Without you, I'm not happy, I'm very sad. This time, I won't let you walk behind me anymore. This time, we will hold hands and walk shoulder to shoulder together. I've come, wait for me."

When people found the girl, she was already lying at the crossroads, just like when the boy died, covered in blood. But the corners of the girl's mouth were always raised. She kept smiling. She left with a smile because the boy said he liked her smile, so she wanted to find him with a smile.

Why does love always end so tragically? Why do lovers always part ways? It's because when we love, we don't know how to cherish. If you love him (her), let him (her) know. If you love him (her), be nice to him (her). If you love him (her), don't hurt him (her). Not every story has a perfect ending, but every story can be made beautiful. Cherish the one you love, and let them know. Treasure what you have now, and make your love more beautiful.
