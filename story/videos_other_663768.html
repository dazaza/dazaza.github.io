Psychology expert: "Acting young" for stress relief must be moderate "Compared to binge drinking and violent venting, returning to childhood can be a healthy and feasible way to relieve stress, but it's best not to get too absorbed in this role." Regarding the stress-relief method of white-collar netizens "acting young," renowned psychology expert Zhu Qiang, director of the psychological counseling center at the Hunan Provincial Armed Police General Hospital, pointed out that it reflects a mentality of escaping from real-world conflicts, similar to how many office workers now like to suck on baby bottles or wear children's clothes. If one gets addicted to the child role they are playing, developing a dependency, it won't help with psychological maturity. Netizens might consider this as occasional "psychological massage" rather than a life norm, which would be more beneficial for healthy stress relief.
