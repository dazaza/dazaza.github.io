Some ideas for making money as a website owner: diligence plus accurate positioning.

by nilaima588 on 2010-03-25 14:42:28

One of my friends studied SEO with me in June 08. After mastering SEO, he started his own business, while I continued to work for the company as the manager of the technology department due to work reasons. A few months later, he told me that his website had started making money and his monthly income had already exceeded what I earned from working. My salary at the time was a base salary plus commission, totaling around 4,000 yuan per month. I found it strange how he could make money so quickly through his website? I've also seen many webmasters online who have been running their websites for two or three years, even four or five years, but still haven't made much money.

I think many friends are probably like me, wanting to understand how he managed to earn more than 4,000 yuan per month within just a few months by simply creating websites. The other day when we were chatting, he mentioned that he planned to spend tens of thousands of yuan on a large portal site. The other day, he also mentioned wanting to buy a car. I believe that after more than a year, he has at least made over 100,000 yuan.

Getting back to the main point, based on his actual case, I will tell everyone why most individual webmasters don’t make money, and why even if they do, they only make small amounts through affiliate programs.

First, I want to talk about the old topic: mindset. As the saying goes, "Mindset determines outcome." However, I believe that among the more than 3 million individual webmasters in China, most are not any less intelligent than anyone else. Everyone must have thought of some good ideas that can indeed make money, but why is it that in reality, they haven't made money? Why is it that some people can make several thousand or even tens of thousands of yuan per month from running websites, while others only make a few dozen yuan and work themselves to death?

I want to ask everyone a few questions:

1. How much time do we spend each day on planning and maintaining our websites?

2. Since we started making websites, how many websites have we created, and how many of them have we truly maintained with daily updates?

3. How much of our website content have we carefully edited and kept original?

4. Do we add enough quality external links to our websites every day?

5. Are we tirelessly searching for articles that can be referenced on our websites every day?

6. Are we really thinking every day about how our websites can make money and actively testing different methods?

Mr. Kazuo Inamori, one of Japan's renowned entrepreneurs and one of the "Four Saints of Management," wrote in his book that the reason he was able to develop a product in a research institute that was about to go bankrupt, which even large global corporations couldn't produce at the time, was because he fully devoted himself to his work. During the toughest times, he even brought his personal toiletries to the company until he achieved results. If you want to make achievements in a certain field, "effort" is something you must put in.

As for the friend I mentioned earlier, do you know how many websites he currently has? When we chatted yesterday, he was embarrassed to say all of them, only mentioning that he has over ten. I believe he has far more than ten websites. Moreover, he updates them every day. Of course, now he has hired employees, so he has become more relaxed. But before he hired employees, he said he sometimes worked until midnight and still got up early the next morning to continue. It was this kind of dedication that allowed him to transform from an employee earning a few thousand yuan per month into an entrepreneur earning tens of thousands per month after just a year and a half. He continues to grow, and I believe that by next year, he might even be driving a BMW.

Some friends might say, "I know that you need to work hard and put in effort, and I want to do it too, but I need direction. I don't know where to start." Taking the first step is the hardest, and we all know that. This is why in my previous two articles, I always emphasized the importance of positioning first. I completely understand everyone's feelings, especially those who are new. Without a good teacher to guide you, it’s easy to get confused. The reason my friend and I have achieved a little bit of success in our respective fields is because we both did some business before learning about networks and went through years of working for others. After gaining more knowledge and experience, when we turned back to the internet, we found our perspectives broadened significantly.

Therefore, my suggestion to new friends is that if you're still working, try to do your best in your current job and use your free time to create websites or learn about making money online. Of course, I understand that it can be tiring to explore and learn on your own, but for tomorrow's success, I hope everyone can put in enough effort.
