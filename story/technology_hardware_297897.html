The Hong Kong people may not fully comprehend the abstract concept of revolutionary faith. In their understanding, the little people in chaotic times, such as peddlers and porters, using their lives to protect a stranger who is a revolutionary, shouting a few revolutionary slogans is definitely not enough. Therefore, during the creation process, they found reasonable secular motivations for each character. Compared with some main melody films, their motivations are more credible, down-to-earth, and their sacrifices more touching. On the contrary, Liang Jiahui, as the revolutionary, due to the lack of secular motivation, his every move appears pedantic as an idealist.

After leaving the cinema, I met Wei Junzi, and we both couldn't help but marvel at how good the drama was. It was so good that, despite our lifelong love for martial arts films, we actually felt the action scenes were too long. Donnie Yen's fighting was spectacular enough, but since this scene disrupted the overall rhythm, we wished we could fast forward through it. In my movie watching experience, this situation happened for the first time.

By the end of the film, I cried three times. As a screenwriter, a movie that makes me completely engrossed and forget about technical analysis is the best movie. EveryoneMovies, we look forward to seeing better Hong Kong films next year, and we look forward to "Ip Man 2".

It must be said, "Bodyguards and Assassins" is the best Chinese language film I've seen this year. In recent years, films like "Ip Man", "Painted Skin", "The Warlords", and now "Bodyguards and Assassins", Hong Kong filmmakers have become increasingly familiar with the tastes of mainland audiences, to the point where there is almost no difference between them. The style is concise and grand, and the production quality is excellent. Apart from the last segment featuring Donnie Yen's fight scene, "Bodyguards and Assassins" has almost lost the flavor of Hong Kong films. This kind of evaluation, whether it's good or bad for Hong Kong filmmakers, remains to be seen.

The righteous merchant responsible for receiving him knew that a group of assassins sent by the Qing government were planning an assassination, so he immediately hired a group of highly skilled bodyguards to escort him, successfully sending Sun Yat-sen back to Guangzhou safely.
