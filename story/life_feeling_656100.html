That bleak and sorrowful autumn

by lili1 on 2012-01-06 21:25:59

That faint concern, that ambiguity at the corners of the eyes, those intentional and unintentional misunderstandings. They cover up the heart that had already become sad and lonely years ago when the beloved person left. As the longing gradually fades away and stops, it loses the ability to express love.

Reciting the same poem, singing the same song, playing the same game, stepping on the mountain where the sun meets the fiery clouds, the gentle light slants and shines on the birch forest paradise. Each beam of light, like mischievous children, forcefully penetrates into this joyful world. We walk among the trees, as if we were on a star-studded avenue, with many mischievous children shaking their glow sticks around me. Suddenly, a gust of wind blows, and the leaves "rustle" in applause. Is it welcoming us? I enjoy the beautiful and tempting scene, breathing in the lively and cheerful atmosphere, and stay on this island like the sound that makes sailors lose their minds. This is the last perfect feast of the beauty of autumn, the final celebration before all the splendor fades away.

I am still thinking, not missing those days, would you remember our time? Would you still cry for me? Would you still laugh for me? Do you still have me in your heart? You can never go back, being your friend is worthwhile. It's always been me listening to you talk, could you be the listener this time? In fact, I have always followed your wishes as my own. In a broken family, an accident made it even more desolate, bleak, and incomplete. At school, due to some misunderstandings, I was abandoned by everyone. My world fell apart overnight. I shrank into a dark corner, unable to see the dawn's light, not even a speck of it. Just when I was in great pain and deep thought, someone came into my world. He opened the skylight for me, and suddenly my world was bathed in sunlight. I clearly saw this face, which has since been deeply imprinted in my heart, becoming an unforgettable memory, witnessing our friendship. If you don't turn away, I won't walk ahead of you, nor do I want you to be hurt in any way. If you are willing to continue this friendship, I will accompany you until the end, giving everything I have, until there is no way to stop your departure.

Do you know? When she left, I felt the helplessness in her eyes that couldn't be concealed, but I let her go without trying to hold her back or be with her. Is this what you wanted to see? But things have changed, and eventually, I never saw her again. You said you liked her, and even if you didn't say it, I understood. And I knew everything, but I just didn't want to say it. I willingly played the role of a facilitator, promoting the fusion between you two, urging you to hint to her. But things went wrong, the more I tried to fix it, the worse it got. Misunderstandings and wrong impressions ended up landing on me. At that moment, my brain went blank, and I let things take their natural course. It seemed like fate was deliberately teasing us. We were tricked, and in the blink of an eye, everything was missed. Once missed, it was all destroyed.

Years ago, there was someone who really liked you. You might have forgotten, but I remember. That person was crazy about you, unwilling to give up until the very end. But you never noticed her, like sediment filtered out by your retina, leaving no impression. I read her heart through her eyes, those big watery eyes that only had you in them. Every move, every smile, every word from you would make her excited for half a day, her red cheeks looking like happiness in a blood-red romance. But these things you never saw. Besides sighing regretfully, I could only admire her stubbornness and determination.

My feelings for her seem to have risen to another level. Meeting her makes me jump around like a little deer. Perhaps this is what we call unrequited love! But I didn't dare to confess because her heart was filled with you. I watched her leave with my own eyes, her back growing thinner because of you, gradually disappearing from my sight until she vanished at the horizon. I still gazed greedily, waiting for a miracle, waiting for her to turn around. But it would never come. Disappointed, I turned back and buried myself in a sea of problems to consume my longing for her until I exhausted all my passion. What else could I do?

Looking up at the sky at a 45-degree angle, tears streaming down my face, sorrow and loneliness are not my flaws, happiness and joy I am not lacking. It's just that I've gotten used to and fallen in love with this melancholy rhythm. Maybe one day I'll inexplicably want to escape, but my heart is still frozen in September - that tragic and sentimental autumn.

Those who have left have gone, those who remain have stayed, and for what I've lost and forgotten, I will try my best to make up for it. Looking back on past hurts, I will sing the joys and sorrows of life, and once again sing Han Han's "Elopement":

The beginning of a beautiful story / The tragedy is counting down / Flying as usual / Not wanting to stop / The cold rain of autumn / A reckless elopement / The towns dried by the wind / Is this what you imagined?

Unchanging secular rules / We avoid them / Things keep changing / Then we pay with our lives / The beginning of the story / The countdown to tragedy / Day after day it's the same / I want to leave... ...

Let me sing your favorite song, "Elopement," for you once more.
