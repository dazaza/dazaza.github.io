There is a story about two young men living in a small mountain village who both longed to become wealthy. One day, an opportunity arose when the village decided to hire them to transport water from the nearby river to the water tank in the village square. The two worked diligently, carrying water back and forth with buckets. One of them came up with the idea of building a pipeline, so he stopped using buckets and started digging trenches for the pipeline. The other continued carrying water with buckets. On the day the pipeline was completed, he turned on the faucet, and water flowed out abundantly, making him the richest person in the village.

This is the story of the pipeline. The two young men faced the same opportunity, but through different ways of thinking and working methods, they achieved different results. Whether it's individuals or businesses, instead of sticking to old rules and staying the same, it's better to shift your mindset and seek a "pipeline" that can bring in wealth continuously. The process may not go as smoothly as imagined, and there may even be some obstacles. Regardless of what ideals or goals you want to achieve, great sweat and effort are required. In this ever-changing era, if you insist on "carrying buckets" and choose to be content with the status quo, you could be eliminated by competition at any time. Therefore, you must build your own pipeline.

For individuals, it doesn't necessarily mean abandoning "working for others" and choosing "entrepreneurship." Whether working for someone else or starting your own business, it depends on your choice, but one thing is certain: without clear goals, steps, and planning, you will never reach the peak of success. For businesses, it's also about breaking through, changing the current situation, and keeping up with the times. For example, when the market changes and marketing concepts shift from "product-centered" to "customer-centered," businesses cannot remain stuck in the past, clinging to so-called traditions and refusing change. Otherwise, the result can only be elimination.

So, how can a business build a "pipeline" that brings in wealth continuously? Perhaps the pipeline is far more than just one. It starts with changing mindsets. Implementing CRM itself is also a process of changing mindsets and embracing new concepts, namely being "customer-centered." Customers are the direct source of profit for a business, so finding new customers and retaining existing ones are ways for a company to maintain profit growth. The cost of developing new customers is much higher than retaining existing ones, so preventing customer loss is a core focus for businesses. The role of a customer relationship management system is to help businesses center around the concept of "customer-centricity," manage customer relationships well, prevent customer loss, maximize customer value and corporate value, and ultimately improve business efficiency.

The customer relationship management market shows a trend of fierce competition, from brand competition, product competition, to service competition, etc. Of course, when businesses are building pipelines, they need to consider costs. When it comes to choosing CRM, suitability is the most important factor. Only by selecting a suitable CRM can the power of the pipeline be fully utilized, accumulating more wealth.
