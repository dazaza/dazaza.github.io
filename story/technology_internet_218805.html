Second-hand Laptop Buying Guide

An increasing number of friends are becoming interested in laptops. Although Intel Centrino laptops will soon be widely available in the market, the older mobile technology (Pentium III-M or Pentium 4-M) has not seen a significant price drop due to this. The high price of laptops has affected many enthusiasts who are interested in them. After all, these are investments that often exceed ten thousand yuan. At this point, second-hand laptops have become an option. According to relevant surveys, people who now want to own a second-hand laptop mainly fall into the following categories:

1. College Student Tribe: This group has relatively strict price requirements for laptops, mainly due to financial constraints. After all, students with plenty of money are few. This is also the reason why mobile PCs sell well. In addition, students who require higher performance (especially those in science and engineering) often have high-end machines configured in their laboratories, so cost-effective machines are their main goal.

2. Practical-minded White-collar Tribe: This tribe has relatively higher income. Their companies often provide them with dedicated machines, but they don't like them and want to own their ideal brand. This tribe often has a high sense of brand identity, is fashion-conscious, but isn't very concerned about performance.

3. Enthusiasts: They have a religious-like obsession with classic models, such as the IBM 600 series, which enthusiasts regard as a classic, especially its classic keyboard height, and also its velvet-like feel.

Models and Prices

In the second-hand laptop market, they are actually all branded machines, with fewer small brands. They are mainly dominated by IBM, TOSHIBA, Compaq, and DELL. This indicates that the quality of large manufacturers is better, with very few problems occurring. However, among these, there are certainly foreign "junk" products as well. Therefore, when buying a second-hand laptop, one must be careful and cautious. Moreover, from the market perspective, consumers also prefer branded products. However, one must be wary, as some unscrupulous merchants take advantage of buyers' lack of knowledge about laptops and deceive consumers with refurbished machines after modifications. Some consumers have the mentality that "good enough is fine," and merchants precisely exploit this mentality by privately modifying and reducing accessories, passing off inferior goods as good ones. Therefore, understanding the basic market conditions of second-hand laptops here is still quite important. In the current second-hand laptop market, taking Beijing's Zhongguancun market as an example, IBM accounts for the highest proportion, followed by Dell, Toshiba, and Compaq. From the tier perspective, they are mainly divided into three tiers:

Pentium MMX and below models: These models generally have configurations of Pentium series CPUs, including Pentium MMX series CPUs, with clock speeds generally below 200 MHz (there are also Pentium MMX models with clock speeds up to 300 MHz). Memory is below 32MB, and hard drives are below 4GB. Display performance is quite low, with most video memory being within 2MB. Of course, 1.44MB floppy drives and CD-ROM optical drives are standard configurations. In most cases, these types of laptops do not come with modems, and network cards are even rarer. Additionally, these laptops were produced earlier, generally with screens of 12.1 and 13.3 inches, with very few ultra-lightweight products, and most are relatively "clunky."

In terms of functionality, these laptops are relatively simple, capable of ordinary text processing and running Office series software without any issues. However, due to speed and memory limitations, internet browsing is not ideal, especially for web enthusiasts who like to open multiple web pages simultaneously; these laptops are insufficient for such use.

Another thing to note is that whether these laptops can play back (full screen) normally is an important performance indicator. Generally speaking, the processing speed of Pentium 133 and above models is sufficient, but video memory is also a critical bottleneck affecting DVD playback quality. Generally speaking, notebooks with 1M video memory cannot play VCDs normally in full-screen mode (quarter-screen usually works fine), such as IBM's early model 315ED. Models with 2M or more video memory generally can play DVDs normally in full-screen mode.

For this tier of laptops, another extremely important performance indicator is whether they support USB interfaces. Generally speaking, only models with MMX 166 and above are equipped with USB interfaces. Due to the popularity of various mobile storage devices, this small accessory has become very important. In the market, machines with USB are more expensive than those without by over 100-200 yuan.

White-collar workers who only perform general text processing

Laptop players with nostalgic sentiments

Suggested price: Within 2000 yuan

Pentium II tier models: The computational performance of Pentium II models has significantly improved compared to the previous tier. The clock speed of Pentium II is generally between 233MHz and 400MHz, and Celeron CPUs of the same tier are also common. Hard drives range from 2GB to 10GB. In terms of video memory, many Pentium II-level commercial laptops adopt 2.5MB video memory, which can play DVDs and handle pure 2D games without any issues.

Some models of laptops have started to be equipped with early 3D accelerator graphics cards, with video memory reaching 8MB. However, due to the overall performance limitations, such 3D capabilities cannot meet the requirements of modern large-scale 3D games. Screen sizes have become more standardized, with 13.3-inch and 14.1-inch laptops becoming mainstream. In terms of optical drives, CD-ROMs dominate the majority, and high-clock-speed models with large video memory also come with DVD drives, capable of playing DVDs normally. In terms of networking functions, laptops of this tier generally come with 56KBps modems, making internet access a basic function, and laptops with built-in 10M network cards have also become widespread. Almost all models are equipped with USB interfaces.

From the appearance perspective, thin and light products have started to appear in this tier of laptops. There are products with built-in optical and floppy drives, interchangeable optical and floppy drives, and externally connected optical and floppy drives.

This tier of laptops sells very well in the second-hand market, with numerous brands and large sales volumes. There are two reasons for this: one is that they are economical and practical, fully capable of handling common text processing, watching DVDs, and internet access functions for general computer users at a moderate price. The second is the ample supply. Because of the development of laptop technology, many Pentium II laptop users have started upgrading their computers, causing a large influx of this tier of second-hand laptops into the second-hand market. Among these laptops, IBM products are still the most numerous. Many 600E and 600X models can be seen, and even earlier 570 models can be found. According to the author's understanding, the 600E should be the ancestor of the current top-tier IBM T series, with a very modern appearance and substantial sales at the time, almost every second-hand counter could see its presence. Taking Beijing's Zhongguancun second-hand market as an example, the price of IBM Pentium II laptops varies based on appearance, performance, and condition, ranging from over ¥2000 to 4000 yuan. Pentium III and above tier

Although current laptop technology is developing rapidly, with Pentium 4 gaining popularity and Centrino emerging, in the second-hand laptop market, Pentium III remains the undisputed high-end product.

Second-hand Pentium III laptops in the market, due to relatively short usage time, generally look new. However, the current second-hand Pentium III frequencies are concentrated in the low-frequency segment of 500-700 MHz. The reason is simple: users of the new Pentium III around 1 GHz have no need to sell their machines. The memory size is mostly 64 MB and 128 MB, and users can also request memory expansion themselves. Hard drives range from 6.4 GB upwards to 20 GB, which is already basically sufficient for laptops. In terms of screen size, 14.1 inches is mainstream, with 13.3 inches also quite common, and 12.1 inch products are generally ultra-lightweight. For early Pentium III laptops, display performance is one of the most important performance indicators. Because during this period, notebook 3D graphics cards had developed relatively maturely, whether equipped with powerful 3D graphics cards made a huge difference in gaming and entertainment aspects. During this period, both Nvidia and ATI launched their notebook 3D graphics cards, with some having 16 MB of video memory, and a very few even reaching 32 MB. Even if this tier of notebook computers does not have professional 3D graphics cards, their display performance has qualitatively improved compared to the Pentium II era, all having certain 3D functions (like S3 series graphics cards) starting from 4-8 MB of video memory. Playing regular 2D games and simple 3D games is very easy. DVD drives have become optional items in second-hand shops, and in general, optical drives can be directly replaced with DVD drives, which is good for consumers as they can purchase according to their needs. Second-hand Pentium III laptops generally still come with USB 1.1 interfaces, with some even having two, and modems and network cards have become standard configurations.

During the Pentium III era, ultra-lightweight products began to be favored by fashion-conscious white-collar workers, but there are very few second-hand ultra-lightweight products. In terms of appearance, overall, these early Pentium III laptops have already paid more attention to appearance and weight, looking generally pleasant, unlike the old Pentiums which were "heavy and thick".

In the Zhongguancun second-hand market, Pentium III laptops are still dominated by IBM, with prices generally around 5000 yuan. There are also models priced over 4000 yuan, but generally not exceeding 6000 yuan. Other brands like Dell follow closely behind, and classic models like Compaq M700 are also relatively common. Functionally speaking, this level of laptops can already fully meet the requirements of non-3D designers and hardcore gamers. With the advent of Centrino laptops and the implementation of low-price strategies by various manufacturers, second-hand Pentium III laptops are no longer as strong as they were a few months ago, with merchants quietly lowering prices. Prices have dropped by about 200 yuan compared to last month. It is estimated that in the next few months, second-hand Pentium II and Pentium III laptops will drop further.
