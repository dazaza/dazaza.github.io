The rainy season of life, the lingering sorrow of departure

The alluring Blue Rose blooms gently at the tip of the fingers.

Tender affection overflows faintly from the lips.

In the long nights, thoughts surge like an overwhelming tide.

Messy strands of hair cover misty eyes.

A fragment of memory blurs the youth of two people.

The rainy season of life ultimately becomes damp slowly until it rots.

Those pale and powerless fragments can never withstand the passage of time.

I think, in the end, I have lost you, lost my flowery years.

Time freezes memories but cannot hold your gradually retreating steps.

For a long time, I was stuck helplessly in a swamp of despair.

I think, my youth is destined to start decaying.

Often, I selfishly fantasize that if one day, when I am on my last breath, will you stop your hurried pace and turn around to look at me?

Summer gradually fades away, passion and hope pass each other by. In my heart, there is indescribable sorrow. Every day, stepping on the fragmented sunlight, yet feeling more and more coldness by my side.

Closing my eyes, I see a startling expanse of whiteness. Regarding happiness, I always thought that after pain, I would possess it, finding a bit of coolness under your shadow every summer.

We remain silent to each other, mirage-like scenes where reaching out only touches desolation. Standing under warm sunshine, between the flicker of fingers, what slips through are those years gone by.

When we both calmly talk about parting ways, from your silence, I see the heart-wrenching sorrow inside me.

We promised not to reminisce, not to feel heartache, not to be extravagant, yet every day, what repeats in my mind is just that pale silhouette.

Life now is filled with confusion, unable to see where the road ahead leads. All I know is that I only have the little ideal left within me.

I understand, in the journey of life, there will always be a few people who eventually become insignificant passersby.

Each time I try to smile towards those memories, I end up shedding tears instead. Behind our smiles lies one scar after another.

I've gotten used to brewing a strong cup of tea alone, then savoring its bitterness carefully. Sometimes, I feel life is truly like this strong tea in my mouth - bitter at first, but sweet in the end, leaving an endless aftertaste.

Perhaps youth is just like this. Tears can rampage freely across the face, but after crying, life must still go on.

Feelings can never be the entirety of life, just as one person can never be the sole focus. Our lives contain too many attachments and gains and losses; gaining doesn't necessarily mean happiness, losing doesn't necessarily mean pain. Those things we shouldn't possess, let's abandon them in the most magnificent manner possible, even if we love them deeply.

The rainy season of youth will bring spring blossoms after the tears dry.
