Greek aid delay hits investors, international gold price falls 0.40%, US Dollar Index

by sunxiys1st7 on 2012-02-16 14:54:09

Huitong News, February 16 - During the Asian session on Thursday (February 16), international gold slightly declined. It reached a high of $1729.17 and a low of $1720.51, currently trading around $1720. News about the delay of Greece's bailout has dampened investors' hopes for a resolution to the eurozone economic issues.

According to foreign media reports on Wednesday (February 15), citing several EU (EU) sources, Eurozone finance officials are considering a full or partial postponement of Greece's second bailout package while ensuring that the Greek government avoids defaulting on its debt. The Eurozone officials are considering delaying the second bailout until after the April general election because Greek party leaders have not shown sufficient sincerity in committing to implement severe spending and wage cuts as well as public sector layoffs, which has caused them concern.

The leader of Greece's Panhellenic Socialist Movement, George Papandreou, signed the commitment for reform and austerity plans on Tuesday (February 14). Meanwhile, Antonis Samaras, the leader of the conservative New Democracy Party, who was previously unwilling to sign the agreement, also submitted a written commitment dedicated to the new austerity plan on Wednesday. Both parties assured that they would continue to follow the fiscal austerity policies required by international creditors even after the election.

The Greek government stated on Tuesday (February 14) that the Greek cabinet had agreed to further reduce expenditures by 325 million euros in 2012. Last Sunday (February 12), the Greek parliament passed the strict fiscal austerity agreement demanded by international creditors.

By now, Greece has fully met the requirements set by the finance ministers of the 17 Eurozone countries. On last Thursday (February 9), the finance ministers of the 17 Eurozone countries held a meeting and decided that if Greece meets three conditions - the Greek parliament passes the bailout agreement, the government reduces the deficit by an additional 325 million euros, and the main coalition parties commit to reforms - then the bailout agreement will be approved.

Officials from the "Troika" - the European Commission, the European Central Bank (ECB), and the International Monetary Fund (IMF) - arrived in Lisbon, the capital of Portugal, on Wednesday to evaluate the bailout plan for Portugal. They may mainly express supportive attitudes to alleviate the impact of Greece's problems.

The international rating agency Moody's announced on Thursday that it is taking rating actions on 114 financial institutions in 16 European countries to reflect the effects of the European debt crisis and the deterioration of creditworthiness in the region’s governments.

The Federal Reserve (FED) meeting minutes showed that some officials believe that a new round of bond-buying programs will soon be needed, while others think more data should be observed.

Iran claimed progress in nuclear technology, including a new type of centrifuge that can accelerate uranium enrichment. This could intensify the confrontation between Iran and the West over nuclear issues. In response, U.S. State Department spokesperson Victoria Nuland said that Iran's nuclear developments are not big news for them.

According to market gold ETFs data monitored by Huijin Network on Thursday, the market gold ETF-SPDR Gold Trust's gold holdings remained unchanged compared to the previous trading day at 1278.26 tons on Wednesday; IShares' gold holdings continued to remain stable at 179.27 tons.

As of 1:30 PM Beijing Time, international gold was quoted at $1720.99, down $6.92, with a decline of 0.40%.
