Under the chaos of educational aids, how can we find a good tutor for our children

by edurqusr on 2012-02-28 13:48:22

The nationwide private tutoring market is booming! It has even given rise to listed companies mainly engaged in tutoring businesses!

However, behind the prosperous scene lies a mess!

Through interviewing some parents who have hired tutors, reporters learned that the current tutoring market mainly has the following problems:

1. Passing off inferior goods as good ones. Going to a tutoring institution, the institution promises that the classes will be taught by frontline teachers with experience in overseeing college entrance exams! But it turns out to be a postgraduate student from a local university! During the tutoring period, parents have no way to check any of the teacher's credentials and can only trust the institution.

2. Exaggerating excessively. Promising score improvements, and if scores don't improve, they promise a refund. In reality, the money is basically non-refundable, at most you retake the same tutoring course, but courses that have already proven ineffective are just a waste of time.

3. High fees. Taking Beijing as an example, the hourly fee for math courses for senior three students reaches over 300 yuan! Parents often pay more than a thousand yuan at once to the tutoring institutions.

In order to provide some guidance to parents when looking for tutors, reporters specially consulted some education experts and student parents. The following methods can help you find a good tutor:

1. First, look at their familiarity with the textbook. Finding a good teacher is important! If you hope to find an experienced teacher, first check their familiarity with the textbook; experienced teachers will definitely be very familiar! You can randomly mention a chapter, and they should be able to tell you the main knowledge points inside!

2. Experienced teachers have their own teaching theories! These are very systematic and logical teaching theories; good teachers make it understandable even for ordinary people! At the same time, they must have dealt with many students and have their own teaching methods for different students.

Often, when encountering a new tutor, you don't know their reputation, and we cannot rely on one-sided opinions!
