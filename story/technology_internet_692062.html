How to write an English graduation thesis, proxy writing of English graduation thesis (English graduation thesis topic selection. The topic selection of English graduation thesis can be carried out in three major directions: English literature, linguistics, and translation studies. Within each major direction, smaller directions can be chosen, as detailed below:

1. English Literature

The screening of graduation thesis topics in English literature can be conducted from three directions: national literature research, literary criticism theory research, and comparative literature research.

2. Linguistics

Choosing a graduation thesis topic in linguistics can be done in two major directions: general linguistics and applied linguistics, separately opening up and easing emotions; English learning can also be very happy. General linguistics research is the study of any aspect of the English language, such as the study of a part of speech, or a tense, or spelling, intonation, etc. (e.g., "General Present Tense and its Communicative Functions"). Applied linguistics includes the research on teaching methods and other emerging branches of applied linguistics. Students in normal programs or those engaged in teaching tend to choose the teaching method direction. In choosing a topic in this direction, one should avoid broad-ranging topics and focus on studying a specific issue, most importantly combining with teaching practice or experiments. Good topics in this direction include: "Personality and English Teaching," "The Influence of Dialects on English Learning," etc.

3. Translation Studies

Translation studies topics are generally available in two directions: translation theory and translation activities. The study of translation practice involves exploring a certain type of translation practice, etc. Comparatively, there are more studies on translation activities; these topics can involve the translation of a linguistic phenomenon or the translation of a rhetorical device (e.g., "The English Translation of Chinese Idioms"). It should be noted that when conducting research on translation activities, it often requires support from some translation theory, summarizing laws, and evaluating the activity, avoiding merely listing examples.
