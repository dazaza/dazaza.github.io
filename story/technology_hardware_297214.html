I once got so close to happiness. -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; Happiness is very simple, so simple that when it comes to us, we can't even notice it at all. Happiness is a feeling. When you feel it, you own it. Cherish everything you have, and you will be the happiest person.    Always thinking, how far is happiness from me? But each reason I come up with refutes this thought. Originally, happiness has always been by my side. When I was very young, I had my parents' warm embrace, and dear and lovely companions to play freely with. We sang songs together, and our voices echoed in every corner given to us by nature. At that time, happiness was right by my side.    Time passes, and we are all growing up. As we grow, after each exam, we bring home certificates of merit; in the eyes of affirmation and praise; when we overcome difficulties, we all experience the taste of happiness. Although there is also distress, we still feel happy. Happiness is by our side.    After growing up, we face the joy, pain, and entanglement of love and romance. We find ourselves no longer that blank sheet of paper, but filled with too many patterns. After experiencing love and pain, we suddenly realize that happiness comes fast and goes fast. Only when happiness is with us, we didn't try hard enough to hold on to it. It was ourselves who let happiness go, back to the starting point, walking alone. Maybe many people are the same. When happiness is around, it feels faint, but once lost, we realize its value. But at that moment, happiness will not stop to wait for you anymore, it won't.    Life constantly intertwines in the changes of time. Many once special experiences gradually calm down in our minds, or even disappear without a trace. Occasionally, some memories ripple like the wind passing through. Life seems like a computer browser, once a link is chosen, it is destined that one cannot turn back. Wanting to turn back is either unwillingness or impossibility. Thus, we can only continue moving forward, even if we are already physically and mentally exhausted!    Since then, I began telling myself that happiness is not far away. Perhaps it is waiting for my appearance somewhere. If there is an affinity, we will meet; if not, we will pass by each other. From then on, everything becomes clear.    I have always felt that I am not far from happiness, yet I can never find it. Thus, I decided to be a wandering person, but unknowingly, I seemed to get closer to happiness.    Perhaps, happiness is not about perfection and eternity, but the resonance and harmony between the soul and the myriad things of life. It is the beauty of life and process, and the feeling and insight of the heart towards life. Just like flowers blooming before dawn, autumn leaves falling in an instant, tearful eyes gazing at each other, the waxing and waning moon in the heart... Every happy moment is happiness. What is happiness? It is the feeling inside oneself, not others' comments. True happiness and sorrow, only oneself understands. The meaning of happiness varies for everyone, doesn't it? Luxurious cars and wealth do not necessarily mean happiness. A bamboo fence, a thatched cottage, a few cups of tea, a short flute and a long pipe, smiling at each other with your loved one, who could say this is not the happiness and joy of life? Then finally, I realized that happiness is actually a feeling. When you feel it, you possess it. Cherish what you have, and that is happiness. For so many years without seeing her, when I stood in front of her again, she stared at me in surprise. She said I was no longer the trouble-making boy, nor the ambitious big boy full of plans for the future. I had become a tall, mature man. And she was no longer the shy girl who blushed while talking. I took her to eat, told her about my life these past few years, and she also talked about her work and life situation, but she avoided mentioning anything related to feelings. Finally, I gathered all my courage and said to her: "Marry me, I hope saying this now is not too late." We both fell silent. Her eyes were moistened, tears flowing down from the corners. After a long time, she said: "I waited for this sentence for seven years, but now it's too late. Two weeks ago, I registered for marriage with someone else." She continued, she always thought I liked her, and she liked me too, but I never personally confessed to her. When I was admitted to university, she thought she wasn't good enough for me and became disheartened. But every time I visited her, wrote letters to her, and saw those hints, she would reignite her hopes. However, each time, I never mentioned it. Even after I started working, I never heard her confess. Her family, her colleagues, kept introducing her to different suitors, but her heart only wanted to wait for me. Yet, I disappointed her time and time again. A man might marry at forty and it wouldn't be considered late, but a girl, being twenty-six or twenty-seven and still not finding a partner would be looked down upon. Finally, she agreed to marry a man who had pursued her for a long time. Forever blessing Each other. I've also read many romantic novels where the male and female protagonists have a telepathic connection, whether it's one year, two years, three years... no matter how many years, they always end up holding hands; I've also heard elders often advising me to establish a career before settling down, telling stories of many great people who gained lifelong happiness after establishing their careers; however, perhaps these are all beautiful legends, they haven't manifested in my life. I was once so close to happiness, close enough to catch it with my hand... But regardless, in this lifetime, I will remember her, that kind, pure, and shy girl. I will give her eternal blessings. I was once so close to happiness, close enough to catch it with my hand... "Happiness, is in your hands." & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; I suddenly realized that I let happiness slip away.
