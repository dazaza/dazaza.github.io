1: What to look at in the processor CPU

The processor is the core control of the computer, and a reasonably matched computer can reveal the overall level of the machine from the processor.

So what are the main parameters to look at in the processor? Mainly the core and clock speed, etc. The core refers to dual-core, quad-core, etc., and the clock speed mainly looks at the frequency value. In general, the higher the clock speed and core count, the better. For example, the processor in the author's computer is Intel E4300, dual-core with a clock speed of 2.10 GHz, which belongs to the previous generation of processor products. Clearly, the clock speed of 2.10 GHz is not high, and currently dual-core processors and above have reached a clock speed of over 2.6 GHz and are multi-threaded.

2: What to look at in the motherboard

The motherboard is the core component of the computer, equivalent to a platform where different hardware works. The most important things to look at in the motherboard are its work and chipset, and aspects such as sufficient material usage, whether it is a full solid capacitor design, and how well it performs in overclocking are all important parameters. The motherboard has little impact on the computer's speed, so we focus on the stability and overclocking ability of the computer motherboard. Generally speaking, motherboards with a full solid capacitor design have good stability, and branded motherboards have better craftsmanship and materials.

Expansion Reading: The configuration of a computer generally refers to the high-end degree and cost-performance of the computer's hardware accessories. The performance of the computer mainly depends on:

(1) CPU: Determines the running speed, for example, Celeron D2.66G, where [2.66G] refers to its computing speed. However, the unit [G] here is different from the [G] of the hard disk, it's not about size. The [G] of the CPU is [GHZ], which is frequency, meaning it can perform 2.66G calculations per second.

(2) Motherboard: Determines the running speed and stability. Due to the different chips applied in the motherboard, there can be many types, such as 845, 865, 895, 815, etc.

(3) Hard Disk: Determines the speed and size of reading and storing data, for example, 80G/7200/0.8M, where 80G is the size, 7200 is the rotational speed, and the rotational speed determines the speed of reading and storing data. Also, 0.8M is the cache of the hard disk, which also determines the speed.

(4) Graphics Card: Determines the quality and speed of image display. Its performance index generally depends on its video memory and bits, like the commonly mentioned dual 128, meaning both the memory and bits are 128.

1. CPU: This mainly depends on frequency and L2 cache. The higher the frequency and the larger the L2 cache, the faster the speed. In the future, CPUs will have L3, L4 caches, etc., all affecting response speed.

2. Memory: The access speed of memory depends on the interface, the number of particles, and the storage size (including the memory interface, such as SDRAM133, DDR233, DDR2-533, DDR3-800). Generally speaking, the larger the memory, the stronger the data processing capability, and the faster the speed.

3. Motherboard: Mainly the processing chip. For example, the i965 of a laptop has stronger processing capabilities than i945, and i945 has stronger data processing capabilities than i910, and so on.

4. Hard Drive: In daily use, people consider it less, but it does have some impact. First, the rotational speed of the hard drive (divided into high-speed hard drives and low-speed hard drives, high-speed hard drives are generally used in large servers, such as 10000 RPM, 15000 RPM; low-speed hard drives are used in regular computers, including laptops), desktop computers generally use 7200 RPM, and laptops generally use 5400 RPM, mainly considering power consumption and heat dissipation reasons.

The speed of the hard drive also varies due to different interfaces and rates. Generally speaking, it is divided into IDE and SATA (which we often call serial port) interfaces. Earlier hard drives were mostly IDE interfaces, which are slower compared to SATA interfaces.

In recent years, with the development of the market, the cache of hard drives has increased from 2M to 8M or more. Like the CPU, the larger the cache, the faster the speed.

5. Graphics Card: This has a direct connection to the response speed of running ultra-large program software, such as running CAD2007, 3DStudio, 3DMAX, and other graphic software. In addition to hardware-level distinctions, there is also the existence of [shared video memory] technology, which differs from general built-in video memory chips. This [shared video memory] technology requires reading video memory from the memory to handle the needs of corresponding programs. Some people call it: dynamic video memory. This technology is more commonly used in laptops.

6. Power Supply: As long as the power is sufficient and stable, it's fine.

7. Monitor: The interface between the monitor and the motherboard also has an impact, but people generally don't care too much about it.
