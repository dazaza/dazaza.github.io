Sina Weibo product manager interprets Page

by anonymous on 2013-10-19 11:57:03

Recently, someone on Zhihu asked: What is the purpose of Sina Weibo's Page? In response, someone humorously replied that even Sina Weibo itself doesn't know what it's for. This comment prompted Liu Xinzhen, Senior Product Operations Manager at Sina Weibo, to personally answer the question. Below are the key points from Liu Xinzhen's explanation:

1. Page is one of Weibo's key projects this year. It is positioned as an information aggregation page for a particular individual, organization, or interest point (product), essentially serving as the homepage for individuals, organizations, and products.

2. What is the function of Page? Liu Xinzhen gave examples: When you visit a singer’s Weibo page, you can see their music works and listen to them; when you visit a writer’s Weibo page, you can see their published works and complete online reading or purchase orders.

3. Possible values generated by Page: (1) Displaying public image; (2) Potential business center; (3) E-commerce platform.

4. Sina Weibo is improving a service similar to WeChat's public service platform, named Fan Service Platform, which allows private messages to be used to push articles. (Many regular Weibo users have already been disturbed by this.)

Below is Liu Xinzhen's original response:

Page is one of Weibo's key projects this year. However, because it spans many departments, even within the company, many people are still unfamiliar with it. Finding someone who can perfectly answer this question isn’t easy. Although a significant part of my work involves this, I may not be the most suitable person to answer this question, so I will try to explain it as best as I can...

Since the end of last year, the company has reconsidered the relationships between individuals, organizations, and interest points on the internet and has promoted a process internally called “Page-ification.” Besides personal users, we have added two other roles on the platform: organizations (enterprises, media) and interest points (music, movies, books, items, locations). We have also redefined the three types of relationships between these nodes: "like," "follow," and "subscribe."

I am responsible for driving the operation of user Pages, which means transforming the personal user's Page (i.e., the current Profile page) into a network showcase center or business center for the individual, rather than just a place to view historical Weibos. For example, if you visit a singer’s Weibo page, you can see their music works and listen to them. If you visit a writer’s Weibo page, you can see their published works and complete online reading or purchase orders. Visiting a Zhihu author's page, you can see their excellent answers on Zhihu. Furthermore, we will collaborate with third-party businesses to make their pages become business centers, such as store displays and business consultations.

Why are we doing this? Because Weibo has already become the largest online entry point for individuals. If you want to learn about someone, what is the fastest way? Visit their Weibo. But merely looking at their historical Weibos is insufficient. If we can integrate the valuable things they do elsewhere and the valuable content they create, then Weibo undoubtedly becomes a better place to display one's public image. This is equivalent to us building a personal website for each user, a website that can be conveniently accessed via PC and mobile devices. It helps users better showcase themselves and also helps others quickly understand the user. This is the value of this project for users.

Beyond image display, a user's personal page could also become their business center, which might be a more valuable aspect. When you get to know someone through Weibo, you might become more interested in the services they offer. For example, a tech-savvy person recommending a Taobao store, a witty and skilled dentist providing dental care services, or an idol star offering membership services—this is the value of interpersonal marketing. We can achieve PC and mobile dual-platform one-stop services by collaborating with relevant third parties to incorporate these functions into user Pages.

After forming the idea, over the past one or two months, we have been preparing some product-related aspects, such as quickly loading specific modules for certain users and ensuring that users have complete unloading and handling permissions. At the same time, we have also been communicating with some websites and mobile applications, listening to their thoughts on this project. After I posted related invitation cooperation tweets, many UGC sites responded enthusiastically.

Zhihu is our first attempt at integrating valuable user content. Zhihu is China's most professional knowledge community, with very high content value. Both sides have a strong willingness to cooperate. In about two weeks, we integrated some Q&A from Zhihu users into their personal pages, adding a Zhihu module. However, this quick launch did indeed cause some controversies among users. The main point of contention was that users didn't want some "heavy-taste" questions they answered on Zhihu to appear on their Weibo. Additionally, due to the underlying structure of the Page not being fully developed, the module couldn't be quickly deleted. After careful consideration, we decided with Zhihu to temporarily take it offline. Once we optimize content filtering and presentation format and ensure users can delete quickly, we will relaunch it. From another perspective, we verified the perception of Weibo in users' minds—a platform for displaying personal public images. Apart from Zhihu, collaborations with other valuable content creation platforms like Guokr, PAPA, and Qyer are also progressing systematically.

Taobao models were our first attempt at service collaboration. Taobao models are a group of clothing models on Taobao. After completing the Taobao model's Page, visiting a Taobao model's Weibo allows you to see not only their Weibos and check-in locations but also the products they endorse and the photo shoots they've done. More importantly, other users can directly book photo shoots with them here—of course, these services are based on Taobao's appointment shooting function.

Internally, we collaborated with the Micro Music team and the Micro Reading team to provide music Pages and book Pages for music users and book author users first. For example, opening the Weibo homepage of the band @Yuquan, you first see their latest music works, which can be played and listened to directly online. Opening novelist @WangYuewen's Weibo homepage, his novels are prioritized. Visitors can read them online or go to Dangdang.com and other websites to purchase them. All user operations on these works will be recorded on the platform and recommended to relevant people according to user relationships.

Based on user Pages, we provided users with a framework for aggregating content and services. In the future, there will be different module libraries available for different users to install and use. The content and forms presented on the Weibo homepages of different types of users may vary greatly. Of course, all changes aim to help users better showcase themselves and better market themselves.

Since we've talked about marketing, let me mention another keyword of ours this year: service.

Weibo Feed is an information stream based on follow relationships, a highly efficient way to obtain continuous information after following. In fact, it has also become the flattest online marketing center. This year, we will improve another information channel, namely the message stream based on private messaging relationships. Due to its precise push mechanism, the delivery and acquisition of information are more efficient, making it more suitable for one-on-one services.

We hope to make Weibo a platform that provides both marketing and service functions, where the Feed stream is used for public marketing and the message stream is used for one-on-one services. This is also the reason behind some recent adjustments we made to the private messaging product. Based on the revamped message stream platform, we built a product called Fan Service Platform.

The part I am responsible for is the operation of this product similar to WeChat's public service platform for individual users. This product allows enterprises, media, and quality individual users to send targeted private messages to specific groups. The content of private messages can be pure text, mixed text and images, or voice.

To ensure it is not abused, regarding quality individual users, only fans who have completed the "subscription" will receive private messages. Subscriptions are triggered by two mechanisms: the first is when fans actively send private messages DY to PO owners, and the second is when fans send specific keywords to obtain specific articles, thus completing subscriptions to PO owners. We hope this will enhance the media capabilities of individuals, promote the growth of personal media brands, and increase the accuracy and conversion rate of marketing. For example, a study abroad consultant can set keywords, with the keyword "USA" for American study abroad articles and the keyword "Australia" for Australian study abroad information. When a fan sends a specific keyword, it also tells the PO owner the destination, allowing the PO owner to target this type of user more accurately in the future. The mechanisms for media users and enterprise users are slightly different from the above. In the long term, we still hope that these two types of users will primarily use developer-mode service functions.

In addition, I have another task: optimizing the verification process for Orange V certification to make it more consistent with user expectations. After the transformation, the processes for users to apply for V certification, modify nicknames, and update certification descriptions will be more convenient. For example, we will allow media and enterprises to certify their employees with Orange V and modify certification descriptions, enabling users to complete various operations through Weibo's existing interaction methods without being directed to unfamiliar pages through multiple links. For example, using the Fan Service Platform to realize the mobility of the certification process, or collaborating with third-party websites to verify users.

I am not the most suitable person to answer these questions. I only discussed matters related to individual users, but I hope my understanding is correct... sweat...
