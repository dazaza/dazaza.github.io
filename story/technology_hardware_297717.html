Beijing lawyer to defend Wen Qiang, formerly served in the Supreme Procuratorate for 10 years

by 8clt601y1m on 2009-12-07 13:19:08

Core tip: The reporter learned that, entrusted by Wen Qiang's family, the well-known Beijing lawyer Yang Kuangsheng would be Wen Qiang's defense lawyer. According to the records, Yang Kuangsheng had worked in the Supreme Procuratorate for ten years. Apart from Yang Kuangsheng, several other lawyers from Beijing would also appear in court as defense lawyers for defendants involved in organized crime cases. On December 7th, Wuhan Evening News reported that on the 5th day, after being verified by the reporter, it was confirmed that Wen Qiang's family had entrusted Beijing lawyer Yang Kuangsheng to be the defense lawyer. As of now, Yang Kuangsheng has not received any notice about the trial from Chongqing.

On the 5th day, the reporter learned from an informed person that Yang Kuangsheng, a lawyer from Zhongtong Law Firm in Beijing, had been entrusted by Wen Qiang's family to be the defense lawyer for Wen Qiang's case. Prior to this, he had already met with Wen Qiang in Chongqing. According to the records, Yang Kuangsheng had worked in the Supreme Procuratorate for ten years. In 1998, he left the Supreme Procuratorate and became a lawyer. Now he is the deputy director of the Criminal Law Professional Committee of the Beijing Bar Association.

On February 23rd this year, Jiang Yong, the former director of the Chongqing Planning Bureau, who was involved in the highest level and largest amount of bribery in the real estate corruption case in Chongqing, went on trial. At that time, Jiang Yong's defense lawyer was precisely Yang Kuangsheng. An informed person believed that it might be due to his outstanding performance in that case that Wen Qiang's family found him and requested him to appear in court to defend Wen Qiang.

It was learned that in November, Wen Qiang's elder sister went to Beijing and met with Yang Kuangsheng, reaching an agency agreement. Subsequently, Yang Kuangsheng personally went to Chongqing and met with Wen Qiang. However, the details of the meeting were not disclosed temporarily according to the informed person.

At the beginning of this month, relevant persons in Chongqing revealed that Wen Qiang's case would be tried locally in Chongqing this month. On the 5th day, the reporter verified this information with Yang Kuangsheng. Yang Kuangsheng stated that as Wen Qiang's defense lawyer, he had not yet received any information about when and where the trial would take place from the official side of Chongqing, and politely declined the interview.

According to informed persons, Wen Qiang's family initially only planned to hire Yang Kuangsheng alone for the defense, but Yang Kuangsheng thought that one person's energy was limited, so he suggested that the family hire another lawyer.

In the second wave of anti-organized crime trials in Chongqing, several famous lawyers from Beijing will appear in court as defense lawyers for defendants involved in organized crime cases.

Apart from Yang Kuangsheng, there are also famous lawyers such as Xu Lanting, who handled major cases like the "Shenyang Mu Ma Case" and Liu Xiaqing's case, and Xuan Dong, who has repeatedly defended individuals involved in organized crime during the anti-organized crime storms in Northeast China. The day before yesterday, Xu Lanting confirmed that he would appear in court as the defense lawyer for the defendant in the Chen Mingliang organized crime gang case. On the same day, Xuan Dong also confirmed that he would appear in court as the defense lawyer for the defendant in the Chen Mingliang case.

According to the records of the Chongqing prosecution authorities, the organized crime group led by Chen Mingliang, Ma Dang, and Lei Deming consists of more than 40 members. Both Xu Lanting and Xuan Dong stated that they have not yet been informed about when the trial for the Chen Mingliang case will take place. Additionally, there were reports that the defense lawyer for the Wang Tianlun organized crime case scheduled to begin on December 7th was also a lawyer from Beijing, but the reporter has not been able to confirm this.

Extended reading: Li Qiang's lawyer claims insufficient evidence for the Chongqing prosecution's organized crime charges. Zhao Changqing, a top-level figure in the field of criminal law in China, while defending "red-topped businessman" Li Qiang, claimed that the public prosecution authority's charge of "organizing and leading a black society" against Li Qiang was based on insufficient evidence. During the defense process of this case, Zhao Changqing negated the prosecution's accusation of "black society" against Li Qiang and simultaneously expressed that the "anti-organized crime campaign" should be carried out according to law. Lawyer Zhou Litai, who defended Li Yi, stated that the "Chongqing anti-organized crime movement has expanded."

Wen Qiang's organized crime case will go to trial within the month, and Chongqing requires the case to "return." Recently, the reporter confirmed from various sources that Wen Qiang, the former high-ranking official in Chongqing who fell from grace during the "anti-crime storm," will soon face public trial locally in Chongqing. There were previous reports indicating that Wen Qiang's case would be tried outside the jurisdiction in Guizhou, but ultimately, the Chongqing authorities decided to bring the case back.
