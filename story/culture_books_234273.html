"Step by Step to Success" - The Promotion Path of a Mayor

by jqrxass on 2009-09-07 12:11:32

An ordinary civil servant without any political background, how can he be steady in the ever-changing officialdom? How can he overcome all difficulties and rise step by step? And become a mayor of great influence after several years? Li Chunping's "Step by Step" breaks the conventional套路 of official novels which focus on "exposing and fighting against corruption". With a positive writing attitude, it focuses on the governance wisdom and leadership art of leading cadres, bringing a fresh breeze to the creation of official novels. The novel takes an official's growth history as the longitudinal line, and being a person, doing things, and being an official as the transverse line. It has a grand theme with a strong "national consciousness", known as the "reading Bible" for civil servants, and is a must-read for administrative officials in many government agencies. What readers see is not the thrilling anti-corruption stories or sentimental official conversations, but profound insights into governance capabilities.

Step by Step Part One

On Gu Changshu's desk lay a set of new county annals. It was recorded that since the establishment of Daming County in the eighth year of Yongle in the Ming Dynasty, in nearly six hundred years of history, there had been more than seven hundred magistrates, but only less than ten were truly remembered. One of them governed cleanly all his life, regarding the common people as parents. He personally went on an expedition during a campaign against bandits and died under the knife of a bandit. The common people bid him farewell for ten miles, leaving behind a generous and sorrowful song. Another magistrate obtained his position through unusual channels (in the Qing Dynasty, buying an official position was called unusual channels or donation). His level of education was only recognizing five words, his name plus his courtesy name. Once while stealing in Jin'an Prefecture, he was caught publicly. It turned out that this bought magistrate was actually a thief and an illiterate. Gu Changshu looked melancholy, feeling heavy-hearted, and caressed the annals with both hands as if touching the pain of history. He secretly sighed that hundreds of years of prosperity and decline passed instantly, how many truly good officials could there be?

Step by Step Part Two

Gu Changshu did not argue. In the evening, he quietly observed the situation. After his son fell asleep, Gu Changshu got up from the sofa and sneaked into the bedroom, deciding to ambush his wife. Zuo Xiaoli liked to sleep naked. With a clear target, some details were skipped. He stood by the bed, smelling Zuo Xiaoli's body, and said, "So fragrant!" Zuo Xiaoli opened her eyes and said, "There's no one as shameless as you, truly disgraceful!" Gu Changshu climbed onto the bed and said, "Now I'm just smelling you, there's something even more shameless!" Then Gu Changshu covered his wife's mouth. The next morning, Gu Changshu woke up in high spirits, washed his face hurriedly, and rushed to the city Political and Legal Affairs Commission.

Step by Step Part Three

Gu Xiao felt a bit heartbroken, tears slowly seeping out again. To actively propose breaking up with the man she loved, and an excellent man at that, who was in a period of thriving career, required courage. Of course, besides genuinely considering the other party, Gu Xiao also had to consider her future husband. When she was with her current boyfriend, they talked lovingly and affectionately. But every time she thought about her relationship with Gu Changshu, she felt guilty. Before getting married, she had already put an iron green hat on him, which was something she couldn't bear. Therefore, for the sake of two men, she had to exit one side. She understood that choosing to exit one side was to protect both sides.

Step by Step Part Four

Gu Changshu was approaching forty, right in the prime of a man's life, a fully mature age. His power was growing larger, his experience richer, his handling of affairs more seasoned, and his behavior more unrestrained. Compared to when he was the secretary of the Youth League Committee in Daming County, he had changed greatly. Back then, he was just a handsome young man basking in success; now he had completely become a figure in Jin'an City. Power had become fertile soil, nurturing and enriching the charm of Gu Changshu as a mature man. Gu Xiao paid close attention to Gu Changshu's influence and personal performance in the municipal Party committee office. He was very influential in the municipal Party committee office, and his every move attracted her, infected her, and of course, also attracted and infected others in the office. Including the posture of Gu Changshu smoking and the tone of his speech, there were people secretly imitating him.

Step by Step Part Five

On the evening of Gu Changshu's birthday, the couple talked for a long time. To ensure Gu Changshu could rest well, their son had been sleeping separately with the nanny in another room since last year. Even though the couple lived together every day, their marital life was not frequent. Mainly because Gu Changshu was too tired, they could only maintain a frequency of once a week. Zuo Xiaoli became increasingly proactive, often teasing Gu Changshu. As they spoke, Zuo Xiaoli's hand pressed on Gu Changshu's abdomen. Gu Changshu let her touch him without moving. Zuo Xiaoli said, "People say thirty is like a wolf, forty is like a tiger. You seem not as good as before?" Gu Changshu said, "That's what people say about women. Thirty is like a wolf, forty is like a tiger, standing and inhaling wind, sitting and inhaling soil, lying down and inhaling bedding!" Zuo Xiaoli giggled and said, "Not inhaling bedding, then inhaling you."
