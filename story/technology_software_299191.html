The performance of Beijing Night: Moon Dance in the period of ancient times and early slavery society

by yerui0021 on 2009-12-08 16:41:32

China is an ancient civilization, and the history of its music culture is also very long. From about 50,000 years ago to the Neolithic period of five to six thousand years ago, our ancestors created simple musical instruments and dances. Bone whistles and clay ocarinas unearthed in Jingcun, Wanquan (now part of Wanrong County) in Shanxi, and Hemudu in Yuyao, Zhejiang, belonging to this period, some have already been able to play simple melodies composed of two or three notes. At the same time, there were also percussion instruments such as drums and chimes, as well as wind instruments. The earliest music was a reenactment of human labor life, directly produced from labor. In primitive times, music was often combined with poetry and dance. The close relationship between primitive music and dance and labor life was so evident that its social function was also self-evident. But as society continued to develop forward and social production relations became increasingly complex, the content and role of music art also expanded accordingly, and were widely used for physical exercise, expressing feelings, worshipping gods and praying for a good year, as well as entertainment, courtship and other aspects of social life; the aesthetic factors increased, and the form of music became more rich and diverse. From then on, music gradually evolved into an independent art form, as one of the forms of social consciousness, continuously developing forward with the evolution of society. When primitive society began to collapse during the time of Xia Yu, slavery society gradually formed and class distinctions appeared in society. Music art gradually infused class consciousness. For example, the representative music and dance of the Xia Dynasty, "Da Xia", directly praised the rulers themselves, which was quite different from the past practice of praising totemic ancestors. It is said that "Da Xia" praised Xia Yu's success in water control. The class differentiation of music is also reflected in the application of musical instruments. The slave society created valuable musical instruments like bell chimes and stone chimes, which was a manifestation of the progress of music. However, from the very beginning, these types of musical instruments could only be the exclusive property of the aristocratic ruling class. Slaves need not be mentioned, and ordinary tribal members were also fundamentally unable to possess and use them. They could at most play relatively simple instruments like ocarinas. The ancients often referred to noble families as "families with bells ringing and cauldrons steaming," which was a true depiction of this reality. From ancient times to the early stage of the slave society, it was the period when Chinese music gradually took shape and began to develop. During the Shang Dynasty, due to the development of smelting and other technologies, the progress of musical instrument manufacturing was promoted. Bronze percussion instruments such as bells and cymbals appeared, and the development of tube instruments also advanced.
