Striking the Military Camp 10: Female Pilots

On August 31st, China’s first batch of female fighter pilots donned a new style of flight suit. A month later, these female fighter pilots will fly for the first time over Tiananmen Square for a military parade. They have pioneered many firsts, such as "the first batch" and "first to fly," terms that have accompanied them for years. So what are these female soldiers really like? The documentary series "Striking the Military Camp" episode 10, "Female Pilots," aired on September 18th at 20:03 to reveal all.

The battle uniforms of the female pilots

While they were undergoing intensive training for the National Day parade, our reporters met them at a certain air echelon training base.

Reporter: "Why has this now been changed into a one-piece?"

Pilot: "It looks good, showcasing feminine beauty, and it's more convenient to wear, especially convenient."

Reporter: "Actually, no one sees you inside the cockpit during flights, so why be so particular about your clothes?"

Pilot: "Love for beauty, we're still women after all. In fact, there was once an excellent foreign pilot who would comb her hair beautifully before getting on the plane. It's also a kind of respect and love for flying... and we do the same."

Reporter: "Wasn't the previous uniform a bit troublesome to wear?"

Pilot: "Quite troublesome, and it had to be tucked in."

Reporter: "Now, isn't it easier to get dressed quickly?"

Pilot: "Just zip it up and it's done. This mainly highlights the female characteristics. These puffed sleeves make the girls look more fashionable, and since they aren't on the shoulders but slightly higher up, it's better for doing actions."

Reporter: "What benefits does it have for flying?"

Pilot: "It makes it slightly easier to lift your arms. If it was here, it might feel a bit stuck unless it's very large and loose. When designing this for us, they included puffed sleeves and even lace ones. Lace ones could get caught somewhere, so we chose this; it looks prettier and more beautiful."

Reporter: "Did it consult your opinion?"

Pilot: "Yes."

Reporter: "Which opinions were yours and eventually realized?"

Pilot: "Metal buttons, one-piece, small collars, sleeves. Previously, it was a turn-down collar, now it's a stand collar, looking a bit cooler."

This flight suit is made from the most advanced pilot clothing material and high-strength flame-retardant materials currently available worldwide. It has functions such as being lightweight, breathable, anti-static, and resistant to high-speed airflow, providing maximum protection for pilots' aerial safety. Other equipment for the female pilots is also newly issued.

Pilot: "Previously, this was white, but the material has probably changed, making it much lighter than before. Wearing it doesn't make your head feel heavy. Moreover, I think this color looks better than white. Inside, there's this microphone..."

Reporter: "Is this itself already inside the helmet?"

Pilot: "Yes, it's built-in, and the sound quality is better now. The padding is relatively softer than the old one. The biggest difference with this oxygen mask is here; it's much softer now. Our previous green one was very hard, and after long periods, the rubber smell became strong. This one is better. Because the material is extremely soft, it won't press against your nose. I particularly like this part."

Reporter: "This is right below the nose."

Pilot: "Yes, previously it was green and exactly pressed here."

The female pilots in their new flight suits look particularly spirited. The design of the uniform highlights the unique physique of women, adding some grace to the almost exclusively male air echelon training base. However, they remain mysterious. Their ability to soar in blue skies makes China one of the few countries, following the US, UK, and others, to have female fighter pilots.

Why cultivate female pilots

Interviewing Wang Baqun, Political Commissar of the Third Flight Academy of the Air Force.

Reporter: "Why did you decide to cultivate female fighter pilots?"

Wang Baqun: "On one hand, the development of our People's Air Force to a certain extent requires this, to build a powerful People's Air Force. Another reason is the inevitable result of our technological development reaching a certain level. Also, it is to improve the structure of our pilot team and reserve forces for astronaut cultivation."

They are the eighth batch of female pilots in our country. In 2005, the Chinese Air Force selected 35 people out of 200,000 graduating high school girls. Four years later, after basic education and training at the Air Force Aviation University, primary trainer aircraft training, and one year of advanced trainer aircraft flight, only 16 people remained. Approaching China's first batch of female fighter pilots, can their veil of mystery be lifted just a little?

Since the training of the 16 female fighter pilots in domestic new-type fighter jets is still under strict confidentiality, during the unified interviews organized by the Air Force, our reporters were unable to showcase their fierce and majestic demeanor. However, through interactions on the ground, we could still sense their professional qualities.

Reporter: "I am standing right now at their training site. This apparatus is called a hanging ladder, and the next one is called a rolling wheel. These devices actually train the balance ability of our female pilots because they need to perform various movements at high altitudes, especially as female fighter pilots, these are necessary training subjects. It's hard to imagine how difficult it would be for an ordinary person to undergo this training. But for these girls, these have become their daily routine."

Reporter: "What else does your school offer besides these sports projects? There are two hanging ladders and rolling wheels, and something that trains dizziness?"

Answer: "Spinning yourself in place, gymnastics."

Reporter: "Do you get dizzy, when the plane spins or turns?"

Answer: "At first, yes."

Reporter: "Have you ever thrown up?"

Answer: "That's all in the past."

Although they are still freshly graduated students, they must face the three main challenges faced by fighter pilots: high G-forces, high intensity, and psychological resilience. Under these standards, there is no distinction between men and women.

Reporter: "Do you find pulling this resistance machine strenuous?"

Answer: "Not too strenuous, using the right force is key."

Reporter: "How much force is there? Can you give me a try? Let's see, just a random try. I'll add one more."

Answer: "How many are there now?"

Reporter: "There's one now. I'll add one more, see if you can do it, three times is enough."

Reporter: "Impressive, sweat is coming out, do you usually practice physical fitness or other projects?"

Answer: "Sometimes strength training, push-ups, and sit-ups."

Reporter: "How many push-ups can you do?"

Answer: "We do around 20 per set, three sets."

Compared to peers, these female pilots appear healthier, more mature, and more confident. Out of the 35 girls initially selected from high school graduates, their personalities varied greatly. After four years of flight training, the remaining 16 female pilots seem to share one common trait deep down: they refuse to lose.

How are their skills honed?

Participating in the National Day parade differs from regular flight training. Female fighter pilots need to conduct formation flying in the air, just like other air echelons, requiring them to fly over Tiananmen Square with precision down to one second and one meter. How do these female pilots, with an average flight time of 270 hours, achieve such exquisite flying skills? What are their distinctive features?

Reporter: "I'm currently standing in the female pilots' simulated flight lab, where they are undergoing simulated flight training. Can you talk right now? Is there any difference between simulated flight and actual flight?"

Zhang Xiao: "There is a difference. It lacks stick force, it's just for practicing procedures and forming stick habits, and it's more sensitive than actual flight."

Reporter: "What is this mainly training?"

Zhang Xiao: "Mainly some procedures and your operational capabilities."

Reporter: "Didn't you start training these things when you were in school?"

Zhang Xiao: "Yes, when we started flying, we flew simulators first, practiced procedures, then got a feel for the aircraft and its operability, and then went on real flights."

Reporter: "This seems pretty simple."

Zhang Xiao: "You'll know how simple it is once you try it."

Reporter: "Tell me how to test it."

Zhang Xiao: "This is the throttle, this is the stick. Pull it back and up. That's your lead aircraft, you need to follow it in formation, and there's also a rudder below."

Reporter: "How do you form a formation with the lead aircraft?"

Zhang Xiao: "Just follow it."

Reporter: "What should we do now? Should we go up?"

Zhang Xiao: "We've lost sight of it now, www.wzws.gov.cn/ch/, pull up, we see it now, increase throttle, move closer to it."

Reporter: "Isn't this more sensitive than actually flying a plane? You barely moved and the lead aircraft disappeared, couldn't find it? Why is the lead aircraft getting farther away now?"

Zhang Xiao: "Just press down slightly, and it immediately corrects."

Reporter: "We're about to hit the ground, rise, isn't this part of your simulated formation?"

Zhang Xiao: "Yes, this situation means you've lost the lead aircraft, can't find it anymore."

Reporter: "Would this really happen in actual flight?"

Zhang Xiao: "No."

Simulating aerial formation flight, the reporter's hands didn't exceed five moves before losing the position of the lead aircraft. At this point, the screen showed the aircraft landing, indicating that this formation flight attempt failed and required a re-takeoff.

Reporter: "How many actions do you need to perform during take-off? Have you counted or calculated?"

Zhang Xiao: "More than a hundred."

Reporter: "Can you remember them all?"

Zhang Xiao: "Of course, from starting the engine, checking many switches, inspecting the entire instrument panel, and following some procedures."

Beyond Training

Even though it's just a simulation, we can still tell from the female pilots' expressions that the entire operation process isn't easy. Imagine how hard they must work to successfully complete their mission of flying over Tiananmen Square. But these female fighter pilots are still young women in their early twenties. Besides rigorous training, how do they show their youthful vitality?

Reporter: "Behind me are the dormitories of these female pilots. As male colleagues and media members, we must obtain their consent to enter their dormitories. Therefore, after consulting with their leaders in advance, a female pilot will soon come to pick us up, and we'll visit their dormitory together."

Lü Pin: "Hello, are you Reporter Zhai?"

Reporter: "Yes."

Lü Pin: "Welcome, welcome."

Reporter: "Let's take us inside your dormitory."

Lü Pin: "All our parade personnel stay here. These are promotional posters."

Reporter: "Are they self-made?"

Lü Pin: "Yes, mainly featuring us as the background."

Reporter: "And news talents."

Lü Pin: "Yes, these are our role models, their outstanding deeds are reflected in the bulletin board."

Reporter: "What's your name?"

Lü Pin: "I'm Lü Ping."

Reporter: "Who is this?"

Lü Pin: "That's our instructor."

Reporter: "Where does the instructor live?"

Lü Pin: "Instructors are on the second floor, we're on the third floor."

Reporter: "I see many pictures of ours posted."

Lü Pin: "These are when we received the aircraft."

Reporter: "For the first time. Which one is you?"

Lü Pin: "This one."

The pictures in the corridor of the dormitory document the growth of these female pilots and convey their goals. Astronauts are idols in the hearts of each of them.

Reporter: "This is entering your dormitory area."

Lü Pin: "Yes, these are all from the Third Flying Institute, these two are also from the Third Flying Institute, and this one was invited from our squadron."

Reporter: "Did he come to give a lecture?"

Lü Pin: "He shared some stories, exchanged his experiences as an astronaut with us. This is Zhai Zhigang and the other two astronauts, when they came, we took photos with them. Alright, let me take you to our dormitory."

To our surprise, the arrangement of the female pilots' dormitory is no different from that of the male pilots. But where are the secrets we learned about the girls beforehand hidden?

Political Instructor Liu Yong: "While completing various tasks, the innate love for beauty in girls still bursts forth. Including applying makeup indoors and looking at themselves in the mirror before going out. Sometimes they take out casual clothes to try on, even if they may not go out, but they enjoy wearing and appreciating themselves."

In fact, to prepare for the grand National Day parade, the female pilots haven't left the training base for a long time. We couldn't guess whether this answer was to keep their secret, but this small episode still stirred emotions within us. For the development of the Chinese Air Force, they have sacrificed much of the peaceful life that ordinary girls of their age enjoy. They practice hard on the ground and soar high in the sky.

Family Support

Her name is Zhang Xiao. The honor wreath of the female fighter pilot weaves through her youth. Along the way, how have her parents paid attention to every step of her growth? Our reporter called her home.

Reporter: "Do you know which aircraft your daughter is on?"

Father: "She hasn't told us, we don't know."

Reporter: "Do you want to know?"

Father: "It's a military matter, it's not convenient for us to say. She might tell us later, or she might not."

Reporter: "If you see the formation of female fighter pilots during the parade, would you be able to recognize your daughter? Or do you want to know?"

Father: "Yes, how could I not want to know?"

Zhang Xiao: "I'll tell you later, anyway, you'll know when you see our formation."

Zhang Xiao: "Every time I call them, they ask how my health is, how my life is. As for training content and missions, I never mention them. One is to avoid worrying them, the other is due to confidentiality. So they don't know much, and I don't tell them these things. And I think it's better that they don't know. Knowing too much would make them worry about me. I hope they are proud of me."

In fact, these girls far from home are not alone, as countless people silently care about them behind the scenes.

Reporter: "Friends, while walking through the military camp, I'll show you a very spectacular scene. Now these officers and soldiers are all the ground crew and maintenance personnel ensuring the successful National Day parade flight of our female fighter pilots. They are now taking a group photo. This is a great opportunity for us to understand how many people need to work quietly behind the scenes to ensure the safe flight of the female fighter pilots. We can see that they are standing in six rows, and each row is approximately 60 meters long. From this, we can see the vast number of people ensuring the success of this parade flight."

Zhang Bo: "Our relationship with the instructors is the closest. They will spare no effort to pass on all their skills to us. The eagerness of a mentor in any industry to impart all their lifelong knowledge to you, even hoping you surpass their skills. Because this is the foundation to ensure your safety during future flights. They hope your flying skills are as high as possible, and as safe as possible."

It is these unnamed officers who have bestowed dazzling halos upon these female fighter pilots. With their hard work, they have lifted the soaring warbirds into the sky, realizing the dream of the Chinese Air Force towards brilliance. On Teachers' Day, September 10th, when the female fighter pilots presented flowers to their instructors, long-buried feelings burst forth.

Zhang Bo: "I don't know if everyone feels that silence is the best way to express gratitude towards someone. You don't need to say anything, you don't need to do anything, even when you leave, they just hope you occasionally call to report safety. They don't expect you to become a general or achieve great accomplishments. The heart of an instructor is actually very simple, basically the same as parents."

On October 1st, 16 female fighter pilots will make a stunning appearance before the world. At that moment, they will carry pride and confidence as they step out from the mysterious veil, achieving a new glory and dream for the Chinese Air Force.
