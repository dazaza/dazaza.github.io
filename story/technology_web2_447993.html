In the "Military Operations" chapter of Sun Tzu's Art of War, it says: "Therefore, they are ordered by means of culture and aligned through military discipline; this is called certain victory." This means that soldiers should be taught using "cultural" methods, which refer to political righteousness, and their steps should be unified using "military" methods, which refer to military discipline. An army disciplined in this way will surely succeed in battle. The phrase "ordered by means of culture and aligned through military discipline" reflects a principle of military governance that values both cultural and military approaches, as well as moral authority and disciplinary rigor. This principle also applies to corporate management, where Sun Tzu’s "cultural" and "military" approaches correspond to today's "soft" and "hard" management styles. Soft management focuses on "people," aiming to inspire and mobilize employees' subjective initiative based on their thoughts and characteristics, using shared organizational values, cultural concepts, and spirit for humane and personalized flexible management. Hard management centers on "tasks," aiming to achieve organizational performance through job systems, rules and regulations, and administrative discipline, conducting procedural and orderly mandatory management.

"Ordered by means of culture" —— Corporate "Soft Management"

(1) Unified Value Concept: Using culture to unite employees

The amount of water a bucket can hold depends not only on the length of each plank but also on whether the planks are tightly joined. Without sufficient tightness, even the longest planks are useless. What strengthens the tightness of the bucket is precisely the "corporate culture and value concept" adhesive.

In an organization or team, different professional values are constantly intersecting and integrating. Whether the organization or team forms a unified professional value greatly affects the entire enterprise's vitality and life force. A unified professional value refers to the fundamental value judgment recognized by all members of an organization or team, the common goal and value concept of the team, directly determining the members' thinking style and behavior. When this unified value concept is sufficiently understood and accepted by team members, it can be transformed into powerful cohesion and centripetal force, comprehensively guiding the thoughts and wills of all members, allowing team members to be guided under a unified professional value concept, based on the responsibilities and goals of the organization, to boost morale and maintain high spirits.

(2) Unified Career Objectives: Integrating personal pursuits into corporate growth

"Victory belongs to those who share the same desires," meaning success comes from unity. Only when there is a shared pursuit and common behavioral norms within an organization can the thoughts and actions of all members be unified, thereby forming strong competitiveness.

To align employee and corporate growth objectives, one must link individual career development with the future growth of the enterprise, ensuring that the company and employees become a community of shared interests – the company's growth relies on the growth and ability enhancement of employees, while the company's growth provides a broader platform for employee development.

The company should design different career growth paths, such as managerial, professional, and composite paths, and plan different career growth directions based on employees' professional interests, personal characteristics, and competence. At the same time, through comprehensive training and education programs, genuinely assist employees in rapid growth, achieving a win-win between individuals and the company.

(3) Unimaginable Rewards: Motivating employees to realize their self-worth

Motivation, whether in military or corporate management, is the most important function. According to "Records of the Grand Historian," at the beginning of the struggle between Han and Chu, Xiang Yu had an army of over 400,000, four times that of Liu Bang, commanding the whole country with great power. However, because he could not distinguish between the wise and the foolish, his rewards and punishments were unclear, "he did not record others' merits, nor forget their faults, and after victories, there were no rewards, and after capturing cities, there were no enfeoffments," so even though he had capable people, he could not use them. As a result, Cheng Ping, Han Xin, and others "chose good trees to perch on and virtuous masters to serve," eventually leaving him. Due to Xiang Yu's lack of understanding of motivation principles, unclear rewards and punishments, and inability to use people effectively, the "force-pulling mountain, air-covering world" overlord ultimately ended up performing the tragedy of "Farewell My Concubine."

William James, a behavioral scientist at Harvard University, found in his research on employee motivation that workers paid by the hour generally utilize only 20%-30% of their abilities; if adequately motivated, they can utilize 80%-90%, with the difference of 50%-60% attributed to motivational effects. Soft management has a strong motivational role in today's corporate operations, mainly satisfying employees' higher-level needs, especially self-actualization and a sense of achievement. Therefore, motivating employees should not only consider material benefits but also spiritual motivation. In modern enterprises, honor-based incentives such as award certificates, naming inventions after employees, as well as training incentives like providing more opportunities for external training, rotational positions, and practical work experiences, have very good results.

Regular rewards within employees' expectations become routine, reducing the incentive effect to a maintenance factor, unable to return to special motivational roles. Facing specific tasks, employees weigh "possible rewards versus possible costs," deciding whether to act or not. Companies should comprehensively consider various incentive strategies, especially for core employees, adopting policies such as entrusting heavy responsibilities, salary tilts, and granting shares to reward employees with "unimaginable rewards," better stimulating their work enthusiasm and achieving unexpected results.

"Aligned through military discipline" —— Corporate "Hard Management"

Sun Tzu believed that governing troops requires not only "ordering by means of culture" but also "aligning through military discipline." "Aligning through military discipline" means using laws, regulations, and systems to discipline the troops and standardize the soldiers' actions. In the "Military Shape" chapter of The Art of War, Sun Tzu proposed: "Good commanders cultivate the Way and preserve the Law, thus being able to control the balance of victory and defeat." This means that only by cultivating politics and ensuring legal enforcement can one control the decision-making power over victory and defeat. In the "Terrain" chapter of The Art of War, Sun Tzu emphasized again: "If you cannot command despite your kindness, cannot issue orders despite your love, and cannot govern despite disorder, then you are like a spoiled child, incapable of being used." Soldiers should not be overly indulged or left to their own devices; they must be governed by clear laws and regulations. From Sun Wu's "execution of two concubines during the Wu Palace military drill," one can see his strict military discipline.

(1) Planning Growth Tracks: Improving Human Resource Management Systems

To achieve the "consistent pace and unified action" mentioned in the "Nine Grounds" and "Military Contention" chapters of The Art of War, laws must first be established. Laws serve as the basis for "aligning through military discipline" and the measure for standardizing subordinates' actions.

The idea of "aligning through military discipline" offers insights for corporate human resource management: First, clarify job responsibilities so employees know what they should and should not do; standardize work processes to ensure employees follow standardized procedures, preventing arbitrary actions; specify what level of performance is required to meet standards; compare job responsibilities and standards to help employees identify areas of competence and deficiencies; secondly, establish a standardized performance evaluation system to comprehensively assess employees' quality, attitude, methods, and abilities, encouraging employees to voluntarily comply with management systems and guiding them toward the company's desired direction. Thirdly, develop a comprehensive employee training and advancement plan, targeting skill gaps, knowledge deficiencies, and experience shortages with tailored improvement plans to quickly close the gap between employees and job requirements; promote and adjust salaries for employees who can independently handle key businesses and reach a certain technical level, and demote or dismiss those whose growth lags behind the organization's standards.

(2) Strict Enforcement: Strong Guarantee for Legal Compliance

"Those who enter the factory gates please abandon all autonomy" is a famous quote from Engels' "On Authority." Socialized organizations eliminate independent governance through hundreds of established management systems and technical standards, bringing corporate management into an orderly and scientific track.

Establishing rules and regulations is necessary, but enforcing them is even more crucial. If rules and regulations are merely "written on paper, hung on walls, spoken about verbally," their existence would be more harmful than non-existence. Therefore, having laws that must be enforced truly brings out the efficiency of punishment itself.

How to enforce laws strictly and make them effective? As Sun Bin said: "Punishment is used to correct disorder and make the people fear the authorities." For behaviors that ignore administrative regulations and violate discipline, relying solely on positive guidance and persuasion is far from enough. "Correcting errors must go beyond the norm," and for employees who dare to violate the organization's minimum administrative legal requirements, strict management and severe punishment are necessary, giving serious reprimands to warn others, and when necessary, decisively dismissing them to ensure the efficiency of the entire organization.

In summary, in management, employees must be treated with both "love" and "strictness"; love must be measured, and strictness must be scientifically applied. Words like love and strictness, culture and military, soft and hard, may seem contradictory but are interdependent, complementary, and harmonious in management. All these elements are indispensable in modern corporate management. Only by combining them – integrating culture and military, soft and hard – can talent management achieve the best results: "attracting, utilizing, managing, and retaining." -- David Chen, December 4, 2008, 4:51 PM
