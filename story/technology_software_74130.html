How to check if you have become a meat chicken for others

by xloong on 2008-06-13 18:57:05

We must regularly check if our computers have been hacked. If we find any intrusion, we need to try every possible way to remove the Trojan programs. When necessary, we would rather reformat and reinstall the system than give attackers any chance.

Next, we will introduce some phenomena of "meat chicken" computers to remind netizens to pay attention when encountering these phenomena. At the end of the article, we will introduce a few tool software to assist in analyzing the security of your computer.

Phenomenon 1: Abnormal login reminders for QQ and MSN

When you log in to QQ, the system prompts that the IP address of the last login is completely unrelated to you. For example, you were clearly only at home in Shanghai, but QQ reminds you that the last login location was in Shenyang.

Also, when you log in to MSN, a friend may send you a message asking what you just sent, but you are very clear that you never sent any messages to this friend.

Phenomenon 2: When logging into online games, you find that equipment is missing or does not match the position where you last logged off, or even cannot log in with the correct password

It is obvious that someone else logged in for you when you did not log into the game.

Phenomenon 3: Sometimes you suddenly find that your mouse is unresponsive, moving even when you do not touch it, and it even clicks buttons to perform operations

If you didn't move it, then someone else did. Note that this movement of the mouse is significantly different from the automatic drift caused by a low-quality optical mouse. You can feel that someone is controlling your computer.

Phenomenon 4: While surfing the internet normally, you suddenly feel it becoming slow, and the hard drive light is flashing as if you were copying files

This situation is likely due to the attacker trying to copy your files. When a large number of files are being copied, disk read/write activity obviously increases, and the system slows down. At this point, you should unhesitatingly disconnect the network cable and immediately check if there are any abnormal system processes.

Phenomenon 5: When you prepare to use the webcam, the system prompts that the device is already in use

Finished. The attacker has already stolen your webcam usage. In such cases, the webcam's working status is invisible.

We strongly recommend that you cover the lens when you're not using the webcam. When the attacker sees a dark image, they will naturally understand the issue.

Phenomenon 6: When you are not using network resources, you notice that the network card light keeps flashing. If you set it to display status after connection, you will also see the network card icon flashing in the lower right corner of the screen.

Under normal circumstances, when you use fewer or no network resources, the network card's flickering will not be significant, and the data flow transmitted through the network will not be too high.

The above phenomena are basically subjective feelings and are not entirely accurate, but they require your attention.
