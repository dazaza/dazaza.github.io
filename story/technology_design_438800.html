According to a report from AFP in Kuala Lumpur on the fifth day, Malaysian aviation tycoon Fernandes said today that the newly established long-haul budget airline AirAsia X will launch operations in July this year, with tickets to the UK possibly as low as RM9.99 (2.84 USD).

Fernandes said that AirAsia X will open other routes to Europe, China, and India. Fernandes is also the founder of Southeast Asia's largest budget airline, AirAsia.

He said, "AirAsia X will offer a revolutionary product, ensuring passengers enjoy comfort and convenience suitable for long-distance travel."

The newly established airline will be operated by Malaysia's Fly Asian Express (FAX), which is also founded by Fernandes and currently operates only remote routes in Borneo.

AirAsia X will use the budget terminal at Kuala Lumpur International Airport, and seats to the UK can be booked online starting in mid-spring.

Fernandes said that AirAsia X expects to carry 500,000 passengers in its first year of operation and will purchase 20 new aircraft.

He said, "Ticket prices for all routes will average about half the market price." At the launch ceremony, company staff wore shirts embroidered with "9.99" to promote extremely low-cost tickets to the UK.

Fly Asian Express said that AirAsia X will also form alliances with other budget airlines "to enhance connectivity and route networks."

The budget airline AirAsia was founded in December 2001 with only two planes and now operates over a hundred domestic and international routes, flying throughout Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, Singapore, Cambodia, Vietnam, and the Philippines.
