Why can't Michael Jackson come to China?

by kaxiya on 2009-07-07 11:42:13

Tao Duanfang, Visiting Scholar in Canada

The passing of Michael Jackson shocked and saddened fans around the globe. A plethora of commemorative events followed, making it seem as if everyone mourned the loss of an extraordinary musical genius.

Jackson, who received much posthumous honor, was a highly controversial figure during his lifetime. In 1993 and 2005, he became embroiled in child molestation scandals. Some of his famous music videos, such as "Black or White" and "Thriller," were aired in edited versions rather than their original cuts. In the 1990s, he had several live concerts temporarily canceled, earning him the nickname "King of No-Shows." In the new millennium, his tribute song for "9/11" was quickly pulled from shelves after its release.

It is said that Michael Jackson wanted to hold a concert on the Chinese mainland but was ultimately unsuccessful. The reason was that Chinese authorities reviewing his performance found some of Jackson's live concert movements "inappropriate" and "not in line with moral customs in mainland China." This kind of ban was, for many domestic fans, undoubtedly a permanent regret.

Many entertainment and cultural celebrities, like Jackson, possess both remarkable talent and significant flaws. How should society view them? Should cultural censorship departments intervene?

In fact, Michael Jackson faced similar troubles not just in China: his "9/11" memorial hit was removed because one of his collaborators had homosexual tendencies; the original version of the "Black or White" MV has rarely been seen due to more "excessive" bold dance moves considered "unsuitable for children"; his concerts were canceled multiple times, partly due to his own tardiness, but also because of "regulations," which included reasons ranging from parental protests to Jewish groups claiming he had made offensive gestures.

From this, we can see that not only in China, but in any country, there are societal moral standards applied to artists and artworks, and tolerance for those who "cross the line" is limited. Similarly, cultural management departments in every country have their own limits and will block what they deem inappropriate.

The issue is that despite criticism and regulation in many countries, Jackson still managed to perform and appear on screen, whereas in China, he was simply "refused." In many countries, Jackson was warned against "indecent behavior on stage," while in China, he wasn't even allowed on stage.

As the saying goes, genius often coexists with recklessness. Not only in the pop music world, but there are countless figures like Jackson—talented yet problematic. For example, bands like The Rolling Stones and U2, or in sports, Diego Maradona, who could dribble past five players yet used the "Hand of God" and had frequent drug-related scandals.

Maradona's drug use is not exemplary, but his skills on the field cannot be denied. Similarly, certain flaws in the Jacksons of the world can and should be filtered out, but it would be wrong to reject their unique performances entirely based on these reasons.

Some say that pornography and art are separated by a thin line. In commercial performances, what constitutes elegance versus vulgarity, or staying within boundaries versus crossing them, varies depending on individual perspectives. Artistic expression can only be deciphered through an artistic lens. Filtering, reviewing, and gatekeeping all require reliance on an "artistic eye," ensuring that passion isn't mistaken for provocation and fleeting moments of artistic brilliance aren't confused with toxic moments.

During the Wei-Jin period, there were countless drunkards, but few could write immortal poems like Ruan Ji. In the 1960s and 70s in America, the "Beat Generation" numbered in the tens of thousands, but there was only one Rolling Stones band. While people know about Maradona's drug use and Michael Jackson's various "eccentricities," what makes so many people remember and cherish them forever is their ball skills and musical genius. In this sense, Jackson's passing might even bring a sigh of relief to those who loved him—he will no longer create controversy, but his music will endure forever.

Michael Jackson's dream of holding a concert on the Chinese mainland remained unfulfilled, leaving an eternal regret for both him and domestic fans. With the past now gone, what future generations can and should do is, while strictly adhering to moral standards, not forget to approach geniuses with understanding and tolerance.
