Good news for expectant parents planning to have a child in the USA! According to Xinhua News Agency, in 2011, the number of mainland Chinese citizens applying for U.S. visas exceeded one million for the first time, with an approval success rate nearing 90%! This sets a new historical record. Recently, President Obama announced inter-departmental measures aimed at making it easier for business and leisure travelers coming to the United States. The visa service improvement initiatives mainly include:

1. Striving to increase the processing capacity for non-immigrant visa applications from Chinese citizens by 40% this year;

2. Ensuring that 80% of non-immigrant visa applicants are interviewed within three weeks of receiving their application;

3. Expanding the scope of partial visa waivers, including renewals for certain applicants without requiring an interview;

4. Launching a visa pilot program to expedite the processing of non-immigrant visa applications.

According to data from the U.S. Department of State, in the fiscal year 2011, the number of visa applications processed in China increased by 34% compared to the previous fiscal year. The U.S. Department of State stated that measures are being taken to address the growing demand for U.S. visas among Chinese citizens.

Specifically tailored policies for China and Brazil

The main contents of the new policy planned to be launched include:

1. Interview waiting times could potentially be shortened to less than one week (currently, the waiting time for interviews at U.S. consulates in China does not exceed 8 days);

2. Plans to add more visa processing windows: 8 additional ones in Beijing (currently has 14), 22 in Guangzhou, 20 in Shanghai, and 8 in Chengdu;

3. Plans to extend the validity period of visas, providing longer-term visas for Chinese tourists and businessmen, such as multiple-entry visas valid for more than one year;

4. Dispatching 50 additional visa officers (currently, there are fewer than 100 visa officers in China).

According to the head of visa affairs at the U.S. Department of State, in order to promote employment and attract more visitors from increasingly affluent countries to the U.S., the country plans to process four million visa applications in China and Brazil by 2013, with 2.2 million in China and 1.8 million in Brazil. This figure is more than double the current amount, and to achieve this goal, the U.S. Department of State will take various measures in these two countries, including dispatching more visa officers, increasing processing windows, and extending processing hours to improve visa handling capacity. The official said that insufficient personnel fluent in Chinese and Portuguese is one of the main challenges for meeting the visa demands of China and Brazil.
