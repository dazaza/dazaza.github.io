The upward movement of the market encountered pressure, with securities firms and steel sectors taking turns to support the market＿stocks

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:45:22

The Shanghai Composite Index opened low this morning at 3268.00 points, after which the index experienced several waves of bottom probing under the drag of the PetroChina and Sinopec duo along with the banking sector stocks. During trading, it touched its lowest point for the morning session at 3243.75 points. Subsequently, the steel and securities sectors jointly supported the market, causing the index to continuously vibrate and rise. It then touched its highest point for the morning session at 3270.37 points during trading, but under the pressure of yesterday's closing price, the index again vibrated and retreated. From a support and resistance perspective, the index takes the 10-day and 20-day moving averages as potential support, and yesterday's closing price as potential resistance. From a gap perspective on the board, there are still two major short-term gaps below the market at 2635.31 points and 2803.86 points that need to be filled, and the lowest gap below the index at 1762 points has yet to be filled, implying that technically, the Shanghai Composite still has long-term adjustment needs. The next long-term target above the index should be the gap left by the sharp opening lower on January 22 last year, ranging from 4818.00 points to 4891.28 points. Therefore, the outcome of the confrontation between bulls and bears will determine the directional choice of the index. As of noon today when trading was suspended, the Shanghai Composite Index was reported at 3255.61 points, down 14.14 points compared to the closing price of the previous trading day, with a decline of -0.43%, and a turnover of 97.529 billion yuan.

The Shenzhen Component Index also opened low this morning, opening at 13789.08 points. Following the weakness in the real estate, brewing, and electrical appliance sectors, the Shenzhen Component Index narrowly retreated, soon touching its lowest point for the morning session at 13675.07 points. After continuous vibrations washing out funds, the steel and securities sectors significantly strengthened, driving the Shenzhen Component Index to turn red during trading, and touching its highest point for the morning session at 13822.91 points. From a support and resistance perspective, the closing price of the previous day exerted significant pressure near the opening price of today's morning session, while the 10-day moving average provided potential support for the index. From a gap perspective on the board, previously, the Shenzhen Component Index had gaps below at 11874.90 points and 11300.03 points that needed to be filled, and the earlier gap at 7266.41 points still suggested the necessity for a long-term adjustment of the index. As of noon today when trading was suspended, the Shenzhen Component Index was reported at 13734.68 points, down 65.82 points compared to the closing price of the previous trading day, with a decline of -0.48%, and a turnover of 66.191 billion yuan.

From a sector perspective, the initial shareholders of the Shanghai Disneyland have been determined, with Lujiazui holding 60%. Lujiazui opened with a limit-up, stimulating Disney concept stocks to perform relatively strongly after opening. Shares like Jielong Industry, SiMik, and Pudong Jiniao currently lead in gains. General Manager Song Liping of the Shenzhen Stock Exchange stated that margin trading is now ready, with funds becoming both financing targets for margin trading and financial targets for hedging risks using stock index futures. Affected by this news, the stock index futures concept made a comeback, with individual stock surges being strong. Stimulated by rumors of margin trading, securities stocks also performed strongly overall in the early session. The Ministry of Finance announced on November 1st that after the new corn harvest in Northeast China in 2009, the central government would provide one-time fixed-fee subsidies to southern feed-consuming provinces purchasing the new corn and transporting it locally. This news stimulated agricultural stocks to become the largest hotspot in the current market. Additionally, the transportation and logistics sector indices also showed good growth trends. With the global economy warming up, the recent increase in raw material demand has been noticeable, and the recent coal demand peak season has caused some freight companies to experience slight price increases. However, most stocks in the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) fell, but Huayi Brothers and Dayu Water Conservation, which closed at limits yesterday, rose over 4%. In summary, computer application services, trusts, GEM, agriculture, telecommunications equipment, securities, and other sectors currently lead in gains, while banks, aerospace military industry, insurance, nonferrous metals, real estate, and others perform relatively poorly.

As of today's close, a total of 711 stocks in the Shanghai and Shenzhen markets rose, with 16 stocks hitting their daily upper limits, including six S, ST, *ST class stocks. The three newly listed stocks—Santai Electronics, Rihai Communications, and Yazhi Shares—all saw opening gains exceeding 80%. By midday close, the gains were respectively: Santai Electronics up 109.16%, Rihai Communications up 99.96%, and Yazhi Shares up 78.12%. A total of 850 stocks fell across the two markets, with two stocks in the Saige series—Saige Samsung and Shenzhen Saige—hitting their daily lower limits. Among the top 50 weighted stocks in the Shanghai market today, excluding Shanghai Automotive's suspension, 11 blue-chip stocks rose, 34 weighted stocks fell, and 4 large-cap stocks remained flat. Clearly, the short side held a significant advantage among the weighted stocks in today's morning session. For future market opportunities, positioning counter-cyclical rebound varieties at lower levels, adjusting positions, and deploying ahead of time could allow one to enjoy the upward momentum of individual stocks.
