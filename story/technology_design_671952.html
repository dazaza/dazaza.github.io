The price trend of high-end and low-end jadeite is now divided into two poles.

by fbfshtwk0423 on 2012-02-22 14:57:26

A jade processor told a reporter that after the Spring Festival, which is usually the peak sales season for traditional jade, retailers have remained largely inactive this year. The reason being that since two years ago, due to the decreasing availability of green and purple jade materials, previously undervalued colorless or white jade materials have started entering the market. These are now branded with prestigious titles such as "Glass" or "Ice" and have become highly priced. From the beginning of last year to this year, jade raw material prices experienced a dramatic rise and fall, with a continued downward trend, leaving many at a loss.

High-end remains strong, low-end weakens

Shanghai Collectors Association President Wu Shaohua told reporters that this decline has squeezed out the speculative bubble in the market from the past two years of excessive speculation. Thus, the market also teaches consumers a lesson: not all jade can be considered an investment. Wu Shaohua predicts that this round of jade price drops will lead to declines in other art pieces like Huanghuali wood and Shoushan stones. However, Wu Shaohua still reassures holders to remain calm and not panic, as there is a cycle between investment and returns. One cannot expect high returns immediately after a spring auction, "After all, high-end jade, due to the near depletion of raw materials, may appreciate again after stabilization."

Investment returns must have a cycle

What kind of jade catches the eye of big investors? Miao Liyi told reporters that high-end jade must possess three key elements simultaneously: first, it must have a "bright color", with blue, gray, or greenish hues being unacceptable; second, it must be "deep green", with black, bluish-green, or light green being unsuitable; third, it must be of the "Ice" variety, meaning glass-like quality with fine texture, high transparency, and high light refraction. Miao Liyi said that many jade merchants today complain about not being able to procure good materials. Without good materials, they dare not sell their premium goods, leading some merchants to start hoarding their stock. Many high-end jades are now worth over 100 million yuan. Sellers and speculators have had no choice but to transform into collectors. Last Spring Festival, national-level jade carving master Li Boseng's masterpiece "Taking Son to Court", micro-carving master Yuan Yao's jade work "Fishing Alone on the Cold River Snow", along with works by masters Dai Yongcai and Zhang Jianping were exhibited, but this year during the Spring Festival, these works are rarely seen.

Sales of various jade items have cooled down before and after the Spring Festival, breaking the myth of continuous price increases for jade, including emerald and Hetian jade. Recently, CCTV's finance channel reported that the overall jade industry is sluggish, with almost no movement in the Guangdong jade market. Reporters learned yesterday from several major antique jade markets in Shanghai that similar to Hetian jade and emeralds, other types of jade like Huanglong jade that were once heavily speculated on, have also seen both quantity and price declines.

Good materials are hard to come by, and excellent works are rare to see.

Jewelry merchant Zhang Tiejun, who just returned from Myanmar, the world's emerald origin, spoke listlessly about the recently concluded international emerald raw material trading conference because "there were too few good things." At this year's conference, speculators have withdrawn like the tide, yet even so, experts found it difficult to find good emeralds. A few-ton heavy emerald ore might only yield dozens of grams of high-end raw material, hence the price of high-end emeralds continues to rise. On the contrary, with the departure of speculators, the blindly inflated prices of low-end emeralds began to drop. Reporters learned that during this year's Spring Festival, jade pendants, beads, bracelets, and zodiacs made from ordinary emerald materials were very popular, priced in the hundreds to thousands of yuan, with prices dropping by 20-30% compared to a year ago. The price trends of high-end and low-end emeralds are now polarized.

Shanghai Gold Jewelry Association jewelry expert Miao Liyi introduced to reporters that since the second half of last year, due to the withdrawal of speculative capital, the overall emerald market price has decreased. However, the price of high-end emeralds remains strong, even showing "aggressive growth," while the low-end emerald market returns to rationality. "In the past few years, emerald prices were excessively hyped, growing by 10-15% per quarter, and some could increase by more than 50% in a year. Now, real estate developers involved in playing with emeralds have withdrawn large sums of funds used for speculating on mid-to-low-end emeralds, thus causing significant fluctuations in the market within months."
