Unhealthy moving patterns are not conducive to health.

by bbeij62s on 2009-12-03 16:27:15

Exercising to the point of drenching sweat may make one feel as though they've had a more thorough workout, but in reality, it only causes you to lose a few pounds of water. Besides being unhealthy, it doesn't have any other benefits. "One of the things that has puzzled me for three or four decades is that people are still trying to lose weight by sweating more," said Mark Occipinti, president of the American Fitness Professionals Association. "They exercise in high-temperature environments, thinking that doing so will help them lose weight, but in fact, all they're doing is dehydrating themselves." Excessive sweating can also lead to cramps and other sports injuries. When exercising, please keep a bottle of water within reach so you can replenish your fluids at any time.

Reading while exercising

If you focus on reading a fashion magazine, it means you cannot simultaneously concentrate on the exercise you are performing. Amy Hoff, a fitness consultant at New York Sports Club, said that reading while working out is the worst thing to do. "If you're going to exercise, you need to focus on your body," she said. If you need to do something else simultaneously to make your workout less boring, Hoff suggests wearing headphones to watch TV, which does not require as much concentration as reading.

Only riding a stationary bike

Simply riding a stationary bike or running on a treadmill won't provide the effects of strength training. "Walking a mile burns 100 calories; but in the same 20 minutes, if you do weight training with equipment, you can burn 300 to 400 calories," Occipinti said. Strength training can also help you strengthen the muscle groups used in daily life activities such as climbing stairs or carrying heavy objects, and help you maintain muscle shape, delaying muscle relaxation due to age.

Avoiding weightlifting exercises

Women often fear that practicing weightlifting will make them look like bodybuilders.

In fact, there's no need to worry. The belief that weightlifting or strength training will cause women to develop large muscles is a common misconception. "It's impossible unless you're taking growth hormones," Occipinti said. "Weightlifting won't turn you into a scary monster."

Imitating others without understanding

Pretending to know everything when going to the gym won't benefit you. One of the worst habits for gym newbies is to go around the gym and try to imitate what others are doing.

There are usually some trainers in the gym. Occipinti recommends making good use of these trainers. "If you really have questions and want to learn the correct form of exercise, don't hesitate to ask them," she said. "You must know how to avoid sports injuries." Similarly, if you are new to a fitness class and experience any discomfort or concerns, let the instructor know; your body will benefit from it.

Exercising on an empty stomach

Exercising on an empty stomach is like driving a tank without fuel; your body needs energy to function properly.

Some healthy snacks, such as oatmeal or a banana, can be digested during the drive to the gym and provide extra energy needed for your upcoming exercise. This is especially important when exercising in the morning because after a night's rest, your stomach is already empty, and your calories have been consumed. You need to refuel it to get it started again.
