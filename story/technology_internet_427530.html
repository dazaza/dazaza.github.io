56-Year-Old Man Takes Care of Paralyzed Mother for 20 Years (www.dnfaa.com)

by wukong02252 on 2011-05-08 18:36:31

Fan Shusheng, 56 years old, lives at No. 33 of Hedian Road Alley in Yuzhong District and works as a patrol officer in the Hedian Road Community. In 1990, Fan's mother, Liu Benhua, suffered from cerebral infarction which led to hemiplegia. As his three sisters had already married, the responsibility of taking care of their mother fell solely on Fan Shusheng. In 1995, due to personality differences, Fan divorced his wife, and he was left with the custody of their over two-year-old daughter. Since then, apart from looking after his paralyzed mother and raising his young daughter, Fan also had to work to support his family.

Reported by Zhang Shuihong with photography

Every morning at around five-thirty, Fan Shusheng gets up to prepare the meals for the day. He then divides them into portions so that his mother can heat them up with her crutches at noon. Afterwards, he sends his daughter to daycare and rushes to his workplace. Seeing Fan's hardships, many friends and colleagues around him have introduced potential partners to him. "Friends have introduced me to so many people that I've lost count," Fan Shusheng said. Many times, he refused outright without even meeting the person. Even those he met were scared off by his condition - "The partner must be able to take good care of my paralyzed mother and treat my child as if she were her own." "Both the elderly and the young at home need care, it's such a heavy burden, who would willingly take it on?" Fan Shusheng half-jokingly said. Reluctantly, he politely declined the well-meaning matchmakers around him and devoted himself entirely to taking care of his mother and daughter.

Liu Benhua, 77 years old from Yuzhong District, has been paralyzed for 20 years. Her son Fan Shusheng has taken care of her day after day for many years. Therefore, residents of the Hedian Road Community in Yuzhong District all regard Fan Shusheng as an exemplary filial son.

Looking back on the past 20 years, Fan Shusheng repeatedly said: "Tired, indeed very tired." Just for the three meals a day, if he doesn't go home to cook, the whole family would starve; if the floor isn't mopped, the dust accumulates thicker and thicker; if dirty clothes aren't washed, they just pile up... All household chores inside and out are handled by Fan Shusheng alone. After his mother became ill and unable to speak with limited mobility, whenever he has time, Fan chats with his mother: "To keep her from feeling lonely and bored." Although his mother's responses are often just smiles, nods, and shakes of the head, Fan still talks nonstop, talking to himself...

"Mom, are you hungry? I'll go make food right away." During lunch break the other day, Fan Shusheng quickly bought groceries and went home to cook. Because there is a hemiplegic mother waiting for a meal at home, Fan Shusheng must return home for every meal of the day.

Regarding the residents holding him up as a model of filial piety, Fan Shusheng kept saying that he wasn't qualified: "When parents are sick, taking care of them is the duty of children, what I do are just ordinary small things."

Fan Shusheng said, actually, how could he not want someone to share the load with him? How could he not fantasize about the warmth and sweetness of having a family sitting together? But thinking that if the partner didn't take good care of his mother or treated his daughter poorly, he would feel even more distressed, both physically and mentally exhausted, Fan Shusheng chose to endure the hardship himself: "Being tired is just physical fatigue." Thus, he has remained single.
