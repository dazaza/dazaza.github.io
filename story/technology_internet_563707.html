Women love beauty and apply lipstick, which may pose risks to physical health_482

by vhcpth76 on 2011-11-17 08:56:31

99 Health Network Guide: What are the taboos for women using lipstick? The love for beauty is universal, and it is understandable for women to apply lipstick. However, experts warn that women should pay attention to the following when pursuing beauty: First, choose according to your suitability, avoid long-term continuous application of lipstick, and makeup should be light rather than heavy. Second, wash your face before sleeping and thoroughly remove makeup to reduce the irritation of lanolin on the lip mucosa. Third, develop good habits, wipe off lipstick before meals to prevent it from entering the body with food. Fourth, if symptoms such as itching or pain occur after applying lipstick, stop using it immediately, clean it thoroughly, and seek medical attention promptly.

The clinical manifestations of "lipstick disease" include chapped lips, swollen lips, itching, pain, severe cases may present with lip nodules (keratinized lips), and changes in lip mucosa lesions.

According to statistics, about 10% of women who frequently wear lipstick suffer from "lipstick disease." The main components of lipstick are lanolin, wax, and dyes. Lanolin is complex and can easily cause allergic reactions in people with allergic constitutions. Additionally, lanolin has strong adsorption properties, which can attract dust, bacteria, viruses, and some heavy metal ions onto the lips. Special attention should be paid to the fact that certain chemical dyes in lipstick can be harmful to the human body. If one does not pay attention while drinking water or eating, these harmful substances can enter the body and affect health.

Women should pay attention to the following when pursuing beauty:

1. Choose according to individual suitability, avoid long-term continuous use of lipstick, and prefer light makeup over heavy makeup.

2. Wash your face before sleeping and thoroughly remove makeup to reduce the irritation of lanolin on the lip mucosa.

3. Develop good habits, wipe off lipstick before meals to prevent it from entering the body with food.

4. If symptoms such as itching or pain occur after applying lipstick, stop using it immediately, clean it thoroughly, and seek medical attention promptly.

99 Health Network Warm Reminder: Women like to use lipstick, but do you understand the risks it poses to the human body? If you enjoy using lipstick, make sure to fully understand these risks. (Editor: Fan Xuejiao)
