Finger Regeneration Network

by akuijielun on 2009-11-02 10:35:47

For any reason causing the amputation or absence of the fingertip (toe tip), whether it is an old or new injury, it can be treated. We no longer use surgical treatments such as replantation of the fingertip, finger reconstruction, shortening and suturing, skin grafting, or flaps for treating severed fingers.

We use a traditional Chinese medicine technique called "regeneration of severed fingers (toes)." There is no need for injections or oral antibiotics; simply by applying the external ointment "Shenyi Jinkui Powder," a finger identical to the original one (including muscle, blood vessels, nerves, nail, and fingerprint) can regrow within 100 days, with its function restored as before.

This technology fills the international gap in finger regeneration techniques and adds a brilliant page to the history of traditional Chinese medicine. It overcomes the drawbacks of microsurgery in hand surgery for finger replantation. In fact, we have possessed this technology since 1984, curing a large number of patients and accumulating rich clinical experience, which was 21 years earlier than the United States' finger regeneration research. Dean Jiao is truly the father of world finger regeneration!

In April 2008, the U.S. military invested in a project named "Regenerative Medicine Research on Human Tissue" with a budget of $250 million. In May 2008, the U.S. government invested over $500 million into finger regeneration research. The U.S. military stated that this is the largest investment in the field of regenerative medicine in the United States. The U.S. government believes that finger regeneration technology holds the potential for a new industry worth billions of dollars.
