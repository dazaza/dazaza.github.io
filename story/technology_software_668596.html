2. Why Make It a Forum

Forums are known to not collect advertising fees as well as regular web pages. They also don't make as much money as regular web page sites. However, I think forums are lively, which is why I like them. Because they gather a circle of people, making it easy to retain users. They have strong interactivity. But this isn't absolute; not all sites should be turned into forums. This article only provides forum operation experience, so those who don't like forums don't need to read it.

3. What Kind of Forum to Create

I suggest everyone choose based on your interests and resources. Forums in any industry can succeed. Personally, I feel excellent examples include Tianya, an industry-specific example like Houtai, and Xike Webmaster Forum, both of which are quite accomplished.

4. What Kind of Space Do You Need for a Forum

If your forum attachments are small or few, the required space is less. The forum program is usually only a few MBs, but the database gradually grows larger. This is a specific issue that needs specific analysis.

5. How Many "Diligent Moderators" Are Needed for Your Forum

If you want to operate a forum, it's best not to rely on just one person. Recruit moderators as soon as possible. Initially, you can adopt measures such as Q-coin rewards. Moderators need to work hard at the beginning. At least, the content updates need to come up. I do not recommend collecting content from other sources (personal opinion). Attachments should be localized as much as possible because some resources may become outdated. Besides updating content, moderators need to set rules for each section, register some small accounts, post replies to create an atmosphere where it seems like many people are replying.

6. New Forums Need Some "Actions"

Since new forums are cold and rarely attract people who like to stay, your promotion and retention won't be effective. It's suggested to appropriately take some "actions." Nowadays, most forums have virtual user count systems, and you can register a batch of members yourself. Don't have too many, and ensure the content and reply volume are proportional. For instance, you can start with 20 online members and make sure the detailed online list is displayed (of course, once the forum grows larger, this isn't necessary).

7. Forum Reward Systems

Besides moderators, you need to retain members. At least, the content needs to attract these members. To create an atmosphere, new forums can use a system where posts are hidden until replied to. However, pictures and attachments must be readable by guests. Clear reward systems should be established. For active members, upgrade permissions, promote positions, distribute forum coins, Q-coins, or physical rewards. These are all good strategies.

8. Establish a "Consumption System"

Forum virtual currency is the motivation for users to post and is also a symbol of identity. Many members like being the "rich people" in forums. For example, downloading a post attachment might cost a certain amount of money, while posting content adds a certain amount of money. This needs to be adjusted according to your own forum.
