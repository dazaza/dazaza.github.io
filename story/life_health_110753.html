Exercise can prevent impotence (www.jn9l.com)

by sunshine on 2008-12-11 16:04:19

Erectile dysfunction (ED) in males refers to the inability to achieve or maintain an erection when there is a desire for sexual activity, or having an erection but not hard enough. Alternatively, although an erection occurs with a certain degree of hardness, it cannot be sustained long enough for sexual intercourse, thus hindering or preventing the completion of intercourse.

Many office workers dislike exercising and are accustomed to staying in air-conditioned rooms. Once they exercise, they become out of breath, sweat profusely, and experience body aches for a week. Many people view exercise as a burdensome task. However, exercise has numerous benefits; apart from helping you maintain a good figure, it also significantly aids cardiovascular health. The advantages don't stop there. According to recent studies, exercise can greatly benefit men's sex lives. A large-scale study led by Professor Zhang Zhibin at Jinan Jiulong Urology Hospital showed that men who consume at least 200 calories through daily exercise have a much lower probability of experiencing erectile dysfunction compared to those who do not exercise.

Many middle-aged men, after entering middle age, have stable jobs and are already married, no longer needing to maintain their appearance, thus losing the habit of exercising. Or they believe that since they don't smoke or drink excessively, their weight is relatively standard, and they have no major health issues, exercise isn't necessary. However, when the topic involves "sexual function," fewer men may hold an indifferent attitude. Professor Zhang believes that the results of this study might encourage some men to start exercising.

Exercise can prevent both cardiovascular disease and erectile dysfunction, which is based on the same principle—whether there is sufficient blood flow to the organs that need it. Exercise keeps the blood vessels clear. In fact, erectile dysfunction can be seen as an early warning sign of cardiovascular disease because when the blood cannot circulate properly within the body, the state of penile erection becomes more apparent.

There are many forms of exercise, and it doesn't necessarily mean going to the gym. Walking counts as a great form of exercise. The only unsuitable exercise is "bicycle riding" because it increases the likelihood of erectile dysfunction.

In the United States, one-quarter of men over the age of sixty-five suffer from erectile dysfunction. Some resort to taking Viagra to improve the problem of insufficient penile blood flow. On average, every three seconds, a Viagra pill is consumed. However, taking Viagra is not a fundamental solution. The correct approach should involve prevention rather than seeking medical help after experiencing ED. But once someone truly suffers from erectile dysfunction, it's important not to avoid treatment and to seek timely care at a reputable hospital.

Recently, under the joint efforts of Jinan Jiulong Urology Hospital and the U.S. National Clinical Laboratory (NCCLS), the national key medical research project "STR AiPu Sexual Function Rehabilitation Technique" achieved a breakthrough, allowing 5 million patients with erectile dysfunction to regain sexual happiness. This technology eliminates the shortcomings of traditional methods, such as lack of lasting effects and significant side effects, by directly activating dormant sexual nerve cells, strongly and persistently exciting the patient’s central nervous system related to sexuality. This makes the treatment of erectile dysfunction safer and more enduring. Due to his participation in this research, Professor Zhang Zhibin of Jinan Jiulong Urology Hospital was honored by the Chinese Urological Academic Conference and the European Urological Academic Conference, becoming the first Chinese person to ascend the international urological academic lecture platform globally.
