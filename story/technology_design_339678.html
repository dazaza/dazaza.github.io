8, Xin Ting Marquis Times have changed, during the Northern Song Dynasty, a famous blacksmith Han Qi discovered the site of the Shang Dynasty's Great Temple in a deep mountain and opened the seal to obtain the fragments of the three evil knives: Dragon Fang, Tiger Wing, and Dog Spirit. At that time, "there was an ominous black vapor within the broken iron, which would trigger upon contact." Later, after a joint effort by Han Qi and the court swordsmiths, it took one year and eight days to forge the three guillotine knives - Subdue Dragon, Suppress Tiger, and Sever Dog. These were personally approved by the Emperor of Song Dynasty to be stored in Kaifeng Prefecture. Bao Zheng, who served as the Magistrate of Kaifeng Prefecture at that time, became the first wielder of the "Three Kaifeng Guillotines."

Evaluation: Purely an "evil artifact" destined to kill its owner, comparable to Japan's famous blade "Demon Sword Muramasa."

3.4.5, Dragon Fang, Tiger Wing, Dog Spirit Ancient renowned knives, originally owned by Duke Wen of Jin among the Five Hegemons of the Spring and Autumn period. In later generations, it was said that during the third Jin-Chu war, they were defeated by the famous sword Zhan Lu.

10, Pao Ding Cleaver One of the three great ancient evil weapons, the maker is unknown. It is said that the forging materials used many poisonous substances and were bound by various curses. During the late Xia Dynasty, it was owned by King Jie, and subsequently, tyranny began. The three knives were enshrined in the Xia Dynasty's Great Temple. According to historical records, when King Tang of Shang attacked the Xia Dynasty's Great Temple, dark clouds covered the sky, ghosts wailed, and spirits cried. The three great evil knives—Dragon Fang, Tiger Wing, and Dog Spirit—transformed into three demonic winds and attacked, instantly causing numerous casualties in the Shang army. King Tang dismounted from his horse, discarded his spear, and wielding the tactical straight sword made of the Yellow Emperor's Xuan Yuan Gold Sword, single-handedly broke into the main hall of the Great Temple, swiftly slashing with the sword. The three great evil knives were struck into fragments and sealed underground.

Evaluation: Just from the material's credentials, Ming Hong Dao could be compared with the Xuan Yuan Sword. If it had achieved some victories in the Battle of Zhu Lu, its status would not be inferior to the number one sword in the world, the Xuan Yuan Gold Sword. However, the Yellow Emperor feared it might "steal the show," thus sealing the future of this famous knife.

Evaluation: Qing Long Yan Yue, just like Guan Yu, has become the embodiment of "god" in the hearts of the Chinese people. It can be said that among the knives I have reviewed today, it is one of the more successful ones. Regardless of its material, history, achievements, or aura, there is nothing to criticize, truly deserving the title of "Ancestor of Chinese Knives."

Evaluation: Almost all knives are lethal weapons for killing, but this particular knife promotes the Taoist classic philosophy of health preservation. Going with the flow, being passively inactive, letting everything happen naturally, even ordinary objects can be "effortlessly sharp." A knife doesn't need to be sharp, but rather in the hands of the person using it. Pao Ding Cleaver is the most simple and unadorned knife, yet it is the most perfect knife.

9, Cold Moon Miao Dao, a type of short knife. The blade is 12 inches long and curves outward. The back follows the edge and bends, with two blood grooves on each side and two wave-like nail print patterns. The edge is exceptionally sharp, with a handle measuring three to four inches long, made from two pieces of wood, buffalo horn, or animal bone, clamped together and fixed with rivets.

In ancient times, when the Golden Sword of Emperor Huang Di was forged, there were leftover raw materials. Due to the high temperature not dissipating, the still molten casting materials spontaneously flowed to the bottom of the furnace, cooling into a natural knife shape. Emperor Huang Di believed that its spontaneous knife intent was too strong, enough to counteract its wielder. Fearing that this knife would fall into human hands, Emperor Huang Di attempted to destroy it with the Xuan Yuan Sword, but unexpectedly, the knife transformed into a cloud sparrow, turning into a red streak and disappearing into the clouds.

6, Da Xia Long Que The battle knife used by the famous general Guan Yu at the end of the Eastern Han Dynasty, belonging to the heavy cavalry broadsword type. Having gone through countless battles, it was invincible, so there’s no need to elaborate further; it is indeed a "common knowledge" worthy of its name.

At the end of the Han Dynasty, when General Zhang Fei was initially conferred the title of Marquis of Xin Ting, he ordered the blacksmiths to smelt Chi Zhu Mountain iron into a knife, which he carried with him. After Guan Yu's death, Zhang Fei embarked on a campaign against Eastern Wu to avenge his brother. Before setting out, he drunkenly whipped his soldiers, who could no longer endure it. Taking advantage of the night, they sneaked into Zhang Fei's camp, using the Xin Ting Marquis knife to decapitate Zhang Fei, then fled to Eastern Wu under cover of darkness.

Evaluation: A typical case of "novices wielding divine weapons," what's the point of a garbage assassin having such good equipment? It's purely a waste of resources.
