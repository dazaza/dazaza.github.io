Baidu Corporate Promotion

[Baidu's Core Values]

• Providing people with the most convenient way to query information

　 • Always maintaining an entrepreneurial spirit

　 • Making progress every day

　 • Tolerating failure and encouraging innovation

　 • Fully trusting employees and promoting equal communication

[Baidu's Mission]

　 • To provide network users with the highest level of network technical services, creating a business miracle for Chinese Internet companies

　 • To make Baidu a leader in brand, user satisfaction, and operational performance within the industry

　 • To enhance the technological component of China's Internet and help more Internet companies become profitable faster

[Baidu's Vision]

　 • To become the best provider of Chinese information retrieval and delivery technology on the Internet

　 • To be an excellent representative of global network technology enterprises in China

[Baidu's Value Concepts]

• Employee Philosophy

　　 • Employees are the most important assets

　　 • Employee management philosophy based on trust and respect

　　 • Listening to employee opinions, considering employee interests, motivating employees, ensuring fairness, etc.

　 Efficiency Philosophy

　　 • Accurate budgeting

　　 • Strict systems

　　 • Open and relevant communication

　 Competition Philosophy

　　 • Results-oriented

　　 • Ensuring capability

　　 • Setting direction

　　 • Implementing responsibility

　 Upstream and Downstream Philosophy

　　 • Promoting sales, focusing on sales

　　 • Reducing costs, focusing on cost control

　 Change Philosophy

　　 • The future will be different from the past

　　 • Responding to challenges with regular changes

　　 • Always pursuing new perspectives, new services, new technologies, and new goals

[Product History]

January 2000 Two young men returned from the U.S. with venture capital from Silicon Valley to Beijing's Zhongguancun to create Baidu.

May 2000 Baidu signed its first client, Silicon Power (enet.com), and Baidu products began serving users.

June 2000 Baidu officially launched the world's largest, fastest, and latest Chinese search engine and announced its full entry into China's Internet technology field.

August 2000 Baidu started providing services to Sohu (sohu.com).

September 2000 Baidu officially launched the Net Story Information Retrieval Software aimed at enterprise-level users.

September 2000 DFJ, IDG, and other internationally renowned venture capital firms invested substantial funds in Baidu.

October 2000 Baidu started providing services to Sina (sina.com).

October 2000 Baidu established its Shenzhen branch.

January 2001 Baidu provided comprehensive search services to 263.

February 2001 Baidu provided comprehensive search services to TOM.COM.

August 2001 Launched the Beta version of the Baidu.com search engine, transitioning from backend service to independent search service provision and pioneering the pay-per-click ranking business model in China.

October 22, 2001 Officially launched the Baidu search engine.

June 2001 Baidu established its Shanghai office.

October 2001 Baidu provided the global Chinese web page retrieval system for Shanghai Online.

October 2001 The People's Bank of China Financial Information Management Center adopted Baidu's "Net Story Database Retrieval" software.

January 2002 CCTV International fully integrated Baidu's "Net Story" information retrieval software.

March 2002 Baidu President Robin Li was selected as one of the "Top Ten Entrepreneurial New Stars in China."

March 2002 Baidu took over all aspects of 263's search channel.

March 2002 Baidu initiated the "Lightning Plan," continuously maintaining a technological leading edge advantage.

May 2002 The Qianlong-Baidu Chinese Information Retrieval Technology Laboratory was established.

June 2002 Baidu launched the popular "IE Search Companion."

July 2002 Baidu launched the industry's first "Competitive Intelligence System" software and held a national tour exhibition.

August 2002 Baidu and NetEase entered into a deep collaboration, with pay-per-click rankings significantly enhanced.

September 2002 Digital China, Sinopec signed agreements with Baidu for the corporate competitive intelligence system; the State Council Information Office signed agreements with Baidu for the news monitoring system.

October 2002 The national agent conference for Baidu's pay-per-click ranking business was held; Baidu began providing services to Yahoo! China (yahoo.com.cn). Yahoo! also joined Baidu's pay-per-click ranking program.

November 2002, released MP3 search; launched the Search Tycoon game, which saw enthusiastic participation from the general public; and began providing services to NetEase (netease.com).

December 2002 China Mobile signed agreements with Baidu for the corporate competitive intelligence system; international well-known companies such as Konka, Lenovo, Coca-Cola became clients of Baidu's pay-per-click ranking service.

January 2003 Baidu President Robin Li received the title of "First Annual Top Ten IT Influential Figures in China."

February 2003 Launched the commonly used search function, integrating various small practical functions together for easy user access;

March 2003 Baidu held a large event at the Century Theatre in Beijing titled "Living Search, Changing Life - Baidu Search Passion Night."

June 2003 The "10,000 People Public Evaluation" conducted by the Computer Education Newspaper under the Third Party Tsing Research Group published its evaluation results. Baidu surpassed Google to become the preferred search engine for Chinese netizens.

June 2003 Baidu launched the Chinese Search Hot List;

June 2003 According to statistics from Alexa, a third-party authoritative American statistical agency, among the most popular Chinese websites, Baidu had already ranked fourth, indicating that Baidu had become the world's largest Chinese search engine.

July 2003 Baidu launched two technically advanced search engines for images and news, consolidating its position as the top Chinese search engine in the industry.

August 2003 Launched the Baidu website channel.

September 2003 Baidu carried out the "September Marketing Revolution," conducting market promotion activities for the paid search service "Pay-Per-Click Ranking" in nearly 100 cities across the country, achieving significant market impact.

October 2003 Baidu President Robin Li received the honorary title of "Outstanding Entrepreneur of the Second Session of the Zhongguancun Science Park."

December 2003 On December 1st, Baidu successively launched revolutionary functions such as regional search and "Tieba," marking the entry of search engines into the community era; simultaneously released were three functions: advanced search, time search, and news reminders.

January 2004 Baidu and Hurun jointly created the "2003 China Rich List Popularity Rankings."
