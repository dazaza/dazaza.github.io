Frog language

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 11:44:06

Spring is drawing near.

On the willow branches by the roadside, there are faintly plump buds in the making.

In the vacant lot behind the building, tender green stems and leaves are stretching out delicately.

On the streets, simple and elegant T-shirts are spreading out while playful and bright long skirts are fluttering. In the alleys, clear child voices jump around, echoing off the gray walls, and by the stone benches, gentle whispers confirm the bright spring sunshine in the sky.

Since school started on the seventeenth day of the first lunar month, the little one has been counting on their fingers.

That year, that month, that day, a little one came into this world.

The little one is now eight years old.

Could it be, could it be -- that this day isn't extremely important? The certainty is all over the little one's eyes: Hmm, what kind of cake should I choose? What kind of gift will I get? This needs some serious thought...

Ha, this little lamb of spring!

A red and white pencil case, a little princess doll in a light purple dress, ignited a spark of joy in the little one's eyes. The little one grows up day by day, it's not easy to find gifts that make them leap with joy, but even a small spark of joy is enough, ha.

The day before, the little one was asked: You don't like eating cake anyway, so let's skip it, okay? The little one dragged out their voice and said: Well... well... well, okay!

Thinking about it, formality is still quite necessary. The cake represents love. And love, in everyday life, has been diluted by trivialities into a form of restraint and control. On this special day, let it take center stage once again.

So, at noon today, when the little one was led to the dining table by two adults and saw the white little lamb quietly lying on a pink base surrounded by two grapes, two slices of lemon, and two cherries, their eyes lit up, ha.

Little lamb of spring, happy birthday, may every day of your life be filled with happiness.

The phone had been water-damaged and wasn't functioning well, so a Nokia was purchased online.

Wall (a nickname) has always been quiet, and these days after work, he sits silently on the sofa studying the Nokia intently. That look clearly resembles a mischievous child.

Early spring, with its sudden warmth followed by cold, Wall dug out last year's navy blue suit. Little Lamb praised Daddy for being handsome. Handsome? His face is slightly dark, his expression honest, mature and steady as a clock. It's true that he doesn't easily attract attention, hehe.

Looking at the navy blue suit, the housewife remembered last year.

Last year, also during early spring, Wall encountered difficulties at work.

During those days, Wall lost his appetite and couldn't sleep peacefully. In the evenings, he no longer went online, instead grabbing a pen, furrowing his brow, scribbling until late at night. In the mornings, as soon as the housewife opened her eyes, Wall was already staring at the ceiling with a furrowed brow, deep in thought...

The housewife watched all this in her heart, sometimes silently accompanying him, sometimes earnestly consoling him. Many times, the two of them, nearing middle age, embraced and wept together...

Wall's low mood lasted for two or three months before slowly improving.

Fortunately, all that is in the past.

At this time this year, looking at Wall seriously playing with the phone on the sofa, she recalls the lyrics: "You are happy, so I am joyful."

Lifting her head, she looks at the distant sky.

Earthquakes, tsunamis, nuclear radiation.

These terms, mixed with the March spring breeze, blow over coldly. Ethnic grievances, the fragility of life, the gravity of disasters, human compassion -- countless words converge into a single wish -- Peace.

Melamine had just been forgotten when clenbuterol took center stage.

Peace. The word "peace" weighs heavier than ever. Thousands of words, only transformed into a long sigh -- Peace, how hard.

She retracts her gaze.

The small city quietly rests on the Loess Plateau.

Familiar neighborhoods, similar years pass by.

Isn't this tranquility a form of happiness?

Even this humble woman. Disapproves of "everything is ephemeral," only wanting to guard her small plot of land, live in a modest home, with loved ones, children, gently holding onto the threads in her hands, living a stable life.

This is Frog Talk, from Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd.
