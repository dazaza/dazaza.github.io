Shanghai Industry and Commerce Bureau launches new policies for college students to register companies

by xiaorenwu128 on 2009-05-03 11:53:36

Compared with the general public entrepreneurship, university graduates face greater risks in starting a business due to the lack of social experience and sufficient start-up capital. The country has provided certain economic and policy incentives to encourage college students to start their own businesses. Fully understanding these incentives and skillfully applying these policies is a right that every college student entrepreneur should not give up.

Since 2003, Anhui Province has implemented a policy of exempting administrative charges for registration and management for college graduates who engage in individual businesses.

According to relevant regulations, recent college graduates who engage in individual businesses, except for industries restricted by the state (including construction, entertainment, advertising, sauna, massage, internet cafes, etc.), will be exempt from all administrative charges for registration and management within one year from the date the industry and commerce department approves their operation.

The specific exempted fees include: individual industrial and commercial household registration fee, individual industrial and commercial household management fee, market management fee, economic contract certification fee, economic contract model text cost; tax registration certificate cost; private medical institution management fee, health monitoring fee, health quality inspection fee, preventive physical examination fee, preventive vaccination labor fee, health permit cost; private non-enterprise unit registration fee (including certificate fee); labor contract certification fee, vocational qualification certificate fee; special industry license cost; tobacco monopoly retail license fee (including temporary retail license fee); other registration and management fees related to individual operations approved by the State Council, the Ministry of Finance, and the National Development and Reform Commission.

The conditions required for exemption from relevant fees are: the individual must present their ID card, graduation certificate, and valid documents approving individual business operations issued by the industry and commerce department to the relevant charging units such as industry and commerce, taxation, and health departments.
