Leading real estate enterprises

by jinyi268 on 2012-02-14 17:22:49

The sales area of leading real estate enterprises has dropped sharply, which may lead to a new round of price cuts. (Jinyi Futures Studio http://hexun.com/jinyiqihuo/default.html Professional futures research.) The sales data in January was gradually disclosed, showing a sharp year-on-year decline in both the sales area and sales amount. The real estate market became increasingly depressed, increasing the pressure on real estate enterprises to recover funds. Industry insiders pointed out that the poor sales at the beginning of the year, along with the recent firm statements from management, will prompt developers to recognize the situation faster. Some leading real estate enterprises are already preparing for a new round of price cuts to promote sales. (Jinyi Futures Studio http://hexun.com/jinyiqihuo/default.html Professional futures research.)

Sales suffered a heavy blow at the beginning of the year. Vanke's sales brief for January showed that it achieved a sales area of 1.19 million square meters and sales revenue of 12.2 billion yuan, representing year-on-year declines of 28% and 40%, respectively.

While sales were declining, Vanke slowed down its acquisition of new projects. Since the release of the December 2011 sales brief, Vanke has only newly participated in the renovation project of the Wen Chong Petrochemical Road West urban village in Huangpu, Guangzhou. The land area for development handled by Vanke is about 129,000 square meters, with an expected comprehensive renovation cost of approximately 1.497 billion yuan.

According to the report from Shanghai E-House Real Estate Research Institute, among the 10 typical cities included in the statistics, the total commercial residential property transaction volume in January was 2.14 million square meters, a significant year-on-year drop of 71%.

As of January 2012, the total inventory of newly built commercial residential properties in China's 10 typical cities was 59.01 million square meters. Compared to the same period last year, there was an additional inventory of 17.01 million square meters, an increase of about 40.5%. Month-on-month, it decreased by 1.62%, but the inventory remained at a high level. The downward pressure on housing prices further increased.

A quiet new wave of price cuts is approaching. The sudden increase in sales pressure and the pessimistic market expectations have led real estate enterprises to lower their sales targets for 2012 one after another.

Greentown China, which frequently faced crises last year, reduced its sales target from 55 billion yuan in 2011 to 40 billion yuan in 2012, an increase of only 4.7 billion yuan compared to its actual sales amount of 35.3 billion yuan in 2011.

While cautiously setting their annual sales targets, many real estate enterprises have quietly started a new round of price cuts to promote sales. Recently, reporters from the China Securities News (Weibo) found in the Shanghai market that some flagship projects of leading real estate enterprises began to lower prices again, with prices falling compared to the fourth quarter of 2011. Vanke's Golden Domain project in Jiading District, Shanghai, launched a 98,888 yuan discount during the Valentine's Day period. The 89-square-meter fully furnished small three-bedroom apartment starts at 1.1 million yuan after the discount.

To cope with the sales difficulties, Greentown Chairman Song Weiping recently stated that they will comprehensively reform their sales model, shifting from passively waiting for customers to actively seeking them, while significantly increasing sales commissions in hopes of reversing the weak sales situation.
