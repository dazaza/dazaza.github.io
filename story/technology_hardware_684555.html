A young man from Zhengzhou, Niu Yuliang, expressed his dissatisfaction after reading an online article about a company's recruitment notice stating "Henan natives already fully recruited" and Shenzhen Human Resources and Social Security Bureau's response claiming it did not constitute discrimination.

At around 9 in the morning yesterday, Niu Yuliang sent a suggestion letter to the Shenzhen Human Resources and Social Security Bureau, suggesting administrative penalties for the Shenzhen enterprise that posted the "Henan natives already fully recruited" recruitment advertisement, ordering it to take responsibility for its illegal actions. "As long as one has the ability, regardless of where they're from, they should have the opportunity to get the job," he said.

It is understood that on February 4th, Mr. Cai, a native of Henan, while job hunting in Shenzhen, found a "Henan natives already fully recruited" recruitment advertisement in a street advertisement board, which caused his dissatisfaction.

Mr. Cai stated, "The subtext of 'Henan natives already fully recruited' is not hiring Henan people. My colleagues joked about this, but I think it's employment discrimination."

A salesperson from the Shenzhen enterprise responded, "Our company has around 100 people, with 43 being from Henan. Many Henan people have become backbone members of the company and are doing very well. How can the company discriminate? We just want to give people from other provinces some opportunities."

Renowned anti-discrimination public welfare lawyer Li Fangping believes that according to the "fair employment" principle in the Employment Promotion Law, whether it be any type of job position, the main consideration should be whether the work capability matches. Factors unrelated to the job requirements, such as household registration, age, and social identity, should not be considered; otherwise, it constitutes suspected employment discrimination.

National People's Congress representative and known as the "migrant worker commander," Zhang Quanshou, chairman of Shenzhen Quanshun Human Resources Development Co., Ltd., stated that nearly 90% of the staff in his company are from Henan, and he has sent many Henan people to numerous enterprises in Shenzhen, receiving widespread recognition from these enterprises.

Zhang Quanshou believes, "This Shenzhen enterprise is nearly half Henan people, proving that Henan people are recognized by companies. However, writing 'Henan natives already fully recruited' in the recruitment announcement is inappropriate and should be removed."

In the afternoon yesterday, Mr. Cai told reporters that the "Henan natives already fully recruited" recruitment advertisement had been removed from the original advertisement board.
