ISO/TS 16949 standard certification

by chuqi1059 on 2010-05-29 10:41:28

The ISO/TS 16949 standard (hereinafter referred to as TS16949) is officially named "Quality System - Automotive Supplier - Special Requirements for the Application of ISO9001:1994". It is commonly abbreviated as the ISO/TS 16949 standard. Currently, the international automotive industry is preparing to implement audit and certification activities based on the ISO/TS 16949 quality system. A globally recognized quality system certification specifically tailored for the automotive industry will replace national automotive industry quality system certifications. The ISO/TS 16949 Quality Management System Standard was jointly drafted by the automotive departments of the United States, France, Germany, Italy, and the United Kingdom, and was jointly developed by the national automotive federations under the International Automotive Task Force (IATF), including ANFIA, AIAG, ISO13485 certification, CCFA, FIEV, SMMT, and VDA. After formal approval by GM, it became known as ISMS and was adopted equivalently with various national automotive standards (such as VDA6.1, QS9000, AVSQ, and EAFQ). This means that companies with a TS 16949 certification do not need to undergo QS9000 or VDA6.1 certification. In other words, the new quality management standard, ISO/TS 16949, will primarily benefit automotive suppliers whose customers are located in different countries, as they will no longer need multiple certifications.

The ISO/TS 16949 standard incorporates modern quality engineering concepts, methods, and technologies recognized by the automotive industry, providing highly detailed and specific content. For example, it requires the use of advanced quality planning and potential failure mode and effects analysis (PFMEA), emphasizing customer satisfaction, continuous improvement, multi-party argumentation, and characteristics of products and production processes. Wang Binggang explained: Potential Failure Mode and Effects Analysis refers to identifying issues that do not meet customer requirements before product launch. This avoids situations where products are modified while being sold after launch, focusing on post-launch quality improvements. By reviewing whether two basic conditions are met, it ensures that the products launched are of high quality. Although similar content exists in the ISO9001 standard, the new standard provides more detailed and automotive-specific requirements.

The content of the ISO/TS 16949 standard closely resembles the QS9000 quality system developed by the Big Three American automakers and also incorporates the quality control standards compiled by the German Association of the Automotive Industry (VDA), as well as the main contents of the quality system documents prepared by Italian and potential automotive industrial organizations. For domestic Sino-American, Sino-German, Sino-French, Sino-Italian, and Sino-British joint venture automotive enterprises, this standard aligns closely with the quality system standards they currently implement.

**Benefits of ISO/TS 16949**

ISO/TS 16949 became an ISO-approved technical specification (TS) for the automotive industry in mid-1999. The automotive industry expects the following benefits:

1. Improvement in product quality and process quality.

2. Increased credibility in procurement processes worldwide.

3. Release of resources previously used for numerous quality tasks.

4. Compliance with standard requirements at the start of the supply chain.

5. More unified interpretations through the use of a common language.

6. Reduced number of customer audits on systems.

7. Reduced third-party duplicate certifications.
