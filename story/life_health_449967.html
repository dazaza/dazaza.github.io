So how long after pregnancy can it be detected?

Experts kindly remind: Actually, the question of how long after pregnancy can it be detected is quite simple. Women who are mindful in daily life should know. Let's take a look at the expert's introduction.

The most common and earliest reaction of pregnancy is delayed menstruation. If your menstrual cycle is usually regular, you had sexual intercourse without any contraception, but your menstruation is delayed for no reason for more than 10 days, then your first reaction should be to consider if you are pregnant. Another situation is if your menstrual cycle is not always on time. If this menstrual cycle is delayed by more than 10 days, you cannot make an early judgment about pregnancy. You should look for other signs.

Firstly, if your menstrual period is prolonged and you experience symptoms such as dizziness, fatigue, cold intolerance, sleepiness, loss of appetite, accompanied by varying degrees of nausea, picky eating, craving sour food, or aversion to greasy foods and special odors, etc., secondly, it should be particularly noted that some people's appetites and preferences may suddenly change. One moment you want to eat one thing, the next moment you want something else. Foods you usually like you don't want to eat anymore, and foods you didn't like before you now crave. In severe cases, vomiting may occur, or there may be obvious symptoms such as breast swelling pain, darkened nipples and areola with stabbing pain, and frequent urination. At this point, you can roughly determine that you are pregnant. You can buy a pregnancy test strip and test at home. Experts suggest that morning urine is generally more accurate, which is the first urine when you get up in the morning. Testing at other times is also possible, but sometimes it is not very accurate.

In fact, under normal circumstances, if women pay attention, they can generally detect pregnancy around 18 days after intercourse or 7-10 days after the menstrual period is delayed. After the sperm and egg combine into a fertilized egg, it takes 6-8 days to implant in the uterine lining, then 7-10 days for the placenta-secreted HCG to enter the urine. The working principle of the pregnancy test strip is to detect HCG levels, i.e., human chorionic gonadotropin levels. This hormone is produced by the placenta and will appear in the urine a few days after pregnancy, but due to the small amount, it is not easy to detect initially. It becomes increasingly evident between 10-14 days. Adding both periods together, detecting pregnancy from urine 18 days after intercourse is scientific.

The above is the answer to the question of how long after pregnancy can it be detected. Female friends should have a general understanding. Experts say that the detection time varies from person to person. Some may be able to detect it with just a one-day delay in menstruation.
