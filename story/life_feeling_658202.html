A Person Stands Alone Amidst the Fallen Flowers, Swallows Fly in Pairs in a Drizzle

After several days of scorching 30-degree weather, Guiyang has finally seen some rain. Undoubtedly, this has brought a refreshing drop in temperature to the stifling heat, and the cool air invigorates the spirit. Even before dawn, birds were chirping incessantly outside my window. Upon opening the window, a fragrant breeze carrying the scent of soil mixed with fallen flowers greeted me. Beneath the gardenia tree, petals scattered across the ground after being beaten by the rain. A sudden thought came to mind: "A person stands alone amidst the fallen flowers, swallows fly in pairs in a drizzle." This is a line from Yan Xiaoshan's poem "Lín Jiāng Xiān," which I greatly admire. Xiaoshan was the seventh son of Yan Shu, the renowned Song Dynasty poet who served as prime minister, and he is known as Xiao Yan. Both he and his father Yan Shu were adherents of the ci form known as xiaoling (small tunes). After Xiaoshan, the small tune ci gradually disappeared.

An Yiru has already depicted Yan Xiaoshan clearly. For such a talented individual, capable of perfecting the small tune ci to such an exquisite level, it is truly admirable. Xiaoshan experienced great highs and lows in his life. From a young age, he was accustomed to luxury and wealth amidst silk and powder. His father, Yan Shu, held the position of Academician of the Jixian Pavilion and passed away at the age of sixty-five during Emperor Renzong's second year of Zhihe. Yan Shu was also a famous poet. Following his father's death, Xiaoshan's family fortunes began to decline. As the saying goes, when people leave, tea cools. The once bustling household was left with only embers remaining. Xiaoshan fell from a life of comfort and privilege into the cold realities of human relationships.

However, Xiaoshan was a man of true character. His refusal to rely on the powerful was one of his defining traits. His writings "would not include a single phrase suitable for a new scholar," and he showed no interest in managing finances—"spending millions without hesitation" while leaving his family cold and hungry. Moreover, his kindness often went unrewarded—"people betray him a hundred times without resentment; he trusts others and never doubts their deceit." This is a brilliant portrayal of Xiaoshan. Despite the hardships in his life, Xiaoshan maintained an aristocratic demeanor, characterized by his obsession and pride. In an era obsessed with fame and fortune, Xiaoshan believed that integrity and dignity were more important than anything else—this was the last line of defense he would hold onto.

The high-rise locked after waking from a dream, the curtain hangs low after sobering from wine. Last year's spring regrets return again. A person stands alone amidst the fallen flowers, swallows fly in pairs in a drizzle.

I remember the first time I met Xiao Ping, her double-heart embroidered robe. She played the pipa, expressing longing. The moon that night still shines, once illuminating her graceful figure like clouds returning.

"The falling flowers" harmonize with the "drizzle" to create a scene of beauty tinged with sorrow. Though the poem appears to describe the scenery, it actually conveys feelings of reluctance and parting. Throughout the entire poem, there isn't a single mention of "emotion" or "sorrow," yet the poem perfectly captures melancholy, helplessness, pain, and separation. This is the soul and essence of the poem. One can imagine the situation: a disheveled Xiaoshan, standing on a balcony after waking from a drunken dream, the madam mercilessly urging him to leave. What a poignant farewell! He steps out of the building and stands on a flower-lined path, gazing far off at Xiao Ping, reluctant to part. Unknowingly, a pair of swallows passes through the light rain and flies over his head. Even the swallows can fly in pairs, but what about people?

She expressed her longing on the strings of the pipa, the moon that night still shines, once illuminating her graceful figure like clouds returning. Vividly portraying Xiaoshan's desolate loneliness, in his memory, their initial meeting marked a mutual promise of love, unforgettable for a lifetime. This is Xiaoshan—immersing himself in poetry and wine, possessing a deeply sincere and passionate love consciousness. This was his way of resisting the pursuit of fame and fortune. The lingering, heart-wrenching small tunes became the sole solace for his soul...
