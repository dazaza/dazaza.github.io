Yu Family Stone Village - Spirit of Stones in the Deep Mountains of Yan and Zhao

by tianxiangyi on 2012-02-11 14:14:58

In the deep mountains of Yan Zhao, stones have spirits - Preface to "Yu Family Stone Village"

The ancients said: A mountain need not be high, it becomes famous if there's a fairy; water need not be deep, it becomes spiritual if there's a dragon. Fairies and dragons are too far away from us, we cannot see their beauty. But these two sentences tell us that mountains and waters originally have no spirituality. The spirit of mountains and waters is all given by external factors. So, who gives the spirit to mountains and waters? In fact, it is not fairies or dragons, but the people, the most intelligent beings in all things. Mountains and waters gain spirit because of human participation; they gain brilliance because of human efforts. Therefore, mountains and waters have temperaments, personalities, and cultures. They can show their beauty, express their grandeur. They can laugh, get angry, sing, roar, always resonating with human hearts. Moreover, they carry and record the great historical traditions of the Chinese nation.

In the western part of Jingxing County on the land of Yan Zhao, there is a village famous for its stones. Its name is Yu Family Stone Village. Stones can be found anywhere, why is this village famous for its stones? The reason why the stones of Yu Family Village are famous is because of the Yu family who have lived in the village for generations. The Yu family has made the stone village, and the Yu family has made this village famous far and wide.

Yu Family Stone Village covers an area of only 10 square kilometers, with about 400 households, but its rich and profound history and folk cultural landscape attract scholars and tourists from near and far. Relying on the mountains, the people of Yu Family Village use local materials and create a unique stone culture with their diligence and intelligence. Stone temples, stone theaters, stone roads, stone wells, stone courtyards, everything possible is made of stone. Entering Yu Family Village is like entering a world of stones. Almost all male villagers in the village are skilled in stonemasonry from generation to generation. All kinds of stone structures in the village are the works of the villagers over the generations. If you carefully observe each stone building or facility, you will find the hard work and ingenious craftsmanship invested by the people of the stone village.

Since its establishment in the Ming Chenghua period, Yu Family Stone Village has a history of more than 500 years. Upon entering the village, ancient relics and steles can be seen everywhere. The Qingliang Pavilion standing at the entrance of the village was built in the Ming Wanli period, the Zhenwu Temple and Guanyin Pavilion were built in the early Qing Dynasty, and the Yu Family Ancestral Hall records the origin of the Yu family. Many well-preserved ancient courtyards also have long histories. Most importantly, these ancient buildings and residences display a segment of history that has disappeared, or rather, a living display of the past history. If there were no modernly dressed figures occasionally flashing by, and no popular music floating from somewhere, you might feel as if you were wandering through ancient times. These numerous ancient relics not only narrate the history of the life and struggle of the people of the stone village, record their way of life, but also reflect many valuable traditional cultural concepts.

The "Regulations for Rectifying Village Rules" stele erected during the Ming Wanli period was aimed at purifying customs and eliminating bad habits. Regarding gambling, it states: "There is no greater harm in the village than gambling. Some gather openly to gamble, others lure good people to hide and gamble secretly. Wasting money and destroying property, all sorts of crimes are committed. Corrupting the wind and ruining the custom, nothing is worse than this. Thus, the villagers gathered to discuss banning it." Once discovered, gamblers in the village would be severely punished. If the gambler escapes, then "the whole clan will be penalized." The "Liu Chi Ban" stele erected during the Qing Qianlong period was aimed at regulating villagers' water usage: "If the population is large, water collection will be unfair," therefore it was stipulated that "except for single men, orphans, widows, and sick families, each family is allowed one jar of water, two loads of ice. Those who store more will be fined fifty silver coins per jar of water and twenty silver coins per load of ice." These precious folk data reflect the Chinese people's pursuit of a harmonious and thick social environment since ancient times, reflecting the traditional virtues of the Chinese people.

In the 1920s, when the oracle bones unearthed at Xiaotun, Anyang, Henan caused a sensation in academic circles, Mr. Wang Guowei proposed the famous "Double Evidence Method", i.e., using underground artifacts to corroborate transmitted documents to study history. Now, we may add social surveys to the "double evidence" basis, using the "Triple Evidence Method" to conduct historical and cultural research. Many valuable historical, folk, and cultural data are still preserved in real society. The "Triple Evidence Method" will enrich our research content and enhance our research methods. Villages like Yu Family Stone Village are typical examples.

Speaking of this, we cannot help but mention the origin of the Yu family. It is said that the Yu family is the descendant of Yu Qian, known as the "Rescue Chancellor" in the Ming Dynasty. This history is not only passed down orally within the Yu family, but also many evidences have been found by historians and local cultural workers. However, some mysteries remain unsolved until today. If we can further uncover this historical mystery through our in-depth research and investigation, it will undoubtedly enhance our understanding of that period of history in the Ming Dynasty, and enhance our understanding of Yu Qian and his aftermath.

Go and visit Yu Family Stone Village. It is a treasure house. Go there to feel history, experience tradition, unravel the historical fog, and converse with the ancients. This book will become your useful guide.
