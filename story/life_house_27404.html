Wood floor returns to the retro road

by kjp1982 on 2007-08-13 14:27:24

"Stylish living, free enjoyment" is the ultimate dream of city dwellers who navigate through urban jungles of steel and concrete every day. Everyone aspires to a simple, fashionable, comfortable, and healthy lifestyle. Amidst the intricate demands of home life, people have realized that what truly lingers in their hearts is the classical and subtle. Thus, classical trends that are simple yet timeless are making a comeback, with wooden floors leading the charge by quietly embracing a retro style...

If you were to ask what's trending in wooden flooring this year, then "retro" is undoubtedly the pinnacle of fashion. Recently, reporters observed at various shopping malls that renowned brands are all launching retro-style wooden floors. Moreover, depending on the home environment, the styles and varieties of retro wooden floors are quite diverse.

A closer look reveals that the theme of "retro" isn't just limited to the wooden flooring industry; it permeates the home furnishings sector, especially interior design styles. Whether it’s European, American, Chinese, or even minimalist designs, none seem to escape the influence of the "retro" trend. The element that most prominently highlights a home's style is undoubtedly the floor. It can balance the solemnity of classical aesthetics while infusing modern spaces with cultural flair. Therefore, the natural and healthy vibe emanating from retro floors, even the longing for rural forests, can all be fulfilled through these small wooden planks. When used for flooring, whether paired with rustic interior designs and vintage furniture, European royal-style furniture, or even modern minimalist home environments, retro wooden floors harmonize beautifully, feeling both natural and cohesive. Retro wooden floors have even become a "versatile" fashion element.

When the advantages of industrial products are pushed to their limits, people begin to yearn for the precision, individuality, and "uniqueness" that handcrafted items offer, which cannot be replicated by industrial products. Therefore, the pinnacle of retro flooring is hand-carved. Not all types of wood are suitable for hand carving; only those with clear wood grain, good wear resistance, suitability for deep processing, and inherent texture can qualify as raw materials for retro flooring.

Compared to traditional floors, hand-carved wooden floors require skilled artisans to craft them manually. Depending on the type of wood, different textures are carved, engraved, and polished to make the harder wood grains stand out, thereby enhancing the three-dimensional feel of the wooden floor. Since this craftsmanship preserves the growth rings of the wood as much as possible, leaving behind traces of its growth period, some say that hand-carved wooden floors not only record the passage of time but also bring the warmth of nature back into the home, evoking a sense of closeness to the natural world.
