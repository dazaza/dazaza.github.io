This article comes from Taobao 8848 http://www.8848.cm Chapter Four Another Unlucky Incident "Ah~~~~~" Seeing the car disappear in a cloud of dust, Su Yixuan finally couldn't help but let out a loud scream. The successive blows, coupled with Lan Jialuo's malicious teasing just now, made Su Yixuan feel an overwhelming urge to bang her head against a wall. All passersby without exception turned their gazes towards Su Yixuan. There was a strange glimmer in their eyes. This girl—could she possibly be insane? Almost everyone had this thought cross their mind. After all, Su Yixuan had been running with her suitcase earlier, and now she was screaming like this. It was easy for others to misinterpret her mental state. Realizing that the pedestrians around her were all looking at her curiously, Su Yixuan became too embarrassed to shout loudly. She hung her head, dragging her suitcase aimlessly forward. But inside, her thoughts were filled with curses. She mentally berated every generation of Lan Jialuo's ancestors. Perhaps even heaven felt sympathy for Su Yixuan's misfortune, as it shed a few drops of sympathetic tears. However, this sympathy seemed excessive, and soon large raindrops mercilessly pelted Su Yixuan's body. Oh no! Now even you are bullying me? Su Yixuan looked up at the sky in anger, so flustered that she couldn't form any words. Her hesitation allowed what was initially a moderate drizzle to quickly turn into a downpour. Frightened, she hastily dragged her suitcase and ran toward a place where she could take shelter from the rain. "Ah!!!" Before she had taken a few steps, a startled cry escaped her lips. In her haste to run and avoid getting rainwater in her eyes, Su Yixuan had been looking down while running. As a result, her body collided with a child, knocking him to the ground. "Youngster, are you alright?" Su Yixuan asked anxiously as she helped the child up. "Wah wah, my hand hurts. I want to find my mom, mom... wah wah..." The child clung to Su Yixuan's hand and started crying. The rain hit his somewhat dirty face, making it impossible to distinguish between tears and rainwater. Su Yixuan's heart ached slightly. She seemed to see herself years ago, crying in the rain just like this child. That intense sorrow of heartache and helplessness moved her deeply, prompting her to soften her voice. "Youngster, where did your mom go? Hmm, let's take shelter from the rain first. We'll look for your mom after the rain stops, alright?" A flash of surprise passed through the child’s clear eyes; he seemed to doubt Su Yixuan's sincerity. But soon, this astonishment was replaced by a gleam of joy. "Okay, thank you, sister." He held onto Su Yixuan's hand and walked towards the nearby public bus stop. "Hmm, we should hurry, or you'll get soaked," Su Yixuan said kindly as she shielded the child's head with her hand. Her action caused the child's body to stiffen momentarily, a faint flicker of emotion welling up in his eyes. "Sister, I'm sorry," he muttered softly before forcefully pushing Su Yixuan away and sprinting off down another street. Though the push wasn’t strong, Su Yixuan still stumbled back several steps. Staring in shock at the disappearing figure, she suddenly remembered something terrifying. This child might be a scammer. Hastily, her hand darted into her pocket, only to find it empty. Her heart plummeted, an indescribable feeling almost overwhelming her. What to do? What should she do now? Even her last belongings had been stolen by this child. Now, how was she supposed to survive? Su Yixuan stood frozen in place, the heavy rain soaking her entire body. A chilling cold emanated from within her bones. In this instant, even the resilient Su Yixuan felt a deep sense of despair. Passersby glanced back at Su Yixuan standing dumbfounded in the rain, either indifferent or with a hint of sympathy in their eyes. But none of them stopped to offer her shelter under their umbrellas. Is this what humanity is? Is this truly the real society? Su Yixuan repeatedly questioned in her heart. Her hands clenched tightly into fists, her fingertips digging into her palms, causing excruciating pain. Yet, her eyes grew increasingly bright, like the most dazzling and brilliant star in the night sky. I, Su Yixuan, will absolutely not be defeated by this!
