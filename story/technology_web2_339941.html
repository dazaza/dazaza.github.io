The Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer is a high-intensity catalyst and multifunctional additive. Adding 0.3%-0.5% in the existing Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel can effectively increase the heat value of Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel, enhance its stability, reduce volatilization, thereby making the production of alcohol-based fuels simpler, safer, and significantly reducing costs while greatly enhancing the firepower.

Functional composition of the Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer:

1. Increase the heat of Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel; adding 0.3%-0.5% in the existing Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel can significantly increase the heat value of Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel; adding 0.3%-0.5% in methanol and then adding a certain proportion of water, fully stirring produces a Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel with quite high heat value, which can be widely used as combustion oil in factories, school canteens, hotels, restaurants, boilers, etc.

2. Reduce the activity of methanol molecules, increase the stability of methanol, and reduce volatilization. We all know that methanol is a very volatile flammable liquid that can turn into flammable methanol vapor around 64 degrees Celsius. If not controlled, it can be very hazardous and easily wasted. One function of the Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer is to reduce the activity of methanol molecules, increase the stability of methanol, reduce volatilization, making it more stable and safer during production, transportation, and storage, while also greatly reducing usage during combustion, which is why the combustion time of Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel is longer than that on the market.

3. Change the traditional emission method, converting exhaust emissions into soluble solid discharges in the furnace. The combustion of any fuel will produce harmful gases such as sulfur dioxide and carbon monoxide. Methanol, especially primary methanol, waste methanol, and recovered methanol, contains many chemical raw materials harmful to human health or the environment. If these are discharged in gaseous form, they would be very harmful to human health and the environment. The Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer converts the harmful components in the exhaust into soluble solids discharged in the furnace. During daytime use, the temperature in the furnace can reach several hundred degrees, and when the temperature drops to room temperature at night, a large amount of water vapor forms in the furnace. These generated soluble solids dissolve in the water vapor and are absorbed by the dry environment in the furnace, which is why there are slight white substances after using the furnace, and these white substances disappear by the next morning with water droplets appearing on the inner walls of the burner core.

4. Fully decompose methanol molecules and water molecules, allowing them to burn completely. The heat value of methanol combustion is around 5300 kcal, which does not meet the required temperature and heat for cooking. We need to add high-heat-value additives to solve this problem. However, high-heat-value additives are quite expensive, such as acetone or other ether chemical products, whose heat values are quite high but cost around 15,000 yuan per ton. In this case, the resulting Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel price might be similar to diesel, failing to save money and making promotion quite difficult. Therefore, we can only solve it from the fuel itself. The Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer can decompose oxygen molecules in methanol, allowing methanol to burn fully in an oxygen-rich environment. Simultaneously, the Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer can decompose water in methanol into hydrogen and oxygen, making it burn more fully and completely, thereby increasing the heat value and firepower of the fuel. This is why adding a little bit of the Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel heat stabilizer to methanol or commercial Kuoshima alcohol-based fuel immediately changes the flame color to red diesel-like, increases the flame height by one-third, and extends the combustion time.
