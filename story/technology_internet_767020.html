Baidu Actively Prepares for Internet Finance, Plans to Follow Alibaba's Lead in Launching a Fund

by anonymous on 2013-10-02 17:13:50

September 29 news, the success of Alipay's Yu'e Bao has triggered a butterfly effect. According to Hong Kong media reports, Baidu is in close contact with several companies including ChinaAMC, negotiating on customized fund products.

Baidu also proposed that the product must be attractive enough, aiming for high returns, low risk, and good liquidity. "The annual return should reach 8%, higher than Yu'e Bao." An insider said that Baidu's sales positioning this time is clear: to launch a better product than Yu'e Bao, which can only be sold on the Baidu platform, with certain differentiation from Yu'e Bao, and an annual return reaching 8%.

In the previous few days, the official Weibo accounts of three financial products under Baidu, namely "Baidu Finance", "Baidu Wealth Management", and "Baidu Securities", have quietly been verified and launched. It is speculated that Baidu Finance will officially go online in October. Although Baidu is currently very low-key and unwilling to disclose too many details, relevant people from Baidu revealed that there might be some moves in Baidu Finance after October.

In fact, Baidu has been actively laying out its internet finance business since the beginning of this year. As early as July this year, Baidu's payment tool Bai Fu Bao obtained a third-party payment license, and at the end of August, the test version of Baidu Finance was quietly launched online. In September, Baidu confirmed that its application to set up a physical micro-loan company in Shanghai has been approved, and it will be located in Jiading.

A person in the fund industry once told the media that Baidu does not have an advantage in the e-commerce field, but stepping out of e-commerce and looking at the big picture of the Internet, Baidu's user scale and quality are no worse than other companies. Therefore, as long as they find a suitable way to enter, design unique products, they can also occupy a place in internet finance.

Another person in the fund industry revealed that Baidu's involvement in internet finance mainly has three levels: one level is to do financial search, occupying the recently emerging financial search market; another is to cooperate with other funds like Alibaba to promote Yu'e Bao; and the last one is to directly engage in internet finance. Among these, Baidu has advantages in the first two levels.

Among them, online search comparison has become the internet entry point for netizens to choose and apply for financial products. According to data from authoritative research institution Forrester, every year, 70 million Americans and 10 million British people use online financial comparison websites as their first stop for applying for financial products. Nearly 70% of users indicated that using search comparison changes their choice of financial institutions and products.

Financial vertical search is also a highly watched model in China's internet finance sector. It is expected that financial search will become the main sales channel for banks, solving information asymmetry issues in the financing loan process through big data technology. After Rong360 announced receiving $30 million in Series B funding, naturally Baidu is also unwilling to abandon this market.
