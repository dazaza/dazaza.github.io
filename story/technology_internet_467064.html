Benchmarking (General Manager, male, 44 years old)

Management incentives are divided into two categories: one is material incentives, also known as salary incentives; the other is spiritual incentives, also called growth incentives. If we refer to salary incentives as the external motivation to improve employee enthusiasm, then growth incentives are the internal motivation to improve employee enthusiasm. Salary incentives can play a good role in maintaining employee enthusiasm, while growth incentives can make employee enthusiasm last longer. Your company has done a great job in salary incentives. If you can write more articles about employee growth incentives, it will take your company's management incentives to a higher level.

Negative Incentives (Human Resources Manager, female, 32 years old)

You may consider another type of incentive: negative incentives, which means canceling existing incentives. For employees who lack enthusiasm for work, cancel the bonuses they should receive. Doing a good job is the duty of employees. If they cannot well invest their enthusiasm into work, they should be penalized or even dismissed.

In conclusion, to improve employee enthusiasm, we must incorporate spiritual incentives while doing a good job in material incentives for employees, making material and spiritual incentives support each other and interact. The energy will be reactivated.

In 2004, we built a "Five-Star Employee Growth Incentive System" for a five-star hotel in Shenzhen. We must admit that the situation you described is often seen. We usually call it the "Energy Decay Syndrome." Business leaders using bonuses to increase employee enthusiasm is one of the more effective incentive methods. But as time goes on, a game of incentives and being incentivized forms between the enterprise and employees. Bonuses become symbolic rewards, and employees' demands for rewards keep getting higher. Once these demands are not met, it leads to employee turnover. Therefore, relying solely on salary methods to increase employee enthusiasm is akin to patching up a dilapidated house. So how do we solve this problem? Let's start with corporate management incentives!

Black Diamond Consultant (This issue's on-duty consultant: Researcher Gao Yan of the China International Management Research Institute):

So how do we solve the problem of corporate material incentives losing effectiveness?

You can select service benchmarks in various service positions, play the role of advanced examples, allow more employees to find the gap between themselves and the service benchmarks, encourage employees to strive towards the goals of the service benchmarks, thereby enhancing employee work enthusiasm.

I am the vice general manager of a chain supermarket, in charge of the human resources department. Every year, we hire a large number of service personnel. At the beginning, the performance of new employees is very satisfactory, but soon after, their work enthusiasm drops. To boost employee enthusiasm, we increased the bonus distribution significantly. This measure was extremely helpful in initially motivating employees, but issuing bonuses only had a temporary "happy pain-relief" effect. Employee work enthusiasm could not be sustained for long. On the contrary, employees became increasingly numb to receiving bonuses to maintain work enthusiasm. Previously, issuing a 200 yuan bonus could effectively motivate work enthusiasm, now issuing 500 yuan does not elicit any reaction, and some employees have even left. We have rewarded what we should reward and cared for what we should care for, yet the employees do not reciprocate. What does Black Diamond Consultant think about this?
