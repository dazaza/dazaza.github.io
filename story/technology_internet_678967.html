Pre-job training for mold major graduates is booming in 2012

by gotook2012 on 2012-02-27 17:40:19

Once again, spring comes early. College students who have graduated in 2012 or will graduate in 2013 start to prepare for work, CAD mold training or UG software (pre-job professional training) become new ways of employment for college graduates. Many enterprises require design staff to be proficient in professional knowledge. Many newly-employed students have the feeling that what they learned in school is quite different from what enterprises actually use...

Under the current situation of continuous expansion of universities, it has become a very objective reality that college students face difficulties in employment. This is also the result of the phenomenon of "high consumption of academic qualifications". There are three reasons for this:

1. Supply and demand relationship: The number of today's college students far exceeds the employment demand.

2. Teaching quality: Regardless of the major, learning is ultimately for work. However, many universities now focus more on expanding their campuses and making them look beautiful, rather than fostering an academic atmosphere. Their teaching materials and methods are also detached from reality.

3. Enterprises are unwilling to hire fresh graduates or require experience as a basis. In addition, many enterprises have experienced breaches of contract and job-hopping by fresh graduates, causing a series of losses.

Should college students still attend training?

Indeed, after completing three years of university and still needing to attend a training course to find a design job is something many people used to find incomprehensible. What is learned in university classrooms is mostly comprehensive theoretical knowledge, with cultural courses taking up half of the class hours. Professionally speaking, there is little connection to practical applications. As a result, during interviews, these students often appear unprofessional. Of course, if one is willing to spend 1-2 years starting from the front line and improving their knowledge, it is not a bad choice. However, by choosing professional training, you can shorten this process. Under the guidance of experienced mentors, you can address some issues encountered in work and gain some experience. This way, you will feel more confident during interviews or while working.

Choosing training or gaining experience on the front line

It is difficult to absolutely judge which is better. If you can meet a mentor or leader willing to teach you while working on the front line, then this choice would be excellent. However, the problem is that in some companies, once a new person is recruited, the senior employees may first think about being replaced by the newcomer. After their self-protection awareness rises, they may even exclude the newcomer instead of teaching them. A good training institution usually charges fees and aims to enhance its reputation. As long as the instructor has real technical expertise, responsibility, and practical experience, and can patiently guide the students, one can accumulate 1-2 years of work experience within 5-10 months through hard study. This way, you can directly find a design position and engage in design work. For example, our previous students are good proof of this. Also, if you encounter any problems at work, you can always consult by phone or bring the issues over, so you don't have to worry about being excluded at work.

Which type of training or work can improve technical skills faster?

Regarding this issue, you can refer to the exclusive interview with us published in the March 2007 issue of "Professional" magazine. Here's a brief explanation: First, the mentor must be experienced and patient, able to simplify complex technical issues. Case-based teaching with actual work tasks is preferable, allowing students to learn synchronously because learning should be applied in practice.

Will I get a job after training with you?

If you want to engage in design work right after graduation without pre-job training or mentorship, it is generally unrealistic. Due to employee loyalty concerns, companies used to require certificates or deposits as collateral. However, according to the new labor law last year, companies cannot adopt such methods anymore (some illegal individuals might exploit loopholes). Companies typically do not recruit technical personnel from campuses or job fairs anymore but prefer poaching or collaborating with specialized technical training institutions. After third-party professional training companies screen candidates, they are employed for 2-3 years. Upon contract expiration, the training costs are returned in the form of bonuses. In case of breach, companies suffer no significant loss. This is why we collaborate with thousands of companies, making our center's graduates highly sought-after.

How do you ensure that the technical level after training meets job requirements?

The key lies in the teaching method and the combination of knowledge. Firstly, proficiency in software operation is essential as design software is a tool that must be mastered, just like writing requires knowing how to write characters. Secondly, although universities teach many theories, they need to be systematically digested in conjunction with real-world situations. Thirdly, case studies and practical work are crucial. No matter how well you learn initially, there will inevitably be omissions in practice, such as assembly, processes, features, etc. During the first month, we mainly teach software operations, progressing from simple to complex surfaces, simultaneously imparting theory. These two aspects are combined and taught concurrently. After class, practical case assignments are given immediately. Starting the third month, we simulate a working state where tasks are completed independently, followed by addressing issues and refining skills. This process continues until one can work independently.

Explanation of the concept of third-party training:

Third-party training primarily addresses issues faced by those without technical skills or insufficiently skilled. It involves pre-job training for enterprise technicians and providing trained personnel for enterprises to select suitable talents. This allows enterprises to fully understand the talents while avoiding upfront training expenses, reducing cost risks. Since enterprises do not have to worry about initial investment in various aspects, they have fewer concerns, which helps solve social employment problems. This method has been effectively solving employment issues abroad for a long time.
