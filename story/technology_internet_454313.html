The differences between the Hong Kong version and the mainland version of Du Qi Feng's "Election"

by jlik473911 on 2011-06-30 16:01:14

The social security net provides a safety net for people's livelihood and economic restructuring.

The second fall exacerbates the social moral crisis.

This "Triad" by Johnnie To, after passing through content edits that "fit the mainland conditions", was finally smoothly released in mainland China. Although the plot wasn't drastically altered to become unrecognizable, the original film length was still cut by more than 10 minutes. Many Mandarin dialogues and plots were deleted or modified, and the original ending was not spared either. Below, we will discuss the many differences between the Hong Kong version and the mainland version.

Firstly, the original title of the Hong Kong version was "Triad", while the mainland version was renamed "Dragon City Chronicles".

Secondly, at the beginning of the film, the Hong Kong version shows an ancient initiation ceremony of the Hung Mun, but this part is cut in the mainland version. Instead, white words on a black background are used to finish the credits, and the movie starts directly.

At the beginning of the film, when a group of triad leaders are discussing who should be the "boss", the police rush in and take everyone away. In the Hong Kong version, Uncle So's dialogue is: "You must arrest me, right? I need a head cover." In the mainland version, it is changed to: "You must arrest me, right? I need a head cover."

All dialogues in the film use the term "boss" instead of "headman" in the mainland version.

In the lunch conversation among three policemen at the police station:

Original Hong Kong version dialogue: Female Policeman A: "Even the triads have democracy. Why don't they do something good, but instead hold small-circle elections like 'Xin Ji'. If fathers could just pass the position to their sons, there would be no disputes." Policeman B: "They choose their boss 100 years earlier than Hong Kong chooses its Chief Executive. As long as it's undecided, we have to keep a close eye on them."

Mainland version modified dialogue: Female Policeman A: "They have nothing better to do but hold 'headman' elections, like 'Xin Ji'. If fathers could just pass the position to their sons, there would be no disputes." Policeman B: "They have been selecting their headmen for over 100 years. As long as it's undecided, we have to keep a close eye on them."

In the street-side restaurant, before counting the money, Uncle Lung asks Jimmy (played by Louis Koo): "Jimmy, are her breasts real?" After this, there is a scene where the girl in yellow is called in to shake her chest. At the end of this scene, when Uncle Lung discovers the money amount is wrong, he says: "Damn it, they gave me 200,000 counterfeit notes and only 100,000 real ones. Is Big D not interested in winning anymore?" The above-mentioned scenes are exclusive to the Hong Kong version. In the mainland version, it only goes up to Uncle Lung receiving the money, and the rest is all cut.

In the scene where several uncles of the society discuss who should be the "boss", the line from Chun Pao in the Hong Kong version is "Clear your mother's head", which is slightly modified in the mainland version to "Clear your head".

After Coffin Son and Uncle Lung are nailed into a wooden box by Big D's people, the mainland version has two policemen reporting to their superior that Ah Lok was chosen as the new boss and Coffin Son and Uncle Lung were taken away by Big D. This plot is unique to the mainland version.

When Jimmy listens to a class in a large classroom, the course in the Hong Kong version is taught in English, while in the mainland version, it is changed to Mandarin.

When Ah Lok visits Uncle Dang, one line of dialogue in the Hong Kong version is: "In 1970, when I became the boss, there was a ceremony with big gongs and drums, lion dances and dragon dances, dozens of tables set up, and all members of the societies were invited, even the 'Four Great Detectives' came." In the mainland version, it is modified to: "In 1970, when I became the boss, there was a ceremony with big gongs and drums, lion dances and dragon dances, dozens of tables set up, and all important people from the societies were invited."

There are three modifications made in the mainland version during the conversation between Big D and Chicken in the Bone Qi Restaurant.

Hong Kong version original dialogue: ①"Are you done? Where's the stick?" "It's in the mainland." "Where?" "Guangzhou." "Three hours away, round trip six hours, plus two hours for eating and having sex, it should have arrived." ②"Last week you went to Dongguan for prostitution, bring it back immediately." ③"Wan Chai leader? Just two smelly and moldy strip clubs, if I didn't support you with money, would you be where you are today?"

Mainland version modified dialogue: ①"Are you done? Where's the stick?" "It's in the mainland." "Where?" "Guangzhou." "Three hours away, round trip six hours, plus two hours for eating and dating, it should have arrived." ②"Last week you went to Dongguan, bring it back immediately." ③"Wan Chai leader? You just have two poor and moldy bars, if I didn't support you with money, would you be where you are today?"

In Guangzhou, the conversation between Uncle So and Tong Ge.

Hong Kong version original dialogue: "Stop playing around, how's your sister-in-law? Last time I helped you get your sister-in-law to give birth in Hong Kong, I haven't seen her yet. The child is already two years old, have you thought about sending him to study in Hong Kong? He has a Hong Kong ID card."

Mainland version modified dialogue: "Stop playing around, last time I helped you get your sister-in-law admitted to the hospital to give birth, I haven't seen her yet. The child is already two years old, have you thought about sending him to a prestigious school? You only have this one son."

After Uncle So gets the Dragon Stick, he is caught by the arriving mainland police. In the Hong Kong version, there is an additional character played by You Yong, who was tasked with infiltrating the Hong Kong triads. He catches Uncle So, has a conversation with him, and then lets him go. In the mainland version, this part is heavily edited, only showing Uncle So getting the Dragon Stick, and when the police arrive, they escape.

The Dragon Stick is transported from the mainland to Hong Kong by truck. On the road, the content of the truck driver's phone call is also modified. Although only two words are changed, the meaning changes quite a bit.

Hong Kong version original dialogue: "Is Channel Three clear? Definitely before the shift ends, my license plate is 'Yue ZFV18 Gang', thank you Director, I'll treat you to dinner when I come back."

Mainland version modified dialogue: "Is Channel Three clear? Definitely before the shift ends, my license plate is 'Yue ZFV18 Gang', thank you, I'll treat you to dinner when I come back."

After Big D and Ah Lok reconcile, there is a 2-3 minute segment introducing the history of the Hung Mun and the initiation ceremony, which is cut in the mainland version. Only a small part of the oath-taking ceremony of the Nine Districts leaders of Lian Sheng is retained, and the blood oath ceremony is also cut.

After Big D and Ah Lok enter Tsim Sha Tsui, there is a scene where Jimmy visits Uncle Lung in the hospital. Uncle Lung tells Jimmy to aim for the biggest and most powerful position in the triads. In the mainland version, this scene is moved to the end of the film. Jimmy becomes a police mole, and Uncle Lung becomes a key witness. The dialogue also changes to "Don't join the triads" as a moral lesson.

In the original Hong Kong ending, Ah Lok kills Big D with a big rock and then kills Big D's wife with a shovel. Finally, he drives off with his son, and the film ends. In the mainland version ending, just as Ah Lok kills Big D with a big rock, the police suddenly appear and capture Ah Lok. Big D's wife and Ah Lok's son are also taken away by the police.

Finally, there is a unique scene in the mainland version. Before the film ends, a policeman reports to his superior that Big D died from his injuries, Ah Lok is charged with murder, and all related criminals are arrested. Additionally, through dialogue, it is revealed that Jimmy is a mole planted by the police in the triads.
