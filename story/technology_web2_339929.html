Related thematic articles: Untitled Document Xia Ruixi knew that the conflict between the eldest and second branches of the family had a long history, with minor conflicts happening all the time. This incident was merely an opportunity carefully designed by Lady Xia, who had waited patiently for a long time to completely strike against Lady Wang. It cannot be said that Lady Wang walked into a trap on her own this time because even if she did not create trouble, Lady Xia would still have made an opportunity for her to come out and cause a commotion. Uncle Xia has always been a dissolute son, fond of cockfighting, gambling, and drinking. When he runs out of money, he steals things from home, regardless of whether they belong to the old lady or his brothers. Lady Wang loves comparing herself with others and showing off her wealth, looking down on those who are inferior to her. She is easily swayed and useless. However, she has good luck as she gave birth to three sons in a row, making her feel superior to Lady Xia, who only gave birth to three daughters. She considers herself a great contributor to the Xia family. For a period of time, she even looked down on the old lady until she was severely disciplined a few times. This couple spends the money earned by Master Xia and his wife without any guilt, yet they still envy Lady Xia's management and authority. They complain about receiving too little and often find excuses to cause a commotion every now and then. Their three sons have all studied in the clan school, but none of them are talented enough to pass the examination for becoming a scholar. The eldest son, Xia Ruizhu, has read books for more than ten years but has made no progress at all. Seeing no hope in his studies, after getting married, Master Xia let him learn how to do business. After learning for three years, the first time he was given a chance to conduct a business deal on his own, he lost five thousand taels of silver for Master Xia. Master Xia didn't say anything, but Lady Wang wouldn't let it go. She insisted that Master Xia was selfish and wanted to monopolize the business. He didn't sincerely teach her son how to do business but deliberately gave Xia Ruizhu a business deal destined to lose money, just to drive Xia Ruizhu away. She completely forgot that the Xia family had already declined before she married in, otherwise, a daughter from her family would not have married into the Xia family. The current estate and business were all earned by Master Xia himself, and her whole family was benefiting from it. Despite being incapable, Master Xia still took care of the relationship between brothers and didn't want to upset the old lady. Seeing that his eldest nephew wasn't cut out for business, he then took his second nephew, Xia Ruyan, to learn business. Xia Ruyan was considered an exception in the eldest branch, as if all the intelligence of the family was concentrated in him. He quickly learned everything skillfully. However, instead of becoming Master Xia's good helper, he became a big headache. Anything that passed through his hands, even iron plates, would be scraped off two liang of rust. His wife was even more formidable than him. Lady Wang's third son, who is several months older than Xia Ruixi, was spoiled more than Xia Ruimiao, the only grandson. At sixteen years old, he still clung to Lady Wang, acting childish. Also, since he was not engaged yet, he boasted that he would not marry unless it was to a beauty. Lazy and gluttonous, he failed in studying and refused to learn business. Instead, he spent his days gambling and fighting with Xia Dabo. Master Xia, after supporting this family for more than ten years, received only one benefit - these three nephews showed outward respect towards him. When Lady Wang caused a commotion, they always helped the second branch to persuade her. Xu Zhimo, of course, only they themselves know their true thoughts and intentions. Xia Ruixi estimated that it mostly couldn't escape the word 'profit'. She usually feared dealing with people from the eldest branch and was always puzzled why Master Xia and his wife tolerated such parasites. Later, she found out that Lady Xia was always concerned about the eldest branch, fearing that if the second branch ignored the eldest, the family could not be divided as long as she was alive, and they should endure what they deserved. Xia Ruibei ran back and forth, serving tea and massaging shoulders for Lady Xia, admiringly saying: "Mother, you're really smart. I've been fed up for a long time. The money is clearly earned by father, yet some people, relying on being born earlier, contribute nothing financially. The whole family eats for free, gambling daily, wasting the family fortune, and acting arrogantly. They eat well and drink fine wine, demanding the best of everything, stealing things for their own homes, and causing commotions from time to time. They don't value your and father's hard work at all, thinking they deserve everything, believing they are extraordinary just because they have those few sons, and grandmother always protects them. White-eyed wolves! When our Ruimiao succeeds in the future, they will surely die of anger." While applying medicine to Liniang's swollen face, Lady Xia scolded softly: "Are these words suitable for you, a junior? You lack discipline!" Seeing Xia Ruixi still standing in the yard giving orders to the maids, she kindly waved her over: "Xixi, it's so cold outside. You've been standing here for quite a while. Why are you still standing there like a fool? Aren't you afraid of getting sick? Your hand warmer must be cold by now. Come inside. Today, the three of us will have lunch together." Xia Ruixi quickly walked in: "Mother, am I right to worry that grandmother seemed unhappy just now?" Lady Xia smiled lightly, helplessly: "She hasn't been happy for more than ten years." Xia Ruixi guessed that the unsaid sentence from Lady Xia was: "If my grandmother were happy, the person who would faint today would be me." Leaning on her, Xia Ruixi sighed: "Mother, must we live like this forever?" Lady Xia, observing her actions along with Xia Ruibei, had a very clear purpose - it was simply a vivid lesson prepared for their future life in their mother-in-law's house. Lady Xia embraced her and Xia Ruibei, softly saying: "You two understand my hardships. Being a woman is tough, being a daughter-in-law is tough, and not being able to bear a son is even tougher. Now, I only hope that your sister can give birth to a chubby son as her first child. If the two of you can get along harmoniously and live happily and sweetly in the future, and your brother becomes successful, I won't ask for anything else." The three women hugged each other, rarely in harmony. Xia Ruibei even reached out to hold Xia Ruixi's hand: "Sister, I'm sorry. I was immature before. Can you forgive me?" Reflecting on the shared fate of being women born in this difficult era, Xia Ruixi felt a sense of empathy and smiled while holding Xia Ruibei's hand: "We are real sisters. I won't blame you. Mother works too hard. Let's not make her worry anymore in the future." Xia Ruibei nodded in agreement, tightly embracing Lady Xia, burying her head in her bosom: "Mother, I won't make you worry anymore in the future." Lady Xia approvingly patted her head: "You are both born from my ten-month pregnancy. I won't favor one over the other. Since your personalities are different, the way I treat you is also different, but it's all for your own good." "Today, I punished you not only for show. There was indeed a genuine intention to punish you. You need to remember that those who can gain the respect of the masses are not those who think they can do whatever they want. One must be flawless to gain respect. Babei must resent why I beat Hong'er so harshly, right?" Xia Ruibei's eyes flickered, shaking her head: "I don't resent it. Mother naturally has your reasons." Lady Xia said: "Having a maid like Hong'er around you will eventually bring you great trouble! Zou's situation today is a reflection of her future! Can such a person stay by your side? And Hong'er becoming this way, you also have an inescapable responsibility! So, I want to tell you that masters have the responsibilities of masters, and servants have the duties of servants. If you can't manage the people around you, how can you manage others? In this aspect, you really should learn from your second sister. Doing things cannot rely solely on temporary bravery; you need to use your brain!" Xia Ruixi secretly sighed, Lady Xia was the person they should most learn from. Lady Xia usually tolerates much from the eldest branch and doesn't say much, being gentle and polite. Secretly, however, every move of the eldest branch falls under her watchful eye, waiting for such an appropriate opportunity to unleash a powerful reaction. To describe this approach in one sentence would be: "I don't provoke you regularly. You like small fights, so go ahead and fight. Others will only say you are boring, and I am magnanimous. When I seize the opportunity, I'll slap you so hard that you can't even cry, and you'll never recover." Xia Ruibei sneered and sarcastically said: "After falling this time, Second Sister seems to have become smarter, as if she has changed into another person." She had tried several times to scheme against Xia Ruixi to embarrass her and make her scolded, but the effects were not obvious at all. Xia Ruixi's heart suddenly jumped, and she hurriedly glanced at Lady Xia's expression. Seeing Lady Xia's unfathomable expression, she dared not say more and only lowered her head: "I am far less clever than Second Sister. When Auntie fainted, although I was anxious, I had no solution at all. Still, Second Sister had many solutions." Lady Xia said: "Stop talking nonsense. You are both young ladies now. Learn how to manage a household properly. It will only benefit you." As they spoke, the maids had set the table, and the three had just eaten a few bites when Liniang came in and said: "They woke up on the other side and are making a fuss. Threatening to kill themselves, saying some very unpleasant things." "What did they say?" Xia Ruibei frowned. Xia Ruixi also hurriedly put on an extremely angry expression. Liniang looked at Lady Xia helplessly. Seeing Lady Xia remain silent, she whispered: "Cursing us, cursing the old lady for being partial, saying that the sacrifices and tomb-sweeping in the future still depend on her son. That is truly excessive. Don't we also have Fourth Young Master?" Lady Xia, without raising her head, said: "It's not the first time she has caused a commotion. Let her scream. With the New Year approaching, naturally someone will take care of her." After the three finished eating, Lady Xia touched her forehead: "My head hurts." Xia Ruixi hurriedly helped her lie down: "You must be tired and caught a cold from the cold wind. Liniang, quickly go call the doctor." Xia Ruibei's expression changed, pulling Xia Ruixi: "It's all due to that ungrateful wolf. We will deal with her." Lady Xia angrily shouted: "Do not go! If you dare to disgrace me, I will disown you!" Just as she was speaking, the old lady's maid came to invite her: "Second Madam, the old lady asks you to come over once." Xia Ruibei angrily said: "No sense of propriety! Haven't you seen that my mother is ill?" The maid, troubled, rubbed her cuffs and quietly said: "The old lady said that no matter what, Second Madam must come over once. She has urgent matters to discuss." Lady Xia had no choice but to sit up. Liniang's eyes instantly reddened. Xia Ruixi quickly thought of something: "Mother is sick. I will go with you." Turning back to Lady Xia, she smiled: "Mother, rest assured. I will see what Grandma needs. If I can help, I will do it. If I can't, I will come back and ask you." Lady Xia nodded: "Liniang, fetch a thick cloak for Second Miss." Xia Ruixi wanted to say it was unnecessary, but Liniang insisted and forcibly draped a thick fox fur on her, stuffing a hand warmer into her arms, and quietly instructed: "Second Miss, be patient when you get there." It was just a few courtyards away, yet it seemed like going far away. Xia Ruixi didn't understand why, but she knew it was for her own good. Upon arriving at the old lady's courtyard, the maid went to report, and a little maid blocked the door and wouldn't let her in: "The old lady instructed that she wants to chant scriptures now, and no one shall disturb her. Those who want to see her can just wait. She will naturally come out after she finishes chanting." She didn't allow Xia Ruixi to enter either, just letting her stand in the courtyard and blow in the cold wind.
