Definition, Classification and Characteristics of Coal Sludge

by hx515317 on 2012-03-06 16:59:59

Three Types of Coal Sludge

The coal sludge we usually refer to generally means the semi-solid material formed by coal powder containing water, which is a by-product in the process of coal production and washing/selection. Due to differences in the variety and formation mechanism of coal sludge, the properties of various types of coal sludge also vary greatly, thus leading to significant differences in their usability. There are roughly the following types:

First Type: Flotation tailing coal from coking coal washing plants

This type of coal sludge is generally regarded as waste abroad, with properties similar to washed gangue or medium coal. Due to differences in coal quality, there are significant variations in the quality of flotation coal sludge. For example, the coal from Pingdingshan is rich coal or 1/3 coking coal, and the extraction rate of flotation clean coal can reach 70%~80%, resulting in higher ash content in the flotation tailing coal, making its quality close to that of washed gangue; for Huainan's gas coal, the extraction rate of the flotation process is only 30%~40%, so this type of coal sludge has relatively low ash content, and its quality is closer to that of washed medium coal.

Depending on the different recovery processes of coal sludge, there are significant differences in its physical properties. For example, coal sludge recovered by a filter press has a more uniform particle distribution, weaker adhesion and water retention, which helps reduce moisture content. Another method is the coal sludge sedimentation pond or tailings field, where solid particles naturally settle in water to achieve solid-liquid separation and produce coal sludge. This process has the function of grading by particle size, with coarse particles settling easily and mostly concentrating near the inlet of the coal sludge water, fine particles located in the middle position, and extremely fine particles at the end. The coal sludge at the end has high adhesion and high water retention, resembling sticky rice dumplings—fine and soft. Even after being sun-dried for several months, it appears dry on the surface, but the internal water content hardly decreases. This type of coal sludge is the hardest to handle.

Second Type: Coal sludge produced from coal-water mixtures

For example, coal sludge from the washing plant of power coal or coal sludge produced after hydraulic coal transportation. This type of coal sludge is sometimes better in quality than raw coal, and when the quantity is small, it is often mixed into the finished coal. When the quantity increases, only a small part can be mixed, and a large amount of high-quality coal sludge may be produced, requiring proper handling, which might adversely affect the economic benefits of the coal mine.

Third Type: Coal sludge carried by mine drainage and coal sludge washed down from gangue mountains

These collected coal sludges all belong to the dirty miscellaneous coal sludge of the coal mine. Their characteristics include a small quantity, unstable quality, but generally better quality than flotation tailing coal.

Main Features of Coal Sludge in Washing Plants

1. Strong water-holding capacity, high moisture content

Coal sludge dewatered by disc vacuum filters generally contains more than 30% moisture; coal sludge dewatered by belt filters contains 26%~29%; coal sludge dewatered by filter presses contains 20%~24%.

2. High viscosity

Due to the general presence of a large amount of clay minerals in coal sludge, combined with high moisture content and fine particle size composition, most coal sludges have high viscosity, some even have certain fluidity.

3. High ash content, low calorific value

Based on ash content and calorific value, coal sludge can be divided into three categories: low-ash coal sludge with ash content of 20%~32% and calorific value of 12.5~20MJ/kg; medium-ash coal sludge with ash content of 30%~55% and calorific value of 8.4~12.5MJ/kg; high-ash coal sludge with ash content >55% and calorific value of 3.5~6.3MJ/kg.

4. Fine particle size, high micro-particle content

Especially micro-particles smaller than 200 mesh account for about 70%~90%.

Due to these characteristics, the stacking, storage, and transportation of coal sludge are all relatively difficult. Especially during storage, its form is extremely unstable, prone to loss upon contact with water, and becomes airborne when dried by wind. As a result, not only are valuable coal resources wasted, but serious environmental pollution is also caused, sometimes even affecting the normal production of the washing plant, becoming a rather thorny issue for the washing plant.
