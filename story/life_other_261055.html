1. [How to Make "Drool-Worthy Fish"]

Ingredients: One grass carp, two large tablespoons of starch, about half a liang (25g) of fried peanut kernels, two large tablespoons of roasted white sesame seeds, a small piece of old ginger, one garlic clove, five green onions, two tablespoons of fermented black beans, three tablespoons of cooking wine, two tablespoons of soy sauce, one tablespoon of vinegar, one teaspoon of sugar, one liang (50g) of red chili oil, two large tablespoons of Sichuan peppercorn oil, appropriate amount of salt and monosodium glutamate (MSG), one large tablespoon of sesame oil.

1. Clean the fish and slice it into pieces. Mix with salt and cooking wine evenly and marinate for a few minutes. Then mix with water and starch to coat the flavor. Chop the fried peanut kernels into fine granules, mince the fermented black beans, and finely chop the green onions, garlic, and ginger.

2. Heat oil in a pan until it is six-tenths hot, then add the fermented black beans, minced ginger, and minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.

3. In a large bowl, mix salt, MSG, sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, stir-fried ginger, garlic, fermented black beans, red chili oil, Sichuan peppercorn oil, sesame oil, and fried peanut kernels evenly to make the seasoning sauce.

4. Place the wok over high heat, add oil until it is four-tenths hot, then add the fish slices and stir-fry until cooked through.

5. Drain the oil and transfer the fish slices to a bowl.

6. Pour the seasoning sauce over the fish slices, sprinkle with roasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

7. Mix well and serve.

2. [How to Make Water-boiled Fish]

(I)

1. Buy a carp and slice it into fish slices.

2. Marinate the fish slices (with salt, MSG, green onions, ginger, alcohol, Sichuan peppercorn, or if you like, you can use five-spice powder or other flavors you prefer).

3. Bring a little water to a boil, add the fish slices and blanch until they are 80% cooked, then immediately remove them. Transfer to a serving pot and discard the water.

4. Heat a lot of oil, then add dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns (adjust according to personal taste). When the chili color turns slightly black, pour the oil over the fish slices.

5. In restaurants, water-boiled fish often has many yellow bean sprouts underneath the fish slices. The fish slices are only laid on top of the bean sprouts. At home, you can skip the bean sprouts.

6. When the oil is just poured on, be careful not to let the splashing oil scald you due to the high temperature. Therefore, the utensil used to hold the water-boiled fish must be large enough.

(II)

Bean sprouts, or your favorite vegetables, ginger (a large piece, pounded and sliced), garlic (one head, lightly pounded, no need to cut), an appropriate amount of green onions, doubanjiang (or chopped pepper), Sichuan peppercorn, dried red chili, chili powder, salt, MSG, white pepper, cooking wine, a little soy sauce and vinegar, edible oil. Also needed: cornstarch, cooking wine, a little salt, one egg white.

1. Clean the fish thoroughly, chop off the head and tail, slice the fish into pieces, and chop the remaining fish bones into several chunks. Marinate the fish slices with a little salt, cooking wine, cornstarch, and one egg white, mix well, and marinate for 15 minutes. Place the head, tail, and fish bones separately in a dish and marinate using the same method.

2. Boil a small pot of water, wash the bean sprouts, and blanch them briefly in boiling water. Remove and place in a large bowl, sprinkle with a little salt according to personal taste, and set aside.

3. In a clean wok, add three times the amount of oil used for regular stir-frying. Once heated, add three large tablespoons of doubanjiang (or chopped peppers) and stir-fry until fragrant. Add ginger, garlic, green onion, Sichuan peppercorn, chili powder, and dried red chili, and stir-fry over medium-low heat. After the aroma emerges, add the fish head, tail, and bones, switch to high heat, stir evenly, add cooking wine and soy sauce, an appropriate amount of white pepper and sugar, continue to stir-fry for a moment, then add some hot water. Season with salt and MSG while tasting the saltiness. Once the water boils, maintain high heat, add the fish slices one by one, separate them with chopsticks, and turn off the heat after 3-5 minutes. Pour the cooked fish and all the soup into the large bowl containing the bean sprouts.

4. Take another clean pot, pour in about half a catty (250g) of oil (the specific amount depends on the size of the container being used, ensuring that when poured into the large bowl, the fish and bean sprouts are completely submerged; this can be visually estimated). Once the oil is heated, turn off the heat and let it cool slightly. Then add plenty of Sichuan peppercorn and dried red chili (depending on personal preference for spiciness and numbness), and slowly stir-fry over low heat to bring out the fragrance of the Sichuan peppercorn and chili. Be sure not to use too high heat to avoid burning.
