Compared to 2.1, 5.1 speakers, etc., in the usual sense of multimedia speakers, portable speakers are not only associated with activities and computers, MP3 players, and other products, but their compact size and battery-powered design allow users to use them without charging, meeting the needs for outdoor use. This has been the main reason for a growing number of users purchasing portable speakers over the past two years. In the first half of 2011, new products have further enriched the functionality of portable speakers, which have begun to replace items like radios and alarm clocks for domestic use.

From the current mainstream perspective, while products have become more feature-rich, the size of portable speakers hasn't decreased further but has evolved mainly in two aspects. On one hand, most manufacturers have only slightly altered the design form, establishing their own design accounts (like Xbox Live Gold), so that consumers can purchase personalized products. On the other hand, although the size of the enclosure may significantly impact sound quality, especially on the low-frequency side, changes in the structure of the unit and cabinet have also become a focus for all manufacturers. The sound quality of portable speakers will no longer be an easily surmountable shortcoming.
