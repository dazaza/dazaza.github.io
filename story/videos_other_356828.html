At the police station making statements, taxi driver Wang told reporters that he had not seen the Toyota driver inside the station, but he did see the municipal bureau inspector and heard that an investigation would be opened into the matter. Until 9 pm, after Wang left, the "tall man in a jacket" was still making statements outside the police station, while the Toyota driver and the taxi driver with the license plate number JIAZ1897 disappeared without a trace. The red Toyota car with the license plate number "JIO01000" was also not present at the police station. Yang Yong said that the Toyota car was driven away by someone after the accident.

Not long after, the 110 patrol police arrived at the scene. "At the time, the two people in the Toyota car were supposed to be taken by the 110 patrol police to the taxi police station, and the patrol police asked others to go to the taxi police station to make statements," Yang Yong said. But when they arrived at the police station, they only saw a tall woman in a jacket and never saw the short driver.

Violations Involving Police Cars and Case-Related Vehicles

According to reports, last night the Jilin Provincial Public Security Department held a meeting to further study and promote special management work regarding violations involving police cars and case-related vehicles. The meeting also announced the hotline numbers established by all cities and prefectures in Jilin Province, welcoming public supervision and reporting.

According to reports, "Is the car number 'cool' enough? Drunk driving! Running red lights! Beating people after causing an accident!" At around 6 p.m. last night, at the intersection of Fourth Street and Min Kang Road in Changchun City, a red Toyota SUV with the license plate number "JIO01000" collided with a taxi. Subsequently, the Toyota driver got into a dispute with the taxi driver and another taxi driver who tried to mediate.

Witnesses: Drunk driving, running red lights, provoking fights, and beating people

After everyone persuaded the two groups to stop, Yang Yong immediately called 110 and 112 to report the incident and used his mobile phone to photograph the Toyota driver. At that time, an unfamiliar woman pulled Yang Yong and taxi driver Mr. Wang aside, claiming it was inappropriate to make the matter too big and that she could handle it. However, Yang Yong and others refused and questioned her identity. Without saying anything, the woman left immediately.

"It's infuriating! They were drunk, hit another car, got back in their car and beat people, even hitting those who tried to mediate!" As a witness to the incident, Yang Yong was very agitated. He said that the incident occurred at around 6 p.m., when the red Toyota was traveling north along Min Kang Road toward the People's Square. When it reached the intersection of Min Kang Road and Fourth Street and encountered a red light, it collided with a taxi traveling south from Fourth Street, with the license plate number JIAZ1897.

Because the police are currently investigating the matter and have not yet confirmed whether Yang Yong's account is entirely true, some city officials believe that if what Yang Yong said is true, the reason the driver dared to act so arrogantly is due to the car's license plate. Therefore, the police should investigate the authenticity of the license plate and announce it to the public. At that time, either the person who forged the license plate will be heavily punished, or the "arrogant bully" will be severely punished.

Citizens: The authenticity of the license plate should be investigated

Yang Yong recalled that after the incident, both cars stopped. He noticed that the right rear bumper of the taxi and the right rear bumper of the Toyota were slightly damaged. "Actually, the matter wasn't serious at first, but then something unexpected happened." Yang Yong said that two men, one tall and one short, got out of the Toyota. The short man was the driver, about 30 years old, wearing a blue and white striped T-shirt. The tall man stood by the driver's side, about 40 years old, wearing a jacket. Both had bloodshot eyes and were clearly drunk. As soon as they got out of the car, they started cursing and physically assaulted the taxi driver.

At 8 p.m., the small hall of the Changchun Taxi Police Station was brightly lit, with reporters from various media surrounding Yang Yong.
