Subsequently, the reporter interviewed the Mudu Team of the Suzhou Public Security Bureau Traffic Police Detachment responsible for investigating the above-mentioned incident. According to a team leader, the above-mentioned accident happened at around 6:30 AM yesterday. Preliminary investigations revealed that at the time of the incident, the school bus was traveling south on Hong Kong Street, while a locally licensed small car was traveling west on Huayuan Street. At the intersection of the two roads, the small car collided with the side of the school bus, causing the school bus to lose control and flip over. Since the speeds of the two vehicles were not too high, the accident did not result in any casualties. The involved school bus, although a used car, had passed all reviews. The driver also held the necessary driving qualifications. At the time, there were five students, one leading teacher, and one driver on board. It was understood that the cause of the above-mentioned accident might be improper operation by the school bus driver, and further details were still under investigation.

According to Suzhou Wujiang District Education Bureau, currently, there are 10 approved migrant workers' children schools in Wujiang District, each school has its own school bus, "basically all second-hand", but these cars have undergone multiple inspections. He told the reporter that the school currently has six school buses, all of which are used cars: "A brand new medium-sized bus costs at least 100,000 yuan, and the government does not subsidize this area, so we can only buy used ones."

Currently, relevant departments in Wujiang District are discussing subsidy matters for school buses at migrant workers' children schools. Soon, the approved migrant workers' children schools in the district may enjoy school bus subsidies provided by the government.
