Nanning Sugar Industry: Rising sugar prices boost company performance_stock basics

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:27:33

Nanning Sugar Industry (000911) (enter this stock bar, new version of market conditions) surged strongly in the early session today, but eventually narrowed gains due to the drag of the falling market. By the close, it was up 6.49%, with a full-day turnover rate of 7.12%. The company is the largest state-owned listed company in China's sugar-making industry. The sugarcane industry it belongs to is a sector supported by the state and also a pillar industry for Guangxi and Nanning, enjoying various preferential policies from the state over a long period. The company's comprehensive earnings per share forecast for 2009-2011 are 0.38 yuan, 0.76 yuan, and 0.64 yuan respectively, corresponding to dynamic P/E ratios of 63, 32, and 37 times; currently, there are 24 analysts tracking the company, with 2 giving "strong buy" ratings, 18 giving "buy" ratings, and 4 giving "hold" ratings, with a composite rating coefficient of 2.08.

The supply in the sugar market for 2009-2010 will still be tight, and domestic sugar prices will remain in an upward cycle. It is expected that sugar production in the next two years will be around 12.5-13 million tons, basically level with the previous season. In the third quarter of 2009, the company achieved operating revenue of 695 million yuan, up 25.57% year-on-year, operating profit of 43 million yuan, up 6852.34% year-on-year, net profit attributable to the parent company's owners of 37 million yuan, achieving earnings per share of 0.13 yuan. The gross margin for the first three quarters was 18.81%, up 0.16 percentage points year-on-year, and up 2.1 percentage points compared to the first half, with the company's profitability continuing to recover.

According to quarterly reports, as of now, the old sugar inventory for the 08/09 season nationwide is only about 3 million tons, which is not much pressure compared to normal years. If the start time of the 09/10 season is delayed, then there may be a slightly tight supply-demand situation, and sugar prices in the fourth quarter will continue to rise slowly. For the 09/10 season, the planting area in Guangxi region remains flat, while other regions across the country continue to reduce production. Therefore, the variable for the new season's output lies in Guangxi's yield. Based on the current growth trend, limited by the planting area, it is almost impossible for the national sugar production in the new season to increase significantly year-on-year. The supply-demand gap for the 08/09 season exceeded 1 million tons, and the gap for the new season will be close to this number.

For the 09/10 season, the company's sugarcane planting area has basically remained unchanged at 1.08 million mu. Therefore, the production of refined sugar in the new season mainly depends on the unit area output of sugarcane and the sugar content. From the current reports on various professional websites, although southern provinces have been continuously dry and rainless since July this year, the impact on the Nanning region has been relatively small, and the growth of sugarcane is slightly better than last year, with the sugar content likely to be slightly higher than last year. Shanxi Securities estimates that the company's sugar production for the 09/10 season could reach 6 million tons, and sugar sales from 2009-2011 are expected to remain at the 6 million ton level.

Huatai Securities believes that sugar can be called the 'colored' variety in agricultural products, but its demand is more rigid than colored metals. Since the next season's reduction in production both domestically and globally has become inevitable, the data on supply and demand, as well as inventory data, are all relatively transparent, making the growth of the sugar-making industry certain. Given that the planting area in Guangxi may be lower than market expectations, the supply-demand relationship next year will be even more favorable, maintaining a 'recommend' rating.

Risk factors: (1) Risk of sharp decline in international market sugar prices; (2) Risk of production loss caused by natural disasters.
