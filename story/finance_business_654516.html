Tsui Hark interprets "Detective Dee": no tribute, start from zero (photo)

by zhlhmntk80 on 2012-01-05 14:29:17

"Detective Dee and the Mystery of the Tian Dynasty" (hereinafter referred to as "Detective Dee") has been filming in Hengdian for a month. It has always been mysterious. Apart from the eerie atmosphere revealed in the leaked photos, people still have no idea about the real story and style of this movie. What new tricks is Tsui Hark going to play this time? A reporter visited the set in Hengdian and spent two whole days on the set, finally getting the chance to grab director Tsui Hark and ask the questions on his mind. Tsui Hark's style was still as inscrutable as ever - "Detective Dee is neither 'Once Upon a Time in China' nor 'Swordsman', it is a brand new attempt starting from zero!"

Tsui Hark Question: What kind of style does Detective Dee want to achieve?

Background: This year marks the 25th anniversary of Tsui Hark's films, and "Detective Dee" is also Tsui Hark's 50th work. In terms of character styling, you can see some "tributes" to Tsui Hark's past movies, such as Li Bingbing's male attire resembling Lin Ching-hsia's Dongfang Bubai, and Deng Chao's costume reminiscent of甄Zhen Zidan in 'New Dragon Gate Inn'. So the outside world has been speculating whether the martial arts style of "Detective Dee" will be more like 'Once Upon a Time in China' or closer to 'Swordsman'.

Answer: "I basically didn't find any traces of the past, I started from scratch."

In this movie, Director Tsui once again tried many things he hadn't attempted before. "Shooting grand scenes is very difficult, and I discovered many new combinations, such as suddenly making dozens of large wooden pillars emerge from the water. At first, I couldn't figure it out, but after gradual experimentation, I managed to solve it. There are also many special effects in post-production because Dee and his team create many strange things in the water. I don't want to deliberately pay tribute through the characters' costumes; Bingbing and Deng Chao are different from Lin Qingxia and Zidan."

Tsui Hark said that he had initially wanted to create a different kind of martial arts film in his mind, "Solving cases in period dramas are relatively rare, and this atmosphere is suitable for establishing a new direction." On the set, Tsui Hark also constantly changes his shooting methods based on the actors' performances and feelings. "The world is very magical, there are many things that cannot be explained by logic. Everyone in the movie is very smart, and the biggest highlight is that so many wise people are not recognized. They are absolutely more brilliant than Sherlock Holmes."

Question 2: What do you think "Detective Dee" and "Empress Wu" should be like?

Background: Choosing Andy Lau to play Dee caused a lot of controversy. Why isn't Dee fat as everyone is familiar with, and why did he suddenly become so handsome? And Carina Lau's makeup completely conforms to the murals, with one eyebrow appearing extremely "shocking".

Answer: "Everyone has their own version of Dee and Wu."

The appearance of Dee and Wu doesn't matter, because no one knows what they look like, so he hopes to express these two characters in an exceptionally romantic way. "Regardless of what movie I shoot, 'romance' is the most important element. The characters in this movie are very fascinating, and when the audience sees them, it's like seeing themselves on the screen, because everyone has romance in their hearts."

When something happens, everyone can't see the truth, only Dee can understand it at the first moment, "It's like he can perform magic, everyone else gets misled by false clues, only he can return to the correct clue." As for Empress Wu, "She should be like a mountain, very majestic, she represents the prosperous Tang dynasty."

And the unconventional Tsui Hark has his special way of making the movie look "romantic." Everyone saw a scene where Dee is released from prison and summoned by Empress Wu. Tsui created a lot of wind on the set, explaining: "A ruler meeting a prisoner, this is a strong contrast. Wind represents mood, when the troops are moved by the wind, the conflicts between the characters become sharp. This is romanticizing and emphasizing effect, movies need to dramatize life to show the audience."

Question 3: How to balance history and folklore?

Background: After the release of 'Red Cliff', it received a lot of criticism, with people condemning the absurd lines and plots. 'Detective Dee' is set in the Tang dynasty, and from the currently filmed story, it's clear that a lot of fictional elements have been added. However, Wu and Dee were real historical figures, and there are many historical facts that cannot be avoided, such as Dee's marital status.

Answer: "Folklore represents people's expectations of them."

Under Tsui Hark's romanticism, love affairs are bound to happen. If Dee develops a triangle relationship with Wu and Shangguan Jing'er, would it shock everyone? "Even if they fall in love, it's under a very special situation. Historically, Dee certainly had a wife, but in this movie, his wife won't appear." Tsui Hark says he doesn't mind if people scrutinize the origins, if mistakes are pointed out, he will improve them in the next movie.

Probe Notes: July in Hengdian, both "hot" and "noisy"

Hot, as Zhang Yi put it in his blog - when the whole country isn't hot, Hengdian is particularly hot; when the whole country is hot, Hengdian is especially hot. But ironically, most of the productions here are period dramas, so the actors still wear layer upon layer of costumes and thick head coverings. During our stay in Hengdian, Andy Lau happened to be dressed as a prisoner all the time. We saw that just as the director shouted "cut", he quickly took off his head covering and handed it to his assistant, putting it back on when the next scene was about to be shot. Repeatedly doing this made us feel annoyed, let alone himself. As for the beard, once glued on, it couldn't be removed, so Andy could only hold it up with his hand while fanning his neck vigorously with a fan. His shoes were also thick and tightly sealed. When he was unmade up, reporters said they saw his feet soaked in sweat until they turned white and wrinkled.

Noisy, with over thirty production teams simultaneously in Hengdian. At night, the lights used for night shoots illuminated Hengdian as if it were daytime. While we were watching 'Detective Dee' shoot night scenes, there were white lanterns hanging on the nearby buildings, inscribed with the words "Jia Family", implying that 'Dream of Red Mansions' had just finished shooting the "Grandma Jia's Death" scene here. Suddenly, an extra in Arab costume ran out from the adjacent garden, seeing the cast of 'Detective Dee' dressed in Tang dynasty costumes, he sheepishly smiled at us: "I look really ugly."

But amidst such a "lively" environment, Mr. Tsui Hark roamed around energetically every day with a white towel wrapped around him. When the actors were already resting and drinking mung bean soup in their trailers, he was still busy selecting and arranging the scenes. In this movie, the leading actors Andy Lau and Li Bingbing are respectively called "male model worker" and "female model worker" by the public. But even in front of Mr. Tsui (Hark), these two couldn't help but admit defeat. Li Bingbing showed us the bruises on her arms from cupping while laughing and saying: "I've already thought of the question for you - 'Filming 'Detective Dee', model workers die from exhaustion.'"
