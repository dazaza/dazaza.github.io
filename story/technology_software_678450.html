I don't want to use drugs to achieve sleep effects~ Improve sleep quality? We know that sleep is extremely important for brain health. People generally need more than 8 hours of sleep, and high quality must be guaranteed. If the sleep time is insufficient or the quality is not high, it will have adverse effects on the brain, making it difficult to recover from fatigue. In severe cases, it may affect brain function. If sleep is inadequate or of poor quality, the sleep time should be appropriately increased, such as taking a nap in summer, and methods to improve sleep status should be found.

According to general views, sleep is the main way to eliminate brain fatigue. If long-term sleep is insufficient or of poor quality, it will seriously affect brain function, and even smart people will become confused. Many people suffer from neurasthenia and other diseases, often due to serious sleep deprivation.

In short, one-third of a person's life is spent sleeping. Good sleep can regulate physiological functions, maintain the balance of the nervous system, and is an important part of life. Poor or insufficient sleep will lead to headache, dizziness, and weakness the next day. The relationship between sleep, health, work, and study is very close.

The following aspects can improve sleep quality: * Sleep in moderation 1. Our important view is: don't sleep less. In many books, it is said that adults usually sleep 7-8 hours a day. However, Dr. James Mass, an American psychology professor, recently pointed out that 6-7 hours of sleep at night is not enough. His research on sleep shows that only 8 hours of sleep can make human body functions reach their peak. Therefore, what is "moderate" mainly depends on "restoration of spirit and physical strength" as the standard. Human sleep is divided into slow-moving eye sleep and rapid-moving eye sleep. Concentrated rapid-moving eye sleep occurs in memory storage, tissue maintenance, information sorting, and new learning and performance, all happening in the final stage of rapid-moving eye sleep. Rapid-moving eye sleep usually occurs in the later part of the 8-hour sleep period and can last about 90 minutes. Although we may not realize it, most of us actually don't get enough sleep, which not only reduces the quality of life but can also cause diseases.

To make up for this common lack of sleep, Dr. Mass advocates "napping." This napping refers to taking a 20-minute nap after waking up from formal sleep every day, which is much better than going to bed earlier at night.

We particularly emphasize that although middle school students say they have "reduced burdens," due to various exam pressures, they are not relaxed, and many people have significantly insufficient sleep time. Compared to the past, it is actually loosely tight. This is actually a loss for both society and families. We believe that only by sleeping well can one study well. Sleeping well will not hinder one's future: sleep time must be guaranteed!

* Sleeping environment To get good sleep at night, it is very important to pay attention to the three dos and don'ts before sleeping.

The three dos are: Take a walk before sleeping. The book "Ziyan Yinsu • Yangshu" says: "Walk around the room a thousand steps before sleeping, then go to bed... Because mental labor causes fatigue, fatigue leads to rest, and extreme movement seeks stillness."

Sleeping should have a suitable environment, mainly a quiet bedroom and comfortable bedding. Ventilation is an important condition for the bedroom, as fresh air is more important than anything else. Regardless of the outdoor temperature, open the window to change the air before sleeping. Choose a comfortable bed, generally with a soft-hard moderate palm-tight bed or soft wooden board mattress being appropriate. The pillow should be moderately soft and hard, trying to stay warm in winter and cool in summer.

Have the correct sleeping posture. Generally, it is recommended to lie on the right side, slightly bend the legs, relax the body naturally, place one hand under the pillow, and place the other hand naturally on the thigh.

Develop good sleep habits. Whether it is nightly sleep or daytime naps, try to go to bed and get up at the same time, even on holidays. Conduct regular moderate activities.

* Follow the biological clock If we wake up on time every day and regularly greet the morning sun, our biological clock will run on time. Studies show that this is one of the key elements to improving sleep quality. One of the factors affecting the operation of the biological clock is body temperature. Research proves that fluctuations in human body temperature have a great impact on the rhythm of the biological clock. A drop in body temperature easily induces sleepiness, which is an effective method of regulating the biological clock using body temperature. If body temperature regulation fails, it will cause the sleep biological clock to become disordered. There are many ways to control body temperature, such as taking a bath before bed or doing 20 minutes of aerobic exercise before bed, which will lower body temperature during sleep.

In short, after forming a habit, people will fall asleep on time. Teenagers should develop good sleep habits, which is the most important. The biological clock cannot be easily disrupted, so never stay up late on Saturday and Sunday nights and sleep in during the day, disrupting your own biological clock.

* Regulate diet We often see such situations: some people consume large amounts of coffee, chocolate, cola, tea, etc., at night, and subjectively feel no problems with their sleep, but experiments have shown that their deep sleep is adversely affected. Therefore, do not consume these things before sleeping.

To obtain good sleep, there are several things to avoid before sleeping: Avoid overeating Dinner should be seven to eight parts full. Don't eat before sleeping to avoid increasing gastrointestinal burden. Avoid drinking strong tea and coffee Don't drink strong tea, coffee, etc., at night to avoid affecting normal sleep due to excitement or frequent urination. Avoid drinking alcohol Research has proven that drinking alcohol seems to help people fall asleep, but it is actually incorrect. Alcohol releases a natural stimulant during metabolism, disrupting our second half of the night's sleep.

* Noise Many teenagers, due to frequently being in noisy environments, become accustomed to it, adapting to this unhealthy environment. This is detrimental to our sleep, reducing our deep sleep time. Therefore, the sleeping environment should try to avoid noise interference.

Additionally, for those prone to insomnia, one should only go to bed when feeling sleepy. Going to bed early often results in "haste makes waste," only increasing psychological pressure. Some experiments have shown that in certain situations, going to bed late and getting up early, reducing sleep time, can help improve sleep quality.

* Sleeping time To improve sleep quality, the time of sleep must be noted; the best time to fall asleep to obtain good sleep quality is from 9 pm to 11 pm, from 12 pm to 1:30 pm, and from 2 am to 3:30 am. At this time, the body's energy decreases, reactions slow, thinking slows, emotions are low, which helps the body transition into slow-wave sleep, entering sweet dreams.

What kind of sleep is the best? Sleep should be an unconscious pleasant state. Even if the sleep time is short, if one feels energetic the next day after waking up, it indicates good sleep 'quality'. However, if one still feels tired after sleeping for a long time, it indicates poor sleep quality.

Insomnia is a persistent physiological disorder where the quality and/or quantity of sleep is unsatisfactory. Worry or fear about insomnia is a psychological factor contributing to this syndrome. Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that can be secondary to physical, environmental, neurological, and psychiatric diseases. This syndrome is due to mental tension, anxiety, fear, and worry about insomnia, so-called primary insomnia. Its symptoms include difficulty falling asleep, shallow sleep, easy awakening, early waking, and frequent dreams. After waking up, one feels fatigued or lacks clarity, and experiences daytime sleepiness, severely affecting work efficiency or social function. Typically, insomnia occurs at least three times a week and lasts more than a month. To distinguish different stages of insomnia, insomnia is divided into difficulty falling asleep, unstable sleep, and early waking. Difficulty falling asleep is characterized by lying in bed for 30 minutes or even 1-2 hours without being able to fall asleep, tossing and turning, restless, and uncomfortable. By the time one falls asleep, it is already midnight. Unstable sleep is characterized by shallow sleep, frequent dreams, easy awakening, and being able to fall back asleep, but waking up 3-4 times or more per night. Sleep efficiency is low, resulting in losing 20% of sleep time each night, while normal people lose no more than 5%. Due to frequent awakenings during the night, sleep quality is affected, leading to symptoms of insufficient sleep. Early waking is characterized by being able to slowly fall asleep, but waking up after only 2-3 hours and repeatedly unable to fall back asleep, waking up 2 hours or earlier before dawn and unable to fall back asleep or being in a semi-sleep state, repeatedly dreaming. Since waking up in the middle of the night and having to wait until dawn to get up, if everyone at home is still sound asleep, the insomniac becomes more irritable and angry. Many cases of insomnia are caused by discomfort in certain parts of the body, as follows: 1. Central nervous system diseases, such as brain injury, brain tumor, cerebrovascular disease (brain hemorrhage, cerebral infarction), Parkinson's disease, senile dementia, epilepsy, migraine, etc. 2. Respiratory system diseases, such as chronic bronchitis, chronic obstructive pulmonary emphysema, etc. 3. Urinary system diseases, chronic renal failure often results in short and fragmented sleep. Only kidney dialysis or kidney transplantation can effectively solve diabetes, diabetes insipidus, urinary frequency caused by urinary tract infections, which can also interfere with sleep. 4. Allergic diseases, also often interfere with sleep, such as skin itching, nasal obstruction, making sleep impossible. 5. Digestive system diseases, such as peptic ulcer, enteritis, dysentery, etc., causing abdominal pain, heartburn, nausea, vomiting, etc., also significantly interfere with sleep. 6. Circulatory system diseases, especially heart failure, angina pectoris, hypertension, arteriovenous inflammation, etc., can all cause insomnia. 7. Inflammation and pain in bones, muscles, and joints are common clinical diseases, causing varying degrees of sleep disorders. In these diseases, insomnia is mostly manifested as a concomitant symptom, and insomnia generally recovers when the disease is cured. It is more important for the elderly's health.
