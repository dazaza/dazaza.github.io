The last women's basketball game of 2010

by cha1214n on 2010-05-30 12:46:28

The final women's basketball game of 2010 - an unexpectedly hard farewell.

The game has ended, but the story is just beginning.

We are a team of twelve people, well, thirteen if you include the coach...

On April 13th, 2010, a Tuesday, the weather was cold and overcast. That was our first meeting. The coach at the time wasn't the one who would later become our coach.

On April 14th, 2010, a Wednesday, the weather was slightly rainy. That was our second meeting. And from then on, the coach became the one who led us till the end.

At the very start, the freshmen had to practice Tai Chi in the morning. Our training was scheduled for noon. Fortunately, at the very beginning, it wasn’t too hot yet, and there was always a cool breeze at noon. But as time went by, it got hotter. We trained under the blazing sun, dribbling and shooting over and over again. Finally, when the freshmen's training ended, ours was moved to the morning, with occasional adjustments back to noon.

The coach made us play games. There were many kinds. They were designed to improve our agility, speed, and endurance. One day when the sun was particularly strong, a group of us ran around the field chasing and dodging each other. I felt very tired while running and told the coach, "Coach, I can't run anymore. Can we stop this game?" The coach said in an upward tone, "If you give up now, isn't that like surrendering on the court?" At that moment, I was speechless and could only continue running silently.

One of the most memorable games we played later was called 'Monkey Chase'. Four people form a group, three standing in a triangle passing the ball to each other, while the fourth person in the middle tries to intercept the ball. Whoever intercepts the ball swaps places with the person whose pass was intercepted. If the person outside manages to make ten passes without being intercepted, the person in the middle gets punished. Our group followed the principle of mutual assistance, ensuring no one failed to intercept ten consecutive passes, thus avoiding punishment. Another group wasn't as united.

Another game involved dividing into two teams to dribble and shoot hoops. This game was played twice, with the losing team getting punished. Our team never lost.

I read a sentence once: "Every player goes through the process of improving their shooting accuracy from low to high, and the first stage—low accuracy—is quite a long period. Without a spirit of perseverance, they would have given up long ago." I was deeply moved by this. As a "player" who has been playing basketball since childhood but with consistently poor accuracy, I am still in the phase of low shooting accuracy. But I haven't given up, neither have my teammates.

The most frustrating game for me this season was the day Wang [name] and Wang Genfei came. My shooting accuracy was zero that day—two three-step layups, two close-range shots, and two free throws—all missed. I was extremely dissatisfied with myself. However, luckily, Dongdong's last-second shot helped us win the basketball game against the College of Distance Education and Continuing Education with a soccer-like score of 3:2. Wang [name] said that when he saw our team score the second goal, he already knew we were going to win, even though there was still a considerable amount of time left. Wang Genfei said that whoever had the ball on the court, it seemed like everyone else wanted to strangle them and take the ball away.

During the two weeks of the matches, I often dreamed about playing games and scoring goals. But I never dreamed about "winning". Well, let's hope for more good dreams next year.

Initially, I used to post daily match reports on my social media space, but after losing several times, I became more composed. I originally thought I would cry or get overly emotional after the matches ended, but surprisingly, I found myself in a very good mood after the games were over. Alright, I admit, winning or losing has become less important to us. Even though we lost, we still showed our style. For example, today Meng Pan’s shoe was mysteriously taken off in the crowd on the court. Also, Dongdong boldly committed a foul and was eventually penalized off the court.

I added up the total, this season we scored 27 points in 8 games with a total of 32 quarters (quietly saying, I scored 12 points~). Although we averaged less than one point per quarter and less than two balls per day, some excellent players can score this much in just one quarter—but I am still very happy. What we have is called joyous basketball.

It's been a long time since I've been so obsessed with something. For basketball, I couldn't sleep properly, constantly dreaming about how to play better. For basketball, I seriously took care of my eating and sleeping habits to maintain energy. It's been a long time since I've been so calm and composed while staying up late. From now on, this summer, I don't have to wear sports clothes and sneakers anymore. I can wear comfortable sandals, slightly slimmer pants, and avoid the scorching sun every day. I don't have to call home every day to report the results, I don't need my father to give me brilliant strategies remotely, and I don't have to worry my parents about whether I'm injured or not. The coach won't shout at me from the sidelines anymore, “Luyao, where is the rebound? Where is the bank shot?”; no classmates will remind me anymore, “Watch out for the person behind you”; and I won't be panicked and confused on the court anymore. ...

Finally, thank you mom and dad for your continuous support and encouragement. Thank you coach, thank you teammates, thank you sunshine and raindrops, and the refreshing breezes that passed over the northern playground. Thank you Henan University; thank you College of History and Culture; thank you Student Union; thank you Sports Department of the Student Union; thank you Guo Tianwang, Zhao Huiyuan, Zhu Xiaojing, Ye Xiaoting, and those student union members whose names I still do not know, for cheering us on, waving your arms for us, and loudly applauding for us. Most importantly, thank you for the cool mineral water every day; thank you all friends who watched my games, for your care, support, and encouragement ~~~~~~~~~~

On the north playground, you can calmly see the thousand-year-old iron tower standing tall. Whenever I see it, I feel excited and moved. When we weren't here, it waited for us; when we leave, it quietly sees us off.

These past few days, I’ve been muttering the line from Zeng Yike's song, "At the time of parting, unexpectedly unable to let go."

I've always been such a person. Rambling and verbose, trying to hold onto time, or at least leave a shadow of it behind.

At the time of parting, unexpectedly unable to let go. Always having to wave goodbye, bidding farewell to one beautiful memory after another, then moving forward.

Dear girls. I will miss these days.

May 17th, 2010, Monday, sunny weather. Our first game. Facing the Literature College, calmly scoring 6 points. Lost.

May 29th, 2010, Friday, sultry weather. Our last game. Facing the New Media College, casually scoring 4 points. Lost.

Won, lost, fell down; shoes dropped, eyes swollen, fouls committed—Not bad at all~

So many highlights, so many splendid days, none of us missed them~

The game has ended, but the story is just beginning. We are a team of twelve people, well, thirteen if you include the coach... And just like that, it's passed~
