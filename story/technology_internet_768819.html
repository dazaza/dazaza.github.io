Baidu Wenku involved in infringement again, claimed for 18.18 million yuan

by anonymous on 2013-11-25 11:13:19

Baidu Wenku, operated by Beijing Baidu Netcom Science and Technology Co., Ltd., was sued for allegedly infringing copyrights by providing electronic versions of three books owned by China Youth Publishing House's subsidiary, China Youth Media Corporation, through its website. The company is being asked to pay 18.1865 million yuan in damages.

This case marks the first instance in China where a publishing house has filed a lawsuit against Baidu Wenku for copyright infringement.

Alleging two instances of unauthorized e-book distribution

According to the complaint filed by China Youth Media Corporation, it legally holds the exclusive publication rights and information network dissemination rights for the best-selling works "The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People," "Now, Discover Your Strengths," and "Koala Wu's English Learning Diary." Without authorization, Baidu provided various versions of these three works online to the public via the "Baidu Wenku" website.

China Youth Media Corporation claims that Baidu violated the copyrights of its publishers and authors, causing significant losses. As a result, they have filed a lawsuit demanding Baidu cease the infringement and compensate them with 18.1865 million yuan in damages.

"In March 2011, Baidu had previously deleted the aforementioned three works from its website that were suspected of infringement. However, not long after, Baidu once again made these works available to the public on its site, and this situation has persisted until now," said Liu Wei, head of China Youth Media Corporation. Investigations revealed that Baidu's business license scope is "Internet Information Services," which does not include "publishing, education, or medical care."

Baidu claims it has removed the infringing e-books

Yesterday afternoon, Baidu also responded. It stated that Baidu Wenku, as an information storage space provided by Baidu for netizens, is an open platform for sharing documents online. This platform greatly satisfies the needs of internet users for knowledge-based documents.

Additionally, Baidu emphasized that it has always placed great importance on copyright protection, assigning dedicated personnel to promptly respond to complaints and reports. For any documents suspected of infringement, upon notification by the rights holder, Baidu guarantees their immediate removal.

Currently, Baidu has launched an "Anti-Piracy DNA Comparison System," established a green reporting channel, and so far, Baidu Wenku has collaborated with over 1,000 copyright institutions, collecting more than 10 million authorized documents.

Regarding the lawsuit filed by China Youth Media Corporation, Baidu stated that after receiving complaints from the copyright holders, they immediately deleted the three electronic documents suspected of infringement and banned the user accounts responsible for uploading them.

According to China Youth Media Corporation, Baidu proposed mediation during an informal hearing held on October 10. In response, Baidu did not provide any comments.

Writers' Alliance previously sued Baidu Wenku

Prior to this, Baidu Wenku had been embroiled in multiple copyright infringement disputes and was sued by several writers. This is the first time a publishing house has independently sued Baidu for alleged copyright infringement in China.

In March 2011, 50 writers jointly condemned Baidu Wenku without success, subsequently forming the "Writers' Rights Protection Alliance" and initiating legal action. Writers such as Han Han, Hao Qun (pen name: Murong Xuecun), and Han Ailian (pen name: He Ma) subsequently sued Baidu.

On July 26, 2011, the National Copyright Administration imposed administrative penalties on Baidu for copyright infringement. On September 17, 2012, the Haidian Court in Beijing announced its verdict in the copyright infringement dispute cases brought by writers Han Han, Hao Qun, and Han Ailian against Baidu, ruling that Baidu's actions constituted infringement and ordering compensation of 173,000 yuan for damages and reasonable expenses.
