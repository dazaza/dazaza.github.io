Introduction to White Light Penetrating Glasses

White light glasses, also known as fluorescent imaging glasses, are one of the anti-counterfeiting products. Under the irradiation of fluorescent lamps with different wavelengths, white light glasses show different fluorescent imaging reactions: long waves present a kind of color fluorescence response, while short waves present another color fluorescence reaction.

There are two types of white light glasses: long-wave type (365 nanometers) which sees the external environment as pale red and short-wave type (254 nanometers) which sees the external environment as pale blue. Human eyes are particularly insensitive to light in this range, generally unable to see clearly or completely invisible. To observe, it is necessary to have an excitation source that must be ultraviolet light or red light with a wavelength between 400-560 nm.

Products of white light glasses include: filter lenses, light-changing penetrating glasses, all of which are high-quality white light glasses, comfortable and ultra-thin. Good quality white light glasses have no side effects on humans and can also be used for magic performances under white light lamps. Only by adding special ink on the poker cards, fonts invisible to the naked eye on the original poker cards can be seen when wearing white light glasses. The invisible words on the back of the poker card can appear in visible light colors, just like the principle of banknote verification lights and UV lights, so they are also used in entertainment industries such as poker and mahjong.

Different Types of White Light Penetrating Glasses Advantages and Disadvantages:

Advantages:

- Low water content: Minimal environmental impact, stable lens parameters, higher hardness, easy operation, higher refractive index, almost any lens care solution can be used, good lens repeatability, less likely to cause corneal staining due to water evaporation.

- High water content: High DK, more flexible, faster recovery after deformation.

- Low refractive index: Normal humidity, normal flexibility.

- Ionic: Better moisture, fewer tear protein denaturation.

- Non-ionic: Less likely to precipitate, does not adhere to charged particles.

- Spherical: Clear vision, good movement, fixed base curve.

- Aspherical: Good fit with the cornea, good comfort, good central positioning.

Disadvantages:

- Low water content: Low DK, poor flexibility, difficult thin lens operation.

- High water content: Fragile lenses, prone to precipitation, easily affected by the environment.

- Low refractive index: Poor parameter stability and repeatability, difficult to manufacture too thin, water evaporation, not recommended to use heat disinfection method.

- Ionic: More lens precipitation, attracted charged particles easily affected by PH changes, leading to lens parameter changes.

- Non-ionic: Difficult to keep moist, more tear protein denaturation.

- Spherical: Slightly worse corneal fit, slightly worse center positioning.

- Aspherical: No fixed base curve, slightly poorer movement.

Maintenance of White Light Penetrating Glasses:

1. Follow the instructions on the solution for maintenance. Each bottle of maintenance solution emphasizes different points and is not necessarily better or worse. "In fact, proper maintenance can achieve the same cleaning results." Also, pay attention to the production date and shelf life of the maintenance solution, do not use expired solutions. Discard the maintenance solution within three months after opening even if it is not finished, do not continue to use.

2. Regularly replace white light penetrating glasses, including hard-type white light penetrating glasses, should be updated every 1-2 years. In addition, do not treat daily wear as bi-weekly wear or bi-weekly wear as monthly wear, extending the usage period will harm the cornea.

3. Regularly remove proteins. If using hydrogen peroxide solution, need to remove proteins first before soaking in the solution; those using multi-functional maintenance solutions are advised to remove proteins weekly to reduce infection opportunities.

4. Do not store lenses in saline. Saline has no sterilization effect, storing lenses in it can easily grow bacteria.

5. Clean the storage box at least once a week and replace it every three months to avoid becoming a breeding ground for bacteria. The cleaning method can use cotton balls dipped in cleaning solution to wipe the inside of the small box, then put it in hot water above 79°C for disinfection, then dry it, try not to place it in the bathroom because humidity easily causes mold.

6. Do not soak the lenses in the solution for a long time, the solution will gradually lose its disinfection function, instead becoming a breeding ground for bacteria. For those who don't wear white light penetrating glasses often, it's best to change the soaking solution every two days, and remember to re-disinfect and clean before wearing.

7. Do not wear white light penetrating glasses while swimming. Chlorine, bacteria, impurities in the swimming pool can attach to contact lenses, damaging the corneal tissue or causing infections, even corneal ulcers.

8. If you want to change the solution due to new product launches or friend recommendations, you must pay special attention to the condition after using the new solution. If there are symptoms such as redness, swelling, itching, foreign body sensation, dryness, tearing, secretions, etc., you must stop wearing immediately to confirm whether it is an eye or solution problem. If it is caused by the solution, it is suggested to stop using it and return to the original solution.

Glasses Maintenance:

1. Single-handed removal and wearing can damage the balance of the frame, leading to deformation. It is recommended to hold both sides of the temple along the cheeks parallel direction to remove and wear.

2. Generally fold the left temple first when removing and wearing to avoid deforming the frame.

3. It is recommended to rinse glasses with clear water and dry with a paper towel before using a dedicated glasses cloth to wipe. Hold the edge of the frame on the side being wiped and gently wipe the lens to avoid excessive force causing damage to the frame or lens.

4. When not wearing glasses, please wrap them in a glasses cloth and put them in the case. If temporarily placed, please face the convex side up to avoid scratching the lens. At the same time, glasses should avoid contact with insect repellents, toilet cleaners, cosmetics, hair gels, medicines, etc., corrosive items, avoid prolonged exposure to sunlight and high temperatures (above 60℃), otherwise, it can cause degradation, deterioration, discoloration, etc., of the lens and frame.

5. Regularly visit professional stores for reshaping adjustments. Deformed frames can burden the nose and ears, and the lenses are prone to looseness.

6. Do not use glasses during intense activities to avoid strong impacts causing lens breakage, resulting in eye and facial injuries; do not use worn lenses to avoid light scattering causing vision decline; do not look directly at the sun or strong blinding light to avoid eye damage.
