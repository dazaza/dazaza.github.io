How Newcomers in Foreign Trade Can Get Potential Customers to Place Orders Quickly

by fubu88482 on 2011-08-29 09:44:18

As a newcomer, in the absence of seniors to teach you, first of all, you need to position your product direction, and then accumulate and learn step by step. If you want to do clothing, you must understand clothing; if you want to do hardware, you must know its process. Don't try to do everything without being professional, because you will do nothing well. With good products, there will definitely be good customers and good orders.

1. Price is the hard truth, your price must have an advantage.

2. If the customer says they want to inspect the factory, then your opportunity has come. Never complain about the trouble. Only big customers will inspect the factory before placing an order.

3. Do not exaggerate existing achievements to potential customers.

4. What you promise must be achieved. Even if you can't finish it, you should tell the customer in advance, not wait for the customer to ask. Integrity is very important, not only for the company, but also for individuals. Even if the deal is not made, at least keep the integrity in front of the customer, which is greatly beneficial to both business and future development.

5. Quotation needs skill. If the quotation for an MP3 with the same capacity is higher than Sony's, who would be interested?

6. When receiving customer inquiries, reply promptly. Even a general format reply will let the customer know your efficiency and respect for them. Sometimes, by the time you consider how to reply and quote, the customer may already be gone.

7. Explore what are the main factors that customers consider when deciding whether to place an order.

8. Do not easily say no to customers. Smooth handling is a good choice. For example, if the target price set by the customer cannot be met, you can say I will help you negotiate with my boss or recommend a product that can reach the target price to the customer.

9. When attending exhibitions, it is best to go on the first day, because except for the first day, most exhibitors' salespeople lack enthusiasm. They almost passively respond to customer inquiries. Some salespeople think they have sharp eyes and treat customers differently. These are all fatal mistakes. Leave a good impression on every customer who comes to your booth during the exhibition days.

10. Customer development should be purposeful.

Sitting in the office, repeating the same work over and over again, sending emails, receiving emails... Many people have worked for months without any orders, or even without any clues. I believe most salespeople have experienced such situations. The reason is that they don't focus on key customers, but contact everyone generally, so it is naturally difficult to get results. In doing business, before sending emails to new customers, you must confirm that your email is valuable to the customer.
