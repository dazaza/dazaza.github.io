Looking at the Future of Online Commerce from "Lemon Green Tea"

by loveruly on 2009-03-29 10:21:30

Looking at the Future of Online Businesses through "Lemon Green Tea"

I published an article last month titled: The Ceiling for Online Businesses is Not on the "Platform." In it, I referenced the case of "Lemon Green Tea." Today, we will still use "Lemon Green Tea" as an example to discuss the development prospects of online businesses. Perhaps we cannot articulate the ultimate form of online business development, but its reference value must not be overlooked. By analyzing the development process of "Lemon Green Tea," I hope to provide some references for the many online merchants growing on Taobao.

From the establishment of "Lemon Green Tea" to now, we can divide its development process into four steps:

Step One: Market Expansion and Accumulation

From a timeline perspective, this phase started in August 2003 when "Female Lemon" began her entrepreneurial journey and lasted until March 1, 2005, when Shanghai Ailaiwu Information Technology Co., Ltd. was established.

This period lasted two years. First, a fortunate event occurred: although Lemon did not count among China's first batch of online merchants, she was undoubtedly one of the first entrants on Taobao. Whether it was in terms of store credibility or practical operational experience as an online merchant, substantial accumulation was achieved.

Additionally, this period marked a crucial change in China's online retail market. Taobao successfully replaced eBay EachNet with its free strategy. Meanwhile, the positions of Dangdang and Joyo in the traditional B2C market were ultimately solidified. Following this, China's online retail industry entered a period of explosive growth.

Step Two: Transition from Online Merchant to Internet Enterprise

This step was crucial in the entire development of Lemon Green Tea. Successfully transitioning from an online merchant to an internet enterprise, Lemon Green Tea achieved qualitative leaps in operations, management, and corporate brand building, laying a solid foundation for its future development and expansion. This transformation had significant impacts on traditional online merchants in the following two aspects:

Corporate Structure and Operational Model:

When most online merchants were still operating part-time on Taobao out of interest and entertainment, Female Lemon decided to become a full-time online merchant and established Shanghai Ailaiwu Information Technology Co., Ltd. on March 1, 2005. Currently, Lemon Green Tea's team has grown to over 260 people. It includes departments such as the Marketing Center, Retail Division, Human Resources and Administration Department, Procurement Department, Wholesale Department, After-sales Service Department, Audit Department, Warehousing and Logistics Department, IT Technology Department, etc. Through corporate structures and operational models, efficiency was improved, enhancing competitiveness. By elevating service levels, user shopping experiences were enhanced. A large and comprehensive sales and service team allowed Lemon Green Tea to promptly and accurately grasp market dynamics and adjust its business accordingly. With its vast sales terminals, these resources could become critical bargaining chips for resource integration with major players. Additionally, good cooperation with over 1000 upstream suppliers ensured the quantity and speed of shipments while also guaranteeing cash flow. Such an efficient and advantageous corporate operational model made it difficult for any other online merchant to compete. As Lemon Green Tea puts it, they are strengthening internal capabilities, improving management, and scaling their online store.

Corporate Culture Construction:

The author of "Built to Last," Jim Collins, has consistently emphasized how to build a great and enduring company? What management philosophy can withstand the test of time? This is the culture of the enterprise, which is referred to as the soul of the company. Whether it is customer positioning like "Building a network supermarket for white-collar women" or the principle of employing people regardless of their background but seeking "reliability," including their emphasis on service and their future diversification strategy, all reflect this corporate culture. Their customer service team of 100 people and a dedicated after-sales service team of 20 people embody this customer-first corporate culture concept. When Lemon started constructing all of this, most Taobao store owners were still in the family workshop stage. Establishing this corporate culture laid a solid foundation for maintaining a leading position and future development.

Step Three: Brand Building

Regarding brand building, I initially thought about placing it in the second step, but eventually, I put it in the third step. Because looking at the entire development process of Lemon Green Tea, its brand construction includes two aspects, and I personally believe that the second aspect of brand building will have a greater impact on its future.

Establishment of the Online Store Brand:

In the early years of Taobao, the platform was essentially in its primitive state. And the lack of integrity on China's internet was most evident at this moment. For Lemon Green Tea, its brand construction can be traced back to when Female Lemon became a full-time online merchant. The establishment of Shanghai Ailaiwu Information Technology Co., Ltd. can be seen as a milestone. Because this was not only a guarantee to customers, but through corporate management and operation, it ensured the reliability and safety of its product sources. By the end of 2006, it had completed 110,000 transactions, accumulating over three years and five months. However, in 2007 alone, it completed 200,000 transactions. Clearly, Lemon Green Tea has become a gem and a benchmark on Taobao. Becoming Taobao's No. 1 can be considered a breakthrough in achieving the brand value of its online store. At this point, it transforms from merely being an online store to becoming an important channel. To meet the requirements of diversification and further development, coupled with its excellent grasp of market dynamics, expanding the brand from its online store to the products it operates becomes inevitable.

Creating Its Own Brand:

There is no doubt that creating its own brand is essential. Any online merchant operating on a third-party platform should consider creating its own brand as an excellent choice. Here, an example illustrates the value of building its own brand. Initially, Female Lemon decided to sell yoga wear in her online store, finding a small factory to produce it on consignment, which yielded unexpected results. When this factory accepted Lemon Green Tea's OEM order, it began regular production with quality assurance and time constraints, underestimating the online sales potential. Eventually, the factory had to dedicate four of its six production teams solely to processing products for Lemon Green Tea. From this example, it is evident that it fully leverages the advantages of intangible brand assets. Its control over upstream and downstream channels is also apparent. By selling under its own brand, it gains market operating initiative, not only earning commercial profits but also obtaining part of the processing and manufacturing profits. This enhances its ability to withstand market risks and realizes the transformation from a sales agent to a production agent for customers.

Step Four: Diversified Operations

Diversification can be described from three aspects, and these three aspects are closely linked. Successful diversified operations have driven the healthy development of Lemon Green Tea. Of course, all of this is inseparable from its earlier structural reforms, cultural accumulation, and brand building.

Product Diversification:

For Lemon Green Tea as an online store or for the entire online retail industry, it is merely a channel, a terminal. If you want to maximize its value, horizontally expanding your product line is an inevitable choice. The fact reflects its philosophy: "Specialization in skills, no boundaries in commerce." Those who specialize gain mastery of all things, and those without geographical boundaries gain vast markets. Lemon Green Tea deeply understands this. While maintaining its advantage in cosmetics, it boldly ventured into the clothing sector and gradually expanded into shoes, bags, and accessories. Through diversified operations, various resources interact, assist, and complement each other. In pursuing the primary goal of becoming a cosmetics counter in the online market, it steadily laid the foundation for becoming a supermarket for female white-collar workers in the online market, achieving scale expansion and win-win development. It was precisely through diversified operations that it easily overcame another bottleneck in its development.

Business Model Diversification:

Regardless of how we think about it, handling 10,000 transactions per day and supporting a team of over 200 people is quite a headache. Thus, exploring new marketing models became an inevitable choice for Lemon Green Tea's development. Currently, the "Lemon Green Tea Member Community" website and shopping forum were launched not as independent B2C websites but as auxiliary websites to its Taobao headquarters, supplementing special needs that Taobao cannot meet, thereby enhancing the online store's shopping experience and firmly retaining customers. This pioneering new model is ingenious, effectively combining Taobao, which provides "platform" services, with Lemon Green Tea, positioned as a "channel." If Jack Ma aims to create an online "Walmart" providing infrastructure like "coal, electricity, oil," then in reality, it might resemble a commercial real estate company. However, Lemon Green Tea's new business model completely integrates "platform" with "channel," making it more akin to a Walmart.

Channel Diversification:

When the sales volume of an online store breaks through a certain limit, changes are bound to occur. For a retail terminal, there are two choices: one is horizontal expansion, lengthening its product line; the other is extending up and down the channel to increase profit margins. Here, the final point we focus on is its integration and reconstruction of the channel. The diversification of channels can be traced back to August 28, 2004, when Lemon Green Tea's first physical store opened in Hua Sheng Street, People's Square, Shanghai. As the business scale expanded, attempts at channel diversification never ceased. By establishing its wholesale platform to enter the B2B industry and building its own B2C platform to further enhance terminal competitiveness, it gained greater price advantages and faster response times through deeper cooperation with upstream channels.
