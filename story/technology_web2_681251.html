Wu Dong Qian Kun: Wife spends a million to support her husband's study abroad, man asks for divorce after graduation

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-02-28 12:10:15

Chutian Golden Newspaper (Weibo) reported that after spending 4 years in the U.S., Daqing man Li Shiyuan had all his expenses, amounting to 1.5 million yuan, covered by his wife. However, upon obtaining his doctoral degree, he refused to return to China, requested a divorce, and sought to divide the millions of assets his wife had independently accumulated over the four years...

In September 2007, one afternoon in Daqing City, Heilongjiang Province, Wang Yuanyuan's family gathered in the living room to discuss her husband Li Shiyuan’s plan to study in the U.S. After Li Shiyuan's earnest persuasion, Wang Yuanyuan and others finally agreed. Li Shiyuan promised that once he obtained his doctorate in the U.S., he would immediately return to assist his wife in her entrepreneurial endeavors.

In early July 2008, Li Shiyuan went to the U.S. More than twenty days later, he called from the U.S. to inform his family that he had been admitted to a university. Wang Yuanyuan was very happy. To allow her husband to focus on his studies, she refused to let Li Shiyuan work part-time in the U.S. and decided to bear all the costs of her husband's study abroad (about 1.5 million yuan). Wang Yuanyuan had the confidence to do so because she had just started a company with promising prospects. However, managing the company independently outside and taking care of her in-laws and child at home was no easy task for Wang Yuanyuan.

Under Wang Yuanyuan's careful management, the company gradually grew stronger, with total assets approaching ten million yuan. Friends began calling her a "millionaire." Wang Yuanyuan smiled it off; in her heart, she always looked forward to Li Shiyuan's return, hoping they could work together to make the company bigger and stronger.

However, as time passed, Wang Yuanyuan found that her communications with her husband were decreasing day by day. Li Shiyuan would hang up hastily after saying only a few sentences, citing busy research topics. Wang Yuanyuan didn't think much about it but instead worried about him, reminding him to take good care of himself.

On September 15, 2010, Wang Yuanyuan's mother-in-law suddenly suffered a cerebral hemorrhage and was saved after emergency treatment. During this period, Wang Yuanyuan was extremely busy, shuttling between the hospital, the company, and home every day, losing a lot of weight. Li Shiyuan did inquire about his mother's condition several times, but after learning that his mother had recovered and been discharged from the hospital, he never called home again.

In July 2011, Li Shiyuan finally successfully obtained his doctoral degree. Upon hearing the news, Wang Yuanyuan felt that her hard times were finally over, and good days were ahead. But a month later, she received her husband's "cruel call." Li Shiyuan said he planned to settle in the U.S. and had already found a suitable job there.

Wang Yuanyuan was shocked. She, their child, and his parents were all in China—how could her husband not return? After repeated questioning, Li Shiyuan finally admitted that he had fallen in love with an alumnus in the U.S. and planned to marry her.

After hanging up, Wang Yuanyuan burst into tears. Subsequently, when she tried to call Li Shiyuan again, no one answered. In December 2011, Wang Yuanyuan received an email from Li Shiyuan containing a divorce petition.

Li Shiyuan's father publicly criticized his son as a "modern Chen Shimin."

On January 16, 2012, the Civil Division Two of the Sartu District People's Court of Daqing City publicly heard this divorce case.

Li Shiyuan claimed that due to long-term separation, his feelings with Wang Yuanyuan had broken down. He requested the court to legally rule on their divorce and division of marital property. Li Shiyuan also presented a large amount of evidence proving that their marital property after marriage had reached ten million yuan.

Regarding the property details presented by her husband, Wang Yuanyuan stated that these properties had nothing to do with Li Shiyuan, as they were all acquired through her own efforts. To prove her point, Wang Yuanyuan also presented a large number of written evidences.

Unexpectedly, during the trial, Li Shiyuan's father appeared in the witness stand, publicly criticizing his son as a "modern Chen Shimin," accusing him of failing to fulfill any obligations to take care of the elders and children over the years. All these years, it was his daughter-in-law Wang Yuanyuan who had been making sacrifices. The elderly man said that if Li Shiyuan insisted on divorcing Wang Yuanyuan, he would sever ties with him.

Wife willing to accept husband back

On February 6, 2012, the court dismissed Li Shiyuan's lawsuit in the first instance. The judge explained that such a ruling was mainly due to Li Shiyuan failing to fulfill any obligations to take care of the elders and children while studying abroad, which responsibilities were entirely borne by defendant Wang Yuanyuan. Additionally, Li Shiyuan clearly erred in the divorce case concerning the above two points, leading to the court's judgment.

The judge mentioned that he had mediated in this case. During mediation, Wang Yuanyuan firmly stated that as long as Li Shiyuan changed his mind, she would still accept him. Li Shiyuan said he would reconsider the matter of divorce. It is understood that after the first-instance judgment was issued, Li Shiyuan did not appeal.

On February 15, Wang Yuanyuan told reporters that regarding whether to get divorced, she and Li Shiyuan had reached a consensus. After handling matters in the U.S., Li Shiyuan would return to China, and their conflicts had been alleviated.

The judge hoped that this case would serve as a reminder to couples or lovers living apart. Short-term separation might increase mutual longing, but as the separation time grows longer, such longing might gradually fade, possibly leading to divorce or breakups.
