CCTV10 "Approaching Science", a boring program.

by jiong on 2008-11-27 12:51:03

1. In one episode, it was said that there were strange noises every night at midnight in a certain village, waking up all the villagers. No one dared to go out and check, they were trembling with fear and stayed awake until dawn. After interviewing a bunch of elderly villagers, it was rumored that wild beasts roamed here and caused trouble in the village every night, creating panic... The music was so terrifying! And it was even split into two episodes for more suspense! In the end, it turned out that it was a fat man in the village snoring while sleeping! Is this fat man reincarnated from a diamond Arhat, or an illegitimate son of Pavarotti?! Can snoring wake up an entire village? K! I want to smash the TV!

2. Another episode said that a family kept finding fresh red liquid seeping out from the seams of the tiles on their living room floor, making a mess everywhere. All the villagers didn't know what was going on, guessing if the place was originally a graveyard or something like that. Then they invited numerous experts to study the geographical location and terrain conditions of their house, and the conclusion was that scientifically, it was impossible for underground minerals to seep up... So, the final conclusion was: the liquid was spilled by the family themselves to gain fame!

I fell down with a "bang"!

3. Another episode said that a cowhide drum that had hung for several years suddenly grew cow hair! This was quite a famous incident locally. The program interviewed many people, researching from Xiamen University all the way to the central government, but no one could explain it. Finally, they had no choice but to blame the drum maker for being unprofessional and not removing all the hair, claiming that the cow hair wasn't scraped clean at first? I'm dizzy... Isn't this questioning the professional level of the drum maker? Haha!

To dismiss me with such conclusions, is it looking down on my intelligence or is the intelligence of the director looked down upon by others!

4. Another episode said that an ancient temple in Mount Emei, located deep in the forest, never had a single leaf on its roof. With a long, profound, comprehensive, and multi-angle analysis, they interviewed the monks interning in the temple, tourists, security guards, lay practitioners, the director of the cultural relics administration bureau, and even his cousin's neighbor's dog, and finally concluded - it was blown away by the wind.
