Google updates its terms of service, "Share Recognition" will open user reviews

by anonymous on 2013-10-13 13:51:48

Google recently updated its terms of service again. Any ratings or reviews made by users through Google products or based on Google services will be considered as "approval of sharing." Google can use this content to deliver targeted ads and display the user's account and avatar to enhance the credibility of these ads. The specific effect is shown in the figure below:

The newly added "approval of sharing" option also allows Google to share user reviews and followed content with third-party companies and advertisers, who can then use this information for targeted advertising. However, if a user restricts their sharing permissions — for example, limiting it to friends only — then these ads will only be shown to that user's friends.

Google’s new terms were partly inspired by Facebook’s “Sponsored Stories,” a feature the social network giant launched two years ago that uses user comments and likes to deliver customized ads. Twitter, which is also planning an IPO, similarly offers “Promoted Tweets” as commercial content, though Twitter does not directly use user avatars or comments in its advertisements.

The "approval of sharing" has sparked discontent among many users, with some claiming they will abandon all of Google’s products — the new terms have touched on users' most sensitive concerns regarding commerce and privacy. Another issue, as pointed out by The New York Times, is that Google did not clarify whether businesses are willing to choose the "approval of sharing" service for ad placements. A reader of Ars Technica also raised a valid question: “If a user publicly gives a negative review, would Google still display it?”

Attached at the end of the article is the full text of Google’s new terms of service:

Google makes it easy to find great recommendations from your friends. For example, while browsing the Google Play Music store, you might see that a friend has +1'd a new album by your favorite artist. When you search for a restaurant, you may see an ad showing that another friend gave the restaurant 5 stars.

We call this "shared endorsements." Here's what you need to know:

You're in control: Your content will only be shared with others when you choose to share it, and shared endorsements won't affect who can view your posts or profile.

To help your friends and others discover great content online, your activity (such as reviews, +1's, follows, shares, etc.) along with your name and photo may be used in commercial or promotional contexts.

For shared endorsements in ads, you can choose whether your name and photo are used to help friends find content you like (and avoid content you don’t). You can manage this setting at the bottom of this page.

The name and photo shown in shared endorsements will be the ones you've chosen as public on your Google+ profile.

Settings: Shared Endorsements in Ads

This setting allows you to control how your name and photo are used in "shared endorsements" within ads. It applies only to actions displayed by Google in advertisements. The "Summertime Spas" example above shows a shared endorsement in a Google Search ad. Changing this setting will not affect how your name and photo are used in non-advertising content (for example, when you share music recommendations from the Play Store). You can also control the visibility of a particular action in non-advertising content by deleting it or adjusting its visibility settings.
