Domestic fuel price increase may be delayed, bullish sentiment is evident

by tnntj7767 on 2012-02-28 10:07:27

Supported by factors such as the Iranian nuclear report, dollar weakness and the latest favorable economic data from various countries, international oil prices have continued to rise recently, creating the longest continuous increase in over two years. As a result, the three-location crude oil change rate monitored by several domestic institutions has also exceeded the 4% red line. Industry insiders predict that oil prices may continue to rise this week, opening the window for an upward adjustment of retail refined oil prices on March 8th. However, the price adjustment might be postponed until late March due to the appearance of a reluctance-to-sell mentality caused by the rising domestic refined oil market prices. By the close of trading on the 24th, benchmark New York crude futures had risen for seven consecutive trading days, with a $1.94 increase in the April delivery light crude futures price, closing at $109.77 per barrel, setting a nine-month high. For the entire week, New York oil prices rose by $6.53, an increase of 6.33%, while the total increase last year was only 11%. Meanwhile, the April delivery London Brent crude futures price increased by $1.85, closing at $125.47 per barrel, the highest price since April 29th of last year, with a weekly increase of $5.89, or 4.93%.

The sustained rise in international crude oil prices has quickly lifted the three-location crude oil change rate above the 4% price adjustment red line. According to data provided by GoldSilverIsland, a bulk commodities e-commerce platform, as of February 24th, the daily average price of three-location crude oil was $124.210 per barrel, with a 22-day average price of $116.947 per barrel, representing a 4.37% increase compared to the benchmark price on February 7th. The three-location crude oil change rate calculated by Zhongyu Information has reached 4.5%, while the change amplitude calculated by XiWang Energy is even larger at 5.04%.

According to China's current refined oil price adjustment mechanism, when the moving average price of Brent, Dubai, and Cinta crude oil changes by more than 4% over 22 consecutive working days, domestic refined oil prices will be adjusted. From February 8th when the National Development and Reform Commission raised domestic refined oil retail prices, to March 8th, it will reach 22 working days, officially opening the window for an upward adjustment of domestic refined oil prices.

However, analysis by GoldSilverIsland, Zhongyu Information, and XiWang Energy believes that considering the "two sessions" factor, the price adjustment might be postponed to late March according to precedent. In the most recent price adjustment, the National Development and Reform Commission explicitly stated that the price adjustment time was appropriately delayed due to the Lantern Festival.

As a country with an external dependence on crude oil exceeding 56%, the significant rise in international oil prices will undoubtedly have a major impact on China's economic development. Analyst Shen Tao of Zhongyu Information pointed out that a sharp rise in crude oil prices will directly increase the refining costs of domestic refineries. To avoid excessive losses for domestic refining enterprises, domestic refined oil retail prices must be adjusted in a timely manner. However, an oil price increase will directly affect the healthy development of the domestic logistics industry, increase the consumption cost of domestic gasoline and diesel, and exacerbate inflationary pressures.

"When the price adjustment window opens, the three-location crude oil change rate may already have risen to 6% to 8%. If the NDRC decides to postpone the upward adjustment of refined oil retail prices, it will inevitably lead to an increase in domestic refining cost pressures and double the expected increase," Shen Tao said.

Professor Lin Boqiang, Director of the Energy Economics Research Center at Xiamen University, believes that according to the current situation, international crude oil is likely to continue to rise, breaking through $110 per barrel. Before reaching $120 per barrel, there is still room for further upward adjustments in domestic refined oil prices. However, once it exceeds $120 per barrel, domestic price pressure will immediately become apparent, and the upward adjustment will be restricted.

Although there is still some time before the official opening of the price adjustment window, the atmosphere in the domestic refined oil market has already begun to be affected, with prices looking set to rise. The upward trend in blended oil products, which are more sensitive to international oil prices, is even more evident. According to feedback from blended oil traders in the South China region, it has recently been difficult to find blended raw material resources in the market, with prices continuing to rise, driving up regional blended oil prices and also boosting the price of standard gasoline sold by main distributors in the South China region, with price increases ranging from 50 to 100 yuan per ton.

Under the expectation of rising prices, a reluctance-to-sell mentality has emerged. "Currently, even if there are discounts in some areas, the scope is very limited, and large orders are required. In some areas, there is even a halt to batch sales and controlled marketing," introduced He Jiaying, analyst at GoldSilverIsland.

In addition, speculative demand has also started to heat up. According to an analysis report by XiWang Energy, some social gas stations have already begun planning to stockpile oil. However, due to excessively high wholesale diesel prices in many regions, plus the still sluggish finished oil consumption in the current sales market, most social gas stations are still in a wait-and-see state. Moreover, due to the recovery in diesel demand and the fact that prices in some regions are still inverted between wholesale and retail, the phenomenon of super-priced diesel sales at social gas stations has worsened again recently.

Analyst Wang Jintao of Zhongyu Information calculated that next month, the settlement cost of crude oil for domestic refineries will continue to rise significantly. Downstream product prices will be supported by high costs, and naphtha prices may soar by around 500 yuan per ton, forming a strong pull on solvent oil and the entire chemical industry. In the case of rising naphtha prices and unchanged retail refined oil prices, main distributors may adjust their processes to encourage naphtha production, potentially affecting the output rate of refined oil, further stimulating the refined oil market from the supply and demand side. If the refined oil price adjustment is postponed until mid-to-late March, due to warmer weather, stimulated by transportation, infrastructure projects, spring plowing, etc., the domestic diesel market will gradually start up. With the beginning of refinery maintenance, starting in March, the refined oil market may see another wave of price increases.
