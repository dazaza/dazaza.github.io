Gold time for left-over ladies Advance screening of "Bloody Siege" in Changsha moves audience to tears

by letaoedh on 2011-05-19 13:12:13

The intense scenes of "Blood-soaked Lonely City" moved the audience to tears and they rushed to the stage to grab the microphone

The screening of the day attracted many well-known entertainers from Hunan TV, Mango TV to come and support. Hosts like Du Haitao, Wu Xin, Ke Dou, Chang Ran, KK, Li Yang, Mingming, Lu Po, and Wu Luan all came to the event to support. These young and beautiful hosts also felt a bit excited after watching the movie, and they all expressed that domestic war films could be so exciting. Hunan Economic Television host KK said: Everyone says that people in Changde are united, but after watching this film, I understand where the spirit of Changde comes from, this movie makes me feel passionate. In contrast to the excitement of male hosts, a group of female hosts said that the movie was very touching, and the three handsome actors were full of manliness, especially Yuan Wen Kang's cool eyes, which often electrified them. Xiong Xiao Wen said: Such an intense war made the actors' performances more touching, from a woman's perspective, the love story in the movie touched me deeply, it is rare and precious today. Li Yang said: The movie made me realize that only when facing life and death can we weigh the importance of emotions, I cried, and I think everyone with a heart should watch this movie.

War epic appeals to all ages, 90s audience feels tense

Audience in tears thanked the actors

The war epic "Blood-soaked Lonely City", with an investment exceeding 50 million yuan, tells the story of a fierce and unusual battle between Chinese and Japanese forces that took place in 1943. The Kuomintang army, codenamed Hubin, followed the secret order to defend the city until death, exhausting their ammunition, without reinforcements, and almost no soldiers left, yet they fought for sixteen days against the Japanese army. The film features over 60 minutes of intense battle scenes, comprehensively showcasing eight types of modern warfare situations such as bombing, charging, bayonet fighting, angry battles, city sieges, street fights, and massacres. The tragic and emotional war scenes intertwined with the love story deeply moved the audience, making them cry uncontrollably.

An audience member said: After watching such a passionate and blood-filled movie, I am very excited. I am not from Changde, but I want to tell everyone that this movie is not only for people from Hunan to watch, but for all Chinese people, because we need to tell the real history to the future, to our descendants!

After the screening, the creators went on stage to exchange with the audience. Director Shen Dong and lead actors Louis Koo and Xie Meng Wei sincerely recounted their mental processes during filming, leading everyone back to that unforgettable tragic history. Shen Dong said: We already know about many heroes, but there are more sacrifices hidden behind the memories, we should not forget. The Hubin soldiers who fought in the bloody battle of Changde were determined to fight to the death, and our movie is about remembering those sacrifices before we completely forget them.

The audience found it hard to contain their excitement, and more than 300 people surrounded the stage, with mobile phone flashlights shining continuously. There were even audiences with red eyes who broke through the media encirclement, climbed onto the stage, and grabbed the microphone from the creators. He said: I am just an ordinary audience member, what I want to say is thank you to the creators. I am a tough guy, I have never cried, but look at my eyes now, they are still red.

"Blood-soaked Lonely City" has tearful audiences grabbing the microphone

The images in "Blood-soaked Lonely City" are intense and the subject matter is serious, and the theme of recreating history and remembering sacrifices is somewhat heavy, which once worried the producers and creators about the limitations of the audience base. However, from the audience reactions after yesterday's large-scale screening, the film basically appeals to all ages, and there is no need to worry. The audience on site not only included middle-aged people around 40-50 years old but also quite a few naive-looking 80s and 90s generations. As soon as the viewing ended, the media surrounded the girls layer by layer, asking about their viewing feelings. A girl said: I originally didn't dare to watch, but after watching, I found it very engaging, now such masculine plays are rare.
