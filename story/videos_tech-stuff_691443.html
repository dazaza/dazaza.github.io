Facebook valuation returns to $10 billion, Zuckerberg's net worth declines

by aswee25 on 2012-03-05 16:11:02

March 5, Beijing time - According to a foreign media report on Monday, Facebook's valuation reached $100.14 billion based on the transaction price of the US secondary market trading platform SharePost last week. However, when Facebook completes its initial public offering (IPO) in the coming months, the value of founder Mark Zuckerberg's shares may be less than $21 billion.

Facebook's IPO prospectus shows that the company plans to issue more than 500 million Class B common shares through the exercise of stock options and restricted stock units, but these shares have not been included in the total share count in the filing materials. Therefore, the data indicating that Zuckerberg holds 28.4% equity is incomplete.

Sam Hamadeh, head of research firm PrivCo, said that the shareholding ratio in the prospectus could be misleading, attributing shares that do not belong to Zuckerberg under his name.

During the IPO, Zuckerberg may sell about $1.75 billion worth of Facebook shares to pay the taxes generated from exercising stock options for 120 million shares. This transaction will dilute Zuckerberg's shareholding ratio from approximately 28.4% to around 21%. If Facebook's valuation remains around $100 billion, then the value of Zuckerberg's personal holdings will be about $21 billion, less than the $28.4 billion calculated from the prospectus.

Jonathan Thaw, a Facebook spokesperson, refused to comment.

The prospectus submitted last month showed that Facebook intends to issue 138.5 million Class B shares through the exercise of the 2005 stock option plan. The weighted average exercise price of these shares is approximately 83 cents.

Facebook will also issue 380.7 million restricted shares, plus the already issued 1.88 billion Class A and Class B shares. After the listing, Facebook's issued shares will approach 2.4 billion. Based on a market capitalization of $100 billion, each share will be worth about $41.75.

Zuckerberg's stock option exercise price is 6 cents. If he purchases all 120 million shares at this price, he will gain $5 billion. Calculated at a tax rate of 35%, Zuckerberg needs to pay $1.75 billion in personal income tax. He will need to sell 42 million shares for taxation purposes, leaving his total holdings at 491.8 million shares.

Hamadeh said that if you multiply the number of shares Zuckerberg holds by the expected issue price, his wealth is still far from $28 billion.

However, Facebook's stock price is likely to soar on the first trading day. For every $1 increase in the stock price, Zuckerberg's wealth will increase by $500 million. If the stock price rises by 35%, the value of his held shares will reach $28 billion.
