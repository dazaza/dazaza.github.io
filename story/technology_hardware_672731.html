Viewing the Evaluation and Investment Philosophy of Domain Name Value through the IPAD Trademark Dispute

by xwkj0983 on 2012-02-23 10:09:32

"The removal of IPAD from the shelves due to trademark infringement, and the seizure of products by the industrial and commercial department may sound like a joke to many people. How could Apple, whose market value just exceeded the combined total of Microsoft and GOOGLE, infringe on trademarks? If there was an infringement, it should be the other way around! However, after detailed investigation, we found that the court's judgment was not groundless. Shenzhen Proview's registration rights were established as early as 2000. The reason for today's favorable ruling for Proview is that when Apple entrusted an agent to purchase, three subsidiaries of Proview did not sell the trademark rights together.

Mentioning trademarks, as long as they are not obviously malicious registrations, the principle followed is that ownership belongs to whoever registers first. This makes us immediately associate domain names with it. After all, in the Internet world, domain names are a form of trademark. With the gradual "division" of this limited scarce resource, many enterprises' brand domain names also face the awkward situation of being preemptively registered. For us who are engaged in domain name registration and investment, how can we avoid the situation where Proview was sold to Apple's agency for only £38,000? How can we evaluate the value of domain names and grasp the philosophical logic in domain name investment? Through this heated IPAD trademark dispute, let's analyze and discuss together.

1. Inherent Value and Acquired Value of Domain Name Registration

In fact, domain names, including trademarks, have two values: inherent value and acquired value. Inherent value refers to whether the domain name itself is short enough, memorable enough, and specific enough. This is reflected in the high value of domain names within four characters, which still have a natural advantage even if they have not been used by enterprises. English word domain names are also "inherent nobility", with even higher prices. Then comes the double pinyin domain names, which have a very high natural value.

The acquired value of domain names is determined by enterprises. If a domain name has not been used by an enterprise in conjunction with business, then investors mainly speculate on its inherent value, while the use of domain names by enterprises determines their acquired value. Like the familiar Tmall double pinyin domain name, the initial transfer price was only hundreds of thousands. If we knew that Jack Ma would rename Taobao Mall to Tmall, the value of this domain name would definitely not be around one hundred thousand yuan. Of course, enterprises often choose domain names with better inherent value because these domain names are short, easy to remember, and easy to promote. Once a domain name is selected by a powerful enterprise, its value will quickly soar, and its value assessment will no longer stay at the level of inherent domain name characteristics.

2. How to Evaluate Domain Name Value and Invest in Domain Name Registration

So far, famous domains with higher natural value have basically been divided up. It is difficult for new domain name investors to register concise domain names unless they make investment acquisitions. But this method is only suitable for investors with substantial funds and not applicable to general investors. Therefore, one must choose between investing in domain names with high natural value or discovering creative domain names with appreciation potential according to their financial situation. Regardless of the method adopted, it involves how to evaluate the value of domain names and determine the target for investment registration.

Generally speaking, when evaluating the value of domain names, we should prioritize domain names with high natural value as our preferred investment targets. These are called "hard currency domain names". Even if no enterprise selects them, their resale price will not be low. The second choice is double pinyin domain names, especially those that can be judged according to their meanings for application in certain fields and potential purchasing companies. After such evaluation, one can select targets with more potential buyers. Finally, select some distinctive triple pinyin or domain names related to hot topics, such as well-known auspicious meanings like Hao Zhao Tou and Hao Ri Zi, and newly popular domain names like Jeremy Lin.

3. Skills and Philosophy in Selling Domain Names After Purchase

In this IPAD trademark dispute, what Shenzhen Proview mentioned most was that they believed Apple's acquisition at the time was "fraudulent", and they were deceived into selling for only £38,000. In fact, Apple's approach is a common practice in the industry. This way, they can prevent being overcharged and prevent planned product information from being leaked, etc. We can only say that Proview thought £38,000 was a good offer based on their position at the time, hence made the transaction decision. After all, before Apple launched its IPAD product, this trademark was purely a word trademark, and its value was only judged based on its inherent value. In domain name investment, we will also face the same issue: how to avoid selling good domain names at a low price and regretting it later?

Before selling already purchased domain names, one must do their homework, deeply understand and analyze the potential uses of the domain name, and include the applicable fields and potential buyers as value judgment factors. Then combine its inherent value for a reasonable evaluation and set an acceptable psychological expected price for oneself. Next, when contacting the buyer, use skills in quoting prices. Increase the quote by a certain ratio based on your psychological expected price, leaving room for negotiation. Also, try to analyze and search for information about the buyer to confirm their real identity and purchase intention. If the purchase is made through a lawyer or intermediary, it often hides undisclosed end customers. Directly purchasing end customers can allow you to make a reference quote based on their information, but beware of small companies commissioned by Apple pretending to be end customers. Do the final step of domain name trading well to avoid regretting the transaction price later. Remember one point: people who can maximize profits not only have strength but also luck factors. Don't blindly expect to occupy the maximum value. As long as you have worked hard and reached your expected return on investment, it is already a success for investors!"
