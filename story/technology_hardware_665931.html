Farmers Singing Becomes Internet Sensation - Practicing Songs While Farming for 20 Years (Photo Series)

Villagers say Zhu Zhiwen resembles an old child. Despite his age, when something pleases him, he can be seen climbing walls or hugging trees with ease.

He said that while he could sing well in the village, he was unsure about how he would fare in the county, let alone at the provincial level. At the time, he was very worried about "singing poorly and being laughed at."

"Don't call him by his nickname anymore," a middle-aged woman signaled to Wang Meizi. Some of Zhu's fellow villagers from Zhulou Village were basking in the sun near a wall corner. Li Yuhua was among them. She was fidgeting with her hands and remained silent.

During the toughest times last year, Zhu's family ate only their own vegetables and grains without spending any money, stretching one yuan for 15 days. The children would clamor for candy, but Zhu consoled them by saying, "We can't eat it; it'll make our teeth hurt."

Zhu Zhiwen: "Do what you want to do. Sometimes I see those singers on TV and they don't seem free. Now I feel like a wanted criminal. I'm really scared."

Zhu Zhiwen has never received professional training. He dropped out of school after three years of elementary education at the age of 12 when his father passed away. He returned home to help with farming and chopping firewood.

The Beijing News: Why is it good?

Zhu Zhi Xing believed this might be the origin of Zhu Zhiwen's love for singing.

After watching a few episodes, Zhu became slightly interested. He mentioned it to his wife, Li Yuhua, who agreed and told him to go ahead.

At the age of 20, Zhu Zhiwen spent 15 yuan to buy a tape recorder in Kaifeng's second-hand market and another 1.5 yuan on a cassette tape called "Karaoke for Everyone."

Clap, clap, clap, some audience members stood up to cheer, followed by thunderous applause.

At the audition site, seeing two to three thousand people gathered, Zhu Zhiwen felt uncertain and wanted to leave.

Midway through, the judges suddenly cut off the music and asked him to perform acapella. One judge "tested" him by asking if he was a disguised professional singer, demanding he take off his military coat.

The Beijing News: Why now? Have you ever participated in competitions before?

A few years ago, while working in Beijing, an elderly lady noticed his passion for singing and gave him an old electronic keyboard. He set it up at home and later created a practice room.

Zhu had experienced hunger. At the age of 16, he worked in Beijing with other villagers. In the early days, unable to find work, they pooled their money to buy a bowl of noodles. By the time it was Zhu Zhiwen's turn, there was only soup left.

Shandong Satellite TV's talent show "I Am a Big Star" aired, featuring Zhu Zhiwen dressed in a military overcoat, standing out among thousands of contestants. His simple appearance contrasted sharply with his powerful voice, earning him the nickname "Big Coat Brother" online.

That morning, under the glaring sun, Wang Meizi ran a mile and a half from the west end of the village to see just how famous "Three Big Mouths" had become.

In recent days, various cars from the city have driven over the bumpy village roads, stopping in front of Zhu Zhiwen's house.

"Three Big Mouths" is Zhu Zhiwen's nickname. He loves singing, has a loud voice, and a wide mouth, thus earning him the title from the villagers.

Nowadays, Zhu Zhiwen quickly gained internet fame, and his singing videos spread widely.

The Beijing News: What do you feel when you sing?

Several years ago, he bought a 12-inch color TV for more than a hundred yuan and installed cable television.

Before going on stage, Zhu Zhiwen's legs trembled so much that he almost couldn't stand.

The Beijing News: Do these changes suit you?

Practicing singing for 20 years, did you ever feel bitter?

Zhu Zhiwen: No, I didn't. I got used to it and didn't think it was hard.

Working in the fields, Zhu would often encounter neighbors. If someone said, "Hey, sing us a song," Zhu would put down his tools, gesture with his hands, and start singing. Cousin Yang Yuling said the more people listened, the more enthusiastic Zhu became.

Looking at the beauty pictures on the wall, Zhu Zhiwen blushed. He explained that the walls were shedding plaster, exposing the bricks, making it necessary to cover them with pictures he picked up while working elsewhere. On the ground lay a red carpet he also picked up, saying it helped him find the feeling of a stage.

Zhu Zhiwen's house is located in Zhulou Village, Guocun Town, Shanxian County, Heze City. The village is remote, requiring several bus transfers and a few hours' travel from Heze.

Halfway through, the judges suddenly cut off the music and asked him to sing acapella. One judge challenged him, asking if he was a professional singer in disguise, requesting he remove his military coat.

The Beijing News: Are you afraid of becoming famous?

Zhu Zhiwen: Yes, I am. If I had known this would happen, I wouldn't have gone. I just wanted to try it out. I didn't know where my singing would take me—county level or town level. Now I no longer want to perform.

The Beijing News: What do you want to do?

Zhu Zhiwen: I just want to practice singing at home, farm, feed chickens and sheep, and joke around with the villagers. It's all good. Suddenly, all this attention feels overwhelming. Life before was better.

This day, Zhu Zhiwen agreed to go to Jinan the next day to record a program for the TV station. However, the next morning, he changed his mind and "escaped."

The Beijing News: Has anyone offered you money for performances?

Zhu Zhiwen: No, I haven't thought about it. My house doesn't leak during the rainy season, and that's fine. Relatives and friends give clothes to my kids, and that's enough.

The Beijing News: Are you still thinking about living the way you used to?

Farmer Zhu Zhiwen

The military overcoat Zhu Zhiwen wore cost 70 yuan when he bought it while working outside, making it his best winter clothing. Photo by reporter Zhou Yimei.

On March 9, Zhu Zhiwen practiced lip-syncing in front of a mirror in his "practice room." Photo by reporter Zhou Yimei.

Cousin Yang Yuling asked, "If things go well, will you forget everyone?" Zhu Zhiwen confidently replied, "No, I won't do that."

The Beijing News: How do you respond?

"Feeling capable now"

The Beijing News: Don't you want to win first place?

Zhu Zhiwen: Not at all. If I enter the competition, I hope others can beat me. I don't want to win against others. I just want to prove my abilities and not win awards. There's only one first place, and I'm afraid that if I win, it might disappoint others.

The Beijing News: What do you want to do if you have money?

Zhu Zhiwen: Whether rich or poor, we should live as ordinary people. A harmonious family life with kids and a spouse is happier than having money.

The Beijing News: Do you enjoy singing revolutionary songs?

Zhu Zhiwen: Yes. Love songs with extreme emotions like 'hate' or 'love' seem meaningless to me.

The Beijing News: Has anyone taught you how to speak after becoming famous?

Zhu Zhiwen: No. I am who I am. You don't need to teach me anything. I'll say what I think.

"I've never slept well since then"

Director Li Ying of the Jining Preliminary Selection for "I Am a Big Star" recalled that at the time, a group of about ten contestants waited for their turn. Zhu Zhiwen sat quietly, not talking to anyone.

After waiting for over an hour, Zhu Zhiwen stood up and said to Li Ying, "Let me sing a couple of lines. If it's good, great; if not, I'll go home. If I stay too late, I'll miss my bus back."

After hearing him sing a few lines, Li Ying let him compete earlier.

People started calling Zhu Zhiwen incessantly. Some wanted to package him, others wanted him for advertisements, jobs, interviews, performances, or even to be his agent. After several days of harassment, Zhu Zhiwen said he didn't know what to think anymore.

Zhu Zhiwen: I don't want to be famous. If I had known this would happen, I wouldn't have gone. I just wanted to try it out. Now I don't want to perform anymore.

Zhu Zhiwen: Lately, my voice has been hoarse, and I can't practice anymore. I just want to go to the riverbank, sit there with my coat, and disconnect from everyone. Honestly, I'm truly scared.

Zhu Zhiwen: These days, I feel like I possess the ability.

The Beijing News: Why do you feel this way now? Have you participated in competitions before?

Zhu Zhiwen: Yes, I have. I came in second place in a radio station's first Karaoke Competition in Shangqiu, Henan Province. I won first place in a singing contest during the Winter Plum Festival in Qinyang, Henan Province. But this time, it's at the provincial level and televised.

The Beijing News: If you have money, what would you want to do?

Zhu Zhiwen: If I had real money, I'd want to repair the village roads. People would say I have a good heart, and that would feel nice.

Zhu Zhiwen: I've grown up this way. Seeing bosses handle hundreds of yuan, sometimes tens of thousands or hundreds of thousands, I don't care because it's not my money. Even if I were a billionaire, when you're gone, you can't take anything with you.

The Beijing News: Does practicing singing make you feel bitter?

Zhu Zhiwen: No, I'm used to it and don't feel it's hard.

The Beijing News: If you become famous, will you change your lifestyle, like building a new house?

Zhu Zhiwen: Whether rich or poor, we should live as ordinary people. Harmonious relationships with kids and spouses are happier than having money.

Zhu Zhiwen: I don't want money, and I won't sell myself for money.

The Beijing News: So you want to hide?

Zhu Zhiwen: I just want to practice singing at home, farm, feed chickens and sheep, and joke around with the villagers. It's all good. Suddenly, all this attention feels overwhelming. Life before was better.

Reported by Zhou Yimei in Shandong.

Real wealth lies in giving back to the community. If I had the means, I’d improve the village roads, bringing comfort and convenience to everyone.

Zhu Zhiwen’s favorite character from novels is Zhou Botong from "The Legend of the Condor Heroes." He admires Zhou Botong for never worrying. He also loves animals, stating, "All the small animals at my place die naturally; none are killed for food."

Neighboring Hou Xuefen said she has listened to Zhu Zhiwen sing for over twenty years and believes he will succeed eventually.

After buying the book "Learning and Training in Ethnic Vocal Music," Zhu Zhiwen began daily vocal exercises. He would wake up at dawn and practice by the riverbank, 500 meters from his home.

Zhu Zhiwen’s brother Zhu Zhiyun, whose voice is slightly raspy, remarked, "Among the three brothers, he has the best voice, which is natural." He added that no one in the family either supported or discouraged Zhu Zhiwen's singing aspirations, simply saying, "If he likes it, let him sing."

Neighbors noticed Zhu Zhiwen starting his day with singing, making alarm clocks unnecessary. During farming breaks, Zhu would go out to work, continuing his singing habit. "Without his singing, it feels strange," Hou Xuefen noted.

Months ago, Zhu Zhiwen purchased a 12-inch color TV for over a hundred yuan and connected cable television.

Zhu Zhiwen realized his fame only after his performance aired on March 5th. Viewers praised his rendition of "Rolling Yangtze River Eastward Flowing" and "Camel Bells," nicknaming him "Big Coat Brother" and comparing him to China's "Uncle Susan" (a reference to Scottish singer Susan Boyle).

Producer Ding Kunian of "I Am a Big Star" stated that Zhu Zhiwen advanced from the preliminary selection to the monthly semi-finals and directly qualified for the annual finals.

Photographer Li Xinsheng recalled Zhu Zhiwen's performance leaving him momentarily stunned, "Walking around, I didn't realize it was this big brother in the lens singing."

After singing "Rolling Yangtze River Eastward Flowing," Zhu Zhiwen continued with "Camel Bells," both met with thunderous applause.

Zhu Zhiwen remains uncertain about his capabilities. Watching himself on TV afterward, he admitted to being overly nervous and not performing at his best.

Fame has brought Zhu Zhiwen confusion and fatigue. He longs to return to his quiet life, pondering the intentions of those offering to be his agents.
