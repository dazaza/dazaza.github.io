Introduction to the plot of "Boys of Fire":

"Boys of Fire," the first story film shot using 3D stereoscopic technology, attempts for the first time to bring Chinese audiences an "extraordinary theater-style audio-visual entertainment experience" through the use of "3D stereoscopic technology." This marks the synchronization of domestic films with world cinema. With special shooting techniques and narrative styles, it tells the humorous and determined entrepreneurial experiences of the post-80s generation, interwoven with the sweetness of love, making vitality and liveliness the first theme throughout the film. The collaboration of three major industry giants has injected a noble lineage into the "Boys of Fire," born in a golden cradle, while the care of an elite team ensures its growth.

It is the first musical epic to depict young people's entrepreneurship and will feature about 20 minutes of 3D footage. According to music director Xiao Ke, songs and dances account for one-third of the film’s content and serve as the finishing touch, with the actors' youthful energy being the focus of the movie.

Inspiration for "Boys of Fire":

The poster for "Boys of Fire" was unveiled at the premiere where all the main creators were present, showing great importance and vigorous promotion for the film. The cast expressed that "Boys of Fire" holds significant meaning for them, and director Lin Huquan described this as his debut work entering the mainland market, saying, "The inspiration for this movie comes from the early American 3D music documentary 'Woodstock Festival.' Even if we don't talk about China's first 3D stereoscopic or musical elements today, just talking about our joint efforts in doing this thing makes it extraordinarily meaningful."

The Story Behind the Name "Boys of Fire":

Regarding why the film is named "Boys of Fire," the production team revealed at the premiere that since filming began, the crew has referred to the project as "Lohas Tribe," symbolizing "a healthy and sustainable lifestyle." It aims to convey an optimistic and healthy attitude to the audience beyond being a youth musical. After understanding the meaning of the title, the contestants from "Super Boy" expressed their intention to become "Lohas Boys." Specifically, they said, "We now have more environmental awareness than before, and our mindset towards life has become healthier. We hope that through the movie 'Boys of Fire,' we can give confidence to all disheartened people, making them more optimistic and positive, experiencing growth like the 'Boys of Fire.'"
