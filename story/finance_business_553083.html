The Winter of Beijing Real Estate

2011 has been an unusual year for the real estate market. With the continuous upgrading of real estate market regulation, the housing markets in major cities across the country have gradually fallen into a dilemma of declining both in volume and price. For Beijing's real estate market, it can be said to be making things worse that the "Golden September and Silver October" failed to arrive as expected, instead bringing a decline in both volume and price known as the "Copper September and Iron October." Moreover, this trend is likely to worsen. As more and more developers face increasingly tight funds, this will inevitably intensify the trend of "price-cutting promotions." According to an online survey, most citizens believe that Beijing's future real estate market will continue to fall, with suburban areas being the hardest hit, especially Daxing and Tongzhou where price drops are expected to reach new highs. Currently, some newly opened residential complexes in Beijing have even offered ultra-low opening prices of 10,000 yuan per square meter. It seems that the winter for Beijing's real estate has just begun.

Beijing's Sea Cucumber Market Booms, Prices Soar

In stark contrast to the real estate market, Beijing's sea cucumber market (www.haishen010.com) has shown strong growth throughout the year. In the just-concluded month of October, various sea cucumber brands performed exceptionally well in the Beijing market, with sales at sea cucumber specialty stores described as "booming." Furthermore, based on the current market performance, the peak sales season for Beijing's sea cucumber market has just started. In the coming months, the sea cucumber sales market will see a development trend of rising both in volume and price.

As sales continue to grow, the price of sea cucumbers in Beijing also shows a strong upward trend. This is partly due to the growing demand in the market, but more importantly, because of the rapid increase in sea cucumber production area prices. According to Mr. Huang, the director of the Beijing Direct Sales Center of Gongpin Sea Cucumbers, "It is currently the peak season for sea cucumber harvesting. From the current price trends in the production areas, the acquisition price of fresh sea cucumbers this autumn has significantly increased compared to the spring in all regions. With the continuous rise in the acquisition price of fresh sea cucumbers, the cost of sea cucumbers keeps increasing. Therefore, the retail price of sea cucumbers nationwide will definitely see a significant increase this winter."

On one hand, Beijing's sea cucumber market is experiencing booming sales with continuously growing demand; on the other hand, the price in production areas keeps climbing. Thus, the trend of rising prices for Beijing sea cucumbers this winter is very evident. Currently, there hasn't been much movement in the prices of various brands mainly because the products being sold now are from inventory. Sea cucumbers harvested in the autumn generally enter the market around December.

Price Advantage of Well-known Sea Cucumber Brands' Direct Sales

According to investigations by reporters, Beijing's well-known sea cucumber brand — Gongpin Sea Cucumbers (www.haishen360.com) — through channel innovation and brand operations, has accumulated good word-of-mouth among consumers. Its large-scale e-commerce platform — Gongpin Sea Cucumber Online Shopping Mall — under the increasingly improved environment of information networks, logistics distribution, online payments, and merchant integrity, has become the first professional-level sea cucumber online shopping platform domestically, achieving perfect complementarity with direct sales centers in various locations. The fully direct sales model ensures that Gongpin Sea Cucumbers maintains extremely strong price competitiveness.

Regarding recent rumors about fluctuations in sea cucumber prices, the person in charge of Gongpin Sea Cucumbers Beijing Direct Sales Center expressed his views: "Firstly, most of our fresh sea cucumbers come from our own bases, so relatively speaking, the acquisition cost of sea cucumbers won't increase too much; Secondly, the company is continuously optimizing its supply chain, reducing unnecessary channel costs to offset the increase in sea cucumber costs; Thirdly, we also hope to increase sales through thin-profit high-turnover and manufacturer direct sales models, compensating for the reduction in unit profit through increased sales volume. Ultimately, we hope that both new and old customers can consistently buy the best products at the most affordable prices."
