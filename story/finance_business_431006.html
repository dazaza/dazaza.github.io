Nursing measures after the treatment of port-wine stain

by hdudkjsk on 2011-05-14 13:28:37

The appearance of a port-wine stain makes parents very anxious. Port-wine stains usually appear when an infant is born and are a benign congenital vascular malformation. As the baby grows, the color of the port-wine stain changes from pink to dark red, then to purplish red, gradually deepening. Port-wine stains can severely affect a child's mental health. Experts from the Laser Cosmetology Department of Huaxia Hospital in Jinan emphasize that treatment for port-wine stains should be carried out as early as possible.

Director Shen of the Laser Cosmetology Center at Huaxia Hospital in Jinan points out that laser treatment for port-wine stains uses the selective photothermal effect of light on objects of different colors. Lasers or intense pulsed light within a certain wavelength range selectively act on hemoglobin in blood vessels, heating it and destroying the dilated blood vessels, while other skin tissues remain undamaged, thus clearing the dilated blood vessels. Post-treatment care is also quite important. (To learn about the cost of laser treatment for port-wine stains, you can click on online expert consultation.)

1. Keep the treated area of the port-wine stain clean to avoid infection and friction.

2. Any scabs on the treated area should fall off naturally; do not peel them off by hand, otherwise, there may be severe pigmentation and scarring.

3. After the scab falls off, there may be temporary pigmentation locally. To prevent or reduce this situation, sunscreen and spot-removal products can be used appropriately.

4. After treatment for port-wine stains, there may be a slight feeling of heat and mild redness in the treated area. This is a normal reaction. If necessary, local cold compression for 20-30 minutes can help alleviate or eliminate the red heat phenomenon.
