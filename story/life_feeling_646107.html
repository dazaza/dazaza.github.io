Seventy years of love, a lifetime of regret

That year, he was 7 years old and she was 6.

They were classmates and neighbors. Every day they went to school together with their bags, held hands going home together, and shared an umbrella when it rained. She was very pretty, and all the boys liked to be with her. But their way of expressing affection was always strange; they pinched her hand and pulled her hair, making her cry in pain. He would suddenly appear beside her and tell all the boys: "She's my sister, don't bully her!"

Being a hero comes at a cost, he got beaten up by a group of boys because of her, but he achieved what he wanted - they no longer bullied her. As time passed, they reached middle school. He had grown into a tall and handsome young man, while she seemed plain. Beautiful girls all hoped to replace her by his side, but none could. For so many years, every morning he rode his bike to take her to school. They agreed that in the future, they would attend the same school for higher education so that he could continue to take care of her.

He was excellent and had many admirers. She often had to act as a courier, as those girls always asked her to deliver their love letters to him. He never read them, just threw them aside indifferently! She carefully asked him: "Have you read those letters?" "Yeah," he answered absentmindedly. "Do you like any of those girls?" "No." The more times she delivered the letters, the more times she asked, until one day he became furious with her: "Don't show me these letters anymore! Don't meddle so much!" She said委屈地to him: "You give me no sense of security!" Then she angrily ran off.

After graduating from high school, she didn't go to the same university as him, but instead parted ways far apart. That year, he was 19, and she was 18. Occasionally during summer vacation, when they met again in the same courtyard, he would ask: "Are things okay at school? Is anyone bullying you?" She replied lightly: "It's fine, I'm not so easy to bully!"

Looking at her back, a kind of indescribable pain slowly gnawed at his heart like a little rat. Her mother said that she stayed in that city, with a boyfriend who loved her dearly. He smiled and blessed her, yet felt lonely inside.

The year he graduated from university, when he came back, there was a girl by his side. That was his girlfriend, too cute to describe, but he found some habits similar to hers on this girl. However, that year, she happened to come back alone. When they met on the street, she looked at the girl clinging to his arm and laughed: "Finally, someone has taken your spot!" He awkwardly introduced her to the girl as his sister.

He asked in a brotherly tone: "Where is he? Why didn't he come back with you?" "Him?" She gave a cold laugh, "We broke up long ago! He, like you, had too many girls chasing after him, and I felt no sense of security!" She twirled her hair and said to the girl by his side: "But, my brother is really a good man. Once he loves someone, he will love them wholeheartedly forever!" The girl shyly smiled and said: "He has too many admirers. In the past, we were good friends, many girls saw our good relationship and asked me to help them send letters to him, later I wrote one myself..." Hearing this, her face suddenly turned pale, and she gloomily told them: "Sorry, I feel a bit unwell, I need to go rest!" He watched her leave in a state of disarray, and suddenly realized something.

Back at home, while his girlfriend was helping his mom cook, he found the pile of sealed love letters in the corner of the bookshelf. He searched through them one by one, always feeling that there must be one written by her. Finally, in the last stack, he found the light blue envelope with her elegant handwriting. He sat on the ground in frustration. "In fact, I have always hoped that I am not your sister. Although you have always loved me in the way you love your sister, only I know that I hope to sit on the back of your bike forever, hope to always hear you say that you will protect me, hope that every promise you make to me can be fulfilled. I hope you can see this letter, and your attitude towards me, and my attitude towards you, will be decided by this letter. If you don't like me, naturally I won't cling to you, I will quietly step away, however far, I will go..."

Tears fell on the paper, yet still couldn't dispel the pain of loving her but hurting her. The next day, he wanted to find her, holding the letter. But when he arrived at her doorstep, he hesitated.

Could he betray the girlfriend who followed him back to his hometown? She had been taking care of him since school started, her love for him, as she put it, was such that without him, she would die! He couldn't betray the woman who was completely devoted to him. Those few nights, facing his girlfriend, he had no enthusiasm. He thought a lot, and the next day, he hugged his girlfriend and said: "I'm sorry..." But when he went to her house again, her mother told him that she had already left, and was assigned to work in another city, even farther away. A few months later, he simply packed his luggage and went to the city where she was. When he appeared in front of her, she was stunned.

He smiled and hugged her tightly: "I've come to take you home!" "But..." She raised her right hand, which wore an engagement ring: "I'm getting married!" He was surprised looking at her, how could it be so fast? In just a few months, she was about to get married! "Do you know? The woman I have always loved most is you, and that letter was just discovered by me..." "Stop talking!" She sighed deeply, "You should be responsible for her, you can't betray others just because of a letter... Like me, I also need to repay him, so I choose to marry him!" She spoke so decisively, he listened with a broken heart.

That year, he was 26, and she was 25.

She got married and stayed in her husband's city; he also got married, his wife was a simple and virtuous woman. His parents were sick and needed care, and his wife was even more enthusiastic than him.

When she returned again, although her husband was by her side, she still didn't dare to look straight at him. So, they often were, she chatting with his wife, while he got along well with her husband. Their topics were still the embarrassing stories from their childhood, but that feeling lacked much sweetness and nostalgia. They had to consider the two people deeply in love with them by their sides. They sighed, moved by each other’s companions.

That year, he was 32, and she was 31.

Later, every year she would return with her husband for the New Year celebrations, and every year they would have a reunion dinner together. His child called her aunt, her child called him uncle. The feelings between them seemed to have truly returned to the initial brother-sister relationship.

When their children were about to enter university, he quickly called her far away: "Sister, are there any good universities over there? I want my child to take the exam there. This child is too disobedient, always upsetting his mother, I'll send him there to study, and you can help me supervise him!" She laughed in the phone: "Is that so? I also want my child to take the exam there! Our child is also disobedient, doesn't listen to his father's discipline, this girl says she only listens to her uncle..." She paused and said: "Why not let them both take the exam for the same school? This way they can take care of each other, and when we visit them, we can discipline both children together." His hand holding the phone trembled, pulling his heart back to many years ago.

Under the arrangement of their parents, the children entered the same university. He told his son: "You must take good care of your sister, don't let anyone bully her!" She told her daughter: "Don't upset your brother, don't keep causing trouble for him."

Perhaps there was already a premonition, when he and she received calls from their son and daughter saying they wanted to get married, they both laughed. At the children's wedding, he sat beside her, looking at each other's gray temples, he gently said: "We finally became one family!" She nodded, her face showing a tired smile: "Just waited too long, just in the end, it's the continuation of our lives that stays together."

That year, he was 67, and she was 66.

Later, he was diagnosed with cancer. He lost hope, rejected everyone, refused medication and treatment, his emotions completely out of control, yelling at his wife, son, and daughter-in-law whenever he saw them. His wife stood outside the hospital room door, sighing painfully, and said to her son: "Call your aunt, no, your mother-in-law! Only she can cure your dad's problem!" When she knocked on his hospital room door, she only said one sentence: "If you still want to see me, listen to the doctor, take the medicine and undergo chemotherapy; if not, then I'll leave immediately, and I won't care whether you live or die!" He looked at her and burst into tears.

...

She stood in front of his grave, her eyes already devoid of tears.

The cemetery was desolate and empty, a gust of wind brushed her silver hair, as if his response, or perhaps his crying.

Turns out, love left in the heart will forever become regret.

That year, he was 77, and she was 76.
