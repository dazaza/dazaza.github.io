Eight Fashionable Ways to Tie Winter Scarves:

Method One: Tongue Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck with the right side on top.

Step 2: Pass the right end through the middle gap.

Step 3: Pass it back through the gap, leaving part overlapping. If the scarf is long, extend the "tongue" part. This method suits lively and cute individuals.

Method Two: French Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck and tie a knot, leaving some space.

Step 2: Pass the right side over the left and through the gap.

Step 3: Pull the scarf through the gap. This knot gives a heavy feel, suitable for tall individuals.

Method Three: Asymmetric Knot

Step 1: Cross-wrap around the neck with the left side on top. Pass the right side through the gap following the arrow.

Step 2: Pull the scarf through the gap. This knot gives a rebellious feel, perfect for those who find it cool.

Method Four: Balanced Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck and cross-tie in the front and back.

Step 2: Pass the front part around from the back.

Step 3: Follow the arrow direction to pass through the gap.

Method Five: Bow Tie Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck with the right side on top. Pass the back part once around the left following the arrow.

Step 2: After wrapping, pass the scarf through the gap.

Step 3: Pull the scarf through the gap. Similar to tying a tie but may appear bulkier; use thinner silk scarves.

Method Six: Small Bow Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck once and tie a knot.

Step 2: Adjust the knot to the front-back direction and tie another knot. Easy to tie, appearing generous yet not overly feminine, suitable for stylish girls.

Method Seven: Large Bow Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck and tie a knot with the left on top and the right below.

Step 2: Fold the right side over itself.

Step 3: Place the folded part under the left and wrap the left around the other segment following the arrow.

Step 4: Tighten the knot. This method can also be used for silk scarves.

Method Eight: Neat Knot

Step 1: Wrap around the neck once.

Step 2: Cross-tie the two ends. This is the most basic method, very simple and suitable for busy mornings before heading out.

Various Ways to Tie Scarves:

Long Silk Scarf Tying Method One:

1. Fold the long silk scarf into a 2cm-wide strip and wrap it around the neck, tying a slipknot.

2. Twist the two sides together into a spiral.

3. Continue twisting until the bottom.

4. Use your fingers to wind the twisted strip counterclockwise around the knot.

5. Continue winding until the top, leaving about 5cm length below the flower, then pin the flower to the knot and adjust the length below.

Silk Scarf Tying Method Two:

1. Fold the silk scarf diagonally four times into a strip.

2. Tie a red scarf knot.

3. Adjust the scarf into a tie shape.

Silk Scarf Tying Method Three:

1. Fold the silk scarf lengthwise into a strip.

2. Tie a knot at one end.

3. Pass the untied end through the knot.

Silk Scarf Tying Method Four:

1. Fold the silk scarf lengthwise and loosely tie a knot about 10cm from one end.

2. Fold the other end into a 3cm accordion pleat.

3. Pass the accordion pleat through the hole and tighten the knot.

Silk Scarf Tying Method Five, Six, Seven, Eight:

1. Fold the silk scarf diagonally.

2. Cross-tie at the back of the neck.

3. Tie the other end at the back.

Silk Scarf Tying Method Nine:

1. Fold the silk scarf lengthwise tightly and twist it.

2. Wear it around the neck, fold the ends, and pass them through the loop, then adjust the shape.
