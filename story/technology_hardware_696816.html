The temple fair matchmaking event will see the participation of 80,000 people, 70% of whom are seniors attending on behalf of their children.

by yanu394ixue on 2012-03-08 10:54:40

Yesterday, at the matchmaking fair of the Beijing International Sculpture Park Temple Fair, a man was using his mobile phone to take pictures of the information on the matrimonial wall.

[Beijing International Sculpture Park]

According to reports, yesterday's temperature began to drop, but it did not dampen the enthusiasm of single men and women for attending the temple fair's matchmaking gathering. The most popular place in the Beijing International Sculpture Park was the matchmaking fair. According to statistics from the organizers of the matchmaking fair, by yesterday, over 50,000 people had come to the temple fair for matchmaking during the event period, and it is expected that by the end of the temple fair, the number of people participating in matchmaking will reach 80,000.

Photos of singles covered the matrimonial wall

The matchmaking fair was located in the square near the north gate of the Sculpture Park. There was a matrimonial wall here, where photos and personal information of single men and women were posted. Singles would bring a notebook and pen, and like visiting a temple fair, they would stroll from the north end of the matrimonial wall to the south end, carefully checking each one to find someone who met their criteria, and some even took pictures with their phones.

"With my conditions, I probably won't find anyone easily. It's too hard to find a partner in the city," said Liu Qin (a pseudonym), aged 38. He is only 1.6 meters tall, has a low income, although he has a Beijing hukou (household registration), he doesn't own a house. He wants to find a girl in her early twenties, but fears she might look down on him. On Liu Qin's little notebook, there were many telephone numbers written down, all of which interested him, and he planned to contact them later.

More young people attended this year's matchmaking fair

"Do you want to find a girl with certain conditions?" Seeing a suitable young man taking notes, an old woman struck up a conversation. She hoped to find a match for her daughter. In front of the matrimonial wall, there were two rows of elderly people "setting up stalls." Each of them sat on a small stool, placing the information of singles on the ground, and if someone was interested, they would chat.

There was also an old man holding several small notebooks, recording the telephone numbers of those anxious to find partners for their children, and writing down the requirements for finding a partner, "If there is a match, I'll let you know." Hearing about this good opportunity, many elders gathered around to register.

"Parents worried about their children make up 70% of the participants in this temple fair's matchmaking event," according to the staff of the China Social Work Association's Marriage and Family Committee, the organizer of the matchmaking fair. This year's Spring Festival temple fair has attracted over 50,000 people for matchmaking, more than in previous years. Last year's temple fair matchmaking was almost exclusively attended by older people, but this year, there are more young people.

Hao Pengfei, the head of the organizing committee, stated that after the popularity of TV matchmaking shows in recent years, it has sparked the enthusiasm of young people for matchmaking, changing the idea that matchmaking is embarrassing.
