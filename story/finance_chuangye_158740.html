Check out some of my previously published planning cases - a few heartfelt words from an old planner.

by longersing on 2009-06-10 21:11:39

Today, I met an old "colleague" online. He knows about my development in Guilin. I told him that I am still in a struggling phase, and some things are very contradictory, not as affluent as he and my classmates imagined after graduation. He asked me how many companies I have worked for, how many cases I have done, and introduced some promising projects for him to do. Finally, he added that it would be best if he didn't have to spend any money and could make a sure profit.

This reminded me of an old saying: "There is a gain for every loss, and if you want to succeed, you must pay first." At this point, I can only say sorry, there is no such thing as a sure-profit project without spending money. But I didn't say it so directly. After all, some newly graduated college students cannot handle the pressure, let alone my colleague who was just a "colleague" playing games with me years ago. When I went to Beijing, he spent four more years reviewing his books at school - impressive, admirable. A person who has received 19 years of textbook education.

Speaking of projects - it's not to say that there are no cost-free projects. In fact, working for others is a cost-free project; it's just a matter of perspective. Some people like to be born bosses, while others prefer to be employees. Also, the words "sure-profit" - I really can't achieve that. Collectors may be affected by collection fever, stock enthusiasts may be excited by stock market fluctuations (the reason why nine out of ten people lose money when trading stocks), there is no such thing as sure-profit. But we can try to minimize losses.

Project planning is the premise of doing a project. Every project has its original reason for existence. For example, event planning focuses on popularity. Based on these reasons, we can plan to ensure the project proceeds normally and healthily. We can enjoy the fun while making some money, and that's what planning is. If you want more fun in life, you need to plan your life. If you want to win a woman's heart, you need to plan emotionally (you can refer to one of my previous writings, "Men Love Shoes Like They Love Women - Joke Story Planning"). If you want to buy a house, you need to make a savings or investment plan for the next few years under the condition of insufficient funds. Planning is not a very professional term; it is just the details of our daily lives (planning requires a good mood).

I organized some planning cases I have used before, and reviewed what I've experienced over the years. It turns out: people really live in planning. My planning involves too many areas, including gourmet food, travel websites, baby products, local specialties, wedding planning, e-commerce department establishment planning, Internet cafe event planning, etc. Almost covering all aspects of life. But in the end, I returned to myself - wedding planning. I prefer romantic and sentimental planning, just like my 2010 wedding planning and wedding website planning.

Hope for a good mood every day. Thank you for your attention.
