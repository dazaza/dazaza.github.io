Everyone hopes to gain more and lose less, hoping to become a constant victor. However, without the mental preparation for potential losses and solely focusing on making big money, it's difficult to ensure that one won't end up losing. The correct attitude is to have both the hope of profit and the readiness for loss. After purchasing gold, analyze the trend of the market. If the market trend is favorable, patiently wait and aim to extend profits. However, when the market trend turns unfavorable, especially when you feel the trend is not right, don't overly consider gains and losses. Accept small losses and close your position. Overly calculating gains and feeling dissatisfied with small losses leads to stubborn waiting, which sometimes results in worsening situations.

Less experienced investors often think of closing their positions immediately after seeing profits after buying or selling certain goods. While closing at a profit seems easy, capturing the right timing for profit is a skill. Experienced investors determine the timing of closing based on their judgment of price trends. If they believe the market will continue to develop favorably, they will patiently wait, allowing prices to move further in their favor, thus extending profits. Closing at small profits doesn't equate to securing good returns; in the end, it might result in fewer gains and more losses.

Gold trading differs from stock trading. People often focus solely on prices while ignoring the upward and downward trends of gold prices. When gold prices rise, they become increasingly expensive, making people hesitant to buy as prices increase. Conversely, when gold prices fall, they seem cheaper, tempting people to buy as prices decrease. This often leads to forgetting the adage of following market trends, resulting in counter-trend trading mistakes. In an upward trend of gold prices, there is only one wrong buying point – when prices reach their peak, akin to reaching the ceiling where no further ascent is possible. Apart from this point, any other buying point is correct. Similarly, in a downward trend of gold prices, there is only one wrong selling point – when prices have already hit rock bottom, akin to reaching the floor where no further decline is possible. Apart from this point, any other selling point is correct.
