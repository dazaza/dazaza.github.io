February 16th market review. (Futures Forum. First Futures Network. www.dyqhw.com. Stock Index Futures, Futures Account Opening.) The morning session opened lower and then accelerated upward, approaching the year's new high. There was significant resistance near the six-month moving average line, followed by a retreat in stock index volatility. The Shanghai Composite Index broke through the support of the 5-day line and briefly probed the 10-day line before stabilizing. Near the close, under the pull of banking stocks, the decline narrowed, holding onto the 5-day line. Heavyweight stocks were relatively sluggish, with the short side having an advantage. Trading volumes for both markets decreased compared to yesterday.

By the close, the Shanghai Composite Index reported at 2356.86 points, down 9.84 points, a drop of 0.42%, with a trading volume of 83.8 billion yuan; the Shenzhen Component Index reported at 9622.83 points, down 50.61 points, a fall of 0.52%, with a trading volume of 81.5 billion yuan.

On the board, sectors such as power, cloud computing, and educational media led the gains, while insurance, coal, and cement among the majority fell. The ST sector ended its strong uptrend and started to fall.

Thursday saw narrow fluctuations in the main board, with reduced trading volumes. Major players operated cautiously ahead of the six-month moving average line. Currently, the stock index is in the collision period between short-term and long-term trends. Even if it breaks through the six-month line, it will immediately encounter the more resistant 30-week line, so don't expect too rapid development of the market. After the bottom stocks have experienced widespread catch-up growth, the market needs new bullish forces to gather popularity. Otherwise, relying solely on catch-up growth of theme stocks will find it difficult to drive the stock index to rise strongly. Short-term individual stock opportunities are still worth noting. From the analysis of the stock index shape, since the rebound from 2132 points began, the index has shown a wedge-shaped oscillation within the range of two upper and lower trend lines. The future space is getting smaller and smaller, and the stock index still faces directional choices. Since the index is now at the end of the wedge channel, it is recommended to be cautious in the short term. For stocks with large previous increases but no performance support, consider reducing positions when they reach high levels.

The main board has chosen to break upwards, and the catch-up growth of individual stocks has also begun to unfold slowly. 2400 points could become the last push of this wave of rebound. Friday is the last trading day of this month for the stock index futures, which deserves special attention tomorrow.

From a technical analysis perspective, the MACD strength on the daily K-line continues to weaken, but the 5-day moving average line hasn't been breached. As long as the 5-day moving average line is firmly held by the bulls, the upward trend can continue to challenge the 120-day moving average line, which will only increase the demand for short-term adjustments. Once the adjustment begins, it will be more intense. Moreover, this upswing is nearing its end, making it difficult to keep pace with the market rhythm.

From the perspective of market hotspots, Huaxun Finance believes that the catch-up rally continues. Cold sectors such as power, furniture, water supply, and gas supply make up for the gains, showing that the market's desire to chase highs is not strong. However, this benefits the activation of market sentiment, with obvious profit-making effects. It is suggested that investors focus on controlling their positions recently, avoid chasing highs, wait for the market to make directional choices, and pay attention to some individual stocks in the catch-up concept sector.

On Thursday, the main board adjusted with vibrations, rebounding slightly near the close due to the pull of hot sectors such as power and textile clothing. Throughout the day, the board remained active. The index faces short-term pressure from the six-month line and may experience vibrations, but the rebound trend remains unchanged. The recovery of over-sold stocks is expected to continue. It is recommended to continue paying attention to some individual stocks below the 60-day moving average line. (Futures Forum.
