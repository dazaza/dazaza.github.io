When it comes to the World Cup, Mr. Fan is well-versed in the champions, runners-up, third-place finishers of each edition, as well as star players and coaches. Mr. Fan told reporters that he became a fan of football starting with the 1990 Italy World Cup. Since then, he has been passionately obsessed with football, even neglecting his family to chase matches. At this point, Mr. Fan pulled out a stack of photos from his pocket, all of which were taken with fans and celebrities in the football world. The reporter saw a photo taken with former Luneng coach Sanktari.

On June 29th, our newspaper reported (by reporter Chen Huaiyu and intern reporter Li Tao) that a plate of peanuts, a lottery ticket, a World Cup schedule, and two bags of soy milk accompany him every night. He turns off the lights, lowers the TV volume, sets the alarm clock, and doesn't miss a single match among the three nightly games. On the 28th, the reporter met Mr. Fan Jianhua, known as "Mr. Big Beard Fan," a die-hard football fan. When talking about the World Cup, Mr. Fan knows everything about the champions, runners-up, third-place finishers, star players, and coaches of each edition. Mr. Fan told reporters that he started liking football during the 1990 Italy World Cup. Since then, he has been crazily obsessed with football, even neglecting his family to follow the matches. Regarding peanuts, soy milk, and the alarm clock, Mr. Fan said it's for boosting immunity. "Watching three matches a night while still having to work, the body can't handle it. Peanuts and soy milk help boost immunity; the alarm clock is usually set for 2:30 am and 6:00 am. I get up at 2:30 to watch the game and at 6 to go to work."

When asked if his family would have any objections to his obsession with football, Mr. Fan chuckled awkwardly: "Of course my wife has objections and sometimes grumbles non-stop. But after getting used to it, I pretend not to hear, turn off the lights, lower the TV volume, and just watch my own TV." Mr. Fan also told reporters that his younger daughter lives with her grandparents and won't come home to fight over the TV. What he fears most is his older daughter coming home on weekends. When the older daughter returns, there will be no chance to watch TV pumas store. Fortunately, the older daughter has exams coming up and won't have time to return on weekends, so he will still be able to watch the games peacefully.

In the past, Mr. Fan traveled all over the country to watch football games and celebrate with fans, seeking the atmosphere and feeling to release his passion for football. Now, however, he prefers to quietly watch the games alone at home. "Friends invite me to watch the games at barbecue stalls or bars, but I always decline. In the past, I was crazy just to release my passion for football, missing some exciting moments. Now I'm more rational and have started appreciating football, finding it enjoyable. Besides, barbecue stalls are smoky and noisy, disturbing the surrounding residents. Watching TV at home allows me to rest a bit and still go to work the next day." Mr. Fan explained.

As the reporter was about to leave, Mr. Fan said that he would continue to love football, though not as wildly passionate as before. He plans to rationally enjoy watching football, treating it as an aesthetic experience."
