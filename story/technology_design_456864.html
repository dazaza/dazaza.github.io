The magnificent main gate, fully functional monitoring center, collection-rich geological museum, and thrilling scenic area栈道... A brand new Langshan Scenic Area unfolds before people's eyes. In mid-September, after an on-site inspection by the IUCN China Danxia World Heritage Evaluation Team experts, it was concluded that Langshan’s conservation and management is standard and orderly, reaching international standards.

Langshan is a national scenic area, national geological park, national natural heritage site, and a world natural heritage nomination site. Langshan is characterized by its dense dome-shaped Danxia clusters and peak forests, resembling galloping horses; its massive ravines, slot canyons, and natural bridges showcase unique Danxia karst landscapes; its vegetation exhibits prominent "ecological island effects" and habitat-specific phenomena, making it the most complete region for the succession of Danxia plant communities and representative of co-evolution between plants and animals. Its Danxia landscapes possess rare natural beauty and primitive attributes. Among the series of world heritage nomination sites, it represents the mature stage of peak cluster - peak forest landforms and serves as a model for major types and fundamental characteristics of Danxia landforms.

In the past three years, Xinning County has carried out comprehensive renovations on Langshan based on the United Nations' "Convention Concerning the Protection of the World Cultural and Natural Heritage," the "Operational Guidelines for the Implementation of the World Heritage Convention," and relevant national laws and regulations, effectively improving environmental quality and conservation management levels. More than 2,170 residential buildings have been renovated, 182 unsightly structures removed, and 32 polluting enterprises along the route relocated or shut down. Advertising signs, promotional boards, and shop nameplates along the roads have been standardized in design, style, and specifications. Xinning County has also invested 4.7 billion yuan to enhance the protective management facilities of the Langshan heritage nomination site, creating a new situation where "resource protection reaches a high level, service management takes a step forward, and tourism economy grows significantly."
