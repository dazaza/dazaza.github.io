Intel's first quarter net profit was 2.4 billion US dollars, increasing by 288% year-over-year

by tfseo8888 on 2010-04-15 22:03:53

Sina Technology News, April 14th early morning Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, Intel announced its financial report for the first quarter of the 2010 fiscal year today. The report shows that due to the strong revenue performance in the mobile chip business, Intel's net profit in the first quarter increased by 288% year-over-year, exceeding analysts' previous expectations.

In this fiscal quarter ended March 27, Intel's net profit was $2.4 billion, an increase of 288% compared to the same period last year and a 7% increase from the previous quarter; earnings per share were 43 cents, which was much better than the same period last year. In the first quarter of the 2009 fiscal year, Intel's net profit was $629 million, or 11 cents per share. Intel's operating profit in the first quarter was $3.4 billion, an increase of 433% compared to the same period last year, and an increase of 38% compared to the previous quarter. Intel's revenue in the first quarter was $10.3 billion, up 44% year-over-year, but down 3% from the previous quarter.

Intel's first-quarter results exceeded analysts' previous expectations. A Thomson Reuters survey showed that analysts had previously expected Intel's first-quarter earnings per share to be 38 cents and revenue to be $9.8 billion.

By region, Intel's revenue from the Americas in the first quarter was $1.906 billion, compared to $1.51 billion in the same period last year. Intel's revenue from Europe in the first quarter was $1.404 billion, compared to $1.273 billion in the same period last year. Intel's revenue from Asia-Pacific in the first quarter was $5.888 billion, compared to $3.647 billion in the same period last year. Intel's revenue from Japan in the first quarter was $1.101 billion, compared to $715 million in the same period last year.

By department, Intel's PC Client Group's net revenue in the first quarter was $7.674 billion, compared to $5.361 billion in the same period last year; operating profit was $3.143 billion, compared to $701 million in the same period last year. Intel's Data Center Group's net revenue in the first quarter was $1.871 billion, compared to $1.264 billion in the same period last year; operating profit was $835 million, compared to $266 million in the same period last year. Intel's other operating groups' net revenue in the first quarter was $369 million, compared to $149 million in the same period last year; operating loss was $21 million, compared to an operating loss of $153 million in the same period last year.

Intel expects second-quarter revenue for the 2010 fiscal year to be $10.2 billion, with a fluctuation of $400 million; gross margin to be 64%, with a fluctuation of two percentage points; operating expenses (R&D, general and administrative) to be approximately $3.1 billion; stock investment, interest and other income to be approximately zero; depreciation expense to be approximately $1.1 billion. A Thomson Reuters survey showed that analysts on average expected Intel's first-quarter revenue to be $9.8 billion. Intel expects the gross margin for the entire 2010 fiscal year to be 61%, with a fluctuation of two percentage points, previously expected to be 61%, with a fluctuation of three percentage points; operating expenses (R&D, general and administrative) to be approximately $12.4 billion, with a fluctuation of $100 million, previously expected to be $11.8 billion, with a fluctuation of $100 million; R&D expenses to be approximately $6.4 billion, previously expected to be $6.2 billion; capital expenditures to be $4.8 billion, with a fluctuation of $100 million, consistent with previous expectations; depreciation expense to be $4.4 billion, with a fluctuation of $100 million, unchanged from previous expectations; effective tax rate for the second, third, and fourth quarters to be approximately 31%.

On the day, Intel's stock price rose $0.23 in regular trading on NASDAQ, closing at $22.77, an increase of 1.02%. In subsequent after-hours trading until 16:37 Eastern Time on the 13th (4:37 AM Beijing Time on the 14th), Intel's stock price rose again by $0.88 to $23.65, an increase of 3.87%. In the past 52 weeks, Intel's highest price was $22.82 and the lowest price was $14.96. (Tang Feng)
