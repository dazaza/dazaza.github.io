Company Operating Chen Kun's WeChat Account "Unknown", Business Operations Suspected of Violations

by anonymous on 2013-09-18 22:08:27

Since WeChat launched its commercialization, various new features have continuously emerged. Recently, the WeChat public account of celebrity Chen Kun introduced a membership system. Fans who follow Chen Kun's WeChat public account can become members by paying 18 yuan per month and enjoy various value-added services and privileges. It is reported that within just one day of launching the paid membership system on his public account, Chen Kun made over 7 million yuan. This has caused the entertainment and media industries to exclaim that the WeChat public platform will become a good business for celebrities.

Chen Kun's WeChat public account made 7 million yuan in just one day after introducing the membership system.

Chen Kun's WeChat membership costs 100 yuan for half a year.

On the homepage of Chen Kun's WeChat public account, there are eight sections: Member Discussion Area, Walking Power, Portraits, News, Books, Music, Voice Customization, and Personal Center. In the personal center, users can pay to become members.

The fees are divided into four levels: 18 yuan per month, 50 yuan per quarter, 100 yuan every six months, and 168 yuan per year. After becoming a member, users can read Chen Kun’s books, listen to music recorded by Chen Kun, view his private photos, participate in member discussions, and customize voice pushes.

Industry insiders estimate that Chen Kun has more than 57 million fans on Sina Weibo, and it should not be difficult for his WeChat public account to have over a million fans. If 10% of these 1 million WeChat fans are willing to pay to become members, then Chen Kun's public account would make at least 16.8 million yuan per year.

In addition to the existing various privileges, with these hardcore fans willing to spend money on idols, Chen Kun's WeChat public account can also sell T-shirts, CDs, and other derivative products in the future, and develop more ways to monetize. From a longer-term perspective, celebrities can interact directly with fans through the WeChat public platform without relying on third-party agencies. With this new platform, celebrities can set up their own studios to maximize profits.

Tencent says the star membership system is still in trial operation

Regarding the introduction of a paid membership system on the WeChat public platform of stars, Tencent replied to Beijing Youth Daily reporters that the star membership system is still in the trial operation period and will gradually be opened to all enterprises and stars willing to try this cooperation model.

Tencent emphasized that any star public account wanting to activate a paid membership must be operated by a legally compliant company. The business model of the WeChat membership system is only aimed at enterprises; therefore, the WeChat Pay interface is temporarily not open to individuals.

It is worth noting that Tencent stated that WeChat provides free services to third-party operators and does not currently involve any revenue sharing. "Besides the star membership system, we also welcome other accounts to create more innovative business models on the WeChat platform."

Beijing Youth Daily reporters learned that actually, Chen Kun was not the first WeChat public account to try out the membership system. Last month, the WeChat public account 'Logic Thinking' announced the launch of 5,500 memberships, including 5,000 memberships charging 200 yuan per year and 500 hardcore memberships charging 1,200 yuan per year. It is said that these 5,500 membership slots were sold out within 6 hours, earning the operator 1.6 million yuan.

The company operating Chen Kun's account is "not well-known"

Beijing Youth Daily reporters noticed that Chen Kun's public account is serviced by a company called 'Shenzhen Bitsea Information Technology Co., Ltd.' This Shenzhen company's main page is not about online marketing but app development. Currently, the company has developed four apps: Super Star Face, Lovers Wallpaper, Table Guardian, and Short Video Selection, but none of them are very famous.

In addition to operating Chen Kun's WeChat public account, the WeChat public account of online writer Nanpai Sanshu is also operated by Bitsea. It has also launched a paid membership system, charging 5 yuan for a monthly card, 30 yuan for a half-year card, and 55 yuan for an annual card. Paid members can read Nanpai Sanshu's latest works and participate in member discussions.

Clearly, the membership service of Nanpai Sanshu's WeChat public account mainly focuses on paid reading, making the WeChat public account a competitor to traditional online literature websites. Yesterday, someone from an online literature website questioned the qualification of WeChat public accounts providing paid reading services, “These accounts are operated by third-party companies. Do they all have the qualifications to engage in internet publishing activities?”

Beijing Youth Daily reporters found that the business registration information of Shenzhen Bitsea Company shows that its business scope indeed does not include electronic publishing or internet publishing.

However, people familiar with corporate law pointed out that currently, the supervision of business scope by industrial and commercial departments and administrative departments is not as strict as it used to be. As long as it is not selling specially permitted, restricted, or prohibited goods, exceeding the business license scope usually will not result in penalties.

Expert opinion

Chen Kun's WeChat commercial operation suspected of being non-compliant

IT legal expert Zhao Zhanling believes that the commercial operation model of WeChat public accounts is a worthwhile attempt, but there are risks involved, especially preventing illegal fundraising by public accounts, which could harm user interests.

Zhao Zhanling pointed out that currently, Bitsea, the service provider for Chen Kun's public account, only has an ICP filing number, not an ICP license. "Any company that provides paid information services to internet users via the internet should have an ICP license."

This means that if Bitsea is not only the service provider for Chen Kun's public account but also the agency operator, it is strictly speaking non-compliant because although Bitsea is a legally registered company, it does not have permission to operate on the internet, "This non-compliance nature is not very serious, and local communication administrations do not regulate it strictly."

A senior person engaged in online marketing told Beijing Youth Daily reporters that actually, several years ago when Super Girl was most popular, there were third-party companies using online platforms to offer VIP membership services to fans. At that time, these companies mainly interacted with star fans on platforms like Baidu Tieba and YY Voice Platform, "Just the scale was small, and the attention was not as high as WeChat."

Zhao Zhanling believes that fan economy is not a unique product of the internet. Internet products like WeChat public accounts are just tools that provide a more advantageous platform for the commercial operation of fan economy, "If the operating amount reaches millions or even tens of millions, it is indeed necessary to implement stricter management."
