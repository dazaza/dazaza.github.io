What are the benefits of soaking feet in vinegar, and what are the benefits of washing feet with vinegar?

by xiaoxiao67 on 2012-03-06 23:18:37

Adding 100-150 grams of rice vinegar or aged vinegar to water for soaking feet will have a good effect. Soaking feet in vinegar can not only increase skin elasticity and make the skin smooth, but also remove rheumatism and improve symptoms of human body discomfort such as cold aversion.

Edible vinegar can reduce the alcohol concentration in the gastrointestinal tract and blood, and has a sobering effect.

Edible vinegar plays a good protective role on skin and hair.

Ancient Chinese medicine has records of using vinegar as medicine, believing that it has the effects of promoting hair growth, beauty, and lowering blood pressure.

Medical research has found that vinegar plays a good role in protecting the liver, and can promote the secretion of digestive fluids and enhance the appetite of patients with liver disease.

The amino acids contained in edible vinegar can prevent fat accumulation and prevent obesity. Although it cannot reduce weight quickly, adding a little vinegar to daily diet will gradually achieve the effect of weight loss.

Pay attention to quality when selecting edible vinegar.

Good quality vinegar is brown or dark brown (white vinegar is colorless and clear liquid), clear, without suspended matter and sediment; poor quality vinegar has deeper or lighter color, turbid, and has sediment after being stored for a period of time.

In addition to the sour smell, good quality vinegar also has a strong vinegar aroma, while poor quality vinegar has a lighter taste; compound vinegar generally has no aroma.

Dip a chopstick into a little vinegar and taste it. If the acidity is moderate, slightly sweet, non-irritating to the throat, and leaves a long-lasting vinegar aroma in the mouth, it is high-quality vinegar.

Organic acids and amino acids in edible vinegar are prone to deterioration at high temperatures or direct sunlight, so edible vinegar should be stored in glass containers in a cool place. After opening the cap, it should be consumed as soon as possible to avoid mixing with miscellaneous bacteria. The cap should be sealed after consumption.

There are many kinds of edible vinegar. Because of the different tastes and aromas, their characteristics should be effectively utilized when used. Rice vinegar, fragrant vinegar, aged vinegar, etc., can be used as condiments in cooking, while apple cider vinegar tends to be used as a seasoning juice and health drink.

Dietary taboos

People taking sulfonamides, alkaline drugs, antibiotics, diaphoretic Chinese herbal medicines, etc., should not eat vinegar. Vinegar acid can change the pH of the local environment in the human body, thereby making the above drugs ineffective and even causing side effects.

People with gastric ulcers and excessive stomach acid should not eat vinegar, otherwise it will aggravate the condition.

Eating mutton should not be accompanied by vinegar, otherwise it will weaken the therapeutic effects of both and produce substances harmful to the human body.

Drinking large amounts of vinegar can lead to calcium loss in the body.

After eating dumplings dipped in vinegar or dishes with more vinegar, you should rinse your mouth in time to protect your teeth.

Vinegar should be stored in a well-sealed glass container and kept in a cool place.
