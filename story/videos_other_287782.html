Get Rich 3 in Fashion Leader

by y1fy0p9o6 on 2009-12-01 16:15:34

How to Get Rich in Sword Network 3 Nowadays, there are several aspects: beating for materials, copying books to sell, living skills to make things, gathering materials to sell, and grabbing limited-time goods.

The money I've earned is basically sold on 5173, where the cheapest items are mine. Everyone who needs them can come and buy them anytime.

To beat for materials, it's crucial to understand what goods are in short supply or what goods are always in demand on the market at the moment.

If you can grasp this point, then immediately find a place and focus on brushing (grinding). For example, the price of silk and water wave silk in our server is good now, so I will go to Longmen and brush slowly. Generally, I can brush four sets of silk and two sets of water wave silk in an hour. During this time, if blue books or manuals drop, that's extra income.

You can also go to Phoenix Island in Yangzhou to brush for washing flowers and cinnabar, two materials that are always in demand. Note: when beating for materials, be sure to choose a location near a store so you can clear your bag anytime. At the same time, the overall value of all the things you can get should be high.

For example, although Heavenly Spider Silk is very expensive, it drops from spiders, but apart from the silk, there are no other valuable items. The total material value within the same time is obviously lower than beating humanoid monsters, so generally, it's not recommended unless you're specifically after this material.

I know everyone thinks beating for Heavenly Spider Silk is profitable... but actually, beating humanoid monsters is still better in part.

Next, regarding the issue of copying books, due to reputation and some manuals obtained from reading books, the current book market is very popular. Blue books have a cooldown of 6 hours, so if you want to get rich, don't waste this once-every-6-hours cooldown. Basically, as long as you copy and sell them, you won't lose out.

Similarly to beating for materials, you need to know the market - what's lacking, gather that, and make that. Recently speaking, flying fish pills and related herb gathering can be said to be the most profitable at the moment.

Here I would like to specifically explain the recipe for flying fish pills, which can be obtained not only by turning in the complete set of books from Wanhua but also drops from the common Tian Gong No.2 - and it's not account-bound!

From a super-profit perspective, naturally it's about grabbing limited-time goods from stores. The most famous one is Qian Deli, the mysterious merchant at Nanping Mountain:

The golden armor pieces that refresh only four times a day have become incredibly profitable. Of course, there are many people trying to grab them. If you think you can't grab them, consider going for osmanthus flowers instead. Although the total profit isn't high, since osmanthus flowers refresh up to 52 sets a day now, they're quite easy to grab. You can use a secondary account to grab a few sets for pocket money.

Finally, to become a true tycoon, it relies on monopoly and being a fashion leader. This requires predicting the market and then acquiring large quantities of materials at low prices to form a near-monopoly situation.

The super-profits from monopolies are very significant, but so are the risks. If judged incorrectly, you could lose a lot of gold. Each viewer should consider carefully. When the market begins to show signs of shortage, use a secondary account to release small amounts at high prices to inflate the prices, then start selling at relatively lower prices, but don't release too much inventory. Give consumers the illusion of "If I don't buy now, there will be none left soon, and prices will be even higher." In this way, earn large amounts of gold.
