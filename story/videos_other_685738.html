How beneficial is autumn cold water bathing?

by ciaifuke on 2012-03-01 16:35:59

Autumn can be considered a good season for exercise. Experts remind that in autumn, the weather is clear and refreshing, with temperatures of the air, water, and human body all being relatively close. The cold water has less stimulation on the human body. If one starts exercising with cold water baths at this time, it will help improve adaptability to environmental changes, enhance the function of the cardiovascular system, and make it easier to adapt to future climate changes. However, some precautions should be taken when bathing.

"Cold water baths have obvious health benefits; they can enhance the excitatory functions of the nervous system, making one feel refreshed and mentally clear after bathing." Dr. Zhang Chaoying from the Cardiovascular Department of the Second Affiliated Hospital of Jiaotong University said that if the human body is frequently stimulated by cold water, the muscles will tense and contract, and quickly resist, thereby enhancing the body's ability to adapt to temperature changes and strengthening immune function. This can prevent common colds and other upper respiratory infections, and enhance the body's resistance to diseases. If one starts taking cold water baths from autumn and continues consistently, it will greatly benefit the body’s adaptation to the cold in winter and prevent colds.

"The duration of each cold water bath should not be too long, and the water temperature should not be too low." Dr. Zhang explained that during a cold water bath, the water temperature should be around 15 degrees Celsius, and the bathing time should ideally be about 15 minutes. Additionally, before bathing, corresponding warm-up exercises should be done, such as rubbing the skin with your hands until it becomes warm. When bathing, you can first sprinkle water on the limbs to let the body adapt before starting the wash.

It should be noted that people with fever, those who are drunk, those with weak constitutions, and women during their menstrual period should avoid cold water baths. Cold water baths should not be taken on an empty stomach or right after meals. If symptoms such as uncontrollable shivering, goosebumps, or even dizziness occur during the cold water bath, one should stop immediately. Patients with coronary heart disease, acute hepatitis, severe hypertension, active rheumatic diseases, and high fevers should not take cold water baths. Furthermore, if insomnia, weight loss, physical discomfort, or decreased appetite occur during cold water bath training, one should stop the cold water bath.
