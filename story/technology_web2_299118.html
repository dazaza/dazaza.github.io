11-Year-Old Girl Suffers from Leukemia, Adoptive Mother Hopes for Her Parents to Appear and Donate Bone Marrow

by j0371zxy on 2009-12-08 15:57:00

Since October of last year, Zhang Yi has been staying at the Children's Hospital for treatment, with her father Ran Ersi constantly by her side to take care of her. By now, more than 10,000 yuan has already been spent on Zhang Yi's medical expenses, and the entire family's income relies solely on Zhang Qibai doing temporary farming work. Mrs. Ran stated that it has been over a month since the news about Zhang Yi searching for her biological parents was released, but there is still no word from them. When asked if she wanted to say a few words to her biological parents, Zhang Yi, in the manner of a child, said, "I hope they appear soon; I don't blame them."

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter contacted Zhang Yi's class teacher Mr. Huan Xiaosheng. Mr. Huan said that Zhang Yi has excellent grades, is very obedient, and has been the class monitor for many years. Upon hearing about Zhang Yi's leukemia diagnosis, he was deeply saddened and organized teachers and students to donate several thousand yuan to her.

Before getting sick, her mother did not reveal the truth.

With less than a month until her 12th birthday, Zhang Yi, an orphan who was abandoned two months after birth, was unfortunately diagnosed with chronic myeloid leukemia. Now lying in the children's hospital bed, she made an early birthday wish: to find her biological parents who abandoned her, so they can undergo bone marrow matching, giving her another chance at life. It is reported that Zhang Yi’s bone marrow transplant will cost approximately 400,000 yuan.

On November 21st, Ms. Li went to the Children's Hospital to visit a friend's child and immediately came to see Zhang Yi upon hearing about her situation. Afterwards, Ms. Li would visit the hospital whenever she had time. She also posted on Xiaoyu.com, hoping that more people would contribute their efforts to help Zhang Yi.

Abandoned 18 days after birth

By reporter Zheng Jun and intern Li Mengxiao

On January 6, 1998, on the pedestrian street near Pengshui County Committee, a basket was found emitting weak cries of a baby. A passerby, Mr. Zhang Qicheng, curiously approached and saw an abandoned female infant in the basket. There was a note inside indicating that the baby girl was born on December 19, 1997, at 11 PM, just 18 days old. Mr. Zhang quickly took the endangered infant home and named her Zhang Yi. Not long after, Mr. Zhang Qicheng and his wife Ms. Ran gave birth to a daughter, and Zhang Yi and her sister became the "twins" in the eyes of their parents, telling outsiders the same story.

Not blaming her biological parents

In September of this year, Zhang Yi was diagnosed with leukemia. The doctor said that the child needs a bone marrow transplant surgery to have any hope of recovery, preferably with a blood relation match. The Zhang couple were embarrassed and originally planned to tell Zhang Yi the truth when she grew up. However, for the sake of saving her, they revealed the secret, hoping to find Zhang Yi's biological parents for a match. Currently, Mr. Zhang Qicheng has accepted relevant fees, hoping to use social forces to find a match for Zhang Yi. After knowing her origins and condition, Zhang Yi did not become depressed but acted like usual, being affectionate in her mother's arms.

A citizen posts online to help find her biological parents.
