New car still not repaired after more than 70 repairs in one year

by yjankuib37 on 2011-11-03 13:59:14

Mr. Zhu provided the reporter with a thick stack of car maintenance service交接 slips, one of which showed an outrageous 15 items in need of repair. Photographed by Li Haiyong.

Recently, Mr. Zhu from Danyang city filed a complaint with our newspaper: Last year, he purchased a Zhengzhou Nissan Staker car from the Oriental Shengxuan Automobile Company in Changzhou. Since then, over more than a year, this car has had numerous issues and has been repaired over 70 times for various problems. Mr. Zhu bitterly joked that he visits the 4S shop to repair his car more frequently than the employees of this 4S shop come to work.

Intern Zhu Zhen and reporter Li Haiyong

Claiming it was a good car "you'll know once you use it," within four days of hitting the road, the car began to have problems.

The 4S shop only offers repairs and no replacement, and the manager who strongly recommended the car now avoids him.

Mr. Zhu lives in Danyang and due to some business needs, he often uses a car. Thus, on April 6 last year, he spent 79,000 yuan to buy a diamond silver Zhengzhou Nissan Staker car in Changzhou.

When purchasing this car, the sales department manager Yang of the company at the time confidently said, "Have you seen 'The Incredible Hulk'? This car is our 4S shop's 'Incredible Staker.' You won't regret buying it; it has all the classic configurations, and anyone who uses it will know!" However, on the fourth day after Mr. Zhu took delivery of the car, the airbag started making abnormal noises, leaving Mr. Zhu very upset, "A new car should be auspicious when used, but how could there be problems so soon?"

Subsequently, Mr. Zhu negotiated with the 4S shop selling the car, demanding a refund or replacement. However, the 4S shop firmly stated that they could only repair the car and would not replace it. Mr. Zhu tried to find Manager Yang who had strongly recommended this car, but the other party avoided him entirely.

After being repaired 70 times, how can one word "annoyance" suffice?

Almost every single part of the car has broken down from start to finish, and there are even instances of brake misalignment on highways.

During subsequent use, the "you'll know once you use it" car completely turned into a "headache once you use it."

According to Mr. Zhu, the car successively experienced issues such as abnormal airbag sounds, abnormal instrument panel sounds, sunken dashboard, cracked windshield wiper motor, damaged shock absorbers, damaged muffler, and brake failure. Almost every single part of the car broke down once, and some parts kept breaking after repairs, while new problems continued to emerge. "Sometimes on highways, there were life-threatening situations like brake misalignment and floating steering!"

Mr. Zhu recalled that the car broke down on average every few days. Roughly calculated, in over a year since buying the car, it had been repaired at least 70 times, big and small issues alike. "In this year, the oil expenses I incurred traveling back and forth between the Changzhou 4S shop and the Zhenjiang testing station would be enough to buy several sets of wheels!" Mr. Zhu helplessly remarked.

During this period, the most frustrating thing for Mr. Zhu was driving the car to a larger-scale 4S shop in Zhenjiang. After a comprehensive inspection, the result left him speechless: "Too many problems, impossible to fix!"

A stack of repair slips, tears of bitterness

Under the help of the Consumer Association, the 4S shop still maintained a hard stance, offering only to repurchase the car at a discounted price of 65,000 yuan.

Faced with this car full of endless problems, Mr. Zhu was extremely troubled. He had considered selling the car online or in the second-hand car market, but Mr. Zhu also felt that selling the car in its current condition to someone else would be irresponsible to the next owner.

Out of desperation, Mr. Zhu sought help from the Changzhou Consumer Association. With the assistance of the Changzhou Consumer Association, the 4S shop agreed to repurchase the car for 65,000 yuan. However, compared to the original purchase price of over 90,000 yuan along with other miscellaneous fees, there was a difference of over 30,000 yuan, which Mr. Zhu found too much of a loss. Ultimately, he did not agree, thinking: "Since it's a vehicle quality issue, why should the consumer bear the cost?"

Later, Mr. Zhu approached a media outlet in Changzhou to reflect on this issue, but the 4S shop maintained a tough stance: only repairs, no replacement. If replacement was insisted upon, the car would be repurchased at a discounted price of 65,000 yuan, requiring additional money to get a new car. Mr. Zhu said that he himself drives long distances, and now driving this car on the road, with sounds coming from one side and then the other, there was no sense of safety.

■ Journalist's Investigation

Only a few repair records exist in the 4S shop's computer system.

Yesterday afternoon, Manager Yang of the Changzhou Oriental Shengxuan Automobile Company told the reporter that Mr. Zhu's car indeed had many problems, but to a large extent, these were caused by frequent high-speed driving. However, regarding the issues with this car, Manager Yang confirmed that they had always actively repaired it and claimed compensation from the factory. Regarding the number of repairs mentioned by Mr. Zhu, Manager Yang believed it to be somewhat exaggerated, with probably only about four or five recorded in their computer system.

Regarding the matter of replacing the car, Manager Yang finally stated that Mr. Zhu could first drive the car to the 4S shop. After evaluation by the appraiser, Mr. Zhu would only need to appropriately subsidize a certain fee to exchange for a new car. The reporter conveyed Manager Yang's decision to Mr. Zhu, who felt that the response from the 4S shop remained unchanged from before: "They evaluate my car at a discounted price and ask me to subsidize to exchange for a new car, which I cannot accept. It's not that I don't want to drive this car, but it's simply undrivable. As for Manager Yang saying the problem lies in running on highways, I must ask, if a car produced cannot run on highways, can it even be called a car?" Mr. Zhu angrily said yesterday afternoon. Regarding the repair records of Mr. Zhu's car within a year, after learning that the 4S shop only had three to five repair records, Mr. Zhu expressed significant dissatisfaction and indicated that he would subsequently collect all the repair records and hold the 4S shop and Zhengzhou Nissan accountable. Our newspaper will continue to follow up on this matter.
