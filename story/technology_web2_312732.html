Looking ahead - Barca vows not to repeat mistakes of 3 years ago, Messi rests and misses semi-final

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 17:06:07

Preview - Barcelona determined not to repeat mistakes of three years ago; Messi rests and misses semi-final

Sina Sports - Barcelona will begin their FIFA Club World Cup campaign today, with their first match against Atlas. The game is widely expected to be an easy one for the Catalan giants, as Guardiola himself joked: "When isn't Barcelona the favorite?"

The two teams share some similarities beyond just their red-and-blue kits. Atlante's head coach Jose Cruz is a staunch follower of former Mexican national team coach Ricardo La Volpe, who is also a coach that Guardiola admires.

In predictions, Barcelona is so heavily favored that the organizers have allowed them to wear their traditional red-and-blue kit, while Atlas must wear indigo blue. Mexican media joked that this color is reminiscent of the Caribbean Sea. In fact, both teams had agreed to decide the kit colors by draw.

Atlas doesn't seem too bothered by this, given they only brought three fans and one journalist along. Atlante was founded in 1916 and has long represented the working class, but since the 1980s, it has increasingly fallen into the hands of big capitalists, moving further away from the common people. Currently, the club is even in financial trouble, without proper locker rooms or training facilities, forcing them to train on a golf course.

However, Atlante's players are relatively young and play good football. If Barcelona underestimates them, it could make a big mistake. Barcelona's Mexican center-back Rafael Marquez said: "Atlante has a strong lineup, especially their spine of the team is quite perfect." Marquez is familiar with Atlante's goalkeeper and striker. Interestingly, Atlante's striker is also named Rafael Marquez, sharing the same name as him. "The game won't be easy," Marquez warned his teammates.

The best praise for Atlante comes from their opponents' mouth. Guardiola never takes any opponent lightly and values every game. He stated: "If we can't get past the semi-finals, there won't be a final. You need to earn your place in the final on the pitch; everything else is empty talk."

Barcelona's trophy cabinet is filled with various cups, except for one – the 'Club World Cup'. Historically, Barcelona has participated twice in the FIFA Club World Cup (including its predecessor, the Toyota Cup). In 1993, they lost 1-2 to Sao Paulo from Brazil, and in 2007, they lost 0-1 to Internacional from Brazil. If Barcelona wins this time, it would fill a significant gap in their history. Moreover, achieving the historic sextuple title would be a remarkable feat. In 2009, Barcelona already won five titles: La Liga, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League, Spanish Super Cup, and UEFA Super Cup. Winning the FIFA Club World Cup would create an unprecedented miracle.

However, Guardiola is currently not interested in talking about the sextuple. "I am more focused on figuring out how to beat Atlante on the football field. If we don't concentrate on the football itself, we'll make a mistake. If we're not ready for the semi-final, it's entirely possible we won't reach the final."

Marquez also mentioned that Barcelona must not repeat the mistake of arrogance. In 2006, Barcelona defeated America from Mexico 4-0 in the semi-final, making the whole team overconfident, which led to their defeat in the final by Internacional from Brazil. Guardiola also referenced Barcelona's loss to Sao Paulo in 1992, saying: "At that time, neither the players nor the coach knew how to win that trophy." Psychological counseling began on Tuesday, with Guardiola looking serious, and Barcelona players understanding the importance of preparing seriously for the match against a weaker opponent.

Lastly, Messi's absence might open a historical door for Pedro. Although the Argentine participated in normal training yesterday, Guardiola still considers starting him in the semi-final unlikely. Pedro expressed: "I hope to participate in this important Club World Cup game. If I score and get written into history, that would be perfect. I look forward to scoring a goal, but most importantly, the team needs to win." Currently, Pedro has scored in five competitions: La Liga, Copa del Rey, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and UEFA Champions League. Scoring in the Club World Cup would create a legendary record of scoring in all six competitions in one season.

Maraca's predicted Barcelona starting lineup:

Valdes / Alves, Puyol, Pique, Abidal / Xavi, Toure, Iniesta / Pedro, Ibrahimovic, Henry
