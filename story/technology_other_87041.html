John Woo's "Red Cliff"

by webgogo on 2008-08-24 14:57:47

I am a fan of epic movies, from Brave Heart, The Lord of the Rings, Troy to Kingdom of Heaven and 300, epic movies have reached their peak. In the past, William Wallace in Brave Heart shouted "Freedom" and Han De in Dragon Spear cried out "Long Live the State of Wei!" The magnificent scenes and touching spirits in these epic movies are nothing new for fans of this genre. Chinese epic movies that blindly copy western blockbusters always end up with similar results and cannot succeed.

Among the four ancient civilizations, Chinese history is brilliant and grandiose. We don't need to create an imaginary world like The Lord of the Rings, or introduce mythology like Troy, or make a mountain out of a molehill like 300. China has more talent for making epic movies than any other country. However, it's like the Chinese who invented Cuju (ancient football) can't play soccer well, Chinese epic movies have also never been successful. Big-name directors such as Chen Kaige, Feng Xiaogang, and Zhang Yimou have all failed at making epic movies, which makes people wonder: Are Chinese directors not good at making epic movies? Or have we seen too many successful Western epic movies so that audiences have become overly critical?
