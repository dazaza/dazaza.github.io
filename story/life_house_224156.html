Teaching you to identify several scams in the decoration industry

by longersing on 2009-08-17 16:49:57

Almost all consumers are unfamiliar with the building materials industry, and they only consume these things once or twice, buy them and be done with it. With little experience and lack of understanding of the industry, consumers often fall into traps unknowingly. It's really hard for those who are not in the industry to explain these intricacies.

In the production field of decorative building materials: The building materials industry also "private label"

"The building materials field also popularizes private label production, and the ceiling industry is even more dazzling." A founder of a domestic ceiling brand said, "There are many building material factories that have only one brand, but no factory. Some are a company, an employee, a brand, all handled by the boss alone. Such factories print their materials dazzlingly, and even put on the label of a joint venture with foreign countries.

There are no factory photos on the materials of these brands, or they use a computer-generated image to deceive people. The common practice of these brands is high pricing, high rebate, high discount, get a contract by deception, and then entrust a no-name factory to produce." "Since the products of small factories' brands are mostly processed by several no-name factories, and the production capacity of these no-name factories is limited, often you produce this, I produce that, and then combine them together, which makes the coordination error surprisingly large."

"Riding on famous brands and stealing technology is another trick of small factories." According to reports, there are currently a large number of 'Kang' prefixed ceilings in the market. Moreover, in order to reduce costs, these no-name factories have all kinds of raw materials, and there is absolutely no quality inspection. In order to deceive buyers, these factories forge certificates."

In the sales field: "Deceptive tricks" keep emerging

Regarding the black hole in the circulation field, the person in charge of the Building Decoration Association said that when consumers go to the home decoration market to buy building materials, apart from insiders, the vast majority of people have had experiences of being deceived. According to the introduction, the building materials products sold in the market usually adopt the following several "deceptive tricks".

Confusing similar names is the most common trick used by dealers, especially common in solid wood flooring sales. A building materials dealer told reporters that most consumers have only a superficial understanding of the names and properties of building materials. Solid wood floor panels have various names such as common name, scientific name, alias, market name, etc. For example, the alias of Menkeli wood includes Kempas, Golden Unchanged, Nanyang Redwood, etc., and the market name is Steel Cypress Wood.

Precisely because the names are complex, consumers can't figure them out immediately, giving unscrupulous operators an opportunity. Some vendors often call beech wood "red beech", birch wood "cherry wood", etc. In fact, there are fundamental differences between these woods.

Passing off inferior goods as good ones is another "black hole" in the building materials distribution process. A dealer told reporters that there are many ways to produce inferior products. One way is that the product itself is made of inferior materials, such as using aluminum alloy with low aluminum content as normal aluminum alloy; another way is substitute materials, such as passing off products with an engineering plastic surface as stainless steel.

In the home decoration field: It's common for decoration teams to cut corners

Reporters learned that in terms of home decoration, some consumers often choose the easy way out and "package everything up", while some careless consumers never inquire when the decorators are working. This easily gives some unethical decoration teams the chance to cheat. These teams pass off inferior goods as good ones when purchasing home decoration materials, and cut corners during construction. Most owners don't have experience, so they can't find problems during acceptance. After living there for a while, problems arise, but the decoration team has already left.

"The root cause of this phenomenon lies in the insufficient recognition by consumers of the importance of building materials products; there are also few home decoration companies that truly value corporate brands, and the widespread existence of rogue decoration teams makes the building materials industry even murkier," a manager said.
