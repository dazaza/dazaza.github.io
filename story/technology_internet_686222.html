Forget about practicing badminton hard, focus on honing your verbal skills.

After retiring, Bao Chunlai was not lonely. After disappearing from the public eye for a while, Bao has recently appeared frequently in various occasions: as a director, host, and even an actor... Each of his appearances left people in awe. It seemed like a new idol-entertainment star was about to be born. However, Bao himself said that he was still an amateur, whether it be hosting or acting, none of these felt as natural to him as playing badminton.

Bao joined the outdoor travel show on Qinghai Satellite TV called "I Am the Adventure King," which has already recorded two or three episodes. For Bao, the biggest challenge now is still the issue of [looking at the camera]. [In training, competition, and daily life, there's no need to deliberately look at the camera, but recording a program is completely different. You need to treat the lens as an audience member, interact with it, and communicate.] Of course, there’s also the matter of [verbal skills]. At the beginning, Bao once had the embarrassing experience of having to read his lines more than ten times. For new things, he always feels curious, fresh, and excited. Of course, sometimes awkwardness can't be avoided. For example, during the episode filmed in Hainan, he really experienced [the feeling of seeing the sea]. [To get to the island, we needed to take a boat for over an hour. I am prone to seasickness, but at first, I was fine. Later, I started vomiting continuously, even infecting other staff members who began vomiting alongside me. But, it was the most beautiful island I've ever seen. The seawater was particularly clear, and the underwater scenery could be seen clearly. The first episode was filmed in Hainan, and the second one in Jilin, with only two days in between. The sudden change from wearing short sleeves to down jackets was quite significant. But every place I went to had its own unique local customs and traditions. For instance, when we visited the home of an eagle-raising uncle at Chagan Lake, we had a meal together. They were very热情 (hospitable) and treated us as friends.] Sri Lanka will be Bao's first overseas filming location, where the ambassador has already extended a warm invitation. As an athlete, Bao had been to many countries, but back then, he never had the time to properly explore or walk around. Now, as a host, this special identity allows him to fulfill his dreams. [Everyone has many choices, especially when reconsidering their lives. Plus, I might be suitable for this role, and being a host is also a kind of challenge, which fits my current state of mind.]

Not long ago, Bao also participated in the micro-film "Soul Transfer Station." This million-yuan micro-film was planned by Ning Caishen, supervised by Wei Nan, directed by Wei Min, and technically supported by the production team of "The Flowers of War." Bao said that it was just a [free ride] without pay. [I participated in this micro-film because it wasn’t commercial; it was a gathering of young people pursuing a dream of micro-films, conveying the principles of punishing evil and promoting good, and cause and effect. Such works with public welfare effects are ones I am willing to participate in and learn from. My role is just one representative among many 'Gods of Death,' a cute and cool big boy. I have also watched other film and television works portraying 'Gods of Death,' hoping that I can perform in a more down-to-earth and positive manner. It was all very fresh. I think there are many opportunities here that I want to try, to experience the feeling, even though it was just a cameo. But I thought the movie and actors were all great, very dedicated.] His first "touch of electricity" made him feel a different kind of hardship, but after continuous shooting for several hours in the cold night and seeing the finished product, it gave him a sense of accomplishment: [Those two days, the temperature in Beijing was very low. To shoot one scene, we had to endure various lenses for several hours outdoors, which was quite grueling, but finishing a shot gave a strong sense of achievement. Being an actor is really hard work, something you don't know until you experience it, but every attempt brings rewards.]
