Agent revelation: how does 360 compete with Baidu?

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:36:32

After causing a stir online, 360 Search ultimately still needs to focus on monetization. Therefore, establishing a search marketing system has become one of the main tasks for 360 this year. Under the immense pressure from Baidu's nationwide channels, how is 360, as a latecomer, managing to break into the market?

The reporter interviewed some local agents of 360 Search, revealing the expansion situation of 360's search marketing system. We learned that 360's rapidly growing market share made the agents smell an opportunity to make money. Meanwhile, Baidu’s tightened agency rights over the years indirectly led to agents defecting to 360. The number of 360's agents and order volume have both increased significantly since the beginning of the year.

The specific details are as follows:

1. In fact, the agency rights for 360 Search are highly sought after by agents. So far, 360 Search agents cover 16 provinces and cities, totaling 27 agencies, whereas four months ago, this number was only 13. Moreover, many local agents have already started developing sub-agents. The agency rights for 360 Search are quite popular in certain areas. "Just like the early days of Baidu, many agents at that time gained considerable benefits. So now that 360 has entered the search market, they are all eager to secure the agency rights as soon as possible," said an agent from Henan province to Sohu IT. They obtained the 360 agency rights last month.

2. The development of 360 agents also benefited from the void left by Baidu's retraction of agency rights. The aforementioned Henan agent mentioned, "After so many years of Baidu's development, many places have had their agency rights reclaimed, with branch offices or provincial general agencies set up." 360 does not have branch offices in various locations yet and mainly relies on agents. Among these agents, some have previously handled Baidu advertisements, some have managed Sogou and SouSou ads, and even some have handled 3721 ads. However, currently, they have shifted their focus to 360, with most agents establishing independent companies specifically for handling 360 advertisements.

3. Low prices are a significant advantage for 360 Search ads locally. An agent from Wuhan believes that the current advantages of 360 Search ads are: low competition, low price, and high conversion rate. "Some keyword ad prices for 360 Search are less than one-fifth of Baidu's," he said. With the reputation of being the "second-largest search engine" and the education provided by Baidu in its early days, the agent stated that promotion isn't too difficult. Nevertheless, many clients remain on the sidelines because 360 just entered the market, and they "also want to see if 360 is truly willing to develop the search engine properly."

4. In the past six months, the order volume for 360 Search ads has grown rapidly. We reported in March that 360 Search completed about 1,000 orders in Beijing over three months. A salesperson from Tian Di Online, a 360 agent in Beijing, told us that they can now exceed 600 orders per month alone. There are two agents for 360 Search in Beijing: Tian Di Online and Ju Tao Culture. The latter entered later and is still relatively small. Henan San Bai Liu, which just received the agency rights last month, has already completed more than 1,000 orders.

5. The average order value is around ten thousand yuan, which is not low. The minimum account opening fee for 360 Search ads is 4,600 yuan, including a 600 yuan service fee and a 4,000 yuan pre-deposit for ad placements. The Wuhan agent revealed that the current average order value is approximately ten thousand yuan. For comparison, Baidu's customer average revenue last quarter was 14,500 yuan.

6. 360 Search ads have covered all industries. Currently, 360 Search ads cover all industries except healthcare. Recently, they also opened up the previously restricted health supplement and adult product industries. Notably, 360 only accepts corporate clients and does not accept individual clients, and each company can only place ads for one site (Baidu accepts individual clients). Multiple agents mentioned that they are developing offline customers such as those in the education and consulting sectors.

But can 360 threaten Baidu? No.

Although 360's agent system has initially taken shape, its competition with Baidu is still at a very basic stage. One Baidu agent frankly stated: "Currently, 360 does not pose a threat to Baidu."

From the feedback of various 360 agents, the current competition is not yet intense. "360 Search is now providing customers with a new market and a new choice," one agent said. Many customers will advertise on both platforms.

However, this is definitely a phased situation. 360's search volume is still small and requires more subsequent investment. However, the monetization of search ads is undoubtedly the next most imaginative income growth point for 360. When 360's client base truly grows and prices rise, there may be another inevitable battle between 360 and Baidu.
