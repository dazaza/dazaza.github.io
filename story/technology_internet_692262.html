On the football field, spectacular goals are the most attractive charm for fans, while off the field, the transfer operations of various powerhouses often grab the attention of football fans. Below is a list of the top ten unexpected transfers in history, taking you back to those shocking transfer events that once stunned the world.

10. Johnstone (Celtic - Nantes - Glasgow Rangers)

In 1989, Johnstone transferred from French team Nantes to Glasgow Rangers, but he had previously played for Celtic, Rangers' arch-rival. The Celtic fans hated his betrayal, and the Rangers fans despised him for scoring many goals against their own team. What made this transfer even more controversial was that it wasn't just about football; it also involved religion. As a Roman Catholic, Johnstone chose to join Rangers, who represented the Protestants, breaking the clear religious divide between the two clubs at the time. He was the first Catholic player to join Rangers. Some radical Rangers fans even refused to celebrate with him after he scored.

9. Cantona (Leeds United - Manchester United (Weibo))

In 1992, Manchester United signed Cantona from Leeds United for £1.2 million, shocking the football world. This could be considered one of Ferguson's best transfer deals during his managerial career and possibly the biggest mistake by then-Leeds United manager Howard Wilkinson. Later, Wilkinson recalled: "I called Ferguson just to find out if Manchester United was interested in selling Denis Irwin, but I don't know why, in the end, we sold our most influential player, Cantona, to Manchester United." Leeds United has long since disappeared from the Premier League stage, while thanks to Cantona's outstanding performances and crucial goals, he helped Manchester United win the Premier League title four times in the next five seasons. Leeds fans still can't understand why they sold Cantona to their arch-rivals.

8. Andy Cole (Newcastle - Manchester United)

Some transfer rumors are obviously fake, so people choose to ignore them. The rumor about Andy Cole transferring to Manchester United fell into this category, but unlike other fake news, it eventually became true. In January 1995, Andy Cole transferred from Newcastle to Manchester United, leaving everyone puzzled as to why Newcastle would sell their top scorer to their main title rival. When Newcastle fans protested against the club's foolish decision, Kevin Keegan appeared on the steps of St James' Park to explain the rationale behind the transfer. "Look! We got Keanu Giles!" Alright, you got Keanu Giles, but may I ask who Keanu Giles is?

7. Campbell (Tottenham Hotspur - Arsenal (Weibo))

Sol Campbell was a star nurtured by Tottenham Hotspur, joining as an apprentice at 16 and moving up to the first team at 18. After Campbell became famous, Spurs handed him the captaincy, and Spurs fans saw him as the hope for the team's revival. In 2001, as Campbell's contract neared expiration, knowing they couldn't keep their star striker, Spurs hoped Campbell would renew his contract and claimed they wouldn't interfere with his transfer. This move by Spurs was merely to gain some transfer fee, but faced with waving checks, Campbell rejected Spurs and joined Arsenal, their North London derby rivals, as a free agent. Of course, Campbell achieved great success during his four years at Arsenal, winning the Premier League title twice and almost reaching the pinnacle of Europe. However, transferring to a fierce local rival during his prime meant Campbell would forever bear the name of traitor.

6. Ferdinand (Leeds United - Manchester United)

Ferdinand was described most accurately by former Leeds United chairman Peter Ridsdale as a "hypocrite." During the transfer saga, Ferdinand cried publicly, expressing his unwillingness to leave Leeds United, but secretly flirted with Manchester United, building a close relationship, even forcing the club to accept Manchester United's £30 million offer. Ultimately, Ferdinand transferred to Manchester United for a record-breaking £31.1 million, and Leeds United fans withdrew the award for the club's season's best player to express their dissatisfaction with his defection. At the time, the British media believed Manchester United was crazy, calling it an "inconceivable" deal. But afterward, Ferdinand's performance was convincing; he formed the top central defensive partnership in the Premier League with Vidic and later wore the England captain's armband. When he looks back on his past, will he remember the Leeds United team he once cried and shouted not to leave?

5. Vieri (Inter Milan - AC Milan)

It's not easy to make Inter Milan fans fall in love with a player, but it's very easy to make them hate one—just have him transfer to AC Milan. Vieri was one of the best strikers in the world at the time. In the summer of 2005, after Vieri parted ways with Inter Milan, when Inter fans were still sighing over his departure, no one expected that just four days later, Vieri would wear the AC Milan jersey. Inter fans were completely enraged, and extreme fans even smashed his car and restaurant. After transferring to their arch-rival, Vieri said something that made him forever unforgivable by Inter fans. He said: "AC Milan is the best club I've ever played for in my career, and in the past, I mistakenly stayed at the other club in Milan." After Vieri left, Inter Milan began to continuously win Serie A titles, while Vieri only won one Serie A title in his lifetime, and that was at Juventus.

4. Beckham (Manchester United - Real Madrid (Weibo))

Beckham and Manchester United were seen as a "perfect match" until February 13, 2003, when a flying shoe changed everything. After Manchester United lost 0-2 to Arsenal, in the dressing room, Ferguson kicked a shoe in the corner which hit Beckham's face, causing blood to flow from the stud wounds on his handsome face. On June 17, 2003, Beckham transferred to Real Madrid for £25 million. It's hard to say whether Real Madrid brought Beckham for the 90 minutes on the pitch, but after Beckham left, Manchester United's position as the "richest club in football" was taken over by Real Madrid. And Real Madrid officially ushered in the era of the "Galácticos."

3. Ronaldo (Inter Milan - Real Madrid)

After transferring from Barcelona to Inter Milan, Ronaldo's nearly perfect performances drove Milan city wild, but he could never shake off the "demon" of injuries. During the time he was away from the pitch due to injury, Ronaldo received unprecedented care and fatherly encouragement from Moratti. Ronaldo gave his best to Inter Milan, but he always waited for his first league title. In the last round of the 2001-02 Serie A season, Inter Milan played away against Lazio, needing only a draw to secure their first league title in nearly a decade. In the end, Inter lost 2-4, and the image of Ronaldo sitting on the bench covering his face in tears after being substituted became a lasting pain for Inter Milan fans. That was also the last time he wore the blue-black jersey. After shining in the 2002 World Cup, Ronaldo transferred to Real Madrid for €45 million. The following year, Ronaldo won his first championship trophy.

2. Roberto Baggio (Fiorentina - Juventus)

In the hearts of Italian fans, Roberto Baggio with his ponytail has always been a symbol of purple. In the summer of 1989, Count Pontello, the owner of Fiorentina, "for the consideration of the team's economic interests," reached an agreement with Juventus owner Agnelli, and Baggio transferred to Juventus for $12.8 million. When he learned of the news, Baggio, who deeply loved Fiorentina, tried to stay by finding Count Pontello. Even though joining Juventus would triple his salary compared to Fiorentina, Baggio said, "To stay in Fiorentina, I am willing to take a pay cut." But he still couldn't convince his money-hungry boss. Months later, when Juventus visited Fiorentina, Baggio, representing Juventus, created a penalty but refused to take it. When he was substituted off, all Fiorentina fans stood up to applaud him, and as he walked off, he picked up a Fiorentina scarf from the ground and kissed it. Later, Baggio said a sentence—"My heart will always be purple."

1. Luis Figo (Barcelona - Real Madrid)

In July 2000, Figo transferred from La Liga's Barcelona to their arch-rival Real Madrid. Figo was considered by many to be the best player in the world, and Real Madrid and Barcelona were regarded as the fiercest pair of rivals in the world. Therefore, this transfer naturally became the biggest news of the summer. Real Madrid president Perez was largely elected because he promised during the election to bring in Figo, who was still under contract with Barcelona. Eventually, Figo transferred to Real Madrid for a record-breaking $56 million. In interviews, Figo remained stubborn, claiming: "Although Barcelona has always treated me well, I cannot ignore myself. I am very satisfied with this transfer and have no regrets." Naturally, Figo, who chose to oppose the world, "enjoyed" being pelted with lighters, water bottles, and the deafening cries of "Judas" from tens of thousands of Barcelona fans when he accompanied Real Madrid on an away game against Barcelona.
