Breaking the Standard Puzzle: Smart Home Development

At the turn of the year, it's time for industries to review and look forward. When reviewing the development path of smart buildings last year, the reporter found that smart homes have emerged as a much-anticipated "new star" in the smart building industry in 2011.

With the expanding market demand, many enterprises from different fields have joined in. Currently, Chinese society is in a period of transformation, with increasing wealth gap and social instability factors. People are increasingly paying attention to their own safety and property security. According to statistics, last year, China's household consumption expenditure was 11.2 trillion yuan, and the expenditure on smart homes and home security in first-tier cities and eastern regions reached 900 billion to 1.1 trillion yuan!

Under the temptation of such a large market "cake", many home appliance enterprises and IT enterprises have plunged into the smart home industry with their own business concepts and cutting-edge technologies. In 2011, Sichuan Changhong Electric Co., Ltd. established the Four Seas eHome project team across group business units, exploring the industrial integration between real estate and home appliances, creating an industrial model of smart home terminals and platforms, forming a complete eHome industry chain from R&D design, production, promotion to application. At the same time, the company also applied intelligent products and Internet of Things technology to smart families and communities, realizing the interconnected home.

Huawei, a giant in China's IT industry, announced its entry into the security field at last year's Shenzhen Anbo Conference. The company exhibited a full series of M2M module products and series solutions covering 2G and 3G at the exhibition, some of which can meet the needs of smart neighborhoods.

In addition to IT enterprises and home appliance enterprises, long-term smart home manufacturing enterprises have also clearly felt the changes in the market. Shuzhongming, product manager of the Intelligent Living Space Division of Schneider Electric (China) Co., Ltd., said that with the gradual popularization of smart products like Iphone and Ipad, high-tech products have narrowed the distance between users and smart homes. Currently, Schneider's smart home business volume is gradually expanding, and the related product and solution business volume accounts for 20%~30% of the division's business volume, and this figure still has a large upward space.

The clustering of enterprises, the growth trend of related product business volumes... These changes are only a glimpse of the rapid development of the entire smart home industry. Under the impetus of large enterprises, high technologies, and large funds, the entire industry has entered a period of rapid development.

Enterprises operate independently, making it hard for the industry to grow strong. Home appliance enterprises, IT enterprises, and security enterprises have continuously "crossed over" into the smart home industry, creating a prosperous scene. However, behind the prosperity, there are hidden dangers in the entire industry.

In the smart home market, early enterprises mainly engaged in intercoms and electrical products, such as Guolin and安居bao. Their related technologies are mainly applied in families and neighborhoods. Enterprises expand the original functions of their products to form a complete smart home solution, becoming the main force in the smart home industry. In addition, some home appliance enterprises find it difficult to break through in the traditional white goods market, adjust their business strategies, and actively develop the emerging smart home market, preparing for the next round of competition. Haier, TCL, and Meidi are good examples of well-known domestic enterprises. This year, Changhong and other enterprises have announced their entry into the smart home industry, with business ideas similar to those of Haier and TCL enterprises.

Many enterprises see the broad market prospects of smart homes and crowd into this emerging industry. However, while competing for the market share, each manufacturer has certain differences in technical operations, leading to independent operation. For example, some IT enterprises introduce elements like the Internet of Things and cloud computing into the smart home industry, creating the concept of "cloud community," allowing residents to effectively "communicate" with lighting, curtains, alarms, TVs, air conditioners, water heaters, and even medical, educational, cultural, and other living resources outside the community through mobile phones and the Internet; some enterprises integrate security technology into smart homes, forming a smart home solution focused on security prevention; some lighting enterprises and curtain enterprises intervene, using automation technology to monitor and manage home appliances... Different enterprises tie their smart home concepts with their respective specialized technologies, forming smart home systems with distinct technical styles. This makes it difficult for users to choose.

Industry analysts believe that the current smart home market is relatively chaotic, with many enterprises hyping up the concept of smart homes. Many smart home products on the market only introduce some elements of intelligence, such as video intercoms and anti-theft alarms, and are not truly smart homes in the real sense. Due to the lack of unified standards in the entire industry, many immature products are rushed to the market by enterprises, prematurely depleting the future of smart homes.

Guo Weijun, an expert from the National Intelligent Building and Residential Area Digitalization Standardization Technical Committee, believes that due to the lack of unified standards in the industry, the quality of smart home products on the market varies greatly. After networking, various problems often arise, causing inconvenience to users and resulting in low market recognition. Unstable product quality makes many enterprise-developed products and systems remain in the experience stage, unable to achieve industrialization. And without scale and industrialization, the industry cannot continue to develop healthily.

He suggests establishing a complete set of smart building and smart home standard systems under the support of relevant ministries, creating a smart home technology verification platform and comprehensive service platform based on the Internet of Things.

Zhang Yonggang, deputy director of the National Intelligent Building and Residential Area Digitalization Standardization Technical Committee, believes that the emergence of China's smart home industry is not long ago, and it is still in the initial stage of development. The entire industry will go through a long process from hype to rationality and then partial application in some areas. Currently, we can only continuously improve services and strengthen standardization construction to promote product interoperability and push the industry towards scale and industrialization.

Liang Feng, director of the Electronics Information Department of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, pointed out that the imperfect standards of the smart home industry and the Internet of Things restrict the healthy development of the industry. However, from the current situation, it will take some time to establish sound standards; after the standards are established, how to implement them becomes a challenge before everyone. Therefore, practicing in standards and improving standards in practice to promote the development of the smart home industry still requires a long journey.
