Deep love, without letting you know...

by mingzhuang2009 on 2009-06-12 17:17:16

On a rainy day, with few customers and nothing to do.

Someone pushed aside the bead curtain and came in, politely leaving their umbrella outside.

It was a very pretty girl, with a clean face, clear eyes, and neat attire.

She said hello, and then I left her alone to browse freely in the store.

She carefully picked up each item she liked, looked at it for a moment, gently touched it, and then put it down. I noticed that she always checked the price first.

I presumed her financial situation wasn't too good.

In the end, she chose a fashionable wristwatch and a delicate small backpack. It was somewhat surprising to me; those two items weren’t cheap at all.

Are you getting these two? I asked without showing any expression.

Is there a discount? She asked softly, a bit shy, her cheeks slightly red.

The weather is bad, there are few customers, and it's rare for you to come. Let's make it 20% off. Having been a shopkeeper for two years, I could handle such situations easily.

She hesitated for a moment but finally nodded without further haggling, handing me the items.

I was about to package them as usual when she stopped me. She said, let me give you some money first. Please put them back where they were, and I'll come back later to get them, okay?

I was a little puzzled but quickly agreed. Some customers do reserve items by paying a small deposit. But I didn't expect her deposit to be more than half of the total price. The remaining amount didn't exceed one hundred yuan. What a strange girl! If it's like this, why not just take them with you?

Though I had doubts, I didn't ask much. It's the customer's freedom. Since she paid so much, according to custom, I should have packed them up and waited for her to come back and pick them up.

She still stopped me, saying quietly, please put them back where they were, alright? Her eyes carried a hint of request.

I smiled, nodded, and carefully placed the bag and watch back where they were.

Thank you, I'll be back soon. Her eyes lit up happily as she took the umbrella by the door and quickly disappeared from my sight.

What a strange girl!

Not long after, she came back, but not alone. Behind her was a tall boy whose demeanor matched hers perfectly. Both had clean faces, clear eyes, and wore cotton khaki coats.

I was just about to hand her the bag and watch she'd prepaid most of the cost for, but she seemed not to see me. Instead, she turned around, grabbed the boy's hand, and said, I like that bag, look, the red one with many pockets. Before I could react, she had already pulled the boy to stand in front of the bag.

If you like it, we'll buy it. The boy reached out and took the bag down, handing it to her while saying, it does look nice.

She nodded, holding the bag close to her chest, and continued searching around.

I held my breath silently, watching what she would do next.

She acted as if it were real, touching this and looking at that. Two minutes later, she "surprisingly" spotted the fashion watch again. She grabbed it, turned around, and started pouting, pulling on the boy's arm, saying, you promised to give me two gifts for my zodiac year birthday.

The boy's face reddened slightly as he softly said, sure, as long as you like it.

She almost jumped with joy, taking the two items she'd already chosen and walking over to me, asking, boss, how much? As she spoke, she winked at me behind the boy's back.

I suddenly understood, feeling warmth in my heart, and blurted out, you really know how to choose. These two are today's special sale items. Then I quoted a price.

She turned to look at the boy.

The boy's face turned even redder, gently pushing her and saying, so cheap, too cheap, let's buy something else, I have money.

I want these. She hugged the items tightly and said, I like them. Her voice became stubborn, urging him to pay quickly.

The boy obviously was used to following her whims. Without another word, he hurriedly took out about four or five hundred yuan from his pocket, surely prepared money for buying her gifts.

I smiled, took one hundred yuan, and while giving change, casually picked up a pair of cartoon couple cups from behind me, saying, all customers celebrating their birthdays who buy gifts here will receive a gift. I shoved the cups into her arms and said, happy birthday.

Because she was so happy, her face instantly flushed red, repeatedly saying thank you. The boy followed suit, speaking sincerely.

Then they left, her hugging the gifts, safely nestled in his protective arms. The rain wasn't light, but they shared an umbrella. I knew neither of them would get wet because they both loved each other so well.

He loved her, that kind of indulgence was evident. She loved him, giving him enough dignity without letting him know. They were undoubtedly a poor couple, but the weight of material possessions in their love was thin, losing any significance.

Love? How much love? Not much more, just deep love, and not letting him know.
