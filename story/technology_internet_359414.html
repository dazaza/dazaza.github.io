The new greeting among Zhejiang businessmen: "Have you done venture investment?"

by zjsr2010 on 2010-06-29 12:01:00

　　

　　“Have you sought venture capital?” This question is becoming a greeting among entrepreneurs. From initially not knowing what venture capital was, to now when the venture capital industry is thriving, more and more Zhejiang businessmen are catching this wave of domestic venture capital.

　　After the 2008 global financial crisis, Lin Dong began repositioning his role and started his "side business": being an investor.

　　Guo Yu, who co-founded Gonghe Network with Lin Dong, also officially transitioned in 2010: while operating Gonghe Network, he devoted half of his energy to finding projects for his 1 billion yuan investment fund, “Transitioning to become an investor is the new direction I gave myself in 2010.” Guo Yu said.

　　Two other well-known Zhejiang businessmen have also made moves in investments: Ma Yun as one of the initiators established a multi-million investment fund, while Guo Guangchang formed alliances with foreign venture capital institutions like Carlyle Group, starting his path of internationalized investments.

　　Lin Dong: Invests 20 million RMB annually seeking "potential stocks"

　　In the next two years, Lin Dong plans to inject 3 million USD into his "Lvsheng" brand. “‘Lvsheng’ itself is constantly innovating; besides making beef jerky, I am continuously developing derivative products.” Lin Dong said that after two years of digesting the 3 million USD investment in "Lvsheng", it will stand on a new platform.

　　The global financial crisis did not bring down Lin Dong and his "Lvsheng", but it forced him to think about his future: “Lvsheng” operations have matured, he has funds and time, so what else should he do?

　　“I also started from scratch, I deeply understand the agony and pain involved; now although I have funds, I may not have the courage to start again from zero. ” It was already 8 PM, after a busy day, Lin Dong rarely had some free time, sitting in his office talking to reporters about the initial reasons for locking his "secondary entrepreneurship" in the "investor" industry.

　　Lin Dong, who has served as a mentor for college student entrepreneurship for many years, understands the inherent entrepreneurial impulse within the college student group, “They have the advantage of youth, they have drive, what they lack is funding and experience. And these two points happen to be my strengths.” Thus, Lin Dong found the synergy point: establish an investment fund, providing funding for college students full of entrepreneurial impulses and passion.

　　Lin Dong's first project as an investor was Hangzhou Hunter Catering Management Co., Ltd. Lin Dong said that during this process, besides investing funds and leading these newly graduated college students step by step, he does not directly participate in daily management, thereby freeing up time to find other projects.

　　"The growth potential of 'Hunter Catering' exceeded my expectations, from a 30-square-meter small shop on Wulin Road in Hangzhou, it has now been upgraded to a 'Happy Restaurant', 'Invested 3 million RMB, the cost of the first store is expected to be recovered within a year.' He told reporters.

　　Lin Dong set a goal for his "side business": invest 20 million RMB annually. "I see the greatest opportunities in the huge business opportunities created by the combination of traditional industries and the internet, but I won't limit my investment field to the internet. As long as it conforms to the national advocated industrial direction, and has potential, I will take action."

　　To give himself more time for investments, Lin Dong started stepping back from the management team of Changxiang Network, which he founded, at the end of last year. By May this year, he had invested in seven projects, with Changxiang Network being just one of them. "In investments, I only position myself as the 'second largest shareholder,' otherwise one project would consume a lot of energy."

　　Guo Yu: Entrepreneurs doing venture capital "more professional"

　　For foreign venture capital funds, clearly distinguishing between LP (fund providers) and GP (fund managers) is already the norm; but for Zhejiang businessmen accustomed to controlling their own financial autonomy, they prefer to integrate the roles of LP and GP, steering the investment fund themselves.

　　Guo Yu, the founder of Gonghe Network, truly decided to transition into a professional investor at the beginning of this year. In his view, entrepreneurs with successful entrepreneurial experiences doing venture capital are not "unprofessional," but rather "more professional."

　　"The most important point is that the entrepreneurial experience accumulated over more than ten years allows us to provide more guidance to those just starting out, not just providing funds, but more importantly sharing social network resources, and strategically overseeing the development direction of these projects." Guo Yu told reporters.

　　When I met Guo Yu, he was busy preparing for a reception party, the main character being the college student entrepreneur of the board game project he invested in. This project, discovered in a college student entrepreneurship competition in 2007, originally invested with 20 million RMB, has seen its value increase nearly fivefold after three years, "Our goal is to make it the leader in the domestic board game field."

　　In 2007, the board game placed in front of Guo Yu was nothing more than a proposal. "Why has this board game project succeeded? Besides the potential of the creative idea itself, it also relies on my existing channels for promotion, thus bursting with potential in the shortest possible time, seizing market opportunities." Guo Yu said.

　　Now, Guo Yu has six or seven similar investment projects in hand; in his view, each project has the potential to grow into a leader in the细分fields of the internet industry.

　　"In my understanding, if one focuses on running a single enterprise, at most one can create an industry leader; but by putting my experience, funds, and social resources together with capable entrepreneurs, it’s possible to simultaneously create 5 to 10 leaders in specialized fields, and I believe the latter brings more sense of achievement." Guo Yu evaluated his "investor" role in this way.

　　"Cluster" doing investments highlights the transformation impulse of Zhejiang businessmen

　　Besides Guo Yu and Lin Dong, Alibaba Chairman Ma Yun, Fosun Group Chairman Guo Guangchang, etc., have also started exploring or increasing their participation in the investment market.

　　The Yunfeng Fund, officially established in April this year, brought the investment enthusiasm of private entrepreneurs to its peak: gathering 10 leading figures in the domestic business world including Ma Yun, Giant Interactive Chairman Shi Yuzhu, ENN Group Chairman Wang Yusu, and Silver Taizhou Investment Chairman Shen Guojun.

　　"Ma Yun is already a leading entrepreneur in the internet industry." Lin Dong commented on Ma Yun's status in the industry. Even Ma Yun could not resist the temptation, taking up part-time work as an investor, forcing people to reassess the "investment fever" surging among Zhejiang businessmen.

　　After the global financial crisis, Guo Guangchang not only established a 5.5 billion RMB real estate fund under Fudi Jingye, but also co-founded the first partnership-based equity investment enterprise in China, Carlyle Fosun, with Carlyle Investment Group, intending to create a globally recognized investment platform with a private background.

　　This wave of investment impulse among Zhejiang businessmen occurred under a specific background.

　　2008 was called the turning point of China's PE market. When the originally dominant foreign PE in the Chinese market declined due to the impact of the global financial crisis, Renminbi funds began to flourish.

　　This trend continued to strengthen in 2009 and 2010. A report from Tsing Capital, a research institution for venture capital and private equity, stated that the number of PEs in China (including venture capital) has grown from dozens 10 years ago to more than 1200 today; in the first quarter of this year, Renminbi funds surpassed foreign currency funds for the first time in terms of investment amount.

　　Sources of Renminbi funds, besides social security funds and state-owned assets, include private enterprises, and Zhejiang is a place where private capital is relatively abundant, so active investment is reasonable.

　　"Now, for domestic investment funds, the opportunities are just beginning." Lin Dong said that he makes investments based on the premise that his main business is already stable and mature, so there is no risk of "not sticking to one's primary business."

　　However, in the view of Chen Bin, president of Cybernaut Investment Management Co., Ltd., the enthusiasm of entrepreneurs for part-time investment reflects only a transitional state where Zhejiang businessmen desire to transform from industrialists to investors, unable to become the norm.

　　"After 30 years of capital accumulation, Zhejiang entrepreneurs will have the impulse to transform into investors, but becoming an investor is not that simple, still requiring time to hone, having investment funds operated by professional teams is the trend." Chen Bin analyzed.

　　Invest like Buffett

　　Entrepreneurs participating in investments, especially when the investment targets point to college students at the start of their ventures, such investments not only create economic value but also have positive social significance.

　　As one of the main initiators of the Yunfeng Fund, always calm and wise Ma Yun did not lose his head: the invited fund manager Yu Feng also comes from an industrial background, and this time Yu Feng bid farewell to his identity as an industrialist, becoming a full-time investor.

　　In this wave of participating in investments, most Zhejiang businessmen maintain a rational attitude. Under this premise, how can entrepreneurs doing venture capital improve success rates? The success of Fosun Group might offer some insights.

　　In 2009, Fosun's total external investment reached 12 to 13 billion RMB. Currently, its invested companies exceed 100, with more than 20 already listed. With the expansion of investment scale, Fosun's involvement spans from core industries such as medicine, real estate, steel, mining, retail, rapidly extending to consumption, services, chemicals, new energy, and more.

　　With such a vast investment, how does Fosun manage to cover all aspects? Fosun Group CEO Liang Xinjun recently revealed the essence in an article published in "China Entrepreneur": they invest like Buffett holding Coca-Cola, rather than simply purely doing PE investments; if certain industries can maintain high-speed growth for the next ten to twenty years, Fosun is happy to hold onto them, rather than rushing to exit like PE, focusing more on short-term benefits.

　　Behind the enthusiasm for investment, if some entrepreneurs merely aim to "make quick money," the nature of the investment changes flavor.

　　Sun Deliang, chairman of NetSun Technology, pointed out a prerequisite for entrepreneurs engaging in part-time investments: based on already strengthening their main businesses.

　　Sun Deliang warned: Entrepreneurs who think operating real businesses is too hard and making money too slow, hoping to make "quick money" through venture capital, should best resist the temptation. "It is the ideal state for industrialists to transform into investors, but the worst fear is trying to handle both ends, ultimately possibly failing to manage either, affecting both investment and real business operations."
