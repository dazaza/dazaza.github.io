Li Shufu: Volvo will go ahead of BMW and Mercedes-Benz

by zjsr2010 on 2010-06-24 15:50:08

http://www.zjsr.com/news/33092.htm

Tianxia Zhejiang Businessman Network June 23 News (Reported by Xuying Xia from Beijing): On June 19, 2010, the "Sixth China Private Enterprise Investment and Development Forum," jointly hosted by Peking University's Research Institute of Private Economy and the Publicity and Education Department of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, was held at Peking University. Geely Group Chairman Li Shufu delivered a speech. In his speech, Li Shufu revealed Geely’s strategic layout after becoming the new owner of Volvo Cars: "Restoring Volvo's international independence."

Li Shufu stated that one reason for Volvo's lack of success during the Ford era was because there were too many people from Ford managing it excessively. After Geely became the new owner of Volvo Cars, they would not send over many people but instead give Volvo more autonomy to unleash their own potential.

Another issue with Ford was treating Volvo as a department rather than an independent car manufacturer. All of Ford's automotive safety technology was developed within Volvo. Li Shufu explained that Geely's approach would be completely different — restoring Volvo's original independence and reviving its former competitiveness. They aim to continue promoting Volvo's traditional leading position in safety and environmental protection, respecting Volvo's core value concepts and mature commercial civilization, consolidating and strengthening Volvo's positioning in traditional European and American markets, while also developing emerging markets including China. They plan to extend and broaden Volvo's product line to meet multi-level users' demand for safe and environmentally friendly cars, re-establishing Volvo's once absolute global leadership.

"It's not about saying Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Volvo, Audi; my philosophy should go ahead of Mercedes-Benz and BMW, not follow behind them." Li Shufu expressed great ambition, "We will achieve this goal through the power of the Chinese market."

Li Shufu: "The theme of my report: Innovative Practices in the Era of Economic Globalization."

For companies, shifting the mode of economic development means transforming the company's core competitive methods. Fundamentally, it is also a transformation and elevation of the company's core values and entrepreneurial spirit. This is my superficial understanding of the shift in the mode of economic development.

Under the care and support of various levels of party committees and governments, the United Front Work Department of the Central Committee, the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, and all sectors of society, Geely has grown step by step from nothing to something, from small to large, from simple manufacturing to independent innovation. Starting from May 2007, Geely entered a period of strategic transformation. Instead of simply engaging in price wars, Geely now fights with technology, quality, service, corporate ethics, and brand enhancement, as well as international expansion. The overall strategy has changed. Secretary Quan's speech was very important. He spoke similarly on multiple occasions, each time bringing different feelings and educational significance. Success or failure of a business, I believe, is first determined by strategy. If the overall strategy is wrong, even meticulous execution may still lead to error. First, the strategy must be correct. Strategy determines direction, and seriousness determines success or failure. Details determine success or failure. Therefore, under the major policy background of the Central Committee and the State Council's call for economic development transformation, Geely has decided to implement strategic transformation to ensure alignment with national policies.

Geely takes talent as the core competitiveness for innovation. Strategic transformation means fighting with technology, which relies on talent. Quality battles also cannot be separated from talent, and corporate ethics battles are even more dependent on talent. If the talent within a company lacks high moral standards, it is difficult to conduct ethical battles. Because the transformation of China's economic development direction, from another perspective, is determined by the quality of corporate personnel, and the competitiveness of the national economy is ultimately determined by the quality of its citizens. Therefore, enhancing human quality is crucial. Hence, we have decided to focus on talent and innovation as key areas. As the core competitiveness of enterprises, innovation also cannot be separated from talent; innovation without talent is empty. We search globally for excellent talents who align with our core values and development strategies, truly coming together with shared aspirations.

Currently, more than three hundred foreign experts work at Geely, hailing from over a dozen countries and regions around the world. Geely advocates mutual learning, where everyone is both a teacher and a student. Geely is not only a company but also resembles a school. We extensively cultivate technicians and skilled workers, as well as undergraduate, master's, doctoral students needed by Geely, including postdoctoral training. Through exchanges between domestic and international experts, mutual learning, joint work, a large number of Geely automobile talents and international talents are cultivated and exercised.

After the strategic transformation, Geely has achieved certain developments, with continuously improving independent innovation capabilities, sustained user satisfaction increases, general employee quality improvements, and significant enhancements in corporate responsibility and moral qualities. Practice has proven that Geely's development path can be further explored.

Everyone is highly concerned about Geely's acquisition of Volvo. In response to the Central Committee and the State Council's call to encourage companies to 'go out' and 'bring in', on March 28th this year, Geely signed a purchase agreement for Volvo with Ford USA. This matter actually caught my attention starting from 2002, when I believed that the global economy would undergo tremendous changes and the global automobile manufacturing industry would experience significant adjustments. From 2002, we predicted that General Motors and Ford in the U.S. would face bankruptcy. At that time, many people thought this impossible, but we believed it would happen. GM has already gone bankrupt, and Ford is almost bankrupt. This is what Secretary Quan mentioned earlier about studying laws, respecting laws, and acting according to laws, which is very important. Many things happening worldwide are governed by objective intrinsic essences. GM's bankruptcy was not accidental, and Ford's near-bankruptcy was also not accidental.

By 2006, Ford had basically agreed to sell Volvo. It had many well-known luxury brands under its wing, but Volvo was its best brand and one of the top luxury car brands in the world. In June 2007, we formed a project team, and in April 2008, we submitted our first proposal to acquire Volvo to Ford. In November 2008, I went to the U.S. to formally discuss this matter with Ford's CEO. In January 2009, we went to the U.S. to discuss this major matter with Ford's chairman. Finally, on March 28th this year, we signed the contract.

Why did we acquire Volvo? Under the backdrop of globalization, Chinese enterprises must understand their actual image, status, and real competitiveness in the world. Chinese automobile companies face two major challenges, which I believe cannot be resolved within twenty years. One challenge is intellectual property rights. If Chinese automobiles want to be sold in the U.S. or Europe, the problem of intellectual property rights cannot be avoided. Currently, sales in China are doing well, but the issue of intellectual property rights has not been solved. If sold in the U.S. or Europe, they won't be so friendly towards us. Without intellectual property rights, the technology is far behind, making it uncompetitive in Europe and America. If the technology is relatively advanced, it must be copying others. In such circumstances, if Chinese automobile industry wants to be respected worldwide, it must solve the problem of intellectual property rights. Up until today, no Chinese automobile company has resolved this issue, and I believe it cannot be resolved within twenty years.
