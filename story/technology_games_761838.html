The way traditional game consoles die is not by beheading, but by slow slicing.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-13 10:09:33

The emergence of smartphones has already impacted the home console gaming market, and now Android-based consoles are on their way. Despite differing market positions and player demographics, home console gaming has begun a slow decline, akin to boiling a frog gradually.Recently, an article in TechCrunch pointed out that the evolution of the gaming console market won't be as rapid as the replacement of feature phones by smartphones; rather, it will resemble glaciers slowly melting. It's less of a revolution and more of an erosion.Ouya is an open-source gaming console system based on Android. Clearly, this kind of small-scale gaming console cannot currently compete with Microsoft or Sony. The same situation applies to BlueStacks' recently announced Android gaming console. Defeating giants doesn't happen overnight.Sony, Nintendo, and Microsoft previously divided the gaming sector, but smart TVs, Google devices, and Apple products are now eroding their market share. Many people expect to see a turning point where new forms of gaming overtake the old giants, but such a scenario won't occur.Evidence suggests that traditional gaming consoles are gradually losing market share. For instance, Nintendo's quarterly performance shows a sharp decline in consumer interest for the recently launched Wii U console. As for Sony, the Japanese giant just released its fiscal 2012 report. Although it managed to turn a profit after massive losses in previous years, most of the good news is concentrated in the smartphone sector. PlayStation sales continue to decline, raising concerns about the profitability and prospects of the gaming division. The Xbox 360 from Microsoft has performed relatively well, but over time, sales of motion-sensing accessories like Kinect have started to slow down.The slowing sales of Kinect undoubtedly serve as a barometer for the overall health of the traditional gaming console industry. It's time for the established giants to inject fresh vitality into the gaming console market to counter the invasion of mobile smart devices. They need to create some buzz, such as motion-sensing games or features like the screen of the 3DS game console. Just as compact cameras need unique features to differentiate themselves from smartphone cameras, console gaming needs innovation to attract new consumers. However, Ouya founder Uhrman once stated that traditions in the gaming console industry run deep.The competition in the gaming console space remains fierce. The elimination of unattractive gaming handhelds like GP2X Wiz and Gizmondo is merely a footnote to the current state of the industry. Even though much hope is placed on newcomers like Ouya, they aren't immune to following in the footsteps of these predecessors. While the industry continues to fuel the rise of new gaming formats like mobile gaming, there will always be a market for Microsoft and Sony consoles due to their loyal fanbase. The question is how large this market will be in the future.It can be foreseen that more and more players will think: Since I can play games on convenient and affordable platforms like smartphones or Ouya, why should I play on PlayStation or Xbox? Consoles like Ouya will draw players away from established markets, with price being their trump card, especially since Ouya is based on Android, leaning on the strength of Google.However, we must rationally recognize that Ouya's arrival won't significantly impact Microsoft and Sony yet; it merely pulls back the curtain on an era of change.This kind of transition has happened several times before, much like how the once-popular Xiaobawang game console has almost disappeared today.
