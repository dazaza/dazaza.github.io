Pre-marital trial love: Beauty or Bubble

by anonymous on 2011-07-29 10:27:36

Nowadays, the cohabitation of unmarried men and women before marriage no longer arouses excessive curiosity among people. However, women have always been puzzled: what is the real purpose of men's persistent request for cohabitation?

In love, it is understandable that most men demand premarital sex. However, specific situations need to be analyzed specifically. In general, there are four main reasons why men require premarital sex:

Firstly, to verify whether the partner is a virgin.

In modern society, although being a virgin is not necessarily a compliment, almost every man hopes to marry a virgin. Men with a "virgin complex" still exist in large numbers. Based on this complex, some men will go to great lengths, regardless of the woman's repeated opposition and refusal, to insist on "testing her virginity."

If the test confirms that she is a virgin, then both parties are happy, and the man can dispel his premarital concerns. If the test shows otherwise, he might make other plans, or even avoid breaking up. In short, they do not allow themselves to take any risk of marrying a non-virgin. The more open the society becomes, the fewer virgins there are, and the more they want to take this "peace-of-mind pill."

Our view on this is: perhaps the man's "virgin complex" is understandable, even pitiful. But disregarding the woman's feelings, engaging in selfish and even despicable conspiracies for personal gain, is extremely sad and shameful.

Secondly, driven by excitement, unable to wait.

I have always believed that men who have not experienced sexual activity are mostly immature. This is because they cannot understand from certain details how a woman should be caressed by a man. This kind of caressing has its limits; it involves both indulgence and restraint, both gentleness and wildness.

For a woman who has not married you and has not yet decided or is unwilling to give her body to you, perhaps all she can offer is understanding, tolerance, and patience.

In our opinion, if this point is achieved, it reflects an even deeper form of love. It is this love that makes them rational, able to suppress their excitement, and willing to wait despite their eagerness. They learn to balance desire with responsibility, and this maturity is far more reliable, long-lasting, and powerful than the maturity gained solely through sexual activity.

Thirdly, believing that only by giving oneself does one truly love the other.

Frankly speaking, I still don't understand the twisted logic popular on the internet —— "Love him, so give yourself to him." So I can't help but ask: Is it really necessary for a woman who loves a man to definitely give her body to him, and must this happen before marriage when he cannot provide her with any security? I think such a judgment is neither logical nor emotionally sound. There is no love without cause, just as there is no unconditional giving.

The giving of anything is only reliable when it is done willingly, and only genuine when it is water reaching the channel. Otherwise, any forced or reluctant actions are immoral and laughable.

To all men who hold such thoughts, I would like to say: If you truly love her and want her, treat her well, show her your sincerity, resolve all her worries, and eliminate any doubts she may have, so that she gives herself to you willingly and without hesitation.

Fourthly, testing the harmony of post-marriage sex life.

In real life, although the failure of a marriage appears to have many reasons on the surface, more than half of these failures are related to a lack of harmony in sexual life. Therefore, from this perspective, I am not entirely against men demanding premarital sex. Unfortunately, in reality, very few men have purely this thought. They may focus more on their own pleasure during sexual activity, often neglecting whether the woman experiences happiness. Their so-called testing of post-marriage sexual harmony mostly stems from personal feelings.

Of course, it must be pointed out that the harmony of sexual life between men and women cannot be simply verified by short-term cohabitation before marriage. Everyone has different physiological cycles, and living environments and work pressures are constantly changing. More importantly, sexual techniques can largely compensate for disharmony. Therefore, this reason, while not entirely baseless, is mostly just an excuse.
