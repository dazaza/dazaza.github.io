Love you, Female Section Chief Embezzled 30 Million Yuan to Trade Stocks and Fled to Canada for 8 Years Before Being Persuaded to Return

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-07 09:10:57

As the finance department director in the South Sea, she embezzled 30 million yuan of public funds to trade stocks and then quietly returned the money before moving to Canada.

On the afternoon of February 21, 2012, Lin, who had fled to Canada for eight years on suspicion of misappropriation of public funds, arrived at Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport according to her agreement with the procuratorate, and then went to the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate of Foshan City with the prosecutors waiting there for investigation.

Lin never expected that when she moved to Canada in 2003 with admiration from others, she would return to her hometown in such a way.

In 1997, Lin was the director of the finance department of the Nanshan Housing Fund Management Company (now the Nanhua District People's Government Housing Fund Management Center). On July 24 of the same year, she and Li Mou, deputy manager of the company, embezzled 30 million yuan of housing funds from the center, and then applied to subscribe to new shares of "China Pan-Travel" under the names of companies such as "Yadi Decoration" and "Greenland Decoration". After the successful subscription of new shares, they quietly returned the 30 million yuan on July 28 of the same year. In September of the same year, Lin received 100,000 yuan from the proceeds of subscribing to new shares.

Due to fear of being wiretapped, Lin gradually stopped contacting her friends, and due to concerns about getting into trouble, Lin spoke and acted very cautiously. Gradually, her personality became sensitive and suspicious, and she began to experience symptoms such as neurasthenia and hypertension. Her social circle in Canada became narrower and narrower, and finally only her son remained.

She thought everything could be done secretly, but in 2004, when the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate investigated Li, who was already the director of the Nanhai Housing Fund Management Center, for embezzlement and corruption, this criminal fact was discovered. By this time, Lin had already moved to Canada in 2003 and was in the process of applying for citizenship. Facing the requirement from the Nanhai District Procuratorate to return to China for investigation, Lin resolutely refused. She believed that since she had returned the misappropriated funds and was abroad, the Nanhai District Procuratorate could not do anything to her, and she would not "stupidly" turn herself in.

In May 2004, the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate submitted an application for assistance in investigation and extradition of Lin through the judicial assistance office of the Guangdong Provincial People's Procuratorate to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Liaison Office in China. Since China and Canada have not signed an extradition treaty, China's repatriation request was politely rejected as expected. However, the Canadian police also suspended the processing of Lin's citizenship application, blocking her attempt to evade legal prosecution by obtaining Canadian citizenship.

Considering the special circumstances of the case, after research, the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate believed that pressure should not be the only approach towards Lin; instead, a combination of soft and hard measures should be used to help her overcome psychological obstacles and return to China for investigation.

Thus, over the next eight years, despite changes in leadership from the chief prosecutor and anti-corruption leaders to the heads of the anti-corruption bureau and investigation department, the habit of calling Lin to greet her during holidays and visiting her nearly eighty-year-old mother did not change.

Lin's mother lived in her old home in Nanhai. Approaching eighty, her vision had severely deteriorated, and she relied on touch for daily tasks like buying groceries and cooking. Prosecutors from the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate often visited Lin's mother to keep her company, conveying their care and sincerity to Lin across the ocean.

According to Lin's recollection, in her impression, the prosecutors on the other end of the phone were always polite and factual. They not only explained policies, laws, and principles to her but also helped her analyze the pros and cons from her perspective, gradually changing her thoughts.

In September 2011, the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate successfully prosecuted another important person, Li, involved in the housing management fund series case through Sino-Australian judicial cooperation in Australia. This news greatly shook Lin's thoughts.

After thorough discussion and analysis, the Nanhai District People's Procuratorate formed a persuasion team and flew to Canada to meet with Lin. They prepared brochures and materials about Nanhai, recorded a video of Lin's mother, and bought local specialties.

Finally, Lin expressed her intention to return to the prosecutors and flew back to Guangzhou from Canada on February 21, 2012. This eight-year-long persuasion work was thus brought to a successful conclusion.
