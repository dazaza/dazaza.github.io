"Love Beyond Reach" - A Time-Traveling Woman's Redemption in Love

by jqrxass on 2009-09-28 16:21:00

On the eve of the wedding, Song Huan Yu discovered the double betrayal of her fiancé and best friend.

With difficulty to let go, she drowned her sorrows in drink, only to exchange souls with another heartbroken person from another time - Dong Yujie, the daughter of a certain dynasty's Grand Tutor...

Dong Yujie boldly proposed marriage to Xie Shenyan, the first talent of the capital city, but was rejected.

Xie Shenyan's father was framed by others, his official position was stripped, and the Xie family fell into ruin. The rejected Dong Yujie turned love into hatred, privately imprisoning Xie Shenyan in a separate courtyard, torturing him repeatedly, leading to his disability.

Waking up drunk from a dream, Dong Yujie had become Song Huan Yu. She felt compassion for the battered Shenyan, tenderly caring for him, asking about his well-being. However, Shenyan remained cold and silent towards her. Amidst intertwined love and hate, feelings grew secretly between them, yet their identities were vastly different, and rumors spread... Their love was full of thorns; how could they hold each other's hand and grow old together?

Unyielding Love Part 1

The night I crossed over, I was drunk. In fact, I only drank a bottle of red wine, but since my stomach was empty while drinking, I felt nauseous and couldn't vomit, just feeling dizzy and disoriented, falling backward onto the bed. In a daze, I floated in a black corridor. The darkness wasn't scary, it was calm and comforting, allowing my exhausted soul to take a breath. Vaguely, I sensed another soul also floating in the dark passing by me, equally laden with weariness, despair, and sorrow. When I opened my eyes again, it was bright. My head hurt so much I wanted to throw up, my eyes were dry and uncomfortable. Looking at the canopy embroidered with phoenixes flying through clouds and flowers blooming, I knew I wasn't in my own bed anymore. Afraid that moving would make my headache worse, I dared not sit up, only turning my eyes to look around. This glance made me sit up immediately, causing me to scream out loud due to the headache. Holding my head, I took another look, hoping it was an illusion caused by the headache, but the terrifying scene didn't disappear. There was a naked man, bloodied and hanging just a few steps outside my bed. His head hung low on his chest, his messy hair covering his face, his body covered in whip marks and burns, his feet half a foot off the ground, pointing to a small pool of black blood on the floor.

Unyielding Love Part 2

Nearly a month passed, and this day was sunny and beautiful, the spring light was lush. I wore a light yellow blouse, wrapped in a pale red brocade quilt, holding "The Analects" and leaning back on a wooden recliner on the porch reading. Xinghua sat nearby on a small stool doing needlework. I read very slowly, forgetting what I read quickly afterward. This is the realm of scholars, so one book can be read many times. The books here are thread-bound, some even handwritten. Not to mention the complex characters, there aren't even punctuation marks. I chose "The Analects" because its famous quotes are still commonly used in modern daily life, some of which I could understand, so I conveniently learned traditional characters along the way. After reading for a while, the words in the book seemed to blur together. I pulled out a hairpin from my head, letting my hair fall down to cover my shoulders. I used the tip of the pin to point at the sentence breaks, struggling to digest those words. Upon reaching a certain part, I sighed deeply, using the pin to tap the scroll in my hand, staring blankly without seeing anything. The spring breeze brushed past, a strand or two of hair fluttered onto the scroll.

Unyielding Love Part 3

I marveled, it seems people from noble families really are different. Even when fallen on hard times, they still carry themselves with grace, naturally commanding service from others. As Xinghua was about to do the same, I reached for chopsticks and a spoon myself. Though I had no appetite, I knew the rules of the household required the master and servant not to eat together. Even outside these formalities, the master must start eating first, so I hurriedly began drinking porridge. Xinghua and Uncle Li also started eating. Out of the corner of my eye, I saw Xie Shenyan slowly lift his hand, pick up the spoon, and begin eating as well. I sighed in relief; if he refused to eat in front of me, I might have left immediately.

Unyielding Love Part 4

We all ate dinner in silence. Xie Shenyan ate very slowly, keeping every bite in his mouth for a long time before swallowing. I feared he was having trouble swallowing due to fish bones. Qian Yan didn't touch anything in front of Xie Shenyan until after he put down his chopsticks. After we finished eating, Qian Yan poured all the leftovers on the table into his large bowl. He picked up the leftover fish with his hands, sucking and slurping until the meat was gone in an instant, biting the fish head into pieces, spitting out the tiny bones like grape skins quickly, then satisfiedly tossing a perfect fish bone frame onto the table, more professional than any cat. Xinghua opened her mouth, but we were all trapped in the oppressive atmosphere at the table, none of us saying anything.

Unyielding Love Part 5

Qian Yan's words played repeatedly in my ears, and I kept thinking about how Xie Shenyan wouldn't let me see his wrist. Due to my low spirits, I've been taking care of him these days to distract myself. He never explicitly showed rejection, giving me at least a bit of face. But if I were to say, as Qian Yan hinted, that he likes me, I simply couldn't believe it. It doesn't make sense psychologically or logically; who could endure such humiliation and disability without harboring deep hatred? That young lady is gone, if he doesn't hate me, then who does he hate? His father and brother? He's met them; my brother treated him kindly, and my father even told him to relax. These all indicate they indeed didn't interfere... Qian Yan said he's constantly worried, which can only be because he laments his own injuries. Xinghua didn't tell Qian Yan about that incident; if Qian Yan knew the truth, he'd realize Xie Shenyan's sighs have nothing to do with me. Thinking it over, I warned myself not to indulge in self-pity, otherwise, I'd only humiliate myself.
