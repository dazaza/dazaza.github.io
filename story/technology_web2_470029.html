Behind the smiles, it's the scene of killing dogs.

Source: Zhengzhou Evening News

Are these people from Zhengzhou? Why was this post spread across major websites days ago?

Evening News reporter Honggang Dong

Yesterday, a local website posted on its homepage with the title "Appalling! Zhengzhou post-90s men and women skin live dogs and take selfies."

When clicked open, the title was changed to "Appalling! A group of post-90s men and women skin live dogs in the street," the two characters for "Zhengzhou" disappeared.

A search revealed a bunch of web pages popping up, most with titles like "Appalling! A group of post-90s men and women skin live dogs in the street," which had already spread throughout Mop, NetEase forum, Daqi website, Tiexue website, etc., with some websites having over ten thousand replies. The word "Zhengzhou" did not appear in any of the titles.

There were 7 photos in the post, showing several young people tearing at dog legs and fur, quite bloody. In one photo, a girl smiled easily, with the background showing the scene of killing a dog; what made netizens most unable to continue looking was a photo where three youths spread their blood-stained hands, making ghost faces.

The poster said, "I don't know if it's out of fun or to show off! Were they not afraid at all during the time of skinning? Girls who usually scream for help when seeing a cockroach, why wasn't it you who got skinned alive!"

Netizen comments: Expose them

The reporter reviewed various posts, and the click rate for this post was very high, with most netizens issuing accusations.

"People are the cruelest animals in the world, the cruelest and most perverted ones are just like those people..." a Suzhou netizen said many extreme words.

Netizen "Shanghai Laoshu" believed that this was a return to barbarism, "back to the wilderness, raw swallowing and living peeling. People dominate the top of the food chain, primitive exposure."

What was most unacceptable was the smug look on the girl in the photo. "Were they not afraid at all during the time of skinning? Girls who usually scream for help when seeing a cockroach!" Some netizens couldn't help but exclaim.

Many criticized the irrational actions of the post-90s generation, some netizens accused part of the post-90s generation of lacking necessary conscience, wanting to "expose" them.

Some netizens: Scenes of animal slaughter are everywhere

There were also netizens who believed that similar bloody scenes of animal slaughter were very common.

A netizen from Shandong replied, "Pretense is needed, now people, seeing a skinned dog and saying it's cruel. Have you really not eaten dog meat? Have you not eaten chicken, duck, fish, etc.? Why pretend to pity animals here!"

Netizen "LS": "Street killings of dogs have been reported by many media outlets, such scenes are not uncommon in our country. There is no need to criticize these young people, the key is to improve the larger environment." He said, the key is to truly respect animals, to truly see animals as human friends.

Website response: It's uncertain whether they're from Zhengzhou

Upon investigation by the reporter, this post was made by netizen "Duan Zui Zhe," and the post did not say they were from Zhengzhou.

The posting date for this local website was "November 10, 2010," and there were words like "Phoenix, NetEase Forum" on the photos.

The reporter found that as early as November 6, this post had appeared on NetEase Forum. Many Henan netizens also questioned whether this incident occurred in Zhengzhou.

Are these young people from Zhengzhou?

In the afternoon yesterday, the reporter called the customer service hotline of this website, and a male staff member said, "This was posted by a netizen, it's uncertain whether they are from Zhengzhou."

Some netizens said that the young people in the photo didn't look like post-90s, they were somewhat older, don't push all bad things onto the post-90s.

Some netizens suspected this was a performance, many people want to become famous, using some radical behaviors to attract societal attention, and becoming famous through it.

Where exactly are the people in the photo from, and what was their motive? Currently, it cannot be determined.

However, as netizen "David" said, we hope these young people come forward and apologize.
