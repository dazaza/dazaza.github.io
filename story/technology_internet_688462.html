The mortgage rate for first home of the Big Four banks will not be higher than the benchmark rate

by loved7p7 on 2012-03-03 10:50:52

Xinhua News Agency (Su Manli, intern Lou Sailing) - Yesterday, we learned from a state-owned bank that the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (4.43, 0.02, 0.45%), Agricultural Bank of China (2.74, 0.00, 0.00%), Bank of China (3.07, 0.01, 0.33%), and China Construction Bank (4.89, 0.03, 0.62%) have recently jointly studied differentiated mortgage policies, which mention [truly meeting the loan demand for residents' first purchase of self-occupied ordinary commercial housing, reasonably pricing according to the benchmark interest rate within the range based on risk principles]. This means that the first-home mortgage rates of the four major banks will not exceed the benchmark rate.

Many banks have returned to the benchmark rate

Previously in 2011, due to tight funds and other reasons, some banks implemented first-home loan rates at 1.1 times or 1.05 times the benchmark rate, significantly increasing the cost of purchasing a first home. However, since the beginning of this year, with the increase in bank credit quotas, bank mortgage policies have subtly changed.

In October last year, the Construction Bank increased the first-home mortgage rate by 5%, implementing it at 1.05 times the benchmark rate. However, starting from the end of January, the bank began to implement the benchmark rate. Another bank that raised the first-home rate last year — Shenzhen Development Bank — also reduced it to the benchmark rate starting February 1st. It is understood that currently, all four major banks are implementing the benchmark rate for first-home loans. Other joint-stock banks mostly use the benchmark rate as a basis, with some applicants experiencing a certain increase based on their credit status. Some foreign banks offer a 95% discount.

This year's loan applications are much better than last year

In addition to keeping the rate within the benchmark rate, the four major banks have also explicitly improved loan approval efficiency. It is understood that the Construction Bank requires a maximum of two weeks for mortgage approvals. The Industrial and Commercial Bank of China and the Bank of China take about one week for approvals. However, during the third and fourth quarters of last year, due to tight quotas, borrowers had to wait 1-2 months before they could apply for loans.

A bank staff member informed us that the situation is much better now compared to the second half of last year. Last year, due to a lack of quota, anxious buyers had to pay more to get their loans processed quickly. Now, at least the factor of quota is gone. As long as income is stable and there are no significant issues, it is relatively easy to get approved. The interest rate follows the policy of the head office, generally implementing the upper limit of the policy.

First-home buyers see a reduction in payment costs

Zhang Dawei, research director of Centaline Property, indicated that in 2011, some actual self-residential purchasing needs saw their payment costs significantly increase as rates went from a 70% discount to the benchmark rate or even higher, affecting the entry of self-residential needs into the market. With the easing of first-home mortgage policies, this benefits genuine first-time homebuyers who can enjoy the advantages brought by price adjustments due to regulation.

It is understood that apart from differentiated credit policies for first homes, the four major banks clearly support well-qualified developers in constructing ordinary commercial housing with market demand, increasing the effective supply of ordinary commercial housing. Real estate stocks quickly responded, and yesterday real estate stocks became the leading sector in the A-share market. From the index trend, the Shanghai real estate index surged 3.73%, while the Shenzhen real estate index soared 4.22%.

■ Background

Regulatory authorities frequently signal support for first-home purchases

Since the end of last year, regulatory authorities have shown a "differentiated" tendency in credit policies for first-home purchases.

On December 23, 2011, Minister of Housing and Urban-Rural Development Jiang Weixin stated that next year, real estate control must continue without wavering, strictly implementing differentiated housing credit and tax policies, supporting reasonable housing demands of residents, and prioritizing the loan needs of first-time homebuyers.

In February 2012, the People's Bank of China announced on its official website that in terms of mortgages, the central bank will continue to implement differentiated housing credit policies, supporting the construction of ordinary commercial housing and meeting the loan needs of first-time homebuyers. Following the Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development, this again signaled satisfaction of first-home mortgage demands, further increasing market expectations for the relaxation of first-home interest rates.

Calculation

A million yuan mortgage at a 90% discount saves 100,000 yuan in interest

"If there could be a discount, it would save a lot of money, and our home-buying pressure would be reduced quite a bit," said Miss Shen, who is currently house-hunting. After working for two years, Miss Shen plans to get married but has been worried about not having bought a house yet.

Currently, under the mortgage policies of many banks, the down payment for a first home is 30%, with the benchmark interest rate applied. For example, if borrowing 1 million yuan over 20 years, if the benchmark interest rate is applied, the monthly payment would be 7783 yuan, with total interest of 867,927 yuan. However, if there is a 90% discount, the monthly payment decreases to 7364.76 yuan, and the total interest reduces by 100,385 yuan to 767,542 yuan.

Miss Shen stated that although this cannot compare with the conditions several years ago when there was a 70% discount on interest rates, for the just-in-demand group, a discounted first-home mortgage rate compared to the previous 1.1 times or 1.05 times rates, whether in terms of monthly payments or total interest, reduces repayment pressure accordingly.
