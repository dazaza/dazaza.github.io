LED Display: High-end looks at the brand, low-end goes through channels [Lianchengfa]

by lcfledcom on 2012-02-16 10:37:44

As people gain a deeper understanding of the concept of LED, the LED display industry enters a fast lane of rapid development. With a mix of companies (mainly referring to production-oriented companies) vying for market share and greater profits, each company devises its own unique strategies. From the current industry situation, companies in this field can be divided into high-end and low-end categories. Here, the terms "high" and "low" do not refer to the inherent quality of the products but rather to the relative technical complexity of the products, namely full-color LED displays versus single or dual-color LED displays. How do companies with different positioning compete for their respective markets? To summarize: high-end focuses on brand, while low-end relies on channels.

### How High-End Brand Companies Establish Their Brands

#### Advertising Promotion

There are specialized professional brand planning teams or companies dedicated to promoting them, including systematic planning and promotion from online platforms to industry websites or relevant journals, as well as from trade shows to related media.

#### Project Establishment

By undertaking relatively influential LED screen projects, they create a perception in public opinion that they only handle large-scale projects, thereby enhancing the company's high-end brand image.

#### Public Relations Momentum

With favorable policy support and government personnel conducting surveys and inspections, they secure strong positive publicity from influential media outlets.

#### Own Brand Extension

Some companies previously engaged in products related to LED displays. Seeing the market potential, they leverage their strong brand advantages to extend into this industry.

#### Regional Advantage Established Early On

Especially in foreign markets when the industry was just starting out a few years ago, some companies quickly established brand recognition. Now returning to the local market, these companies still possess significant competitiveness.

Currently, many peer companies rely heavily on the powerful push of sales personnel, spreading the company's brand, positioning, and products nationwide through various projects. Some companies focus exclusively on a specific type of product, such as indoor three-in-one full-color LED displays or three-to-one full-color LED displays, to capture niche markets.

High-end brands generally do not have agents or set up distribution channels, but it is undeniable that they often establish local offices responsible for assisting local engineering firms with project finalization and post-project maintenance.

#### Low-End Relying on Channels

The current situation involves casting a wide net across the country, setting up points everywhere. However, due to overexpansion, there is some disorder, with occasional occurrences of unauthorized shipments or speculative behavior. The strategy used is to lure customers with low prices and gradually infiltrate the market with their brand; by attracting attention through low prices, they then play word games within the industry. From a market perspective, their influence and market share are still quite good, and I believe they should serve as models for other peer companies at the same level.

The larger the LED display market becomes, the fiercer the competition. To maintain one’s advantage and remain competitive, it is necessary to first examine the product positioning and industry status of one’s own company, avoid blind actions, and ensure a clear direction.

The target customer base for low-end products includes engineering firms (usually dealing primarily with whole screens, sometimes assembling their own screens before installation), advertising and lighting companies (projects are generally not large, they mostly assemble their own screens, sourcing from local wholesalers or directly from manufacturers if the volume is high), and LED wholesalers (mainly located in major first-tier cities, with regional advantages, especially wholesalers in Zhengzhou, Henan). Different strategies need to be adopted for different customer groups, along with varying price protection measures, which require in-depth research.
