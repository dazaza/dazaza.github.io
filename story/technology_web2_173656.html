SEO Soft Article Case - Looking at the Recovery of Shanghai's Decoration Industry from the Perspective of People's Consumption Ability

by longersing on 2009-07-09 13:24:24

As a very practical tactic in the process of online promotion and SEO, soft articles have always been favored by webmasters. This issue will bring you a case of soft article from SEO Tribe, teaching you how to write a high-quality article that can not only promote your website but also gain the appreciation of portals and authoritative departments.

By Longer (SEO Tribe)

What is a soft article? I believe everyone has already had some understanding through checking online materials, and you often see some soft article works on the Internet. And I will summarize it in a few simple sentences: An article that obviously looks like a soft article is at best considered as a third-party advertising medium. However, what we aim to achieve today is to make you, as a browser, have the following feelings after reading the article:

1. This is an authoritative report, concerning social, economic and other factors;

2. It relates to problems I may encounter in certain aspects in the future;

3. It's definitely worth my time to read;

4. After reading, I will look at issues based on the viewpoints mentioned in the article;

5. In the future, when I encounter problems, I might even come back to consult the authoritative institutions mentioned in this article.

In fact, soft articles are just that simple. As a webmaster, you may need to use them. As an SEOer, you must be able to write them.

Comment analysis and product review type soft articles are more popular among portals. These types of articles usually have a higher click-through rate because the products involved in these reviews indeed have a large number of "information seekers" on the Internet. For example, when we need to buy something, we first search online for detailed parameters of the product. The same principle applies: Soft articles are created for this search result, boasting about and showcasing ourselves in front of the demanders.

Portals prefer analytical articles that focus on specific events. These articles have a broad audience and contain fewer advertisements, so they can meet the requirements of most portals and some small to medium-sized comprehensive websites.

Nowadays, there are still many soft articles that are highly favored by merchants: such as embedding product names into articles with story plots; replacing and modifying certain keywords of official information for a product; using mythological stories to introduce the importance of modern products; pretending to belittle a product under the guise of discussing industry impacts related to certain regulations (actually exploiting readers' curiosity - "those who don't understand will search for the product"); etc. However, these soft articles do not perform well in actual promotions, especially those articles where links are added to the keywords. Unless written smoothly, they may instead damage the product image because many people, in order to meet the keyword density and add links, even turn the article into nonsensical "Chinese dictionaries".
