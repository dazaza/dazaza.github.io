The 20 Most Romantic Films in Movie History

by qingchuyulan on 2010-04-10 22:00:14

1. Sleepless in Seattle (1993)

Starring: Tom Hanks and Meg Ryan

"That movie made us trust even more in the magic of love, that we all have different destinies, and that the life paths of people who are meant to be together will converge."

2. Titanic (1997)

Starring: Leonardo DiCaprio and Kate Winslet

"Everyone dreams of having this kind of perfect love, experiencing that fleeting spark. And the scene in the movie where the elderly couple lies in bed, holding each other's hands, calmly waiting for death, almost moved me to tears."

3. Gone with the Wind (1939)

Starring: Clark Gable and Vivien Leigh

"Every man wants to be Rhett Butler, always surrounded by a group of women, and Clark Gable is every woman's dream lover."

4. Casablanca (1942)

Starring: Humphrey Bogart and Ingrid Bergman

"The tragic tone of this film, its extreme dramatic nature, Ingrid Bergman's sorrowful eyes and flawless face, almost broke my heart."

5. Pretty Woman (1990)

Starring: Richard Gere and Julia Roberts

"Each of us is like an unpolished gem; what we need is to find the right person to polish us, turning us into real, shining diamonds. This movie gives us hope in finding our ideal partner."

6. When Harry Met Sally (1989)

Starring: Billy Crystal and Meg Ryan

"The most unforgettable part of this movie is on New Year's Eve when everyone is immersed in the celebration, and Billy Crystal runs to Meg Ryan's house. His words were brilliant: 'When you finally realize you're in love with someone, you don't want to spend another night without her.'"

7. Ghost (1990)

Starring: Patrick Swayze and Demi Moore

"True love never dies."

8. An Affair to Remember (1957)

Starring: Cary Grant and Deborah Kerr

"Every time I see Cary Grant understand why Deborah Kerr couldn't meet him at the Empire State Building, I can't hold back my tears—it’s so moving!"

9. Romeo and Juliet (1968, 1996)

Starring: Leonard Whiting and Olivia Hussey (1968 version); Leonardo DiCaprio and Claire Danes (1996 version)

"This movie is almost indescribable with mere words. Even if you know the ending from the start, it still holds no relief."

10. City of Angels (1998)

Starring: Nicolas Cage and Meg Ryan

"Someone would give up eternity for love, which is incredibly touching."

11. You've Got Mail (1998)

Starring: Tom Hanks and Meg Ryan

"This is how many people meet today; we might also encounter someone online whom we've never met in real life. Meg Ryan and Tom Hanks are just a perfect match."

12. Love Story (1970)

Starring: Ryan O'Neal and Ali MacGraw

"The film shows the true meaning of conditional love—taking care of the one you love."

13. Somewhere in Time (1980)

Starring: Christopher Reeve and Jane Seymour

"This movie has a heartstring-pulling power, making us believe even more in the concept of fate between people."

14. The Bridges of Madison County (1995)

Starring: Clint Eastwood and Meryl Streep

"Every married man has such a fantasy, but only a few get the chance. Only mature women can truly appreciate this movie; seventeen or eighteen-year-old girls lack the life experience to fully understand it."

15. Dirty Dancing (1987)

Starring: Patrick Swayze and Jennifer Grey

"The story between good girls and bad boys is always fascinating."

16. While You Were Sleeping (1995)

Starring: Bill Pullman and Sandra Bullock

"When you fall in love with someone, you cannot recognize your true love. This movie is genuinely believable, and it ends with a hopeful grand reunion!"

17. Hope Floats (1998)

Starring: Billy Crystal and Sandra Bullock

"It shows that despite emotional setbacks, there are still many people who care about you, and some may even love you more."

18. French Kiss (1995)

Starring: Meg Ryan and Kevin Kline

"True love means letting go at the right time, as long as the person you love is happy. And the sound effects in this film are particularly great."

19. Sabrina (1954, 1995)

Starring: Humphrey Bogart, Audrey Hepburn, and William Holden (1954 version); Harrison Ford, Julia Ormond, and Greg Kinnear (1995 version)

"Cinderella stories are enchanting, but princesses may need to be a bit smarter."

20. An Officer and a Gentleman (1982)

Starring: Richard Gere and Debra Winger

"Debra Winger was saved from the monotony of factory life, and Richard Gere out of his military uniform is just incredibly handsome!"
