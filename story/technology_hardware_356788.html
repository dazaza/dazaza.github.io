When learning English, it's important to make good use of situational modes.

by aiourstudy on 2010-05-29 06:42:25

Being busy at work, having limited time to study, lacking a language environment and sufficient practice opportunities, these are all unfavorable factors that white-collar workers face when learning English. To overcome the various difficulties of learning English, it might be helpful to listen to the advice given by professionals.

First Strategy: Choose Learning Content Based on Your Goals

A white-collar worker from a certain company, Xiao Zheng, believes that the lack of time and energy is a major obstacle for white-collar workers learning English. "Working people have many things to do, often missing classes because of being busy with work, and there's no time to make up for the missed lessons. After missing too many classes, the motivation to learn is lost, and in the end, it's left unresolved. The tuition fee is wasted."

Liu Jinquan, the course advisor of Qu Gang Quickstep English, believes that there are too many related contents in English learning, and white-collar workers with limited time need to find their own learning direction. Many white-collar workers have this misconception when learning English, which is to take the most complete English courses as soon as they think about studying English, hoping to learn the most perfect English, but often end up feeling overwhelmed.

Anna, the course advisor of New Dynamics International English, introduced that white-collar workers who have already mastered the basics don't need to start over again; they can focus on learning specialized business English, using listening and speaking practice to drive the improvement of reading and writing skills.

For most white-collar workers, mastering necessary business English, such as business etiquette, dining, product introduction, booking tickets, attending meetings, hosting meetings, shopping, asking for directions, etc., is enough to deal with possible English problems in daily work and life, allowing them to handle situations where English is needed with ease. This allows white-collar workers to see the progress in their English learning, find motivation, and build confidence.

Second Strategy: Make Good Use of Time to Simplify Complexities

The contradiction between fast-paced work and life and English learning makes white-collar workers even more lacking in sufficient time for English learning. In the situation where English learning time constantly gives way to work, it becomes difficult to quickly master English.

Regarding the problem of insufficient English learning time for white-collar workers, English training professionals believe that white-collar English learning must simplify complexities and make good use of scattered time during daily work to complete learning. Therefore, it's best to choose courses that do not require fixed English learning times, so as not to give up helplessly when conflicts arise between learning English and work. After giving up once or twice, it basically means giving up English for a long time. For example, New Dynamics International English implements small-class teaching, with each class consisting of 4 people, flexibly arranging study time based on the students' situations. However, there are strict requirements for the weekly class time, prompting students to study through rigid regulations.

White-collar workers should also pay attention to choosing easy and convenient English courses. Easy refers to content that is interesting and useful, while convenient means that the course content should be relatively independent short segments. For instance, some English courses only require 20 minutes to help learners complete the English learning of a scene, making learners feel relaxed and not bored.

This kind of English learning takes place in a relaxed and joyful atmosphere. For white-collar workers with limited time, it does not require a large amount of fixed time or money to learn English. By utilizing casual leisure time and a small expense, continuous English learning can be achieved, offering a good cost-performance ratio.

Third Strategy: Create an Environment for Using English

In fact, many white-collar workers have no difficulty reading English articles, but they cannot speak fluent English. Experts believe that the reason for this situation is that white-collar workers generally possess some basic English knowledge, but lack practical application of this knowledge, causing them to only "recognize" English but struggle to "speak" it.

Since learners are in China, they lack the necessary language environment and rarely come into contact with real foreign life scenarios. Moreover, there is no atmosphere or environment for using English at home or at work.

In fact, many English training institutions have specially set up courses simulating real language environments, creating an authentic English life atmosphere for learners. The materials selected are close to life, light-hearted and practical, able to stimulate learners' interest and make learning enjoyable.

For example, the englishtown teaching system developed by EF Education emphasizes real-life communication. The iLab allows unlimited communication with foreign teachers, and Career Services provide various English exchange activities, all reflecting the functions of the English circle. The multimedia learning software developed by New Dynamics International English has authentic situational dialogues and has a shadowing function, enabling students to quickly enter the language learning environment.

Additionally, there is even less atmosphere for English learning at home. In this case, Qu Gang Quickstep English suggests that learners can adopt the method of memorizing articles for self-study, or after reading an article and understanding its general meaning, try to retell or write it down in their own words. This will also yield good learning results.
