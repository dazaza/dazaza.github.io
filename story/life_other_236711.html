Eight money-saving home装修 tricks you must learn

by longersing on 2009-09-11 18:28:32

Eight money-saving home decoration tips you must learn, from decoration facilities, material selection, purchase and production to design, etc., all aspects have ways to save money. Let us teach you these ways. As follows.

1. Display cabinets without side panels

To save money on carpentry, you can consider not installing side panels. The function of cabinet side panels is mainly for moisture prevention and weight bearing. If the wall does not have water seepage problems and heavy objects will not be placed, the side panels can be omitted. For example, the display cabinet in the master bedroom is mainly for the homeowner to place personal collections and cosmetics, so there is no need for side panels, which saves money and maintains aesthetics.

2. Use domestic kitchen appliances

To save money in the kitchen, you should focus on kitchen appliances and wall/floor tiles. Considering the budget, you can use all domestically produced goods.

3. Three-sided aluminum windows are the most cost-effective

On the balcony wall, there was originally an aluminum window installed, but due to its old age, it needed to be removed and replaced with a new one. The most economical way for aluminum windows is to use three-sided aluminum windows paired with 5mm thick tempered glass. If you choose grid-style aluminum windows, the price will be more than double.

4. Grid glass instead of grid sliding doors

For the sliding door leading to the back balcony, the homeowner originally hoped to install a grid-style aluminum sliding door, but the budget was insufficient. Therefore, the designer used grid glass as a substitute, achieving the same effect as a grid sliding door while saving more than half the cost.

5. Partitioned dressing room

Creating a dressing room is cheaper than making a wardrobe. The designer "borrowed" nearly 1 ping (1 ping equals 3.3 square meters) of space from the children's room, partitioning it into a dressing room using plywood. For aesthetics, the designer also specially carved out holes in the side panel for styling.

6. Raised floor as bed frames

Since the homeowner has two daughters, at least one double bed or two single beds are needed in the bedroom. The designer considered that since wooden flooring was going to be installed, why not raise the floor and place two single mattresses directly on the floor, thereby saving the cost of two single bed frames. Although raising the floor requires additional costs, it is still cheaper compared to buying two bed frames.

7. Polished quartz tiles instead of marble flooring

The homeowner liked the smooth texture of marble and hoped to have marble flooring in the living room. However, even without installation costs, the material cost of marble is very expensive and the budget did not allow it. The designer then suggested using polished quartz tiles as a substitute, which saved quite a bit of money.

8. Shoe cabinet instead of entrance screen

To prevent guests from seeing the living room at a glance when entering, the homeowner hoped to install an entrance screen. Considering practicality and budget, the designer suggested using a shoe cabinet as a replacement. Although the shoe cabinet is more expensive than the entrance screen, it is more practical. Most importantly, it also saves the cost of the entrance screen.
