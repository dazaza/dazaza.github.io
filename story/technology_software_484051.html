A Chrome browser plugin that synchronously publishes status updates to Google+ and Facebook (with G+ invitation attached)

by xdash on 2011-08-31 20:23:13

Google's latest high-profile social service, Google+, which was released not long ago, is said to have already reached 27 million users, a very encouraging figure. However, there are also quite a few industry observers who believe that "there is no community in the world that can be built overnight." Due to a lack of sufficiently practical innovation and humanistic care concepts, the overnight success of Google+ may end up being all bark and no bite, starting strong but failing to deliver in the long run.

At least for me personally, I haven't yet immersed myself fully into using it as a product, but rather more so for experiencing its novelty and maintaining contact with my geek friends. In the absence of enough female users and celebrity users, those who initially rushed in and stayed for an extended period are mostly Google's tech-savvy fans. Perhaps, as mentioned by a senior member of the G team in public statements, "the worst outcome would be that Google+ becomes a geek community." Hopefully, this won't turn out to be true.

Let's get back to the point and introduce today's Google Chrome extension —— Google+ & Facebook Status Sync (I must say, developers, couldn't you have come up with a shorter, catchier name...).

This extension can achieve two-way synchronization of statuses between four major social networking sites: Google+, Facebook, Twitter, and Plurk. After installation and binding, when you post a status on any of these four sites, you can check boxes under the input field to select other platforms for simultaneous posting (the ability to manually select sync targets is excellent, as choosing appropriate channels for sharing relevant insights is responsible to both yourself and your readers). As shown in the following image:

Some points to note:

After installation, conflicts with some previously installed extensions might occur, causing the extension to crash. It took me two attempts to successfully install.

After installation, by default, a line like "I'm using XXX" will be written to your G+ status, which you can either delete manually or leave as a recommendation to your friends.

Supports designated Google+ circles. Does not support Facebook's Fans Page.
