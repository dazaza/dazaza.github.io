Farmer borrows money to accomplish fire fighting mission and wins prize of a fire engine after 22 years (Picture)

by tmyb9304 on 2012-03-05 10:10:50

"Why do you need money? Firefighting is still the priority." Ruan Bingyan said, though it was a bit of hardship, as long as he could still move, he would always save money from his meager resources to put out fires. From the earliest ten-horsepower fire pump to now three fire pumps, two electric tricycles, one handcart, twenty-two reels of fire hose, in 2007, he even borrowed hundreds of thousands of yuan to buy a crane for high-altitude firefighting.

He feels incredibly proud (according to Qianjiang Evening News (Weibo)). The picture shows Ruan Bingyan. "Firefighter A'san" has led a family volunteer fire brigade for 22 years.

Last year, an unfortunate incident happened in his family when one of his sons suffered brain injuries due to a car accident. Since the driver who caused the accident fled, this financially struggling family had to bear medical expenses amounting to several hundred thousand yuan, leaving him heavily in debt. However, just a few days ago, he spent all the three or four ten-thousand-yuan government bonus on purchasing firefighting equipment.

When asked if he wanted a monetary reward, he replied, "Why do I need money? I think firefighting is more important."

Seeing his actions, some people called him "stupid," and others sarcastically referred to him as "stupid A'san," but he remains steadfast and without regrets.

"Our place is 50 miles away from Shaoxing and also 50 miles from Shangyu. Once a fire breaks out, by the time the fire department arrives, the fire will have almost burned everything. And every time I see my fellow villagers suffer, I feel so heartbroken."

For this reason, Ruan Bingyan, a farmer, works very hard. He leases 11 mu of farmland and raises dozens of pigs each year. Every dawn, A'san rides his electric tricycle to transport pig feed. After breakfast, he goes to the fields to work. In the evening, he returns home to clean the pigsty, busy all day long. After deducting costs, he only makes about six to seven thousand yuan annually, which he then invests entirely into firefighting.

Upon receiving the fire truck, Ruan Bingyan expressed that he would immediately learn how to drive it, "I hope I can start using it as soon as possible."

Ruan Bingyan doesn't know the exact value of this vehicle, but it's reportedly around ten thousand yuan, "This is something I don't feel comfortable asking about."

"I am the first one to be honored,"

"The leader said either give you money or the fire truck, and I said I don't want the money, I just want that fire truck." On November 29th, "Firefighter A'san" Ruan Bingyan spoke with the reporter in his native Shangyu dialect. His words are as simple and unadorned as he is.

Once A'san became the captain, he established three rules for his team: Regardless of day or night, no matter how busy they are, they must respond to alarms; the dangerous tasks such as entering burning buildings are done by him; the cost of gasoline and machine repairs during firefighting missions, along with annual subsidies for a pair of sneakers for each team member, are borne by him.

Zhejiang man voluntarily fights fires for 22 years, cumulatively extinguishing 156 fires, saving economic losses of over 6 million yuan for his fellow villagers.

Very determined,

No regrets.

In Ruan Bingyan's home, there's hardly anything valuable to be seen. The most valuable items are the firefighting suits and helmets hanging on the wall, and the firefighting equipment piled in the corners of the rooms, as well as the two electric fire trucks with PLA Armed Police license plates in the side room. To support the operation of this family volunteer fire brigade, he spends at least five to six thousand yuan annually.

The fire truck has yet to arrive. "When it will arrive, I still need to ask the leader," Ruan Bingyan said. "It's a four-wheeled motorcycle with a water pump and fire hose at the back, specifically designed for firefighting. It's relatively professional, I've never used it before. Once I get it, I need to study it carefully." Ruan Bingyan described it this way, his tone filled with curiosity and anticipation.

Ruan Bingyan is a nationally renowned "firefighting hero." This ordinary farmer from Daoxu Town, Shangyu, known locally as "A'san" because he is the third son in his family, formed Zhejiang Province's first family volunteer fire brigade in 1989 and became famous in the area for voluntary firefighting. Over the past 22 years, he has extinguished 156 fires, saving economic losses of over 6 million yuan for his fellow villagers.

The Ministry of Public Security held a commendation meeting, awarding a total of four fire trucks, one of which was awarded to him.

A few days ago, the Ministry of Public Security held a commendation meeting. "I am the first one to be honored," Ruan Bingyan proudly said. At the commendation meeting, the Ministry of Public Security awarded a total of four fire trucks, and Zhejiang received only one, which went to him.

Ruan Bingyan said that at the time, the leader asked if he wanted a cash reward, but he declined, "Why do I need money? I think firefighting is more important."

Thus, in 1989, A'san used 4,500 yuan earned from transportation to purchase portable fire pumps and other firefighting equipment. He also rallied his three sons and two discharged soldiers from the village to establish the province's first family volunteer fire brigade.

To fight fires, he invests at least five to six thousand yuan annually.
