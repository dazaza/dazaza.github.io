The degree of joint pain is often related to the severity of joint swelling.

by wwwguke on 2012-02-08 09:12:06

Many arthritis patients initially experience localized pain in only a few joints, with the disease progressing slowly and the pain alternating between relief and exacerbation. As the condition worsens, joint pain and swelling appear, and the number of affected joints gradually increases. Specifically, what are the clinical symptoms of arthritis? Clinical experts from Chongqing St. Paul Orthopedic Hospital (Chongqing Orthopedic Hospital) have provided a clear explanation:

1. Morning stiffness: Morning stiffness refers to the tightness, stiffness, lack of flexibility or restriction in the joints that occurs in the morning or after waking up. Mild cases can be relieved or disappear after getting up or warming up, while severe cases may not be relieved all day. The duration of morning stiffness is one of the standards for measuring the activity level of the disease. Morning stiffness is an important specific symptom for all patients, a warning sign, and also one of the important pieces of evidence for clinical diagnosis.

2. Joint swelling and pain: Joint pain is also a specific symptom for all rheumatoid patients. The degree of joint pain is often related to the severity of joint swelling; the more obvious the swelling, the more severe the pain.

3. Affected joints: Rheumatoid arthritis can invade synovium, cartilage, bone, tendons, ligaments, bursae, fascia, etc., in the human body. The frequency of joint involvement, from high to low, is as follows: proximal interphalangeal joints, metacarpophalangeal and wrist, elbow, shoulder, ankle, hip joints, etc.

4. Migratory and symmetric: In the early stage, the migratory nature of the joints is relatively obvious. The interval period of migration is relatively short. Besides the early migratory pain, the migration of arthritis is often symmetrical.

5. Limited mobility: The degree of limitation is related to the extent of swelling, pain, joint damage, and damage to surrounding structures. About 90% of patients involve finger joints, especially the proximal interphalangeal arthritis, while the distal interphalangeal joints are rarely affected.

6. Joint deformity or stiffness: Due to the destruction of muscles, ligaments, and joint capsules around the inflamed joints, the joints present a certain special deformation, such as swan neck deformity, buttonhole deformity, etc.

From this, it can be seen that arthritis has a very significant impact on daily life. If you have any unclear areas, please consult the online clinical experts at Chongqing St. Paul Orthopedic Hospital (Chongqing Orthopedic Hospital).
