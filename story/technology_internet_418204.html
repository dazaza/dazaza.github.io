Abstract of the Painting Room Catalog 123456

English Name: painting room

A spray painting room, also known as a painting booth, is equipment that provides a dedicated environment for coating operations. The artificial environment created within the spray painting room meets the requirements of temperature, humidity, brightness, and cleanliness needed for coating operations. It ensures the safety and hygiene of the operators, treats waste emissions from coating operations to protect the environment from pollution. Broadly speaking, it is designed to promptly remove the floating paint particles (mist particles) generated during spraying and coating, thereby ensuring coating quality, protecting the environment, and safeguarding the physical health of the operators. Coating operations are typically carried out in small rooms with local drainage facilities, which is what constitutes a spray painting room.

Spray painting rooms are generally divided into dry-type, wet-type spray painting rooms, water curtain spray painting rooms, water-wash spray painting rooms, oil-wash spray painting rooms, pumpless spray painting rooms, and vortex spray painting rooms, among others.

For dry-type handling of paint mist particles, the process usually involves exhaust, filtration to capture paint mist particles, adsorption of mist particles using baffles, and forced ventilation. Afterward, cleaning of the baffles is required, and filters need frequent replacement, making it unsuitable for large-scale operations.

Wet-type uses water curtains to absorb or adsorb paint mist particles. Spray painting rooms can be used continuously for long periods, with high efficiency in purifying paint mist, simple operation and maintenance, flexible and convenient installation, no wastewater discharge, and clean safety, offering higher advantages.

Water curtain spray painting rooms use the positive and negative pressure of the atmosphere, installing air intakes above the operator's head. In front of the spray table, a water curtain wall is installed. Behind the water curtain wall, powerful fans extract air, forming an airflow from the top of the head to behind the water curtain wall. The air carries oil and gas ions through the water curtain and filters them into the water.
