"Resident Evil 4: Afterlife" sees Alice return to the screen.

by dy5200 on 2012-02-23 18:18:06

From the moment the virus got out of control, it has been rampaging and spreading at a speed unimaginable to people, bringing terrible devastation and destruction to the whole world... People kept getting infected, eventually turning into flesh-and-blood zombies, making one city after another instantly become terrible hell on earth. Alice (Milla Jovovich) lives up to her mission, continuing her adventurous journey to find survivors, and figuring out ways to bring them all to a safe place. As for the irreconcilable battle between Alice and Umbrella Corporation, it has developed to a new peak, but she unexpectedly received help from an old friend.

Just in the critical moment, fortunately, a new leader-level figure emerged among the uninfected people, claiming that he could lead everyone to take refuge in Los Angeles, and then establish a safe quarantine area away from zombies there... However, when they finally arrived, they found that the virus had already spread throughout the city before them, now filled with thousands of slow-moving zombies - obviously, Alice and her companions have stepped into a fatal trap.

Behind-the-scenes Trivia

As the most popular zombie series enters its fourth chapter, "Resident Evil: Afterlife" also opens a new and cutting-edge technical space for zombie films, becoming the first action blockbuster to be filmed using 3D + IMAX technology since James Cameron's epoch-making "Avatar". The main creative force behind this, Paul W.S. Anderson, will once again return to the series as director - since the 2002 "Resident Evil", he served as writer and producer in "Resident Evil: Apocalypse" and "Resident Evil: Extinction", but this time his decision to direct again is mainly due to the series' first attempt and adoption of 3D technology. Anderson said: "In my view, we are experiencing a completely satisfactory cycle process because when the series reaches the fourth story, adding 3D technology not only means a new beginning for us but also represents an exciting and thrilling 'return'. I would like to say that 'Resident Evil: Afterlife' is undoubtedly the most astonishing film in this series, and I am very proud of it. Although 3D has flooded the movie industry in an outrageous way over the past year, it still shows an undeniable trend that 3D will definitely become a basic development trend of future entertainment industries."

Although Hollywood is no longer unfamiliar with the concept of 3D, its true emergence as an irreplaceable trendsetter was triggered by the release of "Avatar", Paul W.S. Anderson admitted: "Indeed, ever since then, wherever you look, as long as it's related to movies, you can find the word '3D', of course, including some substandard works... So our goal became increasingly clear, which is to make a real live-action 3D movie that truly matches this technology, because in my opinion, since 'Avatar', I haven't seen any live-action movie with strict 3D specifications."

There have been countless examples proving that 3D technology doesn't always play a positive role in promoting or enhancing effects, but can also produce extremely terrible 'side effects'. Paul W.S. Anderson said: "Sometimes, quantity cannot measure whether something has matured... Even though 3D technology has flooded the movie industry over the past year, it is still in its infancy. However, it cannot be denied that it has become an element to stimulate fans' excitement. From my perspective as a filmmaker, I hope to bring everyone a unique 3D experience through this opportunity, and I have deeply realized that it depends on how you use this technology and 3D cameras, including scene settings and camera placement positions. Since there is no precedent to follow, you can only gradually summarize experiences based on your understanding of movie knowledge. Even during editing, it was a highly challenging process because 'Resident Evil: Afterlife' might be the first movie entirely edited in 3D mode. No matter where, in the editing room or on the set, we had to carry these big glasses with us all the time - after going through all this, my biggest wish now is that someone can improve the design of 3D glasses to make them more humanized, lighter, and easier to carry. Perhaps watching a simple movie may not feel too much, but if you have to wear them all day, you will understand how heavy a burden it is."

Meanwhile, Paul W.S. Anderson also regards IMAX as another highly technical and overly demanding movie technology, he continued: "Especially for 3D movies, IMAX absolutely belongs to a choice that enhances strength... I think, even until today, no one dares to easily combine 3D and IMAX in a single film, especially for an R-rated horror movie like 'Resident Evil: Afterlife'. We can be considered pioneers in this field - if I'm not mistaken, the previous IMAX version of an R-rated movie was 'Watchmen', and they made such a bold attempt because their distributor Warner Bros. has deep ties with IMAX. Transforming this movie from IMAX to 3D+IMAX was a complicated and arduous process, but it was recognized by everyone, and the reason we did so is because we all know what kind of movie effect this choice can achieve for us. As I have always emphasized, when I thought it was appropriate to use 3D technology to shoot this movie, it wasn't because everyone was doing it, but because this decision was entirely correct. I think filmmakers bear significant responsibilities like this, instead of simply deceiving audiences into cinemas with certain selling points, we should convey a genuine audio-visual feast through the big screen, just like what James Cameron did before."
