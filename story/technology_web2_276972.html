Headhunting companies are happy, commissions keep increasing

by tfbgfnbv on 2009-11-25 14:50:27

In this great revolution of talent mobility, the ones who benefit the most are undoubtedly the headhunting companies. Whether it's individuals looking for job changes or companies seeking talented professionals, they all rely on headhunting firms in Beijing. Mr. Wang, who recently took a new position as department manager in a joint venture, believes that bustling talent exchange markets clearly do not suit mid-to-senior level talents. For them, channels for job-hopping tend more towards headhunting companies.

Headhunting companies in China can roughly be divided into three categories: First, foreign headhunting companies entering the Chinese market with multinational corporations. These companies often appear in the form of advisory consulting firms and their contracts are mostly signed overseas, such as Korn Ferry, Heidrick & Struggles, Egon Zehnder, etc.; Second, domestic Beijing headhunting companies that have been officially registered, mainly focusing on mid-to-senior level positions needed by Sino-foreign joint ventures. Such as ChinaHR, Bosen Talent, Kerui Consulting, etc.; Third, the senior talent search departments of general talent exchange institutions, like those hosted by the Labor and Personnel Bureau.

China's headhunting industry has only been around for about 10 years, but it has grown rapidly. Currently, there are nearly 3,000 headhunting companies and consulting institutions engaged in headhunting services in our country, mainly concentrated in cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou. Headhunting companies in Beijing are gradually becoming an important channel for enterprises to acquire senior talents and for the mobility of senior talents.
