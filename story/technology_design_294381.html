Will the market hotspot shift to large-cap blue chip stocks?_Stock Forum

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:42:54

The overall market continued to rise, but the number of declining stocks was significantly greater than the number of rising stocks. Are the main players going to use the strategy of pulling blue-chip stocks to keep the index rising while letting the stocks with relatively large previous gains enter an adjustment phase? Will market hotspots shift towards blue-chip stocks?

I. Today, the market experienced a volatile day with sharp rises and falls.

Today's news was clearly bearish:

Firstly, to counteract the crazy increase in small-cap new stock prices, the issuance pace will accelerate next week - bearish.

Secondly, the U.S. stock market fell slightly yesterday, with the Dow Jones Index down 0.84%. Gold and oil also saw some decline - slightly bearish.

Thirdly, Shenzhen Stock Exchange punished speculative traders of Jifeng Agricultural Machinery by suspending one account for three months - bearish.

Fourthly, the former chairman of the CSRC said that stock index futures will soon be launched - bearish.

Fifthly, over eight weeks, the central bank has netted capital withdrawal amounting to 687 billion yuan - bearish.

Sixthly, Social Security Fund invested 32% of its funds in the stock market, gaining 12% in the first ten months - bullish.

Only the last piece of news is positive.

The Shanghai Composite Index rose to 3310 points at eleven o'clock under the lead of the banking and insurance sectors. Afterwards, the bears suddenly exerted force, causing a significant fall, and by 1:15 PM, the Shanghai Composite Index plummeted to 3214 points. In less than an hour, the Shanghai Composite Index dropped nearly a hundred points. Then, the bulls quickly pulled the index back up, with Sinopec and PetroChina showing good increases, eventually recovering all losses. The Shanghai Composite Index closed at 3317 points, rising 52 points throughout the day, an increase of 1.61%, while the Shenzhen Component Index rose 125 points throughout the day, an increase of 0.91%.

Today's index exhibited a pattern of sharp rises and falls.

II. Blue-chip stocks performed well today.

Another feature of today's market is that the large-cap blue-chip stocks showed relatively good upward trends.

In the banking sector, CITIC Bank (601998) and Ningbo Bank (002142) both closed with limit-up gains, averaging nearly a 4% increase. In the insurance sector, China Taiping Insurance (601601) closed with a limit-up gain, averaging more than a 6% increase. Sinopec (600028) and PetroChina (601857) also had notable increases.

It was precisely due to the significant rise of these blue-chip stocks that the index managed to maintain a certain level of increase despite most stocks falling.

III. Will the market continue to rise?

From today's index performance, the overall market is continuing to rise. The Shanghai Composite Index once again approached its previous high. It should indicate that the bullish forces have the advantage, and the market might continue to rise.

However, considering the situation where most stocks fell today, it is also concerning.

Investors have reason to believe that the main players may adopt this method: switching hotspots, allowing previously lagging large-cap blue-chip stocks to continue rising, thereby driving the index higher continuously. Meanwhile, stocks that have seen significant gains earlier would enter a phased adjustment.

Thus, the index continues to rise, hotspots take turns rising, and adjustments occur in rotation.

IV. Will market hotspots shift towards large-cap blue-chip stocks?

Today's market hotspot clearly shifted towards large-cap blue-chip stocks. Investors are very concerned about whether market hotspots will shift towards the previously lagging large-cap blue-chip stocks.

Our view is: judging from just one day of relatively good performance by large-cap blue-chip stocks, there may not be sufficient evidence to conclude that the hotspot has shifted. From a cautious perspective, it's best to observe for a few more days before making a judgment.
