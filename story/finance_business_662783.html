Chapter Sixty-One

Lu Ye took a taxi, while Yin Zhu walked into the neighborhood, still carrying groceries. If she didn't hurry, Shen Junfei would start jumping around again. Thinking about Shen Junfei made the corners of Yin Zhu's mouth curl involuntarily, a fact she hadn't even noticed herself. Just as she was lost in thought, she saw Shen Junfei come out.

"What did you go out for?"

"You're still thinking about curry rice? Don't you know we've run out of rice?" Shen Junfei had started cooking the beef but realized they didn't have enough rice when it came time to cook.

Yin Zhu chuckled, "Didn't I miscalculate your portion earlier? You can manage with what's left."

Shen Junfei hooked his arm around her and spun her halfway around, "That's right, all the rice you have is eaten by me. I'll make up for it."

Yin Zhu asked curiously, "Isn't the rice shop supposed to deliver? You even helped me copy down their number." Why would he make this extra trip?

Shen Junfei pulled her forward, "The rice from the rice shop isn't fresh. Let's go to the supermarket and choose some."

They were standing at the entrance of the residential area, talking without noticing the traffic around them. Just then, a car entered quickly, and another car tried to follow closely behind, moving faster than usual. Standing inside, Shen Junfei only had enough time to pull Yin Zhu towards him and turn to shield her when he noticed the incoming vehicle. Even so, the side mirror of the minivan still bumped into Shen Junfei, causing both of them to stagger several steps sideways.

What was originally an unintentional move during a crisis moment took on a completely different meaning in the eyes of someone watching intently. Lu Ye’s car was stuck at the intersection, and he captured this entire scene clearly. All of Feng Yin Zhu's previous statements now seemed like pretense, not just pretense, but deceitful words that pierced deeply into Lu Ye's heart.

When Lu Ye reported back, he directly announced that Wu Yelai had already divorced. All the talk Lu Ye had with Feng Yin Zhu turned out to be mere posturing. Since they had already divorced, even the military couldn't interfere anymore. The immediate priority now was how to help Wu Yelai get over this.

Wanting to help but unable to find a way to do so, Lu Ye felt frustrated. Especially when facing Wu Yelai, any useful words he could say had no effect, and anything he couldn’t say couldn’t be mentioned at all, which made him even more uncomfortable. So, when drinking, he avoided finding Wu Yelai, fearing that if he got drunk, he might blurt out something inappropriate and hurt his brother-in-arms.

Lu Ye's abnormal behavior went unnoticed by Wu Yelai, who wasn't like them, drinking every fifth day. He mostly wouldn’t join even when invited. However, the subordinates soon began whispering, saying that Lu Ye probably had issues with Wu Yelai, as anyone suggesting inviting the captain would be opposed by the instructor.

Wu Yelai initially dismissed these rumors as nonsense and paid no attention to them. But as the whispers grew louder, he couldn't completely ignore them. Thus, despite not being invited, he still showed up at the dinner table.

He and Lu Ye had their own understanding and didn't bother with pretentious pleasantries. They stood united against outsiders and immediately challenged two boastful individuals claiming boundless alcohol tolerance.

As the drinking progressed, without Wu Yelai asking, Lu Ye hugged his shoulders and said, "Brother, I misjudged things. I failed to resolve the issue, and I'm sorry!" Saying this, he raised his glass of liquor to finish it in one gulp.

Wu Yelai had consumed quite a bit but remained relatively sober. He reached out and forcibly snatched the cup from Lu Ye's hand, "You drink to your heart's content, but you owe me an explanation."

Lu Ye glanced around the table and saw that only the two of them were still capable of speaking. Deciding to let it all out, since he couldn't keep avoiding Wu Yelai forever, he continued, "You deserve to understand. It's about your family matters, your wife..." Lu Ye didn't want to leave things half-said, but he found it hard to express, considering others' privacy and especially the privacy of a former spouse.

Wu Yelai's hand holding the cup dropped dejectedly, then suddenly rose, "What am I supposed to understand? I'm the most confused person here!" He gulped down the remaining half-cup of wine in one breath.

Lu Ye couldn't tolerate such behavior from others in front of him. He grabbed an empty cup and poured himself a drink, "I'll accompany you with this cup. After drinking, put this matter aside and live like a real man. Don't let others laugh at you!"

Wu Yelai didn't respond. He simply flipped the cup upside down on the table, rested his forehead against the bottom of the cup, making very light movements and speaking softly, almost only to himself, "Who would laugh?" Here, all he could hope for was that she might glance at him once more, which was no longer possible.

These days, he began slowly recalling the events between him and Yin Zhu, reflecting on his feelings for her. She had always been the one chasing after him, and he had always reluctantly accepted her advances. Why, when faced with a breakup, did he feel so reluctant to let go?

After much contemplation and recalling many incidents, Wu Yelai realized that he was the first one to fall in love. Laughing at her immaturity, yet being provoked by her competitiveness and showing off in front of her, he was actually more immature. Verbally disapproving of her social lifestyle but secretly envying her carefree youthfulness; barely knowing her but unable to tolerate others tarnishing her reputation; annoyed by her persistence but worrying if she was misbehaving or studying diligently when she wasn't around; seeing her name on the list of successful candidates for the military academy brought him genuine relief; in the face of her unreserved love, occasionally responding until eventually passively accepting everything she offered—whether it was dating or marriage, hiding his own feelings while accepting everything she gave.

Why maintain passivity? Perhaps because he knew he could offer too little. Although he might seem impressive now, he was uninteresting. He enjoyed listening to Yin Zhu enthusiastically narrate stories, gesturing wildly and interspersing with bursts of laughter—something he himself could never do. He excelled at listening but struggled with outward expression. Fortunately, Yin Zhu didn't see him daily, and occasional meetings kept her entertained.

What about their relationship? Wu Yelai knew this wasn't a deliberate result, at least not what he wanted. He could sense that Yin Zhu disliked being probed, dissected, or laid bare entirely.

Her aversion must have influenced him somewhat, forcing him to restrain himself. Each time she turned away, was it a childlike pout?

Being alone in the army, did he truly miss her, miss home? Looking back, he dared to think and admit that he did. Especially during certain missions, before boarding the plane and writing wills, he didn't write one. At that time, he thought he had nothing to entrust—leaving the household to Feng Yin Zhu, he was at peace. If he had written, it would have been about unresolved worries, and those would have been solely about her—a sign of weakness he didn't want to show, especially not at such a critical moment.

If given another chance, could he stop pretending and recognize his love for her sooner? Indeed, he wasn't dishonest; he just hadn't realized it himself. He was accustomed to their interaction, accustomed to being this kind of person and having such a Feng Yin Zhu, accustomed to this awkward dependence and love. Habitual to the point where he believed this was correct, believing their life was this way, expecting each day to continue similarly until old age, when they wouldn't talk about love anymore, naturally staying together and keeping each other company. Yes, his love began without ever imagining it would change—standing by her side, never looking elsewhere.

Thinking about these things, Wu Yelai also understood that this wasn't much, even pitifully little. His love could only go so far. All expressions were merely reflections of his mood.

Always worried about drifting apart, always fearing that giving everything would become a joke. Therefore, living within means, providing exactly what was needed, not going beyond, not guessing her thoughts. Probably, this way, he never truly cared for her;

Probably, this way, he never made even the simplest gesture of retention. Later, wanting to act but unable to lower his pride, wanting to act but unsure where to start, wanting to act but not knowing how to hold her or ask for another chance.

Wu Yelai's thoughts dared not go further, dared not think more. The hand that once clutched his sleeve now held someone else's arm. How could he dare to think more? His time stopped at any past moment, paused there.

Chapter Sixty-Two

Yin Zhu anxiously waited for many days, seeing no further news from Lu Ye, her mind gradually settled. Her thoughts increasingly focused on Shen Junfei, developing indescribable feelings.

Previously, being taken care of by Shen Junfei, Yin Zhu didn't pay much attention, even feeling burdened. Recently, however, whether out of habit or otherwise, their interactions improved, and Yin Zhu became proactive. For instance, when Shen Junfei cooked, she would rush to wash dishes and tidy the kitchen; when Shen Junfei shopped, she would buy things for him when she went out.

At night, when he lingered until after 11 PM, Yin Zhu wouldn't wait for him to leave before bathing and sleeping. With him busy in the living room, she'd sleep peacefully knowing someone was outside.

Of course, Shen Junfei's belongings increased under Yin Zhu's indulgent allowance, cluttering her small coffee table to the point of collapse.

"Can you find another place to put your stuff?" Yin Zhu held a plate of sunflower seeds, trying to watch TV but finding it uncomfortable to sit on the sofa due to lack of leg space.

"Where do you think I should put them so they don't get in the way?" Shen Junfei asked without looking up. To work overtime and pick Yin Zhu up early, he had to bring work home, sometimes working until dawn, driving home for a quick shower, and heading straight to work.

Yin Zhu quickly responded, "Everywhere seems inconvenient." Even the bathroom had Shen Junfei's face wash and toothbrush, undoubtedly a serious issue.

"Then I'll move them into the bedroom," Shen Junfei readily agreed, picking up a pile from the coffee table and heading toward the bedroom.

Yin Zhu didn't even have time to set down her plate before rushing after him. In the small apartment, the only private space referred to as "inside" was Yin Zhu's bedroom. "Shen Junfei, no entry!" She was desperate, resorting to a foul move by hooking her arm around his waist to block his progress.

Inside the bedroom were clothes she had just changed out of, and the bed was unmade, leaving the blanket crumpled either on the bed or on the floor. She wasn't sure which.

The bedroom was Yin Zhu's free zone, where Shen Junfei rarely ventured. Hence, she had relaxed to an extremely messy state. If he entered, she feared being laughed at, appearing well-groomed in public but living like a slob.

Still, she was a step too late. Shen Junfei had already opened the door, exposing her sanctuary entirely.

Yin Zhu squeezed past him and blocked his path, "Look, where can you possibly put your things? There's nowhere, right? The coffee table is yours, I won't compete for space anymore..."

Seeing Yin Zhu's flushed face, Shen Junfei couldn't bear teasing her anymore and turned to return to the living room. Unfortunately, Yin Zhu blocked his path, and in the limited space of the doorway, they collided, resulting in Yin Zhu sitting down backward. A pile of documents scattered, and she laughed nervously, somewhat awkwardly.

Now Shen Junfei had to enter, picking up some documents, sitting down, and roughly organizing them. He placed them aside, picked more, and organized further.

Yin Zhu sat nearby, passing items. On each document cover were small caricatures drawn by Shen Junfei, varying in expression. She chose one with an exaggerated open mouth and handed it to him, "What does this mean?"

"The most urgent and important," Shen Junfei replied.

Yin Zhu marveled, "Are you this happy when you have work to do?"

"No, that's how I feel after reviewing it." Shen Junfei had his own method for handling tasks, prioritizing based on urgency and importance, all processed mentally after review.

"After so long, you're still drawing this well." Yin Zhu flipped through Shen Junfei's sketches, "You used to draw in books, flipping pages quickly to see animated expressions. That was really good."

"Do you remember?"

"How could I forget?" Though not always remembered, whenever triggered, the memories rushed back vividly. Whether from setbacks with Wu Yelai or unsatisfactory exam results leading to gloom, or even menstrual pain, whenever her mood dipped, Shen Junfei often found ways to cheer her up. Changing faces of stick figures was one common method. He drew various classic cartoon characters, capturing their essence in a few strokes. Yin Zhu particularly liked his foxes, complete with actions and expressions, cunning yet adorable. The tail was frequently used as a prop—covering its face, curled beneath it, or raised behind it, and occasionally even tucked between its legs. Each appearance was unique, fitting Yin Zhu's current mood.

Memories, once triggered, surged forth powerfully. From a small image, they reminisced about amusing and embarrassing high school and college experiences, people they both knew, silly deeds, laughing loudly at each other.

Amidst the laughter, silence suddenly fell, unsettlingly quiet. Yin Zhu leaned against the bed, Shen Junfei against the wall, both supported yet feeling unstable, leaning forward.

Yin Zhu lowered her head, unable to look at Shen Junfei. The eerie silence scared her yet hinted at anticipation. Before she could decide whether to start another topic or simply crawl up, Shen Junfei leaned close, stopping less than an inch from her face.

"How...?" She managed only one word before Shen Junfei silenced her.

Shen Junfei's lips touched hers gently, making no movement, just pressing closer, trembling slightly, careful not to startle or break the moment. As Yin Zhu worried about his next move and how she should react, Shen Junfei suddenly pulled back slightly. Yin Zhu instinctively looked up, but his hand pressed her head down, guiding it to rest on his shoulder.

Yin Zhu lay in Shen Junfei's embrace, reliving the contact. Honestly, Yin Zhu had no clear memory of her first kiss with Wu Yelai. It was merely an incidental bonus, overshadowed and neglected.

Subsequent sensations were gradual adjustments without the initial surprise and wonder of a first kiss. But just now, even such a gentle touch sent tingles from her lips to her heart.

"Let's continue!" Before Yin Zhu could react, Shen Junfei's lips found hers again. This time, he transformed completely. If the previous touch could be likened to a dragonfly skimming water, this was a sweeping mountainous tide. Yin Zhu felt herself being absorbed and swallowed whole. Yet strangely, Shen Junfei caused her no pain, no roughness, his technique surpassing her expectations. Of course, she briefly wondered about his skill level but decided not to dwell on it, captivated by his fervor.

What followed embarrassed Yin Zhu for a long time.

Shen Junfei lifted her like passing a ball, tossing her onto the bed. Struggling to sit up, she was quickly pushed back down. She knew clearly what would happen if she didn't resist. With one hand restraining her and the other removing their clothes...

In this situation, she abandoned her resistance too easily, without hesitation. She had time to consider, opportunities to resist. She even knew that saying "no" would immediately make Shen Junfei stop. But she didn't, her physical resistance was merely token, quickly crumbling under his advance.

Reflecting later, Yin Zhu realized she couldn't refuse. This person who had always cared for and tolerated her, if rejected now, might not just reject this moment but could truly hurt him and cause him to walk away. She wasn't the same Feng Yin Zhu from years ago. She didn't want to lose him, couldn't lose him. She couldn't imagine life without him, the house without him, the silent phone without his calls, or her emotions without his concern. Not resisting was an attitude, a direct response to her heart's desires. Of course, this realization came much later.

For Shen Junfei, rejection was unbearable. He wanted, but he feared Yin Zhu would think his desire was simple. He feared questioning and denial. Thankfully, Yin Zhu's hands resisted only initially, never interfering crucially. Her voice was firmly locked inside her throat.

Between them, there was no conversation during the act, nor afterward did they comment on it. Thus, the nature of the established fact was ambiguously passed over, confirming their cohabitation.

Chapter Sixty-Three:

Of course, Yin Zhu still had many questions about that night.

"You restrained yourself at one point, didn't you? I thought you would stop right there." After a less exhausting session, Yin Zhu asked.

"When?"

Shen Junfei didn't recall having such thoughts. Every expression and action of Feng Yin Zhu that night seemed to indicate acceptance. In that situation, how could the word "restraint" appear in his mind?

"Huh???" Yin Zhu stretched closer, reaching him, "Right after that, didn't you pause for a moment?"

Shen Junfei chuckled, "Did you think I was restraining myself then?" At that moment, he was rapidly reviewing the materials and images he needed, lacking personal experience and hastily piecing together references. This wasn't the time to expose his inadequacies; he wanted to love her, ensuring she felt no discomfort, captivating her as much as he was captivated. Judging by the outcome, it seemed effective.

Yin Zhu hesitated, "Not?"

Shen Junfei shook his head, "If there was restraint, it wasn't then."

"Ah?" Yin Zhu suddenly found herself pinned, now conditioned to interpret his actions differently.

"My restraint started right here." Shen Junfei explained step-by-step his thoughts at the time.

"When my hand was here, I worried about hurting you, so I restrained myself???"

"When I kissed here, see, right here, I feared pricking you, so I restrained myself???"

"When I pressed down on you, worried about causing pain???"

"Doing the full set beforehand was exhausting, but I feared startling you???"

"Here, I wanted so badly, so desperately to just rush in, but I still restrained myself???"

Yin Zhu covered her ears in embarrassment, "Shen Junfei, I don't want to hear anymore!" Originally, she sought sweet words, though Shen Junfei was considerate, he rarely expressed affection.

"You've heard it all, haven't you? What you need to block isn't your ears. Come, block this," Yin Zhu's hands were placed over his mouth by Shen Junfei. Seeing her attempt to withdraw, Shen Junfei warned her, "If you take them away, I'll babble nonsense again???"

His subsequent words were instantly silenced by Yin Zhu.

Despite covering his mouth, Yin Zhu felt no regain of control. His lips and tongue swept across her palm, lightly tickling, making her heart flutter and itch. Physically, his prior explanations had ignited fires everywhere, now limiting her hands facilitated his exploration. Covering his mouth, Yin Zhu seemed to forget her ability to speak, relying on dodging to evade.

Thus, they returned to that night, the first night, engaging in deep and heavy collisions without conversation.

Under Yin Zhu's tacit approval, Shen Junfei gradually moved important items, swiftly occupying half the space.

To merge meant conflict, like the daily struggle for the bathroom in the morning. Despite not being newlywed, Yin Zhu lacked cohabitation experience with the opposite sex. She couldn't tolerate washing her face while Shen Junfei casually entered to use the restroom, and vice versa. The latter scenario occurred only once due to her oversight and was promptly eliminated.

"You should give me basic respect and adhere to basic etiquette."

"I'll be more careful next time. I was just in a rush to make breakfast for you," Shen Junfei smiled, clearly unconcerned.

Another issue was his habit of tidying things. In household chores, he was highly proactive. Yin Zhu's items were quickly reorganized, causing her to shout daily, "Where are my things?" Surviving independently in this house was becoming increasingly difficult. Once, she even couldn't find instant noodles, too lazy to go downstairs to buy, and thus skipped a meal.

"Shen Junfei, are you doing this on purpose?" Watching him pretend to be busy irritated her. He seemed to have endless work but meticulously controlled her life, fully taking over the household and her.

"Hmm?" Sitting at the new desk in the living room, he acknowledged her with a single word while busy.

"You're turning me into an incompetent person." Sometimes, Yin Zhu felt aimless, unsure of anything she needed to do. Work-related matters weren't heavy enough to require bringing home. Household chores were entirely handled by the capable Shen Junfei. Even her next day's outfit was prepared neatly by the bedside.

Previously, he traveled regularly. Now, even when traveling, he returned the same day unless absolutely necessary to stay overnight. Thus, Yin Zhu's sole function seemed to be sharing the bed. Such a leisurely life, though not necessarily bad, still made her bored and occasionally suffocated.

"Haha, weren't you already?" Shen Junfei joked without taking it seriously.

Yin Zhu sighed, unwilling to continue. She was accustomed to independence, not seeking care, and actively doing everything, which didn't end well. Now, losing one role, she became entirely passive. Being cared for and cherished was nice, and she couldn't deny her gratitude. Yet, she always felt like she was floating, insecure. Being carried by this cloud allowed her to experience all sorts of scenery, but when faced with beautiful views, she had no interest in looking, only worrying about falling off.

Indeed, what virtue or ability did she possess to deserve such an outstanding person revolving around her for so many years? What worth did she have for him to care for her so sincerely? Yin Zhu couldn't understand why her confidence, unshaken by Wu Yelai's lack of response, was now questioned by Shen Junfei.

"Overthinking," Shen Junfei wiped his hand across Yin Zhu's face, "Your face will become squished like a bun."

After the joke, he suddenly said, "Yin Zhu, I want to be your big tree, for you to lean on, to seek shade under, or even chop branches to warm yourself in winter. I won't move; I'll be right there. But I won't expect you to build a nest on me. Even if I dry up and fall, you don't need to fear. You'll still be you."

Yin Zhu stared blankly for a moment, then asked, "Shen Junfei, are you rich?"

"What?"

"Because what you just said sounds extravagant."

Shen Junfei burst into laughter, "It shouldn't, it's adapted from a drama line."

Yin Zhu rolled her eyes, uncaring about her image in front of Shen Junfei, feeling she had none to preserve anyway.
