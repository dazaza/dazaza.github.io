"The Dead End" newspaper's top leader's history of decadent风情

by jqrxass on 2009-09-03 11:12:22

With the ups and downs of Lin Jiawei's official career and his emotional entanglements with several people as the clue, it looks through the myriad phenomena of society and the vicissitudes of life, deeply describing the protagonist's mental process towards a dead end and the social soil that breeds such situations. It is a rare work of warning and awakening.

Part One

He lit a cigarette and began to smoke it leisurely. After smoking for a while, his mind was still lingering on the concept of "Valentine's Day". He thought, which country invented this foreign festival, and how could it have such a great impact? In just a few years, it has become popular even in China, a country with traditional Confucian teachings. At this moment, he suddenly heard someone knocking on the door, so he said come in. As soon as the words were out of his mouth, a graceful figure appeared before Lin Jiawei, and his eyes immediately lit up.

Part Two

Xiang Tao is the ace reporter of the newspaper, and also a very capable person that Lin Jiawei appreciates. His articles are as upright as he himself is, elegant in style, fond of criticizing current ills, and powerful in writing. In recent years, he has been immersed in the grassroots and has written many substantial in-depth reports. It can be said that under the commercialized times, there are not many reporters who stick to the essence of being a literati like him. For such reporters, you have no reason not to respect their opinions.

Part Three

He had long been tired of Zhang Guihua. This woman had almost drained all his vitality during his prime years. How could he possibly still love such a woman? If anything, it was merely sympathy. Although, as he reached middle age, with the change in his status, her attitude also changed significantly, but no matter how much she changed, the petty bourgeois inferiority complex inherent in her could not be eradicated, and would occasionally erupt, constantly reminding Lin Jiawei of his past pain and humiliation.

Part Four

After putting down the phone, Lin Jiawei let out a long sigh of relief. The accumulated frustration and irritability in his chest for many days were completely washed away by DouDou's unintentional sobs and coquettishness. That empty heart seemed to finally find its place, instantly feeling the spirit return to its original position, feeling extremely spirited and full of vigor.

Part Five

When Lin Jiawei heard that there was a problem with the newspaper and it needed to be recalled, he couldn't help but feel a bit nervous. His head seemed to buzz loudly. To recall the newspaper, there must be a major political issue, otherwise, it wouldn't be such a big deal. In the morning, he was busy with other affairs and only skimmed through the headlines of the new paper, without having time to read the content carefully. So, he didn't know where exactly the problem lay. Now, it was too late to check again, so he could only organize a meeting with the editorial staff and logistics personnel at home.

Part Six

These days, the atmosphere in the newspaper office was somewhat unusual. He clearly heard laughter and chatter in the large offices of the editorial and reporting departments about the experience of collecting newspapers, but as soon as the leadership appeared, the chatter stopped immediately. He clearly heard them talking about how fierce Beijing reporters were when they went to the municipal government, but when they met alone, they didn't mention a word. Despite this, Lin Jiawei felt something from the unusual atmosphere. That was, he sensed a subtle flattery and intimacy from many people's gazes. However, whenever he caught those fleeting glances of flattery and intimacy, he avoided them immediately. He didn't want to get involved.

Part Seven

Lin Jiawei imitated Minister Chen's meaningful "hum" sound, thinking that Fang Xiangming was not entirely the kind of person who followed the wind, he did have a certain level of political acumen and sensitivity, so he valued him more. But he also felt that some things should not be said thoroughly in front of him, just touch upon them. Just like when Minister Chen talked to him, he never said it outright, just gave a little hint, and he would understand immediately.

Part Eight

Zhang Guihua was chopping something in the kitchen, the knife sounds were pounding on the cutting board tightly like drums, making one's heart restless. Lin Jiawei thought, there might be nothing worth her chopping so hard under the knife, probably using chopping vegetables to reflect on him. Chop if you want, as long as you don't chop on me, do as you please.

Part Nine

Thirdly, Miss Wang is both a courtesan and not an ordinary person. For her, time is money. Drinking tea and chatting with men can earn money, singing and dancing with men can earn money, sleeping with men can earn even more money, and her money is earned easily. If she takes a taxi to the countryside to collect 200 yuan, it would be unprofitable. If she brings back 200 yuan worth of grain and sells it at the grain station, after deducting labor costs and taxi fare, there would be almost nothing left. What makes her feel most uninteresting is that she would waste a whole day, which is extremely unprofitable for her. Therefore, the fact that Miss Wang went to the countryside to collect grain is hardly plausible.

Part Ten

Chang Lin was not an ordinary person. Upon hearing this, she understood the meaning immediately and said, "She is my shadow, I miss her if I don't see her for a day. We originally agreed to eat small hotpot, but somehow she dragged me here, saying that Mr. Li would treat us. I came uninvited!"
