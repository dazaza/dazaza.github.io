Must-see Huangshan travel recently

by jj0505 on 2012-03-01 13:28:23

Shrimp returned from a four-day independent trip to Huangshan, Hongcun and Xidi with his girlfriend on March 31. The travel strategy will not be detailed here as it can be found everywhere online. This trip was quite rewarding, so I'd like to share my feelings.

For independent travel, it's important to plan a reasonable route and pay attention to weather forecasts, which allows you to enjoy the beautiful scenery while saving energy and ensuring safety.

Huangshan Tour

Accommodation at the foot of the mountain: We arrived in Tangkou on the afternoon of March 27. It is now the off-season for Huangshan, so there are not many people, and rooms are very cheap. Shop around.

Accommodation on the mountain: On Sunday, Shrimp stayed in a small bungalow opposite the Beihai Hotel, where four people shared two bunk beds suitable for two couples. Two bottles of hot water (for instant noodles but need more time) were provided, and each person paid 80 yuan.

It is close to the sunrise observation point, Dawn Terrace, only five minutes away. We got along well with the boss of the bungalow, Mr. Wang, who gave us two military coats for watching the sunrise. He also said he would like to serve students like us, and his contact number is 13855977268.

Shrimp's insight: The location of the accommodation on the mountain should be determined according to the climbing route. For independent travel, Paiyun Building is a better choice because it is closer to the entrance of the West Sea Grand Canyon, which can save a lot of time and energy.

Shrimp's insight: Girls can bring more cucumbers when climbing the mountain. They are refreshing and hydrating, and bring a great sense of accomplishment: Halfway up the mountain, two cucumbers cost 5 yuan, at the top, they cost 10 yuan, and at scenic spots, they cost 15 yuan. Eating your own cucumbers makes you feel happy.

Shrimp's insight: Many people think that replenishing water is the first priority, but they don't realize that sweating while climbing the mountain also loses a lot of salt. Replenishing salt in time is equally important. Pickled chicken feet and spicy golden mushrooms are good choices, as they are appetizing and salty.

Shortcomings: There are very few open attractions. Huangshan is known for its danger and strangeness, and official policy is to avoid dangerous areas. Due to some peaks still having snow and ice, places like the West Sea Grand Canyon, Lotus Peak, Lion Peak, and Danxia Peak are generally not open until after April 1, which is a small regret.

Shrimp's insight: Inadequate intelligence collection led to significant changes in the marching route. Travelers should know their own capabilities and the situation, so as to fully enjoy the trip.

Shortcomings: Although it is said online that graduate student IDs get half-price tickets, this time they did not get the discount. Graduate students are also full-time students, and whether Huangshan's practice constitutes an infringement requires expert interpretation.

Shrimp's insight: If I had known, I would have made a fake ID... Honesty cost me some money...

Shrimp's insight: It's easier to climb up than down! After Shrimp and his girlfriend saw the Welcoming Pine, they took the cable car down the mountain. The feeling was numb: Was it really so easy to go down the mountain?!

Hongcun and Xidi Tour

There are direct buses from Huangshan to Hongcun, which is very convenient and runs until 4 PM at the latest. Arrange the time to go down the mountain reasonably. Now is the season when rapeseed flowers cover the mountains and fields, offering a stunning visual effect!

Both Hongcun and Xidi offer half-price student tickets. Just show your full-time student ID, and it's very straightforward and heartwarming. The Hongcun ticket is valid for entry and exit within four days, while the Xidi ticket is valid for same-day entry and exit. Tickets must be carried at all times.

Bus between the two villages (departing on the hour) costs 3 yuan per person.

Hongcun is very beautiful, poetic and picturesque. Shrimp couldn't help but write a poem:

On sunny days, the lake and mountain colors blend together,

On rainy days, the stone slabs and green tiles complement each other,

In foggy weather, the cooking smoke rises indistinguishably,

At night, the lights across the river are hard to distinguish reality from illusion.

Accommodation and dining: 80% of Hongcun are farmhouses. Stay in a farmhouse, eat farm food. Don't rush to check in, do what makes you happy. Standard rooms generally range from 40 to 60 yuan. Shrimp was taken by a warm-hearted aunt to a guesthouse called "Yijia Guesthouse", which is a three-story building and the tallest structure in Hongcun. The price was negotiated down to 30 yuan, and a home-cooked meal with two dishes and one soup cost 30 yuan, nice. Aunt Zhu's phone number is 05595541311, she is very warm-hearted.

Shrimp's insight: To understand Hongcun culture, it's best to follow a tour guide! The advantage of wandering on your own is that you can take pictures without others in the background. Fortunately, you can hire a free guide with just two people, and she can help you take group photos, hooray!!

Shrimp's insight: You'll find that souvenirs and specialties in Hongcun are very cheap, and buying them is a good deal. However, you can still bargain. For example, Wucheng bean curd (a box of 14 small bags) originally costs 15 yuan, but you can get it for 10 yuan. Hui ink candy costs 12 yuan but can be negotiated down to 8 yuan.

Shrimp's insight: The night market barbecue over the small bridge in Hongcun has a unique flavor, and you must visit it. Moreover, things like crafts and pastries are cheaper there.

Shrimp's insight: After visiting Hongcun, go see Xidi. Xidi has a much richer historical and cultural atmosphere, and lacks some of the rural pastoral charm of Hongcun.
