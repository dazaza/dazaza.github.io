**Must-read for Bosses: Things to Consider When Selecting Work Phones for Employees**

When using "Enterprise Access Master," some bosses choose to let employees use their own phones, and in return, give them a small subsidy for using personal devices. Other companies prefer to provide employees with unified work phones. Both methods are good. If you choose the first method, please pay attention to the following issues:

1. The differences between phones on the market can be significant. Although they are all smartphones, variations in smartphone operating system versions and manufacturers may lead to different experiences when using "Enterprise Access Master." Please remind your employees that it's best not to use low-quality, non-brand-name phones. These phones may have quality issues, making it harder for us to determine whether problems arise from the phone or the software system we provided.

2. Avoid using phones produced many years ago, such as smartphones with a resolution of 240x320. These phones may exhibit poor display effects and slow operation speeds when using "Enterprise Access Master."

If you choose the second method—providing employees with unified company phones—the company might opt to collaborate with carriers to handle phone services. This has several advantages:

1. The company doesn’t need to pay for the phones directly but can receive one as part of a consumption agreement.

2. The phone numbers belong to the company, so when an employee leaves, the number can be reassigned to someone else.

3. Employees can make free calls to each other, and in some cases, carriers even offer free calls between mobile phones and the company’s landline.

However, there are also things to note:

1. Carrier signals vary by region. In some areas, China Telecom provides better coverage; in others, China Unicom or China Mobile may be better. Try to get input from your employees.

2. China Mobile phones often have slower internet speeds, with only 2G signals available in many regions and no 3G. If you need to use "Enterprise Access Master" (especially for uploading photos), test the speed beforehand. China Unicom phones sometimes have weak signals in remote areas, so if your employees frequently work in rural areas, consult with them.

3. Opt for carrier contracts lasting 1 or 2 years. Some carriers promote deals where the company consumes over 3 years to receive a free phone. However, the industry consensus is that the lifespan of a smartphone is around 2 years. Choosing a 3-year contract increases the risk of many phones being obsolete or damaged after 2 years.

Regardless of whether you purchase phones through a carrier agreement, consider the following when selecting phones:

1. What happens if a phone breaks or is stolen? It could directly impact business operations. For example, if orders cannot be placed or sales data cannot be reported for a day, it would be difficult for the company to accept. Therefore, always prepare for this possibility. We suggest either keeping spare phones on hand or encouraging employees to use their personal phones if the company-issued one breaks, or ensuring the logistics department can quickly replace them.

2. Smartphones consume a lot of power, so when choosing phones, try to select ones with longer standby times. Generally, phone batteries have a milliampere-hour (mAh) rating, such as 1500 mAh or 2000 mAh. A higher number usually means a longer standby time. Phones with dual batteries would be even better.

3. Finally, after providing phones to your employees, we recommend equipping them with silicone protective cases to significantly extend the life of the phone!

After mobile phones became indispensable companions, the influence of mobile internet began to permeate into enterprises. It's now time to incorporate location information into corporate management considerations. Developed by Zhongtai Group, "Enterprise Access Master" is a software that uses location information provided by mobile devices to enhance the efficiency of managing employees.

In traditional models, it was difficult for company managers to monitor the real-time locations of employees working outside the office. Data collected by these employees had to be communicated via phone or manually entered upon returning to the office. With the development of smartphones and mobile internet, more convenient location-based management allows companies to manage field employees just as effectively as office-based employees.

Through a mobile app, employees can sign in more promptly and accurately and transmit data collected during their fieldwork directly to the company intranet. Such needs are common in industries like fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) and logistics, where employees frequently work outside the office. Statistics show that over 30 million company employees work outside the office nationwide, and with the increasing penetration of mobile internet into daily life, the popularization of mobile office software is an inevitable trend.

Beyond basic attendance management, "Enterprise Access Master" also offers comprehensive mobile office services, such as managing and updating customer profiles via mobile devices, recording new business opportunities, uploading materials to the company intranet, viewing company notifications, receiving new tasks, writing summaries, applying for expenses, and registering for business trips through the app.
