Investment of the day: ZF Industrial has great performance elasticity＿Stock Knowledge

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 22:00:01

Zhongfu Industrial (600595) (enter the stock bar, new version of market conditions) mainly engages in the production and sales of electrolytic aluminum, stock knowledge. The company's comprehensive earnings per share forecast values for 2009-2011 are respectively 0.44, 0.91, and 1.06 yuan, corresponding to dynamic price-earnings ratios of 53, 25, and 22 times; currently, there are a total of 11 analysts tracking the stock, with 2 analysts recommending "strong buy", 8 analysts recommending "buy", and 1 analyst giving a "hold" rating, with a comprehensive rating coefficient of 1.91. The industrial chain establishes a low-cost advantage. Reviewing each crisis, the secret to the company's leading recovery comes from its self-owned power plant. Even calculated by the total scale after capacity expansion of 670,000 tons, at this stage, the company will have at least 69% of its production capacity able to achieve self-sufficiency in electricity, and the future goal of 100% self-sufficiency in electric energy is not far off. The electricity price will be about 15% lower than the entire industry, and only in terms of electricity, the cost of producing one ton of aluminum is approximately 900 yuan lower than the entire industry.

The company's Q3 performance jumped beyond expectations. During the reporting period, the company cumulatively achieved operating revenue of 42.4658 billion yuan and realized net profit of 2.511 billion yuan, equivalent to a basic earnings per share of 0.38 yuan. In the third quarter, it achieved operating revenue of 18.6677 billion yuan, an increase of 10.16% year-on-year, realized net profit of 1.6942 billion yuan, an increase of 133.14% year-on-year, equivalent to a basic earnings per share of 0.26 yuan. Performance jumped and significantly exceeded expectations.

Southwest Securities stated that the recovery of production and sales volume was one of the important reasons for the jump in performance. During the reporting period, the company is estimated to have cumulatively achieved an electrolytic aluminum output of about 3.2 million tons and sales of about 3.14 million tons, with an output of about 1.2 million tons and sales of about 1.28 million tons in the third quarter. The utilization rate increased, and both production and sales volume returned to normal levels. An increase in government subsidies was another reason for the jump in performance. During the reporting period, the company's non-operating income reached approximately 545.2 million yuan, an increase of 482.84% year-on-year, contributing approximately 0.07 yuan to earnings per share. Excluding this factor, the cumulative earnings per share for the first three quarters were 0.30 yuan.

A horizontal comparison still shows undervaluation. Currently, the premium of the company's stock price relative to the value per share of contained aluminum is lower than other aluminum companies. Meanwhile, looking at the PE valuation, the average PE for comparable other aluminum companies in 2009 was 73 times, and in 2010, it was 59 times, while Zhongfu Industrial had only 58 times and 25 times, respectively. Considering comprehensively, the company is still undervalued in a horizontal comparison.

Southwest Securities said that the company has significant earnings elasticity and high potential for explosive growth. The production cost of 400KA is approximately 700 yuan lower than older slot types. If the aluminum price increases by 200 yuan, EPS will increase by 0.12 yuan, and the earnings elasticity should receive more attention. Given the improvement in the aluminum market (average aluminum price in the first quarter was 12,246 yuan, second quarter 13,300 yuan, third quarter 14,621 yuan, with quarterly increases all exceeding 1,000 yuan), the company's 2009-2010 profit forecasts have been raised to 0.57 yuan and 1.08 yuan. Southwest Securities maintains a "buy" rating on the company's stock, raising the target price to 35.6 yuan.

The procedure for buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. Moreover, this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the commission includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When placing a buy/sell order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must match. At the same time, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks being bought or sold: the quantity of stocks commissioned for purchase must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks commissioned for sale does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four methods of commission: counter submission, telephone automatic commission, computer automatic commission, and remote terminal commission.
