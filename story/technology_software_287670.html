Female stars who don't want to marry into riches

by gulum1154 on 2009-12-01 15:28:53

By Ye Xixi

In the entertainment circle, female stars generally fall into two categories when it comes to their attitudes towards relationships. One type will go to great lengths to enter a wealthy family; the other places love as the top priority, refusing to marry even if offered wealth without genuine affection.

Among those who don't value wealth, there are several notable examples.

Take Maggie Cheung for instance. Her famous saying is: "Love is always something I care about. A woman's success is having a lover by her side when she takes her last breath."

Previously, while watching movies, I used to think Brigitte Lin was the kind of woman steeped in romance, but later realized that Maggie Cheung pursued spiritual connection even more than her.

Maggie Cheung rarely gets entangled in scandals with rich men, and every one of her romances has been deeply heartfelt. At each stage of her life, her requirements for love have varied: at 20 years old, she wanted someone who would pamper her; at 30, she sought longevity in a relationship; at 40, she simply desired happiness.

Regardless of the stage, the person by her side must genuinely love her, but doesn't need to be wealthy. Maggie Cheung is a woman who refuses to spend a man's money. She buys her own clothes and houses, advocating for spiritual independence and self-respect. Perhaps for this reason, she dislikes young people who only pursue material wealth without seeking spiritual fulfillment. In the chaotic world of entertainment, Maggie Cheung lives with such dignity, inevitably leading to a certain loneliness.

Another example is Big S (Siao-suan), who pays more attention to whether the person beside her is handsome or not. Falling in love with a good-looking man requires courage, and Big S has never lacked in this aspect. Her idol, Takeshi Kaneshiro, is a handsome man with charming eyes. For Big S, rich men are far less appealing than handsome ones.

Pearl奶茶 (Ruby Lin) has also never been involved in any scandal with wealthy men. Pearl is a workaholic with little time for romance. In her limited free time, the people she encounters are often married or already in relationships. For her, who is steadfast in her pursuit of true love, it's better to remain single than to settle for someone unsuitable.

These women who view money lightly naturally place greater importance on emotions. The needs of love and marriage may always differ. The intensity of romantic passion cannot last forever, and the feelings that persist through marriage should be as bland as water, not intense but enduring.

Yet, they find it hard to completely ignore material aspects.

Living alone, they appear lonely in the eyes of outsiders. Only they themselves know that everything is still alright. When alone, Maggie Cheung can go out without makeup, wear no luxury brands, ride a bicycle or take the subway across the streets of Paris. If there's no good movie role, she'd rather stay home and cook, enjoying the pleasure of fine cuisine; Pearl can stay at home for seven days without going out, enjoying the joy of watching DVDs, soaking in the bathtub, and reading books. Big S still eagerly anticipates her next romance with a handsome man.

Love is also about fate, and cannot be forced. Let things be as they are, cherishing the present happiness is the most important thing.
