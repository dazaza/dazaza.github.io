Lenovo sets target: to surpass HP in the European market by 2015.

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:52:35

Even as it begins to expand into the tablet and smartphone markets, Lenovo Group, the world's largest PC manufacturer, still aims to surpass HP in the European market by 2015, becoming the largest PC manufacturer in that market.

Gianfranco Lanci, President of Lenovo Group for Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, said that Lenovo believes it has "strong" potential for expansion in the European market. Lanci stated, "We hope to become the leader in this market within the next 18 months. This is expected to happen by early 2015. The entire European region offers very significant growth opportunities; there is still room for growth in this market."

Data from market research firm Gartner shows that although HP remains the leader in the European PC market with an 18% share in the second quarter, its share is being steadily eroded by Lenovo Group. Lenovo's PC shipments in Europe surpassed Acer in the second quarter, increasing its market share to 12%.

By launching new laptops and devices that integrate laptops and tablets, Lenovo Group aims to become the dominant force in the European PC market. This week, Lenovo unveiled three new ThinkPad laptops, all lighter and thinner than previous models.

To date, Michael Thacker, a spokesperson for HP, has not commented on this report.

Entering the European Market

Lenovo's other devices are also entering the European market. On Thursday, the company showcased a high-end smartphone in Berlin to challenge market leaders Samsung Electronics and Apple. Lenovo's new smartphone, the Vibe X, features a 5-inch display and runs Google's Android 4.2 operating system. It will be launched in the Chinese market in October this year and internationally in December.

Lanci said in an interview, "We plan to enter the European smartphone market by mid-2014." The price range for Lenovo smartphones in Europe will be between €149 and €499 (approximately $200 to $650 USD).

With affordably priced phones, Lenovo has become China's second-largest smartphone manufacturer and the world's fourth-largest. Now, Lenovo is planning to launch devices that can directly compete with Samsung's Galaxy S4 or Apple's iPhone. In May, Lenovo released its flagship smartphone, the K900, and the introduction of the Vibe X will further expand the company’s smartphone lineup.

Lenovo has also set a target to capture 5% of the tablet market share in Europe, the Middle East, and Africa this year, aiming for 10% by 2014. Lanci stated, "We need to achieve the same level of market share with tablets as we do with PCs. This will take at least two years."

Organic Growth

Lanci said that to sustain the company's expansion plans, "from a geographical and product perspective, if it makes financial sense, Lenovo is open to acquisitions. Of course, we don't rely on acquisitions for growth; we can achieve organic growth." Lanci added that Lenovo is currently not considering any specific acquisition issues.

Lanci said, "The investment required for smartphone and tablet businesses far exceeds that needed for the PC business. This is why we believe the industry will see further consolidation. Without sufficient scale, one cannot effectively compete in the market."

Lanci indicated that Lenovo's three divisions are currently profitable, though the PC division enjoys higher profit margins due to the greater investment needs of the tablet and smartphone businesses. Lanci stated, "We have been very careful in balancing profitability across all our divisions."
