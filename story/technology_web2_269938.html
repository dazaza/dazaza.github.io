Four Techniques for Assessing Upside Potential

When the market is in an uptrend, it's crucial for investors to quickly assess the upside potential and approximate range of the main upsurge. This way, investors can adopt suitable investment strategies and corresponding holding periods based on the main upsurge's potential and space for increase. The upside potential of a bull market can be assessed through the following four aspects:

1. Assessing from the rhythm of the rise

If the market's upward rhythm is very monotonous, with a smooth straight-line rise, it is instead unfavorable for the sustained rise of the market. If the market's rise maintains a strong rhythm with three steps forward and two back, with constant occurrences of volatile adjustments, it is instead favorable for the continuous development of the market. For example, after the "5·19" rally in 1999, it triggered a bull market lasting for two years, with the stock index rising from over 1000 points to 2245 points. However, the entire rise was not smooth; it experienced multiple ups and downs. Only in a continuously oscillating and washing-out rise can the main force fully establish positions and stir up the market, making it more conducive for the market to develop further.

Not only is this true for the overall market, but it also applies to individual stocks. Investors should be cautious about stocks that have risen too smoothly and excessively, as they may accumulate risks.

2. Assessing from trading volume

Many investors mistakenly believe that the higher the trading volume during a bull market, the better. This is incorrect. Everything must have a limit. If the trading volume increases too early or excessively, it will excessively consume the upward momentum, causing the stock index to stagnate after a brief rise.

On the contrary, if the trading volume moderately increases, the energy won't be overly consumed. This indicates that there is still some caution among investors, and the general public has not unanimously turned bullish. Therefore, the market still has the potential to continue its upward trend. The most ideal scenario is when the trading volume moderately and continuously increases during the initial stage of the market movement, but slightly decreases as the market enters a normal upward channel, maintaining this state for a longer period.

3. Assessing from market hotspots

A cohesive leading stock and sector act as the engine of a rising market. In a sense, the type of leading stock determines the type of rising market. Stocks with deep potential for further development, continuous upward potential, appeal, and convenience for large-scale mainstream capital inflows and outflows often drive a powerful upward trend.

The most noteworthy hotspot sector in the market is undoubtedly blue-chip stocks. Due to the substantial financial strength behind blue-chip stock speculation, their trends often exhibit inertia characteristics. Mainstream capital participates in blue-chip stock operations for a long time and takes a long time to exit, making it difficult for the main force to use quick-exit trading techniques.

Under normal circumstances, blue-chip stocks require a repeated oscillation and consolidation selling technique. In terms of price patterns, they often exhibit a rounded top or multi-peak pattern for selling. This provides investors participating in blue-chip stock speculation with ample time to profitably exit. Investors can utilize the speculative characteristics of blue-chip stocks to sell at high prices when indicator stocks show obvious signs of stagnation, aiming to maximize profits.

4. Assessing from policy factors

A strong bull market cannot proceed without significant policy support. Several major market movements in China's stock market have been initiated and driven by policies. For example, Deng Xiaoping's southern tour speech in 1992 triggered a bull market with more than a double increase. The "5·19" rally received substantial policy support. When the stock index rose to over 1300 points and the market showed hesitation, editorials were published stating that the current market was a restorative market, fundamentally strengthening investor confidence. Thereafter, substantial and concrete major policy benefits were continuously implemented, such as allowing three types of enterprises to enter the market, thus establishing a grand bull market.
