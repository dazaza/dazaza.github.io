Electric blankets and other sleep-related items are nine things to do with sleep.

by wan789d4ex46 on 2011-04-29 09:23:10

1. Going to bed with makeup on

Some women, especially young women, don't remove their makeup before sleeping. Residual skin cosmetics can clog pores, not only likely to cause acne but over time will damage the skin and accelerate aging.

2. Sleeping in a bra

The bra plays a protective role for the breast during the day, but sleeping in a bra at night may induce breast cancer.

Studies have found that women who wear bras for more than 12 hours a day have a risk of breast cancer more than 20 times higher than those who wear bras for a short time or don't wear them at all. This is due to long-term pressure on the breast, which blocks lymphatic return, causing harmful substances to be retained in the breast.

3. Sleeping with jewelry on

Some women go to sleep without removing their accessories, which can be very harmful to the body. Some jewelry is made of metal, and long-term wear can cause some wear and tear on the skin, unknowingly causing toxic reactions. Some luminous jewelry can produce radioactive radiation, although the accumulation of weak amounts, but over a long period, it may lead to adverse consequences. Sleep is not conducive to wearing jewelry metabolism, which easily causes local skin aging.

4. Drinking alcohol and going to sleep

With lifestyle changes, young women’s nightlife is now more abundant, particularly professional women who are more involved in entertainment activities, often accompanied by drunkenness before sleep. According to medical studies, drinking alcohol before going to bed is prone to suffocation after falling asleep, usually around two times per night, about 10 minutes each time of suffocation. Over time, people become susceptible to heart disease and high blood pressure and other diseases.

5. Hooded sleeping

Some people love to sleep hooded, especially in the winter. Because air does not flow, the blanket doesn't have enough oxygen for the body organs to get enough oxygen supply, resulting in waking up feeling dizzy, fatigued, lacking energy, and also inducing dreaming and frequent nightmares, making one easily awakened from dreams.

6. Parents and children sleeping together

Young children sleeping with parents often result in the child being in the middle, causing discomfort for the parents.

7. Clothing on behalf of

In cold winter nights, some parents fear their children catching a cold, so they often let kids wear sweaters, cotton vests, or even sleep in Maoku. In the sleep state, the trunk, limbs, and muscles are relaxed, and sleep is sounder and more relaxed for the muscles. Wearing too many clothes while sleeping will affect the relaxation of the muscles, not conducive to the circulation and respiratory functions. Due to clothing pressure, the child may experience nightmares, night terrors, etc., as well as possible suffocation leading to illness. Excessive clothing leads to high temperatures, easy sweating, and if the children don't wake up to change wet clothes and add clothing promptly, they are prone to catch colds.

8. Sleeping with arms elevated under the pillow

Many people like to sleep with their arms elevated under the pillow. Sleeping in this position is very harmful to health. Because the arm elevation is not conducive to the shoulder and upper arm muscles relaxing, over time it can cause shoulder pain and discomfort. Placing the arm on a pillow will also affect the upper limb blood circulation, causing hand numbness. Additionally, arm elevation may cause an increase in intra-abdominal pressure, making food easy to regurgitate from the stomach into the esophagus, along with digestive juices, stimulating esophageal mucosa, causing congestion, edema, and even reflux esophagitis.

9. Sleeping with an electric blanket

Leaving the electric blanket on all night not only makes people wake up feeling thirsty but also makes them susceptible to colds. The ideal temperature when sleeping in bed is 33-35 ℃, with relative humidity of 55%-60%, in this rest and adjustment. If the electric blanket heating time is too long, continuous high temperature inside the bed will cause skin blood vessels to expand, blood circulation and breathing become deeper and faster, reducing the ability to resist bacteria, easily leading to colds. Therefore, the proper use of electric blankets is recommended: power on for 10 minutes before going to sleep, turn off the power when bedding is preheated, and as long as the covers are warm enough to not feel cold suddenly.
