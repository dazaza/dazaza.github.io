The first wave of ace mobile games on WeChat begins to "recede" collectively.

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:38:46

The first batch of three WeChat mobile games has begun a collective retreat. Although the industry understands that the next wave of WeChat games will continue to sweep in, leaving the industry at a loss, the occurrence of the retreat phenomenon somewhat reflects the current situation of WeChat's single-player casual games: WeChat mobile games have a cycle. Even with WeChat, it is impossible to create eternal greatness; they are normal game products.

Today, looking at the iPhone revenue chart, "Rhythm Master" currently ranks 7th in the revenue chart, "Everyday Love Elimination" ranks 9th, and the simpler-to-play "Everyday Connect" has dropped to the 22nd position in the revenue chart. The iPad chart is even more evident: "Rhythm Master" ranks 13th in the revenue chart, "Everyday Love Elimination" ranks 24th, and "Everyday Connect" ranks 48th. From this ranking, WeChat mobile game products can maintain their position in the top 10 of the App Store revenue chart for 6-8 weeks, while individual products, such as "Everyday Connect," only maintained their top 10 revenue position for 2 weeks. On the free chart, WeChat mobile games couldn't continue to dominate the top 3. "Jailbreak!" mysteriously occupied the third place, pushing "Everyday Love Elimination" to the fourth place, indicating a decline in the daily downloads of single WeChat games.

Another ace product, PopCap's "Plants vs Zombies 2," could not escape the dilemma of the lifecycle of single-player games. Currently, "Plants vs Zombies 2" ranks 27th on the iPhone revenue chart and 14th on the iPad revenue chart. Although "Plants vs Zombies 2" remained in the top 10 of the App Store revenue chart for 5 weeks and received an update, it is still a single-player game. However, "Plants vs Zombies 2" will officially release its Chinese Android version across various channels this month, and its revenue will remain high or even increase significantly.

The performance of these three WeChat games is believed to be within Tencent's acceptable range. After all, from the perspective of input-output ratio, these three relatively low-investment light games quickly generated nearly 100 million yuan in monthly income, which is extremely profitable. However, after this "retreat," the industry has also discovered some patterns. These single-player games will eventually find their rightful place, but they may not long-term dominate the top 10. Moreover, if even user-adaptable single-player casual games launched by Tencent cannot maintain their revenue rankings, then wouldn't launching heavy games also encounter some unavoidable practical problems?

If it were a heavy mobile game product on WeChat, after the novelty wears off for users, would Tencent really just let the revenue ranking drop? Who will continue to guide traffic for WeChat games? Would Tencent have to join the army purchasing ad traffic and brand promotion resources? Gamelook believes that Tencent must have many preparations for mobile traffic, but competing for traffic resources with other CPs (Content Providers) is not without possibility. If this happens, it will exacerbate the rise in user costs in the industry.

Which type of mobile game—light, moderate, or heavy—will be the reliance for Tencent's dominance in the rankings? If I were to choose for Tencent, combining the current status of LINE and KAKAO, I would prefer moderate games for Tencent. Moderate games can effectively utilize WeChat's viral channel to attract users and generate scaled income. For light games, Tencent needs more engaging game products. For heavy games, they possess explosive power in the short term for Tencent, but in the long term, external forces might still be needed to maintain stable income. What is this external force? Tencent hasn't shown yet.

What will Tencent do? As WeChat continues to launch 10 new games in the second half of the year, the industry will understand WeChat's strategy, preparing for the heavyweight game showdown next year.

This analysis provides insights into the dynamics and strategies surrounding WeChat’s mobile gaming ecosystem, highlighting the challenges and opportunities ahead for Tencent.
