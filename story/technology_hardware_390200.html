For clothing brands, there should be no delay in setting up an online store.

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:31:46

Should traditional clothing brands open online stores? Are online stores taking customers away from physical stores? Online stores are more suitable for standardized products, but are they appropriate for fashion? Will the prices on online stores impact physical stores? These questions have caused hesitation among decision-makers of clothing brand companies regarding whether to invest in opening online stores. Currently, there are many discussions about "online stores vs physical stores." Based on my experience in helping a traditional Chinese-style brand open an online store, I believe that it is timely and necessary for brand companies to open online stores.

Attitude: Numerous doubts about opening online stores

Currently, many well-known traditional brands still have various doubts about opening online stores. These doubts mainly include the following aspects: For example, if a brand's target consumers are young people aged 14-28, who are also the main group of internet users, the brand worries that as this part of the consumer base shops more online, sales through offline channels will decrease; Another example is a traditional old-established merchant whose target consumers are women over 35 years old. This group of consumers does not overlap much with the primary users of the internet. If an online store is opened, it would merely move the original offline business to an online platform, attracting basically the same consumers, raising concerns about whether it can expand the number of consumers; There are also worries about the presence of counterfeit and substandard products on online shopping platforms, inconsistent product quality, chaotic product pricing, leading to complaints from offline agents or franchisees, reducing consumer recognition and evaluation of the brand, and damaging the consumer experience.

In today’s rapidly developing e-commerce environment, one online store brand after another has emerged, such as Taobao, Vancl, Masamasso, etc. These emerging online stores, which specialize in clothing sales, are absorbing new consumer groups at an astonishing speed, devouring the customer base of traditional clothing brands. Their performance is growing exponentially, quickly reaching tens of millions in revenue. Of course, while online stores are rapidly developing, they also have many inherent shortcomings. Since online stores are virtual shops, most consumers usually question their credibility. Additionally, because consumers cannot directly touch the products, they cannot experience them, giving them a feeling of intangibility.

When most brands with physical stores are struggling to integrate emerging e-commerce with traditional businesses, some large online stores have already begun to seize territory, even setting up their own physical stores to solve the experience issues of online customers. Some traditional large malls, under the impact of the new wave of e-commerce, feel very confused, worried about being used as large fitting rooms for brands. They are also contemplating change, realizing that if they do not quickly enter the network era, they will be attacked from all sides. Traditional clothing brand companies should more quickly raise the banner of brand credit, rapidly develop their official online flagship stores, and establish a model of factory + mouse + consumer.

Regarding whether current online stores are profitable, many voices say "in exploration," but in fact, many brand enterprises have already started making big money by integrating online stores and physical stores in their marketing strategies. For instance, Aimer Underwear's online store has sold tens of thousands of units of certain items, and Li-Ning Sports Apparel's online store has reached hundreds of millions in revenue. Therefore, investing in an online store is also investing in the future and valuing the new experiences of consumers. Do not focus solely on immediate interests. When the brand's own online experience is sufficient to make consumers dependent, that will be the time of great profit for the online store.

Case: Initial results from pioneering enterprises

Now let's look at some specific data from a clothing company providing online store services. This is a company with about 15 years of history, mainly designing, producing, and selling Chinese-style clothing, such as cheongsams, formal wear, leisurewear, and wedding dresses. It currently has more than 40 physical stores, with a customer base aged 35 and above, mostly women. In fact, when we discussed opening an online store with this traditional brand, we encountered the issues and concerns mentioned earlier. After nearly six months of negotiation, they decided in September 2010 to open an online store, i.e., the brand's official online flagship store.

In the three months since the online flagship store went live, without any advertising investment, statistics show that the website attracted over ten thousand views from nearly 30 countries worldwide. The daily website traffic ranges from dozens to hundreds of visitors, with nearly 20 transactions completed. From analyzing the sources of consumer orders, there are orders from abroad like the United States, orders from regions within the country where there are no physical stores, orders from young children buying gifts for their parents, orders for young people preparing for weddings or events, orders purchasing discounted items during promotions, group-buying orders, and off-season purchase orders (e.g., summer products not available in stores during autumn and winter). At the same time, many people hope to buy in physical stores, asking for the location of physical stores and customizing details.

Actually, opening an online store is not just about placing product photos on the online store and calling it a day; it also requires positioning for both the online store and its products, as well as promoting and building the online store's brand.

When setting up this brand's flagship store, we established several priorities beforehand: First, the prices marked on the online store should not be lower than those in physical stores; second, the product displays in the online store and physical stores should be basically consistent; third, the online store should aim to develop new customer groups for the brand while maintaining good service for existing customers. With these basic elements in place, the work of the online store will have clear direction. For example, we defined this brand's online store as the official online flagship store, and in the early stages, heavily promoted the concept of a traditional brand opening an official online flagship store through online resources, allowing people to quickly associate this online store with the brand's existing reputation. At the same time, on the basis of retaining mid-to-high prices, we conveyed the brand value and the concept of getting value for money to the target group through online marketing methods. During the promotion of the online store, we received many emails reflecting that the brand's products were too expensive and asking if they could be cheaper. Thus, a mid-to-high-end brand online store was established.

In the second phase, we appropriately introduced some discounted products. When people see low-priced products based on the impression of high prices, they may feel the urge to purchase. Of course, appropriate discounts can bring good experience and consumption desire to consumers. In fact, many online shoppers hope to buy high-quality products at a good price, especially branded products, which is the driving force of the brand. From the results of the past three months, more people purchased products priced over 1,000 yuan, and even ordered products close to ten thousand yuan. Group-buying transactions facilitated through the online store brought in hundreds of thousands of income. Subsequently, in interviews with physical store employees, some employees expressed that they originally worried that more customers would switch to shopping online, but in reality, the online store introduced many customers to the physical store, which was confirmed through the interactive code system between the online store and the physical store. Meanwhile, the online store provided a convenient way for customers who tried clothes in physical stores but did not complete purchases to buy their favorite products again. Through the aforementioned interactive code system, it also brought post-sale commission income to the employees of physical stores.

Focus: Fully emphasize resource integration

Through the above specific case, we summarized: Opening an official online flagship store for a well-known brand can not only interact between physical stores and online stores but also expand the brand's consumer group (80% of registered users are under 35 years old, mostly women), covering a wider sales area, serving as an excellent supplement and extension to physical stores. Online stores can make up for the shortcomings of physical stores in terms of time, providing 24-hour service anywhere, meeting many demands outside the 8-hour operating hours; it can help brand manufacturers effectively sell out-of-season goods, promoting full-season goods sales (which physical stores cannot achieve); it can also deliver many customers to physical stores.

Although theoretically, online and offline sales channels for clothing brands can be perfectly integrated, this integration comes with conditions. If a company relies entirely on its own strength and invests a lot of effort into developing online channels, it might take detours. The fusion of online and offline channels also requires a long period of time. To solve this problem, we suggest outsourcing the work of establishing traditional brand online stores to professional companies. This can save the company's costs, and opening a new channel is like opening a new physical franchise store. Because for a brand to independently invest in an online store requires many resource inputs, such as hiring IT professionals. These professionals may lack work experience in the clothing industry, requiring long-term cooperation and training with the existing team. This is equivalent to entering a new business field, far from the brand's original business. The development of online store business should be a process of resource integration. For example, Oboda Brand Management Company uses rich experience in integrated marketing and brand management, as well as IT professional background, to help traditional clothing brand companies realize their dream of e-commerce by opening online flagship stores with minimal cost.
