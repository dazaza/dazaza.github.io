ALIBABA Group VP Liang Chunxiao: The development concept for Taobao's next ten years

by ci4837223 on 2011-08-25 10:57:26

Recently, the tenth China Internet Conference and the third Chinese Netizen Civilized Festival was held at the Beijing International Conference core. The theme of this conference is "Network China Responsibility and Vitality". It has set up three major sectors including the Internet and services, the Internet and advanced culture, and the Internet and people's livelihood, and held more than ten characteristic sub-forums on mobile Internet, electronic commerce, cloud computing, entrepreneurship, IPv6, network media, etc. to jointly review the development process of China's Internet and look forward to future trends. The holding of this conference received warm response and support from the industry, gathering government leaders, representatives of universities and research institutions, representatives of investment institutions, entrepreneurs in the field of mobile Internet and traditional Internet.

Below is the live broadcast record of the speech titled "The Development Concept of Taobao Network for the Next Decade" delivered by Liang Chunxiao, Senior Vice President of Alibaba Group.

Liang Xiaochun: The host just said too much, which made me feel a lot of pressure. Good afternoon everyone. Today I will briefly share with you my latest insights on e-commerce in recent times. Last year, our total e-commerce transaction volume was approximately 450 billion yuan, with retail transactions around 500 billion yuan. The overall transaction volume accounted for about 4%, while retail accounted for about 3%. The total transaction volume reached the trillion yuan level, which is a preliminary conclusion of scale. In fact, we may have moved from the billion yuan level to the trillion yuan level after 2006 and 2007. According to the 12th Five-Year Plan, there is a planned goal to reach 12 trillion yuan in e-commerce transactions by 2015, accounting for about 20% of GDP. Similarly, according to retail projections, it should reach two trillion yuan by 2015. I have also seen more data suggesting three trillion yuan, which would account for around 10% of the total social consumption goods at that time.

Now, we are currently at a very critical stage, as we move from the billion yuan level to the trillion yuan level, and from retail to the hundred billion yuan level entering the billion yuan level era. New things are emerging, leading to many insights about e-commerce.

Firstly, in recent years, the rapid rise of e-commerce may be due to important events happening in this industry, which have changed the landscape of e-commerce. Before, when we looked at e-commerce, we mainly focused on its applications. Now, we see that to a large extent, we regard e-commerce services as a powerful engine. Regardless of the continuous evolution of the entire national industry itself, the earliest platforms or trading platform service industries emerged, providing support. In the last one or two years, we have observed that the fastest-growing active sector is the emergence of e-commerce derivatives. You may notice that Taobao mentions many third-party partners collaborating on Taobao. There are thousands or even tens of thousands, all engaged in e-commerce services.

Warehousing and IT websites have extremely fine divisions of labor, and these derivative service industries have developed rapidly in recent years. Currently, there are roughly two types: one type is what we commonly refer to as business process outsourcing, where each link in the business process can give rise to a new emerging service industry. The second type is IT service outsourcing, which together form the main force of the current development of derivative service industries. How it will further diversify in the future remains unknown. Every one or two months, we discover new species, and there are constant interactions and fusions between species, giving rise to new species. This has been an exceptionally rapid development in e-commerce over the past two years.

Conversely, because e-commerce is active, it reduces our e-commerce costs, improves efficiency, and promotes the development of e-commerce applications.

The second point I want to make is that previously, we largely viewed e-commerce at a microeconomic level. Ten years ago, when we talked about enterprise applications of e-commerce, reducing costs and improving efficiency, we were addressing this issue at a microeconomic level. In reality, with the emergence of e-commerce services, many industries have undergone tremendous changes, not just at the microeconomic level. It may be an industry-level issue that continues to develop. Nowadays, we increasingly talk about economic structural transformation, changes in economic growth patterns, etc. We can see that the activity of e-commerce is evident. Recently, a particularly significant change is that many enterprises, scholars, and governments view the impact and role of e-commerce from a macroeconomic perspective. I now feel that high-level decision-making institutions' attention to e-commerce has surpassed the microeconomic level.

Starting from the end of last year, I began researching e-commerce with the Social Sciences Academy. We felt that the impact of e-commerce exceeds the scope of the economy. It affects employment, work methods, and even societal development models. I just came back from Yiwu, where the entire market underwent a complete transformation. After the traditional markets moved away, the entire community's housing was rented out, but later e-commerce brought it back, far exceeding the original situation, and the entire economy has changed. Therefore, our re-recognition of the second point is that we may no longer limit e-commerce to the microeconomic level, or even merely at the economic level. Instead, we view its impact more from a societal perspective.

Thirdly, many friends here have been paying attention to e-commerce for the past ten years. Back then, the most frequently mentioned topics were the three big mountains: credit, payment, and logistics. Looking back, overcoming these three big mountains has achieved some results. Solving payment problems led to the emergence of several third-party payment systems. To establish credit, many credit systems were created, and the credit evaluation system is Taobao's core thing. Similarly, logistics have continued to develop. If we have reached this stage, our understanding of the three key factors constraining the future development of e-commerce has evolved. My opinion is that issues related to credit and payment have already been entirely resolved. Logistics, on the other hand, is not that it hasn't developed; it's just that its development speed hasn't kept up with the development of e-commerce. Last year, there was a statistic showing that the number of packages handled by China Post every day was 10 million, with six million coming from e-commerce, accounting for 60%. This proportion will be higher this year without a doubt, and it should be over 70%.

This development speed is too fast, and logistics will still become a particularly crucial problem we need to overcome in the future. We may also encounter two problems that we didn't face ten years ago: the first is data. The sheer volume of data and its importance were probably not well recognized ten years ago. E-commerce continuously generates data, with dozens of Gs of data being generated every day, constantly increasing. This poses high demands on processing efficiency and cost. The entire role of data in business is also becoming increasingly apparent. In the past two years, I have observed a particularly interesting phenomenon.

There is a seller on Taobao who might not originally have been involved in business but studied mathematics. During one of our evaluations of online merchants, among the top 30, one was a doctor, not a student. Why are they so knowledgeable? If you carefully study, it is largely due to their emphasis on data. In the application of e-commerce, it represents an entirely new business model. The entire process of data centralization shows that the role of data is becoming increasingly significant. From two aspects, we need to consider how to respond to the challenges brought by data.

In 2008, many people noticed that many existing policies, laws, and regulations at the institutional level are increasingly incompatible. Officially speaking, the contradictions between production relations and productive forces will become increasingly prominent. Solving these issues may take some time, and we need to remain optimistic. We must continuously adjust our policies and regulations to adapt to the original business activities, thereby creating a better environment and a better situation for the development of e-commerce.

We are transitioning from the three big mountains of credit, payment, and logistics ten years ago to the three key factors of system, logistics, and data for the next decade. How we grasp these three key factors is both a challenge and an opportunity for everyone in the internet industry and those interested in future economic development. Thank you.
