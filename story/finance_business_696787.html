critically ill child leaves a will, father applies for relief and hijacks Red Cross staff

by yanx328fxue on 2012-03-08 10:43:55

Little Sun Kai: The note left by Sun Wenhui for his wife

Report by our station's correspondent in Cangzhou, Dai Qing (text and images)

Key points:

On one side is the son whose condition has worsened, on the other side is a family heavily in debt. On March 31, 36-year-old villager Sun Wenhui from Guosi Village, Guozewa Township, Hejian City went to Beijing to apply for relief funds for his son. Due to not meeting the regulations, he failed to get the money and lost control of his emotions, holding a knife to the staff of the China Red Cross Foundation, and was detained at the detention center in Dongcheng District, Beijing. This made the already suffering family even worse off. On April 19, after our station's correspondent in Beijing reported on the plight of Sun Wenhui's family, our station's correspondent in Cangzhou revisited Sun Wenhui's home, visiting Little Sun Kai who was seriously ill, and interviewing the hardships and helplessness of this unfortunate family.

■Villagers' Comments: He had no other choice

"Sun Wenhui is such an honest person." "He had no other choice, that's why he did something foolish."

...

In the afternoon of April 19, when the reporter visited Sun Wenhui's home in Guosi Village, Guozewa Township, Hejian City, many villagers surrounded the reporter and spoke non-stop.

Neighbors told the reporter that Sun Wenhui usually relied on collecting sheep to supply kebabs for barbecue stalls, while his wife Liu Wenfang worked in a restaurant. The couple were very hardworking. They had just managed to repay the debts they incurred during their wedding, but then their child fell seriously ill. "To treat their child, the couple even sold blood, it was so pitiful." "Little Sun Kai is such a likable child. When we learned about his illness, the whole village donated money for him." A sister said. Many villagers expressed that farmers struggle to make ends meet, and facing such a serious illness, no family could come up with hundreds of thousands of yuan. "Wenhui just felt as a father, without money to treat his child, he felt sorry for his child, so he had to take such an extreme path. Although he broke the law, we all understand him."

■Unable to Obtain Relief: The family cried together

At Sun Wenhui's home, the reporter met Little Sun Kai who was sick and his mother Liu Wenfang. Compared to the photos taken before he got sick, Sun Kai's face was obviously swollen. Due to persistent high fever over the past few days, the child looked listless.

When talking about her son's illness over the past year and the experience of applying for relief from the China Red Cross Foundation, Liu Wenfang choked up several times.

Upon learning that the Red Cross had no specific relief for acute aplastic anemia, Liu Wenfang said, "We walked out slowly with our child, crying loudly at the entrance of the Red Cross, feeling that the last bit of hope was gone." More touching was that seeing the sorrowful and desperate looks of his parents, 11-year-old Sun Kai comforted them saying, "It's too hard to ask others for help. You don't need to beg people for me anymore. Buy me some good food, and even if I die, I won't feel wronged."

■Poor Child: Solemnly stating three "wills"

Liu Wenfang choked up again and said that around December 25th or 26th of last year, she and her husband took their son who had a persistent high fever back to Tianjin for treatment with the 6000 yuan they had borrowed with great difficulty. The doctor shook his head, indicating "the chances are slim." In the ward, Sun Kai called his parents over and insisted they agree to his three conditions.

Liu Wenfang said that her son solemnly said, "First, don't hold a funeral after I die, don't spend any more money on me; second, after I die, you must have a younger brother or sister, I will bless your happiness and health in heaven; third, after I die, mom and dad must not argue anymore, both of you have it tough."

After hearing these words from her son, Liu Wenfang said that not only her couple, but also the patients and doctors in the ward cried.

Seeing how understanding his son was, Sun Wenhui felt even more distressed. He told Liu Wenfang more than once that if one day his son passed away, he wouldn't want to live either. "The relationship between my husband and the child was particularly close," Liu Wenfang said, since childhood, her husband never hit their son. Sometimes when she lost her temper and hit the child, her husband always pleaded for leniency. "The child is already 11 years old, but he still likes to sleep with his dad, often asking his dad to carry or hug him, being affectionate, and instead, I, the mother, feel neglected."

In Sun Kai's medical record book, the reporter saw a long piece of text: "Wenfang, I'm sorry, I have to leave again. I can't leave our son. I must find a way to save our son."

Liu Wenfang said that after the Spring Festival, when the child urgently needed money for blood transfusions and platelets, her husband left this message intending to go out and raise money, but was stopped in time. Unexpectedly, on March 31, her husband left again without saying goodbye, until that night when she received a call from the China Red Cross Foundation, she found out that her husband, in order to treat his child, actually held a knife to a staff member.

■Deep Father-Son Bond: Agreed to be father and son in the next life

Liu Wenfang told the reporter that after the incident involving Sun Wenhui, the government of Guozewa Township sent someone to Beijing, paying ten thousand yuan to help treat the child. At the same time, the local area of Hejian also helped issue proof materials, finally obtaining the forgiveness of the staff member who was held hostage. "On April 7, when accompanying my husband for a psychiatric evaluation, I saw him, he only asked one question, which was how the child was doing, and then he didn't say anything else." Liu Wenfang said, looking at her emaciated husband, her heart was extremely pained.

On April 15, to fulfill her son's wish to see his father, Liu Wenfang carried her son to Beijing again, but the police at the detention center refused to allow them to visit. "When the child heard that he couldn't see his father, he rushed in, shouting 'Dad' loudly towards the big iron gate. Two policemen caught up and dragged us out." Liu Wenfang said, the child was too weak, usually not even having the strength to walk, and after running like that, he started vomiting profusely. She could do nothing but silently shed tears.

After returning home, Sun Kai wrote in his diary: "Dad, on April 15, I went with mom to the Dongcheng Detention Center to see you, but they didn't let me see you. I rushed in, calling out 'dad' several times, but you didn't hear me. Dad, I'm sorry, it was my fault, please forgive me."

Throughout the interview process, Sun Kai quietly listened from the side, without avoiding anything. Taking advantage of his mother's absence, he quietly told the reporter: "Auntie, I know my illness is serious, it means death for me. Now I just want to see my dad, seeing him would make me feel better. Seeing him, what I most want to say is, Dad, you've sacrificed too much for me, in the next life, you'll still be my dad, and I'll still be your son."
