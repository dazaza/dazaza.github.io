Yishu said that, considering everyone's travel fatigue, there would be a small crab feast with some freshwater fish for dinner tonight. Tomorrow, we would have a hearty meal! Everyone nodded in agreement. The female postdoctoral student who ran the charity debt was heavily in debt, owing nearly 500,000 yuan. To be continued, more pictures are stored in the space album... Upon entering the restroom, wow! The toiletries had an extremely provocative appearance!(Photos are stored in the album, breast enhancement ads video, do not disturb minors!) After settling in the hotel and coming out again, it was already dark. Due to bad luck midway through the game, I was taken off the table and had time to look around. A bottle of Lu Ling Wang quietly appeared on the table, accompanied by various river delicacies, and everyone ate happily... except there were too many flies... Holding one in my hand to play with, looking at the crab's claws waving and eight crab legs scratching, I thought they rarely had natural enemies in Liangzi Lake except for humans! The island restaurants all had large glass tanks in front of their doors, filled with crabs separated by weight and gender. Under the flashlights, the crab shells reflected a chilling bluish luster. The water area of Liangzi Lake is about 127 square kilometers, approximately 1/30th the size of Dongting Lake. Without seeing land, there was no sense of security, and I felt a little uneasy inside... After meeting up, we took the Wu Huang Expressway towards Ezhou. Yishu's skilled driving performed excellently, and around 5 pm, we arrived at the Liangzi Lake pier. Back at the hotel, after brewing hot tea, looking outside the window with no lights or places to visit, under the call of aged Pu'er tea, we started playing landlord. Frankly speaking, playing landlord around Wuhan made me feel like I was in the embrace of an organization, extraordinarily intimate! Wuhan is undergoing great development; upon entering the urban area, construction was everywhere, plus the gray sky, making it feel like a big construction site. This glance wasn't trivial, as I actually discovered two shocking slogans behind the door! It sent chills down my spine, almost causing me to spit out the tea in my mouth! Liangzi Lake is so vast, with no visible edges on all sides... All other tourists on the boat had retreated into the cabin, except for me and aged Pu'er sitting at the stern. The overcast sky and damp air blew coldly into our clothes. Not good, I'm wearing too few clothes! After enduring about 30 minutes of cold, we disembarked at the Liangzi Island pier. The air seemed filled with the smell of freshwater fish, including the Chinese mitten crabs. On October 20th, I was "enticed" to Wuhan by Yishu, along with others including aged Pu'er. The first stop of this trip was Liangzi Lake in Ezhou, Hubei Province - an inland freshwater lake that isn't particularly remarkable, but since it's currently the season for tasting Chinese mitten crabs, eating became the top priority. Yishu said that today was lucky not to be the weekend, otherwise foodies from surrounding areas like Wuhan, Huangshi, Xianning, and Ezhou would have swarmed in, resulting in a noisy scene full of crab shells, which wouldn't have been as peaceful now. As I expected, once we entered the village, Chinese mitten crabs were being sold everywhere, truly amazing!(Rhetorically, without crabs, would we even come?) Looking at the prices, compared to Shenzhen, they were surprisingly cheap: those weighing about four taels could be purchased for less than 30 yuan, and they looked exceptionally fierce! A food street stretched before us, with not many tourists. Leaving the airport, we first met up with Yishu on Xudong Street. Along the way, osmanthus fragrance occasionally wafted into the car, lifting our spirits. Aged Pu'er began chatting with the driver, and from their conversation, I discovered that what I had always boasted as my comprehensive knowledge of Wuhan was only mediocre...
