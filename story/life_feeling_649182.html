The Love of Stones

In the second year of college, Miss "Corn" fell in love with "Soybean". He was Corn's teacher, an oil painting instructor who liked to paint fruits and women. He had a flat-top haircut, a mustache, sometimes wore a fishing hat, rarely smiled, and even seemed a bit cold. But all of this, to Corn, was a kind of charm. Corn, who considered herself rather aloof, finally knocked on his door one moonlit night: "Teacher, I'm so cold..."

Without saying a word, Soybean took her inside, embraced her, and gently closed the door. Their age difference was 10 years, which wasn't a problem. The real issue was that at the end of the 1980s, "teacher-student romance" was not allowed, so they could only meet secretly, late at night, somewhat like the rendezvous between a female ghost and a scholar in "Strange Tales from a Chinese Studio." This was exciting for Corn; she enjoyed this kind of clandestine romance. Choosing what kind of boyfriend is actually choosing a certain way of life, or simply showing off your personal taste. Corn has always been arrogant. In her view, although she wasn't the class beauty, she was definitely the most unique one. So, in class and in the art studio, she didn't miss any opportunity to have "eye contact" with Soybean. Indeed, her figure and lively, enthusiastic personality were unmatched. Soybean was an introverted man who liked fiery girls like gunpowder. He usually paid special attention to this student. The eyes of the classmates were sharp; everyone knew he liked Corn. But what made Corn grind her teeth in hatred was that this cold-blooded man never directly gave her even a hint of an unusual look. At most, he would linger behind Corn for a few more minutes, watching her paint attentively. Every time this happened, Corn would feel a faint fragrance emanating from her body, and the teacher's breathing behind her would become increasingly coarse. This was an enticing yet startling signal. After receiving it multiple times, Corn finally made the bold move to knock on his door under the moonlight that night. Thinking back to that night, Corn still feels scared today. It turned out that she could be as brave as Liu Hulan, the female hero, but instead of facing executioners, she faced the love assassin, a male teacher named "Soybean."

Their midnight romance was extraordinarily beautiful. Soybean gradually relaxed, and the wildness he had hidden in his heart finally erupted. Under his influence, Corn's boundless tenderness also unfolded like clouds and mist.

At this moment, a fierce knocking on the door shattered their dream. Leaders from the Art Department and school security guards, each holding a flashlight, stood solemnly in front of Soybean's door, like monuments... The next day, Corn was publicly criticized, and Teacher Soybean was required to write a self-examination letter and deeply reflect; otherwise, he might be expelled.

Corn was extremely frightened because her father had been beaten to death during a previous "movement." At the same time, she felt wronged. She and Soybean had never done anything too outrageous. They believed that the "final line" should be crossed on their wedding night. Their love was just in the "preamble" part. They were still browsing innocently on the first page of love. They drew each other's nudes, using each other as models, but they really maintained their inner passion. However, the relevant leaders did not believe them. If a young man and woman were locked in the same room in the middle of the night and nothing "happened," no one would believe it.

This was a "miscarriage of justice," involving a pure midnight love. At this point, Corn decided to abandon her enrollment. Back then, getting into university was very difficult, but Corn wanted to protect this love and Soybean's career. She applied to drop out of school to the relevant authorities. Thus, she was no longer a college student, more precisely, she was no longer Soybean's student. Therefore, they were no longer involved in a teacher-student romance. Hence, they could come out from underground and openly love each other. Changing roles, they could still happily share various different breaths. Unexpectedly, the relevant parties readily agreed to Corn's request. Soybean opposed Corn making an "unnecessary sacrifice," but he couldn't stop her. Finally, they were together again. Corn rented a bungalow outside and took on some private tutoring jobs. Life must go on. She continued to pretend to be a college student in that city because she couldn't tell her mother the bad news about being expelled.

This incident was resolved satisfactorily. However, their love soon faced a crisis. Public love, for Soybean, lost its fighting power. He no longer held Corn while lecturing her with his vast knowledge. Instead, every time Corn came to see him excitedly, he would light a cigarette and smoke melancholically on the side, almost silent. Each time, Corn had to take the initiative to ask him, and he would lazily answer. Initially, Corn thought he was disheartened by the "incident," but half a month later, Corn keenly sensed an ominous atmosphere. He no longer loved her. He even stopped kissing her. Even when he did kiss, it was icy cold. Corn finally couldn't bear it. That night, she grabbed his shoulders with both hands, looking into his eyes and asked: "Tell me, don't lie. Have you lost feelings for me?" Soybean nodded. Corn slowly let go of him, tears streaming down her face. A year and six months of love sprinting ended this way, like a dream, the first half was a good dream, the second half was a nightmare. Waking up, she realized she had nothing left.

Corn walked through the cold wind of midnight until dawn, feeling pain, then self-reproach. Why had she made so many sacrifices? But in the end, the person she loved became a thief, a thief of emotions who stole her heart and then discarded it casually.

The best medicine for healing such wounds can only be time.

Corn understood that she couldn't fall apart; she had to stand up. So, she worked hard, filling every day tightly with activities. Her jobs were varied: private tutoring, advertising design, drawing portraits for passers-by, painting cultural shirts, and even knitting sweaters... During one sale of cultural shirts, a female college student brought a stone and asked Corn to add a few strokes with a brush to make it a small gift, as her boyfriend was born under the sign of the tiger, hoping Corn could paint the stone to look "tigerish"...

This event greatly touched Corn. Yes, why not open a gift shop specifically for couples in love? Each gift would be different, with the basic material mostly being small stones, then she could use her painting skills to add lines and colors, wouldn't that create a vivid and unique love gift? And if placed in a gift box, each stone could truly sing!

Beautiful stones singing songs of love. Saying do, she acted immediately. After a month of preparation, Corn's "The Love of Stones" boutique finally opened for business. Business was good, and her mood improved too. The scale wasn't large, but she put a lot of effort into it. Corn used this beautiful work to heal her old wounds deep inside, earning money while beautifying her own life and utilizing her artistic talent. This was good.

Corn is now 35 years old and still single. She has created tens of thousands of love gifts, given temperature and spirit to tens of thousands of cold stones, and sent blessings to tens of thousands of people. Each stone she personally picked from the riverbed in the wild, natural, unpolluted, each with its own uniqueness. If she painted a heart on these silent stones, they became precious. Looking at them was like reading love stories, and she would unconsciously smile.

Frequent smiles are now Corn's life. In her store, there was a signature shelf composed of "female" and "child" forming the character "good." She said, if men and women were always "good" together, how wonderful that would be!

If fate allows, she doesn't reject the return of love, including from the painter called Soybean. Even at the age of 100, Corn still doesn't refuse love, provided that her teeth haven't fallen out because she likes to bite and check if the ring her boyfriend gives her is the purest gold.
