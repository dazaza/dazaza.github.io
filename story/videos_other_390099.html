1. Positioning

Do you want to do virtual goods or use virtual goods to build credit?

1. Doing virtual goods

So-called: Experts see the details, amateurs see the excitement. Not entering this industry, you'll never know how it works. You'll just sit there criticizing, knowing only the surface. Now you can sit there looking down on me, but when I succeed, I'll glance out the car window at you.

① First, you must know that the profit margin for virtual goods is tragically low. For example, earning 20 cents per order, charging 1000 orders earns only 200 yuan. By then, replying to customer inquiries might make your hands sore, but how much credit does 1000 transactions bring? You can calculate it yourself. Therefore, consider carefully where the focus of doing virtual goods lies. It's better to lose a little money on recharges to gain credit rather than wait idly for a few cents. This is something even an outsider might know.

② Secondly, you need to be familiar with industry operations and have a comprehensive understanding of Taobao knowledge to better address customer questions and promptly answer buyer inquiries.

③ Most importantly, after-sales service is crucial. Most of your customers are likely Taobao newbies with little knowledge about opening a store. Therefore, the focus should be on after-sales service, not just providing an activation code. We need to teach new sellers everything from three-day virtual repayment - listing products - store decoration - software usage - anti-fraud knowledge - promotion experience. Although these are included in the package, as a newcomer, they won't know where to start and may treat your package as junk, leading to complaints. Teaching a new seller takes one to three days, and any issues during their sales process should still receive patient explanations. These are things sellers in this industry should do. If done well and customers are satisfied, is it still a pyramid scheme? It's just tuition! Outsiders definitely don't know this.

2. Using virtual goods to build credit

Virtual recharges indeed build credit quickly because virtual goods eliminate the need for packaging and logistics times. If clothing, food, housing, and transportation are essential in life, with houses secured, clothes available, food not a concern, and convenient transportation, what market is currently the largest? Just think carefully, and you'll realize it's our telecommunications network. But don't just blindly build credit; while building credit, you should also learn more, as you're using virtual goods to build credit. You must learn more about physical goods experience. When you feel your credit is sufficient and your experience has reached a certain level, you can switch to physical goods. With credit and experience, isn't it easier than starting directly with physical goods?

3. Credit

It was mentioned above that virtual goods build credit quickly, which is normal for sellers who continue to do virtual goods. For sellers switching to physical goods, some might say that this is credit from virtual goods, and buyers will turn away. Moreover, switching to physical goods means no sales record for physical goods, essentially starting over. Indeed, these are concerns worth worrying about. But remember, most buyers are attracted by credit (at least when I shopped on Taobao, I didn't distinguish between virtual and physical goods). For example, a store with 1 diamond credit (built through virtual goods) and another with 1 heart credit, both with 100% positive reviews, selling the same items, which would buyers choose? 80% would choose the 1 diamond store, and virtual credit wouldn't significantly affect this. Every transaction of virtual goods leaves positive review records, earned through honest service. I believe buyers will appreciate this. Furthermore, during the recharge process, you can hint to buyers about your transition, letting them know your integrity and service attitude, ensuring they remain your customers after the switch. Additionally, if your store sells physical goods alongside virtual goods and your store decoration is appealing, wouldn't buyers be inclined to browse your store? Wouldn't this increase traffic? How much promotion cost and time would physical goods need to achieve this? If after reading these points, you still can't weigh the pros and cons, then I'm speechless! Moreover, doing virtual goods allows you to save time to learn more things, stabilizing your mood while building credit. On the other hand, some new sellers of physical goods who persevere are often losing money to attract customers through flash sales, one-yuan auctions, compensating price differences, and special shipping bids, all for credit, losing even more and exhausting themselves. In reality, after flash sales and one-yuan auctions, few people actually buy from the store.

4. Service

Physical goods emphasize service, but virtual goods require even more service. As mentioned earlier, our industry focuses on after-sales service, full tracking, and teaching until mastery. Fully dedicating ourselves to teaching downstream sellers is the essence of proper after-sales service. Many people in our industry are assisting downstream sellers in listing products at 2 AM, eating meals hurriedly, and rushing to help decorate stores. Every day feels like there's not enough time. I once ordered food to my dormitory and forgot to eat it for hours. Although I do this full-time, it's really tiring. Unless you engage in very complex promotional activities, you need to continuously update your Taobao experience to better serve newcomers. Our responsibility is to teach everyone well, even if they can't make money, ensuring they feel the value of spending hundreds of yuan on tuition, helping them navigate Taobao successfully. Therefore, I usually emphasize or even force new sellers to read fraud prevention tips, as scammers are everywhere. Commissions are indeed high, sometimes astonishing, but they are earned through service and experience. It's not simply recruiting agents, making money, and walking away. That tarnishes the industry. While we are merchants, we are also their friends, guiding them to avoid detours. This is our responsibility and service, with long-term satisfactory after-sales service as our goal.

5. Benefits of Newbies Doing Virtual Goods

1. Full after-sales service from upstream sellers helps newbies quickly become familiar with the Taobao store-opening process.

2. Learning everything from three-day virtual repayment - listing products - store decoration - software usage - fraud prevention knowledge - promotion experience.

3. Virtual goods can quickly build credit, transforming newbies into experienced old-timers.

4. More time to learn store-opening knowledge, how to manage and develop stores while selling virtual goods.

5. Having someone to ask questions - the upstream seller. Of course, finding a good upstream seller is essential.

6. Spending a few hundred yuan might not make real money, but it improves credit and buys valuable store-opening experience. Is it worth it?
