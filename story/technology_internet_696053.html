Folk Stock God Lin Yuan: The bull market has arrived, sell pots and pans to buy stocks

by df1iaiw7 on 2012-03-07 19:45:59

"The market is definitely going to have a bull run. What's the standard for a bull market? It's when the majority of people make money. For the majority to make money, the index needs to surpass the market high of 6124 points. Looking back at today after many years, we will find that what should have been considered then was not what stocks to buy, but whether or not to buy stocks. If you don't buy, you'll miss a great opportunity." Lin Yuan was resolute about the market conditions in 2012. [Vote: Do you think the bull market has arrived?] The three products under Lin Yuan's management had recovered to their net asset value after the low point in 2008. According to private equity ranking data, the three products under Lin Yuan's management ranked in the top 20 among similar products in the past year. In the interview, Lin Yuan made a bet with the reporter: "The net asset value of the product will double in the next three years."

Wealth Intelligence: What's your view on this year's A-share market?

Lin Yuan: Now is not the time to consider what stocks to buy, but whether or not to buy stocks. Under such market conditions, one should sell everything and buy stocks.

In my view, the upcoming market trend is a bull market. First, it's because the prices are cheap, and the entire market is undervalued; secondly, the economy has no problems. Compared to historical valuations, the main profit-making companies in the market are not expensive, and many stocks in the market have opportunities.

Wealth Intelligence: According to your judgment, how bullish will the bull market be?

Lin Yuan: The index will set new highs, with the Shanghai Composite Index exceeding the historical high of 6124 points. What's the standard for a bull market? It's when the majority of people make money. For the majority to make money, the market needs to exceed the high point of 6124 points.

Of course, this setting of new highs may not necessarily happen within this year, but setting new highs in the coming few years would not be surprising.

Wealth Intelligence: Many people believe that China's economy is currently declining.

Lin Yuan: Chinese people's income is increasing, and life is getting better year by year, especially for low-income earners whose incomes have significantly increased. This will lead to an increase in purchasing power on one hand, and on the other hand, it will also lead to an increase in labor costs. However, in the current situation, the rise in labor costs does not affect the economy.

China's growth rate may decline, but we believe it won't slow down too much. Therefore, the basic judgment is that there's no problem with the economy.

Wealth Intelligence: How large is your current position?

Lin Yuan: Since 1993, I have always been fully invested, and after 2007, I haven't changed my positions. Due to the lack of substantial returns from the overall market, our gains over these years have come from the internal growth of enterprises, unrelated to the overall market.

Of course, there will be fluctuations when individual stocks fall with the overall market, but I still haven't reduced my position because I am optimistic about its future profitability. Moreover, I am very confident in the net asset value of my future products, which will double in three years.

Wealth Intelligence: Why hasn't the market yet seen a sector with market appeal?

Lin Yuan: The market now is like a patient recovering from a serious illness, everyone is still restoring their strength. The valuation of many stocks needs to be raised. According to historical patterns, the overall market must first see a rebound of more than 30% before leading sectors emerge, at which point people will forget the previous pain and take out money to buy stocks.

Currently, there is no discovery of which sector is the leader, but the leader must meet several conditions: large market capitalization and high market acceptance. So, it can only be banks, insurance, and brokers. This also depends on people's mindset. The reason why Minsheng Bank led the rally during the 2007 bull market was because it had high recognition among stock investors.

Wealth Intelligence: Which sectors do you think have opportunities?

Lin Yuan: I am optimistic about the financial sector, such as bank stocks and broker stocks. Firstly, they are cheap; secondly, there is no problem with China's economy itself; thirdly, financial enterprises in our country are monopolistic enterprises. Cheap and monopoly are two important factors for investment.

Wealth Intelligence: Investors often worry about the issue of bank refinancing.

Lin Yuan: In the future, the financing of bank stocks will gradually decrease. The reason why banks have been rapidly financing in recent years is because they are in a stage of rapid development. As the economic growth slows down, the expansion of banks will also slow down.

The current price of bank stocks is already quite cheap, with a P/E ratio of 6-7 times. Whether there will be a market trend within 12 months is uncertain, but the potential is significant.

Wealth Intelligence: What percentage of your portfolio is in financial stocks?

Lin Yuan: It accounts for 15%, and the majority of the portfolio is in consumer stocks and pharmaceutical stocks.

Wealth Intelligence: But last year's consumption data was not as good as expected.

Lin Yuan: So we need to look at it dynamically. When national income is relatively low, the profit growth of consumer companies won't be too fast; once national income increases, the profits of these companies will significantly increase.

You see, first of all, the population base is large, so there's volume. Then, coupled with price growth, there will be significant growth, possibly exceeding the expectations of most people. For example, in the consumer sector, we are optimistic about white liquor.

Wealth Intelligence: Aren't white liquor stocks all blue-chip stocks?

Lin Yuan: My characteristic is to only invest in industry leaders. Also, focus on certainty, not dark horses. White liquor is currently an undervalued category.

Wealth Intelligence: What about sub-sectors of consumption?

Lin Yuan: Quick-service food is also something we are optimistic about. In the future, China's catering industry will not be as developed as it is now. From an international perspective, with the increase in income, the catering industry shrinks. The reason is simple, labor costs become expensive, eating out becomes costly, in such a situation, you will choose quick-service food.

Wealth Intelligence: Now, everyone is quite worried about real estate. Does real estate stock have investment value?

Lin Yuan: Real estate is indeed a variety that many people buy, but now house prices are too high, which will suppress consumption. Whether house prices rise or fall is hard to predict, but I no longer buy real estate stocks.

Wealth Intelligence: Is your investment considered value investing?

Lin Yuan: I'm not sure what you mean by value. At this point in time, my value lies in buying stocks.

Editorial Plan: From bearish to bullish, shouting bottom, is there a bull market in the Year of the Dragon?
