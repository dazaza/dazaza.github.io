Alibaba's CBU is rumored to be spun off into the Local Life Division

by geekzhang on 2013-05-07 18:09:23

According to information provided by industry insiders, Alibaba's China Business Unit (CBU) is about to face a split. A part of the CBU that has already been confirmed will be merged into the Local Life Division, which will be overseen by Jianfeng Zhang (Xing Dian), while Peng Ye will be transferred to the position of Vice President at Alibaba Group. His specific responsibilities have not yet been disclosed.Data shows that in the email published by Alibaba, the division managed by Zhang Jianfeng, JuHuaSuan and Local Life Divisions rank fifth, while the CBU led by Ye Peng ranks eighth. Analysts say that the ranking of JuHuaSuan and Local Life Divisions represents Alibaba's emphasis on and reorganization of its O2O business. Public information lacks sufficient details about Zhang Jianfeng himself, only indicating that he is the Vice President of Technology at Alibaba, previously mainly responsible for internal information systems.Prior to this, the B2B topic community website "I Love B2B" had written an article pointing out that Alibaba's data suggests that the B2B department is declining within Alibaba. Its B2B is trending towards integration with Taobao, moving towards C2B. Further speculation suggests that Ye Peng's leadership of Alibaba's B2B is not going well. If this adjustment goes through, it can be considered as a footnote to this judgment.Further Evolution of CBBSIn July 2012, when Alibaba adjusted from its original subsidiary structure to seven business groups, the re-emergence of the CBBS concept garnered attention. Behind this concept, how the internal connections between consumers, distributors, manufacturers, and e-commerce service providers become tighter became an important consensus for people's expectations of Alibaba's future development. An important concept in this relationship is the supply chain - how the supply chain between manufacturing and distribution, distribution and consumers, and sometimes the e-commerce service providers in between is smoothed out, becomes an important issue in Alibaba's CBBS era.From this perspective, the integration of Zhang Jianfeng's JuHuaSuan and Local Life Divisions into the split CBU seems logical due to their rich experience in consolidating the supply chain. After the integration of CBU, the traditional function of B2B as an information flow tool still exists, but exploring the B2B2C model may become a new direction for Alibaba's domestic trade B2B business.At the beginning of 2013, Alibaba China had already achieved account interoperability with Taobao. In exploring the B2B2C model, data-driven should become the foundation for the development of this model. This has two implications: one is that, led by the corresponding business departments of Alibaba, through the organization and judgment of data, some sample transactions of B2B2C will be created; the other is that e-commerce service providers, especially data service providers, on Alibaba and Taobao, may become important participants in another sample transaction model. E-commerce service providers on the Alibaba Group platform may thereby gain greater growth space.Currently, among the enterprises on the Ali CBU platform, apart from those ultimately flowing into C-class consumption, there are still a large number of enterprises facing B-class production. Currently, how these types of enterprises evolve towards the B2B2C direction has not yet seen a relatively mature and universally applicable model emerge in the industry.Therefore, in such adjustments, the form in which the part handled by CBU will adjust is not yet clear. Perhaps we will only know on May 10th.Other Effects of CBU AdjustmentAlibaba recently announced an investment in Sina Weibo, acquiring an 18% stake in Sina Weibo. According to the announcement, this investment was made by Alibaba (China), i.e., what we usually consider as Alibaba's CBU Division. Industry experts believe that with the identity of Alibaba CBU making the investment, this implies considerable room for imagination in the cooperation between this division and Sina Weibo.Beyond Sina Weibo becoming an advertising platform, the data on the platform is expected to be integrated with Alibaba's data, which will change the traditional path of market research for enterprises and may become an important data support for the development of B2B2C, possibly bringing a new growth point for B2B.This round of adjustments by Alibaba may also impact the B2B industry. Initially, when Alibaba's B2B company delisted, other listed B2B companies had already lost an important reference point for valuation. The current split of Alibaba's CBU could lead to a long-term loss of this stock price reference point. If Alibaba lists as a whole in the future, due to the complex relationships within its internal organizational structure, how traditional B2B business is defined, whether it will become part of the business details disclosed in financial reports, all remain unknown. This will make the analysis and evaluation of the B2B industry (domestic trade), and the commercial value analysis increasingly difficult. The domestic trade B2B industry may face a value winter, and media and research institutions' attention and study of the traditional B2B industry might enter a new low point.
