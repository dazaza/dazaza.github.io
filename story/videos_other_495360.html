Generally speaking, Jinan Wuzhou Hospital divides lumbar disc herniation into the following three types:

1. Lumbar Disc Protrusion: The annulus fibrosus ruptures, and the nucleus pulposus squeezes out from the rupture, breaking through the posterior longitudinal ligament and becoming free within the spinal canal, compressing the nerve roots or spinal cord. This is one of the common types of lumbar disc herniation.

2. Lumbar Disc Bulging: The annulus fibrosus has not completely ruptured, but the nucleus pulposus protrudes from the damaged area, pressing on the nerve root.

3. Lumbar Disc Herniation: The annulus fibrosus ruptures, and the nucleus pulposus squeezes out from the rupture, compressing the nerve root. This is the typical type of lumbar disc herniation.
