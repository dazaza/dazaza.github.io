The future of online marketing under the community-based Internet scenario

m How enterprise marketing responds to the emerging trends

(This article is from the perspective of enterprises to deal with online marketing. Internet practitioners should also look at their own marketing value from the perspective of enterprises. Many values still have a lot of space for exploration.)

In early July 2008, Giant Network acquired 25% of the shares of social networking site 51.com for $51 million in cash. Soon after, Qianxiang Interactive, which owns the community website Xiaonei, acquired domestic web game developer豌豆互动for 10 million yuan. The concept of online game community and community gaming began to attract the attention of the media and industry professionals.

The main goal of cooperation between online game companies and social networking sites is to achieve synergistic benefits. Taking the cooperation between Giant Network and 51.com as an example, Giant aims at 51's huge target user base. Through capital cooperation, it can bring a real strategic level of cooperation foundation, bringing a continuous stream of user groups for Giant's games. Just like QQ's instant communication user base brings a user base for QQ games, 51's delayed interactive space-based website can also provide a solid user base for Giant's games. At the same time, this cooperation is more important through advantageous interaction, enhancing the ability of synergistic benefits. 51.com obtains capital support, gaining space for long-term development, solving the worries of capital problems, and can focus on how to stick to users, such as how to provide more services to users. At the same time, it can also connect games with 51's network platform, increasing the stickiness of its website and meeting the needs of some users. For 51, due to the increasing homogenization of social networking products, social networking sites can even be achieved through a simple website-building program, which raises the question of why users stay. To make users stay, more fun applications become one of the important factors. Cooperation with the online game giant Giant Network can conduct cooperation in online games, providing more experiences for 51 users to stick around. The gamification of communities reflects the efforts of social networking sites to stick to users under product homogenization.

Giant and other online game providers have actually already made attempts at community building, such as the game "Journey Online", which has many community-based attempts, such as same-city dating and families. It is precisely these community functions that extend the vitality of the "Journey Online" game and increase the playability of the game. Because of this, Shi Yuzhu has shown great interest in the development of communities.

If we broaden our perspective to the entire Internet, we can see a broader context. The cooperation between online game companies and community websites is just one scene in the development process of the Internet in recent years, only because its actions are relatively eye-catching. It shows from one side the profound changes that have occurred in the entire Internet environment: the wave of community-based Internet. The entire Internet is gradually evolving into a community-based Internet, which is an emerging trend. Everything on the Internet is becoming community-based, not just social networking sites, but also Web 2.0 sites, IM, search engines, and e-commerce. QQ has established China's largest community space, QQ Space, and a complete set of community systems; search engines have also achieved remarkable results in community building. Baidu's Zhidao, Tieba, and QQ's Wenwen are all attempts to meet user needs by establishing community question-and-answer systems when machines cannot accurately answer questions. Korea's top search engine Naver defeated foreign search engine giants through its Knowledge-In query system. Many e-commerce websites have grown through communities. The recently funded group-buying website Zhu Liwang developed its strength through its popular forum community model. In short, almost all websites are becoming community-based, and community-based has become the essence of the Internet and one of the most important keywords for the future of the Internet. The Internet is no longer just a simple provider of information services, game services, or basic communication services. It has begun to enter the era of community-based Internet. This has become more noticeable with the rise of MySpace, Facebook, Xiaonei, and 51. The wave of community-based Internet also reflects the efforts of various Internet companies to seek victory in the current severe product homogenization competition. Whoever sticks to more users will occupy a larger territory in China's Internet world.

Currently, the Internet is still in the early stage of community-based development, and the evolution process of Internet community-based is far from over. Although today China's Internet users have exceeded 200 million, more people will join in the future, and this has not ended yet. As people increasingly get used to using the community-based Internet for life, work, study, entertainment, business, etc., such as answering doubts, the community-based Internet will play an increasingly important role. For Internet companies, community-based has become a fundamental need, a need to stick to users.

Faced with the surging tide of community-based Internet development, how should enterprise marketing respond to this emerging trend? In an era of rapid change, competitors are no longer the focal point of enterprises. The focus of enterprises should be on customers. Community-based Internet will profoundly impact customer behavior patterns, perceptions of value, and methods of connection between customers and enterprises. To achieve corporate goals, the focus of enterprise marketing needs to change, adapt to trends, and even lead the way.

One influence of community-based Internet: Impact on user purchase decisions

When users buy something, they are never alone in making the decision. There will be a bunch of people supporting them behind the scenes, such as friends, family, or authoritative experts. With the advent of community-based Internet, user purchase decisions have become more complex, with more influencers involved. News and information used to serve as references for purchase decisions, whether newspapers, magazines, TV, or Internet news. However, with community-based Internet, there is more user sharing, commenting, interacting, and faster information flow, resulting in more sufficient purchase decision-making information. Often, a single post in a community can determine a person's purchase decision. Nowadays, many Internet users refer not only to price comparisons when shopping online but also consider netizens' opinions and check for any bad reputation. Opinions from experienced users are especially influential, and users often willingly share their experiences.

For instance, buying baby formula, if it's a younger generation, especially a nuclear family, and if there are no friends or relatives around who have had children, decisions about purchasing baby formula are often made through the Internet, especially through communities, which is the most natural choice. This can be done through Baidu's Zhidao, Tieba, QQ's Wenwen, or various community-based channels related to parenting. Among the younger generation, there is a large user group called Internet dependency syndrome. When they have problems, they first do not consult family or friends in real life but instead first consider using search engines or communities to find answers.

Nowadays, some manufacturers are starting to notice this trend. Even if outdoor advertisements are very tempting, users still prefer to go online to check and compare before making the final purchase decision. Therefore, some manufacturers directly add a search engine address bar in their advertisements with keywords, automatically guiding users to perform queries.

Thus, the change in the group influencing purchase decisions leads to a shift in the focus of customer marketing. It is necessary to study the opinion leaders in community-based Internet, understand their needs, research user behaviors, desires, and how users influence purchase decisions through information exchange.

Therefore, no enterprise can ignore the Internet, especially the community-based Internet, and must use this big platform for marketing services, understanding consumers, guiding consumers, and gaining advantages in fierce competition.

The second impact of community-based Internet: Influence on corporate brands

Corporate brands appear more vulnerable under the community-based Internet. A brand can quickly rise through the Internet platform within a few years, but it can also end up looking shabby under the community-based Internet. Thus, public relations on the Internet have become increasingly important. Enterprises with brand demands need to establish specialized Internet marketing departments to cope with the rapidly changing community-based Internet.

The different reactions of Vanke and Wang Laoji during the Sichuan earthquake clearly demonstrated the power of the Internet as a whole community platform. This Internet includes Tianya, Sina Blogs, Baidu Tieba, and also instant messaging tools like QQ and MSN. In front of major events, the entire Internet is a whole, not just certain websites. The strong presence of community-based Internet means that enterprises no longer face traditional media like newspapers, TV, radio, or magazines. These platforms were difficult for ordinary consumers to gain a voice, whereas the Internet allows more people to have more voices. Moreover, when dealing with issues arising from traditional media, enterprises usually have some buffer time, but the Internet does not. Events can fundamentally change at any moment, requiring public relations departments to have skilled handling techniques. Behind these skilled handling techniques lies a deep understanding of the Internet platform, especially the community-based Internet platform. Community-based Internet is not just about posting a few posts, it involves many user groups. Understanding where these groups form influence, how they operate, and what their needs are requires in-depth research.

Thus, Internet marketing differs from traditional online marketing. In the past, the control of newspapers, TV, and radio was in the hands of service providers, but the Internet is an open platform. This platform means anyone can express their opinions, especially opinion leaders who wield more media power. Therefore, for enterprises, paying attention to online reputation is more important than ever.

How should enterprises respond? Larger-scale enterprises should establish specialized Internet marketing departments. This Internet marketing goes beyond just setting up a corporate website or doing search engine marketing or simple website user surveys. It involves using the Internet platform to understand user formation, needs, and desires, taking proactive actions to guide users before they express positive or negative opinions.

The third impact of community-based Internet: Active consumer participation and the development of personalized customization needs.

When a user sees a TV ad or outdoor ad, it is difficult to interact directly with the enterprise. These ads convey information that the enterprise subjectively believes is the most important to communicate to customers. In reality, consumers have more personalized needs. Community-based Internet provides a platform for interaction between consumers and enterprises, leading to more active consumer participation and personalized customization needs.

Because information flows quickly among customers, information becomes increasingly flat and symmetric, meaning consumers will have more dominance.

Currently, Internet advertising comes in three stages: First, hard advertising, which borrowed traditional advertising formats in the early development of Internet advertising, such as display ads on portal sites, which are essentially outdoor ads moved online. Of course, display ads can be clicked, allowing further interaction. Second, search engine-based advertising, which recommends relevant ads based on search interests, creating what is considered the perfect business model. In these two business models, users are more studied as objects, with less active participation and dominance.

As community-based Internet develops, information flow among community users will increase. Users no longer rely solely on search engines or portal news for information but generate more links through user interactions, making user interactions one of the main ways people obtain information, possibly rivaling search engines as entry points in the future. Thus, a third form of advertising emerges: community-based interactive advertising. This type of advertising places more emphasis on user participation and user dominance.

At this stage, users have stronger dominance and interactivity, continuously giving rise to new online marketing models. For enterprises seeking to actively experiment in marketing, gain more market share, and enhance brand value, online marketing is constantly changing in the increasingly community-based Internet environment, with new marketing models continually emerging. For these enterprises, it is essential to keep up with the development trends of community-based Internet, particularly the interaction among users in community settings and the flow of information, as well as how they establish connections with enterprises.

The fourth impact of community-based Internet: More cross-platform marketing modes will emerge.

Since community-based is a fundamental feature of the Internet, and websites are independently owned, each site has its own marketing platform and model for commercial benefit. However, enterprises conducting marketing need not just one site's users but more users across multiple sites. This promotes more cross-platform marketing. Mergers and acquisitions between game companies and social networking sites have already occurred, as have website alliances (future website alliances will not just be among small and medium-sized sites; large sites may also form temporary alliances to counter superpowers or for shared commercial interests). This provides more convenience for cross-platform marketing.

Taking the cooperation between game companies and social networking sites as an example, due to the relationship between Giant and 51, joint marketing between social networking sites and games could occur if needed in the future. For example, joint marketing between 51 and the "Journey Online" game. Both parties' users can be utilized for joint marketing, incorporating marketing into the game's upgrade system and the community's friendship system. Once tied to user benefits, users will interact, passing the product's value to users during the gaming and socializing process.

Not just games, other ways of marketing combined with communities will emerge, gradually flattening and expanding the entire Internet marketing platform.

Enterprises should actively adapt to trends and lead the way

In a community-based Internet environment, the future of online marketing will have more community-based characteristics. Online marketing should take advantage of this Internet environment, even exploring new ways to innovate enterprise marketing.

Enterprises that actively respond to the trend of community-based Internet can gain more competitive advantages in the future intense competition. Below are two examples of enterprises using community-based Internet for their own benefit, achieving good results.

Essentially utilizing the trend of community-based Internet means listening to customer voices on a better and more interactive platform, seeing what true value the enterprise can offer to customers. Faced with this trend, enterprises can choose to ignore it or actively participate. Somerset, the world's largest yacht manufacturer, chose the latter. It fully utilized the Internet, allowing customers to participate in the shipbuilding plan via the network, obtaining knowledge of what the future ships would look like through photos, and whether they liked them. This method satisfied user personalization needs and significantly increased the company's online sales. When the company was still a small company in the 1970s, it fully utilized community-based elements, allowing locals to wander around the shipyard, getting to know the shipbuilders and observing the shipbuilding process. In the Internet age, the company expanded the concept of community to a broader platform, laying an important foundation for the company's great achievements.

Another example is the story of a South African "Storm Valley" winery, which marketed through blogs, discussing various backstage stories of running the vineyard in the blog, filled with happiness, hardships, and expressing the enterprise's various insights and opinions on wine. Here, users see not just vendors trying to sell their wine but friendly readers who are willing to share their views and express what kind of wine they need. The result was that the company name's search volume on Google increased 40 times in just a few months, and sales rapidly improved.

Regarding the trend of Internet community-based, it seems simple on the surface, but its powerful force has yet to be fully displayed. Enterprises should not stop after doing a few interactive or viral marketing campaigns. Instead, they should deeply realize the big trend of Internet community-based and actively think about customer needs in this future, seizing opportunities for their enterprises on this large platform. In the rapidly changing future, external environments will have an increasingly significant impact on enterprises, and enterprises should pay more attention to the external world and environment. The core of this environment is customers. Customers are the main body in this wave of community-based Internet. Embracing community-based Internet is equivalent to embracing a hopeful future.

Faced with the rapidly changing external world, enterprises can no longer be obsessed with internal issues or merely rely on ERP systems for management. They need to build their management systems centered on changes in external customers, and community-based Internet provides an important channel for enterprises to face customers and establish connections with them. This is a revolutionary change. The examples mentioned above of Somerset and Storm Valley are just two out of many. If enterprises fail to participate in time, they will inevitably be eliminated by the times. There is no middle ground here.
