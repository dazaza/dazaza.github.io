"Double Eleven" should be a day to cherish life: A 28-year-old Taobao store owner in Wenzhou dies suddenly

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:24:19

Yesterday, all the products from a five-star Taobao children’s shoes store in Lucheng, Wenzhou were taken off the shelves. The reason for taking them off was lamentable: the 28-year-old owner of the store passed away suddenly while managing his online store late at night.

The Double Eleven Online Shopping Carnival is coming soon. All online shop owners should take care to rest and not overwork themselves.

An IT guy doing an online store part-time; rented a new warehouse just before the incident

According to insiders, the owner who had the accident, Mr. Cai, was 28 years old, local, and worked at an IT company during normal hours. He started operating a Taobao online store a year ago, mainly selling children's shoes from nearby physical stores. His daily routine was basically working during the day, then managing his online store when he got home at night. Business wasn’t good at first, but it gradually improved in recent months, keeping him very busy every day, sometimes staying up until three or four o'clock in the morning. With the arrival of the Double Eleven festival, he even asked his cousin to help out.

At around 5 p.m. yesterday, I went to the scene of the incident, on the fourth floor of a residential building in the urban area. There were two rooms on that floor with lights on, but both were locked tight. Through the small hole in the door, you could see several boxes of goods inside. An insider pointed to one of the houses and said this was where Mr. Cai lived, and the other was a newly rented warehouse. Because business had been good recently, Mr. Cai had just rented a second room as a warehouse these past couple of days. Who would have thought that the night before last, after just moving the goods into the warehouse, such an unfortunate event would occur late at night? It was truly regrettable.

According to a cleaner nearby, in his impression, Mr. Cai was amiable. Every time he sold goods, Mr. Cai would give the cardboard boxes to him as scrap, which made the cleaner and his wife very grateful.

In the middle of the night while managing the online store, he suddenly collapsed onto the keyboard

Insiders revealed that at around 11 p.m. the previous evening, Mr. Cai was managing his online store in front of the computer when he suddenly collapsed onto the keyboard. His friends nearby mistakenly thought he was joking, so they didn't pay much attention. But soon after, he fell off the chair onto the ground, prompting everyone to rush to check on him. After about two minutes, Mr. Cai regained consciousness. At the time, his friends urged him to go to the nearby hospital for a check-up, but Mr. Cai said he had fainted once before, and after a few minutes, he felt fine again. Plus, it was already the middle of the night, so he didn't go to the hospital. This delay unfortunately missed the opportunity to seek medical treatment.

A little while later, Mr. Cai said he was tired and wanted to go to bed to rest. Around 4 a.m. yesterday, his friends noticed in shock that Mr. Cai's body was already cold, so they quickly rushed him to the hospital for emergency treatment.

Medical personnel involved in the rescue said that when the man was brought to the hospital, he was already dead. Based on the stiffness of his body, it was preliminarily judged that the man had been dead for about two hours by the time he was sent to the hospital. The exact cause of death is still unclear.

However, it was understood that the man often worked long hours and surfed the internet without getting proper rest, likely causing him to die from potential health issues triggered by overwork.

After the unfortunate incident, Mr. Cai's family reported it to the police. The family stated that the police had largely ruled out foul play. Mr. Cai's parents are rushing back from abroad.

Opening an online store looks beautiful, but it's actually exhausting

Ms. Zhang, who has been operating an online store in the urban area for many years, said she also sells children's shoes on Taobao. Operating an online store may look easy, but in reality, it's very difficult, consuming both physical and mental energy. When business is bad, you're anxious and worried; when business is good and you make money, you're exhausted.

Ms. Zhang said that they have to go out and check goods, pick them up, then come back and take photos, create graphics, restock, and list items on the website. When customers place orders, you need to pack and ship them promptly. If customers are dissatisfied, you have to handle returns and exchanges and other after-sales issues. Not a single step can be neglected. Although there are four people sharing the workload, everyone still has to stay up late, consuming a lot of physical and mental energy, leading to irregular living habits. Sometimes if a particular item runs out temporarily, you still have to find ways to help buyers find it to avoid complaints that lower your store's credibility, directly affecting future business. They are four people running one online store, and they already feel quite strained, while Mr. Cai mostly managed his store alone, so his physical exhaustion must have been severe. Ms. Zhang sighed, saying that she had heard news of Taobao store owners passing away suddenly elsewhere, feeling it was far away. After hearing about Mr. Cai's situation, she said she would try to take time to rest in the future and no longer "trade life for money." With Double Eleven approaching, she wouldn't push herself too hard either.

Dying from running an online store isn't a myth

On July 15th this year, 36-year-old Wu Lijun, former chairman of the Taobao brand Yu Nifang, passed away due to prolonged labor, suffering from cerebral venous sinus thrombosis.

In October 2012, 29-year-old Taobao Crown Store owner Xu Wenjun died suddenly from overwork, earning over a million yuan monthly before his death.

In July 2012, a 24-year-old female Taobao seller from Hangzhou passed away suddenly while managing her online store and preparing for her wedding.

In June 2012, in a rental unit in Jiangsu, 25-year-old Crown Seller Xia passed away and was discovered by a courier several days later.

In May 2012, Su Su, an 80s-born 'spicy mom' and full-time Taobao store owner, passed away from subarachnoid hemorrhage. She left behind a child only one year old.

How far are you from "overwork"?

Insomnia, loss of appetite, irritability, shortness of breath, chest tightness, general weakness... how many of these symptoms do you experience? These symptoms are all precursors to "overwork." Senior auditors, IT industry workers, elites from top 500 foreign enterprises, factory workers, web editors, online store owners, public relations employees, online writers, models, police officers, doctors, private enterprise bosses, and young university teachers are the 13 professions most prone to "death from overwork." Have you been affected?

According to a survey conducted jointly by the media and well-known companies in 2012, the number of people dying from overwork in our country has reached 600,000 annually, averaging more than 1,600 people per day dying from illnesses caused by fatigue. We have now surpassed Japan to become the country with the highest number of "overwork deaths."

Expert prescription: Long-term staying up late is unacceptable

Experts suggest that e-commerce workers, foreign enterprise employees, medical staff, and others who work under high intensity and long hours should reasonably regulate their workload. Adjust working hours based on feedback from your body, trying to minimize situations like staying up late for long periods or sitting for extended durations. Pay attention to balancing work and rest, don't stare at the computer screen for long periods, and move around indoors more often. If possible, it's best to take a nap for one or two hours during lunchtime and focus on resting after work. Ensure sufficient sleep at night and try not to stay up late whenever possible.

Stealing moments of leisure to listen to music, dance, or sing can effectively relieve fatigue and relax tense nerves, serving as a spiritual remedy to prevent fatigue syndrome.
