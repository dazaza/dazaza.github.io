Dream interpreter: A father with kidney failure refuses to take medicine and stop dialysis to treat his daughter who suffers from leukemia

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-03 10:19:50

On December 20, Xue Yandan was in an isolation ward and could only communicate with the outside world by phone. (File photo) On December 21, Jinan - reported by reporter Liu Shuang: Xue Zhenwu from Tancheng County, Linyi City, was diagnosed with renal failure in both kidneys, while his daughter was found to have acute leukemia, which undoubtedly added insult to injury for this family. In order to treat his daughter's illness, Xue Zhenwu resolutely gave up his own treatment and sought help everywhere. On December 20, the Shandong Red Cross Rescue Network delivered a donation of 40,000 yuan.

Both Father and Daughter Suffer from Serious Illnesses

Xue Zhenwu is an employee of the well-drilling team at the Tancheng County Water Resources Bureau in Linyi City. He is 48 years old. In 2008, during a routine physical examination at work, he was diagnosed with dual kidney failure. His wife had been laid off since 1995, and the three-person family relied solely on his salary for survival. This economically strained household could not afford the high medical expenses for Xue Zhenwu. Due to limited treatment options, Xue Zhenwu's condition gradually worsened, and he urgently needed a kidney transplant surgery. With no other options, his wife Zhang Lili went to work in another place to earn money.

Misfortunes Never Come Alone. On September 23, 2010, Xue Yun Dan, who had just entered the Shandong Media Vocational College, suddenly fainted at school. The Jinan Military Region General Hospital diagnosed her with acute leukemia. Zhang Lili, who was originally full of confidence in saving money for her husband's kidney transplant, was almost devastated by this shocking news.

In order to treat their daughter's illness, Xue Zhenwu and his wife sold their house and all their assets, borrowing money from all relatives and friends. To save money, they returned home as soon as their daughter’s condition slightly improved. Unexpectedly, while waiting for a suitable donor match, Xue Yun Dan experienced two infections and now has returned to the Tianjin Blood Disease Hospital for treatment. Her physical condition remains unstable, and her lungs have been infected, making it impossible to immediately undergo surgery even if a suitable donor match is found.

Refusing Medicine and Stopping Dialysis for the Sake of His Daughter

Seeing his daughter's condition worsening day by day, Xue Zhenwu repeatedly refused medication and stopped kidney dialysis, even rejecting the doctor's request to drink a pack of milk daily. He was determined to save the money for his medication to treat his daughter and provide some nutrition for his wife. "She (his wife) has become too thin from taking care of us; if she collapses, what will happen to our family?"

Due to severe malnutrition, untimely treatment, overwork, and mental pressure, Xue Zhenwu's condition gradually worsened. Now, he needs to rest after walking just a few steps, but his daughter is his spiritual pillar. He borrowed money for his daughter everywhere and then smiled as he gazed at his daughter through the isolation ward window.

"Regardless of anything, we will never stop treating Yun Dan," said Xue Zhenwu. After the second round of chemotherapy, Yun Dan can only talk to her parents outside the isolation room via phone. "I haven't gone out for several months, and I thought about a lot here. Most importantly, I want to get better quickly, return to school, and give my parents a hug."

To help Yun Dan's family, the Shandong Red Cross Rescue Network (www.sdjz.org.cn) initiated a fundraising campaign. On December 20, the website staff delivered the already-raised donation of 40,000 yuan to Xue Zhenwu. However, this amount is just a drop in the bucket for Yun Dan's treatment. Therefore, the Shandong Red Cross Rescue Network has set up a dedicated donation page on its homepage, hoping more kind-hearted people will come forward to help them.
