Methods to relieve stomach discomfort after drinking alcohol

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-23 14:24:49

What to do if your stomach hurts after drinking? Many people have experienced this. Due to business entertainment or friend gatherings, they end up drinking too much out of politeness, leading to vomiting and diarrhea, and extreme discomfort in the stomach. What should be done in such a situation? First, it is important to understand the cause. Drinking excessively can overly stimulate the stomach, affecting its normal function and easily causing damage to the gastric mucosa. Therefore, after drinking too much, it is natural to experience stomach discomfort, nausea, and loss of appetite. In such situations, focus on nourishing the body. Simply taking measures to sober up is not enough because the stimulation to the stomach has already occurred and cannot be resolved solely by sobering up.

Therefore, what we should do is protect the gastric mucosa. Eat some light porridge and vegetables, drink a cup of hot milk or hot millet porridge, which are very beneficial for the stomach. These foods play an excellent role in nurturing the stomach function and repairing the damaged gastric mucosa. Focus on nourishment, and also remember to control yourself when drinking. If you have no choice but to drink, take some protective measures beforehand. Below are some tips we have summarized for you:

Precautions to keep in mind when drinking:

1. Before drinking, eat until you're 70% full and do not drink large amounts of water (this is to leave room in your stomach for alcohol).

2. Half an hour before drinking, drink a bottle of milk, preferably pure milk or sweetened milk (it is said that drinking milk before drinking forms a protective film in the stomach).

3. While drinking, avoid cold water, lemon water, and other stimulating beverages (otherwise, the milk you drank earlier will be wasted).

4. When drinking hard liquor, don't drink it all at once; sip it in several small portions. When drinking beer, wait for the foam to settle before drinking, otherwise, you may feel bloated or even vomit.

5. After drinking, do not drink vinegar or tea to sober up as these are misconceptions. Neither vinegar nor tea can effectively sober you up.

6. If you vomit continuously after drinking, immediately drink a bottle of mineral water to prevent vomiting blood due to an empty stomach.

7. After vomiting, do not eat immediately, especially barbecued food.

8. Before sleeping, place a container next to your bed in case you wake up suddenly needing to vomit and cannot make it to the bathroom in time.

9. The next day, if you feel acid reflux, take antacids like Gastrosed and only drink water after the acid reflux subsides.

10. If you feel stomach pain, take appropriate stomach medication based on the situation. Do not take painkillers as they have little effect on stomach pain.

11. For breakfast, choose warm drinks like soybean milk or milk. If you need to work, energy drinks like Pulse or Red Bull can help you stay alert and ensure you don't miss work.

12. Remind yourself that you should never drink like this again, but often forget after some time and repeat the same behavior, resulting in the same discomfort.
