Red-nosed scissors fish

by chang79059 on 2008-08-16 20:34:51

English name: Hemigrammus rhodostomus, native to Brazil in South America.

Characteristics

The body shape is similar to other species in the same genus. The head is red, and the snout is bright red, hence it is also called Red Nose Fish. The whole body is silvery white, almost transparent. There are black and white stripes on the caudal fin similar to those of the scissorfish, hence the name. The difference between the Red Nose Scissorfish and the common Red Nose Fish lies in the fact that the Red Nose Fish has three black and white stripes, while the Red Nose Scissorfish has five black stripes and four white stripes. Body length is 50-60 mm.

Red Nose Scissorfish Breeding Characteristics

The fish is robust and easy to breed. It has a gentle temperament and can be mixed with small fish of the same size and character. The water quality for breeding should be slightly acidic soft water (pH 5.4-6.8), with suitable water temperature ranging from 22 to 26°C. The color of the snout changes with the health condition of the fish and the changes in water quality. When the water quality and temperature are unsuitable or the physical health is poor, the bright red color will fade to pink and lose its brightness. It likes to eat small insects on the water surface.

It prefers to live in groups. The number of Red Nose Scissors in a single aquarium should not be less than three, otherwise the Red Nose Scissors will curl up in the corner and bear loneliness and pressure alone. And the grass tank for breeding Red Nose Scissors should have some areas where they can hide. The pH value of the breeding water body cannot change too much, which will directly affect the life of the Red Nose Scissors.
