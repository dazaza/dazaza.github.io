Introduction:

Plot Summary:

The first film "Master Head" was very popular when it premiered in 2003. At the end of the movie, the boss ordered Tou Zhi to attend college. In "Master Head 2", after graduating from university, Tou Zhi becomes a high school teacher, while the boss enters high school as an older student to keep up with the times. The interesting story begins here... Compared to the first episode, Tou Zhi's situation as a teacher in the second episode is more complicated. Besides dealing with the headache-inducing English and undisciplined students, his biggest challenge is how to face the boss who is now one of his students. Can he treat all students, including his own boss, fairly?

In the first episode, Kim Sang-doo, who pretended to have graduated from university, is completely captivated by a female teacher, practically abandoning his personal life. After Big Head's marriage, he has a son who looks exactly like him. Whenever anything happens, the news reaches his wife's ears. How will they protect their big brother Tou Zhi this time? Can they peacefully navigate through school life? Let's wait and see.

Key Techniques for Production:

Key Technique One: Tou Zhi's First Day of School

On the first day of work as an intern teacher, two JAGUR cars worth 180 million Korean won, four luxury cars, and two BMW motorcycles were used. The grand scene of Tou Zhi going to school was filmed using a heavy-duty camera crane system, almost renting out the entire line of Road No. 8.

Key Technique Two: The Scene Where Tou Zhi Witnesses Mei Ting's Car Accident

For the car accident scene involving Mei Ting shot at the Paju Cultural Publishing Industry Base in South Korea, eight water sprinklers were used. To realistically portray the accident scene, four camera cranes, JIMMYJIB crane, high-speed cameras, and digital high-speed cameras were mobilized. This scene not only highlights the authenticity of the accident but also marks a turning point in Tou Zhi's anger. The production team invested nearly 100 million Korean won into creating this scene.

Key Technique Three: The Ultimate Action Highlight

At the funeral ceremony of Mei Ting held at Kyung Hee University Suwon Campus, a fight scene between Tou Zhi's gang and the New Namgang Gang was filmed. Over 200 extras were used on set. To mourn the deceased Mei Ting, the production team made approximately 9,000 chrysanthemum petals, valued at about 12.5 million Korean won. Despite the cold weather, they employed four large blowers to make the petals float beautifully in the sky. Among the action scenes, the most prominent part is played by the boss, Kim Sang-jong. To emphasize his image as the big brother, the production team specially made him a leather coat worth 3 million Korean won. Additionally, to make the boss riding a motorcycle look even more impressive, three cameras were used to capture all the coolest moments. To bring texture to the martial arts scenes, the filming lasted for five consecutive days. The enthusiasm of the staff and actors added many appealing elements to the movie.

Key Technique Four: Completion of the Movie Background Mainly Relies on Artificial Scenery

The main shooting location for the movie "Master Head 2" is a school. Nowadays, classrooms in most schools have fewer than 40 students, so the classroom designs are not very large. The high school that allowed the filming of this movie is no exception. Therefore, the movie "Master Head 2" needed to find a sufficiently large space to shoot the classroom scenes. Thus, Class 8 of Grade 3 is larger than an actual classroom, allowing the 34 students to move around well and providing better conditions for the camera to shoot.

Key Technique Five: Image Upgrade Mainly Depends on Costume Design

To vividly present the characters in "Master Head 2," the production team focused on upgrading costumes. First, for actor Jung Jun-ho, the production team used very high-quality fabrics to tailor his clothes to reflect the changes over the past five years. For Jeong Woong-in, due to character requirements, his costume was designed to be different from other actors, being delicate and unique. And Kim Sang-joong's attire even more prominently reflects the demeanor of the big brother. Overall costume upgrades also aim to reflect the image upgrade of these characters after five years.

Key Technique Six: Multi-talented All-rounder Jung Jun-ho

Actor, producer, director, teacher... Jung Jun-ho takes every role seriously. To adapt to the role of an ethics teacher in this movie, he started reviewing ethics courses a month before filming began. Besides his duties as an actor, he played a crucial role in selecting shooting locations for the production team. As the publicity ambassador for Gyeonggi Province, Jung Jun-ho quickly persuaded the necessary shooting locations within Gyeonggi Province. However, Kyung Hee University Suwon Campus, chosen as the location for the fighting scenes, was a tough negotiation. The school's reason was that it would affect students' studies, and the shooting time coincided with the exam period. Fortunately, since Jung Jun-ho graduated from Kyung Hee University, the school agreed to let the production team use the location because the lead actor of the movie was Jung Jun-ho. Moreover, nicknamed "Director Jung," Jung Jun-ho often guided new actors with less experience and actively exchanged ideas with the director about the movie, providing constructive suggestions. For this movie, Jung Jun-ho not only put in efforts as an actor but dedicated his entire self to the movie.

Key Technique Seven: Historically Strongest Trio, Three Lead Actors Communicate Through Eye Contact, Full of Chemistry and Passion

From the start of filming to its completion, the schedule for "Master Head 2" was packed. Their shooting continued for over two months without a single break across fifteen segments. This is all thanks to the actors who reunited after five years. They could understand each other's intentions just by looking at each other, and they were familiar with each other's acting styles. Despite the tight schedule, they still completed the tasks excellently. The actors of "Master Head 2" would come to the set even when they didn't have any scenes, practicing continuously to better portray the characters in the movie. Their passion became a model and motivation for the crew. From the beginning, the filmmakers clearly stated that they intended to create a movie that was not carelessly made or simply meant for a quick laugh.

Key Technique Eight: Special Group 3-8 Class’s Enthusiasm in "Master Head 2"

Tou Zhi's class is 3-8, which includes 34 students such as actors Kim Sang-jong, Han Hyo-joo, HAHA, etc. All the students in the class are loyal fans of the first installment of "Master Head," so they were eager to participate in the filming of the second part. Composed of students from Kyung Hee University, Seoul Arts University, and a high school drama and film class, they voluntarily joined the filming of "Master Head 2" without any compensation. Their role was significant in the school shooting parts. During the total of 20 school shooting sessions, even if the shooting went late into the night, they devoted all their passion and effort to the production team.
