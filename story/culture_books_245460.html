This was a period of prosperity forgotten by history.

It was sandwiched between the dark years of the Chenghua era of the Ming dynasty and the reckless times of the Zhengde era, yet it brought the Chinese a life that was sufficiently happy and memorable. This era was the eighteen years of the Hongzhi reign.

It was an era of national peace and stability: the fires of war at the border had been extinguished, the economy in the interior had developed, the people lived in peace and contentment, and officials were clean and self-disciplined. Before this, there was the recklessness of Emperor Chenghua Zhu Jianshen, the rampant corruption of officials and eunuchs' misrule, the reality of the national economy nearing bankruptcy, and the suffering caused by natural disasters ravaging both the north and south of China...

Who was it that used what kind of magic to change all this?

It was a man named Zhu Youchang, known as "Ming Xiaozong," whose era name was "Hongzhi," and he ushered in a great age of prosperity.

Emperor Yingzong was captured and taken to the Mongolian Plateau to gnaw on raw mutton. Someone had to clean up the mess. With the emperor captured, the enemy had reached the doorstep, and it was not possible to flee southward like during the Song Dynasty! Fortunately, his younger brother took care of it; Brother Zhu Qiyu inherited the throne and changed the era name to Jingtao, finally ending the frustrating Zhengtong reign. Emperor Jingtao trusted the capable minister Yu Qian, who successfully organized the defense of Beijing and defeated the enemy. He then applied diplomatic pressure to force the Wala to release the Yingzong to return as the Grand Emperor, thus avoiding the fate of Emperor Huizong dying in exile. After all the chaos, not only was the ancestral territory almost lost, but the imperial throne was also gone. Such a farce could not be blamed on others.

Why did they strike first? First, these people had notorious records, and public resentment against them was high. In short: those who deserved to be dealt with and were easy to handle, if not used as sacrifices, then who else?
