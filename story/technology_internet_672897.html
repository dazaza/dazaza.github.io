Company Name

The company name ends with "Limited Company," and the pre-approval of the company name is processed; generally, a company name should be composed sequentially of the following parts: [Shanghai (City) + Trade Name (Business Name) + Industry (or Industry Characteristics) + Limited Company]; companies whose names start with [Shanghai] must comply with the relevant provisions of the "Shanghai Municipal Enterprise Naming Management Measures"; companies whose names start with [China] must be approved in Beijing. In general, for Shanghai registered companies [http://www.fubaosp.com], the naming of wholly foreign-owned enterprises strictly follows the amount of registered capital, for example:

1) With 1 million RMB of registered capital, only the county/district (city) name can be used, for example: Shanghai Changning District XX Garment Co., Ltd. or Shanghai XX Garment Co., Ltd.;

2) With 3 million RMB of registered capital, the name can include the major city's name, for example: Shanghai XX Bag Co., Ltd.;

3) With 5 million RMB of registered capital, the provincial name can be used; for example: Zhejiang Province XX Resin Process Co., Ltd., Shanghai Registered Company [http://www.fubaosp.com].

6. Pre-Approval Licenses

For companies applying to operate in the following industries, special permits, also known as pre-approvals, are required:

Gas stations, refined oil storage, fuel operation rights, slaughterhouses, vocational schools, adult education, religious organizations, printing industry, auction industry, pawnshops, seal engraving industry, parking lots, legal and accounting services, bookkeeping agents, talent intermediaries, employment agencies, overseas study institutions, investment immigration institutions, construction, decoration, engineering cost estimation, project supervision enterprise qualifications, road freight, port construction and operation, internet cafes, audiovisual, newspapers, journals, book retail, publishing industry, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, food production and operation, travel agencies, medical institutions, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, safety technology prevention product operations, pesticides, veterinary drug operations, insurance industry, banking industry, etc.

7. Registered Capital and Capital Arrival

Trade-type registered capital: 500,000 RMB (minimum registered capital 30,000 RMB), production type: 1,000,000 RMB, consulting advisory type: 100,000 RMB; registered capital can be fully paid at once (fully paid within six months from the date of issuance of the business license), or it can be paid in installments over one to five years. If paid in installments, a minimum of 15% must be paid within three months after the completion of the company registration. The remaining part will be paid in subsequent installments. The length of time for installment payments depends on the size of the registered capital; fewer installments can reduce verification costs.

8. Business Scope of Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprises

In China, all industries' business scopes are strictly and precisely defined. Foreign-funded enterprises can only conduct commercial activities within their permitted business scope, which will be indicated on the business license. Any modifications require application and approval. Currently, industries where foreign businesses can operate include: production enterprises that comply with environmental pollution regulations; high-tech enterprises that improve China's productivity; financial institutions and insurance companies that meet certain conditions; information technology companies, investment advisory companies, software development industries, trading companies, consulting advisory companies, etc.

9. Import and Export Business of Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprises

After the establishment of a wholly foreign-owned enterprise in China, it simultaneously obtains an import and export approval certificate, which specifies the allowable range of imports and exports. The Chinese government generally allows wholly foreign-owned enterprises to export their own products, and the Chinese government has gradually liberalized trade businesses, bringing the business scope of wholly foreign-owned enterprises very close to that of domestic enterprises in China.

To meet the requirements of wholly foreign-owned enterprises engaging in import and export trade activities in China while not affecting the normal operations of domestic trade companies, the Chinese government has specially established tax-free zones in more developed and convenient areas, such as the [Shanghai Waigaoqiao Tax-Free Zone] and the [Shenzhen Yantian Tax-Free Zone]. Foreign investors can establish import and export trade companies in these tax-free zones to engage in import, warehousing, processing, packaging, and export business activities. If goods enter or leave the tax-free zone within the Chinese market, they are handled according to normal import and export procedures.

10. Annual Inspection

The Chinese government requires annual inspections for wholly foreign-owned enterprises. Zhuoyu specializes in handling annual reports and inspections for enterprises. Zhuoyu only charges agency fees for annual inspections, and government inspection fees are reimbursed based on actual expenses. The annual inspection takes place from January 1 to June 30 each year.

1) Trading Companies/Non-Production Companies:

Original board meeting records or resolutions of the wholly foreign-owned company's investment company; determine three or more company names; confirm registered capital and business scope; feasibility report; articles of association; copy of the investment company certificate; original and copy of the identity proof of the Chinese legal representative and two one-inch color photos; original bank credit reference issued by the investor's overseas bank in two copies; lawyer notarization (notarization by the local notary office for overseas companies and certification by the Chinese embassy or consulate in the area); office address: own property or leased commercial office building (original lease contract in duplicate, registered and stamped by the local leasing office, when leasing, need to rent under the name of one of the shareholders or under the name of the overseas company, 30 square meters or more, lease term of one year or more, use for office).

2) Production Companies:

Original board meeting records or resolutions of the wholly foreign-owned company's investment company; determine three or more company names; confirm registered capital and business scope; feasibility report; articles of association; copy of the investment company certificate; original and copy of the identity proof of the Chinese legal representative and two one-inch color photos; original bank credit reference issued by the investor's overseas bank in two copies; lawyer notarization (notarization by the local notary office for overseas companies and certification by the Chinese embassy or consulate in the area); production address: original real estate lease contract in duplicate or own property certificate. Office address: own property or rented commercial factory (original lease contract in duplicate, registered and stamped by the local leasing office, when leasing, need to rent under the name of one of the shareholders or under the name of the overseas company, 200 square meters or more, lease term of one year or more, use for factory). Fire protection, environmental protection approval certificates (this content is self-handled);

3) Overseas Individuals:

Original board meeting records or resolutions of the investment company; original appointment documents and identity proof copies of the legal representative, board members, supervisors, and managers of the Chinese company; lawyer notarization (identity notarization by the local notary office for overseas investors and certification by the Chinese embassy or consulate in the area); two one-inch color photos of the legal representative; feasibility report; articles of association; bank credit reference issued by the investor's overseas bank in duplicate; provide relevant materials according to the relevant regulations for setting up a production enterprise if establishing a production enterprise, and provide relevant materials according to the relevant regulations for non-production enterprises if establishing a non-production enterprise.

13. Procedures for Setting Up a Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise

Delegate Procedure: Letter of Authorization (signing agreement) → Name Reservation → Pay Advance Fee → Submit Required Documents → Review Documents → Submit to Relevant Government Departments for Processing → Inform Progress → Approximately 20-50 Working Days to Complete → Notify Customer to Collect Documents → Pay Remaining Fee → Sign Receipt;

Customer Operation Procedure: Board resolution of overseas investors → Name Reservation → Confirm Business Scope → Confirm Registered Capital → Confirm Shareholders → Appoint Legal Representative of the Chinese Company → Determine the Chinese Office Location (consider fire protection and environmental factors if necessary) → Obtain Lawyer Notarization and Certification by Designated Official Institutions in China → Bank Credit Reference → Delegate Handling → Capital Verification → Post-Approval Approval (if necessary).

Government Processing Flow: Go to the Administration for Industry and Commerce for Name Reservation (reserve trade name/name approval) → Special Industry Pre-Approval (if necessary) → Apply for Foreign Investment Approval Certificate at the Trade and Industry Bureau → Apply for Business License at the Administration for Industry and Commerce → Engrave Seals at Designated Police Department Institution → Apply for Enterprise Organization Code Certificate at the Quality and Technical Supervision Bureau → Apply for National and Local Tax Registration Certificates at the Tax Bureau → Apply for Foreign Exchange Registration Certificate at the Foreign Exchange Administration → Open Basic Account at the Bank (non-basic accounts are handled independently) → Apply for Import and Export Registration at Customs.
