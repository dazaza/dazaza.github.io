I see the east wind

Gently blowing from the depths of the season.

Outside the window,

Large areas of cherry blossoms are blooming in order.

Those smiling reds

Are clearly your most beautiful dowry,

Emitting a scent that makes hearts race.

Beautiful spring,

Today, you are my bride.

At this moment,

Any kind of posture

Can make me forget that cold,

Forget that merciless rain and disaster.

Those once moving poems and hymns

Bloom ridge by ridge with mountain flowers.

At this moment,

I quietly lie in the most touching place of the season,

In a posture never moved before,

Listening to your tender songs,

Reading your stunningly beautiful figure.

My eyes moisten time and again,

For the heartfelt words flowing out of you

Are the accumulated soft love talk of my entire life.

Come,

Beautiful spring,

Today you are already my bride,

So bravely bloom like spring flowers in my heart.

This is the homeland of our lives,

And I will protect you with my life.

Under the charming sunlight,

We together harvest touching whispers.

Next year, at this season,

We will reap the gentle brilliance.
