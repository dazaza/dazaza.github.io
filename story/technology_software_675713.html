Chen Qi: I didn't think too much when saving the person

Chen Qi, 26 years old, shy and reserved, at first glance, it's hard to tell that he is a veteran. Chen Qi told the reporter that on that day, his family of three went to Xining for business. Since it was his first time driving out of town, he was especially cautious along the way. When the car reached the section of Factory 504, he suddenly saw something black ahead. As he got closer, he realized it was a person lying on the ground, and the person's head was full of blood. "At that moment, I didn't think too much, I quickly got out of the car to check, found that the elderly man was still breathing, and immediately dialed 120 and 110." After waiting for a while and seeing no one coming, he drove to the toll station 10 kilometers away and told them about the injured person, asking them to contact the relevant departments. Chen Qi thought it over and decided that since he encountered this situation, he must save the person. He then told his father his thoughts, which coincided with his father's ideas. He quickly drove back to where the elderly man was injured. At 4 a.m., the temperature had already dropped below 0℃. In such cold weather, the father and son took turns calling and signaling passing vehicles to avoid the scene.

"Having been a soldier, there is a sense of protecting others in my mind"

Chen Qi told the reporter that he actually had quite a lot of feelings after the incident. Thinking about stopping cars on the road at midnight, there were basically no pedestrians, many vehicles just slowed down to take a look, but no one got out of the car to ask about the situation. Perhaps because of being a soldier for two years, he has an instinctive sense of wanting to protect others. He laughed and said: "People often say, veterans don't fade, it's an instinct, I really didn't think too much. But I remember clearly, when the 120 and 110 arrived, my father and I were both frozen numb, we couldn't even speak, but we felt very reassured inside."
