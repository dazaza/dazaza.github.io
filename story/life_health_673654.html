How does diabetes develop into diabetic foot?

by sxykmgy on 2012-02-23 18:09:44

How does diabetes develop into diabetic foot? Diabetic foot is one of the more serious complications of diabetes, severely endangering the physical health of patients. Therefore, we need to understand how diabetes develops into diabetic foot so that we can take preventive measures based on the causes of the disease, thereby reducing its occurrence. Let's take a look at how diabetes develops into diabetic foot.

How is diabetic foot formed? It is generally believed that the causes of diabetic foot are closely related to medical history and age, as well as peripheral vascular disease, neuropathy, and infections. The causes of diabetic foot include the following factors:

First, the older the patient, the longer the medical history, the higher the blood glucose level, and the poorer the control of blood glucose during treatment, the more likely they are to develop diabetic foot. The occurrence of diabetic foot is mainly due to the combined effects of peripheral neuropathy and vascular disease in the lower limbs of patients.

Second, it is related to the constitution of diabetic patients, including qi deficiency and cold凝聚 (cold凝聚refers to a traditional Chinese medicine concept where coldness leads to stagnation or blockage in the body), resulting in yang energy not reaching the four extremities.

Third, how is diabetic foot formed? The risk factors for diabetic foot can be divided into external and internal factors. External factors include obesity, poor blood sugar control, foot injuries, infections, and improper foot care. Internal factors mainly include long duration of diabetes, kidney damage, visual nerve damage, vascular disease, peripheral neuropathy, old age, and foot deformities.

Prevention and nursing care for diabetic foot are particularly important. At any time, walking barefoot should be avoided to prevent damage to the skin of the feet. When washing your feet, use your hand to test the water temperature to avoid scalding caused by high water temperatures. After washing your feet, you should dry between the toes with a towel. In daily life, wear clean and comfortable cotton socks. Socks that are too tight can affect blood circulation in the feet.
