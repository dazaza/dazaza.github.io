Who says we're dull and lack sex appeal? Seven reasons to choose big companies

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 15:05:22

English Title: 7 Reasons Why You Need To Work For A Big Company

This article is written by Mark Stephen, the founder and CEO of IDR Solutions.

Large companies are often seen as slow-moving and lacking in excitement, while startups are considered flexible, efficient, and full of fun. I am currently running a startup, but to be honest, I am glad that I once spent an unforgettable period working for a large company. Below are the reasons why I think big companies have their own unique advantages:

1. Exposure to both brilliance and absurdity

In big companies, you'll encounter both exceptional insights and ridiculous inefficiencies all under one roof. You will witness groundbreaking ideas about source code control as well as endless meetings that drain motivation. While large corporations may not always seem dynamic, they have their reasons for scaling up successfully. Even the most ordinary institutions have hidden talents. Uncovering the logic behind how large companies operate and identifying employees who excel both technically and politically can become a significant asset for you.

2. Talented people around you

In some departments of large companies, you’ll find clusters of smart individuals. Working alongside such talented people is, in my view, a wonderful experience — it feels like intelligence permeates the air around you. These intelligent individuals often choose large companies due to family and responsibility considerations, unwilling to take the risks associated with startups. Experienced core employees can teach you much more than what you might learn from colleagues at startups, who tend to be younger and less experienced.

3. Networking benefits

Twelve years ago, I worked for a large company. To this day, I remain in touch with many of my former colleagues, and I often run into them in various places. These connections are incredibly valuable. In today's era of social media, it remains crucial for people to know who you are and what you can do. Your employment history at a large company is documented, and when you need to conduct business with other large companies, this experience can open doors for you.

4. Enjoy great benefits

I miss the cafeteria, gym, and that summer in Amsterdam that my previous large company provided... There were also Oracle training courses fully funded by the company, and tuition reimbursement for an MBA program. It all sounds amazing! In contrast, at a startup, getting your book purchases reimbursed would be a rare treat. So, would you rather spend someone else’s money to gain knowledge at a large company or work tirelessly for 100 hours a week at a startup just to chase a distant dream?

5. Learn the art of corporate politics

At a large company, you don’t just learn technical skills; you also develop "diplomatic" and "political" skills. When you transition from being an employee to a partner of a large company, these skills will come in handy. Large companies love to buy things and aren't afraid to spend money. Understanding how they operate can help you better conduct business and tap into more potential clients through them.

6. Embrace the slower pace

Startups move at a breakneck pace, whereas large companies often progress more slowly. While this may lack the thrill and excitement of a fast-paced environment, it offers more time for learning, self-reflection, and personal growth. Sometimes, this "slower pace" can even have unexpected positive effects. For instance, I won’t tell you that a major media company avoided spending millions on a terrible web project simply because their decision-making process was too slow, which ultimately saved them from the dot-com bubble burst...

7. Understand your principles and pursue your dreams

When you first enter the workforce, you may feel fearless and ambitious. However, after experiencing the ups and downs of working for a large company, you’ll gain clarity on what is worth doing and what isn’t, what truly matters, and what doesn’t. You'll also understand what your lifelong dreams are and what your unbreakable principles are. Defining your professional creed can help you perform better in your career.

These are the reasons why I enjoy working for large companies. What are your reasons?
