Tencent QQ also wants to benefit from China Telecom's 189 mobile phone.

by herohu on 2009-04-13 09:53:13

Yesterday I wrote an article about the Tianyi 189 mobile information website, and I wanted to share some insights with everyone, so I decided to reserve a nice QQ number as a dedicated QQ number for the site. My work number is definitely not suitable, and the number of friends is also approaching the limit. Therefore, I want to get a good QQ number for the website, which will facilitate communication with netizens. That's why I thought of getting a QQ premium number.

When I arrived at the QQ Number Mall, there were many types of premium numbers. However, since I'm not doing business, these numbers are generally rarely used by people. You must activate membership, and there are selection fees. Even worse, you don't own the number; if you pay, you can use it, but if you don't pay, Tencent will reclaim the number. For business purposes, the instability of the number is a big taboo.

I noticed that Tencent has also launched 189 premium numbers in a timely manner. The initial selection fee is around 200 Q-coins, though personally, I feel they're quite average—just repeated digits, like 189AABAAB, all nine-digit numbers.

To activate this number, you have to pay the initial selection fee plus 3 months of QQ membership. I saw that particularly attractive premium numbers from the past have already been selected. The type of premium number I wanted, such as 189153133, had no record left. Later, I casually looked at a decent enough number. Thinking that the initial selection fee was only over twenty yuan, and considering the business need, investment was necessary. So, I decided to choose a 189QQ premium number.

It wasn't until the payment step that I realized I made a mistake: 200 Q-coins isn't 20 yuan, but rather 200 yuan, even with a 10% discount, it's still 180 yuan. Dizzy! The initial selection fee for a slightly better 189 number has caught up with the price of a Telecom 189 mobile phone number. It's overwhelming how much Tencent capitalizes on the popularity of Telecom's Tianyi 189 mobile services. A similar situation exists with other prefixes, such as Mobile's 188.

The scariest part is that despite such high costs, some friends actually chose them. Haha, I guess it's either because they have money or it's a business necessity. Tencent really knows how to seize the opportunity to make money, grabbing whatever they can, since it doesn’t cost them anything.

I suggest that Tencent open applications for regular 189 QQ numbers. I just want one starting with 189, after all, since the Tianyi 189 Mobile Information Network is doing Telecom's 189 business.
