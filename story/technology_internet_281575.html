English: Resident Identity Card of the People’s Republic of China [Edit This Section] One of the Origins of China's ID Card The earliest ID card system in China should be the "Resident Certificate System" established by the Ningxia Provincial Government of the Republic of China in 1936. Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region (historically known as Ningxia Province), located in the northwest border area, was economically and culturally backward in its early days, but became the birthplace of China's ID card system for historical reasons. [Edit This Section] Historical Reasons Ningxia Province was established on January 1, 1929, bordering Gansu, Shaanxi, Suiyuan, and Mongolia. In 1933, Ma Hongkui was appointed as the Chairman of the Ningxia Provincial Government by the central government. To strengthen his rule, Ma Hongkui began a province-wide household registration check and established the Baojia system from 1934. In May 1936, the Central Red Army began its western expedition, liberating places like Yanchi, Yuwang, and Tongxin in Ningxia. To resist the Red Army, Ningxia formulated various anti-Communist and anti-Communist strategies, issuing and collecting "Resident Certificates" as one of them. Residents aged 15 and above, regardless of gender, were required to wear resident certificates. [Edit This Section] Resident Certificate - The Earliest ID Card Ningxia's "Resident Certificate" was made of white cloth, measuring 7 cm long and 3 cm wide. It included details such as name, age, place of origin, occupation, height, appearance, characteristics, and the shape of handprint patterns. The "Resident Certificate" was stipulated to be sewn inside the upper garment for inspection. Although this "Resident Certificate" differed from the later National ID cards, it already possessed all the elements of an ID card, making it the earliest prototype of China's ID card. However, the implementation of Ningxia Province's "Resident Certificate" system was not smooth. The main reason was that the cloth-made resident certificate was sewn onto clothing, and due to washing and wear and tear, it quickly became blurred. Therefore, this system only lasted for over a year before becoming defunct. In the summer of 1940, Ma Hongkui ordered the issuance of the "Ningxia Province National Identity Card" based on the household registration and resident certificate checks. For this purpose, the "Ningxia Province National Identity Card Issuance and Inspection Law" was also formulated. Alongside issuing "ID Cards," "Residence Permits for Non-Native People" and "Passports" were also issued, aiming to "prevent traitors, spies, traitorous parties, and bandits." In reality, Ningxia Province's establishment of the ID card system was mainly to blockade our Shaan-Gan-Ning Border Region base and further strengthen control over the Ningxia people. Since the invention of the national identity card, it became much easier for Ma Hongkui to conscript soldiers. After male laborers were drafted, their identity cards were immediately confiscated by the military. If a conscripted soldier escaped, the family would be held responsible. Escaped soldiers without ID cards would find it impossible to move around in Ningxia: either they would be caught again or escape to other provinces. The implementation of the national identity card system played a special role in Ma Hongkui's rule and control over the people, preventing and capturing deserters.
