WeChat "Shake" finds a handsome "boyfriend", three young women willingly pay 1.44 million yuan

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 17:38:54

At 10:06 on October 25th, the Dongxin Police Station of Hangzhou's Xiacheng District released a message through their official Weibo account "@Dongxin_COP" stating that they had "arrested a dating scammer," accompanied by an ID photo below the post.

(Image from Dongxin Police Station's official Weibo)

The man in the photo has thick eyebrows and big eyes with regular features. The police officer handling the case said that the real person is even more handsome, bearing some resemblance to the mixed-race actor Takeshi Kaneshiro, with a melancholy gaze that has a strong "fatal attraction" to girls.

"Shaken" to a Good Boyfriend

The "melancholy man" surnamed Zhu, 32 years old, is from Wuyi, Jinhua, without a fixed job.

In March 2012, Zhu came to Hangzhou for a vacation. Bored, he opened WeChat's "Shake" feature. At that moment, Ms. Li, less than 900 meters away, also took out her phone.

Zhu's WeChat profile picture was a sharply defined side face. Without asking too many questions, Ms. Li quickly accepted his friend request. They chatted well, feeling like they met too late.

A few days later, Zhu invited Ms. Li to meet, pouring out deep confessions. Ms. Li is the same age as Zhu. In her eyes, Zhu has a good physique, is handsome, dresses well, and is witty—truly talented and good-looking.

After establishing a boyfriend-girlfriend relationship, Zhu was very attentive and generous.

Ms. Li's parents have only one daughter. They worked hard in Hangzhou for her sake. After working hard for most of their lives, they bought a house in Hangzhou. Ms. Li also wanted to find a good man who could take care of her parents in the future. Zhu not only cared for Ms. Li but also occasionally sent gifts to her parents. Ms. Li's friends all said she was lucky to have met such a "good man."

Took 350,000 RMB and Disappeared

In June last year, Zhu told Ms. Li that he was going to invest in a chain supermarket project in Shandong but lacked funds, still needing 200,000 RMB.

Ms. Li is a salaried worker, and her family's financial situation is not affluent; she couldn't come up with so much money at once. Zhu became somewhat unhappy. He said that since he was preparing to marry Ms. Li, he was trying every way to find projects to make money. The investment was for their common future. Ms. Li was deeply moved and secretly mortgaged her family's house to the bank, borrowing 350,000 RMB.

Zhu took 270,000 RMB. Before leaving, he solemnly promised Ms. Li that his parents had run supermarkets for many years at home, and he had rich experience; he would surely successfully negotiate this project.

After half a month, Zhu appeared disheveled before Ms. Li. He said that the journey was tough, the project was difficult, and just hiring people had cost him a lot of money. The more than 200,000 RMB he brought along was all used up.

Seeing Zhu dejected and listless, Ms. Li felt heartbroken and gave him the remaining 80,000 RMB without hesitation.

Unexpectedly, after taking this "emergency fund," Zhu disappeared like a ghost, leaving no trace. When Ms. Li called him, it was either turned off or unanswered.

Met Three Girls and Scammed 1.44 Million RMB

This June, Ms. Li's 350,000 RMB loan was about to mature, but Zhu never reappeared. She worried about being unable to repay the money and losing the property deed. If the matter was exposed, she feared her parents might fall ill.

With no other choice, Ms. Li decided to report the incident to the police.

On the morning of October 24th, the police officers from the Dongxin Police Station found Zhu according to tip-off clues and investigation information in a budget hotel in Hangzhou.

Zhu confessed that since 2010, he had successively met three women through online social platforms like Jiayuan, QQ, and WeChat, borrowing money from them under various pretexts such as "investing in supermarkets," "being in a car accident," and "business capital turnover," totaling over 1.44 million RMB.

Zhu enjoys showing off, and almost all the money he obtained has been squandered. Although he usually spends extravagantly, his family's financial condition is not as prosperous as Ms. Li imagined. What he referred to as "parents running supermarkets at home" is actually just a small shop selling sundries.

Former Girlfriend Still Doesn't Believe He's a Scammer

Zhu said that during their interactions, he often emphasized "marriage as the goal," gaining the trust of the other party. With this "trump card," the women rarely doubted him.

When meeting Ms. Li, Zhu was still in contact with his "former girlfriends" Guo and Zhou, repeatedly asking them for money, amounting to over 1 million RMB combined.

Guo and Zhou are both from Zhejiang Province, living in different cities, unaware of each other's existence. Upon receiving a call from the Dongxin Police Station officers, they were quite surprised.

Guo said that she indeed lent Zhu a sum of money, but later, Zhu went to another city, and their feelings gradually faded, so she didn't bring up the repayment issue anymore. She thought, after all, they were "friends" once, if he couldn't pay back, it's fine.

Zhou was also unwilling to believe that Zhu was a scammer. Regarding the money she lent him, Zhou straightforwardly stated: the money was voluntarily given, and if he wants to return it, he can; if not, it doesn't matter.

According to Zhu, Zhou and Guo's families are wealthy, and hundreds of thousands of RMB mean nothing to them.

The police said that regardless, they still hope Zhou and Guo will contact the authorities to assist in punishing the crime.
