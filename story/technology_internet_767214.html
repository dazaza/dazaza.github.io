Next-generation Galaxy Tab rumored to adopt Android + Win RT dual systems

by anonymous on 2013-10-10 18:40:34

October 9 news, according to foreign media reports, Samsung's next generation of Galaxy Tab tablets may, at Microsoft's request, adopt a dual-system with Android and Windows RT.

It is reported that Microsoft asked Samsung to consider adding Windows RT and Windows Phone systems respectively on their Android tablets and smartphones, giving consumers the choice to use them.

An insider also revealed that Samsung has started developing a 12-inch ultra-thin tablet computer equipped with an ultra-high-definition display. People who have used this dual-system device claim it runs very smoothly and quickly.

It was reported last week that HTC is also considering integrating Windows Phone into future Android smartphones. In order to expand its mobile market share, Microsoft seems to be trying every possible way, reportedly offering attractive conditions such as reducing system licensing fees and providing compensation to attract cooperation from these manufacturers.

Notably, the news about Samsung's new generation of Galaxy Tabs adopting a dual-system was exposed by Eldar Murtazin, a well-known Russian tech blogger and analyst.

Murtazin's leaked information has been proven credible multiple times. For instance, he was one of the first to report the news about NVIDIA launching reference design tablets. Of course, not all of his "leaks" are correct; for example, he incorrectly reported Microsoft's plan to connect Windows Phone with Windows RT in Windows 9.
