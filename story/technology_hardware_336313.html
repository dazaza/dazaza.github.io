The term "gossip" is generally derogatory. Today, when used about my son, it seems a bit amusing. My son just turned fifteen years old; though he's tall and big, he's still very childlike. He always boasts that he's an adult, sometimes even tries to act cool in front of me, but deep down, he's extremely simple. He's always chattering, either about one thing or another. Sometimes I find him annoying, thinking boys should be more profound. Why is he so much like a woman? But through his chatter, I learned about their school, teachers, classmates, and even the girls from the neighboring class. Recently, he's been talking mostly about changing seats, how they communicate with the organization committee member, which male classmate helped his "girlfriend" get a good seat, whose phone was confiscated by the teacher, and how they scared her and then comforted her; the female class president admires the handsome biology teacher, the math teacher got married but didn't give them wedding candy... Anyway, our dining table is never lonely. The discussion topic in our group today was "the issue of children interacting with the opposite sex." This is a topic we often talk about, since they were little. I know all the girls who have a good relationship with him in his class, and I also understand the few girls he liked in middle school and elementary school. I asked him: "Do you plan to fall in love during high school?" "No." "Why not? Aren't there two couples in your class?" "I would never! Even if I like someone, I'd only secretly love them and wouldn't express it. Think about it, once you say it out loud, will she like you? If she doesn't, how embarrassing; even if she does, everyone will know you're in a relationship, and you'll be at the center of attention. Teachers and parents will bombard you, and classmates will happily talk about you." Haha, so that's the plan! "What if you get stuck in secret love and can't get out, affecting your mental health and studies?" He looked at me with a smile: "Is your son really that stupid?" Haha! I continued to "gossip" with him: "Then what about the few girls you liked in elementary and middle school?" The boy seemed a bit embarrassed: "We were just playing around. There wasn't any real liking, just a bit of admiration at most." "I remember when you were little, you vowed to marry an American wife. American girls are hard to please, with serious feminist ideas." "That was in elementary school. In middle school, I wanted to marry a Russian wife, how beautiful! Then Uncle Chen said Russian women get fat after thirty-five, like beer barrels. That's so scary!" "Now it's high school, find a French girl, how romantic. French people are romantic to the bone." "Mom, let's not talk about this for now. There's no target right now. Think about it, romance can't be eaten, and being romantic every day would be exhausting, right?" After saying this, he wiped his mouth, pushed away his bowl, and left. The gossip between the gossipy mom and gossipy son has come to a temporary end!
