Wumai Network: 1+1>2

by ybcz0519 on 2010-12-19 12:12:55

It has been almost 10 months since the launch of the first version of Wumii at the end of February. Today, as we introduce the product Wumii Network to our users, we want to share the path that Wumii envisions taking. The philosophy behind Wumii is to make the internet smarter and more personalized, and the foundation for achieving this philosophy is "1 + 1 > 2." Why is 1 + 1 greater than 2? When I first encountered recommendation systems during my graduate studies, it was through an article introduced by my advisor titled "Collaborative Filtering..." which translates to 协同过滤 in Chinese. My initial reaction was one of surprise: recommendations are about giving you more information that interests you, so how does it become filtering? Later, I realized that recommending content you're interested in essentially filters out what you're not interested in—it's just a matter of perspective. What truly stuck with me was the significance behind collaborative filtering: many things individually may not have much use, but when combined, they can create new value. This simple principle can even be applied in algorithms. The principle of collaborative filtering is very straightforward: people have similarities, and things have likenesses. To recommend a book to you, we only need to find someone whose tastes are similar to yours and recommend books they like but you haven't read yet—you'll likely enjoy them (since your tastes are similar). Two readers with similar interests each clicked on articles they liked; these clicks individually might be useless, but together they can recommend content to each other. A single neuron doesn't do much, but countless neurons meaningfully connected form our brains. Similar examples abound in daily life, and this seemingly simple logic became the soul of Wumii: the company name "Er Mu" (Two Trees), the recommendation algorithm based on collaborative filtering, and today's introduction of the Wumii Network based on interconnected websites.

Starting with the Related Articles Plugin

A few months ago, Wumii developed a related articles plugin that has been loved by website owners, with many well-known domestic blogs using it. The related articles plugin has existed for a very long time, and a quick search on WordPress alone yields hundreds of results. So why did Wumii decide to recreate it? The fundamental reason is that plugins have limitations and offer little room for innovation (these plugins refer to those generally attached to blog frameworks like WordPress or Z-Blog).

Common Practices

The most common method used by related articles plugins is reliance on article tags (Tag). Identical tags indicate some degree of relevance between two articles, and the more identical tags, the more relevant. This is easy to understand, the algorithm is simple, and it generally doesn't put too much pressure on the website server. Based on similar ideas, some plugins also consider article categories, content, and time, but all these approaches share a fatal flaw.

Drawbacks

Most of the algorithm processing for general plugins is completed within the plugin itself, meaning that the plugin must run very quickly, otherwise it will affect the webpage loading speed. This is the main reason why most site administrators dislike installing too many plugins, as it can slow down their sites. As a result, innovations in related articles are greatly limited. Here are some areas where related articles could improve but are difficult to implement due to plugin environment restrictions:

1. Introducing behavioral data can help readers discover more good articles. Behavioral data refers to features you often see on Taobao or Amazon such as "bought this also bought that" or "viewed this also viewed that." The classic collaborative filtering method in recommendation systems requires relatively longer processing times, making it difficult for general plugins to achieve.

2. The weight of article tags may vary. An article may have multiple tags, but one of those tags might truly represent the theme of the article. If the varying weights of different tags can be discerned, it will help find more relevant articles.

3. Processing heavy tasks such as images and videos usually takes longer and is difficult to complete directly within the plugin.

4. Perhaps in the future, related articles won't be limited to just being related articles but will evolve into recommendations—content that users are interested in but may not necessarily be related to what they're currently viewing. Or perhaps related articles won't be limited to the same site but can include any relevant article from anywhere on the internet.

Cloud-Based Related Articles Plugin

Some might think that these improvements bring limited value and may not be worth doing. However, when Google started doing search, the search industry wasn't looked upon favorably either. To break free from the constraints of typical plugins, Wumii adopted a cloud computing model:

The main task of the plugin is to send a request to the Wumii server and return related articles. This approach not only avoids putting any pressure on the website owner's server but also gives Wumii ample space for innovation. Wumii can perform any complex algorithm to calculate relevance and conduct time-consuming image and video processing, simply caching the results. There are a few companies abroad that have adopted a similar model, but their performance regarding the relevance of Chinese websites always falls short. Because there is room for innovation, Wumii's related articles plugin now brings an average of 10%-20% more page views to websites.

Wumii Network

Wumii developed the related articles plugin specifically for "Wumii Network," a product we've been preparing for a long time and a bold experiment we hope all site administrators will participate in.

What is Wumii Network?

The Wumii Network consists of individual websites. Within this network, site owners can request connections with any other website, and once approved, the connected websites will share resources and create more value. At this stage, connected websites will display each other's content in the related articles section. Wumii ensures the relevance of articles, the fairness of exchanged traffic, and anti-cheating measures among other technical issues.

What Value Does This Network Offer?

From the Reader's Perspective: Reading is no longer limited to a single website. For example, if a reader sees an article on "Google plans to spend $6 billion to acquire Groupon" on one site, they might next want to read "Groupon rejects Google acquisition," which is on another site. Before websites were connected, readers would be restricted to the information available on a single site; after connection, information can complement each other.

From the Site Administrator's Perspective: Reader volume rapidly increases. Assuming your website has 10,000 readers, connecting with another website makes the total number of readers 20,000, and connecting with 100 websites makes the total number 1 million. Even with some overlapping readers, the scale of this network effect can be astonishing.

From the Marketing Perspective: Those who have managed small websites know how hard it is to promote a site when it's just starting out. Most of what you do includes exchanging friendly links with other websites, mutual visits, sending IPs, leaving your website link everywhere, etc. Recently, I saw a certain online marketing forum that skyrocketed within one or two weeks to become one of the top 500 sites in China, showing just how high the demand for this is among site administrators. The quality of a website's content and whether the administrator understands marketing aren't directly related. Asking administrators to spend so much energy on marketing is, to some extent, a waste of resources. In the past, there was no platform like Wumii Network, but if administrators could save this time and focus directly on creating better content, I believe the entire internet would become better.
