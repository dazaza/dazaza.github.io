1. Post-feeding procedures

Every time after feeding milk, lift the baby and gently lean them against your shoulder. Use your hand to slowly pat the baby's back to help them expel the air they inhaled.

2. Feeding frequency

Artificial feeding differs from breastfeeding, so there must be an appropriate feeding frequency. If the intervals and number of feedings are not regulated, undesirable consequences will appear. Breastfeeding encourages "feeding on demand," whereas artificial feeding requires scheduled feedings. How should one feed?

Previously, we have already learned how to calculate the daily amount of milk and water. Let’s take a 5-kilogram infant as an example. A 5-kilogram infant needs 550 milliliters of milk daily. Divide these 550 milliliters into 7-8 feedings, each being about 70 milliliters. The interval between each feeding is 3-4 hours. For instance, if you feed at 7 AM, the next feeding would be around 10-11 AM. During the interval between two milk feedings, you should give the child some plain water. Distribute 200 milliliters of water during the gaps between milk feedings. At night, to allow both mother and child to rest well, stop feeding once.

3. Bottle-feeding posture

When feeding milk, the rubber nipple should be lower, and the bottle bottom higher, ensuring that the milk covers the rubber nipple part. This prevents air from being fed into the baby's stomach. If the baby inhales too much air, they may easily spit up milk.

4. Milk volume adjustment

The elderly should carefully observe the baby's reaction. If the child cries shortly after feeding, you can appropriately increase the milk volume; conversely, if the child cannot finish the calculated milk volume, you can feed less milk. For artificially fed children, regularly measuring their weight to adjust the milk volume is also an effective method.

5. Preparations before feeding milk

First, disinfect the milk according to the previously introduced method, and sterilize the feeding utensils for standby. After filling the bottle with milk, drop a little of the milk onto the back of an adult's hand. If it does not feel hot, you can then feed the child.
