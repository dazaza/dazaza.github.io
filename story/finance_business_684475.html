How to Choose Appropriate Children's Clothing for Your Baby

by tonzhuanyin0y on 2012-03-01 01:31:58

I. About Styles:

1. Summer children's clothing with square necklines, round necks, and small pointed collars are preferable.

2. It’s best if the clothes open at the front; there shouldn’t be too many buttons for ease of dressing and undressing by the child themselves.

3. Choose wide-waisted dresses to conceal a child’s protruding abdomen and lack of defined waist, providing a loose and cool feeling.

4. Children's pants can include sun pants or field pants.

Note: Many parents like to dress their children in tights, but if these are too short, they can restrict lower limb movement, which is not conducive to a child's development.

5. When selecting tops, sleeves should not be too long as overly long sleeves can hinder arm movement, preventing fine motor actions, reducing finger activity opportunities, and negatively impacting brain development.

6. It’s best if the clothes open at the front with not too many buttons for ease of dressing and undressing by the child themselves.

In general, when purchasing children's clothing, one should primarily consider the nature of children. During play, the comfort level of the clothing is an important factor and should feature relaxed and natural casual wear. As children are still developing, wearing comfortable, neat, and loose casual clothes allows them to conveniently play games and engage in sports. This not only aids physical development but also gives off a sense of gentleness, cuteness, comfort, and spontaneity.

II. About Colors:

1. The color of children's clothing should be bright and beautiful. Preschool children already have feelings and preferences for colors. If interesting animal patterns or other colorful decorative patterns can be added to children's clothing, it will spark interest in wearing them and bring endless joy to the child.

2. Babies are naturally sensitive to colors and have unique preferences. Therefore, when selecting clothing for babies, judgments should be made based on the child's body shape and skin tone. For darker skin tones, high brightness and high purity clothing colors are preferred, making the child appear vibrant and spirited. For lighter skin tones, the adaptability range of colors is wider. For example, pink, yellow, and red make people look lively and radiant, while gray and black give a sense of elegance and refinement, creating an impression of "suitable whether heavily or lightly made up."

While paying attention to the adaptation of color to a child's skin tone, one must also consider the pairing of the child's body shape with the color of the clothing. For a relatively plump baby, cold or dark-colored clothing such as gray, black, or blue is recommended because cold and dark colors create a contracting effect, compensating for the child's bodily imperfections. For thinner children, warm-colored clothing like green, beige, or coffee brown is preferable as these colors expand outward, giving a warm feeling.

Of course, there is no fixed format for children's clothing color matching. Excessive standardization can seem rigid and lifeless, while too much variation can appear chaotic. The sole principle is that the color combination should be aesthetically pleasing and comfortable to look at.

Therefore, when selecting children's clothing, in addition to considering color, style, and fabric, we as parents should fully take into account the physiological characteristics of the baby. The clothing should reflect softness, breathability, comfort, safety, and health. Here are some points to note:

1. Various labels on children's clothing

a. Whether the product has a trademark and Chinese manufacturer's name and address.

b. Whether the product has size markings.

c. Whether the product has composition markings, mainly referring to the material and lining composition markings of the clothing. The percentage content of various fibers should be clear and correct. Clothing with filling materials should also indicate the composition and content of the filling materials.

d. Whether the product has washing instruction symbols and explanations, and understand the methods and requirements for washing and maintenance.

e. Whether the product has a certificate of conformity, product execution standard number, product quality grade, and other marks. Additionally, if the product indicates formaldehyde content, for infants aged 0-24 months, it should be <20 mg/kg, for children over 24 months, clothing in contact with the skin should be ≤75 mg/kg, and non-skin-contact clothing should be ≤300 mg/kg.

a. Whether there are obvious weaving defects on the main parts of the children's clothing surface.

b. Whether there are color differences and fabric "slippage" or "alignment" issues in the main stitching areas of the children's clothing.

c. Pay attention to the quality of various accessories and decorations on the children's clothing, such as whether the zipper slides smoothly, whether the buttons are secure, and whether the snap fasteners are appropriately tight.

d. Check whether there are any de-lamination, bubbling, or seepage issues on the surfaces with interlinings, such as collars, lapels, pocket flaps, and front openings.

Safety considerations are paramount. For children under 1 year old, metal buttons should be avoided to prevent injury during dressing and undressing.

III. Quality Identification of Children's Clothing Sewing:

a. Visually inspect whether the sewing lines on the main parts of the children's clothing are straight and whether the seams are flat.

b. Check whether the symmetrical parts of the children's clothing are consistent. There are many symmetrical parts on children's clothing, so you can fold the left and right parts together to check whether the symmetrical parts are accurate.

V. Don't Let Innocence Be Lost:

Nowadays, some children's obsession with brands is truly astonishing. A five-year-old child upstairs, upon seeing his mother buy a new piece of clothing, curiously asked, "What brand is it, Mom?" The mother said this was all due to kindergarten influence. Nowadays, children even compare each other's outfits: "Why are you so proud wearing Winnie the Pooh? I'm wearing Mickey Mouse today!" "What brand are your socks? What about your shoes?" Even at a young age, children talk about brands fluently and refuse to wear unbranded clothes when going to school.

The advantages of branded children's clothing need not be overstated, nor does the love parents have for their children. However, none of these should be reasons for children to blindly compete. In this era where children start paying attention to "brands" and seek confidence through clothing, the influence of parental consumption views is the original source. Packaging a child is like packaging oneself—when a child dresses well, the parent feels dignified. Because of this, it's common in malls to see parents spending hundreds of yuan on branded clothing for their children. While having the economic means is certainly understandable, children's innocence is fragile and cannot withstand the long-term influence of materialism. Perhaps, giving children a rational consumption view is truly what being tasteful is all about.

VI. How Should Children Dress in Different Seasons for Health?

In different seasons, the clothing requirements for children vary. The younger the child, the faster they dissipate heat, so their clothing should be warmer. In summer, it's not only about wearing less, but the material and style should also be ventilated and breathable. Winter clothing may consist of multiple layers, but if the indoor temperature is stable, indoor and outdoor clothing should differ—wearing the same amount indoors and outdoors can easily cause a cold.

In hot weather, girls should preferably wear sleeveless dresses. However, clothing should not be overly "revealing"—strapless and halter tops are unsuitable for children. As children have tender skin, excessive sun exposure can make it dark and rough. Therefore, when taking children out in summer, avoid overly revealing clothing to protect their skin from UV damage. Boys should preferably wear short-sleeved shirts or vests, shorts, or suspenders.

Children's outerwear should have pockets for handkerchiefs. In spring and autumn, according to changes in weather temperature, appropriate thick fleece clothing or woolen jackets, knit sweaters, or padded coats can be worn over shirts. Outdoor winter clothing should have good insulation properties and prevent cool air from entering through buttons, collars, and cuffs. Children should also have a thick coat, especially necessary in northern regions. Coats can be made with cotton or down.

Size Elements in Selecting Children's Clothing:

How can one choose a fitting children's garment? Generally speaking, the 'A' or 'Y' on children's clothing labels refers to age, sometimes denoted by 'T'. M usually represents months, while A, Y, or T represent years. So, 2A is equivalent to 2Y, meaning for 2-year-olds, and similarly, 3A equals 3Y for 3-year-olds. Typically, a child's head accounts for 20% of total height, while the body accounts for 80%. When choosing clothing for children, the body height is usually used as the standard.

For instance, for a girl who is 100 cm tall, her body height is 80% of 100 cm, approximately 80 cm. If selecting a dress, the length would be 70% of 80 cm, i.e., 56 cm. If selecting a shirt, the length would be 50% of 80 cm, i.e., 40 cm. When shopping for children's clothing away from home, this formula can help select a satisfactory and fitting outfit for the child. Currently, the sizing standards for children's clothing in the domestic market are very chaotic. Except for branded children's clothing, nine out of ten items have inaccurate sizes. Therefore, when buying children's clothing, one should primarily look at the actual size of the clothing rather than the size label. Even if the size on the clothing is accurate, it might still not fit properly due to varying rates of child development. Children grow at different speeds at different stages; growth is faster between ages one to four and slower between ages five to eight. At this stage, children's clothing needs approach that of adults.

Measurement Methods:

Children's Clothing Size Measurement and Calculation Formula:

A child's head accounts for 20% of total height, while the body accounts for 80%. When selecting clothing for children, the body height is usually used as the standard.

Short pants for children are approximately 30% of body height;

Shirts for children are approximately 50% of body height;

Long pants for children are approximately 75% of body height;

Jackets for children are approximately 49% of body height;

Suits for children are approximately 53% of body height;

Coats for children are approximately 70% of body height;

Dresses for girls are approximately 78% of body height.

For example, for a girl who is 100 cm tall, her body height is 80% of 100 cm, approximately 80 cm. If selecting a dress, the length would be 70% of 80 cm, i.e., 56 cm. If selecting a shirt, the length would be 50% of 80 cm, i.e., 40 cm.

Clothing measurements should be based on actual measurements, as size standards vary among manufacturers and styles. When choosing a size, please refer to specific lengths! Each mother has a different concept of what fits well, and we cannot judge whether something is "appropriate" for you. Measure the length of similar clothing for your baby, as all dimensions are measured from real items. Due to the stretchability of fabric, there may be a 1-2 cm error; please allow for this when selecting.
