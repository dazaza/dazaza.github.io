Brief Introduction to Ten Natural Detox and Beauty Foods

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-17 16:32:08

As environmental pollution becomes increasingly serious, modern people are paying more attention to their own health. Experts point out that only by promptly removing harmful substances and excessive nutrients from the body and maintaining cleanliness of the internal organs can one maintain physical fitness and beauty. Here, we recommend 10 natural and economical detoxifying foods.

Cucumber: Sweet in taste, neutral in nature, also known as green cucumber, Chinese cucumber, or prickly cucumber, it originated in India and has obvious effects of clearing heat and detoxifying, quenching thirst. Modern medicine believes that cucumbers are rich in protein, sugars, vitamin B2, vitamin C, vitamin E, carotene, niacin, calcium, phosphorus, iron, and other nutritional components. Additionally, cucumbers contain propanediol, cucurbitacin, soft fine fibers, and are rare detoxifying and beautifying foods. The cucurbitacin in cucumbers can promote human metabolism and expel toxins. The vitamin C content is five times higher than watermelon, which can whiten the skin, maintain skin elasticity, and inhibit the formation of melanin. Cucumbers can also inhibit the conversion of carbohydrates into fat, benefiting the lungs, stomach, heart, liver, and excretory system. Eating cucumbers helps alleviate inflammation when feeling irritable, thirsty, with a sore throat or excessive phlegm during summer.

Lychee: Sweet and slightly sour in taste, warm in nature, it has the effects of tonifying the spleen, nourishing the liver, quenching thirst, and detoxifying. Li Shizhen wrote in *Compendium of Materia Medica*: "Regular consumption of lychees strengthens the brain and promotes health..." *Suishenju Dietary Spectrum* records: "Lychees are sweet, warm, and fragrant, they invigorate the mind, enhance intelligence, replenish essence and fluids, eliminate odors, relieve pain, nourish the heart, and enrich the liver and blood. They are excellent fruits, especially fresh ones." Modern medicine believes that lychees contain vitamins A, B1, C, pectin, free amino acids, proteins, and various elements such as iron, phosphorus, and calcium. Modern medical research proves that lychees have kidney-nourishing, liver function improvement, accelerate toxin removal, promote cell generation, and make the skin delicate, making them ideal fruits for detoxification and beautification.

Black fungus: Sweet in taste, neutral in nature, it has detoxifying, cleansing intestines, harmonizing blood, and stopping bleeding effects. Ancient books record that black fungus "benefits qi, reduces hunger, lightens the body, and strengthens willpower." Black fungus is rich in carbohydrates, gelatin, lecithin, fiber, glucose, xylose, choline, carotene, vitamins B1, B2, C, proteins, iron, calcium, phosphorus, and many other nutritional components, earning it the reputation of "meat among vegetables." The plant gelatin in black fungus has strong adsorption ability, concentrating and adsorbing dust and impurities remaining in the human digestive system, then expelling them from the body, thereby playing a role in detoxifying and cleansing the stomach.

Honey: Sweet in taste, neutral in nature, has been an excellent supplement for health and beauty since ancient times. *Shennong's Herbal Classic* records: "Long-term consumption strengthens willpower, lightens the body, prevents aging, and prolongs life." Honey is rich in vitamins B2, C, fructose, glucose, maltose, sucrose, high-quality proteins, potassium, sodium, iron, natural flavorings, lactic acid, malic acid, amylase, oxidase, and various components, having significant effects on moistening the lungs, relieving cough, promoting bowel movements, detoxifying, and beautifying. Modern medical research proves that the main components of honey, glucose, and fructose, are easily absorbed and utilized by the human body. Regular consumption of honey can achieve the effect of expelling toxins and beautifying, and is also beneficial for preventing cardiovascular diseases and neurasthenia.

Carrot: Sweet in taste, cool in nature, it has the effects of nourishing blood, detoxifying, strengthening the spleen, and harmonizing the stomach, often referred to as "little ginseng." Carrots are rich in sugars, fats, essential oils, vitamin A, vitamin B1, vitamin B2, anthocyanins, carotenes, calcium, iron, and other nutritional components. Modern medicine has proven that carrots are effective detoxifying foods, not only containing abundant carotenes but also large amounts of vitamin A and pectin. After binding with mercury ions in the body, they can effectively reduce the concentration of mercury ions in the blood and accelerate the expulsion of mercury ions from the body.

Bitter melon: Sweet in taste, neutral in nature. Traditional Chinese medicine believes that bitter melon has detoxifying, beautifying effects. *Compendium of Materia Medica* states that bitter melon "removes evil heat, relieves fatigue, clears the heart and brightens the eyes." Bitter melons are rich in proteins, sugars, crude fibers, vitamin C, vitamin B1, vitamin B2, niacin, carotenes, calcium, iron, and other components. Modern medical research has found that bitter melons contain an active protein with obvious anti-cancer effects, which can stimulate the immune system's defense functions, increase the activity of immune cells, and remove harmful substances from the body. Although bitter melons have a slightly bitter taste, they leave a sweet aftertaste and have gradually become popular on the dining table in recent years.

Kelp: Salty in taste, cold in nature, it has the effects of eliminating phlegm, relieving asthma, detoxifying, and promoting bowel movements. Kelp is rich in alginic acid, mannitol, proteins, fats, sugars, crude fibers, carotenes, vitamins B1, B2, C, niacin, iodine, calcium, phosphorus, iron, and other components. Especially rich in iodine, it is very beneficial to the human body and can treat goiter and iodine deficiency-related diseases. The proteins it contains include eight kinds of amino acids. After being absorbed by the human body, the iodine compounds in kelp can accelerate the elimination of pathological changes and inflammatory exudates, lower blood pressure, prevent arteriosclerosis, and promote the excretion of harmful substances. At the same time, kelp also contains a substance called sulfated polysaccharides, which can absorb cholesterol in the blood vessels and expel it from the body, keeping the cholesterol content in the blood at normal levels. In addition, there is a layer of sweet-tasting white powder on the surface of kelp, which is mannitol with high medical value, having good diuretic effects and can be used to treat drug poisoning and edema. Therefore, kelp is an ideal food for detoxification and beautification.

Tea: Cool in nature, sweet and bitter in taste, it has the effects of clearing heat, relieving irritability, aiding digestion, losing weight, and promoting urination. China is the homeland of tea and places great importance on it. Ancient books record: "Shennong tasted hundreds of herbs, encountering seventy-two poisons in a day, and was cured by tea," indicating that tea has excellent detoxifying effects. Tea is rich in iron, calcium, phosphorus, vitamin A, vitamin B1, niacin, amino acids, and various enzymes, its effects of refreshing the mind, improving head clarity, quenching thirst, and cooling the body are particularly significant. Modern medical studies show that tea is rich in an active substance - tea polyphenols, which have detoxifying effects. As a natural antioxidant, tea polyphenols can clear reactive oxygen free radicals, promoting health and delaying aging.

Winter mushroom: Sweet in taste, cool in nature, it has the effects of tonifying qi, strengthening the spleen, detoxifying, and moisturizing dryness. Winter mushrooms contain glutamic acid and 18 other amino acids, including 7 of the 8 amino acids required by the human body. It also contains more than 30 enzymes, as well as glucose, vitamin A, vitamin B1, vitamin B2, niacin, iron, phosphorus, calcium, and other components. Modern medical research believes that winter mushrooms contain polysaccharides that can enhance the body's immunity and detoxification ability, inhibit cancer cell growth, and strengthen the body's anti-cancer ability. In addition, winter mushrooms can lower blood pressure and cholesterol, prevent arteriosclerosis, strengthen the heart, protect the liver, calm the mind, promote metabolism, and enhance the excretion of waste products in the body, making them the best edible fungi for detoxification and strengthening the body.

Mung beans: Regular drinking of mung bean soup can help expel toxins from the body and promote normal metabolism. Sweet in taste, cool in nature, it has the effects of clearing heat, detoxifying, and reducing fire, and is commonly used by Chinese medicine to counteract poisoning from various foods or drugs. Mung beans are rich in vitamin B group, glucose, protein, amylase, oxidase, iron, calcium, phosphorus, and other components. Many people experience symptoms such as itchy skin, acne, and heat rash after consuming greasy, fried, or hot foods, which are caused by damp toxins overflowing onto the skin. Mung beans have powerful detoxifying effects and can remove various toxins. Modern medical research proves that mung beans can lower cholesterol and have liver-protecting and anti-allergic effects. During summer and autumn, mung bean soup is an excellent choice for detoxification and beautification.

Other beauty uses and precautions for these foods:

For dry or cracked lips, honey is the best option. When your lips are dry or peeling, you can carefully let honey penetrate the lips before bedtime and then apply lip balm. After a few days, your lips will recover their softness and smoothness. However, the best strategy is still to drink plenty of water regularly and not forget to consume starches.

Since mung beans are cold in nature, people with weak spleen and stomach should not consume too much; those with low blood pressure and women during menstruation should avoid eating them. Additionally, mung beans should not be overcooked to prevent the destruction of organic acids and vitamins, which would reduce their heat-clearing and detoxifying effects.
