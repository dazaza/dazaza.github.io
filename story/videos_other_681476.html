Breaking the Standard Puzzle: Smart Homes Can Develop

At the end of the year and the beginning of a new one, it is the time for all industries to summarize and look forward.

When reviewing the development trajectory of intelligent buildings last year, the reporter found that smart homes made a stunning rise in 2011, quickly becoming a much-anticipated "new star" in the intelligent building industry.

Expanding Market Demand, Cross-Border Corporate Involvement

Currently, Chinese society is in a period of transformation, with growing wealth disparities and increasing social instability factors. People are paying more attention to their personal safety and property protection. According to statistics, last year's household consumption expenditure in China was 11.2 trillion yuan, with first-tier cities and eastern regions spending between 900 billion to 1.1 trillion yuan on smart homes and home security!

Enticed by the massive market "cake," many home appliance companies and IT enterprises have dived into the smart home industry, bringing their own business philosophies and cutting-edge technologies. In 2011, Changhong Electric Co., Ltd. of Sichuan established the cross-group business unit known as the Four Seas e-Home project team, exploring the industrial integration between real estate and home appliances, creating an industrial model for smart home terminals and platforms. This has formed a complete e-home industry chain from R&D design, production, promotion to application. Meanwhile, the company also applied intelligent products and Internet of Things (IoT) technology to smart families and communities, achieving interconnected homes.

Huawei, a giant in China's IT industry, announced its entry into the security field at last year's Shenzhen Security Expo. The company showcased a full series of M2M module products and solutions covering 2G and 3G formats at the exhibition, some of which can meet the needs of smart neighborhoods.

In addition to IT and home appliance companies, long-established smart home manufacturers have also clearly felt changes in the market. Shu Zhongming, product manager of the Intelligent Living Space Division of Schneider Electric (China) Co., Ltd., stated that with the gradual popularization of smart products like iPhones and iPads, high-tech products have bridged the gap between users and smart homes. Currently, Schneider's smart home business volume is gradually expanding, with related products and solution businesses accounting for 20%~30% of the division’s total business, and this figure still has significant growth potential.

The clustering of companies entering the market and the increase in business volume of related products... These changes are just a glimpse of the rapid development of the entire smart home industry. Under the impetus of large enterprises, advanced technologies, and substantial capital, the entire industry has entered a phase of rapid development.

Companies Operating Independently, Industry Struggles to Strengthen

Home appliance companies, IT companies, and security companies have continuously "crossed boundaries" into the smart home industry, creating a prosperous scene. However, behind this prosperity, the industry harbors hidden dangers.

In the smart home market, early participants were mainly manufacturers of intercoms and electrical products, such as Guanlin and Ajubao. Their related technologies were mainly applied within households and neighborhoods. Companies expanded the original functions of their products to form a complete smart home solution, becoming the main force in the smart home industry. Additionally, some home appliance companies struggling to find breakthroughs in the traditional white goods market adjusted their strategies and actively developed the emerging smart home market, preparing for the next round of competition. Haier, TCL, Midea, and other well-known domestic companies set good examples. This year, Changhong and other companies announced their ambitious entry into the smart home industry, with business strategies similar to those of Haier and TCL.

Many companies see the vast market potential of smart homes and flock into this emerging industry. However, while competing for market share, each manufacturer has certain differences in technical operations, leading to fragmented efforts. For instance, some IT companies introduce elements such as the Internet of Things (IoT) and cloud computing into the smart home industry, creating the concept of "cloud communities" where residents can effectively "communicate" with lighting, curtains, alarms, TVs, air conditioners, water heaters, and even external medical, educational, and cultural resources through mobile phones and the internet; some companies integrate security technology into smart homes, forming security-focused smart home solutions; some lighting and curtain companies use automation technology to monitor and manage home appliances... Different companies tie their smart home concepts with their specialized technologies, forming distinctive smart home systems. This leaves consumers feeling like they're "viewing flowers in a mist," unsure of what to choose.

Industry analysts believe that the current smart home market is relatively chaotic, with many companies hyping up the concept of smart homes. Many so-called smart home products on the market only incorporate certain elements of intelligence, such as video intercoms and anti-theft alarms, rather than being true smart homes. Due to the lack of unified standards in the entire industry, many immature products are hastily launched, prematurely depleting the future of smart homes.

Establishing Unified Standards to Break the Industrial Dilemma

Guo Weijun, an expert from the National Intelligent Building and Residential Area Digitization Standardization Technical Committee, believes that due to the lack of unified standards in the industry, the quality of smart home products on the market varies greatly. After networking, various problems often arise, causing inconvenience to users and resulting in low market recognition. Unstable product quality means that many products and systems developed by companies remain in the experience stage, unable to achieve industrialization. Without scaling and industrializing, the industry will struggle to sustain healthy development.

He suggests establishing a comprehensive standard system for intelligent buildings and smart homes under the support of relevant ministries, creating a smart home technology verification platform and comprehensive service platform based on IoT.

Zhang Yonggang, Deputy Director of the National Intelligent Building and Residential Area Digitization Standardization Technical Committee, believes that the Chinese smart home industry is relatively young and is currently in its initial stages of development. The industry needs to go through a long process from hype to rationality and then to partial regional applications. Currently, we need to continuously improve services and strengthen standardization construction to promote product interoperability and drive the industry toward scale and industrialization.

Liang Feng, director of the Electronics Information Department of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, pointed out that the incomplete standards of the smart home industry and IoT constrain the healthy development of the industry. However, from the current situation, it will take some time to establish comprehensive standards. After the standards are formulated, how to implement them remains a challenge. Therefore, practicing in standards and improving them in practice to promote the development of the smart home industry will require a long journey.
