Don't underestimate these minor illnesses after the Start of Spring - Medical Inquiry Network

by lokes350 on 2012-02-24 15:18:24

After the Beginning of Spring, the weather warms up a bit. These days, the bright sunshine has greatly improved the moods of people who have just celebrated the Spring Festival. However, health care in the spring should not be neglected. Therefore, citizens need to prepare for the prevention or treatment of several common diseases that are prone to occur in the spring, so as to enjoy the good mood brought by spring in a healthy way.

1. Heatiness (上火)

Although the climate in spring is suitable, there is more wind and less rain. The water in the human body is easily lost through sweating and breathing. Plus, the constantly changing weather makes it difficult to maintain the balance and stability of human metabolism, leading to physiological dysfunction. Symptoms such as sore throat, chapped lips, and dry stools may appear due to "heatiness."

Countermeasures: To prevent "heatiness," one should first live a regular life, balance work and rest, and get proper rest; eat more vegetables and fruits, and avoid spicy foods; drink more water or cooling beverages to promote the excretion of "heat-inducing substances" from urine and sweat; if necessary, under the guidance of a doctor, take heat-clearing medicines such as Niuhuang Shangqing Pills, Sanhuang Tablets, Qingguo Pills, etc.

2. Depression, Insomnia

In addition to making people sleepy, spring can also cause insomnia. Dr. Xu Yi from Zhejiang First Hospital told reporters that spring is a high incidence period for various mental illnesses. Due to low air pressure, it can easily lead to hormone disorders in the brain, and the ever-changing weather often causes rapid mood swings in normal people.

Countermeasures: For normal people, mood fluctuations are normal, but they should know how to regulate them. When you find yourself in low spirits, pay attention to shifting your negative emotions. When feeling down, you might listen to music or participate in sports activities. Meanwhile, on sunny days, you can go for walks in the countryside to breathe fresh air. If these methods cannot relieve bad moods, you can seek professional psychological counseling for psychological guidance.

3. Spring Fatigue (春困)

Looking out at the bright scenery outside the window, people stuck in the office always seem listless and lack energy, which is known as "spring fatigue." "Spring fatigue" is mainly related to disharmony in weather, work, diet, sleep, and exercise. Sometimes this phenomenon also indicates sub-health.

Countermeasures: For those who have "caught" "spring fatigue," they can do more deep breathing and aerobic exercises to increase lung capacity, drink more water, eat less greasy and hot foods, especially hotpot. Eating more foods rich in vitamins such as carrots, cabbage, and citrus fruits, and getting enough protein helps improve one's energy levels; office workers who feel drowsy can alleviate symptoms by doing head massages.

4. Asthma

When spring arrives, all kinds of flowers and grasses bring new colors, making people's eyes light up. However, it also makes some particularly "sensitive" people uncomfortable. Spring is the high season for asthma, and if daily living details are not taken care of, it is easy for asthma to recur.

Countermeasures: Clothes and bedding for asthma patients should preferably not use down or silk materials. Pay attention to whether certain foods trigger asthma attacks, such as shrimp, milk, peaches, etc., which should raise awareness; be cautious or avoid using certain drugs that may induce asthma, such as aspirin, indomethacin, propranolol, etc.; reduce the inhalation of pollen, and avoid going out during the day or in the afternoon.
