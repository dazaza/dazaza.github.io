Last Saturday, the transfer expert Mora of Qsvs TV station confirmed again that "Montolivo and Natali have signed with AC Milan (Weibo), and it is certain that they will join in June." Regarding Natali, Mora doesn't mind saying a few more words, "He will replace Yepes, who has decided to leave Italy after the season."

If we talk about acquiring from Fiorentina, then it's not surprising that Natali, the player considered as the "big sieve" in Serie A, comes as a complementary product to Montolivo. However, the issue is that this transfer is a free transfer, which means that Natali's arrival is an autonomous choice of AC Milan. But people familiar with Serie A all know that although Natali is 3 years younger than Yepes, there is a world of difference between their fighting capabilities.

The reason why AC Milan chooses Natali is actually all because of Raiola, this isn't a secret. Natali will become the first player under Raiola's management in AC Milan's team. Currently, Eli and Desole in the youth team, Ibrahimovic, Emmenelson, and Van Bommel in the first team, and Diakate on loan, are all players under Raiola's management. By examining the timeline of these transfers, it's not hard to discover the correlation among them. Eli and Desole joined during the process of AC Milan's operation of Ibrahimovic's transfer, while Diakate joined due to Van Bommel's arrival. In fact, while Ibrahimovic and Van Bommel have performed excellently, Eli and Desole have been the weak links in the youth team, and Diakate has long been on loan. The package deal, which includes both the pig and its bristles, is something Raiola always likes. When Inter Milan (Weibo) introduced Ibrahimovic, Moratti was almost forced to accept Maxwell and the "seal" Kerlon, who spent two years recuperating from injuries at Inter Milan. Nowadays, the situation with AC Milan is similar. Raiola is a unique figure in the agent world, he can help AC Milan achieve some seemingly impossible tasks, such as the transfer of Ibrahimovic and the free transfer of Van Bommel, but you must also accept some of the garbage he pushes onto you. Overall, the balance sheet still shows a profit.

Natali's arrival also implies that AC Milan may once again benefit from Raiola. Raiola had publicly revealed that "he would bring a top-level forward to AC Milan this summer," and subsequently, Robin van Persie's name surfaced. Considering that Ibrahimovic had openly expressed his hope for Van Persie's arrival, and taking into account Raiola's influence in the Netherlands, the name of this forward is no longer a secret. Meanwhile, Pedullà also revealed: "Although there is no marriage for now, the relationship between AC Milan and Balotelli is far beyond just a promise."

Moggi once evaluated Raiola: "His eye for talent is incredibly accurate. If he were to build a team, it would certainly be world-class." In today's transfer market where big-money spending is difficult, AC Milan has found a broker with such insight who can turn 1 million into 10 million worth of deals. Swallowing a bit of garbage in the process, really, how significant is that?
