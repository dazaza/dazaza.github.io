Ranking of the most respected brands in the technology industry: IBM first, Apple second

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:57:01

According to a survey by the brand and market research company CoreBrand among 10,000 corporate executives at the vice president level, Apple ranks second among the most respected brands in the technology industry.

CoreBrand has compiled a list of "The Most Influential Brands in America," interviewing executives from many of the world's largest companies and asking them to rank brand familiarity and brand favorability. (The companies listed have all been publicly traded for at least five years. The brands listed on the list are those that CoreBrand has tracked for over five years.)

Here are the top five most admired tech brands by executives:

1. IBM

Although Apple is highly favored by consumers, IBM provides unparalleled IT services for businesses. IBM specializes in data analysis and cloud computing and has experienced rapid growth in emerging markets.

2. Apple

Apple has launched new products such as the iPad Air and the iPhone 5s with iOS 7 operating system, and it is ready to reclaim the title of "the most innovative company." The reason Apple ranks second may be due to its underwhelming performance for most of this year.

3. Microsoft

Although Microsoft's Windows operating system does not match Google's Android, Microsoft's platform remains the first choice for many developers.

4. Yahoo!

Yahoo! CEO Marissa Mayer is well-known for her ability to turn struggling brands into gold. After Mayer joined, Yahoo!'s homepage and email services were revamped, and the company acquired the microblogging platform Tumblr.

5. Texas Instruments

Texas Instruments is renowned for semiconductor production, and the company’s strong business ethics have always been highly praised.

Due to being tracked for less than five years, Google was not included in CoreBrand's list. Corebrand stated that if only surveyed among senior corporate executives, the ranking results would not be surprising.
