Chinese business circle

by 7mui547i9c on 2009-12-02 13:04:10

In today's business world, there is a club-like social circle. The people who enter this circle are all famous entrepreneurs who share difficulties and benefits alike, with quite a chivalrous character.

The eighth issue of China Weekly magazine cover story: Chinese Business Circle - When Huayi Brothers went public on the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM), behind Wang Zhongjun and Wang Zhonglei were other brothers -- Jack Ma, Charles Chen and David Lu; when COFCO acquired Mengniu, Ning Gaoning and Niu Gensheng are "close buddies" in the Chinese Entrepreneurs Club; when Fosun invested in Legend Holdings, Liu Zhiquiang and Liu Chuanzhi are old acquaintances from Taishan Club... Placing these corporate stories together reveals an interesting phenomenon: In today’s business world, there exists a social circle akin to a club. The individuals who enter this circle are all renowned entrepreneurs who share each other's burdens and benefits, possessing a distinct chivalrous color.

We have selected four such new-style business circles for interpretation: the most active Chinese Entrepreneurs Club, the Taishan Club with the strongest historical sense among private entrepreneurs' organizations, the Yabuli Forum (Chinese Entrepreneurs Forum) with the strongest forum attributes, and the Huaxia Alumni Association that best represents the sentiment of being classmates.

These organizations are mostly spontaneously formed. Entrepreneurs here form alliances to gain satisfaction from having their own values recognized, and use everyone's strength to enhance social influence. With their easy interactions, they can finalize marketing cases that the press hypes up; their power is formidable enough that no one can ignore them, and when they organize events, people from all walks of life flock to attend; they are knowledgeable and wealthy; they also initiate environmental protection and charity activities to establish the image of corporate citizens.

These phenomena narrate the relationship between business circles and society, reflecting the traditional Chinese circle culture in today's market economy. The commercial history of modern China tells us that only by improving the market system, standardizing profit-seeking behavior, strengthening self-discipline of enterprises and individuals, can this business circle become a healthy and important commercial force that promotes social progress, rather than a trigger for social resentment towards the rich, or degenerating into a gray interest group or a new privileged class.

Emerging Friend-like Business Circles

A kind of salon-like entrepreneur organization has gradually emerged in society. They are all famous entrepreneurs who like to do big business together.

Reported by Song Heying, Beijing Correspondent

On October 30th, after ten years of preparation, China's Growth Enterprise Market opened in Shenzhen. The opening day saw a 122.74% increase in stock prices, which made Wang Zhongjun, chairman of Huayi Brothers, smile broadly.

Among those attending the opening ceremony with him were the company CEO Wang Zhonglei, director Feng Xiaogang, etc. The third-largest shareholder and vice-chairman, Jack Ma, did not appear. At that time, the 13.1 million shares of Huayi he held were worth approximately RMB 834 million.

Many people have been curious: Why did Jack Ma invest in a film and entertainment company? What kind of connection does he have with Wang Zhongjun and others? How did he get onto the board of directors of Huayi Brothers?

This needs to be explained starting from the entrepreneur's circle.

Brother Goes Public, Friends Come to Support

The prospectus of Huayi Brothers roughly recorded Jack Ma's process of entering the company: In June 2006, Jack Ma bought 10.7% of Huayi Brothers' equity for RMB 535,000 and immediately entered the Huayi Brothers board of directors. In the following three years, Huayi Brothers underwent several capital increases and equity changes, but Jack Ma's equity always remained above 10%. In January 2008, Jack Ma was elected as the vice-chairman of the Huayi Brothers board of directors and has held this position ever since.

Public data shows that Jack Ma is one of the 18 initiators of Huayi Brothers and currently holds the largest amount of shares outside the actual controllers of the company, the Wang Zhongjun and Wang Zhonglei brothers. Two other well-known shareholders of Huayi Brothers are Charles Chen (chairman of Focus Media) and David Lu (president of Wanxiang Group, son of Lu Guanqiu), each holding 5.904 million shares, accounting for 4.69%.

It seems that David Lu's Wanxiang Group's main business is industrial manufacturing, which is unrelated to Huayi Brothers' entertainment industry, but coincidentally, David Lu and Jack Ma both belong to Zhejiang businessmen. They jointly initiated and invested in Jiangnan Society. They often communicate, so Jack Ma introduced Huayi Brothers to David Lu. A reporter found that "when Charles Chen and David Lu first purchased 'Huayi System' shares, some of the shares were sold to them by Jack Ma."

The unit cost of shares held by Jack Ma, Charles Chen, and David Lu back then was very low. Some media reported that it was around RMB 0.5 per share. If this claim is true, then the opening price of Huayi Brothers at RMB 63.66 has already made their investment increase more than a hundred times.

There's no need to be amazed at their extraordinary investment acumen. In fact, Jack Ma and Wang Zhongjun, Alibaba and Huayi Brothers, had an extraordinary cooperative relationship long before.

Tracing back to Jack Ma's rise to fame, one person must be mentioned -- Fan Xinman, who later became Zhang Jizhong's wife. In 1996, Fan Xinman, a fellow Zhejiang native, once filmed a documentary about Jack Ma promoting "China Yellow Pages" in Beijing. Later, under her introduction, Jack Ma met Zhang Jizhong.

Jack Ma loves martial arts novels and highly respects Zhang Jizhong, and is willing to sponsor Zhang Jizhong's projects. The 2006 TV series "The Return of the Condor Heroes" had Jack Ma's investment.

Investment brings returns. In 2005, after just completing the acquisition of Yahoo! China, Jack Ma urgently needed to boost the domestic reputation of Yahoo! Search. By the end of the year, he quickly gathered three major directors, Zhang Jizhong, Chen Kaige, and Feng Xiaogang, to shoot advertisements for him, and simultaneously launched the "Yahoo! Star Search" event led by these three directors. Since domestic "talent show" activities were at their peak at the time, Zhang Jizhong's participation greatly enhanced the promotional effect of Yahoo! Search.

During the cooperation with Zhang Jizhong, Jack Ma's connections also extended to Feng Xiaogang and the upper management of Huayi Brothers. A widely discussed case of embedded advertising is in Feng Xiaogang's New Year film "A World Without Thieves," released at the end of 2004, where the bandits played by Fan Wei and others each held a small flag with the words "Taobao.com".

Later, both parties further deepened their cooperation. First, Taobao.com publicly auctioned off the props used by the stars in the movie starting from RMB 1 via the internet platform; then the original cast shot another advertisement titled "With Alipay, No Thieves", helping Alibaba promote its products. Through mutual assistance, both sides in this circle leveraged their respective platforms to enhance their brands and reputations to varying degrees.

Just like Jack Ma's investment in Huayi Brothers, it is relatively common in the entrepreneur circle to give a brotherly company a push before going public.

For example, Niu Gensheng served as an independent director of Alibaba and held 100,000 shares of the company. This was significant for Alibaba, which listed in Hong Kong in 2007, as before that, Mengniu Dairy had already been familiar to local investors.

Similarly, Baidu CEO Robin Li and NetEase CFO Thomas Ting, due to their companies' earlier successful listings on NASDAQ, later became independent directors of New Oriental, playing a crucial role in Yu Minhong's company's IPO on the NYSE in 2006.

One helps in times of need, eight come to support

Compared to mutual support and shared profits when enterprises are rising, whether one party can step forward to rescue the other in temporary difficulties more clearly demonstrates the depth of friendship between entrepreneurs. People like Niu Gensheng, Ning Gaoning, Liu Chuanzhi, and Lu Zhiquiang have undergone such tests.

In September 2008, due to the "melamine" incident, Mengniu Dairy's stock price plummeted. As Mengniu had previously pledged part of its equity to Morgan Stanley, if it could not promptly replenish the margin, the company would face the risk of foreign acquisition.

In a critical moment, Niu Gensheng sought financial support from many well-known entrepreneurs. Soon, Liu Chuanzhi, Yu Minhong, and others rushed to provide emergency assistance funds ranging from RMB 50 million to RMB 200 million. In July 2009, after the crisis was resolved, Mengniu welcomed a huge RMB 6.1 billion investment from COFCO Group and Hopu Investment. This move allowed COFCO Hopu to acquire 20% of Mengniu's equity, becoming its largest single shareholder. Mengniu thus completely rid itself of the nightmare of foreign acquisition.

Although this acquisition was based on concepts like "big food" and "full-chain grain and oil food," analysts pointed out that the默契and "instant agreement" shown by Ning Gaoning and Niu Gensheng throughout the process were not unrelated to their personal feelings.

A relatively obvious piece of evidence is that after being controlled by COFCO, Niu Gensheng and his senior management team continued to steer Mengniu, while COFCO Group occupied only three non-executive director seats out of eleven director positions. Ning Gaoning later told the media that under the condition of basically stable equity ratios, COFCO had no plan to continue increasing its stake in Mengniu at present. This means that Niu Gensheng's position in Mengniu will remain safe for a longer period.

A newspaper in Inner Mongolia even captured some external details of their friendly relationship: On June 30 this year, a bottling plant jointly established by COFCO Group and Coca-Cola was laid down in the Shengle Economic Zone of Helin County, Hohhot City.

After officially settling down, this enterprise was just across the street from Mengniu. According to reports, at least four large passenger shuttle buses sent by Mengniu were responsible for transportation tasks during the groundbreaking ceremony, and the reason why COFCO Coca-Cola Company chose to settle in this park was due to Niu Gensheng's matchmaking and strong invitation.

Similar to this, the chess game of "Fosun acquiring Lenovo" orchestrated by Lu Zhiquiang and Liu Chuanzhi also permeated with mutual help and friendship.

On September 8, Fosun Group acquired 29% of Lenovo Holding's equity at a price of RMB 2.755 billion, becoming the third largest shareholder. This move enabled Lenovo Holding to smoothly return to the Liu Chuanzhi era.

In the reorganized five-person board of directors, two came from Sinopharm Holding, two from the employee shareholding association, and one from Fosun Group, with Liu Chuanzhi serving as chairman. In contrast, in the previous seven-person board structure of Lenovo Holding, four came from Sinopharm Holding, and Liu Chuanzhi was merely the vice chairman.

Afterward, Lu Zhiquiang revealed the details of this cooperation to the media: "Between Fosun and Lenovo, or between me and Chairman Liu or the Lenovo team, we have had a relatively long period of mutual understanding and established a certain degree of friendship. This friendship was built on credibility. Credibility has value, so our decision-making process was relatively faster. One might even say, this was a very important basis for decision-making."

According to his recollection, around 2004, Fosun Group had signed a number of land deals at once, and unfortunately encountered the "8·31" deadline (that year, the Ministry of Land and Resources and the Supervision Department stipulated that after August 31, agreements to transfer operating land use rights citing historical legacy issues would no longer be allowed, and instead, public bidding, auctions, and listing transactions must be conducted), causing a crisis in Fosun's capital chain. "At a critical moment, Lenovo gave Fosun great help, and we will never forget that."

Circles Maintaining Friendship

On the surface, these cases reflect the private relationships between entrepreneurs, but behind these private relationships, there are some rarely heard-of entrepreneur organizations. These organizations solidify their private relationships in forms such as clubs, forums, and alumni associations.

The aforementioned Jack Ma, Wang Zhongjun, Niu Gensheng, Ning Gaoning, Liu Chuanzhi, Yu Minhong, Charles Chen are all members of the Chinese Entrepreneurs Club. In this club, their friends also include Ma Weihua of China Merchants Bank, Zhang Ruimin of Haier, Pan Shiyi of SOHO China, and other top figures in the business world.

They would form groups to visit one another's companies; when planning businesses, they would first think of their friends within the circle; when a friend encounters difficulties, other friends would try their best to provide help.

After Niu Gensheng and Ning Gaoning jointly announced "COFCO's acquisition of Mengniu," some media analyzed that "if the investor Mengniu introduced this time was not COFCO, it might possibly be another company from the Chinese Entrepreneurs Club."

The origin of Liu Chuanzhi and Lu Zhiquiang's relationship can be traced back to another entrepreneur circle.

Lu Zhiquiang recalled that he and Liu got acquainted in the Taishan Industrial Research Society in the 1990s. This was once a salon similar in function to the Chinese Entrepreneurs Club, with many top figures from China's private technology enterprises active in it, including Feng Lun, chairman of the board of Wonton Group, and Shi Yuzhu, chairman of Giant Network.

Feng Lun is also an important member of the Huaxia Alumni Association, which is organized under the name of EMBA classmates from schools like Cheung Kong Graduate School of Business, representing another form of entrepreneur clubs.

Ouyang Ping, chairman of Hong Kong Baishida Company, and Wang Bing, chairman of Dingtian Asset Management Company, are classmates of Niu Gensheng at Cheung Kong Graduate School of Business. Their classmate bond prompted them to step forward and buy Mengniu stocks during the "Mengniu Crisis" period, helping Old Niu stabilize the stock price.

Private relationships are the core of these organizations' existence and development, so they generally keep a low profile. Some have never disclosed their membership lists externally.

However, regardless of how others view it, for these entrepreneurs, circles are undoubtedly an excellent resource network.

As early as when he was nominated for CCTV's Person of the Year in Economics in 2005, Jack Ma once delivered a speech saying that his greatest wealth is his friends, "If I were to leave this company, when I step out of this door, I believe that with a phone call, RMB 10 million would arrive within three days."

Four years later, Kai-Fu Lee turned Jack Ma's hypothesis into reality.

On September 4, 2009, he announced his departure from Google after four years of service. Three days later, he raised RMB 800 million in committed investments to start Innovation Works. Those providing these investments were all famous entrepreneurs, including Richard Liu, chairman of WI Harper Group, Steve Chen, co-founder of YouTube, Terry Gou, chairman of Foxconn, Liu Chuanzhi, and Yu Minhong.

Most of these people are old friends of Kai-Fu Lee, and everything was settled with a phone call, a dinner of Peking duck in the US, or a meal of sashimi in a Japanese restaurant.

According to media reports, Kai-Fu Lee guessed that among these investors, perhaps only Liu Chuanzhi carefully read his business plan.
