During sexual activity, the amount of vaginal discharge increases, serving as lubrication for the vagina to facilitate sexual intercourse. Around the middle of the menstrual cycle, the vaginal discharge increases, becoming thin and transparent; after ovulation, it becomes thicker, more turbid, and less in quantity. In the middle of the menstrual cycle, when the ovary is about to ovulate, due to the vigorous secretion of the cervical glands, the vaginal discharge increases, becoming transparent, slightly sticky, and resembling egg whites. Experts from the Gynecology Department of Zhengzhou Anti-Aircraft Force Hospital explain:

When women suffer from chronic cervicitis or endocervicitis, the vaginal discharge appears milky white or pale yellow, purulent, with a larger volume, an odor, and often accompanied by abdominal pain. If the yellow vaginal discharge is caused by Trichomonas vaginalis, it may be yellow or even yellow-green, thin with a foamy consistency, or resemble rice water, grayish-white in color, with a foul-smelling vaginal discharge. If the yellow vaginal discharge is caused by fungal infections, it is usually thick in texture, but sometimes can also be thin. Typically, it is milky white, like cottage cheese, or resembles coagulated milk-like white patches or lumps.

In cases of gonorrhea, the vaginal discharge is yellow and pus-like. In cases of fallopian tube cancer, due to tumor stimulation causing exudation of the fallopian tube epithelium and necrosis of diseased tissue, the vaginal discharge presents as intermittent, clear, yellow-red liquid, flowing out of the vagina continuously in waves. In cases of cervical cancer, vaginal cancer, or uterine body cancer, the "vaginal discharge" secreted usually shows characteristics such as increased volume, resembling rice washing water, turbid, and having a foul smell, sometimes mixed with blood appearing light pink and in large quantities.

Yellowish Vaginal Discharge: When the vaginal discharge turns yellow, it is often a sign of reproductive tract infection, generally caused by bacterial infections. Common conditions include vaginitis, cervicitis, endometritis, acute or chronic pelvic inflammatory disease.

Reddish Vaginal Discharge: When the vaginal discharge turns red, it often indicates bleeding in the reproductive tract. The redder the color and the greater the volume, the more serious the bleeding. In young women, red vaginal discharge is often due to infections or trauma; in older women, besides infections, malignant tumors such as cervical cancer or endometrial cancer should also be considered. Pregnant women experiencing red vaginal discharge should be alert to the possibility of miscarriage or preterm labor.
