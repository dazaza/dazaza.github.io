Xu Hongtao's reputation ruined by match-fixing, calmly states that the prospects for Chinese football are optimistic

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-12 18:41:45

Xu Hongtao's reputation ruined by match-fixing: calm and collected, he expressed optimism about the future of Chinese football. The anti-gambling and fake-match storm in Chinese football has uncovered another promotion-related match-fixing scandal. Yesterday, public security authorities confirmed through a CCTV program that the 2007 away game between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng was a fixed match. Former Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei confessed to the police that in order to ensure Chengdu Sheffield United's promotion to the Chinese Super League (CSL), Xu Hongtao, chairman of the board at Chengdu Sheffield United Club, and vice president You Kewei bribed the Hailifeng team with 500,000 yuan.

For their promotion, Chengdu Sheffield United paid 500,000 yuan for a fixed match. This game between Chengdu Sheffield United and Hailifeng was the second confirmed domestic league fixed match after the Guangzhou Pharmaceutical and Shanxi Lutiger game. Unlike the gambling-related match involving Wang Bo and others in the Guangzhou Pharmaceutical case, this one involved Xu Hongtao and You Kewei taking risky actions to bribe the Hailifeng team to ensure Chengdu's promotion to the CSL.

According to police details, on September 22, 2007, when Chengdu Sheffield United faced Qingdao Hailifeng, it was a critical moment for Chengdu's promotion campaign. To win this match, You Kewei and Xu Hongtao conspired to buy the game. After arriving in Qingdao, You Kewei met secretly with then Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei to plot the fake match. Ultimately, Chengdu offered 300,000 yuan in cash and allowed Hailifeng free winter training at their base for a month (valued at 200,000 yuan) as a condition for Hailifeng to "let up" during the game. In the end, Chengdu Sheffield United easily won 2-0.

Police spent six days and six nights capturing Liu Hongwei. Police revealed that the case involved deep collusion among those involved, with a long time elapsed since the incident, making arrest and investigation very difficult. One of the suspects, Liu Hongwei, fled upon hearing rumors but was eventually captured after six days and six nights of police surveillance and stakeout.

After criminally detaining You Kewei, Xu Hongtao, and Liu Hongwei, the police stated that they manipulated match results through commercial bribery, violating Articles 163 and 164 of the Criminal Law,涉嫌犯罪, and would continue to interrogate them.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Confessions from Those Involved

Three parties showed three different expressions

In yesterday's CCTV Focus Interview program, the three main individuals suspected of being involved in the fake match between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng displayed three different expressions while facing the camera. They all expressed regret for their roles in the fake match but had differing views on the future of Chinese football.

A Sigh

Liu Hongwei: Low skill levels make even fixing hard

"Chinese football skills are too low; even if you want to fix a match, you can't." In yesterday's program, former Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei, as the recipient of the bribe, revealed details of the fake match. Although Hailifeng fielded several young players in this game, Chengdu Sheffield United still couldn't score in the first half. At halftime, Chengdu Sheffield United vice president You Kewei had to call Liu Hongwei, who reassured him on the phone, saying "Don't worry, we'll let it go."

Regarding this fake match, Liu Hongwei sighed deeply, believing that Chinese football's level is so low that they can't even fix matches properly. When asked by the TV reporter if insiders could tell, Liu Hongwei dismissed it, saying "Insiders might be able to tell, but there are too many such incidents in Chinese football."

A Cry

You Kewei: Fixing matches was not my original intention

As the first exposed protagonist in this fake match case, former Chengdu Sheffield United vice president You Kewei tearfully expressed his regret in front of the camera, saying "(Fixing matches) was not my intention, I'm sorry!"

You Kewei has been notorious in the Chinese football circle for betting and fixing matches, but yesterday, facing the camera, he shed bitter tears, saying "I myself am an athlete, but the club proposed (fixing matches)...", implying that he felt compelled to do so against his will, saying "It really wasn't my original intention!"

Calm Composure

Xu Hongtao: Optimistic about the Future of Chinese Football

Xu Hongtao is undoubtedly the most significant figure in this fake match case, and also the heaviest-weight individual since the anti-gambling storm began in Chinese football. When interviewed by reporters, Xu Hongtao reflected on his journey from initially "upholding principles" to later "going along with the crowd." Facing the camera, Xu Hongtao remained calm and composed, offering his opinion on the anti-gambling crackdown in Chinese football: "Chinese football is like a critically ill patient needing supplements. This anti-gambling crackdown is like surgery to help the patient recover better!" Therefore, Xu Hongtao expressed optimism about the future of Chinese football, at least for now.

"During the 2007 match, it was a critical moment for our promotion. For safety's sake, we entertained the idea of buying the game," Xu Hongtao said, acknowledging his undeniable responsibility for the "buying matches" incident, lamenting his impatience and expressing regret.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Key Figures

From Legendary Agent to Chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United

Xu Hongtao's Reputation Ruined by Match-Fixing

In the latest fake match case announced by public security authorities, Xu Hongtao, chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United, is not only the most important figure in this case but also the heaviest-weight individual since the anti-gambling storm began in Chinese football.

Previously, Xu Hongtao was considered the most legendary agent in Chinese football, having arranged overseas transfers for many Chinese players. Notably, Hao Haidong, Li Tie, and Sun Jihai, representative figures of Chinese football, going to play in England were his masterpieces. In late 2005, Xu Hongtao arrived in Chengdu as a representative of Championship side Sheffield United to negotiate the acquisition of Chengdu Wuniu. In 2006, Xu Hongtao officially took over the Chengdu club and participated in that year's China League Two (second tier). In 2007, the team, named "Chengdu Sheffield United," high-profilely competed in the second-tier league and ultimately promoted to the CSL.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Arrest Details

Xu Hongtao Hidden in Shenzhen Still Arrested

On the 29th of last month, news spread again that Xu Hongtao had been taken away by the police. By then, Xu Hongtao's mobile phone was already unanswerable. Previously, the Chengdu side explained that Xu Hongtao and head coach Wang Baoshan had gone to Brazil to recruit foreign players, but after Wang Baoshan returned from Brazil, Xu Hongtao was still nowhere to be found.

Although everyone wanted to know where Xu Hongtao was, the Chengdu side kept silent about it, including club staff, coaches, and players. Over the weekend, Chengdu Sheffield United held a special internal meeting, where Vice President Luo Xiaowei required all members of the team not to contact Xu Hongtao in any form. Nevertheless, despite hiding in Shenzhen, Xu Hongtao was eventually taken away by the police.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Reactions from All Sides

Chengdu Football Association Chairman Temporarily Assumes Role of Chengdu Sheffield United Chairman

To Fly to the UK for Negotiations with Sheffield United

The arrest of Chairman Xu Hongtao cast a shadow over Chengdu Sheffield United's prospects. As the only club in the CSL co-owned by a local football association, the Chengdu Football Association formulated corresponding countermeasures yesterday. It is reported that Chengdu Football Association Chairman Gu Jianming will temporarily assume the role of Chengdu Sheffield United Chairman and travel to Hong Kong and the UK to negotiate with representatives of Sheffield United.

Speaking about the attitude of the joint venture partner, the English Premier League Sheffield United Club towards Xu Hongtao's involvement, Gu Jianming said, "They support the anti-gambling efforts actively, and our cooperation will definitely continue."

Chengdu Football Lightly Penalized or Heavily Demoted

Following Guangzhou Pharmaceutical Club, Chengdu Sheffield United became the second team embroiled in the "Gambling Gate." According to relevant regulations of the Football Association, both clubs, which successfully promoted from the 2007 China League Two, are highly likely to face the severest punishment of demotion ordered by the Chinese Football Association. Referring to the Guangzhou Pharmaceutical Club incident, Chengdu Sheffield United is more likely to face point deductions.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Voices

Nan Yong: These People Have Spoiled the Children

Yesterday, while CCTV News Channel broadcasted the latest anti-gambling news, Vice Chairman of the Chinese Football Association Nan Yong watched the entire news at the Football Association office building. According to CCTV reporters, Nan Yong attentively followed the latest developments in the anti-gambling campaign. In the TV news, a suspect incited players to gamble by saying "Don't listen to the club," which infuriated Nan Yong, who angrily condemned: "It's these people who have spoiled the children."

Chengdu Fans: Chinese Football Has Ruined Xu Hongtao

After the fake match scandal between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng was exposed, fans from both cities were shocked, especially Chengdu fans who found it hard to believe that club chairman Xu Hongtao had been arrested. A responsible person from the Chengdu fan organization "Red Blades" angrily expressed: "It's Chinese football that has ruined Xu Hongtao!" According to Chengdu media colleagues, Chengdu fans generally have a good impression of Xu Hongtao, particularly appreciating his previous commitment not to engage in match-fixing.
