The New iMacs: Faster. Bigger. Brighter.

by sindy143 on 2006-09-07 12:50:50

Since hooking up with Intel in June 2005, the performance of iMac has soared significantly. Now Apple is about to launch a new range of iMac products equipped with 64-bit processors — Core 2 Duo.

The newly launched iMac series will not only retain the existing models with 17-inch and 20-inch displays but also introduce a new model featuring a 24-inch display, imagining how it feels to sit in front of it and surf the internet (how unambitious, just knowing how to go online). Apple claims: The new iMac will be 50% faster than the previous Core Duo iMac (20" model).

The release of the new product has also prompted Apple to lower the original pricing. The previously priced $1299 for the 17"/Core Duo/iMac is now available at $999 (Core 2 Duo 1.83GHz); $1199 (Core 2 Duo 2GHz); $1299 (Core 2 Duo 2.16GHz).

The price for the 20" models are $1499 (Core 2 Duo 2.16GHz); $1749 (Core 2 Duo 2.33GHz).

The all-new 24" model is priced at $1999 (Core 2 Duo 2.16GHz); $2249 (Core 2 Duo 2.33GHz).

The new iMac series also features an integrated camera and comes with a remote control that looks like an iPod Shuffle, used for playing music videos or browsing photos.
