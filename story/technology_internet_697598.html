Couples mortgage their property to the pawnshop and are overwhelmed by high penalty charges

by pp70gaugna on 2012-03-08 16:27:12

In May 2011, Mr. and Mrs. Xiao Sun, in order to achieve the purpose of quick financing, mortgaged a jointly owned property to a pawnshop for a real estate mortgage loan [http://www.zhetong.cc/]. Both parties agreed that the pawn amount would be 1 million yuan, with a monthly comprehensive fee rate of 2.7%, a monthly interest rate of 0.3%, and a loan term of three months. Additionally, it was agreed that if Mr. and Mrs. Xiao Sun breached the contract, they would have to bear a penalty of 20% of the loan amount, and if repayment was overdue, they would have to pay fees calculated at 0.5% per day of the unpaid total until the debt was fully repaid. After signing the contract, both parties completed the relevant real estate mortgage registration procedures. On the same day, the pawnshop disbursed the loan as agreed.

After the three months expired, both parties signed an "Extension Supplementary Agreement for Real Estate Loan Mortgage Contract," extending the repayment period to October of the same year. After the extension period for the real estate mortgage loan [http://www.zhetong.cc/] ended, Mr. and Mrs. Xiao Sun still failed to repay the loan on time. As a result, the pawnshop took them to court, demanding that they repay the principal of 1 million yuan, pay a penalty of 200,000 yuan, and pay overdue fees of 5,000 yuan per day until the defendants fulfilled their repayment obligations. Mr. and Mrs. Xiao Sun argued that although they had indeed breached the contract, the agreed-upon penalty was clearly too high, far exceeding the sum of the monthly comprehensive fee rate and interest rate during the pawn period. Due to their current financial difficulties, they were unable to pay such a large amount of compensation and expressed willingness to pay the penalty at a monthly rate of 3%.

The court ruled that according to the law, when the agreed-upon penalty is excessively high or low relative to the loss caused, the parties may request the court to adjust it. Ultimately, the court ordered Mr. and Mrs. Xiao Sun to repay the pawnshop 1 million yuan and pay a penalty at a monthly rate of 3% based on 1 million yuan.

**Question: Should the defendant bear responsibility for breach of contract?**

Answer: The "Real Estate Loan Mortgage Contract" and the "Extension Supplementary Agreement for Real Estate Loan Mortgage Contract" signed between the plaintiff pawnshop and the two defendants represented the true intentions of both parties and were legally valid. The parties should fully perform their obligations as agreed. The plaintiff has fulfilled its obligations as agreed, but the defendants have not fulfilled their repayment obligations, constituting a breach of contract. They should therefore bear corresponding legal responsibilities.

**Question: Can the agreed-upon penalty amount be adjusted?**

Answer: Regarding the plaintiff's request for the defendant to pay penalties and overdue payment fees, both penalties and overdue payment fees fall under the terms for holding the defendant accountable for breach of contract. The defendant raised that the agreed-upon penalties were too high and unreasonable. According to the law, the court can be requested to make adjustments.

Article 114 of the **Contract Law of the People's Republic of China**: Parties may agree that one party shall pay a certain amount of penalty to the other party based on the situation of the breach of contract, or they may agree on the method for calculating loss compensation arising from the breach of contract. If the agreed-upon penalty is lower than the loss caused, the parties may request the people's court or arbitration institution to increase it; if the agreed-upon penalty is excessively higher than the loss caused, the parties may request the people's court or arbitration institution to appropriately reduce it. If the parties agree on a penalty for delayed performance, after paying the penalty, the breaching party must still perform the debt.
