If the areola is too large but the breast is not large or not sagging, a simple partial excision of the areola skin can be done to reduce the size of the areola. The surgical method is: centered on the nipple, draw a circle with an appropriate radius, outside this range, the ring-shaped areola skin is excised, the dermis layer and subcutaneous vascular network should be retained to prevent insufficient blood supply to the nipple and areola. Cut out a triangular piece of skin from both sides of the transverse diameter of the outer circle skin to reduce the radius, making it consistent with the new areola's radius. Finally, after slightly freeing up the subcutaneous tissue of the outer circle, suture it into position.

4. Women should not undergo surgery during menstruation, pregnancy, or within six months of breastfeeding.

Postoperative Care for Areola Reduction Surgery:

1. Avoid raising your arms after surgery, if necessary, the chest can be bandaged;

2. Breast massage can begin 4-5 days after surgery, squeezing in all directions, morning and evening each once, to prevent capsule contracture;

3. Take an appropriate amount of vitamin E after surgery, if redness and swelling or slight exudation is found at the wound site, or the subcutaneous tissue hardens, physiotherapy can be conducted;

4. Avoid sexual intercourse within one week after surgery, and be extremely careful with the breasts for the next month.
