Recently, the largest group-buying website in China, lashou.com, has reached a strategic cooperation with IBM. IBM will provide lashou.com with advanced ERP implementation services globally. Lashou is also the first group-buying enterprise to introduce information management, which will effectively manage internal resources and group-buying operations, significantly improve operational efficiency to ensure service quality of lashou.com. This also proves that the era of smart group-buying has arrived. Lashou trusts IBM's information management and customized overall solutions for group-buying. And IBM has initially attempted to serve group-buying enterprises by specially creating financial, human resources, supply chain and comprehensive budget management consulting services exclusively for group-buying companies, thus promoting this "ERP romance" within the group-buying circle.

As we all know, the combination of China's e-commerce industry with informatization has already achieved many successful cases in logistics and IT architecture. Internal enterprise informatization is undoubtedly the key to enhancing operation management capabilities and directly affecting production efficiency. The rapidly growing group-buying industry over the past two years has the characteristics of multiple cities, multiple sub-stations, and numerous employees. Rational allocation of online and offline human resources and systematic management of financial budgets will effectively promote corporate efficiency improvements.

According to the latest survey data, the total sales volume of the national group-buying market exceeded 11 billion yuan in 2011, among which lashou.com led the "billion-yuan club" with sales of 1.63 billion yuan. With enormous market potential and a team of thousands of people, facing fierce market competition, strengthening internal skills is inevitable. With the help of IBM's global e-commerce enterprise management experience and strong information system support, group-buying enterprises will achieve leaps in both the quality and quantity of orders and maximize profits.

We see that after more than two years of exploration, the group-buying industry has expanded its initial local group-buying services into a local service mall. Group-buying orders based on urban development have also expanded from food, drink, entertainment to physical goods, life services, and more types of services, including brand malls, coupon discounts, real estate, etc. Such explorations allow group-buying to offer increasingly wide-ranging services, showcasing the concept of "only what you can't imagine, not what you can't buy."

"Science and technology are the primary productive forces." The advancement of informatization injects strong impetus into the group-buying industry with large order volumes and extensive group-buying projects. Effective management of human resources and supply chains will indirectly enhance employees' handling abilities and efficiency in logistics, delivery, order processing, and website systems, effectively improving employee productivity and ensuring the high-speed operation of group-buying enterprises.

Lashou.com has successfully "held hands" with IBM, officially launching their cooperation. This means that lashou.com has pioneered the introduction of an information system in the group-buying industry and will successfully lead the way in information application when the e-commerce spring arrives.
