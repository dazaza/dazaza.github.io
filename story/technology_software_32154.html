"Love country" virus shocks the Internet

by xloong on 2007-10-24 19:55:47

Recently, a virus with the function of "acting opportunistically" has appeared on the Internet. Unlike common computer viruses, this virus coded as Win32/KillDPT will decide whether to terminate or invade and destroy based on the type of operating system of the machine, and it is praised as "specialized in killing systems of other languages, but safe for Chinese people", causing some netizens to exclaim "the virus also loves the country".

The "angry youth" virus not only spares the simplified Chinese operating system but also gives English systems a pass, though it will pop up a dialog box saying "Your luck's so good!" This is obviously a Chinglish sentence, which literally translates to "you have really good luck".

An engineer named Yang from the Beijing Jiangmin Company Antivirus Center stated that judging from the intent of the author, it is likely to vent dissatisfaction. According to the current monitoring situation at the center, the virus has not yet caused any adverse effects on Chinese systems. However, he also reminded computer users to be vigilant, "with slight modifications, it may cause adverse effects on any language operating system."

No impact on Simplified Chinese operating systems

It is understood that this virus is a kind of "partition table" killer virus, and the first to disclose this virus was an antivirus center in Beijing. It is introduced that after the virus runs, it will first read a key value in the registry and determine the type of the current operating system. If it is a simplified Chinese operating system, the virus will automatically exit and will not affect the computer in any way; if it is a Japanese or Indonesian language operating system, the virus will destroy the hard disk partition table and automatically fill the computer hard disk with garbage data, thereby causing the system to fail to find the boot system after restarting and eventually leading to the complete paralysis of the computer; and if it is an English system, it will also automatically exit but simultaneously pop up a dialog box saying "Your luck's so good! (This is obviously a Chinglish sentence, which literally translates to 'you have really good luck')".

In addition to the above "extreme" methods, this virus also has an anti-"Taiwan independence" "patriotic function". Once the virus detects that the target computer is using a traditional Chinese operating system, it will exit and pop up a message saying "Ah Bia, step down." From the screenshots provided by the antivirus center, despite the messages not being very fluent in language, they are filled with "patriotic sentiments", including phrases like "Comrade Ah Bia, you like Taiwan independence so much, do you have any shame... Do you want to be a criminal of history? Get out, you! We are Chinese! Even if we die, we are Chinese souls."

Subsequent investigations by reporters revealed that computer viruses targeting Japanese language operating systems for venting or even attacking were not unprecedented. In fact, more than three years ago, a variant virus called W32.Welchia.B, known as the "Shockwave Killer," had spread online. This virus would only attack Japanese systems and would not maliciously attack Chinese or English systems, and was then referred to as a "patriotic" virus or "anti-Japanese" virus.

The author of the virus is identified as an "angry youth"

This time, the Win32/KillDPT virus, which includes "Taiwan independence elements such as Ah Bia" within its attack range, has been identified by some netizens as an "upgraded version" of W32.Welchia.B. Some netizens have questioned the behavior of the virus author and thus positioned the author as an "angry youth".

Regarding this, Engineer Yang from the Beijing Jiangmin Company Antivirus Center expressed that judging from the intent of the author, it is likely to vent dissatisfaction. According to the current monitoring situation at the center, the virus has not yet caused any adverse effects on Chinese systems. However, he also reminded computer users to be vigilant, "with slight modifications, it may cause adverse effects on any language operating system."

Past and Present

On February 13, 2004, Rising Global Anti-Virus Monitoring Network was the first in China to intercept a variant of the "Shockwave Killer" virus, W32.Welchia.B. This virus only attacked Japanese systems and displayed landmark dates such as the September 18 Incident, the July 7 Incident, the Nanjing Massacre, the Pearl Harbor event, and the Japanese surrender commemoration day, while not only refraining from malicious attacks on Chinese and English systems but also helping these users download Microsoft patches to fix system vulnerabilities, and was then referred to as a "patriotic" virus or "anti-Japanese" virus.

On March 22, 2004, Rising Global Anti-Virus Monitoring Network was the first to intercept the "Mo Defense II" (Win32.MGF.Hoker.10) virus. This virus monitors the system time, and when the date is the fourth week of May, it displays a dialog box titled "Chinese Red Hacker". The content of the dialog box is: Diaoyu Island is Chinese territory, pirates and dogs are not allowed to enter! Unconstitutional deployment of troops abroad, denial of historical facts, beautification of wars of aggression, worship of war criminals enshrined in the "Yasukuni Shrine", Japan is shameless! I advise you not to encounter Chinese red hackers, otherwise, we will beat you until you crawl on the ground!

If the user uses a Japanese system, then when the system date is August 15 (Japanese surrender day), the virus will release a "win.com" file in the system directory and display on the screen "You can not continue, because your country - Japan killed 40,000,000 peoples since 1894-1945!!!!!" ("Your machine is dead, because your country - Japan killed 40 million people between 1894 and 1945!") and then force the system to crash.
