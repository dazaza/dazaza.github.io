Synopsis:

The movie begins with Connie (Maria Canals Barrera), Steve (Ed Jaunz), and Mitchie Torres (Demi Lovato) - an ambitious young musician hoping to become a singer (Who Will I Be) having breakfast at home. Mitchie wishes to attend the Rock Camp but is rejected by her mother due to the high registration fee. However, Mitchie's mother gets an opportunity for her to work in the camp's dining services, allowing Mitchie to participate in the activities of the Rock Camp at a discount - on the condition that Mitchie helps her mother in the kitchen.

Soon after arriving at the camp, Mitchie meets Caitlyn Gellar (Alyson Stoner), Lola Scott (Aaryn Doyle), Barron James (Jordan Francis), and Sander Loyer (Roshon Fegan). She also encounters Tess Tyler (Migna Jett Martin), the queen of the Rock Camp - the daughter of pop star T.J. Tyler (Jennifer Ricci), along with Tess's two "best friends" - Peggy Warburton (Jasmine Richards) and Ella Shang (Anna Maria Perez de Tagle).

The spoiled rock star Shane Gray (Joe Jonas) arrives at the camp with his bandmates Jason (Kevin Jonas) and Nate (Nick Jonas). Due to his bad attitude and reputation, he is forced off the bus upon arrival and assigned as the camp's dance instructor for a month. As the bus is about to leave, Nate informs Shane that he needs to duet with the winner of the camp's closing ceremony concert. The closing ceremony is a concert where all campers perform and compete to be crowned the champion. When Shane arrives at the camp, he is mobbed by fans and hides in the bushes near the cafeteria. Mitchie, finding the cafeteria empty, begins playing and singing her original song (This Is Me - Piano Version) on the piano, which Shane overhears while hiding in the bushes. The content of the song inspires and motivates Shane. Just as Shane decides to meet her, Mitchie leaves the cafeteria.

During the opening concert, Mitchie is impressed by Lola's performance (What It Takes) and accidentally meets Tess. In her desire to fit in, Mitchie lies to Tess, claiming her mother is the host of the Chinese version of a popular music show. Tess, Peggy, and Ella are amazed and invite Mitchie to move in with them. The next morning, Mitchie sneaks out to help her mother in the kitchen, where she encounters Shane. To avoid being recognized, Mitchie covers her face with flour. Shane complains about the breakfast, upsetting Mitchie. In the afternoon, Shane serves as Mitchie's dance instructor and teaches them Hip-pop (Start the Party).

Shane later starts searching for the female voice he heard but fails to find her. Caitlyn mentions Shane's search to Mitchie, but Mitchie believes Shane couldn't have heard her sing and concludes it isn't her. Tess accidentally discovers her connection with Shane and learns Mitchie is the chef's daughter. After Connect 3 performs their new song (Play My Music), Tess exposes this secret to everyone, leaving Shane heartbroken.

Later, Tess overhears Shane talking with Brown and learns that the female voice Shane seeks is Mitchie. Determined to prevent Mitchie from participating in the closing ceremony, Tess uses a bracelet given to her by her mother, places it in the kitchen, and falsely accuses Caitlyn and Mitchie of stealing it. When Brown finds Tess's bracelet in the kitchen, he bans Caitlyn and Mitchie from all subsequent camp activities, including the closing ceremony.

Just before the closing ceremony begins, Tess, Peggy, and Ella are doing their final rehearsal. When things go awry, Tess blames them, prompting Peggy to quit the group, followed by Ella. During the closing ceremony, Ella joins Barron and Sander's performance (Hasta La Vista). Tess sees her mother in the audience and gains confidence during her performance; however, when Tess notices her mother more interested in a phone call than her performance, she becomes distracted and almost falls off the stage. Tess then retreats backstage to cry.

The last-minute act is performed by Margaret Dupree (Peggy). After Peggy finishes her song (Here I Am) and returns backstage, Tess apologizes to her and Ella and compliments Peggy's performance. As Shane, Nate, and Jason judge the performances, Mitchie sings (This Is Me) on stage. Shane realizes the voice he has been searching for belongs to Mitchie and briefly duets with her (Gotta Find You). Peggy wins the closing ceremony championship, and Tess admits to lying about the bracelet incident and apologizes to Mitchie and Caitlyn. Finally, all cast members perform We Rock together.

◎Behind-the-Scenes Trivia

This film was Disney's major push in 2008, premiering on Disney Channel to great success, attracting approximately nine million viewers across the U.S., second only to High School Musical 2. After its broadcast, it aired on ABC Saturday night’s "Disney's Magical World," making it the second Disney Channel Original Movie to air in this time slot.

The first episode achieved an impressive viewership of 146 million people across 160 countries and was translated into 30 languages for global broadcasts, nearing the record set by High School Musical's first episode with over 225 million viewers.

Last weekend, [Camp Rock] attracted millions of young viewers, with eight hundred ninety million tuning in on Disney Channel on Friday night and three hundred sixty million on ABC on Saturday night, easily becoming the highest-rated entertainment program of the year.

Disney had high hopes for [Camp Rock], starring the popular teenage idol group Jonas Brothers and young singer Demi Lovato.

Disney Channel's latest original TV movie, [Camp Rock], performed quite well in terms of viewership. Similar to the channel's very popular series [High School Musical], this movie not only made new teen stars famous but also contributed significantly to Disney's original movie business.
