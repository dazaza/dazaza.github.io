The protagonist Duohè (played by Sun Li), along with her mother and younger sister, joined a group of over 3,000 fleeing refugees. Along the way, hunger and disease claimed many lives, reducing the group from over 3,000 to just a hundred within half a month. As winter approached, some women, including Duohè, were captured by county security forces, who auctioned them off publicly at a price of one dime per kilogram. When they reached the wilderness, they discovered movement in the sack; the person inside was still alive. After some debate, Master Zhang agreed with his wife's suggestion to rescue the girl from the ditch, unable to let a living person freeze or starve to death.
