Holding your hand, growing old with you.

Dear J, we have known each other for more than 7 years. I have always wanted to write our story into a thick book. However, the ending will be on the day we hold hands and return to the soil. Now we have endured too much. I want to start writing about our past from today onwards, forever remembering it in my heart. Today's day has not come easily.

7 years ago, on that Halloween, our story quietly began to sprout with the song "Unfinished Ghost Love." That year, I was 15, and you were 17. In those youthful and reckless days, I would drag your clothes and walk through that dimly lit staircase. Love always arrives hastily when there is no time to think.

At that time, I was very capricious, rebellious, and full of fantasies. We would always hang out in internet cafes, indulging in the world of online games without any burden, only thinking that being happy was enough. That shallow liking back then laid the groundwork for today's deep love.

Our first breakup was actually a tragedy I imagined.

Later, you went far away, but eventually, we still came together. Remembering 5 years ago, the silly me and the silly you, during your rest times, you would always call me and tell me about everything happening around you. And I would excitedly run through streets and alleys when I learned there were letters for me. Our love was very pure, all the way until you returned to the neighboring city. I never dared to take the initiative to hold your hand, but when crossing the road, you would hold mine. That was my happiest moment back then because of you, I had someone to rely on.

We grew up, didn't we? We developed an intimate relationship. But at that time, we never thought about the consequences, nor did we know how to cherish ourselves. We had our first child. The reason was simple: you weren't free, and I was still young. In the end, the baby's life stopped on the cold operating table. I thought I could be very strong, but as I walked out of the hospital gate and a friend asked, "Does it hurt?" tears still fell from my eyes. I just shook my head silently. How can physical pain compare to heartache? Then came the second accidental pregnancy, which wasn't really an accident. Medication isn't 100% effective. This time, I faced the baby's second departure from my body alone.

Later, I met him. You asked me what makes him better than you. No need to compare. In my heart, no one can replace your position. Many times, we met online, and you asked me if I was doing well. How I wish I could tell you, "I'm not doing well because you're not by my side." But I couldn't. I only wanted you to be happy and live a blissful life after this. On the day of our breakup, after you walked out of my house, the foolish me broke down in tears.

Our second breakup was due to my inferiority complex. Perhaps even now, you wouldn't know the real reason. Because of an unintentional remark of yours, you said your family opposed us being together, they didn't like me. I knew that I used to be the bad kid in the eyes of adults. I also feared I might never have the right to have another baby. Looking back now, it was because I didn't love you firmly enough back then, and I didn't trust your ability to protect me.

5 years have passed. You've returned. In the year since you came back to our familiar city, I couldn't help but think about you constantly. Every time I got drunk, I thought about you intensely. Every time I overflowed with thoughts of you, I sent you messages or called you. Actually, I was conflicted. I didn't want to disturb your life, I just wanted to hear your voice, whether you still remembered someone like me.

Until that cursed year 2009, the year my grandmother, grandfather, and grandmother left me, our story began anew in autumn.

After being apart for three years, we met for the first time. There was no dramatic scene. You came to my place under the pretext of drinking coffee. I knew you wanted to see me. But we were like the most familiar strangers, with not much to say. How I wanted to lean on your chest and tell each other how much we missed each other. But there were too many constraints, preventing us from doing so. Watching your sleeping face, my heart ached so much. I spent the night sleepless in the adjacent room.

In that bustling city where I worked, that night I drank a lot to muster the courage to go with you. Unfortunately, I drank too much and lost all memory afterward. Your embrace still carried the familiar warmth, and after leaving, there was so much reluctance. But reality always has to be faced. Until I discovered you also had another her.

Perhaps it takes experiencing much sorrow to learn strength and appreciation. It takes losing a lot to learn to fight for what matters. This time, before you let go of my hand, I absolutely won't give up. I don't want to ask about your past with her because when you feel like talking, you'll naturally tell me. You two broke up, and you were very determined to be with me. But that girl also loved you deeply. It's not that I don't trust you.

It's just that when people seriously consider love, they become very petty. Because I love you, I care about you, and I endure watching her call you, visit your home. But please allow me to vent a little when I'm sad. Hearing her cry while calling me, I soften a bit. But love is selfish, and I won't let her repeated lies make me doubt you. Trust between couples is crucial, and I know that.

I know your family still doesn't like me. That night, it took a lot of courage for you to bring me home, didn't it? For me, this is already enough. After all, it's not easy for us to be together again. So please rest assured; this time I won't let go of your hand. I will accompany you through every season ahead.

Dear J, I know you've always been afraid I would leave you again. But this time, please believe me, alright? I'm ready to face all the storms ahead. Whether it's heavy rain or the end of the world, I will firmly hold your hand until our story perfectly comes to an end. If our lives were a game of Monopoly, no matter how many chances we get to start over, the result would always be the same. Because you are my destiny!

In the place where we grew up, in that bustling city, in that vast city, we must tightly interlock our fingers and never separate.

March 7, 2010

To commemorate our past, I write this for J.

Holding your hand, growing old with you.
