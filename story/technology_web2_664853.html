Classification of Content Time Limitations

Jurisdiction Dispute Accident: In case of jurisdiction disputes, the common superior public security organ should make a decision within 24 hours and notify all parties involved.

Post-accident Evidence Submission: If the parties did not report the accident at the scene and later request the traffic management department to handle it, the evidence must be submitted within 10 days from the date of the request.

Inspection and Appraisal: Inspection and appraisal of the physiological, mental, corpse, speed, etc., of the parties should be assigned to professional appraisal departments within 5 days after the on-site investigation ends.

The inspection and appraisal should be completed within 20 days. If an extension is needed, it can be extended by 10 days with the approval of the municipal public security traffic department. For cases exceeding the limit, provincial public security traffic departments' approval is required.

After receiving the inspection and appraisal results, the public security traffic department shall deliver copies of the conclusions to the parties within 2 days.

If the parties have objections to the inspection and appraisal conclusions, they can entrust other qualified institutions for re-inspection and appraisal within 3 days after receiving the conclusions, and inform the public security traffic department.

Corpse Handling: After the inspection and appraisal ends, the families of the deceased should be notified to handle funeral arrangements within 10 days.

For unidentified corpses that remain unclaimed after 10 days, the county-level or above public security traffic department's responsible person may approve the handling.

Accident Liability Determination Document: It should be issued within 10 days from the date of on-site inspection.

In cases of hit-and-run accidents, it should be issued within 10 days from the date of apprehending the escapee or vehicle.

If the escapee or vehicle cannot be apprehended, it should be issued within 10 days from the date of the party's application.

For cases requiring inspection and appraisal, it should be issued within 5 days from the date of confirmation of the inspection and appraisal results.

Accident Mediation: If both parties in the accident agree to apply for mediation by the public security traffic department, they should submit a written application within 10 days from the date of issuance of the accident liability determination document.

The public security traffic department's damage compensation mediation period is 10 days.

Death starts from the completion of funeral arrangements, injury from the end of treatment, disability from the date of disability determination, property loss from the date of loss determination - all within 10 days.

Accident Litigation: Personal injury litigation starts from the date of knowing or should know, with a time limit of 1 year.

Civil litigation generally has a statute of limitations of 2 years.

The People's Court shall send the copy of the complaint to the defendant within five days from the date of filing, and the defendant shall submit a defense statement within fifteen days from the date of receipt.

Precautions Regarding Accident Handling Time:

1. An important aspect in handling traffic accident cases is the situation of liability assignment, which relates to future compensation amounts and whether the offender bears criminal responsibility. Nowadays, the traffic accident determination document is only a piece of evidence under the law. Parties cannot file administrative reconsideration, although they can raise objections to this evidence in litigation. However, if you cannot provide sufficient evidence to prove your objection, according to judicial practice, the court generally relies on the accident determination document. Parties rarely actively collect on-site evidence after an accident but passively wait for the traffic police to assign responsibility. Therefore, victims are especially reminded: after an accident occurs, one must confirm whether the traffic police's on-site record and investigation records are consistent with reality, confirm the insurance status of the offending driver and vehicle. During the liability determination process, one must strive to negotiate liability handling with the offending party so that the offending party assumes full responsibility. In our handled cases, some parties told us that the traffic police said the main responsibility was on the other side, but when the traffic accident liability determination document came out, it turned out to be equal responsibility. Even more, one party reported that when negotiating mediation with the offending party, they found out that the offending driver had been replaced, and it wasn't the original offending driver. Since the party did not confirm the identity of the offending driver, the traffic police denied it, and there was no evidence proving that the driver was impersonating. Therefore, after a traffic accident occurs, victims must confirm the aforementioned points to prevent some offenders from attempting to evade responsibility through illegal means.

2. Another important aspect is the disability grade appraisal. One should pay attention to preserving detailed hospitalization records and ensure that disease diagnoses are thoroughly documented without omissions. For example, for a tibial plateau fracture at the ankle joint, it should be clearly stated rather than simply as a tibia/fibula fracture.

3. When discharged, the discharge certificate should specify the number of caregivers during hospitalization, whether further care is needed after discharge, the rest period after discharge, the amount of subsequent treatment fees, and the need for nutritional supplementation after discharge.
