Consumers call for increasing subsidies and simplifying procedures in the trade-in program

by siyleo6g on 2009-11-19 23:50:19

Among the consumers who have participated in the activities, 79% of them are due to the need for updating household appliances (57%) or living necessities (22%), which belong to rigid demand. The "trade-in" activity has stimulated the purchasing desire of these consumers. However, for the consumers who participated in the activity due to the factor of "reasonable price" (12%), the influence is not significant, reflecting that the discount of the activity has not met the expectations of consumers.

From a geographical perspective, Guangzhou consumers are relatively familiar with the trade-in policy. However, Foshan and Dongguan consumers reported that they rarely learned about the discount and policy transmission of the "trade-in" activity in their regions.

Data statistics show that among the consumers who did not participate in the "trade-in" activity, 43% mainly because of "less subsidy for buying new appliances", while 28% of consumers believe that "the trade-in process is cumbersome" and thus do not participate in the activity; another 29% of consumers are because "there are no old appliances at home". It seems that improving and simplifying the "trade-in process" and appropriately increasing the appliance subsidy is a good way to attract consumers to participate in the "trade-in" activity.

As high as 84% of consumers recognize the "trade-in" activity of household appliances and support the implementation of this people-benefiting policy. Unexpectedly, only 26% of consumers have participated in the activity. 69% of people generally understand the "trade-in" policy for household appliances; 43% think the subsidy ratio for buying new appliances is too small; 45% hope that the government department strengthens supervision to prevent companies from raising prices and prevent the resale of recycled appliances; 38% suggest simplifying the sales and recycling process... The "trade-in questionnaire survey" activity of our newspaper ended on November 13, and the data statistics showed the above information.

The data shows that as high as 84% of consumers recognize the "trade-in" activity and support the implementation of this people-benefiting policy. About 16% of consumers have reservations about the policy of recycling old appliances. These consumers are mainly concerned whether the country can effectively monitor the handling of recycled appliances to prevent secondary sales.

Survey data shows that 49% of consumers learn about the "trade-in" policy and activities through newspapers, proving the importance and effect of newspaper dissemination.

Since the formal release of the survey questionnaire by our newspaper on November 4, within just ten days, the number of participants in the voting has exceeded one thousand. The voting readers come from all over Guangdong, including key cities in the Pearl River Delta such as Guangzhou, Dongguan, Foshan, and Shenzhen.

Among the consumers preparing to participate in the activity, 49% of consumers believe that "environmental protection and energy saving" appliances are the first choice, reflecting the enhancement of residents' environmental awareness; only 16% of consumers require "fashionable and beautiful", which also reflects that fewer consumers consider consumption from the perspective of enjoying life.

Interestingly, this survey also reflected the other side of the matter - still 74% of consumers have not participated in the "trade-in". Although the activity is widely popular, the number of participants is not many. Why?

In the survey of "how much do you know about the 'trade-in' policy for household appliances", 69% of the consumers, reaching 816 people, have a superficial understanding of the "trade-in" policy; only 21% believe they understand it well.

Statistics found that in the first six days, our newspaper had already received more than 80% of reader votes, and the enthusiasm of readers even exceeded expectations. The coverage area of the questionnaire also well represented the key areas of the "trade-in" for household appliances in Guangdong, making it very representative. Many enthusiastic readers wrote down their suggestions and opinions on the "trade-in" in detail via email or fax.
