Yonsei University has a long history. Can you introduce its history to us?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Yonsei University is historically the strongest university in Korea, established in 1885. It began internationalization from the very start, making it one of the earliest universities in Korea to embrace internationalization. Yonsei University (Yonsei University) is located in Seoul, South Korea, with a peaceful and beautiful campus, only 20 minutes away from Seoul's business center. Founded in 1885, as the oldest university in Korea, Yonsei University celebrated its 100th anniversary in 1985. It is not only a top-tier prestigious university in Korea but also ranks among the world's top 100 prestigious universities. Yonsei University has grown to become a comprehensive university with 17 colleges, 83 departments, and 11 specialized diploma education institutions. All 83 departments offer bachelor's degree programs, 53 offer master's degree programs, and 49 can confer doctoral degrees. Yonsei University currently has more than 40,000 students.

I've heard that Yonsei University's medical program is quite famous. Is that true?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Due to historical reasons, the medical school holds a significant position within the university. This system includes the medical school, dental school, nursing school, and medical research center. Additionally, Yonsei University has a College of Liberal Arts, College of Engineering, and other 16 colleges, as well as graduate schools for education, economics, and others.

As the most historic modern medical school in Korea, Yonsei Medical School is often the first choice for Korean students during college entrance exams. The medical school boasts excellent faculty, with many renowned professors being cultivated within the institution. A steady stream of talents in clinical, research, and educational fields emerge from their classrooms.

Associate Professor Lee Han-mo, Yonsei University Class of 1992 Medical Doctor: Good teaching methods are essential for students to learn effectively. Since large classes are common, with around 170-180 students per class, simply reading from textbooks isn't effective. Clinical cases, both common and abnormal, must be photographed and made into slideshows so that students can directly see them during lectures, making it easier for them to remember.

The Medical Research Center at Yonsei University is a pioneer in the field of Korean medicine, including the Cardiovascular Research Center, Cancer Center, Severance Hospital, etc. Due to its cutting-edge technology and teaching methods, it leads medical education in Korea. Senior students can choose courses based on principles of diversification, specialization, and personalization, allowing each individual to maximize their potential and broaden their horizons.

Besides teaching, the Medical Research Center tightly integrates clinical and basic research, ensuring continuous updates and maintaining leadership in the fields of medical knowledge and technology.

Fourth-year student at Yonsei University Medical School, Kang Hyun-seok: Yonsei University Medical School is the best in Korea, requiring extremely high college entrance exam scores for admission. For instance, if the maximum score is 400 points, one should score above 395 to get in.

Fourth-year student at Yonsei University Medical School, Kim Hyun-jung: Professors care deeply about students and frequently discuss future graduation directions with them. Due to their world-class level, we can also acquire world-class knowledge.

Chinese international student, Pathology Ph.D. candidate Li Long-shan: Our laboratory mainly focuses on tumor detection gene research. My professor is quite renowned at Yonsei University, he is very strict and maintains a serious attitude towards scientific research. I've learned a great deal from him.

Yonsei University Class of 1988 Medical Doctor, Medical Pathology Professor Kim Ho-geun: In the past, most medical school professors were involved in clinical research, but now half focus on fundamental discipline research, indicating Yonsei University's emphasis on foundational studies. Many foreign scholars and graduate students come to our medical school for further studies, with over twenty Chinese teachers and doctors coming here annually for training or research. Furthermore, there are exchanges between graduate students.

Besides the medical field, Yonsei University has made remarkable achievements and contributions in promoting the development, research, and teaching of language and literature. Could you elaborate on this?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Hangul represents the soul of the Korean nation and the power of cultural creation. Under the ideals of truth and freedom, Yonsei University has made significant contributions to upholding patriotism and academic spirit through the use of language and script amidst difficult historical times.

As one of the oldest colleges, Yonsei University’s College of Liberal Arts has consistently been an excellent base for humanities education in Korean history. The European Languages Department and Social Sciences Department offer majors in Korean Language and Literature, Chinese Language and Literature, English Language and Literature, and ten others. Perhaps due to the deep historical ties between Chinese and Korean characters, the Chinese Language and Literature major is popular among students.

I've heard that Yonsei University was the first school in Korean history to conduct student exchanges. Is that correct?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Yonsei University was the first school in Korean history to conduct student exchanges. To date, it has signed student exchange agreements with over 400 universities worldwide and continues to expand its external exchanges, providing opportunities for foreign students to study Korean history, culture, and language. Since its establishment in 1959, nearly 40,000 international students from 105 countries have studied and exchanged ideas at the Korean Language Institute.

How is the employment situation at Yonsei University?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: During the educational process, Yonsei University continuously implements reforms in the education system. Starting from 1996, the school began implementing a departmental system aimed at broadening students' academic horizons and increasing future employment opportunities.

Currently, Yonsei University's implemented departmental system divides its 16 colleges into four major series: humanities, social sciences, medicine, and engineering. Students enter according to these series without specifying a major initially. They study general foundational courses for a year. After a year of university life, students determine their future paths and begin specialized course studies from the second year, based on their preferences and academic performance, upon approval by the school.

This is considered pioneering in Korean universities.

What do students think about this "first, then choose" situation?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Based on feedback from students I've helped apply successfully, most are quite satisfied with this departmental system.

Psychology student, Li classmate: I thought entering university would allow me to relax and enjoy the atmosphere of Korea. Later, I learned about the departmental system, so I couldn't slack off and had to study hard every day. Through studying, I found a suitable major in psychology, gradually improving. Influenced by what I learned, I was recruited by a good company just before graduating in my senior year. Therefore, this departmental system is excellent and practical.

Bachelor Guidance Professor Moon Hwan-dragon of Yonsei University's College of General Studies: Yonsei University was the first in Korea to implement this reform. In Western universities, this system is relatively common, but in the East, where traditional thinking is valued, this reform is not easy.

Can you specifically talk about how Yonsei University fully develops students' personalities?

Korean Study Abroad Expert: Yonsei University's College of Music offers four majors: classroom music, composition, instruments, and vocals. The college encourages students to actively participate in various performances to enrich their experiences, advocating the combination of theory and practice, as well as the integration of national and Western music. This is a place where talented individuals emerge, including Korean singer Choi Hyun-woo, who won first place 13 times in the Italian Vocal Competition and the Tchaikovsky International Vocal Competition.

Choi Hyun-su, a graduate of the Class of 1981 from Yonsei University's College of Music and Director of the Vocal Department at the Korea National University of Arts: I entered Yonsei University's College of Music in 1977. The scenes of practicing voice in the open-air theater and forest on campus are still vivid in my memory. My mentor was an 82-year-old professor who still frequently performs on stage. From him, I not only learned music but also life lessons.

These performance posters guide people to the university's open-air grand theater.

An upcoming large concert will be held here. This theater can accommodate 8,000 people, and each seat in the stands is engraved with the name of a graduate who contributed to the construction of the theater.

Inter-university sports events have always been popular among students. Yonsei University has year-round training teams for basketball, soccer, and baseball, winning championships multiple times in Korean university leagues. The performance of this basketball cheerleading team has further enhanced Yonsei University's fame. One of my students is a basketball enthusiast. He initially thought he would feel lonely in a foreign country without companions, but he soon joined the basketball team, blending in with classmates, thus finding friends and support.
