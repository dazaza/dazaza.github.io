Knowledge of online stores

Best Answer:

The process of opening an online store begins not on the internet, but in your mind. You need to decide what kind of store you want to open. In this aspect, opening an online store is no different from a traditional one - finding a good market and having competitive products are the foundation for success. The second step is choosing a platform or website to open your store. You need to select a website that provides a personal store platform and register as a user. This step is very important. Most websites require real name registration with valid ID documents. When selecting a website, factors such as popularity, whether it charges fees, and the nature of those fees are all very important indicators. Nowadays, many platforms offer free store-opening services, which can save you quite a bit of money.

The third step is applying to open a store on the website. You need to carefully fill out the classification of the products your store will provide. For example, if you sell fashion watches, they should be categorized under "Jewelry, Watches, Glasses" as "Watches," so your target customers can find you accurately. Then, you need to come up with a catchy store name. Internet users are more likely to click on stores whose names attract them. Some online stores display personal information, which should be filled out truthfully to build trust.

The fourth step is sourcing goods. You can source from familiar channels and platforms, controlling costs and buying at low prices being key.

The fifth step is logging products. You need to fill out each product's name, origin, location, nature, appearance, quantity, trading method, and trading deadline on the website, preferably accompanied by pictures of the product. The name should be as comprehensive as possible, highlighting its advantages, because when others search for similar products, only the name will appear in the list. To increase appeal, the quality of the picture should be as high as possible, and the description should be as detailed as possible. If mailing is required, it's best to state who will cover the postage. An extremely important thing during login is setting the price. Typically, websites provide options like starting price, reserve price, and fixed price set by the seller.

For example, if a seller wants to sell a piece of clothing bought for $100 and plans to sell it for $150, a traditional shop owner would simply start at $150 and gradually lower the price if it doesn't sell. However, online bidding is different. The seller first sets a starting price, and buyers bid upwards from there. A lower starting price tends to attract more interest; some sellers even set a starting bid at $1 to draw attention. However, a too-low starting price risks ending up with a final sale price that’s also too low, so it's best to set a reserve price simultaneously, such as $105, to ensure the item isn't sold below cost. Another drawback of a low starting price is it may imply you're willing to sell at a very low price, causing bids to linger at a low level. If the seller feels waiting for the auction to finish takes too long, they can set a fixed price. Once a buyer is willing to pay this price, the deal is done immediately. The downside is that even if multiple buyers are interested, the price cannot be driven higher. Sellers should adjust these settings based on their specific circumstances.

The sixth step is marketing promotion. To increase the popularity of your store in the early stages, appropriate marketing and promotion should be conducted, but limiting it to just online isn't enough. Promotion through various online and offline channels is necessary. For instance, purchasing ad space on high-traffic pages, making product names bold in category lists, and adding images to attract attention. You can also use free advertising methods, like exchanging links with other stores and websites.

The seventh step is mid-sale service. When customers decide whether to buy, they often need much more information than you've provided. They might ask questions online anytime, and you should respond promptly and patiently. Note that many websites prohibit any personal contact information, such as email or phone numbers, to prevent private transactions that evade transaction fees. Violations may result in penalties.

The eighth step is the transaction. After a successful deal, the website will notify both parties' contact details, and transactions can be completed either face-to-face or via remittance and mail. Transactions should proceed quickly to avoid doubts about your creditworthiness. Whether to offer additional after-sales services depends on prior agreements between both parties.

The ninth step is evaluation or complaint. Credit is a crucial factor in online transactions. To help build a trustworthy environment, if satisfied, it's best to give positive feedback to the other party and obtain good reviews through excellent service. If the transaction fails, a negative review or complaint to the website should be given to minimize losses and warn others. If complained against, handle it promptly to avoid tarnishing your own credit.

The tenth step is after-sales service. There's no need to elaborate much here - perfect and thoughtful after-sales service is a very important factor in keeping business thriving. Maintaining contact with different customers and doing customer management work well is crucial.

Currently, there are hundreds of large shopping websites in China offering online store-opening services, but only a few have significant influence. Here are introductions to some major related websites:

eBay (www.eachnet.com): Founded in Shanghai on August 18, 1999, by Yi Bo Shao and Haiyin Tan, it is the largest Chinese online trading platform globally, providing C2C (individual-to-individual) and B2C (business-to-individual) network platform construction and services. In March 2002, eBay invested $30 million in EachNet and formed a strategic partnership. In June 2003, eBay added another $150 million investment. So far, EachNet has attracted nearly $220 million in foreign investment, becoming the company that attracts the most foreign capital among all returnee entrepreneurs in the country.

EachNet (www.ebay.com.cn) is one of the earliest shopping websites in China offering online store-opening services. Registration for an online store is charged, requiring payment for product reserve price setting fees, item listing fees, transaction service fees, and advertising value-added service fees.

Taobao (www.taobao.com): It is the leading domestic C2C (individual-to-individual) online trading platform, founded on May 10, 2003, with an investment of $450 million from Alibaba, the world's best B2B (business-to-business) e-commerce company, aiming to become the world's largest individual trading website.

Taobao currently offers free registration, free certification, and free store-opening services.

Yipai (www.1pai.com.cn): Established in early 2004 by a joint venture between Sina and Yahoo called Beijing Xinyazhixian Information Technology Co., Ltd., Yipai is a new, fully functional online buying and selling community serving small and medium enterprises and individuals in China.

Yipai currently offers free online store-opening services.

8848 (www.8848.com): Founded on May 18, 1999, 8848 was once the flagship of China's e-commerce enterprises. In September 2003, it began preparing a new business development plan, shifting its focus to establishing China's first professional shopping search engine - the 8848 Shopping Engine, the 8848 Online Store for SMEs, and providing e-commerce solutions for corporate users to expand their businesses. 8848.com was officially launched on January 1, 2004.

8848 currently offers free online store-opening services.
