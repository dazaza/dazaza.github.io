The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said that the report about China selling weapons to Syria was totally baseless http://www.sina.com.cn 2012-02-11 14:04 China News Service Weibo According to the official website of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, on February 10, 2012, Liu Weimin, spokesperson for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China presided over a regular press conference and answered reporters' questions on issues such as the protection of the safety of Chinese citizens overseas, the Iranian nuclear issue, etc., and responded to reports about China selling weapons to Syria, saying that these accusations were totally baseless. The following is the transcript of the press conference published on the official website of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs: Q: Yesterday, 29 Chinese citizens who were held hostage in Sudan have returned home safely. What are the considerations of the Foreign Ministry in strengthening the protection of the safety of Chinese citizens overseas? A: The Chinese government has always attached great importance to protecting the safety of Chinese citizens overseas. In recent years, it has continuously increased investment and focused on mechanism building and capacity building, achieving remarkable results. Under this circumstance, the "four-in-one" mechanism consisting of central authorities, local governments, enterprises and embassies and consulates abroad is continuously improving, and the emergency response capability for various major and special emergencies overseas is continuously strengthened. Preventive consular protection work methods such as risk warning, publicity and education, and safety training are constantly enriched, and the level of safety protection work for Chinese citizens and institutions overseas has significantly improved. China will continue to follow the concept of "people-oriented, governing for the people", and make every effort to do a good job in protecting the safety of Chinese citizens overseas. In the past five years, under the leadership of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, relevant departments, localities, and enterprises have been instructed to properly handle more than 120,000 cases involving our consular protection caused by political turmoil, kidnapping, natural disasters, accidental accidents, labor disputes, etc., and carried out repatriation missions more than 10 times, effectively safeguarding the safety and legitimate rights and interests of hundreds of thousands of Chinese citizens and enterprises. The hostage-taking incident involving Chinese citizens in Sudan was an occasional event, but it also reflected the increasingly severe overseas security situation faced by Chinese citizens and enterprises going global. In this regard, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs once again reminds Chinese citizens and enterprises that, whether they go abroad for business, travel, study, work, or life, they must take effective measures to enhance their self-protection capabilities and guard against all kinds of security risks. Q: First, there are reports that China directly or through Iran sells weapons to Syria. What is your comment on this? Second, according to reports, many Myanmar civilians have crossed the China-Myanmar border into Yunnan to escape the war. Please introduce the relevant situation and what relief measures has China taken? A: Regarding the first question, these accusations are totally baseless, and we cannot accept them. The Chinese government has always adopted a cautious and responsible attitude towards the export of military products. Based on the international obligations undertaken by China and domestic laws and regulations, all military product exports are strictly managed, and a complete set of strict management systems has been formed. Regarding the second question, some media reported that a large number of Myanmar "refugees" had entered the Chinese side of the China-Myanmar border. After verification, these reports do not match the facts. Recently, due to sporadic clashes between the Myanmar government army and individual ethnic minority local armed forces, some Myanmar border residents temporarily entered China for safety reasons and returned to Myanmar immediately after the situation calmed down. These people are not refugees, and the number is far less than what was reported by the outside world. China and Myanmar are friendly neighbors. When handling this matter, China has always adhered to humanitarian spirit and provided necessary living convenience to Myanmar border residents entering China. The problem of Myanmar's ethnic minority local armed organizations has a long history and is Myanmar's internal affair. China has always urged relevant parties in Myanmar to resolve relevant issues through peaceful negotiations and consultations, so as to effectively maintain peace and tranquility in the China-Myanmar border area. Q: Recently, there have been some new developments in the Iranian nuclear issue. Please introduce China's views on promoting dialogue and negotiation. A: China has always maintained that dialogue and cooperation are the only correct way to promote the resolution of the Iranian nuclear issue. The urgent task now is for the six countries and Iran to restart the dialogue process, and for the International Atomic Energy Agency and Iran to strengthen cooperation. China has always been committed to promoting peace talks. At the invitation of Bagheri, Deputy Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council of Iran, Assistant Minister of Foreign Affairs Ma Zhaoxu will visit Iran from February 12 to 13 to further exchange opinions with the Iranian side on the Iranian nuclear issue. We are willing to work together with relevant parties to strive for the resumption of talks between the six countries and Iran as soon as possible.
