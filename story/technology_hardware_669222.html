The Most Favored Pair of Jordan Footwear for Basketball

Webster’s Dictionary defines the term "popular" as, "commonly liked or accepted." Jordan shoe enthusiasts would describe the entire Jordan line in the same way. In fact, every single basic version of the Jordans signature sneaker line could be described as popular. However, when it comes to identifying the most popular Jordan shoe ever made, there is a clear standout winner. The Jordan 11 stands head and shoulders above all the other shoes that make up one of the most popular types of basketball shoes in the history of sneakers.

The legendary Jordan 11 was first released during the 1995-96 NBA regular season. This shoe became the most famous and sought-after model in the Air Jordan line. Michael Jordan wore the Jordan 11 during this time, the year he came out of retirement for the first time. After trying his hand at professional baseball, Michael returned to the NBA and quickly re-established himself as the dominant presence he had been before retiring. He led the Bulls to their fourth NBA Finals Championship, was named the Most Valuable Player for the NBA regular season, All-Star game, and NBA Finals, and averaged 30.4 points per game—all while wearing the iconic Jordan 11.

Perhaps what makes the Jordan 11 so popular is its extraordinary silhouette, which was established during the design process. The man responsible for this superior silhouette was the renowned Nike shoe designer Tinker Hatfield. This shoe not only marked the return of Michael but also the return of Hatfield. Hatfield had designed the Air Jordan series starting with the Jordan 3. For the Jordan 11, Hatfield added patent leather to the upper part of the shoe and placed a clear rubber sole on the outsole, completing what many consider the most popular Jordan shoe ever created.

On December 23, 2011, the most popular colorway of the most beloved Jordan shoe is set to make a triumphant return to the sneaker world. The Jordan 11 "Concord" is arguably the most favored version of the most epic shoe ever worn by MJ. This predominantly white shoe with black patent leather accents and a clear, translucent sole will be available for purchase, marking the third consecutive year that a Jordan 11 Retro will launch during the holiday season.

If there is still any doubt about which pair of Jordan shoes is the most popular for basketball, consider this: in 2000-2001, when the Jordan 11s were retroed in fifteen different colorways, they sold out faster than any other Jordan shoe. The upcoming Jordan 11 "Concord" is expected to sell out within hours. This shoe has been loved and approved by sneakerheads worldwide—in other words, it's undeniably popular.
