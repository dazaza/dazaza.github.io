Shanghai is expected to realize self-selection of community broadband, with third-party operation and final access.

by diglog on 2011-09-07 21:57:51

Shanghai is attempting to have the "last mile" network of newly built communities managed by an independent third party, with access services open to operators. People want fast and cheap internet access, but currently cannot freely choose their network operator because the "last mile" of the network reaching households is often "operated" when the community is initially built, leaving residents no choice upon moving in. This morning, I learned from the Shanghai Fiber-to-the-Home Construction Promotion Meeting that Shanghai is pioneering a new network household operation model nationwide. The Shanghai Building Communication Network Co., Ltd. has been established as a third-party professional institution to uniformly operate the "last mile" before the network enters households, allowing various network operators the opportunity to "compete on the same stage" within the community and be equally chosen by the public. The "last mile" in the network world refers to the switch in the operator's equipment room in the community to the computer terminal in people's homes. At the beginning of the construction of a new community, property developers will introduce a certain operator to build network supporting facilities and reach a network exclusive operation agreement, thus the "last mile" is "operated".
