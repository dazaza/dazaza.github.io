Sina Weibo: Getting Closer to Facebook, Further from Twitter

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:21:26

If we compare Weibo to television, then it currently only has two channels: one news channel and one crosstalk comedy channel. However, if a TV station only broadcasts these two programs, it cannot sustain itself in the long run. There should be more food programs and music programs added.

Vice General Manager of Sina Weibo Division, Lin Shuiyang, gave such an apt analogy for Weibo, also revealing the direction Sina Weibo is striving towards in its future product development.

On May 7th this year at the GMIC conference, Sina Weibo officially launched the Page product. Before the appearance of Page, every "person" on Sina Weibo was an information node, and all information exchange activities revolved around people. The launch of Page changed the information dissemination mechanism that Sina Weibo had been following since its inception. According to Liu Bin, the product manager of Sina Weibo, Page is a new way of information aggregation, through which more valuable information can be discovered. Page no longer transmits information from the perspective of "people," but rather aggregates it around certain places, movies, music, or other "things." When users see the Page page, they are actually seeing information from all the people interested in it. Page shifts Weibo's original focus on people to things, and information aggregation based on things is what Weibo truly wants to accomplish in the future.

Sina Weibo Lacks "Users"

According to user activity data from market research firm Global Web Index at the beginning of this year, the user activity of Sina Weibo decreased by nearly 40% from the second quarter to the fourth quarter of 2012. Compared to the peak periods, there has been a significant decline in daily posts per user, and the overall traffic of Sina Weibo is also declining. Such a situation is not hard to understand; since its launch, Sina Weibo has consistently pursued a celebrity strategy, helping it accumulate users early on and gain an advantage in competing with similar products. However, this strategy made ordinary users followers rather than participants from the very beginning. Furthermore, numerous marketing accounts have anonymously exploited high-quality content created by others to gain followers and profit, causing even those originally somewhat participatory and creative "middle-class" users to gradually withdraw.

As mentioned at the beginning of the article, users' need to browse Weibo has been compressed into reading news and jokes. However, neither of these programs allows users themselves to participate, nor can they bring actual commercial monetization possibilities to Sina Weibo. On the other hand, the intense focus on current political affairs also puts Sina Weibo's operational policy risks at a high level, necessitating a large investment of human resources for content review.

Sina Weibo's Commercialization Attempt

The enormous user base of Sina Weibo and its extremely disproportionate profitability have always subjected it to criticism from the outside world. In April last year, a "Sina Weibo Q2 2012 Price List" revealed that Sina Weibo had started full-scale commercial operations. However, this approach more closely resembled the direct sale of space by Sina.com as a traditional portal, which did not quite align with Sina Weibo's "social" identity. Two months later, Sina Weibo launched a membership service, but the privileges enjoyed by members were far from appealing.

This year, Sina Weibo successively launched advertising tools for businesses called "Fan Channel" and e-commerce guide tool "Sina Showcase." Both would forcibly insert ads into users' information streams, sparking widespread complaints. However, with improvements in the underlying algorithms behind Sina Weibo, I believe the interference caused by information stream ads should gradually decrease. Based on Facebook Exchange experience, users do not particularly resent advertisements they are interested in.

From Social Media to Social Network

At the end of April, David W. Chao, CEO of Sina, wrote in a Weibo announcing the upcoming strategic cooperation between Sina and Alibaba: "Weibo is currently a social media platform, but in the future, it can also become a social commerce platform, that's a true ecosystem." So far, key nodes such as personal homepages, musicians, writers, enterprises, media, LBS, books, applications, and music have already gone online, achieving uniformity in both product form and experience. As the core carrier for Sina Weibo's social commerce practice, Page is proceeding in an orderly manner. However, Sina Weibo, which has been wavering between social media and social network, seems to gradually reveal its direction, but it is not the former:

From the previous enterprise edition, media edition Weibo, to now the Page-ified celebrity homepage containing more content types, Sina Weibo Page increasingly resembles Facebook's pages and Renren's public homepages. Liu Bin also revealed that they are indeed considering introducing more third-party tools in the future to help celebrities and artists customize richer personal homepages, such as organizing fan events, selling concert tickets, etc. Such operations have already achieved relatively successful practices in micro-topics.

Prior value of Weibo mainly lay in the spread of information within the information flow, and through Page, the originally scattered personal information is structured and organized, enabling information aggregation based on each interest or object node. For example, it's difficult to see what someone did yesterday in the information flow, but it can be found on the interest and object nodes, which is equivalent to excavating submerged information through another method. Only on this basis can users be attracted to visit these nodes, and Weibo can achieve commercialization through merchant claims on these nodes and cooperation with Blue V accounts.

Sina Weibo aims to break the status quo where discourse power is monopolized by big Vs, hoping more grassroots users will participate in content production. There are two main significances here: one is to promote the diversification of Weibo content, so that everyone can see what their friends are doing recently, what movies they are watching, and what music they are listening to in the information flow, instead of the current screen filled with orange Vs, blue Vs, and marketing accounts constantly sending sensational news and joke segments; on the other hand, only when users post enough content and interact with other users can Weibo obtain their interests and thus accurately push ads to achieve its own commercial goals.

Social Commerce on the Road

Although Sina Weibo does not want to easily replace its painstakingly cultivated media name, the launch and evolution of Page undoubtedly prove that Weibo is trying to make itself closer to a social network, bringing more daily life and consumption scenarios into play, and conducting data mining and interest recommendations based on this. This is also a business model with more value and development potential beyond traditional advertising placement and third-party developer revenue sharing. The key issue Sina Weibo faces now is how to build a solid data foundation in the fragmented information flood and construct various mature and attractive scenarios for users. It can be said that the success or failure of Page directly relates to whether Sina Weibo can enter a healthy growth commercial track in the next few years.
