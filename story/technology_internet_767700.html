Google Executive Takes 150 Pills a Day to Seek "Immortality"

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:39:04

Google Engineering Director and futurist Ray Kurzweil

According to the UK's Daily Mail, Google Engineering Director and futurist Ray Kurzweil stated that humans will soon be able to achieve immortality, and he will unwaveringly lead humanity towards this day. Ray Kurzweil believes that through a series of "bridges," the human body can become increasingly robust, thus achieving this historic breakthrough. He also compared human biology with computer software, believing that the biological "programs" currently carried by humans are all "outdated."

Extending life, awaiting the biotechnology revolution

At 65 years old, Kurzweil now takes 150 vitamin supplements daily, believing that vitamins may represent the first "bridge" to immortality. With enough "bridges" built, human life can be extended until the day when longevity technology is realized.

In an interview with a Canadian magazine, Kurzweil expressed his hope that vitamins would keep him healthy. "I will never say: 'I have achieved this, I am already immortal.' Because the goal of 'forever' is unattainable. What we're really talking about is how to give us the ability to reach the next target point. The first bridge I mentioned refers to maintaining our physical health through dietary control, exercise, and existing drugs, with the aim of reaching the second bridge. The second bridge is the biotechnology revolution, which can reprogram our 'biological code,' keeping us away from diseases."

Kurzweil pointed out that there is no endpoint for what he calls "bridges." "The third bridge transcends biology and reaches the nanotechnology revolution, at which point we can manufacture very small robots, such as 'nanobots,' that can enhance our immune system. We can build an immune system capable of identifying all diseases; once new diseases appear, the immune system’s program can be reconfigured to eliminate new bacteria." Kurzweil stated that these "nanobots" can be used to combat diseases, improve human health conditions, and allow humans to remain vital for longer periods of time.

The "biological programs" in the human body are outdated

Kurzweil also indicated that biology resembles the process of a computer program solving problems. "Our bodies consist of countless cells, each controlled by this biological program. The 'programs' running within us now are outdated, evolved in an era vastly different from today's world."

Kurzweil pointed out that everyone carries genes that absorb fat, aimed at "not missing any source of calories." "This was very useful 10,000 years ago, because people back then could only consume a little bit of calories after working hard all day. There were no refrigerators, so heat had to be stored in fat cells."

However, modern life allows people not to store as much fat. "I want to tell my genes to stop working so hard to store fat," Kurzweil said.

Kurzweil even conducted experiments, successfully "shutting down" the fat absorption function in a mouse. This mouse continued eating but remained slim, did not develop heart disease, and lived 20% longer than an average mouse.

Kurzweil previously predicted that in another decade or so, humans will be able to eat "junk food" freely, as by then, "nanobots" will be implanted in every person's body, providing necessary nutrients while reducing fat intake.

Google makes a "bet" to solve health issues

Google recently established a new company called Calico, aiming to address health issues related to human aging.

Google CEO Larry Page posted: "Although this is obviously a long-term bet, we believe that with the right goals and the right people, we can make good progress within a reasonable timeframe."

Sources indicated that Google will invest heavily in Calico, enabling it to invest in various projects.

Page acknowledged that the new company seems to deviate from "Google's current business." But he stated: "If our investments seem strange or speculative compared to our existing internet business, don't be surprised. Remember, these new investment projects are very small compared to our core business." Last year, Google's revenue reached $50 billion.

Health issues are a personal area of interest for Google co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin. In May this year, Page announced that he suffers from a rare neurological condition that restricts the movement of his vocal cords and prevents him from giving public speeches.

Brin announced that his probability of developing Parkinson's disease is higher than average, as his mother suffers from this disease. He believes that through strict dieting and physical exercise, he can reduce his chances of developing Parkinson's disease from 50% to 13%.
