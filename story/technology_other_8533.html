[Latest News][Former Iraqi President Saddam and his cousin sentenced to hang]

by jay on 2006-11-05 21:35:53

According to the French media reports on November 5th, the Iraqi High Court officially announced today the verdict of Saddam in the "Dujail Village Case". Among them, Saddam and his cousin Barazan Tikriti were sentenced to death by hanging. Three co-defendants were sentenced to 15 years in prison, while another defendant was declared not guilty.

The Iraqi High Court announced the verdict of the Saddam case today. The four main defendants are former Iraqi President Saddam Hussein, former head of the Iraqi intelligence department Barzan Ibrahim al-Tikriti, former Iraqi Vice President Taha Yasin Ramadan, and former president of the Iraqi Revolutionary Court Awad Hamid al-Bandar. The Iraqi prosecution accused them of retaliatory killings of about 148 Shiite villagers in Dujail Village in 1982, which is located about 60 kilometers north of Baghdad.

In the ongoing trial, Saddam was sentenced to death, three co-defendants in the Saddam case were sentenced to 15 years in prison on the spot, and the co-defendant of Saddam, a former official of the Ba'ath Socialist Party, Muhammad Azawi al-Ali, was declared not guilty. After hearing the verdict, Saddam's body trembled, and he shouted slogans such as "Long live Iraq! Long live the Iraqi people! Allah is greater than the occupiers."

The Iraqi government is concerned that whether or not Saddam is sentenced to death, it may trigger unrest in Iraq. To prevent armed personnel from taking advantage of the situation to create violent incidents, the Iraqi government negotiated with the US troops in Iraq on the 4th, planning to impose curfews in areas such as the capital Baghdad and Dujail Village. The Iraqi Minister of Defense ordered on the 3rd to cancel all military leave.

Baghdad residents had different reactions to the prospect of Saddam possibly being executed. The Sunnis were filled with anger over the possible death sentence, while the Shiites welcomed it. Hussein Hammad once served as a guard at Saddam's Republican Palace. He said: "We know that the death penalty will be the end for Saddam. A sick lion will have many monkeys dancing on its back. His rule was one hundred percent better than today. Nowadays, no one in Iraq feels safe."

An armed group in Iraq named the "Sadr Republican Army" issued a statement on the 4th, which was published on a website close to Sunni Muslims. The armed group stated in the statement that the Iraqi High Court would officially announce the verdict of the Saddam case on the 5th. Although the judges claimed that the verdict was fair, there is solid evidence showing that Iraqi Prime Minister Maliki had already informed the United States of the verdict beforehand.
