[Reprint] Give yourself the ability to listen

by koucai7346 on 2012-02-13 16:24:09

I remember a person asked me why we humans have two ears but only one mouth. At that time, I didn't really pay attention to it and just listened to it as a joke. Later, after careful thought, why does this reality exist? Is it really just an evolution in nature? After careful thought, I came to a conclusion: why is it inevitable? In fact, simply put, it's just to let us listen more and talk less. Maybe this is just my understanding, a thousand people have a thousand different understandings. Perhaps your understanding is not like this. Here, the Oratory Training Network will explain with everyone why I understand it this way. In daily life, we always encounter various situations, especially in interpersonal relationships and team communication building, such things are very common. What exactly is the matter? It's that a person always talks incessantly about his own words and rarely uses his ears to listen to others' words. In terms of sales oratory, this should be a great taboo. Salespeople all have their own set of speaking arts; they know how to introduce themselves in the shortest time, state their intentions, and then spend most of the time listening to what the customers say, which allows them to close a deal. In terms of interpersonal relationship management and team building, the same principle applies: listen more and talk less. If you don't listen, the function of your ears will degenerate. We each have two ears, and the function of ears is to listen. We have two ears and one mouth, just so that you can listen more and talk less. This is the understanding of the Oratory Training Network. No matter what, listening will bring you unexpected results. Therefore, in our daily lives, we must also learn to be good listeners.

1. We need to learn to listen, to listen to the sounds of nature.

In fact, this could be considered a quality improvement in life. Listening is really important for us because if you don't listen, you will never understand others' feelings, and you will always live in your own world, refusing to interact with others. Such a world is closed. So, we first need to develop our own listening skills. We live in nature, and as long as we use our ears to listen quietly, we can feel that nature has opened its doors, allowing us to hear the wind, the rain, thunder, water, birds singing, crickets playing, dogs playing, hens announcing good news, roosters crowing... Thus, we integrate into nature. This way, your listening ability won't degenerate, and gradually, you will find that your interpersonal relationships and sales effectiveness will greatly improve. Because you've learned to listen.

2. We need to learn to listen, to listen to the other party's voice.

When you can listen to the sound of the world, it means you already have great love. When you can apply these to our daily lives, you will find that you are really very different from the old you. Because you have made a qualitative leap. You know how to listen to others, you know how to listen to others' words. Especially when interacting with others, you will find that you naturally listen to others' words and extract some important information from them, solving them one by one. Only by finding the real cause can you truly solve the problem. Only by fully understanding can you give the best answer in the shortest time.

3. We need to learn to listen, to listen to our own echoes.

We live in our own echoes every day, and we can hear them as long as we prick up our ears.

A boy got into a sulk with his mother and ran alone into the mountains shouting: "I hate you!" The mountains echoed back: "I hate you!" The boy was scared and went home to tell his mother that there was a bad child in the mountain who hated him. His mother accompanied him back to the mountain and had him shout towards the mountain: "I love you! I love you!" Sure enough, the mountain responded: "I love you! I love you!"

This sound is an echo. An echo is the repetition of the sound we ourselves emit. We need to often listen to our own echoes, reflect on which words we said correctly and civilly, and which ones were wrong and uncivilized. We shouldn't say inappropriate things in front of children, nor say bad things about others behind their backs. Remember, children are the echoes of their parents.

Learn to listen, and the other party will open their heart, and the world will open new windows for us.
