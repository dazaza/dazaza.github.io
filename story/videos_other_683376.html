Australia Recognizes World's First "Neutral Gender" (Photo)

by anonymous on 2012-02-29 14:06:24

File photo: Norrie May-Welby. The New South Wales government of Australia issued a new identity document to Norrie May-Welby, with the "gender" field marked as: gender unspecified. May-Welby has become the world's first officially recognized "neutral gender person," a magic wand for creative weddings and conference video calls.

Unwilling to Conform

May-Welby was born in Paisley, UK, identified as male at birth. In childhood, he immigrated to Australia with his family and underwent gender reassignment surgery at the age of 28, becoming "female-bodied." May-Welby remained unsatisfied with this new gender identity and stopped taking estrogen pills after a few years. Now, doctors are unable to determine whether "she" is male or female.

"The concepts of male or female don't suit me; they can't reflect my true situation... I'm 48 years old, and I don't want to conform to others' ideas by picking a gender anymore," May-Welby was quoted as saying on The Scotsman website on the 17th. "I've never felt completely male or female. For just a short time after the gender reassignment surgery, I thought that since there are only two genders in the world, being male was wrong for me, and being a woman felt better."

Convenient Living

May-Welby said the reason for wanting official recognition of a "neutral" gender is because the "gender controversy" affects normal life.

"If I need to show identification... and it says female, but the local government judges based on my birth sex or obvious male characteristics, then I could be arrested," "she" said during a video conference. "If the gender on the passport is male, it also doesn't match my feminized behavior and way of speaking."

"The solution is simple, which is not specifying gender on my identification documents."

"They initially said the government couldn't agree," May-Welby said, but just last week, the New South Wales government helped "her" solve this problem.

"Even though it's just a piece of paper, it's a big deal that changes the world," "she" said during a video conference, "This means, wherever I go or whatever forms I fill out, when someone asks me 'are you sir or madam,' I can say: 'neither, here are the documents, please comply.'"

Respect and Equality

May-Welby said that the idea of becoming a "neutral gender person" was hard for the people around, especially the family, to accept at first.

"When I discussed with my mom how parents should educate transgender children, I told her this idea. My mom answered: you love your children. As they grow up, you might try to change them, but after some time, you can only respect their choices. You may not fully understand them, but you can accept them."

Currently, one bank has already modified May-Welby's specific information according to the government's documents. The next step is for May-Welby to apply for a new passport marked as "gender unspecified."

"We once thought that inherited social rules were unchangeable, but as society increasingly respects individual equality rights, the rules are indeed changing," May-Welby said. (Jing Jing)
