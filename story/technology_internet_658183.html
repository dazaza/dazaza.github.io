What are some things about Internet Protocol telephony that you don't know?

by jojozou on 2012-02-02 17:07:28

Internet Protocol telephone (short for VoIP, from English Voice over Internet Protocol), which we can also call broadband telephone or network telephone. In the past, IP telephones were mainly used within the intranets of large companies, where technical personnel could reuse the same network to provide data and voice services. This not only simplified management but also improved productivity. IP telephone is a telephone service enabled by network technology content according to the international Internet Protocol, translated in Chinese as "network telephone" or "Internet telephone". Simply put, it is a real-time voice transmission service through the Internet. It uses the international Internet (Internet) as the medium for voice transmission, thus realizing a brand new communication technology for voice communication. For those who frequently make international long-distance calls, they will not be unfamiliar with Internet Protocol telephone. It is also called "VOIP telephone", where users can directly dial the landline or mobile phone of the other party via the Internet. Compared to the traditional landline method, its greatest advantage is the low cost. Taking Skype, the giant of international network telephones, as an example, using one of its international cards to call the United States or Canada costs 0.19 yuan per minute, while the IP telephone fee is 2.4 yuan per minute. The price difference between the two is obvious at a glance. Therefore, Internet telephones are welcomed by users. According to incomplete statistics, the number of domestic network telephone users in 2009 has reached 18 million, and Skype's user base in China has already surpassed that in the United States.
