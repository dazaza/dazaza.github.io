A family of five died of oxygen deprivation after privately connecting natural gas for heating.

by bct999888 on 2012-02-10 13:37:39

The incident occurred at Building 11, on the first floor of Fengdexinyuan Community, No. 269 Youdian Road, Fengxian District. A 120-square-meter roughcast house had not been decorated at all. As soon as one enters the room, a warm air flow rushes to the face with a special smell accompanying it. According to nearby neighbors, someone in the dead family was an individual peddler who made a living by selling tropical fish. Because rearing tropical fish requires a high indoor environmental temperature, this family kept their windows and doors closed for a long time. In order to maintain the temperature, they illegally connected a flexible pipe from the natural gas pipeline in the kitchen to continuously supply gas to the heating equipment in the room. Finally, five people in the family were all suffocated to death because there was insufficient oxygen indoors. The fire department reminds us: in winter, we must guard against gas and natural gas poisoning. We need to pay attention to opening the windows and doors when using gas. We must frequently check the integrity of gas and natural gas connecting pipelines and the safety of various devices. We must resolutely prevent the occurrence of privately pulling and connecting pipelines and illegal use of gas.

The room was still very sultry and hot. "A friend of theirs could not contact them four days ago. Their mobile phones were always turned off, so he came over. He found that the light was on, but there was no response when knocking on the door. Later, through the window, he saw several people lying motionless on the bed and on the ground," said a community security guard.

Entering the room, the reporter of the Morning Post saw two plank-built beds and clothes scattered around. There were dozens of glass aquariums neatly piled up in the two larger rooms on the north and south sides of the room. Several kinds of tropical fish were being raised in the water tanks.

At about 10 o'clock last night, a family of five renting in Fengdexinyuan Community, No. 269 Youdian Road, Fengcheng Town, Fengxian District, were found dead due to suffocation in the house. After investigation by the gas department, it was found that the house illegally connected to natural gas and used it improperly. Due to insufficient oxygen indoors while burning natural gas, they died.

Not long after the incident, the police and the gas department conducted an investigation at the scene. After identification, it was determined that the combustion of natural gas caused insufficient oxygen indoors, resulting in the suffocation deaths of the five people indoors.

The reporter found that the lighting in these two fish farming rooms was dim, and the gaps in the windows were all sealed with packing tape. The entrance was isolated with quilts to prevent cold air from entering. The glass of the room was covered with a thick layer of water vapor, and the humidity inside the room was very high. It was quite sultry and uncomfortable to enter the room.

According to nearby neighbors, Fengdexinyuan is a new community. Zhu Mou from Jiangxi brought his wife and a pair of children to live in this set of rooms. His Sichuan-born son-in-law Li Yonggang, who was in his twenties, sold tropical fish. Because of the cold weather, he was worried that the tropical fish would have an accident due to the cold weather, so he discussed with his family to privately connect the natural gas pipeline to the room to heat and keep the tropical fish warm. Due to the tight closure of the room and the long-term burning of heating equipment, the five members of the family were unknowingly suffocated and eventually unfortunately died.

After checking the surroundings, the reporter found that a red flexible pipe about 20 meters long was connected from the gas pipeline in the kitchen, leading to the two fish farming rooms. The end of the flexible pipe was connected to two iron heating stoves. Upon touching it with hand, it was still warm. Right at the source of the flexible pipe, a paper note covered with oil stains warned about gas safety, and the first rule was "Do not privately connect gas pipelines."

"Four days ago, I couldn't contact them."

The reporter saw that the thermometer placed in the fish-raising room showed that the indoor temperature reached 28 degrees Celsius, and the ceiling was full of water droplets.
