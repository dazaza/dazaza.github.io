Question Three: Should not eat oranges, fruit juice, sugar, chocolate and medicine with milk? Around 1 hour before or after drinking milk, it is not suitable to eat oranges. Since once the protein in the milk meets the fruit acid in the orange, they will generate coagulation, thus affecting digestion and absorption of milk. In this time period, other acidic fruits should not be eaten as well.

80% of the protein in the milk is casein. When the acidity of the milk is below 4.6, a large amount of casein will generate aggregation and precipitation, making it difficult to digest and absorb, which could even lead to indigestion or diarrhea in severe cases. Therefore, it's not advisable to add acidic beverages like fruit juice to milk.

The lysine contained in milk can react with fructose under heated conditions to form toxic fructoselysine, which is harmful to the human body. Sugar should not be added to fresh milk when boiling, but rather after the milk has cooled down. Is there any benefit to adding sugar to milk?

Milk contains rich protein and calcium, while chocolate contains oxalic acid. Eating these two together will form insoluble calcium oxalate, greatly affecting calcium absorption. It may even cause dry hair, diarrhea, slow growth, etc.

Some people like to use milk instead of water to take medicine. However, milk significantly affects the body's absorption of drugs. Milk easily forms a covering membrane on the surface of the drug, causing the calcium and magnesium minerals in the milk to chemically react with the drug, forming non-water-soluble substances, thereby affecting the release and absorption of the drug's efficacy. Therefore, milk should not be consumed within an hour before or after taking medicine.

Question Five: Which makes you fatter, yogurt or milk?

When it comes to calories, it's necessary to understand its composition. Although different brands vary, the content generally includes cow's milk, pectin, fructose, and spices, etc. Due to the fact that most Chinese people cannot adapt to overly sour yogurt, manufacturers often add sugar or fructose to adjust the taste, thereby increasing many calories.

Low-fat milk of about 240cc contains approximately 120 calories, while the same amount of low-fat yogurt contains 150-200 calories (depending on the brand), so the calories are higher than expected. If you want to drink yogurt with fewer calories, we recommend choosing yogurt labeled as fat-free and low-calorie, as it only contains around 100 calories for the same 240cc.
