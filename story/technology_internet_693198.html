It is important to conduct a patent legal status search when introducing technology.

by keyizhang008 on 2012-03-06 14:58:46

Patent (www.k8008.com[http://www.k8008.com]) legal status search refers to the search for the current state of a patent or patent application. Its purpose is to understand whether the patent application has been authorized, whether the authorized patent is still valid, whether the patent holder has changed, and other information related to the legal status of the patent. For the legal status search of key target patents, the main goal is to determine if the patent is currently valid and if there has been any change in the patent holder. Especially in technology introduction, many foreign companies bundle their patents for licensing, but among them, quite a few patents are expired or invalid. Additionally, differences in remaining protection periods can also affect the value of the patent. Therefore, it is very necessary to conduct a legal status search on key patents before introducing technology or seeking a license. So what information does the patent legal status search include?

1. Patent Authorization

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent has been granted authorization, and until the next payment deadline after the search date, the patent remains valid. This legal status is called patent authorization.

2. Publication of Patent Application

China implements an early publication and deferred examination system for invention patents. After passing the preliminary review, the invention text is published and undergoes substantive review within three years. If the legal status shows as authorized, it means the patent has obtained patent rights in the application region. After filing an invention patent application, if the preliminary review is passed, 18 months later, the National Intellectual Property Administration will publish the patent and conduct a comprehensive review of its novelty, inventiveness, and practicality. If no objections are raised within three years, the patent is granted authorization.

3. Expiration of Patent Validity Period

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent has been granted authorization, but by the search date or prior to it, the validity period of the patent has exceeded the term stipulated by the patent law (including the extended term). This legal status is called the expiration of the patent validity period. The expiration of the patent validity period means that the patent is no longer protected by patent law.

4. Patent Application Not Yet Authorized

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent application has not yet been published, or has been published but not yet granted a patent right. This legal status is called the patent application not yet authorized.

5. Withdrawal of Patent Application

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent application was withdrawn by the applicant or deemed withdrawn by the patent office. This legal status is called the withdrawal of the patent application. A patent deemed withdrawn does not enjoy the effectiveness of patent rights and does not have exclusivity.

6. Rejection of Patent Application

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent application was rejected by the patent office. This legal status is called the rejection of the patent application.

7. Termination of Patent Rights

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent had been authorized, but due to non-payment of patent fees, it became prematurely invalid before the expiration of the validity period of the patent rights. This legal status is called the termination of patent rights.

8. Invalidity of Patent Rights

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent had been authorized, but due to the establishment of reasons for invalidation, the patent rights were declared invalid by the patent office. This legal status is called the invalidity of patent rights. The invalid patent is considered invalid from the beginning.

9. Transfer of Patent Rights

On the day of the search or prior to it, the searched patent or patent application experienced a change in the patent holder or patent applicant. This legal status is called the transfer of patent rights.

10. Deemed Abandonment of Patent Rights

After the Patent Office issues a notice of granting patent rights, if the applicant fails to complete registration procedures within the prescribed time limit, it is deemed as abandoning the right to obtain patent rights.
