The Internet, word of mouth and TV have become the best ways to learn about online shopping.

by cfan64165422 on 2008-06-26 14:18:32

Recently, CNNIC has completed the preparation and release of the "2008 China Online Shopping Survey Report". The report shows that the total number of online shoppers nationwide is 46.41 million people. In the four municipalities and 15 sub-provincial cities, the online shopping amount in the first half of 2008 reached 16.2 billion yuan. Among these, the Internet, word-of-mouth, and television have become the most important channels for users to learn about online shopping.

Overall, the Internet is the primary channel for netizens to learn about shopping websites, with 70% of shopping netizens considering the Internet as one of their cognitive channels. The second most important channel is word-of-mouth from family and friends, with more than half of the netizens hearing about a shopping website from others.

In terms of shopping website dissemination channels, traditional offline channels such as television, radio, magazines, and newspapers are not very important. Among the traditional channels, television remains an important cognitive channel for netizens. Additionally, magazine advertisements have become the second largest traditional offline channel for netizens to learn about shopping websites, surpassing outdoor advertisements and newspapers.

Beijing netizens have a relatively higher proportion of hearing about shopping websites on TV, while Guangzhou has a relatively higher proportion of knowing about online shopping websites through the Internet. In other cities, word-of-mouth from family and friends plays a stronger role.

From different gender perspectives, women are more easily influenced by family and friends and have a relatively higher acceptance of word-of-mouth information. 56.9% of female shopping netizens have heard about online shopping websites from others. Women are relatively more affected by traditional channels, whether it be television or magazine and outdoor advertisement channels, where female users have a higher acceptance than male users.
