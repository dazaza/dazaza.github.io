Designers available on the market now can be divided into two categories: A) Those with a science/engineering background. These designers are more rigorous, have a strong sense of space, and require good craftsmanship. B) Those with an art background. These designers tend to have more ideas, focus more on the surface, and pay less attention to the craftsmanship aspect. Additionally, it is suggested that when choosing a designer, their age should ideally be between 23-35 years old, as they would have a certain amount of life experience. It's also best to choose someone who has been in the industry for a certain period of time, at least 3 years. This is because many newly graduated design personnel do not have much design experience, which is especially evident during construction design.

Based on your own and cohabitant’s lifestyle and living standards, first determine what you like and what suits you best. Then take a look at your property, think about how to plan it, and what you need. Decoration is an overall integration of all details, and must have a main design theme. All details revolve around the "resident." Planning should preferably have a time concept, such as 3-5 years, considering what changes might occur.

This has nothing to do with whether you trust the decoration company. Having one more supervisory function is not a bad thing.

Observe and listen more to the stories of friends and colleagues regarding their home renovations. Since renovation knowledge covers a wide range, it is essential to accumulate more knowledge. When choosing a designer, make sure to show them the floor plan and structure diagram. Usually, some good designers will raise necessary questions and engage in exchanges, presenting their own ideas. Do not disclose your own thoughts immediately; see if he can identify any shortcomings in the property. There are usually problems, good designers can spot them, average ones cannot. Building design is influenced by area and cannot be without issues.
