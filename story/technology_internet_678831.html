The new policy about the indigenous brands of future official cars

by jintaimqr on 2012-02-27 16:48:36

In recent days, with the news of the new Roewe 750 based on the new Regal platform getting the Shanghai brand, speculations within the industry about the country's upcoming policy favoring the purchase of self-own-brand official cars have increased. In fact, not only has the Shanghai brand car, which has been silent for many years, chosen to make a comeback this year, but also other self-own-brand mid-to-high-end new cars such as Hongqi C131 and Besturn B90 will be introduced to the Chinese people this year. Almost all mainstream self-own-brands are developing mid-to-high-end models suitable for official use, including Guangzhou Auto Trumpchi, which has already been launched and explicitly expressed its intention to enter the official car market. These should not be simple coincidences.

Thinking about Audi, which has always been the representative of official cars in China, it has changed its attitude of ignoring worldly affairs in the past few years and started to strengthen market promotion and brand publicity. On one hand, this is due to the pressure from BMW and Mercedes-Benz, but more importantly, Audi's monopoly in the official car market will be challenged by more and more self-own-brands in the near future.

On February 24, relevant policies were finally released. The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology released the "2012 Annual Model Catalogue for Official Cars of Party and Government Organs" (hereinafter referred to as the "Catalogue") and solicited opinions from the whole society. According to the listed models in the "Catalogue", all 412 models are self-own-brands, with no joint venture brands included. The deadline for soliciting opinions is March 9. According to regulations, the "Catalogue" is an annual catalogue, and is原则上formulated and released once a year. It can be seen that the 2012 annual Chinese official car procurement market may be monopolized by self-own-brand cars.
