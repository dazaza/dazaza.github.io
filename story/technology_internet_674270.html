Page 1: Editor's Preface

Editor's Preface: How can two tigers coexist on one mountain? The answer lies in geographical isolation, each occupying its own territory. In the CPU domain, AMD and Intel have long dominated as dual powerhouses. Both companies have a very clear understanding of their product positioning, channeling limited financial and human resources into areas that yield maximum profits - an ideal business model. For consumers, a clear market structure helps guide purchasing decisions in the right direction, achieving the greatest value for the least investment, which is the ultimate goal of shopping.

So how are AMD and Intel products positioned? How can regular people make correct choices in this transparent product landscape? What are the options for different consumer classes? Today, I will introduce some insights to you. First, consumers need to determine their purchasing needs, i.e., the purpose and positioning of the computer. Excessive performance represents unnecessary spending, especially in these times of rising prices and increasing inflation. Cost-performance ratio remains the guiding principle of shopping. After analyzing, I've identified some common reasons why gamers buy computers: 1) Pure auxiliary learning and office use; 2) Accompanying study with daily entertainment; 3) A loyal companion for gaming enthusiasts; 4) A toy for overclocking enthusiasts; 5) Choices for the rich second generation. Below, I will recommend some accurately positioned motherboards based on purchasing needs.

Page 2: Pure Auxiliary Learning and Office Use

For handling everyday work and study tasks, there's no need for a powerful computer. As long as the platform is stable and the price is low enough, it suffices. Thus, older Core 2 processors and AMD’s current main push, the APU, can both meet these requirements. The older Core 2 processors have gradually been replaced by SNB (Sandy Bridge) and the new generation of performance king SNB-E processors, but they are more than enough for learning and office use. As for APU, it goes without saying that it is AMD's heavyweight bomb dropped in the entry-level computer field. The lower-end A4 series processors have already been launched, offering excellent cost-performance ratios. Below are some recommended entry-level motherboard platforms for your reference, whether you're an "A fan" or an "I fan."

Page 3: Accompanying Study with Daily Entertainment

For ordinary students, computers have become essential tools for learning and another gateway to the treasure trove of knowledge beyond books. However, entertainment during leisure time is indispensable, as teaching through enjoyment is the essence of education! Therefore, a computer with basic performance capable of functioning as a gaming machine is necessary. I recommend the high-end A8 APU platform and the low-end series of Intel's SNB platform. Netizens have confirmed that the A8 series processor can run most 3D games solely with its integrated graphics, showing very strong performance. On Intel's side, H61 series motherboards also support integrated graphics output. If higher performance is required, a beginner-level graphics card can be purchased, keeping the overall platform price still affordable.

High-end APU Platform:

The booming sales of APU sufficiently prove the success of AMD's "fusion concept." With its powerful integrated graphics performance and relatively high cost-performance ratio, Llano APU processors are now widely used in entry-level computers and HTPC platforms. A75 and A55 are the new chipset series developed by AMD specifically for APUs, using the latest Socket FM1 interface. However, the A75 chipset natively supports six SATA3.0 and six USB3.0 ports, which are not available in the lower-end A55 chipset. If you have high requirements for hard drive and USB transfer speeds, the A75 chipset is currently the best choice. The A75 motherboard I recommend is priced at 579 yuan, and interested friends can inquire about it.

Editor's Conclusion: The product layout of AMD and Intel processors is very clear. APU, as AMD's main push this year, leverages the innovative "fusion concept" to achieve unprecedented integrated graphics performance. Whether desktops will evolve towards integrated platforms in the future remains unknown, but the release of Intel's Ivy Bridge 22nm processors will surely impact the integrated computer field, and we can wait and see. In the high-end sector, Intel still reigns supreme, with SNB-E having dethroned the X58 era and crowned itself as the supreme ruler. When choosing a processor, gamers must consider usage and budget carefully, ensuring their money is spent wisely.
