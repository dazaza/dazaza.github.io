Good-looking movie comedy film

by hoouk260 on 2012-03-03 20:12:59

Jumper, also known as Moment Hacker, features a protagonist who can teleport instantly, like from home to the bank and back! The male lead is incredibly handsome, and I watched it mainly for his looks. Some people criticize it as a superficial movie, and yes, superhero movies of this kind have been overdone. It feels like everyone in the world has special powers now; what depth is left to explore? But the primary function of movies is entertainment. There's no need to say more, or it would turn into a film review. The male lead also starred in a romantic comedy called Virgin Land, filled with good-looking men and women, based on The Decameron.

Iron Man: A genius with a wounded body transforms himself into Iron Man, adapted from a comic book. Speaking of which, we must mention Superhero, which parodies Spider-Man. Frankly, Spider-Man made me extremely frustrated, but this movie let out my breath and made me laugh for an hour.

A Clockwork Orange: The main character has violent tendencies and is later captured, forced to watch violent films with his eyes held open until he realizes the error of his ways.

Lockout: Hilariously funny, where a man becomes stronger to protect his own behind!

The Thirteenth Floor: Has a more ingenious concept than The Matrix.

Spawn: Classic old film.

Gremlin’s Notebook: Slightly childlike.

Knowing: Nicolas Cage can foresee events two minutes into the future.

Léon: The Professional: A classic, touching story about a milk-drinking, flower-growing hitman and a little girl. Highly recommended!

Mask 1 & 2

The Invisible Man: Several versions are pretty good!

Hellboy 1 & 2: The villainous Nazi officer in Part 1 who modifies his body is super cool! He captivates me more than the main character. Personally, I prefer films with religious and mythological elements, so even though the main character is ugly, I can tolerate it!

National Treasure 1 & 2: The helical underground treasure in Part 1 is stunning. How many people must have died to build it?!

Don’t Mess with Zohan: Quite funny. Zohan isn't a superhero but feels stronger than one, haha.

Scary Movie 1, 2, 3, 4: Parodying classic horror films, very funny. Originally, I wouldn't dare watch anything remotely related to horror, but once at a friend's house, I accidentally saw it and couldn’t stop laughing.

Cast Away: Modern version of Robinson Crusoe. Think about how useful the ice skates in the unopened box at the end could have been!

Equilibrium: Sci-fi combining dystopian cities with high technology.

Tales from the Darkside: The Movie: Old sci-fi film.

The Shawshank Redemption: Notable for its seven Oscar nominations, often called the most perfect film ever. Beware of institutionalization!!

Onion Movie: Satirical humor.

Ghost Rider: Starring Nicolas Cage, turning into a fiery skeleton on a motorcycle. I value the appearance of the main character! Many people criticized me for judging "movies" by appearances, but I am quite attached to looks! So at first, I didn’t intend to watch this movie, but when I noticed how stylish Nicolas’ transformed skull looked, I couldn’t resist watching it for that cool skull head alone, ignoring Nicolas Cage.

Speaking of skulls, I must mention The Nightmare Before Christmas, a clever clay animation film. Smooth, cute, and realistic, I used to watch it as a horror film when I was young. Jack Skellington inside is so elegant, I love him!

Corpse Bride: A 3D animated second claymation film by the director of The Nightmare Before Christmas. You can hear the British accents clearly, but they aren’t too strong. Ghosts have more humanity than humans.

Face/Off: Starring Nicolas Cage.

The Fifth Element: Starring Bruce Willis. An old movie, but I watched it recently after seeing Bruce Willis' guest appearance in Friends. It felt average (don't throw things at me, just personal feelings). The famous soprano segment combined beautifully with the film, but I was very disappointed with the appearance of the heroine described as "perfect." Foreigners' aesthetics seem a bit unconventional! Still worth watching though.

Dave's Space Ship: Funny sci-fi with a humanoid alien spaceship full of aliens.

Forrest Gump: As a child, I yawned through it, but grew to find it touching, especially at the end when Forrest waits at the bus stop for his son, making me cry. The movie version of Forrest is cuter and more straightforward than the book, which is darker.

Braveheart: Ancient foreign war movie, similar to King Arthur, with half-naked bodybuilders, grand natural landscapes, and thrilling battles.

Constantine: Starring Keanu Reeves. I find Keanu incredibly handsome + melancholic + charismatic.

Indiana Jones: Parts 1-4. Honestly, I found them mediocre and never finished any. At the time, Jurassic Park dazzled me, making me believe everything this director touched was gold. However, the legend of the crystal skull seems real, and those skulls are indeed beautiful...

Kill Bill: Parts 1 & 2 Action movie, watched for Lucy Liu. Later, her scalp being peeled off shocked me greatly, somewhat violent.

The Graduate: Starring Dustin Hoffman, his breakout role. The theme song is my favorite!!!

The Mist: Worth a casual watch! The ending makes you think deeply. Persevere!!!

Starship Troopers 1: Sci-fi classic.

The Silence of the Lambs 1 & 2: This isn't a horror film, so I'm not afraid. Hannibal Lecter reminds me of Hayakawa Kazuo... Japanese people are weird.

Edward Scissorhands: Alternative love story, moving. His hands are scissors, and I cried buckets. The director was cruel to design the moment when Edward's father dies of a heart attack right before installing his hands. I bled internally while watching.

Night at the Museum: Funny with a touch of fantasy.

The Lord of the Rings 1, 2, 3: No need to elaborate, feeling similar to Harry Potter, set in ancient Middle-earth with orcs, beasts, giants... The makeup is incredibly realistic, and I want Gandalf's white staff badly.

Van Helsing: A commercial classic of vampire films.

Speaking of vampire movies, we must mention Interview with the Vampire. In the instant Louis (Brad Pitt) turns into a vampire, the angel statue's eyes opening and closing overwhelmed me with religious sentiment. I prefer Lestat (Tom Cruise) over Louis, and the little vampire Claudia seems less refined and beautiful to me personally.

This Is Sparta: Funny scenes make you laugh out loud. The beginning mocks Brad Pitt and Angelina Jolie, adopting children is a good deed, deserves praise.

Transformers: Doesn't feel much like the cartoon, but the robots are done exceptionally well. I like the horse stance of the female lead.

Hancock: Will Smith, whom I adore so much, a black superstar. Another black actor I find attractive is Orlando Jones, starring in Evolution (funny ending, defeating the alien monster with shampoo, and parodying at the end). I don't know why, but I fell for his face.

King Kong: The new version is pretty good. The first half-hour meticulously shows the rich biodiversity of the island where Kong lives—giant insects, carnivorous plants, cannibals, extinct dinosaurs—a strange world combining ferns, carnivorous plants, primates, and dinosaurs. I wonder how many scientists would faint upon seeing it.

Men in Black 1 & 2: Will Smith, a powerful film not relying on special scenes for humor. The dialogues are witty; watching the English version is essential. Adapted from an animated series, I watched it as a child, finding the dialogue wise despite the lackluster visuals.

The Truman Show: From birth to adulthood, the whole world watches your life without you knowing! It makes you feel a bit down; comedies should be comedic! Being unable to laugh or cry is uncomfortable. Just a recommendation.

The Last Samurai: Starring Tom Cruise. Tom wearing Japanese samurai armor... somehow connects to Japan, so I reflect on my wavering stance while watching.

Babylon AD: The male lead is from Fast & Furious and Pitch Black, all about being cool! I only watched because of the word Babylon, one of the four ancient civilizations. Watching Raiders of the Lost Ark 1 was somewhat comforting due to the profound Mayan culture.

Shut Up: Jean Reno. Two prisoners escaping, one talks a lot, the other doesn't. Extremely funny, highly recommended!

Fantastic Four 1 & 2: I prefer the first one.

Superdog Mission: A parody of Superman, creating a superdog... speechless.

Grimm Brothers: Starring Matt Damon. Magical fairy tale... Can this even be called a fairy tale?! You can find many clues from Grimm's fairy tales here, but this is more like a twisted fairy tale. I appreciate the dark style, a nice change of pace occasionally.

The Producers: Comedy, just two people making a movie.

Taxi Driver 1, 2, 3, 4: Taxi Speed Delivery, funny.

Amélie: Starring Audrey Tautou. The female lead falls for a man who collects photo fragments.

Daredevil: Starring Ben Affleck. Based on a comic.

Hotshot Teacher: Chandler (Matthew Perry) from Friends plays this role. People say Tony Leung's eyes are melancholic, sorry, I can't see it (don't hit me! Don't accuse me of favoring foreigners), but Matthew's silence moves me deeply. This isn't a comedy, quite touching, crying through a pack of tissues.

Swordfish: Crime thriller. The male lead is incredibly handsome.

The Incredibles: 3D animation, sci-fi comedy. The hair of the two female leads is superbly designed, lively and shiny!

Saving Private Ryan

Windtalkers: War

Eragon: Riding a flying dragon, fantasy genre, same level as Lord of the Rings.

The Chronicles of Narnia: Adapted from a novel J.K. Rowling also likes, better than the second part. I have some preconceived notions, but the boy playing Edward is truly adorable! More refined in the second part, that's why I watched it.

Mr. & Mrs. Smith: Their fight scenes are pretty good. Pitt kicking Jolie, Angelina wearing Pitt's shirt showing long legs is quite appealing.

Ace Ventura: Pet Detective 2: Better than the first time, quite funny!

10,000 BC: The final scene is quite spectacular.

The Scorpion King: One fight scene stands out.

Troy: Greek mythology is what I adore, but this film... differs from my imagination, still decent overall. Helen is very beautiful, arguably the most beautiful woman in the world, though opinions vary.

Charlie and the Chocolate Factory: Everyone says it's childlike, but I find it a bit serious... The scene where Charlie reunites with his father is quite warm.

Cold Mountain: Jude Law and Nicole Kidman are reasons I watched it. Adapted from a best-selling novel, set during the Civil War, the outdoor scenery is magnificent.

Monster House: 3D animation, a living haunted house.

WALL-E: Pixar's latest work. The pure-hearted and adorable robot WALL-E is truly heartfelt. Watching him yearn for new friends moved me to tears. Funny but mostly heartwarming.

Cleopatra’s Task: French production, Cleopatra bets with Caesar to build a palace within three months, a comedy, mediocre, optional.

The Da Vinci Code: Sophie Marceau is the only highlight. Skulls don't scare me, neither do mummies, this isn't a horror film, but it has a quiet eerie vibe. Mummy legends fascinate me.

Cheaper by the Dozen 1 & 2: Heartwarming family comedy, with cute kids and good-looking men and women. Disney spent big bucks on it, suitable for family viewing on weekends, enhancing family bonds. I prefer the second part, where the youngest son grows into a handsome young man, and the daughter-in-law is tall and handsome...

No! This is a good movie. That's the point.

Game Plan: Disney production, a good movie for all ages. A lighthearted comedy about an American football star suddenly having a daughter from the sky, their mutual adaptation is both warm and funny.

The Devil Wears Prada: Highlight: Anne Hathaway's fashion show, watching beauties get exploited by their boss, solving impossible tasks.

Get Smart: Female lead Anne Hathaway, male lead is rather ugly, not in my aesthetic range, but the plot is quite funny. Adapted from an early American TV series, telling the story of a wannabe spy and an excellent spy executing missions.

High School Musical 1, 2, 3: A musical featuring beautiful teens, even the negative roles are quite likable (I prefer the second female lead over the first). The songs are worth listening to, and the voice of the female lead Gabriella is beautiful, just her eyes look a bit seductive, not entirely fitting the shy nature of the character.

Pride and Prejudice: Keira Knightley's version, filmed in 2005, quite fitting the elegant atmosphere of Britain.

Monty Python and the Holy Grail!!! This film I must highly recommend!!!! Produced early in Britain, from the opening credits to the ending, it's full of parodies! Even the previews before the film are hilarious. The film tells the story of King Arthur and his Knights of the Round Table searching for the Holy Grail, showcasing the unique British satirical humor. I searched for years and only downloaded it unintentionally this past half-year.

The Day After Tomorrow: Disaster film, visually impactful. Seeing the icicles on the Statue of Liberty gives me goosebumps. A few people warming themselves by a stove in the library where the son and his friends are located makes me instinctively grab a blanket.

Legally Blonde 1 & 2: Beauty can have brains too, valuing beauty and legendary romance! In the second part, the female lead wears 60 pairs of Jimmy Choo heels, just watching the blonde's fashion show is interesting, not to mention it's a traditional comedy, making you feel good.

The Women: This film revolves around women. Meg Ryan, always my favorite actress, played a wife cheated on by her husband. No man's face appears in the film, only women playing tricks, wife, wife's friends, mistress, daughter, each woman is intricate and distinctive.

The Simpsons Movie: Animation, recommending the movie version. Not exactly an adaptation, more like an extra chapter. Now The Simpsons has reached season 19, very popular in America, a milestone in American soap operas. This film is hilariously funny, making me spit rice several times!

Step Up 1, 2: Who says only music can stir the soul? The main dance in the first part is modern dance, the second part focuses on street dance. Both parts feature outstanding music and dances led by good-looking men and women.

Love Actually: British comedy, I have a soft spot for British comedies. Hugh Grant is my idol. Notting Hill, Four Weddings and a Funeral, Bridget Jones's Diary are all great films, but my favorite is Love Actually. This film not only features Hugh Grant but also "Mr. Darcy," weaving 5-6 small stories together, each illustrating the beauty of love. The washed-up old singer's "Christmas Is All Around" (probably named something like that) as a background track feels both fun and warm. Latest movie of 2012.
