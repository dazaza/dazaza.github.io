Symptoms of Rectitis (http://www.28267777.com/disease/8/index.html)

The symptoms of rectitis can be divided into four types:

1. Acute rectitis: Symptoms develop suddenly, with systemic symptoms including fever and loss of appetite. Local symptoms include anal swelling and burning pain, frequent urges to defecate, stool mixed with mucus and blood streaks, tenesmus, difficulty in urination, and frequent urination.

2. Radiation-induced rectitis: Gastrointestinal symptoms may appear early, usually within 1-2 weeks after the start of radiation therapy. These include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, excretion of mucus or bloody stools, occasionally low-grade fever. Sigmoidoscopy examination may reveal mucosal edema and congestion; severe cases may show erosion or ulcers. In the late stage, if acute symptoms persist or significant symptoms appear 6 months to several years after the end of radiation therapy, it indicates that the disease has continued.

3. Tuberculous rectitis: Patients feel discomfort in the intestines, sometimes alternating between constipation and diarrhea. As the condition progresses, symptoms are mostly dominated by diarrhea. The stool is watery, mixed with pus and blood, has a foul smell, accompanied by tenesmus, lower abdominal pain, loss of appetite, and weight loss.

4. Chronic rectitis: Alternating constipation and diarrhea, stool containing mucus and blood streaks, anal burning pain during bowel movements. Due to secretions stimulating, the skin around the anus may peel off, sometimes forming fissures and itching. There is a feeling of fullness and discomfort in the lower abdomen, loss of appetite, weight loss, and general malaise.
