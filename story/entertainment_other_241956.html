Sad diary: The first and last confession of love

by lwf225 on 2009-09-23 17:10:54

The boy was a little ruffian with poor academic performance. After being expelled from school, he had nothing to do all day. The boy was melancholy and rarely spoke, but he had many friends who were also expelled or unwilling to study. No one cared about them. Perhaps in order to get attention, they often did some small bad things. However, the boy was different. Although he was friends with them, he never bothered those who hadn't offended them. What the boy liked most was sitting on the embankment by the river and looking into the distance. There seemed to be a direction that attracted him. He would also appear at the school gate with his friends, but no one saw him bother any girl. He just stood there silently with his friends. The boy was very loyal. In his friends' fights, he always stood first, which made him offend many people. It seemed that the boy never laughed...

Three years have passed since then. The girl looked for the boy she once knew, but his friends said that he had been sent to labor camp because of fighting. Disappointed, the girl left again. She wanted to tell him that she had been admitted to a university...

Life in the university gradually made the girl forget about the boy. She lived happily every day and even thought of accepting a boy who had pursued her in school for a long time. The boy seemed to be gradually fading away from the girl's life. She no longer thought of him. One night when the girl was preparing to sleep, the phone rang. When she picked up, no one spoke. Just as she was about to hang up, the boy's voice came: "It's me." Even though she hadn't heard his voice for three years, the girl immediately recognized it. She was so excited that tears flowed out... The girl refused the boy who had been pursuing her in school. At the moment the tears fell, the girl realized that it was him she had in her heart all along. Her deep first love had never changed. The girl returned during the summer vacation. The boy hadn't changed much; he still didn't talk much. The boy took the girl to the amusement park by the seaside. On the high Ferris wheel, the girl saw the boy smile for the first time, and it was such a warm smile. The girl's heart was captivated. Looking at the boy, she felt like staying with him forever. The girl liked bumper cars, and the boy accompanied her to play. He said this was too childish, but he still happily played with the girl until she was happy... Like many couples, they held hands and did many happy and blissful things together. Every night, the boy would hold the girl to sleep. He would watch the girl fall asleep, then slowly smoke alone. The girl was actually not sleeping. She didn't want to sleep. She was feeling the love the boy brought her.

The night before leaving, the boy still held the girl in his arms as usual, waiting for her to fall asleep. The girl opened her big eyes and asked: "Am I not beautiful?" The boy smiled and asked why she asked that. The girl said that for a month, he only hugged her without saying anything. The boy looked at the girl for a long time and finally said: "Because I can't bear to let you go..." That night, the girl cried. She knew that he was the person who could love her for a lifetime...

After returning to school, the girl received calls from the boy every day. The boy never said sweet words to coax the girl. The girl often complained that the boy didn't know how to be gentle, but he still wouldn't say anything. Gradually, the girl got used to it and stopped demanding anything. She would just say, "When you tell me you love me, I will marry you..."

July 8th was a day the girl would never forget. That day, she got angry with the boy for no reason and even proposed breaking up. In fact, the girl just wanted to hear the boy say "I love you". The boy still didn't say it, only saying, "I'll call you tonight," and hung up. The girl was furious and decided not to answer his call either. At 12 o'clock, the boy called. The girl had waited for a long time and ultimately answered the phone. The boy seemed to have drunk a lot. The girl asked why he drank so much. The boy didn't speak for a while and then said, "I love you, but I can give you nothing. I am nothing. If you marry me, you will only suffer. I really want to take care of you for a lifetime. I love you, but I don't have the qualifications to love you. My love for you has never changed. I've only ever loved you in my entire life..." The girl couldn't listen anymore. Tears fell like beads onto her slender fingers. The girl told the boy that she wasn't afraid of anything as long as he was with her. "I'll go back tomorrow and we'll get engaged," she said. The girl heard the boy cry... "Ah, wait a minute, hang up. Remember, I love you..." This was the last thing the girl heard the boy say before the call ended. A sense of foreboding flashed through the girl's mind. She kept calling the boy's phone, but no one answered. Again and again, all she heard was the busy tone. The last time, the phone was turned off. An hour later, the boy's brother called the girl and said something happened to the boy... The girl froze, laughing and saying, "Big Brother, you're joking, right? We just talked on the phone. We're going to get married. I'll go back. You're lying!" The girl heard the brother's crying and chaotic voices. The girl screamed and cried helplessly. From afar, the girl was so helpless. She wished she could be by the boy's side. The girl arrived at the hospital where the boy was as quickly as possible the next day. Along the way, the girl prayed to heaven for another chance, even if it was just once, to exchange her life for the boy's. Even if she died, it didn't matter. The family kept calling the girl's phone, but she ultimately didn't see the boy's last face. Looking at the boy's portrait and coffin, the girl walked forward and pounded on the coffin desperately, shouting at the boy, "Why? Why are you like this? Why didn't you keep your promise! Why didn't you wait for me? Tell me! You said you loved me, that you would protect me for a lifetime! That you would never leave me and make me sad or hurt! Why didn't you wait for me? You're not human! You don't keep your promises! Get up! Look at me! I'm back! I'm back..." Everyone pulled the girl back. Her heart-wrenching cries made everyone present shed tears. The girl's small body gradually weakened, and her cries became fainter. Her family took the fainted girl home. When the girl woke up, the boy had already been buried. The girl screamed and cried, wanting to see the boy one last time. Afraid that the girl couldn't withstand such a shock, the family firmly didn't let her go. The girl grabbed the knife on the table and slashed herself fiercely. Blood flowed down her arm. The family didn't dare stop her and let her go, but they chased after her to prevent accidents. The familiar small road that she used to pass every day to school now seemed so long. The girl ran with all her strength, her tears falling on the familiar road, accompanied by the blood dripping from her arm. Finally, the girl saw the boy's familiar face, so serene, but no longer looking at her tenderly. At that moment, the girl's heart broke, the pain unbearable.

The girl developed a condition called aphonia and couldn't speak. Every day, she clung to the clothes the boy wore, which were the couple outfits they bought together. The girl looked forward to the arrival of the night, hoping to see the return of the man she deeply loved in the darkness, because she had heard that after death, some lingering people and events would come back to complete themselves. The girl waited for the boy to come and admit his mistake. In her heart, the girl hated the boy for not keeping his promise and leaving ruthlessly, but the boy never came...

The girl's mother called the boy's friends. It turned out that the place where the boy sat on the embankment and gazed into the distance was for her. Long ago, the boy's eyes only saw the girl. The boy only wore white clothes because the girl casually said she liked seeing the boy in white. In the three years since the girl left, the boy worked hard to earn money just to provide her with a warm home in the future. The boy would play bumper cars alone and laugh foolishly by himself. The boy's shoes were always clean because the girl liked stepping on them. In five years, the boy rejected all the girls introduced by his friends. The boy didn't allow the girl to acknowledge being his girlfriend because he didn't want to involve her. The boy's mobile phone only had one name in it, the name "Wife"... It turned out that the last "wait a minute" was said to the assailant. The girl cried heavily, fainting and crying repeatedly. The girl yearned to hear the boy say "I love you" one more time...

The girl was filled with guilt. If that day she hadn't argued with the boy, if the girl had stayed by the boy's side, if the girl understood the boy better, if the girl cherished the person who loved her, if the boy hadn't drunk so much, if the girl hadn't made the call... maybe the boy would have caught the dagger aimed at him...

Now, the girl's heart still aches so much. She still waits for the night to come...

The girl lived near the boy's house and often saw him but never spoke, as if they were strangers. The girl liked watching the boy from afar. Once, after arguing with her family, the girl ran out in fear. The dark night enveloped her like a devil. Afraid, she ran toward the light and saw the boy drinking with his friends, seemingly having consumed a lot. In the darkness, the boy's eyes shone brightly. He looked at her without speaking. His friends surrounded her and said, "Come, have a drink with us..." The girl was scared and helplessly looked at the boy, hoping he would save her, but the boy didn't move and continued drinking. The girl despairingly looked at the people in front of her. That day, the girl drank a lot of alcohol. When she woke up in the morning, the boy was by her side. She didn't know what had happened and repeatedly asked the boy. One of his friends said, "Nothing happened. We're not as bad as you think. He carried you back." The girl sighed in relief upon seeing her intact clothes. Thus, the girl started dating the boy. The boy didn't allow the girl to acknowledge being his girlfriend, making her feel wronged, but she accepted it silently. Gradually, she got to know his friends, realizing they weren't bad people. On the contrary, the girl thought they were kind-hearted. Slowly, they all liked talking to the girl, and she became part of their group. That year, the girl was 16, and the boy was 17...

Such a life lasted for a year. The girl's family was moving away. The boy didn't try to stop her, only saying, "Study well, I'll wait for you..."

This is a true story. The boy suffered 11 stab wounds and turned off his phone with his last breath. Perhaps he didn't want his beloved to worry about him. To this day, the murderer may escape the death penalty due to connections within the court system. The girl doesn't know what she can do for the boy in the end. She hopes everyone who reads this will share it. Relying on his family's influence in the judicial system, the murderer may escape capital punishment and remain free. The girl's only wish is for her beloved to rest in peace. Repaying a life with a life is self-evident. Those who are moved by this story should cherish the person they love and not realize their love only after losing them...
