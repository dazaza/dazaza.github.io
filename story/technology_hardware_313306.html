Beckham: It's Not Impossible for Milan to Catch Up with Inter, Willing to Accept Any Position in the New Formation

by yinding999 on 2009-12-18 06:49:31

Xiao Bei: Milan surpassing Inter Milan is not impossible, willing to accept any position in the new formation.

At seven o'clock in the evening, the bustling streets of Beverly Hills have become deserted. After sunset, neither the star-chasing tourists nor the enthusiastic vendors selling star maps can be seen. On this smart map indicating the residences of film, TV stars, and sports stars, Beckham's residence on San Isidro Avenue is one of the most clicked addresses. The taxi driven by the special correspondent Olivero of "Milan Sports Daily" was immediately questioned by the security system as soon as it approached the intercom at the entrance to the villa. Originally worried about not being able to enter without an appointment, but after confirming the identity of the visitor, the gates of the Beckham family unexpectedly opened slowly. Close to the entrance path is a garage, with a motorcycle and two Lexuses parked there. There is also a basketball hoop hanging on the wall of the garage. In addition, the luxury vehicles parked on both sides of the path lined with short shrubs constantly attract attention: two black and white Porsches, a Cadillac, a Jeep, and a Land Rover. After walking along the path for about 150 meters, Beckham's house finally comes into view. Beckham and his two eldest sons, Brooklyn and Romeo, are waiting in the hall. Through the glass window of the entrance door of the residence, their figures are still clearly visible.

Walking into the hall, Beckham, dressed in a white T-shirt, black casual pants, and red non-slip socks, smiles warmly. The entrance hall of the villa is paved with mosaic marble floors, and a large wooden long table is fully decorated with various ornaments, candlesticks, and a double-eared large vase. The door on the left side of the hall leads to Beckham's huge kitchen, where three servants are busy preparing dinner under the interested gaze of Beckham's eldest son, Brooklyn, and his third son, Cruz. Victoria, however, is nowhere to be seen. Romeo went to the living room alone to watch a DVD, and a huge LCD TV hung on the wall is playing an old movie by Tom Hanks.

Since it was a sudden visit, the special correspondent of "Milan Sports Daily" obviously couldn't stay too long, especially since it was almost dinner time. However, looking at Beckham's smile, the reporter still asked if he could talk to him about AC Milan.

"Of course," Beckham answered while hugging Brooklyn, who was dressed for summer and trying to run out. Although Los Angeles winters aren't as cold as Milan, the temperature that once reached 17 degrees during the day quickly drops after sunset. As soon as AC Milan was mentioned, Beckham felt warmth in his heart, showing that returning to Italy encouraged him, and his words proved it.

-David, how are you?

-I feel much better now. My ankle is already healed, and I have no physical problems.

-Do you want to restart your journey with AC Milan?

-I am very happy to return to Italy: I can't wait to return to Milan, and I'm counting down the days. There are only two weeks left now.

-Have you talked to Leonardo?

-We often exchange text messages. I think he expressed congratulations, and he is doing very well now, although the team lost their last game to Palermo. We will be able to chat face-to-face soon.

-Are you following AC Milan's games this season?

-Of course, I watched all of AC Milan's games, and I am very clear about what happened in Italy.

-So you know that AC Milan is currently second in Serie A, five points behind Inter Milan. Do you think AC Milan has a chance to win the Serie A championship?

-There is still a long way to go to win the Serie A championship; the team needs to catch up and surpass Inter Milan. But anything is possible. Of course, it won't be easy, but all AC Milan players are very strong, and they are performing exceptionally well now, which makes me believe AC Milan can win the Serie A championship.

-But you always wanted the Champions League trophy more.

-That's normal. The teams competing in the Champions League are the biggest and strongest in Europe, like Real Madrid, Barcelona, Manchester United, and Chelsea. I hope to win the UEFA Champions League trophy because it's a special performance. But traveling across Italian cities to play in the Serie A is also beautiful: I enjoy the Serie A.

-AC Milan might face Manchester United in the round of 16 matches. Would you be happy or sad if that happens?

-I would be very happy if we possibly faced my former team. Since I left Manchester United in 2003, I haven't played at Old Trafford again: it's been seven years, and I really want to step onto Old Trafford again. It would be a great thing if I could face Manchester United, wouldn't it?

-Great but challenging.

-It will definitely be very challenging, but also very exciting. I like big challenges, and I like playing such games.

-Since you are following Serie A and AC Milan, you must know that Leonardo has changed the formation. Can Beckham find a position in the current 4-2-1-3 formation?

-Who knows... I can only say one thing: it doesn't matter whether I understand the position I will play or understand Leonardo's thoughts about me now. I want to be part of AC Milan, and I will accept any position Leonardo assigns me. Whether it's midfield, attacking midfielder, right flank, or even the bench, it doesn't matter. It's not important which position I play, but how I perform in that position. And I want to reiterate: what I want most is to be part of this magical team.

-The World Cup in South Africa will start when this season ends in June next year: do you think England is one of the hopeful teams to win the championship?

-Of course, there is no doubt. England is ambitious, but it's not the only one. Many national teams are as competitive as us. It will be a wonderful World Cup.

-Which team would you like to meet in the final?

-Of course, the Italian team.

So, David, see you in Milan, or even, Johannesburg.

(Garibaldi)
