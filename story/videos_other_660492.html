What should psoriasis patients avoid eating after catching a cold?

Firstly, psoriasis patients should avoid drinking too much tea and cold drinks after catching a cold because drinking strong tea can keep the brain in an excited state, make the pulse quicken, raise blood pressure, thereby increasing the body temperature of psoriasis patients and causing restlessness. At the same time, tea water can also affect the decomposition and absorption of drugs, reducing the efficacy of the drugs. If fever caused by bacterial dysentery or other infectious diseases due to unclean food leads to gastrointestinal function decline, drinking too many cold drinks will worsen the condition of psoriasis patients, aggravate the disease, and even endanger their lives.

Secondly, dietary health is very important for psoriasis patients who have caught a cold. Patients should avoid eating too much spicy food and force-feeding. This is because if the patient's body temperature rises and metabolism becomes more active, eating ginger, garlic, chili peppers, and other warm, spicy foods will exacerbate heat symptoms, worsening the condition and hindering recovery. After catching a cold, eating spicy food not only fails to improve appetite but may also cause nausea and even vomiting or diarrhea, worsening the condition.

Thirdly, psoriasis patients should avoid eating too many eggs and honey after catching a cold. Eggs are rich in nutrients, but they are not suitable for patients to eat during a fever. This is because the protein in eggs generates extra heat when decomposed in the body, raising the body's temperature and prolonging the duration of the fever, increasing the patient's suffering. During a fever, patients should focus on clearing heat rather than nourishment. Consuming too much honey can prevent the internal heat from being properly cleared and eliminated, and it may also lead to other complications.
