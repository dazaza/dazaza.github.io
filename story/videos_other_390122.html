III. Market Research Analysis

1. The 21st century is the digital information age, the Internet era. According to the data published by Taobao, in 2007, Taobao's transaction volume was 43.3 billion yuan, ranking second in China's retail industry after the Bailian Group. Currently, the average daily transaction volume provided by Alipay exceeds close to 3 billion yuan. In contemporary society, with the deepening of China's reform and opening-up, cities are getting bigger, work hours longer, and traffic more congested. Shopping through e-commerce platforms (online shopping) will only deepen and be adopted by more people.

Advantages of Online Stores: Buying a seasonal piece of clothing using traditional methods requires setting aside dedicated time, going to commercial areas, browsing, selecting, trying on, and purchasing. If dissatisfied later, there’s also the hassle of returning it. In this regard, buying the piece of clothing involves not only the price tag but also additional time and transportation costs, which can be exhausting. In contrast, with online shopping, clicking a mouse allows you to complete selection and comparison within half an hour. Although you cannot try it on, everything else is handled, allowing you to wait for the clothes to arrive at your door conveniently, and the price is definitely much cheaper than in malls (because the mall-related property, personnel, advertising, etc., costs are not factored in). This saves both time and money.

At the same time, we can frequently distribute surveys, conduct end-user investigations, understand what services the end-users truly need, and figure out how to promote our brand, always keeping track of market conditions.

2. Target Market Setting: Men's wear, women's wear, and children's wear focus on young consumer groups aged between 20 and 40. These consumers will be our store's largest customer base.

3. Analysis of Product Consumer Groups, Consumption Methods, Consumption Habits, and Major Factors Affecting the Market: With the development of the internet and e-commerce, most young consumers increasingly prefer online shopping because it saves shopping time and provides a large selection of products for customers to choose from, making it very convenient. Modern youth adapts well to online shopping, giving our online store a significant advantage and providing numerous business opportunities. However, the biggest concern about online shopping remains credibility issues. Therefore, our marketing model will involve simultaneous operation of physical stores and online stores, significantly improving the credibility of our online store.

4. Market Trend Forecast and Market Opportunities: After our market research, the proportion of daily necessities purchases has significantly increased. The increasing trend of daily necessities purchases will continue in the future and ultimately become the mainstream of online shopping. Therefore, we have chosen this category as our main product.

IV. Competitive Analysis

1. Industry Monopoly: No industry monopoly

2. Main Competitor Situation: Company strength, product situation (types, prices, features, packaging, marketing, market share, etc.): After investigating several similar product online stores, we found that our competitors are relatively mature, but their business models contain some obvious mistakes. We conducted in-depth research on some consumer groups, and they still worry most about being deceived when shopping online (for consumers, the popularity of clothing items greatly affects their purchase decisions). Through these in-depth understandings, I believe it is very beneficial to the development of our online store. Currently, these online stores have a decent variety of products, but their credibility is not high enough for consumers, and the product popularity is insufficient, which benefits us.

3. Product Competitive Advantage of Our Company: We adopt the simultaneous operation of online and physical stores to enhance the credibility of our online store; furthermore, during the early stages of online store operations, we will vigorously promote advertisements to increase the popularity of our products. On this point, we will collaborate with major clothing manufacturers, sewing our online store labels or logos onto the back collars of each garment we sell and the waistbands of pants. This way, our online store advertising costs will decrease significantly, while simultaneously boosting our store's popularity. Additionally, we provide real-time online consultations to help customers understand our products.

V. Main Strategic Development Goals and Stage Goals of the Online Store

1. Strategic Goal: Make online shopping simpler, more efficient, more trustworthy, and more acceptable to different demographics. Let our customers fully enjoy happy and simple living.

2. Stage Goals: Find reliable and quality-assured sources for our online store, gradually penetrate the market, and develop customers. To boost the popularity of our store in its early stages, appropriate marketing promotions should be carried out, but promoting only online is insufficient; various channels both online and offline should be used together.

3. Progress Schedule:

First stage: Market research, analysis of questionnaires, understanding customer needs;

Second stage: Determining supply sources, developing customers;

Third stage: Opening of the physical store, opening and maintenance of Taobao store;

Fourth stage: Promotion and publicity;

Fifth stage: Online store and physical store entering regular operation;

Sixth stage: Expanding and strengthening the online store;

Ultimate goal: Using the online store to create a globally connected "store" network, i.e., setting up warehouses across the country to deliver customer orders accurately and promptly to specified locations.

VI. Marketing

1. Sales Policy Formulation: Physical store marketing + online marketing. Having a physical store that can be found in real life gives customers a sense of security and reliability when shopping online. At the same time, in the early stages of online store marketing, since advertising costs are relatively high, part of the funds can come from the profits of our physical store sales. When the online store grows big and strong, it can, in turn, positively impact our physical store, thus creating a mutually promotional and mutually publicizing relationship that naturally brings faster economic returns.

Using multiple online stores to sell the same products from one online store, i.e., initially adopting multiple Taobao accounts to uniformly sell our online store's products. By including our brand in multiple Taobao stores, we can naturally increase the popularity of our online store and the awareness of our brand.

2. Sales Channels, Methods, Marketing Links, and After-sales Service: Online sales + physical store sales, focusing on service.

From storefront selection to design, considering differences in region, season, consumption habits, and economic levels comprehensively for distributors. Helping with reasonable inventory allocation, carefully planning every aspect like promotional gifts for opening, comprehensive services, powerful market strategies, advertising promotion, image packaging, and crisis management planning.

① The physical store will be located in a central city area adjacent to the suburbs, ensuring a lower cost while maintaining a steady flow of customers.

② During the early stages of the online store's operation, delivery via express courier will save on logistics expenses. Once the online store grows significantly, the number of packages to be shipped will increase substantially, at which point we will opt for self-operated logistics to reduce expensive courier fees.

③ Warehouses in various regions will stock different products based on local consumption levels and habits.

3. Main Business Relationship Status: Our online store belongs to the retailer category.

Qualification standards policy at all levels: Payment method: Online payment, cash on delivery, etc., available for consumer choice; Freight method: Express delivery and postal services initially, followed by logistics later; Discount policy: Products are already sold at discounted prices to ensure low costs.

4. Sales Team Status and Sales Welfare Distribution Policy: Besides salaries and commissions based on sales volume, we will also allow all employees to own shares in our online store to fully motivate employee enthusiasm.

5. Promotion and Market Penetration (Methods and Schedules, Budget)

① Main promotion methods: Giving small gifts, offering free shipping depending on price, etc., doing everything possible to satisfy the customer.

② Payment methods: Multiple options such as Alipay, online banking, and cash on delivery coexist to enhance user experience and promote sales.

③ Advertising/PR strategy, media evaluation: Adding advertisements on large video websites (e.g., Youku, Tudou, Pipi Video), placing ads on Taobao's homepage, advertising on blogs, and posting promotional plans on Baidu Tieba forums.

Marketing Model Structure:

Self-developed online sales website + online stores (Taobao, EachNet, Paipai) + Blogs (NetEase Blog, Sina Blog, Sohu Blog).

Core Marketing Operation Model:

User Membership System:

Although the membership system is old, it is a core part of marketing online stores, serving as the link between the store and members. The success of the membership system is the standard for evaluating the success of a marketing online store. From a Web 2.0 perspective, designing the membership system aims to activate users, enhance interaction, encourage consumption, and retain users.

① User Initiative: Provide users with a natural and free experience, allowing them to browse products freely and comment or review products.

② User Interaction: Establish a new section on the online store where users can exchange ideas, discuss, and express opinions about clothing styles, colors, fabrics, etc.

③ User Incentive: Implement a points system where all user activities on the online store can earn points. For example, commenting on products, sharing personal maintenance methods with other users, and purchasing products. Points can bring users benefits such as membership level classification, discounts, free gifts on special holidays, etc.

Product Structure Optimization:

① Friendly and Comprehensive Product Display: Present products in a friendly, comprehensive, and clear manner to leave a good first impression before transactions. Due to the complex product systems of online stores, the overall feel can be cluttered. Now, we categorize products in detail, specifying the number of products under each series.

② User-Oriented Product Display: Break down all products according to target user groups, i.e., user-oriented, so users can quickly find suitable products.

③ Intelligent Product Display: Add search functions for products and knowledge, making it convenient for users.

Word-of-Mouth Marketing:

Leverage user promotion to advertise our online store and products. Users recommending others can earn points and discounts. Word-of-mouth is the best form of marketing. Users can promote online and offline word-of-mouth. Online methods include sending our website or product page URLs via QQ/MSN/email to friends; offline methods include introducing our store and products via phone, text messages, and verbal recommendations to family and friends.

Online Store Marketing Model:

Taobao (C2C/B2C)

Fully utilize these online stores to directly attract buyers online, thereby bringing users to the main online store. (The key lies in practice and mastering online store trading skills.)

Blog Promotion Model:

Fully leverage major portal blogs to promote products and online stores. Don't underestimate the power of these blogs; some blog sections have millions of online users daily.

Purpose and Advantages of the Online Store:

Establishing a New Corporate Image: For a lifestyle creative goods enterprise, the corporate brand image is crucial. Especially in today's highly developed Internet technology, most customers learn about corporate products, images, and capabilities through the Internet. Therefore, the image of the corporate online store often determines customer confidence in the company's products. Building an international-standard online store can significantly enhance the overall corporate image.

Optimizing Internal Corporate Management: Building an online store will introduce a new management model within the company, with the online store serving as the platform for this model. It plays a huge role in reducing internal resource waste, cutting costs, enhancing communication and connections among employees and between the company and employees, ultimately achieving maximum optimization of the company's operations and processes.

Enhancing Sales Power: Sales power refers to the manifestation of a product's comprehensive advantages in sales. Modern marketing theory posits that the success of sales depends not only on whether the product's various advantages can be fully communicated but also on how much effective information the target audience receives.

Due to the "one-on-one" nature of the Internet, target audiences can autonomously select useful information, indicating that consumers are already interested in the information presented. This means that information dissemination is no longer subjectively imposed on consumers but rather actively absorbed by them. Simultaneously, through advanced online store design, product information combines the extensive information volume of newspapers with the audio-visual-electric stimulation of television, effectively capturing the attention of the target audience. Therefore, the effectiveness of product information dissemination will be greatly enhanced, thereby increasing the product's sales power.

Increasing Added Value: Many people know that buying a product includes not just visible physical goods but also invisible after-sales services. This is the added value of the product, and the higher the added value, the stronger the competitiveness in the market and the more popular it becomes with consumers. Therefore, companies must strive to increase the added value of their products. Traditional after-sales service methods are no longer sufficient to meet customer needs. Providing convenient, effective, instant 24-hour online services represents a new direction to enhance project added value. Customers worldwide can download required materials, get answers to questions, or submit issues anytime via the online store.

Online Store Goods Promotion Plan

① Discount Promotions: Since online sales cannot give a comprehensive and intuitive impression of products, cannot be tried on or touched, and due to the complexity of delivery costs and payment methods, online shopping and ordering enthusiasm may decline. Large discounts can encourage consumers to attempt online shopping and make purchasing decisions.

② Freebie Promotions: Benefits include increasing your brand recognition; encouraging people to visit the online store frequently for more discount information; and analyzing marketing effects and product reactions based on consumer enthusiasm for freebies. Be cautious not to use substandard or inferior products as freebies, as this could have the opposite effect.

③ Limited-Time Offers: "Limited-Time Offers" are always tempting to consumers, like "Only 2 days left at a discount!" However, limited-time offers must be genuine to avoid losing consumer trust.

④ Establishing More Friend Links with Other Online Stores: As the saying goes, "More friends mean more paths." Online, this translates to "More links mean more windows." Exchanging store links forms a small network, enhancing mutual influence. When someone gets featured on the homepage, everyone benefits. Similarly, when you're prominent, others benefit too. We will collaborate with sister stores, such as gifting small items from sister stores when customers buy from ours, and vice versa, to promote our products.

⑤ Utilizing Other Promotion Methods: Our salespeople can promote our products on various forums, QQ groups, and MSN chats.

VII. Risk Analysis

1. Resource (Raw Materials/Suppliers) Risk

Our online store primarily selects goods meticulously by the owner, integrating fashion, individuality, and quality. Secondly, we aim to include products that can bear our online store label (adding our online store label or logo to the back collar of garments and the waistband of pants). Despite this, our store is committed to providing the best, most exquisite, and beautiful products, aiming to offer the most economical and convenient gift services, specializing in fashionable, personalized, and high-quality clothing. Primarily, selecting the right raw material suppliers and establishing good supply-demand relationships ensures access to the latest trendy goods at low online prices. All products must feature our online store label/logo.

2. Inventory Risks

① Single inventory source not favored by customers.

② Quality issues with goods purchased from wholesale markets that cannot be returned or exchanged.

③ Work errors by inventory personnel.

④ Inaccurate procurement forecasts exceeding budget.

⑤ Accidents during transportation (traffic accidents causing casualties).

3. Market Uncertainty Risks

Market share is an important indicator of a company's successful development. Most of the capital for e-commerce enterprises is used for product procurement and inventory. The quantity of goods must be determined based on market share and sales volume. Analyzing target market selection through market size, current status, trends, network consumer demand, pricing, Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd., marketing strategies, promotional activities, product procurement, inventory management, safety stock levels, etc.

4. R&D Risks

Personal preferences for styles and varieties may not align with customer preferences. Incomplete product ranges and specifications may lead to product accumulation, causing financial crises for the enterprise.

5. Production Uncertainty Risks

Product quality is critical to a company's survival. The superiority or inferiority of quality significantly impacts the enhancement of corporate credibility. One consumer can influence at least 20 other consumers, showing the importance of quality to customers. Merchants bear responsibility for any product quality issues.

6. Inventory Risks

① Accumulated inventory prevents capital circulation, lacking funds for restocking.

② Accumulated inventory occupies warehouse space, preventing the purchase of new goods.

7. Cost Control Risks

Analyzing financial conditions mainly from capital investment, product cost analysis, period costs, product sales profit, projected cash flow statement, projected profit statement, projected balance sheet, main business profit margin, total asset profit statement, cost-expense profit margin, etc.

8. Cash Flow Risks

① Unsold accumulated inventory prevents receiving payments.

② Unable to purchase new goods, leading to outdated products and reduced customer desire to purchase.

③ Unable to pay workers' wages on schedule.

9. Competition Risks

In a competitive era, it's necessary to clearly understand competitors' situations, comparing technical aspects, product prices, services, etc., to formulate competitive strategies, identifying strengths and weaknesses to better adapt to the times.

10. Transaction Security Risks

① Data theft or damage during network transmission.

② Damage during transportation or incomplete cardboard packaging.

11. Policy Risks

In the 21st century, e-commerce has become a technical means and method of economic activity in the information age, entering the broadest application field of the Internet. Economic crises can bring risks, and unpredictable market changes prompt government decisions to address fluctuations. Predict government macro and micro policies.

12. Financial Risks

Since enterprises borrowing funds inevitably carry risks, it's essential to minimize blindness and enhance scientificness at the start. When the pre-tax interest rate on borrowed funds exceeds the interest rate on borrowed funds, profits from using borrowed funds cover interest and leave surplus, thus increasing equity profits. Otherwise, enterprises must repay borrowed funds and interest, risking financial difficulties. Thus, it's crucial to strictly control fixed costs as a percentage of sales revenue. Due to the uncertainty of the difference between pre-tax interest rates on borrowed funds and interest rates on borrowed funds, a reasonable capital structure is needed, maintaining appropriate debt levels, leveraging debt to gain financial benefits, and enhancing profitability.

13. Management Risks (Including Personnel/Employee Turnover/Dependency on Key Employees)

Investment is a business of managing talent. Initially, we build a management team with direct technology and experience, making feasible decisions for the company, executing the most efficient tasks, and inviting professionals with technical expertise and management experience to join and hold important positions. Due to employee turnover, a reasonable hiring criterion is needed to create a sustainable and harmonious environment, controlling emergencies.

14. Bankruptcy Risks

Due to increasingly fierce market competition, lack of management experience, low management quality, single business operations, inability to fully accept our products, and product stagnation in online stores, the risk of bankruptcy is significant. Only by stimulating customers' purchasing desires and making our products unique can we secure a place in the market.

VIII. How to Deal with These Risks

1. Procure from large surrounding wholesale markets. When procuring from wholesale markets, strong bargaining skills are needed to negotiate the lowest possible wholesale prices while building good relationships with wholesalers. Clarify terms regarding exchanges to avoid future disputes. Find products not commonly seen offline or in the market, sourcing the cheapest, high-quality, stable supplies. Instead of relying on a single supplier, seek relatively unique products and design new products for manufacturers to produce.

2. Purchase at extremely low prices and resell online, leveraging online sales advantages to profit from geographical or temporal price differences. Regularly monitor market changes.

3. After purchasing, notify both parties' contact information and transact according to agreed methods, choosing either face-to-face transactions or remittances and mail. Monitor items during transit.

4. Give sufficient attention to customer relations, establish customer contact cards, and widely promote positive customer feedback.

5. Set clear goals for each marketing method, informing customers or potential customers of the next steps.

6. In the information age, use the Internet to regularly send relevant product introductions to customers, keeping them informed about the latest discounts, promotions, and new products.

7. Innovation comes with failure, but continuous exploration minimizes risks, fostering good psychological resilience.

8. Find unique selling points for your online store.

9. Regularly send newsletters to customers. Use the email addresses and contact information provided by customers to regularly send related discount product information, member rewards, and thoughtful birthday gifts for members.

IX. Required Skills

1. Select products, set prices, and target specific consumer groups.

2. Experiment, experiment, and experiment again (as you'll learn many things, accumulating bit by bit).

3. Learn value innovation. Selling products solely based on product features won't generate profits; the money earned will barely cover daily expenses, also known as pocket money.

4. Familiarize yourself with the rules of online trading platforms.

5. Master sales techniques, maintain customer relationships, welcome customers, and operate with integrity. Answer inquiries promptly and honestly, resolving buyer doubts and building trust. Online stores have low costs, require no complicated procedures, and generally offer affordable prices. Some stores use methods like "discounts," "free gifts," and "lotteries" to benefit customers. In online business, credit is crucial; only good credibility can win more stable customers. Some stores use star-rated customers, exchange life experiences, and publish life musings to transform their shops into small communities, turning customers into friends.
