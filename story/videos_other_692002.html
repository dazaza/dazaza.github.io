Citizens Buy "Blood Eggs", Manufacturer Claims Female Chickens Are Having Their Period (Picture)

Core Tip: Recently, Ms. Bai from Qingdao, Shandong Province reported that among two bags of "Insect Eggs" she bought at the supermarket, one egg had a red egg white. On the afternoon of March 4th, a reporter contacted the egg manufacturer, and a responsible person surnamed Zhang said, "If only one or two eggs have this issue, it must be a special case because the female chicken is on her period."

"I bought an egg with red egg white!" Recently, Ms. Bai from Yancheng Road reported to our newspaper that among two bags of "Insect Eggs" she bought at the supermarket, one egg's white was red.

At Ms. Bai's home, the reporter saw that compared to ordinary eggs, the shell of the "Insect Egg" was much redder, and the egg white was red and transparent. Ms. Bai said, "The supermarket people said that the chickens laying these eggs are fed with insects, and the price is higher than ordinary eggs. I paid 14 yuan for a bag of 15 eggs, and now I don't dare to eat the remaining eggs." The reporter then went to the supermarket where Ms. Bai bought the eggs, and the staff claimed, "The red egg white is normal. If it were like ordinary eggs, they wouldn't be insect eggs."

On the afternoon of March 4th, the reporter contacted the egg manufacturer, Qingdao Rensheng Insect Industry Technology Co., Ltd., and a responsible person surnamed Zhang said, "If only one or two eggs have this problem, it must be a special situation because the female chicken is on her period."

Regarding this, Dr. Ji Haifeng, a livestock expert from China Agricultural University, stated that there could be two reasons for blood-colored egg whites: one is due to severe inflammation of the oviduct caused by E. coli infection in the hen, and the second is due to problematic feed being added during the rearing process. Ji Haifeng said, "Female chickens do not have periods. So-called 'insect eggs' mean that during the feeding process, it is impossible to feed the chickens entirely on insects; it is only possible to add insect components to the feed. If you encounter bloody egg whites, it is suggested that citizens do not consume them."

Citizens Buy "Blood Eggs," Experts Advise Not to Eat

We've all seen eggs before, with yellow yolks and clear whites. However, after buying eggs, Ms. Nie from Tongzhou District was shocked when she cracked them open, "I cracked two eggs, and one turned out to be a blood-red, murky liquid!" Regarding this, experts explained that the specific cause of "blood eggs" has not yet been scientifically determined, and they suggest that everyone should not consume them.

Opening the Shell Reveals "Blood Eggs"

Ms. Nie said that yesterday at noon, she wanted to make tomato scrambled eggs. She took "Gegeda" brand eggs from the fridge and cracked two into a bowl. The first egg was normal, but the second one had red egg whites flowing into the bowl. "I've seen red yolk eggs, but this is the first time I've seen an egg with red egg whites. It looks scary, could there be a problem?"

Ms. Nie uploaded pictures of the eggs in the bowl to Weibo, quickly attracting many comments and guesses.

After-sales Personnel Claim Absolute Safety

From the supermarket receipt Ms. Nie kept, the reporter saw that the date of purchasing the eggs was February 12th. The box of 15 eggs clearly marked the production date as February 2nd, 2012, indicating that they were not expired.

Regarding this, the reporter called the Gegeda hotline, and an after-sales staff member explained that environmental stimuli or differences in nutrient absorption could lead to changes in the female chicken's endocrine system, especially during egg-laying. The capillaries in the chicken's oviduct might rupture, causing blood to flow into the ovum, "It may look uncomfortable, but we guarantee absolute safety."

Experts Advise Not to Consume

Professor Tong Qigen from Beijing Agricultural College's Food Science and Engineering Department, who specializes in egg research, said in an interview that the appearance of "blood eggs" could be due to fungal infections in the chicken, resulting in the production of red pigments, or it could be due to ruptured capillaries or inflammation in the chicken's oviduct. Currently, the scientific field has not provided a definitive conclusion regarding this phenomenon, so if anyone encounters blood eggs, they should avoid consuming them.
