Butterflies never stop flying, and this kind of love is unbearable for anyone.

One evening in the second semester of her sophomore year, while she and a female roommate were crossing the road in front of the school, they were hit by a car that was driving illegally. The driver fled after leaving her bleeding on the ground, and her roommate cried helplessly. At that moment, he picked her up and ran to the nearby hospital. Thanks to timely rescue, she escaped danger safely.

Soon, she learned from her roommate that he was an alumnus of the same university as them. His home was in a small village deep in the Taihang Mountains. There were only ten or so households in the village, with no electricity all year round. They had to carry water from a spring 5 kilometers away. Due to insufficient farming, the people in the village were hungry for half a year every year. Both of his parents passed away when he was 12 years old. He was able to enter this university with the help of the villagers.

She did not understand such hardship and difficulty because her father was the mayor of a provincial capital city, and her life had been marked by superiority since birth. She guessed why he, who was a stranger to her, would come to her rescue. Was it out of real selflessness or did he have other intentions?

There was a reason for her to think this way. Because of her special family background, along with her beautiful appearance and outstanding figure and temperament, ever since she entered this university, she had become the target of pursuit by many males. Among the pursuits filled with genuine and fake affection, she calmly refused them all. She understood that in this era where practicality is pursued, love sometimes helplessly falls into being a means to achieve certain purposes. However, the love in her heart was pure and sacred without any impurities.

After being discharged from the hospital, she found him and insisted on inviting him to dinner to express her gratitude, but he simply replied with "nothing" and turned away. His rejection made her feel an unprecedented blow to her self-esteem, and her roommates suggested that she teach him a lesson for not knowing his place.

A week later, during dinner at the cafeteria, she and three of her roommates sat in front of him. Before she could talk to him, one of her roommates said to him: "I noticed that you only eat pickles and steamed buns. If you don't mind, I'll give you these leftovers to improve your meal."

Before he could react, she extended the food box to him. After hesitating for a moment, he took the box, poured the food into his own, said thank you, and started eating. She and the other roommates exchanged glances, then placed their boxes in front of him one after another. After devouring all the food like a storm sweeping through, he stood up, thanked everyone, and left.

Looking at his back, a roommate exclaimed in surprise: "Why does he eat so much? Does he usually get enough food?" She felt a sourness welling up in her heart. That night, she couldn't sleep, and his image kept flashing in her mind.

Encountering someone you truly care about may require enduring wind, frost, snow, and rain for a hundred years, or it may just take a flash of lightning. She felt flowers quietly blooming in her heart. She knew she had fallen in love with him - his strength, his selflessness, his frugality...

Every aspect of him attracted her perfectly. She began to pursue him romantically, but he refused her for various reasons: "If you're just thanking me, this joke has gone too far", "There's too much between you and me, I'm not suitable for you",

"My future is in my hometown's small mountain village, which is very poor. Your future is in the skyscrapers, bright lights, and bustling cities"...

No refusal could stop her persistent pursuit. She was confident that she could win his love, and even change his fate and future. Finally, under her relentless pursuit, their arms intertwined. Although their almost completely different growth backgrounds created many differences between them, under the fusion of love, she naturally accommodated and got closer to him, making everything become enchantingly beautiful.

Time spent in love always passes too quickly. Soon, graduation was approaching, and the two began to argue frequently about their destinations after graduation. She hoped that after graduation, he would go to the city where she grew up with her to create their future together; however, he firmly stated that after graduation, he would return to the depths of his hometown's mountains to become a teacher.

They argued again and again, neither willing to give in.

Finally, the day of graduation arrived, and everywhere was filled with the sorrow of parting. She almost pleaded with him: "From childhood to now, I've always been the treasure in my parents' hands, pampered by those around me. Since meeting you, I've been constantly trying to change myself to adapt to you. I've made so many sacrifices for you, can't you make a compromise for me..."

His expression was desolate, but his tone remained unusually firm: "I promised the elders in my hometown that I would definitely return after graduation. I cannot break my promise." Tears rolled down her cheeks, and she choked out: "We can express our gratitude to the villagers in other ways. Donating money, building schools... I only ask that you stay by my side." His response cut her heart like a knife: "Some things cannot be solved with money. I must return."

The complete disappearance of hope plunged her rationality into despair. She screamed: "I've sacrificed so much for you, yet you won't sacrifice once for me. You don't even love me..."

...

Beautiful love suddenly shattered amidst the persistence of each other's beliefs and the helplessness of reality. He returned to his hometown's small mountain village with his luggage, becoming an ordinary mountain teacher; she returned to the city where she was born and raised, working in a government agency.

From then on, the two were separated, losing contact.

The pale moonlight flickered gently on the water surface, and the purple lavender bloomed year after year. Six years and over 2000 days finally calmed her pain. In October of that year, she finally became the bride of the man who had pursued her for six years. Her husband's love and smooth career brought smiles back to her face. All vicissitudes were past, and life seemed to start anew beautifully. However, about a year after marriage, her husband was suddenly diagnosed with late-stage uremia, and there was only one way to prolong his life: a kidney transplant.

Some things are beyond the power of money. Due to the lack of a suitable kidney source, her husband's life entered a countdown. She rushed around, appealing desperately to save her husband's life: newspapers, TV stations, the internet... The plea spread quickly through various media across their city and further afield. However, her husband's rare RH blood type made her efforts increasingly futile.

One day, the doctor suddenly told her that someone wanted to donate a kidney to her husband. But the donor made a very peculiar request, refusing to reveal his condition to anyone, including not seeing her or her husband. She nodded repeatedly, promising the hospital not to inquire about any information regarding the donor.

The matching was successful, and on the day of the transplant surgery, although she was preoccupied with her husband, she secretly waited near the donor's operating room door. She wanted to see the person who was willing to help her husband unselfishly and express her gratitude. Finally, the operating room door opened, and the cold bed carrying the donor was pushed into a regular ward. She noted the room number and returned to the operating room where her husband was.

Her husband's kidney transplant surgery was very successful, and post-surgery observation and treatment began in a sterile room. That evening, she walked into the donor's ward. There was only one bed in the ward, and the person on it had already awakened from anesthesia. When she saw the donor's face, she was stunned – it was him.

Separated by seven years, she suddenly realized that the flowers she thought had withered in the fleeting years left behind crystal seeds when all the petals scattered. And on this unforeseen day seven years later, these seeds blossomed on both sides of time.

Tears silently filled her eyes as she asked him dazedly: "Why did you transplant your kidney to my husband..." He smiled naively, as if comforting her: "Not all butterflies in love can fly together. Since I can't accompany you in flight, I can help you ensure that the butterfly accompanying you doesn't have its wings broken by misfortune..."

Silently loving someone in your heart requires no return. Falling in love with someone is a blessing from heaven. I am moved, cherish, pay attention from afar, bless, and elevate myself. Although I have to endure the unbearable mental torment, the scorching of longing, and the tearing of loneliness, having love in my heart gives vitality to my life and awakens my soul – Butterflies never stop flying!
