VoIP Network Phone and Traditional Phone Principle Introduction:

VoIP network phone services have achieved a significant breakthrough and technological revolution in the field of telephone communication. VoIP network phones, also known as IP phones, transmit voice through the Internet Protocol (Internet Protocol). Traditional international calls transmit analog signals, converting voice into signals and transmitting them through copper cables to the other party. Network phones convert sound through a gateway (gateway) into data signals, compressing them into data packets (packet), then sending them out via the Internet. When the receiving end receives the data packet, the gateway decompresses it and converts it back into sound for the other party to listen to.
