Don't you want the rabbit in this article to turn into a little deer?

by uggboots1m on 2010-03-29 15:51:34

A rabbit ate the wolf and wild boar. One day, a rabbit was writing an article in front of a cave when a wolf came over and asked: "Little rabbit, what are you doing?" The rabbit replied: "Writing an article." The wolf then asked: "What's the title?" The rabbit said: "A Brief Discussion on How Rabbits Eat Wolves." The wolf laughed loudly and said he didn't believe it. So the rabbit led the wolf into the cave.

After a while, the rabbit walked out of the cave alone and continued writing. A wild boar came over and asked: "Rabbit, what are you writing?" The rabbit answered: "An article." The boar then asked: "What's the title?" The rabbit said: "A Brief Discussion on How Rabbits Eat Wild Boars." The wild boar didn't believe it, so the same thing happened again.

Finally, inside the cave, a lion was sitting among a pile of bones, satisfied with reading an article given by the rabbit. The title was: "An Animal's Ability Depends on Who Their Boss Is."

One day, this rabbit accidentally told one of his rabbit friends about this, and the news gradually spread throughout the forest.

When the lion found out, he got very angry and told the rabbit: "If no food enters the cave this week, I will eat you." So the rabbit continued writing at the entrance of the cave.

A young deer came over: "Rabbit, what are you doing?" The rabbit replied: "Writing an article." The deer then asked: "What's the title?" The rabbit said: "A Brief Discussion on How Rabbits Eat Wolves." The deer laughed and said that everyone in the forest knew about it, and he wouldn't be fooled to go inside.

The rabbit said: "I'm retiring soon, and the lion wants to find someone to replace me. Don't you want the rabbit in this article to become a deer?"

The deer thought for a moment and couldn't resist the temptation, so he followed the rabbit into the cave.

After a while, the rabbit walked out of the cave alone and continued writing. Then a young horse came over, and the same thing happened again.

Finally, inside the cave, a lion was sitting among a pile of bones, satisfied with reading an article given by the rabbit. The title was: "How to Develop Subordinate Animals to Provide Food for the Boss."

As time went by, the lion grew bigger and bigger, and the rabbit's food was far from enough to fill its stomach. One day, the lion told the rabbit: "My food intake needs to double. For example, instead of one deer every four days, now it's one every two days. If the situation doesn't change within a week, I'll eat you."

So the rabbit left the cave and ran deep into the forest. He met a wolf: "Do you believe a rabbit can easily eat a wolf?" The wolf laughed loudly and said he didn't believe it. So the rabbit led the wolf into the cave.

After a while, the rabbit walked out of the cave alone and continued deeper into the forest. This time he encountered a wild boar: "Do you believe a rabbit can easily eat a wild boar?" The wild boar didn't believe it, so the same thing happened again.

It turned out that the animals deep in the forest didn't know the story between the rabbit and the lion.

Finally, inside the cave, a lion was sitting among a pile of bones, satisfied with reading an article given by the rabbit. The title was: "How to Transition from a Sit-merchant to a Traveling Merchant to Provide More Food for the Boss."

Time flew by quickly, and soon the rabbit became famous in the forest because everyone knew it had a powerful boss.

This little rabbit started to act arrogantly, bullying others, and no animal dared to offend it. It often remembered the humiliation of racing against the turtle.

It found the turtle and said: "See my boss in three days!" Then it left triumphantly. The turtle cried sadly.

At this moment, a hunter appeared. The turtle told him the story, and the hunter laughed loudly. Then something significant happened in the forest - the hunter, dressed as a lion, was eating rabbit hotpot with the turtle. On the ground, there was half a piece of paper with the messy words written: "There is always a higher mountain outside the green mountains, and a stronger person beyond the strong ones!"

For a long time, peace returned to the forest, and the story of the rabbit eating wolves seemed to be forgotten by everyone.

However, after hearing this story, a young tiger was inspired. So he caught an antelope and said to the antelope: "If you can bring me food like the rabbit did before, I won't eat you."

Reluctantly, the antelope agreed, and the tiger leisurely entered the cave. However, after three days, no animal was brought into the cave by the antelope. Unable to hold back his impatience, the tiger decided to come out and check the situation.

The antelope was nowhere to be seen, which made the tiger extremely angry. Just as he was fuming, he suddenly noticed an article written by the antelope. The title was: "To Be a Good Boss, You Must First Know How to Retain Your Employees."
