At that moment, I saw an angel③

by lk0gamef on 2009-07-18 00:05:59

Not long ago, our dormitory got a mirror, and the dusty comb finally came in handy. We took a bath every two days, combed our hair every day, fiddled with ourselves in front of the mirror for a while, and then headed to the Internet cafe satisfied. In order to play with Henny, we all endured the torture brought by cigarettes because she didn't smoke and even hated it. So when we really craved smoking, we would sneak into the toilet and have a few hearty puffs. Actually, it was self-inflicted suffering. Although she never said she minded guys smoking, we still followed the rules to win her good impression, acting like grandsons.

For a girl who doesn't drink or smoke, we call her a lady. However, even ladies can say shocking things sometimes. Once, I, Old Yo, Huanhuan, Old Thief, and Henny were sitting together roasting pigs. When we were happily luring pigs, roasting monsters, chatting, and playing around, Henny suddenly said: "Wait a moment, I'm going to poop..." The volume wasn't too high or low, but everyone within ten meters heard it clearly. Old Thief immediately sprayed water all over the screen that he had in his mouth. Even Brother Chu Liuxiang, who we thought was always steady, was so shocked that he kept digging in his ears: "Did I hear wrong?" But Henny seemed completely unconcerned and walked towards the toilet leaving us all in shock.

At this time, some friends in the game asked: "Why isn't Henny luring monsters anymore?" I said: "She's pooping..." That mage scolded: "You're disgusting, I'm eating cake!" Old Thief joked: "Still eating cake? Eat poop instead." Since then, all of us at the Internet cafe developed a habit. Instead of greeting each other with "Have you eaten?", we would say "Have you pooped today?" Even Brother Chu, who was mature, experienced, and elegant, often joined us in teasing: "Let's eat first, I'll poop first!"

Rising with the sun and resting with its setting, besides obligatorily dozing off in class listening to the teacher every day, the rest of my time was spent at the Internet cafe as if it were home. I was addicted to this game, addicted to the characters in the game. Unfortunately, I was just a shadow beside her. She lived in the spotlight, surrounded by numerous admirers and some unknown infatuated ones. Even some gaming amateurs ran to mix legends for her. The Internet cafe owner secretly recharged 100 yuan more on her membership card each time, saying she brought economic benefits. Gradually, I found our group getting bigger. She and Brother Chu simultaneously sat in the leader's position, and thus she became busier. Pacifying internal conflicts in the guild, recruiting members, organizing activities, there was always something to do. At that time, accompanying her to level up was an unreachable dream for me because a senior mage was always bringing her to roast pigs. Every time, she politely told me: "Someone is bringing me, you go ahead and play."

I went to Woma alone, tediously slaying monsters. Every time, I trained a level three pet so I could save some effort and let the pet fight the monsters while I could indulge in some thoughts. The most restless one was definitely Old Thief. This guy, with seemingly endless nonsense, always liked to shout in the guild: "What's Shadow doing?" I said: "Leveling up in Woma." He shouted again: "Where's Henny?" She said: "Roasting pigs..." He shouted again: "I'm at computer number one, there's a spot next to me, who's coming to accompany me?" Old Yo cursed: "Go away..." After about two minutes of quiet, this guy started bothering again:

"Where are you, Shadow?"

"I'm leveling up in Woma..."

"What are you doing, Henny?"

"I'm roasting pigs..."

"To be exact, what are you doing, Shadow?"

"I'm screwing your ancestors..."

Buddhism says, when tolerance reaches its limit, no need to endure further. But this guy continued as if nothing could bother him, continuing to say: "Henny, I miss you..." What a shocking statement! All of Henny's "fans" in the guild were furious and cursed: "Idiot..." But Henny lightly said: "Thank you for missing me..." What kind of logic is this? No one knew, probably it was a very decent way to reject someone. I immediately deleted the words "Henny, I miss you too!" that I had typed in the chat box because I feared hearing the phrase "thank you for missing me". I didn't have Old Thief's thick skin or enough courage. Actually, seeing her smile every day was enough for me.

Before we knew it, we had been getting along harmoniously for over a month. That day was Brother Chu's birthday, and our friends who often played Legend together decided to have a meal together. Because Brother Chu had many friends and was socially active, some celebrities from the server also came that time. These small figures in the game were now standing right in front of us. Sometimes I felt that the internet and reality were not far apart. The only thing that annoyed us was that after those so-called celebrities saw Henny, their eyes went straight. Each of them said like green-headed flies: "Wow, such a beauty, Old Xu is really lucky to have such a pretty wife..." Before they finished speaking, Old Thief slapped the table and yelled: "What the hell are you talking about? What crap Old Xu, what crap wife?" The scene became somewhat awkward. Brother Chu seemed unaware of the situation and quickly tried to smooth things over: "What's going on? Who's Old Xu?" That person looked confused and said: "Old Xu is Yun Feixiang, isn't Henny his wife?" It turned out to be Yun Feixiang, that senior mage who often brought Henny to roast pigs. He had status, reputation, and equipment. In short, mentioning this person, many people knew him. He often introduced Henny as his wife in his friend circle, and Henny accepted it. Maybe being together for a long time could lead to feelings. Henny also got angry and said: "Old Thief, what do you mean? He didn't say anything wrong, after all, he's a guest. Besides, today is Brother Chu's birthday, how can you act like this?" Old Thief said helplessly: "When did you get a husband, why don't I know?" Henny said: "You didn't ask, why should I tell you these things..." Old Thief raised a glass of wine and said to Brother Chu: "Big Brother, I wish you a happy birthday, I have to leave early..." Then he drank the wine in one gulp and returned to the dormitory. I also raised my glass of wine and said to Brother Chu: "Happy birthday, you keep drinking, I'll go accompany him." After finishing the wine, I left the restaurant without looking back. Walking on the street, I realized my heart was slowly bleeding...
