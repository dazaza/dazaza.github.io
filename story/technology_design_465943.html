Seven robbers stole a huge amount of money on the streets of Hong Kong. Two people were seriously injured while fighting to protect the money.

by jyq2011668 on 2011-07-26 10:31:04

The injured owner was sent to the hospital for treatment.

The police closed off the scene afterwards for investigation.

According to a report on July 26 by China News Service citing Hong Kong's Wen Wei Po, a near-miss of a major street robbery occurred in Kansu Street, Tsim Sha Tsui, Hong Kong on the 25th. Three Indian employees of a trading company were escorting HK$5.1 million in cash to the bank when they were attacked by seven South Asian robbers who threw chili powder and physically assaulted them to attempt the robbery. Two owners fought to protect the money despite being injured, managing to throw the money bag to their colleague who drove away from the scene, ensuring that the large sum was not lost. The robbers fled quickly out of fear of failure.

Since the robbers seemed to have knowledge of the timing and route of the delivery, an insider might be involved. The serious case team of Yau Tsim District has taken over for further investigation, with no arrests made yet. The Indian owner of the victimized trading company expressed that they would consider hiring a security company for future cash escorts to reduce the risk of robbery.

The two injured Indian employees, named Akash, 22 years old, and Hundal, 27 years old, were both hurt during the incident. Akash suffered head injuries but was treated at the hospital with no major issues; Hundal was beaten on his abdomen but refused hospitalization after being examined on-site. It is reported that these two are employees of a trading company located in New Harbor Centre, Tsim Sha Tsui, which operates a mobile phone business owned by Indians. According to the owner, due to the bank holidays last Saturday and Sunday, the company had accumulated over HK$5 million in cash, which was divided into two bags yesterday morning. They were escorted by a 35-year-old driver named Singh, an accounting manager, and an assistant, who transported the money to a bank at No. 8 Kansu Street for deposit.

According to the scene reports, at 11:50 AM yesterday, as the Mercedes-Benz car carrying the three individuals arrived at the building of Gran Centre, the accounting assistant just got out of the car when suddenly two South Asian men approached and threw chili powder into his eyes while attempting to snatch the money bag. Fortunately, the owner managed to evade it in time, and the headscarf partially blocked the eyes from being fully affected. Upon seeing this, the accounting manager sitting in the backseat immediately exited the vehicle to provide assistance, only to be surrounded and beaten by five more South Asian men from the roadside. In the chaos, the accounting manager sustained a head injury but still managed to throw the money bag back into the car before the driver sped away and called the police. Realizing their failure, the robbers scattered and fled.

A large number of police officers rushed to the scene upon receiving the report, sending the head-injured owner to the hospital and dispatching personnel to search for the robbers nearby, unfortunately without success. On the ground at the scene, a pile of red chili powder was left behind. Across the road, along the alley leading from Gran Centre to Canada Square, a wrapped iron pipe with newspapers and tape was also found.

The Indian owner of the company that narrowly avoided losing a large sum of money stated afterward that it was customary for company staff to handle cash escorts. This was not the first time the company had been robbed; a few years ago, a batch of mobile phones worth US$66,000 was stolen right outside the company. To avoid future incidents, they would consider hiring a security company for cash escorts in the future.
