Born as a waste, I freely walk in a different world. Continuing the story of the woman who donated a kidney to save her husband and later suffered from stomach cancer: Her son works hard to save his mother.

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-03 10:53:39

Zhu Ai Yun is frail and emaciated, and at this moment, she needs everyone's help the most. The neighbors nearby often come to help Zhang De Qian take care of his wife. Their son works hard, hoping for nothing but his mother's recovery. On the cabinet in the bedroom, there are plenty of medications that Zhang De Qian needs to take. In 2009, Fei Xi resident Zhang De Qian was diagnosed with uremia, and his wife Zhu Ai Yun voluntarily donated her kidney to her husband. After Zhang De Qian recovered from his illness, three thieves returned the stolen goods to the owner along with a letter of repentance.

This year, Zhu Ai Yun was diagnosed with stomach cancer, and Zhang De Qian earnestly pleaded with the doctor, "If I give her my entire stomach, would it work?"... Yesterday's A04 edition of our newspaper published an article titled "Can I Give Her My Entire Stomach?", reporting on the touching sincerity of this couple during their hardships, moving many readers.

It has been a month since Zhu Ai Yun was diagnosed with stomach cancer, and aside from chemotherapy, there is no other method of treatment. She is currently relying on folk herbal remedies to sustain her life.

"Unfortunately, just a few months later, I also fell ill and can't even get out of bed," Zhu Ai Yun murmured while lying in her bed, two streams of clear tears rolling onto her pillow. "The doctor told me to move around even when lying down, otherwise, I might develop intestinal adhesions, but every movement causes pain in my abdomen and back. This long illness has dragged down our children too; it will be hard to find a partner now. Now we even have a 70-year-old grandmother fetching water for me every day, and my heart feels like it's being pierced by needles."

Neighbor Grandma Becomes the "Nanny" - "This Family Is Full of Hardship"

The elderly lady mentioned by Zhu Ai Yun is Aunt Zhang, a neighbor. At Zhang De Qian's doorstep, Aunt Zhang tightly held the reporter's hand, repeatedly saying, "You've come at such a good time. Everyone in this family is full of hardship. We are powerless, but your arrival brings hope."

"I saw a long scar on her body when I gave her a bath; she's so thin..." Although Aunt Zhang has no blood relation to the Zhang family, the suffering and love of the couple deeply moved this grandmother over seventy years old. Speaking about this family, Aunt Zhang couldn't help but shed tears.

During Zhang De Qian's absence, Aunt Zhang took on the responsibility of taking care of Zhu Ai Yun. "This little bit of rice porridge, made yesterday, I add some lard and feed it to her." Aunt Zhang lifted the pot cover and took out the remaining small amount of white rice porridge. There were several fresh strings of cured meat on the stove at home, all given by neighbors. Zhu Ai Yun cannot eat them, and Zhang De Qian is also reluctant to eat them. "Sometimes she can't even drink the porridge, only able to sip the thin liquid on top," Aunt Zhang pulled the reporter around the house, wiping away tears as she told the reporter, "They are truly at a dead end. If you don't help them, no one else will."

"I heard that one session of chemotherapy costs ten thousand yuan, oh my..." Aunt Zhang lowered her voice, telling the reporter that she also helped conceal Zhu Ai Yun's condition. "You must not say the wrong thing. She doesn't know yet, and we dare not tell her." After saying this, she hurriedly prepared the decocted Chinese medicine and fed it to Zhu Ai Yun.

Son Works to Support His Parents - Earning 1700 Yuan, He Gives 1600 Yuan to His Family

Due to Zhang De Qian and Zhu Ai Yun both having serious illnesses, their two children left school early and came to Hefei to work.

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter met Xiao Zhang, who works as a prep cook in a Hefei restaurant. The 20-year-old Xiao Zhang looks quite refined, resembling his mother Zhu Ai Yun somewhat. Every morning at 9 o'clock, he starts working until 2:30 PM, takes a short break, then resumes work at 4 PM until 9:30 PM. "I get two mornings off each month and can learn skills from the master," Xiao Zhang has persisted for a year despite such hardships.

"This child, though young, seems to bear a lot inside, keeping everything bottled up without speaking." Chef Xu, Xiao Zhang's mentor for a year, sees Xiao Zhang as more mature than others of the same age. "We used to not be at this restaurant; the previous place closed down, so I brought him here. This child has been with me for over a year, works steadily, and loves to research and think things through on his own."

"I've never seen him spend money on himself." Chef Xu feels very sorry for Xiao Zhang. "Do you know, as soon as he got his salary last month, he handed it all to his family." Chef Xu told the reporter that Xiao Zhang once gave 1600 yuan to his family, while his monthly salary was only 1700 yuan. "This child is too understanding. We all know about his family's situation, but he rarely talks about it, and neither do we."

"I read your report today. We can only offer limited help, so I hope the media can assist this family more; they are too pitiful, too pitiful." Chef Xu repeatedly entrusted the reporter.

Cooking Several Dishes for Parents During the New Year

"I've learned a few dishes from my master, such as sour cabbage fish and fresh-poured white throat. This year during the Spring Festival, I'll cook these dishes for them as part of the New Year's Eve dinner," Xiao Zhang said. In previous years, it was always his parents who cooked, but this year he wants to show off his newly acquired culinary skills and let his parents taste them.

As for what specific dishes to prepare, Xiao Zhang scratched his head, "I haven't thought about it yet. Spicy food isn't suitable for Mom, so I'll definitely prepare more light dishes for her."

"I won't buy any other gifts. Mom doesn't let me buy things for her." Xiao Zhang said, recalling when he was still in school, on one occasion, he secretly saved his lunch money for three days and bought a bouquet of carnations for his mother's birthday. "She was angry, saying I had money to waste, and she forbade me from buying her anything ever since."

"Actually, I don't even know what my parents like. Since I was little, they've been reluctant to spend money, always saving the best for me."

"All I wish for is to have enough money to treat my parents' illnesses, hoping they get well soon." Xiao Zhang told the reporter that he has no other wishes, only hoping for a miracle to happen to his family and help his parents.

"Please Save My Mom"

"Save my mom, she is the greatest mom ever." Talking about his parents, Xiao Zhang's tears streamed down. "Since I was little, my parents have spoiled me. My mom wouldn't eat anything herself, always saving the best for me."

"And my dad, he is a very responsible person, driving all night for years." Xiao Zhang said, "I was pretty naive when I was little, but now that I'm working, I understand their hardships."

"I know many people have helped my parents, including my aunts and my mom's colleague Aunt Shi." Knowing that many people are paying attention to his family and want to help his parents, Xiao Zhang quietly told the reporter, hoping to raise the money soon to let his mom see a doctor as soon as possible. "Thank you."

What This Family Needs Most Is Money

What this family lacks right now is money.

They already owe 320,000 yuan in debt and can no longer afford the chemotherapy expenses of at least eight or nine thousand yuan per month.

And the story of this couple in adversity has touched many hearts.

Once the report was published, it immediately received attention from all sectors of society. Major portal sites such as Sina, Sohu, Yahoo, etc., have reprinted it. As of last night's press time, the article on Sina Weibo had been forwarded more than 3,657 times and commented on more than 1,201 times.

Many enthusiastic netizens have left messages or privately messaged our newspaper, requesting to organize donations or publish donation accounts. Netizen @Ai Yong Heng sent a private message to the journalist at 16:55 yesterday, expressing the desire to contribute a little to this couple. "I donate money every month through various channels. Although it's not much, it's my heartfelt contribution."

An editor from the magazine "Zhizhen" also contacted this reporter, expressing the hope to reprint this story and help the couple through the power of the magazine.
