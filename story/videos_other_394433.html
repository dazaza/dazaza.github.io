67-year-old Grandpa Successfully Opens an Online Store, Becomes a Hit Across the Web

by anonymous on 2011-03-28 10:26:21

While you are sighing because of the lack of business in your Taobao shop, please come and take a look at this post..

The spirit of Grandpa Chao is admirable. Whether his shop is doing well or not is secondary; it's his fighting spirit that we should learn from.

Go to Baidu and type in "Jiang Shihong", and you will see special reports on Grandpa Chao opening a Taobao shop on various websites.

At the age of 67, can he still open an online store? Not only did he open one, but he also took over the "mess" left by his 30-year-old son. So, how has he been doing after six months?

Reasons for the Son's Failure

Son Jiang Ke said that when he was running the online store, he would just put the products online without decorating the shop or being willing to spend time learning. With no increase in sales, he gave up.

Grandpa's Success Secrets

Grandpa Jiang Shihong not only researches diligently but also has great patience when communicating with customers.

Unlike his son who simply answers "no way" or "okay", Jiang Shihong patiently explains why to the customers. He also takes the initiative to chat with buyers, asking them what they do and how many people are in their families, and gradually becomes friends with them.

Grandpa Opens Online Store

Son Fails, Seeks Help from Father

At 10 o'clock yesterday morning, at No. 39 Beshu Street, Building 4, Room 7-1, Héchuān District, Jiang Shihong's Taobao Wangwang was flashing non-stop. "Shopkeeper, you're already 67 years old? I can call you grandpa," ID QQRI sent a surprised expression. Jiang Shihong laughed as he replied, "If you're from the post-80s generation, you can indeed call me grandpa. Is there anything I can help you with?" "Grandpa Jiang, isn't managing an online store tiring?" QQRI browsed into Jiang Shihong's online store, not buying any product but instead started chatting.

Jiang Shihong is 67 years old. Before retiring, he was a staff member of the Heyang Town Government in Héchuān District. On Taobao, he opened an online store called Beauty E Home Furnishing Shop, mainly selling accessories for clothing racks. Every day, he spends eight hours online receiving buyers. He posted his personal information in his store, attracting the curiosity of many buyers.

"Perhaps because buyers think such a store owner is rare, they all like to chat with me," Jiang Shihong said. Since opening the store, many buyers have actively chatted with him, and some have even added him as QQ friends.

"Buying things from Grandpa Jiang is very reassuring," said customer Su Yan from Changzhou, Jiangsu Province. Jiang Shihong always patiently explained things to her, providing thoughtful service. Now they have added each other as QQ friends and become good friends.

Currently, Jiang Shihong's online store has completed 66 transactions, selling more than 5,000 yuan worth of goods per month, with a monthly net profit of more than 1,000 yuan. "To achieve such results is not easy," Jiang Shihong said. The online store has been opened three times. The first two times were mainly operated by his son, but both ended in failure. This time, he tried operating it himself, and unexpectedly, it worked well. Next, he hopes to become a crown-level seller.

Operating an online store should be the forte of young people, so how could this elder surpass them?

Son Fails, Seeks Help from Father

In fact, it was the son's failure that made him succeed.

Jiang Shihong's son, Jiang Ke, works at the Colorful Laser Digital Printing Center in Héchuān District and enjoys online shopping. "Seeing others do well online, I wanted to try too," Jiang Ke thought. As digital photos became popular, he could handle the printing of digital photos online. On September 13, 2008, he registered a Taobao store, taking orders online for printing five-inch photos at 50 cents each.

"This price was high compared to peers, resulting in few orders," Jiang Ke said. Not only were there few orders, but the profit per photo was less than ten cents. After two months, the store closed.

After closing, Jiang Ke started selling Héchuān peach slices, Héchuān meat slices, and pickled chicken feet. However, these products hung on the online store for two months without a single customer inquiry...

Last September, Jiang Ke contacted a manufacturer of clothing rack accessories through acquaintances. "I still want to sell online, contact the buyers, and the manufacturer directly ships the goods, so I don't need to stock up." But remembering his previous failures, Jiang Ke decided to seek help, thinking of his father who could use computers, take photos, and use PS.

"I'm old, I don't have such skills," Jiang Shihong declined repeatedly after hearing about it. But unable to resist his son's persistent persuasion, Jiang Shihong reluctantly agreed.

Start Learning and Start Over

Since he had promised his son, he couldn't be careless. Before opening the store, Jiang Shihong bought books about opening Taobao stores and followed the steps in the book, learning bit by bit. When encountering difficulties, he would call his son for advice or find teachers on "Baidu Zhidao". More often, he would go to Taobao University to learn the secrets of opening stores shared by netizens.

After a week of hard work, Jiang Shihong finally figured out how to list products on the online store. "Seeing that all the items listed on the online store were done by myself, I felt indescribably happy," Jiang Shihong said. Encouraged by his success, he immediately decorated the homepage of the store. After five days of effort, the store looked completely renewed. His wife, He Qingxiu, seeing him sitting silently in front of the computer all day, always worriedly said, "Go out and walk around," but he wouldn't listen.

Just hanging products on the online store wasn't enough. Jiang Shihong saw that clicking on a certain picture in other stores could instantly display multiple product pictures. "I was very envious," Jiang Shihong then tried to create hyperlinks. It looked easy but was difficult to do. After struggling for three or four days and still failing, he accidentally found a post about making links on Taobao University. He was overjoyed, reading it twice, and then following the steps carefully until he broke through this obstacle.

Jiang Shihong types slowly, and if customers ask a few more questions, he gets confused. Later, he thought of categorizing the common questions asked by customers. When customers consulted, he copied the relevant lines to the Wangwang chat window, calmly handling inquiries from multiple customers.

Gradually, his business began to improve.

However, the trendy Jiang Shihong still has a "old-fashioned" side: he uses a computer to sell goods but counts money with an abacus. He said he was used to using the abacus and found calculators not as convenient.

"It's rare for an elderly person to be enthusiastic about doing online stores," said Xiaoqiao, a Taobao staff member yesterday. And someone like Jiang Shihong who does it well is even rarer.

An Elder Who Can't Stay Idle

"The old man has been busy all his life," said his wife He Qingxiu. Jiang Shihong loved keeping up with trends when he was young. When TVs first came out, most people couldn't afford them, but he built one himself. He also assembled radios, learned sewing to make clothes, photography, painting, and typing online. In 1998, he spent 10,000 yuan to buy a computer and surfed the internet daily to read news and learn graphic design.

When talking about Jiang Shihong, his old colleague Wei Fuzhong gave him a thumbs up, "Among people our age, no one likes to 'trouble' themselves like him." Wei Fuzhong said that the elderly around him either play mahjong or go fishing, and few keep learning like Jiang Shihong. Moreover, after opening the online store, Jiang Shihong's relationship with his son improved. Previously, despite living together, the two generations had little common language. Now, the father and son have endless topics to discuss: what was sold today, which customer was interesting, and how the online store can be improved further. "These changes in life are envied by peers."
