Whose smile makes passers-by admire; whose sweet waiting makes others fantasize; and whose warm care makes friends fall in love?

There are always some people who seem never to know what sorrow is, as if they are always favored by the goddess of luck. They always give generous advice when others are unhappy, using their cheerfulness and even some foolish smiles to infect them.

Reasons at home, or the object of affection, or even the reason of friends, there are always reasons in life that make people upset and sad. Repeated over and over, after N times, you can only submit to that sentence: These things, only oneself knows, that's all.

Just be happy. Maybe it's the worst answer in the world.

When the hurt has seeped deep into the bone, you may think of finding a close friend to chat for a while, even for a short while. But almost every time, it's half-spit and half-swallowed. Well, well, others can't understand, so don't add to your friend's sadness in vain. Always, always deeply cover up your own sadness. Until one day, a friend around you asks quietly: Why are you always so happy?
