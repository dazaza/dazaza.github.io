Product Description:

The Hai Zhi Hospital Management System includes the following functional modules: Outpatient Pricing and Billing System, Pharmaceutical Warehouse Management System, Inpatient Management System, Dean's Decision Support System, Outpatient Electronic Medical Records, Maintenance Management System, Doctor-Patient Management, Membership Management, Logistics Material Management, Medical Order Management, Financial Management, etc.

Outpatient Pricing and Billing System - Function Introduction:

This module mainly includes outpatient pricing, outpatient billing, billing audit, refund, prescription return, re-printing of receipts, arrears clearance, prescription inquiry, billing inquiry, etc.

1. Classify prescriptions by Western medicine, traditional Chinese medicine (patent), Chinese herbal medicine, medical supplies, calculate the cost of the prescription, and generate a prescription number for billing use.

2. Outpatient billing - including prescription billing and other project billing, print invoices, calculate responsible income, self-payment amount, etc.

3. Cash deposit input - realize the cash deposit input of the billing staff, thereby checking the income of the billing staff and the financial income.

4. Audit billing staff - audit the cash deposit and actual cash income of the billing staff, thus querying the actual income situation and deposit situation of the billing staff.

5. Daily report of revenue staff - statistics on the daily income situation of the billing staff, print reports to be submitted to finance.

6. Return prescription form - according to the prescription number, realize the function of returning the prescription form. If the prescription has already been dispensed, the inventory of the corresponding drugs in the pharmacy should also be updated.

7. Refund - according to the patient's invoice, implement refunds. If the fee involves a prescription, a prescription return operation must also be performed.

8. Reprint receipt - reprint receipt according to the receipt number.

9. Clear arrears - clear overdue payments for patients.

10. Outpatient pharmacy checkout (dispensing) - based on undispensed prescription forms, realize the function of dispensing from the outpatient pharmacy. It can either process all undispensed prescriptions at once or select individual prescriptions for dispensing.

11. Query prescriptions by doctor or pharmacy, query prescriptions based on payment status (already dispensed or not yet dispensed), and statistics on drug consumption in the pharmacy within any time period.

12. Billing inquiry - according to a certain time period, query outpatient revenue by cashier, department, doctor, and various billing items, including details and statistics.

13. Medical account fee management - realize the input of pre-paid fees for patients with medical accounts, query patient expenses, and statistics on the fees of the patient's organization.

14. Refund inquiry - query refund details by cashier.

15. Three-in-one for pricing, billing, and dispensing - based on some hospitals' business processes, it is necessary to simultaneously realize outpatient pricing, billing, and dispensing functions. The three-in-one feature handles such business operations.

Pharmaceutical Warehouse Management System - Function Introduction:

1. Handle normal drug entry and exit operations, realize drug entry and exit operations according to different types of entries/exits.

2. Query detailed entries into the pharmaceutical warehouse - you can query the income details of a specific pharmaceutical warehouse or all warehouses, as well as the entry details of a particular drug.

3. Query detailed exits from the pharmaceutical warehouse - you can query the outgoing details of a specific pharmaceutical warehouse or all warehouses, as well as the outgoing details of a particular drug.

4. Statistically analyze drug entry and exit situations over any time period - you can statistically analyze the entry and exit situations of a specified trading partner or the entry and exit situations of a particular drug across all selected categories, e.g., analyzing the entry situation of a specific drug from all suppliers.

5. Query drug inventory in the pharmaceutical warehouse - you can set up the inventory of drugs for a specific trading partner or query the inventory of drugs in a specific category, e.g., querying the inventory of all externally purchased drugs.

6. Query single-item drug inventory - you can not only query the current stock of drugs but also query the inventory at any past moment and draw an inventory trend chart accordingly.

7. Realize the function of reprinting entry and exit forms.

8. Implement special operations such as returning drugs to suppliers or returning drugs from sub-warehouses to the main warehouse.

9. Realize confirmation of dispatch to sub-warehouses.

10. Implement accounting management functions to track the payment status of a specific entry form.

11. Implement drug price adjustment functions and calculate profit and loss conditions of each pharmaceutical warehouse after price adjustments.

Inpatient Management System - Function Introduction:

1. Patient admission registration, prepayment operations, entry of billing items, prescription return operations, medication returns, changing patient departments (transferring departments), interim settlement, discharge operations, etc.

2. Query prescriptions and patient medication statistics by pharmacy, doctor, and patient.

3. Query daily billing reports of billing staff, query billing details of billing staff within a certain period, and daily billing reports are also known as cash daily reports.

4. Query patient prepayment details, query fee details and statistics of patients within a certain period, and an overview of hospitalization fee conditions for hospitalized patients. Fee details within a certain period, if counted daily, are also called one-day lists of patient expenses.

5. Query inpatient income (including statistical analysis of billing conditions by department, doctor, designated doctor, statistical analysis of inpatient billing item income, and statistical analysis of billing staff billing conditions), and query rosters of inpatients by department, etc.

6. Realize interface with social security systems, print specialized reports required by social security, generate data to be reported in multiple formats, and upload data to the social security bureau via file, email, FTP, etc.

7. Realize the recall function for discharged patients to enable re-settlement of patient expenses.

Dean's Decision Support System - Function Introduction:

The Dean's Decision Support System provides real-time dynamic queries on income and inventory of medicines for various departments and pharmacies in the hospital, and offers intuitive charts for display. This allows the dean to maintain close contact with each department, enabling timely identification and resolution of issues, thus ensuring the normal operation of all hospital work. The system mainly includes four modules: inventory and costs, outpatient billing, and inpatient billing.

Outpatient Electronic Medical Record - Function Introduction:

The outpatient electronic medical record includes two parts: establishing outpatient patient records and electronic prescriptions. The outpatient electronic medical record completely solves the problem of lost prescriptions in hospitals, laying a solid foundation for achieving higher-level management in hospitals.

Traditional manual prescriptions have the following defects:

1. Irregular writing of prescriptions often leads to errors in pricing and dispensing in the pharmacy, easily causing disputes between the hospital and patients.

2. A large number of prescriptions are not charged, resulting in lost prescriptions, which causes incalculable losses to the hospital's economic benefits.

3. Poor confidentiality of manual prescriptions and many irregularities in management.

The outpatient electronic medical record (electronic prescription) thoroughly solves the many problems of manual prescriptions and mainly accomplishes the following functions:

1. Establish medical records for visiting patients.

2. Record information about prescriptions, examinations, treatments, and handling for each visit of the patient, facilitating doctors to track the medical history of patients.

3. Realize the function of electronic prescriptions, printing prescription slips for billing use.

Maintenance Management System - Function Introduction:

1. Basic Information Maintenance - Includes operator information, department information, doctor information, drug information, supplier information, pharmacy/pharmacy/outlet unit information, outpatient billing items, inpatient billing items, procurement personnel information, and maintenance of the self-payment ratio of social security personnel. The information maintained in this part is basic information required for the correct operation of the hospital system, hence referred to as basic information maintenance.

2. Medical Account Maintenance - Includes maintenance of the patient's unit and medical account. Some hospitals need to track the patient's treatment records and statistics on the fees incurred by the patient within a specified time period. In such cases, an account needs to be established for each patient.

3. Data Maintenance - Includes data backup, data recovery, system optimization, dictionary upgrade, etc.

4. Initialization - Used to configure the hospital's business process. This part should be set according to the hospital's own business situation. When the hospital's business process changes, initialization can be used to reconfigure. Initialization also completes operations such as clearing data tables, setting the number of printed lines, and setting the hospital name.
