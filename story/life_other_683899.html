How to Treat Chilblains and Calluses on Feet

Chilblains on Feet

During winter, if you don't pay attention to keeping warm, chilblains can appear on your feet, causing pain and itching, which can be quite annoying.

To prevent cold feet, wear thicker cotton socks that keep your feet warm and absorb sweat. You should also wear well-insulated cotton or leather shoes.

An important aspect of preventing cold feet is to keep them dry; sweaty feet can easily become cold, leading to chilblains. If there's a significant temperature difference between indoors and outdoors, prepare different pairs of shoes and change out of your cotton shoes indoors to avoid sweating.

After developing chilblains, you can soak your feet in hot water every day. Soaking in vinegar is said to be effective, so you might want to try it. The method involves placing half a bottle of vinegar in a basin, filling it with hot water until it's slightly scalding, then soaking your feet. While soaking, continue adding hot water to maintain the temperature, soaking for about half an hour. Doing this for several consecutive days can yield results.

Calluses on Feet

Foot calluses are thick patches of skin that commonly develop on the joints of the toes, the ball of the foot, and the heel. Thick calluses can easily cause cracks in the heels. On the soles of the feet, where calluses are particularly thick, they can press on local nerves due to the weight of the body, causing pain and even affecting walking.

Once calluses form, do not tear them off with your hands as this can easily lead to tearing, causing pain and bleeding.

Instead, soak your feet in warm water to soften and whiten the calluses, then gently rub them off with a pumice stone.

Pumice stones are light and porous, similar in size to a mouse, and come in various shapes. They usually cost a few dollars and are often found in the daily necessities section of supermarkets or at small stalls.

When rubbing off calluses, don't apply too much force to avoid damaging the underlying skin. Don't rub too frequently, as doing so can stimulate the skin to keratinize faster, resulting in calluses growing more quickly.

If the calluses on your feet are very thick, you can seek treatment from a dermatology department at a hospital or find a professional foot-care specialist to handle it. They will use sterilized blades to carefully shave off the calluses layer by layer. Do not let just anyone trim your feet, as inadequate sterilization and shared tools can lead to infectious diseases.

Regularly massage your feet and apply moisturizing cream after washing them.
