TVs, computers and mobile phones that can be rolled up or folded are expected to come into the market.

by anonymous on 2013-11-22 12:02:01

Have you ever heard of rollable or foldable TVs, computers, and mobile phones?

Imagine one day, after we return home from work, we take out a rollable TV and spread it on the wall like an ancient scroll to watch and relieve the fatigue of the day. After watching TV, we can roll it up again like a painting.

Imagine during business trips, laptops and iPads, these indispensable tools for work and life, become lightweight, foldable, and rollable objects, making our journey more comfortable and stylish.

Imagine one day, personalized decorations on our clothes replace rigid mobile phones as our tools for communication with friends. These infinite beautiful aspirations for life are inseparable from the development of rollable and foldable displays. Thanks to the emergence of graphene, this new material, these dreams may soon become reality.

Professor Chen Xianfeng and Dr. Tao Haihua's research team from the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Shanghai Jiao Tong University have developed a proprietary ultraviolet (UV) light/ozone vacuum-type device. By using UV photochemical reactions to clean and dope graphene, its electrical conductivity is effectively enhanced. This helps graphene serve as a transparent conductive material in industrial applications, specifically in bendable and foldable electronic displays, thereby making rollable TVs, computers, and mobile phones a reality.

In the field of electronic display devices, obtaining flexible displays has always been a dream. However, electrode materials have become one of the key factors constraining the development of this technology. In the traditional flat-panel display industry, ITO films are the commonly used materials. Due to their brittle nature and high film-forming temperature, they are not suitable for ordinary flexible organic substrates. When graphene is used as an electrode material, it is primarily supported by single-layer or few-layer carbon atom films. Large-area grown graphene films can be transferred onto any flexible material and can bend or fold accordingly.

The research team believes that graphene is a precious gift for humanity to develop flexible displays, and the UV light/ozone vacuum equipment provides an accurate and controllable dry surface treatment technology, which may play an irreplaceable role in this mission.

Dr. Tao Haihua introduced that in the UV light/ozone vacuum equipment, the photochemical reaction site adopts a vacuum chamber, which not only effectively excludes the original gas in the chamber but also controls oxygen pressure or introduces other gases to achieve the purpose of accelerating or slowing down the photochemical reaction. It has accurate controllability and good repeatability. "We have initially carried out some research on the surface modification of graphene and other materials in this field, with significant results," he said.

Experts believe that the UV light/ozone vacuum equipment is not only a revolution in UV photochemical reaction technology itself but will also have a significant impact on basic research and industrial application in many fields such as materials, devices, medicine, and biology. It is expected to become an important link in the future industry chain.

Currently, the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Shanghai Jiaotong University is actively exploring specific cooperation matters with high-tech companies, dedicated to developing commercial UV light/ozone vacuum equipment and conducting industry-university-research cooperation. By fully utilizing the advantages of enterprises and universities, they aim to develop UV light/ozone vacuum equipment with automated control and operation, providing feasible solutions for forming an industrial chain in the future.
