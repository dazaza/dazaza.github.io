After the release of the development plan for southern Beijing, the second-hand housing prices in Daxing rose to break through 15,000 yuan.

by 1btw728o1p on 2009-12-02 16:24:45

Core tip: Less than a month after the official announcement of Beijing's southern urban development plan, the second-hand housing price in Daxing has already broken through 15,000 yuan per square meter, and the initial signs of hesitation among home buyers have begun to show. It is reported that subway lines will be built as part of the southern urban development plan, which will significantly shorten the time it takes to travel from Daxing to the city center.

Legal Evening News reported on December 1st — less than a month after the official announcement of the southern urban development plan, the price of second-hand housing in Daxing has already surpassed the 15,000-yuan-per-square-meter threshold.

Recently, when visiting several real estate intermediaries in the Daxing area, reporters found that the prices of second-hand homes, originally around ten thousand yuan, had rapidly increased by 2,000 to 5,000 yuan, with the rising speed leaving both home buyers and experts speechless.

Phenomenon: A sudden increase of five thousand yuan leaves home buyers exclaiming "unthinkable!"

"Who would have thought that even the second-hand houses in Huangcun are now priced at fourteen or fifteen thousand yuan!" Ms. Zhang, a home buyer residing in Xihongmen, Daxing, told reporters yesterday.

According to Ms. Zhang, after seeing the southern urban development plan, she planned to sell her one-bedroom apartment located in Malianwa, Haidian District, and buy a second-hand house in Shunchi Linghai community in Daxing District for her parents to move into South City. However, before her current property was sold, the price of second-hand houses in Shunchi Linghai had already risen from 11,000 yuan per square meter to about 15,000 yuan per square meter, a price far exceeding her budget.

One set sold, another one put up for sale; intermediaries lament "too fast"

At the beginning of this month, when reporters visited Huangcun, Daxing, they found that the prices of second-hand houses in many well-known residential areas within the district were still around ten thousand yuan, while those further away from the Daxing subway line were below ten thousand yuan. However, last weekend, when reporters revisited Huangcun, Daxing, they found that house prices had changed dramatically.

Han Zepeng, a salesperson at Yingcan Real Estate located opposite Shouyi Shangcheng in Huangcun, said that the actual price increase happened within these two short weeks. "Now, 'cheap' houses costing just over ten thousand yuan basically don't exist anymore—they've been sold too quickly!" he said.

Han Zepeng added that after the completion of the Daxing subway line, traveling to the city center would be very convenient, and with so much investment in construction, Daxing would become the second Wangjing. "The house prices near the subway in Daxing reaching 20,000 yuan per square meter definitely won't be a problem," he said.

Shortage of sources leads some homeowners to hold back sales waiting for price hikes

The direct cause of the rise in Daxing's housing prices is the enormous demand for purchasing homes. According to our understanding, apart from regular living needs, many investors from central Beijing have locked their investment targets on this area, leading to a shortage of second-hand houses, price increases, and a growing number of homeowners adopting a "hold back sales waiting for price hikes" attitude due to the favorable market conditions.

On the internal network of Yingcan Real Estate's housing sources, the remarks for some relatively lower-priced properties indicate either "already sold" or similar notes like "the homeowner says to wait a bit" or "the house is rented out, not for sale temporarily." The truly available housing sources have dropped to less than one-third of what they used to be.

After viewing a small unit at Shouyi Shangcheng, homeowner Ms. Xiao expressed that since the announcement of the southern urban development plan, the homeowners in the community were particularly optimistic about the future development prospects of the real estate market in Daxing. Especially with the nearby subway station being enclosed and under intense construction, expected to open next year, this has further strengthened her confidence in the appreciation of her property.

Impact: Rapid price increases lead to initial signs of hesitation

Regarding the current housing prices in Daxing, both home buyers and salespeople from intermediary companies expressed that they are "too high." Liu Hui, a salesperson at Lianjia Real Estate, told reporters that higher-priced properties have started to experience stagnant sales.

"Too expensive, let's wait and see," "Notify me if there's something cheaper," "Too expensive, I'll check other areas,"—the information recorded on the internal network of intermediary companies reflects that home buyers are starting to hesitate when faced with higher-priced properties.

Owner Forum Outstanding Comments:

★Shunchi Linghai: From more than 8,000 yuan in April to over 15,000 yuan now—it's excessive! This kind of house isn't worth it. If it doesn't sell, it might as well keep rising, exploiting one customer at a time!

★Shouyi Shangcheng: Houses with 50-year ownership rights selling at the price of 70-year ownership rights—Daxing's high housing prices start here. Don't buy houses in this area.

★Nal Crystal City: Houses in North Fifth Ring sell for 12,000 yuan, fashion handbags, but here it's already 15,000 yuan—who would buy?

Investment Advice: Investors should not blindly chase price hikes

Based on the reporter's understanding of the market, the prices of commercial housing and second-hand housing around the eastern, southern, and western fifth ring roads are generally around 15,000 yuan per square meter.

For example, the opening price of Rongjingcheng in Shijingshan this month is about 14,000 yuan per square meter, while the opening prices of Meilifang near Lishuiqiao and Huamao Cheng projects in Beijing do not exceed 15,000 yuan per square meter. The average price of Meilifang LOFT is only 9,100 yuan per square meter.

Compared with the housing prices in the above-mentioned areas, the housing prices in Daxing breaking through 15,000 yuan per square meter have already overstated its future value. Wang Yong, executive director of Hanbo Consultant, said that although generally speaking, future housing in areas with metro access and special resource advantages such as schools has stronger preservation and appreciation capabilities, the rapid price surge in the past month has diminished the advantage of the southern urban area as a "price trough."

He believes that although with the improvement of the subway system and the implementation of investments, the southern urban area will become more convenient, the previous excessive overstatement has left little room for further price increases. Therefore, he suggests that investment-type home buyers do not blindly chase the price hikes in southern urban second-hand housing. For self-use home buyers, purchases can be made according to individual needs.

(Source: Legal Evening News)
