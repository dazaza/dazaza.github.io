Conditions for public affairs applications for overseas travel by citizens are as follows:

According to the "Regulations for the Implementation of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Entry and Exit Administration", the term "public affairs" for citizens traveling abroad on public business refers to: residing, visiting relatives and friends, studying (including self-funded and publicly dispatched study), tourism (including individual and group tours), working, inheriting property, and other non-public activities. Specifically:

1) A spouse residing abroad or invited by a settled relative to reside abroad, inheriting property abroad, or obtaining a residence permit from the destination country may apply to reside abroad.

2) Invited by a relative with permanent residency rights in the destination country or holding valid long-term residency documents in that country, one can apply for a visit abroad.

3) Those sent abroad for study by national or agricultural units or self-financed students can apply for overseas study.

4) Joining an overseas tour organized by a travel agency approved for "inbound tourism" or individual overseas travel can allow application for overseas tourism.

5) Signing employment contracts with foreign employers or authorized overseas employment service institutions, or being dispatched by domestic labor companies, allows application for overseas work.

6) Business personnel from various enterprises who need to be dispatched abroad for business purposes may apply for business travel.

7) For other public activities requiring overseas travel.

Conditions and Procedures for Self-funded Overseas Study

Eligibility:

1. Self-funded study is not restricted by academic qualifications or age. Anyone meeting the conditions can apply to study abroad at universities (junior college, undergraduate), pursue graduate studies, or attend vocational training.

2. Graduates and self-funded students applying for self-funded overseas study can directly apply to the local public security authorities with relevant documents.

3. Self-funded students currently enrolled or graduates with education below junior college level must serve a certain number of years domestically or repay their higher education compensation fees before applying for self-funded overseas study. (Service years: Junior college graduates 5 years, postgraduate graduates 3 years, doctoral and private university graduates 2 years; Repayment amounts: Doctoral students 1,500 yuan per year, undergraduates 2,500 yuan per year, master's students 4,000 yuan per year, doctoral students 6,000 yuan per year.)

4. Doctoral graduates pursuing postdoctoral research abroad do not have to pay higher education compensation fees.

5. In addition to the above conditions, self-funded overseas study applicants should also possess: First, sufficient foreign exchange sponsorship or overseas scholarships obtained through legal means, such as economic guarantee letters required by sponsors (which must be recognized by the destination country), or scholarship or financial assistance certificates issued by foreign universities, research institutions, or foundations. The foreign exchange sponsorship or overseas scholarship should be sufficient to cover study and living expenses abroad. Second, they should have adequate proficiency in a foreign language.

Procedures and Relevant Regulations:

After receiving admission letters and foreign exchange sponsorship certificates, if the applicant is not a current student or employed, they should first submit an application to their unit or school for approval. Then, with the necessary documents and unit-signed proof, the applicant should apply to the local public security department for an overseas passport. The public security department will issue a visa according to relevant regulations. With the passport, the applicant should then apply for an entry visa at the embassy or consulate of the destination country.

Undergraduate, junior college, and graduate students at higher education institutions who study abroad may retain their student status for one year. Self-funded overseas students pursuing graduate studies abroad may opt to become self-funded publicly dispatched students, receiving a "National Dispatched Overseas Study Personnel Certificate" from Chinese embassies or consulates without changing their passports or visas.

Self-funded overseas study applications are handled by local public security departments. Current students or employed individuals applying for self-funded overseas study should obtain approval from their schools or units.
