Shenzhen Special Zone Daily News (reporter Yiping Liu) The fourth Shenzhen Sex Culture Festival opened yesterday morning in Hall 6 of the Convention and Exhibition Center. Renowned domestic and international sexologists and relevant personnel from the Municipal Health Commission jointly cut the ribbon at the opening ceremony. On the first day, it attracted about 6000 citizens to visit.

The ancient sex artifact exhibition area is the biggest highlight of this year's sex culture festival. Professor Dalin Liu of Shanghai University's Department of Sociology and founder of the "Chinese Sex Culture Museum" brought hundreds of classic ancient sex artifacts. The Chinese Sex Culture Museum has collected more than 2000 rare sex artifacts, including ceramics, erotic paintings, bronze ware, etc. The forms of the collections are diverse, and many exhibits are already unique and precious pieces. This time, Dalin Liu brought hundreds of treasures to participate in the Shenzhen Sex Culture Festival, giving Shenzhen audiences a feast for their eyes. A female college student majoring in design told reporters that although these artifacts come from ancient times and are very simple, many of their design concepts are still similar to today's. "The preface part of the artifact exhibition area specially introduces 'sex in language and characters', with 40 commonly used Chinese characters such as 'father', 'high', 'auspicious', 'abandon', etc. After seeing them, I learned that they all originate from sex, some related to male genitalia, which is quite interesting." In addition, there are introductions to different sex cultures from Europe, Africa, Japan, India, etc., also increasing knowledge.

On one side of the sex artifact exhibition area, there are several male sculptures standing in a row, all with exaggerated reproductive organs, making it impossible for passers-by to ignore. Standing at the "end" is a wooden African pregnant woman sculpture, with a hole in her navel. This is a divination device from Tanzania. When a woman is pregnant, will she give birth to a boy or a girl? At the time, without advanced ultrasound, it was used for divination: put a paper ball into the navel of the wooden sculpture, if the paper ball falls into the round groove under the pregnant woman's feet, it's a boy; if it falls into the square groove, it's a girl; if it falls outside, there will be pregnancy complications. This ancient "divination device" is quite amusing, attracting many viewers to stop and watch.

This year's sex culture festival lasts for three days. The population and family planning departments of all districts have made meticulous preparations, striving to make the exhibition areas distinctive, showcase the latest achievements in population and family planning work, and introduce knowledge about sex and reproductive health. At the sex culture festival site, almost every exhibition area is freely distributing condoms. Staff members from the municipal family planning center's pharmaceutical station told reporters that in just an hour and a half in the morning, nearly ten thousand were distributed.
