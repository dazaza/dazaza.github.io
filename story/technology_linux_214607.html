Market Analysis of Domestic Telecommunications-grade High-end Routers

by lk0pcff on 2009-07-26 15:45:01

High-end router products have mainly developed to meet the high-speed growth and applications of the Internet. According to a common view in the industry, so-called high-end/core routers should at least be equipped with high-speed interfaces such as gigabit and 2.5Gbps, switching capacities exceeding 20G, and packet forwarding capabilities over 25Mpps.

The application areas of high-end routers are very extensive. Apart from the backbone layer of the Internet network being a traditional application area for high-end routers, major operators are now building their own backbone IP networks and broadband IP metropolitan area networks, opening up another important application area for high-end routers. At the same time, with the increase in backbone network bandwidth and the continuous development of broadband network applications, enterprise and campus networks (LANs) are also expanding, leading to higher capacity and processing requirements for network equipment. Therefore, high-end router products also have great potential in enterprise networks, campus networks, and other networks.

The high-end router product market is growing rapidly and has an excellent future prospect. According to RHK Company analysis, the router market reached $2.1 billion in 2000, and it is expected to reach $12 billion by 2003. The domestic high-end router market is also showing a trend of high-speed growth. Data analysis shows that the market size reached around 1 billion yuan in 2000, about 3 billion yuan in 2001, and is expected to reach 13 billion yuan in two years.

Before domestic manufacturers launched high-end router products, the domestic high-end router market was dominated by foreign equipment. Some domestic communication enterprises, led by ZTE Communications, have introduced domestically produced high-end router products. From the overall structure and related technical indicators, domestically produced high-end routers have reached international advanced levels. After going through the research and experimental prototype stages, they now possess industrialization capabilities and can be deployed on a large commercial scale.

Since the high-end router market was once entirely dominated by foreign equipment, domestically produced high-end routers still need to fully demonstrate their system stability and network service characteristics at this early stage. However, disregarding psychological factors, domestically produced high-end router products, whether in providing high-speed ports, large switching capacities, line-speed packet forwarding capabilities, rich protocol support, or any other relevant aspects, can meet the requirements for broadband IP network construction. Moreover, domestic equipment has significant advantages in terms of price, service, and customized business provision capabilities.

Based on network service demands and actual network construction conditions, domestically produced equipment has broad market opportunities in the backbone network, metropolitan area networks, and specialized networks.

● National/Provincial Backbone Networks

Backbone applications are the traditional market for core routers. Although national-level backbone networks have been basically constructed, from the perspective of network coverage and the capacity and capabilities of network node devices, there is still potential for backbone network expansion. However, operators generally recognize the mature and reliable features of foreign equipment, and the backbone network has already adopted all foreign equipment. The price advantages of domestic equipment cannot be reflected in a short period of time, which may lead operators to continue favoring foreign equipment in future backbone expansions.
