Home decoration company answers five questions about underfloor heating systems

by longersing on 2009-10-31 22:47:03

With the popularity of geothermal heating methods, floors specifically designed for geothermal use have gradually increased. Not only are there more products available, but new technologies and concepts are also emerging rapidly, making it difficult to distinguish between truth and falsehood. After investigating the market and interviewing relevant experts, reporters found that salespeople's statements about materials, thickness, installation, underlay, and other aspects all contain some degree of misleading information. Therefore, before purchasing, please try to avoid these "man-made" cognitive confusions.

Confusion One

Can solid wood flooring be used for geothermal heating?

On October 24th, at the Lanyinglijia Dazhongsi Home Plaza, a salesperson from Nature Flooring told reporters that there were only two models of solid wood geothermal flooring available because only oak had enough hardness to be used in geothermal environments. However, at the Jingranzhijia Beisihuan store, another floor brand, Shiyou, directly promoted its “Geothermal King” —— true anti-geothermal pure solid wood flooring with a large advertisement. From its promotional brochure, we learned that this floor, after undergoing "unique high-temperature heat treatment technology," solved the problem of traditional solid wood flooring easily deforming or cracking in geothermal environments. Its thermal conductivity efficiency is greater than 8℃/h, meeting national standards, so it can be fully used in geothermal environments. Among its floor categories, in addition to oak, there is also ash wood.

Mr. Wang, who was on-site selecting flooring, expressed doubts about using pure solid wood flooring as geothermal flooring, such as whether pure solid wood flooring would deform more easily.

Expert Opinion

Can be used after special treatment

Wang Jun, an expert committee member of the China Forestry Industry Association and deputy director of the Jilin Provincial Forestry Academy, introduced that since in geothermal environments, floors need to withstand certain amounts of heat, when moisture in the air is absorbed by the wood, the wood will expand; when the air is relatively dry, the moisture in the wood runs back into the air, causing the wood to shrink dryly. "Ordinary solid wood flooring is prone to cracking and shrinking gaps, so it cannot be applied in geothermal environments." Pure solid wood flooring that can be used in geothermal environments must have undergone special treatment. There are currently two ways: 1. High-temperature carbonization, which involves high-temperature heat treatment of the raw material of the floor at 160℃～230℃ conditions, reducing the wood's moisture absorption function or making it less sensitive to changes in air humidity. 2. Fully sealing the wood with paint, i.e., sealing all exposed outer surfaces of the wood, including the back panel and tongue-and-groove joints, with paint, preventing the wood from contacting the air, thus eliminating the impact of humidity on the floor.

If pure solid wood flooring is used in geothermal environments, extra attention needs to be paid during installation. After sawing the floor, the newly generated cross-sections or longitudinal sections must be sealed with wax to ensure the floor is completely in a closed state; the floor should be installed as much as possible at one time, strictly preventing the destruction of the closed coating at the floor lock joint during repeated installations, which could cause the floor to deform due to changes in air humidity. Additionally, before installation, check if the ground is smooth and level, and remove any protruding sand particles while cleaning thoroughly. "Mainly to prevent sand particles from causing damage to the moisture-proof film and the moisture-proof coating of the floor."

Confusion Two

Is bamboo flooring really heat-resistant and doesn't deform?

In front of the Chunhong Bamboo Flooring booth at Lanyinglijia, the salesperson introduced that the product has been specially treated, making it suitable for geothermal environments. "We undergo high-temperature carbonization during production, so no matter how high the temperature is, we don't worry." However, consumer Mr. Zhang still expressed that he usually hears that geothermal flooring consists mainly of laminate flooring and solid wood composite flooring. For bamboo-based geothermal flooring, he truly doesn't know if it can handle the job, indicating his uncertainty.

Expert Opinion

Higher risk of cracking and shrinking gaps

Bamboo flooring typically has two structures: one is ordinary bamboo flooring (aligned longitudinally); the other is three-layer structure bamboo flooring (aligned perpendicularly). In terms of deformation amount, although ordinary bamboo flooring deforms less than solid wood flooring, it far exceeds solid wood composite flooring and laminate flooring, so using ordinary bamboo flooring in geothermal environments poses significant risks.

However, Wang Jun indicated that if it's three-layer structure bamboo flooring, its dimensional stability is very similar to that of three-layer solid wood composite flooring, which can be considered as solid wood composite flooring and can be used for geothermal purposes. But the moisture content of its surface layer should be controlled below 8%, otherwise, surface layer cracks may occur. "If the bamboo flooring undergoes carbonization, its stability will improve somewhat, and the risk will also decrease relatively." Nevertheless, experts still recommend that using bamboo flooring for geothermal purposes carries a higher risk of surface layer cracking and shrinking compared to laminate and solid wood composite flooring. "Of course, if bamboo flooring is used in geothermal environments, using a humidifier to maintain indoor humidity between 50%-60% ensures that using bamboo flooring is not a problem."

Confusion Three

Does thickness affect thermal conductivity?

Generally speaking, the thinner the floor, the greater its heat flow. When asked if the thickness of the floor affects the thermal conductivity of geothermal flooring, a salesperson from a certain brand stated that as long as it is geothermal flooring, it generally meets thermal requirements. "Regardless of how thick it is, if it's geothermal flooring, it shouldn't have issues affecting its thermal performance."

This answer naturally does not solve consumers' doubts. Mr. Wang, who is interested in pure solid wood geothermal flooring, expressed that such flooring is usually around 17mm thick, and he indeed worries that it might cause poor thermal conduction. Mr. Zhang also mentioned that thicker flooring feels better underfoot but affects indoor temperature, so he doesn't know how to make a choice.

Expert Opinion

Not much difference in continuous heating

How to balance thickness and heat transfer? Wang Jun said that generally, the thicker the floor, the more it affects heat transfer, but "the impact is not significant." Floors with thicknesses of either 8mm or 12mm can be used in geothermal environments. Comparatively, 8mm laminate flooring heats up faster than 12mm, but in continuously heated environments, the difference in heat transfer between the two is not obvious; 12mm flooring feels better underfoot and costs more. For continuously heated areas, in places like Beijing where room temperatures aren't too cold, if the heating system can ensure basic indoor temperature requirements are met, slightly thicker flooring, such as 12mm-15mm, is fine. If in colder areas like Northeast China, 8mm thick flooring can be considered, as it has better thermal conductivity.

Wang Jun reminded that under geothermal conditions, the temperature the floor bears is relatively high, and as the temperature rises, the speed of formaldehyde release increases, so floors with low formaldehyde emissions should be chosen as much as possible. "Choose branded flooring, not only ensuring product quality, but also having good after-sales service."

Confusion Four

Is three-layer solid wood the most suitable?

Many salespeople claim that three-layer solid wood composite flooring is the most suitable for geothermal heating. Is this true?

Expert Opinion

Higher risk of cracking and shrinking gaps

Wang Jun explained that as geothermal flooring, it's best not to choose too wide or too long boards. The larger the size of the board, the bigger the gap produced in geothermal environments. Especially for wide-surface three-layer solid wood composite flooring, if the moisture content of the surface layer is too high during production, it will also crack under geothermal conditions. Therefore, when producing three-layer solid wood composite flooring intended for geothermal use, the moisture content of the surface layer must be strictly controlled.

Under geothermal conditions, when the moisture content of the floor changes, the expansion amount of the strips differs, and noticeable rib-like patterns will appear on the surface of the floor. If the surface layer thickness of the three-layer solid wood composite flooring is above 3mm, this phenomenon won't occur. Thus, three-layer solid wood composite flooring with a surface layer thickness of 2mm or less is not suitable for geothermal use.

Confusion Five

What kind of underlayment is the best?

When mentioning geothermal flooring, many salespeople bring up underlayment. And they all say their own is the best. A salesperson from Kanglong Flooring stated that they use cork underlayment made from pure natural oak bark. "It doesn't deform, it's environmentally friendly, and has good insulation properties." A salesperson from Shiyou Flooring then stated that they use plastic film with tin foil attached, "to enhance thermal conductivity." Another company uses regular plastic film. So, which underlayment is the best?

Proper Installation and Maintenance

1. Not too dry

The normal use environment humidity for flooring should be above 40%, with the optimal humidity being 50%～60%. Due to overly dry environments, it can also lead to winter shrinkage in laminate flooring. Therefore, in geothermal environments, reasonably adjusting the room's humidity is the most effective way to prevent floor deformation.

2. Careful maintenance

When using multi-layer solid wood composite flooring for geothermal purposes, the surface layer thickness is often 0.6 millimeters, and the surface compressive strength is relatively weak. If protective measures are not taken for chair legs in the room, sitting on the chair can damage the floor surface.

3. Temperature control

Before installing the floor, a pressure test should be conducted on the geothermal system to prevent floor damage caused by leaks in the geothermal pipelines. The water supply temperature of the geothermal system should not exceed 60℃. During the initial geothermal heating period, the temperature increase should be gradual, and the water supply temperature should be controlled within 10℃ higher than the current environmental temperature, not exceeding 32℃. It should run continuously for 48 hours; thereafter, every 24 hours, the water temperature increases by 3℃ until it reaches the design water supply temperature. The ground temperature that directly contacts the floor should not exceed 42℃.
