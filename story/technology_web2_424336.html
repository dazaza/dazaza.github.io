Shandong College Entrance Examination 2011 Feng Shui Room Layout for the college entrance examination

by gaokao203 on 2011-05-04 14:58:56

The 2011 college entrance examination is approaching, and countless parents are worried about their children. They are afraid that their children will not perform well or that they will be unable to bear the mental pressure. Parents want to share the worries of their children, or they fear that their children may faint at the examination site and affect their test scores, etc. In response to the requests of many blog friends, I now announce the following Feng Shui layout for the college entrance examination, please take it with a smile.

Dear friends, you don't have to worry about your child's birth chart. The following methods are unrelated to the child's birth chart, so even if you do not know the child's astrological chart, you can still create a good academic chart. However, due to different house layouts, the methods will certainly vary.

Here is a brief explanation of the method I use in my layout. People live in time and space, including filling out volunteer forms, so I use the space-time method. To put it simply, it is to let students achieve the best possible results in a reasonable time and suitable environment, and this method does not reduce other factors to maintain balance.

The layout is as follows:

1. If the door of the study room (such as: living room, bedroom, study, etc.) faces west

Layout: Please hang a red Chinese knot on this door, and move the desk to the west side. The left or right side of the table should be against the wall, and the person should face south. If there is a window to the south, the effect will be better. Then place four lucky bamboo plants to the south of the table, and write the four words "Wen Chang Jia Gui" on the bamboo, and that's it.

2. If the door of the study room faces east

Layout: Move the study table to the west, sit facing east. Don't worry about the door clash; it can achieve no clash without activation. But similarly, a closing move must be done, which is to place a large tripod in the center of the table, made of soil or pottery. Place four thin willow leaves inside the tripod and press them down with a five-cent coin. Also, fill half of it with water, thereby completing the layout.

3. If the door of the study room faces south

Layout: Move the study table to the southwest corner but slightly to the north, sitting west facing east, next to the south door, and place a hollow wooden fence between the south door and the table. Hang four white-haired brushes on the west wall behind it, with the tips pointing down. Place a potted aquatic plant on the table, thereby completing the layout.

4. If the door of the study room faces north

Layout: There is no layout available for studying in this position. Please move the study table to a room facing any of the above three directions, and it will work.
