Manchester United's flying wing is more stylish than the "Stylish Brother" and becomes the Devil's second brother; Real Madrid can hardly buy him with 20 million.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 22:50:57

Manchester United's Flying Wing is more dashing than "S潇洒哥" (Babenko), becoming the Red Devils' second most important player; Real Madrid's 20 million offer finds it hard to buy him.

Since entering November, a new firepower point has emerged for Manchester United: Antonio Valencia. The Ecuadorian has scored 3 goals in 6 league matches, averaging one goal every two games. The situation where he only provided assists and no goals has been rectified. Currently, in Manchester United, he has played in 16 league matches and scored 4 goals. Although scoring one goal every four matches might not seem like much, considering his recent performances, Valencia has become Manchester United's second most important attacking player after Rooney.

It’s somewhat embarrassing that despite Manchester United having 14 players scoring goals in this season's league and cup matches combined, Rooney stands far above with 13 goals alone. The second-highest scorer, Owen, has only 7 goals, and Owen has scored merely two goals in the most important league matches. Berbatov's four league goals were spread out over time. Only Valencia has consistently scored recently, and each of his goals has been more beautiful than the last. Although on the league scoring chart within the team, he is tied for second place with Berbatov, clearly fans recognize Valencia's recent form as far superior to Berbatov's.

Today's goal vividly demonstrated how strong teams, while maintaining high-speed counterattacks, emphasize the combination of individual creativity and opportunity-grasping ability. Any team can launch quick counterattacks, but simply pursuing speed without proper positioning during passes or wildly off-target final passes renders fast attacks nothing more than empty talk. In this instance, Scholes’ precise through ball set up the play, followed by Berbatov's imaginative backheel cross—a perfect blend of technique and imagination. Finally, Berbatov adjusted his center of gravity and finished with a stunning right-footed volley reminiscent of classic finishing touches from strikers like Sheringham and Solskjaer, fully replicated today by Valencia.

After the match, Ferguson lavishly praised Valencia: "That finish was absolutely brilliant. Antonio can certainly increase his goal tally significantly because we all know in training that he is actually an excellent striker, and today's goal is a great example. Antonio has made tremendous progress. His performance in the game was particularly expansive, and he executed it very well. He continues to improve himself, and we are very satisfied." What does "expansiveness" mean? It refers to Valencia's playing style—his activity across the midfield has expanded, allowing him to move and interchange positions more comfortably rather than being just a specialist on the right wing as he initially was. Multiple one-touch passes throughout the game showed high accuracy, requiring a high level of understanding between teammates running along different routes while maintaining overall high-speed progression. Valencia's willingness to operate this way indicates that he has now fully integrated into the current Manchester United team. With this in mind, the number of future goals and assists from the 17 million star should, as emphasized by Ferguson, inevitably increase substantially.

Manchester United should feel fortunate for securing Valencia. This summer, Bayern Munich offered 18 million pounds, and Real Madrid went up to 20 million, prices both higher than what Manchester United paid. Fortunately, Wigan Athletic didn't care about the difference of a few million pounds and emphasized that Valencia's destination would depend solely on himself. Valencia, already accustomed to playing in the Premier League, ultimately chose Manchester United. Looking back half a year later, this deal has begun to significantly recoup its cost for Manchester United. Although Valencia hasn't yet reached Cristiano Ronaldo's level of destructive power, his performance of 4 goals and 3 assists in 16 matches, especially his impressive form since entering November, makes people believe that although his position is somewhat fixed, at just 24 years old, Valencia is advancing towards the great goal of becoming an absolute right-wing mainstay who will leave an important chapter in Manchester United's glorious history, spanning an entire era.
