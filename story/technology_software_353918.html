Comprehensive information and tips on the Korean version of Dragon's Valley cheating!

by sdhyxcu7q on 2010-05-01 23:26:06

Korean Dragon's Dogma can be simply understood as a 3D version of DNF (Dungeon and Fighter). Both Dungeon & Fighter (called DF in Korea) and Dragon's Dogma (called DN in Korea) are games developed by South Korea's NEONX company. These two games have many similarities in terms of currency collection. In terms of operability, DN is much higher than DF, requiring a high operational coefficient. The game also distinguishes itself through fatigue levels and dungeons. If someone says this game imitates DNF, they're completely wrong because this game and DNF are developed by the same company with almost identical names, DN and DF. Therefore, this game can be considered an evolved version of DNF. It is believed that after the Chinese server opens, it will attract a large number of DNF players.

The difficulty levels of this game's dungeons vary from the simplest Easy (easy mode) to the hardest Abyss (abyss mode). Among these, Easys can be cleared solo by anyone, even fools—it's very simple. However, no equipment will drop at all; even dropping white gear is quite difficult. Generally, only some junk stones will drop. Normally, people clear Normal (medium difficulty) solo, where white gear usually drops, occasionally green gear. For Hard (hard difficulty), it’s recommended for two players; if your operation skills are extremely excellent and proficient, you can solo it. Early maps can be soloed entirely, but not later ones. Green gear drops more frequently, with a very low chance of opening a box to get blue gear. For 2-4 players, it's recommended to clear Master (master difficulty), which has a higher difficulty with more monsters, thicker health points. A lot of green gear drops, with a medium chance of opening a golden treasure box to get blue gear. For a 4-player team, it's suggested to clear Abyss (abyss difficulty), where there are many strong monsters with thick HP. Many purple monsters (drop blue gear) and bosses with multiple health bars exist. In some maps, the final boss of abyss difficulty might have two. The difficulty is very high. Our team spends on average 2 revival coins per person to clear one abyss. Overall, clearing one abyss usually results in dropping one piece of blue equipment from the monsters (Here, I must mention that dropping blue gear or better requires rolling dice. You should never roll randomly if it's not for your own class because once you get the equipment, it cannot be traded). When RP bursts, two or more pieces of blue gear may drop. If you encounter a big treasure chest, it can open 2-5 pieces of blue gear. My highest record is three pieces. After playing for four days, I've seen three big treasure chests. The final evaluation of passing the abyss difficulty is generally A, S, SS, SSS, and a golden or silver treasure box can usually be opened. The golden treasure box definitely contains one blue item, though whether it belongs to your class or not is unknown, or it could be blue material. When RP bursts, you might get purple gear or blue gear—some say they’ve even gotten yellow gear! Silver treasure boxes generally contain green items, copper treasure boxes usually contain white gear. With good RP, you might get a green item, while with bad RP, you'll just get junk materials or stones. Iron treasure boxes generally contain junk stones. With good RP, you still get stones; with bad RP, you still get stones.

Regarding Dragon's Dogma tasks: There aren't many tasks in Dragon's Dogma. Generally, you just need to accept tasks and level up, as tasks clear very quickly. At each dungeon entrance, there's a signboard where you can usually accept six tasks. The first task generally requires you to pass through once. Tasks two through five, since I don't fully understand Korean, involve collecting things inside the dungeon, gathering items, or passing through the dungeon. The sixth task usually requires you to pass through an abyss difficulty dungeon once. Each task yields rich experience, so it's absolutely worth clearing all tasks!

SP in Dragon's Dogma: Learning JN in Dragon's Dogma requires SP, which is quite tight. You get 20 SP for each level-up, and SP tasks are few. From level 1 to 16, tasks currently only give me two bottles of 5SP medicine water. In the early stages, learning a JN requires 10-15 SP, and upgrading a JN requires 3-6 SP. This is during the early stage (level 1-15). You can add points freely in the early stage because after level 15, when you change your job, points will automatically reset. At this point, you won’t notice how tight SP is. But when you reach level 18, you will. During the mid-stage (level 15-20), learning one JN generally requires 20 SP, and leveling up requires 6-8 SP. In the mid-late stage (level 20-35), learning one JN generally requires 48 SP, though the exact amount is unknown but definitely more than 6. In the late stage (level 30-45), learning one JN requires 50-100 SP. The last JN at level 45 requires 100 SP... with only 20 SP per level... the exact amount required now is unknown but definitely more than 6! In Dragon's Dogma, there are around 20 second-turn JNs, and about 10 first-turn JNs. Many first-turn skills are fundamental and require higher points. My second-turn JNs are divided into four major categories. It's impossible to learn all four, otherwise, SP won't be enough. Considering you only get 20 SP per level, learning a JN after level 20 requires over 48 SP, and you still need to level it up. Since there are many JNs in Dragon's Dogma, adding points must be carefully considered!

Dragon's Dogma mail and trading system: Trading in Dragon's Dogma has an exchange market. However, for some reason, it seems you can only sell six items per week in the exchange market, which is limited. Buying has no limit. Due to this limitation, I created a secondary account to send items via mail to the secondary account. Another infuriating thing happened: you can only send five emails, then you can't send anymore. Whether it's five emails per day or five per week is unknown (this applies to adult accounts, not child accounts).

Dragon's Dogma fatigue system: Dragon's Dogma gives 600 points of PT daily, which is PL. People who play DNF might think 600 points would never run out, but that's incorrect. PL in Dragon's Dogma is consumed quickly. Clearing a map generally requires 95 PL, meaning 600 points can only clear seven times. Each time generally takes around ten minutes to complete. On Saturdays, an additional 1200 weekly PT points are given, making Saturday have 1800 points. These 1200 points can be used within the week if not finished on the same day. Korean servers give an extra 300 points daily in internet cafes (all discounts in Korean servers are in internet cafes, regardless of DNF or Dragon's Dogma or other games, unlike certain brain-damaged promotions like black diamonds. Korean DNF internet cafes give over 220 PL). Since the Chinese server is operated by Shanda, it will likely have some kind of VIP system.

Dragon's Dogma PVP system: In PVP, I believe there is significant imbalance between classes. Especially the swordsman. Every time I fight him, I get comboed to the ground. As soon as I get up, I'm comboed again. His knockback seems a bit high. Also, before level 10, the swordsman is somewhat slow, but after level 10, he becomes extremely fast, running swiftly and grabbing people like lightning—kind of like a magpie! The lowest PVP is 1v1 and the highest is 8v8. 8v8 might be a bit laggy on small maps. For 1v1, I feel the swordsman is the strongest, followed by the priest. The priest's cross stuns people, causing knockback, leading to combos. Luckily, the range is a bit small. For single PVP, I feel the mage is the weakest. In group PVP, mainly rely on rear firepower while front-line warriors and priests harass. Rear mages and archers operate to win, and of course, ensure the safety of rear archers and mages.
