80-Year-Old Woman Creates Animal World with Paper Folding in 62 Years (Photos)

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-02-29 09:33:17

Liu Guangying can now fold over 130 different patterns. She doesn't just sit and fold; even when lying down to sleep, she can still fold with ease. "I guess you could say I'm quite proficient," the elderly woman said humbly.

When young people started learning paper folding, there were very few of them. She worried that one day no one would know how to fold paper anymore. "I yearn to compile all my lessons and methods from these years into a book so everyone can learn how to fold paper. I don't want any rewards; I only wish to pass on this skill."

In reality, what's in front of us is just the tip of the iceberg. This 88-year-old woman can actually fold over 130 different patterns, most of which are her original creations. When talking about paper folding, the elderly woman has endless things to say...

The reporter couldn't help but want to learn a couple of tricks after seeing how effortlessly the elderly woman folded and how beautiful the patterns turned out. Liu Guangying was enthusiastic and taught the reporter step-by-step how to fold finger puppets. "First, fold a rectangular piece of white paper into a square. The edges must align perfectly, or it will become misshapen later..."

Although it seems simple at first glance, paper folding isn't as easy as it looks. With great patience, Liu Guangying taught the reporter several times until they finally managed to cut out a rough human shape. It turns out that paper folding is more challenging than expected.

Even though Liu Guangying's legs aren't as convenient as before, she can't stay idle. Even though doctors advised her not to sit for long periods and discouraged her from paper folding, she can't let go. Not only does she fold every day herself, but she also often teaches children in her home. In 2010, a teacher from a school learned about Liu Guangying's paper folding skills and brought students to learn. "The children are adorable and love paper folding. Teaching them something they enjoy makes me feel happy."

Liu Guangying has been practicing paper folding for 62 years. Since becoming an elementary school teacher in 1949, she began exploring paper folding. "Paper folding helps students better understand concepts. Back then, we didn't have projectors, so I folded things to show the children what lions and tigers look like."

Her small room is already filled with various sizes of folded paper. Five large sheets of paper, each a meter long, are densely covered with all kinds of patterns. The elderly woman is meticulous, separating different types of paper folding: birds, mammals, electrical appliances, musical instruments... Each category has a variety of shapes. A lion stretches its four limbs forward while running but still turns its head to look back at everyone. A gray monkey stands on one leg while scratching its head with the other hand, looking very playful.

As time went by, Liu Guangying's desire grew stronger—to write a book, without asking for any compensation, just hoping to pass on this skill.

"Come, take a look at these, they're all mine..." Liu Guangying invited everyone and took out a big box from the inner room. The box was full of colorful and varied folded papers, mostly animals. Monkeys, tigers, kangaroos, camels, and various birds, with their features drawn on and poses added, made one feel as if they had entered a condensed version of the animal world.

"Look at this." Suddenly, many small dolls appeared on the elderly woman's fingers, dressed in colorful clothes, with cute expressions, and even little braids. "These are finger dolls, fun, right?" As she bent her fingers, the small dolls seemed to nod and wave at people. While speaking, she couldn't help but smile playfully.

Liu Guangying's hope is to publish a book free of charge to teach the art of paper folding.
