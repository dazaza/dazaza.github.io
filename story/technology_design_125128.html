Have you outperformed the returns of "30% of the stocks doubling" in this rebound?

by xxywing on 2009-02-17 09:49:14

On Monday, the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets hit new highs, approaching 2400 points. So far, the rebound that started from 1664.93 points has seen a cumulative increase of over 40%. During the same period, the total market value and circulating market value of the two markets have significantly increased. Statistics show that the total market value of the two markets broke through 16 trillion yuan yesterday, with more than 400 varieties seeing their stock prices double, accounting for nearly 30%. So, have your gains doubled in this wave of rebound?

Below is the record of participants' doubled earnings in the real trading competition for your reference.

February 16th, Wednesday. The "Hesui Cup" Real Trading Competition is proceeding in a heated manner amidst the booming market conditions. Participant Shanlang's yield rate today was 9.91%, making him ascend to the top spot for the first time. The heavily held China Sports Industry by this participant surged by 10.05% today. Another holding, TCL Group (100 shares), also saw a small rise of one percentage point. By the close of the market on Monday, the total yield was 97.44%. Participant Dungen's position in Foton Motor fell against the trend today, causing his total yield to drop by 2.7 percentage points, thus giving up the top spot.

Participant Shanlang's current securities market value is relatively low compared to when he started participating, at only 15,420 yuan. However, achieving near-doubled returns with such limited funds is quite remarkable. What ingenious strategy allowed participant Shanlang to achieve great returns with small capital? We can examine the historical settlement records of this participant. Participant Shanlang's settlement records span just four pages but involve a large number of traded stocks, densely packed, amounting to no less than 20 stocks. Most operations were short-term swing trades, entering and exiting quickly, frequently switching stocks, each bringing about a 3-4 percentage point yield. Since participation began, the highest yielding stock was the China Sports Industry which closed at a limit-up high today, sold at its peak, with a yield of 22.782%, greatly contributing to Shanlang's ascent to the top.

Looking at today's competition, there are many fine examples of trading strategies, which we will not list here in detail as it might seem promotional. Today's top performer is participant Tangu Lunjin, with a yield of 10.13%. This participant's holdings brought significant gains, propelling the ranking up by 26 positions. By the close of the market on Monday, the average holding rate among participants was 77.37%. 48 participants traded today, similar to the previous trading day. Most participants are enjoying the gains from their earlier positions without chasing higher to build new ones. The average yield among participants is 21.59%, steadily rising along with the continuous upward movement of the market. By the close of the market on Monday, the top ten ranked participants are: Shanlang, Dungen, Laozhe, Shanghai Leilei, Hao Xinqing, Qicaihong, Gongfu Panda, Growth Power, Ranran, and Tiaotiao Long.

The trading volume of the two markets reached 273.7 billion yuan today, preserving the third gap. The game of hot potato continues, with a batch of stock prices approaching or even exceeding the upper edge of the long-term equilibrium value zone, accumulating risks. Despite the market trend remaining unchanged, the accumulated risk increases correspondingly and requires attention. The next issue at hand is whether participants who entered based on investment logic should pay attention to whether the prices of their investment targets remain within reasonable value ranges, and whether speculative participants have set good profit-taking or stop-loss levels.
