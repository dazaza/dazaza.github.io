How to make boiled fish?

by lk0cswa on 2009-08-06 06:36:47

Ingredients: Bass or Perch (both can be translated as perch, give it a try) or Tilapia (tilapia)

or Catfish (catfish) are all acceptable. Live fish is preferred, do not use Carp (carp) one

Accompaniments: bean sprouts, or vegetables of your choice

Seasonings: ginger (a large piece, loosen and slice), garlic (one head, slightly crush, no need to cut), appropriate amount of green onion

Broad bean paste (or chopped chili), Sichuan peppercorn, dried red chili, chili powder,

Salt, monosodium glutamate, pepper powder, cooking wine, soy sauce, a little vinegar, edible oil

Also need: cornstarch, cooking wine, a little salt, one egg white

Instructions:

1. Clean the fish after killing it, chop off the head and tail, slice into fish slices, and chop the remaining fish bones into several pieces. Marinate the fish slices with a little salt, cooking wine, cornstarch, and one egg white for 15 minutes. Place the head, tail, and fish bones in another plate and marinate them using the same method.

2. Boil a small pot of water, wash the bean sprouts, then blanch them in boiling water, remove and place in a large bowl, sprinkle a little salt according to personal taste, set aside.

3. In a clean wok, add three times the usual amount of oil used for stir-frying. After the oil heats up, add three tablespoons of broad bean paste (or chopped chili) and stir-fry until fragrant. Add ginger, garlic, green onion, Sichuan peppercorn, chili powder, and dried red chili, stir-fry on medium-low heat. Once fragrant, add the head, tail, and fish bones, turn to high heat, mix evenly, add cooking wine and soy sauce, pepper powder, an appropriate amount of sugar, continue to stir-fry for a while, then add some hot water, add salt and monosodium glutamate for seasoning (taste the saltiness). Once the water boils, keep on high heat, add the fish slices one by one, spread out with chopsticks, cook for 3-5 minutes before turning off the heat. Pour the cooked fish and all the soup into the large bowl that contains the bean sprouts.

4. Take another clean pot, pour in half a catty of oil (the exact amount depends on the size of the container you prepare, so that when poured into the large bowl, it completely submerges the fish and bean sprouts, estimate visually). After the oil heats up, turn off the heat and let it cool down a bit. Then add plenty of Sichuan peppercorn and dried chili (depending on personal preference for spiciness), stir-fry slowly on low heat to bring out the fragrance of the Sichuan peppercorn and chili. Be careful not to use too high heat to avoid burning.

5. When the chili color is about to change, immediately turn off the heat, pour the oil and Sichuan peppercorn and chili from the pot into the large bowl containing the fish carefully! It's very hot!!

Note:

1. The amount of water for cooking the fish should not be too much, just enough to cover the fish slices after they are added. After pouring into the bowl, some fish slices will be exposed outside.

2. Fry part of the Sichuan peppercorn and chili before cooking, during the cooking process, it can fully extract the red pigment from the chili, making the oil bright red.

3. When marinating the fish, do not add too much salt, otherwise it will be too salty and also destroy the freshness of the fish meat.

4. I think the Sichuan peppercorn and chili bought in the U.S. are not as fragrant as those in China, so it's better to fry them first before pouring oil. The materials in China are good, so you can directly pour hot oil over the chili and Sichuan peppercorn.

5. After eating the fish meat, you can put the soup back into the pot, add tofu or noodles or konjac, etc., and it becomes a hotpot of boiled fish. Or simply put the cooked fish into an electric hotpot at the beginning, after eating the fish, just turn on the fire.

Boiled Fish Recipe

Ingredients: 250g fish meat, 150g green garlic, 100g celery heart, 15g dried chili, 40g Pixian broad bean paste, 200g clear oil, 15g soy sauce, 1g monosodium glutamate, 10g ginger slices, 15g garlic slices, appropriate amount of water bean starch and clear soup.

Making method:

1. Remove the bones and thorns from the fish (generally speaking, it's better to choose boneless fish), cut the fish meat into thin slices measuring 5cm long and 3cm wide, place in a bowl, marinate with soy sauce and cooking wine, mix evenly with water bean starch.

2. Wash the green garlic and celery thoroughly, cut separately into segments and blocks measuring 6.5cm long.

3. Heat oil in the pot, add dried chili and Sichuan peppercorn, fry until brownish red (do not burn, just to extract color and aroma), remove and chop finely.

4. Heat the original oil in the pot, add green garlic and celery, stir-fry until cooked, then place in a dish.

5. Heat oil in the pot, add Pixian broad bean paste, stir-fry until red appears, add soup (the amount should be appropriate, too much makes it tasteless; too little makes the bean starch easy to fall off, the soup thickens) and simmer slightly, remove the broad bean paste residue, put the green garlic, cabbage, and celery back into the soup pot, add soy sauce, monosodium glutamate, cooking wine, white pepper, salt, ginger slices, garlic slices, cook thoroughly and flavorfully, then scoop into a deep dish or lotus leaf bowl.

6. Pour the fish slices into the original soup pot with slightly boiling soup (the soup should be slightly boiling. If it's not boiling, the bean starch will fall off; if it's boiling too vigorously, the fish slices will easily become tough). Use chopsticks to gently spread them out, once cooked, pour into the prepared dish or bowl, sprinkle with dried chili powder and Sichuan peppercorn powder, then pour boiling oil immediately to enhance the rich spicy aroma.

One Method of Making Boiled Fish

Preparation before making:

1. One grass carp with scales and fins removed, 2.5-3.5 pounds (those who like fish heads can also use bighead carp);

2. One pound of yellow bean sprouts (blanch in boiling water until eight-tenths cooked and set aside);

3. A small bag of榨cabbage ("Yuquan" brand is fine);

4. Peel two heads of garlic, remove the roots and set aside (need to lightly smash with the knife side);

5. Cut a piece of peeled ginger into slices (about 50mm square);

6. Appropriate amount of Sichuan peppercorn and dried chili (based on my experience, "Weihaomei" brand can be used, about one small bag each);

7. A small bowl of salad oil;

Start now:

1. Chop off the fish head and split it into two halves;

2. Place the fish body flat and use a sharp knife to separate the two large pieces of fish meat and fish bones;

3. Continue to slice the two large pieces of fish meat into appropriate-sized fish slices;

4. Divide the fish bones into three or four sections and place with the fish head for standby;

5. Put one egg white, a little salt, dry cornstarch, and cooking wine into the fish slices, mix evenly (best to mix with hands);

Now you can start cooking:

1. Pour a small bowl of salad oil, all Sichuan peppercorn and chili into the pot, fry slowly on low heat;

2. After the chili changes color, use a spatula to scoop out half of the oil and Sichuan peppercorn and chili, set aside;

3. Turn the heat up, add the smashed garlic cloves and ginger slices, once fragrant, add the fish head and fish bones;

4. Stir-fry twice, pour about a small bowl of cooking wine, half a teaspoon of salt, add three or four bowls of boiling water;

5. Once the fish head soup boils and flavors come out, place the fish slices one by one into the boiling soup;

6. The fish slices will cook quickly, before serving, add an appropriate amount of chicken essence, white pepper powder, and Sichuan pepper powder;

Ready to serve in a basin:

1. Must use a large enough basin (electric hotpot can also be considered), place the already blanched yellow bean sprouts and榨cabbage in the basin;

2. Pour the fish slices and soup into this basin;

3. Finally, pour the scooped out chili and oil on top;

Some points to note

One, Fire control, absolutely cannot fry the Sichuan peppercorn until burnt, since it's fried with oil, do not use high heat, otherwise it will all be burnt affecting appetite and health.

Two, The taste of the fish meat is marinated beforehand, so cannot adjust flavor again later as in general methods, must marinate thoroughly. Can add a little chicken essence and more salt.
