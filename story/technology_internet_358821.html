“【Suo Ha】” Introduction

The Suo Ha game is mainly popular in Guangdong, Hong Kong, and Macao of China. Due to its simplicity and excitement, it contains both skills and a large element of luck, so it has spread widely. Since the Suo Ha game is often used for gambling, it is often mistakenly regarded as a gambling game. In fact, the Suo Ha game itself has no gambling color. Playing this game well requires good memory, comprehensive judgment, calm analytical ability, and some luck. The art of playing cards reflects life, and participating in the game with the right attitude is undoubtedly a reflection on oneself.

[Game Rules]

The Suo Ha game uses 28 poker cards, taking the 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A of spades, hearts, clubs, and diamonds for the game. The number of players can be 2-4 people.

After the game starts, each person is dealt 2 cards first. The second card is revealed automatically, and everyone can see the open card. Starting from the person with the larger card face, betting proceeds clockwise. The person who bets first can choose “not to add” (not to bet chips). After someone else bets, then consider whether to follow or raise. After someone bets, others who want to continue can choose “followed” (bet the same number of chips as the previous player), also choose to raise (bet more chips than the previous player), or choose the “abandon” button (abandon the already bet chips). When the conditions for all-in in the game room are met, you can choose "all-in", which increases the bet to the maximum chip value allowed by the game room.

The final winner gets all the chips on the table in this round.

Please note: If the user's chips are less than the maximum all-in value allowed by the game room, they will no longer be able to play the Suo Ha game.

It must be emphasized that gambling using Suo Ha is strictly prohibited. Once discovered, all responsibilities lie with the user.

2. Card Type Explanation

Straight Flush: A straight of the same suit

Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank

Full House: Three cards of the same rank with a pair

Flush: Five cards of the same suit

Straight: Five consecutive cards, 8 and A do not count as consecutive

Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank

Two Pair: Two pairs

Pair: Two cards of the same rank

High Card: Other card types that do not form any of the above combinations

3. Card Type Comparison

(1) Card Types: Straight Flush > Four of a Kind > Full House > Flush > Straight > Three of a Kind > Two Pair > Pair > High Card

(2) Card Values: A > K > Q > J > 10 > 9 > 8

(3) Suits: Spades > Hearts > Clubs > Diamonds

(4) Straight Flush: Compare the largest card value, if the same, compare the suits

(5) Four of a Kind: Compare the card values of the four cards of the same rank

(6) Full House: Compare the card values of the three cards of the same rank

(7) Flush: Compare the largest card value, if the same, compare the second largest card value, and so on

(8) Straight: Compare the largest card value, if the same, compare the suit of the largest card

(9) Three of a Kind: Compare the card values of the three cards of the same rank

(10) Two Pair: Compare the size of the larger pair, if the same, compare the smaller pair, if still the same, compare the single card, if still the same, compare the largest suit in the larger pair.

(11) Pair: Compare the card values of the two cards of the same rank, if the same, compare the sizes of the remaining three cards sequentially. If all sizes are the same, compare the largest suit in the pair.

(12) High Card: Compare the largest card, if the same, compare the second largest card value, if the five card values are all the same, compare the suit of the largest card.

4. Game Terminology

(1) Follow: Bet the same amount of chips as the previous player

(2) Raise: Increase the bet based on the previous player's bet

(3) Abandon: Give up the already bet chips and lose

(4) All-in: Increase the bet to the maximum chip value allowed by the game room

(5) Not Add: Do not bet chips

5. Game Rules

After revealing the card, every time a card is dealt, betting starts clockwise from the person with the larger card face. Other users choose options like "follow" or "abandon" based on their own cards.

6. Points Explanation

The gain or loss points in the game are the same as the gain or loss of wealth values in the game, i.e., winning 1000 wealth points on the table corresponds to +1000 points; losing 1000 wealth points on the table corresponds to -1000 points.

If a user escapes, the escaper is responsible for paying all the chips on the table to the user with the largest open card at the time, and other users' chips remain unchanged.
