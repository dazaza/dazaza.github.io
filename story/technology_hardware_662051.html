by nbtie943 on 2012-02-09 16:49:17

Lou Hongshun has to take care of his critically ill wife and young son, but he still remains unrepentant. A serious illness of his wife has shaken a happy family of three - the life of Feng Hua, 32 years old, used to be a happy and complete family. However, a serious illness last year completely changed the fate of the whole family. Although his wife's condition worsened and she was on the verge of death, her husband Lou Hongshun did not give up the hope of curing his wife - she, sitting in a wheelchair, hopes to stand up again and hold her husband's hand to go out and play with their child. On one hand, he takes good care of his paralyzed wife and young son; on the other hand, he studies medical knowledge hard to understand his wife's condition. Despite being financially ruined, 38-year-old Lou Hongshun still sticks to his wife's dream.

- Reported by our reporter Chen Min, with photography

At around 2 p.m. on January 10, 2010, Feng Hua suddenly felt an intense headache while doing housework at home and then almost fainted. Taking advantage of the last bit of consciousness, Feng Hua called her husband. Upon hearing the news, Lou Hongshun rushed back home and quickly dialed 120 to send his wife to Haikou People's Hospital.

According to Lou Hongshun's account, his wife had suffered from neck injury during childhood due to a violent collision, causing cervical spine displacement. Over the past decade, she intermittently experienced severe headaches. Thinking that this time it was just a recurrence of her old ailment, little did they know that after more than two hours of diagnosis, the hospital informed Lou Hongshun that his wife was detected with subarachnoid hemorrhage, or left anterior communicating artery aneurysm, which required immediate treatment. Following the doctor's advice, Lou Hongshun accepted the cerebral vascular interventional embolization therapy. Three days later, Feng Hua's condition did not improve but instead deteriorated further, resulting in high fever accompanied by convulsions. In the following half a month, the hospital conducted six lumbar puncture surgeries, but the deterioration continued. By March, Feng Hua, lying in bed, had completely lost her ability to take care of herself. Adding insult to injury, during his wife's critical illness, neglecting his young child led to the boy developing a high fever without timely detection. Fortunately, friends and relatives came to the rescue in time, preventing a tragedy.

During the entire 56-day hospitalization, Feng Hua underwent multiple surgeries on her head and waist. Lou Hongshun spent over 200,000 yuan on medical expenses but received no good news. With all savings exhausted and dissatisfaction with the hospital's treatment method, Lou Hongshun took his already completely paralyzed wife home.

With tender care, using love to protect his wife's dream

As each surgery failed, Feng Hua's limbs began to atrophy. Combined with long-term convulsions, her neck, right arm, and other joints showed twisted situations that could not be restored temporarily. To alleviate this situation, Lou Hongshun repeatedly massaged various parts of his wife's body and continuously encouraged her to stay strong and not give up. During her hospitalization, Feng Hua often couldn't sleep due to pain, so Lou Hongshun stayed up all night by her side, telling her funny stories about their son to make her happy and divert her attention. Later, Feng Hua also developed issues with her brain nerves, frequently experiencing various hallucinations and sometimes talking nonsense. Every time these symptoms appeared, Lou Hongshun treated her like a child, patiently complying with her requests and coaxing her. Once, Feng Hua kept shouting "I'm itchy," so Lou Hongshun asked her where it itched. She kept staring at the "quiet" character on the hospital wall, loudly shouting, "It's itchy there at 'quiet.' Scratch it for me." Lou Hongshun, completely ignoring others' strange looks, obeyed his wife's wishes and gently scratched the "quiet" character on the wall while asking if she felt better. He only stopped when she said she wasn't itchy anymore.

A Mr. Yang told reporters that Lou Hongshun's wife, during her hospitalization, shared the same ward as his critically ill mother. "His wife later became incontinent, yet he didn't mind at all. He always made sure to keep his wife clean and tidy every day." In Mr. Yang's view, Lou Hongshun is not only a model husband but also a very optimistic person.

Never abandoning, selling everything to help his wife stand up again

Before his wife was hospitalized, Lou Hongshun ran an advertising shop, and his wife also had a well-paid job, earning over 100,000 yuan annually. Since his wife started receiving treatment in the hospital, Lou Hongshun sold the shop he had painstakingly operated for many years, and all his savings accumulated over more than ten years were invested into the expensive medical expenses. Despite the family being financially ruined, Lou Hongshun did not give up hope. He moved out of the big house and rented a less than 20 square meter civilian house in Qianyanpo, Longkun South, Haikou, where the family of three squeezed into the narrow space. To ensure his wife received better nutrition, Lou Hongshun was willing to skip a meal himself so that Feng Hua could have rich meals for every meal.

Nowadays, Lou Hongshun has quit all his jobs and is fully devoted to taking care of his sick wife and young son. After bringing his wife home from the hospital, he not only has to take care of the daily lives of his wife and son but also tries various methods to control Feng Hua's condition. "I believe that as long as I don't give up, there will always be hope," Lou Hongshun said. Even in the hardest times, he was unwilling to give up treatment.

Studying medicine, just to understand his wife's pain better

When Lou Hongshun sold all the things he could sell, he deliberately kept a computer. Because he wanted to use the internet to collect information related to his wife's illness and consult relevant experts online.

Lou Hongshun also bought a copy of "Neurosurgery Handbook" and "Surgery Science". In his spare time, he buried himself in hard study. "Every day my wife has to fight against the pain, as her husband, I feel deeply sorry for her. I want to understand this kind of pain." This is the only belief that supports Lou Hongshun's hard study of medicine.

At the end of the interview, Lou Hongshun frankly told the reporter that he plans to gather some money to send his wife to a better hospital for examination. "If my wife really can't be saved, I hope she doesn't leave with any regrets." Lou Hongshun said word by word.
