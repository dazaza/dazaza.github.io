WeChat Launches Full Compensation Guarantee after being Criticized by Alipay for Being Unsafe

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:19:53

Core Tip

Not long ago, Fan Zhiming, President of Domestic Business Group of Alibaba Micro Financial Services Group (under preparation), criticized WeChat's "unsafe payment" and said that "WeChat Moments has already become a base for selling counterfeit goods."

Just one day later, relevant responsible persons from Tencent responded by stating that WeChat Pay is currently the safest mobile payment tool on the market. They have cooperated with PICC Property, the largest property insurer in Asia, to provide users with full compensation guarantees.

"WeChat Moments has already become a base for selling counterfeit goods." The other day, Alibaba Finance launched the new generation of Alipay Wallet. Regarding their biggest competitor, Fan Zhiming, President of Domestic Business Group of Alibaba Micro Financial Services Group (under preparation), criticized thusly, believing that the biggest problem with WeChat Pay at present is safety issues.

Just one day later, WeChat announced yesterday that they would cooperate with PICC Property, the largest property insurer in Asia, to provide users with full compensation guarantees. Relevant responsible persons from Tencent stated that WeChat Pay is currently the safest mobile payment tool on the market, and there have been almost no losses so far.

Alibaba Executive Criticism:

WeChat Moments "is a base for selling counterfeit goods"

The other day, Alibaba Finance launched version 7.6 of Alipay Wallet; prior to this, WeChat version 5.0 officially launched the WeChat Pay function. WeChat, which controls over 400 million users, declared its entry into the payment field, sparking heated discussions within the industry about whether it will shake up the original market position of Alibaba's Alipay business. Multiple executives from Alibaba Micro Finance expressed that day, "WeChat poses little threat to us."

However, through the new features of version 7.6 of Alipay Wallet, one can see that they are actively planning to advance onto WeChat's territory. In this version, the Alipay Open Platform makes its first appearance, meaning that users can add public service accounts similar to WeChat within the Alipay Wallet and use third-party application functions. Currently, merchants include CITIC Bank, Ping An Bank, China Mobile, China Unicom, etc., allowing users to check bills, change phone packages, etc., services very similar to those provided on WeChat Official Accounts.

Fan Zhiming stated that these two products belong to different fields: one is in the payment domain, the other in instant messaging. WeChat starts from user service, with over 400 million users and continuously increasing daily user activity, making it a huge mobile payment entry point in a short time, forcing Alipay to raise its guard and quickly upgrade functions in the new version to counter WeChat Pay.

Fan Zhiming also criticized: "WeChat Moments has already become a base for selling counterfeit goods." Fan Zhiming stated that WeChat is merely an information exchange tool and cannot guarantee security. From a security perspective, Alipay has been operating for ten years. Chen Dawei, Director of the Research Institute of Alibaba Micro Financial Services, also stated that the Achilles' heel of WeChat Pay is security. Due to the lack of accounts and reliance on card binding, if a WeChat ID is stolen, there is a risk to funds.

In addition, Chen Dawei revealed that Alibaba will not consider P2P business for the time being. The reason is that the P2P default rate is as high as 2%, and the market has not matured to a certain degree, with low recognition of risks.

WeChat Quick Response:

Partnering with PICC Property to launch full compensation

Just one day after Alipay questioned the security risks of WeChat Pay, yesterday, WeChat announced cooperation with PICC Property, the largest property insurer in Asia, to provide users with full compensation guarantees, launching the slogan "Dare to pay, dare to compensate." It is understood that if users suffer losses due to theft while using WeChat Pay, they will receive full compensation from PICC.

According to Tencent insiders, so far, the preliminary application scenarios of WeChat Pay include payment within WeChat Official Accounts, QR code scanning payments, and App-initiated payments. Currently, WeChat Pay is not fully open, and merchants are mainly B2C, all requiring strict certification. It is understood that the first batch of online merchants covers multiple sub-sectors of e-commerce, such as air ticket booking, online shopping, movie ticket group buying, transportation card recharging, and some categories of goods and services, specifically including Yixun, Yihaodian, Vipshop, Hong Kong Airlines, Hainan Airlines, Dianping, and other well-known merchants.

In fact, recently, some Yu'E Bao users reported that 40,000 yuan in funds had been mysteriously transferred out. Previously, the media reported that Mr. Huang, after the launch of Yu'E Bao by Alipay, subscribed to the service and deposited 300,000 yuan into his Yu'E Bao account. On October 12th, without Mr. Huang's knowledge, 40,000 yuan was transferred out of his account.

Yuan Leming, General Manager of Alibaba Micro Financial Services Division, responded that there indeed exists a risk of theft for Yu'E Bao, with a theft risk rate of one in ten thousand. "From the transaction amount and number of transactions of Yu'E Bao, its theft risk rate is not high," Yuan Leming said simultaneously. He also indicated that theft risks cannot be completely avoided, therefore, Yu'E Bao promises that in case of theft, the cooperating party Ping An Insurance will provide full compensation to consumers through theft insurance.
