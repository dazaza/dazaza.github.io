Mobile QQ 2013, I think it's fine!

by geekzhang on 2013-05-17 08:56:28

Author: Liu WenzhiThe following content may cause discomfort to some people, please choose to ignore it if necessary. Today, I installed and experienced the mobile QQ 2013 with tens of thousands of bad reviews. I felt that Mobile QQ 2013 was much better than before, at least, I would keep using it. Seeing many people complaining and criticizing, I decided to record my own feelings, just my personal feelings:

1. Product structure and interaction experience, although imitating WeChat, but reasonable and justified, no problem.

Many people criticize this point, but I think this is where the mobile QQ team has matured. Since WeChat has already cultivated user habits, then follow this line of thinking. So-called innovation, etc., is not suitable for the current stage of mobile QQ. Innovation is risky, and mobile QQ clearly needs a safer strategy now. First of all, it must ensure that it can live well and prosperously, and then talk about innovation. After all, it bears important responsibilities, and at this time, what is needed is almost cold-blooded rationality and decision-making.

As for the online status issue. Product development has a process. The changes in the new version are relatively large, and the construction of the main structure is the most important milestone of this version. From the main structure of Mobile QQ 2013, I personally feel that it is very practical. Lists and online statuses are merely an iteration version of up to 2-3 weeks, but the premise is that the foundation and structure have no problems.

It may not be the most perfect, but it should be a relatively reasonable choice. So I feel it's fine, no problem!

2. From the perspective of usage scenarios, embracing mobile internet, no problem.

Due to the huge difference in usage scenarios between mobile and PC, the previous mobile QQ always used a mindset that extended from the PC end to the mobile end. It can be seen that the continuation of this mindset did not bring much development to mobile QQ, and was even surpassed by WeChat when mobile QQ was complacent.

Now, Mobile QQ is built according to the thinking of mobile internet. Personally, I think this is in line with trends and trends, and also meets the needs of mobile client usage, which is going with the flow. Just say that many people are still looking at mobile QQ with the habits of traditional QQ, this shift is a process, a bit painful, but it is a beginning, a courageous beginning. If we stick to the old ways and continue to cling to the set of practices on the PC end, I believe everyone has seen the situation of the previous mobile QQ, I won't say more.

This shift, I think there is no problem.

3. From the perspective of product decision-making, no problem.

Making such changes requires determination and courage. I believe that the seasoned mobile QQ product team is not unaware of the effects of such a shift, nor would they impulsively do it without thinking. Such changes must have been backed and worked on in various ways behind the scenes. It is not an exaggeration to say that this is a difficult decision. Is this self-revolution?

Rest assured, users will still curse while using it. Tencent's mobile QQ's grasp of user habits is absolutely top-notch (to be honest, there are few teams that can go through such a large user base and such a long period of product operation), so don't use those tens of thousands of bad reviews to say anything, it doesn't represent anything. There is a saying, "Don't worry about giving you one star, worry about not even giving a star." To put it bluntly, even if the new version of mobile QQ released today does not have a message sending button, these users will find a way to send messages tomorrow because it is QQ, it's that simple.

The new version is risky, but this risk has been measured and must be endured.

4. Looking at Tencent's internal free-range and competition from the perspective of Mobile QQ 2013.

About the story of mobile QQ and WeChat, I won't say much. That fire lit by WeChat, who got burned the most, mobile QQ knows it best. The release of the 2013 version could not possibly be done without review or just by a snap decision within the company. It can be clearly known that this was deliberately done. I believe that the senior management of Tencent (even Pony Ma) have nodded their heads.

We say that making products should respect users and be user-centered, but products are ultimately not charity, they are manifestations of commercial behavior. Being user-centered is only a principle to ensure commercial behavior. Therefore, if making products does not have its own stance, it will be led around by the nose. Wasn't mobile QQ dragged far enough before?

This time, aiming at WeChat to do it, it's simple, openly saying that I'll play your way with you. I have several hundred million users behind me, who am I afraid of? Who is afraid of whom? At worst, we homogenize!

This is a declaration of war, this is determination!

As for Tencent's consistent strategy, healthy competition among product teams is necessary. Playing like this, the company that benefits the most is the company itself, so let you guys go all out!

Just wrote down what I saw and felt myself, it may not necessarily be right, welcome to exchange ideas, trolls automatically avoid!
