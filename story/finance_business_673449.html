Take a Walk in the Boston Parks

The City of Boston represents a liberty icon, and people expect so much from it when they come here. A city filled with amusement and entertainment, Boston is jam-packed with excitement and events. There are numerous beautiful parks in Boston, and many tourists enjoy taking a walk in them. One of these is Castle Island and Fort Independence, where you can enjoy peaceful walks and capture an amazing view of the harbor. Ames Nowell Park is great if you are fond of boating or fishing, offering numerous facilities for these activities. Nature lovers should visit the Horseneck Beach State Reservation while in Boston and enjoy the sandy beaches there. All types of parks can be found in the city of Boston, and they are a pleasant way to spend your free time while in Boston.

Attending the Boston Marathon

The Boston Marathon is a significant event on the schedule of every athlete in the country. The marathon is the oldest competition of its kind in the modern era and is also one of the five Major Marathons in the world. The race is held annually on the streets of the city on Patriots' Day in April. Nowadays, it is one of the most special and well-known racing events worldwide. Both amateurs and professionals gather here for a roughly 42-kilometer run on several routes from downtown Boston, with the Boston Public Library as the finish line. People run in this event for different reasons, such as athletic achievements or cash prizes. When you are in Boston in April, you can arrange your stay at one of the numerous Boston hotels and either participate in or attend this major sports event.

Things to See in the Boston Common

When in Boston, you should not miss the Boston Common, one of the most popular attractions in the city. This is the centerpiece of the Emerald Necklace, a generic name for the parks that are spread throughout the city. It was built in 1634 and has been used for many activities, from military purposes to public hangings. Martin Luther King Jr., Gloria Steinem, and Pope John Paul II gave speeches to the public here. Nowadays, the park is a public one, open to the community. You can view many historical monuments here. Several historical monuments are located within this park. George F. Parkman is honored here with a statue due to his significant contribution to the preservation of the Boston Common. The Civil War troops are honored at the Soldiers and Sailors Monument located on Flagstaff Hill. You can bring your kids here to play in the summer or skate during the winter.
