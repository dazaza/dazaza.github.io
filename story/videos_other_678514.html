Chapter 1: "Mother, please don't let Brother go. Father hasn't returned yet. We must at least bid farewell to him." In front of the Bai General's residence, Young Master Bai of the Bai family had a pair of beautifully arched eyebrows knitted together tightly, and with both small hands, he clutched onto the hem of a youth who was a head taller than him.

A young woman stepped forward and gently pried her son's hands away, consoling him tenderly, "Your father has been invited by Mr. Jiang on urgent state affairs; how could he return so soon? Tang'er, be obedient. Your elder brother is going to Shaolin, and time cannot be delayed."

But no matter how she coaxed him, Young Master Bai refused to let go. He had always been close to this elder brother, even though the elder brother wasn’t much of a talker, he was incredibly kind to him. With tear-filled eyes, he stared at his elder brother, "Brother, I don't want you to go," his voice trembling slightly as if about to cry.

The elder brother felt genuinely heartbroken inside. He stroked his younger brother’s head and gave a faint smile, revealing the dimples in his cheeks, "Tang’er, be good. I'll be back soon. Shall I bring you something tasty?"

"Cheater! I clearly heard Mother and Father say that you have to observe filial piety for three years!" Upon hearing these words, Elder Bai's face turned sorrowful, his brows involuntarily furrowing. But quickly, he bent down and embraced his younger brother, shielding him from Concubine Jin's view, adjusting his emotions to mask them.

"Tang’er, it's only three years, it will pass quickly. You're already a big boy now, why are you still crying, hmm?"

"I'm not crying!" Rubbing his slightly red nose tip, "Then it's settled. You must definitely buy me some treats. I want candied haws, sweet buns, sugar-glazed rice cakes, sticky rice dumplings, and..."

Elder Bai shook his head with a wry smile. His younger brother had always been like this since childhood – despite having a 'Tang' character in his name, he had an insatiable love for sweets. "Alright, I'll buy everything you want. You must listen to Father and not upset him anymore. Otherwise, you'll be in for a treat!"

"Hmph," Bai Tang raised his little face, "I'm not afraid." And with a mischievous grin, his narrow phoenix-like eyes were indeed captivating.

Concubine Jin, seeing her son being so close to his elder brother, was quite displeased. She abruptly pulled her son aside, "It's getting late, He'er. You know your maternal home didn't provide much dowry. The household expenses here are more outgoing than incoming, and we can hardly gather enough silver. Here are some scattered coins, take them with you. It's not like home outside..."

"Thank you, Auntie." Bai He coldly interrupted before Concubine Jin finished speaking, took the money, and walked directly towards the carriage.

"Hmph, ungrateful brat." Concubine Jin curled her lips, not speaking too loudly but just loud enough for everyone present to hear. Bai He clenched his fists, trying hard to suppress his anger, increasingly convinced that Mother's advice was correct – he needed to leave this "home."

"Brother, let me go, let me go!"

"Ahh, you little rat, you actually bit me!" Originally, Bai Tang wanted to chase after Bai He, but unable to break free from Concubine Jin's grip, he bit her hand instead. Though wanting to chase and beat Bai Tang, Concubine Jin ultimately couldn't bring herself to do it, lowering her raised hand.

"Brother," Bai Tang suddenly hugged his elder brother from behind, "You must come back, I'll wait for you properly."

Bai He patted his younger brother's small hands, "When you grow as tall as me, I'll be back."

Saying this, he pried off the tightly clinging hands wrapped around his waist, turned around without looking back, and instructed the coachman. The carriage slowly moved forward. Sitting inside the carriage, Bai He closed his eyes tightly. Bai Tang's voice, filled with a sob, grew fainter and fainter until it disappeared completely. Slowly exhaling, in this world, the one who truly cared about him was this younger brother. Looking out of the carriage window, watching the city where he had lived for thirteen years, Su City, he softly muttered, "When I return, I will be the master of this city!"

As dusk fell, Bai He and his servant arrived at a remote village. Asking the coachman, they learned it would take at least ten days to reach Shaolin. Bai He thought for a moment and said, "Uncle Zhong, there's no need to rush so much. Traveling day and night might be too much for your health."

"The young master is observing filial piety and should hurry to Shaolin to pray before the Buddha on behalf of the Lady."

"Observing filial piety, it's all about intention, not those formalities. If Mother sees us from heaven, she wouldn't want me to exhaust you, would she?"

Upon hearing this, Uncle Zhong was moved to tears, "Lady was so kind-hearted. If it weren't for her taking me in when I was old and frail, I would have surely perished long ago! Alas! Heaven has blind eyes, why should Lady endure so much suffering! Most hateful is that Jin woman. If it weren't for her crying and begging for help in front of Lady's carriage back then, she would have been sold to a brothel long ago. Yet she repays kindness with enmity! Once inside the house, she did so many evil things. Now, with the young master traveling far and alone, without relatives or acquaintances, she even provided such shabby carriages, dressed in common servants' clothes! This Jin woman..."

Uncle Zhong grew angrier as he spoke but was interrupted by Bai He, "Uncle Zhong, these are old matters, let's not mention them. For Mother, perhaps dying is better than living."

Upon hearing this, Uncle Zhong knew the young master was thinking of their deceased mother again. He scolded himself for causing unnecessary sadness to the young master and wiped away his tears, saying, "It's all my fault. Lady was such a kind person, she must have reached the Western Paradise by now. Young master, stop worrying, otherwise, Lady won't rest peacefully either."

Bai Zhong gave a bitter laugh, "Uncle Zhong is right. Now that it's just the two of us outside, calling each other master and servant might cause trouble. If bandits overhear, they might get ideas. Let's call each other uncle and nephew instead, pretending to visit relatives. What do you think?"

"This, this, this is too much honor for me."

After Bai He's persuasion, Uncle Zhong finally agreed, thinking that the servants and maids in the mansion always said the young man was dull and sullen, sometimes even snapping at the servants. Now seeing him, the young master was not only intelligent but also kind to his subordinates. Clearly, what he had heard was false, likely another rumor spread by Jin. This trip, upon returning to the mansion, he must praise the young master well to the master!

Inside the carriage, Bai He removed his brocade robe and changed into the servant's clothes prepared by Concubine Jin. Bai He sneered inwardly – Jin was indeed clever, placing some brocade robes on top of the chest while the lower layers were all servant's clothes. This suited Bai He's intentions perfectly. People often say wealth shouldn't be flaunted; this way, it was safer. Taking off the jade pendant around his neck, which his mother left him, this jade pendant was the token of affection given to his mother by his father during their wedding. Remembering how happy his mother used to be when recounting stories with his father, ever since Concubine Jin entered as a concubine, his mother's health deteriorated day by day. She would often sit alone in her room, lost in sorrow, muttering, "Lingfeng, how could you change? Why?"

Her tears flowed incessantly, her complexion worsening day by day, accumulating worries into illness, becoming increasingly severe in recent years, eventually passing away untreated.

Collecting his thoughts, "Since I've decided to leave this home, Bai He, why dwell on it? I, Bai He, will show you all what I'm capable of!"

In Su City, the Bai Mansion.

"The master has returned." Concubine Jin hurriedly welcomed Master Bai into the house and ordered the servants to prepare dinner, sending a maid to invite the second young master.

"Master, why do you look so worried?"

"Has He'er left? When did he leave?" Although disliking Bai He normally, he was still his biological son, and leaving alone made him somewhat concerned.

"Master, not to criticize He'er, but he's become increasingly disrespectful! Ten days ago, I prepared his belongings, but he rejected everything I prepared, saying... saying he doesn't want anything from me, and..." Sobbing ensued.

Master Bai noticed her bandaged hand, pretending to pull it back slightly, then leaning against his shoulder, crying softly.

"This unfilial son, arguing with me is one thing, but today daring to defy me like this when I'm not around!"

"Big Brother didn't do that!" Bai Tang's childish voice rang out, glaring angrily at Concubine Jin.

Concubine Jin panicked, "Tang'er, how dare you be so impolite! Quickly bow to your father."

"Hmph! You're a big liar!" Bai Tang pointed at Concubine Jin, his little face flushed red with anger.

"Tang'er! How dare you be so disrespectful! Apologize to your mother immediately!"

"Hmph, you're a fool! Fool!" Then he ran out of the hall.

"Quickly, catch him, make him stand against the wall for reflection! Without my order, he's not allowed to leave the house!"

Master Bai roared with anger.

Chapter 3: After being confined by Master Bai for a few days, Bai Tang was released through Concubine Jin's plea. Concubine Jin delivered a stern lecture, but Bai Tang paid no attention, letting it go in one ear and out the other. Seizing the opportunity when the tutor wasn't paying attention, he slipped out of the private school through an abandoned garden. Behind this garden was a narrow path, usually taken by people seeking shortcuts. Blocking the hole in the wall, Second Young Master Bai leisurely crossed a patch of weeds towards a forest. This forest was discovered secretly by his elder brother when they were children. From the outside, the forest seemed ordinary, but inside was a flat grassland with wildflowers and a stream. This was their secret base.

To this day, Bai Tang vividly remembered the scene two years ago when he secretly followed his brother here. At that time, Bai He was crying – it was the first time Bai Tang saw his elder brother cry. Recalling today's scolding of his elder brother, Bai Tang overheard the servants mentioning that Madam's condition seemed to have worsened. Feeling guilty, although still young, he inadvertently heard the servants blaming his mother. Everyone blamed his mother, maybe she really was wrong?

Only after seeing his elder brother calm down did Bai Tang sneak up and cover Bai He's eyes, "Big Brother, guess who I am?"

Bai He chuckled, "Sugar, you little idiot!" Grabbing Bai Tang behind him, he began tickling him, "Ah, ah, Big Brother, stop! Hahaha, it's itchy, itchy."

Stopping, Bai He asked, "How long have you been following me?"

Bai Tang smirked, "I didn't follow you, I found out earlier while playing behind the private school."

"Hmm?"

Seeing Bai He's skeptical gaze, Bai Tang grinned, "You can come, why can't I?"

Walking to the stream and jumping around, "Big Brother, there are fish, there are fish!" Suddenly pushing Bai He into the river.

"Alright, Sugar, you dared to trick Big Brother, now it's your turn!" Both played joyfully in the stream. Thinking about it now, Bai Tang couldn't help but smile, but those days were gone forever.

Recently, Bai Tang often sat melancholically by the stream. "Brother, are you alright? Brother, I miss eating candy; Brother, no one rides me as a horse anymore since you left, Brother..."

Bai Tang was gazing at a cloud shaped like a bun in the sky when someone pinched his neck, "Little thing, don't shout, or else..."

"Not, not, Big Brother, there's no one to save me here, right?"

"Hmph, I doubt a little kid like you can stir up any trouble." The black-clothed man released his hand and sat next to Bai Tang, "Brat, go get something to eat."

"You, why is there blood on your corner of your mouth?" Bai Tang asked softly, fearing he might anger the black-clothed man and endanger his life.

"Brat, remember, don't ask questions you shouldn't. Even telling you isn't harmful considering your age. I... am the one who just assassinated Her Majesty the Empress!" The black-clothed man looked arrogantly at the child, thinking he wouldn't dare report it anyway. Unexpectedly, the child only looked astonished and excitedly asked, "Can you teach me swordsmanship?"

The black-clothed man smiled faintly, this child was unique, "Are you not afraid of me?"

"Heh, if you wanted to kill me, you would have done it already. Besides, you still need my help finding food, right?"

The black-clothed man raised an eyebrow, never expecting kids these days to be so smart, "Alright, if you can handle this, I'll take you as my disciple."

"Good, you must keep your word, lying makes you a dog." Bai Tang confidently smiled and left. When he returned, he brought not only food but also a cotton coat, "I think your clothes should be changed too, so you can leave the city easier. There's no news spreading in the city yet. Are these buns enough?"

"How didn't you escape when you had the chance, and came back instead? Do you really want to learn martial arts that much?" The black-clothed man asked while changing clothes.

"I, Bai Tang, once I decide on something, I won't change!" Saying this, he raised his little face.

"Ho, big words! So, apprentice, how do you plan to settle your master?"

"Heh, that's simple, Master, follow me." They left the streamside, only the water continued flowing gently. Meanwhile, Li Miaomiao saw him flash a charming, sunny smile.

Chapter 4: After returning home, Bai Tang surprisingly took the initiative to apologize, admitting he shouldn't have skipped school to play, claiming he was bullied by older children and was saved by this uncle. To thank his benefactor, he hoped to find work in the mansion and pleaded with Concubine Jin not to tell his father, fearing another scolding. Always favoring her own son, Concubine Jin readily agreed. Observing the benefactor, who appeared about thirty years old with a slightly gray complexion, slightly unkempt beard, and lively eyes, yet rather thin, "He seems honest. From now on, you will serve the young master. Although the mansion doesn't lack staff, if the young master likes you, it's your good fortune. Manager Wang, explain the rules to him. Today, reward him ten taels. Dismissed."

"Thank you, Mother. I'll take my leave." Not waiting for Concubine Jin's response, he rushed out of the hall.

That evening, when Master Bai returned home, he immediately ordered no one to go out easily and declined all invitations from court ministers, losing his appetite for dinner. Returning to his room, he remained deeply troubled.

"Master, you've established so many rules since you returned, pacing back and forth, making my head spin. What's happened? Tell me."

"Sigh. Someone assassinated the newly favored Consort Xie today, just after news of her pregnancy spread. Alas, it seems a storm is coming."

"What Master speaks of must be the Consort Xie from the common folk the Emperor bestowed the title upon. I heard the Emperor met her during an undercover visit, saying she resembled the former Crown Princess greatly. Truly, the Emperor is devoted. Tsk tsk, poor Consort Xie, enjoying only a few days of happiness before meeting her end."

"You foolish woman, do you know what you're saying? If these words spread, you'd lose your heads multiple times over!"

"Master, I was just talking to you. Why so angry?"

After some comforting words from Concubine Jin, Master Bai calmed down.

Jin Country, established sixty-three years ago, the current emperor Jin Wendi ascended the throne at thirty years old, achieving unprecedented prosperity within ten years. Emperor Wendi disliked women, despite having three thousand beauties in the palace, rarely indulging in their charms. Consequently, his progeny were scarce, with only two sons: Crown Prince Jin Xiangyu born to Consort Wu and registered under Empress Zhang, weak in health but gentle in nature, and Prince Jin Fengxi born to Empress Zhang, fond of martial arts and cheerful in demeanor.
