Writing is expressing your own thoughts through language. There are two elements here: language and thought. For successful writing, both are indispensable. When using our native language to write, we generally don’t need to spend much time or effort thinking about the language itself, so the focus is on the content. However, when learning to write in a foreign language, both language and content need attention. If the relationship between these two isn’t handled well, we may never master writing in a foreign language.

Generally speaking, there are three steps to learning how to write in a foreign language, although these steps cannot be strictly separated.

First, learn the basic vocabulary and grammar of the language. At this stage, learners haven’t yet mastered the basic usage of the foreign language, so they are still in the foundational language learning phase. Writing training at this point is usually non-existent, or if it exists, it’s only to practice vocabulary and grammar. These exercises are generally very simple in content, emphasizing that the vocabulary and grammar used by the learner must be correct.
