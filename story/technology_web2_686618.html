If you are a self-guided tourist to Fenghuang without contacting any travel agency, do not easily trust the cheap services recommended by departments or individuals without proper tourism qualifications. Due to the serious problem of touting (also known as "pig killing") in Fenghuang Ancient City, these cheap services often come with additional conditions that you may not know about before using them. If something happens to you, it will be difficult to trace. Your rights will not be guaranteed! (Common incidents include: urgent matters requiring cancellation of the itinerary; theft in the room; being kicked out of the guesthouse; harassment, etc.)

The price of rooms in Fenghuang Ancient City often fluctuates greatly, depending on weekends vs non-weekends, holidays vs non-holidays, peak season vs off-season. Therefore, don't compare room prices at different times, as this may affect your mood while traveling. As long as you think the amount you paid for the room matches the service you received, that's fine.

When traveling to Fenghuang Ancient City by yourself, you may often encounter blatant touts. Although this behavior is not allowed in Fenghuang, it is extremely difficult to control, so the problem of touting still exists. Most touts are women. When encountering them, it's best not to pay attention to them, or tell them: we already have friends who have arranged everything for us, or say we are going home.

If touts keep following you when you're looking for a guesthouse in Fenghuang, the owner may raise the price because these touts will tell the owner in the local dialect that you are their customers. The owner then has to give them a kickback, which actually comes from the tourists.

If you buy tickets for scenic spots at some legitimate ticket offices, they may later tell you that you were overcharged and offer you a very low price. They may provoke you to ask the office for a refund or follow their suggested itinerary. At this moment, do not easily believe them. Simply put, if they don't stir up trouble, they won't have any room to survive. Moreover, after you leave, they may go to the ticket office to demand kickbacks and cause trouble. There have been many such cases, sometimes even blocking tourists from boarding buses to scenic spots in order to get kickbacks. So, be sure to avoid dealing with these touts. They do not have any tourism business qualifications, and if something happens, you will have no protection.
