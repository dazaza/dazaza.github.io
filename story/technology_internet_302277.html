Why do companies build websites?

I. Seizing the strategic foothold for development in the new century

Regardless of what business you are in, you cannot ignore the importance of the Internet. In fact, as long as you go online and take a look, you may be surprised to find that your competitor's "webpage" has already been published on the Internet.

II. Building a new corporate image

On the Internet, a company’s image is no longer determined by its scale, strength, number of employees, performance, credibility, or history, but rather by a new set of rules, such as standardization, speed, convenience, friendliness, aesthetics, expert consultation, global comparison, personalized choices, interactive use, etc. Therefore, the old adage that “the big get bigger and the strong get stronger” no longer applies.

III. Directly opening up international markets

Through your website, you have already opened channels to enter almost every country’s market around the world. In fact, many companies that have not planned to export their products should realize that once your webpage is published, it will inevitably bring you international business opportunities. Needless to say, for export-oriented or companies intending to enter overseas markets, building a presence on the Internet is undoubtedly the most cost-effective way to open up international markets.

IV. Conducting 24-hour online marketing

Telephones, telegrams, faxes, and telexes are traditional communication tools you use to communicate with people and sell your products or services. However, through your website, you can more vividly, intuitively, and inexpensively promote yourself, your products, or your services to a wider audience. And it works non-stop, 24 hours a day!

V. Efficient and cost-effective targeted promotion

By casually entering a very obscure technical term in Yahoo, you might find that the search results number in the hundreds or even thousands. Even in highly specialized niche markets, you can find your peers and potential customers. By joining professional trade associations, news groups, and email lists online, you suddenly discover that your previously little-known products or services are now hot topics among many people.

VI. Establishing the most direct communication mechanism with customers

Through your website, you can provide cross-regional customer service around the clock: maintaining post-sale contact with customers, listening to customer opinions, and answering frequently asked questions.

VII. Shortening the cycle for launching new products and entering new markets

Launching new products and entering new markets can be expensive. A few large enterprises can spend huge amounts on advertising and promotions, but for most companies, there is neither enough money nor enough time to wait, collect, and analyze sufficient feedback to decide how to improve products or sales strategies for entering new markets. Through your website, you can showcase your new products with multimedia, detailing all their advantages and features without missing a thing; you can advertise and promote to more customers in new target markets at an extremely low cost. Then, you will find that opinions about new products and feedback from new markets come flooding in, and from this, you derive convincing data. Tasks that were previously time-consuming and costly are now quickly completed at a low cost, with results that are hard to believe.

VIII. Developing customer relationships with an attractive customer base

In general, domestic users who frequently use the Internet are mostly well-educated professionals earning high salaries or expected to earn high salaries. On the other hand, most universities in the country are connected via educational networks to non-commercial users, and college students are the most active group. Therefore, any domestic company that wants to create a brand and aims to grow big cannot ignore the Internet and its constantly expanding user base. Almost every successful businessman has had similar experiences: an accidental meeting where business cards are exchanged, resulting in big deals. In fact, every shrewd businessman knows that what matters is not how much you know, but how many people you know. On the Internet, you can send "business cards" to tens of thousands of potential customers, introducing them 24 hours a day to what you can do for them, and inviting them to contact you if needed.

IX. Maintaining contact with your own sales team

Salespeople across the country need to stay informed of the latest information and instructions from company headquarters, while the headquarters needs to understand the sales progress and performance of each branch. Through your website, only local call fees are required to connect, allowing secure and convenient communication between headquarters and branches, which significantly improves management efficiency and reduces operating costs.

X. Targeting the local market

Your website is a powerful tool for opening up international markets, but at the same time, you should not ignore the local market.
