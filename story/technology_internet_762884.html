This is an era of great change, an interesting era.

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:49:37

This is the notes of Brother Feng from attending a class at an international top business school. This is an era of great change, an interesting era.

One

The PRADA flagship store in New York. Each piece of clothing has an RFID code. Whenever a customer picks up a PRADA item and takes it into the fitting room, the RFID will be automatically identified, and the screen in the fitting room will automatically play a video of a model walking down the runway wearing that piece of clothing. When people see the model, they subconsciously beautify themselves.

At the same time, the data will be transmitted to PRADA headquarters. The data for each piece of clothing, including which city, which flagship store, what time it was taken into the fitting room, and how long it stayed there, is stored and analyzed. If a piece of clothing has low sales, the usual practice is to eliminate it directly. However, if the data from the RFID shows that although this piece of clothing has low sales, it is frequently taken into the fitting room, then it can explain some issues. Perhaps the fate of this piece of clothing will be completely different.

Is it reasonable yet unexpected that luxury brands use cutting-edge technology?

Two

There is a restaurant in Thailand that can accommodate 5000 people dining simultaneously. It's quite large. Its menu is like the Yellow Pages of Beijing, very thick, with just Sichuan cuisine under Chinese dishes having three categories. If you were in a small restaurant, and you ordered Mapo Tofu and Twice-Cooked Pork, and both dishes were served almost simultaneously, you wouldn't be surprised. But in this Thai restaurant, if you order Japanese cuisine and Mapo Tofu, they are also served almost simultaneously, and the taste is quite authentic. You can't help but be amazed.

How does it achieve this?

In the kitchen. There are many chefs from different countries. In front of each chef is a display screen, telling the chef which dish to cook now. The Japanese cuisine and Mapo Tofu you eat are made by two chefs who may not even know each other and are far apart.

To ensure both dishes are served simultaneously, the system will automatically calculate. If the Japanese cuisine needs 1 hour, and the Mapo Tofu needs 10 minutes, the system will notify the Japanese chef to start cooking immediately, and notify the Chinese chef to start making tofu after 50 minutes, so that both dishes are cooked almost simultaneously.

Three

There is a bank call center. In front of the operator is a computer screen. There is a line on the screen. The points appearing above this line indicate that the user can bring enough profit to the bank. Points appearing below this line indicate that the user cannot bring enough profit to the bank.

When a call comes in, the user says, another bank offers me a 50% discount to become its user. The operator will see where the point for this user is located on the screen. If it is below, the operator will say: Okay, then cancel your account, wish you a pleasant journey. If it is above, the operator will say: I have negotiated with the client manager, we will offer you a greater discount, please stay.

The call center is a profit center.

Four

UPS moves from the express delivery industry into the financial industry.

Traditionally, when a package is sent from the manufacturer's warehouse to being signed by the user, it requires the guarantee of a bank. Only after the user signs to confirm will the money be paid to the manufacturer. And usually this would take 2 weeks.

UPS can tell the manufacturer, I don't need 2 weeks, I only need 2 days to pay you the money. The manufacturer will be happy. UPS can do this because it is the first to know when the goods are shipped and when they are signed by the user. Usually, the first action UPS takes when picking up the goods is to scan the barcode. The last action UPS takes when completing the delivery is also to scan the barcode.

With financial services, UPS's advantage in the express delivery industry will be further deepened. Moreover, UPS gains more income and profits.

Five

Traditional business models must choose one of two options: either satisfy breadth or satisfy depth. For example, pharmaceutical manufacturers satisfy breadth, a cold medicine can cure all kinds of colds for everyone. Family doctors satisfy depth, a doctor can only provide home consultation services for a few people. Breadth and depth cannot be achieved simultaneously.

But technology will change this. Genetic drugs will allow large pharmaceutical manufacturers to satisfy both depth and breadth. Because they can provide each patient with individually customized genetic drugs containing their own genetic codes.

Media is the same. Weibo satisfies both breadth, providing information for everyone, and depth, everyone's information is different, personalized. Moreover, whether in terms of breadth or depth, it exceeds all previous media forms.

Six

The Spanish clothing brand ZARA has shortened the design and production cycle to 2 weeks. And each design is produced in very small quantities. This makes piracy unsustainable.

Previously, the design and mass production cycle was several months. When the model walks down the runway, piracy begins. By the time the clothing is mass-produced and launched on the market, there are already people on the street wearing pirated clothes. No one is interested in these clothes anymore. This is a blow to the fashion industry.

But ZARA has solved this problem with a powerful IT system. Now the boss of ZARA is the richest person in Spain.

Seven

The impact of social problems on business is greater than that of technological innovation.

Aging population and uneven distribution. By 2050, Russia's population will shrink by 33%, Italy by 40%. The Middle East's population will double. The number of elderly people will exceed that of young people for the first time.

By 2025, water will become the most limited resource. In every gallon of water on Earth, only one drop is drinkable. China accounts for 22% of the global population but only has 8% of the water resources. India accounts for 19% of the population but only has 4% of the water resources. Thousands of people die every hour from diseases related to water pollution.

Kids born after 2000 have an average life expectancy of 120 years.

Many countries in Europe will go bankrupt. Because they didn't anticipate that life expectancy would increase significantly. They previously planned insurance and welfare programs based on an average life expectancy of 80 years. But when these people live 20 years longer, everything changes.
