Preparing for the Transition Before Hibernation in Centipede Breeding

When young centipedes have been raised in small containers for 1.5 to 2 months, their first winter after birth is also approaching. At this time, the young centipedes have completed their first molt after becoming independent and are entering their second molt. All young centipedes should be cleaned once, grouped according to size and molting progress, and placed in different breeding pools or breeding troughs for raising. The breeding soil in the breeding pool must be loose, and the temperature inside the breeding shed should be kept relatively stable to facilitate the smooth entry of young centipedes into hibernation.
