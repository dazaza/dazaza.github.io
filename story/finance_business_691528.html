Families of deceased miners have no way to claim compensation; the town government claims inability to act

by se2yv4dfdty on 2012-03-05 16:33:23

The long road to compensation for the families, small illegal coal mines are difficult to govern. Staff of the Zaitang Town government introduced that the regular mines in the district have basically been shut down, but private mining and over-exploitation is still relatively serious. The government has dispatched a large number of law enforcement officers to inspect, but with little effect. Currently, the town government is actively cracking down on illegal mining to prevent a repeat of the tragedy.

Two men died in an illegal coal mine. Zhang Ting, daughter of Zhang Junyang, said her father had worked in Beijing's state-owned coal mines for many years. Later, due to the closure of these mines, Zhang Junyang became unemployed. When there was no work, he would do other things to make a living. When there was work in illegal coal mines, the boss would find experienced workers and ask them to mine, "earning three or four hundred yuan a day." Yang Cuiying, wife of Zhang Junyang, said that on the evening of May 15, the illegal coal mine boss had dinner with Zhang, Guo and others. Subsequently, the boss asked them to go down the mine to explore whether there was coal to be mined. Unexpectedly, after Zhang and Guo went down the well, they never came up. Feeling something was wrong, Yang Cuiying reported it to the police for help.

According to a staff member of the Zaitang Town government, at around 8 o'clock in the morning on May 16, the Zaitang Town police station received a report from Yang Cuiying, saying that Zhang Junyang was buried while mining on the evening of May 15 at the coal mountain air vent. After receiving the report, the Mentougou police immediately rushed to the scene for investigation, working together with other departments to actively carry out rescue operations. Around 1 p.m. on the same day, Zhang and Guo were rescued and sent to a nearby hospital, but unfortunately, they both died after unsuccessful rescue attempts. Investigations found that Zhang and Guo went to a destroyed coal air vent to look for coal. Due to lack of oxygen inside the vent, they both died of carbon monoxide poisoning. Regarding the compensation issue, this staff member said that the town government can do nothing about it, only by finding the coal boss can the problem be solved. If there is really no way, the town government can only provide partial compensation, "which is done out of humanitarian considerations."

The deceased Zhang Junyang and Guo Bin were classmates from Sichuan. Their death certificates read: carbon monoxide poisoning.

Guo Jun, younger brother of the deceased Guo Bin, said that the Zaitang Town police station officers once asked him to identify the coal boss, "I recognized him immediately," but the officer said that the testimony of the family members was not enough, and more witnesses' statements were needed, so no control measures were taken against the identified coal boss. Guo Jun said that he heard Guo Bin mention that Wang Yongsheng was from Junxiang Village in Zaitang Town, "I used to work for him, so I recognize him."

On May 27, the reporter went to the petition office of the Zaitang Town government with the families of Zhang and Guo. The response given by the receptionist was: "We can't handle it." The Zaitang police station said that the police were still investigating the matter and hoped for the cooperation of the families.

Subsequently, the rescuers saved the two and sent them to the hospital for emergency treatment, but unfortunately, both died. Afterwards, Yang Cuiying searched multiple times for the two coal bosses, Wang Changsheng (phonetic) and Tao Suo (phonetic), but "couldn't find them anymore."

Report by correspondents Xiao Yue and intern Li Binbin: As of yesterday, half a month has passed since the deaths of the black coal mine miners Zhang Junyang and Guo Bin. On the evening of May 15, Zhang Junyang and Guo Bin went into an illegal coal mine near Zhangjia Village in Zaitang Town, Mentougou District, to look for coal. Unfortunately, they suffered from carbon monoxide poisoning and died after unsuccessful rescue attempts. The family said it was the boss of the illegal coal mine who asked the two to go down the well. The family sought help from the Zaitang Town government, but because the coal mine boss had "disappeared", the police investigation had no results, and the town government said "we can't handle it."
