When the world revolves around Mobile...

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 18:09:24

If we consider the world in which my father's generation, commonly known as the Baby Boomers (post-war baby boom), grew up, it revolved around "television." In contrast, my generation, commonly referred to as Generation Y, has grown up in a world revolving around "PC" and "Web."

Various signs indicate that my son’s generation, known as Generation Z, will grow up in a world revolving around Mobile – the combined shipments of Smartphones and Tablets officially surpassed those of PCs in 2011 and are projected to widen this gap to five times by 2015.

What differences exist between a world revolving around Mobile and the previous one centered on PC + Web? Bill Gurley of Benchmark Capital and Bubba Murarka of DFJ recently wrote articles titled *Transitioning To A Mobile Centric World* and *9 Ways A Billion Dollar New Mobile Company Might Be Created*, respectively, outlining their observations and predictions.

Combining their points with my own observations, I believe there are several key considerations for entrepreneurs:

**Saturation?** – Although smartphone penetration has exceeded 50% in many countries, this might suggest a potential slowdown in growth. However, from another perspective, there’s still half the population left to reach, and tablet penetration still has a long way to go.

**Time Share** – More importantly, Mobile will increasingly dominate consumers’ “time,” especially tablets, which will draw attention away from PCs, TVs, and magazines.

**Design** – On mobile devices, due to smaller screen space and primarily “touch”-based interaction, which is an intimate form of engagement, the importance of design (not just beautification) will only increase.

**Android** – The discussion among developers has shifted from prioritizing Android or iOS to focusing on which versions and models of Android should take precedence. This highlights both the fragmentation and the growing significance of Android.

**Apps** – In the web world, the browser was the most important channel for accessing the internet. In the mobile world, the browser is just one app among many, and its importance is weakening. Previously, we accessed 200 million websites via browsers; in the future, we will discover and use millions, tens of millions, or even hundreds of millions of apps through Mobile Desktops, App Stores, and Play Markets.

**Cross-Border** – Internet companies originating from desktop web now need to figure out what apps they can create to capture users transitioning to mobile. Conversely, mobile-first internet companies cannot ignore the web. As a result, the complexity of development and operation for future internet companies will undoubtedly increase.

**Search** – In the mobile world, the importance of search has significantly diminished, and Google Search's dominant position faces serious challenges.

**SEO** – With no single dominant search engine, the effectiveness of driving traffic through Search Engine Optimization (SEO) will naturally decrease, reducing the importance of SEO over time.

**Promotion** – Promoting apps will continue to be challenging due to the lack of long-term effective Earned Media channels like SEO.

**User Value** – Leaving a website requires just one mouse click, whereas uninstalling an app involves multiple steps. Combined with the high cost of promotion, an app installed on a user’s Smartphone/Tablet will have far greater value than a website visitor.

**Payment & Commerce** – Convenient payment methods on mobile are expensive – both Play Store and App Store charge a 30% transaction fee. Meanwhile, cheaper payment methods are inconvenient. Therefore, mobile payments and mobile commerce still have a long road ahead (my estimate is 5-10 years).

The above are some observations about the emerging Mobile world, hopefully providing you with some food for thought.
