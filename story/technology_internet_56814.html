Guangdong mobile phone users can enjoy 3G services in early June, and Beijing's TD services are expected to be available within a month.

by 127702 on 2008-03-25 09:50:50

Word has come from the industry that China Mobile will next week start selling domestically produced 3G mobiles in eight cities including Beijing, Tianjin, Shanghai, Guangzhou and Shenzhen. Ordinary consumers will be able to go and buy them. This means that China's 3G services will formally enter the pre-commercialization stage. Guangdong Mobile made no comment on this news. A reporter learned from an insider that Guangdong Mobile would this week strictly check the TD-SCDMA network built by Datang Mobile and would next month issue a small number of codes to friendly users using the 157 area code. Two TD showrooms in Guangzhou will open next month. An insider revealed that the TD networks in Guangzhou and Shenzhen were currently at the further optimization stage and Guangdong Mobile planned to check the network on March 25 and decide on the scale of issuing codes next month according to network quality. Although there are reports in the industry that some business halls will next week start directly selling TD mobiles to the public, a person close to Guangdong Mobile said that as terminal quality was not currently stable, Guangdong Mobile had no plan to issue codes on a large scale to the public. However, two TD showrooms on Haoyue Road and Zhongbinjiang Road in Guangzhou would be open to the public next month. Professor Li Jinliang, a member of the Science and Technology Committee of the Ministry of Information Industry, has been using a TD mobile for two weeks. In an "experience report" exclusively sent to this newspaper, he said that between March 10 and 22, he used a ZTE U980 mobile via the TD network in Guangzhou or Shenzhen to call GSM mobiles or fixed telephones in Beijing and Tianjin and the voice was clear. "At 9 o'clock on the morning of March 20, I took the Harmony Express high speed train back to Guangdong from Shenzhen. I made nine calls while the train was moving and called Guangzhou, Beijing, Tianjin and Shenzhen. The highest speed was about 200 kilometers per hour. The voice was clear and I did not find any jitter," Li Jinliang said. He originally thought that the networks in Guangzhou and Shenzhen were island style separate networks and that there would be a blind spot from Shenzhen to Guangzhou. "It now appears that the line is basically connected throughout." But he also said that TD mobiles definitely had the situation of excessive energy consumption. Progress in issuing codes in various cities is inconsistent. China Mobile President Wang Jianzhou confirmed last week that TD would this year conduct trial commercialization and issue codes on a large scale in April. Reports say that starting next week, in the eight cities where China Mobile builds networks, large business halls will handle TD services for ordinary users. "In the early stages of TD, ordinary users might be subsidized 80 yuan per month to attract users to upgrade to 3G." A management person from Tianjin Company of China Mobile disclosed that 3,000 TD mobiles would be given to friendly users to experience in the Tianjin region. This person also said that in the early stages, China Mobile would give a TD mobile to high-end customers and give 800 yuan credit but fee details for ordinary users have not yet been worked out. Guangdong seems more cautious. An insider revealed that before TD terminals matured, Guangdong Mobile would not issue codes on a large scale to the public. Currently only Guangdong Mobile, Datang Mobile and very few experts have tested TD mobiles. Guangdong ordinary consumers will be able to enjoy 3G services around the end of May to early June.
