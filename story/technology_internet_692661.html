Introduction:

Tears, which are the crystallization of emotions and carry joy and sorrow, flow out from the eye socket as the most sincere feelings in one's heart. However, there is a special group of people whose tears flow not because of happiness or sadness —— they are patients with lacrimal duct disease, who suffer and struggle from eye diseases. Director Tang Hongming has been working in ophthalmology for 40 years and pioneered the Y-type intubation technique. With this innovation, many lacrimal duct disease patients no longer wander aimlessly or shed unnecessary tears. For this, CNTV's "Appointment for Health" conducted an exclusive interview.

Video address: CCTV.com "Appointment for Health" Exclusive Interview [Father of Retrograde Intubation] Tang Hongming [http://www.pr021.com/hospital/201203066405.html]

Director Tang Hongming [http://www.pr021.com/przj/2011/0426/84.html], graduated from the Jinan Military Region Medical College at the age of 15. He has been engaged in clinical ophthalmology work for exactly forty-eight years. A lifetime of military service and medical experience has brought blessings to many eye disease patients and spread light.

Starting point: The army hospital since 1968

Director Tang Hongming [http://www.pr021.com/przj/2011/0426/84.html] joined the military recruitment selection with friends when he was 15 years old. His companions wavered at the last moment, but he chose to persevere and thus embarked on his military career. At the age of 17, he became a military doctor, and from then on, the colors of national defense green and white coats became the two tones of his career.

In the army, he had to diagnose and treat patients all over the place. Eight years in Northeast China, fifteen years in Shandong, and then followed the army to Nanjing, traveling south and north without complaint. Treating every patient with full effort and relieving their pain is his work principle and also the source of his happiness. Every time he saw patients come with furrowed brows and leave happily, Dr. Tang felt immense comfort, as the saying goes, "Giving roses to others leaves fragrance in your hand."

Pioneering the Y-type retrograde intubation technique Renowned in the medical field

Director Tang Hongming [http://www.pr021.com/przj/2011/0426/84.html] began treating lacrimal duct disease patients in 1986. At that time, the treatment of lacrimal duct disease in the industry generally required bone-cutting surgery, where holes were drilled through bones for treatment. Patients suffered immensely, both physically and mentally, but had no choice but to accept it for the sake of curing the disease. Director Tang saw the suffering of the patients in his eyes and was deeply worried in his heart. At this moment, a bold idea sprouted in his mind - could the step of drilling holes be bypassed, and the problem solved directly by inserting a tube through the nostril?

To realize this bold concept, Director Tang, who usually paid close attention to the latest developments in lacrimal duct disease, started researching new treatment technologies intensively. Countless simulation experiments were conducted, and after years of exploration, he encountered countless detours, setbacks, and confusions, which made him consider giving up research multiple times. However, the strong will forged during his long military career supported him until the end. In 1992, it was a day worth celebrating for chronic dacryocystitis and lacrimal duct disease patients. After overcoming numerous obstacles, the retrograde intubation technique pioneered by Director Tang Hongming was finally successfully developed. This technology was a great blessing for lacrimal duct disease patients, as treatment did not require surgery; instead, it could be performed simply by inserting a tube through the nostril, completely resolving the patients' suffering. Moreover, using this technology to treat lacrimal duct disease had no side effects, with a success rate exceeding ninety percent. Director Tang even personally treated a Red Army veteran over 90 years old, alleviating the disease that had troubled the veteran for more than 50 years.

The retrograde intubation technique for chronic dacryocystitis and lacrimal duct disease eventually won the provincial scientific research achievement award and was extensively reported by prestigious media such as the People's Daily, Health Newspaper, Science and Technology Daily, and the PLA Daily. After years of hard work, Director Tang's aspirations finally came true, and he earned widespread praise from the industry with his breakthrough innovation.

Edging towards Puri Bright Future

In 2000, Director Tang retired from his position as a military doctor. After a lifetime of busy medical work, he could finally take a break and spend more time with his family who had always silently supported him. But he couldn't stay idle; more precisely, he couldn't leave the medical profession he loved so much. In April 2011, he chose Shanghai Puri Hospital, continuing his surgical career at this well-regarded ophthalmology hospital, utilizing his skills to serve society.

Not only is Director Tang highly skilled, but he also wins the high trust of patients with his excellent professional ethics. In his view, treatment is always a rigorous matter, and he has the responsibility and obligation to make pre- and post-operative treatments transparent, ensuring patients fully understand before undergoing surgery. For some economically disadvantaged patients in urgent need of surgery, Director Tang even pays out of his own pocket, hoping to contribute his small share to ease the strained relationship between doctors and patients.

"Rigorous, serious, responsible, and deeply reflective about common diseases, frequent diseases, and difficult cases," is the deepest impression Director Tang leaves on people. When asked about his life creed, he succinctly summarized: "One's character should be cheerful, work should be serious, and friendships should be genuine." Now, this physician over sixty still adheres to his life beliefs, diligently serving on the frontlines of medicine, radiating warmth and comforting countless eye disease patients.

Lacrimal Duct Disease:

Primarily refers to the obstruction of the lacrimal duct (including upper and lower lacrimal canaliculi obstruction, common lacrimal duct obstruction, nasolacrimal duct obstruction, etc.). Patients with lacrimal duct obstruction often have a face washed in tears for a long time, affecting vision and appearance. Chronic dacryocystitis patients may also experience pus discharge from the corners of the eyes. These patients have a large number of bacteria growing in their tear sacs, bringing endless troubles and pain, and as potential infection factors, may trigger diseases such as corneal ulcers.

Chronic Dacryocystitis and Retrograde Y-type Lacrimal Duct Intubation Technique

Scope of Application

Closure of the punctum, obstruction of the lacrimal canaliculus, traumatic lacrimal canaliculus disconnection, obstruction of the common lacrimal duct, obstruction of the nasolacrimal duct, chronic dacryocystitis, blockage after dacryocystorhinostomy for chronic dacryocystitis, etc.

Technical Advantages

A minimally invasive interventional treatment method for nasolacrimal duct obstruction, simple operation, safe, and time-saving;

● The treatment process can be completed within about 10 minutes, painless, basically bloodless, with a high success rate of surgery;

● Broad applicability, different types of medical silicone tubes can be used for treatment tailored to any part of lacrimal duct obstruction;

● Patients tolerate it well, does not alter the original anatomical structure of the lacrimal duct, no skin incision, no scars left behind, maintaining aesthetics;

● The designed intubation has the function of draining tears and drugs, allowing direct action of drugs locally, promoting epithelial regeneration and wound healing, facilitating tissue repair;

● Silicone tubes have a fixed shape, with moderate hardness, providing support in the lacrimal duct, enabling connective tissue to grow around the scaffold, allowing epithelial cells to crawl along the tube wall, avoiding adhesion and obstruction of damaged epithelial tissues;

● Side holes have the function of draining tears and drugs, allowing direct local inflammation relief;

Currently, nearly ten thousand lacrimal duct disease patients from all over the country have undergone retrograde intubation treatment, with an efficacy rate exceeding 90%. It passed the provincial appraisal in 1992 and was awarded a scientific and technological achievement prize.
