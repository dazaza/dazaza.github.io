JD.com changes its employment style after deep reflection

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 19:07:38

The core team decides the fate of a company. But JD Mall seems to be under a spell, with news of senior executives leaving last year, and even suffering reputational damage due to the inappropriate behavior of a senior vice president.After deep reflection, recently JD Mall CEO Richard Liu publicly stated that in the future, 70% of the company's senior executives will be promoted and cultivated internally, while 30% will be brought in from outside.Unable to retain core talentLast year, it was revealed that JD Mall Senior Vice President Wu Sheng had used a cooperating company to encroach on JD Mall's interests. Wu Sheng was the main planner behind the famous e-commerce event, the June 18th price war. The related transactions not only raised doubts about JD Mall's internal audit mechanism but also highlighted issues with JD Mall's hiring practices.Wu Sheng was originally a senior executive at Vancl and had not been at JD Mall for long. As the scale expanded, JD Mall continuously introduced external talents, but few were retained. At the end of last year, JD Mall confirmed that its CTO, Wang Yaqing, had formally resigned due to family reasons, having served less than a year. Before joining JD Mall, Wang Yaqing was the Global Vice President of Oracle (Oracle). In June last year, Jiang Haidong, the vice president responsible for information technology at JD, left to join Amazon China. Similarly, in June last year, Yi Rangyue, the strategy vice president of JD Mall, resigned. Prior to this, Yi Rangyue had worked at Huawei.Quota-based promotion is not advisableIn the past few years, JD's senior management has been entirely brought in from outside, including JD's CMO Lan Ye and COO Shen Haoyu, who were both parachuted in. The subsequent problems have led them to look towards the veterans within the company, with plans for 70% of future senior executives to be promoted and cultivated internally, and 30% brought in from outside. However, experts believe that quota-based promotion is not a good approach.Feng Lijuan, a special economic commentator for the Shanghai Business Daily and chief human resources expert at 51job.com, believes that career advancement requires timing and adapting to the needs of employment. Appointing the right person to the right position is crucial. Richard Liu’s proposal to promote 70% of future senior executives internally is not rational, and the practice of setting quotas for promotions is not advisable. It appears more like a publicity stunt.Feng Lijuan noted that e-commerce is a young industry, and individuals with over three years of experience can be considered seasoned. There is some validity to internal promotions, but Richard Liu's proposal to promote 70% of senior executives internally is not rational.Positions such as finance, administration, and human resources are universal, making it relatively easier to recruit externally. However, roles like sales, logistics, and marketing have unique characteristics specific to e-commerce, making it difficult to hire ready-made talent externally. Internal promotions have certain advantages: first, they adapt quickly, newly promoted executives have higher execution efficiency, and performance improvements can be seen in the short term. Second, internal promotions can stimulate employee enthusiasm. Feng Lijuan told reporters that if a company needs to launch new businesses or make significant changes, it tends to seek executives externally. Many executives from foreign-funded enterprises are now moving to private companies because the management style of foreign companies is more scientific. Particularly in the IT industry, if a company plans to go public, internet companies will consciously recruit a group of executives with overseas backgrounds to prepare for the IPO.E-commerce recruitment stabilizingJD is no longer recruiting people as aggressively as it did in the past. Richard Liu explained that as the company's business scale grows larger, maintaining a growth rate that doubles might lead to disastrous results. In previous years, two-thirds of JD's employees were new hires, and one-third were veterans, which severely diluted the company culture. This year, there are more veterans than newcomers at JD, giving the company a good opportunity to catch its breath and use this time to thoroughly reorganize its structure, laying a solid foundation for growth in the next decade. Regarding this, Feng Lijuan told reporters that e-commerce industry recruitment has entered a stable period. Overall, e-commerce recruitment in 2013 started off steady, with recruitment volumes matching those of 2012.Regarding how excessive recruitment can dilute JD's corporate culture, Feng Lijuan believes that although JD holds the second place in the e-commerce industry, overall, it is still a young company. JD is currently unprofitable, and its stability and sustainability still need to be evaluated. As the founder, Richard Liu's personal traits are quite distinct, and it is still too early to discuss corporate culture at this point.
