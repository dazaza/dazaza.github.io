How to Identify the Authenticity of Seller's Credit

Experienced friends must know that as long as you are a seller, different Wangwang (Taobao instant messaging tool) accounts will send you advertisements for boosting credit every day. This is also my experience. Every time I hear the sound of an incoming message, I am always excited thinking it might be a buyer, but it turns out to be these advertisements. There are at least one or more than a dozen such messages every day. Therefore, one of the daily tasks for every seller is to block these people. Haha, just a side note.

It is very obvious that with the existence of these people, it means there is unfairness, and it means there is a market for such practices. As the saying goes, "The bigger the tree, the more wind it attracts." Now everyone knows that Taobao is becoming increasingly influential. However, some dark sides come along with this influence. This is a necessary stage in the historical development of any platform, just like how counterfeit Procter & Gamble products, Amway products, and even counterfeit N95 phones (which are actually XXN95 instead of Nokia N95) flood the streets. In such a situation, we can only sharpen our eyes and minds so that we do not fall into traps. After all, some things cannot be completely eradicated (like robbery or theft), and the only way is to improve our own vigilance and discernment.

Let’s get back to the main point and talk about how we can identify whether a Taobao store's credit rating is genuine.

First Method: Thorough Observation

Why do I say this? Because for anything, we need thorough observation and investigation before making comments, right? Chairman Mao once said, "Without investigation, there is no right to speak." So, how do we observe? We can start from the following points:

1. Look at the number and quality of items in the shop.

2. Check how long the shop has been established.

3. Review the feedback from buyers.

Based on the above observations, we can draw the following conclusions:

1. If a shop has only been established for a short period of one or two months but already has a bright diamond credit rating, there is undoubtedly something fishy. Although this conclusion may seem hasty, I believe most new sellers would agree. When starting a new shop, regardless of how hard you work in the first two or three months, the results are often minimal. This is because most buyers, including myself, base our decisions on the seller's credit rating. Moreover, we usually prefer higher credit ratings. So, what is the likelihood that a newly opened shop with zero credits could sell dozens of items a day to reach a diamond level within one or two months? (Of course, there are exceptions; some exceptional individuals might achieve this.)

2. If the majority of the seller's credit comes from a small group of people who each buy three or four items at a time, then this is also suspicious. It is true that some buyers might genuinely purchase multiple items they like, or even collaborate with friends to make bulk purchases for better deals. However, it is highly unlikely that 80-90% of the buyers from a particular store would all buy several items at once. So, if you see multiple items reviewed by the same buyer consecutively, and this happens with many buyers, beware!

3. If a shop has few items, average quality, and no significant price advantage, yet still boasts high credit ratings, then it is also likely to have inflated its credit. For most experienced buyers, they usually compare prices among multiple shops to get the best deal. However, some new buyers, due to their lack of understanding of the market or operational skills, might simply buy whatever catches their eye. (I once encountered such a buyer on YiXun, where the buyer registered on the same day they purchased my item and made payment without contacting me first. I was hesitant at first, wondering if it was a scam or a competitor trying to mess with me. However, after calmly communicating with them over the phone, we completed the transaction successfully.) Some buyers might not even use Alipay but contact the seller through the web version of Wangwang, asking for local delivery. (This happened to me too, which resulted in a loss of some credits, but it doesn't matter as long as the buyer is satisfied.) Therefore, if a shop has few items, average quality, and no significant price advantage, yet still boasts high credit ratings, it is likely to have inflated its credit.

4. Check the content of buyer reviews. Some reviews are default positive evaluations from the system, which may mean the buyer is a novice who does not know they need to write reviews. Some buyers are lazy and directly give a positive review without saying much, which I think is more authentic. On the other hand, if a review contains a lot of words and obvious praise, it might be a fake evaluation. The logic is simple; nowadays, all buyers consider themselves as gods. Unless they are also running an online store and have a good mindset, they generally won’t write lengthy reviews unless they are deeply impressed. This is human psychology, don't you think? For example, one of my battery buyers was from the same city as me. He himself was a five-star seller. After purchasing my item, I braved the rain and rode my electric scooter to deliver the goods to him. This moved him, and he gave me a thank-you note. I'm not boasting here, but I hope everyone can investigate clearly.

After discussing the first method, let's share some thoughts. Actually, brushing credit for online stores is quite easy to understand. It reflects a kind of social phenomenon, similar to how some directors and stars verbally oppose unwritten rules but still follow them themselves. Or like how some officials claim to fight corruption but are the most corrupt themselves. Sometimes, being in such situations leaves one no choice. Everyone should adopt an attitude of mutual understanding. Sigh, this is society. Even Confucius had biases against agricultural production, let alone ordinary people.

Now, let's look at the second method—Calculation.

Assuming that completing one transaction takes an average of 5 minutes (including searching for desired items, reading descriptions, contacting the seller, bargaining, comparing prices among multiple sellers... etc., plus unexpected situations like the buyer going to eat or using the restroom). Thus, in one hour, 12 transactions can be completed, and in 24 hours, 288 transactions. It is certain that except for a few hardcore online shoppers, no one would be studying what to buy at 2 or 3 o'clock in the morning. Even sellers need sleep. Considering some quick customers who directly place orders and pay within less than two minutes, and considering the fact that the shop is not always open for business, the figure of 288 needs to be discounted. Let's assume that a normal, reasonable, and theoretically feasible daily transaction volume is 60% of 288, which is 173 transactions. In one month, this could increase the credit by 5190 times (of course, if there are such high volumes, it is inevitable to receive some neutral or negative reviews). According to this calculation, it would correspond to five diamonds on Taobao. However, is this possible? Can a new store reach five diamonds in one month? Obviously not. Some stores take even one or two years to reach one diamond, let alone five diamonds in one month. So, everyone can calculate it themselves. Of course, my calculation is just my personal understanding. Perhaps you have a more scientific and reasonable way to calculate, and by doing so, you can uncover some issues.

Third Method—Tracking

Carefully observe, if you notice that certain familiar buyers frequently appear in some stores every month and buy multiple items each time, it is also likely that the credit is inflated. Of course, there might be fixed wholesale partners, but you can judge from the items they buy. If they are real fixed partners, they are likely to regularly order specific items in large quantities and similar categories. This is what wholesale looks like.

Fourth Method—Inquiry

Undoubtedly, if a seller has truly sold a lot of items, they must be thoroughly familiar with their products, answering questions fluently. However, if a seller claims to have sold a lot but hesitates when asked, even needing to check product specifications, it is clear that they cannot immediately provide accurate information about the products they claim to have sold in large quantities. Such a seller cannot be considered a genuine hot-selling store or a reliable owner of hot-selling products.

Fifth Method—View Store Favorites

I once saw a two-diamond store with only one favorite. I wonder what everyone thinks about this point. Similarly, we can use some basic principles to calculate. Let's take one diamond as an example. If a store honestly reaches one diamond, then, at least, around a thousand people should have browsed its products. To reach one diamond, assuming a quarter of those visitors make purchases, which is already a very high conversion rate (meaning that for every four people entering the store, one makes a purchase), the conversion rate is unrealistically high. Not every visitor will buy something, nor will every visitor find the store's products appealing, right? If this were the case, every store owner would be rich. Furthermore, if a store reaches one diamond, it indicates that its products are generally well-received and worthy of being favorited. Assuming that out of a thousand people visiting the store, even at a 1% favorite rate, there should be ten people favoriting it. If even 1% of the visitors cannot be impressed, it shows that the store's products lack appeal and competitive pricing. If that's the case, how did it reach one diamond? Doesn't that make sense? With a little calculation, you'll find the truth. I believe that a truly attractive store should have many favorites. Conversely, an ordinary store would have none. A store with diamonds but only single-digit favorites is unusual and worth considering.

In fact, I feel that credit ratings sometimes are like a person's name, just a symbol. Just like credit cards, having a credit card does not necessarily mean the person is trustworthy. Instead, they might use it for cashing out and often fail to repay. Personally, I believe that as long as the products are good, buying from low-credit or even no-credit sellers is not necessarily a bad thing. Their service tends to be good. As the saying goes, "Big customers bully stores, big stores bully customers," which is also the reason.

Many newcomers do not know which stores sell genuine products and which ones do not. So, I will discuss how to identify the authenticity of sellers' credit evaluations.

It is evident now that a high online credit rating does not necessarily reflect actual credibility. Below, I will explain some identification techniques based on reviewing existing seller credit evaluations.

First, check the negative reviews. Regardless of how many positive reviews a seller has, thousands or tens of thousands, start by looking at the negative reviews. By clicking on the credit evaluation, you can see all the positive and negative reviews. If the content of the evaluations involves selling counterfeit goods, then potential buyers should click on the product link to check. For items that are obviously counterfeit, it can be confirmed that the store sells fakes. If there are many negative reviews, and each comment mentions selling fakes, then there is no need to browse this store anymore. However, if there is only one negative review involving counterfeit goods, potential buyers should not blindly believe it, as malicious evaluations are possible. First, open the product link yourself to confirm if it is counterfeit. If unsure, you can also check the neutral reviews.

Second, check the neutral reviews. Some buyers purchase counterfeit goods but give neutral reviews because they think the seller's attitude is good and the price is cheap. Some may even give positive reviews. Some neutral reviewers mention that the seller's attitude is good, shipping is fast, but the product seems not genuine. In such cases, potential buyers should open the product link themselves and make a judgment. The price is also a crucial factor.

By combining the analysis of negative and neutral reviews, potential buyers can determine whether the store sells counterfeit goods. If a store sells fakes, there is no need to browse it further, as it is impossible to determine which items are genuine and which are not. Many pictures are copied from genuine product images. If the store's credit is generated by a few old usernames, be cautious. Also, check if those giving positive reviews have only bought from this store, which is suspicious and likely to be credit manipulation. If the store genuinely sells items, all reviews should be diverse, not monotonous praise.

If all reviews are positive but contain few words or excessive positive reviews for the same product within the same time frame (e.g., 13:48 a positive review, 13:49 another positive review, 13:51 another positive review, suspected of forming a positive review alliance), there is a strong possibility of deception. However, we should not generalize. There are still 100% genuine and fair-priced stores. What I want to emphasize is that buyers should comprehensively evaluate each store's information.

1. You can first check in which year the seller received the credit. If a seller receives many positive reviews in a short time, you should think carefully and judge whether it is genuine.

2. Then check if the products they sell are very popular. If the products are popular but the seller's joining time does not match the number of evaluations, we should think carefully again. Is this genuine?

3. Is the price the best? If the price differs significantly from the actual market price and there are many buyers, you should carefully observe these buyers. Check if these buyers are experienced and have high credit.

4. Does the product quality match the seller's description? This is relatively easy to observe. Whether it is a neutral or negative review, unsatisfied buyers will inevitably mention some product issues. Please analyze the complaints in the reviews and search online for articles commenting on this product. From these four points, you can at least determine whether the seller's evaluations are genuine.
