Xian Guo Network releases iPad magazine reading software, Xian Guo Live Broadcast

by daodao on 2011-02-28 00:42:46

Freshfruit Live is a free iPad application recently released by Freshfruit, a long-established domestic online RSS subscription service. It presents various reading materials in a magazine-like format. Freshfruit Live displays millions of blogs, news websites, newspapers and magazines, e-books, and web novels from the Freshfruit platform on tablet computers in a magazine-reading style. Furthermore, by linking to a Sina Weibo account, users can track friends' activities in real-time, stay updated with the latest popular news flashes, and participate in discussions, forwards, and collections based on social relationships from Sina Weibo or Freshfruit.

Back in 2005 when I started writing blogs, there were several emerging concepts that were highly promoted at the time, one of which was electronic magazines: Zcom, Poco, Xplus, among others, received nearly $1 billion in venture capital investment as if they were playing tickets. However, these once-capital-favored darlings now face various operational difficulties, forcing them to either transform or shut down. Looking back now, electronic magazines might have just been a graceful and enchanting sprite created by capital. Nevertheless, a few years later, the emergence of the iPad, I believe, provided an excellent opportunity for the rebirth of electronic magazines. At this moment, I think the concept of electronic magazines can be redefined and repackaged! A comeback is not impossible. Before this, I conducted a simple analysis, and I believe that one of the significant reasons for the failure of electronic magazines was that entrepreneurs did not clearly understand what role they should play entering this field—content providers? Channel distributors? Or technical supporters? This also relates to the then restless start-up environment. Facts have proven that technology-driven companies like Zcom and Poco ultimately failed to gain market recognition due to the lack of quality content and flexibility, which fundamentally caused them to fade from user sight.

Liang Gongjun from Freshfruit said that for RSS to become a mass application still requires lengthy exploration. However, I think the essence of RSS—"reading"—does not need to be understood or defined, nor does it require prolonged exploration; the union of Freshfruit Reading and the iPad excellently conveys Freshfruit's commitment to the concept of reading. Freshfruit has been working on online reading products for many years, from content aggregation, to content presentation, and then to content distribution. Freshfruit, as such a platform, effectively connects all aspects of internet content from creation to consumption, possessing all the necessary elements to enter the electronic magazine industry. The Freshfruit team has been focusing on the reading field for years, and in my view, they were just waiting for this great leap forward and explosion of the iPad era! In the future, if Freshfruit can operate the Freshfruit Live (iPad version) product as an independent brand and increase operational and product efforts, I believe it will easily generate more value, including commercial and strategic ones.
