Survival and Dignity

We often hear a remark about a certain type of person: "For survival, there's no other way." In other words, for the sake of so-called survival, one is willing to lose their dignity. For example, those who seek official positions (they are certainly not financially struggling individuals) are often very aware of their own humility and lack of dignity before they achieve their goal. However, they console themselves with the saying "Endure great hardships, and you will become a superior person," misinterpreting it in this way. Thus, it becomes a vicious cycle; those who succeed in seeking positions cause others below them to also lose their dignity.

In an economically developed society, everyone shows their unique abilities like the Eight Immortals crossing the sea. It seems that having money and power makes one a grandpa figure, while lacking both makes one a nobody. To get things done, one must rely on connections, spend money, and follow unwritten rules. How can one maintain dignity while surviving? What dignity do you still want? Sometimes when handling affairs or reporting, one must also tell lies, flatter, and say nauseating things. There's no other way but to endure it for the sake of survival.

Maslow's theory divides needs into five categories: physiological, safety, social, respect, and self-actualization, progressing from lower to higher levels. When a person is hungry, they won't be interested in anything else; all their energy will be focused on obtaining food. From the hierarchy of needs, it's clear that to allow people to have dignity, their physiological needs must first be met. If a person can't even ensure basic survival, how can they care about dignity and integrity? All for the sake of survival, what has happened to our dignity? The more I think about it, the more confused I become.

The journey was not as lonely as I had imagined. I could calmly sit on a leaky bus, chatting aimlessly with the driver. Looking at the greenery unseen in the northwest brought a different kind of taste. As the bus jolted, it shook me out of my restless mood. I had initially planned to invite someone to accompany me to Zhan Tree to see the medicinal herb market here. But after much thought, there was no suitable person. Since I had already been here once, I decided to go alone. Actually, traveling alone was not as lonely as I had imagined. I could comfortably sit on a drafty bus, exchanging random words with the driver. Seeing the greenery unseen in the northwest gave me a unique feeling. As the bus jolted, it woke me from my long-restless thoughts.

The sky was beautiful, with a gentle breeze brushing against my cheeks. Lifting my head, I gazed at the azure sky. At that moment, my heart felt like the rain-soaked mountains—extraordinarily peaceful, tranquil, clear, and open. Upon reaching my destination, due to the busy schedule of the person I was meeting, I took a walk around. I called to inform them that I might be late. When traveling away from home, one shouldn’t neglect oneself. I rented a mini room to rest. The room suited me well—it was small yet cozy. Since it was newly opened, everything was brand new. I could sleep soundly. Turning on the TV, I spaced out for a long time. Unintentionally, I thought about the things that had made me sad and heartbroken these days, the things I couldn't let go of. All my thoughts became crystal clear at that moment. Perhaps because my mind suddenly became clear and open, the matters that caused me sleepless nights and heartache didn't stir even the slightest ripple in my heart now. Carefully reflecting on the days we spent together, the times we had, the tears, the laughter, the joys, the pains, thinking about life's insights, I instantly felt as if a heavy burden had been lifted. A belief spontaneously arose: Regardless of who it is, life should not be occupied by clinging, unease, and guilt. Therefore, I should treat every person who helps me grow kindly, even if they help me grow through harm. I pulled out my phone and sent a text message. At that moment, I felt the sky was so blue, the water so clear. Is this forgiveness? Or letting go? Or enlightenment? No matter what, suffering should be sublimated in bondage, and pain should transform in growth.

Finally, I received the call, and we agreed to meet for dinner with a group of people I wasn't very familiar with. After introducing each other, we happily finished eating. Men and women were talking about their ongoing businesses worth hundreds of thousands or even millions. Suddenly, I no longer felt the envy and imbalance I used to feel. I thought that in a few years, when I reach their age, I would surely be able to realize my dreams. Yes, my goals have become clearer, or I have become more confident. Anyway, I am now calm and relaxed.

Actually, everyone's heart has many steps, especially when you are filled with resentment towards others, and you are troubled and sorrowful. Those long steps always prevent you from standing at a higher point to overlook the beauty of life. However, when you gather the courage to overcome obstacles, you will understand that true happiness comes from forgiveness and letting go.
