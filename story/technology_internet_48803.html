We know that AFF companies in sales come in all sizes, and their daily businesses vary in size as well. When we do fake sales, we must consider various possible situations. If we find a sales company that can be faked, and immediately do a large amount, the result will only be death! So when doing it, we must think from the advertiser's perspective on how to prevent them from discovering our cheating. Some SALE companies receive tens of thousands of online orders a day, while others get very few. This depends on the scale of the company's online sales business. Sometimes during holidays, orders increase significantly, and processing speeds up. Sometimes at the end of the month when settlements begin, order processing accelerates. These are opportunities we can take advantage of. Some students ask why companies still pay us for fake orders. The answer is simple: they haven't discovered that our orders are fake. These companies process a lot of orders every day, so a few invalid or incorrect orders are normal. If the company's business includes both online and offline sales, the order flow might make it unclear where the orders originated, so the money will enter our accounts. Some companies place all orders on hold for a period before processing, and fake orders are handled at the end of the month. If the volume of orders is large, processing becomes troublesome, so these invalid orders are ignored, and the money is paid out. The principle for LOAN companies is the same. However, if we try too hard to guess the advertiser's thoughts, it might be counterproductive. As long as we follow certain rules and don't be greedy, I think there won't be any problems. 4. I've applied many times for ads on kolimbo.com, including ncen/ukpl/phoenix university etc., but none of them were approved. I've applied more than 10 times, and the site should have no problem?

If the site has no problem, then you should be able to apply successfully. If you couldn't, then it's either the site or the address. 6. I think account management is very complicated, it gets messy when there are too many. How do you manage them?

Stillwater also encountered this problem before. Generally, I create a table, categorize by type and company, and put the account and password into it. When there's a big update, I print it out and save it. If I start doing a lot of companies this month, I will arrange all the company codes in the format of

username, password, mailbox, code

and place the advertisements in a local page for operation. I will record how many each one did separately in another text file according to the page order. One point to note is that you should have a basic plan for how much you do each month, what you do, and how many accounts you apply for.
