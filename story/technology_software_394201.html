Best Answer:

The process of starting an online store begins not on the internet, but in your mind. You need to decide what kind of store you want to open. In this regard, opening an online store is no different from a traditional brick-and-mortar shop; finding a good market and ensuring that your products are competitive are the foundations of success.

Step Two: Choose a platform or website for your store. You need to select a site that provides a personal store platform and register as a user. This step is very important. Most websites will require registration with your real name and valid identification documents like an ID card. When choosing a website, factors such as popularity, whether it charges fees, and the nature of those fees are all crucial indicators. Many platforms now offer free store-opening services, which can save you a considerable amount of money.

Step Three: Apply to the website to open your store. You must carefully categorize the types of products your store will offer. For example, if you sell fashion watches, they should be classified under "Jewelry, Watches, Glasses" in the "Watches" category so that your target users can easily find you. Then, you need to come up with a catchy name for your store. Online users click on stores in lists more based on how appealing the name is. Some online stores display personal information, which should be filled out truthfully to increase trust.

Step Four: Procure inventory. You can source goods from channels and platforms you are familiar with, controlling costs and securing low prices being key.

Step Five: List products. You need to fill out each product's details on the website, including its name, origin, location, nature, appearance, quantity, trading method, and trading deadline. It's best to include pictures of the products. The product name should be as comprehensive as possible, highlighting its advantages, because when others search for similar items, only the name will appear in the list. To increase appeal, the quality of the pictures should be as high as possible, and the descriptions should be as detailed as possible. If mailing is required, it's best to specify who is responsible for the shipping costs. Another very important matter during listing is setting the price. Typically, websites provide options for sellers to set starting prices, reserve prices, and fixed prices. Let's say a seller wants to sell a piece of clothing purchased at 100 yuan, intending to sell it for 150 yuan. If this were a traditional shop owner, they would just initially mark it at 150 yuan and gradually lower the price if it doesn't sell. However, online bidding is different; the seller first sets a starting price, and buyers then bid upwards from there. A lower starting price tends to attract more buyer interest. Some sellers set their bids at 1 yuan to draw attention.

However, a too-low starting price carries the risk of ending up with a final selling price that is also too low. Therefore, sellers should ideally set a reserve price simultaneously, such as 105 yuan, to ensure the item isn't sold below cost. Another drawback of a very low starting price is that it may imply you're willing to sell the item at a very low price, causing the bidding to linger at a low level. If the seller feels waiting for the auction to end takes too long, they can set a fixed price. Once a buyer agrees to pay this price, the transaction is immediately completed. The downside is that even if several buyers are interested, it's impossible to drive the price higher through bidding. Sellers should use these settings according to their specific circumstances.

Step Six: Marketing and Promotion. To boost your store's popularity in the early stages, appropriate marketing and promotion efforts should be made. However, relying solely on online methods is insufficient; both online and offline promotional channels should be utilized together. For instance, you could purchase ad spaces on high-traffic pages labeled as "Hot Product Recommendations," bold the names of your products in the categorized listings, or add pictures to grab attention. You can also use free advertising methods, such as exchanging links with other stores and websites.

Step Seven: Mid-Sale Services. When customers decide whether to buy, they may need much more information than what you've provided. They might ask questions online anytime, and you should respond promptly and patiently. Note that many websites prohibit any private contact information (such as email addresses or phone numbers) between buyers and sellers to prevent bypassing the platform’s fee structure, otherwise penalties may apply.

Step Eight: Transaction. After a successful sale, the website will notify both parties of the contact details and arrange the transaction according to the agreed-upon method, either through face-to-face transactions or via remittance and mail. Transactions should be conducted as soon as possible to avoid doubts about your creditworthiness. Whether to provide additional after-sales services depends on prior agreements between both parties.

Step Nine: Evaluation or Complaints. Credit is a very important factor in online transactions. To help build a trustworthy environment, if you're satisfied with the transaction, it's best to give positive feedback and gain favorable reviews through excellent service. If the transaction fails, negative feedback should be given or complaints filed with the website to minimize losses and warn others. If complained against, handle it quickly to avoid tarnishing your own credit record.

Step Ten: After-Sales Service. There's no need to elaborate on this point; a complete and considerate after-sales service is a very important factor in keeping your business thriving over time. Maintaining contact with clients in various ways and doing customer management work well is essential.

Currently, there are hundreds of large shopping websites in China offering online store services, but only a few have significant influence. Here are introductions to some major related websites:

eBay.cn (www.eachnet.com): Founded in Shanghai on August 18, 1999 by Gary Yao Bo and Tan Haiyin, it is the largest Chinese online trading platform globally, providing C2C (individual to individual) and B2C (business to individual) network platforms and services. In March 2002, eBay invested 30 million USD into eBay.cn and formed a strategic partnership. In June 2003, eBay added another investment of 150 million USD. eBay.cn has attracted nearly 2.2 billion USD in overseas investments to date, making Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd. the enterprise attracting the most foreign capital among national returnee entrepreneurs.

eBay.cn (www.ebay.com.cn) is one of the earliest shopping websites in China offering online store services, charging for online store registration, requiring payment for product reserve price settings, item listing fees, transaction service fees, and advertising value-added service fees.

Taobao.com (www.taobao.com): It is the leading domestic personal transaction (C2C) online platform, founded on May 10, 2003, with an investment of 450 million RMB from Alibaba Group, the world's best B2B (Business to Business) e-commerce company, dedicated to becoming the world's largest personal transaction website.

Taobao currently offers free registration, free certification, and free store opening services.

Yipai.com (www.1pai.com.cn): Established by Sina and Yahoo in early 2004 as a joint venture - Beijing Xinyazhixian Information Technology Co., Ltd., Yipai.com is a fully functional new high-quality online buying and selling community, providing smooth trading platforms and multiple trading models for Chinese small and medium-sized enterprises and individual users.

Yipai.com currently offers free online store services.

8848.com (www.8848.com): Founded on May 18, 1999, 8848 was once the flagship of China's e-commerce enterprises. In September 2003, it began planning a new business development strategy, shifting its focus to building China's first professional shopping search engine - 8848 Shopping Engine, serving small and medium-sized merchants through 8848 Online Stores, and providing e-commerce solutions to expand business for corporate users. 8848.com was officially launched externally on January 1, 2004.

8848 currently offers free online store services.
