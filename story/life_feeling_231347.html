QQ Zone melancholy diary: Worn memories midnight flight. If only.

by lwf225 on 2009-08-31 18:17:01

I don't know why I have to witness the sky above me turn from day to night, and then from night to day, again and again. Just as I don't understand why insomnia keeps finding me over and over. I am such a kind and innocent child.

If a normal night's sleep has to come at the cost of being awake all night, then I'd rather not have it.

I really want to cry. Who can tell me what to do? Who will save me?

May Day is coming, and when time can be measured in hours, my silent joy is self-evident.

Thinking this way, I finally become immune to insomnia. Thus, I start to interpret life from another perspective. Reading books that haven't been touched for a long time, thinking generously about people I haven't seen for a while, or just sitting there doing nothing. My mind stays calm, without any ripples. Walking back and forth crazily in the large and slightly empty room. At night, I don't wear high heels, so there's no sound like Wang菲's footsteps in '2046'.

At some point, I also start to not know what exactly I'm doing, everything seems powerless and pale.

Both batteries of my mobile phone are out of power. There's a vague irritation in my heart.

Gradually, I realize that my words have nothing to do with the outside world. They're just my personal belongings, perhaps already isolated from the outside world.

Work starts to be frequent like a rush.

Some familiar yet strange faces make me irritable. All those people are skilled in their own lives, while I stand out awkwardly like a fool among them.

All is quiet. I think this silence is like a disease.

Later. Later. When I'm exhausted from thinking, I smoothly enter into dreams.

Blurry images. A man looks at me with eyes full of pity, then touches my head and gives me a lollipop. He mutters "Baby, be good."

Then, I wake up suddenly.

Someone says, don't be sad, it will pass, I'm here. Someone says, fill your mouth with旺仔little cakes, and smile happily.

Someone says I've lost weight, and must have been having a hard time recently.

When I hear this, I feel like crying.

He sits on the rotating chair in the dim corner of the private room, spinning around constantly. The room is filled with noise. They sing loudly and tell vulgar dirty jokes. But I see him staying silent amidst all the commotion.

I hold a delicate music box in my hand. I keep winding the spring, so the little Cupid in the center keeps spinning in the darkness. It looks happy like a child.

Looking up at the mottled sky, the gray clouds gradually dissipate. The moon pushes through the clouds and is surprisingly bright. The night breeze is slightly cool. Insects chirp incessantly. There are stars faintly visible, but they're not bright. Like life, there's no passion, yet I can't say exactly what's wrong.

I start to try to reduce my activity range as much as possible, wanting to erase the traces of my existence in many places.

It seems like everything is at an end. The indoor air conditioning is strong, I lie on my back on the floor. My skirt instantly slides down from my knees. Looking at myself, my skin is rough, and my eye circles are heavy. I look exactly like a clown. I use my hands to comb my hair, often pulling out big clumps.

When typing, I habitually listen to music through my earphones. Some songs bring joy upon first listen, but become boring after repeated listens. Just like those coarse loves that can't withstand scrutiny or chewing. Thus, I realize that nothing is eternal, if there is, then it's loss. Ba Jie said this sentence was too desperate. But after seeing, hearing, and experiencing so much, we can't help but view this thin life in this way. Standing in the gaps of one segment of life after another, we only have blank faces, unable to find our direction. Helplessly looking around, tears streaming down our faces.

The city at dusk suddenly experiences a violent windstorm. The rain beats fiercely and chaotically against the windows, everything happens so suddenly. A grand and unprecedented rain arrives. The only thing I'm sure of is that this city has gone crazy, completely crazy. The madness fills me with fear and panic. Power outages in some areas, the clamor of the crowd makes me uneasy. Yet, I'm grateful for this grand accident, giving me time to ponder things I once thought were forgotten. What I didn't know is that they had always been hidden in some corner, never leaving. In the warmest and dampest part of my heart, a strange and beautiful plant begins to grow, blooming flowers. I immerse myself in the loneliness within me, but time deliberately pushes me towards desolation.

Turning off the computer and all switches, I stand on the balcony gazing at the distant horizon. Once again, I clearly smell the earthy odor rising from the soil, which suddenly makes me miss the can of Lotte tea-flavored gum. Missing some far-off and blurry faces I don't know where, missing the recently departed crowds, worrying about the upcoming several farewells. Streaks of bright lightning cut across the gray sky, drawing glaring lines, illuminating the entire horizon, like accidental fireworks blooming at the edge of the sky. Instinctively, I step back, but can't help but look up. Then helplessly watch as the window's light turns dark in an instant, making it impossible to see my fingers. I'm waiting for the next sunny day.

Lying on the soft bed, my feet start to get cold, so I tighten the thin blanket.

In the darkness, I punctually turn on the radio, hearing the favorite voice. But I discover it's her last time hosting this program, feeling disappointed. Perhaps a person never thinks how their actions or inactions affect others. I just know that years later, at the same time and frequency, hearing the familiar voice still there brings immense joy.

Getting used to the sincere speech of people around me, I assumed everyone was speaking sincerely. Maybe I should thank those who hurt us, because being continuously hurt makes us tougher, makes us think, makes us write. Then I find out that there aren't that many sincere people, and no one deserves a lifetime of waiting. Finally realizing that easily spoken vows actually require all one's strength to uphold, and discovering that this is such a tiring task.

In elementary school, I had my first favorite singer. Nicholas Tse. I repeatedly listened to 'Thank You For Your Love 1999'. Back then, I was still a child carefully copying books at the desk with a water-blue fountain pen, making a rustling sound with my fingertips. The girl who did homework with me was very thin. Time flies. Many years have passed, but we haven't seen each other again.

And now, the boys I like have bright smiles and white teeth, looking radiant when they laugh. Seeing him, I think of a sunflower. However, he doesn't like writing. Too many people say that those who touch words tend to be unhappy. The girls I like have cute faces, warm laughter like the sun, and are very kind. They're all suited for summer.

My mother likes staring at me while talking non-stop, likes reading the words I carefully jot down, and likes watching sentimental or funny idol dramas with me. So, in front of her, I always talk smilingly without end, write texts frequently. Remembering to download updates for serialized idol dramas on time. Remembering to wear her favorite clothes when going out with her. As long as she's happy, I'll do anything.

The gentle wind blows my hair into my eyes, and I reach to rub them. Leaning on a pillow reading a book for more than an hour, my heart feels oppressed. My eyes feel sore and painful, as if dazed. Sometimes I turn off the lights and light a sea blue candle. Inside, there are colorful shells and small conches, emitting a faint fragrance when burning. By nearly 10 p.m., the sky is still bright. Somewhere in the distance, intermittent bright flashes of lightning illuminate a large area of the sky. My mother says she's scared, and soon falls into a deep sleep. But I remain awake. My body doesn't move. Often, just before dawn, I gradually lose consciousness and fall into a drowsy sleep. Having occasional nightmares, no splendid and luxurious dreams. Anne found in 'Wei An' that even as adults, we're still a group of women who dream constantly. Lonely people mock loneliness, and the pure mock purity. The 'Paper Airplane' in the mp3 softly sings all night. Happy sounds. Fragmented memories. Often waking up in the middle of the night. Getting up. Not turning on the light, fumbling in the dark. Drinking large gulps of water, walking barefoot in the dim room, stretching out my hand, and finding that I can already see my five fingers.

Getting up in the middle of the night to wash my face. The sound of water seems extremely abrupt.

Abrupt. Abrupt. Abrupt. The whole world seems abrupt. Enough. That's enough.

Looking at these fragmented words in front of me. Right now, this is all I can do.

I stay silent. I'm at a loss for words. I want to sleep. I gain and lose in great silence.
