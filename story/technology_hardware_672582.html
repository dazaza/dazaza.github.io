Eternal Witness

by yrtu6849 on 2012-02-23 04:05:02

She is 14, he is 17. They met online and chatted happily; she recognized him as her brother, and he accepted her as his sister.

She asked: Am I your first sister?

He said: I lost count.

She is 15, he is 18. They exchanged phone numbers on New Year's Eve; she couldn't live without him, and neither could he.

She asked: How are things with your girlfriend?

He said: We love each other very much.

She said: The person who marries me must offer a bakery as a dowry.

He said: It will come.

She is 16, he is 19. During the summer vacation after her high school entrance exam: She broke up with her boyfriend, and he also changed girlfriends.

She asked: When can we be together?

He said: When we're both single.

On July 29th, he asked: How many brothers do you have?

She said: Among ten, nine are my cousins.

He asked: Who is the remaining one?

She said: You are my last brother.

He said: Be my girlfriend!

She said: Alright, when I turn 18, let's meet.

She is 17, he is 20. She called him: Love is slowly fermenting...

She asked: Do you love me?

He said: I love you.

He asked: Do you love me?

She said: I do.

She asked: Will you make me cry?

He said: Just once, and then only happiness afterward.

She is 18, he is 21. They finally met: She wasn't beautiful but was cute; he wasn't handsome but was sunny.

She asked: How long can you love me?

He said: Until I can't love you anymore.

She asked: When will that be?

He said: Death.

She asked: Will I have a bakery?

He said: Yes, you will.

She is 19, he is 22. Their love grew steadily: She entrusted herself to him, and he carefully took her.

She asked: Will you marry me?

He said: Yes, I'll only marry you!

She asked: Will I have a bakery?

He said: Yes, you will.

She is 20, he is 23. They fought against their parents: She told her parents that she was his woman; he told his parents that she was his woman.

She asked: Will we succeed?

He said: Yes, because nothing can stop our love.

She asked: Who will die first?

He said: You, you need to warm my bed in the afterlife.

She felt a bit sad.

He said: I don't want you to cry for me, so, you go first.

She is 21, he is 24. Their love finally blossomed: She received his bakery, and he proposed to her.

The night before the wedding, she asked: Why did you marry me?

He said: To warm my bed.

On their wedding day, he got drunk and entered the wrong bridal suite, feeling extremely guilty... But she just smiled and said she wouldn't hold it against him.

She asked: Will we get divorced?

He said: No, just like the bakery won't close.

She asked: When should we have a baby?

He said: In two years.

She nestled in his arms to sleep, and he held her tightly.

She is 23, he is 26. Their love finally bore fruit: A pair of twins. However, her figure had changed, enjoying spring.

She asked: Am I very unattractive now?

He said: No, you're still beautiful.

She no longer slept in his arms: Afraid he would find her fat; He embraced her tightly after she fell asleep: Afraid she would feel hurt if he said he didn't love her.

After waking up, she cried...

She is 30, he is 33. Their family is very complete: Two lively children, they still love each other as before.

She asked: How long can this life last?

He said: Until death.

She asked: How long can the bakery last?

He said: Forever.

She is 37, he is 40. Their lives are good: Good feelings, filial children. But wrinkles crept onto her forehead.

She asked: Am I old and ugly now?

He said: Not as old as me, and more beautiful than me.

She asked: Is the bakery almost gone?

He said: My salary can support our family.

She doesn't know, he and the children maintained the bakery with their salaries because her illness was too severe...

She is 57, he is 60. Their love has become sweet water, light yet fragrant. The children have grown up, and they are both old.

She asked: The bakery finally survived.

He said: Yes, the children have grown up.

She didn't know that he and the children used their salaries to maintain the bakery because her illness was too severe...

She is 58, he is 61.

He asked: Have you ever cried just because of me?

She said: In front of you, only once; behind your back, many times.

She said: I'm leaving, I'm going down to warm your bed...

He said: I know, wait for me, don't drink the soup of forgetfulness.

She said: Don't rush down, it's cold down there, it takes many years to warm the bed well.

He said: We can warm the bed together.

She said: I know, don't walk into the wrong bridal suite or climb into the wrong bed again...

She closed her eyes, he cried: She cared about that incident on their wedding day, making him even more remorseful...

He is 81. Lying in bed, holding her photo: He missed her...

He said: I'm coming to find you, it's been 20 years, the bed should be warm by now...

He said: I haven't remarried, I only love you...

He said: I won't walk into the wrong bridal suite again...

He said: I'm going down, wait for me...

He quietly closed his eyes, with a slight smile on his lips: Because he saw her.

Hearing her say: The bed is warm, come down...

No matter how the children cried out, he still closed his eyes.

He has no regrets: In this life, with her, he has no regrets...
