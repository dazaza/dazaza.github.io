Strategies and Tactics in the Bottom-Fishing Phase

Many stock investors started bottom-fishing when the index was at 5000 points, all the way down to 3000 points, repeatedly buying and getting stuck, resulting in a significant reduction in market value. It's important to know that "we don't predict tops during rallies, nor do we predict bottoms during declines," as a bottom isn't formed in just one day. Major bottoms are often constituted by pot-shaped bottoms, double bottoms, triple bottoms, and multiple bottoms. If you buy prematurely or replenish your position too early, you will bear the risk of being stuck. However, this does not mean we should be passive. Investors can use certain strategies and tactics to make profits.

Strategically: The stock market of any country is influenced by internal and external factors such as natural disasters and macroeconomic conditions. If stock prices fall rapidly, it is often because the company's fundamentals have changed qualitatively along with the macroeconomy, or the stock price has severely overestimated its valuation. When the market is on a downward trend, buying too early or replenishing positions will result in being more stuck. However, in the domestic market, the index has fallen by more than 54% in the last 8 months. At this point, investors should not blindly liquidate their positions. In the long run, the stock market will not keep falling endlessly because there has been no reversal in the development of China's macroeconomy. The previous difficulties affecting the stock market are temporary. When stock prices fall below their intrinsic value, and there are widespread cases of "breaking issue price" and "breaking net asset value," the real bottom will appear, which is suitable for strategic position building.

Tactically: Once the market and stock prices enter a relatively bottomed-out area, it is important to focus on stock selection tactics.

First, observe the overall trend. Under the dual pressures of "internal troubles and external threats," the continuous decline of the stock market has negatively impacted investors' psychology. If the stock price falls more drastically than the bad news itself suggests, and when policy incentives are introduced but the market remains indifferent, it's time to consider buying stocks.

Second, emphasize value. "Focus on trends during rallies, and quality during declines." When the trend shows signs of a turning point, choose individual stocks whose intrinsic value is clearly higher than their current stock price.

Third, watch trading volume. Before stock prices hit bottom, trading volumes will continue to shrink. When the market stabilizes and changes occur, select active stocks with moderate increases in trading volume.

Fourth, pay attention to patterns. Choose stocks whose prices are far below their historical trading density zones and recent trapped buyers' trading density zones, and are above the downward trend line.

Fifth, analyze indicators. When selecting stocks, don't just look at daily line indicators; also consider stocks where daily, weekly, and monthly line indicators show successful bottoming out simultaneously.
