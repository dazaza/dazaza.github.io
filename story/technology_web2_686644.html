by yanlabav on 2012-03-02 10:33:34

Liu Yi

In February, the methanol futures price experienced a trend of rising, then falling and finally oscillating. At the beginning of February, under good market expectations, the methanol futures price once rose to around 3030 points. However, due to the lack of effective follow-up in the spot market, and the expansion of the futures-spot price difference which created nearly a 100 yuan/ton arbitrage space for Shandong factory warehouses, under immense pressure, the methanol futures price quickly rebounded and dropped to around 2900 points where it gained effective support and subsequently maintained a consolidation trend. In March, we remain optimistic about methanol futures.

Frequent positive news creates a favorable market atmosphere

Entering February, the domestic and international macroeconomic situation was relatively optimistic. The US economy maintained an upward trend, with first-time unemployment benefit claims dropping to the lowest level in four years, indicating that the US job market is continuously recovering. Moreover, the Dow Jones Index and Nasdaq (Weibo) Index hit their highest levels in two years. The influence of the Eurozone debt crisis has weakened, with Greece's second round of bailout plans being approved, temporarily avoiding bankruptcy. The People's Bank of China also reduced the RMB deposit reserve ratio for deposit-taking financial institutions by 0.5 percentage points on February 24th, releasing 40 billion yuan in funds. The latest HSBC PMI index rose to 49.7, signaling a slowing decline in the Chinese economy. These numerous macroeconomic positives have created a favorable market atmosphere for the methanol market.

Enterprises are actively producing, and social inventories are normal

As of February 10th, the overall operating rate of domestic methanol production enterprises stood at 52.88%, slightly declining from the start of the month mainly due to inspections of some facilities in Northwest and Shandong regions. Excluding inspection factors, domestic production enterprises still maintain high production enthusiasm, reflecting that downstream markets still have a certain receiving capacity, and production enterprises have good expectations for the future market. Currently, methanol inventories are at around 550,000 tons, which is relatively high. However, we noticed that methanol inventories are mainly concentrated in midstream traders, and the inventory pressures for production enterprises and downstream factories are not significant. That is to say, once downstream demand improves, the pressure on traders will quickly ease. Therefore, structurally speaking, the social inventory of methanol appears normal.

Downstream demand will gradually improve in March

Regarding formaldehyde, the domestic formaldehyde market remained stable in February. After the fifteenth day of the first lunar month, formaldehyde enterprises in Shandong and Hebei resumed production, but sales were not optimistic, with low demand from downstream plywood enterprises. We believe this aligns well with the market conditions. In February, domestic temperatures remained low, affected by extreme cold weather, most areas in the north saw temperatures drop by nearly 4 degrees Celsius compared to last year, while most southern areas saw temperatures drop by 2 degrees Celsius. Low temperatures slowed down the home decoration market, curbing the demand for plywood, preventing terminal consumption from starting, thus inevitably affecting the methanol market. This situation is expected to ease in March as temperatures gradually rise, and formaldehyde demand could gradually start.

Regarding acetic acid, after returning from the holiday, downstream acetic ester and acetic anhydride facilities gradually started operations, but terminal demand was light. Direct downstream products of glacial acetic acid also showed signs of sluggishness, with poor sales. By the end of February, there was slight improvement in acetic acid demand, and the market was in the process of destocking. In March, the domestic acetic acid inventory pressure is expected to ease.

Regarding dimethyl ether, the market conditions for dimethyl ether improved near the end of the month. Rising liquefied gas import prices, along with domestic refineries entering maintenance periods, caused continuous increases in domestic liquefied gas prices, driving up dimethyl ether consumption. Dimethyl ether manufacturers saw better sales and reduced inventory pressures. Starting in March, the liquefied gas market enters its traditional peak season, effectively transmitting to the dimethyl ether market, which is a positive sign for the methanol market.

Conclusion

Due to the favorable macroeconomic atmosphere both domestically and internationally, combined with the upcoming start of methanol downstream demand, the methanol market in March is worth looking forward to. Of course, price increases cannot happen all at once; short-term futures price gradual progress or even slight fluctuations are also quite normal. We suggest investors closely monitor downstream demand signals and consider buying on dips when appropriate.
