When I was with my fellow countrymen, I learned to think in their way

by yangli on 2010-09-10 14:48:01

When I was with my fellow townspeople, I learned to think and look at things in their way, truly integrating myself into them. The distance between me and them became smaller. My longing to go home gradually no longer tormented me so urgently. It was at this time that the chairman of the team's Poor-Peasant Association came to me with the returned urban repatriation application report. He said, you are a child of our village, you came here and have been doing well, but now that you are sick, it is important for you to go back and get treated. We will send you back properly. All the poor and lower-middle peasants in the village guarantee that you can go home. I personally will go to the commune to speak on your behalf. The words spoken by the chairman of the team's Poor-Peasant Association were as if they were chiseled onto a stone mill, every word sincere and reliable. For the first time, my gloomy heart became open. The women and girls loved to chat about how to make all kinds of food, just like the spiritual feasts of city people. They introduced each other on how to make millet flour steamed buns with bran taste less hard, how to roll mixed bean noodles; how to remove the bitterness from locust tree flowers, etc. They also eagerly invited me to try their skills in their caves. During breaks from work, Tiger's mother mostly dug wild vegetables. She has three round-headed tigerish sons. Her family never had enough grain. I followed her to learn to recognize various wild vegetables. She would rub the wild vegetables she dug out in her hands and eat them directly. designer wedding shoes I also learned from her to dig and eat while working. Sweet roots taste very much like sweet potatoes. Spicy roots taste like mustard greens. Tiger's mother also invited me to her house to eat the wild vegetables she made mixed with tofu residue. She was proud that during the time when food was scarce, she could invite me to eat such good things. In the eyes of city people, the Shaanbei folks have never enjoyed life; they are merely struggling for survival. But for the Shaanbei folks, the process of struggling for survival is enjoyment. I said to myself that not only should I look like a Shaanbei local on the outside, but my inner self should be like them too.
