Female boss was sentenced to 4 years in prison for extortion, once quoted two billion yuan at random

by yang906axue on 2012-03-09 17:11:43

The final ruling in the case of the Wong and Group extortion of 200 million yuan has been made: Liang Muye sentenced to four years imprisonment.

Report by our staff reporter Liu Yi Ming - Liang Muye, a female CEO of a new material company, took advantage of the opportunity when the Wong and Group was preparing for an IPO. She fabricated the role of "Brother De" and claimed to the president of Wong and Group, Mr. Lu, that "Brother De" had evidence of tax issues within the Wong and Group. She demanded 2 billion yuan from her or else "Brother De" would disclose the materials affecting the IPO of Wong and Group.

Recently, Foshan Intermediate People's Court sentenced Liang Muye to four years in prison. In her appeal, Liang Muye even claimed that the "two billion" she mentioned to Mr. Lu was not in reference to Renminbi (RMB), but rather "sperm".

Female CEO conspired with others to extort Wong and Group

According to the judgment document, Liang Muye, aged 47, is the general manager of Zhongshan City Run New Material Co., Ltd. The court found that Liang Muye heard during casual conversations about some upper management personnel of Guangdong Wong and Group Co., Ltd. being dissatisfied with the group company, thus giving her the idea to commit extortion.

Liang Muye compiled fictitious information about tax issues within the Wong and Group into written materials. She then approached Luo Moumin, who was related by marriage to the wife of the president of Wong and Group, Mr. Lu. They discussed using this information to extort Wong and Group and agreed on a split of the proceeds.

On December 29, 2010, Liang Muye met with Mr. Lu through Luo Moumin and lied to him that her friend in Beijing, "Brother De", already had information detrimental to Wong and Group's IPO and showed these written materials. She demanded that Wong and Group "show sincerity."

Mr. Lu felt that although there were no such "tax issues," he did not want to make the matter bigger and affect the IPO. He indicated that he could offer between 300,000 to 500,000 RMB. He asked Liang Muye to call "Brother De" so he could communicate directly, but Liang Muye refused citing it was too late at night.

Haggling over two billion recorded

On December 30 and 31 of the same year, Liang Muye and Luo Moumin met again with Mr. Lu. Mr. Lu brought other senior executives of the company to meet them in a hotel in Shunde. During the meeting, Mr. Lu insisted that Wong and Group had no financial problems and hoped the other party would not interfere with Wong and Group's IPO. After pressing for "Brother De's" demands, Liang Muye stated that "Brother De" wanted 2 billion RMB.

Upon hearing this outrageous demand, Mr. Lu immediately expressed his inability to accept it, and the negotiation ended without agreement. Later, Liang Muye arranged for Wang Mou in Beijing to create the illusion that the materials were sent by "Brother De" in Beijing. On January 15, 2011, she once again met with Mr. Lu and other senior executives of Wong and Group. Mr. Lu and others indicated they were willing to settle with 3 million RMB, but the negotiation failed.

Mr. Lu and other senior executives believed the whistleblower letter provided by Liang Muye was false and that "Brother De" did not exist. Mr. Lu had recorded their conversation. After leaving the hotel, Mr. Lu reported the incident to the police. On January 17, 2011, the police arrested Liang Muye.

On November 3, 2011, the People's Court of Shunde District sentenced Liang Muye to four years in prison for attempted extortion. Dissatisfied, Liang Muye appealed. In her appeal, she raised several reasons, among which the most controversial were two.

Controversy One:

Was the original intention for cooperation?

Liang Muye claimed that her actions were not aimed at illegal possession, but rather to make Wong and Group grateful to her so as to have business cooperation with them, and therefore did not constitute a crime.

After reviewing the case, Foshan Intermediate People's Court believed that the victim's report statement, witness testimonies, and confessions of accomplices all corroborated each other, proving that during the period when Wong and Group was preparing for its IPO, Liang Muye met four times with the president and senior executives of the victim unit, lying that there were people in Beijing who would report Wong and Group, threatening the victim unit to achieve the purpose of extortion. Liang Muye's actions subjectively had the intent of illegal possession, objectively implemented the method of threats, forcibly extorting public and private property from others, therefore constituting the crime of extortion.

Controversy Two:

What does "two billion" refer to?

Liang Muye also stated that the "two billion" she mentioned was said alongside "sperm," as a joke, and the determination of the amount "two billion" was incorrect. Her defense lawyer pointed out that since there was no actual criminal amount in this case, Liang Muye's criminal circumstances were minor, and according to law, she should be exempted from criminal punishment or given a suspended sentence.

What does "two billion" refer to, RMB or "sperm"? This was mentioned in Liang Muye's confession as well as in the testimonies of Luo Moumin and Mr. Lu. In Liang Muye's confession, she described the situation when she proposed "two billion": "Later, Mr. Lu kept asking how much money the friend in Beijing wanted, so I jokingly said the other side wanted 2 billion sperm, after hearing this, Mr. Lu said there was nothing to discuss."

Foshan Intermediate People's Court believed that the involved amount was supported by Liang Muye's confession, the confession of accomplice Luo Moumin, and numerous witness testimonies, sufficient to determine Liang Muye's criminal amount.

Accordingly, Foshan Intermediate People's Court made the final ruling, dismissing Liang Muye's appeal and upholding the original judgment. It was also learned that Luo Moumin was sentenced to three years in prison in the first trial, with a four-year reprieve.
