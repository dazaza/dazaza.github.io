Septuagenarian recalls escaping from fire by climbing scaffolding, using water to prevent scalding

by iface001 on 2011-04-07 15:45:30

"We wanted to check if the stairs were clear, but we found they were already blocked. It was at this moment that we saw a young man on this floor who also originally intended to take the stairs, but now had to give up," Chen Xiaqing said.

Escape

Rescue

Before our second attempt to climb the scaffolding, this young man smashed the glass of a fire hydrant and then turned on the fire hose, pouring water over our heads so that it wouldn't feel too hot when we climbed the scaffolding," Han Mingxia pointed to the man in the upper right corner of the photo and said.

The door was blocked by fire, leaving no choice but to climb the scaffolding

"When we reached the 10th floor, we heard the sound of firefighters clearing the corridor. They later also saw us," Chen Xiaqing said, "They extended the fire axe to us, letting us climb back along the axe. My wife and I went forward, I didn't pay attention to those two people in the picture, but they should have been rescued too."

The Shanghai TV camera recorded the moment they were rescued. The firefighter carried Han Mingxia on his back, followed by Chen Xiaqing behind him. The only sentence Chen Xiaqing said was "There are still people behind."

The old couple followed the firefighters' guidance and continued down the stairs. When they reached the second floor, Han Mingxia fainted due to exhaustion.

Chen Xiaqing, upon looking out from the balcony, immediately judged that the fire would spread upwards, so people should go down to be safe. But the outside was already filled with smoke, so Chen Xiaqing pulled his wife and started climbing onto the scaffolding outside the south balcony.

Finally, a reporter learned from the mother of an injured person at Huadong Hospital that she seemed to have seen the elderly person on the scaffolding currently receiving treatment at Changhai Hospital, and the situation was relatively stable.

Incident

Black smoke seeped into the room through the door crack

In order to provide an answer, the reporter began searching for the people in the photo. In the first few days after the disaster, everything was still unstable. The reporter used the most primitive method, but also the only way: visited all the hotels where the displaced people were staying, went to all hospitals accepting the injured, visited the post-disaster command center, and asked many people near the community, maybe they had seen these people before.

When Chen Xiaqing woke up and saw this black smoke, he immediately realized that there might be a fire. When he opened the door, a large amount of black smoke accompanied by flames rushed towards him.

"I told her, 'Girl, you keep climbing, don't stop.' At that time, the scaffolding wasn't too hot, probably because we were soaked with water, but once the water gradually dried, we might get scalded, so I told her to hurry," Han Mingxia said.

The reporter quickly went there and successfully found her. She told the reporter that her name is Han Mingxia, and narrated the story of how she and her husband Chen Xiaqing (the one in gray suit in the picture, blocked by a middle-aged woman) escaped from the fire.

Extinguisher paved the way for five people "meeting" downstairs

Following the descent, as Han Mingxia and others climbed down more floors, they got closer to the fire hoses. When they reached below the 15th floor, they could already be sprayed by water from the fire hoses below.

Chen Xiaqing said, "We then covered our mouths and noses with towels to avoid inhaling too much black smoke. Suddenly, we saw an old neighbor living on the 23rd floor coming down along the scaffolding, telling us that the fire on his floor was also very intense."

At this moment, a couple happened to prepare to descend with them. After discussion, they decided to use extinguishers to clear the way, and then the five of them continued to climb down. According to Han Mingxia's recollection, the young couple was relatively agile while climbing the scaffolding, whereas her 80-year-old husband and herself felt physically weak due to the exhaustion of energy.

Additionally, another young man not shown in the photo climbed faster during their subsequent ascent on the scaffolding and left alone. The middle-aged woman in the picture was severely injured, with serious burns on her feet due to not wearing shoes, and her stamina was starting to drain.

In the eyes of fellow patients, 80-year-old Chen Xiaqing is a "hero." During the escape process, due to his calm mind, accurate judgment, and meticulousness, he successfully implemented self-rescue and rescue within a relatively short period of time. /Reported by Li Hongpeng and Special Correspondent Shen Weihuang

Meeting

Because the building's exterior walls were under construction to prevent dust from entering the house, the couple living on the 20th floor, Mr. and Mrs. Han Mingxia, kept their windows tightly closed.

For 76-year-old Han Mingxia, the noon of November 15th was like any other day. Returning home from the senior university, eating lunch, and then watching TV in the living room. Her 80-year-old husband, Chen Xiaqing, was taking a nap inside.

Around 2:15 PM, Han Mingxia suddenly noticed black smoke seeping into the room through the door cracks. Although she wasn't sure what it was, she immediately woke up her husband.

"My old man told me that there was a big fire, and we couldn't go out," Han Mingxia told the reporter. But at that time, they did not realize how intense the fire would be.

The couple then decided to escape via the scaffolding.

Before reaching the 10th floor, the firefighters tried to rescue them using a ladder, but abandoned this method due to the thick smoke.

Han Mingxia and Chen Xiaqing climbed down two floors on the scaffolding, noticing that the fire on the 18th floor wasn't too intense, and since that floor was under renovation with open windows, they climbed inside through the window.
