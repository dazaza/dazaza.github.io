Courier exposes chaos: using counterfeit phones to switch iPhones 4S

by anonymous on 2013-08-02 10:11:50

Why does our sent mobile phone turn into a "stone"? Can express delivery personnel really earn over 10,000 yuan per month? How deep are the waters in the express delivery industry? In 2006, Luo Pingyun (a pseudonym), then 31 years old, came from Honghu, Hubei to Guangzhou to join his fellow townsman in the express delivery business. Before he knew it, he had been in the express delivery industry for seven years. Last month, he resigned from Guangzhou YTO Express and called the Guangzhou Daily hotline to expose various black curtains in the express delivery industry.

Is there "theft" in express delivery? Unpacking and taking items is not uncommon

Reporter: Some readers complained about losing items when sending express deliveries or being replaced with inferior goods. What's going on?

Luo Pingyun: The biggest possibility is that it was taken or swapped by the courier or other relevant personnel. Every day, the company distributes the express items to us. Some couriers see something valuable inside broken packages and will take some if they like it. As for the express items we personally collect, since we know what's inside, some couriers may also take some, such as tea, fruit, mooncakes, etc.

We're not afraid to take mobile phones; it's just that we don't get the chance. If we do, we'll take them. You send an Apple 4S mobile phone, I can easily buy a knock-off phone for 500 yuan on the street and put it inside, then reseal the box. Once, a customer sent several large boxes of mobile phone batteries, and some couriers took a few batteries from each box and then delivered them for signature confirmation.

Reporter: Is this phenomenon common? Do couriers unpack and take items?

Luo Pingyun: Everyone around me who works in express delivery has done this, but they only take things they like or find useful. We rarely take personal express items, mainly focusing on bulk cargo.

Boldly? Companies tacitly allow couriers to take things

Reporter: What happens if customers refuse to sign due to damaged express items or lost items and complain?

Luo Pingyun: If you want to file a complaint, it involves many steps, but every step is passing the buck. If the item isn't particularly valuable, many customers won't spend too much time dealing with the issue, plus it's hard to gather evidence.

Additionally, if the recipient truly refuses to sign, we couriers will hand the express item back to the company, which will then return it to the sender, and that's it. Since the current compensation standard is too low, for any uninsured items that are lost or damaged, compensation is only required at 3~5 times the shipping cost.

Reporter: Does the company have no related management system for courier behavior?

Luo Pingyun: Express companies are aware of these situations regarding couriers taking things but generally turn a blind eye. Unless the item is particularly valuable, the company won't single out any specific courier. This has become an unwritten rule, an industry norm.

Earning over 10,000 yuan monthly? Most people only make two or three thousand yuan

Reporter: There are rumors online that couriers earn over 10,000 yuan monthly. What's the real situation?

Luo Pingyun: Earning over 10,000 yuan monthly is rare. Most couriers only make two or three thousand yuan monthly. The job is tough, starting early and working late, very difficult. Actually, the express delivery industry is a highly profitable one, but much of the money flows into others' pockets. Couriers are just workers who rely on increasing their business volume to earn commissions.

Reporter: How is the courier's income divided?

Luo Pingyun: For example, sending a local document costs 8 yuan. The courier buys a form from the company for 3 yuan, pays 1 yuan for shipping fees, gets a 1 yuan commission, and the boss earns 3 yuan. The 3 yuan form includes 2 yuan for delivery fees, but the actual cost of the form is only a few cents. However, the courier usually only gets 1 yuan or 8 jiao from the 2 yuan delivery fee. For example, if I collect 1,000 yuan worth of goods today, I can get a 100 yuan commission.

Therefore, for couriers, business volume is crucial. But big clients mean big business volumes, and how much one earns monthly depends on big clients.

Black transactions? Kickbacks and bribes to win big clients

Reporter: How do couriers give kickbacks to big clients?

Luo Pingyun: There are many ways, such as raising prices, falsely increasing weight, filling out fake express forms, etc., ultimately conspiring together to embezzle the company's money. For example, in a software park information port area I once managed, two companies were consistently receiving kickbacks. For instance, a computer weighing 3 kilograms was written as 5 kilograms by the courier, thus generating more money. Also, some fake express forms were filled out, pretending to send items that weren’t actually sent, just to increase shipping fees. The extra money was then returned as a kickback to the person responsible for sending the items.

In addition to these fictitious kickbacks, express companies generally also give an additional 10% of the shipping fee as a kickback to the person responsible for sending items. For example, from the second half of 2009 to now, three couriers have been continuously handling express deliveries for a computer distribution company. On the surface, this company spends 100,000 to 200,000 yuan monthly on express fees, but in reality, the volume of goods is only 30,000 to 50,000 yuan. Thus, the courier and the person responsible for sending conspire, ultimately harming the company.

YTO: Courier theft has greatly decreased in recent years

Yesterday, the reporter called the Shanghai headquarters of YTO Express. Mr. Zhou, responsible for media interviews, responded to the reader’s tip-off, stating that the situation of couriers taking customers' items has greatly decreased in recent years. Regarding the collusion between couriers (express companies) and big client shippers to take kickbacks, Mr. Zhou said that according to feedback from the company's safety inspection department, there hasn't been any recent occurrence.

Do couriers really take customers' items? Mr. Zhou stated that such incidents sometimes occur, and customers do complain. However, enforcement has been strict recently, and such occurrences have greatly decreased in the past two years.

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter called the Guangzhou YTO customer service hotline. Ms. Gao, responsible for media interviews, said that if customers complain about lost items, they need to provide the tracking number, and then the company will help find the missing items according to internal procedures. "Express delays are unavoidable, whether there is theft or kickbacks, the company finds it hard to answer," Ms. Gao said.
