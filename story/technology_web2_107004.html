The Sunflower Manual for SEO Beginners: 55 SEO Techniques

by longersing on 2008-11-28 16:02:50

1. If you must use JavaScript drop-down menus, image maps, or image links, make sure to place their text equivalents somewhere on the page so search engine spiders can crawl them.

2. Content is king, so be sure to focus your best, better, and unique content around your primary keywords.

3. If content is king, then links are queen. Use your keywords as anchor text in links, and build a network of high-quality backlinks. Remember, if there’s no logical reason for a bad site to link to you, avoid it.

4. Don’t obsess over PageRank; it’s only a small part of the ranking algorithm. A site with low PR can actually rank higher than one with a higher PR.

5. Ensure that each page on your site has a unique keyword in its title tag. Also, if you must include your company name, put it at the end of the title unless your brand is well-known enough to generate searches on its own.

6. Fresh content helps improve rankings. Regularly add new, useful content to your website. Adding fresh content will attract the attention of search engines.

7. Be sure to use keyword-rich anchor text when linking to your site. In other words, if your target is “blue widgets,” use “blue widgets” as the anchor text instead of “click here.”

8. Focus on search phrases, not just single keywords, and place your location in the text, such as "Beijing mobile phone wholesale" rather than "mobile phone wholesale." This will help you gain local search traffic.

9. Don’t design your site without considering SEO. Make sure your web designer understands your expectations for natural SEO. Don't let them build a Flash-based site where updates become difficult. Spiders can crawl text, not Flash or images.

10. Appropriately place keywords in text links, image alt attributes, and even domain names.

11. Check the canonical issue between www and non-www domains. Decide which format you prefer and use a 301 redirect from one to the other. For example, if http://www.seo-tribe.com is your preferred setup, then http://seo-tribe.com should redirect to it.

12. Check how all pages on your site link to the homepage. Is index.html under your main domain? If so, differentiate your links. External links should point to http://www.seo-tribe.com, while internal links point to http://www.seo-tribe.com/index.html.

13. Frames, Flash, and Ajax all share a common problem — you can’t link to a single webpage. It's either all or nothing. The best SEO results come from avoiding frames and Flash, and using AJAX sparingly.

14. URL extensions don’t matter much. For SEO purposes, using .html, .htm, .asp, .php, etc., makes no difference.

15. Want search engine spiders to visit your new site? Submitting through Google’s form may take weeks. The fastest way is to get a quality site to link to you.

16. If your site’s content isn’t frequently updated, consider adding a blog because search spiders love fresh text. Update your blog at least three times a week with good, fresh content to feed those little crawlers.

17. When building links, focus on quality, not quantity. One good, authoritative link is far better than ten poor ones that could harm your site.

18. Search engines want natural language content. Don’t try to stuff your text with keywords; it won’t work. Search engines analyze keyword frequency, and excessively high frequencies can work against you.

19. Not only should your links use keyword-rich anchor text, but the surrounding text should also relate to your keywords. In other words, surround these links with descriptive text.

20. If you use shared hosting, do a blacklist check to ensure you're not sharing space with penalized or spammy sites. Their negative reputation could affect your rankings.

21. When registering a domain and using privacy protection to hide the owner information, Google might view you as a potential spammer.

22. When optimizing your blog posts, remember to extract the post titles to optimize the title tags.

23. The fundamentals of SEO revolve around text, links, popularity, and reputation.

24. Make your site easy to use. This affects your link-building efforts and popularity, thereby impacting your rankings.

25. Give links freely, and you’ll receive them happily. Don’t be stingy with outgoing links; this encourages others to link back to you.

26. Search engines like unique content and quality content. There’s a difference. Ensure your content offers both.

27. If you must make your homepage flashy with full Flash or a large image, include text and navigation links alongside.

28. Some of the most valuable links may not appear on websites but in email newsletters, ezines, and similar forms of communication.

29. Paid links usually yield only a few clicks unless embedded within content and not obviously marked as sponsored.

30. Links from .edu domains are often highly valued by search engines. Look for non-profit .edu sites that may be seeking sponsors.

31. Give people something worth talking about. Link bait is simply good content.

32. Each page should focus on one keyword group. It’s best not to try optimizing a page for multiple keywords.

33. If you have an insufficient or nonexistent call-to-action, SEO won’t help. Ensure your call-to-action is clear now.

34. SEO is not a one-time effort. The landscape of search engines changes daily, so expect to persist with your SEO work every day.

35. Engage influential bloggers and authority sites who might link to you, your images, videos, podcasts, or request reprints of your content.

36. Get a blog from the site owner or CEO. It’s invaluable! A CEO’s influence on a blog equals official company statements. Responding to readers' comments boosts your credibility quickly.

37. Optimize the text in your RSS feeds just like your articles or webpages, using descriptive, keyword-rich text in titles and descriptions.

38. Use captions in images. For instance, a news photo with a keyword-rich caption.

39. Pay attention to the text surrounding images. Image rankings depend on the text around them on the same page. Watch for keyword usage and captions.

40. Don’t rely on crawlers to automatically discover site pages. Good global navigation and links serve you better than just an XML sitemap.

41. There are two ways to disable personalized Google search results: log out of Google or add the parameter &pws=0 to the end of the search bar URL.

42. Links from (especially deep links) high PR sites are worth gold. High PR signifies high trust, so these links boost site quality.

43. Use absolute links. This reduces issues with your site's link navigation (like linking from https pages) and, if someone references your content, you'll gain external backlink juice.

44. When moving to a new domain, check if your host provider offers “sticky” forwarding. This temporarily forwards old URLs to the new domain while showing the new URL in the address bar, helping users transition gradually.

45. Understand social marketing. It’s part of SEO. Learning more about sites like Digg, Yelp, Del.icio.us, Facebook benefits future search competition.

46. To give your videos the best chance of being discovered by crawlers, create a list and video sitemap in your Google Webmaster Tools account.

47. In Google's blended search results, videos aren’t limited to YouTube. Confirm submission to other high-quality video sites like Metacafe, AOL, MSN, and Yahoo.

48. Use keyword-rich text around video content on the page. Search engines analyze surrounding content to define video usage for queries.

49. Use the word “image” or “picture” in photo alt descriptions and captions. Many searches combine keywords with “image” or “picture.”

50. Enable “Enhanced Image Search” in your Google Webmaster Tools account. Images are a big part of new blended search results, so allowing Google to find your images aids your SEO work.

51. Add viral widgets to your site or blog — ratings, sharing, commenting, viewer notes, etc.

52. Expand your services to include video, podcasts, news, social content, etc. Now SEO isn’t just about blue links.

53. When considering purchasing or exchanging links, check the cached date of the linking page in Google. Search “cache:URL” replacing URL with the actual site address. A newer cache date is preferable. If the page doesn’t exist or the cache date is over a month old, it has little value.

54. If some pages on your site are very similar (your duplicate content concern) and you want to ensure the correct one is indexed, include the preferred page URL in your sitemaps.

55. Check your server headers. Search “check server headers” to find free online tools. Ensure your URLs report a “200 OK” status or, if redirected, a “301 Permanent Redirect.” Any other status indicates issues, so check and confirm your URLs are set correctly and consistently used across the site.
