How to accurately calculate the heat allocation of kiln feed for rotary kilns

by hx515317 on 2012-02-20 09:17:33

As is well known, coal is a mixture of organic and inorganic minerals, and it is the most uneven mineral in nature. The raw coal entering various enterprises, even if it's from the same batch, has a relatively large variation in calorific value. At present, vertical kiln cement plants are spread all over China, most of which have developed by "adapting measures to local conditions and using local materials." Therefore, the fuels used in cement production are relatively complex; some factories use bituminous coal or a mix of smokeless coal and bituminous coal due to the lack of smokeless coal locally, and they also add coal gangue as a component, with the calorific value of the coal gangue calculated using an empirical formula for the calorific value of bituminous coal, which results in greater errors. It becomes even more difficult to accurately calculate the heat ratio when these three types of fuels are mixed into the raw material.

In the measured cement plants, the calorific energy of most black raw materials is around 3000 joules, and the clinker thermal energy consumption is calculated as 4796 joules. Although the above results only represent a few cement plants, they cover a relatively wide range, including both northern and southern regions, indicating that there is still much room for reducing the thermal energy consumption in the vertical kiln cement production process. Coal consumption in vertical kiln cement production in China is generally high, with significant differences among enterprises, which can be attributed not only to enterprise management, production processes, equipment, and calcination operation methods but also directly related to the backwardness of coal quality analysis and testing techniques.

With the advancement of science and technology and the advent of small computers, manual operations and tedious calculations have been replaced, accelerating the speed of analysis. This progress forms the basis for further development, whether it be regular or fully automatic calorimeters, all controlled by computers. Software development is tailored to the needs of cement production, incorporating various calculations for calorific values, standard coal consumption, and commonly used coal quality indicators in cement production. The performance of these instruments has reached the level of foreign counterparts, with measurement accuracy unaffected by the source and type of samples. The temperature resolution is: 0.0001℃; precision ≤0.15%; measurement time: 15 minutes.

The rotary kiln and vertical kiln have different calcination processes. In the rotary kiln process, coal is crushed, dried, and ground into powder before entering the kiln, which is actually also a process of homogenization. As long as the coal used in production and the coal powder entering the kiln are strictly controlled, the stability of the calorific value can be guaranteed. However, many vertical kiln cement enterprises have relatively backward process equipment, making it difficult to achieve homogenization treatment of coal. The fluctuating calorific value in the black raw material balls entering the kiln directly affects the thermal stability inside the kiln. Therefore, it is necessary to use a calorific value measuring instrument to strengthen the timely and accurate control of the calorific value of the raw material leaving the mill and entering the kiln, ensuring the stability of the heat ratio of the raw material entering the kiln.
