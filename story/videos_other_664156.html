Maldives: A Blue Love Letter, Embracing the Paradise of Love

Maldives, in many people's minds, is a symbol of romance and love. Perhaps it's not about someone you go on vacation with, but rather this island in the ocean.

Returning from the Maldives to Beijing felt like a sigh of relief. At least Mr. Zhang's couple was quite satisfied with their honeymoon, and Akka has started to become addicted to taking vacations on small islands to watch fish. I am very happy as long as these three people are satisfied.

This journey can basically be described as continuously rising from a low point to a climax. The first night on the island, the hotel room they gave me almost made me break down. There was a simple bed, a buzzing air conditioner, and a bathroom that was beyond repair. When I went to the front desk at midnight to request a room change, no one was there. If it weren't for Akka's comfort, I don't know how I would have faced the first night on Embudu Island.

In the morning, I found the sales manager at the front desk and told him about our room situation. After some persuasion, he didn't put up much resistance and changed the room for us, and it was right next to Mr. Zhang's room. My mood improved significantly after entering the new room. Maybe because of the stark contrast between the two rooms, I suddenly thought the small island was beautiful.

The first morning greeted us with a group of crabs crawling all over the beach on the island. Their movements were extremely agile, and every time Akka tried to pounce on them, they would quickly hide in the rock crevices. It took about 15 minutes to walk around the entire island. We took advantage of the time when the sun wasn't too strong to tour the island first. The side where we stayed was facing the wind, and the strong and humid sea breeze swayed us back and forth. Sometimes, the wind was so strong that it splashed seawater onto our bodies. But when we turned to the side of the dock, the situation completely changed. There was no sign of any wind, and the clear seawater in front of us was like a large piece of jade placed before us. For convenience, we named different locations on the island: Coral Beach because there were large coral reefs close to shore; Fool Bird Reef because there was always a big bird standing on that reef, staring blankly at the sea; Crab Dam because there were countless crabs hiding inside the long concrete dam... Every day thereafter, we referred to these places by our own names.

Our first dive was at Coral Beach. Here, the water was clear and the wind calm, making it very suitable for Mr. Zhang's couple and Akka who had never snorkeled before. After getting used to it for a while, Akka and I swam further out. The coral of the island was not as colorful as the Club Rannadle Island I visited last time, nor even as good as the coral in Tioman Island Marine Park. However, the clarity of the seawater was absolutely unparalleled. Not long after we started swimming, we saw a large sea turtle swim past us. How could Akka and I miss such a good opportunity? We swam up to the sea turtle and said hello to it. The morning snorkeling was just a warm-up. After lunch and a nap, we began our second dive under the pier in front of our door. The afternoon sunlight shone directly into the water, and the color of the sea here was extraordinarily blue. There were more fish under the pier than at Coral Beach, and their colors were also very bright.

On the first evening, we arranged for a night fishing trip. Under the sunset, our group of six set sail. After about ten minutes, we arrived at the fishing spot outside the island. The boat captain provided each of us with a set of fishing gear. In fact, the fishing gear was just a wooden board tied with fishing line, with fresh fish meat attached as bait. After everyone dropped the bait into the sea, we began to wait patiently. A few minutes later, we heard Mary shout that there really were fish. The boat captain quickly came over to help, and after a bit of commotion, a red big fish appeared, which immediately excited everyone. Then, Mr. Zhang’s baited hook had only been in the water for a few seconds when a poor passing fish got hooked in its gill and was pulled aboard. Within just ten minutes of starting fishing, we had already caught two fish, and everyone's enthusiasm soared. Each person was pulling on the fishing line, fearing that they might miss a bite. However, within the first hour, we only caught these two fish. Everyone started discussing that their positions were not good, so they swapped places hoping for better luck. The boat captain looked around and decided there seemed to be no big school of fish nearby, so he moved us to another place. Sure enough, the fortune changed: fish started coming up nonstop. Finally, my bad luck continued despite me shouting, "Heaven, give me a fish!" Eventually, I felt a sudden tug at my fingertips. Pulling it up, sure enough, it was a fish. But upon closer inspection, I realized it had bitten Mr. Zhang's hook, and mine had coincidentally snagged onto it... Thus, I was mocked endlessly by the other five during our return. However, seeing them all so happy, I was naturally happy too. Moreover, our catch would appear on the dinner table the following evening.

Maldives, a place brimming with symbols of romance and love, truly lives up to its name as a paradise on earth.
