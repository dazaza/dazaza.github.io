Blizzard Designer Reveals StarCraft II Features - Northern Net - Gaming Sentiment

by diglog on 2007-05-25 04:05:59

On May 19th, world-class game development company Blizzard released the sequel to "StarCraft," titled "StarCraft 2."

At the WWI event, the lead artist responsible for the development of "StarCraft 2" showcased over 30 minutes of gameplay, leaving players in awe. With the unveiling of "StarCraft 2," various rumors that had been circulating have come to an end. What remains are questions regarding the release date of "StarCraft 2" and related issues such as new races. For this, we interviewed Blizzard developers.

Mike Morhaime, one of Blizzard's founders and current head of Blizzard!!!

Dustin Browder, Chief Game Designer of StarCraft 2!!! He was previously involved in leading the development of "Red Alert 2" at WESTWOOD.

Q: When will the game be released?

A: After developing "Warcraft 3," work began on "StarCraft 2." Development has progressed significantly, and currently, the game can basically be played with all three races. The release date will be after the game is fully developed.

Q: Which new race has been added?

A: In "StarCraft 2," only the Zerg, Protoss, and Terran races appear. Three races are the most suitable for a strategy game. Although consideration was given to adding a new race, it was decided that instead of adding a new race, it would be better to add new units to the three existing races, balance the game well, and enhance its diversity.

Q: What were the biggest challenges and budget during the development of "StarCraft 2"? And what is the size of the development team?

A: Deciding to develop "StarCraft 2" was quite a difficult process due to high expectations worldwide. However, we are prepared to tackle various challenges. There are 40 core developers working on "StarCraft 2." In addition to these core developers, there is also another support team. The budget hasn't been specifically set because it is an important development project. We will invest sufficient funds to develop it until it reaches a satisfactory level.

Q: What conditions are needed for the further development of esports in the US and Europe?

A: First, it must be a good game; second, it must have a venue for gaming, and we feel Battle.net has been quite successful; third, it must be easy to pick up. Blizzard’s focus in RTS development is multiplayer games, followed by single-player games.

Q: How will "StarCraft 2" be distributed? Will it use the same distribution method via Battle.net as now?

A: The multiplayer mode of "StarCraft 2" will also be realized through Battle.net. Many new features will be added to Battle.net in the future. We currently have various ideas for interesting uses of Battle.net, which will be gradually revealed.

Q: Some people say the game speed is slower than the previous version.

A: Since today's demo part was for showcasing the game, the speed was intentionally slowed down. It wouldn’t be possible to explain the game in detail if it were fast. Don’t worry based on the demo video; the actual game speed will be similar to "StarCraft." Moreover, the new units and skills of the Terran and Zerg races have not yet been disclosed. Professional players are helping us maintain game balance.

Q: What are the system requirements?

A: The system requirements have not been finalized yet. The graphics card supports Pixel Shaders 2.0. "StarCraft 2" has high compatibility and can be played on Windows Vista and Web 2.0.

Q: Will the main characters from the previous game appear?

A: Of course. The core characters from "StarCraft" will reappear. However, specific plots and content will be gradually revealed in the future.

Q: What was the motivation for developing "StarCraft 2"?

A: After developing "Warcraft 3," we planned to create a sequel to "StarCraft." At the time, with the technology available, we couldn't develop the game we envisioned. Currently, with advanced 3D technology and improved computer configurations, the content we planned can be fully realized, so we started developing "StarCraft 2."

Q: What is the status of the development of "StarCraft: Ghost"?

A: Currently, the development of "StarCraft: Ghost" is temporarily postponed. Blizzard doesn't develop many games at once. Due to internal decisions prioritizing PC games over console games, we are focusing our efforts on developing "World of Warcraft" and "StarCraft 2."

Q: Watching the game video, it seems that "StarCraft 2" compared to the previous version lacks revolutionary innovation.

A: The main content of "StarCraft 2" is competition, which is the same as the previous version. New elements might become obstacles for intense strategic battles between players. Therefore, no revolutionary new content that could overturn the previous version has been added. We just want to bring new fun to players by further developing the game. As an RTS, the map editor has significantly evolved compared to the previous version. The map system is the core of the "StarCraft 2" system. Through diverse maps, player interaction will be greatly enhanced.
