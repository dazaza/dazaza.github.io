Edible oil should be changed regularly

There are a wide variety of edible oils on the market, each with its own characteristics. Health experts suggest that people should not consume a single type of oil for long periods of time. Oils should be alternated and can be appropriately mixed as blended oils to truly benefit health. Below is an introduction to the characteristics of various oils:

Olive Oil: Olive oil contains the highest amount of monounsaturated fatty acids among all edible oils. It can lower low-density cholesterol (bad cholesterol) and increase high-density cholesterol (good cholesterol), thus helping prevent cardiovascular diseases and reducing the occurrence of gallbladder inflammation and gallstones. Olive oil also contains vitamins A, D, E, K, and carotene, which can improve digestive function, enhance calcium deposition in bones, and delay brain atrophy. However, due to its high price and bland taste lacking the enticing fat aroma that people are accustomed to, many find it difficult to accept. Nevertheless, considering one's health, it is still advisable to consume some olive oil.

Corn Oil: Corn is referred to as the "golden crop" because of its high nutritional value. Most of the nutrients in corn, especially various vitamins and minerals, are concentrated in the germ. Therefore, the oil extracted from the corn germ should be very nutritious. Corn oil is rich in essential vitamin E and unsaturated fatty acids, with a content exceeding 80%, mainly consisting of linoleic acid and oleic acid. Linoleic acid accounts for more than 50% of the total oil content. Linoleic acid is an essential fatty acid that the human body cannot synthesize on its own. It has the ability to lower cholesterol and blood pressure, soften blood vessels, enhance muscle and heart functions, prevent and improve arteriosclerosis, reduce the occurrence of heart disease, and alleviate the onset of prostate conditions and dermatitis.

Corn germ oil is easily absorbed by the human body, with an absorption rate as high as 97%. For most people, especially the elderly, it is an ideal health-preserving cooking oil. People with cardiovascular health needs should consume it regularly. According to experiments, continuous consumption of corn oil for 4-5 months can significantly reduce high blood lipid levels. Corn oil, rich in linoleic acid, is also a raw material used in the pharmaceutical industry to manufacture drugs such as Panton, Linoleic Acid Pills, and Yishouning.

Additionally, corn oil contains phytosterols and phospholipids, which have a unique effect of preventing cholesterol deposition in human serum and inhibiting the absorption of harmful cholesterol. Japanese scientists have found that among all plant oils consumed long-term, corn germ oil has the highest reduction rate in human blood cholesterol concentration, reaching 16%.

Canola Oil: Contains a high level of monounsaturated fatty acids, which helps promote bile secretion. Canola oil is the cheapest among all oils rich in monounsaturated fatty acids, making it widely popular. The human body absorbs canola oil very well, with an absorption rate as high as 99%. Therefore, the linoleic acid and other unsaturated fatty acids, along with vitamin E and other nutrients contained in it, can be effectively absorbed by the body, providing certain benefits in softening blood vessels and delaying aging. Since the raw material for extracting oil is the seed of a plant, it generally contains a certain amount of seed lecithin, which is crucial for the development of blood vessels, nerves, and the brain. Canola oil contains very little or almost no cholesterol, so those who need to control their cholesterol intake can consume it with peace of mind. Canola oil is a type of oil with particularly high erucic acid content, and whether it causes myocardial fat deposition and damages the heart remains controversial. Therefore, patients with coronary heart disease and hypertension should consume it sparingly.

Peanut Oil: Contains abundant oleic acid, lecithin, and various vitamins and bioactive natural polyphenolic substances, thus having the function of lowering platelet aggregation and reducing total cholesterol and bad cholesterol levels, as well as preventing arteriosclerosis and cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases.

Soybean Oil: Rich in polyunsaturated fatty acids and vitamins E and D, it has the efficacy of reducing the incidence of cardiovascular diseases. It can also boost immunity and increase the weight of physically weak and thin individuals. The lecithin it contains has important positive physiological effects on the human body, which is rare in general foods and oils. Among various oils, soybean oil is the one most prone to oxidation, so it should be consumed while it's "fresh."

Salad Oil: Is the most refined edible oil, free from harmful substances like aflatoxin, and undergoes high-temperature treatment, making it "cooked oil." Salad oil is suitable for stir-frying, deep-frying, pan-frying, and cold mixing. Repeated high-temperature heating destroys the nutritional value of salad oil, making it unsuitable for consumption. Additionally, although salad oil has the effect of preventing cardiovascular diseases, excessive consumption can still have a certain negative impact on the heart and brain vessels and easily lead to obesity.
