A Glimpse into Jewish Thought

by liqiao359 on 2010-04-17 10:13:43

Website for the Complete Works of Jewish Wisdom: http://www.judahcn.com   How do young Jews commit to upholding Jewish traditions and helping all humanity in the world? One answer lies in the life of this compassionate young Jewish man, the wisdom of Jewish wealth. This is a short article he wrote titled "How I Express My Jewish Commitment."

How do I express my Jewish commitment? It happens every day in aspects involving marriage, family, career, character, and inner improvement. I married my beloved wife Rosa on May 2, 2007. Since that day, we have been nurturing our marriage under a warm and loving Jewish background — mutual respect, commitment, and growth have become the focus of our lives. For us, it is a very exciting yet extremely challenging period; we are constantly facing the challenges of life, communication, humor, compromise, and promoting each other's spiritual growth and insight.

The love and warmth of adherence to Jewish tradition have truly enriched our marriage, giving our marriage profound meaning and direction. Just six months ago, we began the beautiful journey of living together in a Jewish household. We are also becoming increasingly observant of the Sabbath, which has become an indescribable "island of happiness" for us.

We have participated in a series of activities within the Jewish community. We have become active members of a dynamic and modern synagogue where our young and energetic rabbi has become a great friend and spiritual mentor to us. We also host egalitarian Sabbath prayers and spiritual services at our home. We have started studying a part of the Talmud every week. In summary, we have both found our progress, though slow, far exceeds what we could achieve individually.

As for relationships with family, I want to express my Jewish commitment through respecting my parents, being a good son, maintaining contact with my sister and three brothers, supporting them, being reliable and loving. This is difficult, but I am making efforts to let them know my intention is to practice good character traits rather than preach to anyone. This is also an important challenge related to my relatives and my wife's family.

At the same time, I try to remember my deceased parents who had a significant impact on my life and Jewish identity. Based on these principles, I have been very actively involved in recording my family history and researching ancestors who lived in Hungary and Lithuania according to genealogical studies. Of course, there is also my Zionism. At the same time, I study the development of Israel and learn Hebrew (learning again and again) and try to understand the dynamics and wonders of this precious land and society.

In my career as a doctor, I express consistent courtesy and propriety by smiling and interacting with colleagues and patients we meet and treat. This is also very challenging, especially when the workload is busy and many demands are placed on me. At the same time, I regularly study the latest developments in the medical field to improve the clinical skills I am dedicated to. Maintaining kosher dietary laws at work is also an interesting thing. I think my non-Jewish colleagues must have learned quite a bit about Judaism.

In my personal behavior, character development, and spiritual growth, I strive to improve myself by studying the law and adhering to traditional Jewish principles of behavior and character development. In fact, these activities, despite not pursuing them all at once, and some being abnormal, still have a very positive impact on helping me grow. I attend prayers several times a week, attend Talmud classes at the rabbi's synagogue, have weekly phone calls and research ethical learning with a Jewish companion, read books daily on how to improve one's character, spiritual discoveries, and the dangers of rumors. At the same time, I study a part of the law every week, along with ancient Jewish scholars' commentaries, sometimes alone and sometimes with my wife.

I find studying very interesting and enlightening, and it is very challenging in terms of maintaining smooth spiritual growth. Striving to improve oneself, striving to grasp the concept of humility (let alone trying to practice humility), battling one's own anger, etc., wow, these are all very difficult and seem endless.

But it can be very uplifting to know, learn, and develop oneself, to grow together with Rose, to motivate oneself to become a better person, a better Jew every day.
