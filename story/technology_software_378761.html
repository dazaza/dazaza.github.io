### 1. What are Rogue Apps?

In simple terms, rogue apps are similar to the notorious rogue software from a few years ago. They are applications developed or modified by a small group of developers/interest groups with ulterior motives for illegal profit, taking advantage of the open characteristics of smartphone platforms like Android. Once these apps infiltrate a user's phone, they will secretly carry out unauthorized and illegal operations in the background without permission, causing direct or indirect losses to users.

Typically, rogue apps have the following characteristics:

- Forced or bundled installation

- Hijacking system resources/services

- Interfering with the normal operation of other software or system functions

- Difficult to completely uninstall using normal means

Once rogue apps are installed, the most direct loss may be secret charges. Additionally, personal information privacy is also at risk.

### 2. How Do Rogue Apps Secretly Charge Fees?

Through analysis of multiple APK installation packages of rogue apps on the Android platform, we can understand that the charging process mainly involves the following steps:

- Developers implant malicious charge code during the development phase (usually at the very beginning of the program launch) and package the final application into an APK, which is distributed through various channels.

- Users download the APK package of this app and successfully install it locally.

- Thereafter, each time the program starts, it automatically runs the malicious code, which may secretly collect the user's phone number, IMSI, ICCID, IMEI, etc., and send this information to a dedicated server (some even encrypt it to prevent being caught).

- After receiving the information, the server returns instructions informing the app to execute a segment of malicious charge code, sending a text message to a specified phone number (or secretly installing software, opening a special URL with the browser, etc.).

- After sending the text message, the charge number generally returns a charge notification. At this point, the malicious code forcibly intercepts the receipt SMS, so the user does not receive any notification and unknowingly pays the money.

Additionally, some rogue apps bundle additional third-party apps themselves. Based on the promotion effectiveness of these bundled apps, they earn commissions from the bundled party. Typically, for every successful promotion of a bundled app, the commission ranges from a few cents to 1 yuan. With the huge volume of bundled apps (especially those embedded in popular third-party ROMs), these rogue apps also generate substantial profits.

Some rogue apps use both methods mentioned above to make exorbitant profits, earning income from both users and the bundled apps, making them highly malicious.

### 3. How Do Rogue Apps Succeed?

- Unscrupulous developers/publishers, seeking short-term gains, implant rogue code during the development phase.

- A large number of underground publishing channels for (cracked, beautified, localized, third-party) apps provide fertile ground for rogue apps.

- The review mechanism for application uploads/releases on official channels is inadequate.

- Users lack professional knowledge, identification skills, and vigilance, making them easy to deceive.

- There is a lack of corresponding tools for detection and reporting on the market.

### 5. How to Deal with Rogue Apps

The most basic precaution comes from the user's own awareness. Please obtain apps through legitimate channels, and try to avoid unreliable third parties. For uncertain quality apps, check their user reviews online first and cross-reference with the rogue app blacklist. If already installed, uninstall it quickly (before doing so, retain necessary evidence).
