5s Shortage and 5c Production Cut: Apple's iPhone Combo Strategy Faces Challenges

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:20:13

Currently, it seems that Tim Cook, the CEO of Apple, has not received support from Apple users for his idea to expand market space through a product portfolio approach.

In the iPhone launch event in September this year, Apple introduced two new phones simultaneously for the first time, named iPhone 5s and iPhone 5c respectively. Since then, there have been continuous reports about the poor sales of the iPhone 5c.

Recently, foreign media reported that Apple had notified suppliers to reduce production orders for the iPhone 5c. Prior to Apple's response, major sales channels at home and abroad had already initiated price cuts due to the slow-moving sales of the iPhone 5c. In China’s grey market, the price of the iPhone 5c was significantly lower than the official retail price right after its launch.

This is something Apple does not want to see. Regardless of which link bears the loss from the price cut, Apple is unwilling to see the price of its newly released product continuously decline. Therefore, Apple must respond to this situation.

There are no clear data yet on the inventory status of the 5c, but from the actions taken by various market and channel players, the situation appears less than optimistic. Cook himself is extremely sensitive to inventory issues. He once said that inventory products are basically devils.

Apple hopes to control the inventory of the 5c through reduced production and influence the price trend as much as possible. However, compared with the 5s which is in short supply, both the market strategy and product positioning of the iPhone 5c have problems.

Cook's Fondness for "Dual Product Strategy"

Under Steve Jobs' leadership, Apple maintained a rhythm of releasing one new product per year in the smartphone and tablet computer fields. Except for the first generation iPhone which was immediately discontinued after the second generation came out, older models like the iPhone 3G to iPhone 4S were chosen to continue selling at discounted prices after newer models were launched. Jobs used a "one new, one old" combination to highlight the market advantages of the new product while appropriately extending the user base and maintaining a healthy inventory state.

However, Cook had different plans.

In October 2012, Apple launched the iPad mini and iPad 4. This was the first time Apple introduced two tablets in a single event, breaking the previous "rules" left by Jobs. If we also count the iPad 3 (the new iPad) launched in March that year, Apple completed a "triple release" of tablet products within a year.

Cook expected that the 7.9-inch iPad mini would unleash the long-standing demand for a portable iPad in the market. The actual reaction confirmed his judgment: even without a retina display, the iPad mini still sold well.

Cook's first attempt at a product combination won applause from the market, but it did not bring Apple any benefits.

According to Apple's financial report, in the second quarter of 2013, Apple's iPad sales dropped by 14% compared to the same period last year, and the iPad profit fell by 22%. This not only indicated that the dual product combination of the iPad 4 and iPad mini did not perform as well as the previous model, but also under the combination strategy, the profit margin of individual iPads also decreased.

In an interview, Cook admitted that the profit margin of the iPad mini was below the average level of Apple products.

Although Apple has never disclosed the sales data of the iPad 4 and iPad mini separately, third-party analysis agencies believe that the iPad mini with lower profits clearly impacted the sales volume of the high-profit iPad 4, which was the main reason for the decrease in iPad product profits.

The "Overcorrection" of the iPhone 5s/5c

An important reason for the failure of the iPad dual product strategy is that Apple invested more effort into the iPad mini. Compared to the previous generation iPad 3, the higher-priced and higher-profit iPad 4 had almost no significant updates at the time.

Some analysts even considered the iPad 4 to be a hasty work by Apple.

In this year's new iPhone launch event, Cook seemed to have learned from the mistake made in the dual iPad strategy, significantly enhancing the upgrade of the high-priced and high-profit iPhone 5s, while for the low-cost iPhone 5c, whether in terms of product innovation or hardware performance, it was very similar to the previous generation iPhone 5, and the pricing did not create enough gap with the high-end 5s.

Unlike the situation where the iPad mini competed with the iPad 4, this time Apple's iPhone combination achieved completely opposite results. The high-priced and high-spec 5s was in short supply, while the low-priced and low-spec 5c had poor sales - facing the awkward situation of price cuts by retailers and production cuts by suppliers.

This can be seen as an important adjustment to the dual product strategy during the Cook era, but it may have gone too far in correcting the error.

The iPad combination sacrificed the sales and profits of the iPad 4, while the iPhone combination sacrificed the market space of the 5c. This large swing stems from Apple's inability to make sufficiently reasonable market positioning for the two concurrently released new products.

Voting with Their Feet by Users and the Market

On Apple's official website in China, the page for ordering the iPhone 5c shows that the product will be shipped within 24 hours. For the iPhone 5s, however, customers need to wait 2-3 weeks.

This situation has persisted for a long time.

In Apple's official stores in Hong Kong and the U.S., we see the same information. This indicates that the sufficient inventory of the 5c and the general shortage of the 5s is a global phenomenon across Apple's official channels.

The same applies to third-party channels. On mainstream e-commerce websites like JD.com, all versions of the 5s are out of stock, while the 5c is not only abundantly stocked, but various e-commerce platforms are also offering discount promotions such as cashback coupons.

On Taobao, China's largest C2C platform, the 5s was being resold for 6000-9000 yuan over the past period, whereas the 5c was embarrassingly being constantly discounted, currently priced around 3800 yuan for the official version on Taobao, and as low as 3200 yuan for the grey market version.

In carrier channels, the situation is roughly similar. Not long ago, Beijing Unicom even raised the prepaid phone bill threshold for the gold edition 5s, due to its "rare and valuable" status. An unnamed insider from a domestic carrier stated that this is a sensitive period; Apple is an important partner for carriers, so they cannot disclose the sales figures for the 5s and 5c.

Gervais Pellissier, CFO of French telecommunications company Orange, said that the sales volume of the 5c is far lower than the higher-priced 5s.

Users and the market have made their own choices regarding Apple's dual product strategy. From the information available so far, there was not much difference between the initial production and supply volumes of the 5s and 5c. The main reason for today's shortage of the 5s and surplus inventory of the 5c is the huge difference in market purchasing power.

A survey by a US consumer market research firm showed that in the US, the sales volume of the 5s is more than twice that of the 5c.

Localytics' report revealed that the global sales ratio of the 5s to the 5c is as high as 3.7:1.

Apple's dual product design failed to give the 5c enough product competitiveness, and at the same time, the pricing of the 5c was not enough to truly attract mid-to-low-end smartphone users who are sensitive to price.

The Fate of the 5c Depends on "Apple's Choice"

A week ago, an anonymous person claiming to be a Foxconn employee said that both the Zhengzhou and Shenzhen Foxconn plants had reduced the production of the iPhone 5c, and some employees processing the 5c would be retrained to switch to producing the 5s.

Insiders from Foxconn and Pegatron Corporation said that Apple has instructed these two main manufacturers of the iPhone 5c to lower their capacity. Foxconn will slash one-third of the original 5c shipment volume, and Pegatron needs to reduce its capacity by 20%. According to industry insiders, in the near future, the production volume of the 5c will be reduced to half of its original amount.

The news is not good, but for Cook and Apple's senior management, this is easier to handle than the predicament faced by the dual iPad product strategy before —— after all, this time the poorly performing product is the one with lower profit margins.

Research firm IHS iSuppli issued a report stating that the component cost difference between the 16GB versions of the 5s and 5c is $26, accounting for only 26% of the price difference between the two —— the standalone profit of the iPhone 5s is higher.

Therefore, shifting part of the 5c production capacity to the 5s is a wise move. It can meet the market's demand for the 5s, increase shipping volume and profit, and at the same time help alleviate the inventory pressure of the 5c, mitigating the price drop of the 5c in cooperative channels.

Another internal message from Apple's manufacturers suggests that even after a 50% reduction in production, the daily output of the 5c might still reach 150,000 units —— this number exceeds the main production capacity of many other brands' flagship phones. However, for Apple, whether the market can smoothly absorb the daily 150,000 units of the 5c remains a challenge.

After domestic and international third-party channels have reduced the actual price of the iPhone 5c by 500-1000 yuan, it will be difficult for Apple to raise its price back to the original level —— forcibly restoring the price would be suicidal. However, Apple's consistent pricing strategy is not to lower prices during the product's primary life cycle until the next generation of products is launched.

The 5c has just been on the market for less than 2 months, and it would be hard for Cook to challenge Apple's "tradition".

It is worth noting that throughout his career at IBM, Compaq, and Apple, Cook has extensive experience and capabilities in handling manufacturing, distribution, inventory, and supply chains. The current problems faced by the 5c should fall precisely within Cook's area of expertise. The only awkward aspect lies in that Cook needs to use his skilled abilities to address issues possibly caused by his risky ventures in areas he is less familiar with.

Apple has three possible options for the future of the 5c.

Apple can still firmly refuse to lower the official price, but allow channels to discount freely. Once the 5c reaches a reasonable price, its sales volume could increase. After all, from a product perspective, the 5c still holds market potential.

Apple can also gradually reduce the production of the 5c, pushing harder for the higher-profit 5s, and phase out the 5c from its product lineup. Doing so could further boost the production of the 5s, but it would also announce Cook's failure in the dual-product strategy for iPhones.

Apple's final option would be to proactively lower the price and reposition the market and target audience for the 5c. While this approach aligns with channel demands and satisfies consumer preferences, it poses immense pressure on Apple itself. The profit margin of the 5c is not as high as that of the 5s, and further price reductions would diminish the profitability of the 5c, potentially impacting the high-margin sales of the 5s.

The iPhone 5c has the potential to increase sales and improve its market performance —— but it depends on what Apple is willing to sacrifice for it.

Conclusion

Apple hopes to control the inventory of the 5c through reduced production and influence the price trend as much as possible. However, compared to the 5s, which is in short supply, the market strategy and product positioning of the iPhone 5c both have issues.
