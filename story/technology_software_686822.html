First trick: You need to pay attention, at intersections with electronic eyes, there are diamond-shaped grooves dug before and after the warning line, where the inductive coil is buried. But you should remember, the electronic eye only starts 3 seconds after the red light is on! Also, if you drive over the line, the first photo will be taken, regardless of whether the front or rear wheels cross the line, do not move, absolutely do not move, moving will trigger the coil to take the second photo! Three photos are required to be valid. The rules of photo-taking evidence collection are as follows. Generally, the evidence photos are like this:

The first one is the front wheel crossing the warning line.

The second one is the rear wheel crossing the warning line.

The third one is your vehicle crossing the warning line on the opposite side of the intersection.

Only when these three photos are complete can they be submitted to the police, and then you will receive a fine notice. Haha, 200 yuan!

So, if the front wheel crosses, it doesn't matter much, taking the first photo is okay, if you don't move, the second photo won't be taken. Let's talk again, if earlier your front wheel was photographed, some people might think to back up a little bit, right? Wrong, backing up triggers another photo! Haha!

Revealing the working principle of electronic police:

1. The electronic eye uses an inductive loop to sense the pressure transmitted by vehicles on the road surface, and through sensors, the signals are collected into the X processor, investment management, and sent to the register for temporary storage (this data is valid within one red light cycle);

2. Within the same time interval (red light cycle), if two pulse signals are generated simultaneously, it is considered "effective". Simply put, if the red light is on, your front wheel crosses the line while the rear wheel has not yet crossed the line, only one pulse is generated. Without consecutive two pulses, no photo is taken;

3. In some cases: Some people drive their front wheels across the line, fearing being photographed, so they reverse back inside the line, but still get photographed. Why? Because one front and one back, generate a "pair" of pulse signals (this pair of pulses is produced within the same red light cycle), I've been penalized n times due to this, ###;

4. When the yellow light is on, the photography system delays for 2 seconds before starting; when the red light is on, the system has already started; when the green light is about to turn on, the system shuts down 2 seconds in advance, mainly to prevent accidental photography. Therefore, many taxi drivers know that it's almost time to go, and there's nothing wrong with this reasoning. However, I suggest not doing this, because it's hard to grasp the timing.
