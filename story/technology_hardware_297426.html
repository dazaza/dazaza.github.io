Manchester United's depleted team wins big, holding the golden rule of winning, and reversing Chelsea by the end of December is not a dream.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 08:38:58

Manchester United's makeshift team achieved a great victory, holding the golden rule of winning; reversing Chelsea by the end of December is not a dream. Sina Sports reports that Manchester United played away against West Ham, and the Red Devils' defense experienced an unprecedented lack of manpower. The main central defender Ferdinand was absent for a long time due to a calf injury, another main central defender Vidic was unexpectedly absent due to flu, the third central defender Evans was also absent due to a calf injury; the main right-back O'Shea could not participate in the game due to a thigh injury, and although the main goalkeeper Van der Sar's knee injury had been resolved, Sir Alex still gave the starting opportunity to Kuzczak who has been in very good form recently. After a crazy three-goal spree in 11 minutes of the second half, Manchester United won 4-0 away; two and a half hours later, better news came, former player Tevez scored the decisive goal against Chelsea, reducing the points gap between Manchester United and the Blues to 2 points.

Manchester United’s makeshift lineup still managed a resounding 4-0 victory despite the absence of four out of five key defenders.

In the previous 117 encounters, Manchester United had achieved a record of 52 wins, 24 draws, and 41 losses. Compared with the starting lineup from last week's 4-1 away victory over Portsmouth, Sir Alex made changes in two positions this match, replacing Vidic and Carrick with Anderson and Gibson respectively.

West Ham's defense was simply a complete mess. In the past three league matches, the Hammers conceded eight goals, and since August, they had never kept a clean sheet in the Premier League. Since the fifth round when they lost 0-1 away to Wigan, including a mid-term League Cup match, West Ham had been scored against in all 11 consecutive matches before this game. However, West Ham was not entirely helpless. Since the sixth round when they lost 2-3 at home to Liverpool, up until this match, West Ham had scored in all 11 consecutive matches. Moreover, in the past three Premier League matches where Manchester United played away against West Ham, the Red Devils lost two games. Of course, last season, Manchester United double zeroed West Ham both home and away.

"When you face a team like Manchester United, you have to believe you can win, otherwise they will beat you," said West Ham captain Scott Parker, trying to boost his teammates' morale, but compared to Manchester United, this West Ham was simply too weak.

Fletcher playing as a right-back, Carrick playing as a central defender, along with midfielders Gibson, Anderson, and Scholes, plus Giggs who can also play as a midfielder, Manchester United once had six players who usually play in the midfield on the field simultaneously. With three main defenders and the main goalkeeper Van der Sar all absent, there was only Evra left in the defense, making Manchester United's lineup truly a makeshift one. But despite numerous difficulties, Manchester United still managed a resounding 4-0 victory away.

Since the 12th round of the Premier League when Manchester United lost 0-1 away to Chelsea, in the following three league matches, Manchester United not only achieved three consecutive victories, but also scored 11 goals in total, conceding only one penalty, achieving a net goal difference of 10 against their opponents, showing astonishing offensive firepower, thus gaining plenty of net goals for the Red Devils.

Manchester United's many shining records

This match marked Ferguson's 1300th official game as Manchester United's manager, with a coaching record of 763 wins, 305 draws, and 232 losses, achieving a win rate of 58.69%. The players delivered a satisfying victory in this milestone match for the boss.

Since the start of this season's Premier League, whenever Manchester United took the lead during a match, they always won. In league matches against Birmingham, Wigan, Manchester City, Stoke, Bolton, Blackburn, Everton, Portsmouth, and this match against West Ham; in Champions League matches where Manchester United defeated Beşiktaş and CSKA Moscow away; and in EFL Cup matches where Manchester United successively defeated Wolves, Barnsley, and Tottenham, Manchester United maintained a perfect record of 9 wins in 9 matches in the league and 14 wins in 14 matches across all competitions (Community Shield matches before the start of the new Premier League season are not included), which is indeed an astonishing record.

Besides this, Manchester United is also the best away team this year. In 2009, Manchester United has so far won 13 away matches in the Premier League, more than any other team.

This is not the strongest Manchester United, but it is the Manchester United Sir Alex hopes to see most.

The processes of scoring the third and fourth goals by Manchester United were almost like passing the ball into the opponent's goal. Currently, Manchester United's three main offensive initiators are striker Rooney, left winger Giggs, and right winger Valencia who can instantly use speed to tear apart the opposing defense. The reason why Manchester United could achieve such a great victory in this match is directly related to the excellent performances of these three players, along with veterans, new signings, and young talents.

Veterans rejuvenated. Whether it's Giggs or Scholes, both performed impressively in this match. The former assisted Gibson in scoring the goal that extended the lead, while the latter helped Manchester United break the deadlock first in this match. As for the starting captain Gary Neville in this match, the central defense he guarded barely made mistakes before he was injured and had to leave the field.

New signings further integrated into the team. Valencia, worth £17 million, once again contributed one goal and one assist in this match. The day before the match, he was selected as the club's best player of November by Manchester United's official website. Since this selection was entirely based on votes cast by Manchester United fans on the club's official website, it shows how popular Valencia has become among Manchester United fans.

Young talents grew strong and showed tendencies of gradually taking on major responsibilities. In recent matches, Sir Alex discovered "long-range shooting king" Gibson and Anderson who has shown rapid progress and signs of awakening. Given time, the 22-year-old Gibson and the 21-year-old Anderson have great potential to shoulder the midfield responsibilities of Manchester United.

Experienced veterans + practical new signings + continuously emerging young talents + middle-aged pillars represented by Rooney, this is not the strongest Manchester United, but it might be the Manchester United Sir Alex really hopes to see.

Tevez assists his former club, shifting the pressure onto Chelsea

Tevez said last week that no matter what, the Manchester United fans booing him made him feel deeply hurt. After all, during his time at Manchester United, he gave his all every time he stepped onto the pitch. However, in this round of matches, Tevez's free-kick goal helped Manchester City successfully reverse Chelsea, objectively helping his former club Manchester United reduce the points gap with Chelsea. After this round of matches, Manchester United is only 2 points behind Chelsea.

In the upcoming matches, Manchester United will first challenge Bundesliga champions Wolfsburg in the Champions League away next Wednesday, then return to the Premier League to play Villa and Wolves at home, and challenge Fulham and Hull City away; then on the last day of 2009 Beijing Time, they will play Wigan at home. On the contrary, Chelsea will first play a meaningless match against Apoel at home next Wednesday; then in the Premier League, they will successively play Everton and Portsmouth at home; and challenge West Ham and Birmingham away; the last match of 2009 will be against Fulham at home. Both teams have 5 remaining league matches in 2009, 3 home and 2 away. Once Chelsea, who has already suffered two consecutive defeats in all competitions, makes any mistake, Manchester United will surely catch up.

After defeating Everton, Manchester United's main left-back Evra expressed the wish to win consecutively before the New Year: "We are now 5 points behind Chelsea, but we must keep winning until the end of December (i.e., aiming for 8 consecutive league wins). We have already qualified for the next stage in the Champions League, and the World Cup qualifiers have ended, so we can focus our efforts on the league because we want to win the quadruple league championship!"

Now it seems that Evra's wish is not impossible to achieve, after all, in December, Manchester United's remaining league opponents are mostly limited in strength. Moreover, Manchester United has successfully reduced the points gap to just 2 points, shifting the pressure onto Chelsea, especially considering that next month Drogba, Essien, Mikel, and Kalou will participate in the Africa Cup of Nations. If Chelsea gives Manchester United any chance, Ferguson and his disciples have a high possibility of achieving the unprecedented feat of four consecutive Premier League titles. Don't forget, after 15 rounds last season, Manchester United was in third place, trailing Chelsea and Liverpool, but how many people would have thought that the Red Devils would then go on an incredible 11-match winning streak in the league?
