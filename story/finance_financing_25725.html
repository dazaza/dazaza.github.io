How to Save More in Summer Finance: Here Are Five Tricks for You!

Summer means electricity peak, water use peak, taxi taking peak, and heat relief peak... It also means the peak of spending. How to "cut down on peak spending"? The author has invited five "peak-cutting" experts to show off their "magic weapons" and deal with the challenges of summer.

Energy-saving Products: A Sustainable Approach

Recommended by: Mr. Chen

From: www.coko365.com

The phrase "picking up sesame seeds but losing watermelons" is not appropriate when it comes to household finance.

In fact, some "sesame seeds" must be picked up, and over time, they can accumulate into a big watermelon. For example, fully adopting energy-saving products is a very thorough way of saving money. Although these products cost more than ordinary ones when purchased, which may seem like losing a "watermelon," they can adjust the motor to its most economical state, and the electricity saved within three to four years can make up for the price difference at purchase.

Take air conditioners as an example, assuming they operate for 120 days in both summer and winter, running 5 hours each day, a 2500-watt energy-efficient air conditioner with an efficiency ratio of 3.0 compared to a regular wall-mounted air conditioner with an efficiency ratio of 2.5 can save over 180 yuan in electricity bills per year. Currently, the price difference between a regular air conditioner and an energy-saving frequency conversion air conditioner is only a few hundred yuan.

As for electricity-consuming giants like refrigerators, comparing a daily power consumption of 0.5 degrees for an energy-saving refrigerator versus 0.9 degrees for a regular one, you can save 0.4 degrees of electricity per day, amounting to around 89 yuan per year; under the same material, volume, and class function conditions, the price of an energy-saving refrigerator is only 300-800 yuan higher than that of a regular one.

Energy-saving lamps have an even better cost-performance ratio. A common 40-watt incandescent bulb costs 1.5-3.5 yuan, while an 8-watt energy-saving lamp tube with the same brightness costs about 10 yuan; if used for 6 hours a day, an 8-watt energy-saving lamp can save 70 degrees of electricity per year, saving over 40 yuan per year; considering its lifespan, it's even more cost-effective.

End-of-season Clothing Purchases: Cost-effective and Stylish

Recommended by: Miss Gu

A woman wants to look dazzling, and this cannot be achieved without the enhancement of fashionable clothing, especially in summer when daily wardrobe changes are needed. How can one buy the most suitable clothes with the least amount of money? My method is to shop at the end of the season. For example, GIVANA's white suit set was priced at over 400 yuan when it first came out in early May, but by August, it was discounted to 30% of the original price. Last year, I bought a set of skirts, casual skirts, and linen dresses all at once, totaling only 230 yuan, which was half the original price. These clothes were worn from August until early October, allowing me to enjoy fresh styles and carry them over to the next year.

Even more cost-effective was the leather jacket I just bought. When I first spotted it last December, it was priced at over 900 yuan with no discounts, but last month, it was already on sale at half price. Following this approach to buying clothes, one can save at least two to three thousand yuan per year.

Moreover, different purchasing locations can lead to significant differences in clothing prices. Rent prices for stores in certain areas tend to be higher, and merchants will definitely factor this cost into the product price. Therefore, in some ordinary streets, one might find quality clothes at reasonable prices. If one is familiar with their various sizes, online shopping on Taobao can also be an economical option, but it's important to choose large and reputable websites for safety.

Home-based Fitness: Practical and Reliable

Recommended by: Ms. Liu

Last year was my first summer after having a baby, and beauty and weight loss quickly became my top priorities. To be honest, summer is a great time for weight loss: eating less than usual, burning more calories, and the exposed parts of your body constantly remind you to stick to your goals. However, going to the gym to lose weight is expensive, and it's inconvenient to take care of the baby. After much consideration, I decided to implement a "home-based fitness plan."

Firstly, exercise according to the areas where fat accumulates:

Running in place: Choose a spot indoors or in a hallway with about one square meter of space, run barefoot for 15 minutes every day.

Climbing stairs: Go up and down stairs three to four times a week, each session lasting 30 minutes continuously, burning about 400 calories and strengthening the muscles in the calves, thighs, and hips.

Walking: Walk at a speed of 4.8 km/h for about 45 minutes after meals, burning calories quickly. Walking again two to three hours after meals yields even better results, focusing on exercising the abdomen.

Simple Yoga: Sit on a chair, right hand holding the left knee joint, left hand behind or on the right hip joint, inhale and twist the body, hold still for 15-30 seconds, breathe naturally, then exhale back to the original position. Do four times on each side. Three to four times a week, not only strengthens muscles, increases flexibility and agility, but also maintains a slim figure.

The second part is beauty care, the secret is to buy nutritional supplements instead of cosmetics. For example, a 30g brand whitening cream costs at least 100 yuan, but if you buy ginseng, astragalus, and red dates to make soup, it can last for two to three months, ensuring a rosy complexion and excellent skin tone. Qi and blood are the fundamentals of beauty care, these Chinese herbs have properties of replenishing blood and qi, and can be easily purchased at regular pharmacies at reasonable prices, generally 20-50 yuan/kg, much more practical than buying cosmetics.

Timely Leisure: Graceful and Enjoyable

Recommended by: Mr. Shao

I'm not sure if the temperature stimulates the secretion of certain hormones, but anyway, everyone's enthusiasm for gathering and leisure always peaks in summer. When it's your turn to host, remember to take advantage of discount periods.

For example, KTV singing: Going to KTV on weekends or evenings usually costs more, but after midnight, there's a 50% discount, and if it's not a weekend, there's a 70% discount after midnight, making it even more cost-effective with a VIP card. In fact, for those who love KTV singing, getting a membership card at their favorite KTV is relatively economical. Some KTVs offer a membership card for 100 yuan, along with a 300-yuan consumption package containing a 100-yuan voucher, which can be used immediately upon purchase.

Besides KTV, many sports venues also offer discounted rates during certain hours, such as bowling and indoor swimming, where prices vary depending on the time. If you can arrange your time properly and take full advantage of these discounts, your leisure and entertainment will be even more enjoyable.

Off-season Travel: Straight to the Heart of the Experience

Recommended by: Mr. Zhang

It must be acknowledged that off-season travel has pros and cons. The biggest issue is that you may not see the most beautiful moments of the attractions, but the benefits include more favorable prices, more relaxed services, and exclusive scenery for yourself. For example, traveling to Hainan, Thailand, and Hong Kong in summer is a good choice.

I once went to Thailand in August, expecting it to be unbearably hot, but it turned out to be the rainy season there, which is said to be the best time for tourism. As for places like Beijing, which mainly feature cultural landscapes, it's better to visit during the off-season.

Additionally, traveling during the off-season avoids unpleasant situations like "lighting fires under the moon" or "making noise in the forest." You can go straight to the heart of the experience and intimately connect with the beautiful nature.
