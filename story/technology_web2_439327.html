Industry insiders teach you how to spot eight scams in automobile products

Many car owners have such feelings: since they bought a car, at least half of their hearts are devoted to their beloved cars - how to maintain, decorate or modify the car. Automobile products can be dazzling - photocatalyst deodorization, nano fuel-saving agent, cooling spray, oil-free driving wear-resistant agent, etc. These products make people feel excited just by hearing their names. However, these names are often "traps" that tempt you to spend money, because most of them are just scams! Following our last week's focus on how to "save money on car repair", this week we invited Mr. Zhu, who was once an automobile product distributor on Yongfu Road, to expose those scamming automobile products for us.

Scam Ranking 1: Instant Cooling Inside the Car

Scam: Can instantly cool down the hot interior of the car

Mr. Lei was attracted by a product named "Car Spray Quick Cooling Agent" when he passed by an auto parts market. The product claimed to be the "Cooling King", which could cool down the car even if it was exposed to the scorching sun in the desert for two hours and the car temperature reached over 60℃. Just a light spray would make the car "refreshing and pleasant". Seeing the price was only tens of yuan, out of curiosity, he bought a bottle. After spraying it a few times in his car, the so-called "instant" cooling effect did exist, but it only lasted for a few seconds, and the car was filled with a hard-to-disappear strange smell.

Mr. Zhu's Comment: These sprays claiming to be quick cooling agents for cars attract special attention from car owners in summer. They all say they use special cooling materials and apply the law of conservation of energy in heat transfer, and just a few sprays can achieve the effect of cooling the whole car, but most of them are exaggerated by merchants. If it were really that good, not only would the air conditioning in the car seem redundant, but also the home air conditioning could be replaced.

Scam Ranking 2: Fuel Saver, Fuel Saving Ball, Fuel Saving Sticker

Scam: Has a "miraculous" fuel-saving effect

Far infrared photon energy fuel saving ball, TT nano fuel saver, TT environmental protection fuel saver - there are countless brands of fuel-saving products in the auto parts market. Each fuel saver claims that its working principle is scientifically reliable, such as "using far-infrared penetration technology to change the gasoline molecular bond and activate oxygen molecules, achieving the functions of combustion promotion and fuel saving", "adding nano-materials into rubber tubes, breaking up aggregated oil molecules into single oil molecules, promoting the full mixing of fuel with air when entering the cylinder and achieving complete combustion." It sounds quite convincing.

Mr. Zhu's Comment: Most fuel savers, fuel saving balls, and fuel saving stickers are pure scams! For example, the products claiming to use infrared energy to achieve fuel-saving effects, actually infrared energy makes gasoline more volatile, I don't know what their principle is? Some say they use "nano" technology, but nano is not energy, how does it help combustion? If the product were really good, why don't so many car manufacturers adopt it? A fuel saver costs only a negligible fraction of the price of a car. So the fuel saver is just like the concept of a "perpetual motion machine", purely a scam.

Scam Ranking 3: Fuel System Cleaning Additives

Scam: Self-cleaning fuel system, no need to go to the repair shop

When doing maintenance and repair, if the vehicle's fuel system is dirty or even clogged, it needs to be cleaned. The mechanic usually suggests the owner do a professional fuel system cleaning, but it requires some cost. There are many "fuel system cleaning gasoline additives" in the auto parts market. Some product instructions claim that they use nano technology, which can automatically clean the fuel system, remove dirt from the fuel system, and achieve the effects of improving car power and reducing fuel consumption. The advantage for car owners is that using these products is not only convenient to add, but also costs only about one hundred yuan to "clean the fuel system by themselves", without going to the repair shop for professional cleaning services. However, don't be tempted by these "benefits".

Mr. Zhu's Comment: The manufacturing threshold for fuel system cleaners is not high, which leads to the flooding of the market with some inferior small brands. If the car owner buys these inferior products to save money, not only will the fuel system not be cleaned properly, but it may also damage the engine. It should be noted that the main component of fuel system dirt is gum. If the fuel system cleaner claims to dissolve or separate the gum, it may also corrode the parts of the fuel system that are not dirty. If the components of such products can burn, they may change the calorific value, ignition point, and burning speed, which has a great impact on the engine; if they claim to be non-burning products, they may deposit on the inner walls of the cylinders, valves, spark plugs, etc., then it's just a matter of time before the car is damaged.

Scam Ranking 5: Magnetized Oxygen Supply Device

Scam: Converts exhaust gas into oxygen

A repairman recommended a filtering magnetized oxygen supply device to Xiao Chen, the owner of an Elantra. The packaging of the product reads: adding it to the exhaust manifold will magnetize the exhaust gas, while generating oxygen, making the combustion more complete, thus enhancing the car engine power, and saving fuel by 6%~16% when driving on different roads! Xiao Chen spent over 500 yuan to buy and use it for a month, but didn't feel any effect, instead causing some trouble - needing to return to the store regularly to clean the device.

Mr. Zhu's Comment: Trying to convert exhaust gas into oxygen with a magnetic field is difficult in principle. Converting exhaust gas into oxygen belongs to a cracking reaction. The volume of a cracking furnace in a regular laboratory is like a refrigerator, and the volume of an industrial cracking furnace is larger than an oil tanker. If such a small magnetized oxygen supply device exaggerates its function, the car owner should pay extra attention! In fact, some of these products are just an iron pipe with a magnet added, with little technical content, similar to pulling off the intake manifold. Using it may instead lead to dust being sucked into the cylinder, exacerbating wear. So use it cautiously!

Scam: Precise positioning, powerful functions

If we count the most common products in the automotive accessories market, in-car GPS navigation devices must be one of them. There are so many brands that they dazzle the eyes. Every product manual claims to be "accurate" in positioning and powerful in function. However, a recent random inspection by the provincial quality supervision bureau exposed some brands' "rumors". After inspecting 40 batches of in-car navigation instruments in Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Zhuhai, and Dongguan, it was found that as many as three-quarters of the products failed to meet quality standards. The pass rate for system positioning accuracy was less than half, and the sampling pass rate for "radiation interference field strength" was also only half. More than half of the products lacked longitude and latitude information display, making it impossible to test positioning accuracy.

Mr. Zhu's Comment: Listing GPS car navigation as a scam blacklist is not unfair, because the quality of products on the market varies greatly, some of which are even patched together by merchants using old products and called the latest GPS navigation products. These products not only have false parameters but also low positioning accuracy, easily leading to wrong guidance; radiation interference products, long-term use can cause drivers headaches, insomnia, memory decline, etc. GPS car navigation is particularly loved by new drivers who are "road blind". However, after knowing that some products have such strong radiation power and such weak guiding functions, would you still dare to buy them randomly?

Scam Ranking 7: Photocatalyst

Scam: Miraculous odor removal, "cure" bad smells

After buying a new car, the strong smell of interior decoration troubles many car owners. To get rid of the smell, many car owners try various methods and pin their hopes on "photocatalyst" products. However, doing a photocatalyst treatment is not cheap, ranging from 500 yuan to over a thousand yuan. Since it's expensive, there should be a reason for it - some photocatalyst products claim to have miraculous deodorizing effects, even "curing" bad smells. But many car owners regret it after doing it because the results are not as significant as they claim.

Mr. Zhu's Comment: Some photocatalyst products on the market only cost dozens of yuan, but when turned into a "maintenance package", the profit margin is no less than that of anti-explosion film products. Deodorizing products definitely have limited effects, and the results cannot be immediate. Photocatalyst products are divided into water-soluble and oil-soluble types. Water-soluble photocatalysts are relatively environmentally friendly and safe, but the technical issue is a challenge faced by many photocatalyst manufacturers; most manufacturers use oil-based solution photocatalysts, but due to the unknown organic solvent components, it is impossible to ensure that they will not produce toxic effects. The sterilization effect of photocatalyst products is still pretty good, but there are no products on the market that can completely eliminate car odors with one or two treatments. Placing charcoal bags for formaldehyde removal in the car to accelerate odor removal is more reliable.

Scam Ranking 8: Car Sealing Wax

Scam: Permanent protection, shining like new

After buying a new car, car owners generally consider doing a full car sealing wax, which makes the car like "coated with a protective membrane", maintaining the paint surface always bright and new. Some even claim that after sealing wax, there is no need to wax for a year. When Mr. Zhang bought his car, he did a sealing wax project. The product claimed to be an "imported product" developed by a foreign institution. Two months later, after Mr. Zhang's car got scratched, the painted area repaired at the 4S store looked brighter than the area where the sealing wax was applied.

Mr. Zhu's Comment: The price of sealing wax is not cheap, and doing car sealing wax is like polishing. Currently, some sealing wax products on the market are of poor quality, with many counterfeit and inferior products. And so-called imported products from abroad are mostly made in inland small factories. Car owners should be cautious about spending money on ineffective products. Especially for sealing wax beauty treatments with short construction time (less than one hour) and fewer processes, they can be considered as useless.

Claiming that sealing wax has unlimited effects is definitely a scam. Usually, after two months, the car wax begins to oxidize, and after six months, it is completely oxidized. After oxidation, it not only cannot protect the car paint for a long time, but it may also corrode the car paint. Sealing wax is just a self-made term in the domestic car beauty industry. Abroad, it is deep waxing. A normal waxing only costs dozens of yuan, while sealing wax costs hundreds or even thousands of yuan. Simply put, sealing wax is just a concept introduced to deceive car owners into paying more money.
