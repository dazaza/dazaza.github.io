[Android Tool]豌豆荚手机精灵: A one-stop sync management tool dedicated to Android phones

by xdash on 2010-09-22 10:05:12

As the first incubated project publicly announced by Innovation Works led by Lee Kai-Fu, the name "Wandoujia Phone Spirit" has, since its unveiling, attracted a great deal of attention in the industry due to its inherent advantages.

I'm sure those who follow industry trends or use Android phones are familiar with Wandoujia. Previously, niche software sites like Guao and ifanr have introduced it, and ifanr also conducted an exclusive interview with the team. People are all trying to guess Lee Kai-Fu's psychology through a probing lens: aside from his lingering affection for his former employer Google, what reasons prompted him to choose this project which isn't exactly groundbreaking?

According to the official explanation, Wandoujia Phone Spirit is a dedicated PC client for managing Android smartphones. Users can install Wandoujia on their PCs, connect via a data cable (the 1.2beta version released at midnight today already supports Wifi), and conveniently execute a series of applications and operations on their computers, including local installation of Android applications (supports double-click installation of APK format), backup and export of phone contacts and SMS information, sending and mass-sending text messages, automatic search for songs and retrieval of album covers and lyrics, phone screen capture, download from domestic video websites, and audio/video format conversion.

The way Wandoujia is used resembles Apple's iTunes for iPod/iTouch/iPhone, or Nokia's official PC suite.

Although the product concept is not a revolutionary original, similar products have appeared in the domestic market before, but Wandoujia's biggest feature compared to similar ones lies in its focus, using Android as the entry point and foothold, exploring the actual usage needs of Android users, continuously expanding in product depth, rather than merely being satisfied with achieving basic functions and then hastily extending product reach to other popular platforms.

In this sense, at least Wandoujia is successful in positioning strategy. When users try to find a suitable management tool for their newly purchased Android phone, they will think of Wandoujia first, rather than considering which one to choose among many homogenized products on the market.

Besides importing and exporting contact information and SMS, Wandoujia's overall planning currently leans more towards entertainment. The official indeed mentioned in interviews that the current target demographic includes "mobile multimedia content consumers".

Considering the current situation of mobile phone function usage by domestic users (apart from the most commonly used call-making and SMS sending/receiving functions, games are the top demand), and sales data from various online application stores (App Store, Android Market, etc.) (games and entertainment applications have always been the biggest winners), Wandoujia's direction is indeed in line with trends and has a broad market (about 40% of downloads in the Android Market come from Entertainment category applications).

However, it should be noted that the majority of current Android system users, excluding those who are curious about trying new things, are mostly tech enthusiasts, or at least loyal to the Google brand and willing to join its ranks. For such users, entertainment may only be one of their needs, but not the most urgent one (otherwise, for the same price, they could consider getting an iPhone which is better suited for entertainment or other home gaming terminals).
