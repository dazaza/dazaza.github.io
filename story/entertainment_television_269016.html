"Secret War" is not a pure espionage drama. More accurately, it should be considered a confidentiality-themed TV series. "Here, there is a comprehensive and in-depth display of the theft and counter-theft struggles in our country's contemporary society. It is also a confidentiality awareness education for all citizens."

"Secret War" centers around the operation to destroy the attempt by foreign intelligence organizations to steal data from a synchronous orbit satellite - Starcom Six - that China is developing. Foreign intelligence organizations approach and threaten scientific researchers, security personnel, and government officials related to the development of Starcom Six through various means such as infiltration, subversion, temptation, deception, and kidnapping, setting up layer upon layer of puzzles and traps to steal satellite test data. Our side, consisting of a joint investigation team made up of the Confidentiality Bureau, Security Bureau, and Public Security Bureau, engages in thrilling confrontations with the foreign intelligence organizations.

The use of modern high technology in espionage throughout the entire series will amaze the audience, such as stereoscopic real-time surveillance equipment, network tracking and monitoring, voice processing technology, mobile phone eavesdropping, and various high-tech espionage devices, making the audience feel the omnipresence and invisibility of modern espionage methods.

Plot Summary:

Captain Liu Zhijun of the Security Bureau Reconnaissance Team leads his team in executing the arrest of a spy codenamed "Leopard." Unexpectedly, he discovers that what the enemy intends to smuggle out of the country are the design plans for the "Starcom Six" satellite. The Confidentiality Bureau receives instructions from the central government to form a special case inspection team together with the Security Bureau and the Public Security Bureau, with Wu Mei, head of the Supervision Department of the Confidentiality Bureau, serving as the group leader. The inspection team conducts investigations on the satellite research and development institution No. 322 Institute but encounters numerous obstacles, achieving minimal results. Meanwhile, foreign espionage organizations launch attacks on the No. 322 Institute's network. With the help of Shen Weiguo and his son, the beleaguered inspection team successfully uncovers the spy Fang Yao hidden within the No. 322 Institute. Wu Mei originally intended to follow the trail to uncover the mastermind behind the spy organization, "Silver Fox," but instead falls into the trap set by "Silver Fox." Fang Yao is killed by the assassin "Butterfly," and the clue suddenly breaks off.

"Butterfly" escapes to Binyang, where Lin Xiaolian, a spy who has been lying low in Binyang for a long time, provides support. Lin Xiaolian's mission is to act against the Binyang Satellite Telemetry and Control Experimental Base Station 727, but the inspection team pursuing "Butterfly" disrupts his plans. Spy Liu Peng dies in a car accident during the chase, and in a hurry, Lin Xiaolian pulls Secretary Tong Yifan of the Municipal Party Committee into the mess. With the help of Tong Yifan, "Butterfly" enters Station 727 but is discovered by Qin Feng of the Binyang Security Bureau. In a life-and-death struggle, Qin Feng unfortunately sacrifices himself, and "Butterfly" is also killed. Unable to bear the guilt, Tong Yifan voluntarily confesses. Lin Xiaolian, about to leave the country, is brought to justice.

With their actions repeatedly thwarted, the spy organization has no choice but to make a desperate last attack on Aerospace Institute Nineteen. The security captain of Institute Nineteen, Luo Bing, becomes a tool exploited by the spies. Wu Mei sends Pang Jian undercover into Institute Nineteen to capture "Silver Fox," quietly starting a covert battle where you have me and I have you. After both sets of espionage plans by "Silver Fox" fail, he kidnaps Wu Mei's daughter Tingting, threatening Chief Engineer Zhou Xu to exchange core secrets for her daughter's life. Faced with the threat to national interests and her daughter's safety, Wu Mei steps forward to engage in the final confrontation with "Silver Fox"...
