Online worship exceeds cyber begging

by mcnishwddx on 2010-04-05 19:21:16

Online memorial services are a new type of commemoration method that has emerged in recent years. It leverages the spatiotemporal跨越 characteristics of the internet to "move" real-life memorials and cemeteries onto computers, allowing people to pay respects to their deceased loved ones anytime and anywhere. This method does not contradict traditional forms of worship but is rather an inheritance and extension of them.

The phrase "thinking of dear ones more during fine festivals" aptly describes how people always visit their ancestral graves to burn incense and sweep tombs during the Qingming Festival. In today's world of rapid material enjoyment and spiritual civilization development, many people, such as those who work away from home or go abroad, often find it difficult to fulfill their wish to honor their deceased relatives due to various reasons. By "inviting" ancestors online, even if one cannot physically be present at the cemetery, they can still enter the ancestor's virtual tomb anytime, recall their appearance and laughter, review their ordinary yet great lives, and re-experience their earnest teachings. One can also light a candle, offer flowers, and leave a message at the online tomb they have built for their loved ones... This provides some spiritual comfort. Currently, domestic websites offering such services include Global Memorial and Blessing Network, Netong Memorial Network, Endless Love Memorial Network, Root's Way Memorial Network, Paradise Online Memorial Hall, Huaxia Memorial Network, China Mausoleum Network, Tonghuai Network, Taiyuan Xianju, Si Huai, Yan Huang Memorial Network, etc. These websites all provide functions to establish online memorial halls or online cemeteries. Some websites eliminate the illusionary feeling of online memorials by adopting simulated rituals, further promoting online memorials. Among these, the interactive simulated online memorial platform created by the Global Memorial and Blessing Network fully simulates real life from establishing a cemetery to conducting the memorial service, bridging the gap between the internet and reality, making online memorials less illusory. This website also features unique functions like sending letters to heaven (with replies) and performing ceremonies for the deceased, which are gradually being loved and accepted by people as they originate from life yet transcend it.

"The drizzling rain falls like tears on the Mourning Day; the mourners' hearts are going to break on their way" depicts the scene of people busy sweeping tombs during the Qingming Festival each year. However, with the advent of fashionable online memorials through the internet, this scene will no longer exist. People now favor green and modern methods, pursuing a relaxed and perfect way of worship. You can complete the entire memorial process by simply clicking a mouse, giving you the feeling of being there in person. It also makes you realize the arrival of a whole new era of memorials.

The Ministry of Civil Affairs issued the "Notice on Doing Well in Relevant Work During the Qingming Festival Regarding Public Memorial Activities". The notice promotes online memorials and requires guiding the public to abandon outdated funeral practices and advocate civilized methods of worship during concentrated memorial activities around the Qingming Festival. It also promotes civilized memorial methods such as "family remembrance meetings" and "online memorials".

Online memorial services use the spatiotemporal characteristics of the internet to "move" real-life memorials and cemeteries onto computers, making it convenient for people to pay respects to their ancestors anytime and anywhere. It does not contradict traditional forms of worship but is an inheritance and extension of them.
