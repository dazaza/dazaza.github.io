4.15 Emergency Order! Respond to Interest Rate Hike with Offense as Defense

by huaxun892 on 2010-04-15 13:17:28

& nbsp, Stock Recommendations; & ldquo; Who said anything about raising interest rates? & rdquo; The statement by the Governor of the People's Bank of China, Zhou Xiaochuan, did not dispel the market's increasingly intense expectations of an interest rate hike. This morning, the National Bureau of Statistics will release the main economic data indicators for the first quarter of the national economy, with the CPI once again becoming a focal point of attention. From various forecasts, if the economic data shows signs of overheating in the economy, the interest rate hike "shoe" could drop. First, economists predict that the GDP growth rate for the first quarter is expected to reach as high as 11.6%, meaning that China's economic growth rate in the first quarter has rebounded to the highest level since the outbreak of the global financial crisis this time, which would boost inflation and further pressure the government to withdraw economic stimulus policies with greater force. Second, according to surveys by Dow Jones Newswires and Reuters, China's consumer price index in March is expected to rise by 2.6% year-on-year, slightly lower than the 2.7% increase in February. However, the figures for March will be among China's first monthly economic data for this year that have not been severely distorted by seasonal factors. The rise in refined oil prices and steel prices will further push up the CPI. Standard Chartered Bank estimates that China's overall inflation rate in May-June will exceed 7%, and the full-year consumer price increase will reach 6.5%. The super-expected economic growth and inflation rate have made the market worry that the central bank's timing for raising interest rates will be significantly advanced. Additionally, usually speaking, after adjusting the reserve requirement ratio two or three times, there will be one interest adjustment. In this round, the reserve requirement ratio has already been adjusted twice, so the expectation for an interest rate hike is quite strong. In fact, regarding the timing of the interest rate hike, there is still no consensus in the market, but "an interest rate hike in the second quarter" has become a consensus in the industry. Once the interest rate hike starts, it will be a significant shock for all sectors. From the statistical results, the reaction degree of various industries to the interest rate hike decreases sequentially from machinery manufacturing to agriculture, where industries with more capital-intensive operations are affected more than labor-intensive industries. As key industries for national macroeconomic regulation, such as building materials, cement, and real estate, are at the forefront of impact; industries supported by national policies, such as agriculture, transportation, and water-electricity production, are minimally affected by interest rate hikes; the impact on the machinery equipment manufacturing industry will be significant. However, for banks and insurance companies, interest rate hikes are seen as positive. Once the interest rate hike begins, it will increase the net interest margin and net interest spread of commercial banks. Banking stocks will benefit from the interest rate hike factor, thus leading to a further enhancement of sector valuations. However, banks are significantly affected by the real estate sector, so the impact of the interest rate hike on the banking sector is neutral. Choosing insurance stocks as an "offensive defense" investment target is a win-win situation. The "offensive" aspect is reflected in the upcoming launch of stock index futures, providing opportunities for large-cap blue-chip stocks to perform well, with insurance stocks as weight stocks expected to collectively rise. Secondly, insurance stocks are the best defensive varieties against interest rate hikes. In the short term, interest rate hikes directly affect bank deposit rates, and insurance companies' fixed-term deposits will effectively benefit from the rate increase, with the size of the benefit depending on the proportion of floating-rate deposits and the proportion of deposits maturing in the short term. Both China Life Insurance and Ping An Insurance Company's fixed-term deposit assets can timely benefit from the rate increase. Moreover, in the long term, bonds are the main investment assets for insurance companies, and their yield changes compared to the stock market are more stable. Therefore, the increase in bond investment yields is crucial for enhancing the overall yield of insurance funds. Consequently, the long-term yield levels of insurance companies show an upward trend.
