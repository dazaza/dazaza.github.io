The Wenchuan earthquake is unprecedented, with more than 70,000 people dead. The Chinese people's struggle against this great disaster has not yet ceased. The people in the disaster area are experiencing the pain of parting with the living and the dead. As all 1.3 billion Chinese people mourn together for the victims, South Koreans have revealed their true face. They can't wait to gloat over China's misfortune and shout out loud. When a Global Times reporter checked the comments left by netizens on South Korean websites, he couldn't help but feel a chill down his spine. Many netizens showed no sympathy for China's earthquake, instead gloating and making sarcastic remarks, which was heartrending. No normal human being would look at pictures of victims and secretly rejoice, or even utter extremely insulting words. This is an outrageous act that the Chinese people cannot accept or forgive! I believe there are good people in South Korea, as well as humane individuals, but I must say that humane South Koreans are in the minority. What is more often presented before the Chinese people is the indifference and even rude insults from most South Koreans. Misfortunes reveal the true character of people, and I think this is the real face of South Koreans.

There were Chinese students who went to South Korea for further studies and wrote articles expressing their disappointment with South Korea, while also being surprised at the hostility and distortion shown by most South Koreans towards the Chinese. Some friends also mentioned the arrogance and low-quality behavior of South Koreans in China. However, the Chinese are a tolerant and generous nation and did not dwell on these issues. They have always been friendly and热情towards South Koreans, giving them special care. For example, during the 2008 Spring Festival travel rush, China was hit by a severe snowstorm. The N589 passenger train traveling from Zhangjiajie to Guangzhou was stranded at the Changsha East Station on the Shi-Chang line for 24 hours due to the coal and snow impact. All 1600 people on board, including the crew, were without electricity, water, food, and were surrounded by extreme cold. Upon learning that there were 78 South Korean passengers on this train, the train attendants specially organized their transfer to the sleeper carriages.

China has an annual trade surplus of $10 billion with Japan, but a trade deficit of $20 billion with South Korea. In foreign trade, South Korea enjoys all the benefits. South Korean products and dramas are very popular in China, but South Korea openly bans the broadcasting of Chinese films and TV series, limiting the import of Chinese products. For these unequal treatments, the Chinese, in order to protect Sino-Korean friendship, did not count the cost. It can be said that the South Koreans have taken advantage of the Chinese tolerance to gain substantial political, economic, and cultural benefits in China. However, when China was in its most difficult time, the South Koreans mocked and gloated, showing such ingratitude, which is truly unreasonable!

Many South Koreans, upon hearing about the tens of thousands of deaths and millions of displaced people in China's Wenchuan earthquake, could not hide their joy and shouted out loud. Many people cannot understand why South Koreans are so cold-blooded. In fact, South Korea is an extremely narrow-minded nationalist country. Under the influence of hatred education and unique national history and culture psychology, the national character includes dual-split personalities of humility and extreme arrogance. Its people are narrow-minded, biased, arrogant, lacking in humanitarianism and tolerance. A clear example is when a South Korean student killed 32 teachers and students at Virginia Tech University in the United States in 2007. South Koreans, according to their own mindset, believed that the American public would retaliate against South Koreans in the U.S. Therefore, the entire South Korean nation was very panicked, and civilians and media continuously apologized. The government even held meetings to discuss countermeasures. However, what South Koreans never expected was that the American media and public called for people not to discriminate against Asians because of this incident. The tolerance of the American people was incomprehensible to South Koreans. South Koreans used their narrow-minded mentality to judge others, revealing their own narrow and ugly souls.

The narrow-mindedness and extreme nationalism of South Koreans were fully demonstrated in the recent Olympic torch relay. During the process of protecting the torch relay, some Chinese students exhibited radical actions. Although these behaviors were inappropriate, they triggered the sensitive nerves of South Korean extreme nationalism. South Korean Prime Minister Han Seung-soo stated at a state council meeting: "This incident has caused serious damage to the self-esteem of our citizens, therefore we will take legal and diplomatic measures to restore the dignity of our citizens." South Koreans, whose so-called "self-esteem" was "damaged," united to deal with Chinese students in South Korea. The media continuously incited hatred towards Chinese people. Chinese students encountered varying degrees of threats and insults in schools, workplaces, and farmland. Many South Koreans immediately frowned upon seeing Chinese people, as if their self-esteem had been "damaged." If the same incident happened in Japan, the Japanese government and public would not react so radically, forming a stark contrast.

South Korean extreme narrow-mindedness is also evident in daily life. A Beijing netizen pointed out that South Koreans living in Beijing prefer to consume in restaurants and stores owned by fellow countrymen, no matter how far away. The reason is very simple: they don't want to give money to the Chinese. Such extreme narrow-minded ethnic groups are quite rare in the world.

Today's South Korean mindset is very similar to that of pre-war Japanese. In recent years, South Koreans' extreme actions of cutting off their own fingers during anti-Japanese activities resemble the fanaticism of Japanese militarists back then! After World War II, Japan learned from the war lessons, abandoned militarist ideology, implemented democratic constitutional governance, respected human rights, and reshaped the national character. Today's Japanese public is no longer the same as the militarist fanatics of the past, and Japan's civil society development level is much higher than South Korea's. From the perspective of national character, today's most dangerous and aggressive people are precisely the South Koreans.

There is an ancient Chinese saying: "Good people learn from good people, bad people learn from bad people." Some Chinese often take South Koreans' extreme actions and radical thoughts in anti-Japanese activities as models to imitate, which is very harmful. Chinese people frequently see South Koreans' "boycott Japanese goods" grand gestures online, but Chinese visitors to South Korea are surprised to find that South Koreans do not actually boycott Japanese goods. According to the latest data provided by the Korea International Trade Association (KITA): In 2006, South Koreans imported $46.1 billion worth of "Made in Japan" goods. In the same year, South Korea only exported $21.7 billion worth of goods to Japan, less than half of the import amount. The trade deficit reached $24.4 billion.

Where does the money South Koreans spend on Japanese goods come from? If we look at it from the domestic trade balance sheet, it can be said that this money comes from the pockets of the Chinese. In 2006, South Korea exported $49.8 billion worth of goods to China, while importing only $29.6 billion worth of goods from China in the same year. The trade surplus with China was $20.2 billion—this figure almost exactly compensates for South Korea's trade deficit with Japan. To put it simply, South Koreans sell their goods to the Chinese and then use the money earned from the Chinese to buy Japanese goods. In fact, South Korea's so-called "boycott Japanese goods" is deliberately staged for the Chinese audience, aiming to pave the way for South Korean products in China. Many civilian organizations supported by the South Korean government hire a large number of Chinese-speaking netizens to praise and promote South Korea on Chinese-language networks, spreading negative information.

South Korea's narrow-minded and radical extreme nationalism has been despised by an increasing number of Chinese people. A great nation should possess a broad mind and a spirit of tolerance. China is a great power, and the Chinese nation is a great one. In the process of modernization, we should consciously resist narrow-minded and radical thinking and become a nation with a broad mind and fairness.
