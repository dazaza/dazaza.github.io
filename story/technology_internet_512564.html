III. Things to Note in Zhangjiajie City

On the train, many people will approach you to promote Zhangjiajie multi-day tours. At this point, it's best to ignore them. Once you ask a few questions, you'll be surrounded by overly enthusiastic people. When you get off the train, head straight to the large square on the left side of the exit.

There are public buses to the city center at the square. Buses 5 and 6 cost 1 yuan per person (2 yuan from 22:00-06:00), and both go to the city center. Bus 5 can drop you off at the bus station to transfer to a bus to the scenic area; bus 6 stops at the department store, then turn left and walk for two minutes to reach the west bus station.

Of course, you can also choose to take a taxi directly to the city center or the scenic area from the train station square. If you're staying in the city, the taxi fare is a flat rate of 10 yuan. If you're going directly to the scenic area, the fare is between 80 to 100 yuan, negotiable down to 60 yuan to the forest park with a reasonable chance. Of course, if the price is too low, don't take it. There's no such thing as free lunch!

Accommodation options include the Armed Department Hotel, Jian Yin Hotel, Min Nan Hotel (4-star), Bin Peng Hotel, Jin Du Hotel (bank operated, open to the public), and the Municipal Party Committee Guesthouse, etc. Prices are generally fair. You can negotiate prices around 160 to 220 yuan. Most three-star hotels can be booked for around 180 yuan. Two-star hotels range from 70 to 120 yuan.

IV. Eating and Playing in the City

Zhangjiajie city is not very large but has developed commerce. The larger and more comprehensive shopping places are Wan Jia Shopping Center and Mei Ni Supermarket. These are recommended. Mei Ni Shopping Center is spacious and clean, recommended. No matter which hotel you stay at, just take a minibus and tell the driver to go to Wan Jia Shopping Center, and it will only cost 1 yuan.

The first floor of Guang He is worth visiting. They sell Miao ethnic silver jewelry, reasonably priced and distinctive, usually non-negotiable, fair pricing. In the corner of the first floor, there are places selling specialty items, worth taking a look.

On the second floor, you can buy daily necessities, everything you need.

After Wan Jia, you will see a memorial tower on the opposite mountain. It's a free park, where you can enjoy the view of the city, recommended. The scenery on the mountain is nice, lush and green.

Another recommendation: Tujia Folklore Park, which has the world's tallest stilt house, highly recommended for its viewing value.

Also, the starting fare for taxis in Zhangjiajie is only 3 yuan, extremely cheap.

Not far from Wan Jia, there's a large People's Square. Across from the square is a temple called Pu Guang Temple, but the sign says Bai Yang Ancient Temple. The entrance fee is 20 yuan, with amazing sights inside, recommended.
