Vasculitis is also known as thromboangiitis obliterans, which is a periodic and segmental inflammatory condition affecting arteries and veins in young adults. The lesions primarily affect medium and small arteries in the limbs, with pathological features such as thickened vessel walls, reduced elasticity, and non-specific inflammation. Patients may experience numbness, pain, ulcers in the limbs due to vascular occlusion, limb ischemia, and infection, and may even face amputation due to gangrene.
