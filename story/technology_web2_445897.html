Interior decoration plastering work specifications

Basic requirements:

After the carpentry and plumbing work is completed and inspected as qualified, the site should be thoroughly cleaned before starting to install right-angle strips, paint walls, apply paint to avoid dust and powder affecting the construction quality.

The painted surface should be smooth and flat without any rough feel, no brush marks, wrinkles or missed areas.

* Specifications for latex paint construction: To be done after the main body is completed

Clean off stains, oil stains, and old paint from the wall. Level out the obviously protruding parts of the wall and fill in the obviously recessed areas before applying putty to make the wall as close to perfectly flat as possible.

Use a right-angle strip at the positive angles of the wall to ensure straight lines on the corners, prevent collision damage. Mix putty with white latex glue and material, and apply a 3-centimeter-wide slurry on both sides of the corner. Stick the right-angle strip onto the corner, align it vertically and horizontally, scrape off the excess slurry, and after it dries, you can normally apply putty. For the negative angles of the wall, it's best to use HT-Y1 type right-angle strips. The positioning groove not only facilitates quick and easy construction but also ensures very flat and straight lines on the negative angle, significantly improving efficiency.

At the cracks and joints of the wall, use LW-VP type joint tape from Right Angle Music. Although the price is relatively high, the special bonding method ensures maximum crack prevention protection and will not cause the common problem of joint tape curling.

If the original wall has been polished, it must first be sanded with sandpaper to make it rough before applying putty to prevent poor adhesion, which could cause bubbling.

Gypsum board screws on the ceiling must be driven in 0.5-1mm and treated with rust-proof paint. Wooden surfaces on the ceiling must be brushed with a coat of clear varnish before applying putty to prevent rust spots or yellowing.

The joints between gypsum boards or the gaps between gypsum boards and wooden surfaces must be filled with a mixture of latex glue and gypsum powder. After drying, cover with indeed cloth (right-angle music) and then apply putty.

When applying putty, it must not be too thick, otherwise, hollow drumming and cracking may occur. It must be layered and applied multiple times until the surface flatness is below 3mm-2mm.

After repeatedly performing the procedures of leveling with putty and sanding with sandpaper, achieve no shadows under strong side lighting (i.e., wall painting is qualified after being illuminated by a lamp). Then, have the construction supervisor confirm and approve before starting to paint the walls.

B) Applying latex paint

Before applying latex paint, the power switch socket panels, furniture edges, and ceiling edges must be sealed with non-drying adhesive paper tape (pay special attention: do not use transparent tape), and the powder layer on the wall must be swept clean with a clean broom.

Before applying latex paint, the color code of the latex paint should be calibrated to match the design requirements. Only then can the latex paint be applied. If the latex paint needs to be mixed on-site, it must be guided on-site by the designer or construction supervisor and approved by the owner before construction can proceed.

For large areas of latex paint, use a roller brush; for small areas and wall corners, use a hairbrush.

If there are two or more colors on the same wall, the boundary between the two colors should be clearly defined, with no mixed color staining. Spraying latex paint is costly.

By brushing twice in this manner, achieve a result where the paint film is thick and even, dust-free, and the color transition has clearly defined boundaries with a good feel.
