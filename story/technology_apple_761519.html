Not so fast! Apple's next big thing isn't hardware

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 17:01:06

According to a report on April 29 in Beijing Time, a commentary article was published on the well-known technology website BusinessInsider. The article claimed that Apple's next big product might not be hardware but software, and the upcoming Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC) would mark the true arrival of the Tim Cook era.The main content of the article is as follows:Apple's next major product may not be hardware like the iPad or iPhone, but rather completely new software.From June 10th to 14th, Apple will hold this year's Worldwide Developers Conference in Los Angeles. Thousands of developers will gather at the conference site to hear about the essence of Apple's latest software.Apple may not release a new iPhone or other hardware devices at this year's Worldwide Developers Conference, so you might think that this year's Worldwide Developers Conference is not very important. However, quite the contrary, this year's Worldwide Developers Conference is much more important than previous years!The products released at previous Worldwide Developers Conferences were all approved by Steve Jobs before his passing. This year will be different; it will be a developer conference without any Jobs elements, marking the true arrival of the Tim Cook era.Cook and his team will present and interpret their own vision for the future development prospects of technology at this year's Worldwide Developers Conference. We do not know what their final plan is for Apple's future hardware products, but we do know that they must upgrade Apple's mobile software and iCloud services.This year's Worldwide Developers Conference is where the Tim Cook era begins.By the time this year's Worldwide Developers Conference opens, Apple will have gone 230 days without releasing a new product. This is unprecedented in Apple's history.This year's Worldwide Developers Conference will provide Apple with an excellent opportunity to change direction. Apple can take the opportunity to announce its grand plans for the future development of iOS, its most important software. Apple's iPhone, iPad, and iPod touch all use the iOS software. These products contributed $32.6 billion in revenue to Apple last quarter, accounting for 75% of the company's total revenue! The revenue from these products is roughly equal to the combined revenue of Google and Microsoft last quarter.In these 230 days without new product releases, Apple's stock price has been in a downward trend, falling again and again without end.In fact, the decline in Apple's stock price is most closely related to its iPhone business. The iPhone business has been too dominant over the past few years, and the high-end iPhone market has approached or reached saturation. All that remains is a downhill path.Cook has served as Apple's CEO since August 2011. As the Jobs era gradually fades into the distance, Apple and its products will increasingly be guided by Cook.Last year, Cook quietly made adjustments within the company. Apple released a brand-new iMac, an iPad mini, the iPhone 5, a new iPod, and a new MacBook. At the same time, Cook also adjusted the structure of the company's management team.Cook dismissed Scott Forstall, who founded and led the iOS department, and replaced him with Jony Ive and Craig Federighi. Previously, Ive was Apple's chief hardware designer, but now he heads the Human Interface department, meaning he will oversee both software and hardware design work. Federighi previously managed Mac software and now leads both Mac and iOS software businesses.Eddy Cue, responsible for internet services, took over Siri and Maps.What changes will these executive adjustments bring to Apple? We'll see at this year's Worldwide Developers Conference.Ive is a famous hardware designer whose hardware designs are deeply loved by consumers, making Apple a globally popular brand. Ive's work achievements can be considered timeless art pieces. Ive has been granted a knighthood in the UK.Ive's designed products are known for their rounded and compact features. Apple's software design has always been rich in animation and illustration elements, which make Apple's software look very similar to the physical products it aims to replace.For example, last year, Apple released a new app called Passbook for saving digital tickets and some other retail apps. When users use Passbook digital tickets, the tickets slide out of Passbook just like sliding out of a paper cutter.This is just one example among many exquisite designs in the iOS system. Apple's Notes app looks like a real standard notebook. Its Game Center looks like a real gaming table.Industry insiders generally believe that under Ive's guidance, the iOS software is likely to abandon those animation and illustration elements. Ive's new design may reduce realism and become more minimalist.However, Ive has never done software design work before. Or if he did, it probably wasn't particularly outstanding; therefore, letting Ive lead software design indeed carries risks.Besides, beautiful appearance is only superficial and shallow. Improving the visual design of iOS can only solve half of Apple's problems.Even Apple's most enthusiastic supporters have started to complain about flaws in the most basic user interaction mechanisms of the iOS system. In the iOS system, some basic tasks such as turning on WiFi are not easy to accomplish. Anonymous design blogger Kontra wrote earlier this year: There are six projects in the iOS system (GPS, WiFi, cellular radio, Bluetooth, notifications, and display brightness) that consume a lot of power, and starting these projects is not easy, requiring multiple clicks in different places.Kontra also said that the already criticized Apple Maps service is not only inaccurate but also has bugs because it uses a very poor search engine.Then there's iCloud cloud storage service. While it's great for backing up iPhones, it's not very user-friendly for developers trying to synchronize data.Perceptive Apple blogger John Gruber stated that due to synchronization issues, iCloud is currently in a miserable state.It is reported that because of data synchronization problems, developers have consistently avoided using iCloud.We will see for the first time at this year's Worldwide Developers Conference how Ive adjusts the appearance design of iOS and how he and Federighi improve the user experience of iOS. We will also see how Eddy Cue makes iCloud better.In summary, at this year's Worldwide Developers Conference, we will see the first steps taken by Tim Cook since he assumed office, and these steps definitely won't be world-shocking hardware.
