"The price of 'Guo IV oil' may increase, and domestic oil prices might break through the major barrier of 8 yuan per liter. "

by jintaimqr on 2012-03-05 15:45:44

The Modern Express reported yesterday that, in less than half a month since the last price adjustment, domestic fuel prices are once again facing an increase in the near future.

On March 8, the pricing window for refined oil products will be open again.

If prices rise in the short term, together with the increased price of [National IV standard fuel], car owners are concerned that at that time prices might exceed the big barrier of 8 yuan per liter.

International crude oil prices have soared

According to the domestic refined oil pricing mechanism, two conditions need to be met for a price hike: firstly, the change rate of crude oil prices in three areas must exceed 4%, secondly, it must have been more than 22 working days since the last price adjustment.

In fact, from late February, international crude oil prices have continued to soar and the change rate of crude oil prices in three areas has already broken through the 4% price adjustment red line and has continued to rise in recent times.

The most recent domestic refined oil price increase was on February 8, so the condition of having passed 22 working days is not yet satisfied.

Industry experts predict that if the upward trend of international crude oil prices is not relieved, the condition of [22 working days] will be reached by March 8, which means that within a week the pricing window for domestic refined oil will be opened.

Car owners are worried about prices breaking through 8 yuan

This news makes many car owners feel somewhat [unable to cope].

Mr. Xue, a car owner, believes that the current price of 93-octane gasoline has already risen to 7.45 yuan per liter. If there is a price increase in the near term, according to previous price increases, the price of each liter of oil will increase by two or three mao. This way, the new price after another price increase could reach around 7.7 yuan per liter.

A new situation currently faced in Nanjing is: On April 1, there will be a full replacement with [National IV standard fuel], at which time there will also be [price increases].

According to notices from price control departments, taking 93-octane gasoline as an example, the price of each liter of National IV standard fuel needs to add an extra 0.32 yuan based on the latest oil price. If calculated this way, at that time the oil price might very likely break through the [barrier] of 8 yuan per liter.

Price increases may not happen so fast

However, regarding whether oil prices will be adjusted upwards in the near future, industry insiders generally believe it will not happen so quickly.

An analyst from the bulk commodities e-commerce platform JinYan Island believes that considering the large environment of high domestic prices, there is still some uncertainty as to whether the Development and Reform Commission will immediately adjust prices this time.

So what is the current situation in the domestic refined oil market?

According to information provided by the bulk commodities e-commerce platform JinYan Island, although there is still some time before the official opening of the pricing window, the atmosphere in the domestic refined oil market has already begun to be affected, with a mindset of holding out for higher prices emerging.

On February 29, the standard 93-octane gasoline traded by Sinopec and PetroChina reached around 9720 yuan/ton, whereas on February 1, its price was between 9250 to 9300 yuan/ton, rising by more than 400 yuan per ton.
