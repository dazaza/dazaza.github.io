Analysis: Apple's new iPad mini price is a "big risk"

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:29:33

Apple held a new product launch event today at the Yerba Buena Center for the Arts in San Francisco, comprehensively updating its tablet, desktop, and notebook product lines. They introduced five new hardware products: the iPad Air, Retina (high-resolution screen) version of the new iPad mini, new 13-inch/15-inch MacBook Pro Retina, and the new Mac Pro. Additionally, Apple released the official version of OS X Mavericks during the event and announced several free software plans, including the OS X system, iLife, and iWork. In response, the industry believes that what truly stood out and even surprised people about Apple's event today was the introduction of the iPad Air and the Retina version of the new iPad mini.

With the unveiling of the new products, comments from the industry began to flood in. The American blog site Business Insider wrote an article stating that Apple setting a high price for the Retina version of the new iPad mini is indeed a "big risk."

Apple launched two main versions of the iPad tablet this time: one is the larger iPad tablet renamed as the iPad Air; the other is the iPad mini tablet equipped with a Retina display, priced at a high $399. In fact, Apple increased the price of the new version of the iPad mini, which is also the first time Apple has raised the price of an iOS device.

In 2012, when the iPad mini was initially launched, it was priced at only $329. The industry previously believed that the price of the new version of the iPad mini that Apple would release today should still remain at $329. In response, the industry also believes that this move is a bit strange and will be a significant high-risk for Apple.

In reality, the current tablet market is facing increasing competition due to low-priced devices from Amazon and Google. The 7-inch Amazon Kindle HDX tablet is priced at only $229, and Google's Nexus 7 is also priced at just $229. There is no doubt that these two tablets are inferior to the Retina version of the new iPad mini, as the Retina version of the new iPad mini features a larger display and a richer ecosystem, especially with over 450,000 applications. However, the operating systems of Amazon and Google are not particularly bad either. With the upcoming holiday sales season, many users may opt to buy cost-effective tablets from Google and Amazon instead of the Retina version of the new iPad mini.

For Apple, the company may lose users who favor Google and Amazon products. Moreover, users upgrade their tablets much more slowly than they do PCs, and not as quickly as smartphones, meaning these users might only buy new tablets after 3 to 4 years, and perhaps when they purchase again, they will continue to choose Android-based tablets.

Of course, the iPad business is very popular, selling millions of units each quarter, but the situation seems to have worsened recently. In the second quarter of this year, iPad sales declined by 14% year-over-year, and the industry predicts that sales in the third quarter may decline further. Therefore, how the higher-priced new version of the iPad mini will win the market is hard to imagine. Additionally, the non-Retina version of the iPad mini is still priced at a high $299, but these products face significant challenges under the pressure of lower-priced Android tablets.

It can be imagined that once the Retina version of the iPad mini hits the market, there will still be a large number of users lining up to purchase it, but the number of buyers will certainly drop significantly. If Apple had priced this device at $329, the outlook might have been much more optimistic.

Of course, Apple has its reasons for raising the price of the Retina version of the iPad mini, namely that this device has a larger display. But remember, Apple originally priced the iPad mini at only $329, so compared this way, it may not be a good result for Apple.
