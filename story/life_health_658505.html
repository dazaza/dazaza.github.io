Toothache: How to Relieve Pain

Author: Toothache Source: Internet Time: August 3, 2011 22:57:10 Clicks: 2437

Toothache is really uncomfortable. How can we quickly relieve tooth pain?

I. Treatment for toothache should be based on different causes:

1. For residual roots, residual crowns, and diseased teeth that cannot be retained, they should be extracted as soon as possible, and a false tooth can be installed after two months.

2. Food impaction pain: Use dental floss to remove the food debris causing the impaction.

3. Acute pulpitis and acute periapical periodontitis: Perform pulpotomy and drainage. Placing a cotton ball soaked in camphorated phenol or clove oil in the cavity can relieve pain.

4. Acute periodontitis and periodontal abscess: When the abscess has not formed, clean the dental plaque, take periodontal Ning, antibiotics, and apply iodine glycerin externally; if the abscess has formed, incise and drain it while using antibiotics to control the infection.

5. Dentin hypersensitivity: Avoid brushing horizontally and avoid forceful movements. Brush vertically. Use anti-acid, desensitizing toothpaste or brush with a 10% sodium bicarbonate glycerin mixture for certain therapeutic effects. Alternatively, go to the hospital for desensitization treatment.

II. Home remedies for toothache relief:

1. Saltwater rinse: Take one spoon of salt and half a cup of boiling water, stir until the salt dissolves. After cooling, rinse frequently to alleviate the immediate pain. Saltwater not only clears food residue from the mouth but also kills bacteria and detoxifies.

2. Apply a cold, wet towel or ice pack/ice cube to the cheek where the toothache occurs for 15 minutes at a time, repeating 3-4 times a day, which can help relieve the pain.

3. Press or pinch the Hegu acupoint (the intersection of the thumb bone and index finger bone) with your fingernail or fingertip to reduce or eliminate toothache. If the left side hurts, press the right Hegu point, and vice versa.

4. Sichuan pepper oil drop: Fry 10 grams of Sichuan pepper in 100 milliliters of sesame oil until charred. Remove the Sichuan pepper and let it cool before dropping into the cavity to quickly stop toothache.

5. Garlic paste filling: Crush fresh garlic heads into a paste. Place a small amount into the cavity to stop cavities-induced toothache.

6. Take 9g of Sichuan pepper, 6g each of camphor and evodia rutaecarpa, add 200ml of water, boil and extract the juice. Use a sterilized cotton swab to dab the medicine on the affected area, and place a cotton ball soaked in the medicine between the upper and lower teeth, biting down tightly. Generally, the pain will subside within 15-30 minutes, and after 3-5 treatments, the pain can be relieved.

7. Soak an appropriate amount of high-concentration liquor or 95% alcohol in a cotton ball and insert it into the external auditory canal, ensuring close contact with the surrounding skin. If the cotton ball dries, add more liquor. Generally, only the affected ear is used, but both ears can be used if both sides hurt. The pain will stop within 3-5 minutes.

8. Massage therapy: Locate the Jianjing acupoint (located in the depression on the shoulder). Press this point on both sides until you find the exact location. Press hard enough to endure, pressing for 30 seconds, then release and repeat until the toothache subsides. Generally, after 1-3 minutes, the pain will disappear. When the toothache prevents sleep, these simple tricks can often work wonders.

9. Collect dried ripe cedar seeds. When toothache is caused by excessive stomach fire, oral ulcers, or sensitivity to sour, sweet, or spicy foods, soak an appropriate amount of cedar seeds in vinegar. Bite the soaked cedar seeds onto the painful tooth after 10 minutes to treat the toothache.

10. Finger pressure therapy: For upper teeth pain, press the "Yingxiang" acupoint. For lower teeth pain, cross your fingers, place your thumb along the radial extension of the middle finger of the upper hand, locate the tender spot, exhale while pressing for 10 seconds, and repeat 20 times.

11. Take an appropriate amount of bee hive, add pure alcohol, light a fire, and burn until the bee hive turns to black ash. Dip your finger in the ash and apply it to the affected tooth. Generally, the pain will stop within 4-5 minutes.

12. Take a small amount of raw pig fat and new cotton, wrap the pig fat in the cotton and heat it, bite down on the painful tooth for a moment, replace once per session, and repeat several times.

13. Take 1-2 pills of Liu Shen Wan, crush them and place them on the gums of the affected tooth for 5-10 minutes, once daily, generally not exceeding 3 times.

14. Put 100 grams of liquor in a cup, add 10 grams of salt, stir until dissolved, and bring to a boil. Hold a mouthful over the painful area (do not swallow), and the toothache will immediately stop.

15. Chew a clove of carnation flowers until crushed, fill the cavity, and the toothache will disappear within hours, and it can last for a longer time without recurring (carnation flowers can be purchased at Chinese pharmacies).

16. Toothache (neurogenic, allergic, or cavity-related): Soak 10 peppercorns in one liang of liquor for ten minutes, hold a mouthful on the painful area, and the effect will be felt within minutes. Do this twice daily for 10 minutes each time, and the condition will heal within 3-4 days.

17. Periodontitis, gingivitis: Mix the white of one egg with an equal amount of liquor, drink a mouthful, hold it in your mouth for 5 minutes, then spit it out. Repeat twice daily (one egg per day), and the inflammation and pain will subside within 2-3 days.

18. Bleeding gums (frequent bleeding or caused by brushing): Soak 10 peppercorns in three liang of vinegar for 2 days, then hold it in your mouth for 3 minutes at a time, twice daily, for five consecutive days for excellent results.

19. Acupuncture therapy: Locate the toothache point (on the palm side, between the 3rd and 4th metacarpal bones, 3 mm proximal to the transverse palmar crease). Insert a 3 cm needle quickly, about 1.5 cm deep, rotate and lift extensively while inhaling deeply. Leave the needle in for 5 minutes when a strong soreness is felt. For right-side toothache, needle the left side, and vice versa.

20. Herb decoction: Boil 30 grams each of Plantago asiatica, Houttuynia cordata, Zanthoxylum simulans, Cynanchum wilfordii, 50 grams of gypsum, and 5.5 grams each of licorice and chrysanthemum in a sand pot. Drinking this decoction can ensure long-term relief from toothaches.

23. Fresh Acorus tatarinowii 30 grams, pound it into juice, drop 3 drops into the affected ear hole, repeat several times a day until the pain stops.

24. Boil 6 grams each of Sichuan pepper and bee hive, with a little salt, rinse with the cooled solution, spit it out, and repeat as needed. Crush an appropriate amount of garlic, warm it, and apply it to the painful area to relieve pulpitis, periodontitis, and toothache.
