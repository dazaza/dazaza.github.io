China News Service, Beijing, June 23 (reported by Zhou Zhaojun and Zhang Weiran) - Auditor General of China’s National Audit Office Liu Jiayi presented a report on the audit of the implementation of the central budget for 2009 and other fiscal revenue and expenditure to the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on June 23. He disclosed that as of May 2009, in the construction contracts worth over 3.6 billion yuan signed for the western section of the West-to-East Natural Gas Transmission Line II project, nearly 80% were not tendered according to regulations. Among these, contracts worth 1.01 billion yuan were directly subcontracted in violation of rules, and there were issues such as artificial division of bid segments and irregular determination of bidders in the bidding process for contracts worth 2.7 billion yuan.

Liu Jiayi indicated that last year, the National Audit Office increased its efforts in auditing government investment projects and focused on organizing follow-up audits and investigations on key projects such as the Beijing-Shanghai High-Speed Railway, the western section of the West-to-East Natural Gas Transmission Line II project, the Xiangjiaba Hydropower Station on the Jinsha River, and 1,981 investment projects aimed at boosting domestic demand.

Problems found during the audit included: non-strict management of bidding and tendering processes, relatively common issues with unauthorized subcontracting; insufficiently standardized investment management for some projects; incomplete implementation of land and environmental protection policies.

In discussing the audit situation of financial institutions, Liu Jiayi puma numostro mentioned that through tracking audits of the structure and direction of new loans in 2009 by the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, the Construction Bank of China, and the Bank of China, it was discovered that the structure of some new loans was not entirely reasonable. The main issues were high proportions of medium- and long-term loans, rapid growth in real estate loan businesses, insufficient credit support for agriculture and small enterprises, and some loans being directed in ways that did not comply with regulations.

Furthermore, Liu Jiayi also pointed out that some local governments faced significant pressure in repaying debts, posing certain debt risks. Judging from the ratio of debt balances to available financial resources for the year, debt risks were more concentrated at provincial and municipal levels and in western regions. Seven provinces, ten cities, and fourteen counties exceeded 100%, with the highest reaching 364.77%. From the perspective of debt repayment sources, in 2009, these regions repaid 274.546 billion yuan of debt principal and interest through borrowing new debts, accounting for 47.97% of their total debt repayment amounts, indicating insufficient fiscal capacity for debt repayment.

Liu Jiayi stated that after discovering the aforementioned problems, relevant departments and localities had already formulated corrective measures.
