"Emerging" career observation: Professional tutoring

by edurqusr on 2012-02-28 15:12:08

Speaking of [private tutoring], it is not unfamiliar to everyone. In people's traditional concept, doing private tutoring is a means of work-study and a part-time job for making money. Is there anyone who does private tutoring full-time? In the past, probably no one would believe it.

However, with the development of society and increasing competition, parents who expect their children to become successful have hired private tutors and enrolled their children in tutorial classes, increasing the demand for private tutoring, which has then become a career that can be done full-time. On the other hand, with the increasing difficulty of college students finding employment, some graduates have joined educational tutoring institutions and become professional private tutors. Thus, [private tutoring] has continued to develop, becoming more professional and specialized.

Professional Private Tutors in Listed Companies

On October 21, 2010, Xueersi Education successfully listed on the New York Stock Exchange, with its stock price surging by 50% on the first day. The founder of this institution was originally a part-time private tutor during his university days, subsequently establishing a private tutoring company and gradually developing it into a listed company within less than seven years. Meanwhile, several other larger basic education tutoring institutions in the country are also eager to list overseas.

The employees working in these institutions are exactly what we call [professional private tutors]. They either rush around giving home tutoring or teach small classes within the institution. There are teachers who joined right after graduation, experienced teachers, and also students working part-time.

I learned that professional private tutors earn substantial incomes. Currently, in Beijing, a bachelor's degree graduate who becomes a professional private tutor can earn 3,000-4,000 yuan in their first month, higher than the average starting salary. Their income includes basic salary + hourly fee sharing. For example, if a parent chooses a tutoring institution and signs up for 10 lessons, with an average hourly fee of 200 yuan (average price for Beijing college entrance exam tutoring), the tutor can earn dozens of yuan per lesson. If calculated at 50 yuan per hour, working 8 hours a day, with 20 working days a month, there could be an income of 8,000 yuan.

Just in Beijing, there are over thousands of private tutoring institutions, with over ten thousand private tutoring professionals. Tutors within these institutions enjoy insurance, housing fund, training, expansion, and other benefits similar to employees in other industries, indicating a high level of professionalism.

Professional Private Tutoring Teachers

Another aspect reflecting the professional development of private tutoring is that the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security's China Employment Training Technology Guidance Center has approved a new vocational training project - Private Tutoring Teacher. In September 2010, the training for private tutoring teachers was first launched in Beijing. By the end of the year, China's first batch of private tutoring teachers will receive their certificates and officially start work.

According to the introduction, the main target group for private tutoring teacher training are current and graduated college students, as well as other individuals interested in private tutoring. Currently, the training fees for current college students are around hundreds of yuan, while the training fees for other groups are about thousands of yuan. After the training, participants will receive certificates issued by the labor department.

However, opinions on this matter vary. I interviewed some college student tutors on Ivy League (http://www.jiajiao400.com[http://www.educqt.com/]), and many students expressed that they are not very familiar with this and are not very interested. Some students mentioned that the school's career guidance center has already issued [tutoring certificates] to students doing part-time tutoring as proof. The parents of the students they teach mainly value the "companion learning" aspect of university students. Some professional tutors indicated that their institutions require them to have teaching qualification certificates, which are considered more authoritative and universal. Some private tutors welcome this change. Working with certification makes the process more formal, enhancing trust and serving as a proof of ability! This is very common on the Ivy League Nanjing tutoring website. Many Nanjing tutors upload their teaching qualification certificates to the website and obtain certification marks. Mission of Professional Private Tutors

Under the requirements of parents and institutions, improving students' scores in certain subjects might be the first priority for professional private tutors. However, if the only purpose is to teach children how to solve problems and improve their scores, it probably won't bring long-term development to this industry. [http://www.jiajiao400.com/]

In May 2010, the State Council reviewed and approved the "National Medium- and Long-Term Education Reform and Development Plan". Among the ten reform pilots clearly outlined in the plan, promoting quality education reform and balanced development reform mainly focus on basic education. The current situation of [determining one's life by scores] will inevitably undergo changes.

I believe that promoting students' academic improvement should be the result and phenomenon of professional private tutoring, rather than the goal. The advantage of professional private tutoring lies in the ability to provide one-on-one guidance to students. By eliminating the traditional teacher-student relationship, tutors can communicate more amicably and understand the students' personalities and strengths and weaknesses in learning more thoroughly. Thus, they can provide more targeted guidance, increase students' interest in learning, and play an enlightening role in the students' character, moral values, learning ideas, and methods! This improves the students' academic performance.

Today, we have already witnessed the vigorous development of the basic education tutoring industry in our country, resulting in the emergence of more and more professional private tutors. Home tutors, like traditional school teachers, bear the responsibility of educating and nurturing, and are equally respected by society! A teacher transmits knowledge, instructs in skills, and dispels doubts! We thank more and more teachers, whether at home or in schools, for diligently inspiring our children and the future of our country!
