A scientific diet is important at any time, especially after working out.

by ciaifuke on 2012-02-28 17:30:13

Many people often experience muscle swelling, joint pain, and fatigue after intense physical exercise (fitness products, fitness information) or sports activities (sports products, sports information). To quickly relieve the fatigue, they buy chicken, fish, meat, eggs, and other foods to eat in large quantities, thinking that this will replenish their nutrition and meet the body's needs. In reality, consuming these foods at this time not only fails to alleviate fatigue but can also have adverse effects on the body.

Human food can be divided into acidic foods and alkaline foods. Acidic foods typically contain abundant proteins (protein supplements), fats, and carbohydrates, with more acid-forming elements, which form acidic substances after metabolism within the body, lowering the pH levels of blood and the body. Vegetables and fruits, on the other hand, contain elements such as potassium (K), sodium (Na), calcium (Ca), and magnesium (Mg), which generate alkaline substances after metabolism, preventing the blood from becoming overly acidic. Therefore, even though some fruits taste acidic, they are considered alkaline foods rather than acidic ones. Foods like chicken, fish, meat, eggs, and sugar may not taste acidic but are classified as acidic foods.

A British pathologist, after extensive research, pointed out that only when the body fluids remain slightly alkaline can human health be maintained. After engaging in physical exercise, people often feel muscle and joint soreness and mental fatigue. The primary reason for this is the substantial breakdown of sugars, fats, and proteins within the body, producing acidic substances such as lactic acid and phosphoric acid during the process. These acidic substances stimulate bodily tissues and organs, causing sensations of muscle and joint soreness and mental fatigue. At this time, if one consumes only meat, eggs, and fish rich in acidic substances, it will further acidify the body fluids, hindering the relief of fatigue. Instead, eating vegetables, sweet potatoes, citrus fruits, apples, and similar fruits can eliminate excess general acids from the body, reduce the acidity of urine, increase the solubility of uric acid, and decrease the likelihood of acid forming stones in the bladder.

Therefore, after physical exercise, one should consume more alkaline foods such as fruits, vegetables, and bean products to maintain a basic balance of acidity and alkalinity within the body, preserve overall health, and quickly alleviate fatigue caused by exercise.
