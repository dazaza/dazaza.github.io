Since the announcement of the nomination list for the 30th Hong Kong Film Awards, most people's attention has been focused on the candidates for Best Actor. Chow Yun-fat, Tony Leung Ka Fai, Jacky Cheung, Nicholas Tse, and Nick Cheung - these five nominees are truly the best representatives of three generations of male actors in Hong Kong cinema. However, despite the presence of these esteemed predecessors, the one receiving the highest acclaim is actually the youngest among them, Nicholas Tse. Both Wong Jing and Man隽 see him as a likely winner for the Best Actor award. This is also Nicholas Tse's first nomination for the Best Actor at the Hong Kong Film Awards. Previously, he won the Best New Actor Award in 1998 for his role in "Young and Dangerous" and the Best Supporting Actor Award in 2010 for his role in "Bodyguards and Assassins". From winning the Best New Actor Award to his first nomination for Best Actor, Nicholas Tse has spent over a decade honing his craft.

As the Hong Kong Film Awards approach,

Wong Jing and Man隽 believe Nicholas Tse will win the Best Actor Award.

Nick Cheung and Nicholas Tse were both nominated for Best Actor this year for their outstanding performances in "Eyes Wide Open". Since "Witness", they have gradually become a top-notch police-criminal duo. After "Eyes Wide Open", they both joined "The Inn of the God of Wealth" together as police officers. Facing these two inseparable good partners, director Wong Jing cast his vote for Nicholas Tse: Nick Cheung already won several Best Actor awards a few years ago, it's time for Nicholas Tse to be crowned. In "Eyes Wide Open", not only did he work hard physically, but his acting skills have improved significantly since "Witness" and "Bodyguards and Assassins". I think Chow Yun-fat and the others would agree with me too. Additionally, former chairman of the Hong Kong Film Awards and renowned filmmaker Man隽 also sees Nicholas Tse as the likely winner. He said: In fact, the character in "Eyes Wide Open" reminds us of Chow Yun-fat's performance in "A Better Tomorrow". Back then, Chow Yun-fat won the award, so why wouldn't it be Nicholas Tse's turn?

"The Inn of the God of Wealth" is set to premiere during the summer.

Nicholas Tse and Nick Cheung will perform a cat-and-mouse game.

Not just Wong Jing and Man隽, but many other industry insiders and outsiders are also optimistic about Nicholas Tse's chances. In discussions about who will win this year's Hong Kong Film Awards, Nicholas Tse's name is repeatedly mentioned. Passing the torch is a traditional spirit of Hong Kong cinema, and among young male actors, Nicholas Tse's efforts stand out. An actor willing to shed his idol status and work hard on set for his family deserves respect.

The dual nominations of Nicholas Tse and Nick Cheung have added luster to the upcoming release of "The Inn of the God of Wealth". In the film, they will leave behind the heavy emotional burdens of "Witness" and "Eyes Wide Open" and humorously portray their roles as yamen constables in ancient times. Besides the basic elements of Hong Kong fashion comedies, the film integrates detective, suspense, and treasure-hunting content. What audiences look forward to the most is the continued success of this top-notch duo.

It is reported that as one of the key blockbusters of Bona Film Group in 2011, "The Inn of the God of Wealth" will premiere during the summer. At that time, audiences can enjoy the Oscar-worthy performances of Nicholas Tse and Nick Cheung in theaters.
