Inter's attacking data dominates 2009 Serie A; golden trio aims to become the strongest forward line in a decade.

Sina Sports reports: The visiting Inter Milan drew 1-1 with Atalanta, and central defender Lucio became the scapegoat for the team being equalized.

In this match, Mourinho fielded Lucio and Cordoba as the central defensive duo. In fact, Lucio's performance in the first 80 minutes was commendable. In the 6th minute, he headed a corner kick but narrowly missed the target. In the 18th minute, when Atalanta's forward Pinto shot from outside the box, Lucio bravely blocked the shot, getting slightly injured in the process. In the 26th minute, after Sneijder lost possession in the backfield, Atalanta organized an attack, but Lucio jumped high in the middle to clear the cross.

In the second half, Lucio remained active at both ends of the pitch. In the 53rd minute, during an Inter free-kick, Lucio headed Sneijder's delivery into the goal, though it was ruled offside. On the defensive side, Lucio made accurate interceptions in the 60th minute and crucial clearances in the 78th and 80th minutes.

However, all of Lucio's efforts were erased by the conceded goal in the 81st minute. At that time, Atalanta sent a long pass from the left flank, and Lucio misjudged the situation near the penalty area, failing to reach the ball with his outstretched leg. Substitute forward Thierry Bokyi easily scored after receiving the pass. As a result, Inter missed the chance to widen their lead over AC Milan and Juventus in the standings.

Had it not been for this mistake, Lucio's performance in this game would have been nearly perfect: nine clearances and four tackles, which fully matched his status as captain of the Brazilian national team. However, due to this error, Lucio became the culprit for Inter. Describing this mistake as a "disaster" would not be an exaggeration. After the game, on Inter's official website scoring system, Lucio only received an average score of 4.52, setting a new low since joining Inter.

Actually, if we look back at Lucio's performances for Inter, we can see that his strengths and weaknesses are quite prominent. Lucio's strength lies in his aggressive style and precise positioning. When he performs well, he can certainly become a reliable barrier in front of Inter's goal. Overall, his arrival has indeed enhanced Inter Milan's strength. But at the same time, Lucio sometimes handles the ball rashly at certain moments, and some low-level mistakes may occur.

Lucio has participated in 15 league matches for Inter so far. Some of these mistakes are still fresh in people's memories. In the opening round's 1-1 draw against Bari at home, one of Lucio's low-level mistakes prevented the blue-black team from achieving a successful start for the third consecutive year; in the Champions League home match against Dynamo Kyiv, Lucio's clearance once again mistakenly went into his own net... In this match, had it not been for Lucio's mistake, Inter could have possibly entered the last round of the league this year with a seven-point lead.

In fact, just a few days ago, Roberto Mancini, Inter's former coach, pointed out in an interview that if he led the current Inter team to the Champions League final, then among his 11-player lineup, the defenders would be "Julio Cesar, Maicon, Samuel, Cordoba, and Chivu." Notably, there is no mention of Lucio's name here. And Lucio is the only player in this blue-black team whom Mancini would not include in his starting lineup. For a center-back, maintaining stability and avoiding mistakes is too important. It is noteworthy that after Inter conceded in this match, Mourinho, who was sitting in the stands, suddenly became expressionless. Perhaps, only Lucio himself knows what the coach is thinking. (By Bicheduo)

Reported by journalist Chen Jingjing: At the beginning of the season, Mourinho complained that Inter's forwards had quantity but lacked quality. But after more than three months, the Portuguese man's view should have changed.

The numbers best illustrate this point: in the last 20 league matches, Mourinho's team only failed to score in one game, which was the 0-1 loss against Sampdoria at the Marassi Stadium on September 26. In the first 16 rounds of this season, Inter scored in 15 games. And in these 15 encounters, there were only two instances where neither Eto'o, Milito, nor Balotelli appeared on the scorer's list. One was against Udinese (the scorers were Stankovic and Sneijder), and the other was against Catania (the scorers were Montari and Sneijder).

In the midweek Champions League, it was Balotelli and Eto'o who stepped up. In Bergamo, it was Milito's turn, who started in the last six consecutive matches. He broke free from the clutches of two defenders at the start and scored the 199th goal in Inter's history against Atalanta.

In the first 16 rounds of the league this season, Inter scored a total of 35 goals, averaging over 2 goals per game, making them the team with the strongest attacking power in Serie A. Among them, the three main forwards scored a total of 21 goals, exceeding one-fourth of Inter's total number of goals in 2009. And the total of 74 goals also makes the blue-black army very likely to become the team with the strongest attacking power in Serie A in 2009 (Roma, who scored 68 goals, is second).

Although Inter regrettably missed the best opportunity to expand their lead in points, the team's offensive strength has already been fully demonstrated in this season's matches. The combination of Eto'o, Balotelli, and Milito also has great potential to become the strongest attacking triangle in Inter Milan's last 10 seasons.
