Busan Film Festival: Star Fashion Coordination PK

by donttte67 on 2009-11-25 20:24:42

The red carpet at the Busan Film Festival is like a pre-rehearsed play, with female celebrities' dresses all either black or white - truly classic and elegant. But at first glance, it's like Zhang Yimou's Olympic collective dance. The only bit of flexible freedom in choice is whether to show the chest or the back - of course, showing both would be better. Adding a bit of color might improve it.

**Gao Joon-hee**: Her stiff silk dress emphasizes a cold beauty. The deep V-line, although having an obvious cutting effect, is very obedient and well-fitted. Outstanding tailoring, with loose decorative structures on the back that highlight soft feminine curves.

**Ke Eun-ya**: A high-necked shoulder-baring plain black dress is a great test of one's figure and a rather confident choice. It particularly suits those with standard hanger-like collarbones. The styling focus must be on neat and noble coiled hair.

**Hong Eun-ya**: Such an ultra-beautiful back display is already quite explosive and eye-catching in conservative Busan, making Busan's mediocrity and conservatism seem somewhat hypocritical. In fact, everyone wants to play the role of a sexy temptress.

**Yoo In-young**: The lace waist structure of her dress adds nothing but layers to the gown. The exaggerated U-shaped neckline has no use for her A-cup modest curve, resulting in a rather mediocre black dress look.

The Tokyo Film Festival’s green carpet was filled with excitement. The Tokyo Film Festival specially set up a Chinese Film Week, where 8 Chinese-language films such as "Forever and Ever" and "Taipei Snowfall" were screened. This also allowed top Chinese stars like Fan Bingbing, Zhao Wei, and Chen Kun to make large-scale appearances, making the usually environmentally themed green carpet of the Tokyo Film Festival lively and highly anticipated.

**Simple Beauty**: The dress of Simple Beauty is indeed simple. Regrettably, there's no beauty visible. The chest design lacks technical content and looks much like a strapless white summer dress from a roadside shop, just with a more extravagant skirt.

**Min Hyo-rin**: Since you've chosen the pure image of long hair falling over your shoulders, why not also match it with an equally innocent smile? A face full of arrogance and severity, along with a pursuit of nobility but poorly executed dress, makes people uncomfortable.
