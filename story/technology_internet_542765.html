Two women in Nanjing were pinned down by an armored car - more than 30 citizens worked together to lift the car and rescue them

by iface011 on 2011-11-09 10:36:27

Just as everyone was waiting for the support of firemen, the situation of the woman stuck under the car became worse and worse. In order to save time, more than 30 passers-by worked together, shouting "one, two, three" and lifted up the 2.3-ton armored car, AiRong.com.

When the reporter arrived at the scene after receiving a tip-off from a reader, it was found that many passers-by had already gathered around. The traffic in nearby sections was jammed with long lines of vehicles. A "JinDun Guard" armored car was parked beside the southwest flower bed near the crossroads of Zhongshan South Road. Several security escorts appeared at a loss.

Some witnesses said that the accident happened when the armored car drove from BaiXia Road, then turned left at the intersection of Zhongshan South Road and drove onto Zhongshan South Road. At that time, the two women who were hit were crossing the zebra crossing from west to east on the south side of the crossroads. Everyone did not pay much attention at that moment, but after two screams, two women pedestrians were seen being hit. Strangely, after hitting people, the driver of the armored car continued driving forward about 10 meters as if nothing had happened. The eyewitnesses said that the armored car was too fast, so after knocking down the pedestrians, it didn't even notice and dragged them nearly 10 meters.

After the incident, the reporter contacted the office director of Nanjing JinDun Escort Guard Company, who said, "We have already delivered a check for 100,000 yuan in cash to the hospital." On the afternoon of the previous day, Captain Xu of the Traffic Police Detachment No. 2's Accident Handling Group told the reporter that currently the injured people had safely passed the dangerous period and the cause of the accident is still under investigation. (Text and pictures provided by Yangtze Evening News)

At the left front wheel of the armored car, a middle-aged woman in black lay on the road with blood flowing from her nose and mouth, unable to speak. Another middle-aged woman wearing a pink top was trapped under the fuel tank of the armored car. Due to the narrow space under the fuel tank, her legs were stretched straight, her face pressed tightly against her legs, making the scene extremely perilous. Dozens of citizens were unable to pull her out from under the car, and the medical personnel who arrived at the scene were also at a loss.

In Nanjing, two women were run over by an armored car. More than 30 citizens worked together to lift the car to rescue them. One woman was crushed under the wheels. Another woman was stuck at the fuel tank area, unable to move.

According to the attending doctor, both injured women were middle-aged. The woman in the pink top was trapped under the car, with four broken ribs and fractures in other parts of her body. She also developed a pneumothorax problem, causing relatively difficult breathing. The woman trapped under the left front wheel may have been hit directly in the face, resulting in bleeding from her nose and mouth. When her head hit the ground, she suffered from skull fracture and brain hemorrhage. After expert consultation, the severely injured woman in black must undergo surgery immediately.

Both women involved in the accident sustained serious injuries.

It was reported that at around 8:20 AM the day before yesterday, at the intersection of BaiXia Road and Zhongshan South Road in Nanjing City, a "JinDun Guard" armored car turning left from BaiXia Road onto Zhongshan South Road collided with two women on the pedestrian crossing. One of the women fell under the left front wheel of the armored car, with blood flowing from her nose and mouth, and her pants torn in multiple places. The other woman wearing a pink top was trapped under the fuel tank of the armored car. Due to the narrow space under the car, the woman was pressed into a crouching position on the ground, screaming in pain continuously.

At the accident scene, medical personnel quickly sent the woman in black to the ambulance, but how to remove the woman trapped under the fuel tank posed a great challenge. The police brought jack stands, but since the armored car weighed 2.3 tons, the jack stands were unable to lift the vehicle. Finally, the police had no choice but to seek help from the fire department.

The armored car dragged the two women nearly 10 meters.
