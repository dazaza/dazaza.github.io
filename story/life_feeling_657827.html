They -- my bygone days that I can never finish singing about

by mimi456 on 2012-01-28 22:04:02

They -- the songs I can never finish singing

By chance, I stumbled upon them; they are melodies that have already been phased out, sealed away in the corners of the music kingdom, covered with a thick layer of bygone days.

I can still find them, so I will reignite them to recall the beauty of the past.

Code 89757

September 2005. I met him. His dimples, small eyes, and the habit of grabbing his clothes while singing. I still remember Code 89757's blue eyes, silver-white skin, and his rule prohibiting love for his master. The sound of Code 89757 in the music constantly spins in my mind; he was my belief, and I also wanted to have a master, and one day, I fell in love with her.

One Thousand Years Later

I like to find a place where I am alone during the quiet times after class, to feel the possible emotions that may be passed down. It has been so long, just like the future the teacher once mentioned, it will only be a pile of lifeless white fossils. If it lasts forever, it becomes a fuel worth using. In fact, if you persistently love, a lifetime might be too late, and a thousand years is truly forever. A millennium-long love that can be passed down leaves endless mysteries and dreams for those who wish to guard it for a thousand years.

The Summer That Was Blown By the Wind

I still remember yesterday, that summer. I was only 13 years old, knowing only how to play, laughing innocently. Barefoot, I chased and played around the flower beds outside the classroom, hid in the dense corners to play hide-and-seek, jumped around on the empty grounds in front of the hall, and secretly became a flower thief during the season when osmanthus blossomed. Even though my foot got cut, even though my body was full of crushed leaves, even though I fell flat on my face, even though I was caught by the teacher, it was still my most innocent youth.

Love As We Go

May 2008. I was about to graduate, leaving behind my three years of pure innocence. I started to become difficult to see clearly, as if floating in a vacuum universe. I understood the meaning of letting go: if you don't want to, just say the last "I'm sorry" to me, but I still love you, just that we cannot be together. Tell me why love always becomes clear only after it's lost, please don't cry. At least I still exist. I understand, so I will comprehend the beautiful future of giving up.

The Mermaid Who Fell in Love With a Shark

It was the most romantic theme song from their beloved youth idol drama. But I don't think so; it is unique music, representing the memories of youth. Very strong vibrations, it was the rhythm of my journey on that path, step by step with vitality, step by step towards direction. I couldn't understand the lyrics, but I could feel every note.

Even Beethoven Wants to Tell You

Listen to this song with a perfect mood, return to reality with a broken heart. Everything is too fake; the initial low tones on the piano seem like an evil laugh luring me into the abyss. In just two short months, my aspirations turned into a wisp of murky smoke, dissipating. For the first time, I felt pain, thinking that what followed would be sweet happiness, but instead, it was the beginning of suffering.

Unparalleled Under Heaven

Back then, she was still there. I seemed to have lost any hope, like a wild beast without direction, unable to see the way back, only sorrow. I thought, let myself sink deeper, until one day I leave smoothly, die peacefully. She always guided me, telling me that in the future I would surely find my love, but I couldn't continue being so decadent. There are paths for what is lost, and no need to grieve over what remains. I didn't understand before, but now I do.

The Wind of Happiness

Listening to it again, it is now. You see through my coldness, kiss my worries, not caring about my past mistakes. If it weren't for your tender gentleness, I would have thought true love was just a legend. Please believe in my commitment, even though it's a bit clumsy. But I see the wind of happiness. If I put my hand behind my back, would you be willing to hold it and walk together? Haha, I've met such a person, and she was right. Now, I have found my own happiness.

Can you accompany me throughout this life? Can you remind me when I am no longer pure?
