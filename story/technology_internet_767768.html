Apple rolls out three new measures, thickening competitive barriers

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:29:03

By Wang Jieceng

Following the conclusion of the autumn new product launch event, Apple Inc.'s stock price has shifted from a slight increase at today's opening to a slight decline. The lack of investor approval on the day of the launch is not the first time, but whether Apple can once again leave these data-focused Wall Street people speechless is difficult to say.

In this launch event, Apple completed its final major launch of 2013, finishing the complete puzzle of its three main product lines: mobile phones, tablet computers, and computers.

With respect to craftsmanship design, software, and performance, Apple has brought out its usual three-pronged approach. Behind the increasingly mature product line, what the outside world perceives more clearly is the change in Apple from innovation to defense.

The Three-Pronged Approach of Hardware

From a product line perspective, Apple has presented a full range of consumer electronic devices: mobile phones, tablet computers, and Macs are Apple's three-pronged approach.

The focus of updates for this launch event lies with the iPad tablet computer and Mac series. The release of the Retina iPad mini and iPad Air will still allow Apple to maintain its competitiveness in the tablet computer market.

Especially the Retina screen mini product; in terms of screen experience, it is easy for users to transition from bad to good, but after using the Retina screen for a long time, it becomes hard to accept traditional screens. The grainy appearance of the old mini model was one of the important reasons for consumers holding off on purchases.

In addition, the previous generation's mini used the processor and hardware environment of the previous generation, meaning that the iPad mini could be considered a retired-level product. But this time, the mini comes equipped with the new A7 processor and an increased price, marking the complete leadership of the iPad mini as a new product line.

The iPad Air addresses a core issue: weight. The original 1.4-pound tablet computer still felt heavy, while the new iPad weighs only 1 pound and is thinner. This allows it to truly bear the "air" moniker.

Another significant update is the MacBook series. With the upgrade of Intel's Haswell chip, the Pro Retina product line now boasts a standby time of 9 hours. At the same time, Apple has chosen to lower the pricing of the series.

Adding the already released iPhone 5s, Apple has completed its hardware layout for this year.

The Unavoidable Topic of Free Software

iLife is free, iWork is free, even the OS X Mavericks operating system is free. What Apple is doing is changing the business model of selling CD keys that the software industry has been accustomed to for decades, and the primary target is Microsoft.

Even in the presentation slides, Apple made comparisons with Win8. "Win8 Pro sells for $199? OS X is free."

In all the latest iOS and Mac devices, the free availability of iWork and iLife represents an important change. iLife mainly includes photo and music editing software, serving as a crucial link to transform iPads and Macs from content consumption devices into content production devices. Meanwhile, iWork has gone from being priced in the tens of dollars to being completely free, significantly reducing the entry cost.

The biggest change is in the operating system. Outside of enthusiast systems in the Linux domain, operating systems have traditionally been expensive, a core mindset instilled by Microsoft among global users. By making OS X free, Apple has greatly lowered the user migration threshold.

Regardless of how advantageous the mobile market may be, Mac's share in the PC market remains relatively low. In recent years, the secret to the growth in Apple's Mac revenue has been the promotion of Mac sales driven by iPhone and iPad users. It appears that Apple hopes this reverse nurturing effect will be even better this time around.

This truly mocks Microsoft. After decades, Microsoft still relies heavily on individual operating system licenses for substantial income, posing a significant challenge.

Adequate Defense, Insufficient Innovation

Apple is undoubtedly one of the most innovative technology companies in the world, producing some of the best products, but from an industry perspective, Apple's various product lines have become more mature, yet there is less innovation.

It takes two generations to mature a product line, which is exactly what happened with the iPad mini. Since the trial sale in 2012, the iPad mini has carried the experiment of small-screen tablets, helped Apple resist part of the Android camp, and assisted in consuming a large amount of early components. However, it also reduced profit margins and affected the sales of the iPad 4.

There are advantages and disadvantages, so how should this product line develop? The Retina-screen mini provides the answer. This has become a brand-new product line, featuring both flagship models and older generation products.

The lack of innovation is even more evident in the Mac product line. Apart from the relative push from the latest technologies provided by suppliers like Intel, Apple's integrated design has become more mature, but it has not come up with another tool that leads the industry.

Some people say that Apple is preparing for a comprehensive upgrade in 2014, but under Samsung's rapid updates of two generations per year and some domestic manufacturers' three generations per year, Apple's annual update pace might need to accelerate.
