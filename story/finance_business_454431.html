No companion required

by aitshjck on 2011-06-30 16:47:04

The temporary obstacles that appear during the course of business operations are mostly due to problems in management. The work of enterprise management is specific and detailed. When conducting a corporate system diagnosis, how to grasp the basic ailments of an enterprise in the shortest time is an important part of verifying whether a project consultant has the ability to manage the project, and it also relates to the cooperation issues of various departments within the enterprise during the project process. No ailment can avoid the management process, and the details within can reveal the crux of the problem. All the wisdom of management masters is crystallized in the nuances of each process, and these seemingly insignificant details, when gathered together, form a unique management style.

1. Viewing Management through Meeting Records

Each time I conduct research and diagnosis for a company, reviewing the company's meeting records is one of the main contents I start with. Many management issues can be discovered from the meeting records. For example, if the records are not standardized and not properly preserved, it can be preliminarily judged that there are flaws in the company’s management, and the preliminary diagnosis would be that execution is lacking. If most of the content in the meeting records documents only the general manager's speeches, then the company clearly has unclear hierarchical responsibilities, blurred management responsibilities, and incomplete processes. If the meeting records show repeated discussions on the same matter or discussions without decisions, then the company's problems are more severe, with low work efficiency, dragging feet, and passing the buck. It is evident that there are cliques or factions within the company, which indicates problems in the hiring mechanism.

2. Viewing Management through Sanitation

Enterprise sanitation management is the most superficial type of management but reflects substantive issues in overall enterprise management. Tidying up for inspection shows the speculative nature of the enterprise management responsible person; neglecting corners in sanitation cleaning indicates the superficiality of enterprise management; if you find the worst-case scenario of dirtiness, disorder, and poor conditions, it directly reflects the chaos in the company's management. If all three of these occur simultaneously and significant efforts are not made to reform processes, the company will go bankrupt within half a year and suffer heavy losses. Management must start with sanitation. "If one cannot sweep a single room, how can they clean the whole world?" This thousand-year-old saying is no exaggeration! Sanitation management should ideally achieve transparent supervision, using positive incentives to motivate employees. All individual regional hygiene rewards and punishments can serve as an important assessment for promotion and salary increases.

3. Viewing Management through Employee Habits

Mindset determines destiny. Thoughts guide personal actions, and actions over time form habits. Habits shape one's fundamental character, and character determines lifelong fate. Enterprises are schools that cultivate people's destinies, and nurturing good professional habits in employees is the basic task of the enterprise. The cultivation of employees' good habits and the formation of corporate culture are synchronous. Enterprises must maintain the institutionalization of certain periods to lay the groundwork for the formation of employee habits or corporate culture. The enterprise must persist alongside its employees. Corporate culture is not something achieved overnight. In work, forge patience and perseverance in employees, continuously seek passion, encourage innovation, advocate brainstorming, and continue some good actions to form habits. Then summarize and categorize them, incorporating them into the enterprise's daily management for promotion. Only such enterprises can gradually form cohesion, and corporate culture can grow systematically.

As a project consultant, it is often difficult to find any sanitation issues when entering a company for the first time. Most enterprise management layers have the mindset of a "shy bride," so even if things were like that before, they will clean up for the sake of their image during the first meeting. Usually, this is handled by the office director. However, for a project consultant to diagnose the enterprise, it must be done under real normal circumstances to be targeted. At this time, you can request to review all document compilations, including meeting records. On the second visit, do not notify beforehand, but instead conduct a surprise inspection, preferably entering after work hours. This way, you can observe the entire company's sanitation. The next morning, simply wear the company's uniform and take a walk around various departments or workshops (without needing accompaniment). As long as you do not raise the alertness of the company's employees beforehand, you will gain deep insights into the habits of the employees!

By mastering these firsthand materials from the enterprise, further investigations and consultations can be conducted based on the identified problems, delving into each process. Combined with industry trend analysis, genuine diagnostic results are not hard to produce.
