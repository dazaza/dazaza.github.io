<p>New Express News (Reported by Li Guohui, Correspondents Zhang Kejun and Jiang Xiongfeng) - A young man named Rong, while in high school, robbed a fellow student of over 60 yuan. After the crime, he fled to Dongguan, Guangdong to work. Six years later, he thought the incident was minor and that it was safe now, so he planned to travel north to do business. However, when he recently prepared to board a train at Dongguan East Railway Station, his status as a fugitive was identified by railway police officers, leading to his arrest.</p>

<p>At around 11:00 AM on the 20th, the police officers of Dongguan East Railway Station discovered a young man who appeared nervous and suspicious while patrolling at the entrance. Upon investigation, this man turned out to be an online wanted fugitive for a school robbery incident.</p>

<p>Upon further investigation, the man's name is Rong, aged 25, from Zhongjiang County, Sichuan Province. Rong has always had poor academic performance and disliked studying. After entering high school, he befriended the wrong people, picked up some bad habits, and started spending extravagantly. The monthly living expenses provided by his family were insufficient. In order to obtain more money, on the afternoon of September 21, 2004, he colluded with two other classmates to rob ten students in the school dormitory with a knife, obtaining a total of 63 yuan. After the incident was exposed, Rong, who had not yet graduated from high school, fled to Chengdu, Sichuan, and then to a factory in Dongguan, Guangdong. After nearly six years in hiding, he believed everything was fine and planned to take a train to Hebei to conduct business, only to be caught at the railway station.</p>
