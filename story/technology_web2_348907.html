Looking out the window at the azure sky and deep blue sea, the water and sky blend seamlessly into one another; since it wasn't the weekend, aside from a few honeymooning couples enjoying the sunbathing, the pristine white beach appeared slightly desolate. Feeling somewhat tired and irritable, the fatigue stemmed from an overwhelming amount of work.

When busy, it seemed as if everything witnessed that night in the rear garden of the Yin family had been forgotten, but when there was downtime, the image of those two white figures tightly embracing and kissing would always surface in his mind. Over these days, he had deliberately avoided thinking about it by focusing on work, yet at this moment, his heart felt suffocated, oppressed with annoyance that left him feeling trapped without an outlet. Subsequently, he rose from the comfortable leather chair, stood up to adjust his clothes, and walked out of the spacious and bright office.

Even though that night he had boasted confidently in front of Yin Zhihou about testing each other's sincerity, afterward, Xia Zaijing's heart felt uncertain. Gu Junbiao and Jin Sicao had experienced countless storms because of love and had agreed upon a long-term commitment; their feelings should have been unbreakable. Although several years ago, he knew that Yin Zhihou liked Jin Sicao and had stayed by her side all along, could a single kiss change their past mistakes? "Mind your own business, today we drink this." Gu Junbiao glared back at her defiantly.

"Wow, Gu Junbiao..." Xia Zaijing turned her head away, held the glass and sipped the wine, saying, "...you haven't changed a bit."

Thus, the two teased each other while drinking a lot of alcohol, only leaving the bar when it closed.

After retrieving their cars from the parking lot separately, Gu Junbiao drove along the seaside road, suddenly recalling Xia Zaijing's resort. He quickly turned around and sped toward the direction of the resort; there was a room where he and Sicao had once spent a night together, and he wanted to see it because he would be flying to the U.S. starting tomorrow.

The Gu family, for the convenience of visiting clients, had reserved rooms available anytime in this high-end resort. Thus, Gu Junbiao easily entered this highly secured and upscale resort.

Inside, Gu Junbiao followed the memories in his mind, retracing the route he and Sicao had once taken, lingering for quite some time at the astronomical telescope they had used to observe constellations together. Finally approaching the door of that room, he merely wanted to test whether the door could open; extending his inquisitive hand to grasp the stainless steel handle glowing faintly white, the door easily swung open with a turn to the left.

"How come the door isn't locked?" he muttered to himself, thinking perhaps the housekeeper forgot to lock it. Without suspecting anything else, after entering, he gently closed the door behind him and pressed a switch on the wall near the entrance to light up a soft decorative lamp.

He was very familiar with this room, so familiar that even in the dark, he could find the switch by touch. Several years ago, on that night, he had spent the entire night sitting in this room watching Jin Sicao sleep on the bed; later, unable to bear the loneliness, he paced silently around the room measuring its size...

"Ooh... Ooh..." The sound faintly came from the bathroom within the room.

Gu Junbiao startled at the sound, noticing now that the bathroom door was slightly ajar, letting out a dim orange glow. As he thought of leaving quickly, he heard faint crying sounds from inside. Curious, he tiptoed closer to the bathroom door.

"Damn Gu Junbiao, disgusting Gu Junbiao, I, Xia Zaijing, will love you forever in this life." This was followed by more vomiting sounds of "Ooh" "Ooh".

Xia Zaijing felt she had vomited enough, then stood up to rinse her mouth at the sink.

Standing behind her, Gu Junbiao became somewhat embarrassed. He felt flustered in this small space, almost suffocating. Coupled with the excessive alcohol consumed earlier, his face began to heat up, prompting him to want to leave immediately.

"Wah, Gu Junbiao, your face is so red!" Xia Zaijing turned around and saw him, laughing.

"You woman." Gu Junbiao said sheepishly as he moved towards the door.

"Wah, Jun," Xia Zaijing quickly jumped over and climbed onto his back, lightly biting his left ear.

"Hey, you woman..." Gu Junbiao exclaimed softly; that was his most sensitive spot. Yin Zhihou leaned his head against the bedpost, rubbing his eyes with his hands before gazing out the window.

Grandpa had just drawn aside the heavy curtains, revealing the lush green trees and vegetation in the courtyard behind the yard.

"The Backyard"—these two characters had become a wound in his heart since that night, amplifying everything that happened there infinitely. He only felt intense pain, lazily lying listlessly and sinking back into the covers.

"Tsk tsk tsk, my child, you're almost 25 years old and still so fond of sleeping!" Grandpa stood at the doorway saying. After waiting a while and receiving no response from beneath the covers, he entered the room.

Standing by Zhithou's bed for a long time, seeing no reaction, Grandpa bent down and lifted the thin blanket covering Zhithou's body. Patting his closed eyes, seemingly deeply asleep, Grandpa said:

"Zhithou, your professor has called home multiple times, you should return his call!"

Saying this, he sighed lightly, "Ah..."

"Hey, also, the phone in the living room has been ringing off the hook with calls from Yubin!" Grandpa continued, then mused aloud, "I wonder what that child wants, calling you so many times."

After speaking, Grandpa shook his head lightly, glanced at Zhithou who seemed reluctant to talk, then placed his phone on the bedside table and exited the room.

Listening to the fading footsteps of his grandpa, Yin Zhithou lazily opened his eyes, staring at the white ceiling lost in thought for a moment, then slowly sat up. After a while, he turned sideways and reached for the phone on the bedside table.

Rubbing his eyes and sliding open the phone cover, he noticed that several missed calls were from Song Yubin, and further down, two missed calls were from Ding Haiming, a good friend from Macau he hadn't contacted for a long time. Pondering for a moment, just as he was about to dial Yubin's number, a message reception prompt tone sounded, so he pressed the receive button first.

Seeing only an unfamiliar phone number, but reading the content below, it read: "Alliance, it's all up to you now! Xia Zaijing."

These few words resonated like a pebble dropped into the tranquil lake of his heart—"Ding Dong," creating ripples.

Repeating this several times, confirming it was indeed a message from Xia Zaijing, as the area code indicated it was from the U.S., a smile graced his lips, and a pale, melancholy handsome face seemed to gain some rosy hue.

Xia Zaijing, you really have it! Truly swift and decisive!!

Yin Zhithou chuckled inwardly, genuinely impressed.
