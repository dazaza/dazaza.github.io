Whitening starts with sun protection. Dark spots are caused by the overstimulation of melanin pigmentation due to ultraviolet (UV) rays. The longer you are exposed, the more pigments will form, and the darker the spots will become. If you have a predisposition to developing spots, the area of these spots can easily expand. Even if you don't have such a predisposition, excessive exposure to sunlight will eventually lead to the formation of spots. Therefore, sun protection is an absolute necessity.

UV rays are mainly divided into UVB and UVA (which can further be split into UVA1 and UVA2). UVB causes your skin to redden and burn, while long-term exposure to UVA1 can cause roughness, dryness, keratinization, indentation, loss of elasticity, and ultimately wrinkle formation.

The best way to prevent dark spots is to avoid sun exposure. The first priority is to block out UV rays. Wherever you might get sun exposure, remember to wear a hat or carry an umbrella. When going outside, make sure to apply sunscreen or foundation.

When engaging in outdoor activities, since you'll sweat a lot, it's important to frequently reapply UV-blocking creams or cosmetics. Even indoors where sunlight can reach, proper care should be taken. Especially during the luteal phase, extra attention should be paid to not exposing yourself to UV rays. From 10 days before your period until its onset, try to avoid sun exposure as much as possible.

Whitening starts from daily life. To achieve healthy and lasting whiteness, it’s recommended to change daily habits and diet. Even if your skin tone is predetermined by genetics, beyond just applying products on your face regularly, there are many methods that can help you whiten and combat aging. Below are some easy-to-follow whitening tips from everyday life:

1. Get enough sleep: Work pressure, over-fatigue, staying up late, and insufficient sleep can lead to poor metabolism and a dull complexion. Remember to go to bed early during the beauty hour from 11 PM to 2 AM to let your skin naturally rejuvenate.

2. Avoid smoking and drinking: Smoking and drinking can cause melanin deposition. People who smoke or drink regularly may appear more sallow, prone to spots on their cheeks, and have darker lips. With age, this condition worsens, and no amount of whitening skincare might fix it later.

3. Drink plenty of water: Staying hydrated helps metabolize waste substances in the body. Drinking water is the cheapest and simplest way to whiten your skin.

4. Consume whitening vegetables: Such as cucumbers, rape, okra, green beans, lemons, cauliflower, strawberries, coix seeds, cabbage, asparagus, bitter melon, sweet potato leaves, brewer's yeast, bell peppers, mustard greens, bamboo shoots, tomatoes, spinach, etc.

5. Regularly replenish water and Vitamin C.

6. Use day and night whitening products: Whitening emulsion, isolation cream; for summer use: Sunscreen oil, Christmas window stickers. Choose whitening skincare products containing:

- Direct whitening: Vitamin C, Superoxide Dismutase

- Reflecting UV rays: Titanium Oxide

- Absorbing certain wavelengths of UV: Suncreeen

- Fading dark spots: Hydroquinone

After winter, your skin may appear lighter, but this also means your defense against sunlight has weakened. Most people only start whitening efforts when the sun gets stronger in June or July, or after they've already tanned. However, once the skin is damaged and melanin produced, restoring it to its original state becomes very difficult.

Whitening should start with basic skincare, from cleansing, toning, using emulsions, to diet and exercise. Consistently integrating whitening into your daily routine not only maintains the skin's basic nutrition needs but also achieves long-lasting whitening effects. Of course, effective whitening ingredients need to penetrate deeply into the skin layers to work effectively.

Though whitening is crucial, having the right concept is equally important to achieve healthy, white skin. It’s essential to have realistic expectations about whitening and understand skincare principles. It's not just about choosing products that make your skin whiter; whitening requires long-term commitment and integration into daily routines for lasting results.

Basic skincare combined with massage enhances absorption. Whitening begins with good hydration. When the skin is well-hydrated, the texture naturally appears brighter. Adding herbal or essential oil components can reduce stress-induced dullness. Pairing deep breathing and massage with basic skincare can relieve stress, improve circulation, and enhance product absorption, gradually improving dullness and spots caused by stress and fatigue. You can pre-treat with a toner-soaked mask to increase the moisture content of the stratum corneum, helping subsequent whitening ingredients penetrate better.

Improving metabolism brightens the skin. Normal skin metabolism cycles every 28 days, but pollution and stress can slow this down, making dullness more noticeable. Besides regular exfoliation, using whitening massage creams daily can help boost circulation and metabolism. Massage creams are typically smoother, reducing the risk of over-pulling the skin. For normal moisturizing creams, choose richer, smoother textures. For oily skin in summer, adding some toner can dilute the cream for a fresher feel.

Regular exfoliation refreshes the skin. In summer, with increased oil secretion, old dead skin cells accumulate more easily, making the skin appear rough and dull. Not exfoliating properly can hinder product absorption and clog pores, causing acne. Oily skin types should exfoliate twice a week, dry or sensitive skin once a week, and combination skin can exfoliate twice weekly on T-zones and once elsewhere.

Thorough sun protection combats melanin and photo-aging. Sunscreen is the foundation of whitening. Due to environmental pollution, UV damage is becoming more severe, necessitating protection against melanin formation and photo-aging. For daily life, SPF 15 is sufficient, while those who often go out or have spots should use SPF 30+. PA+++ offers the best protection. Remember to apply sunscreen 20 minutes before going out and avoid prolonged sun exposure between 10 AM and 2 PM. If you must go out, wear hats, umbrellas, sunglasses, long-sleeved shirts, and pants to protect your skin from UV rays. Select sunscreen suitable for your skin type and apply it 20 minutes before going out, reapplying every four hours. Proper sun protection can significantly enhance whitening effectiveness.
