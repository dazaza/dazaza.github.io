Purpose of this experiment: Due to adjustments in Baidu's algorithm, many webmasters find their new sites are not being indexed quickly. I wanted to conduct this experiment to summarize methods that allow for rapid indexing by Baidu.

If Baidu hasn't indexed your site using the following methods, you should look into the reasons:

1. Your website contains politically-related articles.

2. Your site has content related to pornography, gambling, or drugs.

3. Your domain has a prior history of issues.

This method is intended for webmasters who don't understand SEO.

As for those aiming for high rankings for two-character keyword searches, don't believe what the so-called "experts" say about paying protection fees to Baidu—it’s true because even Baidu's executives need to eat!

Experiment Requirement One:

1. After setting up a new site, exchange friendly links with 3-5 friends. Just 3-5 are enough.

2. Don't overly concern yourself with the other party's PR (PageRank) or relevance. As long as their site updates regularly, hasn't cheated, and is indexed well, it's fine.

Experiment Requirement Two: Utilize popular forums:

1. On popular forums, post threads, then edit them to add links...the more replies, the better the effect.

2. On Baidu Tieba (Baidu Forums), pay attention to moderation. (My experience is posting in the early morning—why? Because the moderators are asleep.) Also, spiders are more active at this time. Avoid very popular forums where posts sink quickly. Follow up on newly posted threads and add links in your replies.

Experiment Requirement Three: Appropriately use Baidu Zhidao (Baidu Knows):

After setting up a new site, besides exchanging friendly links, I also go to Baidu Zhidao to answer relevant questions and include links. Here are eight tips:

1. Answer in the "Unanswered Questions" section to leave links.

2. Find relevant questions to answer. Use keyword searches to make your answers valuable rather than just randomly answering any question for the sake of leaving a link.

3. In the unanswered questions section, ensure there are no other outbound links on the page. This way, when you add a link, it becomes the only outbound link on the page, forcing the spider to follow your link.

4. Choose pages with more text content. For example, if someone has already written hundreds of words in response, you can supplement with your own answer and include a link. Such pages increase the relevance of your link.

5. I usually only answer two questions each day, leaving 1-2 links per question. I'm cautious about leaving too many links within a few minutes, fearing it might attract Baidu's attention. Of course, this might be excessive caution on my part.

6. If you can't find suitable questions, register multiple Baidu accounts and ask and answer your own questions. This improves the quality of the page.

7. Unanswered questions pages are typically not indexed by Baidu. You can try searching for some URLs from these pages in Baidu. Once you've answered the question, track the inclusion status of the page. Once the page is indexed, your page will soon follow. If it coincides with an update in Baidu's index, the inclusion efficiency will be surprisingly high.

Note:

1. Baidu Tieba can also be used.

2. Don't overuse Baidu Tieba and Baidu Zhidao, especially for new sites. Don’t frequently post promotional content on Tieba or excessively answer questions on Zhidao to create links, as Baidu may consider this as cheating.

For one of my sites, Yueguangbaohe (Moonlight Box), located at http://www.yueguang.org.cn, after setting it up, I only posted two threads daily on Baidu Tieba and made three connections daily on Baidu Zhidao.
