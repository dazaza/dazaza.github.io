Fortunately, the elder brother was found no abnormality and was discharged 18 days later. The younger brother had mild symptoms of oxygen deficiency and a small amount of bleeding in the cranium. The department simulated a constant temperature and high humidity environment inside the uterus in the most advanced imported incubator and precisely planned a detailed diagnosis and treatment plan every day. After 50 days of treatment, the younger brother was also discharged safely.

The Hubei Metropolis Daily (Weibo) reported (reporter Chen Yan, correspondent Zhou Jianyue, Wang Yongping) that a pair of twin brothers were born, and the elder brother weighed more than twice as much as the younger one. After a tenacious fight for 50 days, the younger brother, who only weighed 895 grams, finally returned home safely to reunite with his elder brother yesterday.

At the age of 35, Ms. Li became pregnant this year. During prenatal checkups, it was discovered that she was carrying twins, but the fetuses were of different sizes, with a developmental difference of about five weeks. The doctor then diagnosed her with twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome, indicating a significant risk to the fetal growth. However, Ms. Li insisted on giving birth to the children.

On October 25th, seven weeks before the due date, for the safety of the fetus in her belly, Ms. Li was admitted early to the Hubei Maternal and Child Health Care Hospital. On the day of admission, the doctor's examination revealed that surgery must be performed immediately, or both the mother and child would be in danger. That day, Ms. Li gave birth to a pair of twins via cesarean section, and the babies were immediately sent to the neonatal intensive care unit.

Dr. Hu Yulian, deputy chief physician of the Neonatal Department of the hospital, introduced that the weight difference between the two brothers at birth was staggering: the elder brother weighed 2030 grams, while the younger one only weighed 895 grams (as shown in the figure). She said that although monozygotic twins share a placenta, they usually have independent circulatory systems. However, when some blood vessels are connected, blood will flow to the side with better circulation and less resistance, resulting in twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome. This situation is relatively common, but generally, the weight difference between fetuses does not exceed 15%. A weight gap as large as these brothers' is quite rare.

Currently, the two brothers are growing up healthily. The elder brother now weighs 3100 grams, and the younger brother weighs 1800 grams.
