How To Play Backgammon - Understanding The Rules

When it comes to playing backgammon for the first time, it could be quite confusing to understand the board setup, placing backgammon chips, and the gameplay. Backgammon is played according to specific rules that are unique and enhance the gameplay. Learning these rules will help you understand how to play backgammon. After setting up the board and selecting black or white chips to play with, it's time to roll the dice and start the game.

Each player throws the dice to determine who will play first. If both players roll the same number, roll again. You need to roll both dice to find out who will play first.

Backgammon chips always move in the forward direction, starting from the farthest right-hand upper corner. You will be moving counterclockwise, as will your opponent. Your objective is to move the chips according to the numbers you roll (from 1 to 24) toward the inner board on your side. Each number you roll corresponds to one point. For instance, if you roll a 4 and a 2, the 4 corresponds to 4 points, and the 2 corresponds to 2 points.

After rolling, you can move either two chips or one, depending on the strategy you want to follow and the availability. In simpler terms, you can move one chip 4 points and another one 2 points. Alternatively, you can move one of your chips 6 points if you have a clear course. Finding a clear course can be quite difficult. You are not allowed to move to a point where your opponent has two chips, which is considered a block. Your chips can only be placed at points that already have your own single chip, are unoccupied, or only have a single chip of your opponent. If none of these options are available, you must give up your turn. In instances where you only have the option of moving one chip legally but not the others, you must make such a move.

When you roll doubles, that is considered a special scenario, and you are allowed more move options. For example, if you roll two fours, then you can either move one of your chips to 16 points, move two chips 8 points each, move one chip 12 points and another 4 points, move two chips 4 points each and another one 8 points, or move four of your different chips 4 points each.

Now, if your chip lands on a point already occupied by a single chip of your opponent, that chip is considered a blot, and your chip landing on top of it represents an attack. The blot or the chip of your opponent under attack now has to be removed from play and moved to the bar located in the center of the board. Your opponent is not allowed to make further moves until they can legally move the blot from the center. Additionally, the blot can only be placed back on the inner board, meaning your opponent has to start over again. You also have to start over again if you get the blot.

To secure a win, you are required to have all your chips on your inner board, which means they all must be placed on your 1 through 6 points. After that, you can commence the "bear off," which means moving your pieces off the board according to the points you get on rolls. The first player to do this wins the backgammon. A double victory is attained if your opponent could not bear off even a single chip of theirs. A triple victory is awarded if your opponent could not reach the bear-off stage and still has blots or any chips in the inner table on your side.
