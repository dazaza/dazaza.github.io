The difference between three types of drinking water: purified water, mineral water, and natural drinking water.

by shinian60 on 2011-08-25 11:57:37

Purified Water

Bottled purified drinking water refers to water sourced from water that meets the hygiene standards for domestic water supply. It is obtained through methods such as distillation, deionization, or ion exchange and reverse osmosis, removing almost all substances from the water. It contains no additives and can be consumed directly. Its pH index ranges from 5.0 to 7.0, making it acidic. Besides regulations on harmful chemical substances like lead and arsenic, there are no requirements for beneficial minerals and trace elements (Reference: National Standard for Purified Drinking Water GB 17323-1998). Due to its lower source water requirements, urban tap water has become the preferred choice for purified water manufacturers to save costs.

Mineralized Water

Drinking mineralized water refers to drinking water made by adding mineral food additives or natural mineral extracts to purified water. It has certain requirements for minerals and trace elements but does not specify silicic acid. The pH index ranges from 5.0 to 8.5 (Reference: Zhejiang Provincial Standard for Mineralized Drinking Water DB33/339-2001). However, in reality, the pH of mineralized water is often more acidic than purified water! There are two reasons for this. First, it is made by adding artificial mineralizing liquids to acidic purified drinking water, which creates an inherent deficiency. Second, the artificially added mineralizing liquids containing potassium chloride and magnesium sulfate are acidic. These acidic artificial mineralizing liquids decompose in purified water, producing potassium, calcium, sodium, magnesium, and iron ions along with a large amount of chloride and sulfate ions. Moreover, these acidic ions dominate in quantity, leading to further acidity. Therefore, the acidity of mineralized water may be even lower than that of purified water! The pH value is generally less than 6.0, which is already below the pH level of human death state, and some are even lower than the national acid rain standard (5.6).

Natural Water

Among the three types of drinking water, natural water has the most stringent standards. Management departments have strict definitions regarding its source, source protection area, processing, minerals, and trace elements. Regarding the source, it must be high-quality natural water that meets drinking water hygiene standards, and urban tap water cannot be used under any circumstances. In terms of source protection, a protection zone must be established with fixed signs within the zone. Within a radius of 30 meters around the lake (reservoir) water intake point, unauthorized personnel are prohibited from entering, and items unrelated to water intake equipment are not allowed. Within a radius of 50 meters, residential areas, factories, toilets, water pits, garbage, waste residues, and pollution pipelines are forbidden, and the use of pesticides and fertilizers is strictly prohibited. All activities that may destroy water quality are also forbidden. During processing, only high-tech disinfection methods can be used, and no food or chemical additives can be added. For minerals and trace elements, clear specifications are provided for the content of various required minerals and trace elements, and these must be naturally occurring. Most importantly, the pH of natural water is generally above 7, i.e., weakly alkaline. Finally, the water source for this type of drinking water must not undergo any chemical treatment through public urban water supply systems or municipal water supply systems (Reference: Zhejiang Provincial Standard for Natural Drinking Water DB33/383-2005).
