Leukemia mom gives birth successfully at 7 months pregnant

by xue72lwsh on 2012-03-08 15:26:42

"Da He's Love" donated 6000 yuan to her, and an editor of our newspaper also sent 1200 yuan through someone else. Currently, the mother is emotionally stable and hopes to get well soon so she can take good care of her child.

On April 11, our newspaper published on page A11 "At Seven and a Half Months Pregnant, Expectant Mother Suddenly Contracts Leukemia." In just two days, Wu Li (a pseudonym) has garnered attention from all sectors of society. Yesterday, staff members from the Henan Charity Federation delivered 6000 yuan to Wu Li as part of the "Da He's Love" charity fund's contribution to her.

Wu Li's father told reporters that yesterday morning, an editor from our newspaper's health section sent 1200 yuan through someone else and encouraged Wu Li to actively seek treatment for her illness. "There was also an elderly man in his 60s from Zhengzhou who left behind 500 yuan and then walked away without even saying his name," Wu Li's father choked up while saying, "There are still many kind-hearted people in this world. I really don't know how to thank everyone."

Additionally, there is some good news. Wu Li gave birth naturally at 4:30 AM on April 11, delivering a boy. Due to premature birth, the baby is currently still in the neonatal intensive care unit. Shi Jie, Wu Li's chief doctor, said that Wu Li's delivery process went smoothly, with no dangerous situations such as heavy bleeding occurring. From appearances, the baby also looks healthy, and further checks are being conducted in other areas.

"After the safe birth of the child, Wu Li's emotions have stabilized significantly. She hopes to get well soon so she can take good care of her child," Wu Li's father said.

News Follow-up - Part 2

"Panda Blood" Patient Out of Danger and Also Wants to Be a Volunteer

"Our child is out of danger now. Thank you to everyone who cared about us, especially those who donated blood for my child," said the mother of Wang Mou, a "Panda Blood" patient at the Second People's Hospital of Luohe, when we met her the day before yesterday.

Our newspaper published on March 31, page A11, "Five Cities Collaborate Overnight to Save 'Panda Blood' Victim." The report detailed how Wang Mou, after a car accident that put his life in critical condition, urgently needed a blood transfusion. Because he had the rare "Rh" negative blood type known as "Panda Blood," the Second People's Hospital of Luohe quickly contacted the city's mere 30 plus individuals with the same blood type and sought help from neighboring cities.

In the ward, Wang Mou currently appears to be in good spirits, with a rosy complexion and bright eyes. "Thank you to all the kind-hearted people who showed concern and helped me. I feel very happy right now," Wang Mou said to reporters.
