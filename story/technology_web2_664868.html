High costs lead to price increases, and coating companies are "unable to bear it"

by jhfgskkgfef7 on 2012-02-14 08:38:43

High Costs Lead to Price Increases: Coating Companies Are "Struggling" – Market Observation - Industry News - Building Materials Network

The rise in raw material prices has become a certainty, with companies such as International Paints, PPG Industries, and Jotun Paints increasing the prices of their marine coatings. As downstream industries of coatings, shipbuilding companies are facing dual pressures: rising steel prices and continuously increasing marine coating prices. Therefore, shipbuilding enterprises need to continue promoting the implementation of modern shipbuilding models, accelerate the development of integrated hull outfitting and painting, to improve the utilization rate of marine coatings and reduce production costs.

High Costs Leading to Price Increases

Recently, multiple international large-scale coating companies, including Jotun Paints, International Paints, PPG Industries, and the Marine Coatings Business Department, have raised the prices of their marine coatings. Among them, Jotun announced that it would increase product prices in Europe, the Middle East, and Asia starting from March 1, 2011. PPG Industries' Protective and Marine Coatings Business Department also recently announced global price increases within the scope allowed by contracts. In fact, since late November 2010, domestic paint and coating prices in China have already started to rise, with an increase of 5%-10%.

Multiple coating companies stated that the fundamental reason for their price increases is the rise in raw material prices. It is understood that most raw materials for paints and coatings are petrochemical products, making them susceptible to fluctuations in international crude oil prices. Due to significant increases in the prices of major raw materials such as chemical solvents, synthetic resins, and titanium dioxide, various brands of coatings and paints have had no choice but to follow suit and raise prices. Additionally, many other paint-making raw materials like epoxy resin, titanium dioxide, solvents, and copper have been affected by inflation, leading to very noticeable price increases. So far, there has been no sign of easing in the upward trend of these raw material prices. Moreover, coating manufacturing companies are also facing pressures from rising labor costs and operating costs, which is why these companies have had to adjust their product prices appropriately. According to statistics, in 2010, the average price of major raw materials for coatings increased by 10%-60%. Industry insiders stated that continuous rises in the prices of petroleum and other main raw materials will further increase the cost of the petrochemical products industry.

Of course, the rise in coating prices also prompts coating companies to adjust their product structures, increase research and development efforts, improve product production processes, enhance enterprise management levels, and digest the huge pressure brought about by the rising costs from all aspects. Multiple coating companies expressed that they will strive to fully leverage purchasing advantages externally and actively implement cost-saving measures internally, aiming to reduce the negative impact of future raw material price increases; at the same time, they will actively cooperate with customers to help them find the most economical solutions that meet their needs.

Improving Coating Utilization Rates is Imperative

Currently, the cost of marine coatings generally accounts for 5%-20% of the total ship cost (the specific proportion depends on the ship type and the shipowner's requirements). For example, the coating cost of a 30,000-ton multi-purpose heavy-lift vessel usually accounts for around 20% of the total ship cost. Therefore, for shipbuilding companies, the increase in marine coating prices will undoubtedly greatly increase the company's production costs and increase operational pressures. Moreover, due to the implementation of the PSPC standard, the usage of marine coatings by shipbuilding companies has also significantly increased. According to calculations by Wenchong Shipyard Co., Ltd., the single-ship coating consumption of some of its vessels will increase by about 8%, while Shanghai Waigaoqiao Shipbuilding Co., Ltd. estimates that the proportion of single-ship coating costs for bulk carriers in total costs will increase by more than 1%.

For this reason, relevant experts suggest that all parties should actively carry out work to establish national standards, industry standards, and enterprise standards for ship coating. Strict control must be maintained over coating design, construction, and management. At the same time, shipbuilding enterprises must accelerate the establishment of modern shipbuilding models, implement precision shipbuilding, make improvements in production processes, continuously study new shipbuilding methods, enhance coating technology, improve the utilization rate of marine coatings, and alleviate the cost pressures caused by price increases.
