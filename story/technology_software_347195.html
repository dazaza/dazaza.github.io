**Ferrous Sulfate**

**Chemical English Name:** Ferrous sulfate

**Molecular Formula:** FeSO₄·7H₂O

Ferrous sulfate is primarily used as a coagulant. It has excellent coagulation effects, good decolorization capabilities, and can also remove heavy metal ions, oil, phosphorus, and bacteria. It is particularly effective for the decolorization of dyeing wastewater and the removal of COD in electroplating wastewater through ferrite co-precipitation. Additionally, it is cost-effective, making it the preferred choice for treating wastewater from dyeing, electroplating, and other industries.

Ferrous sulfate is classified by its water content into anhydrous, monohydrate, and heptahydrate forms. By application, it is divided into agricultural grade, industrial grade, pharmaceutical grade, food grade, etc.

Heptahydrate ferrous sulfate (green vitriol) is used to manufacture iron-based pigments, iron-based catalysts, and for water purification and sewage treatment. Volcanic rock; monohydrate ferrous sulfate is mainly used as a feed additive.

Ferrous sulfate is a byproduct of the sulfuric acid method used in the production of rare metal products. The product appears as pale green or pale yellow-green crystalline solids, odorless, with astringent taste. When added in the prescribed amount, it can adjust the pH of alkaline water, combine organically with suspended particles in water, and accelerate sedimentation. It is mainly used for water purification and industrial wastewater treatment and also has bactericidal properties.

**Physical Properties:**

- Relative density: 1.899 (at 18°C)

- Melting point: 64°C

- Loses 6 molecules of crystallization water at 90°C

- Loses all crystallization water at 300°C

- In dry air, it easily effloresces; in humid air, it gradually oxidizes to form brownish-yellow basic ferric sulfate.

- Anhydrous form: white powder, relative density changes when reacting with water to turn blue-green.

- pH (1+10 solution): 7

- Solubility: 1g dissolves in approximately 5mL of water at 25°C or 0.5mL of boiling water, insoluble in ethanol.

- Theoretical iron content: 20.45%

**Appearance:** Pale green or pale yellow-green crystalline solid, soluble in water and glycerin, insoluble in ethanol, corrosive, hygroscopic, and prone to efflorescence.

**Applications:** Wastewater treatment, fertilizers, pesticides, catalysts, disinfectants, magnetic materials, and other industries.
