Introduction to the Episodes of "The Soldier's Heart Remains Unchanged"

Episode 1:

In the mid-1980s, just before his discharge, Feng Xiaobing hurriedly came to Nanjiang City to find an old comrade-in-arms, Zhou Lingyun, the factory manager of the Sino-foreign joint venture Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory. Zhou Lingyun thought it was about arranging a job for the old comrade-in-arms Chen Zhijiang, but unexpectedly, Feng Xiaobing requested Zhou Lingyun not to accept Chen Zhijiang, which made Zhou Lingyun very difficult.

Chen Zhijiang, Zhou Lingyun, and He Ningsheng are comrades-in-arms who have experienced life and death on the battlefield. After being discharged, Chen Zhijiang proposed to work at Zhou Lingyun's Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory. The old political instructor He Ningsheng accompanied him to the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory. Unexpectedly, the Japanese representative Fujiwara agreed to consider letting Chen Zhijiang join the factory as deputy factory manager.

The city military transfer office, considering Chen Zhijiang's disability, arranged for him to work in the agency as deputy director. However, feeling idle in the agency, Chen Zhijiang found it unbearable. One day, while changing his bandages at the hospital, he met a farmer patient who couldn't afford medicine. This incident touched Chen Zhijiang again, prompting him to resign from his public position. Chen Zhijiang joined Zhou Lingyun's Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory as deputy factory manager. Although the old comrade-in-arms Zhou Lingyun took care of him in various aspects, Chen Zhijiang still wanted to engage in sales to understand the pharmaceutical market.

After two years of work practice, Chen Zhijiang discovered that the Shan Zhu Root Granules produced by the Red Star Factory did not sell well. He proposed the idea of developing new drugs. Zhou Lingyun told him that developing new drugs was not only a large investment but also a high-risk endeavor, something a small factory like the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory couldn't afford. Currently, maintaining the status quo and paying the employees' salaries was enough. He advised Chen Zhijiang not to indulge in unrealistic fantasies, but Chen Zhijiang still believed that relying solely on the current Shan Zhu Root Granules would be hard to have a promising market prospect.

Zhou Lingyun and Chen Zhijiang had differences in their factory management philosophies. After joining the factory, Chen Zhijiang realized his insufficient knowledge and insisted on further education at the Traditional Chinese Medicine University. At this time, an outbreak of hepatitis A occurred in coastal areas, urgently requiring Shan Zhu Root Granules, which became a hot commodity overnight. The Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory's inventory of over 120 tons of Shan Zhu Root quickly found buyers, with favorable market conditions. Zhou Lingyun was quite pleased.

Just then, the Japanese representative Fujiwara, who was on vacation back home, suddenly returned, demanding that the Red Star Factory stop domestic sales and use all its Shan Zhu Root exclusively for export, raising the export price. Zhou Lingyun readily agreed.

Episode 2:

To seize this opportunity and earn more money for the factory, Zhou Lingyun signed a contract with Fujiwara to increase the export of 120 tons of Shan Zhu Root. It was stipulated that no Shan Zhu Root should be shipped out domestically, waiting for overseas transportation. As this was unplanned export, Zhou Lingyun sought help from Feng Xiaobing, who worked in the Foreign Trade Bureau. Feng Xiaobing quickly helped him complete the customs export procedures. Upon hearing that Zhou Lingyun had so swiftly completed the export procedures, Fujiwara notified the Japanese headquarters to immediately transfer all the payment into the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory's account.

While running sales outside, Chen Zhijiang learned unintentionally that there was an urgent outbreak of hepatitis A in the coastal regions. The Shan Zhu Root Granules produced by the Red Star Factory had become a highly sought-after product in the market, with insufficient supply.

He Ningsheng's son, He Mingcong, who was also studying pharmaceuticals, came to the Red Star Factory with Dean Bai from Pujian Hospital, hoping to obtain some Shan Zhu Root Granules through his relationship with He Mingcong. Unexpectedly, he was rejected by Zhou Lingyun.

Upon learning that the Red Star Factory intended to export all its stock medicines, Chen Zhijiang felt very confused. While domestic Shan Zhu Root was in short supply, a batch of imported high-priced Shan Zhu Root Granules appeared in the domestic market, causing skyrocketing prices and complaints from patients and the public. Therefore, Chen Zhijiang rushed back to the Red Star Factory and made an unexpected decision without consulting the factory manager Zhou Lingyun. He sent all the stock Shan Zhu Root to the coastal epidemic area and exposed Fujiwara's behavior of repackaging the Shan Zhu Root Granules produced by the Red Star Factory and re-importing them into China, profiting from China's national disaster.

Due to Chen Zhijiang sending the medicine to the coastal epidemic area, the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory breached the export contract. Upon hearing that it was Chen Zhijiang who had acted independently and sent away the Shan Zhu Root, both Zhou Lingyun and Feng Xiaobing had intense conflicts with Chen Zhijiang. In anger, Chen Zhijiang submitted his resignation. Due to affecting the export, the Japanese representative Fujiwara insisted that Chen Zhijiang personally take responsibility and demanded a penalty of 100,000 yuan.

Episode 3:

Knowing he could not escape the fine, Chen Zhijiang planned to pay it using the family's limited savings, which was opposed by Feng Xiaobing. Feng Xiaobing blamed Chen Zhijiang for making everything a mess, but Chen Zhijiang believed he had done nothing wrong because he had delivered the medicine to the hands of the patients in the epidemic area. To retain Chen Zhijiang, Zhou Lingyun gathered 100,000 yuan and handed it to Chen Zhijiang, asking him to personally deliver the money to Fujiwara under his personal name to ease the conflict between the two.

However, Chen Zhijiang secretly took the family's entire 100,000 yuan severance pay and gave it to Fujiwara. But Chen Zhijiang required Fujiwara to write a receipt in front of him, exposing the despicable actions of the Japanese businessman in the receipt. Although Chen Zhijiang paid the fine, the conflict with Fujiwara not only failed to ease but became even sharper. Zhang Chengli, the factory manager of Hongtu Pharmaceutical Factory, due to poor management and heavy debts, asked the planner Hu Yue for an idea, and Hu Yue gave him a creative idea to "develop new drugs" without changing the essence.

Although Zhou Lingyun repeatedly tried to retain him, Chen Zhijiang eventually chose to resign. But after resigning, Chen Zhijiang felt aimless. Feng Xiaobing asked Chen Zhijiang what his next plan was. Chen Zhijiang said he wanted to be a boss and start a pharmaceutical factory. Feng Xiaobing believed that since he had no money or connections, it would be impossible to establish a pharmaceutical factory. One day, Chen Zhijiang met a demobilized soldier named Song Xiaojie by the canal. Song Xiaojie's entrepreneurship and success inspired Chen Zhijiang.

Chen Zhijiang decided to start from scratch like Song Xiaojie. But how to take the first step? Wandering on the snack street, he saw an elderly woman selling dumplings. Recalling that he used to make dumplings in the army, he decided to start by setting up a stall to sell dumplings, embarking on his own entrepreneurial path.

Episode 4:

Chen Zhijiang decided to start all over again. While attending lectures at the medical college, he also set up a stall to sell dumplings. His dumplings were named "Veteran Dumplings," attracting many demobilized and transferred military personnel to eat them. Feng Xiaobing and Zhou Lingyun opposed Chen Zhijiang setting up a stall. Zhou Lingyun persuaded Chen Zhijiang to return to work at the factory, but unexpectedly, Chen Zhijiang set up his dumpling stall right in front of the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory, causing frequent conflicts between Chen Zhijiang and Zhou Lingyun, making Feng Xiaobing feel uncomfortable caught in the middle.

After the epidemic was resolved, the sales of Shan Zhu Root Granules greatly decreased, leaving the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory in dire straits. Zhang Chengli, the factory manager of Hongtu Pharmaceutical Factory, proposed cooperation with Zhou Lingyun to produce new drugs, but in reality, they were just changing names and forms of existing drugs. Unexpectedly, both pharmaceutical factories lost money. Feng Xiaobing earnestly persuaded Chen Zhijiang to abandon the dumpling stall and look for a new job in the city. Feng Xiaobing also asked Zhou Lingyun to help think of ways for Chen Zhijiang, but Chen Zhijiang was determined to start his own pharmaceutical factory.

He Ningsheng, the former political instructor of Chen Zhijiang and Zhou Lingyun, was already the regimental political commissar. Facing the simplification and restructuring of the army, He Ningsheng proposed to be demobilized and work in local areas. His wife opposed it, but his graduate student son He Mingcong understood and supported him very much.

After conducting research on the Chinese pharmaceutical market, Fujiwara reminded Zhou Lingyun that if the Sino-foreign joint venture Red Star Factory didn't launch truly new drugs, the Red Star Factory would eventually collapse. Simultaneously, Fujiwara informed Zhou Lingyun that if valuable secret formulas or folk remedies could be found, the Japanese headquarters would fully support the development of new drugs. Zhou Lingyun realized that finding secret formulas and developing modern traditional Chinese medicine was the only way out for the Red Star Factory. Chen Zhijiang was also thinking about the direction of his factory. Under the guidance of Professor Lu from the Medical College, Chen Zhijiang was inspired by the works of the ancient medical sage Zhang Zhongjing and determined to develop modern Chinese medicine by exploring the treasures of traditional Chinese medicine.

Feng Xiaobing, in order to help Chen Zhijiang find a new job, approached leaders at various levels of the municipal party committee and government. Under the care of the party secretary, the municipal government decided to arrange a new job for Chen Zhijiang as a state cadre, assigning him to work at the Beijing office of the municipal government.

Episode 5:

Zhang Xiaodong deliberately led Liang Jun to the dumpling stall in front of the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory to treat Liang Jun to a meal, but Zhou Lingyun wanted to drive Chen Zhijiang away. Chen Zhijiang learned that the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory was operating poorly. Zhang Chengli introduced a girlfriend to Zhou Lingyun, but Zhou Lingyun mysteriously refused her, leaving Zhang Chengli very puzzled.

Feng Xiaobing informed Zhou Lingyun about the news of Chen Zhijiang being re-employed and hoped that Zhou Lingyun would go together to find Chen Zhijiang, but Zhou Lingyun said that Feng Xiaobing should go alone.

Feng Xiaobing excitedly shared this good news with Chen Zhijiang, but unexpectedly, Chen Zhijiang flatly refused, making Feng Xiaobing very sad. Feng Xiaobing then left Chen Zhijiang with her daughter Chen Yuanfan. After being demobilized from the army, He Ningsheng decided to go to Nanjiang City to find Chen Zhijiang and start a business together. He Ningsheng's son He Mingcong was already a graduate student in Traditional Chinese Medicine University and came with his father to Nanjiang. He Ningsheng told Chen Zhijiang that this time he was demobilized rather than transferred, and he did not want to rely on state care but instead wanted to achieve something with Chen Zhijiang.

Fujiwara handed over a list of Chinese medical masters he obtained to Zhou Lingyun, hoping to get one of the master's secret formulas and folk remedies. Fujiwara clearly told Zhou Lingyun that if the Red Star Pharmaceutical Factory could get the secret formula from a famous old Chinese doctor, the Japanese side would invest again in the Red Star Factory and deepen cooperation.

Chen Zhijiang, He Ningsheng, and He Mingcong studied the factory establishment plan together. Chen Zhijiang proposed to definitely develop new drugs. He Mingcong's mentor was the master of Chinese medicine Gu Shixing, and Chen Zhijiang heard that Gu Shixing had a precious secret formula called Xinshuyin. Determined to get the secret formula, Chen Zhijiang decided to start his own business, which caused great concern for Feng Xiaobing.
