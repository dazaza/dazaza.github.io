_______ World Card Master】Renard Green【My Idol_______

by 4s8t0f2c on 2012-03-02 03:55:05

Since I came into contact with magic, Mr. Leonard Green has always been my idol. Known as the King of Cards in the world, his performance style is very unique. Suffering from severe nearsightedness, he mostly relies on his own intuition during performances. With a deck of cards and a pair of hands, he accomplished many tasks that seem impossible to ordinary people. Additionally, most of Green's wrist techniques are original, without any trace of boasting, yet they create wonders amidst chaos. Especially in 1988, when he participated in the World Magic Competition, due to the depth of his original methods, the judges thought he used special props. In 1991, when Green joined the magic competition again, he performed the same magic trick using the cards provided by the judges, ultimately winning the title of World Close-up Magic Champion. At the "World Close-up Magic Master Performance" held in Beijing, Green performed this championship-winning magic trick again, leaving the audience with an unparalleled visual shock.

Magic master Leonard Green was born on December 15, 1941. He is a world-renowned close-up magician, especially skilled in card magic, enjoying the title of the King of Cards. He currently resides in Gothenburg, Sweden. The card magic performances of King Green are unimaginable and full of humor. His magical techniques change infinitely. He can use an ordinary deck of cards to complete performances that were previously unthinkable, bringing you to the highest level of card magic. If you have watched the King's performance, including the use of X-ray glasses for poker, you will deeply understand that watching Green's magic is a pure enjoyment.

Green never performs in formal attire; he only wears regular clothes. He never boasts about his card skills, believing that magic is meant to bring happiness. Green introduced that he developed an interest in puzzles, difficult problems, and mysterious things at a young age, but started learning magic at the age of 18. During university, Green studied medicine and worked as a doctor for 15 years after graduation. "For many years, I only performed for friends or at small gatherings. All the props were ordinary items. It wasn't until 1992 that I became a full-time magician. Viewing magic as an interesting hobby, letting things develop naturally. Then, one day you might realize that magic is your destiny! That's my process." Green indicated that being a full-time professional magician is hard work, but being semi-professional is a very good option and much safer.

Currently, many people have developed an interest in magic and want to know the correct way to learn it. Green expressed that ordinary enthusiasts can find a lot of magic tricks online, but for a magician, the most beneficial education is always live performances and exchanges: "Showcase your skills in theaters, in front of many people, and exchange with them to improve."

The rule among magicians is not to reveal secrets, but in exchanges within the magic community, Green is extremely "generous", often performing or even explaining his techniques to other magicians. "There are no eternal secrets in magic, so what's there to be afraid of? Moreover, sharing your research results with others is itself a joyful thing. Only continuous decryption can promote constant innovation in magic. If everyone clings to their little tricks, it's not only bad for personal progress but also hinders the overall development of magic." Green once said: "Personality is very important for magicians. First, magicians should be humble and not arrogant. Besides, they should not have the intention to deceive others. The fundamental point of a magician's work is to entertain the audience and make everyone happy. This kind of happiness must not be obtained by mocking the audience."

Everyone encounters various setbacks and difficulties in life. So, what was the biggest challenge the "King of Cards" Green ever faced?

He said: "About three or four years ago, my eyes suddenly failed me, and the vision in my right eye dropped sharply. Although I had surgery, I still had to wear thick glasses. Even so, the vision in my right eye remains terrible. There's no need to mention the troubles caused in daily life, but the most critical issue was on stage, where the significant difference in vision between my two eyes made it almost impossible for me to see the cards in my hand, let alone remember them."

"So how is your current vision?" the reporter asked next.

"Not much different from before, but now I've gotten used to the trouble caused by the difference in vision between my left and right eyes," Green said.

(Side note: Now I understand. Before the interview, to avoid causing him inconvenience, I wrote him a note asking when would be convenient for him. I noticed that when he read the note, he almost stuck it to his glasses. At the time, I was puzzled: Was the writing too small?)

Life is always filled with choices. If given the chance to choose his career again, what would the "King of Cards" choose?

Without hesitation, he told me: "Doctor."

"Why a doctor?" the reporter was a bit confused.

He said: "In fact, before 1992, I was a surgeon."

(Side note: Dizzy! The "King of Cards" before our eyes, who dominates the magic world, was once an angel in white robes who saved lives!)

I really like Green. His hands, nearly as large as A4 paper, can hide anything, but they cannot conceal his simple, honest, and kind heart. I recommend that everyone go online and look up Green's videos when they have time. I believe you will definitely be amazed by Green's performances and techniques!
