Transformation One: Timely Orthodontics for Misaligned Teeth in Children and Adolescents

Little Ling, a very cute and well-behaved girl in the second year of junior high school, has misaligned teeth that "don't listen," which causes her great distress. The beautiful girl doesn't dare to speak or laugh loudly in front of others, always covering her mouth for fear that others will see her "buck teeth." Her mother took her to the hospital for a comprehensive examination and treatment of her teeth, and soon Little Ling will be able to face the new academic year with confidence and healthy, neat teeth.

Dr. Tao introduced that misaligned teeth are medically referred to as "malocclusion" and are common among adolescents. With the improvement in people's living standards, there is an increasing trend of misaligned teeth in children. Misaligned teeth can lead to facial aesthetic defects, making children prone to feelings of inferiority, affecting oral function, causing facial deformities, and ultimately harming their physical and mental health. Therefore, parents should pay more attention and take action promptly if they notice misaligned teeth in their children. Dr. Tao pointed out that orthodontic treatment not only gives children charming smiles but also prevents diseases such as gum recession and loose teeth.

Dr. Tao also reminded parents to observe their children's oral habits carefully. If you find that your child has bad habits like biting pencils, sucking fingers, biting lips, or sticking out their tongues, it is necessary to correct them in time. Early intervention with simple treatments can effectively prevent and control the development of malformations, achieving twice the result with half the effort.

Transformation Two: White-Collar Workers' Tooth Troubles Solved by Porcelain Veneers and Cold Light Whitening

At the age of 28, Miss Li enjoys a high-salary job that many envy. Her excellent educational background and efficient working style have made her successful in her career. However, she has her own worries: her teeth are not white enough, and she fears leaving clients with an impression of being untidy or unclean. Recently, after visiting the hospital on a friend's recommendation for dental beautification, she finally exuded a sense of ease and confidence in her smile.

Dr. Tao pointed out that many white-collar workers suffer from tooth discoloration due to long-term smoking, drinking tea, or coffee, or because their teeth turn yellow with age, leading to feelings of inferiority that significantly affect their social lives. Regular teeth cleaning methods are often insufficient to restore discolored teeth to their original whiteness. However, Dr. Tao mentioned that porcelain veneers and cold light whitening technology can help white-collar workers regain pearly white and lustrous teeth.

Dr. Tao introduced that porcelain veneers are a high-tech dental treatment that not only protect the teeth but also restore chewing functions. They provide a strong "protective layer" for the teeth, shielding them from external bacteria and various physical and chemical factors. Porcelain veneers not only make the teeth crystal clear and beautiful but also treat many oral diseases such as cavities and periodontal disease, making the teeth stronger and the gums healthier. Cold light whitening uses advanced cold light technology to convert organic pigments from carbon ring structures into non-pigmented hydrophilic structures, achieving tooth whitening effects. In just 30 minutes during lunch breaks or after work, white-collar workers can easily experience its magical effects, making their teeth white, healthy, and radiant.

Transformation Three: Elderly People Suffering from Tooth Loss Can Benefit from Dental Implants

Grandpa Sun, aged 67, has suffered greatly due to his teeth. As he grew older, his teeth began to loosen and fall out, accompanied by symptoms such as sensitivity to cold and heat. "Sometimes, I wish I could pull all my teeth out!" Grandpa Sun said painfully, pointing to his scattered teeth. Even more painful was that chewing food had become a burden for him, leaving his few remaining teeth weak and powerless, forcing him to puree his food before eating. Dr. Tao said that most elderly people endure the pain of tooth loss, drastically reducing their quality of life. Moreover, missing teeth can harm their health. Besides affecting facial aesthetics and speech function, the reduction or loss of chewing ability means that undigested food enters the gastrointestinal tract, increasing the burden on the digestive system and potentially causing gastrointestinal disorders, affecting the absorption of nutrients. In severe cases, it can lead to digestive system diseases. Therefore, it is urgent for elderly people with missing teeth to install dentures to restore chewing function.
