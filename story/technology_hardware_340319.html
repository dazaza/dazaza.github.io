The Easter egg is absolutely classic.

by tr24359 on 2010-04-05 19:27:57

On Easter Day, people light candles in front of the church to sanctify and welcome the holy candle into every household. On this day, the happiest thing for children is to deliver the holy fire to each house. They light branches with the holy fire in front of the church, then run to deliver it to every household, filled with a joyful festival atmosphere. In Bavaria, Germany, residents hold torch races every Easter to celebrate Jesus' rebirth. The rolling firewheel on Easter in Luetzhoehe, North Rhine-Westphalia, is also well-known. Six giant wooden wheels are set on fire and roll down the valley, like six fireballs descending from the sky, illuminating the dark valley. It shines alongside colorful fireworks, once again showing that fire has brought new life to humanity. As the only minority group in Germany, the Sorbs celebrate Jesus' resurrection with a hundred-voice choir. Sorbs dressed in black shirts and black hats ride on horses decorated with ribbons, flowers, and white shells, marching grandly on forest paths. They sing hymns with robust voices as they go, creating a magnificent scene.

In the West, Easter is more orthodox, and typical Easter gifts are related to spring and rebirth: eggs, chicks, rabbits, flowers, especially lilies, which are symbols of this season. On the eve of Easter, children decorate eggs for friends and family. These eggs are either hard-boiled or just empty shells. On Easter morning, children will find their Easter baskets filled with chocolate eggs, Easter bunnies, fluffy chicks, and dolls. It's said that the Easter Bunny hides colored eggs indoors or in the grass for children to find. The annual White House Easter Egg Roll in the United States is often broadcast live by television stations.

In most Western countries, grand religious parades are usually held during Easter. Parade participants wear long robes, carry crosses, and walk barefoot. They dress up as characters from Christian history, singing hymns to celebrate Jesus' resurrection. Nowadays, holiday parades have lost much of their former religious color. The festive parade is filled with celebratory atmosphere, having strong folk characteristics and regional features. In the United States, the parade includes clowns in cowboy costumes walking on stilts and the beloved cartoon character Mickey Mouse. In Britain, parades mainly introduce local history and customs, with participants dressing up as Scottish bagpipe bands and palace guards, attracting many tourists. The arrival of Easter also makes people change into new clothes. In the past, Christian believers would go to the church for baptism before the festival and then wear their new robes to celebrate Christ's new birth. The custom of wearing new clothes has been preserved until now because people believe that not wearing new clothes during the festival will bring bad luck. During Easter, people also like to thoroughly clean their homes, symbolizing the start of a new life.

The German government stipulates two days off for Easter. During the holiday, families gather together to taste various traditional foods, and relatives and friends greet each other. Eggs, fire, water, and rabbits, which symbolize life, have become lucky charms for Easter. Eggs and rabbits in the West are symbols of new life and prosperity. The natural color of eggs represents the sun, while dyeing eggs red symbolizes happiness in life. During Easter, parents specially prepare chocolates shaped like eggs and rabbits for children. Relatives exchange colored eggs. In some towns in the middle Rhine region and eastern Hesse, the ancient custom of "colored egg trees" still exists today. People paint hundreds of eggshells and string them into chains, hanging them on pine trees on Easter to make colored egg trees. Adults and children sing and dance around the colored egg tree to celebrate Easter. And young women in the Alps express their love through the gift of red eggs. If a girl gives three red eggs to a certain young man on Easter, it means the girl is expressing her love to the young man.

There is a legend about why rabbits have become lucky charms for Easter. In ancient times, on the day of Jesus' resurrection, it coincided with the "Spring Sun Festival" celebrated by the residents of Scandinavia. As one of the most prolific animals among grassland and forest animals, rabbits symbolize the revival of spring and the birth of new life. They are also the pet of the goddess of love Aphrodite and the candle-bearer of the Germanic earth goddess Holda. Therefore, rabbits are now messengers who deliver Easter eggs to children and are deeply loved by children. On Easter, children will surely receive rabbit-shaped gifts.

Fire not only brings light to humanity but also gives new life to the earth. As a symbol of Jesus' rebirth, many Easter activities are related to fire.
