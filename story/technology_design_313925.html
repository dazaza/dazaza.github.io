Mourinho's Unpredictable Changes Baffle Italian Media; A Masterstroke That Lavezzi or Ferrara May Never Achieve

Sina Sports - Before the Coppa Italia match against Livorno, Mourinho’s list of 19 players left the Italian media in deep thought. With only four defenders (Mc孔, Donati, Lucio, and Chivu), what kind of formation would he deploy?

The Sport Mail predicted: “Cambiaso will partner with Lucio, with Chivu and Mc孔 on the flanks.” All Sports had a slightly different idea: “Donati starts, Cambiaso partners with the sole center-back Lucio, and Chivu is on the left.” If the media could easily guess Mourinho’s intentions, then perhaps Mourinho wouldn’t be Mourinho. Lucio as the center-back was correct, but his partner was Chivu, while in Chivu's usual position stood Montari…

“The boss told me to play as a left-back, so I did as he asked,” said Montari calmly. From the midfield to the flank, from a midfielder to a defender, it seemed he felt no discomfort because “I played left-back when I was very young.”

After the game, on Inter Milan's official website fan ratings, Montari scored 7 points, an average score for the defense, indicating that in the eyes of most fans, the Ghanaian’s performance was competent, and the game process largely reflected this. In the 14th minute, Pieri crossed for Lucarelli, but Montari moved early and headed the ball out for a corner before him. However, his usual rashness wasn't curbed by the positional change. In the 22nd minute, Montari fouled Dani Levićius just outside the box, giving Livorno a direct free-kick opportunity. Montari showed no lack of physicality in defense, but lacked a bit of composure. Just like in the Champions League against Barcelona, he was repeatedly hit on the left flank by Alves in the first 45 minutes, showing little adaptability or willingness to improve, which often led to the same mistakes recurring. However, as mentioned earlier, Montari possesses the fighting spirit and tenacity that Mourinho desires. In the away leg of the Champions League against Kiev, it was his long-range shot from the left flank that led to subsequent finishes by Milito and Sneijder.

Mourinho loves to think about positional changes for players. When Sneijder was injured and absent, he even considered putting Montari as an attacking midfielder. For other players, Cambiaso has been used as a center-back, Balotelli has been placed as a winger, and Santon has shifted from right to left. As for Sane蒂's positional changes, they need no further explanation.

During the match, Rai's cameras captured details of Mourinho’s tactical board, showing four players' names with a horizontal line between the first two. In almost every game, Mourinho can be seen scribbling on a small notebook. Such actions were once considered a way for Mourinho to relieve stress, akin to Ferguson chewing gum or Ancelotti smoking. But the truth may not be so simple. The constantly changing tactics of Inter Milan undoubtedly show that Mourinho is always thinking, and meaningfully so. From trying out four formations in the past 18 months at Inter Milan to continuously adjusting player positions, Mourinho is actively and proactively building his team.

In fact, this is Inter Milan's advantage over AC Milan and Juventus, an absolute advantage that Mourinho holds over Leonardo and Ferrara. Such thinking, without the long-term refinement in Spain, Portugal, England, Italy, and on the Champions League stage, might be something you could never fully grasp in a lifetime.

Juventus lost 2-3 at home to Napoli, with Edy Reja's substitution of Dátolo in the second half playing a decisive role. After this substitution, Ferrara had no reaction whatsoever, watching as the 2-0 lead was squandered. Ferrara does make changes, such as bringing on De Ceglie when trailing and pushing Grosso forward, but that's all there is. Leonardo's situation is slightly better due to AC Milan's results, but the hesitation in substitutions during the Milan Derby, which delayed Gattuso being taken off, should leave a lasting impression on him.
