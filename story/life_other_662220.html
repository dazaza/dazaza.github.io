Are we still here after a year?

Everything happened without any warning around me, and before I knew it, it was Christmas Eve again followed by Christmas.

After the Christmas Eve activities ended, it started snowing lightly. The snowflakes were so light that you could easily miss them if you weren't paying attention.

The group booked a room in the east part of the city. When I say "group," in the end, there was only one man with whom I spent time. I still let my temper flare up at him more than once. Occasionally, he would get upset and ignore me, but most of the time, he indulged my habitual bad mood.

Even the highest temperature setting on the air conditioner couldn't satisfy me, and even with two extra blankets, it was no use. Instinctively, I moved closer to where he was; I just felt a bit cold. The endless e-commerce exam points were the worst part of Christmas Eve.

Dozens of hours later, as expected, I remember everything that happened. Love is like this: when you're deeply involved, everything seems perfect. One day, or rather in an instant, without any warning, you detach, and all you feel is disgust.

The Christmas Eve exam was the most tragic gift the school gave us at the end of 2010. But, like all clichéd people, we didn't have the courage to resist all the unequal rules set by the authorities. We've always pretended to be sensible children, respectful students, yet deep down, we are filled with dissatisfaction with reality. I don't want to pretend, but I understand that the smartest way to survive is to go with the flow and not stick your neck out.

I remembered a rumor I heard a few days ago. The college secretary was reportedly unhappy about my transfer to probationary status, citing the reason as being too public about our relationship. However, if this is considered high-profile, Pan and I might as well read Mao's quotations under the streetlight every night to meet Mr. Yu's expectations for how a party member should act subtly.

Life is like this: all sorts of strange and messy things come to you mysteriously, playing with you until you look foolishly cute, then they proudly announce the truth. In this 2010, such incidents were not few.

We decided to enjoy the Christmas atmosphere only after the exams ended. I didn't expect Pan to agree. Actually, I'm not that eager to do anything; many times, I just ask. Even I don't know why.

My girlfriend left her materials in the bag along with mine on the long bench behind the dormitory aunt's seat. Pan, holding his urine, searched for me in the supermarket within the campus, which he told me while complaining. I just smiled stupidly in response, without much guilt. Over time, with more experiences, I began to no longer believe in any sacrifices or changes made for me. Although I see the changes, I prefer to close my eyes as if I didn't see them. I don't want to owe anyone feelings or money; it would lock me down. Perhaps "living with one's tail between one's legs" fits better. As long as I can't see or remember, then everything that happened should be treated as if it never happened.

I know I am bad enough, but no one calls me a bad woman. Perhaps we should exclude Pan's previous woman and those self-righteous people who stood up for her. In their mouths, I am the worst woman in the world, making that woman unloved, making her lose him, and making her lose her place to return. Of course, in their view, my name has become another unpleasant word after Pan, often paired with those vulgar terms. I know what they hate most is that I continue to be with him as if nothing happened, ignoring their insults. But, whether you know it or not, my education and upbringing teach me to remain silent when I have no fault, merely observing coldly from the sidelines.

Heaven, I've gone off-topic again. This quiet early morning makes all my emotions burst forth, thus seeming somewhat inexplicable. What I'm doing is finding someone to confide in, but I don't want to scare everyone away like Sister Xiang. Essentially, I am that kind of woman, repeatedly and tirelessly recounting many unhappy things from the past. But I swear, I'm not seeking sympathy; heaven knows how much I hate being pitied.

Actually, what I initially wanted to say is that I forgot to take back the books and materials I left with the aunt to bring upstairs. Also, during our video call, I accidentally spilled cookie crumbs on my clothes and pants. Moreover, I'm too lazy to wash the clothes I changed last night. Furthermore, I looked through the photos on Q-zone again.

Some of the photos are from before I met Pan, from high school and university, showing significant changes. Some are from after I met Pan, featuring various hairstyles in 2010, from long straight hair to big waves, then to a bob cut. Some are from the day after being intimate with Pan, where I went on a trip with my family as if nothing had happened.

What touches me the most is the deception towards them, causing an indescribable discomfort in my heart.

I want to stop this deception, meaning I want to stop this romance. This has nothing to do with love or no love, it's simply about whether or not to continue.

As usual, this calm attitude and these words trigger his insecurity. The exclusive ringtone rang immediately, and I didn't dare to answer, fearing that my girlfriend would think I was being unreasonable again. So I sent him a QQ message.

Thinking back to Christmas Eve, he suddenly hugged me tightly, pressing his face against mine and saying those words. He said he was afraid, that without warning, I would run away. I told him I wouldn't. That's exactly what I thought at the time.

Can love be paused, stored in an iceberg, and retrieved when we decide to love again?

Can the process be stopped, waiting until one day when everyone hopes we'll be together, effortlessly getting married?

Can we wait until that day to fall in love again?

After a year, will we still be here? Where will we be?

Will we still remember last year's Christmas Eve and Christmas? How deeply we must have loved each other, and how sincerely we must have hoped. But I've forgotten. Just like last night, whether I dreamed of certain people or certain events, I've forgotten everything.

I ask myself, can I, should I?

But the answer remains the same as before.

I want to dye my hair a different color, going against my mom's wishes. How would she react if I did?

I started a romance, deviating from my dad's expectations. What would he say if it happened?

I want to know, after a year, who owns those things, those people?

My fingers typing have frozen, but the orange-yellow light from the desk lamp hasn't diminished. Yet, I no longer feel like speaking.
