Ye Yiqian's Weibo post mocking Shanghai's Oriental Pearl Tower as a trash can; clarification statement is hard to justify

by edivista on 2011-11-07 09:22:11

Click to enter Yiqian Ye's Weibo account

Recently, Yiqian Ye posted a photo on Weibo taken in front of the Oriental Pearl TV Tower in Shanghai. However, the accompanying text reads: "The Bund, crowded with people and accompanied by the smell of sweat, prevented me from appreciating its beauty. I hastily took a commemorative photo and left. There was a famous trash can behind me!" After this Weibo post was published, it drew criticism from many netizens: You may not like Shanghai, but please don't insult it.

Yiqian Ye's Weibo caused controversy

In this Weibo post, Yiqian Ye uploaded a photo she took at the Bund in Shanghai. The background of the photo is the famous Shanghai Oriental Pearl TV Tower, shining brightly in the night. What caused the controversy was Yiqian Ye's candid feelings accompanying the photo: "The Bund, crowded with people and accompanied by the smell of sweat, prevented me from appreciating its beauty. I hastily took a commemorative photo and left. There was a famous trash can behind me!"

Describing the Bund as having "the smell of sweat" and referring to the iconic Shanghai building, the Oriental Pearl TV Tower, as a "trash can," these sensitive words immediately sparked dissatisfaction among netizens (especially those from Shanghai).

Netizens criticize her remarks as excessive

As a public figure, don't you think these words are excessive?! On Weibo, a netizen named Huolanwu_Fiona expressed opposing opinions: "You may not like Shanghai, but please don't insult it. Shanghai may have so many flaws, but as a native of Shanghai, we all love this city! If you didn't come to Shanghai, there wouldn't be this issue of crowds and the smell of sweat."

At the same time, the netizen also attached a screenshot of Yiqian Ye's Weibo post. In the comments condemning Yiqian Ye's Weibo post by Huolanwu_Fiona, most netizens protested against Yiqian Ye, saying she should not refer to Shanghai's iconic buildings as "trash cans." A small number of netizens claimed: "Artists also have the right to express their own views; don't make a fuss over it."

Hot Comments

Real-name Weibo posts are more than just casual talk

Yiqian Ye got into trouble. As a public figure, a biased Weibo post filled with personal emotions attracted countless criticisms from netizens.

Actually, from a personal perspective, it is perfectly fine for Yiqian Ye to evaluate a certain attraction however she wants. Even using eye-catching terms like "trash can" falls under the category of freedom of speech, but this must be limited to expressing personal opinions within a small circle. Once she chooses to speak through a real-name Weibo account, what she says goes beyond merely expressing personal opinions.

Weibo Queen Jincheng once said in CCTV's "See" program that for ordinary people, Weibo might be a personal expression platform, but for celebrities and public figures, it is not a personal platform. The Weibo of public figures should have boundaries.

As a public figure, one should be more cautious when speaking through a real-name Weibo account. I would like to advise with a line from the movie "Crazy Stone": "Manners, pay attention to your manners." - Jiawei Wu

In the early hours of yesterday morning, Yiqian Ye uploaded a photo on Weibo showing a trash can beside her, and made a statement: "I posted this photo to clarify the misunderstanding caused by a Weibo post last year. 1. The trash can mentioned in the picture is the real trash can next to me, unfortunately, it wasn't captured in the photo I posted earlier. 2. After posting the Weibo, it caused everyone to misunderstand that I was referring to the Oriental Pearl TV Tower behind me, so I deleted the original Weibo and explained. 3. This incident happened last summer. I didn't want to mention it again, but due to recent reposts by many Weibo friends, I apologize once again to those who misunderstood because of this Weibo post!"

Yiqian Ye's clarification seems difficult to reconcile. Because the "famous trash can" mentioned in the controversial Weibo post was clearly not an ordinary trash can. Moreover, the ordinary trash can in the clarification Weibo post was beside her, not behind her. As a public figure with a real-name Weibo account, always remember to be cautious in speech and conduct.
