The Furthest Distance in the World

Author: Unknown Origin: Unknown Announcement Time: 2008-5-31 15:50:22

Since returning from Beijing, my mood has been unsettled. I really don't know why I ended up flying to Beijing by some twist of fate. In fact, I am very clear that it was only to see him.

Eight years ago, quite by chance, I received his first letter. Afterwards, we communicated through letters and phone calls despite being in different places. We had never met face-to-face but talked about everything. In his letters, he once told me: "Once you have seen the vast ocean, it's hard to be impressed by mere water; having witnessed the clouds of Wu Mountain, all others seem pale in comparison." However, as people living in the real world, we both eventually built our own families in separate cities a few years later. When his son was six and a half years old, I ended my marriage. This had nothing to do with him, but I never mentioned this matter to him. I once asked him, "Are you happy?" He countered with the same question for me, and in the end, neither of us answered.

After my divorce, I felt lonely and isolated, so I called him. While chatting idly, he said he would soon be going to Beijing on business. After hanging up, I also booked a ticket to Beijing. Before leaving, I called him, but his phone was off. I instantly felt lost, repeatedly redialing, but still without success. Then I called his workplace, asking every person who answered the phone to help find him. Although they were full of doubts about this unfamiliar woman's long-distance call, they still managed to contact their leader and, after several hours, I heard his voice. At the moment he called out my name, I almost cried. I told him I missed him and wanted to see him.

The road ahead is often filled with thorns. That day, due to heavy rain, the flight was delayed by three hours. This poured hot oil onto our already anxious and eager hearts. I prayed to heaven again and again, "Please make the rain stop quickly! Please let the furthest distance in the world disappear!" Finally, I stood in front of him. In the crowded hall of the Capital Airport, we had never met before, yet we immediately recognized each other. First came a gentle smile, then a tight embrace, and a deep kiss. Nine years had passed, and finally, we could break through the barriers of reason, confessing our longing for each other in an unrelated city. At that moment, we were like mandarin ducks separated in a past life, now reunited as husband and wife.

On the afternoon of the second day, both of us drank a lot of alcohol. Back in his room, I lay drunk on the bed. He helped me take off my shoes, and then his tall figure heavily pressed down on me. He softly called me "wife" and passionately kissed my forehead, eyebrows, eyes, nose, lips, neck... At that moment, I hesitated, resisted, even doubted what I was doing coming here to see him. But his one sentence, "Shall we return to our respective lives with regret?" immediately broke down all my resolve. I knew that as a woman, the consequences of such actions might bring unexpected troubles, but at that moment, the many years of vague affection burst forth like the Three Gorges Dam releasing its floodwaters, breaking through all worldly restraints. We happily united.

Happiness in this world is always fleeting. At that moment, I thought of the movie "Unfaithful" starring Richard Gere. Yes, we did not intend to destroy each other's families. We were not seeking thrills in illicit affairs. We just wanted to give closure to the dreams of our youth or perhaps draw a painful conclusion to this nine-year-long sincere emotion. In real life, we are the best and most responsible individuals. Together, we promised that this little episode would not affect the main melody of our lives. We will bury our nostalgia and pain deep in our hearts and remain unrepentant for each other's happiness.

Tagore said:

The furthest distance in the world
