Interestingly, non-ferrous metals have carved out a bloody path in the chaos, with investment varieties led by China Gold surging to the top of the gainers list after bottoming out. So friends can't help but ask: Has the investment era for non-ferrous metals arrived?

We analyze problems differently based on the object of study. For cyclical industries, there are generally three types: one is price-sensitive, mainly focusing on product prices, such as resource and agricultural industries; the second is related to the economic cycle and fixed asset investment, such as cement and machinery equipment; the third is the consumer goods industry, related to policies, consumption habits, population structure, and other long-term trends, such as the automobile and clothing industries.

Non-ferrous metals belong to the first category, so we break through from this perspective. Most commodities have their own production and demand cycles. Despite violent fluctuations in commodity prices, supply and demand relationships remain the main thread throughout. Under the influence of supply and demand relationships, many commodities' prices show seasonal characteristics, with off-seasons and peak seasons. For example, during the harvest season, product prices often fall; while in the lean period, supply decreases and prices rise again—this is the impact of seasonal factors on prices. Through time series analysis, we can identify the seasonal characteristics of commodity prices.

Based on the multiplicative model of the Census X12 method, the time series can be expressed in the form Y = TC (trend and cyclic factors) × S (seasonal factors) × I (irregular factors). The figure below shows the performance of each factor after the decomposition of copper futures prices.

From Figure 1's trend and cycle items, we can see that from mid-2005 to the end of 2008, copper prices went through a complete cycle. Starting from 2009, they entered a new cycle. From the seasonal factors of copper prices, there are seasonal upward trends from January to April and from May to July.

From the above, we understand that: "Since commodity prices exhibit seasonal fluctuations!" Would this price fluctuation characteristic be transmitted to the stock market, causing the share prices of companies selling these commodities to also exhibit seasonal fluctuations? To this end, we selected copper, aluminum, and gold as the studied commodities and chose Jiangxi Copper, Nanshan Aluminum, and Shandong Gold as corresponding stocks to analyze whether the seasonal characteristics of commodity prices could reflect on the corresponding stocks. The figure below shows the seasonal characteristics of copper futures prices, showing that copper has a seasonal rising trend from January to April and from May to July. We found that this seasonal characteristic basically reflects the actual market conditions of copper in real life.

Seasonal Characteristics of Copper Futures Prices

Data Source: China Nonferrous Metal Research Institute

Copper prices tend to rise seasonally due to pre-purchase by copper-consuming enterprises and increased downstream demand, preparing for the arrival of the copper consumption peak seasons in April and September; from September to December, entering winter, consumption shrinks, and enterprises mainly clear inventories, leading to a higher probability of falling copper demand. After the Spring Festival every year, SHFE copper usually sees an uptick. Since the Spring Festival is the longest legal holiday in China, customs clearance cannot be conducted for a relatively long time, making it impossible to carry out normal import procedures for spot goods, so spot traders usually choose to prepare inventory in advance. Moreover, at the beginning of the year, prices are generally lower compared to the whole year, and post-festival prices usually rise, which is highly attractive to spot traders. The surge in pre-festival inventory preparation naturally drives up copper prices. Another festival factor is weather and human factors. The Spring Festival usually falls in January or February, often accompanied by heavy snow, plus the busy traffic of the Spring Festival travel rush, which is extremely unfavorable for the transportation of spot goods. Inconvenient transportation leads to smooth spot supply, causing copper prices to rise. Looking at the data since 2005 (Table 1), copper prices have a higher probability of rising in February, April, and July. Related stock Jiangxi Copper, its prices all rose in February and April, and it also had a high probability of rising in July and November, at 80%.

For gold, we can see through the data that gold has a higher probability of rising from August to September and from October to February next year. Since October is India's Diwali Festival, which is the local gold consumption peak season, gold prices in August to September are easily influenced by consumption expectations, and the end of the year and the beginning of the year are also peak seasons for gold jewelry consumption, usually resulting in rising gold prices.

Looking at the data since 2005, gold prices have a higher probability of rising in January-February, September, and November. Related stock Shandong Gold, its prices also have a higher probability of rising in February, September, and November.

For aluminum, it shows a seasonal rising trend from March to April, June to July, and November to December. Electricity is the main component of aluminum production costs, accounting for about 26% of electrolytic aluminum production costs, so a rise in electricity prices will stimulate a short-term increase in aluminum prices.

Looking at the data since 2005, aluminum prices have the highest probability of rising in December. There is also a certain probability of rising in February. And related stock Nanshan Aluminum, its prices have a higher probability of rising in February, April, and November-December.

In the previous article, I mentioned that cyclical investment varieties are divided into three categories. Among the above three categories, the first type has a shorter cycle, and investors often focus on their price trends. If they can grasp their seasonal characteristics, they can control the investment rhythm well. The seasonal characteristics of metal prices revealed in the previous section and the interconnection of related stocks indicate that mastering this rhythm can bring stage-based market opportunities to investors. The seasonal features extracted through statistical analysis are merely general rules, while specific price rises and falls are affected by various factors such as output, inventory, weather, transportation, and consumer demand at the time. However, analyzing the seasonal characteristics of commodity prices can help investors pay attention to relevant influencing factors in advance and seize investment opportunities.
