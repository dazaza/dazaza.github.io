One of them, James reunited with an old friend.

by abcad123f on 2012-02-06 09:45:52

(Click to watch the high-definition picture package) Sina Sports News - Today, "Emperor" LeBron James faced a challenge from his old team, the Cavaliers. There were three highlights of this game: First, King James reunited with old friends. Walker himself also mentioned that secondly, the two Cavaliers' lottery picks met, and thirdly, James' recently promoted "Cavaliers model" encountered the real Cavaliers team.

In the opening, both teams probed each other. After 7 minutes silently passed, there was only a low score of 6-7. They allowed their opponents' shooting percentage to be over 50%, with the Heat slightly leading. This is when James started his first one-on-one play. James received the ball in the low post and easily backed down Anthony Parker, finishing with a one-handed hook shot. Then the Cavaliers suddenly heated up, scoring six points with three consecutive dunks, creating a small 6-2 run. At this moment, the Emperor rallied, first assisting Norris Cole for a layup, then personally hitting a mid-range jump shot for two points. After Cole scored another two points, the Cavaliers' substitute guard Daniel "Emperor's Old Pal" Gibson hit two consecutive three-pointers. James responded by sinking a long-distance two-pointer at the buzzer as the Heat ended the first quarter trailing 17-18. In the first quarter, James played all 12 minutes, making 3 out of 5 shots for 6 points, 1 steal, and 1 assist.

In the second quarter, the Emperor rested for half a quarter according to custom. During this half-quarter time, the Cavaliers were having fun either making threes or dunking inside the paint. When the Emperor returned to the court, the Heat trailed 30-34. After returning, the Emperor still did not rush into attacking himself. He first passed to Chris Bosh, who sank a high-position jump shot. Then he set a screen for Cole, who scored on a layup. The Heat thus leveled the score before the Emperor broke through and drew a foul, making one of two free throws. Subsequently, turnovers and missed shots flew across the field, and the game ended in a 37-37 tie at halftime.

Media Source: Sina Sports

By halftime, the Emperor had played 18 minutes, making 3 out of 8 shots for 7 points, 2 steals, and 1 assist. However, the Heat made 10 turnovers as a team, and it seemed that the entire team's challenging atmosphere was somewhat absent-minded, which led to the unexpected halftime tie.

In the third quarter, after switching sides, the Emperor first set a pick-and-roll with Dragon King (Bosh). Bosh drove into the lane, receiving James' pass and drawing a foul, making both free throws. Then the Emperor hit a turnaround jump shot from the low post, adding another point to the scoreboard. The Cavaliers showed no fear, relying on this year's rookie lottery pick Kyrie Irving's fast breaks and passes to take the lead at 49-45. At this moment, the Emperor again held the ball low and passed it to Bosh at the high position, who sank a jump shot to stabilize the situation. After Shane Battier hit another three-pointer, the Heat regained the lead. At this moment, the Emperor could no longer hold back, starting from outside the three-point line, breaking straight through like entering an unmanned area, rising up for a powerful dunk, giving the Heat a three-point lead. The Cavaliers quickly called a timeout.

After the timeout, both teams fell into a defensive battle. The Heat intentionally increased their defensive pressure, with James taking the lead. Just as Jamison grabbed the ball, the Emperor took a step forward into the veteran's embrace, directly knocking Jamison to the ground. At this moment, the Emperor remembered their old friendship, helping the elder up, and clapping him on the back three times as a sign of friendliness. Later, the Cavaliers' new lottery pick Kyrie Irving broke the deadlock with a successful shot. On the other side, the Heat were pushed close to the 24-second mark. At this moment, James, standing two steps beyond the three-point line, received a pass from his teammate. With no time left, what should he do? But James didn't panic. With a slight smile, he faced the defense and shot from where he stood. The ball drew a super-long arc and swished through the hoop, prompting people to exclaim, "Our Emperor is truly a great shooter!"

But this game was destined to be complicated. Afterwards, both teams once again fell into a deadlock of blocked shots and traveling violations, seeing players come and go without the scores rising. As the last minute of the third quarter approached, the Cavaliers narrowed the gap to just two points. At this moment, the Emperor got serious, hitting a high-position stop-and-pop jumper, allowing the Heat to maintain a four-point lead. Unfortunately, the young Cavaliers today were truly fearless calves, managing to bring the game to within one point at the end of the third quarter.

In the fourth quarter, both teams sent out substitutes, but the ugliness of the game did not improve. James rested for four minutes before re-entering the game. At this point, the Heat led by only four points at 67-63. At this moment, everyone had finished playing iron, and began showcasing Barcelona's special skills of falling down. Amidst the chaos, James and Bosh executed an inside-out play, resulting in Dragon King being assisted by the Emperor to complete a 2+1. However, thereafter, James himself committed two consecutive turnovers, allowing the Cavaliers to narrow the gap to three points.

At this point in the game, James' expression had become extremely unpleasant. Frowning, he charged towards his old friend Anderson Varejao. After missing a layup, James unwillingly picked up the backboard rebound, attacking Varejao again. Finally, unable to withstand the pressure, Varejao could only commit a foul. James scored two points and earned a free throw. Although James missed the free throw, such a fierce Emperor ignited the Heat. They defended against the Cavaliers' offense and then Bosh sank a three-pointer from beyond the arc, putting the Heat ahead by eight points!

However, as the saying goes, the barefoot are not afraid of the shoed. Parker retaliated with a three-point shot. At this moment, it was still James holding the ball, circling around half the court before assisting Udonis Haslem to hit a baseline mid-range shot. Alas, Haslem's brilliance lasted only a fleeting moment, immediately getting dunked on by Alonzo Gee on the defensive end. Fortunately, the Emperor remained calm, finding gaps in the joyfully oblivious Cavaliers' defense, passing to Bosh who again completed a 2+1!

As Bosh hit another mid-range shot, the game entered the final two minutes with the Heat leading 84-76. And with only 13.3 seconds remaining, James missed a layup, but the Cavaliers hit a three-pointer, narrowing the gap to 83-86, creating some tension on the field.

Ultimately, the Heat won this game at home with a score of 92-85. Although the opponent was weaker, the game was quite ugly. To be fair, in this game, the contemplative James did not bring the Heat into the so-called Cavaliers mode, but rather the Dragon King used his excellent performance to lead the team into Raptors mode. However, in this game, the Emperor played for 37 minutes, making 8 out of 21 shots, scoring 18 points, 5 rebounds, 5 assists, and 2 steals, but also committing 5 turnovers. Despite this, when he was on the court, his team could outscore the opponents by nine points, the highest in the team.

The Cavaliers' "angry wife" type owner Gilbert once went crazy stabbing scarecrows after James left, declaring "I will definitely win a championship before James." From this game, it seems unlikely, and the gap appears to be widening, because James is certainly not fighting alone.

This is precisely: The Emperor's abnormality in slaying his former master, welcoming the New Year with the dragon crossing the river.
