Bill Si - What to do if the team's execution ability is not good

by stonepx918 on 2012-03-01 09:47:41

Generally, people with an "active personality" can face many things with an optimistic and open-minded attitude. Especially when encountering difficulties, an active personality does not give in to challenges but chooses to face them bravely. People with an active personality will secure more opportunities, and their chances of success will be greater. Having an active personality doesn't guarantee success, but successful people share one common trait: they are proactive in what they do.

Let's take two 30-year-old women as an example, let's call them Xiao Wang and Xiao Li. They were classmates in university and one day met on the street.

"Why do you look so down? Are you in a bad mood?" Xiao Li said, "Oh no! What happened to Xiao Wang? How will I deal with that scoundrel in the future?"

Xiao Wang replied, "I'm exhausted. I got divorced. My life is completely ruined. Why am I so unlucky?"

Xiao Li also had just gone through a divorce but responded differently: "What? Divorced? You don’t know! I’m actually thrilled. Finally, I've escaped from that trap, I'm free now and ready to live my own life!"

Despite facing the same event, their attitudes towards divorce were vastly different. Although it's normal to have different perspectives on the same matter, as time passed, their situations began to change.

A few days after her divorce, Xiao Wang still couldn't recover. She didn't go to work, blaming the man who took away her love and family, believing all men were heartless. Every day she cried until her eyes were red and swollen.

Eventually, she had to continue living and reluctantly went back to work. Her colleagues noticed her haggard appearance; she had lost a lot of weight and looked like a different person. When asked what was wrong, she burst into tears at her desk.

"Poor thing!" The colleagues came to comfort her, helping her with water, food, and even some of her work. This could last for a day or two, three or four days at most, but after a week, she was still gloomy.

Her colleagues advised her, "If you're divorced, so what? It's not the end of the world. Just think positively. Find someone better."

Xiao Wang snapped, "It's easy for you to say that. Could you handle it if it happened to you?" In anger, she threw the phone in her hand. The well-meaning colleagues were left feeling awkward and disinterested, and no one bothered to console her anymore.

She had no heart for her work, and two weeks passed with her face still filled with pain. She ignored her clients, causing her sales performance to plummet sharply.

The leader called her to the office. A month had passed and she hadn't recovered. He said, "Xiao Wang, I know you're upset, but this can't go on forever. Look, you haven't dressed up for work lately. If you did, you'd look energetic and pretty."

"Who would I dress up for? I don't have the heart. I've been in a bad mood recently," she replied.

The leader suggested, "Since our department deals with so many customers daily, why don't we transfer you to another department? See how that works out?"

"What? Transfer me? What does that mean? Am I not doing my job well?" Xiao Wang argued.

"There's nothing special about it. We'll transfer you to another environment. Once you adjust, you can come back," the leader explained.

"How dare you treat me like this!" Xiao Wang shouted. After working in the department for four or five years, she felt she deserved credit for her hard work. The leader became impatient, "Think it over yourself. The leadership team is considering your situation these days."

"So what? Who cares!" she retorted, "Is this your way of letting me go? Fine, I'll leave."

And so, she quit her job. On her way home, full of resentment and without a job, she thought about her family and siblings who cared about her. Upon reaching her house, she heard laughter inside.

"Goodness, you're laughing happily while I'm suffering from this painful divorce!" She slammed the door open. Seeing her return smiling, the family stopped laughing and consoled her, urging her to stay positive.

"You laugh when I'm not around. Laugh all you want! Now I'm miserable, yet you're happy. How can I live knowing my own family treats me this way!" she exclaimed, slamming her bedroom door shut again and crying into her pillow.

Day after day, month after month, year after year, ten years passed.

After her divorce, Xiao Li felt an unprecedented sense of relief, thinking she was finally free to live her life according to her own ideas. On the third day after her divorce, she invited her colleagues, classmates, and friends to a gathering at her home. Everyone enjoyed tea and chats freely, having a great time.

Naturally, Xiao Li was proactive at work, always cheerful and willing to take on extra tasks. "Business trip? Sure!" "Overtime? Yes!" she would say.

When the leader asked if anyone could go on a business trip, others hesitated because they had families and children, but Xiao Li said confidently, "No problem. I'm single, so I'll go."

The leader was moved, "Thank you so much. You’ve really helped a lot. I was just worrying about who to send."

With her positive attitude, she earned good relationships with her colleagues and gained more clients, improving her sales performance month by month and year by year. She even funded her own MBA education and successfully obtained her degree. Occasionally, she would visit the gym with friends to exercise and maintain her figure and beauty treatments at the salon. Over time, she looked younger and more radiant.

One day, the leader called her to the office and said, "Xiao Li, over the past two years, you've made outstanding contributions to the company. Your skills have improved significantly. Both clients and colleagues have high praise for you, and the company leaders are satisfied with your work. After discussion at the general manager's meeting, it has been decided to promote you from provincial manager to regional manager, overseeing sales in five provinces."

"Keep up the good work! There's more to come!" he encouraged her.

One day, Xiao Li, who was already the vice president of the company, met her old friend Xiao Wang on the street after ten years.

"You still look energetic! How do you look so young? You still seem to be in your thirties!" Xiao Wang said.

"Do you still feel okay? Why do you look so tired? And your hair has turned gray so quickly. Don't you look like a woman in her fifties? Your life is truly ruined," Xiao Li remarked.

However, Xiao Li countered, "Not necessarily. Ten years ago, I told you, after the divorce, I was free and could live my life better. See, I wasn't wrong!"

This MBA case gave me a profound insight. People with similar backgrounds but different personalities lead to different careers and lives. Choices outweigh efforts. Successful people choose an active, upward, confident, and brave work attitude, leading to brilliant careers. However, if you choose a negative, evasive, timid, and disappointed work attitude, your career will be bleak, and you won't achieve much in life.

Positive people seize opportunities and don't easily miss them. Different characters lead to different destinies.

In China, there were two prodigies. The gifted class at the University of Science and Technology of China was once known as the "prodigy camp." Over the past thirty years, thousands of "young university students" emerged, among whom Ning Bo stood out as the first person in the university's gifted class. Extremely intelligent and obedient, Ning Bo embodied all the elements of a perfect child in the hearts of the Chinese people. In 1978, newspapers, magazines, and televisions across China reported on Ning Bo.

At the age of 2.5, Ning Bo could recite over 30 Mao Zedong poems, according to reports. At 3, he could count to 100, at 4, he learned over 400 Chinese characters, at 5, he started school, at 6, he began studying "An Introduction to Traditional Chinese Medicine" and using herbs, and at 8, he played Go and read "Water Margin." Almost overnight, this bespectacled prodigy became known nationwide.

Today, however, Ning Bo has become obscure, known only as a teacher at the University of Science and Technology of China. In 2003, he became a monk.

After graduating from university, Ning Bo strongly wished to enroll in graduate school but repeatedly abandoned his hopes. The first time, he withdrew after registering, the second time after the physical examination, and the third time, even after receiving his admission ticket, he backed out at the last moment before entering the exam hall. He never pursued similar opportunities again.

Ning Bo's misfortune wasn't due to intelligence but character. Worrying about his abilities, fearing failure, and being unable to actively face and challenge himself, he believed he couldn't accept failure because no one would accept the failure of a "prodigy." Thus, he lost the most precious qualities in humans: initiative and positivity, even becoming hesitant and afraid to reach for what he desired.

Meanwhile, Zhang Yaqin, another prodigy of Ning Bo’s era, chose a different path. Despite being hospitalized due to illness during the college entrance exam, Zhang Yaqin participated positively and confidently, paving the way to a successful career:

At 12 years old in 1978, he entered the gifted class at the University of Science and Technology of China.

At 23 years old in 1989, he received his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from George Washington University.

At 31 years old in 1997, Zhang Yaqin was awarded the title of Fellow by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, becoming the youngest scientist in its 100-year history to receive this honor.

In August 2000, he became the Chief Scientist and Dean of Microsoft Research China.

In November 2001, Zhang Yaqin became the founding dean of Microsoft Research Asia.

In January 2004, he was promoted to Senior Vice President of Microsoft Corporation.

Besides being smart, Zhang Yaqin was very proactive in everything he did. Proactivity is more important than intelligence itself.

Both started with the same aspirations and ideals. But ten years later, some surpassed others. Feeling lost, sometimes looking at peers or colleagues, one might blame fate's unfairness. In reality, it isn't due to privilege. If your current situation differs from theirs, it's because you weren't proactive enough. Perhaps they were simply more proactive, confident, courageous, and determined. These slight differences led to significant disparities in career and life.

Persistence in doing things is crucial for proactive individuals.

Case Study: Sylvester Stallone's Journey to Success

Stallone's mother was a choir singer, and his father was a barber. As a child, Stallone was sent to foster care, spending lonely days reading comic books. To emulate the heroes in those books, the mischievous boy broke eleven bones. He was expelled from fourteen schools before eventually choosing the American College in Switzerland to study acting. His portrayal in a student-created play, "Death of a Salesman," was highly acclaimed, making him realize that acting was his lifelong pursuit.

After returning to the U.S., Stallone entered the drama department at the University of Miami. Unfortunately, no teachers appreciated him, and he dropped out without completing his studies. Despite this, he refused any job outside of acting.

In New York, Stallone lived in dire straits, sleeping in a Volkswagen Beetle. Yet, his determination to become an actor remained unshaken. With droopy eyes and unclear pronunciation, these were Stallone's most recognized flaws. Despite his confidence, movie companies rejected him due to his plain looks and speech impediment. Nevertheless, after countless rejections, he started anew from the first studio. By the 1500th rejection, opportunity arrived.

During a random occasion, Stallone watched a boxing match between Ali and a small-time boxer who managed to fight for fifteen rounds. This inspired Stallone to write the legendary story of Rocky within three days.

Carrying his script, Stallone sought directors, insisting on playing Rocky himself, which discouraged many producers. After 1855 rejections, Stallone finally found a film company willing to produce his script. Within a month, "Rocky" was completed. Upon release, it grossed $225 million, winning Academy Awards for Best Picture and Best Director. Stallone was nominated for Best Actor and Best Screenplay. "Rocky" established Stallone's status in global cinema, and he went on to star in numerous films, becoming a hero in fans' hearts.

Anyone aiming for success should remember Sylvester Stallone's journey from 1855 rejections to becoming a world-renowned star. Before encountering 1855 rejections, please don't give up; keep striving positively. If you surpass 1855 rejections without success, consider trying 10,000 times, as that's the number of failures Edison faced inventing the light bulb.

According to a recent survey by World Manager Magazine, 80% of renowned CEOs admit that special talents didn't bring them to their current positions. None of these individuals ranked top in their classes. Their rise to prominence was due to their proactive and persistent attitudes. No one is born a CEO; if one's career progression is marked by unfinished endeavors, it's unlikely they'll become a CEO.
