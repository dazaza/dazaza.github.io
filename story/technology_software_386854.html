Zhao Weiming lives in a flat of more than 70 square meters.

Zhao Weiming is with the children of the school. Photo by Shenzhen Business Journal reporter Chen Ximing

In the 19th century, there was a man from Shandong named Wu Xun, who through begging "accumulated money to buy land and build charitable schools," and received widespread praise. Today, in Bao'an District of Shenzhen, there is also a modern-day Wu Xun - Zhao Weiming, a "Senior Shenzhener" over 60 years old. For more than a decade, he has invested over 20 million yuan to found the Migrant Workers' Children School called "Weimin Primary School," even going so far as to demolish his own factory and residential buildings, selling his Mercedes-Benz car and villa. Education professionals in Bao'an District respectfully refer to Zhao Weiming as the modern-day Wu Xun.

By Shenzhen Business Journal reporter Chen Guanglin, with correspondents Zhou Haibin and Zhang Luyu

Zhao Weiming is from Shijian, Shenzhen. The Weimin Primary School he founded covers an area of over 10,000 square meters, with a construction area of more than 7,000 square meters. Real estate developers have offered prices exceeding 100 million yuan to purchase it, but Zhao Weiming remains unmoved. Despite having assets worth hundreds of millions, he chooses to live in a flat of just over 70 square meters.

The Weimin Primary School was built according to the highest standards for facilities, equipment, and teacher recruitment, yet its fees are among the lowest in similar schools. As Zhao Weiming himself puts it, it's like "high-end decoration with food stall pricing." His goal is simple: because "the transient flowers also need sunshine and rain."

Zhao Weiming's educational benevolence has benefited thousands of migrant workers' children and has drawn attention from society. Yesterday, our reporter specially visited Weimin Primary School to understand Zhao Weiming's educational journey.

Seeing that children had no school to attend, 50-year-old Zhao Weiming started an education project.

Speaking about Zhao Weiming's establishment of the school, it dates back to 1998. That year, Zhao Weiming was 50 years old and worked as a manager at the Shijian Power Supply Office. As a native "Senior Shenzhener," he owned around 100 mu (about 6.67 hectares) of land on Shijian Street and a 3,000-square-meter factory building. Just this one factory alone brought him nearly 40,000 yuan in monthly rent. Even if he did nothing, he could live a worry-free life. This was also the lifestyle of many of his neighbors: playing cards, drinking tea, and living leisurely.

Shenzhen developed rapidly, attracting more and more migrant workers who brought their children. Every day, these children either aimlessly wandered the streets or helped with work in the vegetable fields. Most of them were of school age. "Why don't they go to school?" Zhao Weiming repeatedly asked these children and their parents, and the answer was always: "There are no schools." Indeed, at that time, Shenzhen's public services were still insufficient, lacking enough educational resources to meet the needs of the rapidly increasing number of school-age children.

The offspring of migrant workers are like transient flowers; they also need sunshine and rain. A desire to start an educational project began to sprout in Zhao Weiming's heart. However, starting an educational institution is easier said than done. How to start an educational project was something Zhao Weiming himself didn't know much about, especially since he hadn't received much formal education. When it came to founding a school, he was completely clueless. But in Zhao Weiming's view, many difficult things become simpler when broken down. A school? Isn't it just a matter of having a venue, equipment, teachers, and students? Venue? Didn't he already have a factory? Zhao Weiming made a decision and emptied out the factory that brought him nearly 40,000 yuan in monthly income, turning it into classrooms. Equipment? He bought some basic items first. No teachers? He recruited them, believing that offering good benefits would naturally attract top talent. Soon, more than 20 teachers successfully joined the team.

With everything ready except for enrollment, the next step was recruiting students. In reality, Zhao Weiming could have set higher tuition fees. First, his school was the first and only private school in the area. Public schools had high thresholds, making it difficult for migrant workers' children to enroll. If they wanted to study, they had to come to his school. Second, he had invested significantly in school construction. The loss of factory rent was substantial each year, and teacher salaries were set at higher levels. Only charging high tuition could compensate for his investment. But Zhao Weiming didn't do that. He hadn't forgotten the original purpose of founding the school - so that migrant workers' children could attend school. The reason he named the school "Weimin" (For the People) was precisely for this reason. Therefore, he set the lowest possible tuition fee - 650 yuan per semester. Suddenly, more than 350 students enrolled, almost all of them being children of migrant workers from all over the country.

On the opening day, Zhao Weiming looked at the children standing neatly under the national flag and was deeply moved. "I touched the head of every child." That moment left an indelible impression on him. Zhao Weiming fully understood that bringing these children to school meant taking on responsibility and making sacrifices. To ensure that children from farther away wouldn’t be late for class, he spent money buying nearly 300 bicycles to give to the children for free; when a child’s family faced difficulties, he provided consolation gifts and funds; if a child owed tuition or even graduated without paying it off, he would never chase after payment and might even subsidize their living expenses.

As the school improved, his own living space became smaller.

After the first batch of students enrolled, Zhao Weiming continued further along the path of establishing and developing education. The school was up and running, but Zhao Weiming wasn't satisfied with the current situation. Looking back at old photos today, the scenes from the 1999 Children's Day cultural performance remain vivid in his memory. The neatly lined-up children singing stood on large plastic colored strips, beneath which lay soil.

At that time, the school didn’t even have a cement playground; the plastic strips served as the colorful tracks for the students. But the dust raised during sunny days and the mud on rainy days greatly affected the children's learning and activities. Moreover, the classrooms converted from factories and the rudimentary teaching equipment were all hard for Zhao Weiming to accept. From then on, Weimin School entered a period of rapid development.

In 2002, Zhao Weiming demolished the factory and built the Lide Building on the same site. In 2004, he tore down three old houses belonging to his family and raised funds to build the Lizhi Building.

With the development of Shenzhen's economy, property prices and rents in Shijian were rising. Many neighbors took advantage of every opportunity to build "handshake buildings" for rental income. But Zhao Weiming uniquely chose to demolish his own home to build a school. After the teaching buildings were constructed, he decided to build a high-standard playground. To build the playground, land was needed. A plot large enough for a 200-meter track belonged to more than 30 households. Thus, Zhao Weiming went house by house to negotiate, purchasing the land piece by piece. The smallest piece of land was only the size of half a single bed. Eventually, he managed to acquire the land, build the playground, and allow the children to happily participate in physical education classes.

Whenever Zhao Weiming had money in hand, he would think about how to make the school better. For this purpose, this local person who was not particularly wealthy ended up investing more than 20 million yuan. These funds included compensation for the approximately 90 mu of land seized from his family, income from renting out the factory over the years, and proceeds from selling cars and villas. In 2008, to provide better learning conditions for the children, Weimin Primary School aimed to be established as a district-level school. To raise funds, Zhao Weiming sold his Mercedes-Benz car. In 2009, when establishing it as a city-level school, Old Zhao invested another 3 million yuan, building 26 function classrooms and equipping all 18 classrooms in the Lizhi Building with electronic whiteboards. Just the computer room with brand-new LCD computers cost him between 600,000 and 700,000 yuan. Last year, a 1,000-square-meter plot of land adjacent to the school was available for transfer. Many people acted swiftly, and Zhao Weiming quickly spent more than 4 million yuan to purchase it as a biological garden for the school. To do so, he sold his villa, liquidating more than 3 million yuan and shouldering a debt of over 1 million yuan. Now, his entire family lives in an old flat of just over 70 square meters.

After painstakingly running the school for 13 years and investing more than 20 million yuan, Zhao Weiming has not made a profit. The school now owns 10,086 square meters of land and 7,252 square meters of construction. Developers have offered prices exceeding 100 million yuan hoping to acquire it. Why doesn't Zhao Weiming feel tempted? In response to this question from the reporter, Zhao Weiming answered: Since the students have been admitted, he must take responsibility for them and cannot close the school for profit. He sighed and told the reporter that he can only continue down the path of running the school.

Building the school into a home for the children of migrant workers

Precisely because Zhao Weiming does not treat running a school as a business, he offers very generous benefits to the teachers, attracting more than 50 excellent teachers, including nine senior teachers, providing quality education for the children. The average salary of the teachers at Weimin Primary School reaches 2,376 yuan. Moreover, teacher apartments are provided for free accommodation. Even those who choose to live outside receive a monthly subsidy of 200 yuan.

Also, because Zhao Weiming does not treat running a school as a business, after completing all this work, he still sets the lowest possible tuition fees because these children's families are not well-off. Zhao Weiming told the reporter that last year, the Shenzhen Welfare Lottery Center selected Weimin Primary School as a donation target, providing 500,000 yuan in assistance. The Welfare Lottery Center conducted a survey and learned that the parents of the school's students mainly worked as recyclers, waiters, factory workers, street vendors, vegetable sellers, and construction workers, with an average monthly income of around 1,000 yuan.

Currently, the number of students at Weimin School has reached 1,496, making it a prestigious school in the area. "Other private schools have to recruit students everywhere, but here we are always packed, and we have to persuade latecomers to go to other schools," Zhao Weiming said proudly when talking about the social recognition the school has received.

Weimin School also exudes a sense of home. Principal Xiao Hui was one of the first teachers hired by the school. She progressed from a Chinese language teacher to the director of moral education and eventually became an independent principal. Gradually, people tried to poach her, offering tempting benefits and conditions, but she remained unmoved. This was because she had grown accustomed to the strong sense of home at Weimin School. "Every year during the Spring Festival, the chairman invites the teachers staying at the school to his home for New Year's Eve dinner, and his wife personally cooks meals for us," she said. Pregnant female teachers always receive special care. "The chairman's wife will personally stew chickens, and there are never fewer than five chickens, which is very touching." Such an atmosphere keeps the teaching staff stable at Weimin School, greatly promoting the improvement of teaching standards.

Each year, Weimin Primary School organizes a group of teachers for training. "Some private schools worry that organizing teacher training may lead to improved skills and subsequent resignations," Xiao Hui said. "This concern is understandable, but at Weimin School, under the influence of Chairman Zhao Weiming's personal charisma, despite numerous challenges ahead, the teaching staff remains extremely stable, and none of the teachers sent for training have left. Currently, among the 62 faculty members, more than 94% have a college degree or higher."

Many details in the campus of Weimin School reflect Zhao Weiming's educational philosophy. On the Lide Building, a banner hangs saying, "You have the best future ahead of you. Study hard! Knowledge is power." Although Zhao Weiming hasn't read much, he strives to teach the children that knowledge is power and that knowledge can change fate.

To educate the children on how to be good people, when selecting marble panels for the railings, he specifically chose black and white colors, symbolizing clarity between right and wrong, urging the students of Weimin School to be discerning individuals. He also personally made dozens of bird nests, hanging them on trees around the playground and in the zoo to provide homes for transient birds. On a tree with a bird nest, the reporter also saw a wooden board with the words, "As long as you try, the birds will also have a home."

"Our chairman has persisted and strived for 13 years with the hope of building the school into a home for the children of migrant workers," Principal Xiao Hui told the reporter. "Although the chairman is not good at expressing himself, righteousness, kindness, simplicity, and great love are perfectly embodied in him. In his eyes, every student is his child."

Today, walking around Weimin School, the beautiful teaching buildings and high-quality tracks easily evoke thoughts of pride and happiness for the children within. Whether it's the joyful children on the playground or the resonant reading voices in the classrooms, these are the treasures that Zhao Weiming values most.
