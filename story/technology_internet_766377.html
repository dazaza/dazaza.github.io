A Detailed Analysis of the 8 Highlights and 6 Regrets from Apple's Launch Event

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:37:54

At the early hours of September 11, Apple finally unveiled the not-so-mysterious veil of the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s. The overall impression was more of a shock without joy. However, the shock was only limited to confirming a few key points. Without further ado, let's take a look at our summary of the highlights and regrets of this Apple event.

1. Two new iPhones launched simultaneously

In a strict sense, this Apple event actually brought three new products. However, the 8GB version of the iPhone 4s is merely a capacity change, and there’s not much to say about its price of 3288 yuan. Personally, its 3.5-inch screen is outdated in today's era of large screens. By the way, starting today, Apple has changed the last letter of the names of its on-sale iPhone models to lowercase.

Apple introduced two new phones in the iPhone 5 series: the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s. This clearly differs from Apple's previous pace of releasing one iPhone per year. Launching two models simultaneously is undoubtedly a more reasonable strategy given the increasingly rapid changes in hardware specifications.

2. Finally playing with color seduction

Whether it's the three colors of the iPhone 5s or the five colors of the iPhone 5c, the number of options far exceeds that of previous iPhone products. The officially released protective cases for both models come in six different colors. Color itself is also a major selling point of the iPhone 5c. A more reasonable interpretation of "c" would be "color" rather than "cheap," as its domestic price starting at 4488 yuan is certainly not cheap. As for whether the iPhone 5c is expensive or not, comparing it with other brands holds little significance. However, compared to the discontinued and soon-to-be-liquidated iPhone 5, the body shell has been changed to plastic but features multiple bright colors, with no difference in functionality and configuration, while the launch price (domestic) is 800 yuan cheaper.

The iPhone 5s comes in gold and space gray, with the black version being discontinued, while the silver version continues to be available. Among the new colors, gold is expected to sell well, especially in the Chinese market. Although the appearance of the iPhone 5S has not changed much compared to the iPhone 5, the colors make it easy to distinguish between the two products, giving buyers of the latest iPhone 5s a natural sense of superiority.

3. China region launches simultaneously for the first time

Apple CEO Tim Cook's three visits to China have achieved a historic breakthrough, with the first simultaneous launch of new iPhones in the Chinese market, something even Steve Jobs did not accomplish. The significance of launching iPhones in the Chinese market is self-evident and is one of the best ways for Apple to demonstrate its sincerity through action.

The new iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s will be available for pre-order after 6 PM on September 13, with the fastest delivery expected by September 20 at Apple retail stores. Versions compatible with China Unicom and China Telecom are also expected to be available at the same time. In terms of pricing, the domestic versions of the iPhone 5c (16GB/32GB) are priced at 4488 yuan and 5288 yuan respectively, while the iPhone 5s (16GB/32GB/64GB) versions are priced at 5288 yuan, 6088 yuan, and 6888 yuan respectively.

4. China Mobile version arrives

After years of negotiations, Apple and China Mobile are finally collaborating. Although the details won't be confirmed until the Apple China event at 10 AM on September 11, information from the Apple Hong Kong website already reveals key details: the iPhone 5c model A1529 and the iPhone 5s model A1530 both support TD-LTE and FDD-LTE networks, with TD-LTE being China Mobile's 4G standard. Given that the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology license hasn’t been issued yet, it’s normal for the China Mobile versions of the iPhone 5s/5c to be delayed.

As for who convinced whom between Apple and China Mobile, there will likely be various interpretations and stories later. All I can say is that this collaboration is a significant benefit for both parties.

5. Added fingerprint recognition feature

Although fingerprint recognition has been used in some phones for many years, it has never been widely adopted. With Apple's current influence, introducing fingerprint recognition on the latest iPhone 5s has significant implications for accelerating industry progress. It is foreseeable that more brands and products will follow suit.

Apple named its new fingerprint sensor Touch ID, which can be used for phone unlocking and app purchases, supporting 360-degree fingerprint reading and recognition of multiple fingerprints, thus enhancing both security and convenience. Of course, everything has pros and cons. The integration of Touch ID into the Home button may increase its frequency of use, raising concerns about its lifespan.

6. Processor upgrade

The iPhone 5s is equipped with the latest A7 processor, with the central processing unit and graphics processor performance both doubling that of the A6 processor. In addition to being faster, the A7 chip makes the iPhone 5s the world's first 64-bit smartphone, simply meaning that its performance is closer to that of a PC.

With the use of the A7 processor, the iPhone 5s also features a new M7 coprocessor, specifically designed to measure motion data from accelerometers, gyroscopes, and compasses, thereby reducing the workload on the A7 processor and decreasing network monitoring, ultimately saving battery life. Whether the actual power-saving effect is noticeable still requires further testing, as there is no significant improvement in battery life for the iPhone 5s.

7. Camera optimization

The camera configuration and shooting functions of the iPhone 5 were not top-of-the-line, but they provided excellent user experience and image quality. The pixel count of the latest iPhone 5s camera remains unchanged, but the iSight camera sensor size increases by 15%, allowing each pixel to grow (reaching 1.5 microns), combined with an increased aperture of f/2.2, enabling the camera to capture more light, thereby improving nighttime shooting capabilities.

The LED lights on the back of the iPhone 5s have changed to two, a clear difference from the iPhone 5. Through a white LED and a yellow LED flash, the True Tone flash of the iPhone 5s works with smart algorithms to combine over 1000 variations of flash intensity and color temperature, providing better lighting effects for objects requiring flash assistance.

Additionally, the iPhone 5s is currently the only Apple device offering burst mode, capable of capturing up to 10 photos per second; there's also a new slow-motion video function that can record 720p videos at 120 frames per second, then play them back at 1/4 speed.

8. More details on iOS 7

After the event, the iOS 7 GM version (final developer test version) was finally released. Following the domestic version, it is reported that this update gave the Hong Kong version a nine-key input method and added a large number of built-in ringtones and wallpapers. The official iOS 7 version will be available for download on September 18.

The iPhone 5s equipped with the 64-bit A7 processor has a pure 64-bit iOS 7 version and native apps. However, most third-party applications are still developed using 32-bit architecture, so the benefits of 64-bit cannot be fully realized within a certain period.

After discussing the eight highlights, let's now examine the six regrets.

1. Confidentiality work deteriorates instead of improves

Previously, Apple's product confidentiality was quite effective, but since the Cook era, it has become worse. This time, both the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s were completely leaked in terms of model, configuration, and design. Many people who stayed up all night to watch the live broadcast expressed: "There was no surprise."

In addition to affecting itself, the early leaks give competitors ample time to respond, which is undoubtedly disadvantageous for Apple, a company that focuses on creating premium products.

2. Screen size and resolution remain unchanged

As Apple's annual product, the iPhone 5s maintains the same screen size and resolution as the iPhone 5, namely 4 inches with 1136x640 pixels. Although a 4-inch screen allows the iPhone 5s to be operated with one hand in more situations, its screen size lags behind the mainstream products of other brands. J and Wong from Meizu commented: "The biggest weakness of the iPhone is still the small screen; browsing and reading on such a small screen has become unbearable for my eyes."

Besides, the ppi of the iPhone hasn't changed in three years and doesn't even reach the 720p level, which is the standard for mid-range Android phones. For a leading brand in the industry, this upgrade speed is too slow.

3. Classic colors abandoned

The new iPhone 5s and 5c, despite featuring multiple colors, coincidentally lack the black version. Perhaps Apple made this decision after extensive research, but it is still regrettable. Black has always been a timeless trend color and pairs well with accessories.

Another theory is that the paint peeling issue (especially on black iPhones) prompted Apple to sacrifice black to avoid further peeling problems. Whether the iPhone 5s solves the paint peeling issue remains to be seen.

4. Camera pixel count not upgraded

Although the iPhone 5s claims to improve its photography performance and functions, it hasn't upgraded the lens pixel count, and the front-facing camera remains at 1.2 million pixels. Stagnation in this key and easily understandable metric may affect consumer perception, as many ordinary consumers still evaluate based on camera pixel counts. Facing a wave of 13 million pixel or higher cameras, the 8 million pixel iPhone 5s seems relatively thin.

5. Still no NFC

The iPhone 5s introduces the less popular fingerprint recognition but does not follow up on the increasingly hot NFC technology for either model. Similar to the restrictions on iOS Bluetooth functionality, it appears closed-off. NFC (Near Field Communication) is a short-distance high-frequency wireless communication technology that allows non-contact point-to-point data transmission (within ten centimeters) between electronic devices, making data exchange fast and convenient.

Without NFC and with restricted Bluetooth transfer, exchanging data between unjailbroken iOS devices and other smart system devices remains cumbersome.

6. iPhone 5 becomes the shortest-lived Apple phone

Apart from the main introductions of the iPhone 5c and iPhone 5s, the classic iPhone 4s now features an 8GB version, available for pre-order starting today, with delivery in 7-10 working days. However, the iPhone 4 has faded from the stage, and more heartbreakingly, the iPhone 5 has also been removed from the Apple online store, with less than a year from launch to discontinuation. The once "best iPhone ever" and "fastest-selling model in Apple history" has unfortunately become the shortest-lived Apple phone so far.

As for why the iPhone 5 was discontinued so early, there were mainly two theories before: one was the continued issue of paint peeling, and the other was poor yield rates of its embedded (in-cell) touch panels. With new products available, Apple naturally ended its lifecycle earlier.
