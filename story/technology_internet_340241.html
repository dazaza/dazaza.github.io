Ningbo Bank's vice president Qiu Shaozhong's sky-high annual salary of 6.16 million yuan causes public outrage

by xia2oo007 on 2010-04-05 14:20:57

Spring is the season for sowing, as well as reaping. Sowing will lead to reaping. On the last day of March, Nanjing Bank's annual financial report was released without much fanfare. Adding to this, Ningbo Bank also unveiled its annual results at the end of February, making two city commercial banks that have revealed their annual performances.

Overall, in 2009, both banks significantly expanded their deposits and loans. The expansion of asset scale drove the increase in management expenses, while the rise in the management expense ratio showed high costs during the enterprise’s expansion process. Moreover, in Nanjing Bank's annual report, it was discovered that ten executives earned over a million yuan annually, similar to Ningbo Bank.

Nanjing Bank experienced rapid expansion, with deposit growth reaching 63%. According to Nanjing Bank's annual report data, the bank saw a 67% increase in loans and a 63% increase in deposits in 2009. Both the loan-to-deposit growth rates were significantly higher than the industry average, ranking first among listed banks. Among these, personal deposits reached 13.558 billion yuan, an increase of 28.69%, personal loan balances were 8.035 billion yuan, an increase of 72.88%, debit card issuance increased by 16.48%, and credit card issuance grew by 104.35%. CITIC Securities stated in a research report that Nanjing Bank's strategy of scale expansion was evident, with general loans replacing bills and deposit term structure leading to a sequential increase in net interest margin, and a 14% sequential increase in net interest income.

Analyst Cheng Jiaoyi from Qilu Securities said that the most outstanding aspect of Nanjing Bank's annual report was the scale expansion, with deposits growing by 63%, compared to only 45.3% for Ningbo Bank. Especially within Jiangsu Province, the expansion was significant. Unlike other banks where assets drive liabilities, Nanjing Bank’s liabilities drive its assets. In terms of branch expansion, relative to Beijing Bank and Ningbo Bank, Nanjing Bank's expansion pace in 2009 was faster. Depending on different positioning and approval strategies, Nanjing Bank emphasizes provincial branch expansion, whereas Beijing Bank and Ningbo Bank prioritize out-of-province branches with provincial ones as auxiliary.

Industry insiders noted that although both city commercial banks claim to target small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), their expansion processes are clearly different. Nanjing Bank focuses on state-owned enterprises, relying more on government support. Additionally, manufacturing processing plants in cities like Nantong, Taizhou, and Suzhou within Jiangsu Province provide Nanjing Bank with abundant resource advantages. Given the regional resource advantages, Nanjing Bank prioritizes provincial expansion.

In contrast, Ningbo Bank relies on import-export foreign trade and values SME deposits and loans. According to the formula for asset scale, which states that the growth of interest-earning asset size multiplied by the net interest margin equals the growth of net interest income, Ningbo Bank's loan-to-deposit ratio has consistently been higher than Nanjing Bank's in recent years. From the perspective of business expansion, the three banks—Nanjing Bank, Beijing Bank, and Ningbo Bank—have similar expansion trends in the same regions such as Shanghai, all adopting a prudent approach. Although each bank may consider different locations, there isn't much differentiation between branches in the same region.

Nanjing Bank's wealth management income tripled, but analysts say intermediary services lack an advantage. By comparing the two annual reports, the Wealth Management Journal reporter found that in 2009, Nanjing Bank's net income from intermediary services accounted for 9.05% of operating net income, an increase of 2.23 percentage points from 2008. Among these, intermediary service revenue from wealth management business reached 41.58 million yuan, 3.2 times that of 2008. Additionally, advisory consulting fees increased by 49.5% to 131.191 million yuan, becoming the largest item of intermediary service income, agency business fees increased by 38.3% to 130.574 million yuan, settlement business grew by 153%, generating 5.117 million yuan in revenue. These project increases brought the overall fee income to 355 million yuan, up 52% year-on-year, with net fee income at 328 million yuan, up 49% year-on-year.

Chen Xi, a researcher at First Entrepreneur Research Institute, said that for a regional bank, retail business and bank scale are two basic standards for measuring intermediary services. Although Nanjing Bank's increase of 2.23 percentage points in the proportion of net income from intermediary services and a 49% year-on-year increase in net fee income rose quickly, overall it still lacks a significant advantage. This is because intermediary services mainly consist of fee income and financial commissions, which are related to the completeness of outlets. The more extensive and dense the outlet distribution, the more settlement fees will be generated, indicating a more complete range of business licenses.

Conversely, during the same period of high-scale expansion, Ningbo Bank's fee and commission income in 2009 was 521 million yuan, up 21.39% year-on-year. It issued 58 wealth management products, with cumulative sales reaching 4.243 billion yuan, and the number of active personal online banking customers reached 94,775. Achieving such results under relatively weak regional advantages and limited scale is indeed commendable.

For the outlook on intermediary services in 2010, both banks expressed emphasis on developing intermediary services. However, through analyzing the annual reports, it was found that the loan-to-deposit ratios of the two banks in 2009 differed significantly. As of the end of 2009, Ningbo Bank's loan-to-deposit ratio was 74%, much higher than Nanjing Bank's 64%.

Cheng Jiaoyi, analyst at Qilu Securities, mentioned that Nanjing Bank operates at low leverage, with most funds allocated to bonds. If aiming for a balanced balance sheet structure, fewer assets would be placed in loans, with nearly 30% of assets already invested in bonds, a very high proportion compared to other banks.

Ten executives earn over a million, the two banks are identical. The release of the first bank annual report in 2010 immediately drew attention when it was revealed that Vice President Qiu Shaozhong of Ningbo Bank earned 6.16 million yuan last year. Apart from Qiu Shaoshong, ten senior executives of Ningbo Bank earned over a million yuan, just like Nanjing Bank, where exactly ten executives also earned over a million yuan.

Regarding the criticism of unexpectedly high executive compensation, industry analysts noted that Nanjing Bank's rapid scale growth made it difficult to compress employee costs. In 2009, Nanjing Bank's business expenses increased by 47%, and employee expenses increased by 36%, compared to a scale growth of 60%. Considering giving shareholders satisfactory returns, salaries exceeding a million yuan are not excessively high. Specifically, Nanjing Bank's cost-to-income ratio rose sharply in 2009, with the full-year cost-to-income ratio being 31%, six percentage points higher than in 2008. From the quarterly view of 2009, the cost-to-income ratio climbed rapidly from low to high, mainly due to the fast increase in business expenses and employee expenses.

From the cost expenditure perspective, Nanjing Bank's total administrative expense expenditure was 1.138 billion yuan, increasing by 39.3%, with an administrative expense rate of 31.4%, rising by six percentage points. Employee compensation reached 618 million yuan, increasing by 36%. The increase in the administrative expense rate shows the high cost incurred during the company's expansion process.

The analyst believed that for Ningbo Bank, if the vice president's salary exceeds that of the president, it might be due to relatively high incomes when they were recruited. From the perspective of the bank's operational efficiency and value assessment, this is justified. However, if there hasn't been a general salary increase for employees, the situation where executives earn significantly more could indicate some employees' costs have risen faster. (Zhang Li, Retail Banking Lab Researcher, Wealth Management Weekly)
