Love Needs to Warm Each Other

He finally met her. In the chilly winter of December, she said she felt cold and wanted to go to his city. Then, when the midnight train at 12 o'clock let out a long whistle, he saw her on the quiet platform among the scattered crowd, wearing a sky-blue fleece coat, with a pink scarf around her neck, carrying a faded light blue travel bag on her back, and wearing elevated white sneakers on her feet. She looked at him from afar, gazing steadily for several seconds. He saw her melancholy eyes filled with joyous sorrow as she walked towards him, meeting her tearful eyes.

As he approached her, she suddenly turned around and started running. He was shocked but instinctively began chasing after her. He ran faster than her and soon caught up. She struggled to run with her heavy backpack. By the time he reached her, she was already panting heavily and sweating slightly on her forehead. As he got closer, he could clearly hear her rapid breathing. Spreading his arms, he hugged her tightly from behind, whispering in her ear with a slight gasp: "Ruo, please stop running, okay? We won't be separated." He heard her soft crying, and her body began to tremble violently.

He took her to her residence, filling a tub with hot water for her. He knew she liked soaking herself in hot water. Then he handed her a set of sky-blue down pajamas. She took the clothes he offered, smiling lightly at him before turning and closing the door. Hearing the sound of water moving, he went into the room and lay down on the bed, starting to daydream quietly.

They had been in love for three months, through an online romance. When he first met her, she was a girl suffering from severe depression. He unintentionally entered her online space and read many of her articles. His heart tightened painfully. Later, every day, he often visited her space multiple times, leaving messages each time. At first, she ignored him and didn't talk to him, continuing to write articles day after day. He didn't mind, still regularly visiting her space to read her articles and leave comments. Her space always attracted many visitors, but few left messages. He understood that everyone was just a passerby, often indifferent to others' pain. But she made him feel deeply sympathetic.

After a month, she gradually stopped rejecting him. Occasionally, she would chat with him or joke with him a little. However, she still rarely smiled. For some reason, he could read her emotions from her writing. There were no smiles in her words or speech. He hoped she would be happy, so every day, he would find beautiful pictures for her because she said she was editing a beautiful fairy tale story that needed beautiful pictures. Sometimes, he would imitate animal and insect sounds to make her laugh, hearing her giggling made his heart sweet.

Their relationship was established suddenly, without any warning. One day, they were chatting on QQ. Suddenly, she paused for a long time without responding. He thought she might have become unhappy again and didn't want to talk. Just as he was thinking about how to cheer her up, she replied. She said she wanted to be his girlfriend. A simple sentence, followed by a period. On the other side of the screen, he saw this sentence and without hesitation typed a "yes" in response, with a happy smile on his face. She was a bit domineering, and he liked it.

Suddenly feeling warmth on his forehead, he opened his eyes to realize he had fallen asleep while daydreaming. And she, not knowing when, had already finished bathing and sat next to him, stroking his head with her hand. After taking a bath, her whole body smelled of mint, which he loved, her hair slightly damp, and there was a faint smile on the corner of her mouth. He looked at her, spacing out again. After a while, he came to his senses, got up, held her on one side of the bed, covered her well with the quilt, turned off the light, climbed onto the other side of the bed, held her in his arms, and softly said: "Sleep now, Ruo. You won't be cold anymore. Be good. Sleep well." He gently kissed her forehead, and she really fell asleep peacefully in his arms, without insomnia.

She often said it was cold every day, and she couldn't sleep. So he frequently stayed up late texting with her, quietly talking with her. Now, looking at her, he felt full of energy in both body and mind. He was willing to hold her like this forever, never letting her suffer from insomnia again.

He recalled their shared moments together. He didn't know what the future would bring them, but he would never let go of her hand. He would hold her hand and walk with her until the end of their days. He thought quietly, satisfied and happy, closing his eyes and falling into a deep sleep.
