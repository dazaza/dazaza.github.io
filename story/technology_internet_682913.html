Obama's Google+ page 'hijacked' by Chinese netizens

by abcfofas on 2012-02-29 10:31:41

Recently, the US President Obama who has just been authenticated on the social network Google+ encountered an unexpected event-his Google+ account became the focus of clicks, posts and comments of hundreds of Chinese netizens. Although most posts and comments were mainly jokes or flooding, this phenomenon still aroused intense interest of American netizens and media. Some media joked that these enthusiastic [bystanders] Chinese netizens launched a [possessing Obama] movement. Zhou Ning, Dean of the School of Liberal Arts at Guangzhou University, said in an interview that the President of the United States is the leader of the world's largest economy, Chinese netizens communicate with Obama not out of other motives, but with an entertainment attitude to find an opportunity to exchange with him in the virtual space, which also became a hot incident on the Internet. In addition, such enthusiasm for posting would not be as serious as Eastern media worried, causing network paralysis, and most netizens had no malice.

According to the U.S. Fox News Network and The Washington Post and other media reports on January 26, many Chinese netizens first discovered Obama's Google+ account on January 20, then launched an online [occupation], reaching its peak on January 24 and 25, making almost all the messages on Obama's home page in Chinese. Even if there were a few English messages interspersed, the names were actually Chinese pinyin. Someone joked that Obama's home page was successfully [occupied] by hundreds of Chinese netizens, effortlessly turning it into a Chinese version.

Google+ is a community service system launched by Google last year, aiming to compete with the well-known social networking site [Facebook]. Due to Google's previous announcement of exiting the mainland China market, this product has little influence in mainland China. Social media once played a great role in helping Obama win the 2008 presidential election, so he paid great attention to social networks. Obama, who is striving hard for re-election, had his Google+ account authenticated on January 30. American media commented that Obama hoped to win more voters' support through the Internet, but unexpectedly, his threshold was trampled by Chinese netizens first.

When logging into Obama's Google+ account home page on January 27, it was found that most Chinese netizens leaving messages for Obama were just joking around or talking about some light-hearted topics. For example, someone asked him for a green card. Someone indicated that they wanted to rent out the comment section as advertising space. Some netizens wrote, [asking for free meals], or [President, hello, I will treat you to dinner when you come to China]. Some people cared, [Mr. President, are these posts ghostwritten?], [Will such high popularity help in the next presidential campaign?] There were also netizens who wrote, [Mr. President, I want to teach you Chinese, so you can understand all the posts. Consider it, you can send me a private message, thank you]. Some finally managed to say a word, exclaiming: [Finally got a parking spot].

It was noticed that due to too many commentators, each post reached the upper limit of 500 replies and could no longer be commented on. Therefore, some Chinese netizens said: [Freedom of speech, why not allow comments anymore? You still boast of freedom!] But then a netizen replied: [After checking, it seems that the maximum number of comments is 500, beyond this number, no more comments can be made. It's not set by Obama.]

The news that Obama's social networking site homepage was [watched] and [occupied] by Chinese netizens aroused great interest from the Western media, with some media using [flooded in] to describe Chinese netizens. The British Broadcasting Corporation accompanied its report on this news with a photo of Obama's visit to the Great Wall in China in 2009. The Associated Press reported that the phrase [occupying Obama] was inspired by the term [occupy Wall Street]. However, the [occupation] behavior also raised concerns among some people. Some said that China has the largest number of netizens in the world, and in the future, if a large number of Chinese netizens log in to American social networking sites, the US will have to do everything possible to protect its own websites to avoid collapse.

It was learned that on social networking sites [Facebook] and Twitter, Obama's friends respectively reached 25 million and 12 million. Many netizens understood the whereabouts and opinions of their friend Obama from these social networking sites. However, Obama publicly admitted in 2009 that he actually never personally operated Twitter, and all the [tweets] were pushed up by a team of secretaries.
