Basic concepts of stocks

by weiwei0518 on 2009-01-13 21:45:24

1. Stock Concept www.gupiaowww.cn

A stock is the abbreviation of a share certificate, which is issued by a joint-stock company to shareholders as proof of holding shares and for obtaining dividends and bonuses. It is a kind of security.

Each stock represents the shareholder's ownership of one basic unit of the enterprise. Stocks are part of the capital composition of a joint-stock company, which can be transferred, bought, or mortgaged at a valuation, serving as a primary long-term credit instrument in the capital market.

2. Characteristics of Stocks

Stock investment is a long-term investment without a fixed term. Once stocks are purchased, as long as the issuing company exists, no stockholder can withdraw their shares, meaning they cannot request the return of their principal from the issuing company.

Similarly, the shareholder status and rights of the stockholder cannot change, but they can sell their stocks through the stock trading market, transferring their shares to other investors to recover their original investment.

3. Functions of Stocks

(1) After listing, the listed company becomes an investment target for the general public, making it easier to absorb savings funds from the public and expanding the sources of financing.

(2) After listing, the equity of the listed company is distributed among millions of investors of varying sizes. This equity dispersion effectively avoids the danger of the company being solely controlled by a few shareholders, granting the company greater operational freedom.

(3) The announcements of stock prices and regular financial statements by the stock exchange for listed companies serve as advertising effects, effectively enhancing the recognition and credibility of listed companies.

4. Par Value of Stocks

The par value of a stock is the face amount marked on the issued stock by the joint-stock company, expressed in units of yuan per share. Its function is to indicate the capital amount contained in each stock. In China, the par value of stocks traded on the Shanghai and Shenzhen Stock Exchanges is one yuan per share.

One role of the par value is to show the proportion of investment that the stock subscriber holds in the joint-stock company, serving as the basis for determining shareholder rights. For example, if a listed company's total share capital is 1,000,000 yuan, owning one share indicates holding 1/1,000,000 of the company's shares. Another role is to use the par value as a reference for setting the issue price during the initial issuance of stocks. Generally, the issue price of stocks will be higher than its par value. When stocks enter the circulation market, the par value has little relation to the stock price. The stock price depends on how high the investors want to push it.

5. Net Asset Value (NAV) of Stocks

The net asset value (NAV) of a stock, also known as book value or net assets per share, is the asset value per share calculated using accounting methods. It is calculated by dividing the company's net assets (including registered capital, various reserve funds, accumulated profits, etc., excluding liabilities) by the total number of shares, resulting in the NAV per share. The higher the book value of a joint-stock company, the more actual assets owned by the shareholders. Since the book value is the result of financial statistics and calculations, with precise and highly credible data, it serves as an important basis for stock investors to evaluate and analyze the strength of listed companies. Investors should pay attention to this data of listed companies.

6. Issue Price of Stocks

When stocks are issued for listing, the listed company considers its own interests and ensures the success of the stock listing, so the stocks are not issued at their par value but at a more reasonable price. This price is called the issue price of the stock.

7. Market Price of Stocks

The market price of a stock refers to the transaction price agreed upon by both parties during the trading process. Usually, the term "stock price" refers to the market price. The market price directly reflects the trend of the stock market, serving as the basis for investors to buy stocks. Due to the influence of many factors, the market price of stocks is constantly changing. The stock price is a concentrated reflection of the market value of stocks, so this price is also called the stock market price.

8. Liquidation Price of Stocks

The liquidation price of a stock refers to the actual value represented by each share when a joint-stock company goes bankrupt or closes down and undergoes liquidation. Theoretically, the liquidation price per share should be consistent with the book value of the stock. However, during bankruptcy liquidation, the value of the company's assets is calculated based on the actual selling price, and the selling price during asset disposal is usually lower than the actual value. Therefore, the liquidation price of stocks may not be consistent with the net asset value. The liquidation price of stocks is only used as a basis for determining the stock price when the joint-stock company goes bankrupt or loses its legal person status and undergoes liquidation, and has no significance in the issuance and circulation of stocks. !
