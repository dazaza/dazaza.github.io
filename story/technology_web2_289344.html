Traveling to Yilan, how we ended up in tears.

by hartyqekb on 2009-12-02 17:23:57

Abandonment is an elegant turn in life. Abandonment requires courage, but at certain times, a person can only live more meaningfully by protecting oneself; abandonment sometimes is for exchanging a larger space. Perhaps, life itself is a process of continuous abandonment: abandon the carefree childhood to fulfill the expectations of growing up; abandon the beauty of youth to gain the wisdom of maturity; abandon the sweetness of love to exchange for family stability; abandon the allure of applause to exchange for inner peace... Acceptance or not, sometimes there's no choice. Living always comes with a price.

When young, one clings to promises, only to find out later that many promises are just beautiful flowers that will always wither. Love becomes profound because it cannot be possessed; dreams become beautiful because they cannot be fulfilled. Life always carries a beauty with imperfection, a melancholic beauty due to its lack.

It's during the low points of life that people realize who stays by their side, never abandoning them. Suddenly, they notice those previously unnoticed touches of emotion, warming their hearts. Then they understand the simple yet complex truth: God closes a door but always opens a window. Yes, things won't always be suffocating.

No matter what you've experienced or are experiencing, you should realize that the road of life must continue. As long as we don't have the resolve to end our own lives and choose to survive, we can only save ourselves, making ourselves live with as little pain as possible. Boxers on the boxing ring, after being hit hard and knocked down, feel immense pain, perhaps thinking they can't or don't want to get up, hoping the match could stop, hoping to rest like this... But they must get up, whether it's after the referee counts to one or ten, win or lose, they must face it standing up.

Perhaps when facing hardships, we truly feel immense pain and truly want to give up on life. However, we still endure, no matter how tearful and helpless we were at first. Time is the best medicine for all hidden wounds. Slowly, you won't cry anymore; slowly, you'll find things to pass the time; slowly, you'll know what to do tomorrow; slowly, your smile will return; slowly, your life will return to normal; slowly, everything passes.

Life has no eternal pain, no matter how deep the wound, it will eventually heal.

Life has no insurmountable obstacles. You cannot sit by the obstacle waiting for it to disappear; you can only find ways to cross it.

Life has no eternal love, relationships without conclusion will always end; people who cannot be possessed will eventually be forgotten.

We grow up through repeated blows, the weak may collapse under these blows, but the strong become deeply resilient. We should learn from willows, which seem fragile but are resilient, not easily broken by strong winds; overly rigid tree trunks break in the wind.

Learn to let go, learn to bear, learn to be strong, learn to smile, that's another kind of beauty! Appropriate abandonment is an elegant turn in life.
