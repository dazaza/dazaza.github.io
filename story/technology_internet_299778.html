Post-80s entrepreneurship

by ki582ganxi on 2009-12-09 10:05:12

How is the generation of post-80s? I have been thinking about this question repeatedly. In the suspicious eyes of others, we show excessive self-esteem and inferiority complex. We have crossed a **** full of joy, but are facing various invisible pressures.

We have been pampered by our parents all along, but looking back on the road we've walked, there's not a single footprint of our own. We try hard to get rid of our dependence on our parents, but when our parents distance themselves from us a bit, a sense of helplessness comes uninvited.

Among us, many are gold worshipers, and many are idealists. However, it is precisely these so-called "isms" that make our originally clear hearts, capable of distinguishing right from wrong, commit small mistakes continuously in real life without making any major errors.

Rather than holding an easily breakable golden rice bowl, we would rather not take an unbreakable iron rice bowl. I believe many people are like me. With my family background, I could have easily become a civil servant, but I refused to take such a path that would leave no footprints of my own.

I started my business not for money, but just to prove myself. Just this point makes me seem very childish. Maybe gold worshipism is more realistic than idealism, but I have become a thorough idealist.

After graduating from university, I chose to start a business despite my parents' opposition. I rented a house outside and began developing software. Although my parents agreed to give me financial support, I knew they still disapproved of my entrepreneurship. How respectable my parents were, and how detestable I was.

All my living expenses were covered by my parents. Later, my girlfriend resigned from her job in Shenzhen and came to find me after traveling a long distance. My girlfriend thought it was not good to keep asking my parents for money, so she quickly found a job and took on our living expenses. Do you know? It is already a great disgrace for a son in his twenties to be supported by his parents, and for a man to be supported by a woman, what kind of disgrace would that be?

I must endure the disgrace because I can't earn even a cent on my own. Seven months passed, programming is often tedious. I often sleep only five hours a day, sometimes stay awake for two days straight, and going out once every half a month is already a rare event. Later, I developed severe "occupational burnout", but after consulting a psychologist, I improved somewhat.

Enduring hardship and humiliation is the price that must be paid before starting a business. This year will soon be over, and my father has issued the order that I must register a company within this year, otherwise, I must go home obediently and be a civil servant. None of my friends support my entrepreneurship, only I am gritting my teeth to persevere. What I bear is far more than just pressure.

According to the project schedule, I can register a company around October, and I have already selected a satisfactory office building. But you should know, completing the development is just the beginning of entrepreneurship. I don't know what else awaits me ahead. No one teaches me what to do, I can only feel my way forward. I may take many detours, or even be washed away by the river.

I said to myself, if you really want to be strong, then persevere, even if it costs your last ounce of vitality. This is the characteristic of a strong person. He will be almost dead because of setbacks, but every time he recovers, his life fire burns more vigorously, waiting for stronger setbacks to come and becoming stronger with each setback, or... dying proudly.

Registration, decoration, recruitment, management, production, publicity, sales, after-sales service... I still have many roads to walk. If I fall down just because of the mental stress and physical exhaustion of seven months, ha, then I fail too pitifully. Before the waves subside, another wave rises. Three college classmates decided to resign from Shanghai, Wuhan, and Guangzhou respectively to join me. This should be a happy thing, but as their "old class leader", will I ruin their future?

The one from Wuhan is the strongest warrior I want most. In a school programming competition, our trio group finished an hour earlier and still won the first prize, among which his contribution was the greatest. In terms of technology, I have to admit that I am inferior to him in many aspects. After graduation, he participated in the team development of many large projects. Originally having strong technical skills, his experience accumulated, making him even more powerful. If the "development department" is handed over to him, then the situation is settled.

But his original salary was more than 2400 yuan per month, and coming here, I might not be able to pay him half of that for a long time. Although I really want this person, I can't harm my old classmate. I told him the truth, but unexpectedly, he said: "Don't worry, since I have decided to follow you, I haven't planned to live a good life, hehe!" That day, I wanted to cry so much, I really wanted to cry, feeling like a strong hand supporting me from behind just when I was about to fall.

Many people who have started companies know that it's hard to recruit people now, and harder to find talents. Now getting help from a benefactor, it must be said that the lucky star has strongly shone on me once. Is this God's favor towards me? Perhaps it is, God's greatest favor to me is, letting me realize that I have so many people to thank, and I will continue forward with a grateful heart.

A senior once told me: "Do you think being a boss is as easy as on TV? Do you think exploiting others will let you avoid being exploited? Exploitation is everywhere, employees are exploited by bosses, and bosses are exploited by the country. If you want to exploit others, you must first learn to exploit yourself." I think I understand a little of what this means now. Being a boss is often harder than working, and this hardship is exactly the process of me learning to exploit myself.

What is a boss anyway? From a big group to a small fruit stall, regardless of the size of the business, I think there is no essential difference between businessmen. They are all bosses, they all make money relying on their own wisdom and willpower. People often only see the silver in their hands, but never think about the dark days when they started their business. When we complain about fate, do we realize that this is the result of our overly comfortable lives?

After the company opens, I won't worship the god of wealth, I will worship Guan Gong. The reason is simple, in today's society, if you lose credibility, you're worthless. "No businessman is honest" is wrong. I have seen many bosses who base their business on honesty. Any rogue merchant cannot stand firm or go far in competition. I will be honest with customers, honest with employees, and honest with myself.

To all the post-80s entrepreneurs: Regardless of whether your starting point for entrepreneurship is "gold worship" or "idealism", regardless of whether you run a "company" or a "small shop", I hope you can persevere through these darkest days. Don't forget that many people are going through hardships and even humiliations just like you. Perhaps in the near future, some of us will flourish, and some will fail, but these entrepreneurial days will be unforgettable for life. No one will witness our hardships for us, only ourselves. Keep going, wish you good luck, and wish myself good luck!
