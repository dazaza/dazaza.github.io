[You can also apply for an email address with a personalized domain name]

by jay on 2006-10-11 17:41:30

The current situation of "uniform" email addresses used by Internet users is about to change. Thanks to the cooperation between the transnational giant Microsoft and China's CN domain name registration service provider - Enames China, Internet users will be able to register personalized emails with a CN suffix in the future.

Recently, Microsoft and Enames China officially announced the launch of the Chinese version of Live Mail.

Based on Microsoft's email system, users can freely choose a CN domain as their email suffix, and freely activate their own private mailboxes and allocate email accounts for others. Currently, CN domains are the first recommended email domains by Microsoft's LIVE Mail.

In the past, the email addresses used by Internet users were all public emails provided by portal sites or professional email websites with uniform suffixes. This time, the "personalized email" launched by LIVE Mail in conjunction with CN domains has given 120 million Internet users in China 120 million different Internet identities. Each Internet user can name themselves, choose themselves, and define their favorite names as email suffixes. For example, Zhang Xiaoli can own multiple domain names such as xiaoli.cn, lili.cn, etc., and then activate them respectively at Enames China's LIVE Mail. In addition to being able to set up personalized emails like zhang@xiaoli.cn, zhang@lili.cn, users can also arbitrarily allocate emails to others without any limit. Users only need to have one CN domain to get unlimited free LIVE emails from Microsoft. Users only need to log in to live.ename.cn, even if they do not understand any technology, they can easily complete the registration within a few minutes.
