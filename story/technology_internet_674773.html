What should I do if I have bromhidrosis? Five major characteristics of having bromhidrosis:

Generally, bromhidrosis has five characteristics, which can also serve as criteria for judging bromhidrosis.

Characteristics of bromhidrosis:

1. The ear canal is moist or sticky.

2. Axillary sweat is sticky, underwear has an unpleasant smell and turns yellow.

3. Sweat smells heavy and salty after sweating.

4. White or pale yellow secretions on armpit hair.

5. A pungent smell can be detected by others or oneself.

Judging by the time the odor occurs: An odor lasting more than three years may be related to genetics. If bromhidrosis is not treated in time, it may be accompanied by bad breath and foot odor, and can lead to genetic continuation. Severe bromhidrosis may also be accompanied by thin earwax, which can erode the eardrum over time, causing tinnitus and leading to deafness.
