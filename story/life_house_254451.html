Ten Misconceptions about Choosing Underfloor Heating for Winter Renovation

by longersing on 2009-10-31 22:47:30

The indoor environment in winter pays great attention to the heating system. There are ten common misconceptions when choosing radiant floor heating panels for winter decoration. Otherwise, it will make a whole family spend the cold winter in low temperature. The following are these ten misconceptions, hoping to be helpful to everyone.

One, "Is all kind of flooring suitable for floor heating?"

"Can your floor be used as a floor heating panel?" The salesperson answers: "Absolutely no problem, dedicated floor heating panel." Almost every store selling floors gives such a very definite answer, and even shows user examples, some even show inspection reports, so consumers feel that "all kinds of flooring can be used as floor heating panels." In fact, to be suitable for floor heating, four elements must be met: floor heating conductivity, dimensional stability, anti-pollution environmental protection, and service durability. The flooring industry is formulating flooring standards to quantify these four elements and establish quality inspection specifications for flooring installation and warranty periods, making technical indicators for installation and maintenance also quantifiable.

Two, "Is the name 'specialized floor heating panel' correct?"

In the current flooring market, there are not only many types of floor heating panels, but also chaotic names: such as "heat-resistant flooring," "anti-floor heating flooring," "floor heat-resistant flooring," etc., the most commonly called ones are "heating flooring," "floor heating flooring," "heat-resistant flooring," "floor heating flooring," "specialized floor heating flooring," etc. We believe that some names are unscientific, and some seem inappropriate. Currently, especially during the initial development stage of ground radiation heating systems, the names must be standardized and should be called "floor heating panels." Its definition should be: wooden flooring suitable for laying in ground radiation heating systems and transferring heat to the room through the board surface.

Three, "Is thicker flooring better for insulation?"

The thickness of wooden flooring is an absolute factor determining whether the foot feeling is comfortable, so many consumers require a thickness of 15-18mm when selecting flooring, especially when selecting solid wood, multi-layered solid wood flooring or bamboo flooring. Therefore, when selecting floor heating panels, they still use this method to choose, unaware that wood is a poor conductor. The thermal conductivity of wood substances is usually between 0.17-0.34, while water is 0.5. If the flooring is too thick, it will be even more difficult for heat to conduct through the flooring to the board surface, and most of the heat will be consumed in the conduction process. Regarding insulation, since the temperature difference between the top and bottom of the floor is large, it will inevitably lead to significant deformation of the floor, and both "thermal expansion and contraction" and "wet expansion and dry contraction" will cause the floor to warp, twist, bend, and crack, making dimensional stability unreliable.

Four, "Is a wider specification better for floor heating panels?"

Nowadays, consumers buy solid wood flooring not only for comfort but also to highlight their identity. Therefore, when selecting, they often choose larger specification flooring, especially in terms of width, which can reach up to 180mm. Such widths, experts do not recommend users to select them in normal flooring installations, to avoid seams due to moisture expansion and dry shrinkage, let alone under heated conditions where moisture dissipates faster, making it easier for the floor to separate. Therefore, experts suggest that consumers try not to select overly large specification flooring when purchasing floor heating panels.

Five, "Are aluminum foil underlays specifically for floor heating panels?"

This is the most typical misconception. Practice has long proven: in ground radiation heating systems, heat transfer is mainly by radiation, secondly by convection, and lastly by conduction. If aluminum foil is placed on the heat dissipation surface, it forms reverse radiation. Under the same condition of maintaining the surface temperature of the floor heating, the thermal resistance of aluminum foil foam pads is higher, while ordinary foam pads have lower thermal resistance. Clearly, ordinary foam pads have better thermal conduction effects than aluminum foil foam pads, and are more conducive to heat transfer from the floor to the room. Therefore, users should choose ordinary foam pads or materials with small thermal resistance, good moisture resistance, and good corrosion resistance when selecting floor heating panels.

Six, "Can plywood or fine woodworking boards be added under the floor?"

First, laying plywood or fine woodworking boards under floor heating panels is not conducive to heat conduction, invisibly increasing heat loss during the conduction process, and increasing the heating cost of the floor heating system.

Second, if the quality of these boards is not up to standard, it may lead to excessive indoor free formaldehyde content, or due to non-standard moisture content, after laying, the moisture is absorbed by the floor, causing the floor to warp or arch. Moreover, this base material board has more disadvantages than advantages, truly turning cleverness into folly and adding unnecessary complexity. It is even more unacceptable to lay padding under the floor.

Seven, "Can only floating installation methods be used?"

Due to the special structure of the floor heating system, nails cannot be used on cement floors when installing flooring. Therefore, for a long time, most floor heating panels can only be installed using the floating installation method. In fact, with the continuous advancement of technology, there are two other installation methods: adhesive bonding method and joist installation method. Recently, a quick-install joist has appeared on the market. This type of joist installation does not require nails; glue can be used directly to fix the joists on the cement floor, and then the floor can be fixed on the joists through clips on the joists. This not only enhances the foot feel of the floor but also allows air to circulate between the joists, facilitating heat circulation and making the floor heat more evenly, reducing deformation, and improving the thermal efficiency of the room. This is currently a recommended installation method.

Eight, "No floor warming test after installation"

After the installation of floor heating panels, in addition to routine acceptance, the most important thing is to perform a temperature rise acceptance test on the floor. After the floor is laid or when winter heating begins, the first temperature rise of the floor heating system should be gradual. When the base ground reaches 25 degrees Celsius, it should be kept at a constant temperature for 12-24 hours. Afterwards, the temperature should be gradually increased by 1 degree Celsius per hour until the desired indoor temperature is reached. The temperature should not be too high, and the surface temperature of the wooden floor should be less than or equal to 28 degrees Celsius. In a short period, the extreme temperature should be less than or equal to 32 degrees Celsius. The higher the temperature, the greater the heat loss, the shorter the life of the heating system, and the more likely hazards will occur, and the floor is prone to warping, twisting, bending, and cracking deformations.

Nine, "No environmental protection acceptance after floor installation"

Since most of the flooring used in floor heating systems are composite flooring, which inherently has formaldehyde emission issues, and when used as floor heating panels, heating exacerbates formaldehyde release. Therefore, after installing floor heating panels, it is essential to perform environmental protection acceptance to ensure that the free formaldehyde emissions meet the specified standards. To ensure the normal use of the floor, two points must be followed when purchasing: 1. Conduct environmental testing before laying the floor, generally testing is free, treatment requires a fee, and then perform environmental acceptance testing after laying. 2. The environmental protection indicators of the floor and auxiliary materials must meet the standards. Do not prioritize price over environmental protection and buy cheap goods, otherwise, the consequences can be endless.

Ten, "Placing legless combined furniture or carpets on the floor disrupts the floor's heat dissipation surface"

With the continuous improvement of modern people's living standards, people are increasingly pursuing personalized home decoration. As a result, many personalized modular furniture pieces have emerged, which can be freely assembled according to the owner's preferences. However, these furniture pieces are often legless and directly contact the ground. If such furniture, carpets cover large areas of floor heating panels indoors, it will cause the heat to concentrate excessively in certain areas, leading to uneven heating of the floor, causing deformation, wasting heat energy, and lowering the indoor temperature.
