When the object or loaded vehicle is placed on the load-bearing platform, under the force of gravity, the load is transmitted through the load-bearing platform to the weight sensor, causing deformation of the elastic body of the sensor. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance, outputting an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. Then, through A/D conversion, the signal becomes a digital signal, processed by the microprocessor (CPU) of the instrument, and finally displayed as weight data. If configured with a printer, the weight data can be printed and recorded, and if configured with a computer, the measurement data can be input into a computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it's a scale or a balance, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the object's weight into an electrical signal, which is sent to the conditioning circuit, A/D converter, and computational circuit (MCU), and then converted into digital display.
