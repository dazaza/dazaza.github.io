**I. Structure of Refrigerator Molds:**

The mold adopts an I-beam structure. The cavity and core are made from P20, NAK80, and 2738 materials, while the sliders, guide slots, and inserts are made from 2316 or 718. The guide pillars, guide sleeves, gate bushings, pull rods, and reset rods use standard components.

Mold frame: LKM.

Good materials help us save on production cycles, extend mold life, reduce maintenance time and repair frequency, thereby improving production efficiency.

**II. Cooling System Design for Refrigerator Molds:**

Cooling system design must consider both cooling effectiveness and uniformity, as well as the impact of the cooling system on the overall mold structure. A good cooling system can reduce cooling times during injection molding, decrease single-shot molding time, improve production efficiency, and lower production costs.

**III. Maintenance of Refrigerator Molds:**

Maintenance of refrigerator molds is more important than mold repairs. The better the mold is maintained, the longer its lifespan will be; conversely, the more frequent the repairs, the shorter the mold's lifespan.

1. **Daily Maintenance of Refrigerator Molds:** Lubricate all moving parts such as ejector pins, slides, guide pillars, and guide sleeves. Clean the mold surface, and ensure water channels are clear.

2. **Regular Maintenance of Refrigerator Molds:** In addition to daily maintenance, regular maintenance includes cleaning exhaust grooves, adding vents where burn marks occur due to trapped air, and repairing any damaged or worn areas.

3. **External Appearance Maintenance of Refrigerator Molds:** Paint the outside of the mold block to prevent rust. When removing the mold from the injection machine, apply rust-proof oil to the front and back molds. Ensure the mold is tightly closed when stored to prevent dust from entering the cavity.
