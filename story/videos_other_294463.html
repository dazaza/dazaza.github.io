The market continued to rise, and with the initiation of large-cap stocks, there were signs of an accelerated increase in the index. The successful transformation of short-term hotspots has created expectations for the upcoming "2-8" market trend. In terms of operation, special attention can be paid to Vanke A (000002). As China's most competitive real estate listed company, it will continue to share in the fruits of China's rapid economic development.

Industry Leader, Rich Project Reserves

The company is a domestic real estate benchmark enterprise with clear main business and development strategy. In the third quarter of 2009, it achieved operating revenue of 7.7 billion yuan and net profit of 430 million yuan, increasing by 46% and 101% year-on-year respectively, with earnings per share of 0.04 yuan. Moreover, the company has relatively rich land reserve resources. From the project reserve situation, from January to September, the company added a total of 30 projects, with a planned construction area of 8.31 million square meters and rights area of 6.33 million square meters, with an average land cost of about 2,686 yuan per square meter. By the end of the third quarter report, Vanke's planned rights project reserve area reached 218.7 million square meters. Due to the company's active attitude towards acquiring land this year, the newly added projects are mainly in second-tier cities. Judging from the land acquisition cost and local housing prices, the company still has the potential to achieve ideal profit margins in the future.

Focus on Urban Circle Strategy, Sales Surge

In the next ten years, Vanke will focus its business on urban economic circles, especially the Yangtze River Delta, Pearl River Delta, and Bohai Rim three major urban circles (which account for only 4.1% of the country's total land area but contribute 40% of GDP, with residents' savings balance accounting for one-quarter of the nation’s total, and per capita consumption expenditure being twice the national average). The company has entered the Ningbo market in the Yangtze River Delta region and the Qingdao market in the Bohai Rim region, making its layout more complete. According to the 2009 interim report, the Pearl River Delta market achieved sales areas of 1.101 million square meters and sales amounting to 10.44 billion yuan; the Yangtze River Delta market achieved sales areas of 929,000 square meters and sales amounting to 9.83 billion yuan; the Bohai Rim market achieved sales areas of 1.026 million square meters and sales amounting to 7.47 billion yuan. In addition, the company had advance receipts of 23.946 billion yuan at the end of 2008, plus sales income of 52.69 billion yuan from January to October 2009, ensuring significant growth in annual performance.

Strong Trend, Positive Outlook for the Future

On the secondary market, the company's stock price recently showed a strong rebound supported by the 60-day moving average line. Looking at the trend, the stock has been consolidating for a long time, accumulating certain upward momentum. Recently, there have been signs of the initiation of large-cap stocks. As the absolute leader in the real estate sector, the stock's market performance is worth paying attention to.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When trading stocks, you must specify whether you want to buy or sell, including the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you wish to trade, the quantity, and the price of the stock you intend to buy or sell. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, while the stock code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must match when placing an order. Additionally, there are certain rules regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks ordered to buy must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks ordered to sell does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place orders: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
