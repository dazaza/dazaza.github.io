The average P/E ratio of new shares has fallen for four consecutive months.

by anonymous on 2012-02-25 11:40:16

The much-anticipated new round of reforms in the Initial Public Offering (IPO) system is accelerating. At the same time, the pace of new share issuance has also started to slow down, with the issue price-earnings (P/E) ratio continuously declining over the past four months. Besides market depression factors, the reduction in the supply of new shares and the decline in valuation are closely related to the regulatory authorities' tough shift in regulatory direction. Several investment banking professionals have indicated that the industry-wide average P/E ratio published by China Securities Index Co., Ltd. (shortened as "CSI") and Shenzhen Securities Information Co., Ltd. (shortened as "SZSE Info") will become a hard indicator for future IPO pricing inquiries. "Although previously the valuation of already-listed companies in the same industry was used as a comparison benchmark, it did not form an industry rule. Now, it is confirmed that it will be implemented in writing," said a senior investment banking professional from a southern securities firm.

In February, the weighted average P/E ratio of new stocks fell below 20 times. Statistical data shows that entering into 2012, except for holiday factors in January, calculated based on the start date of online issuance of new shares, there will still be 17 companies issuing stocks for the first time in February. Compared to the previous months where over 30 companies were issued, the issuance has clearly slowed down.

With the issuance slowing down, the recent issue P/E ratios have also continuously dropped, even as the index rebounded somewhat in February, resulting in a fourth consecutive monthly decline. High-issue-P/E-ratio companies have also been decreasing, with the monthly average issue P/E ratio having fallen for four consecutive months.

Looking at the average issue P/E ratios of each month since the resumption of new stock issuance, starting from the short-term small peak of the average issue P/E ratio in November last year, the issue P/E ratio has been declining. In November 2011, the weighted average issue P/E ratio for new stocks still exceeded 35 times, which was a small high point since May 2011. Since then, it has been continuously declining, with the average issue P/E ratio dropping to 25.78 times in January this year, and falling below 20 times in February.

In February, although Hualue Baina's issue P/E ratio exceeded 80 times, the issue P/E ratios of other companies were all below 40 times, with over 60% of the companies being below 30 times. The issue P/E ratios of China Communications Construction and Dongfeng Shares were both below 20 times. And according to current data, China Communications Construction is also the company with the lowest issue P/E ratio since the resumption of new stock issuance in 2009, at only 10.59 times.

The new stock issue price has also been declining for four consecutive months. From the issuance situation of the past few months, the number of stocks priced below 20 yuan has gradually increased.
