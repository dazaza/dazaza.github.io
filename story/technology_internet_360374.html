High temperature: The public is fiery, and the merchants are also fiery

by topxfcjf on 2010-07-10 14:22:20

     Pacific Gateway NewsRecently, an orange high-temperature warning has been sounded in the skies above Hebei, Shanxi, Shaanxi, Henan and other regions. A wave of scorching heat has swept across this area, with temperatures in some areas reaching as high as 40 degrees Celsius. This prolonged and abnormal high-temperature weather has had a significant impact on various aspects of people's lives.

 

    On the morning of July 6th around 8:30, a Beijing bus air-conditioned vehicle caught fire spontaneously. The accident investigation revealed that the high temperature caused the engine compartment to overheat, leading to oil pipe joints expanding and leaking oil, which ignited a localized fire at high-temperature points. Despite the activation of dry powder fire extinguishers, they were unable to prevent the entire vehicle from catching fire. Although there was no loss of life, it is clear that one cannot underestimate the power of such high temperatures.

 

     On July 6th, the scorching sun baked the land of Shandong, where 46 meteorological monitoring stations broke historical records. Jinan issued its first red high-temperature warning this year. Interestingly, air-raid shelters have become a new destination for citizens. Recently, 17 air-raid shelter cooling spots in Shandong Province will be opened to the public. These cooling spots cover a total area of 35.14 million square meters and can accommodate 67,694 people, potentially alleviating citizens' fear of the high temperatures.

 

    On July 6th, the temperature in Shijiazhuang reached 40.5 degrees Celsius. The sweltering heat posed a significant challenge to the start of the summer transport season. At the train station, crowds of passengers waited to board their trains. To ensure the smooth operation of summer transport, the Shijiazhuang railway department implemented various measures to provide heatstroke prevention services to passengers—spraying water on the ground, setting up sunshades, ensuring round-the-clock air conditioning, providing hot water and mung bean soup, offering free heatstroke prevention medicines, and reminding passengers to reduce travel during high-temperature weather.

 

    The high temperatures directly led to a surge in electricity and water usage in various regions, also impacting different industries to varying degrees. If everything has two sides, then the high temperatures indeed brought economic benefits to some industries or groups, thus giving rise to what is known as "high-temperature economy."

 

    High temperatures have led to a surge in sales of air conditioners, fans, and refrigerators, significantly increasing sales figures. Various cosmetics brands' sunscreen skincare lines have entered their "hot-selling season." Some savvy merchants have launched multiple product bundles, combining sunscreen, skincare, and nourishing products into sets and offering discounts on the sunscreen series, attracting many customers. Cold noodles, cold rice noodles, and similar foods have started gaining popularity. Some merchants even turned hot porridge into fruit ice porridge, which proved very popular among customers. Beach resorts have also become the top choice for tourists seeking relief from the heat.

 

    High temperatures have directly given rise to the "lazy economy." As the temperature rises, more and more people are unwilling to leave their homes, either staying indoors or hiding in their offices, even refusing to leave the comfort of air-conditioned rooms for meals or shopping. This seemingly simple lifestyle inadvertently fueled the "lazy economy."

 

Online shopping, a convenient way to save money and effort, not only became the top choice for women who love to shop but also fear the sun, but also made men who dislike shopping and pursue a simple and fast-paced lifestyle feel delighted. Multiple B2C e-commerce websites, such as Taobao, Joyo, and Rakuten, reported increased sales of summer products like swimsuits, skirts, bamboo mats, and sunglasses. Taobao's daily traffic reached around forty million. In terms of sales volume, women's cosmetics and clothing still lead the way. Meanwhile, B2B websites like Alibaba, Hc360, and Pacific Gateway have seen new opportunities. In recent days, air conditioners, refrigerators, and fans have topped their internal search rankings. Alibaba's daily traffic is approaching the ten-million mark. Online shopping and online trade are thriving under the high-temperature environment.

 

    High temperatures have also made takeout orders from restaurants more popular. The phones for takeout orders ring non-stop, with fewer people dining inside. However, the staff have to endure the scorching sun while delivering food to every corner of the city. Taxis marked with air conditioning signs shuttle through the streets, busy and bustling...
