Beijing Law Firm's Cleanup of the Relationship between the Supplementary Investigation System and the Prosecution System

by lilan2333 on 2009-12-08 11:08:44

& nbsp, Beijing Law Firm; 1. From the perspective of prosecution standards, supplementary investigation in the review and prosecution process & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; As mentioned above, the standard of sufficient evidence is the standard for prosecution, which is also the standard that does not require supplementary investigation. The Supreme People's Procuratorate has formulated "Rules" based on the "Criminal Procedure Law" and the "Six Organs Regulations", which are important references for procuratorial organs to carry out criminal litigation activities. Article 279 of these "Rules" clearly stipulates the situation where the facts of a crime have been ascertained, namely the standard of sufficient evidence during the review of prosecution: First, for cases involving a single offense, the ascertained facts are sufficient for conviction and sentencing or relevant facts related to conviction and sentencing have been ascertained, and facts that cannot be ascertained do not affect conviction and sentencing (a decision not to prosecute should not be made for this reason, as it may lead to leniency towards crimes, which is a dilemma); Second, for cases involving multiple offenses, part of the offenses have been ascertained and meet the conditions for prosecution, while other offenses cannot be ascertained; Third, if the whereabouts of the tools used in the crime or stolen goods cannot be ascertained but there is other evidence sufficient for conviction and sentencing of the defendant; Fourth, the main plot of witness testimonies, confessions and defenses of the suspects, and statements of the victims are consistent, with only individual plots being inconsistent and not affecting conviction.③ & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; The standard of evidence at the time of prosecution is greatly influenced by criminal policy. Our country has always emphasized the principle of severe and swift punishment, which will inevitably lead to a certain degree of lowering of this standard, so that more cases can be submitted to court for trial, thereby realizing our criminal policy. From the prosecution standard set by the "Rules", this judgment is basically valid: the public prosecution authority does not need to wait until all issues in the case have been clarified before prosecuting, but rather, according to the principle of divisibility of suits, as long as the evidence affecting conviction and sentencing has been basically clarified, prosecution can be initiated. In cases involving multiple offenses, one can be prosecuted as soon as it is clarified. Therefore, under circumstances where some facts are unclear, supplementary investigation is not necessarily required, nor should the suspect be detained for this reason. & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; 2. From the standard of non-prosecution, looking at supplementary investigation in the review and prosecution process & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; Compared to the standard of prosecution, the connection between the supplementary investigation system and the non-prosecution system is more direct. Theoretically speaking, a direct consequence of repeated supplementary investigations is that the case cannot be smoothly transferred to the court for trial. Procedures are interlinked and constantly moving forward. If it remains in a closed-loop system, going back and forth without any external output, this indicates serious problems in the system, and the negative phenomena generated by the current supplementary investigation system in our country are external manifestations of this issue. & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; The design of procedures must consider how to handle situations where no solution is found, instead of allowing the procedure to endlessly circulate within the system without any opportunity for output. Because criminal proceedings always involve human rights protection. If a case is left hanging indefinitely without handling, it constitutes a serious violation of human rights, which is absolutely unacceptable in a rule-of-law society. Therefore, if there is no hope of obtaining a guilty verdict based on existing evidence, and the deadline for obtaining evidence has expired, a decision of non-prosecution due to insufficient evidence must be made. This is a newly added type of non-prosecution in the amendment process of our country's "Criminal Procedure Law" - non-prosecution due to insufficient evidence. This type of non-prosecution has strict substantive and procedural conditions, its substantive condition is essentially the situation of insufficient evidence stipulated in Article 286 of the "Rules"; ④ while the procedural condition refers to the completion of supplementary investigation and reaching the statutory number of times, if after discussion by the procuratorial committee it is deemed that the requirements for prosecution still cannot be met, a decision of non-prosecution due to insufficient evidence can be made. & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; Regarding the supplementary investigation system, the provisions on supplementary investigation in the revised legislation must be combined with this new form of non-prosecution due to insufficient evidence to understand the true intention of the legislation: it is precisely through restrictions on the time and number of supplementary investigations that the procedural conditions for non-prosecution due to insufficient evidence are indirectly set, which is the significance of supplementary investigation. Unfortunately, the painstaking efforts of the legislators cannot be implemented, causing the cases of insufficient evidence encountered by the procuratorial organs during the review and prosecution phase to become an unpleasant half-cooked dish, like a hot potato, difficult to get rid of. Therefore, to improve our country's supplementary investigation system, it is necessary to closely link it with the prosecution system from a legislative perspective, promptly terminating litigation procedures including supplementary investigation when the case does not meet the conditions for prosecution, preventing their repeated operation, and any judicial interpretation contrary to this spirit should be carefully considered. & nbsp; & nbsp; & nbsp; Of course, it should also be pointed out here that although the decision of non-prosecution due to insufficient evidence is an effective decision, it is not a final decision. If the investigation and prosecution authorities can later collect sufficient evidence, they can still prosecute. However, during the period from the decision of non-prosecution to the initiation of re-prosecution, it does not involve detention issues or the supplementary investigation issues currently stipulated by law. And the substantive and procedural conditions for re-prosecution should also have strict restrictions ⑤, which will not be discussed in detail in this article. & nbsp;
