Symptoms of gynecological inflammation include:

1. Cervicitis: Acute inflammation causes cervical congestion, redness, swelling, lower abdominal distension, sometimes fever. 2. Vaginitis: Trichomoniasis vaginitis shows increased secretions, foamy in nature, with burning and stinging sensations. Candidiasis vaginitis presents with external genital itching, local erosion and ulcers with burning sensation, urinary pain, and frequent urination. Increased secretions are also observed.

3. Bacterial vaginosis: Often associated with unpleasant odor and increased vaginal discharge. Common complications include cervicitis and pelvic inflammatory disease.

4. Adnexal inflammation: Acute adnexal inflammation commonly presents with fever and abdominal pain, with temperatures reaching over 38℃, chills, bilateral lower abdominal severe pain, sometimes one side being more painful than the other. Chronic adnexal inflammation has no obvious fever or pain, but rather a feeling of lumbar soreness or discomfort, or a sense of heaviness in the lower abdomen.

5. Pelvic inflammatory disease: Symptoms include lower abdominal heaviness, pain, and lumbar and sacral pain, increased menstrual flow, and menstrual disorders. Chronic pelvic inflammatory disease easily leads to inflammation in the fallopian tubes, ovaries, and uterus, potentially causing infertility and various complications.

6. Urinary tract infection: Symptoms such as frequent urination, urgent urination, painful urination, itching, and swelling may occur. If untreated, it can damage nearby normal tissue structures, leading to vaginitis and pelvic inflammatory disease.
