New Material Industry Gains Attention, Technology Transfer Rate Awaits Improvement

by keyizhang008 on 2012-03-02 15:51:45

The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) recently released the "12th Five-Year Development Plan for New Materials Industry". The plan proposes that during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, China's new materials industry is expected to reach a total output value of 2 trillion yuan, with an annual growth rate exceeding 25%. It will promote 30 key varieties of new materials and implement several demonstration and promotion application projects.

New materials refer to materials that are newly emerging or under development, possessing excellent properties and special functions that traditional materials do not have; or materials that use new technologies (processes, equipment), significantly improving or generating new functions in traditional materials. Generally, some key materials that meet the needs of high-tech industry development are also considered as new materials.

The materials industry mainly includes: textile industry; petroleum processing and coking industry; chemical raw materials and chemical products manufacturing; chemical fiber manufacturing; rubber products industry; plastic products industry; non-metallic mineral products industry; ferrous metal smelting and rolling processing industry; non-ferrous metal smelting and rolling processing industry; metal products industry; medical materials and medical products industry; electrical engineering materials and electronic components manufacturing, etc.

The new materials industry is widely recognized as one of the most potential high-tech industries in the 21st century with significant impact on future development. It is not only listed by various countries as a strategic emerging industry but is also regarded as the cornerstone for the development of all strategic emerging industries. It is the main force in enhancing corporate competitiveness and an important measure of a country's economic, social, scientific and technological progress, and national defense strength.

Currently, China's economy is entering a critical stage of transformation and upgrading, with the proportion of traditional industries gradually declining, while emerging industries are facing faster development. China has proposed developing strategic emerging industries, and new materials are widely used in emerging industries. As the foundation of manufacturing, materials play a crucial role in industrial upgrading and transformation, and new materials bear the important task of promoting the development of high-tech industries. A well-developed new materials industry can drive the upgrade of other industries; if it does not develop well, it will constrain the development of other industries.

The current situation of China's new materials industry

At present, the global market scale of new materials exceeds $400 billion annually, and the market generated by new products and new technologies brought about by new materials is even broader, with annual turnover breaking through $2 trillion. It is understood that although currently China's new materials output accounts for a small proportion of GDP, its development speed is relatively fast.

The plan points out that since the beginning of the new century, China's new materials industry has developed rapidly, reaching over 650 billion yuan in 2010, with an average annual growth of about 20% compared to 2005. Among them, rare earth functional materials, advanced energy storage materials, photovoltaic materials, organosilicon, superhard materials, special stainless steel, glass fiber and their composite materials, etc., have production capacities leading the world.

Some key technologies have achieved major breakthroughs. China's self-developed tantalum niobium beryllium alloys, amorphous alloys, high magnetic induction oriented silicon steel, diphenylmethane diisocyanate (MDI), superhard materials, meta-aramid, and superconducting materials production technology have reached or approached international levels. The variety of new materials is constantly increasing, with the security capabilities of high-end metal structural materials, new inorganic non-metallic materials, and high-performance composite materials significantly enhanced, and the self-sufficiency level of advanced polymer materials and special metal functional materials gradually improving.

However, China's overall development level of the new materials industry still lags behind developed countries, and the industry faces some pressing issues to be resolved, mainly manifested in: weak independent development capability of new materials, insufficient innovation motivation of large material enterprises, insufficient security capabilities of key new materials; disconnection between production, education, research, and application, short industrial chains, difficulties in promoting and applying new materials, imperfect industrial development models; lack of comprehensive planning and policy guidance for the new materials industry, insufficient and scattered R&D investment, and relatively weak basic management work.

Opportunities for the development of the new materials industry

The plan points out that the 12th Five-Year Plan is a critical period for comprehensively building a moderately prosperous society and a tough period for accelerating the transformation of economic development methods. The strategic adjustment of the economic structure provides important development opportunities for the new materials industry. On the one hand, accelerating the cultivation and development of energy-saving and environmental protection, next-generation information technology, high-end equipment manufacturing, new energy, and new energy vehicles and other strategic emerging industries, implementing major projects for the national economy and national defense construction, requires the new materials industry to provide support and security, providing broad market space for the development of the new materials industry. On the other hand, China's raw materials industry is huge in scale, with excess capacity in some industries, and constraints from resources, energy, environment, etc., are becoming increasingly stringent. There is an urgent need to vigorously develop the new materials industry, accelerate the transformation and upgrading of the materials industry, and cultivate new growth points.

The downstream industries of the new materials industry involve a wide range, including automobiles, aviation equipment, communication equipment, home appliances, IT industry, as well as real estate, transportation, urban construction, and many other fields. These areas in China are currently in a period of rapid development, with a huge demand for new materials.

The plan shows that during the 12th Five-Year Plan period, China's new materials industry will focus on developing special metal functional materials, high-end metal structural materials, advanced polymer materials, new inorganic non-metallic materials, high-performance composite materials, and frontier new materials.

Key projects during the 12th Five-Year Plan include special projects for rare earth and rare metal functional materials, carbon fiber low-cost and high-end innovation demonstration projects, high-strength lightweight alloy materials special projects, high-performance steel materials special projects, high-performance membrane materials special projects, advanced battery materials special projects, demonstration application special projects for new energy-saving and environmentally friendly building materials, electronic information functional materials special projects, biological medical materials special projects, and special projects for the construction of new materials innovation capabilities.

The need to enhance the innovation capabilities of the new materials industry

As one of the three key technologies in the 21st century, new materials are the foundation and pioneer of high-tech development, an important material basis for developing information, aerospace, energy, biology, and other high-tech technologies, and have become the driving force for the rapid growth of the global economy and the focus for countries to enhance their core competitiveness.

Although China is a major materials country, it has not yet become a strong materials country. Some high-quality, high-tech content, and high-value-added new products still mainly rely on imports. In the field of new materials, China lags significantly behind developed countries. The main manifestations are: insufficient original innovation capabilities, few products with independent intellectual property rights; lagging behind in the transformation of scientific and technological achievements and industrialization development, low technical content and added value of enterprise products, as well as due to weak scientific research and innovation capabilities in new materials and immature production and manufacturing technologies, resulting in insufficient quality stability and reliability of new materials products; a large amount of high-end new materials products in the domestic market still depend on imports; low industry concentration, small enterprise scale, and lacking international competitiveness.
