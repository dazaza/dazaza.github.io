Eat these 10 foods often and you won't get sick easily

by xiaoxiao67 on 2011-12-22 16:55:10

1. Berries - Eating berries regularly can prevent illness

According to research by the US Department of Agriculture, blueberries contain the highest level of antioxidants among fruits, followed by cranberries, blackberries, and strawberries. The color of berries comes from anthocyanin pigments, which are antioxidants that can neutralize free radicals. These free radicals can lead to chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease. Berries, especially cranberries, can also prevent urinary tract infections.

It is recommended to eat about one cup of berries daily. You can have them as a snack, put them on yogurt, or mix them with pancakes, salads, or smoothies.

2. Dairy Products - Eating dairy products regularly can prevent illness

Dairy products not only provide calcium but also contain large amounts of protein, vitamins (including vitamin D), and minerals. These elements are key in fighting osteoporosis. The US government's nutritional guidelines suggest consuming three servings of low-fat dairy products daily, along with regular weight-bearing exercise to strengthen bones. (If you cannot do this every day, other calcium-rich foods include vegetables like kale and cabbage, as well as tofu, calcium-fortified juices, and cereals.)

Besides helping to strengthen your bones, dairy products may also help you lose weight. Studies are ongoing, but there isn't enough evidence yet to prove that three servings of dairy products daily can help reduce belly fat and ultimately lead to weight loss.

Low-fat dairy products make for excellent snacks because they contain both carbohydrates and protein.

"Dairy products are not only the best snacks for people with diabetes but also for anyone else. They can help maintain your blood sugar levels," said Bonnie Taub-Dix MARD, a spokesperson for the American Nutrition Association.

Having smoothies with low-fat yogurt or milk is great. You can also add some orange juice or a few strawberries, which can replace your usual snacks and are much healthier.

3. Fatty Fish - Eating fish regularly can prevent illness

Fish contains large amounts of Omega-3 fatty acids. These fish include salmon and tuna. This fatty acid can fight disease, help lower blood fat levels, and also prevent blood clots related to heart disease.

The American Heart Association recommends eating fish at least twice a week, with each serving not less than two portions (especially fatty fish).

Lichtenstein said: "Eating fish can reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease."

Another benefit of eating salmon and tuna is that it reduces the saturated fat you get from high-fat foods.

You can cook salmon and tuna at home, whether grilled or boiled, making for a delicious meal.

4. Vegetables - Eating vegetables regularly can prevent illness

Among anti-disease foods, vegetables are also one of the best. These include spinach, kale, cabbage, and lettuce. They all contain large amounts of vitamins, minerals, beta-carotene, vitamin C, folic acid, iron, magnesium, carotenoids, phytochemicals, and antioxidants. A Harvard study found that eating foods high in magnesium, such as spinach, can reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes.

Next time you make a salad, you can add some of these vegetables, including nutrient-packed spinach and other green leafy vegetables. Such foods can help you fight disease.

5. Whole Grains - Eating whole grains regularly can prevent illness

In the morning, grandma always urges us to eat oatmeal, but does she know that the soluble fiber in oatmeal can lower blood cholesterol levels?

Whole grain foods include nutrients extracted from refined grains. These components contain folic acid, selenium, vitamin B, and other heart-healthy elements. They can also control your weight and reduce the risk of diabetes. The fiber content keeps you feeling full between meals and promotes digestion.

Eat at least three servings of whole grains daily: whole wheat, barley, rye, millet, quinoa, brown rice, wild rice, whole wheat pasta, bread, and cereals. According to the American Dietetic Association, the daily recommended fiber intake is 21-38 grams, depending on your gender and age.

6. Sweet Potatoes - Eating sweet potatoes regularly can prevent illness

If you want to improve your diet, you should add sweet potatoes to your meals. This sweet food contains large amounts of antioxidants, phytochemicals, including beta-carotene; vitamins C and E; folic acid; calcium; copper; iron; and potassium. The fiber in sweet potatoes promotes digestive health, and its antioxidants play a role in preventing heart disease and cancer.

The natural sweetness of sweet potatoes makes them delicious even without any seasoning when roasted.

7. Tomatoes - Eating tomatoes regularly can prevent illness

These red tomatoes in summer are filled with lycopene, an antioxidant that helps prevent certain cancers. Tomatoes also provide abundant vitamins A and C, potassium, and phytochemicals.

Eating tomatoes with every meal is great. You can eat them raw, cooked, or sliced as a snack. Combining spinach, cheese, and tomatoes can create a very delicious dish.

8. Beans - Eating beans regularly can prevent illness

These foods are highly nutritious, containing large amounts of phytochemicals, fat-free, high-quality protein, folic acid, fiber, iron, magnesium, and small amounts of calcium. Beans are good sources of protein and are also the best choice for low-calorie vegetarian diets.

Regularly eating some beans and planning your diet accordingly can help reduce the risk of certain cancers, lower cholesterol and triglyceride levels in the blood, and stabilize blood sugar. Generally, you won't consume too many calories from beans, so they play a significant role in helping you control your weight.

When making salads, soups, or stews, don't forget to add some bean products.

9. Nuts - Eating nuts regularly can prevent illness

We all know that nuts contain a lot of fat. However, nuts are a very healthy food. Whether monounsaturated or polyunsaturated, they can help lower cholesterol levels and aid in preventing heart disease. To obtain large amounts of protein, fiber, selenium, vitamin E, and vitamin A, nuts are a good choice.

A small portion of nuts can boost energy and overcome hunger, helping those who want to lose weight maintain a normal diet. Of course, nuts contain a lot of calories, and if you're not careful, you might eat too many.

So enjoy nuts, but be sure not to eat too much. You should eat no more than one ounce per day, which is approximately 28 peanuts, or 14 walnuts, or 7 Brazil nuts.

10. Eggs - Eating eggs regularly can prevent illness

Research results show that saturated fat (which is present in low amounts in eggs) plays a bigger role in raising cholesterol levels in our blood than cholesterol itself.

Eggs are rich in low-cost, high-quality protein and also contain carotenoids, lutein, choline, and xeanthin. In fact, eggs are an excellent source of choline, an essential nutrient - especially important for pregnant women. Studies show that some components provided by eggs promote eye health and help prevent age-related macular degeneration, reducing blindness in the elderly.

The American Heart Association has praised the value of eggs and recommends eating them daily. Of course, you must control your daily cholesterol intake to 300 milligrams. If you can do this, then you can enjoy an egg every day.
