Online Shopping Enters the Era of Full Network Sales: B2C Replaces C2C as the Market Dominator

[Lead] Currently, B2C sales account for 80% of the entire U.S. online shopping market share, while C2C only occupies 20%. Among the top ten online retailers, half are B2C websites operated by physical chain enterprises. Ultimately, the development of China's online shopping market will also be dominated by B2C.

During the lively New Year's goods purchasing season from offline to online during the Spring Festival, the rapid development of China's e-commerce has made "online New Year's goods shopping" another landscape of the Spring Festival. However, "urgent courier services" continue to be the biggest headache for online New Year's goods shopping. Although some websites have posted good news that they will still deliver on the first day of the lunar new year, this is still a minority. There is no reason to doubt the arrival of the full network sales era in China's e-commerce development. From the rich variety of New Year's goods to the colorful websites for buying New Year's goods, it was a bustling and slightly regrettable "online New Year's goods shopping" movement.

XXX is an online shopping expert. When he thought about buying an LCD TV, Wii game console, thermos, or Hetian jujubes, his first thought wasn't to go to the mall but to search online. Joyo Amazon, Dangdang, JD.com, Newegg, Yixun, and Taobao became the essential bookmarks in his internet explorer. These are XXX's Gome, Suning, Pacific, Walmart, and Carrefour.

However, there are still too few consumers like XXX who take online shopping as their first choice. Some users only think of online shopping when buying books, but when it comes to purchasing appliances and other items, they "relapse" and run to chain stores. According to iResearch Consulting statistics, this can be explained — in 2009, China's online shopping transaction volume approached 250 billion yuan, but only accounted for approximately 2% of the total retail sales of consumer goods. The scale of online shopping users will exceed 100 million.

In the rapid development of e-commerce, more websites, more products, and more access platforms have truly brought e-commerce into the era of full network sales. In this era, B2C occupying the mainstream becomes increasingly evident.

B2C Trend

XXX won a laptop in the annual meeting draw, but he found no website could auction it. The C2C (consumer-to-consumer) platform Taobao has largely turned into a B2C (business-to-consumer) platform, becoming a gathering place for most small vendors. Users use Taobao more for shopping than for personal transactions.

Personal transactions have retreated to localized forums and niche forums, such as PChome-like forums, or even Kaixin.com and Sina Weibo, or even MSN signatures.

In fact, this trend is also evident globally. Currently, B2C sales account for 80% of the entire U.S. online shopping market share, while C2C only occupies 20%. Among the top ten online retailers, half are B2C websites operated by physical chain enterprises. Ultimately, the development of China's online shopping market will also be dominated by B2C.

Era of Full Network Sales

E-commerce has attracted traditional enterprises and various internet companies to join, gradually forming a full-network sales trend. Meanwhile, diversified terminals enable more users to access online shopping, continuously increasing the user penetration rate of online shopping, making it an important part of consumers' lives.

E-commerce is constantly expanding, building a huge and extensive network on the Internet to attract more users.

Currently, the number of web weavers is continuously increasing. From portal sites (Sina Mall), search engines (Baidu Youa, and joint venture Rakuten Mall), communities (Tianya Community Shopping Street, Renren Aigou), video sites (Ji Dong Shopping Channel), and other high-traffic media are all intervening in e-commerce operations in different forms, forming a full-network marketing situation. Online shopping is constantly infiltrating various services on the Internet.

Moreover, traditional enterprises such as Suning, Gome, COFCO, Belle, and Li-Ning have started venturing online, building comprehensive sales channels combining online and offline. Taobao has also begun to establish full-network sales channels including the Internet, television, and physical stores.

The entry points for online shopping are also continuously increasing. The integration of sales channels allows users to access online shopping through multiple entry points. Besides traditional internet, consumers can place orders through mobile phones, televisions, e-books, etc., to enter shopping websites. Currently, Taobao, Dangdang, and Joyo have all launched mobile versions of their websites. Huashu Group, TCL, Konka, and others are all vigorously promoting IPTV to realize internet shopping via television.

With the increase in internet penetration, online shopping is gradually reaching various groups. Currently, most domestic e-commerce companies target white-collar workers aged 20-30. New entrants in the e-commerce market often start by catering to specific groups' needs. Lemall specifically adjusts its products for the baby and mother population; YiMaJiu serves the middle class, selling imported wines; Boqi focuses on pet-related e-commerce. iResearch analysis believes that the segmented markets targeting specific groups and specific needs are not yet saturated.

Geographically, Wang Hanhua, president of Joyo Amazon, believes that although currently, China's online shopping transactions are mainly concentrated in first-tier cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen, they are also quickly expanding to second and third-tier cities and regional markets in central and western China.

From Specialization to Departmentalization

China's e-commerce development stage has transitioned from initially choosing specialization to the current departmentalization path. JD.com started with digital products and expanded to four major sectors: home appliances, mobile digital, computer products, and daily necessities. Both Joyo and Dangdang started with books. In the process of ten years, Joyo achieved the goal of having book business below 50% in 2009, while Dangdang is still striving towards this goal.

As consumers become more accepting of online shopping and develop habitual online consumption, the departmentalization of websites can provide one-stop shopping convenience for netizens and leverage the existing consumer base to increase sales. Clothing, home furnishings, infant products, and other departmentalized goods have higher profit margins and purchase frequencies. Expanding product categories can balance the profits of main products.

This has formed a situation where the range of goods in China's online shopping market continues to increase, involving various aspects of residents' lives, including clothing, food, housing, transportation, and entertainment. Specific product categories include books, clothing, cosmetics, mobile phones, food, jewelry, etc. The transaction goods in China's e-commerce market are evolving from highly standardized to non-standardized personalized ones, gradually capturing more consumers' preferences.

"The future e-commerce infrastructure should make it as easy for users to obtain as tap water and electricity," said Yang Zhizhong, General Manager of UFIDA Wecan Company.

Provincial, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government, planned single-listed city commerce authorities, and Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps Commerce Authorities:

To implement the "Opinions of the General Office of the State Council on Stimulating Circulation and Expanding Consumption" (Guo Ban Fa [2008] No. 134) and the "Several Opinions of the General Office of the State Council on Accelerating the Development of E-commerce" (Guo Ban Fa [2005] No. 2), the following opinions are now proposed to further accelerate the development of e-commerce in the circulation sector under the current economic situation:

I. Fully Recognize the Importance of Accelerating the Development of E-commerce in the Circulation Sector

E-commerce is a modern circulation method based on information technology and the Internet. Due to the late start and weak foundation of e-commerce development in the circulation sector in our country, the participation of circulation enterprises and the public in e-commerce still needs to be improved, and the supporting service system for online transactions is not yet sound. Currently, under the strategic deployment of the Party Central Committee and the State Council to expand domestic demand and stimulate economic growth, accelerating the development of e-commerce in the circulation sector can not only expand the online consumer group, cultivate new consumption models and consumption fields, but also help reduce circulation costs and accelerate the final realization of the value of goods and services. Therefore, accelerating the development of e-commerce in the circulation sector is an effective means for our country to deal with the financial crisis, expand residents' consumption, and maintain the momentum of economic growth. It is also a necessary requirement to improve the efficiency of commodity circulation and transform the mode of economic development.

II. Clearly Define the Main Goals of Accelerating the Development of E-commerce in the Circulation Sector

The main goal of accelerating the development of e-commerce in the circulation sector is to take the market as the orientation, enterprises as the main body, and informatization driving the modernization of circulation as the main means to accelerate the promotion and application process of e-commerce in the circulation sector. Support traditional circulation enterprises in applying e-commerce to open up the online market, cultivate a number of professional online shopping enterprises with standardized management and broad market prospects, and support a number of online wholesale transaction enterprises with strong influence and cohesion. Improve the awareness and participation of the public in e-commerce, explore resident consumption areas suitable for online transactions, cultivate and expand the online consumer group, and strive to increase the proportion of online shopping transaction volume in our country's total retail sales of consumer goods to more than 5% by the end of the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan".

III. Promote Traditional Circulation Enterprises to Open Up the Online Market

Encourage large circulation enterprises that are key to cultivation to integrate resources, build integrated e-commerce platforms, improve economies of scale and comprehensive competitiveness. Continuously promote the deepening development of e-commerce applications of large circulation enterprises, forming a virtuous cycle of technical improvement, system upgrade, value creation, and capital attraction. Support small and medium-sized circulation enterprises to carry out technical transformation and management upgrades related to online sales through third-party technology service platforms, reduce operational costs such as product display, personnel, inventory, and capital occupation, choose flexible and appropriate business models, and meet the needs of customers at different consumption levels and preferences.

IV. Promote the Application and Promotion of Online Transactions in the Commodity Wholesale Link

Encourage circulation enterprises to apply e-commerce for commodity wholesale transactions, improve circulation efficiency, expand intermediate demand, and drive final demand. Give full play to the role of large backbone circulation enterprises and bulk commodity markets, and carry out online wholesale transactions of agricultural products, daily industrial products, and production materials. Relying on industrial clusters, support the development of industry-oriented e-commerce enterprises, integrate industrial chains and supply chains, and provide third-party services for online transactions between production enterprises and distribution enterprises, and between wholesale enterprises and retail enterprises.

V. Accelerate the Development of Professional Online Shopping Enterprises Facing Consumers

Cultivate a number of well-known, strong, and standardized professional online shopping enterprises, build new commercial websites with rich trading commodities and diverse service contents, vigorously develop the sales of commodities suitable for online transactions such as clothing, home appliances, home decoration, books, and audio-visual products, and deeply excavate the consumption potential of various netizen groups. Accelerate the development of third-party e-commerce platforms facing consumers, encourage small and medium-sized enterprises and individuals to conduct online transactions through e-commerce platforms, promote the connection and interactive exchange of supply and demand between both parties in the transaction, promote enterprises to operate collaboratively and intensively, form enterprise clusters with both competitiveness and flexibility. Encourage the construction of community e-commerce platforms, promote the integration of various commercial service resources within the community, and provide convenient and fast services for community residents.

VI. Promote the Organic Combination of Physical Market Transactions and Online Market Transactions

Encourage circulation enterprises to use online sales to drive store sales and use store sales to support online sales, continuously exploring the interactive promotional operating methods of "online market" and "offline market". Utilize the brand advantages, physical outlet resources, and logistics distribution systems of circulation enterprises to provide rich commodities, good reputation, and quality services for online sales. Use the low-cost and all-day commodity display and transaction channels on the Internet to effectively promote the expansion of sales of physical stores. Encourage various circulation enterprises to develop mobile e-commerce, carry out relevant facility transformation and management upgrades.

VII. Improve the Supporting Policies for the Development of E-commerce in the Circulation Sector

Actively utilize central service industry development funds and small and medium-sized trade enterprise development funds to promote the improvement of weak links in the development of e-commerce in the circulation sector, improve the policy promotion system for e-commerce in the circulation sector, promote the introduction of supporting policies and measures by commerce authorities at all levels, further improve the content of support, and increase the intensity of support.

VIII. Carry Out Demonstration and Guidance Work for the Development of E-commerce in the Circulation Sector

Promote the advanced experience of e-commerce development in the circulation sector in advanced regions, select circulation enterprises with high standardization levels and strong representativeness in the application of e-commerce, study and promote mature operation modes and excellent solutions, lead circulation enterprises, especially small and medium-sized enterprises, to improve management levels and standardize business behaviors around e-commerce, and promote the continuous healthy development of the entire industry.

IX. Improve the Development Environment of E-commerce in the Circulation Sector

Adhere to the principle of protecting market vitality and regulating transaction behavior equally, seriously implement domestic trade industry standards such as the "Norms for E-commerce Model" (SB/T10518-2009) and the "Norms for Network Transaction Services" (SB/T10519-2009), and carry out related training and exchange activities. Actively guide e-commerce enterprises in the circulation sector to establish and improve credit management systems, improve service integrity, and enhance consumer confidence.

X. Effectively Prevent Risks in the Online Transaction Market

Targeting the new characteristics and problems of the combination of modern information technology and traditional circulation methods, effectively maintain the order of the online transaction market, prevent and resolve various transaction risks brought by the virtuality of the Internet. Strengthen departmental supervision cooperation and information sharing, improve and extend the real-world market supervision system, avoid the lack of law enforcement in online market supervision. Actively cooperate with relevant departments, legally and promptly abolish and expose illegal acts in online transactions, and prevent the replication and expansion of illegal acts from enterprise registration, website filing, information release, fund flow, and commodity delivery in all links.

XI. Establish a Promotion Work System for E-commerce in the Circulation Sector

Local commerce authorities at all levels should establish an organizational guarantee system and working mechanism to accelerate the development of e-commerce in the circulation sector, clarify responsibilities and divisions, and implement tasks and goals. Play the role of e-commerce industry organizations, provide information services and policy guidance, and solve actual business difficulties for enterprises. Integrate circulation enterprise resources, combine promotional activities during festivals to carry out online exhibitions and recommendations, organize various forms of popularization activities such as e-commerce entering enterprises and households, and improve residents' participation enthusiasm and practical operation ability.

Ministry of Commerce

November 30, 2009
