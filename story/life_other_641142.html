The Real Life Situation of a Post-80s Couple with a Second-Tier Bachelor's Degree in Shanghai

by gege on 2011-12-22 18:20:14

I am a girl born in 1987, graduated in 2010, and have been in Shanghai for one year and three months. My boyfriend, born in 1985, is my senior, and has been in Shanghai for two years. He currently serves as the head of the major clients department at a travel website. I resigned at the end of August and am currently unemployed and looking for work.

In Shanghai, being the head of a department team should come with a decent salary. However, his company, which has around 70 employees and should be considered a medium-sized company, is just a shell. His team originally had seven people, but recently three have left, leaving only three, including himself, making a total of four. He received his salary on the 10th day of the month, after deducting housing provident fund and social security, he ended up with 3052 RMB. Tragic! Even more tragic is that he was supposed to receive a 600 RMB bonus on the 15th, but upon checking his account, there was only 181 RMB. The next day, when he went to the personnel department, he learned that due to being late twice, he was fined 50 RMB each time, a customer complaint cost him another 200 RMB, and an operational error resulted in a loss of 119 RMB to the company, leading to a deduction of 119 RMB. Therefore, his bonus was 600 - 50 - 50 - 200 - 119. Before I resigned, I worked in an administrative and human resources role at a design company. Since only comprehensive insurance was paid, I received 3500 RMB plus a 156 RMB meal allowance. Salaries were also issued on the 10th, but since the 10th fell on a Saturday, it was postponed until the 13th. On the afternoon of the 13th, I checked my bank card and found only 3200 RMB, which seemed strange (by this point, I had already left). I called the company and learned that the meal allowance hadn't been issued yet. As for the additional 300 RMB, the company believed that since I initiated my resignation, they didn't need to pay it. This was our income last month, totaling just over 6000 RMB.

Next, let's talk about our expenditures:

1. Rent: 1400 RMB, shared rental with a common kitchen and bathroom, the room has a TV, bed, summer chair, stool, cabinet, no air conditioning. Water, electricity, and gas costs about 120 RMB per month for both of us. Total monthly expenditure is 1520 RMB.

2. Food: We basically cook ourselves, especially now that I'm at home and have time to cook. Average daily vegetable purchase is around 25 RMB. My boyfriend eats breakfast outside for about 4 RMB, while I usually have a pack of soy milk and biscuits. We eat noodles for lunch, and he takes a homemade bento to work. Total daily food expense is around 32 RMB. Occasionally, we eat out once a week, costing about 80 RMB for both of us. Monthly total is 1280 RMB.

3. Transportation: As everyone knows, transportation in Shanghai is expensive. He takes the bus and subway every day, a one-hour journey, costing 10 RMB round trip for 22 days. On weekends, we sometimes go out, approximately recharging 250 RMB per month (not including occasional taxis, minimum 14 RMB per ride). Recently, I've been job hunting, and the subway is my main mode of transportation. After half a month, I recharged 150 RMB, with 31.9 RMB remaining on the card. Combined monthly transportation expenses for both of us are approximately 500 RMB.

Adding up these three expenses totals 3300 RMB. Please note, this does not include our regular living supplies, occasional snacks, or increasingly expensive fruit costs.
