Should I add my boss and parents on WeChat or not?

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 13:47:02

Recently, WeChat has become extremely popular. Not only can you share photos at any time, but you can also see private photos or recommendations shared by your friends. Additionally, games like "Plane Striker" and "Clearance" are also very popular among users. However, if your immediate superior or your parents suddenly send a WeChat verification request, would you pass it immediately or pretend not to see it? If you use the "Shake" function on WeChat or search for nearby people and get information about someone offering "special services", how would you react? WeChat brings various conveniences, but users also experience various dilemmas.

Shouldn't I add my boss on WeChat?

Boss assigns tasks through WeChat

"Xiaochen, write an article for me now; I will need it tomorrow. Xiaochen, I've reviewed the previous proposal, and I sent the parts that need modification via WeChat. You should modify them and send them to me tomorrow..." Xiaochen, a post-80s white-collar worker employed in a private enterprise in Haizhu District, usually works from 8:30 am to 6:30 pm. However, since one day when he received a friend verification message on WeChat from his boss, his regular life was disrupted. "When I saw the verification message was from my boss, I really felt a shadow over my eyes," Xiaochen complained. "If I don't add (my boss as a WeChat friend), he will definitely have thoughts about me. If I do add him, I feel like my privacy will be probed by my boss, so I won't dare to casually post complaints about work in my Moments."

Thinking that being more restrained in Moments would allow him to coexist peacefully with his boss on WeChat, Xiaochen started receiving "overtime" messages from his boss after work and at night. "Within a few days of finishing a plan, my boss would send WeChat messages asking me to make changes here and there at midnight, and he would also say that I had to submit the modifications to him the next day." Helplessly, Xiaochen had to get up several times to "work overtime".

Xiaochen complained that his boss often posts so-called "inspirational quotes" and "motivational posts" in Moments, advising young people to endure hardships for future success. "Whenever I see my boss posting such things, I feel particularly annoyed, so sometimes I 'ignore' or directly overlook what he posts." Xiaochen also mentioned that he would reduce the frequency of using WeChat in the future, just to avoid dealing with work-related matters after work.

Afraid to easily express feelings

Miss Xu works at the Guangzhou branch of a Hong Kong company. In her words, not adding her boss on WeChat is simply not an option. "Our boss is trendy. As soon as I started working, the boss asked me to add his WeChat and join a group chat with all employees."

"Actually, this is quite annoying. Now I don't dare to post personal feelings in Moments. The boss is in the group, who dares to act carefree? The most troublesome thing is that if the boss is dissatisfied with our work, he will mention it in the group chat, and we have to revise it. Even after work, I still have to check if there are any new messages on WeChat; it doesn't feel like a private space anymore."

"You think about not adding the boss, but everyone else in the company has added him. What does it mean if you don't?" Miss Xu told reporters, "Now technology is advanced, but it also causes some burdens."

HR Suggestion: Apply for two WeChat accounts, separate public and private

Mr. Cai, the human resources supervisor of a company in Zengcheng, suggested that employees encountering bosses who require them to add their WeChat can apply for two WeChat accounts, one for public use and one for private use. "Use the public QQ number or WeChat account to handle work-related requirements." He also mentioned that this situation varies from person to person. "Some people think that extra overtime is beneficial for future development and for the impression the boss has of them, so they don't need to distinguish too much. Others believe that work and rest should be separated, and they don't want to work overtime unnecessarily, which is also understandable based on personality preferences."

Parents' WeChat, add or not?

Adding saves phone bills

As the Mid-Autumn Festival approaches, Xiaowang, who works at a company in Zhujiang New Town, plans to give his parents two smartphones as gifts for the festival. However, he is a bit conflicted about whether to add his parents on WeChat. After investigating, reporters found that many citizens share this dilemma.

Xiaozhang, who works at a company in Guangzhou, went back to Hubei province in August while on a business trip. He gave his old smartphone to his father and taught him how to use WeChat to contact him. "My parents think this is great; you just press and hold to send voice messages. It saves a lot on phone bills," Xiaozhang said to reporters. "This thing isn't actually difficult; it's easy to learn. My dad is almost 60 years old, and now he uses WeChat even more enthusiastically than me."

Not adding, fearing one's expressions might affect parents

Similarly, Xiaowang, who also plans to give his parents smartphones, thinks it's better not to add his parents on WeChat. "I know you can set blacklists and such, but I personally feel strange doing that. And another issue is that sometimes parents send messages, but if I'm busy with work and don't reply in time, my family will worry," Xiaowang said. "Using WeChat to communicate with parents isn't entirely appropriate yet. I occasionally post something in my Moments, some articles and pictures that I think are good. Sometimes, out of happiness, I post my own feelings, and sometimes out of sadness. These things aren't necessarily embarrassing, but I think it's better that my parents don't know. After all, as children, we only want our parents to know that we're doing fine. If my emotions affect my parents' emotions, that wouldn't be good," Xiaowang said.

Xiaozhang said he thought carefully before adding his parents on WeChat. "I thought it through initially. Elderly people generally don't bother with these apps much; everything was set up by me. I removed their access to Moments and 'Shake' functions and set permissions so that my parents couldn't see my Moments."

There are many other dilemmas...

Searching nearby people leads to sexual services

Yesterday, near Shiqia Bridge Metro Station in Tianhe District, using the "Search Nearby People" feature on WeChat, the reporter found four similar pieces of information providing sexual services within half an hour in a 1000-meter radius. These users almost exclusively conveyed bold and obvious sexual information through their profile pictures and nicknames. When opening the user albums, some obscene images were unbearable to look at. Following the telephone number in the album, a female voice answered, indicating that she could provide "door-to-door service" as long as the hotel address and price requirements were provided. When the reporter tried to inquire further, the line was quickly disconnected.

Recently, media reports indicated that some users, due to frequent use of the "Greet Nearby People" feature on WeChat, had their functionality suspended by Tencent. Some netizens questioned whether there was a limit to the number of greetings allowed.

Tencent Customer Service: Continuous improvement of features

Yesterday, according to Tencent's official online customer service, there is no specific daily limit on the number of "greetings" a WeChat user can perform. However, if a user frequently operates within a short period, the system detects potential security risks and temporarily restricts the user's account. Furthermore, the reporter learned that some users suggested that frequent operation of this feature may not necessarily involve security risks but could be a personal habit. If this functionality is suspended, wouldn't many innocent people suffer as a result? Customer service personnel stated that they would optimize this feature as soon as possible to continue improving it. They also suggested that users avoid frequent operations to prevent inconvenience. Subsequently, according to Tencent's official online customer service, for the dissemination of pornographic information on WeChat, they aim to continuously improve and optimize related product features to offer users a better experience. "The product indeed has imperfections, and we hope users help maintain a green network environment together."

The pros and cons of social networks cannot be cut off abruptly

In this internet era, our thoughts and feelings are directly externalized into bits and pieces shared on WeChat Moments or other social software. This is why many people do not want their parents to join the same network. Once joined, it is easy to blur the difference in family status between us and our elders, which is exactly what we do not want to see.

Similarly, in a traditional social organization, the organizational structure is often clearly hierarchical. The thoughts and actions of grassroots employees often go through multiple layers of reporting before reaching the decision-making level. The advantage of such an organizational structure is that decision-makers can stand at a macro level and find the most urgent issues from the filtered information to solve. The introduction of WeChat groups causes the flattening of social organizations. On one hand, problems faced by grassroots employees can be solved quickly and effectively, but on the other hand, it may cause decision-makers to fail to solve problems from a macro perspective and even become a burden on employees.

Our discussion on how to use social networks is not aimed at finding a definitive answer, but rather hopes to help us use it better or provide simple inspiration for users facing various dilemmas.
