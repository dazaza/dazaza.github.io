800 Simple Stir-fries, so you no longer worry about cooking! 1. Spicy and Sour Potato Shreds: Preparation Process 1. Peel the potatoes, cut into thin shreds, and soak in water to wash away the starch, which not only avoids discoloration but also keeps them tender and crisp when stir-fried. Drain before cooking. 2. Remove seeds and stems from green and red peppers, then cut into thin shreds; slice dried chili peppers. 3. Heat a wok over high heat, add peanut oil until hot, add chopped green onions to burst fragrance, stir-fry dried chili peppers until fragrant, then add green and red pepper shreds and potato shreds, stir-fry until eight-tenths cooked, add salt and monosodium glutamate for seasoning, drizzle with sesame oil and aromatic vinegar, stir-fry until fully cooked.

2. Braised Eggplant: 1. Wash eggplants, remove tops and tails, cut into chunks, soak in water, drain, sprinkle with a little cornstarch and mix evenly. Wash scallions, remove tops and tails, cut into sections. 2. Wash pork, cut into slices, mince, then add 1/2 tablespoon of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, 1/2 tablespoon of oil, and 1/3 tablespoon of chicken powder, mix well, marinate for 10 minutes. 3. Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a pan, stir-fry minced meat until dispersed and white, set aside. 4. Add another 10 tablespoons of oil, stir-fry scallion sections until fragrant, add eggplant and stir-fry on high heat quickly, add 5 tablespoons of water and stir-fry until eggplant softens, about 5-6 minutes. 5. Add previously stir-fried minced meat, stir-fry for 1 minute, sprinkle with 1/3 tablespoon of salt, 1/5 tablespoon of chicken powder, and 1/2 tablespoon of soy sauce for seasoning, ready to serve!

3. Fish-flavored Eggplant: Ingredients: 2 eggplants, a little meat shredded, ginger, garlic, scallions, spicy bean paste, vinegar, sugar. Method: 1. Wash and drain the eggplants, cut into small round pieces and set aside. Clean garlic and chop finely, clean scallions and cut into scallion flowers, chop ginger finely and set aside. Put vinegar, sugar, and cornstarch into a bowl, add water and set aside. Note: Put sugar first, then vinegar. The level of vinegar should be even with the sugar. 2. Heat the oil, put in eggplant pieces and fry until golden yellow, then drain oil. 3. Stir-fry ginger, garlic, and scallions until fragrant, add meat shreds, then add fried eggplant and stir-fry briefly, add seasonings from the bowl and stir-fry, then plate.

4. Green Pepper Omelette: Method: 1. Wash green peppers with clear water, remove seeds, and cut into thin strips. Beat eggs into a bowl and stir with chopsticks. 2. Heat oil (40 grams) in a pot, pour in egg mixture, stir-fry until done, then set aside. 3. Pour remaining oil into the pot, heat, add chopped green onions to burst fragrance, then add green pepper strips, stir-fry a few times, add salt when green peppers turn emerald green, add previously stir-fried eggs and monosodium glutamate, stir evenly, drizzle with aromatic vinegar, and serve.

5. Three Delicacies from the Earth: Ingredients: 1 potato, 2 eggplants, 1 green pepper. Seasonings: Cooking oil, soy sauce, sugar, salt, chopped green onions, minced garlic, cornstarch, and broth as needed. Method: 1. Peel eggplants and potatoes, cut into irregular chunks; tear green peppers into small pieces by hand. 2. Heat more oil in a pot, at seven-tenths hot, first put in potato chunks, deep-fry until golden yellow and slightly translucent, then set aside. 3. Then put eggplants into the oil pot, deep-fry until golden yellow, add green pepper chunks and scoop out together. 4. Use a little hot oil to burst the fragrance of chopped green onions and minced garlic, add broth, soy sauce, sugar, salt, eggplants, potatoes, and green pepper chunks, stir-fry briefly. 5. Add water and cornstarch, stir-fry over high heat until the sauce thickens.

6. Dry-Fried Green Beans: Ingredients: 400 grams of flat beans, 150 grams of minced meat. Seasonings: 4 dried chilies, 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce, appropriate amounts of preserved vegetable crumbs, Sichuan peppercorns, minced garlic, salt, and monosodium glutamate. Method: 1. Tear off the old strings at both ends of the flat beans, break into two sections, wash and drain. 2. Heat 250 grams of oil, put in flat beans, deep-fry until the skin wrinkles, then scoop out and drain oil completely. 3. Heat another pot, use 1 tablespoon of oil to burst the fragrance of minced meat, dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorns, preserved vegetables, and minced garlic. 4. Add dark soy sauce, flat beans, stir-fry until thoroughly cooked, turn off the heat, add salt and monosodium glutamate, stir evenly.

7. Chicken Stir-Fried Cauliflower: Ingredients: Chicken breast, cauliflower. Seasonings: Scallion threads, ginger crumbs, star anise, edible oil, cooking wine, salt, monosodium glutamate, oyster sauce, cornstarch. Method: 1. Wash chicken breast, cut into slices, marinate with salt, cooking wine, and edible oil for a while, then mix evenly with cornstarch. 2. Wash cauliflower, split into small florets, blanch in boiling water, cool under running water, drain, set aside. Cut scallions into threads, ginger into crumbs, garlic into slices. 3. Heat a different wok, add oil, once warm add star anise, then ginger crumbs, add chicken breast and stir-fry until color changes, set aside, then stir-fry scallion threads and ginger crumbs until fragrant, add blanched cauliflower and stir-fry. 4. Add cooking wine, monosodium glutamate, oyster sauce, add previously stir-fried chicken breast, stir-fry quickly, add salt, stir-fry, serve on a plate.

8. Steamed Meat with Rice Flour: Ingredients: A piece of fatty pork belly. Seasonings: One star anise, some green onions, several slices of ginger, a little cooking wine, some red chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, rice, black rice, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, chicken essence, sugar, broad bean paste. Method: 1. Slice fatty pork belly. 2. Marinate the meat with cooking wine, green onions, ginger, and star anise. 3. After marinating the meat, prepare red chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, rice, and black rice. 4. Place prepared ingredients in a pot and stir-fry. 5. Stir-fry ingredients in the pot over medium heat until the rice becomes fragrant and slightly yellow. 6. Grind the stir-fried ingredients in a blender. Do not grind too fine, keep some larger grains. 7. Pour into the marinated meat, add dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, sugar, chicken essence, a little sesame oil, mix evenly. 8. Finally, add a big spoonful of broad bean paste. Add a little water to moisten the powder.

9. Scrambled Eggs with Green Onions: Preparation Method (For two people) Ingredients: Eggs (4), green onions (3). Seasonings: Salt (1/3 tablespoon), oil (6 tablespoons). 1. Wash green onions, remove white parts, cut green parts into green onion flowers, place in a large glass bowl. 2. Crack four eggs into the bowl, tilt the glass bowl at 45 degrees, stir in one direction with chopsticks, beat until the egg mixture becomes sticky. 3. Heat the pot until all moisture evaporates, heat for about 1 minute, add 6 tablespoons of oil to the pot, swirl to cover the entire bottom, then reduce to low heat and pour in the egg mixture. 4. Quickly stir the egg mixture in one direction, after about 1 minute, the egg mixture will solidify, immediately remove from heat to prevent overcooking.

Cooking Tips: 1. When stir-frying eggs, the pot must be very hot, this way the eggs will be tenderer. If the pot isn't hot enough, it's easy to fail. 2. Eggs taken out of the fridge should be left out for an hour before cooking. 3. Avoid adding monosodium glutamate when stir-frying eggs, as heating monosodium glutamate can mask the natural flavor of the eggs. 4. Eating too many eggs can increase metabolic waste products and burden the kidneys, eating 1-2 eggs per day is sufficient.

10. Tomato and Egg Stir-Fry: Method One Explanation: Everyone knows how to make tomato and egg stir-fry, but making it taste good is difficult. There are a few common mistakes, which I'll detail shortly. Ingredients: Tomatoes, eggs. Condiments: Vegetable oil, salt. Note: There's no need to add chicken essence or monosodium glutamate to tomato and egg stir-fry because tomatoes naturally release umami flavors during cooking. This is also why no green onions, ginger, or garlic are used in this dish. Preparation Method: 1. Cut tomatoes into unevenly sized chunks, crack eggs into a bowl, whisk, and add a little salt. 2. Heat an appropriate amount of oil in the pot (about 2/3 the volume of the egg liquid is ideal). Once the oil is hot (details on judging oil temperature follow), pour in the egg liquid. At this point, the egg liquid will naturally coagulate; do not disturb it. Wait until the entire egg liquid has coagulated (be careful not to let the oil dry out and burn the eggs), gently insert the spatula along the edge of the eggs to flip them over and fry briefly. Once both sides have turned golden brown, remove the eggs from the pot (they can also remain in the pot if preferred). There should still be some oil left in the pot, add the tomatoes and stir-fry briefly. Since tomatoes contain a lot of water, water will be released. At this point, add the previously stir-fried eggs, a little salt, stir briefly, and serve. PS: Some people like to add water, but I do not recommend it since the water content in tomatoes is usually sufficient. Additionally, due to the poor quality of tomatoes nowadays, the dish may taste bitter. You can add a little sugar just before serving.

11. Stir-Fried Silk Gourd and Eggs: Ingredients: Silk gourd, eggs. Seasonings: Oil, salt, cooking wine, chopped green onions. 1. Beat 2-3 eggs, add a small amount of salt and cooking wine, mix evenly and set aside. 2. Peel the silk gourd and cut into slices or cubes and set aside. 3. Heat a wok with oil, once the oil is hot, pour in the eggs and stir-fry until cooked, then set aside in a bowl. 4. Heat the wok again with oil, once the oil is hot, add the silk gourd and stir-fry until cooked, then add the pre-cooked eggs and stir-fry together. Adjust to taste with salt and chopped green onions, stir briefly and serve.

Health Benefits: Silk gourd is highly nutritious, containing protein, fat, carbohydrates, crude fiber, calcium, phosphorus, iron, citrulline, riboflavin and other B vitamins, vitamin C, and saponins found in ginseng.

Practical Cooking Tips:

1. When boiling dumplings, add a large scallion or a little salt after the water boils, then add the dumplings. The dumplings will be fresh and non-sticky; when kneading dough, add one beaten egg per 500 grams of flour to make the dumpling wrappers firm and non-sticky.

2. When stewing meat, add a few orange peels to the pot to remove odors and greasiness and enhance the freshness of the soup.

3. Add a small spoonful of vinegar when boiling bone soup to dissolve phosphorus and calcium from the bones into the soup and preserve the vitamins in the soup.

4. Stewing chicken: Wash and cut into chunks, stir-fry in hot oil until the moisture evaporates, then add an appropriate amount of vinegar and stir-fry quickly. Once the chicken chunks start to sizzle, immediately add hot water (covering the chicken chunks), boil on high heat for ten minutes, then add seasonings and transfer to low heat for another 20 minutes. Drizzle with sesame oil and serve. Salt should be added after the soup is done or when the temperature drops to 80～90°C or just before eating. Adding salt early causes the water inside the chicken to seep out due to osmosis, proteins coagulate, causing the chicken to shrink and become tough and chewy.

5. When boiling meat or bone soup, add a few fresh orange peels to enhance the flavor and reduce greasiness.

6. When cooking tofu, add a little fermented tofu or its juice for aroma.

7. Stir-fry mung beans in an iron pot for 10 minutes before boiling to speed up the process without burning.

8. Add a little vinegar to the water when boiling eggs to prevent the shells from cracking; adding a little salt beforehand also helps.

9. Add a few drops of vinegar when boiling kelp to soften it quicker; adding a few spinach leaves works too.

10. Before boiling ham, coat the skin with some sugar for easier cooking and a fresher taste.
