My Schedule, I Control - Graphic and Text Review of Three Synchronization Time Management Applications [iPad]

by xdash on 2011-09-23 11:32:28

Just after the Mid-Autumn Festival and before National Day, a flood of work arrangements and holiday plans need to be prioritized in order to improve efficiency. As the saying goes, "If you want to do a good job, you must first sharpen your tools." Here we introduce three time management applications for the iPad (of course, we only talk about those with synchronization functions), hoping that everyone can hold their own schedule and have a relaxed and pleasant long vacation. (Note: All tools mentioned in this article can be found on the App Store.)

1. Awesome Note

Awesome Note is an innovative note-taking app and to-do list manager that allows you to flexibly combine notes with to-dos. You can customize the appearance of Awesome Note through themes to display different folder icons, colors, fonts, and paper backgrounds as you wish.

Clear and concise options, along with colorful interfaces, make note-taking no longer a troublesome task.

Awesome Note provides a richer notebook application and to-do list management function, allowing users to freely and flexibly combine notes and to-dos.

The sticky note-style quick notes feature is particularly considerate, allowing users to quickly jot down their flashes of inspiration. Additionally, for easy organization later, sticky note content can also be saved as notes.

Awesome Note also offers rich synchronization, transfer, backup, and restoration functions.

In the Evernote synchronization test, all kinds of information were fully synchronized, with no omissions or text garbling issues in notes, tags, or annotations. Notably, in the folder settings, you can separately set whether a particular folder needs to be synchronized, effectively avoiding the repeated transmission of trivial information. Furthermore, Web sharing makes backups free and easy, without being restricted by iTunes.

Since it's note-taking, there are bound to be things you don't want others to know. The password lock function of Awesome Note not only locks the program but also provides some privacy protection for information synchronization, allowing users to use their mobile devices more securely.

If you're someone who likes traveling, then Awesome Note is perfect for you. You can attach photos of your journey via email to share with family and friends at any time. Synchronization with Google Docs/Evernote also ensures that your ideas and plans are not scattered everywhere.

2. Calvetica Calendar

Calvetica is a calendar application focused on scheduling. In terms of content addition, Calvetica does a detailed and comprehensive job, allowing users to easily set the start time and minute precision of an event.

Additionally, the latest version of Calvetica Calendar supports gesture operations. For example, you can swipe to hide month information, freeing up more space to focus on a particular day's information. On the iPad, multi-touch control is even more convenient when switching between events and tasks. To set a timer-like alarm, simply click the "+" at the top of the screen and set 5 minutes, one day, or any other time period for reminders. In the iPhone version, custom alert tones can also be set, allowing users to quickly regain attention in a noisy office environment.

For task management, users can set different color markers to manage tasks in groups, and set titles and add annotations to indicate the priority of events. The timeline display makes the continuity of task planning more intuitive.

In terms of synchronization, Calvetica supports sharing with Pocket Link, and the operation is relatively simple. Although it cannot yet synchronize with Evernote, because Calvetica is mainly used for recording schedule management information, the inability to synchronize with note-taking applications has little impact on practical use.

Notably, Calvetica uses Helvetica font and adopts the classic red, white, and gray color scheme commonly used with Helvetica, making the overall interface elegant and classic. This kind of application is absolutely essential for business people when traveling or working from home!

Of course, during use, there is one unavoidable trouble: if you are a jailbroken user, using Calvetica may cause headaches because setting an event might lead to constant crashes. So, if possible, please support the official version.

3. Timeli (Timeline Management)

Timeli, both in terms of functionality and appearance, is not as advanced as the previous two applications, but its straightforward project arrangement and practical timetable can tell you at a glance which order is primary and which is secondary among multiple projects. Timeline avoids rigid daily schedules and favors continuous, smooth scrolling timelines. Simply drag and drop to flexibly select the start and end times of tasks.

In actual use, users can set multiple related tasks on a single timeline, and the completion rate of tasks can be adjusted and recorded in real-time, just like a download bar, clear and intuitive.
