Although international gold prices have been rising since the holiday, they experienced a three-hour plunge of 100 US dollars per ounce last week. Against this backdrop, the market's attitude towards gold is no longer solely bullish. Yesterday, I discovered that several banks have recently launched gold-linked financial products. Some of these products bet on a [bullish] gold price, but quite a few also bet on a [bearish] outlook. Additionally, traditional [paper gold] businesses have started adding short-selling functions. Industry insiders analyze that the rollercoaster-like movements in gold prices, as seen on Gold Insight [http://www.octopus63.com], and the clustering appearance of bearish gold financial products, make this year's gold price trend full of uncertainty.

Reported by this newspaper’s Qiu Yufeng from Changsha.

Banks Launch Bearish Gold Financial Products

Since the beginning of the year, international gold prices have rebounded from around $1550 per ounce to about $1780 per ounce, with an increase exceeding 10%. Affected by this, the issuance of gold-related financial products has surged. According to statistics from PuYi Wealth, as of February 28, 2012, banks had issued 15 gold-related financial products, whereas throughout last year, banks only issued a total of 29 such products. The issuance volume in the first two months of this year has already surpassed half of last year's total. However, regarding the future trend of gold prices, there is a clear divergence among major banks, with many products showing signs of 'pessimism' in their design.

[In less than a month, six gold-linked financial products have been launched.] A wealth management manager at the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (ICBC) branch explained that, for example, one RMB-denominated gold-linked product offered by the bank is linked to the price of gold per ounce in US dollars. If it remains below the boundary level (2100 USD/oz) during the observation period, the highest annualized return could be 4.8%; the worst scenario would be if the linked price ever exceeds or equals the boundary level (2100 USD/oz) during the observation period, resulting in only a minimum annualized return of 3% upon maturity.

Paper Gold Adds [Short-Selling] Function

Last Wednesday, the international gold price suddenly plummeted by 100 USD/oz within just three hours, a drop exceeding 4%, completely erasing gains made over recent periods, marking the largest decline in three years. Many who bought gold before the holiday were instantly 'trapped,' while those involved in gold T+d transactions or gold futures trading faced direct margin calls, suffering heavy losses. However, it was noted that these bank-issued bearish gold price financial products are mostly principal-guaranteed floating types, starting at 50,000 yuan, with investment terms mostly ranging from 1 to 3 months, and expected annualized returns generally between 3%-6%. In other words, even if investors misjudge the direction of gold prices, they won't lose their principal.

Not only are gold-linked financial products no longer solely profitable when gold prices rise, some banks have also introduced [short-selling] services for paper gold. For instance, ICBC recently launched personal account gold (paper gold) two-way trading business, fully opening up two-way trading for account gold, silver, and platinum. When customers predict that precious metal prices will fall, they can achieve investment returns through a sell-first-buy-later transaction method. Moreover, Bank of China has also launched a two-way paper gold trading service named [Two-Way Treasure].

Financial Planner: Prefer Products with Smaller Amplitudes

Should one choose bullish or bearish gold financial products? Zhou Liu, a financial planner at CITIC Bank Changsha Branch, believes that investors need to have some judgment on the trend of gold prices themselves. If one lacks personal judgment, then one should check the set price range of the gold-linked financial products. It is best to choose products with smaller amplitudes to avoid receiving the lowest returns due to fluctuations in gold prices.

The popularity of bearish gold-linked financial products, does it mean that gold prices are about to hit a turning point? FX168 Senior Financial Analyst Wei Jun stated that from the perspective of gold trends in 2012, despite a certain degree of adjustment recently, gold overall still maintains a relatively strong state. From a broad perspective, bearish-type products diverge from the direction of gold trends, exposing investors to market volatility risks. For bearish gold-linked products, clients familiar with gold trends can achieve higher returns if they make reasonable forecasts. If one cannot accurately grasp the direction of gold prices, it is highly likely to receive the lowest returns. Industry insiders expect that with the strengthening of the dollar since the beginning of the year and the gradual easing of the European debt crisis, the main factors supporting the upward movement of gold prices have weakened. It is unlikely for gold to experience another bull market within the year, and a high-position oscillation trend is more probable.
