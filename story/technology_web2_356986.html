Speaking of Google G2, I believe many of you have been paying attention recently. After all, the price of this Android phone is very attractive at the moment, having once dropped below 2000 yuan. However, the market can be ruthless, and the white version even rose to over 2200 yuan. The LG BL20 has a low cost-performance ratio, but fortunately, the price of the white version of Google G2 has now dropped again. Today, according to the quote from Ruibai Digital, the price for G2 is 1800 yuan, regardless of whether it's black or white. This is a great opportunity for those who like the white G2. Those who have been considering trying out the entire Android system might want to consider this beautiful G2.

The dimensions of the Google G2 are 113×55×13.65mm with a weight of 118.5g, making it slimmer than the G1 in terms of specifications. At first glance, the G2 still has some resemblance to the G1, with similar button layouts and navigation keys. However, the menu key on the G2 is more personalized and uniquely placed on the left side of the navigation key. The phone supports HSDPA/WCDMA 900/2100 MHz GSM/GPRS/EDGE 850/900/1800/1900 MHz networks. It also features an internal GPS chip that supports positioning and navigation. Additionally, Bluetooth 2.0, WIFI, and USB functions are all included. In terms of hardware, the Google G2 uses a Qualcomm MSM7201a central processor with a clock speed of 528 MHz, along with 512MB ROM + 192MB RAM, ensuring fast operation speeds.

The Google G2 is currently receiving high attention due to its reasonable price compared to Nokia phones, while not falling short in performance when compared to other smart devices in the same price range. The new white version priced at 1800 yuan is quite good, especially with the release of Android 2.0, allowing fans to fully experience the joy brought by the Android system.
