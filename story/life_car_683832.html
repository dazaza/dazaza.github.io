The phenomenon of ride-sharing is very common in major cities. On one hand, it saves taxi costs and time; on the other hand, it increases the income of taxi drivers. Originally, it was something everyone tacitly agreed upon, but after the additional fuel surcharge was added, how this fee should be collected and who should pay it became the focus of discussion. A netizen from car.wodingche.com named Alovelyday said, "If the taxi driver only carries me, I would pay the 1 yuan fuel surcharge. If it's a shared ride, I wouldn't pay the 1 yuan fuel surcharge. I haven't seen buses charge me extra for fuel fees either, and buses carry dozens of people sharing the ride." When the reporter asked a taxi driver about this special situation, he said, "Generally, the 1 yuan surcharge is waived in cases of ride-sharing. After all, picking up passengers along the way already increases income, and 1 yuan isn't much. Everyone makes it convenient for themselves, and I make it convenient for everyone." Not only that, but for long-distance passenger transport, this 1 yuan might also not be charged. "I believe most taxi drivers are like this," the driver said.
