### Classification

Mainly look at patterns and fabric.

"Choosing bedding requires first understanding some basic concepts," Miss Gao introduced. The classification of bedding mainly focuses on patterns and fabrics.

Generally speaking, from the perspective of pattern processing technology, bedding sets can be roughly divided into three major categories: printed, embroidered, and jacquard. Removing dampness, eliminating dampness and strengthening the spleen are the most popular topics among women and middle-aged and elderly people in winter.

These three types of patterns each have different characteristics. Among them, the patterns on printed bedding are printed onto the fabric after it has been woven. Printed products have bright and lively colors, a wide variety of pattern types, excellent dust-proof performance, high cleanliness, and good colorfastness, but the cost is higher than pigment printing; embroidered bedding patterns are machine-embroidered, and embroidered products have the characteristics of good breathability and moisture absorption; jacquard bedding has patterns woven with different colored yarns on the fabric. Compared to embroidered fabrics, jacquard fabrics have higher production costs, more complex processes, and produce bedding that is softer, smoother, has better luster, a better hand feel, and superior quality and breathability, as well as being more luxurious.

From the perspective of fabric structure, bedding can be divided into plain weave, twill, and satin weave. Plain weave products feature many interlacing points, firm and stiff texture, smooth surface, identical appearance on both sides, and cannot have too high a density, making them relatively light and thin with good wear resistance and breathability; Twill has a distinction between the front and back, fewer interlacing points within a complete organizational cycle, longer floats, soft hand feel, higher fabric density, thicker product, and strong three-dimensional effect; Satin weave fabrics are soft in texture, smooth and delicate on the surface, glossy, and require higher costs compared to similar plain weave and twill products.

### Parameters

Indicators for measuring high-quality bedding

"To understand the quality of a set of products, it is necessary to understand some technical parameters of bedding, which are important indicators for measuring the quality of bedding." Miss Gao stated that the technical parameters of general bedding are manifested in the following three aspects:

Firstly, the count, where the length of 1 gram of yarn in meters is called a certain count. For example, if a cotton plant can make 40 meters of yarn, then it is 40-count. The higher the count of the yarn, the finer the yarn, and the thinner and softer the fabric made from such yarn will be.

Secondly, the density, which refers to the number of warp and weft yarns arranged in a unit length. Generally, the higher the density, the higher the requirements for manufacturing technology and cost.

Lastly, the shrinkage rate, barium value (an indicator for measuring fabric gloss), colorfastness, and whiteness, all of which are technical indicators used by the state to measure bedding. The shrinkage rate of fabric refers to the percentage of contraction of the fabric after washing or soaking. Generally, synthetic fibers and their blended textiles have the highest shrinkage rates, followed by wool products and linen products, with cotton products being moderate.

"A good set of bedding must reach or exceed the national standards for these technical indicators," Miss Gao said, adding that Fuan娜 bedding leads the industry in these technical parameters.

Additionally, high-quality products have a flat and uniform fabric surface, fine texture, clear prints, luster, and even sewing. If the fabric surface of home textile products is uneven, the texture is loose, the patterns are chaotic, and the sewing is rough, then the quality may not be guaranteed, and the water wash size change rate and dyeing fastness may exceed the standard.

### Selection

Satisfying environmental friendliness and comfort

"Understanding some basic concepts and technical parameters of bedding makes it easier to choose bedding accordingly," Miss Gao said, emphasizing that choosing high-quality bedding suitable for oneself should meet the requirements of comfort and environmental friendliness.

Many customers primarily focus on color and patterns when purchasing bedding, followed by price, without paying attention to the yarn count and density of the bedding or its environmental friendliness. In response, Miss Gao gave her own suggestions, pointing out three things to pay special attention to when selecting bedding:

Firstly, pay attention to the materials used in the product, especially those that come into contact with the skin, such as sheets, duvet covers, and pillowcases. It is best to choose pure cotton or silk fabrics. Natural cotton fibers have excellent skin-friendliness, strong moisture absorption and breathability, safety, comfort, and no irritating odors;

Secondly, clarify the yarn count and density of the bedding, and compare the craftsmanship and comfort of the bedding;

Finally, ensure the product is environmentally friendly. The yarn count, density, and craftsmanship of bedding determine its durability, while environmentally friendly production determines its safety for use. Bedding cannot be separated from color and dyes. Some companies do not pay enough attention to environmental protection or cannot strictly control during the production process, so although the products may appear novel and colorful, they can cause significant harm to human health. People spend a lot of time in close contact with bedding, so the environmental friendliness of the product must be taken seriously.
