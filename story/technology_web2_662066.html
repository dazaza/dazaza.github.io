The traffic police talked with Grandpa Zhang. The Traffic Police Corps of Shapingba District provided the photo. When one gets old, one becomes like a child again. Grandpa Zhang, who was over 70 years old and still had a childlike heart, went out to find his childhood playmate without telling his family. After all, it was a playmate from more than 60 years ago. After wandering around for half a day, he didn't find his playmate but got lost himself.

An elderly man muttering on the street

At 5 p.m. on July 7th, by the side of the street in Jingkou, Shapingba District, an old man with a walking stick was muttering on the side of the street, "Hua'er, where are you hiding? Won't you come out... If you keep hiding, it won't be fun anymore."

"This old man is so strange! Does he have mental problems?" Passersby shook their heads.

The old man came to the traffic police platform in Jingkou, sat down, and continued muttering. Policeman Lü Qinyun started a conversation with him, "You were just muttering, what exactly were you saying?" The old man leaned on his walking stick and sighed deeply, "I'm looking for someone!"

It turns out that the old man's name is Zhang Mingyong, born in 1936, already 76 years old, originally from Renhe Village, Santiao Town, Hechuan.

Where to find someone from 60 years ago

"Looking for someone? Sure, tell me the name and age, I'll help you look." Lü Qinyun opened the population information network. But how easy was it to find?

Old Mr. Zhang said that this person was a playmate he met over 60 years ago near Shuangbei, "I don't know the name or address, I only remember 'Ah Hua'." With only a nickname, there was no way to search. The old man was very disappointed, his wrinkled eyelids drooping.

Why look for a childhood friend? With fragmented memories, the old man began to slowly recall. Before liberation, because he had many brothers and sisters and his parents couldn't support them all, when he was about 14 or 15 years old, he came to work as a child laborer near Shuangbei to support his family. At that time, he met Ah Hua, who also worked as a child laborer. They were about the same age and quickly became good friends. Ah Hua's home was near old Shuangbei, and after finishing work, Zhang Mingyong often went to play at Ah Hua's house.

In those tough times, they often couldn't get enough to eat, so the two boys would go down to the river to catch fish, crabs, and eels, and dig wild vegetables on nearby mountains. After liberation, Zhang Mingyong returned to his hometown in Hechuan to farm after land was distributed. Thus, he and Ah Hua parted ways and lost contact thereafter.

Family went crazy looking for him

More than 60 years passed, Old Zhang now has a big family with children and grandchildren. Before, his son worked in Shenzhen, and after his wife passed away, he lived alone in his hometown. As Chongqing developed rapidly, he called his son back.

Last year, his son worked at a company in Jingkou with a decent income, bringing his father to live together in Jingkou. Not worrying about food or clothing, after living comfortably for a while, the old man started missing Ah Hua. One day, after his son and daughter-in-law went to work, he was bored at home and wanted to inquire about Ah Hua's whereabouts. If he was still around, they could catch up. He became more and more excited and couldn't contain his excitement. After lunch, he went to Shuangbei alone to look for people.

Little did he know that decades had passed, and the Shuangbei of his youth had undergone tremendous changes. The docks he remembered were gone, replaced by bustling traffic and skyscrapers. As he walked and asked around, he found no clues and eventually lost his way home.

Through the "Mechanical Co., Ltd." mentioned by the old man, the traffic police contacted his son. "Heavens! The whole family went crazy looking for him, almost posting missing person notices, and he was looking for someone!" The son felt relieved upon receiving the call.

The son said that he had never heard his father mention Ah Hua before, nor did he know that his father wanted to find Ah Hua, "Maybe I thought too simply; what he needed was companionship."

Chongqing Morning Post reporter Feng Yu

Having memories is a kind of happiness

What are memories? They are catching crabs, digging wild vegetables, rolling iron hoops, folding "up-down-left-right"... In everyone's memory, there is an unforgettable beautiful memory.

For 60 years, through half a century, the friendship between the old man and his playmate has withstood the test of time. This precious emotion surpasses superior material life. How many people can hold onto such a memory for 60 years?

Fortunately, the old man's son now understands that what his father needs more is companionship, not superior material life. He needs family and memories to help alleviate loneliness.

Having memories is a kind of happiness. "Visit home often," spend more time with your parents.
