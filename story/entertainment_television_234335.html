Outline of the story:

The Birth of the Black Warrior

McTye always had a sharp business mind since childhood and understood the truth that doing business leads to prosperity. Thus, he studied hard and completed his MBA at Stanford University in the U.S. through sheer effort, searching for opportunities there.

After graduation, McTye saw potential in Ho Wun Tin's company and proactively approached him, bluntly stating that he didn't want to work as an employee but only as a partner, willing even if holding just 0.01% of the shares. Ho Wun Tin, seeing McTye's uniqueness, allowed him to join as a partner. Their relationship became both teacher and friend, working seamlessly together, specializing in acquiring companies and selling them off, becoming renowned snipers in the American business world.

Later, McTye became known as the ruthless Black Warrior in the business world, merciless in his actions, gradually gaining prominence. Similarly, the differences of opinion between McTye and Ho Wun Tin increased. For the company's interest and development, McTye decided to fully acquire Ho Wun Tin's company. Unable to bear the blow, Ho Wun Tin drove under the influence of alcohol. McTye learned that Ho Wun Tin's car lost control and fell into the sea. After the incident, Ho Wun Tin's body could not be found, but McTye firmly believed that Ho Wun Tin was dead, feeling immense guilt. Was the value of a business deal really greater than friendship, or life? McTye reflected on everything in the past and determined not to be the Black Warrior anymore, deciding to retire from the business world. From the newspaper, McTye learned that Ho Wun Tin's widow and children were living in difficult circumstances in Hong Kong. McTye felt responsible and decided to return to Hong Kong to take care of Ho Wun Tin's family.
