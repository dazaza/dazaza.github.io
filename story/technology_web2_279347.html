Specialized criminal defense, the tasks and basic principles of the procedures for public security organs handling criminal cases

by lilan2333 on 2009-11-26 17:24:55

Article 1: In order to ensure the implementation of the "Criminal Procedure Law of the People's Republic of China," guarantee professional criminal defense, ensure that public security organs correctly fulfill their powers in criminal proceedings, unify case handling procedures, ensure case quality, and improve case handling efficiency, these regulations are formulated.

Article 2: The task of the public security organs in criminal proceedings is to ensure the accurate and timely investigation of criminal facts, correctly apply laws, punish criminals, protect innocent people from criminal prosecution, educate citizens to voluntarily abide by laws, actively combat criminal behavior, maintain socialist rule of law, safeguard citizens' personal rights, property rights, democratic rights, and other rights, and ensure the smooth progress of socialist construction.

Article 3: The basic powers of the public security organs in criminal proceedings include initiating cases, investigating, and pre-trial reviews according to the law; deciding and executing compulsory measures; not filing cases for those who are not legally held criminally responsible, and revoking cases already pursued; transferring cases that should be prosecuted to the People's Procuratorate for review and decision-making; legally handling suspects who need administrative punishment but do not warrant criminal penalties; enforcing sentences for prisoners sentenced to less than one year in prison or with remaining terms under one year; executing punishments such as control, detention, deprivation of political rights, deportation, and temporary execution outside prison; supervising and examining parolees and those sentenced to suspended detentions or suspended imprisonments.

Article 4: Public security organs must rely on the masses when conducting criminal litigation, base decisions on facts, and adhere to the law. All citizens shall be treated equally before the law, and no privileges shall be allowed.

Article 5: Public security organs must work with the People's Courts and the People's Procuratorates, dividing responsibilities, cooperating mutually, and restraining each other to ensure the accurate and effective enforcement of the law.

Article 6: Public security organs must accept legal supervision from the People's Procuratorate during criminal proceedings.

Article 7: Public security organs should establish and improve internal supervision systems such as case responsibility systems and error accountability systems when conducting criminal litigation.

If higher-level public security organs find errors in decisions made or cases handled by lower-level organs, they have the authority to revoke or alter them or instruct the lower-level organs to correct them.

Lower-level public security organs must execute decisions made by higher-level organs. If they believe there is an error, they can report it to the higher-level organ while still executing the decision.

Article 8: Public security organs must value evidence and investigative research when handling criminal cases, not easily trust confessions, and strictly prohibit torture for confessions.

Article 9: Public security organs must guarantee the various litigation rights enjoyed by participants in litigation according to the law.

When handling juvenile crime cases, special institutions or personnel should be responsible, implementing the policy of education, reform, and salvation, considering the physical and mental characteristics of juveniles, and ensuring their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 10: At all levels, public security organs must legally request arrest warrants and transfer cases for prosecution to the same-level People's Procuratorate, strictly adhering to the time limits for criminal case processing.

Article 11: Public security organs must provide translation services for participants in litigation who do not understand the locally used language or script.

In areas predominantly inhabited by ethnic minorities or mixed ethnic groups, interrogations should be conducted in the locally used language. Publicly released litigation documents should be written in the locally used script.

Article 12: Public security organs should strengthen mutual cooperation and coordination between regions when handling criminal cases, strictly fulfilling their duties for investigations and cooperation.

Article 13: According to international treaties signed or participated in by the People’s Republic of China and bilateral cooperation agreements signed by the Ministry of Public Security, or based on reciprocity principles, Chinese public security organs and foreign police agencies may mutually request criminal judicial assistance and police cooperation.
