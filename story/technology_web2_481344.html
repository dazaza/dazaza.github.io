Wedding Apparel: Choose The Perfect Gothic Wedding Dress

So, you've chosen the Gothic theme for your wedding, and now it's time to pick from a wide variety of wedding outfits.

But it’s quite a bold, daring choice. Let’s explore the characteristics of this theme and the style of dress that would be best for you.

Gothic is a very brave choice; although it’s quite unusual, it will likely be remembered forever by all of your friends. But how do you embrace it properly?

What is Gothic?

The Gothic culture is greatly influenced by music and the macabre. I would say the term Gothic was first coined in the 18th and then 19th Centuries to describe a type of fiction that delved into occult, death, and other creepy subjects. Shelley’s 'Frankenstein'? Yes, that's a Gothic title.

This later spawned a subculture of individuals who have embraced the gloomy and dark lifestyle, which includes clothing, makeup, music, and art.

Gothic Wedding

A Gothic wedding is likely to be much darker than a traditional white wedding. This does not mean it has to be gloomy; however, you may want to incorporate some fun elements that showcase your 'darker sensibilities.'

Consider dark shades like blacks, grays, and scarlets.

Your cake could be decorated with crimson, denim, and silver. You might even choose a crucifix or skull motif instead of the standard tiered cake.

But the most eye-catching aspect of your special day is sure to be your Gothic Wedding Dress.

Most brides may find that a completely black gown is rather too intense, but you can easily purchase a white dress and have it customized with denim applique and brown accents in the skirt. On the other hand, a dark red dress could be really cool too.

Lace is a crucial element and should definitely be included as part of the dress design. White lace on your bodice, veil, and perhaps most of the skirt will truly help you look like a real femme fatale.

Black rhinestones add a mystical sparkle to a dress that already has some chocolate-colored evening dresses from South Africa. Consider them for your bodice.

You might opt for a dark gray veil, which certainly adds a twist to the regular veil. If you can't find a dark gray veil, you can either dye a white one or use fabric like organza.

A truly fabulous addition could be dark-colored lace elbow-length gloves.

Don’t forget that more classical styles of dresses are possibly going to fit better with the Gothic style compared to modern cheap clothing. So, you might want to consider an A-line knee-length wedding dress, Ball gown, or Empire style wedding dress.

All of these have that classical, old-world style that could match well with your theme. Short outfits and more modern styles like mermaid cut dresses should be avoided when looking for cheap wedding dresses.

Your bridesmaids should also fit with your theme, so ensure to choose appropriately dark hues for their attire. Practical ideas include crimson, ebony, or charcoal tones for your group.

Finally, you might carry a very interesting bouquet. Nowadays, it's possible to get dark roses, so these could be a great fit. Otherwise, go for a bouquet of dead flowers to create a creepy mood.

This is a truly fun theme for your big day, so go all out and make it as Gothic as possible! No one else will have done it before, so ensure it's memorable.

Where to order wedding outfits? It's ideal to try shopping online. You'll find that online stores offer better options than large chain outlets, and many are far cheaper!
