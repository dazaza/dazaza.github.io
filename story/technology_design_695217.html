Only after the scandal is exposed can it be terminated.

by 45871139 on 2012-03-07 14:55:51

Once, Northeast organic coarse grains, "three public expenses" experienced an era of "state secrets", citizens had no way of knowing the accounts, let alone the details of the accounts. Under the relentless social public opinion, the government gradually disclosed the "three public consumption", and the Ministry of Science and Technology took the lead, which is undoubtedly commendable. However, some departments only half-heartedly "complied". Under this kind of "half-covered" compliance, we can't help but suspect whether the relevant department's three public expenditures are made public just for the sake of publicity. If "three public consumption" is only made public to ease public opinion pressure, then such publicity doesn't have much meaning. The "three publics" represented by "three public consumption" completely violate the "three publics" represented by "openness, fairness, and justice". In this sense, this "three publics" still has a long way to go to reach the other "three publics".

The two sessions are about to be convened. In the online survey of twenty hot issues of the two sessions on People's Network, the attention and click rate of "three public disclosure" ranked in the top three. Netizens still criticize last year's "three public consumption". It is worth noting that the Central Committee of the Jiusan Society plans to write a proposal, suggesting that public-funded eating, private use of official cars, and public-funded tourism, etc., "three public wastage" should be included in the scope of regulation under the Criminal Law. The drafting of this proposal elevates the "three public consumption" from the level of public opinion supervision to the level of legal regulation, which is undoubtedly commendable; however, if everyone notices the "three public expenses included in assessment" proposed later by the Jiusan Society, then two concepts appear here: one is "three public wastage", the other is "three public expenses". Then, how to distinguish between "three public wastage" and "three public expenses"?

From the founding of New China to now, hundreds of relevant bans targeting public-funded eating have been issued. However, hundreds of red-headed documents cannot match the mouth of an official, who frequently issues documents with one hand and uses public funds for eating with the other. Red-headed documents are nothing more than empty words, with almost zero binding force. The reason is not due to the complexity of officialdom like scheming against each other, but because the makers, executors, and supervisors of the red-headed documents are themselves the main force behind "three public consumption". Similarly, the characters who have the authority to engage in "three public wastage" and use "three public expenses" are all officials of a certain rank. Who dares to thoroughly investigate? Unless China's judiciary is already independent, otherwise, how can an un-independent judiciary act freely? Furthermore, even if China's judiciary becomes independent, what is the standard for conviction? What is the reasonable consumption limit for "three public expenses"? What is the warning line for "three public wastage"?

Even if reasonable standards for "three public expenses" and "three public wastage" are set, the main force behind "three public" consumption itself is the power organ. Under the influence of unwritten rules, making fake accounts is as common as daily meals. How can fake accounts be prevented? These issues don't hold up on paper, let alone being curbed in execution? Last year, Singapore's Lianhe Zaobao published an article saying that it is quite difficult to uncover the veil of "three public expenses" in China. There are at least two reasons: one is that some officials have deeply rooted ideas of official-centered thinking and systematism; the second is that "three public spending" indeed has many unsavory "tricks" as expected by outsiders. The second reason also makes "three public wastage" fall into the category of "three public expenses". Moreover, criminalizing "three public wastage" is merely post-facto punishment, which can only be terminated after the scandal breaks out. So, what about officials who are currently engaging in "three public wastage"? The effect of such punitive measures remains limited.

For reforms in the "three public" field, they essentially belong to the self-reform within the privileged class. Facing various pressures and obstructions during this internal reform is inevitable. However, criminalizing "three public wastage" is still commendable. If there is no reform from the institutional and systemic aspects, it will still fail to be effectively implemented, ultimately ending in failure. The integrity of the London mayor riding a bicycle to work and Canadian officials applying for rental cars when on business trips does not stem from face-saving projects of imperialism or capitalism but originates from good political systems. Returning rights to citizens and realizing true self-governance through the right to vote, electing genuine representatives of their own interests as supervisors and officials, anyone daring to engage in "three public wastage" will be punished by ballots and forced off the stage. Therefore, whether it's the London mayor or Canadian officials, under such a political system, the government has no choice but to be frugal, and no one dares to engage in "three public wastage".
