NBA Rockets player Shane Battier was a guest on the September 5, 2009 episode of "Happy Camp," where he revealed some interesting behind-the-scenes stories about Yao Ming.

On the evening of September 1st, NBA Houston Rockets player and Peak-signed athlete Shane Battier appeared as a guest on Hunan TV's "Happy Camp." During the recording, Battier showcased his gentlemanly, humorous, warm, and meticulous personality, allowing the audience in Star City to gain a more comprehensive understanding of this NBA star's life outside the brutal basketball court.

**Dressed in a Suit, Singing Jazz**

When the stage lights dimmed and the spotlight focused on the lift, Battier emerged dressed impeccably in a suit, waving to the audience with charm and charisma. Unexpectedly, instead of greeting the audience directly, Battier opened his "golden voice" and surprised everyone with a jazzy rendition of "New York, New York." Although Battier's singing might not be as polished as his defense, it was undoubtedly heartfelt, and his performance was fully committed, showcasing great stage presence.

**Five Words to Sum Up Battier**

The "Happy Camp" production team summarized this "Batman" with five English words: high, air, papa, perfect, and Yao. "High" refers to Battier's height of 2.03 meters, while "air" highlights his indispensable role on the Rockets. "Papa" reflects one of Battier's current identities—"Tomorrow is my son's 15th month birthday." This simple statement from Battier revealed the tender side of this tough court warrior: "My son is now my heart and soul. No matter what happens on the court, seeing my son at home always makes me feel warm. The joy brought by any honor on the court can hardly compare to my son." Battier also shared that when his son was born, he cried for ten minutes out of sheer happiness.

"Perfect" represents people's affirmation of Battier, though he humbly stated, "I'm far from perfect."

**Yao Refers to Yao Ming**

As Yao Ming's teammate, Battier showered him with praise: "He is an excellent person who well represents China's image. He deserves all Chinese people's pride." However, Battier also disclosed some amusing anecdotes about Yao Ming. Every year before the NCAA tournament, players in the Rockets' locker room would bet $100 for entertainment. When Battier asked Yao Ming for the money, the Chinese giant would often hesitate and say, "Can I not participate... $100 is too much." Battier joked, "At such times, we would tease him, 'Yao, you're the one who earns the most in our group, but it seems like you're squeezing water out of a rock when it comes to handing over $100.'" It appears that Yao Ming's "frugality" has become one of the jokes in the Rockets' locker room.

**Professional Basketball vs. Street Basketball - Battier Wins Narrowly**

Two street basketball enthusiasts were invited to the show. In the challenge, professional basketball faced off against street basketball—each round, one side could shoot in any posture, and the other side had to replicate the same shot to avoid losing. Battier took on two young street basketball players. Despite facing numerous challenges, including various fancy moves, Battier managed to narrowly win. As a reward for his victory, Battier received a birthday cake since September 9th was his 31st birthday.

**Playing "Guess What I Mean" with Xie Na**

Despite the language barrier between Battier and host Xie Na, their cooperation was seamless and invincible. High-difficulty terms like "juicer," "hen laying eggs," and "washing machine" were all successfully guessed. Battier's mimicking talent amazed everyone—he imitated Michael Jackson with great accuracy, even performing the moonwalk despite his towering height, which surpassed Jordan's imitation years ago. His portrayal of a "washing machine" was so vivid that it left the hosts impressed. Battier jokingly remarked, "Some people say I'm better at guessing games than playing basketball."

Additionally, under the guidance of a cartoon teacher, Battier displayed his drawing talent by creating a caricature of Yao Ming that won the audience's praise for capturing Yao's essence.

Despite the lengthy recording process, Battier cooperated fully, utilizing his strengths to interact positively with the audience. Even after a tiring promotional tour, he demonstrated remarkable professionalism by fully committing himself to the program's recording.

Battier participated in Hunan TV's "Happy Camp," revealing some of Yao Ming's "little secrets" under the host's persistent questioning.

In the show, the hosts employed various methods to get Battier to share stories about Yao Ming. Eventually, Battier lived up to expectations by exposing Yao Ming on live television. Despite earning over $100 million annually, Yao Ming was described by Battier as being quite frugal: "Every year during the NCAA tournament, everyone in the locker room places bets of $100 each, choosing their favorite teams. Yao Ming, unfamiliar with the participating teams, would always say, 'Can I not participate? $100 is too much.'"

Battier also revealed another surprising habit of Yao Ming in the locker room: "Yao Ming's fastest habit that surprises his teammates is his lightning-fast shower speed." Battier laughed, "You know, Yao Ming may move slowly on the court, but in the locker room, his showering speed is unmatched."

Battier used owls and gorillas to describe himself and Yao Ming: "I think I am an owl because owls are highly observant creatures. They are usually quiet, but if provoked, they can retaliate with sharp claws." Regarding Yao Ming, Battier said, "Many people think Yao Ming is like an elephant, but I believe he resembles a gorilla—caring for his companions and the next generation. Every time I see a gorilla grooming its child, I think of Yao Ming."

The enthusiasm of fans deeply moved Battier: "Each time, I am surrounded by the same passionate energy."

At the "Happy Camp" recording site, despite the long recording process, Battier fully cooperated, actively showcasing his interaction with the audience. On the last day of his visit to Zhuzhou, Battier said, "On the final day, I will still bring the greatest joy to the fans." The fans in Zhuzhou reciprocated with even greater passion. Originally, September 9th was planned as Battier's birthday.

"Happy Camp" quickly positioned itself in the Chinese television entertainment landscape with its fresh, youthful, joyful, and relatable game style, maintaining its influence and promoting the concept of happiness.
