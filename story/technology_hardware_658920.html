Rangers' Hamilton confirms alcohol relapse

by akengw94 on 2012-02-04 17:28:50

Hamilton said specifically that he had a "weak moment" from "personal reasons" involving the origin of family members. He said that he went to the restaurant for dinner, ended up "ordering drinks and probably had three or four drinks." In January 2009, he drank excessively at a Tempe bar in Arizona, and later apologized when dozens of pictures were posted online showing Hamilton leaving the bar, dancing, and hugging several young women. Then he said that he had been sober since October 2005. "To everyone I have hurt, everyone - fans, kids, people with addictions who look up to me - I apologize to you," he said. "When you do this, you don't intentionally hurt anyone; you just feel like you're hurting yourself, but I know that I've hurt a lot of people."

The Texas Rangers' slugger and recovering addict said that he had three or four "drinks" and continued drinking at a Dallas restaurant and bars after dinner on Monday evening. "In general, I make the right choices in my life, I try to stay in recovery, and as far as my baseball goes, everything revolves around my relationship with the Lord," Hamilton said earlier at the Rangers Ballpark. "I see it like this: you all know how hard I try to play on the field; I give it everything I absolutely have. When I don't do that, both on and off the field, I leave myself open to a weak moment."

Hamilton and General Manager Jon Daniels said that the outfielder would meet with Major League Baseball in New York soon to evaluate his ongoing recovery with doctors and counselors.

Although Hamilton told Ian Kinsler that he did not intend to be competitive, Hamilton later admitted that he returned to where they left off and had more drinks. Hamilton did not explain or provide any real indication of what these things were.

The team announced last month that Hamilton's father, who is legally employed as a special assistant to the staff, was hired as an accountability partner, but Michael Dean Chadwick has decided not to accept the position due to "family considerations."

After having a few drinks during dinner, Hamilton called Ian Kinsler to come and hang out with him.

"Things happened personally that I'm not proud of after I drank, they are personal, and I deal with them," he said at a specially requested press conference on Friday to address the incident. "Knowing this will be reported in the media, Twitter, people get excited. There's me having a beer with someone or something similar and taking any picture, but I didn't take pictures with people."

While the team knew about Hamilton's off-field issues when they acquired him from the Cincinnati Reds in December 2007, his drug use was monitored with weekly tests three times a week, and he had an accountability partner to support his recovery - although the position is now vacant.

Hamilton said that Kinsler was unaware that he had been drinking, and he never had a drink in front of his teammate, even when they left the restaurant for 25-30 minutes before it closed and went to another place. Kinsler then drove him back to where he lived nearby.

This is Hamilton's second known alcohol-related relapse within three years. Both occurred during the off-season, while this one happened as the Rangers were discussing a contract extension with him.

"It would be great if it was about the contract being discussed, but we'll put that aside for a bit before we leave the room," Daniels agreed, having already agreed with Hamilton's agent, Mike Moye, to pause contract negotiations. Daniels said there is no timeline for recovery.

Hamilton said that he hasn't taken any drugs and has no plans to do so. He mentioned that he has undergone two drug tests since Monday as part of his normal legal procedure.

Hamilton could become a free agent after this season, and talks about extending his contract with the Rangers have been ongoing.

Hitting coach Johnny Narron played a major role in supporting the former No.1 overall draft pick, but Narron left the Rangers for Milwaukee in November.

Hamilton was suspended for over three years due to drug and alcohol use while in the Tampa Bay organization. The No.1 overall draft pick missed the entire 2003, 2004, and 2005 seasons but has since become one of the best players in baseball, helping his team win the American League pennant in the past two years. He was the AL MVP in 2010.

"I don't know, I wasn't there," Daniels said. "It's up to Josh on how to handle these things, now."

After his public apology earlier in the day, Hamilton appeared at a Christian men's gathering near Houston, Texas, which was scheduled for Friday night. He again did not take any questions, only speaking to the crowd.

At 30 years old, Hamilton said his latest actions "hurt a lot of people close to me." At one point, he closed his eyes and seemed to force a smile again. He did not take any questions during his 12-minute appearance.

Daniels said hiring someone for the job was a process that had reached the final stages before the recent events. He said the announcement would be made next week.

While Daniels said that Hamilton's relapse created some emotions, including disappointment, the GM stated that the primary concern was for Hamilton and his family. Hamilton and his wife have four daughters, the youngest of whom was born last summer.

"I can hide the shame and withdraw tonight without showing it, but I don't want to do that," Hamilton told the group while reiterating his Christian faith. "What I do, I had to fess up today; I want it to be personal. I fess up knowing people will call me a hypocrite, but I am a sinner."

Josh Hamilton shed no tears and used no prepared statements as he apologized and shared some details about his alcohol relapse. Nonetheless, it was clear that he was disturbed by what he had done.

Daniels, who spoke in town and later on a conference call, was asked if he was concerned that this incident was merely Hamilton having a few drinks. The GM referred to Hamilton saying that alcohol led the outfielder to do things he wasn't proud of.

But Daniels said that such a person might not always be with Hamilton during the off-season when he goes home to be with his family, which was the case this week.

On Friday, in a tweet, Hamilton's wife, Katie, wrote: "Really appreciating all the encouragement and support calls we've received. God is faithful and forgiving, so thank you all... Showing us such love and encouragement during this time."
