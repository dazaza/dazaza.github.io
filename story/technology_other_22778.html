Stocks are investment, not speculation.

by hmjhao on 2007-06-18 13:49:39

The day when the Market interviewed Huang Xiaokun, General Manager of the Research Center of Wanlian Securities, coincided with the phase after the news of stamp tax increase was released and when the stock market was relatively depressed. Passing by the trading hall of a business office of Wanlian Securities, one could obviously feel the gloomy atmosphere. The crowd opening accounts was no longer bustling, but orderly. Dr. Huang Xiaokun's first sentence was: "Only through bloody lessons can we know what risk is."

"In my eyes, there are no stock traders, only investors," Huang Xiaokun emphasized. Regarding the currently hot stock market, Huang Xiaokun believed that Renminbi appreciation and continuous growth in Gross National Product indicated that, from a long-term perspective, the stock market would be in a strong upward trend and would experience a long-term bull market. In such a bull market, ordinary investors should focus on the micro rather than the macro, industries and companies rather than the stock market itself. Only by focusing on value investment can one share in the fruits of China's economic growth. Stocks are merely symbols; the industries and companies behind the stocks are what investors need to analyze and invest in. Only by holding onto the stocks of excellent enterprises for a long time can one share in the wealth effect brought about by the capital market. "If you don't want to hold it for a year, don't hold it even for a day." "If you want to buy the biggest bull stock in 10 years, choose the best company in 10 years." Huang Xiaokun illustrated with examples.

Nowadays, many new stock traders lack securities knowledge and cannot select stocks. They often act on rumors, move quickly, frequently buy and sell, and even just aim to make quick money by buying junk stocks. Regarding this phenomenon, Huang Xiaokun's viewpoint was very clear: "Always remember one thing: what you buy is not a stock, but a company." In his view, one should buy stocks as if making an industrial investment, always keeping in mind that the investment is a project, and seriously spending time and effort studying this project like an industrial investment, grasping the intrinsic value of the project. Focusing on value investment, investors should pay attention to changes in the fundamentals of the company rather than the overall market and constantly changing prices; price is merely the result. Excellent enterprises are the biggest bull stocks in a bull market and are more resistant to risks. Only by having a grasp on industries and companies can one steadily earn money. Given this, Dr. Huang Xiaokun does not recommend short-term trading but suggests heavy positions and long-term holding, which allows investors to grasp China's long-term bull market and obtain good returns in the stock market.

Huang Xiaokun believes that now the financial industry is continuously moving towards specialization and refinement, and the stage of individual stock trading has already passed. If one lacks time or relevant knowledge and cannot grasp industries and companies, it would be better to buy funds and entrust the funds to professionals for management, choosing the best company's fund and trusting expert financial management.

"Market fluctuations are uncontrollable; what can be controlled are industries and companies, so risks must be controlled strategically," Huang Xiaokun repeatedly emphasized: "What to buy is always more important than how to buy; in the stock market, it's a test of who can be the long-distance running champion, not the short-distance running champion." And the analysis of companies and industries is often beyond the grasp of ordinary people.

Speaking of what kind of mentality to use when participating in the stock market, Huang Xiaokun said meaningfully: "Cultivation." One can only experience it after going through sleepless nights of pain. "It's like walking along the Pearl River, where one can see a bright moon as well as the eternal sky." For every investor, maintaining a peaceful mindset and guarding against market risks is very important.
