How to deal with the environmental pollution caused by sand and gravel materials

by hx515317 on 2012-02-27 13:45:47

The design of the sand and gravel processing technology should, under the premise of meeting the technical requirements for concrete aggregates, choose dry production processes that have a relatively lighter impact on the environment as much as possible. When selecting wet production processes, reasonable wastewater treatment processes must be adopted to comprehensively utilize wastewater and waste residue, turning waste into treasure, minimizing the disposal of waste residue, and ensuring compliance with environmental protection requirements.

During the mining and processing, it is easy to cause damage and harm to the natural environment and human production and living environments. This mainly includes the destruction of vegetation and slope safety stability caused by material field mining, the impact of dust and noise generated during the crushing and screening process on air quality and human survival environment, as well as the impact of wastewater and waste residue generated from washing and screening on the natural environment of rivers, valleys, and land.

Especially during the crushing and screening process of sand and gravel, in order to ensure the quality of finished sand and gravel and reduce processing costs, processing methods generally include full dry production, full wet production, or a combination of both processes based on the characteristics and scale of the processed raw materials. The crushing of sand and gravel will produce a large amount of dust and noise. The vibrating screen makes the material move linearly or circularly on the screen surface through a motor-driven exciter. The jumping of the material on the screen surface and the mechanical movement of the vibrating screen will both produce noise. Dry screening will produce a large amount of dust, while wet screening requires handling a large amount of sewage.

Sand and gravel processing equipment are mostly strong sources of noise and dust, causing pollution to the surrounding environment and posing a threat to the health of production personnel. These factors should be considered in advance during system equipment selection and technical design.

1. Selection of crushers should meet the determined crushing ratio in the process, and among similar equipment, priority should be given to those with a low center of gravity, small rotor or blade motion amplitude, and excellent protective performance, controlling noise at its source to the minimum extent.

2. On the basis of ensuring screening efficiency, the screen mesh should be selected with good shock absorption effects, such as polyurethane screen mesh.

3. From an environmental protection perspective during the system planning stage, the system's location should be chosen far away from office and residential areas, while the crushing and screening workshop should preferably be located in a mountain concave area to avoid the diffusion of dust and noise.

4. When designing the foundation for equipment placement, structures capable of reducing equipment vibration should be adopted, or new materials beneficial for reducing equipment vibration can be used.
