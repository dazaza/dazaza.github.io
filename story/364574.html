In the cyber world, when the Dao reaches a certain height, the magic rises even higher.

by yorkey0926 on 2010-09-15 17:05:46

 

The Internet is a virtual entity with strong vitality and astonishing reproductive power. It brings the whole world together and tightly attracts hundreds of millions of netizens to each terminal. The development of the Internet has undergone some qualitative changes, from initially accessing the Internet for convenience and necessity to now seeking huge profits or even causing chaos.

The Internet has become an indispensable part of daily life. In order to regulate its legal operation and development, the state continuously issues monitoring laws to provide a legal environment for the healthy development of the Internet. Although the means of Internet monitoring are relatively stringent, there are always those who manage to operate on the fringes of the law.

Hacker attacks are an ageless legend

In the public eye, hackers are "computer troublemakers." Since 1997, when hackers first emerged, the legend of hacking has rapidly developed. From hacker software like Back Orifice, to BO, and then Trojan horses, many netizens began their first experiences with hacking.

Chinese hackers have also made waves during international incidents. In 1998, the large-scale massacre, rape, and abuse of Indonesians of Chinese descent infuriated Chinese hackers who were just learning to walk. They gathered in IRC chat rooms in groups of eight to six and sent spam emails to Indonesian government websites and attacked Indonesian sites using ping methods.

Since then, events such as NATO's war against Serbia, the bombing of our embassy in Yugoslavia without cause, the failure of the "Dong Shilang Nanjing Massacre" case in 2000, and the "US-China plane collision incident" in our South China Sea region have repeatedly united Chinese hackers, stirring up nationalist sentiments.

The current hostage crisis involving a Hong Kong tour bus in the Philippines and Japan's tough stance on detaining our fishermen have also led to Chinese hackers launching attacks on these countries' official government networks.

In some industry competitions, there are inevitably many hacker activities - attacking competitors' main websites, which can be a significant loss for companies conducting product transactions online. IDC providers are undoubtedly the most affected in these attacks because they host all company websites. If an attack occurs, the direct bearer of the impact is the IDC company. If an IDC provider does not have a robust anti-hacking system, its position will be precarious. A typical example is Xinwang Interconnect and Guangzhou Henghui Network Communication Co., Ltd., both of which were attacked by hackers in September this year. It was found that these attacks were likely from industry competitors. As a result, Xinwang Interconnect's main website was nearly paralyzed for a month, while Guangzhou Henghui Network Communication Co., Ltd., with a better anti-attack system, managed to take timely and effective measures, minimizing the losses.

Therefore, a fortress-like anti-attack system is necessary.

Spam mails everywhere

Spam mail generally has the characteristic of being sent in bulk. Its content includes money-making information, adult advertisements, commercial or personal website ads, electronic magazines, chain letters, etc. Spam mail can be divided into benign and malignant types. Benign spam refers to various promotional advertisements that have little impact on the recipient. Malignant spam refers to destructive emails.

Spam mail can be considered one of the most controversial byproducts of the Internet, and its proliferation has already overwhelmed the entire Internet.

1. Occupying network bandwidth, causing email server congestion, thereby reducing the overall efficiency of the network;

2. Infringing on the privacy of recipients, occupying mailbox space, wasting the time, energy, and money of recipients. Some spam even uses others' email addresses as sending addresses, severely damaging others' reputations;

3. Being exploited by hackers as tools of destruction. For example, in February 2000, the hacking attack on five major popular websites like Yahoo! was one such instance. Hackers first infiltrated and controlled some high-bandwidth websites, concentrating the bandwidth capabilities of many servers, then bombarded the targets with billions of spam emails, causing network congestion and eventual paralysis;

4. Severely affecting the service image of ISPs. Internationally, hosts frequently forwarding spam are listed in international spam databases by upstream international Internet service providers, resulting in the inability to access many foreign networks. Moreover, users receiving spam may switch to other ISPs due to the lack of a comprehensive spam filtering mechanism. A survey shows: ISPs spend $75 to acquire a user but lose 7.2% of users annually due to spam;     

  5. Misleading people, scamming money, and spreading pornographic content through spam emails, which has already caused harm to real society.

Edit section on how to handle spam

Some people collect email addresses from multiple BBS forums, newsgroups, etc., online and sell them to advertisers, leading to spam being sent to these addresses. Often, in these emails, you can find a "remove from the mailing list" link. When users follow the link instructions, advertisers know the address is valid, and users will receive more spam.

As the problem of spam becomes increasingly severe, developers of corporate email systems have introduced anti-spam software. However, "as the Dao rises, so does the Devil," and the formats of spam emails are becoming more innovative to evade detection by such software.

Some spam organizations or illegal information disseminators, in order to spread information widely, often use multiple machines to send massive amounts simultaneously, attacking email servers, causing significant bandwidth losses and severely disrupting normal email delivery operations.

Currently, many countries have legislated to try to eliminate spam. Many network service providers include anti-spam policies in their service agreements and set up email addresses for complaints. Some online groups offer email analysis and complaint services on behalf of customers to relevant ISPs.

The “You时空” brand corporate email system has also launched an anti-spam service package to help customers, which is considered a thoughtful value-added service in the industry. Amidst positive customer feedback, many suppliers in the same industry have followed suit!

This is also a requirement for the development of a secure network environment in the evolution of corporate email systems.
