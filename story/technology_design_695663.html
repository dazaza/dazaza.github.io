County leader casually came up with the first half of a couplet, and in 5 years no one has been able to come up with the second half (picture)

by z3bsoa45119 on 2012-03-07 16:43:27

Is the couplet on the right pillar of the arch really an absolute match?

Who can come up with the lower half of this "casual couplet"? They will get free Yuchuan rice noodles for life.

"If anyone can neatly come up with the lower half of the Yuchuan rice noodle couplet, we will confer upon them the title of 'Honorary Citizen of Yuchuan', allowing them to eat Yuchuan rice noodles for free for life." Recently, Yang Guangyan, the county magistrate of Yuchuan, said these words during a hometown friends' symposium in Yuchuan, immediately prompting some of the attendees to jump at the chance.

Can a simple "rhyme" become an absolute match?

In fact, Yang Guangyan's statement isn't new. Five years ago, Yuchuan County had already widely solicited the lower half of the Yuchuan rice noodle couplet from society. At that time, relevant departments in the county issued a "heroic challenge": whoever could extremely neatly come up with the Yuchuan rice noodle couplet would be allowed to eat Yuchuan rice noodles for free for life. However, five years have passed, and no one has been able to come up with the couplet.

One day in 2004, a certain leader of the Yuchuan County Committee who enjoyed writing poetry was inspired while eating rice noodles for breakfast, and casually recited: "Yuchuan is good, good Yuchuan, Yuchuan rice noodles get better and better as you eat them." The leader initially didn't realize it, but then suddenly discovered how wonderful this sentence was - "Yuchuan" rhymes with "yue chi", and the last characters of the first two sentences combine to form the last word of the third sentence "hao chi" (delicious), which perfectly describes the excellent taste of Yuchuan rice noodles. This leader became even more enthusiastic: why not use this as the upper half of a couplet, and solicit the lower half from society?

That year, Yuchuan planned out a snack street on the south gate section of the original Huancheng East Road, named "Rice Noodle Street", and erected a large archway. The upper half of the couplet on the archway was "Yuchuan is good, good Yuchuan, Yuchuan rice noodles get better and better as you eat them", while on the side where the lower half should be, there were several large question marks "?" continuously marked.

5000 lower halves failed to neatly match

Ever since the archway was erected, it was common to see people under the archway deep in thought, pondering over their responses. Some even posted their own lower halves on the question-marked pillars of the archway, such as "Yincheng is good, good Yincheng, Yincheng people welcome the beautiful morning of Yincheng", "Taiyuan is beautiful, beautiful Taiyuan, Taiyuan moon too round too hard to circle", etc., all of which were forced and far from matching.

Of course, some lower halves were downright amusing, like "Remarriage is difficult, difficult remarriage, remarried individuals find remarriage harder and harder", etc. As a result, people kept pasting their responses, making the originally clean and beautiful archway dirty and messy, damaging Yuchuan's urban image. To prevent some citizens from randomly pasting on the archway, the county government even had to dispatch a police officer to stand guard in front of the archway.

It is said that within the first year after the upper half of the couplet was marked, Yuchuan County received over 1000 lower halves. After being hyped by media inside and outside the province, they received even more, accumulating to a total of 5000 lower halves. However, after careful screening, not a single one was found to completely and neatly match.

Of course, among the many lower halves, some seemed to roughly match at first glance, but upon closer analysis, still did not meet the requirements. For example, "Jiulong thrives, thrives Jiulong, Jiulong Square long prospers", although structurally consistent with the upper half, its "long prospers" seems to be artificially created to match the upper half.

A cultural personage in Yuchuan analyzed: "Regarding the Yuchuan rice noodle couplet, after deep thought, I realized that this couplet is indeed difficult to match. The main reasons are threefold: first, the sentence structure is progressive, 'the more...the more...'; second, the homophonic 'Yuchuan' and 'yue chi' form a 'five-layer water'. To come up with an extremely neat lower half, one must first overcome the two hurdles of progression and 'five-layer water'. Relatively speaking, forming 'five-layer water' is easier, but forming a sentence structure with a progressive relationship is harder. In Chinese, words expressing progressive relationships are usually 'more', 'even more', 'increasingly', excluding the 'more' in the upper half, the lower half can only use 'even more' and 'increasingly'. Many people have also thought of this point, thus coming up with the following lower halves: 'Yumin is clear, clear Yumin, Yumin officials become increasingly transparent'; 'Yuxi is fragrant, fragrant Yuxi, Yuxi cigarettes become increasingly fragrant as you smoke them', etc. Third, the 'more' in the upper half is a仄sound, to form a neat match, one must start from either 'even more' or 'increasingly', both of which also belong to仄sounds, not conforming to the principle of仄against平in neat matches."

This cultural figure frankly admitted: "If these three problems cannot be solved, it might truly become an absolute match."
