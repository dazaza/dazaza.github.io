It means identifying the customer demographic for your products. Are they brand enthusiasts? Fans of cost-effective goods? Trendsetters? Targeted towards a specific age group? To simplify further, product positioning in a store is about product pricing and store style. Regardless of the type of products you operate, just search for the major category on the Taobao homepage, and you'll find at least ten thousand stores selling similar goods. In the same market, others are experienced veterans, and you are a newcomer. To carve out a place among so many competitors, your store must have its own unique features and style. Of course, there are other conditions, such as promotion, service, integrity, etc., which we will not discuss here. Today, we mainly talk about store positioning.

How to determine the store's positioning and style, please first look at the characteristics of your supply source.

If your supply source is directly from the factory, generally speaking, you shouldn't worry about the price issue. Therefore, your positioning can be based on factory goods, and the factory goods' style will be the style of your store.

If you source from the wholesale market, it depends on the overall level of the wholesale market. In some big cities, such as Guangzhou, Shanghai, and Beijing, the selection of wholesale markets is vast, and the goods are relatively new. If your vision is good, you can position yourself as fresh, fashionable, and trendy. This group values the speed of product updates and the degree of alignment with fashion trends, allowing you to set prices slightly higher.

But if the goods from the wholesale market don't stand out significantly, it's best to set your prices low and win by price. Specializing in specific demographics, like some stores specializing in elderly clothing, maternity wear, or student uniforms, can create a stable market.

There are many other ways to define the style of your store, in summary, whether you can summarize the commonality of the products in your store with one or two keywords. If your current store can't achieve this, it indicates unclear positioning, in which case, you must calm down and adjust accordingly.

Why should new stores have clear positioning?

For new stores, product positioning must be clear because generally, new store owners have limited funds. If the product positioning spans too widely, it reduces the choice range for different consumer preferences. If I am someone who likes buying branded goods, upon entering your store, seeing a hundred or more items initially makes me happy. But after browsing a couple of pages, I realize only a third or less suits my needs, I won’t waste more time choosing in your store. I’d rather go to stores that specialize in brands, saving my search time.

I have deep insight into this. Below, I will share my personal experiences to illustrate the importance of store positioning. My store specializes in foreign trade women's clothing. In the first three months of operation, overall sales were better than expected. Basically, even during the worst times, sales occurred every two to three days, and each sale usually involved more than one item. Because my sourcing channels were relatively fixed, all foreign trade products, plus price advantages, the number of store favorites kept increasing. Within four months, I had risen to three hearts, making me very happy. Simultaneously, since the store's products were mainly knitwear, I had little confidence in summer sales, so I began actively seeking new sources to enrich the product variety and attract more types of customers. Due to different sources, the price positioning was higher than before, but due to limited funds, I diverted part of the original channel funds, restricting the variety of new channel products. Consequently, my store's products ranged from 9 yuan to over 400 yuan, but the choices within each price range weren't extensive. Significant changes ensued. By the second half of April, despite unchanged click rates, sales suddenly disappeared. Later, both click rates and conversions declined, and the number of store favorites ceased to increase. I was puzzled, searching for reasons. Initially, I thought the pictures weren't professional enough, so I bought a professional photography studio and increased participation in forums, which helped boost click rates, but sales remained stagnant. Later, comments from a few friends led me to identify the issue. They said the items in my store recently seemed mixed. Suddenly, I remembered the importance of product positioning. Recalling previous high-sales periods, I realized the products were clearly positioned, mainly inexpensive foreign trade products priced below 40 yuan. However, things became inconsistent, and the decline in sales started precisely then. I immediately adjusted the product structure, reallocating most of the working capital back to the original channel. One week after new arrivals, click rates and sales showed signs of recovery, and the number of store favorites began to grow again. I sighed in relief!

Based on my experience, new stores should not exceed two major product categories; more would create confusion. Simultaneously, the price range for each major category should not exceed 150 yuan, ensuring clarity. Of course, when you develop well and stabilize sales, enriching your store's product range is entirely possible and will ensure sales stability.

For newcomers, the growth of a store isn't merely a simple accumulation of credit and increasing sales, but a process requiring continuous exploration, summarization, and adjustment. During slow business periods, new sellers should carefully analyze reasons and seek solutions. With effort, success is sure to come.
