This is considered the most expensive film in Turkish history, based on the historical battle where Sultan Mehmet II conquered Constantinople, the capital of the Byzantine Empire, in 1453. It is an epic-scale film comparable to Hollywood productions.

The Muslims' capture of Byzantium closed the trade routes between East and West, forcing Europeans to seek new paths by sea, leading to the Age of Discovery. Meanwhile, before the conquest of Byzantium, the Islamic world had been gradually declining, becoming increasingly backward and stagnant—a situation that has continued to the present day. Reflecting on history feels truly ironic!

Behind-the-Scenes Trivia:

• The film premiered nationwide in Turkey at exactly 2:53 PM on February 16, 2012, drawing over 1.5 million viewers in its opening weekend and rewriting the history of Turkish cinema.

• Production began in 2009 and took nearly three years to complete.

• The first filming took place in a nearly 4,000-square-meter studio, where 40 designers and animators worked together to create 3D reconstructions of Byzantine and Edirne palaces. Most of the story's settings were digitally enhanced. The initial shooting lasted for four weeks.

• The second round of filming moved to the city of Edirne in the northwest, specifically at the Bayezid II Mosque. Filming there lasted six weeks.
