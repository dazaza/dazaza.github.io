Automobile Weibo Marketing: Manufacturers Boost Popularity Through Weibo

by edivista on 2011-11-22 08:50:55

Click to enter the full list of automakers' Weibo accounts.

One day, a friend posted a Weibo message on a rainy street: "All-powerful Weibo, quickly give me a car." Of course, the all-powerful Weibo did not immediately respond to her, but more and more automakers began to pay attention to the needs of Weibo users. After all, the number of Weibo users has reached a certain scale, and the close interaction provided by this platform is indeed quite advantageous.

In recent days, Dongfeng Honda announced its sponsorship of Sina Weibo's short video competition. Industry insiders believe that this cooperation will become another milestone for the automotive industry to explore social marketing through the Weibo platform. This comment clearly has some exaggerated elements, or perhaps since the emergence of Weibo, the term "milestone" no longer exists because there are too many milestones, at most it can be called a new model.

Weibo boosts popularity

Mercedes-Benz had already tried a more closely integrated marketing model on Twitter. When the new Mercedes-Benz C-Class Coupe was launched, the Super Bowl in the United States was about to take place. Thus, the execution company linked the number of tweets to the car. In the advertisement, you could see the relevant keyword tweets posted by users, and these tweets could control the car's movements. They equipped four cars with special devices, each team having specific keywords, such as one called MBteamsGL. When users post tweets with this keyword, accumulating four tweets would make the car move 1 mile (the destination being the Super Bowl venue).

Among these four teams, the first to arrive would win a corresponding prize, either a Mercedes-Benz car or a Super Bowl ticket. This competitive game was a great success, with more followers than expected, and the Mercedes-Benz C-Class Coupe naturally left a deep impression.

The reason Mercedes-Benz adopted such a marketing method is due to Twitter's user base. With the rapid rise of domestic Weibo in China, domestic automakers have increasingly valued this platform, and Weibo marketing has almost become one of the essential marketing methods for automobile enterprises.

According to statistics, the Weibo account opening rate among Chinese auto internet users reached 64% in the first half of the year; and the opening speed is rapidly increasing; internet users use Weibo frequently, with nearly half of auto internet users often logging into Weibo; two to three tenths of auto internet users often forward or comment on Weibo posts, over 70% of internet users have more than 10 followers, and nearly 15% of auto internet users have over a hundred followers. Moreover, nearly three-quarters of auto internet users stated they would follow the official Weibo accounts of automakers; when auto brands launch their official Weibo accounts, information about new car releases or listings and daily usage tips are very popular among auto internet users. Over 80% of auto internet users expressed interest in forwarding or commenting on content from auto official Weibo accounts.

Although the sound of a cooling car market is rampant, the domestic demand for cars has not actually decreased. If a more precise and cost-effective marketing method can be found, automakers would certainly welcome it.

Currently, the vast majority of mainstream automakers have already opened official Weibo accounts, and some companies and brands have over ten thousand fans. For example, Mercedes-Benz Smart opened a Weibo account early on, initially only posting PR soft articles and other information, but without much attention. Later, after realizing this, they adjusted their direction, started posting global events with many beautiful pictures, and gradually increased their fan base. Not only trendy cars value Weibo, but recently, Changan Automobile also announced that it has teamed up with its dealers to collectively open Weibo accounts, providing a new communication channel for car enthusiasts. Besides product introductions, various activities were held on Weibo, including collecting netizens' energy-saving methods for cars and brainstorming ideas for future cars.

Weibo marketing tests skills

Automakers' use of Weibo goes beyond just posting information. Some companies have also begun trying to sell cars via Weibo. Besides individual marketing promotion cases, various manufacturers have also set up online showrooms, gradually starting to sell cars online. However, relying on Weibo to directly sell cars is not yet practical, and it is still mainly used as a marketing promotion tool and an information acquisition channel.

Interestingly, through comparison, we found that automakers' optimism towards the Weibo platform is significantly greater than that of other companies. According to the American marketing information website clickz.com, according to Twitter's Chief Revenue Officer Adam Bain, in the company's first-quarter online advertising revenue this year, 12% came from advertisements placed by various automakers. Currently, Twitter's promoted account service is sold at auction, with advertisers paying based on the cost per follower, targeting certain keywords in user account data, such as which other users the account follows, etc. Bain revealed that automobile advertisers pay up to $70 per follower, but the average price in this field is between $10-$40.

Sina, which has always claimed not to rush to monetize Weibo, of course, saw this point. The number of Sina Weibo users has reached 200 million. Calculated by browsing time, Sina Weibo's market share is as high as 87%, with an average stay time of 60 minutes per user, and more than 75 million blog posts are published daily; 50,000 companies have settled in. In July this year, Sina announced the launch of its micro-video strategy, focusing on valuable short videos, and launched the industry's first micro-video channel. The first Sina Micro Video Competition sponsored by Dongfeng Honda clearly conveys the marketing significance of micro-videos: low threshold, easy to spread, long cycle. How can automakers who want to save money and leave a deep impression not be tempted?

However, automakers need to note that Weibo marketing is far more than just spreading information. Excessive posting of information may backfire, frequent hype can also cause fatigue. Perhaps automakers really should carefully consider giving their fans the right to know, speak, and participate sincerely during marketing, building a deeper trust relationship with their fans. This is a skill-intensive task.
