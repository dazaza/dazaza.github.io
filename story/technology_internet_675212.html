Physical Development Characteristics

The baby's development includes physical and sensory development. Physical development includes height, weight, head circumference, chest circumference, etc., all of which have certain indicators for reference. Sensory development includes vision, hearing, smell, etc. If there are any special aspects about the newborn, parents must pay attention.

Physical Development Status

In the first month, the newborn will experience a relatively noticeable change in height and weight, which delights the parents as they watch the baby grow day by day. Didine [http://www.banglikonggu.com/]. Parents hope their baby grows faster. In fact, infant physical development follows certain rules and has certain indicators. Although each baby is different, as long as the child is healthy, they generally reach these indicators, so parents don't need to worry too much if the baby isn't fat enough or tall enough; health is the key.

◆ Height: At the end of the first month, the average height of male infants is centimeters (51.2～60.9 centimeters).

◆ Weight: At the end of the first month, the average weight of male infants is kilograms (3.5～5.7 kilograms).

◆ Head Circumference: In the first month, the head circumference of newborns increases by about Didine [http://www.banglikonggu.com/] centimeters. At birth, the average head circumference of males is centimeters (34.7～39.5 centimeters).

◆ Chest Circumference: At birth, the chest circumference is smaller than the head circumference by centimeters. As age increases, the growth rate of the chest circumference slowly surpasses that of the head circumference, reaching equality at around months old. Thereafter, the chest circumference gradually becomes larger than the head circumference. In the newborn period, the chest circumference increases by about centimeters. At birth, the average chest circumference for males is centimeters (female average centimeters (32.9～40.1 centimeters)).

◆ Anterior Fontanelle: On the forehead of an infant, there is a diamond-shaped soft area without skull bones called the anterior fontanelle. A bulging or sunken anterior fontanelle is abnormal and should be checked by a doctor.

◆ Upper Body Length: The upper body length refers to the distance from the top of the head to the upper edge of the pubic symphysis, usually indicating the length of the upper body. At birth, it is usually centimeters, and by the end of the first month, it can exceed centimeters. Generally, the upper body length value for male infants is slightly larger than that of female infants.

◆ Lower Body Length: The lower body length value refers to the distance from the upper edge of the pubic symphysis to the sole of the foot, usually indicating the length of the lower body. At birth, it is usually centimeters, and by the end of the first month, it can reach centimeters. Generally, the lower body length for male and female infants is similar.

◆ Teeth: It's not yet time for newborns to teethe. However, some newborns may have yellowish-white sesame-sized particles on their gums, slightly raised, or growing along the midline of the palate, commonly known as "horse teeth." These white particles are formed by the accumulation of keratinized epithelial cells. They usually disappear after a few weeks, neither being teeth nor a disease. When encountering such a situation, never use a needle to prick them or a cloth to wipe them off [horse teeth].

◆ Subcutaneous Fat Thickness: Subcutaneous fat thickness is one of the important indicators for observing nutritional status. Usually, at the intersection of the clavicle midline and the umbilical horizontal line, the subcutaneous fat is gently lifted with the thumb and index finger, and pinched together. If the thickness between them is about centimeters, it falls within the normal range. Newborns, when just born, have relatively thin subcutaneous fat, usually not reaching centimeters. If fed properly, by the end of the first month, they can reach the aforementioned standard.

◆ Body Shape: After birth, according to the above measurement results, all parts of the body develop proportionally. If the head is too small or too large, the limbs are overly short, the spine protrudes backward or has a lump, or there are facial organ defects, these all belong to pathological body development.

◆ Sleep: During the first month of a baby's life, i.e., the neonatal period, they generally sleep hours a day, but this is not absolute as the length of sleep varies individually. Sleep is very important for babies, so room temperature and bedding should be fully considered to create a happy and comfortable sleep environment.
