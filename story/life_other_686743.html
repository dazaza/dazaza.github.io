According to the Modern Express, more than half a month ago, a car hit a bus stop on Jichuan East Road in Taizhou. To outsiders, this was an ordinary traffic accident, but after local residents measured it, they found that the thickness of the steel pipe supporting the bus stop was only 4 millimeters. Previously, Taizhou Bus Company had stated that the wall thickness was 8 millimeters.

On March 1, a reporter from the Modern Express went to Taizhou for an interview. A responsible person from the Taizhou Bus Company admitted that the standard thickness of the steel pipe wall was 8 millimeters, and the specifications of the steel plates purchased by the construction party were only 4 millimeters, 5 millimeters, and 6 millimeters. This means that none of the newly built 201 bus stops in Taizhou met the design requirements. This project, which was bid at over 11 million yuan, has been pushed to the forefront of public criticism.

Ms. Liu said that the construction design drawing of the bus stop specified the steel pipe size as 200 millimeters x 250 millimeters. However, in reality, the steel pipe was 150 millimeters wide and 200 millimeters long. In response, a responsible person from Taizhou Public Transportation Co., Ltd. explained: In order to add a tactile paving, the design of the bus stop was modified before construction, and what Ms. Liu saw was not the latest drawing.

Citizen Tip-off: The Wall Thickness of the Steel Pipe at the Bus Stop is Half

A few nights ago, Grandma Wang, who was selling strawberries by the side of Jichuan East Road in Taizhou, heard a loud collision sound.

Grandma Wang quickly ran out to check and found that a car had hit the bus stop by the roadside and rushed onto the slow lane. "At that time, the bus stop was completely crashed through, part of the steel pipe supporting the east side of the bus stop was broken, and the scene was in chaos."

Subsequently, Ms. Liu (a pseudonym) brought a vernier caliper to this damaged bus stop. She measured the thickness of the steel pipe wall at the break. The measurement result surprised her: the wall thickness was only 4 millimeters. Ms. Liu remembered that last year, a responsible person from the Taizhou Bus Company had mentioned in the media that the wall thickness of the steel pipe supporting the bus stop should be 8 millimeters. The reporter found a video of the program aired on October 17 last year on the local TV station's website. In the video, a responsible person from the bus company stated: "The wall thickness of the steel plate is 8 millimeters."

Ms. Liu said that last year, she learned during a casual conversation with a friend that the Taizhou bus stops had serious cutting corners behavior, "the originally tendered square steel pipe of 200 millimeters x 250 millimeters had shrunk to 150 millimeters x 200 millimeters."

Ms. Liu said that at first, she didn't quite believe it and even took a ruler to measure the bus stop. But the measurement results were the same as what her friend said: the steel pipe supporting the platform was 150 millimeters wide and 200 millimeters long. By chance, she saw the construction design drawings for the bus stop when it was initially tendered, which indeed specified the steel pipe size as 200 millimeters x 250 millimeters. This meant that the steel pipe for the bus stop had become narrower compared to the original design.

In October 2011, Ms. Liu posted the information she had obtained online. Regarding why the steel pipe became thinner, a responsible person from Taizhou Public Transportation Co., Ltd. explained on television: In order to add tactile paving, the design of the bus stop was modified before construction, and what Ms. Liu saw was not the latest drawing. This responsible person also indicated that although the length and width of the steel pipe had decreased, the originally designed 6-millimeter-thick wall had been upgraded to 8 millimeters, so safety would also be guaranteed.

With the broadcast of the news, this matter came to an end. Until the bus stop on Jichuan East Road was hit, Ms. Liu reviewed the previous news online and confirmed that the responsible person from the bus company had personally stated that the wall thickness should be 8 millimeters. "Could the design have been changed again?"

Are All 201 Newly Built Stops Problematic?

Bus Company: None Meets Design Requirements

Subsequently, reporters from the Express contacted Taizhou Public Transportation Co., Ltd., the construction unit of the bus stops.

A company responsible person introduced that these bus stops started construction at the end of 2009, with a total of 201 stops. At that time, through the bidding office of Taizhou Construction Bureau, two construction units were determined via public bidding, with a budget exceeding 11 million yuan. All works were gradually completed by 2011, but due to quality issues, no acceptance inspection was conducted.

This responsible person said that the standard thickness of the steel pipe wall is 8 millimeters, and there indeed existed cutting corners during the construction of the bus stops. This responsible person revealed that because of the incident at the end of last year, the Discipline Inspection Commission of Taizhou Transportation Bureau had already intervened in the investigation of the bus stop construction.

In addition, the Discipline Inspection Commission retrieved the original purchase receipts from the steel plate factory where the construction party bought materials. The receipts showed that the specifications of the steel plates purchased by the construction party were only 4 millimeters, 5 millimeters, and 6 millimeters, with no purchase of 8-millimeter steel plates. This means that none of the 201 newly built bus stops in Taizhou met the design requirements.

This responsible person said that because of this matter, each construction unit still had 2 million yuan of project funds withheld and unsettled.

Why Did the Engineering Quality Have Problems?

Construction Unit: Bus Company's Project Funds Were Not in Place

After much effort, the reporter contacted Gao Manager, the responsible person of one of the construction units.

Gao Manager on the phone said that throughout the two years or so, the bus company often did not pay the project funds on time, leaving him full of grievances about this project. So, did they cut corners because of this? Gao Manager said he was driving and would call back later. However, up until the reporter's deadline, Gao Manager's mobile phone remained in a "text message call" state.

Ms. Liu then told the reporter that Taizhou would build another 200 bus stops this year, and she would then take out her vernier caliper one by one to measure them, fulfilling her duty as an ordinary citizen.

How Should the Already Built Platforms Be Handled?

Construction Party Response: No Hazard If Thickness Reaches 4 Millimeters

What should be done with the already built 201 platforms that do not meet the design requirements?

The responsible person of the bus company said that they had already asked a professional company to assess the safety coefficient of the platforms. The assessment results showed that as long as the wall thickness reached more than 4 millimeters, there would be no safety hazards, meaning that although these 201 bus stops cut corners, they did not affect usage. "If there are any with a thickness less than 4 millimeters, they will definitely be dismantled and rebuilt," this responsible person said, with costs borne by the construction party.

The reporter contacted the unit responsible for the safety assessment, Wang Deping, the chief architect of Jiangsu Tongsheng Architectural Design Co., Ltd. He told the reporter that an 8-millimeter-thick wall was unnecessary for bus stops, "only factories use 8-millimeter or 10-millimeter thick steel pipes."

Wang Deping said that a 4-millimeter-thick steel pipe was sufficient unless there was a problem with the quality of the steel. Regarding this statement, Ms. Liu did not agree: "You say 8 millimeters is unnecessary, did the people who designed the drawings know that? This is definitely an excuse."

Can Cutting Corners Save 60% of the Cost?

What Are the Construction Standards for Nanjing Bus Signboards? Is There Any Cutting Corners?

It is understood that Nanjing's bus signboards generally include advertisement boards and booths. Yesterday, the reporter from the Express interviewed the responsible person from Mediaplayer Advertising Company. This responsible person said that according to different requirements for signboard construction, the standards for bus signboards vary across cities. There are currently no mandatory national standards for this area.

The responsible person from Mediaplayer Advertising Company said that the bus signboards in the main urban areas of Nanjing are basically all constructed by them. Their requirement is to design and install according to a 15-year lifespan. Both ends of the advertisement boards are stainless steel pipe columns with a wall thickness of 6 millimeters, and the booths are made up of square seamless steel pipes with a wall thickness of 6 millimeters plus top canopy advertisement boards.

According to the design requirements, the booths and signboards must be able to withstand 11-grade winds and 8-grade earthquakes. It is required that after being hit by a car, there should be no splinters from the signboards and booths.

This responsible person frankly admitted that there are no mandatory national standards for the construction of bus signboards and booths, so the standards vary in different places.

He stated that the service life and strength of booths and signboards is a systematic project related to various details, such as pre-buried parts and good waterproofing treatment, rust-proofing treatment on the surface of steel pipes, and daily maintenance and inspections. They send people every day to patrol and inspect, and for booth steel pipes with problems, they usually need to repair and replace them every three years.

Due to different construction standards, the material prices of booths in different places vary greatly.

This responsible person gave an example: For instance, some booths cost 50,000 yuan to build according to their requirements, while some "wild chicken" manufacturers in other places cut corners and can get it done for 20,000 yuan. Advertising companies, in pursuit of maximum profit, are prone to choose low-cost booths, leading to short lifespans, low strength, and rapid corrosion.

Although the appearance may be the same, the internal differences can be significant. The rampant production of substandard and low-priced signboards frustrates many reputable manufacturers. It is hoped that the country will introduce relevant standards to unify and regulate the construction of bus signboards and reduce the hidden dangers caused by cutting corners.
