Dream interpreter: A father with renal failure refuses to take medicine and stop dialysis to treat his daughter who suffers from leukemia

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-03 10:37:24

On December 20, Xue Yandan communicated with the outside world only by phone from inside an isolation ward. (File photo)

Jinan, December 21st - (Reporter Liu Shuang) Xue Zhenwu from Tancheng, Linyi was found to have kidney failure in both kidneys, and his daughter was diagnosed with acute leukemia. This is undoubtedly adding frost on snow for this family. In order to treat his daughter, Xue Zhenwu resolutely gave up his own treatment and sought help everywhere. On the 20th, the Shandong Red Cross Rescue Network delivered 40,000 yuan in donations to him.

Both father and daughter suffer from serious illnesses

Xue Zhenwu is a worker at the well-drilling team of the Tancheng County Water Conservancy Bureau in Linyi. He is 48 years old this year. In 2008, during a physical examination at his workplace, he was found to have kidney failure in both kidneys. His wife had been laid off since 1995, and the family of three relied solely on his salary. This financially strained household could not afford Xue Zhenwu's high medical expenses. Due to limited treatment options, Xue Zhenwu's condition gradually worsened, and he urgently needed a kidney transplant surgery. With no other choice, his wife Zhang Lili had to work in another place to earn money.

Misfortunes never come singly. On September 23, 2010, Xue Yun Dan, who had just entered the Shandong Media Vocational College, suddenly fainted at school and was diagnosed with acute leukemia at the General Hospital of the Jinan Military Region. Zhang Lili, who was originally full of confidence in raising money for her husband’s kidney transplant, was almost struck down by this bolt from the blue.

In order to treat their daughter, Xue Zhenwu and his wife sold their house and all their assets, borrowing money from all their relatives and friends. To save money, when their daughter's condition slightly improved, they returned to their hometown. Unexpectedly, while waiting for a matching donor, Xue Yun Dan experienced two infections and has now returned to the Tianjin Hematology Hospital for treatment. Currently, her physical condition is very unstable. Her lungs have already been infected, and even if a suitable match is found, surgery cannot be performed immediately.

Refusing medication and stopping dialysis for his daughter

Seeing his daughter's condition worsening day by day, Xue Zhenwu repeatedly refused to take medicine and stopped kidney dialysis. He also refused the doctor's request to drink a pack of milk every day. He was determined to save the money for his own medication to treat his daughter and provide some nutrition for his wife. "She (his wife) has become too thin from taking care of us. If she collapses, what will happen to our family?"

Due to severe malnutrition, untimely treatment, overwork, and mental stress, Xue Zhenwu's condition gradually worsened. Now, he needs to rest after walking just a few steps, but his daughter is his spiritual pillar. He borrows money everywhere for his daughter and then gazes at her for a long time outside the isolation ward with a smile on his face.

"Whatever happens, we will not stop treating Yun Dan," said Xue Zhenwu. Due to the second round of chemotherapy, Yun Dan can currently only communicate with her parents outside the isolation room via phone. "I haven't gone out for several months. I've thought about a lot here. What I want most is to get better quickly, go back to school, and give my parents a hug."

To help Yun Dan's family, the Shandong Red Cross Rescue Network (www.sdjz.org.cn) initiated a fundraising campaign. On the 20th, the staff of the website delivered the collected 40,000 yuan in donations to Xue Zhenwu. However, 40,000 yuan is just a drop in the bucket for Yun Dan's treatment. Therefore, the Shandong Red Cross Rescue Network has set up a dedicated donation page on its homepage, hoping more kind-hearted people will come to help them.
