The Birth of the Dark Knight

From a young age, Mai Ti Shuang demonstrated exceptional business acumen and fully understood the principle that doing business was the way to prosperity. With this in mind, he studied hard, completing his MBA at Stanford University in the United States through sheer effort. While there, he actively sought opportunities.

Upon graduation, Shuang recognized the potential of He Wentian's company and proactively approached Wentian with an offer. He openly stated he wasn't interested in being just an employee but rather wanted to be a partner, even if it meant owning only 0.01% of the shares. Impressed by Shuang’s unique approach, Wentian allowed him to join as a partner. Their relationship became one of both mentorship and friendship, seamlessly collaborating on acquiring companies and selling them off, becoming formidable snipers in the American business world.

Later, Shuang became widely known as the "Black Knight" of the business world for his ruthless and merciless tactics, gradually gaining prominence. However, the divergence of opinions between him and Wentian also grew. In pursuit of the company's interests and development, Shuang decided to acquire Wentian's company entirely. The blow was too much for Wentian, who turned to drunk driving. Upon learning that Wentian had lost control of his car and fallen into the sea, Shuang felt immense guilt when Wentian's body could not be found. Was the value of a deal truly greater than friendship or life? Reflecting on everything, Shuang resolved to no longer be the Black Knight and decided to exit the business world. He learned from the newspaper that Wentian's wife and child were struggling financially in Hong Kong, so Shuang took responsibility and decided to return to Hong Kong to care for Wentian's family.

Lin Miaomiao's Debt Collection Pursuit

Upon returning to Hong Kong, Shuang met Wentian's wife, She Mulian, coincidentally staying at Tang Ji's thug-run hotel. There, he encountered Tang Ji, a middle-aged playboy heir from a commercial dynasty, notorious outside but filial to his father, surrounded by beautiful women.

By chance, Shuang met Lin Miaomiao at a luxury store, creating animosity between them. Miaomiao was a compulsive shopper who owed a large amount on her credit cards, forcing her to work as a debt collector. With her motto of "no success, no fee," she became the ace of the collection company. Mulian owed Miaomiao's finance company a significant sum due to her grandmother Zhou Xiaoxiao being scammed. Miaomiao was responsible for collecting the debt, leading to another encounter with Shuang. When Shuang found Mulian's family, he took over Mulian's debt, causing Miaomiao to mistakenly believe Shuang was Mulian's ex-husband who abandoned his wife and children, thus forming a negative impression of Shuang. Unaware that her husband's death was indirectly caused by Shuang, Mulian still believed her husband, Wentian, was alive. Mulian's grandmother often accused Shuang of being the murderer of her son, Wentian. Initially shocked that Mulian discovered the truth, Shuang later realized the grandmother suffered from early-stage dementia, making everyone dismiss her words as mere ramblings of a madwoman.
