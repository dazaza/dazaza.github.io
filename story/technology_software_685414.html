"God-Slaying Technique: An 87-Year-Old Veteran Searches for His First Love, Lying on the Deck Sighing Deeply 65 Years Ago When They Parted."

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-01 15:10:03

Old Man's Photograph

■ Editor's Note

They, in the just-concluded Year of the Rabbit, whether through touching, heart-wrenching, or joyful stories, all had extraordinary life experiences that transformed them from ordinary people like you and me into news figures under media spotlight. Starting today, our newspaper will launch a series of reports titled "News Figures Celebrating the New Year," focusing on those who became the subject of public discourse in the past year and how they are welcoming their new Year of the Dragon.

On the 29th day of the 12th lunar month (New Year’s Eve), light snow began to fall in Hangzhou. Xu Yougen, aged 87, sat in his warm home where, starting in the afternoon, family members were coming and going, busy purchasing and preparing various New Year goods. “In the evening, the whole family will gather for a lively New Year’s Eve dinner.”

Past Memories

This year, the elder will spend the New Year’s Eve at his granddaughter’s house. In the festive and bustling atmosphere of the New Year’s Eve night, the old man said he is doing well but still misses the woman across the Shenzhen River. "I hope she is as fortunate as I am, with many descendants, enjoying a lively New Year’s Eve dinner and celebrating the New Year happily."

Sixty-five years ago, Xu Yougen was still a soldier. His unit, after receiving the Guangjiu Railway following Japan's defeat in World War II, was stationed in Shenzhen. At that time, Li Yu, a 19-year-old girl studying in Hong Kong's Yau Ma Tei, had a relative detained by Xu Yougen’s unit. Frequently visiting her relative, Li Yu came to know the young Xu Yougen, and soon they fell in love, even discussing marriage plans. However, due to his young age at the time, Xu Yougen did not meet the marriage requirements, and their wedding was not approved by the army.

Subsequently, the civil war broke out. Xu Yougen originally planned to take off his military uniform and stay in Guangdong, but ultimately decided to move north with his unit because he feared being unable to support himself outside the army. He said, "On the day I left, Li Yu cried herself into tears and collapsed at the dock. I didn’t dare look back at my close friend Aiyu, shedding tears myself, as I forced myself onto the warship. Lying on the deck, I sighed to the heavens!" Due to the aftermath of the war, he suffered hearing loss but personally wrote down their love story. "When we parted ways, I gave Li Yu everything valuable I had for her living expenses and promised: if the ship could dock in Shanghai, I would definitely leave the army and find a way to bring her over," recalled Xu Yougen.

But this long-distance relationship between Shenzhen and Hong Kong ended up lasting for 65 years. The love and separation of 65 years ago have either faded or deepened through the trials of war and time. Xu Yougen has never received any news about Li Yu since then, and finding his lost contact with his first love from Hong Kong due to the chaos of war has become Xu Yougen’s greatest wish.

New Year's Eve Thoughts

After the South China Morning Post reported on Xu Yougen’s wish to find someone, within just over a month, he received dozens of messages and calls from various media outlets on both sides of the strait and numerous enthusiastic readers offering help and advice. Some provided suggestions and ideas to help the old man locate Li Yu, while others were deeply moved by Xu Yougen and Li Yu’s story and wished to send blessings and greetings to the old man. Some students studying in Hong Kong offered to help post a missing person notice in Hong Kong newspapers and distribute advertisements on his behalf.

However, Xu Yougen reiterated to the South China Morning Post reporter his true wish. He said that what he actually hoped for was not necessarily to find Li Yu but simply to learn about her current situation. Considering Li Yu’s perspective, Xu Yougen admitted that he somewhat regretted initially using the media to search for her, "What if she doesn't want to see me and feels that it's disturbing her life?" But Xu Yougen added that he only hoped that his departure and unfulfilled promise many years ago wouldn’t continue to trouble or hurt Li Yu.

The old man said that every year, he spends New Year's Eve happily surrounded by his large family, leading a very happy life. Yet during such times, he also often thinks about the Hong Kong girl from 65 years ago, wondering where she is, what she is doing, and whether she is doing well and as happy as he is. As he grows older, his desire to reconnect with Li Yu becomes stronger.

New Year's Wishes

Apart from some hearing difficulties, Xu Yougen, in his eighties, remains in good health and high spirits. His children and grandchildren want to take him to visit Hong Kong this year, "Although he (Xu Yougen) always says he leads a good life without regrets or dissatisfaction, we all know he has this wish." Once in Hong Kong, they hope to reach out to relevant departments to express their desire to connect with Li Yu.

Xu Yougen himself views traveling to Hong Kong as one of his New Year's resolutions for the Year of the Dragon. Not only because it was once the place where Li Yu lived, but also because he spent many youthful years on the other side of the Shenzhen River. After more than sixty years, he wishes to revisit this place in his lifetime to seek memories of the past.

The old man said he hopes to send his New Year's blessing to Li Yu, even though she may never receive it. But he will always pray for her happiness and peace during festivals.

Written by Chief Reporter Wang Ying of the South China Morning Post

■ Character Flashback

Sixty-five years ago, 22-year-old Xu Yougen was stationed in Shenzhen after the victory of the Anti-Japanese War. There, he met Li Yu, a Hong Kong female middle school student who frequently visited her detained relatives at the military camp. Thus, a pure love story unfolded in Shenzhen. Sixty-five years later, Xu Yougen, who separated from his lover when he moved north with the army, returned to Guangzhou to pay respects to his comrades-in-arms. During these reminiscences, the 87-year-old Xu Yougen personally wrote down the moment of his parting with Li Yu and handed it to the South China Morning Post reporter. On the night of the New Year’s Eve, he hopes that in 2012, he can find his Hong Kong first love whom he was forced to separate from for more than half a century due to war.
