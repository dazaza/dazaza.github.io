Manchester United's flying wing is more dashing than Balotelli, becoming the club's second star; Real Madrid can hardly buy him for 20 million.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 10:04:33

Manchester United's Flying Wing is more stylish than the "Showboater" and has become the Devil's Number Two; Real Madrid's 20 million can't buy him.

Since entering November, a new firepower point for Manchester United has emerged: it is Antonio Valencia. The Ecuadorian has scored 3 goals in 6 league matches, averaging one goal every two games. The situation that once plagued him—only providing assists without scoring—has now been rectified. Currently at Manchester United, he has played in 16 league matches and scored 4 goals. Although 4 goals in 16 games might not seem like much, considering his recent performances, Valencia has become the second biggest attacking force at Manchester United after Rooney.

It’s somewhat embarrassing to note that despite having 14 different players score in both the league and cup competitions this season, Rooney's 13 goals stand far above everyone else. The second highest scorer, Owen, has only managed 7 goals, with only two of those coming in the league. Berbatov's four league goals have been spread out over time. Only Valencia has consistently scored recently, each goal more beautiful than the last. While he is tied for second place on the team's league goalscorer list with Berbatov, fans clearly recognize Valencia's recent form as superior to Berbatov's.

Today’s goal vividly demonstrates how strong teams emphasize not just speed and efficiency during counter-attacks but also the combination of individual creativity and opportunity-grasping ability. Any team can launch quick attacks, but simply pursuing speed often leads to poor passes and missed opportunities. A single misstep in any phase can render a fast attack ineffective. In this case, Scholes' precise chip pass set the tone; Berbatov's overhead kick combined skill and imagination perfectly; and finally, Valencia's right-footed volley from a well-adjusted position sealed the deal. This classic finish, reminiscent of the style seen from Shearer and Solksjaer, was beautifully replicated by Valencia today.

After the match, Ferguson lavishly praised Valencia: “This finishing touch was absolutely brilliant. Antonio will definitely increase his goal tally, because we all know during training that he is actually an excellent striker. Today’s goal is a great example. Antonio's progress has been enormous. He plays particularly expansively, interpreting his role very well. He keeps improving, and we are very satisfied.” What does "expansive" mean? It refers to Valencia's playing style—his activity in the midfield and forward areas has broadened significantly. He can now move and change positions more freely, unlike when he first joined and was more of a dedicated right-winger. His multiple one-touch passes were highly accurate, demonstrating a high level of understanding with his teammates running along different routes. Valencia's willingness to execute such tactics shows that he has fully integrated into the current Manchester United. Thus, the future number of goals and assists for this 17 million-pound star should, as Ferguson emphasized, increase significantly.

Manchester United should feel fortunate to have secured Valencia. This summer, Bayern Munich offered 18 million pounds, while Real Madrid went up to 20 million pounds—both offers higher than Manchester United's. Fortunately, Wigan Athletic didn’t care about these few million pound differences and stressed that Valencia’s destination would be decided by himself. Already accustomed to playing in the Premier League, Valencia ultimately chose Manchester United. Half a year later, looking back, this deal has started to yield significant returns for Manchester United. Although Valencia hasn’t yet reached the destructive level of Cristiano Ronaldo, his performance of 4 goals and 3 assists in 16 matches, especially his impressive displays since November, makes people believe that despite his somewhat fixed position, the 24-year-old Valencia is advancing towards becoming an absolute right-wing mainstay who could leave an important mark in Manchester United's illustrious history.
