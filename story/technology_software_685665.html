Father of Foshan crushed girl donates another 30,000 yuan to help people, says he feels no hatred

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 16:08:50

Yueyue's father, Wang Chichang, donated 30,000 yuan for a seriously ill child, Li, who was being treated in Beijing. On behalf of the Li family, Ms. Guo accepted this charitable donation. Photos were taken by Sun Junbin, a reporter from the Southern Metropolis Daily.

Report from the Southern Metropolis Daily: Give back to society what comes from society. Yesterday afternoon, a critically ill girl in Beijing named Li Huiqian received a token of love from Foshan, Guangdong - a donation of 30,000 yuan. The donor was Yueyue’s father, Wang Chichang, and these funds came from the charitable donations made by all sectors of society for Yueyue.

This was the second donation Wang Chichang made recently. On the morning of October 31, he donated 60,000 yuan to a 25-year-old leukemia patient whom he did not know. This young man, named Chen Liangfa, was being treated at the People's Hospital of Guangdong Province and urgently needed hundreds of thousands of yuan for a bone marrow transplant surgery.

The Love Donation Model of Yueyue

After Yueyue passed away, how the donations from all sectors of society for Yueyue would be handled became a focal point of public attention. Regarding the amount of donations, Wang Chichang said that he had not yet verified it. On October 18, he established this account with media reporters, and at that time, there was over 270,000 yuan in the account.

"This account has been publicly disclosed by the media, and anyone can check it anytime," Wang Chichang repeatedly promised the media when accepting the donations that this money would be used for Yueyue's treatment, and the rest would be fully transferred and donated to those who need it more.

"We will not donate arbitrarily, nor will we donate all the money to one person." Wang Chichang explained that arbitrary donations might lead to the money not going to those who truly need it, and donating all the money to one person is not as effective as dividing it to help more people.

According to him and his family's idea, their donation targets are concentrated on children facing urgent financial needs for life-saving situations. "There is no geographical restriction, and all donations will be verified and witnessed by the media before being donated." He also emphasized that the donation funds will be directly handed over to the recipients and will not go through government organizations or other public welfare institutions.

"Child, you must get better"

The donations to Chen Liangfa and Li Huiqian were both operated under this model. The donation to Chen Liangfa was helped and verified by a TV station in Guangdong. A kind-hearted person surnamed Zhou had already deposited 200,000 yuan in one lump sum into Chen Liangfa's hospital account. However, there was still a shortfall of several ten thousand yuan needed for his bone marrow transplant surgery.

The donation to Li Huiqian was helped and verified by the Southern Metropolis Daily. Li Huiqian comes from Dangshan County, Anhui Province. In April this year, the 11-year-old girl was diagnosed with extremely severe aplastic anemia in Xuzhou, Jiangsu.

On October 1st, her father, Li Feng, transferred her to the No. 307 Hospital of the Chinese People's Liberation Army in Beijing for treatment. During the treatment period, in order to relieve the pressure on his daughter, he repeatedly fabricated beautiful lies. His story moved many people, and on October 21, Xinhua News Agency reported it under the title "A Father Who 'Lies' Out of Love."

It is understood that Li Huiqian's condition has improved somewhat, and the target for bone marrow transplantation matching has been found. However, the tens of thousands of yuan shortfall in surgical costs has left them at a loss.

"Child, you must get better." When donating 30,000 yuan, Wang Chichang called Li Feng and blessed Li Huiqian.

Wang Chichang, Yueyue's father: "I have no hatred, so how can I forgive?"

There is a TV advertisement that goes like this: Without killing, there is no trade; start with oneself. The most appealing sentence to me is starting with oneself.

(If any of the 18 passersby previously encountered situations where helping someone led to false accusations, and then they didn't save Yueyue, would you forgive them?) I myself have no hatred, so how can I forgive? ―― Wang Chichang

In recent days, the Yueyue incident has attracted the attention of domestic and foreign media. Under the spotlight, every move of this family is exposed to the public eye. How does this family feel during this time? What is their life like after Yueyue passed away? "This month can be considered a turning point in life, with too many things happening." For a long time after Yueyue's untimely death, his phone was turned off, and yesterday, a weary-looking father of Yueyue, Wang Chichang, accepted an exclusive interview with the Southern Metropolis Daily.

"When going out, sometimes I feel many people looking at me with strange eyes."

Southern Metropolis Daily (hereinafter referred to as "South Daily"): During the eight days from Yueyue's passing to her cremation, you almost disappeared from everyone's sight. How did you spend those days?

Wang Chichang: Just stayed at home. My grandparents bought groceries; I felt quite sad inside. Sometimes when going out, I felt many people looking at me with strange eyes, so I hardly went out unless there was something to do.

South Daily: Is it the look of suspicion and questioning, or sympathy?

Wang Chichang: Sympathy, but also some suspicion, but very few.

South Daily: So you actually don't want others to recognize you?

Wang Chichang: Yes.

South Daily: Do you know people are questioning you?

Wang Chichang: Very few, newspapers and magazines write very little about it, including what is said on TV (about doubts), generally comes from the internet. If these... well, I don't know what the questioners have in mind, seeking fame? Seeking clicks? Seeking this and that, I don't understand.

"I haven't taken good care of my child, and I've been blaming myself."

South Daily: Has Yueyue's post-mortem affairs been properly settled?

Wang Chichang: Not completely.

South Daily: Grandpa said you dream about her every day?

Wang Chichang: Yeah, every night.

South Daily: What do you dream about?

Wang Chichang: Playing with the child, holding her, carrying her, taking her everywhere.

South Daily: Do you have a feeling that she hasn't left, that she is always by your side?

Wang Chichang: Yes.

South Daily: Will you continue to stay in the hardware city?

Wang Chichang: No plans to. There, I always think about that event.

South Daily: Have you ever felt guilty or regretful at any moment?

Wang Chichang: I haven't taken good care of my child, and I've been blaming myself.

South Daily: Can we say that much of the pressure now comes from your inner sense of guilt?

Wang Chichang: Yes.

South Daily: The mother fell ill because she often thought about this matter?

Wang Chichang: Because of this.

"I myself have no hatred, so how can I forgive?"

South Daily: Have you seen news reports about rescuers being falsely accused before?

Wang Chichang: Yes.

South Daily: What would you do if you were in the same situation?

Wang Chichang: I would still save the person if I were present.

South Daily: What do you think when you see such incidents where rescuers are falsely accused?

Wang Chichang: It's ridiculous. I saved a person, and they falsely accuse me. Don't you find it ridiculous?

South Daily: Assuming among the 18 passersby, some indeed did not rescue Yueyue due to similar previous experiences, would you forgive them?

Wang Chichang: I myself have no hatred, so how can I forgive?

"People cannot easily judge another person guilty; let the judges decide."

South Daily: What kind of result do you hope for regarding the hit-and-run driver?

Wang Chichang: Still listen to the law, follow the government.

South Daily: If the court finally determines that the driver unintentionally caused the secondary run-over, can you accept this result?

Wang Chichang: ... (Silence for nearly ten seconds)

South Daily: Do you deep down feel that the hit-and-run driver acted intentionally?

Wang Chichang: I'm not sure. People cannot easily judge another person guilty; let the judges decide.

South Daily: Yueyue's case eventually became a landmark event concerning the moral discussion of our country. Do you think China's morality has declined?

Wang Chichang: I'm not sure. I just feel that my own morality has not declined.

South Daily: What will you do?

Wang Chichang: There seems to be an advertisement on TV that says: No killing, no trading. Start with oneself. The most appealing sentence to me is starting with oneself.

"I hope everyone gradually forgets Yueyue's name."

South Daily: Do you like Foshan?

Wang Chichang: Well, this place is pretty nice.

South Daily: Do you hope Yueyue gradually fades out of everyone's sight, or forever be remembered by everyone?

Wang Chichang: I think forgetting a bit is better.

South Daily: Including this incident?

Wang Chichang: This incident should definitely be remembered, but I hope people forget this name. Not because of what someone did, but to make people regain a bit of conscience.

"(Donations) If not through the media, the kind of doubt and questioning, it's too terrifying."

South Daily: Do you know the total amount of donations now?

Wang Chichang: No, I haven't checked. These days, it cost nearly ten thousand yuan.

South Daily: Everyone is also concerned about the use of these donations. How will you verify each recipient?

Wang Chichang: Through the media, I can't verify it myself.

South Daily: Why must it be verified through the media?

Wang Chichang: If not through the media, I definitely can't handle it. That kind of doubt and questioning is too terrible.

South Daily: Do you think words can be dangerous?

Wang Chichang: Yes, words can be dangerous. People can talk others to death.

Written by intern Li Wenjie and Southern Metropolis Daily reporter Zhang Hao.
