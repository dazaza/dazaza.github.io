This is because, in the event of an accident, broken glass could become a dangerous weapon. Considering this, most car front windows use safety glass. This type of glass contains a tough transparent film called polyphenyl, which forms a spider-web structure between two layers of glass. Its characteristic is that after breaking, it only develops cracks resembling a spider web, ensuring forward visibility. Another type is the tempered glass used on side and rear windows, made by heat-treating a single piece of glass. To avoid harming passengers, once cracked, it shatters into fine powder. If left unattended, the crack may expand, necessitating replacement of the entire glass. Therefore, repair it as soon as possible before the crack expands.
