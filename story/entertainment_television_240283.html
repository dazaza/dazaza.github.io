Five words to describe Battier

The "Happy Camp" production team used five English words to summarize this "Batman": high, air, papa, perfect, and Yao. High refers to Battier's 2-meter-3-centimeter height, while air refers to Battier's indispensable role in the Rockets. Papa is one of Battier's current identities—"Tomorrow is my son's 15-month birthday." Battier's usual statement allowed all the audience to see the tender side of this tough court warrior, "My son now is my heart and soul. No matter what happens on the court, seeing my son at home makes me feel warm. Any honor from the court can hardly compare to my son." Battier also revealed that when his son was born, he cried tears of joy for 10 minutes. Perfect is the affirmation people have for Battier, but he said, "It's too far away."

Yao, of course, refers to Yao Ming. As Yao Ming's teammate, Battier showered him with praise, "He is an excellent person. He represents China's image very well, and he deserves all Chinese people to be proud of him." However, Battier also revealed a small embarrassing moment about Yao Ming. Before the annual U.S. college basketball tournament, players in the Rockets' locker room would bet $100 for entertainment. When Battier collected money from Yao Ming, the Chinese giant always found it difficult and said, "Can I not participate...$100 is too much." Battier laughed and said, "Every time we tease him, 'Yao, you are the one who earns the most among us, you hold onto your money so tightly that you could almost squeeze water out of it.'" It seems that Yao Ming's "frugality" has become one of the jokes in the Rockets' locker room.

In the professional basketball vs. street basketball showdown, Battier barely won

At the program site, two street basketball enthusiasts came to challenge. When NBA professional basketball faced off against street basketball, who would win? In each round, one side could shoot in any posture, while the other side had to score using the same method, otherwise they would become the loser. Battier took on two street basketball youths. Despite them posing many challenges and performing layups in various street basketball poses, Batman still had the upper hand and barely won. The victorious Battier received a prize—a birthday cake, as September 9th was his 31st birthday.

Playing "Guess the Word" with Xie Na, language barrier no obstacle

Battier and host Xie Na were absolutely language-incompatible, yet their cooperation remained perfectly synchronized. Difficult words like "juicer," "hen laying eggs," and "washing machine" were all guessed by the two. Battier's talent for mimicry amazed everyone—his imitation of Michael Jackson was spot on, and despite his towering height, he even performed the moonwalk, which was more impressive than Jordan's imitation of dance moves years ago; imitating a "washing machine," Battier's performance was impeccable, making the show hosts feel inadequate themselves. "Some people say playing the word guessing game is more dangerous than me playing basketball," Battier said smugly.
