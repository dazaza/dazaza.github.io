Electricity industry: Pay attention to the opportunities of asset injection and individual stock investment, recommend 4 stocks＿Stock Forum

by xl0958870 on 2009-12-05 02:40:07

After the electricity industry presented the "five golden flowers" in 2003, the market attention on the power sector gradually declined. In 2003, the market value of the power sector accounted for 9.73% of the total market value, while currently, the sector's market value only accounts for 2.74%; the industry's market value ranking has dropped from fifth to thirtieth; comparing the annual turnover rate of the sector, the trading activity of the power sector also shows a declining trend. Therefore, before the industry attributes undergo fundamental changes, it is difficult for the power sector to present overall investment opportunities in the short term.

In 2010, with increased demand and supply contraction, equipment utilization rates are expected to rise. Under the impetus of downstream demand, the four high-energy-consuming industries in China—steel, electrolytic aluminum, cement, and chemicals—are expected to fully release their capacities in 2010. The electricity elasticity coefficient will return to over 1, and under the expectation of an 8.5% GDP growth in 2010, electricity demand is expected to grow by 9.4% year-on-year. The power generation equipment is expected to add approximately 40 million kilowatts in 2010, including 20 million kilowatts of thermal power, with growth rates of 4.7% and 3%, respectively. The number of hours for power generation equipment is expected to stop falling and start rising, with a predicted increase of 240 hours nationwide in 2010, among which thermal power will increase by 220 hours.

The probability of coal price increases grows under the backdrop of inflation expectations and energy-saving emission-reduction policies. We judge that the overall balance of coal supply and demand in 2010 will increase the probability of coal price hikes driven by rising oil prices. Additionally, under the national energy-saving and emission-reduction policies and the government's commitment to reduce carbon emissions per unit of GDP by more than 40% by 2020, the policy-induced costs of coal will increase, inevitably pushing up coal prices. We predict a 5%-10% increase in coal contract prices in 2010.

Investment Strategy:

From the perspective of sector market attention and current valuation levels, we believe that the power sector will still lack significant overall investment opportunities in 2010. We are more optimistic about individual stock investment opportunities with a PE below 20 times in 2010.

The investment approach should follow operating transformation, performance reversal, and asset injection theme stocks. It is suggested to pay attention to Guiyang Electric Power and Yue Electric Power in the first half of the year; and Guangzhou Hengyun, Guiguan Electric Power, Shanghai Electric Power, and Huadian Electric Power in the second half of the year.

Key Company Recommendations:

Guiyang Electric Power (600310.sh), Guangzhou Hengyun (000531.sz), Guiguan Electric Power (600236.sh), Huadian International (600011.sh)

Risk Warning: Significant risk of coal price increases; economic growth lower-than-expected risks.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. This order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price at which you intend to buy or sell the stock. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks being bought and sold: the quantity of stocks ordered to buy must be a multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks ordered to sell does not have to be a multiple of 100.

There are four types of orders: counter submission orders, telephone automatic orders, computer automatic orders, and remote terminal orders.
