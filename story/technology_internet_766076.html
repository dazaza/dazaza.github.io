What products were released during the two and a half year "honeymoon" period of Nokia's collaboration with Microsoft?

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 15:00:39

When Nokia CEO Stephen Elop left his position as president of Microsoft's business department in 2010 to become the CEO of Nokia, some joked that he might become a Trojan horse. There were rumors that Elop had gone to Nokia under authorization from Ballmer and Microsoft's board of directors with the aim of transforming Nokia into Microsoft's new Windows Phone headquarters. Now, not only has this joke come true, but reality has proven to be even more imaginative than fiction.

Let’s look back at the heavyweight products Nokia launched during the two-and-a-half-year "honeymoon period" of collaboration before Microsoft acquired Nokia.

The image shows the new Windows Phone 8 device, the Nokia Lumia 1020, equipped with a 41-megapixel PureView camera.

On February 11, 2011, Nokia CEO Elop and Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer jointly announced a strategic partnership in London. The partnership included: Nokia adopting Microsoft's Windows Phone as its smartphone operating system; Bing being integrated across Nokia's devices and services; and Nokia Maps becoming the core of Microsoft's mapping services, among others.

In January 2012, Nokia unveiled the highly anticipated Lumia 900 at CES. The Lumia 900 won 13 international awards upon release and was hailed as a product crucial to Nokia's survival.

In February 2012 at MWC 2012, Nokia launched the Lumia 610. Priced at €189, it seemed poised to shake up the market.

On September 5, 2012, Nokia introduced the Lumia 920 and Lumia 820. The Lumia 920 incorporated Nokia's advanced second-generation PureView camera technology, while the Lumia 820 offered excellent value for money with the same Qualcomm Snapdragon S4 1.5GHz dual-core CPU as the Lumia 920, though with a 4.3-inch screen.

On October 23, 2012, Nokia released the Lumia 510, an entry-level smartphone product. The interface was still based on Windows Phone 7.5, and the price was less than approximately 1000 RMB.

In February 2013 at MWC 2013, Nokia launched two mid-to-low-end smartphones, the Lumia 720 and Lumia 520. The Lumia 520 was the cheapest Windows Phone 8 device on the market. The Lumia 720 featured a 4.3-inch display, comparable to the Lumia 820, and supported "inky black" and ultra-sensitive touchscreen technology.

On May 14, 2013, the metal-bodied Lumia 925 was unveiled in London. The design of the Lumia 925 was different from other Lumia series phones as it boldly adopted an aluminum frame. The Lumia 925 was also Nokia's fourth smartphone to support PureView technology, following the Nokia 808, Lumia 920, and Lumia 928.

On July 11, 2013, the new Windows Phone 8 device, the Nokia Lumia 1020 with a 41-megapixel PureView camera, made its debut in New York. The Lumia 1020 became the new representative of cameras in the mobile phone industry.
