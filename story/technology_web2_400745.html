Several banks cut the limit of online banking payment, which troubles the group of online shoppers recently.

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:35:21

Hualong News Network (Reported by Journalist Xiao Yan Liu) - Recently, the group of online shoppers has started to feel troubled. The reason lies in multiple banks reducing the single-transaction limit for online banking payments, causing inconvenience for many citizens who enjoy spending large sums of money online. Recently, China Merchants Bank announced that it would reduce the single-transaction limit for online banking payments, citing the prevention of online shopping risks as the reason. In addition to China Merchants Bank, several other banks have also recently reduced the payment limits for online shopping.

Online Shopping Enthusiasts Encounter Payment Troubles

Ms. Huang, a human resources supervisor at an IT company, is an avid online shopper who loves browsing Taobao and other online malls in her free time. Ms. Huang particularly enjoys purchasing cosmetics online, with each purchase amounting to hundreds or even thousands of yuan.

Yesterday, Ms. Huang selected a large number of cosmetics online as usual, totaling 598 yuan. She opened Alipay and chose to pay using her China Merchants Bank credit card. After entering her card number and password, the online bank displayed a notification: "Starting from April 11, 2011, our bank will adjust the transaction limit for online payments made through third-party payment companies." After carefully reading the notice, Ms. Huang found that the single-transaction and daily limits for online payments would be reduced to 500 yuan in the future.

Multiple Online Banks Reduce Limits

It was learned by reporters that before China Merchants Bank reduced its online banking limit, multiple banks had already begun reducing the limits for online banking transfers and payments. As early as 2006, Industrial and Commercial Bank of China reduced the payment limit for ordinary users from 3,000 yuan to 300 yuan. Minsheng Bank reduced the single-transaction limit for its mass version of online banking from 1,000 yuan to 300 yuan, and the cumulative daily limit from 5,000 yuan to 300 yuan.

In terms of transfers, the highest single-transaction limit for intra-bank or inter-bank transfers for wealth management customers of Bank of China was reduced from a daily cumulative limit of 1 million yuan and 2 million yuan to 100,000 yuan and 200,000 yuan respectively. Construction Bank also reduced its online banking transfer limit earlier this month, changing the single-transaction limit for personal online banking shield customers from 1 million yuan to 500,000 yuan, and the daily cumulative transfer limit from 2 million yuan to 1 million yuan.

Prevent Risks? Users Don't Buy It

Regarding the reduction of online banking payment limits, China Merchants Bank stated that it was to reduce the risk of online payment transactions and ensure the financial security of cardholders.

It was learned by reporters that not long ago, the website of a large state-owned bank was cloned by a phishing site, pushing the issue of online banking security to the forefront. Affected by this, multiple banks have successively reduced their online banking payment limits, and "anti-phishing" has become the first issue that online banking must face head-on.

However, most customers are not satisfied with the measure of reducing limits. Ms. Huang said that online shopping is becoming increasingly developed, and the development of online banking should keep pace with the times. Reducing limits will hinder customer use of funds, "preventing risks should start from the bank's own system."

Some Taobao store owners also expressed dissatisfaction. The reduction in single-transaction limits will lead some customers to choose multiple purchases, after which the store owner will manually deduct shipping fees, increasing the workload for Taobao stores.

Industry Advice to Resolve Awkwardness

For the claim that large online payments cannot be made due to limits, China Merchants Bank stated that customers can make safer online payments, mobile payments, and credit card large-amount payments through the professional version of their services. It was found during investigations that the payment limits for the professional version of online banking for most banks are much higher than the regular version.

Industry insiders advised consumers that since professional version transactions use hardware encryption methods, they have a higher security level and are less likely to be deceived by phishing sites. Therefore, all banks are recommending the professional version of online banking. For those who do not wish to apply for the professional version, they can choose the "multiple recharges" method. For example, if a consumer on Taobao has an order amount of 990 yuan, exceeding the bank's single-transaction limit of 500 yuan, they can first recharge Alipay and then make the payment via Alipay. Each recharge can be 495 yuan, which the bank will consider as one single transaction. After two recharges, there will be 990 yuan in Alipay, and then the payment can be made through Alipay.
