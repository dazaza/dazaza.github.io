From 2007 to 2008, Ye MaZhong's agency took on the full planning of two domestic cosmetics brands, PLEJAB and Dr.Ji. While achieving great success in these projects, we realized that China's cosmetics industry currently faces both "dangers" and "opportunities".

The industry is growing increasingly prosperous, but domestic brands are struggling.

Cosmetics, cigarettes, and infant formula are known as the fastest-growing consumer goods categories in China. According to statistics, in 1978, there were only a few dozen cosmetic companies in China, with only a few varieties such as clam oil, petroleum jelly, and snow cream. Today, there are over 4,000 cosmetic companies in China, offering more than 2,500 varieties. In 2007, the national cosmetics sales exceeded 70 billion yuan, with an annual growth rate of over 13%. It is predicted that by 2010, China's cosmetics market will exceed 100 billion yuan.

On one hand, the industry is becoming increasingly prosperous; on the other hand, the most profitable part of this market remains firmly in the hands of international brands. Of the more than 4,000 domestic cosmetic companies, only about 50 have annual sales exceeding 100 million yuan. A small number of foreign enterprises can generate several hundred million or even billions of yuan in sales. In 2007, L'Oreal Group sold 5.445 billion yuan worth of products in China. When reporting on this, many media outlets rounded the figure to an integer - "L'Oreal (China) sold 5.4 billion yuan in 2007." The media may not realize that the leftover amount - 45 million yuan - exceeds the annual sales of 99% of domestic brands.

In 2007, among China's top ten cosmetics, only Dabao was a domestic brand. In 2008, Johnson & Johnson acquired Dabao, leaving no domestic brands in the top ten.

Facing blockades, domestic brands continue to seek breakthroughs.

Currently, the most commonly used method for domestic cosmetics is "channel strength": selecting strong regional markets, building strong channels and terminal points, and then attracting consumers with high cost-performance products. The overall principle is: focusing on second- and third-tier markets where international brands are relatively weak.

Using this method, many companies have achieved success. Danzi and Daiweili may be unknown to many, but their annual sales exceed 100 million yuan. Rou Ting may be unheard of by many consumers, but it generates seven billion yuan annually through its self-operated chain of beauty salons! This performance can rival that of "Baicaoji."

Ye MaZhong's agency fully supports the "channel strength" approach, as seeking a strong position in areas where competitors are weak is a good strategy for overcoming disadvantages.

At the same time, Ye MaZhong's agency also reminds companies that besides "channel strength," domestic cosmetics must build "consumer brands" within the next three years!

Reason One: International brands are rapidly expanding into smaller cities and towns. For example, Shiseido plans to open 5,000 contracted specialty stores in China by the end of 2008, covering all counties, cities, and even towns. In a few years, there may not be many blank spots left for domestic brands to claim.

Reason Two: International brands have a wide range of product lines in reserve. If necessary, they have a rich selection of mid-to-low-end products to engage in price wars in second- and third-tier markets, or they can directly acquire strong domestic brands to compete at lower price levels. Domestic mobile phone manufacturers once thought they could survive for a few years by relying on "second and third-tier markets" and "low prices." However, when international brands launched low-priced models, they reduced the market share of domestic phones by 30 percentage points in just two years and then almost completely pushed them into the "shanzhai" category. Although shanzhai phones appear lively now, they are actually engaging in self-destructive competition. For any brand looking to re-enter the branded path, the cost would be too high for most shanzhai phones to bear. With recent examples close at hand, the cosmetics industry should deeply reflect.

Reason Three: Currently, international brands are building increasingly higher brand barriers, and the later domestic brands act, the higher the cost will be.

Everyone wants to build a brand, but the key is how?

Faced with the strong brand barriers built by leading brands, domestic cosmetics can easily stray from the right path. If millions of investment do not quickly show results, doubts about the correctness of this path will soon arise, followed by distorted actions, shifting from "building a brand" to "doing promotions," and eventually reverting strategically to "channels above all."

Therefore, if the correct path cannot be found, the "brand road for Chinese cosmetics" will remain empty talk.

Effective methods for the cosmetics brand road

In serving "PLEJAB" and "Dr.Ji," Ye MaZhong's agency has summarized an effective set of methods for cosmetics companies to quickly break through brand barriers, achieve the transition from "channels above all" to "brands above all," and break through bottlenecks to return to high-speed development.

"PLEJAB": 100% Growth

Background: PLEJAB was founded in November 2003. In 2004, its sales reached 40 million yuan, increasing by 80% in 2005 and 30% in 2006. By the first half of 2007, the growth rate had dropped below 20%, indicating the company had hit a bottleneck.

As the company put it: "We originally planned to double our sales every year, tripling them in three years. In the first year, we quickly established our position in the market, and in the second year, our sales basically doubled. But in the third year, it became very difficult, with only a 30% increase. Despite continuously launching new brands and products, our sales didn't rise. By 2007, we had lowered our target to a 25% increase, but even that seemed challenging."

In July 2007, Ye MaZhong's agency began collaborating with PLEJAB. Through detailed industry research, internal and external corporate investigations, distributor visits, terminal surveys, and consumer studies, we concluded:

The channel strength has been exhausted.

Since PLEJAB transformed from a distributorship into an integrated trade, industrial, and technological enterprise, its channel capabilities were very strong. Moreover, due to direct contact with consumers, its understanding of demand was accurate. In its first year, the company achieved a miracle of 40 million yuan in annual sales by developing specialty stores and daily chemical channels. Building on this success, the company continued to launch new brands and various ground activities, further strengthening its channel efforts and achieving another doubling of sales in the second year. However, by then, the channel strength had been exhausted. The expansion speed slowed down, and the various new brands launched in the third year also showed limited growth. As the company put it: "When others sell 'whitening' or 'anti-acne' successfully, we have similar products with comparable quality, packaging, and activities, but they just don't sell."

Under these circumstances, PLEJAB must build a consumer brand for two purposes: First, to add aerial strikes to the existing ground forces by leveraging brand pull to ignite the consumer market; Second, to recharge the "channels" through "branding" and drive them to expand again under the combined force of brand and consumers, propelling the company into a new phase of rapid growth.

The key to building a consumer brand is establishing absolute product power!

While the strong brand barriers discussed earlier are objective facts, breaking through these barriers requires a certain cost. However, tactically respecting the enemy while strategically despising them, quoting Chairman Mao: "In the face of absolute product power, any brand barrier is just a paper tiger."

China's recent brand miracles are essentially miracles of product power.

Wanglaoji broke through the barriers of Coca-Cola and Pepsi with "Drink Wanglaoji to prevent heatiness," reaching 10 billion yuan in sales; Bawang broke through Procter & Gamble's barriers with its heritage as an herbal family, reaching 1 billion yuan in sales within three years; Qipai menswear, previously a somewhat vague brand, grew from 300 million yuan to 1 billion yuan in one year with its Chinese stand-up collar and the slogan "Men should be tough on themselves"; Yake candies, virtually unknown, sold 300 million yuan worth of "Yake V9" in four months; Wujiang pickled mustard, which had always been sold as low-grade pickles for hundreds of years, doubled its price with the concept of "three squeezes" and reached 1 billion yuan in annual sales... The latter three cases were directly planned by Ye MaZhong's agency, so we clearly know that in the face of absolute product power, any brand barrier is just a paper tiger. PLEJAB would be no exception; as long as its product power is fully highlighted, it can easily tear through brand barriers and establish a strong brand. To create absolute product power, we took three steps:

Step One: Selecting "Ocean Water Power" - Products do not equal product power.

Wujiang pickled mustard had existed for hundreds of years, but it was only through the concept of "three squeezes" that its product power was truly highlighted, followed by subsequent integrated marketing communication to maximize its impact, creating a miracle.

PLEJAB offers numerous product categories and concepts, encompassing almost everything available on the market. However, strategy is about choice, and choice means sacrifice! Among the long product line, we could only choose one point to highlight its power completely, then "one person attains enlightenment, and all creatures ascend," lifting all categories with this single point.

After repeated testing, we found that in a market dominated by whitening and anti-wrinkle products, the hydration category still lacked a leader brand. Therefore, we swiftly decided: Quickly occupy the position of hydration expert, using hydration as the breakthrough point.

Among hydration products, we screened based on different price ranges and concepts, ultimately selecting the "Ocean Water Power Series" as the candidate. Now we needed to build the absolute product power of this candidate, focusing on five absolutes:

1. Absolute Concept - Pioneering the concept of 320-meter deep ocean water, not hydration, but "deep deep deep hydration expert."

2. Absolute Technology - Collaborating with the internationally renowned Korean KOMA Company in research and development, utilizing advanced ultra-fine emulsification technology and unique active micro-carriers.

3. Absolute Quality - Adopting an international-style packaging design, simple, elegant, and aesthetically pleasing, comprehensively covering the hydration needs of consumers from dozens of yuan to over 100 yuan, from basic skincare to special care, from moisturizing types to refreshing types.

4. Absolute Communication - Choosing the dual-title celebrity "Beauty Queen" DaS as the spokesperson, promoting the Ocean Water Power series with clear positioning and imagery, making it instantly recognizable as a hydration product. Amidst the strong call of "deep deep deep hydration expert," the product impacts the market.

5. Absolute Image - Revolutionizing the previous VI system, integrating water elements throughout the entire brand.

Thus, we completed the preliminary leap from product to "product power."

Step Two: Fresh Morning Water, Nourishing Evening Water: Only absolute products can exert absolute power.

Through the first phase of promotion, the Ocean Water Power series became PLEJAB's main product series, with skyrocketing sales. However, Ye MaZhong's agency believed this was far from enough. With "Ocean Water," PLEJAB could enjoy good sales for at most two years, but this product hadn't elevated the PLEJAB brand to new heights! Clearly, renovation wasn't enough; next, we needed to create something—an "absolute product"—that could unleash absolute power!

Immediately, we tested this statement among consumers, confirming that they already had some awareness but hadn't yet discovered how specific cosmetics could achieve this. Opportunity arose! Tylenol can offer daytime and nighttime tablets, and PLEJAB can introduce morning and evening waters! Fresh Morning Water, Nourishing Evening Water—make your skin younger twice a day!

Results: Three battles, three victories

Battle One: Launch of "Ocean Water Power"

In October 2007, the launch of "Ocean Water Power" resulted in three months of robust sales, enabling PLEJAB to successfully meet its annual target of 150 million yuan.

Battle Two: Launch of "Fresh Morning Water, Nourishing Evening Water"

In April 2008, "Fresh Morning Water, Nourishing Evening Water" officially launched with a retail price of 139 yuan per bottle, completing the positioning as a mid-to-high-end product in the daily chemical channel.

Sales of 200,000 bottles in two months; the market experienced a stockout situation.

"Morning and Evening Waters" complemented each other; stores with high sales of "Morning and Evening Waters" saw a sharp increase in "Ocean Water" sales. In the peak month, "Ocean Water" shipments increased ninefold.

The volume of incoming orders far exceeded expectations, and new franchise signings in blank regions further expanded PLEJAB's network of terminal points.

"Dr.Ji": Speed is the winner

Looking at the present from the future

Compared to PLEJAB, this is a completely different type of enterprise. This company specializes in herbal skincare products with solid fundamentals, excellent products, and steady marketing strategies, consistently balancing channels and brands. However, over ten years, sales have only reached 80 million yuan. The primary reason isn't any deviation in business operations but rather that the herbal cosmetics market has only recently entered a period of rapid growth. Like Baicaoji, it wasn't until around 2003 that financial statements started looking good. Similarly, Dr.Ji has entered a phase of rapid growth in recent years, with annual growth rates between 35-50%.

Therefore, this is a company that hasn't yet encountered a bottleneck. The entrepreneur's mindset, judgment of the company, and understanding of the role of branding differ significantly from those of companies in a bottleneck phase.

Ye MaZhong's agency's perspective is: "Prevent illness before it occurs." For a currently healthy company, we need to look at the present from the future, accurately positioning the company within the trends of the next ten years and making optimal choices today; otherwise, temporary health might blind us to strategic opportunities, leading to regret in a few years.

Herbal cosmetics are entering an industry ranking phase

After studying industry trends, analyzing market conditions, and examining internal and external factors of the company, Ye MaZhong's agency concluded: Herbal cosmetics are entering an industry ranking phase. To be precise, relatively stable top-three brands will emerge within the next five years. Therefore, Dr.Ji must accelerate its brand accumulation, creating distinctive brand concepts to capture consumer mindshare and secure an industry position.

Dr.Ji: Creating a new category, being the essence of herbs among herbs

Facing the initial establishment of the herbal cosmetics competitive landscape, with "Baicaoji" and "Ke Cai" occupying the first and second positions in herbal skincare, we deeply understand the immense power of categories. From "No less than pagers, mobile phones, quick translators" to "Guo Degang's not-so-famous crosstalk comedian," Ye MaZhong pointed out that Dr.Ji must secure the most advantageous position by creating a new category to reverse its disadvantage.

As a guerrilla warfare brand, our first task is to avoid the dominant strengths of the leading brand and find suitable细分markets for our efforts. Thus, Ye MaZhong's agency proposed establishing a new herbal category.

Dr.Ji aims to be the essence of herbs among herbs.

Herbal Market: Lack of Distinctive Herbal Brands

Through research, we discovered a peculiar phenomenon: within the large herbal category, there is no distinctively personalized herbal brand.

Whether it's the first-tier "Baicaoji" or the advantageous "Ke Cai," their brand communications focus on the fundamental attributes of herbs, from "Han medicine essence" to "Chinese herbal beauty," everyone finds selling points in herbs—whether natural, healthy, or safe. Specifically speaking, competitors strive to communicate and build not brand characteristics but more like category communications, collectively constructing a large herbal brand without securing a place for their own brand in consumer minds.

In other words: The market is filled with herbal brands, but in reality, there is only one large herbal brand!

Only Individuality Creates Brand

Examining the entire herbal market, only Ke Cai stands out with its unique "blue pattern" design, creating its distinct herbal personality.

When consumers mention Ke Cai, they perceive it as a high-end, mysterious (additional value brought by color), and purer Eastern brand.
