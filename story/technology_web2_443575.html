I am most convinced by Brother Zhang's insight. When we arrived at Huaxing Cinema, Auxiliary Police Cai Dongfa also started talking. Right at the parking lot of Huaxing Cinema, Zhang Hui captured the city's first car theft case using a jammer. It was a new method of committing a crime—neither smashing nor prying, leaving no trace on the car, and at the beginning, no one knew how the suspect committed the crime. Auxiliary Police Cai Dongfa said that after watching the surveillance footage, Zhang Hui had identified the suspect—a small flat-headed man standing 10 meters away from the stolen vehicle. Zhang Hui's reasoning was that the flat-headed man always turned his back after the driver got out of the car and fiddled with something in his hands, which definitely seemed suspicious. After capturing the flat-headed man, the police found a car lock jammer on him, providing a sample-like method for solving similar cases in the future.

Brother Zhang is both a strongman and a good man. Du Fuliang, who has been an auxiliary police officer for many years, evaluated Zhang Hui this way. Du Fuliang said that seven years ago, Shuangyushu was the area in Haidian District with the highest crime rate but low case-solving efficiency. In the first year Zhang Hui came, he alone captured more than 400 suspects. Under his leadership, the current police station often ranks first in case-solving rates within the division, and many thieves dare not come to Shuangyushu anymore.

That happened in March when there were always traffic accidents on this road, leading Zhang Hui to suspect it was due to a gang of scammers. Every afternoon at 3 PM, Zhang Hui would bring the auxiliary police here to stake out. During the evening rush hour, they noticed three women and two men forming a line, occupying both sides of the road. When vehicles approached, they would rush forward to scrape against them and then demand compensation from the drivers.

Brother Zhang captures over 200 thieves every year, working too hard... As he was speaking, Tian Weiqiang pointed to the intersection of Kexueyuan South Road and told the reporter: Brother Zhang is coming. After hearing many stories about Zhang Hui, I thought he must have a physique as tall and robust as Hunter Zhang, but the person who arrived wore a gray-blue scarf, a semi-new blue down jacket. Upon closer inspection, his skin was rough, with fine wrinkles around his eyelids, looking like an ordinary middle-aged man.

Brother Zhang is reviewing the surveillance footage and will be here soon. He asked us to take you on patrol first to experience it. Speaking was Tian Weiqiang, the captain of the auxiliary police force and a native of Shandong Province. Since Zhang Hui was transferred to the Shuangyushu police station seven years ago, he has been following him to solve cases. In this area of Shuangyushu, there's a story about Brother Zhang at every step. Tian Weiqiang led the reporter to the south side of Shuang'an Mall and pointed to a road only 6 meters wide, recounting the most troublesome case of last year.

Discovering the suspects is easy, but capturing them is difficult! Tian Weiqiang recalled that because evidence needed to be collected, Zhang Hui followed these people every day. Unexpectedly, this group had extremely strong counter-surveillance abilities. After committing their crimes, they would drive around Beijing, first going to Xidan, then Chongwenmen, sometimes even Tongzhou, before finally returning to their temporary residence in Fengtai.

At noon on December 24th last year, in front of Shuang'an Mall, bustling and prosperous, the reporter visited Detective Zhang Hui at the Shuangyushu Police Station of the Haidian Public Security Bureau. Zhang Hui, nicknamed Hunter Zhang, captures over 200 thieves per year and is widely regarded as an idol in the police academy.

Although somewhat reticent, Zhang Hui still revealed some tricks of catching thieves—thieves always look back; during meal times, thieves don't look at restaurant signs but only observe customers inside through the glass windows; driving criminals usually divide tasks among themselves, members don't walk side by side after getting out of the car, instead walking in a straight line, with the first person finding targets, the middle ones committing the crime, and the last one keeping watch... These little details are all Zhang Hui's secrets to solving cases and catching thieves.

Brother Zhang's endurance surpasses that of ordinary people. While staking out at the temporary residence, to avoid being discovered, he often stayed in the car for six or seven hours. Ordinary people would feel stifled and want to vomit, but Zhang Hui didn't seem to care, his eyes continuously fixed on the suspect's home door. He suffers from asthma, usually coughing uncontrollably when holding his breath, but once handling a case, his illness seems to improve. Finally, Zhang Hui successfully found effective evidence, capturing the entire gang.

Are you Zhang Hui? Yes, I am. Saying this, Zhang Hui stood on the steps of a restaurant. Standing high up allows one to see the entire street. Although he was talking to the reporter, Zhang Hui's gaze constantly swept across the street, not missing any pedestrians or vehicles.
