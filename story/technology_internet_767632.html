Is Facebook the world's largest open source company?

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:48:52

Who is the biggest open-source company today? Red Hat? Google? No! The answer is Facebook. An article on Wired's website points out that Facebook's open-source initiatives have touched every area of the internet, from hardware to software. Through the Open Compute Project, Facebook has built its entire database on an open-source foundation. Other internet companies are also actively participating in open-source projects, and "open-source" has become both the lifeline and an effective path for technological innovation for modern internet companies. Below is a summary of the article:

Who is the world’s largest open-source company today? You might think it's Red Hat, after all, this company generates over $1 billion annually by selling services related to open-source software (Linux and JBoss).

However, at the end of 2009, I believed that Google was the world's largest open-source company, just look at the massive amount of code it contributes—not to mention its source repositories and significant contributions to projects like Hadoop. But in my view, the current largest open-source company is none other than the social networking giant—Facebook.

Facebook: Open Source Everywhere

As the company itself says, “The Facebook platform is built from top to bottom on open-source technology. It can be said that without open-source, Facebook wouldn't exist.” However, Facebook isn’t the only company making such claims. Google has also consistently emphasized that without open-source, they wouldn’t have achieved their current scale in both performance and technology.

But Facebook's efforts in promoting open-source go beyond what other companies can match, as no other company has managed to build its entire data center on an open-source foundation like Facebook has. This project is called "Open Compute," a grand initiative: from network switches, server design, power systems, storage, and cooling equipment. Open Compute invites companies worldwide to help build data centers on Facebook, then expand and improve them. What a bold project!

Open Compute is just one of many open-source projects Facebook has announced. Others include:

Cassandra (NoSQL database), Thrift (cross-language service development framework), Apache Hive (a database infrastructure built on Hadoop), among others. Additionally, Facebook has developed many practical pieces of software and is an active participant in open-source projects like Apache Hadoop, MySQL, and PHP.

How many lines of code has Facebook contributed? It's hard to say exactly. In fact, the question is no longer important. Facebook's impact on "open-source" is evident through the projects it participates in and the code it contributes.

Internet Giants: A Hotbed for Open-Source Innovation

Facebook may currently be the world’s largest open-source company. However, it is not alone in witnessing the standardization process of open-source. Open-source is the "lifeline" for internet companies. For example, LinkedIn and Netflix have also contributed large amounts of open-source code. Twitter is also an active participant in open-source projects like MySQL.

In reality, across the global internet industry, "open-source innovation" has taken off at an unprecedented pace because open-source is the most effective way for internet companies to solve technical challenges.

These internet companies cannot delve into Microsoft Research to find answers to their technical problems. Using outdated technologies cannot address their urgent issues. Despite Microsoft, Nokia, and other suppliers investing billions of dollars in R&D, true innovation occurs within internet companies capable of handling scalability issues for billions of users.

The Open-Source Economy

Luckily, the rest of us can benefit from the open-source projects of Facebook and other internet companies. Facebook, Twitter, and others have seen the competitive advantage of open coding rather than restricted coding. "Open-source" has fundamentally changed how technology companies operate, which is why Facebook can fully commit to open-source projects while traditional software suppliers like Oracle cannot.

From hardware to software, Facebook's open-source initiatives have touched every field, showing people an effective method of innovation for the 21st century.
