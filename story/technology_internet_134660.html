China Telecom teams up with Microsoft MSN to create Tianyi Live - Sweet IT.Net

by xjyzhenai on 2009-03-27 11:59:50

The entry of telecom operators into the IM sector began in July 2006 with China Mobile's Feixin public beta test. In October of the same year, China Unicom launched Chaoxin, while the original China Netcom's IM product was called Lingxin. China Telecom is also about to launch a similar function named "Sky Wing Live". The mobile instant messaging (IM) application co-developed by China Telecom and Microsoft Corporation is about to go online officially.

In 2006, Microsoft launched the "Live" online service brand, gradually replacing its original MSN brand. This cooperation with China Telecom integrates the "Live" brand, jointly promoting the joint brand - "Sky Wing Live".

According to reports, the cooperation between China Telecom and Microsoft started in November last year. Based on the strategic cooperation agreement signed between China Telecom and Microsoft, both parties will jointly develop mobile Internet services based on Microsoft's Windows Live instant messaging service platform. "Sky Wing Live" will be an integrated information service platform that consolidates China Telecom's broadband, Internet, and CDMA network resources, achieving the integration of fixed and mobile networks, providing users with multimedia audio-visual communication such as text, voice, and video, and carrying many businesses of China Telecom.

It is understood that after Microsoft transferred its online business to the "Live" brand, the operation of MSN has been barely satisfactory. In 2008, Microsoft attempted to acquire Yahoo to enhance its online business, but it failed. The "Live" strategy became a sensitive topic within Microsoft.

Later, there were rumors that Microsoft might abandon the "Live" brand. Now, Microsoft's cooperation with China Telecom on the "Live" brand is considered Microsoft's last attempt to leverage the popularization of 3G to boost its online business.

As a brand of China Telecom's instant messaging services, the "Sky Wing Live" client can run on CDMA2000 EV-DO phones, as well as on PCs and laptops.

The "Sky Wing Live" client has two versions: PC version and mobile version. The PC version integrates wireless broadband access, IM functions, e-home communication, online customer service, e-home special zone applications, etc.; the mobile version centers on mobile IM services, enabling instant message interoperability between mobile IMs and between mobile IM and PC version IM, and provides services for downloading pictures, mobile movies, and iMusic, which are China Telecom's own value-added services.

The biggest selling point of the "Sky Wing Live" service is: the phone number is the chat account, and it can also interconnect with MSN, making communication convenient for users; users can also make (receive) calls and send/receive SMS messages through the client at a cheaper rate; through "Sky Wing Live", users can watch HD programs and send/receive emails without a second login; "Sky Wing Live" users can also enjoy 7x24-hour online customer service.

China Telecom users must log in to "Sky Wing Live" using the 189 email pass from Telecom.
