Disabled teacher leans on crutches to stay at the lectern for 25 years (picture)

by gy45tr3aop on 2012-02-28 16:54:03

Yesterday, at the Heqian Village School in Wushan Town, Kaixian County, Mr. Wang Dai Xue used his cane to help guide students with their homework. All pictures in this set were taken by reporter Ms. Wu Shan. Mr. Wang Dai Xue ringing the class bell; Mr. Wang seeing the students off at the school gate.

It's not difficult to do one thing, but it is hard to stick to doing one thing for decades. In the deep mountains of Heqian Village, Wushan Town, Kaixian County, there is a teacher nearing fifty years old who has been teaching on a single leg and using a cane for support, steadfastly standing at the podium for 25 years. He is the well-known "one-legged teacher" in Kaixian County, Mr. Wang Dai Xue. "I hope I can stand on this three-foot-wide podium forever and contribute my efforts for the children." As he approaches the 26th year of his teaching career, Wang Dai Xue, now gray-haired, revealed his greatest wish.

During breaks, he warms himself by the fire to reduce his pain.

On the morning of February 13th, after the car wound its way over one mountain ridge after another, the reporter arrived at Heqian Village, located in the most remote area of Kaixian County. Upon learning the reporter's purpose, two motorcycle taxi drivers voluntarily offered to act as guides. "He is quite famous in the village. Over the years, all the children in the village have been taught by him," said the motorcycle driver as he led the reporter up the steep hillside.

Upon arriving at the Heqian Village School situated halfway up the slope, the reporter noticed that the two-story brick building looked exceptionally simple. The school only had two classes with 48 students, and only two teachers, one of whom was Wang Dai Xue.

The reporter observed that due to years of walking with a cane, Wang Dai Xue’s back was slightly hunched, and his sparse, gray hair made him appear much older. "This year marks my 26th year of teaching," said the 49-year-old Wang Dai Xue.

The reporter saw that in Wang Dai Xue's office, there was a wooden heater. Every time class ended, Wang would place his immobile right foot on the heater. "There's nothing I can do about it. My foot gets chilblains every winter, causing pain and itching," said Wang Dai Xue. He could only use this method to try and alleviate his suffering as much as possible.

Starting to teach on the high mountain

Wang Dai Xue explained that his disability in his right leg stemmed from polio when he was a child. In June 1986, Wang graduated from high school. Despite his excellent grades, he was rejected from university due to his disability. After failing the college entrance exam, he was hired as a substitute teacher and began his teaching career at Qingming Village School.

At that time, the Qingming Village School was located on a mountain more than 1000 meters above sea level. Due to the retirement of the private teacher at Qingming Village School, and the inability to find a suitable replacement willing to go up the mountain, more than thirty students faced dropping out! The school leadership was extremely worried and came to the village to recruit people.

At this moment, Wang Dai Xue stepped forward and said, "I'll go!" The leaders shook their heads repeatedly and said, "No! The mountain is too high and the road too dangerous. How can you get up there with just one leg? Moreover, the conditions at the school are very poor." Wang Dai Xue earnestly replied, "Principal, please don't worry. Even if I have to crawl, I will crawl up there! Qingming is still a place where people can live!" The leader was moved by his persistence and determination, and agreed to let him try.

Wang Dai Xue recalled that he took some simple luggage and embarked on the journey to Qingming Village School. From 7 am to 2 pm, it took him a full seven hours to finally climb to the school. On the rugged and narrow mountain path, he mostly crawled on all fours, and by the time he arrived, his fingers and knees were all scraped raw.

300 villagers petitioned for his regularization

In September 1999, Wang Dai Xue was transferred to teach at Heqian Village School. When the local villagers heard that a new teacher was coming, they rushed over to see. However, upon seeing this one-armed teacher who appeared "elderly" and disabled, everyone sighed and felt disappointed, muttering, "Can he even teach?"

However, after one semester, the average score of the students increased by more than twenty points, and the parents finally became convinced. The following spring, many parents working away from home sent their children to study in Wang Dai Xue's class. A batch of students who had previously transferred to the central school also returned to his class, and even students from neighboring townships' schools came to study under his name. By graduation, the number of students in Wang Dai Xue's class had increased from the original eighteen to sixty-five.

In the summer of 2007, after the "substitute-to-public" policy was announced, Wang Dai Xue was once again rejected due to his physical disability. Upon hearing the news, more than three hundred villagers jointly wrote a letter to the Kaixian County Party Committee and County Government, unanimously requesting the government to handle Wang Teacher's regularization as a special case. Later, after investigation and visits by the Kaixian Education Commission, they learned about Wang Dai Xue's deeds and decided to allow him to take the "substitute-to-public" exam. Wang Dai Xue did not disappoint everyone's expectations, scoring high and being successfully recruited.

Saving students from floods

Due to the long distance to school and the difficulty of navigating the mountain paths, apart from diligently teaching his classes, Wang Dai Xue also had to take on the responsibility of escorting students safely home and preparing lunch for them.

"There is a river below the school. When there is less rain, Teacher Wang often watches the students cross the river before returning to school," said Liu Shunju, a villager from Heqian Village. Teacher Wang escorts the children home every day. Every year when the flood comes, the mountain roads are destroyed, and Teacher Wang endures the severe pain, supporting himself with a cane and together with the villagers, uses a hoe to dig the roads again. In May two years ago, a sudden downpour occurred, and Teacher Wang dismissed school early to send the students home. However, on his way back to school, he suddenly encountered a flash flood and was knocked down by the river water while crossing the river. Later, he managed to save himself by tightly holding onto a large rock.

Liu Shunju said that every noon, with the help of his wife Lei Di Mei, Teacher Wang cooks meals for the students for free. The firewood used for cooking was picked by Teacher Wang during Saturdays and Sundays when he went up the mountain. In winter, the cold wind howls, making the classroom freezing, and the students shiver from the cold. Teacher Wang then thought of a solution: lighting a fire outside the classroom. The students would take turns warming themselves by the fire to warm up their stiff bodies, and then continue their lessons. In this way, the students' studies were not delayed at all.

Heartfelt Words

Wang Dai Xue: "Wish to stand on the podium forever"

"Whenever I see the students I have taught leading happy lives and having jobs, I feel very content," said Wang Dai Xue happily when talking about his 25-plus years of teaching experience in the mountain village. Now that the roads have been repaired, many villagers have moved down the mountain and naturally sent their children to the central school with better facilities and stronger faculty.

Principal Qian Zhiyun of Dasheng Central Primary School in Wushan Town introduced that in recent years, more and more students from village schools have transferred to the central school for schooling, and many village schools have been merged or closed. Currently, the entire Wushan Town only retains a few of the remotest primary schools like Heqian Village School. The reason why Heqian Village School is temporarily retained is because the villagers of Heqian Village are reluctant to part with Teacher Wang. Parents believe that Teacher Wang teaches conscientiously and takes care of the children like a nanny.

"I have been teaching in the village school for 25 years, and I can no longer leave these children. If I were to say what my biggest wish in life is, it would be to stand on this three-foot-wide podium forever and make contributions for the children," said Wang Dai Xue, looking at those lively and cute students, tears welling up in his eyes.

Wife: "The office is our home"

Wang Dai Xue's wife Lei Di Mei is nine years younger than him. When Wang Dai Xue returned to the classroom to teach, the reporter saw Lei Di Mei busy cooking in the kitchen next to the classroom. "All this is for the students in the class," said Lei Di Mei. Because her husband has an inconvenient leg, she naturally takes on the tasks of washing clothes and cooking for Wang Dai Xue. Since almost all the students in the class are left-behind children, they bring rice from home to school, and she is responsible for cooking. Although she does not receive any wages for doing these tasks, it is the best support for her husband's work, and she is willing to wash clothes and cook for her husband for the rest of her life.

The reporter saw that there was a wooden bed in the corner of Wang Dai Xue's office. Lei Di Mei said that since her husband started teaching at Heqian Village School, in order to better take care of the students, this office became their home. "For so many years, I've already gotten used to it. He can endure despite his poor health, so my hardships mean nothing," Lei Di Mei smiled.

Colleague: "He gives me the determination to persevere"

As the other teacher at Heqian Village School, 58-year-old Chen Tianwen has been steadfastly stationed in the mountainous education post for years. "He really has it tough," said Chen Tianwen, introducing that he has worked alongside Wang Dai Xue for seven years and is most qualified to speak about Wang Dai Xue's deeds. Despite his physical limitations, Wang Dai Xue remains steadfast at the lectern in the mountains, deeply moving Chen Tianwen and giving him more determination to stay and teach the children in the mountains. Normally, he tries to do as much of the school's affairs as possible to lighten Wang Dai Xue's load.

Student: "Teacher buys us meat every week"

"He is very kind, like a father," said ten-year-old Guan Bingxin with a smile when asked about his homeroom teacher Mr. Wang's daily interactions with the students. In class, whenever everyone answers questions correctly, Mr. Wang always encourages them, and after class, if there are any problems, Mr. Wang patiently provides explanations. In life, Mr. Wang not only picks vegetables from his own garden to give the students extra snacks, but every week he spends his own money to add meat to the students' meals. Previously, the classroom walls were drafty, and the desks and chairs were broken. Mr. Wang personally repaired them and even climbed onto the roof to repair the tiles, using mud to patch up the cracks in the walls and smooth them out. "Having such a good teacher like Mr. Wang, we are really lucky, and we all thank him very much."
