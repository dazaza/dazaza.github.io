Symptoms of kidney deficiency in men:

1. Frequent urination at night:

Generally, nocturnal urination occurs more than twice, or the urine volume exceeds one quarter of the total daily urine volume. In severe cases, nocturnal urination occurs once every hour, and the urine volume is close to or exceeds the daytime urine volume. This situation belongs to frequent nocturnal urination. Normal urination during the day but frequent urination at night is a characteristic of this condition, mostly caused by weak kidney qi.

2. Dizziness, weakness, insomnia with many dreams:

The kidneys, as one of the important organs in the human body, nourish and warm other organs. If other organs have been sick for a long time, it is easy to injure the kidneys. Many chronic diseases such as chronic hepatitis, coronary heart disease, bronchial asthma, and hypertension often accompany symptoms of kidney deficiency.

3. Asthma:

The kidneys have the function of receiving qi. Due to kidney deficiency and inability to receive qi, shortness of breath and panting may occur, with more exhalation than inhalation, making you feel unable to breathe comfortably. In severe cases, accompanied by wheezing, symptoms such as worsening breathing difficulty and profuse cold sweats may appear.

4. Back pain:

Back pain is fundamentally due to kidney deficiency, which can be divided into internal injury and overwork. Internal injury kidney deficiency generally refers to congenital insufficiency, prolonged illness leading to physical weakness, or excessive fatigue. Mild cases make it difficult to bend or stand up, while severe cases may result in heel pain and weakness in the waist; Overwork refers to excessive physical burden or long-term engagement in the same fixed posture work (using computers, driving, etc.), which will eventually damage kidney qi and lead to insufficient kidney essence.

5. Dizziness and tinnitus:

Many people have experienced dizziness, and the feeling of blurred vision, vertigo, nausea, and vomiting is not pleasant. Moreover, patients with dizziness often experience tinnitus that interferes with hearing, and if left untreated for a long time, it may even lead to deafness. The causes of dizziness and tinnitus are mostly related to liver and kidney problems. In traditional Chinese medicine, the kidneys store essence and produce marrow, and the accumulation of marrow forms the brain. Therefore, kidney deficiency can lead to insufficient marrow sea, malnutrition of the brain, and symptoms of dizziness and tinnitus.

6. Constipation:

People with constipation often experience anal fissures, hemorrhoids, and other symptoms due to difficulty in defecation, affecting work and life, causing great suffering. Although constipation is due to the dysfunction of the large intestine's transmission function, its root cause is due to kidney deficiency because the kidneys open the two yins and control bowel movements. The transmission of stool must be stimulated and nourished by kidney qi to function normally.

7. Cold intolerance and cold extremities:

Cold intolerance refers to the feeling of being afraid of cold and wind. Cold extremities refer to cold hands and feet, or even coldness extending to the elbows and knees. Cold intolerance and cold extremities are often accompanied by kidney deficiency symptoms such as soreness and pain in the waist and knees, fatigue, lethargy, shortness of breath, laziness in speaking, bland taste without thirst, etc.

8. Excessive sexual activity:

In traditional Chinese medicine, the kidneys store essence. Kidney essence generates kidney yin and kidney yang, which nourish and warm the five zang and six fu organs. Kidney yin and kidney yang are interdependent and mutually restrictive within the human body, maintaining the physiological balance of the human body. If this balance is disrupted or one side declines, pathological changes will occur, and men may experience erectile dysfunction, premature ejaculation, spermatorrhea, and seminal fluid diseases.
