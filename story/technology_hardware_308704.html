Sina Sports reports: A 1-1 away draw against Atalanta saw Inter Milan's center-back Lucio become the scapegoat for the equalizer.

In this match, Mourinho fielded Lucio and Cordoba as his center-back pairing. In fact, Lucio’s performance was commendable for the first 80 minutes. In the 6th minute, he headed a corner kick attempt that just missed the target. In the 18th minute, Atalanta's forward Freire-Pinto shot from the edge of the box, but Lucio blocked it with a self-sacrificing effort, even sustaining an injury. In the 26th minute, after Sneijder lost possession in the backfield, Atalanta mounted an attack, but Lucio jumped high to clear the cross.

In the second half, Lucio remained active at both ends of the pitch. In the 53rd minute, during a free-kick opportunity, Lucio headed Sneijder’s set-piece into the net, though it was ruled offside. Defensively, Lucio accurately intercepted a through ball in the 60th minute and made two crucial clearances in the 78th and 80th minutes.

However, all of Lucio’s merits were wiped out by the goal conceded in the 81st minute. At that moment, Atalanta sent a long pass from the left flank, and Lucio misjudged the situation near the penalty area. His stretched leg failed to reach the pass, allowing substitute striker Thiliboqui to easily score. As a result, Inter missed the chance to widen their lead over AC Milan and Juventus in the standings.

Had it not been for this mistake, Lucio’s performance in this game would have been nearly perfect: nine clearances and four interceptions, fully worthy of his status as captain of the Brazilian national team. However, because of this error, Lucio became the culprit for Inter. Describing this mistake as a "disaster" would not be an exaggeration. After the game, on Inter's official website scoring system, Lucio only received an average score of 4.52, the lowest since joining Inter.

In fact, if one reviews Lucio's performances at Inter, they can see that his strengths and weaknesses are quite prominent. Lucio's advantages lie in his fierce style, accurate positioning, and when he performs well, he can indeed become a reliable barrier in front of Inter's goal. Overall, his arrival has undoubtedly enhanced Inter Milan's strength. But at the same time, Lucio's attitude towards handling the ball at certain moments is somewhat rash. Some low-level mistakes might occur in his play.

In Inter's 15 league games so far, some of Lucio's errors remain vividly remembered. In the opening round, a 1-1 home draw against Bari saw a major blunder by Lucio, preventing the Nerazzurri from securing a winning start for the third consecutive year; in the Champions League home game against Dynamo Kyiv, another clearance by Lucio went awry, deflecting into his own goal... In this game, had it not been for Lucio's mistake, Inter could have entered the final round of matches this year with a seven-point lead.

In fact, just days ago, Roberto Mancini, Inter's former coach, mentioned in an interview that if he led the current Inter side in the Champions League final, his starting lineup would include "Julio Cesar, Maicon, Samuel, Cordoba, and Chivu" on defense. Notably, there was no mention of Lucio's name. And Lucio is the only player in this blue-black squad that Mancini wouldn't include in his starting lineup. For a center-back, stability and avoiding mistakes are too important. It is worth noting that after Inter conceded in this game, Mourinho, sitting in the stands, suddenly became expressionless. Perhaps only Lucio himself knows what his coach is thinking. (By Bi Qieduo)

Reported by Chen Jingjing: At the start of the season, Mourinho complained about Inter having quantity but lacking quality among their forwards. But more than three months later, the Portuguese coach's view should have changed.

The data best illustrates this point: in the last 20 league matches, Mourinho's team failed to score only once, which was a 0-1 loss at Marassi Stadium on September 26 against Sampdoria. In the first 16 rounds of this season, Inter scored in 15 matches. Out of these 15 games, there were only two instances where none of Eto'o, Milito, or Balotelli appeared on the scoresheet. One was against Udinese (the scorers were Stankovic and Sneijder), and the other was against Catania (the goals came from Montari and Sneijder).

In the midweek Champions League match, Balotelli and Eto'o stepped up. In Bergamo, it was Milito's turn, who has started in six consecutive games recently. He broke free from the double-marking of two defenders right from the start and scored the 199th goal in Inter's history against Atalanta.

In the first 16 rounds of the league this season, Inter scored 35 goals, averaging over 2 goals per game, making them the most potent attacking force in Serie A. Among them, the three main forwards combined for 21 goals, exceeding a quarter of Inter's total goals in 2009. With a total of 74 goals, the Nerazzurri are likely to become the strongest attacking team in Serie A in 2009 (Roma, with 68 goals, is in second place).

Although Inter ultimately regretfully missed the best chance to expand their lead, the team's offensive power has been fully demonstrated in this season's matches. The combination of Eto'o, Balotelli, and Milito has a good chance of becoming Inter's strongest attacking triangle in the past ten seasons.
