Abstract: Urban sculpture not only reflects the image of a city, but also embodies the cultural characteristics of the city; urban sculpture construction is not only about building artistic works, but also about establishing systems and mechanisms.

Keywords: Sculpture, Construction, Decoration, and Research

At the same time as beautifying the living environment space for people, urban sculptures enrich people's spiritual lives and are an important symbol of cultural positioning that reflects the spirit of an era. Whenever people use their era's sculptural cultural landscapes, they reflect aspects of an era, including its geography and cultural connotations. Sculpture reflects the cultural characteristics of a city, conveying the specific features of the times through this medium, imbued with profound cultural significance. The cultural representation of sculpture mainly manifests in three areas: the spirit of the times, reflecting nature and cultural heritage, and embodying art, thought, and humanistic concern. Therefore, the success of urban sculpture is primarily influenced by the human living environment and time.

Urban sculpture reflects the human environment, encompassing stories related to cities and urban sculptures, city history, city psychology, city customs, and other aspects of the cultural environment. In other words, while form and aesthetics are important considerations for urban sculpture, with the integration of specific urban culture and history, the comprehensive relationship between this sculpture and social relations is the first reason for the success or failure of the entire city. For many sculptures in spatial environments, perhaps they will not be effective individually. When viewed independently in a city, there isn't a strict sense of evaluation standards for the quality of urban sculptures. Only when a particular sculpture is placed within a specific context can it continuously engage in dialogue with the history and residents of the city and town, gradually revealing its charm. For example, Shenzhen's sculptures, through dialogue with the city over time, have been enriched with more elements, making them irreplaceable symbols of the city or landmarks. It is hard to imagine any means by which another work in Shenzhen could replace it.

For the survival environment of humans, focus and timing are more important than aesthetics. In fact, this emphasizes the sociological perspective of urban sculpture. In the realm of urban sculpture, society is more important than mere aesthetics. Urban sculpture faces a broad social audience, and its success is not entirely determined by the sculptor. The most crucial factor is the opportunity the city provides, and sculptors alone cannot guarantee the success of sculptural art. Modern sculpture has broken free from the single, conservative traditional meaning, moving beyond static and stable historical models, transcending the limited understanding of traditional sculpture in terms of space and form. This makes people realize that sculptures do not have to be central monuments but could be closely related to the public or address issues of social activity, allowing more people to participate. Open public art has become an artistic form of public social service, providing a rest space for the public.

Urban sculptures designed according to different urban environmental contexts can be categorized into garden sculptures, square sculptures, campus sculptures, commercial sculptures, street sculptures, etc., reflecting the spirit of the times and serving as carriers of culture. Since they are always located in public spaces, they inevitably influence people visually and spiritually.

Urban sculptures possess unique expressive languages, adapting proportionally and materially to the surrounding environment, radiating captivating artistic charm. To ensure harmony between urban sculpture and the environment, attention should be paid to known factors such as theme, location, and scope, requiring overall coordination in aspects like environmental materials and colors. Specific methods should be adopted based on these factors. To make urban and environmental sculptures more prominent and harmonious, consider the following points. The selection of materials and colors for urban sculptures placed in outdoor spaces must withstand the test of seasonal weather, maintaining their allure throughout cold winters and hot summers. These materials must be durable, such as steel, stainless steel, aluminum, titanium, concrete, stone, fiberglass, synthetic materials, etc. The application of these materials greatly enriches the expression of urban sculpture. At the same time, as an important means of creating atmospheric and spatial ambiance in urban spaces, these materials enhance spatial recognition and prevent monotony by leveraging the diverse performance characteristics of various materials, achieving harmonious and unified effects, thereby beautifying the city's atmosphere. The selection of materials and colors for sculptures should be more vibrant and dynamic, enhancing the consideration of expanding spatial authority through the texture and color application of the material, thus playing a pivotal role in beautifying the urban environment through sculpture.
