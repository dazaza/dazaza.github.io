Special Contribution: Eating loofah during winter can treat wind-cold cough.

Ingredients: One old loofah (after maturation with black seeds), 2 liang of rock sugar.

Steps:

1. Wash the loofah and place it on a plate for steaming. If the loofah is too large, fold it and tie it with a string before placing it on the plate.

2. Place 2 liang of rock sugar on the plate and steam it together with the loofah.

3. Steam for 20 minutes in a pressure cooker or regular steamer, then let it cool down.

4. Squeeze out the water from the steamed loofah, approximately one small wine glass of distilled water, and keep it aside.

5. The loofah water mixed with melted rock sugar tastes cool, sweet, and slightly bitter, and should be consumed in one go.

6. 1-2 steamed loofahs can suppress coughing for several days without medication.
