Photonic Hair Removal

Professor Zhang Dechun said: "Photonic hair removal uses the patented American light-heat treatment technology (LHETM) to remove unwanted body hair. The specially pulsed light emitted by the hair removal device can penetrate the skin directly to the root of the hair follicle. The heat energy released is conducted via the cross-section of the hair shaft deep into the hair follicle, causing the temperature of the hair follicle to rise rapidly until it solidifies and necroses, achieving the effect of permanent hair removal."

(I) What are the advantages of photonic hair removal compared to other methods?

Compared to all other hair removal techniques, photonic hair removal achieves better long-term results. It is faster, more comfortable, and has fewer side effects. The new generation of photonic hair removal machines have greatly improved compared to previous single-wavelength laser hair removal machines: adjustable pulse width allows for the removal of hairs of different thicknesses, whether fine lip hair or coarse leg or chest hair. The pain during hair removal is minimal, with only a slight needle-like sensation, and very little risk of skin burns, which are unmatched by electro-acupuncture or laser hair removal.

(II) Why is photonic hair removal the most effective?

Photonic hair removal works on the principle of using the characteristics of photons (selective photothermal therapy), which are absorbed by the melanin cells in the hair shaft and hair follicle, thereby destroying the hair follicle without harming surrounding normal tissues.

The hair growth cycle can be divided into three phases: the anagen phase, the catagen phase, and the telogen phase. When the hair is in the anagen phase, the hair follicle is fully developed and contains the melanin that determines the color of the hair. At this time, if specific wavelength long-pulsed photons are used for irradiation, the melanin can be destroyed, leading to the atrophy of the hair follicle and thus achieving the purpose of hair removal.

Photonic hair removal primarily targets hairs in the anagen phase to destroy the hair follicles and achieve hair removal effects. However, generally speaking, human hair exists in all three growth phases simultaneously. Therefore, to achieve permanent hair removal, 3 to 5 treatments or more may be required to completely destroy the anagen-phase hair follicles and achieve the best hair removal results.

(IV) Can photonic hair removal be used for all types of hair?

The design of intense pulsed light technology can provide ideal treatment for your hair of different colors, structures, and different body parts. Any type of hair containing pigments (black, brown, red, even light yellow) can be effectively treated.

(V) Is photon safe for all skin types?

Intense pulsed light technology is the world's first photon hair removal technology certified by the FDA as safe and effective for removing excess hair from all kinds of skin.

(VI) Is there a possibility of side effects from photonic hair removal?

Side effects are very rare. There may be mild redness or localized swelling at the treatment site, but such reactions usually disappear within a few hours. Reducing sun exposure to the maximum extent before and after each treatment will lower the likelihood of side effects occurring. After treatment, you can quickly return to your normal work and life.

(VII) Things to Know After Photonic Hair Removal

1. Avoid sun exposure for 3 months to half a year after treatment, and apply the sunscreen lotion indicated by your doctor to the affected area to reduce sun exposure.

2. Mild swelling may occur after treatment, along with sensitive skin and feelings of heat or itching. If you feel pain, use ice packs to relieve the pain.

3. Avoid washing the treated area with hot water or scrubbing forcefully.
