1. Viscosity Indication:

The viscosity of lubricating oil is often indicated by SAE grades, where SAE stands for "Society of Automotive Engineers." For example, SAE40, SAE50, or SAE15W-40, SAE5W-40. The "W" stands for winter, and the smaller the number before it, the thinner the oil's viscosity, indicating better flow properties and suitability for lower temperatures. This ensures better engine protection during cold starts. The number after the "W" (after the hyphen) represents the oil's high-temperature resistance; a higher value means better protection at high temperatures. Higher-viscosity oils create more resistance in the lubrication system, not only consuming more power and increasing fuel consumption but also making the oil prone to oxidation and affecting cold-start protection. Oils with single values like SAE40 or SAE50 are monograde oils unsuitable for cold winters. Oils with dual values, such as SAE15W-40 or SAE5W-40, indicate advanced "multi-grade oils" suitable for a wide range of temperatures, providing comprehensive engine protection.

II. Quality Indicators:

SL/SJ: Indicates gasoline engine use

CF/CG: Indicates diesel engine use

Specifically: API stands for the American Petroleum Institute, and the API grade represents the quality level of engine oil. It uses a simple code to describe the performance capabilities of engine oil.

API engine oil is divided into two categories: "S"-starting series represents gasoline engine oil; "C"-starting series represents diesel engine oil. When both "S" and "C" letters are present, it indicates that the oil is for both gasoline and diesel engines.

The letters after S or C mean: From "SA" to "SL", each increment in the letter indicates improved oil performance with more additives used to protect the engine. The later the letter, the higher the quality grade. In international brands, oil grades are mostly above SF. For example, Shell Helix Plus is API SL grade, while Shell Red Helix Motor Oil is API SG grade, indicating that Helix Plus has a higher quality grade than Red Helix.

3. Basic Classification of Oil:

Currently, oils on the market can be roughly divided into mineral oil and synthetic oil based on their base oil. The highest grade oil belongs to synthetic oil.

The biggest difference between the two is that synthetic oil can be used across a wider temperature range, has a longer usage period, and is more costly. To achieve the same oil film requirement, synthetic oil can meet the need with a lower viscosity, whereas mineral oil requires a relatively thicker viscosity to reach the same requirement. In similar working environments, synthetic oil has a much longer usage period than mineral oil, thus being more expensive. However, when considering the reduced frequency of oil changes, synthetic oil does not cost significantly more than mineral oil.

4. Which Oil Should Be Used in Electronically Controlled Fuel Injection Vehicles:

Nowadays, the proportion of electronically controlled fuel injection vehicles is increasing, and many car owners take pride in owning such vehicles. Indeed, electronically controlled fuel injection vehicles have many advantages, such as an average increase of 15%-20% in engine power compared to carburetor engines. However, they also have stricter requirements for oil, especially in terms of anti-high oxidation technical indicators.

Electronically controlled fuel injection engines equipped with three-way catalytic converters have even stricter requirements for oil. The installation of three-way catalytic converters must meet two conditions: one is the implementation of lead-free gasoline because the lead in gasoline would cause the catalyst to become poisoned and ineffective; the second is that electronically controlled fuel injection vehicles with three-way catalytic converters must use oil with phosphorus content below 0.12%. If the phosphorus content in the oil is too high, it will result in the catalyst becoming ineffective, shortening the lifespan of the three-way catalytic converter, and preventing the exhaust emissions from being reduced to the minimum. Only oil with phosphorus content below 0.12% can ensure the normal operation of the three-way catalytic converter. Therefore, if your vehicle is an electronically controlled fuel injection vehicle with a three-way catalytic converter, you should choose SH or higher grade oil when adding oil to ensure smooth and safe driving.

5. Specific Selection of Oil:

Viscosity refers to the internal friction force, i.e., flow resistance, experienced by a fluid (including gases and liquids) when it flows. For example, 1. Increases fuel consumption. High-viscosity oil creates higher resistance, leading to increased friction inside the engine and thus increased fuel consumption. 3. Increases engine wear during startup. After the engine hasn't been started for a while, the oil originally attached to the upper part will flow back to the oil pan, leaving insufficient oil to maintain the engine during startup. If the oil viscosity is too thick, its movement will be slower, increasing the probability of wear. 4. ● Based on Ambient Temperature:

Almost every car manufacturer suggests the most suitable oil viscosity in the owner's manual. Porsche is the most demanding car manufacturer globally regarding oil, as it uses air-cooled engines that rely entirely on oil for cooling. Their internal technical bulletins evaluate each tested oil (requested by oil manufacturers). However, fundamentally, they still require drivers to choose oil based on the ambient temperature of their usage environment. In Shanxi province, the lowest temperature does not go below 25°C Celsius, and the highest has never exceeded 40°C Celsius. Within this range, generally speaking, 5W-40, 10W-40 are more suitable. These are the basic knowledge points you need to understand when selecting oil, determining oil viscosity based on the ambient temperature of your driving environment, and then considering whether to use mineral oil or synthetic oil based on your budget.

● What Oil Should Pure Commuters Use?

If your car is just a commuting tool and you only use it for work commutes, normal mineral oil 10W-40 would suffice for your needs. If you want to save on fuel costs, fully synthetic 5W-40 could meet your requirements due to its faster flow and ability to reduce engine wear during startup. By the way, 4S shops use 10W-40 for QQ cars, but do you know if it's mineral or synthetic oil?

● What Oil Should You Use if You Like High Revs?

Higher engine RPM corresponds to higher temperatures, so you should use oil with higher viscosity. For instance, 5W-50, 10W-50, or 15W-50. However, as mentioned earlier, higher viscosity also means greater resistance, slightly reducing engine performance. Synthetic oil can compensate for this issue because it can meet the same working conditions with lower viscosity compared to mineral oil requiring higher viscosity. However, you should consider the displacement output of your vehicle's engine. For four-cylinder cars with less than two liters, the RPM quickly reaches the redline, so slightly thicker oil (e.g., 10W-40 relative to 5W-40) might be more appropriate. The gap between the cylinder and piston rings is sealed by oil, and higher viscosity provides better sealing results. For six-cylinder or larger engines with over three liters, the RPM usually doesn't reach the redline (though there are exceptions), so they don't require as much attention. Another way to determine viscosity is to listen to the engine valve noise after running for a while. If the "tapping" sound is very obvious, you should increase the viscosity.

● What Oil Should High Compression Engines Use?

High-viscosity synthetic oil is the only choice, such as 5W-50, 10W-50, or 15W-50. If you frequently rev the engine to the redline, you can even consider 10W-60.

Six, Discussion on Oil Change Cycle:

According to general statements, the oil change cycle for car engine oil is 5000 kilometers. Many car owners regard this as a fixed concept. However, each vehicle differs in engine condition, type of oil used, and usage environment, so the reasonable oil change cycle should vary accordingly and cannot be generalized.
