Makeup for Pregnant Women is Very Particular

By Maternity Dedicated Skincare Products at 2012-2-24 in Maternal Skincare Maintenance | 0 comments

Maternal makeup is quite particular, and this also falls under the content of pregnancy precautions.

I. In terms of makeup:

It's best not to use whitening products. Moreover, cosmetics with special effects such as freckle removal or acne treatment should also be avoided if possible because they might irritate the skin or even harm the fetus. Pregnant women can wear light makeup, but the cosmetics used must avoid hormones and heavy metals like copper, mercury, and lead. They should choose products with simple ingredients that are primarily natural, gentle in nature, free of fragrances, low in alcohol content, and non-irritating. Drawing eyebrows and eyeliner is fine for pregnant women, but they shouldn't get permanent makeup, including eyebrow tattooing, eyeliner tattooing, or red lip embroidery; they also shouldn't pluck eyebrows but instead use a brow shaver; and they shouldn't apply lipstick or nail polish either. At this time, health is the most important!

II. Skin problems are the easiest to bother pregnant women:

After becoming pregnant, due to changes in hormone levels, pregnant women are prone to some special skin problems, such as itching, allergies, pigmentation, heat rashes, and insect bite dermatitis. If these issues arise, how should they be correctly handled? How should pregnant women choose skincare products?

1. Increased pigmentation: Pregnant women often experience increased pigmentation issues such as spots and freckles. Therefore, when going out, they should wear hats or carry umbrellas, and also apply sunscreen or use skincare products with sun protection. Additionally, consuming vitamin C is also important.

2. Acne breakout is also normal. It's best not to take oral medications or apply topical drugs because medications generally take a long time to show results, and prolonged medication use can easily be detrimental to the fetus, potentially leading to fetal deformities. The correct method is: If acne occurs, first gently massage the affected area while washing your face to unclog pores. Second, bring the currently used medicines, skincare products, and cosmetics to a dermatologist for review, allowing the doctor to determine if they are related to the acne. Never try to cover up acne by applying thick layers of foundation or spot concealer, as doing so will only exacerbate pore blockage.

3. Sweat spots may also appear. Pregnant women who sweat a lot are prone to this issue. If sweat spots occur, pregnant women should not handle it themselves but should use antifungal solutions under a doctor’s guidance to apply locally. Remember not to use medications arbitrarily, as pregnant women are prohibited from using certain medications. Not taking medicine is one of the most important precautions during pregnancy, and it will be too late to regret it later.
