In one month, Shanghai received 52 reports of airplane ticket fraud cases; 400 telephone numbers were utilized.

by cu22f009 on 2009-12-04 16:58:39

Last month, Ms. Xu in Shanghai found a company online that offered discounted air tickets. She immediately called the 400 toll-free number listed on the webpage. The person on the other end claimed to be a staff member of a well-known airline, stating that she only needed to transfer the money into a designated account to purchase the discounted ticket. Ms. Xu subsequently went to the bank and transferred 2800 yuan. However, when she requested her ticket, the other party kept delaying with various excuses.

This type of scam, where cheap air ticket information is posted online to lure ticket buyers, resulted in 52 reported cases in Shanghai from early June to July 8 this year, slightly higher than the same period last year. These scammers often post such fraudulent information on large travel websites, portal site blogs, or even set up fake "airline online ticket booking websites."
