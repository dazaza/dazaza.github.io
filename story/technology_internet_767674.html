The Daily Do's and Don'ts of Twitter Founder Jack Dorsey

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:08:03

Twitter founder Jack Dorsey has evidently become a central figure in the tech world over this period, not only releasing a series of articles but also seeing a book reflecting on Twitter's early days set for publication. Today, he spoke at Y Combinator’s Startup School, though during the conversation, Dorsey avoided all unnecessary and awkward questions. Instead, he demonstrated his wisdom by reciting passages from some of his favorite books and sharing with the audience what he does and doesn't do daily.

Dorsey first discussed "The Art Spirit" by painter Robert Henri, pointing out the similarities between the artistic creation described by Henri and the experience of becoming a startup founder. In short, founders (like artists) need to find their own way to create products they love. That is, relatively speaking, the execution of ideas is more important than the ideas themselves.

Then Dorsey talked about "The Score Takes Care of Itself" by Bill Walsh. Dorsey uses this legendary coach of the San Francisco 49ers NFL team’s book as his leadership manual. The advice listed mainly revolves around how to become a leader ("be yourself," "pursue excellence," "stay positive") and how to manage others ("show patience," "become the best partner for some employees").

However, perhaps the most interesting part was Dorsey's own useful lists. He told those present that he creates notes for everyone he meets, detailing important matters discussed during meetings or tasks to be delegated. Additionally, he maintains a daily list of things he must do and things he must not do.

Must-do list:

· Live in the moment

· Embrace vulnerability (Be vulnerable)

· Only drink lemon water and wine

· Do six sets of squats and push-ups every day

· Run three miles every day

· Reflect on this list every day

· Stand up straight

· Punch the boxing bag for ten minutes

· Greet everyone

· Sleep seven hours every day

Do-not-do list:

· Don't avoid eye contact

· Don't be late

· Don't set overly high expectations

· Don't eat sugar

· Don't drink spirits or beer on weekends
