Three child traffickers failed to sell the children

by anonymous on 2012-02-05 21:41:21

Three child traffickers, after failing to sell a child, brutally murdered a 6-year-old child by pushing him under a vehicle's wheels. They then pretended to be the child's family in an attempt to fraudulently claim a large compensation payment. Recently, the Lancang County public security authorities have officially transferred the case to the Lancang County Procuratorate for review and prosecution.

On May 6 at about 9:50 PM, Mr. Wu from Lancang County was driving past a section of road approximately 100 meters from the sugarcane factory where he worked when he encountered an oncoming vehicle. On both sides of the road were three adults and one child walking slowly.

Due to the narrow passage, Mr. Wu had no choice but to stop his vehicle and wait for the oncoming car to pass before continuing forward. However, just as his wheels began to turn again, there was a sudden "bang" from the right rear of the car. "Stop the car immediately! You've run someone over!" came shouts from both sides of the vehicle. A man in his forties shouted loudly: "How do you drive? You've killed my child!"

Seeing the "relatives" of the victim standing on both sides of the vehicle, Mr. Wu thought to himself that since his truck was newly purchased and equipped with protective barriers on both sides, if it had hit someone, the person should have been thrown off rather than ended up under the wheels. Despite his doubts, Mr. Wu still got out of the car to check. Upon seeing the bloodstains on the wheels and the deceased child's "father" who was continuously wailing while holding onto the child’s legs, Mr. Wu felt at a loss. "Since the incident has happened, why don't we first hide the body somewhere and discuss how to resolve this," suggested the child's "father" suddenly, showing unexpected understanding. The other two "relatives" also agreed, saying: "Yes! Since the incident has occurred, let's first hide the child's body; otherwise, the police will find out, and you'll end up in jail." For a parent whose child had just been crushed to death, such consideration seemed highly unusual, causing significant suspicion in Mr. Wu and his wife. However, honest and simple-hearted Mr. Wu chose to report the incident. "I thought, since the family is so understanding, I must give the deceased a proper account and take responsibility for what I should bear," said Mr. Wu.

The "family's" answers raised numerous doubts

Soon, the traffic police arrived at the scene. When asked for the child's name and age, the child's "father" could not answer. The behavior and words of the "family" aroused the suspicion of the officers.

Upon examining the household registration book presented by the child's "father," the police discovered that it clearly recorded his address as being in the Xiao Fofang Village Committee of Qianli Township in Lancang County, with one son and one daughter, the son already 11 years old. However, the child who was run over appeared to be only six or seven years old, clearly inconsistent with the records in the household registration book. Facing these discrepancies, the police took all the parties involved back to the Lancang County Traffic Police Brigade for further verification.

On the morning of May 7, the police learned that the "father's" son was still studying at a primary school in Qianli Township in Lancang County. Moreover, among the three "relatives" of the deceased, one, who claimed to go home to fetch warmer clothes due to the cold weather, disappeared from the scene. The statements of the other two did not match. The police preliminarily determined that this was likely a premeditated conspiracy. Subsequently, the Lancang traffic police handed over this bizarre traffic accident to the Menglang Town Police Station for investigation.

The plot to defraud money involving three traffickers

After questioning, the boy's "father" and another accomplice confessed. The "father" was named Chen Xiaosan, the other accomplice was named Chen Xueyan, and the missing "uncle" of the deceased was named Chen Gaiming. Chen Xiaosan and Chen Xueyan admitted that on the afternoon of April 19, Chen Gaiming spent money to abduct a six-year-old boy and kept him in a shack in Qianli Township in Lancang County. Afterwards, Chen Gaiming brought in his eldest son, Chen Xueyan, and his younger brother, Chen Xiaosan. The three decided to sell the child to repay gambling debts. After contacting potential buyers, the highest offer they received was only more than 10,000 yuan. In order to obtain more money, the three devised a more sinister plan —— bringing the child onto the road, pushing him under the wheels of a vehicle, and then demanding a considerable amount of death compensation from the "reckless driver." The three clearly divided their roles: Chen Gaiming would play the role of the child's uncle and be responsible for directing the entire operation. Chen Xiaosan, carrying his own household registration book, would make the boy appear as his 11-year-old son. Once the opportunity arose, Chen Xiaosan would personally push the child under the wheels of the vehicle to crush him to death. Chen Xueyan, pretending to be a relative, would join the "father" and "uncle" in surrounding the "hit-and-run driver" to demand compensation.

At around 3 PM on May 6, the three brought the child from Qianli Township to a section of road near the Mengbin Sugar Factory, about 9 kilometers from the Lancang County town. At around 9:50 PM that day, as they walked together on a road about a hundred meters away from the Mengbin Sugar Factory, they saw Mr. Wu driving and stopping to avoid an oncoming vehicle. Seeing the opportunity arise, Chen Gaiming quickly took the child from Chen Xiaosan, quietly moved to the side of the truck's right rear wheel, and when Mr. Wu restarted the vehicle, Chen Gaiming suddenly pushed the child under the truck's right rear wheel... Thus, the young life came to an end.

At 1 PM on May 19, unable to endure the harsh outdoor living conditions and the immense psychological pressure brought on by the police's comprehensive pursuit, Chen Gaiming turned himself in.

Journalist Xie Yuxin (Yunnan Legal System Daily)
