QQ Emotional Diary: The protagonist of this story is not me

by lwf225 on 2009-09-11 22:18:43

Tags: QQ Emotional Diary, Non-mainstream Diary, Emotional Diary - Sitting alone in the office, my colleagues have all gone to eat, and I am the only one eating a sandwich for lunch. After just one bite, I lost my appetite. It's not because I'm eating bread alone, but because of something you said yesterday.

Yesterday, I asked you, "What do you like about me?" You answered me, "Because you look very much like my ex-girlfriend."

You have always been good to me, accommodating me, indulging me, treating me as if I were the most precious doll, cherished in your palm. In these six months, we haven't had any arguments. To others, we are undoubtedly the most matching golden boy and jade girl.

At first, I also thought I was very happy. Until one day, you casually mentioned your past, and I began to mind your history. Although everyone has their own past, there is an unusual nostalgia in your words. In your eyes, she from the past is like a goddess, noble and perfect. I am just a doll, incomparable to a goddess.

I don’t want to pry into your past, but every time you look at me, it’s as if you're looking at a fragile porcelain statue, full of emotion and care. I start to doubt that what you’re really looking at isn't me.

Yesterday, I asked you to kiss me, and you did. Unfortunately, it was just a touch of lips, just a mix of saliva, just a continuation of desire. There was no taste in the kiss. Unable to bear it, I pushed you away.

"Not the same, completely different," there was no deep affection in your eyes during our kiss, the feeling was obviously different.

You asked me, "Are you uncomfortable?" Yes, I am uncomfortable, uncomfortably in my heart.

"What do you like about me?" You didn't answer me.

"What do you like about me?" I asked again. You still didn't answer.

"Answer me. Why won't you answer me? Are you afraid I'll know or are you too scared to say?"

"Do you really want to know?"

"Yes," I said firmly, with a bit of cruelty in my eyes, cruel to him, even more cruel to myself.

"Because you look very much like my ex-girlfriend."

"Like her in what way?"

"Do you really need to know? If you're going to be unhappy, why should I even say it?"

You said why bother? But you've already said it. If you were really afraid I would be hurt, you should have lied to me early on, saying you liked my arrogance, my stubbornness, or even my naivety, anything would be better than letting me know the truth.

I fell silent because I cared. A woman's intuition is truly accurate and hateful. Sometimes, I wish I had no feelings, no suspicions, no concerns, willing to be ignorant.

But if I were truly too ignorant, I would despise my own worthlessness.

You, as usual, called me to ask about my day, then picked me up after work, ate dinner, and finally sent me home, as if nothing had happened. But gradually, I realized that what you gave me was only concern and dependence. Two warm hands held each other tightly, but without love being transmitted, they eventually became cold, leading to our breakup.

The more anxious you were about me, the more repulsed I felt; the tighter you held my hand, the more I wanted to leave you. No matter how good you were to me, the deepest part of your heart wasn't reserved for me. Perhaps I was just a substitute, providing some comfort until the real thing is found. A substitute will never have its own value.

I became irrationally quarrelsome, finding topics to argue about whether there was a reason or not, hoping to get a little attention and love from you in the sound of arguments. But you kept tolerating me, regardless of whether I argued, cried, shouted, or even hit you, you still tolerated.

Couples who don't argue, can they really be considered couples?

One day, I finished work early, so I went to your office to wait for you. Actually, I wanted to end this kind of emotional life. Your love I could only share a tiny bit of, and I absolutely minded being someone else's shadow.

When I got to the parking lot, I saw your car, but there was another woman in it. Just one glance, and I knew she was the goddess deep in your heart, because we had very similar eyes. Finally, I understood why you looked at me with such deep affection.

I turned around and left, unwilling to see anymore, continuing to watch would only make it someone else's story. I was prepared to leave early, but when the actual breakup came, I couldn't let go. This isn't freedom, it's helplessness.

My phone rang, I knew it was you again.

Heartlessly, I hung up the phone, wanting to cry, but knowing I had to leave, I had to go. That wasn't my story, that wasn't my place. Choosing to leave is better than begging you to love me...
