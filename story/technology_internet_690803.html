CBA Guangdong Dongguan Bank

Year of establishment: 1993

Year joined CBA: 1995

City: Dongguan

Home arena: Dongguan Gymnasium

The Guangdong Hongyuan Basketball Club was established in 1993. After more than a decade of arduous entrepreneurship, the team has continued to grow stronger. They won three consecutive CBA championships from the 03-04, 04-05, and 05-06 seasons, finished as runner-up in the 06-07 season, and then achieved another three consecutive championships from 07-10. With six championships in seven years, the dominance of the Guangdong dynasty is unshakable.

In the 06-07 season, the team underwent significant adjustments with some veteran players retiring. Three young players - Chen Jianghua, Zhou Peng, and Liu Xiaoyu - were promoted to the main squad, making the team younger. In the 07-08 season, after Yi Jianlian joined the NBA, two young centers Su Wei and Wang Zheng were also promoted to the team. Over the years of CBA competition, the lineup featuring both veterans and rookies has gradually matured, forming the tactical characteristics and style of being fast, accurate, agile, and versatile. In the 08-09 season, they easily defeated Xinjiang in the finals to successfully defend their championship. In the 09-10 season, they once again defeated Xinjiang, winning their second three-peat, and establishing their dynasty with six championships in seven years.

Hongyuan Brief History:

An Outstanding Roster Achieves Remarkable Success

The team's head coach is Li Chunjiang, former head coach Li Qun, along with players Du Feng, Zhu Fangyu, Yi Jianlian, and Wang Shipeng. They have respectively received titles such as the Best Guard Nationwide, Most Valuable Player, Best Forward, Rookie of the Year, and 08 Olympic Star. They are one of the teams in the sixteen A-level teams that have gained the most honors. In recent years, numerous players from Guangdong Hongyuan Basketball Club have been selected for the national team. Among them, Yi Jianlian, Zhu Fangyu, Du Feng, Wang Shipeng, and Chen Jianghua have brought glory to the country in international competitions, making significant contributions to Chinese basketball.

Guangdong Hongyuan Basketball Club is China's first professional basketball club, and the Guangdong Hongyuan Bamaishi Basketball Team, known as the "South China Tiger," has become the third team in CBA history to win the championship after many years of hard training and fierce competition, following two consecutive regular-season championships.

Speed, agility, accuracy, versatility, and ferocity are the tactical styles formed by the Guangdong Hongyuan team over the years. Their lightning-fast counterattacks, quick-moving man-to-man defense, and flexible offensive and defensive tactics based on opponents' formations often leave rivals struggling to adapt and returning empty-handed.

New Generations Rising:

In the 1995-1996 season, Hongyuan first joined the CBA. Before this, they had won first place in the National Men's Basketball Second Division League. In their first CBA season, Hongyuan, relying on a group of retired former national players and some provincial and municipal talents, had an average height of 1.919 meters and an average age of 29. Their frontcourt featured former national players Huang Yunlong, Zhang Yongjun, and Li Chunjiang. On the guard position, there was Li Qun, who made the Chinese All-Star Team in the 1995 Eight Strong Tournament while playing for the Guangzhou Military District team. They quickly became league runners-up, and thus "Guangdong Hongyuan" began to make a name for itself in the CBA.

In Hongyuan's first three seasons, they consistently relied on older players and achieved notable results, finishing third in their second CBA season. Since its establishment, Hongyuan Club has always emphasized the cultivation of reserve forces, transitioning naturally between old and new players. By the 99-00 season, after significant personnel changes, the team's average age was 21, much younger than the initial 29 years. The average height was 1.95 meters, making it the tallest and youngest team since the establishment of Hongyuan Basketball Club.

In the 99-00 season, head coach Zhang Yongjun, based on the physical conditions and technical characteristics of the players, emphasized speed - quick throw-ins, rapid shooting, and swift pursuit. Gradually, a technique and battle style centered on speed was formed. Hongyuan successfully completed the transition between old and new players according to plan. Initially, the outside game had sharpshooters Zhang Yongjun and Li Chunjiang, replaced later by Li Qun and Liu Yong, who also excelled in long-range shooting, complemented by foreign players to strengthen the inside game. However, in the regular season, Hongyuan only managed a 15-7 record out of 22 games, advancing to the finals where they finished third with a 3-4 record.

Ten Years of Hard Work Pays Off: Coach Li Chunjiang’s Contribution Is Indispensable

In the 00-01 season, the addition of Du Feng and Jisen made Hongyuan even stronger. Although Hongyuan only finished sixth in the 00-01 season and seventh in the 01-02 season, they laid a solid foundation for the future dynasty, injecting a new batch of promising young players. By 2003, Hongyuan had undergone ten years of refinement since its establishment, becoming more mature and perfect. At the time, Hongyuan had national team players Zhu Fangyu and Du Feng, as well as Wang Shipeng, Yi Jianlian, and Chen Dawei, who moved up from the national second team to the CBA first team. Yi Jianlian was widely recognized as the next giant prospect after Yao Ming, with limitless potential. Alongside veteran Li Qun, the championship roster was complete.

Coach Li Chunjiang began serving as an assistant coach in the 1999-2000 season and officially took over as head coach on January 15, 2001, during a difficult period for Hongyuan. In the 00-01 season, Hongyuan's performance dropped from third place in the 99-00 season to sixth place, and further to seventh place in the 01-02 season. Despite the heavy responsibility, Li Chunjiang remained confident and composed, leading the team through various challenges. His coaching experience improved significantly through major competitions. Strict military discipline, strict management, and rigorous training kept the team highly motivated. Ultimately, through the team's efforts, Hongyuan achieved the best historical result of winning the regular-season championship and finishing as the runner-up in the finals in the 02-03 season. They met the veteran champion Bayi team in the finals but fell short due to the lack of experience among the young players, although this gave the young players confidence in battle.

Four Championships in Five Years: The Rise of the Hongyuan Dynasty

Although Hongyuan narrowly missed the championship in the 02-03 season, after a year of training and effort, in the 03-04 season, Du Feng and Zhu Fangyu gained recognition at the national level and played core roles in the Hongyuan team. Coach and captain Li Qun remained the soul of the entire team. Rookie Yi Jianlian, who made a standout appearance halfway through the rookie season in 02-03, and the undeniable inside talent of foreign player Jisen marked the transition from relying on veteran players to having young players take the lead. They bravely won the championship, achieving the best historical result.

In the 04-05 season, with veteran Li Qun still performing admirably, prime-aged Jisen, young national players Du Feng, Zhu Fangyu, and Yi Jianlian who participated in the 2004 Athens Olympics, and rapidly growing young players Wang Shipeng, Zhang Hao, and Chen Dawei, Hongyuan had a stronger overall strength. They formed an accurate long-range shooter group represented by Zhu Fangyu and a powerful inside line consisting of Du Feng, Yi Jianlian, and Jisen. This resulted in strong offensive capabilities, accurate shooting, and multiple attack points. With a young team averaging only 22 years old, the combat-ready Hongyuan ultimately realized their dream of defending the championship.

After winning the championships in the 03-04 and 04-05 seasons, Hongyuan won the championship again in the 05-06 season. In CBA history, only Bayi, which continuously won six championships after 1995, and Guangdong Hongyuan, which reached the CBA finals for four consecutive years and won the championship three times in a row, could truly be called dynasties. Guangdong Hongyuan finally announced the arrival of their dynasty.

The 06-07 season was Yi Jianlian's last season in the CBA. He would decide to participate in the NBA draft in June of the same year and officially join the NBA in the 07-08 season. Wang Zhizhi, who returned from the NBA to join Bayi, undoubtedly greatly enhanced the team's strength. Wang Zhizhi's return was crucial for Bayi, and ultimately Guangdong narrowly lost to Bayi led by Wang Zhizhi, missing out on a fourth consecutive championship.

Even though Hongyuan lost their formidable inside power Yi Jianlian in the 07-08 season, under the leadership of captain Zhu Fangyu, Hongyuan still retained championship-winning potential. Bayi was unexpectedly swept by the young Liaoning Pangu team in the 07-08 season and failed to reach the semifinals, avoiding a fierce battle with the veteran champion Bayi. Instead, they faced the young and energetic Liaoning Pangu team, who entered the playoffs for the first time in 13 years. Facing experienced and powerful Hongyuan, Liaoning ultimately lost 1-4 due to insufficient experience and youth, allowing Hongyuan to reclaim the CBA crown after missing out in the 06-07 season.

2008-2009 Season:

The regular season unprecedentedly lasted 50 rounds, and Guangdong achieved an unprecedented glorious record of 21 consecutive victories. With a 45-5 win rate of 90%, they declared their super strength to their opponents. The mid-season addition of “God” Park added double insurance to Guangdong's championship defense. In the finals, Guangdong easily defeated Xinjiang 4-1, with Zhu Fangyu winning the Finals MVP for the third time. After the game, Captain Zhu attributed this honor to longtime foreign player Jisen's loyalty to the team over 10 years.

Regular season: 45 wins, 5 losses, 1st place

Playoffs: Champion

Personal honor: Zhu Fangyu defended the Finals MVP

Other achievements: 2009 11th National Games Champion

2009-2010 Season:

On April 25, the 2009-2010 CBA Finals revealed the final mystery, with Guangdong defeating Xinjiang 103-94 at home, thus winning the CBA championship again with a total score of 4-1. This was Guangdong's sixth championship in the past seven years, and they achieved their second three-peat here. Stepping on the corpses of losers like Xinjiang, Shanghai, and Bayi, another Guangdong dynasty shone brightly at the peak of the CBA!

Regular season: 30 wins, 2 losses, 1st place

Playoffs: Champion

Personal honor: Zhu Fangyu defended the Finals MVP

Notable Figures:

Yi Jianlian

Yi Jianlian is one of the most promising players in Chinese basketball, with comprehensive skills, coordinated movements, and versatile play. He is a current national team member. In 2002, he joined Guangdong and consecutively won the CBA championships from the 03-04, 04-05, and 05-06 seasons. In 2002, he joined the Chinese youth team and has been selected for every national team since 2004. In 2007, he was drafted in the first round as the sixth pick by the NBA Milwaukee Bucks. In December of the same year, he scored a career-high 29 points in the NBA and was named Eastern Conference Rookie of the Month. He was traded to the New Jersey Nets before the 2008 draft.

Zhu Fangyu

Zhu Fangyu (born January 5, 1983), from Liuzhou, Guangxi, is a current key player on the Chinese national men's basketball team. Zhu possesses excellent psychological qualities and is renowned for his precise three-point shooting. He has a tenacious style and was part of the main lineup in the 2004 Athens Olympics and the 2005 Asian Championship. He won the Finals MVP in the 04-05 season. In 1995, he entered the Guangxi Sports School; in 1998, he joined the Guangdong team and was promoted to the Guangdong Hongyuan first team in 2000, leading Guangdong to consecutively win the CBA championships from the 2003-04, 2004-05, and 2005-06 seasons. In 2000, he was selected for the national youth team; in 2001, he was selected for the national team and has been selected for every national team since. In the 2007-2008 season, he helped Guangdong win the CBA championship and was awarded the Season MVP and Finals MVP. He is nicknamed “Three-Point Rain.”

Du Feng

Born in 1981 in Xinjiang, Du Feng joined the Guangdong youth team in 1996 and was promoted to the Guangdong Hongyuan first team in 1999, leading Hongyuan to achieve a three-peat in the CBA from the 03-06 seasons. He was selected for the national youth team in 2000 and began being selected for the national team in 2001. Despite being slender, he has incredible jumping ability and is known alongside Zhu Fangyu as one of the "Twin Stars" of Guangdong. In 2006, he was promoted to captain of the Guangdong team, becoming a key figure in the team.

Major Events:

1993 - Establishment of the club, the first professional basketball club in the country, known as the "South China Tiger."

2003-2004 season - Won the CBA championship, becoming the third team in CBA history to win the championship. Subsequently, Guangdong men's basketball won the CBA championship twice more, achieving a three-peat in the 2005-2006 season. In the 2006-2007 season, Guangdong lost in the finals to Bayi after Wang Zhizhi's return, ending their consecutive championships. However, in the 2007-2008, 2008-2009, and 2009-2010 seasons, Guangdong won the CBA championship again, increasing their total number of championships to six.
