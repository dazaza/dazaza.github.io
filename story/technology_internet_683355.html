Taobao and JD.com Pour Big Money into Mobile End: The Share of Vertical Mobile E-commerce Has Exceeded Half

Source: Webmaster News 365 & nbsp; 2012-02-29

When domestic e-commerce companies were fiercely competing on the PC end, a new round of battles on the mobile end has begun. JD.com CEO Richard Liu said at a small-scale media event that JD.com would focus on the mobile end in 2012, and the investment in e-books alone had exceeded tens of millions of RMB. Mobile Taobao Market Director Zhongtao Lu revealed to Phoenix Technology that Mobile Taobao's funding input scale in 2012 would exceed hundreds of millions of RMB. Dangdang.com Vice President of Mobile Division Yi Hu revealed that continuous funding would be invested in the mobile end with high-level support.

Planning ahead and occupying territory is something that domestic Internet companies are good at and often do. A CEO in the mobile security field told Phoenix Technology that being prepared always works particularly well in such a harsh competitive environment in China because death and bankruptcy can happen overnight.

Catering to users' needs, the mobile end has become a must-contest place for traditional B2C companies.

The most direct benefit of a mobile strategy for traditional e-commerce websites, according to Le Tao Vice President Chen Hu, is having an additional sales channel to acquire more new users.

Dangdang.com Vice President of Mobile Division Yi Hu told Phoenix Technology that mobility is the future trend and a necessary path for e-commerce. Early layout is beneficial without any harm.

He stated that the big background of mobile e-commerce is the change in user terminal usage behavior. With the increasing popularity of smartphones, mobile e-commerce will inevitably become the main battlefield of e-commerce. Dangdang can only cater to the changes in user shopping behavior. Additionally, smartphones will bring some new characteristics to shopping, such as the integration of online and offline and socialized shopping.

Vancl PR Department revealed that in 2011, the total number of Vancl mobile app users exceeded 4 million, the PV of the Vancl mobile WAP site broke through ten million, and the highest daily order volume exceeded 50,000 orders. Users who purchased Vancl products on their mobile phones placed an average of 3-4 orders throughout the year, and the highest daily order volume exceeded 50,000 orders. Orders from the mobile end already accounted for 10% of the total orders.

MaiMaibao Vice General Manager Hongfei Liu discussed the reasons why traditional e-commerce companies started to favor the mobile end, analyzing three reasons: one, the mobile end extends the time spent on the PC end; two, it allows users to browse goods more effectively during their fragmented time; three, the number of mobile end users is several times that of the PC end.

2012 will be the year when large B2C companies like Dangdang.com, JD.com, and Vancl exert effort, and also the year when pure mobile e-commerce companies face pressure and change. Analysys data shows that the scale of China's mobile shopping market was about 4.1 billion RMB in 2010, and the transaction amount this year is expected to approach 10 billion RMB. Although mobile end business does not yet have a substitution effect on PC end business, it will inevitably become one of the main drivers of income growth in the B2C market in 2012 from a long-term perspective.

Long Investment Cycle for Mobile B2C, No Technical Threshold

Public information shows that shoe B2C website Letao was the first B2C website in China to venture into the mobile end. Despite Letao CEO Sheng Bi's statement that "[e-commerce is a scam]" still ringing in our ears, "[the survivors are the kings]" has also become one of the reasons for them to persist.

In September 2010, Letao released its iPhone app, becoming the first B2C company in China to sell physical goods via an APP. By August 2011, the V5 version of Letao's Symbian mobile app was launched, and the APP applications basically covered the mainstream Apple and Android smartphone platforms. As of now, Letao's APP client monthly download activation volume is approximately 160,000, and the total Letao client download activation volume is 1.6 million, accounting for 6.5%.

Letao Vice President Chen Hu told Phoenix Technology that Letao formulated a mobile end strategy in the second half of 2010. Currently, the benefits are having an additional sales channel to acquire more new users, and when users generate shopping needs anytime and anywhere, they can directly place orders. Moreover, the greater value lies in when users become old users, the marketing cost will directly reduce to zero. He revealed that the order volume generated from the mobile end is 2,000 orders per day, accounting for 20% of the total order volume.

Chen Hu frankly admitted that there are pros and cons. The disadvantage is that mobile requires a certain amount of continuous investment, which is costly and has a long cycle for medium and small-sized e-commerce companies and those with insufficient technical backgrounds.

Wireless Taobao Market Director Zhongtao Lu told Phoenix Technology that technology cannot be a threshold between competitors like vertical B2C companies, and Wireless Taobao's positioning is a comprehensive shopping platform.

Vertical B2C MaiMaibao Vice General Manager Hongfei Liu frankly admitted when discussing the entry threshold for traditional e-commerce companies that due to their financial advantage, they might snatch some ad spaces, including some spaces with less favorable cost-performance ratios.

Richard Liu stated that the download volume of various JD.com mobile clients approached 10 million, but the activation ratio was not high, with daily orders ranging from 15,000 to 17,000. It is expected that by the end of this year to the first half of next year, it will be the time when the proportion of sales from various mobile client entrances increases significantly, and it is expected to grow to 20%-25%.

High Funding Thresholds for Small and Medium-sized E-commerce Companies, Taobao and Dangdang Invest Heavily

Undoubtedly, as the largest B2C on the PC end, can Taobao replicate its advantages to the mobile end and reclaim the top spot? Phoenix Technology connected with Wireless Taobao Market Director Zhongtao Lu, who provided a set of data: the mobile Taobao transaction volume in 2010 was 1.8 billion RMB, 11.8 billion RMB in 2011, and it is expected to achieve fivefold growth in 2012.

He stated that because the situation on the mobile end is different from the PC end, it does not plan to completely replicate the Taobao model on the PC end. He stated that the current business composition of Mobile Taobao is WAP + client, with 60% of the transaction volume coming from WAP and 40% from the client. It is expected that in 2012, the transaction volume from the client will increase to 50%.

He revealed that Mobile Taobao will cooperate with the three major operators to pre-install smartphones across the board. Regarding the financial investment of Taobao in mobile business, he stated that it is inconvenient to disclose, but indicated that the investment level is hundreds of millions of RMB.

As a listed e-commerce company, every penny spent by Dangdang.com will be reflected in the financial report. Despite this, Dangdang.com Vice President of Mobile Division Yi Hu still emphasized that mobile e-commerce is a strategic area for Dangdang, and continuous investment will be increased. The main resources will be invested in improving customer experience rather than blindly burning money to grab customers.

Yi Hu revealed that Dangdang's latest financial report showed that the daily order volume from mobile terminals reached tens of thousands of orders, and the activation volume of mobile applications has reached over a million. Currently, it accounts for about 5%-10%, with a much higher growth rate than the number of people ordering via computers.

Analyst Shousong Chen believed that the layout of e-commerce companies on the mobile end is an inevitable trend, and neither technology nor capital investment is a decisive threshold. Whether entering early or late will not be a decisive winning factor. Shousong Chen believed that the camp of mobile e-commerce layout would completely replicate the PC end, meaning that the first and second-tier camps on the PC end would still be the first and second-tier camps on the mobile end.

Another analyst believed that the ecosystem structure = source price + order processing + technical backend support. Large e-commerce companies would adopt smartphone pre-installation and establish their own R&D teams to occupy market share, while small and medium-sized e-commerce companies would rely on traditional third-party technical support to develop the mobile end to reduce costs.

Mobile payment is not yet mature, and JD.com and others take self-built payment channels

In stark contrast to the grand entrance of e-commerce companies into the mobile end is the lagging behind of mobile payment. Analyst Shousong Chen from Analysys International believed that given the immaturity of mobile payment methods, e-commerce companies may consider cash-on-delivery to lower the payment threshold and development cost.

When asked if mobile payment is safe, Le Tao Vice President Chen Hu said that there is no safety issue with mobile payment at this stage. The reason is that mobile payment is built on mature Internet payment technology and experience, with complex verification mechanisms and preventive measures in the usage environment and process, sufficient to reach the safety standard of ordinary payment.

Chen Hu revealed that B2C websites of different scales have different strategies for laying out the mobile end. Companies with a certain scale will inevitably pursue the best user experience and long-term low fees, while small companies focus on solving mainstream payment issues.

For this, Phoenix Technology connected with Dr. Zhonghong Zou, a mobile security expert from NetQin. He stated that the current situation is that mainstream e-commerce terminals are relatively safe, all equipped with certain password verification and payment confirmation measures. However, since mobile hackers usually adjust their attack methods based on the security coefficient of the attack target, users still bear certain security risks if they cannot identify in time.

He revealed that the security situation of small and medium-sized e-commerce on the mobile end also varies. From the layout mode, currently, e-commerce mobile payment applications mainly adopt two modes: platform-based and vertical-based. Some e-commerce applications adopt a navigation form of shopping information, providing users with massive shopping information through summarizing network information. Other small mobile e-commerce focuses on vertical fields as their way of survival.

He revealed that small e-commerce companies, to make it as convenient as possible for customers to pay, will try to support the most popular payment channels, such as Alipay, Tenpay, and various online banks. Large e-commerce companies have the capital to bargain with payment providers or build their own payment channels, such as JD.com. But in terms of security risks, they are the same.

Regarding this statement, Guanguang Guan, CEO of Kuaiqian, told Phoenix Technology that currently, small and medium-sized e-commerce companies have no layout in mobile payments. But regarding the mobile terminal layout, small and medium-sized e-commerce companies, compared to large e-commerce enterprises, need to pay more attention to details, such as in the processes of downloading, operation, and consumer regional characteristics, making it more refined. After all, consumers will only be interested in mobile terminals that offer personalized and more convenient experience designs.

Mobile end revenue scale: Taobao first, vertical B2C second

Maibaibao is a company engaged in vertical e-commerce and is one of the earliest companies to venture into mobile e-commerce. Its vice president Hongfei Liu stated that the investment in mobile end funds is clearly less than the PC end, but similarly, orders are far less than the PC end. This is because advertisers have not fully recognized it yet, so the investment intensity is limited.

When discussing the impact on mobile e-commerce, he predicted that it would impact two types of mobile e-commerce: one, those aimed at speculation, i.e., the part with the biggest bubble; two, smaller-scale mobile e-commerce.

He revealed that in terms of revenue scale on the mobile end, the largest revenue scale comes from Taobao, followed by Maibaibao and traditional e-commerce, with Maibaibao accounting for 50%.

When talking about their own advantages, Hongfei Liu stated that Taobao and traditional e-commerce mainly derive their income from the PC end, with the mobile end accounting for less than 30% of their ratio. In contrast, Maibaibao focuses on professional mobile e-commerce. He revealed that Maibaibao's sales revenue in 2011 was nearly 500 million RMB.

According to statistics from a third-party agency, in the third quarter of 2011, the mobile e-commerce market size reached 3.77 billion RMB, with a market share increase of nearly 7% quarter-over-quarter, accounting for 34.8% of the entire mobile internet market, just behind mobile value-added services. China has 900 million mobile phone users, and the proportion of smartphone users is developing rapidly. On this huge user base, all e-commerce companies are accelerating their layouts in mobile internet, further promoting the development of mobile purchase business.
