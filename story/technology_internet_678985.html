Teaching you how to supplement calcium in each stage of pregnancy

by childrenstory126 on 2012-02-27 17:47:57

Generally, food therapy is considered as an ideal way to replenish calcium. So, what are the foods high in calcium? We have specially listed some [experts] among calcium replenishing foods to help pregnant mothers replenish calcium.

Milk and dairy products

Half a catty of milk contains nearly 300mg of calcium. It also contains various amino acids, lactic acid, minerals and vitamins that promote the digestion and absorption of calcium, and the calcium in milk is more easily absorbed. Pregnant mothers are advised to take milk as one of the main foods for daily calcium replenishment. Yogurt, cheese and milk tablets are also good sources of calcium.

Kelp, shrimp skin and seafood

Kelp and shrimp skin are high-calcium seafood. Kelp can be cooked with meat or served cold after being boiled, both of which are good delicacies. Eating 25g per day can replenish 300mg of calcium. Shrimp skin contains even higher levels of calcium, with 500mg of calcium in 25g of shrimp skin. Making soup or filling with shrimp skin are good choices for daily calcium replenishment.

Bean products

Soybeans are high-protein foods and also contain a large amount of calcium. 500g of soy milk contains 120mg of calcium, and 150g of dried tofu contains as high as 500mg of calcium. However, the calcium absorption rate of tofu is much lower than that of dairy products, so it can be used as a supplement to milk.

Vegetables

There are also many varieties of vegetables that are high in calcium. 100g of pickled mustard greens contain 230mg of calcium; 100g of Chinese cabbage, rape, fennel, coriander, celery, etc., each contain about 150mg of calcium. But most vegetables contain oxalic acid and phytic acid, so the absorption rate of calcium in them is relatively low.

Nuts

Nuts contain rich unsaturated fatty acids that are beneficial to the human body and also have a high content of calcium, which can meet the needs of calcium replenishment.

Experts believe that calcium supplementation should start earlier, and the most ideal time for calcium supplementation should begin when preparing for pregnancy. However, at different stages of pregnancy, what are the key points of calcium supplementation, and how should pregnant mothers reasonably eat to healthily and happily spend every day of pregnancy? Let's take a look at the experts' advice on calcium supplementation for pregnant mothers!

Key points of calcium supplementation in early pregnancy

In fact, during early pregnancy, the development of the fetus has not yet taken shape, and the mother's calcium supplementation is mainly for herself. This requires appropriate supplementation based on the needs of the pregnant mother. During this period, calcium supplementation through food is the main method, which can help pregnant mothers effectively prevent insomnia. Be sure not to overdo it, otherwise it may cause symptoms such as nausea and vomiting.

Key points of calcium supplementation in mid-pregnancy

From the fifth month of pregnancy, entering the mid-pregnancy stage, the growth of the fetus accelerates, especially the development of the baby's teeth and bone formation requires a large amount of calcium. The blood volume of pregnant mothers also increases significantly, and uneven diet can easily lead to calcium and iron deficiency. If at this time pregnant mothers think that the strong reactions of early pregnancy have passed and eating more can meet the need for calcium, this is a wrong view.

Key points of calcium supplementation in late pregnancy

As the baby continues to grow, the demand for calcium further increases. Drinking 500ml of milk or yogurt daily, taking 500mg of calcium supplements, and eating some calcium-rich foods can meet the required amount of calcium. Of course, don't forget daily sun exposure. In winter, it is generally no less than 1 hour per day, while in summer it is about half an hour, avoiding the strongest sunlight between 10 a.m. and 3 p.m.
