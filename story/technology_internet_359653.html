Epilepsy patients' "long-overdue spring"

by king0r8k on 2010-07-01 14:15:47

Dear Editor:

Hello!

"I love you, miss you, yearn for you, blame you, my deep feelings will never change. Haven't you ever looked back and thought about the vows of yesterday? Even if you are infatuated with the gorgeous water lilies blooming in the water, don't forget that wild lilies in the lonely valley also have their spring..." Every time I hear Pan Yueyun sing this song "Wild Lilies Have Spring Too," I always think silently: I might be that wild lily, but I don't know if I can wait for my beautiful spring.

I have suffered from epilepsy for more than ten years. For all these years, I have closed my heart, afraid to interact with others in life, immersed in pain, helplessness, and loneliness. My mother told me that when my illness flares up, the symptoms are particularly terrifying—foaming at the mouth, complete loss of consciousness, convulsions in the limbs, and an unpleasant bleating sound coming from my mouth. However, I like perfection and pursue perfection, yet I am so imperfect... I am such a flawed person. I can only walk with my head down, read with my head down, and bury my head deeply, deeply. Or, in the darkness of night, choose a solitary moment to cry. Making friends, falling in love, for me, it's simply daydreaming, it is so far, so far away.

One day, when my mother told me she had seen on your website an article about the Neurotransmitter Balance Seizure Therapy at Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital (www.dx110.com) which could completely cure epilepsy, I cried tears of joy. Could my illness finally be cured? Can I finally be like other girls? Can I make friends too? Has my spring finally arrived?

After consulting with Director Ding Ziwei of Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital by calling 010—59796169, we received his insightful and unique response. That same evening, my mother and I took the last bus into the city and then continued to travel after several days of hustle and bustle, finally arriving at Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital. When my mother saw Director Ding Ziwei, she immediately knelt down, begging the doctor to save me and give me a good life. Seeing my mother shedding tears, my whole body trembled. It turns out that epilepsy not only made me suffer but also made my family cry. I thought no one could understand my pain and bitterness, but in fact, my mother saw everything and felt it deeply in her heart. I must get better, repay my parents, and I secretly resolved.

Director Ding treated me using their hospital's signature therapy: Neurotransmitter Balance Seizure Therapy. In the first course of treatment, I clearly felt that my mind became much clearer, I had more energy, faster brain reactions, and my sleep quality improved significantly. Because of this, my mood improved, and my determination to cure epilepsy grew stronger. In the following days of treatment, I was firm in my confidence and actively cooperated with the treatment. Day by day, one course after another, after three courses, my epilepsy was finally cured. This time, my mother and I hugged each other and cried, we were crying tears of joy. Thanks to the treatment methods of Beijing Jingke Epilepsy Specialty Hospital, thanks to Director Ding's treatment, my fate has taken a turn for the better.

Half a year later, I received the first rose of my life, I started to fall in love. Although my spring came late, it still came. When I heard Pan Yueyun sing "Wild Lilies Have Spring Too" again, I smiled wisely: Wild lilies really do have spring, and epileptic patients can also wait for their beautiful spring.

Epilepsy Recovered Patient: Xiaoye

April 9, 2010
