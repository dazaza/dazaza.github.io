What is the date for Christmas?

by jindan974 on 2012-02-10 08:38:28

December 25th is Christmas Day. Christmas Day celebrates the birthday of Jesus Christ, the founder of Christianity, and is the largest and most beloved holiday in America. According to the holy book of Christians, the Bible, God decided to send his only son, Jesus Christ, to be born among humans and find a mother, so that he could live on earth and help people better understand God, learn to love God, and love each other better. "Christmas" means "celebrating Christ," commemorating the moment when a young Jewish woman named Mary gave birth to Jesus. Mary was engaged to Joseph, a carpenter. However, before they lived together, Joseph discovered that Mary was already pregnant. Since Joseph was an upright man and did not want to disgrace her publicly, he wanted to break off their engagement quietly. While he was pondering this, an angel of God appeared to him in a dream, saying, "Do not hesitate, take Mary as your wife. The child she carries comes from the Holy Spirit. She will give birth to a boy, and you shall name him Jesus, for he will save people from their sins." Although the exact date of Jesus' birth is unclear, it was approximately 2000 years ago. The calendar divides time into BC (Before Christ) and AD (Anno Domini, the Latin abbreviation meaning "the year of our Lord Jesus"). In the first three hundred years after AD, Jesus' birthday was celebrated on different dates. Eventually, in AD 354, church leaders set December 25th as the official birthday of Jesus Christ.
