"The Desire Record of the Post-80s Generation" - the frustrated struggle of the neglected post-80s youth.

by jqrxass on 2009-09-02 10:57:31

Chen Hui was addicted to online games during his university years, neglecting his studies; he struggled after graduation, facing repeated setbacks in the vast sea of job seekers. He yearned for a pure love but kept missing chances with the ones he liked. In a life filled with difficulties, driven by desires, evil thoughts arose... The once naive college student underwent trials of life. How would his character change? Amidst the complexities of the world, could he still maintain the clarity of his heart?

Volume One: My University (1)

Three years ago when I first entered university, I would inexplicably suffer from insomnia at night. Perhaps it was because I had never lived in a dormitory before, always going to bed around 10 o'clock at home and getting up at 5:30 in the morning to memorize books. After starting university and beginning a communal living lifestyle, the lights in the dormitory were turned off at 11 p.m., yet some people would still chat, creating quite a commotion, discussing things like who was rich, which girl was pretty, or who had feelings for whom. Or they would talk about Jin Yong's wuxia novels, debating who had the highest martial arts skills or their favorite protagonists. I found these discussions interesting and soon joined in the conversations. Class monitor Feng Yan hadn't actually read Jin Yong's works. I said, "Really? I envy you." He curiously asked why. I replied, "You don't know how exciting it is to experience them for the first time!"

Volume One: My University (2)

After school started, I attended classes diligently for a few days, though I wasn't completely focused. Wen Tao discussed "Jian Xia Qing Yuan" with Taozi every day, another online game they played after "Diablo". They talked about how powerful the Heavenly King was, lamented the weakness of Wudang, or discussed what great equipment they got today. It all sounded rather intriguing. On Saturday, I accompanied them to check it out and eventually couldn't resist the temptation to start playing myself. Since I planned to take the postgraduate entrance exam at our university, I didn't feel the need to study too hard. The two of them, one being Heavenly King and the other Wudang, began to guide me. I heard that the Heavenly King sect was the most bizarre, so I also created a Heavenly King character, a big fat man wielding a hammer, naming him "The Tenderness of That Hammer."

Volume Two: Life Elsewhere (1)

Finally, I graduated. All the hidden worries, self-reproach, and resentment within me were washed away, and I became a pure soul again. I told myself, "From today on, I will be a different person, a brand new one. He will sprout anew, produce ears, bloom, and finally bear abundant fruit."

The school was clearing out students en masse, and there was no way I could stay in the dormitory anymore. I packed my quilt and mattress, sold my math books by weight, and stuffed all sorts of miscellaneous items into a patterned plastic bag. When tidying up the iron cabinet, I discovered a brand-new mosquito net, dazzlingly white. During my university years, I had never used it. On one hand, I was always up all night and rarely stayed in the dormitory. On the other hand, mosquitoes didn't seem to like me. Taozi said, "Since you only take a bath once a year, the mosquitoes are probably repelled by your smell."

Volume Two: Life Elsewhere (2)

In order to make the old lady believe me even more, I confessed further, saying, "To be honest with you, I didn't want to say this at first, but he doesn't work night shifts. Instead, he stays up all night watching movies and playing games. I really don't understand why he wastes his time like this every day." The old lady asked, "Really? Then where does his money come from?" I replied, "Where else? He asks his family for it. He's such a grown-up now, yet he still spends his family's money without shame."

It was then that I realized I was also spending my family's money, and my face turned red involuntarily. I felt a bit feverish, as if I were sitting next to a stove. Fortunately, we were talking in the dimly lit hallway, so she probably didn't notice. I continued, "It's not entirely wrong to spend a little family money right after graduation, but the key issue is that he shows no ambition and just stays up all night."

Volume Two: Life Elsewhere (3)

My relationship with Zhao Juan was purely professional until it advanced due to a Korean drama. That day, I went to her proofreading room across the hall with a stack of manuscripts. She was fully engrossed in watching the Korean drama "Full House", so I didn't dare to disturb her. I stood behind her for a long while until she occasionally turned back and saw a flash of fear in her eyes. Upon recognizing me, she closed her eyes, sighed deeply, patted her chest, and said, "You scared me to death! I thought it was the editor-in-chief."

I smiled at her and said, "Is the editor-in-chief as tall and imposing as me?" She replied, "Well, that little shorty is indeed fatter than you." I laughed and said, "Don't let Du hear that, he's just in the next room." She said, "No problem, he's not here. He went to Henan for a meeting." I asked, "How do you know?" She said, "Yesterday he came to print an advertisement, saying he would go to Henan today."
