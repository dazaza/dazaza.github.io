16-Year-Old Dutch Girl Completes Solo Round-the-World Sail

by ggh1z9805 on 2012-02-09 18:13:30

Dutch Girl Realizes Dream after Ordeals

Sixteen-year-old Dutch girl Laura Dekker arrived at St. Martin Island in the Caribbean on the 21st, becoming the youngest person to sail around the world alone.

Youngest Record Holder

At 3 PM local time on the 21st, Dekker steered her 11.5-meter-long sailing yacht "Guppy" to St. Martin Island. On January 20th, 2011, she set off from here, sailing westward. On September 20th of the same year, she celebrated her 16th birthday.

The previous record was set by Australian teenager Jessica Watson, who completed her circumnavigation in 210 days in May 2010, just three days shy of turning 17. While Watson did not stop along the way, Dekker made multiple stops, including Panama, the Galapagos Islands, French Polynesia, Tonga, Fiji, Australia, and South Africa. About 400 people welcomed Dekker at the dock in St. Martin Island. She wept with joy and waved to the crowd accompanied by her family.

Struggles and Joys

During her voyage, Dekker faced torn sails, battled harsh weather, slept on a damp bed, narrowly avoided collisions with ships twice, and endured cockroach infestations. At the same time, she found ways to entertain herself: surfing, diving, cliff jumping, and playing the flute.

It took her 47 days to cross the Indian Ocean. Upon arriving in Durban, South Africa, her legs became weak, forcing her to exercise on the pier to regain strength. "I can't fully grasp what I've done," Dekker said in St. Martin Island. "At times during the journey, I asked myself, 'What are you doing?' But I never thought about giving up. It's my dream, and I had to achieve it." Along the way, she often saw dolphins. Near South Africa, she saw whales for the first time, "They dived into the sea right in front of the boat, splashing water onto the ship."

Controversy

Dekker's parents lived aboard a boat. Born on a ship, she first sailed alone at the age of six and conceived the idea of sailing around the world alone at ten. Initially, Dekker planned to set off in early 2010. However, Dutch child protection authorities believed that Dekker was too young, and single-handed sailing was dangerous, unable to ensure her own safety. The education department said Dekker should attend school. Consequently, the Dutch court prohibited Dekker from setting off and placed her under the supervision of child protection officials.

Dekker fled to St. Martin Island but was returned home by the police. In July 2010, the Dutch court ruled that as long as Dekker's parents agreed, she could set sail. She departed from Gibraltar on August 21st, 2010, altering her plan to make St. Martin Island the starting point.

The Guinness World Records organization and the World Sailing Speed Record Council previously announced that they would not recognize the youngest age record for circumnavigation because such activities were too dangerous for minors. Some people supported Dekker's actions. American girl Jody Bell, aged ten, is a fan of Dekker and went to the dock to welcome her with her mother Merlin. Their T-shirts read: I'm crazy about the "Guppy" yacht. "My daughter and I have been following Laura," said Merlin. "We think she's amazing. We are inspired." Dekker initially planned to go to New Zealand after completing her voyage. On the 21st, she said she would first focus on finishing her studies.
