Sun Wukong's Letter to White Bone Spirit VS White Bone Spirit's Reply to Sun Wukong

by aqzyanshengi on 2011-08-29 10:27:43

Dear Ms. Bai,

First of all, I sincerely apologize to you and say sorry. Yesterday, Master wrote me a letter, which I read over and over again. It truly touched my pain points, as if someone had dug into my heart, making me suddenly realize my mistakes. I lay awake all night reflecting on myself, and now everything is clear. I've lived for hundreds of years, causing chaos everywhere, and ended up with nothing today, as if I had lived in vain. When I think about it, I feel very heartbroken. Although the master may be somewhat hypocritical, he was sincere towards me and said some heartfelt words, for which I am truly grateful from the bottom of my heart.

The journey to the West for scriptures was actually a trap set by Jade Emperor and Bodhisattva. The journey wasn't as difficult as we imagined. We could have sent a more skilled deity who could have retrieved the scriptures in no time. However, Master Tang was being groomed for promotion but lacked grassroots work experience, so he was sent down to gain some training, yet they couldn’t let him suffer alone. Therefore, people like me, Bajie, Sha Wujing, and Bai Long Ma, who had made mistakes, were assigned to accompany and serve him.

The eighty-one tribulations along the way were all calculated by the Bodhisattva. Tang Monk was never in real danger. Wherever we went, Guanyin Bodhisattva knew clearly. Whenever things got critical, whether I sought her out or not, she would definitely come herself or send her disciples. With a wave of the lotus, all demonic energy would vanish.

As for those so-called demons, except for ones like you who have no backing and have to fend for yourselves, most of them were either celestial beings' mounts or golden fish raised by Bodhisattvas. They all possessed exclusive divine weapons, how could I possibly defeat them? After exhausting all my strength, when I reached the critical moment, their masters would appear and say, "Wukong, let me take them away." What could I say then? The only ones who suffered were those like you, without any connections, ultimately being exposed in your true form.

I always thought that with my loyalty and bravery in subduing demons, I would earn recognition from leaders and comrades. But after returning, at the summary meeting, all honors were given to the master, while we only received our Buddha statues. Fortunately, the master removed the Tightening Headband from my head, allowing me to breathe a sigh of relief. Later, they were all appointed official positions, but no one took care of me. Helpless, I returned to my hometown of Huaguo Mountain to live off wild fruits and spring water. There were no benefits, but whenever there was something like studying documents, someone would come to check my learning situation, asking me to write self-examination materials, saying that the mistake I committed five hundred years ago was still serious and needed deep reflection. It made me feel as if the Tightening Headband, though removed, was still invisibly on my head.

If only I hadn't hidden in Laozi's furnace back then, resulting in these flawed fiery eyes that can see through others' tricks and ugliness that others cannot see. Seeing these things, I can't help but speak out, and speaking out causes offense. Moreover, during the journey, I fought many subordinates or relatives of leaders, so everyone looks down on me, mocking me for once being so capable but now being less than them.

I naively thought that only Bajie was lustful, and the master was a man of integrity. But after reading his letter, I finally realized the truth. On the journey to the West, he was just pretending. While we were worrying to death, he was secretly having affairs with the Queen of the Daughter Country and the Spider Demons, seriously violating discipline without anyone questioning it. In America, this would lead to resignation due to misconduct.

But they are well-connected, obtained fake diplomas, passed evaluations, and were promoted to higher ranks. Today, I still have nothing. Houses are too expensive to buy, so I can only continue sleeping in the Water Curtain Cave. Now, the age for promotion has passed, and I don't think about it much anymore. I just want to find someone, settle down, and live a good life.

We became acquainted through conflict. Among the women I know, you are the one I admire the most. Although you are beautiful and capable, you do not rely on officials or rich men, instead creating your own path through hard work and courage. Moreover, you are diligent, brave, kind, and virtuous.

Upon reflection, I still like you a lot. Both of us are emotional individuals, romantic, upright, and hate evil. You're not young anymore, and will soon become a leftover woman. Why don't we get together? Going to your White Bone Cave is fine, or coming to my Huaguo Mountain works too. We can develop tourism based on Huaguo Mountain, and I can start a martial arts school. Life will surely be manageable. Whatever hobbies you have, I support. Let’s properly raise our children, sending them to Tsinghua or Peking University to become officials, avoiding our old paths.

I look forward to your response... wishing you happiness.

Wukong

July 16, 2011, at Huaguo Mountain

White Bone Spirit's Reply to Sun Wukong

Mr. Sun,

Your letter has been received.

Thank you for remembering me, but I cannot marry you. I'm sorry.

I'm not someone who holds grudges. In fact, I used to have a hero complex. Back when you revealed my true form, I thought, 'Once you return from the Western Heavens, I'll find you, marry you, bear your children, wash clothes for you in the Water Curtain Cave, and till the soil for you on Huaguo Mountain.' Actually, at the time, both your master and Zhu Bajie and Sha Wujing knew I was a demoness. Your master pretended not to notice, Bajie had ulterior motives because of my beauty, and Sha Wujing, being timid, chose to avoid trouble. Only you had some spirit and a sense of justice, chasing and attacking me under everyone's watchful eyes. At that time, I deserved the beating. I was young and naive, daydreaming and hurting others. You woke me up and gave me a lesson, for which I thank you.

However, later events changed my perspective. After your pilgrimage, your master was praised for his leadership skills and decisive decision-making, and was promoted to a higher position. Bajie, despite his past tendencies, was still assigned to oversee women's affairs. Sha Wujing, considered to have worked hard even without great achievements, especially excelling in logistics, was promoted to Director of Transportation. Only you were deemed impulsive, lacking brainpower, undisciplined, and arrogant, thus being left to fend for yourself. Since then, I've realized that admiring heroes is useless; one must be more practical. Life is full of unexpected turns, and one must not lose direction.

I'm doing well now. I have my own villa, car, and child.

You might not know, but after you returned to Huaguo Mountain to fend for yourself, I started working at Tian Shang Ren Jian (a high-class establishment). Later, I met your master. He is no longer the bookish scholar shouting 'Disciple, save me!' Due to his chanting background, he sings exceptionally well and often comes to our place, spending lavishly. Moreover, he now travels with an entourage, wielding considerable power. Your master also has a good sense of humor. He said I have white skin and a bony beauty, hence calling me White Bone Spirit, making my heart flutter.

Because of our past encounters, every time your master came, he requested me to accompany him. He told me that after returning, he married the princess of the Daughter Country. He also said they didn't get along, as the woman had turned into a tiger, constantly nagging, checking phone calls and QQ chat records. More importantly, after giving birth, she suddenly gained weight, becoming quite unappealing. Your master also said he had liked me back then, but was being evaluated by the organization and didn't dare to act rashly. After returning, he intended to find me, but the woman's father was a close associate of his superior, so he couldn't offend him. In short, your master wanted me to be with him, offering everything except the title. Later, I moved into the villa he provided. Your master also replaced the old white horse with a BMW, which he gave to me. I know he has other women, including Spider Demon, whose file he altered and placed in another unit for reception work, leaving traces here and there. Last year, I bore him a son, who dared not take the Tang surname, and now follows mine, named Bai Tang.

Mr. Sun, please don't be angry or look down on me. If I were with you, I would have status but no money. Perhaps you don't know, I am terrified of poverty. We can't afford houses now, and we can't live in caves forever, right? Nowadays, peaches aren't worth much; a season's harvest doesn't earn as much as one night at Tian Shang Ren Jian. How will we live in the future, raising children and paying for their education? It's all too difficult. Nowadays, as long as you have money, people will treat you differently, regardless of where it comes from or its cleanliness. You mentioned starting a martial arts school, but I think you should forget it. You only know stick fighting, but you can't match Jay Chou's nunchucks, which make far more money. All I can say is, take care of yourself. Take care of your health, peel peaches before eating them, as modern peaches have pesticides that can't be washed off.

By the way, I heard Ox King has passed away. Go visit Princess Iron Fan, who is now a widow. There might still be hope.

Goodbye, Mr. Sun!

YOURS,

White Bone Spirit
