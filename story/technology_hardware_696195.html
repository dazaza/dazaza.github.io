Tiger attacks sightseeing bus, passengers asked to sign compensation agreements

by anonymous on 2012-03-07 21:48:30

The tourists said that they were not saved until about 10 minutes later after they were besieged. There was no signal on the scene, and they couldn't make a phone call, with no staff on site as well. The passengers said that after the incident, they were asked to sign on a blank paper. One of the reasons was that the ticket would be refunded, the other was that part of the cash would be compensated, and the third was that the tourists would have nothing to do with the management office. However, this tourist did not disclose the specific amount of compensation.

The passengers said that when the windshield was broken by the tiger's attack, the bus crashed into the guardrail. At that time, they almost despaired. Ten minutes later, the administrator appeared.

On February 5th, according to the Shandong TV Station's public channel "Minsheng Express", we reported yesterday that the touring bus at Jinan Maoling Wild Animal Park was attacked by tigers. According to the tourists on the bus, in the Bengal Tiger area, when tourists were leaving, two tigers kept circling around them, which attracted four other tigers to circle around as well. There were 28 people on the tour bus, including five children. "Seven or eight tigers surrounded the tour bus and attacked it violently, biting the tires, pulling off the wipers, smashing the glass, and even damaging the windshield. The driver's hand was injured."

From the video, the reporter can hear the screams and cries for help from many tourists. The tourists said that there were six or seven tigers circling around them, and the bus was just an ordinary bus without a protective net. In fact, they had seen a bus with a protective net before, but they were not allowed to use it. The glass on this bus was broken after being hit by the tiger twice.

Yesterday, around 12:30, several tigers in the tiger park of the Runling Wildlife World in Jinan were frightened and scratched the glass of the sightseeing car, trapping multiple tourists inside.
