Two confirmed absences for Arsenal against Liverpool: Wenger refuses Cruyff's call for Fabregas again

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 17:08:39

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absent from Starting Lineup, Wenger Rejects Cruyff's Call for Fabregas

Sina Sports - Arsenal's away match against Burnley this Wednesday is not expected to be too challenging, with the sole aim of securing three points and reclaiming the lead over Villa. However, more injuries have emerged in Wenger's squad. In Sunday's match against Liverpool, main player Denilson suffered a back injury, while Traore injured his groin. Arsenal's official website reported that both players have been confirmed unavailable for the match against Burnley.

However, there is some good news as Eboue, who was sidelined due to a groin strain during the Liverpool game, has returned to the squad and can strengthen the right flank options. Wenger said, "There was too little recovery time after Sunday's game, so we've added some more injuries. Denilson is definitely not going on the trip; he might be available for Saturday's match against Hull City, but we must check thoroughly, only playing if his back is fully recovered. But Eboue has returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits as his team completely shook off the shadow of their heavy defeat by Chelsea. When discussing the championship prospects for this season, Wenger stated that the Premier League title race for the 09-10 season is the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. Wenger told the official website, "No team has been able to maintain an early season leading position. Instead, four teams are closely entangled, and I believe there will be six, seven, or even eight teams surprising everyone on any given weekend. Clearly, this is the most open battle for the league top spot in my 13 years as manager, and I feel many teams have a chance to win the league title."

Respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own team. For Arsenal, Wenger always has a special confidence. He said, "I deeply believed from the start of the season that this would be our year, and I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: look at how well we've improved as a whole, how strong our team spirit and quality is, and how we prepare fully for each game like a battle. From September to next May, all of this will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain its championship aspirations, Cesc Fabregas' importance is undeniable. With Van Persie injured, Fabregas is almost the only guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger, of course, wants to ensure that Fabregas plays as well as possible in every key match. Some external requests that can be rejected will be rejected, such as the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for the 22nd of next month. Catalonia coach Cruyff requested that Fabregas play in this event at Camp Nou, but Wenger firmly rejected it.

Fabregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyff, but Wenger disregards this, as he has the right to reject such requests since Catalonia is not an officially recognized FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fabregas feels his physical condition is fine, and since next week is not a double-header week, he believes he has time to make a trip to Spain. He said, "I'll play in the matches against Burnley on Wednesday and Hull City three days later, but after that, there will be no Premier League games for nearly a week, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

But Wenger can only focus on his own team. There is a crucial match against Villa next weekend. The other three of the big four have lost to Villa, raising questions about whether Arsenal can perform well. Van Persie got injured in an international match for Holland, and Liverpool clearly refused to let Mascherano represent Argentina in the friendly against Catalonia. Wenger, who has always disliked the hassle of international games, naturally would not agree to let Fabregas participate in a match that doesn't count as an A-level international. Wenger said, "We must respect the concept of priority, and the priority is the club itself."

Besides this match, Spain also has another notable friendly next week, the match between the La Liga All-Stars and the African All-Stars. This is called the second Africa Charity Match, jointly initiated by UNICEF and the Kanoute Foundation, and will take place at the Bernabeu. Originally, the African team's list included five Arsenal players: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboue. Clearly, besides native-born players, those with African heritage were also counted. Wenger wouldn't agree to this arrangement either. Therefore, in the final list, there are no Arsenal players, in fact, not even a single active Premier League player. The closest connection to the Premier League, apart from the founder of the Kanoute Foundation, former West Ham and Tottenham forward and current Sevilla player Mali's Kanoute, is probably former Manchester United forward Manucho, who transferred to Valladolid this summer.
