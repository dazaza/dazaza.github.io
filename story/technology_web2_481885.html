Automobile exports, dragged down by the Russian market, are expected to continue slumping into 2009.

by hhhiii83805 on 2011-08-26 13:24:51

As the financial crisis spread, automobile exports ended the high growth in the first half of 2008 and entered a decline channel in the third quarter. According to experts' estimates, the situation in 2009 is even less optimistic, automobile exports will continue to decline, with a drop of more than 20%. This has caused a major blow to self-own car enterprises that rely heavily on exports to a certain extent. While the Ministry of Commerce and other government departments have introduced a number of policies for support, these enterprises are also adjusting their export strategies accordingly, mba.gdufs.edu.cn/site/default.asp, to adapt to the new export situation.

The second half of 2008 has seen four consecutive months of negative growth

(Data source: China Association of Automobile Manufacturers)

According to the import and export statistics data of automobile products organized by the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers from customs across the country, from January to November 2008, automobile exports totaled 649,000 units, an increase of 20.3% year-on-year, with the growth rate falling back by 51.6% compared to the same period last year.

From the chart above, it can be seen that since August, China's automobile exports have shown a year-on-year declining trend for four consecutive months, which is the first time in six years. In November alone, only 36,000 vehicles were exported, a year-on-year decrease of 46.4%, with the monthly export volume reaching the lowest level in nearly 20 months.

Mainly affected by the Russian market

(Data source: China Association of Automobile Manufacturers)

From the perspective of intercontinental automobile exports in our country, the European market saw the most severe decline in the second half of 2008, and the main factor dragging down the export volume was the export to the Russian market. This is because since the second half of 2008, Russia has formulated many policies to restrict automobile imports, including raising certification thresholds and significantly increasing tariffs.

Overall, the reasons for the sluggish automobile exports in the second half of 2008 are multifaceted. According to the latest monitoring report released by the General Administration of Customs, under the impact of the global financial crisis, global automobile consumption has been sluggish, market demand has clearly shrunk, and international automobile manufacturing giants are facing severe challenges. Similarly, China's automobile exports have also faced serious challenges. Apart from the impact of market shrinkage, www.5yinhangka.com, the current automobile manufacturing technology in our country is still relatively backward, www.yuchang.net.cn/js/default.asp, while developed countries such as the EU have raised requirements regarding vehicle emission limits and vehicle safety; additionally, countries in the Middle East and South America mostly refer to and adopt EU standards. Once related technical measures are adopted by other countries, it will increase the pressure on China's automobile exports, leading to a further slowdown in export growth.
