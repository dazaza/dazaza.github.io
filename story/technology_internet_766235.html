The First Batch of 4G Mobile Phones Obtain Access Licenses, with a Speed More Than 10 Times of 3G

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 20:03:36

The first batch of 4G mobile phones to receive network access licenses are over 10 times faster than 3G

It was learned yesterday by the reporter that the MIIT has recently issued the domestic first batch of 4G mobile phone network access licenses. The four models from four manufacturers which received the license are all TD-LTE format. This means that 4G mobile phones can be officially put on the market for sale. Among them, ZTE's Grand Memo LTE received the first 4G network access license.

Yesterday, the reporter logged into the official website for the management of telecommunications equipment network access and searched for the permitted mobile phone products using TD-LTE as a keyword. The results showed that there are currently four products from four manufacturers that have been the first to obtain 4G network access licenses, with the date of issuance being August 30th. The order of the network certificate number shows that ZTE Communications' U9815 model obtained the first TD-LTE mobile phone network certificate domestically.

The other three enterprises and their products that received the license are: Huawei's D2-6070, Sony's M35t, and Samsung's GT-N7108D.

According to ZTE Communications, as the first product to obtain the 4G network certificate, ZTE Communications' U9815 is also known as rand Memo LTE, which is the LTE version of ZTE Communications' annual flagship large-screen mobile phone Grand Memo. It supports TD-LTE/FDD/TDS/GSM four modes, adopts a 5.7-inch screen, is equipped with a quad-core processor, 12-core GPU, and a 13 million pixel camera, and supports 1080P HD video playback and recording.

China Mobile Chairman Xi Guohua once publicly stated that China Mobile will launch the TD-LTE "double hundred" plan this year, hoping to cover more than 100 cities at or above the prefecture level nationwide with TD-LTE networks, procure over one million TD-LTE terminals, cover a population exceeding 500 million, and build the world's largest 4G network. Shanghai Mobile's official microblog posted a few days ago that the first batch of more than ten 4G mobile phones are undergoing testing by relevant institutions under the MIIT, and will soon be officially launched on the market.

4G mobile phones integrate 3G and WLAN into one unit and can transmit high-quality video images. In terms of price, which users are most concerned about, 4G is comparable to fixed broadband networks in price, and its billing method is more flexible and adaptable, allowing users to completely determine the required services according to their own needs.

The speed of 4G networks will reach more than 10 times that of 3G. Downloading a high-definition movie would take around an hour with 3G, but only a few minutes with 4G.
