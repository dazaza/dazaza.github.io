Tricks to Transform Small Spaces into Spacious Duplexes

by longersing on 2009-11-27 12:06:17

Clever use of space can transform high-rise buildings into duplex large spaces, all it takes is the inspiration from interior design. Eight tips to help you completely renovate small spaces into duplex living for urban white-collar professionals. Below are these eight renovation techniques. The tricks to transforming small spaces into spacious duplexes are as follows:

Tip 1: Divide space by function to fully utilize square footage With a floor height of four meters and six, the second floor is planned with a master bedroom and master bathroom, while the back of the first floor includes a guest restroom along with a study and guest room shared in a multi-functional flexible leisure space. Areas are divided clearly according to their usage functions, fully utilizing the efficiency of the space.

Tip 2: Create distinct layers with light and dark building materials In the common area, imported tiles, dark solid wood flooring, white artificial stone, dark mirrors, and glass are used, allowing the space to flow between deep and light neutral tones, making the spatial layers more distinct, creating a visual depth that achieves an effect of expanding the space.

Tip 3: Multi-functional use of the second-floor rest area The second floor is planned with a master bedroom and master bathroom, and a platform is designed as a resting seating area which can also be expanded into a nursery in the future. This space has multiple uses. The master bathroom includes a standard bathtub as well as a separate toilet area, achieving separation of wet and dry areas.

Tip 4: Simple lighting creates an urban atmosphere In the bedroom space, simple color coordination is used to return the space to its most comfortable sleep function without interference. Only line lighting is designed on the walls, through the shading of light and shadow, giving the main bedroom a lively rhythm and creating a fashionable urban atmosphere.

Tip 5: Dual-path stairs do not occupy square footage From the entrance, one can choose to go to the first-floor public area such as the dining room or kitchen, study, or go straight up from the left side to the second-floor private space master bedroom. The dual-path planning at the entrance ensures that the stairs do not occupy any unnecessary space.

Tip 6: Use stairs to plan a dressing room and storage room Along the stairs to halfway up is a fully functional dressing room, while underneath is utilized as a storage room and shoe cabinet, completely avoiding any wasted space. Custom-made system storage cabinets are precisely embedded into the corner spaces under the stairs.

Tip 7: Connect two units to create a larger space Mr. and Mrs. Jiang purchased two small urban units each about 7 pings in size with high ceilings, then connected the two, creating approximately 15 pings of public space. Adding a second-floor mezzanine planned as private space of about 10 pings, they now have approximately 25 pings of space.

Tip 8: Extend the visual upward to create grandeur Descending along the tall white brick wall is the living room and a small dining area for two, combined with an open kitchen forming the public area. The complete tall ceiling visually extends upwards, creating a grand atmosphere akin to a large space.
