The failure of molds in fact is due to the reasons such as local material wear on the surface layer and thus scrapping. Moreover, the processing cycle of molds is very long, and the processing cost is extremely high (especially for precision complex molds or large mold manufacturing processing costs which can reach hundreds of thousands of yuan or even millions of yuan). Therefore, performing surface strengthening on specific parts of the mold that truly endure wear can significantly extend and improve the service life of the mold and die, undoubtedly a method with significant economic significance. In addition, most molds become ineffective and are scrapped because only a very thin layer of material on the surface has been worn out. Thus, by repairing the locally worn areas of the mold and key metal parts, and during the repair process, applying a layer of high hardness and highly wear-resistant metal coating on the mold's surface that actually endures wear, it can "turn waste into treasure." Not only does this allow the mold to be repaired, but the service life of the repaired mold will also be significantly improved compared to the original mold, resulting in enormous economic benefits (for example: repairing a large shaft of a power plant motor, including all preparation time, only takes a few days using a micro-beam cold welder, but can generate economic benefits of over a million yuan).

According to incomplete statistics, the annual consumption value of molds in the mechanical processing industry is five times the total value of all machine tools, indicating how enormous the mold market is in industries such as machinery, metallurgy, light industry, and electronics. For example, in the metallurgical industry, the annual consumption of hot-rolled rolls alone exceeds 300,000 tons, and the value of hot-rolled rolls accounts for more than 5% of the cost of steel production. The large consumption of molds not only directly increases production costs but also causes greater economic losses due to frequent shutdowns of production lines caused by frequent changes in working molds.

A mold repair machine is a high-tech device for repairing surface wear and processing defects of molds. The principle of the mold repair machine is to use the high-frequency electric spark discharge principle to perform heat-free welding on the workpiece, thereby repairing surface defects and wear of metal molds. The main characteristics are a small heat-affected area, no deformation of the mold after repair, no annealing, no stress concentration, no cracks, ensuring the integrity of the mold; its strengthening function can also be used to perform surface strengthening treatment on mold workpieces, achieving wear resistance, heat resistance, and corrosion resistance of the mold.
