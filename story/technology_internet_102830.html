Tianjin Hardware City still has network blind spots.

by langmzz on 2008-11-12 22:46:44

Recently, the author visited the Hardware City of Tianjin and communicated with some hardware distributors, including large brand agents, manufacturers selling directly, and some small dealers who have just started. The most discussed topic was the impact of this global "financial tsunami" on the hardware industry. During the 1998 Asian economic crisis, they didn't even know what an "economic crisis" was; some people merely regarded it as a concept when they heard the term. But today, they have a personal experience of it, to the extent that even the cleaning staff can chat about it for a few sentences.

Their biggest concern is the lack of orders. The author personally experienced this as well because there were almost no customers throughout the afternoon, not even a phone call.

The author suggested they try to develop their market online. In times of economic downturn, doing business through the internet is the best way to reduce operational costs. For example, many purchasers cut costs by laying off employees, reducing field procurement personnel, and turning to online purchasing. Similarly, salespeople promote their products via the internet at much lower operational costs than going out to drum up business.

However, some bosses shook their heads, saying they had been doing online promotions all along but saw little effect. After detailed inquiries, the author learned that their so-called promotion was limited to free promotions on some B2B business websites or search engines like BAIDU. They rarely had their own professional websites, and if they did, these were usually simple display-type sites. Moreover, they didn't know how to optimize or promote from their own website.

The author calculated for them: the cost of renting a store for a year is 100,000 yuan, which translates to 270 yuan per day. However, building a professional website costs less than a week's rent for a storefront. The invisible daily expenses are being lost, yet many hesitate to invest in potential visible benefits. This cannot be denied as a blind spot for some small bosses in terms of marketing methods, marketing investment, especially in the application of the Internet.

Indeed, such situations exist. Although online marketing models started as early as 2000, after eight years of popularization, it seems many people understand the benefits of online marketing, especially businessmen. However, this is precisely where most people mistakenly think they do. A considerable number of entrepreneurs, due to their lack of understanding of online marketing technology, or influenced by the ineffectiveness of some free online promotions, have lost confidence in online marketing.

Therefore, just as Premier Wen Jiabao of the State Council mentioned multiple times after the outbreak of the global financial crisis, maintaining economic growth crucially depends on confidence. Similarly, for the hardware market in Tianjin to continue to flourish as before, at least most operators must have confidence in the platform of internet marketing.
