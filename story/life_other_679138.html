Cough remedy -- stop coughing and moisten the lungs in autumn and winter, teach you how to eat pears

Shortness of breath, sore throat, excessive phlegm... the sudden onset of coughing symptoms often catches people off guard. In fact, in addition to taking medicine for symptomatic treatment, a small pear can also have unexpected effects and help alleviate coughing symptoms.

From the perspective of traditional health preservation, the main characteristics of autumn and winter are "dryness" and "aridity", so the human body often exhibits symptoms such as dry skin, dry mouth and lips, and sore throat. Therefore, it is essential to eat less pungent foods like green onions, ginger, garlic, leeks, and peppers to prevent excessive lung energy. Eating fruits that quench thirst, moisten the throat, and relieve dryness will make one feel refreshed and comfortable instantly. When it comes to eating fruit in autumn and winter, pears are indispensable. Pears are rich in nutrients, containing protein, fat, sugar, as well as various vitamins and minerals. Their flesh is delicate, juicy, and sweet with a crisp and fragrant taste. Traditional medicine records state that pears are cool in nature, sweet in taste, and have effects such as moistening the lungs, eliminating phlegm, and stopping coughs.

How to choose a good pear?

There are many types of pears. The ones commonly sold on the market include Snow Pear, White Pear, Jingbai Pear, and Dangshan Pear. How do you distinguish between good and bad pears?

Good-quality pears - The fruits are fresh and plump, with regular shapes, varying in color from green, yellow, to moon white depending on the variety. They are moderately ripe (about 80% ripe), with fine texture, crisp and tender flesh, few stone cells, abundant juice, and a sweet or sweet-and-sour taste (depending on the variety). There should be no mold, freezing damage, pest damage, or mechanical injury. The high-quality pears of each variety are relatively uniform in size, with an appropriate size, and come with stems.

Poor-quality pears - Irregular shape with a significant number of deformed fruits, no stems, uneven fruit sizes with smaller fruits, rough and unclean surfaces, numerous scratches, bumps, and pressure marks, presence of disease spots or insect bites, tree abrasions, water rust, or dry scars covering one-third to one-half of the fruit surface. The flesh is coarse and of poor quality, with large and numerous stone cells, little juice, bland or overly sour taste, some may even have bitter or astringent flavors. Particularly poor-quality pears may emit a rotten odor.

Did you know? Pears also come in male and female varieties.

There are two types of pears: one is "male pear," which has coarse and hard flesh, less water content, and poorer sweetness. The other is "female pear," which has tender, sweet, and crispy flesh with more water. When buying pears, you can distinguish between male and female based on their appearance. Male pears are smaller at the top and larger at the bottom, resembling a high-footed steamed bun, with a secondary convex shape at the calyx end and no rust spots on the surface. Female pears are nearly isosceles triangular in shape, smaller at the top and larger at the bottom, with a deep concave pit at the calyx end that has rust spots. When choosing pears, first select the variety of pears, as different varieties have different tastes. Secondly, choose those with deeper concave pits at the calyx end, as they are better than those with shallower pits.

Ways to consume pears: What can you do when you have a cough?

Common food therapy recipes:

Stewed Snow Pear with Fritillary Bulb Honey

Stewed Snow Pear with Fritillary Bulb Honey is a common folk remedy for treating dry coughs caused by heat. It is especially effective for coughs caused by lung dryness. Use 10 grams of powdered fritillary bulb, 1000 grams of Snow Pear juice, and 500 grams of donkey-hide gelatin. First, remove the core from the Snow Pear, cut it into pieces, and place all the ingredients in a stew pot. Stew over low heat for 1 hour. After steaming until cooked, form it into Fritillary Pear Paste. Consume 10 grams each time, twice a day. Honey can be added before eating after the fritillary is stewed. Fritillary Pear Paste can nourish yin and moisten the lungs, treating prolonged coughs and blood in sputum. For stewing pears, aromatic and sweet varieties like Fragrant Pears and White Pears are preferable due to their finer texture, while Sand Pears are too coarse and are better eaten directly.

Snow Pear Paste

Snow Pear Paste made from Snow Pears and honey (or white sugar) is an excellent remedy for coughing and phlegm. Its sweet and cooling taste makes it especially suitable for children. There is a story about Pear Paste. It is said that Emperor Li Yan of the Tang Dynasty suffered from illness, experiencing dry mouth, facial dryness, heart heat, and irritability. Despite treatment by renowned doctors across the land, he saw no improvement. Later, Master Xing from Qingcheng Mountain passed by and was summoned for diagnosis. Master Xing prepared Snow Pear Paste according to the above recipe. After consuming it, Emperor Li Yan recovered completely, and thus Snow Pear Paste became famous along with Master Xing's reputation. Pear Paste has been popular ever since and continues to be passed down to this day.

Silver Ear and Fritillary Snow Pear Soup

Soak silver ear fungus for 20 minutes, use 5 grams of fritillary bulb, 1 Snow Pear, and 20 grams of rock sugar. Wash the silver ear fungus, remove impurities, and place the silver ear fungus, fritillary bulb, Snow Pear, and rock sugar in a small bowl. Steam or stew over water for half an hour. This soup has the effect of clearing heat, moistening the lungs, and stopping coughs and phlegm. It is suitable for patients with lung heat cough, yin deficiency hemoptysis, and dry cough without phlegm.

Snow Pear Duck Soup

Use two Snow Pears, peeled and cored, sliced, 100 grams of peeled and sliced water chestnuts, and 250 grams of duck meat blocks. Cook together once a week. This soup has the effects of clearing heat, nourishing yin, and benefiting the liver, suitable for chronic hepatitis patients with yin deficiency and internal heat.

This has the effect of moisturizing the throat and replenishing body fluids for those who suffer from excessive weather dryness, fiery constitution, laryngitis, dryness, and hoarse voices.

The most common - steamed pear with rock sugar: Steamed pear with rock sugar is a traditional Chinese food therapy supplement. It can nourish yin, moisten the lungs, stop coughs, and eliminate phlegm, providing good lubrication and protection for the throat.
