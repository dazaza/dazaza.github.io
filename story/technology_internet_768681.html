Pinterest Monetizes Like Twitter

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:54:39

On November 15, today, Pinterest has opened up some of its real-time data tools to developers of e-commerce websites. These tools will allow marketers to understand the popularity trends of their product Pins. Based on this data, partners can display popular Pins on their own websites. Currently, Pinterest's partners include Disney, Random House, Target, Nestle, Walmart, and Zappos, among others.

Pinterest spokesperson Malorie Lucich stated that one of the advantages of the partnership is providing a seamless user experience on the website. While browsing, you can pin images directly within the site without interrupting your shopping, reading, or browsing.

Pinterest will provide these tools for free to developers, and Steve Patrizi, Pinterest's head of partner marketing, said that this move sends a gesture of goodwill to users.

Building Partnerships

The image-sharing social network Pinterest is becoming the focus of everyone's attention. In recent months, Pinterest has raised substantial capital, recruited for high-level positions, and expanded internationally, yet it still lacks a proper revenue strategy. Pinterest's actions indicate that it aims not only to expand its user base but also to address the thorny issue of generating income.

In October, Pinterest raised $225 million in funding, valuing the company at $3.8 billion. In September, Pinterest also launched an experimental version of "Promoted Pins." By integrating sponsored Pins, Promoted Pins will eventually push ads into the regular content feed on users' homepages. Back in 2012, Pinterest attempted to generate revenue using affiliate marketing tool Skimlinks. Due to the lack of transparency in the affiliate marketing system, user responses were not favorable. Now, Pinterest plans to clearly label areas of the site that are monetized.

Pinterest’s current theme seems to be building partnerships. On November 6, Pinterest announced that Joanne Bradford, president of The San Francisco Chronicle, would serve as the head of the partnerships department. Bradford is responsible for connecting Pinterest with advertisers to establish "global business and content partnerships."

The San Francisco Chronicle stated that Bradford plans to work closely with parent company Hearst International Group to build partnerships between the group and Pinterest for content collaborations. Additionally, when the company begins selling advertisements, Bradford will lead Pinterest's sales team.

Following Twitter's Model

On the same day Bradford was appointed, Pinterest CEO Ben Silbermann expressed that going public is currently not Pinterest's goal. Pinterest has already entered the UK, France, Italy, and Japanese markets, with over 7 million global users, indicating a potentially huge advertising revenue. Similar to Twitter and Facebook, Pinterest is experimenting with revenue models and building a strong executive team.

In 2007, Facebook had approximately 70 million users and generated about $150 million in revenue. In 2008, Facebook's revenue grew to $280 million. In 2010, Twitter had around 70 million users and generated approximately $28 million in revenue. In 2011, Twitter's revenue reached $140 million. According to statistics from market research company Piqora, on average, each Pin generates 78 cents in sales. Once Pinterest starts running ads, its revenue is bound to grow rapidly.

Venture capitalist Jeff Jordan said that Pinterest is extremely appealing. The continuous rounds of funding, Promoted Pins, and hiring executives all indicate Pinterest's continued growth. "When you suddenly become the largest portal overnight, and you're a content-heavy website, expanding your business becomes an achievement." (Edward)
