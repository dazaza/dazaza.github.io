What are the dietary therapy methods for epilepsy?

1. Ketogenic Diet:

The ketogenic diet refers to a high-fat diet. After epileptic patients consume it, ketone bodies are produced in the human body, which is effective for some seizures or can reduce seizures. But because a high-fat diet is often difficult for people to tolerate and is refused. The diet considered effective is still under study.

2. Supplementing Vitamins and Certain Elements:

It has been observed that the food of epileptic patients lacks vitamin B6 and certain elements such as calcium and magnesium, making them prone to convulsive attacks. Therefore, some doctors routinely use vitamin B6 as an adjuvant treatment.

3. Prohibition of Alcohol:

Alcohol and alcoholic beverages may induce epileptic seizures in epileptic patients, so they should be advised not to drink or quit drinking. Although clinically not every patient's seizure is related to drinking, doctors or family members should still advise them not to drink alcohol. If it is found that drinking is significantly associated with seizures, then abstinence should be enforced to ensure safety. During family gatherings or holidays, tea can be used instead of alcohol.

4. Establishing a Scientific Dietary Method:

Epileptic patients should ensure adequate nutrition, and their diet should avoid overeating or excessive hunger. However, it should be noted that if the patient is overweight and some anti-epileptic drugs have the adverse reaction of increasing weight, such as sodium valproate and carbamazepine, then when taking measures to control weight or lose weight, further consultation with a doctor is necessary, and blind action should be avoided. The weight gain caused by drugs is often self-limiting, meaning that after reaching a certain level, it will no longer increase.
