Shenyang Red Children's address - involves a wide range of industries

by honghz8512 on 2009-12-01 23:13:31

"Open small stores in big places, and large stores in small places." In first-tier cities, due to the prosperity of various department stores, large supermarkets, and convenience stores, most maternal and infant products are sold through these channels. Therefore, specialized maternal and infant stores generally take the form of small shops, serving as a supplement to these channels. However, in second and third-tier cities, the situation is exactly the opposite: large specialized maternal and infant stores have stronger terminal control and scale.

Similar to the early maturity phase of every new industry, the maternal and infant industry is currently in a period of fierce competition with a chaotic industrial chain. Competitors are rushing to capture the market: multinational companies are entering the Chinese market with their advantageous brands, domestic brands also want a share of the pie, strong industry enterprises need to be cultivated, and the supply market urgently needs improvement. At the same time, the maternal and infant industry has a complex industrial chain involving many industries. Maternal and infant products involve clothing, plastics, light industry, electronics, medical devices, steel, paper products, and dozens of other industries, which are widespread and scattered.

In this situation, how should investors enter the market? How should existing merchants gain an advantage? How can second-line enterprises get a share from the huge supply chain of this industry?

Industry Analysis

To answer these questions, one needs a clear understanding of the current state of China's maternal and infant market.

Characteristics of Chinese Maternal and Infant Consumers:

At 0:02 on January 6, 2005, China's 1.3 billionth citizen was born at Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital. Since then, China's family planning policy has been in place for more than thirty years, and the pregnant population in our country has fully entered the generation of single children.

At the same time, data from the National Bureau of Statistics shows that in 2003, the number of new births was around 16 million, driving GDP growth by 4%. Currently, the number of urban infants aged 0-3 is over 10 million, and maternal and infant product consumers account for about 3% of the urban population. Market research indicates that newborns in medium-sized cities consume between 2,000 to 5,000 yuan annually (excluding food). Thus, the annual demand for babies amounts to billions of RMB.

A special era and unique national conditions have created enormous business opportunities for China's maternal and infant industry and have nurtured a group of special consumers.

1. Strong demand for maternal and infant knowledge but lack of experience

— Introducing culture and service into the market will be the biggest selling point of the maternal and infant industry in the future.

Due to China's late-childbearing policy, parents of newborns since the 1990s have been relatively older, with higher educational levels, leading to a greater thirst for child-rearing knowledge and enhanced health awareness. They highly value learning maternal and infant knowledge and pay great attention to various health care tips.

On the other hand, these "new parents" usually only have one child, lacking practical experience in pregnancy, but they feel that the traditional child-rearing concepts of their parents' generation are unscientific and do not fit the current situation. This creates a conflict between traditional child-rearing concepts and the modernization process. As a result, modern parents not only focus on material supply for infant health but also have a strong desire for maternal and infant culture and services.

Introducing culture and service into the market will be the biggest selling point of the maternal and infant industry in the future.

2. Price Insensitivity

— High Industry Profit Margins

Compared to other products, the consumer group of the maternal and infant industry is not very sensitive to product prices. A store owner of a maternal and infant specialty store said: "Parents nowadays mostly start having children after they have achieved career success and have sufficient economic strength. As long as the quality is reliable, parents generally tend to spend without hesitation."

Meanwhile, young expectant mothers possess modern ideas and economic power. They want to give birth to a healthy and active baby and also want to maintain their own healthy and beautiful image and vitality. Most of them continue working during pregnancy and thus have high requirements for maternal products.

This results in relatively higher profit margins for this industry compared to other retail products.

3. Extremely High Demand for Product Safety

— Importance of Branding

There is no doubt that this group of consumers considers the safety of products. Within their economic means, consumers always try to choose the brands they trust. According to a reader survey conducted by the magazine "Mommy & Baby," the top ten most consumed baby formulas within the first year of a baby’s life (in terms of market share) are: Mead Johnson, Nestle, Wyeth, Dumex, Yili, Wandashan, Abbott, Wei Quan, Morinaga, and Synutra.

4. Clearly Defined Consumer Group

— Easy to Conduct Database Marketing

Under China's national conditions, maternity hospitals and delivery rooms are undoubtedly specific venues that define this consumer group. Therefore, it is relatively easy to obtain detailed consumer information from these venues, and regional consumer data analysis is also relatively straightforward.

Current State of China's Maternal and Infant Market:

In summary, the entire domestic maternal and infant product market can be described as "three more and three less": many entrants, few dominant players; more businesses focusing on a single main operation, fewer comprehensive operators; more ambiguous business models, fewer clear development directions.

1. Cross-regional branded maternal and infant specialty stores have yet to emerge, and companies are still in the growth stage:

Although some local maternal and infant brands in certain prefecture-level cities perform quite well, these successes are on a small scale, and there are no cross-regional leading brands yet. Most domestic specialty stores are still fiercely competing in local markets in second and third-tier cities, and they are still in the learning and construction stages regarding services and supply systems.

2. Significant Regional Differences:

"Foreign brands target first-tier markets, while Chinese brands target second and third-tier markets." Research shows that in first-tier cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou, foreign brands dominate maternal and infant food and products. In second and third-tier cities, local brands lead the market, and in some cities, the leading maternal and infant product store has very strong control, accounting for almost half or even more of the local sales.

"Open small stores in big places, and large stores in small places." In first-tier cities, due to the prosperity of various department stores, large supermarkets, and convenience stores, most maternal and infant products are sold through these channels. Therefore, maternal and infant specialty stores generally take the form of small shops, serving as supplements to these channels. In contrast, in second and third-tier cities, large maternal and infant specialty stores have stronger terminal control and scale.

3. Lack of Systematic and Comprehensive Supply and Sales Organizations:

Maternal and infant products involve clothing, plastics, light industry, electronics, medical devices, steel, paper products, and dozens of other industries, which are widespread and scattered. Currently, most maternal and infant product stores are limited to goods such as daily necessities, clothing, and food, while items like household goods, special items for pregnant women, crafts, and gifts are not fully available. Moreover, due to the wide range of maternal and infant products, the procurement channels for stores are numerous and varied, resulting in a mix of brands and inconsistent quality. The maternal and infant product market lacks dedicated, systematic, and comprehensive supply and sales organizations.

4. Monotonous and Lagging Sales Methods:

Domestic maternal and infant product stores still use the traditional retail method of "you come, I sell" and "sell and finish." Monotonous and lagging sales methods cause inflexible terminals and product stagnation. Due to the particularity of the maternal and infant consumer group, this industry requires community services, experiential services, convenient marketing, and affectionate marketing. Only various service forms can inject fresh blood into this industry.

5. Unreasonable Existing Price System:

Currently, maternal and infant products present two extremes: on one hand, imported products have excessively high prices that deter ordinary consumers, with a single child stroller costing hundreds or even thousands of yuan, equivalent to the price of a mountain bike; on the other hand, low-cost products cannot meet the demands of middle-class consumers. Mid-range products are a gap in China. Consumers call for mid-range brands suitable for the Chinese market.

Development Trends in the Maternal and Infant Industry:

Sales Terminals:

1. Establishing commercial brands gradually based on regions and extending business operations, focusing on nurturing core businesses and strategic assets. In the coming years, many regional advantage-based infant and child industry terminals will form, completing the industrial diversion from large department store retailing. This diversion will create a situation where infant and child products compete on the same stage with large terminal malls in specialized areas.

2. Transition from a single trade format to a diversified service format. After-sales services will become the core competitiveness in specialized areas, but the advantages of product structure and shopping environment will keep large malls dominant for a considerable period.

3. Establishing one-stop baby shopping venues in large malls and specialty stores will become a trend. In the implementation process, specialty stores will likely lead ahead of malls and supermarkets.

Manufacturers:

1. Gradually establishing product brands with a focus on creating brand value as the development direction.

2. Product structures will gradually improve according to market demand and the need for companies to establish core competitiveness.

3. Greater emphasis will be placed on guiding consumption towards service consultation, post-sale value-added services, and pre-sale consumption guidance.

4. Competition for sales channel resources will intensify.

Consumer Demands:

1. Continuous increase in demand for safety.

2. Greater trust in authoritative brands.

3. Increased functional needs, especially reflected in health and intelligence-enhancing products.

4. Searching for well-known, reasonably priced mid-range brands.

Solution Approaches:

Under full recognition of the current market situation, companies can first develop their "unique weapons" and create their own characteristics and highlights during the industry's upward trend. This way, when the territory is formed, they can establish their own domain, paving the way for leadership in future commercial battles, and laying a solid foundation for either alliances or consolidations in the future.

Three Golden Keys to Unlocking Wealth:

"Considering the current development trend of China's maternal and infant industry, a leading maternal and infant brand in China will emerge within the next two to three years," said an industry insider. Objective and non-objective factors intertwined make it uncertain who will rise, but a leading brand must excel in the following aspects.

Product Segmentation:

The maternal and infant industry is a market that emphasizes segmentation. Those involved in this industry know that for similar products, the finer the segmentation, the more likely consumers within the product category will choose it because consumers perceive it as more targeted.

Take the common baby bottle, for example. Ignoring materials and capacities, just from functionality alone, there are roughly standard bottles, anti-slip angle bottles, temperature-sensing bottles, wide-mouth temperature-sensing bottles, feeding bottles, easy-grip bottles, random-suction bottles, straw bottles, double-layer insulated bottles, juice bottles, and many other varieties. Bottle shapes are designed according to babies' physiological needs at different stages, including round, O-shaped, handle-equipped, animal-shaped, and various forms. Additionally, related products include bottle nipple brushes, bottle tongs, bottle thermometers, bottle chains, and bottle insulating bags.

In the competition between domestic and imported milk powders, this principle is similarly proven. Originally, domestic milk powder was divided into infant milk powder, children's milk powder, and adult milk powder at most; however, the entry of imported milk powders such as newborn formula (0-6 months), growing-up milk powder (6 months to 3 years), and immunity-enhancing milk powder left Chinese consumers dazzled, quickly attracting Chinese consumers. Ultimately, domestic brands had to timely "imitate" to catch up and reclaim some lost ground.

Therefore, to firmly establish oneself in this industry, one must do a good job in product segmentation.

Service:

Johnson & Johnson, a company with a century-long history and ranked among the Fortune 500, offers baby care as one of its main products. How does it provide service?

Let's take a look at its website.

On the Johnson & Johnson website, the company acts like a doting, talkative old nanny, constantly reminding young parents to pay attention to their baby's sleep, diet, crying, temperature, how to bathe them... Young parents suddenly realize that in this hectic and confusing world, they indeed need continuous guidance from someone like this role. And as the child grows day by day, this old nanny will keep handing out "Johnson's Baby Shampoo," "Johnson's Safe Cotton," "Johnson's Diapers," "Johnson's Bibs," "Johnson's 2-in-1 Talcum Powder," "Johnson's Ve Moisturizer," and dozens of other "Johnson... Johnson... Johnson..." products.

When Johnson & Johnson provides services through its website, the data entered by customers also enters its website server. This is a massive asset that will play an immeasurable role in the company's operations, and it is also a reward for its serious service. Of course, the website promises confidentiality for any logged-in customer data, but this information is open internally within the company. It requires users to provide basic information about themselves and the baby and specify their relationship with the baby (mother, father, grandfather, grandmother...). For those willing to provide "baby skin type," "whether they suffer from diaper rash," "how they feed (breast milk, cow's milk, mixed, solid food)," they can receive specialized information services on skin care, treating diaper rash, and feeding. Of course, for inquiries and requests actively sent by customers through the "feedback" column, the website's online service will provide corresponding answers.

Moreover, Johnson & Johnson operates a virtual community called the "National Mothers Center Association." The "National Mothers Center" is a self-organized group of women spread across various states, aiming to "make participants no longer feel isolated and helpless, able to demonstrate their value as mothers, discuss couples' experiences in raising children, and jointly create a friendly environment suitable for children's growth." Now, Johnson & Johnson helps them go online and integrate into its own website, retaining the original exchange function while assisting them in solving problems from relevant scientific research dynamics and information.

In summary, we can see that famous international maternal and infant brands sell not only products but also follow the path of "product servitization and service productization." Service is a necessary condition for brands to advance rapidly into the fast lane.

Sales Terminals:

In the coming years, many regional advantage-based infant and child industry terminals will form. According to insiders, dozens of prefecture-level cities already have maternal and infant specialty stores with annual turnover reaching 20 to 30 million yuan, with the best ones achieving 4 million yuan in annual turnover in a single county. These specialty stores will complete the industrial diversion from large department store retailing, forming a situation where infant and child products compete on the same stage with large terminal malls in specialized areas. Under China's national conditions, controlling these sales terminals holds the most promise and capability to build a maternal and infant industry empire.
