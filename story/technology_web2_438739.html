Do you want to order anything else?

by kfjro099 on 2011-05-29 09:01:31

Jinny asked such a question: I have a friend who eats regularly in the morning and at noon, but only eats apples in the evening. When she first started doing this, I told her that it was not scientific. However, after two years of persistence, she has lost quite a bit of weight and remains very healthy. Is it really okay to skip dinner and only eat fruit?

Indeed, many women when they think of losing weight, immediately consider skipping dinner and eating fruit instead. This method of weight loss is well-reasoned, highly feasible, and can be considered a relatively reasonable long-term weight loss strategy.

Reason 1: According to the habits of Chinese residents, dinner is generally the largest meal of the day, accounting for up to 40% of daily energy intake or even more. If dinner is skipped and replaced with fruit, it usually reduces energy intake by 25-30%, which gradually leads to weight loss.

Reason 2: Because dinner dishes are often rich, the intake of fats and salt is usually the highest throughout the day. By skipping dinner and replacing it with fruit, it helps cultivate a taste for lighter flavors, significantly reducing fat and salt intake. Not only does less fat make it easier to lose weight, but less salt also reduces water retention, helping to achieve a slimmer body.

Reason 3: The body’s mechanism is that metabolism is high in the morning and slows down in the evening. Eating light food at night helps reduce the burden on digestion and absorption, allowing the body to focus on nurturing tissues and organs, thereby promoting health. Proverbs like "bedtime radishes, morning ginger" or "not eating after noon" follow similar principles.

Reason 4: Many women enjoy eating fruit, so if they can eat a plate of fruit in the evening, even without eating regular meals, most women do not feel particularly regretful. Since breakfast and lunch can be eaten normally, they have the opportunity to enjoy other delicious foods during the day, and thus do not particularly crave dinner. Therefore, this method of eating fruit for dinner is easy to implement and feasible.

However, there are some things to note about this method, as it may not work for everyone. The key points are as follows:

Point 1: Eating fruit for dinner doesn't necessarily mean just eating one apple. Fruits with mild sweetness and low energy content, such as kiwis, pitaya, strawberries, and citrus fruits, can be consumed in slightly larger quantities, increasing satiety while providing more nutrients.

Point 2: This method is not a quick weight loss solution; you won’t lose five pounds in three days. It will take a month to lose a few pounds. It's better to maintain a mindset of "just focus on the process, not the result," staying positive and not weighing yourself every day. Weigh yourself only once a month on the same day, and you will surely see pleasant results.

Point 3: Using this method should not reduce your daily physical activity. Some exercise in the evening is recommended, but you must rest early and avoid staying up late, which might lead to uncontrollable snacking. If you feel hungry at night, choose sugar-free herbal teas, milk, or soy milk rather than cookies, pastries, or other snacks.

Point 4: Since the only food for dinner is fruit, and fruit cannot provide enough protein, B vitamins, or vitamins A, D, E, and K, it's important to ensure adequate intake of protein-rich foods at breakfast and lunch to improve nutritional quality. Breakfast and lunch should ideally avoid sweets, desserts, and fried foods, and include protein-rich items. Whole grains and legumes are preferred for carbohydrates to increase B vitamins and vitamin E. If necessary, a multivitamin containing B vitamins can be added.

Point 5: Chinese residents generally lack sufficient calcium intake, and dieting makes it even easier to become deficient. Calcium deficiency may only become apparent after many years, so women on diets must pay special attention to their calcium intake. It is recommended to consume a glass of milk or yogurt daily, along with a serving of tofu and leafy greens. This ensures an adequate supply of calcium. If you feel hungry at night, besides fruit, a small cup of yogurt can be added.

In fact, we don’t need to eat so much or so heavily. Skipping dinner and avoiding social meals not only aids in weight loss but also reduces the intake of contaminants, making the body feel lighter. As long as the above points are followed, having fruit for dinner can ensure balanced nutrition and can be maintained long-term, achieving both beauty and health—a cause for happiness!
