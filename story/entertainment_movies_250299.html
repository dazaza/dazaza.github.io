Synopsis of "Canghai":

In September 1949, after the Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin campaigns achieved victory, the remaining Nationalist forces retreated to Jinma Island. A part of the Third Field Army advanced south of the Yangtze River. Captain Wang Benli of the Red Sixth Regiment's Third Battalion and Political Commissar Li Shanke received orders to prepare for an amphibious landing operation. However, they suffered a crushing defeat. Li Shanke was killed in action, and Wang Benli renamed himself "Wang Shanke" in his memory.

The failure of the "Jinmen Sea Battle" made the high-level leadership of the new China determined to strengthen the Chinese Navy. After the victory of the Korean War, Wang Shanke returned to the Red Sea and joined the Navy, later marrying Dr. Xu Jinyun.

Wang Shanke devoted himself to studying naval warfare tactics. By chance, he won a brilliant naval battle and became famous. However, double Ph.D. Ma Jiancheng looked down on him with disdain. During the construction of the Very Low Frequency (VLF) station, Soviet aid was withdrawn halfway through the project. Wang Shanke strongly recommended Ma Jiancheng as the chief engineer and unexpectedly learned that Ma was actually his wife's first love.

Repeated failures in the VLF station experiments led Ma Jiancheng to desert. Later, Wang Shanke and Political Commissar Lu Xinquan overcame opposition and convinced Ma Jiancheng to return. Taking a life-threatening risk, Ma Jiancheng finally succeeded in developing the VLF station.

Lu Xinquan accidentally learned that Li Shanke had survived back then. Li requested Lu to keep it a secret and hoped that his son Li Qiang would serve in the Navy in the future.

More than ten years later, Wang Shanke's son Wang Haijun wanted to go to university, but Wang Shanke insisted that his son join the army. Unable to reach an agreement, Wang Shanke angrily sent his son to serve as a cook on a supply ship. Ma Jiancheng finally agreed to take up the position of director of the 509 Research Institute, but on the condition of sending Wang Haijun to take the college entrance examination, which Wang Shanke refused.

Ma Jiancheng's report on the "information platform construction" conflicted with the issue of "solving employee housing problems." The arrogant Ma Jiancheng left in anger and went to the supply ship, coincidentally meeting Wang Haijun and encouraging him to have lofty ideals.

Wang Haijun wanted to leave the military, leading to a fierce conflict between father and son. Feeling lonely, Wang Shanke recalled Li Shanke and couldn't help shedding tears. Seeing his father cry for the first time, Wang Haijun decided to stay. However, during a military exercise, he used an underhanded tactic and was punished with 40 days of confinement.

Unexpectedly, the confinement changed Wang Haijun's life as his academic performance improved dramatically. The usually proud Ma Jiancheng finally put aside his pride and sought out Political Commissar Lu to secure 30 million yuan in funding.

Li Qiang was admitted to the Naval Academy but was replaced by Lu Xiangdong, the son of Lu Xinquan. Lu personally apologized at home, and Li Shanke passed away with a smile. Wang Haijun, Li Qiang, and Lu Xiangdong became the famous Three Musketeers of the Naval Academy.

Despite his father's opposition, Lu Xiangdong went into business. After graduation, Wang Haijun and Li Qiang were assigned to the No. 159 warship. Later, Wang Haijun furthered his studies at the Guangzhou Naval Academy and, after graduation, served as the deputy commander of the most arduous South China Sea minesweeper, marrying Ma Yujie, the daughter of Ma Jiancheng.

By this time, Ma Jiancheng was in the late stages of lung cancer. The simulation of the information platform entered a critical stage, and a strong sense of mission extended Ma Jiancheng's life miracle.

In 1990, Wang Haijun and Li Qiang served as captain and political commissar respectively, conducting trial voyages for the domestically produced and modified No. 159 warship to practically test its various performances. The trial voyage was a great success, and Ma Jiancheng passed away peacefully on duty upon hearing the good news.

Lu Xiangdong invested in the Red Sea, helping Li Meihua justly, which caught the attention of the police. After understanding the entire situation, the estrangement between Lu Xinquan and his son finally disappeared.

In 1995, Wang Shanke retired after a lifetime of service.

In the new century, maritime defense became even more important. Wang Haijun and Li Qiang participated in the largest-scale deterrent military exercise in the history of the Navy, "Changjian No. 1." A fire broke out in the boiler room, and Li Qiang was seriously injured while saving his comrades. It was only then that Wang Shanke learned about Li Qiang's background.

As an expert in China's maritime rights and interests, Ma Yujie was invited to give a speech at the Boao Forum for Asia, receiving enthusiastic praise. Wang Haijun excelled both in theory and practice, earning commendations from the Central Military Commission and the Navy Headquarters. In 2007, he was ordered to lead a fleet in a multinational joint military exercise, winning respect from all participating countries with his perfect performance, successfully completing the task of showcasing the strength of our Navy and national image.
