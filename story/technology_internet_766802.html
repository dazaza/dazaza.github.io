Surface 2 Launch Summary: Can the two new models bring more users?

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:49:54

Microsoft's idea of combining work and entertainment on the Surface did not resonate well with the public, as neither the Surface RT nor the Surface Pro sold well. The Q4 financial report released in July showed that Microsoft had spent a staggering $900 million on the Surface RT, sparking a collective backlash from the media against Microsoft and the Surface. In response to media criticism, newly appointed Microsoft CTO Amy Hood promised improvements—especially in the mobile device sector.

This Surface 2 launch event in New York was an effort to fulfill Amy Hood’s promise. As last year, Microsoft unveiled two products: the Surface 2 running Windows RT and the Surface Pro 2 running full Windows.

 

Surface 2

Operating System: Windows RT 8.1 (with Office 2013 RT)

Dimensions: 27.46 x 17.25 x 0.89 cm

Weight: 0.68 kg

Case: VaporMG shell, magnesium alloy (silver)

Buttons: Volume and power buttons

Storage: 32GB and 64GB

Display: 10.6-inch ClearType Full HD display (1920x1080 pixels, 16:9 widescreen, 5-point touch)

CPU & Memory: NVIDIA Tegra 4 (T40) 1.7 GHz quad-core, 2GB RAM

Wireless: Wi-Fi (802.11a/b/g/n), Bluetooth 4.0 low-energy technology

Battery Life: Up to 10 hours, standby up to 7-15 days

Cameras & A/V: 3.5 MP front-facing camera, 5 MP rear-facing camera, 1080p video recording, stereo speakers, dual microphones with noise cancellation

Ports: Full-size USB 3.0, microSDXC card slot, headphone jack, HD video output port, keyboard cover port

Sensors: Ambient light sensor, accelerometer, gyroscope, compass

Power Adapter: 24W

Surface Pro 2

Operating System: Windows 8.1 Pro

Dimensions: 27.46 x 17.30 x 1.35 cm

Weight: 0.9 kg

Case: VaporMG shell, dark titanium steel color

Buttons: Volume and power buttons

Storage: 64GB, 128GB, 256GB, 512GB

Display: 10.6-inch ClearType Full HD display (1920x1080 pixels, 16:9 widescreen, 10-point touch, 1024-level pressure sensitivity for Surface Pen)

CPU & Memory: Intel Core i5-4200U processor (1.6 GHz, Turbo Boost up to 2.6 GHz), Intel HD Graphics 4400, 4GB or 8GB RAM (LPDDR3 dual-channel), TPM 1.2

Wireless: Wi-Fi (802.11a/b/g/n), Bluetooth 4.0 low-energy technology

Battery Life: 42 watt-hours

Cameras & A/V: 720p HD front and rear cameras, microphone, stereo speakers

Ports: Full-size USB 3.0, microSDXC card slot, headphone jack, Mini DisplayPort, keyboard cover port

Sensors: Ambient light sensor, accelerometer, gyroscope, compass

Power Adapter: 48W (including 5W USB for accessory charging)

Surface 2:

NVIDIA Tegra 4 processor

10-inch 1080p display

Windows RT 8.1 operating system

32GB/64GB internal storage

Included RT version of Office 2013 with Outlook app

25% longer battery life

Rear kickstand with two adjustable angles

The black Surface RT has been discontinued; this time, only a silver version of the Surface 2 is available. The new color makes the device appear more vibrant. Starting with the Surface 2, Microsoft is also increasing brand awareness, with the large Surface logo on the kickstand being evidence—"Let them know you're using a Surface."

Surface Pro 2:

Intel Haswell processor

RAM: 4GB/8GB

Internal storage: 64GB/128GB/256GB/512GB

Windows 8.1 Pro operating system

10-inch ClearType HD display with 46% better color accuracy

Rear kickstand with two adjustable angles

Microsoft made many claims about the Surface Pro 2: "Faster than 95% of laptops," "50% GPU performance improvement," "20% overall performance improvement." Playing 6K video recorded by a camera smoothly on the Surface Pro 2 feels impressive.

However, as a productivity tool, the battery life of the Surface Pro 2 deserves attention. Microsoft says the battery life has improved by 75%, lasting all day. We are eager to see how it performs in real-world usage.

New Surface Accessories:

Touch Cover 2:

The second-generation touch keyboard cover is thinner and lighter, measuring only 2.75 mm thick. It features a new backlit keyboard. It is sturdier and records input more accurately, with upgraded sensors that better recognize user input across the entire keyboard. Price: 908 RMB / $119.99.

Type Cover 2:

The second-generation professional keyboard cover is ultra-thin and quiet like the current one but features a new backlit keyboard. Available in red, blue, purple, and black. Price: 988 RMB / $129.99.

Power Cover:

The rechargeable keyboard cover provides the same excellent typing experience as the Type Cover while extending the battery life of Surface Pro, Surface 2, and Surface Pro 2 by up to 50%. Expected to be available in early 2014 at $199.99.

Keyboard Cover Wireless Adapter:

Converts the Surface Touch Cover and Type Cover into a Bluetooth keyboard for connection to compatible devices. Price: $59.99.

Surface Docking Station:

Includes 3 x USB 2.0 ports, 1 x USB 3.0 port, Mini DisplayPort, Ethernet port, audio input/output, and power connector. Price: 1488 RMB / $199.99, expected in early 2014.

Surface Car Charger:

Directly charges the Surface through most car power or ignition ports without additional adapters. It also has a USB port for simultaneously charging a phone or other devices. Price: 388 RMB / $49.99, expected in early 2014.

Arc Touch Mouse for Surface:

An upgraded Arc Touch mouse connects via Bluetooth 3.0. Price: 538 RMB / $69.99, coming in the next few months.

Availability and Pricing:

The Surface 2 and Surface Pro 2 will be launched on October 22nd in 22 initial countries and markets:

Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Hong Kong SAR, Ireland, Italy, Luxembourg, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, UK, and USA will start shipping. China will begin supplying in early November. Microsoft will announce supply dates for other markets in the coming months.
