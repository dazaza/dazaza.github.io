Authoritative report measures who is the fastest player in the world today: Ronaldo ranks first surpassing Robben

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-12 18:40:10

Authoritative report measures who is the fastest player in the world - Ronaldo surpasses Robben to take first place. According to a scientific report by Germany's 'Der Spiegel', Ronaldo's speed during high-speed sprints reaches an astonishing 33.6 kilometers per hour, making him without doubt the fastest player in football today.

The decisive factors for an athlete's speed mainly depend on their muscle type. Generally speaking, sprinters have more white muscle fibers, which provide greater explosive power and better short-distance acceleration ability, while long-distance runners have more red muscle fibers. The report by 'Der Spiegel' points out that Ronaldo's muscle type is naturally white fiber dominant, which is the fundamental reason for his outstanding speed. Although Ronaldo's sprinting speed is still far from the human limit of 44.72 kilometers per hour set by 100-meter world champion Usain Bolt, Bolt also pointed out: "If Ronaldo pays attention to his center of gravity while running, he can definitely run faster."

'Der Spiegel' tested the players' sprinting speed without the ball, not with it. Interestingly, Ronaldo's dribbling speed is even slightly faster than his plain running speed. Previously, Nike conducted a speed test experiment on its players, and the results showed that Ronaldo's dribbling speed was 0.1 kilometers per hour faster than his plain running speed! Regarding this point, Manchester United manager Ferguson also expressed surprise: "Ronaldo's dribbling speed is no slower than his plain running speed, which is incredible."

In this research report, the player with the second-fastest speed after Ronaldo is Arjen Robben, the Dutch 'Flying Wing' of Bayern Munich, whose speed also reached 32.9 kilometers per hour. Following him are Theo Walcott of Arsenal (32.7 km/h), Wayne Rooney of Manchester United (32.6 km/h), and Robin van Persie of Arsenal (32.1 km/h).

However, there is controversy among academia and media about who is the fastest, as this is related to the player's physical condition, field conditions, and even mental state. For example, at the 2008 European Cup, according to the official data released by the tournament, the fastest player should be Van Persie of the Netherlands, whose maximum sprinting speed in that tournament reached 31.5 kilometers per hour, while Ronaldo only reached 29.4 kilometers per hour. OPTA's professional data ranking is different again, with Ronaldo still ranked first, but his speed was only 20.9 kilometers per hour, followed by Fulham forward Andrew Johnson (20.6 km/h) and Liverpool's Torres (20.5 km/h).

Interestingly, regardless of the ranking or testing method used, Barcelona forward Messi's name does not appear in the top five fastest players. This is understandable because Messi's speed is more reflected in his step frequency and quick turns, stops, and directional changes while dribbling. Therefore, when it comes to absolute speed, he is surpassed by Ronaldo, who has better physical fitness.
