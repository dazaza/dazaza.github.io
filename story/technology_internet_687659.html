Many people in daily life suffer from the trouble of hemorrhoids. So, what are the common causes that induce hemorrhoids? In response to this question, experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Anorectal Center will answer them one by one for everyone.

What are the common causes that induce hemorrhoids?

1. Abnormal embryonic development: Abnormal development during the developmental process can lead to many congenital anorectal diseases occurring in the anal and cecal regions, such as congenital anal atresia, congenital cecovaginal fistula, congenital megacolon, etc.

2. Dietary reasons: The refinement or coarseness of food quality, changes in vegetable types and their increase or decrease in quantity, the amount of protein, fat, starch, fiber content, and water intake can all directly affect stool composition, leading to anorectal fissure diseases.

3. Abnormal bowel movements: Both diarrhea and constipation are important causes of hemorrhoids. Constipation is the biggest culprit; long-term retention of toxic substances in the cecum can not only trigger cecal lesions but also cause fecal accumulation, affecting blood circulation.

4. Chronic diseases: Long-term malnutrition, physical weakness, leading to relaxation and weakness of the anal sphincter muscle. Long-standing chronic bronchitis, emphysema, due to coughing and wheezing causing increased abdominal pressure, pelvic congestion. Chronic hepatitis, liver cirrhosis, diarrhea, colitis, etc., are all inducements for anorectal diseases.

5. Anatomical reasons: There are many venous plexuses and anastomotic branches in the venous system of the anus and the cavity vein system at the lower end of the cecum. The venous walls are weak, making them more susceptible to pressure. The loose submucosal tissue of the cecum facilitates the dilation and varicosity deformation of veins, easily forming hemorrhoids.

6. Physiological reasons: The colon and cecum are the main organs for transporting food residue and retaining feces. After food is decomposed and absorbed within the body, the residues often carry a large amount of harmful substances. Long-term retention in the colon and cecum can lead to malignant transformation.

Experts from the Nanjing Armed Police Hospital's Anorectal Center remind us that although hemorrhoids are not serious illnesses, it is unwise to neglect treatment indefinitely. Hemorrhoids that remain untreated for a long time or are left untreated for extended periods can easily lead to a series of complications, and in some cases, even trigger cancer. Therefore, if you have hemorrhoids, you should seek timely treatment, choose the right hospital, and neither ignore nor improperly treat the condition to avoid further bodily harm.
