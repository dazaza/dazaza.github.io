Summary of the Beijing 2011 International Smart Grid Forum

Introduction: Energy is an important foundation and guarantee for economic and social development. Currently, countries around the world are vigorously promoting innovative developments in the energy sector, with an ongoing energy revolution characterized by the utilization of clean energy and the development of smart grids. From September 28 to 29, China State Grid Corporation and the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) jointly hosted the "2011 International Smart Grid Forum" in Beijing, with the theme being "The Robust Smart Grid: The Driving Force for 21st Century Energy Development."

From September 28 to 29, China State Grid Corporation and IEEE jointly organized the "2011 International Smart Grid Forum" in Beijing, themed "The Robust Smart Grid: The Driving Force for 21st Century Energy Development." Representatives from international organizations such as the International Electrotechnical Commission and the International Telecommunication Union, along with government departments, industry organizations, power companies, research institutions, design units, manufacturing enterprises, and universities from 16 countries across Asia, Europe, North and South America, totaling over 400 people, attended this forum.

Energy is a crucial foundation and guarantee for economic and social development. Currently, countries worldwide are actively pushing for innovative developments in the energy sector, with an emerging energy revolution characterized by the use of clean energy and the development of smart grids. A robust smart grid represents the evolution of the smart grid concept, where the high integration of "robustness" and "intelligence" becomes an inherent requirement and inevitable trend for the modern grid's development.

The forum believed that developing smart grids can improve energy structures, optimize resource allocation, and enhance the efficiency of energy production and utilization. It will play a significant role in ensuring secure energy supply, addressing global climate change, improving the quality of life for citizens, and fostering strategic emerging industries. The representatives provided comprehensive introductions to the latest progress made in various countries regarding smart grids, engaged in extensive exchanges and discussions on issues related to all aspects of smart grids, and proposed opinions and suggestions for strengthening cooperation in areas such as smart grid standard formulation. This forum has milestone significance in advancing the global development of smart grids.

IEEE, with over 400,000 members worldwide, includes 45 societies and professional committees. Its interdisciplinary experts have played a role in the development of smart grids. IEEE utilizes its solid foundation and extensive collaboration to develop smart grid standards, share best practices, and publish achievements in energy conversion. IEEE also promotes the development of smart grids through relevant educational products and services. The forum fully acknowledged the reference value of its experience for countries around the world.

The forum highly affirmed and evaluated all the work and achievements of China State Grid Corporation in the field of smart grids. With the support of the Chinese government, China State Grid Corporation has been strongly promoting and actively constructing a robust smart grid, characterized by informatization, automation, and interactivity, using ultra-high voltage grids as the backbone framework, coordinating the development of grids at all levels. It has carried out effective work in theoretical research, standard formulation, key equipment development, pilot project construction, and experimental testing capability construction, achieving breakthroughs in core technologies in multiple fields and accumulating rich experience, making important contributions to advancing the global development of smart grids.

The forum advocates that peers from all countries work together, adhering to the principles of mutual benefit, win-win cooperation, and friendly openness, strengthen exchanges and cooperation, share experiences and results, and jointly promote the innovative development of the global smart grid.
