Hardly use mobile phones and computers: The owner of the NBA Nets becomes the first anti-tech person

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:37:28

In today's world of high-speed information technology development, it is hard to imagine what our lives would be like without computers and smartphones. However, there exists a group of extremely successful individuals in the world who exclude high-tech products such as computers and smartphones from their lives. Recently, The Huffington Post selected the top 12 "anti-technology success figures" globally, with Mikhail Prokhorov, the Russian billionaire owner of the Brooklyn Nets, ranking at the top of this list.

Three years ago, The New York Post had reported that Nets owner Mikhail Prokhorov not only did not have his own mobile phone but also hardly ever used a computer. He primarily obtained information by reading newspapers and preferred to convey his thoughts through handwritten letters rather than emails.

Previously, a team reporter for the Nets revealed that Prokhorov would not selectively use any particular product; he treated all brands of technological products equally. Even the well-known iPad left him uninterested. He said, "I've heard of the iPad, but I hope I never have to use it." Nevertheless, this did not stop other executives of the Nets from using Apple products for work, and every new player joining the Nets received an iPad containing the team's tactical plays and customized training programs.

Regarding why he was averse to technological products, Prokhorov explained during an interview: "I don't use computers because the information on them is too overwhelming, and it's almost impossible to filter it all out."

Of course, as a globally renowned individual, it is impossible to completely isolate oneself from technology. Previously, someone had taken photos of Prokhorov using a mobile phone, and he does have his own blog, Twitter, and Facebook personal page. As a candidate for the Russian presidency, maintaining some level of contact with the outside world is necessary.

Among the 12 people selected by The Huffington Post, besides Prokhorov, there are many other famous figures, such as the "Oracle of Omaha" Warren Buffett, renowned fashion designer Karl Lagerfeld, Mexican telecommunications tycoon Carlos Slim Helú, and others.
