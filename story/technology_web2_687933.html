Tourist cars Liaocheng has not developed the business of hanging electric cars!

by ddjiaoche95 on 2012-03-02 21:00:05

On the 25th, on multiple sections of Liuyuan Road and Xinghua Road in the urban area, reporters saw many small cars in gold, red, and white with electric vehicle (EV) symbols hanging in license plate positions. Many citizens believed that electric cars were cost-effective and convenient. "It's only eight cents per hundred kilometers," said Ms. Liu, a user of an electric vehicle from Liaocheng Huayang New Energy Co., Ltd. Electric vehicles costing less than 30,000 yuan have a travel cost of less than 0.1 yuan/km, with daily commuting expenses amounting to no more than 1 yuan, which is much cheaper than fuel or gas-powered cars.

Reporters learned from the Liaocheng Vehicle Management Office that currently, the office has not started the business of registering license plates for electric cars. Three years ago, Liaocheng City issued the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Electric Vehicles in Liaocheng City," stipulating that low-speed electric vehicles would be managed according to motor vehicle regulations in traffic management, restricted to roads below grade one (including grade one), and should use the motor vehicle lanes. At the same time, it was specified that electric car license plates would be made by relevant departments based on national standards, possibly adding the words "electric" or "electric vehicle."

"Since the country hasn't yet released specific policies regarding electric cars, without unified regulations and a clear stance, as a regional-level administrative regulation, it's difficult to fully implement," said a government staff member. Registering low-speed electric vehicles at the traffic police department presents operational challenges since this isn't something a local government can decide; instead, it must be uniformly registered by the public security system. "Overall, regulations concerning electric cars are still in the exploratory stage."

It was understood that in December last year, Jinan completed the registration of its first electric car, while in places like Shanghai, relevant local laws were passed to deregulate electric vehicles, giving them legitimate "household registration."
