Liu Yishou: A son closed his factory to take care of his paralyzed mother and stayed by her side for 15 years.

by anonymous on 2011-05-19 15:07:24

Li Qingping feeds water to his mother. Liu Yishou.

Every half hour he helps the elder turn over, every hour he changes the elder's mattress pad; he gives the elder a massage every half day and bathes the elder once a day; if the elder has difficulty defecating, Liu Yishou uses a self-made round-headed wooden stick to help, or even uses his hands. This is the daily work of Li Qingping, a 63-year-old farmer from Cheng'an County, taking care of his paralyzed mother. To take good care of his mother, he even shut down his paper mill and quit smoking and drinking. Liu Yishou.

Unbeknownst to others, Li Qingping has been by his mother’s side for 15 years. Under his meticulous care, the elder not only never developed bedsores, but there was also no unpleasant smell in the room. Recently, reporters visited Dongbaozhuang Village, Shangcheng Town, Cheng'an County, and had a deep conversation with the filial son, Li Qingping. Liu Yishou.

Fateful twists and turns

A kind mother became a widow at a young age and became paralyzed in old age. Liu Yishou.

On the morning of May 1, while people were celebrating the holiday in various ways, Li Qingping was still looking after his paralyzed mother as usual. He boldly adjusted the elder's head with both hands, then fed her water spoonful by spoonful, and constantly wiped the saliva from the elder's mouth with a towel. Liu Yishou.

Li Qingping is 64 years old this year and is a farmer from Dongbaozhuang Village, Cheng'an County, Handan City. In his mind, his mother is an extremely virtuous and kind woman, but she has suffered many misfortunes in life and has hardly enjoyed a day of happiness. Liu Yishou.

When he was 10 years old, his father passed away from liver disease, leaving his mother, who was only 29 years old, to shoulder the responsibility of raising two children alone. To support the family, his mother worked hard in the fields during the day and spun yarn and wove cloth at night. Because they couldn't afford lamp oil, she would light a stick of incense and use its faint glow to spin yarn and weave cloth. In the 1960s and 70s, the family didn't have enough food to eat, and could only occasionally enjoy noodles. But each time, his mother would give all the noodles to Li Qingping and his sister, while she herself cooked some wild vegetables with noodle soup. Liu Yishou.

Children of poor families mature early. Influenced by his mother, Li Qingping grew up to be not only hardworking but also enterprising, quickly becoming one of the strongest men in the village. In 1994, he took out a loan to buy the village-run paper factory, making tens of thousands of yuan a year. The tough days were finally over, and the family was full of joy, living a prosperous life. Liu Yishou.

Li Qingping thought that happiness would continue forever, and that his mother, who had worked so hard for most of her life, would be able to enjoy a happy old age. However, on February 29, 1996, Li Qingping's mother suddenly collapsed due to cerebral hemorrhage and lost consciousness. After the hospital's full efforts to save her, the elder's life was saved, but she was left paralyzed on one side and unable to take care of herself. That year, Li Qingping was 48 years old, and his mother was 68 years old. Liu Yishou.

Filial piety uncommon

The son closes the factory and stays by the bedside. Liu Yishou.

With his mother paralyzed in bed and the paper factory needing management, should he return home to fulfill his filial duty or continue running the factory? Li Qingping was torn inside.

My mother's life has been too difficult. She raised us all by herself, enduring so much hardship. Worried about our suffering, she refused to remarry all her life, and only we, as her children, understand the bitterness. Now that my mother is paralyzed and needs care, I absolutely cannot let her suffer even a little. Liu Yishou. Thus, Li Qingping decided to close the factory and stay at home to serve the elder wholeheartedly. His thoughts at the time were very simple: his mother's health was more important than money. Anyway, he had experience, and if his mother recovered, he could start a new business later. Liu Yishou.

To better take care of his mother, after discussing with his wife, Li Qingping added a single bed in front of their double bed and moved the elder into their room. From then on, they began a life of three people eating and living together. Every morning, Li Qingping would get up early to cook, then wash, comb, and feed water and food to the elder. Liu Yishou.

Worried that the elder's limbs might become atrophied due to lack of movement, Li Qingping helped the elder turn over every half hour; because the elder lost the ability to speak and had incontinence, he needed to check the elder's bedding frequently, changing the mattress pad on average every hour; because the elder often experienced difficulty defecating, he used a self-made round-headed wooden stick to assist, or even used his hands. Liu Yishou. Additionally, Li Qingping gave the elder two massages and bathed her once a day. Someone suggested Li Qingping insert a urinary catheter for his mother to avoid having to change the mattress pad constantly, but he was worried it would cause her pain and refused, preferring to go without a good night's sleep for years. Liu Yishou.

As the seasons changed and flowers bloomed and faded, before they knew it, Li Qingping had been by his mother's side for 15 years. Under his meticulous care, the elder was always clean and tidy, not only never developing bedsores, but also without any unpleasant smell in the room. Liu Yishou.

Li Qingping's daughter said that for 15 years, her father abandoned a promising business to take care of his grandmother, quit smoking and drinking, and even stopped visiting relatives and neighbors during the New Year. He no longer cared about external affairs, almost leading a life cut off from the world. Liu Yishou.

Son's Heartfelt Words

Only I truly understand what my mother needs. Liu Yishou.

In most families, women are more detail-oriented and gentle than men and are better at household chores, so the heavy burden of caring for the elderly and sick usually falls on women. But why does Li Qingping insist on personally taking care of the elder instead of hiring a nanny or letting his sister or children do it? Liu Yishou.

Actually, I also hope someone can take care of my mother for me, but I just can't trust anyone else. The elder worked hard all her life, and now she is paralyzed in bed, which is heartbreaking. Whenever he talks about childhood memories, his eyes begin to redden: I don't want the elder to suffer anymore, only I truly understand what my mother needs! Liu Yishou.

Li Qingping believes that taking care of a high-age paralyzed patient who cannot express themselves through language is not as simple as cooking or sweeping. How much water to feed each time, when to start feeding, which parts of the body to massage daily, how much force to apply, etc., are things that others find hard to fully understand. Liu Yishou.

According to a villager, Li Qingping and his mother seem to have a telepathic connection. As soon as the elder hums, he knows what she wants. If someone else were to take care of her, the elder would refuse to eat or drink by turning her head. Liu Yishou.

Because of this, Li Qingping doesn't trust even his own wife and sister, and despite how hard and tiring it is every day, he insists on being personally by his mother's side. Liu Yishou.

There is a saying: "Long-term illness leaves no filial children." But Li Qingping resolutely stopped his paper mill to take care of his paralyzed mother and no longer sought any business opportunities. Does he really have no regrets in his heart? The Li family used to be one of the richest households in the village, but now they rely on their children farming to make a living. Li Qingping has even had to quit smoking and drinking. Doesn't he feel bitter? Liu Yishou.

Regarding this, Li Qingping thought for a long time: Originally, I was a man with great ambitions, even dreaming of becoming a famous entrepreneur. But I never expected my old mother to suddenly become paralyzed, and even less expected her to lie in bed for 15 years. Perhaps this is fate's arrangement. Maybe because the mother hadn't had a moment of rest before, heaven thought she was too tired and deliberately let her rest well. Li Qingping said that as time went by, he had gotten used to everything around him, not only not feeling tired, but even feeling it was a blessing. Now, his greatest wish is to create a miracle in the world: to let his mother live to be 100 years old. Liu Yishou.

At the same time, Li Qingping also particularly thanked his wife and children. It was because of their emotional support and financial assistance that Li Qingping could focus all his energy on his mother and not worry about meals and daily expenses. Liu Yishou.

Li Qingping said that for a long time, because of his single-minded care for the elder, he paid no attention to outside affairs, and his mentality had become very peaceful. Perhaps precisely because of this reason, although he is already 63 years old, his body has never had any problems. Liu Yishou.

Regarding the two words "filial piety," Li Qingping has his own simple understanding: parents have worked hard all their lives and it is not easy. When they encounter difficulties, children must dare to take responsibility and not let the elders suffer. Liu Yishou.
