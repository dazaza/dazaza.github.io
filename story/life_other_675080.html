Life Tips

Smart Use of Moth Repellent in Summer: With the arrival of summer, it's once again time for moth repellent to take center stage. Due to its composition containing some heat-clearing and detoxifying Chinese herbs that reduce swelling and relieve pain, moth repellent not only repels prickly heat and itching, refreshes the mind, and prevents mosquito bites, but also has a certain antibacterial and sterilizing effect. Always having a bottle of moth repellent at home can add more comfort and warmth to life.

● Refreshing and Itch-Relieving: When washing your hair or taking a shower, adding 5-6 drops of moth repellent to the water can provide excellent cooling and sterilization effects, as well as relief from prickly heat and itching.

● Cleaning Hands: When riding public buses, subways, shopping in malls, or going on outdoor excursions, your hands inevitably come into contact with public railings and handrails. Dropping some moth repellent into your palms and rubbing your hands together can effectively clean them.

● Wiping Down Mattresses: In the summer, wiping down mattresses with water that has had moth repellent added can keep them fresh and clean. Of course, it’s best to wipe along the texture of the mattress so that the moth repellent can penetrate into the crevices of the mattress. This way, the cool and comfortable feeling will last even longer.

● Soaking Masks and Towels: Fill a basin halfway with clear water, add 4-5 drops of moth repellent, place cleaned towels and masks inside, and soak for about 15 minutes; or first boil the masks for disinfection, then rinse and dry in the sun. Before using, drop 1-2 drops of moth repellent which can play a certain sterilizing role.

● Wiping Phones and Telephones: Dip a tissue in a little moth repellent, and wipe the body, earpiece, and buttons of the telephone or mobile phone to keep them clean.

● Spraying Living Quarters: Mix clear water and moth repellent at a ratio of 40:1, pour it into a spray bottle, and evenly spray the floor of the living quarters (especially the corners of the room). Then close the doors and windows for 10 minutes before opening the windows for ventilation. Doing this in the morning and evening (or several times if conditions allow) can keep the indoor air clean and fresh.
