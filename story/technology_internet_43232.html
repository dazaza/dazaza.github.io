Top Ten Burning Events in IT in 2007

The battle for standards is a declaration of war from one system to another, where there is no smoke but the quagmire and mire of interests remain. The strong resistance of the Chinese forces changed the situation in the ISO voting where Microsoft documents dominated; the confusion over 3G licenses for operators still waxes and wanes in the TD test field. Mobile TV has triggered an all-out battle between broadcasting and operators, and the 2008 Olympics seem to have become the best practice to fulfill commitments. Mobile chargers have sparked a Sino-foreign war, and dual-port USBs that are humanized and energy-saving seem to be more in line with overall interests.

In the battle against infringement, Sohu won one and lost one. A complete victory over Google's input method copying added points to the protection of intellectual property rights, while the exclusive Olympic advertising strategy ignited a fierce counterattack from other portal alliances. Yahoo finally allowed Yang Zhifeng to make a comeback, while Microsoft ultimately failed to keep Chen Yongzheng from heading to the NBA. The BenQ endorsed by Mayday became Qisda, and BenQ and Chi Mei reached a capital success story that continued on.

Keyword: Standards

1. Documents

On September 5 (September 4 in US local time), the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) announced the preliminary voting results of member countries regarding Microsoft's OOXML document standard application for international standard status. As Microsoft only received 53% support, it did not gain the approval of two-thirds of member countries. Therefore, Microsoft's OOXML standard temporarily did not become an international document standard.
