**Boss Must-Read: Points to Consider When Selecting Work Phones for Employees**

When using the "Master Enterprise Communication" system, some bosses may choose to let employees use their personal phones, and in return, provide a small subsidy for their personal phone usage. Some companies prefer to issue uniform phones to their employees. Both methods are good options. If you choose the first method, please be sure to pay attention to the following issues:

1. The differences between smartphones on the market can vary greatly. Although they are all smart devices, due to variations in smartphone operating system versions and manufacturers, the performance of "Master Enterprise Communication" may differ across different phones. Please remind your employees to avoid using low-quality or non-branded phones as much as possible. These phones often have quality issues, and if problems occur while using "Master Enterprise Communication," it will be difficult to determine whether the problem lies with the phone or the software system we provided.

2. Avoid using outdated phones produced many years ago, such as smartphones with a resolution of 240*320. These phones may experience poor display effects and slow operation speeds when using "Master Enterprise Communication."

If you choose the second method, where the company provides uniform phones to employees, sometimes companies will work with carriers to handle bulk phone services. This approach has several advantages:

1. The company doesn’t need to pay directly for the phones but can receive one as part of a consumption agreement.

2. The phone numbers belong to the company, so when an employee leaves, the number can be reassigned to someone else.

3. Employees can call each other for free, and some carriers even allow free calls between mobile phones and the company’s landline.

However, there are also points to consider:

1. Signal quality varies by carrier depending on the region. In some areas, China Telecom offers better signals, in others, China Unicom or China Mobile does. Therefore, try to get feedback from your employees before making a decision.

2. China Mobile phones tend to have slower internet speeds, offering only 2G service in many regions without 3G. If you need to use "Master Enterprise Communication," especially for uploading photos on-site, test the speed beforehand. China Unicom phones might have weaker signals in remote areas, so if your employees frequently work in rural areas, seek their input.

3. Choose carrier contracts that last 1 or 2 years. Some carriers offer deals where a phone is given after 3 years of consumption. However, the industry generally agrees that the lifespan of a smartphone is around 2 years. Choosing a 3-year contract increases the risk of having many phones become obsolete or damaged after 2 years.

Regardless of whether you procure phones through a carrier's consumption plan or not, consider the following issues when purchasing phones:

1. What happens if the phone breaks or is stolen? It could directly affect business operations. For example, if no orders can be placed or sales reported for a day, it would be hard for the company to accept. Therefore, be prepared. Either keep some backup phones in reserve, encourage employees to use their personal phones if the company-issued one fails, or ensure the logistics department can quickly purchase replacements.

2. Smartphones consume a lot of power. When selecting a phone, aim for models with long standby times. Generally, the capacity of the phone battery is indicated in milliampere-hours (mAh), such as 1500 mAh or 2000 mAh. The higher this number, the longer the standby time. Having two batteries available for the phone would be even better.

3. Finally, after providing phones to your employees, it’s recommended to equip them with silicone protective cases, which can definitely extend the life of the phone!

As smartphones have become inseparable from us, the influence of mobile internet is beginning to permeate into enterprises. It's time to incorporate location information into corporate management considerations. Developed by Zhongtai Group, "Master Enterprise Communication" is a software that uses location information provided by mobile devices to increase the efficiency of employee management.

In traditional modes, it's difficult for company managers to monitor the real-time locations of employees working outside the office. Data collected by employees while out must be relayed via phone or recorded upon returning to the office. With the development of smartphones and mobile internet, more convenient location-based management allows companies to manage field employees just as effectively as office-based staff.

Through a mobile app, employees can sign in more promptly and accurately, and transmit data collected during fieldwork directly to the company's internal network. Such needs are concentrated in industries like fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) and logistics express services, which involve numerous field tasks. Statistics show that over 30 million company employees work outside the office nationwide, and with the increasing penetration of mobile internet into daily life, the popularization of mobile office software is an inevitable trend.

Beyond basic attendance management, "Master Enterprise Communication" also provides comprehensive mobile office services, such as managing/updating customer profiles via mobile phones, recording new business opportunities, uploading data to the company's internal network, checking company notifications, receiving new tasks, writing summaries, and applying for expenses or registering trips via mobile devices.
