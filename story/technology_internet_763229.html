Will Bill Gates return to Microsoft? It's just a wishful thinking.

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 17:32:36

Ever since the release of its financial report, Microsoft seems to be having a tough time in people's eyes. Both media reviews and stock market performance have taken a downturn, especially for Microsoft's Surface RT, which has been heavily criticized. Steve Ballmer's popularity has also dropped, and at this point, news about Bill Gates potentially returning to the scene emerged, with perfect timing and character synergy.

The article states that Microsoft’s current CEO, Steve Ballmer, has just announced a reorganization, but this move is not a solution to the problems. If possible, to retain its position as a software giant, Bill Gates might choose to return.

Well, I could also write a piece: "If possible, to revitalize Apple's innovation, Steve Jobs might choose to come back from the dead," with an attention-grabbing headline, and it would surely attract clicks.

In other words, the speculation that Bill Gates will retake the helm at Microsoft is entirely baseless, labeled merely as a "possibility." The timing aligns perfectly with Microsoft being in dire straits, and people both hope for and are willing to see Bill Gates' return—a textbook case of taking advantage of opportune timing, advantageous conditions, and popular sentiment to create news.

Microsoft has not responded to this, nor has Bill Gates this time.

Many may recall the story of Steve Jobs returning to Apple and saving the company, but Bill Gates left his position as CEO of Microsoft after securing his legacy and making proper arrangements—a situation very different from Jobs' first departure from Apple.

So, who wants Bill Gates back at the helm?

Bob Venero, CEO of Future Tech and a Microsoft partner, hopes and believes Gates will return to Microsoft. He says:

"Gates laid the foundation for Microsoft, had unique insights, and led the company to dominate the market. Compared to Ballmer, Gates clearly has stronger control; the whole world seemed to make way for him."

However, Gates is not infallible. At least, he made mistakes in evaluating the iPad and Surface—disappointing sales for the "frustrating" iPad, and lukewarm reception for the "content-rich" Surface.

After leaving his managerial role, Gates has focused on charity work and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, not significantly participating in Microsoft's direction. From the perspective of tech media, it seems like Microsoft really needs Bill Gates, but stepping away from that angle, the charity sector needs a leader like Gates even more. Moreover, Gates now possesses the mindset and capability to achieve greater good after his successful career.

Gates has repeatedly stated in public that re-taking the reins of Microsoft is almost impossible, as he wishes to focus solely on charitable endeavors, donating all his wealth and resources to his charitable foundation.

Not only does the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation need him, but what position does Microsoft have for Gates? To push Ballmer out? In fact, every time people are disappointed with Ballmer, expectations for Gates rise, and this time is no different. Gates is still the chairman of Microsoft and occasionally participates in some of its affairs, offering opinions. However, it must be acknowledged that Gates left his position as CEO of Microsoft a long time ago.

Finally, Microsoft is not yet in truly dire straits. Revenue and profit in the Q4 fiscal quarter were higher than the same period last year. The lackluster sales of Windows 8 and the failure of the Surface RT tablet cannot shake Microsoft's foundation. Without a fire, why call the firefighters?
