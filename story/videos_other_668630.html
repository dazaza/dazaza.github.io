On the afternoon of July 15, Li Xiao's condition took a turn for the worse, requiring transfer to Ningxia Medical University General Hospital. Within 12 hours, Li Xiao underwent two major surgeries, relied on a ventilator to breathe, and had not yet escaped life-threatening danger. Could he survive the 90-kilometer journey to the new hospital? Wuzhong City formulated plans from technical aspects, route selection, and coordination. At the same time, four regions of Wuzhong City—Litu District, Qingtongxia City, Yongning County, and Yinchuan City—cooperated. Under the joint efforts of all parties, a "green channel" for rescuing heroes and racing against time was smoothly established. The receiving hospital dispatched an ICU ambulance equipped with the most advanced hospital equipment worth millions and the most authoritative experts. Pedestrians and vehicles along the way made way for the hero, and the 90-kilometer journey was completed in just 40 minutes by the ambulance. Currently, Li Xiao and Na Zhendong's vital signs are relatively stable. (Reported by Zhou Zhijun)

At 14:00 on July 14, at the ICU entrance of Wuzhong People's Hospital in Ningxia, more than twenty people gathered together, staring at the tightly closed doors of the ward. Everyone’s heart was clenched up to their throat.

At around 11 p.m. on July 12, Li Xiao, who was back in his hometown of Wuzhong for winter vacation, and his good friend Na Zhendong were cooling off and taking a walk together. “Robbery!” When they reached the entrance of the mobile company business hall on Jinxing Road in Litu District, they were attracted by the cries of two women. These two women, one of whom was eight months pregnant, had been robbed by knife-wielding criminals. After committing the crime, the two criminals fled westward along the street.

Seeing this, Li Xiao and Na Zhendong instinctively chased after them. Running about 800 meters to the south gate of Yihai Trade City, the two blocked the criminals' path. Seeing no way out, the ferocious criminals pulled out their concealed daggers and stabbed towards them. Li Xiao and Na Zhendong did not retreat; instead, they bravely fought the criminals. In the nearly 20-minute life-and-death struggle, Li Xiao was stabbed 13 times in the back, chest, abdomen, etc., while Na Zhendong was stabbed four times, blood flowing down their clothes and pant legs... Frightened, the criminals fled. Severely injured, Li Xiao and Na Zhendong struggled to stop a taxi and told the driver: “These two robbed us, hurry up and chase…”

Last year, Li Xiao earned money by repairing computers and programming for others while studying. After entering university, half of his tuition was earned through part-time work. Despite facing hardships, Na Zhendong also chose independence, and their shared interest in computer learning made them good friends.

“My child doing such things doesn’t surprise me,” said Li Xiao's mother Ma Xiaoyan. Li Xiao, who has been sensible since childhood, could not stand seeing others in trouble. He never told his parents about the good deeds he did outside. “When my child was passing through Changsha, Hubei on the way to school, he donated blood,” Ma Xiaoyan said. “On July 8, the second day after Li Xiao returned home for summer vacation, he went to the train station and donated 300 milliliters of blood.”

Li Xiao, a 21-year-old Hui youth and a first-year student at Jiangxi Yuzhou Science and Technology College; Na Zhendong, a 22-year-old Hui youth who works as a laborer.

Inside the ICU ward, after undergoing two major surgeries, the Hui youth Li Xiao remained in a deep coma, and his fearless friend Na Zhendong lay beside him. Occasionally, when Na Zhendong regained consciousness, he looked at his neighbor bed “Abao” (Li Xiao's nickname), then quickly fell back asleep...

Facing injustice, how should we respond? Should we stay aloof or step forward voluntarily? Li Xiao and Na Zhendong have given us the best answer. Facing knife-wielding criminals with no regard for human life, they showed no fear, bravely fighting with full youthful passion, interpreting the beauty of humanity. Their unwavering bravery in critical moments allowed justice to prevail.

Just at this moment, the patrol car of the Second Police Zone of the Litu Branch of the Wuzhong Public Security Bureau, who were patrolling near the second outpatient department of the city hospital, drove over. "We noticed a taxi coming towards us, and the people inside were waving frantically and shouting to catch the criminals," said Officer Wang Yonggang. The patrol car stopped, and the officers saw Li Xiao and Na Zhendong, covered in blood. They quickly transferred them to the police car to take them to the hospital. However, Li Xiao insisted weakly, "Catch the criminals first!" Following the taxi driver's lead and the identification provided by Li Xiao and Na Zhendong, the officers captured the two criminal suspects within five minutes. Subsequently, Li Xiao and Na Zhendong were urgently taken to Wuzhong People's Hospital.

Inside the operating room of Wuzhong People's Hospital, the rescue operation was carried out seriously. Due to excessive blood loss, blood transfusion became the most urgent means of rescue treatment. The first surgery lasted from 23:30 that day until 4:00 AM the next morning, with over 6000 milliliters of blood transfused. Li Xiao remained in a state of hemorrhagic shock, still in critical condition, making the police waiting outside the operating room extremely anxious.

The Intermediate People's Court of Wuzhong City ruled after deliberation that Yin and Yu disregarded the law, and their actions violated the Criminal Law, constituting robbery, and were joint crimes. Their criminal methods were vicious, their subjective malice deep, and the social hazards great, legally warranting severe punishment. According to Article 263 (5), Article 25, Paragraph 1, Article 57, Paragraph 1, and Article 64 of the Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China, the court sentenced Yin to life imprisonment for robbery, deprivation of political rights for life, and a fine of 1000 RMB; the defendant Yu was sentenced to life imprisonment for robbery, deprivation of political rights for life, and a fine of 1000 RMB.
