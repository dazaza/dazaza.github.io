The 2009 China Interior Space Environment Art Design Competition was successfully held.

by longersing on 2009-11-28 23:32:26

The award ceremony of the 2009 China Interior Space Environmental Art Design Competition and the publicity meeting of the 2010 Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Beijing Annual Meeting were successfully held.

Hosted by the China Building Decoration Association, undertaken by the Design Committee of the China Building Decoration Association, and co-organized throughout by the Academy of Arts & Design at Tsinghua University and Italian BEE Ceramic Tiles —— the "2009 China Interior Space Environmental Art Design Competition" Award Ceremony and the Publicity Meeting of the 2010 Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Beijing Annual Meeting was grandly held on November 25th at the Great Hall of the People in Beijing.

In 2008, the Beijing Olympics brought worldwide jubilation, and in 2009, the 60th anniversary celebration of the founding of the People's Republic of China captured global attention. With the world’s eyes increasingly focused on China, the international community not only paid attention to China's economic and social development but also took notice of China's spatial environmental art design capabilities. China is currently at a critical transition period from "Made in China" to "Designed in China," and from "Product Export" to "Brand Export."

After rigorous reviews and standardized operations, outstanding designers representing the leading level of Chinese interior design emerged.

The 2009 China Interior Space Environmental Art Design Competition, as a preliminary contest for the 2010 Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Beijing Annual Meeting and the Asia-Pacific (China) International Forum for Interior Architects, solicited excellent design works nationwide from May to September 2009. A series of forums themed around “Discourse, Strategy, Morality, Boundary” were held in various provinces and cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Nanjing, Hangzhou, Chengdu, Changsha, and Xi'an. These activities received active responses from local building decoration associations, interior architects, interior designers, and faculty and students majoring in environmental art design at colleges and universities.

According to the host organization, the China Building Decoration Association, by September 30th, over 6,000 indoor design works were collected from all provinces and regions across the country. These works were categorized into 11 types: commercial space, catering space, hotel space, bar space, exhibition space, educational space, model room space, club space, residential space, corporate space, etc. The competition jury was composed of experts selected from the China Building Decoration Association and various local associations.

The organizers further disclosed that after nearly two months of strict review by relevant staff and the tight work progress of association personnel, this competition's award ceremony will produce first, second, and third prizes, excellence awards, comprehensive awards, and industry promotion awards. A group of outstanding designers representing the leading level of Chinese interior design will be selected and recommended to participate in the international event of the interior design field—the 2010 Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Beijing Annual Meeting and the Asia-Pacific (China) International Forum for Interior Architects.

For the entries submitted in this 2009 China Interior Space Environmental Art Design Competition, it was required that the concept statements be clear and accurately expressed, with features of originality, effectiveness, fidelity, functionality, creativity, energy saving, and environmental protection. The competition went through various stages including initiation, organization, promotion, submission, preliminary evaluation, re-evaluation, final evaluation, award presentation, exhibition, special issue, and tour exhibition.

Scientific and strict entry rules, fully transparent review regulations, and standardized and rigorous working procedures ensured the fairness and impartiality of the competition, providing a fair environment for mutual exchange among the interior design industry in China.

The excellent interior design works selected by the competition will showcase the style and charm of each interior designer, reflecting the forefront level of the interior design industry in China's building decoration sector. Taking the interior space environmental art design competition as a platform, it broke many restrictions and bottlenecks for participants. With the aim of promoting mutual exchanges among designers and design groups, the competition not only broadened the horizons of the participants but also gave full affirmation and commendation to designers and design groups with excellent design capabilities, professional levels, and good professional ethics.

In this competition, Italian BEE Ceramic Tiles, known as the "soul of Italian millennium art," was not only a co-organizer throughout the event but also an important strategic partner. Over the years, they have actively contributed to the harmonious and rapid development of China's building decoration industry and expanded the artistic vision of designers. They have also made great contributions to the overall strength enhancement of China's interior design industry in collaboration with the China Building Decoration Association.

On October 9, 2010, the Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Annual Meeting and the Asia-Pacific (China) International Forum for Interior Architects will land in Beijing, China, for the first time. At that time, more than 2,500 design elites from domestic and international well-known design masters, related associations and academic organizations, design institutions, and professional colleges will gather at the Beijing National Convention Center. They will comprehensively collide with new design concepts, deeply exchange ideas and strategies for the sustainable development of interior design, and jointly draw up the long-term blueprint for interior architectural design in the Asia-Pacific region.

As one of the member countries of the Asia-Pacific Interior Designers Federation, the China Building Decoration Association, with its strict self-discipline, professional exhibition operation specifications, and years of hard work, has introduced the council and grand prize competition of the Asia-Pacific Interior Designers Federation into China and obtained the sole hosting rights in the China region.

The Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Annual Meeting and the Asia-Pacific (China) International Forum for Interior Architects will last for three days.

On October 9, 2010, the Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association Council will convene, and the Excellent Works Exhibition of the Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association and the China Green Building Materials Champion Brand Exhibition will open.

On October 10, the theme forum of the 2010 Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association Beijing Annual Meeting and the Asia-Pacific International Forum for Interior Architects will be held, along with a welcome dinner and award ceremonies for various awards.

On October 11, six specialized forums on Olympic and sports building interior design, Expo and exhibition building interior design, hotel design, home design, interior architectural design education, light environment and lighting design will be held.

In addition, the organizing committee plans to publish a series of publications, including the "2010 Asia-Pacific Interior Architecture and Environmental Art Design Competition Excellent Works Collection," the "Asia-Pacific Outstanding Interior Architect Excellent Papers Collection," and the "Album of One Hundred Asia-Pacific Outstanding Interior Architects," as supporting activities for the entire annual meeting. This will create a deeper service platform, allowing participants to harvest and share cutting-edge design concepts while appreciating the latest classic indoor design creations from both international and domestic sources, understanding and sharing the latest scientific and technological achievements in building decoration materials.

The Asia-Pacific Space Designer Association (APSDA) Annual Meeting and the Asia-Pacific (China) International Forum for Interior Architects will be the largest, highest-level, and most influential international academic exchange activity organized since China's reform and opening-up and the establishment of the China Building Decoration Association 25 years ago. For the national building decoration industry, this is a rare opportunity. Allowing domestic outstanding designers to exchange face-to-face with internationally renowned design masters will help improve China's indoor architectural design level, showcase the achievements of China's indoor design after the reform and opening-up, expand the influence of Chinese designers, and promote the implementation of the "going out" development strategy of China's indoor design in international cooperation.
