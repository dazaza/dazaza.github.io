The "Five Don'ts" of Stone Maintenance: Tips to Help You Avoid Troubles

by bjksstone on 2010-03-30 10:50:12

Natural stone, like natural wood, is a porous material that "breathes," making it easy to absorb moisture or pollutants dissolved in water. If stone absorbs too much water and contaminants, it will inevitably lead to various types of stone deterioration, such as cracking, weathering, peeling, blistering, yellowing, water stains, rust spots, efflorescence, and dullness. Therefore, stone surfaces should avoid being washed with water or overly wet mops.

**Do not contact non-neutral substances**

All stones are susceptible to acid and alkali damage. For example, acids often cause the oxidation of sulfide minerals in granite, leading to yellowing, while acids decompose the calcium carbonate in marble, causing surface erosion. Alkalis can also erode the boundaries between feldspar and quartz silicate crystals in granite, leading to grain detachment. Non-neutral substances are therefore a major cause of stone surface damage.

**Do not apply wax indiscriminately**

There are many types of waxes available on the market, including water-based waxes, touch-up fatty acid waxes, oil-based waxes, and acrylic waxes. These waxes generally contain acidic or alkaline substances. Not only do they block the stone's breathing pores, but they also attract dust, forming wax dirt and causing the stone surface to turn yellow. If it is necessary to wax high-traffic areas, professional maintenance companies should be consulted for guidance on selecting the right wax and proper maintenance.

**Do not use non-neutral cleaning agents indiscriminately**

For quick cleaning results, most cleaners contain acidic or alkaline components. Long-term use of cleaning agents with unknown compositions will result in the loss of the stone's surface luster. Moreover, the residue from non-neutral cleaning agents is a primary cause of future stone deterioration.

**Do not cover the stone for long periods with carpets or debris**

To maintain the stone's ability to breathe, avoid covering the stone surface with carpets or debris for extended periods. Otherwise, moisture under the stone cannot escape through the stone's capillaries, leading to excessive moisture and increased water content, which causes annoying stone deterioration issues. If you must lay carpets or place items on the stone, ensure they are frequently moved and thoroughly cleaned.

**Prevent water stains (spots) from not drying**

To keep the stone breathing properly, avoid covering the stone surface with carpets or debris for long periods. Otherwise, the moisture beneath the stone cannot evaporate through the stone's capillaries, leading to problems such as excessive moisture, increased water content, and stone deterioration.

**Protect against wind and soil particles**

Whether it is hard granite (hardness HRS 88-111 ±4-7) or softer marble (hardness HRS 46-77 ±3-5), neither can withstand prolonged abrasion by sand and soil particles. Regularly use a vacuum cleaner and static mop to thoroughly remove dust and keep the surface clean. Place a de-dusting mat at commercial space entrances to filter out sand particles from shoes. In homes, consider changing into slippers upon entering to reduce opportunities for sand and dust to wear down the stone surface.

**Regular maintenance is essential**

Dusting and cleaning alone cannot sustain the brilliance of stone surfaces. It is crucial to regularly engage professional companies to perform maintenance and gloss regeneration work, such as using crystallizing solutions for marble re-crystallization, polishing powders for regenerating the luster of marble or granite, or breathable gloss protectants. These methods can help keep the stone looking new.

**Immediate removal of contamination**

All stones have natural capillaries, making it easy for contaminants (oil, tea, coffee, cola, soy sauce, ink, etc.) to penetrate the stone's interior through these capillaries, forming unsightly stains. Therefore, it is essential to apply high-quality stone-specific protective agents to prevent contamination. Note that no protective agent can completely block contamination forever. If contaminants spill onto the stone, they should be immediately wiped off to prevent penetration into the stone's capillaries.

**Maintain ventilation and dryness**

Stones fear environments with high humidity. Moisture can cause hydration, hydrolysis, and carbonation, leading to issues such as water stains, whitening, weathering, erosion, and rusting, damaging the stone. Therefore, the installation area should always be kept ventilated and dry.

**Regular application of protective treatments**

To extend the life of the stone and prevent water and contaminant penetration, regular protective treatments (depending on the type of stone and quality of the protective agent, ideally 1-2 treatments every 1-3 years) are necessary. The quality of the protective agent is crucial. After application, the stone's breathability, water resistance, and stain resistance must be maintained. Avoid using unverified protective agents, as they may not only fail to provide protection but also complicate stone maintenance.

**Quality requirements for acceptance of natural stone curtain wall projects**

To ensure the longevity of the stone, water and contaminant penetration must be effectively prevented. Regular protective treatments (as recommended above) should be applied. The quality of the protective agent used must be carefully considered, ensuring it maintains the stone's breathability while providing effective water and stain resistance. Never use unverified protective agents, as they may not only fail to protect the stone but also create additional maintenance challenges.
