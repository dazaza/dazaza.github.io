Tens of times of profiteering -- shocking exposure of the dark secrets of mobile phone repair

by likeqing00 on 2007-05-31 10:19:41

The tricks involved in mobile phone repairs are something consumers have long heard about, but perhaps haven't taken to heart. If your phone is out of warranty and it breaks down, you might find yourself in a situation where you're being overcharged. Reading this article may give you a bit more insight!

Replacing a 10 yuan flex cable costs 150 yuan I am a student currently interning at a mobile phone shop. There's a young man there who repairs phones, everyone calls him Brother Xiaofei. Since I'm interning with the phone team, during slow business hours at the store, I would often chat with him and learn about some internal structures of phones.

Once, my classmate's Samsung E330 phone had a completely black main screen, but the backlight was on. Because it was a grey market product without warranty, he didn't hesitate to ask Brother Xiaofei for help. After opening it up, Brother Xiaofei said that the phone's flex cable was broken (the flex cable connects the phone's screen to the motherboard). I asked him how much it would cost? He said they didn't have the E330 flex cable here, but it could be bought at the Huikun Communications Market in Shanghai for around 14 yuan. After checking a few places, I finally purchased one for 10 yuan from a small store. Brother Xiaofei skillfully installed the flex cable, and after turning on the phone, everything worked fine.

At this moment, a customer came in to repair their phone, saying that the phone no longer had ringtones or vibrations. Brother Xiaofei skillfully disassembled the phone for inspection, destroying the warranty seal in the process. He continued as if nothing happened, observing for a while before saying: "It's the flex cable that's broken! It will cost 150 yuan." The nearby intern (me) was shocked but could only remain silent. The customer continued to ask, "When can I get it back?" Brother Xiaofei calmly replied, "I don't have the flex cable for your phone now, if you want to repair it, you'll need to come back tomorrow. Right now, there's no sound, and if it's not repaired soon, the screen will lose its image too. Make your decision quickly!" The customer hesitated for a long time, eventually deciding to pay 150 yuan for the repair. Brother Xiaofei made a 150 yuan deal with a 10 yuan cost, quite a ruthless overcharge!

Falsely reporting faults to deceive people is common I often observe that many customers whose phones are broken lack understanding of the phone's internals, believing whatever the repair staff says, and getting ripped off isn't uncommon! I even saw a female customer come in to repair her phone, saying that the signal was poor. Brother Xiaofei looked at it and said, "Your power amplifier is broken, you need to replace it, it will cost 100 yuan," and even showed her a new power amplifier. The female customer said, "Alright, fix it quickly for me, I'm in a hurry." Brother Xiaofei took out some all-purpose glue, securing the phone's antenna, and then welded a copper wire to the signal transmission area, solving the problem completely at zero cost.

Friendly reminder: The three-guarantees policy for mobile phones has been in place for a long time now. For legitimate products within warranty, it's still best to go to an official repair center. After repairs, a new warranty seal will be applied, which is impossible at ordinary mobile phone repair points. For some county-level areas, if there is no official designated repair point, consumers should try to find shops with clearly marked repair price lists. Generally speaking, repair shops with clear pricing will openly state the startup fee and parts fee, and won't falsely report faults! Additionally, consumers should request transparent face-to-face repairs at these small shops!
