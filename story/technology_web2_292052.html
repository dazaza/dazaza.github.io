◆ Farewell to Pimples

Sunday

I probably didn't sleep well after having too much fun yesterday and drinking a few extra cups. I noticed this morning that a few old pimples had popped up on my forehead and temple area. They are red and swollen, and they hurt when pressed. How annoying. It's good that I don't have to work today. While I'm at home with nothing to do, I changed all my bedding. Pillowcases and duvet covers are places where dead skin cells, dust mites, dandruff, and other dirt easily accumulate. During sleep, these come into prolonged contact with the facial skin, making it easier for bacteria to grow and multiply, which creates a warm bed for pimples. Therefore, it's important to wash and change pillowcases regularly.

Monday

It's time to go to work, I can't go out with pimples on my face. The Vichy anti-pimple pen is great; it can accurately cover pimples while reducing inflammation and preventing their condition from worsening.

Tuesday

With pimples, cleaning becomes even more important. However, it’s not true that the stronger the cleansing product, the better. In my current state, I can only use the most gentle cleansing products, otherwise, it may cause dryness, stinging, redness, itching, or even peeling of the skin, which will increase oil secretion, making pimples worse. It's safer to choose more professional products. The La Roche-Posay acne cleanser comes in handy now.

Wednesday

Today, I made a natural honey and oatmeal mask. Mix two tablespoons of pure honey with three tablespoons of organic oatmeal powder into a paste, apply it thickly on your face, and rinse it off with warm water after 15 minutes. It feels very comfortable. Honey has anti-inflammatory effects and can enhance skin repair functions. Oatmeal effectively absorbs excess skin oil and maintains oil balance.

Thursday

Today, the pimples have faded a lot, so I don’t need to use concealer. Using Lancôme's lightweight foundation with good coverage is enough to cover the pimples.

UV protection is crucial to prevent ultraviolet rays from taking advantage.

Friday

The pimples have matured, with red pus heads on the surface that break easily upon touch. At this point, you can use a pimple needle to clear them without leaving scars—it's the right time to act! Wash your hands thoroughly, then cleanse your face. It's best to use a mask that removes dead skin beforehand to make the skin softer, making it easier to squeeze out the pimples. Then apply a little vinegar, which can also soften the dead skin.

The pimple needle must be sterilized by boiling it in 100℃ water for about 5 minutes, then store it in alcohol before use. Gently insert the pimple needle into the pimple at a 45° angle, then gently press around the pimple with two fingers wrapped in clean paper towels. Clean the area with physiological saline or apply makeup water containing tea tree oil or other antibacterial components with a cotton swab to reduce inflammation and help the redness subside faster after squeezing.

Saturday

Do a nourishing mask to strengthen the fading of pimple marks, and you'll have a clean and fresh face again! If you're still worried about marks, lightly dab Dior's anti-redness spot remover on them, and you'll be ready to go out and have fun again in the evening!

◆ Say NO to Pimples!

1. Removing excessive sebum secreted by the skin is the most important first step to getting rid of pimples: wash your face with warm water, but don't exceed three times a day.

2. Regular exfoliation: regular removal of dead skin cells accumulated in pores can promote cell renewal, preventing dead skin from clogging follicle openings and causing bacterial growth leading to pimples.

3. Adequate and regular sleep: staying up late and stress can easily disrupt the biological clock, leading to hormonal imbalance affecting endocrine function, directly promoting pimple growth.

4. Avoid excessive exposure to sunlight: ultraviolet rays can stimulate pimple development, and the accumulation of melanin in the skin can deepen scars.

5. Reduce intake of fried, spicy, and stimulating foods: such foods can easily trigger pimples. To get rid of pimples, you need to change your eating habits, drink more water, eat more vegetables and fruits, accelerate metabolism, and promote cellular detoxification ability.
