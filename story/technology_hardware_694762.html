Episode 1 (Season 4) Now You Know. Eddie plans to fake a suicide to force Carlos to change his mind. She sets up the scene and hangs herself from the beam when she hears Carlos coming back. However, Mrs. McClatchy, the neighbor, suddenly appears, grabbing her husband and talking non-stop about moving the garbage can immediately. Meanwhile, Eddie inside the room is almost out of breath. Fortunately, Mrs. McClatchy sees Eddie hanging in mid-air, screams, and Carlos manages to save her in time. Although Eddie's plan had some flaws and caused her some suffering, it still achieved its purpose.

Gabrielle sits at home waiting for Carlos's call, ready to elope according to their plan. Instead, she receives an apology call from Carlos saying he cannot leave Eddie. Carlos says their plan might have to be delayed by a few weeks. An enraged Gabrielle interrupts him, telling him it's over between them.

After several weeks of pre-wedding fatigue bombardment, both Susan and Mike are exhausted. Susan even doubts if Mike still intends to get married, but Mike assures her that he eagerly anticipates their wedding day.

Susan visits a doctor who recommends a new person - Adam, Catherine's husband.

At Catherine's housewarming party, she tells Mrs. McClatchy that her happiest times were living on Wisteria Lane. However, Mrs. McClatchy doesn't feel the same way.

Episode 2 (Season 4) Smiles of a Summer Night. Despite Tom's repeated expressions of concern, Lynette mercilessly kicks him out, telling him he makes her very depressed. Lynette hopes Gabrielle stays to accompany her, and Gabrielle reluctantly agrees but keeps leaving the room to fetch things for Lynette. Finally, she admits she hates hospitals because her father died here of cancer when she was five. Her mother often told her to replace tears with smiles. But facing Lynette in the hospital room, Gabrielle can't put on a smile because she fears losing Lynette.

At breakfast, Julie asks to attend an all-night dance in the nearby community, and Susan agrees. However, Mike disagrees because he heard the dance is wild. In the end, Susan agrees with Mike's opinion but secretly tells Julie she can go as long as she hides it from Mike.

Without Julie, Susan and Mike spend a quiet night together. Susan dressed seductively, proudly showing off her enticing figure. Mike mentions driving past the house where the dance was held, glad Julie didn't attend because chaos could be heard from far away. Susan becomes anxious and quickly grabs a shirt and jeans to go pick up Julie. As a result, Susan arrives at the house and, due to inappropriate attire, is mistaken for a hired stripper! Susan quickly drags her daughter away. Julie gets angry and argues with her, saying she really hates her mother's inconsistency. When they return home, Mike is already waiting for them, having learned the truth. Mike sincerely tells Susan that Julie is her child, and she can discipline her however she wants, but once their common child is born, he hopes they can discuss the child's education together. Susan agrees.

Episode 3 (Season 4) The Game. Susan plans a fun evening, but Lynette and Gabrielle make excuses not to come because they find such parties boring. However, when they hear a little secret about Catherine, they immediately decide to attend and persuade Susan to invite Catherine too, intending to pry information out of her during the evening.

Susan doesn't want to see Dr. Mayfair, so she asks Bree to recommend her obstetrician. Bree convinces her for a long time without success and randomly finds a name in the phone book to give her. Susan actually visits, only to be surprised to find the place full of addicts and drunkards.

Gabrielle no longer wants to hide around for dates with Carlos, but he assures her everything will be fine in two weeks. A political rival's wife is caught shoplifting in a store, and Victor is delighted. In conversation with Edie, he casually says he no longer worries about Edie's actions because after the divorce, she can no longer affect his reputation.

Edie plans to host a dinner party to announce her engagement, but Carlos advises her not to do so. Edie becomes furious. She had already bought the engagement ring without telling Carlos. Gabrielle is shocked upon hearing about Carlos's supposed engagement to Edie, telling him she doesn't intend to wait for him forever and announces she's cutting ties with him.

At Susan's party, everyone arrives on time except Susan herself. After waiting for a while, Susan finally shows up. Her car's tires were slashed, and her phone was stolen because the neighborhood Bree recommended has an extremely high crime rate. She angrily confronts Bree, who regrets it deeply but can't explain himself because he was just joking.

Episode 4 (Season 4) If There’s Anything I Can’t Stand. New neighbors, Bob and Lee, move in next to Susan. They're a gay couple. Susan has never interacted with a gay couple before, so she's curious and wants them to like her. However, their first encounter ends in embarrassment. Bob and Lee emotionally talk about "Rafael," and Susan assumes Rafael is the third person in a gay relationship, expressing understanding and sympathy and saying many things to show she's not prejudiced against homosexuals. However, Bob and Lee become furious, thinking Susan is insulting them.

It turns out Rafael is their beloved dog. To apologize, Susan bakes cookies and sends them to the neighbors. Bob asks if there are dried fruits in the cookies because he's very sensitive to them. Susan has to admit that she actually bought the cookies from the store and just warmed them up. Bob mercilessly tells her to keep them for herself.

Rafael roams around on the street and is found by Susan's daughter Julie, who brings Rafael home and tells her mother she plans to ask door-to-door who the dog owner is. However, Susan stops her daughter from returning the dog, planning to wait until the neighbors are desperate and then return the dog as a hero, making them grateful to her for life.

Sure enough, Lee is anxiously searching for Rafael outside. Susan enthusiastically offers help and goes inside to pour water for the sweating Bob and Lee. Unfortunately, at this moment, Mike comes home and opens the garage. Rafael darts out like lightning, knocks over Mike's paint bucket, and stains Bob's $2000 coat! As a result, Mike has to compensate them. Susan can only apologize to the neighbors, saying she just wanted them to like her. Mike tells her not to waste her efforts because her ideas have always proven not to be good.

Episode 5 (Season 4) Art Isn’t Easy. Bob and Lee set up an art sculpture in front of their yard. All the neighbors say it's glaring and ugly, but Susan still wants to gain the neighbors' favor, so she doesn't echo the neighbors' opinions. However, when she finds out that the sculpture is actually a fountain, she has to step forward and protest: the sound of the fountain is too noisy. Susan asks Bob and Lee to dismantle the fountain immediately, but the neighbors refuse, saying they installed the fountain to drown out the noise coming from Susan's house.

Catherine plans to run for the chairman of the homeowner association, a position that has been vacant since the death of the previous chairman. Bob and Lee try to pull Lynette to their side, but she is reluctant until they tell her that Catherine will definitely advocate tearing down the treehouse for her children. Lynette doesn't believe it and specially asks Catherine. Catherine firmly tells her that she cannot guarantee the treehouse will remain. So Lynette decides to also run for chairman.

Lynette hopes Susan votes for her, but Susan says she must dismantle the fountain in Bob's house. In the end, the vote results in a tie. Susan reluctantly casts her vote for Catherine. However, Adam does not congratulate her and directly says Catherine's victory may cost her more friends.
