The advance booking period for train tickets in Beijing is uniformly 10 days.

by yellow606 on 2011-05-11 12:04:43

Our press (reported by reporter Chunrui Liu) - Yesterday, I learned from Beijing Railway Bureau that starting from May 22, the advance ticket sales date for trains departing from the Beijing area and temporary passenger trains will be uniformly adjusted to 10 days.

At the same time, in order to reduce the pressure on the ticketing system during peak ticket sales periods, staggered ticket sales by time slot will be implemented starting June 1 (boarding date).

Adjustment of Advance Ticket Sales Period for Some Trains

Beijing Railway Bureau stated that starting from May 22, the advance ticket sales period for C, D, and Z prefix trains departing from the Beijing area as well as temporary passenger trains will be adjusted from 20 days to 10 days. The advance ticket sales period for T and K prefix and regular passenger trains remains unchanged at 10 days.

It is understood that tickets for travel before May 31 need to be purchased between May 11 and May 21.

Relevant personnel from Beijing Railway Bureau indicated that this adjustment may not remain fixed, and it is possible that the advance ticket sales period for trains may change in the future.

Staggered Ticket Sales in Three Time Slots

In order to reduce the pressure on the ticketing system during peak ticket sales periods, Beijing area will implement staggered ticket sales by time slot starting from May 22.

Beijing Railway Bureau stated that all tickets are currently scheduled to start advance sales at 9:00 AM, which places considerable pressure on the computerized ticketing system. In order to address this issue, measures have been introduced to implement staggered ticket sales by time slot.

Specifically, tickets for June 1 will begin advance sales on May 22, with tickets for T and K prefix and regular passenger trains going on sale starting at 9:00 AM. Tickets for C, D, and Z prefix trains will go on sale starting at 3:00 PM. Tickets for temporary passenger trains will go on sale starting at 7:00 PM.

A relevant official from Beijing Railway Bureau explained that these measures only apply to the 10th day's advance ticket sales calculated from the date of purchase. If passengers buy tickets for previous train schedules, they will not be affected by this measure.

Ticket Purchase Time

Tickets for travel before May 31 need to be purchased between May 11 and May 21.

Advance Ticket Sales Time for June 1

Tickets for T and K prefix and regular passenger trains go on sale starting at 9:00 AM.

Tickets for C, D, and Z prefix trains go on sale starting at 3:00 PM.

Tickets for temporary passenger trains go on sale starting at 7:00 PM.

[Note] Minister Sheng Guangzu of the Ministry of Railways stated that starting from June 1, real-name ticket purchasing will be implemented for all EMU (Electric Multiple Unit) trains nationwide. EMU trains include C, D, and G prefix trains. Previously, some ticket agents mentioned that due to the real-name system, the ticketing system would be upgraded and the advance ticket sales period for Beijing train tickets would be uniformly set to 10 days.

South Station Adds Facilities for Jinghu High-Speed Railway

Our press (reported by reporter Chunrui Liu) - Yesterday afternoon, at the platform of Beijing South Station, a CRH380A type train for the Jinghu high-speed railway was parked at a certain platform, consisting of 16 carriages with some doors open, and staff members were frequently entering and exiting.

A technical personnel of the CRH380A type train said that in the next month, operation tests based on the actual timetable will commence, aiming to integrate the Jinghu high-speed railway into the overall railway network schedule.

Yesterday, barriers were erected around the ticketing areas 2 and 4 inside Beijing South Station, and barriers were also set up in the middle area of the station’s second floor. A worker said that the construction involves moving the original ticket windows forward by 2 to 3 meters as a whole.

Railway department staff stated that in preparation for the operation of the Jinghu high-speed railway, South Station plans to add some service facilities.
