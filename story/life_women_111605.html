Overview of rhinoplasty

by 865512 on 2008-12-15 16:02:10

Rhinoplasty is one of the most common procedures in cosmetic plastic surgery. The shape of the nose holds a very important position in the overall appearance of the face. Over the years, from a large number of patients who have undergone the procedure, it can be seen that the key to surgical success lies in the preparation of the nasal augmentation model. If the model is well-designed and properly made, it is like adding the finishing touch to a painting, allowing the patient to achieve a harmonious and natural beauty. Conversely, if the design is inappropriate and the construction is rough, it will appear clumsy and unnatural, making it obvious that the person has had plastic surgery.

The preparation of the nasal augmentation model is a very important step. Surgeons must consider the condition of the patient's nose as well as other characteristics of the face around the nose, such as the distance between the inner corners of the eyes, the condition of the cheeks and forehead, the size and shape of the face, etc. There was one case: a female with a round face, whose features were relatively balanced except for a slightly low bridge of the nose, and she looked very gentle and sweet when smiling. If the nose was augmented, the line of the nasal bridge would become straight and rigid. Although the nose itself might look better than before, the overall effect was not very harmonious, and the original characteristic of looking sweet when smiling was changed, giving people a sense of seriousness instead. For such patients, surgeons must proceed with caution, discussing the design of the nasal model with the patient beforehand, understanding their requirements and aesthetic views. When necessary, computer imaging can be used to map the patient’s nasal image, and several options can be designed for both the surgeon and the patient to choose from. Then the model is made according to the chosen design. Models prepared in this way often yield satisfactory results.

In addition to the aforementioned preparation of the nasal model, attention should also be paid to the fixation of the model, the angle and shape of its connection with the forehead, as well as the condition of the nostrils and alae nasi. If there is thickening or drooping of the alae nasi, reshaping may also be required to ensure the entire nose is proportionate and balanced. This describes the more common situation of a low and flat nose. Among the patients with collapsed noses we encounter, there is also a much more severe type, mostly caused by disease or trauma, such as syphilis, which can cause perforation of the nasal septum leading to severe collapse of the nasal bridge and tip, resulting in a short nose deformity with the tip turned up. In addition, the base of the nose and the surrounding bones, i.e., the middle third of the face, present a disc-shaped depression. From the side view, the height of the nasal tip is lower than that of the upper lip. The surgery for these patients is more complex and requires hospitalization. Not only does the nose need to be augmented and its length increased, but the base of the nose and the adjacent depressed areas also need to be elevated. The transplant materials used in the surgery can only be taken from the patient’s own cartilage or bones, which are then shaped and filled into the depressed areas around the nasal bridge, tip, and middle third of the face. Only in this way can satisfactory results be achieved.
