Factors affecting technology transfer fees

by keyizhang008 on 2012-03-02 15:49:33

Due to the different basic conditions and methods that technical developers possess during the development process, there are always significant differences in investment. Determining the size of technology transfer fees solely based on the investment in a single technology development lacks a foundation recognized by society. Many factors influence the size of technology transfer fees. It cannot be priced using the method for general commodities, nor can it obtain an exact value through a simple calculation method. So, what factors affect the value of technology when negotiating technology transfer?

(1) The impact of exclusive and non-exclusive transfers on technology transfer fees.

An exclusive technology transfer means that there is a clause in the contract restricting the transferor from transferring the same technology to a third party within a certain geographical area. This represents the entire industry. The market demand curve becomes the demand curve for that factory. This strengthens the transferee's monopoly position in producing and selling products in that region; however, the transferor will lose the market for selling technology in that region. Therefore, the transfer fee for an exclusive transfer is higher, determined by the market in that region and the transferor's valuation of their loss.

(2) The impact of payment methods on technology transfer fees.

The payment methods for technology transfer fees mainly include lump-sum payment (total payment), royalty payment, and a combination of entry fee and royalty. In technology transfer contracts, the transfer fees under various payment methods can be converted among each other. In principle, different payment methods should not affect the size of the technology transfer fee. However, in practice, technology transferors have preferences for certain payment methods. To attract transferees to pay according to their desired method, they often adjust the fees. Generally, transferors prefer a lump-sum payment, so after conversion, it can be found that lump-sum technology transfer fees are often lower.

(3) The impact of different usage rights on technology transfer fees.

Different usage rights refer to clauses in technology transfer contracts that restrict the transferee from expanding production capacity or rebuilding factories beyond the permitted scope. If such a clause exists, it only allows the use of the transferred technology once within the scope of the contract, known as a one-time usage right transfer. If there are no such restrictive clauses, the transferee can use the transferred technology to arbitrarily expand production capacity and build factories, known as a one-time buyout. The transfer fee for a one-time buyout is much higher than that for purchasing a one-time usage right.

(4) The impact of the degree of technology obsolescence on technology transfer fees.

When a technology has aged or is close to being obsolete, its transfer fee is low. For newly developed technologies, especially when the produced goods are highly sought after in the market, the technology transfer fee will be very high. Sometimes, limited by the transferee's conditions and the possibility of transferring the latest technology, the transferee does not always seek the most advanced technology.

(5) The impact of different regions on technology transfer fees.

Generally, transferring technology to technologically advanced countries or regions incurs lower fees compared to transferring to technologically backward countries or regions. This is determined by the transferee's ability to accept the technology and the quantity of required technical services.

To protect the transferee's interests, technology transfer contracts typically include guarantee requirements for the transferor. If the transferor violates their guarantees, the transferee has the right to claim compensation and penalties, even cancel the contract and recover the already paid transfer fees. The stricter the claims and penalty clauses, the greater the risk borne by the transferor. Technology transferors often pre-add the costs associated with claims and penalties as risk fees and unpredictable fees into the transfer fee, thereby increasing the technology transfer fee.
