iPhone 5S is immune to all "money-saving tricks"

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:18:52

Hong Kong gray-market iPhones and carrier contract phones were previously the two main ways to save money when buying an iPhone. However, since the release of the iPhone 5s, users have found that whether they buy a contract phone through domestic carriers or purchase a gray-market phone in Hong Kong or Macau, there are increasingly more technical and warranty restrictions. For example, contract phones strictly prohibit "device-SIM separation," and gray-market phones bought in Hong Kong cannot be serviced under warranty in mainland China. Some industry analysts believe that these various restrictions mean that the iPhone 5s has effectively increased in price.

Carrier Contract Phones: Strict prohibition on "device-SIM separation" this year

"My husband and I are both China Unicom users. He uses more phone credit, and he wants to use his phone number to buy me an iPhone 5s contract phone. His SIM card would then be used in another China Unicom custom phone. I wonder if this is possible." A user recently consulted with a reporter.

According to information from the local China Unicom company, although at the group level, China Unicom has always disallowed device-SIM separation, this practice was previously tacitly accepted by the carriers. It is reported that in many local companies, to boost sales performance, their staff had the authority to assist users in achieving "device-SIM separation." However, starting this year, purchasing an iPhone 5s and achieving "device-SIM separation" for shared resources between couples is almost impossible. The Unicom system will continuously check if the purchased custom phone matches the SIM card; if it does not match, the phone credit subsidy will not be issued, and the user will need to pay high phone bills at the end of the month.

Carriers' Dilemma: Many Users Exploiting the System

"Prohibiting device-SIM separation mainly aims to prevent exploiting the system behavior," said a Guangzhou Unicom official to the reporter. Unicom discovered that many users exploit the system—after purchasing an iPhone contract phone from the carrier, they sell the iPhone at a high price the next day, which is equivalent to having free phone credit.

In addition, the official stated that Unicom provides substantial phone credit subsidies for the iPhone, but the purpose is to cultivate user habits and retain users. If users are allowed to separate the device and SIM card, this would lead to the carrier being unable to achieve its goals of cultivating and retaining users, ultimately turning the contract phone policy into a subsidy for users to buy iPhones. Therefore, after realizing the issue, starting this year, China Unicom has strictly prohibited device-SIM separation.

Apple Hong Kong Specialty Store: Gray-Market Phones Not Covered Under Warranty in Mainland

If purchasing a contract phone for "shared resources" doesn't work, why not just go to Hong Kong and buy a cheaper gray-market iPhone 5s? Unfortunately, as of now, this cost-saving route for purchasing an iPhone 5s has also been blocked.

Due to reasons such as no VAT payment, Hong Kong edition gray-market iPhones have always been cheaper than those sold in mainland China, leading to a large number of mainland users, especially those from Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and the Pearl River Delta region, frequently buying gray-market iPhones in nearby Hong Kong and Macau. However, recently, some mainland iPhone users have discovered that Hong Kong edition iPhone 5s and 5c models cannot be serviced under warranty in mainland China.

Currently, Apple's internal repair system has confirmed that due to different hardware specifications, Apple cannot provide identical parts to repair mainland edition iPhone 5s and 5c models. Repairs for other iPhone models (iPhone 5, iPhone 4S, etc.) remain unaffected.

Apple's Dilemma: After-sales Services Overwhelmed

According to the reporter's understanding, although iPhones do not offer global warranty services, within regions like the EU and Greater China (mainland, Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan), cross-border warranty services are available. Thus, previously, if gray-market phones purchased in Hong Kong had quality issues, they could be directly replaced with new (or refurbished) phones in mainland China. This policy led to a large number of second-hand Apples within warranty periods being recovered, replaced with new phones, and resold, making profits from the price difference between old and new terminals reaching thousands of yuan.

Industry insiders revealed that actually, many recovered second-hand iPhones and iPads were not broken. However, recyclers would damage the motherboard using methods that do not affect the appearance to create "broken iPhones." These "broken phones" would be returned to the manufacturer Foxconn by Apple and then refurbished and resold by Foxconn, causing significant losses for Foxconn.

Reminder

Hong Kong Edition iPhone 5s Does Not Support Mainland 4G

On the technical specification table of Apple's Hong Kong website, I saw that both the Hong Kong edition iPhone 5s and 5c support FDD-LTE and TD-LTE 4G standards. Therefore, many China Mobile users look forward to buying a Hong Kong edition iPhone 5s in Hong Kong once 4G licenses are issued in a few months and using it domestically. However, disappointingly, after testing, some netizens found that the Hong Kong edition cannot use 4G networks in mainland China. This means that if you want to experience 4G, you still need to buy the domestic version.
