Ma Yun steps down as CEO of Alibaba today

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 11:22:29

For problems, find Lao Lu; for no reason, find Lao Ma. Before leaving office, Jack Ma threw this sentence to the Alibaba employees.Today, Jack Ma will announce in Hangzhou that he will completely give up the title of CEO of Alibaba and devote himself to another field: public welfare and environmental protection. Subsequently, his new job is to succeed economist Hu Zuliu as the next chairman of the TNC (The Nature Conservancy) China Council.The Alibaba that he founded also submitted a nearly perfect scorecard before his departure. I noticed that on May 8th, the documents Yahoo! submitted to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission showed that the net profit of Alibaba Group reached $640 million in the fourth quarter of the 2012 fiscal year, with single-quarter profits already surpassing Tencent, becoming the most profitable company in China's Internet industry.Regarding the Internet industry, I am already somewhat old; regarding new things, I am still young." Jack Ma said so when attending a public welfare activity held in Santa Monica, California, USA, under the title of CEO of Alibaba Group. He then donated $5 million to the China Global Conservation Fund (CGCF) established by the TNC board members. Whether Jack Ma can use his experience and influence in the Internet industry to recreate an entirely new public welfare ecosystem has become a focal point of attention.Ali Rebuilds Profit PointsIf we compare Alibaba to his son, then investing in Sina Weibo was the last leg of the journey that Jack Ma accompanied this son through. Alibaba recently announced that it would purchase approximately 18% of Sina Weibo's shares for $586 million, with their partnership being evaluated by the industry as a perfect match.Jack Ma expressed in a media exclusive interview that Sina Weibo is a very good product, and investing in Weibo is a very important step for Ali's future development. He emphasized that Ali does not want to turn Weibo into an e-commerce platform but instead wants to explore how e-commerce can help Weibo, working with Sina to create a Weibo platform belonging to the future.An insider close to Jack Ma within Alibaba also frankly admitted to the "Everyday Economic News" reporter that investing in Weibo was entirely to improve Ali's layout in mobile terminals. The giants on PC undoubtedly are Tencent, Baidu, and Ali, and I can say that at least for three years, no one can beat them. But in the mobile Internet sector, no one dares to call themselves first, and the situation is far from being settled.Based on this sense of crisis, Alibaba has accelerated its pace of mergers and acquisitions. In addition to investing in Sina Weibo and seeking to increase its stake in UC, public data shows that Alibaba has successively invested in mobile applications such as Momo, On The Road, Kuaidi Dache, Dingding Youhui, and Xiami, and spent $80 million to acquire Umeng, a mobile application service platform.Besides continuing to strengthen efforts in the mobile Internet sector, Alibaba is also working on integrating existing businesses.Since delisting, Alibaba's B2B business has once faded out of the industry's view. However, a series of structural adjustments also indicate that Alibaba is integrating the seemingly unrelated B2B and B2C businesses into an overall platform.On May 8th, Alibaba Group announced that it would restructure the CBU (Alibaba China Business). The original CBU service sales system will be integrated with the ICBU (Alibaba International Business) service sales system to create a unified service and sales system for the entire Alibaba Group targeting small enterprise customers.In the technology and product fields, the original CBU technology and related teams will undergo team restructuring with Taobao, forming a trust pass product and service team under the entire Alibaba Group.In addition, the original CBU website and industry operations team will continue to serve B-class businesses for SMEs and integrate with Taobao and Tmall-related businesses, focusing on completing two major service functions: procurement and wholesale. Zhang Jianfeng, a member of the Alibaba Group Management Executive Committee, will oversee this division.Obviously, how to find new integration and growth points between B2B and B2C is an important task left by Jack Ma for the new Alibaba CEO, Jonathan Lu.Jack Ma's New JourneyIn fact, Jack Ma has already planned his way forward well, tinkering with other industries: one is education, the second is green environmental protection, and the third is investment surrounding the first two. Regarding The Nature Conservancy, more people see it appearing in Jack Ma's Weibo ID.I saw on its official website that the fresh page aligns perfectly with the concept of environmental protection, protecting the land, freshwater, and oceans on which all life depends is how the association describes itself.This is an international non-profit non-governmental organization engaged in ecological environmental protection, founded in 1951, headquartered in Washington, D.C., USA, and has become one of the largest environmental organizations globally. Currently, TNC's global assets exceed $3.7 billion, with annual revenue reaching $800 million, and membership growing from 500,000 in 1990 to over 1 million now.In May 2011, Jack Ma opened a real-name Weibo account as a director of the TNC Global Board of Directors, with the ID name "Nature Conservancy Association Jack Ma". Even though the total number of Weibos is only 29, and the number of follows is zero, it has over 7 million fans.To better obtain financial support, in May 2010, Jack Ma decided to allocate 0.3% of Alibaba Group's annual income to a public welfare fund starting from 2010, namely the Alibaba Public Welfare Foundation, mainly used for funding environmental protection projects.Because he is very concerned about fresh water resources, the foundation has funded a series of non-profit projects aimed at restoring clean water quality in multiple provinces, known as the Clear Source Action. Of course, other projects beneficial to environmental protection can also apply for financial support.What Jack Ma will do next is still unclear. He revealed that after stepping down, he would take a three-month vacation for himself.However, it is clear that the public welfare sector has already begun to look forward to his arrival. Wang Ming, Director of the NGO Research Institute at Tsinghua University, told reporters in an interview that influential business figures dedicating themselves to the public welfare industry is itself a sign of social progress in China, like Bill Gates and Warren Buffett. So Jack Ma's choice is also normal, and many experiences, concepts, and methods from his Internet industry can be applied to public welfare.At the same time, he also frankly stated that doing public welfare sometimes is even harder than doing business well. Jack Ma's attempt might promote the perfection of this industry.
