Chengdu Second People's Hospital Weibo appointment registration: 30% of patients miss their appointments within two weeks

by edivista on 2011-11-28 17:04:13

● A private message with your name, telephone number, symptoms, and desired consultation time will be considered as a successful appointment once you receive a reply. Patients who make successful appointments can be exempt from registration fees.

As of yesterday, within 14 days, a total of 39 citizens made appointments through the microblog with the dermatology and plastic surgery department of Chengdu Second People's Hospital and successfully received treatment. Some patients even booked treatments for December.

Starting from November 14, the dermatology and plastic surgery department of Chengdu Second People's Hospital began to try microblog appointment consultations where successful appointments also exempted the patient from the 5 yuan registration fee. This is the first department in Chengdu's municipal hospitals to attempt microblog appointment registration. (Click on the picture to enter the microblog of Chengdu Second People's Hospital)

Although the number of forwards and comments for this microblog opening was minimal, the large number of private messages for consultations indicates that this move has been welcomed by citizens in terms of appointment registration methods, and has also been affirmed by relevant responsible persons from Chengdu Medical Administration Bureau.

However, along with the quiet rise in popularity of microblog appointment registrations, issues such as a high no-show rate of up to 30% among patients have also become apparent.

How exactly is microblog appointment registration faring? Huaxi Metropolis Daily conducted interviews.

Microblog Appointment Offers Convenience to Citizens

Before the opening of microblog appointment registrations, the daily average number of patients visiting the dermatology and plastic surgery department of Chengdu Second People's Hospital was maintained at 20-30 people. In April this year, the department opened a microblog account, and every day it would receive private messages from netizens asking various questions. At the beginning of November, Director He Mei of the department proposed the idea of making appointments through microblogging.

A private message with your name, telephone number, symptoms, and desired consultation time will be considered as a successful appointment once you receive a reply, and patients who make successful appointments will not be charged the 5 yuan registration fee.

Last weekend, Ms. Luo made an appointment through the microblog and went to the hospital for mole removal and hair removal treatment. It was all about convenience. Ms. Luo said that she had been hesitating online for three days, privately messaging the dermatology and plastic surgery department every day asking how much the surgery fee was, whether there would be scars, and how long it would take to heal. After receiving answers, she finally felt reassured enough to make an appointment.

Few Forwards but Many Private Messages for Consultations

The reporter noticed that, as of yesterday, the number of forwards and comments for this microblog post were only two each. Although the number of forwards and comments was small, the number of private messages was very high, with Dr. Lu Jie from the department having to reply to as many as 30 private messages per day. These included inquiries about efficacy, costs, and the specialties of doctors, covering all aspects.

Patients who made appointments through the microblog came from places like Chengdu and Leshan. In addition, netizens from other provinces such as Yunnan also consulted via the microblog.

Previously, the department had also tried QQ appointments, but now it seems that more people are making appointments through microblogging. Dr. Lu Jie said that those making appointments include students, white-collar workers, and housewives, all of whom are avid microblog users.

Daily Treatment Volume of 30 to 40 Patients

There is a microblog duty roster in the dermatology and plastic surgery department. The doctors and nurses of the department work in pairs, managing the microblog and answering netizens' questions.

Although there are many successful cases, there are still some patients who cancel their appointments at the last minute. As of yesterday, patients who successfully made appointments through the microblog but did not come for treatment on time accounted for 30% of the total number of successful appointments.

Dr. Lu Jie said that they had anticipated such a situation before. She replies to every private message with the intention of explaining things and helping people understand skin problems. When there are more people making appointments over the weekend, she advises patients to come for treatment at other times. Based on the patient's condition and treatment method, we control the daily treatment volume between 30-40 patients.

Medical Bureau Attitude

Microblog Appointment Is Not Suitable for All Departments

Microblog appointment is a new way of registration, which can be attempted. A relevant person in charge of Chengdu Medical Bureau said that whether it is by phone or QQ, or even microblog appointment, there will always be patients who do not show up. However, we should not discourage microblog appointment because of this. We encourage hospitals to try multiple ways to provide convenience for patients, after all, this allows patients to avoid unnecessary trips.

This person in charge said that microblog appointment conforms to network life and meets everyone's needs. But this method must be differentiated by department, and not all departments can promote it. Dermatology and plastic surgery patients are not in a hurry, they can have surgery today or tomorrow, sometimes they need repeated consultations before making a decision, so it is suitable to consult first and then make an appointment on microblog.
