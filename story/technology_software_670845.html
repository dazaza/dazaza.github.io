Nightclub Opens with Public Security Bureau's Celebration Banner; Bureau Chief Responds with a Promise for a Thorough Investigation

by anonymous on 2012-02-21 11:55:11

Not long ago, a large nightclub in Fengyang County opened for business. The opening ceremony was quite grand; the entrance was piled high with flowers and firecrackers were going off non-stop. The building of the nightclub was also covered with congratulatory banners celebrating its opening, including one that read "trace32." Nearby residents reacted by saying, "All kinds of organizations sent [banners], even the Public Security Bureau and the Fire Department!"

"How can the Public Security Bureau send congratulatory banners to celebrate the opening of a nightclub?" Faced with this question, Tao Yong, the director of the Fengyang County Public Security Bureau, became extremely angry: "Several of the bureau's responsible persons were unaware of this situation. Who authorized this action?"

### Witnesses:

The Public Security Bureau sends congratulatory banners to a nightclub?

This nightclub is located on Donghua Road in Fengyang County. A witness stated, "It opened on December 18th," adding that the scene on the day of the opening was incredibly lively. This four-story nightclub is relatively large in scale locally, and attracted a lot of attention on its opening day. "The entrance was filled with congratulatory flower baskets," the witness said. What drew more attention was the celebratory banners surrounding the exterior of the building. Witnesses claimed that two banners stood out prominently, signed by the Fengyang County Public Security Bureau and the Fengyang County Fire Brigade. "Moreover, these banners from the two units were hung in relatively prominent and important positions, easily visible upon approach."

This incident immediately sparked heated discussions locally. "How could the Public Security Bureau congratulate a nightclub?" Many citizens expressed that such behavior was highly inappropriate. Others said that this kind of thing was not uncommon, often done by nightclubs themselves without the knowledge of other units, merely for show.

### Nightclub:

Uncertainty about whether law enforcement departments sent banners

In response, a manager of the nightclub, when interviewed, admitted that on the day of the opening, many congratulatory banners were indeed received. Regarding the Public Security Bureau and the Fire Department, the manager said he wasn't very clear: "Generally, after receiving them, we would get a phone call from the sender. I'm not sure if the Public Security Bureau sent one, nor have I received any calls about it." Strangely enough, just a few hours before the interview, all the celebratory banners outside the nightclub were removed.

Based on observations at the scene, these banners not only had the same specifications and lengths, but also used fonts and colors that were largely identical. According to insiders in the entertainment industry, when entertainment venues open, they need more attention, and most of these banners are made and hung by the opening venue itself. A common tactic is to invite various organizations, including the Public Security Bureau, beforehand. Some practices are relatively "fair" — inviting representatives from units, and hanging banners only if they attend. Others hang banners regardless of whether anyone from the invited units actually shows up.

### Public Security Bureau:

No knowledge within the bureau, thorough investigation to be conducted

"Hanging congratulatory banners from the Public Security Bureau, several responsible persons in the bureau were completely unaware. Whether anyone went to the opening ceremony or not, the bureau will thoroughly investigate this matter!" In the face of doubts, Director Tao Yong of the Fengyang County Public Security Bureau was extremely upset. Tao Yong explained that previously, the bureau had no knowledge of being celebrated in this way. The Public Security Bureau learned about this matter when an officer noticed it during a nighttime patrol. "After receiving the officer's call, I ordered an investigation that same evening," Director Tao said. On the morning of December 21st, the Fengyang County Public Security Bureau dispatched the Public Order Brigade to investigate the nightclub and instructed them to remove the banners on the spot. Not only that, but after being "celebrated," the fire department also conducted an investigation. While informing the nightclub to remove the banners, the fire department also immediately rechecked the safety facilities of the nightclub again. (Anhui Business News (Weibo) Ouyang Xu Zhong)
