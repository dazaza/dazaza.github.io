Conditions after treatment for cervical erosion:

Experts explain that physical therapy for cervical erosion may result in bleeding and increased secretions. These are normal phenomena. Often, due to the healing and repair of the wound, there may be a large amount of vaginal discharge and fresh blood. If there is yellow watery vaginal discharge, accompanied by a feeling of heaviness in the lower abdomen and itching, it could be due to incomplete elimination of inflammation.

How long after treatment for cervical erosion can sexual activity resume?

Patients with cervical erosion should maintain cleanliness of the external genitalia, frequently wash the external genitalia, but should not use vaginal suppositories, perform vaginal irrigation or take baths on their own. Underwear should be washed frequently, and sanitary pads can be used if necessary. Since the healing time for the cervical wound is relatively long (approximately 3-8 weeks), it is best to avoid sexual intercourse during this period. Sexual intercourse not only affects the healing of the cervical wound due to mechanical stimulation, but may also cause vaginal inflammation, thus affecting the normal repair of cervical tissue.
