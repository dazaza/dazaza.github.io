Enjoy life! The memories that life brings you are the only property you can take with you after death.

Some might say Li Bai and Tao Yuanming were madmen. Then I must clearly tell him, many people who have left their names in history are madmen. Leo Tolstoy abandoned his noble life and fled home, he was a madman, but he wrote immortal masterpieces like War and Peace, Resurrection, and Anna Karenina; Molière gave up the right of the eldest son to inherit family property, and finally became famous with his play The Hypochondriac; even Mao Zedong, he also abandoned the peaceful life as a landlord at home, devoted himself to revolution, and became the founder of the People's Republic of China... These are not coincidences. These people know how to enjoy life, they are the masters of life.

Enjoy life, enjoy the feeling of failure. No one's life is always smooth sailing. Even the Yangtze River may dry up, the Earth may experience earthquakes, and the whole universe does not know when it will feel the pain of a supernova explosion. Compared to these, isn't our failure too insignificant? Some might say: I've put in so much effort, why can't I succeed? That's right! This question indeed needs thinking, otherwise, you might continue such tragedies indefinitely. But if you treat this question as a burden, you will never truly succeed. When you step onto the battlefield, can you say to your enemy: I've been practicing martial arts for ten years, let me win once and achieve fame. Is that possible? You have struggled for ten years, hasn't others also struggled for ten years? You want to achieve fame and success, don't others also want to achieve fame and success? So, forget about your efforts, or just think that your efforts are less than others (I seem to be off-topic). Anyway, what's wrong with failure? How many people can truly appreciate the feeling of being disheartened after failure? After failure, sleepless nights, staying awake all night alone, personally examining your own wounds, the feeling of dissecting yourself bit by bit, the feeling of heart being gnawed, clear and thorough, bone-deep pain, isn't this kind of feeling a kind of enjoyment? Since you choose life, why not accept its sour, sweet, bitter and spicy flavors together? Maybe, after self-examination and reflection on pain, you will still shout out how refreshing!

Someone has said: Life is no more than a game of chess with Death. The result in front of us is bound to lose, the final result is just a little time gained. Since that's the case, why don't we enjoy the only little time we've gained? Don't be greedy for money, wealth, reputation, beauty in the world, because ultimately, all you really own is just a little time, and even this little time is leased, cannot be taken away, how could other things be taken away? Put down the burdens of the secular world, enjoy life! At least, after accepting the chess game, you can say to Death with ease: The winner of this game is you, but the winner of life is me. I enjoyed all spiritual feelings, I am much richer than you. Now, I can go and enjoy the last thing, that is, the death you give me. Such a sense of release! Who knows, maybe even Death would admit defeat.

Enjoy life, enjoy the process of struggle. Not everyone born as a writer becomes a writer, not everyone born as a genius becomes a genius. Everyone's growth must go through the process of struggle. Madame Curie didn't easily become the top of her class every time, please remember she came first every day and left last; Margaret Thatcher wasn't destined to be a miracle from birth, please remember she sat in the front row of the classroom early every day; Einstein didn't become a renowned physicist without excellent results, don't forget he finished all university courses by self-study. The process of struggle is actually the splashing of sweat. In the process of struggle, you will be very painful, very uncomfortable, you will fall asleep on the table at night due to exhaustion, you will get sick all over your body. However, when you immerse yourself in the process of struggle, you will feel that sweat is glory, pain is a badge, and the sleepy eyes during class are prizes others can't obtain. When others look at you with surprise and disbelief for your near madness for success, a sense of accomplishment will naturally arise. You will feel that you are your own god, you are the ruler of the world, the whole world revolves around you, falls for you, and no one can surpass you, because at that moment, you are the emperor who has reached the peak.

Writing the title "Enjoy Life" has already determined to be questioned by others. The reason is very simple: I haven't yet gained the ability to live independently, how can I talk about enjoying life?

I think those people are too narrow-minded. Isn't enjoying life only about material enjoyment? If that's really the case, then why did Tao Yuanming refuse to bow for five bushels of rice? Why did Li Bai say "How can I lower my eyebrows and serve the nobles, making me unable to be happy?"

Enjoy life, enjoy the experience of loneliness. What's wrong with loneliness? You have more time to think, to examine yourself, to prepare fully to make a name for yourself in a lively crowd. When you're lonely, you're like waving a goose feather fan, watching the world sway randomly, and then proudly lamenting about the declining moral standards, although it sounds a bit narcissistic, but isn't this a beautiful kind of enjoyment? Wandering outside the world, the whole world is turbid except me, all people are drunk except me. Moreover, loneliness doesn't mean eternal loneliness, you can choose to integrate into society at any time. You will feel that you belong to yourself, and can never be controlled by others.
