The Key for Elementary Students to Learn Calligraphy Well 2012

by anonymous on 2012-02-17 13:39:58

The crux of elementary school students learning calligraphy well, and cultivating excellent writing habits in students, especially in lower grade elementary school students, is particularly important. This is not only about cultivating students to write a good hand, but also about moral quality, willpower, intelligence quality, and aesthetic sentiment education. To make naturally active elementary school students develop correct writing postures and excellent writing habits, achieve standardization, neatness, and orderliness, and feel the beauty of Chinese character forms, is indeed not an easy task. The following aspects can be used to cultivate students' excellent writing habits:

1. Enhancing interest: Cultivating excellent writing habits in elementary school students through painting is a prerequisite. Calligraphy for elementary school students is a new thing, and the teaching content mainly involves regular script and clerical script. Its aesthetic thought is not as relaxed as children's art with bright colors. It has more specific and familiar shapes, and even stories are embedded within. However, calligraphy only has a single color and line. If the teacher's teaching lacks creativity, the students will find it rigid and uninteresting, leading to a lack of interest. "Interest (interest), in psychology, refers to our tendency to strive to acquaint ourselves with certain things or enjoy certain activities, manifested as people's selective attitude towards certain things or activities and positive emotional responses." Elementary school students' psychology is unstable. Their acceptance of external things is based on temporary interests or reactions. When they develop an interest in a particular subject, they will actively and enthusiastically engage in learning without feeling burdened. Therefore, how to mobilize students' subjective initiative and enhance their interest in writing is the key to having a good writing class and improving writing quality. Teachers should, according to the inherent psychological characteristics of elementary school students, apply flexible and varied methods, think of ways to stimulate students' interest in writing, and motivate their enthusiasm and initiative.

Throughout China's long history, many famous calligraphers have emerged, and their excellent works have influenced generation after generation of students. Teachers should collect more calligraphy works by these masters for students to appreciate. In the process of appreciating masterpieces, students can experience an aesthetic psychological activity from the density of the work structure, the weight of the strokes, the intensity of the ink color, and the speed of the brush movement. According to students' admiration for celebrities, teachers can tell them touching stories about ancient great calligraphers who practiced diligently. Students will be inspired by the spirit of hard practice in these stories and moved by their noble personalities, thus igniting their passion for calligraphy and participation awareness. Excellent student works can be posted on the walls of the calligraphy room. Timely praise and encouragement should be given to students who take a serious attitude towards studying calligraphy and use correct methods. Under the influence of "environmental immersion," students will gradually form a sense of competition, creating a "competitive" learning atmosphere where they "chase and catch up" with each other, which also helps cultivate a strong interest in studying calligraphy.

Additionally, encourage students to actively participate in various calligraphy competitions organized both inside and outside the school, allowing them to see their own progress.
