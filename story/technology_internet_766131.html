10 Online Coding Contests For Programmers!   

If you possess exceptional coding skills, or even if you're just a beginner who wants to practice and improve your coding abilities, and are willing to compete with top coders, then this article is prepared just for you. This article provides 11 online coding competitions where you can go head-to-head with coders from all over the world.

The websites mentioned in this article each offer their own sets of practice problems, training courses, and competition questions. Additionally, sponsors of these events — including companies like Microsoft, IBM, and Facebook — are closely watching participants, and you could be the next lucky individual to secure an internship or job opportunity at one of these major companies. If you're interested, give it a try!

1. Topcoder

TopCoder is arguably the largest competitive software development community globally, attracting developers from all around the world to participate in contests. The community offers various types of programming competitions, including algorithm, testing, design, assembly, SRM (Single Round Matches), marathons, and more. Sponsors, such as Microsoft and NSA, provide cash rewards for winners. Solutions can be submitted in languages like Java, C++, C#, or VB.

2. CodeChef

CodeChef is a non-profit organization initiated by the Indian software company Directi, headquartered in Mumbai. It is a global programming community that hosts online contests, training sessions, and other activities.

3. Sphere Online Judge (SPOJ)

Sphere Online Judge is one of the earliest competitive coding platforms, supporting over 40 programming languages and compilers. Nowadays, the SPOJ platform has developed into an automated system for evaluating user-submitted projects.

4. CodingBat

CodingBat is a live-coding website offering problems related to Java and Python. The site provides instant feedback on solutions, making it a practical platform for coders to practice and master fundamental programming concepts.

5. Google Code Jam

Launched by Google, the Google Code Jam is an annual programming competition where professional and student programmers can choose their preferred language to tackle complex algorithmic challenges within a limited timeframe.

6. Dream In Code

Dream In Code (DIC) is an online community specifically designed for programmers and web developers. Members have free access to thousands of programming tutorials, code snippets, forum topics, and more.

7. Codeforces

Codeforces is an online programming platform where users can practice various problems, submit competition entries, and compete against submissions from other users.

8. UVa Online Judge

This online coding platform is maintained by the University of Valladolid in Spain. Here, you can find a variety of problems from past programming competitions, including ACM International Collegiate Programming Contests, and you can submit source code in multiple programming languages.

9. Python Challenge

Focused primarily on Python, the Python Challenge is a series of programming competitions. Although any language can be used to solve the problems, using Python will help enhance your understanding of the challenges.

10. Facebook Puzzles

As the name suggests, this set of programming puzzles is provided by Facebook. Users can submit solutions in various languages, including C++, C, Haskell, Java, Perl, PHP, Python, and Ruby.

11. ACM-ICPC

The ACM - ICPC is one of the largest programming competitions in the world, held annually. Organized by IBM for student teams, the competition focuses on algorithmic programming problems. Teams are selected from regional competitions to eventually participate in the world finals. Currently, two languages are supported: C/C++ and Java.

It's worth mentioning that there are also other great platforms such as IEEEXtreme, hackers.org, Timus Online Judge, and DWITE.
