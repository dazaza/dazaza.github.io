Words are lonely scars.

Thank heaven for letting me gain new feelings again. Although, in twenty or thirty years, I will probably feel it's naive, it is still a footprint.

Hearing, feeling, knowing, understanding.

I'm very bored recalling some past events.

A writer said that he didn't know why many of the modern texts were so limp and weak. Haha, that's really vivid. I don't know why either, but most of the time, words are powerless. There's too much self-pity, which seems disgusting. Sharing with others is even more sinful. My apologies to all viewers first. The sun is still fluffy and fragrant, we should be positive, we should be positive.

Every romance starts from unfamiliarity to familiarity, then to deep understanding.

When did your romance start?

In elementary school? You have similar family backgrounds, go to the same primary school, pursue the same things, like the little red flowers on the wall, the "excellent" marked on the homework book, or who is the best in the teacher's mouth. You read "aoe" together in class, draw pictures after class, you think that boy or girl looks good, and you want to play with her forever. Later, you start to look at her carefully, you find out she doesn't like drinking water, so you give her the fruit drink your mom made, she says it's sweet, and you both laugh.

In junior high school? You like similar clothes, it seems that all the clothes that look good in the whole school are like that. You admire his coolness when playing basketball, often imagine him as a character in an idol drama, you're indulged in it. You fantasize about growing old together. So, you recite poems together, do physics, chemistry, biology homework together. But teachers and parents keep emphasizing that puppy love is not good... You have a little sense of guilt in your heart, but seeing a full bag of small notes, you still feel indescribable happiness. It seems to prove another kind of existence.

In high school? You study in the same class, if you're cultural students, you work hard thinking about every problem in learning and the blind spots you don't pay much attention to. You often exchange some very difficult problems, sometimes, you put your breakfast on her desk, she smiles at you, her face without makeup shows a glow like fresh fruit. You secretly swear in your heart, I will marry this girl and stay with her for life. So you encourage each other to study, hoping to get into the same university.

In college? You have very different backgrounds, you meet by chance, understand each other more, one side sincerely pursues, and then you fall in love. When you step into an unfamiliar field, you find out how excellent this boy in front of you is, his basketball has won awards nationwide, his something else is extraordinary. He also hides a skill, playing a certain instrument very well. You begin to marvel at his excellence, and begin to marvel at the multifaceted and interesting nature of people.

Later, either you spend your life together, or you part ways. Then, there's another person who moves you, or the nth person. You find out that what you know about him is not all, not even a quarter of it. You start to marvel at the brilliance and multifaceted nature of every life. And you feel a little sorry for your situation.

Looking at classmates or yourself, presenting a state of "the trend of the world, union leads to division, and division leads to union", feels a bit helpless. Single? In love? Children? Marriage? They seem to be longer-term goals than the current ones like postgraduate entrance examination or scholarships, with a longer shelf life. For me, who's a bit messy, it's very difficult.

How to face it? Someone said that once a girl breaks up, she will shift her attention to some things within her reach.

To fill some inevitable gaps in life. I know, I'm not afraid, and I can accept it.

Take good responsibility for your own affairs, exercise bravely.
