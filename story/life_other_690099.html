Time is a flower with two lives

by lili1 on 2012-03-04 22:47:02

Years are like a double-flowered plant

When I feel uncomfortable or in a bad mood, I don't like to talk. Whoever talks to me, I find them annoying and I just want to be alone in silence. Every time this happens, I always tell myself to be happy. In reality, the gloomy feeling inside lasts for a long time, even when there's still a smile on my face. People say that women have some days each month where they feel down. But now I'm not in "those days" and yet my mood is still bad.

When I first entered the sales department, I secretly felt proud because work was quite free, not as busy as I imagined. Unfortunately, good times didn't last long, and now I've been driven crazy by "all the people in the country", wanting to curse every day.

Sometimes at around 8:30 in the morning, before I even reach the office, someone calls my mobile phone. Once I get to the office, the calls come one after another. It seems like "all the people in the country" are looking for you, as if I am a hotline. They ask for this and that repeatedly, demanding various certificates and reports. I've already given them countless times to countless people, but there are always people who keep asking. With constant calls, I can't focus on my work. But once I ignore the calls, the boss can't find me. He said, you should forward the calls to your mobile phone.

Because of my bad mood, everything looks unpleasant, which makes my mood even worse.

I often tell others that I love my job very much. I don't see it as just work; it's part of my life. But recently, due to my bad mood, I started to hate my job. Work makes me more irritable and impatient. I believe this is a vicious cycle, and if I don't stop and adjust, I will become increasingly repelled by my job.

In the evening, I went with my companions to B's company for Bible study. On the subway, I saw the back and side face of a woman with dyed yellow hair ahead of me. That woman was tall, with rough manners, and her side profile resembled a man. I used my gossipy specialty, left my companions, and rushed up to take a closer look. I was disappointed when I saw that it wasn't a man; I wasn't lucky enough.

Yesterday was the hundredth-day celebration for my cousin's son Yangyang. There were several children, all very playful. I think the more you interact with children, the more loving and childlike you become. My favorite little Yumi really likes me and wants me to hold her, which gives me great comfort.

During the banquet, we had an overseas video call with Jimmy and Sunny. Everyone said a few words to them. Through the lens, seeing so many of us gathered together enjoying delicious food and laughing, made Jimmy miss home even more and yearn for life in Shanghai. Auntie said she understands her son, she knows Jimmy is about to cry. Actually, we miss him too, and we all urged him in front of the camera to return to Shanghai for Christmas. Australia has small villas, cars, fresh fruits, and delicious seafood, but none of it compares to family in Shanghai. Looking at him through the lens, I also felt sad.
