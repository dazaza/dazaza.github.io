The Yellow Emperor lived by the Ji River and took Ji as his surname; the Flame Emperor lived by the Jiang River and took Jiang as his surname. Surnames and family names are two stages of human progress, and they are the products of civilization. The surnames of all Chinese people share a common root. About 5,000 years ago in the era of Fuxi, "surnames" were determined to be hereditary and passed down through the father. The so-called "rectifying surnames and family names, communicating matrimonial intentions, and establishing marriage customs," surnames were given the symbols of "distinguishing from animals and differentiating marriages," becoming an important marker of the progress of Chinese civilization. Every surname carries an interesting historical story, some we know, and others have faded into the memories of a few, becoming the bloodline bond that keeps the Chinese nation thriving. The newly published "Great Dictionary of Chinese Surnames" includes 23,813 surnames. Currently, over 7,000 Chinese surnames are still in use. The Chinese surnames Li, Wang, Zhang, Liu, and Chen can be considered the major surnames in today's "Hundred Family Surnames," with a combined population of 350 million. The sixth national population census was conducted at midnight on November 1, 2010, to clarify changes in our country's population in terms of quantity, structure, distribution, and living environment over the past ten years. And once again, names were placed at the top of the survey content. In this population census, many rarely known surnames surfaced. With the development of society and the migration of populations, families of various surnames have scattered across the land, becoming little known, and even losing contact between family members, becoming strangers. According to the general rule of surname evolution, the number of people with major surnames will increase, while the number of people with minor surnames will decrease, or even become extinct. Starting today, Chengdu Business Daily reporters will take you into the groups of those rarely known and rarely used surnames, examining their origins, differentiation, and characteristics, which can not only inform others but also facilitate communication and rebuild surname culture. The stories presented in the coming days are not just unfamiliar surnames, but also come from the brilliant history and distinctive culture of China over five thousand years. (Gus Bo) Story One: My surname is Bu, my name is Bu Tian Since last month when he came to an internship base in Chongzhou, the 19-year-old boy Bu Tian has brought quite a bit of life "seasoning" to his friends here. "Hehe, your name is Bu Tian, what's your relationship with Nüwa?" "Hey, do you have a younger brother named Bu Di?" "Bu Tian, it's raining again, come out and patch up the sky." Some classmates even call him "Nüwa" every time they speak. Facing these jokes without malice, the gentle Bu Tian laughs them off: "I've seen plenty of these jokes since I was a kid, they're all good friends playing around with me." "Nüwa, my clothes are torn, if I go home, my mom will definitely scold me, can you mend it with colorful stones for me?" On a winter day in 1991, after ten months of pregnancy, Ms. Dang gave birth to a pink and cute baby boy in Chengdu. Ms. Dang's husband surnamed Bu, and she had always wanted a girl, so after much deliberation, she decided to name her son "Bu Tian" after the mythological tale of Nüwa mending the sky. "The character 'Tian' is grand, it's beneficial for the child's growth." The mother's wish was very simple. Bu Tian said: "Every time I enter a new class, the teacher always remembers my name during roll call, and the students find it easy to remember me." Boy Bu Tian has a delicate appearance like a girl and a gentle personality, making him well-liked by teachers and classmates, which pleases Ms. Dang very much. Remember when he started junior high school in the first grade, a female teacher told the students: "We have a student named Bu Tian, does everyone know the story of Nüwa mending the sky?" Thus, the female teacher vividly narrated the mythological legend, captivating everyone's attention. After class, male student Bu Tian gained a loud nickname: Nüwa. "Nüwa, look, it's raining again, quickly mend the sky." "Nüwa, my clothes have a hole, if I go home, my mom will definitely scold me, can you mend it with colorful stones for me?" "Nüwa, accept me as your disciple, I will mend the sky in the future too." Whenever these jokes are mentioned, they always elicit laughter from the people around. However, regarding the meaning bestowed by the name, Bu Tian did not pay special attention to it initially. But during his high school year, once his class participated in a basketball game. Due to his poor performance, affecting the team's performance, they ended up with a terrible loss. After the game, the captain of the basketball team was somewhat unhappy and teased: "Bu Tian, you can mend the sky, yet you can't handle such a small matter, huh?" In life, his father sometimes also uses such words to motivate him, making Bu Tian start contemplating the expectations carried by his name. In 2007, Bu Tian entered a higher vocational college in Chengdu to study engineering cost estimation for three years of high school plus two years of specialized studies. For Bu Tian, who dreams of becoming a musician, this was certainly not his preferred choice. But after repeated discussions with his mother, he accepted her arrangement. After starting to study, he found this major very interesting and promising. "'Bu Tian' is just a joke, I'll start with small things and do them steadily and solidly." The post-90s Bu Tian requires himself thus. The origin of the "Bu" surname can be traced back to three sources Bu Tian was also curious and once asked his father: "Why do we have the surname 'Bu', it's so rare." Regarding this, his father wasn't clear either. Bu Tian's grandfather passed away early, and didn't talk much about genealogies and surnames. Bu Tian only knew that his family moved from Suining to Chengdu during his grandfather's generation. Until one accidental opportunity, his father met another uncle surnamed Bu. Because they shared the same surname, the two became close like brothers. From Uncle Bu, he heard some stories about the origin of the surname. Uncle Bu said, the surname "Bu" migrated from Guangxi and Guangdong regions during the late Ming and early Qing dynasties when Huguang filled Sichuan. A few years ago, when Uncle Bu's grandmother was still alive, she once told the grandchildren some stories. It is said that a pair of brothers surnamed Bu came to Suining area and agreed that one would settle on one side of the mountain and the other on the opposite side. One branch developed into Uncle Bu's hometown - the Bu surname family in Dashishi Township, Bai Ma Town, Suining. However, whether Bu Tian's family originated from this branch remains unclear. The reporter searched "Baidu Baike" and found that the surname "Bu" is pronounced in the fourth tone. Its origins can be traced back to three: First, it originated from the Bu State during the Yan Emperor period, belonging to the surname derived from the state name. As recorded in historical texts like "Tongzhi: Shi Zu Lue", "Bu, surname." Second, it originated from the Spring and Autumn period, belonging to the surname derived from the fief name. There was a place named "Bu" within the territory of the State of Zheng, which was granted to a meritorious official as a fief. The descendants of this official took the name of the fief as their surname, becoming another source of the Bu surname. Third, in ethnic minorities such as the Miao, there are also those who take "Bu" as their surname. Nowadays, in Chongqing City, Huaihua City, Zhenjiang County in Hunan Province, Suining City in Sichuan Province, Yangjiang City in Guangdong Province, Changzhou City in Jiangsu Province, there are people of the Bu surname. Reporter Cong Wang, Intern Journalist Mei Wang Story Two: To Express Gratitude, Mr. Xian Changed His Surname to Xian There are many ways to repay someone for saving your life. At 68 years old, Xian Shilun chose an unconventional way - he changed his surname. This extraordinary action made him one of the few people in Chengdu with the surname Xian. On November 6, Chengdu Business Daily reporter went to Tongqiao Village Group 14, Sangyuan Town, Qionglai City, and found Xian Shilun and his family, listening to him tell the story and origins of the Xian surname. The Xian Surname Was Originally the Shen Surname Xian Shilun's family began using the Xian surname from him, continuing for three generations now. Xian Shilun said that before, his family was surnamed Shen and lived in Datong Town, Qionglai City. During his grandfather's generation, the Shen family had weak offspring. His grandmother gave birth to multiple children, all of whom died young due to various reasons. According to local customs, Xian Shilun's grandfather worshipped a monk as his master and became a lay disciple. Afterwards, Xian Shilun's grandmother gave birth to several healthy children who grew up successfully. By the time of Xian Shilun's father, the Shen family faced more hardships. Thus, Xian Shilun's father sent him to a nunnery in Sangyuan Town for fostering. Under the careful care of the nuns, Xian Shilun, who was originally on the brink of death, survived. Later, Xian Shilun's father sent him to school. After graduating from junior high school, in order to express gratitude, Xian Shilun returned to Sangyuan Town to work as an accountant. Later, the nunnery fell into disrepair, leaving only a stone stele. To commemorate his benefactors, Xian Shilun changed his surname from Shen to Xian. The Shen Surname Originates from the Warring States Period According to the Shen family's account and historical records, the Shen surname originates from three sources: First, it comes from the feudal lord Xian Hou of the Shang dynasty. Located between today's southeastern Shaanxi Province and northwestern Henan Province. Second, it originates from the Ji surname, where the Shen family originally belonged to the Ji surname and was a branch of the Jin State royal family. The book "Guoyu: Jin Yu Si" records: When Duke Wen first ascended the throne, “Xu, Ji, Hu, Ji, Luan, Mo, Bai, Xian, Yangshe, Dong, Han, actually controlled nearby officials.” These eleven families were old families of the Jin State and pillars of Duke Wen's regime. The specific origins of the Xian family have been lost to history. Third, a son of the Zhou dynasty doctor Du Bo served as an official in the Jin State and was enfeoffed in the Xian region. His descendants then took the region's name as their surname, called the Xian surname. During the Spring and Autumn period, there were two generals in the Jin State named Xian Danmu and Xian You. Xian Danmu's son Xian Zhen served as the commander-in-chief of the central army, resided in Pucheng, and his courtesy name was Zizai. The entry in "Cihai" states: "Xian Zhen (? - 627 BC) was the chief executive of the Jin State, with a fief in Yuan (now northwest of Jiyuan, Henan), also known as Yuan Zhen. Initially serving as the deputy commander of the lower army, he later became the commander-in-chief of the central army, responsible for governing the country." Xian Zhen once led the Jin army to defeat the Qin army in the Xiaoshan area, and his son Xian Ju subsequently succeeded him as the commander-in-chief of the central army, defeating the Qin army again in Pengya. Ju had a son named Ke and a grandson named Guo, who successively assisted the rulers of the Jin State, including Duke Xiang, Duke Ling, Duke Cheng, and Duke Jing. Later, due to certain reasons, they were exterminated, and their descendants scattered. Now, there are descendants in places like Zhengning, Gansu, and Hejiang, Sichuan. Some also took the character "Ju" from Ju's name as their surname, called the Ju surname, passed down through generations. When used as a surname, the character "Xian" is pronounced [xián], similar to the sound of "xian". According to the Shen family’s recollection, they originally migrated from Hejiang, Sichuan to guard Qionglai, gradually growing larger and becoming one of the major surnames in Datong Town today. Another theory suggests that there was a mountain named "Shen" in Datong Town, hence the surname was taken from the place name, resulting in the Shen surname. Living Well Is Enough Xian Shilun said that his original name was Xian Shilun, believing this name was poetic, meaning discussing poetry. Unfortunately, when it was recorded, the registrar mistakenly wrote it as "Xian Shilun," and he could only make the best of it. Xian Shilun and his wife Zhou Guoying have four children: their eldest daughter is Xian Xuefeng; second daughter Xian Dan; third daughter Xian Jun, and youngest son Xian Jincheng. As the continuation of Xian Shilun's lineage, Xian Jincheng clearly hasn't thought about clan matters. For Xian Jincheng, living a good life is the most important thing in life. Xian Jincheng has only one daughter named Xian Min. According to local customs, in the future, when Xian Min grows up, gets married, and has children, her children will no longer bear the Xian surname. In other words, this line of the Xian surname will no longer continue. "Of the three unfilial acts, having no heirs is the greatest," this traditional concept, even Xian Shilun no longer regards as something that must be accomplished. Xian Shilun consoles himself like this: "I have adopted many godsons, who also follow the Xian surname... I won't force my son to have another son, times change, and concepts must change accordingly. Whether the lineage continues or not, it doesn't matter now. Those who are alive should live well. Since changing the surname was to express gratitude, remembering her for so many years is also a form of repayment."
