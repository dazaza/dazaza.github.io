Anshan City's Sun God players can't be stopped from chasing Messi and are determined to get Messi's jersey even by pulling strings.

by hgxj00887085 on 2012-03-03 01:30:42

Crown Betting Account Express - In the Champions League Round of 16 match where Barcelona (Weibo) defeated Bayer Leverkusen 3-1 away, a "scandal" occurred involving Leverkusen's two central defenders, Kadlec and Friedrich: From halftime onwards, they were in conflict over who would get the privilege of exchanging shirts with Messi. This led to an unpleasant atmosphere in the locker room. Crown's latest betting manager, Rudi Voller, was severely dissatisfied with the incident, criticizing them for neglecting their duties. He even assured the media and fans that he would confiscate the Messi shirt obtained by these "rebellious apprentices" and auction it off for charity.

During the week, Messi traveled with the Argentina national team to Bern for a friendly match against Switzerland. Upon arrival, fans surrounded the hotel, leading the hotel to call for additional security to avoid losing control of the situation. Messi is extremely popular in Switzerland, not only among fans but also among players. To prevent a repeat of the awkward star-chasing incident involving Leverkusen's Kadlec and Friedrich, Swiss media devised a "smart move." The country's largest newspaper, Blick, purchased several No. 10 Argentina Messi jerseys and distributed them to the 11 Swiss players on the field. After all, national teams are different from clubs; players shouldn't "embarrass" themselves in front of their own people.

After the game, most Swiss players remained quite restrained. However, journalists noticed that Messi entered the locker room shirtless. So, who got his jersey? It turns out an unexpected event did occur. Argentina defeated Switzerland 3-1, and as the referee blew the final whistle, Swiss defender Philippe Senderos approached Messi, pointing at his shirt to indicate a jersey exchange. Messi somewhat hesitantly nodded, and subsequently, the two exchanged jerseys in the locker room corridor!

Post-match, Senderos admitted in the mixed zone, "I took Messi's jersey. Thanks to my former teammate Cesc Fabregas, I managed to secure Messi's shirt through this connection." "Will Messi's jersey be confiscated?" A journalist worried that Senderos' acquired jersey might meet the same fate as those obtained by Leverkusen's Kadlec and Friedrich, being auctioned off by the team. Senderos laughed and replied, "No, it will become part of my private collection."

Chasing stars, especially chasing Messi's star, cannot be fully prevented just by trying to stop it. Senderos walked away with Messi's jersey, but before that, someone else from the Swiss side had already planned to approach Messi. After the match, a young ball boy rushed onto the field directly towards Messi. His name is Eliaq, aged 15, a player for Young Boys Bern and selected for the Switzerland U15 national team. However, his intention wasn't for a jersey; his goal was simpler: to shake hands with Messi and take a photo together!
