Two, Pathogenesis of nail fungus:

Toenails or fingernails turning grayish-red, commonly referred to as "nail fungus." This is actually a nail disease caused by fungal infections, known in modern medicine as tinea unguium. Fungal infections of the nails can present in various forms, most commonly grayish-red, but also brown, gray-brown, or dark brown. Simultaneously, the nails may thicken, become brittle, hollow out in the middle, or become uneven in length or incomplete. Some cases may involve inflammation of the nail groove, with local redness and swelling. Individuals with hand or foot fungus are more prone to contracting nail fungus. Most fungi causing hand and foot fungus can attack finger or toenails, leading to nail fungus. Generally, the development of nail fungus is a slow process, closely related to the type, duration, and severity of hand and foot fungus. Clinically, the most stubborn types of hand and foot fungus are the scaly and hyperkeratotic types, which more frequently lead to nail fungus.
