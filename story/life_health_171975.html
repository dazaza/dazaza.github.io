Misunderstanding 2: No need for sun protection while swimming

The pool is full of cool water, and the skin doesn't need sun protection when surrounded by water.

Expert clarification: This is completely wrong. First, the water in the swimming pool contains bleaching powder (chloride) and salt, both of which are alkaline. They can destroy the hair cuticle and the stratum corneum of the skin, making the hair rough, tangled, and the skin dry. Second, we need to see whether the swimming place we choose is outdoor or indoor. If it's outdoor, then we must choose a good waterproof sunscreen to apply on our body because the reflection rate of ultraviolet rays on the water surface is almost 100%. In other words, swimming in an outdoor swimming pool means withstanding double tests compared to exercising under the sun.

Misunderstanding 3: Homemade masks benefit the skin

With the arrival of summer, the variety of vegetables and fruits naturally increases. Therefore, many women use vegetables or fruits to make homemade masks, believing that this is both economical and natural, and should be very beneficial to the skin.

Expert clarification: Improper use of natural homemade masks can cause allergies. Experts believe that some foods indeed have whitening and spot-removing effects when applied to the face. However, the skin's resistance to the external environment varies in different seasons. Especially during seasonal changes, the skin barrier function decreases significantly, making it easier to develop skin allergic reactions.
