A 12-Year-Old Girl Gives Birth to a Baby Boy, Should We Ponder the Implications? On Thursday, May 27, 2010 at 22:22 - A 12-year-old girl gives birth to a baby boy, should we contemplate the consequences? [Original 2010-05-27 20:18:34] Font size: Large Medium Small. According to reports from the Dongguan Maternal and Child Health Hospital on March 31st by reporter Yingmin Li, a twelve-year-old girl named "Xiaona" recently gave birth to a baby boy in Dongguan city, Guangdong Province. The child was quietly given away, and Xiaona left the hospital with her parents.

Xiaona is from Wuhua in Meizhou, and her parents work in the areas of Boluo and Dongguan. She was studying in the sixth grade at a private elementary school. When Xiaona's mother noticed her daughter bleeding from below, she thought it might be due to some illness and took her to the Dongguan Maternal and Child Health Hospital for consultation early in March. Upon examination, the doctors were shocked to find out that this dark, thin, and still undeveloped twelve-year-old girl was not only pregnant but also full-term and about to give birth.

After an urgent operation, Xiaona successfully delivered a baby boy. Xiaona's parents revealed that the father of the baby boy might be the older brother of one of Xiaona's classmates, who is a student in the third year of junior high school. Whenever Xiaona visited her classmate’s home, this boy would often close the door and "play" with Xiaona inside. No one suspected anything serious was happening. After Xiaona became pregnant, her family did not report the matter to the authorities.

According to reports, twelve-year-old Xiaona is the youngest mother in Dongguan in recent years. Her case is not an isolated incident; every year, there are over a thousand cases of underage pregnancies in Dongguan. The trend of younger minors giving birth is indeed worrying.

Recently, a 12-year-old female student from the second generation of migrant workers suddenly gave birth to a baby boy in the toilet at her school. Last night, I also saw a news program on Suzhou Television reporting that a 14-year-old boy had several sexual partners of the same age and already had three children. For this reason, we do not know why parents, school teachers, and social organizations show such little concern for the lives of minors. How will these children who have already borne children face the harsh competition of society? And who will take care of and raise these illegitimate children born at a time when they shouldn't have been conceived? These are all social problems that cannot be ignored or avoided!

As far as I know, middle school students, especially those from urban migrant populations, are largely neglected by their parents who are preoccupied with making a living. Due to insufficient attention from families, schools, and society, there are not only common examples of children accidentally falling into ponds during summer and winter vacations, but also particularly during the period when boys and girls are sexually confused. If families and society do not care for and guide them properly, it can easily lead to sexual accidents, causing irreparable harm to the physical and mental health of children and leaving significant societal aftereffects. Moreover, when problems arise, everyone tends to cover them up or shirk responsibility, treating them as shameful matters.

For example, according to relevant parties, the aforementioned 12-year-old girl from Hangzhou who gave birth in the school toilet reportedly became pregnant under non-consensual circumstances. However, whether this is truly the case is questionable:

1. Parents, relatives, and school teachers deliberately covering up dishonorable actions is equivalent to evading responsibility for their own mistakes.

2. This indirectly protects the estimated minor father of the baby, preventing the incident from escalating.

3. Shifting from public handling to internal handling may seem beneficial in resolving major issues into minor ones, but if parents and school teachers handle things superficially, it might not solve the root problem so easily. In fact, the number of similar incidents silently resolved behind the scenes could be even more alarming.

I believe that such social problems should be handled and resolved publicly, even if it involves the privacy and protection of minors. Paying more attention to details does not necessarily mean creating psychological pressure or harm to the parties involved. On the contrary, concealing the truth and avoiding medical treatment by suppressing the resolution of the issue at its inception is an intangible harm to other minors. Here, I, Guoping Lu, call upon parents, teachers, and social organizations to pay more attention to the lives of minors on a daily basis. I also advocate for an integrated management system from family to school and society to prevent blind spots in supervision. Furthermore, I specifically urge families, schools, and cities that have experienced "scandals" not to be evasive but to confront their mistakes, take responsibility bravely, and comprehensively analyze the root causes of the problems to achieve the goal of joint management and complete resolution.

Therefore, let us start with the "Toilet Baby Boy Gate" incident involving the 12-year-old girl from Hangzhou! Perhaps the police, women's federations, education bureaus, media follow-ups, and public suggestions can help ensure that such incidents do not occur again.
