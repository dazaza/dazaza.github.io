Secrets to Getting Your Submission Approved and Officially Recommended

by longersing on 2009-08-30 15:02:17

Chinaz provides a great exchange platform for webmasters and is also a place for them to showcase their talents. Today, Chinaz will teach you how to submit articles that pass the review and get recommended to the homepage of the official website. Some experience may be helpful to everyone.

1. Which manuscripts will not pass the review?

1. Articles with replaced URLs from others' articles; this is an extremely disrespectful behavior towards copyright.

2. Articles combined from multiple sources to achieve pseudo-originality.

3. Articles with chaotic structure and unclear expression.

4. Articles unrelated to existing columns; for example, extensive discussions on satellite products or stock market predictions.

5. Articles attacking web developers or IDCs for personal gain; (if sufficient evidence and reasonable complaints are provided, we will not only pass the review but also recommend it highly).

6. Sensationalist articles without factual basis.

7. Articles that obviously mislead others; these are mostly SEO optimization articles or new site experience articles. If the article's viewpoints have not been rigorously fact-checked, distributing them to other webmasters is an extremely irresponsible behavior.

8. Public relations soft articles; some webmasters or online media professionals use soft articles for paid services or promote private servers, pornographic illegal websites. Although the articles may be original, they lack readability.

9. Articles exceeding timeliness; for example, submitting an article from 2007 to our site.

2. Why was my submission deleted?

Your submitted manuscript might fall into one of the nine points mentioned above that do not pass the review. To ensure the progress of the editor's review and reduce the load on the database, we will delete such manuscripts. Of course, this does not mean you cannot resubmit the article. Since each reviewing editor has different standards and understandings, if you believe your article is worth recommending, we suggest you resubmit it and make special annotations at the beginning of the article.

3. Can I submit a reprinted article?

To a certain extent, we do not recognize the so-called concept of "copyright transfer." In current information platforms, many websites choose this submission method, encouraging users to reprint others' articles in large quantities and leave their own URLs for self-promotion. We believe this is both disrespectful to the platform and the original author.

However, the spirit of the Internet is sharing. Without reading and passing among netizens, good articles and viewpoints would not be discovered by everyone. Reading not only enhances oneself but is also a kind of joy.

Therefore, if you think an article is worth recommending, we suggest you leave relevant information at the end when reprinting the article.

4. How to get a homepage recommendation?

As known to all, we adopt relatively strict review standards. Once it is found that the article appears earlier in search engines or even the original author is not the submitter, the submitted manuscript will be directly deleted. (For this, we adopt a non-differentiation principle unless strong evidence is provided. Otherwise, we will not notify the submitter and directly delete the article.)

To get a homepage recommendation, the following three conditions must be met:

1. Strong readability; for example, the author's personal experiences are rich and worth reading.

2. Has a certain degree of guidance; for example, some SEO articles have undergone rigorous factual verification or some CMS usage tips.

3. Has a certain degree of controversy; truth becomes clearer through debate. Without discussion, there is no progress. Let everyone judge who is right or wrong.

4. Reflects the joys and sorrows of individual webmasters, focusing on personal webmasters.

Articles meeting two of the above four conditions can retain the right to superlinks. We believe such articles are worth recommending and worth using our website to help authors improve external link weight.

PS: Many articles from the SEO Tribe, including technical and discussion articles, have been recommended on the Webmaster Station. We strongly agree with one viewpoint: good original articles are never afraid of being unnoticed. Therefore, stick to original content and write good technical articles to truly win the masses.
