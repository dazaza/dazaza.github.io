Strange things have happened one after another in Fuquan Middle School. Should the principal with such bad records be dismissed?

by anonymous on 2012-03-08 20:37:11

Eliminate the "weird" status quo and embrace a new future.

-- An appeal from the faculty and staff of Fuquan Middle School

In our eyes, weird things keep happening at Fuquan Middle School:

1. Position allowances are tilted towards school leaders.

Middle-level cadres (directors) receive about 800 yuan per month as position allowance, while the principal, vice-principal, and deputy secretary receive more than 1000 yuan. Taking full-workload Chinese, mathematics, and English teachers as an example, they only get about 700 yuan each per month, and some teachers' position allowances are just over 200 yuan. Why aren't position allowances tilted towards the majority of front-line teachers?

2. During every holiday (winter and summer), we charge each student 100 to 300 yuan for tutoring fees. Sometimes we tutor for less than a week, sometimes for ten days to half a month, and sometimes for over 20 days. Apart from giving teachers tutoring fees and refunding a little to students (sometimes we tutor for just a few days, fearing complaints, so we symbolically refund a little to students.), the rest is a muddled account (how much is left over? It's not even accounted for.). Why is it a muddled account? Only the director of the grade level and the school can explain. For all these years, the detailed accounts have never been published in written form.

3. Each semester, we collect 300 yuan from each student as material fees. Previously, book dealers gave kickbacks to class advisors, but later Deputy Director Chen Quan of the Academic Affairs Office took over the unified ordering. Who received the kickbacks? How much did each person get? Only Chen Quan and heaven know.

4. Each semester, we also collect monthly exam fees and final unified exam fees from students, with each student required to pay 60-100 yuan or more (increasing with "inflation"). After paying for test papers, proctoring fees, and grading fees, where does the remainder go? Perhaps only Deputy Director Chen Quan and Vice Principal Yang Lai Xiong know.

5. Students pay 120-200 yuan per set for uniforms to the Student Affairs Office, which also involves kickbacks. In the end, who splits the kickbacks? Only Director Meng Li Li and relevant leaders know.

6. Class "commission fees". Every class advisor first extracts several thousand yuan from their class funds to give to the grade level director, then the grade level director gives part back to the class advisors, and the remaining majority is "handled" by the grade level director. Who does it "go to"? Only the grade level director can clearly explain.

7. At the end of each term, Director Yang Quan Guang of the General Affairs Office announces the income and expenditure accounts of Fuquan Middle School, but does not publish the detailed accounts. Why? The ins and outs of all the income and expenditure, no one knows. Should there be audit supervision? Should audit supervision follow the mass line? Is it truly effective supervision to only look at the accounts and not understand the actual situation from the masses?

8. How do canteen contractors obtain their contracts? Neither bidding nor tendering procedures were followed. Why don't they pay contract fees? The principal says it's to "reduce student burden and lower meal fees." Have the meal fees decreased? No, they've increased instead! What's the quality of the students' meals? Very poor! Don't believe it? Compare the food situation at Longmen Middle School (meal fees and quality), and ask the students, everything will be clear.

9. Fuquan Middle School has two small shops that operate day and night, facing around 3,500 consumers. The income wouldn't be "meager", would it? Why don't they pay contract fees? Who is actually operating them? Who are the true beneficiaries? Is there suspicion of using public property for personal gain? Is there suspicion of embezzling public property? Are there acts of corruption? Are there collusions inside and outside?

10. What donations has the school received? What are the amounts? Where have they been used? How much remains? The accounts have never been published, let alone the detailed accounts. In short, teachers haven't used a single cent of the donations.

11. Yang Lai Xiong of Fuquan Middle School has a long list of misconducts but is still holding an important position for a long time. Let's present some examples for our current leaders to examine:

(1) On a certain day, he fought with a young man over a tricycle seat, resulting in being hospitalized after being beaten by the young man, causing Fuquan Middle School to suspend classes for half a day. The police caught the young man and came to the school to handle the matter, but was surrounded by students, tearing the shell of the jeep! The public security authorities couldn't carry out their work, normal teaching order was disrupted, and the social impact was terrible!

(2) Harassing waitresses at "Yifang Shui", being beaten by social personnel.

(3) Harassing women at "Ocean Nightclub", being beaten by the husband of the woman.

(4) Harassing waitresses at "Jinyi Huihuang", being beaten by social personnel.

(5) Having sexual relations with a Hou surname female teacher at "Qingming Tea House", being discovered by the waitresses, and settling with a 300 yuan "cleaning fee".

(6) Having affairs with multiple female teachers at the school.

(7) Beating Lu surname faculty members and Ye surname teachers, and others criticized by him.

(8) Beating Vice Principal Wang Guobang of Fuquan Third Middle School.

(9) Illegally ordering tutoring materials, now stored in the library are 5000+ obsolete books and 48 boxes of tapes.

(10) In late February 2012, Yang Lai Xiong had a conflict with a taxi driver and beat the driver, was taken to the police station for handling, and went berserk drunk, getting handcuffed by the police.

"Can such a person with such a bad record be promoted to principal?"

12. Fuquan Middle School has a history of over a hundred years, right? Teacher strikes are still a novelty. Are there too many troublemakers? No. So what are the factors that induce strikes?

One factor is: the school wants to implement a performance-based salary plan (discussed for about two weeks, with over 90% of the faculty and staff opposing it), and the principal said at a meeting, "Whoever reports issues beyond their rank will have an unsatisfactory annual evaluation," clearly intending to forcibly implement this plan. This plan was drafted by Yang Lai Xiong and Jin Yanhua. According to this plan, school leaders and 51 head teachers will share half of the school's performance-based salary (full workload teachers are 1, less than full workload are 0.8, middle-level leaders are 1.2, vice principals are 1.8, and principals are reportedly 3). Question: What kind of performance is this? Does it comply with the central government's policy on performance-based salaries? Performance-based salaries are meant to motivate frontline employees. The school's plan completely satisfies the interest demands of a small group. That's why everyone requests the finance department to distribute the full performance-based salary without involving the school!

Another factor is: low leadership quality, selfishness, money above all, autocratic style, forming cliques, suppressing those who don't listen, and faculty and staff have no normal channels to reflect reasonable demands. Strikes are a last resort.

The above are various "weird" situations at Fuquan Middle School.

Our appeal: Eliminate the "weird" status quo and embrace a new future.

1. Investigate and punish various corrupt practices and money-first behaviors, eliminate the dregs of the education industry.

2. Strengthen financial auditing work, eradicate campus pests, and let sunlight illuminate the campus.

3. Supervise school leaders, prevent abuse of power, and effectively protect people's livelihood.

Faculty and Staff of Fuquan Middle School
