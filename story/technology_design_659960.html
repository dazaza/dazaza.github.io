Grease fume purifier. A policeman from another place caught the wrong person, who was taken away by the local police and regarded as a robber.

by yyjhdf1377 on 2012-02-07 09:58:31

Yangcheng Evening News, reported by journalist Tieyang Xie: While walking on the road, a low-altitude emission oil fume purifier was suddenly abducted by three people into a car. They robbed his bank card and forced him to reveal his password. The victim thought he was being mugged! It turns out that he was mistakenly identified as a criminal by out-of-town police officers who were in town investigating a case. At around 2 p.m. on November 29th, Mr. Xiong, who does business in Houjie Town of Dongguan City, encountered such an incident. On the 30th, the Houjie police reported that the three men were indeed plainclothes policemen from Heilongjiang Province who had come to Dongguan on a mission. After realizing their mistake, the policemen apologized to Mr. Xiong and dealt with the aftermath according to relevant regulations.

At around 2 p.m. on the 29th, after finishing a bank transfer at Mingfeng Square in Houjie Town, Mr. Xiong was forcibly pulled into a car by three strangers in civilian clothes. They then demanded the password for his bank card. Mr. Xiong's cousin, Mr. Hong, told reporters: "At the time, my cousin thought he was being robbed and struggled fiercely, resulting in a physical altercation with the abductors." During this time, the three men did not disclose their identities. Thinking quickly, Mr. Xiong pretended to have a heart attack, causing the three men to take him to the hospital. Mr. Hong added that once at the hospital, Mr. Xiong discreetly asked a nurse to call the police while the three men were distracted.

Upon receiving the report, the Houjie police immediately took Mr. Xiong and the three men back to the police station. It was then revealed that the three men were police officers from Heilongjiang who had come to Dongguan to apprehend a fraud suspect.

Subsequently, the reporter verified this information with the Houjie Police Division. A relevant leader from the command center of the Houjie Police Division stated that after receiving the report, they promptly confirmed the identities of the three men as police officers with the Heilongjiang police and also verified the fact that these three officers had come to Dongguan to capture a telecommunications fraud suspect. Contrary to what Mr. Xiong's family claimed about "finding a nurse to call the police at the hospital," the Houjie police explained that the only contact they received was a phone call for help from a Heilongjiang police officer surnamed Dong. Furthermore, it was clarified that during the incident, the Heilongjiang police officers did show their police identification to Mr. Xiong.

When the reporter subsequently requested to interview the three Heilongjiang police officers, they declined, citing that interviews needed approval from their superior department.

Afterward, the reporter repeatedly sought confirmation from Mr. Xiong regarding key issues such as "whether it was Mr. Xiong who called the police at the hospital or if the Heilongjiang police actively reported the incident" and "whether the police showed their credentials." However, Mr. Xiong himself never appeared. His cousin, Mr. Hong, replied that the matter had been resolved and that Mr. Xiong did not wish to be interviewed.

By the time the reporter completed the article, the Houjie police responded that the three Heilongjiang police officers had already apologized to Mr. Xiong. Under the mediation of the Houjie police, Mr. Xiong and the Heilongjiang police reached a reconciliation agreement and signed a memorandum.

Police Announcement:

At 17:32 on November 29th, the Houjie Police Division received a call for assistance from a Heilongjiang police officer surnamed Dong. The caller requested assistance in sending a suspect to Renkang Hospital for emergency treatment. Upon arrival, the division officers learned that a Heilongjiang police officer named Dong Xiangjiang and two colleagues had captured a man suspected of telephone fraud named Xiong in Houjie Town. When captured, Xiong claimed to be ill, so Dong and his colleagues sent him to a hospital in Houjie Town for treatment. During his treatment, Xiong loudly claimed he was being robbed, prompting Dong to call 110 for help.

The responding police officers brought Xiong and Dong back to the Houjie Police Division. At the division, Xiong was emotionally agitated and complained that the Heilongjiang police had arrested the wrong person. The Houjie Police Division organized relevant departments to negotiate with Xiong and others in the petition room.

After investigation, it was confirmed that Xiong was not a suspect. On November 30th, after coordination by the Houjie Police Division, Xiong and the Heilongjiang police reached a reconciliation agreement and signed a memorandum, resolving the matter satisfactorily.
