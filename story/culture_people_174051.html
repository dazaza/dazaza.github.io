The most selfless love in the world

by lk0swbb on 2009-07-09 23:44:57

As in previous Spring Festivals, I came to my grandmother's house in the countryside early. And as before, I shouted "Grandma! Grandma!" from far away. The reason is that my father's mother passed away when he was young, and my grandfather also left us when I was a few years old. Therefore, my grandmother took care of me until I was 12 years old, so no matter when, my grandmother is the most important person to me. This year is different from previous years - this year, only my grandmother is at home. Although it is the first day of the lunar new year, my uncle and aunt are nowhere to be found.

When my grandmother heard it was me, she called my nickname and quickly came out to meet me. When I saw my grandmother for the first time, my feeling was - I wanted to cry. Because of half a year of university life, I grew taller and stronger; but my grandmother became much thinner and her back became more hunched. The only thing that prevented me from crying was - the look of expectation, happiness, and satisfaction in my grandmother's eyes didn't allow me to cry.

My grandmother led me into the hall, and then she was busy doing things like chopping firewood, taking oranges, taking sunflowers, etc. The busier my grandmother was, the more painful I felt inside. My grandmother is not used to being idle, so I had no choice but to help her.

But my grandmother wouldn't let me do anything, saying "I can't see my grandson because you study for a year or half a year! Come on, sit by the fire! There was a rare snowfall yesterday. Don't get cold after walking."

"Grandma, I'm really not cold. I'm young, I should be filial to you..." Before I could finish speaking, my grandmother interrupted me.

"What nonsense about filial piety! I've been through hardship all my life, just seeing you makes me happy."

Because I specifically brought some specialties from Henan - "Xinzheng jujube slices" from school for my grandmother, but when I came home, my grandmother was at Aunt Two's house, so I asked my grandmother if she had tasted the jujube slices I brought.
