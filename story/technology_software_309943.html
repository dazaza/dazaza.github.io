Ancelotti doesn't want to draw the Milan teams, Arsenal = weakest top team, Inter Milan = strongest second team

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-15 19:51:49

Ancelotti does not want to draw the Milan duo, Arsenal = weakest top team, Inter Milan = strongest second team. According to Sina Sports, Chelsea drew 3-3 with Everton in the last round of the league, and all three goals conceded were bizarre. Many fans at Stamford Bridge could not accept it and gave the players a whistling sound on the spot. Czech goalkeeper Petr Cech was partly responsible for the three goals conceded, but Cech expressed dissatisfaction with the whistling sound from the home fans in an interview with Chelsea's official website.

"I was very surprised to hear the whistles from the fans. This is the first home draw this season so far. Look at our previous performances in the league, Champions League, and League Cup, we have won all our home games, and scored so many goals. How can the fans react like this? I don't understand," Cech emphasized that the blame for conceding from set-pieces should not be pushed onto him alone. "We missed many first balls, but in set-pieces, it's not the responsibility of one person, it's a collective matter."

In contrast to the poor performance of the Blues' defense was Didier Drogba's double strike. However, as the Africa Cup approaches, the Daily Mail revisited the topic of whether Chelsea has a "Drogba dependency syndrome." But head coach Carlo Ancelotti was unwilling to admit it. The Italian insisted that Chelsea could score even without Drogba during the Africa Cup period. "At this stage, I believe in Drogba, he is very important to us, but I believe we can win without him in January."

A report by Sportlife may make Ancelotti more at ease: Drogba is trying to achieve "loyalty and filial piety". According to the Ivory Coast national team's preparation plan, coach Vahid Halilhodzic hopes that Drogba will return to the team two weeks before the start of the Africa Cup, i.e., at the end of December, but Drogba plans to convince Halilhodzic to extend the deadline, helping Chelsea get through the Christmas devil schedule before returning to the country. "I will talk with the national team coach and Ancelotti to find the best solution. It's difficult to leave the club, obviously, my salary is paid by Chelsea, and the difficulty of leaving for a month is unimaginable. But I think the club also understands, players have no choice when playing for their country because we must represent the national team. I want to stay at Chelsea, but I have to go to the national team, it's really not easy to make a decision. Fortunately, the club respects us, they are very respectful of African players, and hope the team can handle this matter well."

Regardless of how Drogba's Africa Cup schedule is arranged, Ancelotti's biggest wish is to see a healthy Drogba return and not come back injured, delaying the Champions League knockout stage. The draw for the last 16 of the Champions League will take place this Friday. The Daily Telegraph quoted La Gazzetta dello Sport, saying that Ancelotti looked forward to the draw and frankly said "preferably not to draw the Milan duo."

"In February, I don't want to face Leonardo at Stamford Bridge, if it really happens, there's nothing we can do. Things always happen on the pitch, for example, AC Milan met Kaká in the group stage, and if Chelsea draws AC Milan, it will have a very special meaning. From a technical point of view, the only problem if we draw Inter Milan is that they are very strong, I think Inter Milan is the strongest second-place team." Ancelotti knows Italian teams inside out, "it's very difficult to eliminate Italian teams."

Among the top teams, Ancelotti specifically mentioned Arsenal: "The only one lacking experience is Arsenal, a very young team. Compared to the lineup that eliminated AC Milan two years ago, Arsenal now lacks several key players, leaving only Fabregas in midfield, and losing Flamini and Hleb are significant losses. Among the second-place teams, I think three teams are as strong as the top teams: AC Milan, Inter Milan, and Bayern Munich, the other five teams are a level weaker."
