Stock Forum: Logistics Company Vanishes After Accepting Cargo for Delivery, Update: Three Merchants Suffer Another 40,000 in Losses

by tztcnwltg on 2012-03-01 15:37:37

A logistics company had just opened when it collected a batch of goods and then suddenly disappeared, leaving over 300,000 yuan in freight charges from 12 merchants unaccounted for (reported by our newspaper on the 24th).

Subsequently, three more readers called our newspaper, stating that they were also defrauded by the same logistics company, losing nearly 40,000 yuan in freight charges. Among them was a long-haul truck driver responsible for transporting goods for the logistics company, who was also a victim.

Three merchants were deceived, suffering a loss of 40,000 yuan.

Yesterday, reader Mr. Duan called our newspaper, saying his experience was identical to what was reported in our newspaper. He operates a dried goods business at a food market in Shijiazhuang. On the 18th, a middle-aged man with a Handan accent came to his store, negotiated the price of sunflower seeds, and took a business card, indicating he would contact him in a few days.

"Around 10 o'clock on the 21st, this man called me, ordering 3500 yuan worth of sunflower seeds and asked me to deliver the goods to a small truck inside the food market courtyard," Mr. Duan said. When he delivered the goods, he noticed a sign hanging in the middle of the small truck reading "Shijiazhuang transfer to Weixian, Daning, and Guantao, daily departures."

"He told me to find the truck driver at the parking place on the morning of the 23rd to collect the money, but I never saw the logistics company's truck again afterward." Mr. Duan expressed that the buyer and the logistics company's phone lines have been unreachable, and only after seeing our newspaper report on the 24th did he realize he might have been scammed as well.

"This is my first collaboration with that 'buyer' and the logistics company," Mr. Duan said. In the food market where he operates, two other merchants encountered the same situation. The total value of dry fruits, beverages, and other goods lost by the three amounted to about 40,000 yuan. Subsequently, the three merchants reported the incident to the police.

The truck driver worked but couldn't get paid for the freight.

After being taken away a large amount of goods by the "Handan buyer" a few days ago, Ms. Wang, who sells wires, and several other victims have been waiting at the freight station entrance, hoping for the appearance of someone from the logistics company. At around 9 o'clock on the 24th, she saw a man walking toward the gate.

"At one glance, I recognized him - he was the driver of the big truck!" Ms. Wang said, and everyone immediately surrounded him. Just as they wanted to ask him some questions, this truck driver claimed that he was temporarily hired by the logistics company. The truck belonged to him, and the logistics company boss instructed him to transport the goods to Daning, Handan. After unloading the goods, he was supposed to return to settle the freight charges. He himself had no idea that the logistics company had vanished.

Subsequently, everyone brought the driver to the Fukan Road Criminal Investigation Team of the Qiaodong Public Security Bureau in Shijiazhuang. Officer Li immediately questioned the driver and confirmed that the driver was also a victim. The officer explained that after preliminary judgment, this case was a premeditated fraud case, which has now been filed. Further investigation into the specific details of the case is still ongoing.

Text/Intern Reporter from Our Newspaper, Geng Shuo, Zhang Lei
