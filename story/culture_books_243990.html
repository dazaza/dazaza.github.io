Lone Hero of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau: The Tibetan Mastiff Author: Yang Zhijun

by jqrxass on 2009-09-29 11:38:15

The Tibetan Mastiff is a breed of plateau dogs evolved from the Himalayan giant ancient dogs that lived over ten million years ago. Under the pen of Yang Zhijun, the mastiffs such as the tiger-head snow mastiff King of Mastiffs, Gangri Senge, the black lion-head female mastiff Nari and its twin sister Guori, and the former blood-drinking king Dangxiang Luocha who later became Duojilaba, all complete their missions in the process of conquering and being conquered.

My father's way of thinking is that of a grassland person. In the eyes of the herders on the grasslands, wolves are despicable thieves, bullying the weak and fearing the strong, ungrateful and selfish. The Tibetan Mastiff is completely different; it is loyal to its master, righteous, and brave without fear. Wolves fight for themselves throughout their lives, while Tibetan Mastiffs fight for others throughout their lives. For wolves, food is heaven, and they kill only to survive. But for Tibetan Mastiffs, the way is heaven, and they fight for loyalty, righteousness, and duty. Wolves and Tibetan Mastiffs cannot be compared. Therefore, whenever my father evaluated those who liked to harm others, deprive others of their right to survival, engage in internal strife, or were treacherous and deceitful, he would always say: "That is a wolf." On a small booklet called "Citizen Moral Standards," he solemnly wrote a few words: "The standard of Tibetan Mastiffs."

Part One of "Tibetan Mastiff"

In the group of Tibetan Mastiffs guarding the territory on Qingguo Ama Grassland, there is usually a mastiff king in a leadership position. It must be male, very powerful and fierce, and must have established great merits in protecting the territory that both humans and dogs can recognize - killing many steppe wolves and snow wolves, killing many leopards and snow leopards, even injuring or killing Tibetan bears and wild yaks. In addition, they may have likely killed humans just like they would kill foxes - those who dared to intrude on the territory and challenge the owner. Unlike other animals, the birth of a mastiff king isn't necessarily the result of intense battles between Tibetan Mastiffs to determine dominance, because after long-term interactions and shared responsibilities and enemies, the mastiffs know who is the bravest, who is the wisest, and who is both wise and brave. With human recognition, everyone else follows suit and acknowledges the leader willingly.

Part Two of "Tibetan Mastiff"

The white lion Gaba Senge went mad. It had already realized that it could not bring the little white dog Gaga back to Grandpa Niema's house, so it went mad to the point where it didn't even recognize itself. The little white dog Gaga is mine, just mine. You say it's yours, would you dare eat it? But I dare. Don't forget that in the ancient traditions and ancestral habits, Tibetan Mastiffs have the practice of eating their own offspring: to prevent their children from falling into enemy hands and becoming meat for the wicked, those great Tibetan Mastiffs that value their reputation higher than the sky will often swallow their own children into their stomachs.

Part Three of "Tibetan Mastiff"

The call of the black-necked crane rang out brightly. The newborn sun pitifully shone upon the old earth. The king of Tibetan Mastiffs, the tiger-head snow mastiff on the earth, no longer ran briskly toward the sun. Its soul had ascended to heaven, and now, its flesh and bones would also ascend to heaven. When a group of vultures, which were both angels and fierce gods, saw the smoke of Sang offered by the herdsmen of the Muma Crane Tribe, reigning here, the big black mastiff Guori, who had kept vigil all night, licked the nose of the mastiff king and its throat torn apart by Gangri Senge one last time, weeping as it left. It was going back to Xiejigu, to tell the territorial dogs there: the mastiff king is dead.

Part Four of "Tibetan Mastiff"

Gangri Senge quickly stood up and walked forward, though walking slowly, it appeared exceptionally determined. The big black mastiff Nari followed it. The territorial dogs followed it. All the people present exclaimed and followed it. Both the dogs and the people knew that Gangri Senge was heading towards its master, the seven children of the Upper Ama region. They had been imprisoned in a secret place by the ghost-sending person Dachi. People did not know this place, but only Gangri Senge and the big black mastiff Nari beside it knew, and only these territorial dogs following knew. Relying on their sensitive sense of smell, they had already discovered that the seven children of the Upper Ama region were just ahead, in an underground ice cellar beneath the Dangxiang Great Snow Mountain.
