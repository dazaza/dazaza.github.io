Exploring the Benign Mechanism of Corporate MBO from Yutong Bus's MBO Reform

by juyaqufa003 on 2011-12-06 13:44:30

From Yutong Bus's announcement on May 22, 2001, it was learned that the original second largest shareholder, Zhengzhou No.1 Steel Plant, had signed agreements with Shanghai Yutong Venture Capital Co., Ltd. for the transfer of an 8.7% stake in Yutong Bus and entrusted these shares to the latter. The intention was to transfer the 8.7% equity held by Zhengzhou No.1 Steel Plant to Shanghai Yutong. Shanghai Yutong was specifically established to implement the MBO (Management Buyout) of Yutong Bus, with its chairman being Tang Yuxiang, the legal representative of Yutong Bus. However, due to guarantee issues related to Zhengzhou No.1 Steel Plant, the 8.7% stake was frozen by the Higher People's Court of Henan Province, preventing Tang Yuxiang’s MBO plan from being realized.

After Shanghai Yutong failed to acquire the Yutong Bus shares held by Zhengzhou No.1 Steel Plant, Zhongyuan Trust signed an agreement with Zhengzhou No.1 Steel Plant in February 2003 to take over this portion of Yutong Bus shares. Although the shares were still frozen at the time, the transfer proceeded smoothly through a process where the shares were placed under the trust management of Zhongyuan Trust before the transfer. Zhongyuan Trust became the second-largest shareholder of Yutong Bus, leading some market analysts to question the source of Zhongyuan Trust's funds and speculate that Zhongyuan Trust's acquisition of these shares was part of Tang Yuxiang's MBO strategy, though this speculation was never confirmed by relevant parties.

MBO followed by additional acquisitions

On December 7, 2004, Yutong Bus announced an acquisition report stating that Shanghai Yutong and Yutong Development acquired 100% of Yutong Group's equity via auction, thereby gaining indirect control over Yutong Bus. This made Tang Yuxiang and others the actual controllers of Yutong Bus. At this point, after several years of twists and turns, Yutong Bus's MBO was largely concluded.

At the time, when Tang Yuxiang was asked whether he had plans to replace Zhongyuan Trust as the second-largest shareholder, he stated, "This matter regarding Zhongyuan Trust acquiring Yutong Bus shares is unrelated to us; it's Zhongyuan Trust's own business." However, just half a year later, the acquisition was completed.

Yutong Group originally held 18.92% of Yutong Bus's shares, and the shareholding ratios of the second and third largest shareholders were significantly lower. Tang Yuxiang and others' control over Yutong Bus was already relatively stable. Acquiring the 8.73% stake held by Zhongyuan Trust for 107 million yuan only further solidified the outcome of the MBO.

Exploration of a sound MBO mechanism

In fact, the initial enthusiasm for MBO reforms demonstrated its positive effects on asset restructuring. However, the focus of debates surrounding many subsequent MBO cases centered on the price reasonableness of transferring state-owned assets through MBO, which is key to determining whether assets have been lost. Establishing a good information disclosure system for listed company MBOs, timely disclosing methods of pricing and sources of funding, can appropriately prevent disputes caused by MBOs and foster a healthy operational mechanism through social supervision.

In this regard, Yutong Bus has made active attempts. As a similarly questioned MBO reform action, in 2005, Yutong Company voluntarily disclosed the sources of acquisition funds and acquisition pricing to the media.

According to the explanation of the registered capital source of Shanghai Yutong, 838 employees of Yutong Bus jointly contributed 805.38 million yuan, with Tang Yuxiang, then the top leader of the company, contributing 300 million yuan. They all held shares in Yutong Bus, with cash contributions. The acquisition price also eliminated external doubts about the “cheap sale” of state-owned assets. Before the acquisition, Yutong Group's total net assets amounted to 137 million yuan, and after completing the MBO reform, the government received a total of 76 billion yuan from the equity transfer to the management team, representing a premium of 28.48%.

This aligns with the investigation results from the Henan Provincial Department of Finance. According to their report on the Yutong MBO incident, it was concluded that Shanghai Yutong was controlled by the employee shareholding association of Yutong, hence the employees' high enthusiasm for funding, allowing them to complete the capital-raising task within a month.
