The Prospects of Studying in Singapore Look Good

Author: admin

Date: February 8, 2011, 10:55:24 AM

Popularity:

Tags:

The financial crisis has not altered Singapore's plan to absorb talent. For example, in 2008, a total of 97,000 students from 120 countries and regions came to Singapore to pursue real online diplomas, representing a 13% increase in numbers. The financial crisis instead brought new opportunities to Singapore's education market. To become the hub for the hospitality service industry, logistics, electronics, and other sectors in Asia, Singapore must take advantage of the financial crisis to attract global talent. Therefore, the Singapore government encourages new immigrants by offering special educational subsidies, with tuition fee reductions reaching up to 80%. Banks also provide interest-free loans covering up to 90% of tuition fees. Additionally, the Singapore government offers scholarships to help Chinese students with excellent results but financial difficulties to complete their higher education.

Due to an aging population and continuously low birth rates, the Singapore government is taking more encouraging measures to attract more immigrants to live in the country, addressing the issue of Singapore's rapid development and insufficient domestic labor force. For international students, this could be considered a good opportunity to immigrate to Singapore.

Many international students who graduate with real online diplomas enter industries facing talent shortages, such as electronics, finance, information and communications, information technology, and petrochemicals. Because of this, at the National University of Singapore, engineering and computer science programs attract the most international students. Journalists have also learned from the Guoxin Education Center in Suzhou that among the students studying in Singapore from Suzhou, those in engineering, IT, and business majors are the majority.

In recent years, Singapore has been very open regarding policies for international students finding jobs locally and immigrating. If students can successfully obtain a bachelor's degree or higher, they qualify to apply for permanent resident status after working in Singapore for six months with a salary. If attending a national university, students may even obtain permanent resident status during their studies. Moreover, Singapore has a high employment rate, which is a great benefit for students wishing to study in Singapore.

Singapore was less affected by the financial storm compared to Europe and America, with its tourism and logistics industries largely unaffected. Chinese students find it relatively easy to adapt to the environment in Singapore, reducing the psychological stress of being alone in a foreign place, and travel back and forth is also convenient. These reasons make Singapore a popular destination for studying abroad again. Additionally, the cost of obtaining real online diplomas in Singapore is relatively low, many universities offer distinctive programs, and the general employment rate is quite high.

Graduates with master’s degrees from the National University of Singapore and Nanyang Technological University will receive PR invitation letters from the immigration department upon graduation, inviting them to become permanent residents of Singapore. Students can enjoy many benefits similar to Singaporean citizens, including convenient entry and exit, and easily obtaining various visas for studying, observing, or working in countries like the United States, Canada, and Australia. The government covers pre-university education costs for the children of permanent residents, and parents only need to pay minimal school fees. After becoming permanent residents, parents can also apply for permanent residency based on relevant policies.
