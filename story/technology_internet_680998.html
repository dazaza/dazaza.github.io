Owner spent 130,000 yuan to modify a small truck into a camper (in pictures)

The appliances in the carriage are all available. Photographed by Long Yudan. A small truck hides secrets within.

Meals can be cooked, one can sleep, do laundry, surf the internet, and watch TV inside the vehicle.

The cost of the car with decoration is less than 130,000 yuan.

Netizens jokingly call it cheaper than affordable housing.

Kunming has seen the most amazing modified RV! This mobile RV transformed from a small truck is like a tiny home. Although it's only 8.5 square meters, its configuration is quite complete. Beds, wardrobes, LCD displays, water heaters, kitchens, and bathrooms are all present. Inside the vehicle, one can cook, shower, sleep, watch TV, and surf the internet.

According to the owner Mr. Feng, the total cost of the RV including the car and decoration is less than 130,000 yuan. Due to its low cost and full functionality, it has been humorously called the most amazing RV ever by netizens.

Yesterday morning, a Weibo user posted that Kunming has seen the most amazing modified RV. This mobile RV transformed from a small truck has a usable area of 8.5 square meters, like a small home, where one can cook, sleep, surf the internet, and watch TV. The total cost of this RV, including the car and decoration, is less than 130,000 yuan. Because of its low cost and full functionality, it has been humorously called the most amazing RV ever by netizens. Is there really such a vehicle in Kunming? And what kind of person is the owner? Yesterday afternoon, the reporter found the owner Mr. Feng, trying to uncover the modification process of this RV.

Kunming Car Owner

Upgrading a Truck into an RV

Yesterday morning, a Weibo user posted about a very cool thread on a well-known local Kunming forum recently. The post claimed that a certain Kunming car owner, in response to high housing prices, refused to become a mortgage slave and decided to transform a van into a warm and fully equipped RV. This Weibo user also shared a link with detailed pictures showing the internal decoration of the RV transformed from the truck.

"Small as a sparrow, but with all the vital organs," it's not hard to find from the pictures posted by netizens that this modified RV is fully equipped with living supplies. Inside the vehicle, furniture, bunk beds, kitchen, and bathroom are all present, along with an LCD display. Inside the vehicle, besides being able to cook, shower, and sleep, one can also watch TV and surf the internet.

Subsequently, the reporter found the original post on this forum, where the poster identified himself as a true 80s version RV enthusiast. He had always liked RVs and wanted to own one of his own. To achieve this dream, he spent four months transforming a small truck into an RV with a usable area of 8.5 square meters. After completing the transformation, he felt the car was very practical and decided to share it online with other RV enthusiasts.

Regarding the decoration and configuration inside the vehicle, many RV enthusiasts commented asking about the cost of the modified car. The poster responded to the netizens: "This car costs less than 130,000 yuan in total, suitable for 80s generation workers who cannot afford to buy a house, allowing them to travel when they have time and avoid crowded trains during the New Year, making it very convenient to drive home for the holidays."

"130,000 yuan isn't even enough to buy a house in Zijun Village in Kunming; this is undoubtedly a good way to address high housing prices." After viewing the pictures, some netizens posted their reflections. Many others praised the owner's skills and abilities, calling this RV "much cheaper than buying an expensive house," while also considering it "the true economical and practical housing."

Experience

RV "Small as a sparrow, but with all the vital organs"

Is there really such an amazing RV in Kunming? Yesterday morning, according to information provided by netizens, the reporter met the vehicle on the side of the Second Ring Road, where the owner, Mr. Feng, was surfing the internet inside. If it weren't for the small satellite dish revealing the secret, it would be hard to notice that this was a modified RV.

The space inside the vehicle after modification is somewhat narrow. However, the reporter discovered that one could not only watch TV but also connect a 3G card for fast internet access, with all kinds of daily necessities available.

The modifications start from the cargo area. The owner installed a gas water heater near the front right side of the cargo area close to the hood. Right next to the water heater on the outer end of the cargo area, there is a small window with an exhaust fan installed. Upon entering the cargo area, one can see that the entire floor is made of solid wood flooring and bamboo products. Opposite the cargo door, there is a tall shoe rack which not only holds shoes but also several potted plants.

To the right of the shoe rack is the kitchen, equipped with a sink and cabinets. Inside the cabinets, there is a transformer and an inverter. Next to the kitchen is a shower room where one can take a shower or use the restroom. In this space of less than 1 square meter, there is also a small washing machine, and the water source comes from a 400-liter large water tank at the base of the cargo area.

To the left of the shoe rack is the area for going online and eating. Behind the shoe rack is a large wardrobe occupying most of the cargo space. Next to the wardrobe is a storage cabinet containing the computer host and power outlets. Below the cabinet, there is a 38-inch LCD display hanging, which can receive TV signals via an antenna. Across from the TV is an 118-liter refrigerator. Near the tail of the cargo area is a bunk bed made of solid wood with sliding glass. The owner said this is the bedroom's floor-to-ceiling window. On the wall beside the bed, there is a foldable and compressible small table for eating and working.

If the interior modifications are for convenience in life, then the exterior modifications are to provide support for all the household appliances inside. To prevent rusting, the owner wrapped a layer of oilcloth around the outside of the cargo area. On the roof, the owner placed a solar panel, ensuring that the electricity needs of the vehicle can be met as long as there is sunlight. Additionally, at the rear end of the cargo area, there is a red generator that can produce electricity independently.

"There are four power generation facilities on the vehicle. Besides the solar panels and the generator, there is also a set of batteries providing power," Mr. Feng said. If there is an external power source where the vehicle is parked, one can directly plug in the external power source to supply electricity to the RV and charge the batteries simultaneously, making it very convenient.

Owner

Modifying the Vehicle is Just a Personal Hobby

"I've always wanted to own my own RV, and there are many people back home who modify vehicles," Mr. Feng said yesterday afternoon. This truck-based RV took him more than four months to handcraft entirely.

Mr. Feng is originally from Heilongjiang Province. He came to Yunnan at the age of 18, later married and settled in Kunming, purchasing a house near the Second Ring Road. He now has a 4-year-old son attending kindergarten. Mr. Feng mentioned that he only attended technical school but has always enjoyed handmade crafts. He once modified his own bicycle and motorcycle according to his own ideas. In Kunming, Mr. Feng also has a stable job. Since he wasn't busy recently, he bought this small truck in February and started modifying it.

"Initially, I planned to spend 140,000 yuan, but in reality, it only cost less than 130,000 yuan. Compared to current housing prices, this is indeed very cost-effective. With this amount of money, you can't even buy a kitchen in Kunming within the Second Ring Road," Mr. Feng said. The new car plus registration only cost over 80,000 yuan, and the rest was material costs, with the most expensive items being the electrical appliances and generator. There wasn't much design involved in the modification; he just referenced some methods introduced on forums online and his own ideas before starting construction. The floors and home appliances were purchased in Kunming, while the inverter used for conversion and the stainless steel cabinets were bought locally.

After the vehicle was modified, Mr. Feng took his family on a trip to western Yunnan to experience the functions of the car. "At Dali Harbor, many tourists who were originally planning to take a cruise boat didn't leave when they saw our car. They were busy coming over to take photos with us," Mr. Feng said. At that time, he and his wife were preparing breakfast on the car, which caught the attention of the tourists, and they requested to board the car for a tour. In Kunming, if he suddenly drove the car into an unfamiliar neighborhood, it would also attract a crowd. In the past month, Mr. Feng's family of three has almost lived and eaten on the car, returning home only three or four times.

Regarding his beloved car, Mr. Feng said that within the national scope, it is not yet the most impressive. He once saw online a fellow netizen from another province showcasing a similarly modified RV, which could expand its cargo area when stationary, reaching 17.5 square meters, doubling the space of his vehicle.

"I'm not a mortgage slave; modifying this car is purely out of personal interest." Regarding the comments from netizens, Mr. Feng said he doesn't quite understand them. His internet skills aren't great, and the photos online were posted by his elder brother far away in his hometown. Originally, he just wanted to exchange experiences after the modification with netizens, but unexpectedly, it attracted media interviews.

"First, I'll experience it for a while. If any netizens need it in the future, I'd consider selling it and modifying another one myself." Mr. Feng said that the process of modifying this car wasn't complex; one just needs to have some professional knowledge to handle it. If the policy allows, he plans to make modifying cars into a career. (By Li Jiajian, Gao E, Zhang Meng)
