Teenager buys pet online and gets protected animal, rhesus monkey

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 14:19:47

Currently, because of Xiaojuan's ignorance and lack of awareness, the police only criticized and educated her. The two rescued baby macaques have been sent to Jiufeng Forest Park, and this case has been officially transferred to the Wuhan Forest Police for further review. Yesterday, it was learned that Liang had been criminally detained on suspicion of transporting and selling endangered wildlife. Liang was stunned; he had no idea such serious consequences would occur.

A 16-year-old "post-90s" girl named Xiaojuan (a pseudonym) spent nearly 7,000 yuan to buy a monkey online as a pet. When she went to the hospital to visit her friend, she bragged about her "little treasure" to her friend. Unfortunately, there were eavesdroppers, and Officer Duan Lian from the Donghu New Technology Development Zone police happened to hear it. This is a clear case of illegal sale of protected national animals, which is against the law. He immediately intervened in the investigation and lured out the online seller of macaques, Liang. An 80-year-old scavenger made nude donations of 270,000 yuan over ten years to save money by using rainwater instead of water and electricity. Yesterday, Liang was suspected of selling protected wild animals and was criminally detained by the police.

After careful comparison by experts, it was determined that Xiaojuan's "treasure" was a nationally second-class protected animal - the macaque. Xiaojuan and her family also realized the seriousness of casually buying and selling protected animals. She actively provided another important clue to the police: the seller was young and advertised two monkeys for sale online. Officer Duan Lian decided to "lure the snake out of its hole" - posting information online at the same price wanting to buy another macaque. Sure enough, the seller eager to unload quickly responded willing to trade. After negotiation, the trading location was chosen at the bus stop outside Guannan Community.

According to the police, neither the buyer nor the seller knew the seriousness of selling protected wild animals. According to Article 341 of the Criminal Law, illegally acquiring, transporting, or selling precious and endangered wildlife under national key protection and their products shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of less than five years or criminal detention, and fined; if the circumstances are serious, they shall be sentenced to fixed-term imprisonment of more than five years but less than ten years, and fined; if the circumstances are particularly serious, they shall be sentenced to life imprisonment or death penalty, and fined or confiscation of property. The macaque is an endangered wild animal in our country and is listed as a second-class key protected animal.

Xiaojuan, who is only 16 years old, comes from a relatively privileged family, is trendy, and likes online shopping. To be different from peers, she wanted to buy a pet to play with during the Spring Festival. On February 1st, when she saw someone selling a pet resembling a monkey online, she took out her 7,000 yuan lucky money and bought one online, raising it at home. But after playing with it for a few days, she felt this pet looked familiar. Through online inquiries, she increasingly found that this little thing resembled a macaque. That's a national protected animal! She felt somewhat anxious inside.

The picture shows the two rescued three-month-old macaques.

On February 5th at around 4 pm, a taxi stopped at Guannan Station, and a tall, slim young man about 1.7 meters tall got out holding a large black canvas bag. After Xiaojuan carefully identified him, it turned out he was the person selling macaques online. The young man was caught on the spot by the officers lying in ambush, and another three-month-old baby macaque was seized from his backpack.

On February 5th, coincidentally, her good friend went to Hubei Chinese Medicine Hospital Guanggu Branch for treatment, and she went to visit her. She talked enthusiastically with her friend about the fun of this pet, but at the same time, she was very troubled. If it was a macaque, it is a second-class national protected animal, and cannot be registered. Throwing it away would waste so much money, yet keeping it was problematic.

Xiaojuan was therefore very troubled, and when Officer Duan Lian heard this, he was secretly shocked and decided to investigate further. He immediately revealed his identity and asked detailed questions. From his judgment, he thought this monkey could not be traded. On one hand, he reported to his superiors, and on the other hand, he invited animal experts to identify her "treasure" at Xiaojuan's house.
