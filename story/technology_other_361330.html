China's energy low-carbon sustainable development requires a smart grid

by ditan360 on 2010-07-29 17:18:23

http://www.ditan360.com 2010-07-29 08:38 Xinhua News Agency

Yitan News Network - Wu Guihui, Chief Engineer of the National Energy Administration, recently stated at a thematic forum held in Shanghai during the special activity day at the State Grid Pavilion of the Shanghai World Expo that the Chinese government attaches great importance to the development of smart grids. The low-carbon and sustainable development of China's future energy sector also requires strong support from smart grids. Currently, the "12th Five-Year Plan" for the energy industry is being compiled, and the development of smart grids has been included as an important content.

Wu Guihui said that currently, most of China's clean energy and new energy sources such as hydropower, wind power, and solar power have conditions for large-scale development, but they are far from load centers. They require large-scale, long-distance power transmission to implement wide-range energy resource optimization allocation. Therefore, building a robust grid structure is an essential part of China's smart grid development. In the long term, developing smart grids will not only enhance the level of energy security but also guide and change users' energy consumption habits, improving the overall efficiency of energy utilization.

He introduced that in recent years, China's electric power industry has developed rapidly, with an annual average growth rate of 9.2% for installed power generation capacity and 9.1% for total electricity consumption across society, providing strong support for economic and social development. However, the task of ensuring domestic energy and electricity supply remains arduous. Adhering to domestic resources and pursuing diversified development is the basic policy for China to solve its energy problems. It is necessary to actively develop nuclear power, hydropower, and other conventional energy sources while vigorously promoting clean power generation such as wind power and solar power. Relying on the intelligent allocation of resources through smart grids has become a key factor in enhancing China's sustainable energy development.

Wu Guihui introduced that the National Energy Administration highly values the development of smart grids. With strong support from governments at all levels and enterprises like the State Grid Corporation, the construction of demonstration projects for wind-solar-storage-transmission has been initiated. Research and development centers for large-scale wind power grid connection, solar power generation, offshore wind power technology equipment, and others have been successively approved and established. The national smart grid technology research and development center construction plan has passed expert review. Meanwhile, to ensure the healthy and orderly development of smart grids, the National Energy Administration has also joined forces with relevant ministries to form a working group and vigorously promote the standardization of smart grids.
