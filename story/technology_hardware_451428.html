In the winter of 1913, the Beiyang government changed the name of the Ministry of Industry and Commerce to the Ministry of Agriculture and Commerce.

by jmakeoe1 on 2011-06-24 16:40:47

On September 5, 1902, the British authorities signed the "Supplementary Convention on Mutual Trade and Shipping" with the Qing government in Shanghai, which stipulated the establishment of a Trademark Registration Bureau.

In 1903, the Qing government also signed the "Renewal of Mutual Trade and Shipping" with the United States and Japan. The U.S. and Japan made requests similar to those of Britain.

On August 4, 1904, under imperialist pressure, the Qing government announced China's first trademark regulation, the "Trial Regulations for Trademark Registration."

In the winter of 1913, the Beiyang government changed the Ministry of Agriculture and Commerce into the Ministry of Agriculture and Industry, managing the formulation, revision of trademark regulations, and enterprise trademark filing work.

In May 1923, the Trademark Office of the Ministry of Agriculture and Industry announced the "Trademark Law," "Detailed Rules for the Implementation of the Trademark Law," and various official procedures.

On September 15, 1923, the Trademark Office of the Ministry of Agriculture and Industry published the first "Trademark Gazette."

In 1927, the Ministry of Agriculture and Industry was divided into the Ministry of Agriculture and the Ministry of Industry. The Trademark Office was changed to be under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Industry.

On December 1, 1927, the Nationalist Government established the National Registration Bureau in Nanjing, specializing in handling trademark registration matters.

On December 21, 1928, the Nationalist Government separated the business operations responsible for trademark registration from the National Registration Bureau and established the Trademark Bureau under the Ministry of Agriculture and Industry.

In 1930, the Kuomintang government promulgated the "Trademark Law" and the "Detailed Rules for the Implementation of the Trademark Law," which were implemented starting January 1, 1931.

On February 15, 1932, the headquarters of the Trademark Bureau moved from Nanjing to Guizhou Road in Shanghai for office work.

In November 1933, the first large-scale masterpiece on trademarks in Chinese history, the "East Asia Trademark Compilation," was edited and published.

In July 1940, the Wang puppet government promulgated the "Measures for the Organization of Trademark Registration Certificates."

On January 1, 1941, the headquarters of the Trademark Bureau of the Ministry of Industry and Commerce of the Wang puppet government moved from Nanjing to No. 861 North Sichuan Road in Shanghai for office work.

On April 13, 1946, the Su-E An Border Region Government announced the "Interim Measures for Commodity Trademark Registration."

On August 23, 1946, the Jin-Ji-Lu-Yu Border Region Government announced the "Trademark Registration Measures."

On July 18, 1949, the Shaan-Gan-Ning Border Region Government announced the "Interim Measures for Trademark Registration."
