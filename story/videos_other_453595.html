The natural disasters in 2008, international boycotts of Chinese products, the outbreak of the global financial crisis, inflationary pressures, and other adverse factors intertwined, leaving China's retail industry on thin ice. International retail giants such as Walmart and Bountiful have already frozen their recruitment in China. Metro is also abolishing its four regional headquarters, redefining its business areas to focus more on sales and operations. Recently, it has begun to reduce some store personnel. USG Sports, one of the three major players in the distribution channels for sports apparel and footwear in China, which holds nearly 25% of the domestic market share for brands like Nike, Adidas, Reebok, and Converse, has recently announced its closure.

How can China's retail industry eliminate the fog, review the present situation, grasp the trend, and find a way out of the "winter survival strategy" to welcome spring?

New Opportunities

From the history of retail development, every major economic change or transformation period has been a time of change and innovation for the retail industry. The Industrial Revolution led to the emergence of large-scale department stores, while the economic crisis of the 1930s gave birth to cheap and rapidly growing chain supermarkets. China's retail industry is now facing the adverse economic environment of an international crisis, which will inevitably create new opportunities. Today, we must pay close attention to possible new opportunities, such as the emergence of new retail formats, new consumer trends, the maturity of new markets, and industry growth trends, and take proactive measures to make corresponding changes in order to prepare early and seize future opportunities.

New Markets

Policies such as "Appliances to the Countryside" and the domestic market demand development policies formulated at the Third Plenary Session of the 17th Central Committee, centered on promoting rural land transfer reforms, are great news for the retail industry. Rural reform will accelerate China's urbanization process and increase the speed of farmers moving into cities. This means that the growth rate of commercial real estate and the retail industry, which are indispensable in cities, will accelerate, and it also means that the growth rate of total retail sales of consumer goods will increase.

A new dominant consumer group has emerged in China's consumer population: the post-80s generation, who increasingly play such roles. Most of them are only children with new consumption concepts, and their consumption ideas and pursuit of fashion are almost synchronized with the world. As only children, their consumption ability is not constrained by their own income level and may lead to greater intra-family transfer payment capabilities. The new generation of consumers has started a wave of forming new families, bringing a consumption tide, especially in fashionable goods and high-cost-performance goods. They will play a leading role in this regard and also guide the "baby boom" consumption trend after forming families.

The emergence and maturity of these two new markets will undoubtedly make positive contributions to the growth of retail.

New Business Formats

The new retail formats mainly manifest in online stores (malls) and discount stores. With the widespread use of the internet in daily life and the maturity of internet technology, virtual online operations have become very popular. The existence of vast rural markets, the transformation of Made-in-China, and the downward slide of the economy have fostered the rapid growth of discount stores.

Online Stores (Malls)

Online stores cannot replace physical stores in terms of tangible nature, but the rapid growth of online stores is devouring the sales share of physical retail stores, a fact that cannot be disputed. Internet marketing has the characteristic of being unrestricted by time and space, operating seven days a week, 24 hours a day; breaking through the geographical segmentation of traditional consumer terminals, extending its reach to every corner of the world; using internet marketing allows interactive communication with customers, who can propose new demands and service needs based on their own requirements; virtual internet terminals can meet the personalized needs of customers, providing them with maximum convenience.

Every chain enterprise of a certain scale should establish an online store. Some large-scale stores with high sales volume can also open "new experience flagship stores" that integrate the functions of online stores and physical stores. For example, Li Ning's online flagship store sales amount to the sales of an entire province, or in other words, it creates the consumption of an entire province. Physical stores opening online stores will make China's retail industry more vibrant and also expand the new field of retail chain growth.

Discount Stores

Recently, many strong-consuming large cities have begun investing billions of yuan to build Outlets (large discount stores). Outlets are essentially large discount stores. Discount stores originated during economic crises when manufacturing entered a recession phase, and large amounts of accumulated goods could only be sold at a discount. Current realities provide the possibility for discount stores to offer tailor-made goods at low prices.

This year's economic downturn has brought vitality to the expansion of discount stores for two reasons: First, many outward-oriented export enterprises in our country are facing transformation, with some shifting from external markets to internal markets, providing abundant resources for discount stores. Second, hundreds of millions of farmers have entered cities, where they must satisfy their basic living needs through the retail market, significantly increasing the overall retail goods total.

With the transformation of China's manufacturing industry, discount stores could potentially become the focus of the next round of retail industry growth.

Six Strategies to Weather the Winter

In order to quickly emerge from the shadow of the economic crisis and rebuild new hopes, we propose the following six strategies for "surviving the winter":

Cultural Strategy - Talent Strategy

Good employees are the assets of a company and also its core competitiveness. Therefore, Jiang Taigong said, to gain the world, one must win the hearts of the people, and to win the hearts of the world, one must first share the benefits of the world with the masses (employees). Modern corporate competition ultimately boils down to talent competition. However, the current market's uncertainty, the innovativeness of technology, and the variability of organizations make talent mobility and loss a big issue for companies. The retail industry is no exception, whether it's high-level management talent, mid-level managers, or grassroots sales assistants, all face talent "loss." To gain a competitive advantage, retail companies must formulate talent strategies and plans to "drive corporate growth through talent." Currently, too many retailers' talent management systems, processes, and practices are still fragmented and lack focus on building these capabilities. Retailers need to fully leverage the role of talent to achieve excellent service levels, performance, and results. To do so, they must enhance their abilities in defining talent, discovering talent, cultivating talent, and utilizing talent and comprehensively apply them. Integrating these abilities and adjusting them according to key business needs will be more beneficial for the company to showcase its talents, thereby creating sustainable competitive advantages and ultimately achieving long-term value. Only by making "cultural strategy" the top priority of the enterprise and truly relying on talent to compete can the enterprise achieve lasting success.

Military Strategy - Winning Weapon

As retail enterprises or retailers, they must center all work around "creating value for customers," but in reality, retail work doesn't always follow this principle or isn't executed thoroughly. Although customer service is important, it is often missing in shopping experiences. After extensive surveys, Accenture, an international consulting firm, drew the following five conclusions:

● Customer service is the most influential factor affecting purchasing decisions, with most survey respondents ranking it as the top priority.

● However, there are significant shortcomings in customer service and sales personnel in providing consumer shopping experiences.

● Compared to two years ago, most consumers don't feel that shopping has become more convenient. Only 35% of respondents said they could get help whenever they needed it most.

● Only 20% of consumers said that the stores they frequently patronize understand their preferences and help them find suitable products.

● Customer loyalty is not rewarded, with nearly two-thirds of respondents saying that the service they receive at stores they frequent is no different from that received by infrequent visitors.

From the above survey conclusions, it is clear that retailers do not place enough emphasis on respecting customers, customer service, customer experience, and customer convenience. Their ability to build relationships with customers is lacking, and they have not established a customer-centric business strategy. Once retailers fail to prioritize creating customer value, they soon lose business and eventually disappear. In fact, valuing customers is more important than focusing on improving trade and supply chain capabilities, yet in reality, priorities are inverted.

Increasing interaction with customers, providing personalized services, and achieving goals beyond customer expectations are urgent tasks for today's retail industry. Retail companies should prioritize creating customer value. Excellent retail companies can combine excellent customer experiences, a wide range of product choices, and competitive pricing to offer customers. However, this is precisely the "weakness" of China's retail industry. Therefore, whoever takes the lead in doing well will be able to implement outstanding service faster and better, thus creating customer value and winning customer loyalty, thereby surpassing other companies.

Dragon Strategy - Management Truth

Retail companies or retailers should recognize this trend: continuous improvement in market competition and operating environments will lead to the constant appearance of mergers and acquisitions among large retail chains, driving the retail industry towards chain operations, groupification, standardization, and scaling up. This will promote the continuous and rapid expansion of China's large retail enterprises. In 2004, 2005, and 2006, the total sales of goods by China's top 100 retail enterprises were 560 billion yuan, 700 billion yuan, and 870 billion yuan respectively, increasing year-on-year by 33.2%, 29.2%, and 18.1%. In 2006, the retail sales of the top 100 retail enterprises accounted for 11.4% of the total social consumer goods retail sales. Entering 2008, the trend of retail mergers and acquisitions continued, with a more obvious tendency for regional enterprises in second and third-tier cities to be integrated by larger enterprises.

For small and medium-sized retail enterprises, either grow bigger themselves, or if unable to break through the bottleneck of self-growth, choosing to be acquired and integrated is not a bad path. After all, "perseverance" may not necessarily be the best strategy for the enterprise. Meanwhile, large retail enterprises continue to strengthen their power through mergers and acquisitions. If strategic decisions are correctly grasped, moderate mergers can increase industry concentration, benefiting both the industry and the enterprise's healthy growth. However, considering that mergers and acquisitions are tools, ultimately aiming to integrate and enhance strength, everything will come down to specific management because management is the foundation. Effective management can realize all strategic expectations.

Currently, China's retail industry lacks modern management methods and techniques, has low management levels, and employees lack training. The entire industry lacks high-quality talent. Most operate independently, single-handedly, even chain stores are just nominal without centralized procurement centers, with purchase volumes only meeting the needs of individual stores. There are many loopholes in management. Therefore, enhancing management capabilities has become an urgent matter. Management can start from suppliers, organization, credit, and even operational performance to improve operational quality and efficiency.

Tiger Strategy - Execution Law

China's retail industry generally shares similar tactics, unwilling to refine details thoroughly, and lacks consistent perseverance. Often, things start well but then deteriorate. Therefore, for the retail industry to flourish, it is not impossible. Just by consistently paying attention to details, one can achieve desired results. Suggestions include:

● Refine marketing strategies and soften "service yoga."

● Truly achieve the intrinsic transformation from "salesperson" to "shopping assistant."

● Establish retailer reputation and protect it like one would protect their eyes.

● Create a warm shopping experience in the store. Do not mistakenly assume that in times of crisis, consumers only care about cheap goods and no longer value shopping experiences. Economic contraction does not mean consumers will ignore the shopping experience. If consumers have good and strong shopping experiences during economic downturns, it will influence their consumption interests and motives, thereby benefiting sales and profits, possibly even better than during economic booms.

● Pay attention to customer behavior, needs, and preferences, and translate them into data to achieve category segmentation, respond to "one-to-one" marketing, and focus on individualization.

Leopard Strategy - Blue Ocean Essence

Tai Gong said: "When the whole army fights fiercely, either attack the front or the rear. The brave ones cannot fight, and the light ones cannot retreat. It is called 'sudden battle.' Even if the enemy is numerous, their general will surely flee." The meaning is that the whole army attacks fiercely, hitting the enemy's front and rear. The brave enemies cannot fight, the lightly armed cannot retreat, and the enemy general will certainly run away. Facing international retail leaders, Chinese retail enterprises should adopt the "leopard strategy," leveraging local advantages, innovating ways, and directly attacking the market, which can open the door to the blue ocean.

● Quickly develop online stores, interact between offline physical stores and online shops, expand the demographic consumption base, and reduce costs.

● Develop rural markets or urban-rural combined markets, expand survival space, and leverage advantageous forces.

● Based on geographical advantages, utilize the transformation of "Made in China" to develop "discount stores" and secure cash recovery.

● Focus on the market needs of the post-80s generation, ally with peers, and actively invest in brand longevity.

● Dog Strategy - Blocking Strategy

The continuous improvement of openness in the retail industry, the diversification and stratification of residents' consumption, have prompted the rapid development of various retail formats in China's retail industry. Currently, the retail formats in China's retail market cover almost all retail formats worldwide. Chain-operated modern retail formats have become the mainstream of China's retail industry development. However, there are many problems in China's retail industry, such as non-standardized logistics distribution centers, imperfect format functions, small scale, low concentration, unreasonable format structure, single management methods, and inappropriate product combinations.

Compared with international retail leaders, China's retail industry still needs to learn from their advanced practices to avoid and address its own shortcomings. Therefore, China's retail enterprises must learn from the successful experiences of international retail leaders, such as their rapid expansion capabilities, cost control capabilities, marketing skills, financial operation capabilities, brand value capabilities, and talent aggregation capabilities. These aspects are worth learning from to "learn from foreign strengths to counteract foreign powers" and promote the rapid, healthy, and sustainable development of China's retail enterprises.

Welcoming Spring

Due to the active government efforts to stabilize the market, the inherent rigidity of the national economy, the emergence of new markets, and stable internal consumption power, there is hope and brightness ahead. In the current generally sluggish economic environment, what China's retail enterprises need to do now is strictly control cash and operating funds, then further review and think about their main business scope, vision, and competitive strategy, and realistically evaluate actual business performance. By combining market opportunities and threats, determine the main business trends. Once the main strategy is determined, efforts must be made tirelessly toward the goal, formulating various strategies and plans until achieving the expected corporate outcomes.

From the "Cultural Strategy" of actively gathering, cultivating, and utilizing talent, to the "Military Strategy" of focusing on individual specialization and value creation as the core capability, to the "Dragon Strategy" of governing management truths, to the "Tiger Strategy" of executing details thoroughly, to the "Leopard Strategy" of innovation as the blue ocean path, to the "Dog Strategy" of skillfully learning from international retail leaders, implementing each step carefully, how could one worry about lacking a "winter coat"? Winter has come, but spring is sure to follow.
