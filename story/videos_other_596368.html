I. Project Overview

####### Bridge is an important part of the East Bypass Expressway in a certain area. The superstructure consists of five continuous box girders with equal cross-sections made of prestressed concrete structures, while the substructure is pile-column type. The span arrangement is 25.5m + 40m + 63m + 40m + 40m, with a total length of 208.5 meters. The bridge is a double-deck six-lane separated bridge. The design load standard is vehicle - ultra-20 grade and trailer - 120. The box girder adopts a single-box double-cell section, top width 12.5m, bottom width 8.0m, and side cantilever length 2.75m. The girder height is 2.7m, the edge web thickness is 70cm, the central web thickness is 30cm, and the top and bottom plate thickness is 20cm. Prestressed box girders use concrete grade 50. Web longitudinal prestressed steel strands adopt 12-7Φ5 high-strength steel strand, top and bottom plate longitudinal prestressed steel strands adopt 15-7Φ5 high-strength steel strand, Ryb = 1860MPa, anchorages are VLM15-12, 15 anchorage system. Box girders are constructed using monolithic in-situ casting; first, the middle span of 63 meters is casted and tensioned for prestressed steel strands using two-end tensioning method; then, the secondary middle span is constructed and tensioned using single-end tensioning method; finally, the side span is constructed and tensioned using single-end tensioning method. The main pier uses reinforced concrete variable cross-section square column piers with group pile foundations.

III. Current Construction Status

######### Bridge is being constructed by ##### Highway No. 1 Contract Project Manager Department Bridge Division of ********** Road & Bridge General Corporation. During the construction of the right-2 segment box girder (9-9 section), the extension of the R4 steel strand inside the web only reached 50% of the designed value, and grouting treatment has been carried out. The construction does not meet the design requirements. Therefore, necessary reinforcement design and construction must be carried out.

IV. Test Results According to Original Design Documents and Current Construction Status:

The plane truss and spatial structure analysis were conducted separately using QJX program and Super Sap program. The analysis results indicate that under the most unfavorable load combinations, there is no tensile stress at the bottom edge of the mid-span section between the second and third piers (secondary middle span) and the upper edge at the top of Pier No.1, but almost no stress reserve remains. Compared to the original design concept, this bridge should have a certain amount of stress reserve under the most unfavorable loads, but due to insufficient tensioning during construction, the stress reserves at these two sections are not in harmony with the rest of the bridge, resulting in the loss of almost all stress reserves. Compared to other parts of the bridge, these sections are weak points, which may affect the durability and safety of the bridge.

V. Basic Ideas for Reinforcement Design Based on the structural verification calculations performed on this bridge and considering the actual construction conditions (except for the abnormal construction of the inner web R4 steel strand in the right-2 segment box girder, the construction of this bridge generally meets the design and relevant standards and regulations), local reinforcement (second span) is required to ensure the structure meets the design requirements. After analysis and comparison, according to the current bridge design specifications and considering the actual force conditions of this bridge, two reinforcement schemes are proposed: external prestressing and bonding steel plates and carbon fibers. The external prestressing scheme is an active loading scheme, but it has greater construction difficulty and requires regular inspection and maintenance during operation. The bonding steel plates and carbon fibers scheme is a passive loading scheme, but it is convenient for construction and does not require maintenance during operation.

Scheme One: External Prestressing Scheme. This scheme is an active loading scheme, adding new external prestress where the prestressing steel strand R4 tension did not meet the design requirements, raising the existing ultimate state and normal use limit state of the bridge's bearing capacity to the level required by the design.

Scheme Two: Bonding Steel Plates and Carbon Fibers Scheme. This scheme is a passive loading scheme, involving bonding steel plates to the upper edge of Pier No.1 and bonding carbon fiber sheets along the bridge axis direction on the bottom surface of the second span box girder, raising the existing ultimate state and normal use limit state of the bridge's bearing capacity to the level required by the design.
