A man should have four kinds of feelings.

by ncgpvrph06 on 2012-03-06 12:17:11

People have four kinds of emotions. The fourth one, I call it "the sentiment of things." Besides family affection, friendship, and romance, people need the sentiment of things. Of course, some can survive without what I so-called "sentiment of things". These two days, I was browsing through all the issues of the 2010 Psychological Monthly, and I found that this magazine is pretty good, except there are too many advertisements, which take up nearly half of the book. This is the second time I feel a magazine is good after National Geographic. I've been in contact with this magazine for a long time, but until now, I've read it relatively seriously.

I excerpted a paragraph: Everything is work, work is everything. Once it develops to this extent, a person's heart will turn into a desert because what warms our hearts is not money, but emotion, especially love, family affection, and friendship. If all the time is put on work, it means that a person's heart unconsciously only worships power, and love and cherish can no longer be received.

It was when I saw this paragraph that I suddenly had an idea that humans have a fourth kind of emotion. A large part of what makes me happy and constantly improves my sense of happiness comes from this fourth kind of emotion. The fondness for a city, gratitude for all things, warmth given by a friendly passerby, objects one likes, having a young body that can climb every mountain, the petty bourgeoisie of pushing a cart around the supermarket and bringing a big bag of stuff home, the elegance of making a nice meal for oneself, a house where one can smoke and live happily alone, reading...

The fourth kind of emotion is very significant to me and has supported me for a great deal these years. When I broke up with my girlfriend, I said I didn't want to leave this city and be with you mainly because I couldn't bear to leave this city, and I couldn't bear to leave the friends I've established here. If I go to a new place and start from scratch, I would suffer a lot, and I'm afraid that the loneliness would torture myself for too long.

This fourth kind of emotion will continue to support me to move forward. I want to live well and happily welcome all the beautiful things that belong to me. I said I plan to buy myself a new lighter as a gift. Since Valentine's Day is too close and money is tight, let's postpone it to my birthday. Superman asked me when my birthday is, I said the day after Dragon Boat Festival, he said: "Qu Yuan left, and you came?" It turns out that my life is exchanged by Brother Qu Yuan.

Thanks to my cousin, I used his supermarket shopping card to buy a big bag of things back home. When I am alone, I have to do both men's and women's work, which I find funny but also quite beautiful. After returning home, I wash clothes, cook meals, and take showers. During the Spring Festival, I told my mom: "I found out that I like eating dumplings quite a bit this year," and usually, I rarely eat them during the New Year. In the text message, I told my sister: "When people reach a certain age, they start to like many things they didn't like before." From childhood to adulthood, I never ate meat dumplings, and my grandmother always made me vegetarian ones separately. And I haven't eaten any better-tasting vegetarian dumplings than hers. She created her own dumpling filling, mixing seven or eight ingredients together. I have never been as obsessed with the same filling for 20 years as I am this Spring Festival. After leaving home, I still felt unsatisfied and bought three bags of dumplings to eat, but each time, I couldn't taste the aroma of Grandma's dumplings, and every time I cooked them myself, they were not perfect enough, either undercooked or overcooked and stuck together.

After analyzing from various aspects, I think I have some neurasthenia. Online, it says that people with neurasthenia should not consume coffee or chocolate. I often go to the supermarket to buy a bottle of coffee before leaving home because I always have a supermarket card at home that doesn't cost me money. I planned to do the same this year, but after seeing this online, I hesitated and gave up the plan. In the morning, when I just arrived at the unit, Mao Mao sent me a bucket of so-called "Vietnamese coffee." Sigh, don't make bad friends. On the bus going home, I thought, we, about ten people of the same age, gathered together to work, actually none of us really likes each other to the extreme, but surely none of us hates each other, that's enough. Being able to gather together means there must be some commonalities among us, but if you look closely, everyone is different, each with their own style. Who is more fashionable, who has stronger aesthetics, who is more kind, who is more generous, who understands human nature better. Actually, fashion is being yourself; kindness is being yourself; great love is being yourself. A group becomes more perfect because of its multifacetedness.

During the Spring Festival, a friend of Dad's sent a wild chicken to our house. Dad wasn't at home, initially, it was placed in a cardboard box, and both Mom and I thought it was already dead chicken meat. Later, the movement inside made us realize it was a living creature. Mom repeatedly declined politely, saying nice words, hoping he would take it back, but eventually, he didn't. The first thought that came to Mom's mind was that killing a turkey was too cruel. At first, Dad was happy to kill the wild chicken and make a delicious meal for the family, but Mom argued with him: Dogs are lives, chickens aren't? You take care of seven stray dogs, why not chickens? Dad said: Chickens are meant to be eaten. Mom replied: People eat dogs too! I was also very anxious and told Dad: "It's pitiful to kill it." So Dad stood on the side of the chicken. Dad is kind and willing to do anything to make the family happy. The next morning, he went out and bought a cage, which was more expensive than the chicken, to raise the wild chicken. He also looked for food for the chicken from others. Such small matters are enough to make one feel reassured. I took pictures and sent them to my sister, telling her this is our second pet, Ji Ji, and she praised our second pet as very cool~ There were seven puppies born in Dad's workplace courtyard, and during the New Year holiday, he fed them milk and meat twice a day. When he came back, he happily discussed the state of the puppies with Mom. He said: "Since we encountered them, if we don't take care of them, it wouldn't be right. In such cold weather, even adult dogs can't find food, how could they take care of their children." Every time "He He" caused trouble at home, Dad educated it: "Look at those seven little dogs, they are much smaller than you, yet they can survive outdoors. You have endless meat to eat and a warm nest, yet you still cause trouble..." I felt reassured and my eyes moistened. My family is becoming more loving. Just like what Psychological Monthly wrote: "A compassionate heart is one that does not increase the suffering of others. Perhaps it cannot change the overall situation, but it is enough to absolve oneself." Kindness should be pure. Do it if you want, and leave quietly afterward, avoiding others' praise and approval.

I believe that men should cry. A man who doesn't even know how to cry, how can he do other things well?! Crying doesn't represent weakness, and moreover, tears are often not due to weakness but because of being moved, because of resonance. Not crying makes me associate with being heartless and lacking compassion. It's not just the retinal effect, but objective fact. I see more and more "crying", such as Yue Jia, Liu Tong, Li Xiang, and many others in the media. Personally, I think that such people have more beautiful souls, and the act of crying instead highlights the inner sunshine.

Recently, while thinking about interviews, how can one see a person's essence at a glance? I hope I can meet candidates who "look right" with me. If God limits recruitment to only one condition, I hope it is that this person makes me feel that their essence is good, not necessarily outstanding, but just feels good. Sometimes I wonder, after interviewing many people, will I end up not liking the last one? Not because others are not good enough, but because I am not strong enough to see that person's heart at a glance. I hope to hear people say they value family more than career! Believe in God's arrangements, and the right people and events will naturally appear at the right time. Recently, when I went to Yuncheng, I discussed "believing in fate" with Xiao Sun a lot, and I showed her the four characters I took in Wutai Mountain with my camera. I explained to her the difference between "believing in fate" and "leaving it to fate." Believe that God is fair, believe that God's arrangements for you are balanced, and believe that you will eventually get the deserved beautiful things and happiness.

After the New Year, I thought in my mind that this year I should spend 60% of my energy and time on work and 40% on playing. A year has only about 280 days left after holidays, 280/365 = 77%. Listening to 60% seems like being too playful, and I am still worried whether I can spend 60% of my energy on work, the answer is probably no. I won't say the lie that I will put all my energy into my career! Similarly, I worry that I don't have 40% of my energy to play. Books say: Psychologists believe - the purpose of humans "playing" is to escape loneliness and seek recognition and belonging. Men play enough by the age of 50, and women by the age of 35. No wonder adults and teachers have always told us since we were kids that "boys are just more playful."

And the worries of women and men are different. Women's worries mostly come from themselves, while men's worries mostly come from external factors. Women worry about aging, while men worry about failing in their struggles. Few men would think about caring about "an extra wrinkle on their face", "drooping eyelids", or "washing dishes harming their skin."

A topic in today's magazine, I think everyone should consider. Should relatives and friends with serious illnesses be informed or kept in the dark? Many choose to hide the illness from the patient, calling cancer inflammation to relieve pressure, reduce fear, and improve the patient's attitude. But has anyone considered the patient's perspective? If you were the cancer patient, would you rather know your condition no matter how cruel, or be kept in the dark until you can't eat, can't finish unfinished tasks, until you close your eyes and leave the world? We regret the departure of the patient, but would the departing person regret more if they knew earlier about their condition, perhaps they could do many things.

Set a work plan and execute each step according to the plan, and work becomes much easier. Work will never be finished unless you retire or die. But once you set a work plan, after finishing the day's work, you can think of nothing and do whatever you want. The next day's work, you continue to execute according to the plan. If the plan is too much, you'll have to work overtime; if the plan is less, you'll finish early and start relaxing. If the plan is unreasonable, sometimes you may be extremely busy, and sometimes you may be bored out of your mind, but don't worry, with experience, the plan will become increasingly reasonable. Having a planned work, how about life? I've always followed my feelings, never predicting what will happen next or what I will possess. I haven't figured out yet whether life should be planned.
