IT Talent Competition is Fierce, Alibaba Offers 3 Billion Housing Subsidy

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-18 14:03:50

Expecting a company raise or improved benefits is the dream of many urban white-collar workers. The employees of Alibaba have waited for this day.

On the evening of the 16th, according to electronic market conditions, Peng Lei, the Chief Human Resources Officer of Alibaba Group, sent an internal email to more than 20,000 employees, announcing the launch of a 3 billion yuan iHome home purchase loan plan and committing 500 million yuan to establish an education fund to solve preschool and primary school education issues for employees' children. Additionally, considering the pressure from CPI increases, the group will provide grassroots employees with over 40 million yuan in one-time subsidies for living costs and children's education. Industry information

Following Tencent's introduction of a 1 billion yuan interest-free home purchase loan for employees, another internet company has rolled out a large-scale employee benefits plan. Beyond these benefits, competition for talent in the internet industry is becoming increasingly intense.

Loan maximum limit is 300,000 yuan

Many employees on the internal network said, "We can get married next year." An Alibaba employee told a reporter from the "First Financial Daily."

Alibaba's home purchase loan plan is based on employees' years of service rather than geographical location. As long as an employee has served in Alibaba Group for at least two years, works in mainland China, and is purchasing their first home in their work location, they are eligible to apply for the loan. Among them, employees with three or more years of service have a maximum loan amount of 300,000 yuan RMB.

Notably, this welfare plan is heavily tilted towards grassroots and ordinary employees. For instance, directors and above management levels in Alibaba Group are not eligible for home purchase loans.

It is understood that this home purchase loan plan will officially begin implementation from September 1st this year, with an initial operation period of four years, after which adjustments will be made based on specific circumstances; this loan is an interest-free loan with a maximum term of five years.

Currently, the details of how employees repay the loan have not been announced. It is understood that employees can choose to repay the loan in one lump sum or by deducting a fixed amount from their monthly salary in various forms.

IT talent competition is fierce

Under continuously rising prices and inflationary pressures, providing welfare subsidies to employees in various forms such as raises and equity incentives has almost become a universal phenomenon among internet companies.

It is known that around the Spring Festival this year, Sohu implemented a 10% salary adjustment for all employees. In June this year, Tencent officially launched a three-year, 1 billion yuan housing plan, providing interest-free loans of up to 300,000 yuan for employees buying their first home. NetEase also announced a salary adjustment for more than 1,200 employees of its portal website, with about 60% of employees receiving a 25% increase. Recently, many group-buying websites have also announced equity incentive plans to retain high-quality talent.

Behind these phenomena lies the internet companies' thirst for talent. Currently, competition for talent among Chinese internet companies is becoming increasingly fierce, with widespread poaching and counter-poaching through salary increases and equity incentives. A previous related report showed that in May this year, the internet and e-commerce industry leapt to the top of the list with a demand for 140,000 talents. It is estimated that in the next ten years, there will be a shortage of at least 2 million e-commerce professionals in China.

A management expert told reporters that many internet companies are represented by post-80s individuals, who place greater emphasis on ensuring individual and family stability. Alibaba's approach stabilizes the existing team while increasing its appeal to external outstanding talents.

Behind the internet companies rolling out salary increases and benefit plans is the continuous injection of large amounts of venture capital. According to data from Zero2IPO, the most attention-grabbing investment industry in the first half of 2011 was still the internet industry, with 110 investments occurring, far surpassing other industries, representing a 71.9% increase compared to last year's 64 cases; the total investment amount received was 1.553 billion US dollars, representing a staggering 396.2% increase compared to the same period last year.
