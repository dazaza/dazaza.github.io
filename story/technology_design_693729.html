Students studying accounting in China who wish to study at the University of Quebec in Canada can enjoy many favorable policies introduced by the government. For example, they can receive scholarships provided by the government, with a two-year amount as high as 100,000 RMB, along with a Quebec visa. They can also participate in paid internships, automatically obtain a three-year work permit after graduation, and apply for immigration after working for one year.

Canada is known for its difficulty in obtaining scholarships. However, among nearly a hundred public universities in Canada, there is a unique Canadian master's program that guarantees a 90% scholarship. This is a bilateral project between the National Scholarship Committee and the Quebec Provincial Government, saving up to 90%. The tuition fee for the University of Quebec is 11,000 Canadian dollars per year, and with the high scholarship exemption, it can save 10,000 Canadian dollars annually, equivalent to 60,000 RMB.

Due to the limited number of "high scholarship exemptions" each year and according to past experience, the following types of students have a higher chance of winning the scholarship:

1) Those pursuing higher degrees, such as graduate studies over undergraduate studies.

2) Those who receive direct admission, meaning direct admission has an advantage over conditional admission.

3) Those studying French, implying that studying French has a higher chance of winning the scholarship than English.

4) Those who submit applications earlier, as the "high scholarship exemption" adopts a policy of applying and reviewing at any time, processing applications on a first-come-first-served basis until all quotas are filled. Therefore, submitting applications early naturally increases the chances of winning the scholarship.

Application materials:

1) Application Form for the Foreign Student Tuition Scholarship Project of the Province of Quebec, Canada (Attachment One). (A one-inch recent photo of the applicant needs to be attached as required.)

2) Application Form for Exemption from Foreign Student Tuition Scholarship (Attachment Two) (Chinese and English), two copies.

3) Copies of the applicant's academic qualifications and degree certificates (need to be stamped with the official seal of the issuing institution).

4) Copies of the applicant's school transcripts (need to be stamped with the official seal of the issuing institution).

5) Copies of proof of the applicant's foreign language proficiency (need to be stamped with the official seal of the certifying body).

6) Copies and translations of unconditional final admission notices (excluding French language requirements) from relevant institutions in the Province of Quebec, Canada.

Application Time:

The National Scholarship Fund Management Committee accepts applications for the high scholarship exemption for autumn (September) entry before May 31st each year and for winter (January) entry the next year before October 31st.
