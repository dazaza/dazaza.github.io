Top Ten Consumer Complaints in Guangzhou: Online Shopping and Express Delivery Complaints Surge Three to Five Times

An employee of an express delivery company is distributing parcels.

Consumers are being deceived, with last year's top ten consumer traps proving impossible to guard against - online shopping and express delivery complaints have surged three to five times.

Yangcheng Evening News reporters Tan Chao and Chen Qiang, along with correspondent Dai Lian, report: Electronic products promising "lifetime free repairs" actually cannot provide after-sales service; a normal month of internet use results in a 1000 yuan overdue fee; items clearly priced at 68 yuan but the supermarket charges 78 yuan at checkout; replacing a car part only to be deceived by the dubious authenticity of parts from a 4S store... The ever-evolving consumer traps bring immense distress to people's lives, yet they remain difficult to avoid.

On March 10th, the 12315 Command Center of the Guangzhou Administration for Industry and Commerce released the top ten consumer complaints in Guangzhou for 2010:

1. After-sales Service Complaints for Electronic Products

Replacing a shell costs a hundred yuan

Data: In 2010, there were nearly 20,900 cases of complaints and inquiries regarding after-sales services for electronic products, with 12,686 cases being accepted. This represents a year-on-year increase of 286%.

Complaints: Indifferent attitude, refusal of the 'three guarantees' (repair, replacement, or refund), non-transparent fees.

Case: Ms. Ye requested that the "Yuan Ecology Activated Water Purifier Company" repair her water purifier base. The merchant simply replaced the shell and charged her 100 yuan. When she questioned it, the merchant claimed it was due to improper use on her part.

2. Telecommunication Services Complaints

No internet usage yet an overdue fee of a thousand yuan

Data: The telecommunications industry has always been a hotspot for consumer complaints. According to statistics, in 2010, there were 15,325 complaints about the telecommunications industry, an increase of 35.7% compared to 2009.

Complaints: Concealing information, misleading with free airtime, passive deduction of fees from consumers.

Case: A consumer complained that in February of last year, they purchased a wireless network card, prepaying 600 yuan for internet fees. After consulting with the service hall about the fee issues, they were informed that if the fees were used up, the service would automatically disconnect. By March, the prepaid amount was depleted, yet the service continued until mid-April without disconnection. The company stated that the consumer still had internet traffic in May, resulting in over a thousand yuan in internet fees. The consumer believed this was unreasonable as the company did not automatically disconnect the service when the fees were used up, and since the consumer only used the service until mid-April, it was impossible to generate internet traffic in May.

3. Retail (Supermarket) Service Complaints

Overpaying by a thousand yuan without receiving a refund

Data: According to statistics, in 2010, there were 4,205 complaints related to the retail industry. Since January this year, complaints about "price fraud" in supermarkets have sharply increased, becoming a new hotspot for complaints.

Complaints: Two sets of prices, false discounts, exaggerated pricing, ambiguous pricing, fictitious original prices.

Case: Complainant Ms. Chen bought a washing machine at an electrical appliance mall on Zhongshan Road Eight. It was marked at 6,000 yuan, discounted to 4,999 yuan, with the promise that it was the lowest price in the entire city. If a lower price for the same model was found within 19 days in the same city, the difference would be refunded. A few days later, she saw the same product at another electrical appliance mall on Zhongshan Road Eight marked at over 5,000 yuan, with the salesperson stating that after the discount, it could be purchased for 4,000 yuan. Thus, she requested a refund of the difference from the mall where she originally purchased the washing machine. The mall stated that there was no evidence to support her claim and refused to do so.

4. Automobile Sales and After-sales Service Complaints

"Factory Original" accessories lack barcodes

Data: According to statistics, in 2010, there were a total of 4,239 consultations and complaints regarding automobile sales and after-sales services, including 2,343 complaints, representing a year-on-year increase of 110%.

Complaints: Difficulty distinguishing between genuine and counterfeit repair parts, safety issues ignored.

Case: Consumer Mr. Zeng reported that in February 2010, while repairing his car at the Haimei Yue Auto Parts Company, the bumper provided by the merchant, purportedly "factory original" and valued at 1,160 yuan, did not meet specifications and lacked a machine-printed barcode, raising concerns about the quality of such parts.

Consumer Mr. Cheng reflected that the Toyota Highlander he purchased experienced brake failure while driving to Zhuhai in February 2010. After computer detection at the manufacturer's 4S store revealed a brake booster fault, the merchant destroyed the test results, leaving him deeply frustrated.

5. Courier Service Complaints

Sent before the holiday, received after

Data: According to statistics, in 2010, there were a total of 4,082 complaints and inquiries regarding courier services, including 2,261 accepted complaints, representing a year-on-year increase of 500%.

Complaints: Delay in delivery, damage or loss of goods, unreasonable charges.

Case: A consumer reflected that mooncakes sent 21 days before the Mid-Autumn Festival, with the courier company promising delivery within four days, were not received by the recipient until several days after the festival.

Mr. Li once complained that a Guangzhou courier company lost a mobile phone he mailed, worth 2,800 yuan, yet was only willing to compensate 500 yuan. Although mediation increased the compensation to 1,500 yuan, the consumer remains distrustful of courier services.

6. Non-store Retail Sales Complaints

Phishing websites offering fake group buying

Data: In 2010, there were a total of 3,818 complaints and inquiries regarding non-store retail sales, including 1,097 accepted complaints, representing a year-on-year increase of 300%.

Complaints: Prevalence of phishing websites, disappearance of online agents, exaggeration.

Case: Many consumers reflected that when purchasing products on platforms like Taobao, untrustworthy sellers would privately send counterfeit web links requiring buyers to pay orders. After filling out the information, they discovered that the funds in their accounts had been quickly transferred via third-party payment platforms, affecting many victims.

Consumers also reflected that dishes offered in restaurant group buys, compared to their original prices, were significantly reduced in quantity. Issues such as reduced quality of goods or services, refusal to provide goods or services according to group buy descriptions, substituting inferior goods, and discrepancies with physical items are common complaints.

7. Catering Service Complaints

Expensive tea drinking during the Asian Games holiday

Data: According to statistics, in 2010, there were 3,612 complaints and inquiries regarding catering services, including 1,438 accepted complaints, representing a year-on-year increase of 50%. Especially during holidays, there were an average of four complaints per day.

Complaints: Poor hygiene, poor food quality, arbitrary charges, additional service fees, undisclosed minimum consumption and corkage fees.

Case: A consumer reflected that on November 26th last year, they drank tea at a hotel. At checkout, the hotel claimed that an additional 10% fee would be charged as it was a holiday. The consumer believed this was unreasonable, as November 26th was a Friday, merely a day off due to the Asian Games in Guangzhou, not a national statutory holiday. They believed the merchant did not disclose this beforehand and demanded a refund of the excess charge.

8. Beauty Service Complaints

Once you get a membership card, refunds are impossible

Data: In 2010, there were a total of 2,288 complaints and inquiries regarding the beauty industry, including 794 accepted complaints, representing a year-on-year increase of 55%.

Complaints: Traps in prepaid card services, false advertising, misleading consumption, arbitrary charges.

Case: A woman complained that in May last year, she went to a store to inquire about relevant beauty projects. After persuasion by the store staff, she tried a free wellness treatment, with them emphasizing throughout that no fees would be charged. At the time, the consumer signed a blank receipt at their request and then proceeded with the trial. After the trial, the operator filled in the charge amount on the blank receipt where the consumer had signed, claiming the treatment required a payment of 12,000 yuan. The consumer was forced to transfer 12,000 yuan in three installments to the company, during which time they were threatened by the store staff. The consumer felt deceived and demanded a refund.

9. Real Estate Brokerage Service Complaints

Concealing defects for double profits

Data: In 2010, there were a total of 967 complaints and inquiries regarding real estate intermediaries, including 376 accepted complaints.

Complaints: Concealing the true condition of properties, refusing to return earnest money, charging intermediary fees under false pretenses.

Case: A consumer reflected that when purchasing a second-hand house through the Yu Feng Real Estate Intermediary Company, the company concealed actual defects of the property, leading to the inability to reach a purchase (lease) agreement. The intermediary company claimed they had already provided services and would charge a service fee. Moreover, it cannot be ruled out that some intermediary companies collude with sellers to defraud buyers of service fees.

10. Domestic Service Complaints

Lack of experience yet working as a nanny

Data: In 2010, there were a total of 249 consultations and complaints regarding domestic services, including 175 accepted complaints, representing an increase of 94% over the previous year, with a rapid upward trend.

Complaints: Low service quality, causing property damage or personal injury.

Case: Ms. Bao reflected that she hired a nanny through a certain domestic service company. Due to having a young child, she required someone with experience. At the time, the company recommended someone claiming to have 4-5 years of work experience. Ms. Bao signed a one-year contract with the company, paying an intermediary fee of 2,680 yuan and a deposit of 2,000 yuan. However, upon returning home, she discovered that the nanny did not know how to take care of children and could not even use a baby bottle. Later, the nanny admitted to only taking care of a seven-year-old child for six months and had never worked as a nanny before. Ms. Bao believed the domestic service company had deceived her and demanded a full refund.

Recipients travel hundreds of miles to retrieve 'express deliveries'

The Guangdong Postal Administration provides advice on express delivery delays, allowing consumers to file complaints by calling 12305.

Yangcheng Evening News reporters Tan Chao and Chen Qiang report: Nowadays, with the rapid development of online shopping, "calling for express delivery" has quietly become a new way of life for people. However, the quality of express delivery services has consistently failed to gain the approval of consumers: some express deliveries become slow deliveries, taking a month to arrive; some recipients end up becoming fetchers, even traveling hundreds of miles to pick up packages.

Recipients travel hundreds of miles to pick up packages

On January 17th this year, Mr. Yang summoned a business representative from Yunda Express to send a New Year's gift to his friend. After asking whether the package could be delivered to Wuxing Farm in Nanchang, Jiangxi within three days, the representative gave a positive response. After weighing, signing, and paying 35 yuan for express delivery fees, Mr. Yang excitedly told his friend, "The gift will be in your hands within three days."

However, after three days passed, Mr. Yang's friend had not received any package. Anxious because the gifts were all food, Mr. Yang called the express company, receiving the reply: "The package has reached a point in Nanchang, 50 kilometers away from the destination." The express company also stated, "Now we can only deliver to this point in Nanchang, unable to send it to Wuxing Farm. Please have the recipient come to pick it up, otherwise the package will be sent back to Guangzhou." Mr. Yang was very upset, as the items sent were all food with short shelf lives, which might spoil upon return. "In the end, my friend had to drive 50 kilometers to pick up the package, a round trip of 100 miles. Neither the transportation costs nor the time spent were compensated, and I could only accept my misfortune!"

Regarding Mr. Yang's experience, the person in charge of the complaint hotline at Yunda Express's Shanghai headquarters stated, "The sender should negotiate with the local branch and the receiving courier to resolve the issue, or complain to the headquarters. Since Yunda Express operates on a franchise basis, the headquarters does not bear compensation responsibilities."

However, Mr. Yang told reporters that after the incident occurred, he immediately found the courier who received the package, but received an unfriendly response: "Once the package is sent out, it's none of my concern. You need to deal with the receiving location yourself."

"Fast" delivery takes a month to arrive

On January 2nd this year, Miss Xia purchased a brand of thermal underwear on Taobao. After shipping, the Taobao store owner informed Miss Xia, "The item has been sent via Shentong Express to Guangzhou." On January 5th, a staff member from the Longdong branch of Guangzhou Shentong contacted Miss Xia, saying, "The package has arrived, please arrange a time for delivery." However, since Miss Xia was out of town on a business trip, the express company proactively suggested changing the delivery address for someone else to collect it on her behalf.

Miss Xia thought this idea was good and promptly informed the express company of an address that could receive deliveries 24 hours a day. However, after more than ten days had passed, Miss Xia had returned from her business trip, yet the item was still not received. She repeatedly contacted the express company, always receiving the response: "The goods are at the transfer station." Finally, nearly a month after the mail was sent, Miss Xia finally received the thermal underwear that had been transferred around for a month. She helplessly said, "I've spent quite a bit on phone calls, and the express company often just apologizes and that's that. Am I supposed to expend more energy seeking compensation? Wouldn't that be even more troublesome!"

Expressed food gets stolen

Citizen Mr. Xi complained, "Items sent through express delivery have multiple times been opened, or eaten and replaced with other items. This isn't the first or second time I've encountered this."

He said that last November, a friend sent 12 pounds of dried fruit, but upon receiving it, there were only 5 pounds left. Even the deliveryman found it absurd and suggested rejecting the package. "After multiple complaints, the issue remains unresolved to this day... And the whereabouts of the remaining 5 pounds is unknown."

Similarly, last November, food sent by others was received with vacuum-packed food bags that had been opened. More absurdly, after eating, they were sealed again with the company's packing tape. "The express company itself admits that there's nothing they can do, as their couriers have high turnover rates and low quality, so they suggest not mailing food... What kind of explanation is that?" Official Response: In case of express delivery delays, call 12305 to file a complaint

The Guangdong Postal Administration stated that according to relevant provisions of postal industry standards, consumers encountering express delivery delays should file a claim for compensation within one year from the date of sending the package to the express service organization. For issues regarding whether to compensate, the amount of compensation, or the payment of compensation, prior negotiation should take place. If negotiations fail, legal complaints can be filed with the express service organization. Within 30 days of receiving consumer complaints (60 days for international business), if the express service organization fails to respond, or if the consumer is dissatisfied with the handling or response of the express service organization, the consumer may file a complaint with the 12305 Postal Industry Consumer Complaint Center via telephone, letter, fax, or internet. The Guangdong Postal Administration reminds:

● Consumers should choose reputable express enterprises with proper credentials to send packages;

● Valuable items should preferably be insured or use valuation services;

● Except for letters, when receiving mail or packages, the recipient or proxy should inspect the contents before signing;

● Carefully read the terms and conditions of the express enterprise when sending packages, properly fill out the waybill content, and retain relevant vouchers.
