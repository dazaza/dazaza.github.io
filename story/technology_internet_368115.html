History of iPod

by shootero on 2010-10-15 15:32:19

Attention, history class today is the development history of iPod. Since the first iPod was born on October 23, 2001, the entire iPod family has gone through a 9-year development history, and its influence has been increasing. Gizmodo released an infographic summarizing the evolution of the iPod series products. In 2001:

- On January 9, 2001, iTunes 1.0 was launched, most of whose source code came from SoundJamMP acquired by Apple in 2000.

- On October 23, 2001, Apple launched the first generation of iPod with a maximum capacity of 10GB, 10 hours of battery life, and priced at $499.

In 2002:

- The second generation of iPod was released, with the maximum capacity increased to 20GB. At the same time, iPod supported Windows PC synchronization.

- The first limited edition iPods were launched in December, costing an additional $49, allowing users to choose signatures or logos of No Doubt, Madonna, Tony Hawk, or Beck engraved on the back of the iPod.

In 2003:

- The third generation of iPod was released. The price remained unchanged, the capacity increased to 40GB, and the battery life decreased to 8 hours. The product added a touch wheel function to control music playback.

- Apple added a dock data interface for iPod.

In 2004:

- The fourth generation of iPod was released, with a capacity of 40GB, the price dropped to $399, and the battery life increased to 12 hours. Additionally, the color screen iPod Photo was launched.

- The first generation of iPod mini was released, priced at $249, with a capacity of 4GB and 8 hours of battery life.

- The U2 limited edition iPod was released, with a capacity of 30GB, priced at $349, featuring red and black colors, and engraved with the signatures of the entire U2 band.

In 2005:

- The fifth generation of iPod was upgraded to iPod Video, with a maximum capacity of 80GB and playtime increased to 14 to 20 hours.

- The first generation of iPod nano was launched, priced at $249, with a capacity of 4GB and 14 hours of battery life.

- The first generation of iPod Shuffle was released, priced at $149, with a capacity of 1GB and 12 hours of battery life. After its release, sales reached 1.8 million units in the first quarter, with the entire iPod product line selling a total of 5.8 million units during the same period.

In 2006:

- The second generation of iPod nano was released, with the capacity increased to 8GB and battery life extended to 24 hours.

- The second generation of iPod Shuffle was released, priced at $79.

- Apple no longer included CDs with the purchase of iPod.

In 2007:

- The sixth generation of iPod: iPod Classic was released, with a maximum capacity of 160GB, playtime of 30 to 40 hours, and priced at $349.

- The third generation of iPod nano was released, with changes in the body appearance.

- The first generation of iPod touch was launched, with a capacity of 16GB and priced at $399.

In 2008:

- The iPod Classic underwent minor modifications, with the price reduced to $249 and capacity increased to 120GB.

- The fourth generation of iPod nano was released, with the price reduced to $199 and capacity increased to 16GB.

- The second generation of iPod touch was launched, with capacity increased to 32GB.

In 2009:

- The fifth generation of iPod nano was released, with the price reduced to $179.

- The third generation of iPod Shuffle was released, with capacity increased to 4GB and price reduced to $99.

- The third generation of iPod touch was released, with maximum capacity increased to 64GB.

In 2010:

- The sixth generation of iPod nano was released, adopting a full-touchscreen design.

- The fourth generation of iPod Shuffle was released, priced at $49 with a maximum capacity of 2GB.

- The fourth generation of iPod touch was released, maintaining the same configuration as the iPhone: retina screen, A4 processor.

- As of now, the total sales volume of all iPod series products is 269,490,000.
