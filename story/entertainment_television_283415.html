The TV series *We Are Like Sunflowers* is adapted from the famous writer He Dun's eponymous novel. It serves as a chronicle of youth in the 1970s, evoking nostalgia among older generations and fascination among younger viewers. The story depicts the happy days of that era and the harsh realities of youth through the eyes of its characters.

**Plot Introduction:**

Feng Jianjun is an orphan who lost both his parents at a young age. His unfortunate childhood forced him to grow up quickly and begin navigating the complexities of society at an early age. Alongside his childhood friends Liu Jianguo, Li Yuejin, and He Bin, they swore brotherhood, with Feng Jianjun being respected as the "eldest brother."

One day, He Bin (the second brother) was extorted by a local thug named Wang Xiangyang. To help He Bin reclaim the money that was extorted, Feng Jianjun confronted Wang Xiangyang and ignited a bomb in front of him. In the explosion, Feng Jianjun was injured in the head by a flying rock from the blast.

(Note: Below is a general outline of the storyline for the series.)

1. **Early Life and Brotherhood:** The story begins with Feng Jianjun’s tragic childhood and the formation of the four-brother pact. They vow to protect each other amidst the challenges of life in the 1970s.

2. **Struggles Against Bullies:** The group faces various conflicts with local thugs, including the incident where Feng Jianjun risks his life to recover He Bin’s stolen money.

3. **Growing Pains:** As the brothers grow older, their lives take different paths. Feng Jianjun finds love with Peng Chang'e, while others face their own struggles and aspirations.

4. **Social Turmoil:** The narrative delves into societal issues of the time, such as poverty, class divisions, and the impact of political movements on personal lives.

5. **Love and Betrayal:** Romantic relationships develop between the main characters, bringing both joy and heartbreak. Feng Jianjun and Peng Chang'e’s relationship becomes central to the plot.

6. **Challenges of Adulthood:** Each character faces significant life decisions, including career choices, family responsibilities, and moral dilemmas.

7. **Final Resolution:** By the end of the series, the characters have grown and matured, learning valuable lessons about life, friendship, and resilience. The conclusion ties together their individual journeys and offers a poignant reflection on the passage of time.

This heartfelt drama captures the essence of a bygone era, resonating deeply with audiences who lived through it or are curious about the past.
