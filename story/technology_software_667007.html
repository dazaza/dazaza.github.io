I want to divorce after seeing my mother-in-law's text message.

by da2j4g9z5o9 on 2012-02-16 13:03:47

Now, many parents, especially the parents of the post-80s generation, do not realize that after marriage, their sons take on another role: husband. From childhood to adulthood, they have handled everything for their children and want to continue doing so; otherwise, they feel uneasy.

In the divorce office of the Yinzhou Civil Affairs Bureau, a young couple born in 1984 came to file for divorce. As the young man was filling out the divorce application form, tears began streaming down his face. A moment later, he started wiping his nose and tears onto the floor... Staff member Xiao Yu, seeing this scene, quickly pulled out a roll of toilet paper and handed it over: "What's going on? Did you two have an argument?" The young man sobbed uncontrollably.

"Never mind him, just finish the paperwork and get divorced," the girl shot a glance at the young man and continued filling out the forms. After a few seconds, the man stopped writing and said, "I don't want a divorce anymore," and walked towards the door. Seeing this, the girl suddenly turned around, dropped to her knees naked, and pleaded, "Just let me go, if you don't agree to the divorce, I'll harm myself!"

Hearing the girl's threat, the young man gave in, silently completed all the procedures, crumpled up the wet roll of toilet paper in his hand, and threw it into the trash can before walking away. The girl did not leave immediately but sat down and told staff member Xiao Yu about her four-month marriage. Her father-in-law and mother-in-law called their son three times a day.

Xiao Zhang and his wife Xiao Yuan are from the same hometown in Hunan province. They met through family introductions and got married under the urging of their parents on August 8th of this year. Before the wedding, her in-laws had already purchased a 100-square-meter apartment near the Wanda Plaza as their matrimonial home, fully covering all renovation and appliance costs without asking for any contribution from Xiao Yuan’s family. Even before the wedding, her in-laws often bought clothes and jewelry for Xiao Yuan. Although the marriage happened somewhat hastily, it still made Xiao Yuan's friends extremely envious.

However, problems arose after the marriage. "My father-in-law and mother-in-law are too involved in our affairs. Although they live far away in their hometown, they still control us remotely every day," Xiao Yuan complained. She added that her in-laws would call them three times a day—morning, noon, and night. Moreover, her husband was very dependent on his parents. Whenever they argued, her in-laws would call, scolding Xiao Yuan regardless of who was right or wrong.

"For example, once I accidentally stained one of my husband's clothes while washing, and his mother found out and called to scold me," Xiao Yuan said. Such incidents occurred almost every day! What broke her completely was when one day her mother-in-law sent a text message to her husband using her father-in-law's phone, asking how many times they had sexual intercourse in a week! And demanded that it should not exceed three times per week!

How could such private matters be interfered with by her in-laws? After seeing this message, Xiao Yuan argued with her husband again and expressed her desire to divorce. To her surprise, her husband then told his parents about their private argument. Her father-in-law even sent a text message saying that if she wanted a divorce, she must return all the money spent on her! "I was shocked and scared. Since we don't have a child yet, it's better to live separately," Xiao Yuan said, "I am still young, and I don't want to be controlled by them."

The excessive care and interference from the in-laws will surely suffocate the daughter-in-law. Ningbo WeiQing Marriage Counseling National Level II Consultant Zhou Minming believes that the breakdown of this marriage is closely related to the overly caring behavior of the in-laws.

Nowadays, many parents, especially the parents of the post-80s generation, fail to realize that after marriage, their sons take on another role: husband. From childhood to adulthood, they have handled everything for their children and want to continue doing so; otherwise, they feel uneasy.

Excessive care and excessive interference in the affairs of the young couple will inevitably lead to conflicts in perspectives. When even such intimate matters are being managed, the daughter-in-law will definitely feel overwhelmed. Parents should let go and allow their children to live their own lives. Trying to manage everything will only backfire. If they really want to help, it should be in helping their children establish a sense of family responsibility and enabling them to psychologically mature.
