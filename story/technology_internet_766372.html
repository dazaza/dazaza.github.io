Live Broadcast of Apple iPhone 5S/iPhone 5C Release (Images + Videos)

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:37:04

September is a month full of powerful new device releases, and Apple, as an industry benchmark, has also chosen this time to unveil its new phone. Unlike previous years, Apple will break tradition by simultaneously releasing two new phones for the first time, which we all know are the iPhone 5S and the iPhone 5C.

The iPhone 5S is an upgraded version of the iPhone 5, featuring the latest A7 processor, which is 31% more powerful than the A6 processor used in the iPhone 5. It uses a quad-core PowerVR SGX554 MP4 GPU with 1GB LPDDR3-1333MHz memory. It also includes a 12-megapixel camera and dual LED flash.

The budget-friendly iPhone 5C has garnered even more attention because who doesn't love a bargain? The iPhone 5C is almost identical to the iPhone 5 but may use a dual-core A6 processor, a 4-inch IPS display with a resolution of 1136x640, 1GB RAM, and internal storage options of 16/32/64GB. The main camera has been reduced to 5 megapixels, while the front-facing camera remains at 1.2 megapixels. Its dimensions are 124.2x58.4x7.6 mm, weighing 106 grams, and it runs the latest iOS 7 system. Due to its plastic body, production costs have significantly decreased, offering various color options.

In the past, Apple always managed to surprise people, not only through innovation but also through excellent confidentiality. However, before this year's event even began, we were already fully aware of the details of the upcoming products, which could greatly reduce the excitement of the event. This might leave the iPhone 5S/5C in the same predicament as last year's iPhone 5.

We already know the specifications of the new devices and what iOS 7 looks like. The only things left to look forward to during the event are the feature demonstrations and pricing information.

Apple’s online store has already gone offline for updates, clearly for reasons that need no explanation—Apple routinely temporarily shuts down its online store before new product launches.

Can Apple still bring us an interesting event despite the heavy leaks? Let's find out at the launch venue in San Francisco.

The event will officially start at 1 AM Beijing Time, held at Apple's headquarters in San Francisco.

There are always queues at Apple events, and there is already a substantial line well before the event starts.

This is what the media invitation looked like.

There are quite a few people at the venue. It's unclear how it compares to the scale of Xiaomi 3's launch event. Xiaomi had to hold two consecutive events due to the overwhelming number of fans, but it seems unlikely Apple would do the same.

Cook has entered! The event should begin promptly at 1 AM.

Apple executives are taking their places on stage.

True to form, it's an all-Apple-device setup at the event.

An armored truck appears—what does Apple plan to do with it?

To everyone's surprise, former U.S. Vice President Al Gore made an appearance at the venue.

The event started on time, with Cook taking the stage.

Cook jumped straight into the presentation, thanking everyone for coming. He then proceeded to give a visual and numerical overview of Apple's developments and changes over the past year, starting with iTunes Festival.

iTunes Festival, the online music festival where Apple offers free tickets, saw applications from 20 million netizens. Apple conducted live streaming.

Cook mentioned the seventh edition of iTunes Festival, held in London, featuring three nights of live music with performances from Lady Gaga, Katy Perry, and other famous singers.

As usual, Cook concluded with a discussion about Apple's retail stores worldwide.

A picture was shown of an Apple Store located in Stanford, small in size but receiving over 2,000 customers daily.

Apple's new retail store design was showcased, including a bright glass roof and clearly separated sales and service areas.

iOS 7 made its debut, with Cook stating that Apple is putting its full effort into perfecting iOS 7 this month.

Craig Federighi, head of iOS software, took the stage.

He introduced iOS 7, covering features such as the lock screen, home screen layout, Control Center, Notification Center, etc. Many watching the live stream have likely already experienced these features.

iOS 7 photo album introduction

iOS 7 supports all currently available Apple devices, including iPhones from the iPhone 4 onwards, iPad 2, iPad mini, etc.

The official release of iOS 7 is scheduled for September 18th, likely coinciding with the release of the new devices.

Cook returned to the stage to introduce the new iWork application. The updated iWork adopts the flat design style of iOS 7, providing robust image, text, and video processing capabilities. Cook announced that iWork applications are entirely free!

Cook: "Now I want to talk about the iPhone, you might be excited."

Next up is the budget-friendly iPhone 5C content, but most of the leaked information has already been revealed, so much of it is familiar to us.

The iPhone 5C carries all the technology of the iPhone 5, with added fun and more vibrant colors.

Poly carbonate material shell, with a steel internal structure.

Specs are essentially a copy of the iPhone 5.

The price under a two-year contract is quite affordable, but dare you reveal the unlocked price?

A case alone costs $29, creating countless business opportunities for knock-off manufacturers.

It's now time for the iPhone 5S.

You can already see the larger LED lights, with dual LED flashlights for photography.

       The iPhone 5S will come in a new color option, gold (champagne), making a total of three colors: slate gray, gold, and silver.

World's first 64-bit mobile chip, A7, with 64-bit desktop architecture, measuring 102 square millimeters with over 1 billion transistors. Dual-register design and floating-point performance, capable of running both 32-bit and 64-bit applications simultaneously.

Speed comparison

Uses OpenGLES 3.0

They started demonstrating "Infinity Blade 3," a game series considered a benchmark for 3D gaming on mobile devices.

The iPhone 5S comes with the A7 processor and an additional co-processor, M7. It measures data from the accelerometer, compass, and gyroscope to inform users about their motion status.

Battery life remains a sore point for Apple, as the battery size is limited.

Larger pixel points, 1.5 microns

New imaging principle, essentially taking multiple shots and selecting the best one. Equipped with dual LED flashlights.

The iPhone 5S will support 720P HD video recording at 120fps, with higher frame rates leading to smoother footage.

Sample images look decent, though night-time shots are missing.

Now moving onto security issues, traditional unlocking methods aren't secure enough, and the iPhone 5S brings a new fingerprint recognition feature.

New Touch ID sensor, only 170 micrometers thick, with a precision of 500ppi, capable of accurate readings from any direction.

A new HOME key designed for fingerprint recognition, indeed using sapphire material.

However, the fingerprint recognition system requires the support of the A7 processor, leaving the iPhone 5C without this feature.

When it comes to iPhone releases in mainland China, it's usually filled with disappointment, but this time Apple delivered. China is included in the initial launch list, with iPhone 5C pre-orders starting on September 13th and its official release on the 20th. iPhone 5S pre-orders begin on the 20th.

The iPhone 4S now also has an 8GB version.

Page 15: iPhone 5S/5C Event: Cook's Closing Remarks

Cook returned to the stage for his concluding remarks, followed by a performance by Elvis Costello.

Apple's website has also been updated with data. The price of the iPhone 5S is reasonable, while the iPhone 5C starts at 4488 yuan, which makes me want to curse.

Costello shook hands with Cook after his performance.

The event is over.

In summary, this event did not offer much excitement, similar to the iPhone 5, as too many leaks diminished our sense of novelty. The final announced prices were also unsatisfactory. No one expected the iPhone 5C to cost 4488 yuan, making the so-called budget-friendly iPhone 5C anything but cheap. The only positive aspect is that mainland China will finally enjoy synchronized releases.
