Panasonic energy-efficient fluorescent lamp: 1 bulb is equivalent to 2.

by kdchen5188 on 2010-04-10 21:55:19

At the international exhibition area of the recently held 2008 China Beijing International Energy Saving and Environmental Protection Exhibition, Panasonic Electric Works' latest energy-saving and environmental protection products attracted many viewers and lighting manufacturers. A Panasonic Electric Works executive introduced to reporters that this is Panasonic's newly launched e—Hf high-efficiency lead-free energy-saving fluorescent lamp, which applies lead-free technology to high-efficiency fluorescent glass tubes, increasing the glass transmittance, enhancing the phosphor luminous efficiency, improving the brightness of the tube lighting, making it 1.6 times brighter than ordinary fluorescent lamps, saving 32% electricity, and extending its lifespan by 1.5 times.

According to Ito, President of Panasonic Lighting and also President of the Japanese Lighting Industry Association, Panasonic has established a complete system for energy saving, environmental protection, and recycling from manufacturing, use, to the final recycling stage. This achieves "large-scale energy saving" in society and conveys the concept of "coexistence with the Earth's environment" to the Chinese market. They also hope to contribute to the construction of a resource-conserving and environmentally friendly society in China through the promotion and use of e—Hf high-efficiency energy-saving lamps.

"Panasonic e—Hf high-efficiency energy-saving fluorescent lamps, one equals two traditional fluorescent lamps," Ito made an efficiency calculation. If 2 million of these fluorescent lamps are promoted in the Chinese market, the "saving one" substitution effect will reduce the production of 2 million traditional fluorescent lamps in China. Its environmental protection and energy-saving benefits are: reducing mercury usage by 1 million mg per year, protecting 360 million cubic meters of water sources; saving 255 million kWh of electricity annually, equivalent to reducing carbon dioxide emissions by 250,000 tons.
