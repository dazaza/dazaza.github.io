Letter of Credit Process:

The process of documentary letter of credit operations is briefly described as follows:

1. Both parties specify the use of a documentary letter of credit for payment in the trade contract.

2. The buyer instructs the local bank (issuing bank) to open a letter of credit naming the seller as the beneficiary.

3. The issuing bank requests another bank to notify or confirm the letter of credit.

4. The advising bank notifies the seller that the letter of credit has been opened.

5. The seller receives the letter of credit and ensures they can fulfill the conditions specified in the credit, then ships the goods.

6. The seller submits documents to the designated bank. This bank could be the issuing bank or the bank specified in the letter of credit for payment, acceptance, or negotiation.

7. The bank reviews the documents according to the letter of credit. If the documents comply with the credit, the bank will make payment, accept, or negotiate according to the terms of the credit.

8. Banks other than the issuing bank send the documents to the issuing bank.

9. After reviewing the documents without errors, the issuing bank repays the bank that has made payment, accepted, or negotiated according to the credit in the pre-agreed manner.

10. After the buyer pays, the issuing bank releases the documents, and then the buyer takes delivery of the goods with the documents.
