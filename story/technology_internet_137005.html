Future development direction of the training portal

by 304885068 on 2009-04-02 15:49:30

When it comes to training portals, the first things that come to mind are websites like TianTian Training, Beida Qingniao, and NetEase Academy. In iResearch's classification, training portals also include China Training Network, QQ Training Center, Yahoo Training China, and others.

Let’s look at the definition of a training portal: Portal (portal), Training (to cultivate). It is an essential portal system for trainers, career managers, and administrators.

Now, let's analyze the future development of training websites (portals) by expanding on five key points.

### First Point: Broad Interpretation

The Web 2.0 era is an application framework that integrates various application systems, data resources, and Internet resources into a single information management platform, providing users with a unified user interface. This allows enterprises to quickly establish information channels between businesses and customers, businesses and internal employees, as well as businesses and other businesses, enabling companies to release various types of information stored internally and externally.

### Second Point: Narrow Interpretation

A training portal refers to an application system that provides access to comprehensive Internet information resources and offers relevant information services. Initially, training portals provided search engines and network access services. However, due to increasingly fierce market competition, training portals had to rapidly expand their business types, hoping to attract and retain Internet users through a wide range of training services. As a result, current portal sites have become all-encompassing, becoming the "department store" or "online supermarket" of the network world.

### Third Point: From Service Function Perspective

When browsing training portals regularly, one might notice significant differences in today’s training websites. Firstly, regarding search services, my earliest experience with search engines was on large news websites. However, these have been used less frequently since I discovered Baidu and Google, making me rarely use the search function on portals.

As for the training email system, users over the years tend to prefer practicality. Therefore, websites focusing on practicality dominate the majority of the email service market share.

### Fourth Point: From Content Presentation Perspective

In terms of content, it seems like all portal sites have similar content. You would find the same training information on website A as you would on website B, and similarly on website C, with only slight differences in wording, layout arrangement, and photo angles. When you haven't finished reading all the training information, you may realize you're doing the same thing repeatedly. For Internet users, instead of reading news from so many training portals, it might be better to just stick to one portal site.

The above are some issues encountered during work and corresponding solutions, but this situation has prompted me to reflect on the direction of development for training portals. The extensive and comprehensive information base of training portals will undoubtedly remain a fundamental aspect of portals, which is certain and unchangeable. The key lies in creating distinctive content and having a loyal user base. Only by owning a large number of loyal users can the influence of the portal spread more deeply and possess greater media value.

### Fifth Point: Therefore, I have reached a rough conclusion, hoping to see some breakthroughs in content on training portals, cultivating truly loyal user groups, thereby enhancing purchasing desire.
