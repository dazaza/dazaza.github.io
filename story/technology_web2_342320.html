There's only the fragrance of Gardenia on you - Bi Shumin

by gfrw3430 on 2010-04-08 15:59:22

At this moment, a young man came over and said a sentence to her: "I pity you." He pitied the taste of her body. Half-scared, she still woke up to the realization that love had not abandoned her, but had surged into her life. She was not mentally prepared, nor did she know if she could still love, or whether she should be candid with him about her past. She only knew that he was quite an unsuitable young man, and she couldn't return his feelings like an indigenous person in India would throw back a "boomerang". She began to love obsessively and painfully, and the most obvious change was that she started bathing more frequently.

Behind all the reversals and difficulties, she didn’t expect that the arrival of new students would disrupt the original tranquility. One day, while walking on the playground, she felt as if struck by lightning, her heart shattered. She heard a familiar accent from the big town where she used to live; a freshman had come from there. No matter how much she pretended to have forgotten, this boy quickly recognized her.

She was very afraid, foreseeing some painful encounter, like the sound of a fierce storm bringing the smell of blood.

Sure enough, not long after, news about her youthful indiscretions spread throughout the school. Her boyfriend asked her if it was true? Disappointed, this disappointment made her become reckless, and she stared at him fiercely and said, "It's true! So what?" The young man was also no fool, and he straightforwardly said, "Even if it's true, I still love you!" In that instant, she felt the heavens and earth transform; to have such a lover in this world, what else did she have to fear? Thus, they decided together to deal with the gossipy girl who had spread the rumors. They found her by the river and asked her, "Why do you speak ill of us?"

The girl seemed somewhat remorseful but appeared even more justified, saying, "I didn't speak ill of you. I only told a fact about her." She even boldly stared at the girl who hated bathing and said, "Can you deny that this is a fact?"

The girl who hated bathing suddenly smelled the scent of the vagrant from her youth, feeling that the vagrant must have possessed this girl, trying every way to find her and take away the happiness she had gained through great hardship. Years of accumulated resentment boiled over, and she lunged forward, tearing at the mouth of the gossipy girl. She shouted at her boyfriend, "Let’s kill her!"

The young man's crab-like hands gripped the neck of the gossipy girl.

Unexpectedly, people were so fragile; like a little trumpet flower, with minimal force, she stopped breathing.

The girl looked straight at me and spoke calmly. I guessed she must have replayed the scene countless times in her mind, unsure why life was so fragile, and deeply confused for both herself and others.

The lovers were panic-stricken. They looked at the gossiper who had just been compassionate but now had no life left, unsure of what to do next.

"Let's run, to the ends of the earth. When we can't run anymore, let's agree to die together," they almost simultaneously said.

They lay side by side on the banks of the river where the murder took place, without any attempt to conceal themselves. They felt she might suddenly wake up. They hastily took some money and boarded a boat. Afraid to use the main roads, they aimlessly fled towards the wilderness, claiming they were traveling to avoid an early marriage. The money ran out quickly, and they ended up in a deep mountain area in Yunnan called “Lovers’ Cliff,” planning to hold hands and jump off the cliff.

Thus, they spent their last bit of money inviting the townspeople to a good meal before carrying out their suicide plan. The townspeople said, "Your voices sound like those in the national news broadcast. Are you from Nanjing?" Since they were going to die anyway, they confessed without hesitation.

"I've always wanted to see Beijing. At my age, I thought I'd never get to see it. But now seeing two people from Beijing is a blessing," the elder said, using all his resources to give them a sumptuous meal, refusing payment under any circumstances.

They ate a lot with their heads bowed. This was the last meal of their lives, so why not eat until full? Before eating, they felt grateful but also ashamed, discussing and deciding that they couldn't die here. Because the dense forest below the steep mountain would soon reveal their bodies, and the elder would recognize them, which would sadden him. These two people from Nanjing he had seen in his lifetime were fugitives wanted by the police. It wasn't just about Nanjing; they couldn't betray this kind elder.

They left Lovers' Cliff, wandering further without direction. Eventually, someone suggested that since death was inevitable, rather than living elsewhere, they might as well die right here.

As soon as they returned home, they were arrested.

After telling me everything, she then asked, "Can you smell the strange odor on me?"

I said, "I only smell a pleasant fragrance of gardenia on you."

She bitterly cried and said, "This is a very special fragrance, but it doesn't last long. I'm not talking about that smell, it's something else... Can you understand human nature... Can you smell it?"

I firmly replied, "Apart from the smell of gardenia, I don't smell anything else."

She looked at me doubtfully and remained silent. After a long time, she slowly said, "In this life and the next, I will never see him again. Even if there is a next life, amidst the vast ocean of humanity, where could we possibly meet! Niulang and Zhinü may also be a married couple, but they meet once a year on the Magpie Bridge. What a beautiful and delicate bridge it is. As for us, if we ever met, it would only be on the Naihe Bridge. That bridge has white bones as its pillars, and the stream beneath isn't water, but blood..."

I looked at her, filled with sorrow. A girl, suffering severe psychological and emotional trauma from a young age, living a defiled life in society. All this stunted her psychological development. A single harsh word from the thug of her youth controlled her thoughts and actions. As she grew older, it wasn't easy to restore a little dignity, finding a girl who hated herself. Yet due to this darkness, not only did she drag herself into the abyss, but also led the one who loved her into hell.

Bystanders see clearly. We all saw the crux of the matter. But as the party involved, she struggled painfully in the darkness, colliding headfirst until blood flowed, yet powerless to escape the knot of that shackle.

The wounds on the body may heal naturally over time, but the scars on the heart are difficult to repair on their own. The only thing we can rely on is the passage of time. However, some wounds, though buried deeper by time, are temporarily invisible on the surface, but deep down, they still have deep channels. When unexpected changes occur, the scars erupt violently, causing excruciating pain.

Each of us has a record of our mental history hidden in the multifaceted compartments of our minds. Regarding those most secret scars, except for ourselves, no one knows how many tears and blood have dripped onto the ancient pages. Don't hope for them to disappear naturally; that's just a wishful myth.

Taking the initiative to uncover and treat old memories is a task requiring courage and perseverance. Therefore, many people prefer to delude themselves, unwilling to清醒地clearly burn their mental garbage. Those ghosts may unexpectedly manifest at some unforeseen moment, leading us astray.
