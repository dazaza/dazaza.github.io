Treatment for vulvar leukoplakia requires a "prolonged battle"

by bailingdanyanshu on 2012-03-03 16:19:55

Recently, I have often felt unbearable itching in the vulva. After scratching and breaking the skin, it often causes pain. The skin of the vulva has also become rough and thickened. Sometimes, even having sexual intercourse with my partner becomes difficult, which has seriously affected the quality of life. After diagnosis at the hospital, I was told that I have vulvar leukoplakia, which is difficult to treat and tends to recur. Can this disease be cured?

Vulvar leukoplakia, also known as chronic vulvar dystrophy, is a common chronic disease with unknown causes. It is indeed difficult to treat and requires long-term persistent treatment with regular follow-up visits. For lesions with hyperplasia and thickening of the skin and mucous membranes, local application of retinoic acid ointment or topical corticosteroids such as betamethasone cream, triamcinolone acetonide cream, and hydrocortisone cream can be used to improve the thickened skin and mucous membranes and relieve itching symptoms. For localized sclerotic atrophic, dry, and thin lesions, topical testosterone propionate cod liver oil ointment, progesterone cream, or sodium heparin ointment can be applied 3-4 times daily until the skin softens. Traditional Chinese medicine has accumulated certain experience in treating vulvar leukoplakia. By using oral and topical traditional Chinese medicines that clear heat and detoxify, promote diuresis and relieve itching, nourish blood and activate blood circulation, and soothe the liver and nourish yin, some results have been achieved. In recent years, laser therapy and focused ultrasound have also been used to alleviate the pain of some patients.

While adhering to long-term treatment, patients should also keep the vulvar skin and mucous membranes clean and dry. Avoid using alkaline cleaning liquids like soap for washing. When itching is severe, protective ointments can be applied. Try to avoid scratching as much as possible. Do not wear tight or poorly breathable synthetic underwear, and avoid eating spicy foods. All these measures help to alleviate the progression of the lesion and assist in the healing of the damaged skin. Patients with long-term unresolved conditions are advised to undergo a pathological biopsy to detect any malignant changes early, which will facilitate early surgical removal.
