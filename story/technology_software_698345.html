But feel like they haven't understood much either

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 08:50:45

Dear Friend,

I met my current boyfriend in middle school; he was the object of my secret admiration back then. After graduation, we lost touch until fate brought us together again. I confessed my feelings to him and he admitted that he liked another girl, a close classmate of ours. So, he neither accepted nor rejected me.

Our relationship continued like this for two years. During one Spring Festival, he confessed his feelings for me, saying that he didn't want to miss me before going abroad. That's when we officially started dating. Later, due to some reasons, he didn't stay long overseas and returned after about a month. He struggled to find a well-paying job in my city and kept complaining about not having enough experience. Due to conflicts with his manager, he left his job after less than a month.

During this period, I gave him quite a bit of money for rent, food, and other expenses. Even before we started dating, he had borrowed money from me. Since we were now together, I thought it wasn't necessary to keep track of these things anymore.

Eventually, he moved to another city. In fact, since we started dating, we have mostly been in a long-distance relationship except for the first month. But I felt we weren't getting closer emotionally. Long-distance relationships are exhausting. We would meet once a month, usually me visiting him because he didn't have money. When he visited me, we had to stay in a hotel (I live with my parents). He always said that coming to see me would cost hundreds of dollars, whereas going to his place wouldn't be as expensive. Sometimes, standing in his shoes, it seemed reasonable, so I went. However, this was hard to explain to my parents, and each time I went, I got scolded.

My family has always opposed him, thinking he is too immature. Moreover, his family isn't just poor; most importantly, they lack warmth. It's rare for them to eat together at the table, everyone eats separately, which I really can't stand.

As time went on, I feel our commitment to each other started to waver. Also, he keeps saying he can't give me much. Previously, when I asked him what age he planned to get married, he said 25, then 27. Now, he says he won't marry early, probably around 29 or 30. We are the same age, and I feel he gives me too many uncertainties. I sometimes think, if I ignored my family's opposition and his background, and then when I am 28 or 29, he suddenly tells me we aren't suitable, I will truly have no way out. Because of the distance, I feel our hearts are also drifting apart. We often argue, and I don't see our future.

Xiaolongnü, could you help me analyze this? I look forward to your reply!

Reply:

"The most important thing is the feeling of lacking togetherness; it's rare for the family to eat together at the table, everyone eats separately. This is something I really can't stand." I very much appreciate this sentence of yours.

A family gathering around the table every day to happily share a meal is an incredibly important matter. When I was young, although my family was poor, at meal times, my parents would cook in the kitchen while my siblings and I helped by shelling peas and picking vegetables. Then simple yet delicious meals would be served, and we would all eat happily together. My brother and I would talk about school, and our parents would discuss work. We would chat about the news. This is the moment when a family shares life and emotions. Many years later, recalling those moments still brings sweetness.

Therefore, eating together at the table, though an ordinary act, is the most crucial form of uniting a family in Chinese tradition. The fact that you notice and bring up this point shows that you have a strong need for "home." You need a responsible, family-oriented man to create a warm and harmonious home together.

I believe your current boyfriend is not such a person.

Firstly, after you confessed your feelings, even though he liked another girl, he did not immediately reject you but instead maintained an ambiguous relationship with you. Only when he was about to go abroad, perhaps realizing the fleeting nature of youth or limited opportunities overseas, did he accept your feelings. And in the case of a long-distance relationship, he was still concerned about spending money to visit you. All these points only indicate one thing: he doesn't love you enough.

Secondly, his working ability is not strong, and he lacks perseverance and resilience.

Thirdly, his family is also average. As you mentioned, it's not just about being poor; more importantly, there is a lack of a warm family atmosphere.

Fourthly, and most importantly, he does not plan to take responsibility for you. When a man says "I can't give you anything," he is already preparing an exit strategy for himself. His reluctance to get married or make promises means that when you hold him accountable in the future, he will say, "Didn't I tell you I couldn't give you anything?"

You see, with such a man, rather than wasting your youth with him, it would be better to step away early.
