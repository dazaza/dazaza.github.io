Sinusitis is a non-specific inflammation of the nasal sinus mucosa, and is a common and frequent disease in otorhinolaryngology. Sinusitis is a common disease, which can be divided into acute and chronic types.

Acute sinusitis can cause headache, chills, fever, purulent nasal discharge, general discomfort, fatigue, loss of appetite, etc. Children with acute sinusitis may have high fever, convulsions, vomiting, and diarrhea. Acute sinusitis can also spread outward, causing otitis media, pharyngitis, tonsillitis, and even sometimes rare orbital infections. The headache of sinusitis follows a certain pattern, varying with the infection of different sinuses.
