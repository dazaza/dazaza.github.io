Ms. Wang (in the circled area) is Robbed and Chases After the Motorcycle

■ By reporter Zhu Juan

At 2 PM on November 15, Ms. Wang, a 30-year-old company cashier, was walking in a less crowded area near Beicheng Tianjie in Guanyinqiao, Jiangbei District, when suddenly what felt like a "strong wind" passed by, and she realized that the 100,000 yuan she had just withdrawn was gone.

Ms. Wang ran to the Beicheng Tianjie police platform to report the case, trembling all over. She told the police officers, "One person robbed me, and two others were waiting for him—it happened so fast!" Ms. Wang said that after 1 PM, she went to the bank to withdraw money. Since the amount was large, she went to the VIP counter and withdrew 100,000 yuan in cash for the company. She cautiously looked around before leaving the bank and heading toward the flower market.

After walking for a few minutes, Ms. Wang reached outside the Walmart supermarket, and her tense nerves gradually relaxed. Suddenly, a hand violently snatched the bag hanging on her shoulder and fled at lightning speed. Although scared, Ms. Wang still chased after them. When she reached the intersection, the bandit hopped onto a motorcycle where two accomplices were waiting for him.

The three rode off on the motorcycle and disappeared from Ms. Wang's sight.

The police immediately formed a special investigation team and launched an investigation. Following the route Ms. Wang took to withdraw and leave the bank, the police reviewed the surveillance footage from the bank and outside the Walmart supermarket. Two cameras recorded the entire robbery process.

From the video, it can be seen that at 1:30 PM, not long after Ms. Wang entered the bank, a man followed her in. The man, in his twenties, with a buzz cut, wearing a dark blue jacket, kept looking in the direction of the VIP counter where Ms. Wang was located.

At 1:38 PM, after Ms. Wang put the 100,000 yuan she had withdrawn into her bag and left the bank, the man followed her out.

Around 1:43 PM, as Ms. Wang passed near the Walmart supermarket at Beicheng Tianjie, there were almost no people around. The man who had been following her rushed forward and snatched her bag from behind. Two men on motorcycles were waiting for him on the side of the road.

Through technical means, the police identified the identities of the three bandits and discovered that they had already left Chongqing, with two fleeing to Guizhou and one to Jiangsu. The Jiangbei police dispatched more than ten plainclothes officers to other provinces to apprehend them. On November 21, the plainclothes officers arrested one suspect in Kunshan, Jiangsu Province, and captured the other two in Meitan County, Guizhou Province. All 100,000 yuan that had been stolen were recovered.

The three suspects confessed that they had divided the loot in a 4-4-2 ratio and hadn't had time to squander it before being caught by the police.
