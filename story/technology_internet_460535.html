13-Year-Old Deaf-Mute Boy Drives Away on a Bus, Disappears After Leaving Home (Photo)

by yixiaoyanw62 on 2011-07-11 17:26:16

The little boy drove the bus along the bus route from Hongqiao to Leqing, then back to Hongqiao from Leqing. When it passed through the Puhu section, it collided with a pile of stones and stopped. The total distance driven was about 50 kilometers.

Zhejiang Online Hangzhou (reporter Jun Tong, correspondent Meili Qi) At 11:30 PM on April 9th, a bus collided with a minivan in front of Yonghe Soybean Milk at Zhenxing Middle Road in Hongqiao Town, Yueqing, and then fled.

On the evening of April 11th, the traffic police continued to expand their search range and conducted searches overnight in the Hongqiao town area. Around 10 PM, they finally received an important clue: a couple reported to the police station that their child had gone missing and claimed that the person in the newspaper photo was their son.

Video: Surveillance captured a schoolboy stealing and driving the bus for 50 kilometers. Source: Anhui TV's "Super News Field"

Meanwhile, the police also received a report. Bus driver Du woke up early in the morning and found that the bus parked by the road in Shifan Town was gone. This vehicle, with the license plate number Zhe CEXXXX, was an urban-rural bus running between Yueqing and Hongqiao. After investigation, the traffic police discovered the bus in front of a car 4S store in Heqi Village, Shifan Town, Yueqing.

"He is very mischievous and often runs away for several days," Wang said. Previously, Xiao Chen would often leave home for two or three days but was always found. This time, after leaving home on the afternoon of April 5th, he could not be located.

During the investigation, three children around ten years old said they had interacted with the boy. On the evening of April 9th, they were riding bicycles together near the Shifan Second Village market. They played from 8 PM until around 10 PM. One child surnamed Yang mentioned that he remembered the "school uniform boy" riding a pink bicycle with a basket on the backseat. The boy couldn't speak and could only gesture, possibly indicating that he was deaf and mute. Based on this clue, the traffic police searched the area all day but still had no news.

Currently, the traffic police department will continue to intensify its search efforts. Meanwhile, they hope that if the public spots this boy, they will promptly contact the Hongqiao Traffic Police Station.

The traffic police investigation revealed that the one who took the bus was a little boy wearing a school uniform, approximately 12 years old.

The traffic police visited households in Puhu First Village, Puhu Second Village, and Puhu Third Village near where the bus was parked, showing photos of the little boy to each family.

According to mother Wang, her son Xiao Chen is 13 years old, originally from Sichuan province, and is congenitally deaf and mute. Currently, he has dropped out of school and lives at home. Wang and her husband live temporarily in Panzhai Village, Hongqiao Town, with Xiao Chen’s seven-year-old younger brother. Her husband works in a machinery factory, while Wang does some small product work at home. Xiao Chen attended school for only half a year before dropping out and staying with his grandparents in his hometown. He came to Hongqiao one and a half years ago.

The police also searched and compared information at nearby middle schools such as Shifan Middle School, Shifan Primary School No.1, Shifan Primary School No.3, and Tiancheng Primary School, but did not find the boy.

Surveillance showed that the one who drove away the bus was a little boy wearing a school uniform.

How could Xiao Chen drive? Wang said it might be something he learned from video games. Because Xiao Chen liked playing video games, he often went to a game hall in Hongqiao Town to play games, and his favorite game was racing cars. When he had money, he played himself; when he didn’t have money, he watched others play. They would look for him there whenever they couldn’t find him.
