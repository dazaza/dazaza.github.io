The legend of the fried egg girl

by libailou on 2009-11-09 10:22:08

A long time ago, or rather, not so long ago, in a rather desolate small town, although the town was not really desolate. Due to special backgrounds such as history, politics, geography, and culture, etc., the place was abundant with eggs. Anyway, what I want to say is that in a corner of the world, there exists a small town that produces a lot of eggs. It's probably better not to ask why, but anyway, the place had more eggs than it could eat. However, apart from being abundant in eggs, this place was quite deficient in other resources, including grain. Such was the peculiar shape of this town.

Most inconspicuous towns in some corners of the world have their own legends, and this town was no exception. It was said that a long time ago, truly a long time ago. About a hundred years ago, at that time, the town's resources were not as scarce as they are today. The scene back then was indeed nostalgic: free birds gliding over endless fields, then like carefree arrows piercing into the shady mixed forests; occasionally, pleasant winds coming from beyond the valleys slowly swept through the town, gently bathing all lives in the town - a refreshing paradise on earth.

There were many noodle shops in the town, beef noodle shops, char siu noodle shops, and various other types of noodle shops; and people were very willing to go to the noodle shops to solve their food problems. In an inconspicuous corner of the town, there was an egg noodle shop. The business of the egg noodle shop was not as good as that of the beef noodle shop and the char siu noodle shop, and finally, due to poor management, it went bankrupt. But the egg resources owned by the noodle shop were still very large. In order to promote the eggs, they had to cooperate with the beef noodle shop and the char siu noodle shop. They respectively provided fresh, delicious, and fragrant fried eggs to the beef noodle shop and the char siu noodle shop. Once, the niece of the boss of one of the char siu noodle shops helped carry the fried eggs out to the customers. The boss's niece was quite ordinary-looking, but it was summer, she was wearing cool shorts, and her figure was also tall and slender, with legs that looked aesthetically pleasing.
