At Dongjia Bridge in Xiaohao on the south bank, when it comes to the little cat "Ren'ai", there is no one in the courtyard who does not know about it. "Ren'ai" has lived here since it was born, until 2007, when its owner moved to a new home but left it behind. Although the "home" had long been abandoned, "Ren'ai" stayed for three years and could not bear to leave.

"Ren'ai! Come out!" The neighbors said that "Ren'ai" is very obedient, and they are also close to it. If they don't see "Ren'ai" for several days, they will miss it and ask each other where "Ren'ai" went. For familiar people, as long as they shout towards the house, "Ren'ai" will come out quickly. But for strangers, it won't even bother with them.

Sister Zhou said, this little cat is quite strange. Its owner tried to take it away several times, but the little cat seemed to know, and it couldn't be caught, so it wasn't taken away. Until now, the Chen family still often comes back to see the little cat.

Later, the little cat learned many skills and was very good at catching mice. Even if the mice hid in the sewer, it would pull them out.

"Ren'ai", loved by everyone

The little cat "Ren'ai" is a female cat, all white, 50 centimeters long, about six years old. Its "home" is at No. 22-1 Dongjia Bridge in Xiaohao, a public house with one floor up and one down. After the owner moved, the house has been vacant, and the door was locked with a big lock, but the little cat "Ren'ai" continued to live here.

A white drawer inside is "Ren'ai's" nest, and a plastic stool beside it was brought by the neighbors later. "Ren'ai" always likes to jump up and down on the stool. Although the door is tightly locked, it can't stop "Ren'ai". Now that the weather is hot, "Ren'ai" always goes out early in the morning to bask in the sun. When going out, it climbs onto the roof along the pillar, and slides down through the window. When it gets tired of playing and returns home, it crawls into the drawer and sleeps.

As the saying goes: "Dogs love people, cats love home." Even if the owner moves, the dog will follow, but the cat will run back to the original home.

"The cat is hungry, it will meow and look for food from people, quite pitiful." Neighbor Sister Zhou felt sorry for it and often took food from her home to feed it. All the neighbors in the yard know the little cat. Grandma Peng specially cooked food for the little cat. Seeing that the little cat didn't like eating rice, she spent money to buy cat food at five yuan per catty to feed it; Ms. He often boiled fish soup at home and fed the fish meat to the little cat.

"This little cat is very obedient, we call it 'Ren'ai'." The residents hope to find it a good owner, but this cat is wild now, and no one can take it away. It recognizes this place as its home.

That's because in the past, cats usually caught mice to eat in their homes or nearby. Cats have been free-range outside for a long time, and the area around the home becomes their hunting ground. For these cats, food is not given by the owner, but captured by themselves around their home. If the family moves to another place, they will definitely return to the hunting ground that ensures their prey and belongs to their past territory. This is what is called "cats loving home."

"The owner moved to a new home, wanting to take it away, but it wouldn't go." The residents said that this little cat was raised by their old neighbor, the Chen family. In their impression, this cat was raised from a kitten, and the owner liked it very much, always keeping it indoors.

Gradually, the little cat became stronger, able to jump onto trees. It also learned to wash itself. Despite being a "stray cat," it was always clean and white.

Soon, some residents found that the meat they had just taken out to thaw suddenly disappeared. It turned out that "Ren'ai" had given birth, stealing meat to feed her kittens. To make it easier to take care of her, the residents opened a small hole in the corner of the wall for her to enter and exit, and found the key to the door to facilitate entering and exiting to take care of her. The residents brought an old drawer for her to use as a nest to raise her kittens. "When finding owners for the kittens, we carefully selected the families, like marrying off a daughter." The residents hoped to find a good owner for the little cat.

By 2007, due to the flood, the entire street of Dongjia Bridge was flooded. The Chen family, renting public housing from the housing management office, subsequently moved to a new home. However, the little cat continued to live in the old house left by the owner, never leaving.

"If there is a row of rats in the yard, they must have been caught by Ren'ai, it's claiming credit!" Resident Ms. Long said that she often feeds "Ren'ai," and "Ren'ai" helps her chase away the rats.

"Ren'ai" has lived in this old house since the owner moved in 2007 and refuses to leave. The neighbors often put cat food at its doorstep. This is the home it has guarded alone for three years.
