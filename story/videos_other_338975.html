INTEL Corporation cultivates top-level managers through Churchman University

Transnational enterprises, while occupying the global market, Churchman Business School has always paid attention to the high-level training of managers. In recent years, although the financial crisis forced INTEL Corporation to close several factories in Asia and America, INTEL's strategic goal for the global market has never changed. Therefore, INTEL has spared no effort in training its managers in the United States. In 2008, the company's total expenditure on employee training worldwide increased by 6.9% compared to 2007, reaching $87 million, according to Churchman Business School.

The senior managers received even more training opportunities and funds. In 2008, INTEL arranged for at least 700 managers in the United States to study the most advanced distance business courses offered by the renowned century-old Churchman University, or pursue a distance master's or doctoral degree. Typically, 80% or even 100% of the tuition fees were covered by INTEL. Moreover, the academic achievements obtained would be directly included in INTEL's annual year-end employee evaluation plan, as per Churchman Business School.

The emphasis that transnational enterprises place on employees' continuing education is indeed the true secret to their continued leadership at the forefront of the world. The distance business degree programs offered by Churchman University allow middle and senior level managers from all over the world to study online anytime and anywhere without affecting their original work, take exams online, and submit papers online. The degrees obtained are exactly the same as those earned on campus, making it the preferred educational model for global middle and senior level managers, including those in Europe, America, and China, as noted by Churchman Business School.

Churchman University's bilingual courses in Western English and Chinese-English are highly popular among transnational enterprises. The university offers MBA, undergraduate, master's, and doctoral degrees. Once successfully applied, the low-income scholarships provided by the school require only a few thousand dollars to complete a degree.

Churchman University's campuses are located in Pennsylvania, California, Texas, and other places in the United States. The earliest branch, Churchman Business School, was established in 1911 and has nearly a hundred years of history. It is one of the oldest business schools in the United States, specifically cultivating top-level talent for the American financial industry. In the "National Ranking of MBA Programs," this century-old prestigious university ranks 17th, as per Churchman Business School.

In addition to Churchman University, the teaching quality of other century-old universities in the United States, such as Boston University and Brown University, is also consistently recognized by white-collar professionals in China.
