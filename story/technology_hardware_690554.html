The development of the smart home industry abroad started earlier, and the brands are relatively mature.

by gdshstyle on 2012-03-05 10:50:01

DIY in the smart home field is restricted domestically.

Currently, domestic manufacturers in the smart home field are mainly divided into three categories: The first category consists of traditional intercom manufacturers such as Fujian Guanlin, Videosecurity, Legrand, and Ajubao. These manufacturers primarily provide an intelligent integrated platform that consolidates numerous subsystems such as security alarms, appliance control, multimedia audio-visual control management, etc.; The second category consists of traditional home appliance manufacturers like Haier, TCL, Changhong, etc., which mainly offer informatized and networked appliances; The third category includes manufacturers specializing in modules and interfaces for lighting and curtain control such as Suobo and Bocreate. These manufacturers mainly cooperate with the first two types by providing various smart switches and module interfaces.

As more and more product suppliers join the smart home industry, such as access control, alarm systems, intercoms, and home appliances, the markets targeted by the products provided by different manufacturers vary, leading to a situation where almost all manufacturers in the smart home market are fighting their own battles.

In contrast, the development of the smart home industry started earlier abroad, and the brands are more mature, generally focusing on wired systems combining strong and weak electricity. Additionally, foreign users can, to a certain extent, do it themselves, performing some product debugging and installation according to their actual needs for personalized design. The advantage of this type of operation is that the user's cost is reduced, and the number of optional solutions increases. In reality, domestic users rarely purchase and install products themselves; however, this is different abroad. For example, in the United States, users can directly buy smart home products in supermarkets, such as light control devices and smart alarm devices, and users can DIY.

Domestic manufacturers rarely set up specialty stores directly or sell products to end-users through supermarkets and department stores. Typically, in smart home project applications, real estate developers and system integrators provide residents with automated comprehensive control platforms. After the platform is built, residents can purchase smart home systems according to their needs and expand or further improve the functionality of smart homes on the already established platform. Design, installation, and other operational steps are usually handled by system integrators. Currently, domestic smart home products have not fully reached the "idiot-proof" mode of operation and cannot be used directly like traditional household appliances. They involve debugging and installation operations, which are somewhat specialized and complex, requiring professional companies such as strong and weak electrical system integrators to design, debug, and install based on the actual needs of residents.

However, due to manufacturers not directly participating in the design, installation, and other operational steps of projects, if relying on system integrators for installation, their technical level may not be able to complete the entire project, possibly failing to meet user expectations. Alternatively, if uniformly purchased, installed, and debugged as part of real estate developers' refined housing packages, the average cost can be reduced, but residents cannot choose product styles, function types, etc., according to their own needs.

There are many reasons why domestic residents are restricted from doing it themselves. One is cost issues; for instance, hiring a decoration company in China is cheaper compared to abroad, and the cost for residents to decorate themselves is not necessarily cheaper. Another is living environment issues; China has a larger population and more densely populated living environments, mostly consisting of residential buildings, unlike abroad where most are single-family homes or villas, each generally equipped with basements for tool storage and construction. Therefore, compared to abroad, DIY is not popular among Chinese residents in smart home applications, and most installations still rely on real estate projects. Currently, the main market application of domestic smart home products lies in communication with real estate developers, depending on how they position their own properties, the key being whether real estate developers are willing to invest funds in the smart home sector.

Real estate developers often install entire smart home systems in houses to enhance sales volume and increase property value. Abroad, smart homes are an "individual concept," referred to as home equipment control or lighting control, etc., and are not promoted as an overall concept, whereas in China, smart home manufacturers or real estate developers promote them as an "overall concept."
