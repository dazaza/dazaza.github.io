Why are there more and more cerebral palsy patients?

by maoerbao0519 on 2012-02-16 11:48:53

Experts from Yibo Hospital of Taian introduce that in the past, the incidence rate of infantile cerebral palsy was not high. However, under the influence of many factors such as the accelerating life pace, "premature babies" have gradually increased. With the progress of medical standards, children with only 28 weeks' gestational age and small weight can be saved. Because of their short gestational age and immature brain development, they are likely to suffer brain injuries. If a brain injury leads to central motor dysfunction, it might be the cause of infantile cerebral palsy.

It is reported that accompanied by cerebral palsy damages, the physical growth of infants will be severely affected, leading to intelligence and emotion problems as well as language and vision disorders. In appearance, children with cerebral palsy are usually shorter than normal children, have poor nutrition, and often suffer from breathing obstacles and respiratory tract infection diseases, etc., all these have caused an increasing trend of infantile cerebral palsy.

1. The most common physical teratogenic factors causing cerebral palsy include: radiation, mechanical factors, high temperature, severe cold, microwave, hypoxia, etc. Overall, about 20% of cerebral palsy cases are caused by prenatal reasons, 70%-80% by perinatal and delivery reasons, and 15%-20% by postnatal reasons. Generally speaking, asphyxia, immaturity, and severe jaundice are considered as the three main causative factors for cerebral palsy. But in recent years, due to the reduction of cerebral palsy caused by severe jaundice and premature babies, the proportion of prenatal cerebral palsy has shown an increasing trend.

2. Genetic factors: Recent studies suggest that genetic factors play an increasingly important role in cerebral palsy. In a comparative study, it was found that among people whose close relatives had epilepsy, cerebral palsy, and intellectual disability, 65% had two of these conditions. The fertilized egg contains the DNA genetic material from both parents, which forms genes arranged on chromosomes. When there are numerical or structural abnormalities, mutations, or congenital metabolic defects in the chromosomes, congenital malformations may occur, showing abnormal individual development, and cerebral palsy symptoms may appear accordingly.

The key to treating infantile cerebral palsy lies in early detection. Therefore, once symptoms of cerebral palsy are found, timely treatment should be sought at a neurological hospital. Diagnosis and treatment of cerebral palsy require high technical equipment and expert experience levels, so treatment must be conducted at regular professional neurological hospitals.
