Tony Wong posts a picture on Weibo praising the delicious Zhengzhou mutton soup + baked flatbread!

Zhengzhou movie fans are thrilled.

When did he come to Zhengzhou? Where is the stall in the picture? What did he eat at that time? What did he say? (Click on the image to enter Tony Wong's Weibo)

The small stall owner received more than 20 groups of visitors yesterday who were looking for him.

Netizens lamented: "Why didn't you give us a heads up when you came? You're too low-key!"

The young man making the baked flatbread didn't expect that stars could be so close.

Yesterday afternoon, I arrived at the Yingbin Comprehensive Market.

After entering the market gate and walking south, not far away there was a mutton soup shop. The female boss was pouring meat into a large pot used for simmering the soup.

Upon hearing the name Tony Wong, she couldn’t help but say, "Why are so many people asking about this today? There have been over 20 groups!" The male boss looked at me shyly and smiled, "It wasn't our shop; he bought baked flatbread from a stall to the west."

Following the direction he pointed, less than 10 meters to the west, there was a Qingzhen baked flatbread stall. Upon closer inspection, the tall oven used for baking flatbreads, the glass cabinet displaying the flatbreads, and the clothes worn by the two men making the flatbreads were exactly the same as in the photo.

Boss, did Tony Wong buy baked flatbreads from here?

"Yes," the young man making the flatbreads answered without hesitation.

"How did you know it was Tony Wong?"

The young man was taking freshly baked flatbreads out of the high oven while saying, "At first, I didn't pay much attention when he came. Later, when he asked if he could take a picture here, I noticed that he looked like Tony Wong. Many people came to ask today, so I'm sure it's him. I never expected that stars could be so close."

The father of the young man, Master Jin, was adjusting the seasoning for the baked flatbreads. He told me, "I don't know him. It was almost 2 PM in the afternoon, and he bought seven baked flatbreads from us. A woman with him took two pictures."

Master Jin said his flatbread stall has been here for seven years. Every day, many people come, including foreign teachers and students from nearby universities.

"He might have heard of our place and came specially," the young man laughed.

An assistant on-site had previously denied that it was really Tony Wong.

A neighbor of the mutton soup shop said, "If they hadn't covered my mouth..."

The flatbread stall has been found, so where is the mutton soup shop?

The young man making the flatbreads said, "There are only two mutton soup shops in this market, it might be the one to the north."

Following the small path east of the flatbread stall towards the north, after walking just a few steps, there was a mutton soup shop. The shop door faced north, and there were two simple tables outside. A man and a woman were waiting for the boss to serve them the soup. Beside them, the stove was burning fiercely, steam was rising from the large iron pot, and the inviting aroma of mutton was floating around.

This shop was located in a temporarily built shack, without a name, covering an area of less than 10 square meters. On both sides of the shop door, red paper was pasted with the menu written on it. Inside, the male owner was cutting pieces of sheep lung.

As soon as I stepped into the room, I heard two female college students peeking from the window on the east side asking the boss, "Did Tony Wong come here to drink mutton soup?" Before the boss could answer, the female college students looked at me and smiled, "Are you also here to ask about this?"

Upon hearing that I was a journalist, the boss became very enthusiastic. He told me, "Several media outlets have already come to interview me."

He said, yesterday around 2:30 PM, four people, one man and three women, came in. They ordered two bowls of mutton soup. The man stood by the large pot and took a picture. Later, he went south to buy baked flatbreads.

"It was our opposite neighbor who saw them taking pictures and realized that the man looked like Tony Wong." The boss pointed to a youth across the street, "Ask him, he discovered it."

The youth eagerly came to me and excitedly said, "In mid-October, a production team came here and ate Kaifeng soup dumplings. I asked them which stars were there, and they mentioned Tony Wong. When he took a picture yesterday, I immediately felt it was him."

The youth said that this production team was staying at the Jinding Hotel next door. At that time, I even asked the woman taking the picture if he was Tony Wong. That woman said he just looked like Tony Wong but wasn't the real one. I said, "If it's not Tony Wong, then seal my mouth shut."

When they saw someone recognized them, they quickly left.

Subsequently, I went to the Jinding Hotel, and the waiter told me that indeed, a production team was staying there, filming the movie "The Struggle Between Chu and Han". They were shooting scenes by the Yellow River and had been there for almost a month. They weren't there now, but they would return after some time.

"Tony Wong is a star, stars all stay at the Yellow River Yingbin Hotel," the waiter told me.

Tony Wong plays Liu Bang by the Yellow River

According to reports, "The Struggle Between Chu and Han" is a major historical work with an investment exceeding 100 million yuan, starting filming in Zhengzhou on October 20.

The show is planned by renowned storyteller Shantangfang, directed by famous director Chen Jialin who has directed "Kangxi Dynasty", produced by award-winning producer Xiong Cheng, and features action choreography by Hong Kong action director Yuen Bun.

In the drama, film emperor Tony Wong and actress Ke Lan portray Liu Bang and Empress Lu Zhi, respectively, alongside Ren Chengwei as Xiang Yu and newcomer Jin Chen as Yu Ji.

The first collaboration between Ke Lan and Tony Wong has drawn much attention.

"The Struggle Between Chu and Han" is Chen Jialin's final work. Chen Jialin is considered China's top historical drama master, directing grand historical works with majestic structures, epic scale, and exquisite visuals.

The total producer of "The Struggle Between Chu and Han", Xiong Cheng, stated, "To ensure the quality of the work, we prioritized the compatibility of actors with their roles, selecting professional actors with strong acting skills."
