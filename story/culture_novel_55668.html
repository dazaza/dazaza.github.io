Is a new era of reading about to begin?

Reading is an indispensable part of people's lives. Books are humanity’s best friend. From the time of our ancestors tying knots in ropes during ancient times, books have developed rapidly. The great Chinese inventions of paper and printing signify the true beginning of an era of reading in China!

China's development speed has left the world in awe. With five thousand years of historical culture, the great works of ancient scholars are still studied globally today. The mediums for transmitting culture have evolved from bamboo slips and wooden tablets (also known as Jian Du), to silk fabrics and paper. The great invention of paper began with the Western Han Dynasty's Baqiao paper, progressing through Zuo Bo paper, bamboo screen paper,藤 paper, fish roe paper, cotton paper, cloth paper, hemp paper, Xuan paper, Chengxin Hall paper, and more.

In the 21st century today, we've entered an era of nationwide reading. Books are not only a means for people to acquire knowledge but also a way for leisure and entertainment. Newspapers, magazines, novels, comics, and more enrich people's minds and broaden their horizons. Flourishing bookstores provide convenience, and people have a deep love for reading. Every morning, there's a newspaper; every week, a magazine; every month, a novel. The joy derived from reading often makes people overlook the monthly expenses of dozens or even hundreds of yuan. Businesspeople are always perceptive—they realized that people could access books faster and more comprehensively, adopt more efficient reading methods, and find the most comprehensive knowledge without leaving home. Only the internet could fulfill this beautiful aspiration, and thus e-books were born. They integrate the highest cost-performance ratio—cheap prices (sometimes even free), comprehensive book selection, fast updates, timely targeted searches, and the ability to access the entire world while sitting in front of a computer. However, technological development has yet to break through the traditional presentation format of books. Issues such as dense text and incomplete content make reading exhausting, which to some extent restrains the development of the e-book industry. Even in recent years, as e-books have evolved from pure text browsing to e-readers and from monotonous text to multimedia integration, people still seem unable to replicate the reading experience of traditional books. Engineers continuously explore and develop tests, striving for a reading method that offers the high cost-effectiveness of e-books while providing a good reading experience. Technology has the potential to change life, and recently, the emergence of an epic-level software (eREAD) has fulfilled people's wishes. It will pioneer a new way of reading—from finding books, to reading them, to sharing them—all-in-one. It satisfies people's reading experience while retaining the characteristics of e-books. It combines the advantages of the internet and meets the future demands of people. The source of the books comes from vertical search engines, offering tens of thousands of books. Through the company's operations, the quality of the books is ensured. Through the software's conversion of the books, people can bid farewell to dense text and enjoy a relaxed and comfortable reading experience on their computers. Features like automatic page turning, horizontal/vertical book viewing, and point enlargement are humanized settings that will undoubtedly leave its mark in the history of human reading. It's still too early to determine whether it will lead a new trend in reading. Any new product on the market will face challenges, let alone one that might update people's reading habits. Although it's a good product, we're no longer in the era where "good wine needs no bush." I hope the company operating this product runs it well and leads us into a new era of reading!
