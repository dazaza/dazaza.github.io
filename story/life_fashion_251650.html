Interpretation of American Style Representatives

by longersing on 2009-10-24 16:32:46

Interpreting the representative of American style, the country-style decoration is favored in developed countries. The United States, which has many villa households, owns the most beautiful country design. The American country style is also called American rural style, which belongs to one branch of natural style and advocates "returning to nature". It strives to express leisurely, comfortable, and natural country life interests in indoor environments. Natural materials such as wood, stone,藤 (vine), and bamboo are often used for their simple textures. Indoor greenery is skillfully arranged to create a natural, simple, and elegant atmosphere. This is different from the Shanghai decoration.

The American country style has practical, standardized, and mature characteristics. Taking the American middle class as an example, they have quite decent incomes to support themselves, so they can freely develop their preferences in larger living spaces. Design cases also show the taste, hobbies, and life values of their residents to a great extent.

Generally speaking, after entering the door, you can appreciate the public part of the home space facing outward. The living room and dining room are used to entertain guests and invite friends for dinner. In material selection, harder, stiffer, and more luxurious materials are often preferred. The dining room is usually connected to the kitchen, which has a large area, convenient operation, and powerful functions. On the other side of the kitchen opposite the dining room, there is usually a not-so-large casual dining area. The multifunctionality of the kitchen also reflects interpersonal communication within the family, often taking place here. These two areas will connect with the living room into a large area, becoming the focus of family life.

The living room is generally lower and more peaceful than the living room space. Materials are mostly selected for comfort, softness, and warmth, effectively creating a warm and cozy family atmosphere. Televisions and other entertainment items are also placed in this space. One can imagine the joyous fusion of television commercials, the harmony of pots and pans, and the noise of children playing under the "three-in-one" structure.

On the second floor, in addition to bedrooms for family members to rest, spaces such as studies, recreation rooms, multi-functional rooms, and small living rooms are also built according to the requirements and characteristics of the family, to meet the diversified needs of the family. The bathroom design also pays attention to functionality, with safety and convenience being the primary considerations. Additionally, American country style has a high degree of freedom in material selection for bathrooms, as Americans like soaking baths. Waterproof wallpaper and wood on walls are not uncommon.
