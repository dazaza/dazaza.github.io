Borrowing &mdash

by hartyqekb on 2009-12-02 17:27:57

Recently, the TV series "Snail House" has become extremely popular. Why? As the screenwriter, Liu Liu, said: "Every person who owns a 1-square-meter cubicle in an office building, pays off their mortgage monthly, takes the bus when going out, and eats boxed lunches at noon, can find their own shadow in this drama."

The heroine of the drama, Hai Ping, seems to have spoken for many people: "Every day as soon as I open my eyes, a string of numbers pop up: 6000 yuan for the mortgage, 2500 yuan for food and clothing, 600 yuan for social obligations, 580 yuan for transportation fees, 340 yuan for property management fees... This is the cost of me living in this city, these numbers force me not to slack off even for a single day."

Even so, there are still netizens shouting that "being a mortgage slave is unfortunate, but being unable to be a mortgage slave is even more unfortunate." A netizen poll initiated by Tencent attracted 360,000 netizens, and the results showed that eight out of ten people believed happiness was related to having a house. Among them, 69.9% believed that a happy family should have at least one house; 10.5% indicated "not only should they have one, but it should be at least 85 square meters or more"; only 10.0% believed that "houses have nothing to do with happiness."

A Beijing-based netizen on Tencent said: "After striving to enter a key university, moving from the countryside to the city, and obtaining a Beijing hukou (household registration), what's the point? I can't afford a house. Looking at my parents' aged faces and calloused hands, tears came down. I don't have the ability to provide them with a good retirement. It's really frustrating."

A Nanjing-based netizen referred to themselves as part of the "parasitic generation": their parents helped buy the house and were also helping repay the loan. "Sometimes I feel sorry for my parents, but there's no other way. However, I deeply understand that the road of life is long, and only through my own efforts can I achieve a happy life."

There are also netizens (9.5%) who claim that "renting can also lead to happiness." A netizen named "Lucky" from Shandong China said that we should pursue, like Hai Ping in the drama, a home that completely belongs to ourselves, a place where we live with our children and loved ones, even if it's just a "snail house," that house is still a "home."

"Do we necessarily have to buy houses? Hai Ping isn't wrong; in big cities, under the pressure of work and life, home is a freely resting harbor. So-called 'peace of mind is the place to return,' happiness should be placed in the heart, and we shouldn't easily compromise with life. Renting a house can also hold our happiness." One netizen commented.

Jiangsu netizen "Juanzi" strongly agrees with renting. She said that there are many people around her who bought houses, but in order to pay off the mortgage, either they leave the houses empty or rent them out to others while they themselves live in cheaper places. "Thinking about mortgages making it hard to eat well, dress well, and even saving time to visit parents and children. By the time the loan is paid off, the child has grown up and is beyond control, the elderly may no longer be around, and oneself is already old. Is it worth it?"

Yes, the fact of high housing prices we cannot change, but we can definitely change our lifestyle — spending more time accompanying our parents, allowing our children to grow up beside us, reducing stress between couples while increasing warmth, having more time together as a family, and seeing more of the world... Such a life can be equally wonderful.
