Abortion, the eternal pain in a woman's heart

Abortion, a process that ends life before it even has a chance to begin. Whether voluntary or involuntary, the pain is something women try to forget, until the next life begins to be conceived and happiness replaces the pain. Otherwise, it will accompany them for the rest of their lives.

Artificial abortion, also known as induced abortion, refers to the termination of pregnancy through surgical methods, i.e., artificially terminating pregnancy. Artificial abortion can be divided into: medical abortion (medication-induced), vacuum aspiration, and forceps evacuation. Vacuum aspiration is what people commonly refer to as an "abortion" (although this is not entirely accurate since artificial abortion includes other methods). It can be either painless or regular. Painless usually involves general anesthesia; vacuum aspiration uses a hollow tube to enter the uterus and remove the embryo tissue via negative pressure, while forceps evacuation uses oval forceps to extract large pieces of embryo tissue from the uterus. Abortion can be performed up to 14 weeks of pregnancy. For those who unexpectedly become pregnant but do not want a child, perhaps undergoing an abortion is an inevitable choice. It should be noted that abortion, whether minor or major, can cause harm to a woman's body. Therefore, the Gynecology Consultation Network has invited Professor Liu Xia from the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology at Shandong University of Traditional Chinese Medicine to explain issues related to abortion:

Host: Can you please explain what a medical abortion is?

Gynecological Expert: A medical abortion actually kills the embryo with medication, which is then naturally expelled through the vagina. The actual implementation of medical abortion varies from person to person. As mentioned earlier, medical abortion relies on medication to kill the embryo, which contains large amounts of estrogen and progesterone. Who is suitable for a medical abortion? Those with normal menstrual cycles and no history of dysmenorrhea are suitable for a medical abortion. Otherwise, it’s best not to proceed because some people may not completely expel the embryo after a medical abortion, leading to the need for a surgical abortion, causing them double pain. Some people have endometriosis, where the elasticity and contraction of the uterus are poor, so they cannot fully expel the embryo with medication alone, thus requiring a surgical abortion.

Host: Which is better between painless abortion and regular abortion (in terms of physical damage)?

Gynecological Expert: Actually, both painless and regular abortions use the method of cleaning out the uterus to remove the embryo. Painless abortion is conducted under anesthesia, but the actual degree of harm to the body is the same; only the reaction differs. Of course, painless abortion is better, but it’s available in some places and not others, and the cost is relatively higher. However, the degree of damage to the uterine lining is the same. When doctors perform abortions, we sometimes ask about the patient's feelings and the degree of pain, which depends on the doctor's experience. So, if a hospital wants to offer painless abortion services, it must have the appropriate capabilities. Small hospitals generally do not have this service; only large, reputable hospitals do. Nowadays, painless abortion and painless childbirth are only available in large hospitals.

Host: Hello, Professor Liu! Does medical abortion lead to secondary infertility?

Gynecological Expert: Infertility and sterility are two different concepts. Infertility means inability to conceive, while sterility means the embryo fails to develop. Primary infertility refers to never being able to conceive, while secondary infertility occurs after previous pregnancies. Sterility involves repeated miscarriages despite successful conception. Regarding the question of whether medical abortion leads to infertility, it varies from person to person. Some indeed suffer infertility after medical abortion. In our infertility clinic, some patients have undergone one or multiple medical abortions, resulting from various factors. Medical abortion tends to cause secondary infertility at a relatively high rate compared to surgical abortion. Secondary infertility could result from inflammation caused by unsanitary procedures during medical or surgical abortions, particularly in smaller clinics. Without proper antibiotics, infections such as pelvic inflammatory disease or blocked fallopian tubes may occur. Personal hygiene post-abortion is crucial to prevent infections leading to infertility. Hormonal changes after medical abortion can disrupt ovulation or even cause amenorrhea. Immune factors, such as antibodies, can inhibit embryo development. In our clinic, many women who underwent medical abortions last year seek infertility treatments this year. We always inquire about their age and marital status, advising them to avoid unnecessary abortions if possible, as infertility could lead to regret. Women have an optimal reproductive age; missing this window may impact future fertility. I believe the best reproductive age for women is below 28 years old, ensuring overall health and producing healthier children with greater vitality. An example from our infertility clinic shows that many patients, regardless of gender, were the youngest in their families. When they wanted to start families, they couldn't conceive, often blaming their mothers' older ages at childbirth, typically above 35. Older maternal age increases genetic risks, reducing ovarian function and affecting fertilization and embryo development. Conditions like uterine fibroids and endometriosis are often hereditary, so avoiding missed opportunities for reproduction is advisable, especially for those with familial histories of these conditions.
