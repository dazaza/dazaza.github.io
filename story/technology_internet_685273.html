E-commerce: The Trilogy of Product Success

E-commerce is merely a tool that uses an online platform to sell products. But through this tool, how can we sell products better and in greater quantities? We all know that on the internet, content is king, and in e-commerce, the product is king. Sales results are outcomes built upon a solid product foundation.

In the entire product system of e-commerce, I believe the following three points are the core foundation of a product. If done well, making money becomes almost effortless!

1. Quality Determines

Most customers approach shopping via e-commerce with an attitude of trial purchasing.

The reason being, before the customer pays or while the purchased product is still in transit via logistics and has not yet reached the customer's hands, their concept of the product comes solely from pictures and text descriptions. Therefore, consistency between product quality and its description is the basis for establishing trustworthiness on e-commerce platforms. It is also the foundation for encouraging repeat purchases and achieving word-of-mouth promotion.

2. Product Presentation Decides Now

The customer's purchase purpose is the product, not the packaging. However, product packaging can have a 'sugar-coating' effect and provide many added-value information, such as giving customers a direct sense of the company's strength, building commercial credibility, layering advertising information, and promoting corporate philosophy. These actions present a trustworthy business behavior that catches the customer’s eye in today's restless society.

Additionally, if your packaging or a promotional flyer is so well-designed that the customer hesitates to discard it, then I can confidently tell you, you have thoroughly won over your customer.

[http://www.gotook.org/]

3. Service Determines

Service is the most crucial aspect in the sales process of e-commerce. For an e-commerce company, it is an intangible product.

We all know that e-commerce inherently possesses the attribute of speed. And we also know the saying "what comes fast goes fast," which in commerce translates into a "one-time deal." Service, however, is the foundation for continuing the second and third exchanges between buyer and seller.

Additionally, due to the attribute of speed, once goods reach the customer's hands, the probability of issues arising increases. Resolving these problems falls under service.

Finally, besides solving problems, good service can continuously elevate the taste and class of your e-commerce company, creating immense trust from customers. Of course, if you anticipate that along with the growth in market share of the products you sell, the cost of customer service personnel will also increase, then there's no need to view service as a product!

Summary: E-commerce, originally a pure term, has now become chaotic due to the internet. However, if you wish to pursue this work as a career, you must not follow the crowd and become restless.

You should believe that e-commerce is not limited to just one Taobao, one JD.com, one Dangdang, or one Vancl. In the future, as long as the internet exists, e-commerce will certainly continue to thrive, and many more companies similar to Taobao, JD.com, Dangdang, and Vancl will emerge.

Therefore, starting now, focus on improving your product and open up your sales channels! I believe you will surely succeed!
