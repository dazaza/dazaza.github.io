The Science Behind Drinking Beauty Soups

Drinking soup also has its own science. For example, there is a difference between drinking soup before or after a meal. "Drink soup before meals, stay slim and healthy; drink soup after meals, get fatter as you drink." This isn't said lightly. Drinking a few sips of soup before eating lubricates the mouth and esophagus, preventing dry and hard foods from irritating the digestive tract lining, stimulating saliva secretion, and promoting digestion and absorption. Most importantly, drinking soup before meals increases satiety, reducing appetite and avoiding overeating. However, drinking soup after meals is detrimental to health: On one hand, since you're already full from the meal, drinking more soup can easily lead to nutrient excess and obesity. Additionally, the soup consumed last dilutes gastric juices, affecting the digestion and absorption of food.

Moreover, the speed at which you drink soup matters. Drinking slowly allows ample time for digestion and absorption. When you feel full, it means you've eaten just the right amount. In contrast, drinking quickly makes you realize you're full only after consuming more than necessary.
