Various versions of the iPhone 4S operating frequency were clarified on March 6. The frequencies used by China Telecom (microblogging) for the soon-to-be-sold iPhone 4S are in two bands, totaling 30MHz, with a minimum of 1920 ~ 1935MHz for 3G coverage. The telecommunications edition of the iPhone 4S has the same model as the U.S. version sold in China. Both China Telecom's iPhone 4S and the U.S. version of the iPhone 4S have the device model A1387, making them identical products and universally compatible. In comparison, China Unicom’s iPhone 4S model is A1431. Consequently, China Telecom's version of the iPhone 4S runs on a CDMA network, which is considered more environmentally friendly.

Related reports indicate that the channel details for China Telecom's iPhone 4S involve full participation from home appliance phone chains. In Beijing Telecom, the amount of reservations for the first day of the iPhone 4S exceeded expectations, with each person queuing for the telecom version of the iPhone 4S online booking experience (with photos). China Telecom announced the contract packages for the iPhone 4S, with the lowest price being 5780 yuan.

From the U.S. perspective, the U.S. version of the iPhone does not distinguish between CDMA and WCDMA versions. All three major operators selling the iPhone 4S support both CDMA and GSM networks, making it a global mobile phone (non-dual card dual standby). Additionally, the U.S. version of the iPhone 4S features an improved antenna design, enabling intelligent switching between sending and receiving antennas and enhancing call quality.

Regarding the operating frequency, it is reported that China Mobile's Internet-running iPhone 4S operates at the lowest frequency because China Mobile's iPhone 4S can only use the 2G network, not the 3G TD version. China Unicom’s iPhone 4S runs at 1940 ~ 1955MHz (uplink) / 2130 ~ 2145MHz (downlink), totaling 15MHz × 2; the frequency of China Telecom’s iPhone 4S is 1920 ~ 1935MHz (uplink) / 2110 ~ 2125MHz (downlink), totaling 15MHz × 2. In contrast, China Telecom’s iPhone 4S frequency is slightly lower, lacking comparability with China Mobile's iPhone 4S frequency usage due to differences in 3G and 2G networks, leading to varied user experiences. However, China Mobile currently has no distribution rights for the iPhone, so the iPhone 4S can only run on the 2G frequency. The models and sources of the online running iPhone 4S are unclear, but the industry expects most to be parallel imports.

The current information about whether the Unicom version and the telecommunications iPhone 4S are ultimately the same remains a topic of debate in the industry. Previously, at 0:00 on March 2nd, the telecommunications version of the iPhone 4S officially launched its reservation system, allowing users to select China Telecom authorized stores and designated online business contract plans. According to Qualcomm, CDMA mobile phones have a maximum transmit power of only 200 mW, with the power during an ordinary call controlled within a few tenths of mW, resulting in very little radiation. Reducing the mobile phone transmit power and improving receiver sensitivity extends the mobile phone talk time and also prolongs the phone and battery life.

Currently, China Unicom (microblogging) and China Telecom have obtained the marketing rights for the iPhone 4S, while China Mobile (microblogging) has stated that its users can use the iPhone 4S. China Mobile chairman Wang Jianzhou also recently mentioned this during a session meeting.
