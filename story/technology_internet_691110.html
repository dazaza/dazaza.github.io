Citizens buying 1 kilogram of eggs find almost all of them have double yolks

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-05 14:21:50

PHOTO: Tang Jiangtao is beating eggs, and all six of them turned out to be double-yolked, photographed by Fei Xuan from Yaxin News.

Yaxin News (reported by Fei Xuan) - It's always a stroke of luck when you crack an egg while cooking and find it has two yolks. But what if every egg you cracked had two yolks? That’s exactly what happened recently to Tang Jiangtao, who lives on Qianjin Street in Urumqi, Xinjiang.

A week ago, Tang Jiangtao and his mother bought a kilogram of eggs from a convenience vegetable store on Jiankang Road in Urumqi. These eggs turned out to have an exceptionally high probability of being double-yolked; almost any one picked at random would turn out to have two yolks. On the day they purchased the eggs, the store was running a promotion where eggs, whether sold in bulk or individually, were priced at 8.8 yuan per kilogram, attracting many customers. "I just grabbed a bag that weighed about a kilogram," Tang Jiangtao said.

The surprise came the next day when his mother was preparing a meal and cracked open the first egg, only to find it had three yolks. “Then she cracked open two more, and one of them was double-yolked. At that moment, we felt so lucky! We didn’t expect that for the next few days, every egg we cracked would turn out to be double-yolked,” Tang Jiangtao’s mother said.

On the morning of November 1st, at Tang Jiangtao’s home, he brought out a box from the kitchen containing 12 eggs. These eggs were roughly the same size as the ones we usually see, except for a slightly fuller top, making no significant distinction from ordinary eggs.

Tang Jiangtao then cracked open six eggs on the spot, all of which were double-yolked. The egg yolk and whites showed no difference in color, appearance, or smell compared to regular eggs. After personally tasting a boiled double-yolked egg, the reporter found no unusual taste. Tang Jiangtao joked that the whole family combined hadn’t eaten so many double-yolked eggs before, leaving them both delighted and puzzled. “Even though double-yolked eggs are common, how could all of them be like this? Is this phenomenon normal?”

Subsequently, the reporter visited the convenience vegetable store where Tang Jiangtao bought the eggs. A store employee informed the reporter that the store had just opened not long ago and had been running discount promotions every day. The egg promotion had already ended, and no customer had reported consistently finding double-yolked eggs. The reporter observed that the eggs in the vegetable store were of the same brand as those at Tang Jiangtao’s home, but overall, they seemed somewhat smaller.

The reporter contacted the egg manufacturer, and the responsible person explained: “Our poultry farm produces eggs year-round, and the egg-laying chickens are regularly updated. Each batch of eggs laid is sorted by size, and eggs of similar volume are grouped together. Double-yolked eggs typically appear during the early laying period. It's possible that during the sorting process, eggs from the same batch of chickens in their early laying period were accidentally grouped together on one tray, resulting in this rare occurrence. This kind of luck isn't something everyone can experience.”

Dr. Li Haiying from the Department of Animal Science at Xinjiang Agricultural University stated: “Double-yolked eggs occur when two ovum cells mature simultaneously and are released from the follicle into the oviduct together. Sometimes, multiple ovum cells mature and are incorporated into the oviduct at once, resulting in multi-yolked eggs. During the initial laying phase of hens, due to the high hormone levels, the egg-laying process goes through a cycle from low to high and back to low again. Double-yolked eggs are produced during the middle and early phases. Compared to regular eggs, double-yolked eggs have higher nutritional value, but since this is a phenomenon specific to certain periods, the probability is relatively low.”
