NBA Best Defensive Player Announced - Kobe and Howard Lead the Pack

by longersing on 2009-05-08 08:00:46

May 7th, Beijing time - According to NBA official website news, the league today announced the best defensive first and second teams for the 2008-09 season. Dwight Howard, Kobe Bryant, LeBron James, Chris Paul, and Kevin Garnett were selected for the first team. The Houston Rockets' Shane Battier and Ron Artest made it into the second team.

This selection was conducted through voting by the 30 head coaches of the NBA teams. Coaches respectively select their ideal first and second teams. Players receive 2 points for a first-team vote and 1 point for a second-team vote. In the end, two sets of the best defensive teams are determined by total score. Coaches cannot vote for players from their own team.

As the winner of this season's Defensive Player of the Year Award, Howard still showed his strength in this selection. "The Supermen" accumulated a total of 55 points in the voting, including 27 first-team votes. He only had a 2-point lead over Kobe who came in second place with a total score of 53 points. Kobe received 24 first-team votes. The newly crowned MVP of the season, LeBron James, also made the list. He received 22 first-team votes and scored a total of 47 points. Chris Paul, the steals leader, got 15 first-team votes and scored 36 points, securing another guard position. Although Kevin Garnett missed the end-of-season games due to injury, he still received 13 first-team votes and scored 35 points to edge out Tim Duncan for the first team.

In the second team, Duncan scored the highest. He received 10 first-team votes and 10 second-team votes, accumulating 30 points to anchor the center position. Steals experts Dwyane Wade and Rajon Rondo became the double guards. The two forward positions were occupied by the Rockets' defensive duo Battier and Artest, with Battier receiving 6 first-team votes and Artest receiving 5 first-team votes.
