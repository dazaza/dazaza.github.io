"Why the Paper Money Economy Causes Global Turmoil"

by jqrxass on 2009-09-03 11:11:38

Banknotes are the paper shackles of the economy, and banknotes are the ripening agents of the economy.

Banknotes can make you rise to fame, but they can also lead to your downfall.

Once banknotes go astray, it is highly possible that getting rich through labor will become a difficult matter, while at the same time creating one myth after another of "getting rich without labor." It is not scary for an individual to get rich without labor, but it is horrifying for a country to do so. Global "getting rich without labor" is directly equivalent to self-mutilation of humanity. Banknotes going astray have created a world gone astray. The 2008 financial crisis was a collective act of infidelity by the world economy.

Where should China and the world go after the financial crisis? Some advocate resource supremacy, others insist on financial dominance—these are all discourses of resource finance. Resource finance will only exacerbate "getting rich without labor," leading to "more and more people becoming poorer, and fewer and fewer people becoming richer." Because the vast majority of people rely on various kinds of industries for their livelihoods, while excessive resources and resource finance will only squeeze out industry and destructively damage the ecological environment. Human economy and Earth's ecology are at a life-and-death crossroads, either ascending to heaven or descending to hell.

Chapter 1: Full Entry into the Paper Money Era

Since 2008, the financial crisis originating in the United States has swept across the globe. What kind of crisis is this? How does this crisis fit into history? What warnings will it bring to the world? Currently, opinions still vary widely with no consensus reached. Before the crisis, domineering monetarism, arrogant financial innovation, constantly appreciating American land, and the enviable dollar... these splendid things together constituted the "American Dream."

Chapter 2: Paper Money Crisis Through History

Human beings live in a spatiotemporal tunnel, where all major events have historical roots. Why did paper money go astray at this time? Why did paper money collude with resources? After this great crisis erupted, economists and historians worldwide conducted extensive reviews, tracing historical clues to find the historical context of this great crisis. Roughly speaking, the time frame of this great crisis has been locked down from being a "once-in-a-century event" to a "once-in-300-years event," and then to a "once-in-a-thousand-years event." This is an interesting "three-step jump."

Chapter 3: Paper Money Creates a Triangular Relationship

We have fully entered the paper money era, and no one can escape the encirclement of paper money. In the old economic and globalization model, paper money mainly combined with resources, favoring resources and distancing itself from industries. Ultimately, the bursting of the resource finance bubble triggered this great crisis. So why doesn't the combination of paper money and resources work? Because when paper money and finance excessively combine with resources, there appears the terrifying consequence of "an inverted pyramid."

Chapter 4: Embarrassing Paper Money After the Crisis

The straying of paper money caused the crisis. The financial sector cannot evade its blame; what direction will it take? Domestically, can the resource finance bubble be controlled? Internationally, can a world currency and a world central bank be established? Perhaps the only "bright spot" in global economic growth, China, can it learn from similar cases and further promote the healthy development of domestic finance? Can it seize this opportunity to further promote the internationalization of the renminbi? Can it play its due role in world currency and the world central bank?

Chapter 5: Breaking the Great Attraction of Resources to Paper Money

Why do resources have such a strong attraction to paper money? After the termination of those overly leveraged resource finance products in the future, what will happen to the resource industry and resource finance? Will paper money withdraw from the resource finance field or continue to increase its presence? Domestically, can the resource bubble be controlled? Internationally, can a new resource finance model be established? Can China find a form of public ownership in the natural resources sector? Can it develop new energy sources vigorously based on this opportunity? How can we form a truly resource-conserving and environmentally friendly society?

Chapter 6: Industry Must Gild Paper Money

The premise for the economy returning to industry is that paper money must first return to industry. Transitioning from "light on industry, heavy on resources" to "light on resources, heavy on industry" is the only correct choice for paper money to reform. Industry is always the sole source of value. Gold coins have value and can circulate repeatedly through continuous equivalent exchanges within industry; paper money has no intrinsic value and must first comply with and serve the process of adding value to industry in order to "gild" itself, turning into "gold coins" and thereby gaining the "legitimacy" to continuously circulate. That is to say, industry should be the first stop for paper money after it leaves the central bank.
