Summary: Yesterday afternoon, Xiong Jian, a seller from Changsha on Taobao.com, received the first online business license in Changsha from the Tianxin branch of the Changsha Administration for Industry and Commerce (AIC). She became the first individual online retailer in Changsha to implement the "real-name system for online stores," exactly one month after its formal implementation.

Xiong Jian, who runs a store on Taobao.com, obtained the first e-commerce business license in Changsha.

(YiBang Power Network News) "Honesty is a basic requirement for running an online store, it's best to follow it voluntarily. After I hang up the real-name store business license logo, I will gain more trust from consumers." Xiong Jian smiled at the scene. At the same time, Xiong Jian also reminded her peers that natural persons who do not yet meet the registration conditions are not required to forcibly register with the AIC for a business license but should voluntarily provide their true personal identity information to the network commodity trading platform operator.

The online store business license allows infringement complaints to be addressed.

"In order to make my online store bigger and stronger, I want to provide a more honest symbol for my online store." Xiong Jian revealed the original intention of obtaining the business license yesterday when the "No.95 Avenue des Champs-Elysees" online store officially opened on January 1st. "The procedures for individuals to open online stores and obtain business licenses are the same as those for individual businesses, not complicated. All you need are your ID card, property certificate (or purchase contract, housing agreement copy), one-inch photo, Taobao real-name certification, and the highest product value as a deposit."

At the issuance site, reporters saw this newly issued business license. In appearance, there was no significant difference between Xiong Jian's online store business license and the ordinary individual business license. However, one different point is that this license is limited to providing cosmetics and jewelry sales via the Internet. It is reported that as long as the materials are complete, it takes only one or two hours to process. Xiong Jian spent a total of 20 yuan on the cost and registration fee.

The virtual nature of online transactions and the unclear identities of store owners have always been a concern for consumers. "Once there are infringement violations, we can contact relevant network commodity trading platform operators to find specific online store responsible parties," said the head of the Market Division of the Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce. "Generally speaking, the online store is the first responsible party; if consumers complain about the quality of services or products of a certain online store with solid evidence, and the network commodity trading platform operator does not take active measures but lets it develop unchecked, then they must bear joint liability." If a certain online store severely infringes on consumer rights or violates other laws and regulations, the online store will be closed by the network commodity trading platform operator.

"Online stores having a proper license allows buyers to check the store’s license at any time, increasing trust in the store, attracting more consumers to choose online shopping." Miss Chen, an experienced online shopper, said that adding this mandatory procedure frees online transactions from the awkward situation of "having no law to rely on." It is understood that after issuing the business license, the AIC does not charge the store owner business tax. Previously, some netizens worried that the real-name system for online stores would increase the cost of online shopping and lead to price increases, which can now be temporarily put aside. Before this, Guangzhou Vipshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., Shandong, Zhejiang, Hubei, and other places also issued business licenses for online stores.

No Compulsion Required for Online Stores to Obtain Business Licenses

On July 1, China's first administrative regulation standardizing online commodity transactions and related service behaviors - the Provisional Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Related Service Behaviors - began to be implemented. According to the Measures, natural persons engaging in online commodity transactions and related service behaviors should apply to the operators providing online trading platform services and submit their real identity information such as name and address. Those who meet the registration conditions should legally register with the AIC.

Regarding whether all online stores must go to the AIC to obtain business licenses, the head of the Market Division of the Hunan Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce explained, "In fact, enterprises and individual businesses that have already registered only need to publicly display the electronic link logo of the business license information on their website homepage or other prominent positions without needing to go to the AIC again for a special online business license. Natural persons who do not yet meet the conditions for obtaining a business license just need to inform the network commodity trading platform operator (such as Taobao, City Purchase Network, etc.) of their real personal identity and related information."

According to the head of the Market Division of the Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce, there are not many network commodity trading platforms in our province, and the scale is not large, so they particularly need standardization and support. August is the "Service Network Economy Healthy Development Promotion Month" in Hunan Province. (Editor: Shu Xiaochang)
