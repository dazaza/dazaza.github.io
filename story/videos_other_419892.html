On April 22, 2011, HTC will build an open video platform, declining online app stores_IT messages_Blog

by neqctdyi on 2011-04-22 17:42:06

Beijing time February 23 news, HTC CEO Zhou Yongming said at the 3GPP meeting that although HTC would not establish an online application store for smartphones and tablet computers, it would build a video service platform. From January 6 to April 22, 2011, HTC will provide content for partner telecoms and its own programs.

HTC is already the world's fifth largest smartphone manufacturer. According to Zhou Yongming, the company will create an open platform. By providing video content on its platform, HTC aims to bring new value to its partners.

Zhou Yongming said: "At this year's Mobile World Congress in Spain, I found an explosive opportunity in the communications industry." "I hope the company's value can go beyond the hardware and software sectors; such value will come from content and brand recognition."

HTC has no plans to operate an online app store but the established video platform can gain support from global partners, including content providers and operators.

According to HTC's plan, the platform will be launched in the second quarter of this year.

Early this month, HTC announced the acquisition of London-based digital content provider Saffron Digital and also bought a stake in the U.S.-based game service provider OnLive.

Zhou Yongming believes that through acquisitions, new value can be created, which can collaborate with HTC's products to bring different services and products. He also mentioned that about 30% of consumers watch mobile videos on their smartphones, most users are from India, Indonesia, and China, rather than the United States.

Becoming a global premium consumer brand is one of HTC's strategies. Zhou Yongming said HTC has already expanded in these countries through some long-term methods.
