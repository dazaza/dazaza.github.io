2. LNB (Low Noise Block): For the LNB, you can use Pus (or other brands), and dual LNB single output is sufficient; if there's a four-output option, that's even better, as it can directly connect to four eight-way power dividers.

3. Receiver Selection: The receiver must support dual LNBs, and during installation, the local oscillator frequency should be adjusted to match the LNB. Furthermore, the threshold level of the receiver is crucial because after the signal from the LNB is distributed by the power divider to dozens of receivers, the signal reaching each individual receiver is quite weak. Therefore, it's best to select a brand with a low threshold value and good stability. I've encountered a situation where a certain brand of receiver was used in a system with one dish serving over 30 rooms receiving ChinaSat 6B. Several channels had MSK modulation issues, and some had no signal at all. After switching to another brand of receiver, all channels were received successfully. When the former was connected to a system with only one dish serving four receivers, all channels could also be received. This clearly shows how critical the threshold level is.

4. Power Divider Selection: Choose a power divider brand with minimal attenuation and high quality. I usually use Shibei, both active and passive ones are similar. Through my practice, I found that there isn't much difference between the two types in terms of effect, except that the active one is more expensive.

5. Feedline Selection: The quality of the feedline is the most important factor, as it is the key to the success of your entire project. Specifications of 75-5 are fine. When purchasing, pay attention to: the wire diameter should be thick enough, generally 7mm; the feel should have a certain hardness and not be hollow; the shielding mesh should be dense; the shielding aluminum foil layer should be real aluminum foil (not plastic coated); the high physical foam layer should be snow white and flexible; the core wire diameter is generally 1mm, preferably full copper. Do not use the type for surveillance purposes, although it is also 75-5 and well-made, it has an extremely high attenuation for high-frequency signals.

II. Construction Points

1. Dish Location: Try to install the dish in a relatively central position compared to all the receivers. This reduces the length of the feedline and more importantly, reduces signal attenuation.

3PX Central Plains Satellite | DM500 Wholesale | Zhengzhou Satellite TV | Zhengzhou Satellite TV Installation | Hotel, Inn, Community Wired TV Engineering Installation 63059599

2. Power Divider and Installation Position: Reasonably configure according to the number of users. For example, if there are 30 users, first install a 4-way power divider below the dish, divide the 30 users into four parts based on proximity, then install an 8-way power divider in the middle of each part, fixed near the center of these four parts, and then route lines to each user in the shortest distance possible. Note: The distance between the receiver and the 8-way power divider should not be too long, ideally controlled within thirty to forty meters.

3. Avoid using line amplifiers, I've tried multiple times with no significant effect, instead, it leads to fewer channels being received.

3PX Central Plains Satellite | DM500 Wholesale | Zhengzhou Satellite TV | Zhengzhou Satellite TV Installation | Hotel, Inn, Community Wired TV Engineering Installation 63059599

4. Don't use too many series-connected power dividers, preferably no more than two levels. I've conducted experiments, where all channels could be received with the first and second levels, but only horizontal polarization programs could be received with the third level, none of the vertical polarization, and no channels at all with the fourth level. The reason (except for LNBs powered separately): Power dividers not only attenuate the signal but also reduce voltage. The 13V/18V voltage supplied to the LNB through the feedline decreases by at least 0.7V (characteristic of silicon semiconductor tubes) after passing through a power divider, which is 1.4V for two levels, 2.1V for three levels, plus the voltage drop in the feedline, leaving the LNB with about 10V, insufficient for normal operation, resulting in some or all channels failing to be received. Therefore, when increasing the number of users, it's better to increase the number of outputs of the existing power dividers rather than adding more levels of power dividers.
