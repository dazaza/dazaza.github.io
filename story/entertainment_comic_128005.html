The family... the place where I rejected it: The male protagonist of Clannad, Okazaki Tomoya, is someone who refuses to communicate with his family and doesn't want to stay at home. When he was a child, his mother passed away due to certain reasons... His father, subsequently, drowned his sorrows in alcohol every day. The two of them, who should have relied on each other for survival, distanced themselves because neither wanted to understand the other's pain, leading their lives into gradual decline. Eventually, during Tomoya's junior high school years, an incident of domestic violence brought the relationship between father and son to its lowest point. From then on, Tomoya and his father became estranged; their relationship seemed to be maintained only by blood ties and legal obligations. He went to school every day, but whether in class or during breaks, he just spaced out listlessly, continuing his student life day by day without purpose. He didn't participate in any club activities, had no close friends, and even Spring Yanagi, his only "friend" who could speak intimately with him and "sheltered" him every night, couldn't reach the frozen part of his heart... Not interfering with each other's hidden pains was the bottom line that ensured they could continue interacting. Time flowed mercilessly, and the time that seemed to have stopped around Tomoya was about to come to an end... right there, at the bottom of that long slope.
