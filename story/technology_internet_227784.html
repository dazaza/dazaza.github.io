Google's mobile phone location service (no simplified Chinese)

by wodezzz on 2009-08-24 01:28:28

Today, I accidentally discovered the mobile Latitude service (also known as Mobile Follow) launched by Google: Google Latitude. Of course, you can also use it to share your current location with your friends. Google Latitude obtains the geographical location information of each user at any time through mobile base stations or WIFI hotspots. To protect user privacy, you can choose to turn it on or off and decide who to share your location with. It also continues Google's free tradition. Moreover, it currently supports most mobile systems (Android, BlackBerry, Symbian, Windows Mobile, and iPhone). And it will also synchronize your location information to your blog or Gtalk, allowing your friends to keep track of your dynamics at any time. In fact, I think the purpose of Google Latitude is very clear: to obtain more user information for more targeted advertising delivery, and to improve and make Google Maps more practical. Of course, the regrettable thing is that this service does not yet have an official simplified Chinese language option (no wonder rich people immigrate, hehe).
