Thief smashed 10 cars in one night to plunder property

by haojing12 on 2012-02-11 11:06:09

Zhang Yan, reporter stationed in Zhangjiakou for this newspaper. In the early morning of February 8th, a group of thieves wandered into the Shiji Home Community in Qiaodong District of Zhangjiakou City and smashed the glass of ten cars, looting cash, cigarettes, alcohol, navigation devices, and other valuables inside. Faced with such audacious thieves, residents hope that the public security department of Zhangjiakou City will increase their efforts to crack down on crime, solve the case as soon as possible, and bring the thieves to justice.

■ Ten Cars Smashed and Stolen Overnight

On the morning of February 9th, I rushed to the Shiji Home Community in Qiaodong District of Zhangjiakou City to conduct an on-site interview about the incident.

Mr. Huang, a resident, told me that around midnight on the evening of February 7th when he returned to the community, none of the cars were damaged at that time. However, by the morning of the 8th, many car windows had been smashed. The thieves must have acted during the early hours of the 8th. It was reported that in this theft incident involving car decoders, car signal jammers, car lock-picking tools, and remote control decoders, a total of ten car windows in the Shiji Home Community were shattered, and all valuable items inside were completely stolen. One car owner mentioned that a small amount of cash and several packs of high-end cigarettes placed in his car were gone. Another car owner's newly purchased alcohol in the trunk was also stolen, while yet another owner’s navigation device was tampered with.

Residents expressed that these thieves were too brazen, daring to smash ten cars consecutively, showing no respect for the law. Residents hoped that the public security department would quickly solve the case and severely punish these audacious theft criminals.

■ Residents Hope Safety Measures Will Be Improved Soon

During the interviews, many residents pointed out that the community has about a dozen residential buildings, hundreds of households, and nearly a hundred cars, but there has never been any surveillance installed, nor have professional security guards been hired. The property management work is clearly not up to standard. Residents believe that if surveillance cameras were installed in the community, it would greatly deter potential thieves who might avoid communities equipped with such systems. Additionally, if surveillance existed, the police could find more clues, aiding in timely case resolution.

During the interview, I noticed that the community gate was only guarded by two elderly people, and other safety facilities were basically non-existent. When I went to the property management office, the door was tightly locked, and despite my best efforts, I couldn't obtain the contact information of the person in charge of the property management. Residents hope that the property management department will improve various safety facilities in the community as soon as possible, and at the very least install surveillance cameras to better protect the property safety of the residents.

In the afternoon of the 9th, I learned that after the case occurred, the police officers from the Victory Road Police Station in Zhangjiakou City arrived at the scene upon receiving the alarm and conducted an investigation. Currently, the police are actively searching for clues and striving to solve the case as soon as possible.
