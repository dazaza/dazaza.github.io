Energy-efficient buildings

More than 40% of global energy consumption and 21% of greenhouse gas emissions come from buildings. The largest energy consumers in buildings are technical systems and lighting, which together account for 40%-60% of the total energy cost of buildings. Therefore, there is great potential for energy savings in this area. The iconic building of the 2010 Shanghai World Expo - the China Pavilion - is a model of an energy-efficient building. The China Pavilion was built using energy-saving building technologies provided by Siemens.

Green landmark of the World Expo: The China Pavilion was built using energy-saving building technologies provided by Siemens. Building technology

To improve the energy efficiency of buildings, Siemens has introduced an overall building solution that connects building infrastructure, innovative electrical installation technology, and automation systems into an intelligent network. Through its fully integrated energy management solution, Siemens provides perfectly collaborative products and systems for building power distribution. In addition, communication and software components connect the power distribution system with the building automation system. As early as 2009, Siemens delivered a comprehensive energy-saving and emission-reduction solution to the Chaoyang District Government of Beijing, which included both technical and financial solutions. It is estimated that this resulted in an annual electricity saving of nearly 472,600 kilowatt-hours, or 12% energy saving. In 2010, Siemens' solutions were further applied to public buildings and other related areas within Chaoyang District. Energy Performance Contracting

When evaluating the energy efficiency of buildings, all costs incurred throughout the building's entire lifecycle must be taken into account. New buildings are often used for purposes beyond their original plans. If data recorded by building automation systems are used to regularly adjust individual systems according to actual usage, it can save 10%-15% of energy consumption. Additionally, through Siemens' energy performance contracting solutions, comprehensive modernization and energy-saving renovations can be carried out on old buildings, with the potential to save up to 40% of energy consumption. These costs will soon be offset by savings in energy and materials. Lighting

Electricity used for lighting accounts for almost 19% of the world's total electricity consumption. By using energy-saving lighting solutions, more than one-third of electricity can be saved - in fact, some advanced solutions can save up to 80% of electricity consumption. Modern lighting technologies such as intelligent ballasts, LEDs, and multiple sensors can also reduce electricity use in existing lighting systems and improve economic benefits. Building energy prosumption

In developed industrialized countries, an increasing number of buildings have solar photovoltaic panels or small wind power systems installed on their roofs. So-called "smart buildings" transform buildings from mere energy consumers into active participants in the energy market. Smart buildings are highly energy-efficient, capable of generating, storing, and using the energy they need themselves, while managing and controlling these processes autonomously. Equipped with smart meters, smart buildings that can make more accurate electricity usage forecasts and implement proactive building energy management have become part of the smart grid.
