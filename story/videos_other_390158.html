Additionally, Ma emphasized that Taobao must oppose bureaucracy and prevent corruption. Everyone can see that Taobao has set SNS as its top priority, meaning that the mall and group buying are all centered around SNS. With this explanation, I believe everyone understands now! It is expected that Taojianghu will integrate some functions and release them in April, so online stores need to pay close attention to gain an advantage and not fall behind!

Any application that allows users to participate via the internet, such as QQ and Shanda online games, possesses obvious profitability. The core is: user participation. For Taobao, why go SNS?

As an important role, Tencent has already integrated Tencent Weibo, and it is learned from insiders that Pony Ma has said that Tencent Weibo can only succeed, not fail, which leads Taobao. Besides, many competitors are also stepping up efforts. Also, there is a shift in business model. Taobao cannot continue with the extensive management style of the past where merchants just invest heavily in Juhuasuan or hard ads and direct trains to do good business. This development model is abnormal, lacking user participation and stickiness. From the development of the internet, whoever makes users participate more deeply will earn more money. Taobao has realized this point, so it will make significant changes this year. Another point is that online stores should not blindly pursue Juhuasuan for short-term traffic but also focus on increasing user participation and stickiness, otherwise, customers will come and go quickly. Therefore, Taobao will integrate existing guilds, Taojianghu, and develop more applications to attract users to interact more with Taobao and online stores to increase stickiness and participation.

For the vast number of online stores on Taobao:

The internet increasingly reflects real-life realities, and people's social attributes will be displayed more through the internet. Socialized e-commerce aims to spread and recommend products/services through people's social attributes via the internet and achieve better consumption based on the internet. Simply put: in real life, when you buy something, you would share it with your friends via phone calls, text messages, or face-to-face conversations. Your friends might then buy it themselves or tell their friends. In this process, you may also meet friends with similar tastes and discover new products you might like through these newly recognized friends. Now, such a process will be realized through the internet.

SNS brings changes to online retail:

The change from goal-oriented purchasing behavior to socialized purchasing behavior in online shopping. In traditional online shopping models, consumers find the products they need through advertisements or shopping searches, so the relevant parties in the shopping behavior are only the buyer and seller.

However, with Taobao's SNS transformation, first, the transaction relationship between buyers and sellers turns into a social relationship, both entering each other's social networks. If the buyer likes the seller's product or brand, the relationship between the buyer and seller becomes a sticky social relationship, allowing both parties to timely understand each other's dynamics.

Secondly, the relationship between buyers and sellers is no longer one-to-one. Sellers act as information sources, and buyers have become nodes in the diffusion of seller information. Through buyer attention, forwarding, and participation in seller activities, the seller's information enters the seller's social network, causing broader diffusion and transmission.

Challenges brought by SNS transformation:

The shift in e-commerce marketing from dissemination notification to sharing notification. How to stimulate user sharing will be an important topic in e-commerce marketing in 2011:

The sharing psychology of Chinese consumers: Most Chinese consumers are still overt consumers who use the purchase and use of certain products to achieve self-promotion. Therefore, although cost-performance is king during the purchase process, during consumer use, brand image and product quality are king. Therefore, consumers who buy off-season inventory, counterfeit goods, or Taobao market stalls are unwilling to share related shopping experiences.

Taobao's SNS transformation gathers scattered consumers together. On one hand, merchants have a good platform to dialogue with buyers, but on the other hand, the transparency of information between merchants and consumers is getting higher and higher, especially price differences and discount differences, which may lead to buyer dissatisfaction. Meanwhile, product quality and reputation will greatly affect the vitality of a product or even a brand on online sales platforms.

E-commerce marketing shifts from dissemination notification to: unique selling points + creative sharing points combined

The premise for driving consumer sharing is that the product or brand or activity needs to have a benefit point, meaning that the content of the sharing must be valuable, valuable to the recipient. Secondly, the sharing itself must bring benefits, meaning what emotional or material benefits does the consumer get from sharing this information. Therefore, to make the shared content valuable, it must have unique selling points.

Creative sharing points are to achieve participatory e-commerce marketing communication information, making the audience willing to receive and willing to share, which necessarily requires the premise of the topic of the product or activity.

Which methods may stimulate consumer sharing:

1. Big brands, because paying attention to and sharing big brand information is an important way to maintain consumers' sense of fashion, and through paying attention to and sharing related big brand information, consumers have an "I am a fashion trendsetter" emotional return.

2. Big brands will stimulate sharing, but currently there are few big brands in e-commerce. What about small and medium-sized brands? They must have unique selling points. Is cost-performance a selling point? Yes, but it's not a sharing point. No one would show off how frugal their life is or how they use "unknown quality" products, which is not what people consider a high-quality life.

3. Creative activities, making the activity a topic. This reminds me of the creative activity we did for Anta last August 15th, "65th Anniversary of Japan's Surrender, National Goods Must Be Strong." This activity was inherently related to the brand, Anta as a national goods brand, inevitably aroused patriotic passion among consumers on the day of Japan's surrender on August 15th. Regardless of how many people watch Japanese AVs, drive Japanese cars, or use Japanese brands in reality, anti-Japanese sentiment remains a hot topic on the internet.

Therefore, to succeed in the SNS-transformed Taobao, either you are a big brand, and big brands are like big stars, even their sneezes become materials for fans to talk about. If you're not a big brand, your product must be very special, for example, cosmetics with powerful functions, tea with unique taste, etc. If not, at least package a very special concept. Of course, if the brand isn't big and the product isn't special, then make the activity very special.

In summary: Under the environment of SNS-based e-commerce, e-commerce marketing must either be seen by consumers (though this is costly), searched by consumers, or shared by consumers. If none of these three can be achieved, there's no other choice but to wait for failure.

First, what is SNS:

Strictly speaking, domestic SNS is not Social Networking Services (social networking services), but Social Network Sites (i.e., social networking sites). Represented by platforms like Renren (Xiaonei), Kaixin, Bai Shehui, combining some relatively reliable data, it is not difficult to summarize the commonalities of mainstream SNS users.

Simply put: "There will not be more than six people separating you from any stranger (Obama and Jack Ma are no exception), meaning that at most through six people, you can know any stranger. This is the early understanding of social networking. Later, someone established internet services based on this theory, expanding network socialization through "friends of friends."

But "friends of friends" is just one way of social expansion, not the whole. Therefore, the current general meaning of SNS goes far beyond the "friends of friends" level. For example, aggregation based on the same topic (such as Tieba), aggregation based on hobbies (such as Fexion), aggregation based on learning experience (such as Facebook), aggregation based on the same weekend outing location, etc., are all included in the scope of "SNS".

SNS: Social Network Software, social networking, based on knowing friends' friends to expand one's network. And infinitely expanding one's network, being able to obtain help from this network when needed.

In interpersonal networks, to get to know any unfamiliar friend, it usually takes no more than six intermediaries. That is, if you want to know someone, asking friends to find friends who know him won't exceed six people.

In real society, human interaction forms a circle of friends and connections through introductions and handshakes. Each person doesn't need to know everyone directly, but only through his friends, friends of friends, to facilitate a handshake. Ordinary online interactions, however, mostly occur through certain platforms, such as placing oneself on a platform for many people to see and contact. The advantages and disadvantages of both are clear: social interactions are reliable, based on reliable interpersonal networks, but take time and are costly; platform-style online interactions are low-cost but unreliable.

Copying this mechanism onto the internet theoretically combines reliability and low cost. Some advantages include: in SNS, relationships within a friend circle are often highly authentic and reliable, without the so-called "false masks" of the internet, thus making real-name systems easier to implement; SNS is based on person-to-person contact networks, spreading from one to many, from many to many, utilizing the inexpensive and fast internet platform, making the establishment of networks faster and further reducing the cost of building personal networks.
