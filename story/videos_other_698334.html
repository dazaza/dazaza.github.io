※ What is sold during the buying and selling process? Answer: Benefits

Benefits are what bring happiness and advantages to the other party, helping them reduce or avoid trouble and pain.

One, customers never buy just the product itself; they buy the benefits that the product or service brings them;

Two, third-rate salespeople sell products (components), first-rate salespeople sell results (benefits);

Three, for customers, they will only buy when they clearly understand what benefits the product will bring them and what troubles it will avoid.

Therefore, first-rate salespeople don't focus on how much benefit they can get, but rather on the benefits the customer will receive. When the customer truly gains benefits through our product or service, they will put their money into our pocket and thank us.

※ What is the customer thinking during the face-to-face sales process?

Answer: The six constant questions in the customer's mind during the face-to-face sales process?

One, who are you?

Two, what do you want to talk to me about?

Three, what benefits will this discussion bring me?

Four, how do you prove what you say is true?

Five, why should I buy from you?

Six, why should I buy now from you?

These six questions may not be explicitly asked by the customer, but they will subconsciously think this way. For example, when the customer sees you instantly, their feeling is: I haven't seen this person before, why is he smiling and coming towards me? Their subconscious is wondering: who is this person? When you approach them and start talking, they are thinking: what do you want to talk to me about? When you speak, they are thinking: what benefits will this bring me? If it doesn't benefit them, they won't want to listen further, because everyone's time is limited, and they will choose to do things that benefit them. When they feel your product indeed benefits them, they will think: are you deceiving me? How do you prove what you say is true? When you can prove the benefits are real, they will certainly think: this product is really good, are there better ones elsewhere, or could someone else sell it cheaper? When you can give them enough information to let them know buying from you is the best deal, they will definitely think: can I buy again later, next month, or next year? So, you must give them enough reasons to know the benefits of buying now and the losses of not buying now.

Therefore, before visiting your customer, you should ask yourself these questions from the customer's perspective, answer them, design solutions, and provide sufficient reasons. Customers will buy what they think is best and most suitable for them.

※ How to compare with competitors when introducing products after-sales?

One, Do not belittle the opponent

1, You go and belittle the opponent, possibly the customer has some connection with the opponent, such as now using the opponent's product, his friends are using it, or he thinks the opponent's product is good, you belittle it equals saying he lacks vision, making mistakes, he will immediately dislike it.

2, Absolutely do not casually belittle your competitor, especially when the competitor's market share or sales are good, because if they really didn't do well, how could they become your competitor? Your baseless belittling of competitors will only make customers think you are untrustworthy.

3, As soon as you mention the opponent, you say bad things about others, customers will think you lack confidence or have quality issues.

Two, Compare objectively with your three major advantages and the opponent's three weaknesses.

There is a saying, compare with three fields, every kind of goods has its own strengths and weaknesses. When introducing products, you should highlight your three major strengths and compare them with the opponent's three major weaknesses. Even if similar products are compared subjectively, the difference becomes immediately apparent.

Three, USP Unique Selling Point

Unique selling point is something only we have and the competition lacks, just as each person has unique characteristics, any product also has its unique selling point. Emphasizing and highlighting the importance of these unique selling points in product introduction can increase the chances of successful sales.
