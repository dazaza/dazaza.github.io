October 4th, Let’s talk iPhone

by diglog on 2011-09-28 13:22:08

Latest news: Major tech media outlets both domestically and internationally have confirmed receiving the invitation to Apple's new product launch event. The information on the invitation is straightforward, clear, and immediately understandable, once again highlighting Apple's design philosophy of simplicity and depth.

The Calendar icon points to the date of the event: Tuesday, October 4, 2011, local time.

The Clock icon indicates the exact start time of the event: 10 a.m. sharp.

The Maps icon shows that this event will take place at the company headquarters (1 Infinite Loop, Cupertino), breaking away from Apple's usual practice of holding iPhone launch events at the Yerba Buena Center for the Arts or Moscone Center in San Francisco. A few days ago, ifanr.com had predicted the location of the event, and it now seems that these analyses were justified.

Finally, the phone icon is the one that most sparks the imagination among geeks. On one hand, the "phone" pattern indirectly dispels rumors about the release of the iPad 3. On the other hand, the number "1" in the upper right corner of the icon raises doubts about the speculation that Tim Cook would be releasing two models of iPhones (iPhone 4S and iPhone 5) simultaneously. The word "talk" in the theme of the event seems to reveal that the new iPhone will feature Apple's long-awaited advanced voice recognition technology, providing users with native voice input functionality.
