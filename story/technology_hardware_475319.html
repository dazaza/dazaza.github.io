Man stops the city administrative law enforcement officers from being rude and is beaten by them in return

by zzf000zxye0 on 2011-08-12 14:16:54

Chongqing News (reported by Yuan Jing) - Yesterday morning, Ren Daihua (a pseudonym), who lives in Building 10 of the Zhuyuan Residential Area in Huangshachi, Yuzhong District, witnessed several urban management law enforcement officers and a sanitation worker struggling with each other when he passed by the Nangqiao Station in Nangping. He initially intended to intervene but unexpectedly received a punch from one of the urban management officers as soon as he finished his first sentence...

A Blow for Trying to Mediate

At around 9 o'clock yesterday morning, after getting off the bus at Nangqiao Station and planning to transfer to Lianglukou, Ren Daihua noticed a crowd gathered together. Out of curiosity, Ren pushed through the crowd and saw four or five uniformed urban management officers physically assaulting an elderly person.

"Why are you hitting people?" Ren Daihua stepped forward quickly, pushing one officer aside while questioning them. Before he could finish his question, that person swung a punch directly at Ren's head. Dazed, Ren started to confront the officer.

"Several others quickly surrounded me," said Ren Daihua. He was pushed into the urban management patrol vehicle. "You shouldn't go there! If you do, they'll beat you!" Concerned onlookers shouted out loud warnings.

In the hospital, Ren Daihua, with a swollen left eye, held up a phone with three cracks on its screen, shaking his head repeatedly as he told the reporter: "After getting on the vehicle, I felt something was wrong, so I pulled out my phone to make a call but it was snatched away by one of the urban management officers, and my phone was smashed."

Soon afterward, Ren Daihua was taken to the Nangping Street Office. "After bringing me into the office, the urban management officers were about to leave," Ren Daihua recalled. He immediately walked out of the office and blocked the patrol car.

Six or Seven People Beating Him

"There, lying horizontally in front of the car," a staff member from an auto repair shop next to the Nangping Street Office gestured to indicate Ren Daihua's position at that time. Because his path was blocked, a man in casual clothes got out of the patrol car and tried to pull Ren Daihua aside.

This pulling action resulted in physical contact between the two. "Several people quickly surrounded him; one or two were in uniforms, the rest were in casual clothes," the worker said. Six or seven people began punching and kicking Ren Daihua.

"They fought here and then moved over there," the worker added. The beating only stopped when the police arrived at the scene. The entire process lasted five or six minutes. The crowd called an ambulance, which took him to the hospital.

On-Duty Urban Management Officers Claim Ignorance

To verify the incident with the urban management department, at 3:30 PM, the reporter rushed to the Nangping Street Urban Management Duty Room.

"I'm not aware of this matter," said Mr. Qi, the duty officer. He mentioned that he also participated in the law enforcement at Nangqiao Station in the morning. While it's true that the urban management officers had struggled with a man, he hadn't heard about anyone being brought back to the office, let alone being beaten.

"There was a forty-something man who came to the hall with a few urban management officers but left shortly after," a woman in her thirties from the street office said.

Another urban management officer also stated that he participated in the law enforcement at Nangqiao Station but wasn't aware of any civilians being brought back.

At around 5:00 PM, after being examined at the city emergency center, Ren Daihua was diagnosed with a fracture of the lower left orbital bone. The reporter learned that Ren Daihua is 46 years old and is a recipient of low-income assistance.
