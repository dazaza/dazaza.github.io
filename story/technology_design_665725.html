Steel market recovery pushes domestic coke prices higher

by dbfgskkgfen6 on 2012-02-15 09:22:41

The recovery of the steel market has driven up domestic coke prices - Market Observation - Industry News - Building Materials Network. Recently, the steel market has gradually recovered, and major steel mills such as Baosteel have successively raised steel prices, providing room for coke enterprises to raise prices. At the same time, by the end of the year, coking coal prices in many places have also risen, and it is expected that next year's overall coking coal prices will still maintain an upward trend. The increase in costs will also drive up domestic coke prices. However, the situation of over-supply in the coke industry has not fundamentally improved. Currently, most small and medium-sized coke enterprises still rely on chemical products to offset losses, and a high 40% production limit shows that enterprises are still under profit pressure.

The rise in steel prices has driven up coke prices. Baosteel recently took the lead in proposing a comprehensive price increase for major plate varieties in January 2011, with the increase ranging from 150-300 yuan/ton. Subsequently, large and medium-sized steel enterprises in China such as Hebei Iron and Steel Group and Angang Steel Company successively announced their price policies for January next year, with most products showing an upward trend. Analysts introduced that the current steel market inventory is at a relatively low level, production costs provide strong support, and the capital situation at the beginning of the year is relatively loose. It is expected that the steel market will continue to rise.

The price increase by steel mills has provided space for the coke market to rise. At the end of this year’s third quarter, domestic coke prices stopped falling and began to recover, starting to steadily rise in November. Currently, the mainstream ex-factory tax-inclusive quotations for second-grade metallurgical coke in Shanxi region are 1700-1780 yuan/ton, and the carboard tax-inclusive price for first-grade metallurgical coke is 1850-1900 yuan/ton; in Hebei region, the ex-factory tax-inclusive price for second-grade metallurgical coke delivered to factory is around 1850 yuan/ton, and the tax-inclusive price for quasi-first grade metallurgical coke delivered to factory is 1880-2000 yuan/ton; while in Northeast China, coke prices have risen due to cost push, with the current ex-factory tax-inclusive price for second-grade metallurgical coke around 1850-1900 yuan/ton, and first-grade metallurgical coke at 2050 yuan/ton.

Despite some price recovery, the profit situation for coke enterprises remains severe. It is understood that large independent coking enterprises have better economic benefits and can produce coke at full load; while coking enterprises affiliated with steel mills rarely limit or reduce production due to guaranteed demand. The least optimistic situation is for most small and medium-sized coking enterprises in the country. The Hebei Coking Industry Association recently held a December market analysis meeting, proposing to continue maintaining an appropriate production limit to change the passive situation of long-term losses in the entire industry, and appropriately adjust sales prices according to enterprise operating conditions.

Coking coal price increases are eroding profits. Recently, coal enterprises in Northeast China, Shanxi, Hebei, and other places have successively raised the prices of coking coal for January next year. Among them, Shanxi Coking Coal Group has determined to raise coking coal prices by 100-150 yuan/ton, which heralds a "high opening" pattern for next year's domestic coking coal prices. Analysts said that coking coal prices in 2011 are about to rise, with an expected increase of more than 10%.

Recently, the performance of the coke market has been significantly weaker than its upstream and downstream markets. The coking coal prices in major coking coal producing areas such as Shanxi, Northeast China, Hebei, and Central-South China have been significantly increased recently, with increases ranging from 90-150 yuan/ton. However, as a direct downstream product, coke prices have only been raised by 50 yuan/ton based on last month's settlement.

More seriously, currently, steel mills are not eager to stockpile coke, forcing coking enterprises to increase production limits. In places like Shijiazhuang and Handan, the production limits of coking enterprises are all above 40%. Such a large production limit has caused a significant reduction in coke by-products, resulting in a comprehensive increase in the cost per ton of coke, causing coking plants to fall back into a loss cycle.

Although coke prices are expected to continue to rise before the holiday, the status of the coke industry surviving in the cracks has not changed. With an annual output as high as 440 million tons and unreleased capacity reaching up to 40%, these factors will become stumbling blocks for the industry's profit recovery. And due to the high price of upstream coking coal, the production cost of coke has increased significantly, making it very difficult for enterprises to turn losses into profits.
