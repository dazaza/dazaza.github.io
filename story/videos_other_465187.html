"Shang Wine" was named during the Ming Dynasty, with a legendary background. It is said that when Prince Yan swept north, upon reaching Tongzhou South, he heard of a great victory ahead, and decided to reward his troops with the local small-burnt wine called "Tian Chang Quan". Later, Emperor Chengzu, disguised as a commoner, inspected the area and found "Tian Chang Quan" still in existence. He purchased some wine and noticed that the wine seller resembled the dragon-phoenix leading lady in his dream last night. Realizing this, he understood: drinking this wine would stabilize the country; tasting this wine would bring beautiful women. Both mountains and rivers, beauties were fully obtained through "Tian Chang". Delighted, he personally inscribed "Tian Chang Quan", but then thought it inappropriate since he himself was the emperor and naming it "Tian Chang" might offend the heavens. He then named it "Shang" and designated it as tribute wine. There is a poem that says: Pure water falls from heaven, jade dew and divine nectar are pure. First enjoy the stabilization of the world, then taste the beauty.

Drinking warm white spirit reduces harm.

Heating white spirit before drinking is beneficial to the human body. Since the main component of white spirit is ethanol (alcohol), along with aldehydes, excessive drinking can lead to alcohol poisoning. Although aldehydes are not the main components of white spirit, they are much more harmful to the human body than alcohol. However, aldehydes have a low boiling point, around 20℃. Therefore, warming the wine can make most of the aldehydes evaporate, thereby reducing the harm to the human body.
