My original novel The Bastard

This novel is set in a mountainous area at the end of the Qing Dynasty, depicting the conflict and struggle within the Luo family triggered by the birth of a bastard child. It reveals the dark psychology and cruel means displayed by certain social strata in their fight for property and adherence to feudal rites. It also portrays the image of a feudal woman who dares to fight against evil and uses all means to protect the weak.

The basic content of the work can be briefly introduced as follows:

A young man from a mountainous area, Li Huailin, lost his pants while on his way home from doing tea business in the county town. He tried to steal a pair of pants from a wealthy household when it was dawn, but was caught. Instead of punishment, he was retained by the mistress of the house, Chunxiang, who then let him cohabit with her widowed self, leading to her pregnancy and the birth of a bastard child. Faced with the dilemma of her clan uncle trying to seize her property, the mistress spared no effort to raise this bastard child, named Yuer, causing dissatisfaction among her uncle's family and putting the bastard child in a precarious situation of life and death. Even after Chunxiang remarried, Yuer's biological father, Li Huailin, continued to harass her, even deceiving her to sell her to a brothel in the city for gambling funds, which led to Chunxiang's suicide out of hatred. In his mad pursuit of money, Li Huailin further kidnapped Yuer, ultimately meeting his end off a cliff. Master Yuer, for Yuer to have a good living environment and legal protector, adopted a foster son, Luo Xingjun. Because Luo Xingjun later passed the examination for the degree of scholar, the reputation of Master Yuer's family greatly improved, finally changing Yuer's fate to some extent.

The work strives to make the characters vivid, the plot lively and touching, with a rhythm that alternates between tension and relaxation. The entire book totals 110,000 words, divided into twenty-four chapters, each chapter being interlinked yet independently complete.
