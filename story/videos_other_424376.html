PX is the abbreviation for p-xylene. Its Chinese name is 1,4-dimethylbenzene, also known as para-xylene. PX (para-xylene) is used to produce plastics, polyester fibers, and films.

【Structure or Molecular Formula】 C8H10; C6H4(CH3)2 Appearance and characteristics: colorless transparent liquid with an odor similar to toluene

【Dangerous Mark】 7 (flammable liquid) Characteristics: colorless transparent liquid with a fragrant smell, toxic!

I. Health Hazards

Invasion Pathways: Inhalation, ingestion, dermal absorption.

Health Hazards: Xylene irritates the eyes and upper respiratory tract, and at high concentrations, it has an anesthetic effect on the central nervous system.

Acute Poisoning: Short-term inhalation of higher concentrations may cause obvious irritation symptoms in the eyes and upper respiratory tract, conjunctival and pharyngeal hyperemia, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, chest tightness, weakness in the limbs, confusion, and staggering gait. Severe cases may experience agitation, convulsions, or coma, and some may have hysteria-like episodes.

Chronic Effects: Long-term exposure may lead to neurotic syndrome, menstrual abnormalities in female workers, and skin dryness, fissures, and dermatitis in workers.

II. Toxicological Data and Environmental Behavior

Toxicity: Low toxicity.

Acute Toxicity: LD50 5000 mg/kg (oral in rats); LC50 19747 mg/kg, 4 hours (inhaled by rats)

Stimulus: Human eye exposure: 200 ppm, causes irritation. Rabbit skin exposure: 500 mg (24 hours), moderate irritation.

Subacute and Chronic Toxicity: Rats and rabbits inhaling 5000 mg/m3, 8 hours/day, for 55 days, resulted in eye irritation, exhaustion, ataxia, slight decrease in RBC and WBC count, bone marrow proliferation, and 3% to 4% megakaryocytes.

Mutagenicity: Cytogenetic analysis: beer yeast 1 mmol/tube.

Reproductive Toxicity: Minimum toxic concentration (TDL0) in rats: 19 mg/m3, 24 hours (medicine used on days 9-14 of pregnancy), causing musculoskeletal developmental abnormalities.
