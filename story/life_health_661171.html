["Cold"-induced Gynecological Inflammation]

Temperature fluctuations not only make women feel icy hands and feet and experience menstrual irregularities like dysmenorrhea, but according to Director Luo Chunmei of the gynecological advisory group of Xi'an Sanqin Hospital, insufficient warmth can cause "cold uterus," leading to blood stasis, increased vaginal discharge, and decreased hygiene conditions in the vagina, which may trigger vaginitis, pelvic inflammatory disease, and cervical inflammation.

⊙Cervical Inflammation - Beware of Cancerous Changes

The cervix is an important line of defense against pathogens entering the uterus, fallopian tubes, and ovaries. Symptoms of cervical inflammation are not obvious and can easily be overlooked, leading to cervical erosion. Relevant data shows that women with cervical erosion have seven times the risk of developing cervical cancer compared to healthy individuals.

There are two types of cervical inflammation: acute and chronic, with chronic cervical inflammation being more common. It often manifests as cervical erosion, increased vaginal discharge, viscous consistency, occasionally purulent or bloody discharge, accompanied by lower back pain, abdominal pain, and a sensation of heaviness in the lower abdomen, worsening after menstruation, bowel movements, or sexual intercourse. Some may also experience menstrual irregularities, dysmenorrhea, and infertility.

⊙Vaginitis - Easily Recurrent

Healthy women’s vaginas have natural defense mechanisms due to their anatomical structure, preventing inflammation. However, when the natural defense mechanism of the vagina is disrupted, pathogens can easily invade, leading to vaginitis.

Patients with vaginitis often experience symptoms such as unusual itching, increased vaginal discharge, foul odor, and burning pain. Without proper examination and self-medication, what seems like a minor ailment can become persistent and difficult to cure.

⊙Pelvic Inflammatory Disease - Can Cause Infertility

Symptoms of pelvic inflammatory disease are not obvious, sometimes accompanied by low-grade fever, fatigue, insomnia, discomfort in the lower abdomen, pain, and soreness in the waist and sacrum, menstrual irregularities, etc. The editorial team learned that pelvic inflammatory disease is not a serious issue, but if left untreated, it can progress from acute to chronic pelvic inflammatory disease, increasing the likelihood of infertility.

["Cold"-induced Menstrual Irregularities]

Menstruation is an important physiological indicator for women, sensitively reflecting changes in the endocrine system and internal reproductive organs. Ms. Zhang, who came to Xi'an Sanqin Hospital for gynecological treatment, encountered such a situation, “Every winter, whenever the weather suddenly becomes very cold, my menstruation begins to become abnormal. With the recent drop in temperature, dysmenorrhea has started tormenting me again, making it hard for me to focus on anything.”

According to Director Luo Chunmei of the gynecological advisory team, the uterus is an important reproductive organ in women, preferring warmth over cold. Sudden cold weather and insufficient warmth, especially cooling of the lower body, can directly lead to a "cold uterus." Constriction of uterine blood vessels can result in reduced menstruation, dysmenorrhea, or even amenorrhea, causing menstrual irregularities.
