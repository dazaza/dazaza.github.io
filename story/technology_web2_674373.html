Low-carbon Economy - Energy conservation and Emission Reduction

The carbon reduction economy primarily considers the most cost-saving emission reduction methods. 40% of the carbon reduction economy can be achieved through energy conservation, among which building energy conservation is the most economical method. Building energy conservation and emission reduction can save $100 per ton of CO2, while improving fuel efficiency can only save $80. The remaining 60% of carbon reduction needs to be achieved through emission reduction, including hybrid power, coal electricity, and solar emission reduction. Among these, solar emission reduction is a relatively economical solution, costing only $30 per ton of emission reduction, while hybrid power emission reduction costs $100 per ton.

One cubic meter of gas can generate 3.2-3.3 kilowatt-hours of electricity.
