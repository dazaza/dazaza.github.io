Australian Department of Defense statement: Lenovo PCs not banned

by anonymous on 2013-08-02 10:13:10

On the 27th of this month, the Australian Financial Review reported that intelligence agencies from countries including the UK, Australia, and the US have banned the use of Lenovo PCs internally. The report stated that sources within the intelligence community confirmed that this ban was issued as early as the mid-2000s. At that time, some laboratory tests indicated that Lenovo's hardware contained backdoors, and firmware in some chips had vulnerabilities.

In its report titled "Intelligence Agencies Ban Lenovo PCs Over Security Concerns," the Australian Financial Review stated that multiple intelligence and defense sources from the UK and Australia informed the newspaper that around the mid-2000s, the 'Five Eyes' intelligence alliance, consisting of the US, UK, Australia, New Zealand, and Canada, issued a written ban prohibiting intelligence and defense agencies in their respective countries from using Lenovo computers at the "confidential" and "top secret" levels due to concerns about potential malicious hardware vulnerabilities in Lenovo computers that could be remotely controlled by intruders.

However, Lenovo Group subsequently issued a statement saying: "We are not aware of such a ban, nor have we received any information about it. We cannot comment on this. Lenovo continues to maintain good and solid relationships with our government department clients."

Australian Department of Defense also responded to the report recently, stating that Australia has never banned Lenovo computer devices. The Department of Defense emphasized that the report by the Australian Financial Review was inaccurate, and Lenovo computer products have never been banned, whether in encrypted or non-encrypted systems.
