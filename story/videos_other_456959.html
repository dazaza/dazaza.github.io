Skyworth Delivers Best Performance in Color TV Industry, Addition Campaign Aims for 50 Billion Yuan

by zzf000zxyn7 on 2011-07-04 13:48:40

Lang Lang reports from Guangzhou

Digital creator Skyworth (0751.HK) released its annual report for the 2010-2011 financial year on June 30. Although net profit decreased by a small 6% compared to the previous year, its net profit of HK$1.281 billion was still the best in the domestic color TV industry.

According to Skyworth Digital's annual report, the group's turnover increased by 6.9% year-on-year to reach HK$24.339 billion. The average increase over the past five years has reached 18%. The contribution of the set-top box business cannot be overlooked. The revenue from Skyworth Digital's digital set-top box business was HK$3.696 billion, an increase of 95% compared to the previous financial year; TV product sales revenue was HK$20.163 billion, a decrease of 1.8% compared to the previous financial year.

It is worth noting that under the circumstances of a significant 36% drop in net profit in the first half of the financial year (April-September 2010), Skyworth Digital improved its gross margin in the second half of the financial year by accelerating the popularization of mid-to-high-end products such as LED LCD TVs and 3D smart TVs.

Zhang Xuebin, chairman of the board of directors of Skyworth Group, told this reporter, "The situation in the first half of last year was the most severe in the past decade, and Skyworth's color TV business once incurred losses. However, after just one quarter of difficulties, Skyworth emerged as the leader."

For the 2011-2012 financial year starting in April this year, Skyworth has set its own goals: the domestic market's color TV sales target is expected to be 7 million units, which will be an increase of 9% over last year. This includes 4 million LED LCD TVs and 1.5 million 3D TVs. The number of LED LCD TVs will increase by more than 150% over last year, accounting for 57%. The full-year color TV sales target for the overseas color TV market is 2 million units, an increase of 25% over last year.

For Skyworth, maintaining stable growth in the color TV and set-top box businesses is very realistic. However, since last year, Skyworth has started targeting the cultivation of new industries. After missing the opportunity to share Gree's white goods assets in 2005, Skyworth began to focus on building its own base to enter the white goods sector. Its white goods base in Lishui, Nanjing, went into operation in January this year. According to Skyworth Digital's annual report, Skyworth plans to invest another RMB 2.6 billion in Lishui to expand refrigerator and washing machine production capacity and build a liquid crystal TV base with a capacity exceeding one million units.

Apparently, after the subtraction movement of abandoning the computer and mobile phone businesses in recent years, the management of Skyworth has now restarted the "addition" and set a goal of breaking through RMB 50 billion in sales revenue within five years. Previously, it had already launched its own brand of tablet computers and laid out in areas such as OLED and LED chips. Next, it will also take shares in Guangzhou LGD's 8.5th generation liquid crystal panel production line.

However, Skyworth's expansion this time has triggered controversy from outside. Can Skyworth's white goods successfully break through given the scale barriers already formed in the white goods industry? Will the RMB 2.6 billion investment succeed? Luo Qingqi, senior director of the Pari Consulting Institution, said, "As a private enterprise, Skyworth's biggest advantage is its flexible mechanism. It proceeds with new projects step by step, and if problems arise, it will choose to withdraw. It was like this in the computer and mobile phone fields before, so Skyworth will also avoid investment losses."

It is worth noting that Yang Dongwen, vice president of Skyworth Group, revealed that 60% of Skyworth's color TV sales last year came from the rural appliance program. The nationwide rural appliance program will end at the end of 2012. As the policy-driven effect of the rural appliance program disappears, along with the shrinkage already appearing in the first and second-tier markets, how Skyworth and other color TV giants respond is a new challenge.
