**Nasolabial Fold Filling**

The nasolabial fold is located at the junction of the cheeks and upper lip, extending obliquely downward from the outer edge of the nostrils. In young people, the nasolabial fold is only noticeable when making facial expressions such as smiling, pouting, or puffing out the cheeks. As one ages, the facial muscles become increasingly lax, causing the nasolabial fold to become more pronounced. Therefore, a prominent nasolabial fold is one of the key signs of aging. Once the nasolabial fold becomes flatter and less deep, a person immediately appears younger and more attractive.

**What is Nasolabial Fold Filling?**

During adolescence, changes in the shape and original state of the nasolabial fold, whether in facial expression or at rest, give a natural and harmonious impression. After the age of 30, the face gradually begins to show signs of aging, particularly with the deepening of the nasolabial fold becoming most apparent. This is due to the sagging of facial skin accumulating on the cheek side of the nasolabial fold, combined with over three decades of frequent movement in this area. Consequently, in a resting state, the nasolabial fold gradually deepens, widens, and elongates. During various facial expressions, these changes are even more noticeable, which causes much distress for those who value beauty. Thus, they seek various cosmetic procedures, and cosmetic professionals have attempted various methods, such as lower facelifts, fillers, and lasers. However, from an effectiveness standpoint, combining facelifts with filling techniques yields the most ideal results in terms of reducing and smoothing the nasolabial fold.

**Who Needs Nasolabial Fold Filling?**

It is suitable for individuals with deeply sunken nasolabial folds.

**Methods of Nasolabial Fold Filling:**

1. **Surgical Method:**

- Mark the position of the nasolabial fold.

- Administer local anesthesia.

- Insert a small needle scalpel to separate and sever the fibrous ligaments connecting the nasolabial fold to deeper tissues, then apply slight pressure.

- Use a liposuction method via a syringe to extract an appropriate amount of subcutaneous fat from areas such as the abdomen or thighs for reserve use.

- Based on the depth of the nasolabial fold, inject an appropriate amount of fat through the original needle hole into the nasolabial fold area. Apply ointment or cover the needle hole with a bandage.

2. **Injection Filler Method: Artecoll (Ivee) Injection Filler.**

- Mechanism of Action: Artecoll (Ivee) is a new type of injectable medical aesthetic material composed of PMMA (polymethyl methacrylate) microspheres and collagen. The PMMA microspheres are smooth and round, with diameters ranging from 32 to 40 micrometers, suspended in a 3.5% collagen solution carrier. When injected into the treatment area, the smooth, round PMMA microspheres are quickly encapsulated by a fine fibrous membrane, preventing any movement or degradation. The characteristics of the PMMA microspheres ensure that they are not digested or absorbed by the body's macrophages, minimizing irritation to the tissue. PMMA can stimulate fibroblasts to synthesize and secrete collagen. After 1-3 months, the injected collagen gradually degrades and is replaced by the body’s own collagen, while the PMMA remains, providing permanent results and ensuring the softness of the local tissue without hard lumps or unnatural sensations.

**Characteristics after Nasolabial Fold Filling:**

1. Prevent infection: Use antibiotics and maintain oral hygiene.

2. Apply ice packs to reduce swelling and immobilize the area.

3. Avoid using cosmetics for 14 days, but be careful not to apply force if used.

4. Wait several weeks after surgery before engaging in strenuous activities.

5. Few complications; reassessment of the procedure can be conducted after a few months.

**Contraindications for Nasolabial Fold Filling:**

1. Allergy to collagen.

2. Allergy to lidocaine.

3. Immunological diseases.

4. Keloid-prone individuals.

5. Individuals currently using corticosteroids.
