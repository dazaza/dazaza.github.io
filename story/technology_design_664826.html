What's new in the new *Journey to the West*? Is it a return to tradition? In response to recent netizens' numerous doubts, Zhang Jizhong, the chief producer of the series, summarized and responded, stating, "It's not scary to have controversies; the new version of *Journey to the West* can withstand scrutiny."

### Monkey King, Pigsy, and Scary Demon Costumes

Is the new *Journey to the West* just an Animal World?

#### Response: Realistic Restoration of *Journey to the West* Characters

After the premiere of the new *Journey to the West*, many viewers expressed strong opinions about the character designs, saying Sun Wukong's initial appearance resembles Voldemort, Pigsy looks like a wild boar, and demons such as the Black and White Impermanence, the Black Bear Spirit, and the Bull Demon King have been criticized for being "too terrifying, unsuitable for children." Some netizens even joked, "The new *Journey to the West* is basically an Animal World!"

**Zhang Jizhong:** We are filming a mythological story, and making it realistic is our primary concern. Some viewers say we ruined their image of the Black Bear Spirit, but isn't the Black Bear Spirit supposed to be a monster, not a prince charming? Furthermore, imagine demons transforming, fangs protruding, nails extending dramatically, saliva dripping audibly—how could that not be terrifying? Why must we only cater to children? The demons in *The Lord of the Rings* and *Harry Potter* are far scarier than ours, yet they bring us a sense of awe because those creations are believable. Take Pigsy for example. The original text describes him as follows: "His mouth hangs down like a lotus bud, his ears are large like fans revealing golden eyes. His fangs are sharp like steel files, and his long snout opens wide like a fiery basin." Clearly, Pigsy was originally more akin to a wild boar. In previous versions, he was depicted as a domestic pig, which is inaccurate.

### Stiff Expressions, Talking Without Moving Mouths

Do Monkey King and Pigsy Suffer from Facial Paralysis?

#### Response: Limited Funding, No Small Machines Installed

After the premiere of the new *Journey to the West*, aside from the criticism of Sun Wukong and Pigsy's frightening appearances, some observant viewers noticed that their facial expressions were stiff, almost as if they had facial paralysis. Additionally, sometimes their mouths didn't move while they spoke, leading to the sound coming out without visible lip movement. Some netizens joked, "Monkey King and Pigsy truly possess great powers—they've mastered ventriloquism."

**Zhang Jizhong:** The so-called "facial paralysis" and "ventriloquism" of Monkey King and Pigsy stem from limited funding. We couldn't afford to install the small machines in their mouths that would have allowed for more fluid facial expressions. Hollywood-made masks are very advanced, but when you wear one, to make it appear as though you're speaking, your mouth has to stretch back to the base of your skull to show any sign of opening. To address this issue, we applied a layer of glue at the corners of the mouth, keeping it tightly sealed. However, after two or three hours, once sweat sets in, the flexibility decreases, making it hard to control the movements.
