&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Also, don't wait until the fuel light comes on before refueling. Because the fuel pump is located inside the fuel tank, the temperature of the fuel pump during continuous operation is relatively high, but being immersed in the fuel can effectively cool it down. When the fuel light comes on, it means that the fuel level has already dropped below the fuel pump. If you always wait for the light to come on before refueling, it will shorten the lifespan of the fuel pump. Moreover, having too little fuel or running out of fuel may also damage the life of the fuel pump. Here's another common sense tip: when you see a tanker truck parked at the gas station, try not to go there to refuel. Because when the tanker truck fills the underground tank, it stirs up sediment at the bottom of the tank, which could potentially cause some harm to the vehicle.
