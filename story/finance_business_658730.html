According to the Beijing Times, Han Han stated that he would no longer respond to this matter, as such a war of words was meaningless. Regarding Han Han's withdrawal, Fang Zhouzi was not ready to let it go and said he would continue to write articles questioning Han Han. Additionally, Han Han had already entrusted his lawyer to formally submit relevant litigation materials to the Putuo District People's Court in Shanghai yesterday.

Han Han Stops Meaningless War of Words

Since the beginning of the year, Han Han and Fang Zhouzi have been arguing over the "ghostwriting scandal" until now. Han Han said: "I believe many people are also tired of it, and I don't want to talk about it anymore. Looking back now, as a public figure, my initial response was not calm enough. For a writer, their work must be personally written, which is the part I care most about in writing. However, the completely impossible fact that I have a writing team has been baselessly questioned and spread, and at that time, I was indeed in a bad mood. I will no longer respond to this matter. There are too many things that need attention, and such a war of words is meaningless. With this matter, I now put down my pen."

Han Han emphasized again that all his writings were personally written by him. He said: "Literary works can be fictional and imaginative. You cannot determine that I am falsifying just because I said in an interview that I sleep in the lower bunk, but in literary works, I sleep in the upper bunk. Moreover, I have manuscripts, family letters, notes, and witnesses to prove my innocence, but Fang Zhouzi has no evidence to prove that two of my early articles were written by someone else, yet he is 100% sure that those two articles were ghostwritten by a mysterious middle-aged man, based on reasons that I find purely speculative."

Dedicated Writing to Repay Readers

Han Han also expressed that he decided to publish the manuscript collection "Brightness and Integrity" just to prove his innocence. "It includes most of my manuscripts, and even ten thousand words wasted when I wrote 'Three Gates' due to lack of experience in long-form writing." Han Han said: "Reflecting on my writing after becoming famous, sometimes my attitude was not correct. I hastily concluded and published after writing ten thousand words, or started the first part and then left it unfinished. To repay all my supporters, I will write more carefully in the future."

For the lawsuit between both parties, Han Han admitted that taking such legal action was not very unrestrained. "The lawsuit is to confirm my manuscripts and evidence, and also to prevent the industry from setting such a precedent. That is, when you dislike a writer, there is no need for viewpoint disputes, no need for literary criticism, and no need for any evidence. Just say that one of his articles was written by someone else, and thus the writer's reputation will be damaged. If everything is wrong, then the lawsuit is also so that my readers and relatives can simply answer in the future when asked: He was wrongfully accused."

Fang Zhouzi Continues to Question Han Han

Regarding Han Han's decision to stop responding, Fang Zhouzi analyzed that it was because Han Han found it difficult to deal with the increasing number of questions, and could not handle them, thus choosing to withdraw. "Another reason may be that Han Han anticipated that his statements were full of loopholes, and it would be better to say less or nothing at all, so he simply stopped responding." Fang Zhouzi said that he actually hoped Han Han would not withdraw, because then he would have more material to write analytical articles.

Fang Zhouzi stated that he would not stop questioning due to Han Han's withdrawal, and the analysis of Han Han's articles would continue. The material for the analytical articles would not only come from Han Han's writings but would also expand to some interviews Han Han gave on television, online videos, and other media. Regarding Han Han's lawsuit against Fang Zhouzi, Fang Zhouzi said that he had not received any notifications from relevant departments. According to reports from the Shanghai Channel of Xinhua News Agency, upon verification by the Putuo District People's Court in Shanghai, Han Han had formally submitted relevant litigation materials to the Putuo District People's Court through his lawyer on the morning of March 3rd. The court will conduct a filing review within seven working days and provide a response.
