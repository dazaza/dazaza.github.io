Q&A on Photon Hair Removal

Patient: Compared to traditional photon, light quantum, colored light, and pure laser hair removal methods, what advantages does Elaser have?

Prof. Zhang Dechun: The core technology of Elaser consists of three points: radio frequency + light energy + epidermal cooling, featuring the fastest speed, highest efficiency, safest operation, easiest use, and most economical cost. It utilizes the skin's selective absorption of light energy to enhance the absorption of radio frequency by skin tissues. At the same time, the surface contact-type cooling technology of radio frequency eliminates discomfort and complications caused by excessive heat from lasers, significantly improving both efficacy and safety.

Compared to traditional light therapies, Elaser has created the following three new standards:

1. Safety: Elaser can effectively avoid common side effects associated with lasers/intense pulsed light (IPL), while greatly reducing the operational difficulty.

2. Efficacy: The range of eligible customers is broader, immediate results are more noticeable, and long-term effects are more enduring.

3. Service: By avoiding various side effects, it makes the treatment more private and comfortable.

Patient: Is Elaser hair removal safe? Will it cause body odor?

Prof. Zhang Dechun: Elaser hair removal has been certified by the U.S. FDA, EU CE safety certificate, Canadian CSA safety certificate, and Japanese National Drug Regulatory Agency international certification, making it very safe. Since the laser only targets specific objectives, it will not affect the functions of sweat glands or sebaceous glands. Because lasers only act on melanin, and hair follicles contain a large amount of melanin, when lasers of specific wavelengths act on hair follicles, their thermal energy is absorbed by the melanin in the follicles, causing the follicles to coagulate and necrotize, thus achieving permanent hair removal. Sweat glands and sebaceous glands basically do not contain melanin, so the laser has no effect on them.

Patient: Is Elaser hair removal effective for dark-skinned individuals?

Answer: The results are the same as for light-skinned individuals. Elaser technology combines both radio frequency energy and light energy, effectively removing hair while reducing the usage of light energy, thereby minimizing the occurrence of side effects to the greatest extent, making the treatment of various colors of hair and skin safer.

Patient: What are the contraindications for Elaser hair removal?

Prof. Zhang Dechun:

1. Individuals with photosensitive constitutions

2. Those who have recently consumed photosensitive foods or drugs (such as celery, isotretinoin, etc.)

3. Patients with implanted pacemakers or defibrillators

4. Patients with damaged skin at the treatment site

5. Patients with diabetes, heart disease, hypertension, or epilepsy

Patient: What precautions should be taken before and after Elaser hair removal treatment?

Prof. Zhang Dechun:

1. Avoid intense sun exposure one month before and after the treatment

2. Avoid chemical or mechanical stimulation two days before and after the treatment

3. Avoid fillers or other injectable treatments two weeks before and after the treatment

Patient: Is Elaser hair removal permanent?

Prof. Zhang Dechun: Yes. Its principle of action lies in the fact that laser beams can selectively penetrate to the hair follicle area, where they are absorbed by the melanin in the follicles, causing selective destruction, leading to the degeneration and shrinkage of the follicles, thereby achieving permanent hair removal.

Patient: Why does Elaser hair removal need to be done in several sessions?

Prof. Zhang Dechun: Laser hair removal requires 3-5 sessions to be fully effective. This is because hair growth goes through three stages: anagen (growth phase), catagen (regression phase), and telogen (resting phase). During the anagen phase, there is melanin present in the follicles, and the laser can destroy the follicles during this phase. In the catagen phase, there is less melanin in the follicles, and the laser’s destructive effect on the follicles is weaker. In the telogen phase, there is no melanin in the follicles, and the laser has no effect on them. Only when the telogen follicles transition into the anagen phase will the laser have an effect on them.
