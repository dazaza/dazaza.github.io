QQ Zone melancholy diary: It's either fulfilling, or lonely and isolated.

by lwf225 on 2009-08-31 18:18:44

(One)

I smiled, coldly looking at everyone around me. It was a kind of hysterical smile, the intersection of two extremes.

I've forgotten how to cry. Sitting alone in a corner, I expressionlessly stared at the cold walls, drawing the only bit of warmth from the hot tea in my hands.

Looking at the laughing crowd, I still smiled; no one noticed my sadness.

(Two)

Blood kept gushing out of the wound on my hand. I forgot the pain, letting fresh blood drop by drop fall onto the floor. Suddenly, I realized that my blood wasn't bright red; its color was the same as loneliness.

I've forgotten the color of loneliness.

(Three)

I am in the same country as loneliness; this might be fate. In the darkness, I lit a candle, and the dim yellow flame gently flickered, which was the silent heartbeat. When the candle burned out, the darkness swallowed me, without resistance, without struggle.

I have long been accustomed to pitch blackness.

Walking alone on the deserted street late at night, my world is still only myself. Coldness and helplessness quietly spread; I march with pain.

(Four)

The boiling water kept steaming. I blankly looked at it, my thoughts gradually leaving my body.

What am I thinking about?

What can I do?

Continuously asking myself, there was no answer.

I have become accustomed to questioning myself.

Without thought, but with breath—clear breath—I could hear the sound of my own heartbeat, strong beat after beat. I'm still alive after all.

(Five)

Turning on the computer, I heard the crisp sounds of the mouse and keyboard in the quiet night. There was no one on QQ. Suddenly, someone requested to pass identity verification. In his self-introduction section, I saw a reasonable sentence: "Because of boredom, I go online, but once online, I feel even lonelier!"

Without hesitation, I clicked "Pass Verification" with the mouse, then logged off and turned off the computer. Lying in bed, my eyes staring at the ceiling, I kept thinking about that sentence.

It turns out, I've already gotten used to being bored.

(Six)

My life has no meaning, my living has no happiness, because of helplessness, due to ruthlessness. Without goals, I live in my unique way, living in my own world.

Walking on the road, ignoring those who point fingers and give cold looks, I still walk forward calmly and firmly, with an inexplicable smile on my face. Passengers in life, why should I not let go? Aren't you just my passenger? Thinking of you, I put away my smile, stopped my steps, and looked up at the sky—it wasn’t blue, it was the color of loneliness.

I can't force myself to stop thinking about you.

(Seven)

Curling up on the sofa, I kept rubbing my temples hard with my hands, habitually plagued by headaches. On the table was a glass of iced water and painkillers, but I didn't touch them. Closing my eyes, I felt the pressure brought by the pain.

I've gotten used to torturing myself.

Suddenly feeling warmth on my cold hands, it was originally scalding tears. I thought I had already run out of tears.

(Eight)

Angels have wings, I don't, so I'm not an angel.

Devils have magic, I don't, so I'm not a devil.

What I have is helplessness, despair, and the freedom of loneliness.

Hope and despair fiercely fought in my heart, but the winner was helplessness. I've learned to accept helplessness, to compromise with helplessness.

An angel's flaw is being too kind, a devil's flaw is being too evil, my flaw is being too weak.

(Nine)

Gently closing my eyes, I breathed greedily the air without you. Is it freedom? Or is it longing? I can't answer myself. It turns out the air without you is so thin.

I've also learned to adapt to the coldness of the air.

Laughed, cried, argued, made noise, now what I need is just indifference.

(Ten)

Bright sunshine streamed through the window, warmly shining on my body. Opening my eyes, I shielded the blinding light with my hand, covered myself with the quilt, and prepared to continue my interrupted beautiful dream.

Once a beautiful dream is awakened, it cannot be continued. Annoyed, I sat up from the bed, supporting my head with both hands, my hair messily hanging down. The dream ended. I bitterly smiled at myself, accepting reality with despair, welcoming another meaningless day.

I've become accustomed to an unchanging life.

(Eleven)

"The back is real, the person is fake, nothing to cling to, a hundred years ago you were not you, I was not me, sorrow is real, tears are fake, originally no cause and effect, a hundred years later there will be no you or me."

In a daze, I seemed to see you. Reaching out my hand, I only touched emptiness. I know, a hundred years later, you will still be you, just missing my longing.

(Twelve)

The wind messed up my hair, I didn't care, slowly walking on the street. Numbly moving, vaguely seeing you ahead of me, step by step forward, yet you were getting further and further away from me.

I desperately ran towards you until you disappeared before my eyes. Stopping and panting heavily, I began to laugh, laughing at my foolishness, laughing at my stupidity.

(Thirteen)

In the room, books filled the entire table. Sighing while sitting in front of the mountain of books, I fell silent for a moment, suddenly stood up, grabbed those boring old books, and threw them forcefully against the surrounding walls, then heavily fell onto the bed.

After a long time, I got up and picked up the books one by one, putting them back on the table, helplessly smiling, burying myself in the pile of books.

(Fourteen)

Lying on the table, I buried my face deep into my arms, and tears unexpectedly flowed out. I kept taking deep breaths, trying to calm the fluctuations in my heart, but I couldn't control myself.

(Fifteen)

I took off the false smile, showing a tired face. My attachment to you remains, but I've learned to hide it.

My life still continues as usual, the only ripple being the tears when I think of you.

Temptation, our distance is measured in light-years.
