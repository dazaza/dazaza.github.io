Yesterday, the Shanghai International Film Festival held its opening red carpet and ceremony in the rain. Many famous directors and actors from home and abroad, along with numerous film crews, walked the red carpet. Deng Chao, as the lead actor in two films, also appeared on the opening red carpet, promoting his new films with other main creators of the crew. Having just completed his wedding, Deng Chao and Sun Li made an appearance together, becoming one of the most notable celebrity couples of the evening. During his time at the Shanghai International Film Festival, Deng Chao will promote three films he has starred in.

Deng Chao's cold-blooded character造型 is a significant aspect of his role. Directed by Chen Jiashang, this fantasy epic features Deng Chao, Sun Li, Yan Ni, and Liu Yan among others. According to Chen Jiashang, the concept "one thought leads to paradise, another to hell" conveys the essence of the film, which tells the story of a group of people who mistakenly enter a fairyland and undergo transformation. Deng Chao plays the scholar Zhu Xiaolian, who mistakenly enters this fairyland and engages in both dramatic scenes and emotional relationships as he seeks to save himself and his lover.

Today (June 12th), there will be promotional activities for the film, unveiling conceptual posters and releasing a preview trailer. From pre-production to wrap-up, the film has maintained an aura of mystery, and this will be its first major promotional event in mainland China, generating much anticipation. Following his unique albino look in a previous film, Deng Chao’s new character in this movie will again be a highlight, showcasing extraordinary martial arts skills through extensive action sequences. In this film, Deng Chao plays the protagonist Cold Blood, originally created by Wen Ruan. Cold Blood is depicted as a man of few words and no attachments.

In earlier film previews, many media outlets have noted that actor Chen Kun, playing Chiang Ching-kuo, has unexpectedly become the biggest surprise of the entire film. For receiving such affirmation, Chen Kun humbly stated that he only hopes those who walk into the cinema to watch the movie will not be disappointed by the character he portrays.

As the premiere progressed, nine young actors including Huang Shengyi, Huang Xiaoming, Tong Dawei, Wang Baoqiang, Guo Xiaodong, Liu Ye, Shen Aojun, Che Yongli, and Gong Beibi took the stage, reviving some classic lines from their respective roles. Wang Baoqiang’s classic line brought a small climax to the premiere: "There is a landlord's courtyard ahead. The wall is too high; even bombs can't break it. Please send artillery support!"

A powerful lineup of actors including Liu Yiwei, Leon Lai, Chen Daoming, Sun Xing, Ping Jing, and Feng Yuanzheng also participated. When it was Liu Yiwei's turn to speak, he joked about being included in the 'powerful' lineup, saying, "I represent our group of middle-aged actors to greet everyone." When it was Leon Lai's turn, he humorously mentioned that he had been figuring out how to introduce his own character since getting on stage, only realizing his name when seeing the sign below his name tag. On the day, Chen Daoming gave perhaps the briefest introduction: "There's nothing more to say, they've already said everything for me."
