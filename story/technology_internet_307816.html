Leonardo Explains Why He Subbed out Seedorf: Giant Says Milan Only Lost Once in Two and a Half Months

An unbeaten record will always come to an end eventually. For Leonardo, who is coaching for the first time, AC Milan's recent run of 13 games unbeaten, with five consecutive Serie A victories, was already an impressive feat. However, it was somewhat embarrassing that their unbeaten streak ended with a 0-2 home defeat. In the post-match press conference, Leonardo maintained his composure and congratulated his opponents.

"What happened in this match? First of all, we must congratulate Palermo," Leonardo said. "They played very well today, both tactically and physically they were strong. Their style is somewhat similar to ours, but today they did better. It was a very open game, and the two goals at the start of the second half determined the result. I think the players did well; it's hard to keep up with such frequent matches continuously. We couldn't play at our usual pace today, but the team still kept running and didn't perform too badly. Today, Palermo simply played better."

There were questions about Alexandre Pato's lackluster performance after coming on as a substitute and Seedorf being substituted off, which Leonardo addressed one by one: "Pato? When he came on, we were already trailing. He has played more games than others, and it's difficult to maintain the same level in every game. Abate also performed well; it wasn't an easy match. As for taking off Seedorf: we needed more people in the box to support the two forwards. To give them more help, I had to take someone out of midfield. The substitution was for team balance, not because of his performance on the field."

Galliani also gave an interview after the match. He said, "Palermo played better than us. Clearly, participating in the Champions League brings difficulties that affect Sunday's Serie A matches. This can be seen from the fact that all four teams that played midweek games only managed to earn one point in this round of Serie A. Clearly, the situation AC Milan is encountering isn't isolated. Pato's missed shot in the final stages of the match was quite representative. Now we hope to perform well in the next away game against Fiorentina. There are six days of rest between now and then. Then we'll recharge for the New Year. Now, the gap in the standings is rapidly narrowing. It's unfortunate that we didn't win this match; otherwise, we would have been only two points behind the leaders. Our situation was quite good, but the teams behind us are catching up. But don't worry, we will continue looking forward and seriously complete the remaining matches until the end of the first half of the season, namely the upcoming three games against Fiorentina, Genoa, and Juventus. We already knew that Palermo would likely be an excellent team in top form when playing against us. After maintaining an unbeaten record for two and a half months, losing once isn't an unexpected thing. We had good chances but didn't score, otherwise, I think we could have drawn the game, but that's understandable."
