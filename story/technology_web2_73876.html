The Web 2.0 concept seems to have become a cliché these days, as there are indeed too many incompetent sites that also want to align themselves with the Web 2.0 concept. This has resulted in users becoming increasingly unclear about the concept, and eventually dismissing it. However, the true Web 2.0 concept still has clear boundaries and criteria: it emphasizes the interactivity between the site and its users, and this interactivity is interconnected across multiple functions and scopes. Such an overall interactive system can be called the Web 2.0 standard. Today, let us introduce to you 10 sites that we believe absolutely meet this standard, and these sites are indeed very practical and novel. Let's take a look!

1. Thesixtyone [Click here to visit the website]

It is essentially a music-sharing version of Digg. Since it is recommended by music lovers from all over the world with different tastes, the music on the site is all very enjoyable. For those who enjoy collecting different types of music, this site will definitely allow you to listen to all kinds of music in no time.
