Drinking alcohol to keep warm is a misconception.

by bbeij62s on 2009-12-07 16:08:11

    Medical Talent Network www.job900.com; To withstand the cold, many citizens of Nanjing drink more alcohol during gatherings, believing that drinking can keep them warm. However, experts warn that this is not actually the case.

    Yesterday afternoon, Mr. Li from Xiaolingwei was preparing to pick up his grandson from a Taekwondo class and take him home for dinner. At lunchtime, in order to warm himself, he deliberately drank a few extra small glasses of Erguotou liquor. However, after picking up his grandson and heading home, he started to feel unwell, with a runny nose. Upon visiting the nearby Nanjing Integrated Traditional Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital, he discovered that he had caught a wind-cold感冒 (a type of common cold according to TCM). What puzzled Mr. Li was why drinking more alcohol did not keep him warm but instead made him catch a chill?

    Professor Zhang Ming, a cardiovascular disease expert at the hospital, explained that generally speaking, drinking alcohol accelerates breathing, dilates blood vessels, speeds up blood circulation, and increases heat consumption, making people feel warm. Moreover, since alcohol is present in liquor, it can cause a brief excitement of the nervous system, giving the entire body a false sense of warmth and comfort.

    However, this is not a sign of alcohol keeping one warm but rather a precursor to the disruption of the body's temperature regulation center. Especially when too much alcohol is consumed, it can lead to dysfunction of the body temperature regulation center, causing excessive heat loss. At the same time, the stomach becomes anesthetized by the alcohol, significantly reducing its functionality, further weakening the body's ability to produce heat.

    Professor Zhang Ming particularly warned elderly friends that relying on alcohol to keep warm can be very harmful to their health. Elderly individuals are already less sensitive to changes in body temperature, and drinking alcohol can lead to disruptions in the body’s temperature regulation, making it even easier to damage the body’s temperature regulation function.
