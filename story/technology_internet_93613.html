Through this list, we can observe some very interesting phenomena:

1. Success has little correlation with academic qualifications, place of origin, or even media exposure, and even less with hype. Some highly visible post-80s individuals in the media are not on the list, the only difference being that they don't have enough cash on hand.

2. Starting from personal websites, transitioning to company operations, and finally being fully acquired or controlled by investments is a classic roadmap. It can accumulate wealth quickly but also means limited room for growth. Therefore, no one in the club currently has assets worth hundreds of millions, still showing a significant gap compared to internet tycoons from the 70s and 60s generations.

3. Through the varied experiences of these club members, we can see that most people succeeded by doing just one thing well. This is the only common point we can find among them: focus and pragmatism.

4. Innovation in technical fields is the mainstream in the internet sector, while non-technical aspects should be gradually maturing.

5. There are no returned overseas students in the club; almost every member succeeded through their own efforts. These two phenomena indicate that the wave of the Chinese Dream is approaching.

6. The last and most important point: remember that every successful person's achievement is not accidental.
