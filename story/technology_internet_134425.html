Chengdu ADSL broadband users get free speed upgrade, increased by 512K.

by anygreen on 2009-03-26 15:37:24

"M" Access: Keeping Pace with the World's Speed

What does M speed mean? It means faster webpage loading, faster information transmission, faster file downloads, and smoother online video viewing, among other things. In recent years, the number of broadband access users in our country has grown rapidly, making us the country with the most broadband users globally. Last May, the number of broadband users for Chengdu Telecom also surpassed one million, becoming the first city in western China to exceed one million broadband users and the fourth city nationwide where telecom broadband customers exceed one million. However, there is a gap between the rapid growth in user numbers and the improvement in internet speed. Currently, the broadband speeds in developed countries around the world are almost all at the M (megabit) level. This time, the large-scale free upgrade of Chengdu Telecom's broadband has enabled a broad range of broadband users to bid farewell to the "K" era and welcome the "M" era. As a result, Chengdu has become the city with the fastest average broadband speed in the western region and one of the cities with the fastest broadband internet speeds nationwide. This undoubtedly has milestone significance in Chengdu's informatization construction and development and in promoting an information-based lifestyle for all citizens. M speed is not only a new starting point for Chengdu's broadband but also a key point for Chengdu to align with the broadband speeds of today's developed countries, indicating that "Chengdu speed" will truly connect with the world's speed from now on. Starting from March 15th, all residential ADSL broadband users of China Telecom in Chengdu (including suburban counties) can log into the telecom online business hall or call the broadband expert service hotline to enjoy a free speed boost and say goodbye to 512K once and for all. http://www.bjadsl.cn/post/618/
