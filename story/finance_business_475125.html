Here is the travel guide shared by Hongyuan Holiday:

Because I read past travel notes, it brought me a lot of convenience in my trip, so I also contribute to give back.

Xi'an, the capital of 13 dynasties, is a place of talented people and auspicious land in Central Plains, gathering the culture and flavors of multiple ethnic groups and countries, worth slowly tasting and feeling.

Mid to late November, it's early winter in the north, but Xi'an is still filled with the strong autumn atmosphere. Deep green and golden yellow paint this ancient city with brilliant colors. Due to the off-season for tourism, there are very few people at various attractions, suitable for lazy people to spend their afternoon leisurely, absorbing the rich Central Plains culture. We highly recommend visiting Xi'an during this season.

Day 1: Arrive in Xi'an in the evening and check into a hotel. It's said that taxis in Xi'an often refuse passengers, but we easily got one and did not encounter any refusal in the following days.

Day 2: Shaanxi Museum, Big Wild Goose Pagoda. We didn't leave the hotel until around 10:30 am. We had Hunan snacks near the hotel and then took a bus to the museum. We arrived at about 10:30, but there was still a queue for tickets at the entrance. We wanted to see the special exhibition, so we bought a 20-yuan special exhibition ticket nearby. We stayed at the Shaanxi Museum for more than five hours. At first, we spent 100 yuan to hire a tour guide, but we felt rushed and the explanation wasn't detailed enough. If you want to listen to the guide without hiring them, it's almost impossible because the guides use earphones and visitors need to wear matching headphones to hear. The tour lasted an hour, after which we rented an electronic audio guide. The special exhibition is the Tang Dynasty treasures exhibition, which is a must-see. The grandeur of the Tang Dynasty and the high degree of integration of multiple ethnic groups are fully displayed in the items in this museum, making us even prouder of our nation. Ha ha. Suggestion: Read some related historical materials beforehand and have a communicative explanation with the guide, you will learn more. Highly recommend renting an electronic audio guide and preferably prepare a universal headphone that can be used by two people (the headphones provided by the audio guide are loop-shaped, only one person can wear it). We left around 4 pm and had dinner at an old Shaanxi restaurant next to the museum, it was just okay. Then we walked to the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. The Big Wild Goose Pagoda is the largest fountain square in Asia, and the entire scenic area is a huge landscape centered on the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. Whether it's the fountain pool or the surrounding commercial areas, they are all very unique. There are several old-established snack shops on the west side of the scenic area, such as Lao Sunjia.

Day 3: Terracotta Warriors, revisit the Big Wild Goose Pagoda. Again, we didn't leave until around 10 am. We took a bus to the train station. When we got off the bus, many people were trying to solicit customers for trips to the Terracotta Warriors, remember not to stop, go directly to the train station. On the east side of the train station, there are regular buses. Take Bus No.5, or others. Bus No.5 is a clean enclosed coach. It has just been raised from 7 yuan per person to 8 yuan, with a bus every 10 minutes, very convenient. The journey is on expressway and there are no stops along the way, with movies available to watch. After about an hour, we arrived, crossed the road, and entered the Terracotta Warriors commercial street. Since it was the off-season, the commercial street was very quiet, even the bosses looked listless, and no one approached us to solicit business. The toilets on the commercial street cost 1 yuan per person, haha, might as well enter the park first. The ticket price is 110 yuan including the Qin Emperor's Mausoleum, they say the two places have merged, forced consumption! We spent 5 hours at the Terracotta Warriors. We were amazed by the grandeur and震撼of the vast Terracotta Warrior army; we were amazed by the ingenious mechanization technology of the Qin Dynasty. The movie in the circular cinema seemed to be filmed in the 80s, very crude, not very interesting. Lazy people didn't go to the Qin Emperor's Mausoleum. We lingered for a long time on the commercial street, taking photos of the street scenery is also nice. We bought small persimmons, 2 for 1 yuan, pomegranates 2.5 yuan each. Suggestion: Rent an electronic audio guide, the external playback volume is relatively loud, two people can share one. In the evening, we went to the Big Wild Goose Pagoda again to see the night view and took many photos.

Day 4: Stele Forest Museum, Ming City Wall, Bell Tower, Drum Tower, Snack Street. The Stele Forest Museum is very quiet, similar to the Confucius Temple Stele Forest near Beijing's Yonghe Palace, suitable for people interested in calligraphy to wander through. Admission fee is 40 yuan per person, expensive ah. Suggest using a student ID. After leaving the Stele Forest Museum and heading west along the city wall, we reached the South Gate, where we purchased tickets to ascend the wall. Admission fee is 40 yuan per person, again lamenting how expensive it is. The total distance is nearly 14 kilometers, bike rental costs 20 yuan for every 100 minutes, with extra charges for overtime. The road surface of the city wall is all stone bricks, not very flat, so prepare for a bumpy ride. We returned after riding for about 150 minutes. A buddy said he rode until his eyes turned black, probably due to fatigue and exhaustion. In the afternoon, there was folk Qin opera singing at the base of the city wall, powerful and distant. Around 7 pm, the night view from the city wall was beautiful. Suggestion: If you are two people, don't suggest renting a tandem bike, better rent single bikes and plan for 150 minutes to leisurely tour. The Bell Tower is located right in the middle of the central axis, the Drum Tower is not far to the west of the Bell Tower, and the Snack Street is behind the Drum Tower. There are many regional specialty snacks, recommend trying street snacks like rice cakes and pomegranate juice. Suggest going shopping when the lanterns light up, it's more flavorful. Since our flight was at 8 am the next morning, worried about traffic jams, we took a car to stay at a nearby budget hotel near the airport. Hotels near the airport are more expensive than those in the downtown area of the same specifications. The airport shuttle bus from the Drum Tower is the latest line, the last bus departs at 8 pm.

Day 5: Return journey.

Summary: A song that fits Xi'an well is "City Moonlight". The feeling of climbing the city wall is obvious, inside the city are blue bricks and black tiles (Beijing is red bricks and green tiles), outside the city are towering buildings, completely different scenery.

Road conditions and transportation: As the key area of Central Plains, the traffic situation is really not commendable, comparable to Beijing. But buses have dedicated lanes and many routes, very convenient. Surprisingly, I took a 50-cent bus again after many years, Bus No.6 is one of them. There are many motorized tricycles on the streets, very simple, suitable for riding during peak hours, weaving through the city streets and alleys, but the price should refer to regular taxis.

Eat: Shaanxi flavor incorporates the characteristics of Sichuan and Henan, salty, oily, spicy, which is indeed a challenge for people with poor gastrointestinal health. Suggest, must eat in moderation. Prices are not cheap, not quite matching the development status of the western region as imagined, perhaps the local income level is also extraordinary. For example, Fanjia Roujiamo costs 5-10 yuan, about the same price as the one outside Dongzhimen in Beijing, might taste better. One of the eight strange things in Shaanxi legend says bowls and basins are indistinguishable, currently really haven't noticed, probably already assimilated by the tableware in Shanghai-style catering.

Stay: Suggest staying on the central axis, it will be more convenient to go out.

Wear: The temperature difference between morning and evening is large, and many attractions are outdoor, with heavy yin energy, suggest wearing short down jackets.

Looking forward to being romantic in Xi'an again next year.

Related scenic spots: Big Wild Goose Pagoda, Terracotta Warriors, Stele Forest, Stele Forest Museum
