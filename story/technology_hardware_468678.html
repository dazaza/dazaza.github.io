Casual Talk on Modern Street Culture and Street-themed Games (Part Seven) - Crime Edition

by diawudao on 2011-08-01 12:06:59

When it comes to the streets, people often associate them with some negative words - violence, crime, drugs... Crime occurs in any country and any place, and the streets are often the best places for committing crimes. Some of the birthplaces of street culture are located in the black areas at the bottom of society or lawless zones. ROCKSTAR's "The Warriors" vividly portrays the lives of these street thugs. The way of dressing in the HIPHOP style also hides some signals for illegal activities.

Following the principle that heroes emerge in chaotic times, major game developers are endlessly fascinated by the theme of street violence. Moreover, fighting and gun battles themselves are important elements that constitute a game, which leads to the popularity of street violence games.

The "Def Jam" series is a work that blends Hip-Hop music and fighting games. So far, it has released "Def Jam Vendetta", "Def Jam: Fight for NY", and "Def Jam: Icon". Starting from the second installment, the game moves the battle outside the ring, with old warehouses, junkyards, and subway stations becoming battlegrounds. Various kinds of weapons can be used, fully portraying the brutal street brawls. The purpose and gameplay of the game are equally simple: to gain money and status.

Another action game, "Red Faction: Guerrilla", also realistically reflects HIPHOP culture and street violence. This game cleverly combines multiple elements such as street gangs, street graffiti, HipHop music, street gunfights, street brawls, hand-to-hand combat, drugs, underground competitions, high-altitude competitions, secret infiltration,偷拍, climbing, urban leaping, stunts, etc. The protagonist is Trane, a famous graffiti artist who frequently roams every corner of the city. Trane soon gets involved in a gang war and quickly becomes a hero of the city. The work meticulously depicts HIPHOP street culture, whether it's through the music or the way characters speak and dress. "Red Faction: Guerrilla" won the Best Game Music Award across all platforms for the 2006-2007 year and the Best Game Art Award across all platforms for the 2006-2007 year.

When talking about HIPHOP culture and street crime, one cannot help but mention the monster-level game "Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas". Although it's an old game, it is enough to become the most representative street violent crime game. Each installment of the "Grand Theft Auto" series gives a different feeling. "Grand Theft Auto III" showcases an industrial city without sunlight - Liberty City; "Grand Theft Auto: Vice City" is synonymous with sunshine, beaches, and bikinis; while "Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas" deeply describes the street crime life of American blacks. Especially in the early stages of the game, the protagonist lives in a very typical black slum community, listens to HIPHOP music, plays street graffiti, and engages in smashing and looting. The development of the story revolves around gang conflicts and family grudges. High freedom makes this game popular among players. Instead of stealing cars and killing people by the roadside when bored, one might as well carefully savor the authentic street hip-hop culture that the game brings us.
