Webmaster forum operation experience to share with everyone. In fact, what Jack Ma said about the winter does not include us individual webmasters. Our winter has already started long ago, and we have never reached spring. In the past, we could simply do movie alliances and rely on Baidu search to attract a lot of people. Baidu was indeed naive back then, quickly indexing content and easily being manipulated by some webmasters. However, all SP channels for movie alliances have been shut down since 2006. I truly miss the days when Baidu brought in tens of thousands of IPs daily. Such days are not easy to return. Baidu now focuses on website quality and increases manual intervention. There's nothing anyone can do about this. As for those who say that Baidu experienced technical issues, that might be another reason. Baidu's repeated adjustments have caused even current websites to face difficulties.

Let’s talk about forum operations today. Everyone knows that after the state required forums to obtain specific ***, many were closed down, and some people began to retreat. However, I want to tell everyone that if you can persist, then continue doing so.

Here, I will share some forum experiences. I have operated a forum for over a year, with a PV of 150,000, which is considered a small success for my personal website.

1. Forum selection: There are numerous forum systems, from early ones like bbsxp, Discuz!, ChaChaBird.net forum, LeiAo CGI, to the currently popular PHP-based Discuz! forums. Speaking of this, I must thank Kayue Interconnect (http://www.cnidc365.com/) for their customer service representative's patience. Back when I didn't even know how to install a forum system, they recommended me their forum package, including domain + hosting + forum installation. They helped me set up a PHP-based Discuz! forum system and gave me technical guidance. Through my own gradual exploration, I have achieved some success today! In summary, I personally recommend Discuz! because of its high load-bearing capacity. Secondly, I would choose PhpWind. There are specialized articles online comparing Discuz! and PhpWind. You can take a look. Generally speaking, Discuz! doesn't perform as well in terms of search engine weight compared to PhpWind, but it tends to have more indexed pages (not absolutely).

2. Why create a forum: Forums are known to not be as good as webpage sites in collecting advertising fees or making money. However, I believe that forums are active, which is why I like them. They gather a community of people, making it easier to retain users and have strong interactivity. This isn't absolute though; not every site should be turned into a forum. This article only provides forum operation experience, and those who don't like forums don't need to read further.

3. What kind of forum to make: I suggest you choose based on your interests and resources. Forums in any industry can succeed. For example, Tianya, LuoWu, and XieKe Webmaster Forum are quite successful.

4. What kind of hosting is needed for a forum: If your forum attachments are small or few, the required hosting space will be less. The forum program itself is usually only a few MBs, but the database will gradually increase. This requires specific analysis for specific situations.

5. How many "diligent moderators" are needed: It's best not to rely on one person when running a forum. Recruit moderators as soon as possible, and initially, you can offer some Qb rewards. Moderators need to work hard at first. At least, the content updates need to keep up. I do not recommend scraping content for forums (personal opinion). Attachments should be localized as much as possible because some resources may expire. Besides updating content, moderators should also establish section rules, register some minor accounts, reply to posts to create an atmosphere where it seems there are many replies.

6. New forums need to do some "tricks": Because new forums tend to be quiet, few people like to stay. Thus, your promotion and retention efforts won't yield many results. It's suggested to appropriately do some "tricks." Nowadays, various forums have virtual user systems, and you can also register a batch of members yourself. Don't do too many, and the number of users should be proportional to the content and reply volume. For instance, you can start with 20 online members. And detailed online lists must be displayed (of course, once the forum grows large, this isn't necessary).

7. Forums need reward measures: Apart from moderators, you need to retain members. Content needs to attract these members. To create an atmosphere, new forums can use hidden posts that require replies to view. However, images and attachments must be browsable by guests. Clear reward systems are essential. For active members, enhance permissions, promote positions, issue forum coin rewards, Qb, physical rewards, etc., which are all good methods.

8. You need to establish a "consumption system": Forum virtual coins are the motivation for users to post and also a symbol of status. Many members like to be the "rich people" of the forum. For example, downloading a post attachment requires deducting a certain amount of money, and posting content adds a certain amount of money. This needs to be adjusted according to your own forum.
