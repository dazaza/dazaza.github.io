Green. Old Ma

When writing this article, I listened repeatedly to the interlude of Old Ma's final salute. I think it is the only music that matches this plot, with its ups and downs, twists and turns. Except for Xu Sanduo who was chuckling foolishly, almost everyone had tears in their eyes, but no one let those tears fall. I like such farewells, I like men's farewells like this, standing tall in a line, resembling poplars under the sun, the collar flowers and caps reflecting the sun's glow. Realistic, yet not sentimental. Among all these comings and goings, only Old Ma left in civilian clothes, so this color, which best represents soldiers, is given to him. Old Ma's appearance was quite humorous, and it would be unimaginable for anyone to think that this soldier wearing an apron and looking messy was once the most exemplary squad leader of Red Third Company. Watching him argue indifferently with the political instructor, then carefully handing over the apron to the instructor, I felt some relief for Xu Sanduo. Grassland Squad Five might not be the best, but it was indeed the most suitable for Xu Sanduo, because there would be plenty of time here for the rather slow-heating Xu Sanduo, who was over-cultivated but failed in the new recruit company, to truly start over again from scratch in his heart. The drama did not explain why Old Ma came to Squad Five, but the contradictions left on Old Ma were evident. In the vast grasslands, it seemed as if only by sinking deeply into the long time could one find a glimmer of hope for the current way of life. Xu Sanduo's arrival stopped Old Ma's descent, but pushed him to the brink of choosing between unity or self-interest. Old Ma should have genuinely recognized Xu Sanduo from the bottom of his heart, though they were in the same squad but lived in different worlds. After that fruitless squad meeting, Old Ma began to truly have thoughts of turning back, so it is unclear who saved whom, and unclear who changed whom; therefore, Old Ma had a particular fondness for the last soldier he trained. Xu Sanduo, who was diligently repairing the road, was mistakenly transferred to Steel Seventh Company, many people said this opportunity should have been Old Ma's. Indeed, emotionally speaking, we are more likely to accept Old Ma's transfer, after all, this was his last chance, even though this opportunity would only extend his time in the army by one or two years, but it was already the last comfort for this veteran. The decision to retire was within my expectations. One can imagine, the excellent Old Ma mentioned by the political instructor should not be inferior to Shi Jin or Wu Liu Yi. Would such an excellent soldier still remain in the overlap of past and present after Xu Sanduo pulled up all the dead grass in his heart? He took out the last bit of sharpness that hadn't been eroded by loneliness, drawing a perfect period to his military career. The soldiers he trained all came, none cried. But he himself, in the moment of turning around, wiped away the unseen tears with the corner of his clothes. The iron campsite and flowing soldiers, Old Ma may be the truest embodiment of this phrase. What he brought to Xu Sanduo was merely simple recognition, but in Xu Sanduo's heart, it blossomed the first flower, solidifying the last foundation for Xu Sanduo's growth. And himself, under the gradually lengthening sunset, bid farewell to his former comrades without a word, full of reluctance and expectation, all in the slightly trembling lips, that upright turn, in that last familiar yet solemn military salute, vividly interpreted. If time is a painting, I wish you could become this vibrant green, guarding the dreams that endure forever. Old Ma, take care.

Red. Gao Cheng

Gao Cheng's first appearance gave me the impression that he should be red, because of his bloodiness and arrogance, like the Dan Dan flowers blooming across the mountains, dazzling and unrestrained. When receiving the new recruits, Gao Cheng waved his arms and shouted loudly: "Tank battalion don't bother me!" I was a little puzzled, how could this battalion commander be so disrespectful? The more I watched, the more I understood, if not so, it wouldn't be the Gao Cheng who jumps high when receiving new equipment. Actually, Xu Sanduo has always lived according to others' standards. Before enlistment, he was his father's 'tortoise son'; after enlistment, he was Gao Cheng's 'surrendering soldier'. A friend told me he didn't like such a Gao Cheng, feeling somewhat arrogant. But I think this is the most real Gao Cheng. The Seventh Company has already merged into his blood. His requirements for the soldiers of the Seventh Company apply equally to these new recruits. Sometimes I feel Gao Cheng is like a child, love and hate are all over his face. The outstanding Shi Jin and Wu Liu Yi can even play and roughhouse with him without restraint. However, Xu Sanduo, who later became the thorn in the side of the Seventh Company, couldn't even get a contemptuous look from him. Excellence, for us as people, is a somewhat difficult goal, but for Gao Cheng, this is his only standard. From this sense, the source of his character may come from his prestigious family background as a tiger son of a general, but more importantly, it comes from his superiority in being in the Seventh Company. Here, superiority is a positive term, it is the fighting spirit and audacity that a soldier, even a man, should possess. Superior men are often concerned about face, naturally Gao Cheng is no exception. Accepting Xu Sanduo, yet never recognizing him, one could say never verbally recognizing him, but what about inside? When Bai Tie Jun and Gan Xiao Ning helped Xu Sanduo practice reaction time, his mouth clearly raised a smile, for whom was he smiling? When Xu Sanduo did 333 abdominal circles, he stared intently from the window, so much so that his cigarette ash fell unnoticed. Was it regrettable that he gave the CD to Shi Jin? That seemingly impatient gaze, how much pride and praise was hidden inside? Excellent men are often emotional too, Gao Cheng openly expressed his affection for his two favorite soldiers - Shi Jin and Wu Liu Yi, perhaps this was his source of pride, definitely also the source of his repeated rejections of Xu Sanduo. He said he didn't want to waste the best squad leader on a soldier who couldn't stand up, but facing Shi Jin's determined persistence, what could he do except wait for that day to come? Regarding Shi Jin's departure, the drama did not specify how much effort Gao Cheng made, but I believe what he did would not be less than what he did to retain Wu Liu Yi. But when we later learned that the exercise that ended Shi Jin's military career also became the watershed for the reorganization of the Seventh Company, everything he could do, even everything his father as a major general could do, was just like trying to stop a car with a stick. No one could retain his beloved third squad leader from the past. So, at the end of the exercise, Gao Cheng asked Shi Jin drunkenly: "What will you do today, henceforth..." It was the first time I saw his sadness, the first time I heard him call "Jin'er". When Xu Sanduo stood behind Shi Jin with a naive smile, the intertwining love and hatred restored his previous indifference and coldness. Similarly when Wu Liu Yi left, the deeper the love for Wu Liu Yi, the deeper the hatred. That slap, was it on Wu Liu Yi's face or on his own heart? Holding Shi Jin, who was crying uncontrollably, in his arms and hugging the stubborn Wu Liu Yi while crying together, were those white rabbits at the time as bitter as the hot tears rolling down now? Unable to retain Shi Jin, unable to retain the Seventh Company, they left, all gone. At Ma Xiaoshuai's entry ceremony, only Gao Cheng saluted solemnly towards the company flag. How much love? How much pain? Everyone knows, those who left, those who stayed, all know. In the empty barracks, only he and his hell remained head to tail, "I've cried, two hours ago." A very calm sentence, yet it made me think of all the broken glass pieces on the ground, and that majestic and tragic Soviet military song. What is bloodiness? Burying tears in the heart, melting them into the blood, and then seeping deeply into the bones. I think Gao Cheng must feel guilty towards every soldier of the Seventh Company. It was clearly not his responsibility, but the Seventh Company was disintegrated and scattered under his hands, he could only blame himself, intentionally or unintentionally. He forgave Cheng Cai, untied Xu Sanduo's mental knot, and I believe he excelled in both temperament and performance at the headquarters like he used to in the 702 regiment, but that fluttering company flag has already merged into his blood, becoming one with his life. If time is a painting, I wish you could transform into the surging dawn, guarding that lush green. Written beforehand, Shi Jin is the person hardest for me to write about. Although his appearance lasted less than half the series, the shock and emotion he brought me cannot be described in words. This kind of shock is not the spectacular visual effect, but a smile, an action, a silhouette, even a tear. I try to describe my thoughts as much as possible, any inadequacies please bear with me. Blue. Shi Jin Have you seen the sky above the Potala Palace? A clean sky without a speck of dust, that color belongs to Shi Jin. Everyone says Shi Jin is the mother of the army, I think that should be a deeper kind of emotion. Except for Shi Jin, probably no one would use "not bad" to describe the previous Xu Sanduo, would anyone judge someone based on a short conversation? Shi Jin could, but he couldn't bring such a "not bad" Xu Sanduo into the army. The Seventh Company is a high-speed machine, Xu Sanduo might be thrown out before he could catch up with its speed. During the home visit, I noticed some subtle changes in Shi Jin's eyes, was it recalling his own past? Annie said, some people are close the moment they appear, like recognizing something lost again, the memory remains clear in the brain, without error. That closeness has a warm and genuine texture, where one can embrace upon first meeting. For Shi Jin to Xu Sanduo, and Xu Sanduo to Shi Jin, it is mostly this kind of feeling. In the train carriage, Shi Jin loudly ordered Cheng Cai to sit down, but calmly embraced Xu Sanduo whose emotions were almost out of control. Such an action might make many people see maternal warmth and deep affection in Shi Jin. After the new recruit training ended, Shi Jin ultimately couldn't keep Xu Sanduo in the Seventh Company, this wasn't his fault, but he always felt guilty, guilty for betraying Xu Sanduo's expectations, and deviating from his own promise. Yes, that wasn't just a promise, but a hurdle in Shi Jin's heart. The first time, he couldn't overcome it, he looked at Xu Sanduo with a guilty expression, saying things against his heart about the importance of being in Squad Five, then took a swig of beer from Xu Sanduo's hand, and turned away without looking back. Xu Sanduo once again stood in front of the door of the Seventh Company, this time, Shi Jin crossed that hurdle, he wanted to keep Xu Sanduo, even willing to exchange his own military uniform for it, he was determined to do so. Not abandoning, not giving up, if Gao Cheng and Wu Liu Yi's words taught Xu Sanduo this phrase, then Shi Jin's actions made Xu Sanduo fully understand this phrase. He used his profound and vast bosom to accept Xu Sanduo, from then on, the two were tightly bound together. I watched the scene of Xu Sanduo wielding the hammer many times. The usually gentle and humble Shi Jin, with a smiling face, exploded in anger towards Xu Sanduo who was already scared out of his wits: "Do you want to drag me to death, Xu Sanduo!" Rather than saying it was anger at his inability to steel Xu Sanduo, it was more like a release, for his numerous arguments with the company commander, for the brothers outside patiently persuading him, for his long-standing unspoken persistence, and more so for this "turtle son" he brought out single-mindedly but realized belatedly or even unconsciously. Dignity and bloodiness awakened in Xu Sanduo's body, in the resounding collision sounds, Shi Jin smiled, with tears in his eyes. "You are the last soldier Old Ma trained." Saying this, Xu Sanduo was still laughing carefree, Shi Jin lay quietly on the upper bunk, in the dark I couldn't see his expression, was he thinking of saying "you are also the last soldier I trained"? Or was he thinking, when he leaves, will the person below burst into tears instantly? Shi Jin smiled in the rain as he sent off Xu Sanduo, still upright posture, standard military salute, suddenly a graceful turn, it was like a different person. When hit during the exercise, Shi Jin knew his departure was just a matter of time. For Xu Sanduo, leaving him with a perpetually smiling face and a perpetually graceful silhouette was the best farewell. Shi Jin didn't expect the moment of parting to be so coincidentally wrong. Xu Sanduo returned, and chased to the garage. That classmate filled with tenderness in his bones, that classmate he thought he could retain with all his efforts, removed his collar insignia and shoulder badges, in a month's time, became the most familiar stranger right before his eyes. Lying in front of Xu Sanduo, every word he said made me shed tears, what he couldn't put down most was this child. Yes, child, at this moment, Xu Sanduo became his child, this child grew up, but he had to leave him, does it hurt? How could it not hurt? This was a hurdle in his heart, now forcibly flattened, just like the last blade of grass in Xu Sanduo's heart, pulled out not with mud but with blood! We can see Xu Sanduo's sorrow, but we can only empathize with Shi Jin's pain, in the two streams of tears he shed, in the instant he pushed Xu Sanduo's hands away. Shi Jin is not flawless, just like occasionally clouds cover the clear sky, and sometimes the vast night sky becomes incomprehensibly gloomy, but at least we would calmly wait, waiting for the sun to rise again, waiting for a promise of seeing the moon after the clouds dissipate. Most importantly, he is not far from us, as that article said, we all understand love and growth while being hurt, and comprehend the meaning of being human when accepting help in dire straits. Such a person is around you, around all of us. If time is a painting, I wish you could transform into a deep blue sky, embracing the earth, accompanied by the clouds. Brown. Wu Liu Yi Distinctive features, sharp eyes, simple and silent Wu Liu Yi resembles the continuous land and endless mountains of the banyan tree. Wu Liu Yi's appearance gave me a false impression, crouching beside Shi Jin, like a tame animal seeking its master's favor. "Your deputy squad leader, me, promoted to squad leader." Not just "me", but "your deputy squad leader", the dependence on Shi Jin, or more precisely Shi Jin's weight in his heart, is imaginable. And this dependence made him particularly repel Xu Sanduo, repel the Xu Sanduo Shi Jin favored especially, whether in the new recruit company or in Squad Three. Wu Liu Yi and Xu Sanduo were in the same squad, but their hearts belonged to two different worlds, but these two worlds shared a common intersection: Shi Jin. For these two people, Shi Jin was the most important person, the most trustworthy and reliable person. Xu Sanduo tied himself firmly behind Shi Jin with his smile, while Wu Liu Yi, "If the squad leader goes, I won't have any friends left." Wu Liu Yi didn't talk much, but his feelings were very heavy. When Shi Jin scolded Xu Sanduo loudly in the garage and pointed outside, there stood Wu Liu Yi who had been waiting for a long time, "My best brother, the soldier I trained, I broke with him too!" "Brother", I like this word, compared to "friend" or "confidant", it contains more gallantry and bloodiness. Before this scene, Wu Liu Yi persuaded Shi Jin to give up on Xu Sanduo, although he was casually pulling off his gloves, his eyes were already moved, "Don't let yourself go." He understood Shi Jin was gambling, but the stakes were too heavy, heavy enough for him to only watch helplessly as one end of the scale gradually tilted, powerless and without choice. When Shi Jin retired, Wu Liu Yi held his breath and called out the first "good!", but Xu Sanduo's "bad!" disrupted all his parting emotions. Everyone in the dormitory was stirred up by Xu Sanduo, except Wu Liu Yi. He stood by the window, straight and motionless, what was he looking at? Was he looking at the stone bench where he sat and talked with the squad leader countless times? Was he looking at the training field where he walked shoulder to shoulder with the squad leader countless times? Tears flowed, eventually tears still flowed. At this moment, Wu Liu Yi wished the one the squad leader hugged was himself, wished the one the squad leader faced tearfully was himself, wished the one the squad leader touched last was himself. Don't look, don't look, don't want to look, dare not look. Afterwards, he quietly played with the last pack of cigarettes given by the squad leader. From the south to the north, it was just a step away, this sentence Shi Jin told Xu Sanduo, perhaps at some point in the past, he also said it to Wu Liu Yi who followed him closely all the time. In the Seventh Company, Wu Liu Yi was one of the most thoroughly conquered by Xu Sanduo. Compared to Gao Cheng, his initial dislike for Xu Sanduo wasn't limited to Xu Sanduo's consistently poor grades, more importantly, "You're more like a lump of incapable mud!" He finally admitted this to Xu Sanduo, along with his thoughts of Shi Jin, his reddened eyes soaked in cool water, hiding the tears but unable to hide the desire to cry. Unexpectedly, it was this Xu Sanduo he despised the most who made him willingly sacrifice his dream to uphold the last piece of sky for the Seventh Company. He wouldn't preach big principles, nor tell Xu Sanduo what was meaningful, he just walked silently, using his strong arms to carry mountains. During the old A selection, Xu Sanduo insisted on bringing the injured Wu Liu Yi to sprint together to secure the last spot. Wu Liu Yi knew Xu Sanduo's mind was as pure as his eyes, free of any impurity. He also knew that even if he lost this opportunity, Xu Sanduo would drag him to the finish line. But he was Wu Liu Yi, he acknowledged this friend, thus destined to exchange his dream for it. The moment he bit open the rescue bullet, Xu Sanduo was stunned, Wu Liu Yi smiled, it was a smile that moved Yuan Lang too. His dream, in the instant it was within reach, vanished into thin air, never to be reborn. "Xu Sanduo, no one will pamper you anymore in the future." Two gazes met in the ward, Wu Liu Yi left Xu Sanduo with these last words. He lay sideways on the bed, crying like a child, finally able to vent freely, but in the loneliest and most helpless time. Did Gao Cheng's slap hurt? How could it not hurt? He couldn't stay, he was a mountain, even if he fell, he would fall like a mountain. Stubbornly refusing Gao Cheng's embrace, he turned his face away repeatedly, his flushed eye sockets filled with tears, finally collapsing onto Gao Cheng's shiny officer shoulder badge, crying, all crying. Wu Liu Yi was a man with broad shoulders, he knew how painful it was to be stripped of all support bit by bit, but he didn't talk much, just ran ten thousand meters with Xu Sanduo circle after circle. His feelings for Xu Sanduo were extremely complex, so each of his appearances was a stubborn, haughty stance. Seeing the note he gave to Xu Sanduo, "If you can't hold on, write to the squad leader," what Xu Sanduo needed most, he couldn't provide, so he handed it over to the brother he once depended on so much. Such a person was a mountain, broad-minded and towering, using his echoing presence to let Xu Sanduo, Gao Cheng, even Yuan Lang know what it meant to not abandon and not give up. If time is a painting, I wish you could transform into the silent earth and continuous mountains beneath your feet, neighboring the sea, touching the sky. Gray. Cheng Cai What is gray? Confusion? Or maturity? Both, yet neither.
