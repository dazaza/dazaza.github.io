The four "signal lights" of human health

by lyzxyjhyy on 2009-11-29 14:19:20

According to the experts, there are four "warning lights" for human health. They are:

1. Lips: The signal light of internal organs. Pale lips indicate anemia in men, purple indicates lung disease, black indicates liver disease, and red indicates fever. For example, if your lips are slightly red and rough, and your body feels sore and numb, you should drink more honey and eat grated radish, and the heat will subside soon. If your lips are pale due to anemia, you should eat pig liver for a while.

2. Yongquan point (位于脚底): Light a cigarette and bring it close to the Yongquan point at half a centimeter away. If you are a healthy person, you will feel the heat between 10 to 30 seconds. If the time to feel the heat is too long, or if the feeling on the left and right sides are different, it may be that your sympathetic nerve has lost balance, or your internal organs are fatigued or have some disease.

3. Urination: Wanting to go to the bathroom after drinking beer is proof of a healthy kidney. After drinking beer, people in their 20s go to the bathroom after 15 minutes, those in their 30s after 20 minutes, and those in their 40s within 30 minutes, which indicates a healthy body. In general, the stronger the kidney function, the earlier the time to go to the bathroom. Weak urine flow is proof of weak kidneys.

4. Palms: If you encounter any of the following hand abnormalities, you should go to the hospital to find out the cause.

① Abnormal sensation: Waking up in the morning with swollen hands, difficulty in flexing and extending, and gradually improving or disappearing not long after getting up. This phenomenon suggests that you may have heart, kidney, or liver problems or suffer from malnutrition edema.

② Numbness: If you often feel numbness, and you are middle-aged or elderly, or if you usually have high blood pressure, it may be a precursor to a "stroke."

③ Tremors, Vaginitis: If you are addicted to alcohol, consider whether you have chronic alcohol poisoning; if you do not drink alcohol, consider hyperthyroidism; excessive medication, neurosis, and irritability can also lead to hand tremors.
