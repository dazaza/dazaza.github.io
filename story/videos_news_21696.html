A few years ago, in Feng Xiaogang's movie "Cell Phone," Yan Shouyi was astonished after Niu Caiyun precisely determined his location using a mobile phone. Recently, Mr. Li from Yiwu encountered a similar issue. Last weekend, Mr. Li was invited by a friend to visit Hangzhou for a few days. Since Mr. Li's wife had never liked this friend, to avoid trouble, Mr. Li told his wife he would be on a business trip to Shanghai for a few days. Three days later, when Mr. Li returned home happily, he was immediately scolded by his wife. Confused, Mr. Li later learned that it was a mobile positioning SMS inquiry service that "betrayed" him.

A Cheap "Peeping" ServiceIn fact, Mr. Li’s phone was activated with a service called "Find You Find Me," an SMS service developed by a SP operator (mobile positioning network) and available for mobile users to choose and activate. The owners can query each other's locations.According to our understanding, the threshold to join this service is very low. As per the operator, verbal authorization is sufficient to activate the "Find You Find Me" service. Moreover, the cost is not expensive, only six yuan per month.Specifically, it can be done by sending a text message via a mobile phone. In other words, if someone has access to another person's phone for just a few minutes, they could possibly activate the "Find You Find Me" service without the other person knowing. This is because it does not require the owner to show ID, nor does it require the owner to provide corresponding written application materials or personal signature verification procedures.Attorney Zhu Yanqing believes that, at first glance, the mobile positioning function was not introduced to satisfy some people's "peeping desires." However, the problem is that the threshold for joining this service is too low, without a corresponding personal authorization certification system. People who feel "nowhere to hide" are worried about their right to privacy being violated.Citizen Miss Chen said: "If your mobile phone is activated with a mobile positioning service by someone else, that would be terrifying. No matter where you go, others can know, and you will definitely feel uncomfortable. Why should my movements be constantly monitored by others? I guess I need to keep my phone with me at all times and not let others have any chance."The operator stated that some phones will receive a request for authorization when someone queries their location. Moreover, if users are concerned about being monitored, they can always turn off the mobile positioning function.Some citizens also raised concerns: "If the mobile positioning inquiry request comes from your boss or spouse, would you dare to refuse authorization? Besides, what if one day they really have an urgent matter and you happen to have turned off this function at that moment? The consequences would be unimaginable." Misunderstood Mobile Positioning FunctionMobile positioning is also known as mobile location service. It acquires the user's location information through the mobile communication operator's network and provides corresponding services to users with the support of an electronic map platform, which is a value-added service.This function first appeared in European and American countries, allowing users to more conveniently conduct work through location inquiries. For example, when you are in a different place and want to find nearby restaurants, hotels, banks, or hospitals, you can reach your destination most conveniently through the mobile positioning function.Besides self-positioning, the mobile positioning function is also very suitable for the elderly and children. After setting up the phone, it can become an emergency alarm tool: on one hand, the elderly and children can actively send out emergency information to notify family members using the mobile positioning function; on the other hand, if the elderly and children stay out too long, family members can also actively inquire about their locations to understand whether any accidents have occurred.In addition, mobile positioning helps the logistics industry. Currently, some logistics companies in Yiwu have adopted mobile positioning services. Managers can monitor the positions of freight vehicles on the network at any time. After customers place orders, the company effectively arranges logistics delivery based on the distance between delivery vehicles and customer locations, reducing vehicle empty running rates, and saving operating costs.We should say that mobile positioning services, to a certain extent, benefit people's work and life. In comparison, the "Find You Find Me" service, which takes pleasure in finding people, deviates from the original intention of mobile positioning.Expectations for Operators to Improve ServicesAttorney Zhu Yanqing said that the existing national laws have not yet clearly defined the scope of privacy rights, but from a legal perspective, privacy rights are innate, just like the right to one's name or likeness. As long as it falls within the private life range of the rights holder and the individual does not wish to disclose it publicly, it should be considered private information, including the location of the rights holder.Aside from business needs or voluntary authorization, using the mobile positioning function to arbitrarily query another person's location already infringes upon their privacy. The mobile positioning function is akin to a double-edged sword; while it facilitates people's lives, it may also become a tool for others to spy on privacy.Zhu Yanqing believes that when operators open the "Find You Find Me" service, they should take a longer-term view and consider opening other functions of the mobile positioning service, such as more precise map positioning functions like those abroad, rather than just sending rough text messages, thereby improving the service of mobile positioning.In terms of authorization, operators should fully respect individuals' privacy rights. Simple mobile text message authorization will undoubtedly give others opportunities to exploit it. Therefore, it would be best to adopt a combination of owner-written applications, personal identity verification, signatures, or password settings to protect the personal rights of the owner.In addition, some citizens have proposed that mobile positioning functions opened due to work needs should ideally have a time limit to prevent being queried during leisure time. After all, during private time, no one wants to continue being "spied on" by the company.
