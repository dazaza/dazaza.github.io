Online matrimonial advertisements and netizen carpooling are seen frequently, recently, a combination of both appeared on Chengdu's major forums - house-sharing matrimonial advertisement, which has stunned many people. How did this post-80s Chengdu youth consider? The reporter conducted an investigation, and the result was very unexpected.

Post:

Post-80s seeking girlfriend for house-sharing marriage

"Buying a house is like choosing an object, I am a post-80s male, looking for girls with similar conditions for AA house-sharing marriage." Recently, a house-sharing matrimonial advertisement post appeared on Chengdu's local forums. The poster "HARRY520" said that he had been drifting alone in Chengdu for two years, never considering buying a house, and also unintentionally thinking about the matter of an object. Unknowingly, time flies like a white horse passing through a crevice, in a blink, he had worked for more than two years. Inspired by a real estate advertisement, he suddenly realized, wanting to find a girl, to handle the matters of buying a house and getting married together.

The poster detailed his own conditions: undergraduate degree, 25 years old, 180 cm tall, weighing 70 kilograms, "loves sports, standard figure, good-looking", then clearly listed the name of the targeted housing development, a down payment of 100,000 yuan, and a monthly mortgage of 1800 yuan. He also made several requirements for the house-sharing girlfriend.

As soon as the post was made, it undoubtedly caused fierce debates, becoming a hot topic on various websites. Some were shocked: "It's shocking! Marriage should be based on feelings, not on houses." Others opposed: "What do you want? She has more than 50,000 yuan in savings, why don't you gather enough for a 100,000 yuan down payment? I can't understand how you think." Most netizens thought this idea was not feasible, but a few admired his courage.

Investigation: Earning clicks to stir up property? The poster is mysterious

The reporter contacted the poster using the email address left on the forum, and soon, the poster "Mashimaro" responded on QQ.

"Let's get to know each other first, I was born in 84, my family is urging me." In the conversation, [transferred] the most touching photos online, "Mashimaro" described his personal situation as the same as what was described in the online post. He also said that his job was a network editor, from out of town, feeling it was time to settle down and "anchor".

On the afternoon of the 20th, when the reporter asked him again about the reason for posting the house-sharing matrimonial advertisement, he replied that it was not his idea, just forwarding a post. Since the netizen mentioned earlier that he was a network editor, the reporter questioned whether he transferred the post to increase the website's click rate, but he suddenly answered: "I think this is xx (real estate company name) publicity stunt." Since he already believed it was a publicity stunt, why did he still transfer the post? When the reporter further questioned, "Mashimaro" firmly denied being the behind-the-scenes publicity person of that real estate company. Finally, he even revealed that he was a journalist for a certain Chengdu online media outlet and reported his name. However, upon subsequent verification by the reporter, there was no such person among the journalists and staff of that website, "Just opened space, come around!"

In view of this loophole-ridden explanation, the reporter asked again. "Why ask so clearly? It's like checking household registration." Then, no matter how the reporter pursued the question, he refused to respond, citing inconvenience to disclose.

Buying a house is difficult, getting married is difficult, plus the hype of the post-80s generation, the astonishing idea of an AA system house-sharing marriage indeed has sufficient attention. A nearly ten-thousand-click post, besides the creative idea of house-sharing marriage, the most prominent is certainly the name of the real estate housing development that is remembered. There are netizens who have counter-questioned in their blogs whether this is due to the sluggishness of the real estate business, prompting merchants to resort to such tactics to attract consumers, "Is xx maliciously creating chaos, or is xx being maliciously created?"
