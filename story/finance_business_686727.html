Middle school requires students to sign a contract promising not to talk back to teachers

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-02 10:56:41

□ Reporter Shang Jie Core Tip: Schools "sign contracts" with students. Students will be rewarded for improving their grades, and are not allowed to talk back to teachers, idle around in the classroom, or fall in love. If violated, they may be warned, recorded as misconduct, or expelled. Yesterday, a microblog post by a high school student from Pingdingshan City immediately drew public attention. When the reporter contacted the principal of the school, he said that this was only the behavior of some homeroom teachers in the second year of high school, and the school was unaware of it, "the intention is good, but the approach is inappropriate." A high school student posts a microblog saying that the school wants to "sign a contract." Yesterday, when the reporter opened the microblog of the high school student, there was a photo of a "contract." The contract shows that Party A is the head teacher of a certain class in the second year of high school, and Party B is the student. Above the contract, it says: "In order to make our class a harmonious big family, so that we can be happy in this big family, learn successfully, and enter our ideal university, based on mutual agreements, we consciously abide by the following agreement..." Obligations of Party A: earnestly fulfill the obligations of a head teacher and treat every student equally. Obligations of Party B: do not talk back to teachers, do not skip classes, do not sneak out to surf the Internet, do not fall in love, do not use campus violence, do not idly chat in the classroom, do not casually enter or leave the classroom during self-study periods, be honest and trustworthy, do not cheat in exams; in terms of hygiene, consciously fulfill the duties of cleaning duty, ensure the cleanliness of the classroom and hygiene areas; in learning, one should have plans, work hard, summarize well, improve every day, ensure that the ranking in each mock exam does not decline, and those who advance in different ranks will receive different levels of material or spiritual rewards, those who improve by 100 places will receive heavy rewards. The contract also stipulates penalties. Depending on the severity of the violation, warnings, records of misconduct, probation, or expulsion may be given, and those who do not achieve the expected academic goals will self-punish. Questioning Voices Netizens' Comments This microblog post has drawn much attention from netizens. Netizen "Eagle Glance" said, the school's starting point is good, now children know how to "protect themselves"! More netizens, however, raised questions. "Tian You Xiao Qiang" said, this is just indoctrination education, establishing the absolute authority of teachers. It's an individual homeroom teacher's doing School Authorities say Yesterday, the reporter telephoned Wang Guoxiang, the principal of Pingdingshan Experimental High School. Principal Wang was very surprised, indicating that he was unaware of this matter and would investigate it. Subsequently, Principal Wang said that after preliminary investigation, the "signing contract" mentioned in the microblog post did exist, but it was not a school action, rather it was done by some homeroom teachers in the second-year liberal arts classes. This practice had not been reported to the school beforehand, and the school authorities were unaware of it. "The teacher's intention is good, but the approach is inappropriate, we will correct it."
