Advantages and disadvantages of home underfloor heating! What is underfloor heating? What are the advantages of underfloor heating?

Underfloor heating, also known as floor heating or ground radiation heating, is a low-temperature hot water floor radiation heating system that uses pipe materials such as PE-RＴ and PB laid on the ground, with the backfill layer and floor decoration materials serving as effective heat dissipation surfaces. Since entering the Chinese market, it can be said that after many years of technical improvements, underfloor heating design has become very mature. Compared to the convection heating method of radiators, it has many advantages.

Firstly, underfloor heating is the most comfortable heating method. Traditional Chinese medicine health preservation emphasizes: "Cold starts from the feet, heat starts from the head." In rooms with underfloor heating, the room temperature decreases gradually from bottom to top with increasing height, while the indoor surface temperature is uniform and the air quality is good. This temperature curve perfectly matches human physiological needs, giving people the comfortable feeling of warm feet and cool heads. Moreover, floor heating can promote foot blood circulation, thereby improving overall blood circulation, promoting metabolism, and enhancing immunity to a certain extent. Additionally, the "warm feet, cool head" environment can prevent drowsiness, helping to enhance memory, improve learning and work efficiency. In the past, the convection pipeline heating method resulted in temperatures of about 30°C at the top of the room, while the human body's position, especially the feet, had only 15°C or even lower. Sitting for a long time would cause cold feet and legs, which was very detrimental to human health.

Secondly, underfloor heating is energy-efficient and efficient. The heat dissipation of the floor heating method mainly concentrates within the height where humans benefit, so the thermal efficiency is high. Since the circulating water for heating only needs to be between 40-50 degrees, the indoor temperature can reach above 20 degrees, allowing heating like supplying hot water.

Compared to previous heating pipelines largely exposed outside, floor heating reduces a large amount of outdoor pipelines, and all indoor pipelines are encapsulated in the floor, thus reducing much heat loss, no longer appearing the situation where the top floor temperature is high and the bottom floor temperature is low. Moreover, floor heating can achieve zonal temperature control, allowing each household to save up to 30% on energy consumption, while the entire heating system can save around 40%. Given the current gradual depletion of energy resources, this holds significant meaning.

Thirdly, underfloor heating saves money. This can be explained from several angles.

(1) First, heating costs are significantly reduced. With an energy-saving amplitude of up to 30% per household, it means monthly heating expenses can also be reduced by about 30%, while the entire heating system can save approximately 40% in costs. This saves expenses for residents and lightens the burden on enterprises and society.

(2) Underfloor heating installation does not occupy usable area, spending the same amount of money is equivalent to buying a larger house. Currently, housing prices within Beijing's Fifth Ring Road are over 14,000 yuan per square meter. If using radiator heating, calculated based on three bedrooms, two living rooms, and two bathrooms (120 square meters, 7 radiators), at least three to four square meters of area are wasted due to radiator occupation. Using Beijing underfloor heating, since there are no indoor radiators and their branch pipes, three to four square meters of usable area can be added, thus saving costs.

(3) It reduces maintenance costs. Since the underfloor heating coils are all concealed in the floor slab, unless damaged by human error, almost no maintenance issues arise during operation, with a service life exceeding 50 years, no corrosion, and no scaling, greatly reducing the troubles caused by radiator leaks, drips, and repairs for residents, saving maintenance costs. Pipeline convection heating has high maintenance costs and a shorter service life. Based on the national determined service life of 50 years for houses, at least 3-4 re-installations would be required. Additionally, since pipelines are relatively independent, households do not affect each other, avoiding situations where one family's repair causes the whole building to stop heating. Finally, with fewer joints in the pipelines, the failure rate is greatly reduced.

Fourthly, underfloor heating is environmentally friendly, hygienic, and healthy. The heating principle of floor radiation heating is radiant heat dissipation, compared to air conditioning, radiators, etc., which rely on forced convection of hot air for heating, the air movement is much less, reducing the spread of dust and harmful bacteria in the air, making the indoor environment more sanitary and clean, extremely beneficial to the health of bronchitis, respiratory infections, and allergic syndromes. Moreover, reduced air convection leads to less water evaporation, overcoming the discomforts of indoor dryness, dry mouth, and tongue caused by traditional radiator heating methods, avoiding excessive skin dryness due to dehydration.

Fifth, underfloor heating is aesthetically pleasing and safe. First, there is no need for an outdoor pipeline network, unlike before when it looked like a spider web everywhere. Traditional radiator pipes were not only prone to scalding due to high temperatures but also affected the overall decoration style. Secondly, there are no visible pipes indoors, making furniture arrangement easier and creating a tidy appearance.

Sixth, underfloor heating benefits equipment. Since there is no risk of leakage and it resists scaling, underfloor heating pipes will not corrode, eliminating the problem of return water pollution affecting equipment. Therefore, under normal circumstances, maintenance requirements are minimal, which helps extend the life of the equipment.
