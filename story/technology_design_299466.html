The car owner won the lawsuit after 15 years and was awarded 210,000 in compensation.

by mnqyua924 on 2009-12-08 21:07:06

Related thematic articles: A hub fracture caused a terrible car accident, resulting in 2 deaths, 4 seriously injured, and 11 lightly injured. The car owner compensated the families of the deceased and the injured with over 179,000 yuan for economic losses. Since then, the car owner embarked on a long road to protect his rights. This lawsuit lasted for 15 years. Yesterday, Huang Zuhua sighed in relief after receiving the judgment from Jintang Court. After 15 years, the hub lawsuit was finally settled. The hub seller was ordered to compensate 215,532.34 yuan.

A Car Accident:

Hub Fracture Causes 17 Dead and Injured

In May 1994, Wang Daomei and Huang Zuhua went to Jiang Yuzhi's "Wanyuan Auto Parts Store" in Huikou Town, Jintang County, and bought a hub which they installed on their passenger minibus. On July 17 of that year, the left front hub suddenly broke while the minibus was in motion, causing it to flip into a river ditch 13 meters deep. Among the 17 passengers, 2 died instantly, 4 were seriously injured, and 11 were lightly injured. After the incident, the traffic police team of Jintang County rushed to the scene for investigation and handling, and stored the broken hub in the garage of the traffic police station. Subsequently, the traffic police team determined that Huang Zuhua and Wang Daomei were fully responsible. As a result, the couple compensated the families of the deceased and the injured with more than 179,000 yuan for economic losses.

Subsequently, Huang Zuhua sent the offending hub through the Jintang Technical Supervision Bureau to an authoritative department for testing, and the conclusion was that the hub had serious quality issues. Based on this, Huang Zuhua and his wife sued Jiang Yuzhi in court, demanding compensation for their losses.

Courtroom Focus:

Is the Hub Sent for Inspection the Offending Hub?

The focus of contention between the plaintiff and defendant was: Is the offending hub the one sold by Jiang Yuzhi? Is the hub sent for inspection indeed the offending hub?

In court, Huang Zuhua brought in witnesses, including the worker who replaced the hub on the car, the restaurant owner (Wang Daomei claimed they ate at this restaurant after extracting the hub with technical supervision bureau personnel), the driver who transported the hub from the restaurant to the technical supervision bureau, the driver who transported the hub from the technical supervision bureau to Chengdu for testing, the former instructor of the Jintang Traffic Police Team who investigated the car accident site, and the traffic police officer who handed over the hub to the technical supervision bureau.

"After the car accident occurred, I arrived at the scene immediately," said He Zhongzhi, the former instructor of the Jintang Traffic Police Team. "I have been a traffic police officer for 40 years, and I have only seen such an accident once." The situation at the scene was that when the minibus turned right, the left front wheel hub fractured. Such a hub is unique; even if you spent money, you wouldn't be able to find another identical hub. Therefore, he could confirm that the hub sent for inspection was indeed the offending hub. Traffic police officer Jiang Yan said that he personally handed over the hub to the staff of the technical supervision bureau, but since it was just an auto part, no handover procedures were carried out at the time.

The agent of Jiang Yuzhi argued that without written handover procedures, it cannot be proven that the technical supervision bureau took away the hub from the traffic police station. Meanwhile, the written proof from the technical supervision bureau insisted that they did not take the hub from the traffic police station nor eat at the restaurant, but instead took the hub from a pharmacy, unable to prove that the offending hub was purchased from Jiang Yuzhi's store.

Court Judgment:

The Offending Hub Was Sold by the Defendant

Was the offending hub the one sold by Jiang Yuzhi? The Jintang Court ruled that although there was no purchase invoice for the hub transaction, evidence showed that Huang Zuhua had once bought four front hubs from the defendant. The defendant admitted that the plaintiff purchased hubs from them and installed them for the plaintiff. The court found that the offending hub was indeed the one purchased from the defendant.

Is the hub sent for inspection indeed the offending hub? The court ruled that there was witness testimony proving this point. Moreover, after the hub was tested via metallographic experiments, the preserved fragments and screw cap photos matched the offending hub purchased from the defendant, showing consistency.

Finally, the court sentenced the defendant to compensate the plaintiff for a total loss of 215,532.34 yuan, deducting the 79,159.46 yuan already paid by the insurance company, resulting in an actual compensation of 136,372.88 yuan.

Car Owner's Reflection:

Consumers Should Actively Ask for Receipts

Huang Zuhua said that a hub-related lawsuit lasted for 15 years, during which he traveled thousands of kilometers, made dozens of trips back and forth between Jintang and Chengdu, participated in four trials, and throughout the 15 years, the case went through a first trial (February 1996), second trial (August 1996), retrial (April 1999), and re-trial (March 2009).

"Although the legal battle lasted for 15 years, my rights were ultimately protected," said Huang Zuhua. Through this lawsuit, he learned a lot. Consumers must retain evidence and actively ask for receipts. If he had had receipt evidence, this lawsuit would not have required so much effort.
