Introduction:

A few days ago, an incident occurred where a female nurse was gang-beaten. Several men drove to the Xin Beijiang Hospital in Xincheng, Qingyuan City, Guangdong Province, to seek treatment for an injured man who had been drunk. At the time, the man had injuries on his face and legs. Nurse Luo Aiqing prepared to treat the patient's leg wounds according to procedure when she suddenly felt someone touch her waist and shoulder. She reprimanded the man who had touched her, which resulted in her being beaten by a group of people. May 12th is International Nurses Day, and here, the mental health of female nurses urgently needs attention. Female nurses frequently encounter harassment.

Many female nurses encounter many awkward incidents of harassment at work. This incident of a female nurse being gang-beaten is just an escalated version of such awkwardness. Sometimes, when female nurses give injections to patients, some patients deliberately take the opportunity to touch the nurse's hands, using very flirtatious language. There are even a few elderly male patients who, when young nurses come to care for them, intentionally or unintentionally expose their private parts. Even in multi-bed rooms, male patients dare to take the chance to grope and hug female nurses. During night shifts, nurses receive calls where some patients, under the guise of asking about their conditions, vividly describe their sexual experiences over the phone, leaving the female nurses blushing with embarrassment but still having to endure and listen patiently.

Harassment causes mental burden

Whenever encountering such situations, female nurses generally do not report it to the hospital nor express dissatisfaction to the patients, but instead confide in older nurses to relieve psychological pressure. However, the more they do this, the more some male patients become emboldened. A female nurse from a certain hospital strongly resisted the harassment of a particular male patient, and that male patient harbored resentment, taking the opportunity to accuse the nurse of poor service attitude to the hospital supervision department. The involved nurse cried uncontrollably: "I've already done my best, but he vents all his anger on me and even insulted me with foul language. No one can bear such unjust accusations." Since most nurses are women, they must balance work and family, which also puts a strain on their spirits. This incident of a female nurse being gang-beaten has caused deep harm to both her family and herself.

How should female nurses relieve stress?

The nursing profession is mentally and physically demanding, with a very high level of work pressure. Due to nursing safety being related to the life and death of patients, many nurses are extremely cautious with every task. Once there is a possibility of an unsafe event occurring, they become very tense. Over time, symptoms of depression, anxiety, and irritability may appear to varying degrees. However, some people in society do not understand nurses, and some patients have high expectations of nurses. If these patients then engage in inappropriate behavior or make irrational demands, it adds further psychological pressure on the nurses. Currently, most hospitals do not conduct systematic stress-reduction education, and nurses' stress relief is mostly self-initiated. It is hoped that hospital managers pay more attention to the status of nurses, arrange experts to provide stress-reduction education to nurses, and offer psychological counseling to nurses.
