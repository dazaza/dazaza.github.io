Berlin Film Festival Opens with a Star-Studded Event: The Cold Can't Dampen the Passion for Cinema (Photos)

At 7:30 PM local time on February 9th, 2012 (midnight Beijing Time on February 10th), the opening ceremony of the 62nd Berlin International Film Festival was held at the Potsdam Square Film Palace. Members of the competition jury and the cast of the opening film *Farewell, My Queen* took turns walking the red carpet. The winter's low temperatures did not diminish the enthusiasm of cinephiles, as festival director Dieter Kosslick said, "Although the weather is cold, the movies are hot."

Stars Brave the Cold for Beauty

The red carpet at the Berlin Film Festival might be one of the coldest in the world. With outdoor temperatures dropping to -5 degrees Celsius, even wearing down jackets can leave you shivering in the biting wind. Despite this, many dedicated female stars walked the red carpet in bare-back, low-cut, and short dresses, leaving spectators breathless. Diane Kruger, the lead actress of the opening film *Farewell, My Queen*, was among the absolute big names of the event. Fans and media clamored for her attention, requesting photos and autographs. To reward the audience's long wait, festival director Dieter Kosslick pulled Diane Kruger back to the red carpet's end so she could sign more autographs for fans.

In contrast, male stars had it much easier, dressing warmly in suits and ties without any fuss. Jake Gyllenhaal calmly completed his fan-friendly appearance on the red carpet. Additionally, the arrival of old friends like Christopher Lee and Wim Wenders added much luster to the Berlinale.

While German film festivals still cannot match Chinese ones in terms of inviting countless big names for grand openings, and the opening ceremony may still be described by Chinese media as "lacking star power," the passion for cinema here remains no less intense than anywhere else.

Competition Jury Makes a Collective Appearance

This year's opening ceremony featured an unconventional start, with host Anke Engelke and festival director Dieter Kosslick seated high above, commenting on the guests present. Their banter resembled a comedy duo, but Dieter Kosslick’s awkward English and jokes in largely incomprehensible German failed to elicit laughter. Following this, German Culture Minister Bernd Neumann and Berlin Mayor Wowereit delivered speeches. Then, introduced by host Anke Engelke, Competition Jury President Mike Leigh made a slow entrance, followed by the collective appearance of jury members including Charlotte Gainsbourg, Jake Gyllenhaal, and François Ozon. Mike Leigh expressed surprise upon learning he was appointed as the Berlinale jury president and pledged to conscientiously fulfill his duties.

Huo Siyan Makes Her First Appearance on the Berlin Red Carpet

Following her debut at last year's Tokyo Film Festival, Huo Siyan came to Berlin thanks to her starring role in *Eat Drink Man Woman 2*. She also attended the Berlinale's opening ceremony. In response to questions from foreign media, Huo Siyan introduced herself and stated that this was her first time attending the Berlin Film Festival.

On the red carpet, Huo Siyan wore a stunning magenta long dress, adding warmth to the cold Berlin night. Her publicity team noted that this candy-like color perfectly aligns with the "strategy" of Chinese cuisine, where color plays a crucial role in attracting attention.
