The Scars of Sorrow

As the weather gets colder, it's so easy to recall something. There's always a vague sense of melancholy, perhaps that's called longing and sadness. It's like seeing the leaves gradually lose their exuberant flamboyance, becoming quiet and gentle. Those faint memories are like a small boat, meandering gracefully in the waters of dreams, and I also feel the beauty of "just like the spring river flowing eastward." It's as if the pear blossoms have showered from tree to tree, breaking the water ripples, without even saying sorry, lying helplessly in the embrace of the river.

Suddenly, it seems like I remember those days spent at my grandfather's house. That carefree time now appears so luxurious. Would I still be overjoyed by a cotton candy? Would I shyly run away because of someone's kind compassionate smile? Would I burst into tears or even refuse to go to kindergarten just because of a criticism or even a misspelled word? Would I still bury my little head in the arms of adults, letting them lead me around, obediently doing whatever they say? Perhaps this kind of life is gone now. Junior three, we must endure. Everyone says so. My grandfather is gone, the house has been moved, elementary school has turned into junior high, classmates and teachers have changed, even my own character has changed. My quietness no longer represents weakness and submission. I've learned to stand tall, speaking out my feelings and objections loudly. I've become a bit rebellious, knowing the cruelty of reality. So I start studying hard again, being a good child. In fact, all these changes are because I understand this world. You must learn to protect yourself, to change your fate, to make yourself calm and elegant, you need to earn the prideful capital for yourself. But compared to before, who has punctured my fairy tale? Who has mercilessly shattered my imagination? Dreams are so beautiful, almost unreal, but such beautiful things are woven by reality, and life is supported by dreams. So what exactly is life? I can't stand those so-called experts shouting absolute slogans, calling for the abolition of examination-oriented education, claiming that good students can be cultivated without schooling. That's you! With good family backgrounds and conditions. For such a vast country, how easy is reform? It's better to do some practical work.

Memories are beautiful, but what about reality? Will they also bring sweetness after many years? No, no more thinking...
