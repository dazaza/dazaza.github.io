Google's Olympic Gift: China Websites with Soaring PR

by longersing on 2008-08-16 13:24:19

By Longer

Google Update

With the conclusion of Google's major update, we can discover the emergence of many new phenomena. For example: since the PR value update on July 26th and the data update in the following two weeks, Google has overly favored a group of websites from China, especially Chinese portals covering the Olympics. I was also fortunate to receive Google's favor, with all sorts of data improving. Here, I would like to share some of my views.

After a long wait of three months, sites that were downgraded in the 430 update have been liberated. Many data results have unexpectedly improved. For Google, which has always valued high-quality backlinks, this update doesn't seem to be the result of long-term spider crawling feedback site data. Instead, it shows more consideration for the Olympics and Chinese websites. For instance, many sites without backlinks or only having a few low-quality links have received PR improvements. Some newly established sites between June and mid-July have also gained considerable PR values. I've even seen new sites with PR5, but by the third day of the update, it dropped to PR4.

In fact, we don't need to scrutinize the structure of these sites as being too complex or unsatisfactory, nor do we need to examine their backlinks. As long as your site is content-rich and contains a large number of original, readable articles—where readability means being beneficial for search engines to extract keywords that can better match users' search behaviors and results—for the main event of the late July update, the Beijing Olympics in August undoubtedly became the biggest highlight. This also confirms that those collecting new sites not only haven't been neglected but have instead become more favored by Google due to collecting a large amount of pre-Olympic articles. My personal analysis is that the good days for these collecting sites will only last for a few months. When the Olympic searches cool down, it will also be the time when Google pushes these sites back into obscurity. Therefore, quality original sites will still be the ultimate winners.

Portal Competition

In this update, the competition among portals is clear to everyone. However, from a commercial standpoint, there are still competitions regarding the reporting and broadcasting of the Olympics as well as some advertising transactions. In the PR value update, Baidu, representing China's search engine, excellently achieved PR8. Domestic government agencies or portals related to the Olympics have also seen an increase to PR8. Examples include Xinhua News Agency, Sina.com, Sohu.com, and CCTV, all representatives of PR8. Of course, there are also QQ, 163, and TOM, all representatives of PR7. These sites have all benefited somewhat from their association with the Olympics, which can be considered as a bit of care from Google's search engine towards Chinese websites.

Polarization

After this update, many webmasters have said: "Garbage Google, why did you kill my site? Why wasn't my site indexed, but instead lost many pages? Why are there fewer links? Why did my site's PR value drop after the update?" These questions also reveal another side of Google: severe punishment for sites that over-optimize or use black hat SEO techniques (Black Hat SEO Special Topic). At the same time, among those sites that play by the rules, those without any connection to the Olympics and lacking substantial content have once again been neglected by Google. This has caused polarization in this Google update.
