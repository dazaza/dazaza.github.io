Online shopping is considered a patent of women, but in fact, men are crazier than women.

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:28:29

Shopping has always been considered a female privilege. But did you know that when it comes to online shopping, men are crazier than women. According to statistics from Taobao, in terms of online shopping, the proportion of male consumers is higher than that of females, and the average transaction value (the amount each customer spends per transaction) is also 50% higher than that of women.

What do male internet users in Ningbo like to buy? And what are their characteristics? The author conducted a small survey.

The main force of online shopping is male, not female.

Yesterday, a staff member of Taobao sent the author a set of statistical data. By the end of 2010, Taobao had 370 million registered users. In terms of age distribution, people aged 18-34 were the main group for online shopping, especially those aged 18-24. In 2010, the number of this consumer group increased more than any other age group.

In general perception, women are the main force of online shopping. So, are there more men or women who shop online? Statistical data shows that in 2010, the proportion of male consumers on Taobao reached 53.9%, while women accounted for only 46.1%.

Male online shoppers not only outnumber women but are also more generous. According to statistics, the total amount spent by male consumers online is nearly 8 percentage points higher than that of female consumers. Although women surpass men in key data such as the number of transactions and the number of purchases, in terms of average transaction value, men surpass women at a ratio of 1.5:1. This indicates that women prefer to buy cheaper items multiple times, while men are more generous and tend to buy more expensive items.

"Generally speaking, male consumers tend to buy high-priced items such as digital products, furniture, home appliances, sports shoes, and outdoor equipment, while female consumers mainly spend money on clothing, food, and accessories. This leads to the total consumption of men exceeding that of women, with higher single-transaction amounts," said relevant personnel from the Taobao data platform. Even within the clothing industry, the average price of men's clothing consumption is higher than that of women's.

Ningbo situation

Men love to buy clothes and home appliances online most.

Does the situation in Ningbo align with the Taobao data? Yesterday, the author conducted a small survey. There were over 30 respondents, all male, aged between 25 and 45 years old, including doctors, lawyers, bank employees, media professionals, company employees, accountants, and civil servants.

When answering the question "Have you ever shopped online?", 95% of male respondents indicated they had experience with online shopping. About 80% of respondents shopped online once a month, around 15% shopped occasionally, and about 5% shopped almost every week. This shows that the proportion of male internet users in Ningbo who shop online is relatively high, and the frequency of purchase is also not low.

For the question "What have you bought online?", about 80% of respondents said they had bought clothes online, followed by mobile phones, chargers, home electronic products, books, furniture, outdoor equipment, daily necessities, and plants.

Regarding the reason "Why do you like online shopping?", the most common response was convenience—just search for what you want to buy without having to walk around the streets. Additionally, the large selection and lower prices online are also reasons why male internet users like online shopping.

On the question "How much do you spend each time you shop online?", about 60% of respondents chose less than 500 yuan, around 25% chose 500-1000 yuan, and approximately 15% chose 1000-2000 yuan. A few respondents spent over 2000 yuan each time. The author randomly interviewed some frequently shopping female internet users and found that most female internet users spent less than 200 yuan each time they shopped online.

Merchant feedback

Male internet users spend more generously than women.

From feedback from some merchants, male internet users spend more generously than women. "In terms of the number of customers on our website, women outnumber men, but when it comes to average transaction value, men spend more than twice as much as women," said Mr. Zhang, the head of Ningbo Lehuo Kitchen Network, an agricultural product shopping website selling specialty agricultural products such as seafood, poultry eggs, and fruits. He told the author that female customers mostly buy for household use, with lower unit prices, while male customers not only buy for themselves but also as gifts for clients, thus spending more generously. During the Spring Festival, they would buy several boxes or even dozens of boxes of seafood gift packages at once.

Many home appliance stores have opened online malls, where male internet users outnumber women. "Among our online mall customers, male customers outnumber female ones, with a male-to-female ratio of about 6:4," said a relevant person in charge of the Ningbo Gome E-commerce Department. Male customers mostly buy mobile phones, with an average transaction value of around 2000 yuan, while female customers buy more small appliances, with an average transaction value lower than that of male customers.

Online shopping insights

Username: Yan Ouliou (Mr. Hu)

Online shopping age: 3 years

Occupation: Private enterprise employee

Online shopping comment: "Shopping in stores is too troublesome, online shopping is the most convenient."

Many men don't like to go shopping, but it's hard to find a man who doesn't like to surf the internet. Internet user "Yan Ouliou" said that apart from eating and sleeping, he spends most of his time online. Since he bought his mother a small recorder for dancing in the park three years ago through online shopping, he hasn't been able to stop.

Now, "Yan Ouliou" buys something online almost every week, spending nearly ten thousand yuan on online shopping last year. His most recent online purchase was sending a bouquet of flowers to his girlfriend studying in South Korea, costing over 600 yuan for a dozen roses. The clothes and shoes he wears, the mobile phone and computer accessories he uses, and the books he reads are all purchased online.

After several years of online shopping, "Yan Ouliou" has gained some insights. "Online shopping is convenient, but you need to choose carefully. My experience is to choose merchants who have real-name authentication, promise a 7-day return policy, have good credibility, and positive reviews." Once, "Yan Ouliou" bought a Jack & Jones coat online. Attracted by the cheap price, he made the purchase immediately. Upon receiving the item, he realized that the label on the coat read "Qiong Jack," which was due to his own carelessness.

Username: Dao (Mr. Deng)

Online shopping age: 5 years

Occupation: Bank department manager

Online shopping comment: "If you like it, just buy it, don't hesitate."

Last night, Mr. Deng lightly clicked his mouse and bought a set of waterproof jackets, two trekking poles, and one backpack, spending over a thousand yuan. "The waterproof jacket is for myself, the trekking poles are one for me and one for my wife, and the backpack is for my daughter." Mr. Deng likes outdoor activities, and most of the items he buys online are related to this. Digital products are also his favorite; he often searches for the latest trendy phones online and once bought three new phones within two months.

"I usually buy things I like online without comparing like my wife does," Mr. Deng said that he doesn't like to go shopping. Before becoming addicted to online shopping, most of his purchases were handled by his wife. Now, he often buys clothes for himself online, and even if his wife complains that he spends too much, he enjoys it anyway.

"You can't be as smart as the seller, and people often say they get scammed online. I generally don't compare prices much. Quality goods come with quality prices, so I rarely get scammed," Mr. Deng's online shopping insight is: "Buy what's right for you, not what's cheap."

Username: Yan Shi (Mr. Liu)

Online shopping age: 3.5 years

Occupation: Civil servant

Online shopping comment: "The internet is a great treasure trove, everything is available."

Mr. Liu's first online shopping experience was buying something for his wife, specifically cosmetics from a direct sales brand. At the time, he didn't think much of it, thinking it would be better to buy directly from the store. Unexpectedly, now he has also fallen in love with online shopping and buys something online every month.

"Online shopping is very convenient. Some niche items are hard to find in real life, but once you're online, just enter the product name, and hundreds of options will pop up instantly," Mr. Liu initially bought a niche item, a snore-stopping clip, because he had the habit of snoring at night. His wife ordered him to buy one, but after searching through malls and supermarkets without finding it, he finally found it online for only 5 yuan. Recently, he saw an ancient book from the 1940s about Beijing online, which contained an article written by his deceased grandfather. Without hesitation, he bought it for 1000 yuan.

"Online shopping brings a lot of joy to life, but impulsive shopping should still be avoided, otherwise, you might end up buying a lot of useless things," Mr. Liu said he once bought a flashlight and flint for mountain climbing, thinking it was interesting at the time, but never used them after purchasing.
