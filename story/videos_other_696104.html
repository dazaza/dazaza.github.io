Prospect: Seven highlights of China's economic performance in the first half of the year

by n8e6n7r5h2 on 2012-03-07 20:35:53

Expanding domestic demand has played an important role in promoting economic recovery. The national policy of expanding consumption has achieved remarkable results, with the total retail sales of consumer goods increasing by 15% in the first half of the year. After excluding price factors, the actual growth was 16.6%, and the growth rate of rural consumer goods retail sales was higher than that of urban areas. The consumption structure is accelerating its upgrade. A total of 9.61 million (units) of home appliances were sold under the "Appliances to the Countryside" program, 6.1 million cars were sold, and the sales area of commercial housing increased by 33.4% compared to the same period last year. Investment growth momentum remains strong. Fixed asset investment across society grew by 33.5% year-on-year. Investment in agriculture, science, education, culture, health, environmental protection and other fields grew even faster; real estate development investment grew by 9.9%, which accelerated by 5.8 percentage points compared to the first quarter.

The rural economy developed steadily. Summer grain production achieved a record-breaking sixth consecutive year of growth since the founding of New China, reaching a total output of 246.7 billion catties; both the total and per-unit yields of summer rapeseed set new historical records. The livestock and aquaculture industries maintained an overall growth trend. The quality and safety level of agricultural products continued to improve. Scientific and technological support for agriculture further strengthened. Agricultural mechanization levels improved rapidly. Through timely and proactive market regulation measures such as implementing the minimum purchase price for grains and increasing national reserves, the prices of grain, cotton, oil, sugar, and meat and other agricultural products were effectively stabilized.

Industrial production rebounded quickly. In the first half of the year, the added value of industrial enterprises above designated size grew by 7%, including a growth of 9.1% in the second quarter, with the growth rate reaching 10.7% in June alone. The automobile industry showed strong growth momentum, raw material industry production rebounded, and light industry, textiles, and other outward-oriented industries showed signs of warming up. Industrial production in coastal areas, which were heavily impacted by the international financial crisis, also improved. Electricity usage, transportation, and other indicators showed positive trends. National electricity consumption in June increased by 3.8% year-on-year, reversing the situation of continuous decline over the past eight months; the year-on-year decline in railway freight volume further narrowed, and the cargo throughput of ports above designated size has been growing positively for four consecutive months.

Adjustments in industrial structure and energy conservation and emission reduction continue to advance. In the first half of the year, the proportion of the tertiary industry's added value in GDP increased by 1.6 percentage points year-on-year. Enterprises' pace of technical transformation quickened, and significant progress was made in major equipment localization. Emerging industries such as next-generation mobile communications and biomedicine developed rapidly. A number of high-quality energy mega-projects including nuclear power, wind power, and hydropower have started construction, and external cooperation in the energy sector has made positive progress. Efforts to eliminate outdated capacity are proceeding actively, with the shutdown of small thermal power units achieving the "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" target one and a half years ahead of schedule. The unit energy consumption of some main industrial products has decreased further.

Regional development coordination has increased. Central and western regions and Northeast China strengthened infrastructure construction, undertook industrial transfer, and developed advantageous and distinctive agriculture. In the first half of the year, the growth rates of investment, consumption, and industrial production in these regions all exceeded those in eastern regions, opening up new space and adding new vitality to national economic development. Eastern regions have continuously made new progress in promoting industrial structure transformation and upgrading, independent innovation, and enhancing competitiveness.

Employment and social security and other people's livelihood work have achieved positive results. Under the impetus of active employment policies, urban employment increased by 5.69 million people in the first half of the year, completing 63% of the annual target. The employment situation for migrant workers remained generally stable, the employment signing rate for college graduates basically reached the same level as the same period last year, and 790,000 people who had difficulty finding jobs achieved re-employment. Residents' income continued to increase, with the per capita disposable income of urban residents and the per capita cash income of farmers growing by 11.2% and 8.1% respectively after adjusting for price changes.

Reform in key areas has achieved new breakthroughs. Value-added tax reform and oil price and consumption tax reforms were successfully implemented. Medical and health system reform took substantial steps forward, making major progress in addressing the medical insurance issues of retired personnel from closed and bankrupt state-owned enterprises. A number of public health service projects aimed at urban and rural residents were initiated, and supporting documents such as improving urban and rural medical assistance were successively issued. On the basis of improving the stock issuance system, new stock issuance and listing work restarted. Some state-owned shares were transferred through the securities market to replenish the national social security fund. Comprehensive collective forest tenure reform was fully promoted. □(Compiled by Wang Jianjun, reporter of Outlook News Weekly, and intern Li Yuan)
