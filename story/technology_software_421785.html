A chapter of Hunan music is gradually becoming distant

by musicshow5 on 2011-04-27 09:20:45

Xiang dialect is hard to change, it's Yang Wuliuh's crosstalk work of the early years, performed with partner Zhou Weixing. It displays vividly the local Hunan dialect culture from Changsha to Ningxiang, then to Yueyang and Yiyang, leaping to Xiangxiang. The language is sharp and humorous, enough to make people burst into laughter.

Indeed, different sounds can be heard in every ten miles. Various characteristic dialects have their own unique charm. The elders have used their dialects to decorate characteristic music in a long life accumulation, generation by generation, inheriting fragmentarily. Zhangjiajie and Xiangxi folk songs condense the colorful and pleasant life of Xiangxi people. Cutting firewood, jumping into water, looking at mountains, farming, lovers singing to each other, celebrating and sacrificing are all integrated into these folk songs. Although I haven't been to Xiangxi, I've listened to their folk songs a lot. In 08, I specially invited two singers from an art troupe in Xiangxi to record nearly 30 folk songs. The male voice is high and bright, the female voice is melodious and moving. I also followed and sang many times. It's cheerful and easy to sing. I always pay attention to copyright, but after consulting materials, I didn't find the copyright note of the lyricist, composer and performer. I asked an old man in Xiangxi, and he said that these folk songs were passed down by predecessors. The melody doesn't change much, but the lyrics are random, and people sing as they like. There is a saying that people sing different songs on different mountains.

Changsha Tanci originated from Daoqing, embracing the moon琴 while singing, sung in Changsha dialect. According to the elderly, it was once very popular in street tea houses, a popular storytelling art among ordinary people. Singing while playing musical instruments brings joy. I personally think so. But observing the present situation, this locally-born intangible cultural heritage, Changsha Tanci, is gradually disappearing in the long river of history. Mr. Peng Yan Kun, highly respected, is a well-known figure among the elderly in Changsha. His name represents the culture of Changsha Tanci. He spent six or seventy years of his life on the inheritance of Tanci. In his twilight years, he is still striving to protect the fading spiritual heritage, which is worthy of praise.

This kind of music can be considered as representative categories. Some viewers may think that this kind of music is far away from themselves. This statement is wrong. If you recall suddenly, your grandmother or great-grandmother would moan into a tune when encountering happy events or sorrow, filling words according to the event, singing gracefully, laughing or crying. There are no relevant textual records, but it is also a tune of our dialect culture. Later generations just don't care to inherit...

Writing some, my heart is full of feeling. Hunan music, rich soil nourishes all directions, but forgets the famous ancient artists. We, the later generations, should be more grateful to our predecessors, recording bits and pieces in life, passing them down to the world, and there will be hope!
