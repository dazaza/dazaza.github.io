Breaking out of the vicious circle of website weight and traffic: Having weight but no ranking

After the website content is published, it can be indexed quickly, sometimes even reaching the enviable efficiency of being indexed within seconds. From this perspective, the search engine has granted such websites a relatively high weight; otherwise, spiders wouldn't stay on the content page 24 hours a day waiting for updates. However, what's strange is why, without the search engine penalizing the site, main keywords or even long-tail words fail to get good rankings? Binary analysis believes that unclear website theme positioning or unclear development direction causes search engines to be unable to accurately categorize the website.

A: Unclear theme positioning. In the early stages of website construction, the website does not clearly define its development direction, and even the core keywords are unclear. How can such websites rank well? This ultimately confuses search engines. Phenomena like "XXX Information Network" or "XXX Comprehensive Network" often appear. In the early stages of a website, you can start by determining around 20 keywords most relevant to the website theme with relatively lower optimization difficulty, then optimize around these keywords most related to the website theme. When the website develops to a certain extent and has some weight, you can confirm more keywords. Generally, large websites have hundreds or even thousands of keywords and may still achieve good rankings.

B: Website development deviates from its initial focus. Although there was a fairly accurate positioning of the website theme at the beginning, deviations occurred during the development process. Some websites, in order to speed up Baidu snapshot updates, write anything as long as it gets indexed by the search engine. Over time, while the search engine acknowledges the quality of your website, it also leads to deviation in the website's theme, eventually resulting in having weight but no ranking. The ranking here refers mainly to the ranking of key keywords. For example, my website Kaiyue Interconnection focuses on domain names and hosting services. I hope that keywords like "virtual host," "hosting space," "domain name," and "cn domain" would rank first. If unrelated terms like "computer" or "TV" rank instead, it provides no benefit to my website.

II: Snapshot updated daily but no traffic

Even though the website's snapshots are updated daily by search engines, why is there no traffic? Many websites experience this phenomenon. Someone might say: Isn’t frequent indexing representative of high weight? However, Binary Analysis says that according to current Baidu rules, new sites can also achieve instant indexing. My blog is an example; it achieved instant indexing after three days of operation and continues to do so a month later, yet the ranking hasn't reached the front page (due to lack of external links). Instant indexing for new sites differs from high-weight sites; it can be seen as a special favor from the search engine to facilitate future development of the site. Traditionally, even original content on new sites is hard to index quickly, leading to situations where original content gets plagiarized, making it difficult for search engines to identify the original, causing imbalance between original and plagiarized content. Currently, search engines have specific rules for new sites:

1. Search engines judge the website's update time to facilitate regular spider indexing. Some websites update at fixed times daily to attract spiders, which significantly reduces server pressure.

2. Examination of whether it's an excellent site. A great website consistently updates content while adhering to its principles. If a site cannot stick to its principles and starts scraping or pseudo-original content creation due to increased inclusion frequency, it falls into the new search engine rule trap, inevitably getting categorized as spam, reducing server load and avoiding prolonged observation.

3. Special care for new sites. New websites don't have much weight initially, and if high-weight websites plagiarize their content, all the effort put into original content becomes wasted. Therefore, search engines index new site content first but don't grant much weight.

In conclusion, clarifying the website theme, sticking to original content, gaining weight, and achieving ranking is the ideal state any website seeks.
