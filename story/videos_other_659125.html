If I say this is the legendary and famous Google logo, would you believe me? Hey, buddy, your glasses are over here. It's the Year of the Ox now, and all sorts of super impactful events will happen. This, truly, is Google's logo, and it's today's logo.

Today, when I opened Google's homepage and saw this logo, my first feeling was that Google had been hacked. Because I thought, there's no way Google would use such a logo, as its main function is to serve as a distinguishing graphic marker from other websites. But could you associate the image above with Google in any way?

Upon clicking and taking a closer look (see the image below), I realized I was wrong. Google is actually very clever. It turns out this was to commemorate Jackson Pollock's birthday.

Jackson Pollock (1912-1956) was an American abstract expressionist painter. He was a symbol of new American painting worldwide after World War II. He was a pioneer of abstract expressionism and one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, renowned for his technique of dripping paint onto canvas in a casual manner, creating unforgettable natural qualities in his works.

It seems that even Google is embracing post-modernism. I admit, I'm outdated. To prove my normalcy, I've decided to study hard and hold on to the tail of fashion forever and ever. Because, I definitely cannot be as shocking as Google. Even if I want to shock people, I need to be at least a little bit better than Google. (Liu Xingliang)
