Children's bad breath

Cause:

The main reasons for children's bad breath are non-disease-related and disease-related. Non-disease-related ones can include temporary children's bad breath, such as if children have eaten strongly scented foods like leeks or garlic, this kind of children's bad breath can be alleviated by brushing teeth or rinsing the mouth and eating something else or chewing gum.

What about disease-related children's bad breath? Usually, cavities are the main cause of children's bad breath. Children often like to eat snacks, especially sweets. If adults don't supervise them and they rarely brush their teeth on their own, food residues remain in the mouth, fermenting and producing unpleasant odors, thus causing children's bad breath. Therefore, when children exhibit bad breath, parents should promptly take them to see a dentist to prevent cavities. Additionally, diabetes and sinusitis can also cause children's bad breath. With the improvement in living standards, it is not uncommon for children to develop diabetes at a young age, so it is recommended that parents ensure their children's diet is healthy and green.
