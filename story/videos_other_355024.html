Nanjing's real estate market sees a price-cutting trend, with the maximum drop nearing 13% (Part I)

by dsdfsd32 on 2010-05-12 18:25:24

Nanjing's Jiangbei real estate market suddenly hit by price cuts yesterday

With a large number of super-sized developments and consistently leading in both development and sales volume in Nanjing, the Jiangbei real estate market suddenly made waves yesterday! Following the Xuri Shangcheng project's launch of a fixed-price offer for over 300 units at 4500 yuan/square meter, Suning Properties then rolled out another fixed-price strategy for its three projects in North Bridge: the Tianrun City project set a fixed price of 4088 yuan/square meter for 350 units, which is about 600 yuan/square meter cheaper than before. Meanwhile, the Fengyayuan Sanqu second-hand housing market was also affected.

The continuous discounting and promotions by North Bridge projects have led to questions: does their collective price drop mean that Jiangbei housing prices can no longer hold? Is this a sign of an overall decline in Nanjing’s housing prices?

[Price Plunge] Three more projects introduce fixed-price units

The super-large scale Tianrun City project has offered a surprising fixed-price discount of 4088 yuan/square meter, with a total of 350 special-priced units distributed across various sections of the project. The 550 special-priced units account for 16% of the total available units.

Interestingly, two other projects in the same Jiangbei sector of North Bridge also implemented fixed pricing: 150 units at Venetian Water City were uniformly priced at 4480 yuan/square meter, while 50 units at Tianhua Lvgu were uniformly priced at 4188 yuan/square meter. However, it was learned that these projects imposed strict restrictions on the floors of the units, such as Project Yi targeting only the first and top floors, and Project Bing focusing on floors 1-6 of high-rise buildings and the first and sixth floors of multi-story buildings.

In total, there are 550 special-priced units, what proportion do they make up of the available units for sale? Data from Nanjing Online Real Estate shows that these three projects currently have a total of 3360 available units, meaning the 550 special-priced units account for approximately 16%.

An industry insider stated that for a major player like Suning in North Bridge, 550 units spread across three projects is just a drop in the bucket. However, considering the current total available units, a 16% proportion is relatively significant.

Sold to specific groups? Sales office says anyone can buy

Interestingly, Suning Properties' unified statement yesterday was that the 550 units across the three projects were specifically selected to thank society and show social humanitarian care, and were intended for provincial and municipal labor models, advanced workers, military personnel, and others who have contributed to society.

However, when reporters asked the sales staff at these three projects yesterday, they all indicated there were no such restrictions, and buyers could purchase normally.

Why is the developer being so secretive? An industry insider speculated that the developer may be worried that previous buyers might feel they bought at a loss and demand refunds if they see the discounts.

[Public Reaction] Reasons for those who want to buy and those who don't

Citizen Mr. Shi said that if the fixed-price discounts were only for the first and top floors, it wouldn't be very meaningful. However, he noticed that some fixed-price offers weren't exclusively for the first and top floors; for instance, Project Bing also offered fixed pricing for floors 1-6 of high-rise buildings, making units on the 4th and 5th floors priced at 4480 yuan/square meter worth considering.

Mr. Shi also believed that compared to the prices he had been following, these fixed-price units were hundreds of yuan cheaper per square meter, and with several hundred units available, the scope of the price reduction was quite broad. He decided to visit the properties over the weekend.

However, some citizens were unmoved by the price drops in Jiangbei. Citizen Ms. Lu said that recently, she took a bus to look at properties in Jiangbei but was stuck in traffic for over 2 hours on the bridge. By the time she arrived at the property site, she had lost all enthusiasm. Such a transportation environment would be inconvenient for future living. Despite the significant price reductions, she remained hesitant and currently wouldn't consider buying in Jiangbei.

[Industry Analysis] North Bridge housing prices reaching five thousand Yuan per square meter remains a dream

Last year, the housing projects in Jiangbei aimed to break through five thousand yuan per square meter, but now it seems they've returned to their original state. According to Shi Dong, Deputy General Manager of Nanjing Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, from a transaction price average of less than 3500 yuan per square meter last February to struggling to maintain at 4500 yuan per square meter now, breaking through 5000 yuan per square meter in Jiangbei has clearly failed.

This can also be seen from the comparison of sales volumes during the same period: in February this year, the sales volume of Pukou properties dropped by 74% month-over-month, putting pressure on developers.

According to predictions by Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, this year, new residential properties in Jiangbei will reach 3.313 million square meters (slightly higher than the 3.21 million square meters in 2007), with new approvals amounting to 2.865 million square meters, the highest among all sectors in Nanjing. This year, the additional supply of residential units from the 45 ongoing projects in the Jiangbei sector will reach 2.8 million square meters. The concentrated and large-scale supply of commercial residential properties will inevitably intensify competition in the North Bridge area of the Jiangbei real estate market.
