On August 8th this year, the Jingzhai Brigade of the Weiyang Public Security Bureau dismantled a black den that processed "health products" using chicken feed and veterinary drugs. This photo was taken by reporter Zhitao Zhao for our newspaper. Yesterday afternoon, the provincial public security department held a press conference on the "Sword" operation, announcing a series of major cases involving counterfeit Zhonghua cigarettes, Maotai liquor, salt, medicine, soy sauce, soy milk, and dozens of other products, with a total case value of 406 million yuan, causing great harm that is shocking. Deputy Director Zhonglin Ma of the Provincial Public Security Department stated that the province-wide public security organs' "Sword" operation to combat intellectual property rights infringement and the production and sale of substandard goods started on October 19, 2010, and ended on November 30 this year. In more than a year, nearly ten thousand police officers participated in the operation, solving 460 cases of intellectual property rights infringement and the production and sale of substandard goods, arresting 831 criminal suspects, destroying 187 fake production dens, smashing 136 criminal gangs, seizing over 1.0588 million pieces of various pirated, counterfeit, and substandard goods, with a total case value of 406 million yuan. The antihypertensive medicine is actually a health food. "Once, an elderly person said that this antihypertensive medicine works well, one dose lasts three days." Yesterday afternoon, Zhonglin Ma mentioned that during a previous publicity event on counterfeit inferior products, a citizen pointed to a type of "medicine" seized by the police and said it was very effective. In fact, this antihypertensive medicine is counterfeit. Yesterday afternoon, the police officer pointed to a type of counterfeit and inferior medicine called "Le Ya" and said that this antihypertensive medicine is not a medicine at all, but a health food with a food health number. However, the manufacturer claimed it was effective for people with high blood pressure. After patients took it, they all said it worked well. In fact, the manufacturer had illegally added drug components to the health food during production. This health food has significant side effects, and long-term use can seriously endanger physical health. Among the fake medicines investigated by the police, I noticed that they were mainly some hypoglycemic and antihypertensive medicines and health products. For example, 190,000 boxes of lipid-reducing sugar-dissolving capsules, 3,600 plates of Tangren Kang capsules, 14,000 bags (boxes) of Le Ya capsules, 8,000 plates of Hai Ya Bao capsules, and 5,278 boxes of Hong Kong Ruan's Chuan Li Ting were seized, with a case value of more than 30 million yuan. Veterinary medicine and chicken feed made into medicine for human consumption. I noticed that among the fake medicines investigated by the police, there was also a health product targeting men. The police stated that this health product was completely ineffective and harmful to the human body. It was reported that in April this year, the Weiyang police in Xi'an received a report that someone in the jurisdiction of the Weiyang Palace Street Office was producing counterfeit inferior medicines. After several months of investigation, on August 8th, the suspects You Mou and others involved in the production of fake medicine were arrested. Upon investigation, it was found that since February this year, You Mou and others had rented warehouses, purchased processing machinery, recruited staff, and bought raw materials such as maltodextrin and Six He poultry veterinary drugs (chicken feed), illegally processing counterfeit medicines such as "Deep Sea Islet Resurrection," "Shenbao Brand Holy Health Capsules," "Holy Wei Kang Capsules," "Cordyceps Lung Revitalizing Capsules," "Give Power Capsules," and "Male Root Holy Treasure Capsules." They sold these counterfeit medicines nationwide. Since February, they had produced more than 2,400 boxes of various counterfeit inferior medicines, totaling 960,000 plates, with a case value of more than 5 million yuan. Where do counterfeit inferior condiments go? It was introduced that the counterfeiting den in Lantian County was located in a village in Hu Xu Town. The factory was originally abandoned workshops in the village, covered with spider webs, filled with sewage, and the processing raw materials, glass bottles, and garbage were piled together, emitting a foul smell. The glass bottles used for filling honey and white vinegar in the den were all recycled from scrap collection stations, simply washed with tap water before being filled. Where do these counterfeit condiments go? Yesterday afternoon, the police officer who participated in the investigation said that after the investigation, it was found that the "Chongqing Red 99" hot pot base material produced by Ye Mou's gang was mainly sold to Xinjiang and Weinan City, Wei Jia white vinegar was mainly sold to counties around Weinan City, "Da Fu Hao" wild mountain pepper was mainly sold to the Hujiemiao Grain and Oil Wholesale Market in Xi'an City and the "Zhu Yuan Cun" hot pot store in Lanzhou, "Guan Sheng Yuan" "Zi Yun Ying" honey was mainly sold to Xining City in Qinghai Province, and "Haitian" "Tao Da" soy sauce was mainly sold to Xinjiang. Reporter Lichun Liu Soybean soy sauce is mixed with flavoring This September, the Weinan police discovered a huge network of counterfeit famous brand condiments in production and sales in Xi'an and Weinan. On November 3rd, the Provincial Public Security Department uniformly deployed the "Hunt Shark No. 24" campaign against inferior condiments. More than 190 police forces were mobilized, and in Linwei District of Weinan City and Hu Xu Town of Lantian County, 12 production and storage dens of counterfeit inferior products were dismantled, capturing 9 people including Ye Mou, and seizing more than 220,000 bottles of a large number of counterfeit honey, monosodium glutamate, chicken essence, edible salt, hot pot base material, soy sauce, white vinegar, aged vinegar, broad bean paste, wild mountain pepper, and various fake raw materials, with a case value of more than 10 million yuan. The police said that this gang impersonated famous brands like "Haitian" soy sauce and "Tao Da" soy sauce, which looked no different from the genuine ones on the outside, claiming to be soybean products. However, the police investigation found that they were all mixed with some raw flavorings, and did not contain any soybeans at all. The beef tallow used to make hot pot base material was very inferior, "containing a large amount of paraffin, as hard as cement, and standing on it would be fine." The police said that the honey made in this den used mostly non-food raw materials, without any original honey, but instead used salt, cellulose heated up, added sand sugar, syrup, caramel color for seasoning and coloring, and then added benzoic acid, citric acid, and other additives for bottling, which could not serve any health-preserving function, and was extremely harmful to the human body. Be careful when buying condiments at roadside shops The police said that these counterfeit inferior products are generally sold in some roadside small shops, some flow into wholesale markets, and even individual supermarkets. The police reminded the general public to buy in regular large supermarkets and malls whenever possible. If counterfeit inferior products are found, direct reports can be made.
