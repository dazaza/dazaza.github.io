The ISL97682/3/4 have minimal packaging and offer up to 90% power conversion efficiency, making them ideal for designs used in tablet computers and portable consumer electronics.

California, USA, MILPITAS — June 28, 2011 — Intersil Corporation, a world leader in the design and manufacture of high-performance analog and mixed-signal semiconductors (NASDAQ Global Trading Code: ISIL), today announced the launch of its latest backlight LED drivers — the ISL97682, ISL97683, and ISL97684. These devices feature the industry's best high-performance current regulation accuracy and channel matching, making them highly suitable for driving LCD screens in smartphones, tablet PCs, LED spotlights, netbooks, and other portable electronic products with small and medium-sized LCD displays and multi-channel LED backlights.

The ISL97682, ISL97683, and ISL97684 are compact 2, 3, and 4-channel LED drivers with an input voltage range of 4V to 26.5V and an output voltage up to 45V. In bootstrap mode, these drivers can operate at a low voltage of 3V with an output voltage up to 26.5V, supporting direct single lithium battery power supply. The devices automatically switch between PWM and PFM modes to ensure optimal efficiency across the input voltage and output load range (up to 90%). All devices offer current accuracy of 1.5%, with 0.7% channel-to-channel current matching, providing excellent consistency for backlight solutions in 6-15 inch TFT LCD screens.

**Key Features and Specifications:**

- Minimum supply voltage of 3V (using bootstrap mode), meaning no 5V bias supply is required to achieve very high efficiency.

- Optional channel phase-shift technology reduces noise in video and audio.

- Channel-to-channel current matching of 0.7% ensures uniform backlight brightness.

- Dimming frequencies up to 30kHz allow dimming frequencies above the audio range, eliminating audio noise.

- Each string of LEDs has open circuit, short circuit, overvoltage, and overheating protection, ensuring chips function properly in all environments without damage.
