CNN to Decide Within the Day Whether to Apologize to Chinese in Writing, Sources Claim

by wszdw142 on 2008-05-03 11:06:12

Core tip: Regarding the case of New York Chinese suing CNN which netizens are eagerly following, Attorney Hai Ming informed everyone that there is new progress in the case where the Chinese have entrusted our law firm to sue CNN. The written response from the other side is currently being processed urgently, and the CNN vice president expressed over the phone that their company will make a decision within a few days.

On May 3rd, People's Daily Online reported that regarding the case of New York Chinese suing CNN which netizens are closely following, our UN-based correspondent will provide exclusive phased coverage from different angles. This article is the second special report by the reporter on the exclusive interview with the law firm handling this case.

At exactly 12 noon on May 2nd, the reporter arrived at the Hai Ming Law Firm as scheduled for the second time. Attorney Hai Ming informed that a small-scale Chinese media press conference would be held today to introduce a major turning point in the case against CNN. The reporter was eager to understand the detailed situation, but Attorney Hai Ming only said "there has been a positive change." About ten minutes later, reporters from several local Chinese media outlets arrived, with only our correspondent present among the China-based journalists in New York.

At this point, Attorney Hai Ming informed everyone that there is new progress in the case where the Chinese have entrusted our law firm to sue CNN. It was introduced that recently, Attorney Hai Ming had telephone contact with a vice president responsible for international affairs at CNN headquarters, with daily communication, resulting in the current new progress in the case. According to reports, this CNN vice president expressed deep apologies to the Chinese people over the phone to Attorney Hai Ming. CNN will send a relatively friendly written response to the Hai Ming Law Firm and simultaneously submit a similar written apology response letter to the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs. This CNN vice president stated that this statement would appear more friendly than all previous expressions by CNN, hoping for the understanding and forgiveness of the Chinese people. He also hoped that the Chinese people could understand the differences in news systems between China and the United States, and even between China and Western societies, including methods of operation in journalism. This CNN vice president expressed his fondness for Chinese culture and did not wish to offend all 1.3 billion Chinese people due to certain mistakes in news reporting.

Attorney Hai Ming introduced that it is less than 100 days until the Beijing "Olympics," and the "Olympics" need an atmosphere of harmony and joy. Our law firm believes that if CNN can respond with relatively good intentions this time, and if their written apology can gain the understanding and forgiveness of the Chinese people, we do not hope for further escalation of the matter, causing serious opposition between the publics of both countries. The opening of the "Olympics" is on August 8th, and according to the current legal procedure timeline, it would be the most intense period for courtroom debates. If the situation escalates further, how would the other side further attack China? How would the judge ultimately rule? These are all worth seriously considering. We do not hope for an overly tense situation during the "Olympics." We preliminarily decided that if this friendly response from CNN can gain the understanding of the Chinese people, our two clients and the Hai Ming Law Firm might file with the court to withdraw this lawsuit, achieving a reconciliation outcome.

The reporter asked, "Will the other party's response truly be satisfactory?"

Attorney Hai Ming replied that judging from the current mutual communication over the phone, the other party's telephone response is relatively satisfactory.

The reporter asked, "When will the other party's written apology be seen?"

Attorney Hai Ming replied that the other party's written response is currently being processed urgently. The CNN vice president expressed over the phone that their company will make a decision within a few days. This vice president is not only a vice president of CNN but also a vice president of Turner Broadcasting, the parent company of CNN, allowing him necessary communication with the highest levels of the company, but the final draft still requires the decision of the top management of CNN.

The reporter asked, "If so, why has CNN not responded promptly to China's righteous demands?"

Attorney Hai Ming said, there is a communication issue here. Since we took on this case, we have been in constant contact and communication with both sides. According to regulations, after the court issues a summons, the other party must respond in court within 30 days. We did not pressure CNN; we presented a very friendly attitude, allowing some extension of time for them to consider before responding. Our communication has been very smooth. During our communication with them, we learned that they initially did not understand the protests and demonstrations by the Chinese people. They believed they were not aware of the truth. It was understood that in the U.S., an organization claimed to have signed thousands of letters to CNN, stating that our law firm does not represent the Chinese people, but they do, claiming that Chinese public opinion supports CNN and that the Chinese people are manipulated by the nationalism of the Chinese government. Therefore, CNN initially thought they were only opposed to the Chinese government but still maintained credibility among the Chinese public. Our law firm is a civilian-neutral institution. I told them that if you anger 1.3 billion Chinese people, the consequences would be quite severe. This vice president stated that CNN now realizes the severity of this issue and hopes to maintain friendship with the Chinese people.

The reporter asked, "Is the law firm under any pressure to stop the lawsuit?"

Attorney Hai Ming said, this result was not what we anticipated, but after filing the case, we did not expect it to attract such widespread social attention, with both support and personal insults. To be honest, we indeed faced considerable pressure for a while, including criticism from various sources and even from peers in the industry. We once considered ceasing the litigation. However, seeing the strong support from the Chinese people, we did not cease and resolved to continue. We wanted to achieve a relatively good outcome, using legal means to reclaim the dignity of the Chinese people. Originally, we intended to continue the lawsuit but did not wish to cause severe opposition between the mainstream public opinions and media of both countries at this time. I mentioned earlier that compensation of one dollar per person symbolizes more than its practical significance. It should be noted that overseas and domestic Chinese are exerting pressure on CNN through various channels, and protests and demonstrations are important ways. The best action our law firm can take is to file the lawsuit. Our goal is singular: to reclaim the dignity of the Chinese people. Here, it must be acknowledged that the serious requirements and immense pressure imposed by the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs played a crucial role.

The reporter asked, "Which word did the CNN vice president use over the phone to express apologies to the Chinese people, 'sorry' or 'apologize'?"

Attorney Hai Ming said, during our phone communication, I analyzed the pros and cons of the situation CNN faces and suggested they sincerely apologize to the Chinese people. The other party said "OK," indicating willingness to accept my suggestion and promised to quickly draft the written response.

The reporter asked, "Can it be said that so far, representatives of CNN themselves have not personally spoken of apologizing to the Chinese people? Is this CNN vice president so confident that his written response will gain the understanding of the Chinese people? Additionally, how will the law firm measure whether CNN's written response gains the understanding of the Chinese people? In other words, after receiving the other party's written response, will the law firm observe public opinion for a stage before deciding whether to withdraw the lawsuit?"

Attorney Hai Ming answered these questions himself and indicated that once he receives the written response, he will inform the reporter immediately. However, the reporter still has many doubts and will continue to track the development of this situation.
