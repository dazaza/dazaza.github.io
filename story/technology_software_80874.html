Various riddle-solving methods are explained below:

【Semantic Analysis】Understand the overall meaning of the riddle's surface, and then match it to the answer. For example → Face with hooks, head corners hanging fans. Four thick pillars, one small braid. (Guess an animal: Elephant)

【Reverse Thinking】Guess by reversing the meaning of the riddle's surface. For example → Don't use villains (Guess a Chinese herbal medicine: Child Gentian)

【Borrowed Reference】Don't use the original or multiple meanings of the riddle's surface, or reverse its meaning; instead, borrow a new interpretation from the riddle's surface to match the answer. For example → Enlightened (Guess a Tang Dynasty writer: Yuan Jie) "Enlightened" is interpreted as "the start of the Ming Dynasty," which is the end of the Yuan Dynasty, so the answer is "Yuan Jie".

【Indirect Reference】Do not directly understand the original meaning of the riddle's surface, but use multiple meanings to indirectly highlight and match the answer. For example → River maples and fishing fires (Guess a character from The Scholars: Double Red). Here "Double Red" is derived from "maple" and "fire," both of which are red, so "Double Red" matches the riddle.

【Segmented Matching】The characters in the riddle's surface separately match the characters in the answer; sometimes one character matches one, one character matches many, or many characters match one. For example → Look through (Guess a Kunqu Opera title: Fifteen Guan) "Look" is commonly known as "fifteen," and "through" has the same meaning as "guan," so they respectively match "Fifteen Guan."

【Source Tracing】"Source tracing" refers to tracing the origin of the riddle's surface and its connection to the original text, and then matching the answer. This is also called the method of linking context. For example → Peach Blossom Spring water deep thousands of feet (Guess a Chinese idiom: Peerless) This riddle uses the next line of "Peach Blossom Spring water deep thousands of feet," which is "Not as good as Wang Lun sending me off," to match the answer.

【Addition Method】Increase the strokes of some characters hinted at in the riddle's surface or add certain characters together to match the answer. For example → Good mountains and good waters (Guess two characters: Cui, Huai) Here "good" matches "excellent," "good mountains" are "excellent mountains," adding themselves becomes "Cui"; "good waters" are "excellent waters," adding themselves becomes "Huai."

【Subtraction Method】Reduce the strokes of some characters hinted at in the riddle's surface, or subtract certain characters to match the answer. For example → No water in the pond, no mud on the ground (Guess one character: Ye) Removing the three dots of water from "pond" leaves "Ye," and removing the soil radical from "ground" also leaves "Ye."

【Addition-Subtraction Method】Following the hints in the riddle's surface, some characters increase their strokes, while others decrease them, involving both addition and subtraction, finally matching the answer. For example → Remove the top from the bottom, remove the bottom from the top, remove the ends from the middle, remove the middle from the ends. In this riddle, "remove" is the key, dividing it into upper and lower parts that can be combined into the answer.

【Separation-Bottom Method】This riddle reflects the separation of the bottom part in the riddle's surface. When guessing, synthesize the riddle's surface, then match the answer. For example → Seven people (Guess a county name: Kaihua)

【Separation-Face Method】Separate certain characters in the riddle's surface to match the answer. For example → Cha (Guess a Chinese idiom: A family's opinion)

【Pictographic Method】Based on the characteristics of objects and the structure of Chinese characters (pictographic), personify or objectify them, making them more vivid, evoking associations, and increasing interest. For example → Two dots on ice seem excessive, stones press water and water climbs the slope. (Guess a noun: Water pump)

【Pictorial Method】This riddle matches the answer based on the overall pictorial meaning of the riddle's surface. For example → Distant trees in two rows, mountain reflections, a light boat flowing horizontally on the water. (Guess one character: Hui)

【Direct Homophony】When creating the answer, use homophonic or phonetically similar characters to replace the ones that should be used, diverting attention and hiding the answer. For example → Increase ten taels (Guess a city: Tianjin) "Increase ten taels" means "add catty," which is homophonic with "Tianjin."

【Indirect Homophony】First decompose certain characters in the riddle, then use homophones to match the answer. For example → Two different specifications (Guess one character: Shoe) When guessing, first decompose "shoe" into "gui" and "ge" parts, "gui" is homophonic with "specification," "ge" is homophonic with "spec," thus fitting the answer.

【Comparison Method】Place words with similar or opposite shapes or meanings together, compare them, and match the answer. For example → Add one stroke and it's bad, double it and it's too much (Guess one character: Xi)

【Personification Method】Personify the words in the riddle's surface to match the answer. For example → There is a little girl, wearing a yellow shirt, if you bully her, she will stab you once. (Guess an animal: Bee)

【Objectification Method】Objectify people or parts of the human body, or the semantics or objects mentioned in the riddle's surface, to match the answer. For example → Pillow (Guess a Chinese idiom: Put it behind your head)

【Question-Answer Method】This type of riddle usually uses a question-style surface and an answer-style answer. For example → Who commands the 800,000 imperial guards? (Guess a Chinese idiom: First to bear the brunt)

【Classical Allusion Method】Word-based riddles use familiar idioms, colloquial expressions, poems, or allusions as the riddle's surface, reinterpreting their meanings to match the answer. For example → Baoyu proposes marriage (Guess an American historical figure: Lincoln)

【Exclusion Method】Exclude one side and take the other, exclude multiple sides and take one, exclude the easy and take the difficult. For example → Say is not say, take is not take (Guess one character: Zui) Here exclude "say" and take "said," exclude "take" and replace with "fetch."
