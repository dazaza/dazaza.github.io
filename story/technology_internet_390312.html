Summary: Yesterday afternoon, Xiong Jian, a seller from Changsha on Taobao, received the first online business license in Changsha from the Tianxin Branch of the Changsha Administration for Industry and Commerce. Her store, “No. 95 Avenue des Champs-Élysées” (http://xd95.taobao.com), became the first individual online business in Changsha to implement the “real-name system for online stores” one month after its formal implementation.

Xiong Jian, who runs an online store on Taobao, obtained the first e-commerce business license in Changsha.

(Yibang Power Network News) "Integrity is the basic requirement for running an online store, it’s best to follow it voluntarily. After I hang up the real-name online store business license symbol, I will win more trust from consumers." Xiong Jian smiled at the scene. At the same time, Xiong Jian also reminded her peers that natural persons who temporarily do not meet the registration conditions are not required by law to register with the Administration for Industry and Commerce but should voluntarily provide their real personal identity information to the network commodity trading platform operator.

The online store business license makes infringement complaints accessible.

"In order to expand my online store, I want to provide a more trustworthy symbol for my online store," revealed Xiong Jian about the initial intention of obtaining the license. "The procedures for individuals to open an online store and obtain a business license are the same as those for individual businesses, and it's not complicated. You just need an ID card, property certificate (or copy of a purchase contract or housing agreement), a one-inch photo, Taobao's real-name certification, and the highest product value as a deposit."

At the awarding ceremony, the reporter saw the newly issued business license. In appearance, Xiong Jian's online store license was no different from an ordinary individual business license. However, the difference lies in the fact that the operating method and scope of this license are limited to: providing cosmetics and accessories sales on the Internet. It is understood that as long as the materials are complete, it can be processed within one to two hours. Xiong Jian spent a total of 20 yuan on processing fees and registration fees, Guangzhou VIP Information Technology Co., Ltd.

The virtual nature of online transactions and the unclear identity of store owners have always been a concern for consumers. "Once there are infringement violations, we can contact the relevant network commodity trading platform operators to find the specific online store responsible person," said the head of the Market Department of the Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce. "Generally speaking, the online store is the first responsible party. If consumers complain about the service quality or products of a certain online store and have solid evidence, and the network commodity trading platform operator does not take active measures but lets it develop, then they must bear joint liability." If a certain online store seriously damages consumer rights or violates laws and regulations, the online store will be closed by the network commodity trading platform operator.

"With a legitimate license for the online store, buyers can check the license of the online store at any time, increasing trust in the online store, which can attract more consumers to choose online shopping." A netizen with years of online shopping experience, Ms. Chen, expressed that adding this mandatory procedure allows online transactions to break away from the embarrassing situation of "no legal basis." It is understood that after issuing the business license, the Administration for Industry and Commerce does not charge the store owner business tax. Previously, some netizens worried that the real-name system for online stores would increase the cost of online shopping and lead to price increases, but these concerns can now be put aside. Prior to this, Shandong, Zhejiang, Hubei, and other places had also issued business licenses for online stores.

The issuance of business licenses for online stores is "not mandatory."

On July 1st, China's first administrative regulation to standardize online commodity transactions and related services - the "Provisional Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Related Service Behaviors" - was officially implemented. According to the "Measures," natural persons engaging in online commodity transactions and related services shall apply to the network platform service provider and submit their real identity information such as name and address. Those who meet the registration conditions shall legally register with the Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Regarding whether all online stores must go to the Administration for Industry and Commerce to handle business licenses, the person in charge of the Market Department of the Hunan Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce explained, "In fact, enterprises and individual businesses that have already registered only need to publicly display the electronic link identifier of the business license information in prominent positions such as the homepage of their website and do not need to register separately for a special online business license. Natural persons who temporarily do not meet the conditions for registering a business license only need to inform the network commodity trading platform operator (such as Taobao, Tongcheng Purchase Network, etc.) of their real personal identity and other relevant information."

According to the person in charge of the Market Department of the Provincial Administration for Industry and Commerce, there are not many large-scale network commodity trading platforms in our province, and they particularly need to be standardized and supported. August is the "Promotion Month for the Healthy Development of the Network Economy" in Hunan Province. (Editor: Shu Xiaochang)
