Poker terminology

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-02-25 14:43:40

Aces Full: Full house, which is three Aces and one other pair.

Ace-High: Five unpaired cards, with an Ace being the highest card.

Aces Up: Having two pairs, where the higher pair is a pair of Aces.

Act: The way of playing that attempts to give others a false impression. For instance, having a flush but pretending to have a pair of Aces to deceive opponents whose hands are in between.

Action: Refers to various actions including checking/betting/calling. Sometimes, "action" game refers to situations where repeated betting and calling by players lead to a large pot.

Active Player: A player who hasn't folded yet.

Aggressive: Describes players who frequently raise and re-raise but rarely call.

Ajax: Refers to a hand with Ace and Jack as the hole cards, either suited or off-suit.

All-In: When a player bets all their chips.

American Airlines: Two Aces.

Angle: An action that doesn't break the rules but is somewhat deceitful.

Angle-Shooting: Using deceptive strategies.

Ante: Forced bet, commonly used in 5-card or 7-card stud; in Texas Hold'em, it's usually called Blind.

B

Bad Beat: A strong hand being beaten by a lucky card from the opponent.

Backdoor: The final made hand differs from what was expected. For example, starting with J/10 of spades, the flop shows A of spades, 5 of spades, 6 of clubs, but the turn and river come out as K and Q of hearts respectively. As a result, the made hand becomes a straight instead of the initially intended flush.

Backraise: A player who has been calling suddenly starts raising.

Bankroll: Money available in a casino account.

Bet: Placing a bet.

Belly Buster: A card that completes an inside straight.

Bet Odds: Betting probability, for example, if you're considering raising, you need to calculate how many people will call to decide if it's worth it.

Bet the Pot: A player's bet equals the current pot size.

Bicycle or Bike: Straight A-2-3-4-5.

Big Blind: The second person clockwise from the dealer's forced bet, usually casinos also require new participants at the table to make this forced bet.

Big Slick: Hand with A-K.

Blank: A useless card.

Blind: The two people or new joiners clockwise from the dealer must make a forced bet.

Bluff: Having a poor hand but betting or raising actively to make others believe you have a good hand.

Boardcards: Community cards shown openly to everyone, there are five in Texas Hold'em and four in 7-card stud.

Boat: Full house.

Bottom Pair: A pair formed by the player's hole cards and the lowest card on the flop.

Broadway: Straight with an Ace (A-K-Q-J-10).

Bring-In: Forced bet in games like 7-card stud.

Bring It In: The initial bet in the first round.

Broomcorn’s Uncle: A player who has put all their money into the pot.

Bullets: A pair of Aces.

Bump: Raise.

Button: The dealer's marker, passed clockwise to indicate the dealer position.

Buy-In: The minimum amount required for a player to sit down at a specific table.

C

Call: Matching the previous bet.

Call Cold: Calling both the initial bet and a subsequent raise.

Calling Station: A player who doesn't raise much but calls too often.

Cap: The limit on the number of raises per betting round.

Cards Speak: Your card combination is the best possible.

Case Chips: A player's last chips.

Check: Choosing not to bet when no one else has bet or raised (e.g., when it's the big blind's turn).

Check-Raise: When a player checks initially in a round, then raises after someone else bets.

Chop: Returning the blinds to the players because no one else called.

D

Dead Man’s Hand: Two pairs - Aces and Eights (from a movie plot).

Dealer: The person actually dealing the cards.

Dealer’s Position: Dealer position, the last person to bet in a betting round, offering some advantage.

Dominated Hands: Starting hands that can be played but are likely to lose to similar hands if the game continues. For example, A2 might be okay as a starting hand, but if it cannot form a flush, straight, or two pairs, and there's an A on the board, it's easy to lose to an opponent's A with a better kicker.

Dominating Hands: Not only good hands but also ones with significant potential. For example, holding Ah2h and the flop comes with 3h, 4h, and Ac. This already forms a strong pair and has potential for a straight, flush, or even a straight flush.

Door Card: The first card dealt face up in 7-card stud or stud games.

Down Card: Hole cards that are not revealed.

Doyle Brunson: 10-2 as starting cards (Brunson won the World Series of Poker with such cards).

Drawing: Playing a hand with potential but not currently strong.

Drawing Dead: A hand that is destined to lose even if the most favorable card comes. For example, a player holds 2h, 3h, and the community cards show 4s, 6s, As, while the opponent likely has a flush. Even if the player gets a straight, they are still doomed.

Drawing Hand: A hand that needs a specific card to win, usually having potential for a straight or flush.

Drop: Folding.

Ducks: A pair of 2s.

Deuces: A pair of 2s.

E

Early Position: A position where the player bets earlier than most others in a betting round. For example, the two positions left of the blinds.

F

Fifth Street: Also called the River. In Texas Hold'em, it's the fifth community card. In 5-card stud, it's the fifth card dealt to each player.

Flat Call: Just calling without raising.

Flop: The first three community cards dealt face up in Texas Hold'em and Omaha poker.

Flop Games: Poker games that use community cards, such as Texas Hold'em and Omaha.

Flush Draw: Having four cards of the same suit and waiting for the fifth.

Fold: Folding.

Forced Bet: A mandatory bet, players take turns making forced bets to ensure there is always a pot on the table.

Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.

Fourth Street: Also called the Turn. In Texas Hold'em, it's the fourth community card. In 5-card stud, it's the fourth card dealt to each player.

Free Card: A free card. Usually refers to the following situation: You are in late position, and when the flop comes, your hand isn't strong but has potential, such as a flush or straight. You want to see the next card but don't want to pay too much. At this point, it's clear that most people have strong pairs but only one pair, and everyone isn't very confident. Someone bets ahead of you, and the rest follow. When it's your turn, you suddenly raise. Others call, and on the turn, your opponents mostly check, and you check as well, allowing you to see the river for free. The benefit is obvious. For example, in a $2/$4 game, your raise only costs you an extra $2, but you save $4 on that free round. If the card you need comes on the turn, you can continue betting, and if it comes on the river, you can make up for the lost profit by raising again. If nothing comes, you at least save $2.

Full House: Three of a kind plus a pair.

G

Grinding: A style that involves low risk and hopes to win through slow play over a long period.

Gut Shot: Waiting for and completing an inside straight.

H

Hand: The best five-card hand a player has.

Heads-Up: One-on-one play.

High Limit: A game with relatively high stakes.

Hole Cards: The player's hole cards.

House: The casino.

I

Image: The impression you give to other players, such as whether your playstyle is conservative or aggressive.

Implied Odds: The odds you expect from betting in this hand.

Inside Straight: An inside straight, where four cards that could form a straight are present, but the missing card is in the middle, such as A,K,J,10.

J

Jackpot: A reward given to certain players meeting specific conditions. For example, a bonus might be awarded to those who get a royal flush.

K

Keep Them Honest: Calling to the end to prevent bluffing.

Key Card: A crucial card that makes your hand strong.

Key Hand: Typically in tournaments, especially no-limit tournaments, a hand that changes the outcome.

Kicker: The single card outside of the player's main hand that decides the winner. For example, if both players have two pairs, the kicker determines the winner.

Kick It: Raise.

Kill Pot: A high-intensity game. For example, on UB, a player who wins twice in a row must post a double-blind bet ($4 instead of $2), and subsequent bets are doubled.

Knock: Check, as players in land-based casinos indicate a check by tapping the table with their knuckles.

Kojak: Starting hand K-J.

L

Ladies: Two Queens.

Late Position: Late position, such as the one or two positions to the right of the dealer.

Lay Down Your Hand: Fold.

Lead: The first person to bet.

Limit Poker: A game where each betting and raising round has a cap.

Limper: The first person to call.

Limp In: Call, specifically referring to the first person to call the blinds.

Live Card(s): Cards that haven't appeared yet.

Live Hand: A hand that still has a chance to win.

Live One: A player who plays every starting hand.

Long Shot: Playing a hand with low chances or unfavorable odds.

Look: A player who calls the last bet before showdown.

Loose: Playing every starting situation.

Low Limit: Low-stakes game.

M

Main Pot: The main pot, when someone goes all-in, the pot formed after they go all-in becomes the side pot, and the pot formed before is the main pot. Note that sometimes the side pot can be larger than the main pot, so don't judge by the amount.

Maniac: An aggressive player who plays every hand.

Maverick: Starting hand with a Q and a J.

Meet: Call.

Middle Pair: In Texas Hold'em, usually refers to the smaller card in the player's hand pairing with the middle card on the flop.

Middle Position: Positions between early and late positions. For example, the fifth, sixth, seventh, and eighth positions in a 10-player game.

Muck: Fold, more often refers to not showing your cards when you win without revealing them or losing at showdown.

Minimum Buy-In: The minimum amount you need to join a game.

Monster: A very strong hand. In tournament play, a player who rebuilds their stack after having few chips is also called a monster.

N

No Limit: No betting limit game.

Nuts: The best possible hand in the game. A hand that is guaranteed not to lose. For example, if a player has a pair of Aces, and the board shows Ac, 3s, 7h, 8d, Kc, the opponent cannot possibly win regardless of their hand. Thus, this player has the Nuts.

O

Odds: The ratio of the likelihood of making a hand versus not making it. For example, the odds of hitting four-of-a-kind with three of a kind are almost only 2%, since only one card can complete it while the other 48 cannot (assuming the three of a kind appear in the hole cards and the flop).

Offsuit: Cards of different suits.

On the Button: Being the dealer, able to bet last.

Open: The first person to bet.

Open-ended Straight: Also called an open-ended straight, four consecutive cards where hitting either end completes a straight, such as 3456, where hitting a 7 or a 2 completes the straight.

Open Card: A card dealt face up.

Open Pair: A pair dealt face up.

Outs: Cards among those not yet revealed that could improve your hand.

Overpair: A pocket pair that is higher than any card on the board.

P

Paints: Face cards, such as J, Q, K.

Pay Off: Your hand isn't particularly strong, but you still call the opponent to the end.

Picture Cards: Face cards, such as J, Q, K.

Play Back: Raising or re-raising in response to the opponent's bet or raise.

Playing the Board: When your best five cards are all on the board.

Pocket: The hole cards in your hand.

Pocket Rockets: A pair of Aces as your hole cards, considered the best starting hand.

Post: Bet.

Pot: The pool formed by players' bets, awarded to the final winner.

Pot Limit: In this game, the maximum bet each round can equal the current pot size.

Prop: Casino shill.

Q

Quads: Four of a kind.

Qualifier: In Omaha high-low, the minimum requirement for a low hand to win part of the pot.

R

Railbird: A constant spectator.

Rake: The casino's cut.

Rap: Check.

Reading: Analyzing other players' styles and habits.

Re-buy: Chips purchased by a player after going all-in.

Re-raise: Raising after the opponent's raise.

River: The river. In Texas Hold'em and Omaha, it's the fifth community card; in 7-card stud, it's the seventh card.

Rock: A tight player.

Round of Betting: Each betting round.

Royal Flush: A royal flush, consisting of (A-K-Q-J-10) of the same suit, considered the best possible hand in poker.

Run: Bets exceeding the normal amount for such a hand.

S

Sandbagging: Having a strong hand but only calling to pretend it's weak. Usually done to disguise strength, lure others into bluffing, or to let the opponent lose more by re-raising.

Satellite: Small tournaments used to qualify for larger tournaments.

Scoop: Winning the entire pot.

Second Pair: A pair formed by your card and the second-highest card on the board.

See: Call.

Semi-Bluff: Not yet having a strong hand but with the potential for one, betting as if you have a strong hand.

Set: A three or four of a kind formed by your pocket pair and a card on the board.

Shills: Shills employed by casinos to keep poker games running smoothly.

Short-Handed: Fewer players in the game.

Showdown: At the end, remaining players reveal their hands to determine the winner.

Side Pot: The side pot, when someone goes all-in, the pot formed after they go all-in becomes the side pot, and the pot formed before is the main pot. Note that sometimes the side pot can be larger than the main pot, so don't judge by the amount.

Soft Seat: Describes a game where many beginners make it easy to win money.

Solid: Describes a reasonably tight and quite good player.

Small Blind: The forced bet of the person to the left of the dealer, usually half the minimum.

Speed Limit: A pair of 5s.

Split: Tie.

Stack: A pile of chips.

Stay: When a player stays in the game by calling.

Steal Raise: A raise by a late-position player to try to reduce the number of opponents or steal the pot.

Steamrolling: Re-raising, forcing later players to face double the raise.

Steel Wheel: The highest 5-card straight flush (A-2-3-4-5).

Straight: Five consecutive cards of any suit.

Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.

Structure: The rules and limits of a particular game.

Stud Games: Players receive both face-up and face-down cards.

Stuck: A loser.

Suit: Suit, such as diamonds, hearts, spades, and clubs.

T

Tell: An action that reveals a player's actual hand.

Thirty Miles: Three tens.

Three of a Kind: Three of a kind ("trips").

Tight: Tight players refer to those who don't easily commit to large pots. Tight games refer to games where players rarely raise.

Tilt, On: Behavior after losing money, usually referring to negative behavior.

Top Pair: The player's hole cards form a pair with the highest card on the board.

Top Two Pair: The player's two hole cards form pairs with the two highest cards on the board.

Treys: A pair of 3s.

Trips: Three of a kind.

Turn: The turn, the fourth card in Texas Hold'em.

U

Underpair: A pocket pair lower than the lowest card on the board.

Under the Gun: The first person to act, usually referring to the person to the left of the big blind.

Up Card: A face-up card.

V-W

Walking Sticks: A pair of 7s.

Worst Hand: A hand that is definitely going to lose.

WSOP: World Series of Poker.
