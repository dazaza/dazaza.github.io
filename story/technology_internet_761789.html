Taobao's 10th Anniversary: The Change Alibaba Wants to See

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 14:02:29

Jack Ma is stepping down from his managerial position to spend more time with his wife and children, discuss Taoism with the Taoist master, and focus on his role as the president of the Nature Conservation Association. From its humble beginnings to now, Alibaba has witnessed and experienced the explosive growth of China's Internet over the past decade, further establishing Ma's status as the soul figure of Alibaba.When this pivotal figure decides to reduce his involvement in Alibaba's daily management, it signifies a turning point for China's largest e-commerce site. Whether this turn will be for better or worse, we do not yet know. Inevitably, some people will wonder: Can Alibaba remain the same without Ma?Today, I attended the Taobao 10th Anniversary Open Day event in Hangzhou. From 2 PM to 5 PM, there were six speeches given by Liu Yong Changyang, Vice President of Human Resources at Alibaba; Senior Director of Human Resources Guo Xiang Li Xiaoqiong; veteran Taobao employee Shuang'er Shen Qi; representative engineer Zi Liu Zhao Chao; Group Vice President Liang Chunxiao; and General Manager of the Merchant Division Fei Qing Wang Wenbin. These speeches comprehensively analyzed from both internal and external perspectives where Alibaba will head in the post-Ma era.They sent out a strong signal: In the post-Ma era, Alibaba will continue to move forward resolutely towards its goals, eliminating the negative impact of Ma's retirement.The first session was titled "The Growing Flavor of Ali," discussing Alibaba's corporate values and organizational structure from both individual and organizational perspectives to argue for Alibaba's future.Reviewing Ma's recent public speeches, he began discussing small but beautiful commercial organizational structures akin to natural ecosystems, never clearly defining a single word to describe this type of business organization. At the media communication meeting, Liu Yong Changyang said that Alibaba aims to become an ecological organization.What then is an ecological organization? Actually, Changyang did not provide a specific explanation, somewhat avoiding specifics. However, from his and others' speeches on-site, one could see what an ecological organization entails: It is an open, flat, decentralized structure characterized by simple communication styles, closely centered around the company's mission, fully leveraging individual initiative and autonomy.Changyang said:Our intranet has many legendary posts. For example, when Ma wrote something, there would be comments criticizing him, either due to disagreement or pointing out punctuation errors. These things happen all the time at Alibaba. Therefore, Alibaba is a very open, direct, and simple communication environment. During meetings, if someone takes too long to get to the point, we call it "too much foreplay" and ask them to quickly state their main points. Thus, openness and simplicity are our ways of team collaboration and interaction.Changyang also introduced Alibaba's promotion model:Promotion at Alibaba is not initiated by the boss. First, I have to nominate myself. If I feel I am ready, I can propose it, followed by the entire review process. We hope that the people at Alibaba are willing to take responsibility.From the six core values listed by Changyang, it is clear that Alibaba hopes its employees will be individuals who clearly know what they should and should not do, actively identify problems, and find solutions. Such individuals would undoubtedly be troublemakers in traditional organizations that value seniority and hierarchy. However, Alibaba welcomes troublemakers. Guo Xiang Li Xiaoqiong said, "When we were a small enterprise, we were particularly afraid of wild dogs. Now, we fear white rabbits." She explained that white rabbits are obedient and quiet, making no mistakes but also contributing little. When you have a workforce of 25,000 people, finding a quiet place to be a small white rabbit becomes easy.An ecological organization requires human behavior to support it:Ma said, "We want to be a zoo, hoping to have various kinds of animals—some talkative, some introverted—not just one kind like chickens or ducks, which would make us a farm. As we evolve into an organization with diverse animals, our awareness of ourselves, our shortcomings, and our direction becomes increasingly diverse. But overall, our shared mission and vision are recognized by everyone. If someone does not agree, they wouldn't join. And if during their tenure they find something more interesting to do, we bid them farewell and wish them well."Li Xiaoqiong said, "Firstly, idealists are not only Ma. Secondly, culture is not exclusive to Ma. Thirdly, our culture extends beyond today." She explained that Alibaba's corporate culture wasn't built overnight but through each person having a bit of 'Ali flavor.' Through governance and legal means, the culture created by Ma will continue. In essence, even without Ma, Alibaba can maintain its essence and continue moving forward.Changyang and Li Xiaoqiong discussed how Alibaba will maintain its character from the perspective of employees and organizational structure. Subsequently, Shuang'er Shen Qi introduced the martial arts culture, ant culture, and upside-down culture that define Alibaba’s culture. Martial arts culture emphasizes equality, ant culture emphasizes responsibility and diligence, and upside-down culture emphasizes innovative value.Shen Qi revealed Alibaba's internal innovation system:Here is a brief on administrative orders regarding innovation. First, personal growth. Back in 2005, during our second entrepreneurial phase, each employee was tasked with writing a daily diary named after their own growth. These diaries had to be posted on Taobao's internal network. The HR department had a task to check whether your diary was completed. This process of growth was quite engaging, and summaries of these diaries often revealed many innovative ideas within Taobao.Second, weekly innovation competitions. Around 2007, as the Taobao platform grew larger with more products, we lacked innovation ideas. Employees were asked to submit weekly reports containing their innovations. There were competitions between departments, culminating in an innovation award. Back then, a common question among colleagues was, "Have you written your weekly report? What's your innovation?" making it a popular phrase.Third, by 2008, the horse-racing mechanism was implemented and continued until last year. This mechanism meant that any Taobao employee with ideas to adjust current products or services could initiate a project, request resources from the company, and form a project team to execute it. Of course, the company would conduct reviews and approvals to ensure feasibility. One of the most successful projects was Juhuasuan, which became an independent company after succeeding in the 2009 horse race.In Kevin Kelly's book "Out of Control," the second chapter uses bee colonies as an analogy to summarize four characteristics of distributed systems, which also aptly describe Alibaba's goal of becoming an ecological organization:No mandatory central control;Sub-units possess autonomous traits;Sub-units are highly interconnected;Point-to-point influences form non-linear causal relationships through networks.Kevin Kelly also highlights the advantages of such organizations: adaptability, evolvability, resilience, infinity, and novelty. Combining Changyang's summary of Ali people, Shen Qi's explanation of cultural inheritance, and Shen Qi's description of Alibaba's culture itself, we understand that Ma's ideal organization carries a touch of "Out of Control."Above is the internal perspective provided by Alibaba employees explaining why Alibaba can continue to progress. Next, through Liang Chunxiao and Fei Qing Wang Wenbin, we discuss the external aspects of Alibaba's future development.Liang Chunxiao's speech revolved around Taobao. He mentioned that last year's Double Eleven generated a trading volume of 19.1 billion yuan, leaving a deep impression on him. This data reflects the shift in today's commercial infrastructure. He believes this change is fundamental, giving rise to new types of commercial infrastructure capabilities that traditional ones lack entirely.When we talk about the transformation of commercial infrastructure, we're not referring to tables or counters but rather to changes across the entire landscape, a transformation of the new continent. If this land beneath our feet transforms, the entire ecosystem, business model, and commercial landscape will change accordingly. So, what we felt in 2012 was indeed a transformation.Due to the transformation of commercial infrastructure, Liang Chunxiao believes that an e-commerce economy will emerge in the future. He stated that before 2000, people considered the internet a tool, but after 2000, it was recognized as a channel. As e-commerce develops, economic activities surrounding it increase, involving more industries such as retail and manufacturing. E-commerce becomes increasingly important in social, economic, and cultural contexts, hence calling it an e-commerce economy.Therefore, to determine whether an e-commerce economy will emerge in the future, we must first look at the scale to which e-commerce can grow. Liang Xiaochun shared a set of data, emphasizing that B2B e-commerce transactions would account for over 80%.In 2012, the size of the entire e-commerce economy was approximately 8.2 trillion yuan. By 2020, the scale of B2B e-commerce applications is expected to reach 33 trillion yuan, while online retail will grow from 1.3 trillion yuan in the previous year to over 10 trillion yuan. Service industry revenue will increase from around 25 billion yuan in 2012 to 4 trillion yuan.Another set of data:By 2020, the total online retail transaction amount will exceed 16.3%. The e-commerce service industry just released its annual report for last year, showing that the income from e-commerce services last year was 24.63 billion yuan, an increase of more than 70% compared to the previous year.Under the e-commerce economy, newer business models will appear, including C2B (Customer-to-Business). One of the achievements of this model is Huawei's 6.1-inch giant-screen phone, Ascend Mate. The two companies collaborated since November last year, conducting extensive user research, concluding that users need a phone with a screen larger than 5.5 inches, a quad-core processor, a large-capacity battery, and enhanced video playback capabilities.C2B is also known as consumer customization. Liang Chunxiao believes this model can transform marketing and distribution, design and production, logistics and warehousing, and raw material supply. He calls it reverse-conducted transmission because, in the past, production determined consumption, whereas in C2B, consumption determines production. The characteristics of this model include being consumer-driven, offering unique experiences through customized participation, large-scale networked collaboration based on the internet and cloud computing platforms. He also mentioned flexible manufacturing as the cornerstone of the C2B model, believing that e-commerce will promote the development of flexible manufacturing.Liang Chunxiao also believes that the development of e-commerce can stimulate domestic demand. He thinks that stimulating domestic demand involves solving two problems: first, how to enable more people to gain income through entrepreneurship and employment, thereby forming such domestic demand; second, whether there is a modern advanced distribution system that allows consumers to buy what they want when they want it. He used examples of Taobao consumers in Qingchuan, Sichuan, Xinjiang, and Inner Mongolia. Due to the lack of local availability, most of their consumption is cross-regional. In 2012, local merchants placed their specialty products on Taobao, generating an income of 70 million yuan, while the people of Qingchuan spent 40 million yuan on Taobao in one year.Liang Chunxiao shared a survey conducted by McKinsey in March this year:Their research concluded that out of every 100 yuan spent in online retail, about 61 yuan represents transferred consumption, meaning this 61 yuan would have occurred offline without the internet. Additionally, 39 yuan is consumption created by the internet, which would not have happened without it. This proportion is 39%, and according to our online retail transaction volume of 130 billion yuan last year, the additional consumption stimulated by e-commerce and online retail amounted to 50 billion yuan. This is what we call domestic demand.He also discussed other topics, such as how e-commerce can promote service industry growth and employment. In summary, Liang Chunxiao believes that e-commerce brings about a transformation in commercial infrastructure, deeply affecting every aspect of our work and life. His speech reminded me of a statement Ma Yun made this year on the Dialogue program: E-commerce is a lifestyle transformation, destroying not business models but old ways of thinking.In other words, the new era of Alibaba will serve as the infrastructure of our e-commerce economy, functioning not merely as a trading platform but integrating technological means through big data and cloud computing to become a critical force determining production and consumption. I believe this is why Liang Chunxiao emphasized in his speech that believing in the existence of the e-commerce economy requires first believing in the inevitable transition of society from an industrial to an information age.So, what is an information society?In agricultural and industrial societies, matter and energy are primary resources, focusing on large-scale material production. In an information society, information becomes more important than matter and energy. Information economic activities aimed at developing and utilizing information resources expand rapidly, gradually replacing industrial production activities to become the main content of national economic activities.If there really is an e-commerce economy in the future, then Alibaba will become the water and air of this economy, naturally having a bright future. However, whether the future will develop as envisioned by Alibaba remains uncertain.
