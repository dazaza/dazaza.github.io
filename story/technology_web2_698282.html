[Hey] NO.171(1) Buying a Wristwatch

by oiejkend on 2012-03-09 04:14:04

Cause: DUBAI is a country of diverse civilizations, with people from all over the world, as well as brands from all over the world. Here, there are the most star-rated hotels and large shopping malls (MALLs). Naturally, our leisure time is mostly spent browsing through these MALLs. After being here for half a year, I've basically visited all the major MALLs, mostly just skimming the surface, looking at the environment, decoration styles, shops, etc. Therefore, my understanding of brands has increased significantly. After visiting so many, naturally, I developed a desire to purchase something.

With the opening of the global village and the arrival of the shopping festival, I started to feel restless. Before the shopping festival, I discussed with my husband that I wanted to buy a pair of couple watches. Originally, neither my husband nor I had the habit of wearing watches; we mainly used our phones to check the time. After coming here, I made fewer friends, received fewer calls, so naturally, my phone became less necessary for me. The iPhone my husband gave me was basically unused, except for calling my family. Most of the time, it was used by my son as a gaming device. I thought if I had a watch to check the time, it would be best. My husband joked: We're not used to wearing watches, what if we spend tens of thousands of yuan on a watch and then lose it? Since I had the desire to buy a watch, I told my husband: Then let's start getting used to wearing watches; it's convenient to check the time.

Postponed for now: On the eve of the shopping festival, my husband and I discussed which watch to buy. At that time, our goal was only Tissot and Longines. My husband liked Longines more; he said he liked the name first, romantic and meaningful. Buying a pair of Longines couple watches would be perfect. I didn't know much about watches, but I looked up comparisons between these two brands online. In terms of brand, Longines was slightly better than Tissot, so naturally, the price was a bit higher. However, in terms of style, Longines' designs were relatively monotonous, some were very similar. Tissot's designs were more fashionable and diverse. Therefore, friends suggested that buying a medium-priced Longines might as well buy a better Tissot. I was still repeatedly researching which one to buy. Later, I heard from friends about where to buy: If you buy Tissot, go to Saudi Arabia, where the price is cheaper. If you buy Longines, go to Bahrain, where it’s also cheaper. Considering that my husband often travels to these two countries on business, there will be plenty of opportunities to buy later. So, naturally, the plan to buy watches was put off for now.

Positioning: On the day before the shopping festival, while we were by the lake, several family members were exchanging opinions about watches. They all had experience in purchasing watches, so naturally, I was a good listener. Listening to them made me want to get a watch sooner. But considering the price, I decided to put it off for now. During those days, there were always good news from the shopping festival. It was said that many models of watches were buy one get one free or buy two get one free. Prices here were already much cheaper than in China, and with the shopping festival discounts, it was even more cost-effective. I decided to wait until the shopping festival season to see.

Friends' impulsiveness made me excited: On the first day of the shopping festival, we met early in the morning to take the kids to the lakeside. I heard Ms. Yao say she ordered an Omega women's watch last night, and they gave her an Omega men's watch for free. I started to become interested again. First, I admired their impulsive enthusiasm for buying watches. They already had several watches, yet they bought new ones this time without mentioning any plans yesterday. This kind of impulsive consumption reminded me of myself a few years ago, but after going through these years, I wouldn’t do that anymore. I should say I would never have impulsive consumption, especially when it comes to spending nearly 50,000 yuan. While admiring and envying them, I began to listen to her talk about the purchasing process. Due to stock shortages, they had to come back the next day to pick up the watches. Tonight, I planned to go with them to take a look at the watch store.

Understanding Omega: After dinner, we went to the mall with a friend. On the first day of the shopping festival, the mall was crowded with people, and there were even some opening ceremony performances. The friend took us to several larger watch stores. Each store was packed with people, and the salespeople were busy introducing products. We tried on a few watches. The two little ones ran around crazily, so we couldn't delve deeply into understanding. Anyway, we got a rough idea of the brands, styles, and discounts. Finally, we came to a larger chain store called RIVOLI. Their watches were ordered in this store. There were several big brands in the store, and the prices of the watches were all above ten thousand. Some brands I didn't recognize, but the prices were hundreds of thousands for one watch. There was also a row of mobile phone shops. I glanced at one and was shocked; a square gold-edged mobile phone was close to a million yuan, almost equivalent to the price of a house. I thought surely not many people would buy it, but surprisingly, during the half-hour waiting period in the store, quite a few people inquired and even purchased it. I sighed inwardly that there were indeed many rich people.

Torn between buying and not buying: The next day, my friend received the watch, and it was indeed beautiful and elegant, a style I really liked. I started to hesitate whether to buy or not. There were three reasons why I wanted to buy: First, I originally wanted to buy a watch, just unsure when and which model to buy; Second, the discount prices during this shopping festival were very tempting, and I was afraid of missing this opportunity; Third, the price of Omega watches rises every year. From last year to this year, the same model rose by 20% at the same time. If I definitely wanted to buy, it would be better to buy earlier than later. Of course, there were also reasons why I didn't want to buy. These months, I had been saving money to buy a car for my husband. We had agreed to use the savings to buy a car after buying diamonds. My husband was more inclined to buy a car. Moreover, a watch costing tens of thousands of yuan still felt too expensive to me. After thinking it over, I couldn't reach a conclusion. Friends advised me not to hesitate anymore. If I waited longer, the shopping festival would be over, and popular models might be sold out. Finally, I made a decision: I would buy it. I started discussing with my husband.

Raising funds: After multiple analyses with my husband, we finally reached a consensus and started raising funds. Of course, this step could only be handled by my husband. Surprisingly, his social connections were pretty good, and within three days, the money was raised. We could find time to go select a watch. Because these days were all working hours, we decided to wait until the weekend when we had more time.

Waiting: Finally, the weekend arrived. Why did I call it "waiting"? These days, I was constantly worried that the model I liked might be out of stock. That model was indeed beautiful, and many people were buying it. When I saw it for the first time, within half an hour, five people simultaneously purchased it. During these waiting days, what I worried most about was having the money but not being able to buy the one I desired. Finally, the weekend arrived.

One-time transaction: Since I had seen and tried it before, this time I directly went to the RIVOLI store. The Chinese salesperson wasn't there, and a foreign young man served us. We told him our needs and carefully observed the counter. We found that the model I liked wasn't there. My heart started to worry. I immediately asked the young man to check the inventory, but it wasn't there either. Then, I asked him to call other stores to inquire. He said he needed to wait for others to check. During this waiting period, he took out many models for me to try on and introduced them one by one. His meaning was that if the model I liked wasn't available, I could choose another one. Since I had already compared them before, no matter how I tried them on, I still preferred that one. We urged them to check again. The other side called back saying there was only one left, and it would take two hours to deliver. Alas, I had to wait two more hours. To buy something I had long desired, I waited patiently. We exchanged phone numbers and started wandering around the mall. My husband said he would buy a wireless router. Before reaching the supermarket, we received a call from the salesperson asking us to return immediately because they found the last one in the store's inventory. I was both relieved and curious: why couldn't they find it earlier, but they could find it now? We hurried back, saw the one I liked, tried it on, and still liked it very much. The salesperson said there were only two of this model left in all of Dubai, one in another branch, and one here. So, I immediately bought it. The foreign young man joked: This one belongs to you now, and who knows who the other one will belong to? Then, we selected my husband's model. He liked a simple and generous design, with a black dial showing a mature man's charm. The two watches had a bit of a couple-watch feel. After paying, completing the procedures, and adjusting the straps, we completed the one-time transaction. Wearing the long-desired watch felt great! I hope it's not just a fleeting excitement.

January 20, 2012 evening
