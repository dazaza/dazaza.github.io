Hold your own love well.

by meimei234 on 2011-12-30 17:45:10

A woman and a man started dating. One day, the woman saw a couple on the street, and the female had a beautiful platinum ring on her hand. The woman was moved by its beauty and said to the man with an envious tone: "That platinum ring is very nice, I want to have one." The man heard it and kept it in his heart. He wanted to buy it for her but couldn't afford such a beautiful ring.

Not long after, on the woman's birthday, the man gave her a ring, wrapped in translucent oil paper. It was a paper ring. Although it wasn't as dazzling as real platinum, it was still quite pretty and substantial. The woman felt very satisfied, looking at it from all angles and feeling truly happy.

She married a wealthy man. Yes, when the woman got married, she was adorned with platinum and gold jewelry from her ears and neck, down to her arms and even her ankles, glittering with gold. She shoved the paper ring given to her by the man into a corner of the drawer. But soon after, her rich husband got into trouble because of problems with the family company. Her husband was also involved in illegal activities and was imprisoned. The woman was overwhelmed with sorrow and suddenly remembered the man who gave her the paper ring.

One day, the woman unexpectedly met the man on the street. The man was very generous and invited the woman to his house. The man was also married and lived in a rented house. The woman saw that the furnishings in the man's home were still quite austere. The man's wife served tea to the woman, and the woman noticed that the man's wife was wearing a paper ring almost identical to the one she had thrown in the drawer. When the man's wife left the living room, she could feel that the man was living happily, and so was his wife. Unlike herself, who had nothing, not even her husband who was now in jail.

Later, the woman read an article in a magazine titled "Paper Ring," and the author was none other than him. After reading the article, the woman understood everything. She quickly opened the drawer and pulled out the dusty paper ring that she had tossed aside. Carefully peeling off the oil paper, she was shocked to see a pure platinum ring. The article said that, to buy this ring, in that era when everyone was poor, he had to secretly sell his blood because the woman's birthday was approaching, and there was no time to earn or borrow money. The woman cried, tears falling on the ring. She then carefully restored the paper ring back to its original form.

From then on, the woman wore only the paper ring whether at work or off-duty. Her colleagues praised her ring for being exquisite and beautiful, creative, and asked who gave it to her. The woman couldn't help but feel melancholy and said: "Many things, you only realize their value when you've lost them."

Meeting the right person at the right time is lifelong happiness.

Meeting the wrong person at the right time is heartbreak.

Meeting the wrong person at the wrong time is absurdity.

Meeting the right person at the wrong time is a sigh.

In fact, some things are truly most precious and beautiful when you haven't obtained them. Can you clearly distinguish who is your beloved, who loves you the most, and who you will spend your life with? The one you love often hasn't chosen you; the one who loves you the most often isn't the one you love; and the one who lasts the longest isn't necessarily the one you love or the one who loves you the most, but rather the one who appears at just the right time.

Which person in someone else's life would you be?

No one intentionally changes their heart. When he loves you, he genuinely loves you, but when he doesn't love you, he genuinely doesn't anymore. When he loves you, he can't pretend not to; similarly, when he doesn't love you, he can't pretend to.

Smart people like to guess hearts. Although they always guess correctly, they lose their own hearts in the process. Foolish people like to give their hearts away. Although they're often laughed at, they gain the hearts of others.
