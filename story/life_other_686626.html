China News Service, Bangkok, March 1 -- A grenade attack occurred in the morning of March 1 in Satun Province in southern Thailand, injuring 9 people, including a 3-year-old boy.

According to reports from the local police of Satun Province quoted by the media here, two motorcyclists dressed like students threw an M26 hand grenade at a tea restaurant when they passed by the scene, injuring 9 people, including a 3-year-old child, 2 teachers and 1 policeman.

The report said that on the same day, a roadside bomb attack also occurred in Raman District of Satun Province. A pick-up truck carrying 6 soldiers was attacked, injuring 2 soldiers and damaging the pick-up truck; In Yala Province adjacent to Satun Province, a shooting incident occurred in the early hours of the morning, where an assistant village head was shot and injured by gunmen.

In Pattani Province adjacent to Satun Province, almost at the same time in the early hours of the morning, six fires broke out in six different locations in counties such as Kao Pho and Sai Buri. One of the supermarket fires in the commercial center of Pattani City occurred at around 1:00 AM. The fire lasted for nearly 20 minutes before being controlled after three fire trucks arrived at the scene. It is preliminarily estimated that this fire caused about 1 million baht in economic losses.

After examining the scene, the police said that all six fires were caused by arson, but there were no reports of casualties. After preliminary analysis, the police believed that the series of arson incidents were committed by separatist armed groups in the south, with the aim of creating unrest.
