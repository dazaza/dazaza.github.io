The planner registers the "Qianjin" trademark, specializing in recruiting beauties weighing over 200 jin (100 kg).

by xue60pwsh on 2012-03-07 16:16:09

Organizer Hu Zhi: The "Qianjin Combination" performs live on CCTV's "Avenue of Stars"

Recently, folk organizer Hu Zhi successfully registered the trademark for the "Qianjin Combination" and boldly announced to the media that this performance group specifically recruits beauties weighing over 200 jin (approximately 100 kg). In an interview with journalists, he stated that he aims to create China's most "weighty" combination. Hu Zhi initiated the "Qianjin Combination" activity back in 2006, and the recent trademark registration is aimed at further promoting it, hoping that the "Qianjin Combination" will quickly upgrade. He mentioned that he is currently busy recruiting overweight beauties with professional performance skills.

To help obese girls regain confidence, the "Qianjin Combination" was formed.

Speaking about the reasons behind forming the "Qianjin Combination" in 2006, Hu Zhi said it was initially to assist Xiao Yang, a college student troubled by obesity. They tried every method to package and promote her, helping her find confidence. The young Xiao Yang also yearned for love, so Hu Zhi used the media to arrange a matrimonial search for her. Unexpectedly, no boyfriends were found, but many overweight girls showed up instead. These age-matched women even established a Fat Friends Club. Xiao Yang and three other girls particularly enjoyed singing and dancing, which sparked the idea of forming a group in his mind. Hu Zhi noted that many groups have striking names. After much thought, they came up with the name "Qianjin Combination." Since all four are girls, Chinese people often refer to girls as "thousand gold," and together their weight adds up to a thousand jin. Thus, they chose the homophone "Qianjin Combination."

Being overweight has brought them many inconveniences in clothing and travel, and they have faced strange looks and feelings of inferiority due to their weight. However, they remain optimistic and beautiful despite being overweight. A song titled "Fat So What" expresses the sentiments of these four overweight girls.

The "Qianjin Combination" became popular, followed by infringement issues.

Under Hu Zhi's planning, the Qianjin Combination began frequently appearing on television screens and became internet celebrities. Hu Zhi roughly estimated that the "Qianjin Combination" had been invited to perform on over 100 television stations, with various performances keeping them extremely busy, sometimes only having two or three days off in a month.

Because the Qianjin Combination became famous, many people organized overweight groups that impersonated the Qianjin Combination for performances, causing great distress for him and the Qianjin Combination. Out of necessity, Hu Zhi registered both the "Qianjin Combination" and "Qianjin Combination" trademarks, hoping to protect his and the Qianjin Combination's interests as much as possible.

"In this way, no individual or organization can use the names 'Qianjin Combination' or 'Qianjin Combination' for profit-making performances without permission. Otherwise, we will pursue legal action for infringement," Hu Zhi told reporters.

Urgent need for upgrading the combination, looking forward to professional overweight performers joining

As a grassroots-born "pure wild" broker organizer, Hu Zhi works exceptionally hard and has developed quite well. Besides arranging normal performances for the group, he also arranges distinctive artists for well-known variety shows like "Avenue of Stars," "China's Got Talent," "World's Got Talent," and more than 30 satellite TV channels across the country. Currently, he has gathered over 2,000 unique artists from around the country. Moreover, Hu Zhi gradually realized that the few girls in the "Qianjin Combination" lack artistic professionalism, necessitating the introduction of new members with professional qualities to enhance their overall skill level. Additionally, some of the old team members have gotten married, while others have lost weight... Upgrading has become urgent, though there is a requirement for weight, those under 100 kilograms will not be considered.
