1. Abstract:

With the development of the economy, university students as a special group are receiving more and more attention from all sectors of society. Meanwhile, clothing consumption, an important part of student spending, has almost become the largest expenditure after food. As members of the university campus, we are honored to have this opportunity to conduct research on university student clothing consumption.

2. Investigation Purpose and Significance:

Purpose:

(1) To timely grasp the consumption trends of university students and understand their habits regarding brand-name clothing.

(2) To guide university students in establishing correct consumption concepts.

Significance:

Through this survey activity, we deeply recognize the importance of market research. The organic combination of theory and practice allows us to truly apply theoretical knowledge to daily life. At the same time, we deeply feel the great strength of teamwork.

3. Investigation Content and Scope:

(1) This market investigation is aimed at university students on campus; therefore, we follow the principle of proximity by conducting surveys at our school and Shangqiu Normal University. A total of 50 questionnaires were distributed, using campus interception for questionnaire surveys, with a 100% recovery rate.

(2) Survey content:

1 Consumer statistics (age, gender, grade, monthly living expenses)

2 Attitude towards clothing (style, price, quality, brand)

3 Clothing purchase markets (specialty stores, department stores, online shopping)

4 Clothing brands (Anta, Li-Ning, 361 Degrees, Semir, Jeanswest, Yishion, Aiilian)

4 Market Investigation Analysis

In the market investigation, we found that modern university students have a preference for brand-name clothing. The survey showed that 80% of students indicated they would choose to buy branded clothing. The reasons include not only the better quality and unique styles of branded clothes but also many students believing that branded clothes symbolize status and taste. Some also expressed that as social individuals, they need tasteful clothes to support themselves in interpersonal interactions, which is not about competition but rather the necessity of social work. Simultaneously, they mentioned that at this age, they wouldn't massively purchase branded clothes due to price being an important factor for them. Therefore, it appears that price is the primary factor for many students when selecting clothing, with over 50% willing to accept a clothing price range of 100-200 yuan. Thus, despite various competitive mentalities spreading across university campuses, most students haven't followed blindly. Besides quality, another important factor for university students when selecting clothing is service, showing that their requirements for service attitude are continuously improving. Meanwhile, we learned that modern university students prioritize comfort in clothing types rather than just focusing on appearance. In our survey questions, 60% of students chose casual sportswear for their preferred clothing type, while only 25% pursued fashion. It's evident that casual sports attire holds significant weight in students' clothing purchases. Most students are familiar with Li-Ning, Anta, 361 Degrees, Semir, Jeanswest, Meitaisibangwei, and Aiilian. Regarding KAPPA and Jordan, these foreign brand clothes, most students indicated that apart from their expensive prices, they remain enthusiastic about domestic brands compared to foreign ones. Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd.'s "Made in China" products are known for their good quality and reasonable prices.

Regarding university students' favored brand clothing, we conducted brief interviews with their distributors. They stated that selling brand clothing isn't merely to meet adult demands, as 45% of their customers come from university students. They also offer promotions and discounts during holidays to provide students with benefits. They proposed considering students' feedback on clothing styles to continually satisfy contemporary university students' needs. They emphasized that service attitude plays a crucial role in competition and pledged continuous training for their service staff. Simultaneously, they expressed that some students have poor attitudes while shopping and hoped university students could exhibit distinct qualities. For distributors of lesser-known brands, they indicated efforts to ensure good quality at low prices to attract customers.

To summarize: Regarding university student clothing consumption, we hope everyone achieves: pursuing fashion is understandable, but buying brand clothing should be carefully considered.
