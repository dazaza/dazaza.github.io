On April 9th, it was reported that seven ministries including MIIT and National Development and Reform Commission recently jointly issued a document requiring telecom operators to accelerate the construction of fiber broadband networks, speed up fiber-to-the-home in cities and fiber-to-the-village in rural areas. It also proposed that by 2011, urban users should achieve an average broadband access capability of 8M, and rural (I think it should be rural) areas should have an average broadband access capability of 2M.

Seven Ministries Encourage Fiber Broadband Construction

The seven ministries include MIIT, NDRC, Ministry of Science and Technology, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Land and Resources, Ministry of Housing and Urban-Rural Development, and State Taxation Administration, but do not include the State Administration of Radio Film and Television.

In the "Opinions on Promoting the Construction of Fiber Broadband Networks" jointly issued by the above-mentioned ministries (hereinafter referred to as the "Opinion"), it was pointed out that we must fully recognize the importance of fiber broadband network construction and jointly promote the development of network construction.

The Opinion states that the fiber broadband industry is one of the fastest-growing industries in the current information industry with the largest development space. Promoting the construction of fiber broadband networks can upgrade network infrastructure, enhance independent innovation capabilities, drive the development of related industries, respond to the impact of the financial crisis, realize the expansion of domestic demand, ensure growth, promote employment, and even enhance the country's long-term competitiveness, all of which have important strategic significance.

Urban and Rural Users' Broadband Access Achieves 8M and 2M Respectively

The seven ministries clearly put forward the direction and goals of telecommunications operators in carrying out fiber broadband network construction, namely, adopting fiber construction for broadband networks, oriented by demand, and adhering to the principle of bringing fiber as close to users as possible, accelerating the deployment of fiber broadband access networks.

Specifically, new areas will directly deploy fiber broadband networks, while existing areas will accelerate the transformation of copper-to-fiber networks. Commercial buildings and parks with conditions will directly implement fiber-to-the-building and fiber-to-the-office; residential communities with conditions will directly implement fiber-to-the-building and fiber-to-the-home. Prioritize the use of fiber broadband methods to accelerate the construction of rural information infrastructure and promote fiber-to-the-village. Strengthen the joint construction and sharing of fiber broadband networks and effectively utilize them, actively promoting the integration of three networks. Synchronously enhance the transmission and switching capabilities of backbone networks, improve the interconnection level of backbone networks, improve network service quality, and ensure network and information security.

The short-term goal is that by 2011, the number of fiber broadband ports will exceed 80 million, the average access capability of urban users will reach more than 8 megabits per second, the average access capability of rural users will reach more than 2 megabits per second, and commercial buildings will basically achieve an access capability of more than 100 megabits per second.

Expected Investment Exceeds 150 billion yuan within 3 years

According to forecasts, investment in fiber broadband network construction will exceed 150 billion yuan within 3 years, and more than 50 million new broadband users will be added.

In the Opinion, the seven ministries require that at all levels, communication industry authorities should cooperate with urban and rural planning, land resources, and municipal departments in the construction of fiber broadband and ensure equal entry for telecom enterprises, maintaining user choice rights. The communication industry authority organizes telecom enterprises to participate in the formulation of relevant construction plans and project acceptance, and reduces repetitive construction through joint construction and sharing.

At the same time, it requires telecom operators to be market-oriented, collaborate with related enterprises in the industrial chain, leverage their respective network and technical advantages, develop characteristic services suitable for fiber broadband networks, accelerate the innovation of broadband applications, actively promote the development of three-network integration services, promote the integration of industrialization and informatization, and achieve win-win cooperation among all parties.
