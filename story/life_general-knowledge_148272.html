Top 10 astonishing discoveries of Google Earth - The Blood Lake and the ruins of the ancient city of Atlantis

by longersing on 2009-05-12 15:59:19

According to the US "Time" magazine website, Columbus and Magellan endured countless hardships when they explored the world in their time, but now people can see whatever they want from the comfort of their homes. With the use of high-definition satellite images and tools like Google Earth, everyone can conduct an impressive detailed search of the globe via the internet. Below are the ten most extraordinary discoveries made using Google Earth.

1. Discovery of the suspected site of the lost city of Atlantis - Incredible

Could these be the ruins of the mysterious lost city of Atlantis? Some explorers think so. They claim that these grid-like structures far off the coast of Africa are the roads of the sunken mysterious ancient city of Atlantis. Observers say this area is apparently as large as Wales, and if it were a site of ancient ruins, it would certainly be a standout in ancient urban planning. However, the true explanation for these grids isn't quite as exciting: engineers at Google Earth announced that these grid patterns are simply sonar lines traced by sea bed data collection vessels during data processing. While private rumors still persist, it seems highly unlikely that anyone will confirm the existence of this forgotten civilization in the near future.

2. Firefox browser-shaped crop circle - How incredible and strange!

Perhaps alien technology isn't all that mysterious after all. The source of this Firefox crop circle that appeared in Oregon's cornfields wasn't shrouded in mystery. In 2006, Linux operating system users at Oregon State University created this massive symbol, which spans over 45,000 square feet, to celebrate the Firefox browser surpassing 50 million downloads.

3. UFO landing pad? - Is it cool or not?

This is a genuine Google Earth mystery. These very strange structures can be seen above US and UK air force bases. The photo was taken at an air force base outside Norwich, UK. The UK Ministry of Defense refers to it as a motorcycle training ground, but some believe it might be a marker for satellites. To this day, no one knows exactly what it is, and the British military hasn't provided further explanations.

4. Oprah Maze - A magical place

She hosts a highly-rated major TV show, owns a magazine called "O," and has been named by Forbes as the most powerful celebrity in the world. Someone like Oprah, of course, should also have her own corn maze. In 2004, a farmer in Arizona created this design, dedicating it to the outstanding talk show host.

5. Secret Nazi Symbol - The most despicable yet incredible pattern

When workers at the Naval Amphibious Base in Coronado, Colorado designed this complex in 1967, they never imagined it would appear on satellite photos. But in 2007, Google Earth discovered that from above, four unconnected buildings formed an unpleasant symbol — the Nazi emblem. The naval base stated that they had already invested $600,000 to disguise this pattern. A spokesperson said, "We do not wish to be associated with such a hated symbol."

6. Ships lost at sea - Ancient remarkable memories

In 2003, the Bolivian cargo ship "SS Jassim" ran aground on Wingate Reef off the coast of Sudan and eventually sank beneath the waves. It is now the largest shipwreck visible on Google Earth.

7. Human face in the soil - Incredible culture

From above, this pattern in Alberta, Canada looks like a human face, but in fact, it is entirely a work of nature. What is known as the "face of the guardian of the wasteland" is actually a river valley formed by the erosion of surrounding land by water flow. Some people say that this person appears to be wearing headphones, but in reality, those are a road and an oil well. It seems that the guardian of the wasteland hasn't managed to stop human drilling activities.
