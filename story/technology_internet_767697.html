Gionee secretly builds an independent internet phone team: aims to replicate Xiaomi model

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:38:33

In less than 90 seconds, Xiaomi sold 100,000 units of its Xiaomi 3 smartphone last week, creating another "miracle." This company, which has been established for only three years, is like a Mercedes-Benz on the highway, driving fast without any rivals in front or behind.

Many people believe that the phenomenon of Xiaomi's lead seems somewhat abnormal because in China, after an industry hotspot emerges, many companies usually quickly follow suit. But why haven't there been any real "challengers"? Is Xiaomi's model really difficult to replicate?

For this question, Lu Weibing, president of Gionee Mobile, has a different perspective.

Plotting

On the afternoon of October 19th, Lu Weibing, who had just returned from a visit to the United States, revealed to a few media outlets at a teahouse in Beijing about a new project he was involved in. He disclosed that Gionee had invested in and formed a new team a year ago, and their business was to "replicate" Xiaomi's model to create a new smartphone brand.

According to Lu Weibing, the new company already has over 100 employees, with core members having experience in the internet industry. Currently, the new smartphone brand is in the process of registration, and it is expected that the first product will be released in as little as three months.

In terms of the specific operations of the new smartphone brand, this company will completely replicate Xiaomi's model. Not only will they launch an independent ROM similar to Xiaomi's MIUI, but they will also fully adopt an online sales model and establish a powerful online community.

Lu Weibing emphasized that this new smartphone brand is not a sub-brand of Gionee but will be entirely owned by another company, with Gionee merely being the provider of resources and funding for this company.

To ensure such differentiation, the company operates independently, with all aspects of design, research, and marketing unrelated to Gionee. Lu Weibing said that all this is done with the hope that the team can think and operate purely in an internet mode.

Logic

This attempt is actually another case of mimicking Xiaomi. Before this, there have already been many imitators within the industry.

Among these imitators, some chose to sell phones via e-commerce, some opted for higher hardware configurations and lower prices, while others launched phone ROMs. However, after three years, these models have not partially or entirely shaken Xiaomi's position.

Lu Weibing frankly admitted that Gionee had gone through such thought changes before and even considered launching a sub-brand to compete, but ultimately excluded all the aforementioned methods and decided to invest in a new internet team.

Lu Weibing believes that in traditional internet industries, there tends to be a winner-takes-all situation, but in industries combining hardware and branding, there will be niche markets and segmented audiences. Moreover, consumers frequently change their phones, leading to a massive market capacity explosion. "These factors combined have prompted us to decide to create a brand different from Xiaomi, drawing on Xiaomi's experiences to target different demographics and product positioning," Lu Weibing explained.

Strategy

In the current plan, the hardware configuration of this independent brand will surpass Xiaomi's phones, with unique designs, and the system software will undergo many optimizations, striving to be clean and smooth. Lu Weibing said, "We are actually considering whether we can 'counter' Xiaomi using Xiaomi's model."

He said, "MIUI is becoming increasingly 'heavy,' so we will create a clean, lighter system; Xiaomi performs averagely in design and certain functions, so we will do better in design and areas where Xiaomi falls short." "This is a direction of thought," Lu Weibing revealed that phones under the new brand will adopt the top-tier configurations, completely surpassing Xiaomi's phones.

Lu Weibing acknowledged that creating an internet-based smartphone brand is highly challenging, but he stated that they will make long-term investments in this project without setting an upper limit on investment. He added, "Xiaomi's success proves that the internet is the future trend, and companies must embrace such developmental trends."

To ensure the success of this attempt, Lu Weibing said that Gionee maintains an open attitude, hoping for industrial resource funds to come in and boost the entire brand. "If this brand succeeds, Gionee can be a minority shareholder rather than a majority one. The success of this brand is most important," he concluded.
