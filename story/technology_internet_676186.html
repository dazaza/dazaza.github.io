【Forward】Use QQ to drive traffic

by aptech_919 on 2012-02-25 20:34:59

I have been running a website for one and a half years. From the initial 200-300 daily IPs, it has now reached an average of over 2000 IPs per day. With my regular promotion in groups, IPs can sometimes reach between ten thousand to twenty thousand. Below are my experiences in running a website, which I hope to share with everyone here. These are 6 methods using QQ to increase your traffic.

At the very beginning, I was living a hard life everyday maintaining a semi-dead website. The IPs didn't increase, and I was quite anxious. I didn't know how to promote the forum to increase traffic. During that time, the forum server also had frequent problems, always being on and off intermittently. For a year, the daily IPs remained around 500. But I never gave up. The enthusiasm of youth fully manifested itself at this moment - I must persist; one day, my forum will be recognized by everyone. I kept updating the forum resources every day, praying that Baidu would index more. But things don't always go as planned. Looking at the few numbers from the search engine, and the meager income from the ad network, I was really exhausted. Just when I was at a loss, I met my current girlfriend. She is very interested in operating websites, and her enthusiasm made me regain confidence. Watching her update content tirelessly every day, the results were not obvious, and I was getting anxious. Later, I realized that just enriching the content without promoting wouldn't work. So, I started learning about website promotion, SEO, etc. One day, I saw a friend send over a webpage for Mid-Autumn Festival greetings. After looking at it, I felt it was very beautiful. A thought struck me: since it's about a week away from the Mid-Autumn Festival, if we put this webpage on our own site and send out blessings to others. If netizens pass it on from one to ten, from ten to hundred, the effect would be quite significant. Thinking is not as good as action, so we immediately divided the tasks. I was responsible for modifying the webpage to fit our website, while my girlfriend applied for QQ accounts and joined some mobile phone groups. When everything was ready, all that was left was to send out the greetings.

At 8:00 in the morning the next day, we started sending them out. At first, we just wanted to try out the effect, so after sending through three QQ accounts (each group having forty to fifty people), we stopped. Who would have known that by the afternoon, our IPs rose to over 5000. This number was truly beyond our expectations. In addition to the excitement, we didn't forget to apply for more QQ accounts. During the evening peak internet usage period, we launched another round of attacks. By midnight, I wanted to check our performance for the day. Goodness! It actually increased from the original 500+ to nearly twenty thousand, a forty-fold increase. It was simply unbelievable. Originally, increasing traffic could be done with such a simple method. Before sleeping, I sent out the greetings again to each group, so everyone would see our greetings as soon as they opened QQ in the morning. Although some groups might kick you out for sending too many, I didn't care. Thus, I persisted in sending out three times a day, until some friends got annoyed by my actions and remembered my website - this was exactly what I wanted. Those days, my traffic stayed between ten thousand to twenty thousand. But I knew this wasn't sustainable; the Mid-Autumn Festival would eventually pass, and the traffic would definitely not be as good as now. What methods could sustain this state? The groups I joined were mostly related to Dopod mobile phones, and my website was a Dopod mobile software site. I could recommend good software to them, which was a great idea and no one would oppose it. This way, they don't have to visit the forum themselves to find their favorite software, why wouldn't they be happy?

After the Mid-Autumn Festival passed several days later, the traffic gradually decreased, reaching around five thousand daily IPs. That's when I started implementing my other plan. Generally, there are more people online at night, so I began sending messages at night. When I recommended the first software, I only heard friends in the group say "How is it you again?" But when they saw that I was recommending software, they weren't as opposed anymore. Thus, I resumed my previous lifestyle for a couple of days. Sometimes, when I didn't recommend software in time, the netizens would ask why I wasn't recommending any software today. Seeing everyone starting to look forward to my arrival every day, I became more confident to continue. Later, I not only recommended software but also interacted with everyone, discussing mobile software and enthusiastically helping them solve some issues. Once the relationship with everyone heated up, they already remembered the website and would visit the forum themselves whenever needed, so I didn't need to keep recommending software to them anymore. Now, even if I do nothing every day, the traffic remains consistently above two thousand.

Through this practical promotion, I have concluded the following points to share with fellow webmasters:

1. Grasp the right timing for promotion, such as during festivals or the most attention-grabbing news at the moment.

2. Make sure to join groups relevant to your website content so that netizens will visit your site again.

3. Send festival greetings a few days in advance and grasp the timing well (9:00 AM to 11:00 AM, 3:00 PM to 5:00 PM, 8:00 PM to 10:00 PM).

4. Don't insert too many ads on the webpage you send, ideally two or three. Too many ads greatly reduce the aesthetic appeal of the webpage, affecting its forwarding effect.

5. Webmasters can actually apply for more QQ accounts. I used six QQ accounts throughout, sending messages three times a day, taking a total of over an hour. If everyone sends using ten QQ accounts, it takes less than three hours, and it's a method with very fast dissemination speed. You can try it, the effect is quite astonishing!
