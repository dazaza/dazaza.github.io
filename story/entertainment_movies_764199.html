Introduction:

Dante works as a cashier and clerk at a convenience store. On his first day off, he planned to relax comfortably but received a call from his boss asking him to return immediately. Reluctantly, he rushed back to open the shop, only for a nightmare to begin. The first challenge was dealing with an anti-smoking fanatic, followed by his girlfriend's dramatic confession of past events. Amidst the chaos, he had to attend to his friend and rival Randal from the neighboring video store, as well as a hockey battle on the roof. It turns out that life in a convenience store can be so colorful.

One-line Review:

"Just because they serve you doesn't mean they like you." A very funny look at the over-the-counter culture. "I'm not even supposed to be here today!" - Dante. This job sucks, but it delivers.

Behind-the-scenes production:

Especially the attitude of the video store clerk has a bit of the demeanor of Chinese salespeople from before. Made on a budget of just $250,000, this was the writer-director's debut work, showing remarkable observational skills and self-deprecating spirit. It’s no surprise since the director himself worked at that store for four years.

Fun Facts:

- Most of the film's investment was funded through credit cards and loans from friends and family.

- Director Kevin Smith is a comic book enthusiast who sold most of his collection to fund the movie, later buying them back when he had the money again.

- Jeff Anderson, who played Randal, had no prior acting experience.

- Kevin Smith originally decided to play Randal himself, which is why this character has some of the best lines.

- Grace Smith, who played Milk Maid, is director Kevin Smith's mother.

- Director's touch: Dante and Randal discuss which episode of "Star Wars," "The Empire Strikes Back," or "Return of the Jedi" is better. There's also a tribute to "Jaws" when Randal says, "We're going to need a bigger boat." "Jaws" is one of director Smith's favorite movies.

- Walter Flanagan, Smith's childhood friend, appears in the film in several different roles, leading Smith to call him the '1990s Lon Chaney' (the "Man of a Thousand Faces" from the silent film era).

- Kevin Smith said he got the inspiration to make this film after watching Richard Linklater's 1991 movie "Slacker."

- When Randal walks back to the video rental store, saying "Dr. Jones, no time for love!" comes from the movie "Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom." Jay standing outside the video store saying "I'll f**k anything that moves!" is taken from the movie "Blue Velvet."
