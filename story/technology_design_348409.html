The world's largest 400,000-ton ore terminal has already commenced construction.

Reporters learned on March 23 that the 400,000-ton ore terminal located in Dongjia Kou Port Area of Qingdao City, Shandong Province, had entered the construction phase and would trial-operate by the end of the year.

In early March, Chang Deshuan, President of Qingdao Port (Group) Co., Ltd., stated during his participation in the National People's Congress that Qingdao Port was conducting preliminary negotiations with Brazil's Vale S.A. regarding the development of a 400,000-ton iron ore terminal.

The Dongjia Kou Port Area Development Command Post (hereinafter referred to as the Command Post) disclosed to this reporter that Dongjia Kou Port Area would have two 400,000-ton terminals, one of which, invested in by Qingdao Port Group, had already started construction.

According to an informed person from Qingdao port shipping industry, both the Qingdao Municipal Shipping Bureau and Qingdao Port Group had contacts with Vale S.A. "Vale S.A. visited multiple coastal ports in China, and negotiations are still ongoing."

Dongjia Kou's geographical advantages

Dongjia Kou's natural deep-water port advantage is the main factor attracting Vale S.A.

Dongjia Kou Port Area is located in Poli Town, south of Qingdao City, with an average water depth of -15 meters offshore, reaching -20 meters at 1 kilometer from the shore. The port area is planned to cover 70 square kilometers, with about 29 kilometers of terminal shoreline and 112 berths. After completion, its total throughput will reach 370 million tons.

The recently approved "Qingdao Port Dongjia Kou Detailed Control Plan" positions Dongjia Kou Port Area as a "national important energy storage and transportation center and bulk dry cargo distribution center."

So far, the five leading projects entering Dongjia Kou Port Area have all achieved breakthroughs, including the two 400,000-ton ore terminals invested in by Qingdao Port Group and Singapore’s Wanbang Group, the general terminals constructed by Luneng Group and Datang Group, and Sinopec's LNG project.

Information from the Dongjia Kou Port Area Development Command Post indicates that the 400,000-ton ore terminal invested in by Qingdao Port Group has already commenced construction and will realize the transfer of ore business within Jiaozhou Bay to Dongjia Kou Port Area after completion. Another 400,000-ton ore terminal is jointly built by Singapore’s Wanbang Group and Qingdao Port Investment Construction (Group) Company.

A responsible person from the Command Post told this reporter that, up to now, the total investment of projects introduced into the port area has reached nearly 30 billion yuan, using these large projects as core businesses to drive the development of the two operation areas, forming the prototype of a national bulk dry cargo distribution center and energy storage and transportation center.

The port area adopts the "government-led, company market-oriented operations" landlord port model.

Government-led means that the Qingdao Municipal Party Committee and Municipal Government established a city-level Dongjia Kou Port Area Development Command Post, incorporating 26 functional departments such as planning, development reform, land, "one customs three inspections" into the command post members, comprehensively coordinating port area development affairs. Company market-oriented operations mean establishing Qingdao Port Investment Construction (Group) Company, through land, capital, and project operations building an investment and financing platform.

Under such a development model, Dongjia Kou Port Area uses its own natural advantages to attract various port shipping and energy enterprises.

Vale S.A. is an important target for Dongjia Kou Port Area. This global largest iron ore supplier is a regular customer of Qingdao Port. From January to February this year, Qingdao Port's iron ore import volume increased by more than 10%, and the current iron ore terminal capacity cannot meet import demands — last year, the port's import and transshipment volume of iron ore approached 100 million tons.

The newly built Dongjia Kou Port Area will take over most of Qingdao's dry bulk cargo, while the old Qingdao Port Area and Qianwan will handle container imports and exports.

Vale S.A.'s Long-Term Considerations

In Vale S.A.'s long-term plan, establishing an iron ore distribution center in Asia and creating its own iron ore transport fleet is an important move to enhance the competitiveness of its products.

Due to the vast distance between Brazil and China, much greater than the distance between Australia and China, this makes Brazilian iron ore freight rates more than double those of Australian mines.

Taking a 150,000-ton bulk carrier as an example, on March 19, the iron ore freight rate from Western Australia to Ningbo Beilun Port was $10.63/ton, while the concurrent freight rate from Tubarao, Brazil to Ningbo Beilun Port was $25.45/ton.

To reduce transportation costs, Vale S.A. is accelerating the construction of 400,000-ton bulk carriers. Compared to the original 200,000-ton ships, these ultra-large ore carriers can save 23% of transportation costs.

In 2008, Vale S.A. signed a new shipbuilding contract worth $1.6 billion with Jiangsu Rongsheng Heavy Industry Co., Ltd., under which Rongsheng Heavy Industry would build 12 ultra-large ore carriers of 400,000 tons for Vale S.A.

According to Rongsheng Heavy Industry, this is currently the largest tonnage ore carrier ordered in the world, with a total length of 360 meters, a width of 65 meters, and a depth of 30.4 meters. The first ship is expected to be delivered to Vale S.A. for use as early as January 2011, with the other ships also being delivered before the end of 2012.

The large 400,000-ton ore carriers impose requirements on the terminals they berth at. To date, China does not yet have an officially operational 400,000-ton ore terminal. The 400,000-ton terminal being vigorously constructed in Dongjia Kou Port Area of Qingdao extends an olive branch to Vale S.A.

Qingdao Port also faces competition from other domestic competitors. Zhanjiang Port Group Chairman Zheng Riqiang stated during this year's National "Two Sessions" that Zhanjiang Port would also begin constructing a 400,000-ton terminal this year to welcome large Vale S.A. ships into the port.

At the beginning of this year, Vale S.A. expressed through its Chinese public relations company, "It is studying the construction of iron ore distribution centers in Asia (including China)." These distribution centers act like "virtual mines," improving delivery times and meeting customers' special needs, and "helping overcome the obstacles caused by the long distance between Brazilian mines and Asia."

The latest progress in the new round of iron ore price negotiations is that Vale S.A. has reached a preliminary agreement with Japanese steel manufacturers, proposing to replace long-term price agreements based on annual contracts with short-term contracts linked to the spot market.

Chinese steel manufacturers are concerned that if Vale S.A. establishes an iron ore distribution center in China, it will further control spot market prices. Some Chinese steel plants have suggested to the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology to ban iron ore miners from establishing distribution centers in China.
