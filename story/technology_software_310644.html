The first leader of Manchester United's defense has seen his value skyrocket by four times, while a Barcelona mainstay was merely his substitute last year.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-16 12:31:45

The first leader of Manchester United's defense has quadrupled in value, while Barcelona's mainstay was only his substitute last year. According to Sina Sports, in the 110th league start, Vidic finally scored his 9th league goal for Manchester United. Back in January this year, Vidic had continuously scored in the league. After such a long period of no goals in the league, he finally ended the season with a strong finish. This goal may be a symbol, indicating that the Serbian's status in Manchester United's backfield is undoubtedly the first, with no one able to surpass him.

Including cup competitions, Vidic's last goal was against Inter Milan in the Champions League round of 16, where he powerfully headed the ball past Vieira's defense. Today against Wolves, it was a similar move - a powerful header from the front post, full of dominance. Despite the average angle, due to its immense force, even though Hanemann touched the ball, it was futile. This was Vidic's fifth shot on goal this season, and he finally reaped the rewards. This goal once again confirmed to people: the Serbian possesses an absolute advantage in aerial duels. In fact, before Rooney scored the penalty, it was precisely because of his strong impact that forced the opposing defender into a handball foul. So in today's match, Vidic created two goals. Ferguson praised Vidic as the most lethal player in set-piece attacks after the game.

Due to frequent minor injuries this season, Vidic has only played 16 games for Manchester United so far, missing 10 games. With other defenders also having "ups and downs" in terms of injuries, Ferguson has been making large adjustments to the central defensive line. After Rio had to be absent for a long time due to back injury, if Vidic were to have any issues, it would undoubtedly greatly affect the confidence of Manchester United's defensive line. Therefore, as long as Vidic can play, the confidence in defense will skyrocket. In the game, we can see that due to Vidic's presence, young De Laet dares to break through boldly on the right wing, knowing that someone is covering the space behind him. The other center-back Carrick also plays with ease. Of course, this is not a particularly difficult match, and Vidic's commanding ability is displayed more vividly. Don't forget that he was the captain in the starting lineup for this game. Ferguson's trust in him is fully expressed by that small armband.

Recently, there have been reports that Vidic has some friction with the club. His agent stated that the club should increase Vidic's salary to £100,000 per week, but with the contract still having two and a half years left, the club won't take action so early. Even when it comes down to two years left by summer, the club might still not act. Since the management insists on renewing contracts only when they enter the final year, there is a risk of being poached by other big clubs. Although Vidic doesn't outwardly show any dissatisfaction, it's hard for him to control his free-agent broker. If a lucrative contract offer comes from Barcelona, it's hard to say Vidic wouldn't be tempted. Pique, who became an absolute starter at Barcelona, was Vidic's first substitute until last summer. A deal for the best of the best is something Barcelona would certainly be willing to pursue.

But now, regardless of the external hype, Vidic indeed focuses more on the present. Recently, he stated that he only cares about Manchester United and doesn't think about the World Cup, which is still over half a year away. He said: "I don't want to think about things too far ahead. Sometimes all you care about is your injury, so the best way is to focus on the next game and keep going. There's plenty of time before the World Cup, so currently I am fully focused on playing for Manchester United." Such statements are what any Manchester United fan would love to hear.

Before the match against Wolves, Vidic urged his teammates to secure three points. In an interview with the official website, he said: "This is our best chance to play well, score a few goals, and bring back our confidence. It's important to win immediately after losing a game. So you need to play better and make up for the mistakes made in the previous game."

Clearly, he and his teammates achieved their goal. A player like him, who maintains a strong aerial presence at both ends of the box, as long as he stays healthy and fully commits without distractions, could easily handle a team like Wolves with the MVP caliber from last season.

When Vidic joined Manchester United, his transfer fee was £7 million, already quite high for a central defender. But after these few years of refinement, according to Transfermarkt's valuation, Vidic's current worth is around €35 million, or £31.15 million, which is more than four times the original price in just four years. This data speaks volumes, and it's foreseeable that Manchester United will eventually meet his pay raise demands, putting him in the top income bracket in the Premier League. This is determined by Vidic's superior field strength and dominance.
