How to reduce the accident rate of lifting machinery safety

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 09:34:57

As one of the eight categories of special equipment, the accident rate of lifting machinery has remained high in recent years. The reasons are multifaceted, mainly including lagging standards, insufficient design foresight, backward manufacturing process level, strict quality control during installation, and inadequate management during use. To reduce the occurrence of lifting machinery safety accidents, we can start from revising relevant regulations and standards, strengthening supervision and management.

Lifting machinery is classified as one of the eight categories of special equipment according to national regulations. Whether it's the equipment itself or its usage phase, from the perspective of safety requirements, its danger is not as great compared to the other types of special equipment. However, according to the accident statistics compiled by the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine over the past few years, the accident rate of lifting machinery and the absolute number of casualties caused by accidents have consistently ranked first among the eight categories of special equipment. In 2009, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine has made the safety of lifting machinery a key focus of the annual "three action" plan, hoping to achieve the goal of reducing lifting machinery safety accidents through rectification.

Accident Causes Are Complex

Why does the accident rate of lifting machinery remain high? Through our many years of experience in conducting safety inspections on lifting machinery, we have identified several main causes:

1. Lagging Standards. The lag in standard revisions leads to the weakening of innovation design awareness in lifting machinery. The basis for China's lifting machinery design is the "Crane Design Specification" GB3811-1983, and the main design safety requirements come from the "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" GB6067-1986. These two general technical specification standards were introduced in the 1980s and played a decisive role in the standardized management of lifting machinery at that time. However, with the large-scale production and use of lifting machinery now, along with improvements in production processes and the use of new materials, these standards become inapplicable in some situations. The "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" serves as the main theoretical basis for lifting machinery inspection regulations. The new version of the inspection regulations was released in 2009 and came into effect on April 1 this year, but the revised version of the "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" on which it is based has yet to be released. If there are significant differences between the new inspection regulations and the new safety regulations, inspection agencies will find themselves at a loss when inspecting lifting machinery.

2. Insufficient Design Foresight. Some lifting machinery manufacturers fail to adequately consider the load conditions of stressed components under various usage environments when developing new products. The reliability of the hoisting mechanism braking system is a key factor determining the safety performance of the lifting machinery. Most safety accidents involving lifting machinery during use are related to the hoisting mechanism. The hoisting mechanism generally uses electromagnet mechanical brakes, and the key to reliable operation lies in the control of the electromagnet coil. The design of electrical control systems for lifting machinery has been a weak point for Chinese manufacturers for over 20 years.

3. Backward Manufacturing Process Level. There exists a certain difference between China's lifting machinery standards and European and American standards, making it difficult for Sino-foreign joint ventures producing lifting machinery to gain traction in China. The technological content of some medium and small-sized lifting machinery still needs improvement. Moreover, due to market competition, the profit margins of manufacturing enterprises are limited, and thus the funds available for new product research and development are also limited.

4. Strict Installation Quality Control. For a long time, the final acceptance inspection has been implemented for the installation of lifting machinery, with the entire installation process quality control completely dependent on the skills and technical levels of the workers. The installation process of equipment is essentially a continuation of the manufacturing process, with even worse on-site working conditions than the manufacturing stage. The manufacturing and construction specifications for lifting machinery do not clearly define standards and requirements for the process control of construction techniques, leading to low-level construction in the installation phase to a large extent. Particularly, hidden and structural defects caused by violations and insufficient construction techniques create potential hazards for accidents. Starting from April 1 this year, the new inspection regulations have been implemented, revising the final acceptance inspection of lifting machinery to supervision inspection throughout the entire installation process, providing strong guarantees for the installation quality of lifting machinery in the future.

5. Inadequate Use Management. Safety accidents occurring during use constitute the focus of all lifting machinery safety accidents, and the main reason is the inadequacy of management by the using units. This management mainly refers to the management of personnel and equipment. With the restructuring of state-owned enterprises and the development of private enterprises, it is difficult to ensure that every piece of lifting machinery is operated by qualified personnel. Additionally, the weak safety awareness of special equipment owners in private enterprises is also an important cause affecting lifting machinery safety accidents. Furthermore, equipment management includes daily inspections and planned repairs. Daily inspections mainly focus on checking the safety performance of the equipment, which is often easily overlooked. Some companies don't even arrange for the annual repair plans of lifting machinery. Violation operations are also one of the main reasons for safety accidents during the use of lifting machinery.

There are other reasons for the occurrence of safety accidents in lifting machinery, such as the usage environment, load classification, illegal or beyond qualification production of lifting machinery, etc.

Tackling the Root Cause

How to reduce the occurrence of safety accidents in lifting machinery? As lifting machinery inspectors, we believe that efforts should be made from the following aspects:

Firstly, quickly introduce (revise) general technical standards that meet the current production, installation, and usage requirements of the lifting machinery industry while fully considering international synchronization. For example, the revision of the "Safety Regulations for Lifting Machinery" should focus on the control of hoisting mechanism brakes, making full use of the auxiliary braking function of electrical braking to protect the brake.

Secondly, encourage Sino-foreign cooperation in the production of lifting machinery within China, introducing advanced technology to enhance the technical content, safety performance, and overall manufacturing level of China's lifting machinery.

Thirdly, strengthen the supervision and management of the lifting machinery industry, severely cracking down on illegal and out-of-scope production of lifting machinery.倾斜政策向高技术含量和高安全性能的起重机械生产厂家，淘汰或关停一些低技术含量、低安全性能、靠低价位进行市场竞争的企业，鼓励起重机械制造企业引进高技术人才，特别是控制方面的人才，对起重机械进行全新理念的设计。

Fourthly, strictly enforce the statutory training and assessment of operators at all levels of quality supervision departments, especially reinforcing position operation management skills training within using units, emphasizing the responsibility system for enterprise safety production responsibilities, enhancing the safety awareness of the main responsible persons of enterprises regarding special equipment, standardizing the operation procedures of operators, improving the safety awareness of employees engaged in special operations in enterprises, and promoting the implementation of daily inspection management modes for lifting machinery using units.

Fifthly, due to the long-term practice of supervisory inspections being conducted after completion, supervisory inspections during the installation process according to the new inspection regulations require all inspection personnel to change their mindset and improve their professional skills. Theoretically, they need to strengthen the study and understanding of the inspection regulations, understand the weak links in the manufacturing and installation process, thereby formulating practical and feasible inspection operation guidance documents to ensure inspection quality. On this basis, during regular inspections in the future for previously installed and used lifting machinery, in addition to routine inspections according to the inspection regulations, targeted checks should be conducted on links prone to safety hazards and defects during installation and manufacturing, utilizing advanced detection methods to add necessary detection items and prevent accidents.

Sixthly, advocate the management of lifting machinery used in construction sites through seamless communication between supervisory inspections and supervision management, ensuring that construction lifting machinery with safety hazards shall not continue to be put into use before rectifications are completed.
