Strengthening and expanding turns into "striptease"

by qq609208771 on 2009-04-10 09:49:12

MM's tip-off: Company team-building requires a strip tease dance

As the saying goes, when you have a big forest, all kinds of birds will be there.

Nowadays, the environment isn't as good as before; many birds can no longer be seen, but "bird people" are increasing in number.

Take this case for example: A company in Yunnan, in order to enhance its market competitiveness, during an outdoor team-building exercise for employees, actually required male and female colleagues to strip down to their underwear, stand together, and wildly dance. The more exaggerated the movements, the higher the score, allowing them to enjoy a lavish "boss meal" filled with fish and meat. Those unwilling to strip and dance would receive the lowest score and could only eat the "migrant worker meal."

Had I not read it in black and white on newspaper pages, I would never have believed that there was employee training involving a strip tease dance. No wonder Ms. Yin, who recalled the 72-hour outdoor team-building exercise, felt it was a "mental and physical torment": "Some projects are still hard to accept even now."

Shockingly, the coach claimed that many of these projects were imported from abroad. To prevent being "copied," participants were required to swear not to disclose any information.

The first project: Strip tease dance

For this team-building training, Ms. Yin's company in Yunnan spared no expense, paying 1800 yuan per person for the training fee.

On the night of the training, Ms. Yin and her colleagues were taken to an isolated island at Haihe. The first project was to strip and dance, euphemistically called "freeing the soul": Men had to expose 80%, leaving only their underwear; women had to expose 60% to 70%, wearing only their bras and panties, then dancing on stage to spontaneous music. The more outrageous the moves, the better. New, fiery moves could earn an extra point.

"Most of my colleagues were young men and women, many of whom were unmarried," under everyone's watchful eyes, the quiet and reserved Ms. Yin refused to take off her clothes. At the time, her mentor was in another group. She was married but also quite conservative and didn't take off her clothes either. "Later, my mentor said she felt relieved when she saw that I hadn't stripped because at least we'd have company if we were penalized."

Ms. Yin's rejection of "freeing the soul" lowered the team's score, resulting in dinner being the lowest grade "migrant worker meal," which consisted only of plain rice, no dishes, no soup, and not enough food. The male colleagues graciously allowed the ladies to eat first. As a result, during the 72 hours, physically and mentally exhausted trainees often went hungry after completing various projects before they could eat their next meal.

Seventy minutes of hand rubbing leading to hallucinations

Another project that Ms. Yin couldn't accept was hand rubbing. Participants closed their eyes and rubbed their hands following the rhythm of the music. If they stopped, the coach's whip would come down. "I rubbed until I lost all feeling," Ms. Yin said, adding that some colleagues even developed hallucinations akin to schizophrenia. After over seventy minutes, the coach announced that they had set a new record.

Coach drives participants into Dianchi Lake

What Ms. Yin found most unforgettable was "sharing the same boat." In pairs, one person acted as a blind person, and the other as someone unable to speak. During a five or six-hour nighttime mountain trek, the non-speaking person was responsible for guiding the blind person, ensuring they suffered no harm.

"When crossing the梅花桩(méi huā zhuāng - a traditional Chinese martial arts obstacle), the non-speaking person had no choice but to let the blind person step on their feet one by one," Ms. Yin played the role of the non-speaking person and later didn't want to recall the dark, terrifying forest. After the activity, the colleague playing the blind person told her, "Apart from my wife, children, and mother, you are the closest person to me in this world. Thank you for helping me through this journey in life."

At 2 AM, upon returning from the forest, the coach did not allow the participants to rest but instead drove them into Dianchi Lake. "We kept walking into Dianchi Lake, walking and walking, until the water reached our chins," Ms. Yin said. Shorter colleagues swam back to the shore.

At 5 AM, upon returning to the shore, everyone gathered around a bonfire to dry their clothes. In the morning, a new project began... Ms. Yin recalled that during the 72-hour team-building exercise, sleep amounted to only over ten hours. Many team members had to use their rest time to practice for the projects.

Ms. Yin felt that after the concentration camp-like training, the company's team efficiency and collaborative spirit improved somewhat, and colleagues greeted each other warmly like family. "Some projects made people realize the need for mutual help and support in growth, reducing the usual suspicions and barriers," Ms. Yin still remembered the punishment for mistakes in the projects: Participants took off their shoes and exposed their feet. Those who made mistakes repeatedly stepped on their bare feet.

However, half a year later, the "energy boost" effect of the team-building exercise disappeared.

After the fervor subsided, Ms. Yin began reflecting on the scientific and rational aspects of some projects. She told the reporter that whether accepting or rejecting certain projects, their influence on her might last a lifetime, but she couldn't determine which influences were positive and which were negative.

Perhaps I am really old-fashioned, and some things are not fully understood. Training employees' willpower can be achieved through various methods such as outdoor training and military training. Why must a strip tease dance be involved? Is it about cultivating employees' thick skin or flirting skills? [Comic author: Zhu Huiqing]
