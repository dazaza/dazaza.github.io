A Comprehensive Interpretation of Chinese Classification Information Websites

by flying0663 on 2009-10-28 15:37:41

The classification information network can also be called online classified advertisements, which rely on network technology and have emerged with the rapid development of the Internet. It is a fast, convenient, and low-cost way to spread market information. Its publication form is simple, quick to release, inexpensive, easy to update quickly, and highly targeted and adaptable for small and medium-sized enterprises and individuals.

China's online classified advertisement market started in 2001, as part of portal sites. With the increase in the number of Internet users and the development of broadband, in 2004, there appeared website platforms where netizens could freely post personal classified information. In 2005, under the influence of foreign markets, China's online classified advertisement market developed rapidly, with specialized websites emerging like mushrooms after rain. Some traditional portal sites and local information ports also set up classified information channels. By the third quarter of 2006, the number of domestic independent classified information websites had exceeded 200, and including other websites' classified channels, the total was about 3,000.

The classification information network, with its unique dissemination method and low publishing cost, has been quickly accepted by many domestic enterprises, gradually evolving into the best channel for hard advertising.

Currently, China's classified information websites can roughly be divided into the following three categories:

Firstly, the classified channels of portal sites: The three major portals - Sina, Sohu, and NetEase - were all early entrants into the classified advertisement business and have achieved good results so far. They are well-known brands with high traffic and a wide range of content. However, the purpose of setting up classified channels is to provide added-value services for netizens who often browse their websites and to increase advertising revenue, rather than specifically targeting those in need. Therefore, they sometimes struggle to meet user needs in terms of information volume and matching.

Secondly, nationwide classified information websites: These websites have received various investments, excel in network technology, understand successful foreign experiences, and have been established based on foreign operating models. They currently represent the mainstream of China's online classified information advertisements. Focused on the construction of the network classification market, they are full of creative enthusiasm and motivation, operate efficiently, and stay close to market changes. Xiangyue 1998 (http://www.1998.cn) is a typical representative of this type of website. 1998 Information Port is one of the most professional classified information websites in China, providing free information publishing platforms for large, medium, and small enterprises as well as individuals across the country. The content covers real estate, automobiles, food and entertainment, sports and fitness, life services, business travel, personal job hunting, business intelligence, and more, including sections such as Tips, Local Guide, and Life Community. It enjoys high recognition domestically and has strong regional influence.

Thirdly, regional classified information websites in various areas: Although these websites can comprehensively provide information for a specific area, their limitation lies in the restricted group of publishers and users.

From the above, we can see that the online classified information market has great potential, and developing this market requires operators to have substantial financial support, especially since most current online classified information publications are free. Whether to focus on a specific industry or go for a comprehensive classification, whether to concentrate on a particular region or build a national three-dimensional market, different operators have different answers. However, through research on these domestic classified websites, we find that cultivating user habits, expanding service models, strengthening integrity management, and seizing technical and market opportunities are the commonalities among classified information websites. Under this, they have formed different development characteristics such as combining with print media classified advertisements, developing chain and alliance systems, increasing user reviews, and introducing search and map technologies. The true meaning behind all these is "people-oriented."

"Being people-oriented" emphasizes human wisdom and strength and highlights the dominant role of humans in the Internet. In this context, classification information websites should also fundamentally aim to truly meet user needs. Currently, many classification information websites place great importance on building trust mechanisms, such as the previously mentioned 1998 Information Port (http://www.1998.cn), which has taken a series of measures in this regard: carefully reviewing every piece of information to fundamentally eliminate duplicate, false, and illegal information, ensuring that the information presented to netizens is clean, true, and fresh.

As the domestic information industry matures and competition intensifies among numerous players, out of the over 3,000 information distribution networks, who will endure the long night? Who will become the ultimate ruler of the "Internet Jungle"? Let's wait and see.
