Youth is the grave of quilts, a bright sorrow

Many mornings and afternoons have seen youth buried in quilts.

--Preface

Time disappears in the gap between looking up and down. The lead-gray sky outside the window, the scattered clouds, the flowing stars, and the heavy night have all settled into either elegant or melancholy history.

The year 2010 quietly passed away, and 2011 followed closely behind.

In the transition between old and new, I heard the wind of January dance into a disordered youth, as well as the rain on cold winter nights in the north, which was extremely bleak and desolate, soaking the "nest" we snuggled in. It's often at these times that I feel time has flown back and forth, making me feel heartache to the point of wanting to cry, crying for the youth buried under the quilt.

Every time the alarm clock sounds in the tranquil space, it feels unbearably harsh, and I wish I could grab it and throw it out, then continue dreaming sweetly wrapped in my quilt. Thus, I suddenly realize that the "perseverance" we often talk about from elementary school to now is so fragile, shattered under the strange sky, scattering the ground with sorrow.

Countless dawning mornings have seen diligent classmates already standing in the biting wind, forming a breathtaking scene. Their hands holding books are stronger and more energetic than those hiding under the quilt; their faces braving the wind are healthier and darker than those covered by quilts; their hearts beating are more passionate and active than those buried under the quilt...

Countless sunny afternoons have seen hardworking classmates already erecting astonishing statues between a table and a chair. The pens in their hands are as sharp as knives and swords, their smiles as beautiful as flowers in early spring, and their aspirations higher than the sky and deeper than the sea...

—This is the true meaning of youth.

At the moment of realization, the sky displays stunning clouds. But the wind stays there, guarding the tear squeezed out from my painful memory, swaying like a flower.

So I begin to learn to mourn those flickering landscapes in memories, and those vaguely disappearing youths, trying to rebury them again, setting up an opulent tombstone to commemorate all my losses.

Yes, I've lost too much, so much that when I open my eyes, all I see is heart-rending sights.

Before, I was doing everything possible to take care of everything; now, I've decided to disregard everything to take care of everything. When I suddenly look up in the crowd, I feel my hair being blown messy and deeply burying my eyes. My thin clothes let the cold seep through, my smile starts to become sad and restrained... I stand on a known end and another unknown start.

I feel like myself is like a young princess, dressed in a splendid robe, standing on the cliff singing and crying. My soul drifts uncertainly. The happiness in the twists and turns shatters into a pile of glass.

Sometimes I would think about where youth has docked

In the age of brilliant sunshine they once flowed quietly

Now when I think of them again

I don't know if they still bloom

I once read such a passage in a magazine: "In the peaceful era of music and dance, youth grows old in one generation after another, and also grows up in one generation after another. Looking back, it's not just about fine clothes and love, not just about study and fashion, not just about blood and revolution, not just about struggle and ideals, not just about heroes and dedication."

It turns out that some things are really unintentionally incomplete, and some people are really unexpectedly destined... No matter what kind of body God gives me, I have staged eighteen years of joy and sorrow.

As Chagas said, "We always wade alone in a river of youth, and when we suddenly want to look back at the road we've walked, we find that when we look back, even more youth has silently slipped away from our sides."

Half of our youth has already been buried in quilts, it's time to say goodbye to the quilt. Because we are crowned by time with a warm name like a blooming flower - young people. So even if we are sad enough to shed tears, youth is still a requiem mixed with rock 'n' roll flavor. We are not yet at the age where we only have memories left; we can still write happiness into a long-flowing story.
