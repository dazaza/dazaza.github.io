The content on the Internet still lacks personalization.

by baidu123456 on 2007-06-26 16:18:57

On April 24, 2007, which was considered by the outside world as a somewhat belated date, John Doerr personally arrived in China to announce the establishment of the KPCB venture capital fund. Faced with media inquiries about whether KPCB was too late to enter China, he did not think so and joked that "we are coming at just the right time," because he had found two most suitable partners, Joe Zhou and Tina Tu. Therefore, when the media asked about the strategy in China, he always invited Joe and Tina to answer, "because they know more about the situation of the Chinese market."

Recently, John Doerr exclusively interviewed with Sina Tech. In this brief interview, he talked about his views on the development of American Internet companies. He said that the content provided by American Internet companies is still not personalized or humanized enough. The following is the full text of this exclusive interview.

Sina Tech: How do you view the issue that foreign-funded Internet companies, with substantial capital and local start-up company salaries, have failed to succeed in China?

John Doerr: The market environment in China is very different from that in the United States, manifested in several aspects - the level of Internet penetration, the advertising market, payment systems, and regulation, etc. In some cases, if adjustments are not made according to local conditions, the so-called "American model" will not be effectively successful or even impossible in the Chinese market in the near term. Meanwhile, foreign-funded Internet companies must give their Chinese management teams sufficient autonomy to succeed in the Chinese market. However, many foreign-funded companies have not fully empowered their Chinese management teams.

Sina Tech: How do you view the phenomenon of a large number of Chinese Internet companies imitating American Internet companies?

John Doerr: The best Internet companies do not imitate; instead, they innovate. In China, they will succeed through their deep understanding of the Chinese market.
