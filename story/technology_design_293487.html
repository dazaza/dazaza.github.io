From the market situation, the market today shows a distinct "2-8 effect". Financial stocks represented by insurance and banks lead the gains, while other heavyweight stocks also perform significantly better than the overall market. However, small-cap stocks, represented by the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM), which have performed strongly recently, fell to the forefront today under the pressure of short-term profit-taking and the Shenzhen Stock Exchange's "ban" on a speculative account suspected of manipulating the stock price of Jifeng Agricultural Machinery. The future differentiation of small-cap stocks is inevitable.

In terms of news, the total assets of the Social Security Fund have reached 700 billion yuan, with 32% of funds invested in stocks. Dai Xianglong, chairman of the National Council for Social Security Fund, said in October that the total assets of China's social security fund are expected to reach 750 billion yuan by the end of the year, and 1 trillion yuan next year. Future plans still consider increasing the investment scale. The "Development Plan for the Yellow River Delta High-efficiency Ecological Economy Zone" compiled by the National Development and Reform Commission has been reviewed and approved in principle by the State Council and will be announced and implemented soon. Despite the central bank’s continuous net withdrawal of funds for eight weeks, the market's liquidity remains abundant. The central bank issued 7 billion yuan worth of three-month central bank bills through open market operations and conducted 3 billion yuan worth of 91-day positive repurchase operations. The interest rates of both remained unchanged from last week at 1.328% and 1.33%, respectively.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: When trading, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, the quantity, and the price. This order is only valid for the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. The code and abbreviation of the stock must match when placing an order. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks to be purchased must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks sold does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, telephone automatic order, computer automatic order, and remote terminal order.
