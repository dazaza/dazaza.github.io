Saying that grandmother passed away in Hong Kong a moment ago

by mjzd6713 on 2010-04-18 13:18:40

By correspondent Jiao Jian and reporter Sun Ziming -- A beautiful "encounter" led to meeting a wealthy woman. For Li Mou, there are many rich people in Taizhou, so having good luck is not impossible.

The rich woman claimed to be 29 years old, graduated from the Design Department of Zhejiang University, owned luxury cars and villas, and her parents were in business while she herself was involved in the garment export trade.

In reality, her surname was Ke, she was 42 years old, and had only a high school education. Through her boyfriend's circle, she befriended businesspeople and successfully defrauded over 1 million yuan.

Yesterday, the Jiajiang District Procuratorate of Taizhou announced that Ke was arrested on charges of fraud.

Before committing the fraud, she gave herself a pleasant-sounding name: "Wang Yebing."

During a QQ chat, Ke met Li Mou, who worked as a driver for a leader in a certain unit. They chatted very well.

One day last September, she told Li Mou via QQ that her cousin was going through a divorce and wanted him to help find a lawyer. Li Mou agreed, and they met for the first time.

Li Mou said Wang Yebing looked tired, had an average appearance, and was slightly overweight, appearing older than her claimed age of 29.

A few days later, Ke mentioned on QQ that she was very grateful to Li Mou for his help and thought he was a good person. She expressed her desire to start a relationship with him and claimed that she was in charge of domestic clothing design in Guangdong and often worked overtime, which made her look older. She hoped Li Mou wouldn't mind.

Li Mou thought that since she was involved in designing and running a big business, it made sense that she would appear older. He didn't doubt her age and felt that she was sincere, agreeing to continue their relationship.

The Bait of 250,000 Yuan

Not long after, the two started living together.

Ke frequently told Li Mou that her parents and relatives were all in business and were very wealthy.

To make her boyfriend believe her, she impersonated her mother and called Li Mou, asking about their relationship. She said both she and her daughter liked people who worked in government departments and asked Li Mou to give her his ID copy so that they could register a villa at Lanting Garden and a luxury Land Rover under his name.

In Taizhou, many wealthy families prefer to have their daughters marry people working in government departments, and giving a son-in-law a luxurious house is relatively common.

Lanting Garden is a real estate development by Greentown in Taizhou and currently one of the highest-priced properties per square meter in Taizhou. Li Mou did not notice that the "mother-in-law" was actually Ke using a voice-changing technique.

Li Mou's account received 250,000 yuan in cash from the "mother-in-law."

Throughout this period, Li Mou repeatedly requested to meet his "mother-in-law," but Ke always found excuses, such as her mother being abroad.

Double-Act Performance

One day in October last year, Li Mou held a grand banquet at a hotel to announce their marriage to a group of friends. Everyone present envied him for finding a wealthy woman.

During the banquet, Ke claimed that she was doing clothing business with her aunt in Shenzhen, her uncle was involved in real estate in Hong Kong, and she lived in a luxury villa and drove a Mercedes-Benz in Guangdong and Shenzhen.

She also said that her parents purchased the Mercury Homeware brand for more than 7 million yuan many years ago as the general agent in Taizhou, generating daily revenue of several hundred thousand yuan. Additionally, her parents operated a petroleum business and owned a gas station in Luqiao.

Upon hearing about the promising business of Mercury Homeware, Yang couple became very interested and showed strong intentions to open a store. Ke seized the opportunity and said that opening a Mercury Homeware franchise required over one million yuan, including joining fees and order costs. She offered to negotiate favorable terms with her mother for them.

Ke then impersonated her mother again and called Li Mou to inquire about Yang couple's store plans, making Li Mou believe that her "mother-in-law" was willing to help his friends because of him.

Li Mou fully trusted her and encouraged his friends to invest confidently.

Days later, Ke informed Yang couple that they needed to pay 100,000 yuan as an advance order fee, and the joining fee could be deferred. The Yang couple quickly handed over 100,000 yuan in cash and went with her to look for storefronts.

Defrauding One Million Yuan

One day in November, Ke told Yang couple that her mother had bought a villa worth 6.5 million yuan at Lanting Garden for Li Mou and was preparing to transfer it to his name. She asked which residential complex in Jiaojiang was the best.

Yang couple told her that Greentown was good, and they themselves planned to buy a property there but couldn't negotiate the price. Ke said that she intended to buy two top-tier suites at Greentown and her mother knew the boss of Greentown, so she could get a discount and book it under their names.

She even took Yang couple to see the houses, saying the negotiated price was 19,800 yuan per square meter, saving them 180,000 yuan compared to the market price. Yang immediately gave her 100,000 yuan as a deposit and 280,000 yuan for two parking spaces.

Seeing how trusting Yang couple was and how easily they paid, Ke decided to extort more money.

She said that the house required a 30% down payment of 1.29 million yuan, and the company was urging her. Believing her, Yang couple immediately transferred the money. These three transactions were done without any IOUs.

Empty-handed Spending

In late November, after Yang's wife returned from abroad, she heard that Li Mou was breaking up with his girlfriend. Knowing that she frequently lost her temper with Li Mou and wouldn't let him meet her parents, Yang couple suspected they had been scammed. They approached Li Mou, but he refused to believe his girlfriend could deceive him.

Yang couple fabricated a story about a relative causing a fatal traffic accident and needing compensation, borrowing 500,000 yuan from Ke, who gave them 400,000 yuan. Then, citing financial pressure and unwillingness to buy the Greentown property, they demanded the return of the remaining 1.37 million yuan, and she returned 100,000 yuan.

Afterward, Ke used various excuses to delay repayment, sometimes claiming her grandmother in Hong Kong had passed away and she had to go to Hong Kong, other times saying her father had died and she was handling the funeral back home.

On January 18th, Ke said the money had been transferred from Guangzhou, but when Yang went to the bank, the funds hadn't arrived. Meanwhile, Li Mou called to say his girlfriend had been kidnapped on the way to the bank.

On February 3rd, Yang couple reported the case to the Jiaojiang Public Security Bureau. On February 6th, Ke was brought back to Jiaojiang.

Ke confessed that the money she defrauded was almost entirely spent. She used nearly 900,000 yuan to buy a property registered under her son's name and 170,000 yuan to purchase a Toyota Camry for herself. The rest of the stolen money was squandered.
