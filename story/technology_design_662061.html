With Valentine's Day approaching, netizens' attention to single men and women has warmed up again. Recently, a "Top 11 Chinese Cities with the Most Male Bachelors in 2011" ranking list has become wildly popular on Weibo, with Chongqing male bachelors ranking sixth. Since the list didn't present authoritative data, it has drawn doubts from many netizens: "Are there really that many leftover men in Chongqing?" According to the general view of netizens, unmarried men over the age of 30 should be considered leftover men.

Recently, this reporter interviewed large national matchmaking websites and conducted surveys in some office buildings in the main urban areas, analyzing the current situation of leftover men in Chongqing. Among unmarried white-collar workers, those over 30 years old account for 30%. Netizen "A Brick That Loves Eating Fish" expressed: "In the past two years, people asked me to help play matchmaker for leftover women, but now there are more leftover men." In interviews, many single men sighed, "The era of leftover men has arrived."

Online and Offline

An Overabundance of Men

This reporter visited multiple office buildings in our city, surveying 236 men in industries such as investment, design, credit, energy, advertising, technology, securities, and consulting. Among them, 125 were unmarried, accounting for 52.96% of the total surveyed population, with leftover men (over 30 years old) making up 30% of the unmarried population. From the Jiayuan website, we learned that the total number of male members in Chongqing is 890,652, with 297,968 men over 30 years old, accounting for 33% of the total male membership. The number of female members is 528,951, meaning that single men outnumber single women by a ratio of 1.68:1.

From the Municipal Bureau of Statistics, according to the sixth national census data in 2010, the permanent population of our city includes 14,595,900 men and 14,250,300 women, with a gender ratio (with women as 100, the proportion of men to women) of 102.43.

Analysis: CEO of Jiayuan website Gong Haiyan stated that from ancient times to the present, there have been many "bachelor" men who couldn't find wives, while truly unsellable women remain an absolute minority. Due to factors such as irregular working hours for male white-collar workers, closed social circles, frequent business trips, lack of opportunities to meet the opposite sex, and too many opportunities to meet the opposite sex, their chances of being left behind are relatively high.

Age Structure of Male Bachelors

What is the age structure of male bachelors? According to data from the Jiayuan dating website male members, 25-30-year-olds account for 66.4%, 31-33-year-olds account for 14.28%, 34-36-year-olds account for 6.14%, and those 37 years and older account for 13.18%.

Mr. Wang Peng, who works in credit, has been single for two years at the age of 31. "When I was studying, my parents were strict, so I didn't date anyone. After starting work, I was also busy, and my ex-girlfriend broke up with me because I was too focused on my work and didn't consider her feelings." Yesterday, most of the single men aged 35 and above interviewed by this reporter believed that pressure from society, parents, and colleagues put them in an unprecedented state of marital anxiety.

Analysis: Gong Haiyan stated that the pressure of urban life is great, and many white-collar workers have no time to consider personal issues. Academics and careers occupy most of modern people's early lives, which inherently delays their marriage and romance ages. Moreover, the fast pace of modern urban life and narrow social circles mean that many white-collar workers don't have much time outside of work to make friends.

More Attractive Above 30 Years Old

"Do you think men over 30 are more attractive?" In the reporter's survey, 66.71% of single men believed they were more attractive, while 33.29% thought "not necessarily." Most single men believed it was easier to find girlfriends after the age of 30.

Mr. Liu, a customer manager at an advertising company who just turned 30 in January, said, "I'm not young anymore, but I can't marry just for the sake of marrying; I need to be responsible to both parties." He added that dating is easy, but when it comes to marriage, reality sets in. Many women believe that older men are more responsible and have a stronger sense of responsibility.

Analysis: Gong Haiyan indicated that some leftover men are perfectionists in choosing partners, setting excessively high standards for themselves. Some "leftover men" aren't from the lower rungs of the hierarchy but often from the upper echelons. However, precisely because their own conditions are good, they tend to set higher requirements for their partners, wanting someone "equal in status." Unfortunately, one cannot have everything; it's not easy to find someone who possesses virtue, intelligence, and beauty all at once.

Main Reasons: No Property or Car

In the reporter's survey, 47% of single men believed they remained single mainly because they were too introverted and honest, not knowing how to interact with the opposite sex. 34.8% believed they were left out due to lacking property or cars, 11.7% felt their average looks made them less attractive, and only 6.5% believed their economic conditions and appearance were good enough, leading to overly picky and high demands, thus remaining single.

Analysis: Gong Haiyan pointed out that the primary reason for most single men being left out is that their so-called hardware conditions don't meet women's standards.

According to sociological research, there exists a "male high-female low" gradient principle in mate selection: women generally require men to be superior in various aspects of condition and ability; whereas men prefer women's various conditions and abilities not to exceed their own. Although this is an overly materialistic standard, lacking these hardware elements indeed makes it easy to be "left behind."

Interpretation

Leftover Men Are Easier to Overlook Than Leftover Women

Where there are "leftover women," there must be "leftover men." But why does the topic of "leftover women" capture so much attention, while the existence of "leftover men" seems so dim? When searching for "leftover men" on Baidu, there are 11,700,000 results, while searching for "leftover women" yields 41,900,000 results. The frequency of "leftover women" appearing is more than three times that of "leftover men." Clearly, people pay more attention to "leftover women" and neglect the "leftover men" group.

Yesterday, CEO of Jiayuan website Gong Haiyan told the reporter: "The issue of leftover women overshadows the problem of leftover men, which is indeed the feeling of most people." A clear reason is that "leftover women" are mostly high-end white-collar workers and attract attention, while "leftover men" are often overlooked due to poor personal conditions. She analyzed that many "leftover women" are highly educated, materially and spiritually independent girls. They often express themselves openly and enjoy being selective, and their actions and words are easily magnified by media sensitive to public opinion and brought before the public, becoming hot topics. Under the influence of "age crisis," their expressions are also more urgent and more attention-grabbing than those of leftover men. These leftover women unconsciously grasp the "right to speak," attracting public attention.

Reporters Liu Min, Wang Luyao, interns He Qing, and Wang Quan contributed to this report.

Four Levels of "Leftover Men"

On the internet, the levels of leftover women like "Striving Leftovers" and "Determined Leftovers" have been wildly popular, and netizens jokingly divide leftover men into four levels:

1. Ages 31-33 can only wait patiently for opportunities, called "Outstanding Students" (You Deng Sheng).

2. Ages 34-36, successful in career and valuable like diamonds, called "Doctoral Students" (Bu Shi Sheng).

3. Ages 37 and above feel completely forced into being leftover men, respected as "Graduates" (Bi Ye Sheng).

4. Ages 45 and above are Super Santa Clauses (Sheng Dan Lao Ren).
