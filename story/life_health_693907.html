What are the common worries and concerns that postpartum mothers have?

by qingtingluo on 2012-03-06 20:02:38

Having a baby is a great joy, but for postpartum mothers, it can also be a source of great worry. What exactly are the concerns that postpartum mothers face? 1. During pregnancy, in order to meet the needs of the baby's growth and development, eating, eating, and overeating becomes a daily routine! At this time, excessive fat accumulates in the abdomen; and after childbirth, due to lack of exercise and excessive nutrition, it leads to abdominal obesity.

Generally, women gain 10-12 kilograms in weight after giving birth, which is a normal state; however, many women who did not control their diet during pregnancy end up gaining 15-20 kilograms after childbirth, making it difficult to lose weight.

2. Skin laxity: During pregnancy, the uterus grows rapidly with the development of the fetus, causing excessive expansion of the abdominal muscles and skin. The dermis is stretched or torn, resulting in loose skin on the abdomen.

3. Stretch marks: For pregnant women, stretch marks are the biggest nightmare during pregnancy. Although the mechanism behind the occurrence of stretch marks or growth marks is still unclear, some possible speculations include hormonal imbalances, rapid expansion of the skin, and degranulation of mast cells. A more accepted theory is that due to the inability of the skin tension to keep up with the volume tension, the skin tears apart, and after regeneration of dermal collagen fibers, atrophic scars form. This results in red or bluish lines and wavy stretch marks on the skin surface.

4. Dull complexion: Due to changes in female hormones within the body, pigmentation darkens. The main areas where stretch marks appear are on the abdomen because the abdominal circumference expands the most during pregnancy. Other common areas include around the breasts, inner thighs, and buttocks. Some mothers may also experience stretch marks on their buttocks, thighs, and behind the knees.

In general, postpartum mothers face concerns such as figure distortion, weight gain, skin laxity, stretch marks, and dull complexion. Warm reminder: As family and friends of the new mother, it is important to provide encouragement and support to prevent symptoms of depression.
