France's Peugeot Citroen wants to build a new cooperative enterprise with Hafei

by billyyang on 2007-06-18 19:42:41

The conservative French automaker Peugeot S.A. (PSA) has finally decided to make a big move in China. On June 7th, the newly appointed General Manager for China, Mr. Du Sen, made his first public appearance. He revealed that expanding Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën Automobile Company's production capacity and seeking new investment opportunities were his new tasks brought from France. "For three years, PSA has never stopped negotiating with Harbin Hafei Automotive Corporation." This statement by Du Sen was enough to spark much speculation.

● **Du Sen's "Second Coming"**

"Hello!" The amiable Du Sen gave an impression of being like a neighborly uncle as soon as he appeared. His formal title is Executive Vice President of the PSA Group and CEO of China Affairs. From now on, Du Sen will be the overall head of all Peugeot Citroën’s operations in China.

The China Affairs Department managed by Du Sen is located in Beijing and is a newly established department within the PSA Group. In the 12 years since the joint venture with Dongfeng, this is the first time that Peugeot Citroën has moved its China Affairs Department to China. This is a historic move; in the future, the China Affairs Department will directly report to the president. Previously, China's market status was only on par with countries like Malaysia.

"According to the group's arrangements, the Chinese market has a high degree of decision-making power," said Du Sen. In the future, Peugeot Citroën’s new car plans, investment plans, R&D plans, parts procurement, and the search for joint venture partners in China will all be handled by him. The China Department based in Paris will be dissolved.

In the Peugeot Citroën China Affairs Department, Du Sen is a newcomer, but in the Chinese automotive industry, Du Sen actually has deep roots. Du Sen is 56 years old and served as the general manager of Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën Automobile Company from 1996 to 1998. In 1998, Du Sen was transferred back to France, and in February 2007, an appointment letter sent him back to China after a decade.

In fact, Du Sen's sudden assignment to China is the most important part of the "new policies" of the new PSA Group president, Christian Streiff. This former "hundred-day CEO" of Airbus is entrusted with the hope of reviving PSA. In May of this year, he announced a series of new guidelines, among which significantly strengthening business in China was an extremely important content. Du Sen stated that Streiff places great importance on the Chinese market, "He used to come to China frequently, understands China's potential very well, and says that China will become the world's largest economy."

Numbers can illustrate the importance of the Chinese market for the revival of PSA. In 2006, PSA's profit decreased by 8 billion euros. Among vehicle sales, there was only a 43.1% increase in China.

● **Increase Production by 400,000 Units in Two Years**

As one of Du Sen's three major goals in China, Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën Automobile Company's production capacity will increase from the current 200,000 units to 600,000 units.

"The president often asks me what the Chinese automobile market will be like in ten years. My answer is that the official Chinese prediction is that the market size will reach 20 million to 30 million units in ten years," said Du Sen. How many cars PSA should occupy in such a huge expected market, Du Sen is unwilling to predict, but it is obvious that Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën needs to strengthen its production capacity.

Du Sen stated that by increasing production lines and adding production shifts, Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën's first factory's production capacity can be increased to 400,000 units. "The enhancement of the production capacity of the first factory is almost completed. It is expected to reach 400,000 units in 2008. By mid-2009, when the second factory starts operation, the production capacity will reach 600,000 units."

This is the first time PSA has disclosed Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën's production capacity enhancement plan. "The Chinese automobile market is developing so rapidly. If our development does not keep up, we will fall far behind," Du Sen clearly understands the current situation of Peugeot Citroën in China.

It is reported that although Peugeot Citroën has been in a joint venture with Dongfeng for 12 years, its sales volume in China last year was only over 200,000 units, ranking eighth or ninth domestically, far from meeting Peugeot Citroën’s requirements. According to the plan, Peugeot Citroën Group's market share in China will expand to 6%~8% (currently around 4.5%~5%).

"Peugeot Citroën's development is based on Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën, so Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën's development is crucial. We will introduce more new models to Dongfeng Peugeot Citroën to increase its sales," said Du Sen. The new model introduction plan will be determined in September this year, and currently cannot be disclosed. According to the action guideline published by PSA on May 23rd, from 2008 to 2010, PSA will launch 41 new models globally, among which 20 new models will be launched in the Chinese and South American markets combined.

● **Admitting Discussions with Hafei**

On June 7th, without any warning, Du Sen revealed a surprising piece of news: "For three years, PSA has never stopped negotiating with Hafei." Whether it is a joint venture, technology introduction, or contract manufacturing, Du Sen refused to disclose. "PSA also needs to build new factories to expand production capacity because Mr. Streiff and I know that PSA needs more production capacity and new models in the Chinese market," said Du Sen.

This is the first time Peugeot Citroën has publicly admitted to contacting Hafei. Last June, the then leader of PSA, Jean-Martin Folz, had denied rumors, saying that "in passenger cars, PSA will only have Dongfeng as its partner in China." After the major reshuffle in management, PSA's internal stance has greatly changed.

Apparently, the strategy of "only having one partner" is brewing a change. "China's industrial policy allows two joint venture partners, but PSA is still the only multinational auto company with only one partner in China. Regarding this issue, PSA has been thinking about it recently," Du Sen mysteriously said, "I don't know what will happen in the next few months."

It is reported that in 2004, Hafei and Peugeot Citroën began contact, and since then, both sides have conducted several rounds of discussions on the feasibility of cooperation, but there has been no substantial progress. Du Sen frankly admitted that during the three years of discussion, PSA has made many friends at Hafei Automotive.

On June 11th, Zhang Kanghong, vice general manager of Hafei Automobile Sales Company, said that Hafei has never given up seeking international cooperation. "From three years ago until now, considering the special domestic circumstances, both parties have never made this matter public," said Zhang Kanghong. Hafei has two conditions that can attract international giants: good production layout and better hardware conditions; strong product research and development capabilities, and strong production management capabilities.
