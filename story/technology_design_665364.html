Title: The Name Registration on the Property Certificate of the Marital Home

Double residence, growing old together - these are the common expectations of every couple about to enter the matrimonial hall. However, with the skyrocketing prices of commercial housing, the high cost of purchasing a home has turned this originally shared "happy nest" into a hurdle for couples.

This edition of the Real Estate Lecture will allow us to discuss the name registration on property certificates. Pre-marital property and marital joint property.

Special Guest Lawyer:

Pre-marital Property:

Pre-marital property refers to the property that one spouse has already obtained before marriage. Whether it is movable or immovable property, tangible or intangible property, as long as it is legally obtained, it is protected by law.

Marital Joint Property:

Marital joint property refers to the property jointly owned by both spouses during their marriage, regulated by China's Marriage Law. Both spouses have equal rights in handling jointly owned properties.

Definition of Property Rights Before and After Marriage:

"Before marriage" and "pre-marital property purchase" definition:

The confirmation of pre-marital property has two key time points: one is from "before marriage" to "after marriage"; the second is the "property purchase" time point, i.e., when is it considered a successful property purchase.

Post-marriage house purchase by parents may not necessarily be considered marital joint property.

According to the Marriage Law, if parents fund the purchase of a house for both spouses after marriage, it is considered a gift to both unless explicitly given to one party. This means that a house bought by parents after marriage is not necessarily marital joint property.

Detailed Explanation Based on Typical Cases:

Case 1:

Xiaoying and Chenliang are getting married. Not long ago, Chenliang's family bought a new house and paid a down payment of 300,000 yuan. Xiaoying is happy but also worried: the purchase contract lists only Chenliang and his father’s names, yet the remaining 180,000 yuan loan must be repaid jointly by the couple after marriage. Whose property will this house be considered?

Case 2:

Mr. Zhang, aged 45 and a business owner, gave a house as a birthday gift to his girlfriend last year and completed the transfer registration. He also had the gift agreement notarized. Unexpectedly, half a year later, Ms. Hua proposed breaking up. Can Mr. Zhang reclaim the original house?

Lawyer's Response:

1. Parents' funding before or after marriage significantly affects the recognition of marital joint property. According to Xiaoying's description, the portion funded by her future in-laws and its appreciation should be considered Chenliang's personal pre-marital property, which would belong to him in case of divorce.

In divorce proceedings, the court will rule that the house belongs to the party who purchased it, but they must compensate the other party with half of the mortgage payments and the corresponding increase in property value.

2. Mr. Zhang transferred the property to Ms. Hua, completed the transfer registration procedures, and had the gift agreement notarized. His subsequent claim to reclaim the house due to a failed relationship lacks legal basis, so he cannot recover the property.

Two Scenarios for Adding Names on the Property Certificate:

Scenario 1: Property purchased jointly after marriage:

If the property certificate currently lists only one spouse's name and the other wants to be added, both parties can bring their marriage certificate, ID, and property certificate to the real estate office, fill out an application form stating joint ownership, and process the addition of co-owners to the property certificate. Costs include mapping fees and registration fees, generally under 100 yuan.

Scenario 2: Property purchased by one spouse before marriage:

After marriage, if the spouse's name is to be added as a co-owner, it requires going through the property transaction process, either via second-hand property transfer or gifting. In such cases, taxes like deed tax, individual income tax, and business tax must be paid.
