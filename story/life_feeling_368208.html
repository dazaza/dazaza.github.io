Both sides should not say certain words in a relationship.

by ruosi6 on 2010-10-16 13:28:30

Both sides should avoid saying certain words in a relationship. In fact, when people interact with each other, it's quite normal for arguments to happen sometimes. However, we shouldn't lose our temper and say things that can make the other person very angry because of an argument. For example, he or she might say: "If I had known this would happen, I was really blind at that time. I regret it so much." The word "regret" is best not said. Why? Because none of us have the ability to predict the future. At that time, no one put a knife to your neck and forced you into this relationship. It was consensual. Love, once gone, is gone. Face it. What about regretting it afterward? Do you have the right to say that? If you dare to act, you must dare to take responsibility. Initially, it was mutual affection. You naturally came together. If it comes to the point of breaking up, it's better to part ways amicably. Don't end up being branded as a怨妇 (夫). Love is a wonderful thing. Despite its powerful onset, it may eventually fade away completely. Don't compare now with then; that's self-inflicted suffering.

For instance, "This is unfair", "It's all your fault", "I regret it". Like he or she says: "This is unfair to me." Have you ever taken the time to carefully think about the various phenomena the sky presents in a day? On your side, the sun shines brightly on the earth; on his side, clouds gather and disperse suddenly; on my side, it might be overcast with dark clouds, and soon after, it starts raining. Under the same sky, our feelings are vastly different. Things in nature cannot be treated equally, let alone humans who can never be compared with nature. There is no fairness or unfairness in love.

Therefore, these words like "This is unfair", "It's all your fault", "I regret it" should only be said once, and don't repeatedly emphasize them to draw his or her attention. That will only turn small matters into big ones and big ones into breakups.

The concept of fairness is probably similar to the idea that everything has its match.

Finally, talking about mistakes,

He or she says: "It's all your fault." We are all for love, but the methods and ways are not always appropriate, especially during critical moments when impulsive actions occur. Constantly shirking responsibilities from each other will only worsen the situation and hurt feelings. It's better to sit down and talk calmly. If this couple still loves each other, this mistake should be considered differently. He wants to know how deeply you love him; you want to know your position in his heart. Impulsiveness is the devil, remember that well.

So don't easily talk about mistakes either.
