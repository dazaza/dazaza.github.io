Six physical issues women should be aware of

by dada911119 on 2007-08-08 11:49:21

A healthy body is a woman's green pass to lifelong happiness, and you must take responsibility for your own health. Remember: knowledge is power. The more you know about physiology, the wiser your health-related decisions will be.

Question One: When should I have a gynecological examination?

On the question of "whether to focus on physical examinations," many people answer that they only participate in regular physicals organized by their workplace.

Doctors, however, say that women, especially those who have given birth, should take the initiative to have regular gynecological exams to effectively prevent the occurrence of gynecological diseases.

Western Medicine: Decline in physiological functions can easily lead to various diseases

Dr. Feng Chi from Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital explains that a woman's internal reproductive organs include the ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, and vagina, where many gynecological diseases occur. As age increases, the functions and structures of all systems and organs in the human body undergo degenerative changes.

Especially for middle-aged women over 40 who have given birth, due to the decline in ovarian function and lack of estrogen, it can lead to a variety of symptoms throughout the body. At the same time, some diseases such as cardiovascular disease, breast tumors, and gynecological tumors significantly increase in incidence.

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM): Have at least one gynecological exam per year

Dr. Wang Guowei from Beijing TCM Hospital introduces that with the improvement in people's living standards and changes in lifestyle, female tumors have shown a clear trend towards younger patients. Breast cancer, which used to appear around the age of 50, now frequently appears in young women around 35. Therefore, mothers should have at least one gynecological exam annually in addition to routine check-ups like blood pressure and blood tests.

Common Suggestions from Western and TCM:

1. Have at least one gynecological exam per year, including cervical smear, gynecological ultrasound, vaginal discharge routine examination, and gynecological internal examination.

2. During the physical exam, also consider having a breast examination in the surgery department, especially since cases of breast hyperplasia have sharply increased in recent years, people should pay more attention.

3. Simultaneously, during daily bathing, one can self-examine for lumps in the breasts through touch.

Question Two: Hormone Levels

Recently, it has been commonly said that for women, estrogen is not only the source of charm but also the root of aging. The irritability, pain, and aging associated with menopause are closely related to the levels of estrogen in the body. Once a person reaches a certain age, is it necessary to regularly test hormone levels at the hospital? Doctors suggest that as age continues to increase, the hormone levels in the human body dynamically change, and slight fluctuations are normal.

Western Medicine: Menstruation is the marker for observing hormone levels

Dr. Feng Chi from Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital explains that generally after the age of thirty, women's hormone levels will fluctuate to some extent, which is a normal phenomenon. Women at this stage do not need to worry excessively about any issues with their bodies. Meanwhile, as age continues to increase, hormone level changes become larger, especially after entering the age of forty when hormone level fluctuations increase even more. On the other hand, a single detection showing high or low hormone levels does not necessarily indicate an issue with the body; unstable situations can be regulated by the ovaries themselves.

Dr. Feng also introduces that menstruation is the marker for observing whether hormone levels are normal.

Doctor's Suggestion:

1. If menstruation is normal, it indicates that the hormone levels during this period are basically normal, and there is no need to go to the hospital to test hormone levels;

2. If a woman is approaching menopause and her menstrual flow and cycle are in a steady transition, there is no need to test hormone levels;

3. If irregular bleeding, prolonged menstruation, obesity, or lactation occurs, hormone level testing should be conducted under a doctor’s guidance.

Question Three: Identifying Diseases Through Vaginal Discharge

There is a saying: "Nine out of ten women have abnormal vaginal discharge," indicating that vaginal discharge is a unique physiological phenomenon in women. Some people mistake normal vaginal discharge for a pathological condition, feeling anxious and confused. Doctors explain that vaginal discharge, like menstruation, is a normal physiological manifestation in women. It reflects the physiological health of women but is also a sign of certain gynecological diseases.

Western Medicine: Vaginal discharge has both physiological and pathological types

Dr. Feng Chi from Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital explains that vaginal discharge is a small amount of mucus-like substance secreted by women's vaginas, which can be divided into physiological and pathological types, each with different causes and characteristics.

It is introduced that normal physiological vaginal discharge resembles white semi-transparent egg whites, being odorless and non-irritating. However, when diseases occur in the body, the nature of vaginal discharge changes.

Western Medicine Suggests:

1. Yellow vaginal discharge: Often a sign of reproductive tract infection, usually bacterial infection. Common ones include vaginitis, cervicitis, endometritis, acute or chronic pelvic inflammatory disease, etc.

2. Red vaginal discharge: Often a sign of reproductive tract bleeding. In young women, red vaginal discharge is often due to infection or trauma; in older women, besides infection, malignant tumors such as cervical cancer or endometrial cancer should also be considered.

3. Normal vaginal discharge maintains a certain viscosity. During ovulation or pregnancy, it is often thin like clear nasal mucus and can be stretched into long threads. Under normal circumstances, it becomes more viscous. If the vaginal discharge is too viscous, especially if there is an abnormal smell, it may be inflammation.

4. When vaginal discharge turns into tofu residue-like consistency, it is a typical symptom of fungal vaginitis, and patients often experience severe external genital itching.

5. Excessively thin vaginal discharge is not a good sign. If vaginal discharge is watery, early examination is recommended to rule out cancer.

6. Notably, the amount of vaginal discharge increases before and after ovulation, but if it develops an unusual smell, medical consultation is advisable.

Question Four: Unchanging Contraceptive Methods

Some media reports suggest that contraceptive methods should change with time or age. Doctors say that even if you are accustomed to your current method of contraception, you should still ask your doctor during a physical examination if it is still suitable for your current situation.

Western Medicine: Emergency contraceptives should not be used frequently

Dr. Feng Chi from Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital explains that there are currently many contraceptive methods available. In terms of categories, there are drugs, tools, surgeries, safe periods, coitus interruptus, and immunological contraception. Depending on the method of use, they can be further divided into oral, injectable, topical, subcutaneous implants, etc. Contraceptive methods also have permanent, long-term, and short-term distinctions; in terms of gender, there are male and female contraceptive methods. Each contraceptive method has its own effectiveness, along with certain drawbacks. Women of childbearing age should choose the most suitable method for themselves based on their actual situation, under the guidance of a doctor. Regarding the frequency of changing contraceptive methods, Dr. Feng explains that it is not significantly related to the duration of use. Even if the same method is used for a long time, as long as there is no physical discomfort, significant weight changes, or rashes, there is no need to change a method that has already become habitual.

Doctor's Suggestions:

1. The chosen method of contraception should be acceptable to both partners.

2. Special populations, such as those with cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, hepatitis, etc., should consult a doctor and select an appropriate method of contraception under their guidance.

3. Emergency contraception can lead to menstrual disorders and interfere with normal endocrine functions, so frequent use of emergency contraception pills is not recommended.

Question Five: Nutrition After Abortion

Friends around me who have already become mothers sometimes encounter unexpected pregnancies, whether through surgical or medication-induced abortions. Although they have experienced the hardships of childbirth, they still endure untold suffering. Mothers need comfort while also requiring proper nutritional adjustments.

Western Medicine: Reduce consumption of soup-based foods

Dr. Feng Chi from Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital explains that after an abortion, it is still necessary to make reasonable arrangements for various foods in terms of quantity, quality, and pairing to meet the body's needs for protein, carbohydrates, fat, vitamins, minerals, water, and fiber.

Western Medicine Suggests:

1. Pay attention to appropriately supplementing high-calorie, high-protein foods.

2. For women who miscarry before 10 weeks of pregnancy, while supplementing nutrition, they should avoid excessive intake of soup-based foods to prevent milk production.

3. For women who miscarry after 10 weeks of pregnancy, they should also reduce consumption of soup-based foods and avoid spicy or stimulating foods.

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM): Requires dual supplementation of qi and blood

Dr. Wang Guowei from Beijing TCM Hospital explains that according to TCM, post-abortion weakness, pallor, back pain, and easy fatigue are caused by deficiencies in both qi and blood. Excessive blood loss inevitably leads to blood deficiency, often requiring a longer time for dual supplementation of qi and blood.

TCM Suggests:

1. There are many traditional dietary therapy methods worth considering for replenishing qi and blood, such as eating Angelica sinensis lamb soup or millet porridge, which have the effects of replenishing qi and blood and nourishing the stomach.

2. Additionally, pork, animal liver, and animal blood have iron-supplementing and blood-generating effects.

3. Attention should be paid to the reasonable combination of meat and vegetables in the diet.

Question Six: Premature Menopause

Friends around me often complain about feeling depressed, irritable, and talkative before reaching the age of forty. What's going on?

Western Medicine: Irregular menstruation is a precursor

Dr. Feng Chi from Beijing Obstetrics and Gynecology Hospital explains that premature menopause is mainly due to the premature decline of ovarian function, leading to abnormally low levels of estrogen in the body.

The onset of premature menopause first manifests as irregular menstruation, varying amounts of menstrual flow, and inconsistent menstrual periods. Secondly, emotional changes such as irritability occur. Thirdly, symptoms like fatigue, dry skin, etc., appear.

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM): Significant mood changes with early menopause

Dr. Wang Guowei from Beijing TCM Hospital explains that early menopause includes: irregular menstrual cycles and reduced menstrual flow; personality changes, such as irritability, anger, and unexplained anger; sleep imbalance, daytime sweating, and night sweats.

Common Suggestions from Western and TCM:

First, adjust the mindset and learn to relieve stress. Once early menopause occurs, patients should first go to the hospital for endocrine testing to identify the cause promptly.
