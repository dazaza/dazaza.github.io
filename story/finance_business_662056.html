Schematic diagram Windshield Tiger At noon on the 4th, in the ferocious animal area of Jinan Maofeng Ridge Wildlife World, 27 tourists were surrounded by a group of tigers. The windshield of the tour bus was broken, and the tourists narrowly escaped death. The Maofeng Ridge scenic spot said that the reason for the accident was that "the tigers and tourists were too excited." However, this statement was strongly resisted by the tourists. For the same incident, the two parties provided two completely different versions of events.

Video: Real shooting of tiger attacks unprotected tourist bus resulting in multiple people trapped Source: Qilu Network "People's Livelihood Express"

[Scenic Spot Version]

Passengers took a specially made sightseeing vehicle, and the tigers bit off the sealing strip, causing the windshield to fall off.

"If it had been delayed another 5 minutes, all the old and young on our car would have been finished!" At 12:40 on the 4th, the newspaper's news hotline received a call from a citizen saying that an incident happened at the Maofeng Ridge Wildlife World! Multiple tourists were attacked by tigers, and the windshield of the sightseeing vehicle was broken. Passengers who escaped the tiger's mouth were arranged into a meeting room by the scenic spot, with their communication tools turned off, making it impossible for reporters to contact them.

At 15:00 on the 4th, reporters contacted relevant departments of the scenic spot. Staff members said that around 12:15 on the 4th, indeed such an event occurred in the ferocious animal area of the Maofeng Ridge Wildlife World. A sightseeing vehicle was surrounded by five nearly adult Bengal tigers. "However, the glass wasn't broken by the tigers hitting it; how could tigers have that much strength? They bit off the sealing strip of the front windshield, and the glass fell off."

They indicated that the sightseeing vehicles used by tourists are specially made, using double-layer sealed glass with iron railings on the windows, providing very high safety performance. "We were also surprised by this incident. We will definitely take responsibility and actively solve the problem properly."

The passengers on the vehicle stated that the sightseeing vehicle encountered obstacles at the exit, leaving the passengers stranded in the ferocious animal area for 20 minutes, and the gatekeeper abandoned his post. Reporters sought confirmation of this statement from the scenic spot and asked about the casualties of the people on the vehicle and the tigers. Staff members said that the accident is still under investigation, and it is inconvenient to release the results. No one on the vehicle was injured, and the condition of the tigers cannot be easily described.

At 18:00 on the 4th, reporters learned that the Maofeng Ridge scenic spot issued a statement claiming that the incident had been properly resolved. The tigers were in the mating season, and some tourists inside the vehicle exhibited provocative behavior. "Both the tigers and tourists were too excited." No one on the vehicle was injured, and all passengers had received satisfactory compensation.

[Tourist Version]

Ordinary minibus encountered obstacles at the exit, tigers gnawed on the vehicle and broke teeth, jumping up to smash the glass

At 20:30 on the 4th, reporters established contact with a tourist. "The Maofeng Ridge scenic spot is lying, they are shirking responsibility!"

He told reporters that at the place where the sightseeing vehicle was changed, there were specially made sightseeing vehicles, with protective covers on the tires and iron bars on the windows. However, these 27 passengers took an ordinary minibus. The scenic spot explained that the specially made vehicles were contracted out to individual operators and were not working before the Lantern Festival. At the exit of the Bengal tiger area, there was an electric door, but the gatekeeper went to eat, so the vehicle was left in front of the gate, allowing the tigers to gnaw on it. Originally, only two tigers were biting the tires, but one tiger lost a tooth due to biting on the steel rim, attracting seven or eight companions. The front windshield was smashed by the tigers jumping up!

One thing, two versions, which one is true?

This tourist provided 10 mobile phone numbers. "We signed an agreement in the meeting room, promising to keep this matter confidential from the media. But what I say is the truth! Here are the contact details of all the families. Whether what I say is correct, you can verify."

From 20:30 to 22:00, reporters verified each of these 10 numbers individually. Except for two that were turned off, the owners confirmed the incident. "I can guarantee that every word I say is true!" one tourist said.

[Doubt]

Tourists enumerate two major management issues at the scenic spot ——

Unsafe sightseeing vehicles, management personnel abandoning posts

Tourists who escaped from the tiger's mouth expressed that in this incident, the Maofeng Ridge scenic spot bears inescapable responsibility.

They believe that touring a wildlife world involves risks, and the scenic spot should provide secure sightseeing vehicles. "Special vehicles with good protective facilities were not used, and instead, we were taken into the ferocious animal pavilion in an ordinary minibus. This is simply irresponsible."

In addition, they pointed out that the absence of scenic spot staff was also a cause of this unexpected event. "If the gatekeeper had not abandoned his post, our vehicle would not have stayed in the Bengal tiger pavilion for so long. The tigers would not have had the chance to bite the tires, nor would they have broken their teeth and retaliated against us."

In response, scenic spot staff said that reaching the accident investigation result will take some time, and they will responsibly resolve the issue.

[Dialogue]

Reporters connect with tourists who survived the ordeal to recount the incident ——

"Being able to return alive is sheer luck"

Tigers pounced and bit, tires were completely destroyed

Reporter: How did the tigers initiate the attack?

Tourist One: We boarded an ordinary minibus. The vehicle was relatively new, and the windows could be pushed open easily without any external protection. In contrast, the specially made sightseeing vehicles have covered wheels and iron bars on the windows. As soon as we entered the tiger area, the tigers seemed particularly interested in us, with two tigers starting to follow and chase the vehicle from behind.

Tourist Two: Each ferocious animal pavilion in the ferocious animal area is separated. We were enthusiastically watching the Bengal tigers running. But then the tigers kept getting closer, and we started to feel a bit scared. When we reached the exit of the Bengal tiger pavilion, the gate was locked, and the tigers pounced and bit the tires, making us even more afraid.

Tourist Three: We don’t know if they were hungry, but they were biting everything. The tires were squeaking as they were bitten, and basically all four tires were ruined. The electric gate didn’t open, and the driver kept reversing the vehicle.

Reporter: Why didn’t the exit of the Bengal tiger pavilion open?

Tourist One: There’s an electric gate there, and the scenic spot said the staff had gone to eat. We couldn’t get in touch with the scenic spot, so we could only wait in front of the gate.

Tourist Four: Normally, traveling from the Bengal tiger area to the next tiger area would only take two or three minutes, but we waited for 20 minutes, and no one came to open the gate.

Reporter: What measures did the driver take?

Tourist Two: The road was very narrow. We were heading east along the road, and there was a big ditch on the left side of the road. To prevent the tigers from continuing gnawing on the tires, the driver kept reversing back and forth. Although we almost fell into the ditch several times, we were very grateful to this driver. If it weren’t for him, none of us would have survived.

Tourist Five: We moved back and forth, but we were also worried about angering the tigers. After trying for a while, the effect wasn’t great. The tigers broke their teeth and fiercely smashed the window.

Reporter: How did the windshield of the sightseeing vehicle break?

Tourist Six: I’ve never been to a wildlife world before, and today was my first time. I brought my spouse, daughter, son-in-law, and grandson. I was sitting on the last row by the edge when I saw a tiger gnawing on the tire, breaking its tooth and bleeding profusely from its mouth. It became enraged, called over seven or eight companions, jumped up and smashed the window with two paws, breaking the glass. My grandson saw the tiger and was so scared that he crawled under the seat, crying loudly. Then seven or eight tigers began fiercely attacking the vehicle, sticking their heads in trying to get inside, causing the entire vehicle to be filled with immense fear, and screams continuously arose. Twenty minutes later, the gate opened, and we narrowly escaped the tiger's mouth.

Reporter: What did you do? How did you finally escape?

Tourist Six: The driver stepped on the gas and drove forward, blocking the entrance because the gap was too narrow for the tigers to enter. Some of our tourists also called the police. After a standoff for 20 minutes, it’s said that the scenic spot staff saw it on the surveillance, and then they came to open the gate. The driver quickly drove the vehicle into the protective gate, and all 28 people managed to narrowly escape the tiger's mouth. Being able to return alive is sheer luck.
