Woman steals 20,000 worth of property after taking a close friend's keys, whom she'd known for 10 years

by zxytyzzfe on 2011-07-28 14:36:24

Baoji News (reported by Yang Ming) - "I know your face, but not your heart!" Upon discovering that the thief who stole over 20,000 yuan worth of items from her house was actually her bridesmaid from last year's wedding, Ms. Wang felt extremely betrayed. A close friend of more than 10 years had turned against her, and Ms. Wang frankly admitted she was "deeply hurt."

The night before the theft, she had a heartfelt conversation with the bridesmaid.

On the evening of August 21st, after being away for two days, Ms. Wang hurried home to her apartment in Xinhua Road district in Baoji. She found her wardrobe doors open and quickly checked her valuables. It turned out that five pieces of gold jewelry and 2,000 yuan in cash, totaling 21,000 yuan, were missing from the drawers of her bedroom vanity. Ms. Wang immediately called 110.

Soon, officers from the Zhongshan East Road Police Station of the Jintai District Public Security Bureau arrived at Ms. Wang's home to investigate the scene. "The crime scene was clean; the windows and doors were intact without any signs of forced entry. We only found several fresh fingerprints," said Officer Yao Hui. "Initially, the police considered the possibility of technical lock-picking theft, but later ruled it out through technical analysis. The only remaining possibility was an inside job."

The officers asked Ms. Wang to recall all acquaintances she had been in contact with in the two days prior to the incident. She suddenly remembered her female classmate Ma Mou. Prior to the incident, Ms. Wang had frequent interactions with Ma Mou, and they even had a heartfelt conversation the day before the theft.

When Ms. Wang mentioned Ma Mou's name, she immediately rejected the idea herself: "It can't be her. We are good friends of over ten years, we know each other well. She wouldn't do such a thing." Ms. Wang's husband also tried to exonerate Ma Mou.

Ma Mou and Ms. Wang were high school classmates and very close friends. Since Ma Mou went to work for a network company in Beijing a few years ago, they lost touch. In November last year, when Ms. Wang got married, she specifically called Ma Mou to ask her to be her bridesmaid.

However, the police officer's analysis made Ms. Wang somewhat doubtful: "Before my marriage, Ma Mou came to my house several times and knew where I kept my jewelry. Could it really be her?"

The bridesmaid claimed she committed the crime to take revenge on her ex-boyfriend.

Soon, the police's technical identification results came out. The fingerprints left at the crime scene matched Ma Mou's. Faced with irrefutable evidence, Ma Mou confessed to the crime.

It turned out that on the evening of August 20th, Ma Mou called Ms. Wang to retrieve a laptop computer she had stored at Ms. Wang's grandmother's house. They agreed to meet at Ms. Wang's grandmother's house on Changqing Road. During their meeting, Ma Mou confided in Ms. Wang about her relationship issues. Her boyfriend of ten years, with whom she planned to get married, had recently proposed a breakup due to his parents' disapproval of their relationship. At the time, Ms. Wang tried to console Ma Mou, encouraging her to think positively and inviting her to stay for dinner at her grandmother's house. However, Ma Mou insisted on leaving. While chatting, Ma Mou noticed Ms. Wang's house keys carelessly left on the sofa and secretly pocketed them. After leaving, she hailed a taxi directly to Ms. Wang's house, took the valuables, and put them in her handbag. Subsequently, Ma Mou returned to Ms. Wang's grandmother's house under some pretext and placed the keys back on the sofa.

On September 2nd, Ma Mou was led by the police to Ms. Wang's house for on-site identification. "I trusted you so much, how could you do this?" Ms. Wang's heart-wrenching cries moved everyone present.

Afterward, Ma Mou confessed that she committed the crime to take revenge on her ex-boyfriend. Due to her boyfriend breaking up with her, she developed a nihilistic attitude and wanted to make him feel guilty for life through this act. However, the police believed that Ma Mou's explanation was insufficient and merely an excuse for her actions. According to the police, Ma Mou had not been working recently.

Yesterday, when I contacted Ms. Wang, she frankly admitted that this incident had dealt her a heavy blow and caused her immense pain. But now she has moved on, believing there are still more good people in society.
