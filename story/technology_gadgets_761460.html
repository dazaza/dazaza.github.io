HTC's Chief Designer Explains Why It Once Had an "Ugly Back"

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 14:06:10

As the first industrial designer recruited by HTC and now the head of HTC's design team, Mr. Jian Zhilin and his team not only determine the appearance of the majority of HTC phones, but also control the UI interface and even functions of HTC phones.Most designers, when asked which is their most satisfactory work, surprisingly always have something to be dissatisfied with, and they believe the next one will be better. Of course, Jian Zhilin is no exception. Since there is a next one, there must be something unsatisfactory about the newly released HTC One. The only pause during the interview happened at this moment. Jian Zhilin took a sip of water, thought for a while, and said: The least satisfactory thing about the One is that there are not enough color options for the body. Considering mass production issues, I didn't have time to do more colors.A year and a half ago, Jian Zhilin and his team started working on the HTC One (then codenamed M7). At that time, HTC's flagship product ONEX had just been launched. Although the design of ONEX was simple enough and wasn't mocked for being ugly, Jian Zhilin and his team always felt that this phone lacked an element that could truly touch people's hearts. When they saw a CNC (computer numerical control) machined iron block in a colleague's hand, the design inspiration for HTC One came!Daring to challenge common sense leads to innovative designs and functionsHTC is a company that dares to challenge common sense. When designing the OneX, they asked themselves, who says you can't take photos while recording video? Thus, HTC introduced the beauty shot function that allows you to press the shutter while recording video, leading the way in photography innovation ahead of camera manufacturers. In the new One, HTC questioned three 'whys' and once again challenged the common sense we take for granted.Why can't any manufacturer make a full-metal phone? HTC simply used metal for the antenna, resulting in the excellent one-piece metal body of the One.Why does every phone only have one speaker on the front? HTC installed two speakers on the front of the phone, thus creating one of the main selling points of the new One - Boomsound-pleasing sound.Why do people have to pose still when taking photos? HTC made the phone automatically record three seconds before and after taking photos, thus introducing the fun Zoe dynamic shooting function.Metal bodies and antennas are traditionally incompatible enemies in phones; otherwise, there wouldn't have been the iPhone 4's antenna gate, the iPhone 5's obviously segmented body, or HTC's previous Hero phones having a plastic piece on the metal.You fear it less than you embrace it, and then you discover you'll love it! In Jian Zhilin's view, challenging so-called common sense is true innovation.But innovation comes at a cost. Compared to its direct competitor, Samsung GALAXY S4, HTC One has clearly received more recognition in terms of hardware design, body feel, and even software functions. However, the complex process affected production capacity and delayed the release and launch time of the product, inevitably leading some consumers to buy Samsung S4 instead of waiting for One. But Jian Zhilin insists that more consumers are willing to wait a few more days for a good product, and HTC One is worth waiting for.Once ridiculed as "ugly back"The design of HTC One has gained widespread recognition in the industry, but before that, HTC's design was often criticized, even ridiculed by consumers as "ugly back".Jian Zhilin did not avoid this issue. He frankly admitted that HTC once ignored consumer feelings when collaborating with operators. At the time, they thought the Chinese market was large, so they launched many products of different price ranges. Operators and consumers hoped to buy low-end products that looked similar to flagship products at lower prices, so many products appeared to look similar.In the future, HTC will more cautiously control the design of flagship products, and products co-developed with operators will consider operator needs more, but the premise is not to remove the unique DNA of HTC products.Of course, even with the latest products, design cannot escape the limitations of customer demands, technological levels, production costs, supply cycles, etc., and compromises must be made to varying degrees. The bottom of HTC's latest Butterfly phone has a MicroUSB slot protector, but some users have already broken their protectors. The reason for such a design at the time was that customers required the product to have certain waterproof capabilities, but Jian Zhilin said that actually there were better solutions at the time, but customers didn't quite trust the new technology.The turning point in phone design is comingWhen interviewing Jian Zhilin, I brought along three HTC phones from my collection: a Smartphone Dopod 515, an HTC Triumph with a side-sliding standing full keyboard, and an HTC-manufactured HP PDA iPAQ 1940. Starting from 2007, phones have only had large touch screens, and many people, like me, lament the homogenization of phone designs and hope to see QWERTY full keyboards, slide covers, flip covers, and rotating designs on new HTC phones.Jian Zhilin and his design team have certainly tried many things. Once, a QWERTY full keyboard for a phone could attach to the host via magnets like Apple iPad's Smartcover, and with the help of this keyboard cover, the phone could stand directly on the table. But this product was never launched because the majority of consumers have become accustomed to using virtual keyboards on large screens, making physical keyboards redundant. In the era of smartphones, software has taken over what hardware used to do, so phones have become what they are today.Samsung was the first to successfully sell large-screen phones, but competition in the large-screen phone market like Note will become fiercer. As the price of large-screen phones continues to drop, people who originally chose Note for its uniqueness may avoid this market. The demand for large-screen phones was uncovered by manufacturers; I've seen girls unable to hold the Note with one hand while making a call. Personally, Jian Zhilin believes that a slightly more manageable size is the mainstream, after all, our palms won't grow larger due to the development of smartphones.Although HTC also made a 5-inch Butterfly, the width of the Butterfly is the same as the ONEX. HTC used a larger screen but reduced the screen border, so for consumers, it hasn't increased the difficulty of use.When the screen size of a phone reaches a certain size, it will stop increasing and develop in other directions, such as glasses or watches. Jian Zhilin believes that the turning point is near!Jian Zhilin said he thinks watches will be a trend, but more likely as an accessory rather than replacing the phone itself. HTC is also trying out many other possibilities. It is certain that wearable devices will definitely be a direction. For example, when riding a bike or driving, a watch or glasses would be much more convenient than a smartphone, but currently, no company on the market has been able to produce a perfect product.Copying has no soulAs a designer, Jian Zhilin recalled that early on, HTC's design was not recognized overseas, and someone joked: if you hate someone, just buy them an HTC phone. But now it's different. HTC's design has gained worldwide recognition and has been imitated by various companies. Many counterfeit phones are even identical to HTC ones. Being copied is an honor for HTC, and whether it's Jian Zhilin or HTC CEO Zhou Yongming, when discussing the issue of counterfeiting, they both show great confidence and indifference.After all, for phones using Google's Android system, HTC has things others can't copy: a full-metal body; a unique Sense UI and Zoe function that challenges human photography conventions. These things that can't be copied are precisely the design soul of HTC phones.
