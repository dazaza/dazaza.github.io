Microsoft Chen Ziyao Incident 2001 - Early example of human flesh search, netizens sought help publicly through rewarding "community coins". In 2001, a netizen posted a photo of a beautiful woman on the Mop website and claimed that the woman was his girlfriend. However, sharp-eyed people quickly pointed out that the real identity of the woman in the photo was Chen Ziyao, Microsoft's female spokesperson, and posted most of her personal information. Since then, a true意义上的、referred to as "human flesh search" internet search action was born.

Cat Torture Incident February 2006 A fashionable middle-aged woman smiled while hugging a cute kitten, then gently stroked it before proceeding to step on the kitten's mouth and eyes with the sharp heel of her high heels. The kitten looked back in shock and despair, and eventually its head was crushed to death. This was followed by a wave of condemnation online, with some referring to her as "demoness", "cat-destroying maniac", and "scum". Anger triggered the "human flesh search", revealing that the location of the cat torture incident was at Ming Mountain Island Park in Ming Mountain Town, Luobei County, Heilongjiang Province, and the woman involved was Wang, a nurse at the county People's Hospital. Local government and police intervened in the investigation, and Wang was ultimately dismissed from her position.

Tong Xu Gate Incident 2007 A player named "Feng Ren Tou Gu Han" in the popular online game WOW (World of Warcraft) exposed online that his wife of six years had an affair with the guild master "Tong Xu" (a college student) after joining the guild due to playing World of Warcraft. They developed feelings over time in the virtual world and engaged in multiple infidelity behaviors. After "Feng Ren Tou Gu Han" revealed his wife's chat records as evidence, it caused outrage among netizens.

South China Tiger Incident October 2007 Shaanxi farmer Zhou Zhenglong claimed to have taken photos of the South China tiger in Bashan; on the same month's 12th, the Shaanxi Forestry Department held a press conference to showcase the tiger photos. Within hours, posts questioning the authenticity of the "tiger photos" appeared on the Colorless Photography Forum, followed by continuous doubts raised by netizens regarding light, shooting angle, and realistic New Year paintings. On November 15, netizen "Panzhihua xydz" stated that the tiger in the tiger photos was extremely similar to the New Year painting hung in their own home; in the following days, netizens across the country continuously reported discovering the "New Year painting tiger," which led to online discussions about the authenticity of the tiger photos, with voices accusing of forgery gradually taking the upper hand.

Death Blog Incident December 2007 Beijing white-collar female Jiang Yan jumped to her death from a building. In her well-known "death blog" written before her passing, she attributed the cause to her husband Wang Fei's infidelity and posted pictures of her husband and the third party. Three websites - Beifei's Birds, Dafuwang, and Tianya - published and reprinted posts by netizens commenting on this matter, publishing and disseminating articles that "insulted and defamed" him and his family. Netizens launched a "human flesh search," exposing Wang Fei and his family's personal information online. Wang Fei was wanted and pursued online, receiving constant threatening emails; his workplace also dismissed Wang Fei due to harassment.

Zhang Shufan Incident December 2007 In a news broadcast about purifying the network audiovisual environment on December 27 last year at 19:00, Zhang Shufan made a statement. Due to this phrase "very pornographic, very violent," videos, images, parody comics, and posts related to Zhang Shufan spread like wildfire overnight; Baidu Tieba suddenly saw the emergence of Zhang Shufan Bar and Very Pornographic Very Violent Bar; Mopu even placed screenshots of her video in prominent positions, calling for human flesh searches...

Expensive Haircut Incident March 2008 Two female college students were charged 12,000 yuan after getting a haircut at Paul International in Zhengzhou. After the news was released online, netizens initiated a "human flesh search," revealing Paul International's registration information, fixed telephone, mobile phone numbers, and car license plates, leading to gatherings outside the store with banners. Subsequently, Paul International was investigated by relevant departments in Zhengzhou.

Most Beautiful Cleaner Incident April 23, 2008 Beijing Auto Show began when a series of photos of the most beautiful cleaner at the Beijing Auto Show were released online, and Yu Yang quickly became an internet sensation with her delicate appearance, earning the title of "most beautiful cleaner." While many felt strong sympathy for her slim figure, many others were curious about her real-life identity. Recently, under the powerful force of online human flesh searches, the real identity of this young woman was exposed: Yu Yang, 19 years old, a second-year student in the management major at Beijing Modern Music Research Institute, with acting as her personal ideal, having participated in the upcoming film "Red Cliff" and "Human Affection" scheduled for release in July this year.

Liaoning Girl Incident May 2008 An online "search warrant" calling for "1.3 billion people to work together to find her" quickly spread, as a young woman provoked anger among numerous netizens for cursing the Sichuan earthquake in a video. From condemnation to searching, it took less than half a day for the police to arrest her based on clues provided by netizens. In this 4 minute and 40 second video, the woman wearing a colorful striped T-shirt was in an internet cafe, with her feet resting on the table, talking disdainfully about her views on the Sichuan earthquake and the disaster-stricken refugees, filled with intense and dirty words. She expressed a sense of schadenfreude about the earthquake. Her reasons for complaints included the lack of color on websites, all disaster reports on TV, and the mourning period preventing her from playing games.

Diebao Incident May 2008 Shortly after the Sichuan earthquake, a netizen named "Diebao" expressed her feelings on the Douban "Southern Weekly" group (equivalent to a discussion area): she experienced the earthquake for the first time and was excited, hoping the earthquake would be more intense; she learned that the earthquake caused five deaths at a certain middle school, and thought it was not enough compared to the Indonesian tsunami and the Tangshan earthquake. As the serious consequences of the earthquake were disclosed, netizens shifted from expressing empathy to attacking her, and she repeatedly tried to explain herself.
