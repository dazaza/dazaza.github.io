"The Banquet" The Secret of Advancement in the Officialdom

by jqrxass on 2009-10-01 17:57:12

Under certain circumstances, Si Xiaoyin, a female college student of the Wa ethnic group in Western Yunnan, got acquainted with Zhang Xiaofei, the CPC Liao'an Municipal Committee Deputy Secretary and Political & Legal Affairs Commission Secretary, and regarded him as her godfather. After graduation, Si Xiaoyin came to Liao'an and met Qiu Weihan, a young playwright arranged by Zhang Xiaofei for a dinner party. Another identity of Qiu Weihan was the accomplished disciple of Gu Mingfan, the CPC Liao'an Municipal Committee Secretary.

Qiu Weihan fell in love with Si Xiaoyin at first sight.

With the promotion of CPC Municipal Committee Secretary Gu Mingfan, Zhang Xiaofei and Lin Zhixia, the Standing Deputy Secretary of the CPC Municipal Committee, made an intense competition around the vacancy. Seeing that Qiu Weihan took the bait (Si Xiaoyin) but did not whisper to Gu Mingfan as he wished, Zhang Xiaofei designed a plan to push Si Xiaoyin to Lin Zhixia who loved beauties. Then one day after a dinner party, Si Xiaoyin was deceived into the room of drunk Lin Zhixia...

Dinner Party Part One

Zhang Xiaofei was a man who enjoyed the lively atmosphere, and the dinner party was just such a lively place. Therefore, participating in dinner parties was something he delighted in. In his own words, half of his life's career in politics had basically been spent at dinner parties. He also had a set of theories about dinner parties, which I had heard him elaborate on more than once at different dinner parties. According to him, the term "dinner party" did not originate today, but rather from the Song Dynasty. "Party" originally was a term used in the chess world, extended to mean "gambling, gathering, trap." The combination of "dinner" and "party" into one word was a great contribution of ancient literati to the Chinese language and Chinese culture - because there were indeed too many traps at dinner parties.

Dinner Party Part Two

I got up early Saturday morning. For this outing, yesterday afternoon I specially went to the 4S store to thoroughly check my "Sara Picasso," filled it up with gas, and then went to the supermarket to prepare all the food and supplies needed. Although Gege Four expressed that there was no need for me to spend money, when traveling with beautiful women, one should always appear generous. Past experiences have told me that good steel must be used on the cutting edge; spending money on women often brings unexpected rewards. Haven't some people said that men earn money for what? Isn't it for spending on women! Being overly meticulous sometimes saves a few pennies, but can only make you helplessly watch as the beautiful woman leaves with disdain on her face, and that kind of embarrassing situation cannot happen.

Dinner Party Part Three

I secretly rejoiced in my heart, but showed no emotion on the surface, saying: "Then there is no need, I have no urgent matters either, let's wait for him!"

"That uncle seven follow me to sit in the backyard for a while, drink a cup of tea!" I followed Si Xiaoyin along the path paved with pebbles towards He Dongpu's small quadrangle courtyard. Walking shoulder to shoulder, I realized her height was not short, I was barely taller than her by half a head. I secretly glanced at her sideways and felt that she seemed to have hardly applied any makeup, looking very natural, even the fine hairs on her face could be seen through the sunlight, making one immediately think of the poem "Lotus emerging from clear water."

Dinner Party Part Four

I bent down to observe this pot of bright pink chrysanthemums. It was evident that they belonged to the peony type, with outer petals spread flat and inner ones folded inward, the veins glossy, charming in appearance. What made it different was that its petals slightly tilted to one side, resembling a shy girl dressed modestly. I was interested in the flower name, so I asked He Dongpu: "Third Brother, its name is 'Tai Zhen Smiling,' does it have anything to do with Consort Yang?"

"You're quite right. When Yang Yuhuan became a Taoist nun, she lived in Tai Zhen Palace, later favored by Emperor Xuanzong, she was granted the name Tai Zhen. Because this flower has a unique beauty, reluctant to easily show its front, it thus got this name." "Wow, how romantic!" I praised casually, and looked up to see Si Xiaoyin standing behind He Dongpu.
