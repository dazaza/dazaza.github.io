Yahoo Wants to Terminate Cooperation with Microsoft and Consider Developing Its Own Search Technology

by geekzhang on 2013-05-08 11:59:40

On May 8, it was reported that Yahoo! has been trying to find ways to terminate its search cooperation with Microsoft but has not succeeded so far.The portal giant disclosed in a regulatory filing on Tuesday that Microsoft had extended the revenue assurance agreement related to this cooperation. The search cooperation between the two parties took effect in 2010 for a period of 10 years, and the related revenue assurance agreement has already been extended twice.A source mentioned that since CEO Marissa Mayer joined Yahoo! last year, she has been trying to terminate the contract with Microsoft. This source also said that Microsoft has expressed no intention of doing so.Due to the contractual terms, Yahoo!'s revenue per search is worse than when it operated its own search business. Unlike the previous revenue assurance plan announced by Microsoft, the renewal plan published on Tuesday is only valid domestically in the United States.Meanwhile, according to people familiar with the matter, Google expressed to Yahoo! last year that it was willing to cooperate more appropriately with Yahoo! on web pages and search ad technology. Google and Yahoo! attempted to reach a similar agreement in 2008 but were opposed by antitrust officials from the U.S. Department of Justice, leading both parties to ultimately decide against such cooperation.A Yahoo! spokesperson refused to comment on internal discussions within the company. In a statement, the spokesperson said that Microsoft is an important partner, and we will continue to cooperate to expand market share and improve profitability.A Microsoft spokesperson declined to comment on the matter. A Google spokesperson was unable to immediately respond to media interview requests regarding this issue.According to the agreement signed by both parties in 2010, Microsoft can obtain 12% of the revenue from Yahoo!'s search ads, which need to appear next to the search results on Yahoo!'s website. Due to potential challenges faced by the jointly developed search ad system initially, Microsoft needs to provide some compensation for searches conducted within Yahoo!'s site. Regulatory filings stated that the revenue assurance agreement would expire on March 31st, but Microsoft agreed on April 30th to extend it for another 12 months.Market analysts expect that Microsoft's revenue per search is between $12 million to $15 million per quarter.People familiar with the contract say that Yahoo! cannot get rid of its cooperation with Microsoft before mid-2015 because the 10-year contract stipulates that only at that point do both parties have the option to exit. There is a clause allowing Yahoo! to exit if the revenue per search drops to a certain level, but this revenue has been growing without showing any signs of decline.Insiders pointed out that if Microsoft sells Bing, Yahoo! could also terminate this cooperation. Microsoft considered doing this last year.Sources also said that if Google and Yahoo! were to cooperate, Yahoo!'s profit would increase by at least hundreds of millions of dollars.In addition, Mayer and Yahoo!'s executives are considering whether to redevelop search technology, but this strategy carries significant risks.In the first quarter of 2013, Yahoo!'s search revenue increased by 6% year-over-year, reaching $409 million, accounting for one-third of the company's total revenue. Sources said that although search revenue has improved in recent quarters, Yahoo!'s executives still consider their cooperation with Microsoft disappointing.
