The smallest unit of SEO optimization is a single page.

by seo175110 on 2012-02-15 21:17:24

Most people who study SEO are optimizing their websites according to the principles of SEO, gathering every method they think can improve their website rankings, but they fail to grasp the core of SEO optimization: SEO traffic. Therefore, we make selective mistakes in our approach and cannot truly walk on the right path of SEO. In previous articles, Beijing SEO focused on this core and provided a detailed introduction, hoping that everyone can correct their concepts, truly grasp the direction of SEO, and master SEO.

As many friends have said, mastering SEO is indeed difficult because search engine algorithms are constantly evolving, especially Baidu. To keep up with these changes, we must understand Guoping's scientific SEO theory because only by scientifically doing SEO can we fundamentally grasp each step of SEO and gain a significant advantage from the root.

It is not hard to find that many mainstream webmasters now talk about whole-site optimization when discussing SEO. This is very correct for a website's overall strategy, but most webmasters misunderstand this good direction. Many people think that SEO optimization is just optimizing the homepage, so all their work revolves around the homepage: internal links, external links, keywords, etc., all based on this purpose. This is very unreasonable for a website's long-term SEO optimization. This is also the point I want to discuss today: The smallest unit of SEO optimization is a single page, not the entire site.

This view is also repeatedly mentioned by Guoping in his blog. It is the essence of increasing SEO traffic. Based on this view, we need to understand the following three points:

1. The homepage is also just one page. Many people overly emphasize the homepage, but it is still just one page. Indeed, from the development of the website, the homepage often has the highest weight and must be optimized for SEO, but it is still just one page. According to our SEO strategy, it needs to be prioritized, but completing this single page does not mean the job is done. There are three factors affecting SEO traffic: increasing overall website indexing, improving overall website ranking, and enhancing click-through rates. So, a good homepage ranking can only bring traffic from its keywords, while content pages' keywords could potentially bring a large amount of traffic that might otherwise be lost.

2. Use content pages for long-tail keywords. Dividing the website into countless pages allows each page to target one or even multiple keywords, giving us the opportunity to focus on long-tail keywords. Previously, my SEO strategy was like this—optimizing individual pages well brought at least dozens of IPs per day, with excellent returns. I have a small site, www.1751seo.com, which currently has around 200 keywords daily.

SEO Case: Number of Long-Tail Keywords

3. Diversified external link selection. In an article about website SEO and "Qingming Scroll," I organized detailed SEO external link techniques. Considering that the smallest unit of SEO optimization is a single page, this reinforces my earlier thoughts and adds some new insights. Usually, when building external links, we focus on homepage links. With this theory as support, we can more confidently build external links for more content pages and category pages, especially anchor text construction for long-tail keywords, which is crucial for improving keyword rankings and obtaining traffic.

The idea of using a single page as the smallest unit for SEO may not be the most advanced, but it is a very important concept that we need to apply throughout the entire SEO process. It is practical and useful.
