Can lasers completely remove age spots?

The plastic surgery experts at Bidal Beauty Hospital suggest that for those with age spots, prevention and treatment should go hand in hand. To prevent cellular aging, reduce decline, and avoid age spots, one should first adjust the fat content in their diet. It is recommended to eat more fruits and vegetables, strengthen nutrition, and consume foods rich in Vitamin A, Vitamin C, cysteine, and methionine. Engaging in appropriate physical activities, avoiding unfavorable factors and prolonged sun exposure, and preventing ultraviolet and abnormal stimuli are also important.

For already formed age spots, laser spot removal can be used for treatment. When treating age spots, it is important to first determine your own condition. This includes precise diagnosis of skin tone, stratum corneum status, skin health, and skin type, leading to the design of a differentiated treatment model that integrates spot removal with overall skin improvement to ensure optimal results.

The principle behind laser removal of age spots involves the laser instrument producing a beam of high-intensity light. Different colored lasers are absorbed by different colored skin, and the laser energy causes pigment particles to disintegrate and vaporize, sealing off blood vessels. The body then absorbs these pigment particles and expels them, causing the pigmentation to fade.

Laser removal of age spots is currently an ideal technology for removing age spots. This technology uses photochemical effects, with specific wide-spectrum lasers acting on deep pigments and larger molecular pigments. The laser's high-energy burst precisely shatters stubborn pigments into smaller molecules, effectively removing deep pigments. Laser spot removal is non-invasive, leaves no scars, causes minimal pain, and does not involve chemical stimulation, making it an effective method for removing age spots.
