Relatives and friends have introduced several partners, but all relationships ended because of not having time to interact.

by kmakedz1 on 2011-07-23 17:12:55

Companies have resorted to various tactics to retain employees, reflecting the difficulties faced by many labor-intensive enterprises in southern Jiangsu. It is reported that Suzhou has a shortage of 100,000 workers this year, accounting for 33% of the city's total number of migrant workers. A significant portion of this shortage is due to employee turnover.

An employee named Xiao Li reported that his company withheld his salary when he tried to resign. "The company owes me nearly 3,000 yuan," Xiao Li said. He submitted his resignation letter on December 14th last year, and it was approved during the New Year period. Due to the lack of new employees to take over his position, Xiao Li continued working until January 12th, but he had to wait until February 15th to receive all his wages.

In response, the general manager of the company where Xiao Li works, Mr. Qian, admitted to the practice. He explained that the company adopts a two-month settlement system with a one-month delay in salary disbursement. With 400 employees, most of whom are local, there is considerable turnover. Some employees leave after only a month or two, affecting the stability of the workforce. Therefore, the company requires all employees to sign one-year contracts and prohibits them from quitting arbitrarily. If an employee insists on leaving without approval, the company deducts 600 yuan as punishment.

A Suzhou labor department official noted that a survey found that about 40% of migrant workers' job-hopping is "non-productive," meaning they do not gain any benefits from changing jobs.

Some companies resort to withholding salaries to retain employees, hoping they will stay for the money. However, this approach can lead to dissatisfaction and even prompt employees to leave abruptly.

Other companies focus on building emotional connections with their employees. For instance, Xiao Tu from Hunan has worked at a textile factory in Suzhou for four years. He stays not because of higher wages but because the boss values and respects the workers. The boss sends holiday greetings via text messages and allows sick leave while arranging for colleagues to accompany employees to the doctor.

Another strategy involves creating a supportive environment for employees. In Jin Feng Town, Changshu, attention is paid to maintaining a balanced ratio of male and female workers, providing opportunities for young people to settle down locally. This helps reduce turnover rates.

Industry insiders revealed that at least one-third of textile, garment, and manufacturing companies in Suzhou adopt various methods to retain employees around the Spring Festival. These methods can be categorized into three types: high-level strategies (emotional retention), mid-level strategies (community-based retention), and low-level strategies (financial coercion).

For effective employee retention, companies should focus on fostering positive relationships and creating a conducive work environment rather than relying on coercive measures.
