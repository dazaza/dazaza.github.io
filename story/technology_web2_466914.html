Peking University and Tsinghua University presidents use popular phrases in their farewell speeches to graduates 42

by rrva06gj on 2011-07-28 08:53:20

Teachers from various departments of Peking University conferred degrees to students. This photo was taken by our reporter Wang Haixin.

Yesterday, Peking University and Tsinghua University held the graduation ceremonies for the undergraduate students of 2011 respectively. A total of 3,234 undergraduate students from Peking University and 3,312 undergraduate students from Tsinghua University were awarded their bachelor's degrees. During the graduation ceremony, the farewell speeches given by the two university presidents broke away from their usual solemn style when attending meetings, and humorously inserted popular witty remarks from time to time.

■ Scene

Former President Xu Zhihong received the most applause

This year’s graduating class of Peking University was welcomed into the university four years ago by the former president Xu Zhihong. Although Xu Zhihong has stepped down as president more than two years ago, he still received nearly a minute of applause from the students when he appeared on the podium yesterday. Many students remembered this former president who sang "Invisible Wings" in a southern accent every New Year's Eve.

In addition to the enthusiastic applause caused by the appearance of the former president, each school leader also had their own supporters, receiving plenty of applause and cheers. CCTV host Zhang Quanling, who attended the graduation ceremony as an alumnus, remarked that this is the Peking University she is familiar with because the students have their own personalized expressions; this is indeed the Peking University she likes because vice presidents give lectures to undergraduates.

High school principals and security guards receive flowers alike

At yesterday's graduation ceremony site, Peking University invited the principals of 120 high schools nationwide. They received flowers presented by the students who graduated four years ago from their respective schools and then entered Peking University. The principal of a middle school in Tianjin told reporters that he sends students to universities every year. This time, watching them graduate from university, seeing his students finally become talented, he was filled with emotion.

Besides the former high school principals, ordinary security guards, cafeteria chefs, and dormitory building managers also came to see off the graduating students. These ordinary faculty members and staff similarly received warm applause and flowers from the students.

Zhang Quanling gave advice on career choice based on her personal experience

Zhang Quanling, who graduated from Peking University's German major 15 years ago, was invited to speak at the graduation ceremony as an alumnus representative. She specially arrived at the school an hour early and took photos at the building where her former dormitory was located. She frankly told her junior classmates that she didn't like her major at all and found her first job after graduation at Shenhua Company, “which is a coal-selling company, and I didn't want to sell coal.” However, due to her experiences in extracurricular activities at Peking University, she had the opportunity to participate in TV program production and determined her goal, so she seized a recruitment opportunity to work at CCTV.

“If you didn’t choose what you like but what your parents like when filling out your college application; if you didn’t choose what you like but what’s easy to get credits when selecting courses; if you didn’t choose what you like but what pays well when looking for jobs after graduation, then why do you expect to be engaged in a career that you like?” Zhang Quanling talked about her career view with her juniors based on her personal experiences.

■ President's Farewell Speech

President Zhou Qifeng of Peking University

Grades on transcripts are just "floating clouds"

Facing more than 3,000 students to deliver his farewell speech, President Zhou Qifeng of Peking University first deeply bowed towards three viewing stands. Familiar network terms to the students were casually used by the president: “Please forget the scores on your transcripts, whether they bring glory or disappointment, they are really just 'floating clouds'; please forget any unpleasantness that occurred between you and your classmates, and think more about those pure friendships, and you won't feel so 'unable to bear the pain'.”

The president also understood the students' daily lives: “You must not forget the path covered with ginkgo leaves under Building No. 29, the tower reflection in Weiming Lake, the lawn in Jingyuan Garden; also the leisurely 'academic cats' in the audio-visual teaching buildings and science teaching buildings; you will not forget the 'Top Ten' posts on BBS, the 'jokes' shared in the dormitory after the lights are out, and the chicken wings at the west gate and the snack shop that satisfy your appetite even late at night; of course, you will not forget the kind senior brothers who helped carry your luggage on tricycles when you first arrived, and later the cute junior sisters who sat behind you on your bicycles…”

The movie "Here's the Youth" self-produced and directed by the students, the president watched it too, and the lyrics of the theme song left a deep impression on him: “Yesterday's memories come back vividly, you stand before me, the youth in memory, never changed. No matter how far you go, you will always be the children that the alma mater cares about the most; no matter how far you go, Weiming Lake, Boya Tower, Wusi Road will surely 'come back vividly' in your dreams, that is the alma mater's call to you!”

President Gu Binglin of Tsinghua University

Describing students using "VANCL-style"

At yesterday's graduation ceremony at Tsinghua University, President Gu Binglin's speech was equally fashionable, earning a total of 14 rounds of applause.

The current popular alternative graduation photos, the president was aware of them, and humorously said to the students: “Perhaps you can still remember the family photos taken when you first entered the university, while today you wear your academic robes, hesitating about which outrageous pose to strike.”

The most popular VANCL-style, the president could use it too: “In my eyes, you love truth, and you love life; you love yourself, and you love public welfare; you love mechanical drawing, and you love pull-ups... There is nothing to fear and nothing impossible, you are the sunny Class of Seven!” (Because they enrolled in 2007, Tsinghua University habitually refers to them as the Class of Seven)

As a "long-time Tsinghua person" who has studied and worked at Tsinghua for 46 years, Gu Binglin said that today's graduates, compared to his time, are richer in knowledge, broader in vision, more active in thinking, more talented, and more willing to display individuality and express ideas. On their graduation day, he sent these eight words to the students: "Think independently, act effectively."
