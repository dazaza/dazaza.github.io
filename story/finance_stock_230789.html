When is the best time to buy at the bottom - Stock analysis, stock technical analysis, stock market analysis

by sceece on 2009-08-30 16:52:39

When the stock market is about to make a big adjustment, everyone without exception will suffer losses from the plunge. In such circumstances, the normal reaction should be to withdraw from the market and wait on the sidelines.

This is a very simple principle, isn't it?

The problem is, many people make abnormal choices. When there are significant risks in the stock market that need to be avoided at all costs, they repeatedly make mistakes, compounding one error with another.

The ebb and flow of tides, the waxing and waning of the moon, all follow patterns and undergo changes. The fluctuations of the stock market ultimately boil down to a trend.

Major trends determine major market conditions, while minor trends dictate minor changes.

Those who understand the major trends can make a lot of money; those who grasp the minor trends may earn a little. But those who don't understand any trends might even lose money during a bull market.

On the eve of July 29th, based on trend analysis, the blog warned of a series of risks. Investors who acted decisively by selling their stocks or clearing their positions according to the changing signals were correct. Those who stubbornly went against the market inevitably faced being trapped and suffering losses. These funds, which didn't sell promptly before the trend changed, have already made one mistake.

The issue is that under such an obviously declining trend, these individuals did not recognize their errors and correct them. They didn’t decisively stop trading and take a break. Instead, they continued to rush down the wrong path, which is truly unacceptable.

The 30-day moving average has turned downward, and the weekly and monthly line indicators have all shown signs of deterioration. With the major trend no longer reliable, is it realistic to imagine capturing a profitable trend within just five minutes or even less? Are you confused?

Here, we reiterate the three conditions for major profit-making opportunities:

1. The policy environment fully supports bullish sentiment, advocates for more investors, and strongly emphasizes protecting investor rights.

2. Medium and short-term trend indicators start rising again, recovering the 10 and 20-day moving averages, and the monthly and weekly K-line charts show signs of bottoming out.

3. The market sees one-yuan stocks, abnormally low trading volumes, and new shares or funds that cannot be issued.
