Modern marriage is a kind of insurance, invented by women.

by chen987long on 2011-04-01 18:04:43

Eileen Chang once said: Modern marriage is a kind of insurance, invented by women. Here, the modern marriage Eileen Chang refers to is monogamy. It is currently the marital system universally adopted by the vast majority of countries in the world, and it originated from the Western Christian monogamy. Of course, in some Islamic countries and regions, polygamy is still practiced to this day. In Central America and some islands in the South Pacific, there still exists a matrilineal society-based form of polyandry. Additionally, the Mosuo ethnic group's practice of "walking marriage" is not uncommon either. However, upon reviewing many cases of polygyny in Chinese history, I have a feeling: the current monogamous system, on the surface, is a declaration to protect women's basic rights and a spell to restrict men from fooling around everywhere, but at its core, it still represents the fundamental interests of the vast majority of men. Why do I say that? Let's analyze the drawbacks of polygyny together.

In the past, the traditional view held that polygyny was an unequal treaty that discriminated against and oppressed women, a tyrannical clause that treated women as breeding machines and tools for sexual gratification, a monstrous offspring of a typical patriarchal society. This statement is entirely correct. But if we look at it from another perspective—"the moon is full, then wanes; water fills, then overflows"—polygyny (or polygamy) is also an inevitable harm to men.

Let's start with the biggest beneficiaries of polygyny. Who are the biggest beneficiaries of polygyny in China's past? I don't need to say much—I think everyone understands instinctively—it's the emperor. Since the Shang and Zhou dynasties, Chinese emperors (who could be considered the most successful men in ancient China) took pride in having "three palaces and six courtyards," and were ashamed of being "monogamous." They glorified "nightly debauchery" and were ashamed of "long-term abstinence." They glorified "wild orgies" and despised "gradual progression." They glorified "taking aphrodisiacs" and despised "real effort." Mr. Bo Yang revealed in one of his works that during the Zhou Dynasty, the emperor could legitimately possess 121 wives. But even these seemed insufficient, so during the Han, Tang, and Song dynasties, the number of ladies-in-waiting in the palace reached tens of thousands. During Emperor Wu of Han's reign, the concubines were divided into 14 ranks, with more than 3,000 ranked concubines. During the Tianbao period of Emperor Xuanzong of Tang, the number of palace maids and concubines reached a record-breaking 40,000. No wonder the famous poet Yuan Zhen wrote fifty years later: "The ancient palace stands desolate, the palace flowers bloom red in loneliness. The white-haired palace maid sits idly, talking about Emperor Xuanzong." Talking about Emperor Xuanzong? What did they talk about? I estimate many palace maids, let alone being favored by the emperor, probably never even saw the man who had the most wives in the world during their decades-long life in the deep palace. Thus, Mr. Bo Yang exclaimed: An emperor with so many wives indulged nightly in wild debauchery, truly a rare case in human history, no wonder Chinese emperors were almost all short-lived! Mr. Bo Yang's words are completely correct. In ancient China, emperors had the shortest average lifespan and the worst health conditions! Someone made a statistic: There were 209 emperors throughout history whose exact birth and death dates are known. These 209 emperors had an average lifespan of only 39.2 years. Demographers calculated that the average life expectancy in ancient China was 57 years. The average life expectancy of Chinese emperors was 18 years less than ordinary people. Moreover, I found that the more unrestrained emperors were, the shorter their lifespans. Do you know Emperor Cheng of Han? He was the husband of Zhao Feiyan, thin like "sweet and sour spareribs." Before his death, he indulged in debauchery for years. Not only did he marry the low-born Zhao Feiyan, but he also conveniently married her sister Zhao Hede. It's said that Emperor Cheng loved playing "double flying," and ultimately due to excessive indulgence, before reaching forty, our great Han crown prince's back was hunched, and his sexual function greatly diminished. Zhao Feiyan and her sister were unsatisfied, so Zhao Feiyan, who gained favor through sorcery, sought secret formulas for the bedroom, and together with an alchemist, developed an aphrodisiac for her husband to take. Once, due to excessive consumption, the emperor died on a beauty's stomach before reaching the age of forty, becoming a true example of "dying under the peony flower, ghost or not, remains elegant."

There are even more exaggerated examples. When Emperor Duzong of Southern Song first ascended the throne, one night he allegedly engaged simultaneously with more than 100 young and beautiful palace concubines. This is recorded in Bi Yuan's book "Continuation of the Comprehensive Mirror for Aid in Government: Song Annals, Volume 180" compiled during the Qianlong era of the Qing dynasty. Apparently, this gentleman was particularly "capable"! One night he summoned more than 100 women. Perhaps this set a record for the sexual lives of Chinese emperors in ancient times? The result was, he went too far. This young emperor who ascended the throne at 55 years old didn't last ten years on the throne before ascending to the western heavens prematurely.

As an old saying goes, money should not be spent entirely, and blessings should not be enjoyed fully. Emperors with three palaces and six courtyards would "fire cannons" every night. Even medium-class families with three wives and four concubines wouldn't hang up the "no fighting" sign. With so many wives, it might seem glorious, and the happiness seems happy, but "there’s only farmland that can't be plowed, but no ox that won’t die." Morning bells and evening drums cannot withstand the ferocity, and even the strongest man cannot endure such pressure. After all, as righteous people say, lust harms people, and indulgence injures the body. Thus, monogamy came into being. With only one wife, a man can fully conserve his energy and smoothly support his family. Both his wife and mother-in-law would approve!

Even Japanese renowned writer Haruki Murakami, who has always been unenthusiastic about monogamy, frankly admitted that once a man has multiple wives, not only does the economic burden increase, but the mental burden becomes unbearable. Therefore, considering the well-being of men's bodies and the long-term plans of families, monogamy must be implemented.

Monogamy considers the actual needs of "leftover men"

Another factor contributing to the prevalence of monogamy is the consideration of the actual needs of other less fortunate "leftover men." In recent years, two new terms have emerged: "leftover women" and "leftover men." Although both are left over, their meanings differ greatly. "Leftover women" are often highly qualified "three-high women" (high education, high income, high social status), commonly known as "white-bone-precious" (short for white-collar, backbone, elite). On the other hand, "leftover men" are often the opposite—many are low-educated, low-income, low-social-status "three-low men," often unattractive, physically weak, dull, lazy, and unaccomplished.

It is well-known that women choose partners by looking upward. Even if a woman earns ten thousand yuan per month, she would still seek a partner earning twenty thousand yuan per month. Even if she becomes a "white-bone-precious," she still dreams of marrying a learned and respected man like Tang Monk. No one would lower themselves to marry a "shrimp soldier or crab general." A single "white-bone-precious" once confided in me: rather than settling for a useless man for life, she would prefer to be a lover to an outstanding man, willingly becoming his mistress. If she couldn't find someone suitable, she'd rather remain single.

With only so many women in the world, if successful men all had multiple wives, leaving the weak men to become lifelong bachelors or share leftover crumbs, what kind of situation would arise? Or share their partners with other men? Simply put, this would inevitably lead to anger, conflict, and even violence, resulting in societal unrest. In the eyes of many scientists, monogamy is an agreement among men. Through this agreement, they ensure their own interests while preventing mutual strife caused by women. This agreement requires all men in society to enjoy equal rights to a certain extent, even requiring the powerful to adhere to this rule. They abandon polygamy to maintain social stability and unity, ensuring that even the least capable individuals have a reason to find a wife. For men, this cannot be denied as a compromise and sacrifice. Even Japanese writer Haruki Murakami had to admit in his book "This Thing Called Husband" that monogamy is essentially a system that rescues charmless and weak men.

Imagine if successful men all had multiple wives, and women all chased after the men they deemed worthy, even content to be mistresses, what would happen to the large number of "three-low men"? They certainly couldn't remain lifelong bachelors forever, right?

I frequently hear many philandering men complain: Monogamy is bad, against human nature! Ha ha, if polygamy continued, could your body handle it? Even if you took Viagra daily and enjoyed nightly pleasures, when you stepped out, you'd still have to be cautious. Those unmarried men, perhaps already burning with jealousy, might not look kindly upon you. You might not collapse in the arms of your numerous wives but instead fall victim to the slingshots of sexually frustrated "three-low men." That would truly be more unjust than Dou'e!

DDVDD really cracked? Unfortunately, the answer is no. Let's look at this example. Uslix644 released a program called BackupHDDVDD, which actually has nothing to do with DDVDD. What Uslix644 did was add a shell to the AES-128 decryption algorithm that accepts DDVDD encrypted videos as parameters. Since the ACSS and DDVDD specifications are public, anyone can write such a program. This is actually a necessary step in developing DDVDD player software. However, after decrypting, the player must also decode and play. This program directly writes the decrypted content to the hard drive. But if you create a player, can you claim to have cracked DDVDD? Or if you find the lock and keyhole, can you claim to have unlocked the lock (especially since the method of making the lock and the location of the keyhole are public)? Of course not, unless you find the key. So where does the key come from? Unfortunately, it has nothing to do with Uslix644. We must commend MindDVDD for placing the second key unprotected in memory, which led to the current DDVDripp download. In this entire farce, MindDVDD contributed the most. The so-called "hackers" merely bent down to pick up the key graciously offered by MindDVDD. When Uslix644 first announced his results on YouTube, many were misled. For the first week, everyone focused on finding vulnerabilities in PowerDVDD... It seems there hasn't been any success yet.

ACS*** What needs to be done is very simple: immediately revoke MindDVDD's first key. The ACSS key management agency is connected in real-time to replication facilities. Currently, this key used by MindDVDD will not open the security box on newly released DDVDD discs. Without access to the second key, the publicly exposed vulnerability in memory becomes irrelevant. Already released DDVDD films are indeed lost. This is a risk the ACS*** foresaw, but newly released DDVDD titles remain secure unless someone like MindDVDD leaks information again. Looking back at history, this whole farce resembles the DeCSS incident in the DVD era. After the DeCSS keys were leaked and used by a Norwegian teenager to create the CSSdecrypt program (which later became a user-friendly interface with DeCSS), this program was much more genuine than the BackupHDDVDD software. At least it contained effective keys, whereas the latter was just a packaging of a public algorithm, and poorly packaged at that. But this is another story. When the leaked key spread, Hollywood used the revocation mechanism, and within four months, thousands of pirates found their DVD-RIPP tools unable to crack newly released DVDs. However, another program appeared, namely RoboCSS. This program did not rely on any keys but used brute force to crack CSS, which was relatively weak, seemingly 100 bits, and vulnerable to attacks. Computers at the time could brute-force crack it in a few hours. Only after RoboCSS's brute-force cracking appeared was DVD truly cracked. Historically, the Norwegian teenager Jon and the DeCSS program were not the pioneers of cracking DVD. In this Uslix644 incident, compared to Jon and DeCSS, the technical content seems even lower. Protecting movie discs is not easy. You must provide the key, encrypted content, and legitimate decryptor (player) to the end user. The design idea of ACSS/CSS is to create a secure large safe containing the movie, with the key to open the safe placed inside a small secret box. The ACSS/CSS institutions are responsible for issuing keys to legitimate player manufacturers. In the DVD era, the large safe was virtually useless, easily opened by brute force. In the ACSS era, the large safe, or AES-128 encryption algorithm, is theoretically secure and cannot be brute-forced in the foreseeable future. The small box, or player design, may contain errors, but ACSS can correct them, or at least minimize the negative effects of opening the small box during the lifetime of this format.

Some comments say ACSS is a lock, but hackers bypassed it by stealing content through walls. Whether the lock is strong or not is irrelevant. I wish it were so. But as clearly explained earlier, what happened is that civilians picked up the volume unique key lying on the ground and honestly followed the ACSS rules to open the AES-128 lock and retrieve the content. There was no so-called wall penetration. Some say that if Yu Gong could move mountains, he could surely brute-force AES-128. I can only say this commits the empirical error of CSS. Google AES-128 first. Some say that even if ACSS isn't cracked, now we have DRM-free content, so it's the same, right? It's not the same. Consider that the digital pirated DVDs available in the market are limited to releases before autumn 1999. Would you still consider DVDs cracked? Finally, someone says that even if AES-128 cannot be cracked, the system's strength depends on its weakest link. That's correct, but this so-called "weakest link" can be continuously revoked and updated. Uslix644 claims to have bypassed all revocation mechanisms. But when asked what happens if MindDVDD's key is revoked, Uslix644 says, "Don't worry, there will always be generous players like MindDVDD." If you really bypassed the revocation mechanism, why would you need another similar player to appear after MindDVDD's key is revoked? I'm genuinely worried for Uslix644. If this is the level of "hackers," then truly cracking the ACSS system may be far off. Despite the collective efforts of many "heroes" on the internet for two weeks, it seems no one has benefited from PowerDVDD on the same Windows platform. Picking up keys from the ground is indeed much simpler than stealing them from someone's pocket...

The first article already mentioned that this incident provides a good opportunity for ACSS to prove itself. It's essentially a public beta test for the revocation mechanism. Each retail or OEM copy of the same version of the software player contains the same key. In the absence of discovering stolen keys, the software key needs to be updated every 2-18 months (Kevin Collins of Microsoft mentioned a cycle of 100 days recently). Once a player's vulnerability is confirmed, the key will be immediately revoked. This updated blacklist can immediately reflect in the production lines of replication facilities. The entire process will not exceed one month. In fact, I expect that ACSS's statement regarding this incident will be seen as early as next week.

But will the piracy of DDVDD never develop as prosperous as DVD? I believe it will not. It's clear that a one-time permanent brute-force crack like DVD is impossible. However, once someone like MindDVDD appears again, all releases before that point should be considered cracked. Just the possibility of such opportunities will decrease, and the cycles will lengthen.

If this is the case, Hollywood's goal is achieved. In the DVD era, people were 100% sure about ripping, re-encoding, and distributing pirated DVDs. However, in the DDVDD era, this 100% certainty is broken. For example, Warner Bros. released the Matrix trilogy DDVDD set, but the crack solution was delayed or appeared only after a year, even two years. True consumers and movie enthusiasts cannot wait indefinitely. People persistently waiting for pirated versions are not likely to buy legitimate copies anyway.
