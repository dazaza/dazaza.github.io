As an online store, if you want to develop and make great progress, the first problem that troubles you is the source of goods. How to find a competitive and reliable source of goods? I believe this issue causes headaches and sadness for countless shopkeepers. For large sellers or big companies, this situation is not too difficult to solve because, with their conditions and qualifications, finding a supply channel is effortless.

However, in Taobao, although there are many shopkeepers, very few have become large sellers or big companies. Even though we all know that Lemon Green Tea has grown significantly, how many Lemon Green Teas can Taobao produce? By saying this, I am not trying to discourage everyone or belittle e-commerce, but rather this industry still needs adjustment, accumulation, and time. The future is undoubtedly bright. As they say:

Thirty years ago, physical stores changed destinies; thirty years later, online shops change lives.

Since online shops have such a promising future, we should strive to do well, find good sources of goods, and compete with others. During my time as a supervisor in the shopkeeper's purchasing section, I noticed many questions about purchasing while on duty. Below, I will summarize these issues, hoping it helps you. If it is written poorly, consider it as a stepping stone.

1. What should beginners do, and which industry should they enter?

Explanation: There is no standard for this. The most critical point is to base it on your own situation.

1) If you are an office worker with limited time, it is suggested to sell products with lower added value (such as clothing, accessories, etc.) or do some agency work (Taobao currently does not allow agency work, so when doing agency work, follow the relevant rules. There are many agency information on Alibaba, feel free to check them out). The reason for doing this is to minimize risks because running a Taobao store is not an easy task. Don't think sitting in front of a computer burning incense is enough; it requires a lot of energy and time, testing one's willpower and perseverance. Without certain endurance, it is hard to survive. If you buy a large amount of goods, since you are new, you haven't learned much knowledge and don't have the ability to sell the products quickly. The products will depreciate and become outdated, causing losses and severely affecting motivation. Therefore, shopkeepers need to consider potential problems in the future.

2) If you have idle money, can afford losses, and don't worry about having no food to eat, then it is suggested to do what interests you and what you are familiar with. Nothing beats interest in making people succeed easily.

3) Regardless of the situation, always be realistic. Even though the shop may be small, it is still part of entrepreneurship. Plan and prepare carefully.

2. Where to purchase goods and how to purchase them?

Explanation: Currently, shopkeepers mainly use these methods to purchase goods:

1) Manufacturer

2) Wholesale Market

3) Online

Below, I will explain in detail:

1) Manufacturer Goods

Because manufacturers offer cheap prices, we all want to get goods directly from the manufacturer. However, in reality, how many people can actually get goods from the manufacturer? Manufacturers usually have volume requirements, and if you don't meet certain conditions, even if you get the goods, they won't be cheap. Of course, if you have connections, it's different. Here, you shouldn't blame or complain about the manufacturer because they also have their difficulties.

2) Purchasing at Wholesale Markets (Taking clothing as an example)

a) Look like a buyer. For instance, your attire and appearance should resemble someone buying goods. Don't dress more stylishly than stars; you're not walking the runway. Others won't hesitate to rip you off. Moreover, purchasing goods is physically demanding, so simplicity is best. Having a small cart or carrying a black plastic bag is ideal, almost becoming a symbol of clothing purchases. This trick works effectively, as I've personally tested it. When I went shopping, I carried a bag and pulled a small cart, rushing to the market. Wow, the moment I arrived, those adorable little sisters looked at me greedily, calling me boss repeatedly, making me feel happy inside. They offered discounts and minimum quantities, making me laugh in my sleep that night.

b) Have determination when purchasing goods. Plan before leaving, deciding what goods to buy and how much money to bring. Especially for beginners, you must pay attention. In wholesale markets, money seems to disappear quickly. Those beautiful sisters won't care if you can afford public transportation back home. Don't let them confuse you, nor should you go crazy buying things you like. Remember, the items aren't for you but for your customers, and you don't represent their tastes. When I shopped in wholesale markets, the sisters wouldn't let me leave, holding onto the catalog and refusing to let me put it down. Seeing their hopeful eyes, I had to bite my teeth and walk away, despite feeling reluctant. But I knew how much money I had left. You can refer to my experience to know when to say no.

c) Don't rush to purchase goods. Visit several shops first, checking the general price levels. Don't be misled. In the morning, wholesale is the main focus, so shopkeepers can arrive earlier to get better prices. Ask clearly how to purchase goods and how to wholesale. If you don't understand something, ask. Don't be shy or worried about what others might say. The market is vast, not just one place. If they don't treat you well, move on to another shop.

In summary, visiting wholesale markets isn't an easy task, especially for beginners. Due to lack of experience, it's hard to get very low-priced goods, requiring careful planning and wasting a lot of effort. Why? It's like a newly graduated student sitting at the same table as a leader; they handle situations effortlessly, while you are nervous and inexperienced. Moreover, many cities lack such wholesale markets. What should we shopkeepers do then?

Don't worry, we have another option—online purchasing!

3) Online Purchasing

Personally, I believe that with the development of e-commerce, online purchasing will become a mainstream method. Currently, for beginners, online purchasing is the preferred way to obtain goods because the threshold is low; you don't need manufacturer connections or visit wholesale markets. For experienced sellers, even crown-level sellers, it's still a good channel to enrich their shops. Regarding online purchasing, many websites claim to offer this service, but the quality varies. Here, I only recommend Alibaba, not for any other reason, but because it's at least from the same company as Taobao, and Alipay is available.

Previously, people might have had this impression of Alibaba: lots of items, but high prices. It's hard to find low-cost bulk purchases, and quality issues often cause concern. However, this situation is improving. Alibaba has specifically created a purchasing platform for Taobao shopkeepers—the Small-Order Wholesale Platform.

To my understanding, suppliers joining this Small-Order Wholesale Platform must meet three conditions:

1) Support Alipay guaranteed transactions.

2) Support small-volume wholesaling.

3) Offer the highest cost-performance ratio among similar goods.

Currently, many suppliers have joined the Small-Order Wholesale Platform, collaborating with Alibaba for activities, offering extremely low prices. Additionally, participating merchants provide real pictures of their products, allowing direct observation of product quality. Thus, the new Alibaba has absolute advantages in both quality and price compared to other online sources.

Due to the improvements in transaction security and image quality after the new reforms, it is most suitable for beginners. You can search for items you find suitable in terms of price and interest to use as your store's goods.

Here are the key points I've summarized for purchasing small orders on Alibaba:

1) Must choose a Certified Member.

2) Must choose a supplier supporting Alipay guaranteed transactions.

Do not be tempted by suppliers who do not support this.

3) Among similar goods, choose suppliers with actual product photos.

This allows a direct view of product quality. Pay attention to other details, such as return policies and logistics. Start with a small order to confirm quality before deciding whether to add more products.

4) Keep an eye on activities on the Small-Order Wholesale Platform.

Frequent activities include "Direct Supply from Factories," "Special Supply for Drilling," and "Premium Street." These are negotiated by Alibaba staff with suppliers, generally offering good value for money, with most providing actual product photos.

5) Compare and contrast.

How to do it? Besides using search and filtering, here's another method: observe the update speed of the shop, see how long it takes to update its products, and whether it keeps up with market trends. This helps determine if the supplier is dedicated to online channels, typically indicating good service. Comparing similar items also reveals which shop updates fastest, as only factories or large wholesalers can access the latest trendy products, making it easier to find the source of goods.

How to obtain cheap and high-quality goods is a crucial step in opening a store. The choice of goods directly affects the entrepreneur's profit and income status, so caution is necessary in selecting goods. From the perspective of online business universality, goods can be sought from three aspects: manufacturers, primary wholesalers, and secondary or even tertiary wholesalers. Generally, the closer the procurement method is to the source, the larger the profit margin. However, each level of wholesalers has its pros and cons, and entrepreneurs of different strengths should prudently choose based on their current situation.

There are not many goods that can be sourced directly from manufacturers because most manufacturers are unwilling to deal with small-scale sellers. Typically, manufacturers require a high initial order quantity, making it unsuitable for small-batch buyers.

Large wholesalers or primary wholesalers are relatively easy to find. Usually, using search engines like Baidu or Google can yield many results. Some specialized sourcing websites also provide rich and comprehensive information. Several e-commerce websites list various sizes of wholesalers, some with relatively good services and reputations. They generally supply goods directly from manufacturers, ensuring stable supplies. However, due to their numerous orders, services may sometimes lag. Large wholesalers, with many orders and slower shipping, can be understood, but the biggest challenge lies in returns. Requests for returns are often rejected or result in unsatisfactory returned goods, and refunds are impossible. Experienced individuals should clarify product details and service methods before ordering. If the order size is large, it’s best to sign a formal sales contract to avoid disputes later.

When encountering difficulties with large wholesalers, one can turn to secondary wholesalers. Secondary wholesalers are often new to the wholesale business, transitioning from retail, and have a better understanding of the industry's sources and customer demands. Since they are just starting, they lack fixed wholesale clients and recognition, so to attract customers, they require smaller initial orders, and their prices are generally not higher—or sometimes even lower—than those of large wholesalers. Entrepreneurs can negotiate terms with them based on their purchasing experiences, such as pricing and return policies. To attract repeat customers, their after-sales service is usually good. However, the notable drawback of secondary wholesalers is the issue of credibility. Therefore, it’s best to test them with small orders before proceeding with large-scale collaborations.

The space for online stores exists largely due to lower costs. Mastering the source of good quality and affordable goods is key to successful e-commerce operations. Taking apparel as an example, many well-known brands have a unified national price, selling at a minimum of 85% off in regular stores, whereas online, clothing prices are 20%-70% off mall prices.

So, how can one find low-cost goods?

1) Be a market hunter. Closely monitor market changes and take advantage of discounted goods to find affordable sources. For instance, sellers often find high-quality branded clothing during seasonal changes or special sales, reselling them online to gain profits through regional or temporal price differences. Some cosmetic sellers familiarize themselves with high-end cosmetic counters and secure goods at 70% off before new products launch, reselling them online at 90% of counter prices, earning substantial profits due to the high original prices.

2) Focus on foreign trade products. Due to their quality, style, fabric, and price advantages, foreign trade products remain popular online. Many internationally renowned products priced over $100 sell for only hundreds of RMB online, attracting many buyers. New online entrepreneurs with foreign trade factory connections can source goods directly from factories. Among leftover foreign trade orders, there are many good items, often only 1-3 pieces, styles frequently being the most popular next year or now, priced at only 40%-70% of mall prices, highly marketable.

3) Purchase excess inventory of branded goods. Some branded goods have significant inventory overstock, prompting some merchants to sell all their stock to dedicated online sellers. Branded goods are a highly sought-after category online, with many buyers searching directly for their favorite brands. Although some brands may be overstocked in certain regions, the broad coverage of the internet can make them bestsellers elsewhere. With sufficient bargaining skills, acquiring their overstock at low prices can yield substantial profits.

4) Secure discounted goods abroad. World-renowned brands drastically reduce prices before seasons change or holidays approach. If sellers have relatives or friends abroad, they can help secure attractive discounts for online sales. Even at 40%-70% off traditional mall prices, there remains a 10%-40% profit margin. This sales model is gaining attention among some overseas students. For instance, the store run by Japanese student "Peach Boy" sells Japan's latest cosmetics and beauty nutritional supplements, delivered domestically and internationally via air transport. Due to fresher cosmetics and faster availability compared to domestic counters, they receive widespread support. Similarly, some American and European students sell top-tier brands like "Victoria's Secret" and "LV" online, with profits exceeding 30%.

5) Wholesale goods. Running to regional wholesale markets frequently not only familiarizes one with market conditions but also secures very low wholesale prices. After identifying sources, start with small orders for trial sales, increasing order sizes if sales are good. Online, some sellers maintain good relationships with suppliers, only purchasing goods after they are sold, avoiding capital occupation and inventory buildup. In short, regardless of the sourcing channel, low prices are key. Finding good quality and affordable goods lays the foundation for a successful online store.

Comparison of Various Store Sourcing Methods

1) Find the local wholesale market and selectively pick and choose goods.

[Advantages] Goods are self-sourced, products self-selected, ensuring quality.

[Disadvantages] Transportation is a consideration; weather is a consideration; after-sales service needs consideration; price issues need consideration; the risk of overstocking needs consideration; handling images yourself...

2) A wholesale market is nearby; simply browse around for desired goods.

[Advantages] No risk of overstocking, no transportation issues, no weather concerns, personal assurance of product quality.

[Disadvantages] Perhaps only price issues arise because you buy less, haha; handling images yourself. If anything else, it could be that the living area is lively.

3) Operate as a resale website for a physical store.

[Advantages] No worries about shipping, no concerns about product quality (of course, quality issues still need evaluation, preferably visiting the store to assess its scale), minimal funds required, no need to handle images, fixed purchase prices.

[Disadvantages] The supplier has already determined the courier company, unable to change; additionally, if the company-provided images are poor, you cannot alter them. Furthermore, if customers seek more product information, you may be unable to respond or provide additional images.

4) Operate as a resale website for a large online store.

[Advantages] No worries about shipping or images, minimal funds required, fixed purchase prices.

[Disadvantages] The supplier has already determined the courier company, unable to change; additionally, if the company-provided images are poor, you cannot alter them. Furthermore, if customers seek more product information, you may be unable to respond or provide additional images. Importantly, you cannot accurately judge the product quality.

5) Operate as a resale website for a distant wholesaler without a physical or online store.

[Advantages] None noted.

[Disadvantages] Unable to correctly verify the authenticity of product information provided by the supplier; generally, orders are placed online via QQ, MSN, or a mobile number. They may require payment before shipping. Everyone knows that online information resources are abundant, and images can be obtained anywhere.

6) Locate a dense sales area for a particular product and place bulk orders online. These areas are typically far from your location.

[Advantages] Displayed online prices may be very low, with ample supply and some image provision by suppliers.

[Disadvantages] May still require some image processing; product quality cannot be assured until delivery; risk of overstocking; possible difficulty in cash flow.

7) Already operate a physical store while simultaneously expanding into the online market.

[Advantages] No need to elaborate; clearly, there are many advantages, benefits, and profits. Business should be promoted through various channels to earn from multiple streams. Otherwise, why would so many manufacturers choose to advertise, spending heavily on advertising fees?

[Disadvantages] May sometimes be too busy to attend to online customers.

Eight Steps to Find Good Wholesalers for Your Online Store

From initially starting with a playful attitude to eventually becoming a mid-sized wholesaler, I realized that sourcing is very important. Now, I share my experience with you, hoping it helps.

1) Manufacturer Sources: Legitimate manufacturers have ample supplies, good attitudes, and if cooperating long-term, can generally swap out slow-moving goods. However, manufacturers usually require high initial order quantities, unsuitable for small wholesalers. If you have sufficient capital reserves and no risk of overstocking or don't fear overstocking, then find a manufacturer to source goods.

2) Large Wholesalers:

Advantages: Relatively easy to find, generally using Baidu yields many results. They usually source directly from manufacturers, ensuring stable supplies.

Drawbacks: Because they are large, with many orders, services may lag. They often have fixed repeat customers, making it hard to negotiate terms unless you become a major client, potentially securing special discounts or offers. The worst issue is their shipping speed and return policies, which often fall short. While slow shipping for many orders is understandable with advance ordering, the real problem lies in returns. Sometimes received items may have defects, especially jewelry. Encountering good large wholesalers may lead them to send replacements upon receiving the defective items, which is fortunate. However, I encountered some large wholesalers with different attitudes. Once, I found many items worn out upon receipt, emailed them, waited two days without response, and had to post on their message board. They said to send back for replacement. I sent them back immediately but waited nearly two weeks without any reply, whereas normally they could receive the items within two days. I had to post again on their message board, where they replied they would ship immediately. Later, they emailed saying the item was out of stock and would take a few more days, suggesting I wait longer or choose a different item. I chose the latter, but the replacement still had some issues. I didn't bother contacting them further.

Another large wholesaler was even worse. The description listed a bracelet length of 18 cm, but the received item was 21.5 cm, too long to stay on. Their customer service claimed the angel egg series was solid, but the received items were hollow. Evidence from QQ chat records confirmed this. Could I not return them? They said yes, but I had to send them back via express and bear the shipping costs. I asked for a refund instead, but they refused! Following their conditions led to receiving subpar quality goods. These items were given to friends casually, as selling them would invite complaints.

3) Newly Starting Wholesalers:

Advantages: Retailers reaching a certain level of understanding about industry sources and customer needs often transition to wholesaling. Such wholesalers, being new, lack fixed wholesale clients and recognition. To attract customers, their initial order requirements are smaller, and prices are generally not higher—or sometimes even lower—than large wholesalers. You can negotiate terms with them based on your purchasing experiences, such as pricing and return policies. Even if they disagree, they might agree or reach a middle ground. Additionally, to attract repeat customers, their after-sales service is generally good.

Drawbacks: Being new wholesalers, their credibility needs thorough examination. Check reviews on message boards or request creditworthiness proof from them.

4) Pricing: Based on my experience, each wholesaler has main product categories, usually sourced directly from good relationship manufacturers, giving them a price advantage. They often set some of these products at lower prices to attract customers' attention, boosting sales of other products. If not inconvenient, you can source these attention-grabbing products from multiple wholesalers. If inconvenient, opt for wholesalers with good after-sales service, as the expenses and efforts wasted on returns would outweigh the savings from cheaper purchases.

Tip: Prefer wholesalers covering return shipping costs for quality issues, ensuring they thoroughly inspect product quality. Even if issues arise, they cover shipping costs, saving you money.
