China Safety Breast Enlargement Summit Forum: Latest Interpretation of PIP Implant Breast Enlargement

China·Shanghai·February 25

Summary: On February 25, the China Safety Breast Enlargement Summit Forum will be grandly held at Yilemei Plastic Surgery Hospital in Shanghai. During the conference, Dr. Luan Jie, Director of the Breast Plastic Surgery and Reconstruction Center of the Institute of Plastic Surgery of the Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences, will comprehensively and deeply interpret for the public the latest developments regarding the PIP incident for the first time. Furthermore, in conjunction with the Yilemei Asia-Pacific Breast Enlargement Demonstration Base in Shanghai, a formal announcement will be made to all individuals nationwide who have undergone breast enlargement using substandard PIP implants from the 2005 batch or substandard Artefill implants. A campaign to remove these implants will be conducted between February 18, 2012, and March 8, 2012.

Dr. Luan Jie stated that after the European Commission convened an expert meeting, it was concluded that based on the currently limited data, it is not yet sufficient to definitively determine the health risks for women who have received PIP implants. However, the European Commission recommends that "women with concerns should consult their surgeons." It also requires relevant investigation agencies to conduct more in-depth investigations to clarify the health risks associated with PIP silicone implants.

Regulatory authorities must strengthen the monitoring of medical devices, including surprise inspections of products already on the market, strengthening control over reporting institutions, and additional sample testing. There is an urgent need for legal revisions to regulations related to medical devices.

PIP Breast Enlargement Implants

Since the PIP incident caused a stir, the global cosmetic surgery industry has been affected to varying degrees. Although this event had minimal impact on China's breast enlargement industry, it has prompted various sectors of society to begin truly focusing on the topic of breast enlargement.

On Saturday, February 25, 2012, at 10:00 AM, the "China Safety Breast Enlargement Summit Forum" will officially be held in Shanghai. This summit forum focuses on safe breast enlargement. Top breast enlargement experts from plastic surgery hospitals across the country will gather together to focus on the PIP implant incident and provide a comprehensive analysis of the entire process of the PIP incident to society and female friends. Professor Luan Jie will prioritize analyzing issues of public and media concern, such as rupture and carcinogenicity, enabling the public to have a clear understanding and knowledge of the PIP incident. More than 100 media outlets will provide continuous on-site and online coverage. The scale and specifications of the conference will leave a significant mark in the history of the breast enlargement industry, becoming a groundbreaking initiative that stirs the Chinese breast enlargement community.

It is understood that the National Medical Products Administration (NMPA) reported that PIP implants were registered under the trade name "Belle" in April 2009. On December 26, 2011, the NMPA requested the Guangdong Provincial Medical Products Administration to urge Shenzhen Weinjie Co., Ltd., to provide a traceability list of sold products in China. According to the NMPA, by January 5, 2012, 743 PIP products sold domestically had been traced, but these problematic PIP silicone implants have not yet been recalled.
