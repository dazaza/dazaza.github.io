The phenomenon of skin aging begins at the age of 30 and becomes increasingly apparent with increasing age. The degree of aging varies significantly from individual to individual and is influenced by both internal and external environmental factors. The laxity and aging of facial skin are manifestations of overall body aging on the face. The sagging and deterioration of upper eyelid skin is the result of multiple factors. Due to the laxity and sagging of the upper eyelid skin, the shape of the eyebrows can change from a youthful arched form to a downward-sloping "eight-character eyebrow". For those who originally had single eyelids, multiple horizontal skin folds may appear on the upper eyelid, while for those with double eyelids, the double eyelid line may become partially or completely covered. Since the outer half sags more severely, it often forms so-called "triangular eyes." Severe sagging can lead to skin covering the eyelid margin, cornea, and even part of the pupil, causing trichiasis and affecting the field of vision. Therefore, correcting upper eyelid skin laxity serves both aesthetic and functional purposes. Upper eyelid skin laxity can only be corrected through surgery. Surgical methods include brow skin excision, double eyelid plastic surgery, and small incision brow lift in the forehead area. The small incision brow lift through the forehead has not yet been widely adopted domestically, and the first two surgeries are more common. Brow skin excision can be further divided into upper brow skin excision, partial or full eyebrow excision, and lower brow skin excision based on the location of the incision. Upper brow skin excision, also known as a brow lift, involves making an incision along the upper edge of the eyebrows. Its advantage is that it improves the shape of the eyebrows and corrects upper eyelid laxity while preserving all the eyebrows. However, the amount of skin removed is limited, generally not exceeding 0.8 cm according to our experience; otherwise, the scar may exceed the limits of the eyebrows. Additionally, due to the thicker dermis in the upper part of the brow, the resulting scar may be more noticeable.

Brow excision involves removing part or all of the brow skin. This method allows for the removal of more skin, up to 1.2 cm wide, and simultaneously corrects undesirable brow shapes or removes local scars while reducing crow's feet. The downside is the loss of normal brow appearance and its function of shielding rainwater, leaving a visible scar without the concealment of eyebrows, requiring postoperative eyebrow tattooing or makeup for retouching.

Lower brow skin excision involves designing the incision just below the eyebrows and removing the skin below the brows to correct upper eyelid skin laxity. Its advantage is retaining the original eyebrows and eliminating the need to trim unruly hair below the eyebrows in daily life, with less visible scarring. However, due to the limitation of brow length, the amount of skin removed is restricted to prevent secondary brow drooping deformities, generally not exceeding 0.8 cm according to our experience. For patients with more pronounced upper eyelid laxity, this correction may not be thorough.

Double eyelid plastic surgery involves designing the incision line above the tarsal plate and removing skin above the incision to correct upper eyelid laxity. Its advantage is forming double eyelids preferred by many Eastern people while correcting upper eyelid skin laxity, with the resulting scar hidden within the double eyelid line. The disadvantage is that the recovery period of the formed double eyelid line is longer due to the gradually thickening dermis layer beyond the tarsal plate. The more skin removed, the greater the difference in thickness between the dermis layers above and below the incision, leading to less natural double eyelids. To avoid "cat ears" at the end of the double eyelids, the width of the skin removed generally does not exceed 0.5 cm, plus the folded skin width of about 0.3 cm for the double eyelids. Through double eyelid surgery, skin laxity within 0.8 cm can be corrected. Thus, for some patients, simply using double eyelid plastic surgery often fails to satisfactorily correct upper eyelid laxity, and double eyelid surgery after may cause the distance between the eyebrows and eyes to be too short, potentially making the originally drooping eyebrows even more droopy.

Based on personal experience, to achieve better improvement in patient appearance and function, I believe that the correction of upper eyelid skin laxity should be tailored to the individual, specifically as follows: First, determine whether the shape and distance between the eyebrows and eyes conform to normal aesthetic standards; secondly, assess the degree of upper eyelid laxity and measure the required width of skin to be removed; thirdly, determine if the patient originally had double or single eyelids and whether they wish to retain their original double or single eyelid shape. Based on these three conditions, the surgical method can be basically determined. 1 If the patient is dissatisfied with the color and shape of the eyebrows, then brow excision is the preferred option, achieving simultaneous correction of undesirable brow shapes and skin laxity. 2 If the amount of skin to be removed is between 0.5-0.8 cm, in such cases there is generally no obvious brow drooping, and any surgical method can achieve good results. According to our experience, if the patient originally had double eyelids or single eyelids but did not want double eyelid surgery, and the distance between the eyebrows and eyes was over 2.0 cm, selecting a lower brow margin incision would yield more ideal results. If an incision method double eyelid surgery is used, the designed double eyelid line width should not exceed 0.6 cm, and the eyelid margin vascular arch should be preserved as much as possible to shorten the postoperative recovery time. 3 For patients with more noticeable skin laxity where the planned skin removal width exceeds 0.8 cm but is less than 1.2 cm, pure brow skin excision can be performed. Brow skin excision can also be combined with double eyelid plastic surgery; if the amount of skin to be removed is over 1.2 cm, only a combination of brow and double eyelid surgery can achieve better postoperative results.
