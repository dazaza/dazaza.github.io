Rosacea and rhinophyma are the same disease, just named differently by different doctors. Rosacea is also known as rose acne or red nose; all these names are derived from its appearance. Rosacea is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that occurs on the nose and cheeks, commonly affecting middle-aged men. It typically presents as redness of the skin on the nose, especially prominent on the tip of the nose. The occurrence of rosacea affects the appearance of many patients, particularly women. Below, experts from the Department of Dermatology at Shanxi Huimin Hospital introduce the symptoms of rosacea and what causes it.

1. Rosacea mainly occurs on the nose, but as the condition progresses, it can also affect the face and neck. It is more common in women, but men tend to have more severe cases.

2. Patients with rosacea may feel burning, swelling pain, or itching discomfort. Taiyuan Skin Hospital

3. Rosacea has three phases: erythema, papules, and rhinophyma. In the early stage, called the erythema phase, symptoms include diffuse redness on the nose or face, along with dilated capillaries and excessive sebum secretion: subsequently, papules and pustules appear on the erythema, called the papule phase; as the condition further develops, nodules of varying sizes and uneven hypertrophic growths form, called the rhinophyma phase.

Due to the obvious dilation of blood vessels, sometimes the dilated small blood vessels under the skin can be seen in a tree-like pattern. Because of the vigorous secretion of local sebaceous glands, the nose appears red and shiny; as the condition progresses, the skin can thicken, and even develop rashes or small abscesses, with a rough and uneven appearance resembling wine lees, hence the name rosacea. In some people, the thickening of the skin on the tip of the nose is particularly significant, making the rough tip of the nose enlarge conspicuously like a tumor. Although this condition may not cause significant subjective symptoms, it does affect appearance, often causing distress. Taiyuan Skin Hospital

The cause of rosacea is relatively complex, and the exact reason for developing this disease remains unclear. It is generally believed that the occurrence of this condition is mainly related to Demodex mite infection; additionally, it is associated with metabolic disorders; people who smoke, drink alcohol, and frequently consume spicy foods have a higher incidence of the disease; furthermore, cardiovascular diseases, endocrine dysfunction, or infections within the body can also trigger the onset of the disease. Besides mite infections, excessive sebum secretion, vascular dysregulation leading to prolonged capillary dilation, and other factors are closely related to the onset of the disease.

However, if it is dark red bumps left after inflammation has healed, they can naturally fade without treatment, usually taking about six months, although some may form small cysts that are difficult to fade. Because the cause of the disease is still unclear, a complete cure remains challenging. However, starting from preventing triggering factors, controlling the progression of the disease, and alleviating symptoms is achievable. For example, first, one should avoid triggering factors and promptly treat gastrointestinal diseases; properly manage local and systemic foci; reduce consumption of spicy and stimulating foods, quit smoking and drinking alcohol, coffee, exposure to sunlight, and emotional excitement; increase intake of fruits and vegetables, maintain regular bowel movements; appropriately use vitamin-based medications (such as Vitamin B12, Vitamin B6) and traditional Chinese medicine (such as pear leaf lung-clearing decoction, nourishing yin lung-clearing ointment, and modified four-substances decoction), which can achieve certain effects. Topical application of ointments (such as rosacea ointment) can also be effective. Additionally, local treatments should include anti-mite drugs, sulfur ointments or washes, and anti-inflammatory metronidazole ointments. Taiyuan Skin Hospital

A crucial point in treatment is to try to prevent the symptoms of rosacea from appearing. Improving diet and lifestyle can help significantly, including the following aspects:

* A two-week diary of rosacea triggers should be kept, noting possible reasons that could cause flare-ups or worsening, so as to identify and avoid these triggers later.

* Maintain a light diet, eat more fruits and vegetables, avoid stimulating foods and drinks, and correct constipation.

* Avoid contact with irritating substances, use non-soap cleansers, and avoid using astringents and abrasives. Taiyuan Skin Hospital

* Minimize sun exposure, wear wide-brimmed hats, and use sunscreens with at least 15 SPF.
