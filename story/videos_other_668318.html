The Development of the Leather Goods Industry Faces Obstacles, with a Lack of High-end Brands Domestically. The three tiers of the leather goods market are formed due to the natural conditions, economic differences, and varying consumer perceptions across different regions, generally divided into high-end branded leather goods markets, mass-market trend markets, and affordable leather goods markets.

High-end brands are concentrated overseas. The high-end branded leather goods market mainly operates products that are selective in materials, sophisticated in craftsmanship, and expensive, both domestic and international brands. These are primarily found in the superstores and brand specialty shops of major cities in China, with consumers mostly being successful individuals from various industries. High-end leather goods mainly consist of European and American, Hong Kong and Taiwan brands that entered the domestic large malls during the 1980s to 1990s, along with Chinese brands developed domestically. International brands include France's Louis Vuitton, Pierre Cardin, Cartier, YVES SAINT LAURENT, CHANEL, etc. These high-end brands are concentrated overseas, with virtually no domestic brand able to compete with them.

Domestic focus is on affordable leather goods. The mass-market trend market caters to mid-range and upper mid-range genuine leather product consumers, representing a vast and most potential-filled space, also the most fiercely competitive one. Its operating venues are mainly several large professional leather goods wholesale markets nationwide, medium and small-sized wholesale markets in major cities, retail outlets in various local malls and brand specialty shops, with some products exported through foreign trade companies to many countries and regions overseas.

The affordable product market satisfies the needs of lower-income consumer segments at home and abroad, focusing on fabric, synthetic fiber, and artificial leather products. This is the earliest rising market domestically, producing the largest number of products and exporting the most. Hebei Baigou and Zhejiang Luqiao are the largest distribution centers for affordable leather goods in China, with other large leather goods wholesale markets also having partial distributions. The manufacturing processes and quality of affordable leather goods are relatively inferior to genuine leather products, but they offer a wide variety of material options, flexible design possibilities, and low prices, making their products widespread in towns and cities of all sizes across China and selling well overseas.

Affordable leather goods mainly come from Hebei, Zhejiang, Liaoning, Sichuan, and Shiling Town in Huadu District, Guangzhou. Among these, Shiling's products, being closest to the origin of advanced technology and having relatively more accessible information, enjoy advantages in style freshness, rich and unique materials, and relatively stable quality in affordable markets nationwide, thus attracting the favor of many domestic and international leather goods dealers.

Lack of high-end leather goods maintenance domestically. More consumers choose to purchase brands overseas, but due to the distance from the place of origin, many maintenance issues for famous brand leather goods are perplexing. In response to this need, some merchants have also begun to target the high-end leather goods maintenance industry. However, problems such as low industry standards, disputes over rights protection, etc., continue to arise in this industry.

"Buying a bag and having to send it to Hong Kong frequently for maintenance is too costly, and it's hard to find reliable leather goods maintenance stores locally." Xiao Lin, who works at an accounting firm, is a "bag enthusiast," with her collection of various branded bags numbering no less than thirty. Several larger luxury brand specialty shop managers in the city stated that bags can undergo simple cleaning and maintenance in-store, but processes like coloring, washing, and renewal, which require specialized chemicals and tools, are not provided by these luxury stores. "We can also help customers send their bags back to the place of origin, but these costs must be borne by the customer," a staff member said. Since they are only salespeople and their brand has not established repair or maintenance departments in mainland China, they basically only handle sales, with after-sales issues needing to contact the original factory for resolution.

What happens if there are accidents during the maintenance of luxury leather goods? How to define the responsibilities between merchants and consumers? How should the store compensate for damage or loss of leather goods? As a new industry, there are still legal and regulatory gaps in this field. These issues make many domestic consumers wary of luxury goods maintenance.

Due to the relatively short development time of the domestic leather goods industry, there are still some problems in its development process. The overall design level of the leather goods market is relatively low, with a scarcity of various professional technical talents and designers. Few people have received proper education and professional training, resulting in a significant gap between art academy graduates' practical work abilities and professionals grown within enterprises. Most domestic small enterprises with fifty to sixty employees lack long-term vision among operators, insufficient patience in nurturing potential professional technicians, and overly rely on copying popular styles from famous brands, failing to fully utilize designers to leverage their respective product advantages.
