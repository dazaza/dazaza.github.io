Leave integrity, and in return, a sea of spring light.

by musicshow5 on 2012-02-15 14:40:12

After many years of walking, I have profound feelings. Alas, people are not as simple as they used to be. Who among us still holds that sincerity? Now, when I look up at the sky, is it still as azure as before? Our hearts also need to be called again. We need honesty to purify our hearts and return to that azure sky. "Honesty is the foundation, trust is the sky", "words must be trustworthy, actions must bear fruit". The tradition of honesty from ancient times to the present will never fade. A tree that is not straight cannot become a pillar. Only in mutual trust can we open the door to communication and seek joy in our hearts. In the midst of the world's complexity, leave a piece of pure land for honesty, so that we may establish our image and reveal our most sincere brilliance.

In the ups and downs of business, once-pure hearts have been more or less stained by the dirt of profit, losing honesty. Business frauds are everywhere, such as Mengniu milk powder, which has harmed countless young lives. Let's set these aside for now, since I am not an expert and do not clearly understand some of the dealings. I only dare to comment on the dark stories of lotus seeds. Decades ago, in the rivers of Huashi and Juanjiang, carloads of iron lotus seeds with shells were immersed to increase their weight, deceiving the elderly scales. Up until today, the tricks have become even more varied. The reputation of Xianglian has been declining, and it is impossible to count how many tons of whitened lotus seeds processed with hydrogen peroxide have been sold through unscrupulous merchants, eventually reaching bustling cities like Shanghai and Beijing. Recently, there has been the despicable act of mixing lime powder to disguise them as peeled white lotus seeds with a clean color. The former can cause cancer, and the latter can harm the body...

Due to the lack of honesty, the rampant counterfeit goods make one furious. Honesty not only needs time and sincerity to test, but it has degenerated to the point where health must be gambled as a stake. The painstakingly made counterfeit lotus seeds may smell of their so-called fragrance and have an appealing color, but before you buy them, you certainly won't feel the darkness inside them. Once exposed, what was just self-congratulatory elegance quickly turns into regret and disappointment, leading to thoughts of "if only I hadn't done this or that..."

Honesty cannot be priced openly; it is not a totem written on the face for others to admire, but something that needs to be planted in the heart to bloom the most beautiful flowers and bear the sweetest fruits. By leaving behind honesty, one reaps a springtime of their own.

Song Jiang, who could neither stabilize the country nor command respect through martial prowess, became the leader of the Hall of Righteousness solely because of his commitment to honesty—a typical example. As a shopkeeper of tribute lotus seeds and a mere farmer, he understands this principle: being kind to others, treating them with honesty, not substituting inferior goods, not challenging others' intelligence, and not tarnishing the brand he has supported for so long, all in order to display the clarity like the sea.

This is the principle I adhere to: upholding honesty, operating peacefully, promoting the methods and benefits of lotus seeds, resulting in the Xianglian culture that has flown through a thousand years. Savoring the pure lotus fragrance accumulated over the ages.

I wish to hold a lotus flower in my hands, seeking that trace of purity that passes through the streets.
