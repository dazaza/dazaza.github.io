"The Struggle for Succession in the Wei Palace" - the grudges and entanglements triggered by the Cao family father and son's establishment of a successor.

by man8737 on 2011-11-14 16:49:01

In the ninth year of Jian'an era of the Han Dynasty, Cao Cao captured the city of Ye, leading to the downfall of the Yuan family. After numerous campaigns both south and north, Cao Cao gradually unified the northern part of China. As his ambitions grew, so did his desire to replace the Han dynasty, which was opposed not only by other warlords like Sun Quan and Liu Bei, but also by loyal ministers of the Han dynasty. With his advancing age, the issue of choosing a successor became urgent. The debate over whether to replace the Han dynasty or not became intertwined with this succession struggle. On one side was the faction supporting Cao Zhi, led by Yang Xiu and Xun Yu, while on the other side was the faction supporting Cao Pi, backed by the Sima family. This resulted in intense and dramatic power struggles. In the end, Cao Pi was chosen as the heir, and Xun Yu, Yang Xiu, and others were killed for their loyalty to the Han dynasty and opposition to Cao Cao's plans to replace it. After Cao Cao's death, Cao Pi ended the Han dynasty and established the Wei dynasty, posthumously honoring Cao Cao as Emperor Wu of Wei. To eliminate potential threats, Cao Pi poisoned his second brother Cao Zhang and challenged his third brother Cao Zhi with the "Seven Steps" poem. Unexpectedly, their mother, Lady Bian, intervened, sparing Cao Zhang's life. Cao Zhi then composed the famous poem "Boiling Beans While Burning the Husks," effectively dissuading Cao Pi from further plots against him.
