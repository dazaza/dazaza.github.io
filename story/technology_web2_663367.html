Ordinary stores may take your money for dry cleaning but actually wash it with water. Moreover, dry-cleaning oil is harmful to the human body. Really. Experience One: Hand wash is a must. Inside each down jacket, there is a small label printed with maintenance and washing instructions. Careful people would notice that 90% of down jackets indicate hand wash only, strictly avoiding dry cleaning, as the chemicals used in dry cleaning affect its warmth retention and can age the fabric. Machine washing and spin-drying can easily cause uneven filling of the down jacket, leading to deformation, affecting both appearance and warmth retention. Experience Two: Wash at 30-degree water temperature. First, soak the down jacket in cold water for 20 minutes to fully moisten it inside and out. Dissolve the detergent in 30-degree warm water, then soak the down jacket for 15 minutes, and gently brush it with a soft-bristled brush. Rinsing should also be done with warm water, which helps the detergent dissolve completely in the water, making the down jacket cleaner. Experience Three: The concentration of laundry powder should not be too high. If you must use laundry powder to clean the down jacket, usually two basins of water with 4 to 5 tablespoons of laundry powder is appropriate. If the concentration is too high, it is difficult to rinse thoroughly. Residual laundry powder in the down affects its fluffiness and greatly reduces its warmth retention. Experience Four: Preferably use neutral detergent. Neutral detergent causes the least damage to the fabric and down. Using alkaline detergent, if not rinsed thoroughly, the residual detergent can harm the down jacket and easily leave white marks on the surface, affecting its appearance. To remove residual alkaline detergent, after rinsing twice, add two teaspoons of vinegar to warm water, soak the down jacket for a while, then rinse again. Vinegar can neutralize alkaline detergent. Experience Five: Do not wring out. After washing the down jacket, do not wring it out; instead, squeeze out the water and lay it flat or hang it to dry. Avoid direct sunlight and ironing to prevent damage to the garment. After drying, gently pat it to restore the down jacket's fluffiness and softness.
