The results of the 2008 Global PC Gamer Hardware Survey are revealed - Age1983

by hexiaoyao on 2008-12-22 23:48:25

One and a half years later, Valve once again published an investigation on the status of foreign PC gamers' machines. The investigation included detailed data from CPUs to operating systems. From this survey, we can see what mainstream gaming PCs look like today, and some of the data can also point out the future trends in gaming PCs. I hope this article will provide some reference for friends who plan to upgrade their computers by the end of the year. By the way, after carefully reading it, you might find that your configuration, which you think is already outdated, is still mainstream globally.

Without further ado, let's first take a look at the CPU side. From the chart above, we can see that Intel CPUs have a dominant advantage with a 64% market share. Although AMD has been performing quite hard recently, in the previous year's survey conducted in 2007, the ratio between the two was evenly split. Since the launch of the Core 2 series, the balance between the two has gradually been broken. Although the survey did not include specific CPU models, judging from the CPU clock speed, Intel's most popular products are still mainstream offerings ranging from 2.3GHz to 2.69GHz, accounting for as much as 20% of all users.

Next, let's take a look at the data regarding the number of CPU cores. The survey results show that dual-core CPUs are currently the most widespread, with nearly 50% of the total. Judging from recent growth data, the ownership of dual-core CPUs continues to increase, while the ownership of quad-core CPUs, whose prices have dropped significantly, has not shown significant growth. Surprisingly, there are still many players using single-core CPUs. Nowadays, Intel's latest CPU i7 has hit the market, and with AMD's upcoming latest quad-core product Phenom II, next year's AI battle promises to be exciting.

Below, we present the results of the latest red-green (Nvidia vs ATI) graphics card battle that gamers are most concerned about. Although in the past six months, ATI has reclaimed much lost ground with its 4XXX series GPUs, the survey shows that 65% of players chose Nvidia GPUs, making N cards still the preferred choice for PC gamers. Specifically, the GeForce 8800 series GPU has the largest installed base. In terms of onboard GPUs, Intel's products account for 1% of the total, indicating that current onboard GPUs still cannot meet the performance needs of most gamers. DirectX 10 has been officially launched for almost two years now. From the initial performance demonstrations to the current abundance of corresponding games, DirectX 10 is gradually beginning to demonstrate its true strength.

The adoption rate of VISTA bundled with DirectX 10 is also gradually increasing. From the chart above, we can see that the installed base of DirectX 10-compatible GPUs accounts for approximately 47% of the total. At this time last year, DirectX 9 was still the mainstream. Although the transition between generations is inevitable, only about 20% of the total use the combination of DirectX 10-compatible GPUs and VISTA. Is it due to insufficient adoption of VISTA or not enough DirectX 10-compatible games? This is a matter of personal interpretation.

Let's take a look at the operating system, which gamers are more concerned about. Windows XP holds a stable first place with nearly 70% market share. The remaining 30% are mostly using Vista, and this proportion has a clear increasing trend. Surprisingly, the proportion of players using 64-bit Vista accounts for 7% of the total, with nearly one-quarter of Vista users choosing the 64-bit version. This may be related to the significant drop in memory prices in recent times.

In terms of memory, the survey results show that players with 2GB (or more) of memory account for nearly half. Last year, when the survey was conducted, only about 1% of players chose this option. In just over a year, what used to be a luxury configuration has become a mass-market level, which is truly remarkable.

The final item in the survey is screen resolution, which is perhaps the content game developers are most interested in. Unlike the above survey results, global players seem less enthusiastic about high-resolution graphics. Players using XGA (1024x768) and SXGA (1280x1024) account for more than half of the total. Although the overall trend of both is gradually decreasing, the numbers suggest that old 4:3 monitors may still be the most popular this year. In terms of widescreen, players using WSXGA+ (1680x1050) account for 14% of all widescreen users, while players using full HD WUXGA (1920x1200) only account for 3.6% of the total. Full HD is still a luxury enjoyed by a few.

After looking at the survey above, do you have a deeper understanding of your computer? Since this survey was conducted through Steam, domestic players may not have the opportunity to participate in such surveys for certain well-known reasons. Hopefully, one day, domestic players can also participate in such global-scale surveys.
