Is your child's left and right brain development balanced?

by childrenstory126 on 2012-02-23 15:27:57

Observation: Children's behavior reveals secrets of the brain

Dingding’s mom and Xiaoling’s mom are good friends. One Sunday, Dingding’s mom brought Dingding over to Xiaoling’s house for a visit. The moms were chatting leisurely in the living room while the two kids were playing in the room. However, not long after, cries from Xiaoling could be heard from the room.

It turned out that Xiaoling had built a castle with building blocks following the pattern on the instruction manual and showed it to Dingding. Dingding thought it didn’t look nice, so without saying anything, he dismantled Xiaoling’s castle and rebuilt it according to his own design. As a result, Xiaoling accused Dingding of ruining her toy and demanded that he rebuild the castle exactly as per the instructions. However, the complex steps in the manual left Dingding feeling dizzy and confused, unable to replicate the original pattern. Losing patience, he pushed the building blocks aside with a loud "clatter," and the castle disappeared...

Analysis:

Dingding and Xiaoling are typical examples of right-brain and left-brain children respectively. Dingding is creative, enjoys designing and developing his own games, and is willing to try new things, but at the same time lacks patience and has difficulty understanding flat, procedural information. On the other hand, Xiaoling, who is left-brain dominant, can carefully follow the instructions to build an identical castle, demonstrating excellent comprehension and thinking skills, but lacks creativity and冒险spirit, and finds it difficult to accept others' opinions.

Learning: What parents need to know about the brain.

The human brain can be divided into the left hemisphere and the right hemisphere. The right brain, also known as the artistic brain (leaning towards artistic thinking), mainly handles emotional experiences in life, controlling music, sound, painting, graphics, color, emotion, spatial recognition, imagination, creation, etc. The left brain, however, is the logical brain (leaning towards scientific thinking), responsible for rational thinking and analysis, primarily handling language, text, symbols, analysis, calculation, comprehension, reasoning, judgment, etc. Each hemisphere has its own responsibilities.

In response to the different divisions of the brain, the body also has corresponding coordination. The right brain directly commands the motor functions of the left side of the body, such as the left eye, left ear, left hand, left foot, etc. The left brain, on the other hand, controls the motor functions of the right side of the body, such as the right eye, right ear, right hand, right foot, etc. At the same time, the motor functions of the left and right sides of the body also promote the development of the corresponding brain hemispheres.

Check: Some behaviors mislead children.

In real life, many adults intentionally or unintentionally cultivate the activity abilities of the right side of a child's body: making the child use their right hand to grasp objects, using tools with the right hand, balancing by supporting with the right foot... This inadvertently reinforces the "right advantage." Neglecting the development of the left side of the body means the right brain does not get the appropriate exercise, and the potential related to the right brain cannot be fully developed, leading to "imbalance in the development of the left and right brain."

In fact, during infancy, a child's brain development is in a self-balancing period. Sometimes, unbalanced education and brain stimulation by parents may actually hinder the development of a child's brain.

Parents often notice this phenomenon: during infancy, children have a particular fondness for various advertisements on TV. As soon as they hear the sound of a TV advertisement, they will put down their toys or bottles and focus quietly on the TV until the advertisement ends. This phenomenon is the best explanation for the coordination and self-synchronization of the left and right brains in infants. Because the main feature of advertisements is the rapid linkage of sound, images, colors, music, and actions, which can, to a certain extent, stimulate both the left and right brains simultaneously into a harmonious and relatively excited state.

After entering kindergarten, many children start listening to stories, reading books, and writing, stimulating the left brain to become active. Meanwhile, the natural curiosity and love for entertainment, play, and movement during early childhood are intentionally or unintentionally restricted, slowing down or even shutting down the development of the right brain. Of course, some children come into contact with music, learn to play instruments, or learn to paint earlier. Does this develop right-brain thinking? In theory, it seems like it would, but during infancy, a child's understanding of music and painting is spontaneous. If adults impose compulsory demands on their playing of instruments or painting, the child's right brain may resist when receiving these inputs. Therefore, if the interest classes, books, and other activities beneficial to brain thinking that parents choose for their children are ones the child is unwilling to accept, it will be difficult to achieve good results.

Operation: Training + Nutrition, double-pronged approach to create a smart baby.

Child psychologists and educational experts have discovered through long-term research and observation, and proven through practice, that the infant stage is the most valuable period for the development of the brain and intelligence in one's lifetime. Fully developing an infant's intelligence during this period, coordinating and utilizing both the left and right brain, and fully integrating them, has very important significance for a child's overall development in the future, including intellectual psychology and learning and living abilities.

To ensure the full development and balanced growth of a child's left and right brain, training must begin from an early age. Parents should consciously enhance multi-faceted brain development exercises for their children, supplemented by appropriate nutritious foods, and adopt different stimulation methods for children at different stages.

Balanced Development of Left and Right Brain in Children

As stated above, smart children are neither particularly left-brain dominant nor exceptionally right-brain outstanding. Simultaneously awakening the potential of both the left and right brain can create truly intelligent babies. So, what specific plans can help children fully unleash their "intelligence potential"?

Optimal Plan: Reading Classics

Dr. Lin Zhuxiong, who stayed in Germany, analyzed the scientific nature of children reading classics from a physiological medicine perspective. Since children are completely relaxed during the process of reciting classics, sometimes accompanied by free swaying of the body, the brain waves convert from β waves to α waves. Under the promotion of α-type brain waves, the "intuitive learning" of recitation is unleashed. The memory function and subconscious of the brain start interacting, enhancing memory, judgment, attention, inspiration, and creativity. Dr. Lin emphasized that the process of children reciting classics resembles chanting. The eyes looking at the text and the rhythm of chanting directly stimulate and activate the right brain, while distinguishing word shapes for memorization is the work of the left brain. The entire process of reading classics uses both the left and right brain functions simultaneously, increasing learning ability by 2 to 5 times. Through the undulating recitation and training, these classic ancient poems and texts become the "navigation charts" to awaken the dormant brain potential.

Latest Plan: Mental Abacus

In the computer age, why should we still spend a lot of effort teaching children to use an abacus?

During the fastest developmental period of their lives, children mostly use their right hands, correspondingly developing the left brain function better, while the left hand is used less and moves less flexibly, causing imbalance in the development of the left and right brain. Mental abacus education solves this problem both theoretically and practically.

Mental abacus uses both hands to move beads simultaneously; the left hand operates the front part of the abacus and the addition of carry-over digits, while the right hand operates the back part and the subtraction of complement numbers, and the left hand clears the abacus. Through the cooperation of both hands, the overall function of the brain is promoted, making vision, hearing, touch, and the entire muscle group coordinate consistently. Both hemispheres of the brain "work together," jointly directing the operation of the hands. The movement of the right hand develops the calculation and logical functions of the left brain; the movement of the left hand develops the imagination and creative functions of the right brain. During the mental abacus process, the two hemispheres of the brain seem to build an "information interchange bridge," improving the brain's ability to process information. After entering the advanced stage of mental abacus practice, a "brain image circle" forms in the child's brain, training "mental abacus operations," a process mainly dependent on the activity of the right brain. The integration of imagery thinking and abstract thinking strongly promotes the development of the right brain function, thereby promoting the balance and improvement of the overall brain function.

Children who have participated in mental abacus learning show four major achievements in intellectual development: their speed in calculations, instantaneous observation, solid memory, and accurate and rich imagination all surpass those of the control group. These outstanding effects are closely related to the balanced development of the left and right brain through mental abacus.

Game Plan I: [Cat's Cradle] Game

The [Cat's Cradle] game is hailed by foreign experts as one of the "best parent-child interactive games." Few people know that this ancient game, passed down for thousands of years, is also a great contributor to unlocking brain potential and promoting the development of both the left and right brain. Playing [Cat's Cradle] requires the use of both hands, accompanied by complex interweaving and flipping. Each of the ten fingers has its own role, and if one finger's movement is off, the resulting pattern may distort. During the [Cat's Cradle] game, researchers observe that the child's spirit is entirely in a joyful and relaxed state. Their spatial imagination and persistent interest are entirely spontaneous, unlike the rigid practices of painting or instrumental music. The child will not feel weary and cause the brain to issue a "resistance" command, so the alpha waves in the brain waves appear more frequently, holistically enhancing the child's brain function.

Game Plan II: [Finger Puppet] Game

Parents can draw a few "finger puppets" on small cloth bags or wooden sleeves and let the child wear them on different fingers of both hands. If this seems troublesome, you can use fine-tipped markers to draw various interesting head portraits on the fingertips (such as Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, Cinderella and her stepmother and prince, the wolf and the lamb, etc.), then let the child vividly act out the fairy tale "plot." Initially, more finger puppets can be placed on the commonly used right hand and fewer on the left hand, allowing the child to narrate and sing the story smoothly. Gradually, more finger puppets can be placed on the left hand and fewer on the right. You can gradually enrich the details of the story by using the pointing, bending, twisting, and tilting of the fingers to express the sadness, fear, regret, and other subtle emotional changes of the fairy tale characters. When there are more than 10 roles and the finger puppets are insufficient, parents can ask the child to take off their shoes and socks in warm weather and let the toes help.

When the storytelling ability of the toes on the left foot matches that of the fingers on the right hand, the child's overall brain capacity has taken a leap forward.
