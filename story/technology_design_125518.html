"走西口" can be translated as "Going West" or "Journey to the West" in English. However, if you are referring to the traditional Chinese folk song "Zou Xikou," it is often translated as "Going West" or literally "Leaving Through the Western Pass." The term has historical and cultural significance related to migration and trade routes in China.

Going West, this is a powerful and stirring eulogy for the tenacious struggle of Chinese children for survival!

In the early 20th century, Qixian County in Shanxi Province, known for "having no fertile land or benefit of water irrigation", local villagers could only survive by farming on the hills. However, after a major disaster every three years followed by three consecutive years of severe drought, people became famine victims every day, and the people were unable to make a living. As a result, men packed their simple luggage, listened to women sing "Going West" with tears in their eyes, and embarked on the road to the west. It was hard to leave the homeland, but they had to; it was hard to part with their beloved women, but they had to endure the separation. Life was filled with so much helplessness and bitterness!

The Tian Family Courtyard in Qixian County, whose ancestors made their fortune outside the pass by going west and returned to their hometown to acquire property, was a wealthy family. By the time of Tian Yaocu, he indulged in eating, drinking, gambling, and revelry. On the occasion of a celebration for his young master Tian Qing's "Zhuazhou" (a traditional Chinese ceremony for one-year-old children), Tian Yaocu lost both his family property and his wife Shuzhen in a gamble. The matriarch of the Tian family died of anger, and under duress, Tian Yaocu went west. Fortunately, Madame Shuzhen was saved by Xu, a revolutionary party member and a member of the Xingzhong Association whom she had previously rescued, thus escaping from being humiliated by the winner, Xia San. In order to support her son Tian Qing, Shuzhen gave her nine-year-old daughter Dan Dan as a child bride to the Liang family. Ten years later, Tian Qing became a sheepherder. Master Xu, who returned from revolutionary activities, met Tian Qing by chance, and funded his education at a private school while teaching him martial arts. While engaging in revolutionary activities, Master Xu also helped Madame Shuzhen and her son Tian Qing through difficult times. Shuzhen was grateful for Xu's rescue and assistance in the past, admired his uprightness, and gradually developed feelings for him. When this matter was discovered by Tian Qing, Shuzhen intended to commit suicide. It was Dan Dan who explained the ins and outs of the situation to Tian Qing that he came to understand his own background.
