Zhangjiang Independent Innovation: Building a World-class Science and Technology Park by 2020

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 17:37:24

Zhangjiang National Innovation Demonstration Zone, which has incorporated 80% of the knowledge economy-intensive areas in Shanghai, has once again become a focus at the planning level after receiving delegated powers from Shanghai.

Recently, the Ministry of Science and Technology issued the "Development Plan for Zhangjiang National Independent Innovation Demonstration Zone in Shanghai (2013-2020)" (hereinafter referred to as the "Zhangjiang Plan"), clearly stating that the plan will be centered around building a world-class science park. As of now, Beijing Zhongguancun, Wuhan Donghu, and Shanghai Zhangjiang, three national independent innovation demonstration zones, have all released their planning outlines up to 2020. However, insiders from Zhangjiang believe that the relationship between the three locations in many industrial layouts is more about cooperation than competition.

Cooperation Greater Than Competition

According to the outline, Zhangjiang will focus on developing five main industries: next-generation information technology, high-end equipment manufacturing, biomedicine, energy conservation and environmental protection, new materials, as well as two leading industries: new energy and new energy vehicles. After reviewing the planning outlines of the three major independent innovation demonstration zones, it was found that electronic information, biomedicine, and environmental protection are all included in the industrial layouts of the three demonstration zones, with Beijing and Wuhan's plans preceding Zhangjiang's.

In terms of regional strategic positioning, Zhangjiang is an "important engine driving the innovative development of Shanghai, the Yangtze River Delta region, and even the entire eastern region," Donghu will become the "growth pole for the rise of central China," while Zhongguancun will "radiate nationwide."

Therefore, there is a significant difference in the scale of development by 2020 among these three independent innovation demonstration zones. Zhangjiang announced its development goal: by 2020, total corporate revenue will reach approximately 6 trillion yuan, while Wuhan Donghu aims for 3 trillion yuan, and Zhongguancun targets 10 trillion yuan.

Mawen Gang, Director of the Comprehensive Coordination Department of the Zhangjiang High-tech Industrial Development Zone Management Committee, told our reporter that compared to other independent innovation zones, Zhangjiang also has its own characteristics in the planning aspect. The Wuhan Donghu Demonstration Zone adopts a complete urban development model, which is quite different from Zhangjiang.

Compared to Zhongguancun, the lack of large-scale research institutes is considered a "weakness" in Zhangjiang's exploration of independent innovation. Mawen Gang said that the 18 parks and over 90 points of Zhangjiang are laid out according to the axis line of Shanghai's urban development, thus integrating into Shanghai's overall layout and radiating across Shanghai.

Regarding the overlap in industrial layouts, Mawen Gang stated that the three locations have a cooperative rather than competitive relationship in terms of industry. "For example, Huawei has R&D institutions across the country, utilizing various resources from different regions to integrate and participate in international competition."

Promoting Shanghai's Transformation

The planning of Zhangjiang High-tech Industrial Development Zone carries the heavy responsibility of shouldering Shanghai's innovation ecosystem. In terms of spatial layout, Zhangjiang High-tech Industrial Development Zone has expanded from 63 square kilometers to 294 square kilometers, and then to over 470 square kilometers. A feature of the Zhangjiang High-tech Industrial Development Zone's planning is that its various blocks are distributed in a dot-like pattern across multiple geographic areas in the city.

"The scientific and technological innovation resources gathered in various geographic areas not only have strong radiation and driving effects locally but also promote the overall transformation and development of Shanghai through multi-point collaboration," said Hou Jin, Deputy Director of the Shanghai Zhangjiang High-tech Industrial Development Zone Management Committee.

Besides spatial-level promotion, the pilot trials at the institutional level are more important for Shanghai. "On some issues that may not have been fully resolved, broken through, or achieved mature experiences overall, Zhangjiang can play a leading and exemplary role, providing us with very good experience and reference for subsequent follow-up," said Hou Jin.

To this end, the Shanghai municipal government requires Zhangjiang Demonstration Zone to make breakthroughs in five aspects: equity incentives, science and technology finance, talent experimental zone, fiscal and taxation support, and institutional mechanism innovation.

Taking institutional mechanism innovation as an example, one month before the release of the plan, the Shanghai municipal government piloted the delegation of municipal administrative approval powers in Zhangjiang. Currently, the pilot work in the park is ongoing, and the ten offices of the Zhangjiang Management Committee are formulating detailed implementation rules, expected to be issued by the end of August.
