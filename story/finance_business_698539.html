Lotus starts drought-resistant charity sale and nude donation

by ygheojhsdrgm on 2012-03-09 10:02:08

The shocking image of people having to descend hundred-meter deep wells to fetch water has captured the hearts of many readers. This is a microcosm of Yunnan's once-in-a-century drought that has persisted for three years, and also reflects the lives of many simple farmers who rely on the weather for their livelihoods. Following this report, the headquarters of Lotus Cars in Hangzhou immediately responded. After collaborating with the Yunnan Provincial Youth Development Foundation (referred to as the Youth Foundation) to aid Qinghai Lake in Xiangyun County, they took action again to contribute their share as a socially responsible enterprise to Yunnan's drought relief efforts.

Lotus Cars promptly dispatched its local Yunnan dealer, Yunnan Bi Hai Yuan Company, for an on-site investigation and requested a feasible assistance plan. Two colleagues from Yunnan Bi Hai Yuan descended alongside villagers through a mountain cave with a slope exceeding 70 degrees, accommodating only one person at a time, to reach the underground river. After a simple measurement, it was found to be dozens of meters deep, and the underground river could continuously provide water resources, meeting the requirements for corresponding water extraction and drinking water projects.

Donation of a Lotus Sedan to Resolve Water Extraction Difficulties

With the collaboration of our newspaper, after detailed discussions with the local villagers and budgeting for the feasibility and cost of water extraction, it was determined that the initial phase of the project to bring water to the surface would require an 11.5-kilowatt pump, a generator of over 22 kilowatts, high-strength water tanks and pipes to prevent bursting, along with fuel, with a budget of approximately 100,000 yuan. Upon receiving the message back at the Lotus headquarters, the company decided to donate a Lotus L3 coupe as the first installment for water extraction and drought resistance. With the agreement of the Lotus headquarters, our newspaper collaborated with the Youth Foundation, Qujing Municipal Publicity Department, Communist Youth League Qujing Municipal Committee, and Fumin County Publicity Department to conduct a reserve-price charity auction at Zhujiang Source Square in Qujing City on March 10th. The reserve price was set at 50,000 yuan, and by 15:30, the highest bidder would acquire the vehicle. All proceeds would be handed over to the Youth Foundation specifically for the village's drought-resistant drinking water project, with the Youth Foundation issuing tax-free donation invoices. Any surplus would also be used for drought relief in other parts of Qujing. The Youth Foundation also accepts targeted donations from all sectors of society for "Qujing Drought Relief" and has specially set up a 24-hour donation consultation hotline: 13700691177.

Local Dealer Yunnan Bi Hai Yuan Rekindles Efforts

As a local Lotus car dealer, Yunnan Bi Hai Yuan is also a public-spirited enterprise. It has previously organized car club activities to collaborate with the Youth Foundation, donating 110,000 yuan to Baiyan Village in Dali and nearly 100,000 yuan for water delivery and donations in Bailu Township. With strong support from the manufacturer, they have decided to further their efforts for drought relief. If customers place orders for Lotus cars on-site, they can enjoy group-buying public welfare prices and donate the entire 5,000-yuan deposit to the Youth Foundation specifically for drought relief in Qujing disaster areas. It is reported that the group-buying public welfare price of the Lotus L3 has dropped below 70,000 yuan, while the Lotus L5 offers a discount of 10,000 yuan from its market price of 99,800 yuan. We hope that some urgently needed car buyers and philanthropists will pay attention and support or send text messages to 13888099955 to participate in the charity auction, noting the bidding amount.

Interpretation of the Donated Vehicle

Lotus Cars is a project introduced by Youngman Automobile Group featuring British Lotus brand and technology. The company operates under a production philosophy different from most automobile manufacturers, known as "independent enterprises, joint venture model." The Lotus L3 series vehicles were meticulously crafted over three years by British Lotus. Their design standards, process standards, and quality control systems are all executed according to British Lotus standards, embodying the three top technologies of British Lotus: "chassis technology, exterior technology, safety technology," and integrating the technical characteristics of British Lotus sports cars.
