Zhisheg Education Application Guide: Top Ten Institutions for Master of Law List, Wuhan University Included

by qindingdangx on 2012-03-05 13:22:54

Zhishe Education Network [http://www.zsjykj.com/] reports that the on-duty master's degree examination in October 2011 has come to an end. Nowadays, more and more people in society have realized the benefits of postgraduate entrance examination preparation. With the changes in the overall employment market environment, pursuing a postgraduate degree while working has become the choice of more and more aspiring young people, increasingly favored and promoted by everyone. Wuhan Zhishe Training Institution collected and organized a list of the top ten schools for enrolling in law masters, among which Wuhan University, a prestigious university in Wuhan, is included.

Peking University

Peking University's Law School is very strong and could be a dream for any reader. PKU, like Renmin University, offers many lectures each semester, often inviting some well-known scholars and lawyers from home and abroad to give lectures, which is very attractive. However, Peking University's law master's program is quite expensive for the masses. In addition to nearly 50,000 yuan in tuition fees, extra money must be paid for graduation thesis defense. During classes in Shenzhen, each person receives a living allowance of 1000 yuan, and after returning to Beijing, there is a monthly subsidy of 400 yuan. However, due to classes being held in two different locations, all charges and subsidy situations are not very clear. After returning to Beijing, one mainly resides in Wanliu. The employment situation, as seen in 2005, was quite good. In recent years, the number of applicants has remained around 2800. Despite fewer applicants, and a relatively large number of admissions, the admission score line each year remains at a high level, showing Peking University's appeal and student quality.

Renmin University of China

Renmin University of China has a long history of training law masters, with great strength, being one of the first schools to start admitting law masters. Every year, over 2500 people apply to Renmin University, and the acceptance rate is relatively low. Starting from 2004, the scale of enrollment has been reduced, greatly increasing the difficulty of application. Tuition fees are 39,000 yuan for two years, with about 20 public-funded places in previous years, with a public-funded score line around 370. Renmin University places great emphasis on employment work, and every year, Renmin University law masters can find good jobs. The job situation in 2003 was not very good, coinciding with the SARS outbreak, but even so, the employment of Renmin University law masters was still good, slightly better than the general legal field. Most went to government agencies, accounting for more than half. Many stayed in Beijing, with two-thirds of a class staying in Beijing because of the larger proportion of current graduates. Many also joined various ministries, such as the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce, the Ministry of Labor and Social Security, the Ministry of Land and Resources, the Ministry of Public Security, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine, the National Audit Office, and the Taiwan Affairs Office of the State Council. Many who returned to their home provinces worked in provincial-level institutions or provincial branches of large organizations, such as the Insurance Regulatory Bureau. Some joined the public security and judicial systems, such as the Beijing Higher Procuratorate, the First Intermediate People's Court, and district courts. A significant number also joined banks, with many going to Guangzhou besides Beijing, including two at the Guangzhou Daily, the Municipal Party Committee General Office, the Personnel Department, the Shenzhen branch of the Bank of China, and the Guangdong branch of the Bank of China, etc. Some also joined companies, such as ZTE and Datang. Others engaged in education, such as one law master who co-founded a postgraduate entrance examination tutoring class with a teacher, with Huacheng Law Master being one of the training programs.

China University of Political Science and Law

The large number of public-funded places and its location in Beijing attract millions of students, making it the most difficult school to enter. Secondly, it is the largest professional law university in China, with many alumni and good employment prospects, and finally, the tuition fee is not high, offering good value for money. Therefore, the number of applicants to China University of Political Science and Law has remained stable, consistently around 2000 people. The disadvantage is that the law master's classes are held in Changping, far from the city center, taking about 2 hours by bus, possibly losing some opportunities for exchanges, listening to famous professors' lectures, and finding part-time practice. However, starting from 2009, law masters can go to the graduate school in the third year, studying at the Xitucheng campus in Haidian.

Tsinghua University

Tsinghua University's law building is very impressive, with the pillars at the entrance alone worth several million yuan each. The law school is well-funded, developing quickly, and places great emphasis on the cultivation of law masters. Since 2003, the school has increased its efforts to focus on the training of law masters. Accommodation conditions are good, with three girls per room and two boys per room, usually one law master sharing with one law graduate. However, the number of admissions is relatively small, with a large number of applicants each year, making it difficult. Those who pass the exam highly cherish this learning opportunity, with a well-recognized academic atmosphere. Graduates from previous years have had good employment prospects, with some joining the Big Four accounting firms and some large enterprises and public institutions. However, since Tsinghua University is not a traditional legal powerhouse, its overall strength is still inferior, and graduates do not have the same network support as those from Renmin University, Peking University, and Jilin University. Additionally, Tsinghua University has set up a graduate school branch in Shenzhen, admitting 100 law masters there. However, some have reflected that the situation there is not ideal, with a significant gap between ideals and reality.

Southwest University of Political Science and Law

Southwest University of Political Science and Law is a traditional law school with a deep historical foundation, characterized by practicality in its academic features. In 2003, the total number of students was 259, divided into five classes according to their place of origin. Accommodation was in newly renovated dormitories, approximately 30 square meters, with bathrooms, water rooms, 201 phones, broadband interfaces, air conditioning, integrated furniture and beds, costing 1200 yuan/year. In 2004, the recruitment scale was large, but the geographical limitations masked its advantages in employment and traditional networks. Some candidates chose other schools. Additionally, due to the lack of living allowances, scholarships, and free medical care, its attractiveness has decreased. However, honestly speaking, its influence in the legal system is still considerable.

Wuhan University

Wuhan University may have originally placed less emphasis on law masters, but now it has changed somewhat, with new appointments of professors rated as one of the top ten outstanding lawyers nationwide guiding internships. There is a law firm and a disadvantaged group assistance center available for internships, with course instructors being at least moderately famous middle-aged and young professors and doctoral supervisors within Wuhan University, offering decent faculty resources. However, since law masters are still a relatively new concept, there is still misunderstanding among some course instructors, with theoretical law teachers generally having little good to say about law masters, while those teaching administrative law, criminal law, etc., place more emphasis on law masters. Wuhan University's overall school atmosphere is very liberal, with one of the most beautiful campuses, especially when cherry blossoms bloom, it is truly beautiful.

Fudan University

Fudan University leverages the advantages of Shanghai, with a promising future. Fudan students have unique advantages in finding employment in Shanghai. Therefore, it is particularly attractive to students from all over the country who want to develop in Shanghai. However, for non-current graduates, household registration and archives will not be transferred.

Zhejiang University

My impression of Zhejiang University is "three beauties": delicious food, beautiful women, and picturesque scenery. Zhejiang University is right next to the West Lake, with beautiful scenery and good food. As the saying goes, heaven above, Suzhou and Hangzhou below, no need to mention the beautiful women. The Law School used to belong to Hangzhou University before the merger, but after several years of development, it has made remarkable progress. Zhejiang University has substantial funds and spares no effort in attracting talent and improving teaching quality. Zhejiang University's strengths are in legal theory, constitutional law, and administrative law, with Li Long, Sun Xiaoxia, and Lin Laifan being well-known scholars both domestically and internationally. In civil and commercial law, it was indeed relatively weak before, but it will soon become "powerful beyond measure" due to the impending introduction of several super heavyweight figures. In criminal law, a master's degree point has been added this year. 2. Great importance is placed on the teaching of law masters, with full courses in the morning every day, and some teachers prefer law masters to law graduates. Each two students are assigned one mentor, and any academic problems can be addressed at any time. The pressure is quite high because law masters, like law graduates, must publish articles in core journals to obtain their degree certificates. This reflects the rigorous and pragmatic nature of Zhejiang University's training plan. 3. In terms of treatment, there is no difference from other postgraduates. Only past graduates do not receive subsidies or medical insurance. They live in the Master-Doctor Building, with a good living atmosphere.

Nanjing University

Nanjing University has low tuition fees, good employment prospects, and a good reputation. Although Nanjing University's Law School is indeed not as strong as Renmin University and Peking University, because Jiangsu is a major economic province, Nanjing University's law masters can find good jobs within the province. Nanjing University's law masters have a particularly hardworking learning atmosphere, which is very admirable. Basic situation: 1. Admission quotas have always been controlled around 80. 2. There are ten public-funded places, where students do not need to pay training fees or accommodation fees, all current graduates, enjoying monthly postgraduate subsidies. 3. Provides national student loans for self-funded and commissioned students. 4. Fair复试. 5. Provides accommodation for out-of-town students, five people per room, each person 600 yuan/year. 6. All course instructors are the most popular teachers, with the same configuration as law graduates. 7. Mentors are allocated at the end of the first academic year, with each mentor supervising about five students.

Sun Yat-sen University

Freshmen entering Sun Yat-sen University are first introduced to the history of Sun Yat-sen University, its motto, and the spirit of Dr. Sun Yat-sen. The Law School does not have a long history at Sun Yat-sen University and is not among the top few in China, but it is definitely not a bad law school. It also places great emphasis on law masters. Additionally, the majority of Sun Yat-sen University's recruits are from Guangdong Province.
