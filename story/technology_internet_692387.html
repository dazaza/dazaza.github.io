&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； &；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； What to do when an elevator malfunctions or breaks down? 18671079388

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； One, Stay Calm

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；(One) Prying the door? Climbing out the window? Better to wait.

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Passengers trapped in elevators hope to leave the malfunctioning elevator as quickly as possible. Therefore, some trapped passengers forcibly pry open the doors or open the top window of the cabin to escape. Is this safe?

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； When an elevator malfunctions, there may be a failure in the door circuit. At this time, the elevator may abnormally start. Forcibly prying open the door can be very dangerous, which is called shearing, and this kind of shearing can easily cause personal injury. For the same reason, trapped passengers should not climb out from the skylight by themselves. The correct approach is for passengers to wait for professional rescue personnel to come and rescue them.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； (Two) Falling? Suffocating? No need to panic

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Many passengers are afraid that a malfunctioning elevator might fall. In fact, such concerns are unnecessary. Elevators are quite safe in terms of design. There's no need to worry about the steel cables connecting the cabin breaking because the state has specific regulations and requirements for elevator cables. The configuration of the steel cables is not only for carrying the cabin and its rated load but also considers the size of the traction force. Its tensile strength far exceeds the weight capacity of the elevator. Generally, most elevators are equipped with more than four steel cables, so elevators are safe and will not fall into the shaft.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Elevators also have an anti-fall system, including speed governors, safety clamps, and buffers at the bottom. Once it is detected that the elevator is descending at excessive speed, the speed governor will first stop the operation of the elevator drive motor. If the motor still does not stop, the speed governor will activate the safety clamp to grip the guide rail, forcing the cabin to remain stationary on the guide rail. Additionally, within a certain speed range, if the cabin directly impacts the buffer, it will also stop. Regardless of how the cabin stops, it will not cause significant impact on people.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； In a narrow and hot elevator, passengers may worry about suffocation after being trapped. So, will being trapped in an elevator lead to suffocation? National standards have strict regulations on elevator ventilation, and even when trapped, the ventilation system operates normally. Additionally, elevators have many moving parts, such as connection points between the car wall and the car roof and connecting keys, where there are gaps. These gaps generally provide sufficient air for breathing.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； In summary, once trapped in an elevator, please follow the [Alarm Section] below to report the situation and then wait calmly inside the cabin for rescue. Elevators are not enclosed structures, so there is no risk of suffocation; elevators have multiple safety protection devices, so there is no danger of falling.

&；nbsp；

Note:

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； ※ Passengers must never perform dangerous actions such as kicking, prying, or climbing. This is very dangerous.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； ※ When the elevator door is not aligned with the floor, attempting to jump or climb out of the cabin is also dangerous and could result in falling into the elevator shaft.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Two, Alarm

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； 1. Passengers can use the emergency intercom device on the control panel inside the cabin to seek help from building duty personnel. The emergency intercom device is located above the cabin control panel, with a button marked [Alarm Phone] or [Bell]. Pressing the button allows communication with the duty personnel.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； 2. If no one answers the button, passengers can call the [Maintenance Phone] on the safety inspection qualified mark inside the cabin or the [Emergency Repair Phone] on the cabin nameplate to seek help from professional maintenance personnel. If the phone is not reachable, passengers can dial 119 or 110 to report to public rescue institutions.

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； 3. After an elevator power outage or power failure, the emergency lights inside the cabin usually turn on automatically. Passengers can use the light from the emergency lights to make alarm calls. If the emergency lights don't work, passengers can use other light sources (such as mobile phone screens) to find the alarm button or phone number and promptly report for help.

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Three, Rescue

&；nbsp；

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； 1. After receiving the alarm, the duty personnel will immediately notify the elevator maintenance personnel while maintaining contact with the trapped passengers to inform them of the estimated arrival time of the rescuers. The rescuers will slowly move the cabin to the nearest level position using the elevator winding device before opening the hall door and cabin.

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； 2. Passengers should confirm that the cabin is in a safe level position before safely evacuating under the assistance of the rescuers.

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； If suddenly trapped in an elevator, do not panic. Use the telephone or intercom inside the elevator to seek help from relevant authorities, or press the alarm bell on the control panel. People trapped in the elevator cannot confirm the location of the elevator, so they should not forcibly pry open the door, as this would create new dangers. All elevators have safety windows on top, which are only for use by elevator maintenance personnel. Attempting to pry open the safety window and climb out of the elevator would be even more dangerous.

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； Knock on the door and shout for help, or take off your shoes and use them to knock on the door to signal for help. If no one responds, stay calm, observe the situation, conserve your energy, and wait for rescue without continuously shouting for help.
