How to apply a face mask, be careful of various illusions when using it.

How to apply a face mask: Be careful not to fall into common misconceptions. Applying a mask while taking a bath is indeed a clever time-saving method, but it depends on the type of mask you choose. It is recommended to use wet-laid masks; peel-off and jelly masks are not recommended. This is because steam can prevent the mask from adhering tightly to the skin. How to apply a face mask:

1. I love face masks and use them every day.

Masks are like the main course in skincare products. Although they work well, unless there's a special requirement, they should not be used daily in principle. Some masks have clearly marked cycles, such as a 5-day course or 3 masks in 10 days. To achieve the best results, it’s better to strictly adhere to these guidelines. For long-term continuous use, once or twice a week is enough. Even the best food can make you feel full if eaten too much! Of course, in emergencies, they can be used at any time.

Using cleaning masks every day may cause skin sensitivity, even redness, making the immature stratum corneum lose its ability to resist external harm; moisturizing masks used daily can easily cause acne; hydrating masks, however, can be used daily during dry seasons.

2. Apply a mask while bathing

Applying a mask while bathing is indeed a clever time-saving method, but it depends on the type of mask you choose. It is recommended to use wet-laid masks; peel-off and jelly masks are not recommended. This is because steam can prevent the mask from adhering tightly to the skin. If the mask needs to dry completely, the steam will affect its efficacy.

In addition to masks, facial massage creams that are currently popular in the beauty industry are also very suitable for use while bathing. The steam during bathing can help soften the stratum corneum.

3. No need to use eye masks specifically

The thickness of the skin around the eyes is only 1/4 of normal skin, so it requires more special care. Many masks, especially cleansing and moisturizing ones, contain ingredients that can irritate the delicate skin around the eyes, so they should be avoided around the eyes. Therefore, if you want to strengthen the care of the skin around the eyes, using eye masks is necessary. Especially when the skin around the eyes is severely dehydrated and lacks nutrients, intensive maintenance can produce ideal effects. In fact, eye masks should be used persistently, at least twice a week, and combined with eye cream to achieve the best eye-care effect.

4. Use mask-type masks for the neck

Do not ignore the neck skin. Neck skin is most likely to reveal age, but we often wear neck-exposing formal wear at important occasions. So, a few days before wearing formal attire, neck care must be done before makeup. A week in advance, do a moisturizing neck mask to hydrate the neck skin; about 15 minutes before applying makeup, apply the neck mask again to quickly fade pigmentation and even out the neck complexion; then apply a moisturizing isolation cream to the neck, and you're ready for makeup.

5. Never waste the essence in the mask

It is very good to have a sense of saving, but it should be applied in the right place! Exceeding the recommended time for applying a mask can lead to dehydration and loss of nutrients in the skin. Therefore, in addition to following the instructions for use, you can roughly estimate the usage time based on different types of masks: Masks with moderate moisture content should be removed after about 15 minutes to avoid the mask absorbing moisture from the skin after drying; Masks with high moisture content can be used for a longer period, but they should be removed after no more than 30 minutes. If you really don't want to waste the essence inside, it's fine to use it on other parts of your body.

6. Always exfoliate before applying a mask

It is very important to keep the skin clean before applying a mask, but it is not necessary to exfoliate every time. The stratum corneum is the natural barrier of the skin, which prevents water loss and neutralizes acidity and alkalinity. The metabolic cycle of the stratum corneum is 28 days, i.e., every 28 days some dead cells of the stratum corneum are metabolized. Therefore, exfoliation should not exceed once a month. Excessive exfoliation can damage the stratum corneum. Moreover, sensitive skin does not need frequent exfoliation.

7. Absolutely do not use tear-off masks

Tear-off masks take advantage of the full contact and adhesion between the mask and the skin. When the mask is torn off from the skin, it "peels" off blackheads, aged stratum corneum, and oil. Its cleaning ability is the strongest, but it also causes the most damage to the skin. Improper use may cause loose skin, enlarged pores, and skin allergies. There has always been a lot of controversy about tear-off masks because the act of tearing itself can cause damage to the skin, so it is not recommended to use this type of mask. Of course, the advantage of tear-off masks is their strong cleaning ability. If you are really fond of this type of mask, remember to avoid the area around the eyes and eyebrows when applying the mask, and the frequency of use should not be too high, usually once a week is sufficient.

8. My hobby is DIY masks

Many girls see TV and magazine advertisements promoting DIY masks, which sound creative, but in reality, they offer very limited benefits to our skin. Perhaps there is some effect immediately after application, but the next day, the skin quality returns to its original state. The reason is that these homemade masks are very natural, but they have not undergone scientific and technological processing. Generally speaking, the molecules are too large to be absorbed by the skin, so although it is interesting and cost-effective, it is ineffective. Smart you should carefully consider this!

9. Oily skin only needs cleansing masks

It is very important for oily-skinned individuals to apply masks. They can choose three types of masks: oil-control masks, deep-cleansing masks, and moisturizing masks. Because in dry seasons, the skin can still lack water, resulting in an oily yet dry condition. The procedure can be to select one day a week for oil-control and moisturizing masks, and another week for deep-cleaning and moisturizing masks.

10. Don't apply the mask too thickly

Does a mask need to be applied in a thick layer? Yes! When a thick layer of mask is applied to the face, the skin temperature rises, promoting blood circulation, allowing the nutrients to spread better between the cells. The moisture that cannot evaporate from the skin surface will remain in the epidermis, making the skin smooth and taut. The warming effect will also soften the stratum corneum, dilate capillaries, and allow the accumulated dirt inside to be expelled.
