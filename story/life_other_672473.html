The End of Happiness Without You

You cannot accompany me to the very end, and I have always known this. Perhaps in the near future, I will lose you and never see you again.

Will I cry when that time comes? And would you bear to see me cry?

At first, I always thought we had a lot more time together. But I overlooked the fact that no matter how much time I still have, there will be a day, a moment, when I must leave you and embark on a journey filled with confusion and the unknown, to realize the dreams we share.

Do you remember?

The memories we wrote together – those are the most beautiful chapters of my life.

When I was first admitted to the hospital, it was during my third year of junior high school. You kept emphasizing that it was just a minor surgery, telling me not to be afraid. So, I didn't cry from start to finish. I was even worried about what would happen if I failed to get into high school. You kept consoling me, saying that based on my abilities, everything would be fine. Yet, I clearly saw your anxious eyes as you stood alone at the end of the corridor. I am truly grateful to you; it was you who let me know that my illness wasn't scary, giving me the courage to face it, allowing me to calmly take the high school entrance exam. But why do I still feel so sad?

Dad told me that when I was born, it hurt, a lot, but you endured it without shedding a tear. Now, because you have to watch me being wheeled into the operating room, seeing me suffer, you can't bear it anymore, letting your tears flow freely across your face. The wound is on my body, but the pain is always in your heart.

With your support and comfort, I agreed to the surgery, agreeing to let them make a small incision in my body.

Now, I am healthy, happy, and have successfully entered high school, all because of your protection.

You've never pursued my academic results, but I know that you actually care deeply. You're just afraid of putting pressure on me, afraid that I won't be able to handle it. In fact, the less you seem to care, the more I feel that I carry your hopes. But every time, I disappoint you so much despite your great expectations. So after every exam, I always feel an inexplicable fear of going home because I don't want to hear your sighs, even though they are very light.

I can't bear to see you disappointed, just as you don't want to see me hurt. I hate myself so much, so I try my best to make up for it, but one failure after another leaves me in endless darkness. Struggling not to fall, yet falling again and again, until I am covered in wounds. Old scars merge with new ones, layer upon layer, tightly enclosing me. I'm so tired, I can't break through.

It's at these moments that you always give me the most reliable shoulder to lean on. Even though you are physically slim, you can still hold all of my endless sorrows. I realize now that no matter how much I fail now, in your heart, I will always be the best.

I'm not used to leaving you, maybe because of attachment. Leaving home to study, sleeping in unfamiliar places for the first time, jostling for food for the first time, I gritted my teeth and carried on. After coming home, I never tell you about the hardships I endured. Even when you treat my wounds and ask why, I casually brush it off. I endure the pain just so you won't feel it for me.

You gave me all your love, gave me the world, not caring how much you paid, not caring how much you gained, only hoping that I am happy and well. This is you – a very ordinary mother with the simplest wish.

Your heart is like an ocean, and I am just a little fish inside. Though I live in your heart, I understand you far too little. Every movement I make touches your heart. How I long to get closer to you, to tell you that I really love you.

The end of happiness will come without you, but I will always remember you. In every life to come, I will find you, even if you scold me, I will forever cling to you. I will be good, I want you to stay with me, just keep accompanying me, forever.
