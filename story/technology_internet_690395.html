Xiangshan is located in the west of Jingmen City, Hubei Province, facing Dongbao Mountain in the east, forming a famous landscape of Jingmen City. Xiangshan was called Mengshan in the Tang Dynasty; the name "Xiangshan" is said to be in memory of Lu Jiuyuan from the Southern Song Dynasty. Coincidentally, there is also a "Xiangshan" in Guixi, Jiangxi today, named in memory of Lu Jiuyuan. Hence, one Lu Master in the past, two Xiangshans today.

"Hedong Shi Hou 2" Filming Location: Xiangshan Film City

The early architecture of the Xiangshan Film City, where "Hedong Shi Hou 2" was filmed, was built based on the story content of Jin Yong's novel "The Return of the Condor Heroes". The main attractions such as the magnificent Xiangyang City, the mysterious Living Dead Tomb, the elegant Guiyun Villa, the magical Valley of Absolute Affection, and the lush Banyan Forest are unique in style and creative in design. Xiangshan Film City, where "Hedong Shi Hou 2" was filmed, features lakes and mountains, pavilions and terraces, gullies and caves, as well as ancient streets from the Song Dynasty, immersing people into the martial arts ambiance. "The Return of the Condor Heroes" was the first TV drama filmed at Xiangshan Film City. Subsequently, classic martial arts dramas like "The Blood Sword" and "The Deer and the Cauldron" were also filmed here, along with war films like "Jinggang Mountain". Here, you can visit film arenas, attend celebrity meet-and-greets, experience eerie ancient tombs, watch plays at the Yichun Teahouse, or even make cameo appearances in county court trials.

China Fishing Village, where "Hedong Shi Hou 2" was filmed, is the largest eco-friendly seaside leisure and vacation resort on China's east coast, a large-scale leisure seaside tourist destination themed around "Fishing Culture Folklore Tour" and "Seaside Leisure Vacation". It has been rated as one of the "Top Ten Summer Retreat and Leisure Destinations in Zhejiang Province". China Fishing Village, where "Hedong Shi Hou 2" was filmed, consists of two major scenic areas: Sunshine Coast and Fish Mountain Island. As China's largest comprehensive marine cultural leisure and vacation resort, it features Mediterranean-style villas, Caribbean-style entertainment zones, high-quality beaches, authentic and delicious fishing village seafood snacks, green islands, and forest resources. Whether it's the main building of the theme park, the fishing village theme villas, three-masted ancient ships, fishing family stalls, tent villages, or the prominent wind direction markers, fishing boats, and mast lights hanging high, they all comprehensively showcase the life atmosphere of the fishing area.

"Hedong Shi Hou 2" Filming Location: Shoupu Ancient City

Shoupu Ancient City, where "Hedong Shi Hou 2" was filmed, is built along the mountain, leaning against the sea, known as "the city above the port, the mountain within the city." Shoupu Ancient City connects to the fishing port on one side and stretches deep into the mountainous valley on the other. The city walls follow the undulating mountain terrain, and the city gates are constructed according to the shape of the land. Its main feature is controlling the port from a high position, characteristic of a "key maritime defense town." The old houses are built step by step, and the streets ascend gradually, winding and twisting. Here, you can visit venues that integrate fishing culture, merchant culture, and maritime defense culture, weaving fish nets, making fishing lanterns.

"Hedong Shi Hou 2" Filming Location: Wengcheng

Wengcheng, where "Hedong Shi Hou 2" was filmed, is located at the intersection of Wanxing Street, Fujian Street, and Zhong Street. It was initially constructed during the Ming Dynasty and renovated during the Qing Guangxu period. The ancient city wall remains well-preserved today. The reconstructed Wengcheng covers an area of 240 square meters, with an entrance gate, a doorpost placed above, and a double-eaved city tower at the exit, consisting of a guillotine gate. Shoupu is marked by Wengcheng, dividing it into inside the city and outside the city, hence called Ancient City Shoupu. The various venues within the city allow visitors to experience the prosperity of shops in the past and the lively lives of the common people.
