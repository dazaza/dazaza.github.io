In Beijing, the annual income of new graduates in workplaces is close to that of shop assistants and nannies.

by 1dwd181q7w on 2009-11-25 10:48:31

Core tip: According to a newly published survey in Beijing, the annual median income of a new graduate working in advertising design or similar jobs that require knowledge and creativity is 28,000 yuan. This figure is close to the annual median income of 25,000 yuan for postpartum care workers or so-called "moon sisters."

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, November 24th - Want to earn a decent salary in Beijing? A basic strategy is to take care of people rather than dealing with words, taking care of pets, or collecting garbage.

According to a recent survey by the human resources department of Beijing, whether they are high school graduates, college students, postgraduates, or doctoral students, if they work in advertising design or similar jobs that require knowledge and creativity, their annual median income is more than 28,000 yuan RMB. This is close to the annual median income of 25,000 yuan for a postpartum care worker or "moon sister," not much higher than the annual median income of a salesperson—close to 25,000 yuan. Fortunately, this young person who has just left school and mainly earns a living by coming up with some engaging phrases can earn more than ordinary cleaners or pet caregivers. The annual median income of an ordinary cleaner in Beijing is over 16,000 yuan RMB, while the annual median income of a pet caregiver is less than 16,000 yuan.

If one wants to move beyond the level of salespeople and moon sisters and take care of more, higher-end, or more troublesome individuals on a daily basis, the income will be relatively substantial. For example, last year, the annual median income of a market director in Beijing exceeded 100,000 yuan RMB, factory managers, general managers, and other middle-to-senior-level managers or general managers of three-star hotels typically earned 130,000 or 150,000 yuan per year, and the annual median income of a lawyer from a large law firm exceeded 94,000 yuan, while human resources directors usually earned over 75,000 yuan per year.

As for the gap between the high-income and median-income groups, it is most prominent among market directors, human resources directors, and lawyers from large law firms. Their high-income figures reach several hundred thousand yuan, even up to 500,000 yuan RMB, which is four to five times the median income, and in some cases nearly six times as much; whereas for ordinary middle-to-senior-level managers such as factory managers or general managers, the high-income group generally earns between tens of thousands to two hundred thousand yuan RMB annually, none exceeding 1.5 times the median income.

The wage guidance prices for over 1,200 types of jobs in Beijing are reflected in the recently published book by the Beijing Human Resources and Social Security Department titled "Beijing Labor Market Wage Guidance Price Levels and Enterprise Labor Cost Status in 2009."

The wage guidance levels for different occupations or job categories released in this book follow the wage total口径 announced by the National Bureau of Statistics, referring to the total labor remuneration directly paid by enterprises to employees in various positions. This includes the actual wages received by employees, housing subsidies, rental increase subsidies, as well as personal income tax, housing provident fund, and individual contributions to various social insurance funds deducted directly from their salaries by the employer. The data comes from sampling surveys of normal laboring employees in normally operating production and business enterprises, reflecting the total wages for the entire previous year.

Relevant officials pointed out that publishing these guidance levels aims to guide laborers to flow reasonably according to market needs, providing a relatively objective market reference standard for both laborers and employers when negotiating wage levels, ensuring that the labor market has both supply and demand and clear pricing standards.
