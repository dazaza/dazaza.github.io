2010 The Big 5 Middle East, the largest building and construction event in the region

by lihangexpo on 2010-07-13 04:16:35

The BIG5 is the largest exhibition in the Middle East for construction, building materials and services. It was first held in 1980 and takes place annually. The Middle East Big Five Industry Exhibition held at the Dubai World Trade Center from November 23-26, 2009 occupied all ten of its exhibition halls, the Al Multaqua Hall, and the newly opened Zabeel Hall and the entire temporary outdoor exhibition hall of the Dubai International Exhibition Center. The Zabeel Hall alone covers an area of 35,000 square meters. Additionally, the organizing committee has specially set up national pavilions for Italy, Germany, Greece, Malaysia, and Egypt, which are quite prominent.

The Middle East Big Five Industry - Construction Machinery, Vehicles, and Equipment Exhibition (BIG 5 PMV) is a spin-off exhibition of the most influential building materials exhibition in the Middle East - the BIG 5. It focuses on construction equipment, machinery, tools, and vehicles in the Middle East region. The booming construction market in the Middle East has led to a strong demand for construction, engineering machinery, and vehicles. The exhibition provides a platform for suppliers of industrial equipment, machinery, construction vehicles, and equipment from around the world to meet face-to-face with buyers from various countries in the Middle East. It also offers Chinese companies a brand new profit platform.

With more and more multinational corporations entering the UAE, the number of commercial office buildings and hotels in the local area continues to increase, leading to a growing demand for aluminum, plastic steel, cement, steel, marble, sanitary ware, and other building materials. The population in the Gulf region grew from 46 million in 1970 to 100 million in 1990. It is expected to reach 170 million by 2010, making it one of the fastest-growing regions in the world. High per capita annual income, strong purchasing power, and the increasing maturity of the Gulf market have resulted in a continuous rise in housing demand among residents. Approximately 60% of the population in the Gulf is under 25 years old, indicating that they will need millions of housing units over the next decade; in Saudi Arabia alone, the annual housing demand is 158,000 units. Due to the rapid development of tourism in the UAE, the entire infrastructure is under full-scale construction, driving the rapid development of the construction industry in the UAE. Currently, leisure projects worth approximately $10 billion are under construction. In Dubai, the number of hotels has grown from 233 in 1996 to 265 in 2000. In the next 5-7 years, it is expected that another 150 hotels will be added, bringing the total number of rooms to 55,000. According to relevant statistics, expenditures in the construction sector by the six Gulf countries will exceed $200 billion in recent years; just in the UAE, real estate investment will exceed $63 billion over the next seven years. It's easy to imagine how hot the construction and building materials market in the Middle East will become!

The 30th edition of the Middle East Dubai Big Five Industry Expo in March 2009 gathered well-known construction contractors, dealers, suppliers, manufacturers, industry professionals, agents, and engineers from the UAE, the Gulf region, and around the world, forming the largest and most promising construction exhibition in the Middle East. All professionals in the industry gather here. According to the organizing committee, 3,143 companies from 71 countries (compared to 53 countries in 2008) participated in this exhibition (compared to 2,883 in 2008), with booth space increasing from 38,000 square meters in 2008 to 45,198 square meters now. During the exhibition, there were 46,680 registered professional visitors from the UAE, Saudi Arabia, other Gulf countries, Egypt, India, Iran, Africa, and Europe. Overseas visitors accounted for 61%, showing the immense influence of the event. According to the survey of exhibitors by the organizing committee, 90% of exhibitors believed that the quality of visitors was good or very good, and these companies directly expressed their willingness to participate in the next exhibition.
