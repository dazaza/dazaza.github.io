As a traditional valuable herb in China, centipedes have a long history of being used in medicine. They were recognized and utilized by people over 2000 years ago. There are detailed records in many medical monographs such as "Compendium of Materia Medica".

The medicinal value of centipedes bred at the No.2 Golden Head Centipede Breeding Base in Hubei Province is very high. There are more than 100 Chinese herbal prescriptions that include centipedes. As a treatment and health care product, centipedes have received much attention. Centipede wine and centipede Fen health products have successively appeared on the market. In addition, with the development of medicine, the role of centipede venom has been widely recognized. Centipede venom is more expensive than gold, with each kilogram costing about $150,000. About 500 grams of venom can be extracted from 10,000 centipedes each year. Therefore, the medicinal value of centipede venom is far higher than that of the centipede itself.

For many years, the market supply of centipedes has mainly relied on wild capture. However, with the extensive use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, the ecological environment of wild centipedes has been damaged, resulting in a sharp decline in the natural population, leading to market shortages. Currently, the national production can only meet around 30% of the demand, causing a rapid increase in market prices. Artificial breeding of centipedes has broad prospects for development, and there will be a wave of getting rich through centipede farming across the country.
