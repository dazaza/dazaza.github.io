Well-known anti-trafficking volunteer persuades 33 fugitives to surrender online

by zhanghui82 on 2012-02-06 23:36:59

Also under the accompaniment of his family, Gao, a 24-year-old suspect from Taojiang County in Hunan Province, came to Guangzhou from his hometown in Hunan Province overnight and surrendered himself to the Haizhu police. On December 28th, 2006, Gao fled after committing a robbery in Shangchong Village in Haizhu District. Under the strong arrest by the public security organs and sincere persuasion, Gao finally chose to surrender himself to the public security organs. Up to now, the "Clear Web" campaign conducted by the Haizhu police has captured 414 fugitives, among which those who surrendered themselves accounted for sixty percent.

Yesterday morning, "Shangmin Zai Zai" waited at the appointed location for Zeng's arrival at around ten o'clock. At this time, the first post appeared on Weibo urging criminals to surrender themselves, drawing much attention from netizens.

The first appointment failed to meet.

At 16:47, @Gongshang Justice - Zai Zai sent out another Weibo post: "After several days of hard work and patient guidance, it was not wasted. He surrendered! The 33rd one; I hope the law can grant him leniency, society can tolerate and understand him, and give him a chance to start anew! I hope that wanderers outside can return soon. There are only two days left for the Clear Web campaign, seize the time, which is to grasp your own life!"

"I told him that if he voluntarily surrenders himself, he can get an opportunity for lighter punishment under legal conditions," said "Gongshang Zai Zai."

Fugitive seeks help through Weibo

"Gongshang Zai Zai" told Southern Daily reporters that he had been invited by Zeng's relatives to go to their place in Shenzhen, but this time Zeng did not show up. "If this meeting were still scheduled in Shenzhen, Zeng might not dare to come out."

On December 11th, "Gongshang Zai Zai" went to Shenzhen. According to his revelation, the family of this fugitive was unclear about the lenient treatment that fugitives could receive under the "Clear Web" campaign. After the introduction and advice from "Gongshang Zai Zai", the family of the fugitive finally eliminated their concerns and successfully persuaded the fugitive. That evening at 21:03, @Gongshang Justice - Zai Zai sent out a Weibo post: "Communicated all day, the 33rd one, finally convinced. Departing for Guangzhou at 9 pm, will surrender tomorrow."

According to the Haizhu District Public Security Bureau of Guangzhou City, Zeng, aged 49, was initially investigated for suspected fraud and was wanted online by the Henan police in July this year.

Zeng said he fled to gather 200,000 yuan.

At around 11 am yesterday, Zeng finally arrived in Guangzhou. Upon meeting, Zeng's first words were: "I cannot face the society, I cannot face my family, I have long wanted to surrender..." Accompanied by "Gongshang Zai Zai" and others, Zeng returned to Chigang Police Station of Haizhu District Public Security Bureau in Guangzhou City. Around 11:40 am, after confirmation by the police, Zeng entered the detention center.

"Gongshang Zai Zai" urges wanderers to return early.

Southern Daily reports (reporter/Zhao Yang, correspondent/Wu Qiong): "This is the 33rd fugitive that I have successfully persuaded to surrender since the 'Clear Web' campaign began this year." Renowned anti-human trafficking volunteer "Gongshang Zai Zai" said in an interview with Southern Daily. Yesterday, he successfully persuaded Zeng, a Shenzhen-based suspect, to surrender to Chigang Police Station of Haizhu District in Guangzhou City.

"Three days ago, this netizen contacted me again, hoping I could help persuade the suspect to surrender. We agreed on a location in Shenzhen to meet the relative of this fugitive, aiming to eliminate their concerns and together prompt the fugitive to come forward," recalled "Gongshang Zai Zai".

Originally, the fugitive named Zeng is from Guangdong Province, previously suspected of participating in a fraud in Henan Province. The fraud gang defrauded more than 2 million yuan, of which Zeng obtained over 200,000 yuan. After the case broke out, Zeng fled and dared not return home. According to Zeng's friend, Zeng said this fleeing life had pushed him to the brink of mental collapse, and Zeng also had a disease that worsened due to fleeing. Zeng once told his friend that he was fleeing just to gather 200,000 yuan, to hand over to the government in order to obtain lenient handling. "To help Zeng gather this 200,000 yuan, Zeng's family borrowed a lot of money, and it seems they have already gathered enough," said "Gongshang Zai Zai".

"Gongshang Zai Zai" told reporters that he promotes the "Clear Web" campaign on Weibo and persistently persuades fugitives to surrender. More than 20 days ago, a netizen followed him and sent a private message to @Gongshang Justice - Zai Zai, asking questions about the national policies regarding fugitives. After some time, this netizen said there was a friend nearby who was a fugitive.
