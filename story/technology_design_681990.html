Nonagenarian Sues Daughter-in-law, Only Asking Grandson to Go Home and Visit Often

by gg67fyy3ffhj on 2012-02-28 16:42:07

A young man in Longkou city hasn't visited his grandfather for more than ten years, and didn't invite his grandfather to attend his wedding banquet when he got married. Last year, Wang Lao Han, the old man who was close to 90 years old, asked his daughter-in-law (who is also the young man's mother) to move out of his house just for this reason. Under the mediation of a judge, all family members finally resolved their issues, and the old man sold the house at a low price to his grandson.

Wang Lao Han sued his daughter-in-law

Demanding that she vacate the house.

In 1982, Wang Lao Han's eldest son, Wang, joined the army, leaving home. Wang Lao Han rallied the whole family to build a new house, which was intended for his eldest son to use after getting married. After the house was built, the village happened to be implementing a new village plan. Wang Lao Han processed the house ownership notarial deed along with the village, registering it under his own name. In 1986, Wang married Zou and they have been living in the newly built house ever since. In 1997, Wang died in a traffic accident. In 2009, Wang's son Xiao Wang got married and moved out, leaving Zou as the only person in the old house.

On the surface, everything seemed peaceful within the family, but in 2010, Wang Lao Han sued Zou in court, demanding that she vacate the house. The court initially determined that Wang Lao Han's request was supported by the notarial deed from the notary office, making the case simple and the evidence for judging Zou to vacate the house quite clear. However, the judge handling the case was concerned that if judged this way, it might affect the feelings between three generations of the family, and Zou's place of residence would also become a problem.

The lawsuit was just for pride

Wanting the grandson to visit more often.

In order to truly resolve the case, the judge decided to first understand the reasons behind Wang Lao Han's lawsuit.

The judge learned that Xiao Wang was Wang Lao Han's eldest grandson and also his only grandson, who had always been deeply loved by Wang Lao Han. After Wang's death in 1997, Xiao Wang continued to live with his mother, Zou, and never went to visit his grandfather again. During every Spring Festival, he did not even pay New Year's greetings to his grandfather. When Xiao Wang got married in 2009, he didn't introduce his bride to his grandfather or invite him to the wedding banquet.

Wang Lao Han, nearly 90 years old, had looked forward to the day his eldest grandson would get married for many years but couldn't fulfill his wish, so he angrily sued Zou, who was still living in the house, asking her to move out. The judge learned that Wang Lao Han's lawsuit was not about fighting over the house, but rather about gaining some respect. After understanding Wang Lao Han's real thoughts, in order to promote reconciliation between grandfather and grandson, the judge called Zou and Xiao Wang to come, allowing Xiao Wang to apologize to his grandfather and encouraging Wang Lao Han to sell the house at a discounted price to his grandson.

After mediation

All family members opened up their hearts.

Wang Lao Han agreed to the mediation proposal, but at first, Zou claimed she could not accept it, insisting that the house belonged to her and her husband, and that she had the right to live there. She requested the court to rule the house in her favor. Seeing Zou's persistence, the judge adopted a strategy of ruling first and then persuading later.

On the day of the announcement, the judge saw that Zou was completely disappointed after receiving the judgment, so he took the opportunity to bring up the previous mediation proposal. After careful consideration, Zou agreed to pay 20,000 yuan as compensation for the house value to Wang Lao Han, and Wang Lao Han would hand over the house ownership notarial deed to Xiao Wang. Under the persuasion of the judge, the family's decade-long heartache was finally resolved.
