Love or not love

by ykafeifeiw on 2011-09-13 21:56:55

Love or not to love. Loving her does not necessarily mean possessing her; possessing her means cherishing her, loving her well and truly. I haven't deliberately searched for my other half. I like that kind of fleeting love. Or perhaps it cannot be called love but rather a fleeting emotion! Something sudden, something that happens without anyone knowing. How many times in life are worth cherishing? Worth possessing? Only if you have loved and cherished can you say you are happy. Being together until old age is certainly good, but even if you break up, you could still call it happiness because you once loved. Both will retain a beautiful memory...

I am not an expert at love nor am I a novelist who writes famous novels. I am just expressing my inner thoughts and views on love. You might say it's a spontaneous sigh. We, the post-90s generation, have many ideas and views, so naturally there are more sighs. We often daydream, feel emotions upon seeing certain scenes, sometimes even get hurt by those scenes. Let me return to the topic, it seems I've spoken too much nonsense. I don't know about others, but let me talk about myself and the people around me.

The romance novels I've read aren't many, and I'm no intellectual. I just think I've encountered slightly more women. There's a line in a novel: No matter how far she walks with you, eventually she will part ways with you. I also believe this. Even those born at the same time and walk together till old age eventually separate due to either love or life and death. Two people may love each other passionately, but in the end, they have to part, truly heart-wrenching. That kind of sorrow only those who have loved can understand.

Many people are at a loss when in love, losing themselves, unable to find themselves, oh, including me as well. But I haven't fallen in love with any woman. I am the type who seems to be born knowing how to tease women, enjoying joking around with them, flirting with them.

I can allow any woman to deceive my feelings, possess my body, and finally throw me into the abyss, just don't make me fall in love with her. It sounds like I am somewhat pitiful, but it doesn't matter. I don't need your sympathy. I am a strong man, so I don't need your sympathy. Oh dear, I feel someone wants to hit me, or worse, scold me, or spit on me. I didn't see it, so you can't hit me or spit on me. But that was all in the past, now I'll talk less about it.

I am a man who feels emotions upon seeing certain scenes. I can't bear women being nice to me. The nicer they are to me, the deeper I will hurt them. However, I am easily moved by women, but being moved doesn't mean I will fall in love with them, it's liking them. It seems I like many women, yet none of them are the ones I truly love.

For example, when a woman feeds me cake, washes my feet, dresses me, etc., these are all details, nothing major. Different women give me different kinds of moving experiences. Don't scold me, please look at it from another perspective, okay? Sometimes I also feel too deeply, leaving myself battered and bruised, unable to extricate myself, deeply mired in the whirlpool of love, almost drowning. Finally, I managed to crawl ashore...

Perhaps you think I am bad, but I don't think I am that bad. Besides, those were all in the past. Now when I think about it, I really find myself quite amusing. I'm saying these things for you to hear. Why do we always fail to cherish the people before our eyes? Always thinking there's a next station, always thinking we can say sorry. But we never thought that every time we say sorry, every time we hang up the phone or go offline on QQ, it might be the final farewell.

Writing here, I remembered my ex-girlfriend. We dated for two years and ended up breaking up anyway. What's the point of having two people? After two years without seeing her, would I still love her as much? Not necessarily. Our relationship wasn't that affectionate. Oh, I made a mistake, it was she who loved me very much, while I didn't love her as much. Never mind, let's not talk about her anymore. We've broken up, what else is there to say?

Occasionally, I still think about her. After all, we had feelings, memories. Having memories isn't necessarily a bad thing, right? I know that when you truly love someone, that person appears in your mind when you most want to forget, appears in your heart when you're most sad and lost. But after thinking and thinking, you can't decide whether to tell her how you feel now because I care about her feelings, a little troubled. I can only sit there dumbfounded, thinking about her. I want to forget her because she always argued with me, angered me, went against me. Although a bit petty, I still like that feeling. Just talking, actually, I don't really want to forget her. Oh! Talking about this, I remember a woman who once said I was hypocritical (my ex-girlfriend).

Oh dear, I won't write anymore today. Writing has made my hands sweaty, dirty. Time for dinner!

Sorry, this was written on a website a few days ago, and today I've moved it to my space to continue writing.

Alright, I won't mention my ex-girlfriend anymore. We've already broken up, why keep talking about her? Thinking is one thing, but it's better not to talk about it. Right? So we should maintain a certain distance, a spiritual distance? Oh, and reality. Our intersections are gone, those happy footprints have disappeared. Happiness is ahead, not behind, so I should move forward. Oh, I'm talking about how to treat love, not her again. Please don't say I'm too verbose. Don't live for one person. Love requires two hearts to sincerely nurture, that's true love. One person bearing the feelings of two is painful. In the world of love, there is no real eternity or so-called happiness, there are always obstacles, but they need to be faced together by both. At any time, mutual understanding and tolerance are needed, after all, humans have emotions, passions, and desires. Personally, I think two people together need to tolerate each other, turn big issues into small ones, and small ones into nothing, because it's really not easy for two people to come together. Still that sentence, no one will accompany you for a lifetime, even if you grow old together, there will still be separation through life or death.

After putting effort, don't regret losing that love. No one loves only once in a lifetime, doing other things besides. Perhaps when you turn around, she's already left you. Such things are unavoidable and happen every day. Unlike in novels, where it's so touching, because we all live in the present, not in novels. I know women cry because they've truly given up, their hearts ache. Because they know forced love brings no happiness. Men cry because they truly love. They say men don't shed tears lightly, unless they reach the point of sadness. A man's confidence comes from a woman's admiration, a woman's pride from a man's adoration. Never blindly obey the man you love, don't cultivate your love for him. The better you cultivate it, the more counterproductive it becomes. Saying this, I think everyone will think I seem to know a lot, but it's not true. Because I have deep personal experiences. Of course, for different women, I have different ways of dealing with them. I have a habit of making women feel moved, the more moved they are, the more they like me. But I won't fall in love with them, I dislike them acting high and mighty in front of me. If they act that way, it means I've fallen in love with her, so I won't easily let them get away with it, because I don't like it.

Perhaps love is just because of loneliness, needing someone to love, even if there's no outcome. When she turns around and leaves you, watching her silhouette disappear among the flickering lights, it will leave you with unforgettable memories.

Women are auditory and emotional creatures. Upon closer inspection, they too are scarred. I have a little side story. Once deeply in love, they eventually parted ways. The woman still loves that man. When the man feels lonely and remembers the silly woman who once loved him deeply, he can't turn back. Because the harm he once caused her was too great. Actually, that woman still loves him, but she fears he will hurt her again because she can't afford to lose anymore. Loving each other doesn't necessarily mean being together, just hoping the other lives well, happily, which is her greatest joy. Talking about this makes me think of online dating.

There's a kind of fate called net-fate, a kind of love called net-love.

Perfect love, on stage. Some falsehood, some truth. I just think this is also a kind of love, but it all happens in virtuality. Leaving each other with deep impressions, developing mutual fondness, friendship, net-love. Giving each other inexplicable heartbeats, missing each other, remembering each other, growing stronger. Sorry, I mentioned net-love again, it seems I couldn't help myself, sorry everyone, please forgive me!

Returning to the topic

Love, for everyone, is something that can be met but not sought after. When you are mutually infatuated, have you ever thought about whether it can last? Do you know if the love you are giving now is true love? But there is a kind of love that is great, one party watching over the other from afar. Leaving for her happiness, as long as she is happier than oneself. But who knows what kind of love is unforgettable? What kind of love is grandiose? Love is not that simple. You think you're doing it for her good, for her sake, but her feelings are different. So love is destined, as long as you both feel happy and free. Oh, I can't explain clearly anymore. In short, as long as both are happy. This is just my personal opinion!

Mutual infatuation, of course, in love, everyone hopes for mutual infatuation, but after encountering mutual infatuation, why do they still part ways? Love is perfect, but love in real life isn't necessarily perfect. Only time reveals if it's true love. Should we trust love? Is love a test in life? Believe in yourself whether to experience it, and experiencing it takes time.

Some people, once hurt by love countless times, over time, stop daring to love because they are truly wounded, losing faith in love, no longer believing in love. However, conversely, without failure, how can there be success? Without weathering storms, how can it be called love? Would you have enduring love? You dare not confess to the other party, fearing losing them after speaking out, fearing they won't accept you, fearing the failure of love, thinking silent attention is preserving your love. But have you considered, if you don't speak out, you might lose her forever? Perhaps the other party is waiting for your confession. If so, wouldn't you lose her?
