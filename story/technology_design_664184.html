Android 2.2 Usage Tips (Part III)

1. Don't install frequently used applications on the SD card

You may have noticed that when your phone is connected via USB, all programs installed on the SD card become inaccessible. This means you won't be able to use these programs while charging unless you manually move them back to the phone's internal memory each time before charging. Additionally, the read/write speed of an SD card is definitely slower than that of the phone's internal memory, and installing apps on the SD card can affect the app's running speed, especially noticeable on slower SD cards.

2. Use USB tethering for mobile network sharing instead of WLAN hotspot

Although Android 2.2 has a cool feature to share the phone’s network with other devices via Wi-Fi, you might find that your phone battery drains quickly after using it. Therefore, we recommend another way to share the network—use USB tethering to share the phone's mobile network. By connecting your phone to a laptop via USB cable, you will find that this method not only conserves power but also provides better network stability, provided that you have a USB cable handy.

3. Use your phone for night video recording

Android 2.2 allows you to keep the phone's flash light on until the battery runs out (if your phone is equipped with a flash). HTC even offers a flashlight mini-program that utilizes this feature. However, this function has another great use: night video recording. Android 2.2 supports keeping the flash light on during video recording, allowing you to capture decent videos even in pitch-black conditions.

4. Block uncivilized words

In the voice input and output settings of Android 2.2, you can block uncivilized words. This means that you can now say swear words to your phone, and it will understand what you said while filtering out the offensive language. In the phone settings > Voice input and output settings > Voice recognition program settings, simply check the option to block uncivilized words.

5. Better process management

Process management has always been a criticized feature of the Android system, leading to the popularity of many third-party process management apps. Starting from Android 2.2, Google has modified the code to manage how programs terminate other program processes. Moreover, Android’s application management function now supports users manually stopping running programs. For most users, we now just need to trust Google and let Android autonomously manage our phone processes.
