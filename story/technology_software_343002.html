UTStarcom COO Lu Ying: The broadcasting industry has the advantage of time, place and people

by ymaxi68k on 2010-04-10 13:41:25

Recently, UTStarcom announced the appointment of Chief Operating Officer (COO) Ying Lu, and within a certain period of time, Lu will take over as UTStarcom's CEO. Previously, UTStarcom also announced that broadcasting and broadband would be its two main future directions. In response to this, Lu appeared as a guest on Sina.com on the morning of March 26th to discuss this topic.

Video: Exclusive Interview with UTStarcom COO Ying Lu

Media Source: Sina Technology

Guest Introduction:

Ying Lu, UTStarcom's COO, has extensive experience working in multinational corporations and more than 25 years of experience in telecommunications, optoelectronic components, and investment project management. Before joining UTStarcom, Lu served as the co-chief operating officer and general manager for China at Source Photonics, a globally leading optoelectronic component company. Prior to joining Source Photonics, he held various positions at Fiberxon (which was acquired by MRV Communications Ltd. in July 2007), including CEO, COO, and vice president of marketing and sales. Before joining Fiberxon in September 2001, Lu worked at US Business Networks Ltd. (MeetChina.com) where he served as director of business strategy development from March 2000 to May 2001. From May 1988 to July 1998, he worked at China National Technical Import and Export Corporation in various managerial roles. Lu graduated from Huazhong University of Science and Technology with a bachelor's degree in electronic engineering and subsequently obtained an MBA from the University of Southern California.

Key Insights Summary:

- Generally speaking, it is called returning to China. First, I want to tell everyone that returning to China does not mean only doing business in the Chinese market; rather, it refers to strengthening our headquarters, R&D teams, and our focus on the Chinese market.

- I am happy to announce that almost all members of our new management team are Chinese, which is the first time in UTStarcom's history. I believe that with such a team, we will have a better understanding of the situation in China, our employees, and our customers.

- For the future, we focus on two aspects: dedication and innovation. After going through some adjustments and difficulties, perhaps UT may not provide comprehensive telecom solutions like some of our peers do, but we have already invested a lot of resources and will continue to invest in the construction of NGB (Next Generation Broadcasting). We will concentrate our limited resources in this field, refine and perfect them, closely align with the needs of our users and services, and thereby enhance the competitiveness of our products.

Below is the interview transcript with UTStarcom's COO Ying Lu:

Host Quan Jing: Hello, dear Sina netizens, welcome to the Sina Technology video live broadcast room. I am the host Quan Jing. Friends who pay attention to industry dynamics have noticed that in February, Beijing Yizhuang International Investment Development Co., Ltd. reached a strategic cooperation agreement with UTStarcom. There were also two other fund companies involved, completing the restructuring plan. Recently, everyone has received the latest news that UTStarcom has officially appointed Ying Lu as the company’s COO, fully responsible for UTStarcom's broadband division, multimedia communications division, global supply chain, as well as the market and sales operations in mainland China, reporting directly to UTStarcom's CEO and President Peter Blackmore. Today, we have invited UTStarcom's COO Ying Lu to communicate with everyone.

Lu: Thank you.

Host Quan Jing: I introduced your current title as UTStarcom's COO, or COO. When I started chatting with you, I found out that you've been at UTStarcom for four weeks now, starting one month ago. You've learned a lot about UTStarcom and the entire industry, making it a rapid learning month.

Could you share with us your feelings and experiences over the past month?

Lu: What impressed me most is actually our employees and customers' concern for UTStarcom. Every time I communicate with the employees, I can see their eyes and feel their love for the company. They sincerely hope the company will get better.

When I visit our clients, they express admiration for UT and regret over the difficulties we faced before. They also clearly and strongly express their willingness to support and hope to see UTStarcom regain its strength.

Host Quan Jing: Although it has only been a short month, Mr. Lu has developed deep feelings for UTStarcom.

Our Sina Technology was the first to obtain and report the news of UTStarcom's restructuring. Many netizens must be eagerly awaiting detailed information about UTStarcom's restructuring. Could you take this opportunity to introduce the details of the strategic reorganization?

Lu: From three aspects, first, after this round of investment, or financing, there will be a transformation in the company's board of directors; second, there will be a transformation in the company's management team; third, there will be adjustments in structure and strategy, which should be explained from these three aspects.

Firstly, regarding the company's board of directors, through this financing, we have introduced three seasoned individuals familiar with the Chinese situation and the Chinese market to strengthen our board, while two original board members will resign. The newly introduced directors include Mr. Du Baichuan, an expert in the broadcasting industry, known within the industry. I don't need to elaborate much. Mr. Li Xiaoping from Yizhuang International, who is the vice general manager responsible for investment and development at Yizhuang International, has a deep understanding of Beijing's development policies and is experienced in management. Additionally, Mr. Huang Shaqiu from Yellowstone Capital, whose institution played a crucial role in facilitating this reorganization, will provide more strategic value to the company.

Secondly, in the company's management layer, as mentioned earlier, the board appointed me to enter the company and will take over the entire operation and management of the company, which is a heavy responsibility. Also, after a transition period, the original senior executives overseas will gradually leave the company. The remaining new core management team consists of current UTStarcom personnel in charge of business, market, and finance.

I am delighted to inform everyone that almost all members of our new management team are Chinese, which is the first time in UTStarcom's history. I believe that with such a team, we will have a better understanding of the situation in China, our employees, and our customers.

Host Quan Jing: Mr. Lu has mainly introduced changes in personnel appointments. Over the past month, due to these changes, what corresponding changes will occur in the company's business strategy and direction?

Lu: This is the third point I want to mention.

In general, it is called returning to China. First, let me clarify that returning to China does not mean focusing solely on the Chinese market. Returning to China means strengthening our headquarters, R&D teams, and our emphasis on the Chinese market. After this transaction, the headquarters will be moved to Beijing, and we will further strengthen our two major R&D centers in Hangzhou and Shenzhen. In fact, UTStarcom has done well in international markets up to now. After overcoming the difficulties of the Little Smart Phone in the Chinese market, it once weakened somewhat. This return to China means that we will again strengthen our commitment and investment in the Chinese market and customers.

Host Quan Jing: Actually, Mr. Lu mentioned the trend of returning to China. If we look at the changes in China's industries this year, friends who are concerned about the industry might have such impressions: the convergence of the three networks has received great support from the State Council, the State Administration of Radio Film and Television, and the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, including policy and funding aspects. The convergence of the three networks is a major trend in China, what opportunities will it bring to UTStarcom? What benefits will your move of returning to China bring?

Lu: It can be said that the convergence of the three networks presents UT with a rare opportunity, or we are really lucky to be at the right time. In January, the State Council issued a decision to promote the convergence of the three networks, and at the beginning of February, UT announced our new strategic adjustment, timing it perfectly. In fact, we had done a lot of preparation beforehand.

The convergence of the three networks, we understand, is the Party and the country further promoting domestic economic development, allowing people to enjoy higher quality, lower cost entertainment and information services. For us, it is truly a rare opportunity, so we are delighted to see this and prepare for it accordingly.

Host Quan Jing: Speaking of seizing this opportunity, we have also noticed that from the company's development strategy perspective, UTStarcom, originally a telecom company, has thrived in the broadcasting industry and taken advantage of the wind of the convergence of the three networks. Please share with us the essence and advantages of UTStarcom in the broadcasting business. From another angle, why did the state think of cooperating with you when promoting the convergence of the three networks? What strengths in UTStarcom caught their attention? Where will you focus next?

Lu: The state wants to eliminate industry barriers through the convergence of the three networks, barriers formed due to historical reasons. For UT or other communication vendors, there is no distinction between telecom and broadcasting from a technical standpoint. What we need to do is provide efficient and low-cost information transmission services. So technically, it is unified, but there are significant differences in business. For example, our telecom industry mainly focuses on information services, personal or group-to-group information services. Our broadcasting industry primarily carries video services, broadcasting services, while also bearing the social responsibility of public opinion guidance and value establishment. I believe the difference between industries may be caused by this.

Five years ago, even earlier, UT was the first in the world to develop IPTV technology, which is actually the later term for high-efficiency network video pushing and distribution services. I believe this may be our earliest connection to broadcasting services. Based on this, our development in broadband access, transmission, and networking has gradually formed our characteristics and advantages in broadcasting services.

Host Quan Jing: Talking about this, Mr. Lu just discussed the trends in industry development. Let's change the perspective and start with the recently highly anticipated CCBN (China Content Broadcasting Network) exhibition. In the discussions, we can find information that aligns with what Mr. Lu said about the industry. This year's CCBN scale and attention exceeded previous years, and broadcasters took the opportunity to promote their advantages and expand their reach. I'd like to share with Mr. Lu: after three days of the exhibition, what are your impressions? How did people discuss the industry trends during exchanges at the exhibition?

Lu: The exhibition was indeed very lively these few days. My first impression is the correctness and appeal of the government's decision. This is the most popular edition I've ever seen, and the UT booth was so busy that I couldn't even squeeze in sometimes.

Secondly, I would describe this exhibition with "right time, right place, and harmony." "Right time" refers to the correct decision of the government and the determination of the State Administration of Radio Film and Television and the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology to promote the convergence of the three networks, giving everyone confidence in the future of the convergence of the three networks, which translates into great enthusiasm for participating in this exhibition.

"Right place," everyone knows, the digital transformation of the broadcasting system, and even the 3G construction we started a couple of years ago, as well as the strong promotion of broadband construction in the telecommunications system, coincides with the trend of the convergence of the three networks. Everyone has been working hard before the State Council clarified, but the efforts and common direction have always been consistent, which is the convergence of the three networks.

As for "harmony," it can be clearly seen from the exhibition. Everyone's face shows passion, expectation, and dedication. This is my feeling at the CCBN exhibition, and it can be described as a particularly successful exhibition.

Host Quan Jing: Mr. Lu shared his insights from the perspectives of "right time, right place, and harmony." Under this big trend, everyone is also very concerned about how the company's development ultimately manifests in products. At this exhibition, what were the main products showcased by UTStarcom? What kind of product could make the booth so crowded that people couldn't even squeeze in? And where is the innovation in the products?

Lu: This time, UTStarcom exhibited a full range of solutions tailored to the NGB (Next Generation Broadcasting) construction of our broadcasting system. Our idea isn't to sell a single product or a set of products; instead, we aim to deeply understand the technical and service needs of the broadcasting industry and provide comprehensive solutions.

First, at the level of business carrying, based on UTStarcom's greatest advantage in video delivery systems, we have further enhanced and optimized the interactive TV demand of the broadcasting industry, providing a comprehensive solution for interactive TV adapted to different broadcasting networks. We know that the access methods of broadcasting networks vary across regions, and some standards may differ slightly. We offer a complete solution.

Additionally, we have proposed a comprehensive terminal solution to meet the needs of different users, ranging from the most standard, simplest, and easiest-to-use solutions to diversified, full-service high-end home service centers. For instance, this time we showcased our home TV set-top box terminal, named Le Xuan. Not only does it include video enjoyment and interaction, but many viewers stayed for long periods, reluctant to leave, which made us very happy.

We also proposed network solutions. We presented our integrated (EPON+EOC) solution, which won the Innovation Award in Technology Solutions, and also exhibited it. We also showcased PTN (Packet Transport Network), which is our latest product, from the TV all the way to the core of the network, UT provided a complete end-to-end solution.

Host Quan Jing: Mr. Lu introduced the product trends. We initially talked about UTStarcom's restructuring in the past, then the exhibition and products representing the present. In the next part, we want to shift our focus to the future. What are your thoughts on the future industry trends? UTStarcom has many advantages and expertise, and in the convergence of the three networks and NGB, there will be good trends. How will you grasp such opportunities in the future?

Lu: We will focus on two aspects: dedication and innovation. After experiencing some adjustments and difficulties, UT may not provide comprehensive telecom solutions like some of our peers do, but we have already invested a lot of resources. And we will continue to invest in the construction of NGB, concentrating our limited resources in this field, refining and perfecting them, closely aligning with the needs of our users and services, and thereby enhancing the competitiveness of our products.

Secondly, innovation is the most important part of UT's core values historically. Innovation doesn't just mean technological innovation but also market services and even company operations in various aspects to achieve innovation. Its orientation is only one: to provide our customers with the best service and the best products.

Host Quan Jing: Speaking of this, you also mentioned the trends of competitors in the industry. We also want to pay attention to how UTStarcom views industry competition. Finally, we would like to discuss with you how UTStarcom views its competitors in the convergence of the three networks and NGB industries. After the restructuring, what are your advantages and disadvantages compared to competitors? How will you deal with this competition in the future?

Lu: Of course, domestically, we have ZTE and Huawei, and internationally, there are many telecom giants. UTStarcom cannot compete with them in terms of scale, but based on our domain and spirit of innovation, I believe we still have a chance. This "chance" refers to normal market competition. No market or industry can be built by just one or a few companies. Only by everyone putting their efforts together and engaging in healthy competition can the industry develop healthily. I like what our sales team says: friendly competitors, not rivals. Together, we will push the convergence of the three networks and NGB construction to new heights, and I hope to see that.

Host Quan Jing: Thank you very much, Mr. Lu. Although Mr. Lu has only been the COO of UTStarcom for four weeks, from his discussion with all of us just now, we can see that whether it is the challenges currently faced by the company or the opportunities ahead, he has sufficient understanding and confidence. We also know that in the future, Mr. Lu will be fully responsible for the overall business development management of UTStarcom. We hope that after one month and in the longer term, UTStarcom will reach a better development stage under his leadership, combining the advantages of the convergence of the three networks in China to seize better development opportunities. We wish you success.

Lu: Thank you.
