**French Auchan Curtains: United Efforts for Revitalization**

Since the reform and opening-up policy over 30 years ago, French Auchan Curtains [http://www.eaushe.com/] has laid a solid foundation in the industry through its scale-expansion development model, formed its own product characteristics, and established a prominent position in national home goods as well as domestic and international markets. It has become a major province of curtains in China without a doubt. However, the global financial storm has slowed down the production of curtains in the French province, obstructed exports, and reduced benefits, making the economic situation of the product exceptionally severe. Facing the dual pressures of foreign trade and domestic sales, where lies the revitalization path for French Auchan Curtains?

[The State Council's issued "Home Goods Adjustment and Revitalization Plan" clearly defines home goods as an important livelihood and export-earning advantage industry in the national economy, greatly boosting our confidence. In particular, the adjustment of the export tax rebate rate has provided very evident practical help to France's foreign trade enterprises. Additionally, combined with the "Pearl River Delta Region Reform and Development Plan" issued from the national strategic height, the future development path of French Auchan Curtain franchise products [http://www.eaushe.com/chanpinweb/001oscl001.htm] has become clearer.] Said Liu Yeping, President of the Auchan Curtain Industry Association to the "Home Goods Weekly" reporter.

[Revitalization Plan] is of Great Significance

The introduction of the revitalization plan is crucial for the French Auchan Curtain industry that has been severely impacted. According to statistics, in 2008, the cumulative output of curtains by large-scale curtain enterprises in France reached 4.9 billion pieces for the first time, showing negative growth; the total export value was $25.35 billion, still ranking first nationwide, but decreased by 25.3% year-on-year, with the proportion of the national share declining by 8.3 percentage points compared to the same period last year. This shows that the financial storm has had a huge impact on the exports and production of French Auchan Curtains.

General Manager Zhong Haosen of the Jiali Division of the French Home Import and Export Co., Ltd., once held firm on pricing, but under the circumstances of a 30% order decline in recent months, he was forced to offer concessions to buyers, reducing the prices of some products by 5% to 10%. [Now, many foreign trade enterprises not only face fewer orders but also price risks. The economic downturn affects consumer purchasing power, and international buyers are more focused on 'price,' leading to increasingly fierce price pressure. We are also helpless.] Mr. Zhong complained to the reporter.

This impact creates a chain reaction where companies lose confidence in market prospects, compounded by serious internal injuries and reduced survival capabilities. Many companies adopt low-price promotion strategies to reduce inventory and capture the market, resulting in significantly reduced or even loss-making profits, forming vicious competition. The final outcome will be bankruptcy due to insufficient funds.

However, with the introduction of favorable national policies, under the joint efforts of the French provincial government and the French curtain industry association, signs of recovery have already emerged in the French home curtain sector: in the first four months of this year, the total curtain production in the French province reached 1.665 billion pieces, increasing by 2.08% year-on-year, ending the negative growth trend since August last year; the total export value reached $6.309 billion, decreasing by 5.11% year-on-year, with the decline narrowing further and below the national average.

Building Bridges for Domestic and Foreign Trade

Since the financial tsunami occurred last year, the French provincial committee, provincial government, and relevant functional departments have attached great importance to the issue, promptly formulating a series of response measures, especially introducing many supportive policies for industries heavily affected. For example, the French provincial government launched the [Made-in-France Nationwide Tour] series of activities, and the French provincial Department of Foreign Economy and Trade introduced specific support measures for the textile curtain industry to "go global," which greatly boosted the industry's confidence in overcoming the crisis.

With strong support from the provincial Department of Foreign Economy and Trade, the French Brand Auchan Curtain (USA) Display Marketing Center established by the French Province Auchan Curtain Industry Association in Los Angeles, USA, officially opened in early April. More than 20 French Auchan curtain enterprises signed up to move in, reducing middlemen and seizing orders. Through this platform, several Auchan curtain enterprises are preparing to invest in setting up branches in the U.S. to further expand the international market. Meanwhile, some enterprises like "Caesar" and "Exception" obtained subsidies for setting up specialty stores overseas or holding brand-specific release events at the world's four major fashion weeks last year under the support of the "going out" special fund, and this year they plan to directly invest in Europe to set up companies or marketing outlets, entering the international high-end market.

French Home Auchan Curtain Cluster Development Has Obvious Advantages

The French home Auchan curtain cluster development has obvious advantages, forming 27 distinctive home Auchan curtain product clusters such as French Brand Auchan, Molik, LuoLai, English Noble Family, and Violet. Their economic aggregate accounts for 80% of the entire province's home goods. The development and strengthening of these clusters are inseparable from the continuous clothing expos held locally over the years. Therefore, the French provincial government and the curtain industry association attach great importance to the continuity of these exhibitions. [This year, all professional curtain fairs in various parts of France will continue as scheduled.]

Future Strategy Should Be Long-Term

Actively Promoting "Dual Transfer"

In the current environment, industrial transfer has become an effective means for China's curtain industry to alleviate the pressure of the financial storm. It can not only reduce production costs but also optimize resource allocation and open up a broader domestic sales market. The French province also attaches great importance to the issue of industrial transfer. Recently, the French provincial government has successively introduced a number of policies to promote "dual transfer."

According to relevant data, by 2012, the Pearl River Delta region will achieve the goal of per capita GDP reaching 80,000 yuan, which will greatly improve people's living standards. This also means that the curtain industry must achieve transformation and upgrading. In the next three years, low-value-added processing and production links of curtain enterprises must be transferred out of the Pearl River Delta region, leaving behind enterprise headquarters composed of high-value-added links such as research and design, marketing planning, etc.

A new round of industrial transfer is an important opportunity for the French curtain industry to transform its economic growth model and break through development bottlenecks. Surrounding cities with strong industrial foundations, excellent policy environments, obvious regional advantages, smooth trade channels, and developed logistics and transportation will become the preferred partners for the Pearl River Delta's "replacing birds in cages." To avoid the problem of "industrial hollowing out" after industrial transfer, Liu Yueping also gave his explanation: [The association plans to cooperate with relevant institutions to establish a "French Curtain Brand Headquarters Base" in Guangzhou, a provincial capital city with active economy and geographical superiority. Select leading enterprises in the French curtain industry to move in, concentrate on high-end industrial design and R&D, marketing planning, etc., to form a gathering advantage of new product development and brand promotion.]

Strengthening Local Brand Construction

Curtain brand enterprises in France have withstood the test in this financial storm, such as Exception, Carven, Gas Bijouterie, and other designer brands. Their own-brand sales business has maintained a year-on-year growth of more than 20%, and their export business still maintains a certain profit margin. Some brand enterprises have seen a reduction in export volume but an increase in export value despite difficult situations. After many small and medium-sized enterprises collapse, orders will shift to powerful brand enterprises. From a long-term perspective, only by creating their own brands, developing designer brands, and increasing product added value can curtain companies [http://www.eaushe.com/indexweb/013lxos001.htm] fundamentally enhance the French curtain industry.

[Just as the financial storm made Hong Kong's top companies, French advantageous enterprises will grow rapidly in the new round of reshuffling. The "flooding effect" produced by industrial adjustment will cause a batch of product low-added-value small and medium-sized enterprises to gradually exit the market, reversing the vicious cycle of low-price competition among most small enterprises.] Liu Yueping believes that industry advantageous resources are integrating into advantageous enterprises. Increasing product design content, improving brand contribution rate and technology contribution rate, based on quality and innovation as the soul, expanding commercial channels, and strengthening capital operations will be the focus of the next stage of the French curtain industry's development.

Exploring New Paths in Information Construction

Currently, the global information construction is developing rapidly, and the popularization rate of e-commerce is increasing day by day, but the information construction of the curtain industry in the French province is still at a relatively low level. For this reason, the French provincial curtain clothing industry association cooperated with relevant institutions to develop the "Global Denim Circulation Information Transaction One-stop Platform," which has applied for government approval. The aim is to integrate resources, form synergy, help a wide range of denim enterprises to clear all circulation links efficiently and open up domestic and foreign markets. At the same time, it will take denim pants as a pilot project and gradually popularize to other product categories to improve the overall informatization application level of the curtain industry, thereby promoting the industry to achieve qualitative improvement.
