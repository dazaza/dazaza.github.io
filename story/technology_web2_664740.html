**What to Do If Your Speaker Has Electrical Noise?**

In general, active speakers will inevitably contain an amplifier inside, so noise is unavoidable. Therefore, claims of "zero noise" are baseless. The noise in active speakers can be roughly divided into electromagnetic interference, ground wire interference, mechanical noise, and thermal noise. Below, we will briefly analyze the causes of these types of noise.

### Electromagnetic Interference:

Electromagnetic interference can mainly be divided into power transformer interference and stray electromagnetic wave interference. Generally speaking, multimedia speakers often use EI-type, toroidal, or R-type transformers.

Toroidal transformers do not have air gaps or coil radiation issues, so theoretically, they should have very little leakage flux. However, these transformers have relatively high requirements for the power supply environment. If the waveform of the lighting electricity we use is severely distorted, the effect of using a toroidal transformer may even be worse than an EI-type one. Therefore, we need to consider the power supply environment we are in. The leakage flux situation of R-type transformers is similar to toroidal ones, which I will not elaborate on here.

After analyzing the reasons simply, we can solve problems in a targeted manner. Under permissible conditions, adding a shielding cover to the transformer has a very obvious effect, as it can block leakage flux to the maximum extent. The shielding cover can only be made of iron-based materials. In general, we should try to choose products from large brands with solid materials. If the quality of the materials used in the transformer core, copper wires, etc., is poor, the iron loss and copper loss of the transformer will be more serious, leading to an increase in the no-load current of the transformer, which will make the leakage flux phenomenon more obvious. Additionally, using an external transformer is also a good method.

### Stray Electromagnetic Waves and Ground Wire Interference:

Stray electromagnetic wave interference is quite common. The speaker wires, crossover networks, wireless devices, or computer hosts can all become sources of interference. Place the main speaker as far away from the computer host as possible under allowable conditions, and reduce the number of surrounding wireless devices. Furthermore, some users connect their speakers to the front audio interface of the computer while inserting a Bluetooth device into the nearby USB port. This can cause electromagnetic wave interference to a certain extent. If users do not frequently change audio equipment, it is recommended to plug the audio line into the rear interface of the host.

Ground wire interference mainly occurs when low-frequency signal circuits, high-frequency signal circuits, and power circuits generate alternating current noise due to improper grounding position selection. In general, high-frequency signals use loop grounding instead of single-wire grounding, while low-frequency signals follow the principle of independent wiring and centralized grounding. In actual use, we are basically not affected by this kind of interference. These grounding methods are basic knowledge in circuit design, and reputable brands will not make such elementary mistakes. I am just briefly introducing related knowledge here.

### Mechanical Noise and Thermal Noise:

Now let's talk about mechanical noise. As the name suggests, this type of noise comes from mechanical motion, which is unique to active speakers. During the operation of the power transformer, the vibration of the core caused by the alternating magnetic field produces mechanical noise, which is very similar to the buzzing sound emitted by fluorescent light ballasts. Choosing high-quality products is still the best way to prevent this kind of noise. Additionally, we can install a rubber shock-absorbing layer between the transformer and the mounting plate.

There is another point we should pay attention to: if the potentiometer has been used for a long time, the contact between the metal brush and the film may become poor due to dust accumulation and wear, producing noise when rotated. If the screws of the speaker are not tightened, or the port tube is not properly handled, mechanical noise may also occur when playing dynamic music.

Finally, let's talk about the impact of thermal noise on speakers. Since our speakers use resistors, capacitors, transistors, ICs, and other components, the conductive parts of these components contain a large number of free electrons. As the temperature of the components increases, the number of free electrons will also increase significantly, strengthening the random movement of electrons. This strengthening will be reflected in the "hissing" sound emitted by the tweeter unit.

To handle this kind of noise, we can replace it with low-noise components or reduce the working load of the components. Additionally, lowering the operating temperature is also an effective method.

### Conclusion:

In summary, the noise problem of active speakers cannot be avoided. Due to the structural limitations of the product, some electromagnetic interference is inevitable. We can only try to choose products manufactured by large factories, pay attention to placing the speakers away from radiation sources, and avoid using them in environments with excessively high temperatures. Additionally, ensure stable municipal electricity and allocate independent sockets for the speakers, which can help reduce electrical noise to a certain extent.
