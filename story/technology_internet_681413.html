Self-study examination diploma application: beware of critical flaws in the graduation thesis

by qindingdangx on 2012-02-28 13:26:23

According to the news from Zhisheng Education [http://www.hds0110475_dbkj.com/], the application process for graduates of the second half of 2011 self-study examination has begun. Many self-study students' undergraduate graduation thesis often does not meet the requirements. Wuhan Zhisheng Education [http://www.hds0110475_dbkj.com/] reminds self-study graduates to avoid obvious [hard injuries] in their graduation papers.

First, the material is disconnected from the theme being argued.

The material must revolve around the theme and serve to express and argue the theme. However, some candidates feel that the materials they have found are very good, thinking they are vivid, and are unwilling to remove them. As a result, some materials unrelated to the theme are also written into the article, affecting the expression of the theme. For example, when writing about the theme of "loyalty and righteousness," including the story of "Meng Mother's Three Moves" may be touching, but it is unrelated to the theme, so it should be removed.

Second, inaccurate evidence.

Some candidates select inappropriate materials or do not verify their authenticity, using them without discrimination. This results in errors in names, events, times, locations, and numbers. For example, the famous saying "A soldier who does not want to be a general is not a good soldier" was incorrectly attributed by some candidates to Hitler.

Third, lack of organization.

Some candidates fail to write according to the general思路of presenting problems, analyzing problems, and summarizing problems. The entire thesis appears disorganized, making it unclear what is being conveyed.

Fourth, outdated materials.

Some graduation theses use outdated examples, appearing stale and lacking freshness and relevance. The presented materials seem like "stale old grains," making the article lack persuasiveness. For instance, an article about the reform of old industrial bases cited figures from the last century, lacking both freshness and persuasiveness.

Fifth, inaccurate word usage.

Some candidates have poor text expression abilities and do not pay attention to revisions, resulting in inaccurate word usage. For example, "罄竹难书" (a phrase typically used in a negative context) is mistakenly used as a positive term. Some candidates even create their own words, making it difficult for readers to understand their true meaning, such as "deeply many wishes."

Sixth, misuse of punctuation.

Some candidates misuse punctuation marks and make many typographical errors. These mistakes reflect a lack of solid language skills. Undergraduate students should not make these basic errors.
