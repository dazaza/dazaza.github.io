"Anti-TaoBao Alliance", perhaps Taobao should reflect on some of its issues.

by fishmen on 2009-07-28 18:30:57

Upon closer inspection, it turned out that my Alipay account was permanently frozen. To retrieve the money inside, I would have to submit a household registration book. Submitting an ID card and bank transaction proof might be easier, but since my household registration book is at my hometown, submitting a scanned copy would mean spending 300 yuan on round-trip transportation costs, which is quite frustrating.

The frozen account cannot be restored, so I thought it didn't matter much and listened to customer service's suggestion to re-register an Alipay account. However, the problem lies in Taobao's unpleasant policy: a frozen Alipay account cannot be unlinked, meaning that the account I painstakingly operated to become a diamond seller will now be voided. Last night, after several unsuccessful negotiations with Taobao and Alipay customer service, their direct response was to let me re-register a Taobao account and bind it to a new Alipay account. In the end, I decided to abandon Taobao and switch to platforms like "Baidu Youa". If Taobao continues this way, they will only reap what they sow due to their repeated unjust actions.

I feel friendly towards both sellers and buyers who have transacted on Taobao, but I have always found the attitudes of Taobao and Alipay lacking. Since opening my store in 2005, I have encountered several instances of shop closures and account freezes, and this time my account has been permanently frozen. Many reasons for freezing accounts are quite bizarre, and even more nauseating is the attitude of their customer service.

Here are a few points about Taobao: They can freeze your account at any time as it is within their rights, and freezing your funds is their exclusive privilege. Counterfeit goods on Taobao will always exist (even though Mr. Jack Ma recently said he would clean up shops selling fake and substandard products).

As for many buyers complaining about purchasing counterfeit goods and filing complaints, many people know they are buying fakes but still make the purchase, then shout that they were deceived afterward, which is unacceptable.

In certain aspects, Taobao has indeed made life easier for many people, offering good quality and inexpensive products. I have booked airplane tickets four times this year through Taobao, which was very convenient.

In summary, both buyers and sellers are innocent; when they decide to exploit us depends on their mood.

I won't join the "Anti-Taobao Alliance." I no longer have any thoughts about Taobao. It's my right to change my password if there are security concerns with my account, and Alipay has no right to freeze my account. The unpleasant attitude of Taobao's customer service regarding some questions also makes me look down on them. We commoners can only accept their unilateral terms; under China's circumstances, if you want to fight against them, check your wallet first. Mutual understanding is needed for the progress of China's Internet, and Taobao's continued practices in this manner are not sustainable.
