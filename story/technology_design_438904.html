Centipede is an important ingredient in more than 30 Chinese patent medicines such as ginseng再造丸, 大活络丹, and 牵正散, and has remarkable efficacy in traditional Chinese medicine. For example, centipedes can be used to treat phlebitis, blood clot blockage, trigeminal neuralgia, anti-cancer detoxification and dispersing nodules, wind-dispelling and sedation, facial paralysis, tetanus, etc. These applications have sharply increased the demand for centipedes. Besides medicinal use, centipedes can also be made into nourishing food.

With the advancement of social material civilization, the No.2 Golden Head Centipede Breeding Base in rural areas has vigorously developed animal husbandry, which is a good way to rely on rural resource advantages. As a traditional valuable Chinese herb, centipedes have been used medicinally for a long history, having been recognized and utilized by people over 2000 years ago. There are detailed records in many medical classics such as *Compendium of Materia Medica*.

The medicinal value of centipedes bred by Hubei Agricultural Science People's Welfare No.2 Centipede Breeding Base is very high, with over 100 Chinese herbal prescriptions containing centipedes.

Centipedes, as excellent therapeutic and health care products, have received much attention. Centipede wine and centipede Fen health products have successively appeared. In addition, with the development of medicine, the role of centipede venom has been widely recognized. Centipede venom is more precious than gold, costing about $150,000 per kilogram. About 500 grams of venom can be extracted annually from 10,000 centipedes, so the medicinal value of centipede venom far exceeds that of the centipedes themselves.

For many years, the market supply of centipedes has largely depended on wild capture. However, with the extensive use of fertilizers and pesticides, the ecological environment of wild centipedes has been damaged, and the natural population has drastically decreased, leading to insufficient market supply. Currently, the national production can only meet around 30% of the demand, causing a sharp rise in market prices. Artificial breeding of centipedes has broad prospects for development, and there will be a wave of getting rich by raising centipedes across the country.
