My insights on ghostly energy

by lk0gamej on 2009-07-19 06:22:27

I am not a master of reviews; I'm just a loyal player of the Ghost Qi class. Originally, I didn't want to write this stuff, but alas~ with nothing better to do in the evening, and having just seen a few Ghost Qi players who wrote some brainless articles, it made me very upset. Such a great class, yet they don't appreciate it at all.

I've read quite a few articles. Some have good and correct points, while others make me so angry. What's this about Moonlight Ghosts, CD Ghosts, or Ground Wave Ghosts? Are you trying to create some hybrid abomination? Ghost Qi is Ghost Qi, signature skills are signatures for a reason. Regarding the Sword Ghost and Magic Burst builds, I still don't entirely agree. Fully upgrading both 'Ghost Slash' and 'Soul Devour' isn't without merit; at least when clearing maps, it feels insane. The Magic Burst build is even more powerful. Maxing out 'Magic Explosion' and 'Ancient Power', then equipping gear that enhances Magic Burst, the damage dealt is ridiculous. In Player vs Player (PVP), if luck is on your side, it can one-hit KO an opponent. No!?! It makes you the main event. However, I wonder how many good skills the Magic Burst build has to abandon because SP (Skill Points) aren't enough.

Now let me explain my own build: (First off, I prefer PVP)

Ground Wave 1 (It's necessary)

Row Wave 5 + Forced Activation (Some people say just level it once, I've tried it, but then it won't chain properly. In PVP, I like using Row Wave as a follow-up when 'Soul Devour' is on cooldown. This gives you ample time to do other things.)

Upward Thrust 10 with Forced Activation (Since I PVP, hehe, chaining into 'Soul Devour' is terrifying, even against older classes.)

Silver Drop 1, Continuous Slash 1, Three-Section Slash 1, Weapon Mastery maxed out (This is essential. For map-clearing, Continuous Slash might not be necessary. I chose the Longsword because... it looks cool! Though it's expensive.)

Cross Slash 1, Original Mountain 1 (More skills mean more chances. With limited SP, not learning them would be like hitting a wall.)

Ghost Slash 5, Soul Devour maxed out (I originally wanted to max both, as the damage is quite impressive. But there's a difference of double damage between level 5 and max level 20 Ghost Slash. However, it costs an additional 300 SP. - - I hesitated, and Ghost Slash doesn't need forced activation.)

Moonlight 5 leading to Full Moon (The Moonlight Ghosts can go die somewhere. Here, I must say Moonlight doesn't require forced activation, it's completely unnecessary and wasteful, unless you're brain-dead.)

Red Array 5, Green Array 5, Dark Moon 5, Purple Array 1, Ice Array maxed out (The first few are essential. I believe maxing Ice Array is also crucial. Can you freeze enemies (monsters) with only level 3? In later-stage PVP, being able to freeze an opponent once could determine victory. When clearing maps, you'll realize this choice is absolutely correct, as a maxed Ice Array has a high chance of freezing a group of monsters, which offers significant benefits and reduces your chances of getting hurt.)

Residual Image maxed out, Ghost Step maxed out (For PVP, adding 12% speed is very dominant. If you're focusing on map-clearing, you don't need to max these. This is also a godly skill. Most classes need at least basic movement speed to keep up with the pace; otherwise, you'll just get stomped.)

Ghost Flash maxed out (Although after the update, the damage has decreased significantly, it remains a godly skill. Personally, I prefer the full-bodied technique, so I'm considering resetting it to level 1. However, those who want to ensure a high win rate should definitely max it out.)

Tombstone and Infernal Flame (These are like fish and bear paws—both are great skills, but with limited SP, only one can be fully upgraded. I chose to max Infernal Flame and leave Tombstone at level 1. Why? Many of you may be used to Tombstone and love this stylish and broken skill, but its utility in map-clearing diminishes significantly at higher levels. You'll discover this as you progress. The damage difference between a maxed Tombstone and a maxed Infernal Flame is more than half. If a maxed Tombstone can take out 1 health bar, then a maxed Infernal Flame can take out 2 bars, at least. So naturally, we choose the higher-damage option. Many might not be accustomed to using Infernal Flame, but with practice, you'll develop insights, much like with Tombstone. Of course, I rarely use that skill except when showing off, because I feel invincible whenever I use it.)
