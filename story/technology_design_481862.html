GaoPeng.com's Initial Defeat in Operations Leads to a Major Strategic Reversal: Layoff Plans Announced

by pex9wew8n on 2011-08-26 11:33:27

On August 19, one of the general managers of Groupon China, Raphael Strauch, announced to a department of 50 editors in Shanghai: "You are being laid off."

An internal employee of Groupon, Judy (a pseudonym), said that at its peak, Groupon had about 80 offices in China with over 3,000 employees. According to M arbridge Consulting's data, Groupon was spending 15 million yuan per month on advertisements on Google and Baidu.

In fact, "they have been laying people off for at least three months," said James Liu, a photographer at Groupon. This is the third and largest round of layoffs.

When asked about the current total number of company employees, Ouyang Yun refused to disclose this information citing "business confidentiality." Groupon also told foreign media that the company does not disclose employee numbers by country and the exact number of employees at the Chinese joint venture (Groupon China) is currently uncertain.

"Initially, it was oriented towards localized services, but later it gradually shifted to 'product group buying,'" said Judy. Service-oriented merchants need to be negotiated individually by BDs (Business Developers). For products, suppliers will proactively negotiate contracts at headquarters or core stations, meaning one BD can handle contracts for 50 merchants.

This is a chain reaction. As the operational strategy reversed, the value of small city branch offices and ground promotion personnel diminished, and graphic design and photography teams were no longer necessary since brand merchants typically provide product images and descriptions.

On the same day, "we suddenly received notice that colleagues from HR and finance would come to the Jinhua station. After they arrived, they announced: the Jinhua station is being closed," said Li Qiang (a pseudonym), an employee who was laid off. The first batch of employees hired by Groupon was about to complete their six-month probation period. But at the last moment of the probationary period, they were suddenly laid off without any preparation.

Groupon spokesperson Heather Dickinson said, "Groupon's international expansion strategy involves significant initial investment, and as we gain deeper understanding of local markets, the company will gradually refine its strategies. The joint venture in China is one of many examples in emerging markets where we adjust our strategies as the company develops."

Where did the problem lie?

However, high investment did not bring high returns. "Data monitored by iResearch shows that currently among the top 10 shopping websites by visitor count, excluding Taobao Mall and JD.com, the remaining eight spots are all occupied by group-buying sites. Unfortunately, Groupon China did not make it onto this list," said Fu Zhiyong, General Manager of iResearch E-commerce Consulting Center, to Southern Metropolis Daily. The secret to increasing traffic for group-buying sites lies in offering sufficiently large discounts, not through "advertising bombardment."

According to Zhao Zhankui, the lawyer representing some of the laid-off employees of Groupon, so far, 13 regional branches including Qinhuangdao, Yantai, and Nanning have been completely shut down. Additionally, 18 branches including Shanghai, Hangzhou, and Foshan have carried out layoffs, affecting more than 400 employees. He said, "Some employees were dissatisfied with how they were dismissed and eventually received two months' salary as compensation after negotiations."

The crux of the issue lies here. "Before attacking the market, Groupon had meticulously calculated: how much to invest, how long the payment terms should be, how much profit each customer contributes, etc., leaving almost no room for maneuver. However, restaurant owners in China prefer to 'go with their gut.' They may demand cash settlement today and higher rebates tomorrow, not following the contract. Once the market response mechanism fails, Groupon's sales team struggles to secure enough merchant resources, making it difficult to balance between attracting customers and earning profits. Ultimately, the discount levels offered are relatively low." Chen Xu, the market director of a well-known national group-buying site, frankly admitted to Southern Metropolis Daily. The performance evaluation standard for group-buying BDs (salespeople) is roughly signing contracts with 2-5 merchants per month, which can reveal their capabilities within two months of probation. However, Groupon strictly adheres to labor laws —— requiring a six-month probation period for a three-year contract, naturally leading to bloated staffing.

This series of actions indicates a major reversal in the strategy Groupon set months ago to aggressively enter the Chinese group-buying market.

The sudden collapse of outlets and personnel structure

"The next Monday, I would have become a full-time employee. Department reviews were fine, just waiting for the Beijing headquarters to stamp the seal. But at the last moment of the six-month probation period, I was notified of the layoff," described Shaun, an employee who was laid off. His computer was required to be formatted, and he had to sign and leave within an hour, with no room for negotiation!

Neither Groupon nor its parent company responded to this number. Eight months ago, the American "group-buying pioneer" Groupon formulated a grand strategy to aggressively enter China and invested 8.6 million US dollars to own 40% of Groupon China.

Bad news came without warning.

At the "2011 Internet Conference" held on August 23, Groupon China's Chief Operating Officer Ouyang Yun clarified externally, "Groupon has indeed been optimizing its organizational structure and adjusting its overall strategy recently. In this regard, we will slightly reduce our manpower investment in medium and small cities."

In fact, the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission has already questioned the unconventional accounting metrics used by Groupon in its IPO application. The prospectus shows that Groupon's revenue last year was 713.4 million US dollars, but the net loss reached 413.4 million US dollars. Critics claim that Groupon's approach conceals high marketing costs.

After the initial failure, Groupon quickly activated its backup tactics.

The so-called "fine-tuning" refers to "Groupon's strategic thinking remains unchanged —— achieving marginal cost reduction close to zero through scale expansion, meaning the investment needed for one BD to handle one merchant or fifty merchants is the same." Fu Zhiyong analyzed for Southern Metropolis Daily. On the surface, the lack of standards and irregularities in China's service market hurt Groupon deeply, but the hidden secret is: whether it is NASDAQ in the United States, or A-shares and Growth Enterprises Market in China, the gates of the capital market are beginning to close to concept stocks. Notable signals include the shelving of domestic and overseas listings for companies like JD.com and QiJia.com. This means that venture capitalists' "cash-out" exceeds expectations, and group-buying companies may face difficulties in financing in the second half of the year.

The shift from "service orientation" to "product orientation"

The recent personnel changes at Groupon reflect the predicament brought about by the rapid expansion of group-buying sites. South China Morning Post cartoonist: Zhang Jianhui.

According to incomplete statistics by the lawyer representing the laid-off employees of Groupon, the editorial department in Shanghai has already laid off 50 people, the Yantai station has laid off all 12 employees, and the Jinhua station has laid off 9 employees and closed the station... In just one week, 13 regional branches including Qinhuangdao, Yantai, and Nanning have been completely shut down, and 18 branches including Shanghai, Hangzhou, and Foshan have carried out layoffs, affecting more than 400 people.

However, Groupon's Chief Operating Officer Ouyang Yun strongly denied the "large-scale layoffs," instead saying, "Indeed, there have been adjustments in human resource allocation across the country recently. Specifically, we hope to allocate more manpower to medium and large cities. In this regard, we will slightly reduce our manpower investment in medium and small cities. At the same time, we are centralizing our sales operation system, which is why some employees have had to leave us."
