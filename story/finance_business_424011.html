How to improve the efficiency of centipede breeding:

(1) Constant Temperature Breeding - Constant temperature breeding of centipedes is also known as non-hibernation breeding. This involves using artificial temperature control methods to break the hibernation habits of centipedes, keeping them in good growth and development states throughout the year.

The key equipment for constant temperature breeding is a warm room with a certain area that can be heated, temperature-controlled, and well-insulated. These warm rooms can be newly built or existing ordinary houses or plastic greenhouses can be renovated.

(2) Plastic Greenhouse Breeding - During the day, fully utilize the sun's rays to increase the temperature inside the plastic greenhouse. Cover the greenhouse with straw curtains in the evening to maintain warmth, significantly shortening or even eliminating the hibernation period of centipedes, extending their growing time, and improving breeding efficiency. Plastic greenhouse breeding depends on sunlight, so weather changes must be noted: In case of rain or snow, timely temporary heating measures should be taken to avoid centipede hibernation. As spring arrives and temperatures rise, ventilation should be noted.

Methods of Heating Centipede Breeding Rooms:

Breeding rooms must be built with conditions that allow heating, temperature control, and good insulation. The size of such breeding rooms generally depends on the number of centipedes overwintering, heating conditions, and the adequacy of heat energy supply. There are many methods of heating. Coal stoves, sawdust stoves, rice husk stoves, electric heaters, etc., can be used.

Isolated Breeding for Pregnant Centipedes:

Centipedes are dioecious. Female centipedes about to lay eggs have swollen bodies, with abdomens almost touching the ground, moving slowly, and are relatively easy to identify. Centipedes should ideally be isolated during the egg-laying and incubation periods. The method is: place a small pot or use a tin can for individual breeding within the breeding pot to avoid interference from other centipedes and facilitate management.
