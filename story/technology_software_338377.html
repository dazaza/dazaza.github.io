In terms of shopping, some mall service personnel engage in serious idle chatter when there are no consumers making purchases; some shop assistants lack initiative. The report shows that the main issues in the tourism consumption environment include: some catering businesses failing to disclose their charges, and not proactively providing invoices after meals; within tourist attractions, some catering businesses reduce service quality due to high foot traffic, such as having worn-out menus, oily floors, "Beautiful Man Returns" actor Junsu Zhang's Asian tour meet-and-greet event in Beijing, servers giving curt answers to customer questions, or even arguing with customers; some high-star hotels and small inns do not clearly display check-out times; there are wires passing through the corridors of small inns, posing safety hazards; transportation infrastructure needs improvement, such as some larger subway stations lacking public restrooms; transportation services are not standardized, such as drivers and conductors talking in the cabin while driving, taxi drivers using mobile phones while driving; the phenomenon of unlicensed taxis still exists at long-distance bus stations and railway passenger centers, and even some drivers from legitimate taxi companies refuse passengers or overcharge them; important scenic spots or bends within attractions have unclear signs, places with potential safety hazards lack warning signs, Hainan 7 Days Inn; some scenic spot introduction boards have faded characters, and some contain typographical errors; some small travel agencies do not sign contracts with tourists, do not provide official invoices, guides arbitrarily change itineraries, service is not standardized, and they lack professional knowledge.
