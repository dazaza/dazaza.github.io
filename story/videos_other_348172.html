Seize Structural Opportunities Amidst Plunge

by huaxun892 on 2010-04-19 14:54:56

Today, the two city stock markets continued the falling trend from the previous weekend, both opening significantly lower with large gaps. The Shanghai Composite Index opened at 3,096.78 points, while the Shenzhen Component Index opened at 12,248.02 points, dropping by 33.52 points and 168.84 points respectively. Subsequently, under the leadership of real estate stocks, banking stocks, and coal-oil resource heavyweight stocks that were continuously hit by negative news, both markets experienced persistent volatility as they probed for bottom support. Currently, the Shenzhen Component Index, heavily influenced by real estate stocks, has fallen over 3%, whereas the Shanghai Composite Index has also dropped more than 2%, indicating a phase of weak market characteristics.

From today's news perspective, although there was some slightly positive information, such as an insider revealing on the 18th that the list of second-batch margin trading pilot brokers is expected to be announced early in May, with five brokers not included in the first batch likely to directly enter the second batch, there was no significant good news. On the contrary, there were more significant pieces of bad news, making it a clearly bearish situation. First, last weekend, the U.S. stock market and international commodity futures markets fell sharply due to Goldman Sachs being charged by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission for alleged securities fraud, which brought considerable adverse effects to today’s domestic securities market operations, especially impacting the heavyweight stocks led by coal, oil, and non-ferrous metals. According to the operating trends of the three major U.S. indices, although the U.S. stock market does not yet show clear signs of a medium-term top, it has entered a possible short-term peak area, which could have an important negative psychological impact on some investment institutions. Second, after announcing the policy of increasing the down payment ratio and interest rate by 1.1 times for second homes on April 14, the State Council issued a notice on April 17 requiring banks in regions with excessively high commercial housing prices, rapid price increases, and supply shortages to suspend issuing loans for purchasing three or more homes. The continuous introduction of new measures to control housing prices has led to new listings showing signs of price reductions, accelerating the exit of property investors. This news will continue to exert significant adjustment pressure on real estate stocks, and because of their connection with heavyweight stock sectors such as banks and steel, along with their special weight and popularity in the market, it will also bring a significant negative impact on the entire market.

Under the influence of major negative news, the market saw a sharp decline today. From the 15-minute K-line chart of the market, although there are no obvious signs of a bottom yet, there is a possibility of retesting the support near the 3,000-point major level close to the annual line. However, after continuous declines, the probability of further sustained large falls at the current point is not very high. Therefore, aggressive investors with better technical analysis skills can use about 60% of their positions to moderately buy into potential hot sector leaders like pharmaceuticals and agriculture during dips, engaging in short-term trading with quick entry and exit, while paying attention to possible medium-short term low-absorption opportunities in heavyweight sectors such as insurance stocks.
