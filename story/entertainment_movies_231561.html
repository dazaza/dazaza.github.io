"Avatar" Plot Summary:

In the distant future world, humans have launched the "Avatar" program to obtain resources from another planet - Pandora, and mixed the DNA of humans and Na'vi (the natives of Pandora) to cultivate a nearly 3-meter-tall "Avatar", which allows humans to live and mine on Pandora. A disabled ex-soldier named Jake Sully agrees to participate in the experiment and comes to the paradise-like Pandora through his "Avatar". However, in this beautiful yet mysterious place, the creatures crawling on the ground, flying in the sky, and drilling in the soil are all giant and potentially fatal... Jake carries a heavy responsibility, amidst dangerous environments. After an unexpected encounter with the Na'vi people, he opens up the possibility of communication, but will also face an unimaginable, grand conflict. From this, he will also gain unprecedented enlightenment...

The story begins on Earth, where Jake Sully (played by Sam Worthington) is a paralyzed veteran who feels like he has nothing worth fighting for. Therefore, when he is asked to go to Pandora to work for a mining company, he readily accepts.

On Pandora, there is a mineral called "unobtanium" that does not exist anywhere else and could completely change the energy industry of humanity. This is what draws humans here despite the long distance. However, Pandora is entirely unsuitable for human habitation as its atmosphere is toxic to humans and its native flora and fauna are fierce predators, extremely dangerous. The environment has also given rise to a unique species: the 10-foot tall blue-skinned humanoid creatures known as the "Na'vi." The Na'vi are very dissatisfied with the arrival of human settlers and do not like their machines continuously digging mines and leaving scars on their planet.

Due to the harsh environment of Pandora, traditional space suits and mechanical armor are insufficient to protect miners. Thus, scientists turn to cloning technology: they combine human DNA with Na'vi DNA to create a cloned Na'vi that can be controlled by a human consciousness. However, not everyone can control this cloned Na'vi; only those whose DNA matches the human part of the clone can do so.

Jake Sully's brother was the original donor of the human DNA for this cloned Na'vi and was able to control it. However, after his brother was killed, the mining company had to find someone else to control the cloned Na'vi to avoid wasting the money spent on its creation. Naturally, they turned to Jake Sully, who was thrilled at the prospect of walking again.

A few years later, Jake Sully arrives on Pandora. He finds the scenery there indescribably beautiful: towering trees over 900 feet tall, floating mountains scattered across the sky, lush rainforests filled with colorful exotic plants, and various bioluminescent creatures at night... It's like a dreamlike magical garden. However, he soon experiences the danger here as he encounters a venomous wolf, just moments away from being eaten before being saved by an arrow shot by a Na'vi girl (played by Zoe Saldana). Through her, Jake learns more about Pandora.

The Na'vi have always lived harmoniously with other species on Pandora, leading a simple and natural life. As Jake interacts with this Na'vi girl, he gradually changes his views on why humans are mining on Pandora. He realizes he has found something worth fighting for.

However, if Jake Sully wants to join the Na'vi in their war against human invaders, he must pay a great price: he cannot stay in his "Avatar" forever. When the "Avatar" sleeps, his consciousness returns to his own disabled human body, requiring specialized equipment to return to the "Avatar." Once he becomes an enemy of his own kind, he will lose the ability to connect with his "Avatar" and will be confined to his disabled body, losing the Na'vi girl he increasingly likes...

The story takes place in a distant future, where the protagonist Jake is a paraplegic former marine recruited by the government for an experiment, becoming a three-meter tall blue-skinned human-made person. Subsequently, Jake is sent to a distant planet rich in resources, whose inhabitants have their own unique language and culture. Not long after arriving, he becomes embroiled in a battle for survival with the local natives...
