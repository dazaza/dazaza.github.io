E-commerce: A New Marketing Breakthrough for Small and Medium-sized Enterprises

by now on 2008-06-19 16:15:17

The author recently learned that in Jinjiang, Fujian, which is the largest sports shoe production base in China, many shoemakers are feeling the pinch as their profit margins continue to shrink this year due to multiple factors including the appreciation of RMB, increase in raw material prices, and especially a significant rise in labor costs.

Observers believe that this reflects the common predicament faced by SMEs today. Strengthening information technology infrastructure, particularly the application of e-commerce websites, can be highly effective and has the potential to become a powerful tool for alleviating the aforementioned pressures.

It is understood that networking and e-commerce have been regarded as the direction of enterprise informatization development. Vigorously developing corporate e-commerce websites means that companies can conduct transactions through the Internet, which is the best way for enterprises to simplify logistics and capital flow. It can reduce costs in many links such as procurement, production, and sales, while significantly improving service quality and product quality, and even enhancing product prices to a certain extent.

A salesperson from a company in Guangdong told the author that utilizing the corporate website for online marketing can expedite order processing, shorten manufacturing time, and deliver goods to customers within a day, or even a couple of hours, thereby accelerating capital turnover. Real-time cost calculations can be performed for each order, leading to quotations and shortening the settlement cycle.

However, according to incomplete statistics, only 20%-30% of Chinese enterprises are online, and fewer companies use the Internet for business operations. Most enterprises are still at the stage of information release, collection, and exchange. The formal signing of orders and contracts, payments, and distribution activities are mostly still conducted offline. In contrast, most American companies widely conduct business activities via the Internet, with nearly 70% of their business behaviors completed on the Internet.

Experts indicate that the development of e-commerce by domestic enterprises is a long and arduous task. On one hand, the government needs to provide guidance, and enterprises themselves must also pay sufficient attention. On the other hand, the quality of Internet-based services must keep up. The author learned from Now Networks (www.now.cn), a domestic domain registration and website hosting service provider, that SMEs building e-commerce websites have relatively high requirements for space. Most companies choose to purchase VPS hosts or servers for hosting to meet the operational needs of their websites. Amidst the rising costs in the domestic market, the company recently launched a promotion offering up to 1000 yuan discount on server hosting in China Telecom data centers, aiming to attract and promote the construction of online marketing platforms by SMEs.

According to statistics, the transaction volume of the e-commerce market in 2007 exceeded 70 billion yuan, and it is expected to surpass 1 trillion yuan in 2008. The driving force mainly comes from the increase in prices and labor costs, which have brought significant impacts to traditional business models of enterprises. "Change leads to progress," and it is believed that e-commerce will certainly provide SMEs with a new marketing breakthrough. At the same time, increased government support and the huge business opportunities brought by the Olympics will also become important driving forces.
