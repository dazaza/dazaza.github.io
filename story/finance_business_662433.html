County Party Secretary Goes Undercover as a Taxi Driver to Investigate Security; Local Police Reports Decrease

by ggh1z9805 on 2012-02-10 10:39:55

Southern Daily News (Reported by Cui Caixin, Communicated by Liu Wei) - In the past three months, if you took a taxi in Xuwen, it's possible that your driver was the Party Secretary! The author learned that since October last year, the Party Secretary of Xuwen and other county leaders conducted multiple covert surveys on the security situation. Simultaneously, the county launched special operations such as "Autumn Wind" and "Clear Web", implemented grid management, and adopted dual measures of plainclothes police and mobile armed patrols to reverse the severe security situation. Alerts for "double snatching" incidents have decreased month by month.

The Secretary visited markets and stations multiple times.

Affected by various factors, since the second half of last year, there has been a rebound in criminal and public security cases in Xuwen County. There has been a significant increase in criminal alerts, with "double snatching" cases in the county being particularly prominent. Several consecutive serious criminal cases have had adverse effects on society and seriously affected the peace and prosperity of the people.

During the "two sessions" of Xuwen County in September last year, the county leaders promised: to restore clear skies to Xuwen within three months! Immediately, the Party Secretary Zhong Li separately interviewed the chiefs of all police stations in the county and the captains of various police units to find the root causes of the security issues and devise effective solutions.

At around 3 a.m. the next morning, Zhong Li woke up the director of the General Office of the County Committee, Li Yi. The two drove through the streets and alleys of Xuwen County to experience the security situation. Later, they followed the 207 National Highway deep into Hai'an Town. In Beiguan Village, they encountered a couple who were transporting vegetables to the county. Zhong Li stopped the car, chatted with the villagers, listened to their reflections on the security situation, and solicited their opinions and suggestions.

In November last year, some taxi drivers reported being threatened during their operations. Zhong Li specially rented a taxi and disguised himself as a driver, delving into the docks and stations of Hai'an Port and Hai'an Bus Station. He stayed nearly half a day at the docks and stations, chatting casually with vendors and having heartfelt conversations with out-of-town guests, obtaining first-hand data on the security situation in Xuwen.

Within three months, Zhong Li utilized evenings, holidays, and other rest days, either riding a tricycle or driving a taxi while disguised as a driver. He personally experienced the security environment in key places such as markets, stations, and docks, listening to the voices of the people, seeking effective solutions, and addressing chronic problems.

In three months, 215 criminal cases were solved.

On October 13 last year, Zhong Li and Xuwen County Magistrate Lin Haiwu conducted research at the Public Security Bureau, requiring them to turn around the severe security situation in Xuwen within three months. Lin Haiwu used weekends, evenings, and other rest times to delve into the grassroots and listen to the voices of the masses; simultaneously, he went deep into each police station, holding on-site office meetings to solve practical difficulties encountered by the grassroots police stations.

Since October last year, the Xuwen County Committee and County Government dispatched working groups to the Public Security Bureau. Every day, a county leader would sit in at the Public Security Bureau to oversee and direct the strict crackdown and rectification work.

It is introduced that since the National Day last year, Xuwen urgently deployed and carried out a series of special actions such as "Autumn Wind" and "Clear Web". Grid management was implemented, dividing the streets and districts of the county into areas managed by various detachments and police stations. Each township police station arranged police forces every night at key traffic routes entering the county to intercept suspicious individuals. At the same time, plainclothes and mobile armed patrols were combined, setting up a tight net across the entire county.

With the support of the Zhanjiang Municipal Public Security Bureau's special task force, the Xuwen Public Security anti-gang special task force carried out five secret large-scale operations, successfully dismantling a long-standing black society crime syndicate in Xuwen and successfully eliminating an armed robbery criminal gang, solving multiple armed robbery cases.

It is introduced that in the three months from October to December last year, Xuwen solved a total of 215 criminal cases, arrested 184 criminal suspects, and dismantled 19 various criminal gangs involving 76 people. Compared to the previous three months, there were 69 more criminal cases solved, 6 more criminal gangs dismantled, and an increase of 25 people. Successively solved were the "10.25" Xuwen middle school student gun murder case, the "10.23" taxi driver robbery murder case, and the "9.14" Qujie Tianyang villager hacking death case, with a 100% homicide solving rate. According to statistics from 110, the alerts for "double snatching" incidents in the entire county have decreased month by month.
