9. 2011 Zhejiang College Entrance Examination Essay: My Time

Topic-based essay: Each generation has its own idols. Ji Xianlin, Qian Xuesen, Yuan Longping, Zong Qinghou, Zhang Yimou, Pony Ma, Liu Xiang... They are successful individuals from different eras and industries, with countless admirers and imitators following in their footsteps. What they have done, as stated by Ying Jianren, and how they did it, has been written into various motivational books. However, their success is hard to replicate because time changes, everything changes, and the conditions for one's success also change.

Time is not an abstract concept. The seasons change from spring to summer to autumn to winter. Physical time flows second by second with the ticking of the clock, while life's time accumulates through moments of joy and sorrow, and is built through choices right and wrong. Therefore, the true meaning of life does not lie in replicating others' success, but in knowing oneself and doing the right things at the right time!

Based on the meaning of the above material, write an article titled "My Time" with no fewer than 800 words. Requirements: ① Choose an angle, clarify your theme, as stated by Ying Jianren. ② All genres except poetry are acceptable. ③ No plagiarism or imitation allowed.

Commentary: The Zhejiang College Entrance Examination essay is a guiding topic-based essay derived from material, with relatively broad angles and scope, making it easier to handle. The two provided text materials, one closely tied to real-life, offer cross-era, multi-industry successful person examples that are always hot topics of public discussion, while the other suits student thinking dimensions, elevating real-life phenomena to reflections on time, which is actually an evaluation of self-quality of life and life value. Overall, it is fairly easy to write, but achieving high scores is genuinely challenging. Although the difficulty in analyzing the question isn't high, the challenge lies in selecting the content and angle, with the key being novel intent. Most candidates will write about how they scientifically and effectively utilize their time. If candidates write about resolving conflicts within a specific timeframe or event, showcasing noble qualities and moral integrity in their writing, they can make their thoughts clear and return to more familiar and easier-to-write topics such as personal life experiences and experiences. Further enhancing language expression skills with simple yet profound writing, a great examination piece will appear before the examiner.
