Here is a collection of some interesting geographic coordinates. The method to use them is very simple: first, launch Google Earth, then select the "Fly to" option on the left side of the software. Paste the coordinates into the box below and press Enter to "fly" to this place.

Mount Everest (珠穆朗玛峰): 27.9782502279, 86.9221941736. Note: Mount Everest (Jo-mo glang-ma), abbreviated as Zhu Feng, also translated as Holy Mother Peak, is located in the Himalayan Mountains between China and Nepal, covered with snow all year round. It is the highest peak in the world by altitude.

World War II Bomber over England: 52°20′10.87″N 0°11′43.34″W. Note: A bomber that was captured by satellite during the Battle of Britain Memorial Flight display.

The World's Largest Abandoned Building: 39°02′11.11″N, 125°43′48.39″E. Note: The Ryugyong Hotel in Pyongyang, a product of the Cold War. Construction began in 1982 with an initial budget of $750 million, which was 2% of North Korea's total GDP. The Ryugyong Hotel building has a triangular pyramid design, with a slope angle of 75 degrees, standing 330 meters high and covering an area of 360,000 square meters. It was planned to have 3,000 rooms and 7 revolving restaurants. If completed, it would be the tallest hotel in the world and the seventh tallest building.
