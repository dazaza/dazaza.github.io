Three foreign young men shot video showing walking on water and sparked heated discussions (picture group)

by anonymous on 2012-02-25 09:38:47

A screenshot of Sebastian performing the "water gliding". In the video, the camera also gives a close-up of the running shoes they are wearing. According to a UK media report on the 16th, three foreign young men vividly demonstrated the light kung fu "water gliding" from Chinese martial arts novels in a video. In the video, after running on the surface of a lake in Portugal for about 50 meters, they finally fell into the water. They claimed that this was a new type of extreme sport challenging the theory of gravity. After the video was uploaded to YouTube, it attracted more than 2 million views in just two weeks. The video looked extremely credible, and even netizens who questioned whether it was a scam had to admit that it was ingeniously made.

Their feet moved like a high-speed sewing machine. Ordinary people would fall into the water after taking less than two or three steps on the water, but Ofer Gartner and his friends Sebastian Van der Wolf and Miguel Del Forte said that just like when people skip stones across the water, allowing them to bounce far over the water's surface, as long as the speed of running is fast enough and the frequency of lifting the feet is high enough, one can overcome gravity and run on the water. Ofer said: "We first sprinted along the lakeside at the fastest speed, and once our feet touched the water, we began to 'operate' like a high-speed sewing machine. After practicing for a while, we could take 9-10 steps on the water before falling into it."

The display of athletic wear caused doubts. This 3-minute video at first glance seemed as magical as the light kung fu "water gliding" in ancient costume dramas, and many netizens left comments believing it was true. Ofer also stated that "walking on water" was definitely not a miracle; the key was to find a pair of very good quality waterproof rubber running shoes. Some careful netizens noticed that the three young men in the video were wearing athletic clothing from the same brand, which was likely a new marketing gimmick by this sports brand. Others suspected that they had built a transparent plastic platform underwater beforehand, allowing them to run on the lake. A scientist even studied the shape of the splashes created by one person running on the lake, determining that it could only be caused by stepping on an underwater platform. He said: "When a person runs quickly, his quadriceps must rely on a platform to make concentric deceleration movements."

Despite various guesses from netizens, most people believed that the video was very realistic. It was difficult to find flaws, from the posture of the three running on the water to their proposed fresh theory. Some said, "(The video) if it is a web scam, it is the most ingenious one on YouTube recently." Information Times
