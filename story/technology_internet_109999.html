Anxi County is located at the junction of the Xiamen (Men), Zhangzhou and Quanzhou (Zhou) golden triangle in Fujian Province, known as the hometown of China's Oolong tea (famous tea). It has 500,000 mu (about 33,333 hectares) of tea gardens, producing 50,000 tons of tea annually, with a total tea-related output value of 5 billion yuan. Anxi Tieguanyin is famous both at home and abroad, enjoying a good reputation across five continents. In 2004, it obtained the certification for geographical indication product protection, and in 2005, it was awarded the title of China's well-known trademark.

The Zhentian Tea Cooperative, established in 2004, is located in Changkeng Township, Anxi County. In Zhentian Village, there are 116 tea-growing households and 14 tea merchants who have joined the cooperative, accounting for 91% of the total number of households. Among the members of the Zhentian Tea Cooperative, 88 people have obtained national vocational skill qualification certificates, including 2 senior technicians, 8 technicians, 50 senior workers, 12 intermediates, and 16 beginners. The entire village of Zhentian has 392 mu of arable land, 3709 mu of mountainous areas, and 1008 mu of tea gardens. In 2006, the per capita income of farmers was 5154 yuan, of which more than 3100 yuan came from the tea industry.

Since its establishment, the Zhentian Tea Cooperative has achieved significant benefits through measures such as hiring experts and professors as consultants; establishing the first farmer research institution in the country - "Anxi County Zhentian Tea Farmer Tea Research Institute" to serve tea farmers; organizing technical training and exchanges, implementing the "five unifications" method of tea garden management; and setting up 10 direct-sales websites for tea farmers to conduct online transactions directly. First, the quality and safety level of tea has significantly improved, with all tea produced by members fully complying with national tea hygiene and quality safety standards. Second, the cost of tea garden management has significantly decreased, saving the cooperative 100,500 yuan in costs in 2006. Third, tea-making skills have significantly improved, increasing the average selling price of tea in 2006 by 30 yuan per kilogram, representing a 25% increase compared to the same period in 2005. Fourth, the collaborative awareness of members has increased, with the shared-interests and shared-risks mechanism prompting a fundamental shift in the overall awareness of all cooperative members. Fifth, 10 direct-sales counters for cooperative tea products have been established in major and medium-sized cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, Chongqing, Xi'an, Hainan, Fuzhou, and Quanzhou, with annual sales reaching 33 million yuan.
