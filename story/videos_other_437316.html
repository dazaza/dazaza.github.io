In the Wang Junbao murder case, the victim's partial organs were lost, and the government claimed that they had autolyzed.

by iface009 on 2011-05-26 10:59:22

On May 11, the joint investigation team led by the Political and Legal Affairs Commission of the Yuzhou Municipal Committee announced the investigation results to the victim's family regarding this matter, stating: "The possibility of organ theft does not exist. The disappearance of the organs could be due to autolysis, or natural dissolution." In response, the victim's family did not accept this conclusion and raised questions.

On May 11 this year, Feng Haizhang and the deceased's parents received a notice from the Political and Legal Affairs Commission of the Yuzhou Municipal Committee and attended the investigation result announcement meeting at the Xiadu Subdistrict Office in Yuzhou City.

In 2007 and 2009, during the first and second trials, the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang City and the Higher People's Court of Henan Province both determined that Wang Junbao had used extremely brutal methods to murder his wife, resulting in severe consequences, sentencing him to death. In March 2010, after the Supreme People's Court made a decision not to approve the death sentence, the Higher People's Court of Henan Province overturned Wang Junbao's criminal verdict and sent it back to the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang City for retrial.

Due to being unable to meet with members of the joint investigation team, reporters from Dongfang Jinbao were unable to obtain the detailed investigation report of this case.

Under media attention, Jinjin was called the modern version of "Tiying saving her father." However, the Feng family believed that, "They are using a child without independent thinking ability to evoke sympathy, which is a farce." Nevertheless, the Supreme People's Court not approving the death sentence handed down by the Higher People's Court of Henan Province might also be the desired outcome for Wang Junbao's side.

Feng Xiumin is the victim in the highly publicized "Wang Junbao murder case." This case, which took place in Xuchang Yuzhou, gained extensive media coverage last year when Jinjin (a pseudonym), Wang Junbao and Feng Xiumin's daughter, appealed to the Supreme People's Court for clemency, asking them to "spare her father."

At the meeting, the joint investigation team presented their findings: "After the investigation, there are two conclusions: one, the possibility of organ theft does not exist. The investigation team looked into many circumstances at the time, including the management of the funeral home, ruling out the possibility of theft; two, regarding the disappearance of the organs, the expert panel's opinion is that it is related to autolysis, with the possibility of autolysis existing."

On March 12, 2007, at around 6 p.m., in the Yimin Chinese-Western Medicine Clinic in Shentong Town, Yuzhou City, Xuchang City, 36-year-old rural doctor Wang Junbao used a hammer to kill his 31-year-old wife, Feng Xiumin, who he had been together with for nearly ten years, in their own clinic.

The joint investigation team further explained: "Through an in-depth investigation of the management of the funeral home, the storage conditions were found to have no problems, the doors and windows showed no signs of damage, and the camera recordings revealed no issues. Since no clues or traces of theft were found, we formed an expert panel for analysis, whose opinion was that organs are easily subject to autolysis due to the longer storage time." When introducing the situation, the joint investigation team said they couldn't invite local experts, which wasn't a money issue but rather that they didn't handle such business. Therefore, they formed an expert panel consisting of seven forensic doctors from the Yuzhou court, public security bureau, procuratorate, and retired forensic doctors.

On May 15, Feng Xiumin's uncle Feng Haizhang believed: "The disappearance of the heart and liver was discovered during the third autopsy last August. Whether there were any poisoning signs before the victim's death is very crucial for judicial authorities to determine if Wang Junbao's killing was premeditated."

On May 16, reporters from Dongfang Jinbao went to Yuzhou for interviews. Deputy Secretary Zhang of the Political and Legal Affairs Commission of the Yuzhou Municipal Committee informed Dongfang Jinbao through Deputy Minister Hua of the Publicity Department of the Yuzhou Municipal Committee that the investigation situation had been transferred to the Yuzhou Procuratorate, and they should inquire about specific details from the procuratorate. Prosecutor Yan of the Yuzhou Procuratorate then stated, "I am not in Yuzhou. For more information, please consult Deputy Secretary Zhang of the Political and Legal Affairs Commission."

■ News Attachment

Around March 2010, the Supreme People's Court made a decision not to approve the death sentence, believing that some facts in the original judgment were unclear, and remanded the case back to the original trial court for retrial. Subsequently, the Higher People's Court of Henan Province sent the case back to the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang City for retrial.

On April 23, 2010, the Wang Junbao murder case was retried in the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang City, where Wang Junbao and his defense lawyer both insisted it was a "crime of passion." The victim's family and their legal representative sought further proof that Wang Junbao's murder was premeditated, thus submitting an application to the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang City for another examination of the body's toxicity, which was accepted by the court.

Feng Haizhang expressed: "Their investigative conclusions left me with the impression that they were ambiguous, full of possibilities without certainty." Therefore, he requested an official investigation and demanded that "people with unclear connections to this case should not participate in the investigation." This was because he and the victim's relatives firmly believed that the victim's death was a premeditated murder.

Lawyer Shen Teng, when interviewed by the media, mentioned that the innocence of a child really influenced national law. He stated that the lighter sentence upon retrial reflected the humanitarian concern of the law: "Since the father killed the mother, the law shouldn't kill the father, artificially making a child an orphan. The girl's plea definitely moved the judge, playing a certain role in the case review, but not a decisive one."

Political and Legal Organs Investigation Team: "Possibly Natural Dissolution"

On December 27, 2007, the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang sentenced Wang Junbao guilty of intentional homicide, sentencing him to death.

He told reporters from Dongfang Jinbao that everyone's heart is as big as a fist and is muscle tissue, while the kidneys are excretory tissue. If something were to dissolve, the kidneys should dissolve more easily than the heart, yet the reality is that the kidneys are still present, while the heart and liver have "autolyzed" cleanly.

Feng Haizhang immediately represented the victim's family on-site, refusing and rejecting this conclusion. He said, "Anyone with basic scientific knowledge can understand why, under the same environment in the victim's body cavity and under the same frozen condition, all other organs of the victim's body, including the lungs, intestines, and kidneys, are present, except for the heart and liver?"

【Incident】The Heart and Liver of the Victim's Body Go Missing

Uncle Feng Hai Zhang of Feng Xiu Min stated, "Previously, the judicial organs conducted two autopsies, and we were present. Both times, the heart and liver were present. Why did they suddenly disappear?" Soon after, he orally and in writing requested relevant agencies, including the Political and Legal Affairs Committee of Xuchang City, to investigate this incident.

On August 19, 2010, two forensic doctors and a physiological pathology expert dispatched by the Intermediate People's Court of Xuchang City, along with the victim's family, went to the Yuzhou Funeral Home to excavate and examine samples. After the victim's body was thawed and the chest and abdominal cavities opened, the forensic doctors discovered the disappearance of the heart and liver organs. Out of necessity, experts extracted some bodily fluids and kidney samples, re-sewing the body.

Feng Haizhang said, "A relative of Wang Junbao borrowed tens of thousands of yuan from Wang Junbao's family for investment and never returned it. The victim's family knew that some officials were secretly manipulating this matter; now, the most valuable organs for toxicological identification, the liver and heart, have mysteriously disappeared; people who had certain interests with the defendant and his relatives during the incident have also entered this joint investigation team, etc., all allowing the victim's family to suspect: someone fears 'pulling out radishes and bringing out mud,' thereby secretly abusing power."

According to understanding, the victim's family has already appealed against the civil judgment.

Feng Haizhang claimed that the originally scheduled morning meeting was postponed to the afternoon by the Political and Legal Affairs Commission. Originally notified participants, including the village secretary, village director, and personnel from the Comprehensive Governance Office of the village where the incident occurred, were temporarily instructed not to attend the meeting, changing the "information feedback meeting" into an "internal business meeting."

The widely followed "Wang Junbao murder case" continues to escalate: the body of the victim, Feng Xiumin, stored at the funeral home, saw the mysterious disappearance of the heart and liver.

When the Higher People's Court of Henan Province submitted the case for capital punishment review to the Supreme People's Court just a month later, Wang Junbao's sister Wang Shujun and others found Shen Teng, legal advisor and board member of Beijing Taoyang Village. Shen Teng provided free representation in this case, becoming Wang Junbao's agent during the capital punishment review stage. Following this, Wang Junbao's 10-year-old daughter, Jinjin, began writing letters of appeal to the Supreme People's Court, requesting the Supreme People's Court to "spare her father."

Regarding this, the joint investigation team stated that they were not experts and could not answer this question. However, the victim's family demanded that they provide written conclusions of the investigation, which the joint investigation team was unwilling to do.
