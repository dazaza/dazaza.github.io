Kaspersky KAV/KIS V8.0: A Sneak Preview

by xloong on 2007-10-23 07:35:43

The Kaspersky 8 testing timeline is similar to previous reports:

Alpha version release in August

Optimized Beta1 version release in October

Conservative Beta1 version release in December

Below is a partial translation of the Kafan Forum (describing some new features of Kaspersky), with preview images at the end.

========= 11.10 =========== 【Thanks to zerosu6652 for the translation】

As part of the latest information section, we have released screenshots about different types of detection targets. As you know from V6/7, there are only three confirmation dialog boxes, two of which can be customized. In V8, we decided to provide more granular (manual?) control over detection targets, including a new detection technology for discovering suspicious multi-layer compressed files. We are confident that you will help us correct and adjust these new technologies during the forum testing period. They will all be added to the "white" list and "black" list and updated along with the virus database.

========= 15.09 =========== [Thanks to YU88480 for the translation]

1. During the preparation of the Kaspersky 8 product, we selected and analyzed many competitive products from other sources available on the market, including firewalls, security suites, system HIPS solutions, etc.

2. The product will support IPv6, initially planned to appear in KAV 7.0 MP1, but it will be further improved and enhanced in the 8.0 product.

3. At this point, we can definitely say that the technical cooperation between KL's team and other companies is full of miraculous wisdom, and the development of the final product will be confirmed.

4. Several tabs in the main window of the product will be styled as a "task" bar, allowing us to quickly click and operate. For example: creating a rescue disk, arranging spam components.

5. IDS component, phishing and anti-dialer will be combined together, but how, there are various speculations.

6. The anti-popup component will be removed as browsers currently do a good job in this aspect and handle it more meticulously.

7. The virtual keyboard function is most likely to be restored, and of course, it will also include protection against screenshot capture during operation.

8. The underlying driver Klif file in all supported operating systems will be replaced with a new version, supporting Vista and 64-bit operating systems.

9. The stealth data protection component will become a new subsystem, combining with other components like PDM, firewall, etc.

10. It will be possible to see some additional extended information within the range of all IP registration addresses used (rules, alerts). For example: the geographical location of computers with special IP addresses.

11. In several cases, such as when the check wizard (task manager not open, network not working, etc.) appears, the product will display several dialog boxes.

12. There will be no separate dialog box during product updates.

13. The method of controlling all project controls through external buttons will be replaced by a more convenient editing solution.

========= 08.09 =========== 【This translation thanks to YU88480】

The content translated on September 8th may not be perfect; please bear with me. I am translating directly, and some specialized terms are still unclear to me, so I will revise them once I understand them better.

1) One of the employees in the forum has already started monitoring and performing his duties. I must honestly say he might not be well-known in the forum, but he has been paying attention to this for a while.

2) In the product, we will use an additional technology to detect malicious programs, which will undoubtedly bring fierce discussions on the forum.

3) Support for email client plugins such as Eudora and Thunderbird is already in the development plan.

4) The warning messages in the product will become more informative, probably simple warnings that general users will encounter, without confusing information.

5) Several key component settings may be configured to switch from the main window, without any component parts form. More context menus will be set up like in versions 6.0 and 7.0.

6) The default settings of the product will likely pass all leak tests. Additionally, in October, we will publish two articles reflecting our test results in leak tests and the performance of KIS7.

7) The anti-spam subsystem, anti-advertising, and parental control will be combined into a brand-new component... Which one? Everyone has ideas. (Hehe)

8) The concept of reporting will slightly change. Now, we have completely rewritten a data storage layer to support new reports caused by additional loads on the product.

9) The product interface will flexibly unfold previews and several control lists. We are developing our own unique method to control components, replacing all current product list controls on the market. It will allow sorting, grouping, and filtering data.

10) Arranging classifications in bar charts will likely complete automatic combinations according to meaning. Two-arrow groupings will decrease (Today, Yesterday, Two days ago, A week ago, Two weeks ago).

11) It will be possible to automatically send detected target program information to KL’s central database. This database will enable us to better protect users when encountering real dangers and even quickly notify them about epidemics. This option will have a toggle switch, but no confidential personal data will be sent.

12) In the "Update" section, users can see the number of virus database updates, fault types, and make custom time changes.

13) Several key decisions delayed the testing specifically launched for the forum. Before the product release, we want to hear the most information: about any reasonable suggestions and arguments, favoring one way or another.

========= 31.08 ===========

Here are some new messages and answers to questions asked last week:

1) v8 will use a completely new antivirus core engine, offering some advantages compared to the current engine.

2) Due to the use of a new engine, the virus database format will be completely changed.

3) Currently, the operating systems we intend to support are: Windows 2k SP4+, Windows XP SP2+ 32 bit / 64 bit, Windows Vista SP0+ 32 bit / 64 bit.

4) We are currently considering the "mini interface" and support for Vista widgets, which will be developed depending on time constraints if public feedback supports it.

5) v8 will include two new protective drivers.

6) The firewall and active defense subsystems will become new protection modules.

7) The "Protection Scope" category will be fully updated, allowing users finer control over malware scanning targets.

8) A global "Scan Task" for system vulnerability scanning will be added.

9) We may include a packet sniffer in the test version, and its inclusion in the final version will depend on public feedback.

10) The anti-spam subsystem will incorporate some new technologies already in use on email servers, which may be reflected earlier in 7.0 MP1.

11) The v8 forum test version should be released in October.

========= 24.08 ===========

1) The v8 product series will be released in 2008. The public beta version will be released in winter, and the forum test version in autumn.

2) v8 uses a completely new protection concept and technology, and as far as we know, there are no similar products on the market currently.

3) We have established requirements and standards for the product and are now in the development stage.

4) The technical team remains the same as the 6.0 version developers, but some new members from the 7.0 version have joined.

5) Some improvements in v8 will be included in v7 MP1, to be released at the end of the year.

6) During the v8 testing period, we will pay special attention to posts on the forum, although all Kaspersky Lab personnel occasionally monitor the forum, we will assign one person to read all suggestions, bug reports, etc., posted on the forum.

7) v8 will include some new technologies developed in collaboration between Kaspersky Lab and other companies.

8) The new interface is completely re-designed, using only a few parts from v6/7.

9) The main window will be entirely new, unlike v7.

10) The main window currently has six components.

11) We plan to display dynamic data in the product interface using icons and widgets, along with some static data.

12) The product will use some online services.

13) Wireless internet protection will be more comprehensive than in previous versions.

14) One of the product options will conduct comprehensive monitoring through predefined checkpoints to find the most details.
