How much harm does testicular hydrocele cause to men?

If male friends find that they have developed testicular hydrocele, they should take this disease seriously and seek timely diagnosis and treatment, as the harm caused by this condition can be very significant. So, what are the hazards of testicular hydrocele? Let's learn more through the following information.

Under normal circumstances, there is a small amount of serous fluid in the testicular tunica vaginalis cavity, which is very similar in nature to the serous fluid within the abdominal cavity of the body, providing lubrication and allowing the male testicles to move freely within it. If lesions occur in the tunica vaginalis or surrounding organs or tissues, it may easily lead to testicular hydrocele. The greatest hazard of testicular hydrocele is that it can cause male infertility. Experts from the Guangzhou Air Force Logistics Hospital Infertility Department explain that with the presence of hydrocele, long-term retention of fluid within the tunica vaginalis increases internal pressure, leading to testicular ischemia, poor spermatogenic function in the testicles, and directly causing infertility. At the same time, large amounts of hydrocele in adults can affect normal sexual life and also lead to infertility.

In addition to causing infertility, testicular hydrocele can bring other harms:

1. Affecting sexual life: If hydrocele cannot be treated in time and continues to grow larger, the penis will be surrounded by scrotal skin, which is not conducive to normal sexual activity.

2. Causing discomfort symptoms: Patients with a large amount of hydrocele may experience feelings such as scrotal ptosis, swelling, and spermatic cord traction pain. When hydrocele becomes massive, the penis retracts into the prepuce, interfering with urination and sexual life, making walking and labor inconvenient.

3. Leading to testicular inflammation: Hydrocele can trigger diseases such as orchitis.
