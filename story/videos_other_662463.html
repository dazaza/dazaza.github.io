Experts: Probably Short-Tailed Pit Viper

Dr. Hu Huijian of the Southern China Institute of Endangered Animals estimated that these snakes were likely short-tailed pit vipers gathering for mating. Under normal circumstances, a place appearing with a large number of snakes simultaneously can be attributed to three main reasons: one is that the place is a snake habitat area, now the weather gets warmer, hibernating snakes originally started activities; two is the result of recent rain, rainy weather is relatively low, the snake in the underground is more boring, so rain snakes will go to the ground; three is now various animal mating seasons, a large number of snakes appearing in the same area, it is possible to reproduce.

Dr. Hu Huijian thinks such a large-scale emergence of snakes is a natural phenomenon, may be the largest local snake inhabiting region and mating with other occasions, such as geological disaster, earthquake precursor, never mind. It is expected that after these snakes finish mating, they will return to their respective living areas.

At nine fifteen last night, Dr. Hu Huijian identified according to the picture provided by the reporter that the hundreds of snakes in Kwun Tong Village were short-tailed pit vipers, specific species remain to be further identification.
