Note: Li Chengpeng's weibo address: http://t.sina.com.cn/lichengpeng

Li Chengpeng, a well-known domestic football reporter and commentator, posted a microblog on Sina Weibo at noon today, saying that he wanted to be a people's representative and would therefore open a weibo election. He promised that if he became a people's representative, he would disclose his personal property, the origin of each sum of money, and tax payment certificates. He would speak the truth for the common people and make useful proposals without speaking nonsense or flattering corrupt officials. (In addition, please tell Pan Shiyi how to become a people's representative procedurally). He just wanted to do an experiment to prove that China indeed has democratically elected representatives of the people, and ask Pan Shiyi about the procedures to become a people's representative. By the time of the publication deadline (14:30), the microblog had been forwarded 4633 times and received 5291 comments. In response to this move, netizens expressed their support and offered advice to Li Chengpeng, including some celebrities:

"Unfortunately, I am not in your constituency. But I suggest you first organize a network of volunteer campaign teams, including lawyers, to work with the voters in your constituency, especially the civil servants in the constituency, and openly solicit their votes."

"I vote for you. I suggest the first proposal is to form a netizen delegation, truly elected by the people."
