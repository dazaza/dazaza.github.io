A three-generation family encounters a traffic accident on their way home to attend a funeral, causing 2 deaths and 1 injury.

by zsh0827817 on 2012-03-01 13:28:43

Our staff reporter (Liu Wei) - An elderly woman's husband came to Beijing to visit relatives when he suddenly fell ill and passed away. After dealing with her husband's affairs, Grandma Wang, along with her daughter-in-law and grandson, took a taxi to Capital Airport to catch a flight back home. However, on the way, they encountered a car accident. Grandma Wang and her daughter-in-law died after unsuccessful rescue attempts, and her grandson was injured. Yesterday, it was reported that Grandma Wang's family had filed a lawsuit against the taxi driver and his affiliated company in Shunyi Court, demanding compensation of over 860,000 yuan.

It is understood that Grandma Wang and her husband, Mr. Zhang, were both from Guizhou province. Last November, the couple came to Beijing for a family visit, but unexpectedly, Mr. Zhang suddenly fell ill and passed away in Beijing. Subsequently, Grandma Wang's children and relatives rushed from other places to handle the funeral arrangements. After finishing the procedures, the relatives gradually left Beijing.

According to Mr. Zhang, Grandma Wang's son, on the afternoon of November 25th last year, Grandma Wang, along with her daughter-in-law and grandson, also prepared to return to Guizhou. The three took a taxi to Capital Airport to catch their flight. On the road, at the 22nd kilometer mark of the Jingping Expressway in Shunyi District, the taxi they were riding in collided with the rear of a tanker truck, causing serious injuries to all three passengers. Afterwards, Grandma Wang and her daughter-in-law respectively succumbed to organ rupture bleeding and severe brain injury due to ineffective rescue efforts. Grandma Wang's grandson was saved after emergency treatment and escaped life-threatening danger.

Mr. Zhang and other family members believe that the taxi driver Guan, who belongs to the taxi company, should bear responsibility for damages caused by passenger casualties during transportation. Guan drove excessively fast and failed to ensure safe driving, and thus should also bear corresponding responsibilities. They sued for compensation for funeral expenses, death compensation, and other costs totaling over 860,000 yuan.

The taxi company argued that in this traffic accident, the tanker truck driver Liu drove without a license, overloaded the vehicle, did not follow traffic signals, and fled to a gas station to hide after the incident. He should bear the main responsibility for the accident. Based on the transport contract, the company agreed to provide some compensation but believed that ultimately, they should seek reimbursement from the actual perpetrator.

Recently, under the support of his parents, Guan, the taxi driver, arrived at the court with crutches to collect litigation materials. Guan stated that this car accident caused his foot to suffer a comminuted fracture, making him unable to step on the ground and walk normally alone. He admitted that he was slightly speeding at the time and deeply regretted the occurrence of the accident. However, he claimed that due to his poor economic situation, the compensation should be borne by the taxi company, and there would be insurance claims.

Shunyi Court will schedule a hearing for this case on another day. (Editor: SN021)
