Windows XP becomes a collection of the National Museum of China

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:48:42

Yongli Zhang, Vice President of Microsoft Greater China Region, received the "Certificate of Collection".

Even today, Windows XP, released in 2001, still holds a significant share in the global operating system market. However, it is obvious that Microsoft has now made up its mind to retire this product from history. For this purpose, a symbolic "retirement ceremony" was staged yesterday in Beijing.

According to media reports, Microsoft and Intel held a donation ceremony for "Legendary Curtain Call, Classics Eternal" at the National Museum of China, donating the longest-serving computer operating system in human history - Windows XP - to be permanently collected by the National Museum.

Regarding the soon-to-be-historical Windows XP, Yongli Zhang, Vice President of Microsoft Greater China Region and General Manager of Consumer Channels Division, stated: "We see that the future development trend of computers is touch, mobility, and applications, but Windows XP can no longer meet modern users' needs in these three areas. The day Windows XP stops service is approaching, and we sincerely remind users to consider upgrading their operating systems as soon as possible from the perspectives of stability and security."

According to the previously announced schedule, Microsoft will officially discontinue service support for both Windows XP and Office 2003 on April 8, 2014. This means that after this date, users will no longer receive updates regarding functionality or security from the official source.

Currently, Microsoft's latest Windows 8.1 operating system has been officially released for download. The new system incorporates touch and gesture operation elements, making significant adjustments to the user UI, and the system itself boasts enhanced security and stability.
