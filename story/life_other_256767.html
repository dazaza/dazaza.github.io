by gaocan12345 on 2009-11-06 14:30:35

In fact, my mood for writing this article is as direct and contradictory as the title. I have been to many cities, but it seems that my steps have never stepped out of the southern land. Therefore, I have no concept of northern cities. Below, I will only share my impressions of Xiamen from the cities I have been to. It is purely an emotional expression. If there are any inappropriate expressions, please do not mind.

Xiamen is really a very beautiful island city. I am an outsider of Xiamen. Although I also belong to Fujian people, I have always liked to call Fuzhou, Quanzhou, and Xiamen in Fujian as first-tier cities in the province, and others belong to second-tier cities. I come from the latter second-tier city. In these years I have been in Xiamen, I also deal with friends in my concept of second-tier cities, and I found that we all have a commonality: that is, we have a particularly high sense of identity with Xiamen. Fuzhou, except for being the provincial capital, has a very average impression, and it is a bit exclusive. Although the city has been transformed quickly in recent years, in my impression, it seems that only friends from Nanping and Ningde like Fuzhou more, while others tend to prefer Xiamen. My impression of Quanzhou is economic strength, a place where tigers lie low and dragons hide. There are many family businesses and a large number of labor forces. I think Quanzhou is the city that best represents Minnan culture, but at the same time, this city seems to be really more suitable for Minnan people to live in. So my feelings towards Xiamen can be said to be special favoritism, and my thinking is also very narrow-minded. This sense of intimacy is limited to the island, but this is an undeniable fact. Now when people talk about Xiamen, they generally refer to the island. But it does not mean to exclude the areas outside the island. When I worked elsewhere before, I found that many people from other provinces have a particularly good impression of Xiamen. Some even thought it was the provincial capital of Fujian. I frankly admit that I have been to all prefecture-level cities in Fujian, and I feel that except for my hometown which is always ranked first in my heart, the second is Xiamen. This is also the reason that attracted me to give up a good job elsewhere and start again from scratch in Fujian.

Before I knew it, I have lived in Xiamen for nearly three years, so when I calm down and think about how I spent these three years, I suddenly feel really scared. I actually don't have too many memories, nor do I have the youthful passion that young people should have. Perhaps I shouldn't blame Xiamen's life for being too comfortable, comfortable enough to make people feel like they are retiring.

First of all, the weather in Xiamen makes people feel very comfortable, and the four seasons do not change much. You rarely see rain or so-called typhoons. Such comfort makes time fly through the four seasons. Only when the Spring Festival is approaching will people realize that the year is almost over. Here, I mention Guilin where I used to stay. Many people like Guilin, but I really don't like this city. Only those who live in this city can deeply understand the climate changes in Guilin, especially in winter. Sometimes in winter, Guilin can be cold down to a few degrees below zero, and the rain is particularly heavy. Many people from the north say that Guilin's winter is really colder than the north. The wind is bone-chilling and lasts for a long time.

Secondly, the transportation in Xiamen is really convenient. I have been to many cities, but none of them have better transportation conditions than Xiamen. There are few motorcycles and bicycles inside Xiamen Island. Every time I look down from a high-rise building, the roads are filled with neatly arranged cars, giving a strong feeling of a big city. Also, Xiamen's bus and BRT routes are well-developed. Compared with other similar cities, they are not crowded at all. It is very convenient to go anywhere. If you are really in a hurry, you can easily hail a taxi with just a wave of your hand, and the cost is not too high. I take taxis quite frequently. From my personal experience, if you take a taxi in Xiamen and the meter exceeds 25 yuan, it is really considered a long distance. I can take a taxi from Jiangtou to Pearl Bay because Xiamen is too small. Even if you take a taxi around the island, it will cost about 100 yuan, which is unlike Shanghai. Every time I go to Shanghai, I have the feeling that if you want to play happily with friends after ten o'clock at night, it is basically difficult to take a bus or subway home. You must take a taxi home. Shanghai is really too big. It often costs more than 100 yuan to take a taxi from one district to another. Finally, I sigh that taking a taxi in Shanghai is really cheap if it is within 60 yuan. Each time I go to Shanghai, I spend hundreds of yuan on taxi fares alone because taking a bus in Shanghai is really troublesome and slow. Perhaps only the subway is the most convenient.

Furthermore, shopping in Xiamen is also very convenient. Don't underestimate Xiamen, although it is small and only counts as a second-tier city domestically, when it comes to supermarkets, it makes outsiders sweat. There are three Walmarts, a dense ratio absolutely rare nationwide (not referring to quantity), Carrefour, Metro, Tesco (international chain), RT-Mart, Lianhua, Auchan, Renrenle (domestic large chain), plus local Xinhuadu and Yonghui, all typical KA formats of big cities appear in such a small island city. In addition, 24-hour convenience stores are also a symbol of high urbanization. Just among the ones that have formed a scale, there are Yueshi, 88 Chain, Beishun, Sanba Wang, etc., which again reminds me of Liuzhou where I once stayed. It is also considered the second largest city in Guangxi with not a low GDP, but Liuzhou has been monopolized by two large supermarkets, both domestic chains, one is Shanghai Lianhua (more than 60 branches) and the other is Shenzhen Nancheng. You can't imagine that for a city with a population of more than 3.5 million and an area ten times larger than Xiamen, its residents have been monopolized by these two large malls for many years until now. It's like Walmart still can't enter the Guangzhou market.

Moreover, the urban sanitation and greening facilities in Xiamen are really well done. When I often walk around Gulangyu Island, I frequently hear tourists from other places sincerely praise the beauty of Xiamen. I even feel proud, although I am not from Xiamen, but talking about it really makes me proud of Xiamen. Living in this city is really relaxing. If you are in a bad mood, you can walk along the beach of Pearl Bay, stroll on Gulangyu Island, or have a cup of coffee at Xi Ti, which is very bourgeois. However, you cannot relax in other cities. It's not that the conditions in other cities are bad, but it's hard to find such leisure time. When I went to Hong Kong, although Hong Kong people have a material standard that can be considered bourgeois, everyone generally lives under an invisible pressure. Only at seven or eight o'clock in the evening can some white-collar workers enjoy a moment of peace in Lan Kwai Fong. Shenzhen, like Xiamen, is an immigrant city, but it is also a highly commercialized city. Guangzhou, some say it is very relaxed, but I feel its morning tea culture is like the tea culture of Minnan, more belonging to the lifestyle of locals. Zhuhai and Xiamen have many similarities, but people who have been there can compare and find out that Xiamen is better, a kind of intrinsic goodness, a beauty refined to perfection. Actually, I have been to all prefecture-level cities in Guangdong. My overall feeling is that the fighting spirit of Guangdong people is practical, which is really stronger than us Fujian people. They are also very good at living their lives, but it's hard for us to integrate into their lives and culture, which is the inclusiveness of a city. But Xiamen is different in this aspect.

If I were to say which city feels better than Xiamen, maybe Sanya in Hainan could compare, but considering the overall living environment, I would still choose Xiamen, because the infrastructure in Hainan is really too poor. Maybe someone would say Hangzhou and Suzhou are also nice, but I think if we consider the delicate life, they still fall short of Xiamen. Actually, these are all my personal experiences after comparison. Today, I suddenly had the urge to write, and I found that I really fell in love with the city of Xiamen. I just wanted to say whatever came to mind, and ended up being very verbose. You must think I don't know what I'm trying to express. Yes, I know it's a bit messy, but everything above is the most genuine words. Every detail is my feeling towards Xiamen. If there is anything I want to express as disappointment towards Xiamen, it's just like the title says, "It's not easy to say I love you." The housing prices in Xiamen are not designed for young entrepreneurs and workers; they are for wealthy people within the province and across the country to invest in retirement. The wages in Xiamen are not designed for young entrepreneurs and workers either; it is a consumption-oriented city. Its high consumption is paid for by someone else. More people are just wandering on the fringes of the city, enjoying the beauty and modernization of the city while consuming their youth, silently betting with their youth and unwilling to leave. Even if one day they discover they have nothing in this city, they have no regrets because Xiamen is the place where many people's dreams begin.

I am willing to share my impressions of Xiamen with everyone, and it can also be regarded as a summary of my time in Xiamen.
