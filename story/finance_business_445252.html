4. Give plenty of praise.

Parents' praise not only makes children happy but also strengthens the connection between the prefrontal cortex and the amygdala in the midbrain. For example, when an infant is learning to walk, if the parents give praise, the infant will be pleased, and as a result, the brain releases a neurochemical substance that reinforces the connection.

Children exploring everywhere and seeking new experiences help promote intelligence and understanding of the world. When a child lies in the crib, their little eyes often focus on various objects, investigating the new world. They have unlimited curiosity and satisfy their curiosity by touching, rubbing, and even putting things in their mouths. Parents should encourage their child’s curiosity and explore together. You might pick up a toy to take a close look at it and then pass it to the child for study. Or you can crawl on the floor with the child.

2. Listen to music.

Children learn more than just rhythm and melody from music. Researchers point out that teaching 3-year-old children piano or group singing significantly improves their spatial-temporal reasoning abilities. Three-year-olds who have taken piano or singing lessons are more accurate and faster at solving tangram puzzles compared to other children.
