A newly repaired MP3 was sent from Shenzhen through an express company to Tianjin. However, when the recipient, Mr. Shan, accepted the package, he found that the MP3 inside was missing. Was it that the sender did not send the goods, or did the express company have issues during delivery? With doubts, the reporter accompanied Mr. Shan to the Hongqiao station of STO Express to investigate.

Citizen's Account: Receiving an Empty Box

Tianjin citizen Mr. Shan purchased an MP3 a couple of years ago. Recently, he discovered that the battery often ran out of power, so he sent the MP3 to the after-sales department in Tianjin for inspection. They informed him that it was necessary to replace the built-in battery, which could only be done by sending the device to Shenzhen. Mr. Shan immediately sent the MP3 to the after-sales headquarters in Shenzhen. A few days ago, Mr. Shan received a call from Shenzhen informing him that the MP3 had been repaired and was sent via courier, asking him to be prepared for its arrival.

Two days later, Mr. Shan received a call from the courier company notifying him to collect the package. Unfortunately, he was outside at the time and couldn't return home. He negotiated with the staff to reschedule the delivery, which they readily agreed to. However, after waiting for several days without receiving any calls from the courier company, Mr. Shan contacted them. The courier company explained that they were busy recently and suggested that if convenient, Mr. Shan could pick up the package himself. After confirming the address, Mr. Shan went to the Hongqiao station of STO Express and found his courier package. Mr. Shan said that upon opening the courier packaging, he found nothing inside. The courier staff asked Mr. Shan to write "Package intact, opened and inspected in front of the receiver, contents empty," saying they would contact the headquarters.

Journalist Investigation: Obvious Signs of Secondary Sealing

Subsequently, Mr. Shan contacted the sender. The sender mentioned that when sending the courier, the company specifically applied a special type of tape on the seal. If this tape was damaged, it was likely caused by someone from the courier company. Upon immediate inspection, it was found that all the seals on the package were indeed sealed with a special tape with printed text. However, at the top seal, this tape had been torn off and resealed with regular tape.

At the Hongqiao station of STO Express, the journalist saw Mr. Shan's courier package. On the package, there was a tape with the words "Thank you for your patronage, please sign after confirming the product is intact," sealing both the bottom and top. However, the tape at the top seal had been cut across, and a transparent tape was used for resealing. Both the staff at the Hongqiao station of STO Express and Mr. Shan stated that the package showed obvious signs of secondary sealing, and the MP3 inside was likely taken during transit.

STO Express: Contacting Headquarters for Swift Resolution

Mr. Wu, a responsible person at the Hongqiao station of STO Express, indicated that Mr. Shan's situation was the first they had encountered at their station. From Mr. Shan's description and the integrity of the packaging, the courier item had indeed been tampered with. Mr. Wu said that since the courier item had weight verification, according to regulations, if the weight was verified correctly, as a transfer station, no further confirmation would be made, and the item would be directly handed over to the customer.

Regarding how to handle the loss incident, Mr. Wu advised Mr. Shan to continue lodging complaints with the STO headquarters. Meanwhile, their station was also in contact with the headquarters, hoping to find a solution quickly to avoid consumer losses.
