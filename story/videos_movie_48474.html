Introduction:

In order to complete the great cause of unifying the three kingdoms that Liu Bei did not finish, Zhao Zilong at over 80 years old led Zhang Fei and Guan Yu's sons in starting the process of the northern campaign. The film time travels through a span of over 60 years, showing famous battles from the Three Kingdoms period such as the Battle of Chibi and the Battle of Changban through Zhao Zilong's memories. The ever-victorious General Zhao Zilong who excels in fighting on horseback made Andy Lau endure all kinds of hardships: "There are two big war scenes at the beginning and end of the film. Fighting is one thing, but being on horseback all day was simply unbearable for me. My feet were tied, my hands were rubbed raw, and my butt was even chafed raw by the saddle." Regarding the current situation where the film has similar themes to John Woo's "Red Cliff", Rowena Douglas stated there is no competition: "A Vietnamese friend of mine told me about a local saying, 'Many ships do not block the sea.' The Three Kingdoms era is too vast. Now, with only two films, it's far from enough." Director Rowena Douglas explained why he has a particular fondness for Zhao Yun: "Apart from his martial prowess, he is also a hero figure, which suits the commercial elements of the film. Zhao Yun's life is quite worth reflecting on. For the characters of the Three Kingdoms, death is not the most terrible thing. He watched as the Five Tiger Generals left one by one. The pressure he endured during this process was the hardest to get through." At over seventy years old, Zhao Yun came out of retirement to help Liu Bei crush Wei and Wu, but was trapped on Phoenix Cry Mountain, facing the ultimate battle of his life. Reflecting on past events under the setting sun, amidst the flashes of lightning and thunder, scenes of bloodshed and storms of war, along with moments of joy, laughter, sorrow, and worry among the troops, all appeared before the eyes of this old general.

In the chaotic times of the Three Kingdoms, with the Wei army advancing forcefully, the newly appointed engineer Zhao Yun met Luo Ping'an, a fellow townsman who liked to fight injustice. The two hit it off immediately, sharing their aspirations for the turbulent times. Under the strategy of attack as defense by Shu's military advisor Zhuge Liang, Zhao Yun volunteered to raid the enemy camp and achieved great success.
