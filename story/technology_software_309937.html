Top Ten Penalty Nightmares: Baggio and Palermo Lead the List, Arsenal's Duo Become a Laughingstock

Baggio on the Verge of Tears, Henry: What Are You Doing, Pires?

Sina Sports News - Penalties are often called the nightmare of star players, and the bigger the name, the more likely they are to miss. However, in the history of international football, some individuals have gained notoriety after missing penalties. Below is a list of the top ten penalty "fools" as selected by Britain's The Sun.

10. David Batty & Kevin Keegan – June 30, 1998

In the 1998 World Cup, England faced their long-time rival Argentina in an intense 120-minute match that went to penalties. It was Batty’s turn to take a crucial shot; if he missed, England would be eliminated. The TV commentator asked guest Kevin Keegan, “Will this go in?” The former England manager confidently replied, “Absolutely.” But just as he spoke, Batty missed the penalty, sending England home.

9. Pires & Henry – October 22, 2005

Arsenal's two Frenchmen, Pires and Henry, attempted a clever "one-two" trick from the penalty spot, but it turned into one of football's biggest jokes. With Arsenal leading 1-0 against Manchester City, Pires tried to pass the ball to Henry during the penalty, but he completely missed it. Henry ran from outside the box to the goalmouth only to watch helplessly as a City defender cleared the ball from the penalty spot. Afterwards, the embarrassed Henry joked that Pires' leg had "lost sensation" at that moment.

8. William – September 7, 2006

In a South American club cup match, Botafogo player William's penalty was so weak that even though the goalkeeper dived the wrong way, he still had enough time to move across the goal and save the shot. Botafogo was eventually eliminated in a penalty shootout after drawing 2-2 over two legs with Fluminense.

7. John Terry – May 22, 2008

What could make Chelsea captain John Terry replay a moment forty times a day? In the 2008 Champions League final between Chelsea and Manchester United, Terry slipped while taking a penalty and missed, effectively handing the trophy to United. This memory haunted the English hardman for a long time afterward.

6. Peter Devereux

This Lancaster team striker prepared to blast a powerful penalty kick but instead kicked the ground, failing to touch the ball. To add to the comedy, Devereux fell down and began screaming in pain while clutching his foot, all just inches away from the untouched ball.

5. Stuart Pearce – June 26, 1996

The summer of 1996 saw football return home to England, but Stuart Pearce sent it back to Germany with one fateful kick. The 26-year-old central defender became the scapegoat in the Euro 1996 semifinal against Germany, as England lost on penalties—a painfully familiar script.

4. Roberto Baggio – July 17, 1994

In the United States in 1994, the devout Buddhist led Italy to the final but ultimately fell at the last hurdle in a penalty shootout. The World Cup trophy, the highest honor in world football, was decided for the first time via penalties.

3. Diana Ross – June 17, 1994

Had Roberto Baggio seen American actress Diana Ross's performance at the opening ceremony, he might have foreseen his own penalty miss in the final. After singing, Ross playfully took a penalty kick which didn't go in but caused the goalposts to collapse dramatically. Americans always seem to have a knack for humor.

2. Stuart Pearce & Chris Waddle – July 4, 1990

In the Italian summer of 1990, Gascoigne wasn't the only tragic figure for England. In fact, Pearce and Waddle had far more reason to cry. After a 1-1 draw with Germany over 120 minutes, the semifinal went to penalties. Both teams scored in the first three rounds, but England's fourth taker, Pearce, hit the ball straight into German keeper Ilgner. Though Pearce's shot was on target, Waddle then proceeded to miss entirely, ending England's World Cup "adventure."

1. Martin Palermo – July 4, 1999

Is there anything worse than missing a penalty? How about missing three in one game. The Argentine forward described his "hat-trick" of missed penalties against Colombia as a "death sentence." After seeing two penalties hit the crossbar, Palermo must have thought his luck couldn’t get any worse—but his third attempt also failed, with Argentina trailing 0-3 at that point.
