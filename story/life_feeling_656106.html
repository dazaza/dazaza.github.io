Strangers in the beginning, strangers in the end.

by mimi456 on 2012-01-06 21:31:41

At first, we were strangers; in the end, we were unrecognizable.

The winter hues are solemn, the cold water ripples with waves. One day, the springtime fades like a dream, and all the splendor turns to emptiness. We drift further and further apart, our worries growing fainter. Who extinguished the candle of longing?

In the tumultuous winds and rains, through many phases of the moon's waxing and waning, who has forgotten the smiles of yesteryear, sighing at the passage of time?

Yellow leaves scatter, solitary shadows linger, spreading fragments of sorrow on the ground. At this moment, the music lingers with the vows of never parting that once resounded.

In lonely days, the soul cannot rest peacefully. In a daze, who whispers softly by the ear, singing melancholy tunes that pierce the heart? Sadness clings like a shadow.

Time and again, dust is stirred up, but the initial enchantment can no longer be seen! Clinging to the threads of love, stubbornly attempting to move forward, yet silently reappearing in the depths of the heart, only to vanish into silence.

Through countless nights, gazing at the flickering candlelight, the joys and sorrows of the past have become the sprouting grass and chirping birds. Let the boat of memories drift over the stream and bridge. Looking back, how many opportunities in life have we truly possessed?

Whose face grows increasingly blurred, appearing and disappearing faintly? Suddenly realizing, if missed, it will ultimately just be a memory! When desolation reaches the heart, wanting to withdraw is already futile. How many people are truly worth remembering for a lifetime?

Once thought, those feelings that stir the heart are as elegant as flowers blooming on branches, singing in the spring breeze, never to fade. But they couldn't withstand the flow of time, where every moment tells the truth about oneself.

Always believed that after loving, one could never let go, and after pain, one would feel no more pain, leaving behind only a cold heart. Thought it was already calm and serene, simple and composed. Yet, some things, once turned away from, last a lifetime.

A place of solitude, two places of silent contemplation, separated by vast spans of time. Sighing, years pass unnoticed, things remain the same but people change. When tears fall, one realizes that parting is another form of understanding.

One day, you'll grow tired of the person you love. All their virtues will turn into flaws. What matters is not what was said, but the deadening silence that keeps the wounds of nightly loneliness from healing even temporarily.

Looking back, it takes five hundred lifetimes to gather and disperse. A deep gaze at the mountains and rivers confirms the finality of becoming strangers. Sitting at the ferry of life, looking afar, not for romance, but to honor the dust-bound connections.

On the winding paths, the mountains are barren, the waters thin, shadows cross askew. In drunken dreams, flowers bloom and fall, raising a misty rain. Turning around in sadness, still waiting beside, where has the splendid other shore gone?

What night is this? Waiting too long inevitably leads to despair. Without you here, without me there, the flowers have fallen, and we've passed each other on the road. Is the world truly darkened? Why does the sun, blue sky, and white clouds still look the same?

In memory, that warm smile, that comforting self. Is happiness always so fleeting?

Natural or unnatural, is it because it's fleeting that it becomes so deeply etched?

Confused or clear-headed, is it because it's deeply etched that one must carry sorrow?

Light or heavy, some sacrifices in life are impossible to make!

Drifting or sinking, destined to grow old while saying goodbye to each other! If explanations are unclear, why force them? Whoever gazes from afar with compassionate eyes watches the souls struggling in the mortal world!

If life doesn't get entangled anymore, all past events will dissipate like smoke. Overthinking only harms oneself, better not to speak, just to walk silently by. On the orchid boat, who plays a tune of parting sorrow?

Walking through time, through space, through so much, beyond the horizon, who gives their pure affection with just a smile? Not speaking, waiting patiently in heart, letting each other fade, forgetting in the mortal world!

Life's greatest regret is stubbornly holding onto what shouldn't be held, why not let go freely, allowing memories to dissipate like smoke and clouds, thread by thread; easily giving up what shouldn't be given up, since it's already waved goodbye, let it stay in the vast blue sky, wider than the mind, pausing, lingering.

In the swift rush of life, suddenly looking back, the fairy tale has ended. Everything, whether bitter pain or happy joy, instantly transforms into a warm current, warming the weary hearts of old times, comforting the souls once thought to be injured. Perhaps, learning to forget is itself a kind of happiness!

Thereafter, through the changes of sea and land, the blooming and wilting of flowers, one will still weather life's storms and rains. But those unforgettable memories, buried by one's own hands, those unfinished emotions, looked back upon with a smile! Recite a line: "Standing far away in the horizon, leaning lightly against a branch with a faint smile." And say another: "At first, we were strangers; in the end, we were unrecognizable."
