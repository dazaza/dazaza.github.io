VP of Qunar Blasts Ctrip in Text Message Feud

by gelu87689 on 2012-02-10 15:03:27

Screenshot of Dai Zheng's text message: Yesterday, senior executives of Ctrip Travel Network and the public relations staff in North China received a text message from Dai Zheng, Vice President of Qunar.com. Not only did he insult Ctrip employees as "bastards" in the text message, but also claimed that he would publicly collect complaints about Ctrip and use all his resources to have CCTV's 315 program report on Ctrip's negative news.

Reported by our correspondent Wang Fei: "I will use all my strength to make it reported!" At the beginning of the new year, the "verbal battle" between Qunar.com and Ctrip broke out again.

On the morning of February 2, senior executives of Ctrip Travel Network and the PR staff in North China received a text message from Dai Zheng, Vice President of Qunar.com: "If your employee @Laorong Jingyidares to slander Qunar.com with fraud on my Weibo again, I will publicly collect complaint cases against Ctrip, compile them into a book, and hand them over to CCTV's 315 program team. I swear I will use all my resources to make it reported! I give you one last chance to let this bastard learn to talk based on facts. Thanks... Dai Zheng."

Ctrip said it had filed the text message for record.

Ctrip introduced that the "@Laorong Jingyida" mentioned in Dai Zheng's text message was Wu Rongjing, an employee of Ctrip Travel Network's Xiamen branch. They had previously debated on Weibo. The PR staff of Ctrip in North China stated that this was an irrational and blatant threat, so they immediately reported Dai Zheng's text message to the company's legal and PR departments for filing.

"How can Qunar.com, as a company, which should also be one of the subjects supervised by 315, have such reason and audacity to threaten to use all its resources to report Ctrip's negative news?" Lu Yan, General Manager of Market Planning and Public Relations Department of Ctrip Travel Network, was very puzzled about Dai Zheng's text message threat.

Wu Rongjing, who was insulted as "this bastard" in Dai Zheng's text message, felt extremely wronged. He stated that as Vice President of Qunar.com, Dai Zheng had more than 200,000 followers on Weibo, while he himself was just a regular employee of Ctrip. Their debate was originally uneven in terms of strength.

Yesterday, Dai Zheng updated his Weibo post, indicating that he would publicly collect complaints about Ctrip.

It is understood that this SMS "threat gate" originated from a Weibo debate between the two parties. In January 2012, a media outlet in Shanghai reported that a consumer was defrauded of more than 49,000 yuan when purchasing train tickets through a merchant on Qunar.com. However, subsequent reports indicated that the consumer did not find the fraudulent phone number through Qunar.com. Before and after the incident, Ctrip Travel Network released a safe ticket purchasing guide for the Spring Festival travel rush, reminding consumers to beware of fraud when purchasing train tickets and air tickets. Qunar.com believed that Ctrip intentionally targeted them. Therefore, Vice President of Qunar.com, Dai Zheng, and Ctrip's PR personnel engaged in heated debates on Weibo.
