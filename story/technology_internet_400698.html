Reflections triggered by online shopping

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:26:52

I am a person who likes online shopping. It seems that I have been doing it for several years. The clothes, pants, shoes, and even some daily necessities I use are all bought online. Today, while I was buying something online and comparing the prices between physical stores and online malls, I couldn't help but fall into deep thought.

Many people think that online shopping is still very novel, but this is not the case. Online shopping has already started in the West for a long time and has been widely accepted by Westerners. Actually, the biggest reason I like online shopping is because of the wide selection. All kinds of goods are clearly visible, and you can easily compare between different merchants. It's convenient and fast, and generally, there's no need to haggle. After paying directly, the goods will be delivered to your door, saving a lot of time and providing diverse choices.

In fact, whether it's a physical store or online transactions, they both belong to marketing and require various methods to promote sales.

The history of online shopping in China is not long. Most of it is B2C, which means individual merchants to customers, and also C2C, which means enterprises to customers.

Speaking of online shopping, I think it is a market with great potential. In the future, although online shopping cannot completely replace the physical market, with the openness of people's concepts and the standardization of network systems, laws, and various measures, people's trust will increase, and more and more people will choose online shopping. I am very optimistic that in the future urban consumption, online shopping may occupy more than 50% of the market, meaning more people will choose online shopping instead of physical stores.

Compared with physical stores, the advantages of online malls are obvious. There's no rent, no labor costs, no water and electricity fees, etc., meaning basically no cost. Its profit, minus some small expenses, is basically equal to the selling price minus the purchase cost, unlike physical stores where the profit is the selling price minus complicated and large rents, labor, water, electricity, etc.

Speaking of online shopping, we must also mention related industries, the most direct being e-commerce and logistics. E-commerce has only developed recently, and although logistics has a longer history, it has also grown significantly due to the help of online shopping. E-commerce mainly handles the sales, orders, and follow-ups of online stores, while logistics is responsible for delivery.

Personally, I believe that online malls, e-commerce, and logistics management are three projects with bright prospects. Regarding the industries and investments for the future, I think we should choose those with development prospects. What looks good now, everyone will invest in, naturally following the crowd and making it unprofitable. We need to choose those that don't look so good now but will have a promising future. To put it simply, it's like long-term and short-term investment in the stock market. Short-term investment is relatively simple and speculative, requiring observation of its relative market conditions over a period of time. Also, because the time interval between buying and selling is not large, the risk is smaller, and the profit is also smaller. Long-term investment, on the other hand, requires looking at its future, current, and past market trends. One could say that long-term investment is where the big money is made, but it also comes with high risks.

However, there are still some problems with online shopping in China that need to be solved to break through the bottleneck. The so-called bottleneck refers to the fact that people with relatively open minds have already accepted and are willing to adopt this shopping method, but another part of people with relatively conservative views still think there are issues with security, quality, etc. In fact, this group of people represents the largest market. Getting them to accept the concept of online shopping would create huge profits.

People have two main concerns about online shopping:

Firstly, the security issue, which is one of the main reasons people refuse online shopping. They generally doubt whether their accounts are secure and whether fraud exists in online transactions. This situation does indeed exist, after all, online transactions are open and are personal subjective behaviors. Banks or governments do not intervene too much, so some small merchants take advantage of this. But this can be improved. With the perfection of future internet laws and choosing well-known online stores, such problems can be improved or avoided.

The second issue is the quality problem. In fact, at the beginning of online shopping, the service did not catch up. Basically, once you receive the goods, that's it, confirming the concern people had about this issue. However, now the service awareness of each online store has greatly improved. There are services such as returns if unsatisfied, exchanges if there are quality issues, etc. So, this situation basically does not need excessive worry. Choosing merchants with high credibility can provide better guarantees in this aspect. Yet many people still believe that tangible goods in physical stores feel more reliable. If we can change this mindset and let these people believe that online shopping also ensures quality and saves time, then we will gain a large number of customers.

Perhaps, some people think that I write articles just to show off my literary talent or flaunt my thoughts, but it's not like that. I used to have the habit of writing diaries in a diary book, but now I write in my space because it allows me to exchange thoughts and experiences with others. Others can see what I write. But obviously, not everyone understands my articles, and not everyone appreciates them. Just like a pot of aged wine, those who don't know how to drink won't find it fragrant but will find it pungent. Actually, I don't expect everyone to like my articles. As long as I can exchange thoughts and experiences with friends who like my articles, I am satisfied!

My posts carry strong personal ideas and distinct personalities. I have principles when writing articles. First, I don't like to mix other people's thoughts into my diaries, so my diaries are written word by word based on my own thoughts. Secondly, many friends' posts are almost entirely reprints from others, but I don't like that. This is not to say that I am not humble and eager to learn. Of course, if I encounter good ones, I will reprint them. However, I think learning is learning; no matter how many good articles you reprint, advanced thoughts, they cannot be converted into your own knowledge and thoughts, which makes them useless. Ask those who often reprint others' works: after some time, will you still remember the content you reprinted before? This is like how elementary school teachers forced us to memorize idioms. If forced, you might forget after some time and definitely won't apply them flexibly in life. Only by actively and curiously learning can one become articulate and eloquent later. Posts are the same; what you think and record yourself is yours. Other people's thoughts are always theirs. Even if you understand and can interpret them, the key is that they are not yours, meaning you cannot apply them to life and can only stay at the level of understanding. Moreover, since I was young, my teachers have emphasized that a good memory is not as good as a bad pen. Even if you have a fleeting thought or idea, I think it is necessary to record it, otherwise, it will be lost, how regrettable!

Speaking of posts, I must thank my junior high school teacher for making me fall in love with them and never letting go. Some people think that keeping a diary is troublesome and annoying. Indeed, most people don't have this habit or just deal with it perfunctorily. But I think it is very necessary. First, posts can summarize one's daily life, which is a kind of self-reflection, reminding oneself to cultivate strengths and avoid weaknesses. Moreover, each post records one's detailed life, making life full and colorful. This also expresses the trend line of our lives, providing guidance for future behavior. Most importantly, it is a rich memoir. Imagine, reviewing memories by reading posts versus relying solely on memory, which is more touching and true? Obviously, words.

However, sometimes, even if you try hard, the money you earn is limited because your energy and ability are limited. At this point, your income capacity has approached saturation. Now, you need to use them for investment. Simply put, using hard work to make money is relatively solid, primary, and slow, while using money to make more money is advanced and fast, allowing assets to grow exponentially, which the primary method cannot achieve. Here financial matters come into play. Wealthy people around the world can invest flexibly, first earning money through sweat and then through wisdom, which is a process. Of course, these are theories and idealized states, but I am not dreaming of wealth, blinding myself to social reality. Instead, I am constantly accumulating thoughts and experience for the future. I will certainly make every effort!

The highest quality of a person lies in having a thinking brain; everything else is secondary and subordinate. Someone said that practical work is the most important. I have always disliked when people speak or write with a strong official tone or political coloring or rigidly and inflexibly. In fact, the essence of learning is to demonstrate things simply, practically, and popularly, not to confine oneself in dogmas and rules.

Returning to online shopping, if marketing today still focuses only on physical stores, it is too narrow-minded. However, it shouldn't aim too high and want to eat a big cake all at once. Starting from physical stores and expanding to the internet is feasible. I think that because the cost of the internet is very low, the profit that can be sought in this market is greater. Similarly, someone might ask, if the cost online is low, but the selling price is also low, and the cost in physical stores is high, but they sell more expensively, wouldn't the profit be roughly the same? Actually, it's not. For example, if the actual purchase price and miscellaneous small costs of an item in an online store are 5 yuan, and it sells for 8 yuan, it can net 3 yuan. In a physical store, if the actual cost is 4 yuan, plus rent, labor, and utilities, we calculate it as 6 yuan, then the total cost is 10 yuan. To make the same profit as the online store, its lowest price must be 13 yuan. At this point, the 8-yuan price of the online store becomes competitive compared to the physical store. Another scenario is that the physical store reduces its profit from 3 yuan to 1 yuan to enhance its price competitiveness, so the selling price is marked as 11 yuan. In this case, although the price advantage of the online store weakens, its profit is greater than that of the physical store, and many online stores rely on volume. With increased volume, total profit naturally rises. Therefore, regardless of how you look at it, the advantages of online stores are very clear.

What we need to do is to open the doors of consumers' conservatism and let them understand the safety, speed, and efficiency of online shopping.

Looking to the future, with the change in people's awareness, the standardization of the internet system, and the establishment of reputable stores, I optimistically believe that the online market will become very optimistic. Undoubtedly, this will be a place of opportunity, and there will be countless unseen fierce commercial battles. Not only smart but wise businessmen need to seize the opportunity.

To be honest, wherever there is demand, there is marketing. If there were an ideal state in the world where buyers could directly connect with manufacturers, intermediaries and marketers would naturally be unnecessary. But obviously, this is impossible to achieve. In fact, this is like a tree diagram. Manufacturers are at the top, needing more branches to expand and extend their products, and also needing others to promote and get others to accept their products, thus achieving a broader market, hence the existence of marketers and intermediaries. Street vending is marketing, selling items worth millions is also marketing. Everyone can do marketing, but not everyone does it well. Therefore, China doesn't lack marketers but lacks high-level marketers.

There's a saying that really hits the mark: marketing is first and foremost about selling oneself. In fact, I think its significance in our lives goes far beyond simply promoting goods. For example, cooperation between companies, signing contracts and plans, job applications, etc., are all forms of marketing. First, sell yourself, let others accept you, then they will accept your product or plan! This field of study is much wider and more applicable than specialized research or technical operations, so I think this field of study is worth learning for most people, even if you are not in this industry.
