When the object to be weighed or the loaded vehicle is placed on the load-bearing platform, under the force of gravity, the load is transmitted to the weighing sensor via the load-bearing platform, causing the elastic body of the sensor to deform. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance and outputs an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. Then, after being converted into a digital signal by an A/D converter, the microprocessor (CPU) of the instrument processes the weight signal and directly displays the weight data. With a printer configured, the weighing data can be printed and recorded, and if a computer is configured, the measurement data can be entered into a computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it’s a scale or a weighbridge, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the object's weight into an electrical signal, which is sent to the conditioning circuit, A/D converter, and computing circuit (MCU), and then converted into a digital display.
