It's the nasal mucosa that 'produces' the mucus. When healthy, the nasal mucosa continues to secrete mucus. A normal person secretes about several hundred milliliters of mucus every day, equivalent to one to two small packs of milk. However, these mucus flow towards the posterior nasal aperture into the pharynx following the direction of the ciliary movement of the nasal mucosa, and then return to the body through unconscious swallowing movements, so under normal conditions, it cannot be seen flowing out of the nasal cavity.

When suffering from rhinitis or when the nasal mucosa is stimulated, the secretion of mucus will increase significantly. The otolaryngology department of the Armed Police Guangxi Corps Hospital has introduced the American DNR low-temperature plasma ablation technology, also known as the American DNR digital low-temperature plasma ablation technique. The DNR English translation is multi-nier, so it is simply called the multi-nier low-temperature ablation technique. This technology originates from the high-energy military plasma technology of the American military, originally used for nuclear energy and charged particle research in space. Since the treatment probe of the multi-nier technology is only 10 micrometers to 1 millimeter in size, some of them are finer than hair, so it is also called "hair-like technology." The working principle is to use the energy of low-temperature plasma radiofrequency to ablate tissues at a lower temperature, thereby avoiding damage to tissues, greatly reducing the patient's pain, and shortening the recovery period. In recent years, the low-temperature plasma ablation system has been widely used in the field of otolaryngology abroad, and its clinical effects have been recognized by world authorities.
