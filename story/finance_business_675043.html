French is the most popular foreign language among international students other than English, and most students majoring in French have aspirations to study abroad. Since most families in China belong to the working class, parents hope to send their children to countries with low tuition fees, beautiful environments, and high education standards. In recent years, Canada has become very popular among both parents and students due to its high-quality education system and relatively affordable tuition fees. Compared to other popular study abroad destinations such as the U.S., UK, and Australia, Canada's costs are much lower. Tuition only requires 70,000 RMB per year, and living expenses also require 70,000 RMB annually. A French major pursuing graduate studies in Quebec typically needs only about 150,000 RMB over two years.

Currency and Banking:

Canada uses the metric system for measurements. The monetary unit is the Canadian dollar, where one Canadian dollar equals 100 cents. The most common paper bills are in denominations of $5, $10, $20, $50, and $100. Coins come in denominations of 1 cent, 5 cents, 10 cents, 25 cents, $1, and $2. The $1 and $2 coins were introduced within the last 10 years but $1 and $2 paper bills still circulate as legal tender. Canadian banknotes are clearly marked and different denominations have distinct colors.

Banks and Currency Exchange:

While banks generally offer the best exchange rates, some charge fees for currency exchange or traveler’s checks, and posted rates usually do not include taxes. It is advisable to exchange a small amount of Canadian dollars before arriving since banks are only open during business hours (9 a.m. to 5 p.m.) Monday through Friday. Other exchange methods are more expensive, and hotels and stores often offer poor exchange rates or charge high service fees. Regardless of where you go, always inquire about fees before exchanging money. Most shops and malls accept Canadian dollars, and in tourist areas, some stores may accept U.S. dollars. Other currencies must be exchanged.

Banks and ATMs:

Students can easily find banks and ATMs in Canada, especially in large cities, making them more convenient than credit cards and traveler’s checks. You can use your bank debit card or credit card to withdraw cash from ATMs. You can open an account at a local bank to obtain a debit card, which can also be used in many stores like grocery and clothing stores.

Banks offer various services, including currency exchange, safety deposit boxes for passports, and savings accounts. Many Canadians use personal checks issued by banks as an efficient payment tool.

Traveler’s Checks and Credit Cards:

When traveling, it is safer to use traveler’s checks, which can be purchased from major Canadian banks. If lost or stolen, they are easy to replace. Most stores and hotels accept traveler’s checks and credit cards.
