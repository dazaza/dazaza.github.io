Two confirmed absentees for Arsenal vs Liverpool as Wenger snubs Cruyff's call for Fabregas rest

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 10:05:29

Arsenal vs Liverpool: Two Confirmed Absent from Starting Lineup, Wenger Again Rejects Cruyff's Call for Fabregas

According to Sina Sports, the away match against Burnley this coming Wednesday is not expected to be too challenging for Arsenal. Securing three points and overtaking Aston Villa once again is their sole objective. However, Arsène Wenger's team has seen some new injuries emerge. In Sunday's match against Liverpool, mainstay Denilson suffered a back injury, while Traoré pulled his groin. Arsenal's official website reported that both players have been confirmed unavailable for the game against Burnley.

But there is some good news as well. Eboué, who missed the Liverpool game due to a groin strain, has returned to the squad and can bolster the right flank options. Wenger said, "There was too little recovery time after Sunday's game, so we've picked up a few more injuries. Denilson is definitely out of the question; he might be available on Saturday against Hull City, but we must check thoroughly to ensure his back is fully recovered before he plays. However, Eboué has returned to the team."

After defeating Liverpool, Wenger was in high spirits. His team had completely shaken off the shadow of their heavy defeat by Chelsea. When discussing the championship prospects for this season, Wenger stated that the Premier League title race for the 2009-10 season is the most open (in terms of the number of teams involved) since he arrived at Arsenal. Wenger told the official website, "No team has been able to maintain an early lead in the season. Instead, four teams are tangled up together, and I believe that a total of six, seven, or even eight teams can surprise anyone on any given weekend. Clearly, this is the most open battle for first place in the league during my 13 years here, and I feel that many teams have the potential to win the league title."

The respect from other teams does not equate to pessimism about his own squad. For Arsenal, Wenger always maintains special confidence. He said, "From the start of the season, I deeply believed that this would be our year, and I said the same after the Chelsea game. The reason I say this is because: if you look at us as a whole, how well we have improved, how strong our team spirit and quality are, and how each game is fully prepared like a battle. From September to next May, all of this will continue to improve."

For Arsenal to maintain their championship aspirations, Cesc Fàbregas' importance is self-evident. With Robin van Persie injured, Fàbregas is almost the only guiding light for Arsenal. Wenger naturally wants to ensure that Fàbregas plays as well as possible in every key match. Some external requests that can be rejected will be rejected, such as the friendly match between Catalonia and Argentina scheduled for next month on the 22nd. Catalonia coach Johan Cruyff requested Fàbregas to play in this game at Camp Nou, but Wenger clearly refused.

Fàbregas takes pride in his Catalan heritage and admires Cruyff greatly, but Wenger disregards this, as he has the right to reject such requests since Catalonia is not an officially recognized FIFA national team. However, the 22-year-old Fàbregas feels his physical condition is fine, and since next week isn't a double gameweek, he believes he has time to make a trip to Spain. He said, "I'll play in the games against Burnley on Wednesday and Hull City three days later, but after that, there are no Premier League matches for close to a week, so I can return to Spain on the 22nd to play for Catalonia."

However, Wenger's mind only calculates for his own team. There is a crucial match against Villa the following weekend. Of the four major clubs, the other three have lost to Villa, and whether Arsenal can perform well is highly questionable. Van Persie got injured playing for Holland, and Liverpool explicitly refused to let Mascherano represent Argentina in the match against Catalonia. Wenger, who has always disliked international game distractions, naturally wouldn’t agree to Fàbregas playing in a match that doesn’t count as an A-level international. Wenger said, "We must respect the concept of priorities, and the priority is the club itself."

In addition to this game, Spain also has another notable friendly match next week. It’s a match between the La Liga Stars and the African All-Stars, known as the second Africa Charity Match, jointly initiated by UNICEF and the Kanouté Foundation. The original African team list included five Arsenal players: Song, Nasri, Diaby, Sagna, and Eboué. Obviously, the African team composition includes not only native-born players but also those with African heritage. Wenger wouldn't agree to this arrangement either. Therefore, in the final list, there wasn't a single Arsenal player, in fact, not even one active Premier League player. The closest connection to the Premier League, aside from the founder of the Kanouté Foundation, former West Ham and Tottenham forward and current Sevilla player, Mali's Frederic Kanouté, would probably be former Manchester United striker Manucho, who moved to Valladolid this summer.
