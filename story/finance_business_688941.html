Four children drowned in a brick factory's water pit, among whom three were compensated 150 thousand yuan respectively

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-03 16:27:21

One victim's family signed a compensation agreement with the brick factory. Photo by Qin Bin, reporter of our newspaper.

Report by our newspaper (reporter Liu Gang): During the Spring Festival, an incident of children drowning occurred at a brick factory in Sangzi Town, Jixian County, Tianjin. Four children under the age of 8 fell into a water pit formed after the brick factory extracted soil for brick burning and drowned.

As of yesterday, three families of the deceased had reached a one-time compensation agreement with the brick factory, with each receiving 150,000 yuan in compensation.

According to statistics from local villagers, the involved brick factory was built in the 1980s, and up to now, eight people have drowned there.

Narrative: On the seventh day of the first lunar month, four children went missing.

The tragedy occurred on January 29, the seventh day of the first lunar month.

Around 5 p.m. that afternoon, three families surnamed Pan and one family surnamed Wang from Liulin Village, Houjiaying Town, Jixian County, mobilized their relatives and friends to search for the children throughout the entire village.

All four missing children were under the age of 8, including three boys: Wang Yu (pronunciation), Pan Yanbo, and Pan Haocong (pronunciation), who were all 7 years old and still attending kindergarten; and Pan Yuxi (pronunciation), aged 6, who was the youngest and the only girl.

Villagers told reporters that Pan Yanbo disappeared after lunch, "We couldn't find him anywhere."

Around 10 p.m., the bodies of the three missing children, excluding Pan Yanbo, were found in the water pit next to Liulin Village at the Jixian Ruili Brick Factory. The body of Pan Yanbo was retrieved from the same area at around 10 a.m. the next morning.

Scene: Children's footprints remained on the ice.

Villagers who participated in the on-site retrieval described that the water pit was formed after the brick factory extracted soil for brick burning. It froze during winter, but there were several springs in the southwestern corner near the shore that did not freeze.

No eyewitnesses saw the specific process of the children falling into the water. However, family members discovered the footprints of the four children on the ice of the water pit.

According to villagers' speculation, it was possible that the four children were playing on the ice of the brick factory's water pit and accidentally fell into the unfrozen spring, leading to their drowning.

Background: Villagers had previously called for the closure of the brick factory.

The tragic Ruili Brick Factory is located within the jurisdiction of Madao Village, Sangzi Town, Jixian County, bordering Liulin Village, Houjiaying Town.

Villagers stated that the brick factory generally extracted soil locally for brick burning, which over time formed deep pits. To date, two large pits have been formed, with the deepest reaching 20-30 meters.

According to villagers' statistics, the Ruili Brick Factory's water pit has claimed eight lives so far, but "it's the first time someone drowned in winter." This data has not been verified by the official authorities in Jixian County.

On a network forum co-hosted by the Tianjin Government Network and the Northern Net, reporters found that as early as December 2011, villagers had posted online urging the closure of the brick factory. The post read, "There are deep pits around the brick factory, and incidents of children drowning while swimming have already occurred. We urgently hope for its complete closure." The Jixian County People's Government responded, requesting "specific details about the town, village, and community to facilitate investigation. You can also directly report to the relevant town government."

Progress: One victim has yet to be cremated.

Yesterday afternoon in Liulin Village, reporters learned that the families of the drowned children Pan Yanbo, Pan Haocong (pronunciation), and Pan Yuxi (pronunciation) had signed mediation agreements with the brick factory. The Jixian Ruili Brick Factory compensated each drowning victim with 150,000 yuan in a one-time payment. The agreement explicitly states, "This matter is hereby resolved." Villagers mentioned that the actual operator of the brick factory is Pan Huijun, who is also the director of the Madao Village Committee.

The families of the deceased children refused to comment on the drowning incident. Wang Yu's (pronunciation) mother disagreed and did not sign the agreement. As of press time, Wang Yu's (pronunciation) body has yet to be cremated.

According to informed villagers, the contracting contract of the involved brick factory expired at the end of last year. This information has not been officially confirmed.
