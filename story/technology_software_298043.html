Chinese football team is expected to participate in two invitation matches; Greece targets Korea, willing to have a warm-up match with China

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 16:57:48

China's national football team is expected to participate in two invitation tournaments: Greece, grouped with South Korea in the 2010 FIFA World Cup, expressed a willingness to warm up against China - reported by Jia Leishi. At the draw ceremony of the South Africa World Cup, there was still a presence of Chinese football. Journalists captured a photo of Shao Wenzhong with Greek head coach Otto Rehhagel. Taking advantage of this opportunity at the South Africa World Cup draw ceremony, Fotabao Company actively negotiated with participating teams to find rare friendly match opportunities for the Chinese team. Although contracts have not yet been signed, teams like Greece clearly indicated their hope to warm up against the Chinese team, and substantive negotiations are currently underway.

Events like the FIFA draw always attract many national football association figures, not only to build relationships but also to seek cooperation opportunities. During this draw ceremony, Milu saw Fotabao Company employees and was very straightforward, saying to Shao Wenzhong: "I'm here to find a contract." After creating the miracle of participating in several consecutive World Cups, now as an observer, Milu was somewhat reluctant, thus he eagerly hoped to find some luck at the draw site.

Similar to him, Fotabao Company is also here for contracts. Currently, Fotabao is responsible for the 'Team China' project, finding friendly opponents for the national team and Olympic team, which has always been part of its work. However, previously, most contacts were made through faxes and phone calls, and even through brokers, making it difficult to establish good relationships. To change this situation, Fotabao Company took the initiative, with Shao Wenzhong leading his team to South Africa for public relations activities.

After arriving in South Africa, Shao Wenzhong planned to mediate and communicate during the days before and after the draw ceremony. A few days before the draw ceremony, the associations and head coaches of the final round participants had already arrived in South Africa. Fotabao seized these few precious idle days to exchange ideas and communicate with various national associations, clearly expressing the desire to hold friendly matches. Since there are three East Asian teams in the final round, many teams see a very high possibility of being grouped with East Asian teams and showed great interest in warming up against the Chinese team, while indicating that if they were grouped with East Asian teams, they would arrange to warm up against the Chinese team.

After the draw, Fotabao immediately contacted all the participating teams grouped with East Asian teams, covering all 9 teams grouped with South Korea, Japan, and North Korea, achieving remarkable efficiency. For convenience, Fotabao personnel arranged for some to attend the draw ceremony on-site while others waited at the hotel for leaders of these national associations and immediately began specific negotiations.

Currently, the most active teams are those grouped with East Asia, which is also Fotabao's main target. This night was very critical, and Fotabao did not miss the chance. The day after the draw, representatives from each participating national association went to the competition venues to inspect training grounds, hotels, and other facilities in preparation for next year's World Cup. Some national associations even immediately signed usage agreements, with Brazil signing a hotel agreement for a delegation of over 500 people.

Although negotiations just started haven't delved deeper, in exchanges, Fotabao learned that there will be invitation tournaments held in Switzerland and South Africa in May next year. Fotabao positively expressed willingness to participate to the Swiss and South African Football Associations. Due to FIFA regulations prohibiting the use of June World Cup venues for warm-ups in May, the South African tournament is in jeopardy, but upon hearing this news, the Zimbabwe Football Association immediately expressed willingness to take over the event. Given the geographical proximity and similar climate between the two countries, the invitation tournament received responses from many countries. The Zimbabwe Football Association also extended an invitation to the Chinese team.

Although the Chinese team is an observer, it is treated well. Several countries showed high enthusiasm for warming up against the Chinese team. In the grouping, Greece is grouped with South Korea in Group B, and the Greek Football Association President explicitly expressed the "hope to warm up against the Chinese team."

Even FIFA shows favoritism towards the Chinese team. There is one detail that can prove this: at the banquet of the draw ceremony, Nanyong's seat was arranged at the secondary table, where high-ranking officials such as Blatter and local South African dignitaries sat at the main table, and vice presidents of some associations like Zheng Mengzhun sat at the secondary table. Zheng Mengzhun was seated next to Nanyong, but since Nanyong returned home to attend the CSL Awards Ceremony, his seat remained empty. Even the chairpersons of many participating countries in the World Cup Final, such as France and Argentina, were far from the main table, clearly showing FIFA's emphasis on Chinese football.

In Fotabao's exchanges with other countries, there are also unfavorable factors. Due to China's location in East Asia, geographically too far from South Africa, some participating teams are unwilling to travel to the distant East to warm up against the Chinese team before the competition. Therefore, if the Chinese team wants to face off against the top 32 teams, they must conduct warm-up matches abroad, which is an unfortunate necessity, and Fotabao Company deeply regrets it.
