Counting the Top Ten "Delicacies" in the Game

by nuannuanleng on 2009-11-09 22:36:47

"Eating culture" is an important part of gaming culture, and almost all mature games will have a "eating" system. The things to eat are varied. It can be said that "eating" and "fighting" are the two most common and important elements in games. Below, let's take a look at the top ten most typical types of "food" in games.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Items

Collecting various items is one of the greatest pleasures in most games. To enhance the joy of collecting items, "picking up items" is often referred to as "eating items." A representative game would be "Mario Kart," where you can enjoy driving your kart while eating items to harm others, providing endless fun.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Weapons

If collecting items feels great, then there’s no doubt that getting a good weapon with excellent attributes in a game is even more exhilarating. In the past, many people went crazy for a Dragon Slaying Sword in "Legend." Giving a fine sword to a hero, obtaining an ultimate weapon has been the ultimate dream of countless gamers.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Vegetables

Compared to the previous two, vegetables as food are often overlooked by gamers. However, at least they are real "food." When it comes to vegetables, the first thing that comes to mind might be Popeye's spinach. In the game "Fable," the various functions of vegetables are taken to the extreme: eating too much cheese makes you fat, eating celery helps you lose weight, eating tofu makes you pure (this tofu is not the same as the other), and eating too much "Crispy Chicken" turns you into a demon. Oh, by the way, Crispy Chicken isn't a vegetable.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Water

Water is the source of life, and naturally, its role is not ignored in games. Drinkable water in games generally symbolizes recovery, restoring physical strength, energy, vitality, and even detoxifying, making it incredibly effective. The most representative example is the "Prince of Persia" series, where water is especially precious in desert areas. So our monkey, oh no, our prince, relies on finding water sources to restore life and even save progress. Thus, the moment of finding water in a game is also when players can relax their nerves.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Medicines

Perhaps in the gaming world, various "red bottles and blue bottles" are more famous. Nevertheless, I still listed medicines separately, mainly referring to pills and tablets. This can be one of the unique representatives of Chinese martial arts culture. Chinese heroes always carry various kinds of golden wound-healing medicine and antidote pills just in case. The pinnacle work of domestic martial arts games, "Chinese Paladin," is a representative, where Mouse Fruit, Soul Restoring Pill, Snow Lotus Essence, and Heavenly Immortal Dew are well-known medicines familiar to players.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Potions

If someone doesn’t know what "red bottle" or "blue bottle" means, then he must not be a gamer. Since "Diablo" created those small red and blue balls, those seemingly insignificant red and blue bottles have become essential remedies for outdoor adventures and monster-slaying upgrades. The standard action in American ARPG games became desperately clicking the mouse with the right hand and continuously pressing the 1 and 2 number keys with the left hand.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Coins

Perhaps everyone is born with a love for money, and almost all games have a "coin-eating" setting. The ways to get coins are varied: some are lying on the road (and no one picks them up), some drop from monsters (why do monsters carry so many coins?), and the strangest and most classic example is undoubtedly "Super Mario" — coins popping out of stones!

Top Ten Game "Foods": Drumsticks

When you're low on health in an action game, what do you want to see the most? Drumsticks! Yes, drumsticks represent the restoration of vitality in action games, giving players survival and hope. The tradition of eating drumsticks can be traced back to the arcade game "Romance of the Three Kingdoms: Conquest of Heaven and Earth," where there was even a mini-game about eating drumsticks. One wonders how many children had their appetites stimulated by seeing drumsticks in the game?

Top Ten Game "Foods": Buns

If there's anything that represents the eating culture in games more than drumsticks, it has to be buns. It can be said that any game with drumsticks will definitely have buns, and the probability of buns appearing is much higher than drumsticks. In the latest "Conquest of Heaven and Earth 2 Online," buns are even regarded as one of the game's symbols, elevating the eating culture to a whole new level.

Top Ten Game "Foods": Monsters

This... can be considered a consolation prize. The most bullied "dish" in games are undoubtedly the various kinds of monsters. Whether big or small, cute or fierce, strong or magical, flying in the sky or running on the ground, as long as it's a monster, it's delicious "food" in the eyes of players. Truly lamentable...
