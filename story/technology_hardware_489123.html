I'm pleased to be here today to speak my feelings.

by wxnfeifei on 2011-09-14 14:04:12

(Old Stories Touching)

It is very pleasant to be able to express my feelings here today. Could the teacher analyze it for me after reading? I am very grateful.

I was born in 84, and he is two years younger than me. His first girlfriend was a sister in the same dormitory as mine in university. They broke up after half a year together. The reason was that my classmate didn't like him; she liked her ex-boyfriend. When they broke up, he was very sad. So while I was comforting him, we gradually chatted more. At that time, we hadn't met in person, only seen each other through online video chats. I felt that he really loved her, but my classmate insisted on breaking up, and there was nothing he could do. Later, as we kept chatting, we fell in love with each other, deeply so. We called each other every day. By the start of the year, I came to Guangzhou, where he worked, to find him.

Ever since then, both of us have been working in Guangzhou. From 2008 until now, we have been cohabiting. We occasionally argue. My temper isn't good, but he always consoles me. If I get angry, he won't be able to sleep at night because I will disturb him until he can't fall asleep. After two years of living together, I have gradually matured and don't argue with him as frequently anymore. We have always had a deep understanding and love for each other because we are both people who have been hurt emotionally before, so we cherish each other. There were also family members and friends who said he was too young and immature, advising me to leave early. For some time, he didn't have a job, and I had debts to repay. Life in 2008 was very tight, but we have managed to stay together until now because we know how much we love each other. Both he and his ex-girlfriend are from the same city, but their homes aren't close. Therefore, I'm always worried about what would happen if they meet again in the future. He has already deleted her QQ account, or maybe she deleted his. After she left, I started being with him. Since then, my relationship with that classmate hasn't been good, and we haven't been in contact anymore. But I truly did not take him away from her. She left first, and only then did I start being with him. Should I not be with him? But once we met, we fell in love. What could we do?

I am a sensitive woman. Even his small actions, such as playing around with colleagues, make me jealous. I know that the person he loves is me, but sometimes he jokes around with female colleagues. Until July this year, an unpleasant event occurred. He used to never chat aimlessly with women. In internet cafes, he only played games or watched videos. He never chatted idly with other women, nor sent text messages to them. But this one incident made me extremely sad. I cried all night, and I even smashed his phone. Here's what happened: One day, I discovered that he frequently sent text messages to a woman. When I asked her who she was, he lied, saying she was a former female colleague. Later, he said she was a friend of a colleague. When I seized his phone to check his messages, I was shocked to find many ambiguous words. At that moment, I just couldn't believe how he could betray me like this. I always believed that he loved me, but now... these text messages, how can I trust him? I consulted several friends, and they all said that behavior like his is considered infidelity.

My heart turned cold. As I checked his phone, I cried while reading the text messages he sent her. He probably didn’t expect me to see those messages, and he was clearly shocked. He didn't know what to do, just wanting to go back to bed. I wouldn't let him. I said, "If you really love me and want to marry me, stand there quietly." He might have feared losing me, so he stood there quietly. I cried and asked, "How could you treat me like this?" He said it was just because he was bored and curious. I told him to admit to cheating, but he refused. He said he hadn't cheated, so why should he admit something he didn't do? During that period, he often sent text messages saying he missed me and loved me. Sometimes he even said things like, "Why do you love me so much?" Now I think it must have been because he felt guilty, otherwise why would he suddenly say such things when he rarely said them before. In the end, I told him that if he didn't admit his mistake, we would have to separate because his actions clearly constituted infidelity, at least emotionally. I believe he didn't physically cheat because we spend every day together—working separately during the day and being together at night. Finally, he admitted his mistake. Since we still deeply love each other and he didn't physically cheat, we reconciled. Those days, he probably felt guilty and was exceptionally kind to me.

He frequently sent text messages saying sorry and promising to love me better in the future, etc. However, I still couldn't fully trust him and checked his phone every day. He didn't mind me checking. He is the type of person who likes to joke around with female colleagues, and he used to play around with them (but after I complained many times, he has changed quite a bit). Because I am jealous, I get upset, and he has to console me. But I simply cannot tolerate any ambiguity between him and other women. In their text messages, I only saw what he said to her, for example, "Piggy, are you awake?", "Woman, are you awake?", "Are you playing with water?", "Are you teasing me?", "Why don't you reply?", "If you're busy, just say so," "What's your birthday?" — about 20 texts in total. Many of the contents made me feel uncomfortable. At that time, he really seemed interested in that woman. It might be because we've been together for over two years, and my personality isn't great. He might have wanted something fresh. But I really couldn't believe he would do such a thing because I thought he wasn't that kind of person. Previously, I chose him because of his dedication and honesty in love.

Previously, we argued because his ex-girlfriend was my classmate, and I was often jealous. Now we argue because of his past infidelity. How could he treat me like this when I love him so much? How could he send text messages like "Woman, are you awake?" to a woman he's never met while saying he loves me? I'm going crazy. After experiencing these events, I have started paying attention to myself. I dress nicely every day to show him that I am not an inferior woman.

Of course, our sexual life is also good. He always makes sure I am satisfied first before satisfying himself. He has excellent control. We both like intimacy. Men like him are hard to let go of. He loves me, and I love him. Our sex life is good. The only issue is that his work is unstable now, but I can see that he is trying. Should I give up on this relationship?

When I tell these stories to my friends, some say he is a flirtatious man and advise me to be careful. Others say I am too sensitive. Sigh, I don't know what to do. We planned to visit his father's home during this year's National Day holiday and then decide when to get married. Last year, I met his mother, and she left a good impression on me. She even bought us each a jade piece—one a Buddha, and the other a Guanyin.

Due to time constraints, I cannot elaborate further. I have a few questions I'd like to ask the teacher. Could you please analyze and answer them?

1. How can I forget the shadow of his ex-girlfriend? Although he never mentions the past in front of me, I am too sensitive and often bring it up. This causes quite a few arguments.

2. Is he a flirtatious man? He usually likes to joke around with female colleagues and play around. Now that I've complained a lot, he doesn't dare to be ambiguous or play around anymore. But I still can't trust him completely. What if he does it again? What should I do if he finds other women to pass the time when he's not busy? How can I trust him? He never brings up my past or interferes with my interactions with other men. But I monitor him strictly because I love him so much. Am I wrong? I didn't use to monitor him much. It was only after I suddenly found the ambiguous text messages on his phone that I started checking his phone daily. I fear that another ambiguous message will appear, and I'll cry my eyes out. I asked him, "If I sent similar text messages to other men, what would you do?" He angrily replied that it shouldn't happen, and he admitted that sending those ambiguous messages was a mistake without considering my feelings. I asked him, "If you really love me, how could you send such ambiguous messages to other women?" He said it was because he was too bored and curious. Although he later apologized, I still can't fully trust him. Do you think this kind of man is flirtatious? He claims he isn't flirtatious, saying that if he were, we wouldn't have been together for so long. Sometimes I console myself, thinking that he might have only chatted with others because he was bored. He says he sent those ambiguous messages just for fun. I asked him, "If you think it's fun, why didn't you consider my feelings?" We argue, and whenever I bring it up now, he gets very angry and tells me not to mention it. He says he isn't flirtatious, but I've been with him for over two years, and I feel he has treated me well compared to other men. The only thing I can't tolerate is him sending ambiguous text messages to other women. What should I do? Although this matter has become part of our past, I really want to know whether this incident shows that he is a flirtatious man.

3. After my guidance, he has improved significantly. I haven't found him texting other women. He lets me know his QQ password and bank account password. But recently, when I checked his mobile QQ chat records, I found that he was joking around with a female classmate. The content went like this:

Him: When are you getting married?

Her: Are you proposing?

Him: No way, Miss, I can't afford to support you.

Her: Then why did you say that?

Him: I want to get married.

Her: Then let's do it.

Him: My family disagrees.

Her: Why? Don't they like the girl?

Him: Because I'm too young and poor.

And then the conversation ended. But I think, how can a man talk like this with a female classmate? I haven't even met his family, yet he talks to classmates about their disapproval. He always told me that once we meet his family, they won't disapprove. Sigh, I don't know how to trust him again. He said he was joking around to help me find a bridesmaid. I asked him, "Is it your job to find a bridesmaid? Did you even tell me about this?" Clearly, he just wanted to joke around with that female classmate. Anyway, these incidents make me feel that he is becoming increasingly flirtatious.

4. Finally, can a man like him be my husband? His family doesn't allow random women to come home easily. The fact that he wants to bring me home means that I am almost his wife. In the past, when I got angry, I often talked about breaking up. Whenever I mentioned breaking up, he became very nervous. I know we deeply love each other. But if we deeply love each other, why can't we be more faithful to each other? Sometimes I may be overly sensitive and can't tolerate him joking around with other women. Is it my fault? Am I overthinking? Should I look for an older man to be my husband? But we have invested a lot in each other, and it's not something that can be easily ended. What should I do? Am I too sensitive? Or is he too flirtatious?

If you regard love as possession, can your marriage be happy? Love or marriage needs to give men some space, not just allowing them to interact with you alone. If he is truly flirtatious, controlling him won't work.

1: Men don't care about your past. Are you sympathizing with him? If you can't let go of his past relationships, then you don't truly love him, but pity him.

2: Nowadays, young people chat with the opposite sex almost like they chat with the same sex. They say anything because they consider each other just friends. Some might just say things lightly without actually thinking about it seriously. Being too calculating about gains and losses will put pressure on men.

3: If the content could constitute a crime, then you shouldn't love him. There are no perfect men in the world.

4: Be rational and mature. The more you try to tie him down, the more you need to understand him. What does being more faithful mean? Making his entire world revolve around you? Is that realistic? Love can be like that, but after marriage, men will eventually reveal their true selves.

Don't dwell on how he used to be, but rather focus on how he is now. Does he accept your management, or is he escaping your control? You should have a sense of proportion instead of constantly demanding that his whole world revolves around you.
