Eating more than 10 grams of onions and garlic a day can prevent prostate cancer.

by abckvuul on 2012-03-01 18:39:54

Eat more onions and garlic, drink an appropriate amount of red wine. A study by the U.S. National Cancer Institute shows that people who consume more than 10 grams of garlic or onion daily have a 50% lower risk of prostate cancer compared to those who consume less than 2 grams. Additionally, researchers at the University of Texas found that drinking one or two glasses of red wine daily can also inhibit the growth of prostate cancer cells.

Have regular sexual activity. Scientists in Australia surveyed 2,338 men and found that those with normal sexual activity have a lower chance of getting the disease. Moreover, men who masturbate five times or more per week have a 34% lower risk of developing prostate cancer before the age of 70 compared to those who masturbate less frequently. Researchers say that semen contains some carcinogenic substances, and frequent ejaculation helps to expel them.

Regularly test PSA (prostate-specific antigen). PSA is an excellent marker for prostate cancer and is mainly used as an indicator for screening and monitoring prostate cancer. A study published in The New England Journal of Medicine involving 6,691 men found that men over 60 with a PSA value above 2.6 should be cautious. Domestic treatment guidelines suggest that men over 50 should have a prostate cancer screening once a year. This way, the condition can be detected earlier.
