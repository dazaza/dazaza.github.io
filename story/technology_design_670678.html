Disabled woman rescued after being trapped for 4 days and nights in house collapsed by floodwaters (Photo)

by anonymous on 2012-02-21 10:00:07

Xing Yaoyuan cares for his beloved wife in the hospital

By reporters Zheng Jianbin, Jiang Fangfang, communicator Li Xuanhua, Qiu Canwang, Tong Yang (text and pictures)

Key points:

Before the flood destroyed their home, a severely disabled woman from Liaodi Village, Dayuan Township, Jiangle County, Sanming City successfully persuaded her husband to leave, but she herself was buried under the debris of the house that collapsed in the flood. The husband, who witnessed his wife being buried, secretly cried for her but did not want to "trouble the organization," so he searched for the "corpse" on his own.

After 4 days and 4 nights, the wife who had been buried under the rubble for 96 hours was luckily rescued. She said, "Because I am worried about my husband and family, I am waiting for them to be safe." Because of their mutual concern, this couple created a miracle of life. After several days of treatment, yesterday, the wife was out of danger.

In times of peril, the wife forcibly made him leave

This couple's names are Xing Yaoyuan and Ye Changmei.

At around 9:00 AM on June 18th, the rapidly rising floodwaters began to flow into Liaodi Village. Xing Yaoyuan quickly reminded his father, who was nearly eighty years old, to stay at home and take care of his severely disabled wife Ye Changmei, who could only lie in bed. He then picked up a hoe and ran quickly towards the duck farm at the end of the village. "My wife is sick and needs a large amount of money all year round. With more than 400 ducks that are about to go to market, they can't be washed away by the water!" After settling the group of ducks, Xing Yaoyuan ran back home against the floodwaters, woke up his father who was still resting in bed, and led him to a higher earthen mound. "When I returned home again, the fierce floodwaters were already waist-deep, and I couldn't move after picking up my wife." Xing Yaoyuan recalled. "The water is so big that it's hard for one person to walk. Don't worry about me, hurry up and leave!" The usually gentle and virtuous wife quickly persuaded him to evacuate first. Xing Yaoyuan couldn't bear to leave his wife and tried several more times, but he still couldn't move. At this time, the wooden house began to shake and creak, and several nearby houses collapsed with a loud boom. Ye Changmei crazily urged Xing Yaoyuan to escape quickly and take good care of the family.

Xing Yaoyuan placed his wife on another higher bed in the house at all costs, then rushed to a warehouse outside the door to look for ropes and wooden boards to transfer his wife.

Unexpectedly, as soon as he stepped over the threshold, the house collapsed with a loud noise.

Xing Yaoyuan called out his wife's name in agony and was also swept into the floodwaters. Fortunately, in the floodwaters, he grabbed a piece of wood that had been washed down from upstream, then climbed onto a small platform, narrowly escaping disaster.

Afraid of "troubling the organization," he secretly looked for his wife

That night, Xing Yaoyuan stayed overnight on the mountain top with the party cadres who entered the village to evacuate the people. Cadres at all levels required each household to count the number of people that night in order to arrange rescue and evacuation work. Xing Yaoyuan, who witnessed his wife being buried under the debris of the house, remained silent.

At dawn on June 19th, three cadres from the township entered the village again to count the number of people, requiring detailed verification from each household, but the honest Xing Yaoyuan never mentioned his wife's situation. Later, he choked up and told the reporter: "The mountains are still collapsing from time to time, and the heavy rain continues to fall. In such a harsh environment, I don't want to drag the organization down just to save a relative that I watched helplessly get buried."

For several consecutive days, the uneasy Xing Yaoyuan kept wanting to enter the village to search for his wife, but landslides kept happening, preventing him from moving forward.

On the morning of June 22nd, the rain became slightly lighter. Xing Yaoyuan immediately called a few neighbors and entered the village to search for his wife.

Buried for 96 hours, the wife most missed her husband

In the collapsed wooden house wrapped in mud, Xing Yaoyuan and others dug a passage just wide enough for one person to crawl into the collapsed house. "I crawled in and faintly felt a weak sound coming from above. Listening carefully, it turned out to be my lover's voice, so familiar." After more than three hours of strenuous rescue efforts by five villagers, Ye Changmei was successfully rescued. By this time, she had endured unimaginable difficulties for 96 hours in the ruins.

Ye Changmei recalled that on June 18th, when the floodwaters collapsed the house, she was lifted up by the wooden ladder that was swept in by the flood peak. Under the buoyancy of the floodwaters, she and the wooden board on the bed "floated up" together. More fortunately, during the collapse of the two-story wooden house, two pieces of wood happened to insert into the water from different directions.

Thus, she was miraculously "held upright" by the clamped wooden boards, with her head just exposed above the water surface.

Because the rain didn't stop, she couldn't move her limbs, so when she was thirsty, she would try her best to raise her head and let the rainwater drip into her mouth through the gaps.

It was precisely because of her deep concern for her husband and family that allowed her to endure for 96 hours.

Teachers of Tai Ning No. 2 Middle School

Providing support for soldiers is also a way of disaster relief

In recent days, teachers of Tai Ning No. 2 Middle School have done their utmost to provide strong logistical support for the soldiers of a certain coastal defense unit of the Provincial Military District participating in flood control and rescue operations, composing a touching chapter of civilian support for the army.

After this flood disaster occurred, Tai Ning No. 2 Middle School was also heavily damaged, with some school buildings flooded, dormitories without water or electricity, and many teachers suffering varying degrees of damage, affecting normal teaching and living order.

After receiving the order to provide accommodation for the troops, the teachers of Tai Ning No. 2 Middle School immediately vacated 12 spacious student classrooms for the soldiers to live in.

Ensuring the logistics for the soldiers is disaster relief. The school coordinated generators and water pumps through various channels to solve the problems of water and electricity supply for the soldiers; to facilitate the soldiers' meals, the teachers cleaned the canteen full of silt thoroughly.

Seeing the soldiers going out early and returning late, fighting day after day, lifting power poles, clearing silt, and even using their rest time to tutor the third-year junior high students, the teachers all expressed: "The PLA soldiers are especially hardworking, especially combative, and especially willing to contribute. Their actions deeply moved and respected us. Although the flood caused us great losses, the soldiers gave all the teachers of our second middle school a profound lesson in teacher ethics!" Moved into action. There are many mosquitoes in the disaster area, so the teachers provided mosquito repellents for the soldiers and brought televisions, setting up satellite antennas to solve the problem of the soldiers receiving external information during the disaster relief period to a certain extent. When they saw the soldiers return covered in mud and stinking, the teachers would voluntarily wash their clothes and shoes for them.

Regarding the enthusiasm of the teachers, soldier Zhang Jianguo said that he would turn this care and help into motivation for future flood control and rescue operations, repaying this concern and care with practical actions.

Warm words

"I feel deeply sorry that such a serious flood disaster has occurred in such a beautiful and rich place."

Background of the quote: After the major flood disaster in Tai Ning, it touched the hearts of millions of people, including a French businessman named Jean-Jacques Defay. Mr. Jean-Jacques Defay invested in the construction of the French Country Inn project in Tai Ning in early 2009. During the two years he lived in Tai Ning, he had deeply fallen in love with Tai Ning, his second hometown. Upon hearing the news that Tai Ning suffered from a once-in-a-century flood disaster, he was deeply saddened and donated 150,000 RMB for disaster relief.
