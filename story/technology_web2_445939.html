Seven Steps to Importing Second-hand Printing Machines

by import660288 on 2011-06-14 23:43:56

In recent times, many domestic companies and foreign enterprises have been inquiring about the procedures for handling the import customs clearance of second-hand printing equipment. Some companies, due to their lack of understanding of the procedures and policies for importing second-hand machines, have placed orders abroad on their own initiative or purchased second-hand machines through non-legitimate channels. When the machines arrived at the customs port, they were detained by customs, and only then did these companies try to make up the import procedures; some companies were even discovered after the machines had already been installed and put into use in the factory, forcing them to make up the procedures retroactively; a few second-hand machines, because they did not meet the import conditions, had to be returned even though they had already reached the port. All these situations have caused inconvenience and losses to the companies. Therefore, the author would like to discuss the current procedures and issues to be aware of when introducing second-hand machines.

Step One: Drafting a Project Report or Feasibility Study Report for the Introduction of Second-Hand Machines

Generally speaking, if a single machine is introduced, a project report should be written; if larger equipment or a set of equipment is introduced, a feasibility study report should be prepared. The project report can be concise, while the feasibility study report should be more detailed. However, both reports should include the following basic content:

1. Basic information about the company: including its affiliation, ownership nature, fixed assets, annual output value, profits and taxes, main products, production volume, etc.

2. Reasons for introduction: such as adjusting product structure, adapting to market demand, filling gaps and completing sets, reasons for selection, etc.

3. Market demand: briefly explain the market prospects of introducing this machine and its role in the company.

4. Detailed list of imported equipment: including the name, model, unit, quantity, price, place of origin, implementation date, etc.

5. Investment scale: explain the total estimated investment amount for purchasing second-hand equipment, the foreign exchange quota (in US dollars), which should be basically consistent with the actual situation.

6. Source of funds: explain whether the company is self-funding, receiving grants, or obtaining loans from banks or other means.

7. Implementation date: specifically state the time when the company plans to introduce second-hand equipment.

8. Benefit estimation: expected economic and social benefits.

9. Investment recovery period: explain the time it will take for the company to recover its investment.

10. Conclusion: through technical and economic analysis, draw a conclusion on whether the leasing plan is feasible.

Step Two: Prepare a Condition Report for the Second-Hand Machine

To introduce a second-hand machine, it is necessary to understand all aspects of the machine and write a condition report. The main contents include: the year the machine was manufactured and released, the machine's serial number, in which country and factory it was used, how many years it has been used, how many prints it has produced, whether any accidents have occurred during personalized printing, preferably providing photos of the machine being used in the workshop and the final printed samples. In principle, second-hand machines produced after 1992 should be required. Additionally, it is recommended to buy machines used in European factories, as generally speaking, they are well-maintained, have fewer absolute print counts, and it is advisable to buy world-renowned brands of second-hand machines because they are robust, durable, retain value, offer good service, have high credibility, and ensure the supply of spare parts.

Step Three: Submit the Reports for Review and Approval by the Relevant Authorities

The company should take the two reports to the relevant authorities and the Economic and Trade Commission of each province, municipality, and autonomous region for review and approval. Foreign-invested enterprises should submit them to the Foreign Economic Relations and Trade Commission (Foreign Economic Relations and Trade Department) for review and approval. Some provincial planning commissions also approve such projects.

Step Four: The Procedure for Introducing Second-Hand Machines is Consistent with the Import Procedures for Mechanical and Electrical Products

After the enterprise's superior department reviews and approves the company's project report or feasibility study report for the introduction of equipment, the company takes the project report or feasibility study report and the approval to the mechanical and electrical product import and export office under the provinces, municipalities, autonomous regions, planned cities, special economic zones, relevant ministries and commissions of the State Council, and national enterprise groups to obtain and fill out the application form for the import of mechanical and electrical products, affix the company's official seal, then the mechanical and electrical product import and export office signs an agreement opinion, stamps the import-specific seal, and submits it to the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade's mechanical and electrical product import and export office to handle the import procedures.

Whether it is a domestic enterprise or a foreign-invested enterprise, when importing second-hand printing machines, the mechanical and electrical product import and export office of the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade will send a letter seeking opinions to the mechanical and electrical product import and export office of the General Administration of Press and Publication. After the General Administration of Press and Publication replies agreeing, the mechanical and electrical product import and export office of the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations and Trade will conduct the final review and approval.

Step Five: Conduct Business Negotiations and Sign Purchase Contracts

After handling the import procedures for mechanical and electrical products, domestic enterprises without import and export rights can ask foreign trade companies with import and export rights to negotiate with the company and sign purchase contracts with foreign merchants. Then, the company can proceed to customs to handle the product entry procedures.

When selecting a foreign trade company, the enterprise should choose authoritative companies that are familiar with import and export business, provide good service, and charge low fees. Enterprises should select second-hand machine agents that have good credibility, comprehensive services, and highly qualified engineering technicians.

Step Six: Handle the Project Confirmation Letter

After signing the contract with the foreign merchant, the enterprise should register the contract copy with the mechanical and electrical product import and export office of the enterprise's superior department and go to the Economic and Trade Commission of the province, municipality, or autonomous region to obtain the project confirmation letter.

Step Seven: Handle Customs Procedures

After going through the above procedures, the enterprise holding the project report or feasibility study report and the approval, the application form for the import of mechanical and electrical products, the purchase contract, and the project confirmation letter goes to customs to handle customs procedures. Customs levies taxes on imported second-hand machines based on 60% of the price of new machines, resulting in relatively high tax amounts, and the specific tax amount is somewhat vague.

When the machinery arrives at the port, the enterprise must present the delivery order, packing list, invoice, purchase contract, registration form for the import of mechanical and electrical products, and tax collection certificate provided by the foreign merchant to declare and pick up the goods at the designated customs. After the machinery is delivered to the enterprise, the commercial inspection department should be invited to inspect the goods and issue a commercial inspection report. The enterprise itself should also compare the contract and conduct a comprehensive and meticulous inspection, checking whether there are any missing, damaged, or defective parts of the machinery and components, so as to claim compensation in a timely manner.

The above is the basic work procedure and several important issues to be aware of when introducing second-hand printing equipment. For some detailed issues, enterprises can consult the relevant departments for specific advice.
