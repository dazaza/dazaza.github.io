by bn9a6l5x on 2012-02-29 17:03:47

The head coach of the US national team, Klinsmann, stated in an interview that the US will not fear Italy. He revisited his old stomping grounds where he once achieved great success in Italy and offered his own interpretation of the European Championship. At 1 AM Beijing time on March 1, the Italian national team will face off against the US national team at home. Both teams are currently actively preparing in Genoa. As the head coach of the US national team, Klinsmann played for Inter Milan and Sampdoria in the Italian Serie A, thus attracting particular attention. From 1989 to 1992, Klinsmann played for Inter Milan, forming the "German Trains" with Matthaus and Bremer, rivaling AC Milan's "Dutch Three Musketeers." They won the 1989 Italian Super Cup and the 1991 UEFA Cup. In 1997, Klinsmann also played for Sampdoria.

Although Klinsmann revisited his old place this time, as the head coach of the US national team, his main focus is still on the match. Klinsmann said: "I am glad Prandelli accepted my friendly match request. The game against Italy will help us improve our team. In June, we will start the qualifiers for the 2014 World Cup. The game against Italy will be special, I look forward to an intense game, and of course, I hope the stadium will be full with a good atmosphere."

Despite Klinsmann's humble words, he did not show weakness to the Italian team: "We didn't come here to passively take hits; we came here to win. On one hand, we have many players who are absolute starters in European leagues; they are all high-level players. On the other hand, although the level of Major League Soccer in the United States is not as good as in Europe, it improves significantly every year." 2012 is the European Championship year, and as a German, Klinsmann also expressed his views on the European Championship: "The German team is very strong, but I'm not sure if they are stronger than the Italian team. In fact, Italy also has a powerful team, and Prandelli has done an excellent job. The German team has better continuity in recent years. At the same time, we must pay attention to the Netherlands and France. Of course, we need to see who can defeat Spain."

When talking about the future, Klinsmann did not give a clear answer: "I am now only thinking about how to lead the US team into the 2014 World Cup. As for the future, we'll see." Of course, he did not forget Inter Milan, which once brought him glory: "I played for Inter Milan for three years, and their current situation is not good. Wishing them good luck."
