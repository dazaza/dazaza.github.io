International domain name real-name system is implemented, and unverified "blacklist" will stop resolution.

by nuo01 on 2011-07-04 10:05:45

According to relevant reports, on July 1st, the well-known user experience and product community website UCD was blocked from access due to lack of registration. When visiting the UCD page, it showed "Hello! The website you are visiting has been blocked because it is not registered!" After the webmaster's inquiry, it was found that the UCD domain name resolution was still normal. Since it was originally created by Alipay planner Bai Ya and once used a filing number called "Jing ICP Bei 06006822", which was registered as "Bai Ya - User-Centered Design", so in this domain name authenticity review, vulnerabilities were detected, leading to the domain name being closed for access.

Recently, many users and webmasters have received email reminders from registrars with an ultimatum: to timely verify the real information of the domain name, otherwise, the "blacklist" websites will be stopped from resolving. From the case of UDC being blocked from access due to lack of registration, it can be seen that all registrars have already taken action. A famous national IDC service provider China Nuowang reminded users to log in to domain management as soon as possible and submit personal real information to avoid affecting the normal operation of the website.

On June 15th, the MIIT issued a document stating that to strictly implement domain name registration, all international domain names require submission of real-name certification materials. Users must submit real-name materials before June 30th. The MIIT will also conduct a comprehensive investigation of international domain names. If real-name certification is not submitted, they will all be placed in the "sandbox" waiting for disposal or the domain name access will be blocked, and parsing will be stopped on June 30th, 2011, freezing the domain name. It is speculated that this blockage of UCD website access may be related to the real-name certification of international domain names previously promoted by the MIIT.
