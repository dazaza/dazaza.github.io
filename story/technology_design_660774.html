【Comic Introduction】: "Iron Fist vs Steel Fist" is truly the best delinquent youth comic. Its fame not only lies in its early appearance and real reflection of delinquent youth life, but also in its exquisite artwork. "Iron Fist vs Steel Fist" caused a sensation in Japan as early as the 80s and has been adapted into games multiple times, remaining popular to this day. In "Iron Fist vs Steel Fist", the life of delinquent youths has changed from pure violence as depicted by Fujita Koji or Hayashi Tetsu, showing more of their ordinariness. Compared to the glint of knives and blood, there is more humor in daily life here. The two long-time rivals here - the protagonist Iron Fist's Maeda Takatoshi and the rice merchant's Shimabukuro - each have their own principles of life, and they also respectively influence the people around them with their principles. Thus, in Morita Masanori's delinquent comics, there are also the following unwritten strange rules - even delinquents must wear school uniforms, and they must be worn neatly no matter what time it is. Although this can bring some unnecessary troubles, such as easily mistaking companions when hundreds of people fight because everyone wears the same clothes, but rules are rules, and cannot be broken; when preparing to beat someone up, you must first ask the guy to take off his clothes and trousers, fold them into a square and put them aside, and only wear underwear to be beaten (this is probably to prevent blood from splashing on the clothes, giving the beaten person some face), regardless of winter or summer; when there are abandoned cola cans and other garbage on the ground, you must first clean the site, pick them all up and throw them into the trash can before doing serious things like fighting; delinquent youths must speak coarsely, using bad guys' dedicated language, for example, saying "you little brat" instead of "you"; smoking is a must because it represents the demeanor of adult men and is also the symbol of delinquent youths. However, smoked cigarette butts must be extinguished and placed in ashtrays, and even if there is no ashtray within dozens of meters, they must not be thrown on the ground carelessly. This is not only to maintain the demeanor of delinquent youths but also for everyone's good to avoid fires; when the opponent has an emergency, you must let the opponent go to handle it, and help the enemy when possible so that everyone can fight on a fair standard line; even enemies must be helped when they are in trouble. Even the number one fool and incompetent Nakata Heiji in "Iron Fist vs Steel Fist" will occasionally do so reluctantly. As long as they are leaders, they must learn skills, whether boxing or judo, it must be healthy sports, but the premise is that it must be beneficial to being a delinquent youth. How about that? Strange enough? Although it seems exaggerated, don't think it's purely a comedy comic. In fact, from any perspective, it is still a work with considerable authenticity.
