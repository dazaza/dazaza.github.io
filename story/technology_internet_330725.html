Why Western Women Rarely Marry Chinese Men

By Xu Shuguang (Sina Weibo), Chairman of GreenTree Inn and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

I have been to many Western countries and met people from different races and social classes. One very noticeable and strange finding is: the vast majority of cross-border romances involve petite Chinese girls standing beside tall, strong foreign men, and we rarely see gentle Chinese men who can captivate full-bodied, dazzling foreign women. This reflects a fact: Western women are rarely willing to marry Chinese men. Why is that? Friends who have stayed at our GreenTree Inn once gave me an answer: prejudice. Yes! This definitely exists, for example, they say Chinese men are not funny enough, not sexy enough, not gentlemanly enough, and not handsome enough, etc.

I believe one of the biggest reasons is that whether it's Chinese or Westerners, when choosing a partner, they usually subconsciously set the image and qualifications of their partner, which are those traits considered to be beneficial for reproduction, such as a healthy physique that makes one feel all organs are in good condition, this is the most basic standard for biological mate selection; highly talented and high IQ individuals are beneficial for having intelligent offspring and can also improve the quality of life to a certain extent; abundant material foundation can provide high-quality living conditions and growth environment for offspring, etc. Of course, these are just general situations, special preferences of some people are excluded.

First, comparing Chinese men with Western men. The physical build and constitution of Chinese men may not match up to Western men, who grow up on genetically modified, high-protein, multi-nutrient foods from a young age, while most Chinese were just barely out of poverty at that time; the delicate beauty or clever intelligence of Chinese women fits the taste of Western men, some foreigners even take pride in marrying a Chinese wife; but for Western women, besides the fact that their build isn't necessarily smaller than Chinese men, the biggest obstacle might be the self-esteem of Chinese men. For Chinese men, to marry a superior species from a developed country, the first thing to consider should be the issue of compatibility. Even though we all know that superiority in marriage won't lead to long-term happiness, marriages without any sense of superiority can easily make men feel frustrated. Additionally, we must mention that China's reform and opening-up has not been going on for too long, the mentality of looking up to the West and being obsequious still lingers. The "good" image of Western men has also been deeply rooted. Having a foreign friend would make one proud, and marrying a foreign husband would be like becoming a phoenix. Some also say that university campuses often see female students voluntarily entertaining foreign exchange students, claiming it's for maintaining international friendship, but everyone knows it's about vanity. This is also possible.

Secondly, after talking about instinctual mate selection criteria, national strength and national conditions are also important factors. In recent years, among the migrant worker population, it has become popular to marry Southeast Asian women as wives. The reason is that they cannot afford to get married in the city and find it hard to find girls willing to marry poor young men. However, for women from underdeveloped Southeast Asian countries, earning only 2000 yuan per month could already make them consider them as "big shots". The influx of women from Vietnam, Myanmar, Laos, etc., into China indicates what? China's economic level is higher than those Southeast Asian countries, so women prefer places with better conditions. Similarly, women from developed Western countries like the US, UK, and France would find it difficult to marry into less developed countries. Although our Chinese men are good, marrying someone means marrying all his social relations. To integrate into a lifestyle and complex society different from developed countries is also a big challenge. Talking about national conditions, for example, we are a large country that follows traditional thoughts, traditional concepts are still strong. Additionally, China has risen not too long ago, and our backbone has just straightened up. The gap between countries is reflected in confidence, and men lacking confidence naturally lack the manliness they should have. They can't compete physically or in terms of strength, at best achieving parity.

Thirdly, for a long time, the Western world has greatly distorted and vilified China's image, creating a very bad national impression. Naturally, the brand image of Chinese men is not as good as that of Western men.

I am in business, and from this rather popular topic, I also thought of a question: why do people not buy your products or services but choose others? Why do people not stay at XX Hotel but choose our GreenTree Inn? There are both subjective and objective reasons, and it all comes down to competitiveness. For example, are your products and services better than others? Is the cost-performance ratio higher? Can you make customers feel more value for money? These are all factors to consider.

I wonder what everyone's opinion is.
