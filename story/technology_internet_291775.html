Don't be deceived by unscrupulous merchants: Exposure of eight mobile phones with high refresh rates in recent period

by lgfj10dt on 2009-12-04 11:05:38

The worst fear for those buying mobile phones is purchasing a refurbished one. So-called refurbished phones are when unscrupulous merchants take back some second-hand phones, clean them with chemical liquids, change the casing, and then fit them with batteries, chargers (counterfeit), and packaging to pass them off as new models. These phones look almost identical to brand new ones on the outside, but their internal components are all old. As such, the phone's performance is very unstable. Most importantly, this practice constitutes fraud and severely damages consumer interests. Therefore, today I will showcase 8 models currently still being sold on the market but with a high probability of being refurbished units, serving as a reminder when you buy a phone. Additionally, at the end of the article, I'll introduce some simple ways to identify refurbished phones.

BlackBerry Bold 9000 (specifications, pricing, images, forum)

There's no doubt that the brand with the most refurbished units on the current market is BlackBerry. This is because there are no official releases in China, and most models have been on the market for quite some time. The BlackBerry 9000 is one of the more popular phones under BlackBerry and has many enthusiasts domestically. However, currently, the vast majority of these phones priced below 3000 yuan are refurbished units.

The BlackBerry 9000 features a 2.6-inch screen with a resolution of 480x320 pixels, providing extremely fine display quality. It has an integrated 2-megapixel camera, which, although not as good as the 3.2-megapixel on the BlackBerry 8900, delivers satisfactory results. The BlackBerry 9000 uses the BlackBerry OS v4.6 operating system, with a processor clocked at 624 MHz, paired with 128MB RAM + 1GB ROM memory combination, offering satisfactory program running speeds.

Editor's review:

Since there are no official BlackBerry phones available in China currently, extra caution must be exercised when purchasing a BlackBerry phone. Not only should one beware of merchants selling refurbished phones as new ones, but also be mindful of whether the phone is a "14-day unit" from foreign carriers.
