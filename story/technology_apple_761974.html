iOS User Voice: Make iPhone and iPad More Open

by geekzhang on 2013-05-17 08:56:15

May 17, Beijing time - According to foreign media reports, as the Apple Worldwide Developers Conference approaches, last week, the American tech website ReadWriteWeb conducted a reader survey on what features of iOS7 they are most looking forward to. The results showed that iOS users hope that iOS devices will be more open.The following is an abstract of the content of this article:There are still several weeks before the Apple Worldwide Developers Conference from June 10 to 14. It can now be certain that many iPhone and iPad users are looking forward to seeing fresh product designs and more open customization experiences at this year's developers conference.Last week, ReadWriteWeb conducted a reader survey on the features of iOS7 that readers were most looking forward to. The survey results revealed two mainstream views of the readers: hoping that iOS devices will be more customizable, and hoping that iPhones and iPads will have widgets similar to the Android style.When asked if iOS devices should develop into more customizable devices, 64% of respondents answered affirmatively, while only 28% of respondents believed that Apple should continue to strictly control its products to ensure excellent user experience.Based on previous experience, strict control over products is indeed an effective way to ensure excellent user experience of products. However, Apple shouldn't ignore the fact that Google has achieved remarkable results in improving the user experience of Android without sacrificing the traditional flexibility provided by Android products.For years, Apple fans may have been mocking the clumsy imitation of Android, believing that Android has many areas that need improvement. This was true in the past. However, Android has now gradually improved and become a more powerful competitor to iOS than ever before. Apple is facing increasing pressure to loosen its control over iOS and give more control to users. It cannot yet be guaranteed whether Apple will do so (if Apple does so, there is no doubt that this change will be gradual), but the demand of users seems already very clear.Despite jailbreaking, iOS remains popular.This desire for greater control is reflected in the proliferation of unauthorized jailbroken Apple products that remove the restrictions Apple imposes on how users use iOS. According to data from the website of Jay Freeman, the founder of Cydia, although the latest version of iOS has not yet been jailbroken, there are currently at least 30 million jailbroken iOS devices on the market. Indeed, compared to the more than 500 million iOS devices sold by Apple so far, this number is negligible, but the demand for jailbroken products seems to be increasing continuously.At the beginning of last year, when the iOS6 jailbreak tool evasi0n was launched, it became very popular. Not only did the hosting site of evasi0n collapse due to excessive traffic, but also the Cydia app store collapsed due to excessive downloads. Within just four days, 7 million iOS devices were jailbroken. To date, evasi0n is the most popular iOS jailbreak tool.When we talk about this jailbreak phenomenon, Android supporters might unanimously say: Google's mobile operating system has been able to provide certain functions for years, why not choose Google's products? Consumers have an option, which is to switch to Android devices. What they said is not without reason. Many functions that users achieve by jailbreaking their iPhones and iPads are indeed native functions on Android devices or at least applications that can be downloaded from Google Play.In our survey, we asked readers to list the features they would most like to see in iOS7. Among all the replies, the second most used word was customization. Other relatively common answers included improving iOS multitasking, speeding up access to settings, multiple user profiles, and improving the notification center, etc.In general, the feature most requested by the surveyed readers was the use of widgets on the home screen. Users of other operating systems may be very familiar with icons displaying real-time data. We have also seen such icon displays in at least one preview version of iOS7. Clearly, many users are tired of the weather app icons on iOS, which have been using the same sun icon since the launch of the iPhone in 2007.A total of 734 people participated in this survey.
