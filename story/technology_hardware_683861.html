I asked him, "You have to study and take care of your family while growing strawberries to support the family. Can you handle all this pressure?" Xiao Xu, who had been smiling all along, finally fell silent. After a while, he started laughing again: "I've already adapted to living under pressure, I'll adjust myself well."

Xiao Xu says he's very happy.

More than ten years of rheumatoid arthritis has deformed all of his mother's joints, and her left eye has gone blind due to the side effects of long-term medication. It's the middle of winter, and her arthritis symptoms are more severe. She even needs help with going to the bathroom. Xu Longkun is helping his mother comb her hair (video screenshot).

The small shack by the wall is gone. By the strawberry fields, Xu Longkun used advertising cloth, plastic sheets, and bamboo pieces to build a shed of about twenty or thirty square meters, which is larger than before. On the day I interviewed him, it was very windy, and the plastic sheet on the shed was flapping loudly. The temperature outside was less than 5 degrees Celsius, and inside the shed, it was at most six or seven degrees Celsius. Xiao Xu said, conditions are definitely not as good as back in Jiande, but he's gotten used to it.

The strawberries aren't growing too well.

The new home is still a plastic shed.

On the advertising cloth that Xu Longkun used to build the shed, there were two lines of artistic words: "Do interesting things, don't give up, don't abandon" and "WE CAN, WE DO" (I can, I do). This might be coincidental, but perhaps it's destiny.

After taking a break from school for two years, Xiao Xu found studying this semester a bit challenging, and his grades weren't ideal. He plans to focus on his studies this year and reduce the area of strawberry cultivation to avoid neglecting both. However, his biggest wish is still to find a relatively good hospital for treating rheumatoid arthritis so his mother can suffer less.

Xu Longkun is now in his second year at Zhejiang University Ningbo Institute of Technology. He looks like a sunny young man, dark and thin, slightly shy, and always smiles before speaking. Upon meeting, he told me a bunch of good news: the four acres of strawberries have ripened, they no longer live in the old small shack, his mother has been brought to be near him, and kind-hearted people have donated money to him.

Now, the strawberries are ripe, but it seems there aren't many red fruits. After repeated inquiries, Xiao Xu said that because he couldn't manage everything, this year's strawberry growth wasn't too good. In the summer when he planted the strawberries, he borrowed over forty thousand yuan from others. He estimates recovering the cost won't be a problem, but how much profit there will be is uncertain. He looked very embarrassed about not doing well with the farming.

On New Year's Day, Kuaibao Time broadcasted a video by Zhu Zhenshan, featuring a student from Jiande named Xu Longkun. He took a two-year break from school to self-study while growing strawberries and taking care of his paralyzed mother.

"At least let my mom be able to take care of herself and move around a little. I will try my best to treat her until I am no longer able."

Xu's mother is paralyzed, and his father suffers from chronic hepatitis and cannot work too hard. Therefore, the responsibility of taking care of his mother almost entirely falls on his son. Xu Longkun usually brings necessities home after school, feeds his mother before returning to school. On weekends, he works in the fields. Since there is only one bed in the plastic shed, after finishing his farm work, Xiao Xu has to rush back to school to sleep. The distance between where he farms and his school is ten kilometers.

Xu's mother said that sometimes she really thought about giving up her life so as not to drag down her son. But Xiao Xu always consoled her: "Mom, don't think too much. Being able to have our family together is good. You just talking to me is meaningful. These two years, I may tighten my belt a bit and not spend money on you, but one day, I will earn money to cure your illness."

He said that since last summer, after the media reported his story, society has donated over thirty thousand yuan to him, enough for his tuition for two years; teachers at the school also said they would collectively buy his strawberries. "Sometimes when I think about it, I am still happy. I have received care from society, I have my parents, I have a complete family." He is very grateful to them, "Everyone is different. I feel I should cherish what I have now."

However, when I went to the place where he grows strawberries - Dayan Village, Dongqianhu Town, Yinzhou District, and met his family, I realized that the situation was far worse than he described.

After the movie aired, many viewers were moved by Xu Longkun. This video was shot in the summer of 2010, and now it's the season when strawberries mature. How are his strawberries doing? Has his life improved? Is his mother's condition any better? I decided to go to Ningbo to meet him.
