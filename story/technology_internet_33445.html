Professional QQ chat operators appear in Luoyang; psychological counseling for elder women at 50 yuan per hour

by netbeta on 2007-11-06 14:13:43

Luoyang Evening News reported that can a person "plan" a good mood for others through QQ chat? A 53-year-old auntie in our city, to prove that "I can do it too", founded a "Good Mood Planning Company" at home during her spare time. She helps people improve their mood through online conversations.

However, anyone who receives her "planning" on QQ must pay 50 yuan per hour - this is quite rare!

The "Good Mood Planning Company" appears on the Internet

A few days ago, I accidentally saw such an advertisement post on a Luoyang forum. The title was "Do you want to have a good mood?" The post said:

"In recent years, I have often seen reports of college students or doctoral students committing suicide. I believe they must have a lot of troubles that need to be expressed but cannot be spoken. If they had a place to express themselves and someone to guide them before, then the tragedy might have been avoided."

"The purpose of the 'Good Mood Planning Company' is: to patiently listen to your stories, share your troubles, and ensure that your privacy will not be disclosed. We guarantee that you will come with troubles and leave with happiness. Please call 1383847×××× as soon as possible, we are always ready to welcome you. You can also communicate online, we will plan a good mood for you..."

At the end of the post, there was also the "company address: Henan Luoyang". We became very interested and called the poster's phone number, claiming that we were feeling down. The woman who answered the phone introduced herself as Ms. Qu, the "boss" of the "Good Mood Planning Company". She enthusiastically said that she could help us through QQ chat, but it would cost 50 yuan per hour.

When we heard that QQ chat would also be charged, we became even more curious: what exactly does this company do?

The "Company Boss" turns out to be an old auntie

On the morning of April 4th, we met Ms. Qu in a residential building in a state-owned enterprise family courtyard in Jianxi District. Unexpectedly, she turned out to be an old auntie over fifty years old with gray hair. After we revealed our identity, she looked a little flustered, but quickly calmed down.

Ms. Qu said that she is 53 years old and will retire in two years. After working in the unit for several decades, she never felt successful in her career, so she always wanted to explore other ways to use her talents and prove to people that "I can do it too".

Since last year when her family bought a computer, she gradually had an idea: modern people face a lot of social pressure, inevitably leading to various troubles. Long-term accumulation in the heart will affect physical and mental health. So why not use her talent for listening, guiding, and keeping secrets, and use QQ chat tools to start a psychological counseling company online to help others improve their mood?

Subsequently, she began to implement her creative idea and named the "company" as the "Good Mood Planning Company", posting advertisements on many domestic forums. Because she was worried about people around her having "strange views", she did not disclose any information in real life.

People who chat with her on QQ must pay 50 yuan per hour

Since last year, the "Good Mood Planning Company", which opened without a business license, has attracted the attention of many netizens domestically. Some people questioned in follow-up posts, while others really contacted her because of their own troubles.

For those who question randomly, Ms. Qu was initially angry, but later thought it through, "Just do my own thing"; For those who call, she will clearly tell them the service content and charging standards. If the other party agrees, she will add them as QQ friends and chat with them via QQ in her spare time at night.

So far, more than 10 netizens have accepted Ms. Qu's "good mood planning", including people from Luoyang and other places; the chat time is mostly scheduled for 1 hour, and the chat content revolves around the troubles in the other party's work and life, "the specific details absolutely cannot be disclosed".

During the chat, due to her slow typing speed, Ms. Qu generally uses the QQ audio function to have voice chats with the other party. However, she has never used the QQ video function because "I don't want them to see the conversationalist, to avoid adding psychological burden", and most of the time she listens carefully, interjecting not much, because in her view, "what these people really need is an absolutely safe space for confession only".

"Most people's emotions are relatively agitated at first, but as they talk, they will slowly calm down, so my handling method is still quite effective." Ms. Qu said.

Ms. Qu said that most people will transfer the "mood planning fee" to her account in advance according to her pre-explanation requirements; some people proposed to pay after chatting, but disappeared afterwards, making her unable to find them. Regarding this, she was not angry because her real purpose was just to prove her ability, and both she and her husband have jobs and do not rely on this for a living.

Currently, the operation effect of the "Good Mood Planning Company" cannot be considered ideal, and the future development trend is not visible, but Ms. Qu still optimistically stated that she would continue to run it.

"After retirement, if my psychological counseling level improves, I may rent a storefront, get a business license, and open a real psychological counseling company, which is my highest ideal." Ms. Qu seriously said.

The competent authority: Encourage new things

Regarding the "Good Mood Planning Company" solely created by Ms. Qu, a person in charge of the Registration Division of the Luoyang Municipal Administration for Industry and Commerce said that strictly speaking in legal terms, since Ms. Qu adopted a charging method during QQ chat, it constitutes a business activity, and she should obtain a business license and pay taxes. However, the industry and commerce department currently does not intend to take mandatory measures against her, but instead encourages her to a certain extent.

Why? The person in charge explained that first, this behavior is after all a new phenomenon derived from the virtual network, and it is basically beneficial and harmless to society; second, Ms. Qu at most belongs to micro-profit operations, and is far from being significant; third, there is currently a lack of detailed investigation rules for similar online business activities, and daily supervision also has certain technical difficulties.

In view of this, the person in charge indicated that society should encourage the growth of this new phenomenon. However, if it develops into a physical company in the future, it must legally obtain a business license and pay taxes.
