The development of financial support for forestry faces four constraints. Currently, forest insurance in the entire city is still in its infancy, with a single variety of forest insurance, no other types established except fire insurance. When natural disasters such as freezing or pestilence occur, or when there is human theft, the value of collateral is difficult to guarantee.

Firstly, Article 3 and Article 10 of the "Trial Measures for the Administration of Forest Rights Mortgage Loans in Sichuan Province" impose relatively strict restrictions on the use of forest rights mortgage loans, which do not meet current demands. At the same time, the "Measures" restrict the use of forest rights mortgage loans to forestry and agriculture-related production and operation, limiting the development of forest rights mortgage loans.

Secondly, the valuation of forest rights is exaggerated, far exceeding the acquisition price of forest rights. The key method used for forest rights asset evaluation is the "present value of harvest method", and the usage right evaluation adopts the "annuity capitalization method". Financial departments lack professional forest rights value evaluators, so they cannot raise any objections to the authenticity of the evaluation, and can only heavily discount the results from intermediary department evaluations.

Thirdly, the supervision and disposal of forest rights are quite challenging. The disposal and liquidation of collateral are relatively difficult. The current transfer mechanism is not well-established, and there is no forest rights transfer market yet. Once borrowers are unable to repay loans on time, banks will face difficulties in disposing of the collateral.

Fourthly, mechanisms for risk diversification and transfer have not been established yet. Illegal logging and forest fires are significant potential risks for forest rights mortgage loans. Once forestry production encounters natural disasters after the issuance of forest rights mortgage loans, not only will forest companies and farmers suffer economic losses, but it will also affect the repayment of bank loans.
