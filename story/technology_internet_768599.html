A divination for the big sellers of Taobao: "Escaping from Taobao" has truly begun.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:58:11

350. The 19 billion yuan Double Eleven shopping festival was a historic breakthrough, bringing both joy and sorrow.

Double Eleven is not a day of joy for everyone. After all, there are millions of sellers on Taobao, and only a small fraction have reached sales above the hundred-million-yuan level, let alone the billion-yuan mark. Data increasingly shows the threat posed by offline "sky soldiers" to "Taobao natives" (Taobao brands and stores). If one day ZARA arrives, or designers from LVMH, Richemont, Printemps decide to quit their jobs and start original designs on Taobao instead of celebrities creating brands, this could be far more reliable. Would traffic be allocated to them? These represent two possible threats to "Taobao natives". If it aligns with platform development needs, neither technology nor traffic would be an obstacle.

This year's Double Eleven, beyond the B2C platforms, many large sellers also heavily invested in offline advertising. This is a critical turning point where "Taobao brands" began their true "de-Taobao" action - removing the "Taobao" label. Similar to the I Ching’s Ascending Hexagram, which states: “Gathering upward is called ascending.” The clustering effect has become a selection barrier, with too many redundant products and low differentiation. A step upward must be taken to create differences; if one does not move forward, they will be forced by traditional brands, increasing marketing channels, and the platform itself.

A few rejoice while many others grieve; among thousands of arguments, only a few truly proceed. The vast array of Taobao sellers varies greatly. Firstly, their attributes differ, and the same path isn't suitable for everyone. Jizhi roughly divides Taobao sellers into six categories:

Trading Type

In the past era of chasing five crowns, explorers like Exploration, 1981, Junwei men's wear shops, and Shuangsheng'er, Xiaomi Chongzi, Angel City women's wear shops emerged. Now some focus on B-stores, some have reached two to four gold crowns, while others remain stagnant or have disappeared altogether. Some have started original brands, some do private labeling, and some change their logos.

Back then, stories of Lemon Green Tea were still being passed around as myths. Photographs were generally less formal, especially for men's wear, with detail pages having minimal embellishments, and some even outright stealing images. Generally, these stores offered a wide range of SKUs, frequent new product launches, no need to hide brand names, and credit ratings that grew rapidly.

Keywords such as original orders, foreign trade, leftover goods, follow-up orders, premium items, along with popular brand keywords like JJ, ZARA, Lives, Lee, NIKE, AD, CK, G-star, Diesel, Burberry, etc., plus style or design keywords, particularly mass-market basic styles, were commonly used. Shops specializing in relatively niche brands from Korea, Japan, the US, Europe, labeled by style, also performed well. Selling to places like Hong Kong was not uncommon.

In this period, having a source of supply was everything; one piece could sell for the price of a package. Geographically divided, apart from the Jiangsu-Zhejiang-Shanghai bloc, areas like Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Qingdao (Jimo), Xiamen (Putian), etc., benefited from industrial clusters, giving them a natural advantage.

This type of store makes up a large proportion. Tactics keep changing, and traffic acquisition becomes progressively harder. They face issues in management, technology, and marketing. Those who haven’t transitioned to Tmall and continue to focus on goods rather than products naturally find it increasingly difficult. Categories that are hard to transform, those who stubbornly stick to C-stores, see their previous advantages gradually losing effectiveness. Acquiring new customers becomes harder, reducing SKU counts or operating multiple stores may not apply.

For those continuing to deal in goods and sticking with C-stores, there are two main directions:

One is to maintain price points, focusing on category and CRM, since some categories are weak in branding;

Two is to emphasize style and quality, shifting towards buyer-type, tag-based operations. Many niche brands haven't entered the domestic market yet, and if taste and vision gain recognition, that’s something others can't take away.

Buyer Type

Dedicated Taobao-style offerings have already become saturated, whether through Alibaba, factories, or wholesale markets, there is too much redundancy. Buyers represent a significant breakthrough. However, buyers also fall into different directions, with two extremes:

Premium, high-end, top-tier: "Too elite," prices are too high for the average person, but the existing circle remains intact.

Mass-priced with wide SKU ranges: Hando represents this group, similar to ZARA, playing with supply chain operations, not so simple.

The most important aspects for buyers are style and price, followed by model and quality.

Style first occupies a position, not just a location. With Hando already established, trying other Korean styles or trends like Han-style, Japanese-style, American-style, etc., becomes difficult. Differences must be made in models and quality.

Jizhi once said: Labelization is the main direction of the future. Using the entire brand to occupy a style-based label, including product design, store visuals, copywriting, keywords, etc. tone. Beyond that, optimizing supply structures and reducing redundant costs in fine-tuned operations.

Product Type

Liebo is representative of product-oriented types. Liebo has almost become an idol for niche markets, though its performance during this year's Double Eleven was unsatisfactory.

With such a large number of buyers, there will always be those who resonate. This is a unique advantage in China, unmatched elsewhere. In China, Taobao can amplify individuality, but it can also scatter it.

Measured by the "He-Lo Brand Imprint Method," Liebo's brand imprint is not yet deep enough.

If we categorize styles into mass-market basics, personalized mass-market, niche mass-market, and niche personalized styles, Liebo definitely falls into the latter category. Many girls buy Liebo but rarely wear it.

In terms of traffic, conversion rates, and unit prices, even if they aren't inferior, the biggest problem with women's clothing lies in "wear rate."

Product-oriented brands are the most capable of competing with offline brands. The core issue right now is not about increasing selling prices but enhancing value perception. Setting high prices in offline stores is a clumsy method.

Jizhi once said: 1. Move advertisements offline; 2. Create "big single items" (i.e., classic pieces) that allow customers wearing them to spread positive word-of-mouth.

Sidelining Type

Physical store owners, primarily in the 3C category, those selling gray-market phones, were early adopters of so-called O2O, communicating online and transacting offline, not just on Taobao, but also on several IT vertical portals.

Additionally, people with special channels casually open stores, selling if possible, otherwise disregarding the outcome. Trial samples, counter samples, branded promotional gifts make up an important part of their inventory.

Some pregnant women and housewives open stores out of leisure considerations, mostly to pass time.

These stores are usually simpler: selling phones and computers involves directly using parameters and reviews from sites like ZOL, PChome, IT168, though some also drive traffic.

The most comfortable type might be gray-market phones. While some cosmetics have zero cost, most don't sell in volume, with too many B2C beauty brands and original Taobao cosmetic brands. These sellers may not fully enjoy the benefits of e-commerce but can suffer from traditional channel disadvantages.

Simply dealing in transparently priced items like phones, computers, routers, cosmetics ultimately isn't a viable solution. Operating retail, accessories, warranty services, guidance together is the correct approach. Transitioning from buying goods to selling peace of mind and results.

Traditional Type

Traditional brands doing Tmall generally fall into a few categories: clearing inventory, occupying a position, recruiting agents, dividing products, sub-brands, O2O. The first three are common, the last three rare. Naturally, the developmental orientation is for the last three to become the norm.

The performances of Haier, Coolpad today, along with Lingzhi Group consistently making headlines, and many later-rising categories, will inevitably accelerate the pace of traditional brands.

Not for any other reason, the ones truly influencing traditional leaders might not be Jack Ma or other e-commerce players, but rather old rivals who have embraced, scaled, and excelled in the game.

Now, half of Tmall's doors are closed. Coming early is less advantageous than coming at the right moment. Now is the time when technical barriers are at their lowest.

Empty-handed Type

Among Taobao's millions of stores, some are auxiliary, even just for fun.

Some are branch stores opened by large B and large C players; some引流stores operated by medium B and medium C players; some practice empty-hand tactics.

Two major types of empty-hand tactics: (non-famous brands) distribution, overseas purchasing (like Amazon).

These stores rely solely on patience and integrity.

Price Equilibrium Point

Premium pricing is a concern, but premium pricing is actually false; it's merely a manifestation. The fundamental goal is to expand share and thereby increase profits. In the "Three States of Dao", profit is the ultimate result, and pure premium pricing does not equate to high profit. There are three main paths to increase profit: raising prices, expanding markets, compressing costs.

Raising prices alone is suicidal; expanding markets involves maintaining old customers and developing new ones, balancing traffic and CRM; compressing costs is a holistic matter, and compressing costs on products is also fatal.

Jizhi once said: There is a balance point between high profit and large sales volume. Due to China's massive population base, most still feel that traditional retail prices are expensive, while e-commerce starts with being cheap (convenient + cheap), and modern people have adapted to and accepted this.

Take an example: A unit price of 100 yuan with a capacity of 100 million equals 10 billion yuan. If the unit price is 500 yuan, the capacity might only be 10 million, totaling 5 billion yuan. Most Chinese consumers use price ceilings as their consumption decision basis.

Moreover, the overall environment of Taobao, akin to visiting an offline department store where Nike, Giordano, Hangzhou-style clothing, 1.5-discount Valentino, etc., are mixed together, even Hermes might not fetch a high price. This is a platform environment issue, not something achieved overnight. First, value must be maximized, differences created, and groundedness maintained.

Jizhi, despite his lack of talent, plans to soon write a series of articles based on the "He-Lo Brand Imprint Method" on how to create differences, gain trust, maximize value, enhance recognition, and build strong brands.
