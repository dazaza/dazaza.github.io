The official version of iOS 7 is finally here! Attached is the download link.

by anonymous on 2013-09-24 21:19:56

At around 1 o'clock this morning, the official version of iOS 7 began to be pushed out. Many people posted screenshots of the update on social media platforms like WeChat and Weibo. Due to the large number of downloads, many users experienced repeated upgrade failures, taking three or four hours to successfully update. If you're not in a rush, it might be better to download the update later in the day when the success rate will likely be higher.

The installation package is approximately 752MB. For those with slower internet speeds, over-the-air (OTA) upgrades could be challenging. Here are two alternative methods: using iTunes for the update, which may be more reliable even though it’s a bit more complicated.

Apple has released iTunes 11.1, and you need to upgrade to this version before proceeding. Follow these steps to upgrade to iOS 7:

After connecting your device, click "Check for Update" in the version section, and iTunes will automatically download and install iOS 7;

You can also manually find the installation package corresponding to your device, download it locally, then hold down Shift (Option for Mac) while clicking Restore in iTunes, selecting the downloaded firmware to perform the recovery and upgrade.

Of course, before upgrading via iTunes, it's best to back up your data to prevent any issues that would allow you to roll back if necessary.

Summary of new features in the official release of iOS 7:

1. A completely new Control Center. The new control center allows users to adjust basic settings, including WiFi, Bluetooth, screen brightness, etc., and even includes a flashlight option. Additionally, iOS 7 brings a new unlock interface and dynamic weather backgrounds.

2. Multitasking and new gesture operations. In iOS 7, users can switch applications by swiping left or right, and any program pause or exit can be completed through simple gestures.

3. Safari Browser. To align with the flat design of iOS 7, Apple redesigned Safari, making it flatter. Safari now uses 3D web tabs, each with its own overview display (improved favorites), supports full-screen display, intelligent search functions, and adds parental controls and iCloud Keychain functionality.

4. AirDrop. Simply put, iOS users can share files between multiple devices. After selecting the relevant files, they can be directly sent to specified individuals within the network. This feature is only supported on the iPhone 5, iPad 4, iPad mini, and iPod touch 5.

5. Added iTunes Radio streaming music service.

6. Wi-Fi calling. Under iOS 7, users can make voice calls via wireless networks, with more stable signals and less chance of disconnection.

7. App Store purchase improvements. Under iOS 7, Apple's App Store can automatically update apps, and users can search for programs being used nearby based on their geographical location.

8. New camera features. Firstly, the camera app has added Square characteristics and various filters, photos in the album can be automatically categorized by time, and users can share photos to others' "Photo Stream."

9. Official version of Siri. After two years in Beta, Siri is finally official. The interface and voice of Siri have changed under the new system (male voices added). Besides setting the phone through voice commands, Siri has added Bing search functionality.

Finally, regarding the security of iOS 7, if the phone is stolen or lost, the new system's security features allow users to remotely delete data on the phone. Of course, if someone doesn't know the password, the phone cannot be activated.
