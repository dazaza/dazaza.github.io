A Comprehensive Guide to Exploring Hong Kong with Complete Freedom

by hostelhk on 2008-05-29 10:42:16

Hong Kong is a place full of miracles and myths, with an astonishing charm. Amid world-class construction, efficient lifestyles, and fashion-forward entertainment, a strong urban dynamic trend emerges. Searching on the Hong Kong map reveals global cuisines, endless shopping paradise pleasures, and the city's harbor atmosphere blends with its deep cultural connotations, creating a captivating experience that makes people linger. Watching the top-tier Hong Kong Jockey Club races or playing in the large-scale Disney theme park adds to the allure.

If you are traveling freely in Hong Kong without joining a local tour group, here is a suggested five-day itinerary:

**Day 1:** Tsim Sha Tsui, Lan Kwai Fong Night Tour

**Afternoon:** Arrival in Hong Kong.

The sliding roof design of the Hong Kong Cultural Centre has become a landmark of the harbor view. Enjoying Victoria Harbour and Hong Kong Island offers one of the best perspectives.

The Hong Kong Space Museum and Hong Kong Art Museum are distinctive buildings where you can watch a planetarium movie or admire art exhibitions.

**Evening:** Lan Kwai Fong.

Any bar can provide an experience of the vibrant nightlife enjoyed by Hong Kong locals.

**Day 2:** Golden Bauhinia Statue, Ocean Park

**Morning:** Golden Bauhinia Statue, Hong Kong’s Return to China Monument.

Next to the Hong Kong Convention and Exhibition Centre, the flag-raising ceremony at 8:00 am every morning is a popular spot for tourists to take photos.

**Afternoon:** Hong Kong Ocean Park.

Out of ten people from the mainland visiting Hong Kong, nine will go to Ocean Park, and the remaining one who didn’t go will definitely plan to visit next time. Clearly, Ocean Park has become a symbolic attraction in Hong Kong. In October, if you visit Ocean Park, you will experience the largest and most fascinating Halloween Carnival in Asia, known as the "International Ghosts Alliance," which is absolutely thrilling and heart-pounding. If you're timid, after experiencing wave after wave of fright, you'll become bold.

If you decide to join the night carnival, first walk through a time tunnel into a "666-star hotel" with Victorian style, preparing to be unexpectedly scared. In the mystical "Magic Forest," visitors enter a half-awake, half-asleep state within a mirror maze, accepting music, light, and sound effects.

If you're timid, you can participate in daytime activities. Whether you're an adult or a child, you’ll enjoy the hands-on experience of the Halloween scare culture immensely.

**Reminder:** The Ocean Park has signed a "ticket agency" agreement with China Travel Service Holidays and Guangzhou Travel, allowing visitors to purchase tickets in Guangzhou and stay updated on the latest trends at Ocean Park.

Ocean Park daily opening hours: From October 1 to 31, from 10:00 am to 6:00 pm. Night activities are held every Friday, Saturday, and Sunday evening from October 1 to 31. During October, four entertainment horror projects require additional charges.

**Tips:** If you have children, you can also take the Hong Kong Disneyland MTR line to seek the innocence and fun of Disneyland.

**Day 3:** Repulse Bay, Stanley, The Peak

**Morning:** Repulse Bay, Hong Kong's most famous beach, is one of the favorite holiday spots for Hong Kong residents.

Formerly known as Repulse Bay Hotel, Lei Yue Mun has been renovated into a colonial-style high-end dining and shopping mall.

Stanley Market's Murray House was originally built as an old historical building in Central. When dismantled in the 1980s, its beams were numbered and carefully stored. It has now been reassembled and reopened. The second floor houses a restaurant area with a Spanish restaurant and a Southeast Asian restaurant.

**Afternoon:** Take the over 100-year-old Peak Tram from Garden Road in Admiralty to ascend The Peak and enjoy the views of Hong Kong Island and Victoria Harbour along the way.

Opened in August 2000, Madame Tussauds Hong Kong is the first permanent branch in Asia. Each wax figure usually takes six months to create, costing approximately HKD 350,000. Every celebrity wax figure is life-sized, with hair individually embedded.

**Day 4:** Food and Shopping

**Morning:** Dim Sum Restaurant.

Breakfast at a traditional Chinese dim sum brunch, a favorite among locals.

**Afternoon:** Hong Kong Shopping.

Times Square in Causeway Bay, Taikoo Place in Admiralty, Landmark in Central, Harbour City in Tsim Sha Tsui, Yau Ma Tei in Kowloon Tong, and New Town Plaza in Sha Tin are all excellent shopping destinations.

**Evening:** Hung Hom黄埔 New World.

Featuring a food court gathering the most famous and authentic classic cuisines across Hong Kong, it represents the essence of Hong Kong's culinary culture, a must-visit for food lovers and travelers alike. After dinner, continue shopping as Huangpu New World is a large leisure shopping center in the Kowloon area.

**Day 5:** Departure from Hong Kong

Take the Express to Hong Kong Airport and check if your luggage is already full of souvenirs. If still not satisfied, plan another five-day Macao-Hong Kong tour next time.
