However, no one could imagine that misfortune was quietly approaching her - Ren Ying, who was in her second year of high school, was diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis, known as the "incurable cancer" in the medical field. Even though her hands began to shrink and it became very difficult for her to walk, Ren Ying still insisted on taking the college entrance examination. The results came out, and Ren Ying's score exceeded the university admission threshold. However, not long after the college entrance exam, Ren Ying became paralyzed and has never stood up again.

Ren Ying was born in 1966 in Ren Village, Xing Tang Town, Linquan County. She was smart and studious, and the awards she received covered an entire wall at home. At the age of 18, Ren Ying became one of the three students in the county admitted to a provincial key high school.

"Don't give up; there's always hope!"

All dreams and aspirations suddenly seemed unreachable, and Ren Ying, in the prime of her life, felt nothing but darkness ahead. Ren Ying thought of death, first refusing to eat, then swallowing pesticide in the quiet of night... Fortunately, her meticulous father discovered her in time, saving her life.

"Don't give up; there's always hope!" Ren Ying chose to live, and to live meaningfully. Ren Ying began studying on her own while offering free tutoring to the village children. She noticed that some children were eight or nine years old and had yet to attend school, so she conceived the idea of starting a school.

Like a wingless angel, she uplifts the "hope" of rural education.

The first challenge was writing on the blackboard. The chalk was too short, and the wheelchair was too low. Ren Ying, who participated in the "Community Medicine Correspondence Course," thought that syringes and ballpoint pen barrels could be extended back and forth, so she invented a board-writing tool that could be used while seated in a wheelchair: a fine wooden stick with a groove, with a syringe shell attached to one end, and the chalk inserted into the syringe shell, creating a telescopic "extended chalk."

In 1998, with the support of her family, Ren Ying used the empty rooms in her home to establish a preschool class in the village. On the opening day, unexpectedly, 24 students arrived, all from Ren Village, with an average age of 8.

Classrooms were built again and again, and the number of students kept increasing — to meet the conditions for social schooling, Ren Ying borrowed hundreds of thousands of yuan and built two teaching buildings. In 2007, her primary school was incorporated into the Chengguan Town administration by the education department and renamed "Chengguan Town Hope Primary School." Now, it has 8 classes and more than 200 students.

"At the school, Ren Ying not only spreads knowledge but also the spirit of perseverance. 'Some naughty students, Ren Teacher would say: "I, who can't even drink water without difficulty, want to learn more knowledge. How can you waste such good time?" This statement touched us greatly,' said Ren Ying's student Wang Hao.

"As long as I can do a good job in teaching, I can endure any hardship," Ren Ying told her concerned parents and fellow villagers. A year passed quickly, and the first preschool class graduated. The children made significant progress, and the parents came knocking, asking her to continue teaching the first grade. Seeing the trust of the parents, Ren Ying's father hurriedly built two more classrooms. In the second new academic year, not only did the preschool class enroll another 26 children, but the first grade also added 11 students from neighboring villages. Ren Ying also named the school "Ren Village Hope Primary School."
