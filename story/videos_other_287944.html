Many people believe that going to bed early and getting up early is a good way to exercise. In fact, exercising in the morning is not the best time for exercise, and some scholars even think that morning exercise is not good for health.

Not long ago, an article in the British 'Journal of Sports Medicine' pointed out that exercising in the morning is not good for health, the reason being that morning physical exercise is not beneficial to the human body's immune function. Relevant medical experts found through surveys that many swimming athletes who trained in the morning experienced a gradual decline in immune function after a period of training. This result has drawn people's attention. After observation and research, they measured that excessive adrenal cortical hormone is produced in the body after morning exercise, and the level of corticosteroids in the body increases. This cannot only not enhance physical fitness but also often causes blood pressure to rise due to corticosteroids, while at the same time inhibiting the normal immune function of the human body. However, there is no such concern when exercising in the evening because the content of corticosteroids in the evening is significantly lower than in the morning.

The editor recommends: Not only that, the 'Journal of Sports Medicine' also reported that the flow rate of saliva will significantly slow down after morning exercise. Do not underestimate the flow rate of saliva, as it can help resist infections. If the flow rate of saliva slows down, it will make people more susceptible to viral infections. Thus, researchers have come to this conclusion: the best time for people to exercise is not in the morning but in the evening. During this time, the level of corticosteroids in the human body is the lowest, and the flow rate of saliva is the highest. It seems that evening exercise meets the requirements of human health.
