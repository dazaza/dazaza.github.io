Thiourea has a promising future prospect.

by d8eks7dj on 2012-02-09 15:28:58

Thiourea has a promising future and its application fields are continuously expanding. With the development of the national economy, the usage of thiourea in pesticides, pharmaceuticals, textile industry, papermaking industry, electroplating, and metal smelting is gradually increasing.

Pesticides/Pharmaceuticals Field: Thiourea reacts with hydrazine hydrate to produce amino thiourea, which is a major intermediate for pesticides and pharmaceuticals. It is mainly used to synthesize bromadiolone, which has a better rat-killing effect than tetramine and causes minimal harm to humans and livestock. It is an acute rodenticide with great market potential. The use of thiourea in this field will increase several times. Amino thiourea is also an intermediate in the preparation of sulfonamide anti-tuberculosis drugs. In recent years, some manufacturers have increased the production of amino thiourea, which will significantly promote the expansion of thiourea consumption in the pharmaceutical field.

Textile Industry: Thiourea can be used as a bleaching agent, dyeing agent, and antioxidant. Thiourea formaldehyde can be used for printing and dyeing wool and silk. Thiourea formaldehyde resin can greatly reduce the flammability of nylon, serving as a flame retardant.

Papermaking Industry: Thiourea is used to make diazo light-sensitive paper and can also serve as a reducing agent for blue and dark brown tones.

With the increasing national efforts to rectify polluting enterprises and the strengthening of environmental awareness among people, highly toxic cyanides in the electroplating and metal mineral flotation industries will be replaced by thiourea. Therefore, the application of thiourea in this field will see a significant increase.

Process Technology Improvements:

1. Traditional Production Process: The traditional lime nitrogen-hydrogen sulfide method involves using lime milk to absorb hydrogen sulfide to prepare calcium hydrosulfide, then adding lime nitrogen (calcium cyanamide) to synthesize thiourea solution. After filtration, the clear liquid undergoes vacuum evaporation concentration, and after cooling crystallization, it is centrifuged, washed with water, and dried to obtain finished thiourea.

2. New Production Process: The process improvement allows for direct absorption of hydrogen sulfide by lime nitrogen to synthesize thiourea, eliminating the step of preparing calcium hydrosulfide from lime milk. The process is as follows: Mix lime nitrogen with water (recycled mother liquor or wash liquor) evenly in the synthesis reactor. While stirring, introduce hydrogen sulfide gas to carry out the synthesis reaction to generate thiourea solution. Filter the solution, freeze crystallize, separate the crystallized liquid by centrifuge, and dry the crystals to obtain the product.

Advantages of the new process over the traditional one:

① Shortened process flow and reduced product cost: Directly absorbing hydrogen sulfide gas into lime nitrogen solution generates thiourea solution. The crystallization process is changed from evaporative dilution and cooling crystallization to freezing crystallization. The new process eliminates three steps: preparing lime milk, making calcium hydrosulfide solution, and evaporating thiourea solution for concentration. Due to the shortened process flow, fewer operators are needed, and engineering investment is correspondingly reduced. Additionally, replacing thermal crystallization with cold crystallization reduces operating costs, significantly lowering the production cost of thiourea.

② Cold freezing crystallization improves product quality: In the traditional process, some crystals precipitate during the evaporation of thiourea solution, resulting in smaller and uneven crystal sizes, affecting product quality. In the new production process, the crystals produced by freezing crystallization are larger, more uniform, and aesthetically pleasing, improving product quality.

③ Adoption of vibrating fluidized bed drying technology further ensures product quality: Using vibrating fluidized bed drying at a controlled low temperature prevents damage to the crystals due to their slower movement speed, ensuring product quality. In the traditional production process, rotary kilns and other equipment were used for drying, requiring subsequent sieving to produce various particle sizes.

④ Reduced environmental pollution: In the traditional thiourea production process, secondary steam generated during evaporation requires water for mixed cooling, which is usually discharged directly after cooling. In the improved production process, since there is no evaporation step, pollution sources are reduced.

Lime nitrogen produced by the carbide method contains only about 20% nitrogen, generating a certain amount of waste residue during thiourea synthesis. Some research institutes are actively seeking new methods to synthesize lime nitrogen or using other raw materials to synthesize thiourea to reduce environmental pollution. For example, technologies like urea preparation of lime nitrogen for thiourea synthesis, urea dehydration for cyanamide production of thiourea, and direct synthesis of charcoal, sulfur, and ammonia are being researched and developed.
