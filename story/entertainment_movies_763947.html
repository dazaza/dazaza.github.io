Plot Summary:

In 2006, in an ancient city of Transylvania, Romania, after a lightning bolt splits the sky, a heavy rain begins to fall. An unwelcome visitor quietly enters the ancient city and attacks the sleeping vampire. Surprisingly, it's a mosquito that sticks its needle-like proboscis into the vampire's neck! The mosquito skillfully dodges the vampire's angry attack and disappears into the night sky.

One night in 2006, that blood-sucking mosquito is roaming around looking for prey.热血 detective Rodo-Ryeol (played by Kim Soo-ro) has very prominent veins. In an instant, the mosquito bites his vein... From the spot where he was bitten, his veins begin to rapidly turn green... What will be the fate of Rodo-Ryeol? He is a passionate detective during the day and an irrational detective at night. A human during the day, but a vampire at night. His extraordinary double life is about to begin...

Highlights:

001 Establishment of a comedic lineage: Heroic action comedy. The first Korean film in this genre, "Vampire Cop Ricky," introduces fresh blood into Korean cinema. The transformed detective possesses extraordinary abilities, combining the hero image with comedy and action - a first in Korean cinematic history. The movie aims to create a superhero like "Superman," "Spider-Man," and "Batman." However, it intentionally shapes a more Korean-style hero, running through with Korean-style comedic spirit. Unlike previous heroes who may not have brought laughter, now Vampire Cop Ricky will fulfill his comedic mission.

002 The First Korean-Bloodline Vampire: To Be Extraordinary. 100% Korean-made, Korean-bloodline vampire – what makes it different? More realistic, more humanized – this is the Korean-bloodline vampire. Typically, vampires can be divided into two categories: one is the pale, noble vampire attached to beautiful women, and the other is the futuristic warrior-like vampire. In "Vampire Cop Ricky," the vampire is more approachable. Rodo-Ryeol transforms into a kind-hearted vampire who uses his extraordinary abilities to do many righteous deeds. To create a pure Korean-bloodline vampire, the production team avoided artificiality and non-reality, pursuing a style closest to nature. Korean-bloodline vampires lack exaggerated clothing and deformed faces, instead seeking delicate changes in eyes and teeth. Kim Soo-ro's vampire teeth perfectly combine with his real teeth. To show the change in eyes, the production team specially imported custom-colored contact lenses from the U.S.

003 Box Office Assurance for Comedy: Can Kim Soo-ro Deliver Again as a Lead Actor? If you ask if anyone has seen Kim Soo-ro's movies, there are probably few Koreans who haven't. Kim Soo-ro showcased his versatile acting skills through films such as "Attack the Gas Station," "The Foul King," "Volcano High School," "S Diary," "Bold Family," and "The Most Beautiful Week of My Life." Although Kim Soo-ro mainly focuses on comedy, he can also well portray action films and even TV dramas. Kim Soo-ro finally welcomes his first movie where he is the sole male lead, "Vampire Cop Ricky." The role of the vampire detective is unique and new, making it difficult to find a completely suitable actor. However, only Kim Soo-ro received unanimous appreciation from the production team. Always bringing laughter to everyone, Kim Soo-ro also brings his trained sexy muscles this time. In 2006, Kim Soo-ro will showcase all the essence of humor, action, and sexiness. Let's look forward to it together.

Behind-the-Scenes Production:

001 The Birth of the Blood-Sucking Mosquito. Eighty percent of the credit for the birth of the vampire detective goes to the blood-sucking mosquito. The blood-sucking mosquito not only serves as the motivation in the movie but can also be said to play a crucial role like the protagonist. To create a highly realistic blood-sucking mosquito, the production team formed the strongest mosquito CG production team in South Korea. They created a mosquito that resembles a real one but magnified it ten times, making its mouth and legs appear more ferocious. In the scene where Rodo-Ryeol kills the mosquito biting him, the mosquito in his hand was specifically caught by co-actors, being three times larger than an ordinary mosquito. Through the joint efforts of the actors and staff, the birth of the blood-sucking mosquito is expected to be well presented in the movie.

002 Kim Soo-ro's Herculean Efforts to Tackle the Impossible Task. To successfully portray the vampire, Kim Soo-ro challenges everything from high-altitude work to special costumes... Kim Soo-ro's tasks could be said to include water, land, and air battles. As the vampire detective, Kim Soo-ro needs to jump between high-rise buildings and crawl upside down on ceilings. For two consecutive days, Kim Soo-ro endured wire harnessing for shooting, almost breaking his waist, yet he persisted until the end despite the pain.

Finally completing the high-altitude work, Kim Soo-ro's second challenge was even tougher. He had to shoot a scene where a rat crawls into his clothes. Kim Soo-ro, who fears rats the most in his life, shot this scene seven times, and the rats were quite large. Kim Soo-ro's solitary battle, hopefully, will bear fruit in this movie.

003 The Staff's Maximum Efforts to Create a Genuine Vampire. From masks to teeth, to colored contact lenses, a more realistic vampire was born. To make matching masks, the staff made 17 different styles and selected the one most suitable for Kim Soo-ro. The vampire teeth were made according to the various stages of transformation of the vampire. The colored contact lenses were also divided into different stages; initially using yellow and later red when fully transformed into a vampire. To depict the characteristics of the vampire detective guarding the city, they abandoned the usual red-black combination in costumes and opted for a western cowboy-inspired style. To create a realistic vampire, all the staff worked hard to refine every detail.

004 Blockbuster Bombshell - Making of the Comedy Trailer. With a total investment of 1 billion Korean won, the trailer was filmed in the homeland of vampires, the Czech Republic. The local production team involved was the same one that participated in the making of movies like "Van Helsing" and "The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen." The trailer's dubbing was done in two versions: one recorded in Korean for the Korean version and another in English for the international version. Just to shoot the trailer, they flew to the Czech Republic, indicating the production team's hope to deliver a great movie.

005 Kim Soo-ro's Brilliant Spontaneous Performances Beyond Imagination, Beyond Lines, Beyond Limits! This is Kim Soo-ro's brilliant spontaneous talent. Many people like Kim Soo-ro's comedies because he can interpret basic lines in ways that only he can make funny. His comedic skills honed over years in daily life have gained recognition from everyone. In the movie "Vampire Cop Ricky," Kim Soo-ro once again demonstrates his value.
