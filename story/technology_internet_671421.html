"Shangzhijiao" second-hand housing prices begin to loosen

By Yue Zheng and Wang Lu of East Real Estate

Shanghai's old "Shangzhijiao" is spread across the old Huangpu District (excluding the Nanxiang District), the former Luwan District, and Jing'an District. These areas have traditionally been frequented by the elite and are considered prime locations with housing prices that are resistant to falls.

To this day, the scope of "Shangzhijiao" has expanded to include areas such as Gubei in Changning and Lianyang in Pudong. But will these new upscale areas attract buyers away from the old "Shangzhijiao"? And under the current sluggish real estate market conditions, can the old "Shangzhijiao" maintain its reputation for price stability?

Old Huangpu District:

A 15% drop in value

Today's Huangpu District includes the old Huangpu District, the former Luwan District, and the Nanxiang District. This area, known for its excellent transportation and complete amenities, has always been regarded as Shanghai's city center. The luxury housing options here mainly consist of Jin Wai Tan Garden, Shanghai滩 Garden, and Pujiang Manor, with transaction prices ranging between 60,000 to 100,000 RMB per square meter. Jin Wai Tan Garden sees the highest volume of transactions. However, the price resistance in this area is currently weak. According to Zhao Binneng from Century 21 Zhonghua Road branch, the final transaction prices of luxury properties in this district are generally around 15% lower than the asking prices set by homeowners.

Recently, there has been an increasing number of listings, but due to policy impacts, the transaction volume isn't very high. Nonetheless, the maturity of the area still attracts celebrities from various fields, such as Australian photovoltaic energy scientist Dai Ximing, Hong Kong actress Wu Qilin, and film actor Chen Daoming, who have all moved into this region.

Currently, the listed price per square meter is around 50,000 RMB, while the average transaction price is approximately 42,000 RMB. Due to the sluggish market, the transaction volume is not ideal.

Former Northern Luwan District:

Homeowners reluctant to lower prices

The former Luwan District is divided into Northern Luwan and Southern Luwan. Northern Luwan was once the French Concession of old Shanghai, imbued with European charm and French romance for nearly a century. Living here seems to bestow a label of nobility.

Within the Northern Luwan district, garden villas and plane tree-lined paths, leveraging the advantages of Huaihai Road and Xintiandi as two major commercial centers, have gathered a large number of high-end luxury residential complexes, such as Cuihu Tiandi, Jinlin Tiandi, Huaihai Jinghuayuan, and Xiangshan Lisha. These have attracted many successful individuals and social gentlemen to settle here. Among them, the Xintiandi area has become one of the regions with the highest density of expatriates in Shanghai.

Jing'an District:

Always imitated, never replaced

Jing'an District holds an irreplaceable position in the hearts of Shanghai residents. Whether it's old-style lane houses, villas, or new luxury residential complexes, they are all surrounded by streets steeped in old Shanghai charm. Their scarce geographical location and surrounding environment give landlords confidence. According to Bai Dongliang from Hangyu Real Estate Dongxinzhai branch, the number of luxury property listings has been increasing significantly, with most landlords trying to test the market value of their properties. Therefore, the price reduction幅度is not significant. For those genuinely willing to sell, there is only about a 5% negotiation space. The most expensive would be the "Classic Maoming" complex, where the current transaction price is around 100,000 RMB per square meter, belonging to a hotel-style apartment type residential complex. Within the district, other active second-hand property transactions include Jing'an Haojing, Zhongkai City Light, and International Lido City, with prices ranging from 60,000 to 70,000 RMB per square meter. The main buyers are foreign executives and expatriates.

Shanghai rental market enters off-season; Inner Ring tenants concentrate on pre-year house swapping

In November, the Shanghai rental market showed uneven performance between the inner and outer rings. According to monitoring by Shanghai Centaline Research and Consulting Department, at the end of the year, with students on vacation and the peak of local migrant workers returning home, rental demand gradually decreases, leading to a general decline in customer visits and transaction volumes. Based on incomplete sample statistics from Shanghai Centaline intermediary stores, despite the overall downward trend, there is regional imbalance. In areas outside the Inner Ring, rental transactions have dropped significantly. For example, the Beicai and Zhenru sectors between the Inner and Middle Rings have seen a decline of 20-30%, while the Gumei and Waigaoqiao sectors between the Middle and Outer Rings have also decreased by 20%. In contrast, within the Inner Ring, sectors like Xujiahui and Zhongshan Park have witnessed varying degrees of increase in rental transactions, reaching 15% and 50% respectively, with the main tenant groups being corporate managers and high-income white-collar workers.

Song Huiyong, Director of Shanghai Centaline Research and Consulting Department, noted that although urban residential development has rapidly shifted to peripheral areas, the maturity of life-supporting facilities in these areas still lags behind daily needs. Against this backdrop, Inner Ring rental properties become more attractive. High-end rental seekers do not face issues with purchasing power. Under the pressure of regulation, many landlords have switched from selling to renting, making supply more relaxed and providing more choices for pre-year house swapping. Meanwhile, in some secondary peripheral and peripheral areas, rental properties are relatively abundant, and there is no need to worry about situations where "one house is hard to find."
