The Pacific Ocean can't hold all my sorrows.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-01-28 22:08:59

The Pacific Ocean could not contain all my sorrow.

I have isolated myself for many days, not wanting to contact anyone. If someone kindly greeted me, I would become very annoyed. I armed myself so tightly, thinking that no one could hurt me again.

However, my sorrow grew faster and more than the cells I was cultivating. It enveloped me, like a word - "self-inflicted cocoon".

I said I didn't want to let another tear fall. When I sent my daughter to school in the morning and rode my bike on the road, hearing sharp car horns around me, I looked around blankly and realized that I didn't know when I had ridden into the ten lanes of a five-lane road. My mind went blank, and tears suddenly burst out. Was it because of the habit of driving from the entrance, or was I distracted? I don't know. I stood in the middle of the road and struggled to walk out of danger. A few days ago, after I sent my daughter, I kept my head down and walked slowly. I didn't realize when I had ridden to the fourth ring road while my home is near the second ring road. The other day, I saw an astonishing scene in the mall: a grandfather in his sixties, leading two young and beautiful girls shopping in a luxury store. At that moment, I was panic-stricken, as if I had been caught doing something shameful. How can the world be so crazy? How can the world be so crazy? Who can you still believe? It's chaotic...

People say that time is the best medicine for pain. Time can heal all wounds, deep or shallow, even those that pierce through the bone. Why does my wound grow like a small grass in the bottom of my heart, gradually becoming a towering tree, covering all my heart, and not letting a ray of sunshine in? When I stood in the middle of the busy road during the morning rush hour and let my tears flow, all my sorrows couldn't be taken away by the sea, not even the Pacific Ocean could contain them.
