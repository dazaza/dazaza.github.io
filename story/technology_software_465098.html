The working principle of a fuel saver is generally to adopt oxygen supplementation for the engine, increasing the compression ratio of the engine, making the fuel-to-oxygen ratio in the cylinder reach the highest value of combustion, allowing the fuel in the combustion chamber to burn fully. This way, carbon deposits will not form inside the cylinder, thereby improving the combustion rate of fuel.
