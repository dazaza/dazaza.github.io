Deepthroat talks Baidu finance, then gossips about Alibaba and Tencent

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:05:40

Pregnant Peak: Baidu's first phase of financial management at 8% returns. Why is it twice the industry level?

Deep Throat: In fact, this industry is extremely profitable, and Baidu is willing to subsidize to attract users, which is a continuation of the free gene of the Internet. It's not hard to reach 8%. The key is that traditional financial companies are unwilling to do it in order to maintain high profits.

Pregnant Peak: On September 23, the CSRC issued a Weibo post stating that they would re-examine Baidu's financial management plan "Baifat". Everyone thought Baidu was going to be in trouble.

Deep Throat: On the 25th, the CSRC convened several major Internet companies and fund institutions for an industry seminar. A representative from Baidu was present, and before the meeting started, someone from a fund company joked: "You're the main character today, you should sit in the middle." The Baidu representative was quite embarrassed, but then someone from the CSRC said: "Today is a discussion, not a self-criticism session, don't be nervous."

Pregnant Peak: At the center of controversy, how did Baidu and Huaxia manage to resolve the situation?

Deep Throat: Actually, Huaxia was the most anxious. After a personnel shake-up last year, the newly appointed CEO Tianming Teng wanted to achieve something significant. He was very interested in Internet finance and met with Robin Li, which led to their cooperation. However, the statement released by the CSRC was vague, and they were somewhat unclear about the CSRC's intentions.

It turns out that the CSRC generally supports Internet finance. The result of that day was that Baidu's financial innovation was worth promoting. In fact, Baidu is just an Internet distribution channel, while Huaxia Fund is the main body of financial management. This cross-border integration model is different from Yu'ebao, which is actually beneficial to traditional finance. Internet companies help others instead of doing it themselves.

Pregnant Peak: When promoting the 8% return rate, wasn't everything fully settled with the CSRC? Acting first and reporting later?

Deep Throat: This is complicated. Anyway, the leadership signed off on the project that day. Huaxia and Baidu finally felt reassured. That day was Friday, and the release was scheduled for the following Monday. If the CSRC leadership hadn't signed or hesitated even slightly, Baidu's first financial product would have been delayed. In fact, both Baidu and Huaxia had prepared delay statements, originally planned for release on Friday afternoon.

Pregnant Peak: When the Internet collaborates with institutional forces, there’s still some uncertainty.

Deep Throat: Once it passes, it's all good. On the 28th, they issued the product, selling 1 billion yuan in four hours, with 120,000 people purchasing. In fact, their second product "Bai Zuan" (Hundred Earnings), although not heavily promoted, also sold several billion yuan in one day. This data was a huge shock to Huaxia. They felt that even if there were risks, they absolutely made the right decision. Huaxia has many branches, and after more than ten years of operation, their total user base is only two or three million. Baidu attracted over a hundred thousand users in just one day. At this pace, Baidu's financial customer base will soon surpass Huaxia. One day, Huaxia may no longer need physical branches.

Pregnant Peak: The Internet has previously impacted media, commerce, games, etc., and now it's aggressively impacting finance.

Deep Throat: It quickly provided an outlet for the country's excess liquidity, where bank interest rates are actually negative. The explosive growth of Internet finance is unstoppable. It has great significance for the country, the system, and the common people. Yu'ebao fired the first shot, and Baidu followed with its financial products.

The so-called "intermediate page" opportunity exists in the financial industry as well. Common people don't understand professional terms or various thresholds, and buying government bonds requires queuing up at banks. For example, the website Rong360 represents this intermediate page thinking, and CC, the founder of Qunar, also invested in this website.

Pregnant Peak: What exactly is the attitude of the CSRC?

Deep Throat: The CSRC is relatively tolerant towards Internet finance. The cooperation between Baidu and Huaxia is smart; Huaxia provides the transaction process, and Baidu serves as the entry point to attract users. Baidu does not participate in the transaction process, meaning the CSRC can only regulate Huaxia, which remains clean. This is a clean and efficient model. Compared to the initial risky design of Yu'ebao, Baidu's financial products learned from past mistakes and are bold yet meticulous.

Pregnant Peak: Compare Baidu's financial products with Yu'ebao.

Deep Throat: Jokingly speaking, the name "Yu'ebao" isn't ideal. The word "balance" limits imagination. "Baifat" (Hundred Percent) and "Bai Zuan" (Hundred Earnings) are straightforward and resonate with ordinary people. In fact, Baidu's financial products can be closer to users than Yu'ebao. Baidu is an information platform, while Alipay is a payment platform. Especially for new users, investment starts with understanding information rather than making payments. Baidu has the opportunity to become a massive dam like the Three Gorges Dam, isolating Yu'ebao like the Gezhouba Dam. It's the difference between upstream and downstream. Does it make sense?

Pregnant Peak: But Yu'ebao already has ready money in existing accounts available at any time. Baidu neither has ready money nor existing accounts.

Deep Throat: This is why Baidu is vigorously developing financial services—to accumulate accounts. Moreover, financial management is a strong demand, and the appeal to attract users to register won't be small. Those who want to buy financial products must tie their bank cards to Baidu Pay, and reportedly, over a million people have newly tied their bank cards.

Pregnant Peak: How does the traditional financial circle view Internet finance?

Deep Throat: When Yu'ebao first appeared, the financial circle dismissed it. As Yu'ebao and Tianhong Fund grew larger, the financial circle called for strengthened regulation. When Baidu joined forces with Huaxia, the financial circle became anxious, rushing to negotiate with Baidu, Tencent, and Alibaba, hoping to become the next Huaxia or Tianhong.

Pregnant Peak: What about subsequent impacts?

Deep Throat: First, the financial circle is affected, as mentioned earlier—they've started knocking on BAT's doors. Second, ordinary netizens are affected. Among the users of Baidu and Huaxia this time, over 80% registered accounts and bought funds for the first time, with an average consumption per user exceeding 10,000 yuan. The public's enthusiasm for financial management is high, causing Baidu's servers to crash. Third, the real economy is affected. With such a large amount of savings transferred from bank cards into financial management, it might stimulate the real economy.

Alibaba's financial sector might be negatively impacted, perhaps seeing a valuation drop of 20-30%. Originally, Internet finance was dominated by Alibaba alone, but now it needs to become one of the two leading forces or one of the three oligarchs. This is competition.

Pregnant Peak: After everyone spreads out, the 8% return rate can't last long, right?

Deep Throat: Neither Yu'ebao nor Baidu's current model has a future. As the process of interest rate liberalization accelerates, the profit margins of traditional financial industries will increasingly resemble each other, and simply playing with time differences or capital scale differences won't be feasible. Baidu's first wave of products had high returns and came with a treasure box, but the second wave didn't come with the treasure box, and the returns returned to normal, yet they were still wildly sought after. Why? Investors aren't foolish; what they gain from Baidu is investment convenience and simplicity of operation. Explosive returns attract users, but experience retains them. The next step is to talk about differentiated services or stories related to big data.

Pregnant Peak: When did Baidu start doing financial management?

Deep Throat: Who doesn't want to do finance? In fact, Huaxia also contacted Tencent. As one of the first batch of fund companies cooperating with Tencent's Tenpay, their customized product, Wealth Treasure Money Market Fund, received approval on September 6. But essentially, Tencent is not a company that focuses on ecosystem collaboration; their cooperation was repeatedly delayed, allowing Baidu to take the lead.

Tenpay and funds negotiated cooperation, selecting four fund companies simultaneously, saying that whoever went online first would be promoted first, lacking focus, and Huaxia's cooperative product remained offline for a long time. This is probably due to the old dominant style cultivated by Tencent within the Internet industry—flying the flag of openness, but making everyone beg me, indifferent whether you care or not. In large companies, some departments are highly combat-effective, conquering the world, while others are quite weak, parasitic.

Baidu obtained the payment license for Baidu Wallet in July and began contacting Huaxia in August. After one round of discussions, they basically determined the cooperation. In mid-August, Huaxia Fund's CEO Tianming Teng visited Robin Li, finalizing the deal.

Pregnant Peak: Data on financial management?

Deep Throat: The annual growth rate of China's financial product quantity and issuance scale is around 40%. From 2012 to 2013, there were 300 million daily searches on Baidu for investment and financial management, with bank products and securities/fund type products accounting for more than 70%. Users are mainly aged 21-30.

In the eyes of Internet companies, there are significant opportunities to transform traditional industries. Bringing highbrow culture down to the masses is something the Internet excels at. In this era, when common people with some money are still being openly robbed, the notion of universal financial management is not exaggerated.

Pregnant Peak: How big is Alibaba's advantage?

Deep Throat: Alibaba's layout is more focused on the capital chain, aiming to achieve a closed loop, known as the ecosystem. However, this ecosystem is actually closed. Alibaba Micro Loans is based on Taobao and B2B data, which allows them to offer loans. In reality, the scale of Alibaba Micro Loans depends on the growth of Taobao and B2B. If they expand outward, they lack data accumulation. Alibaba's ceiling is visible; how big Taobao is determines how big they can grow. We expect Alibaba to be more open.

Pregnant Peak: What about Tencent?

Deep Throat: Tencent's internal integration is relatively chaotic, which is a problem for them. Who is the main player in doing Internet finance is not clearly defined—is it Tenpay, Tencent Finance, WeChat, or Mobile QQ? Tencent Finance organizes meetings with financial companies, but Tenpay doesn't know about it. They haven't formed a cohesive team yet.

Pregnant Peak: Tencent might be slower, but they will eventually join in. For instance, they dismantled the Soso team the first year and invested in Sogou the second year. Big ships are harder to turn, but their social advantages are significant.

Deep Throat: We hope that Tencent truly opens up, not just pretending to. Baidu has always been more open, and their financial management is also a platform. They have an idea that could serve as a reference for Tencent: Baidu Financial Management wants to do "fan finance," for example, Lin Zhiling's Tieba has millions of users every day. If one day Lin Zhiling wants to set up a charity fund, he can cooperate with Baidu Financial Management to mobilize fans' participation and launch customized products with financial institutions.

Pregnant Peak: Who leads Baidu's financial management?

Deep Throat: VP Mingyuan Li, who was recently promoted. It's said there are 120 people now.
