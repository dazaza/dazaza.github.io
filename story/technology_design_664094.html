Beijing Sprite poisoning case: woman sentenced to 7 years in final verdict (photo)

by zsh0827469 on 2012-02-13 10:11:17

On the morning of yesterday, the "Sprite Mercury Poisoning Incident" was announced in court. After the announcement, Liu Xiaojing immediately covered her face with her hands. Our reporter GuiBin Wang took the photo. The "Sprite Mercury Poisoning Case" Tracking Report: On yesterday's final trial announcement, the Intermediate People's Court No.1 rejected the appeal from the poisoner Liu Xiaojing and her accomplice Gao Xingyuan, sentencing them both to 7 and 5 years in prison respectively for the crime of intentional homicide. Multiple poisonings were caused by emotional disputes. During yesterday's trial, Liu Xiaojing kept her hands on her face all the time, while Gao Xingyuan standing on her right also turned his head to the right side. Both listened to the verdict calmly without saying anything. Previously, in the same court, the victim Ma Sai was sentenced to one year in prison for damaging business credibility and product reputation. According to the court investigation, on November 7, 2009, Ma Sai drank a can of Sprite that had been opened beforehand by his girlfriend Liu Xiaojing and contained toxic substances at the Xidan Joy City, causing a poisoning reaction. During Ma Sai's hospital treatment, he claimed "I personally opened the sealed beverage," which damaged the commercial credibility and product reputation of Coca-Cola Company, leading to criminal charges against him. The reason why Liu Xiaojing did this, as found out by the court, was due to emotional disputes. With the help of her accomplice Gao Xingyuan, she poisoned Ma Sai's food and drinks three times between October and November 7, 2009, resulting in his poisoning (as previously reported by our newspaper). The woman had once received forgiveness from the victim. In March this year, the first instance court in Xicheng District sentenced both to 7 and 5 years in prison respectively for the crime of intentional homicide. Later, they appealed. During the case proceedings, Ma Sai wrote a letter to the court stating, "I have forgiven Liu Xiaojing for the harm she caused me, requesting the court to give her a lighter punishment." In response, the final trial court believed that the facts recognized by the first instance court were clear and the application of law was appropriate, thus rejecting the appeal and maintaining the original sentence. Although both committed the crime of intentional homicide and attempted to illegally deprive others of their lives, the court believed that their criminal circumstances were relatively minor, their attitude towards confession was good, and it was an uncompleted crime, therefore allowing for a lighter punishment compared to a completed crime. Since Liu Xiaojing was the main perpetrator and Gao Xingyuan was the accomplice, and Gao Xingyuan had confessed, Gao Xingyuan should receive a lighter punishment compared to Liu Xiaojing. Dialogue: "It's good that Ma Sai is fine." Beijing News: Do you regret now? Liu Xiaojing: I deeply regret this matter because it violated the law. For Ma Sai, I don't want to talk about who was wrong or right; I just want to say that I really didn't intend for him to die. Beijing News: What were you thinking at the time? Liu Xiaojing: Initially, I just wanted someone to beat him up, vent some anger, and then it would be over. Perhaps I chose the wrong person, and they gave me this idea. If I had known how this stuff could affect him, I definitely wouldn't have done it. Beijing News: Do you think the outcome was due to choosing the wrong person? Liu Xiaojing: I don't want to talk about it anymore; it's good that Ma Sai is fine. Beijing News: Is there anything you want to say to your family? Liu Xiaojing: I believe my parents have already forgiven me. I don't dare to think about my child, who is only three years old... Beijing News: After coming in here, do you regret most not being there for your child? Liu Xiaojing: Not exactly, it's more about not being there for my husband. I believe he will wait for me. Beijing News: When you get out, your child will already be in school. How will you explain this to him? Liu Xiaojing: We'll tell him that I went to work, and I won't tell him about this matter. Beijing News: As you said, your family has always been good to you. Why did it come to this? Was it because of conflicts with your husband? Liu Xiaojing: No, Ma Sai was my colleague at the time. We had a good relationship, often hanging out together, and he helped me a lot at work. That period, my husband was particularly busy, and I am someone who depends heavily on others, hoping someone could keep me company. Beijing News: Do you still hate Ma Sai now? Liu Xiaojing: No hatred. Beijing News: What about your parents? Liu Xiaojing: I hope my mother takes care of her health. It's the first time I feel sorry for her. Actually, inside here, I lack nothing, but I still want them to send me things, just to stay in touch with home. I've never been away from them for so long since I was little.
