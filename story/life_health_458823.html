Preventing and Treating Stomach Cancer Starts with Eating Coarse Grains

Coarse grains are also rich in calcium, magnesium, selenium, and other trace elements as well as various vitamins. These nutrients can promote metabolism, strengthen physical fitness, and delay aging. Selenium, in particular, is an anti-cancer substance that can bind with various carcinogens within the body and expel them through the digestive tract. Molecular cancer prevention experts point out that coarse grains have not only become essential nutrients for the body but can also prevent multiple types of cancers, especially stomach and colorectal cancers and other digestive tract tumors. Coarse grains include foods such as nuts, corn, and grain-based foods that are complex carbohydrates rich in fiber. The high fiber content in these foods can stimulate the gastrointestinal tract, promote bowel movements, reduce the absorption of carcinogens by the intestines, and thus prevent the occurrence of colorectal cancer. The lack of plant fiber in food is one of the important reasons for the increasing prevalence of diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, and cancer in recent years. Dietary fiber can prevent colon cancer, rectal cancer, diverticulosis, and more.

The cancer-preventive principle of plant fiber lies in its ability to "cleanse the intestines." It promotes intestinal peristalsis, shortens the time it takes for intestinal contents to pass through, and reduces the possibility of carcinogens being absorbed by the body. Moreover, excessive intake of proteins, fats, and carbohydrates is also a contributing factor to cancer. Obesity caused by this excess is often a trigger for some endocrine-related cancers. Studies by molecular cancer prevention experts show that a high-fiber diet from coarse grains can hinder the absorption of these three major nutrients, reduce obesity, and effectively prevent cancer. Various grains are originally sources of dietary fiber, but due to processing, especially the refinement of grains, plant fibers are lost. Therefore, we should consume more brown rice, whole wheat, and mixed grains, or even bran.

Molecular cancer prevention experts also remind us that eating fresh coarse grains is preferable. Fresh coarse grains contain richer nutrients and are less likely to be contaminated by aflatoxins. Coarse grains stored for long periods are prone to mold, which not only fail to prevent cancer but may also induce liver cancer in newborns. Expert Cancer Prevention: Preventing Stomach Cancer Starts with Managing Gastric Diseases

Stomach cancer is one of the most common malignant tumors worldwide. In China, stomach cancer accounts for 23% of all deaths from malignant tumors, ranking first among cancer-related deaths. There are even records of children as young as seven or eight suffering from stomach cancer, making the prevention and treatment of stomach cancer an urgent priority. Medical research experts have preliminarily determined that Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) is an important risk factor and initiating factor for stomach cancer. People in high-incidence areas for stomach cancer have higher H. pylori infection rates, and the infection occurs at an earlier age. With increasing age, both the H. pylori infection rate and the incidence of stomach cancer grow synchronously. Individuals infected with H. pylori have a 4-6 times higher risk of developing stomach cancer compared to non-infected individuals. Therefore, preventing or curing H. pylori infections is crucial in preventing stomach cancer. This highlights the importance of starting stomach cancer prevention from childhood, such as performing timely gastroscopic examinations on children with gastric diseases and providing radical treatment if H. pylori infection is detected.

Molecular cancer prevention experts also reveal that individuals who frequently come into contact with stomach cancer-causing factors from a young age are more prone to develop stomach cancer. Besides H. pylori, which was identified as a Class I stomach cancer carcinogen by the World Health Organization in 1994, nitrosamines are also potential stomach cancer-causing substances. Nitrosamines are products formed when nitrates are reduced to nitrites and then combine with amines. Nitrates and nitrites are widely found in pickled vegetables, salted vegetables, salted fish, salted meat, and smoked foods. Reducing the intake of these foods helps prevent stomach cancer. Other methods to adjust diet for stomach cancer prevention include low-salt diets to reduce nitrate and nitrite intake, drinking more milk, consuming more fresh vegetables and fruits (rich in vitamin C, which inhibits the combination of nitrites and amines), and advocating the consumption of refrigerated foods. Japan, a country with a high incidence of stomach cancer, has seen a decline in recent years due to extensive screening and comprehensive preventive measures, including widespread use of refrigerators.

Medical research has discovered that the progression from a normal stomach to stomach cancer usually takes a considerable amount of time. Rapid-type stomach cancer typically requires 7-8 years to develop, while ordinary type stomach cancer takes about 20-22 years. The progression from early-stage to mid-late stage stomach cancer requires approximately 3-5 years, and the process from H. pylori infection to stomach cancer lasts 10-20 years. Clearly, with proper attention, there is ample time to prevent the occurrence of stomach cancer. Precancerous lesions of stomach cancer are easily detectable and diagnosable, such as chronic atrophic gastritis, gastric ulcers, gastric polyps, remnant gastritis, verrucous gastritis, intestinal metaplasia, and dysplasia. Therefore, actively treating these conditions is an important measure to prevent stomach cancer. Through close cooperation between doctors and patients, these diseases can be completely cured. Mild intestinal metaplasia and dysplasia can be reversed, and even moderate dysplasia, which is highly likely to transform into stomach cancer, or severe dysplasia, which should be treated as stomach cancer, can be prevented through surgery. Additionally, many traditional Chinese medicines, such as monkey head mushroom tablets and Weifuchun tablets, have the functions of regulating and enhancing immune function, treating precancerous lesions, and fighting cancer. Therefore, patients with precancerous lesions of stomach cancer can appropriately select these types of traditional Chinese medicine.
