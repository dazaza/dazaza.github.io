"Beyond the Boundary" - A Blessing or a Curse? Predictions for Home Appliance Development in 2012

by anonymous on 2012-03-04 14:08:02

In the first half of 2011, the sales scale of China's home appliance market exceeded 600 billion yuan; for the whole year of 2011, some institutions predicted that the sales scale of the home appliance market would reach 1.2 trillion yuan, an increase of 20% compared to the 1.04 trillion yuan sales scale of home appliances in 2010.

In 2011, the wave of home appliance intelligence was getting stronger and stronger, Chinese home appliance enterprises were merging one after another, and the rural home appliance program / old-for-new policy was gradually expiring, making the home appliance market in 2011 exceptionally lively and further promoting the rapid development of the home appliance market.

In 2012, the Chinese home appliance market still coexists with opportunities and challenges, market competition will be more intense, overcapacity, increased saturation of home appliances, sluggish real estate markets, etc., will all become the focus of many home appliance enterprises. So what development trends will China's home appliance industry present in 2012? Wantai Home Appliance Network timely launched a prediction of the development of home appliances in 2012, hoping it will provide some inspiration and help to everyone.

・Is the development of home appliances & IT "crossing boundaries" a blessing or a curse?

The simple explanation of crossing boundaries is "exceeding boundaries or demarcations."

In the home appliance industry, as home appliances gradually become smarter, "crossing boundaries" development has become the norm. Apple, Lenovo, Google and other traditional IT giants have all targeted the smart home appliance field, and there are rumors that they will launch smart TVs; at the same time, IT products such as tablet computers are becoming increasingly popular, and home appliance manufacturers are also entering on a large scale. Haier, Skyworth (Weibo), TCL (Weibo), Changhong and other traditional home appliance enterprises have all launched their own brand tablet computers, joining the battle; thus, the confrontation between home appliances and IT camps will be more obvious in 2012.

Can the "cross-boundary" development of the two camps achieve anything?

Firstly, regarding IT companies like Apple and Lenovo entering the smart home appliance market, traditional home appliance companies believe that IT giants entering the smart home appliance market indicates great potential, but it will be difficult for them to dominate, not as easy as in the IT field such as mobile phones. The specific reasons are threefold: First, although IT products are close to home appliances, consumers' needs are completely different, and home appliance companies understand Chinese consumers' demands better; second, the channels for home appliances and IT are different, especially in second and third-tier cities, where differences are greater, and IT companies need a certain familiarization process to integrate into home appliance channels; third, policy avoidance, domestic TV development differs from IT and will encounter various policy restrictions, if IT companies enter rashly, they will face many restrictions.

It is not easy for home appliance companies to make big moves in the tablet computer field, such as lacking a sales platform for tablet TVs, insufficient understanding of consumer needs, lack of professional technical talents, no price advantage, etc., which all require an adaptation process.

Represented by traditional IT camps like Apple, Google, and Lenovo entering the home appliance field, and home appliance companies doing IT, who can go further? In 2012, we will wait and see...

・Prosperity & Crisis - Will there be a wave of closures among home appliance e-commerce companies?

In 2011, home appliance e-commerce developed rapidly, especially after Gome (Weibo) and Suning aggressively entered the e-commerce sector, competition became fiercer, the sales landscape kept changing, and consumers' habit of purchasing large home appliances online was gradually forming. In 2012, can the home appliance e-commerce channel take half of the traditional channel's market share? Can it develop a business model truly suitable for the development of China's home appliance online shopping? These are all worth looking forward to.

Currently, the most noticeable aspect of home appliance online shopping is the price competition, having a significant advantage over traditional channels in terms of price. Price wars during holidays are also powerful tools for e-commerce companies, but overall, this method of sacrificing profit for high turnover is not conducive to the long-term development of the entire industry, and the apparent prosperity may just be a fleeting phenomenon.

In 2012, apart from price, logistics delivery and after-sales service will be more important for buyers of large home appliances. For home appliance online shopping companies, only through the improvement of product procurement and sales systems, consumer demand collection systems, and service systems, and by transforming their strategic development models to achieve efficient对接of supply and demand chains, thereby realizing seamless and rapid response in consumer demand, product manufacturing, and product circulation links, ultimately improving the efficiency of the industrial chain, can they achieve sustainable and stable development.

・Content & Olympics - 3D TV proportion breaks through 50%

Many friends who bought 3D TVs in 2011 complained that their 3D TVs were just "ornaments", and the 3D function was just for show. However, this issue may be resolved in 2012.

Firstly, China Central Television's first 3D channel will start broadcasting nationwide on New Year's Day 2012. Previously, many provinces such as Tianjin and Shanghai had trial-run 3D TV channels, but due to regional and content limitations, their appeal to consumers was weak. Entering 2012, CCTV's 3D channel started broadcasting, and Beijing TV, Shanghai TV, Tianjin TV, Shenzhen TV, Jiangsu TV, and other six units are required to provide content sources, significantly increasing 3D content and greatly promoting the development of the entire 3D industry chain.

Additionally, it can be expected that 3D sports events will become the main content of CCTV's 3D channel. Currently, CCTV has purchased the 3D TV broadcast rights for the 2012 London Olympics, providing 300 hours of 3D live broadcasts including the opening and closing ceremonies, athletics, games, gymnastics, etc., which will better satisfy users' desire to watch 3D.

Under the impetus of these numerous favorable factors, industry insiders predict that in 2012, 3D functions will gradually become standard features for large-sized flat-screen TVs, and the sales proportion of 3D TVs could very likely account for 50% of flat-screen TVs.

・Personalization & Intelligence - High-end home appliances break growth bottlenecks

There is a saying in the home appliance field that many people deeply feel - "price wars have exhausted the profits of home appliance manufacturers." In the Chinese market, many home appliance companies rely heavily on "price wars" to form a culture of thin-profit but high-sales, but at the same time, they must endure decreasing enterprise profit margins, severe lack of core competitive products, frequent anti-dumping issues in overseas markets, and increasingly more problems. To solve this problem well and break through this profit growth bottleneck, personalized and high-end strategies are the most recognized by everyone.

Nowadays, being individualistic and stylish has become synonymous with many young people. They don't follow the ordinary path, always having all kinds of strange ideas, seemingly ignoring traditional home appliances and conservative styles.

Many companies have begun to see and pay attention to this, gradually moving towards personalized home appliance marketing by understanding what consumers want to buy and then providing those products, even allowing consumers to purchase TVs that are globally unique. Of course, this customization service is currently not very widespread, but it is believed that in 2012, with the rapid development of smart home appliances, customized services for users will become more perfect and better meet user personalization needs.

Moreover, in 2012, after the application of the convergence of the three networks and Internet of Things technology, all electrical appliances such as refrigerators, lights, air conditioners, TVs, DVDs, audio equipment, microwave ovens, washing machines, etc., will enter the smart era. Through smartphones or other integrated devices, all home appliances can be conveniently controlled, thus bringing development space for the interconnection of home appliances and product upgrades. The intelligent development of home appliances may even comprehensively rewrite the current status of the home appliance market and the industry landscape.

・Renewal & Rural Market - Second and third-tier cities become hotspots for competition

Since the introduction of policies such as "Rural Home Appliance Program" and "Old-for-New," not only has the home appliance market gained much vitality, but it has also greatly stimulated rural consumption markets, playing a significant role in increasing the sales of home appliances. However, with the gradual end of the Rural Home Appliance Program and Old-for-New policies, their impact on the home appliance market cannot be underestimated, especially for some enterprises with weak market competitiveness and large inventories of "Rural Home Appliance Program" products, which will be quite a blow, and this will also accelerate the transformation of the home appliance market.

Since the introduction of the Rural Home Appliance Program and Old-for-New policies, the consumption markets of second and third-tier cities have been quickly activated. Industry insiders predict that in 2012, this consumer demand will continue, and the consumption markets of second and third-tier cities will become a hotspot for competition among home appliance merchants.

Through the guidance of the national preferential subsidy policies in recent years, the level of home appliance consumption in second and third-tier cities has improved to a certain extent, and it has also made some astute home appliance manufacturers realize the consumption potential of rural markets. In 2012, while ensuring stable market share in urban areas, some manufacturers will certainly continue to expand rural consumption markets. Even without policy subsidies, manufacturers will use promotional tactics to mitigate the effects after the policy expires.

2012 is a transition period after the end of the policy. For home appliance enterprises, how to quickly摆脱the dependence on policies, intensify research and development of new products suitable for market demands, and win the market with excellent product quality and perfect after-sales service is also an important topic.
