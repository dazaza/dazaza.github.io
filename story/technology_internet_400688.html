College student fined 80,000 yuan for 'practicing medicine without a license' after buying whitening injections online

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:25:34

A Female College Student Fined 80,000 Yuan for "Illegal Medical Practice"

Source: News Evening Post, November 12, 2010

A female student at a university in the city injected her classmates with "whitening injections" purchased online, causing an allergic reaction. Due to a weak legal awareness, this female student did not realize she was engaging in "illegal medical practice." To date, she has not only compensated the other party with 50,000 yuan but also been fined 30,000 yuan by the Shanghai Health Supervision Institute for practicing medicine without a license.

Experts remind that when purchasing drugs online, one should choose websites with an Internet Drug Information Service Qualification Certificate and consult a physician or pharmacist beforehand. Additionally, medical beauty treatments should be sought at regular and qualified medical institutions, and self-administration must be avoided.

[Event Recap]

Purchased "Whitening Injections" Online and Administered Them Privately

Xiaoqing (pseudonym) and Yuxing (pseudonym) are classmates at a university in the city. Yuxing is tall and fair-skinned, while Xiaoqing has darker skin. Once, during casual conversation, Yuxing told Xiaoqing that her fair skin was due to a certain cosmetic injection she bought online. If Xiaoqing wanted it, Yuxing could purchase it for her online. When Xiaoqing was still hesitating whether to go to a medical beauty clinic for the injection, Yuxing countered that hospital beauty treatments were very expensive. She also recommended that she had a nurse friend who could help. Thus, Xiaoqing asked Yuxing to buy the "whitening injection" for her.

A few days later, Yuxing obtained the injection. That day, Xiaoqing went to Yuxing's house and met her nurse friend, Xiaoshi.

The set of injections purchased online was a four-pack called "Green-well" whitening injections, complete with saline, distilled water, and disposable syringes. Yuxing said that these injections required four weekly doses and charged Xiaoqing 2,500 yuan for the injections. Consequently, Xiaoshi administered one dose of the whitening injection intravenously to Xiaoqing.

A week later, Xiaoqing returned as scheduled. That day, Xiaoshi couldn't make it, so Yuxing offered to administer the injection herself. To reassure Xiaoqing, she claimed she frequently injected herself with cosmetic needles. After calling Xiaoshi to ask how to prepare the injection, Yuxing began injecting Xiaoqing. However, lacking professional medical training, she had no idea how to locate veins or insert the needle properly. Soon, Xiaoqing's hand began to swell. Panicking, Yuxing stopped the IV drip, stored the injection in the refrigerator, and arranged for Xiaoqing to return when Xiaoshi had time.

Fatigue and Fainting After Injection Leads to Complaints to Health Supervisors

A few days later, Xiaoqing returned again. That day, Xiaoshi was present. Yuxing took out the injection from the refrigerator and prepared to inject Xiaoqing again. Shortly after Xiaoshi administered the injection and left,

about half an hour later, Xiaoqing felt cold all over, weak, and almost fainted. Yuxing quickly hailed a taxi and took Xiaoqing to Huadong Hospital nearby for treatment. After emergency care, Xiaoqing's symptoms gradually improved. Upon hearing the news, Xiaoqing's parents rushed to the hospital and demanded that Yuxing compensate for the medical expenses caused by the injection of the beauty needle. However, due to disputes over compensation costs, Xiaoqing eventually filed a complaint with the Shanghai Health Bureau Health Supervision Institute with her medical records.

[Health Supervisor's Explanation]

Collaboration with Police to Locate Parties Involved and Initiate Investigation

Upon investigation, the set of "Green-well" (Granville) whitening injections purchased online turned out to be "L-Cysteine-CEC" whitening injections, mainly composed of Vitamin C and B. Initially, the staff of the municipal health supervision institute judged this to be a typical case of non-physician medical practice.

To quickly locate Yuxing and verify the case, the municipal health supervision institute activated the joint platform against illegal medical practices and promptly contacted the police department for assistance in finding Yuxing. Using special surveillance technology, the police department tracked down the parties involved. A few weeks later, they located her. Initially, Yuxing was uncooperative, even believing that she was helping her classmate and that administering just a beauty injection didn’t count as practicing medicine.

After continuous education and publicity by the supervisors, Yuxing gradually realized that her actions constituted a form of medical aesthetic behavior. Ji Li, Deputy Captain of the Enforcement Team of the Shanghai Health Bureau Health Supervision Institute, stated that according to the regulations of the "Practicing Physicians Law," the health supervision department fined Yuxing less than 100,000 yuan for non-physician medical practice.

[Reporter’s Investigation]

Online Purchased "Beauty Injections" Cost Less Than 100 Yuan

Yesterday, I searched for "whitening injections" on Taobao and found numerous types of whitening injections. Some claimed to be imported from abroad, while others were purportedly domestically produced. Most sold for a few hundred yuan each, with some as cheap as sixty to seventy yuan. Many sellers claimed that buyers could inject themselves.

One seller said they had sold dozens of units and noticed effects after just one injection. When I asked if one could inject oneself, the seller replied that following the instructions was simple. "If you don't want to do it yourself, you can take it to the hospital and have a doctor do it." Those who buy such "beauty" and "face slimming" injections online and inject them themselves are usually budget-conscious individuals seeking beauty enhancements. Compared to regular hospitals where injections cost at least a thousand to several thousand yuan, the prices online are significantly cheaper. Some buyers claimed "no adverse reactions"; however, some expressed "no noticeable effect"; others bought them but were too scared to inject and decided to sell them online instead.

Self-Injection Can Easily Cause Adverse Reactions

From the Renji Hospital Plastic Surgery Department, we learned about a woman who experienced eyeball inversion after privately injecting "face slimming" injections. This 30-something white-collar worker blindly trusted a friend's recommendation, purchased a "face slimming" injection allegedly imported from abroad, and injected it with her friend's help. After one injection, her eyes could no longer close properly, and she slept with her eyes open.

In the past six months, the Time Plastic Surgery Hospital has received six to seven similar cases. Many patients bought "face slimming" injections themselves and invited someone to inject them at home. Some developed crooked mouths, stiff expressions, or asymmetrical faces. "Not having any effect after self-injecting 'face slimming' injections is already quite lucky!" industry insiders remarked.

Authorities at the Ninth People's Hospital Plastic Surgery Department reminded citizens not to inject beauty products themselves, as the consequences could be unimaginable.

Low-Priced Injections Are Mostly "Counterfeit Products"

Many plastic surgery experts questioned the "beauty injections" sold online. Industry insiders stated that any institution without a specific license cannot legally obtain "face slimming" injections, let alone sell them online. These people selling "face slimming" injections through illegal channels often claim they import them from abroad or dilute a single "face slimming" injection into multiple portions.

Additionally, the low prices of "face slimming" injections online raise doubts among experts. Currently, domestic "face slimming" injections used in hospitals generally cost between one to two thousand yuan per unit, while imported ones cost five to six thousand yuan. However, those sold online only cost a few hundred to even just tens of yuan.

Industry insiders analyzed that most "face slimming" injections circulating online are counterfeit products. These "face slimming" injections lack approval numbers and expiration dates. One seller privately revealed that there are many brands of "face slimming" injections on the market with varying quality levels. Products sold online below wholesale price are likely high-quality imitations.

[Expert Reminder]

Medical Beauty Should Be Done at Regular Hospitals; Online Drug Purchases Require Accredited Websites

Despite knowing that the products might be counterfeits, some people still risk DIY whitening or face slimming. Ordinary people unfamiliar with anatomy may inaccurately inject, use excessive concentration or dosage, leading to unimaginable consequences. Even plastic surgeons without specialized injection training may find it challenging. Moreover, before injection, doctors usually conduct consultations to check for allergies or diseases related to heart, liver, and kidneys. The refrigerated injection drugs need to be managed by specialists.

Deputy Captain Ji Li of the Enforcement Team of the Shanghai Health Bureau Health Supervision Institute reminded beauty enthusiasts to seek medical beauty treatments at regular and qualified medical institutions, checking their "Medical Institution Practicing License" before visiting. If non-medical institutions conduct medical beauty treatments, complaints can be made to the health supervision departments at all levels.

Additionally, when purchasing drugs online, choose websites with an Internet Drug Information Service Qualification Certificate and recognize the "National Medicine Approval Number" to avoid scams. Before buying online, consult professional physicians or licensed pharmacists.
