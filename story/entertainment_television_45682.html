As expected, a black-clothed figure attempts to destroy the body of the deceased Butterfly. To prevent this, Mei steps forward and engages in a fierce battle with the black-clothed assassin. When she learned about Wen's vow, she rushed to confess everything to him. Everyone worries that Wen might attempt suicide, but instead, Wen decides to rally the townsfolk to stop Dong from executing Youliang. The group carrying the corpse suffers relentless pursuit, and eventually, they decide Mei should escape alone with the body while the rest stay behind to hold off the pursuers.

Episode 7:

Shang leads the townspeople in protest, but Dong decides to suppress them with force. Suddenly, the Minister of Personnel Yao appears and decides that Shang and Dong will jointly preside over the case, with Shang serving as the main judge. Mei finally brings the body back to the yamen. While examining the body, Wen and Shang discover another wound on it. Just as Wen is about to prove that the murderer is Gui, the farming protectors bring back Huang Xiumei, the madam of Hangzhou's brothel, accusing her of killing Butterfly. The case now presents "one body, two suspects." The next day, Shang makes numerous mistakes during the trial, prompting Dong to criticize him publicly. Eventually, the two even make a bet on their official caps. Shang invites Yao to the courtroom and dramatically reconstructs the case like a play, successfully clarifying all the doubts of the murder case. After losing his cap, Dong gives it to Wen, who is persuaded by Shang to return it to Dong at a banquet that evening.

Episode 8:

Wen does not return the cap and instead publicly accuses Dong of being a cruel official, kicking the cap to pieces. Dong vows revenge. Yao announces Dong's transfer and decides to promote Shang to the position of county magistrate. To thank everyone, Shang specially takes them to Yuruqiong Tower to "drink flower wine." They meet the beautiful songstress Tang Shui and Perfume, and Ting falls in love with Tang at first sight. Shang discovers the emptiness of the county yamen treasury and finds out that Tian owes twenty years of taxes. Fortunately, Shang devises a plan to successfully recover the tax money. Shang donates half a year's salary to organize a grand celebration for the townsfolk during the New Year's Eve festival. Mei and Shang participate in the activities and realize they have fallen deeply in love. On his way home at night, Tian meets Xiang and helps her drive away stray dogs. When Tian returns home, his family notices something unusual about him. A fire breaks out in the abandoned Chenghuang Temple. Everyone works hard to extinguish the fire, and Mei discovers a charred corpse while searching the scene.

Episode 9:

Ting uses the investigation of the charred corpse's identity as an opportunity to show off his excellent qualities in front of Tang. Xiang's mother, Chen Qiao, reports to the yamen that her daughter Xiang has gone missing. She confirms from the remains that the charred corpse is indeed Xiang. Wen examines the scene and concludes that Xiang may have been murdered. Everyone helps Ting get closer to Tang, but unfortunately, Tang's grandmother Tang Yi gets along very well with Ting. In embarrassment, they use sleeping medicine to render Yi unconscious. Shang discovers that Xiang's lover Xu Yaocheng is suspicious, and more surprisingly, Qiao claims to have seen her daughter's ghost. Tang actively invites Ting to eat noodles with her and specifically tells him that on the night of the incident, she saw Tian with Xiang and heard Tian offer money to spend the night with her.

Episode 10:

Eighth Auntie leads the girls to drum and appeal for justice, demanding that Shang handle the matter fairly. Ting volunteers to investigate Tang further but mistakenly enters Grandma Yi's room; he disguises himself as a masked rapist to escape. Sister Mei monitors Cheng and finds that though he pretends to be sick, he cooks medicine. She takes the leftover herbs to Wen for inspection. Shang wants Tian to testify, but Lan insists on stopping him, even engaging in a fierce fight with Mei. The guards try to use knockout smoke, but end up getting knocked out themselves. Luckily, Mei convinces both elders. In court, Tian still gives false testimony, so Shang has no choice but to imprison him. While studying the case with Wen, Wen is inspired by a butcher and re-examines the charred corpse, ultimately discovering that the body is not Xiang's. The constables invite Cheng to the yamen for questioning and trick him into revealing where Xiang is hiding. It turns out that Xiang is still alive, secretly hiding in a cave on a small island.
