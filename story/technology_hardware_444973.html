Corporate website construction, is it better to have a dynamic or static website - Ruich Network

by anonymous on 2011-06-13 09:56:40

In this Web 2.0 era, many companies have started to focus on the interactive experience between their websites and users. Previously, even if a company had its own website, it did not realize the importance of promoting itself and its products online. Usually, customers could only access the website through business cards to find out more. Nowadays, companies are paying attention to online marketing, hoping to promote their websites and increase sales through them. This raises the bar for web development companies. We need to not only make the websites visually appealing but also SEO-friendly and with a greater emphasis on user experience! As a web development service provider in Shanghai, our clients often ask this question: Is it better to build a static or dynamic website for a business?

Clients generally consider the advantages of a static website. They might have heard from some industry insiders that static websites are better for search engine indexing. This is true; static URLs are indeed easier for search engines to index. Search engines do not like complex URLs, such as those with special symbols like & and ?. These symbols usually appear in dynamic websites.

However, I believe that the disadvantages of static websites outweigh the benefits, mainly because they are inconvenient for data updates. Static websites do not have a backend, so if you want to add or delete data, you need to start from the page itself, which requires professional skills. Most companies' website maintenance personnel do not know how to modify codes, and even if they do, it consumes a lot of time. Moreover, not updating the website is not good for SEO because search engines prefer dynamic content. If your website is regularly updated, search engines will frequently visit your site to fetch new data. On the contrary, if your website is rarely updated, the search engine will find the same old content every time it visits, leading it to think that your site has no new content worth fetching. Eventually, it will stop visiting your site altogether, causing your website to be buried deep in the sea of information.

Dynamic websites, on the other hand, have backends that can meet almost all of your needs. Whether it's adding text news about your industry or uploading product images, these tasks can be done by non-professionals within the company. Shanghai-based web development service providers also offer training on backend usage to the company's website maintenance staff, which is highly beneficial for enriching the content of the enterprise website. Additionally, dynamic websites have their own databases, allowing data to be backed up at any time, ensuring that the hard work of adding content won't be lost.

The pros and cons of static and dynamic websites have been mentioned by Shanghai web development companies. The final choice depends on the company's preferences.
