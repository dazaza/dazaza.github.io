Analysis of the Consequences of Not Purchasing Compulsory Traffic Insurance

by lengliu9838 on 2012-02-27 17:10:09

"Unexpectedly, trying to save a few bucks ended up costing me tens of thousands of yuan. This is the consequence of not understanding the law. In the future, I will definitely purchase the corresponding insurance as required by law," said Zhong Xinyi, the owner of the tractor, regretfully after assuming liability for compensation for the damage caused. On August 3, 2010, a road traffic accident compensation dispute case due to not purchasing mandatory third-party liability insurance for motor vehicles (commonly known as compulsory traffic insurance or CTI) resulting in delayed compensation for the injured was settled through mediation at the Luojiang County People's Court.

Zhong Xinyi from a village in Jinshan Town, Luojiang County, bought a tractor to transport goods in rural areas but failed to insure it with CTI due to his weak legal awareness. Consequences of not buying CTI:

On October 17, 2009, at around 7 PM, Zhong Xinyi drove an unregistered tractor along National Highway 108 towards Mianyang from Luojiang. When he turned left at the intersection of Hongyu Village, Jinshan Town, Luojiang County (National Highway 108 km 2124 + 300m), his tractor collided with a CF22U3X ordinary two-wheeled motorcycle driven by Li Xiaoping traveling in the opposite direction from Mianyang to Luojiang. The collision resulted in damage to the motorcycle and injuries to Li Xiaoping and Xiao Dafu who was riding on the motorcycle. According to the traffic police department of Luojiang County, Zhong Xinyi bore the main responsibility for this accident while Li Xiaoping bore the secondary responsibility.

After being injured, Xiao Dafu was sent to the Luojiang County People's Hospital for treatment. He was diagnosed with: mid-shaft comminuted fracture of the left tibia (open); distal comminuted fracture of the left fibula. He was discharged on November 18 of the same year after recovery. A judicial appraisal opinion issued by the Zhengyuan Judicial Appraisal Center of Deyang City determined that Xiao Dafu's injury constituted a tenth-level disability.

Since they could not reach an agreement on compensation, the victim Xiao Dafu had no choice but to file a lawsuit against Zhong Xinyi, Li Xiaoping, and the insurance company where the CF22U3X ordinary two-wheeled motorcycle was insured, demanding compensation of more than forty thousand yuan for various losses on May 18, 2010.

After investigation by the Luojiang County People's Court, it was found that the tractor purchased by Zhong Xinyi was not legally registered or insured with CTI, while the CF22U3X ordinary two-wheeled motorcycle only had CTI coverage.

Since Xiao Dafu was a passenger on the motorcycle and thus a third party relative to the tractor, he should have been compensated first within the CTI limit of the tractor. However, since Zhong Xinyi did not insure the tractor with CTI, Xiao Dafu's losses could only be borne by the responsible parties. Although the motorcycle was insured with CTI, Xiao Dafu was not considered a third party relative to the motorcycle and therefore could not apply the CTI terms. Zhong Xinyi's wife was ill and disabled, unable to take care of herself, requiring long-term treatment, which made their family economically very difficult. Zhong Xinyi declared that he had no money but was resolute. What to do? Legally making a judgment would certainly be correct procedurally and substantively, but it might affect social stability and escalate conflicts.

The presiding judge decided to delay the judgment and organize another round of negotiations between both parties and the insurance company. The insurance company believed that the plaintiff's injury did not meet the CTI terms and refused compensation. The judge then explained the law to the defendant Zhong Xinyi, informing him according to Articles 17 and 76 of the Road Traffic Safety Law: the state implements a mandatory third-party liability insurance system for motor vehicles; in case of traffic accidents involving motor vehicles causing personal injury or property loss, the insurance company shall compensate within the liability limit of the mandatory third-party liability insurance... If CTI is not purchased, the losses incurred can only be borne by the owner of the offending motor vehicle. Through the patient explanation and analysis of the law by the judge, Zhong Xinyi realized his mistake of not legally purchasing CTI and also understood the responsibilities he should bear. Finally, both parties voluntarily reached a mediation agreement: Zhong Xinyi would pay 35,000 yuan, Li Xiaoping would pay 10,000 yuan, and Xiao Dafu would waive other claims. After signing the mediation document, Zhong Xinyi expressed the regret mentioned at the beginning of this article.

Recently, the Huqiu District People's Court of Suzhou City concluded a traffic accident personal injury compensation case. Due to not purchasing mandatory traffic accident liability insurance (CTI) and only subscribing to commercial third-party liability insurance, the part of the loss to the victim that could not be compensated within the CTI range had to be borne by themselves.

On May 5, 2007, employee Hua of Gusu Company drove one of the company's vehicles and collided with pedestrian Chen, injuring him (later assessed as a tenth-level disability). The traffic police determined that both parties bore equal responsibility. Chen sued the court in December 2008, demanding that the insurance company where Gusu Company was insured assume compensation responsibility within the CTI limit. Upon investigation, it was found that Gusu Company had insured two phases of commercial third-party liability insurance (from March 20, 2006, to March 19, 2007; from March 20, 2007, to March 19, 2008) but did not purchase CTI. After the implementation of the CTI regulation on July 1, 2006, the company still did not purchase CTI. Instead, it subscribed to commercial third-party liability insurance (the Supreme Court has clearly defined such insurance as commercial insurance). This meant that Gusu Company could not request the insurance company to pay CTI compensation but had to assume compensation responsibility within the CTI limit itself. For the part of the plaintiff's loss exceeding the CTI limit, it would be shared according to the accident responsibility ratio between the plaintiff Chen and the defendant Gusu Company. Gusu Company could only claim commercial third-party liability insurance compensation from the insurance company after pre-paying the above-mentioned losses, and the commercial third-party liability insurance would determine the compensation ratio based on the insured's responsibility in the accident.

The court rejected the plaintiff's demand for the insurance company to assume compensation responsibility within the CTI limit, ordering Gusu Company to compensate the plaintiff for medical and disability-related losses amounting to 58,000 yuan within the CTI limit. For the part of the plaintiff's loss exceeding the CTI limit, it would be shared according to the responsibility ratio of both parties.

As a result, Gusu Company lost the compensation protection provided by CTI (which does not consider the insured's responsibility ratio in the accident), making its actions counterproductive.
