What has passed, is gone.

Submission online

The friendship I cherished in my memory was left behind by you in the dust of time.

The closer you are to me, the farther away you are. That day, watching your retreating back, familiar yet strange, I suddenly realized that your world had nothing to do with me long ago.

For so many years, we have not been in touch much. What I still wait for, is just our past.

I recall those days we spent together, when we were constantly together, side by side. We laughed and drank freely; we envisioned the future and squandered our youth. Those days gone by, how unforgettable they are.

Perhaps, because it is forbidden. I can't share your emotional world. Even though, you are a good friend, enthusiastic and righteous.

Your heart is like a small city, locked with your passion and romance, innocence and purity. You only open it for the ones you wish. Thus, I watch you sink, unable to extricate yourself, yet powerless to help.

You fell in love passionately, got married, had children, then divorced and became single again. You broke out of the marriage trap on your own, but then like a moth drawn to a flame, you fell in love with a man inside the marriage trap. Standing alone before reality and convention, you charged ahead, ultimately ending up bloodied and scarred. You indulged in self-love and self-abuse, living in a one-person love affair, how brave and disdainful. After crawling through the world of emotions you insisted on pursuing for so many years, I really don't know if you've gained the wisdom a mature woman should have.

Ten years have passed, you are still beautiful and fashionable, yet still lonely and isolated. No matter how strong and independent you appear on the surface, I can still imagine how helplessly you cried in the dark nights. I know that you still need care, support, and dependence. Because, you are a woman.

Of course, some things can only be understood and realized by oneself suddenly. Such realization does not need guidance or preaching, like a sudden enlightenment, making one suddenly clear, without any entanglement or distress. Instead, there's only acceptance and calmness, growth and wisdom.

For a long time, I was always waiting, waiting. In the waiting, I ignored the present moment, unwilling to move forward, yet still dragged along by time, tired and helpless. In fact, growing up is a necessity, aging is an inevitability. Living well depends on one's mindset.

Accepting the current self, adapting to everything quietly changed by the magician called time. Only through acceptance, adaptation. What has passed, will forever pass, cannot reappear, better to let go, better to forget.

But, the beauty and touching moments in memory cannot be altered. You really are the friend I keep in my heart.

What has passed, is gone. But you all, make me grow all the time.
