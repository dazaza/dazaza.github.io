Prologue

In one's life, there are always some people who leave an indelible mark. From strangers to acquaintances, they go through the journey of knowing each other and reach a blissful relationship. But ultimately, they part ways, embarking on separate journeys of love. At this moment, they once again become strangers.

Some say they once loved each other, while others say they hurt each other. Thus, the phrase "love until it hurts" was born. Some say that as long as they love each other, distance is not an issue, but others say that distance disrupts their loving thoughts. Hence, the saying "love stranded by distance."

We are the post-90s generation, and in our eyes, we also have our own concept of love. Yet, in the face of certain matters, we sometimes defy our own wishes and act methodically. I don't know if we're confused by something, numbing our nerves. Regarding some things, we tremble with caution, yet pretend not to care at all. Perhaps, in everyone's eyes, there's a bit of confusion and bewilderment.

Our era is bound to have its little problems. Regarding love, we all have unique perspectives. When giving advice, we speak so profoundly and reasonably, but when these situations happen to us, we feel the world is falling apart, even obsessing over trivialities. The phrase "passing through a sea of flowers without touching a leaf" once expressed such nonchalance, but now it's no longer present. What was once "not hanging oneself on one tree" has now become a complex excuse.

Facing our own feelings, we are so irrational, and even though we love each other, we end up burying each other's hearts. In the moment we turn away, we suddenly realize and regret our former naivety and mischief, regretting pretending not to care. We've hurt not just a heart, but a love that the other person values more than life itself. Perhaps, we just wanted to irritate the other party, making them care, but doing so repeatedly loses all meaning.

After reading so many novels and watching so many TV dramas about love, we finally understand love hastily. However, even if we possess love, we may not necessarily know how to nurture it. Loving someone is simple, and loving each other isn't difficult either. The challenge lies in continuing to walk together without quarrels or awkwardness, every day being warm and happy. I believe that's hard. Until the other person grows old and still holds each other in their hearts—that's love.

When we were still naive, the movie "Those Years, Together We Chased Girls" became wildly popular, garnering immense attention. The reason was its authentic story, which vividly replayed the youthful details of Ke Jingten and Shen Jiayi's romance, one that many admired.

Actually, each of us has our own story, a memory that remains untold and unnamed. It's like a little-known secret that can't be spoken aloud. We continuously mask the gloom within, and despite the sunshine on our faces tinged with slight blushes, we still bury that emotion deep inside. Perhaps because we lack the charm of authors like Jiubadao, we might not have the opportunity to transform our memories into fascinating stories. But I think that's good; at least both parties still remember and have the capital to reminisce about how things were back then.

But, that Shen Jiayi hidden deep in our hearts—a girl whose sight alone makes us dream endlessly, whose big eyes and pouting lips captivate us—do you still remember her clearly? Not everyone has the luck of Ke Jingten to play and release sky lanterns with her in the snow. Even if given the chance to yearn for her day and night, Ke Jingten ultimately couldn't hold her hand and stay with her till the end.

But those people, those memories, those stories, we must remember because they walked alongside us during our youth. These are already enough for mutual reminiscence.

When we make solemn vows about love, we are struck down heavily by "Beijing Love Story." We always thought love was supposed to be like that, but at this point, we are forced to change our optimistic views on love. Love, at certain times, can become utterly worthless. And these are unforeseeable. One cannot help but admit that beneath those seemingly illusory loves hides a small secret. Sometimes, love is so fragile. These moments don't even leave behind any fleeting warmth.

I really don't know how to comment on the complicated love between them. Simple love in real life already causes headaches, let alone complex love chains. Actually, real life exposes our false notions of love, but can we do well by the ones we love? I believe this is not just their story but also the future story we each will face.

As we grow older, there will always be people entering our lives wearing different masks. What should we do? How should we handle friendship and love? We are growing up, maturing constantly, but regarding love, we seem extremely mature.

Some people always like to reminisce, recording their beautiful pasts in diaries. Even if that fleeting love scorched their eyes and shattered their exhausted hearts, those tiny moments, those warm instants, will last forever. That's love. A heart-wrenching experience filled with laughter and joy, ultimately escaping not from a tragic ending.

Throughout life, intertwined with love, there will inevitably be pain, but also warm happiness. The reason for pain lies in pursuing the wrong thing.

Love is like the tide, leaving many eternal moments in its ebb and flow.

Love is simple. As long as the beloved is happy, that's enough.

Content Summary

Nowadays, in the real world, we each play out our own brilliance, squandering our youth. We even feel that among all living beings, under the vast sky, many emotions are pessimistic. Even if wasted, those invested emotions seem unchanged.

It feels like in just a few short years, we saw "Snail House" in carefree days, which brought our moods to rock bottom; just as we escaped from those optimistic yet worldly emotions, thinking love was warm while waiting for "The Pursuit of Happiness," "Beijing Love Story" hit us hard again. We should have embraced the happiness destined for the post-90s generation, but instead silently struggled in the world of emotions.

Faced with this complex world, Jimmy, a child who struggles daily in the emotional realm, harbors a slight hostility towards everything. His loneliness can only be expressed through a pen. Beneath his weak exterior lies a soul as murky as a stream. He smiles happily every day, but it's so fake, so immature, until the smile strains his upturned mouth corners, then he calms down and faces his silent world. He likes writing poetry but often asks himself, "Who is my warm reader?"

Life always brings prosperity unexpectedly. It's not about "thirty years east of the river, thirty years west," even a year, or perhaps a month, can bring unexpected surprises. Just like that, a girl who always smiled entered Jimmy's world. Her name was Janie. She came from the beautiful Sunlight City—Lhasa. Janie stayed close to Jimmy because he happened to be her desk mate. They ate together, studied together, and thus Janie learned many secrets Jimmy didn't want to share with others. Perhaps with time, Jimmy gradually developed feelings for Janie. But he didn't dare tell her, fearing that their friendship would lose its value due to his words.

Days passed, and Jimmy wrote poems for Janie that she pretended not to understand. In fact, no one knew that Janie also liked writing poetry and was familiar with Nalan Rongruo. How could she not understand Jimmy? But she didn't dare cross the line. Both feared their friendship might collapse.

Perhaps fate enjoys playing tricks on Jimmy. One day, Janie received news that she would attend a high school in Beijing. Faced with this situation, they both cried. Unlike before, this time Jimmy held back his tears, not wanting Janie to worry; whereas the once strong Janie couldn't control her inner sorrow and burst into tears, as if they were facing a life-and-death farewell.

On the night Janie was about to leave, they sat silently on the cold bench by the lotus bay where they had spent time together. Occasionally, Janie would say something like "the future is long," but Jimmy understood that after this separation, he might never see Janie again. Or if he didn't confess now, what if Janie found a boyfriend in Beijing? Thinking more made him sadder, and Jimmy shouted, "Talented scholars drift away with the wind, leaving you to listen to whom?" Janie laughed and hugged Jimmy's arm for the first time, quietly saying, "Idiot, we'll meet again. Don't you have more to say to me?" Jimmy's face turned red.

That night, they chatted late into the night. The stars shone brightly, and the moon scattered its light across the ground. The mottled shadows concealed their uneasy hearts about the impending separation. Then, Janie gave Jimmy a beautifully crafted box but mysteriously forbade him from opening it right away. They chatted until they were very tired and returned to the dormitory to rest.

Back in the dormitory, Jimmy, unable to resist his curiosity, rashly opened the box. Inside was a note telling Jimmy not to look at it now. So, Jimmy put the box aside. Maybe Janie anticipated Jimmy's curiosity and wrote it accordingly.

The next morning, Jimmy woke up early, carrying Janie's luggage, and watched her board the bus. Janie didn't look back, fearing meeting Jimmy's eyes that wanted to keep her. She simply waved and said, "Zaxidele." The bus disappeared into the blurry horizon.

Watching Janie leave, Jimmy quickly returned to the dormitory and opened the box Janie left behind. Instantly, Jimmy was stunned. It turned out that in Janie's heart, Jimmy was important. Unfortunately, they had missed the intimate time. Days passed monotonously.

Even in the days they wouldn't meet again, Jimmy and Janie kept in touch, everything calm and peaceful. They expressed their sweet love through letters. They didn't care about the distance, innocently believing that as long as they cherished each other, the distance wouldn't hinder their longing steps.

I want to ask you, do you believe in the statement that distance is not an issue? You may nod, but distance can widen the emotional gap between two people. In the loneliest days, it's easiest to be swayed by others. Life is like a dream, shedding a tear. When Jimmy was feeling low, another empathetic girl appeared before him. Jimmy confided his love for Janie to Anna and even let her slowly appreciate the poems he wrote for Janie. This way, Anna understood the plump yet emotionally complex Jimmy. She treated Jimmy sincerely.

However, spending too much time together, Jimmy inadvertently fell for this girl named Anna. Although Anna was good to Jimmy, she didn't like him. She thought Jimmy was a nice person, suitable to be a lifelong friend.

Jimmy lost Janie and couldn't gain Anna, and his heart instantly froze. In these dark days, he frequently argued with the adorable Anna, and remembering Janie, Jimmy felt guilty inside. Jimmy struggled bitterly in the whirlpool of emotions but couldn't find the door to escape. Seeing Jimmy's worried face every day, Janie felt bad, but what could she do? She herself didn't know. Perhaps Jimmy didn't know what he really wanted either.

Ultimately, Janie chose to leave, going to a strange city, escaping the world's venting. But she couldn't forget those beautiful memories, like enduring fragile feelings. Thus, she silently wrote "Goodbye, Jimmy" in her notebook, and the story began here.

Chapter One: Silent Classroom

"Ding ling ling..."

The bell rang like a death knell. The students unwillingly returned to their seats, preparing for the upcoming despised Chinese class. Everyone has their favorite subjects, but Chinese has become the class unanimously disliked in the grade and won the title of "Most Boring Class" in the election for boring classes.

Jimmy, a boy short in stature with slightly dark skin, due to a lack of excessive love in childhood, always acts cautiously. Because of lacking listeners, he always uses a pen to record the bits and pieces of his life. He likes reading, likes writing, and always quietly stays in the corner of the front row, reading his favorite novels and silently feeling melancholic.

As usual, Jimmy took out a novel, opened it, and laid it flat under the textbook. Carefully but monotonously reading, the classroom remained chaotic, and the Chinese teacher continued writing and drawing on the blackboard.

"Dong dong dong...", a hurried knock on the door made the originally quiet classroom slightly disordered. The students whispered, guessing what would happen next. None of this affected Jimmy, who was immersed in his novel.

"Teacher Sun, this is Jannie from the National Middle School, coming here to study Mandarin. Please arrange a seat for her," said Principal Yang, followed by a girl with a ponytail, slightly dark but handsome, like a fine jade carving, catching everyone's eyes. At this moment, this girl walked straight to the empty seat beside Jimmy, which had been vacant for several months.

Jannie gently placed her schoolbag and quietly sat down, not wanting to disturb the boy who looked so tranquil and focused beside her. At this moment, Jimmy still didn't raise his head to observe what was happening in front of him. Jannie also quietly took out her textbook and silently read it.

At this moment, Teacher Sun approached them and asked Jannie some questions. When he saw Jimmy flipping through the magazine under the textbook, he didn't bother to interfere. He just coughed lightly. Jimmy seemed to sense something and skillfully put the magazine in the drawer. Looking up, he realized that there was such a charming girl beside him. But he acted as if he didn't care.

"Ding ling ling..." Finally, the eagerly awaited recess bell rang. Some mischievous children rushed out of the classroom quickly, either peeking at someone they secretly admire in another class or hiding in the restroom to smoke secretly. Of course, there were also curious students discussing matters related to Jannie.

"You, hello, my name is Jannie, nice to meet you," Jannie introduced herself in broken Mandarin and extended her hand. Jimmy, who had been quiet for a long time, was at a loss, blushing deeply.

"My name is Jimmy, hello," he lightly touched Jannie's hand and quickly pulled back as if electrocuted. Meanwhile, Jannie secretly smiled at the bashful boy in front of her.

"Jimmy, no wonder you have your own seat. You probably expected to have such a beautiful companion!" Canny teased maliciously. Instantly, the entire classroom erupted in laughter, but Jimmy remained silent, sitting at his seat reading his favorite magazine.

This break was so unrestrained. Originally, all the teachers went to a meeting, only symbolically assigning a bit of homework to the class representative. In reality, the teachers understood that this was a drop in the bucket, but not assigning anything would seem unreasonable. So it had to be this way.

Despite the class representative and the monitor trying to maintain order, the classroom was still in chaos. The sounds of playful laughter were so loud it felt like the roof would be lifted off, creating noise that made people feel restless. However, this liveliness made the students feel comfortable, having been suppressed for too long.

Suddenly, Jimmy felt someone poke his back. Turning around to see what it was, the student behind him handed him a note, saying it was from Canny. Jimmy knew that with someone like Canny (a wolf), nothing good could come from it. Thinking it best not to read it, he crumpled it up to throw it away.

Jannie quickly snatched the note, waving it in front of Jimmy and proudly unfolding the note that made Jimmy feel embarrassed. Unexpectedly, Jannie pointed at the words "Dare to kiss her, and I'll treat you to a meal" and asked Jimmy what it meant. Jimmy's face immediately turned red, and many people behind him laughed even louder. At this moment, Jannie seemed to have anticipated something and stopped asking further questions.

People always feel that happy days pass quickly, and time slips away unnoticed, just as students feel that classes without teachers pass quickly. In the blink of an eye, the bell rang again. Even serious students felt that the class seemed unproductive.

It was lunchtime, and unfamiliar with the surroundings, Jannie decided to follow Jimmy to the cafeteria. Jimmy happily agreed, thinking that at least in the cafeteria, they wouldn't feel so lonely. At least it wouldn't ruin the scenery.

Thus, Jannie followed Jimmy, lost in thought, almost bumping into an older student. She wasn't sure what she was thinking, just wandering aimlessly.

For some reason, when Jimmy was alone walking from the classroom to the cafeteria, he always thought the road was long, but now it felt like they arrived in no time. Perhaps he realized that with someone else, especially a girl like Jannie, the journey felt shorter.

Sitting on the chairs in the cafeteria, they didn't say much, chatting casually. After finishing their meal quickly, Jimmy returned to the classroom to continue reading his favorite novel. At this moment, Jannie paid more attention to the boy who seemed so aloof. She guessed that Jimmy must have his own secrets that he didn't want to share, and perhaps she could help him.

Jannie then returned to the classroom. During the noon break, all the students went back to the dormitories to rest, leaving the large classroom eerily quiet. Jannie quietly rested her chin on the table, watching Jimmy read with fascination, and couldn't help but laugh softly. At this moment, Jimmy realized his embarrassing state.

He nervously wiped the tears from the corner of his eyes and stammered to Jannie, "This little story is so moving." Jannie seemed to understand.

"In fact, everyone has one or two touching stories about themselves; we just don't know how to uncover them."

Jimmy looked up at the slightly dark-skinned Jannie in front of him, and his heart suddenly softened, as if he had found a lifeline, feeling so happy and excited. He thought: perhaps this girl named Jannie could be the warm reader he needed.

During this quiet noon, when others were sleeping, the two discussed their dreams, futures, and even many secrets in the classroom. All of this was unexpected by Jannie. Slowly, she understood Jimmy's fragility and offered her sincere heart to give Jimmy sister-like warm love. These actions gradually resonated with Jimmy's lonely heart.

One or two hours passed quickly, and the students gradually returned to the classroom. During this noon, Jannie didn't have enough time to fully understand Jimmy, but she knew that in the days ahead, she should treat Jimmy well. With her warm heart, she hoped to heal Jimmy's inner wounds. Perhaps only in this way could she make him lively and open-minded, allowing him to appreciate her. Jannie thought silently in her heart.

Thus, Jimmy confessed all his experiences to Jannie, telling her how his fragile heart often shattered into pieces. He told Jannie everything, believing that she wouldn't mock his stories. He even felt that Jannie was a child worth completely trusting.

The afternoon schedule was somewhat exciting. Why? Except for music and physical education, the remaining four classes were all self-study periods. Although the homeroom teacher would occasionally visit during self-study, it wouldn't be as boring as regular classes. Finally, Jimmy's face lit up with a rare smile. He thought: finally, he could freely and boldly read his favorite novels.

During the music class, the teacher suggested that students entertain themselves by singing. Immediately, the class became lively. Perhaps they enjoyed relaxing in this way, or perhaps they wanted to exchange things beyond academics.

"Teacher, according to Jimmy, his desk mate sings very well," Canny grinned mischievously and made a funny face at Jimmy.

"Is that true? Then, Jannie, why don't you sing a few songs for all of us?" The teacher didn't actually know Jannie but had heard from other teachers that she was a self-reliant and progressive child. He believed Jannie wouldn't refuse.

Jimmy couldn't contain himself. For the first time in class, he yelled at them, "Whoever wants to sing can sing, don't drag others into it." After that, the classroom fell silent for a moment.

Jannie instinctively tugged at Jimmy's sleeve. Naturally, she stood up from her chair, cleared her throat, and sang "Let's Love Each Other Well." They were astonished; even Canny fell silent, listening intently to the girl's beautiful voice. It seemed like Jannie had become somewhat unfamiliar to them.

When the song ended, the classroom erupted in applause that lasted for a long time. Clearly, the applause contained affirmation and encouragement. They hadn't expected Jannie to have such a melodious voice, and even Jimmy didn't know until that moment.

Amidst the admiration of the classmates, Jannie quietly sat down. She smiled at everyone, then looked up and saw Jimmy staring at her in doubt, making her feel a bit shy. She smiled faintly at Jimmy, causing a subtle tremor in his heart. He didn't know what this feeling was.

Happy classes always end quickly when the students feel time flying by.

Just as the bell rang, marking the end of the music class and the teacher stepping out of the classroom threshold, inexplicably, several classmates approached Jimmy and Jannie mysteriously, praising Jannie's singing skills and envying Jimmy for having such a talented desk mate. This made Jimmy feel a weak sense of pride, albeit without much confidence. Perhaps it was the first time he didn't feel timid after being praised for so long.

"It's just average! That's my fate!" Jimmy appeared helpless, sighing deeply at the end. Despite appearing troubled, he couldn't hide his inner joy. People are often like this, enjoying praise but pretending to be humble. Jimmy was no exception.

"Ouch, stop being sour! Getting praised but still acting modest!" Canny joked, as usual fond of teasing Jimmy.

"How about we switch seats?" Canny then asked Jimmy, seeming genuinely sincere. But Jimmy found it hilarious; why would a wolf say such empty words? Out of politeness, Jimmy merely shook his head and casually said, "No way." Canny then left cheerfully, leaving Jannie quietly sitting on her chair, smiling at Jimmy's slightly sulky appearance earlier. She found it quite amusing.

The next class was Physical Education, but the PE teacher didn't show up. This only outdoor activity period was temporarily changed to a self-study session. The students were rather reluctant. Someone shouted.

"Why don't we let Jimmy's 'someone' sing a song?"

"Who is Jimmy's 'someone'?"

"You're dumb! Who else? Of course, it's Jannie!"

Everyone chimed in, making Jannie feel a bit shy. But Jimmy felt a small sense of comfort. That feeling was strangely pleasant, something new to him. His heart seemed to undergo a subtle change, but Jannie didn't know.

A sports class, a self-study session, requires silence. Otherwise, attracting the attention of school leaders would lead to undesirable consequences. Therefore, the noisy classroom quickly became quiet. This silence seemed unwilling.

Jimmy couldn't focus on his novel anymore. He secretly wrote on a piece of paper, "Jannie, your voice is so enchanting! Hehe..." and then gently pushed it to Jannie, who was doing her homework.

Jannie smiled at him, drew a smiley face with her pen, and wrote, "Do you like listening?" before pushing it
