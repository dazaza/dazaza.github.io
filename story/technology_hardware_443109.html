Sina Sports News: At 02:45 Beijing Time (19:45 local time in the UK on April 12), a focal match of the second leg of the quarter-finals of the 2010/11 season UEFA Champions League was held at Old Trafford Stadium. Manchester United defeated Chelsea 2-1 at home. Giggs assisted Hernandez to score first, Ramires was sent off for accumulating two yellow cards, and Drogba equalized as a substitute, but Giggs then assisted Park Ji-sung to score the decisive goal. Manchester United advanced to the semi-finals with a total score of 3-1.

Manchester United had won their first away victory against Chelsea in 9 years in the first leg, with Rooney scoring the decisive goal. This was Chelsea's 101st Champions League game. Since their first encounter in the Second Division in 1905, this was the 160th meeting between the two teams, with Manchester United having an advantage with 68 wins, 47 draws, and 44 losses. Chelsea hoped to enter the Champions League semi-finals for the 6th time in 8 years. The Blues had advanced 4 times in previous 6 two-legged knockout ties within England, while Liverpool eliminated them twice. Manchester United also aimed to reach the Champions League semi-finals for the 4th time in 5 years, and they remained unbeaten at home in all competitions this season.

Champions League - Messi Scores 48 Goals to Break Ronaldo's Record, Barcelona Wins Away 1-0 to Advance to Semi-finals

Sina Sports News: At 2:45 AM Beijing Time (8:45 PM local time in Ukraine on April 12), the second leg of the quarter-finals of the Champions League began at the Donbass Arena. Barcelona won 1-0 away against Shakhtar Donetsk, advancing to the semi-finals with a total score of 6-1. Messi scored his 48th goal of the season, surpassing Ronaldo's record of 47 goals in a single season at Barcelona, and became the first player in Spanish football history to score 48 goals in official matches in a single season. Guardiola's team won their first away game in 9 Champions League knockout rounds.

In the first leg, Barcelona won 5-1 at home, almost eliminating any suspense. In European competition history, only 3 teams have overturned a 4-goal deficit in the first leg, all occurring in the UEFA Cup and Intertoto Cup, never in the Champions League. The most remarkable comeback was in the 2004-05 season quarter-finals when RCD Espanyol beat AC Milan 4-0 after losing 1-4 at home. Despite facing the El Clasico weekend, Guardiola still fielded his main players. Compared to the first leg, there was only one change, with Afellay replacing the suspended Iniesta.

Shakhtar Donetsk had a perfect 4-match winning streak at home in the Champions League this season, conceding only 1 goal. Due to injuries, coach Lucescu was forced to make changes in both full-back positions. Captain Srna was injured, and left-back Raz was suspended, giving opportunities to Kobin and Shevchuk.
