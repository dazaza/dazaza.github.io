Otherworldly Hellish Asura: Car washer detained for sending harassing text messages to a woman

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-07 13:58:44

Langzhong, March 5 (Cheng Fei) - On the afternoon of March 5th, it was reported that a scam involving "wine touts" defrauded over 20 million yuan, resulting in prison sentences for those involved. However, the bar owner was found not guilty. According to reports from Hanshufang Novel Network (www.hanshufang.com), which provides more chapters with faster updates, a car washer in Langzhong, Sichuan province, was administratively detained for five days for sending harassing text messages to a woman.

Xiaoyu (a pseudonym) is a staff member at the public security window of the Langzhong Municipal Government Service Center. After work on March 4th, Xiaoyu drove her car to a car wash on Binjiang Road. Here, Zhang Xian (a pseudonym), one of the car wash workers, approached her saying, “Let’s get acquainted; we can help each other in the future.” Seeing Zhang's enthusiasm, Xiaoyu thought of her sisters who run a wedding planning company and thus gave her phone number to Zhang, who seemed eager to help.

On the morning of March 5th, as Xiaoyu was getting ready for work, she suddenly received a strange call: “Sister, are you up yet? I’m still in bed…” Hearing this, Xiaoyu was shocked, realizing she was being harassed, and quickly hung up. Soon after, another unknown number called, repeating similar inappropriate remarks by the same man, who also mentioned seeing her at the car wash the previous day.

Realizing she was dealing with a pest, Xiaoyu ignored the calls and went to work at the government service center. Throughout the day, she received over 20 calls but didn't answer any of them. Zhang then began bombarding her with text messages, some so inappropriate that Xiaoyu deleted them without reading further. Only one message remained on her phone: “Sister, you’re too young, haha~”

Feeling unjustly harassed, Xiaoyu could only cry in the break room. Around 10 a.m., Zhang showed up at the government service center window pointing at Xiaoyu and said, “Why did you scold me? You need to apologize to me.” Due to Zhang's unreasonable纠缠(tan3 chen2, harassment), Xiaoyu couldn't focus on her work. After her colleagues inquired about the situation, she revealed that she had been harassed by an unreasonable man.

Subsequently, her colleague called the police. After investigation, the police confirmed that Zhang harassed women via text messages and disrupted office order. During the investigation, Zhang admitted to his actions and signed the interrogation record. The police legally imposed a 5-day administrative detention penalty on Zhang, making him the first person in the area to be detained for "text harassment."
