Paralympic gold medal coach refused to submit million-dollar bonus and was suspended from duty

by yy2afg7tia on 2012-03-02 15:01:23

Minor Controversy: Should Paralympic Gold Medal Coaches Surrender Their Bonuses?

A photo of Wang Chengrong with his disciples at the Beijing Paralympics hangs on the wall of his home. Now, this gold medal-winning coach has been suspended by the Qinghai Sports Team due to issues regarding bonus distribution. This report was photographed by our reporter Fan Yao.

In 2005, Chinese women's long-distance running star Sun Yingjie caused a stir in the sports world when she sought her bonus from her coach Wang Dexian. Now, Wang Chengrong, her husband, has also run into trouble over bonuses. In 2005, Wang Chengrong was hired by the China Paralympic Management Center as a coach. The two athletes he trained won 3 golds and 1 silver at the 2008 Beijing Paralympics. In October 2011, the China Disabled Persons' Federation rewarded Wang Chengrong with 1.4991 million yuan.

Upon hearing the news, Wang Chengrong's unit, the Qinghai Provincial Physical Education Team (referred to as the Qinghai Physical Education Team), requested that Wang hand over the bonus for re-distribution by the organization. Wang Chengrong refused. On December 28, 2011, the Qinghai Physical Education Team gave Wang Chengrong a suspension. Captain Yang Haining stated, "If he refuses to hand over the money, we have other means."

Winning 3 Golds, Bonus Exceeding One Million Yuan

Wang Chengrong himself wasn't sure of the exact time when the 1.4991 million yuan entered his life, but it certainly changed his life.

[Probably around the end of September or early October last year.] Anyway, one morning, having stayed up late caring for his child, Wang Chengrong, a coach at the Qinghai Physical Education Team, hadn't even gotten out of bed when his phone rang twice. His wife, former famous long-distance runner Sun Yingjie, picked up the phone beside him and found a text message notifying her that the bonus had arrived.

On September 5, 2008, the list of the Chinese delegation for the 13th Paralympics was announced, and Wang Chengrong was included [in the list], being the only coach from Qinghai in the entire track and field team. He led six players in the competition, among whom his disciple Zhang Zhen won two gold medals in the men's 5000 meters and men's 1500 meters T11 category, while Qi Shun won the gold medal in the men's marathon T12 category, and He Cheng'en won the silver medal in the men's 800 meters T36 category. On October 6, 2008, the China Paralympic Management Center sent a letter to the Qinghai Provincial Sports Bureau and the Qinghai Provincial Disabled Persons' Federation, stating that Wang Chengrong "[has worked diligently and responsibly during his tenure, training scientifically, with outstanding results]."

Besides written commendations, there was also the aforementioned bonus. [I knew about the bonus earlier; many coaches had already received their money, except mine hadn't arrived yet, so I even called to urge them.] That same morning after receiving the text message, Wang Chengrong went to the bank and found that 1.4991 million yuan had arrived. This was the largest sum of money Wang Chengrong had ever possessed in his life, [I (previously) estimated it would be quite a lot, and I joked with Sun Yingjie that if it exceeded 800,000 yuan, I wouldn't go to work anymore, I'd stay home to take care of the kids and play with you.]

Since joining the team in 1992, Wang Chengrong has been familiar with bonuses over the past 20 years. Previously, the highest amount he received was in 2001 during the Ninth National Games, where he finished seventh in the men's 10,000 meters, preventing Qinghai from finishing at the bottom, and was rewarded with a 40 square meter house, priced at 1,380 yuan per square meter, totaling approximately 55,000 yuan.

Now that the bonus far exceeds 800,000 yuan, he indeed doesn't need to go to work because of this money, he has been suspended by his unit.

Repeated Negotiations, Each Time Asking for Money

Handing over all the bonuses for re-distribution by the Qinghai Physical Education Team, Wang Chengrong might only receive 20% of the total bonus, which is unacceptable to him.

Before December 28 last year, Wang Chengrong had been unable to sleep well for more than half a month. He was called multiple times by his superior unit for discussions, each time with the same content: hoping that Wang Chengrong would hand over this bonus to the unit for organizational redistribution.

During the first discussion, the captain of the Qinghai Physical Education Team, Yang Haining, asked how much money had been deposited into Wang Chengrong's account. Wang Chengrong kept a bit of a secret and said 900,000 yuan. Wang Chengrong said that although there was no written explanation, according to what he understood, the team already had a distribution plan, and he could only get 20% of the bonus. Calculating based on 900,000 yuan, he would get 180,000 yuan, 300,000 yuan would go to the Qinghai Provincial Disabled Persons' Federation, and 420,000 yuan would return to the Qinghai Physical Education Team.

Wang Chengrong believed that during the period of borrowing to lead the team for the China Disabled Persons' Federation, he and his original unit never had any agreement regarding the distribution of bonuses, [as long as relevant documents are provided indicating that I should hand over the bonus, I will hand over whatever amount is required, but if no document can be produced, I won't hand over a single cent.] Yang Haining claimed that there were related documents within the team, just not convenient to show them. Regarding the redistribution of the bonus, Yang Haining refused to elaborate but indicated that it wouldn't be left to any individual, [do you think this is just one person's matter? How many people have served you? If the original unit didn't send you out, sending anyone else out would be the same.]

On December 28, 2011, the Party Committee of the Qinghai Physical Education Team issued the "Decision on Suspending Comrade Wang Chengrong." The document stated: [The China Disabled Persons' Federation rewarded Comrade Wang Chengrong with a high amount of achievement bonus in October 2011, but the comrade concealed the situation and did not comply with the decision of the team. Comrade Wang Chengrong's behavior of concealing from the organization and disobeying management has created a negative influence among the teaching staff.] Wang Chengrong was suspended, and during the suspension period, his salary and welfare benefits were withheld.

Wang Chengrong still hasn't handed over the bonus, [This is the money awarded personally to me by the China Disabled Persons' Federation, why should I give it away?]

Four Notices Issued, Both Sides Still Deadlocked

The "Suspension Decision" failed to take effect, Wang Chengrong still hasn't handed over the money, and the Qinghai Physical Education Team has issued four more notices.

To take care of his wife and child, Wang Chengrong applied for a position change in July 2010 to work in an office, allowing him to return home every day.

Wang Chengrong and Sun Yingjie's combined monthly salary exceeds 5,000 yuan, providing them with a comfortable living standard in Xining. Besides owning a house in Xining, Sun Yingjie was rewarded with a house in Beijing during the Ninth National Games; they also own a Ford car. Their son, born on August 8, 2010, can now dunk basketballs on a children's hoop.

Without this incident, Wang Chengrong's life was very regular: waking up at 6 am, cooking breakfast, taking the bus at 8 am, arriving at the Daba Base 20 kilometers away at 9 am, eating lunch at the base canteen, leaving work at 5 pm, getting home at 6 pm, and then cooking dinner. But now, his life has changed, [I can't sleep every night, at least until 2 am.]

Following the "Suspension Decision," the Qinghai Physical Education Team issued four more notices, demanding that Wang Chengrong "explain the situation." On February 7, the notice required Wang Chengrong to submit a written report on the "relevant circumstances of participating in the 2008 Beijing Paralympics"; on February 13, it required him to truthfully report the bonus amounts during the borrowed period and the total bonus amount obtained from the Beijing Paralympics, along with providing ticket evidence; on February 17, it required Wang Chengrong to report the accurate bonus amount; on February 23, the fourth notice stated, [Dispatched coaching is official duty, the team has the right to know the work situation of dispatched personnel, including bonus income, and honestly reporting is an obligation that dispatched personnel must fulfill.]

According to staff members of the sports team, Wang Chengrong's suspension decision was posted everywhere. Some people said that they originally didn't know about this matter, but on the thirteenth day of the first lunar month, when they went to collect the Lantern Festival rice balls, they wanted to help Wang Chengrong collect them, but were told that Wang had already lost this benefit.

Wang Chengrong tried to make his attitude softer, responding to each notice on time, but he emphasized that he couldn't provide the bonus amount, hoping that the higher department would confirm it by sending a letter to the sports department of the China Disabled Persons' Federation, [they are nervous and dare not ask others about this money.]

Yang Haining indicated that it wasn't just asking Wang Chengrong to return the bonus, but also redistributing other coaches' bonuses. However, Wang Chengrong believed this was a trap set for him, and the bonus amounts were vastly different, [indeed, some coaches' bonuses were collected, but the amounts were very different, one was over 40,000 yuan, another was over 10,000 yuan, another was over 4,000 yuan, and another was over 1,000 yuan.]
