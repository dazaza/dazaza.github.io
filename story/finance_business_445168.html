Illustrated Explanation of Decoration Terms (II)

by huoe0663 on 2011-06-13 11:49:29

16. Design Drawings

Design drawings include floor plans, ground plans, ceiling plans, elevation views, strong electric wiring diagrams, and weak electric wiring diagrams.

A floor plan is a drawing that assumes the room has no ceiling, showing an overhead view of all furniture arrangements in the room. A detailed floor plan includes dimensions and locations of all furniture and major appliances, as well as the positions and sizes of all walls, windows, and doors.

The ground plan, also known as the floor layout diagram, specifies the size, height, position, laying angle, and name of the tiles or flooring used in each room.

The ceiling plan is similar to the floor plan but shows the installation situation of ceiling fixtures and lights.

An elevation view is what you would see if you looked horizontally from one point in the room towards a specific direction, displaying the location and size of furniture and appliances.

The strong electric diagram refers to the wiring for lights, switches, and outlets.

The weak electric diagram refers to the wiring for telephones, computers, audio, video, speakers, and monitoring systems.

17. 45-Degree Beveling

In kitchens or bathrooms, at the corner edges, the edges of the adjacent tiles are beveled at 45 degrees. This creates a /|| shape. A well-done 45-degree bevel by a skilled craftsman looks better than using corner moldings.

18. Wall Scraping

This involves removing excess objects from the wall, such as protruding concrete or old latex paint, to keep the wall surface clean. It usually takes place before applying putty. In some areas, the step of applying putty is also considered part of wall scraping.

19. Applying Putty / Spreading Putty / Scraping Putty

This is the process of applying putty to the wall to ensure a smooth surface. Typically, three layers are applied, then sanded smooth with sandpaper after drying. This step is done before painting with latex paint.

20. Masking Tape

Painters use masking tape to avoid getting paint on other areas, such as glass doors when painting wood. If not removed promptly, it can leave adhesive residue and may even become impossible to remove.

21. PTFE Tape

PTFE tape is used to seal natural gas pipes and water pipe joints to prevent leaks.

22. Wiring Groove / Concealed Wiring / Concealed Pipes

Wiring groove, also called concealed wiring, involves cutting grooves of a certain depth into walls (usually twice the diameter of the conduit) using a saw or other tools, embedding wires and pipes inside so they are not visible from the outside, improving aesthetics. The grooves should generally be horizontal and vertical, without slanted lines.

Concealed wiring refers to strong/weak electrical wires buried in these grooves, usually encased in conduits. Standard practices dictate using 1.5mm² insulated copper wire for lighting, 2.5mm² for regular outlets, and 4mm² for air conditioning lines to prevent overheating. PVC conduits of 4mm diameter are commonly used.

23. Load-Bearing Walls / Non-Load-Bearing Walls

In construction drawings, thick solid lines and walls under non-load-bearing beams are load-bearing walls. Walls without prefabricated beams are definitely load-bearing.

Non-load-bearing walls are usually marked with thin solid lines or dashed lines on the drawings, made of lightweight, simple materials, and are generally thinner, serving only as partitions.

24. Baseboard

Baseboards serve two main purposes: decorative and protective.

In interior design, cornices, waistlines, and baseboards help create visual balance. Their linear feel, material, and color can enhance the aesthetic appeal indoors.

The protective function of baseboards is significant. As the area prone to kicks and impacts, baseboards strengthen the bond between walls and floors, reduce wall deformation, and protect against damage from external forces. Additionally, baseboards are easy to clean, making them convenient for wiping up splashes from mopping.

25. Wainscot

Wainscoting serves three primary functions: decoration, wall protection, and architectural defect concealment.

1) Decorative Function: Wainscoting divides the wall into upper and lower sections, breaking up large spaces and providing warmth through wooden materials.

2) Protective Function: At about one meter high, wainscoting protects walls from furniture like sofas and chairs.

3) Concealing Defects: Pipes, radiator covers, and electrical outlets can be integrated into the wainscot, reducing focus on less attractive areas.

26. Angle Valve

An angle valve is essentially the switch for a faucet, akin to a light switch. There are many stylish designs available, with various methods of operation including twisting, flipping, or pressing down. The standard size is 4/4 inch angle valve. Sometimes, a 3/4 inch angle valve is needed, where the inlet is 4 inches and the outlet is 3 inches, commonly used for imported faucets. Typically, there are two valves each for hot and cold water in kitchen sinks, two for hot and cold water in water heaters, two for hot and cold water in bathroom basins, and one for the toilet.
