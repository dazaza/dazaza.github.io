Netizens who spread rumors are detained, police deny that onlookers of the demolition were injected with anesthetic needles

by jianfei1895 on 2011-08-25 13:08:58

Southern Daily News (Reported by Ding Jianting) - Recently, netizen "Love Alliance" revealed on their blog that "Anhui journalist Binyu's blog exposed 'violent forced demolition' leading to his detention." Subsequently, netizens began reposting the content across major forums and microblogs, questioning the detention decision made by the Hefei Public Security Bureau and urging the police to disclose the process of handling Binyu.

Upon investigation, it was found that on August 22, the Hefei police indeed "administratively detained a man surnamed Wang for posting false information online," which disrupted public order. The reason was that the man posted online about "Hefei using anesthesia needles against onlookers during violent demolitions," which turned out to be fabricated.

The netizen "disappears," is it due to exposing the forced demolition incident?

The day before yesterday, the blog of netizen "Love Alliance" revealed that "Anhui journalist Binyu was detained after exposing violent forced demolition through his blog."

According to "Love Alliance," on the 21st of this month, Binyu received a call from detectives at the Xinzhan Branch of the Hefei Public Security Bureau asking him to come in for questioning. As of today, there has been no news of him. Friends contacted the local police asking for an explanation but were unable to obtain either the ruling document or Binyu’s phone, suspecting that Binyu's "disappearance" might be due to exposing the forced demolition incident.

According to "Love Alliance," the police detained Binyu because of a recent series of disputes over demolitions between the local government and residents being demolished. Blogger "Binyu" recently published three articles on his blog focusing on this issue: On August 10, "Hefei female resident forced to jump from building due to violent demolition"; on August 13, "Hefei uses anesthesia needles against onlookers during demolition"; on August 16, "Hefei peasant woman severely injured after falling from building during forced demolition referred to as 'accident'."

Yesterday, the news of "Binyu's detention" began spreading widely on forums and microblogs. Prominent netizens like "Ten Years Chopping Firewood" and "Wuyue Sanren" also posted the news on their microblogs, calling on netizens to forward and pay attention. This quickly sparked widespread attention and heated discussions among netizens, becoming one of the hot topics online yesterday.

Police claim "anesthesia needle demolition" is false

On Binyu's microblog, his self-introduction reads: "Binyu, corruption issues expert, media commentator. Published over 3000 news works in central-level media such as People's Daily and provincial newspapers above, won multiple provincial and national journalism awards, received the Excellent Author Award from the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection, participated in writing the Anhui Province Party members and cadres anti-corruption education manual. Currently the director of a certain legal magazine."

His blog posts often focus on social fairness issues, with many contents being critical in nature. His QQ space is named "Binyu's Integrity Space." Netizen "Love Alliance" commented on Binyu saying, "He exposed the dark side, allowing society to see some injustices and provided law enforcement agencies with evidence to combat criminals."

Our reporter learned that netizen "Binyu" is actually surnamed Wang and mostly publishes articles under the pseudonym "Binyu." According to the announcement published on August 22 on the official website of the Hefei Public Security Bureau, Binyu was "administratively detained for fabricating facts and disrupting public order." Local media, such as the Hefei Daily, also reported on this matter.

According to the police, on the 13th of this month, netizen "Binyu" posted an article titled "Hefei violent demolition uses anesthesia needles against onlookers" on his blog. However, according to police investigations, the use of anesthesia needles during demolition was entirely fictitious. "On the day of demolition, all onlookers were outside the warning line. There was no conflict between the demolition party and the onlookers, and no anesthesia needles were found at the scene." Nevertheless, after receiving a QQ message from a displaced household claiming "during the demolition, some onlookers were injected with anesthesia needles," Binyu published an unverified blog post titled "Hefei violent demolition uses anesthesia needles against onlookers." After the post was published, it caused some netizens to discuss and created panic among uninformed people.

Based on this, according to Article 25 of the "People's Republic of China Law on Public Security Administration Punishments," the Hefei police decided to administratively detain Wang for 10 days. They reminded the general public that when posting online, they should not spread rumors or fabricate facts to defame others, otherwise, they will face severe legal consequences.

Lawyer

His actions indeed disrupted public order

Regarding whether Binyu's "anesthesia needle" blog post disrupted public order, lawyer Qing Aiguo from Guangdong Guozhi Law Firm believes that according to Article 25 of the "People's Republic of China Law on Public Security Administration Punishments," the punishment imposed by the Hefei police applies this regulation.

He explained that blogs are open public platforms. Although netizens post articles on their own blogs, every netizen can see them, making blog posts public in nature. The information posted by netizen Binyu on his blog was unverified and "can be judged as negative, potentially causing adverse effects on society." Therefore, Binyu's actions indeed disrupted social public order.

Lawyer Mao Jingwen from Guangdong Honggang Law Firm also largely agreed that Binyu's actions disrupted social public order. However, he also mentioned that "whether a 10-day detention penalty is appropriate depends on the social impact of Binyu's blog post." If the click rate of the article is high, causing panic among citizens and affecting normal demolition work in the area, then a 10-day detention penalty is justified. If the impact of the article is not that serious, an appropriate warning or fine could suffice without the need for detention.
