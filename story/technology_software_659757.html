Grade 8 English Teaching Design

Unit 3 Topic 1 I love collecting stamps.

Section A

Da Jie Middle School: Jin Bang Ballpoint Pen

The main activities are 1a, 2a and 2b. The key activities in this lesson are 1a, 2a, and 2b.

Ⅰ. Teaching aims and demands (Teaching Goals)

1. Review the vocabulary related to hobbies.

2. Learn some useful words and expressions:

- collect

- hobby

- used to do sth.

- be interested in

3. Enable students to talk about things they like.

Ⅱ. Teaching aids (Teaching Tools)

Tape recorder/Poster/Stamp album

Ⅲ. Five-finger Teaching Plan (Five-step Teaching Scheme)

Step 1 Review (Step One: Review) (Time: 12 minutes)

1. (Teacher-student dialogue discussing favorite activities, leading into key sentence patterns and vocabulary.)

T: What do you like doing?

S1: I like swimming.

T: What do you love doing?

S2: I love reading.

T: What do you enjoy doing?

S3: I enjoy playing basketball.

T: What do you prefer doing?

S4: I prefer listening to music.

...

(Write on the board the underlined parts.)

swimming, reading, playing basketball, listening to music

2. (Learn the new word "painting." Teacher says it while making the action, letting students guess its meaning, then writes it on the board.)

T: I love painting.

Painting n. Painting

T: Do you love listening to music?

Ss: Yes, I do.

T: I used to listen to music, but now I love painting.

3. (Introduce the sentence pattern "used to do sth.")

T: I used to listen to music.

What did you use to do?

(Guide students to answer: I used to do sth.)

S5: I used to dance.

S6: I used to skate.

S7: I used to play football.

...

(Write on the board)

used to do sth. Used to do something

4. (Take out a stamp album, show the stamps inside to the students, and introduce them.)

T: Look! There are many stamps here. They are very beautiful and interesting. We can learn a lot from them. They are about people, places, history, and special moments. Some of them are very valuable. Collecting stamps is my hobby.

(Introduce through the explanation, learn the new words "valuable," "collect," "hobby." Write the underlined part on the board with definitions, read together.)

collect Collect, valuable Valuable, hobby Hobby

(Teacher-student dialogue practice.)

T: Are the stamps beautiful?

Ss: Yes, they are.

T: Are they valuable?

Ss: Yes, they are.

T: Do you love them?

Ss: Yes, I do.

T: Collecting stamps is my hobby.

What’s your hobby?

S8: Playing basketball is my hobby.

S9: Collecting coins is my hobby.

...

T: Well, what’s Maria’s hobby? Do you want to know?

(Transition to the next step.)

Step 2 Presentation (Second Step: Presentation) (Time: 5 minutes)

1. (Set and write down listening tasks, let students listen to recording 1a with tasks.)

T: Now listen to 1a and answer the questions.

(1) What’s Maria’s hobby?

(2) What about Michael’s?

T: Well, who knows Maria’s hobby? OK, S1, please.

S1: Her hobby is collecting stamps.

T: Good! What about Michael’s? S2, please.

S2: His hobby is collecting telephone cards and paintings.

T: Very good! Now listen again and answer the questions.

2. (Write down the questions on the board, let students listen to the recording again, answer after listening.)

Why does Maria love collecting stamps?

What does Maria learn from the stamps?

What did Michael use to do?

3. (Let students have dialogues based on the passage, underline key words, explain difficult points according to student conditions.)

(See attached keywords.)

pleased ― beautiful ― collecting stamps ― fun ― learn… from― valuable

― used to ― rock ― now ― painting

Step 3 Consolidation (Third Step: Consolidation) (Time: 8 minutes)

1. (Teacher plays the recording of 1a, model for students to follow and mark stress and intonation with a pencil.)

T: Follow the tape and draw “up and down” with your pencil.

2. (Teacher lets students erase the marked stress and intonation, first read by themselves, then read along with the tape, check pronunciation and intonation sentence by sentence.)

T: Clean the marks and now read and check your pronunciation and intonation sentence by sentence. Go!

3. (Man-machine dialogue, i.e., students dialogue with the recorder.)

T: You are Michael. Listen to Maria and make a dialogue with her.

Or: You are Maria. Listen to Michael and make a dialogue with him.

4. (Students perform 1a in pairs, can use the keywords above, not limited to the original text.)

T: Role-play the conversation.

Step 4 Practice (Fourth Step: Practice) (Time: 12 minutes)

1. (Students look at the pictures in 1b, draw a happy face if they like it, a sad face if they don’t.)

T: Now look at the pictures in 1b. If you like it, draw a happy face. If you don’t like it, draw a sad face.

2. (Pair work in 1c, teacher helps and guides students' practice.)

T: Pair work in 1c. Follow the example to make a dialogue, using the things in the pictures.

For example:

S1: What things do you love collecting?

S2: I love collecting …

S1: Why do you like collecting them?

S2: Because …

(Finally, invite 2-3 groups of students to perform at the front.)

T: Now, come to the front and act the dialogs out.

(Guide students to evaluate the performers, summarize and encourage in time, then transition to 2a.)

3. (Use the poster in 2a for picture description practice to learn how to express "be interested in" or "like doing something" with "be interested in", "prefer", "love", and "enjoy".)

T: Look at the picture. What's Kangkang doing?

Ss: He is reading.

T: Yes. Kangkang prefers reading. Do you prefer reading, S3?

S3: Yes, I do.

T: Good. What's Jane doing?

Ss: She is singing.

T: Yes. Jane loves singing. Do you love singing, S4?

S4: Yes, but I prefer dancing.

T: Very good! What's Maria doing?

Ss: She is listening to music.

T: Yes. Maria enjoys listening to music. Do you enjoy listening to music, S5?

S5: Yes, many students in our class enjoy it.

T: Really? Now, what's Michael doing?

Ss: He is playing basketball.

T: Yes. Michael is interested in playing basketball. Are you interested in it, S6?

S6: No, I'm interested in collecting stamps.

(Underline the new phrases "be interested in" and explain usage, then ask students to make sentences.)

T: "Be interested in sth./doing sth." means "be interested in something/doing something." Now make some sentences with it.

S7: I am interested in English.

S8: I am interested in swimming.

S9: I am interested in flying kites.

T: Now read the sentences together.

(Read as a whole class.)

T: Now read in groups.

(Read in four groups.)

SG1: Kangkang prefers reading.

SG2: Jane loves singing.

SG3: Maria enjoys listening to music.

SG4: Michael is interested in playing basketball.

T: Read the sentences again.

(Erase the sentences on the board, let students say them.)

(Invite 4 students to repeat the sentences of the 4 protagonists.)

T: Now I'll get four students to repeat the sentences.

S10: I am Kangkang. I prefer reading.

S11: I am Jane. I love singing.

S12: I am Maria. I enjoy listening to music.

S13: I am Michael. I am interested in playing basketball.

(Finally, guide students to summarize the following usages and write on the board.)

(1) be interested in + doing

(2) prefer + doing

(3) love + doing

(4) enjoy + doing

4. (Look at the pictures and listen to the tape, complete 2b.)

T: Look, listen and match the picture with the right expression.

(After listening to the tape, check answers.)

Answers: 1-e, 2-d, 3-b, 4-f, 5-c, 6-h, 7-a, 8-g

(Listen again, write down full sentences.)

T: Listen again and write down the sentences.

(Invite eight students to repeat the sentences, check answers.)

T: OK. I would like eight students to repeat the sentences, then check the answers.

(Write the eight sentences on the board.)

(1) Wang Junfeng likes climbing mountains.

(2) He loves watering flowers.

(3) The boy enjoys listening to music.

(4) Most students enjoy flying kites.

(5) I prefer collecting stamps.

(6) The boy prefers swimming.

(7) The woman is interested in walking a pet dog.

(8) He loves traveling.

Step 5 Project (Fifth Step: Comprehensive Inquiry Activity) (Time: 8 minutes)

1. [Conduct a survey, interview students in the class, find out their favorite activities, count the number of people with various favorites, (in advance) complete Section B 1b.]

T: Make a survey and find out how many students like... in your class. Finish 1b on page 55.

You can report it to your class like this:

10 students in our class like playing basketball.

2. (After completing the survey, report the results to the whole class.)

S1: 25 students in our class love singing.

S2: 15 students in our class enjoy listening to music.

S3: 8 students in our class are interested in skating.

S4: 10 students in our class prefer climbing mountains.

...

3. Homework:

Try to choose one of the things you like in 2b, then write a passage with 4～5 sentences.

(Find one activity among those listed in 2b that you like, and describe it or create a short dialogue using 4～5 sentences.)

For example:

(Picture a):

Look at the woman. She is walking a pet dog. She loves her dog very much. She usually has a walk with her dog after supper. Walking a pet dog is her hobby.

(Picture b):

T: What is he doing?

S5: He is listening to music.

T: What kind of music does he love?

S5: Look at the man. I think it must be the rock music.
