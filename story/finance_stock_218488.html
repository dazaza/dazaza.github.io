Chemical industry shows signs of stabilizing and rebounding ★★★★

Since last Monday, the long-dormant chemical stocks have shown overall strength. The chemical sector has become one of the most outstanding sectors in the past two days. Although there are factors such as the long-term underestimation of chemical stocks and their inherent need for catch-up growth, it is also closely related to the overall market demand recovery. Yesterday, reporters learned from several insiders that the chemical market has indeed shown signs of stabilization recently, and the high inventory accumulated since last year has also decreased. However, experts reminded at the same time that due to a large amount of delayed capacity which will still be put into operation in the future, the chemical market still faces uncertain factors.

Last Monday, the chemical sector stood out during trading hours, closing with the highest increase among all industry sectors. 18 chemical stocks including Hubei Yihua (000422), Chunhua Shares (000976), Huafeng Spandex (002064), Yuanxing Energy (000683), Yantai Wanhua (600309), and Lanxing New Materials (600299) hit the daily limit simultaneously. Yesterday, chemical stocks continued to soar, performing strongly.

"Although it cannot be called a real recovery, the entire industry has indeed shown signs of stabilizing and rebounding recently," Feng Shiliang, Deputy Secretary-General of the China Petroleum and Chemical Industry Association, told our reporter yesterday.

According to the introduction, stimulated and driven by national macro-control policies and a package of plans to deal with the financial crisis in the first half of this year, the whole industry united and cooperated, successfully passing through a sensitive period where various adverse factors alternated and policy effects overlapped. The economy of the entire industry has already shown signs of stabilizing and improving.

The next stage of market focus will be on exports and resources. ★★★★

In the next phase, the market will shift from being liquidity-driven to being jointly driven by liquidity and economic recovery. The rising expectations of economic recovery in the US, Japan, and Europe will improve China's export outlook. Meanwhile, due to the increased risk preference of global funds, global liquidity will continue to flow into emerging markets and commodity markets, gradually forming an emerging market bubble, making export-related and resource-related industries the next market focus; In addition, domestic economic recovery will still drive the midstream market trend.

From the perspective of global asset allocation, we predict that the dollar index will fall to around 75. The dollar continues to show a trend of falling, and last week the dollar index hit a new low for the year at 78.23. The sharp drop in the dollar index strongly indicates the rise in capital risk preference. Looking at the second half of the year, the bottoming out and recovery of the global economy will be the main trend, and the dollar index will still tend to fall. This is also our main observation indicator. We expect the dollar index to fall to around 75. We maintain our previous optimistic view on major resources and major finance.
