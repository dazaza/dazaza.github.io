"Quickly give your intestines a wash," this familiar advertising slogan, comes from Bishengyuan Company, the "herbal health tea therapy expert." In 2011, one of this company's main products, "Bishengyuan Changrun Tea," had already sold over 1.37 billion bags.

Unfortunately, this company, which was awarded the title of "China's Top Ten Most Trustworthy Health Product Brands," actually relies on brainwashing consumers to dominate the market and ensure its revenue grows rapidly.

Without witnessing how Bishengyuan grew from a small workshop into a listed company over more than a decade, it would be hard to believe that such a "knockoff company" could achieve such success.

How did Bishengyuan manage this? What problems exist with Bishengyuan's products?

The primary effective component in Bishengyuan tea products is simply a laxative.

According to Bishengyuan's prospectus when the company went public and data from the National Food and Drug Administration:

The main ingredients of Bishengyuan Changrun Tea are "green tea, Solomon's seal, ginseng, yam, senna leaf, and Cassia seed."

The main ingredients of Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea are "green tea, honeysuckle, Cassia seed, lotus leaf, Gynostemma pentaphyllum, hawthorn, senna leaf, and honey."

In the ingredient lists for both products, "senna leaf and Cassia seed (Cassia tora)" are both laxatives. The so-called advertised effects of "clearing old feces and expelling intestinal toxins" (essentially causing diarrhea) mainly rely on these laxatives.

Long-term use of these laxatives may lead to cancer.

Senna leaf and Cassia seed both contain anthraquinone components, which irritate the intestinal mucosa. Long-term use can cause pathological changes in the intestinal mucosa, known as colonic melanosis, which has largely been accepted in the medical community.

Colonic melanosis is often accompanied by colorectal polyps, considered a precancerous condition that can lead to colon cancer. According to domestic and international literature, 4.8% to 5.9% of large intestine cancer specimens are accompanied by colonic melanosis. Some domestic clinical observation data shows that most patients with colonic melanosis have a history of using laxatives.

Additionally, senna leaf can cause kidney damage, and clinically, it can also cause abdominal pain, vomiting, and nausea. Long-term users may also develop addiction.

There are doubts about whether Bishengyuan considers these health supplements effective.

In 2009, Bishengyuan commissioned Dongzhimen Hospital in Beijing to conduct clinical tests, where 120 people with bowel movement difficulties drank Changrun Tea for two weeks. The results showed that Bishengyuan Changrun Tea alleviated constipation symptoms without serious side effects.

In 2003, Bishengyuan had 32 mildly obese individuals drink four packets of Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea daily for 30 days, discovering that their weight decreased without any noticeable harm to their health. Bishengyuan believed that the clinical research results were sufficient and no further studies were necessary.

Based on Bishengyuan's official website promotion, they suggest drinking Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea for a cycle of 90 days and recommend following the cycle. For Changrun Tea, they suggest drinking it regularly every day and adjusting intake as needed. However, whether the testing duration for these two products is valid raises questions.

In reality, proving that a product is effective involves a process and characteristics like this: experience cannot determine efficacy—there must be clear pharmacology—it must go through test tube and animal experiments—it must undergo large-sample randomized controlled double-blind clinical trials—development takes time and money. Details can be found in "Why Yunnan Baiyao Is Not Considered Medicine in America." Moreover, in the U.S., health supplements are not allowed to claim any therapeutic effects.

Comparison Reference: A study by Sun Yat-sen University students indicates "Weight loss tea effects are not significant"

In 2007, several students from the Zhongshan School of Medicine at Sun Yat-sen University conducted an experiment titled "Research on the Mechanism and Side Effects of Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea." The experiment involved feeding distilled water and Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea to different mice over seven days and analyzing the final results.

Their experimental result was—compared to those fed distilled water, the weight loss effect of Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea was not significant. Consuming Bishengyuan Weight Loss Tea caused diarrhea, but there was no significant difference in the degree of diarrhea caused by high and low doses of the tea.

Like Bishengyuan's previous tests, this experiment by the Sun Yat-sen University students also had issues with insufficient testing duration, but the conclusions drawn were different from Bishengyuan's official test results. For example, the claim of "all experiencing weight loss" is questionable because the mice fed distilled water also "lost weight," so what's the difference between drinking distilled water and drinking weight loss tea?

Summary of Bishengyuan's Development Model—

1. No need to develop products; bought products can last for many years

In 2001, the Changrun Tea product was purchased for a one-time fee of 500,000 yuan from Huaiyin Huayi, and in 2004, the weight loss tea product was purchased for 10,000 yuan from Beijing Ruipule. Until mid-2011, these two products remained Bishengyuan's main sources of income, accounting for over 99% of total revenue.

Looking at these herbal teas with similar main ingredients makes it easy to understand why Bishengyuan could grow and develop without any R&D—between 2007 and 2010, Bishengyuan invested less than 0.5% of its revenue in R&D. In 2007, Bishengyuan's entire R&D cost for the year was "0."

Not until 2010, when they needed to go public, did Bishengyuan enhance their R&D capabilities by acquiring other companies and teams. To date, only one member of the senior management team is responsible for R&D, who was recruited in 2010.

2. Less than 5% raw material costs versus 30% advertising costs

In 2009, Bishengyuan's raw material costs accounted for 3.1% of revenue, in 2010 it was 3.4%, and in the first half of 2011, it was 1.8%.

In 2009, Bishengyuan's advertising costs accounted for 30.4% of revenue, in 2010 it was 28.6%, and in the first half of 2011, it was 32.3%.

It was revealed that after 2007, Bishengyuan's advertising expenses surged, coinciding with the start of their sales boom in 2007. In the previous year of 2006, Autralis Healthcare had just begun to end years of losses and gradually become profitable.

3. Workshop-style family business

Until 2007, Bishengyuan could independently complete the production process, previously outsourcing to third parties. One manufacturer's shareholder was a relative of Bishengyuan founder Zhao Yihong, while another manufacturer's legal representative was a director of Bishengyuan.

The "Bishengyuan" brand was initially registered by Beijing Ruilongxiang Company and then transferred to Autralis Healthcare. The Bishengyuan weight loss tea product was also purchased from Beijing Ruipule Company. Both Ruilongxiang and Ruipule are controlled by Bishengyuan founder Zhao Yihong.

Bishengyuan's chairman is Zhao Yihong, and the vice-chairman is his wife Gao Yan. Gao Yan's cousin Cui Shan participated multiple times in the restructuring of the Bishengyuan Group and entered the equity conversion role multiple times.

Moreover, in 2007, Bishengyuan's largest supplier was also a company controlled by Zhao Yihong, "Beijing Baicao Changqing Technology."

False advertising persists despite repeated prohibitions

In the prospectus before listing, Bishengyuan listed a risk factor analysis spanning 25 pages, focusing on numerous violations of advertising regulations and frequent consumer complaints about their products among many unfavorable factors.

Such as—"Marketing activities are the key to the success of our group’s products," "The success of the business depends significantly on our ability to maintain and enhance brand awareness."

Considering the high advertising costs mentioned earlier, it is not difficult to see the real secret to the company's success.

According to media reports, in the three years before going public, Bishengyuan violated advertising regulations 23 times. Between 2007 and 2010, Bishengyuan was warned, suspended from selling, or had their approvals revoked in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangdong, Hunan, Liaoning, Jiangsu, Inner Mongolia, and other places due to reasons such as infringing on consumer portrait rights, exaggerating efficacy, and not obtaining approval documents. There were also multiple violations by distributors.

Even now, such advertisements are still ubiquitous—"Bishengyuan: Get rid of 'spring fatigue'" "Moisturize and repair damaged intestines" "Bishengyuan Changrun Tea removes internal waste, cuts off the source of toxic gases, and eliminates pimples and acne."

Don't let Bishengyuan brainwash you anymore

Medicine does not recognize the concept of "stagnant feces," and "detoxification" has no basis.

"Stagnant feces" is not a medical term; in medicine, there is only "constipation."

However, in the "Gastrointestinal Knowledge" section of Bishengyuan's official website, it states—"(Stagnant feces) can accumulate up to about 6.5 kilograms."

If 6 kilograms of feces were in the intestines, it would cause the intestinal wall to signal the brain that defecation is needed, unless intentionally holding it back, it would have been excreted long ago. Even for constipation patients, it is merely difficult to defecate, and it is impossible to accumulate a large amount of feces in the body.

As long as the stool shape is normal (formed stools with moderate moisture), defecation frequency is regular, and there are no uncomfortable symptoms, defecating once every 2 to 3 days to twice a day is considered normal, neither diarrhea nor constipation, and no artificial intervention is needed. Misusing "detoxification" products will not only fail to detoxify but may also harm health.

Drinking too much Changrun Tea can lead to more severe constipation

Rhubarb, senna leaf, Cassia seed, and aloe all belong to the class of anthraquinone drugs, which soften stools and stimulate the large intestine to increase peristalsis and defecation, taking effect quickly, especially suitable for short-term treatment of constipation. However, if used long-term, it reduces the responsiveness of enteric nerve sensory cells, causes degenerative changes in enteric nerve cells, and even turns the intestines black. In such cases, even if there is enough stool in the intestines, normal peristalsis and defecation reflexes cannot occur, possibly leading to stubborn constipation, making treatment more difficult. Therefore, in clinical practice, the use of such drugs generally does not exceed two weeks, and dosage is strictly controlled.

Weight loss should not depend on diarrhea; haste makes waste

After long-term use of laxatives, intestinal peristalsis accelerates, speeding up the expulsion of food from the body, preventing the intestines from absorbing nutrients and water. Therefore, body weight will definitely decrease, but this kind of weight loss leads to "pale and emaciated," completely unrelated to the original goal of healthy and beautiful weight loss.

In fact, a person's weight is mainly determined by three factors: genetics, diet, and exercise. Additionally, emotions, environment, and sleep play roles, but none are as important as the first three, especially genetic factors. To date, "eating less and exercising more" remains the best way to lose weight.

Spending a lot of money to continuously drink a very low-cost laxative for 90 days is not a proper way to lose weight.

In recent years, reports of adverse reactions to senna leaf have repeatedly appeared, and the consensus in the medical community is that long-term abuse of such drugs will lead to serious consequences. Yet, Bishengyuan repeatedly claims that "men, women, young, and old can all drink it, with no toxic side effects, and can be consumed long-term." Furthermore, they plan to enter the over-the-counter drug market, which is truly astonishing.
