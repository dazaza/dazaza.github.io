Five secrets can open a man's heart.

by hysock on 2008-04-27 16:45:07

They all say, "A woman's heart is like a needle at the bottom of the sea," but what about a man's heart? Is it simply impossible to open? Maybe you're just lacking in the magic spell from Alibaba. Here are five secrets...

Secret One: Real men fear rejection

The hope for affirmation and the fear of rejection, especially rejection by the closest woman, is a common ailment among men.

Surveys show that most men feel that women are always nitpicking at them, so they worry that when they open up about their feelings, they will be met with ridicule, which would make them feel extremely humiliated. A man's self-esteem is as fragile as an eggshell. The more recognition you give him, the easier it will be for him to open up about his feelings.

This does not mean that you don't have any opinions or views on the matter. Loving a man means accepting him, not trying to mold him. If he opens up and you immediately respond with "That's wrong, I disagree" or "Where did you get such a crazy idea," he will immediately close up and stop talking.

Before a hen lays eggs, it must ensure that its surroundings are safe because the safety of those eggs after they are laid is crucial to it. Similarly, when people reveal their deepest secrets, they need to ensure that the other party can accept it, because this secret is a treasure in their hearts. Don't assume that men are carefree about everything; before they open up, they are always carefully observing you.

Secret Two: Make your stance clear

Some women, while listening to a man share his inner secrets, will only nod along like woodpeckers. Wrong! What a man needs at this moment is the joy of sharing. He is accompanied by various worries and anxieties while speaking his mind. For example, many men continuously consider questions like "If I tell her, will she leave me?" At this time, you need to make your stance clear, you need to speak up. When he tells you something very personal, you should express something like - "That's not too bad, I've done worse." Or "I really appreciate everything that happened." Let him know that you are just like him, that his experiences are not unique.

Remember to definitely show that you are on his side. Many women, after hearing a man's story, only indicate that his view on the matter is wrong. In doing so, you unconsciously take a stand against him. For example, if he tells you he doesn't get along well with his colleagues, you should look at the matter from his perspective, and not act like you know everything and make analyses, saying things like "Actually, others might not mean it that way" or similar foolish remarks. Create harmony, let him feel that both of you occupy the same planet and live in a similar world. Your honesty and positive feedback are crucial. If you do this, he will become excited due to the resonance and talk nonstop.

Secret Three: Let go when you should

Have you ever had this experience: your conversation with your husband or boyfriend eventually turns into reminiscing about past grievances, including things he has done wrong, ways he has hurt you, and what he owes you now. This happens in every couple or relationship, psychological experts analyze that this feeling makes men shrink back. Actually, there's a phrase you might not want to hear — all relationships are like dancing, while you share the right answers, you also share the mistakes. Do you know what it means when one hand claps and no sound is made? Even if the mistake was his, you cannot say that you have no responsibility for it.

Men fear most when women don't let go of past matters, so they rarely bring up old issues.

Their concern is not without reason. Some women like to play the role of victims and "martyrs," using their grievances about love to feel good about themselves. Perhaps, this method can temporarily tie down some men, but remember, if you pretend to be a resentful woman relying on past sacrifices to hold onto a man, it means your relationship is already in danger.

Secret Four: Build the courage to hear the truth

You need to have the courage to hear the truth.

Many men feel that women want or need beautiful lies. Some of my clients have said that they are afraid to tell their partners what is happening in their lives or their true feelings because it would disappoint the other person. Do you know why you leave this impression on men? Because actually, many women like to use emotions to control men — thereby controlling their relationship. Women often demand specific reactions from men, for example, when she sheds tears, the man should immediately offer his shoulder, accompanied by incredibly gentle whispers. When they don't get it, women become disheartened. In fact, this implies that men shouldn't tell the truth.

We all make this mistake from time to time, but being willing to hear the truth is the beginning of a truly mature relationship. This allows men to feel that they have a reliable partner who can share hardships with them. Don't deceive yourself, and don't lose heart. Maybe you can't accept all the facts right now, but as long as you are willing, you can certainly train your tolerance. Fantasies may make women feel good, but the more facts we accept, the stronger we become. True security does not come from others' approval but from being honest with oneself. Only then can we hear the truth.

Secret Five: Be true to yourself

The best way to help a man open up is to be honest, natural, and real with yourself, and to radiate warmth and acceptance. The various people we encounter in life are like mirrors reflecting different aspects of ourselves. We attract others, and these people make us fall in love with our own strengths. This is why the five topics I raise here are not only aimed at men but also at us women. For example, can you let go of self-evaluation? Can you stop dwelling on past mistakes? Or are you always overshadowed by the shadows of your errors and failures? How can someone who can't forgive themselves forgive others?

When a person fully accepts themselves, they can create a completely relaxed state. Haven't you noticed that some people have a charm where even if we don't know them for long, we are still willing to trust them and confide in them. If you can have a clear understanding of yourself, you will basically free yourself from the prevalent restlessness, changeability, pretense, arrogance, and inferiority complex of this era. People like this naturally exude a sense of familiarity.
