"The Prequel of the Secretary of the Mayor (Part II)" - A New Work of Reflection on Officialdom by Wang Xiaofang

by jqrxass on 2009-08-31 12:52:31

The novel reveals in shocking detail how the protagonist Lei Mo spares no effort to become the mayor's secretary in order to realize his political ambitions. However, the intricate machinations of officialdom and the breathtaking power struggles subject Lei Mo to constant torment of his personality and questioning of his soul. After undergoing a baptism akin to purgatory, Lei Mo reflects on life and the process of existence, gaining a more profound understanding of the meaning of life. Through the soulful competition between Deputy Secretary of the Municipal Committee Li Shaoguang and Executive Vice Mayor Zhang Guochang, as well as the power struggle between Zhang Guochang and Mayor Li Guofan, the novel uncompromisingly criticizes how the worship of power alienates the national spirit. In this work of realism, Wang Xiaofang attempts through self-examination, introspection, and "sacrificing oneself for truth," not only to confront reality but also to confront the soul, linking the two through thought. The novel delves into the depths of the soul through philosophical dissection. Wang Xiaofang tries through this dissection to inquire: who are the creators of corruption? Who are the victims of corruption? What is the root of power worship? Why has power worship become the indigenous religion of the Chinese people? Undoubtedly, while the flesh and blood of this novel are literary, its soul is philosophical, belonging not only to the soul but also to the history of the soul.

Chapter Six: Front Stage and Backstage

After the groundbreaking ceremony, Vice Mayor Zhang did not get into his own car but instead proactively entered Mayor Li Guofan's car. Regarding the issue of changing the main gate of the municipal government and adding floors to the office building, Li Guofan had given Zhang Guochang a small warning. Zhang Guochang felt uncomfortable about it, but since he had just become the executive vice mayor, it was not yet time to challenge Li Guofan directly. He must maintain a good relationship with Li Guofan for now. This incident regarding the gate change ended in a draw. Originally, Li Guofan intended to seal off the old gate, but Zhang Guochang instructed Liu Benshan not to do so, as after the future renovation of the municipal government square, it could serve as a passage for the national flag team. After Liu Benshan reported this, Li Guofan found it reasonable and agreed not to seal the original main gate. In fact, Li Guofan was merely giving Zhang Guochang a small warning regarding the division of responsibilities among mayors, stopping at that point without wanting to escalate the conflict, since he still relied on Zhang Guochang to get things done. Therefore, after Zhang Guochang got into the car, Li Guofan’s attitude was very calm, as if nothing had happened.

Chapter Seven: Accumulated Emotions

Due to its preservation, the original main gate of the municipal government where Zhang Guochang and Li Guofan were secretly competing finally came into play during the completion ceremony, serving as the passage for the armed police national flag team to march out from the municipal government and raise the national flag. The grand ceremony featured colorful balloons floating above the municipal government square and colorful flags fluttering around the square. The podium was set up in front of the Phoenix Wing. With his back to the golden and shiny Phoenix Wing, Li Guofan appeared as if leaning against a huge scepter, looking majestic and speaking passionately.
