The decoration of the bridal home has always been very particular about Feng Shui. As an especially important part of the house, the kitchen is also the part that requires the most attention. For couples who are busy with the decoration of their bridal suite, it's very necessary to understand the Feng Shui rules regarding the kitchen. Today, let's take a look at the taboos of kitchen Feng Shui together.

The kitchen should not face south because the south belongs to fire and so does the kitchen. Fire upon fire is inauspicious for the household and food will easily spoil when facing south. Therefore, it's best not to set up the kitchen in the south. The kitchen should also not be in the center of the house because the center of the residence should not be contaminated. The most suitable positions are the east and southeast of the residence. Both the east and southeast belong to wood in the five elements, which can coexist peacefully with water and fire, making them particularly suitable as good places for the kitchen and breakfast.

The kitchen door should not directly face the main door. As the saying goes, "Seeing the stove when opening the door leads to wasting money." This layout will damage the health of women and make it hard for the family to accumulate wealth, leading to fluctuating luck. If you encounter such a layout, the only way to avoid it is to change the door.

The kitchen and toilet should not share the same door: Some families save space by having the kitchen and toilet share the same entrance and exit, causing the water and fire within the home to be imbalanced, which is very unlucky; worse still, if one must pass through the toilet to reach the kitchen, any appetite will surely be lost!

The floor of the kitchen should not be higher than the living room or bedroom floors. On one hand, this prevents backflow of sewage; on the other hand, due to the distinction between primary and secondary spaces, the kitchen should not overshadow the living room or bedrooms; finally, moving from the kitchen into the dining area to serve food should be progressively higher, otherwise there may be concerns about losing wealth.

The cooking stove should not be placed without support behind it. The stove represents the health, marriage, and fame of the family and should have some form of support behind it. There should not be emptiness behind it, and it must be backed by a solid wall; even a glass wall would be inappropriate.
