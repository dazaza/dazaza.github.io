Spring is still shallow, anxiously awaiting the blossoms.

by lucky6105 on 2012-02-26 00:38:03

The dampness seeps out from the cracks in the earth, and the rain is almost here. It's like a love that yearns to be expressed but remains unspoken, a surge of emotion filling one's chest, words on the tip of the tongue only to be swallowed back down.

"Spring wind dares to comb the willows, night rain secretly nourishes the flowers." This was a couplet I saw at a classmate’s home during a gathering some days ago. Back then, the scroll, written in running script, rich with ink, every stroke bold and unrestrained, full of flavor.

The spring wind, emboldened, runs across plains, shakes through mountains, rolls over rivers. The heartbeat becomes uneven, breaths quicken, countless hands emerge to caress, blowing and teasing, enticing. How could life, pent up all winter, resist such provocation? Hearts warm instantly. Let it sprout, let it bloom! Hot blood boils, and suddenly "peach blossoms are bright, plum blossoms are thick, apricot blossoms flourish, poplar catkins fly across the city."

"Silent drizzle infiltrates the night, nurturing all things without a sound," the spring rain comes swiftly. "Night rain secretly nourishes the flowers," blushing shyly, yet the blood pulses eagerly. Just as the curtain of night opens, it drifts and sprinkles, beautifying the blooms, moistening their stamens. A tender affection, an intimate fusion, deeply longing, fully devoted, they stay together well. Spring arrives this way, seeds and roots can no longer contain their passion, swelling and bursting into countless buds emerging from the cracks in the earth, drilling through tree trunks, the world covered in fresh green, spreading vigorously.

"Peach blossoms, that radiant red of a tree, seem like a sentence spoken by spring: each dewy petal a delicate word, each petal a soft, even breath; smiling, glancing coquettishly, intentionally or unintentionally. Look, she trembles in the breeze, leaving behind a faint trace on the lips of March, a glance, a glance filled with emotion!" Peach blossoms bloom, pink petals and yellow stamens, thin yet pure, nurtured by eighteen years of meticulous care, standing tall like a refined young lady, bashful yet smiling, her lips unopened yet already intoxicating. "A Peach Blossom Poem" is a poem written by Lin Huiyin, love growing from the hearts of talented scholars and beautiful maidens, eyebrows slightly raised, eyes conveying feelings, the world becoming a melody, a painting, how wonderful, how good!

Listening to "Spring Traces". The piano base smooth as silk, like gilded river water splashing eastward. Wild ducks paddle in shallow waters, sometimes in perfect formation, sometimes disarrayed, quacking about.

Listening to "Longing for Each Other". A bent bow, taut strings, walking inch by inch on steel wires. Strings meet, intertwine, merge, separate. Night on the strings, moonlight on the strings, sentiment on the strings. Pulling and pulling, strings filled with emotion, hearts rising and falling with the wrist, moving gracefully, elegant and affectionate.

Listening to Dao Lang's "Love is You and Me". "Love is you and me, a life interwoven with heart; love is you and me, an unchanging promise in adversity; love is your hand, soothing my wounds; love is my heart, listening to your joys and sorrows," sung in duet, simple, deep, meaningful. The melody of "Loving Only You" runs throughout the TV series, Shengli and Xiaoqing, a pair of jade-like lovers, souls appreciating each other, entwined, supporting and accompanying each other, yet life plays tricks, always missing each other despite being so close, for many years... The deepest love should be the admiration, the obsession, the support and companionship of soul to soul.

Accompanied by a cup of clear tea, reading tranquil words, listening to music that resonates with the heart, days pass quietly, a bit lonely but not desolate. Age brings understanding, stepping back opens up new horizons, retreating completes others and frees oneself, truly virtuous.

Out of nowhere, a tumor grows. That wicked thing, lurking in my body, growing day by day, unbeknownst to me. The doctor says it must be cut out (the doctor said it elegantly, but this is my interpretation). The word "cut" scares me, the sharp blade piercing skin, cutting through muscle, removing flesh, it must hurt terribly. As a child, I always fell while running, scraping my knees bloody, crying endlessly, mother would worry, the more she worried, the louder I cried. My son and husband both say, "I'm here, don't be afraid!" Though prepared mentally for a long time, still, as I step onto the operating table, fear lingers. Like a little girl, I cry secretly, tears streaming down my face.

Difficult as it may be, it will eventually pass. Once passed, flowers will bloom beautifully, moons will be full. Beautiful flowers and full moons make good days, stability and joy hidden in every meal, every piece of clothing, warmth found in the烟火气 of daily life.

A few days ago, I visited my friend Ping's new home. She decorated her daughter's room with pink floral wallpaper and curtains, pearl curtains hanging in the living room glass cabinet, very elegant and beautiful. I teased her about her "pink memories" and "a dream behind the curtain," what springtime desires were stirring within her? She laughed until her eyes and eyebrows crinkled together. We talked about many old stories, recalling our youthful exuberance, lamenting how fast time flies, vowing to continue our journey together, heartfelt words exchanged. Sufficiently sentimental, warming the heart yet tingling with nostalgia. Since the age of 16, Ping and I have gone to school together, worked in the same unit, separated and reunited. For someone to accompany another through most of life and continue walking together, it is truly rare.

The wind is gentle, the rain fine, hinting at the blooming of spring flowers. Ha ha, though we are older now, romance still lurks deep within our hearts.

The forsythia hasn't bloomed yet, spring is still shallow. There's no rush. Wait, wait!
