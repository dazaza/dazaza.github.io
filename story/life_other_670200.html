Don't always think you're so smart.

He had a secret crush on her. It was that summer, during their brief encounter after meeting, he hadn’t even clearly seen her face when inexplicably, he fell for her. Drawn by her beautiful hair and captivated by her manner of speech and behavior, he was electrified. He developed a secret infatuation for her.

He always thought she was good to him, that she liked him too. His heart became increasingly occupied by her, yet he didn’t dare to confess his feelings. Quietly, he cared for her, maintaining this secret affection for years. He believed she could sense his love, making confession unnecessary.

After one drunken night, the two had an opportunity to be alone together. Taking advantage of his inebriation, he smiled and said to her: "In front of you, I always feel like a child who has done something wrong. You are always like a mentor. That's not fair."

"Is that so? I haven't noticed," her calmness left him somewhat disheartened.

Gradually, he realized that she was equally kind to everyone. In fact, he discovered she cared more about others than she did about him. In his pain, he felt a childish sense of injustice.

Yet, occasionally, a little bit of care from her would make him feel sweet for many days. Torn between sweetness and pain, he became quiet and withdrawn. He realized that she didn't actually love him; she only regarded him as an ordinary friend, or perhaps not even that—just someone she knew.

He vowed: "I will leave this city." Of course, he still cared for her secretly, protecting her, worrying about her small matters. Sometimes, he even thought he sounded overly fussy.

He secured a position in a border area. During the farewell banquet hosted by his friends, filled with scolding and regret, he got drunk. Waking up alone in the middle of the night, he finally cried out loud while leaning on his packed luggage.

The next morning, he called her to meet. He had never been so relaxed when inviting her before. Previously, he always feared rejection.

For the first time, he wasn't acting like a child in front of her. Overnight, he became a gentleman, speaking without restraint. However, her eyes were brimming with tears. She knew he was about to go far away.

He told her: "Loving someone is painful, especially when you love someone who doesn't love you back; being loved can also be painful, especially when someone loves you but you don't love them back.

To avoid causing you pain, and for my own sake, I'm leaving. Take care of yourself." He pretended to be at ease, but deep down, he hoped she would try to stop him, even if it meant losing his job. He was willing to sacrifice everything.

She softly said: "Go then, there will be better girls for you to choose from there. I wish you all the best." She remained calm, except a few tears betrayed her emotion.

Actually, she wanted to berate him: "Coward! You always think you're so smart. When I didn't have feelings for you, you assumed I did. Now that I'm starting to have feelings, you don't notice and are ready to run away." But he never seriously looked at her face, his gaze always drifting towards the distance.

He left, carrying his simple baggage. He told others he would leave the next day, no one knew he had departed early. He just wanted to feel her care one last time, to let her see him off. She stared straight at his retreating figure, her tears blurring her vision.

Suddenly, he turned around, and she felt excited. But he merely smiled at her and waved goodbye. She stomped her foot and shouted anxiously: "Coward! You always think you're so smart!" But he didn't hear clearly what she said, mistaking it for some blessing. He acted nonchalantly, made a funny face, and turned around, disappearing into the crowd without looking back.
