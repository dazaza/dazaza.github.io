When the cold wave comes, we need to pay attention to exercising more, wearing more clothes to keep warm. In addition, we can also eat more food that helps resist the cold to enhance our body's cold resistance. An important point of winter health preservation is "nourishing the kidneys and preventing the cold." Below are 10 small tips for winter health preservation, allowing you to improve your resistance in the severe winter.

1. Drink More Water

Often drink plain boiled water

Reason: Winter climate is dry, and the human body is extremely prone to dehydration. Drinking plain boiled water not only ensures the needs of the body but also has the effect of promoting diuresis and detoxification, eliminating waste. Even though sweating and urination decrease in winter, maintaining the normal operation of brain and organ cells still requires water nourishment.

Method: The daily water intake in winter should be no less than 2000-3000 milliliters.

2. Sweat a Little

Outdoor activities can promote heat production

Reason: Outdoor activities can promote heat production, regulate metabolic function, and enhance the excitation and temperature regulation functions of the cerebral cortex. Winter health preservation requires appropriate exercise, sweating a little, which strengthens the body. Physical exercise should combine movement with stillness; running and doing exercises should only make one sweat slightly. Excessive sweating leads to loss of qi, which contradicts the principle of storing yang energy in winter.

Method: Exercise should vary according to individual physical condition and age. For example, people with cardiovascular diseases should avoid intense activities like playing ball or climbing mountains; people with respiratory system diseases, such as chronic bronchitis, emphysema, and pulmonary heart disease, should choose times with ample sunlight for exercise to avoid being chilled and prevent triggering acute diseases. Elderly people should pay special attention to keeping warm and avoid getting up too early for exercise to prevent insufficient blood supply to the brain and induce strokes.

3. Prevent Some Diseases

Enhance resistance to prevent diseases

Reason: The cold winter climate can easily cause chronic diseases to recur or worsen, so it's important to stay warm. To keep warm, windows are opened less frequently for ventilation, leading to insufficient air circulation, which creates conditions for bacterial proliferation. Furthermore, whether indoors or outdoors, winter air is not sufficiently humid, and nasal dryness gives bacteria more opportunities. Additionally, the large temperature difference between indoor and outdoor environments in winter requires more energy from the body to adapt to temperature changes, making it easier for bacteria to invade.

Method: Especially, precautions should be taken against the adverse effects of strong winds and sudden drops in temperature on the body, and emergency medicines should be prepared. At the same time, cold endurance exercises should be emphasized to enhance cold resistance and disease prevention, preventing respiratory diseases from occurring.

4. Adjust Emotions

Winter psychological flu

Reason: Winter is a season without vitality, and people tend to be very lazy. Everything lacks vitality, and winter makes it easier to fall into depression. Why do we often feel listless, irritable, and stressed after entering winter? These are symptoms of winter depression, commonly known as "winter psychological flu," a common psychological disorder. Everyone may experience this psychological state at some point in life, and winter makes it easier to appear.

Method: The best method is to choose sports activities suitable for one's health status, such as jogging, skating, dancing, and playing ball, all of which have different intensities. These are excellent remedies for eliminating boredom and nurturing the spirit.

5. Sleep Earlier

Ensure adequate sleep

Reason: The famous Tang Dynasty medical expert Sun Simiao said, "It's not advisable to go out early or return late at night during winter, exposing oneself to the cold." Going to bed early nurtures yang energy, while waking up later solidifies yin essence. There is a saying: Spring drowsiness, autumn fatigue, summer dozing, and three months of sleepiness in winter. Traditional Chinese medicine emphasizes spring growth, summer flourishing, autumn harvest, and winter storage; winter should be a season for conserving energy.

Method: Winter health preservation requires ensuring adequate sleep, which benefits the concealment of yang energy and the accumulation of yin essence. After the beginning of winter, lifestyle adjustments must remember "storage."

6. Supplement Appropriately

Winter is the season to nourish the kidneys

Reason: Winter belongs to the "closing and storing" season. According to traditional Chinese medicine, the kidneys govern sealing and storing, meaning winter is the season to nourish the kidneys. Through winter supplementation, nourishing the kidneys can make kidney "essence" more abundant, improving next year's health and reducing illness, embodying the idea of treating before the disease occurs in traditional Chinese medicine.

Method: Scientific supplementation is essential for winter health preservation. People with yang deficiency can choose mutton, dog meat, chicken, etc. Those with both qi and blood deficiency can use goose meat, duck meat, black chicken, etc. Those who should avoid cold and dry foods can select wolfberry, red dates, black fungus, black sesame seeds, walnut kernels, etc. Herbal supplements must follow medical advice, tailored individually. When starting winter supplementation, allow the gastrointestinal tract to adapt by first choosing dishes like red date stewed beef or peanuts with brown sugar, or cooking ginger red date beef soup to adjust stomach and spleen functions.

7. Protect Feet

Cold starts from the feet

Reason: There is a folk saying, "Cold starts from the feet." Because the feet are the farthest part of the body, they have thin fat layers and poor warmth retention. Traditional Chinese medicine believes that foot acupoints are closely related to internal organs. If the feet become cold, it can lead to colds, abdominal pain, leg pain, dysmenorrhea, and other diseases, so it's crucial to keep the feet warm in winter. Keeping the feet healthy in winter means keeping the whole body fit.

Method: Persistently wash feet with warm hot water every day, preferably while massaging and stimulating foot acupoints. Walk for at least half an hour daily to exercise the feet. Rub the soles of the feet morning and evening to promote blood circulation.

8. Drink Tea

Drinking tea refreshes the mind

Reason: Drinking tea in winter can refresh the mind, invigorate the spirit, enhance memory, stimulate the central nervous system, increase athletic ability, stimulate gastric juice secretion, aid digestion, improve appetite, eliminate bad breath, alleviate fatigue, and promote metabolism. Tea contains various vitamins and trace elements needed by the body. Green tea, in particular, has cancer-preventing properties and provides good health care. Long-term computer users should drink tea regularly.

Method: In winter, drinking black tea is preferred. Black tea is sweet and warm, which can nurture the body's yang energy. It contains abundant proteins and sugars, generating heat and warming the abdomen, enhancing the body's cold resistance, aiding digestion, and removing greasiness.

9. Drink Porridge

Morning porridge for winter health preservation

Reason: Morning porridge in winter helps preserve health. Dinner should be eaten in moderation to nourish the stomach's energy. Drinking a bowl of hot porridge in the cold morning helps keep warm and enhances the body's cold resistance, preventing catching a cold. For those with throat discomfort, inflammation, or pain, warm porridge can soothe the throat and effectively relieve discomfort.

Method: Glutinous rice red date porridge, eight-treasure porridge, and millet porridge are most suitable. Other beneficial porridges include longan porridge for calming the mind, chrysanthemum porridge for clearing fire and brightening eyes, carp porridge for regulating the intestines, poria porridge for strengthening the spleen and nourishing the stomach, sesame porridge for benefiting essence and nourishing yin, walnut porridge for nourishing yin and consolidating essence, jujube porridge for boosting qi and nourishing yin, silver ear porridge for moistening the lungs and generating fluids, and radish porridge for digesting food and resolving phlegm.

10. Ventilate

Open windows for ventilation

Reason: In winter, houses generally have tightly closed doors and windows, leading to poor indoor air circulation. Breathing continuously in this space results in a significant amount of carbon dioxide, lack of oxygen, and problems such as dizziness and chest tightness. Therefore, ventilation is necessary.

Method: Windows should be opened for ventilation for 20 minutes each morning, noon, and evening to keep the indoor air fresh. Experiments show that each change of air removes 60% of harmful gases indoors. Moreover, it's essential to maintain an appropriate indoor temperature, avoiding excessive differences between indoor and outdoor temperatures. Generally, indoor temperatures should be controlled between 16-22°C.
