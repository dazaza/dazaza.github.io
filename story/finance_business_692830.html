10 Fashionable Clothing Combinations for Stylish Post-80s Ladies

by wuren458w on 2012-03-06 11:51:03

Yi Yuan website recommends 10 fashionable clothing combinations for trendy post-80s young ladies, absolutely practical. As the autumn breeze starts to blow, if you no longer want to wear shorts, then seven-eighths pants will be a good choice for you, as your calves can still display sexiness. A pure white suit is very versatile, whether paired with pants or skirts, it gives off a clean and crisp vibe. Wearing mini-skirts in the slightly cool autumn may not be quite appropriate anymore. However, pairing it with a long-sleeved plaid shirt on top will prevent you from being too exposed while giving off a street-chic style. Summer knitted sweaters, when paired with a shirt underneath, can fend off the slight chill, and when paired with a black short skirt, exudes a ladylike charm. The red and black striped long shirt is full of dynamic vitality. When paired with a black pleated skirt, revealing the sweet hemline, it looks very cute. If you want to be a stylish person with an edgy feel, then you might as well choose a small suit for clothing coordination. Hoodies in a sporty style can also come in handy in the fall. European and American style sporty hoodies paired with cuffed denim shorts create a street fashion sense, full of vitality. The summer combo of white T-shirts with flared hotpants may have become too revealing. Pairing leggings with them gives off a strong street style vibe while also providing warmth. Plaid shirts paired with umbrella-shaped shorts have a strong literary flair. Pairing them with a pair of black thigh-high socks creates a slimmer look than in summer. Outside of summer tank tops, adding a red-edged striped cardigan is full of intense schoolgirl charm. The key to mixing this style is choosing a pair of black-rimmed glasses, which makes it more playful compared to the proper British schoolgirl style. Short socks paired with fish-mouth sandals are a trend this season and can also be referenced. This article comes from Fashion Coordination - Yi Yuan website (http://www.daban.org.cn).
