Composite leather seats masquerading as genuine leather, sun films being improperly applied, floor mats with a pungent smell... After buying a new car, many owners feel the urge to add some "extras" to their vehicles. Applying sun film, laying down floor mats, and replacing fabric seats with leather ones are among the most common upgrades. Both 4S dealerships and automotive beauty supply stores offer these services. However, journalists have discovered that some merchants, in pursuit of profit, substitute inferior products for better ones. Therefore, experts advise car owners to carefully differentiate between quality options and not to opt for cheaper alternatives that may cause unnecessary trouble.

**Car Upgrades: Sun Film, Leather Seats, and Floor Mats**

**Be Wary of Composite Leather Passing as Genuine**

"A 1.6L automatic model equipped with leather seats costs about 10,000 yuan more, so I decided to upgrade my own car at a decoration shop," said Mr. Sun, who recently purchased a new Jetta. He believes that upgrading to leather seats is one of the simplest and most cost-effective ways to enhance a vehicle. Journalists learned that many car owners choose to replace their fabric seats with leather ones. However, some owners, lured by lower prices, end up purchasing composite leather seats, which are neither as durable nor as breathable as genuine leather.

An expert from the automotive beauty industry explained that the price differences for leather seat upgrades can vary significantly. Typically, in an automotive beauty shop, leather seats cost between 1,000 to 3,000 yuan, while upgrading to original factory leather seats at a 4S dealership can range from 3,000 to 8,000 yuan or even higher. Currently, there are three types of leather seats available: water buffalo leather, yellow cowhide, and composite leather. Yellow cowhide is the most expensive, offering the best texture and breathability. Composite leather, on the other hand, is the cheapest and often involves processed materials that aren't genuine leather. It also tends to have a strong, unpleasant odor, so consumers need to be vigilant when making their choice.

Therefore, given the relatively high value of a car, it's important not to simply go for the cheapest option. Even if you don’t choose the best, you should at least select something suitable.
