Meitu Mobile: On the Way to Becoming a "Selfie Expert"

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 12:38:23

After launching Meitu Xiuxiu, a popular photo editing app among women and students, Meitu.com introduced its first hardware product this year: the Meitu Smartphone, specifically designed for female selfies. Its hardware feature lies in the upgrade of the front-facing camera from the usual 1.2 megapixels to 8 megapixels. Additionally, to ensure speed and quality in photography, it incorporates Fujitsu's ISP (Image Signal Processor).

Meitu.com's positioning of the Meitu Smartphone is quite interesting. It’s not a "high-end" product competing with Xiaomi, Meizu, or other major brands; instead, it directly targets Casio's latest "selfie wonder," the EX-TR350, aiming to replace it as the new "selfie wonder" that leads the trend among women.

Now I have both a TR350 and a Meitu Smartphone in hand, wanting to know which one—whether it's the "selfie camera" among cameras or the "selfie camera" among smartphones—is more suitable for women. For this, I sought Shi Chun's help, both as a model and to assist me in understanding female users' feelings towards these two products.

We first tested the differences between the Meitu Smartphone and the TR350 under several general scenarios in self-portrait photography, including indoor and outdoor, strong light, weak light, and backlighting. We used default settings and produced images directly. The following images can be clicked to enlarge.

The left image was taken by the Meitu Smartphone, the right by the TR350; the TR350 has higher sharpness.

The left image was taken by the Meitu Smartphone, the right by the TR350; the TR350 tends to have a reddish hue.

In comparison, the photos taken by the TR350 contain richer light and shadow details and are less likely to appear overly white. However, it's easy to see that the Meitu Smartphone and the TR350 have different understandings of "female beauty"—the TR350 believes rosy cheeks make women more beautiful, while the Meitu Smartphone sees natural skin tone as inherently beautiful without excessive embellishment. Shi Chun agrees more with the aesthetic presented by the Meitu Smartphone, finding the TR350's processing effect "too pink and somewhat fake."

The TR350's camera resolution is 12.1 million pixels. If viewed merely for "preview," its photos seem clearer. However, if the two images are enlarged to the same size, the pixel difference isn't that noticeable. At this point, it also becomes apparent that the photos taken by the Meitu Smartphone tend to have noise in the "shadow" areas.

In terms of user experience, the Meitu Smartphone, being a smartphone, is very simple to operate and ready to use right out of the box. In contrast, although the TR350 has a reputation, due to its high price, not all female users own one. Thus, even Shi Chun only saw her friends play with it, making her unfamiliar with its usage—since the TR350 is specially designed for selfies, differing significantly from regular cameras.

Interestingly, the device screen's performance affects users' evaluation of photo quality. After taking photos, when I simultaneously asked her how she evaluated the photos taken by different machines, she thought the Meitu Smartphone had better photo quality because of its high-resolution screen, making the images appear finer. Meanwhile, the TR350 gave a rougher impression. Moreover, despite adopting the same touch screen design, the TR350's screen felt sluggish in response.

From the perspective of post-processing and photo editing functions, the Meitu Smartphone undoubtedly offers many more options. Since it uses the Android operating system, users can choose various apps besides Meitu's own products for photography or photo processing, such as Instagram, Cymera, Pixlr Express, SnapSpeed, Pixlr-o-matic, EyeEm, etc.

To cater to the prevalence of smartphones, the TR350 added a "send to phone" function. Like other camera manufacturers, it transfers images via built-in Wi-Fi, requiring users to download a separate application. When using it, the phone must connect to the camera's Wi-Fi. Therefore, the TR350 can transmit pictures to the phone for processing, but the process is relatively more cumbersome.

From a market pricing perspective, the TR350 undoubtedly belongs to the "rich and beautiful" class, with Taobao prices starting at over 6000 RMB, and if it's the pink or white version, the price can approach nearly 7000 RMB. In contrast, the Meitu Smartphone is priced at just 2199 RMB, only one-third the price of the TR350. However, in terms of imaging quality, there isn't much difference between the two. And considering the angle of "female beauty," the Meitu Smartphone's processing method may align more closely with women's aesthetic views.

At least, for ordinary female consumers who enjoy occasional selfies and recording their lives, the Meitu Smartphone is an excellent "selfie camera."

As a side note, regarding the smartphone launched by Meitu.com, some might say "taking too big steps easily..." However, now "cross-industry" operations are gradually becoming the norm. Whether selling pancakes or making beef brisket, they can all leverage the "Internet strategy." Therefore, it's not surprising for a software company to venture into hardware. Hopefully, Meitu.com will continue to make greater progress in the future.
