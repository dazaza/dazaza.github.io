Snowfall in the north develops to the east, while rainfall in the south brings both benefits and concerns - Weather Forecast News Center, Sina.com.

by psyzems1 on 2012-03-04 16:55:54

However, there are times when rain does good with bad results. Around 7:30 AM yesterday morning, a geological landslide occurred in Tzkai Town, Gongshan County, Yunnan Province, resulting in the death of three civilians. Meteorological data shows that the cumulative rainfall over the past three days in the area reached 31.3 millimeters.

The Central Meteorological Observatory forecasted that today, weak snowy weather would still occur in the northern winter wheat region, with light snow or sleet in eastern parts of Northwest China, western and northern parts of North China, and some areas in central Inner Mongolia experiencing heavy snow. From the 4th to the 7th, the range of snowfall will expand northeastward, with some areas having heavy snow and localized blizzards.

Today, the rainy weather base is still located in Jiangnan and Guizhou regions. Some areas in southern Jiangnan, central-northern Huannan, southern Guizhou have experienced heavy rain, while some areas have even seen torrential rain.

According to China Weather Network, starting from today (the 4th), the snowfall range in the north will extend northeastward. The majority of southern regions will continue to experience overcast and rainy weather. The precipitation intensity in Jiangnan and central-north Huannan remains strong. While drought-stricken areas in Yunnan enjoy the nourishment of the rain, they also need to guard against geological disasters.

Today, small to medium snow or sleet is expected in southwestern Xinjiang, central-eastern parts of the northwest region, most parts of Inner Mongolia, central-western Northeast China, western and northern North China, northern parts of Huaihai, northern Jianghan, etc., among which some areas in central Inner Mongolia and southeastern Liaoning may see heavy snow (3-6 mm).

Yesterday, Beijing, which just went through a weak rain and snow event, appeared somewhat gloomy and cold. Other places in North China and Huaihai lacked sunshine as well, with many places seeing falling snowflakes.

Regions in China with significant precipitation were mainly concentrated in Jiangnan, central-northern Huannan, southern Guizhou, and Yunnan areas, with some areas experiencing heavy rain reaching storm levels. From 5 am yesterday to 5 am today, Hubei southeast, Anhui south, Hunan northeast and south, Jiangxi north, Zhejiang west, Guangxi middle and northeast, and other places received 30-50 mm of rain, with some areas in central Guangxi receiving 70-122 mm.

It seems like the recent rains in Yunnan's drought-hit areas know that Lei Feng Memorial Day is approaching. They have been quite "enthusiastic" in delivering much-needed moisture to parched lands and rivers. From 5 am yesterday to 5 am today, central-north and west Yunnan received 3-10 mm of rain, with some areas in Lincang, Chuxiong, Kunming, and Qujing receiving 20-35 mm, and some areas receiving 45-57 mm. Since February 29, central and western Yunnan have generally seen more than 5 mm of rain, with some areas in Dehong, Baoshan, Lincang, Dali, Chuxiong, Kunming, and Qujing receiving 20-35 mm, and some areas in Lincang and Kunming receiving 50-89 mm.

National Precipitation Forecast Map

Meanwhile, the southwest part of the country still sees small to moderate rain or showers. Relevant departments are reminded to do a good job in drought-affected areas for water storage while being vigilant about possible geological disasters such as landslides caused by continuous precipitation and taking necessary preventive measures.

Southern Shaanxi, southern parts of Huaihai and areas further south will experience small to moderate rain or showers. Among these, southeastern Guizhou, central-southern and eastern Hunan, central-northern and western Jiangxi, southeastern Hubei, southern Anhui, southern Jiangsu, western and northern Zhejiang, northern Guangdong, northeastern and southern Guangxi will see heavy to torrential rain, with some areas in northeastern Guangxi, southern Hunan, northeastern Jiangxi, and southern Anhui potentially experiencing extreme heavy rain (100-120 mm). Some local areas may also be accompanied by short-term thunderstorms, hail, and other convective weather.

For people in Jiangnan, the seemingly endless rainy season feels like the plum rain season, causing feelings of depression yet leaving them powerless. However, for people in Yunnan's drought-stricken areas, welcoming the rain brings joy and anticipation.
