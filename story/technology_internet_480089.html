Fuao Technology teaches you the technical installation and maintenance of video surveillance systems.

by fuaokeji on 2011-08-20 10:47:24

What are the technologies for installation and maintenance of video surveillance systems? Nowadays, with the development of society and the increase in population, the application of video surveillance systems is becoming increasingly widespread, playing an important role in various industries such as roads, public security, forest fire prevention, buildings, factories, and shopping malls. In large-scale surveillance systems, the number of front-end fastballs and cameras can be countless; in large surveillance systems, images from front-end cameras must be transmitted through optical fibers to achieve wide-area monitoring image transmission to the monitoring center. Similarly, other industries' surveillance systems also use a large number of cameras and video optical terminal machines for transmission. Due to the extensive use of video surveillance systems, the installation and maintenance of these systems have become increasingly important. Guangzhou Fao will discuss the installation and maintenance of front-end cameras and video optical terminal machines in video surveillance systems, as well as strong electric measurement, equipment repair, wiring projects, and other comprehensive applications.

In video surveillance systems, since each monitoring point is located in a different position, it takes a lot of manpower and time to install front-end cameras (fastballs) and transmission equipment, as well as lay transmission lines. How to quickly detect and maintain equipment and transmission lines, and reduce maintenance costs, is a headache for engineering companies. For the maintenance of surveillance systems, it usually involves inspecting surveillance equipment and transmission lines. The equipment in the monitoring center of the video surveillance system, such as matrices, video servers, and video walls, is installed in the monitoring center server room, where the equipment is concentrated in a relatively good indoor environment, so the probability of failure of the monitoring center equipment is small; however, the front-end cameras and fastballs are installed outdoors, and the video optical terminal machines are installed in outdoor distribution boxes, where the equipment has to withstand harsh environments such as wind and rain, high summer temperatures, and low winter temperatures, thus having a higher probability of failure. To maintain front-end monitoring equipment, one must be able to test camera images, fastball images, pan-tilt control, video optical terminal machine's optical power, and use a multimeter to test the power supply system, among others, to comprehensively solve possible failures of front-end equipment.

Pan-tilt control maintenance

In security surveillance systems, pan-tilts or dome cameras are widely used, allowing monitoring center staff to adjust the camera angle dynamically. When the pan-tilt or dome camera is out of control or cannot rotate at a certain position, engineering personnel need to carry multifunctional keyboards to test whether the rotation of the pan-tilt or dome camera is normal. However, different pan-tilt or dome camera manufacturers have their own proprietary protocols, so engineering companies need to bring corresponding multifunctional keyboards for testing, possibly needing multiple keyboards to test different dome cameras. Engineering personnel can effectively solve this problem by using a comprehensive video surveillance tester, as it embeds more than 30 protocols such as Pelco-D/P and Bosch, and has a dedicated function for testing continuous rotation of pan-tilts. Engineering personnel can operate the pan-tilt through the instrument to keep rotating, observing whether there are any jamming phenomena during the rotation of the pan-tilt.

Camera maintenance

When the monitoring center does not receive images, engineering personnel first need to check if the camera ball or dome camera is functioning normally. When testing the camera equipment, engineering personnel use a comprehensive video surveillance testing instrument to perform three aspects of detection work: First, use the instrument to test if the camera has an image, and the 3.5-inch color display screen of the instrument can clearly display the image collected by the camera. If the instrument does not receive an image or receives an abnormal image, it indicates that there is a fault with the camera; Second, use the video signal attenuation measurement function of the comprehensive surveillance tester to measure the video composite level of the camera, which should normally be within the range of 800-1000mV. Too low a level will cause the image to darken, while too high a level will cause ghosting; Third, use the multimeter function of the instrument to measure if the transformer power supply of the camera is normal. Generally, the output of the camera's transformer is DC 12V, and the output of the dome camera's transformer is AC 24V, with the transformer being a component with a relatively high failure rate in surveillance systems.
