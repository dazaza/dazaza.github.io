2. Divide Into Two Parts

According to the principle of betting combinations, divide your funds into two parts: one part is defensive, used to ensure the principal, which can be applied to purchasing prize bonds or savings accounts with prizes; the other part is offensive, used for risk-taking, which can be applied to purchasing high-risk, high-yield computerized welfare lottery tickets. The proportion of each part in the total investment can be determined based on the gambling objective. If you expect capital appreciation, the proportion of the offensive part in the investment portfolio can be set higher, such as 70%~80%, while the proportion of the defensive part would then be 20%~30%; conversely, the proportions would be reversed.
