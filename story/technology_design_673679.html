Ha, after finishing the writing, I discovered that there were actually comments left, and many people supported the lack of content in the opening. When writing about management, Comrade Amy Chen wrote two articles that could be used by leaders, which was truly amazing. With everyone being so considerate, I thought whether we should transform this into a management forum for exchange or share some feelings. Tonight, I have no mood to talk about management, as it is something that can be done within 16 hours of work per day. The time now is clearly not within those 16 hours. So, I will share some feelings. Time leaves no trace, life just passes by day by day. I always think of Zhu Ziqing's "Hastily". Actually, in my early years, during university and even after starting work, I loved literature more than anything else, valuing experience over rationality. But I don't know when exactly, sensibility left me, occasionally surfacing deep within, where I can only say: Why do our days never return? When eating, the days flow by the edge of the bowl; when drinking water, the days flow by the water. Dear ones, why do our days never return?

Ha, back then I loved reading literature and martial arts novels, especially the "Storm" series. My favorite character was actually not the protagonist, but "Hua Manlou" from Gu Long's novels. A blind man, yet a cultivated and elegant one, with a faint smile always on his face. I wanted to be such a man, but as time changed, I became the protagonist in my own world. I also loved reading about the love story between Bu Jingyun and Xue Yuan in "Storm", which was so touching that it brought tears to my eyes.

I enjoyed writing letters to my family on rainy autumn evenings, especially when the autumn rain came down hard. Talking about how the tree wishes for stillness while the wind doesn't stop, and how the child wishes to care for their parents who are no longer around. Nowadays, I can only enjoy these wonderful moments alone in the quiet depths of the night, undisturbed.

Time leaves its mark on people, but I feel mine are heavier. Why do I say that?

I've seen people become more radical because of time, fighting against it. I've seen frustrated people who cannot fully adapt to the passage of time. I am neither of these types, but I am someone who adapts to time and forgets myself in the process. Time has changed me, but it's not obvious. It's not obvious because we have strangely unified within this period. Therefore, what it has given me is the deepest and hardest to express.

It is in such times that I follow it in the most extravagant or perhaps the most silent way, trying to make small changes to it. Adapting itself is a kind of silence!
