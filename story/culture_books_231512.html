"The Spokeswoman" mainly includes: The novel weaves together Zhou Jieping's memories and narratives with the past and present of Wang Haowen, bit by bit. There are both the bizarre and laughable realities of life in the present, as well as the refreshing and moving moments when emotions run deep. The characters in the book are distinct and vivid. There are cunning and hypocritical petty people like Liu Zhengda, and also self-smart elderly women like An Jiesheng. However, the most important character in the book is still Wang Haowen. Although she has committed unforgivable mistakes, she is essentially an honest, natural, and straightforward person. Finally, she goes to find a life more suitable for her, one that gives her peace of mind, and begins to correct her previous extreme views of the world. She realizes that although she has suffered injustice from childhood, striving to be a fair person is the best way to heal the pain caused by social injustices...

The Spokeswoman Part One

Her name is Wang Haowen. Before leaving the hospital, she was just an ordinary endocrinologist. She got along well with her colleagues and had received the title of model worker twice.

Later, she was temporarily transferred to help at the Health Department. Not long after, she was officially transferred there. After that, she worked for the municipal government and served as the vice district head of a certain district three years ago.

She gradually became a political star in our Jiangzhong city, rising so quickly that it left people astonished. Before the incident last night, she was the spokesperson for the Jiangzhong Municipal Government, frequently appearing on the evening news of Jiangzhong TV, as well as in media such as the "Jiangzhong Daily," "Jiangzhong Evening News," and "Jiangzhong Metropolis Daily."

The Spokeswoman Part Three

An Jiesheng uncovered so many things about Wang Haowen's past through her investigations. While investigating, she posted "indisputable facts" online. With her background in "daily teachings," she could present facts while making arguments.

She said, a woman with such great influence as Wang Haowen, besides the four main figures and various big brothers, was reportedly accompanied by a man of her age who rode bicycles with her to enjoy the scenery in the suburbs. In any case, she would not need to sell herself or submit to a man like Zhang Qi, who is old, ugly, and does not have enough power, to achieve her goals.

According to her nature, if she were to seek someone, it would be a big brother, someone who could make decisive decisions.

Her analysis made sense, but then, who was An Jiesheng pointing her finger at?

The Spokeswoman Part Four

You must have watched a movie with others before, or read a touching novel together. Especially when you hope that person can, like you, deeply understand how the movie or book educates you.

An Jiesheng continues her investigation, while the persistent Liu Zhengda repeatedly finds me, always asking me to say something new. This irritates me greatly. I use the implication method, hoping he understands my dissatisfaction with his behavior. I said, "An Jiesheng's investigation into Wang Haowen's past is really disgraceful."

I expect Liu Zhengda to feel ashamed from these words. But he doesn't. Like many people who watch movies or read books, he won't recognize himself through others' stories. He won't be moved at all, instead feeling quite calm, because the people in the movies or books are worlds apart from him.
