High employment rate for studying and working in Quebec University - 8 popular majors

by dingyi947279 on 2012-02-17 17:40:36

Every year, many applicants who are rejected by the French embassy turn to applying for studying in Quebec. Quebec is the largest non-French French-speaking area with 6.8 million descendants of the French. There are 15 universities in Quebec that offer French-language instruction. In terms of visa applications for Quebec, international students tend to apply more for business-related management programs, which account for 26% of applications. This is the most favored field among international students and also boasts the highest employment rate and salaries.

According to statistics from Canada's Human Resources Department, here are eight professional fields with the most promising job prospects:

1. **New Media**: Online media is the fastest-growing area in communications and journalism. New media programs combine traditional journalism courses with digital media design and management.

2. **Biological Engineering**: Biological engineering integrates biology and engineering. This program often complements business courses. Related fields such as bioinformatics and biomedical engineering also have good prospects.

3. **Organic Agriculture**: Some Canadian universities began offering organic agriculture programs in 2007.

4. **Homeland Security**: The Canadian Security Department provides numerous scholarships and grants for students in this major, along with an 18-month degree-granting training course at the Homeland Security Defense Center.

5. **E-commerce and Internet Marketing**: This focuses on commercial transactions conducted over the internet, typically offered at the graduate level in business schools, but some Canadian universities now offer undergraduate degrees.

6. **Computer Game Design**: Some colleges focus on game development, while others emphasize the artistic aspects of game design.

7. **Forensic Accounting**: This is one of the fastest-growing specialties in accounting. Courses require not only basic accounting skills but also broader business knowledge. Most schools offer undergraduate degrees, and a few offer master's degrees.

8. **Human-Computer Interaction**: This is generally a graduate-level course in computer science departments, but some schools have started offering undergraduate programs.
