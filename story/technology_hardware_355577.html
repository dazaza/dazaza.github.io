Shanxi tycoon encounters difficulties in Beijing

by oulalala94 on 2010-05-21 12:02:30

Shanxi billionaire Mr. Wang never imagined that his experience of purchasing a house in the capital and some related incidents would make him the focus of competition among various media outlets in Beijing at the end of 2007. In early 2005, Mr. Wang spent 5 million yuan to purchase two luxury villas in the urban area of the capital, second-hand houses in No.6 Li Ren Street, and then demolished them, building a villa on the original site. The new house was under renovation and had not yet been moved into when the Beijing city management department used three large-scale mechanical equipment, taking one and a half days to level it to the ground.

Realizing that his actions violated the current planning system, Mr. Wang wanted to build a house on the original site according to the original government planning plan, but he fell into difficulties because he did not have the qualification for real estate development.

High-priced purchase of a villa

A pile of rubble, red brick fragments, four toilets lying side by side in a corner, and more than 200 kerb stones piled up nearby, the only row of villas in the community disappeared completely, which was extremely inconsistent with the overall style of red walls and blue tiles around. On February 22, reporters were allowed to see this debris in the Beijing International Garden, which cost more than 10 million yuan.

Located next to the west Fourth Ring Road of Beijing, opposite the World Park, even in the capital where land is as precious as gold, the Beijing International Garden could be considered one of the best high-end residences. All villa buildings with courtyards, high housing prices, make ordinary people unattainable. Most of the residents in the park are heavyweight billionaires or celebrities. When Mr. Wang bought the house, the housing price here was more than 7,000 yuan per square meter. After more than two years, it has soared to more than 20,000 yuan per square meter.

At the beginning, Mr. Wang didn't expect the house to appreciate. His business and social circle are mainly in Shanxi and other adjacent provinces. The houses in his hometown and Taiyuan are enough for living. An accident in 2003 changed his life radius.

One day in 2003, Mr. Wang invited his cousin to go out together, and they encountered a car accident on the highway. The two cousins suffered serious injuries to their lumbar vertebrae and both sat on wheelchairs. Beijing's medical conditions are better, and the two cousins need to receive rehabilitation treatment in Beijing. At the beginning of 2005, Mr. Wang bought two row villas, E1 and E2 (i.e., 168-1, 168-2), not far from the rehabilitation center in Fengtai District, Beijing International Garden, covering a total area of 746.36 square meters, and the 5 million yuan housing payment was made by Mr. Wang alone. Mr. Wang thought that it was himself who had dragged down his cousin and wanted to compensate him through this way. One set for each cousin.

The decoration started, and Mr. Wang felt something was wrong: the villa was two floors above ground and one floor underground, and the two cousins needed wheelchairs to enter and exit, which was very inconvenient.

Reconstruction becomes self-construction

What to do with the villa purchased for more than 5 million yuan and cannot be lived in? Let's install an elevator, shared by the two, Mr. Wang and his cousin planned.

To share an elevator between the two row villas, there was only one way: to tear down the wall between the two villas and place the elevator in the middle. The owner, who has mobility issues, expressed this wish to the property management company. Considering that the two cousins were disabled, life without an elevator would be inconvenient, the Beijing Gaozhiyuan Property Management Company agreed to this requirement. The last item of the E1 and E2 decoration contract displayed by Li Huaishu, the general manager assistant of Gaozhiyuan Property Management Company, showed that the content of the decoration was to install one to two elevators.

On May 8, 2006, the decoration began. A week later, on May 15, the security guard of the property management company found that the so-called decoration had changed flavor. Why does installing an elevator require tearing off the tiles on the roof? The agent of Mr. Wang introduced that after demolishing the load-bearing wall between the two villas, the owner worried that the two villas would be difficult to support afterward. To avoid risks, the owner hoped to demolish the two villas for reconstruction. This agent said that this idea had also been approved by the property management company.

Li Huaishu did not recognize the statement of Mr. Wang's agent. He said that on the same day, May 15, when the security guard found that the decoration was somewhat out of control, the security guard had tried to dissuade them. On May 16, the property management company officially sent a public letter to Mr. Wang, requiring him to stop illegal construction, and instructed the security guards to prevent the workers of the E1 and E2 construction sites from entering the community. A small-scale dispute occurred between the two parties. After the police left, the workers came back, and they couldn't stop them.

Under the watchful eyes of the staff of Gaozhiyuan Property Management Company, the original two row villas were completely demolished, and a brand-new detached villa stood up at the end of 2006, covering an area of more than 1,100 square meters, with a market value of more than 22 million yuan.

What Mr. Wang did in his own courtyard, due to the relative independence of his house, basically did not affect the lighting and drainage of the surrounding neighbors after its completion. In the nearly one year that followed, everyone got along peacefully.

Villa eventually demolished

At about 9:30 am on December 3, 2007, after fulfilling all notification obligations such as delivering the "Notice of Limited Demolition" and "Demolition Decision", almost all members of the Fengtai District Urban Management Team of Beijing went to enforce the law at Mr. Wang's residence in Beijing International Garden. Accompanying them were three large pieces of machinery and journalists from various media outlets who had arrived upon hearing the news.
