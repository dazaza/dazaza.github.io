April 06, 2010 12:00 AM

In mid-March, the Shanghai subway network sent explorers to the Expo Park (Pudong and Puxi) for reconnaissance. It was merely a cursory visit; they just looked at the exteriors of various pavilions along the main roads within the park, which already took an hour. Therefore, the idea of touring the entire Expo Park in one day is nothing but wishful thinking.

Best Arrival Time - 8:30 AM

The Expo Park opens at 9:00 AM, so why arrive at 8:30 AM? The answer is to prepare for venue reservations.

Reserve Popular Venues First

Individual visitors must make on-site reservations to visit various exhibition halls within the park. If you are with a travel agency, then you can skip the trouble of making reservations. For super popular venues like the China Pavilion, the organizers implement a 100% reservation system. Therefore, it's essential to make reservations upon entering the park to avoid regretting not being able to visit.

There are 200 reservation machines at each entrance and exit of the park. When you enter, just ask the volunteers where they are located, and bring your valid ID and ticket.

The Expo Park is open from 9:00 AM to 12:00 midnight, a total of 15 hours. The opening hours of each exhibitor's section (i.e., opening time of the pavilions) are from 9:30 AM to 10:30 PM, a total of 13 hours. Entry into the park is prohibited after 9:00 PM daily, and most pavilions prohibit entry after 10:00 PM.

What to Do Between the Opening and Closing Times of the Park and Pavilions?

In fact, the Expo is not just about exhibitions; there are more than 100 cultural performances and activities every day in the park. Among these activities, the most spectacular ones are the daily opening and closing ceremonies of the park. During the entire Expo period (184 days), each country will also have its National Pavilion Day events. The opening ceremony of the park and most of the National Pavilion Day opening ceremonies take place between 9:00 AM and 9:30 AM.

There Are 13 Entrances and Exits

After out-of-town tourists find accommodation in Greater Shanghai, how can they reach the Expo Park fastest and most economically?

According to the plan, the Expo Park has a total of 13 entrances and exits, including 5 in the Pudong area, 3 in the Puxi area, 4 water entrances along the Huangpu River, and 1 dedicated subway entrance.

Puxi Area:

Entrance No. 1, at Luban Road and Longhua East Road intersection;

Entrance No. 2, at Zangnan Road and Zhongshan South Road intersection;

Entrance No. 3, at Bansongyuan Road and Baotun Road intersection.

Pudong Area:

Entrance No. 4, at Xueye Road and Bailianjing Road intersection;

Entrance No. 5, at Xueye Road and Gaokexi Road intersection;

Entrance No. 6, at Yaohua Road and Shangnan Road intersection;

Entrance No. 7, at Yaohua Road and Changqing North Road;

Entrance No. 8, at World Expo Avenue and Hou滩 entrance.

The dedicated subway entrance is Metro Line 13 Madang Road Station, located near Madang Road and Xujiahui Road. Additionally, Metro Line 9 can be directly transferred to Line 13 at Madang Road Station and enter the park after security checks.

Line 13 is the Most Convenient

Pudong Subway: Metro Line 7 - Exit at Houtan Station leads directly to Entrance No. 8 of the Expo Park, and exit at Yaohua Station leads directly to Entrance No. 6. There are also shuttle buses from Changqing Station and Gaokexi Road that connect to Entrance No. 7 and Entrance No. 5. Moreover, Metro Line 8 can also reach Yaohua Road Station, and Metro Line 6 can reach Gaokexi Road Station.

Puxi Subway: Metro Line 13 is the most convenient because taking Line 13 at Madang Road Station allows direct access to the Puxi and Pudong areas of the park after passing through security checks. Metro Line 9 can also transfer at Madang Road Station.

Bus Routes: Nearly 90 bus routes in Shanghai can reach the outer perimeter of the Expo Park, mainly including: Routes 17, 18, 36, 43, 45, 65, 66, 82, 89, 96, 253, 610, 614, 733, 802, 806, 869, 871, 910, and Zhou Nan Line, etc.

Water Route: Tourists who want to enjoy the scenery on both sides of the Huangpu River can choose to "tour" into the park via the water gate. Tourists who book through travel agencies can take boats from Qichang Dock, Qinhuangdao Road, and Shiliupu Water Gate, while individual tourists can only go to Dongchang Road Water Gate.

16 Special Lines Supporting

Additionally, within the 500 meters to 1.5 kilometers radius around the Expo Park, there is a controlled area. About 4,000 Expo-dedicated taxis across the city, under unified dispatching, can enter the priority zone, and after 9:00 PM daily, all taxis will be allowed to enter the controlled area.

If out-of-town tourists have just arrived in Shanghai and want to go to the Expo as soon as possible, what is the most convenient and fastest way to get to the park? According to the Shanghai Expo Transportation Coordination and Assurance Team, there will be 16 Expo special lines connecting to the Houtan, Shangnan Road Intersection, and Bailianjing entrances.

Pudong Airport: There is Expo Special Line No. 15, which can directly reach the Pudong BaiLianJing entrance of the Expo Park. Tourists can also take Metro Line 2 or the Maglev Train to Longyang Road Station, then transfer to Metro Line 7 and exit at Gaokexi Road, Yaohua Road, Changqing Road, or Houtan stations to reach the Pudong area.

Hongqiao Airport: At Terminal 1, there is Expo Line No. 19 leading to the Luban Road entrance of the Puxi area. At Terminal 2, there is Expo Special Line No. 4, which can directly reach the Shangnan Road entrance of the Pudong area. Tourists can also take Metro Line 2 at Terminal 2 to Jing'an Temple Station, then transfer to Metro Line 7 and exit at Gaokexi Road, Yaohua Road, Changqing North Road, or Houtan stations to reach the Pudong area; or transfer to Metro Line 4 at Zhongshan Park Station to directly reach the Luban Road and Zangnan Road entrances of the Puxi area.

Railway and Highway

At the North Square of Shanghai Station, there is Expo Special Line No. 1, which can directly reach the Shangnan Road entrance of the Pudong area, and tourists arriving by long-distance bus at the central station can also choose this line.

At the South Square of Shanghai Station, there is Expo Line No. 31, which can reach the Bansongyuan Road entrance of the Puxi area.

Tourists arriving at Shanghai Station who choose the subway are advised to first take Metro Line 1 to Changshu Road Station, then transfer to Metro Line 7 to reach the park.

At the South Square of Shanghai South Station, there is Expo Special Line No. 2, and at the North Square, there is Expo Special Line No. 3, both of which can directly reach the Houtan entrance of the Pudong area. Tourists arriving by long-distance bus at the southern station can also choose these two lines. Tourists arriving at Shanghai South Station who choose the subway are advised to first take Metro Line 1 to Shanghai Stadium Station, then transfer to Metro Line 4 to reach the Zangnan Road and Luban Road entrances of the Puxi area.

Garden, Zone, Section, Group, Cluster

Five-Level Layout

The Shanghai Expo planning scheme, taking into account walking suitable distances, human scale, and visitor cognition, proposes a five-level structural layout of "garden, zone, section, group, cluster":

--A 5.28 square kilometer Expo garden construction land area, including the fenced area and supporting facilities outside the fence area, with 3.93 square kilometers in Pudong and 1.35 square kilometers in Puxi.

--A 3.22 square kilometer Expo fenced area, with 2.47 square kilometers in Pudong and 0.75 square kilometers in Puxi.

--Five functional sections numbered A, B, C, D, E, with an average land area of 60 hectares.

--12 "groups" of exhibition halls with an average land size of 10-15 hectares, including 8 groups in Pudong and 4 groups in Puxi.

--26 "clusters" of exhibition halls with an average land size of about 2-3 hectares, each cluster can accommodate 40-45 exhibition units, with a total building area of about 2-2.5 million square meters per cluster.

Each cluster is equipped with small-scale public service facilities such as dining, shopping, telecommunications, restrooms, and baby care services according to convenience and proximity principles.

National pavilions are arranged and laid out as exhibition units based on their continent's geographical location in clusters, divided into three categories: the first category is self-built pavilions, the second category is leased pavilions, and the third category is joint pavilions provided free of charge to developing countries.

Small Reminder

In principle, each venue in the park has two visiting routes, with the fast lane dedicated to sightseers. Visitors are advised to take the slow lane at major venues and national self-built pavilions.
