Many people choose to renovate their new homes in the summer, but due to weather conditions, there are many quality and safety hazards associated with summer renovations. Today, we will look at some summer renovation tips summarized by experts that will surely be very helpful to you.

**Precaution One:**

Summer Renovation Prioritizes Safety

The summer temperature is relatively high, and the climate is dry. There are often a large number of materials stacked at the construction site. From a safety perspective, materials should be placed in shaded areas as much as possible to avoid fires caused by poor air circulation and high temperatures. Materials should also be minimized on-site to reduce potential accident risks. Additionally, electrical appliances and tools used during work should maintain a certain distance from the materials. When working with electricity, it’s best to keep a safe distance from materials and create a reasonable division between the work area and material storage area.

**Precaution Two:**

Ventilate Regularly to Prevent Pollution from Renovation

To avoid being harmed during the "high incidence period" of summer renovation pollution, in addition to choosing non-toxic or less toxic decoration materials and hiring reputable home renovation companies, it's important to ensure good ventilation and air purification in the renovated rooms. If possible, try to ventilate the room as much as possible; if not, indoor ventilation devices or air purifiers that can reduce indoor harmful gases can be used.

**Precaution Three:**

Prevent Moisture and Direct Sunlight Exposure

When purchasing wooden products in the summer, including major construction materials like plywood and auxiliary materials such as white wood strips, corner lines, door edges, window frames, and baseboards along the wall, it's essential to check the moisture content of these wood products. In building material markets, most wood product specialty stores should have wood moisture content testers. According to national regulations, the standard moisture content for boards cannot exceed 12%. Experts suggest that during the rainy season, slightly drier materials can be chosen. Once the materials arrive on-site, they can be left for a day or two to adapt to the surrounding humidity, making them more suitable for use.

Additionally, wooden boards should be protected from strong sunlight exposure. If the humidity is high during rainy days, measures should be taken to prevent dampness and deformation. Do not place wooden boards or materials in direct sunlight, as this could cause the paint and glue on wooden decorations to age faster. Also, rainwater can cause wooden products to swell, deform, or even mold, so special attention should be paid to this in daily life.

**Precaution Four:**

Wallpaper and Paint Should Dry Naturally

Paint and wallpaper are commonly used wall decoration materials. In spring, because of the humid air, windows and doors are often opened wide to speed up drying. However, in summer, because of the dry conditions, wallpapers are generally soaked in water before applying glue and sticking them. Opening windows and doors at this time would expose the newly laid wallpaper to crosswinds, causing rapid dehydration and resulting in shrinking and deformation.

**Precaution Five:**

Remove Moldy Odors

In home renovations, latex paint may dry slowly and develop moldy odors in humid weather. The common solution used by renovation companies is to turn on the air conditioning to dehumidify, removing moisture from the air. Furniture cabinet doors should remain open to maintain ventilation. After renovation, various unpleasant odors may linger, affecting health. The suggestion from renovation companies is to place more green plants or put two or three lemons, oranges, or bananas in the room to quickly eliminate odors.

**Precaution Six:**

Avoid Painting on Rainy Days

Experts recommend that whether dealing with wooden products, applying clear varnish or oil-based finishes, painting should be avoided on rainy days. On rainy days, workers should focus on other tasks or temporarily halt work instead of doing painting jobs.

**Precaution Seven:**

Do Not Rush to Fix Seasonal Issues

In spring, the air humidity is high, and walls, floors, and wooden furniture contain more moisture. As summer approaches, the air gradually becomes drier, which might lead to seasonal issues such as floorboard contraction and increased gaps between boards, or gaps appearing between walls and doorframes due to different shrinkage rates. Cracks may also appear on walls. These are all normal phenomena during seasonal transitions. Immediate repairs might not be effective as moisture continues to evaporate, potentially causing further cracking. It is better to wait until the wall moisture aligns with the external climate before conducting any necessary repairs for optimal results.

**Precaution Eight:**

Include Radiators in Your Considerations

Even though you may not need radiators during the hot summer, they will become essential in the future. Since home renovation projects are "irreversible," regardless of when you renovate, you should consider replacing your radiators. Replacing traditional cast iron radiators with modern steel tube radiators, which offer better heating effects and attractive designs, has become a popular trend. However, you should notify relevant departments beforehand.
