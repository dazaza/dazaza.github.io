Xiangcai Securities: Jinjing Technology invests in solar glass, expecting performance release_stock basics

by dx0957095 on 2009-12-03 18:33:06

The company has released a private placement plan, intending to issue no more than 100 million shares to specific investors at a price of no less than RMB 14.97 per share. Shandong Zibo Zhongqi Building Materials Co., Ltd., the actual controlling shareholder of the company, will not participate in this subscription. The funds raised will be used to establish a wholly-owned subsidiary, Beijing Jinjing Smart Solar Energy Materials Co., Ltd., in Daxing, Beijing, and invest RMB 15.1627 billion to build a solar special glass production line with a daily capacity of 600 tons, as well as a solar functional material processing center with a daily capacity of 1,000 tons. After the issuance, the holding ratio of Zhongqi Building Materials will decrease from the current 38.69% to 33.09%, but the control rights remain unchanged.

Solar glass benefits from the growth of the photovoltaic industry. Benefiting from the policy advantages of low-carbon economy and new energy, the global photovoltaic industry demand is expected to grow rapidly, with the installed capacity of photovoltaics likely to maintain a growth rate of over 30%. Currently, domestic photovoltaic manufacturers account for 30% of the total output, and it is expected that this proportion will continue to rise. The rapidly growing output of photovoltaic modules supports the long-term strong demand for solar glass.

Domestic development is rapid, but market competition risks are significant. According to Isuppli statistics, the theoretical capacity of domestic solar glass can already meet global demand. Although market demand is growing rapidly, the competition risks caused by product supply and demand are significant, and the future profit margin of the product may be appropriately compressed. The main suppliers in the domestic solar glass market are Nangbo A (000012), which holds more than half of the market share, with a global market share of 26%; additionally, China Glass (3300.HK) has an annual production capacity of over 100,000 tons. These two companies pose significant competitive pressure on Jinjing Technology's solar glass fundraising project.

The construction period is relatively long, and performance contribution will occur after 2011. The company's ultra-white glass has received Oerlikon certification, and the early procurement by Solarworld and trials by downstream manufacturers such as Tianwei Baobian have opened up a certain market space for the company. Given that the construction period is a total of three years, the gradual release of performance can bring continuous high growth to the company. It is estimated that the company's EPS in 2009, 2010, and 2011 will be 0.25, 0.46, and 0.93 yuan respectively, and it can still maintain high performance growth after 2012. Given that the current stock price has reasonably reflected the company's expected future growth, we give the company an "overweight" rating.

The procedures for buying and selling stocks are as follows: when buying or selling stocks, you must specify whether you are buying or selling, the name (or code) of the stock, quantity, and price. Moreover, this entrustment is only valid on the day it is issued. The content of the entrustment includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock you want to buy or sell. The abbreviation of the stock is usually three to four Chinese characters, and the code of the stock is six digits. When entrusting to buy or sell, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks bought or sold: the quantity of stocks entrusted to buy must be a whole multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks entrusted to sell does not have to be a whole multiple of 100.

There are four methods of entrustment: counter submission entrustment, telephone automatic entrustment, computer automatic entrustment, and remote terminal entrustment.
