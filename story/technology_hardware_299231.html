The responder explains that when it comes to choosing ways to further one's education, academic degree programs are favored for their strong faculty background, systematic learning systems, good exchange platforms, and authoritative national certification. Many people who have already graduated with a bachelor's degree aspire to pursue further studies and obtain a master's degree. However, many are confused about the different pathways to advanced study, such as what full-time master’s programs entail, how part-time master’s programs work, and why some courses offer only a degree without an academic qualification.

In short, the only way to simultaneously obtain a nationally recognized master's degree and academic qualification is through the annual January national unified examination, followed by full-time study at a university. There are three ways to obtain a master's degree certificate: participating in the national master's entrance examination, the unified examination for master's degrees for persons of equal academic ability, and professional degree examinations.

The National Master's Entrance Examination usually refers to the national unified examination held every January. This examination generally requires candidates to have a junior college degree or above. After passing the exam and entering school, students engage in full-time study, complete credits, and meet requirements such as thesis defense to obtain a nationally recognized academic qualification certificate and master's degree certificate. The national master's entrance examination is also the most popular and competitive way to obtain a master's degree. Due to the deepening requirements for knowledge structure and the pressure of talent competition, the number of people registering for the graduate entrance examination has been increasing year by year. Although the number of master's students has been expanding, it still cannot meet the huge demand for registration.

Professional Master's Degree Education differs from the theoretical and research-focused national master's education; it aims to cultivate innovative applied talents that combine theory with practice. Currently, domestic professional master's degrees include well-known ones like the Master of Business Administration (MBA), Master of Public Administration (MPA), Master of Engineering, Master of Law (J.M.), as well as the Master of Accounting (MPACC), Master of Public Health (MPH), Master of Agricultural Extension, Master of Veterinary Medicine, and Master of Education, among others. At the beginning of this year, three new professional degrees were added: Master of Arts, Master of Sports, and Master of Landscape Architecture.

Professional master's degree exams are mainly conducted through the October joint exam, but there are exceptions, such as the MBA, law master, clinical medicine master, and architecture master, which can also participate in the January national unified exam. The joint exam only provides a degree certificate, while participating in the January national unified exam allows for both academic and degree certificates.

Besides the October national joint exam, the recruitment unit will also conduct exams on professional skills and knowledge.

Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam

The Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam, commonly known as equal academic ability application for a master's degree, is a method of obtaining a master's degree that many people do not fully understand or misunderstand. Some people even do not know what it means to apply for a master's degree while working, and some think "participating in this exam is meaningless."

However, Yu Huizi from the Shanghai Teaching Center for Part-time Master's Degrees at Renmin University of China evaluates the Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam as one of the best ways for working professionals to further their education domestically. The initial purpose of the Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam was to recognize those whose academic level or specialized technical level has reached the equivalent level of a graduate student in the applied discipline outside the current channels for granting degrees to graduates.

Since 1999, the Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam has conducted a unified national exam for foreign language proficiency and comprehensive subject proficiency each May.

Overemphasis on academic qualifications becomes a concern in selection

Despite its more practical and convenient features, many people still have doubts about applying for a master's degree while working when choosing continuing education. Part of this doubt stems from a misunderstanding of academic qualifications. Academic qualifications acknowledge the education a student has received during a certain period at school but cannot prove their academic theoretical level and research ability, nor represent their learning ability. A degree, on the other hand, acknowledges a person's research capabilities. Teacher Yu Huizi stated that for education below the university level, academic qualifications have some significance, but for higher-level, research-focused master's education, the degree itself is more convincing than the academic qualification.

The misunderstanding in people's minds that having only a degree certificate is less valuable and secure than having dual certificates poses a certain obstacle to the development of the Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam and makes the January master's entrance exam highly competitive.

However, looking at the trend of educational development abroad, employers and candidates will inevitably increasingly value the degree certificate representing personal capability levels and downplay the role of academic qualification certificates.

What is a postgraduate advancement class?

Many people are unclear about what a postgraduate advancement class actually is and its relationship with applying for a master's degree while working. We can understand it as follows: a postgraduate advancement class is the main bridge to participate in the Equal Academic Ability Master's Exam and obtain an Equal Academic Ability Master's Degree, provided by units based on their own professional settings.

The prerequisite for applying for a master's degree with equal academic ability is to possess equivalent academic and capability levels. However, self-study and summarization solely based on practical experience can easily lead to unsystematic course learning, blind spots, and relatively weak theoretical foundations. The pass rate for the unified exam is also not high. Considering this, schools offer postgraduate advancement classes to provide knowledge organization, introduce the latest cutting-edge research results, and provide targeted guidance on exam points, enabling them to reach the required academic and technical levels.

Five conditions for applying for a master's degree

How to apply for and obtain a master's degree is a question many people are very concerned about. For some, whether they can obtain a master's degree directly affects their choice.

Generally speaking, applying for a master's degree with equal academic ability requires meeting five conditions:

- Applicants must have a bachelor's degree and more than three years of work experience.

- Pass the qualification review of the degree-granting institution.

- Pass the course exams organized by the degree-granting institution, usually consisting of 14-18 courses, where each course must be completed with credits and pass the exam.

- Pass the national unified foreign language proficiency test and comprehensive subject test for persons applying for a master's degree with equal academic ability. Applicants must pass all course exams and national level tests within four years from the date of passing the qualification review; otherwise, this application will be invalid.

- Complete the thesis within one year after passing all exams. If the thesis defense fails, this application will be invalid. However, if the thesis defense committee suggests revising the thesis and redefending, the applicant may redefend within half a year to one year. Failure to pass the defense or failure to apply within the deadline will render this application invalid. Additionally, an academic paper must be published in a magazine with a national unified serial number.

Comparison of Three Pathways

From the perspective of curriculum setting and faculty, there is no difference between the January exam master's program and the equal academic ability master's program. In terms of teaching methods and directions, the equal academic ability master's program focuses more on the combination of theory and practice, meeting the actual needs of part-time master’s students. Regarding certificate acquisition, the January master's entrance exam leads to a double certificate, whereas the equal academic ability master's leads to a single certificate. From the time arrangement, the January exam master's program is mostly full-time, while the equal academic ability master's program offers more flexible class times suitable for working professionals. Although the total amount of study time is reduced for the equal academic ability master's program, the learners themselves already have considerable practical experience, so it does not affect the quality of learning.

From the perspective of application conditions, besides a bachelor's degree and certain work experience, the equal academic ability master's program requires a bachelor's degree. However, not all professional master's degrees necessarily require a bachelor's degree. Secondly, the equal academic ability master's program covers various fields widely, while the currently implemented professional master's degree exams involve only a few professions. From the target audience, both aim at working professionals with certain work experience who wish to further their studies and belong to the part of continuing education, with an educational approach leaning towards non-full-time education.
