Mooncake prices rise by one tenth, long-life mooncakes are no more

by lzxliyas on 2011-08-11 10:14:00

Although there is still more than a month to the Mid-Autumn Festival, the reporter found when visiting major supermarkets and cake shops that loose moon cakes and boxed moon cakes had been on sale one after another, half a month earlier than last year. Due to cost reasons, the price increased by nearly 10%. After the new version of "Food Additive Use Standard" was issued, because of stricter control over additives, the shelf life of moon cakes was shortened from several months to 45 days, and the longest did not exceed 60 days.

According to Jitongbao, this year's moon cakes made an early appearance on the market on the one hand to create a festival atmosphere, and on the other hand it was also for various businessmen to occupy business opportunities as soon as possible to face fierce market competition. The reporter successively visited the Century Lianhua, RT-Mart, Wal-Mart and other supermarkets and cake shops, and saw varieties of loose moon cakes in line, all in conspicuous positions in each store. Most of the moon cake special areas were small loose moon cakes, while the gift box moon cakes with exquisite packaging and dazzling colors were fewer. Although the packaging of moon cakes was general, the price of moon cakes was still not cheap.

The price of various raw materials of moon cakes were rising this year, so the price of moon cakes would definitely rise too. Moon cakes which were sold at 39.8 yuan per catty last year were now sold at 34 yuan, and those which were sold at 28.8 yuan per catty last year were now sold at around 22 yuan, rising by a few yuan. Supermarket guides told the reporters that the rise in the price of moon cakes was largely due to the influence of sugar and other raw material prices. This year, the price of moon cakes rose by about 10%, and the increase was between 8%~20%. However, compared with the price increase of raw materials by two or three tenths, moon cake manufacturers seemed much more cautious about price increases.

Jitongbao statistical data showed that in the 2010/2011 season, the whole country produced 10.4542 million tons of sugar, less than 10.7383 million tons in the 2009~2010 season by 284,100 tons. The sharp increase in sugar production costs and the domestic supply and demand situation forced downstream food enterprises to raise the prices of food including moon cakes. Currently, food enterprises in Northeast China had entered the stage of preparing materials and pricing for Mid-Autumn moon cakes. Most enterprises said that the prices of flour, oil, white sugar and other raw materials used for making pastries had all risen, and the price increase of this autumn's moon cakes would be around 10%~20%. In addition, compared with the same period last year, the price of moon cakes in Chengdu area generally rose by 10% this year, and the increase of boxed moon cakes was nearly 20%.

The new food additive regulations issued in June this year also caused no small impact on the market price of moon cakes. A person in charge of a moon cake manufacturer in Luocun, Nanhai said that only more than ten kinds of additives were allowed to be used in moon cakes according to the new regulations, resulting in a sharp increase in filling costs. Ammonium sulfite was mostly used for whitening lotus paste filling before, but this additive had been deleted in the new regulations. This meant that filling manufacturers could only use physical methods such as washing and soaking more, so the selling price of lotus paste per piece rose from last year's 270 yuan to 320 yuan.

It was learned that in the newly revised "Food Additive Use Standard" on June 20, it was clearly stipulated that the use of 27 categories of food additives was prohibited. The reduction in additives greatly shortened the shelf life of moon cakes, but also made them safer. In previous years, the shelf life of moon cakes was determined according to different categories, and the longer shelf life was 75 days. While this year, with the prohibition of 27 additives including bleaching agents in moon cakes, the shelf life had been shortened to around 50 days, and long-life moon cakes lasting up to half a year were rarely seen.

How long should the shelf life of moon cakes be? Industry insiders revealed that the country did not yet clearly stipulate the preservation period of moon cakes. How long moon cakes could be preserved mainly depended on the repeated tests carried out during the production by moon cake manufacturers. Generally speaking, the shelf life of moon cakes was mostly 2 months, and some Chao-style moon cakes were even shorter. Some double-packaged moon cakes could have their preservation period appropriately extended because antioxidants were added in the outer package.
