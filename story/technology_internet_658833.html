Before filming "Palace", Yang Mi had never read any time-travel literature and knew little about the Qing Dynasty or Emperor Yongzheng. She said if she could travel through time, she would not go back to ancient times but rather see the future world: "Can I go to the future for a look and then come back?!" The ending of "Palace" has Qingchuan and Prince Eight embracing together after traveling back to modern times... Yang Mi said this is an open-ended conclusion, giving everyone much room for imagination.

After the broadcast of "Palace," despite mixed reviews, its ratings remained consistently high. Its popularity reflected two aspects: on one hand, it rapidly boosted the popularity of Yang Mi and other leading actors; on the other hand, the drama also sparked many social discussions.

The producer and screenwriter Yu Zheng said that due to emotional barriers in modern society, people yearn for genuine feelings and need an outlet for their emotions. A drama like "Palace" aligns well with the emotional needs of young people. Professor Zhang Yiwu from Peking University specifically wrote a blog post titled "From 'Palace,' Discussing the Vitality of Youth Culture." He said that although the plot of the drama was absurd, the story was reasonable and touching; though the imagination might seem abrupt, the design was ingenious; and while the thoughts may not be profound, the main theme was positive and uplifting. The sensation caused by "Palace" indicated that Chinese television culture, since the era of "Super Girl," has been gradually becoming younger.

Yang Mi, the lead actress, was very fond of Qingchuan when she started filming "Palace" and was full of anticipation for the show, but such a fiery effect was beyond her expectations. On the day "Palace" aired, she felt both delighted and anxious. In her Weibo post, she said: "My darling Qingchuan is finally going to meet everyone..." On the day of the grand finale of "Palace," she expressed some sadness: "It's almost over. Would you cry for her, him, their stories? Thank you for everything we've experienced together. Keep it in your heart, all understood."

In just over ten days since the airing of this drama, Yang Mi's number of Weibo followers increased from 3 million to 4 million, with the number of comments and forwards for each post surpassing Yao Chen and Hualan Biotech. Perhaps because this change happened too quickly or because she was always shooting on set, Yang Mi only perceived her sudden rise in popularity through the Weibo platform.

But Yang Mi did not become famous overnight. Although she was born after 1985, if we count from her debut work, she absolutely belongs to the category of seasoned actors. At the age of four, Yang Mi starred in "Emperor Tang Minghuang" directed by Chen Jialin. At five, she played the role of Zhou Xingchi's daughter in the Hong Kong film "Wu Zhuangyuan Su Qier." At six, she co-starred with Liu Xiaolingtong in the movie "Monkey Kid." At fifteen, Yang Mi was selected by Rayli magazine as an exclusive print model.

After being admitted to the Film Academy, Yang Mi received many enviable opportunities. She played Guo Xiang in the highly anticipated TV series "The Legend of the Condor Heroes," led the cast as Wang Zhaojun in CCTV's major production "Wang Zhaojun," and portrayed Qingwen in Li Shaohong's "Dream of the Red Chamber."

This is the dazzling star path of Yang Mi. In her Weibo, she labeled herself with these tags: Post-80s, Fox, Zixi, Actress, Virgo, Type B blood, and Beijinger.

Now Yang Mi has two nicknames, Xianggu and Fox. Since Qingchuan was called "Xianggu" in "Palace," during one filming session, an actor accidentally called her "Xianggu," thus giving her the nickname. "Fox" was given to her by a good friend named "Cat."

In the drama, Qingchuan walks out from the hollow willow tree in the Forbidden City's Imperial Garden; outside the drama, Yang Mi, dressed in the spring/summer 2011 new collection, takes us through her acting journey across time.

The superficial beauty, help me take away the weariness in my heart.

On the day of finishing the TV series "Goodbye, Sunny Days" in 2009, Guangzhou Hu Lan Biotechnology Co., Ltd. wrote: "Finishing a project happens in an instant, like a free-falling stone hitting the water, creating countless ripples, and a sense of loss slowly begins to spread." On February 27, 2011, at 2 o'clock in the morning, after finishing the filming of the TV series "Beijing Love Story," Yang Mi wrote: "Roses, candles, music, and a lot of blessings, thank 'Beijing Love' for giving me this perfect ending! The fox has finished filming!" The next morning, she walked into the studio to shoot the cover for BQ.

Q: Compared to two years ago, do you still have those complex emotions now after wrapping up a project?

Y: I don't feel anything anymore, I've gotten used to it. Always in the process of wrapping up, there's basically no buffer time. After wrapping up, it's even busier than before. There are always activities and various works waiting for you. Once something starts, you don't plan to stop it. Learn to be someone who willingly accepts what fate gives you, and bravely bears the consequences, knowing how to step back to move forward. Enjoy giving to be happy.

Q: A few days ago, when you were taking photos for a magazine, you posted a Weibo: "The superficial beauty, help me take away the weariness in my heart!" This is a kind of emotional release, right?

Y: Because it was quite cold that day, around minus several degrees Celsius, it coincided with snowfall in Beijing, and we were shooting outdoor scenes. I was wearing clothes that exposed my shoulders or legs. Such continuous work has been going on for two or three years, so mentally, I am a bit tired. But, work, no matter how hard, is just like this. What else can you do? How big a deal is it...

Q: Actually, entering and exiting roles is a kind of time-travel. You keep time-traveling without any buffer... How many shows did you shoot in the past year?

Y: 11 shows, some were cameos, some were leading roles. As a leading role, there were probably five or six. Besides these 11 shows, there were also many advertisements and events. (So, will the freshness decrease?) It will still be there, because I keep working with fresh teams. Each opportunity I encounter is different. When working with teams I've worked with before, we become more默契. The greatest advantage of being an actor is experiencing different work every day. For example, if something unexpected happens, people will ask you all sorts of gossip questions, and I might face various tests anytime. I never look at outlines before interviews, including talk shows.

Q: Is it because there is no communication?

Y: Not only is there no communication, but I treat it as a new challenge. I enjoy this feeling of being full.

Q: I thought you were a girl who is picky and sensitive, but I found that you have a certain resilience inside, and your words are quite insightful.

Y: Maybe because I am a Virgo, first, I am a workaholic, second, I am a perfectionist, I have high standards for myself, especially at work. Out of 365 days in a year, maybe 360 days are spent working, so I urge myself every day. (Friends call you Sister Mi, which means you're quite righteous?) Very familiar with friends, so they call me this way. I am not afraid of hardship, what I can do is to work hard while I'm young, I think I can exploit myself a bit.

Q: Besides effort, you are also grateful. Many people only start to express gratitude when they achieve success, but you say you are grateful every day when you wake up.

Y: People, regardless of their position, should always be grateful. It will constantly remind you and prompt you to treat all external comments with a normal heart. Because you have a grateful heart, you will value what you currently possess even more. Whether it's something you once had or something you currently have, it's all hard-earned and cannot be achieved by one person alone. It must be done with the assistance of others. Therefore, listen to the honest words of the closest people around you. As people get older or achieve certain accomplishments, fewer people will tell you the truth, and flattery will increase. At this moment, clear judgment and decision-making ability are needed. There must still be someone around you who tells the truth. The staff around me often tell me the truth, often suppress me, give me little whips.

Q: For example, have they suppressed you recently?

Y: Of course, I need their encouragement when I am working hard. These things complement each other. My mentality has been pretty good recently. Now, the whole world is saying that I became famous because of "Palace," but actually, it's okay. For me, "Palace" is just one of many film and television works. It might be due to favorable timing, place, and people, so more people watched this show and liked the character Qingchuan. I think it's my luck, not something I deserve. So, asking what important significance this show has for me, first, because more people watched it, they like me, I am happy; secondly, what it brings me is not something I can control, and I won't let these uncontrollable things affect my mentality.

Q: Rarely do you remain so calm at such a young age amidst the external noise and rapid rise to fame. Is it because you also have a sense of crisis?

Y: It's not a crisis, it's self-discipline. Because Virgos always urge themselves. External forces or crises given by others have no impact on me. My sense of crisis comes from within, stemming from my lack of confidence or failure to do something well, making me feel uneasy, not from others. The recent motto is: Don't look for trouble if there's none, and don't fear trouble if there is.

Q: Remember you said, "It's much better for the phone to get wet than for the brain to get wet"... People fear losing their minds.

Y: I think the most terrifying thing for a person is not knowing their own situation. A book was very popular before, called "The Secret," where the most mentioned word was "gratitude," thanking God, thanking the universe, appreciating what it has given you. Secondly, it's the law of attraction, whatever you think about, it will transmit to you. In life, you will definitely encounter people who hurt you. Only after experiencing pain will you know what not to do and who not to associate with. Having these experiences when you're young allows you to know early on that some words are wrong and some people can't be trusted... This is much better than encountering these harmful people when you're old.

For those who have hurt me, I will use them as motivation for self-improvement. This way, I can not only forgive them but also consider them as friends. In this regard, I am fatalistic, always believing that if someone comes to harm you, another person will appear to help you. Therefore, I accept everything, good or bad, gladly.

Q: Thus, "Palace" making you suddenly famous seems not accidental. You started acting at a young age, and all these years of hard work have been a preparation and accumulation. Luck always favors those who are always prepared...

Y: To be honest, I don't care too much about others' evaluations of me. Fortunately, I have enough self-awareness. My evaluation doesn't come from the outside but from within myself. I know what I am like, so I don't need you to evaluate me because you don't know me. I am very grateful for everyone's criticism and praise, which represents attention to this show. As long as it's correct, I can accept it. For me, "Palace" is neither a beginning nor an end, nor a masterpiece, just a show, a process.

Q: Before, "Sword & Fairy 3" had great significance for you, was it in terms of performance or popularity?

Y: I don't mean that "Sword & Fairy 3" has great influence, but the process of shooting "Sword & Fairy 3" was very hard, and its significance lies in being a turning point in my growth. For a period before that, I was very depressed and didn't want to work. And while shooting "Sword & Fairy 3," I was also shooting "Dream of the Red Chamber." That period was very intense, requiring double mental and physical efforts. It's similar to now, but before that, my mentality wasn't adjusted well. After finishing this show, I felt completely renewed, reborn. Just like when you are in a fixed state and dare not change, suddenly someone hits you with a hammer, breaking you apart. That period was a process of breaking and reassembling. This is a change in my life, unrelated to anything else.

Willing to exchange the entire universe for a red bean

Yang Mi has a hobby of writing lyrics. Two lyrics she wrote in high school were highly praised. She also likes to write blogs in the form of lyrics, with a strong rhythm, easy to read aloud. She joked that the side effect of this habit is that she dislikes adding punctuation when writing long pieces. Yang Mi also has potential in singing, and this time she contributed her voice to "Palace," singing "Love's Supply" and "Bright Moon." "Cutting a piece of candlelight, illuminating the classics, not seeking grandeur, only seeking love..." What Yang Mi sang was also her inner voice: willing to exchange the entire universe for a red bean.

Q: Accompanying your rise to fame, are there people talking to you about singing? This might mean you'll be busier...

Y: People might ask if I will get busier after "Palace," but I have always been busy and tired, it's just that people didn't pay much attention before.

Q: Last year, you shot 11 shows, shouldn't you have the right to choose? Or are you not good at saying NO?

Y: This is a mutual trust between artists and companies. You entrust your work to the team, and you need to trust their arrangements. Understand why they arrange things this way. From the current results, I think their arrangements are correct. This is a tacit understanding of cooperation. When everyone knows the goal of the work, there will be no complaints. Everyone is actually very hardworking, it's just that people see my hard work, but not theirs.

Q: What parts of yourself are imprinted on Qingchuan? Comparing the recognition of you before and after the airing of this show, what do you think is the biggest change in yourself?

Y: First, I really like the character Qingchuan. While reading the script, I found that this person goes through different stages. Initially, she might be like Little Swallow, then later, a villain named Huayin appears, also played by me, which involves a personality shift in acting, satisfying my greed as an actor, making it very enjoyable.

What does "Palace" bring me? Everyone sees it, from my direct observation, my Weibo followers are increasing, and the number of comments has increased significantly. During this period, I have been shooting continuously, so I haven't had time to go out and test how many people recognize me. All I see is that the number of comments has increased significantly, that's all.

Q: The media also notices this. Your Weibo followers increased from 3 million to 4 million in just a few days. Moreover, the popularity of "Palace" has become a cultural phenomenon worthy of attention.

Y: This is both unexpected and an honor for me. I still want to thank everyone who likes this show. Without their support, today's achievements wouldn't be possible. But I think popularity is stage-based. It might be because more people are watching "Palace" now, and they are very interested in you. Later, there might be other hotter dramas and topics. Everything will return to its original position. I treat it with a normal heart. In my view, letting go has never been a difficult thing. Shake off the dust, and it's just starting again. I believe in dreaming wildly and walking grounded.

Q: You just finished Chen Sicheng's directorial debut "Beijing Love Story," collaborating with actors who are usually close friends. This must be a sense of happiness for you?

Y: For me, it's a relatively new attempt because I rarely shoot modern dramas. It's rare to shoot a modern drama in Beijing this time, and having the opportunity to be in Beijing during the New Year is a blessing for me. In a year, there are only so many days to spend time with my parents. Brief warmth is the motivation for all work. Usually, I'm almost always shooting, with little time to go home and accompany them. Luckily, I often call them. (What do they worry about most regarding you?) It's my health, because I'm quite thin and can't gain weight no matter how much I eat. They don't worry about me not getting married, haha!

Q: "Beijing Love Story" is a modern version of love, while "Palace" is a shuttle between modern and ancient times, but no matter which era, "love" is interconnected... You said you like a line from Huang Yidao's song - "Love is a journey that cannot return." Is this your attitude towards love?

Y: This is a line from Huang Yidao's song "Microscopic Love" - "After loving you, then hating you thoroughly, it turns out that love is a journey that cannot return..." I think life is a journey that cannot return, not just love... Otherwise, how could there be that phrase - "If only life were always as beautiful as the first sight." Live in the present, cherish the people in front of you, not just love, but also family and friendship. Some things disappear in the blink of an eye, so we should cherish and be grateful.

Q: Between the eighth prince and the fourth prince in "Palace," if you had to choose in real life, who would you lean towards?

Y: I wouldn't choose either. In life, I like men who have a sense of responsibility and can tolerate and understand my work. The long-term commitment of two people requires a certain amount of courage.

Perhaps every woman has a Cinderella complex. People like the eighth prince or the fourth prince because they stand up to protect their princesses at critical moments. Even the strongest women hope to be protected by men. This thinking might be triggered by the Cinderella psychology.

Q: Many people compliment you on your beauty, and because of this perfection, there will be another type of questioning voice, such as accusations of plastic surgery. With the rise in your popularity, this issue may continue to be raised. Are you prepared mentally for this?

Y: This topic is old news. What I want to say is, when I first shot a magazine at the age of 15, someone said I had plastic surgery. In 2007, while shooting "Door" directed by Li Shaohong, I lost four teeth and my face suddenly became thinner, and people still said it was plastic surgery. Various professional analyses and speculations about what surgeries I underwent. Recently, "Palace" has been broadcast successfully, and everyone saw my previously untried hairstyle without bangs, then said I had plastic surgery again... If people have spare time, they can check out my schedule for the past two years, there really isn't time for plastic surgery. If there was, I would need recovery time first.
