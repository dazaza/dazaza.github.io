The Lonely Flying Bird

If the beautiful memories you leave for me have come to an end, how should I miss the flower that blooms deep in my heart? When you scatter in some unknown place of mine, when you will no longer disturb me and I will not disturb you, do you still show your brilliant youth colors or do you also admire yourself alone in the quiet night? The beauty we once had has lost its initial bond in the unpredictable journey of life, so the fate of being separated is already in its original form.

Still, listening to the songs you like, singing the songs you like, and exploring your mood and steps in the places you like. When I walk alone in a crowd of strangers, I realize that I am originally a lonely flying bird, falling in a place that was once lost and spinning alone, spinning out a weary appearance. Am I waiting for something? Waiting to be remembered? Am I looking for something? Looking for the back shadow I haven't forgotten? Or am I just disoriented and lost, no longer daring to believe in those so-called perfect performances?

The feelings I thought would never change in my lifetime are actually changing quietly. I also got used to living without you. I learned to live alone. Originally, all I remember and miss is the former you. It's irrelevant to me where the wings are now. Originally, those initial memories are the happiness and sweetness I can never get rid of in my lifetime.

After many years, we all changed our hearts, right? Except for the sincere feelings left in our hearts, we stand in reality and hypocrisy, either carefree or indifferent to the past moments frozen in our lives, just like the initial frozen moment has become each other's passerby. Just like we are no longer ourselves at first, only familiar and then strange to pass by. Only buried at the first encounter, only smile at the occasional meeting, only miss after leaving, only search in the pieced together memories, and only deeply understand on the bumpy road of life. So, we gradually shrink our lonely shadows in this noisy crowd, and no one cares about what stories we have on us.

If silence is the cruelest language, then I should keep this silence to myself. The burden is nothing but a few words in dozens of years, slowly crowding on these roads.
