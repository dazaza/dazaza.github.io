Wugang steel pig farming and vegetable planting, Hebei Steel will not expand production in 2012

by abcdfdhehe on 2012-03-09 11:40:09

From the General Manager of Wuhan Iron and Steel Group, Deng Qilin's revelation that Wuhan Iron and Steel plans to invest 39 billion yuan in non-steel industries this year, including the preparation of a [10,000-head pig farm], buying land for vegetable cultivation, and the establishment of an [urban modern service company], etc., Wuhan Iron and Steel has invested heavily in [pig farming and vegetable growing]. For a time, it has become a hot topic of discussion. However, people have various opinions on this practice. Some believe that if steel giants switch to pig farming, what will they do? Others think that Wuhan Iron and Steel's decision to raise pigs is out of necessity. If the profit margin in steel wasn't so low as to be almost unacceptable, why would they go through all this trouble? In such a depressed steel market, innovation is necessary.

According to available information, for two consecutive years, the annual sales profit margin of China's steel production industry has been less than 3%, far below the average profit level of 6% across all industrial sectors nationwide. Meanwhile, China’s steel industry suffers from serious overcapacity, with supply far exceeding demand.

In the bleak context of declining fortunes for steel enterprises, apart from Wuhan Iron and Steel, since as early as 2006, Shougang's non-steel industries have already achieved common development with its core steel business within just five short years. The benefits of Shougang's non-steel industries have also seen significant growth.

However, Hebei Iron and Steel recently stated on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange investor relations interactive platform that the company currently has no plans to develop non-steel industries. Hebei Iron and Steel mainly engages in steel smelting, steel rolling, and sales businesses.

Wang Yifang, Chairman of Hebei Iron and Steel Group, told the media during a break at the National People's Congress and Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference sessions that the group would not expand its steel production capacity in 2012. The next step would focus on developing the logistics sector, with a plan to achieve 30% of revenue coming from logistics by the end of 2015.

Wang Yifang also mentioned that in the future, the group [would not expand production], but rather pursue moderate diversification. There are plans for logistics sector revenue to reach 140 billion yuan by the end of 2015, accounting for 30% of total income.

Wang Yifang further stated that Hebei Iron and Steel Group plans to establish the Hebei Iron and Steel Electronic Trading Center, which will focus on trading steel spot goods. This project is currently undergoing preliminary work and is expected to be completed within two to three years.

Editorial comment: Non-steel industries can be developed, but it is best to focus on upstream and downstream steel-related industrial chains. After all, for professionals in the steel industry, if too much energy and capital is invested in more non-steel industries, there could be inherent risks associated with [diversification]. Moreover, competition in the non-steel sector is equally fierce, while the domestic steel industry still lags significantly behind international counterparts. We should seek breakthroughs within the steel industry itself, not jumping ship to other industries just because they seem profitable. We need to strengthen our basic skills in our own industry, seek breakthroughs in steel itself, think carefully, and we can find a way forward.
