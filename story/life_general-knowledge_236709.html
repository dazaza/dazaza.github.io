How to Create a Romantic Atmosphere with Eco-friendly Home Decor

by longersing on 2009-09-11 18:27:42

How to create a romantic atmosphere in an eco-friendly home? Adding some red items to your home is a simple yet effective way to create a romantic atmosphere. All these practices require us to consider environmental protection, comfort, and aesthetics from various aspects. The following are some knowledge about ecological homes:

Popular term: Ecological Home

"Ecological Home" is a newly emerging living concept in the past one or two years. It is a comprehensive environmental concept proposed in recent years in Europe. It includes multiple basic conditions: sound, light, water quality, geology, green coverage rate, disaster resistance capability and other natural environments, ventilation, air exchange, sunshine, lighting, air cleanliness, temperature, relative humidity, building materials, decoration materials, and construction techniques and other indoor space environments. According to ecological indicators, the indoor temperature should be maintained between 17°C and 27°C throughout the year, and the relative humidity should be between 40% and 70%. Therefore, environmental protection and health are the two key words of ecological homes. Ecological home is a higher standard and broader index of human living environment than green home, reflecting an attitude towards how humans and nature coexist harmoniously.

Many people equate "ecological home" with planting trees, grass, wooden decoration, artificial mountains and waters, and pastoral styles, thinking that these are synonyms for ecology. In fact, this understanding of ecology can only be said to stay on the surface, and often leads to misunderstandings, or even violates ecological principles and goes against them. For example, the wooden decoration themed on returning to nature cannot be considered as a very "ecological" home style because it directly leads to deforestation. The "home ecosystem" is based on being people-oriented, using natural conditions and artificial means to create a comfortable and healthy living environment conducive to living while controlling the use of natural resources, using more artificial, composite, and recyclable materials, benefiting the overall ecological environment, and achieving balance between taking from nature and receiving rewards from nature. Invisible pollution must not be underestimated, and protecting health is the basic problem that the "home ecosystem" needs to solve. The ten national mandatory standards for "limited amounts of harmful substances in interior decoration and renovation materials" have been implemented for nearly two years. People now try to avoid toxic and harmful substances during decoration and consciously use green building materials. However, who would know that there are still some invisible sources of pollution at home? A home is actually a relatively closed space where air flows in a very limited area. Any corner that hides dirt will eventually become a source of indoor pollution, thereby endangering the health of family members. For example, in the bathroom, cheap tiles and sanitary ware purchased to save on decoration costs are full of pores and particles on their surfaces. In a long-term poorly ventilated, warm, and humid environment, they become a breeding ground for bacteria. These molds spread with the airflow, causing the entire room to be polluted.

Related term: What is an ecological home?

Similarly, reviewing all the fabrics, sofa covers, carpets, wallpapers at home, even the green plants we highly recommend, the soil used to nurture these plants also nurtures countless harmful mites, becoming a long-term hidden killer lurking at home. Many sub-health states of modern people may be caused by living in such an environment for a long time. Ventilation plays the role of breathing in the "home ecosystem," but inappropriate layout design can make ventilation produce adverse effects on health.

There is a Chinese saying called "sick bed," which refers to the chronic damage to the body while sleeping. Ventilation problems caused by improper layout may be one of the reasons. Still taking the bathroom as an example, if the bathroom is too close to the bed, the air's humidity is too high or already polluted, then ventilation instead makes the indoor air quality worse, falling into the dilemma of "wind without purification." Resting in such polluted air has a considerable impact on health.

In addition, furniture, audio equipment, gas stoves, walls, doors, and windows in the living room can form a small climate indoors. Temperature differences existing indoors can promote indoor air circulation. This flowing air can bring fresh oxygen from outside and disperse residual harmful gases indoors. Shoe cabinets filled with dirty shoes, wardrobes with poor ventilation, etc., whether the placement of these pieces of furniture is reasonable will affect the health of people in this space.

To ensure better air quality indoors, the placement of plants, lights, furniture, etc., in the living room must be scientific, ensuring that each object has a certain amount of space, creating a healthy "home ecology" environment.
