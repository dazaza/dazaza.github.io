The captain of the criminal police concealed evidence, causing the leader of the mafia to escape punishment.

by zsh0827628 on 2012-02-24 09:14:12

Guangzhou Procuratorate news spokesman Li Xuedong reported the situation. According to the current secretary and director of the Hongshi October district, Li Zhaogang's family had given red envelopes to the elderly during New Year's and Chongyang Festival in recent years. Li Zhaogang's eldest brother, Li Zhenwei, was very disheartened when talking about his younger brother in an interview with reporters. Li Zhaogang's luxurious residence in Wuzi Village, Hongshi October district, Maogang area, Maoming city, covers 7.5 mu (about 1.24 acres) and includes artificial hills and a tennis court.

Written by Information Times reporter Wei Weiwei and communicator Suijianxuan

On May 19th, Guangzhou police announced the crackdown on the major Maoming-based organized crime group led by Li Zhaogang. The case went to trial in Guangzhou on May 16th. On the morning of yesterday, the People's Procuratorate of the city announced the investigation results of three "umbrellas of protection" for the organized crime group led by Li Zhaogang: Li Yongcai, He Mingjie, and Liang Bin.

Protection Umbrella 1: Detective Captain Li Yongcai - Hiding Evidence to "Release" the Crime Boss

On May 31st, 2001, Maoming Public Security Bureau formed a special task force to investigate the organized crime group led by Li Zhaogang. Deputy Captain Li Yongcai of the Third Brigade of the Criminal Investigation Division was appointed as the leader of the materials group of this task force.

However, during the process of preparing the indictment, Li Yongcai deliberately concealed three major criminal facts and evidence regarding the extortion of 15 million yuan from Lin, the intentional grievous injury of Luo, and the kidnapping of Lin's brothers, leading to insufficient evidence and unclear facts in the case. Eventually, the case was dismissed, allowing Li Zhaogang and others to escape legal punishment.

Since 2006, Li Yongcai has colluded with organized crime groups, investing in illegal high-interest loans and gaining over 800,000 yuan in illegal profits.

Protection Umbrella 2: Civil Court Chief He Mingjie - Forging Documents to Help Seize Land

During his term, Chief He Mingjie of the Civil Court of Maogang District People's Court in Maoming used his power as chief judge to smoothly transfer victims' legitimate properties, shares, and land rights to Li Zhaogang's group through judicial means, protecting Li Zhaogang in obtaining huge illegal high-interest loan benefits.

For example, between May and July 2007, Lai borrowed a principal of 16.5 million yuan in high-interest loans from Li Zhaogang. After repaying 6 million yuan, he could no longer pay back the loan. At this time, Li Zhaogang's group "targeted" a piece of industrial land worth 26.57 million yuan owned by Lai in Liwan District, Guangzhou, intending to forcibly occupy it.

To help Li Zhaogang, He Mingjie forged five false promissory notes totaling 23.5 million yuan and civil lawsuits, then fabricated court records and deliberation panel records, executing the seizure according to the forged documents. The land use rights were forcibly executed into a company under Li Zhaogang's name, achieving the purpose of forced occupation.

Protection Umbrella 3: Execution Bureau Secretary Liang Bin - Colluding Inside and Outside to Divide and Sell Proceeds

According to the announcement, Secretary Liang Bin of the Execution Bureau of Maogang District People's Court in Maoming colluded with He Mingjie and Li Zhaogang's group during his term, abusing judicial execution powers multiple times. Through false civil litigation and other unlawful means, they transferred victims' and third parties' properties into Li Zhaogang's group's name.

Other related information is also provided in the article regarding the broader corruption network involving officials at various levels who were either directly or indirectly involved in protecting Li Zhaogang's criminal activities, including details about another major corruption case involving former vice mayor Yang Guangliang.
