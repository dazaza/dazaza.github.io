Perhaps the most shocking and controversial book in the European and American literary circles is none other than Lawrence's Lady Chatterley's Lover. Following this, Lawrence passed away in 1930. "The Legend of Jin Ping Mei" is a book that narrates the process of how "Jin Ping Mei" came into being. This book is written elegantly and anciently, with strong readability. "Jin Ping Mei" is a legendary book passed down through generations, and "The Legend of Jin Ping Mei" is also an extraordinary article.

5. Lolita [US] Vladimir Nabokov

Reason for prohibition and destruction: Pedophilia

Nabokov's magnum opus earned him worldwide fame. This book is well-known in America, where it is read as a "yellow book." Between 1955 and 1982, this book was banned in several countries including France, the UK, Argentina, and South Africa.

6. Nana - Zola [France] Émile Zola

Reason for prohibition and destruction: Murder, incest, corruption

The work explicitly describes murder, incest, adultery, corruption, political conspiracies, wrongful imprisonment, and disregard for human life during the Second French Empire, which was unacceptable to the upper class society of the time and led to repeated bans.

7. Tropic of Cancer [US] Henry Miller

Reason for prohibition and destruction: Primitive sexual behavior

Henry Miller's masterwork is considered one of the finest confessional works since Rousseau. Miller attempts to reclaim the freedom lost in modern civilization through primitive sexual behavior. This work was banned in many countries, including the US, upon its release.

8. Paradise Lost [Japan] Haruki Murakami

"Paradise Lost" is Haruki Murakami's most successful novel about extramarital affairs, depicting a complete and dramatic romantic process from acquaintance, falling in love, reaching climax, to mutual suicide by poison.

9. The Unbearable Lightness of Being [Czech Republic] Milan Kundera

Reason for prohibition and destruction: Sexual description

This is Kundera's representative work that brought him worldwide fame. The protagonist yearns for women but fears them, making sex the entirety of his life. The book was banned due to extensive sexual content.

10. Ten Days' Tales of Women [Former Soviet Union] Liya Voynogenetskaya

Ten young women liven up the monotonous life in the maternity ward by telling 100 personal stories over ten days: first love, seduction, abandonment, rape, revenge, absurd extramarital affairs, awkward sex lives... Behind these amusing yet bitter stories lies the corruption of Soviet society, the ugliness of men's souls, the miserable conditions of women, and their fervent desire and persistence for a better life. When the book was released in the late 1980s, it immediately caused a sensation in the Western world. "Ten Days' Tales of Women" became a banned book in the former Soviet Union because the female author was a dissident, and her sharp pen frightened the authorities. The collapse of the Soviet Union and our increasingly open and relaxed social environment have allowed this book to reach Chinese readers. After experiencing tremendous changes, reading this book will evoke even more feelings and sighs.
