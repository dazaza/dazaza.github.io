"Interview" Delicious Factory: Helping Netizens Share Recipes (Cooking Skills)

by ybcz0519 on 2010-12-27 16:05:52

This article is simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media, please do not reprint! I have previously introduced Meizi Factory (Delicious Factory). The Delicious Factory is a community dedicated to helping netizens share recipes (cooking skills). For two years, the Delicious Factory has been constantly improving and progressing, moved by its persistence and dedication to what they like. Recently, I conducted an interview with norasun from Delicious Factory, allowing everyone to better understand this perhaps niche website that has poured a lot of effort and energy into its operation. If you enjoy cooking, you might just fall in love with this site!

Q1. Please give us a brief introduction about your website?

Hello everyone! I am norasun, the webmaster of Delicious Factory (http://meiwei.me). As you can tell, we're talking about a personal website here, haha. My website has been running for two years now. To this day, the core function of the site hasn't changed — it's all about helping netizens share recipes (cooking skills).

Q2. Please introduce your current team?

Actually, we don't even count as a team. This website was developed by me sitting in bed on a 29-yuan IKEA small square table. When I made the first version, the "J" and "K" keys on my computer were already broken. The other person in the "team," noramoon (my girlfriend), played more of a cheerleader role, watching American dramas while occasionally glancing at my computer screen and saying, "Can this color be adjusted to be softer?" Of course, many friends also indirectly participated in the construction of the website. Some acted as technical advisors, some specifically helped me find faults, some helped me shout out on QQ signatures, and if lucky, someone would leave a spot for me in their website's list of friendly links.

Q3. What are the main user groups that your service targets?

All cooking enthusiasts are potential users. Generally speaking, we set a target audience and cater to them. But I do the opposite; essentially, I'm looking for people like me to use this website, people who share the same aesthetics, philosophy, and pursuits. The most important point is that this website was created out of my passion and sense of achievement. I've carefully designed and spent countless nights coding, hoping that my efforts will win praise rather than pleasing others and turning myself into a servant. You see, I'm not studying how to get rich, but researching how to cultivate an ultimate hobby, purely for entertainment.

Q4. What are the main competitors among similar products? Compared to them, what are the innovative points or advantages of your product?

I once wrote a project summary for investors, and I had to answer who exactly my competitors were... Filling out this blank forced me to drag others onto the battlefield.

I don't want to avoid this question because I indeed studied dozens of recipe websites, and they are all my competitors. But it doesn't really matter. Competing with all existing products limits one's vision. True competitors are those products that haven't gone live yet. Usually, people like to compete at the level of user experience. Frankly, I don't care (even though my day job is user experience). I focus on philosophy and concepts. My priority is "silence > stance > choice > simplicity > user experience." I put user experience at the end. If the first four steps aren't in place, no amount of tricks can save the website's user experience. If the first four steps are done well, then there's no need to consider user experience at all.

My website has no innovation. I just want to study the concept of "recipes" and become an expert. In other words, I want to redefine recipes, which is quite ambitious, isn't it? Haha. Compared to other websites, what are my advantages? Okay, let me solemnly announce my advantage, which is a bit metaphysical, and definitely someone will criticize me. "Others build websites, I build people." Users who have truly spent a few days using my website can understand what I mean. Regarding more advantages, it's a secret, which helps create a sense of mystery, haha.

Q5. Currently, Delicious Factory should have been online for two years. What changes have occurred with Delicious Factory now compared to before? Are there any new functions or features?

Friends who are interested can search for the historical versions of Delicious Factory. There have been significant changes, but there are still clear milestones. Although the first version of Delicious Factory was very simple, with only two parts: recipes and community, the interface design was a bit flashy, and there were some miscellaneous functions, such as "What are you eating now?", "8 glasses of water a day", "Notebook", "Who's buying ice cream?", "What am I eating today", etc. Every time I had an idea, I would stay up all night and turn it into a product and hang it up. By the second version in 2009, after experiencing the failure of entrepreneurship, my thinking changed significantly. I removed a bunch of functions, leaving only recipes and community. The layout became even simpler, removing decorations. The second version was a milestone, setting the tone for Delicious Factory, which was minimalism and a sense of life. In the third version, I made further improvements, hiding the community. The entire website has only one channel, which is recipes. The design became even more extreme, but the spirit was inherited from the second version. You can notice that the website functions have become fewer. I spend my main time building the philosophical system, design direction, and selection mechanism of the website. I think this approach lays a solid and clear framework for the future direction of the product.

If I don't fully understand recipes, I won't bother with other thoughts to attract traffic.

Q6. Can you talk about some future development directions for Delicious Factory?

In 2011, there will be some commercial attempts, small businesses that I can achieve with my financial resources, but these businesses will seamlessly integrate perfectly into the website.

As mentioned earlier, I treat the website as a hobby, and this will not change. The most important thing for me in the future is to master the rhythm, maintaining a relatively slow pace as before, advancing in a way that accumulates slowly over time. Regardless of what users think, I want to be the user who thinks the deepest, not pursuing making users feel amazed as soon as they come to the website, but pursuing the moment when users suddenly exclaim "Wow!" How exciting!

Every world-renowned success has succeeded in a completely new way. Therefore, the core of real success studies should be about thinking about the future, and besides, I'm not studying success, but just a hobby, which makes it much simpler.
