Mayer Rebuilds Yahoo R&D: Recruits 50 PhDs This Year

by anonymous on 2013-08-07 18:50:12

Updated at noon on August 2nd, Beijing time, Yahoo! CEO Marissa Mayer is expanding the company's R&D department, hoping to invest more resources in emerging technology areas such as big data and artificial intelligence. This year, she aims to recruit 50 PhDs for this department.

Large-scale Recruitment

Mayer mentioned in a media interview last week that Yahoo Labs recruited 30 PhDs in 2013 and plans to hire another 20 by the end of the year. The department is led by Yahoo's Chief Scientist Ron Brachman, who previously worked on scientific research for U.S. government agencies.

In addition to revitalizing Yahoo through mergers and acquisitions and focusing on mobile technology, Mayer is also investing in new projects. However, prior to this, Yahoo had cut its R&D expenses for four consecutive years. Yahoo Labs was established in 2005 as an incubator specifically aimed at academic research and bold experiments. During the tenure of former Yahoo CEO Scott Thompson, the budget for this institution was reduced, causing the founder and head of the department, Prabhakar Raghavan, to leave for Google.

"The lab still exists, but it has been scaled down," said Mayer. She promised that Yahoo would restore the vitality of the department through substantial investment.

The current job postings on the Yahoo website include scientists specializing in mobile technology, senior chief scientists in personalization, and scientists skilled in pricing and market technology.

R&D Expenses

When Brachman joined Yahoo in 2005, he helped establish Yahoo Labs. He worked for three years at the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA), which invented the Internet in the 1960s. He also conducted research on artificial intelligence at AT&T's laboratories.

The focus of Yahoo Labs is on mobile, content personalization, and predictive analytics. Recent projects include a search engine that asks users for more background information and analyzes political opinions in Twitter messages.

Including R&D expenses, Yahoo spent $885.82 million on product development last year, a decrease of 27% compared to 2008. This accounted for 18% of revenue, higher than Google's 14%, but lower than Facebook's 27%.

Talent Acquisition

Yahoo has recently made numerous acquisitions, indicating Mayer's intention to attract talent widely in Silicon Valley. She has invested over $1.2 billion in acquiring startups like Tumblr, bringing in talents such as David Karp and Nick D'Aloisio. Despite being only a teenager, Nick D'Aloisio created the news reading application Summly. After Mayer acquired Xobni and Palaran, their founders Jeff Bonforte and Amit Kumar joined Yahoo, both of whom had worked at Yahoo before starting their own companies.

Mayer is also striving to restore an engineer-centric culture at Yahoo. The Yahoo Technology Committee, consisting of co-founder David Filo and Chief Architect Amotz Maimon, plays a critical role in approving new products.

"If I don't approve of an idea from a product perspective, and if David or Amotz doesn't approve from a technical standpoint, it won't be implemented," said Mayer.

On Thursday, Yahoo's stock price fell by less than 1% in regular trading on the Nasdaq, closing at $27.96. The stock has risen by 75% over the past year.
