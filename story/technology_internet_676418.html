When the object or loaded vehicle is placed on the bearing platform, under the action of gravity, the bearing platform transmits the gravitational force to the weighing sensor, causing the elastic body of the weighing sensor to deform. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance and outputs an electrical signal proportional to the weight value. After being amplified by the linear amplifier, the signal is converted into a digital signal via A/D conversion. The microprocessor (CPU) in the instrument processes the weight signal and directly displays the weight data. With a printer configured, the weighing data can be printed. If a computer is configured, the measurement data can be entered into the computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it's a scale or a weighing machine, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the weight of an object into an electrical signal, which is then sent to the conditioning circuit, A/D converter, and computational circuit (MCU), and finally displayed digitally.
