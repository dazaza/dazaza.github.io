In recent years, due to its high-quality education and low tuition fees, Quebec in Canada has become increasingly popular among parents and students. Most senior students or graduates majoring in management have chosen to study at the University of Quebec in Canada. The annual living expenses for studying in Quebec are only 70,000 yuan, and the annual tuition fee is also only 70,000 yuan. A two-year master's degree generally costs only 150,000 RMB. In addition, the Quebec provincial government has introduced a scholarship policy with a 90% discount, offering up to 100,000 RMB for a two-year master's program.

Due to the limited number of "exempted high school scholarships" available each year, based on experience analysis, the following categories of students have a higher chance of winning the award:

1. Those pursuing higher degrees, such as postgraduate studies over undergraduate studies.

2. Students who receive direct admission, specifically speaking, direct admission is preferred over conditional admission for Quebec immigration.

3. Students studying French, meaning those studying French have a higher chance of winning scholarships compared to those studying English for Quebec immigration.

4. Students who submit their applications earlier, as the "exempted high school scholarship" adopts a policy of applying and reviewing anytime, first come first served, and once the quota is exhausted, the process stops. Therefore, submitting an application earlier naturally increases the chances of winning.

Applicant requirements:

1) Love for socialist motherland and good moral character.

2) Holders of valid passports of the People’s Republic of China, excluding citizens with dual residency status.

3) Full-time students who have been accepted by relevant institutions in the province of Quebec, Canada.

4) Possess good foreign language skills for Quebec immigration.

Application materials:

1) Application Form for Foreign Student Tuition Scholarship Project in the Province of Quebec, Canada (Attachment One). (Need to attach a one-inch recent photo of the applicant as required.)

2) Application Form for Exemption from Foreign Student Tuition Scholarship (Attachment Two) (Chinese and English), in duplicate (scroll down to the second page).

3) Copies of the applicant's academic credentials and degree certificates (need to be stamped with the official seal of the issuing institution).

4) Copies of the applicant's academic transcripts (need to be stamped with the official seal of the issuing institution).

5) Copies of proof materials showing the applicant's foreign language proficiency (need to be stamped with the official seal of the certifying institution).

6) Copies and translations of unconditional (except for French language requirements) final acceptance notices from relevant institutions in the province of Quebec, Canada.
