by shenghuahu on 2012-02-10 12:21:18

Applicant for enforcement: Mr. Zhu xx.

Respondent: Traffic Bureau of Linyi City, Shandong Province.

Respondent: Mr. Bai xx.

On October 22, 2004, Bai xx was driving a tractor-transformed transporter registered under the name of the Transportation Management Office of Linyi City, Shandong Province with license plate number Shandong QC3635, returning from Qiaoqi Community in Huangtang Town, Jiangyin City to Zhouzhuang Textile City. At around 8:15 PM, while turning left at the intersection of Longxi Road and the textile city in Zhouzhuang Town, Jiangyin City, the right front corner of the transformed transporter collided with the front part of a Zongshen 125-36 type motorcycle driven by Zhu xx coming from the opposite direction. This resulted in Zhu xx falling off and getting injured, as well as damages to both vehicles in a traffic accident. On November 17 of the same year, the Jiangyin Public Security Bureau Traffic Patrol Police Team issued a traffic accident determination letter, determining that Bai xx bore the main responsibility for this accident, while Zhu xx bore the secondary responsibility.

The tractor-transformed transporter Shandong QG3635 was purchased by Bai xx on February 18, 2004. On February 20, Bai xx signed an affiliation agreement with the Zhu Bian Transportation Management Office (hereinafter referred to as Zhu Bian Transportation Office) of a county in Linyi City, Shandong Province. On February 23, the vehicle was registered under the name of Zhu Bian Transportation Office. The transportation management office is a non-legal person institution under the jurisdiction of the Transportation Bureau of Junan County.

[Judgment]

The People's Court of Jiangyin City believes that the obligor refers to the natural person, legal person, or other organization who shall bear civil liability according to law due to their own or others' infringing acts or other harmful reasons. Bai xx drove the Shandong QG3635 tractor-transformed transporter affiliated with the Zhu Bian Transportation Office, causing a traffic accident with the motorcycle driven by Zhu xx, resulting in injuries to Zhu xx. Since Bai xx is the actual owner of the Shandong QG3635 tractor-transformed transporter, he should bear civil compensation liability for Zhu xx's losses as the direct infringer. Referring to the "Minutes of the Provincial Civil Trial Work Seminar in 2001" issued by the Higher People's Court of Jiangsu Province, if a motor vehicle operating under an affiliation causes a traffic accident resulting in harm to others, the affiliate and the affiliated party should bear joint liability for compensation. Even if the affiliate and the affiliated party agree that the latter is exempt from liability for the consequences of the traffic accident, such an agreement is only binding between the two parties and cannot be used against third parties. Since there is an affiliation relationship between the Zhu Bian Transportation Office and Bai xx, after the Shandong QG3635 tractor-transformed transporter caused a traffic accident, the affiliated party should bear joint liability for compensation with Bai xx. As the Zhu Bian Transportation Office is a subordinate department of the Transportation Bureau and cannot independently bear civil liability, the civil compensation liability that the Zhu Bian Transportation Office should bear should be borne by the Transportation Bureau. Therefore, the court ruled that the respondent Bai xx should compensate the applicant Zhu Jingjun with 930,855.43 yuan, and the respondent Transportation Bureau of a certain county and the respondent Bai xx should bear joint liability for compensation.

The Transportation Bureau of a certain county appealed against the first-instance judgment made by the People's Court of Jiangyin City to the Intermediate People's Court of Wuxi City. On April 16, 2007, the Intermediate People's Court of Wuxi City made the (2007) X Min Zhong Zi No. 0123 civil judgment: dismissing the appeal and upholding the original judgment.

[Execution Situation]

Since the respondents failed to perform the obligations determined by the effective legal document within the prescribed period, Zhu Jingjun applied for compulsory execution to the People's Court of Jiangyin City on May 16, 2007. On May 24, the People's Court of Jiangyin City delivered the execution order. After being summoned multiple times, the respondent Transportation Bureau of a certain county refused to appear in court without justifiable reasons and did not show any attitude of automatic performance. Another respondent Bai xx, as a migrant worker, was judicially detained for 15 days during the execution process but still failed to pay any amount.

At 10:42 AM on September 10, 2007, when the execution personnel were checking the balance of the account No. 91606270020100016969 opened by the temporary institution Lanji Highway Construction Command (hereinafter referred to as the Command) under the Transportation Bureau of a certain county at the Urban Credit Union of the Rural Credit Cooperative League of a certain county (hereinafter referred to as the Credit Union), the Credit Union falsely claimed that the computer system had malfunctioned and refused to provide the balance situation of the account. However, the surveillance video retrieved by the execution court afterward showed that between 11:03 AM and 11:16 AM, staff members at the adjacent counter received relevant personnel from the Command and transferred 1,267,650 yuan from the account No. 91606270020100016969 of the Command to the account No. 37001828601050003308 opened by the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City at the China Construction Bank Corporation Linyi Central Branch. Until 11:30 AM, the Credit Union still insisted that the computer system had malfunctioned and had not been restored.

Further investigation revealed that during the trial of this case, our court froze 455,877.63 yuan in the account No. 20100017324 of the Transportation Bureau of a certain county at the Credit Union on March 30, 2006. However, when our court continued to freeze the deposits in this account on September 27, 2006, due to the obstruction of the judicial personnel of our court by the Transportation Bureau of Junan County, the already frozen 455,877.63 yuan deposit was lost. After March 30, 2006, the Transportation Bureau of Junan County had no fund transactions in the account No. 20100017324 but carried out large amounts of various types of fund transactions through the account No. 91606270020100016969 of the Command and other accounts opened under the name of the Trade Union of the Transportation Bureau of a certain county.

Based on the above facts, the People's Court of Jiangyin City made the (2007) Cheng Zhi Zi No. 1577 civil ruling on September 12, 2007: to transfer 1,050,000 yuan deposited by the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County into the account No. 916062700201016969 opened by the Command at the Credit Union and then transferred to the account No. 37001828601Q50003308 opened by the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City at the China Construction Bank Corporation Linyi Central Branch.

The Transportation Bureau of Linyi City raised objections on September 12, 2007, requesting to revoke the (2007) Cheng Zhi Zi No. 1577 civil ruling made by the People's Court of Jiangyin City and return the 1,050,000 yuan deducted to the objector.

The objector, the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City, argued: First, it is an independent administrative unit from the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County, and there is no management relationship in finance. It has no factual or legal connection with the case involving the Transportation Bureau of Junan County, and the deduction of the objector's funds by the execution court is illegal behavior; Second, the account No. 37001828601050003308 opened by the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City at the China Construction Bank Corporation Linyi Central Branch is a special account, and the funds deducted by the execution court are the fees paid by the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County and have entered the special account of the objector, not the property maliciously transferred by the respondent and the bank. The objector, the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City, provided relevant evidence: one is the regulations for the collection and management of highway maintenance fees; the second is the remittance vouchers from the Transportation Bureau of Junan County transferring fees to the objector from the account of the Command from April 2006 to August 2007.

The applicant for execution, Mr. Zhu xx, responded that the 1,050,000 yuan deducted by the People's Court of Jiangyin City from the account of the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City on September 12, 2007, was not the lawful property of the objector, the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City, but the property maliciously transferred by the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County. The reasons for the objection raised by the objector do not hold, and the court is requested to dismiss the objection.

The execution court believed: First, the Command was a temporary institution established for the construction of the Lanji Highway, and the funds transacted in its bank account at the Credit Union were also related to the construction of the Lanji Highway. Now that the Lanji Highway has been completed for many years, it should have been abolished long ago, and the account should have been canceled. However, from the transaction records of the account, the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County took advantage of the convenience condition that the reserved seal at the Credit Union when the Command opened the account was consistent with its own, not only using the account to pay fees but also extensively using it for other fund transactions, so the account was not a dedicated account for paying fees. Therefore, the account of the Command was actually used by the Transportation Bureau of Junan County, meaning that the funds in the account of the Command belong to the Transportation Bureau of Junan County. Second, for the needs of the execution of this case, our court issued a notice of assistance in inquiry to the Credit Union at 10:42 AM on September 10, 2007, requiring assistance in querying the balance of the account of the Command to decide whether to take freezing or deduction and other compulsory execution measures. The Credit Union should actively fulfill its obligation to assist in execution, but instead falsely claimed that the computer system had malfunctioned and helped the respondent transfer the property that our court was executing to the account of the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City. This act was obviously a malicious collusion with the respondent to transfer the property that the people's court was executing, which was seriously illegal. The funds deducted by our court from the account of the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City were not the fees normally paid by the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County, but the funds transferred by the Credit Union and the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County through illegal acts, and this illegal act could not change the nature of the funds, which still belonged to the respondent Transportation Bureau of Junan County. The objector, the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City, could not obtain the ownership of these funds based on this illegal act. To uphold the dignity of the law and effectively protect the legitimate rights and interests of the parties, the property illegally transferred during the execution should be legally recovered by our court. The argument that the funds belong to the fees paid by the Transportation Bureau of Junan County does not hold. Accordingly, the People's Court of Jiangyin City made the (2007) Cheng Min Zhi Yi Zi No. 9 civil ruling on October 29, 2007, dismissing the execution objection raised by the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City.

The Transportation Bureau of Linyi City filed a reconsideration application to the Intermediate People's Court of Wuxi City on December 26, 2007, against the (2007) Cheng Min Zhi Yi Zi No. 9 civil ruling, arguing that the account of the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City at the Linyi branch of the Construction Bank was a dedicated account for transportation fees approved and filed by the finance department. The Transportation Bureau of Junan County has always paid road maintenance fees through the account of the Command, so the funds belong to the road maintenance fees and are owned by the state.

During the review process, the Intermediate People's Court of Wuxi City set a deadline for the reconsideration applicant, the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City, to submit specific composition vouchers for the 1,267,650 yuan and the basis for the Transportation Bureau of Junan County's use of the Command account to pay road maintenance fees over the years. For this purpose, the reconsideration applicant provided the following evidence: 1. Copies of 23 deposit slips from the Command's credit union account (including two deposit slips from August 2007, namely 69,087.72 yuan on August 9 and 150,798.19 yuan on August 14, and the rest were all September deposit slips before September 2007, with a total amount of 1,267,976.03 yuan). 2. Lists of fee-paying vehicles and owners (with a total amount of 1,267,674.89 yuan). 3. Bank vouchers for the Transportation Bureau of Junan County's payment of road maintenance fees using the Command account in 2002, 2003, 2004, 2005, and 2006.

The Intermediate People's Court of Wuxi City believed: First, the Transportation Bureau of Junan County, as the respondent in this case, should voluntarily perform the legal obligations determined by the effective judgment, but it not only failed to perform but also used its funds through the account of the Command, clearly having the nature of concealing assets. When the Credit Union received the court's notice of assistance in execution, knowing that the court would control the respondent's deposit, it did not actively fulfill its duty to assist but falsely claimed that the computer system had malfunctioned and secretly implemented the act of arbitrarily transferring the respondent's funds, obstructing the court's lawful execution of official duties. This act was a serious violation of the law and should be invalid. Although the funds have been transferred to the reconsideration applicant's account, the reconsideration applicant cannot obtain the ownership of these funds based on this illegal act, so the funds still belong to the respondent, and the execution court has the right to recover them. Second, regarding whether the involved funds belong to road maintenance fees. Article 15 of the "Regulations on the Collection and Management of Highway Maintenance Fees" stipulates: "All levels of inspection institutions should deposit all highway maintenance fees with interest into the special account for highway maintenance fee income opened in the bank, and promptly and fully remit the highway maintenance fees to the special account of the provincial highway authority. The interest income from highway maintenance fees should be merged into the highway maintenance fees for accounting purposes." Therefore, the funds for remitting highway maintenance fees should come from the special remittance account. Although the transferred 1,267,650 yuan was marked as road maintenance fees on the electronic remittance supplementary receipt of the Linyi branch of the Construction Bank on September 10, 2007, this amount came from the account of the Command. The Command is not a road maintenance fee collection agency, and its account is even less likely to be a remittance special account for road maintenance fees. Even if this account was once used for remitting road maintenance fees, it cannot prove that all the deposits in this account belong to road maintenance fees. On the other hand, from the specific composition of the transferred funds, according to the bank statement of the Command account, the account balance on August 21, 2007, was 3,389.31 yuan. Thus, it can be recognized that the two entries in Evidence 1 submitted by the reconsideration applicant during the reconsideration period, dated in August 2007, were remitted earlier than August 21 and do not belong to the components of the transferred 1,267,650 yuan, and the total amount of Evidence 1 and Evidence 2 submitted by the reconsideration applicant during the reconsideration period also does not match the transferred 1,267,650 yuan. The reconsideration applicant cannot prove that the transferred 1,267,650 yuan is the highway maintenance fee that should be remitted. Therefore, the original court's ruling to dismiss the objection of the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City is appropriate and should be upheld according to law.

Accordingly, the Intermediate People's Court of Wuxi City made the (2008) X Zhi Yi Fu Zi No. 002 civil ruling on February 2, 2008: dismissing the reconsideration application of the Transportation Bureau of Linyi City and upholding the (2007) Cheng Min Zhi Yi Zi No. 9 civil ruling of the People's Court of Jiangyin City.
