Post-80s woman becomes university doctoral tutor and gives lectures on the stage (picture)

by zxytyzzfe on 2011-06-27 11:36:29

Du Lan, who was born in the 80s (on the left), looks like a college student. Photo by reporter Guoliang Dong.

When it comes to professors or doctoral supervisors, many people think of elderly scholars with white hair or middle-aged people with steady temperament. However, in several universities in Shaanxi province, some excellent individuals from the post-80s generation have already taken up associate professor positions. At Xidian University, two "post-80s" have even become doctoral supervisors.

From honor back to their posts, Xidian University allows associate professors to be evaluated as doctoral supervisors

On the afternoon of March 29, on a stone bench in the campus of Xidian University, Du Lan, who was born in 1980, was talking with two students about their study and research plans for this semester. With long hair, a white sport shirt, and jeans, her youthful face and attire made her look no different from an ordinary college student. In fact, she is already an associate professor and a doctoral supervisor.

At the end of 2010, Xidian University reformed its doctoral supervisor evaluation methods, allowing associate professors to be evaluated as doctoral supervisors for the first time. Du Lan and another male teacher named Wang Congsi from the "post-80s" generation were promoted from associate professors to doctoral supervisors. "Generally speaking, to become a doctoral supervisor, one must have a professor title," introduced Wen Aijun, deputy director of the university's Development Planning Office. "With the increase in the number of doctoral students, the existing number of doctoral supervisors seemed somewhat tight. To improve the level of doctoral student training and encourage outstanding young teachers to join the ranks of doctoral supervisors, the university reformed its doctoral supervisor evaluation methods at the end of last year, allowing associate professors to be evaluated as doctoral supervisors."

Wen Aijun said that this doctoral supervisor evaluation was conducted through strict reviews and discussions by the school's degree committee and relevant discipline experts, followed by voting. There was also a strict public announcement period. Out of 22 associate professors participating in the evaluation, four were finally rated as doctoral supervisors. Besides Du Lan and Wang Congsi, these two "post-80s", the other two associate professor doctoral supervisors are also very young, both being from the "post-70s" generation.

Both "post-80s" doctoral supervisors have won major awards

The school's data shows: Du Lan was born in March 1980 and is an associate professor in the "Signal and Information Processing" major of the School of Electronic Engineering at the university. Her main research direction is "statistical signal processing, radar signal processing, machine learning, and their applications in radar target detection and recognition."

Her doctoral dissertation won the "Top 100 Excellent Doctoral Dissertations Nationwide" award in 2009. It is reported that this award was selected from more than ten thousand doctoral dissertations across over 80 primary disciplines nationwide, making it very difficult to receive such an honor. At the same time, she has also led and participated in more than ten natural science funds, key laboratory funds, and other vertical scientific research projects as a key researcher.

She is also a "returnee", having worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Duke University in the United States for two years.

Wang Congsi was born in October 1980 and is an associate professor in the mechatronics engineering discipline at the university, currently being the youngest doctoral supervisor at the university. The project he participated in as a key member won the "Second Prize of National Science and Technology Progress Award", and he leads the 973 National Key Basic Research Project.

Wen Aijun introduced that the "Signal and Information Processing" major in the School of Electronic Engineering where Du Lan is located is an advantageous major of the university and also a national key discipline. The "Mechatronics Engineering Discipline" where Wang Congsi is located belongs to the provincial-level key discipline. It is not easy to be evaluated as a doctoral supervisor in these two key disciplines. "These two 'post-80s' are both very outstanding, with prominent scientific research and teaching abilities."

In several other universities in Shaanxi, there are also "post-80s" associate professors

Then, are there any "post-80s" doctoral supervisors in other universities in Shaanxi?

A relevant person in charge of the Degree Office of the Education Department stated that the review rights of doctoral supervisors are in the universities with doctoral degree granting authority. Whether there are any "post-80s" doctoral supervisors, there is no statistical data yet. After consulting with well-known universities in our province such as Xi'an Jiaotong University, Shaanxi Normal University, Northwestern Polytechnical University, and Chang'an University, they all indicated that there are no "post-80s" doctoral supervisors or professors, but "post-80s" associate professors are not uncommon in universities.

Among them, Shaanxi Normal University has seven "post-80s" associate professors, distributed across mathematics, literature, physics, tourism and environment, and other fields and majors. The youngest was born in June 1981 and is an associate professor in the literature major.

Xi'an Jiaotong University has eight "post-80s" associate professors, with the youngest born in December 1981 and being an associate professor in the biomedical engineering major.

Chang'an University also has seven "post-80s" associate professors, with the youngest also born in 1981.

"These 'post-80s' associate professors undertake both scientific research tasks and teaching tasks, some even undertake national-level topic research. Compared to older professors, these young 'post-80s' associate professors work with more passion and have more distinct personalities!" A staff member of the Personnel Department at Shaanxi Normal University commented.

Growth experience of female doctoral supervisor Du Lan from the 80s

Born in March 1980, her father is a teacher at Northwest A&F University, and her mother works in finance at the same university.

She attended an experimental class during elementary school and went to junior high after fifth grade.

She entered the electronic engineering major at Xidian University with the second-highest score at the Northwest A&F University Affiliated High School in the college entrance examination.

After graduating from her undergraduate studies, she was directly admitted into the master-doctoral program at the same university, under the guidance of Academician Bao Zheng of the Chinese Academy of Sciences.

In 2002, when she was in her second year of graduate studies, she began working at the National Defense Science and Technology Key Laboratory for Radar Signal Processing at the university.

In June 2007, she graduated with her doctorate and was promoted to associate professor in July of the same year.

In December 2010, she was promoted from associate professor to doctoral supervisor.

Currently, Du Lan guides five master's students at the university, while also assisting other supervisors in guiding four doctoral students. Starting from the next semester this year, she will guide doctoral students under her own supervision.

The entry threshold has increased, and the promotion process for "post-80s" has been shortened by two to three years.

Why can "post-80s" quickly be promoted to associate professors? Regarding this, relevant responsible teachers from multiple universities expressed that apart from the personal efforts of young teachers, the recent advocacy of cultivating younger talents in our country has provided the background for the emergence of "post-80s" associate professors and doctoral supervisors. At the same time, it also has a certain relationship with the increase in the entry threshold for university teachers.

It is understood that many years ago, most teachers entering universities had graduate degrees. After entering, they would serve as assistant lecturers for two years before being eligible for promotion to lecturer. To be promoted from lecturer to associate professor, teachers with a master's degree needed to serve as lecturers for more than five years. Similarly, to be promoted from associate professor to professor, they needed to serve as associate professors for more than five years. Altogether, it would take 12 years. Only after being promoted to professor would one be eligible to be evaluated as a doctoral supervisor.

However, nowadays, most teachers entering universities have doctoral degrees. Generally, they can become lecturers upon entering, and after serving as lecturers for two years, they become eligible to be evaluated as associate professors. After serving as associate professors for more than five years, they become eligible to be evaluated as professors. Altogether, it only takes seven years. Even if adding the time spent pursuing a doctorate, their career advancement path has been shortened by two to three years compared to previous young teachers.

Wen Aijun believes that the promotion of "post-80s" to associate professors and doctoral supervisors indicates that universities place great emphasis on the cultivation of young teachers. A staff member of the Personnel Department at Shaanxi Normal University stated that this shows that some excellent "post-80s" are growing rapidly. Reporter Hongman Lu
