Disease Strikes Again, She Resolutely Entrusts Her Whole Life

Farewell to post comments, I want to comment

Liu Fen knew that the power of spirit at this moment might be more effective than medicine. Under her repeated persuasion, Yang Hongli submitted a marriage application to the organization.

Written/Photographed by reporter Yang Ming, Li Ying, and correspondents Li Huamin, Tian Liyang, Pu Zhao

The time went back to that heart-breaking moment; on April 9th at 1 o'clock in the morning, in Room 1 of Department One of the surgical ward building at the 421 Hospital of the Guangzhou Military Region, Deputy Platoon Leader Yang Hongli of a certain Engineering Battalion Repair Company gently held Liu Fen's hand, nestled in his beloved's arms, and peacefully passed away at the young age of 29.

"‘I will follow you for the rest of my life!’ A good military wife’s resolute confession, after three years of hardship and trials, has become a more powerful sentiment than any maxim." At Huizhou Business School, a Chinese literature teacher who is also a military wife told the touching story of Liu Fen and Yang Hongli, causing a strong reaction among students. The major vocational colleges and universities in Huizhou City were all stirred! Many classmates saved their meal money to send them nutritional supplements; a group of female university students wrote letters to Liu Fen and Yang Hongli together, vowing "to carry love forward to the end"…

Never Leaving or Abandoning, Even Death Slows Its Steps

After the first surgery, two-thirds of Yang Hongli's stomach was removed, followed by six consecutive chemotherapies. His originally thick black hair fell out completely, and his body weight dropped from over 140 pounds to just over 80 pounds.

After returning to the company, Yang Hongli persisted in physical training and drills, pouring all his passion into his cherished position. In March 2010, Yang Hongli was recommended to study at the PLA University of Science and Technology. He was promoted from platoon leader to deputy company commander and was a key candidate for the position of company commander.

"The first time we got through it, so the second and third times must be possible too!" The robust Liu Fen accompanied Hongli closely, racing against time with every second.

Wedding photos of Liu Fen and Yang Hongli.

On February 14, 2008, at the West Lake scenic area in Huizhou City, Platoon Leader Yang Hongli of a certain battalion repair company and Hunan girl Liu Fen, who worked at an electronics factory, fell in love. In September 2008, due to his outstanding performance, Yang Hongli was selected to study at the Xuzhou Engineering Corps Command Institute. What Liu Fen didn't expect was that their next meeting would be in the hospital, where her vibrant lover lay weakly in bed.

On December 23, 2010, Liu Fen rushed back overnight to Hengyang to retrieve her household registration book. “A woman getting married is like reincarnating again; you should at least think about your own future,” her mother cried, trying to dissuade her.

One night, Hongli wanted to use the restroom and opened the door. The scene before him stunned him: Liu Fen was holding a plastic basin, carefully analyzing its contents. “The doctor said this could help understand the condition and recovery status,” Liu Fen explained.

In the 800 plus days and nights that Yang Hongli fought against cancer, Liu Fen never left his side until his final moments. During Yang Hongli's most difficult times, Liu Fen remained steadfast by his side, writing a moving ode to great love with her resilience and perseverance.

Guangzhou, was once the blessed land where the exemplary military wife Han Su Yun regained her health. Now, the story of "post-80s" exemplary military wife Liu Fen and her husband Yang Hongli is being passed down orally by people, once again warming this city filled with love.

Recently, among the 24 candidates for the Moral Role Model of Guangdong Province, a petite "post-80s" Hunan girl named Liu Fen has caught people's attention.

Acquaintance for Half a Year, Iron-Blooded Man Diagnosed with Gastric Cancer

Miss Zhang from Guangzhou, while accompanying her father at the hospital, visited Yang Hongli and Liu Fen daily; knowing that Liu Fen hadn't bought a new piece of clothing in two years, caregiver Grandma Li insisted on giving her a dress newly bought for her daughter…

However, darkness struck again. In May, Hongli began experiencing severe vomiting. Upon examination, the cancer had recurred. Liu Fen resigned from her job again and hurried to Nanjing.

After being transferred back to the 421 Hospital of the Guangzhou Military Region, the doctors informed Liu Fen that cancer cells were found on the inner walls of Hongli's abdominal cavity and intestinal mucosa, and the cancer cells were spreading.

Carrying Love Forward to the End

A month later, the hospital decided to perform a gastrectomy on Yang Hongli. Liu Fen resolutely quit her well-paying job in Huizhou to accompany him in the hospital. Hongli became soft-hearted, “You are still so young, I can't drag you down!” Before he finished speaking, tears had already welled up. “I am willing. You are a soldier, a man who stands tall under heaven and earth! Let us create a miracle together.”

Admitted 17 times, hospitalized for 374 days, underwent 3 surgeries, received 17 chemotherapies... These figures, the attending physician Zhuang Yongjing could recount offhand. Each time Yang Hongli's condition was checked, the medical staff hoped for good news as if they were waiting to win the lottery.

During his studies in Xuzhou, Yang Hongli sought medical attention due to gastrointestinal discomfort. The result was shocking: advanced gastric cancer! He silently chose to be strong, shedding tears inwardly. Unresponsive to text messages and refusing phone calls... Yang Hongli was transferred from Xuzhou to the 421 Hospital in Guangzhou for treatment. When she came to see him, he ignored her. She cried and prepared to leave. He agreed but added one more sentence: “Let’s not meet anymore.”

From June to December last year, Yang Hongli underwent a second surgery, receiving six chemotherapies and four radiotherapies. He could only sustain life by daily intravenous nutrition infusions. Experts predicted that Hongli could only endure for another two months at most.

In March 2009, Liu Fen saw an advertisement in the newspaper stating that there was a folk remedy for stomach ailments in her hometown of Hengyang. She traveled overnight, standing the entire night on the train, then took a bus and a motorcycle taxi, knocking on the old traditional Chinese medicine doctor's door late at night. The next morning, Liu Fen stood by Hongli's side with a steaming bowl of Chinese medicine. This sturdy Shandong man drank the medicine in large gulps while quietly wiping away the tears rolling down his face with his sleeve.

Hongli's condition often relapsed, and he slept restlessly at night. Liu Fen found a bandage, tying one end to her hand and the other to Hongli's hand, so any movement could immediately be detected.

Liu Fen stayed by his side without leaving. She picked up cookbooks to learn culinary skills, preparing various foods for him. She bought books to study acupoints and massage techniques, insisting on massaging him daily to relieve pain. Since Hongli had difficulty digesting food, she warmed her hands and gently massaged his stomach in the direction of his intestines. For five meals a day, she massaged for half an hour each time without fail. Due to prolonged injection treatments, most of Hongli's arm veins had died. Liu Fen heard that applying potato and onion slices could protect the veins. Thus, she persisted in cutting onions into slices every morning and placing them on Hongli's arms. Almost every time, she was teary-eyed from the fumes, sometimes even cutting her fingers, but she never stopped.

On February 1st, a special wedding was held in the canteen of a certain engineering battalion repair company. Dressed in military uniform, Yang Hongli tightly held the bride Liu Fen's hand as they walked to the center of the "stage," with every soldier present having tears in their eyes!

In the span of five and a half months, Liu Fen consistently helped Yang Hongli soak his feet, wash his body, and gave him massages... She slept on the chair in the ward, ate the leftovers from Yang Hongli's meals, and didn't buy herself a single new piece of clothing. Under Liu Fen's meticulous care and encouragement, Yang Hongli, who experts predicted would only live for a year at most, miraculously recovered and was discharged from the hospital in May 2009.

Kneeling before her mother with a "thud," Liu Fen said, “Mom! Hongli is a good person, entrusting myself to him, even if it's just for a day, it's worth it!” On January 31, 2011, Liu Fen and Yang Hongli finally received their bright red marriage certificate.

Yang Hongli and Liu Fen's story moved everyone around them. Political Commissar Yue Shixin of a certain Group Army shed tears saying: Liu Fen's loyalty to love, her affection for soldiers, represents trust and reward for this unique group of soldiers, becoming a powerful motivation for officers and soldiers to love the army and excel in martial arts, defending the homeland.

Ultimately, the beautiful hope was shattered by the cruel reality. Due to the failure of the last chemotherapy, Yang Hongli's organs failed, and he permanently left his beloved wife who had been by his side day and night. By the bedside, Liu Fen gave Yang Hongli one last body wash, overwhelmed with grief, letting the tears fall as she repeatedly recalled Yang Hongli's promises: “You promised to buy me a new set of clothes, how could you deceive me! Come back quickly!”
