**Translation:**

1. **Triple (Leopard)** > **Straight Flush** > **Flush** > **Straight** > **Pair** > **High Card**; **Special (235 of different suits)** > **Leopard**. Special KQJ... > 432 > A23 (in some places A23 > AKQ). Note: KA2 is not a straight.

4. When a Triple (Leopard) exists, "235 of different suits" > "Leopard". That is, if both a Triple and 235 of different suits exist, 235 of different suits wins. Note: Among all hands, 235 of different suits is the strongest when a Leopard exists (in some places, special only beats a Leopard with three A's).

5. Cards are revealed counterclockwise. In case of identical hands, the first player to reveal loses.

---

**Detailed Definitions:**

- **Triple (Leopard):** Three cards of the same rank, e.g., AAA, 222.

- **Straight Flush:** A straight with all cards of the same suit, e.g., Spades 456, Hearts 789.

- **Flush:** All cards of the same suit but not in sequence, e.g., Spades 368, Diamonds 945.

- **Straight:** Three cards in sequence but of different suits, e.g., Spades 5, Hearts 6, Diamonds 7.

- **Pair:** Two cards of the same rank, e.g., 223, 334.

- **High Card:** Three cards that do not form any combination.

- **Special:** 235 of different suits.

---

**Rules Summary:**

1. The ranking order is as follows: Triple > Straight Flush > Flush > Straight > Pair > High Card. However, the "Special" hand (235 of different suits) is stronger than Triple but weaker than High Card.

2. In comparing ranks, A is the highest, and 2 is the lowest.

3. For hands of the same type, compare the values. If the values are equal, the player who initiated the reveal loses.

4. When a Triple exists, the "Special" hand (235 of different suits) beats the Triple. Note that among all hands, the "Special" hand is the strongest when a Triple exists (in some regions, it only beats a Triple made of three A's).

5. Cards are revealed counterclockwise, and in case of identical hands, the first player to reveal loses.
