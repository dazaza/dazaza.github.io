Early last year, Chengdu resident Liu Jun (a pseudonym) suddenly received a text message from a strange number. A man claimed he could copy SIM cards and eavesdrop on others' conversations. This man said the copied card was ready but due to technical reasons, it had to be paired with a high-end mobile phone worth nearly ten thousand yuan, so Liu Jun needed to send more money. Liu Jun did not suspect anything and sent the money again.

When the victim fell for the scam, every payment would enter an account provided by a specialized organization, which would then transfer the funds into Hu's account. For every transaction processed by this organization, they would charge Hu a 3% handling fee.

But fearing that Liu Jun might be a police officer, the scammer demanded an additional deposit of over ten thousand yuan. The scammer charged under various pretexts, and after Liu Jun had transferred over forty thousand yuan, the man's phone became unreachable. It was then that he realized he had been scammed and reported it to the police.

Not long after, Liu Jun received a call identical to a relative's phone number. The caller was precisely the man who could "copy SIM cards."
