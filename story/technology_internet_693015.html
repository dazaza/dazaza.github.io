Disfigured Teen Enters Beijing Tonight for a "Rebirth"

A Beijing plastic surgery hospital will cover the costs but warns that full restoration is not possible.

Report by journalist Dang Shuai: A burst of "rage" destroyed the face of a 17-year-old girl in her prime [http://news.sina.com.cn/s/2012-02-25/023723990933.shtml]. Tonight, the much-discussed Anhui teenager Zhou Yan will arrive in Beijing to receive "rebirth" treatment.

On September 17, 2011, due to unsuccessful romantic advances, the 17-year-old middle school student Tao Rukun forcibly broke into a residence, splashed gasoline on 17-year-old Zhou Yan, and lit a lighter, severely burning her and potentially causing lifelong disability. After the incident was exposed online, the condition of Zhou Yan became the most discussed topic among citizens.

Recently, I learned from Jiangsu TV (Weibo)[http://weibo.com/jsbcjstv?zw=news] that Zhou Yan has accepted medical assistance from the "Dream Come True" program team.

According to the program's producer, because of the relatively high level of medical facilities and expertise in Beijing, they originally planned to travel to Beijing on March 4th. However, Zhou Yan's condition began to deteriorate, making her unsuitable for long-distance travel, so the trip was postponed.

At around 7:30 PM last night, Zhou Yan's parents finally decided to accompany her northward. Given her condition, it was deemed unsuitable for her to fly, so they decided to take a three-hour high-speed train ride to Beijing. Zhou Yan will have an entire high-speed train carriage as her "exclusive" use, with all travel expenses covered by the program team.

Mr. Liu from Beijing Yimer Plastic Surgery Hospital, who will be treating Zhou Yan, stated that an expert team has been formed in Beijing to prepare for Zhou Yan's first consultation. Based on the current situation, complete recovery is unlikely. Burn injuries require long-term treatment, possibly taking two years or even a lifetime.

The hospital will bear all treatment costs and will arrange a double room for her, solving accommodation issues for Zhou Yan's parents.
