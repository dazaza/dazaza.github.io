Industrial Pipeline Endoscopy Non-destructive Testing, Conventional Inspection Methods for Pressure Pipelines

by avanline on 2011-08-09 16:32:52

Industrial borescopes, as a major category of non-destructive testing tools, play an important role in pipeline maintenance. With the need for more precise quantitative judgment on defect size and characteristics within pipelines, the requirements for non-destructive testing are also increasing. Non-destructive testing methods such as industrial borescopes are increasingly valued, with the aim of ensuring that equipment is free from serious defects before use and meets quality requirements. Regular inspections during use are aimed at discovering any new defects that may have occurred during use, promptly reminding users to repair the equipment. Below, we will briefly introduce the conventional inspection methods for pressure pipelines.

1) Pipe scale removal methods

The inner surface of the pipe comes into contact with various different process media, making it very easy to adhere, accumulate, and deposit various materials, which can even cause blockages in the pipe. Currently, common scale removal methods include mechanical cleaning, chemical cleaning, and high-pressure water flushing.

(1) Mechanical cleaning method

The mechanical cleaning method (including manual cleaning using simple tools) can remove all kinds of dirt, especially chemically insoluble deposits such as sand, coke, and certain silicates, without being corrosive to the metal material of the pipe. However, its efficiency is much lower than that of chemical cleaning.

(2) Chemical cleaning method

The chemical cleaning method is a technique that uses the interaction between a chemical solution and the dirt on the inner wall of the pipe to remove the dirt.

Chemical cleaning methods are usually divided into circulation and immersion types, with the circulation method being the most commonly used. To enhance the cleaning effect, cleaning can be alternated in two directions. During the final rinse with clear water, the same alternating operation can be performed from both directions. Additionally, to prevent corrosion during the cleaning process, if a chemical cleaning agent that is corrosive to the pipe is used, the pipe should be repeatedly rinsed with clear water until the discharged water becomes neutral. This method has a high efficiency, particularly suitable for the cleaning of pipeline systems. Because it can be operated under sealed system conditions, it is widely applied. However, chemical cleaning requires strong professional technical skills; any misstep could not only fail to achieve the desired results but also potentially damage the pipes or even cause accidents.

(3) High-pressure water jet cleaning

This method uses the impact force of high-pressure water flow to clean deposits on the inner walls of the pipe and the outer space of the tube bundle. Adding fine quartz sand to the water flow can further increase the scouring power of the water. If the dirt inside the pipe softens when exposed to oil, the pipe can first be soaked in oil, and then flushed with high-pressure water. The high-pressure water jet flushing method is highly efficient and does not pollute the environment, so it is currently widely used just like chemical cleaning.

2) Methods for repairing thinning pipe walls

After a period of operation, the most common defect in pipes is local wall thinning. Local wall thinning caused by corrosion pits and medium erosion can be handled by welding repairs or partial pipe replacement depending on the severity. For pipes with overall wall thinning, if the thinning exceeds the designed corrosion allowance, there will be safety issues due to insufficient strength. When the actual measured wall thickness is generally less than the minimum allowable wall thickness of the pipe, the pipe should be operated at reduced pressure or scrapped.

3) Methods for repairing pipe cracks

Cracks formed on the pipe walls are roughly divided into two categories: surface cracks and through cracks, each requiring different repair methods. Industrial video borescopes play a good auxiliary role in internal pipe inspections.

(1) Surface cracks, shallow cracks on the surface that have not penetrated the pipe wall are called surface cracks, generally caused by various stresses, fatigue, and material defects (including welding). Before welding repairs, it should be carefully confirmed whether the crack has been completely removed. The welding rod used for repair should be compatible with the base material, and small holes should be drilled at both ends of the crack to prevent its expansion.

(2) Through cracks, when repairing through cracks by welding, attention should be paid to whether stop-crack holes have been drilled at both ends, and the diameter of the holes should be slightly larger than the width of the crack. When welding long cracks, attention should be paid to compensating for contraction and reducing internal stress. It is recommended to weld sectionally from both ends of the crack towards the middle and use multi-layer welding.

4) Eliminating pipe leaks

When a pipe leaks, it must not be repaired while in operation. In general, the operation should be immediately stopped, and repairs should be carried out according to standard methods. Common methods for detecting pipe leaks include: sniffing, listening, visual inspection; using foaming agents (such as soap solution); using test paper or reagents; using gas detectors; using ultrasonic leak detectors; using infrared temperature testers. Before inspection, inspectors should have a comprehensive understanding of the characteristics of the pipe's medium (such as corrosiveness, toxicity, temperature, pressure), structural features (materials, wall thickness, fittings, supports, etc.), and design and installation conditions to decide on feasible detection methods. If the pipe wall leakage is caused by corrosion perforation, and after measurement, the wall thickness of nearby other parts has no significant thinning, the welding repair method can be used.

5) Repairing other pipe defects

① Welds with incomplete fusion, incomplete penetration, over-limit (uneven surface, excessive dimensions, etc.), porosity, slag inclusion, etc., can be ground, removed, and repaired by welding. Volume-type defects such as porosity, if they do not develop after long-term use, may not require repair.

② Local burrs and scratches on bolts and nuts of high-pressure pipes can be polished, but when the accumulated scratches exceed one thread circle, they should be replaced according to regulations.

③ Scratches on sealing surfaces such as pipe flanges and valves can be eliminated by machining with a cutting tool or grinding.
