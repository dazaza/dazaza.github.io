【A Glass of Water to Identify the Quality of Cosmetics】There are many cosmetics on the market, and have MM's eyes been dazzled by them? Now, let me teach you a trick to identify their quality! All it takes is a glass of water.

Take an appropriate amount of foundation or emulsion that you usually use and put it in the water, then observe its reaction. Generally, three situations may occur: sticking to the side of the cup, floating on the water surface, sinking to the bottom of the cup. These three situations indicate their components.

1: Sticking to the side of the cup indicates animal oil, which is extracted from animals. Think about which cosmetic factory has an animal slaughterhouse nearby? None. The slaughterhouse piles up bones and furs to one side. When there is enough, they send them to the cosmetic factory. Who knows how long they've been piled up? If it's summer, will those things not spoil and rot? We would vomit and diarrhea if we eat spoiled food, and the skin is the same. Using cosmetics made from spoiled substances can cause allergies. Manufacturers know this, so they add soothing agents during the manufacturing process. Soothing agents are anesthetics for the immune nerves. At first, using them feels good, but over time, they don't work well. Beauticians all know that after using cosmetics of the same brand for 2-3 years, you need to change brands. What you're changing is the soothing agent.

2: Floating on the water surface indicates mineral oil. Mineral oil is extracted from petroleum mined underground. The first layer is used as gasoline, the second layer is light oil, and the third layer is asphalt, used for paving roads. This type of cosmetics is made from the second layer of light oil. Light oil has excellent heat preservation effects, but it is not breathable, affecting the breathability of the skin, thus accelerating skin aging.

3: Sinking to the bottom of the cup indicates heavy metals such as lead. There have been reports in newspapers that children of mothers who often use whitening products are prone to cerebral palsy.

If the product you use doesn't stick to the side of the cup, doesn't float, and doesn't sink to the bottom, then congratulations! You've chosen a good product, and you can use it with peace of mind. Everyone can try it at home. Girls, don't blindly follow trends and use the wrong products. That would be counterproductive. After all, health is more important than beauty!!
