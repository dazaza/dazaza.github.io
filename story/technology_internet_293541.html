An old man who took in 16 orphans owes tens of thousands of debts

by gjiwefdn on 2009-12-05 02:53:40

For more than a decade, Pan Xianfeng, a retired cadre from Song County, successively took in 16 orphaned elderly people, spending over 300,000 yuan and still owing several ten thousand yuan in debt. Pan Xianfeng said, "This is not really about love; it's just that I feel heartache and sympathy for them. People must have a way to live, and I can't bear leaving them without help."

● Taking in 16 orphaned elderly people

Pan Xianfeng, aged 49, lives in the urban area of Song County.

In 1984, while serving as the deputy secretary of Muzhi Street Township, he met Fu Guiying, a mute woman over 50 years old, in Beigou Village of the township. In the following days, Pan Xianfeng would bring her grain and oil every one or two months.

There were many people like Fu Guiying whom Pan Xianfeng helped. In the winter of 1996, a public vehicle fell into the water, and after pushing the car for too long, his left leg swelled up significantly. Later, during surgery, three veins were removed, and now his left leg is smaller than the right one and often peels. Around 1997, due to his leg condition, Pan Xianfeng was transferred away from township work, but he could not forget these people. "Just giving food and clothing isn't a solution, and the civil affairs department also has difficulties. I thought of renting a place, bringing them together to live, so it would be easier to take care of them." Thus, Pan Xianfeng rented an old building materials factory house and vacant land in Zifang Township for 5000 yuan per year, bringing the orphans and elderly he had helped to live there.

Pan Xianfeng has taken in a total of 16 people, some of whom have become independent and left, while others have gone on to university and entered the workforce. Now, four girls and three elderly people still live together. Over the past decade, Pan Xianfeng has spent over 300,000 yuan, accumulating more than 60,000 yuan in debt.

● "How could I leave them uncared for?"

At Pan Xianfeng's residence, the reporter saw tables used for twenty years, an 18-inch television, and sofas covered in tattered fabric.

Last year, when the children needed to register their household registration for schooling, some people criticized him for caring about too many trivial matters. "I don't care about these criticisms. Fortunately, after running around so many times, the household registration issues for the elderly and children were finally resolved," said Pan Xianfeng.

Regarding Pan Xianfeng's "stubbornness," family disputes have arisen over the years. "The children have grown up and understand things now. Every time I visit them, they always gather around me singing and dancing, making me incredibly happy. How could I possibly leave them uncared for?" Pan Xianfeng said with a full heart of happiness. "Keep working hard and saving money, and one day I will pay off all the debts," Pan Xianfeng confidently stated.

● Earning money by driving tricycles and cleaning septic tanks

On the morning of February 11, 1995, at the De Ting Township automobile station in Song County, then Party Secretary Pan Xianfeng found a baby girl.

However, after medical examination, the abandoned infant was diagnosed with congenital heart disease. For over a decade, to treat her, Pan Xianfeng took her to Luoyang and Xi'an, seeking medical advice everywhere, worrying endlessly.

In March 2006, Pan Xianfeng brought her to the First Affiliated Hospital of Zhengzhou University to prepare for surgery, which required more than 100,000 yuan in surgical fees. Despite his family's opposition, Pan Xianfeng mortgaged his own property certificate, borrowed more than 50,000 yuan from the credit union, and raised another 80,000 yuan from friends and colleagues.

Pan Xianfeng said, "To save more money, I drove a tricycle at night to ferry passengers. Because I couldn't face people directly, I wore a helmet, covering myself completely. When collecting money, I opened a small crack in the vehicle. I've cleaned septic tanks, done landscaping trimming jobs, and even rented out idle houses to make extra money." "Now, my salary is more than 2000 yuan, and my salary card is usually kept by Wang Xin You (one of the people he took in). If they need money, they can go to the credit union and withdraw it directly," said Pan Xianfeng. Currently, Pan Xianfeng still owes more than 60,000 yuan in debt.
