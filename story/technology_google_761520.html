15 Technologies That Could Change The World: Google Glass And Self-Driving Cars Make The Cut

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 17:01:13

According to foreign media reports on April 29 Beijing time, technology has changed our way of life and brought us into a digital era. The emergence of smartphones, driverless cars, Google Glass, robots, and social media has brought us closer to the Internet and also made us inseparable from this virtual world. Recently, the American tech blog website BusinessInsider summarized 15 new technologies that have changed the world. They are as follows:1) Driverless cars: changing our travel methodsCurrently, people have already seen test versions of Google's driverless cars on the roads of California in the United States, but they will not be available on the market until 2018.Google expects that driverless cars can reduce traffic accidents by half every year, which means saving 1.2 million lives annually.It remains to be observed whether driverless cars will be accepted by society, but they will definitely be an option. In addition to Google, other large car manufacturers such as Toyota and Audi are also developing their own driverless cars.2) Google Glass: bringing us into the cyborg eraGoogle Glass is one of the hottest products currently, because the applications of this device are very extensive.Google Glass brings us closer to what we see and perceive every day, enhancing our interaction with the surrounding things.We will be more closely connected to the Internet, and through this device, we can see useful information such as short messages, news, microblogs, and route maps.With Google Glass, we can record everything happening around us and store it in the cloud, allowing us to forever document the details of our lives.3) Wearable health trackers: keeping your health close at handWith applications and services like NikeFuelBand, Fitbit, and ZEO, the data generated in our lives is increasing. These devices can help us track health information, such as calories burned, distance walked, and even the quality of our sleep.Wearable health trackers will help us achieve the goal of quantifying ourselves, allowing users to understand their physiological functions through their own data.4) The Internet: creating a sharing economyCurrently, there are a large number of startups in the sharing economy. In the sharing economy, people are renting out their homes, cars, parking spaces, and even camping tents.These startups include Airbnb, which provides apartment rental services, RenttheRunway for renting fashionable clothes and accessories, and Sidecar, a private car ride-sharing service. None of these would be possible without the Internet, which plays the role of a matchmaker in the sharing economy.5) Robots: replacing laborIndustrial robots that can perform various simple tasks and safely collaborate with humans are beginning to emerge in the United States.Rethink Robotics' first robot, Baxter, can transport raw materials, unload or load goods on conveyor belts, inspect and test components, operate machines, and pack and unpack.Rethink Robotics aims to make the U.S. more competitive by creating low-cost production technologies and processes. If all goes well, Baxter will collaborate with workers to help the U.S. compete with other low-cost labor markets.Baxter's ultimate goal is to perform more complex tasks. Currently, the company is developing software to allow Baxter to communicate with conveyor belts and other machines.6) Smartphones: connecting you and meSmartphones connect us with the entire world and are a very useful tool.Smartphones allow us to check emails, read news, and watch stocks. As long as the phone has a signal, we won't get lost with the help of map applications. Dating apps like Tinder and Grindr can help us meet new friends nearby.People are now inseparable from smartphones. Let's look at some data: within 15 minutes of waking up each morning, 79% of smartphone users check their phones, 48% use their phones at the gym, and 50% use Facebook at the cinema.7) Social media: changing how we interact with othersFacebook and Twitter provide platforms for us to communicate and interact with friends, family, and even strangers. At the same time, social media ignites social actions and provides a gathering platform for people with common interests and goals.8) 3D printing: bringing creative replication3D printing is creating more and more everyday objects and bringing significant breakthroughs in the medical field. This month, humans created the smallest human liver through 3D printing for the first time.The principle of 3D printers is to construct three-dimensional objects by printing layer by layer. Companies emerging in this field include MakerBot, Printrbot, and Shapeways.9) Nanofiltration technology: enabling people to drink clean waterUnhygienic water poses a significant threat to human health. According to data provided by the World Health Organization, about 3.4 million people die each year from water-related diseases.Lifesaver was born accordingly. Lifesaver is a water cup integrated with nanotechnology that can filter water and remove details, viruses, parasites, fungi, and other microorganisms from the water.10) Gesture control devices: perceptual operationComputer mice are outdated. New technologies keep emerging, allowing us to interact with computers just through gestures. With LeapMotion, users can replace the mouse with natural hand gestures, letting you express your thoughts better on the screen.Another technology called MYO is invented by ThalmicLabs. MYO is essentially a sleeve that allows users to control other devices through their body movements. Unlike LeapMotion, MYO does not require a camera.11) Subway station touchscreens: facilitating passenger travelNew York City plans to install at least 77 new touchscreen displays in subway stations across five districts. The new displays will provide subway route maps, subway service updates, delay information, and of course advertisements. Each 47-inch display will be equipped with sensors, cameras, headphones, and Wi-Fi to facilitate communication between subway passengers and MAT. In the future, ControlGroup also plans to provide streaming media and even online gaming services in subway stations through this method.12) Payment apps: changing our shopping experienceApplications are changing our shopping experience. When we enter a retail store, we can complete payments without speaking to anyone.Apple retail stores are often crowded, making it difficult to find staff to pay. With the EasyPay app, we can purchase chargers, software, cases, etc., without interacting with store staff.With the rise of tablets, the shopping experience has been further enhanced. More and more physical stores are using tablets as cash registers to complete checkout. For example, Square's card reader and Register app allow shop owners to accept credit and debit card payments using iPhones, iPads, or Android devices.13) Mobile apps: bringing convenience but also making us lazySmartphone apps are influencing our clothing, food, housing, and transportation. Tech companies are tirelessly developing new apps to make our lives more convenient. For example, calendar apps like Fantastical, note-taking apps like Evernote, and private car-hailing services like Uber.However, smartphone apps also make us lazier and lead to unnecessary expenses. For instance, online food ordering services like Seamless allow you to eat whatever you want without leaving home, and similar functions are offered by online grocery stores like FreshDirect.14) Photo-sharing apps: raising privacy concernsFacebook and Instagram have driven the over-sharing photo economy. Users are willing to share every move of themselves or their friends through photos, which raises privacy concerns.When we use Facebook, Instagram, or even Flickr, we expose a large amount of personal information, which will remain on the Internet forever. However, Snapchat solves this problem by automatically destroying photos after a set time limit. Nonetheless, sharing photos on the Internet still carries risks.15) Drones: commercialization is coming soon, privacy protection is controversialDrones used by governments and law enforcement agencies have raised concerns about privacy leaks. However, some people argue that we don't need to worry about it.The advent of smartphones has brought advanced technologies such as gyroscopes, accelerometers, and GPS. With these tools, we can create low-cost drones. People use drones to search for stranded travelers, conduct aerial photography, and engage in archaeology.The U.S. Federal Arbitration Law will officially allow the commercial use of drones in 2015. However, drone flight altitude cannot exceed 400 feet above ground level and must be at least 5 kilometers away from airports. (kirk)
