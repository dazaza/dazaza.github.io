Critical voices: Some doctors have no moral bottom line

Representative Zhong Nanshan criticizes medical chaos

Some doctors have no moral bottom line

According to Qilu Evening News, since the outbreak of SARS in 2003, people have remembered the name Zhong Nanshan. From being a member of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference for three consecutive terms to becoming a deputy to the 11th National People's Congress, his style of speaking frankly and "high-profile questioning" has earned him titles such as "Cannon King" and "Conscience of the Medical Profession," and has made him a focal point of media attention at the two sessions for many years.

Zhong Nanshan said that to promote China's medical reform and eliminate the chaos currently existing in the medical field, the medical elites represented by tertiary hospitals need to be more proactive. He also believed that medical humanities should also be included in the medical reform.

Throwing live babies away as dead ones, giving people abortion drugs instead of pregnancy preservation drugs... Zhong Nanshan recounted various kinds of chaos he encountered in his work, "This shows that some medical personnel have lost their basic medical moral bottom line." Zhong Nanshan said that if medical personnel can practice medicine with conscience, many doctor-patient conflicts can be resolved.

When talking about the monitoring of PM2.5, Zhong Nanshan said that comprehensive monitoring nationwide should start now. Just monitoring is not enough; specific measures must be taken to solve air pollution problems. "If the environment is not good, even if you have a good lifestyle, it is useless; those diseases will still occur."

For many years, Zhong Nanshan has been known for speaking frankly and truthfully, earning him the title of "cannon hand" at the two sessions. Suggestive voices: Spring Festival holiday should be extended to Lantern Festival

Committee member Gu Yeli

Spring Festival holiday should be extended to Lantern Festival

According to Xin Kuai Bao, "Our citizens have too few holidays." National Committee member and vice president of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies Gu Yeli said in an interview with Xin Kuai Bao that he plans to submit a proposal suggesting the restoration of the May Day long holiday and extending the Spring Festival long holiday to the Lantern Festival, "Holidays can make ordinary people feel tangible happiness, isn't it happy to sleep until you wake up naturally?"

Gu Yeli said that when the May Day long holiday was canceled, it was considered that it caused enormous pressure on transportation and tourist attractions at the time, but now citizens have become more rational about holidays, unlike before when they rushed out en masse, and they can arrange their holiday travel plans well. And now the management of tourist attractions is more mature, so there is no problem of overcrowding.

Finally, Gu Yeli pointed out that increasing holidays can also improve work efficiency. Gu Yeli believes that sitting around every day does not improve efficiency, "To be honest, a week's work can actually be done in three days."

The crime of illegal public deposit absorption should be abolished

Representative Guo Guangchang suggests

The crime of illegal public deposit absorption should be abolished

According to Oriental Morning Post, Guo Guangchang, representative of the National People's Congress and chairman of Fosun Group, said that private enterprises have played an important role in improving the income level of middle- and low-income groups, especially in expanding the middle-income group. Guo Guangchang said that in order to promote the development of the private economy, it is suggested to relax the field of private capital entering the financial sector and cancel the crime of illegal public deposit absorption; cultivate more private banks like Minsheng Bank to reduce the financing burden on small and medium-sized enterprises; expand the loan scale of small loan companies and support reputable small loan companies to take the road of banking; liberalize direct financing and actively encourage the development of equity investment enterprises.

Guo Guangchang also suggested reducing corporate taxes, such as canceling the cultural undertakings construction fee; reducing individual tax burdens, studying individual income tax collection methods based on families, stimulating consumption, and making economic development more dynamic.

Tax increase is faster than Liu Xiang

Tax reduction is slower than a snail

When we add taxes, the speed is like Liu Xiang; when we reduce taxes, the speed is like a snail. China's tax revenue has grown at a much higher rate than GDP for many years. Last year's tax revenue increased by 24.8% compared to the previous year, while the GDP growth rate was only about 9.2%. This far exceeds the normal range.

So far, the number of local governments or departments that have disclosed detailed government budgets remains very small. Ordinary taxpayers have no way of knowing how tax revenues are allocated and where they are used, and tax supervision is even less discussed. At the same time, people often see that the annual average expenditure on official vehicles nationwide is hundreds of billions of yuan, official eating and drinking, going abroad, training, private villas for officials, violation of regulations in building grand buildings and face-saving projects, etc., which continue despite repeated bans. This greatly damages people's understanding and support for public budgets and tax systems.

National Committee Member Zhang Guojun, Audit Partner of PricewaterhouseCoopers Zhongtian Accounting Firm
