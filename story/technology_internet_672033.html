That is to say, before the foot herbal bath, you should wash your feet with warm water, clean off the bacteria, dirt, and sweat on your feet, then you can carry out the foot herbal bath.

Two. People with contagious skin diseases, anal diseases, and external genital diseases should pay attention to the possibility of self-infection. For example: patients with athlete's foot, the bath water after the foot bath cannot be used to wash the anus and perineum to avoid self-infection. You also need to pay attention to the possibility of cross-infection. Patients with athlete's foot or trichomonal vaginitis, the bath water after the foot bath or sitz bath cannot be used by others to avoid cross-infection. Family members should use their own basins to prevent cross-infection or spread of infectious diseases.

Three. It is not advisable to take a foot herbal bath 30 minutes before and after meals. During the foot herbal bath, the blood vessels in the feet dilate and the blood volume increases, resulting in reduced blood flow to the intestines and internal organs, affecting gastrointestinal digestive function. A pre-meal foot herbal bath may suppress gastric juice secretion, which is unfavorable for digestion. Immediate foot herbal bath after meals can reduce the blood volume in the gastrointestinal tract, affecting digestion.

Four. Pay attention to keeping warm and preventing cold. The foot herbal bath works by utilizing the synergistic effect of medicinal and thermal forces. When using it, in summer, wind cooling should be avoided, and in winter, warmth should be maintained. The medicine solution needs to be kept at an appropriate temperature, and if it gets too cold, the medicine solution should be replaced. After the bath, the water on the feet should be wiped dry immediately, warm clothes should be worn to prevent catching a cold. This is especially important for the elderly, children, and physically weak patients.
