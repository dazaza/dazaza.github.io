Manchester United also has its own Lampard, one Chelsea superstar should step down.

by kersfe3f23 on 2009-12-07 08:37:28

Manchester United also has its own Lampard; a Chelsea superstar should step down. Chelsea's two records have come to an end, their flanks were restricted and they made their move too late; one superstar should step down. Sir Alex Ferguson has achieved his pre-match objectives, the defense has regained confidence; Arsenal’s Tsar is back in form, but they still need to be cautious away from home; Liverpool has struggled out of its slump, barely having two pieces of good news.

Barack should make way for Mikel. Although Manchester City played twice in a week, this season whenever they faced one of the top four teams, they would fully commit themselves. In this match, they performed up to their price tag and ended Chelsea's previous record of five consecutive wins without conceding a goal. They also ended the embarrassing streak of losing eight consecutive league matches against Chelsea.

An important reason for Manchester City's victory was their ability to limit the effectiveness of Chelsea's midfield enforcers. They also had specific strategies targeting the vulnerabilities of Chelsea's diamond midfield. In the midfield battle, Manchester City's Barry and De Jong stood out significantly, not only effectively supporting the defense but also restraining Anelka and Deco's performances. Their efforts today limited Chelsea's opportunities.

The soft underbelly of Chelsea's diamond midfield has been exposed again; when facing teams with strong wing attacks, Chelsea's midfield always struggles. For example, in the Champions League against Porto and in the away league match against Villa. In the diamond midfield setup, the offense is too reliant on the full-backs. However, in today's game, Manchester City's left and right full-backs, along with Robinho on the left and Wright-Phillips on the right, completely froze Ashley Cole and the right flank's offensive capabilities. With Deco being neutralized and Cole's attack obstructed, it became very difficult for Chelsea's forwards to create chances.

In the last 10 minutes, Chelsea reversed the situation through substitutions and won a penalty, but it was too late. Among the reasons for this, there was Lampard missing the penalty, but let's analyze why Chelsea almost overturned the situation on the field. In the midfield players, Barack should give up his starting position to Mikel. Compared to the previous round, Chelsea only rotated in two positions: Joe Cole replaced Deco, Mikel was substituted by Barack, and Essien retreated to play as the defensive midfielder.

Barack's physical condition allows him to assist in defense, but he lacks the stamina for attacking runs. When Mikel came on, it freed Essien. Despite being a midfield superstar, in high-level matchups, Chelsea should use the more energetic Mikel instead of Barack. However, Chelsea doesn't need to worry about their top spot yet. Their home record remains perfect, and their next two home opponents are not very strong. Facing Everton and Portsmouth, Chelsea has the opportunity to continue their miraculous home record.

Manchester United now has its own Lampard. West Ham, lacking their main striker Carlton Cole and main center-back Upson, is the team that concedes the most goals at home this season. West Ham has too many young players, and playing ground balls suits Manchester United perfectly. This was a well-prepared and planned match by Manchester United, allowing the key players to fully perform.

Previously, Sir Alex made significant rotations, continuously using new players in the Champions League group stage and League Cup because he believed this round could narrow the gap with Chelsea. He kept the two veterans Giggs and Scholes in good condition, ensuring the team's overall combat capability. Chelsea lost points, but under the leadership of the two veterans, Manchester United smoothly won the match. Without Scholes' long-range goal and Giggs' assists, Manchester United would have found it hard to secure three points away from home.

Manchester United also found a new weapon in this match—Gibson. Through establishing confidence in the League Cup, Gibson scored three goals in two consecutive matches across all competitions. Manchester United now has its own Lampard.

Manchester United's defense can also rebuild confidence. Although West Ham is hovering outside the relegation zone, scoring eight goals in the last two rounds, they have many attacking points. In this match, after Manchester United's starting center-back Gary Neville got injured, they had to use Carrick and Brown as center-backs, and even when Brown got injured, they had to temporarily switch Evra and Carrick, but they completed their tasks.

Kuszczak's performance, starting for the third consecutive match, provided peace of mind in the goalkeeper position. The defense successfully rebuilt confidence. Recently, Manchester United lost two away matches and drew 2-2 at home against Sunderland. Last season, Van der Sar's goal remained unbroken for 14 consecutive matches. This season, this was only the sixth Premier League match where they didn't concede a goal. However, in this match, despite so many injuries in the defense, only conceding one goal in three matches has built the confidence of Manchester United's defense.

Arshavin returns to form, the Tsar takes on a big responsibility. Without a main striker, breaking Stoke City's tight defense is quite difficult. Van Persie has been out for a long time, Eduardo is still recovering, so they can only rely on the Tsar.

Recently, due to failing to qualify for the World Cup playoffs, Arshavin's mood was very low, but now when he needs to take on a big responsibility, he can shake off the bad mood, stand up, and show the value of a star.

This season, Arsenal's three main attacking points are Fabregas, Van Persie, and Arshavin. It is very important for Arshavin to step forward. He also contributed his first league goal in nearly a month. After suffering three consecutive losses, if they can find the feeling of scoring and winning at home, it will be a boost for Arsenal's morale. It was precisely Arshavin's return to form that stabilized the whole team's morale and stabilized their position in the league table.

Arsenal's current problem is that there are indeed too many injuries, and they lack tall players. Once they face a team with a tight defense and tall players away from home, it becomes tricky. Next, visiting Liverpool and visiting Burnley, who has the third-best home record, will be very crucial for Arsenal. Hopefully, Arsenal's injury situation improves, whether they can smoothly earn points is still a bit worrying.

Liverpool's attack can only rely on one person.

This was a very dull match, 0-0, the entire team of Liverpool only had one chance from Engog. Originally, it was thought that this round would be a good opportunity for Liverpool to score points. Blackburn lost all 10 of their previous matches against the top four teams; they played twice in a week and lacked their midfield core Dunn, but unexpectedly, Liverpool only had one chance throughout the match.

Their attack could only rely on Glen Johnson's assists; Gerrard, Riera, and Benayoun's states were all below their usual levels. Blackburn's home style is relatively tough, and Liverpool couldn't gain any advantage.

Liverpool misses Torres and hopes he can return soon.

Barely two positive aspects: the team hasn't conceded in two consecutive away matches. Liverpool's defense is currently the worst among the top four, especially in the central defender pairing, but they didn't concede in this match. Second, although Aquilani didn't play in this match, his recovery is going well, and he might play in the next two home games, which could increase the vitality and creativity of Liverpool's midfield.
