Reported by reporter Huang Yuji: Have college students who are now busy looking for jobs ever thought: if you can wear size 37 shoes, will your employment competitiveness be better than others? Yesterday, at the job fair for foreign language and foreign trade graduates held in the North Campus of Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, a certain employer listed this as a preferential condition for hiring, sparking heated discussions among college students.

Can such a variety of preferential conditions directly affect whether the applicant stays or leaves after the interview? A human resources specialist from a brokerage company that prioritizes chess skills responded: not necessarily. "We once had the president of a university Go Association come for an interview; although his chess skills are good and his personal logical thinking ability is relatively strong, whether he has enough emergency response capability and analytical ability to judge the fluctuations in stock market indices still needs further assessment through the upcoming written test and second round of interviews."

A senior student named Xiao Chuan from Guangdong College of Commerce talked about her earlier interview experience: the interviewer asked detailed questions about her major, and Xiao Chuan answered without hesitation: "I don't know", "I forgot." This led to Xiao Chuan ultimately failing to pass the interview. "In the future, I need to learn from this experience; for questions I don't understand, I should think harder, like cashmere sweaters, actually with careful consideration, other ways to answer the question could still be found."

"That doesn't necessarily mean that size 37 feet can make shoes look good..."

"It might be possible to become a shoe model, which isn't entirely unreasonable..."

In fact, what factors are considered when being able to wear size 37 shoes makes you eligible for priority hiring? Manager Leng of the company explained: this isn't deliberately making things difficult for fresh graduates; it's just that all the sample shoes of the company are size 37. The company hopes that the business personnel recruited can introduce products while also personally demonstrating the sample shoes, thus achieving both objectives.

The reporter discovered at the recruitment site that day that many employers used evaluation methods unrelated to the profession, setting preferential conditions for the graduating students coming for interviews.

At the recruitment booth of a professional brokerage company providing securities and financial futures trading services, the requirements for the "day trader" position they were recruiting that day directly stated: applicants who are e-sports experts or chess masters (Chinese chess, Go) can be given priority consideration. A Guangzhou-based agency specializing in domestic promotion of imported famous brand perfumes and cosmetics put forward the slogan "Looking for people who appreciate fragrances," recruiting dozens of perfume training assistants.

Yesterday, the Employment Guidance Center for Graduates of Colleges and Universities in Guangdong Province held a job supply and demand meeting for the 2010 graduating class - a special recruitment session for foreign languages, foreign trade, international affairs, and management categories. Among the nearly 3,000 job positions introduced on-site, the reporter noticed that a foreign trade footwear company listed one requirement for its reserve sales staff: female candidates who fit size 37 shoes will be given priority consideration.

After attending three or four job fairs, Su, a graduating student in English from Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, realized: going to a job fair must start early - not only to get to the scene early to get a basic understanding of the situation, but also to try to secure a more forward position in the queue, finishing one round of interviews early to enter the next interview phase sooner, improving the efficiency of interviews.

"Only size 37 gets priority? Isn't that discrimination?"
