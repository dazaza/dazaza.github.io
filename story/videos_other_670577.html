How we dominate in the workplace

by itonyjhd05 on 2012-02-21 08:23:20

American magazines evaluate the golden collars in this way: aged between 25 and 45, well-educated, with certain work experience, business planning ability, professional skills and certain social relationship resources, annual salary ranging from $150,000 to $400,000. According to the national statistics bureau's survey, the monthly salary of the high-income "golden collar" class is mostly over 10,000 yuan; they own a house worth about 800,000 yuan; they own an imported car or at least an Audi or Santana level domestic sedan; their food expenses do not exceed one quarter of their total household expenditure. Starting from economic indicators, different societies have different benchmarks. But if we start from the "talent factor", in two societies that recognize "labor power as a commodity", the basic recognition point of the "commodity" is still similar. If there is any difference between "general commodities" and "human commodities", it might be that the former passively reflects value, while the latter actively embodies value. Therefore, we have reason to believe that under reasonable social organization, "high-salary people are all the same, but low-salary people are not quite the same." How can one become a high-salary talent? From many high-salary talents, the career consultant of YiLan YingCai Wang summarizes some common things: having career knowledge oriented towards practicality, professional career skills, career concepts oriented towards value, career attitudes oriented towards dedication, and career psychological qualities oriented towards survival. Knowledge takes "use" as the key. The ancients said: "learn to apply". To learn and apply is indeed not an easy thing. In this society full of "sea of learning" and "exam tide", let alone learning for application, even choosing what to learn is already a headache. For ordinary people, the acquisition of knowledge can be achieved through professional education in various colleges on the one hand, and through social re-education on the other hand. Professional education is relatively systematic, generally the education we choose according to our own situation, which mainly reflects our interests and potential; while re-education is generally additional education considered for our knowledge formation and work needs. From the current social situation, many people's choice of professional education has gradually deviated from the track of their own "interests" and "potential", increasingly tending to "hot" and avoiding "cold". They choose the hot majors that are relatively easy to find jobs, regardless of whether they like it or whether they can learn it. In terms of re-education, many people also have the mentality of being overly ambitious and seeking quick success. From the perspective of one's knowledge composition, the ideal education is based on professional education and social re-education as continuation. With more and more people working in mismatched positions, this continuous education has become impossible. Therefore, the quality of re-education to a certain extent determines a person's development pace. In this society, those who can be called "talents" are either "specialists" or "unique talents". The former are people with rich professional knowledge; the latter are people with special abilities. The continuing education chosen by "specialists" is often continuous education; while the education chosen by "unique talents" is often high-value, relatively advanced re-education. From the current re-education market, the re-education products suitable for "specialists" include various professional postgraduate courses and distance education; those suitable for "unique talents" should be management training and some high-value certification training. These educational products have many common points: targeting sunrise industries, high entry barriers, emphasizing cases and practice, difficult to master. In fact, these characteristics are also the marks of a good educational product. In addition to social re-education, an excellent talent will not miss any targeted internal training in the enterprise. Many enterprises now consider training as a welfare for employees. These targeted, carefully planned trainings aim to improve employees' professional skills and work efficiency, and reflect an employee's learning ability, potential for development, and enthusiasm for self-improvement. Many enterprises also use employees' training performance and results as one of the criteria for selecting talents within the company. For enterprise employees, through these targeted trainings, they can more clearly see the gaps between themselves and the company's requirements, and adjust themselves in time to become the people the company likes and the boss appreciates. Professional career skills oriented towards professionalism are indispensable for an excellent talent. They are the embodiment of execution ability, and more importantly, the reflection of personal thinking and charisma. They not only refer to the attitude towards the profession, but also the methods of thinking and working behaviors that reflect professional and occupational images, thereby improving everyone's work efficiency (performance). In many excellent talents, professional career skills often manifest as: good time management skills, effective communication skills, strong service awareness and the ability to satisfy customers, correct analysis and problem-solving skills, etc. Talent localization is the core work of most foreign enterprises entering China. Many foreign enterprise managers talk highly of the basic qualities and skills of local talents when discussing them, but when talking about the biggest gap between local talents and foreign talents or returned overseas students, they almost unanimously say: insufficient professionalization level. This gap is mainly reflected in the inability to fully understand the core culture of the enterprise, lack of team spirit, and lack of cost consciousness and time concept in daily work. The work focus of modern enterprises is time, efficiency, and profit. Achieving these factors requires a team of professionalized talents as a prerequisite. Therefore, more and more enterprises consider whether an employee should be promoted, given a raise, or cultivated, and whether they possess highly professionalized career skills has become one of the important factors. Career psychological qualities oriented towards survival. From another perspective, human society is also a brutal competitive ecosystem. Even the most senior talents face the fate of being eliminated or abandoned. Therefore, whether one can withstand pressure and endure setbacks is also an indicator of ability for modern people. In ancient times, there were heroes who "strived hard" and "reflected on the wall", and today, similarly, we need men who "fight and lose repeatedly, but never give up." In today's talent competition where "survival of the fittest" prevails, there is no "savior", only oneself can save oneself. "Survival" may not be a bloody term today, but its deeper meaning implies "living with great fanfare or living humbly." In a recent survey questionnaire, the excellent psychological qualities in the eyes of career experts from YiLan YingCai Wang are: able to face setbacks, gaps, failures, and denials in work, able to self-adjust and maintain a balanced mindset, "neither humble nor arrogant, indifferent to honor and disgrace." They also believe that the formation of such psychological qualities requires acute insight, a certain sense of crisis, and great patience as prerequisites. From their views, we can see that only a strong desire for survival can cultivate resilient psychological qualities. Career concepts oriented towards value. YiLan YingCai Wang's survey shows that the higher the talent, the more they emphasize the role of the organizational platform when job hunting because they firmly believe: finding a platform suitable for personal talent expression is more important than finding a stable platform. The internal cause of talent can only play a bigger role when it aligns with the external causes of society and organizations. Therefore, these people usually prefer to focus on selecting harmonious points and balance points rather than the short-term actions emphasized by ordinary job seekers such as money and position. When asked why she stayed in IBM instead of considering a higher position in another company, Guo Xiwén, the Greater China Region Human Resources Director of IBM, said: "People ask, under what circumstances would I think of leaving? I say, when you feel the next wave has come and you cannot keep up, when you no longer add value to the team, it's time to stop. So I always urge myself to make progress every day, otherwise, I will leave." She said, large companies have their own interests, every small company wants to become a large company. "Big" means you have more resources and can do more things. However, small companies have their own advantages, fast action, quick response, and a sense of achievement comes from daily different challenges. They are completely different. We often say, do you like sitting on an aircraft carrier or paddling a boat? There will definitely be people who like big ships and people who like surfing. People have different values and life views, so their career choices are also different. Understanding one's own value, having a clear goal, and then doing everything possible to maximize one's value is the unique feature of successful talents. Of course, they also like money and aspire to wealth, but they don't think about when they will get how much property all day long. Instead, they firmly believe: one's capital is one's own value, as long as one's value can continue to expand, one will surely become wealthy. Dedication-oriented career attitude. The spirit of dedication requires us to abide by our duties, work solidly and diligently, and take responsibility for market goals and plans with wisdom and effort, and be responsible for work quality and efficiency. Yang Jinzhu, General Manager of Huifeng Group, when talking about his understanding of the spirit of dedication, said: "The spirit of dedication is the specific form of people-oriented management thought. Any progress is driven by countless people, especially our market work. Whether the work is done well or not is related to the cultivation of the spirit of dedication, and also the bridge to integrate into the commodity economy society and expand personal development space." In today's market economy that is constantly deepening, dedication is a professional attitude and also a noble manifestation of professional ethics. A person without the spirit of dedication may not be respected and accepted even if he has ability; a person without ability but with the spirit of dedication can find his stage to perform and realize his own value step by step, and eventually may become a widely respected talent. Article excerpted from YiLan YingCai Wang: Cost Engineering Talent Network
