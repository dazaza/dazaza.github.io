Three measures for emergency rescue in residential building fires: 18671079388

by hbjndfwang on 2012-02-29 09:08:38

nbsp nbsp nbsp One: Cherish the fire protection facilities of the floor

nbsp nbsp nbsp Fire protection facilities are well-equipped and strictly reviewed by the fire department, so most high-rise buildings have an advantage. However, if the management department does not cherish and citizens do not care about fire protection facilities, this advantage will become a disadvantage. Last year, a big fire happened in a certain high-rise residence. After the incident, the fire personnel found that the emergency lights on the stairs at that time were not lit, making the staircase completely dark, which seriously affected the escape of residents.

Therefore, residents must not ignore these "life-saving treasures" upstairs. If you find that the fire protection facilities in the building are damaged or have expired, you should report it to the property management department in time; if they drag their feet without handling it, you should report it to the fire department.

nbsp nbsp nbsp Treasure Two: Master the use of fire extinguishing equipment

nbsp nbsp nbsp Even if the fire protection facilities are intact, you still cannot relax. Residents should not only equip themselves with fire extinguishers at home, but also learn how to skillfully use basic fire extinguishing equipment such as fire extinguishers, so that when a disaster strikes, the fire extinguisher in your hand won't be a dud.

In addition, remember the location and usage method of emergency lights and smoke-sensitive automatic sprinkler systems, so that they can be very useful in case of a fire. Moreover, the position of the evacuation indication signs on the stairs and the evacuation route should be thoroughly memorized in daily life. Some experts suggest that each family should have a "fire emergency plan", which needs to be reviewed frequently and practiced constantly.

nbsp nbsp nbsp Treasure Three: Remember the key points of self-rescue during a fire

nbsp nbsp nbsp When the fire starts: You can use the fire extinguisher to put out the fire first, shout loudly, and organize the elderly and children to evacuate the building quickly; When the fire starts in other rooms: Before opening the door, touch the door handle with your hand. If you find it too hot or see smoke entering the room, it means the fire has sealed off the exit, and you should not open the door rashly. After confirming that the fire is not too threatening, leave the room and escape from the fire scene as soon as possible. When the fire and thick smoke have sealed off the passage: The only way is to retreat into the room, prevent air circulation, block the gaps in doors and windows with cloth strips, and use all means to call for help. When trapped on a higher floor, due to panic caused by smoke, some people might jump down impulsively. Remember, under insufficient safety measures, you should not blindly jump from the building. Instead, expose yourself in a place where you are easily visible, and wait for firefighters to rescue you.

nbsp nbsp nbsp When surrounded by fire: You should retreat to a relatively safer bathroom for temporary refuge. Remember, never hide under the bed, inside wardrobes, or in attics during a fire, as these places are hard for firefighters to discover.
