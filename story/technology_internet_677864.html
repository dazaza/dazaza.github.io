Villagers patrol the mountain and pick a nearly 1-meter long wood lingzhi (picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-27 10:24:36

Huge "Wood Ear" Fungus Found in Deep Mountain: Such a large wood lingzhi is indeed rare. Photo provided by Hao Guangzong. Two months ago, Han Suoping, a village cadre of Xiyuan Village in Pingshan County, found a huge "wood ear" fungus measuring 89 cm in length and 60 cm in width while on patrol for forest fire prevention in a rarely-visited mountain. Recently, after identification by experts, this "wood ear" was confirmed to be a wood lingzhi with certain medicinal value.

Xiyuan Village is located deep in the mountains of Pingshan County. Since the implementation of the farmland reforestation policy, the vegetation on the mountain has become lush with dense brambles and shrubs. By the end of October, the grass on the mountain had turned yellow and the trees were withered, so the village cadres needed to conduct routine patrols up the mountain. One day, Han Suoping walked into the forest again, about 3 kilometers away, and came to a persimmon tree. This persimmon tree had been cut down more than ten years ago, leaving only a stump. Suddenly, Han Suoping noticed a huge "wood ear" growing on the stump, which was the largest he had ever seen in his life. Despite not knowing whether it had any value or not, out of curiosity, Han Suoping carefully picked it and carried it home. Upon measurement at home, it was 89 cm long and 60 cm wide. The villagers all marveled at its size, but no one could identify what kind of fungus it was.

Recently, upon hearing about this event, Hao Guangzong, a retired forestry official from Pingshan County, along with his colleague Yang Yeming, also found it strange and went to check it out personally. Hao Guangzong told reporters that after a long period of drying, this "wood ear" had dried and shrunk, but still measured 82 cm in length and 54 cm in width. After identification by experts from the Biology Department of Hebei Normal University, it was confirmed to be a wood lingzhi, belonging to the Polyporaceae family, with certain medicinal values, mainly used for treating insomnia. Experts mentioned that wood lingzhi itself is not rare, but such a large one is quite uncommon.

Due to the difficulty of preserving wood lingzhi, experts suggested drying it and then placing it in a freezer, freezing it at an extremely low temperature of minus 30 to 40 degrees Celsius for ten days to kill insect eggs and bacteria. Moreover, it should not be placed in a damp environment, otherwise, it would easily rot.
