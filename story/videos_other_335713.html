The latest conditions for studying abroad in 2010

Citizens applying for official overseas travel conditions are based on the provisions of the "Implementation Rules of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Entry and Exit Administration." Official overseas travel referred to as "official business" includes: residing, visiting relatives and friends, studying abroad (including publicly funded and self-funded study), tourism (including individual and group tours), working abroad, inheriting property, and other non-official activities. Specifically:

1. If one spouse resides abroad or is invited by a foreign resident friend to reside, inherit property, or obtain a settlement permit issued by the foreign government, they can apply to reside abroad.

2. If invited by relatives or friends who have permanent residency rights in the destination country or possess valid long-term residency documents, they can apply for overseas visits to relatives and friends.

3. Those sent abroad for study by the state or agricultural units, whether publicly funded or self-funded, can apply for overseas study.

4. Those joining tour groups organized by travel agencies approved by the state with "entry tour" operating rights, or traveling individually, can apply for overseas tourism.

5. Foreign citizens who have signed employment contracts with foreign employers or authorized overseas employment service institutions, or are dispatched by domestic labor companies, can apply for overseas work.

6. Business personnel from various enterprises who need to be dispatched abroad for business purposes due to enterprise needs can apply for business travel.

7. For other non-official activities that require going abroad.

Conditions:

1. Self-funded study is not restricted by academic qualifications or age. Anyone meeting the conditions can apply for self-funded study abroad at high schools, colleges (both arts and sciences), graduate school, or vocational training.

2. Graduates and self-funded students applying for overseas study can directly handle self-funded overseas study procedures at the public security authorities in their registered residence with relevant proof materials.

3. Self-funded students below postgraduate level (with associate degree or higher) and graduates, after serving a certain number of years domestically or repaying higher education compensation fees, can apply for self-funded overseas study. (Service years: five years for undergraduate graduates, three years for postgraduate graduates, two years for associate degree and adult university graduates; Repayment of cultivation fees: 1,500 yuan per year for associate students, 2,500 yuan per year for undergraduate students, 4,000 yuan per year for master’s students, 6,000 yuan per year for doctoral students.)

4. Postgraduate students graduating with a Ph.D. do not need to repay higher education cultivation fees when self-funding their post-doctoral research abroad.

5. In addition to the above conditions, publicly funded overseas study also requires: First, obtaining sufficient foreign exchange assistance or overseas scholarships through legal and legitimate procedures, holding economic guarantee letters required by sponsors (which must be recognized by the destination country), or holding scholarship or sponsorship certificates required by foreign universities, scientific research institutions, or foundations. The foreign exchange assistance or overseas scholarships obtained should be able to support their studies and living expenses abroad. Second, they should have a corresponding level of foreign language proficiency.

Procedures and Relevant Regulations:

After receiving the admission certificate and foreign exchange sponsorship certificate, if the applicant is a current student or employed, they should first submit an application to their unit or school, which should sign an opinion. Then, the applicant should take relevant materials and the unit-signed certificate to the local public security department to apply for an exit passport. The public security department will issue approval according to relevant regulations and provide the passport. The applicant then takes the passport to the embassy or consulate of the destination country in China to process the entry visa. Students of higher education institutions, including undergraduates, technical college students, and postgraduate students, may retain their academic records for one year before going abroad. Self-funded overseas students studying abroad as postgraduates may, if they wish, be converted into publicly funded overseas students, receiving the "State Dispatched Overseas Student Certificate" issued by the Chinese embassy or consulate abroad without changing their passports or visas.

Self-funded overseas study is handled by the local public security department. Applicants who are current students or employed should have their applications endorsed by their school or unit.
