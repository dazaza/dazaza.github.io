Synopsis:

The protagonist, Luffy, aspires to become the Pirate King. Due to accidentally eating the Gomu Gomu no Mi (Rubber Fruit), he gains powerful rubber-like elasticity as his weapon. After ten years of rigorous training, he finally masters the ability to control the strength of his rubber body. At this point, he decides to set sail and head towards the legendary "Grand Line." However, being somewhat reckless, Luffy lacks navigational knowledge, can't swim, and doesn't know where the "Grand Line" is located. Thus, he begins a journey typical in shonen manga—recruiting companions.

As a story-driven manga, *One Piece* naturally has its unique qualities. When crafting the stories, each character has their own subplot, which forms the background, personality, and growth journey of the subplot's main character. Each story is handled well, deeply touching the heart. Whether it’s Usopp’s or Nami’s story, they evoke feelings of helplessness and sadness. The transitions between stories are also well managed, with many touching "flashback" narratives. Meanwhile, the main storyline is filled with suspense—"What exactly is the Grand Line?" Those who have been there dare not recall it. The man known as "Hawk-Eyes," who easily defeated Zoro, is just one of the Seven Warlords of the Grand Line. The Grand Line not only attracts the ambitious pirates within the story but also tightly grips our hearts.

Gol D. Roger was the first person to obtain the world's greatest treasure, One Piece, and became the Pirate King. Before his execution, he declared to the world that he had hidden the secret treasure One Piece somewhere along the Grand Line. Aspiring pirates flocked to the Grand Line, marking the beginning of the Age of Pirates.

Luffy is someone who keeps promises with friends. In his mind, there is one unshakable belief: "I am a man who will become the Pirate King!" For him, the straw hat symbolizes this promise. The story revolves around this belief. Along Luffy's journey, he meets six other companions—Zoro, Sanji, Usopp, Nami, Chopper, and Robin. Like Luffy, they each possess their own beliefs and ideals, and they come together. Overcoming various difficulties, they reach the Grand Line, defeating many formidable enemies while striving for their dreams.
