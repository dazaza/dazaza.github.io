Nokia's Sincerity: Unconditionally Support Developers in China

by geekzhang on 2013-05-08 23:09:42

Once a dominant force in the mobile phone industry, Nokia has now fallen on hard times to the point of having to sell its headquarters building. However, despite this setback, Nokia continues to persevere and hopes for a turnaround. As to why Nokia has chosen to stick with one platform, opinions vary. But after today's Nokia Developer Summit, it seems that Nokia may simply be trying to focus on doing one thing well.At the Nokia Developer Summit, Nokia showed strong support for developers of Nokia applications. Nokia has reached an agreement with the Beijing government to create a software industrial park in Beijing to support Nokia application development teams. Nokia will provide venture capital investment, government support, AppCampus, office space, channel promotion resources, media promotion resources, technical training, Nokia testing devices, and developer salons. Furthermore, for more innovative applications, Nokia will offer financial support without taking equity stakes and will promote these applications globally.In addition, Nokia is providing 3D printing technology to help developers innovate on Nokia phone cases. It appears that Nokia is aiming to build an entire Windows Phone ecosystem centered around Nokia.However, within this ecosystem, Nokia is facing issues with smartphone sales. Currently, the smartphone market is dominated by Apple and Samsung, which together hold over half of the market share. If Nokia fails to secure a significant market share, then no matter how attractive the benefits, it won't be able to attract developers.Nokia claims that the advantage of the Windows Phone platform lies in emerging markets, where there are relatively fewer applications, allowing for quicker entry. But for developers, the more practical question is whether they can make money, rather than just entering the market to claim a spot and then fail.Therefore, in reality, more developers prefer to remain观望 (on the sidelines), waiting until there is real potential for profit before rushing in en masse to capitalize on the opportunity.
