Children's clothing investigation report

1. Broad Prospects in the Children's Clothing Market

Currently, there are 380 million children aged 0 to 16 in China. Annually, about 4.6 billion pieces of children's clothing are produced, yet the annual sales volume is only 6 to 8 billion pieces, accounting for nearly 7% of the total national clothing consumption. However, it’s encouraging that in recent years, children's clothing consumption has been increasing at an annual rate of 8%. This is primarily due to young people rushing to have babies during auspicious years. For example, in the year 2000, there were 36 million "Millennium Babies," 30 million "Golden Pig Babies" in 2007, and a significant number of "Olympic Babies" in 2008. According to authoritative institutions, by 2010, the number of newborns will double on the current birth rate. Such a massive new-born group constitutes a strong growth momentum for children's clothing consumption. Secondly, as the population gradually shifts to urban areas, once these relocated populations secure stable incomes through industrial adjustments, their children's clothing needs will also surge rapidly. Each year, there will be an increase of approximately 2.5 million potential consumers for children's clothing. The consumption volume of children's clothing among urban residents is showing an upward trend with an annual growth rate as high as 26.5%, which will also become one of the factors driving the growth in demand for children's clothing. These data all reflect the broad market prospects for engaging in the design and development of children's clothing, and the children's clothing market will enter a new stage of development!

Children's clothing covers the entire wardrobe for individuals aged 0-16. By age group, it can be further divided into infant wear, toddler wear, small children's clothing, medium children's clothing, large children's clothing, and youth wear. From the actual operating conditions of the domestic children's clothing market, it is evident that infant wear, toddler wear, small children's clothing, and medium children's clothing have initially taken shape in China. The industrial hierarchy is relatively clear, each having its leading enterprises, with a relatively larger number of brands.

China's children's clothing consumption shows double-digit growth, making children's clothing a new growth point in the apparel industry. With the further increase in family income and urban residents gradually reaching a well-off living standard, the consumer demand for children's clothing in China has shifted from merely satisfying basic life needs to pursuing fashion and aesthetics. In some economically developed cities, consumer demand for children's clothing tends towards trends and branding.

The current pattern of the Chinese children's clothing market is roughly evenly split between domestic and international brands. Among the 50% share occupied by domestic manufacturers, at most, only 30% have brands, concentrated in first, second, and third-tier city markets; 70% remain brandless, distributed across vast rural markets. Meanwhile, the 50% market share occupied by international brands is almost entirely concentrated in first-tier central city markets.

In recent years, with the acceleration of urban construction, the development speed of commercial outlets in the secondary children's clothing market has increased. As the shopping environment and scale of malls narrow the gap with those in the primary market, it provides a marketing platform for children's clothing brands entering the secondary market. After more brands enter the secondary market, the richness of the market brands continues to improve, offering customers more brand choices. Since the secondary children's clothing market has moved from the brand introduction phase to the brand development phase, consumers' purchasing power is also strong, with a higher desire for brands, leaving ample room for market development and relatively lower operating costs. The promising development prospects of this market have drawn the attention of children's clothing operators, with some children's clothing enterprises beginning to penetrate the secondary market through marketing channels.

2. Severe Current Situation of the Children's Clothing Market

Although the future of the children's clothing market is broad, the current situation is not optimistic.

(1) Design and market are disconnected, plagiarism is rampant, presenting challenges for children's clothing designers.

Children's clothing design must be both fashionable in style and bright in color, while ensuring safety and comfort, durability against dirt, and resistance to wear. Additionally, it must consider the psychological and physiological characteristics of children.

For instance, in style design, to reflect childlike innocence and fun, designers often use various decorative techniques, but the application of such techniques requires extra caution. Following the well-known milk powder incident, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection, and Quarantine of China intensified monitoring efforts across industries, including food and clothing, raising the safety standards for children's clothing again. It was reported that in 2008 alone, dozens of incidents occurred where exported children's clothing was recalled or notified by the EU and the US due to potential dangers posed by cords and small parts. The safety of children's clothing should provoke deep reflection among Chinese children's clothing designers.

Another example is fabric selection. Children are active and prone to sweating, so cotton fabrics that are soft and sweat-absorbing should be chosen. However, cotton fabrics have issues such as color differences, easy fading, deformation after washing, and wrinkling, requiring the use of high-quality dyes, pre-shrinking treatment, and wrinkle-free processing. When selecting dyes, environmental friendliness must also be considered because children's skin is delicate... Numerous details place higher demands on designers.

The current state of domestic children's clothing production is characterized by numerous companies crowding into the same channel, relying on advertising promotion or low-price competition to seize limited profits. For instance, in Zhejiang Zhili Town and Guangdong Foshan, the scale of enterprises, numbering in the thousands, is astonishing. In reality, at least 90% of these enterprises are in the earliest stages of family workshop-style factories. Despite their weak strength, they exist in considerable numbers and maintain a relatively stable overall scale, thus forming an indispensable foundation for local industries. Simultaneously, due to this scale effect, a complete industrial chain from raw material processing to production and logistics has indeed formed locally, achieving cost reduction in production. This cluster-based production trend undoubtedly exerts a driving force on the children's clothing industry, forming a force that is temporarily difficult to shake.

For these workshop-style small enterprises, they rely on quantity and low prices to capture profit, almost without needing design. Once newly developed children's clothing hits the market, they buy it back, dismantle it, record the style and proportions, and then imitate it en masse, flooding the market and posing a threat to truly high-quality, high-class, personalized branded children's clothing.

Faced with this situation, children's clothing designers are powerless. On one hand, they face higher self-imposed requirements, while on the other, they lack market demand because the trend of cluster-based production of children's clothing that does not require designers cannot be shaken in the short term, forcing children's clothing designers to seek development within limited branded children's clothing companies.

(2) Domestic children's clothing is merely a scaled-down version of adult clothing, lacking child-specific features.

Domestic children's clothing started late, without specialized research institutions for children's clothing design or professional talent training bases, lacking regular studies on the physiological characteristics of children. People subconsciously regard children's clothing as a scaled-down version of adult clothing, focusing on quantity rather than brand. Industry experts have pointed out that Chinese children's clothing must follow the path of branding, and the focus of branding lies in rebuilding the "design concept of branded children's clothing."

Branded children's clothing design is not just about the appearance design of styles and colors; more importantly, it involves functional design that adheres to the psychological characteristics of children and meets their physiological needs. Leading countries' children's clothing enterprises generally consider children as a special group in the clothing market, with research expenses on children's psychology and physiology typically accounting for 25% of total expenses.

For example, overseas professional children's clothing designers highly value the "teaching through clothing" design concept. They point out that approximately 50% of learning ability is developed during the 0-4-year-old infant period, 30% during the 4-8-year-old period, and the remaining 20% is completed between 8-17 years old. How to utilize children's clothing to help children develop their brain learning ability is the responsibility of professional children's clothing designers. Many domestic children's clothing enterprises rely on low-price competition and imitation production, disregarding design and treating children's clothing as a scaled-down version of adult clothing. This conceptual gap causes domestic children's clothing to linger at a low level.

(3) Another market gap in the domestic children's clothing industry is the lack of brand operation capabilities.

Most domestic enterprises in marketing are still at the wholesale stage, not considering after-sales service, while international brands adopt direct store operations and franchise chain methods to develop, ensuring after-sales service and further building the brand foundation.

In fact, there are a few large domestic enterprises that have certain strength and have established corresponding marketing channels. They have begun to focus on branding, but the main sales channels for children's clothing are department stores, supermarkets, and children's clothing specialty stores. Currently, unless a children's clothing business has excellent quality, good reputation, and a certain level of market sales performance, it is difficult to enter these channels. Therefore, for a new brand entering the market, it is challenging to secure these sales channels, making it relatively difficult for children's clothing to build a brand. This leads many enterprises unable to bear the interest pressure brought by doing branding in the early stage, continuing to maintain the existing market wholesale marketing method.

(4) Branded children's clothing prices are too high, making them difficult for many consumers to accept, making the domestic children's clothing branding road arduous.

Factors contributing to the high price of branded children's clothing include: brand authorization fees being the main factor causing price increases, higher standards for children's clothing fabrics leading to rising product costs, complex children's clothing processes making it hard to reduce labor costs, more specifications affecting selling prices, and seasonal promotional discounts leading to higher children's clothing prices.

These factors causing branded children's clothing to be "high-priced" are difficult to avoid, but consumers see only the "high price" and not the reasons behind it. Surveys show that when it comes to factors to consider when purchasing children's clothing, consumers still focus on good value for money. Many parents believe that since children grow quickly, there is no need to spend a lot of money on branded children's clothing, hence mentioning brand consumption does not exceed 30%. This indicates that the brand awareness of children's clothing in consumer consciousness is still weak, and the road to branding domestic children's clothing is long and arduous.

3. Direction of the Children's Clothing Market

From the broad prospects of the children's clothing market, it can be seen that as urban residents' lives gradually enter a well-off stage, the children's clothing market is also transitioning from "quantity consumption" to the "brand consumption" phase. Many children's clothing items are in the initial stage of brand enhancement, and everyone is seeking operational methods suitable for their own brands based on their conditions and expectations for the future. To stand out from the low-price competition and move towards brand operation has become a hot spot for enterprise investment. Therefore, experts have pointed out that in future children's clothing consumption, children's clothing specialty stores have become an urgent task for the children's clothing industry, and developing brands is the only way to revitalize China's children's clothing industry. The essence of future competition in branded children's clothing will be the technologization of children's clothing and the child-centric design of brands.

3.1 High-Tech Competition

Children's clothing development must be combined with high technology to continuously launch new products to increase market share. Transitioning from resource competition to high-tech competition, such as health, healthcare functional children's clothing, environmentally friendly children's clothing, and intelligent children's clothing are all future development trends. Only products with high-tech content can have high added value. High-tech levels have become an important part of corporate competition.

3.2 Brand Competition

Products can substitute for each other, but the personality and value of a brand are irreplaceable; products have a life cycle, but brands do not. Brands can be inherited. Therefore, from the perspective of competition, corporate competition will shift from traditional product, price, channel, and promotion to competition centered on brands. Children's clothing no longer relies on functional value to gain consumer trust but depends on brand image, entering the era of image consumption. Establishing a good brand awareness and concept, mastering advanced brand management technology, will be an important factor in future competition among children's clothing enterprises.

3.3 Weakening Competition

The main function of children's clothing in the 21st century is to reflect individuality. Children's clothing enterprises are high-quality enterprises based on high-tech representation. Under the requirement of adapting to new technologies, the children's clothing industry needs to learn to actively cooperate with competitors, form alliances with consistent interests, achieve mutual dependence, harmonious survival, and coordinated development. Thus, although market competition will still exist, the weakening of competition is an inevitable trend.

The above discusses the prospects, current situation, and direction of China's children's clothing market. For China's original brands, it is still a critical stage for brand enhancement. Chinese children's clothing enterprises should seize the opportunity to let original brands become the main force in China's children's clothing industry in the coming years, realizing the path of branding in China's children's clothing industry.
