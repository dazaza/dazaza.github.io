【Qing Dynasty Culture】Fun Stories from Emperor Tongzhi's Childhood

There are many stories about Emperor Tongzhi's childhood. Once, young Zaichun often heard people say that those without knowledge have "no ink in their chest," while knowledgeable people have plenty of ink in their bellies. He also wished to be a knowledgeable person. One day, on a whim, he drank all the ink from the inkstone on his desk. A eunuch walked in and saw the little emperor with a mouth full of blackness, which scared him greatly. He hurriedly asked what had happened. Young Zaichun smiled and said, "Now I am knowledgeable!" The little eunuch still didn't understand. Young Zaichun pointed to the empty inkstone and said, "Now my belly is full of ink!" The eunuch was terrified, realizing his negligence, and quickly reported to the Empress Dowager, summoning the royal physician. By then, the little emperor began to feel nauseous and started vomiting, looking very uncomfortable. It wasn't until after taking medicine for five days that the nausea gradually subsided.

The first time young Zaichun sneaked out of the palace, it was truly an eye-opener; everything was so fresh. He saw a group of children gathered around a cart, each holding a small bowl as the vendor scooped something into the bowls. The children ate it up with their small spoons, making "huh huh" sounds. Young Zaichun was amazed, drooling quite a bit. He went over and asked, "What is this?" The vendor replied, "Young Master, this is called frozen fruit preserves, sour, sweet, and icy cold, very delicious. Would you like to try some?"

Young Zaichun had never been called "Young Master" before, finding it very interesting, so he cheerfully took the bowl and started eating. After one bite, he felt refreshed and thoroughly enjoyed it! Just as he was savoring it, several eunuchs who were frantically searching for him spotted him and rushed over. In a panic, young Zaichun lifted his robe and poured the remaining frozen fruit preserves into his pocket, running back to the palace with a wet and soggy pocket.
