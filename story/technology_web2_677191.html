Welfare Lottery Double Color Ball, Love to the South, Loneliness to the North, 4.18

by d1ge58gfd on 2012-02-26 21:57:32

On April 18th, Zhang Biao's courtyard suddenly became lively with a constant stream of people coming and going. Upon inquiry, it turned out that many unscrupulous Hunan merchants had received the down payment for onions but, seeing no improvement in market conditions, fled without paying for the goods, leaving the agents and onion farmers in great distress. They came to seek Zhang Biao's help. They told him: "Now you are the only one left with some authority. Please persuade them to have mercy and take our onions, even at a very low price. We just want to recover something." An elderly man, supported by his granddaughter, cursed the Hunan merchants as bastards who had lost their conscience. Someone reminded him: "We still need to rely on these Hunan gods. Be careful what you say." The old man angrily said: "They harmed us and now we have to act submissive before them." Zhang Biao gathered everyone in the living room and said: "I understand how the villagers feel. I am willing to help. I will do my best to talk to these bosses. If they agree to take the onions, I will take them to place the order." Someone said: "Call those bosses out and see if they are any different from those who ran away." Afraid that the crowd would vent their anger indiscriminately and hurt the face of his bosses, Zhang Biao did not agree. Immediately someone muttered: "They put on airs and probably aren't good people either." Hearing this, Juanzi quickly went to the room where Wang Chengfu, Li Tie brothers, and her brother-in-law were staying, hoping they could pacify the villagers. Wang Chengfu said: "Let those who harmed them deal with them." Li Tie brothers said: "Seeing them is just asking for trouble." Her brother-in-law said: "We are doing business, not being Buddhas. If we were Buddhas, we'd starve." Juanzi stomped her foot and rushed into my room. Out of breath, she said: "Xiaowen, do me a favor and meet with the villagers." I said: "I'm not a real boss. Would it make any difference if I show up?" Juanzi said: "Just pretend to be a boss and give them some hope." I said: "Even if I give them hope, can I guarantee to fulfill it?" Juanzi said: "I feel there will be a way. The hardest time is now. If the villagers don't see any hope now, conflicts will definitely arise when they return home. Once a conflict starts, anything bad might happen." Even if a conflict occurred, it would only happen between the agents and the onion farmers, not involving Zhang Biao and Juanzi. However, Juanzi's anxious and worried appearance was truly touching, so I agreed to her request.
