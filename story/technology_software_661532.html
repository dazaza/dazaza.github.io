In the early stages of the disease

by qianhola1 on 2012-02-09 10:04:17

What are the specific clinical symptoms of arthritis? I believe many people have this question. Only by understanding its specific clinical symptoms can we achieve early detection, early diagnosis, and early treatment, which is beneficial to prevent the progression of the disease and improve the prognosis of patients. Below, let's follow the clinical experts of Chongqing St. Paul Orthopedic Hospital (Chongqing Orthopedic Hospital) to learn about the specific clinical symptoms of arthritis. 1. Joint pain is a relatively common specific clinical symptom of arthritis. In the early stages of the disease, the pain is often not severe, and the affected joint usually only shows soreness or mild pain. The symptoms may worsen with weather changes or fatigue, and improve with rest. During this period, joint movement is generally unrestricted, and patients may overlook it, leading to delayed medical consultation. As the condition progresses, the pain becomes more pronounced, and different types of arthritis may exhibit different pain characteristics. 2. Joint swelling is also a noticeable specific clinical symptom of arthritis. Swelling is the result of the progression of joint inflammation and generally correlates positively with the severity of the disease. 3. Joint dysfunction is also one of the specific clinical symptoms of arthritis. After inflammation occurs, due to protective spasms of the muscles around the joint and damage to the joint structure, partial or complete loss of joint function may occur. This concludes the introduction of the specific clinical symptoms of arthritis. We hope the above content can be helpful to you.
