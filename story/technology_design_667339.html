The advantages and disadvantages of Shiling, the "Capital of China's Leather Goods," in its leather goods industry

Shiling, known as the "Capital of China's Leather Goods," holds a significant position in the Chinese luggage industry. After 30 years of development and accumulation, various factors in Shiling's leather goods and luggage industry have converged, gradually forming a complete industrial chain with comprehensive supporting functions.

Currently, Shiling's leather goods and luggage products are sold all over China, Southeast Asia, Europe, and America, generating annual sales exceeding 18 billion yuan. The product share accounts for 60% of Guangdong's market and 50% of the national market. In the European mid-to-low-end leather goods and luggage market, more than 60% of the products are "Made in Shiling." However, the enterprises' products lack independent design and top brands, causing their prices to remain in the mid-to-low range. The dilemma of "high production volume but few brands" has restricted the continuous growth of Shiling's leather goods.

It is understood that the leather goods industry in Shiling started arduously in the late 1970s, experiencing a spiral upward development model from "Shiling processing" to "Shiling manufacturing," which is highly dependent on foreign markets. This development model has been referred to as the "Shiling model" by outsiders.

At present, regarding the "Shiling model," its low-level scale expansion still has room for development due to the support of both domestic and international large markets for a relatively long period. However, from the perspective of long-term sustainable development of the industry, enhancing added value and improving core competitiveness are inevitable trends. How to leverage industrial upgrading under the existing industrial development trajectory, shifting from previous reliance solely on quantity and scale expansion to a high-efficiency, high-added-value industry direction supported by high technology, with brand and fashion as its essence, allowing Shiling's leather goods and luggage industry to truly transition from "Shiling Manufacturing" to "Shiling Creation," highlighting cluster competitive advantages, has become a topic on the agenda for the Shiling leather goods and luggage industry.

Industrial Agglomeration Development, Rapid Scale Expansion

The latest industry data information disclosed by the Shiling Industry Research Center reflects that there are currently more than 7,200 leather goods and luggage production enterprises in Shiling Town, over 11,000 sales enterprises, with products exported to 136 countries and regions. The market share accounts for 60% of Guangdong, 50% of the national market, and over 60% of the European mid-to-low-end market, generating annual sales of 18 billion yuan.

If these data seem somewhat monotonous, then the introduction by Zhou Lichang, director of the Shiling Industry Research Center, provides a more intuitive perspective. She told reporters: "Currently, 60% of the leather goods sold in Paris's third district are sourced from Shiling, making the district a showcase center for Shiling leather goods, breaking the monopoly previously held by Jews; Florence is Europe's largest luggage distribution center, with 10-15 containers shipped daily from Shiling."

Shiling's leather goods and luggage industry originated at the beginning of the reform and opening-up era. The trendsetting Shiling people, with their unique business acumen, boldly developed the leather goods and luggage industry, moving from village teams to towns and then to cities, spontaneously forming the embryonic form of the leather goods market.

Starting from 1978 when Zhong Siwen, a returned youth, received the first leather bag order from Guangdong's Department Store Station, surrounding villagers began imitating him, leading to the gradual budding and development of the leather goods and luggage industry locally. By the 1980s, the leather goods and luggage industry in Shiling had largely moved away from the early family workshop model, with some raw material and accessory industries gradually following suit, forming a "front store, back factory" leather goods and luggage street. The leather goods and luggage industry entered its second stage of development, transitioning from a "point" to a "line." By the mid-to-late 1990s, there were already over 2,000 merchants engaged in leather goods and luggage production and operation in Shiling, making the leather goods and luggage industry one of the dominant industries in the town and even the entire Huadu District, though it was still in its initial, disorganized, and weak state.

In 2000, the Huadu District and Shiling Town Party Committee and government recognized the enormous potential stored in the leather goods and luggage industry and the constraints imposed by its disorderly status on industrial development. They began to actively participate, guiding the industry to transform from "spontaneous" to "conscious." One of the most important actions was combining investment attraction and land resource integration to build the largest leather goods professional wholesale market in the country - the Shiling (International) Leather Goods City.

Since 2009, Shiling has built five specialized trading markets including the Shiling (Global) Leather Hardware Market, Naihai Decorative Garden, Xilong International Leather Market, Baofeng Leather Materials Trading City, etc., gradually perfecting the Shiling leather goods industry chain.
