TV Series "War Gaze" Plot Introduction

Suddenly, war descended upon the splendid and glamorous night of Jianghai City. Precision-guided weapons fiercely attacked various critical targets in the city, submerging its skyscrapers under fire and smoke. In the face of this sudden war, the Jianghai Military District was caught off guard, disorganized, with both its command and combat systems paralyzed... The forces led by Jiang Yongliang had all their information sources cut off. Despite their fervor, they were powerless to change the situation—lacking both defensive capabilities and counterattack strength, they were entirely at a disadvantage. In his anger, Jiang Yongliang led his troops to jump into the sea below the cliffs...

This was a nightmare experienced by Colonel Jiang Yongliang, a senior researcher at the Operations Research Office of the Jianghai Military District. Recently, Jiang Yongliang has been focusing on research regarding new military transformations. Although it was just a dream, it expressed Jiang Yongliang's anxious state of mind. As an operations researcher, facing the globally emerging new military transformation and the increasingly imminent military conflict situation, Jiang Yongliang clearly felt the gap in reality. He urgently realized that the current phase of the military in many aspects could not fully meet the requirements of the new military transformation. Meanwhile, the urgent military struggle situation demanded that the military quickly complete the transition from mechanization and semi-mechanization to informatization, achieving a new military transformation with Chinese characteristics. Only by completing this transformation would it be possible to achieve victory in future military conflicts. How to realize a new military transformation with Chinese characteristics, how to effectively enhance the combat effectiveness of the army through this transformation, and prepare to face military conflicts, are currently the most pressing concerns for everyone in the Jianghai Military District.

Despite differing thoughts and perspectives, everyone has recognized that this new military transformation is an urgent task for our military. It relates to the future unification and strength of the country and the prosperity of the nation, entering another crucial period in China's defense modernization construction.

The wave of new military transformation swept across the Jianghai Military District, and the call to prepare for war echoed throughout the barracks. During the annual training results assessment, the Jianghai Military District reformed the previous "segmented" evaluation methods, incorporating the training assessments of all district troops into a system akin to joint operations under an informatized background, naming it "Thunderbolt 04." The Chief of Staff of the Military District, Luo Huaizhong, hoped that through this reform, the troops would strengthen their research on new military transformations, quickly familiarize themselves with the characteristics of joint operations, and promote the informatization construction of the troops. This new type of evaluation method placed high demands on the troops. Time waits for no one, and all units of the Jianghai Military District took this year-end military training evaluation very seriously. Guo Changqing, the political commissar of the Phoenix Mountain Training Base, earnestly hoped that Jiang Yongliang would visit the Phoenix Mountain Base to provide relevant consulting opinions.

The Phoenix Mountain Training Base was newly formed based on the 8th Army Division and was directly commanded by the Jianghai Military District headquarters. The base was tasked with leading educational and training reforms and had achieved many results in the past two years, making significant progress in both software and hardware construction. They were determined to achieve the best results in this evaluation. Inviting Jiang Yongliang beforehand was also due to this reason. Jiang Yongliang was once an outstanding officer of the 8th Division and was a good friend with several current leaders of the base—acting commander Tang Xiangfeng, political commissar Guo Changqing, and National Defense University instructor Cao Tianxin who was temporarily stationed at the base. After obtaining a master’s degree at the National Defense University, Jiang Yongliang has been dedicated to researching and writing about new military transformations, enjoying high prestige within the military district.

At this time, the Phoenix Mountain Base was bustling with activity as preparations for “Thunderbolt 04” were fully underway. Political Commissar Guo Changqing meticulously organized a highly ceremonial mobilization and oath-taking meeting, inviting many key figures from the agency and military media to attend this event. However, his words often sounded familiar. Xu Guanghui, the base's training and operations staff officer, was unimpressed by his speeches in various settings, dismissing them as clichés and empty talk without any originality. Xu Guanghui believed that all the operations currently overseen by Guo Changqing and his team had yet to cross the threshold into the information age. By comparing NATO's publicity on military actions with Guo Changqing's set of practices, the latter appeared quite laughable. Xu Guanghui wrote down his views in a written report. After seeing this report, Guo Changqing became extremely angry.

The U.S. and UK will hold a large-scale military exercise called "Pacific 3" in the Pacific Ocean, inviting Chinese military personnel to observe. Jiang Yongliang has already been selected as a member of the delegation. At this point, news about this exercise has flooded the media. Jiang Yongliang paid close attention to these news reports, recognizing that this was already part of the psychological warfare before the actual war. Luo Huaizhong requested that Jiang Yongliang focus on studying the issue of psychological warfare.

Jiang Yongliang was invited to Haiwang Island, the No. 1 training camp of the Phoenix Mountain Training Base. He was warmly received by the base leadership. When the military helicopter carrying Jiang Yongliang landed on Haiwang Island, a civilian helicopter carrying Tian Menghong, the senior business manager of Yellow River Group, also arrived simultaneously — a cargo ship of the Yellow River Group had encountered an accident near Haiwang Island, carrying thousands of sets of automobile circuit boards. Tian Menghong came to handle this incident. Surprisingly, Tian Menghong discovered that the man standing before her was her drillmaster during university military training. Years ago, this handsome and upright young officer had made her heart race. With the help of the soldiers and Jiang Yongliang, Tian Menghong successfully towed the cargo ship to the Haiwang Island dock and unloaded the valuable goods into a small warehouse by the dock. Tian Menghong's radiant appearance and her intelligence and efficiency left a deep impression on Jiang Yongliang.

Years ago, Jiang Yongliang's fiancée, Song Yujie, a meteorological technician of the 8th Division, was swept into the sea by strong winds while on duty and went missing ever since. Because of this, Jiang Yongliang subconsciously harbored a sense of indifference and hatred towards the ocean. Although the 8th Division had long built a memorial pavilion by the seaside, Jiang Yongliang refused to believe that Song Yujie had sacrificed herself. Every time he was on leave, he would take a small boat to wander around nearby islands, trying to find Song Yujie's traces. This behavior continued for many years until he left the 8th Division. Standing by the sea and gazing at the distant ocean, Jiang Yongliang's eyes looked lost and melancholy. This became a concern for his comrades-in-arms, who collectively believed that only a new wave of affection could wash away the sorrow in Jiang Yongliang's heart. A group of pirates had long been eyeing the goods of the Yellow River Group. In the evening, they drove fast boats, intending to sweep all the goods on the dock clean. Just as the pirates landed, they encountered Jiang Yongliang. In this unexpected event, Jiang Yongliang demonstrated excellent personal military qualities, playing an important role in annihilating the group of pirates. Watching the accomplished Jiang Yongliang, Tian Menghong looked at him with affectionate eyes. Faced with Tian Menghong's enthusiasm, Jiang Yongliang adopted an evasive attitude.

After carefully studying the relevant plans formulated by the Phoenix Mountain Base, Jiang Yongliang felt that these plans still followed traditional operational routes, lacking bold imagination and innovative spirit. After Jiang Yongliang raised his opinion, Tang Xiangfeng, Cao Tianxin, and others also agreed. But Guo Changqing insisted on his own views, and Tang Xiangfeng did not intend to argue with Guo Changqing. Under the pressure from Guo Changqing, Tang Xiangfeng had to take compromise measures, preparing to make minor adjustments to the plan and attempting to persuade Jiang Yongliang. Jiang Yongliang, characterized by his straightforwardness, passion, and a strong sense of responsibility and mission, raised sharp criticisms. To some extent, there was opposition between the leaders of the base and Jiang Yongliang. This put Jiang Yongliang in an awkward position. During this process, Xu Guanghui, the base's training and operations staff officer, repeatedly proposed views similar to Jiang Yongliang's and angered Guo Changqing in the debate. Guo Changqing suspected Jiang Yongliang's attitude towards the Phoenix Mountain Base, believing that Jiang Yongliang might have personal motives involved. In Guo Changqing's view, the Phoenix Mountain Base was the pride of the Jianghai Military District, and its special status meant that its commanding officers would naturally attract the attention of the agency and higher-ups. In Guo Changqing's heart, the Phoenix Mountain Base had become his private domain, allowing no interference from others. Moreover, he did not want the achievements of the Phoenix Mountain Base to become the result of someone else's guidance. Guo Changqing had held the rank of full division for three years and felt confident. He believed that as long as he could maintain the momentum of the Phoenix Mountain Base in the next one or two years, promotion should not be a problem. Guo Changqing knew that Chief of Staff Luo Huaizhong had a particularly high regard for Jiang Yongliang and maintained an unusual friendship with him. Guo Changqing's original intention was to invite Jiang Yongliang to visit the base, receive high-level courtesies, and then speak well of the base to influence Luo Huaizhong, laying groundwork for the base to achieve excellent results. However, the actual situation was developing completely in the opposite direction, forcing the frustrated Guo Changqing to take other remedial measures immediately.
