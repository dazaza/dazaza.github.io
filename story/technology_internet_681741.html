The eight prohibitions are publicized, and parents worry that it's harder to choose schools for the entrance examination of junior high school

by edurqusr on 2012-02-28 15:12:25

On February 23, the Ministry of Education and two other ministries issued the "Eight Measures for the Governance of Irregular School Selection Fees in Compulsory Education" (hereinafter referred to as the "Eight Bans"), explicitly stopping the behavior of school selection admissions and charging fees, such as reducing the proportion of school selection to 10%. Qu Shaobing, Director of the Guangzhou Education Bureau, stated that Guangzhou would strictly control the proportion of school selection this year. This news caused a stir in society. According to reports by the journalist, although the joint examination form of private schools for junior high school entrance is not affected by the "Eight Bans", the two main pathways for junior high school entrance school selection: public school selection and private school joint examinations will be impacted by this new policy. Experts predict that if the policy is implemented effectively, almost all public school selections for junior high school entrance will be shut down this year, and the private school joint examinations will face the most intense competition in history.

Policy Interpretation

Who will supervise the donation-assisted education funds is still unclear

The "Eight Bans" on school selection issued by the Ministry of Education this time clearly defined governance goals and specific requirements, with significantly greater force than before (see detailed report on page A07 of our newspaper on February 25). According to investigations by the journalist, three clauses of the "Eight Bans" are most concerning for parents of students entering junior high school in Guangzhou: First, [to stop cross-district admissions and fee behaviors], which stipulates that [if abnormal cross-regional admissions exceed 10%, a special plan must be made to reduce it to less than 10% within three years]. Second, standardizing gifted student admissions and stopping the behavior of charging fees through admitting gifted students. Except for schools approved by provincial education administrative departments to admit sports and art gifted students, compulsory education schools shall not admit students under the name of gifted students. Schools are strictly prohibited from collecting any fees under the guise of admitting gifted students. Third, strictly prohibiting the collection of donation-assisted education funds linked to admission. Standardize the behavior of schools or educational administrative departments accepting donations from social organizations and individuals. When collecting donation funds, legally issue receipts. Local governments, relevant departments, and schools that violate regulations by collecting various fees related to enrollment and promotion shall have them resolutely refunded upon investigation. If unable to refund, they shall be confiscated into the national treasury, and relevant responsible persons shall be held accountable.

Regarding the "Eight Bans" that are widely concerned by parents of students entering junior high school, Xian Dezai, former researcher at the Yuexiu District Teaching Research Office and principal of the primary school department of Lishang Education, believes that from the content perspective, only one clause is new; this document mentions the definition of donation-assisted education funds for the first time, but it may not directly affect school selection for junior high school entrance. Xian Dezai said: [The key issue is who will supervise? Who will enforce? If enforcement and supervision are not determined, even if the donation-assisted education funds are clarified, in the current reality of uneven distribution of quality resources, the behavior of school selection through donation-assisted education will still be difficult to effectively control.]

Xian Dezai also analyzed that the government issues documents about the other seven clauses every year, just that previously they were [scattered] announcements, while this year's "Eight Bans" systematically regulated a series of problems regarding school selection in compulsory education.

Policy Impact

Various districts may successively halt public school selection exams

Regarding the control of school selection ratios mentioned in the "Eight Bans," many parents worry that it will significantly impact the public school selection for junior high school entrance this year. In response to this, Feng Yongzhen, principal of the Guangzhou branch of Xueda Education, analyzed that controlling the school selection ratio to 10% indeed makes the public school selection for junior high school entrance this year very tense.

Especially when Education Bureau Director Qu Shaobing recently mentioned: [Guangzhou will strictly control the school selection ratio this year, while increasing quality seats and improving teacher quality to enable children to attend nearby schools. Additionally, Guangzhou will establish 'Responsibility Zone Supervisors' in various districts this year to strictly regulate admissions.] Feng Yongzhen said that some areas in Guangzhou urgently halted public school selection exams in June last year. It is expected that under the new policy, various regions will increase their monitoring of selection exams, and there will be successive announcements halting selection exams in schools or regions.

Private school selection exam competition will become more intense

However, Xian Dezai believes that everyone needs to pay attention to one issue here. Although the "Eight Bans" indeed mention controlling the school selection ratio and strictly controlling it below 10% within three years, and Guangzhou's current school selection ratio has decreased from 30% two years ago to 20% last year, with this year's goal being controlled at 10%; however, throughout the entire content, more emphasis is placed on the requirements and control of elementary and high school selection ratios, without specifying the definition of junior high school selection ratios. In previous policy documents for junior high school entrance, there has never been a [school selection ratio] control, with clear annual regulations stating that school selection is not allowed during junior high school entrance in the compulsory education stage, allowing only two paths: computer allocation and direct promotion.

He said that if this "Eight Bans" includes junior high school entrance and the policy is fully implemented, there could be three possibilities this year for junior high school entrance: First, if relevant departments take it seriously and immediately halt public school selection exams, but enforcement remains insufficient, the methods of public school selection will become more covert. For parents, the inequality of information will worsen, and [directly selected students] may become the common choice for many regions to [select quality students]. It is understood that so-called [directly selected students] do not require exams, with middle schools directly contacting primary schools, and primary schools directly recommending students to middle schools, but whether all parents can obtain this method. Last year, certain regions operated this method covertly behind the scenes.

Second, if relevant departments implement this new policy very thoroughly, then the parents of junior high school entrance this year will continue to be troubled, and [middle-class] parents will be even more troubled this year. Because if the policy is implemented thoroughly, there will be basically no opportunity for public school selection this year, and extraplan recruitment by public schools will also be basically impossible.

Finally, affected by this, the competition for private school selection will become even more intense. Regarding this, Cai Zhi, executive vice-principal of Tianhe Branch of Provincial Experimental Middle School, stated that if the public school selection channel really closes this year, the impact on junior high school entrance parents will be more psychological, possibly affecting more sources competing for private schools. However, he also indicated that even without this new policy, the number of applicants to private schools has been increasing dramatically every year. The number of applicants was over 3,000 in 2009, reached over 4,000 in 2010, and set a new record last year with over 6,000 people, while the school's招生 capacity has reached its maximum limit, making competition between candidates increasingly fierce.

Specialty students' chances of entering prestigious schools may decrease

As one of the "eight pathways" for junior high school entrance in Guangzhou, specialty students have become a [convenient shortcut] for many [middle-class] students to enter quality seats. It is understood that according to the opinions published by the Guangzhou Education Bureau last year on the recruitment work of physical education and artistic specialty students for the 2012 "junior high school entrance", in addition to traditional specialty student projects, intangible cultural heritage art projects such as Peking Opera, Cantonese Opera, Guqin, and paper-cutting have also entered the scope of specialty student projects. However, since the qualification recognition for registering as a physical education or art specialty student mainly depends on the awards received in competitions organized by educational and sports administrative departments, but due to the lack of related competitions for intangible cultural heritage art projects such as Peking Opera, Cantonese Opera, Guqin, and paper-cutting, these can be recognized after personal application and school recommendation, with the district (county-level city) education bureau conducting qualification certification. Therefore, this has become a specialty student pathway where many public schools and [middle-class] parents can exploit.

Regarding this year's new policy stipulating that [except for schools approved by provincial educational administrative departments to recruit physical education and art specialty students, compulsory education schools shall not recruit students under the name of specialty students], Zhang Mom from Panyu District told reporters: [Doesn't that mean my child started learning Guqin last year for nothing?]

In response to this, Yun Ziting, director of the New Oriental Bubble Children's Education Center, believed that the opinions on the recruitment of physical education and art specialty students for junior high school entrance in Guangzhou this year have not yet been released, so it cannot be determined whether the newly added specialty student projects from last year will be canceled, but if the "Eight Bans" are strictly enforced, some channels for specialty students to enter prestigious schools may narrow, and the proportion of specialty student recruitment will also be more strictly controlled.

However, some experts also pointed out that although the education department has always had regulations on specialty student recruitment, apart from traditional sports and art projects, including intangible cultural heritage art projects, most are used by public schools to recruit quality students. Many schools still look at students' Olympiad math or English [http://edu.qq.com/en/] (forum [http://bbs.edu.qq.com/b-1000090390]) specialties as additional admission conditions.

Private school admission score lines [http://data.edu.qq.com/college/] may rise

Recently, some private junior high school principals in Guangzhou interviewed by reporters stated that the "Eight Bans" currently do not affect the school's admission work, but it is not ruled out that the number of applicants this year may set a new record, and the school's admission score line may rise. Among them, Cai Zhi, executive vice-principal of Tianhe Branch of Provincial Experimental Middle School, believed that it is still not the enrollment period, so it is not yet known what impact it will have, but judging from the school's admission situation in previous years, due to an annual increase of thousands in the number of applicants, the school's admission numbers basically remain unchanged, leading to an annual rise in the admission score line. It is introduced that the school's admission line was 240 points in 2010, 250 points in 2011, and this year's test difficulty will not change much. However, if there is another increase in the number of applicants, the score line may fluctuate. It is understood that the school's preliminary recruitment plan for this year is 650 people (for details, see the recruitment brochure released at the end of April).
