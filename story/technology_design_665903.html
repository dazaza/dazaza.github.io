Don't take the "soft landing" of the real estate market as a signal to prop up housing prices

by djegskkgfeb6 on 2012-02-15 11:17:39

Don't take the "soft landing" of the real estate market as a signal to prop up housing prices - Market Observation - Industry News - Building Materials Network. Almost overnight, the real estate market has been strongly influenced by a "moderate wind." Advocates have plenty of evidence, some from the macro level and others from the government level. The most obvious evidence is the increasingly "soft" local real estate details. Although the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) has clarified the misinterpreted statements, it cannot stop the enthusiasm of the advocates.

Is the real estate market really going to "softly land"? For this, I interviewed many experts, but their conclusions are opposite to those of the "moderate theorists." They believe that the central government's policies will not stop here. If housing prices continue to be out of control, the government may introduce more combined measures.

The Exploited Macro Uncertainty

On May 24th, Spanish matador Apazio was pierced through the jaw by a bull, exiting through his mouth, creating a terrifying image. But for observers, the more terrifying news was the Spanish Central Bank taking over the large regional bank Cajasur. Observers believe this is a precursor to the European economy entering a crisis.

Meanwhile, due to speculation by hot money and climate reasons, there has been a noticeable increase in the price of agricultural by-products in China. Inflation expectations are coming on strong, forcing the NDRC to issue three documents about prices within ten days.

By this point, the experience of a European bank and the fact that some agricultural product prices in China have risen have been overly exaggerated as "ironclad evidence" of macro uncertainty at home and abroad. The story of Cajasur and perceptible price fluctuations, after being deliberately amplified, have been used as arguments for the "gradual moderation of real estate regulation."

Business Department Deputy Director Bai Ming of the Ministry of Commerce's International Trade Economic Cooperation Research Institute personally expressed to me that the basis for arguing that "real estate policies will gradually moderate" is too far-fetched. He believes that the purpose of the national real estate regulation policy is to suppress the excessively rapid rise in housing prices, reduce the rate of increase, and stabilize its healthy development. "Of course, China must pay attention to the current economic situation in Europe." He believes that the inflation pressure in China is not serious, at most possibly only at the top of mild inflation.

The Amplified Untrue Rumors

Who is deliberately amplifying these messages? Behind this round of twists and turns in the "three-year exemption from property tax" rumors, they revealed their tails.

Even though on the 24th, the NDRC had already made a solemn clarification regarding the false information about the "three-year exemption from property tax," at 17:30 that afternoon, I still received such a text message on my mobile phone: "NDRC officials say that the property tax will not be introduced within three years, denying that there will be any stricter policies, because inflation expectations and macroeconomic recovery are not significant. High-level central government officials have even stated that the government's regulation of the real estate market has encountered a dilemma, and in the short term, there are no new policies for regulating the real estate market."

After verification, I found that this information originated from a real estate intermediary agency. And the sender claimed that they were unaware that the official had already clarified the facts.

Real estate intermediaries generate income from transactions between buyers and sellers of second-hand homes. The significant decline in second-hand home transaction volumes in first-tier cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen may have already left these young brokers unable to sustain themselves. Therefore, when the untrue rumor that the real estate tax would not be levied within three years spread, the urgent mentality of relevant institutions in quickly passing on the information to clients becomes understandable.

It can be affirmed that there are quite a few others who share their "common aspirations."

Misinterpretation of the "Moderate" Second-Tier Real Estate Market

Since May 17th, after the "Ten Measures" had been in place for a month, both buyers and sellers in the real estate market have remained in a state of observation. After the Beijing details, full of a chilling atmosphere, were released, the details of other first-tier cities did not include exciting "restrictions and loan restrictions." Thus, they were labeled as "gentle" by observers.

"The 24 Clauses of Guangzhou" focus on breakthroughs in solving the housing difficulties of the "sandwich class"; the Chongqing details issued on the same day were seen as a "Guangzhou replica" and also did not halt loans for third homes, showing a relatively gentle attitude of having both preservation and suppression.

In Sun Fei's view, the chairman of Guojù Fund Investment Management Co., Ltd., and chief economist, the reasonableness of the local "gentle" attitude lies solely in: "Local policies formulate differentiated details based on the local real estate situation." He believes that the gentle attitude of second-tier cities can be understood.

Sun Fei believes that the root cause of the gentle attitude of some first-tier cities lies in the interest-driven "land finance." Although the "Ten Measures" are merely framework texts, they have had noticeable effects after implementation. Yang Hongxu, director of the Comprehensive Research Department of Shanghai E-House Real Estate Research Institute, also believes: "The fall in housing prices in first-tier cities has become inevitable. What is needed for the overall decline in new home prices is just time. And this time requires only 2-3 months." Data from the China Index Academy shows that from the analysis of the transaction overview of the past week (May 17th - May 23rd), the effects of the new policies continue, and the real estate market transaction volume continues to operate at low levels.

Sun Fei also said that if the housing prices in key regulated cities do not fall, then the government may introduce more combined policies.
