Today's early report: Financial crisis prompts surge in "online accountants"

by fenqu on 2009-09-07 12:46:06

"You ask what aspect of my life has been most affected by the financial crisis? It's that over these past few months, I've become increasingly meticulous in managing my daily expenses. 4 yuan for transportation, 25 yuan for meals, 3 yuan for communication fees... Nowadays, I keep track of my accounts every day, but not on a notebook. Instead, I record them through an online method. This not only teaches me how to manage my finances but also fosters a healthy and positive lifestyle." Xiao Wu, who graduated from university just three years ago and works at a bank, highly praised this new way of keeping online accounts.

Under the backdrop of the financial crisis, the group of "accountants" is on the rise.

As living costs continue to rise, especially with the global financial crisis still lingering, more and more "moonlight goddesses" (those who spend all their monthly income) and "card slaves" (those heavily indebted on credit cards) have realized they should no longer live carefree lives where they spend all their money before running out. Instead, they need to sort out their personal income and expenses to both increase revenue and cut costs. The recent boom in the stock market and real estate has made them even more aware of the importance of good financial management, prompting many to start keeping accounts online.

In this way, online accounting networks have become popular, and "Have you kept your accounts online today?" has gradually become a trend. According to Mou Mingxing, CEO of China’s largest online accounting website Caike Online, the site currently has 450,000 users, most of whom were added during the financial crisis. Their ages are generally concentrated between 25-35 years old, with most having a monthly salary ranging from 2,000 to 20,000 yuan. Among them are many students and newly graduated college students. Zhejiang users account for nearly one-third of the total, and there are even large accounts with millions of yuan flowing each month.

No longer needing to carry or flip through notebooks, nor perform tedious calculations using calculators, this revolutionary online accounting method has been widely welcomed by netizens since its introduction. "Previously, I would use a notebook to record my expenses, but now it's much simpler," netizen DANGYUHUA8 praised enthusiastically about online bookkeeping. With the rise of online accounting websites like Caike Online, these enthusiastic netizens who enjoy online bookkeeping have earned themselves a trendy nickname - "Zhangke" (Accountant).

Big questions lie within small account books

"Previously, I was spending around 700 yuan unknowingly each month on drinks. After consciously saving through online bookkeeping, I managed to control my monthly drink expenses to 200 yuan, effectively cutting down unnecessary expenditures," said "Zhangke" Yu Qian.

Through Caike Online, journalists saw that apart from basic daily entries, liabilities, asset status, and network accounting content such as stocks and funds, one can also manage personal membership cards, bus cards, recharge cards, etc., via this website. Various incomes and expenditures can be displayed graphically through bar charts or pie charts, making it clearer and more understandable than recording on a notebook. Additionally, the website aims to improve customers' financial intelligence by hosting offline events like "Hundred Yuan Week" and promptly publishing articles, collections, and data related to finance online for "Zhangyou" (Account friends) to learn and refer to.

Many "Zhangyou" share their financial management insights and stories through the network, allowing extensive exchanges among numerous "Zhangyou". According to Mou Mingxing, a loyal user from Wenzhou named "Wuyu" won a 450,000 yuan lottery prize in late June. Many "Zhangyou" asked curiously, "How did you buy it?" "Wuyu" does not recommend blindly buying lotteries. He explained that he spent 350,000 yuan on lottery tickets and won 450,000 yuan, leaving him with almost no profit after taxes...

Besides these daily financial functions, Caike Online also offers special features such as sub-user permission allocation, data export, and automatic bookkeeping.

Improving financial intelligence is key

After the emergence of online bookkeeping, financial professionals in the industry gave it their approval. Yu Dongsheng, director of the Wealth Management Center of the Agricultural Bank of China Zhejiang Branch, stated that if one spends 5-10 minutes each day on online bookkeeping, summarizing at the end of the month and asking "why" can help better arrange next month's expenses: "The initial stage of financial management involves increasing income and reducing expenses to have sufficient funds for financial planning."

It is reported that when the first version of "Caike Online" was launched, online bookkeeping websites were still rare in China. In just over a year, more than 100 online bookkeeping websites sprang up like mushrooms after rain. However, after being tested by the market, some websites have closed down, leaving only dozens still operational. "Therefore, although more and more people are joining the 'Zhangke' ranks, it is best to choose a more stable website. The chosen website should ideally have 24-hour monitoring to ensure data security and prevent data loss," warned Mou Mingxing regarding the prevalent hackers on the internet.

Moreover, Mou Mingxing emphasized that merely knowing the direction of each fund flow is insufficient: "Financial management mainly revolves around increasing revenue and cutting costs. Cutting costs requires diligent bookkeeping, while increasing revenue necessitates enhancing personal financial intelligence to achieve financial freedom sooner. In today's society, relying solely on cutting costs is not enough; one must also seek ways to increase revenue. This requires netizens to enhance their financial intelligence through various methods to better adapt to societal development."
