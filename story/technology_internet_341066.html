The best sleeping position for pregnant women

by caiqiao122 on 2010-04-07 10:44:41

The little life in the belly of a soon-to-be mom is the cherished baby of the young couple. How to conceive a healthy and smart baby must be filled with all sorts of questions for the expectant father and mother: Is the baby in my belly healthy? What should a new mom pay attention to? How should the dad take care of the expectant mom...

1. Lying on the left side is most suitable for pregnant women.

Ms. Feng asked: After becoming pregnant, my increasingly large belly makes it uncomfortable for me to sleep. I used to sleep on my back, but now it's obviously no longer possible. May I ask the expert, what’s the best way for pregnant women to sleep?

Expert suggestion: The most suitable position for pregnant women is lying on their left side. This is because sleeping on the left can make the right-rotated uterus rotate to the left, reducing the pressure of the pregnant uterus on the artery, making it less likely for pregnant women to experience leg swelling, varicose veins in the legs, and poor fetal development.

Pregnant women should not sleep on their backs because doing so will let the enlarged and heavier uterus press on the abdominal artery, affecting the blood supply to the uterus and placenta. The ischemic placenta releases a large amount of renin, which enters the maternal bloodstream and can lead to increased arterial pressure, causing or worsening pregnancy-induced hypertension. If the pregnant woman sleeps on her right side, it will make the already right-rotated uterus rotate even more to the right. The blood vessels that provide nutrients to the uterus are pulled, affecting the oxygen supply to the fetus, possibly causing chronic poisoning in the fetus. In severe cases, it could even cause the fetus to suffocate.

2. Leg swelling during pregnancy is normal.

Guangmei asked: Not long after I became pregnant, my legs started to swell. Why is that?

Expert suggestion: Leg swelling during pregnancy is normal. First, after becoming pregnant, the amount of blood flowing from the pelvic cavity to the leg veins increases, while the enlarged uterus also presses on the leg veins, obstructing the return of blood from the lower body and legs. This causes an increase in venous pressure in the legs, leading to swelling in the calves. Second, the total blood volume of pregnant women increases, and the total plasma protein is lower than that of normal people. Third, the endocrine function of pregnant women undergoes a series of changes, with increased secretion of adrenal cortical hormones, antidiuretic hormones, estrogen, etc., enhancing the reabsorption of sodium by the renal tubules, resulting in water and sodium retention in the body, causing edema.

3. Do not hold urine when experiencing frequent urination in early and late pregnancy.

Xiaoru asked: After becoming pregnant, I experienced frequent urination, often feeling like I need to go to the toilet, always having the feeling of incomplete urination. Doctor, is this normal?

Expert suggestion: Frequent urination and the feeling of incomplete urination are common in pregnant women during the early and late stages of pregnancy. This is caused by the expanded uterus pressing on the bladder due to pregnancy. During the middle stage of pregnancy, the uterus rises, relieving the pressure on the bladder, and the phenomenon of frequent urination disappears. However, during the late stage of pregnancy, the baby's head descending into the pelvis will press on the bladder, so the phenomenon of frequent urination reappears. Therefore, the fluctuation of frequent urination in early pregnancy, no frequent urination in mid-pregnancy, and frequent urination again in late pregnancy is normal, but never hold your urine. If you notice cloudy urine or pain during urination, seek medical attention promptly.

4. What should be noted when carrying triplets?

"Devotion Zeng" email asked: Hello doctor, my wife went for a check-up on March 1st, and the doctor said she is carrying triplets. I don't know what I should pay attention to. Can you tell me?

Expert suggestion: First of all, congratulations to you. At the same time, it is suggested that your wife should reduce physical activity, avoid lifting heavy objects, and appropriately supplement folic acid, taking prenatal medication as prescribed by the doctor. She should also pay attention to strengthening nutrition and moderate sexual activity to avoid threatened abortion. It is also recommended that you closely monitor the development status of the triplets and conduct regular monitoring. Considering the relatively low survival rate of triplets, extra care is needed for both the pregnant woman and the babies during pregnancy.
