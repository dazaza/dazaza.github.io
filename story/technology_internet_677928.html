Zaanse Schans is the name of an open-air museum located in the Zaanstad municipality, which comprises Zaandam, Koog aan de Zaan, and Zaandijk. This area, surrounding the Zaan River, started as a hub for both the timber and shipbuilding industries. Since the 16th century, windmills have been crucial in providing power locally. The prosperous shipbuilding industry not only attracted Tsar Peter the Great to visit and learn but also spurred the development of flour, paint, mustard, and other commercial sectors. Due to the demand for power, over a thousand windmills were built here within two centuries.

&；nbsp；&；nbsp；&；nbsp； As time progressed, when windmills faced the dilemma of losing their function, the Zaanse Schans Open-Air Museum came into being. Various uniquely shaped windmills and green wooden houses with pointed or bell-shaped gables were gathered here for joint display. Visitors can learn about the different structures of Dutch windmills based on various commercial needs and explore the vibrant daily life of ancestors through exhibits at the Clock Museum, Bakery Museum, Cheese Farm, Albert Heijn Grocery Store, and Wooden Shoe Factory. The vivid displays allow you to easily immerse yourself in memories of past prosperity and are definitely worth recommending!
