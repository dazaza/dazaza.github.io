The Sorbs, the only minority in Germany, celebrate Jesus' resurrection in the form of a hundred-ride chorus. One by one, the Sorbs, dressed in black shirts and wearing black hats, ride on horses decorated with ribbons, flowers, and white shells, marching in great numbers along forest paths. As they walk, they sing hymns in deep, robust voices, creating an extremely grand scene.

On Easter Day, people light candles in front of the church to sanctify and welcome the holy candle into every household. On this day, the happiest thing for children is to deliver the holy fire to each house. They light branches with the holy fire in front of the church, then run to deliver it to every household, filled with a joyful holiday atmosphere. In Bavaria, Germany, residents hold torch races every Easter to celebrate Jesus' rebirth. The rolling firewheel of Luetzel Easter in North Rhine-Westphalia is also well-known near and far. Six giant wooden wheels are set on fire and roll down the valley, like six fireballs descending from the sky. The pitch-black valley is illuminated by the fiery wheels, which intertwine beautifully with the colorful fireworks, once again showing that fire has brought new life to humanity.

In the West, Easter is more orthodox, and typical Easter gifts are related to spring and rebirth: eggs, chicks, rabbits, flowers, especially lilies, which are symbols of this season. On the eve of Easter, children color and decorate eggs for friends and family. These eggs are either hard-boiled or just empty shells. On Easter morning, children will find their Easter baskets filled with chocolate eggs, Easter bunnies, fluffy chicks, and doll toys at the foot of their beds. It's said that the Easter Bunny hides the colored eggs indoors or in the grass for children to find. The annual White House egg-rolling event in the United States is often broadcast live on television.

In most Western countries, there are usually grand religious parades during Easter. Parade participants wear long robes, carry crosses, and proceed barefoot. They dress up as characters from Christian history, singing hymns to celebrate Jesus' resurrection. Nowadays, holiday parades have lost much of their former strong religious color. The festive parade exudes a celebratory atmosphere, with strong folk characteristics and regional features. In the United States, the parade includes clowns in cowboy costumes walking on stilts, as well as lively cartoon characters like Mickey Mouse. In the UK, parades focus more on introducing local history and customs, with participants dressing up as Scottish bagpipe bands and palace guards, attracting many tourists. The arrival of Easter also prompts people to change into new clothes. In the past, Christian believers would go to church for baptism before the festival, then put on their new robes to celebrate Christ's new birth. The custom of wearing new attire has been retained to this day because people believe that not wearing new clothes during the festival brings bad luck. During Easter, people also enjoy thoroughly cleaning their homes, symbolizing the start of a new life.

The German government stipulates two days off for Easter. During the festival, families reunite to taste various traditional foods, and relatives and friends greet each other upon meeting.

Symbols of life such as eggs, fire, water, and rabbits have become auspicious signs of Easter. Eggs and rabbits in the West symbolize new life and prosperity. The original color of eggs symbolizes the sun, while dyeing eggs red symbolizes happiness in life. During Easter, parents specially prepare chocolates shaped like eggs and rabbits for their children. Friends and relatives exchange colored eggs. In some towns in the middle Rhine region and eastern Hesse, the ancient custom of "colored egg trees" is still preserved. People paint hundreds of eggshells and string them into chains, hanging them on pine trees on Easter day to make colored egg trees. Adults and children sing songs and dance around the colored egg tree to celebrate Easter. Meanwhile, young women in the Alps express their love through the gift of red eggs. If a girl gives three red eggs to a certain young man on Easter day, it means the girl is expressing her love for the young man.

There is a legend about why rabbits have become auspicious signs of Easter.

In ancient times, the day of Jesus' resurrection coincided with the "Spring Sun Festival" celebrated by residents of Scandinavia, where rabbits, one of the most prolific animals in the grasslands and forests, symbolized the revival of spring and the birth of new life. At the same time, it was the pet of the goddess of love Aphrodite and the candle-bearer for the Germanic earth goddess Holle. Therefore, now rabbits, as messengers delivering Easter eggs to children, are deeply loved by children.

Fire has not only brought light to humanity but also renewed the earth. As a symbol of Jesus' rebirth, many Easter activities are related to fire.
