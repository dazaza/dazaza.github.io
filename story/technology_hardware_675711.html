Maintenance methods for different stages of a car

by malayo on 2012-02-25 11:42:58

Although cars are made of steel and do not have flesh and blood, their lifespan and condition are roughly similar to humans, also having a life cycle. Cars in different periods have significant differences in their condition. The lifecycle of a car can be roughly divided into five stages. In these five stages, the focus on maintenance and repair is also different, and car owners can take corresponding maintenance measures based on the stage of their car's life.

Stage One: Peak Stage - One to Two Years

In the first two years, the car is at its peak period, which can be considered the best time for the car, equivalent to a person's youth. During this period, people are full of passion, energetic, daring to take risks, with seemingly endless energy, as if they never get tired. Similarly, the car during this period is in excellent condition. It only requires routine maintenance and generally won't have any problems. However, after two years, some parts that wear out easily need to be replaced. But some cars of poor quality can only last about a year. Therefore, manufacturers only offer a one-year warranty because new cars generally don't have major quality issues within the first year.

Stage Two: Fatigue Stage - Two to Four Years

After being used for two to four years, the car begins to enter the fatigue stage. It no longer has the same vitality and energy as before. During this period, the car frequently needs some parts replaced and its energy replenished. Here are a few examples for reference:

Brake pads: After using the car for two or three years, it may be necessary to replace the brake pads. When a car is sent for repair, the brake pads should be checked first because they are the first line of defense for driving safety. Based on experience, brake pads usually wear out after about two or three years, though the exact usage period depends on the driver's habits. If you're the type who likes to press the accelerator heavily and then suddenly slam the brakes, the brake pads will wear out more quickly and have a shorter lifespan.

Fuel pump: After driving the car for about two years, the fuel pump might need cleaning or replacement.

Shock absorbers: When the car has been used for three or four years, it may not run as smoothly as a new car. In such cases, the shock absorbers should be checked. The function of the shock absorbers is to reduce the vibration of the car; they are part of the suspension system. If the springs in the shock absorbers lose their elasticity, the car will shake violently at high speeds, especially dangerous during rainy weather.

Stage Three: Middle-Aged Stage - Four to Six Years

Cars that have been used for four to six years enter the middle-aged stage. Just like people entering middle age who tend to have mood swings, cars in this stage often encounter various problems. This is because many components of the car begin to age, leading to occasional oil leaks, water leaks, and numerous small issues. However, if the car is well-maintained and still looks fresh and smooth from the outside, it retains some charm. Some savvy car owners choose to sell their cars during this period, as they can fetch a good price while passing on potential future problems to others.
