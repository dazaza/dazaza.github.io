[Working Principle]

Firstly, understand the working principle of the scale/vehicle scale: The vehicle scale/scale mainly consists of components such as the load-bearing platform, weighing display instrument, weighing sensor (resistance strain type), connecting parts, limit device, and junction box, as well as optional external equipment like printers, large screen displays, computers, and voltage stabilizers. When the object to be weighed or the loaded vehicle is placed on the load-bearing platform, under the force of gravity, the weight is transmitted to the weighing sensor through the load-bearing platform, causing deformation of the elastic body of the sensor. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance, outputting an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. Then, after A/D conversion into a digital signal, the microprocessor (CPU) of the instrument processes the weight signal and directly displays the weight data. With a printer configured, the weighing data can be printed, and if a computer is configured, the measurement data can be entered into the computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it's a scale or a balance, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the object's weight into an electrical signal, which is sent to the conditioning, A/D, and computing circuits (MCU), and then converted into digital display. Generally, electronic scale interference devices mostly adopt the method of changing the zero point. Normally, the scale display remembers the size of the empty scale zero point during calibration. If the zero point changes, the display reading will change! Some also use the method of changing the gain, but all are done by adding or subtracting interference to the sensor signal, thereby achieving addition or subtraction on the weighing measuring equipment.
