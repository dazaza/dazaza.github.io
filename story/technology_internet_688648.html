Weibo's selection of the top ten beautiful lakes in domestic universities is popular among netizens

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-03 12:08:52

Chutian Metropolis Daily report (reporter Xu Xiaohan, correspondent Liu Mingyang, Zhang Chengzhi) - Recently, blog guru "Li Zichi" launched the "Top Ten Beautiful Lakes in Chinese Universities" on his blog and Weibo. Among them, Wuhan area lakes including East Lake, South Lake, and Tangxun Lake made it to the top ten. The list was reprinted by several well-known Weibo accounts with hundreds of thousands of followers, such as "University Fresh News," "@Happy Life Wuhan," and "Study Magazine." In just a few days, the total number of forwards and comments reached tens of thousands.

"Li Zichi," whose real name is Xiao Fei, graduated from the Chinese Department of Renmin University of China, taught at a university in the south for seven years, and then resigned to become a freelance writer in Beijing. In Li Zichi's blog post titled "Ranking the Top Ten Beautiful Lakes in Chinese Universities," he vividly displayed 16 beautiful lakes located within university campuses across the country. Among those ranked in the top ten were: Weiming Lake of Peking University, East Lake of Wuhan University, West Lake of China Academy of Art, Xiangsi Lake of Guangxi University for Nationalities, South Lake of South-Central Minzu University, Qiuzhong Lake of Huaqiao University, Furong Lake of Xiamen University, Wenshan Lake of Shenzhen University, Jiaohu Lake of Nanchang University, and Tangxun Lake of Wuchang Institute of Technology.

Yesterday, "Li Zichi" told reporters in a phone interview that he didn't expect this blog post to attract so much attention from netizens when he wrote it. Talking about this ranking, "Li Zichi" said that he has deep feelings for universities. He studied for four years and taught for seven years. Although he is now in Beijing, he still holds part-time positions in many universities. He previously published several books related to universities. During the writing process, "Li Zichi" realized that the lakes in universities bring vitality to the campuses, which inspired him to compile this list. As soon as the Weibo post "Ranking the Top Ten Beautiful Lakes in Chinese Universities" was released, it attracted attention from netizens. While praising the lakes in Chinese universities, netizens also expressed their pride in being students at these universities with the top ten beautiful lakes. A netizen from Wuchang Institute of Technology named "Jie-Jeanne" left a message online: "I am about to graduate, hoping my alma mater becomes more beautiful and academic."

In the midst of heated discussions among numerous netizens, the reporter found that an environment-friendly and picturesque campus is the desired place for many students to seek knowledge.

"Mountains and waters near universities can not only enhance the cultural atmosphere of the university but also increase the students' vitality. This is one of the important reasons why many universities at home and abroad like to be built near mountains or water bodies," said "Li Zichi." He added, "Hubei is known as the province of thousands of lakes, and Wuhan is called the 'city of hundreds of lakes.' Having three of the top ten lakes in Wuhan is reasonable."
