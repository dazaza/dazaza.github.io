Siemens Washing Machine: Summary of Repair Experience When Strange Odors Occur During Operation

When a washing machine is operating, if there are strange odors or burnt smells, it is necessary to carefully identify the type of odor and generally stop the machine for inspection.

1. Insulation oil smell. For a new motor (or new washing machine), when too many items are loaded for washing or the washing time is too long, the motor may run overloaded, resulting in higher temperature rise. If the motor's insulating paint has not dried completely, the appearance of an insulating paint smell is normal. After the washing machine has been used for some time, the insulating paint smell will disappear on its own.

2. Rubber smell. If the triangular belt of the washing machine is too loose, it can easily cause the belt to slip, generating a rubber smell due to wear; if the triangular belt is too tight, the belt may produce a rubber smell due to excessive friction. Adjusting the tension of the belt appropriately should resolve this issue.

3. Burnt smell. If a washing machine emits a burnt smell, it usually indicates that the motor, water inlet valve, drainage pump, or drainage electromagnet is experiencing excessively high temperature rise, or some electrical load components have been damaged.

The causes of the above faults include:

- Low power supply voltage. Motors and other components have specific requirements for their operating voltage range. If the voltage is too low while the load torque remains unchanged, the operating current will increase, leading to increased copper loss and causing excessively high temperatures in the motor. In such cases, the use of the washing machine should be paused or a voltage regulator used to raise the voltage.

- The motor or other components are obstructed and cannot operate. When the motor or other components are obstructed and cannot rotate, the current passing through them will greatly exceed the normal operating current, causing rapid temperature rise and producing a burnt smell. The machine should be stopped, and the cause investigated and faults resolved.

- Mechanical failure in the motor or other components. Increased mechanical friction will increase the workload on the motor, leading to excessively high temperatures. If a mechanical fault in the motor is identified, it should be repaired or replaced as necessary.

- Faults such as grounding or short circuits between turns in the windings of motor components. When these faults occur, the motor will produce a loud humming sound accompanied by a decrease in speed. If the operation continues for a longer period, it might first emit a burnt smell followed by smoke. Once a burnt smell from the washing machine is detected, the machine should be immediately stopped, the cause investigated, and the damaged components repaired or replaced.
