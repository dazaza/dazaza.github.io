Preface

A sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liner is a new type of geosynthetic material, mainly divided into needle-punched sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners, needle-punched and laminated sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners, and adhesive-bound sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners.

In this standard, Annex A and Annex B are normative annexes, with Annex A equivalently adopting the testing methods from ASTM D5084-1990 of the American Society for Testing and Materials.

This standard was proposed by the Standards Quota Research Institute of the Ministry of Construction.

This standard is under the jurisdiction of the Technical Standardization Unit for Architectural Engineering of the Ministry of Construction, which is the China Academy of Building Research.

This standard was primarily drafted by Beijing Boke Construction Chemical Material Co., Ltd., National Building Materials Testing Center, Jetek High Technology (Suzhou) Co., Ltd., Yixing Nonwoven Fabric Factory, Tianjin Kerun Huida Nonwoven Fabric Co., Ltd., Liaoning Set Waterproof Technology Development Co., Ltd., Metal & Chemistry Research Institute of China Railway Academy, R&D Center of China Materials Group, and Tianjin Xingrong Nonwoven Fabric Co., Ltd.

The main drafters of this standard are: Liu Haibo, Liu Jiwu, Zheng Yongya, Bao Zhihong, Xie Shiping, Tan Zhenpei, Zhu Hequan, Li Liguo, Lang Rongliang, Sun Yunrong.

Sodium Bentonite Geosynthetic Clay Liner

1 Scope

This standard specifies the product classification, specifications and codes, technical requirements and test methods, inspection rules, marking, packaging, storage and transportation of sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners.

This standard applies to waterproofing and seepage prevention projects in the fields of subways, tunnels, artificial lakes, landfills, airports, water conservancy, bridges, roads, buildings, etc., using sodium bentonite as the main raw material, produced by needle-punching, needle-punching and lamination, or adhesive bonding methods (referred to as GCL).

This standard does not apply to waterproofing and seepage prevention projects where there are high concentrations of electrolyte solutions.

3 Classification and Marking

3.1 Classification

3.1.1 Product Type Classification

3.1.1.1 Needle-punched sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liner, a blanket-like material formed by needle-punching sodium bentonite particles between two layers of geotextile, as shown in Figure 1(a). Denoted as GCL-NP.

3.1.1.2 Needle-punched and laminated sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liner, with a layer of high-density polyethylene film laminated onto the non-woven geotextile outer surface of a needle-punched sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liner, as shown in Figure 1(b). Denoted as GCL-OF.

3.1.1.3 Adhesive-bound sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liner, where sodium bentonite particles are bonded to a high-density polyethylene sheet using an adhesive, compressed to produce a sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liner, as shown in Figure 1(c). Denoted as GCL-AH.

4 Requirements

4.1 Raw Material Requirements

4.1.1 The bentonite used in the product should be either natural sodium-based bentonite or artificially sodified bentonite, with the mass of bentonite particles within the range of 0.2mm to 2mm accounting for at least 80% of the total bentonite mass.

4.1.2 The polyethylene geomembrane used in the product should comply with GB/T 17643, and other membrane materials should also meet the corresponding standard requirements.

4.1.3 The plastic weft-knitted geotextile used in the product should meet the requirements of GB/T 17690, preferably using a UV-resistant plastic weft-knitted geotextile with a unit area mass of 120g/m².

4.1.4 Preferably use a non-woven geotextile with a unit area mass of 220g/m².

4.2 Appearance Quality

Surface should be flat, thickness uniform, no holes or broken edges, and no residual broken needles; needle-punching should be even.

5.2 Appearance Quality

Appearance quality should be inspected roll-by-roll (section-by-section) and evaluated per roll (section). The sample surface should be flat, needle-punched evenly, thickness uniform, without holes or broken edges, and no broken needles remaining inside the bentonite geosynthetic clay liner.

5.3 Dimensional Deviation

Length and width should be measured according to GB/T 4667 using a measuring tool with a precision of 1mm, then calculate the dimensional deviation.

5.4 Unit Area Mass of Bentonite Geosynthetic Clay Liner

Spray a small amount of water on the bentonite geosynthetic clay liner to prevent the bentonite from scattering at the cutting edge. Cut samples 200mm from the outer end along the length direction and 10mm from the edge along the width direction, dry them at 105℃±5℃ until constant weight. Measure each specimen’s dimensions with a measuring tool of precision 1mm, then weigh them on a balance. 5.5 Swelling Index of Bentonite

Grind the bentonite sample slightly, sieve through a 200-mesh standard sieve, dry at 105℃±5℃ until constant weight, then cool in a desiccator to room temperature. Weigh 2.00g of bentonite sample, add the bentonite multiple times into a graduated cylinder already containing 90mL of deionized water, slowly adding no more than 0.1g of bentonite within about 30 seconds each time, wait for the bentonite to settle to the bottom of the cylinder before adding more bentonite, with intervals of no less than 10 minutes between additions, until all 2.00g of bentonite has been added to the cylinder. Use a glass rod to make any bentonite adhered to the inner walls of the cylinder settle to the bottom, then fill the water in the cylinder to 100mL (if air bubbles are found in the sediment at the bottom of the cylinder after 2 hours, allow the cylinder to be rotated slowly at a 45-degree angle until the sediment is uniform). After standing still for 24 hours, read the scale value at the interface of the sediment (sediment does not include low-density bentonite flocculants), accurate to 0.5mL.

5.6 Adsorption of Methylene Blue

Carry out according to the method for determining adsorption of methylene blue in JC/T 593.

5.7 Tensile Strength

Carry out according to GB/T 15788, with a tensile speed of 300mm/min.

5.8 Elongation at Maximum Load

Carry out according to GB/T 15788, with a tensile speed of 300mm/min.

5.9 Peel Strength

Carry out according to GB/T 2791. Along the length direction of the specimen, peel apart the woven geotextile and non-woven geotextile or the PE film and non-woven geotextile in advance by 30mm, separate the peeled ends symmetrically clamped in the upper and lower clamps. Start the testing machine, allowing the upper and lower clamps to separate at a speed of 300mm/min.

5.10 Permeability Coefficient

Carry out according to Appendix A.

5.11 Hydrostatic Pressure Resistance

Carry out according to Appendix B.

5.12 Filtrate Loss

Carry out according to the method for determining filtrate loss in JC/T 593.

5.13 Durability of Bentonite

Test method same as 5.5, measure the swelling index of bentonite after being statically placed for 168 hours in a 0.1% CaCl₂ solution.

6 Inspection Rules

6.1 Inspection Classification

6.1.1 Factory Inspection

Each batch of products must be inspected before leaving the factory. Only after passing inspection and being accompanied by a certificate of quality inspection can they leave the factory. Inspection items include appearance quality, dimensional deviation, unit area mass of bentonite geosynthetic clay liner, swelling index of bentonite, tensile strength, elongation at maximum load, peel strength, permeability coefficient, hydrostatic pressure resistance, and durability of bentonite.

6.1.2 Type Inspection

Type inspection includes all requirements in Chapter 4, carried out under the following circumstances:

a) When new products are put into production or when product type approval is conducted;

b) During normal production, once a year;

c) When raw materials, processes, etc., undergo significant changes that may affect product quality;

d) When factory inspection results differ significantly from the previous type inspection results;

e) When production resumes after product suspension for more than six months;

f) When national quality supervision and inspection institutions require type inspection.

6.2 Batch Formation

Products are accepted in batches. Products of the same type and specification, every 12,000m² forms a batch, if less than 12,000m² counts as one batch.

6.3 Sampling and Inspection Frequency

Randomly select 6 rolls from each batch for inspection, and they should comply with the provisions in 5.1.

6.4 Judgment Rules

6.4.1 For the requirements specified in 4.1, 4.2, and 4.3, if one item is不合格 (non-conforming), the roll is deemed不合格 (non-conforming). Among the 6 sampled rolls, if the number of non-conforming rolls does not exceed 1 and all physical-mechanical performance meets the requirements in Table 2, it is judged as a conforming batch.

6.4.2 Among the 6 sampled rolls, if the number of non-conforming rolls exceeds 1 or there are non-conforming items in physical-mechanical performance, then double sampling should be performed in the batch according to 6.3 to re-inspect the non-conforming items; if there is still one item that fails, the batch is deemed non-conforming. Re-inspection results serve as the final judgment basis.

7 Marking, Packaging, Storage, and Transportation

7.1 Marking

On the outer packaging of sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners, it should include:

a) Product name, code, specifications, trademark;

b) Name and address of the manufacturing enterprise;

c) Production date, batch number, quantity;

d) Inspector stamp.

7.2 Packaging

Sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners are packaged in rolls with waterproof material and inserted with a core.

7.3 Storage and Transportation

Sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners should be stored in dry and ventilated warehouses. Do not open the packaging before formal construction and laying. During storage and transportation, attention must be paid to moisture-proofing, water-proofing, and preventing damage and soil leakage.

Appendix A (Normative Appendix) Determination of Permeability Coefficient of Sodium Bentonite Geosynthetic Clay Liner

A.1 Principle

Under a certain pressure difference, sodium bentonite geosynthetic clay liners will generate a slight flow. By measuring the flow through the specimen during a specified hydraulic pressure difference over a certain period of time and the thickness of the specimen, the permeability coefficient can be calculated.

A.2 Equipment

The permeability coefficient determination device includes a pressurization system, flow measurement system, and permeameter, etc. The permeameter houses the specimen and permeable stones, ensuring no side leakage in the clamping part of the specimen. A.3 Test Procedure

Apply a thin layer of high vacuum silicone grease on one side of the bottom cover. Place a flexible film around the specimen (the film should withstand sufficient hydraulic pressure), then install "O" rings at both ends of the specimen using an "O" ring expander.
