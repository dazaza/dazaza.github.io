China Mobile Internet Landscape '2010.09'

by ybcz0519 on 2010-09-22 16:21:42

It has been exactly one month since the last release of the mobile internet map, and the changes within this month have been very rapid. (Last month's mobile internet map can be seen here) (Click here to view the large image)

Changes this month:

In the hardware sector, we can see that Samsung has released the China version of the i9000, and there are also products on the market with prices around 1100 yuan using MTK solutions and Legend molds;

Some mature cloud service products are gradually being revealed, such as Huozhong Contacts, Shanda MaiKu, and Kingsoft KuaPan;

The number of players in the LBS field has increased to over 25. Portals that missed the microblogging wave are now eager to try their hand at LBS;

In the reading sector, Shanda Bambook made a stunning debut at a price of 998 yuan, and it was recently reported that they acquired Yuedu.com, breaking my last psychological barrier - I hope to read magazines on the Bambook.

Products from Innovation Works are gradually being unveiled: Wandoujia, Yingyonghui, Youmeng, PointOS, and Xingyun.

Important Changes:

The number of LBS applications in the market has limited ability to drive the development of China's mobile internet at this stage. If you've watched the video from March this year where Teacher Kaifu called everyone to join the mobile internet wave, you'll find that six months later, the entrepreneurial environment has not improved - terminal prices are still too high, bandwidth fees and stability have not significantly improved, and the growth of 3G users is less than satisfactory (the latest data shows a penetration rate of only 3%).

If viewed from the perspective of the industrial chain, what could currently accelerate the development of mobile internet would be low-cost chip solutions and customized operating systems - both of these would reduce the costs for hardware manufacturers, thereby lowering terminal prices. The good news is that products based on MediaTek and Rockchip will soon be launched on a large scale. Jifeng Network and Innovation Works are also providing integrated system solutions for some manufacturers. Such good deeds deserve praise; Innovation Works feels that talking isn't enough, they're taking action personally.

There's another interesting point - you'll notice that none of the application directions mentioned by Teacher Li in the video have been pursued, perhaps they are just visions. However, from some of the products disclosed recently by Innovation Works, they are imitating Google's strategic layout method, and I admire any team with such capabilities and foresight domestically. Just hope they don't follow Tencent's example and give other startup teams some opportunities.
