Google Crossbar has released a new version: Users can export historical geographic information

by bugbear on 2010-10-08 21:36:11

According to the Google Mobile blog, Google has released a new version of Google Latitude, which allows computer users to access Google Latitude through a simpler web address, rather than having to use iGoogle widgets and Orkut.

The new version of Google Crossbar has an important feature update, which is to record user history geographic information and provide export functions. It includes a new function "record" - "information center", you can view the previously recorded location information, and can export it as a KML file, the exported KML file can be viewed in Google Earth, after a trial, I found that the use of Google Horizontal on the smart phone is more terrible, as long as the Google Map is opened in the phone, The system will automatically record your address location every 2 minutes, which is simply a tracker ah, definitely a good tool for cross-province pursuit.
