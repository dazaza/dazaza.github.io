If this is the ending

When choosing a mood, your mouse moved back and forth many times. There was no word that could describe the feeling at this moment. Heavy? Release? Bitter? All of them were similar but none of them were exactly right. In the end, happiness was chosen. Yes, happiness, there should be happiness, shouldn't there be?

A person who once waited wholeheartedly and without reservation, after giving you countless illusions, hopes, and expectations, suddenly one day, without any warning, showed you the gift he was going to give his girlfriend and told you how much that woman had sacrificed for him. His seemingly casual words still sounded like a thunderbolt on a sunny day. You gathered all your courage to force back the mist spreading in your eyes, pretending nothing happened and preparing to flee hastily. That person still maintained an air of having everything under control, insisting on sending you home as usual. And you, with no way out, could only act according to his instructions like a puppet.

Treat every aspect of life as an art form - that's him. The last supper, sending you home. Thinking rationally now, it was all prepared, wasn't it? Fine, he wanted to be a gentleman, he wanted to leave a good impression, and you fulfilled all of it. Initially, you blindly agreed to let him send you home, then became incurably attached. Now thinking about it, everything seemed like a pre-designed plot?

Proud as you are, you would never entangle yourself in confusion. Despite so many tears, you kept smiling until you turned away. He had already explained things clearly enough, hadn't he? But what have you been all this time? Your mysterious self-respect made you unwilling to turn around, unwilling to bow down, even refusing to ask a single question, gritting your teeth and enduring, not complaining about the pain, not mentioning a word. At work, you could still smile like before, but your pale complexion and swollen eyes still gave it away to someone opposite the office. Actually, it makes sense; if someone truly cares about another person, they become very sensitive, and often the guesses that come up at such moments are correct. But what can be done? Didn't you know that your uncontainable happiness initially brought others' dimmed expressions? Now, despite your disheveled appearance, instead of schadenfreude, you see concerned looks and questioning eyes. You should thank heaven for its kindness, right? This world has many good people, but lucky good people are few. What can be done? This is reality.

When a person loses their fighting spirit, it seems they also lose creativity. You played a very clichéd game of disappearing. Let the happy ones be happy, let the dispirited ones hide away to heal their wounds. But he didn't seem to realize this point, finding you more eager than ever to chat, caring whether you're happy or not, asking about your warmth and coldness. After several ups and downs of emotions, you suddenly felt really tired and annoyed. Autumn came, cooling the heart and chilling the eyes. What exactly does he want? Does he think you're a good confidante? Does he want to maintain his gentlemanly demeanor, responsible image, worried that you might do something irrational? Or does your naivety remind him of the girl from the front row whom he used to pick up and drop off? Is this what you want? No, none of it is. Then, go away, thoroughly.

For a whole week, you disappeared from his sight. He suddenly asked where you had gone, still deliberately finding topics to talk to you, still inviting you to dinner at his house with either a domineering or negotiating tone. He even commented on you saying "fallen flowers are silent, people are as indifferent as chrysanthemums." These eight characters, so precious, could only be privately sighed over. Are these "indifferent" chrysanthemums indicative of indifference? Because you didn't bring it up, because you no longer expressed emotional sentences? Ding Dian's first impression of Ling Shuanghua was "people are as indifferent as chrysanthemums," the same four words, yet they are worlds apart!

If this is the ending, at least it's not such a bad ending. So many unsent letters, so many unspoken words, so many emotions, so much repressed pain. In the end, it exchanges for eight words: "fallen flowers are silent, people are as indifferent as chrysanthemums." Whether it's misinterpretation or beautification, everything ends here, no more love or hate.
