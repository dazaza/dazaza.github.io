At around 21:00 on the 1st of the month, a woman surnamed Zhang in her 40s was heading home after work as usual when she passed by the Wugong Mountain area of West Second Ring Road in Yuelu District. Suddenly, a cold dagger was pressed against her neck. After being robbed, Zhang was dragged into a culvert under the Wugong Mountain overpass where she narrowly escaped an attempted rape thanks to the timely passing of a pedestrian, which caused the suspect to flee upon hearing their approach. The next day, another similar robbery occurred in the Wugong Mountain area of West Second Ring Road.

Yesterday, I learned from the Yuelu Patrol Police Brigade that these two late-night robberies have been solved. Surprisingly, the suspect turned out to be a 14-year-old boy who confessed to the police that he committed the robberies just to play online games.

Reporter Zhi-Jie Liu, Communicator Bin Liu, Changsha Report

Case: Teenager Robbed Women for Two Consecutive Nights

Recently, two consecutive robberies happened in the Wugong Mountain area of West Second Ring Road in Yuelu District, with victims both being women in their 40s.

At around 21:00 on March 1st, while walking alone under the Wugong Mountain overpass on West Second Ring Road on her way home from work, Zhang was followed by a tall and slim teenager. When they reached a secluded area, the teenager suddenly covered her mouth with his hand and pulled out a dagger, threatening her to hand over all her belongings. Zhou Liang (a pseudonym) took the only 50 yuan cash Zhang had but felt unsatisfied. He then developed lustful thoughts towards her fashionable attire, saying, "You're forty or fifty years old, yet you dress so seductively." He then dragged victim Zhang into a culvert under the overpass, intending to rape her, but abandoned the attempt due to the sound of passersby and quickly fled the scene.

At around 23:00 on March 2nd, the suspect Zhou Liang came again near the Wugong Mountain overpass in Yuelu District, Changsha, following victim Tang to a secluded area and using the same method to rob her. He managed to snatch a handbag and 18 yuan cash before fleeing the scene.

Arrest: Suspect Caught While Reconnoitering Again

After the case occurred, Captain Xiang-Ming Wu of the Yuelu Patrol Police Brigade paid high attention and immediately dispatched elite police forces to investigate the case and organize patrols in key areas. "He's about a dozen years old, tall and slim, his clothes are somewhat dirty, and he speaks with a Changsha accent." Through patient and detailed visits and investigations by the police officers, it was found that the suspect characteristics and modus operandi described by the two cases' victims were basically similar. Therefore, it was judged that the two cases were likely committed by one person, and there was a possibility of further offenses.

At around 11:00 on March 3rd, a suspicious man caught the attention of plainclothes police officers. This man was seen loitering around the green belt near Wugong Mountain and following lone female pedestrians, showing significant suspicion of planning another crime.

The plainclothes police suspected that the man might be reconnoitering again and decisively approached him, revealing their identities and questioning him. Upon seeing the sudden appearance of the police, the man’s face changed drastically, and he tried to run away but was intercepted and arrested by patrol car No. 33 that happened to be passing by.

Reminder: Women Walking Alone at Night Should Preferably Travel in Groups

Upon intensive interrogation, it was revealed that Zhou Liang is from Wangcheng District and is only 14 years old. He admitted to committing robberies against two middle-aged women in the Wugong Mountain area of Yuelu District on March 1st and 2nd, 2012. "Why don't you study? You're so young," the police expressed deep regret regarding Zhou Liang's behavior.

Zhou Liang said that due to poor academic performance, his family thought studying was useless, and he dropped out of school before finishing junior high school. "I just wanted to rob some money to go online." Prior to the incident, Zhou Liang hadn’t returned home for several days, staying up all night in internet cafes. His parents, who run a mahjong parlor in Yuelu District, did not take this seriously.

Currently, the suspect Zhou Liang has been criminally detained, and the case is under further investigation. The police remind women friends that when walking at night, it's best to travel in groups, avoid taking remote side roads, and do not carry large amounts of cash or valuable items. If someone is following or tracking you, stay calm; if encountering a thug, do not resist blindly. Try to remember the thug’s physical features, accent, and other basic information. Ensure your safety first and then report the incident promptly to the police.

A child's healthy growth requires two key elements: one is giving them love, making them feel warmth at home. When someone outside offers them small favors, they may feel more cared for than by their own parents; the second is necessary discipline.
