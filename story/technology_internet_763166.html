Yahoo Confirms Media Chief Rosen Is Leaving Sept. 1

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 16:29:37

Yahoo! reported to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on Monday that Mickie Rosen, the company's media business director and senior vice president of global media, will leave the company on September 1st. Rosen has served as Yahoo!'s media business director since 2011.

Marissa Mayer has been the CEO of Yahoo! for a year now, leading the company in its efforts to rejuvenate. Rosen is one of several executives who have left Yahoo! in recent months. According to the report submitted by Yahoo!, Rosen's departure was "without any reason," and she will receive severance compensation from the company.

Since Mayer took office, she has emphasized the development of online video businesses to increase revenue for this largest American online portal. Mickie Rosen was responsible for Yahoo!'s online video business. Her resignation comes after Yahoo!'s failed attempt to acquire controlling stakes in the French video website Dailymotion and its unsuccessful bid for the video site Hulu. Hulu canceled its auction plans at the beginning of this month.

Yahoo! spokesperson Sara Gorman stated, "In the past two and a half years, Rosen has made significant contributions to Yahoo!'s media business. We wish her all the best." The statement did not disclose the reasons for Rosen's departure.

During her tenure, Rosen led several initiatives related to video content websites and effectively maintained the audience size for Yahoo!'s media content. Content provision has always been a key focus of Yahoo!'s business, and in the past year under Mayer's leadership, the company shifted its focus to mobile services.

Before joining Yahoo!, Rosen worked at News Corporation's former Fox Interactive Media division and Walt Disney, among others. Yahoo! stated that Rosen's severance package would include six months' salary equivalent, some additional benefits, health reimbursement, and early vesting of part of her stock options.

Also on Monday, Yahoo! directors Dan Loeb, Michael Wolf, and Harry Wilson announced they would resign from their board positions at the end of this month. These three directors joined the Yahoo! board a year ago and pushed for the hiring of Mayer as CEO. Dan Loeb's hedge fund Third Point will also sell back 40 million shares of Yahoo! stock to the company. Third Point will retain the remaining 20 million shares, reducing its stake to less than 2%. Through this transaction, Third Point realized a gain of $1 billion.
