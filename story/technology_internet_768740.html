It turns out that Wang Feng has made significant contributions to WeChat as well.

by anonymous on 2013-11-21 14:02:01

Yesterday (November 19) marked WeChat's third birthday, and a grand birthday bash — the WeChat Partners Conference — was already held in Guangzhou to celebrate ahead of time. This event instantly took up the most prominent positions on various tech media outlets. At this lively birthday party, Zhang Xiaolong, the most important soul figure of the WeChat team and founder of WeChat, as well as Senior Vice President of Tencent, still did not appear. His extreme modesty is due to his personality.

A few months ago, I attempted to contact Zhang Xiaolong for an interview, unsurprisingly, it didn't happen. I then tracked down his university roommate, graduate school classmates, teachers who once taught him, former colleagues, current colleagues, friends... bit by bit, piecing together the vivid growth history of this father of WeChat.

Zhang Xiaolong entered university in 1987, completing both his undergraduate and master’s degrees continuously in the Telecommunications Department of Huazhong University of Science and Technology (then called Huazhong Institute of Technology). During his time at school, he was quite ordinary, far from being a legendary figure. He didn’t like attending classes much, preferring a nocturnal lifestyle, stepping out in slippers during the day, and often staying up with his roommates around an alcohol stove cooking noodles while playing Double Ascend (a card game).

During his graduate studies, he was assigned to the microwave specialty but didn’t like it, feeling rather disheartened for a period. Later, with the support of his mentor, he started working on computer software development. His living habits were similar to those during his undergraduate years, waking up at noon every day, riding his bike to the lab on Mount Luojia, sitting in front of a 386 computer until midnight. This kind of life continued for three years without change, very typical of a Huazhong University science and engineering male student.

In 1994, after graduating from his master’s program, Zhang Xiaolong’s first two jobs were at two small computer companies in Guangzhou, later he developed Foxmail himself. This completely free email service didn’t earn him a penny, but it became his masterpiece. Zhang Xiaolong had no concept of money; cashing in on his fame and fortune while things were hot was something he simply didn’t have time to think about.

After various versions of Foxmail were released on the internet, he would receive dozens to over a hundred emails daily, some praising him, some offering suggestions, some asking questions. He would spend a lot of time replying to each one individually.

The first turning point in Zhang Xiaolong’s life came in 2000 when Broadnet acquired Foxmail for more than 15 million yuan. Five years later, Broadnet sold Foxmail to Tencent. Pony Ma didn’t require Zhang Xiaolong to report to Shenzhen, instead allowing him and his team to remain in Guangzhou. This special treatment has been maintained until now, known as the Guangzhou Research Lab (Guangyan).

Pony Ma initially acquired Zhang Xiaolong to optimize QQ Mail. The start wasn’t good, and for a long time, the team was in a state of confusion and low morale. In 2006, when Zhang Xiaolong clearly understood the direction and announced at the annual meeting "We are going to make the seven-star mailbox, the best mailbox," the engineers below laughed.

The next year, Zhang Xiaolong's team indeed won the innovation award within Tencent.

Zhang Xiaolong's achievements today are inseparable from Pony Ma. Without this patient, tolerant, and visionary boss, this team that had remained obscure for years without achieving results would have been marginalized or even disbanded long ago.

During that time, a friend of Zhang Xiaolong's came to Guangzhou on business and invited him to drink tea on Baiyun Mountain, asking him why he didn't share his experiences. He smiled and said: if I say it, I'm afraid later generations will be too scared to write programs anymore.

Zhang Xiaolong's living habits have never changed. He sleeps until noon every day, goes to the office in the afternoon, works until two or three in the morning before returning home. He has a heavy smoking habit, his index and middle fingers stained yellow. He doesn't have many hobbies, just playing tennis occasionally. Normally, he doesn't talk much, and only a few people inside Tencent know him. When WeChat began to take off, there was even a rumor circulating internally that this app was created by a quiet, introverted college graduate.

Zhang Xiaolong, like Pony Ma and Jack Ma, is a deep fan of technology philosophy god Kevin Kelly (KK). If you're determined to work in the legendary WeChat team, please grit your teeth and conquer KK's masterpieces "Out of Control" and "What Technology Wants" (you should know, "Out of Control" is as thick as a brick!). Because Zhang Xiaolong said that any interviewee who says they've read and liked KK's books and can explain why, he'll give them an offer on the spot.

In Guangyan, every product manager has a copy of "Out of Control." This is because Zhang Xiaolong emphasized: a product manager who hasn't read "Out of Control" has an incomplete knowledge structure.

Steve Jobs' influence on Zhang Xiaolong is no less than Kevin Kelly. Every engineer in Guangyan has a copy of "Steve Jobs" biography. On Zhihu, the objects he follows, besides WeChat and QQ Mail, are Steve Jobs and Apple Inc. He hasn't missed a single product launch video from the Steve Jobs era.

WeChat initially gained fame as a “hook-up tool,” largely thanks to the “Shake” feature. This function has a specific nickname within Guangyan: "stroke-stroke." Zhang Xiaolong said, this is humanity's most primitive posture, and the most primitive often provides the best experience.

Zhang Xiaolong is highly interested in the lifestyle of primitive humans, spending effort researching the origin of humans, why humans began walking upright, and he even asked on Zhihu: what did primitive humans use to cut their nails?

When WeChat first launched, saying Zhang Xiaolong promoted it with his life wasn't an exaggeration at all. At one gathering, the shy and introverted him directly approached several unfamiliar women and said, download WeChat, you can send free text messages, pictures, voice messages, and you can shake to meet nearby people and greet them.

At that time, he constantly paid attention to various feedbacks on WeChat from social platforms, once spending more than two hours in the middle of the night helping a stranger solve the problem of importing phone contacts into the Android version of WeChat.

The opening screen of WeChat, a lone figure standing before the Earth, is the result of Zhang Xiaolong's strong insistence. At the time, everyone preferred two people standing before the Earth, but Zhang firmly disagreed, believing that two people couldn't convey WeChat's meaning: people and loneliness, hence the need for communication.

Many people don't know that you can post pure text on Moments, but you need to press the camera button in the upper right corner for three seconds. Zhang Xiaolong said there's no need to actively tell users, nor does he worry that users won't find it, "if you look for it, you'll naturally find it."

Zhang Xiaolong has always opposed the filter function when sending photos, as he believes: photos should truthfully reflect life without embellishment. Although filters were eventually added after many requests, they were canceled after some time.

Zhang Xiaolong advocates simplicity. In the early stages of WeChat's development, some ideas were often imitated by competitors and evolved into new functions. Pony Ma once asked him: should we do further refinement so others can't implement the functions we haven't thought of? Zhang firmly opposed, saying: only by achieving simplicity can we be unbeatable.

Finally, the title of this article isn't clickbait. Zhang Xiaolong is a seasoned rock youth. In his car CD player, and in the late-night Guangyan office, Michael Jackson, Cui Jian, and Wang Feng are always playing.

He regards Michael Jackson as someone spiritually connected to him, placing his silhouette and the phrase "You say I'm wrong, then you'd better prove you're right" on WeChat's welcome page, thanking his music for bringing him a "smooth and refreshing feeling."

He once wanted to place Wang Feng's "A Hundred Million Tons of Faith" on WeChat's startup page, but no one could understand, so it was abandoned. However, later in an internal speech, Zhang Xiaolong played "A Hundred Million Tons of Faith" before speaking, and hundreds of people listened quietly. Then he said: he felt Wang Feng's song and WeChat were alike, both discovering a certain undercurrent in society.
