2. Forum Promotion

The forum promotion mentioned here absolutely does not mean posting ads on every section of a forum or adding your website address to your signature and then spamming. That consumes energy and often yields poor results. Forum administrators can easily delete all your posts with a few clicks and ban your ID. Such actions have negative effects, much like spam emails. I once saw a more sophisticated method of promoting a website on a forum. It was a small site providing job hunting, employment, and learning materials. Their promoters turned the various articles from the site into clickable links, categorized them properly, and posted them on different forums. This way, they could help those in need while avoiding direct advertisements, leading to better promotional effects. If these materials are posted on forums that align with the site's positioning, they will surely attract many clicks. I recently opened a new site, check it out when you have time and help promote it for the webmaster. :)

Note: If your site is related to a specific field, find relevant forums. Use search engines frequently, such as Webmaster Forums.

4. Email Marketing Method

Permission-based email marketing differs from spam. Permission marketing has clear advantages over traditional marketing methods or unsolicited email marketing, such as reducing ad irritation for users, increasing the accuracy of potential customer targeting, enhancing customer relationships, and boosting brand loyalty. Depending on the form of the user email address resources used in permission-based email marketing, it can be divided into internal list email marketing and external list email marketing, or simply internal lists and external lists. Internal lists are usually referred to as mailing lists, which utilize the registered user data of websites for email marketing. Common forms include newsletters, member communications, and e-publications. External list email marketing uses professional service providers' user email addresses for email marketing, i.e., sending information to the service provider's users in the form of email advertisements. Permission-based email marketing is a relatively independent method within the system of online marketing techniques, which can be combined with other online marketing methods or used independently.

11. Internet Cafe Promotion

Many people start using computers in internet cafes. If their first online experience includes browsing your website, the impression will be profound. How do you promote? If you have a good relationship with the cafe owner, ask them to set your website as the homepage for all the cafe's computers (usually, without administrative rights, it cannot be changed). If you don't know the owner, knowing the network administrator works too. First-time internet users often ask the administrator for help, inadvertently promoting your site.
