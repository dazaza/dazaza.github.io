PaoPao Network Optical Drive Channel March 18th - Last Friday, at around 1:00 PM on March 11th, a massive 9.0 earthquake and the subsequent tsunami severely damaged northeastern Japan, causing incalculable losses! We sincerely hope that Japan can recover quickly and overcome this difficulty.

The international giant SONY, headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, was also caught up in the disaster brought by the strong earthquake. Fortunately, there have been no reports of SONY employees being injured or killed in this earthquake. Moreover, the SONY Tokyo headquarters not only opened its rest areas to employees who were unable to return home smoothly due to transportation disruptions on the day of the earthquake but also allowed individuals near the station to spend the night in the headquarters building, fulfilling their social responsibility during emergencies. Additionally, since the Tokyo area was not significantly affected, operations at the SONY headquarters and nearby facilities are continuing normally, so consumers need not worry.

Lack of blue-ray core components laser head, production capacity will be blocked

However, six factories located in the most severely affected areas of Miyagi and Fukushima were shut down due to the earthquake. These six factories belong to SONY Chemical & Information Device Corporation (SCID, producing IC cards, magnetic tapes, Blu-ray discs, and various other devices), SONY Shirakawa Semiconductor (producing semiconductor laser heads used in Blu-ray players), and SONY Energy Device Corporation (producing lithium-ion rechargeable batteries). The SCID factory in Tagajo was hit by the tsunami, resulting in damage to factory equipment. Although the other factories did not suffer equipment damage, they remain closed due to unstable local power supply, with no estimated resumption time.

On the afternoon of the 13th, SONY officially announced a donation of three billion yen and thirty thousand radios to assist in disaster relief, establishing a disaster relief fund and mobilizing company employees to donate. SONY will match the amount donated by its employees. ■
