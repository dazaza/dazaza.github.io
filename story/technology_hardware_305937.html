Behind Pato's heroic performance, Leonardo was betrayed by his own trust

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-14 10:45:22

Behind Pato's injury-prone performance, Leonardo was betrayed by his own trust. According to Sina Sports, AC Milan lost 0-2 to Palermo at home. There was nothing to say about the two goals conceded in defense, but the failure to score in attack left much regret. The most bizarre scene occurred in the third minute of stoppage time when Ronaldinho used all his strength to dribble past several players and made a wonderful pass. Antonio Nocerino delivered the ball to an unmarked area in the penalty box. Faced with such a sure goal that even an amateur player could score, Pato incredibly failed to hit the ball properly, thus squandering the last chance to level the game.

This was a reflection of Pato's poor performance throughout the match. After Miccoli scored, Leonardo immediately substituted Pato on at the 50th minute, but the Brazilian, who had just won the "Golden Boy Award," played in a daze. At the 69th minute, Ronaldinho took a powerful shot from the edge of the penalty area, but Pato stood in the center of the small penalty area like a defender, blocking what would have been a world-class goal by Ronaldinho; at the 82nd minute, Ronaldinho made a precise long pass, but Pato and Inzaghi ended up competing for the same position, wasting a good opportunity; less than a minute later, Ronaldinho crossed from the left flank, Pato ran to the right spot, but his header went nowhere.

Everyone has off days, and Pato should not bear too much criticism for his poor performance. Leonardo admitted after the match: "No one plays as many games as he does, it is not easy to maintain the same level of performance in every game." Before the match, Leonardo was aware of Pato's condition and did not start him, which was the correct decision to protect Pato. However, substituting him on when they were trailing and expecting him to play the role of savior was not very wise. Despite Pato's strong abilities, at this stage, it is difficult for him to turn the tide with individual brilliance, especially given his shocking form, which not only failed to help the team's offense but also undermined the players' confidence.

Substituting Pato for Abate instead of replacing the obviously vulnerable Zambrotta was the first flaw in Leonardo's substitution strategy. The second substitution bringing on Inzaghi was fine, but taking off Seedorf was questionable. Compared to other teammates, Seedorf's form could be said to be second only to Ronaldinho. Their默契 coordination created numerous opportunities and was one of the most reliable cores of Milan's attack. Leonardo did not take off the mediocre Pirlo but removed Seedorf instead. He explained after the match: "We needed to bring on a forward while maintaining midfield balance, so we took off Seedorf."

It seems Leonardo believed that Pirlo's "defensive midfielder" identity was more capable of maintaining midfield balance than Seedorf, ignoring the fact that Pirlo's form was far inferior to Seedorf. Actually, Seedorf was never unfamiliar with the defensive midfielder position, whether in physical confrontation or defensive ability, he was superior to Pirlo. The logic behind substituting Pato for Abate was similar. Leonardo insisted on using formations and positions as a starting point, if the right winger was ineffective, then substitute another right winger, without considering the actual conditions of Pato, Abate, and Zambrotta. Abate's physical condition was actually very good, able to run along the flanks, but lacked the ability to score in the penalty area. Given Pato's form today, insisting on playing three forwards was meaningless. Why not change tactics and use Inzaghi and Borrello as a double striker earlier, with Ronaldinho and Abate providing support from the wings?

During the 13-match unbeaten streak, the drawbacks of Leonardo's over-reliance on a single lineup have already been pointed out. After the main players expended too much energy, it was inevitable that they would suffer a defeat. Having a stable set of starters and a stable formation is a good thing, but sticking to a group of players to the extreme can lead to situations where Pato is forced onto the pitch and performs poorly. The invincible streak being broken is also an adjustment opportunity for Leonardo. Formations are rigid, people are flexible. Only by placing the right person in the right position at the right time can the best effect be achieved.
