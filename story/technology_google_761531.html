Google Releases Core Source Code for Google Glass

by geekzhang on 2013-05-03 17:04:56

Google GlassApril 30, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, recently Google has been releasing explosive information about smart glasses. Now, Google has also released the core software of Google Glass, which is already available for developers to download.Tech blogger Karthik wrote that he saw the source code of the core software of Google Glass on the Google Code website last Saturday and it was downloadable. In this short blog post, Karthik said that the source code of the core software might not be suitable to stay on that webpage for a long time, and it would be better if it were placed next to the Android core source code webpage. Google may make adjustments soon.It is reported that the size of the core software of Google Glass is 65.7MB, and within 24 hours after being uploaded, it has been downloaded 3506 times. The software is completely open-source and uses GNUGPLv2 license certification.After checking these source codes, Karthik said that developers might be able to add mobile payment functions to Google Glass. He said, "After checking the core code, I found some places need NFC (Near Field Communication) support. Will Google Glass support NFC function? Also, considering that this core code is built on the Android code, could it be the default NFC driver program?" However, Karthik also said that his analysis may not be correct.Previously, Google has clearly stated that Google Glass allows wearers to record videos and browse the web. Regarding whether it supports NFC, Google has not made an official statement. Some media have already contacted Google about this issue, but the company said they have nothing to disclose in this regard.Other code snippets show that users of Google Glass might just need to blink to take photos, and move their fingers to zoom in or out of browser windows.For developers, Google has provided them with formal documentation and some sample codes of the Mirror API so as to develop various applications for Google Glass in the future.Recently, there have been many big events concerning Google Glass:On April 15, Google disclosed the technical specifications of Google Glass. Among them, the camera of Google Glass will support 5 million pixel photos and 720p video; available memory reaches 12GB, up to 16GB flash memory supported, and synchronization with Google cloud storage is supported; in terms of wireless connection, Wi-Fi (802.11b/g) and Bluetooth technology are supported; in audio, transmission will be through bone conduction sensors (boneconductiontransducer).On the same day, the Explorer edition prototype of Google Glass priced at $1500 began to be sold to developers. However, ordinary consumers may have to wait another year or half to buy Google Glass.Last week, Google's CEO Larry Page officially confirmed that Google Glass runs on the Android system, although this was no longer a secret.On Wednesday of last week, Google Chairman Eric Schmidt called Google Glass the weirdest product (theweirdestthing), and said it might take a long time for the public to truly get used to and accept this product. This comment seemed to pour cold water on Google, because currently the entire company is full of passion for this product.On Friday of last week, Jay "Saurik" Freeman, the developer of the Cydia app store for iOS devices, said that he had successfully jailbroken Google Glass. That evening, Stephen Lau, the technical director of Google Glass, said that this did not affect anyone, because we did not impose any restrictions on this device. So, as long as geeks are willing, they can test anything on Google Glass.
