A Beijing citizen was "applied for a credit card" without consent: an annual fee of 600 yuan is required.

by yyusk on 2009-08-11 11:29:21

Three years ago, a citizen named Yang Zhuo (a pseudonym) got a credit card from CITIC Bank. It was set to expire in July this year, and he had not processed any formalities. However, Yang Zhuo recently received two new credit cards. Recalling the current phenomena in society such as "being employed" and "being trained," Yang Zhuo immediately guessed that he had been "given a credit card without his knowledge." Yesterday afternoon, customer service personnel from CITIC Bank's Credit Card Center said that they would automatically process new credit cards for expired cardholders. If the customer is not satisfied, they can cancel the card by phone.

During the 2006 World Cup football tournament, Yang Zhuo obtained a CITIC World Cup MasterCard at CITIC Bank Beijing Century City Branch. According to the data provided by Yang Zhuo, this type of credit card was set to expire in early July 2009.

In mid-July 2009, Yang Zhuo received mail from CITIC Bank's Credit Card Center. "Upon opening it, I found two new credit cards," Yang Zhuo said. By checking the detailed information, he learned that these two new credit cards were respectively the "eLong Card" and the "Amex Card," and both required an annual fee of 300 yuan each. "No one informed me in advance about issuing new cards, and I did not apply for them. How could the bank issue new credit cards without my knowledge? Do credit cards not require any procedures?" Yang Zhuo was very puzzled about this. He also worried that being given a credit card without his knowledge might lead to his information being leaked, affecting his credit.

Subsequently, he consulted relevant situations through CITIC Bank's customer service hotline and the hotline of the bank's Credit Card Center but did not receive satisfactory answers.

Yesterday, the reporter contacted the customer service of CITIC Bank's Credit Card Center. The operator stated that the World Cup card Yang Zhuo had previously processed expired in July this year. However, after expiration, if the cardholder does not personally process cancellation, they have no authority to cancel it. As long as it is not canceled, to avoid affecting the normal use of the client, the Credit Card Center will automatically process a new credit card for the cardholder and mail it to them. If the original World Cup card expires and the customer processes cancellation, then no new credit card will be issued. Due to the long time span, they cannot recall whether the terms were communicated to the card applicant at the time of application. If the cardholder is not satisfied, they can cancel the newly issued credit card at any time via the customer service hotline.
