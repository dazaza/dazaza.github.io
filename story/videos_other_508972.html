Spider SEO (Search Engine Optimization): How to Choose Keywords? A Discussion on Keyword Selection Techniques and Strategies

Regarding SEO (Search Engine Optimization), there are rarely any absolute conclusions. Therefore, practical experience is the most important, supplemented by theory, which can better enhance one's SEO skills.

What are keywords? How to choose them? This question is the first thing everyone needs to consider when doing SEO for a site. Keyword selection techniques have always been a topic of discussion. As for how to choose keywords, there hasn't been a very good conclusion yet. The main reason is that everyone has different purposes and perspectives when building their sites. (1) Core Keywords

As long as you're a webmaster, you should be able to state your core keywords. However, I advise webmasters not to overestimate their abilities. Some friends added me on QQ and asked, "How do I optimize 'mobile phone' to the first page?" I suggest you save your time. Many popular keywords have already been monopolized by authoritative sites or Baidu bidding (PPC). However, you can choose other related terms as your core keywords, such as 'mobile games', 'mobile pictures', etc.

(2) Related Keywords

This is easy to understand because all search engines provide references. Baidu, Yahoo, Google all offer related searches, and Yahoo also has a hot search feature showing frequently used related terms recently. You can use related keywords as website categories. The advantage of using them as categories is that they highlight the keywords, allowing other inner pages to assist with SEM, and enabling reasonable multiple appearances within the site. Careful observers will notice that often pages without relevant content appear in search results simply because the category matches the searched keyword.

(3) Long-tail Keywords

SEO enthusiasts should all have some understanding of the long-tail theory. Long-tail keywords help users quickly find the content they need, for example, "2007 latest spider SEO experiment" is a typical long-tail keyword. When selecting long-tail keywords, you cannot rely on your own search habits. First, consider the majority of users, analyze how they usually search, and then determine the long-tail keywords that best fit your website content. Do not underestimate the role of long-tail keywords, especially for commercial sites. Such traffic is the most valuable.

(4) Incorrect Keywords

Some users unintentionally input incorrect keywords when using search engines. Many website administrators frequently discuss this issue, which is a small technique. How can it be reasonably applied? Without affecting the overall image of the website, we can create several related pages, with the title and content appearing a few times. As for what keywords to choose as incorrect ones, this depends on your specific site. A typical example is the name of my area, "Heze," where even some publications and government sites mistakenly use "He Ze."

(5) Obscure Keywords

You can find obscure but related keywords based on your website content. Although these keywords bring little traffic, they are easier to rank high. Perhaps a single page could rank on the first page the day after being indexed. Supplementing with related pages rather than creating junk pages brings more traffic. Especially for local or industry sites, this is a relatively good method. One of my websites showed in the traffic statistics that a certain obscure keyword was only searched dozens of times a month, but all the obscure keywords combined accounted for 10%.
