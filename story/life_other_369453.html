The father silently organized the gifts he had bought for his daughter's 6th birthday not long ago - a Barbie doll and a Mickey Mouse schoolbag personally selected by the daughter.

After the seven-day holiday, children returned to their classrooms with their schoolbags. However, in Mr. Liu's home on Danfeng Street in Jinan, the laughter and joy in the child's room were gone, replaced by the loud cries of the child's mother and grandmother as the brand-new first-grade textbooks lay neatly arranged. On October 3rd, while the parents were taking the child to buy stationery, a ladder suddenly fell over and struck her at the back of the head.

On the 8th, Mr. Liu's house appeared particularly crowded. Since the accident involving his daughter DanDan (a pseudonym), many people had come to their home, including DanDan's grandmother, uncle, and several of Mr. Liu's friends.

Mr. Liu paced back and forth between two rooms because his wife and mother were being attended to in different rooms, both on the verge of mental collapse. They cherished DanDan's life more than anything else.

This tall and slim man, Mr. Liu, was also in poor mental condition. He walked to DanDan's desk, stacking up the books one by one, along with a cake hat adorned with innocent patterns.

"Twenty days ago, the child just celebrated her 6th birthday. I came back from a business trip out of town, took her to the mall, and bought her a cake..." Mr. Liu obviously did not want to recall these events.

Beside him, the grandmother, who had just calmed down a bit, cried again almost to the point of fainting. Mr. Liu quickly comforted her, burying his head deeply into his mother's shoulder.

"The child had just started first grade, always ranking first in her academic performance. It is such a pity..." Mr. Liu's friend told the reporter.

While the girl was playing, a ladder fell over

On October 3rd, while returning to Jinan from a business trip in Qingdao, Mr. Liu received a call that felt like a bolt from the blue: "Your child has been hit by a ladder, and the situation is very bad."

Mr. Liu rushed back to Jinan. At around 4 AM on the 4th, the two pieces of paper he received were cold and lifeless: a "Death Certificate" and a "Corpse Refrigeration Form." Twelve hours after the accident, DanDan left this world.

Through his wife's account and multiple confirmations, Mr. Liu recounted the general course of events to the reporter: DanDan's mother originally worked at Zhongheng Mall. On October 3rd, DanDan wanted to buy some stationery, so she went to Zhongheng Mall with her mother. Around 2 PM, DanDan went to another store inside the mall to play with a childhood friend. About five minutes later, his wife heard someone shouting: "Quick, your daughter has been knocked down!"

His wife immediately ran to the nearby incident location and saw DanDan lying motionless on the ground, blood flowing from her nostrils, and a large bump on her forehead. Subsequently, the child was urgently sent to the nearby Shandong Traffic Hospital.

At 3 PM, Mr. Liu received a call from his wife. By the time he arrived at the hospital, his daughter was already undergoing emergency treatment. On DanDan's diagnosis report, it was written: "Unconscious due to head trauma, injured by heavy objects on the head, immediately unconscious, life-threatening." At around 2:30 AM the next day, DanDan passed away due to severe injuries.

Mr. Liu said that he found multiple informants to understand the situation and learned that what struck his daughter was a tilted ladder weighing about 100 kilograms, welded together from multiple steel pipes.

Parents seeking answers but no sight of the mall manager

In the afternoon of the 8th, the reporter accompanied Mr. Liu's friend to the scene of the accident, near the mall entrance. The "culprit" ladder had vanished without a trace. When the reporter asked nearby stall owners about the matter, they all claimed ignorance. Mr. Liu's friend tried to find DanDan's playmate at the time but failed.

"The little girl who played with my daughter also narrowly avoided being hit and is still too scared to speak," Mr. Liu said.

According to Mr. Liu, after the incident, a staff member from Zhongheng Mall stayed with him at the hospital. After his daughter passed away, Mr. Liu negotiated with the mall multiple times, but the other party never gave a clear response.

The reporter went to the second-floor office of Zhongheng Mall. After explaining the purpose, the people inside said that the relevant responsible persons were not there and asked the reporter to contact the office, but did not provide the specific address of the office. During the subsequent search process, a security guard said, "Are you a journalist? Want to find the office? This matter is difficult to handle..." then turned around and left.

According to Mr. Liu's friend, on the 7th, he once negotiated with the relevant responsible person of the mall, "They said the police have already intervened in the investigation, and there is no need for further negotiation, and it can only be resolved through legal channels."
