The Best Way to Lose Weight in Spring

I once lost 10 jin (approximately 5 kg) in one month using this method during the spring. The arduous task of losing weight is almost over, and the results have become relatively stable. I consider this to be the best way to lose weight in the spring. Now, I am sharing my weight loss experience on a weight loss blog for future reference by others. Why do I say that losing weight is so difficult? Losing weight is a systematic project that requires long-term persistence, strong willpower, confidence, and scientific methods. If your physical condition is not very good or you lack determination, then don't bother trying—it could harm your health without yielding results, leaving you empty-handed.

The first week of starting to lose weight is quite painful for us because our diet undergoes a complete change. At the same time, we need to detoxify our bodies, removing toxins before proceeding to the next step, which will make it easier.

First Week Diet Plan:

Every day, you only consume eggs (can be tea eggs), milk (cannot substitute with yogurt), cucumbers, and tomatoes. Nothing else should be eaten. Coffee and tea are best avoided temporarily as well (drinking coffee/tea when hungry can cause dizziness and palpitations). You can only eat one egg per day, up to 500g of milk per day, unlimited cucumbers and tomatoes, but these two vegetables cannot be eaten at the same time to avoid destroying their nutritional components; if you must eat them together, there should be at least a half-hour gap between them. Additionally, eggs and milk should only be consumed when you're hungry; if you're not hungry, don't eat them. Remember not to eat just because it's mealtime, regardless of whether you're hungry or not. If you want to eat eggs or milk, they must be finished before 4 PM. After 4 PM, no food should be consumed except for cucumbers and tomatoes. This is a crucial week. If symptoms of low blood sugar occur, eat a piece of chocolate or suck on a piece of candy.

Starting from the second week, a small piece of soy sauce beef can be added at lunch for the first few days. Gradually, each breakfast can include eggs, milk, or whole wheat bread. Every other day at noon, you can enjoy a proper meal. Lamb and pork are best avoided during the weight loss period due to their high caloric content. Soups and porridges should also be avoided. Rice is the only staple food allowed. Starting from adding proper meals, we can appropriately consume some fruits such as kiwis, apples, and pitaya. Of course, fruits should not be consumed excessively since the fructose component in fruits can also cause the newly lost weight to return. On days when you have proper meals, you can choose one fruit from the aforementioned list, but only one. And remember, everything you eat must be finished by 4 PM. Only cucumbers and tomatoes can accompany you after 4 PM.

Just having such dietary rules isn't enough; effective weight loss must be combined with exercise. It is recommended to exercise for one hour every evening until you sweat. Since no food is consumed afterward, this is the most effective and fat-burning time. For suitable weight loss exercises, I suggest brisk walking.

From here on out, continue to persist in this manner until you reach your ideal standard weight. After finishing your weight loss journey, you can resume normal three-meal-a-day eating, but note that dinner should not include any staple foods anymore. Exercise can also be reduced to once a week or twice a month depending on your schedule.

The former chubby girl has now reached the standard. I am 166 cm tall and weigh 99 jin (approximately 49.5 kg), which is who I am now. In total, I lost 33 jin (approximately 16.5 kg) in about three months. Of course, specifics may vary from person to person.
