The Beauty of Waiting

"Waiting," is the online name of a friend of mine. In reality, however, he is a man of many talents. His poem mourning the old city revealed to people the lost ancient Lu'an city. The "Observing World Affairs" in the Dragon Boat Festival group chat was a tribute to the loyal souls of a generation. Last night's poem for seeing off an old classmate showed what a dashing man he is.

In real life, waiting holds many beauties:

When crossing the zebra crossing, if you wait for one minute until the green light comes on before proceeding, it ensures safety for others as well as yourself; the waiting room at the obstetrics and gynecology department is filled with people eagerly awaiting the arrival of a new life; a pot of freshly cooked rice becomes more delicious and tender after ten minutes of waiting; when feeding milk to a baby, wait for it to warm up so it doesn't scald the infant's tender little mouth; elderly people can avoid sudden illnesses by leaning against the headboard for a few minutes upon waking; when young lovers go on dates, a patient wait from the boy will surely be accompanied by a beautiful flower!

Waiting is that beautiful legend:

Because of the waiting on the seventh day of the seventh month, there is the meeting of magpies forming a bridge for Niulang and Zhinu; because of that thousand-year wait, there is the mythological love story of Bai Suzhen and Xu Xian, a love that remains steadfast till death.

Waiting is a dream:

A child who hasn't grown up yet, resting his chin in his hands, looking at skyscrapers and crowds passing by, will weave many dreams about what he/she will do when he/she grows up; lovers facing obstacles from all sides will say to each other: "I'll wait for you my whole life"; two widowed elders in love but opposed by their children will make a pact to wait until the next life; those immersed in online romances will look forward to that surprising encounter every day.

Waiting is a brilliant landscape line in life:

Look at these places: crowded bus stations, bustling docks, airport security checkpoints, subway platforms, hospitals, schools, supermarkets, markets... nowhere is without waiting.

Waiting permeates life, and life is full of waiting at every moment. Flower-like children wait to grow up; young people in the bloom of youth anxiously await the arrival of Cupid; carefree middle-aged individuals wait for success in their careers; elderly people in their twilight years wait for a house full of grandchildren. Many of life's highlights occur in that moment of waiting.

"Like a spring breeze overnight blooming a thousand trees with flowers, and blowing down stars like rain. Luxurious horse-drawn carriages fill the fragrant roads. Flute sounds echo, jade pots reflect the light turning, fish and dragons dance all night long. Beautiful women adorned with gold thread and snow willow branches, their laughter lingers as they pass by. Searching among the crowd for him a thousand times, suddenly looking back, that person is found where the lanterns are dim." Look: this is such a beautiful result of waiting.

Ah, my friends, let us join hands, embrace tomorrow, and together possess a "wait" in the beauty of tomorrow!

"Waiting," my friend, your old classmates look forward to reading more of your beautiful works!
