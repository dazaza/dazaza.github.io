Breaking the vicious circle of website weight and traffic: High weight but no ranking

After publishing, the content of a website can be indexed quickly, sometimes even reaching the enviable efficiency of being indexed within seconds. From this perspective, the search engine has given such websites a relatively high weight; otherwise, spiders wouldn't stay on the content page 24 hours a day waiting for updates. However, the strange thing is, why can't main keywords or even long-tail words get excellent rankings under the condition that the search engine hasn't reduced the website's weight? Binary analysis believes it is due to unclear or vague positioning of the website theme or unclear development direction, leading to the search engine's inability to accurately position the category of the website.

A: Unclear theme positioning. In the early stage of website construction, the development of the website lacks a clear and definite direction, and even the core keywords are not very clear. How can such websites achieve rankings? This ultimately leads to search engines getting lost. For example, "XXX Information Network" or "XXX Comprehensive Network" often exhibit such phenomena. In the initial stage, a website can first determine about 20 keywords most relevant to the website theme, optimizing those with relatively lower difficulty. When the website develops to a certain extent and has some weight, more keywords can be determined. Generally, large websites will have hundreds or even thousands of keywords, and may even achieve good rankings.

B: Website development deviation. Although there was an accurate positioning of the website theme at the beginning, there was a deviation during the growth process. Some websites, in order to speed up Baidu snapshot updates, write everything as long as the search engine can index it. Over time, although the search engine recognizes the quality of your website, it also leads to a deviation in the website's theme, eventually resulting in having weight but no ranking. Here, the ranking refers mainly to the ranking of key keywords. For example, my website Kayue Interconnection focuses on domain names and hosting services. I hope that keywords like "virtual host", "host space", "domain name", and "cn domain" rank first. If words like "computer" or "TV" rank well, it won't benefit my website.

II: Having snapshots but no traffic

The website's snapshots are updated by the search engine every day, but why does it not generate traffic? Many websites have experienced such a phenomenon. Someone might say: Didn't you mention earlier that frequent indexing represents high weight? But Binary Analysis would say: According to current Baidu rules, new websites can also achieve instant indexing. My blog is an example. It achieved instant indexing after three days of opening, and it has been doing so for a month now. However, the rankings still haven't reached the front page (due to lack of external links). The instant indexing of new websites differs from high-weight websites. It can be considered a special favor from the search engine to help the future development of the website. Because, according to traditional search engine indexing rules, even if a new website publishes original content, it is hard to be indexed in a short time. This results in original content being plagiarized, and the search engine cannot identify it, leading to an imbalance where original content becomes collected content and vice versa. Binary Analysis believes that the current search engine's special attention to new websites includes the following rules:

1. Search engines judge the website's update time to facilitate future regular spider indexing. Some websites do this regularly to attract spiders, updating the site at fixed times each day. This greatly reduces server pressure from the search engine side.

2. Testing whether it's an excellent site. An excellent website tirelessly updates its content daily, sticking to its principles. If it cannot stick to its principles and sees an increase in inclusion frequency, it may resort to collecting or pseudo-original content. If this happens, it falls into the new rule of the search engine, and the website is likely to be classified as spam. This also reduces server pressure and avoids long-term observation of the website.

3. Special care for new sites. New websites themselves don't have much weight, and once high-weight websites reprint their content, the painstakingly created original content turns into plagiarism. Even with effort, there's no way out. Therefore, the search engine indexes the content of new websites first but doesn't give it much weight.
