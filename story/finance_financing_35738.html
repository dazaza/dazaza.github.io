Online accounting, the new online manager for accountants ---- from Hangzhou Daily report

Throw away paper and pens and calculators, move the mouse and all accounts are under control. Only after keeping accounts do you realize how hard it is to earn money and how fast it spends, so even the moonlight clan can't afford a taxi in typhoon weather.

1 bottle of yogurt (Kedi store), expense ￥2.4; 1 pair of pants (Yintai), expense ￥350; National Day supermarket card, income ￥500; ...

Netizen "Xiang Rufei" opened the ledger and recorded each day's income and expenditure according to categories such as food, clothing, and allowance. However, the "ledger" is not the paper ledger that many people remember their parents using, but rather a network ledger.

At some point, some netizens, especially white-collar workers, began using accounting software or creating independent ledgers for themselves and their families on accounting websites.

Nowadays, this has become a fashion trend, and they have gained a name as bright as "shaking guests" —— accountants. "CPI is rising, we cannot control the rise and fall of prices, so we can only control our own salaries and expenses!" "Xiang Rufei" exchanges insights with other accountants online: The network ledger is my "online manager." Network ledgers are very detailed, and where the money goes is crystal clear.

Currently, most financial management websites offer easy-to-use network ledgers. "It's as easy as applying for an email or starting a blog," says accountant Xiao Zhang, who demonstrates the process by opening a certain network accounting website for the reporter: After user registration and login (free), the ledger interface automatically generates with seven function menus including "General Accounting," "Statistical Analysis," "Planning Arrangements," etc. The "General Accounting" menu has submenus such as "Income and Expenditure Flow Account," "Cash Account," "Bank Account," "Debt," and "Credit."

After entering relevant data about yesterday's expenditures, the system automatically generates related statistics and tables, reminding you of your income and expenditure situation.

If you don't manage your finances, your finances won't manage you. "The network ledger lets you clearly know how and where your money is spent," Xiao Zhang says, quoting an advertising slogan from Caike Online Accounting Network (www.coko365.com).

Network accounting touches the "moonlight clan"

During my idle browsing of these financial management websites, I found that network accounting is mostly done by urban women. Although accounting is a result of the ancient teaching of frugality, nowadays, urban women keep accounts more for the grasp of their own lifestyle.

Accountant Xiao Zhang is an editor at a publishing house in Hangzhou and graduated from university last year. "With a salary of over 2000 yuan, shopping for clothes, going to the supermarket, taking taxis often, by the end of the month, there's almost nothing left in my card," Xiao Zhang said, adding that she usually keeps a muddled account of her spending.

In June this year, she saw an introduction to network ledgers in a magazine, found it particularly fresh, and decided to bid farewell to being a "moonlight goddess." On June 26, she started her own network ledger, recording her daily income and expenditure. One day, her boyfriend saw it and was delighted, praising her for knowing how to live a good life.

On September 19, Typhoon Wipha came, and after work, Xiao Zhang prepared to go to her boyfriend's company canteen as usual to "freeload," but it was pouring outside. "My boyfriend asked me to take a taxi, but I thought about it and decided to take the bus instead. This way, I can save more than ten yuan," Xiao Zhang smiled and said that network accounting had quite an impact on her. Now, she reduces unnecessary expenses and knows how to save, "Besides, we still need to buy a house."

As for accountant "Xiang Rufei," both she and her boyfriend work in the same company. Initially pursuing romance, the two were "monthly lights." In May this year, her boyfriend's family helped pay the down payment on a house, and the two began making monthly payments, causing their expenses to suddenly become tight. By the end of July, she learned how to keep accounts online and frequently exchanged money-saving secrets with others online, "No longer feeling like every day at the end of the month is dragging on."

Publishing accounts online can also bring psychological satisfaction

"Xiang Rufei's" network ledger is open.

Here, everyone can publicly disclose their account information, see how others spend their money, and let other netizens analyze for them how to increase revenue and reduce expenses. Some websites also have BLOG functions, allowing users to easily write diaries besides keeping accounts online.

Relevant personnel indicated that it is difficult to communicate personal wages, expenses, and other financial privacy with others in modern society. By disclosing accounts online, one can relieve inner distress and gain psychological satisfaction, "Financial management is secondary, it's more like a display of personal lifestyle."

There is another type where account information is not disclosed, and no one else can see it except for yourself. If you do not wish to disclose your family's detailed accounts to others, you can also register anonymously.

"Installing accounting software on the home computer is faster," "Xiang Rufei" compared accounting software with network ledgers and finally chose the latter. "As long as you are online, you can keep accounts without carrying anything around, and the functions are comprehensive."
