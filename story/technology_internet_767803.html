Tablet release day: Nokia and Apple both picked the wrong enemy

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 13:50:34

Nokia, after being "cheaply sold" to Microsoft, has become increasingly sour. However, after Apple's new product launch event, Nokia unusually showed friendliness on its official Twitter: "Looks like today is the day for tablet releases... Samsung didn't catch up..." Even more thought-provoking was a PS-edited picture showing parking spaces for both Nokia and Apple already occupied, while only the small space reserved for Samsung in the middle remained empty.

Regardless of whether Samsung participated or not, just considering the almost overlapping release dates of these two "Mars collision"-like events, they have actually chosen the wrong opponent. Although Nokia's single challenge to Apple was very clear, and Apple seemed ready to respond, they were actually targeting the wrong competitor.

The application functions of Lumia 2520 overlap with Surface 2

When directly comparing their products, there isn't an obvious advantage or disadvantage; they should be products resulting from different market segmentation strategies. Despite Nokia CEO Stephen Elop pointing at Apple's iPad during yesterday's press conference, the real opponent of Lumia 2520 is actually its own "godfather," the Surface 2.

Lumia 2520 continues many key elements of the Surface 2, still adopting a 10.1-inch full HD large screen and recommending a keyboard base, with support angles greatly similar to the Surface 2. Equipped with a Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 2.2GHz processor and running the Windows RT 8.1 system, Lumia 2520 leans more towards business use in terms of system and applications, with its main competitive field being the mobile portable PC market.

The market positioning of Lumia 2520 and Surface 2 is too close, even their prices differ by only about 200 yuan. Although Elop tried hard to describe Lumia 2520 as more suitable for outdoor use during the press conference, this minor difference is not enough to separate it from its "senior brother."

Therefore, the biggest issue for Lumia 2520 after its launch will likely be the unavoidable direct competition with Surface 2.

iPad sizes competing against each other

For Apple's iPad lineup, this morning's press conference also indicates that their actual competitors are themselves. In other words, the situation of the iPad is similar to Nokia's, with the possibility of "cannibalization among siblings."

The new generation iPad Air and Retina iPad mini now have increasingly smaller differences. After slimming down, the iPad Air's weight has dropped by 135 grams, and its width has shrunk by 19.8 millimeters, eliminating user complaints about the inability to operate it with one hand. This makes the gap between the iPad Air and the Retina iPad mini, which maintained the same size as the previous generation, further reduced in terms of appearance and portability.

In core hardware configuration, both are equipped with Apple's A7 dual-core processor, and the addition of a Retina high-resolution screen makes the display effect almost identical. With only a 700-yuan price difference (using the 16GB model as an example), this makes it difficult for users to make a choice.

In fact, this kind of "self-cannibalization" began to show signs a year ago when the iPad 4 and the first-generation iPad mini were released.

In October 2012, to stop the decline in the tablet market, Apple unprecedentedly updated the iPad product line just six months after the release of the previous generation. Most importantly, they launched a smaller version, the iPad mini, hoping to unleash new purchasing power and improve Apple's overall performance in the tablet market.

The iPad mini sold well as expected, but Apple wasn't happy. Data shows that after the launch of the iPad 4 and iPad mini, Apple's share in the tablet market continued to fall, occupying only 32.4% of the market by the second quarter of 2013. That is to say, the iPad mini did not reclaim market share from competitors but instead took away the survival space of its sibling, the iPad 4.

Third-party data shows that the iPad mini accounted for 61% of iPad series sales in the second quarter of 2013, far exceeding the simultaneously released iPad 4. And after both iPad models were updated this year, this competitive relationship will likely intensify further.

An online survey conducted by Tencent Digital today shows that 75.58% of netizens are either observing with cash in hand or even negating the purchase of the newly released iPad Air, while 48.89% of netizens said they would definitely buy the Retina iPad mini. Although after the upgrade, the two differently sized products seem to be on the same starting line, the accumulated user expectations for the iPad mini over the past year might lead to explosive sales after the addition of Retina, inevitably taking away a significant portion of potential iPad Air buyers.

Although there is a classic saying in business, "It's better to be killed by yourself than by your competitor," neither Nokia nor Apple seems prepared to be "killed" by their own products.
