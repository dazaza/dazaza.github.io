Profile of the Four Chinese Super League Teams Competing in the 2010 AFC Champions League: Beijing Guoan Overcomes Japanese Teams but Struggles Against Korean Ones, Shandong Luneng and Changchun Yatai Compete to Be China's Flag-Bearer in International Competitions

Sina Sports News - On December 7 at 16:00, the draw for the 2010 AFC Champions League will take place at the AFC headquarters in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The four teams representing China are Beijing Guoan, Changchun Yatai, Henan Jianye, and Shandong Luneng.

With China failing to qualify for the 2010 World Cup, the two most important events in Chinese football next year will be the AFC Champions League and the Asian Cup qualifiers. However, due to its longer duration and greater number of matches, the AFC Champions League offers more excitement. The performance of China's top four teams will be closely watched. Below is a brief profile of each team.

Beijing Guoan: Mixed Results Against Japanese and Korean Teams

Best AFC Performance: Third Place in the AFC Cup

Among China's new "Big Four," Beijing Guoan, the newly crowned league champions, have slightly less AFC experience than Shandong Luneng. However, if we consider the year they first participated in an AFC competition, it was actually earlier than Shandong Luneng. In the 1997-1998 season, Beijing Guoan reached the semi-finals of the AFC Cup, which remains the best AFC result among China's current top four teams.

To date, Beijing Guoan have played 24 matches in AFC competitions, with a record of 13 wins, 2 draws, and 9 losses, giving them a win rate of 48%. Their best AFC result was third place in the AFC Cup, but despite participating in the AFC Champions League twice consecutively in 2008 and 2009, they failed to advance from the group stage.

Beijing Guoan's biggest characteristic is that they dominate Japanese teams but struggle against Korean ones. In six matches against Japanese teams, they have a record of 3 wins, 2 draws, and 1 loss, placing them in a dominant position. However, in five matches against Korean teams, they have suffered five consecutive defeats, a rare occurrence even for Chinese national teams, let alone club teams.

This trend was evident in the 2009 AFC Champions League, where they achieved 1 win and 1 draw against Newcastle and Nagoya Grampus respectively, which was a promising start. However, they suffered two consecutive defeats against Ulsan Hyundai FC from Korea, which almost shattered their dreams. Despite having a good chance to advance, they chose to abandon the last two matches prematurely!

For the upcoming season, Beijing Guoan face significant changes. Although they won the CSL in 2009, their advantage was not substantial, making them one of the weakest champions in the history of the top-tier league. Key players such as the Griffiths brothers and Martin Petrov are unlikely to renew their contracts, signaling a major overhaul of the squad. This will undoubtedly add more uncertainty to their performance in the new season.

The biggest question mark for Beijing Guoan in the AFC Champions League is whether they can overcome their fear of Korean teams. Under the new draw rules, every Chinese team will be grouped with at least one Japanese and one Korean team. Performing well against Korean teams will be crucial. If Beijing Guoan cannot overcome their fear of Korean teams, they will struggle to make a historical breakthrough. Advancing beyond the group stage remains the primary goal for the newly crowned CSL champions.

Shandong Luneng: The Most Experienced in AFC Competitions

Best AFC Performance: Quarterfinals of the AFC Champions League

Among the new "Big Four," Shandong Luneng had the worst performance in the 2009 CSL, finishing fourth. There were even controversies regarding their qualification over Shanghai Shenhua and Tianjin Teda. However, there is no denying that Shandong Luneng have the most AFC experience among the four Chinese teams. If they can regain their form next year, it would be something to look forward to.

Since participating in the AFC Cup in the 2000-2001 season, Shandong Luneng have risen domestically almost in sync with the inception of the AFC Champions League. To date, they have played 27 official matches at the AFC level, achieving a record of 16 wins, 3 draws, and 8 losses, with a high win rate of 59%.

This is something that Beijing Guoan, Changchun Yatai, and Henan Jianye cannot match. However, Shandong Luneng's performances in international competitions have been a mix of highs and lows. In the 2005 AFC Champions League, they advanced from the group stage with six consecutive victories, particularly impressing fans by defeating Yokohama F.C. twice. That year's group stage left a deep impression on many.

However, Shandong Luneng have also experienced several heartbreaking defeats. Notable examples include losing 2-6 to J.League champions Jubilo Iwata and 0-6 to K.League champions Suwon Samsung Bluewings in the 2000-2001 AFC Cup; a 2-7 away defeat to Al Ittihad in the quarterfinals of the 2005 AFC Champions League; being eliminated after losing 0-3 to Seongnam Ilhwa Chunma in 2007 despite only needing a draw to advance; and a 2-4 loss to Sriwijaya FC from Indonesia in the final match of the 2009 AFC Champions League when a victory would have secured their passage to the knockout stages.

Shandong Luneng's characteristic in AFC competitions is their passion, but they lack composure, especially in crucial matches where they often suffer unexpected upsets. This has been the fundamental reason for their major disappointments in the 2005, 2007, and 2009 AFC Champions Leagues.

Similar to Beijing Guoan, Shandong Luneng also face significant changes in the new season. After six years of coaching, Bora Milutinović has left, and key foreign players like Milan Janković and Cristian González have also departed. A restructuring of the core framework is inevitable. Due to the earlier start of the AFC Champions League compared to previous years, all preparations must accelerate. The quality of the new head coach and foreign players will determine how far Shandong Luneng can go in the 2010 AFC Champions League.

Changchun Yatai: The Only Team Among the New "Big Four" That Has Never Lost to a Korean Team

Best AFC Performance: Failed to Advance From the Group Stage of the AFC Champions League

Two years later, Changchun Yatai once again emerged as a strong contender, finishing second in the league and securing a spot in the AFC Champions League. Expectations are high for both Beijing Guoan and Changchun Yatai in the upcoming AFC Champions League, reminiscent of the 2008 edition.

In the 2008 AFC Champions League, Changchun Yatai’s elimination was nothing short of tragic. In six matches, they remained undefeated with three wins and three draws but still failed to advance. They could have qualified by defeating Adelaide United at home but ended up drawing, leading to their elimination from the quarterfinals.

Despite this, Changchun Yatai left a lasting impression during that tournament, particularly by maintaining a 1-win, 1-draw record against Pohang Steelers, the eventual champions of the 2009 AFC Champions League. While Beijing Guoan consistently lost to Korean teams, Changchun Yatai never did, showing a stark contrast in their approaches.

Among the new "Big Four," Changchun Yatai is the only team that has never lost in AFC competitions. Their elimination process was regrettable. In terms of team stability, they surpass the likes of Beijing Guoan and Shandong Luneng. With Chen Xiangfu confirmed as the new head coach, the three high-quality foreign players have also committed to staying with the team.

Carlos Alberto and Melkan are considered the most successful foreign signings by the club. Immediately after the 2009 season concluded, Changchun Yatai renewed their contracts with Carlos Alberto and Melkan for two more years. Ricardo, who joined in the second half of the season, performed exceptionally well and was quickly re-signed for a one-and-a-half-year loan contract.

The domestic players are also stable, with key players like Dong Yu continuing to fight hard for the AFC Champions League in the new season. Given the early start of the 2010 AFC Champions League, having a well-formed team provides a significant advantage. Compared to Beijing Guoan and Shandong Luneng, Changchun Yatai's preparations appear more adequate.

The only disadvantage for Changchun Yatai is their lack of experience against Japanese teams. Unlike the 2008 AFC Champions League, this time they will definitely face a Japanese team. If they can carefully study the characteristics of Japanese teams while maintaining their edge against Korean teams, their chances of advancing from the group stage will increase significantly.

Henan Jianye: AFC Newcomers Aim to Secure At Least One Win

Best AFC Performance: No Previous AFC History

Although they were once title contenders, Henan Jianye ultimately finished third in the 2009 CSL, securing a spot in the AFC Champions League. As newcomers to the AFC scene and one of the biggest surprises of the 2009 CSL, realistic expectations should be set, with securing at least one win being a practical goal.

The likelihood of being placed in a "group of death" in the new AFC Champions League is high. If grouped with teams from Japan, Korea, and Australia, it becomes an absolute "group of death." If grouped with teams from Japan, Korea, and Southeast Asia, the situation becomes relatively easier. For Henan Jianye, securing at least one win in a group with teams from Japan, Korea, and Australia would already be a significant achievement.

This scenario is similar to Tianjin Teda's experience in the 2009 AFC Champions League. As newcomers, their goal was to secure at least one win, but they ended up winning two matches, including victories against an Australian team and a J.League team, making them the most successful of the four Chinese teams, much to everyone's surprise.

Henan Jianye lacks significant capital, with Tang Yaodong, the CSL Coach of the Year, being the core of the team. In the 2009 season, without any star players, this young coach turned the team into one of the biggest surprises of the season. Three key foreign players—Olisadebe, Naldo, and Song Taeyoung—have renewed their contracts, along with national team players Zhao Peng, Zeng Cheng, and Xu Yang, ensuring the team maintains strong competitiveness. The main goal for Henan Jianye, the dark horse of the CSL, is to become a dark horse in the AFC Champions League as well.
