Ground will be broken in January for a new Google-only terminal.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:37:31

November 10 news: Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC for short), located in Silicon Valley, will construct a large private jet terminal next January. The project, costing $82 million, is expected to specifically serve Google's private jets.

The terminal is being invested in and built by U.S.-based fixed-base operator Signature Flight Support. This company has already subleased most of the hangars at the terminal to Google’s private aviation subsidiary, Blue City Holdings.

This new terminal will feature seven dedicated hangars, five of which will be used to store Google's private jets, meaning other companies will have limited access to the facility. The terminal spans 29 acres (approximately 117,000 square meters) with hangar space totaling 270,000 square feet (about 25,000 square meters). It is the largest private jet terminal constructed in the U.S. in the past 30 years.

Maria Sastre, Chief Operating Officer of Signature Flight Support, told the media in an interview that the project is expected to create 200 construction jobs and bring in $2.6 million annually in rent for the city of San Jose.

Once completed, the terminal is expected to generate millions of dollars in rent and other fees for the city of San Jose. However, Google is still considering whether to long-term lease Moffett Field, where their private jets are currently parked. Since 2007, they have been paying the U.S. federal government $1.3 million per year in rent.

The use of private jets and the new terminal project is not without controversy. Within Google, Sergey Brin and Larry Page debated whether it was too extravagant and high-profile to house their private jets at a private terminal.

However, Google has been troubled by finding a suitable location for its private jets. Although their aircraft are currently parked at Moffett Field, it hasn't been very popular; and the new terminal construction project at Mineta San Jose International Airport has also faced difficulties.
