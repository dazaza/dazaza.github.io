**Service Rules**

1. **Pre-service Regulations**

Before serving, the server must stand in the area behind the baseline, between the center mark and the assumed extension of the sideline. The ball should be tossed into the air in any direction using the hand, and struck with the racket before it touches the ground (athletes who can only use one hand may use the racket to toss the ball). The service is considered completed when the racket makes contact with the ball.

2. **During Service Regulations**

Throughout the service motion, the server must not change their original position by walking or running. Both feet must remain within the specified position and must not touch any other areas.

3. **Server's Position**

(1) At the start of each game, the server begins from the right service court behind the baseline. After gaining or losing a point, they must switch to the left service court.

(2) The served ball must cross over the net and land in the opponent’s diagonal service court or on its surrounding lines.

4. **Service Faults**

Missing the ball; the served ball touches a fixed object (other than the net, center strap, or net cord) before landing; violating the service stance rules. After the first fault, the server is allowed a second service attempt from the same position.

5. **Invalid Serve**

If the serve touches the net but still lands in the opponent’s service court, or if the receiver is not ready, the serve should be replayed.

6. **Switching Serve**

At the end of the first game, the receiver becomes the server, and the server becomes the receiver. This alternation occurs after every game until the match concludes.
