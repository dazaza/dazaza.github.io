In the mid-19th century, as the Industrial Revolution deepened and large-scale machine production became widespread, the textile industry became one of the industries that benefited the most. A young British man named Jules Dormeuil saw the huge potential in exporting high-quality British fabrics to France. In 1842, at just 22 years old, Jules Dormeuil opened his first fabric store in London. Since then, Dormeuil has gradually transformed from a young man's dream into the pursuit of countless people around the world.

In the 1920s, Dormeuil launched its first fabric with woven edges called "Sportex," followed by wool fabrics. Today, an elegant worsted tweed fabric has become the mainstay on golf courses in England, Scotland, and continental Europe. Dormeuil is undoubtedly the top supplier of high-end fabrics for haute couture stores in Paris, France, and Savile Row, London, in the early 20th century. It is also one of the major suppliers of high-quality wool and worsted fabrics for men's and women's clothing worldwide.

As one of the partners of the GOLDWOOLMARK Golden Wool Mark, Dormeuil will showcase its "Master" series. Frederic Dormeuil said: "This fabric is one of the contemporary classic fabrics of the Dormeuil brand, popular all over the world, and it is truly a high-end fabric that stands out. This ultrafine wool fabric with 180 counts is produced exclusively in England. It is delicate, soft, lightweight, and comfortable to the touch, captivating the wearer. Master tailors around the world choose the 'Master' series because of its flowing drape and natural smoothness after movement. This beauty is indescribable, and I hope everyone can come and experience it personally so we can share the joy."

Dormeuil selects only the finest quality wool and uses the most skilled craftsmanship for weaving. The first secret lies in the fine texture of the fabric: 14.5 microns. The second is the traditional weaving method, using unique British wool finishing techniques and washed with the purest water in Yorkshire. Creativity and technical expertise have been passed down through generations of Dormeuil's technical know-how and continuous pursuit of perfection. This new series consists of multiple contrasting bright colors and matte plain weave fabrics. Dormeuil creates flowing fabrics to create a sense of continuous motion and features complex designs showcasing intricate embroidery. Classic narrow bands are perfectly combined with herringbone patterns and broken line effects. Some Dormeuil designs even combine stripes with patterns in the same color palette.

DORMEUIL (Dormeuil) has more than 100 years of brilliant history. Many people believe that it is the second choice after Scabal on Savile Row in London. DORMEUIL is especially famous for its checks, and we deeply regret that our photography technology cannot fully display the beauty of its checks. Someone praised Dormeuil: Its post-processing technology is so good that Dormeuil's 100-count fabric feels more luxurious than 120 or even 140-count fabric elsewhere!

Dormeuil offers more than 2,000 varieties, including luxurious Guanashina, Ambassador, Kirgyzwhite series, while the Ice, Amadeus, Royal12, Drytwist, and Dorsilk series are known for their high cost-effectiveness. Dormeuil produces a rare and precious Royal Qiviuk fabric made from musk ox hair. Musk ox hair is extremely light, softer than silk, and eight times warmer than regular wool. Moreover, collecting musk ox hair requires waiting until the snow melts each spring when specialists gather the hair left by musk oxen on shrubs and trees. Therefore, Royal Qiviuk is produced in limited quantities annually, only 1,000 meters, equivalent to about 200 suits, making it very precious.

Dormeuil was founded in 1842 and is currently led by the fifth generation of the family, Mingli Du Mi Ao. Initially focused on exporting high-end British fabrics to France, it later developed innovative fabrics, creating many premium fabrics and becoming a leader in the industry, symbolizing high-quality fabrics and establishing Dormeuil's classics.
