Which is better, surgical abortion or medical abortion?

by lll755 on 2011-06-28 11:41:18

Which is better: medical abortion or surgical abortion? For women who want to have an abortion due to an unplanned pregnancy, many don't know whether a medical or surgical abortion is better. Sometimes they ask many friends, some say medical abortion is better, others say surgical abortion is better. So, which one is actually better? Below is a comparison by the gynecological experts of Beijing Jingtan Hospital regarding which is better between medical and surgical abortions, hoping it can help women in need.

The gynecological experts of Beijing Jingtan Hospital introduce that if we are comparing which is better between medical and surgical abortions, we must look at the situation comprehensively rather than blindly assuming one is better than the other. The analysis should be conducted from several aspects.

First, let's look at the time required for surgical and medical abortions; time factors play a significant role. Modern people value efficiency, and even if a method is good but requires a lot of time, many would still not favor it. Therefore, time is quite important when comparing which is better between medical and surgical abortions. First, let's see the time required for a medical abortion. Medical abortion usually starts with a hospital examination, followed by taking medicine for three days at home, then going to the hospital on the fourth day for the abortion. The first three days won't affect work or study. However, the medical abortion process on the fourth day at the hospital takes an entire day, requiring frequent trips to the bathroom (about ten times). Afterward, for safety reasons, observation in the hospital for 20 minutes is necessary. In the most favorable scenario, from starting the medication-induced abortion to its completion, it takes at least two and a half hours. Now let's look at painless surgical abortion. After completing the examination for a painless surgical abortion and making an appointment, the abortion surgery follows, which lasts about ten minutes and is very quick. After waking up, intravenous fluids are administered, and after observing for any issues, one can leave. From the start of the surgery to leaving the hospital, it takes approximately an hour at most.

When choosing between medical and surgical abortions, patients are more concerned about the effectiveness. Therefore, the question of which is better between medical and surgical abortions mainly depends on the effect. The effectiveness of medical abortion is as follows: a 98% success rate and a 2% failure rate. Among the 98% successes, there is a small portion where the abortion isn't entirely clean. If it isn't clean, further curettage surgery is required to avoid symptoms like heavy bleeding. Painless surgical abortion generally succeeds, provided that both medical and surgical abortions are performed within the optimal timeframe for the procedure.

Regardless, both medical and surgical abortions cause harm to the body. Let's examine the different levels of harm caused by each. Under normal circumstances, medical abortion causes the least damage to the uterus among all abortion methods. For women experiencing their first pregnancy, some doctors may recommend a medical abortion. Additionally, women who have undergone multiple surgical abortions and need another abortion might also be advised to opt for a medical abortion. On the other hand, surgical abortion, whether traditional curettage or painless suction curettage, undoubtedly harms the uterus. Therefore, the recommended rest period after a painless surgical abortion is at least half a month.

Beijing Jingtan Hospital's gynecological experts state that the question of which is better between medical and surgical abortions is not merely a matter of personal opinion but should be based on selecting the appropriate abortion method according to different situations.
