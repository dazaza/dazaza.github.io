Suspect in murder case fugitive for 9 years surrendered out of concern for his wife and children

by zzf000zxye0 on 2011-08-08 16:55:49

Guangxi News Network reporter, Lu Donglin

A murder suspect from Yizhou turned himself in after nine years of fleeing, and recounted his psychological journey to the reporter.

Wei, from Lemar Township in Yizhou City, stabbed someone to death in the year 2000 following an argument. To evade legal punishment, he fled far away for nine long years. During this time, Wei constantly missed his wife and then seven-month-old son back home. In November 2009, unable to bear the mental and physical torment any longer, Wei returned to Hechi City to turn himself in. On January 13th, inside the Hechi City Detention Center, he recounted his nine-year-long escape experience to the reporter.

Stabbing leading to death, fleeing far away

On January 13th, the reporter met Wei, now 32 years old, at the Hechi City Detention Center. Dressed in a prison uniform with a buzz cut, Wei looked strong and healthy, with a ruddy complexion. Wei said that when he committed the crime, he was only 22 years old, married just over a year. Recalling the incident, Wei kept shaking his head.

It was a night around the Lantern Festival in 2000. Wei and a few coworkers were making a fire for warmth inside the warehouse of their workplace. At that moment, a security guard came over and reminded them not to make fires after work hours, urging Wei and others to leave the warehouse. However, as the security guard turned to leave, a few whistles sounded from behind him. These were originally taught by Wei to his coworkers, but the security guard thought Wei and others were intentionally provoking him, leading to an argument between them. The situation escalated into a physical fight. In a panic, Wei pulled out a fruit knife he had on him and stabbed the security guard.

After the incident, Wei ran home and hid in a cave on the hill behind his house. A few days later, he heard that the security guard had died from his injuries. To avoid legal consequences, Wei left behind his wife, whom he had been married to for just over a year, and his seven-month-old son, taking only over 200 yuan, and fled to Wenzhou City in Zhejiang Province. This escape lasted for nine years.

Hiding and running, like a frightened bird

According to Wei's recollection, after arriving in Wenzhou, he only had about 100 yuan left. Cold and hungry, he found a place to stay, paying 20 yuan in rent. Afterwards, he began living off picking up trash.

Months later, Wei found work at a construction site. At this point, his longing for home grew stronger. With tears streaming down his face, Wei said: "At the construction site, I never dared to tell anyone my real address. When others went home during holidays, I could only lie in bed crying. I often wondered how my wife was getting by these years, and what my son looks like now."

Wei stated that during his years of fleeing, he changed jobs more than fifty times. His nerves constantly taut, Wei gradually became fearful and suspicious. Every time he slightly felt something amiss, he would change where he worked. He said: "I encountered police checking residences several times. As soon as I saw them, I quickly avoided them and moved out the next day."

In 2005, Wei had bought tickets to secretly return home for a visit. But upon reaching the station, he noticed train police checking IDs. Filled with fear, Wei hastily refunded his ticket and dared not go home again.

Unable to endure the torment, returning home to surrender

According to the investigating officers, due to a lack of clues at the time, they were unable to capture the perpetrator. Nine years later, because the victim was not local, it became difficult to find the deceased's family. Nevertheless, based on the records and evidence from the time of the incident, the procuratorate has already approved the arrest of Wei on charges of intentional injury.

The investigating officer said that during his escape, Wei never drank alcohol. The main reason was his fear of becoming drunk and losing his alertness, thus being captured by the police, or accidentally revealing his secrets while intoxicated.

Life on the run was extremely tedious and unbearable. Due to constant anxiety, Wei rarely got a good night's sleep. His dreams always ended with him waking up startled, finding himself surrounded by police. Upon waking, he realized he was drenched in cold sweat. Wei said: "Every day living in fear, the torment made me think of committing suicide. But thinking of my wife and child, I abandoned that thought."

The reporter learned that since there was no telephone installed at home and Wei didn't know how to write letters, during his nine years of fleeing, he had no way to contact his family to relieve his longing.

Finally, after enduring immense psychological torment, Wei chose to return home and surrender in November 2009. Before surrendering, he went home and brought back over ten thousand yuan he had saved from working. Wei said: "As soon as I entered the door, I saw my father sitting in the hall. We looked at each other and immediately burst into uncontrollable sobs." Subsequently, Wei's wife and son also came out from the inner room. Watching Wei and his wife cry together, his son appeared at a loss.

After returning home, Wei learned that his son, due to a lack of discipline, had developed habits of petty theft. Heartbroken, Wei said: "This is all my fault. If I get the chance in the future, I will certainly try to be a good husband and a good father."
