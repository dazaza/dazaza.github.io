She dared not have surgery and put the diagnosis certificate in her schoolbag, "The money should be left for the old people to see a doctor and for the children to pay tuition fees." After receiving this donation, Wang Ying burst into tears. She kept saying thank you, "I didn't expect that after such a short time, there are still people who remember us. With this money, the tuition fees for the children for the whole year will finally be settled!" As for the future, Wang Ying dared not think too much, "I know I may not last long, but I can't stop." Wang Ying has another wish, "To make money and then buy two rooms in the hometown, so that my parents cannot live in the dangerous house anymore." (All the characters in the article are pseudonyms) During the day, Li Bing helped his father with the work at home in the fields and on the mountains. In the afternoon, he carried farming tools and ran to Wang Ying's house to continue working, cutting firewood, tilling the land, digging sweet potatoes. After finishing the work, he wiped his sweat and left without saying much. Hearing that Wang Ying's younger brother had no tuition fees for school, Li Bing tied a linen bag around his waist and went alone into the mountains to pick the fruits of the tight trees, staying there for several days. He sold them for 20 yuan in the town and gave the money to Wang Ying. Every year when the water volume decreases during the winter-spring transition, Li Bing's father would carry buckets to the stone well in front of Wang Ying's house to fetch spring water. Sometimes Li Bing also followed, but never said a word. "At that time, the village was very secluded, and the parents' discipline was strict, so it was impossible to look at him more than once." When talking about her husband, the 40-year-old Wang Ying bent her fingers, looked up, and smiled shyly. However, misfortunes never come singly. Just a few days ago, Wang Ying was diagnosed with bilateral uterine fibroids and must undergo immediate surgery. "At first, when I came out of the bathhouse, I didn't know anything else," Wang Ying recalled. In the winter of 2007, the year her husband just passed away, she often got on the wrong bus, "In the past, he always took me out. Now that he is gone, no one will care about me anymore." To replace her husband in supporting the two children's education, providing for her in-laws in their seventies, after handling the funeral arrangements, Wang Ying stayed in Beijing and worked tirelessly day and night. However, calamities were frequent. A month before the verdict, Wang Ying's hometown in Sichuan was hit by an earthquake again, and three rooms of the old house collapsed. Her in-laws could only live in a tent. Wang Ying worked even harder after knowing this, "In my dreams at night, I dreamt of the house collapsing. After waking up, I sat on the bed stunned for half an hour. They cannot get into trouble again, otherwise this family will really be ruined." Wang Ying said.
