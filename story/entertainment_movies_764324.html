Introduction:

On June 25th, the King of Pop, Michael Jackson, passed away at the age of 50. At 10 a.m. on July 7th (1 a.m. Beijing time on August 8th), a memorial service for Michael Jackson was held at the Staples Center in Los Angeles, USA. Former South African President Nelson Mandela wrote an eulogy for him, and many stars sang in tribute to him. It was reported by American media to be the most watched funeral since Princess Diana's passing. On that day, approximately one billion people around the world watched the largest celebrity farewell ceremony in history via the internet, mobile phones, and television.

The public memorial service for Michael Jackson was held at the Staples Center because he had frequently rehearsed there for his London concert in the final days of his life. Besides the public service, another private ceremony was held at Forest Lawn Mortuary for close family members only. At 7 a.m. local time, the entrance to the cemetery was blocked off by police, and only journalists with press passes were allowed entry. Outside the cemetery, over a hundred media representatives gathered early to wait. To capture footage of the private ceremony, American and global media deployed more than 20 helicopters, forming a "circle" in the air around the Forest Lawn Cemetery, creating a spectacular sight.

Due to the large number of fans attending the memorial service in Los Angeles, it was difficult to maintain order on-site. The US police initially dispatched 1,400 officers to maintain order but later increased this number to 5,000 officers on the day. Along the half-hour drive from the cemetery to the memorial site, despite the black glass of the hearse making it impossible to see inside, many fans waited along the road hoping to send Michael Jackson off on his final journey.

Former South African President Nelson Mandela wrote an eulogy for Michael Jackson

The memorial service began at 10 a.m. local time (1 a.m. Beijing time on August 8th). Despite the early hour, countless fans were already mourning on-site. Among them, 20,000 fans who had tickets attended the event, while many others without tickets gathered outside the Staples Center in Los Angeles, hoping for a chance to enter the memorial service.

At around 10 a.m., under the escort of his family, Michael Jackson's casket adorned with red roses slowly entered the venue. Everyone stood up, and tens of thousands of fans shouted Michael Jackson's name. In a choral hymn, his casket was carried to the center stage.

Throughout his life, Michael Jackson contributed significantly to charity and world peace. During today's memorial service, renowned singer Smokey Robinson, who was once from the same company as Jackson, read out the eulogy written by former South African President Nelson Mandela: "Thank you, Michael, for sharing your gifts and music with the world. We miss him, and we will continue to miss him for a long time. Let us all be strong."

Tributes and songs from several prominent stars

According to Michael Jackson's brother, holding a grand memorial service was MJ's wish. Today, many of MJ's famous friends chose to remember him through song. Maria Carey performed MJ's early hit "I'll Be There," Lionel Richie sang "Jesus Is Love," Usher performed "Gone Too Soon," and a 12-year-old British boy named Charlie Jeffrey sang Michael Jackson's "Who's Loving You." The memorial also featured clips of some of Michael Jackson's classic moments and "You Are Not Alone," which caused excitement among those present. Near the end, various stars performed one of Michael Jackson's most iconic songs, "We Are the World."

In addition to many musical celebrities, basketball superstar Kobe Bryant, who considers himself a fan of Michael Jackson, also attended the memorial service and spoke on stage: "He was a unique person, equally fascinating both on and off the stage. He always cared for those in need, and his actions proved this. He set numerous Guinness records, gave a lot, and did so consistently over a long period. Therefore, he will always be with us."

Winona Ryder choked up, Jackson's brother cried on stage

At today's memorial service, neither of Michael Jackson's two ex-wives, Catwoman and Debbie, appeared, but actress Winona Ryder, with whom he had previously dated, was present. She met Michael when she was 13 years old and became emotional several times while recalling their happy memories together: "Although we couldn't get married, we shared many wonderful moments. His smile was the purest, he was humorous, and he had a broad heart and valued friendship. He was more fragile than I imagined, but his heart was full of love."

It was rumored that the Jackson 5 would perform on stage, but Jermaine Jackson came on stage to sing "Smile" and shed tears emotionally. The loss of this talented brother deeply saddened the others, and the brothers wore their signature white gloves on stage to console each other.

Jackson's daughter delivered an emotional speech: "He was my best father"

Near the end of the memorial service, Michael Jackson's only daughter tearfully addressed the audience: "Since I was born, he has been my best father. I just want to say that I love you, I really do." Finally, under the escort of his family, Michael Jackson's casket left the venue.

According to American media reports, there are no specific burial arrangements for Michael Jackson's body yet. One speculation is that his body may be buried in his beloved "Neverland."
