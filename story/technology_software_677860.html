Aquarium shark's surprise C-section after attack gives birth to 8 pups

by anonymous on 2012-02-27 10:22:40

China Daily website Global Online News: According to a synthesis of foreign media reports, on November 11th, at the Kelly Tarlton's Underwater World in New Zealand, a female shark was suddenly attacked fiercely by another shark, and a large wound was bitten open on her belly. Subsequently, the female shark smoothly delivered 8 cute little shark pups from this wound. The visitors present were amazed to witness this rare and extraordinary scene. The scientific research dilemma was helped resolved by video conferencing for seed enterprises.

The "Cesarean" female shark

A spokesperson for Kelly Tarlton's Underwater World introduced during an interview that when the visitors saw the female shark being attacked, they all felt very surprised. But even more unexpectedly, after the female shark was injured, there suddenly appeared 4 little shark pups around her. Then, they quickly called the staff of the aquarium.

The little shark pups were "born" from this wound.

"The wound on the belly of the female shark was long and deep, this situation is indeed very rare. The 4 little shark pups were born from that wound," said the spokesperson.

Due to the worry that the little shark pups might be eaten by stingrays or other sharks in the same tank, the staff arrived and took out the little shark pups from this tank and placed them into another one.

When the staff prepared to scoop out the female shark to suture her wound, they found there were actually another 4 little shark pups inside the belly of the female shark. With the help of the staff, the female shark then "delivered" another 4 little shark pups.

The lucky little shark pups

The spokesperson then stated: "Now, the physical condition of the female shark is good, we are also closely monitoring it. After all, such incidents have no precedent, so we are not quite sure whether the female shark will recover in the future."

The shark breeder Davis at this underwater world said: "Whether in the aquarium or in the sea, the situation where sharks attack each other often happens. But I have never heard of sharks giving birth through such a method!"

Davis also said that if the female shark had not been suddenly attacked, those 8 little shark pups would likely have been born on the evening of November 11th. Since no one would have been present at that time, the little shark pups would probably have been eaten by stingrays or other sharks. Generally speaking, sharks are completely "independent" from the moment they are born, and the mother sharks do not protect their little shark pups.

Related link: Sharks can reproduce asexually

In May 2007, a joint research team from the United States and the United Kingdom discovered that female sharks can conceive the next generation without mating with male sharks.

After analyzing the DNA of a dead baby shark, the researchers found that the baby shark was produced asexually. It only had a mother, but no father. That baby shark was born in 2001 at a zoo in Omaha, Nebraska, USA. At that time, there were only three female sharks in the zoo, and they had not been in contact with male sharks for three years. Unfortunately, the baby shark was soon killed by a stingray in the fish pool.

Before this, people generally believed that all sharks could only reproduce through sexual intercourse between males and females. The research team initially also doubted their own findings. However, after repeated tests, they did not find any chromosomes of male sharks in the DNA of that baby shark.
