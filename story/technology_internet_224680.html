Curry Beef Rice Bowl

[Ingredients] Beef, potato, carrot, curry powder, garlic, ginger, green onion, salt, MSG, cornstarch, cooking wine, rice

[Production Process]

1. Cut the beef into small square pieces and place them in boiling water until cooked to remove any residual blood from the beef. Dice potatoes and carrots, cut a head of garlic into chunks, slice ginger, and mince green onions.

2. When the oil in the pot is heated to 80% hot, add garlic cloves and ginger slices. Once they release their aroma, add the pre-cooked beef and stir-fry. Add cooking wine, allow the alcohol to evaporate, then add diced potatoes and carrots. Stir evenly, then add curry powder and salt. Stir well, add just enough clear water to cover the ingredients. Once it boils, switch to medium heat without covering the pot until the soup thickens. Add MSG, mix evenly, thicken with cornstarch, sprinkle with chopped green onions.

3. Place the cooked rice into a bowl, press the surface down firmly, then invert onto a plate. Pour the freshly cooked curry beef over the top.

Curry Chicken Rice

Ingredients: 100g rice, 45g potato cubes, 40g carrot cubes, 20g onion cubes, 70g chicken breast cubes, 80g broccoli florets, appropriate amount of curry powder, 5g starch, 10g oil, appropriate amount of salt

Method:

1. Wash the ingredients, peel (remove chicken skin from chicken breast). Marinate the chicken breast with curry powder, starch, and salt for 15 minutes.

2. Add oil to the wok, then add onions, broccoli, potatoes, and carrots, stir-fry until slightly softened, then add chicken breast and mix-fry.

3. Add a little water, simmer until cooked, then thicken slightly at the end.

Making the topping.

For a family of three, use 50 grams of rehydrated black fungus, 50 grams of winter bamboo shoots, 100 grams of pork liver, and 50 grams of celery as the main ingredients, slicing the bamboo shoots, pork liver, and celery. Also prepare 2 grams of ginger slices, 20 grams of chopped spring onion whites, 1 gram of pepper powder, 1 gram of monosodium glutamate, salt, cornstarch, lard. Heat the wok on high heat, add lard until it reaches around 150°C, then you can start stir-frying the toppings. Marinate the pork liver slices with salt and cornstarch before stir-frying, quickly put them in the pot to stir-fry, adding ginger slices during the process, then add black fungus, winter bamboo shoot slices, and celery slices to stir-fry together, quickly add pepper powder, monosodium glutamate, and chopped spring onion whites, add a little water, stew into a white sauce. Remove the pot from the heat. First serve 3 bowls of rice, then evenly scoop the topping materials and sauce onto the top of the 3 bowls of rice, ready to serve.

Beef Topping Rice

Prepare beef, the fatter the better, then slice it thinly.

Also prepare some soy sauce, a bit of cooking wine, and a bit of beef powder. Mix the soy sauce and cooking wine in a 1:1 ratio with the beef slices, set aside for a while to marinate. One onion, sliced into strips. Slice along the grain to prevent it from breaking apart easily.

Layer the onions at the bottom of the pot, then lay the beef and its marinade on top. Add a bowl of water, cook over medium heat until the beef is cooked. Reduce the sauce until it becomes slightly thick.

Place the beef on top of the rice, done!

Mother and Child Topping Rice

The origin of Mother and Child Topping Rice comes from the main ingredients being chicken and eggs; chicken as mother, eggs as child, hence the name Mother and Child Topping Rice. Traditionally made with diced chicken, I find using minced chicken more flavorful and easier to eat. Some stores in Japan have become famous due to their improved minced chicken recipe, and additionally, each store has its own secrets for the broth. Unlike Chinese dishes which use rich broth, Japanese dishes generally use dried fish, shiitake mushrooms, kelp, etc., to make the broth, which is light and oil-free. For home cooking, using shiitake mushrooms or kelp broth is sufficient. This dish uses the natural flavor of the broth as the base, combined with the soy sauce flavor that pairs well with chicken, and the unique aroma of three-leaf herbs (similar to Chinese cilantro), making it simple yet extraordinary despite the few ingredients used.

Materials and Preparation:

A Broth 100cc: Soak one dried shiitake mushroom or a piece of dried kelp about 4x8cm in 100cc water, soak the former for an hour and the latter for two hours. Soaking time can be shortened with warm or hot water. Afterward, remove the shiitake or kelp, reserving the broth for later use.

B 80 grams of diced chicken breast or minced chicken. Quarter an onion weighing 80 grams, and use fresh shiitake mushrooms or the dried shiitake used for broth, all sliced thinly.

C One egg, lightly beaten.

D Appropriate amount of Japanese three-leaf herbs (Chinese cilantro also works) washed and cut into inch-long segments. If unavailable, use an appropriate amount of scallions cut diagonally into thin slices, or finely chop green onions into bits.

Seasonings: One tablespoon of Japanese cooking sake, one and a half tablespoons of Japanese dark soy sauce, one teaspoon of mirin and sugar each. Mirin is optional, if using Chinese soy sauce, adjust the amount accordingly.
