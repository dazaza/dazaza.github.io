"We are salaried workers, employed at a supermarket with only a few thousand yuan monthly income. "On the day of the incident, my husband and I were not home; only my daughter and her aunt were present. Tao entered my house, took out prepared lighter oil, poured it on my child's head, and lit it." Recalling her daughter's ordeal, Ms. Li was in tears.

"Initially, hospitalization and treatment fees were indeed covered by Tao's parents," Ms. Li told reporters that in October, when Xiao Zhou was still in the intensive care unit, Tao's parents presented a document acknowledging his active rescue efforts and surrender on that day for them to sign. "After we refused to sign, they stopped paying for the treatment."

Ms. Li is the mother of the victimized girl Xiao Zhou. According to her introduction, Xiao Zhou is 17 years old. The incident occurred shortly after her 16th birthday last year. "Tao was born in 1995; both he and my daughter attended Shouchun Middle School, different classes but the same school. He had been persistently harassing my daughter. To avoid Tao's harassment, in September 2010, I transferred my daughter to Cuozhen Middle School, but this did not stop the boy's harassment. Afterwards, my daughter had to drop out of school."

According to the introduction, after the tragedy, Xiao Zhou underwent seven days and nights of emergency care in the intensive care unit of Anhui Medical University Affiliated Hospital before escaping life-threatening danger. However, her injuries remained severe. Her head, face, neck, and chest were severely burned, one ear was burned off, the burn area exceeded 30%, reaching second and third-degree burns, leaving her completely disfigured.

"At this stage, under the supervision of the procuratorate, both parties can first conduct their own civil mediation. This way, the injured party can receive treatment sooner. Moreover, resolving the civil matter first does not affect the subsequent criminal trial," Lawyer Li analyzed.

"Before the incident, we repeatedly contacted Tao's parents, asking them to manage their son and stop him from harassing our family. We even knelt down and begged Tao to leave my daughter alone, but it was all in vain," Ms. Li told reporters that five months after the incident, due to the inability to bear both physical and psychological blows, her daughter has now become mentally unstable.

In September 2011, Xiao Zhou returned to school. About a month later, on the afternoon of September 17, the tragedy occurred.

According to the family of Zhou, Tao's parents work respectively at Hefei Audit Bureau and Hefei Planning Bureau.

Yesterday afternoon, we learned from the Yaohai District Criminal Investigation Team that suspect Tao has been criminally detained on suspicion of intentional harm due to emotional issues. He is currently held in custody. The investigation is complete, and the next step will be transferring the case to the prosecutor's office for review and prosecution.

Currently, the suspect has been criminally detained. In about a week, once the injury appraisal results are out, the case will enter the judicial process.

Current Situation: Payment of Medical Fees Ceased Due to Refusal to Sign

Ms. Li further informed reporters that what worries and frightens them now is, "Since we refused to sign, they stopped paying the medical fees, fearing it would delay the child's treatment; we also fear they might find ways to secure bail for Tao, who could then harm us again."

So far, the total cost of her daughter's hospitalization and treatment has amounted to over 400,000 yuan, with Tao's parents covering 330,000 yuan. They still owe the hospital more than 100,000 yuan. Currently, daily ointment expenses amount to over 300 yuan. Helplessly, Ms. Li said, they had to discharge their daughter from the hospital.

"I heard a scream, rushed over to discover the child engulfed in flames, while Tao kept shouting 'die'. I quickly used a blanket to extinguish the fire and called 120," Xiao Zhou's aunt told reporters that during this time, Tao stood by doing nothing.

Subsequently, I contacted the victim's representative lawyer. Regarding why the injury appraisal has been delayed, the lawyer explained, "The appraisal department stated that due to the severity of the victim's neck injuries, an appraisal can only be conducted after skin graft surgery is completed. However, this means waiting at least three more months. For a victim urgently needing treatment, this is unacceptable."

Just because his advances were rejected, a 17-year-old middle school student named Tao from Hefei forcibly broke into a residence, splashed gasoline on a 16-year-old girl, and ignited her with a lighter, causing severe burns and possibly lifelong disability. Following this, the injury appraisal was delayed, payment of the girl's medical fees ceased, the suspect requested bail, almost driving the victim's family to despair.

The tragic fate of the flower-aged girl, whether current treatment can continue, and when the judicial process will start, Star Newspaper will continue to follow up (Market Star Newspaper - Reported by Haiquan Liu).

As of press time, we learned from Ms. Li that yesterday afternoon, the Hefei Public Security Bureau had initiated the injury appraisal. The appraisal has been completed, and the results will be available in a week.
