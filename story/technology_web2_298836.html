56. Excessive Testosterone Leads to Strange Behavior in Men: Researchers announced on the 26th that they had discovered through research that excessive testosterone can kill brain cells. This discovery can explain why the abuse of steroids can lead to behavioral changes, such as becoming aggressive and having suicidal tendencies. Laboratory trials on brain cells showed that an appropriate amount of male testosterone is beneficial, but excessive testosterone causes brain cells to self-destruct, a process similar to the damage Alzheimer's disease inflicts on brain cells. According to Reuters, Barbara Ehrlich, the leader of the study from Yale University, said, "Too little or too much testosterone is harmful, but an appropriate amount of testosterone is very beneficial." Testosterone plays a crucial role in the growth, development, and classification of cells. Both men and women secrete testosterone, just that men secrete 20 times more testosterone than women. Testosterone is also an important substance that promotes male sexual development and maintains sexual characteristics. However, testosterone is abused in sports. Athletes often abuse testosterone or take steroids to gain an unfair competitive advantage; steroids can be converted into testosterone within the human body. Barbara said, "Our experiments show that when the level of steroids in the human body is too high, the level of testosterone will also be too high, and excessive testosterone can kill nerve cells. We know that when people lose brain cells, they lose some functions." Barbara's scientific team also conducted the same experiment using female hormones (estrogen). She said, "We were surprised by the results of the experiment, but it seems that estrogen has a protective effect on the nervous system. If there is estrogen present in the human body, fewer brain cells die." The researchers published their findings in the journal Biochemistry. Barbara and her colleagues wrote in the article that their findings suggest that people should think twice before supplementing with testosterone, despite its ability to make muscles grow strong and aid recovery after training. They wrote, "The effects of testosterone on the nervous system can have long-term impacts on brain function." Barbara humorously wrote in a statement, "Next time on the highway, if a muscular man driving a sports car speeds past your car recklessly, don't get angry. You'd better take a deep breath because it's not his fault." Testosterone can cause abnormal behavior, such as being more aggressive. The cell death process is called apoptosis, or programmed cell death. Barbara and her colleagues wrote in the article, "Apoptosis is crucial for the brain, which needs to remove some brain cells. However, if this process happens too frequently, people will lose too many brain cells, and diseases will follow." Excessive testosterone increases the frequency of brain cell death. The pathogenesis of Alzheimer's disease is similar to the damage caused by excessive testosterone on brain cells. Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia in the United States, affecting approximately 4.5 million Americans. Huntington's disease also follows a similar mechanism. The researchers wrote in the article, "Our experimental results indicate that the reaction of brain cells to excessive testosterone can be compared to these two diseases."

59. Why Are Male Testicles Outside the Body? Testicles are the most important sexual organs in males; they produce sperm and secrete male hormones, fundamentally making men what they are. However, why did God place such an important organ outside the body, where it is easily damaged, rather than inside the body where it would be relatively safer? For testicles to maintain normal physiological functions, the optimal temperature required is around 35 degrees Celsius, whereas the body's internal temperature of 37 degrees Celsius is detrimental to their function. Some cryptorchid patients (due to embryonic developmental disorders causing the testicles to fail to descend normally into the scrotum) have poorly functioning testicles due to prolonged exposure to high temperatures, resulting in poor or no sexual function. Since testicles are highly sensitive to temperature, the scrotum takes on the task of regulating testicle temperature. When exposed to cold and the testicle temperature drops too low, the scrotal skin contracts to prevent heat loss; when the testicles become too hot, the scrotal skin relaxes to dissipate heat. Men should easily understand these changes in their scrotum. However, it must be clear that the scrotum's temperature regulation function has limits. When temperature changes exceed its regulatory range, the physiological functions of the testicles will inevitably be harmed. Therefore, care should be taken with your testicles as it affects their heat dissipation. Similarly, sitting for long periods on loose sofas also affects lower body heat dissipation. In daily life, wearing loose pants, maintaining ventilation and dryness in the lower body ensures a comfortable working environment for the testicles, allowing them to function optimally.
