3.5-month-old male infant was slashed on the neck by his own mother

by uelu6c6b on 2012-03-05 09:48:29

Infant's neck slashed by mother, life in danger. Photo by Wu Sheng.

At around 5 a.m. yesterday morning, near the refinery in Qixia District of Nanjing, a mother slashed the neck of her three-and-a-half-month-old baby boy with a kitchen knife at home. After discovering this, the family immediately sent the injured infant to the Nanjing Children's Hospital for emergency treatment. Upon examination, doctors found that the right side of the infant’s neck had been cut open with a wound over ten centimeters long. The baby was in critical condition and being urgently treated. Currently, the violent mother has surrendered herself to the Qixia police station. It is understood that due to issues related to life and emotions, the parents of the baby are currently separated, and the baby is temporarily being cared for by his birth mother and maternal grandmother. On the night of the incident, the mother and the grandmother had an argument about the child. Neighbors speculate that the pressure from various conflicts may have become too much for the child's mother to bear, leading her to commit such an act and causing this tragedy.

Scene: Three-and-a-half-month-old boy brutally slashed on the neck by his own mother

At around 5 a.m. yesterday morning, Ms. Chen, who lives near the refinery in Qixia District, was suddenly awakened by the cries of the baby in her house. When she walked into her daughter's room, she was shocked by what she saw. A shiny kitchen knife lay on the ground, her daughter's hands were covered in blood, and the cries of the baby beside the bed grew louder. "Mom, I'm sorry, I don't want to live anymore." When Ms. Chen approached her daughter, she noticed that her three-and-a-half-month-old grandson had already been soaked in blood. Upon closer inspection, it was discovered that there was a deep wound on the baby's neck, still bleeding profusely. After realizing her grandson was injured, Ms. Chen quickly called the police and rushed her grandson to the Nanjing Children's Hospital for emergency treatment. It turned out that the baby's own mother had slashed her child's neck with a kitchen knife. After the incident, the mother, possibly under great shock, appeared mentally confused and then turned herself in to the Qixia police station.

Yesterday at around 8 a.m., I met the injured infant at the 12th floor ward of the Nanjing Children's Hospital. Due to the severity of the wound, the doctors were preparing for surgery. By the bedside, the baby's father had also arrived upon hearing the news. Witnessing the scene before him, the father was almost overwhelmed with grief, shouting his baby's name while tightly holding onto his son's little hand, refusing to let go. After being persuaded by medical staff, he finally cooperated with the rescue efforts. The doctor told us that the wound on the right side of the baby's neck was estimated to be about 10 to 15 centimeters long. Fortunately, the major arteries and trachea were not damaged; otherwise, it would have been impossible to save him. The baby had already shown signs of shock, with low hemoglobin levels in his body. His current condition remains unstable, and the specific situation will need to be assessed after the surgery. At the time of writing, we learned that the surgery was completed at 3 p.m., but the child had not yet come out of danger.

Investigation: Separation of young couple and family conflict leads to tragedy

During interviews, both the injured baby's grandmother and mother-in-law arrived at the hospital. Due to their emotional excitement, they began arguing over the child's injury but were soon stopped by those present. The baby's father told reporters that he works in Nanjing and got married in 2009. His wife was a high-age pregnant woman, over 30 years old when she conceived the baby. Originally, the family should have taken extra care of her. Since they lived in Qixia District, in order to take good care of his wife and elders, he quit his job in the city and found a new one near Xianlin University City, which was closer to home and allowed him to balance both ends. However, things did not last long as the two families often quarreled over trivial matters. His wife would get angry easily during her pregnancy, and his mother-in-law would never back down when she had a point. Helplessly, he eventually filed for divorce at the Xuanwu District Court and applied for custody of the child. Afterwards, her family refused to let him see the child, and despite multiple visits, he was always turned away at the door. He never expected his wife to harm their own flesh and blood.

During the interview, the baby's maternal grandmother insisted that after the child's birth, neither the son-in-law nor his family showed any concern, leaving her daughter and grandchild behind. The entire burden fell on her alone, which she felt was unjust. That night, she had only said a few words to her daughter, and unexpectedly, such a tragedy occurred. Due to differing accounts from both sides, it is unclear who is right or wrong.

Lawyer: Mother's self-surrender can lead to lighter sentencing

Even tigers do not eat their own cubs—why would a mother harm her three-and-a-half-month-old child? Currently, local police have intervened in the investigation. Regarding the details of the incident, the police stated that they are still investigating and cannot disclose information at this time. Lawyer Chen Weidong from Jiangsu Jinda Law Firm mentioned that as the child's mother, her actions might constitute intentional homicide or intentional injury. No matter how sharp the family conflicts were, one should not inflict such harm on a newborn baby, especially one's own biological offspring. After the incident, the child's mother voluntarily surrendered to the public security authorities. According to legal regulations, this could result in lighter or mitigated punishment.
