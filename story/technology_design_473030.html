Whether it was this part of the content, we do not know.

Living well means staying low - water stays in places that are despised by others, such as low-lying areas; the residence of a sage is a place that does not show off.

Doing without taking credit, achieving success without arrogance

The "Tao Te Ching" says: "The highest good is like water. Water benefits all things and does not compete with them, it stays in places that others despise, hence it is close to the Tao. Living well means staying low, having a heart like a deep pool, being kind and trustworthy in words, governing well, being capable in action, and moving well in timing. Because of non-contention, there is no blame."

Speaking well means being trustworthy - although water does not speak, it balances highs and lows, has a sincere and unchanging nature; the words and actions of a sage are consistent, with honesty as the foundation.

The philosophy of Laozi is vast and profound, rich in meaning. Laozi's philosophy is actually a profound interpretation of the wisdom of the "I Ching" and the Taiji thought. Here, I can only briefly introduce it in a cursory manner. Friends who are interested can take the time to study and appreciate related works.

Seeking "existence" must be sought in "non-existence"; seeking "easy" must value "difficult"; desiring "longevity" must first start with "shortness"; desiring "height" must first act "low"; desiring to spread sound "outward", must first emit sound "inwardly"; desiring to be "ahead", must first be "behind". In short, one must use the concept of dialectics, starting from the opposing side of what is sought, allowing it to naturally transform qualitatively and quantitatively towards the positive, achieving "bitterness turning to sweetness". Likewise, after achieving success and possession, we must maintain a clear mind, otherwise, we will regret our overconfidence, "extremes lead to reversal", leading to decline after prosperity.

The "Tao Te Ching" says: "Thus existence and non-existence give birth to each other, difficult and easy complete each other, long and short measure each other, high and low determine each other, sound and voice harmonize each other, front and back follow each other." Existence and non-existence, difficult and easy, long and short, high and low, sound and voice, front and back, their relationships are all mutually opposing, interdependent, and convertible. If they cannot be dialectically viewed, conflicts cannot be resolved well. People universally seek existence, ease, longevity, height, fame, and precedence, while disliking their opposites, often resulting in the pain of unattainable pursuits due to unscientific methods.

Governance excels in governance - water can cleanse filth, renewing the world; a sage governs with integrity, governing effectively.

Being adept at ability - water can be still or moving, fast or slow.

Having a heart like a deep pool - water hides its depth, containing without showing; a sage has humility, broad-mindedness, and never boasts.

The highest good is like water, skilled but not overly assertive.

As Tagore said: "You never seek fame and reverence, yet the god of love discovers you." On the contrary, if you become arrogant after achievements, you may end up with nothing despite your efforts.

Fan Li, after helping Goujian achieve political success, retired gracefully and disappeared into obscurity. Perhaps the least successful person in history was Han Xin, who couldn't let go of his achievements and was eventually killed by Liu Bang. In fact, after achieving success, you don't necessarily have to retire, the key is to be able to control yourself, not to rely on old merits, not to overshadow your master, this is called "knowing when to retreat," and people will respect you. Whether Wan Mingjian's departure from TCL was related to not listening to Laozi's advice, only he knows.

The "Tao Te Ching" says: "All things arise without rejection, creation without possession, action without reliance on self-intelligence, success without arrogance. Precisely because there is no arrogance, the merit remains forever."

Water benefits all things without harming them; a sage interacts kindly, benefiting oneself and others, contributing voluntarily without expecting returns.
