Nitric acid prices continue to rise, while rubber prices fall.

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-30 16:40:51

Products with larger increase this week: In Galaxy Securities' key tracked products, liquid chlorine (East China), 10.0%; PTA (East China region), 5.6%; ammonium nitrate, 4.5%; gasoline (Singapore), 4.5%; and naphtha (Singapore), 4.4%.

Products with larger decrease this week: In Galaxy Securities' key tracked products, R22 (East China region), -16.7%; fluorspar, -7.5%; styrene-butadiene rubber (East China 1502), -7.2%; dichloromethane (East China region), -5.7%; and butadiene rubber (East China), -4.7%.

Nitric acid prices continue to rise: Nitric acid prices continue the upward trend. This week, Anhui Jinhe's 98% nitric acid price increased by 4.3% to 2450 yuan/ton, accumulating a 28.9% increase since early July. This is mainly due to two reasons: first, nitric acid companies are shutting down for maintenance or switching some facilities to produce ammonium nitrate, causing tight supply of nitric acid; second, rising raw material prices provide cost support. We expect that the tight supply situation of nitric acid will continue, especially with the upcoming peak season for polyurethane, the demand for nitric acid is expected to rise, leading to continuous price increases. It is recommended to pay attention to Jinhe Bio-Engineering Co., Ltd. and Xinghua Shares.

PTA shows significant increase: This week, the price of PTA in East China rose by 5.6% to the 10,000 yuan/ton mark. The price increase is mainly due to: (1) The restart time of CNOOC Huizhou PX facility has been postponed until October, with PX facilities in Asia-Pacific undergoing concentrated maintenance recently, leading to a tight PX supply in September-October, and the rise in PX prices driving up PTA prices; (2) Domestic PTA inventories are low, with acceptable demand, and temporary shutdowns at Zhuhai BP and Hanbang affecting market supply. September is the peak season for polyester demand, and it is expected that PTA prices will remain high.

Continuous decline in rubber prices: The uncertain economic prospects of the US and Europe have suppressed commodity demand, causing natural rubber prices to fluctuate downward. This week, both butadiene rubber and styrene-butadiene rubber in East China saw significant declines, falling 4.7% and 7.2%, respectively, from last week to 32,700 yuan/ton and 31,000 yuan/ton. This is mainly due to the decline in styrene and butadiene prices, as well as resistance from downstream demand. With the weakening trend of natural rubber, it is expected that synthetic rubber prices will show weakness.

Galaxy Chemicals Prosperity Index this week was -2 (7 falls, 5 rises, 8 stable), compared to last week’s index of 2 (6 falls, 8 rises, 6 stable), indicating a weak oscillation in the Galaxy Chemicals Prosperity Index. Affected by the sharp drop in crude oil prices, the recovery pace of chemical product prices faces some slowing down. However, with the arrival of the peak consumption season for most chemical products in September and October, it is suggested to pay attention to the industry-wide improvement brought by the rebound in chemical product prices. Zirconium chemicals benefit from rapidly increasing exports, enhancing prosperity; the price difference between chlorinated zirconia and zircon sand has risen from 8,000 yuan in May to 15,000 yuan, with Zhejiang Huasheng Biotech being most benefited by the prosperity of chlorinated zirconia, given a recommendation rating.
