The Second Kuaibo: How Baidu Player Grew

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 20:45:00

By converting BT seeds directly into online viewing mode, Kuaibo quickly rose. During its growth, a certain type of video can be said to have contributed significantly. Later on, the search volume of Kuaibo on Baidu continued to surpass that of QQ, making it the client software with the highest search volume on Baidu. The rise of Kuaibo also stirred some movements in Baidu, and thus at the beginning of 2011, Baidu launched BaiDu Player. Although it was later than Kuaibo, its model was almost identical to Kuaibo.

The same technology, the same approach

Last year, Zhu Daxin from Kuaibo revealed in an interview:

"In fact, it wasn't Baidu poaching our people. It was our employees who voluntarily left and went to Baidu to describe Kuaibo's model in detail. Later, Baidu started developing client products, which is normal. Even if Baidu didn't do it, someone else would, just like Xunlei did before."

Kuaibo developed a P2P accelerated streaming media on-demand system for streaming media on-demand, ensuring low bandwidth and resource consumption during on-demand playback. It also introduced a compatible BT protocol on-demand system, supporting direct playback of BT seed files and web-based online on-demand. The technology used by BaiDu Player was almost identical. P2P technology allows small and medium-sized movie sites to consume very little bandwidth, as they only need to hang seeds or links to allow users to smoothly watch pirated content. These have reduced the operational costs for small and medium-sized movie sites, naturally leading them to promote or even force users to install Kuaibo or BaiDu Player, otherwise they wouldn't be able to watch videos. There is not much difference in technology, but BaiDu Player has more advantages over Kuaibo. Baidu’s own search can bring traffic to these small and medium-sized movie sites, and Baidu's advertising alliance can bring more revenue than Kuaibo. If Kuaibo only relies on past resources, it won't last long.

Baidu's child, promoted through search

The growth of BaiDu Player cannot be separated from the resources of its parent company, Baidu. Compared to Kuaibo, BaiDu Player could be said to have been born with a golden spoon. Whatever Kuaibo had, BaiDu Player had it too, and whatever Kuaibo didn't have, BaiDu Player still had it. In terms of promotion, BaiDu Player used attractive slogans such as HD, free, and early access to blockbuster movies, hitting all pain points. Meanwhile, following Baidu's bidding ranking style, naturally, good resources were given to their own product. In search suggestions, keywords related to BaiDu Player were inserted (if it weren't for promotion, BaiDu Player could easily rank in the top three in searches, so why couldn't Wang Feng make it to the front page?).

In addition to directing traffic to BaiDu Player when searching for movies and other video resources, other related video categories, such as variety shows and TV series, also directed traffic to BaiDu Player. On Baidu Video's website, there are 3-5 links within one page guiding users to download BaiDu Player. Any Baidu-related content involving videos can be said to have entry points set up for BaiDu Player. Baidu gave BaiDu Player significant resources in both search and video areas, and since its own resources don't cost money, it's just a few lines of code, it would be foolish not to use them.

China's internet circle has various types of alliances, and Baidu Alliance is one of them. Last year, Baidu Alliance shared 2 billion yuan with its partners, and this year it is expected to exceed 4 billion yuan. The strong profit motive makes it almost impossible for small and medium-sized site owners to refuse Baidu. This has helped BaiDu Player quite a bit, as Baidu spares no effort in cooperating with various sites and software to pave the way for its own product. Especially for these small and medium-sized movie sites, embedding BaiDu Player's online playback within their web pages. Baidu can bring traffic to them, and the websites rely on promoting downloads and advertisements to survive. No one would reject money, so naturally they wouldn't say no.

In the rapid rise of Kuaibo, it was inseparable from a certain type of website. From posting Xunlei download addresses to posting Kuaibo playback addresses, adult websites have also evolved. Kuaibo converted BT seeds into online viewing mode, which was no different from Xunlei's promotion of pornography through download addresses; it merely advanced the viewing process by eliminating download time. Kuaibo did not impose any restrictions on adult information, claiming that it only provided technical support to movie sites and did not operate content itself. These adult informations contributed to Kuaibo's rise, but also led to Kuaibo's current brand image. The borderline pornographic promotion even made Kuaibo a representative of "watching tools". Nowadays, BaiDu Player's status on adult websites is no less than Kuaibo. Due to the similarity in technology, embedding BaiDu Player's online playback on adult websites is not difficult. The conversion rate of such adult websites is still quite high, after all, people will try installing any player to watch videos.

The shift from ownership to viewing rights

With the increase in network bandwidth and the richness of film and television resources, user demand for watching videos is shifting from ownership to viewing rights. Previously, due to the lack of high-definition content, users had high search costs, and after finding content, they would use download tools to save it locally for viewing. Nowadays, resources are gradually becoming abundant, whether legitimate or pirated, the cost of searching for content has decreased significantly. This has triggered a shift in user needs: I no longer need local ownership rights, as online playback that satisfies viewing rights meets my needs. For Kuaibo, simply providing smooth and clear solutions for users and site owners is no longer an advantage, and its tool value will gradually fade.

When bandwidth increases and content becomes sufficiently rich, users' requirements for online viewing of film and television resources become higher, with smoothness and high definition always being hard demands. Kuaibo is now providing technical support to large Chinese video websites, with current partners including Phoenix New Media, JiongTV, and Ku6. Its main selling point is helping video websites save huge server bandwidth costs. Kuaibo provides technical support, turning around to provide services to video enterprises, offering backend solutions for online viewing. The shift in viewing rights also brings new opportunities, as users have richer application scenarios while watching videos. This is also the reason why Kuaibo promotes Kuaibo Big Screen and Xiao Fang smart TV boxes. For Baidu, functionally speaking, the launched Baidu PlayBar doesn't offer much novelty. In the future, the competition will still focus on search and resources, and whoever can provide the content users need the fastest will be the key.

Piracy remains an issue

Yesterday, Tencent Video, Sohu Video, Letv.com, and other video websites jointly issued the "Declaration of Joint Action Against Online Video Piracy," stating that they will jointly combat increasingly serious online video piracy and illegal linking behaviors by Baidu and Kuaibo, and claim 3 billion yuan in compensation from Baidu.

"Without the permission of Sogou and Youku, Baidu arbitrarily uses its software such as Baidu Video and BaiDu Player to provide the public with the playback and even downloading of a large number of films and TV shows through targeted links, peer-to-peer transmission, embedded browser playback plugins, and active recommendations. Baidu's infringement on video websites covers four major fields: PC web pages, PC clients, mobile clients, and TV boxes, mainly through four products: Baidu Video Search, BaiDu Player, Baidu Video APP, and Baidu PlayBar. The forms of infringement mainly include illegal linking and providing comprehensive support such as technology, traffic, and income to pirated video websites."

Xunlei once stumbled in its IPO due to copyright issues. Now, BaiDu Player and Kuaibo inevitably face copyright problems. Baidu claims to be combating piracy, but this has been going on for a long time with much talk but little action. Piracy is not just a phenomenon; it has a complete industrial chain. The various interest relationships intertwined in this chain make it difficult to eradicate in the short term.

In China, being noticed by a large company is not necessarily a good thing for small companies. The probability of being acquired for a lump sum of cash is still relatively low. After noticing, large companies can quickly develop a similar product, add their platform advantages, invest more resources, and occupy part of the market without much pressure as long as the product isn't too bad. BaiDu Player can be considered a replica of Kuaibo, but it has many more resources than Kuaibo, naturally developing smoothly. However, this is not a good thing. When the problem of piracy is unresolved, the risks for large companies are much greater than for small ones. No matter how big BaiDu Player grows, as long as piracy exists, it can never be completely whitewashed.
