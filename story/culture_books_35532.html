Office Survival Rules

■ Always maintain your professional level in office politics.

■ Only play the games that are being played, not the ones you want to play.

■ Be kind to people and greet every colleague with a smile.

■ Respect others and don't be arrogant.

■ Don't make enemies, even if they're seemingly insignificant individuals.

■ Learn to cooperate and don't burn bridges.

■ Build mutually beneficial relationships with key figures.

■ When expressing opinions, focus on the issue, not the person.

■ Don't form cliques or spread rumors.

■ Remember the rule that you can't wrap fire with paper; don't stir up trouble.

■ Keep in mind that your boss is one of your important clients.

■ Don't dream of the day when you will step on your boss.

■ Help your boss demonstrate their professional competence.

■ Be loyal not only to your boss but also to your subordinates.

■ Strive to be consistent in words and actions, decisive yet calm, without appearing overly ambitious.

■ Handle office romances with caution and avoid dating in the office.
