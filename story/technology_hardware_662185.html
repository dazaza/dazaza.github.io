Variety management does not mean that chain drugstores replace the categories of products they operate without a common category.

by yashan2769 on 2012-02-09 22:45:21

Category management refers to the practice of chain pharmacies dividing their products into different categories and managing each category as a fundamental business activity, emphasizing the provision of superior products and services to consumers in order to improve operational efficiency and effectiveness. This article analyzes the existing problems in category management for chain pharmacies in our country and proposes solutions.

1. Four Common Problems in Brand Management and Solutions

1. Lack of Systematic Planning

Issue: Most chain pharmacies do not have a dedicated category management implementation department. Category management aims to establish a comprehensive and efficient operating system, which includes techniques, methods, and principles that must be systematically planned. Currently, some chain pharmacies only feel the need to implement category management or believe they cannot fall behind others who are doing it. Often, without understanding systematic planning, they merely conduct training, hold a few meetings, and then proceed with implementation. Additionally, the existing power and interest structures between procurement, operations, and marketing within the chain can hinder the implementation of category management, requiring strong organizational restructuring and evaluation mechanisms to break through these barriers.

Solution: Establish a category management project team led by the general manager or branch manager to comprehensively analyze the SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) and develop a systematic category management enhancement plan. Promote this approach through institutionalization and an evaluation and reward mechanism.

2. Lack of Budget Allocation

Issue: Category management requires analyzing sales data and conducting consumer research within the business district. This involves defining categories, making category plans and adjustments, formulating category strategies, and developing new category cultivation plans. All of this requires hardware and software investment as well as cost allocation, which many chain pharmacies are unwilling to spend on.

Solution: When creating the annual operational plan, allocate a budget specifically for category management. Whether it's for the analytical information system used in category management or for adjusting marketing activities related to categories, there should be budgetary allocations. Relying solely on suppliers to fund these efforts will not achieve a complete consumer-centric approach.

3. Lack of Clear Corporate Positioning Makes Category Management Like Blind Men Grope for an Elephant

Issue: Most chain pharmacies currently lack clear positioning. Without a clear strategic position, it is impossible to know the target consumer group and thus effectively manage brands based on their needs. It is akin to trying to build a skyscraper on an unstable foundation.

Solution: Understand that the core idea of good category management is consumer demand orientation and satisfaction. All marketing and management logic starts from fulfilling human needs. Our ancestors summarized all business management and service methods as "Confucianism," meaning fulfilling the "needs" of people. If you satisfy the needs, you can succeed; otherwise, it will be difficult. However, the condition is that you first define the group you serve, your positioning, and the corresponding business model. Different groups require different categories. For example, a pharmacy positioned to attract young adult customers or chronic disease patients might consider blood pressure medication as a target category, while a regular community convenience pharmacy might treat it as a conventional category. Therefore, clear business model positioning is essential for effective category management.

4. Category Management Limited by GSP, Medical Insurance Card Swiping, and High-Margin Products

Issue: Pharmacies practicing category management differ from supermarkets as they cannot fully adhere to market sales rules. Additionally, high-margin product push commissions exist, but pharmacies must first comply with GSP (Good Supply Practice). Some product sales are not the result of market-driven choices but are influenced by medical insurance定点 stores and consumption patterns. High commission-based customer pushes also do not reflect natural sales, leading to errors in demand statistics and affecting brand management and decision-making subjectivity and scientific accuracy.

Solution: Consider these issues comprehensively and make informed choices based on the level of consumer demand satisfaction.

2. Issues with Category Definition and Solutions

Meaning of Category Definition: A category refers to a set of products that consumers perceive as interrelated or substitutable, satisfying similar needs and difficult to manage together. Categories are formed by specific products.

1. Issues with Category Definition

The inability to define categories according to the marketing attributes of goods is the biggest problem. For instance, the definition of "head care products" includes all products and tools that provide external and internal hair care for consumers. Subcategories include shampoo, dandruff remover, conditioner, hair styling, gray hair treatment, and softening agents. These categories are sold together in retail space management. However, due to GSP requirements, pharmaceutical categories that theoretically meet the same needs may not be sold together. For example, cold medicines include Western medicine, traditional Chinese medicine, herbal decoctions, health supplements for treating colds, Rx cold products, and OTC cold products, which cannot be managed and sold together. This is an issue in pharmaceutical category management. Additionally, many products have similar therapeutic functions and high substitutability due to the limited number of disease symptoms, making it challenging to group similar categories together.

2. Solutions for Category Definition

We must understand that defining pharmaceutical categories based on pharmacological properties differs significantly from true consumer-centered category definitions. Categories are meant to satisfy needs. Without violating combined medication treatment principles, try to group larger and smaller categories together as much as possible. For example, if a cold is accompanied by coughing, connect it with cough medicine; if there is fever, pair it with antipyretics and thermometers; if there is inflammation, use some anti-infection drugs; and combine them with immunity-boosting and vitamin-related medications. According to pharmacological properties, these different medicines are scattered across shelves categorized as cold medicines, anti-infection medicines, cough suppressants, devices, and nutritional supplements, causing inconvenience for consumers. Therefore, pharmacy category management should both fundamentally comply with but not be limited by GSP requirements and aim to fulfill consumer needs for treating diseases and assisting in treatment. Define products that can be used together and associated sales as one category. Based on this, implement space management. If they cannot be displayed together, use empty box displays, reminder placements, and optimize product selection based on operational gross margin requirements to enrich category definitions. Additionally, chain pharmacies must have product managers or market personnel knowledgeable in pharmacology, medicine, and consumer demands to study disease segmentation and optimize rich marketing for new category definitions.

3. Issues with Category Roles and Solutions

Just like Peking Opera has roles such as Sheng, Dan, Jing, Mo, and Chou, different medicines play different roles in pharmacies. Category roles refer to the role that a product or class of products plays in meeting consumer needs. For example, some categories bring foot traffic, others bring sales volume, some bring profits, and others highlight store images. Currently, the most issues lie in two aspects:

1. Lack of Scientific Standards for Category Roles

One issue is that pharmacies define category roles not based on consumer demand-oriented marketing but on their own gross margins. Categories are divided into high, medium, and low-margin product clusters, giving different attention levels based on profit contribution, reflecting self-interest orientation. Another method divides categories into A, B, and C classes based on sales share percentages or importance into core products, basic products, QR products (Quick Response Products), and supplementary or regional products, focusing solely on their own sales.

Another issue is the conflict between role classification and GSP management. Medicines are special commodities with pharmaceutical classifications like Rx and OTC areas, decoction piece areas, etc., and must be further divided based on treatment细分 scopes. These are not marketing classifications like beer and diapers in supermarkets, posing legal and regulatory challenges when implemented in pharmacies.

2. Breakthrough in Category Roles

First, classify based on consumer needs and marketing principles as the primary standard, assigning initial attention levels accordingly. Then classify based on the pharmacy’s gross margin for secondary attention levels. Finally, consider GSP requirements for tertiary classification. It must be clear that category role division primarily focuses on fulfilling consumer needs. Pharmacies should not be overly constrained by GSP and high-margin considerations; otherwise, category management will be difficult to fully implement. Based on consumer needs and marketing principles, drug category roles can be defined as target categories, regular categories, seasonal categories, necessary categories, and immediate benefit categories.

4. Issues with Category Strategies and Solutions

1. Existing Issues

Major issues include the lack of active strategies for category cultivation and promotion, passively waiting for in-store sales to test whether products can sell and whether reductions are needed, and relying heavily on suppliers.

Another issue is the lack of differentiation in category construction based on business model positioning and execution, often adopting authoritative strategies without distinction.

The third issue is the lack of category strategy construction reflecting category roles. Different categories play different roles in pharmacies, but chain pharmacies fail to develop marketing strategies tailored to different category roles.

2. Resolution Suggestions

Following the principle of combined medication usage, focus first on people and diseases,细分 minor illnesses, and equip corresponding categories. Each small category should have branded products, high-margin products, and products related to combined medication usage.

Learn from industrial enterprises by establishing category management managers (equivalent to product managers) who develop annual marketing strategies for each old category and conduct sales analysis and improvement plans for each major category.

We know that from "knowing" to "doing" is a long journey, and from "doing" to "doing well" is even longer. Currently, chain pharmacy category management is still in the transition phase from "knowing" to "doing," far from reaching the "doing" stage. "The revolution has not yet succeeded; comrades must continue to strive."
