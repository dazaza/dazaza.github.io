Yiwu boss relies on online store to open up domestic sales channel

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:26:31

Zirconia has replaced sapphire, and glittering Rhinestones have substituted diamonds. In the past few days, a "Diana Sapphire Engagement Ring" that costs $2 became a hot search item. Just at the end of December, when the engagement ring of Britain's Prince William appeared, imitations in Yiwu, Zhejiang Province were on shelves within 24 hours. The Yiwu MW Name望 Decorations Co., Ltd put this "Diana Sapphire Engagement Ring" on the counter of Alibaba.com, the world's largest procurement wholesale market. At the same time, it was also listed on Taobao stores, making people admire Yiwu bosses' keen sense once again.

Yiwu has always been strong in wholesaling and weak in retailing. However, this weakness is now being addressed. The rapid development of online retailing has a strong driving effect on Yiwu's wholesale industry. Liu Wengao, Deputy Secretary-General of Yiwu Online Business Association, revealed to reporters yesterday that according to relevant data, Yiwu's e-commerce transaction volume exceeded 50 billion yuan in 2010. And from relevant parties, we learned that in 2009, the total transaction volume of Yiwu's collective market was 55.6 billion yuan, reaching 62.116 billion yuan in 2010. The online goods sales which started only a few years ago is equivalent to recreating an entire Yiwu market.

70% of Yiwu online store purchases are local

"Previously, we basically did not do domestic sales, now we start selling ten or dozens at a time," said Chen Xiao, a stall owner in Yiwu International Trade City. In late 2008, affected by the financial crisis, many orders disappeared, so she simply hung a sign at the entrance of her shop, 'There is stock inside, accepting small orders'. Many inventories were thus consumed by these small customers. Now these small orders are increasing more and more, accounting for 10%~20% of annual sales.

"In our early stage, 90% of the goods were purchased locally in Yiwu," the person in charge of Hengyou Home Goods smiled and told the reporter. And more small shopkeepers who entered the online store army with low-cost investment treat shopping in the international trade city as their essential homework, "My first business was buying several bottles of shampoo from the trade city and selling them online, earning a few cents." Little Ding, who now earns over ten thousand yuan per month, told the reporter.

Liu Wengao indicated that the estimated amount of purchases made by Yiwu online merchants from the Yiwu market and local manufacturers is about 70%, and for those just starting their businesses, this proportion is over 90%.

In a warehouse of an online store, the picker was putting a pair of thermal slippers into a box, and the shoes, priced at over 50 yuan online, had packaging marked with "Yiwu XX E-commerce Co., Ltd." "They all personally deliver the goods here," said the procurement manager of the online store. Here, quite a number of small and medium-sized enterprises have emerged that develop through the online store platform, and he often encounters factory salespersons knocking on his door. Mr. Wu, who owns his own factory, revealed to the reporter that his originally produced socks were all sold in bulk in the market, but since last year, a considerable part has been transferred to supply online stores, "It's just that the production capacity can't keep up, the demand is very large."

From the statistics department, we learned that as of October last year, Yiwu had 136,500 various market entities, an increase of nearly 5,000 compared to 131,600 in the first half of the year. And a large part of these enterprises aim at online store sales channels.

The "picky" online store managers promote Yiwu manufacturing improvements

"However, we now have many requirements for local Yiwu manufacturing, if the product is not good, we will choose new suppliers," said the procurement manager of a Crown Store.

Speaking about the "picky" nature of online store managers towards suppliers, Liu Wengao said: "The development of network economy has greatly touched Yiwu's manufacturing industry, promoting the quality improvement of Yiwu products." In the past, goods produced by Yiwu enterprises went through market distribution, level by level wholesale and distribution, and by the time they truly faced consumers, they had already gone through several stages. Some goods, even if the quality was not good, consumers had no way out after purchasing them. "Online shopping directly changes this pattern, buyers have the right to give neutral or bad reviews and return goods. They have two or three days to review the quality of the goods." After cutting off a large number of intermediate links, the face-to-face relationship between sellers and buyers makes sellers control the quality of goods for the reputation of their shops, thus urging factories to improve quality and require production enterprises to pay more attention to practicality and cost-performance ratio.

"However, as far as I know, some plastic product enterprises have changed significantly, they dare not use recycled materials, there have been improvements in color differences and plastic thickness," Liu Wengao said.

Perfecting the industrial chain is more important than capital

"The formation of the industrial chain is more important than capital." In Yiwu, we can feel this more profoundly, sources, sales, and logistics, and each part can be further refined and extended. For example, in logistics flow, because Yiwu itself is the world's largest small commodity market, it has a very complete logistics system. Under the promotion of the E-commerce Association, a logistics structure specifically serving online store sellers has gradually formed, "from Yiwu to the whole country". "From the order data of Huitong, we can understand the rapid development of e-commerce," Liu Wengao said, when Huitong first entered Yiwu, it received about 500 orders per day, and now it is close to 30,000 orders per day.

In addition, during my interviews, I deeply felt that these 80s-born who are immersed in e-commerce are very familiar with each other. Many small bosses involved in e-commerce are also from the 80s generation, and the supply model of "cost price plus one cent" seems to be common in their circle. This cost advantage makes Yiwu products more competitive on the Internet.

Expert Perspective

E-commerce

Brings Yiwu Products Upgrades

Zheng Qingliang, Director of the Department of E-commerce at Yiwu Industrial and Commercial College

The growth points of domestic trade in the small commodity city are basically driven by Yiwu online merchants. E-commerce has brought new changes to Yiwu's economic pulling and business models, and has become a new growth point for Yiwu's economy. In the next few years, it will rapidly develop at double the speed every year.

In addition, due to the special attention paid by online merchants to business reputation, the requirements for products will also increase, substantially driving the upgrading of Yiwu products. We found during visits to production plants that Yiwu's production technology is not bad, and many equipment are imported. As the factory owners say, "A 20-yuan umbrella can be made, and a 6-yuan umbrella can also be made, all following the demand." The high transparency of network information also makes many production enterprises increasingly value quality.

1+1>2, several years later, Yiwu's e-commerce can not only recreate a Yiwu International Trade City, but also surpass it.

Taobao Data

Sells 48,000 items per minute

On average, 48,000 items are sold per minute, including 846 pieces of clothing, 36 mobile phones, 880 cosmetics, 85 books, 53 packs of diapers... Yesterday, at the Water Cube in Beijing, Taobao's annual event, Taobao.com released a series of data under the hot online shopping trend in 2010 and revealed the popular trends of online consumption in 2010 and 2011. Taobao data showed that in 2010, Taobao's registered users reached 370 million, with 8 billion online products, and the most visited Taobao daily was 60 million people; simultaneously, B2C business represented by Taobao Mall quadrupled in 2010.

In 2010, the average number of transactions per user on Taobao increased by 35% compared to 2009, and in the recent month, there were 13.5 million buyers who made over five transactions on Taobao, with the highest single-day transaction amount reaching 1.95 billion yuan. "In the future, consumers, manufacturers, third-party partners, logistics and other aspects of the e-commerce industry chain will have higher requirements for the platform," Taobao CEO Lu Zhaoxi said yesterday.

According to data released by analysis agency Analysys Mason, in the third quarter of 2010, Taobao Mall led the domestic B2C market with a 33.5% market share, more than twice that of the second place.

By the end of last year, Taobao created over 1.823 million direct employment opportunities, and if calculated based on each person opening a store on Taobao, it would drive 2.85 related industries' employment opportunities. Thus, Taobao has created over 5 million job positions.
