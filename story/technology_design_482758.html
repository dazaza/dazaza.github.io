Crystal collection - the best time to hide crystals

by shunjing001 on 2011-08-29 10:58:20

The Donghai County of Lianyungang in northern China used to be China's largest quartz production area, but now its output has decreased and it has transformed into China's largest quartz trading hub. Therefore, it is easier to find larger pieces of quartz in the north; whereas in southern China, for example in cities surrounding Guangzhou, there are many small quartz processing plants, mostly producing bracelets, necklaces, pendants, etc., which are generally of ordinary quality. It is not easy to find good quartz items. Therefore, if you want to find good collectibles, one option is to go to Donghai, another is to visit some crystal carving masters, and a third option is to go to crystal workshops. You can also pay attention to auctions, but crystal items rarely appear at auctions, so it is uncertain when they will be available. Once, a senior executive from an international renowned auction house approached Mo Daoming, hoping that he could provide a batch of collections for the auction, but was politely declined by Mo Daoming.

Now is a good time to collect crystals: firstly, people haven't fully realized the value of crystals yet, and the level of attention is still low; secondly, crystals have hardly been hyped in the market, and are currently at their price bottom. Therefore, starting to collect now will have great potential for future appreciation. Mo Daoming revealed that there are still opportunities to find bargains in the market, and such chances are quite numerous. Unlike jade and emerald, or the four famous stones, these mature categories of collection almost have no chance of finding bargains. Mo Daoming reminded that precisely because of these reasons, one must have high standards when collecting crystals, only the best ones have collection value. The so-called best ones need to be evaluated in terms of size, color, clarity, carving craftsmanship, impurities, historical value, etc.

Some people say that quartz has a large output, the mineral reserves are still abundant, and quartz shops can be seen everywhere on the streets, so the collection value of quartz is not great. Mo Daoming pointed out that this actually confuses collector-grade quartz with ordinary quartz. Although there are many quartz products in the market and quartz shops can be seen everywhere, collector-grade quartz is very rare. He said: "It's even harder to find than jadeite and jade. When you go to the raw material production area or an auction, you may still be able to find jadeite and jade, but for collector-grade quartz, it all depends on luck, even the raw material production area may not have any." He disclosed that good quartz mainly circulates within this circle.

In recent years, the output of quartz in Brazil and Uruguay has remained stable, while the output of Donghai quartz in China has become very scarce. With the decrease in production, the price of collector-grade quartz has continuously increased, rising five to six times in just a few years. Occasionally appearing at auctions, they often fetch hundreds of thousands to millions of yuan. As a result, there is now also fake quartz on the market, but the difficulty of making high-quality fake quartz is very high, so there aren't many successful fakes.

There is a concept that needs to be clarified: the so-called Austrian quartz and Swarovski quartz sold in the market, as well as some quartz wine glasses, are just artificial quartz or imitation quartz, essentially glass, with a higher content of silicon dioxide. People who don't know better might think that these special glasses are synthetic quartz, but in fact, real synthetic quartz has a very strict definition and can replace natural quartz in electronic industries, ultrasonic generators, optical instruments, and analytical instruments, functions that these special glasses do not possess.
