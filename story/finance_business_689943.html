I habitually opened QQ, habitually clicked the stealth option, habitually put on my headphones, and habitually looked at those flashing avatars. Silently, silently, silently, I just hoped for one month, two months, three months... Yet deep in my heart, I yearned to see which avatar would flash again, possibly letting me 'hear' her voice once more. My avatar used to shine for her, and it dimmed for her too. But what remains unchanged is that sentiment.

I can't remember clearly which day it was, as if it's so far away. It's said there's a chat tool called QQ, and thus I had the courage for my flashing avatar. Friends increased one by one, acquaintances joined one by one, and the avatar kept shining brightly. One day, from a close friend, I learned her number. Thus, in my list, there was an additional flashing avatar - a lovely avatar. From that day on, among all the people in the entire list, everyone else's presence no longer mattered.

Hastily, going online and waiting for her became my habit. Slowly, thinking about her became my daily required course. I lit up my avatar, letting her know of my existence; I lit up my avatar, hoping for her appearance. Through her jumping avatar, I seemed to be able to see her face, understanding her joys and sorrows.

If things continued this way, if that day I hadn't crossed the boundary of friendship, if she refused me the first time and I retreated, if we could have remained friends afterward without insisting...

But there are no ifs in reality, even though sometimes it feels empty and sometimes it feels helpless...!

From a certain moment onward, her avatar on my QQ turned gray, a dead gray, never lighting up again. And my avatar also turned gray, never lighting up again either. But it wasn't a dead gray. Beneath it lay a heart filled with hope. I always hoped that when I went stealth, she would come online. Even just seeing her flashing avatar would satisfy me. I became a genuine member of the stealth group and later, it became a habit.

Sometimes, I realized that habits are easily formed but hard to change, especially when others need you to change.

Later, I found out that among many close friends who are stealth users, some avoid meeting certain people or want to see certain people. Perhaps only the stealth users themselves understand!

QQ carries the dreams of many people and continues to fulfill its mission in the symphony of logins and logouts. But in the eyes of some people, it seems like a pain. I've also thought about changing my number, but I know that some things are unforgettable forever.

When love has become a thing of the past, when being stealth has become a habit, what's left for me is silence and numbness, resignation and heartache. Or perhaps at least there's still a new hope. In such a night, is your QQ online? When your QQ flashes, for whom does it shine? In such a night, is your QQ stealthed? When your QQ dims, for whom are you stealthed?
