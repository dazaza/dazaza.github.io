Exclusive Interview with Liu Chuanzhi: Lenovo Holdings May Acquire Hotel Business

by tuoqing1188 on 2011-08-04 09:55:18

Chairman and President of Legend Holdings, Liu Chuanzhi

Sina Technology News, June 3rd early morning news, Chairman of Legend Holdings Liu Chuanzhi accepted an exclusive interview with Sina Technology in Chengdu yesterday afternoon. He revealed that Legend Holdings has selected financial advisory companies for its Hong Kong listing, and at the same time, to obtain a good market value during the listing, the company's business might also expand into the hotel sector.

Video: Exclusive dialogue between Sina Technology and Liu Chuanzhi

Media source: Sina Technology

Selected Financial Advisory Company

Liu Chuanzhi disclosed during the exclusive interview yesterday afternoon that Legend Holdings has already selected financial advisory companies, and the preparatory work before the listing has been arranged or is being arranged. He also disclosed that the highest management of Legend roughly reviews the listing matters once a month.

Legend Holdings was established in 1984, founded by the Computing Technology Institute of the Chinese Academy of Sciences with an investment of 200,000 RMB and 11 scientific researchers. In 2010, the comprehensive turnover of Legend Holdings reached 146.6 billion RMB, total assets were 112.1 billion RMB, and the total number of employees was nearly 40,000.

Since A-share markets in China do not allow simultaneous listings of parent and subsidiary companies, according to the plan of Legend Holdings, the earliest possible listing will be in Hong Kong in 2014. According to Liu Chuanzhi, much of his current focus is on the listing matters.

Possible Expansion into Hotel Business

Currently, Legend Holdings is an investment holding company involved in multiple unrelated diversified industries, currently covering IT, investment, real estate, modern agriculture, and modern services, among other fields. It owns five core subsidiaries: Lenovo Group, Digital China, Legend Investment, Rongke Zhide, and Hony Capital.

Liu Chuanzhi said that Legend Holdings hopes to ensure that after the listing, it can grow at an annual rate of 30%. "Therefore, we need to invest or develop some businesses at this point that can show profits later, such as projects in modern agriculture and chemical materials."

He revealed that if the profit from hotels is indeed good and complete, Legend Holdings may also consider acquisitions or investments. He pointed out that the principle for selecting industries by Legend Holdings is that the business can guarantee a certain market value when the company goes public, and promote rapid profit growth after the listing.

In January this year, Liu Chuanzhi had mentioned to The Asian Wall Street Journal at the World Economic Forum in Davos that Legend Holdings is considering acquiring a U.S. medical equipment company and entering the hotel and catering industry. (Reported by Luo Liang from Chengdu)

Below is the full interview transcript:

Sina Technology: First, thank you, Mr. Liu, for accepting the exclusive interview with Sina Technology. Now, Legend Holdings' business has entered sectors like real estate, agriculture, modern services, even coal chemical industries. What are the selection criteria for Legend Holdings when entering these industries?

Liu Chuanzhi: Legend Holdings plans to go public between 2014 and 2016, and there needs to be a certain market value at the time of listing. This market value comes from the profits at that time. For instance, Lenovo Group itself has already gone public, so its profits have to be significantly discounted. Additionally, Legend Holdings' investment business is also discounted. Except for the real estate business of Rongke Zhide, we must rely on some new businesses. Thus, we added businesses like modern services, which can quickly form profits.

These businesses mainly depend on investment purchases, but at the same time, we must ensure that after the listing, we can maintain an annual growth rate of 30%. Therefore, we need to invest or develop businesses ourselves that can showcase profits later. For example, agriculture and chemical materials projects require preparation and investment beforehand.

Sina Technology: Apart from these industries, do you have any specific plans to enter new areas? Previously, there were reports that Legend Holdings plans to acquire hotels in the U.S.?

Liu Chuanzhi: Acquiring hotels is also possible; it can be considered part of the service industry. If the profits from hotels are indeed good and complete, they can be acquired or invested in. Doing so would contribute to the profits at the time of listing. Our general principle is to ensure a certain market value at the time of listing, and after the listing, ensure high-speed profit growth. Additionally, these ventures should benefit the national economy and people's livelihood more significantly.

Sina Technology: Recently, renowned economist Xiang Songzu used Lenovo as a negative case study for specialization, reminding companies to resist the temptation of diversification. How do you view the specialization versus diversification of enterprises?

Liu Chuanzhi: I am willing to further discuss this with them. Why most companies fail to specialize well—what are the problems? After studying this issue, Legend Holdings made its decision. For example, the computer industry and the investment industry seem worlds apart, yet Lenovo's computers have ranked third globally and continue to improve. Both Legend Investment and Hony Capital are recognized as leaders in the investment industry.

We have indeed done well, and we have studied why we succeeded. Without going into too much detail, it has a lot to do with organizational structure, talent selection, incentives, culture, etc. We have thoroughly studied these aspects and then moved forward. When Lenovo started investing in 2001 and ventured into real estate, during one of my lectures at Cheung Kong Graduate School of Business, an EMBA student said, "Mr. Liu, you have been very successful in computers, but please don't enter the investment field; otherwise, it could lead to..." Ten years later, we have done quite well in these fields. As for other areas, give us a few more years, and you will see whether we succeed or not.

Sina Technology: With all these businesses under Lenovo now, how do you allocate your time?

Liu Chuanzhi: The key is that I don't need to allocate my time because everything runs smoothly. If I tried to manage everything myself, it would only make things worse. Each of our fields has its own responsible individuals who are experts or owners in their respective areas, carefully selected over time. This is one of the main reasons we have succeeded. If I relied solely on allocating my time, things wouldn't work out.

Sina Technology: Yesterday, Lenovo Group conducted a merger in Germany, acquiring shares of German electronics manufacturer Medion. What role did you personally play in this acquisition?

Liu Chuanzhi: Actually, I didn’t play a significant role. Six months ago, I met someone from the acquired company and learned about the purpose of the acquisition. The primary goal was to open up the European consumer market. This aligns with our strategic objectives, the price was reasonable, and the person’s values aligned closely with ours. Under these circumstances, the board supported the move.

At the board meeting, the directors required the lowest possible price while still aligning with the strategy. At this moment, I expressed that the strategy should be prioritized. I showed support, and everyone agreed easily since it was a progressive decision.

Sina Technology: You recently stated that the acquisition of IBM's PC business has been successful. During the six years of this process, what moments stand out in your mind?

Liu Chuanzhi: Of course, the moment the financial crisis hit stands out. The financial crisis was actually a necessary result of management issues. The financial crisis was merely the trigger; the real bomb was already there. The bomb itself was the cultural integration problem. Cultural integration refers to whether people from different backgrounds and countries can work together effectively.

During this cooperation, Lenovo indeed encountered some problems. We repeatedly examined how to solve these issues, figured out solutions, and replaced key personnel accordingly. Yuanqing organized an excellent team. Now, I believe the success of the acquisition is not due to performance alone but mainly because we have a great team. This team formulates good strategies and executes them resolutely, forming a positive corporate culture. This is my standard for measuring success. Although challenges and setbacks may arise during progress, there won't be any catastrophic issues.

Sina Technology: Recently, you mentioned during a financial report meeting that you enjoy playing games on a Pad. Over the years, have you developed any new insights regarding the computer industry?

Liu Chuanzhi: These insights mean that we realized quite early on that this industry would undergo significant changes. Primarily, after the advent of mobile wireless technology, we believed that its connection to the internet would inevitably bring major changes to product forms and back-end operations. Clearly, the emergence of mobile wireless internet has made customer demands more diverse. Simultaneously, product forms will become increasingly simplified.

For example, a phone is small, but the backend needs to be strengthened, like cloud devices, which represent a future development trend. In this situation, we need a vanguard team to monitor these trends and invest in research and development accordingly. Frankly speaking, we currently lack the ability to lead the trend. If we cannot lead, it will cause significant problems because others won't follow us, and if we blindly follow others, we will die.

Sina Technology: Recently, Lenovo Group has focused on mobile internet as a crucial development area. Could you share your communication with Yuanqing regarding your determination and confidence in the mobile internet domain?

Liu Chuanzhi: Yuanqing and the main management team of Lenovo are extremely determined. I personally witnessed in the 1980s when the computer industry itself underwent fundamental changes—when traditional computers transformed into personal computers. Many large companies insisted on following the old path, leading to their eventual elimination. Now, another opportunity arises where product forms may fundamentally change. In this context, we must pay extra attention to clarifying the path ahead and preparing for necessary investments, which is our consensus. Therefore, our determination is immense.

Our confidence stems from the belief that we must succeed. We feel confident but also prepared to withstand potential setbacks. Currently, we are not the trailblazers; being trailblazers entails greater risks and pressures. However, even if we are not the trailblazers, we still face significant risks and pressures. If we do not make necessary investments in advance, we will be eliminated. Fortunately, Lenovo now has substantial human resources and economic strength, such as having 3 billion USD in liquid funds, allowing us to handle emergencies.

Sina Technology: Regarding this acquisition strategy, I wonder if you and Yuanqing have discussed continuing it?

Liu Chuanzhi: Yes, we will continue, but acquisitions must meet our conditions. Every acquisition has a purpose. The acquisition of IBM's PC business aimed for three things: acquiring a good brand, acquiring technology, and acquiring international resources to enable us to go global. All three objectives have been largely achieved.

The acquisition of Japan's NEC was primarily to gain a larger share in mature markets, while the acquisition of this German company aimed to expand consumer-related businesses in mature markets. Since we are unfamiliar with these markets and have struggled to enter them in the past, having someone guide us in is very clear in purpose. We will not blindly spend money to expand scale.

Sina Technology: Finally, Legend Holdings is planning to list as early as 2014. Is there any substantial progress, such as confirming investment banks?

Liu Chuanzhi: The financial advisor has been confirmed. Matters such as what preparations need to be made for Legend's IPO in 2014 have been fully arranged or are being arranged. Additionally, which profits to extract in the first wave, from which fields, and how to proceed subsequently, all have dedicated teams considering these issues.

Currently, the top management of Legend roughly reviews these matters monthly. Besides me, the other executives meet weekly to review and continuously move towards our predetermined strategic route. By carefully stepping along this path and solidifying it, we will be ready to sprint ahead.

Sina Technology: Thank you very much for accepting the exclusive interview with Sina Technology.

Liu Chuanzhi: Thank you.
