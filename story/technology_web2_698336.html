And then it will always...

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 08:41:33

A long time ago, many people got lost in the virtual world of the Internet. It was during this time that they came to know each other through the connection of the network. Perhaps from this perspective, everyone would think that the Internet is originally virtual, and such a situation would not become reality, even if they really trusted each other's existence and the mutual affection between them.

At that time, he was in his third year of junior high school; she was just entering her second year. Their acquaintance could only be described as an accident.

At that time, she had just started getting into the Internet, just learned how to use QQ, and everything about it seemed so strange to her. An accidental opportunity led them to meet on QQ, introducing themselves to each other. Their lives were similar, their school troubles alike. From then on, almost every day, the two would chat online, sharing funny incidents from the previous day or confiding in each other about the unpleasant events, like failing exams or conflicts with friends. But it seemed these trivial matters deepened their feelings for each other.

As the final exams approached, both needed time to review, so they temporarily left the Internet but began exchanging letters, usually one per week. They continued sharing with each other, adding more care and... some worries.

After the final exams, the so-called winter break arrived. Unlike the previous year when they went home separately, this year they both stayed indoors by unspoken agreement. They resumed their daily chats on QQ. This sweetness lasted for a long time, making her feel like she had already started liking him, though she couldn't be sure because she had never liked anyone before, not even in her first love.

One day, a good friend asked her what their relationship was now. She hesitated and said they were just ordinary friends. The friend disagreed: "What do you mean, just ordinary friends? You're spending so much on phone bills because of him; your grades have dropped because of him; you've wasted so much time on the Internet because of him..."

Alone at home one day, she thought for a long time, finally picking up the phone and dialing that familiar number. On the other end, a familiar voice answered. All the words she had prepared vanished from her mind. They chatted aimlessly until she suddenly remembered what her friend had said and asked, "What are we now?"

He paused and said, "What do you think?"

"I don't know, but I feel like I have some kind of attraction towards you."

"I feel the same way about you, but I'm not sure if it's love, and I'm afraid to say it."

"Actually, I feel the same way inside. Looks like we both think the same thing. I like you!" she blurted out.

He laughed on the other end, "I like you too."

They officially became boyfriend and girlfriend. They still communicated through letters and QQ. He even quit smoking for her, just because she once said she didn't like smokers. Though they lived far apart, they knew each other's circle of friends well through the Internet.

Her friends noticed the sweetness in her stories about him. However, some of his friends started saying that their relationship wasn’t real, that they wouldn't truly be together. He remained confident that it was real! They indulged in the sweetness of their love...

When high school ended and college life began, perhaps because he was older, he understood more. In high school, he met more people who all knew about their relationship. Some agreed they should continue, while others thought it was unrealistic and advised him to let go before it got worse. Over time, his tolerance for these opinions decreased. Sometimes, he himself wondered if they really shouldn't be together, that it was a waste of time. But he didn't know how to bring it up, how to say it without hurting her deeply. Yet, every time he received her letter, he would get excited. Every day, he thought about these things, torn between the fact that it was unrealistic and how much he truly liked and loved her. If it turned out to be unrealistic, what would he do? What would she do?

With too much thinking, conflicts arose between them. They began small quarrels, though they always made up eventually. These conflicts reinforced his belief that their relationship was unrealistic. During another argument, he brought it up, saying it was unrealistic and impossible to become real, that he didn't want to waste time on something fictional. She was shocked—it was exactly as her friends had predicted, as she had feared. Her hand holding the phone grew weak, slowly lowering down. The phone emitted lonely beeps... "Why does he hang up so fast, doesn't he want to leave?" she thought.

The arrival of high school life accompanied her only with the sorrow of a breakup, yet she didn't want to give up. She wondered why it had come to this; he wasn't like this before. Daily pursuits and efforts finally revealed their true thoughts and stances. Inside, he was in pain. When he said those words, he regretted it so much that he quickly hung up, not wanting her to hear him cry. But he didn't know that because of those words, she cried all day.

Their reconciliation seemed precious to both. However, like every couple, they faced the issues of breaking up and making up. From their sophomore year in junior high until his sophomore year in university and her freshman year, they broke up and reconciled countless times. Yet, they always ended up fine, laughing and joking after each breakup, though their relationship changed to that of ordinary friends.

In his first year of university, he applied to the university in her city, and they finally met face-to-face. Both were excited but restrained due to many reasons before meeting.

She, in her senior year of high school, often came out to play with him on weekends, walking around the streets happily every day. In her heart, this was love, though she didn't realize it was already deeper than mere affection. He didn't know how to tell her it was love. He understood her—she was stubborn and once she decided something, right or wrong, she would stick to it. So he didn't want to force her, enjoying the current tenderness was enough. Even if they were just ordinary friends...

Neither seemed to adapt well to this face-to-face meeting but being just ordinary friends. Neither knew how to express it, nor did they dare to.

On a weekend, he rushed to her school gate to pick her up. It was dismissal time, and the school gate was crowded. When she finally emerged from the crowd, seeing him waiting there for her made her heart warm. To be more honest, her heart was already bursting with joy. Of course, this was her inner feeling. As they walked together, encountering her good friends, everyone smiled knowingly—they understood, they were a perfect match. Meeting younger students, they innocently asked, "Sister, is this your boyfriend? You two look so suitable together, envy and jealousy everywhere!" Then they laughed and walked away, leaving her red-faced and him standing beside her. Perhaps at that moment, they were thinking the same thing. Who knows?

Because of the innocent questions from the younger students, they found an opportunity to reconcile. This time, it seemed they both understood each other's thoughts, though she was still somewhat naive. Maybe because she was spoiled at home, sometimes her thoughts were overly simple and innocent, but he seemed to like this about her. Whenever she asked some childish questions, he would laugh and call her cute. At those moments, she would furrow her brow, wondering where she was mature and why he called her cute, and would keep pestering him about it. She seemed really adorable!

Back in junior high, he had promised her a Doraemon toy for her birthday, but due to lack of time, he never sent it, and she never received it. In his heart, she was like his Doraemon—so lively and lovable.

Perhaps because of her stubbornness and lack of understanding whether it was love or just affection, she often did things that drove him crazy. Though he could tolerate, boys still cared about their dignity, sometimes feeling he shouldn't endure such things. Thus, in the second semester of her senior year, they broke up again.

After the college entrance exam, it was time for her to fill out her application forms. She didn't know which school to choose, where to go. She sought his advice, and he laughed, telling her to apply to the school where he was, close to her home. Thinking about it, and having her preferred major, she happily decided. But a few days before filling out the form, due to some conflict outside, she angrily decided to go far away so her parents couldn't control her anymore. Thus began her journey to Wuhan University.

Upon arriving in Wuhan, she regretted her decision. She shouldn't have acted impulsively, running so far away for trivial matters. There, no one was familiar, nothing was familiar, and she lacked everything she wanted. So, she could only confide in him, only share her true feelings with him. He would comfort her tenderly, fearing she was still immature, fearing she lacked something. Every day, he reminded her to eat, sleep, wear more clothes, reduce clothing... When she was sick, he worried so much. She even told him that if 2012 was really the end of the world, she would marry him! Between them existed emotions invisible to others, emotions she herself didn't fully understand.

For unknown reasons, he suddenly told her on QQ that they should stop contacting, that he didn't want to continue, finding it painful, worrying daily if she was okay, if she was bullied, among many other concerns. Most importantly, he didn't want her to remain unaware of their love.

The next day, he disappeared from QQ, leaving no trace. She cried all night.

During the time after he left, she made many mistakes, not knowing how to take care of herself, often falling ill, often feeling sad, but no one knew, no one listened to her slowly recount her story. Only she, in the quiet of the night, remembering the past, felt she had been happy.

One day, she caught a serious cold. Suddenly, she remembered the old days, when she was in high school and fell ill, he would run from his school to bring her medicine and care for her... But things weren't like that anymore. People say illness makes one prone to nostalgia. In her dreams, she saw him coming to Wuhan to see her, forcing her to take medicine and injections, crying in her dream, tears streaming down her cheeks, waking up laughing. Upon waking, she realized it was just a dream, but at that moment, she wished it were true. When she went to the hospital, the doctor said she had a fever and throat inflammation, giving her many precautions, but she heard none of it. All she could think about was that in the past, it was always him by her side when she went to the doctor. After leaving the hospital, the boy accompanying her repeated the doctor's instructions, but she just replied indifferently, "Oh," and walked away. If only he were there...

Even though things had changed, she still wanted to contact him, wanted to know what he was doing, if he ate, if he smoked again... But she didn't have the courage to ask others for his number. She was afraid he wouldn't respond, that he would think she contacted him out of boredom. All this was because she understood now! Yes, she understood now that all the past was because of love, not just affection! She wanted to tell him, she understood now, she really understood everything now, and she wouldn't make those rookie mistakes again. When she finally gathered the courage to ask others for his number, she was thrilled but mostly worried, worried he wouldn't acknowledge her, worried he had changed. After re-adding him, she initiated a conversation, but the reply was not from him. Her heart trembled—was it his new girlfriend? Another reply came—"He's gaming, I'm his roommate." Her anxiety subsided, but he ignored her all night. Just before sleeping, she received a text from him, "Give me a reason to contact again." She didn't know how to reply but quickly responded. Throughout the night, they exchanged texts, but from his messages, she sensed he had changed, becoming unlike his former self, becoming distant, unenthusiastic, becoming... unfamiliar. Because he said, "It was you who left, now don't bother me, my smoking is my own business." "Huh, he says it was me who left, now he doesn't want me to bother him!" Thinking about it, she was furious but powerless since it was her reaching out to him. But... maybe she was angry and confused, thoughts rushing out. Still, she rationally thought and replied, "It was you who chased me away, now I want to interfere, even if you call me nosy, I won't let you smoke, you yourself promised not to smoke anymore, you can't go back on your word!" But he didn't reply.

The next day on QQ, she initiated a conversation again, but he didn't reply, no matter how many times she tried, he kept ignoring her until he logged off. She suddenly felt ridiculous, feeling her face had thickened. Why was she bothering someone who didn't want to talk to her? How absurd! Looking at his darkened avatar, she bitterly smiled at the computer screen. Wanting to let it go, but thinking about it, wasn't she trying to reconnect to share her true feelings? Wasn't it to tell him what she hadn't understood before? But he ignored her, what to do? Leave a message? Yes, leave a message! On QQ, she wrote about what she hadn't understood before but now did, and everything she wanted to say. Others said she was delirious, saying and doing whatever without caring about the outcome. But she felt, regardless of what happened later, at least she understood now and expressed her thoughts. Even if they couldn't reconcile, at least in the future, she wouldn't regret anything.

That night, unable to sleep due to her cold, she hesitated whether to call him, asking why. After much deliberation, she picked up her phone and dialed. A few rings later, he answered with a very indifferent tone, leaving her unsure how to start. He repeatedly asked why she wasn't speaking, and after half a minute of hesitation, she finally spoke, though still unsure what to say. He asked, "What did you say yesterday, what was that phrase you once told me, I forgot, remind me now." As she hesitated whether to say it, she heard laughter and voices from his end: "Ha, calling now for what? Say it, why aren't you saying it anymore, isn't that what you wanted to say, ha!" Hearing these, she was extremely upset. She knew those people were talking about her, mocking her. Suddenly, she felt everything was meaningless. She had mustered so much courage, only to be mocked by them, and he probably thought the same. Huh, she didn't want to say anything anymore. All she wanted was to sleep and never wake up. After a long silence, he spoke again, "Do you have nothing else to say to me?" "Um..." "Nothing else?" She thought for a moment, "Um!" "Okay, sleep then, bye!" And he hung up. "Huh, he used to never hang up first, always letting me do it first because he said the sound of hanging up was too empty, he didn't want me to hear that lonely sound. She chuckled in her heart."

Maybe we all think this is how it should end, as neither wants either to be hurt further. At least she expressed what she didn't understand before, now everyone understands, and looking back, it might be a good memory. At least they have loved!
