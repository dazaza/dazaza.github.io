2. Deep Cleansing

Exfoliation can be divided into three types: natural exfoliation caused by metabolism, chemical exfoliation through certain chemicals softening the skin surface making it easier to remove. Physical exfoliation does not use any chemicals but only physical methods to make the keratin float on the epidermis, making it easier to fall off, such as steaming or massaging.

Dead skin cream, gel, and scrub are chemical exfoliants.

Skin that cannot be exfoliated:

① Allergic skin and skin around the eyes

② Acne-prone skin during inflammation

③ Severe redness, thin skin surface [Jiangsu Beauty and Hairdressing School http://www.jsainisi.org]

④ Exfoliation according to skin needs generally once every 1~3 months, each time lasting 3~5 minutes.

3. Massage

Specific understanding of the following points:

① Purpose and effectiveness of massage

A. Promotes blood circulation, providing nutrition to tissues

B. Increases oxygen delivery, promoting cell metabolism

C. Helps skin excrete waste and CO2, reducing oil accumulation

D. Makes skin tissue firm and elastic

E. Drains excess subcutaneous water, eliminating swelling and sagging, delaying aging

F. Relaxes nerves, fully rests, eliminates fatigue, reduces muscle pain, invigorates the spirit.

② Preparations before massage

A. Cleanses the face, beautician cleans hands, maintains hand warmth contact

B. Selects targeted massage cream, body massage should not be done within 30 minutes after meals or when hungry

C. The person being massaged closes their eyes, avoids talking, enters a semi-sleep state is best.

D. Can open the mist appropriately based on skin type for 3~10 minutes, increasing the lubrication of the massage, promoting nutrient absorption, massage duration is 15~20 minutes, too long or too short will not achieve the purpose.

③ Basic principles of massage

A. Consistent with the direction of muscle texture

B. Direction is from bottom to top

C. From inside to outside, from center to sides

D. Minimize pulling of the skin

④ Basic requirements for massage

A. Skillful actions, adjusts hand shape, fingers, and wrists based on different states of facial muscles, maintaining flexibility and coordination of both hands

B. Steady, accurate acupoint pressing

C. Frequency starts slow then fast, light then heavy, with pressure penetration

D. Adjusts strength based on location

Note: Serious sensitivity, acne, trauma inflammation areas are prohibited from massage.

Severely red-faced skin or infectious diseases prohibit massage.

Serious asthma patients, swollen joints, swollen glands prohibit massage.

4. If using instruments for skincare, choose instrument projects based on need, such as blackhead extraction, ultrasound, etc.

Inevitably you must see the classification chart of face shapes.

Many girls may not be very familiar with the classification of face shapes. So what are the classifications of face shapes? Face shapes can basically be classified into five types: egg-shaped face, long face, square face, heart-shaped face, round face.

Face Shape Classification Chart [http://www.shoulian100.com/] —— Egg-shaped Face

An egg-shaped face is an ideal face shape, also known as a melon seed face. Its characteristic is that the forehead and cheekbones are almost the same width, while slightly wider than the jawline. The face width is approximately two-thirds of the face length.

Face Shape Classification Chart —— Long Face
