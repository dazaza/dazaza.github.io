by leekic on 2009-08-11 19:37:08

The foreign website winaddons has selected 300 top free software programs (TOP 300 Freeware), which has been widely reprinted domestically in a short time. However, I believe in thinking twice before acting, so before reprinting on my own platform (Shan Yong Jia Ruan), I examined it and found three questions:

① Are there really 300 software programs? This question isn't very significant, but since we have convenient tools, not counting would be wasteful. After calculation, there are actually 296. (How to calculate: those who have used VIM 7.0 will understand immediately: %s/-//gn. If anyone has a better method, feel free to share.) Of course, adding a few more wouldn't be difficult, but it's unnecessary.

② Some software is, due to the times (such as offline browsers), in my opinion, of little significance to include; some software seems less well-known (although I've used limited amounts, I often browse foreign shareware download sites) or of average quality, or not necessarily suitable for Chinese users, and should be noted to distinguish them from truly top-tier software.

③ Some domestic software has not been included because foreigners are unfamiliar with it. It's worth making a note or doing a simple comparison with similar software.

In summary, providing reviews helps ordinary users understand and use these software programs; it also promotes free software more effectively and reduces piracy. The principle of reviewing: practicality, responsibility, and brevity. For some software, I've used them for years and have many insights; for others, though I haven't used them regularly, I've followed them for a long time. In both cases, I'll provide more reviews. For those I'm unfamiliar with, there's no need to google (readers can do that themselves). "Knowing is knowing, not knowing is not knowing," this is the way to handle things. If I can reduce piracy behavior and habits even slightly, I would feel deeply gratified.
