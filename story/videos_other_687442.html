Principle of electronic dogs

Industry Technician Exposes the "Principle of Electronic Dogs" Inside Story!

Electronic Dogs: There are two types of electronic dogs - broad-sense and narrow-sense.

1) Narrow-sense electronic dog refers to early anti-speed detection products. Although there are still a certain amount of these on the market, they can only play a limited role in some areas. An electronic dog is essentially just a wireless signal receiver; its principle and cost are even simpler than a radio. Electronic dog navigation devices sell for around 200 RMB in the market, but they cannot provide warnings for mobile photography at all. In fact, the S-line coils laid on the ground, which are widely used in various countries, do not produce signals. Usually, manufacturers will pre-bury transmitters 300 to 1000 meters before fixed photography locations. As long as the vehicle passing through the section has a voice-type warning device installed, it can receive warning signals. Therefore, some merchants must specify where their electronic dog products are applicable when selling them. The disadvantages of this product are quite obvious. First, regardless of whether the camera is actually working, the signal transmitter will emit a signal, which is a false alarm for the driver. Second, due to changes in roads and fierce commercial competition, manufacturers often sabotage each other's transmitters or the transmitter batteries run out, causing the warning device to fail completely, resulting in significant losses for consumers. Therefore, one should be particularly cautious about such products. Additionally, the use of radio frequencies in our country also requires approval from the Radio Committee. However, P-band has not been approved, so it can be detected by the Radio Committee's detection vehicles and may be shut down or interfered with at any time.

2) Broad-sense electronic dog refers to many people's habit of calling all anti-speed detection products "electronic dogs." Such products are now generally called anti-speed radars, with product packaging labeled as safe driving instruments or radar warning devices. After studying the current anti-speed radars, Zhongguanbao categorizes anti-speed radars into the following four types based on their functions:

(1) Full-frequency anti-speed radar

Full-frequency anti-speed radar is a type of car equipment that emerged with the widespread use of radar speed detectors. Radar speed detectors calculate the movement speed of objects based on the frequency shift of received reflected waves. Currently, radar speed detectors are widely used in urban traffic, mainly to measure whether cars are speeding. They come in fixed and mobile versions. Fixed ones are installed on bridges or intersections, while mobile ones are usually installed in patrol cars.

Full-frequency anti-speed radar is a device that detects radar speed detectors, installed inside cars. It can detect radar speed detectors within a certain distance during driving. When a car approaches a radar speed detector, the anti-speed radar will issue a warning, giving the driver 2-3 seconds to slow down to normal speed within a range of 100-400 meters. Full-frequency models can receive all radar signal bands (earlier products no longer need classification, and manufacturers have stopped producing them). Some better products include Conqueror 206, Antarctic Star 5500, Antarctic Star X3 (6200), etc. These products are effective for all radars, responding sensitively on highways, usually forecasting 500 meters to 2 kilometers ahead. This product cannot determine the car's speed, so it cannot remain silent when not speeding, and it cannot distinguish directions, sounding alarms wherever there are radars, thus potentially disturbing residents excessively. Moreover, most cities now use speed-sensitive coil loops buried under the road to detect speeding, rendering radar detectors completely ineffective (only relying on manufacturers installing radar transmitters near speed-check points to give warnings, but this practice is illegal and difficult to maintain). Hence, the second and third types of products we will discuss emerge.

(2) GPS anti-speed radar

This type of anti-speed radar does not rely on receiving radar waves to warn of speeding. The GPS function works like this: it pre-stores latitude and longitude data of fixed speed-check points or red-light cameras (which can be upgraded and updated, or users can create their own coordinates). Through GPS positioning, it triggers an alarm when approaching a target point at a predetermined distance. Since it includes latitude and longitude data of speed-sensitive coil loops buried under the road, it provides more reliable warnings for fixed speed checks. Combined with an electronic compass, it calculates and displays the vehicle's speed and direction, eliminating the trouble of being unable to determine the direction. It automatically remains silent when not speeding. This type of product also contains a large amount of traffic information and life information, with voice prompt functions, making it a good assistant for driving. Some well-known brands include Conqueror 101, Prophet X-611, Pathfinder 303, and Antarctic Star MINI. However, this type of product is ineffective against mobile speed checks.

(3) GPS full-frequency anti-speed radar

GPS full-frequency anti-speed radar combines the functions of the first two types of anti-speed radars. It can accurately forecast fixed radar speed detectors and also predict mobile speed checks, comprehensively solving the shortcomings of the first two types of products. This type of anti-speed radar can be divided into two kinds: one is integrated, and there are relatively fewer of these products now. Representative examples include Prophet X-605, Conqueror 968, and Antarctic Star M3. The other more common kind is separate. Based on the mentality of discovering earlier and preparing more, it enhances the radar detection external machine (for example, the enhanced external machine of Antarctic Star improves measurement distance by over 600% compared to traditional radars). The advantage of this type of product is increased warning distance, preventing those who cannot operate anti-speed radars well from braking too abruptly and causing rear-end collisions. However, installation is slightly more complicated, but it's not a big problem. Reading the manual should make things clear. If you don't want to do it yourself, finding a mechanic won't cost much money. Representative products include Conqueror 210, Antarctic Star 388, Conqueror 528/588, and the Antarctic Star 9 series.

(4) Bluetooth GPS full-frequency anti-speed radar

These products mainly appear in the Conqueror and Antarctic Star series and can be upgraded to satellite navigation systems: connecting to Bluetooth-enabled phones, PDAs, laptops, and navigation electronic maps can upgrade them to GPS satellite navigation systems. Zhongguanbao believes that this product can only be considered a direction for development, and it is not yet a mature product because this GPS navigation connection is relatively professional. Of course, some friends who already own Bluetooth phones, PDAs, or laptops can find free navigation software to use, which should still demonstrate the value of such products.
