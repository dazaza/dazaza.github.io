The funeral with the most attendees in the world

by cqflw on 2009-07-08 22:22:56

The funeral of Michael Jackson, who died recently, was the most attended in the world, even surpassing the scale of US President Obama's inauguration ceremony.

Facebook and MySpace stated that their website members could watch Michael Jackson's funeral online. Through a collaboration with CNN, Facebook users were able to watch the live stream of Jackson's funeral on the day. MySpace partnered with entertainment giant AEG.

Foreign media estimated that at least 160 countries around the world broadcast the memorial service, with up to three billion television viewers tuning in, more than for Princess Diana of Britain's funeral and Obama's inauguration ceremony, making it the largest funeral in the world.

Interestingly, however, officials in Los Angeles complained bitterly about the funeral. Since the California government, to which Los Angeles belongs, is nearing bankruptcy with cash running out by July 24th.

To maintain order during the funeral, nearly 2,000 police officers and government personnel were deployed. Dennis Zine, a Los Angeles City Councilman, told the media that commemorating Jackson would cost the government $2.5 million, which is adding insult to injury for the increasingly strained municipal funds. There’s no need for Los Angeles taxpayers to spend money on security for a private event. Interim Mayor of Los Angeles, Jan Perry, informed the media that part of the $2.5 million expenditure would come from the government's emergency budget, while the rest would hopefully be covered by Jackson's family.
