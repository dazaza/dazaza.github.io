Urgency of Multiple Financial Report Disclosures Prompts Changes in Institutional Portfolio Adjustment Strategies

by ww0910844 on 2010-04-18 14:04:01

Editor's note: The market is currently in a period of intensive earnings announcements. As the disclosure of 2009 annual reports draws to a close, the disclosure work for the first quarter of 2010 reports is entering its climax, and at the same time, some companies are starting to disclose their mid-year performance forecasts for 2010.

In such a mixed period, two-way long and short positions have gradually become the norm. Investors should calm down and carefully examine the listed company's financial statements, look at how institutional investors invest, and see what new insights they can bring you.

New opportunities emerge during the intensive earnings disclosure period. As of April 16, a total of 1,283 A-share companies in the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock markets have disclosed their 2009 annual reports, 66 companies have disclosed their first-quarter 2010 reports, and 30 companies have forecasted their mid-year 2010 earnings. From the current statistical data of these companies' financial reports, the profits of the vast majority of listed companies are increasing both year-on-year and quarter-on-quarter. With the gradual cooling down of theme stocks, fundamentals may become the most reliable selection angle for investors at present.

Post "2009 Annual Report Period": Stocks bought by institutional seats for the first time have greater opportunities. Currently, the number of A-share companies that have announced their 2009 annual performance accounts for 73.27% of all A-share companies, and less than 30% of companies have not yet disclosed their annual reports. With the hot release of the first quarter of 2010 reports, the market is entering the post "2009 Annual Report Period."

In the previous hype around the annual report, "high dividend and bonus shares" was a major hotspot. Although everyone understands that "high dividend and bonus shares" is just a numerical game played by listed companies, people are still enthusiastic about the expectation of filling rights. From the market performance, before the annual report is released, people speculate on the expectation of dividends and bonus shares, such as the skyrocketing of individual stocks like Hechang New Materials and Donghu High-tech; after the formal distribution plan is implemented, investors then turn to speculating on the fill-right trend, such as the speculation on Oriental Landscape, Sanan Optoelectronics, DNH Chemical Technology, and Goer Acoustic. However, with the official release of the first quarter of 2010 reports, the star effect of speculating on the "high dividend and bonus shares" of the 2009 annual report is gradually losing its luster. Data shows that among the 19 companies that launched high dividend and bonus shares since April 10, only Dasha Co., Ltd. achieved a daily limit-up, while Mingliu Real Estate and Lepu Medical experienced significant declines.

At this point in time, from the perspective of annual report investment, investors can pay attention to those annual reports showing concentrated chips and recent first-time institutional seat purchases, which present certain opportunities. Latest cases show that once some annual reports showing concentrated chips appear on the trading seat dragon tiger list, there often follows a noticeable upward trend. For example, institutional dedicated seats bought Chuangyuan Technology for 123.42 million yuan on April 8 and again for 187.84 million yuan on April 12. By April 15, the stock price had risen by 34.09% over six trading days. Similarly, after the annual report data of Chongqing Beer was released, institutional dedicated seats net bought 117.37 million yuan worth of shares on April 14, resulting in two consecutive daily limit-ups. Other companies like Tielong Logistics and Huadong Technology also saw significant stock price increases after being significantly purchased by institutions for the first time recently.

First Quarter of 2010 Reports: Enter industries with high prosperity levels and unexpected performance. On April 8, Juren Shares unveiled the curtain of the first quarter of 2010 reports, thus officially replacing the speculative trends around the 2009 annual report.

As of April 16, the first quarter 2010 financial reports of 66 companies have been published. Excluding *ST Hua Yuan without net profit in the first quarter of 2009 and Shenjian Stock and Danfu Stock without net profit in the fourth quarter of 2009, the remaining 63 listed companies collectively realized a net profit of 3.011 billion yuan in the first quarter, representing a year-on-year increase of 103.34% compared to the first quarter of 2009 and a quarter-on-quarter increase of 17.09% compared to the fourth quarter of 2009. In addition, the widespread pre-announcements of positive first quarter results indicate that pre-announcements will be the mainstream of this year's first quarter performance forecasts. Excluding companies that have already published their first quarter reports, among the 242 companies that have pre-announced their first quarter results, 197 expect positive results, accounting for 81.4%. Stocks like Zhangzi Island, Ningbo Huaxiang, Fara Electronics, and Jinzhong Seed Wine have seen sharp rises after announcing substantial pre-announcements of their expected performance.

While investors focus on companies with significantly increased first-quarter earnings, they should also pay attention to the overall prosperity level of the industry in which the company operates. Only by fully understanding the development status of the industry in the first quarter of this year can one buy stocks with good industry prospects and first-quarter earnings exceeding expectations. According to the latest research report from CITIC Securities, it is believed that sectors with first-quarter earnings exceeding expectations mainly include aviation, electronics, machinery, agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry, fishing, pharmaceuticals, and automobiles. The latest macroeconomic data also shows that heavy industry grew by 22.1% in the first quarter of this year, light industry grew by 14.1%, automobile sales grew by 39.8%, furniture sales grew by 37.6%, and household appliances and audio-visual equipment sales grew by 29.6%. These data confirm the possibility of exceeding expectations in the first quarter performance of the sectors identified by CITIC Securities.

Mid-Year Earnings for 2010: Emerging Opportunities

With the sequential disclosure of first-quarter performance, the mid-year earnings forecasts for 2010 are also beginning to take shape. Currently, a total of 30 A-share companies have taken the lead in issuing mid-year earnings forecasts, with 27 companies reporting positive forecasts including profit growth, slight growth, continued profitability, and turning losses into gains. Among them, 25 companies have growth rates exceeding 30%, fully demonstrating the ongoing "spring on branches" trend.

From the statistics of the announced growth rates, among the 27 companies with positive mid-year earnings forecasts, five are expected to achieve profit growth in the second quarter similar to the first quarter, with growth rates exceeding 100%. For instance, Welltech achieved a net profit of 1.4203 million yuan in the first half of 2009. If the forecasted 50% growth is realized, the mid-year earnings for 2010 could reach 2.1304 million yuan, successfully reversing the loss of 0.2365 million yuan in the first quarter of 2010. Goer Acoustic expects a net profit growth of 280%-310% in the first half of 2010. Based on a net profit of 2.06004 million yuan in the first half of 2009, if the growth reaches 310%, the mid-year net profit would be approximately 84.46 million yuan. Deducting the 27.0404 million yuan achieved in the first quarter, the single-second-quarter profit could reach 57.42 million yuan, with a quarter-on-quarter increase of up to 112.35%. The main reason for the improved performance is the improvement in the company's own operating conditions rather than non-core investment returns such as financial or real estate investments.

Fund Investment Cautiousness: Growth Stocks Gain Favor

Facing the complex market environment in the first quarter and the uncertainty of policies in the second quarter, how will institutional holdings change? Although the quarterly reports of funds and other institutions that clearly reveal fund holding directions have not yet been released, the latest disclosed partial first-quarter reports of listed companies give us an initial glimpse.

Institutions Overall Outperform the Market

As of April 16, a total of 66 companies have disclosed their first-quarter 2010 reports. From the changes in their top ten floating shareholders, 55 stocks have institutional (including funds, QFII, insurance, social security, etc.) presence in their top ten floating shareholders, among which Ge Er Acoustic, Wuhan Fanggu, and Borei Media have all institutional top ten floating shareholders.

From the currently disclosed first-quarter reports, stocks newly purchased by institutions include Wuhan Plastic, Jinggu Forestry, Dase Nan, and Three Gorges Water Conservancy, etc. Additionally, newly listed stocks held by institutions include Beijing Kerui, Danfu Stock, and Shenjian Stock, etc. Among the 25 stocks where institutions increased their holdings in the first quarter, seven stocks were increased by more than 10 million shares, including Huafang Textile and Danhua Science & Technology, whose stock prices rose by more than 20% in April. Compared with the 2009 annual report, institutions have continuously increased their holdings in Guomai Technology, Kang Enbei, Rizhao Port, and Supersonic Electronics, leading to stocks like Guomai Technology rising against the trend by 31.32% in the first quarter despite the index falling by 5.13%. Although the stock fell by 3.14% in April, the Shanghai Composite Index only rose by 0.68% during the same period, proving the strength and resilience of continuously increased institutional holdings.

Overall Market Value Changes in Institutional Holdings in April

As of April 16, the overall market value of 55 stocks in April increased by 3.3%, while the Shanghai Composite Index only rose by 1.80% during the same period. Of the 55 stocks, 28 outperformed the Shanghai Composite Index. Therefore, from the current situation, the first-quarter reports show that institutional holdings perform better than the market average in comprehensive capabilities.

Increased Holdings Tend Towards Defensive Weak-Cycle Stocks

Due to the overall adjustment state of the market in the first quarter, large-cap blue-chip stocks performed poorly, while small-cap stocks were active. From the current stocks increased by institutions, growth potential is the main factor considered by institutional investors when selecting stocks. Among the 21 stocks rated by institutions, except for 6 with neutral ratings, the remaining 15 are recommended for increase or purchase.

The valuation levels of these 21 stocks are relatively high, but short-term overvaluation does not hinder institutional holding intentions. Institutions value the growth potential of these stocks. According to institutional prediction data, although 16 of these 21 stocks had valuation levels exceeding 50 times at the end of the first quarter of 2010, from the institutional predictions for 2011 valuations, only three stocks—Wuhan Plastic, Zhongtong Bus, and Yimin Commerce—exceed 50 times. Precisely predicting the super-expectation performance of individual first-quarter reports has become an important reason for some institutions to increase their holdings in these stocks, such as Yi Min Commerce, Yiatong, and Da Yang Motor, which saw significant first-quarter earnings growth, with single-quarter earnings per share increasing by 170%. Similar examples include Da Southeast, *ST Hua Yuan, and Shunfa Hengye, whose single-quarter earnings per share growth exceeded 60%.

From the current industry attributes of institutionally increased holdings, the stocks increased are not the cyclical industry stocks imagined by many, but are concentrated in non-cyclical industries or technology stocks, represented by electronic information stocks like Supersonic Electronics, Ge Er Acoustic, Guomai Technology, etc., and pharmaceutical stocks like Kang Enbei, *ST Tianfang, Zixin Pharmaceutical, etc. Looking back at the first quarter of 2009, among comparable stocks, institutional increased holdings mainly leaned towards chemical and real estate industry stocks.

Social Security, QFII Increase Holdings While Funds Become Cautious

From the current data, the largest institutional investor, funds, have begun to shift towards caution. According to existing first-quarter reports, although 114 funds have intervened in 43 stocks, they have reduced holdings in 21 stocks to a certain extent, especially firmly reducing holdings in stocks with larger prior increases, such as Fujian Glass reduced by 26.81 million shares by Shangtou Morgan Inner Need Power and 15.26 million shares by Hua An Small Cap Growth, Guangfa Poly Abundant reduced 18.59 million shares in Tebian Electric, and Southern Component Selection reduced 17.13 million shares in Changhang Oil Transportation.

In fact, the cautious sentiment of funds has also appeared in the process of new fund establishment. Taking the newly listed Changxin CSI Central Enterprises 100 this week as an example, according to the fund announcement, as of April 9, the fund's stock investment amount was only 249,600 yuan, accounting for only 0.03% of the total fund assets. Since its establishment half a month ago, the fund has invested only more than 200,000 yuan in stock configuration, indicating its cautious degree of establishment.

In addition, from the quarterly fund investment review published by China AMC, we can also see the cautious attitude of China AMC. It has generally enhanced its defensiveness, made structural adjustments, started avoiding cyclical industries, and increased holdings in growth industries. Its flagship fund, China AMC Large Cap Select, also showed good defensiveness in its first-quarter investment portfolio, flexibly grasping structural investment opportunities.

Compared to the caution of funds, social security and QFII have carried out certain increases. Among the eight stocks where social security holdings appear, except for only reducing holdings in Heimao Stock, holdings in the remaining seven have been increased. Notably, Fujian Glass and Tebian Electric, which were significantly reduced by funds, became stocks significantly increased by the Social Security Fund. Similarly, among the 11 stocks held by QFII funds, QFII generally holds the main position, with only minor increases in a few individual stocks. The largest buying move was Columbia University's new purchase of 13 million shares in Rizhao Port through QFII.

Stocks Held by Fund Companies Are Worth Paying Attention To

Although only 66 listed companies have disclosed their first-quarter reports so far, there are 11 stocks where multiple funds under the same fund company hold them collectively, with the feature most prominently displayed by China AMC. Four funds under China AMC heavily hold Wuhan Plastic and Danhua Technology, three funds heavily hold Guomai Technology and Beijing Kerui, and four funds under Yinhua Fund heavily hold Nanhua A. All three funds under China Post Fund heavily hold Yi Min Commerce, showcasing the institutional confidence in the stock through collective holding. Judging from the recent market performance of these stocks, the funds seem to value their future market performance.

Additionally, among the top ten floating shareholders of Shenjian Stock, there are only two funds under BOYING Asset Management, and among the top ten floating shareholders of Huafang Textile, there are only two funds under Morgan Stanley Huaxin Asset Management. Stocks dominated by a single fund management company are also worthy of investors' close attention.

Consumption Industry Becomes the "Favorite" in a Volatile Market

Since the beginning of the year, amidst the widespread decline of "strong cycle" industries, the defensive characteristics of the consumption industry with clear earnings growth and favorable expectations have become prominent. In the first quarter, the CSI 300 fell by 9.69%, while the medical and catering tourism sectors rose by 4.3% and 9.9%, respectively, demonstrating certain resilience.

Medicine: "Export Recovery + Domestic Demand Pull" Prospects Look Promising

With the increase in national investment and the promotion of the basic drug system policy this year, the revenue and profit of the pharmaceutical industry have resumed high-speed growth. In January and February, the pharmaceutical manufacturing industry realized cumulative revenue of 143 billion yuan, growing by 28%; achieving a profit total of 14.75 billion yuan, growing by 45.7%. As of last Friday, a total of 11 pharmaceutical companies have published their first-quarter reports or performance forecasts, among which Zhejiang Medicine, Xinhecheng, and others have net profit growth exceeding 100%.

CICC believes that the positive impact of the expansion of the medical insurance catalog and the basic drug system will continue to be reflected in 2009-2010, with industry sales revenue expected to grow by 25%-30%. "Export recovery + domestic demand pull" is the investment theme of the industry, with chemical raw material pharmaceuticals affected by export recovery and traditional Chinese medicine companies entering the medical insurance catalog with exclusive varieties due to domestic demand pull, deserving investors' attention.

The basic drug catalog was introduced in August 2009, and provinces began bidding for basic drugs at the beginning of 2010. Over 100 traditional Chinese medicine varieties were selected into the catalog, with exclusive varieties accounting for more than 20%. Basic drugs listed in the catalog will be mandatorily used, beneficial for the increase in the share of traditional Chinese medicine. Due to the lack of competition from identical varieties, the potential price reduction threat for exclusive products is much smaller than for other varieties, and some products might even see price increases. Therefore, traditional Chinese medicine companies with exclusive products benefit more significantly. For example, Yunnan Baiyao's varieties entering the catalog account for 53.8% of sales revenue. In addition, CR Snowland, Ma Yinglong, Jinling Pharmaceuticals, and Qianjin Pharmaceuticals will also benefit.

On the other hand, with the recovery of the macroeconomy and the gradual recovery of exports, the worst times for the chemical raw material pharmaceutical industry have passed, and raw material drug prices are gradually rising. The sub-industry's performance growth rate turned positive at the end of last year, and in the first two months of this year, it achieved revenue growth of 31.69% and profit growth of 67.32%, becoming the biggest highlight of the pharmaceutical industry. Vitamin A, vitamin E, and vitamin B5 are generally used as additives in livestock feed to supplement nutrients needed by animals. Analyst Wu Yang of Bohai Securities believes that "with the arrival of the traditional export demand peak season in May-June, export volumes will see significant growth, providing short-term investment opportunities for relevant raw material pharmaceutical companies."

Analyst Luo Ruoyang of Shenwan Hongyuan predicts in an interview with Red Weekly that the prices of vitamin A and vitamin E will moderately rise in 2010, with Zhejiang Medicine and Xinhecheng having 30%-40% growth space in performance, and their current valuations are low, making them suitable for value investors to buy on dips. Xinfu Pharmaceutical has absolute pricing power in the vitamin B5 industry and raised prices by 10% after the Spring Festival. Wu Yang stated that "this price increase is just the beginning, and vitamin B5 prices are expected to continue to rise, contributing approximately 20% to its performance this year."

Tourism: Regional Concepts and Event Factors Interact

In 2009, affected by the financial crisis and swine flu, the global tourism market generally declined, but domestic tourists in China still increased by 11%, reaching 1.9 billion visits, making it the brightest spot in international tourism. During the Spring Festival Golden Week in 2010, the country received 125 million visitors, generating tourism revenue of 64.62 billion yuan, increasing by 14.8% and 26.9%, respectively, indicating that the tourism industry in China is improving. Moreover, from April to October each year is the tourism peak season for most areas in China, plus the upcoming "May Day" Golden Week, securities industry analysts generally expect the tourism sector to improve in the second quarter.

Su Ping, an analyst at China Merchants Securities, revealed to our magazine that policy-wise, the National Tourism Work Conference is expected to be held within the second quarter, possibly bringing further industry-friendly policies, and other event factors may also boost the tourism sector.

"There are rumors that the 'Yunnan Construction Plan as the Bridgehead Facing Southwest Asia' is about to be approved. It is expected that more regional development plans will be released in the second quarter, with the possibility of Yunnan and Guangxi provinces releasing regional tourism incentive policies being relatively high. I am optimistic about Lijiang Tourism, Expo Shares, and Guilin Tourism located in Yunnan and Guangxi," he said.

Additionally, the Shanghai World Expo will open on May 1st, expecting about 30% of the 70 million visitors to continue touring in the East China region. Based on the latest market expectations, supported by a huge domestic tourism market, the 70 million visitor projection for the Shanghai World Expo is conservative, estimating that the Shanghai World Expo will receive between 80 and 100 million visitors. The 16th Asian Games will be held in Guangzhou, China, from November 12 to 27, 2010. Su Ping believes that Huangshan Tourism, Jinjiang Shares, and Dongfang Hotel benefiting from the World Expo and Asian Games can be tracked. ■
