What should patients with ovarian cysts eat? http://www.fuukee.com/thread-40-1-1.html Cervical erosion is a manifestation of chronic cervicitis, mainly referring to the complete stripping of the mucosa of the cervix within the vagina, exposing the underlying rough tissue surface, like an ulcer, without epithelial coverage. Usually, a circle of varying sizes or irregularly shaped red areas can be seen on the cervix within the vagina. These areas are covered by a single layer of columnar epithelium. Beneath it, capillaries are congested and edematous, glands are blocked, storing sticky or purulent secretions. The surface often appears bright red, uneven, which is what is referred to as cervical erosion. After cervical erosion occurs, painless abortion may happen, often resulting in increased vaginal discharge, viscous, occasionally purulent or bloody discharge, accompanied by lower back pain, abdominal pain, and a heavy feeling in the lower abdomen. It may also cause sexual contact.

Ovarian cysts clinically often manifest as mild abdominal pain, discomfort in the lower abdomen, increased vaginal discharge, yellowish vaginal discharge, foul-smelling vaginal discharge, menstrual irregularities, and usually a solid and painless mass can be felt in the lower abdomen. Sometimes pain may occur during intercourse. When the cyst affects hormone secretion, symptoms such as irregular vaginal bleeding or excessive body hair may appear. If the cyst undergoes torsion of the pedicle, there may be severe abdominal pain, bloating, difficulty breathing, decreased appetite, nausea, and fever. Larger cysts can press on the bladder area, causing frequent urination and difficulty urinating. Especially when these symptoms are more severe, bleeding occurs frequently and simultaneously, the possibility of women having ovarian cysts is higher, and the harm of malignant ovarian cancer is even greater.

In the early stages, ovarian cysts do not have obvious clinical manifestations, and patients are often found during gynecological examinations for other diseases. Later, as the tumor grows, the patient will feel something. Symptoms and signs vary depending on the nature, size, development, whether there is secondary degeneration or complications;

So what causes ovarian cysts?

1. Genetics. Statistics show that 20% to 25% of ovarian tumor patients have a family history.

2. Endocrine factors. Although the ovary is small, it is an important organ for producing eggs, ovulation, endocrine hormones, and balancing endocrine functions. Ovarian tumors mostly occur during the reproductive age when endocrine function is vigorous. Clinically, many ovarian cyst patients and polycystic ovary syndrome patients' basic pathological physiological changes are due to the ovaries producing too much androgen, and the overproduction of androgen is the result of the combined action of multiple endocrine system dysfunction within the body.

3. Lifestyle factors. Long-term dietary structure, bad living habits, excessive psychological stress lead to excessive acidification of the body, overall decline in human function, reduced immune function, thereby leading to abnormal hyperplasia of ovarian tissue, eventually leading to ovarian cysts, and even canceration.

4. Environmental factors. Food contamination, such as plant growth hormones used in vegetables, and hormone components like clenbuterol in livestock and poultry feed. In recent years, with the improvement of living standards and changes in eating habits in our country, and some young and middle-aged women abusing hormone drugs and tonics such as breast enlargement, weight loss, and delaying aging, it is also possible that ovarian tumors are becoming more common and younger.
