Girl drugged netizens in retaliation after being deceived for sex by net friends

by mingdaolin on 2009-11-27 12:02:15

After being deceived into bed, she retaliated by administering "date rape drugs" to the man. The female internet user "Last Empty Encounter" truly ended up with nothing.

By reporter Chen Yanggen and correspondents Jiang Jian and Xin Cheng

Zhejiang Morning Post - A 23-year-old girl named Xiao Zhu from Yongkang, Jinhua, enjoyed chatting online under the username "Last Empty Encounter." Afraid of being deceived by men on the internet, she always carried a so-called "mind control drug," which was rumored to make people feel dazed after consumption and compel them to tell the truth. However, despite this precaution, Xiao Zhu was still tricked into having sexual relations by an older man. In retaliation, she used the "mind control drug" on him, extracted the truth, took his money, and left. Ultimately, she was arrested by the police and ended up losing everything.

In January this year, Xiao Zhu met a 36-year-old Wenzhou man named Lao Cheng online, whose username was "Big Laugh." Lao Cheng was eloquent and claimed that he had been too busy with business to have a girlfriend. Although Xiao Zhu had a boyfriend, after six months of intense chatting, she gradually developed feelings for Lao Cheng, and they exchanged phone numbers.

On June 15th, Xiao Zhu suddenly received a call from Lao Cheng, who said he was in Yiwu and invited her to meet up. Xiao Zhu traveled to Yiwu by bus. After meeting, Lao Cheng said he was going to Hangzhou to discuss business with a friend and invited Xiao Zhu to join him, promising they would return the same evening. Although hesitant at first, Xiao Zhu eventually agreed after Lao Cheng's persuasion.

Upon arriving in Hangzhou, Lao Cheng called his friend but then claimed that the friend couldn't join them for dinner due to unforeseen circumstances. Subsequently, he took Xiao Zhu out for dinner. By the time they finished eating, it was already nighttime. Lao Cheng proposed spending the night in Hangzhou before returning home, and Xiao Zhu didn't object.

They checked into a hotel room. Feeling that Lao Cheng genuinely cared about her, especially since she had recently quarreled with her boyfriend, Xiao Zhu engaged in sexual relations with Lao Cheng that night, naively considering him as her new boyfriend. However, Lao Cheng's phone kept ringing, and he deliberately avoided Xiao Zhu while answering calls. When Xiao Zhu asked who was calling, Lao Cheng became evasive, causing her suspicions to grow.

When Lao Cheng fell asleep, Xiao Zhu secretly checked his phone and discovered numerous flirtatious text messages between him and other girls. Realizing she had been played, Xiao Zhu remembered the "mind control drug" she carried with her. She poured a cup of hot water, added the drug, and woke Lao Cheng up. After drinking a few sips, Xiao Zhu asked, "Do you have a girlfriend?" In a daze, Lao Cheng mumbled, "We broke up; I don't like that woman," before falling back asleep.

Feeling increasingly angry, Xiao Zhu realized that all his previous words were lies. She then took 1100 yuan and a mobile phone from Lao Cheng and fled overnight to Yiwu by taxi.

After waking up, Lao Cheng reported the incident to the police. On July 6th, Xiao Zhu was arrested at an internet cafe in Jinhua. At the time, she was already pregnant and claimed the child was Lao Cheng's. However, without a DNA test, this claim could not be verified. Subsequently, Xiao Zhu was placed under house arrest.

Yesterday, the Jianggan District People's Procuratorate prosecuted Xiao Zhu on charges of robbery.
