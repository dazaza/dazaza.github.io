The boy's violent behavior is uncontrollable and the hospital can't find the cause.

by chanel289 on 2010-04-09 19:20:07

□Chief Reporter He Zhengquan, Correspondent Li Xin "When he was a child, he would suddenly throw things at others when the family wasn't paying attention, and then laugh maliciously. Now that he's grown up, his behavior has become even more bizarre and brutal. Can anyone tell me what's wrong with my son?" Ms. Hu Yinxiu from Huangchuan County said helplessly yesterday. Her 14-year-old son takes pleasure in harming animals and others. This strange behavior has caused much worry for the family. They have taken him to many hospitals and seen psychiatrists, but they still can't find the cause. Cruel treatment of animals during childhood Ms. Hu's son is named Xiao Bao (a pseudonym), and he is in the sixth grade of elementary school. Due to malnutrition during her pregnancy, Xiao Bao weighed only over two kilograms at birth. After Xiao Bao was born, he often cried and didn't learn to walk until nearly two years old. His balance was poor, making it hard for him to walk straight, and he had an especially bad temper. Ms. Hu said, "Xiao Bao's destructive power was astonishing. Before he turned one year old, he would tear books apart, throw things around, and then giggle." We all thought the child was just mischievous and didn't pay too much attention. "As the child grew older, he gradually exhibited more abnormal behaviors," Ms. Hu recalled. "Since he started walking, during meals at home, he would deliberately throw away his chopsticks and bowls, break things; when playing with other children outside, he would suddenly push them down or throw objects at their heads, sometimes even stab people with scissors or sickles. He treated large and small animals cruelly; once, he bit off a pig's teat, and because an old hen chased him, he killed all twenty-some chicks by pinching them." These strange behaviors continued and became increasingly intense as time went on. More uncontrollable behavior as he grows up Currently, Xiao Bao always puts himself at the center when playing with other classmates at school. If things don't go his way, his emotions suddenly become very extreme. According to his teacher, when the teacher criticized him, if another classmate looked at him, he would immediately rush over and hit that classmate, ignoring the teacher's scolding. While tidying up her son's room, Ms. Hu discovered that he had torn a book to shreds with a pen and crushed some iron boxes, possibly trying to vent his frustration this way. "Seeing my child like this breaks my heart," Ms. Hu said. Xiao Bao himself is also troubled, believing there's something wrong with his psychology. "But I just can't control myself. I often feel inexplicably irritable. Only by destroying things and hitting people, finding a place to vent, do I feel relieved," Xiao Bao said. Now, standing at 1.67 meters tall and weighing 50 kilograms, Xiao Bao looks pale, has a good appetite, and eats a lot. He says he feels sleepy all the time, but when he lies down, he finds it hard to fall asleep. He urinates frequently at night, feels dizzy while walking or exercising, and could fall down at any moment without willpower to control himself. In order to treat Xiao Bao, Ms. Hu and her husband took their son to many hospitals, where all the tests came back normal. "The doctors also can't explain clearly what's causing this. The child's emotions are always so intense, and he can't control his behavior. No one knows what kind of out-of-control thing he might do one day?" Ms. Hu worries deeply.
