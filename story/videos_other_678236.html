Man challenges to swim from Wuhan to Shanghai for Expo, plans to arrive in 35 days

by yuuuy8257 on 2012-02-27 12:53:21

Our news (reported by Huang Ying, communicated by Xu Dongdong, and intern Li Hui) "The first stop is the Hubei Pavilion!" In the midst of the deafening sound of gongs and drums, a 56-year-old "City of Rivers Knight" named Bao Zhengbin excitedly waved his flag and shouted. On the morning of yesterday, he began a marathon swim that would cover 1200 kilometers. Starting in Wuhan, he plans to swim 40 kilometers daily, divided into 32 stages, and aims to reach the Shanghai Expo Park in 35 days. As of press time, Bao Zhengbin had arrived at Gezhai Town in Ezhou.

**First Station Arrival**

Midway cramps caused an additional hour delay

At 7:55 AM yesterday, Bao Zhengbin started warming up. At exactly 8:00 AM, under the watchful eyes of well-wishers and media reporters, he approached the Han Yang Guan entrance to the river. With a loud "boom," the "City of Rivers Knight" dived into the rushing waters, beginning his unique journey swimming to the Expo.

After 7 hours and 10 minutes, Bao Zhengbin arrived at Gezhai Town in Ezhou at 3:30 PM yesterday. According to accompanying staff, Bao experienced cramping midway but continued swimming. Due to strong winds and rough currents, he was about one hour late compared to the scheduled arrival time.

**A 44-Year Dream**

An idea conceived at age 12, realized at age 56 through a 35-day marathon swim

"I want to swim all the way to Shanghai; this thought has persisted for 44 years." Before setting off yesterday, Bao Zhengbin told reporters that when he was 12, he was deeply moved by the scenery along the shore during his first boat trip to Shanghai, which sparked this idea. With the support of his workplace, his dream will finally come true due to the hosting of the World Expo in Shanghai.

It is reported that over the next 34 days, Bao Zhengbin will swim a "marathon" every day, covering a total of 32 stations including towns and scenic spots. This includes almost all cities along the Yangtze River from Wuhan to Shanghai, mainly Ezhou, Huangshi, Qichun, Wuxue, Huangmei, Jiujiang, Hukou, Pengze, Anqing, Tongling, Wuhu, Ma'anshan, Nanjing, Zhenjiang, Yangzhou, Jiangyin, Nanchong, and Shanghai. He plans to land at Wusongkou in Shanghai on August 13th.

"The first place I will visit upon arriving in Shanghai is the Hubei Pavilion at the Expo, where the water culture of our Hubei province is displayed." Bao Zhengbin said.

**Safety and Logistics**

Friends guard front and back, with homemade "Three Lucky Treasures"

Bao Zhengbin's actions have received support from three good friends who closely follow him, responsible for safety and logistics.

Throughout the entire swim, there is a large boat leading the way ahead, a small boat providing rescue services behind, and someone driving a car on land assisting.

Bao Zhengbin introduced that to prevent accidents en route, he combined years of experience to create personal protective equipment known as the "Three Lucky Treasures" — To avoid being cut by fishhooks or rocks underwater, he made a pair of plastic shoes full of holes, humorously naming them "water straw shoes"; For sun protection, in addition to purchasing a set of professional swimwear, he also made a rubber cap, adding a touch of knightly charm when worn; To address the danger of losing contact with logistics personnel as happened before, he placed his phone inside a waterproof plastic cover, allowing him to make calls in the water. Simultaneously, he attached a "swimming companion" around his waist containing water, food, towels, and clothes. These supplies ensure sufficient replenishment and smooth communication while in the river, maximizing his personal safety.

**Dietary Arrangements**

Mainly fruits, cooking meals after landing

It is understood that over the 35 days, Bao Zhengbin primarily consumes fruit daily and usually does not eat while swimming. After landing, he cooks his own meals and rests locally.

The entire event costs more than 50,000 yuan, mostly borne by Bao Zhengbin himself.

**Environmental Promotion**

Willing to encounter crocodiles during the swim

On this Expo journey, Bao Zhengbin will also distribute environmental protection brochures in various cities along the way. "Whenever I swim to a new location, I habitually mark the sewage outlets in that area," Bao Zhengbin said. Some citizens are concerned about Bao Zhengbin's safety, fearing he might be bitten by Yangtze alligators. Bao laughed and said, "If a crocodile eats me, it would mean the environment has improved!"

**City of Rivers Knight**

Challenging the Mississippi River next year

Bao Zhengbin, 56 years old, resides in the You San Community of Xinhua Street in Jianghan District, and is a well-known figure in the swimming community of the City of Rivers. Previously, he set 12 Chinese mass sports records, including crossing the river five times within a day, earning him the nickname "City of Rivers Knight." Last July, he crossed the "Chinese Bermuda" - the Devil Waters of Poyang Lake, taking 3 hours and 45 minutes.

It is reported that crossing the ten-thousand-li-long Yangtze River by swimming from Wuhan to Shanghai makes Bao Zhengbin the first person to achieve this feat. After completing this Expo "swim," Bao Zhengbin also plans to challenge the Mississippi River in the United States next year.
