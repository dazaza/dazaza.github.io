Taobao "breaks" barriers, and negative effects follow

by ci4837223 on 2011-08-26 15:09:33

After the split of Taobao, negative effects also followed.

Firstly, the resources within Taobao will show a certain degree of reset and waste. For example, the Mall can only promote its own brand during market promotion, but cannot use the Taobao brand; originally integrated technical and customer service staff are now divided among various subsidiaries, which means that each business unit needs to recruit talents again. In the view of Jiang Peng, president of Taobao, this is a problem that Taobao must face. After so many years of development, Taobao has many things that can be shared, so there's no need for redundant construction on a large scale. As long as there is agreement on a larger level, some minor repetitions won't have a significant impact.

Despite this, the three more focused subsidiaries will still attract doubts. Taking Etao.com as an example, Alibaba separated it from Taobao to a certain extent to make the commitment of full-network search more credible. However, merchants outside Taobao that are indexed might subconsciously question the priority and fairness of product display. Therefore, in the future, if Etao wants to develop various business models under the condition of search, it will face considerable difficulties.

As for Tmall, its platform model advantage lies in the front end, i.e., product display, understanding of customer needs, promotional methods, and buyer traffic, etc., but it is not proficient in the back-end supply chain integration, warehousing, logistics, and other issues. In fact, the quality of the back-end links will directly affect the profitability of an e-commerce company. Whether Taobao can integrate the logistics platform well not only relates to the competitive situation with other B2C giants but also directly determines whether the company can build a complete e-commerce ecosystem in the future. The evolution of the ecosystem is the ultimate goal of this spin-off.

From this perspective, for the mall to be independent, having an independent domain name is far from enough. Thus, in June of last year, Taobao officially announced its long-planned grand logistics plan, which includes the Logistics Treasure platform, logistics partner system, and logistics service standards system. Even so, to achieve this huge plan, the mall still has a very arduous road ahead.

Nowadays, although Taobao has signed up a large number of third-party warehousing and logistics companies, most Taobao merchants still solve delivery problems on their own. Some merchants have even never heard of Logistics Treasure. Moreover, Logistics Treasure faces competitors not only from B2C enterprises but also traditional logistics companies that hope to transform into platforms. Compared to those logistics companies that already have large warehouses set up all over the country, whether Taobao can quickly integrate resources remains uncertain.

Returning to C2C, for a long time in the future, the mall will still need the marketplace to provide traffic. The several companies spun off cannot operate completely independently yet. Then, while Alibaba is vigorously promoting the mall, how does it protect the marketplace to a certain extent? How can the value of the marketplace be further explored in the future?

In Zeng Ming's eyes, what the group is currently doing is to get the team onto the right track as soon as possible, and some things may not be so clear and explicit. Anyway, thinking and doing at the same time is always better than just talking about it, after all, the nature of the Internet itself is to move forward with changes.
