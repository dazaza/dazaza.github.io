Stop paying coaches for driving lessons unnecessarily

by zxcdm331dl on 2011-08-26 13:23:39

Learning to drive at a driving school can cost upward of three or four thousand yuan. In addition to basic tuition fees, the "biggest expense" is actually collected by instructors under various names such as "showing respect to examiners." Worried about being targeted during exams, students have no choice but to obediently offer bribes.

In recent years, the "unspoken rules" of driver's license exams have taken root and spread in Hechi like a cult. Many students detest these practices yet feel powerless to stop them, leading to increasing calls to crack down on these unspoken rules. "If any examiner or instructor is found to engage in eating, drinking, demanding favors, or accepting bribes, they must be severely punished after an investigation confirms the facts," said traffic police authorities in a strong condemnation of the practice of collecting "black money" during driving exams.

The suspension of driving tests has its reasons.

Recently, netizen "Injustice Must Be Addressed 66" posted on the Hechi Net forum questioning whether it was true that "Hechi's driving exams were halted due to excessive bribery among examiners and suspected cheating." The post drew significant attention from netizens, making the unspoken rules of driving exams a target of public criticism.

According to information from the traffic police department, the suspension of Hechi's driving exams had valid reasons. It was explained that the reason for pausing part of the subject two test items (nine indoor items) was due to regulations stipulated in the "Regulations on the Application and Use of Motor Vehicle Driver's Licenses" (Ministry of Public Security Order No. 111) and Article 43 of the "Regulations on the Business Operations of Motor Vehicle Driver's License Services." Starting from April 1, 2011, "For those applying for large buses, city buses, medium-sized buses, large trucks, small cars, small automatic cars, and disability-specific small automatic passenger cars, the subject two test must be conducted using a site driving skills examination system. After the exam, the results will automatically upload to the computer management system."

Based on the Ministry of Public Security order and work standard requirements, the Guangxi Autonomous Region Public Security Department closed the manual input function for site exam scores on July 1st. Six municipal detachments, including Hechi, paused part of the subject two test items due to non-compliance with hardware standards. Starting from July 1st this year, the vehicle management office of the Hechi Traffic Police Detachment suspended the subject two site exams. The Hechi subject two driving skills exam site is expected to complete construction by September this year and resume normal exams, achieving automatic upload of exam results to the computer management system.

"Paying money to pass" is a lie.

It is introduced that the driving test consists of three subjects. Subject one mainly tests road traffic safety laws, regulations, and related knowledge; subject two is the site exam; and subject three is the road test. Currently, since subject one exams have all adopted computerized paperless exams and subject two pole tests have achieved intelligent assessment, it completely eliminates the possibility of human manipulation. Next, the other site driving skill exams for subject two will also achieve computerized intelligent assessment by September this year, and subject three will ensure the entire exam process is open, fair, and just through video and audio surveillance.

The traffic police department strictly follows the price control department's charging standards for exam fees and requires examiners to give learners a "pre-exam lesson" for each subject, announcing exam discipline, publishing exam fee standards, and emphasizing that the traffic police department does not collect any fees other than exam fees during the exam process. Therefore, the traffic police department absolutely does not allow instructors to collect money under the name of examiners and reminds candidates not to easily believe the lie told by some instructors: "As long as you pay money, you can pass the exam."

"Since the current driving test has been computerized, even if students want to bribe their way through, it's impossible, so why waste the money?" A responsible person from the vehicle management office pointed out that under high transparency, driver's license exams score and announce results on-site, leaving very little room for examiners to cheat. At the same time, paying money to influence the examiner's question selection is entirely baseless because question selection occurs on the morning of the exam day before coaches and students enter the exam room when examiners collectively select questions. During internal question selection, coaches are not allowed to be present, thus eliminating the possibility of human manipulation.

"The exam is actually testing psychology, knowledge, and skills," the responsible person pointed out. As of July 20th this year, 13,438 people in our city have taken the driving test, with 10,965 passing, accounting for 81.59%. Therefore, as long as students study diligently and hard, they can pass the exam and obtain their driver's licenses smoothly.

Severe crackdown on "black money" collection behavior

It is introduced that there are currently 25 car-class driving schools in the city, and competition is fierce. For a long time, to attract students, various schools have resorted to lowering training fees to attract students. Relevant personnel pointed out that on one hand, the current driver's license examination system and social environment both have varying degrees of deficiencies, leading to candidates taking exams with a侥幸 mentality by paying money; on the other hand, it cannot be ruled out that some instructors increase their income by collecting "black money" through unspoken rules, resulting in the persistent existence of unspoken rules in driving exams.

It is understood that the traffic police department is the main authority responsible for exams, only handling applications from students, conducting exams, and issuing driver's licenses. Instructor qualifications are issued by the transport management department, but their management and constraints belong to the driving schools where they are employed.

"The school management has always been strict, absolutely not allowing instructors to solicit bribes from students or require students to 'collect funds' to buy off examiners." During interviews, a responsible person from a driving school in the Hechi urban area emphasized that the school has always severely cracked down on so-called "unspoken rules." When instructors come to teach, they must first sign a commitment letter with the school, guaranteeing self-discipline; if students encounter random charges, they can report to the school. Once verified, the school will immediately dismiss the instructor.

"Some instructors hint to students to pay 'passing fees,' which stimulates the students'侥幸 mentality, which is detrimental to improving training quality and fosters unhealthy social winds." A responsible person from the vehicle management office pointed out that instructors collecting 'black money' from students is an illegal act. If any bribery behavior is discovered, it can be reported by calling the hotline. When reporting, detailed time, location, examiner badge number, name, or other forms of evidence should be provided to facilitate investigations by the public security disciplinary inspection department.

In April and May this year, two driving schools in Yizhou and Jinchengjiang were reported for having instructors collecting 'black money.' After investigations by the traffic police department, they were given two-month business suspension rectification treatments. The traffic police department pointed out that driving schools where instructors confirmed to have collected 'black money' would be subjected to examination suspension measures. Only after rectifications and successful inspections would the school's examination applications be accepted. If illegal activities are constituted, relevant responsible persons will be handed over to relevant departments for legal accountability.
