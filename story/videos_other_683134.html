Summer skincare focus: Hydrate your skin thoroughly

by cshelive9520 on 2012-02-29 11:57:15

Hydrate your skin in the morning: Moisturizing and hydrating is much simpler than sun protection. All you need is a good moisturizing water and some sheet masks. Get up ten minutes earlier every day, use the sheet mask with the moisturizing water, and apply it to your face for about 5 minutes. Hydrating your skin in the morning can ensure that your skin stays hydrated throughout the day.

Small TIPS: If you have non-sensitive skin, you can use hyaluronic acid water instead of moisturizing water. It not only has great hydrating effects but also makes your skin elastic. After using the mask, pair it with a lotion to keep your skin moisturized all day.

Avoid direct confrontation with the scorching sun at noon: Sun protection is the foundation of summer skincare. The best way to protect against the sun is to avoid the intense heat as much as possible. Office workers should try to reduce their exposure to the hot sun. Generally, the ultraviolet intensity is relatively weak before 8 am and after 6 pm, so choosing to travel during these times is best.

Between 11 am and 3 pm, the sunlight is the strongest, and the damage caused by ultraviolet rays is also the greatest. Traveling during this time is self-destructive for the skin. For those who must confront the sun directly, in addition to wearing sun hats, sunglasses, long skirts, and shawls, they must also apply sunscreen with at least SPF 30.

Small TIPS: Even if you stay at home, you must apply sunscreen with SPF 15 because ultraviolet rays are everywhere. Celery, coriander, and white radishes are all "photosensitive" vegetables. These vegetables make skin prone to spots more likely to develop pigmentation, so it's better to eat less of them.

Three key points for night skincare:

1. Exfoliation: In summer, due to the accelerated metabolism of the skin, waste and old keratin layers quickly accumulate, making the skin lose its tender luster. The first step to rejuvenating the skin is to remove the old keratin. As temperatures rise, the keratin layer tightens to protect the skin from UV radiation, causing the accumulated old keratin on the skin surface to thicken easily. This leads to a decrease in the skin's ability to breathe and absorb moisture, even clogging pores and causing annoying pimples, dirty blackheads, and making the skin look dull. Therefore, regular exfoliation is very necessary.

Small TIPS: If your keratin layer is thick and your pores are large, you can choose a gentle scrub. But if your skin is sensitive and fine-textured, using a scrub-type exfoliant may cause adverse effects. It is recommended to use a cleansing mask that gently removes keratin. The wings of the nose and the corners of the mouth are areas where melanin tends to accumulate and are hard to clean. Before removing keratin from detailed areas, you can first apply a mask to soften the keratin for easier cleaning.

2. Combat UV rays and prevent sunspots: Sunspots are caused by long-term exposure of melanin in the body to UV rays, leaving melanin on the epidermis, leading to dullness and sunspots. In addition to sun protection, it is important to promptly clean the skin. Deep cleansing can effectively remove dirt from the skin and reduce the occurrence of sunspots to a certain extent.

Small TIPS: Getting sunburned is unavoidable, but you can learn to soothe your skin. Place cucumber slices in milk and refrigerate them for about 15 minutes before applying them to your face. This can not only whiten and hydrate but also soothe the skin. During the refrigeration process, you can give your skin a deep cleanse, which can actually reduce spots caused by melanin.

3. Supplement collagen and stay away from aging: Beauty-conscious girls know that after being exposed to the sun, UVA light waves in sunlight penetrate the epidermis and reach deep into the skin, damaging collagen and elastic fibers in the skin, leading to wrinkles. Eating more collagen-rich foods like pig trotters and cartilage can slow down the aging symptoms caused by collagen loss. At the same time, ensuring adequate sleep can also delay skin aging.

Small TIPS: More than 90% of premature skin aging is caused by improper sun exposure. Avoiding excessive sun exposure is essential to staying youthful. Additionally, consuming antioxidant-rich vegetables and fruits such as carrots, grapes, and tomatoes can slow down skin aging.
