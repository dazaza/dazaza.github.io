Beijing launches mobile app for appointment registration, supporting 141 secondary and tertiary hospitals

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 14:18:29

Following the telephone appointment service at 114 and the official website appointment, Beijing has recently launched mobile client appointment registration. Number sources from 141 secondary and tertiary hospitals can all be "palm-reserved". Although the reservation steps and platforms are consistent, if you want to cancel the reservation, you still have to log in to the website or make a phone call.

"Please click q.114menhu.com to download the '114 Life Assistant' app for easy registration." After downloading the "114 Life Assistant", I immediately saw the word "registration" in a relatively prominent position on the homepage, with the display of "Beijing Unified Appointment Registration Platform". Currently, this software supports reservations at 72 tertiary hospitals and 69 secondary hospitals across the city.

Based on automatic positioning, the software searches for the 11 hospitals closest to the mobile user's location, sorted from nearest to farthest. Each hospital also displays daily number release times, arrival routes, specialty department introductions, etc. Except for the restriction of "stopping next day reservations at 3:00 PM," each hospital shows the availability of numbers for the next 90 days, allowing instant reservations or direct transfer to 114 for manual consultation and reservation.

Although I had already registered on the unified reservation platform, after selecting the hospital and department on the mobile version and clicking "reservation," whether entering my name or phone number, I was informed that "this user does not exist." After re-entering the username, password, phone number, and verification code to register again, I had to input my ID number and real name in the reservation interface to successfully reserve.

After a successful reservation, within one minute, I received a confirmation text message including pickup time, required documents, and reservation identification codes. However, I found out that if you want to cancel the reservation after a successful booking, you still need to log in to the website or make a phone call to cancel it; currently, the mobile app cannot cancel the reservation.

During my experience, I found that mobile appointment registration is still somewhat unstable. For example, during the initial test, regardless of the selected hospital or department, it always showed as "numbers full, please choose another hospital," requiring multiple attempts to successfully book an appointment. However, in recent days, the mobile app has been updated and is now more stable.

In addition, I noticed that the software has opened up proxy registration services, but when actually operating it, it was never successful. The system either showed that the SMS sending verification code failed or there was a login error.

□Reservation Reminder: Children's Hospital requires filling in guardian information

For patients without an ID number, other valid documents supported by the unified platform can be used to reserve appointments. For those who have declared their household registration but have not yet obtained an ID card, they can normally register and make reservations on the unified platform using their ID number and present relevant valid documents (such as a household register) associated with the ID number for check-in at the hospital.

In addition, registration for Beijing Children's Hospital and the Research Institute of Pediatrics is relatively special. Registration information can fill in the guardian's real identity information, and the reservation process must fill in the patient's basic information according to regulations.

A mobile phone binding cannot exceed 5 users. If the hospital's specified time limit is exceeded without picking up the number, the reserved number will be canceled.

The same mobile phone number cannot bind more than 5 registered users or ID numbers; the same mobile phone number cannot reserve more than 15 numbers within 3 months; cancellation of numbers within 3 months cannot exceed 15 times. Users who violate the rules will be placed on a blacklist and will only be able to log into the unified reservation platform and cancel reservations, no longer being able to make new reservations.

□Reporter Investigation

Private software sends confirmation messages identical to the official version

Tied to the unified reservation platform

The popularity of reservation registration methods has also made people see "business opportunities." Searching for "reservation registration" in the APP reveals 49 different types of software available for download. I found that apart from the officially launched mobile client for reservation registration, there are 5 "private" versions specifically targeting hospitals in Beijing, one of which is free to download, three charge 6 yuan for downloading the client, and another named "Beijing Medical Care Assistant" charges 12 yuan.

All these software are "tied" to the unified reservation platform. For example, if you have already registered on the "Unified Reservation Platform," you can directly log in; if not, you can complete registration on the mobile phone. Taking the free version of "Beijing Reservation Registration" as an example, I smoothly logged in by entering the registered name and ID number on the official reservation platform along with the verification code displayed by the software.

The "Beijing Reservation Registration" software shows that it can reserve numbers from over a hundred hospitals in the city, including private hospitals, secondary hospitals, municipal and ministry-level tertiary hospitals, and even different campuses and branches of large hospitals. I saw that the Huilongguan campus of Jishuitan Hospital offers reservations for 21 departments.

Premium version required for popular hospitals

I noticed that private software only had free versions two or three months ago, and premium versions were only recently introduced, adding reminder functions for registration and medication. Although the free version can also reserve numbers, most popular hospitals such as Peking Union Medical College Hospital, Fuwai Hospital, and Peking University Hospital can only be booked through the premium version.

After a successful reservation, I similarly received a confirmation text message containing pick-up time, visit sequence number, and reservation identification code. Strangely, the reservation text message I received ended with "Download 114 Life Assistant" and "5 yuan cash coupon gift service," making it difficult for me to distinguish which was the confirmation text message for the successful reservation via "114 Mobile Assistant." The Huilongguan campus of Jishuitan Hospital stated that with this confirmation text message, you can pick up the number at the hospital on the same day.

However, both the official software and private software use the same method to cancel reservations.

More humanized than the official software

I found that compared to the official version, the private versions are more humanized. For example, in each department section, you can see the specialties of doctors at different levels, registration fees, outpatient times, and the number of available and remaining numbers. This not only helps patients choose the right doctor based on their condition but also greatly improves the efficiency of registration, especially for the sought-after departments of popular hospitals, making it more convenient.

"Beijing Reservation Registration" also added leftover number monitoring, automatically reminding users when there are leftover numbers available. Relevant technical departments indicated that developing these software applications is not difficult, not only generating download fees but also occupying some platform resources.

>Response One

Beijing Unicom

No exclusivity in reservation channels

Yesterday, I interviewed a relevant person in charge of Beijing Unicom regarding non-official reservation registration software. He said that according to the authorization of the Beijing Municipal Health Bureau, the official versions of the reservation registration platforms are the 114 telephone reservation, Beijing Unified Reservation Registration Website, and the newly launched 114 mobile client. All hospitals' reservation numbers must be released through these three channels, but this cooperation has no "exclusivity." That is, large hospitals can also choose other cooperation methods to relieve the pressure of window registration, which is why I could successfully reserve a number at the Huilongguan campus of Jishuitan Hospital in other versions of mobile software. It is understood that Peking Union Medical College Hospital and 301 Hospital have cooperated with some banks for reservation registration, and number reservations can be made on bank self-service machines.

This person in charge stated that there are no charges for reservations made on the official unified reservation system, purely a public welfare act, but reminded patients that non-official reservation software not only guides patients to use the paid version through the free version, creating potential traps for registration, but also poses a risk of leaking users' ID numbers, names, and phone numbers.

Regarding the issues of duplicate registration currently existing in the mobile version of the official software, this person in charge stated that the mobile reservation software is still in the testing phase and will be further improved and refined.

Regarding the situation where the confirmation text messages sent by private reservation software to successfully reserved patients are exactly the same as those from the official version software, this person in charge stated that Beijing Unicom has never collaborated with any software company on the development of reservation software, is unaware of the matter, and will conduct investigations.

>Response Two

Beijing Municipal Health Bureau

No cooperation with any commercial websites

The Beijing Municipal Health Bureau revealed that so far, the Beijing Unified Reservation Registration Platform has not cooperated with any commercial websites for reservation registration and does not allow any websites, organizations, or individuals to commercially exploit the unified platform. The unified external service reservation telephone number is 114, and the domain name of the unified external service reservation registration website is www.bjguahao.gov.cn.

According to requirements, 80% of hospitals' numbers must be invested in the reservation platform, and 20% should be placed at the hospital windows.

And according to regulations, secondary and above public hospitals providing reservation registration services through social platforms should do so through the city's unified platform and should not entrust other social platforms to provide reservation registration services to ensure the public nature of the service.

The Beijing Municipal Health Bureau stated that it welcomes major websites to introduce the unified platform but prohibits them from using it for commercial activities to increase their visibility, attract netizens, or boost traffic. They should not mislead patients by claiming to provide reservation registration services simply by offering links, increasing the risk of patient information leakage and wasting patients' time. In case of any of the above situations, the Beijing Municipal Health Bureau will work together with industrial and commercial, information management, and network management departments to hold relevant websites and organizations accountable.

Data shows that since the launch of the unified reservation registration service platform in Beijing for two years, a total of 521.87 million numbers have been released, including 123.5 million expert numbers, with a reservation rate of 41.1%.
