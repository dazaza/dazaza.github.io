Wuhan suspected surrogate pregnant woman abandons operation and flees overnight after being questioned by doctor before delivery

by dfasd98fh on 2012-02-24 16:14:47

The pregnant woman from Shanghai surrogacy [http://www.daiyunbaobei.com] was about to give birth, yet neither her [mother's family] nor her [mother-in-law] could clearly state her husband’s name. In the end, these three individuals not only refused a life-saving surgery but also abandoned the 6000 yuan deposit for the operation and quietly left. The doctor suspected that this woman was a surrogate mother.

This strange incident occurred at a large hospital in Wuchang. Yesterday, a reporter interviewed the on-duty doctor.

She recounted that around 2 a.m. yesterday, three women arrived at the hospital's obstetrics and gynecology department. Among them, a young woman appeared heavily pregnant, claiming to be 29 years old and 36 weeks and 5 days pregnant, seeking emergency medical care due to premature rupture of membranes (water breaking).

Upon examination, it was found that this pregnant woman had several abnormalities including premature rupture of membranes, umbilical cord around the neck, and breech presentation. The doctors recommended immediate admission for a cesarean section; otherwise, both the mother and baby's lives would be in danger.

During the consultation, the attentive doctor noticed various suspicious points. The two older women claimed to be the pregnant woman's mother-in-law and sister, but the three of them seemed unfamiliar with each other. The expressions and actions of the mother-in-law and sister showed no appropriate concern.

When asked for the name of the pregnant woman's husband, more doubts arose: the sister said it was "Zhou Jun," while the mother-in-law said it was "Zhang Hongjun." Meanwhile, the pregnant woman remained silent. The doctor became increasingly suspicious and directly questioned them, "Are you involved in surrogacy?" All three denied it.

When signing the surgical consent form, the already suspicious doctor informed them that the situation would be truthfully reported to the hospital's medical affairs department and other relevant departments. Upon hearing this, the three became uncertain and ultimately decided to abandon the surgery and leave.

The doctor offered to call an ambulance for them, but they refused. They even left without reclaiming the 6000 yuan deposit for the surgery.

In the interview yesterday, the doctor said, "Watching the young woman walk away, we all felt sorry for her and worried about her current condition and whether she is safe."

She told reporters that the reason she decided to speak out about this matter was because not long ago, the department had treated another surrogate mother who was carrying twins. She suffered from sudden massive bleeding and was only saved after half a day of emergency treatment.

From a medical perspective, pregnancy itself carries risks such as amniotic fluid embolism and massive bleeding. Surrogacy is not legally protected, making it even riskier.
