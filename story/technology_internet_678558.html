Help you analyze the prospects of stone stream frog farming

by sxwi9j4r on 2012-02-27 15:19:28

The scientific name of Shixi frog is Spiny Chest Frog, also known as Stone Chicken and Mountain Frog. It is a valuable aquatic product in the hilly areas of South China. The Shixi frog has peculiar biological characteristics. In its natural wild state, it likes to live under the damp, quiet, low-light, near water flow, cool mountain rocks. It usually crouches in stone caves, and is active at night, mostly going out to forage in the evening. Breeding Value - The flesh of Shixi frogs is tender, delicious, and rich in nutrition. It also has the effects of clearing heat, detoxifying, and nourishing the body. It is said to have anti-cancer effects, with both high edible value and medical value. Biological Characteristics - The Shixi frog has characteristics such as feeding on live prey, hibernation, and strong reproductive ability. Feeding activity: The Shixi frog likes to eat live animal feed and does not or rarely eats dead animals or other motionless feed because of its special vision that can only see moving feed. Its preferred animal feed includes earthworms, mealworms, fly maggots, loaches, small fish and shrimp, and other insects. Tadpoles prefer to eat tender green aquatic algae plants. Hibernation: The Shixi frog has the habit of hibernation. When the outside temperature drops below 10°C, it stops eating and enters hibernation. Under climatic conditions south of the Yangtze River, the hibernation period lasts about four months, generally from late November to mid-March. During this period, there's no need to feed them; just keep the water quality fresh. The adult Shixi frog and tadpoles have strong cold resistance. As long as the winter water temperature remains above 0°C, they can safely overwinter, and summer water temperatures not exceeding 30°C ensure safe summers. The suitable water temperature for vigorous growth is between 15°C-22°C. If indoor industrialized breeding is adopted with artificial temperature control measures, the vigorous growth period can be extended, the hibernation period shortened, and the growth rate of Shixi frogs accelerated.

Strong Reproductive Ability: Under normal management conditions, frogs weighing more than 200 grams can lay eggs twice a year, mainly concentrated in April-May and July-August. Each pair can lay up to 1,000-2,000 eggs per time. The eggs are sticky, large with thin membranes, often adhering in patches to stones or the side walls of ponds. After 10-15 days of incubation, they hatch into tadpoles with a hatching rate of over 90%, and a tadpole-to-young-frog transformation rate of around 80%. In places with good water quality, about 1,000 tadpoles per square meter can be raised, 150-200 young frogs, and 50-80 adult frogs. One full-time breeder can handle all tasks for about 100 square meters of breeding pools.
