A Happy Paradise for Migrant Children

by lmxbbwudi23 on 2012-02-24 17:19:29

Under the guidance and recommendation of the women volunteers, migrant children learned to choose books correctly, mastered reading methods, and consolidated their reading results.

With the new semester beginning, the migrant children from Yanjiang Street in Pukou District of Nanjing will release their accumulated energy to embrace new challenges. "This winter vacation was both fulfilling and interesting, I'm all set for the new semester!" said Tan Ru, a migrant child, with seriousness.

Tan Ru came to Nanjing from Fuyang, Anhui, with her parents and has been living in Yanjiang Street for only two years, currently studying in the second grade at Yanjiang Primary School. After the final exam results were announced, Tan Ru's mother was especially happy: "Since the child came to this school, her academic performance has improved a lot, and she also learned paper-cutting in the 'Vacation School'."

Tan Ru's mother told reporters that Tan Ru has liked drawing and making handicrafts since she was young, but they, as migrant workers, neither had time nor the ability to teach her. Since the school teachers encouraged her to participate in the 'Vacation School', "My child can cut out all sorts of beautiful paper-cut paintings, and our family has even made a special showcase cabinet for her works. Her excellent works have also been displayed in the school windows, making her more confident and sensible. We really thank the 'Vacation School' founded by the Women's Federation for nurturing and educating the children."

The 'Vacation School' mentioned by Tan Ru's mother is the joyous paradise for the migrant children of Yanjiang Street. Here, every winter and summer vacation, the teachers from the Yanjiang Primary School Rural Youth Palace and the women volunteers voluntarily organize various activities for the migrant children such as reading, calligraphy, paper-cutting, recitation, and psychological salons, allowing the children to spend meaningful vacations.

Yanjiang Street in Pukou District of Nanjing is an area where there is a relatively high concentration of migrant population. Due to work reasons, parents tend to neglect the care of their migrant children. The principal of Yanjiang Primary School introduced that the school has a total of 831 students, among which 60% are children of migrant workers, indicating a large number of migrant children.

Upon learning about this situation, the Women's Federation of Pukou District and Yanjiang Street began planning and raising funds for a 'Vacation School' specifically serving migrant children to avoid them being neglected or unmanaged during holidays.

Since the start of student vacations in 2009, the Yanjiang Street Women's Federation has innovatively collaborated with Yanjiang Primary School to establish the 'Vacation School', incorporating the local migrant children into effective management. They have built a networked educational platform led by the Women's Federation, with schools as carriers and cultural and sports stations as bases, successively carrying out various community activities suitable for the characteristics of migrant children, such as "[I and My Child Grow Together - Colorful Summer Community Activities]" and "[Fragrance of Books Overflows the Community, Heart Education Promotes My Growth]."

Deputy Chairperson Wu Jimai of the Yanjiang Street Women's Federation introduced that each phase of the activity is meticulously planned, and characteristic courses such as Song Ci poetry, stories in paintings, and Olympic knowledge contests have been launched, deeply loved by the migrant children, becoming a distinctive activity brand creating a "healthy, happy, and joyful" life for the migrant children.

Qin Bin, a migrant child from Wufeng Tujia Autonomous County, Yichang City, Hubei Province, transferred to Yanjiang Primary School in 2006. His parents both work in private enterprises in Yanjiang Street. Speaking about the changes in her child, Qin Bin's mother could not contain her inner excitement. She said: "We have lived in Yanjiang Street for over five years, and what makes the whole family most grateful is the 'Vacation School' held regularly every winter and summer vacation by the Yanjiang Street Women's Federation and Yanjiang Primary School. The child enrolled in the reading club and computer class, developed a love for reading, and also learned computer skills, winning awards in national reading-writing competitions and computer production contests."

In order to allow migrant children to enjoy the same treatment as other children and own the "same blue sky," the Yanjiang Street finance department allocates 100,000 yuan annually, exclusively used for the education and activity expenses of migrant children. Moreover, in the annual selection of advanced models such as 'Happy Families' and 'Virtuous Girl Role Models,' migrant children and families account for more than 30%.

In addition, the Yanjiang Street Women's Federation actively seeks government support, providing free health insurance for 0~3-year-old migrant children who meet family planning requirements. To date, a total investment of 150,000 yuan has been made, ensuring that migrant children receive equal services as registered children.

The Yanjiang Street Women's Federation also uses the community's 'Women and Children’s Home' and 'Family Station' as platforms to establish early education bases and archives for migrant children. Based on the different situations of the migrant children, they promptly provide them with health care, psychological counseling, and economic assistance, ensuring that migrant children do not feel lonely and do not lose family affection while moving around.
