[Principle of Pimple Scar Formation]

Pimple scars are formed due to infection or external pressure, often because pimples were not treated promptly and appropriately during their outbreak. The inflammatory response of skin cells causes damage to skin tissue, resulting in scar formation. Since there are various types of acne, there are also many forms of acne scars. The more severe the inflammatory reaction during an acne outbreak, the more serious the skin tissue damage; the deeper the inflammation, the deeper the skin tissue damage, and the more severe the potential pimple scars left behind.

[Basic Principles for Removing Pimple Marks]: Some people try various methods to remove pimple marks, some of which are correct and others are just misinterpretations. Therefore, when choosing methods to remove pimple marks, it is essential to follow the following three basic principles.

1. To remove pimple marks, one should first do a good job of eliminating pimples, preventing those pesky zits from popping up again. It would be futile to waste energy if pimple marks keep coming back after being removed. Therefore, the first principle of removing pimple marks is to thoroughly cure the pimples on your face before attempting to remove the marks.

2. Many people using scar-lightening products seek quick and effective results, hoping for immediate effects. As a result, many people apply large amounts of pimple mark removal products on their faces. This is not a wise move, as pimple mark removal products often contain certain hormone stimulants. Long-term use will only further damage already injured skin.

3. Sunscreen must be done well when removing pimple marks. Excessive ultraviolet rays outdoors cause skin pigmentation to accumulate and settle, making pimple marks darker and heavier. If this phenomenon persists, it will only make the process of removing pimple marks increasingly difficult.

[Aspects to Note When Removing Pimple Marks]

1. Don't forget to improve old pimple marks

Pimples generally leave traces on the face, whether deep or shallow spots, which can be quite annoying. Like imprints on the face, they are hard to erase, and no matter how much treatment is applied, it cannot restore the original condition; it can only be improved. Uneven skin with large areas of pimple scars can be smoothed out by laser treatment, similar to smoothing uneven wood, removing excess skin, and reducing surface differences. However, laser treatment is not the only option for old pimple marks. If the scar is sharp and deep, it might be better to surgically remove it or transplant skin from elsewhere on the patient’s body to fill it, yielding better results.

2. Prevention is better than cure

Many people think that having pimples during youth is fine, assuming that once puberty is over, the skin will naturally return to its original state. This mistaken belief has misled countless young skins! In fact, once pimple marks form, they leave permanent traces on the face. Therefore, dermatologists repeatedly emphasize that all treatments are less effective than addressing the root cause - "anti-acne".

3. Seize the golden time to remove marks

To rescue clear and white skin, acne sufferers must seize the golden time within 2 months of pimple mark formation. After acne inflammation, it destroys the epidermal structure of the skin, and in severe cases, it affects the dermis and subcutaneous tissue. At this point, the skin activates its repair mechanism, usually stabilizing within 3-6 months. Therefore, within 2 months of pimple mark formation, early intervention can reduce tissue damage to the minimum, or even restore the original smoothness and fineness.

4. Pay attention to special constitutions

When dealing with pimple marks, people with keloid constitutions should be particularly cautious. Keloids commonly occur on the chin, chest, back, arms, etc., and since this constitution easily leads to scar proliferation issues, improper handling may result in red hard lumps forming after acne heals, sometimes even larger than the original pimples.
