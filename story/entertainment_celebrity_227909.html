The much-anticipated beauty pageant event, the 2009 Miss Hong Kong competition, concluded successfully on the evening of the 22nd. The winner was 23-year-old Connie Lau Chin-ting, who claimed the crown of Miss Hong Kong. The first runner-up was contestant number ten, Cecilia Lee Tze-man, while contestant number eight, Ingrid Xiong Ying-po, took home the title of second runner-up. Contestant number nine, Daphne Yuan Jia-min, won the "Most Photogenic Miss" award, and contestant number six, Kelly Wong Ka-lai, was crowned "Miss International Friendship."

Connie Lau is a securities analyst with a university degree. Cecilia Lee is 21 years old and a university student. Ingrid Xiong, aged 25, works as a marketing assistant and holds a university degree. Daphne Yuan, 24, is a film distribution manager with a university education. Kelly Wong, also 24, is a doctoral student.

The top ten finalists competed for the titles of Miss Hong Kong 2009, first runner-up, second runner-up, "Most Photogenic Miss," and "Miss International Friendship," as well as total cash prizes and gifts worth nearly HK$3.5 million.

This year's judges included the legendary veteran actor Simon Yam Tak-wah, renowned beauty Michelle Li Ka-shin, and Olympic gymnast-turned-celebrity Liu Xuan. The "Most Photogenic Miss" judges were pop singer Kary Ng, actress Tiffany Liu, renowned makeup artist Rick Chin, and model Amanda S. The emcees were once again veteran comedians Charlie Young, Patrick Tse, and Kelvin Tsui.

Additionally, celebrities Raymond Lam, Kary Ng, and Aaron Kwok tested the contestants' quick wit and eloquence with questions about their backgrounds, rumors, or current events. The opening segment featured an extra special performance by eight-year-old prodigy Connie Talbot, who gained overnight fame through the UK's popular talent show "Britain's Got Talent."
