Exclusive Interview with the Chairman of the Global IPv6 Forum: China's Internet Hits the Ceiling

by tfseo8888 on 2010-04-15 22:02:40

Sina Technology News, April 8th midday news, the chairman of the Global IPv6 Forum, Latif Ladid, said in an exclusive interview with Sina Technology today that China's Internet is developing at high speed currently, but IP address resources, especially safe and usable IP addresses, can no longer meet the demand. "The booming Chinese Internet is encountering a ceiling."

Latif introduced that currently there are about 340 million available remaining IPv4 addresses globally, divided into 22 blocks. However, among these, 300 million addresses belong to 'Dirty Address', which should not be allocated but are still being used by some people, making them easy to be exploited by hackers and posing significant security risks. Therefore, only about 40 million safe-to-use 'Clean Address' remain globally, while the global annual demand for IPv4 addresses reaches 200 million.

According to statistics, China currently owns 230 million IPv4 address resources, second only to the United States. "China's Internet has developed rapidly; in the year 2000, China had only 80 million IP addresses. Currently, China's annual new demand for IP addresses is around 40 million," Latif stated, "but China has run out of Clean Address to use."

It was learned that the Asia-Pacific Network Information Center (APNIC) recently allocated 16 million new IP addresses for China, known as 'Block 1'. These addresses all belong to 'Dirty Address'. "They are unsafe, and it is recommended that China does not use them. The high-speed development of China's Internet may come to an end," Latif said. "ISP service providers and government agencies have realized this problem but deliberately ignore it. They also do not actively participate in IP address applications. Last year, APNIC held a distribution meeting in Kuala Lumpur, and China did not attend."

"Looking forward, the development of the Internet of Things, smart grids, and cloud computing will require more and safer IP address resources. The construction of the IPv6 network is imminent," Latif suggested that the Chinese government should implement mandatory IPv6 Ready certification for network equipment providers, which the US government has already done.

On a positive note, currently, China Telecom has become the first company globally to pass the IPv6 Enabled ISP certification. Latif highly evaluated China Telecom's IPv6 network commercialization plan, especially since the plan has received government support.

According to his introduction, currently, out of the global total of 220,000 ISPs, one-tenth are using IPv6 technology. It is estimated that China could apply for approximately 13 to 20 blocks of IPv6 addresses. (Zhang Wei)
