In our country, the concept of vitiligo dates back thousands of years. For example, the ancient silk manuscript "Fifty-Two Prescriptions" already described this condition, referring to it as "[white without creases]." The "Plain Questions - Treatise on Wind" states: "[Evil winds lurking between the skin, unable to communicate internally or dissipate externally], eventually leading to blood stasis, skin losing nourishment, turning white, and resulting in this disease." In 601 AD, Chao Yuanfang of the Sui Dynasty wrote in "Treatise on the Origins and Symptoms of Various Diseases -白癜候" about vitiligo: “Vitiligo refers to changes in the color of the skin on the face, neck, and body, differing from normal skin color, without itching or pain, called '白癜’.” The "Surgical Golden Scale" also records: "This condition affects the face and neck, where the flesh suddenly turns white, resembling spots, without itching or pain. If left untreated for a long time, it may spread throughout the body. It arises from wind evil attacking the skin, causing loss of qi and blood." The Song Dynasty's "Comprehensive Record of Holy Remedies" also mentions: "...mild cases only have white spots, severe cases result in the entire body becoming spotted within months... hair also changes color, remaining undifferentiated for years." These descriptions detail the characteristics of this disease.
