Qingdao Housekeeping, Shanghai World Expo Theme: Better City, Better Life!

by lxbj2009 on 2009-11-29 09:02:45

City, making life better. Better City, Better Life. A city is the crystallization of human civilization. The modern American philosopher Lewis Mumford once said: "A city is a special structure, this structure is dense and compact, specifically used to pass on the fruits of human civilization." The word "civilization" in many Western languages comes from the Latin word "Civitas" (meaning "city"), and this is no coincidence. The inclusiveness, comprehensiveness, and constantly updating characteristics of cities promote the perfection of human social order.

In 1800, only 2% of the world's population lived in cities, by 1950, this figure quickly climbed to 29%, and by 2000, about half of the world's population had moved into cities. According to predictions by the United Nations, by 2010, the world's urban population will account for 55% of the total population.

It cannot be denied that, in the rapid development of cities today, people's urban lives are increasingly facing a series of challenges: the high-density urban lifestyle inevitably triggers spatial conflicts, cultural friction, resource shortages, and environmental pollution. If left unchecked, the disorderly expansion of cities will exacerbate these problems, ultimately eroding the vitality of cities and affecting the quality of urban life.

The 1996 Istanbul Declaration issued by the United Nations Human Settlements Programme emphasized: "Our cities must become places where humans can live dignified, healthy, secure, happy, and hopeful full lives." The root cause of all the challenges faced by cities, whether it be overcrowding, pollution, crime, or conflict, lies in the disharmony between various relationships during the urbanization process—between humans and nature, between people, and between spirit and matter. Long-term disharmony will inevitably lead to a regression in the quality of urban living and even a regression in civilization.

For this reason, the 2010 Shanghai World Expo will respond to the call of "City, making life better" with the concept of a "Harmonious City."

The concept of "harmony" is hidden within China's ancient culture. Chinese culture advocates harmony among people, harmony between heaven and man, and harmony of body and mind. At the same time, "harmony" is also seen in the ideals of Western sages. For hundreds of years, discussions on the model of a "harmonious city" have never ceased. From "Utopia," to the 18th century's "ideal city," to the "garden city," a series of theories, propositions, and models have all been exploring how to establish balance and harmony in space, order, spiritual life, and material metabolism in cities.

Since the 1980s, with the increasing severity of environmental issues and developmental issues, the concept of sustainable development has emerged accordingly. The strategies proposed by urban governments around the world to implement the Agenda 21 mostly revolve around how to rebuild harmony between humans and cities, humans and nature, and ultimately achieve harmony between the present and future generations.

From this, we can see that the pursuit and practice of "harmonious life" and "harmonious cities" runs through the development history of human society, and is becoming increasingly evident in the blueprints people are drawing up for tomorrow's cities.

A "harmonious city" is mainly reflected in the harmonious coexistence of multiculturalism, harmonious economic development, harmonious living in the technological age, harmonious operations of community cells, and harmonious interaction between cities and rural areas.

Qingdao city is equally harmonious!
