Four years, so long

Online submission

The soft glow of the setting sun bathes the simple ancient town. The winding alleys, stone-built arch bridges, and ancient houses are all enveloped in a hazy twilight. Buildings that appear weathered have withstood centuries of wind and rain, exuding a sense of沧桑 (weathered charm) while also conveying a hint of tranquility. The structures of the town follow the natural contours of the land, nestled against green mountains on all sides, arranged in an irregular yet harmonious layout. They possess both the natural beauty of a mountain city and the unique charm of a water town. This place feels like a paradise beyond the mundane world, filled with a simple and pure atmosphere, leaving one reluctant to leave.

The sound of trickling water is drowned out by rising antiphonal songs. Walking on the textured bluestone pavement in flat sandals, listening closely to the faint sounds underfoot, brings a sense of genuine peace.

I've always loved ancient towns, especially their streets. In fact, I even grew fond of the rainy season because of these roads. Standing by the window, watching the curtain-like rain pour down and wash the road clean, makes it feel so pristine and comfortable.

Once, I yearned for a carefree life, pursuing a vivid and warm existence. However, who among us can live a life smooth until old age? To escape, I chose to retreat into this quiet realm, exiling myself for four long years.

From the initial haze of confusion to gradually becoming clear-headed, from struggling with every step to now feeling at ease, life slowly regained its colors. Time slips by quickly, and the days pass like water, temporarily making me forget troubles hard to let go. A simple life began to bring me back to my true self.

Four years, used to return to the original four years. The times of love, the times of hate. Everything, everything, finally fades away in time, unable to see each other, unable to see the original form. If I had once loved someone with all my might, and we eventually parted ways, my heart would ache unbearably. But still, I would stand there silently.

Farewells and goodbyes are just traces left by the past. Some people get used to hiding, while others truly no longer care. A peaceful life will eventually come to an end. One must strive to live and work hard to find their original dream.

Four years, so long, I no longer wish to waste four years on anyone. I am stingy with every second. Self-consoling, self-protecting, just not wanting to get hurt again. No matter how far I go, I'd rather be alone, embrace solitude, and perhaps if I meet someone, I might love with my heart, but I may also habitually guard myself.

Four years, this thread of emotion stretched so long. I hope when returning, everything returns to calmness, and everything is let go, living a good, striving life.
