13 Bizarre Things We Learned About Twitter's Jack Dorsey From His Big New Profile In The New Yorker

According to Business Insider, Twitter is preparing for its IPO. If successful, the company’s CEO, Jack Dorsey, will become a billionaire. The New Yorker recently published an article detailing Dorsey's fascinating life, revealing 13 lesser-known bizarre facts about this genius.

(1) His skin once turned orange.

Dorsey was a vegetarian and consumed too much beta-carotene, which turned his skin an incredible shade of orange. Now he follows the "Paleo diet," avoiding sugar and grains entirely.

(2) Takes public transportation to work every day.

A bus ticket costs two dollars, but Dorsey has a monthly pass, so it only costs him $1.75 per ride. He enjoys this routine.

(3) Never reads printed books.

When people give him books, he notes the titles and downloads the e-books onto his iPad to read, deleting them after finishing.

(4) Eats at the same restaurant every weekday.

From Monday to Thursday, Dorsey has fixed dining spots; on Fridays, he doesn't go anywhere.

(5) Not thrilled about becoming a billionaire.

Data from Forbes shows that Dorsey is the sixth youngest billionaire in America. However, seven years ago, he was just an indebted ordinary person. Dorsey has mixed feelings about money; he admits he likes money but dislikes the part of himself that likes money.

(6) Once had blue and orange braids.

In late 1999, Dorsey moved to San Francisco and founded a Web-based dispatch company. At the time, he sported a very unique hairstyle with blue and orange braids.

(7) Was once a terrible masseur.

Dorsey planned to open a massage parlor in San Francisco specifically for programmers, but when he returned to San Francisco in 2005, he found that "everyone was a masseur," so he abandoned the idea.

(8) Once wore a nose ring.

"Wearing a nose ring really hurts," Dorsey said, "It was definitely a mistake."

(9) Rewrote the online ticketing system for the Alcatraz ferry.

Dorsey discovered vulnerabilities in the Alcatraz ferry's online ticketing system that made it prone to fraud, so he helped write a more secure system.

(10) Hired two anarchists to work at Twitter.

Dorsey recalls that these two programmers often made incomprehensible moves during internal company meetings.

(11) "Stole" money from New York Mayor Mike Bloomberg.

Dorsey used Bloomberg's credit card to demonstrate Square. At the time, people hadn’t realized they could deduct money from accounts using this method. Of course, Dorsey later returned the money to Bloomberg using the same method.

(12) Enjoyed meals cooked by Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg.

For a period, Dorsey regularly met with Zuckerberg for dinner. When they were together, Zuckerberg usually did the cooking, and they would often exchange views on technology trends while eating.

(13) Wants to exit the tech world after getting everything done.

"I like fully experiencing life," Dorsey said, "In the future, I hope to become an artist if I get the chance."
