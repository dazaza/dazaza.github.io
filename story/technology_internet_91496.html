The 13 strongest unnamed soldiers in the Three Kingdoms~~~

by ufo on 2008-09-12 17:22:03

I. Yang Fu - After his seven younger brothers were all killed by Ma Chao, he was still able to fight to the death despite being hit by five spears when facing Ma Chao, Pang De, and Ma Dai, two super experts and one top expert. Incredibly, he survived to receive rewards in Xuchang, truly a god-like person!

II. Ma Zhong - He was able to capture Guan Yu alive and behead Huang Zhong, making all heroes tremble at his name!

III. Fan Jiang, Zhang Da (as a duo) - Zhang Fei, who could defeat ten thousand enemies and cut off the heads of generals as easily as picking something from his pocket, faced no retreat from Fan and Zhang. They could take Zhang Fei's head effortlessly. Zhang Fei couldn't even last one round against them. Fan and Zhang are truly miracles of the next world!

V. The three retainers of Xu Gong - These three righteous men sought revenge for their master. Even facing Sun Ce, the young overlord at the height of his fame, they dared to attempt assassination. Their loyalty rivals that of the Peach Garden Oath, their courage equals that of Yu Rang and Jing Ke. Ultimately, they perished together with Sun Ce. These three have great righteousness, so they are listed together. It is said that Japan has a shrine dedicated to them, and their achievements are celebrated by Japanese daimyos throughout history.

VI. Qin Qi - Guan Yu crossed through passes and slayed generals, killing Kong Xiu, Han Fu, Bian Xi, Wang Zhi, etc. When he reached Qin Qi, the young general showed remarkable bravery. Facing the bloodthirsty Guan Yu, he still angrily condemned his brutality and expressed condolences for his fallen colleagues. When Guan Yu presented Yan Liang and Wen Chou as proof of his accomplishments, General Qin Qi didn't flinch and said, "They're all nameless minor generals," then fought fiercely until his death. What a valiant man!

VII. Gongsun Zan - This gentleman is basically similar to Qin Qi. Facing the violent acts of Lu Bu, the world's greatest troublemaker, Fang Yue, Mu Shun, Wu Anguo, and others who went to enforce the law all ended up either dead or disabled. However, Mr. Gongsun bravely decided to arrest him regardless. Although he was a small figure who could be easily dealt with by the second-rate thug Wen Chou, he still put his life on the line to maintain social stability and unity, demonstrating the grand image of an ordinary public security officer.

VIII. Wei Xu - General Wei was a subordinate general under Lu Bu. During Cao Cao's battle with Yan Liang at Baima, Wei Xu's companion Song Xian was defeated within three rounds and beheaded by Yan Liang. Even Cao Cao exclaimed, "Truly a brave general!" Yet our General Wei showed no fear! He shouted, "Killed my companion, I will go to avenge him!" Although Wei Xu knew his martial arts were inferior to Song Xian's, for the sake of brotherly friendship, he went to avenge him, how tragic! Truly, the wind blows cold over the white horse, and the hero departs never to return. Thus, General Wei Xu only exchanged one round before being chopped down with a single blow to the head.

IX. Wang Lang - This old man, over seventy-six years old, still disregarded personal danger and engaged in verbal sparring with the iron-toothed and copper-tongued later generation Zhuge Liang on the battlefield. Though he knew Kong Ming could curse from Jiangsu to Sichuan, and from Sichuan to Gansu, Old Wang Lang remained full of spirit and boldness, declaring: "With just a few words, I'll make Zhuge Liang surrender willingly, and the Shu troops will retreat without a fight." Truly worthy of being the grandmaster of cursing! Despite his eventual demise.

X. Du Yuan - This gentleman was a mountain king who, along with Liao Hua, abducted Lady Gan and Lady Mi, wives of Liu Bei. Even knowing it was Guan Yu as bodyguard and Liu Bei's wife, Du Yuan did not retreat. Instead, he practiced benevolence and righteousness, willing to split the ladies with Liao Hua, truly shaming Wang Ying and angering Song Jiang! Alas, Liao Hua betrayed his classmate and killed King Du Yuan. But the image of Duke Du, who valued beauty over life, left a deep impression on me.

XI. Pei Yuanzhao - This brother started as a horse thief, determined to continue stealing horses until the end, aiming only for famous generals' steeds. The first time he failed to steal Guan Yu's Red Hare, the second time he targeted Zhao Yun. Actually, this person had average martial arts skills; even if given a good horse, he couldn't ride it. But he respected his duties and stayed loyal to his job, refusing to switch careers, truly embodying "love what you do" and setting an example for modern youth!

XII. Duyou - This unnamed hero, facing the legendary trio of Liu, Guan, and Zhang, still upheld principles, demanding every penny of bribe money, regardless of your status as an extraordinary person and imperial descendant. Truly a model and spiritual leader for future corrupt officials!

XIII. Zhou Shan (This is the one I admire most)

Sun Quan called him "the most courageous". Master Zhou dared to smuggle Lady Sun and A Dou right under the eyes of elites like Zhuge Liang, Guan Yu, Zhang Fei, and Zhao Yun, what magnificent spirit! When Zhao Yun jumped onto the ship and insulted Lady Sun, Zhou Shan endured humiliation and acted wisely, speeding the boat back to Dongwu with extreme force, almost capturing Zhao Yun alive. Unfortunately, Zhang Fei appeared, preventing this grand feat of history! In his final moments, facing the fierce Zhang Fei, Zhou Shan wielded his sword to meet him for one last fight, sacrificing gloriously.
