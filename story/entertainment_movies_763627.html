Synopsis:

For Pete Calvin (Paul Rudd), who has just turned 30, nothing could be more embarrassing than this current situation - a few nights ago, he proposed to his girlfriend Zooey (Rashida Jones), and she accepted. Normally, this would be quite a joyous occasion, but as Pete starts preparing for the wedding, he realizes that money isn’t an issue, time isn’t an issue, but everything else is - booking hotels, choosing dresses, picking honeymoon destinations, all these are problems. The biggest problem is that Pete can't find anyone suitable to be his best man! Yes, despite being successful in his real estate career and earning plenty of money, with numerous women flirting with him, Pete hasn’t had a male friend he could talk to in years. The reason is simple; Pete has always been too focused on relationships with women and neglected his male friendships, treating them lightly. Consequently, his close friends have drifted away from him.

Pete’s father recommends family members like his brother, but Zooey strongly disagrees because Pete's relationship with his own family isn’t that great either. Initially, Pete considers finding a random colleague to fill the role, but Zooey takes their wedding seriously. She tells Pete, "My maid of honor is my best friend, your best man should be your best friend too, otherwise it would be disrespectful to our wedding!" Seeing how serious Zooey is, Pete abandons the idea of just picking someone randomly and begins earnestly searching for a guy who could become his good friend, while also being well-mannered, sharing common interests, and having an outgoing personality – all these are Zooey's requirements because she believes that the character of one's friends reflects who they are as a person.

This poses a dilemma for Pete. The best man must not only be able to become his friend but also excel in various aspects without overshadowing Pete himself, since he is the main focus of the wedding. Pete meets many people, including even a homosexual, but none fit the bill as a satisfactory best man. Then he meets Sydney (Jason Segel), whom he finds to be a potentially good choice. They begin to see if they can become friends. Surprisingly, Sydney and Pete hit it off so well that they often spend time together talking and debating, inadvertently neglecting Zooey. Watching this behavior, Zooey gets angry and starts contemplating calling off the wedding. It's then that Pete realizes the gravity of the situation - he can't neglect both his friend and his fiancée. Together with Sydney, they start planning how to salvage the marriage…
