Alibaba Changes E-commerce Strategy: Telesales Switched to Channel Model

by itissing on 2006-08-09 13:06:10

Recently, Jianhang Jin, Vice President of the well-known electronic commerce website (B2B) Alibaba announced that Alibaba would change its "telephone direct sales model" to "distributor sales model" for the first time in its integrity pass business in four areas including Shanghai, Sichuan, Chongqing, and Hebei. In other words, Integrity Pass, the main product of Alibaba since 2002, suddenly took the initiative to transform while the market was continuously experiencing high-speed growth. This is also the first time in the history of Alibaba to change the product sales model. Although the "telephone direct sales" model remains unchanged in other regions, the strategy changes of Alibaba have shocked the industry.

Alibaba's original "telephone direct sales" model has been recognized by the market. According to the Survey Report on E-commerce of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences in 2005, Alibaba has occupied 87.2% of the domestic market share, which has pulled away from its competitors. This attempt to sacrifice profits for market share can not help but make people feel surprised. The person in charge of the main responsible company in the same industry revealed in an interview with reporters that once Alibaba’s this effort to popularize and improve e-commerce succeeds, the total amount of the entire market could increase by one or two times, or even five or six times. Even if the market share occupied by Alibaba remains unchanged at that time, its annual online transaction volume will exceed 40 billion yuan or 80 billion yuan very easily.

Alibaba stated in media interviews that the marketing adjustment in the four regions definitely would not involve price, and the annual membership fee for "Integrity Pass" would still be 2300 yuan.
