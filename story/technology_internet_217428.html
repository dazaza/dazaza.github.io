Solution to reduce computer noise

by cqe on 2009-08-03 17:33:37

Building your own silent PC is not a very difficult task. By making some choices on a few key components, you can easily achieve it. The noise from a computer generally comes from these areas: power supply fan, CPU fan, case fan, and graphics card fan noise; the noise from hard drives and optical drives spinning; and resonance sounds. The "buzzing" sound we hear when the computer is running is the result of all these noises combined. To effectively reduce computer noise, using just a single quiet power supply or a quiet CPU fan often doesn't yield good results. It's necessary to address each source of noise individually.

1. Eliminating Fan Noise

The noise from power supply fans and CPU fans are the main sources of noise. The issue with power supply fan noise is relatively easy to solve - simply buy a quiet power supply.

The method for reducing power supply fan noise usually involves lowering the fan speed. However, if the CPU fan speed is reduced, it will inevitably affect the CPU's cooling performance. Therefore, the methods for reducing CPU noise mainly involve lubrication of the bearings and the design of the fan blades. Due to the high speed of the CPU fan, achieving a noise level comparable to that of a quiet power supply is still somewhat challenging. Nonetheless, we should still try to choose fans with quiet functionality. Of course, manually adding some lubricant to the fan bearings is also a good way.

The noise produced by graphics card fans and case fans is relatively smaller, but installing too many case fans in the case can increase computer noise. Minimizing the installation of fans is an effective means of reducing noise. However, for computers that must have multiple case fans installed, you can choose larger diameter, low-speed case fans.

2. Reducing Hard Drive or Optical Drive Noise

Due to advancements in hard drive technology, the annoying "clicking" sound when reading/writing data has been mostly eliminated. However, the "buzzing" sound from certain hard drives while spinning is still noticeable. Currently, hard drive manufacturers have also launched some hard drives with a "silent" concept, such as Seagate hard drives with "liquid bearings" technology and Samsung V series hard drives, which are both good options.

The noise problem with optical drives is often difficult to solve. Some optical drives produce noise that is astonishingly loud. As some friends say, when reading optical discs, it "sounds like a helicopter." Some friends have installed software like DVD-ROM Utilities, which effectively reduces optical drive noise when using the drive, mainly during watching DVDs.

3. Eliminating Resonance Noise

Not every computer will produce resonance noise, and the method for eliminating resonance noise is actually quite simple - adjusting the placement of the case or adding some soft padding is enough. Adding sound-absorbing cotton inside the case is not a good method, as this often affects the case's cooling performance.

The above are just common methods. In fact, if everyone puts a bit more thought into it, the noise issue won't be much of a "problem" anymore.
