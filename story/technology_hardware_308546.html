Manchester United's frenzied attack struggled to break through Villa's iron bucket defense, and at this moment, Red Devils fans began to miss Cristiano Ronaldo.

Villa's last victory over Manchester United was on the opening day of the 1995-96 season. It was that match which led to Alan Hansen's famous remark: "You'll never win anything with kids." Sir Alex Ferguson later silenced Hansen with results. This latest defeat to Villa again calls for Ferguson to produce results to silence the media once more.

So far this season, in three top-four clashes, the Red Devils have scored zero goals in two matches (against Liverpool and Chelsea), while their game against Arsenal saw a Wayne Rooney penalty and a Diaby own goal. Manchester United has yet to score from open play against the top four this season.

The aftermath of Cristiano Ronaldo's departure is beginning to show. The Red Devils thrashed Wigan 5-0 earlier this season, beat Manchester City 4-3, crushed West Ham 4-0 last round, and also defeated the Bundesliga champions 3-1 in the midweek Champions League. However, it cannot be denied that these opponents often lacked tight defensive organization and an open formation. When faced with Villa’s strategy of setting up an impenetrable defense after scoring, the Red Devils began to miss a killer like Ronaldo who could turn non-opportunities into goals.

In this match, Manchester United had 19 shots on goal, but alas, Villa's defenders were tightly packed and frequently threw themselves in front of shots, leaving the Red Devils with only three on target. The best performer for United was Wayne Rooney, who has scored five times against Villa: In the 27th minute, he cleverly flicked Evra's pass to Valencia at the back post, but unfortunately, the Ecuadorian didn't arrive in time; Four minutes later, he executed a sublime first touch to control Evra's cross, bypassing Cuellar in the process, before smashing the ball onto the crossbar!

This was Manchester United's closest attempt of the match. As the lone striker, Rooney dropped too deep several times to take long-range shots, but he was off-target all night. It was evident how anxious the little fat one was, as he even tracked back to the defensive third to dispossess Heskey in the second half.

However, anxiety is precisely the fatal flaw when trying to break down an iron bucket defense. In his urgency, the little fat one resorted to unsportsmanlike conduct: In the 25th minute, he tried to dive inside the box to win a penalty but was caught out by the referee, earning himself a yellow card. Relying on his national treasure status, the little fat one won’t face the same media backlash as Eduardo of Arsenal did for diving, but his reputation has been tarnished nonetheless.

Giggs, showing no signs of aging, might have been preoccupied with thoughts of the BBC Sports Personality of the Year award to be presented on the evening of December 13th, contributing little beyond hitting the side netting just before halftime. Owen, who came on as a substitute, was almost invisible, while Berbatov, who underwent minor knee surgery just last Monday, was clearly not in form. In the 73rd minute, Carrick delivered a fine through ball to Berbatov, but his shot from the penalty spot completely missed its mark!

In the 80th minute, Rooney delivered a corner kick, and Vidic's header was somehow blocked by Downing on the edge of the area, costing United their final chance. Where was Macheda, who scored here last season? The Italian youngster didn't even make the bench! Faced with the crowd's cries for "attack," Sir Alex admitted afterward: "Their defenders ran themselves into the ground, always blocking players and using their bodies to stop shots, pulling out all the stops. Villa's defense was truly hardworking!"

After the match, a helpless Ferguson said: "We didn't create many chances in the first half. But in the second half, we bombarded them relentlessly, practically camping in their penalty area. Unfortunately, despite having quite a few good opportunities, we couldn't find the net. If we had taken one of them, the situation would have changed. But today, the ball just wouldn't go in."

To defend his disciple, Ferguson even descended into mysticism. In reality, the issue lies in whether a team aiming for an unprecedented quadruple can justify its failure to score simply because the opponent's defense was well-organized.
