Abnormal driving of a car requires special attention if there is an unusual sound. If delayed, trouble will follow. Any abnormal sound from the car indicates that some changes have occurred in its operation, and it's best to identify them. This article outlines some common sounds and basic solutions.

### Sounds from the engine compartment

1. **High-frequency Scream**: If a scream occurs when the engine is running or during cold start, it may be due to slipping drive belts. Check and adjust the belt tension appropriately. Also, check the edges of the V-belt to see if it has thinned out. A thin belt becomes slippery and cannot properly turn the pulley, requiring replacement in this case.

2. **Soft Metallic Impact Sound**: If your engine makes a sound as if something is colliding inside, also known as "backfiring". This happens when the air-fuel mixture in the combustion chamber fails to burn fully, often due to using low-grade gasoline, which can cause engine overheating. Check the coolant temperature to ensure the engine operates at normal temperatures. If not, switch to high-grade gasoline. If the sound persists, use additives to increase octane value. Another possibility for "backfiring" is excessive carbon buildup in the combustion chamber. In such cases, adding high-quality fuel system cleaners can remove dirt and extend engine life.

Severe "backfiring" can damage the combustion chamber and pistons. However, mild sounds during acceleration and climbing are usually not problematic. Persistent "backfiring" under normal gasoline usage should prompt a mechanic inspection.

3. **Small Tapping Sound or Tick**: If you hear rhythmic tapping or ticking sounds while accelerating, it could be due to improper valve clearance adjustment, insufficient engine oil, or an overdue oil filter change. First, check the oil level and add as necessary. If the noise persists, inspect and adjust the valve clearance to appropriate levels. These are cost-saving maintenance measures. If the noise continues, a comprehensive engine repair may be needed.

4. **Detonation Sound**: If you hear a sound like someone hitting the engine with a heavy hammer, it may indicate serious problems. Loud, deep knocking sounds often signal severe mechanical damage, such as damaged cylinder liners, bearings, or connecting rods. Do not drive the car further to avoid more severe damage.

5. **Hissing Sound**: If you hear a sound like air escaping through a narrow hole, it might be due to a broken vacuum hose or vacuum leak, causing the engine to lose power and efficiency. The challenge is identifying the leakage point. Carefully observe and listen for the source. If it's just a simple disconnection, reconnecting it to its original position should fix the issue. If the vacuum hose is torn, replace it with a new one. Another possibility is a leaking cylinder head gasket.

### Brake System Abnormal Sounds

1. **High-frequency Scream During Braking**: Sometimes new brake discs produce a screeching sound, especially with light braking. Although annoying, it's normal and part of the break-in process. Many modern brake discs are designed to reduce noise significantly.

2. **Scream Without Braking**: If you notice a screech while driving but it disappears when you press the brake pedal, it’s time to replace the brake pads. Most brake pads today include a layer of soft metal bent toward the rotor. When the friction material is nearly worn out, this metal layer contacts the rotor, producing a warning screech. Even if some friction material remains, consider replacing the pads soon.

3. **Metallic Friction Sound**: If you hear a harsh screech during braking, it means you should have replaced the brake pads and shoes long ago. All friction material is worn out, and the metal parts of the disc are now contacting the rotor or brake drum. Continuing under these conditions will require replacing the brake drums, increasing repair costs significantly.

### Other Abnormal Sounds

1. **Rhythmic Thud**: If you notice a dull thud when accelerating or decelerating that disappears when the car stops, it could be a tire problem. Check for damaged, bubbled, or flat-spotted rubber, possibly accompanied by vibrations. Carefully inspect and feel for issues; a torn tire exposes the steel band lining. If the problem is a flat spot (often caused by sudden braking), it’s not dangerous but very annoying. You can wait until the sound and vibration become unbearable before replacing the tire. If the defect is severe, replace the tire immediately.

2. **Loud Crack or Boom**: If the car emits gun-like sounds, it might be due to exhaust system backfiring or carburetor issues. This phenomenon results from delayed spark plug ignition, causing excessive accumulation of air-fuel mixtures in the combustion chamber. It can also occur in older cars when a valve opens during spark plug ignition. Regardless, your engine needs adjustments.

3. **Loud Roar or Boom**: If your regular car suddenly emits a roar or rhythmic bang like a sports car during acceleration, it’s time to inspect the exhaust tailpipe and muffler. While not a severe issue, it can be troublesome, drawing attention from neighbors and police.

4. **Roar or Groan**: If you hear a low, slow sound while turning, it indicates insufficient power steering fluid, possibly accompanied by difficult steering. First, check the reservoir fluid level and refill it. Next, identify any leaks. Lack of lubrication or incorrect lubricant in the front drive shaft can also cause this problem.

5. **Clicking Sound While Turning**: Common in front-wheel-drive or four-wheel-drive vehicles, the sound originates from the steering bearing. It affects driving and should be inspected at a service center.

6. **Dull Metallic Sound**: If you hear a dull metallic sound while shifting gears or releasing the throttle abruptly, it often indicates a transmission shaft gap. If the sound just started or is getting louder, consult a mechanic for inspection.
