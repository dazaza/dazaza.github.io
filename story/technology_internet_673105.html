Eight Textile Skills Selected - Guidelines for the Development of Key and Personalized Technologies in the Industry

by king2z9o on 2012-02-23 13:49:19

The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology announced on July 7th the "Guidelines for the Development of Key Generic Technologies in Industries (2011)," which lists eight key generic technologies related to the textile industry. These include the industrialization technology of cotton-like polyester fibers and their textiles, high-tech fiber technology, high-temperature resistant filtration material technology, batch production key technology of highly efficient ultrafine filtration nanofiber membranes, new bio-based fiber key technology, etc. These key generic technologies are of great and far-reaching significance for the textile industry to achieve sustainable development goals and establish itself as a strong textile nation.

### Indicating Future Directions for the Industry

Key generic technologies in an industry often predict its direction of development. For example, ultra-cotton-like technology has been highly regarded by domestic and international textile science and technology professionals in recent years and has become a focus of research and development efforts. This technology can make chemical fibers very similar to natural cotton, with some technical indicators even surpassing those of cotton. Simply put, [ultra-cotton-like] products possess the excellent characteristics of both cotton and synthetic fibers such as polyester, in terms of visual appearance, tactile sensation, and functionality (skin-friendliness, comfort, washability), achieving results that resemble or even exceed cotton. [Ultra-cotton-like] products not only ensure the excellent softness and warmth retention of cotton but also have good mechanical and chemical properties, and may even have functions such as anti-static, anti-pilling, antibacterial, flame retardant, far-infrared, and UV protection. As the [ultra-cotton-like] technology becomes increasingly perfected, the added value of the products will also increase.

Donghua University's Vice President of Research and Director of the Key Laboratory of High Performance Fibers and Products, Wang Huaping, believes that [ultra-cotton-like] technology has opened up new development space for the chemical fiber industry. He stated that China's cotton-like products still have significant gaps compared to foreign countries, especially Japan. The key technology system, product system, and standard system for the polymerization, spinning, weaving, dyeing, and finishing processes of [ultra-cotton-like] products have yet to be formed and need further strengthening. He suggests that the industry should strengthen research on the dynamic thermal and moisture comfort performance mechanisms of cotton-like fibers and their products, clarify product positioning and objectives, and conduct research on capillary wicking effects and kinetics, moisture absorption and transfer capabilities, and dynamics of cotton-like fine denier or ultra-fine denier fibers, hydrophilic copolymer modification, organic blending modification, surface roughening, cross-sectional shaping, and other fibers and fabrics. This research will provide a scientific basis for formulating testing methods and standards for cotton-like functions and regulating market behavior. It is understood that to alleviate the supply-demand contradiction of cotton and effectively utilize excess polyester capacity, relying on the strategic alliance for technological innovation in the chemical fiber industry, the Ministry of Science and Technology prioritized the launch of the national science and technology support plan project for the "Twelfth Five-Year Plan" - "Industrialization Technology Development of Ultra-Cotton-Like Synthetic Fibers and Their Textiles" at the end of 2010. This project involves more than 20 units, including the China Academy of Textile Science, Donghua University, Yizheng Chemical Fiber Co., Ltd., and Taicang Jinhuichemical Fiber Industry Co., Ltd., working together on its development.

### Achieving the Goal of a Strong Textile Nation

The soon-to-be-released "Textile Industry Development Plan for the Twelfth Five-Year Plan" outlines the development blueprint for China's textile industry over the next five to ten years. Among its priorities is breaking through major technological bottlenecks currently constraining industry development, conquering a series of key generic technologies, and enhancing core technology and independent innovation capabilities, which are clearly defined as the central tasks of the plan. To achieve the goal of becoming a strong textile nation by 2020, we must independently innovate high-performance fiber products and realize their large-scale industrial production. These key generic technologies represent the scientific and technological strength of a country. The development of science and technology cannot proceed without the participation of high-performance textile materials. Carbon fibers, para-aramid fibers, high-strength and high-modulus polyethylene fibers, etc., are among the advanced fiber technologies emphasized in the recently announced "Guidelines for the Development of Key Generic Technologies in Industries (2011)." Aerospace, large aircraft, high-speed rail, new energy, and other industries all rely on these high-performance composite fibers in the textile sector. After several years of effort, China's carbon fiber industry is forming an industrial scale. Technologies such as carbon fiber precursor, pre-oxidation, and carbonization have matured, and key equipment like pre-oxidation furnaces and large carbonization furnaces can now be produced domestically. Thousand-ton level carbon fiber equipment and small bundles of carbon fibers (1~6k) can already be mass-produced; the technology for high-strength and high-modulus series varieties such as T700 and T800 carbon fibers has been somewhat expanded; the thousand-ton level industrialization technology for para-aramid is continuously improving; meta-aramid has already achieved industrial production, with total output exceeding ten thousand tons; high-strength and high-modulus polyethylene fibers have increased in variety and production capacity, achieving industrialization.

Some domestic high-tech private enterprises have seized the opportunity of national industrial policy adjustments and ventured into the field of high-performance fibers. Some capable state-owned large companies have also increased their financial investment in science and technology, playing the role of the main force of the national team and advancing into the high-tech fiber field. It can be foreseen that the industrialization and investment scale of China's high-performance fibers will further expand, and a new growth level in the textile industry is taking shape. It is understood that to support the development of China's large aircraft C919, a domestic private enterprise has successfully developed a composite fiber product for the aircraft's rudder and has passed relevant department evaluations. All indicators meet design requirements and can satisfy the production needs of some key components of China's large aircraft.

### Development and Harmonious Coexistence

Resource shortages, population growth, and environmental changes require a country's economic development to balance global interests. Changing growth patterns, looking to the future, grasping the trends of world textile science and technology development, and making early arrangements for basic theoretical research and frontier technologies to lay a solid foundation for future industry development have become a consensus within the industry. The development of the textile industry has traditionally relied on resources such as cotton, mulberry hemp, and oil. However, limited resources have created bottlenecks for the industry's development, and the original state cannot continue. Therefore, the raw materials for textiles have expanded into other fields, seeking fibers from the ocean (algae fibers, chitosan fibers), and diversifying raw materials (bamboo fibers, coconut shell fibers, sugarcane fibers). Moreover, energy conservation, emission reduction, and green environmental protection are becoming key factors for the textile industry to achieve sustainable development. Thus, the key technology for batch production of highly efficient ultrafine filtration nanofiber membranes required for desalination, high-temperature resistant filtration material technology for supplying power plants with high-temperature resistant filtration materials, and new bio-based fiber key technology have come to the forefront. Tianjin Polytechnic University has been dedicated to researching various membrane materials, including hollow fiber membrane materials. From providing water purification equipment for disaster areas after the Wenchuan earthquake to reverse osmosis membrane technology for desalination; from urban sewage treatment to providing dual-membrane method desalination engineering equipment for Singapore, the university has made outstanding contributions to the development of the textile industry. High-temperature resistant filtration materials play a crucial role in filtering harmful particulate pollutants in coal-fired power plants. At 200 degrees Celsius, the fibers in dust collection bags must use high-temperature resistant materials such as polyphenylene sulfide, polyimide, and polytetrafluoroethylene. For a period, China could not produce them domestically and had to import them. Now this situation has greatly improved. Companies like Sichuan DeYang and Jiangsu Ruitai have achieved phased breakthroughs in the production of high-temperature filtration bag materials. Jiangsu Ruitai Technology Co., Ltd. has realized the industrial production of polyphenylene sulfide (PPS) short fibers, producing 10,000 tons of PPS short fibers annually and 3,000 tons of PPS filaments. Sichuan DeYang Special New Materials Co., Ltd.'s PPS resin, PPS modified materials, and other PPS series products have filled the gap in China's industrial production of PPS.

As key generic technologies in the textile industry continue to improve, the research and industrialization of many key technologies have become opportunities for the textile industry to cultivate and develop strategic emerging industries. Of course, due to the high difficulty and long cycle of key generic technology research, the development of industrial key generic technologies cannot be achieved overnight. However, as forward-looking and directional technologies in the textile industry, especially in basic materials, key processes, core components, and system integration, we must firmly grasp these key generic technologies because they represent the future of textiles.
