High costs push up steel prices, Kunming steel market enters high-risk period

by lljrkkgfew2 on 2012-02-08 10:20:32

High Costs Drive Up Steel Prices, Entering a High-Risk Period in Kunming's Steel Market – Steel Information - Industry News - Building Materials Network

As an information analyst for Kunming Iron and Steel Holding (Group) Co., Ltd. and a special researcher for the All-China Federation of Enterprises and the China Enterprise Association, Xue Heping has developed such a work habit: "Prepare a price analysis report on domestic and international iron ore markets every month, and write a summary of the international steel market every half month."

Currently, iron ore prices have risen to near historical highs.

Data shows that within a month, the spot price of 63% grade Indian ore increased by $35 per ton, with a rise exceeding 30%. In the spot market, taking 63.5% grade Indian fines as an example, it rose from 1030 yuan/ton on March 1st to 1350 yuan/ton on April 19th, showing a trend towards the psychological high of $200/ton. Similarly, iron ores from Brazil and Australia have also reached high levels.

With the rise in international iron ore prices, steel market prices have started to fluctuate upward, www.ieoi.net, and under the push of rising costs, steel prices seem to have found a reason to increase.

However, the current steel market is truly hard to "understand." In this murky situation that no one can comprehend, steel prices are fluctuating up and down, dealers' actions vary, and people’s views on the future market remain "unclear."

Frequent Price Adjustments Increase Profit Margins for Steel Mills

"Domestic steel prices have been continuously rising since early March. According to statistics, steel prices across the country have generally increased by about 800 yuan/ton in just over a month, with a rise reaching 20-25%," said Wang Chensheng, vice president of Yunnan Jinhai Shen Trading Co., Ltd., a first-tier agent for DeSteel.

Some say that steel mills, pressured by rising costs, must transfer their cost pressures downstream by increasing steel ex-factory prices. "In addition to the cost pressure of iron ore, coal, electricity, oil, and transportation have all risen comprehensively, resulting in a year-on-year increase of 15-20% in steel production costs," Xue Heping told the reporter.

This point has been confirmed, especially in the recent wave of steel mill "price hikes" over the past month, which undoubtedly explains everything. The overheated momentum of spot ore has already been transmitted through steel mills to the steel market. The frequency of price adjustments by major domestic steel mills has changed from once a month to once every ten days. Faced with the continuous rise in iron ore prices, steel mills in various regions have accelerated their price adjustment steps, and steel enterprises in Yunnan have not fallen behind.

From March 26th to March 31st, Kunming Iron and Steel adjusted the execution price of building steel in Kunming for six consecutive days, with adjustment ranges varying between 30 to 50 yuan, resulting in a general increase of more than 200 yuan for various specifications of wire rods, second and third-grade rebar, and coiled rebar in just six days. After another round of price adjustments from April 6th to April 10th, there was a general increase of 150 yuan per ton across all product lines. In the most recent price adjustment on April 14th, the price of building steel was raised by 30 yuan per ton. Thus, in just 20 days, the price of building steel from DeSteel generally increased by more than 400 yuan per ton.

Similarly "crazy" is DeSteel: From March 26th to March 31st, DeSteel made six consecutive price adjustments to the execution price of building steel in Kunming, with adjustment ranges varying between 30 to 50 yuan, resulting in a general increase of more than 200 yuan for various specifications of wire rods, second and third-grade rebar, and coiled rebar in just six days. After another round of price adjustments from April 7th to April 10th, there was a general increase of nearly 200 yuan per ton across all product lines. In the most recent price adjustment on April 13th, the price of building steel was raised by 50 yuan per ton. Thus, in less than 20 days, the price of building steel from DeSteel generally increased by nearly 500 yuan per ton.

Wang Chensheng calculated for the reporter: According to the highest spot price of iron ore on the day (April 19th) at $190 per ton, plus various production costs, and adding a profit margin of 150 yuan per ton under normal circumstances, the current price of wire rods should be around 4570 to 4580 yuan per ton. If calculated based on the Q2 iron ore contract price accepted by Nanjing Iron and Steel Group and other steel mills (basically equivalent to an 86% increase over last year's long-term contract price), this figure should be around 4000 yuan. However, the current prices of wire rods and rebar have already reached around 4700 yuan, indicating that steel mills undoubtedly have a significant profit margin.

The data calculated by Wang Chensheng is roughly similar. Zhong Huajian, Vice General Manager of the Marketing Center of Kunming Guanglin Steel Pipe Trading Co., Ltd., introduced to the reporter that it generally requires 1.6 to 2 tons of iron ore and about 1 ton of coke to produce one ton of steel, plus other costs, making the current cost of each ton of steel around 4400 yuan. Therefore, steel mills definitely do not want to lower prices.

According to reports, the sales profit margin of the domestic steel industry from January to February this year was 3%, which has undoubtedly increased since March.

Is There Demand at High Prices?

However, in the spot market, high steel prices have not ignited the enthusiasm of dealers.

"You can see, the entire market is relatively quiet. The market is full of parked vehicles, so shipping should not be too good," Zhu Hong, business manager of the Kunming office of Shuicheng Iron and Steel (Group) Company, told the reporter. Steel prices have been rising rapidly, and everyone says buy when prices rise, but no one is buying.

On the afternoon of April 19th, the reporter visited the Yunnan Steel Market located between the second and third ring roads in the eastern part of Kunming City. This is a market integrating steel receipt and dispatch, transshipment warehousing, trading, processing, and distribution. Various heavy trucks were parked everywhere, and many drivers were napping in their vehicles. "I haven't shipped anything today, and I'm very bored," one driver told the reporter.

"This job is exhausting and stressful," Zhu Hong sighed. "We don't know whether it's due to price factors or demand factors, but the market isn't doing well, and we really can't understand it."

Subsequently, the reporter went to the HaoHong Steel Trading Market and TieGongJi Steel Logistics Port, three kilometers away. During the steel consumption peak season, every afternoon, the road from the provincial steel market to here would be crowded with fully loaded steel trucks, sometimes even causing traffic jams. However, on that day, the reporter did not see such a bustling scene, and the number of passing vehicles was slightly sparse.

"The high purchase volume in March has already exhausted this month's demand, and the demand in April is far worse than in March, with more construction sites starting in March," Wang Chensheng told the reporter. However, overall, it's still much better than before March 10th. Before that, many dealers were forced to sell at a loss due to financial pressure, but now, with the rapid and large price increases by steel mills, dealers have finally caught their breath.

Data shows that demand is not that bad. According to Wang Chensheng, national market inventory has decreased somewhat. Compared to before March 10th, the inventory of wire rods and rebar has decreased by 1 million tons, but it is still over 200,000 tons higher than last year; "Kunming's social inventory (excluding steel mills) has dropped from 580,000 tons to around 430,000 tons currently, but this is still quite high," he said.

Zhong Huajian, Vice General Manager of the Marketing Center of Kunming Guanglin Steel Pipe Trading Co., Ltd., stated that the industry generally uses the Shanghai market as a benchmark. Currently, inventories in the Shanghai market are decreasing, and inventories in 14 major and medium-sized cities are also declining.

Mixed Trends in the Spot Market

"Many dealers who have accumulated resources have a 'cash out' mentality and are beginning to unload goods; however, some continue to accumulate stocks, either to meet the task volume set by steel mills or because they remain optimistic about the future market," Zhong Huajian told the reporter.

"However, once prices fluctuate, intermediaries will sell steel at low prices, exacerbating fluctuations in steel prices," one dealer said. It is no longer the best time to stockpile steel, and risks associated with stockpiling steel are gradually accumulating. Previously, steel dealers were concerned about repeating the sharp rise and fall in steel prices at the end of last year, and some dealers are now selling off their goods systematically.

"Now all the risks have shifted to the dealers' hands. If we buy, we dare not buy too much, fearing that we won't be able to sell or that the market will suddenly change; if we don't buy, steel prices are still rising," the dealer said.

In other words, some dealers' mindset has shifted from stockpiling for anticipated price increases to realizing profits and reducing risks. For market prices, downward pressure will become increasingly evident. Some analyses suggest that this week is the last chance for steel mills to raise ex-factory prices, as these prices are about to reach the 5000 yuan/ton mark. Given the continued high-speed release of domestic capacity and the lack of completion of market de-stocking, it will be difficult for market prices to follow up to the high of 5000 yuan/ton. It is expected that this round of price adjustments by steel mills will cause a shift in market sentiment.

The sustained rise in steel prices has lasted for over a month. Intensified supply-demand conflicts and operations by some merchants to cash in on profits may increase short-term downward pressure on the market.

"Today (April 19th), Kunming's market quotations have not changed, but some dealers in the market are raising prices while others are lowering them. On the 16th, prices were rising nationwide, but today, Shanghai is falling while Beijing is rising. We really don't understand," Zhu Hong was puzzled.

The only consensus among all participants in the steel market seems to be "I don't understand." Some market insiders vividly described it: there exists a large ambiguous zone between domestic steel demand and inventory, easily leading to distortions in supply and demand relationships. "Demand is not strong, capacity is too large, the steel market may have entered a high-risk period, a difficult period," Zhu Hong said.

The Possibility of a Sharp Rise in the Future Market is Small

Since March, the main reason for the rise in steel prices has been "cost-driven", but the industry believes that the hype of cost increases and the successive rises by steel mills have persisted for several days, and their boosting effect on spot steel prices has significantly diminished compared to before.

Given the excessive preemption of rising cost expectations and the performance of terminal demand, there hasn't been a surge-like concentrated procurement behavior. Terminal user demand has remained stable after entering 2010, and this real demand cannot support a rapid rise in steel prices.

Regarding the future market, a staff member of Kunming Haichuang Economic and Trade Company in the HaoHong Steel Market told the reporter a seemingly paradoxical statement: "We definitely look forward to the future market, but feel that it won't rise much further."

According to the monitoring data from XiBen New Line, last Thursday, the overall price of grade-three rebar in the Shanghai area fell by 10 yuan/ton, then fell by 70 yuan/ton on Friday, and continued to decline on Monday, with different product drops ranging from 10-30 yuan/ton, marking a rare continuous correction in the past two months.

A report released by the China Iron and Steel Association on the 19th indicated that due to factors such as the continued rapid growth of steel production, the maintenance of high social inventory levels, and the still low total volume of steel exports, the contradiction of oversupply in the domestic market has not significantly improved. The possibility of a sharp rise in market prices in the later period is small, and the overall trend will be characterized by fluctuations.

The report pointed out that the prices of raw materials required for steel production are constantly rising, placing companies under enormous cost pressure. Especially the substantial increase in international market prices of iron ore, coking coal, scrap steel, and freight rates will inevitably lead to a significant rise in steel production costs. Although raising steel prices can offset part of the cost pressure, considering the bearing capacity of downstream industries, steel prices will not rise indefinitely. The situation faced by the steel industry is extremely severe.

At this time, the judgment of major distributors often carries more representativeness. Kunming Guanglin Steel Pipe Trading Co., Ltd. has annual sales reaching 7-8 billion yuan, making it the largest steel trading enterprise in Yunnan. Vice President of the Marketing Center, Zhong Huajian, made a judgment: "Although the short-term national regulation policy on the real estate market has some impact on steel supply, the situation of high-cost support in the steel market will not change, and we still look forward to demand. With the advancement of infrastructure construction such as roads and bridges in Yunnan and the acceleration of water conservancy projects, the demand for steel will also increase. I believe steel prices will still rise, and now the port iron ore inventory is very small. The spot market price may be even higher."
