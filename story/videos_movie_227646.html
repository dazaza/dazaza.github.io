Plot Introduction:

"The Silver Empire" depicts the Shanxi bank "Tianchengyuan", which can be considered the "financial giant" of the Qing Dynasty. It controlled the national finances and also operated in deposits and loans. During its peak, it had twenty-three branches across China, as well as in Russia, Mongolia, Japan, and Southeast Asia, with wealth rivaling that of a nation. The main storyline of this film is about the succession between two generations of the "Tianchengyuan" business empire. In addition to the entangled triangular romance involving a father, son, and extraordinary woman, it also uses this process of succession to portray the ideological conflict between Legalism and Confucianism in Chinese thought. Through background displays of historical events such as rampant kidnappers, the Eight-Nation Alliance, and the Xinhai Revolution, the film highlights the spirit of righteousness valued by Jin merchants - the importance of merchants upholding justice.
