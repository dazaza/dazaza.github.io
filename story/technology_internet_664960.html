According to interbank sources, yesterday in the open market primary dealers only declared 28-day and 91-day positive repo needs to the central bank. Central bank bills did not appear on the first futures forum. The central bank had consulted twice last week about the central bank bill needs, but ultimately it was just a consultation. Industry experts analyzed that the central bank's heavy reliance on positive repos and continued suspension of central bank bills might indicate that a February cut in the RRR has become a low-probability event.

Last Tuesday and Thursday, the central bank respectively resumed 28-day and 91-day positive repo operations, withdrawing 46 billion yuan. Thus, the open market realized a net withdrawal of 4.4 billion yuan last week. Meanwhile, last week, the one-year central bank bills were suspended for the fifth consecutive week, and the three-month bills were absent for the seventh consecutive week.

Different from the inquiry price of one-year central bank bills last week, yesterday the central bank only inquired about positive repos. A trader from a state-owned commercial bank analyzed that in the coming period, the central bank might replace the issuance of central bank bills with positive repo operations to achieve liquidity control, while reducing market expectations for a reduction in the reserve requirement ratio through this change.

He further analyzed that the cost of recovering liquidity by issuing central bank bills is significantly higher than the reserve requirement ratio, which would undoubtedly increase the cost of the central bank's liquidity control. Through short-term positive repos, both liquidity adjustments can be achieved and the pressure of interest payments reduced, and at the same time, expectations of monetary policy in the market can be stabilized. From the current perspective, positive repos "taking the lead" and central bank bills continuing to "go missing" will be the optimal channel for the central bank to transmit monetary policy. Yesterday, the Shanghai Interbank Offered Rate (Shibor) basically maintained a stable trend. The overnight to 7-day repo rates fluctuated by only about 1 basis point. Among them, the overnight rate remained stable at 2.6% to 2.7% for six consecutive trading days, indicating that the current short-term funding situation has basically stabilized. At the same time, the 7-day and 14-day rates were respectively at 3.65% and 3.85%, also retreating to relatively low levels earlier in the period. Yesterday, the fixed repo rates of various types operated stably without significant fluctuations.

Analysts pointed out that compared to central bank bills, positive repos can both increase the amount of funds due in the next few months at a lower cost and leave flexible space for future monetary policy adjustments. Considering that the current funding situation is moderately loose, the central bank is concerned that the demand for one-year central bank bills may lower the issuance yield and that the fund freezing period is relatively long, thereby strengthening the market's expectation of policy easing. For stability considerations, the central bank continues to suspend the issuance of central bank bills.

According to convention, on Wednesday, the central bank will conduct a demand declaration for 3-month central bank bills. Then, whether the central bank bills will resume issuance this week will be determined.

The amount of central bank bills due in the open market in February is only 12 billion yuan, and there are no maturing positive repos. The scale of central bank bills and positive repos maturing is sharply down more than 90% month-on-month compared to January's 126 billion yuan.
