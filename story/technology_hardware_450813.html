Bedroom Decoration Design Principles

I. General principles of bedroom design

In the design of bedrooms, the pursuit is for a perfect unity of function and form, with an elegant, unique, simple and brisk design style. In terms of bedroom design aesthetics, designers should strive for fashion without being frivolous, dignified and elegant while not lacking a sense of ease and romance.

By utilizing diverse material applications, organic integration of geometric shapes, full expression of line rhythm and melody, and three-dimensional application of lighting shapes, etc., create a warm, soft and romantic bedroom space.

The bedhead background wall is the highlight in bedroom design. The design uses more elements such as points, lines, and surfaces to follow the basic principles of formal beauty, making the shape harmonious, unified, and varied. Curtains and drapes often exude the most tender emotions. The gentle sway, the shy movement, and the elegant color matching are like a song-like board, romantic and warm.

Lighting in the bedroom is the finishing touch; spotlights resemble starlight spots. Multi-angle settings make the three-dimensional shape of the straight-angled lighting even richer and more colorful.

Bedrooms should be designed according to the age, personality, and preferences of the residents.

Bedroom floors are preferably made of materials such as wooden floors, carpets, or ceramic tiles.

The walls of the bedroom are preferably decorated with wallpapers, wall fabrics, or latex paints, and the colors and patterns should be chosen based on the age and personal preferences of the residents.

The ceiling decoration of the bedroom is preferably done with latex paint, wallpaper (fabric), or partial suspended ceilings.

Artificial lighting should consider both overall and local lighting, and the lighting in the bedroom should be soft.

The bedroom should have good ventilation, and if the original building has poor ventilation, it should be appropriately improved. The air conditioner outlet in the bedroom should not be placed directly facing where people stay for long periods.

II. How to design the master bedroom:

To design a good master bedroom, six aspects need to be considered.

(1) The floor of the bedroom should have warmth properties, generally using neutral or warm tones, with materials such as wooden floors or carpets.

(2) About one-third of the wall area is covered by furniture, and human vision focuses mainly on the indoor furniture except for the space above the bedhead. Therefore, the wall decoration should be simpler. The main space above the bedhead can be designed with some personalized decorative items, selecting materials that match the overall tone to enhance the atmosphere of the bedroom.

(3) The shape and color of the ceiling are one of the key points in bedroom decoration design, generally preferring a simple, elegant, and warm color series.

(4) Colors should ideally be unified, harmonious, and elegant. Careful consideration should be given to the combination of local primary colors, and stable tones are more popular. For example, the green series are lively and energetic, the pink series are cheerful and graceful, the blue series are cool and romantic, the gray or tea color series are transparent and elegant, and the yellow series are passionate and full of a warm atmosphere.

(5) The lighting in the bedroom should take a warm and cozy yellow tone as its base. Spotlights or wall lights can be embedded above the bedhead, or spotlights can be embedded in the decorative cabinet to make the room more romantically comfortable and warm.

(6) The bedroom should not be too large, with a space area of about 15-20 square meters usually sufficient. Essential furniture includes a bed, bedside table, wardrobe, low cabinet (TV cabinet), and dressing table. If there is a bathroom in the bedroom, the dressing area can be arranged inside the bathroom. The curtains in the bedroom are generally designed with one layer of gauze and one curtain to make the indoor environment more atmospheric.

III. How to design the secondary bedroom:

Secondary bedrooms are typically used as children's rooms, youth rooms, elderly rooms, or guest rooms. Different users have different design requirements for the functionality of the bedroom.

A child's room usually consists of sleeping, storage, and entertainment areas. For school-age children, a study area should also be designed.

The floor of a child's room generally uses wooden floors or wear-resistant composite floors, or soft carpets can also be laid; the walls are best designed with soft packaging to avoid bumps, and children's wallpapers or wall fabrics can be used to reflect childlike fun; the furniture should be designed with rounded corners as much as possible, and fire-resistant boards with bright colors can be selected for furniture materials. If space is limited, multifunctional combined furniture can be designed; the sleeping area of a child's room can be designed in a Japanese style, with tatami mats plus spring mattresses, which are both safe and comfortable.

Youth rooms, in addition to the above functional areas, also need to consider the makeup area. If there is no study room, the design of the secondary bedroom should include desks and computer desks to form a study area. Youth rooms should reflect a quiet scholarly atmosphere.

Rooms are typically divided into three main functional areas: sleeping, storage, and playing. For children under eight years old, the play area is an indispensable part of life, so toys and games must be inspiring, allowing them to learn through play.

Therefore, the materials used for walls and floors must be durable and easy to clean.

Floor design is another focus, and carpet is an excellent choice. Children always love to roll on the ground, and insufficiently soft floors can easily damage their skin and bones. Elastic rubber flooring is also commonly used in children's rooms.

In the design of children's rooms, the play area is indispensable, with three main principles: anti-collision wall corners.

First, the materials and equipment in the children's room must be durable.

The play area needs a lot of storage space to place toys.

Besides, noise is also an issue to consider in the design.

No matter how beautiful the room design is or how novel the equipment is, safety measures in the room must never be overlooked. First, fire prevention measures must be sufficient. Moreover, electrical facilities in the room should be installed at higher positions.

Elderly rooms mainly satisfy sleep and storage functions, and the design of elderly rooms should be practical.

IV. Art of Bedroom Lighting

1. General lighting in the bedroom

To meet the requirements of functional lighting, two methods are adopted: one is to install lamps with dimmers or computer switches; the other is to install multiple lamps indoors, controlled separately, and determine the range of lights to turn on based on needs. General lighting in the bedroom often uses flush-mounted lights or recessed lights. Ordinary rooms can also choose fluorescent lamps.

2. Local lighting in the bedroom

One is for reading at the bedside, and the other is for makeup lighting.

V. Bedroom Walls

There are many materials available in the market for bedroom wall decoration, including interior wall coatings, PVC wallpapers, and glass fiber wallpapers, etc. When choosing, you should first consider whether they coordinate with the room tone and furniture.

The tone of the bedroom should primarily focus on tranquility and harmony. For larger bedrooms, there is a wider range of choices for wall decoration materials; for smaller bedrooms, small flowers, warm tones, and light patterns are more suitable.

When choosing decorative materials for the bedroom walls, the colors of the materials should preferably be light and elegant. Too strong colors generally fail to achieve satisfactory decorative effects and should be noted when selecting.

VI. Bedroom Arrangement

The principle of arranging the master bedroom is how to maximize comfort and privacy. Therefore, the arrangement and materials of the master bedroom should emphasize freshness, soundproofing, softness, and flexibility.

The biggest difference between children's rooms and master bedrooms lies in maintaining a certain degree of flexibility in design. It is enough to roughly define the rest area, reading area, and clothing storage area for children's rooms. Attracting children with indoor colors is the key to designing children's rooms. Children's rooms get dirty easily, so materials that can be cleaned and replaced should be used during decoration. Waterproof paint and plastic boards are the most suitable materials for decorating children's rooms, while high-grade wallpapers and thin wood panels are not recommended.

Guest rooms and maid's rooms should be simple and generous, with complete living conditions, i.e., beds, wardrobes, and small display stands, but all should be miniaturized, with simple shapes and fresh colors.
