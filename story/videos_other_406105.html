Is the romance buried in the tomb of marriage?

by lenka219 on 2011-04-06 14:21:58

Young men and women in the heat of romance always have endless sweet nothings to whisper, and inexhaustible honeyed words to诉.

Although lovers in ancient times were not as diligent in writing love letters as before, the time and money spent on long phone calls are incredibly remarkable. The words used throughout history, both domestically and abroad, to describe love always seem exceptionally fitting for every young couple in the throes of passion. However, after marriage, couples appear to have exhausted all their sweet talk during their courtship, and daily life conversation becomes very brief, like "Hey, is dinner ready yet?" or "Go buy a pack of salt quickly!" or even "Why are you so annoying?"

Research has found that many people believe that once they become husband and wife, they are all part of the same family; he loves me, I love her, it's only natural, so why bother repeating it endlessly? As husband and wife, he should do his part, earn money to support the family; she should also fulfill her duties, assist her husband and raise the children, there's no need for hypocritical politeness between the two... This is the stance some married couples take towards emotional communication. Under this mindset, Chinese men and women who are married often reverse the intimacy and excitement of their courtship period, and post-marriage expressions of affection between spouses often appear shy and awkward, sometimes even reaching a state where there's almost nothing to say. Such couples indeed suffer from what might be called "Love Silence Syndrome."

The main symptoms of Love Silence Syndrome include:

△ Rarely saying anything particularly sweet to one's partner;

△ Never apologizing to one's partner;

△ Never jointly discussing issues related to their sex life;

△ Rarely considering what items one's partner might need;

△ Often feeling that chatting with one's partner is a waste of time;

△ Preferring to do things alone without consulting one's spouse;

△ Thinking that deliberately trying to please the other person is vulgar;

△ Being unclear about how one's partner feels about them;

△ When one's partner does something they're proud of, showing indifference, thinking it's not a big deal, and not worth celebrating;

△ When encountering conflicts or problems, the couple often sulks in silence;

△ Thinking that admitting fault in front of one's partner is embarrassing;

△ Having many grievances but being afraid to express them for fear of hurting the marital relationship;

△ Not knowing in which aspects one's partner is dissatisfied with them;

△ Rarely sitting down post-marriage to exchange feelings;

△ When one's partner is upset, often ignoring it;

△ Having many things one doesn't want to tell one's partner;

△ When expressing ideas in front of one's partner, the partner often seems distracted;

△ Sometimes when the two are together, feeling bored;

△ Rarely trying to understand why one's partner is often in a bad mood.

There are many causes leading to Love Silence Syndrome. Of course, if the couple no longer loves each other, or if one party has an affair, then Love Silence Syndrome is merely a symptom accompanying these issues.

So, how can Love Silence Syndrome be treated?

1. Break incorrect notions. After marriage, life indeed becomes more realistic, but only by continuously nurturing a love similar to that before marriage will ordinary life become interesting, allowing one to savor happiness amidst the mundanity of life. Otherwise, focusing solely on daily trivialities will gradually lead to boredom, thereby validating the notion that marriage is the grave of love.

2. Learn to jointly create a new life. Arrange more entertainment activities within family life and opportunities for emotional exchanges because this is not only necessary for strengthening and developing the marital relationship but also serves as an adjustment to busy and stressful work, enabling one to break free from tense work and continue working and studying with high spirits and full strength.

3. Don't always worry about one's dignity. Between couples, one shouldn't joke about who initiated first or who lost face, etc. Because taking the initiative and being enthusiastic is itself a form of respect and dependence towards one's partner, and if the partner mocks such actions, isn't that showing ignorance of good intentions?

4. Enhance the harmony of sexual life. Harmonious sexual life is a kind of adhesive that strengthens the bond between couples. If there are obstacles in sexual life, one must seek specialized scientific guidance. Otherwise, several decades of one's life will be spent in pain.

5. Correctly understand what it means to be a real man. A true man should be someone who understands both righteousness and subtleties; someone who has emotions and desires but knows how to express them appropriately. Men lacking warmth and being cold-hearted are actually psychologically unhealthy.
