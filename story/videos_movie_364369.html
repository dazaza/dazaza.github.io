Title: Fist of Fury

Director: Johnny To

Cast: Donnie Yen, Shu Qi, Huang Bo, Huo Siyan

Genre: Action, Drama

Region: Hong Kong

Language: Cantonese

Release Date: July 14, 2010

Plot Summary:

The epic action film "Fist of Fury" co-invested by光线影业 and Media Asia Films started shooting in Shanghai on November 15th. The film is confirmed to be produced by Andrew Lau Wai-keung and directed by Johnny To. In addition to the main cast of Donnie Yen and Shu Qi, Huang Bo, who has become a top box office actor through his "Crazy" series, is also expected to undergo a significant transformation in "Fist of Fury", moving away from his usual small character roles.

"Fist of Fury" gathers three top box office directors in a new "Iron Triangle" combination

On the day of the event, Media Asia Films CEO Cheung Chun stated: "Chen Kaige, Johnny To, and Donnie Yen, who have all created box office hits before, will form a new generation 'Iron Triangle'. Together, they are expected to open a new chapter in Chinese action films." As the supervising producer, Andrew Lau gained recognition from mainland audiences last year with "Painted Skin", which also made him the fifth Chinese director to join the ranks of "200 million directors" after Zhang Yimou, Feng Xiaogang, Peter Chan, and Wu Yusen. Another top box office director, Johnny To, once pioneered a new height for police-criminal genre films with "Infernal Affairs". The three-part "Infernal Affairs" series alone broke the 100 million yuan mark in Hong Kong, injecting new vitality into the sluggish Hong Kong film market. His 2009 film "Romance in the Rain" further solidified Johnny To's status as a top box office director. Additionally, Donnie Yen serves as both the lead actor and action director. Previously, Donnie Yen served as the lead actor and action choreographer in films like " SPL: Sha Po Lang", "Dragon Tiger Gate", and "Flash Point", showcasing his unique martial arts style known as "Donnie Yen Kung Fu" and earning him three Best Action Choreography awards at the Hong Kong Film Awards.

With strengths in visual storytelling and action special effects, Johnny To, skilled in narrative and emotional shaping, Andrew Lau, and Donnie Yen’s powerful and swift "Donnie Yen Kung Fu" form a brand-new iron triangle. This combination is highly anticipated and makes "Fist of Fury" poised to become a pinnacle work in Chinese action cinema.

Three top box office actors make a perfect match, aiming for a 300 million yuan box office hit

In addition to having three top box office directors—Andrew Lau, Johnny To, and Donnie Yen—"Fist of Fury" also features three top box office actors. Shu Qi, through her performances in "If You Are the One" and "Romance in the Rain", has generated a box office revenue of 500 million yuan, gaining unanimous praise for her acting skills; Donnie Yen, through "Painted Skin" and "Ip Man", has achieved nearly 400 million yuan in box office revenue, with his "Donnie Yen Kung Fu" creating a strong visual impact on audiences. In recent years, Huang Bo has gained immense popularity in mainland China through films like "Crazy Racer", quickly becoming a top box office star. The box office appeal of these three actors has been affirmed by both the market and audiences. Now, as they come together in "Fist of Fury", audiences can't help but look forward to whether they will spark new chemistry among themselves, leading to endless speculation about the box office potential. Zhang Zhao, President of光线影业, expressed: "The investment scale of "Fist of Fury" reaches 100 million yuan, and we have three top box office directors and three top box office actors. Therefore, this film will undoubtedly be an unprecedented action blockbuster, and we have confidence in challenging a 300 million yuan box office target."

Donnie Yen, Shu Qi, Zhou Yang - Superhero Love Triangle Leads to Complicated Relationships

Action superstar Donnie Yen revealed at the press conference that he plays a superhero in the film who saves the nation from peril. As the saying goes, even heroes find it hard to resist beauties, and naturally, there are beauties accompanying our superhero Donnie Yen. It is reported that Shu Qi will break away from her previous roles and undergo a major transformation, engaging in a "special" romance with Donnie Yen. Furthermore, Zhou Yang, who played a female spy in the CCTV hit espionage drama "Special War", has become a popular new idol. She will engage in a duel with Shu Qi in the film, both willing to go through fire and water for the superhero portrayed by Donnie Yen. When asked by the host how the two beauties "compete for love", Shu Qi playfully smiled and said: "It wouldn't be interesting if I told you now." On the side, Zhou Yang added mysteriously: "My role is very challenging." Their playful banter adds a sense of continuous love and tangled relationships.

All Main Actors Undergo Transformations, Dual Identities Add Mystery

Whether it's Donnie Yen's superhero or Shu Qi and Zhou Yang's "beauty duel", all the big-name actors mentioned the concept of "transformation" at the press conference, making their identities in the film elusive. Two heavyweight actors, Huang Bo and Tony Leung Chiu-wai, also did not lag behind, fully embracing the "transformation" concept in the film.

Huang Bo, known for playing small characters such as "Blackie" in "Crazy Stone", "Racer" in "Crazy Racer", and "Niu Er" in "Ash Is Purest White", rose to fame on the big screen through a series of small-character roles. In "Fist of Fury", Huang Bo continues to play a small character, but according to Huang Bo himself: "Although I still play a small character, it's a significant role, undergoing a major transformation in 'Fist of Fury', which is quite surprising."
