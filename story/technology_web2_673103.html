iPad trademark dispute heats up domain name registration, Lin Shuhao has been pre-emptively registered

by xwkj0983 on 2012-02-23 13:48:31

The dispute over the iPad trademark between Apple and Shenzhen Proview is escalating. Although the focus is on the trademark registration dispute, the issues reflected go beyond just the fight for trademark registration. After investigation, it was learned that with the rapid development of e-commerce, cybersquatting on website domains has also become a trend, and even a new way to make money. If this chaos is not curbed, it may become another kind of "trademark dispute."

1. The cost of domain name registration is not high.

When querying trademarks on China's Trademark Network and entering the term "iPad," 47 results are displayed. Among these, Proview Technology (Shenzhen) Co., Ltd. has 2 entries, while Apple Inc. has 16 entries covering nine categories including computer products, sports goods, and leather. In addition, other companies have also registered the iPad trademark. Records show that Proview applied for the iPad trademark on January 10, 2000, with exclusive rights lasting until June 20, 2021.

On the afternoon of January 16, Yang Rongshan, chairman of Proview Technology, stated that Proview started developing personal computer products similar to tablet computers in 1998. Later, they launched a computer named iPad, which was once sold in countries and regions such as Europe and South America but was later discontinued. However, the trademark has been retained. Meanwhile, according to the trademark network data, Apple also registered and filed the iPad trademark, first using the trademark preferentially in July 2009, but the transfer was only completed in July 2011. Some legal experts expressed that due to the continuous emergence of electronic products in recent years, it has triggered a rush to register domestic trademarks.

In fact, apart from trademarks, the internet domain names are also being registered aggressively. According to the latest statistics from the international statistical agency "webhosting.info," by January this year, the total number of ".com" domain registrations exceeded 100 million, with China ranking second globally in terms of registrations. When checking domain name registration on China's Wanwang (a domain registrar), it was found that English domain names charge from 88 yuan per year to 500 yuan, while Chinese domain names charge between 280 yuan/year to 480 yuan/year.

Among the 17 English domain names, there are familiar ones like ".com" and ".cn," the two most popular domains in China, charging 139 yuan/year and 99 yuan/year respectively. The highest fee is for the ".tv" media domain, which will be the mainstream domain in the broadband era, costing 500 yuan per year.

"The cost of domain name registration is not high," relevant responsible persons introduced. The renewal fee for the second year is the same as the first year, or one can buy packages for three or five years, making the price cheaper.
