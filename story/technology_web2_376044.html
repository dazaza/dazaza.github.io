Sweet Potato: Activity/Event-based "check-in" service

This article was simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media, please do not reprint! Sweet Taro is a platform that allows you to conveniently share what you're doing and find like-minded friends. Users can easily share the TV shows they're watching, the music they're listening to, the events they're attending, the books they're reading, their thoughts, the games they're playing, etc. Of course, through such a site, you may discover many people who are doing the same things as you, and you can communicate with them conveniently. The "Check-in" action by Foursquare will have profound impacts on future Internet/mobile Internet services. This "Check-in" action essentially connects information with geographical locations, thereby reorganizing information according to location. Many current Internet services have adopted this "Check-in" model and applied it to other services. I previously referred to services like Sweet Taro as activity/event-based "check-in" (check-in) services, which essentially categorize user activities (behaviors) via check-ins and form user communities based on that.

Currently, Sweet Taro provides an iOS client application suitable for iPhone, iPod touch, and iPad. Download the app here: [Link](http://tianyutou.com/)

Similar services:

1. **Hot Potato**: An online service that provides various activity/event "check-ins" (check-in). Users can check in with content such as the TV show they're watching, the music they're listening to, the conference they're attending, the book they're reading, what they're thinking about, the game they're playing, or any other activity they're engaged in. Of course, through such a site, you may discover many people who are doing the same things as you, and you can communicate with them conveniently. There were once rumors that Facebook was going to acquire Hot Potato, and combining such a service with Facebook could indeed be a good match. [Via]

- Link: [http://hotpotato.com/](http://hotpotato.com/)

2. **Miso**: An online platform about what TV shows/movies you're watching. Users can view currently airing TV shows/movies, then check in, see which other users are using similar TV shows/movies, and check which ones are the most watched by everyone. Miso currently collaborates with the video website Revision 3, allowing users to "check in" while watching videos on the Revision 3 website, telling others what TV shows/movies they're watching.

- Link: [http://gomiso.com/](http://gomiso.com/)

3. **PlayPhilo**: Its primary mode encourages users to share TV programs via check-ins, earn points, and receive rewards. PlayPhilo's operational model is simple; just like Foursquare users checking in at various locations, PlayPhilo involves users checking in when they watch a certain TV program, such as a drama, reality show, concert, etc., via smartphones like iPhone, Android, or web platforms, sharing what TV programs they're currently watching and their thoughts. PlayPhilo aims to help users discover a new way of searching and sharing TV programs via check-ins, integrating mobile phones, PC internet, and television in another form. To encourage users, PlayPhilo also adopts Foursquare's point-earning and mayorship incentive methods, offering tangible rewards such as viewing paid programs and obtaining DVDs of certain TV series.

- Link: [http://www.playphilo.com/](http://www.playphilo.com/)

4. **GetGlue**: A personalized content recommendation system where users can check in with the books they're reading, the movies they're watching, etc. As users rate movies, books, games, and music, related recommendations will continuously appear. Through this check-in, users will earn different bookmarks.

- Link: [http://getglue.com/](http://getglue.com/)

**Watsonxu's Words**: Facebook acquired Hot Potato in August this year, and combining it with Facebook's Places service would be a great combination. Services like Hot Potato, through the active check-in method by users, can effectively classify users—those who like reading, those who like gaming—and combining these segmented user groups with Facebook's advertising platform would be very meaningful. Currently, Sweet Taro draws inspiration from Hot Potato both on its website and mobile client, but I hope that as a service targeting domestic users, it can better integrate with local services and provide better service to domestic users. In China, combining with platforms like Douban might be quite good.
