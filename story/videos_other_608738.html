Culture and Media: New media content regulation may become the next theme

Investment highlights:

Company dynamics: Dadi Media borrows shell *ST Xin'an for listing; Guangbo Information's major reorganization is approved by CSRC; Huace Film & Television's equity incentive plan draft revision is approved by CSRC; Fangzhi Technology changes the sponsor representative of its initial public offering.

Cultural industry: The State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television implements eight measures to implement the spirit of the Sixth Plenary Session, emphasizing "letting network audio-visual new media become an important platform for strengthening mainstream public opinion"; the opening ceremony of the national-level network audio-visual industry base was held in Shanghai.

Film and television: The State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television issued a guidance document suggesting that cinema box office revenue sharing should not exceed half.

Network new media: Tudou.com invests in establishing a new company, increasing investment in animation - Guangzhou Lan Biological; European court rules that ISPs cannot be forced to filter content; Swiss government considers downloading music and movies legal; YouTube redesign emphasizes social elements, with daily mobile browsing volume exceeding 400 million.

Industry views: Regarding the revenue-sharing dispute caused by Zhang Yimou's new film Guangzhou Hualan Biotechnology Co., Ltd., the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television issued a guidance document suggesting that cinema box office revenue sharing should not exceed half. In recent years, the proportion of revenue-sharing for Chinese film producers has been slowly rising due to ongoing negotiations, fundamentally reflecting the enhanced bargaining power brought about by improved production quality. We believe that as information dissemination channels continue to expand, the monopolistic position of traditional distribution channels such as cinemas will gradually decrease, and high-quality film content producers will enjoy more substantial returns.

The high price of TV drama copyrights has made self-produced dramas one of the new means for video websites to attract users and advertisers. Extending the industrial chain effectively avoids excessive copyright purchase competition while differentiated content can form a unique style, enhancing user stickiness and advertising value. As the self-production capabilities of video websites improve, they will increasingly pose a threat to the core competitiveness of traditional content production media.

The State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television has successively introduced restrictive policies targeting TV series advertisements and entertainment program content. Online video new media, due to their internet attributes, currently enjoys a more relaxed regulatory environment and may benefit in the short term. From the measures implemented by the State Administration of Radio, Film, and Television to implement the spirit of the Sixth Plenary Session, it emphasizes "letting network audio-visual new media become an important platform for strengthening mainstream public opinion." Regulatory tightening of new media channels is likely to be the next theme.
