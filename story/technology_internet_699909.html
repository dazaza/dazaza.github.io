Hollow drill: also known as core drill or hole opener, can be divided into high-speed steel hollow drill and tungsten carbide steel hollow drill.

Features of high-speed steel hollow drill: This type of drill is sharp, can be repaired, and is cheap, but not durable. Features of tungsten carbide steel hollow drill: Made by welding hard alloy tool heads, compared to high-speed steel drills, it is more expensive, cannot be repaired, has a higher number of holes drilled, and has a high cost-performance ratio. Currently, 95% of customers in the market use this type of drill head.

Hollow drill handle types: There are three types - right-angle handle (two planes), universal handle (one plane three points), and four-hole handle (four points).

For domestically produced magnetic base drills that need to use hollow drill bits, a Morse conversion sleeve must be purchased. Currently, most magnetic base drills produced in China use right-angle handles (locked with two planes).

Imported magnetic force drills can mostly use both right-angle handle drill bits and universal handle drill bits, such as British Mag, Dutch FE, German Ou Ba, German Bai De, etc. Japanese Ri Dong magnetic base drills are relatively special and can only use universal handle drill bits because their magnetic base drills are designed for quick installation, and right-angle handle drill bits cannot be used.

The four-hole handle drill bit is relatively special and less circulated in the market because only Pan-yin magnetic base drills use it.

Hollow drill specifications: 12MM to 130MM; The main materials of the drill bit include high-speed steel, powder metallurgy, and tungsten carbide.
