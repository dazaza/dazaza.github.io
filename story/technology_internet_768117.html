The Happy Life of Romanian Programmers

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 18:18:54

Why I'll never leave Romania as a software developer

I have been working as a +Perl programmer for three years, and I have been programming professionally for seven years. I live in Cluj-Napoca, which is the second largest city in Romania.

Four years ago, I started pondering over a question: how much does my standard of living as a programmer differ from that of programmers in other countries? At that time, my after-tax income was approximately 700 euros per month (about 5830 RMB, with 1 euro = 8.331 RMB). Given Romania's economic level at the time, the average salary across society was less than 325.91 euros per month (2715 RMB), so my income was nearly double the national average. This made me feel like I would never want to leave here. However, this was just a psychological feeling; despite earning more than others, I was actually struggling financially every month. This financial difficulty stemmed from my poor income management, and the underlying reason was that my wife had just become a lawyer and her current income was almost zero.

The income situation for programmers in Cluj-Napoca

Despite the fluctuating market, the basic income for an experienced programmer in Cluj-Napoca is generally around this level (all figures mentioned are after-tax net incomes): initially 400 euros per month (3330 RMB), with a raise of 100 euros (833 RMB) every six months.

This number may vary depending on individual circumstances, potentially better or worse.

In better scenarios, a programmer’s salary can experience a significant increase through the following methods:

- By directly requesting a raise

- By switching jobs

- By leaving the company and then being rehired

- By negotiating a raise based on exceptional performance

In worse scenarios, if any of the following situations occur, a programmer’s salary will grow very slowly:

- Poor company performance

- Ineffective negotiation skills

- A boss who doesn’t care about employees

- Poor personal performance

Therefore, it’s not surprising when you find a programmer with five years of experience earning a monthly after-tax salary of 1500 euros (12,500 RMB), while another with seven years of experience earns only 1700 euros (14,000 RMB).

Comparison of income and cost of living in several major cities

Recently, I discovered the Numbeo website, which collects information on consumption and income levels in major cities around the world. Moreover, it allows you to compare the price levels, salaries, and purchasing power between two cities, helping you estimate the income needed to live comfortably in another city. Based on the data provided, I realized that with my current income in Cluj-Napoca, it would be impossible to live well in any other city in the world. (All amounts in the table are in euros.)

Meanings of these numbers

Reference Income – This is the income level required in that city to achieve the same quality of life I currently enjoy in Cluj-Napoca. Assuming my current income in Cluj-Napoca is 1500 euros per month, the figures shown in the table above are all after-tax net incomes.

Income 1 – This is the net income required in that city to achieve the same quality of life I currently have in Cluj-Napoca, where my monthly income is 1700 euros. I listed the 1700-euro level because it closely matches my current income, which is the average for a programmer with seven years of experience.

Rent Price – The cost of renting a three-bedroom apartment outside the city center.

Income 2 – This is the sum of the figures in the Income 1 column and the Rent Price column.

Annual Net Income – This is the figure in the Income 2 column multiplied by 12 months.

Need for more data

If we could add a column to the table showing the average income for programmers in each city, it would provide even clearer insights. Unfortunately, most websites I’ve reviewed only provide pre-tax annual incomes. Since tax rates vary significantly from place to place, it’s impossible to calculate after-tax incomes. Therefore, if you are a programmer living in one of the cities mentioned above and know the local average net income, please share it in the comments section, and I will update the article accordingly.

Life as a programmer in Cluj-Napoca

Due to Romania's relatively low purchasing power, people's daily consumption levels are also relatively low. Check out the price level information for Cluj-Napoca on the Numbeo website.

With 1700 euros per month, you can lead any lifestyle you desire — especially if you enjoy socializing. Your life might look something like this:

- Three meals a day (3 meals * 12 euros * 30 days = 360 euros/month)

- Weekend outings to the most expensive clubs (one night * 30 euros * 8 nights = 240 euros/month)

- Drinking the most expensive beer in bars during weekdays, assuming maximum consumption (5 beers * 1.5 euros/beer * 22 days = 165 euros/month)

- Taking only taxis every day (5 euros/day * 30 days = 150 euros/month)

- Rent and utilities (370 euros)

- Total: 1285 euros/month

If you live more healthily, your expenses will be lower.

Cluj-Napoca is a university town, and its population increases by 33% during the school year (with 100,000 students here). There are cultural events, concerts, club activities, or large-screen projection events almost every day. Being surrounded by so many people makes me feel younger (I’m not old, I’m 31).

I live on one end of the city, and my company is in the city center. It takes me only 10 minutes to drive there, which is very convenient.

Conclusion

Besides money, I consider many other factors, such as entrepreneurial opportunities. This is why I chose to compare the aforementioned cities: they are the most active in terms of entrepreneurship and IT development. If the idea of starting a business ever takes over me, these cities will be my targets. But for now, I still prefer to stay here and be an active member of the local entrepreneurial community in Cluj-Napoca.
