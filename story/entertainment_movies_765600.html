Synopsis:

Eleven-year-old Jesse Aarons (Josh Hutcherson) has always dreamed of being the fastest runner in his school. Just as he wins the track championship for his grade, a new girl named Leslie Burke (AnnaSophia Robb) suddenly appears and becomes his strong competitor on the track. The transfer student Leslie, who seems faster than anyone else, is quite mysterious. Gradually, classmates who get to know her tell others that there's no TV in Leslie's home, and the entire house is filled with all kinds of books. Enthusiastic and sociable Jesse quickly befriends Leslie, who is generous and talkative. One day, Leslie tells Jesse about a kingdom called Terabithia deep in the forest. There are no bad people, no teachers constantly making you study, and no annoying classmates... Everything is wonderful. Even everything can be decided by themselves, doing whatever they want.

Naturally, Jesse, who loves movement and freedom, yearns for this mysterious kingdom. So, Leslie asks him to close his eyes and "imagine" entering the mysterious Kingdom of Terabithia together. They then wander happily in this fantasy kingdom they have imagined. From then on, Jesse and Leslie often fantasize about Terabithia together, considering themselves the king and queen. Despite the numerous complexities and challenges in the real world, they would meet in this beautiful fantasy world when tired. However, this place beautiful like heaven also has terrible enemies. They must lead the kind little fairies to fight against the evil dark forces...

Main Cast Introduction:

The two young protagonists in the film, who bravely battle the dark forces in the mysterious forest of the mysterious kingdom, are both accomplished actors. Josh Hutcherson and AnnaSophia Robb are the big names in this movie. Although young, their acting skills are remarkable, and they have both worked with Hollywood superstars. AnnaSophia Robb, who plays Leslie, is the mean gum-chewing girl in "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory." Her co-star, Josh Hutcherson, has already challenged the limits of imagination in "Jumanji II," showing an unexpected maturity. Two adult supporting characters are also familiar faces — Zoe Saldana from "Winter Passing" with Ed Harris becomes a loving art teacher in this film. And the famous liquid robot T-1000, Robert Patrick, no longer quarrels with Arnold but instead plays the boy's caring father, showcasing his versatile acting abilities.

Fun Facts:

This film has a curious connection to "The Chronicles of Narnia." Both are jointly produced by Disney and Walden Media, with visual effects handled by Weta Workshop, and the exterior scenes were shot in New Zealand. The favorite book of the female lead in this film is "The Chronicles of Narnia," so she named the mysterious jungle "Terabithia" — taken from a small island in "The Chronicles of Narnia: The Voyage of the Dawn Treader."

Behind the Scenes:

Under the influence of the "Harry Potter" series' "magical wonders," films adapted from children's literature increasingly show a strength disproportionate to their target audience's age. In 2005, "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory" earned nearly five billion dollars worldwide, while "The Chronicles of Narnia" triumphed over the mighty "King Kong." "Bridge to Terabithia" also adapts to the trend, becoming another typical example of this genre. The film is adapted from the eponymous novel by Chinese-born author Katherine Paterson, which won the Newbery Award (the most authoritative children's literature award in the world). The characters in the book are full of imagination: walking tree spirits, sea otters wearing hats, flying little monsters, and lightning-fast iron knights. To bring these elements to the big screen, a director who only knows how to tell stories clearly isn't enough. Gabor Csupo, born in Hungary and having worked as an animator for many years, served as the animation supervisor for "The Simpsons" and is a technical expert. This film is also his feature film debut. David Peterson, the son of the original author, assisted him in perfecting the script and also served as a producer.

Although Disney did not prominently schedule the release of the film during the hot summer season, it was cleverly inserted after the New Year season. It is believed that with its fantastical and interesting story content and high production standards, the film will also make its mark in the box office competition.
