Part One: Function Comparison

Common Software Function Comparison

GENESIS CAM350 or other commonly used CAM software

This is a functional comparison between the GENESIS software and ordinary software like CAM350 during the data preparation process. Although it may not be entirely accurate, this does not mean that GENESIS software is all-powerful while other CAM software is useless. However, just as V2001 software was popular a few years ago, now it's almost impossible to find anyone using it, and those who still use it will have difficulty finding work. Currently, in the PCB engineering industry, only a few popular software are widely used, including GENESIS, CAM350, Genesis2000 video, GC-CAM, and UCAM. Regardless of whether it's large factories (which mostly used GENESIS a few years ago) or small factories, they have largely switched to Genesis2000. CAM350 is popular in MI due to its excellent printing function and simple user interface, but it's mainly used for creating simple data due to its limited functionality. GC-CAM is also widely used by many people, with many functions being stronger than CAM350, but due to its version and functionality being somewhat redundant, it's neither here nor there. UCAM and PARCAM software have powerful functions, but due to their lack of automation and complexity, they are only used by a small number of people.

Currently, the popularity and powerful features of GENESIS software are generally recognized. The common belief is that using GENESIS software to create multi-layer boards and complex motherboards is very good, but for some simple boards, CAM350 or other software might be faster and more convenient, which is true. However, when comparing the basic versions of 350 and GENESIS, this is due to the lack of automation in GENESIS. If GENESIS is equipped with fully automatic steps, modified ERF files, and default software system parameters, it can basically achieve fully automated operations for simple boards, with speed surpassing or at least matching other software. Different people have different opinions; this is the result of my years of engineering experience and discussions with industry friends. However, we believe in trends. Over time, CAM software with insufficient functionality will be abandoned, and most users will likely choose to upgrade to GENESIS, which offers more powerful functions!
