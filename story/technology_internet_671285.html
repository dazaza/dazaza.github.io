Product Introduction

Great Wall Wine is a famous brand under COFCO Group, one of the world's top 500 enterprises. It is China’s first wine brand, recognized as a "China Famous Brand Product" and an "Industry Landmark Brand." For many consecutive years, it has ranked first in production and sales nationwide. In 2004, the "Great Wall" trademark was officially recognized by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce as a China Famous Trademark. In 2006, Great Wall Wine became the official wine product for the Beijing 2008 Olympic Games. The same year, with a brand value of 12.587 billion yuan, it topped the "Top 500 Chinese Brands 2006" list in its industry. In 2009, Great Wall Wine was designated as the exclusive wine for the 2010 Shanghai World Expo.

Product Features

The Great Wall Huaxia 95 Cabernet Sauvignon Dry Red Wine is made from Cabernet Sauvignon grapes cultivated in one of China's most renowned grape-growing regions. Following international O.I.V standards, the wine exhibits a deep ruby red color, with a smooth, rounded, full-bodied balance and a delicate aroma of vanilla. After aging in French oak barrels and further maturation in bottles for several years, this dry red wine is a premium collectible.

Wine Review

The wine displays a deep ruby red hue, with a harmonious, round, and balanced flavor profile, complemented by a delicate fragrance of vanilla.

Winery Introduction

Great Wall Wine owns three prestigious wineries located in China’s best grape-growing regions: Hebei Shacheng, Hebei Changli, and Shandong Yantai. Its renowned product lines, including Great Wall Sanggan Winery Series, Huaxia Small Terroir Series, Star-rated Dry Red Series, and Coastal Wine Series, have won top awards at numerous international professional wine tasting events in Paris, Brussels, London, and more. These products are exported to over 20 countries and regions, including France, offering consumers a rich and diverse wine experience with its unique style and taste. Great Wall Wine is the only Chinese wine brand to be included on the "Top Luxury Brands of the 21st Century" list compiled by the authoritative American Gallup organization.

Sanggan Winery, established in 1979 along the Sanggan River in Hebei Shacheng, features a unique 75-hectare soil vineyard filled with ancient fossil deposits. Located at the same latitude (North Latitude 40 degrees) as Bordeaux, France, it lies within the world's golden belt for wine grape cultivation. Benefiting from its microclimate, the superior quality of the 30-year-old vines, and adherence to traditional winemaking techniques, Great Wall Sanggan Winery wines exhibit a typical robust river valley style: layered, varied, clear structure, and concentrated flavors. The "Gesha City Vineyards" mentioned in *The Travels of Marco Polo* refers to today’s location of Great Wall Sanggan Winery. With its 2 million-year-old sedimentary fossil soil, unique microclimate, and 30-year-old prime grapevines, countless advantageous conditions make this place destined for producing top-tier wines. Over 30 years of refinement, Great Wall Sanggan Winery has become a Chinese benchmark among global wineries, standing shoulder-to-shoulder with world-renowned wineries like Lafite and Margaux.
