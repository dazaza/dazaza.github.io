Inter Milan's goal-scorer chasing 5 legends over 12 years - his efficiency rivals Ronaldo and surpasses Vieri

According to Sina Sports, Inter Milan drew 1-1 with Atalanta away, and it was Milito who scored again. If you randomly choose one game from the 14 league games Milito has participated in this season, there will be a 64.3% probability of seeing him score.

64.3% may not seem very high, but so far this season, only Milito has scored in 9 matches across the entire Serie A. In the blue stadium of Atalanta, Milito only got one shot opportunity in 77 minutes. It is precisely this 100% efficiency that makes 64.3% even more precious.

That goal came in the 15th minute, an opportunity that seemed like just a chance. Sneijder sent a through pass from 25 meters away from the goal, and when Milito received the ball in the penalty area, he was immediately surrounded by three defenders. First, Bellini tightly followed behind him, Milito accelerated to break free, simultaneously bypassing Bianco's sliding tackle. Near the small penalty area, he used his very proficient skill of controlling the ball, turning around, and flicking it back, shaking off Bellini who had been closely following him. With another fluent move, while flicking the ball, he also bypassed Manfredini who was coming to cover, and finally completed the shot with his right foot.

"Who is the best forward in Serie A? I really admire Milito, and also Pato." Not only does Cagliari goalkeeper Marchetti think this way, but at the beginning of December, "Gazzetta dello Sport" made a horizontal comparison of Inter Milan forwards, concluding that Milito is on par with Ronaldo and better than Vieri, Adriano, Ibrahimovic, and Eto'o.

In Ronaldo's first season at Inter Milan in 1997, he scored 9 goals in the first 14 rounds, the same as Milito. Among other forwards, Vieri and Adriano scored 7 goals, Eto'o 6 goals, and Ibrahimovic only 5 goals. Of course, such comparisons are still hard to fully explain Milito's true value to the current Inter Milan. As mentioned earlier, against Atalanta, he only had one shot. His 10 goals in the first 16 league games were achieved with only 33 shots. Dino Baggio, who scored 11 goals, took 54 shots, and Eto'o scored 7 goals with 47 shots.

In the match against Kazan, Mourinho began to use a new formation of 4-2-3-1, with Milito as the center-forward leading upfront, Balotelli and Eto'o flanking on both wings. This was actually a test for him. Compared to the past where Eto'o pulled wide to create space for him, Milito would now start to provide more support for his teammates. In terms of data, Eto'o had 5 shots in 90 minutes, while Balotelli and Sneijder, who were substituted off and sent off respectively, each had 2 shots. Milito’s opportunities were compressed, but he still performed well.

If we say that Milito's current ability is on par with Vieri and Ibrahimovic, it would be quite a bold and risky assertion. Vieri single-handedly supported the entire front line of Inter Milan, and from 1998 to 2002, he was always the core player in the Italian national team's attack; Ibrahimovic won 5 league championships in 5 years in Serie A and claimed one golden boot. In terms of influence and achievements, Milito lags behind these two. However, the ability to score 10 goals in 14 league games cannot be ignored. When evaluating a star, due to nostalgia, the past often seems better than the present, and no one dares to easily say that Milito has the same dominance as Vieri, even though the facts might indeed be so now. (Giotto)
