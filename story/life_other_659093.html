The Apricot Flowers Have Bloomed

One

This seat was originally not Luoduo Ming's. He was so tall, and he wasn't even nearsighted. The hardest part to bear was every time after school, he would shout with all his might: "Bie Xiaoyuan... lend me your notes." The word 'yuan' was dragged out longer than the Great Wall. Of course, I didn't easily lend my notes to boys, except for him. Because he knew my secret, just like how his words were always filled with threats when he spoke to me.

I walked home with my schoolbag, everything in my world was so quiet, perhaps only accompanied by the sunset. Occasionally, couples on bikes would pass by, their backs radiating happiness. The leaves on the trees seemed to dance with them.

"Screech-screech" before I could react, a bike stopped in front of me, and the person on it was Luoduo Ming. "Bie Xiaoyuan, let me take you home! So you don't have to enjoy loneliness alone on the road!" His words were clearly full of mockery and rudeness, of course, only I knew this. "Thank you, no need, I'm afraid your beloved bike will fall apart." This sentence seemed to anger him. "Bie Xiaoyuan, I'm afraid you won't dare. I know you're afraid..." He didn't finish his sentence, naturally, this was at the cost of the tears in my eyes. He turned around and rode away.

I walked home alone with some scattered steps, but my heart still couldn't calm down, maybe because of him. Luo was already standing on the balcony next door, looking down at me. I looked up at him, suddenly feeling the distance between us was so far. I had no time to care about these things, I ran to the balcony and watered the seeds in the flower pot that wouldn't sprout. "Hey, Bie Xiao Chi, the sunset today is beautiful!" He enthusiastically shouted across from me. I looked up and saw his unique challenging gaze, as if it was going to melt me into thin air. I no longer paid attention to him, turned around and went inside. The loneliness of the sunset spread through the window into my bedroom. If what Einstein said was true, I wish so much that the particles of my thoughts could travel back along the light beam to the past, seven years ago, that sunny afternoon.

Two

That year I was nine years old, you were one year and three months older than me. That day was very hot, just standing in the courtyard without moving would make you sweat all over. I ran out of the yard to your house. When I got to your place, it felt like it was raining, but the heat only made me beg you to buy me an ice cream. "Big brother, it's so hot, let's go buy some ice cream!" You didn't answer me immediately, I saw something bright shining in your eyes. "Yuan Yuan, I'm leaving, going to a very faraway place." You blinked while pulling my hand and said. My mind went blank instantly, as if I heard thunder. "Will you come back later?" I asked, sobbing. "Yes," you firmly told me, then pulled me under the apricot tree and picked an apricot pit from the ground. You handed it to me and said, "Yuan Yuan, this is our apricot pit, plant it, when the apricots ripen, I'll come back and pick them for you to eat." I wiped away my tears with my hand, tightly holding the apricot pit, as if clutching all my hopes.

In the end, you still turned and left. Blue raindrops fell from the horizon, I don't remember what I cried into, I only know my world was blurry, the only clear thing was your back and the faint yellow bloodstain on your uniform. All the fragments of memory flew through my brain, shattered here, I couldn't pick them up and piece them together into your image...

I only remember that you were handsome as a child, but a bit clumsy. When I was three years old, you held my hand and walked in the yard. I walked very nimbly, but you swayed like a drunk man. But you gradually became stronger, because you were a boy, back then you liked watching martial arts movies. You said boys must protect girls, so you would always be around me. In the scorching summer, you fanned me despite the sweat dripping off your head, you ran to the street corner to buy me ice cream. There was an apricot tree at your house, when the apricots were slightly bigger but not yet ripe, you climbed the tree to pick them for me to eat. I saw the sweat on your face mixed with mud, it looked like a Peking Opera mask, so I laughed. You also stood there holding the apricot and smiled foolishly...

When I was five years old, you were sent to school, I also cried and begged to go to school, but I was too young, they wouldn't let me go. As a result, you lost interest in school and skipped classes every day to play with me at home. Finally, uncle had no choice but to let you start school a year later with me. Initially, we didn't sit together, I sat with a boy. One day, you saw him bullying me, you came over and beat him up, then picked up your bag and sat next to me, saying, "From now on, I'll sit here." At that time, you sounded very serious, like a soldier rushing to battle.

Later, you learned how to ride a bike. One day after school, you parked your bike next to me and said, "Yuan Yuan, let me take you home!" You patted the backseat of your bike, signaling me to get on. I knew you had just learned to ride a bike not long ago, it was obvious your bike had been abused beyond recognition. But seeing your clear eyes, I got on the bike. Your eyes were so bright, you were my most trusted big brother. As soon as I sat on the bike, I could feel your hands shaking a little. At that moment, I felt a bit scared, so I clutched your clothes tightly. You started pedaling hard, sweating profusely, "Ah—" and suddenly, my head hit the ground hard, everything went dark, and I knew nothing more. When I woke up, I was lying on your bed, with a band-aid on my forehead, but my head still hurt a little. You sat next to me and asked, "Does it still hurt?" I grinned and said, "No pain!" I asked you how I got back, and your answer was enough to make me faint again. You said, "I drove you back with my bike." When I looked up at you, a glaring bloodstain caught my eye, I knew it would forever remain on your shoulder. You wore that uniform when you left, but the bloodstain had faded to a pale yellow.

After that incident, I developed a terrible fear of riding or sitting on bikes. I only remember that every time after school, you always pushed your bike and walked home with me.

Do you remember that apricot pit? I planted it in a flower pot and put it on the balcony, watering it every day, and this continued for seven years.

Three

Luo has returned, sitting right beside me. The wheels of time never cease, the details of seven years sketching a spatial composition. The shells lost seven years ago at the seaside are not so easy to pick up now, this may be the distance left by space.

"Bie Xiaoyuan, can I borrow your eraser?" It was a familiar voice, but to me, it felt so strange. "Hey, didn't you hear? Bie Xiaoyuan." "Don't you have an eraser? Why do you need mine?" I kept my head down, focused on doing homework. Maybe I was afraid of meeting his bright gaze. "I want to use yours, who made you my little sister!" My pen slipped from my hand and fell to the ground, I felt wetness in my eyes, I hurriedly bent down to pick it up, my long hair covering my face, but tears cannot escape the gravity of Earth, almost spilling over. "Luoduo Ming, you two are talking in class again." The teacher's loud voice interrupted my train of thought, I could only keep my head down facing the textbook. I wasn't afraid of being criticized by the teacher, I was just a bit worried about him. He was expelled from the city high school and transferred to our school. "Teacher, I didn't, it was Bie Xiaoyuan who initiated the conversation." His voice was so resolute. Unfortunately, I couldn't see the expression on his face. "Is that so? Bie Xiaoyuan!" My thoughts wandered back to the past, escaping this terrible reality, I had no extra energy to answer her question. I remembered the big brother who took on everything for me, always protecting me. Once, I passed a note to the girl in front saying her skirt was pretty, and when the teacher found out, it was he who stood up, causing the whole class to laugh. His face was red but he stood still. I think Earth really has strong gravity, tears aren't round, they're droplet-shaped, sharp-tipped, otherwise why would they cause pain. Fortunately, my long hair hid all this from him, I wondered what he'd laugh like if he saw. Perhaps what we owe others must eventually be repaid. Maybe my big brother wasn't named Luoduo Ming, Luoduo Ming, I don't owe you anything anymore.

After class, I followed the rhythmic click of her high heels into the office. In front of her, I was numb like a record player, but she didn't know, the power had never been turned on. I could only apologize for keeping her late, though I couldn't pay her overtime.

As I came out of the office, there were few people left in the campus, the only conspicuous ones were him and that bike standing stupidly there. "Bie Xiaoyuan, she didn't kick you with her high heels, did she? Tell me, I'll fight her!" I suspected if he had switched careers to become a comedian or novelist. I just kept walking my solitary path. I could hear his bike following behind with a clicking sound. I stopped in front, turned around like a model on the T-stage, but instead of a smile, my face was full of anger. He stopped clicking behind me, sometimes looking up at the sky, sometimes stroking his beloved bike. I had many things to say, but none came to mind. He clicked all the way home with me. "Bie Xiaoyuan, can I come in?" He spoke so politely, someone who didn't know him might think he was a good youth. He followed me into the bedroom, then ran to the balcony. I just hugged my Snoopy and stared absentmindedly. A while later, he ran over seriously and said, "Bie Xiaoyuan, it will definitely bloom." He pointed at the flower pot on the balcony with his finger. Then he raised his shining eyes, this ray of light was like a set of program codes, instantly activating my almost crashed machine. Indeed, I forgot to water the flowers today, I rushed to the balcony and picked up the watering can. I saw the soil in the flower pot had been disturbed, as water poured into the flower pot, it felt like a new force was injected into my body. "Bie Xiaoyuan, you must be strong." This voice seemed to come from the depths of my heart.

Four

I haven't seen Luoduo Ming's figure this week. For students like him, skipping class to go crazy somewhere isn't surprising. On Friday afternoon after school, I packed my bag ready to leave. She called me at the door: "Are you Bie Xiaoyuan?" Her name was Rong'er, I've seen her before. Lu came here with her for the first time, but I didn't know the relationship between her and Lu. Every time, I was like an ostrich, never asking. "Yeah, what's up?" I maintained a calm posture. "Lu was hit by a car, at the People's Hospital. He was practicing riding a bike with a wooden box on the street, when turning, a car came from the opposite direction and..." Her voice was very low, this sound invaded my body without permission, colliding, reflecting, strengthening in my chest, waves of severe pain overwhelmed my entire body, I forgot to cry and rushed out of the classroom. I almost ran to the hospital, I didn't know how the passersby looked at a long-haired girl crying as she ran past them.

When I entered the hospital, I wiped away my tears with my hand. Standing outside the intensive care room, I could only sense the unknown stabbing pain accompanied by irregular heartbeats. "What's your relationship with him?" A young nurse carrying an IV drip approached me. "I... I'm his sister, how is he?" I restrained my urgency and asked her. "The patient has been unconscious for three days, just woke up, his body is still very weak, visiting is not allowed." She said and opened the door to walk in. Through the crack of the door, I saw Luo lying there with a pale face, his forehead wrapped with layers of gauze, which obviously couldn't stop the bleeding. The sunlight shone through the window onto his face, making him look so haggard. Yet, I clearly saw traces of tears flowing from his eyes.

The nurse changed the IV drip, the liquid dripped into the intravenous tube, flowing into Luo's body, and also into my bloodstream. I could no longer withstand the impact of this liquid, it surged out of my eyes. I vaguely watched the nurse walk towards the door, "You should go home first! I'll inform you if there's any situation." I turned around, Rong'er was standing behind me, we walked out of the hospital. We reached the small fountain pool in the park, I looked at her, I didn't know if it was the tears that gave me courage or the spring water that gave me strength, I finally spoke, "Can you give Luoduo Ming back to me?" I said this sentence to her like a beggar on the street. She looked at me calmly, I didn't know what lay behind this calmness, but her composure startled me. She turned her gaze to the surging spring water, something bright flashed in her eyes, "When I was nine years old, I followed my mom to a family in the city. Mom pointed to a man and said, Rong'er, from now on, he is your dad. I tugged at mom's clothes, crying and screaming to leave, it was Lu who helped me through those painful days, always staying by my side and protecting me. One day, I asked him why he was so nice to me, he said 'Because you remind me of a girl, her name is Yuan.'" I looked at the blurred spring water, all memories rolled in my mind like spring water. "Lu's academic performance used to be very good, during the high school entrance exam, he was admitted to the key high school in the city, but he told his dad he wanted to attend high school in the county. Naturally, his dad disagreed. I only remember that later he skipped classes, violated rules, and was eventually expelled from school, then transferred here. His dad pulled some strings to transfer him to your class, because he told his dad this was a good class, he sat in the front row next to your position..." My heart felt as if pierced by something sharp, it was as if the strength accumulated for seven years burst forth all at once. I cried uncontrollably, I didn't know how I managed to drag my disheveled steps back home.

Five

I sat on the balcony at home, the sun shone warmly, very gentle. I seemed to hear a voice echoing in my ears, "The sunset today is so beautiful!" This voice was left here by Luo. I looked up at the sunset covered by red clouds, nearing dusk but still so powerful. I stood up, thinking of watering the flowers, the soil in the flower pot was a bit loose, I felt its vitality, the nutrients hidden for seven years were about to burst forth, the flowers were about to bloom.

In a month, I listened to lectures alone, took meticulous notes, and walked home alone through the shaded road, sensing the lively leaves under the sunshine. Every day I enjoyed the sunset at home, watering the flowers. Watching the flowers sprout, grow, until they bloomed. It was a kind of flower seed I couldn't name. Purple vines stood with pinkish flowers, so fresh, so fragrant. Vaguely, there was a taste of happiness, I thought it should be called Luo, this was the hope sown by Luo.

Six

Luo was about to be discharged from the hospital. That day I wore a checkered skirt. When I entered the classroom, he was leaning on his seat, his gaze met my bewildered eyes. His gaze revealed tranquility but stirred the sea within me. My overly imaginative brain began searching for lines where Luo would confess his love to me, even recalling Zhou Xingchi's line, "For ten thousand years." Before I could finish imagining the lines, "What are you doing! Daydreamer, class has started." He pointed to my seat. "Ah!" I sat down flustered and embarrassed, taking out my textbook. I could clearly feel he was sneaking glances at me, I turned around and looked at him seriously, but he nonchalantly pointed at the timetable on the blackboard, "Heaven!" I realized I had taken the wrong book, I hastily swapped it and listened to the teacher. I could only feel the cheeks covered by my long hair burning, not knowing what color they had turned. "Bie Xiaoyuan, can I borrow your notes?" He actually borrowed my notes at this moment, I lacked the courage to turn my head and say "no", I pushed the notes to his desk without looking up. Thankfully, the teacher's face, as congealed as Fe(OH)3, threw two eyes full of coolant at me, pulling me back from the equator.

The fourth period was math class. The math teacher stepped up to the podium with her steady and powerful high heels, following a linear function route. Luo had finished copying the notes, he placed the notebook on my desk, with a note attached: "Bie Xiaoyuan, your skirt looks very pretty." I quickly and stylishly wrote "Idiot" on the back and pushed it back to his desk. I almost burst out laughing seeing his frustrated face trying to climb onto the desk. When the teacher's gaze landed on Luo following a parabolic trajectory, he froze instantly like a four-wheel drive car whose battery was removed. His last dying glance fell on me. "Bie Xiaoyuan classmate, wait for me after school!" This sentence was so gentle it made me feel a bit uneasy.

The bell rang, I bolted out of the classroom like an athlete hearing the starting signal, breaking records. Walking alone on the shaded road, I seemed to hear the joyful melody of the leaves dancing under the sunlight. "Bie Xiaoyuan." I heard my name accompanied by the crisp bell ringing from behind. Before I could turn around, Luo had drifted his bike in front of me, so close, it seemed only air flowed between us, and only air sensed my heartbeat. "Get on." Luo said crisply like the bell. "I'm not a wooden box!" I teased with a smile. "True! You're even dumber than a wooden box!" Luoduo Ming grabbed my hand and pulled me to the bike seat, "Take a deep breath, slowly step on." He spoke softly, his bright eyes giving me confidence and courage. I held Luoduo Ming's hand, stepped on the bike stand, and sat on the bike. I felt my body trembling. "Hold onto me tightly." This voice transmitted through Luo's back, my overthinking brain went blank. "What are you thinking about! I'm afraid you'll fall again, now you're so heavy, how can I carry you?" I hesitated, "Ah, am I that heavy?" I tightly embraced Luo, fearing he would suddenly escape again. How nice it would be if time could freeze at this moment! Sunlight filtered through the gaps in the leaves, falling on us, like pieces of memory being picked up again. I rested my head on Luo's back, quietly, I heard Luoduo Ming's heartbeat.

Seven

The wheels clicked and turned, like a time-traveling machine, carrying us all the way to the end of Huayang Forest. I vaguely smelled the fragrance of the flowers on the balcony again, so fresh, so fragrant, like happiness.
