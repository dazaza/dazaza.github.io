The Five "Unities" of Team Support_11948

by tpro0509 on 2011-10-11 09:12:02

For a team to operate efficiently, it should achieve the principle of "ten-to-one", meaning a team of ten should function as cohesively and effectively as one individual. To reach this level, a team must unify in five aspects: a unified goal, unified thinking, unified rules, unified actions, and a unified voice. Achieving these five unifications is extremely challenging, but during the process of team building, we should strive towards these goals.

**Unified Goal**

A goal is the prerequisite for a team. Without a goal, there can be no team because a goal precedes the formation of a team. Setting a team goal is merely the first step in goal management. More importantly, the second step is to unify the team’s goal, which involves ensuring every team member identifies with and strives towards achieving the team's goal.

**Unified Thinking**

If the team's thinking is not unified, individuals may pull in different directions, much like when a person experiences internal conflict, leading to reduced action efficiency. As a team leader, it is essential to regularly work on aligning the thoughts of employees. Wang Yongqing, for instance, was an expert at aligning thoughts. He believed that results are not spoken into existence but naturally occur when a team is well-managed. Wang did not emphasize results but focused on three aspects:

1. He hoped to have a considerable level of consensus with his subordinates so that steps would be consistent without any disagreement.

2. He required subordinates to continuously improve and persist, without specifying what they should do.

3. He demanded strict adherence to his orders from subordinates, allowing no negligence.

**Unified Rules**

Every team must have its set of rules, which inform members what they should and shouldn’t do. The “shouldn’t do” defines the bottom line for team behavior. Without setting this bottom line, people will continuously cross it, and an organization that constantly breaks behavioral boundaries cannot be considered a proper team.

**Unified Actions**

When a team takes action, there needs to be mutual communication and coordination to ensure actions are unified and orderly, making the entire process reasonably connected with each detail tightly interlocked.

**Unified Voice**

After a team makes a decision, everyone must speak with one voice. There should be no situation where individuals remain silent during meetings but gossip afterward or act differently behind closed doors. If a team has too much noise, it significantly reduces team efficiency. Internal conflicts of opinion within the team are reasonable, but in front of a decision, everyone must present a united front. A foreign-funded enterprise I trained does this exceptionally well; their management encourages fierce debates during meetings, even with the general manager, but once a decision is made in the meeting, everyone strictly follows it without any disharmonious voices.

**Practical Guide:**

During the process of team building, we should often consider this question: how to achieve unity.
