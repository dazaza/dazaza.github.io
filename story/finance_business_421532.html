White wine is commonly known as "burning wine", which is a high concentration of alcoholic beverage, generally between 50~65 degrees. Depending on the saccharification and fermentation strains used and the brewing process, it can be divided into three major categories: large-curved wine, small-curved wine, and bran-curved wine. Among them, bran-curved wine can further be divided into solid-state fermented wine and liquid-state fermented wine.

White wine is China's unique traditional product with a long history. As a special commodity, white wine adds infinite joy to people's lives: wine for curing diseases, wine for longevity, wine for completing rituals. In fact, for thousands of years, the role of wine has gone far beyond these three aspects. In the public's view, although wine is not a necessity of life, in social life, wine has irreplaceable functions that other items cannot match.

China's five-thousand-year history has given birth to a wide variety of fine wines, such as "Du Kang Wine" and "Ban Qiao Banquet". Each of them has its own historical development footprint, fragrant aroma, smooth and soft taste, yet each has its own unique flavor, bringing China the reputation of being the "homeland of white wine". The "Shang Wine" produced by Beijing Yifei Wine Co., Ltd. is also a fine wine brewed from a deep historical background.

"Shang Wine" got its name during the Ming Dynasty, and its naming has a legendary color. It was said that when Prince Yan swept the north, he reached the south of Tongzhou and heard about the great victory ahead, which secured his great treasure. He rewarded the army with the local small-burnt "Tian Shang Quan". Later, Emperor Chengzu went out incognito and found that "Tian Shang Quan" was still there. He bought wine. Seeing the wine girl resembling the dragon-phoenix guiding girl in his dream last night, he realized: drinking this wine, the country will be stable; tasting this wine, the beauty will come. Both the country and beauty are fully obtained from "Tian Shang". Delighted, he personally inscribed "Tian Shang Quan", but felt it inappropriate since he was the emperor. Thus, he named it "Shang" and designated it as the tribute wine. There is a poem that goes: The pure water falls to the mortal world, the jade dew and ambrosia are pure. First appreciate the stability of the world, then savor the beauty.

Drinking white wine warm causes less harm

Drinking white wine after warming it up first is beneficial to the body. Since the main component of white wine is ethanol (alcohol), along with aldehydes, excessive drinking can lead to alcohol poisoning. Although aldehydes are not the main component of white wine, they are much more harmful to the body than alcohol. However, aldehydes have a low boiling point, around 20°C, so warming the wine a bit can make most of the aldehydes evaporate, reducing the harm to the body.
