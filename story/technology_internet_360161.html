Chronic rhinitis led to failure in the college entrance examination.

by szklyybk on 2010-07-07 16:10:43

On June 16th, Dr. Hua Qiang, director of the Otorhinolaryngology Department at Suzhou Kangli Hospital, received a brocade banner as soon as he started work. It was learned that the patient who sent the banner was Wang Zhang, a student treated and cured by Dr. Hua a week ago. Today, he came to the otorhinolaryngology treatment area of Kangli Hospital early to wait for Dr. Hua Qiang. In June 2010, Wang Zhanghua, an all-A candidate from a key high school in Suzhou, suffered from a headache during the exam, losing focus on answering questions, which resulted in his inability to complete the last subject test normally and completely shattered his dream of attending his ideal university, Tsinghua University. According to the communication between the author and his head teacher, Zhang was one of the top students with great hope of entering Tsinghua. Such an outcome made everyone very heartbroken. However, the ultimate culprit causing the headache turned out to be his four-year history of sinusitis. After being carefully treated by Dr. Hua, Zhang recovered. According to Zhang, his current nasal inflammation has been completely cured, and he is very confident about entering Tsinghua next year.

Chronic Sinusitis Ruins Tsinghua Dream

"We've reached the last subject, the child had a great chance to enter a prestigious university and achieve his dream." Speaking of her son Zhang's failure in the college entrance examination, Ms. Wang was deeply grieved.

According to Ms. Zhang, her 18-year-old son, Wang Zhanghua, attended a key high school in Suzhou, with outstanding academic performance, achieving A in every subject. Teachers from all subjects had high hopes for Zhanghua to enter Tsinghua University. However, no one expected such an outcome. Ms. Zhang said that although Zhanghua was only 18 years old, he had a four-year history of sinusitis. Since junior high school, he felt that his nose was always blocked, and he would suffer from nasal congestion whenever he caught a cold. Severe headaches occurred at times. Initially, the family thought he had a cold, but taking cold medicine proved ineffective. After a hospital checkup, it was discovered that he had sinusitis. Although he was treated several times, he still frequently experienced dizziness and headaches, and his memory declined. During last year's college entrance examination, what the family feared most happened: during the math exam, his headache suddenly worsened, making it difficult for him to concentrate on answering questions. Eventually, he gave up on the last subject exam.

Zhanghua, who failed the college entrance examination, decided to retake a year of study and definitely wanted to enter Tsinghua University. Ms. Zhang didn't sit idle either; she inquired everywhere about hospitals treating rhinitis for her son, worried that traditional treatments might recur, so she didn't let her son receive treatment lightly. In May this year, Ms. Zhang saw in the newspaper that Suzhou Kangli Hospital specifically launched a set of treatment plans for student patients and also introduced a special outpatient clinic for college students. She took her son to the Otorhinolaryngology Department of Suzhou Kangli Hospital on holidays.

Rhinitis Can Be Harmful or Affect Intelligence

The doctor who treated Zhanghua was Dr. Hua Qiang, the chief physician of the Otorhinolaryngology Department at Suzhou Kangli Hospital. Dr. Hua used the internationally advanced German STORZ electronic endoscope technology to examine Zhanghua, diagnosing him with chronic sinusitis. Subsequently, Dr. Hua performed surgery using the American plasma "low-temperature ablation" technique on Zhanghua. The surgery lasted about ten minutes. Immediately after the surgery, Zhanghua felt much more relaxed. After a brief rest, Zhanghua went home to continue reviewing. A week later, during the follow-up visit, Zhanghua's rhinitis had already been cured. "If I had known how good the technology here was, I should have brought my son for treatment earlier," seeing her son free from the troubles of sinusitis, Ms. Wang finally felt relieved.

Dr. Hua Qiang of the Otorhinolaryngology Department at Suzhou Kangli Hospital pointed out that since many people consider rhinitis as a "minor issue" involving nasal congestion and runny nose, they miss the optimal treatment opportunity. Unbeknownst to them, rhinitis can affect brain tissue, leading to dizziness, headaches, memory loss, and reduced intelligence.

It was learned that Suzhou Kangli Hospital uses the "golden" combination of German STORZ electronic endoscope technology and American plasma "low-temperature ablation" technique to treat rhinitis, sinusitis, nasal polyps, and other nasal diseases. By utilizing electronic endoscope technology to easily enter the narrow cavities of the nasal cavity, the pathological conditions within the nasal cavity are magnified dozens of times. When the pathological areas are enlarged, they are clearly displayed on the television screen. At the same time, combined with the accompanying American plasma "low-temperature ablation" surgical instruments, the entire surgery becomes more precise, effectively preventing the recurrence of polyps. This surgery is non-invasive, safe, painless, does not require hospitalization, has a quick postoperative recovery, and low comprehensive costs.
