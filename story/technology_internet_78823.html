The "Feast" of Online Finance Through the Eyes of Grassroots Entrepreneurs

Shanghai Business News, Intern Reporter Zhou Jie

"Do you keep track of your expenses?" This was the first question reporter was asked upon meeting Tian Keshan. If the answer is "no," Tian would explain many benefits of tracking expenses. If the answer is "yes," he would then enthusiastically persuade you to abandon traditional paper-based methods and switch to online expense tracking. These two grassroots entrepreneurs operate their accounting software "Caike Online" through such daily persuasion.

Absolute grassroots-level entrepreneurial dreams

Graduating in 2004, Tian Keshan never thought about coming to Shanghai. With a major in physics education, according to his family's wishes, he was supposed to become a teacher after graduation, living a stable life. For a young man from a small county in Jiangxi, this was already enough.

His first job did not follow his family's wishes to become a teacher; instead, at the persuasion of an internet friend, he went to Huzhou to work as a salesperson for a B2B website company. Although Tian discovered that the boss also didn't know how to operate e-commerce and it was "a bunch of clueless people fumbling around," this first career choice changed Tian's original intentions.

"I lived on the edge of the county, and all I could see were mountains. The will to venture outside began to become my belief." After working for more than a month at the B2B website, again at the persuasion of an internet friend, Tian came to Shanghai.

Just out of school, knowing nothing and having no experience, Tian had been in Shanghai for over a week without being captivated by its dazzling lights. Still unemployed, he was more worried about his livelihood. His internet friend issued an eviction notice, as the local internet friend also lived in a company dormitory. With 800 yuan in his pocket, Tian temporarily stayed with another internet friend for a week before once again becoming homeless.

"After coming out with two bags from a friend's place, I recharged my phone with 50 yuan, leaving me with no money left. Given my family's not-so-wealthy conditions, I could only seek help from a former roommate, waiting until midnight at a bank entrance before finally finding temporary lodging with a classmate. However, I must admit that the few internet friends I encountered back then were good people." Tian recalls those initial hardships with gratitude.

"In the first few years of being in Shanghai, I kept changing jobs, having worked over ten different positions, with the longest tenure being only one year. Many people thought I was restless, indeed, my mindset might have been bad, and bosses wouldn't like employees who frequently change jobs like me." Tian chuckled, but he was very certain that he didn't want to be an employee forever. Entrepreneurship was his ultimate goal.

He had run a coffee shop during college and opened a silk store in the metro shops while working in Shanghai. As Tian put it, time and energy were wasted, but he gained valuable experience from two failed attempts.

A single word “Yes” persisted for three years

As Tian was preparing for entrepreneurship in various ways, the other soul figure of "Caike Online" started preparing to use online means to record accounts.

"The idea of developing an online finance project originated from a very accidental reason. In the winter of 2005, soon after arriving in Shanghai, I lived in a dilapidated house in Zhangjiang. At the time, my salary was low, but my expenses were huge. Every month, I earned several thousand yuan, but I didn’t know where all the money went. So, I thought I should carefully record my expenses and figure out where the money was going.” Initially studying chemistry at Shandong Normal University, Mu Mingxing later switched majors and was admitted to Zhejiang University’s Software Engineering program. After studying software programming for a year, he became the developer of the "Caike Online" software.

In 2005, there were already some desktop accounting software programs like "Caizhi Family Finance" available on the market. But Mu Mingxing found that these software programs had a defect—they could only be installed on one computer, making them inconvenient. "If we could check our accounts online, it would be much better." Excited by this idea, he searched online and found that there was nothing similar domestically at the time. When he shared his idea of creating an online accounting software with a good friend, the friend's simple "Yes" gave Mu Mingxing the determination to create an online accounting software.

On February 10, 2006, the first version of "Caike Online Accounting Book" was launched, followed by subsequent versions. The earliest prototype of "Caike Online" was initially named "Financial ALA".

"My project faced opposition from countless people, including friends and relatives, none of whom were optimistic. The single 'Yes' from my friend in June helped 'Caike Online' persist for three years. Between June and October of that year, the market remained calm. Searching for online bookkeeping still showed only our site. Perhaps due to the market not yet taking off, 'Caike Online' developed slowly. The database structure kept changing, and the design direction was uncertain. Initially, it supported multiple users, but then it was deemed unnecessary and removed. Later, it was added back. At this time, the users were mainly friends and family, with no real external users."

When talking about the early days of starting the online bookkeeping website, Mu Mingxing felt the situation was awkward.

Now, seeing some websites boastfully claim to be the earliest bookkeeping sites, the best bookkeeping sites on Earth, or the most professional bookkeeping sites, Mu Mingxing does not make any comments. Meanwhile, "Caike Online" has been steadily doing its own thing.

Technology needs to quickly "marry" the market

After the first version was released, "Caike Online" achieved basic income and expenditure functions. Perhaps technical personnel generally possess the characteristic of pursuing perfection. Mu Mingxing made the technical architecture of "Caike Online" extremely complex, causing subsequent development to become less smooth. By June 2006, a completely new architecture for Caike Online was created. And at this point, after widely soliciting opinions, the previous name "Financial ALA" was officially changed to "Caike Online."

"In the following months after multiple version updates and launches, we focused on simplifying the architecture and redesigning the interface. During this period, Caike Online developed slowly." From February 10, 2006, to May 10, 2007, "Caike Online" only managed to develop 499 members.

By October, the direction of Caike's development became a problem. On this point, Mu Mingxing and his initial partner had serious disagreements. One advocated practicality above all, focusing on functionality first, then developing others. The other insisted on improving the interface first and developing a community. Since both were technical personnel with little knowledge of the market, Caike Online's development temporarily fell into a deadlock.

After 2007, within expectations, online bookkeeping websites sprouted up everywhere, with almost a new one emerging each month. Some even launched aggressive promotional campaigns, and a few had already surpassed "Caike Online." Some of Caike Online's initial ideas, like SMS bookkeeping, were also plagiarized. At this point, Mu Mingxing realized that if things continued like this, it wouldn't work. They needed capable marketing personnel to reverse the declining trend.

At this time, Tian Keshan's experience across multiple websites turned the crisis into an opportunity, leading to rapid progress after several months of promotional efforts.

"In 2005, I created a 'Smart Saving Website.' I gave 3000 yuan to a colleague who understood technology to develop a set of programs. When I proposed many modifications, I found that this colleague disappeared. However, I still wanted to build an '积分网' (Points Network) website." He met Mu Mingxing through the internet, who agreed to help Tian Keshan create a free set of programs. What started as a helping hand eventually turned into a partnership alliance.

Promoting "Caike" during interactions

Although Tian Keshan changed jobs frequently and his highest salary was only 3000 yuan, he was very clear that all his work experience was preparation for future entrepreneurship.

Tian Keshan and Mu Mingxing, after meeting and discussing, realized they each had their strengths and decided to collaborate on entrepreneurship. "Caike Online" and "积分网" (Points Network), each person had one website. After deliberation, "Caike Online" became their final choice. Even though they operated the same website together, strategic differences often led to arguments, sometimes refusing to take each other's calls, but eventually, one side would always compromise.

"From my perspective, I still favor the '积分网' (Points Network) model, but after all, 'Caike Online' has been running for a year." Tangible things are always easier to handle than intangible ones. Tian Keshan's persuasive skills were greatly utilized, and within a short time, "Caike Online's" Alexa ranking improved from over a million to around 200,000. Daily registrations increased from fewer than ten people to a maximum of 1500 people in one day, and daily IP numbers continued to rise.

Currently, Caike Online has over 80,000 members, rising from the bottom to the industry leader, with its Alexa ranking increasing to 120,000, which was over a million a year ago.

Now, the two partners have clear goals: to fully focus on building the "Caike Online" platform well. They will only consider the profitability of the website after the product features are gradually perfected. Future plans include promoting advanced services to charge membership fees, with 20% of members willing to pay according to their own surveys; planned mass emails to those earning over 5000 yuan, aiming for precise ad placement for advertisers, is also considered a potential profit model by Tian Keshan.

"Members don't need to worry about personal information security issues. Members registered on the website use virtual identities." Although Tian Keshan is uncertain about the feasibility of these plans, the 5% uniqueness of Caike Online's features has become its core competitiveness.

To get closer to their goals, money becomes the primary issue. "We're not unwilling to spend money on promotion, but we simply don't have the funds. After joining Caike, we wanted to hire graphic designers but couldn't afford it. We wanted to hire financial staff, but we were still short of funds." Once they have money, Tian Keshan's top priority would be promotion.

"Previously, we just relied on persuasion to gather members. Out of 10 people, maybe 2 would help us promote 'Caike Online,' and the number of 80,000 members came from this method. But what 'Caike Online' needs now is strong penetration to cultivate more registered users. We mimicked the functionalities of standalone accounting software and have been exploring on our own ever since. Foreign websites with similar models have successively received funding, even though they started later than Caike Online." Regarding the future, both Mu Mingxing, who focuses on technology, and Tian Keshan, who handles the market, are confident in "Caike Online: www.caakee.com".
