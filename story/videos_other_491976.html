She is no ordinary woman.

by dcvtaoupg on 2011-09-20 11:44:31

They borrowed a friend's warehouse, which was briefly tidied up and used as a bedroom. The cold warehouse was like an ice cellar; without a warm quilt to wrap her body, she often woke up frozen in the middle of the night. He tightly held her, trying his best to press her against his chest, warming her with his own body temperature.

She was not an ordinary woman. On the contrary, she was quite excellent and had many admirers pursuing her. But she turned them all away and resolutely followed him. At that time, he was penniless, working in a factory with an income that wasn't enough to solve their hunger and cold. For him, she lost her family and her job.

A few years later, his situation gradually improved. He became wealthy, started his own company, and soon bought a house and a car. They bid farewell to their days of hunger and cold. The interior decoration of their home was extremely meticulous, with imported floor tiles and wallpaper, even the faucets were top-of-the-line. He wanted to give her the warmest home possible. Living in such an environment, she felt somewhat hesitant. When they moved, all the things from the original warehouse were discarded except for that quilt, which she insisted on keeping. They had been using it for years, and it had already worn out in several places. He said, "Throw it away, let's buy a new one. Everything else here is elegant, but this will be an eyesore." She said, "No, this quilt has accompanied us through several cold winters, and covering ourselves with it always feels so warm." He shook his head but didn't insist any longer.

One day, he came back from outside, carrying a new quilt, insisting she throw away the old one and replace it with the new one. With no other choice, she obeyed. After replacing the old with the new, every night she couldn't sleep as peacefully and comfortably as before. A streak of sadness passed through her heart, and often in the middle of the night, tears of grievance unknowingly wet her pillow. In her heart, she said, "Do you know how much effort it took to buy this quilt? That day, I didn't go around distributing flyers at all—I went to sell blood! The first time I sold blood was to buy this quilt! This quilt means so much to me! And you, you threw it away like it was garbage." She felt that he didn't love her as much as he used to, and although they were now covered by a new quilt, it lacked the warmth it once had.

Once, when he went to the bathroom, he forgot to turn off his laptop. By accident, she discovered that he had a personal blog where he posted daily. In one of his diary entries, he wrote: "That day, she came in from outside, her pale face scared me. To earn enough money to buy a quilt, she actually distributed flyers. That night, we slept under the new quilt, it was so warm, and she never slept so soundly. Accidentally, I noticed that there was a red swelling on her hand, pierced by a needle. Distributing flyers was actually her delicate lie—she went to sell blood. This quilt was actually bought with her blood! Her body was so frail! That night, I cried secretly for the whole night, vowing that I must rise to prominence and give her happiness. After years of hard work, I finally succeeded. Yesterday, I also went to the blood station, asking them to draw my blood. I just wanted to feel the tiny needle piercing into the vein, the cool pain made me unprepared, but it was also so happy. I took the money and bought this quilt."

Her eyes were already blurred. Originally, his heart, like his love for her, was so delicate. On a cold winter day, he gave her a warm quilt, bringing her the entire warm spring.

One day, she returned from outside, looking dazed and pale. He asked, "What's wrong? Are you sick?" She said, "Nothing, just a little tired." Then she immediately cheerfully took out a red bill from her pocket, waving it in front of him, excitedly saying, "We have money now, let's go buy a warm quilt!" Where did the money come from? She earned it. He asked, "How did you earn it?" So easily. By handing out advertisements, one by one, standing from morning until now, earning 100 yuan in tips. They went to the street to buy a quilt, not being very selective, purchasing according to the price of 100 yuan. From then on, during the harsh winter, they had a quilt, and she no longer woke up frozen in the middle of the night.
