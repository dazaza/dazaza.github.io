The memories of life are like old photos, stored in the mind. They may be happy or sad, joyful or sorrowful, but all of these are the most important fragments of the journey of life.

In the afterglow of the setting sun, an elderly lady with a full head of white hair sits in a wheelchair, pushed by an elderly gentleman of the same age. Along the way, the two walk and stop intermittently. There is no verbal exchange or physical communication between them. However, their faces reveal identical expressions, and their eyes meet with the same sense of fulfillment. Sharing both happiness and hardship, they accompany each other in their old age.

A young pregnant woman with a round belly walks leisurely, enjoying the ice cream in her hand. The man beside her is filled with happiness as he embraces her, refusing the ice cream she offers to him. She insists that he take a bite, and they both laugh. He squats down to tie her loosened shoelaces. The sweetness of the remaining ice cream lingers in their mouths, filling their hearts with warmth.

The father wears his son's small cartoon hat on his head, with his delighted son riding on his shoulders. A Winnie the Pooh water bottle hangs from his chest, and a large bag full of snacks rests on his back. His left hand supports his son. The wife is dressed coolly, with a small bag slung over her arm, while her other hand is held by her husband. Despite being drenched in sweat from exhaustion, the husband does not let his wife share the burden of the heavy load.

These scenes unfold continuously on the streets, street corners, or in parks, leaving behind a lasting fragrance for those who witness them.

In today's society where houses, cars, and savings dominate, the beauty of life has been invaded by money and extinguished by desires. In pursuit of satisfying one's own vanity, we have forgotten the original meaning of happiness.

Flowers and fine wine may not necessarily touch someone's heart, but a meaningful glance in a crowd could stir emotions.

When someone is willing to cook for you, are you willing to hold an umbrella for them to shield against the wind and rain?

When love arrives, be braver, retreat less; be more resolute, hesitate less; have more confidence, show less weakness; hold on tightly without letting go, because missing this opportunity might lead to lifelong regret.

Loving someone actually tires them out, so treat them twice as well, give double the effort, show double the consideration, and understand twice as much...

Falling in love is easy, staying together is not hard. Learn to think from the other person's perspective, learn to cherish and be grateful. Why not make a pact to last forever?
