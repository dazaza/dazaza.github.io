The Art of War mentions in the "Plan" section: "Warfare is the way of deception," meaning to attack where the enemy is unprepared and strike when they do not expect it. This principle also applies to the space wars in "Star Empire." Each of the three major races - Koma, Imperial Star, and Galon - have their own strengths, but simple attacks alone are unlikely to secure victory. Players can fully utilize the game's mobility by seeking reinforcements, seizing opportunities when the enemy's main fleet is away on campaigns, and attacking them unawares!

In "Star Empire," when the military strength of both attackers and defenders is equal, the defender will have a clear advantage. The attacker must face the counterattack from the opponent's warships while also enduring heavy fire from the defense system. It is extremely difficult to quickly break through this defense. Therefore, the attacker must first probe the enemy's strength - knowing oneself and one’s enemy ensures success in a hundred battles. Even when the attacker's strength is insufficient to defeat the enemy, one should not give up.

"Attack where the enemy is unprepared, strike where they do not expect it." Players can also flexibly use feints - pretending to attack. The attacker can send out a cheap warship to initiate an attack, while the defender cannot see the number of the attacker's forces. Thus, after several victories, the defender may lower their guard and send troops to explore planets for colonization. At this opportune moment, the attacker can deploy their "main force" directly to the enemy's home planet for plunder. Facing attacks from all directions, the defender will be at a loss, unable to immediately recall their ships that are out on campaign. Their remaining defenses will be powerless to withstand the attacker's fierce surprise assault, ultimately leading to a惨defeat.

However, by combining Sun Tzu's Art of War with large-scale star warfare strategies, the attacker can crush the opponent even when their overall strength is weaker than the defender's. "Attack where the enemy is unprepared, strike where they do not expect it" - this is the subtlety of Sun Tzu's Art of War!

Today, the guide lecture for "Star Empire" brings you – how to defeat the strong with the weak and win with fewer numbers. Success or failure of the third race in "Star Empire" - Koma, Imperial Star, and Galon - does not lie in the inherent advantages of the races themselves, but more so in the strategic steps and tactics between the two sides. Choosing the right strategy and tactics for each race in different combat scenarios will directly determine the ultimate success or failure.
