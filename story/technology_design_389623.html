[1] Don't wait for me to tell you before you act

After the statement "It's the people who are supporting you, so you better figure it out yourselves!" Premier Wen's recent comment during a government work report symposium, "Don't wait for me to tell you before you act," has once again been warmly embraced by netizens.

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the government work report was 4.6%, while the Institute of Sociology of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences published a social blue book revealing an urban surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4%. Why would there be a 5.2% difference between the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security and the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences' unemployment statistics? The public had serious doubts about this. In light of this "quarrel," Premier Wen expressed:

"You, the Ministry of Social Labor Security, should quickly go online, or you should quickly hold a press conference, you explain the difference between the 4.6 and the 9.4 from the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, don't wait for me to tell you before you act."

Why are these two numbers different? Li Peilin, director of the Institute of Sociology of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, explained it this way:

"For the past dozen years, there has always been a difference between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, usually the latter being higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, the two data points are close, but when the employment situation is bad, there will be a large gap between the two data points. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, such as migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, they are difficult to be counted after becoming unemployed."

However, the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security has another explanation:

"The 9.4% survey unemployment rate is the result of a sample survey of seven thousand people, among which there are 2288 urban economically active population, among which there are more than 200 unemployed people, this number is derived from the comparison of these two figures."

In fact, regarding these two sets of data, we ordinary people all have our own opinions:

"Like me, graduates who have worked in the city for only a few years, our hukou (household registration) is still at school, where can we register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, we can only quickly find a job ourselves, how dare we tarnish the country's unemployment rate."

For this calculation method that does not include people who are neither employed nor registered, it has been questioned by domestic and foreign scholars. Premier Wen should make them explain not just the seriously underestimated urban registered unemployment rate, but also give us a number that can withstand scrutiny.

Some departments indeed act without waiting for the premier to speak. After the Guangdong provincial government officially announced the "May Day" holiday plan, Hangzhou, Suzhou, and Nanjing proposed to jointly restore the May Day long holiday, making the issue of whether the May Day Golden Week should be restored a focal point of discussion.

In response to the current heated discussions, the General Office of the State Council officially responded: no localities may arbitrarily restore the May Day long holiday.

Should the total number of working days in a year be unified? Although some comments reference Germany's central government only stipulating some unified statutory holidays, leaving the rest to be determined by each state, raising the question of whether "May Day" holidays could be decided by localities, but have they considered whether this is suitable for China's national conditions under different political systems?

Actually, I just want to ask, why do various places engage in such long-distance shouting without approval from the General Office of the State Council?

[3] Can every province save money?

The case of Yunnan police officer shooting someone recently caused controversy due to a settlement agreement. Mengzi County Public Security Bureau reached a reconciliation agreement with the deceased's family, paying a huge compensation of 550,000 yuan to the victim's family.

Regarding the case of police officer Ji Zhongchun shooting someone, the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau initially categorized it as a personal act, having nothing to do with his identity or position as a police officer. Since it was a personal act, why did the Public Security Bureau pay the compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it this way:

"The reason the Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances. Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked at the Public Security Bureau for more than 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home. Out of humanitarianism, the Public Security Bureau gave the deceased's family a prior humanitarian compensation. And this 550,000 yuan is office expenses saved by the Public Security Bureau."

It is well known that the incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun had a dispute while reversing his car. How can a police officer who owns a private car be defined as having poor economic conditions at home? This cannot help but raise the question: what exactly constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the "Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police," if a police officer illegally uses weapons, causing unnecessary casualties or property damage, the relevant authority to which the police officer belongs shall provide compensation according to the relevant provisions of the "State Compensation Law." Why doesn't the Mengzi Public Security Bureau admit that this compensation is state compensation? If it indeed isn't, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money for private gain?

[6] Overhyped online entrepreneurship

Suddenly, as if overnight spring breeze came, thousands of online shops sprouted everywhere. In the current economic crisis, the Internet seems to become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only the media repeatedly promotes online employment as a great path for students, even during the two sessions period, there were representatives suggesting that the state should subsidize college students starting businesses on Taobao.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, online entrepreneurship became an instant darling, and many domestic colleges and universities have opened online shop training classes to help students start businesses. Yes, if everyone follows the trend and opens online shops now, the employment problem can be solved immediately, but what about one year later, ten years later? How many people can truly succeed? Let alone issues like medical insurance and social security. And the security and legal standardization of the Internet have always been the most neglected difficulties in the information economy tide. Are our experts and professors considering this point while advocating that college students choose online entrepreneurship?
