Beijing knock-off ATM steals account passwords

by hpreal3470 on 2010-06-24 23:10:47

　Recently, on the streets of Beijing, a new type of scam has emerged that is hard to guard against. Unlawful individuals install fake ATMs to extract card numbers and passwords from cardholders, subsequently copying the bank cards to steal money. A counterfeit ATM was found outside a tobacco and liquor store at the corner of Guang'anmen Wai Xijie Street in Xuanwu District. On June 15, Mr. Ma, a citizen, inserted his card into this machine to check his balance but only received the prompt "unable to provide service." Two days later, he discovered that 5000 yuan had vanished from his account. The same day, another person claimed to have fallen victim and reported it to the police. Currently, the police have arrested the suspects and are conducting further investigations.

"The scammers nowadays are too 'high-tech,' they've even come up with a counterfeit bank ATM," said Mr. Chen, a resident of a nearby neighborhood near Guang'anmen. Around 8:30 AM the other day, three police cars drove into Guang'anmen Wai Xijie, and several plainclothes officers began investigating the matter.

At 5 PM the previous day, the reporter arrived at the scene and observed that the mentioned fake ATM was located at the corner of a residential building in No.1 Guang'anmen Wai Xijie. This machine was housed inside a "glass house" next to a tobacco and liquor store. The glass house was labeled as "24-hour self-service bank" with the logo of Hong Kong Hang Seng Bank. That afternoon, staff from Hang Seng Bank arrived on site and confirmed that "this is a fake bank ATM," stating that Hang Seng Bank had never installed an ATM here.

This fake ATM had a high level of realism. Above the machine were various financial institution logos, with warning signs around it such as "image acquisition area" and "do not enter while in use."

"At first glance, it's hard to tell if it's real or fake. But upon closer inspection, you can spot the differences," Mr. Chen from the neighborhood explained. This fake ATM showed no bank name on its screen, only displaying "please insert card." Additionally, its "cash outlet" had no actual opening, the anti-peeking shield was glued together, and multiple "customer service phone numbers" marked on the ATM could not be dialed.

The owner of the tobacco and liquor store stated that a week ago, he rented the space to someone claiming to be a bank employee, unaware of any other details. Another nearby resident noted that a week ago, people came to install equipment there. It was a closed operation with white curtains and paper blocking visibility from the outside. "After they left, we saw that an ATM had been installed, and we were all happy, thinking it would be convenient," said a resident.

On June 15, Mr. Ma, a nearby resident, used the fake ATM for convenience to check his balance on his Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (ICBC) card. He said, "There should be only a few dozen yuan in the card, but I wanted to know the exact amount." After inserting his card and entering the password, he found that the screen displayed "temporarily unable to provide service." Thinking he might have entered the wrong password, he tried again, but still couldn't check the balance, "I assumed the machine was broken."

On June 17, Mr. Ma's brother-in-law returned 5000 yuan borrowed from Mr. Ma and deposited the money into Mr. Ma's ICBC card. Mr. Ma then confirmed it at a legitimate bank branch. The previous afternoon, Mr. Ma learned about the fake ATM incident and quickly went to a nearby ICBC branch to check the balance on his card.

"The money's gone, it's really gone!" Ten minutes later, Mr. Ma, sweating profusely, ran home holding the card and shouted to his wife waiting at the door. He claimed that at least 5000 yuan had disappeared from the card, leaving only 30 yuan as the remaining balance. Reminded by his wife, Mr. Ma promptly called the police. At the police station while giving a statement, Mr. Ma met another victim who had also come to report the crime. The person claimed that on the morning of the same day, all the money in their account had been transferred, suspecting it was related to the fake ATM next to the tobacco and liquor store because they had also used that machine.

"There should be other victims, but most likely, they haven't realized their money has been transferred yet," Mr. Ma said, adding that every day he could see many people operating the fake ATM, "Some tourists staying at nearby hotels often check their balances with backpacks on."

"I saw a suspicious man." The owner of a shop opposite the fake ATM said that he gets up at 5:30 AM every day to take care of his shop and always sees a man with a large backpack appear near the fake ATM. After entering, the man squats down, opens the front cover of the ATM, "seems to take something out and put it into the backpack," then walks to the back side of the ATM, "fiddling around" for a few minutes before leaving with the backpack. Every afternoon around 5 or 6 o'clock, the backpack man reappears. The shop owner said, "I thought he was a bank employee coming regularly to inspect the machine, but now it seems he isn't, quite suspicious."

Around 5 or 6 o'clock the previous afternoon, the backpack man did not show up. Many residents had already learned about the incident and gathered around the ATM to observe. The shop owner said, "The suspect might have sensed something."

According to the police notification, at 9 AM on June 19, the 110 emergency hotline of the Municipal Public Security Bureau received a report from a citizen who suspected using a counterfeit ATM in Xuanwu District, followed by theft of cash from their card. Upon receiving the report, the police took it very seriously, and the Criminal Investigation Brigade worked with the Xuanwu Branch to immediately establish a special investigation team to conduct the investigation, quickly locking onto the suspects. At 6 PM on June 20, the special investigation team captured the suspect Huang (male, 30 years old, from Jilin Province) near Wangfujing. After preliminary review, the suspect admitted to installing a homemade counterfeit ATM near a tobacco store in Shoupaikou Bridge, Xuanwu District, to carry out credit card fraud. Currently, the case is under further investigation and trial.

Yesterday afternoon, the fake ATM was taken away by the police for technical examination.

According to insiders, at 11:30 PM last night, the police brought the suspect back to the location where he was caught for further searches. The suspect confessed that the method of committing the crime was devised by himself, after which he spent money in Beijing to buy the relevant equipment to begin implementation. When victims insert their cards to check, the computer system inside the fake ATM can extract the bank account and password. Afterwards, he copies the information extracted from the system, makes counterfeit bank cards, and withdraws cash from bank branches.

The police remind cardholders to use automatic deposit and withdrawal machines located beside legitimate bank branches whenever possible. If any credit card fraud activities are discovered, contact the police as soon as possible.
