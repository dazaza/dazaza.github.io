Some farmers in Wuhan who tried using vegetable greenhouses to breed centipedes found that the number of centipedes decreased just a few days after starting with the Golden Head No. 2 centipedes. They had initially placed the centipedes at a density of 10 per square meter. Normally, there should have been at least a dozen centipedes under each breeding bed, but now many beds were completely empty. The first thought was that there might be predators. Although snakes, scorpions, and centipedes form a toxic triangle and can suppress each other, it was unlikely that they caused such a massive disappearance of centipedes. Later, it was discovered that the centipedes had escaped through the glass seams around the greenhouse.

Although they cannot climb smooth surfaces, the joints of glass strips still provide an opportunity for escape. This small glass opening caused the farmer to lose over ten thousand yuan worth of centipedes. Worse still, the escaped centipedes bit students at a school near the breeding farm. Preventing centipedes from escaping thus became an urgent task.

Hearing that centipedes are afraid of water, the breeder dug a trench around the greenhouse and filled it with water, hoping this would deter the centipedes and keep them inside the greenhouse. However, contrary to expectations, centipedes are near-sighted and cannot see whether there is water or not; they only rely on their antennae and persistently try to climb out. Like all living things, centipedes also have vulnerabilities. Since their spiracles are located on their abdomens, even slightly deep water can cause them to suffocate and die, resulting in further losses for the breeder. It was clear that digging trenches was not an effective way to prevent escapes.

Later, the breeder decided to use plastic film to build a fence around the greenhouse. Because the film is smooth, the centipedes could not climb it, making it like an impenetrable net. All places where centipedes could potentially climb were covered with 50-centimeter-high plastic film. To avoid giving the centipedes any opportunities, the breeder regularly wiped off dust from the plastic film and quickly repaired any damage. After much effort, the centipedes were finally controlled, and there were no more cases of missing centipedes.
