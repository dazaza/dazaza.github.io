Man with obsessive-compulsive disorder feels mocked and strangles neighbor's boy in revenge

by yan64txue on 2012-03-01 22:06:09

This 33-year-old man from Shunchang has been plagued by obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) for 27 years. His studies, career, and love life have all suffered because of it, and feeling like he was heading towards a breakdown, he only wished for death. A man from Shunchang had OCD; his girlfriend left him, he was fired from his company, and he felt that everyone around him was mocking him. Feeling like there was nothing left to live for, he strangled a three-year-old child from the neighboring family with his own hands.

Why did he harm a three-year-old child? The murderer said: "The neighbors have been laughing at me since I was a kid. If I kill their child, I can get my revenge. Moreover, the court will sentence me to death, allowing me to end my painful life." However, the murderer did not die. After the judge's efforts in mediation, the murderer's brother compensated the victim's family with over ten thousand yuan, gaining their forgiveness. Recently, the Nanping Intermediate People's Court sentenced him to death with a two-year reprieve for intentional homicide.

He strangled a three-year-old boy at home. Ni Xiaoshao, from Shunchang County in Fujian Province, is 33 years old this year. On December 2, 2009, at 11 a.m., while his neighbor's child, Xiao Lin, was having lunch at his house, Ni Xiaoshao tricked him into the bedroom and strangled him to death, then hid the body under the bed. Ni Xiaoshao's nephew, Xiao Ni, witnessed this scene and was terrified. Ni Xiaoshao quickly instructed his nephew: "If anyone asks about Xiao Lin, just say you don't know." That afternoon, after Ni Xiaoshao's brother returned home and discovered Xiao Lin's body but found his younger brother missing, he immediately reported it to the police. Two days later, the police captured Ni Xiaoshao, who was hiding in the mountains.

"You should hurry up and execute me! I've long wanted to die!" During the interrogation, Ni Xiaoshao readily admitted to the murder, desiring death which shocked the officers. Upon investigation, it was revealed that Ni Xiaoshao had been suffering from OCD for 27 years, which made him lose love, his career, and also his confidence in life.

At six years old, he developed OCD, causing his academic performance to plummet dramatically. From the age of six, his family noticed that Ni Xiaoshao was different from other children: he would repeatedly check if his homework was correct; upon seeing streetlights or utility poles, he couldn’t help but count them repeatedly; every pen he used daily had to be washed; he refused to sit on any chair that others had sat on... These actions were considered by his family as merely being introverted and weren’t taken seriously. It wasn’t until the psychiatric hospital diagnosed him with OCD that the family realized the severity of the situation.

According to Ni Xiaoshao’s family, during his student years, Ni Xiaoshao was among the top performers. However, entering his senior year of high school, he read about a murder case on campus in a newspaper. Since that day, the incident constantly lingered in his mind, even generating thoughts of killing. He became afraid to go to school, experiencing extreme fear and anguish, suffering from insomnia or constant nightmares at night. In the 1998 college entrance exam, he only managed to get into a junior college. During his time in university, due to his solitary nature, no girls were willing to interact with him. Ni Xiaoshao became increasingly reclusive and dropped out of school after half a semester.

Due to his severe condition, Ni Xiaoshao was hospitalized at the Fujian Minqing Psychiatric Hospital for treatment, diagnosed with OCD. After three months of hospitalization, costing nearly ten thousand yuan, he was discharged. Seeing that he barely stepped outside, was quick-tempered, and often complained about wanting to die, his parents helped him find a job in Shenzhen, thinking that perhaps keeping busy might improve his condition.

His failed romance and unemployment drove the "silent man" to collapse. Ni Xiaoshao said that working in Shenzhen was the happiest time of his life because he had a girlfriend by his side. At that time, he felt his illness had improved and he was no different from others. However, an incident shattered all his self-respect. Ni Xiaoshao discovered that during intimate moments with his girlfriend, no matter how hard he tried, he couldn't fulfill a man's "mission". His girlfriend complained: "Are you sick?" In a panic, he choked her neck, almost strangling her. After this terrifying event, his girlfriend broke up with him citing "mental issues" and "physiological defects," further plunging his mental state into chaos.

This significantly affected his work, and soon after, Ni Xiaoshao was dismissed. "I didn't finish school, my girlfriend broke up with me, and I lost my job. I feel like a failure, living is just pain, it's better to be dead."

A twisted childhood is the cause of his illness. Ni Xiaoshao said that ever since he learned he had OCD, he read many related books and discovered that it was linked to his unfortunate childhood. According to Ni Xiaoshao's recollection, when he was young, his father was fond of drinking and would often come home drunk and fight with his mother. After being beaten, his mother would cry and take out her frustrations on Ni Xiaoshao.

As a child, Ni Xiaoshao performed well academically, so his mother pinned all her hopes on him, hoping that he would attend a prestigious university, allowing them both to leave Shunchang and escape their miserable marriage. Therefore, she set extremely strict requirements for him, insisting he follow her instructions precisely. Whenever his grades were not the best in class, she would resort to physical punishment, accompanied by tears and complaints about her misfortune. During these times, Ni Xiaoshao could only lower his head and remain silent.

One time, while masturbating on the hill behind the village, he was caught by some kids from the same village. Since then, he became the "laughingstock" of the villagers. This incident was the trigger for Ni Xiaoshao's illness. Especially the frequent ridicule from neighbors made him increasingly reclusive, leading to his desire for "revenge against those who mocked him." Long-term silence, anxiety, and irritability gradually led him to develop many strange habits. Villagers thought he was a "freak," exacerbating Ni Xiaoshao's condition.

Obsessive-compulsive disorder has accompanied Ni Xiaoshao for 27 years. To escape the torment of his illness, he contemplated death. Thus, the three-year-old child from the neighboring family unfortunately became the stepping stone on his path to death.
