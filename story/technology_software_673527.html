"Romance of the Three Kingdoms 12" Latest Warlord: Xun You, the Master Strategist

The official website of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms 12" has updated the character design image and introduction of the latest warlord, Xun You. The characters updated in recent days are somewhat similar and belong to a batch that can be easily merged. Xun You is no exception. When Cao Cao's power was not yet strong, he recruited a group of excellent strategists in Yanzhou, laying the foundation for the talent base of his future hegemony. These people include Xun Yu, Xun You, Guo Jia, and Cheng Yu, etc. However, in the "Romance of the Three Kingdoms", whether it is personality appearance, strategy quality, or ideal aspirations, Xun Yu and Xun You are basically the same. The pair of cousins with the same surname often cross-submit strategies, which makes Xun You easy to be forgotten, or even merged into the same category.

In fact, historically, the status and job nature of Xun Yu and Xun You were quite different. Most of Xun Yu's strategies were overall strategies, and then there was talent promotion. Xun You was the traditional strategist who gave advice on tactics, not only a strategist, but also the master strategist. His status was clearly a step higher than Cheng and Guo. And Chen Shou might have considered his moral considerations, so he combined him with Xun Yu and Jia Xu into one biography, and separated him from Cheng and Guo and other strategists, not because there was any difference in strategy. Therefore, as tactical strategists, it is probably meaningless to compare who is better between Xun You and Guo Jia, but in terms of status and role in "Records of the Three Kingdoms", Xun You is indeed a step higher than Guo Jia, possibly due to Guo Jia's frequent illness. In the novel, Xun You was basically neglected, while Guo Jia was greatly promoted, even reaching a level higher than Xun Yu.

As for the appearance, there is no detailed description in historical records. This time, the character design by Ryo is just a typical strategist image, so there is not much to comment on.

Xun You, courtesy name Gongda, was from Yingyin, Yingchuan (today's Xuchang, Henan). He was Xun Yu's nephew and Cao Cao's chief military advisor during the Three Kingdoms period, an outstanding tactician known as Cao Cao's "master strategist", skilled in flexible and varied enemy-defeating tactics and military strategies. During He Jin's reign, Xun You served as the Yellow Gate Gentleman. He was once imprisoned for plotting to assassinate Dong Zhuo when Dong Zhuo entered the capital. Later, he resigned and returned home. After Cao Cao welcomed the emperor to Xuchang, Xun You served as the governor of Ji'nan and then as the minister of ceremonies, and became Cao Cao's military advisor. When Cao Cao attacked Lu Bu, Xun You dissuaded Cao Cao from retreating and proposed a clever plan to flood Xiapi City, capturing Lu Bu alive. In the Battle of Guandu, Xun You's ingenious strategy led to the defeat of Yuan Shao's army, killing generals Yan Liang and Wen Chou. He also devised a clever plan to send Xu Huang to burn Yuan Shao's supplies and strongly advocated Cao Cao to adopt Xu You's plan, achieving great success at Wuchao. During the pacification of Hebei, Xun You strongly opposed the majority opinion and urged Cao Cao to eliminate Yuan Shao's sons, for which Cao Cao submitted a memorial to the court and Xun You was enfeoffed as Lord Lingshu. In the twelfth year of Jian'an (AD 207), Xun You was appointed as the central military advisor and later as the minister of ceremonies. Xun You was careful and low-key in his actions, with countless strategies, and was highly praised by Cao Cao. In the nineteenth year of Jian'an (AD 214), Xun You died on the way to Cao Cao's campaign against Wu. Xun You had twelve empty city plans in his lifetime, only Zhong Yao knew about them, but Zhong Yao died before he could organize them, which is why they are unknown to later generations. During the Zhengshi period, he was posthumously honored with the title of Jing Hou.
