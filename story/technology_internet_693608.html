Introduction to Hong Kong:

Hong Kong is located at 114°15' East Longitude and 22°15' North Latitude, on the southern coast of China. It consists of Hong Kong Island, Kowloon Peninsula, the inland areas of the New Territories, and 262 large and small islands (offshore islands).

Hong Kong is a free port. Except for tobacco, strong liquor, and fuel (gasoline, diesel, etc.), Hong Kong does not impose tariffs on other imported goods. Hong Kong's economy is renowned for free trade, low tax rates, and minimal government intervention. Finance, real estate, tourism, and manufacturing are the four pillar industries driving Hong Kong's economic development. The top ten Hong Kong products include ready-made garments, electronic products, textiles, watches, printed materials, plastic products, metal products, food and beverages, industrial machinery, and jewelry. For many years, Hong Kong has been selected by international famous rating agencies and organizations as the world's freest economic system. Hong Kong is an international financial center, shipping center, and regional trade center in the Asia-Pacific region, with an irreplaceable advantageous position near many countries and regions.

Advantages of registering a company in Hong Kong:

1. Hong Kong companies have no industry restrictions. They can engage in any legal business without requiring additional licenses;

2. Hong Kong is one of the freest trading ports in the world, with free logistics: general cargo imports and exports are not subject to customs duties; Hong Kong allows free inflow and outflow of funds, and various foreign currencies can be exchanged and transferred at any time;

3. Hong Kong company tax policies and tax rates: few tax types and low tax rates;

Hong Kong companies only have two taxes: one is a one-time capital registration stamp duty with a rate of 1/1000; the other is profits tax with a rate of 16.5%;

Special consumer goods (such as tobacco and alcohol) are subject to customs duties upon import and export.

4. Utilize Hong Kong's reputation to create a Hong Kong brand and enhance the company's international image;

5. Allow shell companies to exist and allow offshore business operations;

Conditions for registering a Hong Kong company:

1. Company Name

The general provisions of the Hong Kong Companies Ordinance regarding company names are as follows:

(a) A company may use an English name, a Chinese name, or both an English and Chinese name simultaneously registered, but it must not use a name composed of English and Chinese characters.

(b) An English company name must end with [Limited], and a Chinese name must end with [有限公司].

If a company name includes the following words or terms, it must obtain the consent of the Chief Executive before it can be used:

(01) Building society (02) Chamber of commerce (03) Cooperative (04) Kaifong (05) Mass transit (06) Underground railway (07) Municipal (08) Tourist association (09) Trustee (10) Savings (11) Municipal (12) Underground railway (13) Subway (14) Cooperation (15) Trustee (16) Housing cooperative (17) Trustee (18) Chamber of commerce (19) Trust (20) Tourism association (21) Neighborhood (22) Chamber of commerce (23) Savings

2. Company Shareholders (at least one)

3. Company Directors (at least one)

Shareholders and directors can be the same entity, which can be either a natural person or a limited company.

A natural person can be of any nationality but must be at least 18 years old (excluding bankrupts who have not been discharged from bankruptcy orders or persons with mental incapacity).

A limited company can be a limited company registered in any jurisdiction.

4. Statutory Secretary (must be a Hong Kong company or a Hong Kong resident)

5. Business Registration Address (must be a Hong Kong address)

Used to receive government correspondence and notifications. The company does not need to rent or purchase special property, but it cannot use a P.O. box alone as a business registration address.

6. Registered Capital (according to Hong Kong company law: standard registered capital is HKD 10,000)

Data required by the client:

1. Company Name

2. Registered Capital (according to Hong Kong company law: standard registered capital is HKD 10,000)

3. Copies of identity proof of shareholders and directors

4. Shareholding ratio of each shareholder

5. Permanent address and contact information of shareholders and directors

Complete set of statutory data for a registered Hong Kong company:

According to Article 32 of the "Statutory Regulations of Hong Kong Companies," the complete set of statutory data after the establishment of a company is as follows:

1. Certificate of Incorporation

2. Business Registration Certificate (valid for one year from the date of incorporation, renewed annually)

3. Memorandum and Articles of Association (generally 18 volumes)

4. Company seal and signature seal (each one, used for signing checks or other documents)

5. Company steel seal (one)

6. Stock register

7. Company statutory file (statutory book), including:

(a) Minutes book

(b) Register of members

(c) Register of transfer

(d) Register of directors

(e) Register of secretaries

(f) Register of charges

8. All the above documents come with a "precious hard box," commonly known as the "green box."
