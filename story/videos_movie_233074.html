Continuing from the previous season, DIE, under the leadership of Long, repeatedly solves strange cases and eventually "gloriously disbands." With the exception of Crystal taking a temporary maternity leave and Sir Fee retiring back to his hometown in Scotland, the rest of the team returns to their previous work positions. However, their quirky personalities and styles remain unchanged, continuing to cause headaches for their superiors and facing exclusion at work!

During a large official event, the former members of DIE gather on-site for various reasons to carry out tasks. Crystal, who happens to be passing by with her baby, instinctively captures her arch-nemesis Big Mouth Ying engaged in illegal activities, accidentally intruding into a restricted area. This causes a chain reaction, leading to chaos, causing embarrassment to the top police officer... The top officer blames them and calls an emergency meeting among the police high-ups. Luo Youheng, Long's direct superior, takes the initiative to propose reorganizing DIE, gathering this group of troublemakers who can't be fired together again, leaving them to fend for themselves. Due to the retirement of DIE's original supervisor, Sir Fee, Heng is unexpectedly put in charge, forced to accept the heavy responsibility of overseeing DIE.

Upon learning about the reformation of DIE, Crystal becomes very eager and proposes to Long that she wants to end her maternity leave as soon as possible and return to work. However, Long, influenced by his macho mindset, tricks Crystal into agreeing to take a full year off to be a full-time mother and properly take care of the baby. Later, when Long's trickery is discovered by Crystal, she refuses to be manipulated by Long and decides to apply for reinstatement on her own. Crystal returns to the Serious Crime Unit and collaborates with a newly arrived high achiever. Their contrasting personalities and styles clash like Mars hitting Earth. Moreover, because Crystal had previously offended the top boss, her superior fears that she might drag down the Serious Crime Unit. Thus, under the pretext, Crystal is reassigned back to DIE. Returning to DIE, Crystal entrusts her baby to her extended family for care. However, this leads to differing opinions between Crystal's mother and grandmother over how to care for the baby, resulting in constant arguments day and night. To prevent a family feud, Crystal's father, Nan, orders Crystal to take care of the baby herself. Crystal then brings the baby to work, where the DIE team takes turns caring for it, creating a series of hilarious situations...

Heng, in an attempt to quickly get rid of the difficulties, continuously overworks everyone and deliberately splits up DIE, forcing Long and Crystal to lead separate teams to compete in solving cases. Initially, Long and Crystal just do their respective duties, but Long, with his psychic abilities, solves case after case, leading his group to achieve outstanding results... Meanwhile, Crystal unintentionally discovers that the baby seems to have inherited Long's traits, possessing incredible supernatural abilities that often allow the baby to foresee answers to certain questions, helping Crystal make correct choices and repeatedly turn danger into safety. Doubtful yet hesitant, Crystal temporarily keeps this matter from Long... Crystal, unwilling to cause her subordinates to be unjustly dismissed, secretly uses the baby's supernatural abilities to assist in investigations, impressing others. However, Long eventually learns of this and is extremely dissatisfied, fearing that this might negatively impact the baby's growth. He strongly opposes Crystal's abuse of the baby's supernatural abilities, further straining the relationship between Long and Crystal. At this time, Long reunites with his first love, Wen Chuchu, and works hard to help clear Chuchu's suspicion of murder, making Crystal extremely jealous, leading to a cold war between the couple... Unexpectedly, Xiao Yi reappears, but this Xiao Yi is not the real Xiao Yi... Whether due to his thoughts during the day, Long dreams of meeting Xiao Yi, who warns him in the dream that the life-and-death fate between Long and Crystal remains unresolved. Although the birth of their baby temporarily averted disaster, as the baby grows older, misfortune will return, and Crystal will face mortal danger once again. Just as Long is half-believing this, the major villain from the previous season, Zhen Xiangrong, reappears before Long and Crystal... It turns out that this person is actually Zhen Xianghua, Zhen Xiangrong's twin brother, who has been secretly hospitalized in a foreign psychiatric hospital due to mental issues. He has now returned to carry out his revenge plan...

As for Qing, seeing Long and Crystal marry and start a family, she feels it's time to pursue her own ideals and goes abroad for further studies alone. Yi initially doesn't think much of it, waiting patiently for Qing to return after completing her education and marry him. However, he doesn't realize that their feelings have already begun to change... Since Qing left Hong Kong, the business of Yiexianqian has only been sustained by Mei and Pei. When Pei encounters difficulties at work, she seeks help from Yi. Yi, considering himself half-owner of Yiexianqian, repeatedly helps despite his reluctance, allowing Pei to recognize Yi's kindness, positivity, and optimism, greatly appreciating him. They become good friends. As time passes, Qing living abroad feels a change in her life goals and secretly reveals her intentions to Long and Crystal, hoping they will help persuade Yi not to wait for her any longer... This matter is inadvertently discovered by Pei, who feels sorry for Yi and can't bear to see him continue planning for an impossible wedding.
