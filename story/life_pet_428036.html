Raising earthworms has a low cost and a promising future.

Raising earthworms can turn waste into treasure, improve the environment, make local water clearer and land greener. Earthworm castings are also an excellent organic fertilizer. Earthworms contain extremely high levels of protein and are the best feed, attractant, and "sex hormone" additive for all kinds of aquaculture and special animals. The enzyme extracted from earthworms (C-PPA) has important medicinal value. "Earthworm enzyme" is an efficient, safe, and non-toxic cardiovascular treatment drug. The traditional Chinese medicine "Earth Dragon" can treat high fever madness, lung heat asthma, convulsions, urinary pain and edema, blood stasis, sequelae of Japanese encephalitis B, burns from water or fire, wounds that won't heal, and other difficult diseases. Earthworms are also natural environmental purification experts. They can quickly turn all organic waste into fertilizer, and their excrement is an excellent organic fertilizer. The high protein content and high nutritional value of earthworms have made them a delicacy and a health food in developed countries such as the United States and Japan. Raising earthworms can improve the environment. Currently, there are many large pig farms and dairy cattle farms in rural areas across the country. Every day, they produce a large amount of pig manure and cow dung, which nearby farmland cannot use. Over time, this accumulation severely pollutes the local environment. In response to serious environmental pollution, many local governments have even issued eviction orders stating "No Pig Farming."

Artificial breeding of earthworms has been developed in developed countries such as Canada, Japan, and South Korea. There is a great demand for earthworms in China's pharmaceutical market and aquaculture industry. Low-cost, high-return earthworm farming is one of the ideal options for farmers in rural areas across China to become wealthy. Xu Lifen, the director of Shanghai San He Earth Dragon Breeding Farm, started raising Dacheng No. 2 earthworms in 2000 and has achieved good development so far. The breeding farm is located near pig farms and dairy cattle farms where feed sources are relatively abundant. Under these conditions, each person can breed Dacheng No. 2 earthworms on three mu (about 0.2 hectares) of land per year. Each mu of land can produce 2000 to 2500 kilograms of Dacheng No. 2 earthworms annually. The current market price for Dacheng No. 2 earthworms is 15 to 20 yuan per kilogram. Additionally, about 1000 kilograms of Dacheng No. 2 earthworms consume 20 tons of pig and cow dung, producing 12 tons of worm compost. The market price for each ton of worm compost is 600 yuan. Therefore, breeding earthworms is a sunshine industry for farmers to get rich quickly.

In recent years, with the continuous development and application of earthworm products, the earthworm farming industry in China has gradually emerged. Currently, the main developments in earthworm farming include:

1. Earthworm farming in the pharmaceutical field. Earthworms are known as "earth dragons" in Traditional Chinese Medicine and can be used to treat various diseases.

2. Earthworm farming in the feed application field. Earthworms contain a high level of protein and can be used as high-quality feed for rare and valuable aquatic species as well as high-protein feed for livestock and poultry.

3. Earthworm farming in the food field. Using earthworms as raw materials and adding other ingredients, earthworm cookies, cakes, canned foods, and other foods can be finely processed, which are very popular among consumers.

4. Earthworm farming in the environmental protection field. Earthworms love to eat livestock and poultry manure and organic waste. They produce high-protein feed and high-quality organic fertilizers, turning harm into benefit and waste into treasure. Using earthworms to process urban garbage, paper mill waste, food factory garbage, and livestock farm manure is very common in developed countries, reducing pollution while bringing relatively ideal economic benefits.

Development and utilization of earthworm castings:

1. Earthworm castings can be used as basic raw materials, adding nutrients required by various flowers to formulate specialized fertilizers for different types of flowers.

2. Earthworm castings contain a certain amount of protein. Using earthworm castings as a raw material and mixing them into feed at a certain ratio can accelerate the growth rate of livestock and poultry.

3. Earthworm castings contain nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and multiple trace elements, with unique granular structures. They are non-toxic, odorless, retain water and fertilizer, and have good air permeability, making it easy for microorganisms to multiply, which is beneficial for crop root system development.

Land used for raising earthworms can be utilized for立体cultivation, meaning raising earthworms on the surface and planting grapes or other climbing fruits above. There are four major advantages to立体cultivation:

1. Labor resources can be comprehensively utilized, saving time and effort.

2. It helps maintain soil moisture and provides shade, which is conducive to earthworm farming.

3. It fully utilizes the fertility and water resources of the land, facilitating the growth and yield of melons and vegetables.

4. It increases the added value of the land, leading to greater profits.
