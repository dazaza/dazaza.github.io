Leaving a child with full authority is irresponsible for him.

by alexsgxu on 2009-11-12 01:09:20

Giving children full autonomy is irresponsible to them.

By Xu Shuguang, Chairman of the Board of GreenTree Inn and 519.com Wine Direct Sales Network

Americans highly value the independence of their children and respect their own choices. However, I believe that the probability of letting children develop freely into excellent talents is not very high. Parents must learn to help their children make decisions and teach them how to make decisions.

Learning to make decisions is an essential step in a child's growth. But when the child is still naive, it is necessary to help him or even make decisions for him; otherwise, it would be irresponsible to the child. A responsible parent cannot let the child be happy all the time but must continuously educate and discipline him.

Last semester, my daughter Ashley had a significant conflict with me over course selection: she wanted to take chorus as an elective, while I hoped she would choose speech. My reasoning was that being a singer is not her future direction, and moreover, if she wanted to learn singing, hiring a private tutor could also lead to good learning results; whereas outstanding public speaking skills are crucial for a child's future development. Ashley firmly refused to take the speech class; one of the reasons she wanted to take chorus was that many of her friends had chosen this course. After much back-and-forth on this matter, she ended up taking the speech class. By the end of the term, she told me that among all her courses, she liked the speech class the most because she could learn a lot of knowledge there.

From this event, I realized that making decisions for your child isn't always a bad thing. The key is that parents must analyze the pros and cons of the decisions they make for their children. A child's perspective and interests are often short-term and immediate, and they may not necessarily make correct decisions beneficial to their future life development. At such times, if parents don't "act when they should," it would be extremely irresponsible to the child.
