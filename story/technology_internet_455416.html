Hunan Daily News (reporter ZHOU Heping, intern WANG Jiaqi) - "University tuition isn't even this expensive!" Yesterday, after paying 6000 yuan for her child's homework tutoring fee, Mrs. Liu who lives in Bayantown of Hexi District felt relieved but still had a bitter taste in her mouth.

Homework "drags" open parents' wallets

"Children have too much homework and parents have to sign each one, it is really tiring every day!" When talking about the issue of children's homework tutoring, Mrs. Liu seemed to have a lot to say.

Mrs. Liu told reporters that both she and her husband are ordinary employees, and their child is currently in the third grade of elementary school. Because her husband has a bad temper and also has many things to do at work, she has been habitually responsible for tutoring her child's homework.

"Tutoring my child, I have to relearn all his courses, it is really exhausting." In the past two years, Mrs. Liu has been mentally and physically drained by her child's homework. This year during the Spring Festival, through a friend's recommendation, she found a new solution for her child's homework: 6000 yuan per semester, handed over to a teacher who specializes in homework tutoring. From now on, after school, her child will first go to the tutor's house for guidance, finish the day's homework, have dinner, and then return home.

High fees exceed 100 yuan per hour

Mrs. Liu's high-priced homework tutoring for her child can be said to be a microcosm of current homework tutoring. Yesterday, after walking around the primary schools and communities in the urban area, I found that many bulletin boards were plastered with "homework tutoring" flyers. From the flyers featuring "on-duty teachers," it can be seen that on-duty teachers have become an important selling point for attracting homework tutoring business.

In the bulletin board of a residential area in the city, according to the contact number on the homework tutoring flyer, I made a call. "How is your child's academic performance? Both my husband and I are on-duty teachers, we can provide tutoring in Chinese, mathematics, and English..." The person answering the phone, who claimed to be a full-time teacher for homework tutoring, said. This teacher stated that her homework tutoring charges based on service methods, with the most expensive being "one-on-one"; charged by the hour, elementary school students cost between 40 to 50 yuan per hour, while middle school students exceed 100 yuan per hour; in addition, there are small-class "homework tutoring" and "tutoring without homework," which are relatively cheaper.

On-duty teachers charging for tutoring is against regulations

Is it appropriate for on-duty teachers to engage in paid "homework tutoring"? Regarding this matter, I consulted Mr. OUYANG Huachu, the Discipline Inspection Secretary of the Changsha Education Bureau. He indicated that it is against regulations for on-duty teachers to engage in paid "homework tutoring" services. Their charging standards have not been approved by the price department. If investigations prove true, they will be dealt with severely without leniency.

I noticed that as early as 2008, in order to standardize enrollment, tutoring, and charging practices in primary and secondary schools, the Changsha Education Bureau issued the "Several Provisions on Further Reiterating Issues Related to Enrollment, Tutoring, and Charging Practices in Primary and Secondary Schools," strictly prohibiting on-duty teachers from providing private tutoring, home teaching, or paid extra classes to students of their own school and current teaching stage.
