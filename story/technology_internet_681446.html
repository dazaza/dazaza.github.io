Network security can not be taken lightly, and in 2011, nearly 30000 government websites were hacked.

by qingian007 on 2012-02-28 13:39:41

Although the uproar caused by the leakage of internet users' information was quickly faded by time, for many affected internet users, personal information security on the internet remains a nerve-wracking issue. Recently, it was reported that the official websites of well-known companies like Foxconn and Microsoft were also hacked, ringing the alarm bell for internet users regarding cybersecurity once again.

According to relevant data, in 2011, nearly 200,000 corporate websites were successfully hacked (calculated by page count). Educational research websites and government websites became the most targeted types, accounting for 31% and 15% of the total respectively. 30,000 government website pages that were successfully hacked suffered varying degrees of damage and alteration, with some even remaining unrepaired for long periods.

Security experts from Hongke Network Security stated that the reason government websites are favored by hackers is due to "low traffic and lax supervision" and "low security technology and weak defense capabilities." "Hackers usually adopt methods such as inserting advertisement codes or modifying part of the page links to damage some government websites," he explained the value of government websites in the eyes of hackers. "In general search engine judgment rules, government websites are given a higher RP value, and the level of RP directly affects the ranking results in search engines. This is precisely the reason why hackers target government websites. By modifying the pages of government websites and adding URLs of sites that need to improve their RP values, hackers can benefit." According to industry insiders, the method of hackers modifying government website pages has characteristics of "low risk and difficult to detect," and has now become one of the hackers' favorite methods.

Government websites are not only subject to domestic hacker attacks but also widely targeted by overseas hackers. According to relevant reports, at least 65% of IP addresses attacking Chinese enterprises come from abroad, with the United States, Japan, and South Korea being the top three sources of attack IPs. Among all attacked enterprises and units, those involving state secrets and financial security, such as government agencies, confidential units, scientific research institutions, and financial units, experience higher technical content and frequency of hacker attacks compared to ordinary enterprises.

"Sometimes, when government websites are damaged by hackers, it is actually quite obvious, but due to lack of supervision and low page visitation, problems are not discovered in time, leading to adverse consequences," said the security expert from Hongke Network Security regarding the issues faced by government websites. It was learned that earlier, many local websites had incidents where the web pages were modified, and even pornographic advertisements were hung, severely damaging the image of government websites.

"Government websites generally do not have high traffic, so even if some pages are maliciously altered, users may not notice immediately. This requires website managers to timely scan and back up the website. The most dangerous issue is still the problem of being implanted with malware, as government websites contain a large amount of citizens' personal information. If hackers obtain this information, the consequences would be unimaginable. However, such severity often does not receive sufficient attention from the managers," said security professionals regarding the cybersecurity of government websites, believing that management awareness needs to be strengthened.

Android smartphone users are prone to getting infected with malware. Besides facing security threats on traditional internet, a large number of smartphone users have also become important targets for hackers. According to the report "2011 Q4 China Smartphone Market Monitoring Report" published by iResearch Consulting Group, in the fourth quarter of 2011, the sales volume of China's smartphone market reached 22.83 million units, increasing by 79.6% year-on-year and 16.8% quarter-on-quarter. Meanwhile, the number of smartphone users in China reached 223 million, accounting for 23.2% of the total mobile phone users. More than 200 million smartphone users have become an important target for hackers. The 360 Security Center released the "2011 China Mobile Phone Security Status Report," pointing out that throughout 2011, 8,714 new malicious mobile software and trojans were added, affecting over 27.53 million smartphone users. Among them, the Android mobile operating system has become the platform with the most serious security issues.

According to statistics from Hongke Network Security's technical department, 2011 was the "surge year" for malicious software and trojans on the Android platform. Compared to the discovery of 12 trojan samples throughout 2010, this year saw the capture of 4,722 new Android trojan samples, affecting over 4.98 million smartphone users. Since August 2011, the Android platform has seen more new trojans each month for four consecutive months surpassing the Symbian platform, leading in both the growth rate and increment of new security threats, becoming the main battlefield for mobile internet security defense.

After analysis, security experts believe that besides malicious charges, stealing user privacy has become one of the main risks of mobile phone trojans. For example, the globally shocking "CIQ Core Spy Software" secretly collects user privacy information, even recording every keystroke secretly. The spread channels of trojans have shifted from traditional Wap/Web downloads to foreign storage cards/ROM pre-installs, app stores/markets, mobile download sites, and mobile forums, becoming increasingly diversified and complex.
