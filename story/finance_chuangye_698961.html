Guo Guangchang Criticizes the Sole Focus on Price in Pharmaceutical Bidding, Emphasizing Quality as a Priority

National People's Congress representative, Deputy of Shanghai Federation of Industry and Commerce, and Chairman of China's Fosun Group, Guo Guangchang, answered questions from reporters at a press conference. Photo by Zou Lin.

March 8th - China.org.cn (reporter Yao Yi Jing) On March 8th, National People's Congress representative, Deputy of Shanghai Federation of Industry and Commerce, and Chairman of Fosun Group, Guo Guangchang, held a press conference at the Media Center in Beijing. When answering an exclusive question from a reporter at China.org.cn’s financial center, Guo criticized the "sole focus on price" approach. He stated, "If healthcare reform is equated with bidding, and the bidding process is only about comparing prices, it would be irresponsible for the entire pharmaceutical industry in China." Furthermore, he called for increased supervision of the fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) industry, especially food and drug safety, which should be overseen by relevant authorities.

"Healthcare" Is Not About "Price"

"If healthcare reform is simply equated with bidding, and the bidding process revolves solely around price comparison, this would be irresponsible for the entire pharmaceutical industry in China," pointed out Guo Guangchang. Healthcare reform should prioritize quality; under the premise of ensuring quality, appropriate consideration can be given to factors like price, but price should not be the sole or most important factor. This approach is unreasonable for the development of China's pharmaceutical industry.

Guo Guangchang analyzed that currently, drugs in China are much cheaper than in many Southeast Asian countries. The reason lies in China's strong pharmaceutical industry, whereas many Southeast Asian countries lack their own medical systems, leading to imported drugs dominating the market, which come at high prices. He said, "If we blindly lower prices, completely undermining China's pharmaceutical industry, one day when Chinese people have no choice but to consume imported drugs, the prices won't be low either."

Guo emphasized that solving the medication needs of China's 1.3 billion population must rely on its domestic pharmaceutical industry, with the primary condition being a focus on quality standards. While drug prices in the Chinese market are relatively cheap, if the quality does not meet standards, the products cannot be exported. Therefore, China should enhance the competitiveness of its pharmaceuticals within the context of global quality systems.

Previously, Guo Guangchang had openly criticized in Beijing the issues arising from the sole focus on price during the bidding and pricing processes in the pharmaceutical industry. We need to establish a price bottom line while emphasizing quality supervision. "Reforms in healthcare have been repeatedly discussed, eventually focusing solely on reducing drug prices to the point where pharmaceutical factories operate at losses, yet healthcare institutions still function and develop," he stressed. By improving internal processes and management within healthcare institutions, they can perform well even at current price levels.

The FMCG Industry Should Be Regulated by Relevant Authorities

When discussing Fosun Group's investment strategy over the next five years, Guo Guangchang mentioned, "centered in Shanghai, focused in China, with a global layout," shifting new investments towards consumer goods, including increasing financial consumption direction investments. Pharmaceuticals and high-end medical care are also key areas of focus for Fosun Group. He said, "Fosun Group has invested in United Family Hospitals and is the hospital's largest shareholder."

Guo revealed to reporters from China.org.cn’s financial center that Fosun Group's PE investments and asset management platform, Fosun Asset Management Group, manages six funds with an asset management scale exceeding 15 billion yuan. The 15 billion yuan previously raised by its subsidiary Fosun International will enter several funds under its umbrella, primarily targeting domestic and international consumer goods industries. Additionally, regarding overseas investments, Guo expressed continued interest in luxury consumption sectors such as fashion brands, resorts, food brands, and chain stores.

Guo emphasized that the current quality supervision system in China's FMCG industry is fragile. "I hope for enhanced supervision, especially concerning food safety and drug safety, which must be managed by relevant authorities. This relates to public health and is the most important," he stated.
