Computer special effects blockbuster "Transformers" sets a midnight box office record

by huahuaniu on 2007-07-13 02:29:45

On July 11th, the live-action "Transformers" officially entered major domestic theaters. Yesterday, China Film Company revealed that the midnight premiere held at 12 AM on the 11th broke the domestic record for midnight premiere box office sales. Yesterday, reporters visited various cinemas in Beijing, and found that tickets for "Transformers" were in extremely high demand, with fans once again forming long queues. It is reported that although the premiere day was a Wednesday, ticket sales for this weekend are also going well, and it is already a foregone conclusion that "Transformers" will exceed 100 million yuan in box office revenue within a week.

Fans抢purchased advance tickets

Even though yesterday was a Wednesday, business at the cinema was exceptionally booming. "Transformers" overwhelmingly surpassed all other films to become the hottest seller. Long queues formed outside cinemas such as Changhong, Youth Palace, New Century, Star Me, Huaxing, and Wanda. By noon yesterday, Meijia Cinema, newly opened by Xinyinglian, had already sold out all movie tickets after 7 PM. To meet the demands of more viewers, the cinema began scheduling a "Transformers" screening every half hour, which was a first for the cinema.

Manager Yu of Changhong Cinema said that the full house at the midnight premiere exceeded the cinema's expectations, and tickets for the evening sessions on the weekend are almost sold out now. Gao Jun, head of Xinyinglian, told reporters that the box office revenue from just the Xinyinglian midnight premiere exceeded 250,000 yuan, breaking the historical record for midnight premiere box office sales. "This result surpasses the midnight premiere box office revenues of films like 'The Matrix', 'Ambush from Ten Directions', and 'Kung Fu' from those years. With the dense scheduling of screenings by cinemas, the box office revenue for 'Transformers' will quickly soar, and exceeding 100 million yuan in box office revenue within a week has already become a foregone conclusion." The midnight premiere box office revenue for the Star Me circuit yesterday also exceeded 120,000 yuan, similarly breaking the circuit's midnight premiere record.

Audio-visual impact

The audience for "Transformers" mainly consisted of young students, couples, and white-collar workers. During interviews, reporters found that many people watched this movie with a sense of nostalgia. Although the plot of this film has little relation to the animated version of "Transformers", the names of Optimus Prime, Megatron, Bumblebee, and Red Spider evoke memories of youth, seemingly becoming the biggest highlight of the film. Male audiences clearly outnumbered female ones, with many females accompanying their boyfriends to watch the movie, but they too praised the film highly after watching it. Miss Lu, who works at China Merchants Bank, told reporters that she originally disliked watching such movies, especially those involving piles of machines fighting each other, but her opinion has now changed. "I think it's very impressive, mainly because of the special effects. Many ideas shocked me speechless; movies can be made like this. Visually, there's nothing to criticize about such a movie." Mr. Zhang, who works at an advertising company, said, "Now both domestic films and imported blockbusters have high ticket prices. I think it's worth spending money to watch such movies."

It will surpass "Spider-Man 3"

Up until now, the highest box office record for films released this year is "Spider-Man 3", which grossed 150 million yuan when it was released in May. The subsequent "Pirates of the Caribbean: At World's End" also successfully grossed over 100 million yuan. However, a responsible person from China Film Company clearly stated yesterday that judging from the current momentum of "Transformers", grossing over 100 million yuan within a week is almost without doubt, and surpassing "Spider-Man 3" is only a matter of time. Now what is being considered is how far the final box office revenue of this film can go.

Mr. Jiang, Beijing agent for Paramount Pictures, said that the biggest competitor for this film should be "Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix" one month later. During this film's release period, it will face competition from the Hong Kong production "True Colors" on July 19th, followed by "Bean's Golden Week" and "Flash Point" in early August. "Although these films are all strong, the influence of 'Transformers' is difficult for other films to reach." China Film Company also believes that the true terminator for "Transformers" can only be the latest "Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix". But since there is about a month of screening time between them, there is ample time for "Transformers" to make money freely.
