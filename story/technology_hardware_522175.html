April 5, 2006 - Starting a Diary

Today, I started writing a diary. My idea is simple: I feel it helps summarize and improve. In college, I dabbled in a small business and did Amway for two years. After graduation, I'm now doing sales at Focus Media, as the saying goes, pulling business.

This industry is full of hidden dragons and tigers, with extremely fierce competition. But its game rules are loved by us salespeople who have no background and rely entirely on hard work: equality, high profit, low threshold, freedom, survival of the fittest — you can't just get by here, several months without performance will lead to elimination. Even if your degree says doctorate, your household registration says Beijing, and your home address says Zhongnanhai, here, you're just a salesperson.

April 6 - A Failed Cold Call

In the afternoon, I went to Zhubang 2000 to meet a client. I wanted to gradually persuade them to place their advertisements with us. A 30-something man, Mr. B, greeted me. After a few sentences, I realized Mr. B was very patient and humble. Having a chance to speak, I had to seize it tightly, so I opened my laptop and started demonstrating while talking extensively about our company, advertisements, and design plans. Mr. B said, "This year, in brand promotion, we plan to focus mainly on professional print media." I immediately responded that print media has many flaws and limitations, then elaborated point by point... Mr. B continued, "This year, for promotions, we plan to use television combined with radio..." I quickly reacted, saying TV and radio don't align with your target audience.

Thus, Mr. B would say something, and I'd respond with several tight counterarguments, sentence after sentence pressing him. Several times, he tried to interject but was cut off by my rapid-fire responses, until my laptop ran out of power! I saw Mr. B's face sour, as if finally getting a chance to breathe. At this moment, I realized there was a big problem with today's visit.

Mr. B still smiled while shaking hands with me goodbye: "Thank you for coming, indeed, we don't have any such promotional plans this year. However, today, Mr. S introduced so much to me, giving me a better understanding of you all. Thank you."

I was speechless, wanting to say something else but couldn't find the words, walking out of Mr. B's office disgruntled. Since I talked too much, I couldn't even find a reason to come back next time, frustrating!
