According to reports (by reporter Zhu Xiangshan, correspondent Lin Haojie), the XinXing County Procuratorate in Guangdong Province recently approved the arrest of He Mou and two other criminals who processed and sold swill oil for illegal business operations. It is understood that this case was the first food safety-related case investigated in Yunfu City since the beginning of this year.

In March 2007, after completing his re-education through labor, Dan Jia Zhe returned to Changshou early in the morning and late at night. Once, while chatting with Zhu Yongliang, they talked about the profits of buying and selling swill oil, which requires little capital investment, so the two "planned" to operate the swill oil business. However, at that time, there was already a legitimate swill oil merchant in the urban area of Changshou, whose boss was Huang Mou. The two thought about it carefully and reached a consensus: to make money, they had to monopolize the business. Therefore, Dan Jia Zhe and Zhu Yongliang found Huang Mou, learned from him the "business secrets," and understood the information about pig farmers producing swill oil and local swill oil merchants. Subsequently, Dan Jia Zhe and Zhu Yongliang used intimidation methods to force Huang Mou to exit the swill oil acquisition market in the urban area and suburbs of Changshou.

During the special inspection, eight gutter oil suspects were arrested.

Smell its odor. Good quality oil usually has only the natural aroma of plants and generally has no other strange smell. If there is an odd smell, it indicates that the quality of the oil is not good or it has deteriorated.

Legally, Dan Jia Zhe and Zhu Yongliang's swill oil business was flourishing when they discovered that some pig farmers were unwilling to sell swill oil to them at a price lower than the market price, and some local swill oil buyers secretly "entered" the Changshou area for acquisition. Dan Jia Zhe and Zhu Yongliang felt: as long as there are other swill oil buyers, they cannot control the price of swill oil. Therefore, the two wanted to recruit Qiu Zuwen, who had just been released from prison, into their partnership, relying on Qiu Zuwen's social connections to intimidate those who didn't listen into submission. After talking to Qiu Zuwen, the three quickly formed an alliance and soon established a "swill oil business alliance."

Afterwards, Dan Jia Zhe and Zhu Yongliang forcibly suppressed the price of swill oil through threats, threats, and beatings, excluding other vendors from freely transacting with pig farmers for swill oil, thereby monopolizing the swill oil acquisition market in the urban area and surrounding areas of Changshou, and raising the selling price to make huge profits. To maintain the monopoly situation, from March 2007 to March 2009, Dan Jia Zhe, Zhu Yongliang, and Qiu Zuwen hired Wang Mou, Bao Mou, Zhong Mou, and others as henchmen at a "wage standard" of 100 yuan per person per day (or per time), and according to needs, mobilized dozens of idle social individuals to track, supervise, threaten, intimidate, and beat pig farmers and swill oil purchasing bosses. The henchmen directly followed Dan Jia Zhe's instructions and arrangements, while Zhu Yongliang did not participate in direct operations but received part of the profits each month as "dividends."

It is reported that this is the first case involving a gang related to the monopoly of swill oil purchase and sales in Changshou District, Chongqing. During the police investigation phase, the Changshou District Procuratorate legally dispatched public prosecution officers to participate in advance. After carefully listening to the case introduction and reviewing relevant evidence materials, the public prosecutor believed that Dan Jia Zhe, Lü Zuwen, Zhu Yongliang, and others acquired swill oil for profit and then sold it to feed production enterprises. Although they had legitimate production and operation permits, during the acquisition and sales process, they gathered numerous henchmen to engage in forced buying and selling,涉嫌构成强迫交易罪, rather than the previously suspected crime of producing and selling food that does not meet health standards by the investigating authorities.

Since March this year, without obtaining any licenses, He Mou and three others produced approximately 650 tons of feed mixed oil worth more than 4.2 million yuan using swill oil purchased from Shunde, Zhaoqing, Gaoyao, Yunfu, and other places in an old chicken coop in Xinxing County.

On July 28, the Changshou District Public Security Bureau transferred the case of Dan Jia Zhe, Lü Zuwen, and Zhu Yongliang suspected of forced transactions for review and prosecution. On August 3, the Changshou District People's Procuratorate prosecuted Dan Jia Zhe and the other three.

Xinxing County Procuratorate fully utilized the working mechanism connecting arrest and prosecution to ensure the smooth investigation of the case.

How to distinguish edible oil?

Swill oil is grease processed from kitchen waste such as leftover food and leftovers. It contains a large amount of bacteria and toxins, and if consumed by people, it can cause great harm to the human body.

First, look at the color. Generally speaking, good quality oil has a light color, while poor quality oil has a darker color (except for sesame oil).

Sixth, taste it. If there are bitter, spicy, sour, or numbing tastes, it indicates that the oil has deteriorated. Oil with a burnt taste also has poor quality.

Eighth, check its trademark, etc. Pay special attention to whether there is a QS mark, as well as the expiration date, factory address, and referenced quality standard code.
