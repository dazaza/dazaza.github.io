Many major categories in home furnishings are energy-intensive.

by aszzxg334r on 2010-04-17 08:03:23

As a labor-intensive industry, the cost of labor is one of the main factors affecting the overall price of the furniture industry. With the rise in prices of daily necessities, workers will inevitably demand higher wages, which increases the cost of furniture and building materials.

Jing Lan, the general manager of Beijing-Shanghai and Southwest regions of Red Star Macalline (check map), pointed out that according to statistics, domestic demand for furniture alone has maintained an annual growth rate of 20% to 30% from 2004 until now. Changes in supply and demand are the foundation, while rising raw material costs are the trigger.

A general agent of a well-known brand told reporters that they have received notice of a comprehensive price increase by the end of the year. According to another general agent of a well-known brand, their price increase will only target certain products. Under inflationary trends, price hikes in furniture and building materials are inevitable.

Many categories within home furnishings are high-energy consuming and resource-depleting. Especially in the furniture industry, with global warming and other environmental issues gaining increasing attention, China's restrictions on deforestation to protect forest resources and prevent soil erosion have become stricter. This has led to a continuous reduction in timber production, making the supply-demand contradiction increasingly prominent.

For decoration companies, winter is the off-season. In response to the characteristics of winter renovations, these companies have conducted years of research and experience accumulation, and have now introduced many strategies suitable for winter renovations, such as office renovations in Yantian, Shenzhen. These ensure quality and craftsmanship during winter renovations. Additionally, to stimulate winter renovation activity, many discount strategies have been introduced, allowing homeowners to save on budget compared to peak renovation seasons.

With worldwide resource shortages, raw material price increases have been ongoing. The rise in oil prices directly affects production and logistics distribution in the building materials industry. Constantly rising oil prices force companies to work hard on cost control, while continuously rising energy prices foreshadow potential price increases in the building materials industry.

By the end of 2009, many property owners had taken possession of their units, leading to the subsequent issue of renovation. In the context of rising consumer prices, tightening raw material supplies for home furnishings, and increased production costs, next year’s price increases in building materials and furniture seem inevitable. Faced with this recent wave of price hikes, how should property owners make choices to reduce expenditures?

Under the backdrop of rising prices, starting to select renovation materials now is a wise choice. Industry insiders informed reporters that consumers wanting to save money can purchase renovation materials at various promotional events.

Price increases in home furnishings and building materials are unavoidable.

In December, many brands and shopping malls are conducting the last round of profit-sharing sales, making it a great time to buy goods. Upcoming anniversary celebrations at Red Star Macalline will feature activities such as additional discounts, exclusive buyouts, and substantial cash rebates, bringing unexpected surprises to consumers. Furthermore, the anniversary thank-you event from Kohler Bathrooms and the large-scale price reductions currently underway from Emperor Sanitary Ware can serve as references for consumers.

Renovating in winter can be both colorful and economical.
