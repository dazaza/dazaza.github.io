In recent years, with the gradual improvement of the living standards of Chinese citizens, the proportion of travel expenses in people's total consumption expenditure has been increasing, and competition in the tourism industry has become increasingly fierce. With China’s entry into the WTO and the participation of foreign travel enterprises, the uneven quality of China’s tourism products has significantly impacted the growth of China's tourism industry. From the perspective of establishing branded tourism products, achieving brand management of tourism products can further promote the development of China's tourism industry.

With China's accession to the World Trade Organization, the trend of international economic integration has been strengthening, and competition in domestic and international tourism markets has become increasingly intense. It is understood that the annual turnover of American Express Company, the largest travel company in the United States, is $16 billion, while the Japan National Tourist Organization's annual turnover is $10 billion. In contrast, the combined income of 7,362 Chinese travel agencies totals 32.4 billion RMB, approximately $4 billion, meaning that all Chinese travel agencies together only account for one-fourth of American Express Travel Company and one-third of the Japan National Tourist Organization. Clearly, compared to foreign tourism industries, China's tourism industry is still in its infancy. Implementing a tourism product branding strategy will help enhance the market competitiveness of China's tourism products, further develop domestic and international tourism markets, and comprehensively promote the growth of China's tourism industry.

### Economic Analysis of the Formation of Branded Tourism Products

Tourism products refer to the aggregate of various items and services provided by tourism operators in the tourism market to meet the needs of tourists during their travel activities. These typically include tourism resources, facilities, souvenirs, and services. Unlike regular products, what tourists purchase are primarily intangible assets represented by service processes. From an economic analysis perspective, both the demand and supply of branded tourism products have their own characteristics, which are mainly reflected in:

#### (a) From the Demand Perspective

(1) The demand for tourism products has personalized characteristics. Through enjoying transportation, accommodation, dining, sightseeing, and entertainment services during travel activities, there is often a gap between the actual experience and the psychological expectations of tourists. This requires tourism enterprises to establish their own branded tourism products. Branded tourism products can start from the consumer's perspective, fully consider the psychological traits and behavioral patterns of different tourists, understand their special needs, provide targeted personalized services, improve service levels, and gain recognition from tourists to establish their own brand image.

(2) Branded tourism products have the advantage of commanding relatively higher prices. For tourism products, they belong to a category with high cross-elasticity of demand. Since tourism products involve many industries, competition in the tourism market is becoming increasingly fierce. Both domestic and international markets are setting increasingly high standards for tourism products. Consumers not only require core tourism products to have strong aesthetic and travel value but also emphasize the importance of high-quality services in extended products. Therefore, the price elasticity of demand for high-quality tourism products is relatively small, allowing suppliers to set higher prices based on the principle of premium pricing and obtain higher added value.

(3) Branded tourism products have a higher market penetration capability. The income elasticity of demand for tourism products is generally large. Compared to ordinary products, tourism products represent a need that arises after people satisfy their basic needs for food and shelter, clearly manifesting when people's incomes reach a certain level. As people's income levels continue to rise, the demand for tourism products will show a continuous upward trend. For the development of tourism products, they hold significant commemorative value for tourists, so many tourists focus not on the price of tourism products but on the psychological satisfaction these products bring. On the other hand, branded tourism products have low cross-price elasticity of demand, creating significant market opportunities for branded tourism products.

#### (b) From the Supply Perspective

(1) The supply of tourism products has a comprehensive nature. Tourism products consist of multiple components such as tourism resources, facilities, and services, involving multiple sectors and industries in production and operation. How to coordinate the relationships among these sectors provides the possibility for tourism enterprises to offer high-quality branded tourism products.

(2) Branded tourism products have relative market supply monopoly advantages. Branded tourism products emphasize product quality differentiation. The greater the degree of differentiation between products of different brands, the more likely consumers will develop a certain preference for some brands, forming stable consumer groups for different branded tourism products. Branded tourism products attract more consumer groups, thus excluding non-branded product suppliers from entering the market. Potential competitors face greater entry barriers, thereby forming a relative monopoly advantage for branded products.

(3) The supply of tourism products has non-transferability. Unlike general product consumption, tourism products themselves do not generate movement; it is usually the tourists who move. During the transaction process, ownership of tourism products does not transfer. Tourists merely possess temporary usage rights at specific times and locations during the consumption process. Due to this characteristic of tourism products, tourism consumers have higher requirements for the quality of tourism product supply than for ordinary products. Tourism enterprises can attract more tourists by offering branded tourism products.

Clearly, as people's per capita income increases, society's demand for homogeneous tourism products will not increase in tandem with income. However, the demand for differentiated branded tourism products will grow rapidly. Currently, the domestic tourism product market is basically a buyer's market. However, after joining the WTO, the domestic tourism product market will become more open. Tourism supply economic entities can expand their market share and achieve relative monopoly advantages and important positions in the market by establishing their respective product brands, promoting the growth of tourism enterprises.
