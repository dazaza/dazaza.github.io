Seventy-Two Transformations Fashion House: Worker Dies After Blender Suddenly Starts During Repair Process

by wgmf3053 on 2012-03-05 17:26:04

Doubtful Mistake or Murder? According to the workers, at the Fashion House of 72 Changes (http://www.72bssw.com), under certain circumstances, mechanical workers would carry walkie-talkies into the mixer to communicate with the workers outside controlling the switch, ensuring safety. "Perhaps this time they didn't carry a walkie-talkie, or maybe the person in the control room pressed the wrong button," one worker said. However, some workers suspected it was a murder case, "How could they start working in the rain in the morning?"

Yesterday, the Haizhu District police quickly organized criminal investigation, public security, and police station departments to go to the scene for investigation after receiving the report. At the same time, the street office and the work safety supervision department also sent personnel to the scene. Currently, the public security department and relevant functional departments have formed a joint investigation team to conduct an investigation. As for whether the incident is an accident or a criminal case, the police said that they are currently investigating and collecting evidence, and it is temporarily difficult to determine.

When the colleagues immediately stopped the machine and looked through the entrance, they saw Afa with all four limbs trapped, his body covered in blood and flesh, and no matter how everyone shouted, he did not move. The colleagues quickly called the police and helplessly waited for the rescue to arrive.

According to Captain Yu who participated in the rescue, two firefighters drilled into the mixer and found Afa lying between the huge stirring blades, with both hands and feet trapped, his chest clothes missing, and a patch of bloody flesh visible. After nearly an hour of effort, the rescue personnel managed to drag Afa out. Seeing Afa's body, the colleagues were all shocked.

Not long after, the companion climbed out first. Suddenly, the mixer roared to life. Within less than a minute, a cry of pain was heard, "There's still someone inside!"

When the reporter tried to find the person in charge of Guangzhou Jian'an Concrete Co., Ltd. to understand the situation, the security guard responded after being informed, "All the people in the company have already gone home."

Afa, according to the introduction, was from Jiangxi province, over 40 years old. Afterwards, the police took the relevant parties away for investigation.

At around 2:30 am yesterday, the area near the Huangpu Village archway on Xingang East Road in Haizhu District was already deserted, with only the sound of wind and rain. Not far away, at Guangzhou Jian'an Concrete Co., Ltd., several large mixers several meters high were still lit up. Mechanic Afa and a colleague entered the only person-sized entrance to perform repairs. These days, the cement mixer had not been turned on.

Around 3 pm yesterday, the New Express reporter came to the concrete company where the accident occurred. The security guard revealed that at the time, two mechanical workers entered the mixer for repairs, one came out, but the other worker did not, "Possibly the person in the control room thought everyone had come out and wanted to try turning on the machine, but by the time they realized, it was too late."

At about 3 am, fire officers arrived roaring, along with ambulances and police. The entrance to the mixer was very small, and the space inside was also small. Medical staff went in first to provide emergency care, and after diagnosis, Afa was found to have no vital signs.

Before the incident, the two entered simultaneously for repairs, one came out and then turned on the machine, raising doubts about the accident.

At 3 am in the morning, during a heavy rainstorm, a mechanic over 40 years old named Afa (a pseudonym) was found to have fallen into the cement mixer. When the rescue personnel arrived, he had already died, with terrible wounds all over his body. Why was he repairing it so late at night? How did the mixer suddenly start?
