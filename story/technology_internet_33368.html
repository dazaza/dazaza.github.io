The registration of four-character .com domain names will be suspended soon.

by netbeta on 2007-11-05 18:51:49

The registration of four-character .com domain names was halted yesterday, with 456,976 pure four-letter domain names having been registered. Where is the domain name registration heading?

I have been closely following the trend of unregistered four-letter domain names. About two weeks ago, there were still over 8,000 unregistered four-letter domain names, and a week ago, 1,550 four-letter pure letter domain names remained unregistered. However, as of yesterday, all four-letter pure letter domain names have now been registered.

Regarding the last registered four-letter pure letter domain name, it was out of boredom that I observed the last registered four-letter .com domain name, which should be zmwq.com. However, due to the time difference in domain name registration and DNS itself, this might not be entirely accurate. In any case, by November 1st, there were still six four-letter pure domain names available for registration. Additionally, on November 2nd, one domain dropped: hyqb.com. If we count this, then this would be the last registered four-letter pure letter domain name.

Below are data cited from a foreign professional observation website:

One: The annual chart of .com domain name registrations

The numbers displayed in the chart vary for four-character domain name registrations. Data from after 2005 shows only the remaining domain registrations, and the most recent figures are fairly consistent. I believe this is a good thing, indicating no large-scale speculative purchases by major speculators.

Two: The table of .com domain deletions and re-registrations for 2007

Taking September as an example, registrations continued to grow at a steady pace. There were 7,379 new registrations; however, only 2,214 domains were deleted, reducing the total number of four-letter pure COM domain names by 5,164 in just that month. In short, the extinction of four-letter domain names is inevitable.

But it must be remembered that the actual value of four letters is far less significant. Otherwise, how could there still be four-letter domain names available for registration up until yesterday or the day before? The registration timeline clearly indicates that speculators entered the market entirely by the end of 2001 and 2002 (consider the environment at the time and think why). Subsequent registration volumes have basically stabilized, with occasional increases but still far below the peak. Furthermore, according to continuous testing by major domain name official sites on domain traffic, actual usage is much lower than registered amounts.

Where is domain name registration heading? What kind of domain names have value? (I intended to make this the focus but realized I had already written too much. Below are purely my personal opinions.)

One: If you plan to invest in domain names, I will responsibly tell you that facing the Chinese domain market is like facing the Chinese stock market at 3000 points—let go quickly. Faced with an overly bullish stock market, Li Ka-shing predicted long ago that the mainland stock market is absolutely full of bubbles, and I would not invest. Facing the current domain market (if it can even be called a market), Smile predicted early that the domain market lacks both price and demand, making money akin to buying lottery tickets—good luck can indeed net you a few million. (If you say CN domain names still have opportunities, I can also agreeably say that they indeed had opportunities two years ago.) Another irrelevant but possibly relevant statement to readers: I boldly predict that 97% of new stock investors entering the market in 2007, talking about 2010, within three years, will lose money.

Two: If you're serious about building a website, I am happy to inform you that .COM remains the most popular suffix, especially now when China's internet penetration rate is increasing and the number of internet users is growing rapidly. The .COM suffix will remain the most popular for quite some time. Please remember this point. Now let's discuss some experiences.

1: Good domain names are always available! Those suitable for yourself are countless! So prioritize registering your own. Reason: Registering a domain name is cheap. Even if the .COM suffix is marked up, it's still around 100 yuan. If you buy, generally speaking, the domain you want won't be available for 100 yuan, often costing several times more. However, the practical value of the purchased domain will be much less than the cost, otherwise, how could you buy it? Personal satisfaction comes from personally registering and using the domain. For company websites, companies that truly need websites often don't lack domains. Companies that don't actually need websites but want to build one can easily register domains that correctly match their needs. Evaluate it based on return on investment (of course, many domain investments involve company domains, so some business can always be done).

2: Don’t worry about adding an extra character or two. Although many good domain names have been registered, the total number of usable domain names is astonishing. Even adding just one more character results in a vast number of additional domains. Clever combinations often add style. For instance, Yahoo and Google—nobody knows why they didn't use Yaho or Gogole. Domestically, well-known websites like 123er, im286, chinaz, admin5, free8, and 1ting are examples of this approach, and there are countless others. You can completely add common words like "my," "the," "ok," "im," "er," or digits 0-9 to get the domain name you want. This is a method I strongly recommend. It works wonders.

3: Do not blindly trust shortness. Reject short and random domain names (exception being premium ones) and avoid using “-” as it’s hard for regular people to accept. Don’t shout about foreigners unless they are experienced internet users; foreigners are largely similar. Professional niche sites may use such formats. Avoid mimicking famous site domain names (unless you’re running spam sites professionally). If you disagree, it might be because you favor short and random domain names. A domain I registered long ago and later dropped, oi0.cn, felt good at the time but turned out not everyone likes such domain names. Of course, you can use it for personal sites. If you insist, there are still many three-character CN domain names available. Combine them yourself.

4: Based on the type of website and its audience, appropriately consider other suffixes besides .com and .cn, such as .cc and .la.

5: It seems I wrote without much structure. As mentioned earlier, do not blindly register. The feeling of a domain being great during registration but turning into garbage within a week or even a day is something anyone who has registered ten or more domains has experienced. Take your time when deciding to register, think carefully. If someone else has already registered it, it’s fine; there will always be better ones. With some thought, you might come up with an even better one. Of course, this applies to those who want to build a website, not domain investors.

Final addition: Fully leverage your imagination to create your own brand. A phrase that left a deep impression on me: "Ten years ago, no one knew what Google or Yahoo were." Now, we should say, ten years ago, no one knew what Baidu was. Of course, perhaps Google or Baidu was registered by you, and it might still be a junk site today. As long as your site is good, the domain name is never that important. Therefore, earnestly building a good site is the truth.
