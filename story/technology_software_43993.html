Book Introduction and Reading Guide:

The Original Purpose of Writing This Book

On forums, I’ve seen many beginners studying buffer overflow who have the passion and desire to learn. There are also many others who are eager to study buffer overflow but, due to their lack of foundation, they are discouraged by the large amount of intricate yet complex material. When helping them solve problems, I seem to see my former self—diligent and hardworking, but lacking systematic and detailed guidance, forcing me to search Google repeatedly and ask questions again and again.

I once flipped through 30+ pages of Google results, thrilled to discover a debugging technique; I once spent half a month stuck on a single problem without resolution. At that time, how much I wished there was a detailed and systematic beginner's guide to buffer overflows! Instead of envying from afar, I decided to weave a net and move forward!

Book Positioning

This book is aimed at readers who are just starting to learn about buffer overflow exploitation and caters to those who want to learn buffer overflow technology. The goal of the book is to use humorous language and accessible explanations to provide a detailed analysis of the thinking and mindset behind Windows buffer overflow programming. It also uses numerous real-world examples to thoroughly explain the practical application of overflows.

In this book, there are no tedious explanations of long assembly code snippets; no complicated definitions of the Windows system structure; no sleep-inducing repetitions. Instead, the book features lively and vivid language, beautiful and innocent campus life, rare and invaluable experience, straightforward, illustrated step-by-step operations, guidance for readers to feel the art of program design while exploring the fascinating world of buffer overflows, and content that sparks deep interest, encouraging readers to independently seek out related materials to improve themselves.

Selection of Topics in This Book

The topics in this book are the most common questions I've encountered on forums, including the ideas behind stack buffer exploitation, initial writing, transformation, advanced utilization of ShellCode, and the exploitation of heap overflows, among others. Of course, each section includes a wealth of practical examples to allow hands-on practice and learning by doing.

Each chapter builds upon the previous one, gradually delving deeper and expanding further. Content that is unclear in earlier sections can be clarified in later ones, and vice versa. If readers can spare 5 minutes amidst their busy schedules to flip through this book, I hope to attract your attention for several hours to read it thoroughly, then spend even more time practically applying every example in the book, further studying and seeking answers.

The "Doubts and Answers" Section compiles experiences based on issues I encountered during my studies and the most frequently asked questions I saw on forums. Some of these may be important notes that are difficult to find in any other resource. Finally, I hope reading this book has not wasted your precious time!
