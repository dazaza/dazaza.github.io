Many employees are the second generation of migrant workers from the post-80s and post-90s generations. They love surfing the Internet.

by bhhgsyfk4143 on 2012-02-10 13:03:46

A transportation company in Daliang, Shunde started to cooperate with a vocational school in Sichuan last year to directly train and recruit employees from the school. The manager, Huang Yun, frankly admitted: "It hasn't been very smooth. From last year until now, we have recruited more than 20 people, but only about 50% of them have stayed in Shunde." Huang Yun believed that there were multiple reasons for this, some related to the enterprise itself, and others related to the students.

Direct salary increase or allowing employees to share equity is the most practical care. Last year, under uncertain economic conditions, companies were still holding an attitude of waiting and seeing regarding recruitment. However, after the Spring Festival, as the economic situation gradually became clearer and the order volume of enterprises was determined, there will be a "strong rebound" in labor demand. Under such circumstances, companies are using all sorts of methods to retain talents; some companies raise salaries, some offer interest-free home purchase preferential policies, while others make "emotional investments."

To meet the spiritual needs of post-80s and post-90s workers, Mr. Li, the human resources director of Shunda Computer Factory, stated that although it's currently difficult to recruit new employees, they haven't experienced serious labor shortages. One of their key methods to retain talent is building a cyber cafe within the factory.

In Yongfeng Industrial Zone and Xiaohuangpu Industrial Zone, reporters found that almost no companies set up booths to look for workers; instead, they were all actively reaching out to hire workers. It's reported that currently, the most popular spot for regular recruitment fairs in Shunde is the Dalin Passenger Transport Terminal Station.

During a recent job fair held in Shunde, a chain supermarket's welfare list caught the attention of many job seekers with a clause stating that employees who have worked for half a year can enjoy the ETBSC Employee Trust Beneficiary Scheme, which mainly allows employees to participate in stock ownership.

Galanz Microwave Oven Electrical Accessories Manufacturing Co., Ltd. began setting up booths at passenger transport stations since the fourth day of the Chinese New Year. In addition to the Dalin Passenger Transport Station, they have also set up nearly 20 recruitment points at crowded places in Shunde and Zhongshan. Guangdong Midea also hung banners at the passenger transport station recruiting general workers.

After visiting various companies and talent markets, Golden Edition reporters have summarized five major strategies used by companies to recruit and retain employees.

Despite facing difficulties, Huang Yun believed that cooperation between schools and enterprises was "our only choice." He said, "Previously, we could attract dozens of people to cultivate a certain number of employees, but now it's becoming increasingly difficult to compete for talent, resulting in a severely low pass rate of trained employees. This is a vicious cycle."

4 Establishing mutual assistance funds to let employees feel the warmth of home. Many migrant workers place safety first when working away from home, yet conventional emergency measures sometimes delay due to the need for procedures. In urgent situations, a timely "lifesaving fund" becomes invaluable. Therefore, some companies have begun establishing employee mutual assistance funds. Since 2004, Vanward Group has accumulated considerable experience in setting up and operating these funds. The "Employee Mutual Assistance Fund" is partly donated by all employees and partly invested by the company, used when unexpected events happen to employees.

5 Deepening school-enterprise cooperation to build a strong "back-up" for labor force. When cooperating with schools, companies start functional training and corporate philosophy education early on during students' schooling. "Finally, we select the best ones to come to Shunde, offering them the best conditions to retain talented individuals and truly cultivate suitable employees. Although the process is slow, it is the only promising recruitment method," the report mentioned.

Practice has proven that this method of retaining employees involves small investment but yields great returns. Wan Jia Le’s Enterprise Management and Human Resources Department Minister, Ruan Jiasong, indicated that over the past eight years, the project underwent two rounds of employee fund donations, accumulating over 100,000 yuan in circulating funds, helping more than 20 employees encountering "natural disasters or man-made calamities."

Besides social security and other regular benefits, a new type of auxiliary fund is being implemented in several large enterprises.

Regardless of the measures taken, nothing beats a direct salary increase. Reporters noticed that during this year's competition for general worker resources, many financially strong large enterprises adopted a pay raise strategy. Cai Chuanzhi, the human resources manager of Wan Jia Le, stated that the company's annual wage growth rate exceeds 10%, with income increasing accordingly for each additional year of work experience.

Additionally, after purchasing a house, employees can receive subsidies annually for five years. If the contract price exceeds 550,000 yuan, the subsidy amount is 6,000 yuan per year.

Moreover, Guangdong Galanz also adopted a wage increase strategy. It is understood that compared to last year, the wage level of Galanz frontline workers has increased by at least 10%.
