Analysis of China's Mold Materials Market in 2008

by mouldb2b on 2008-11-07 10:46:00

Currently, the vigorous development of the domestic mold market has put forth high demands on the performance, variety, and quantity of mold materials. Under this impetus, the mold material market has also achieved unprecedented development. First, it is reflected in the fact that major mold material manufacturers around the world have taken root in the Chinese market, such as Sweden's ASSAB, Japan's Hitachi, Daido, and Toyo Tanso, France's Aubert & Duval, America's Finkl, Germany's Glitz and SGL, among which Sweden's ASSAB holds a very large market share; secondly, domestic mold material suppliers are also developing rapidly, especially the development of domestically produced steel, showing an overall upward trend. In terms of both usage volume and sales amount, domestically produced materials occupy the largest market share, but molds made from domestic materials have shorter lifespans compared to those made from imported materials; thirdly, many mold material suppliers not only provide mold materials but also offer many accompanying services such as machining and heat treatment to increase the added value of mold materials.

On the other hand, with the development of the material market, the misuse and abuse of mold materials (mold steels) occurs from time to time, resulting in serious waste of mold steels. This not only increases costs for mold enterprises but also causes pollution, which is not conducive to the orderly development of the entire material market. The reasons for this are mainly the following two points:

(1) Areas where mold steel consumption is high are also areas where mold scrap production is high. For example, in Guangdong province, the waste molds made from Crl2 (Crl2MoV) and 4Cr5MoSiVl (H13) steels amount to tens of thousands of tons each year, and the waste of 9Cr2Mo (9Cr2MoV) steel is also increasing. These three types of materials are used to make cold work molds (ceramic molds, cold stamping molds), aluminum extrusion molds, and cold rolling rolls, respectively.

(2) Temptation of excessive profits. Recycling waste and conserving resources aligns with the principle of increased production and conservation and the concept of sustainable development. However, the prevalence of inferior mold materials has caused enormous losses. Many relevant personnel in steel mills, enticed by excessive profits, purchase waste molds at low prices, melt them down in medium-frequency smelting furnaces, cast them into small steel ingots, and forge them haphazardly into bars and plates. These are then purchased by material suppliers at low prices and sold on the market. Some procurement personnel in mold manufacturing enterprises, without fully understanding mold materials, buy these materials under the temptation of low prices, leading to the rampant use of inferior materials, especially in the numerous family workshop-style mold factories, which are the main destination for these inferior materials.

The production of inferior mold materials not only emboldens speculative individuals but also causes significant losses to mold manufacturing plants and heat treatment plants. Some poor-quality Crl2 and Crl2MoV cold work molds crack during heat treatment, wire cutting, grinding, or even while stored in warehouses. Analysis shows that the eutectic carbides' non-uniformity reaches level 8, the carbides are distributed in a network pattern, and the large blocks of carbides in block-like and angular shapes reach level 5. Both general porosity and central porosity severely exceed standards. These adverse effects caused by inferior materials may partially be due to the inexperience of heat treatment operators, but more importantly, the relevant properties of the mold materials themselves do not meet the corresponding standards. Therefore, to improve mold life and eliminate material waste, it is recommended to take effective measures:

(1) Resolutely eradicate through legal procedures; Mold steels are relatively expensive materials. Reusing scrap steel aligns with the route of sustainable development, but it must be carried out by regular steel mills. Private family workshops must not engage in the re-melting and reproduction of mold steel to prevent technical, process, and testing deficiencies from failing to meet the requirements of alloy mold steel production. Especially, the production activities aimed at excessive profits must be resolutely stopped by legal means.

(2) Establish fair arbitration institutions; Fair arbitration institutions are effective measures to curb the proliferation of these materials. Such institutions must have sufficient technical levels and be required to handle matters fairly to avoid being influenced by monetary interests from material suppliers.

Eliminating inferior materials and correctly selecting mold materials are important factors in enhancing the competitiveness of the mold market. Molds come in various types, and materials are the foundation. The shape of the mold relies on the foundation for support. So what aspects should be considered when choosing mold materials?

(1) Molds with very high part production volumes and stable dimensions should be made from high-performance materials. These mold materials can undergo low-vacuum surface strengthening treatment, elevating the durability of the molds to a new level;

(2) Molds with complex shapes, high precision requirements, long production cycles, and processing costs far exceeding material costs should also be made from high-performance materials;

(3) High-stress molds should be made from high-performance materials, and their durability can be improved using cost-effective surface strengthening technologies;

(4) Molds with smaller production volumes, simple shapes, low stress, easy manufacturing, and frequent modifications can be made from ordinary steel, cast iron, or even non-metallic materials;

(5) Mold material selection should combine national resources and choose steel grades rich in our country's elements;

(6) Materials should be selected based on the concept of sustainable development, opting for new steel grades whenever possible to convert scientific research results into productivity and elevate mold quality to a higher level;

(7) Correctly recognize the development of domestically produced mold materials.
