Four Generous Methods to Distinguish the Authenticity of Swiss Watches -梅花 Watch Price

1. Appearance Review: The trademarks and brands marked on the dial and back cover of Tissot watches (TISSOT) are clearly and completely labeled; the number of jewels indicated on the dial matches the actual number in the movement; the information marked on the back cover corresponds to the material used for the watch case (the stainless steel watch case has a glossy but slightly dark appearance; the semi-steel watch case is electroplated, with a bright white finish); the nominal glass is clean and crystal clear; the external edges of the watch case components have no sharpness, the plating has no bubbles and does not peel off. In contrast, counterfeit Tissot watches have blurry trademarks and brand names on the dial and back cover; the number of jewels indicated on the dial does not match the actual number in the movement.

2. Inspection Mark Identification: Currently, various local commercial inspection departments of Tissot watches (TISSOT) conduct batch inspections on some large markets, supermarkets, and reputable group or individual stores that sell imported watches. Watches that pass the random sampling test are issued with a certificate of conformity and given a laser anti-counterfeiting mark.

3. Checking the Movement: Open the back cover. If you see that the balance wheel of the watch is gold-plated, it is an authentic Tissot watch (TISSOT). If the balance wheel is white, it is a counterfeit. In genuine watches, the movement allows you to directly see red or blue sapphires in most of the axis holes. In shock-resistant watches, there is a golden ring around the gemstone in the axis hole above the balance wheel, which is the shock absorber spring. However, in inferior quality watches, there is often only one gemstone present, and the copper eye of the axis hole has the same color as the plate, just appearing as a round concave shape. Some counterfeit high-defense watches use red paint to coat the axis to fake diamonds, while others show plastic parts inside the movement. These issues directly affect the accuracy and lifespan of the watch, so such watches should not be purchased.

4. Other identification methods include using testing instruments to measure gold content, timekeeping accuracy, water resistance, watch glass hardness, real vs fake diamonds, etc. By comparing the actual measured data with the data and specifications indicated on the imported watch, one can determine the authenticity of the Tissot watch (TISSOT).
