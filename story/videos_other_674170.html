Shenzhen Lawyers Association urgently held an enlarged meeting of the president for this.

by lpoihjkmkk on 2012-02-24 11:00:07

Due to an intellectual property dispute, Li Ruijie violently attacked Ms. Liao Aimin, the female lawyer representing the defendant International Trade Software Alliance. After their reconciliation, a series of remarks made by Li Ruijie was no different from pouring salt on the wounds of the other party. This time he not only assaulted Ms. Liao, but also insulted all lawyers, harming the entire industry and even the construction of a society governed by the rule of law," Pan Xiang said.

Legal professionals

It is understood that after Ms. Liao Aimin was attacked, the Shenzhen Bar Association's Rights Protection Committee immediately followed up and expressed its position to the police multiple times. Lin Changzhi, the chairman of the Rights Protection Committee of the Shenzhen Bar Association, stated that the attack on Lawyer Liao Aimin was a common infringement on the practice rights of lawyers and a threat to their personal safety in the legal community this year. As an entrepreneur, Li Ruijie should set an example by abiding by national laws and regulations and assuming social responsibility, but his words and actions were completely opposite. Moreover, China has been a member of the WTO for over 10 years, and both Chinese and foreign enterprises are equally protected by the law in China. Narrow-mindedness towards foreign-invested enterprises and foreign companies should be avoided.

Reported by You Chunliang

Cui Jun believed that as a listed company, ZQBO should be subject to enhanced management by securities regulatory authorities regarding this incident. "Not only should the corporate governance structure be managed, but the person in charge of the listed company should also receive intellectual property education and legal awareness education. How can high-tech enterprise executives talk about technological innovation or going global if they ignore the law?"

Shenzhen Bar Association spokesperson Li Zhiqiang told the Legal Daily that the legal profession is a very important component of socialist rule of law construction. Lawyers promote fairness and justice throughout society by defending the legitimate rights and interests of individual case parties through the handling of specific cases. Respecting lawyers is a hallmark feature of a society governed by the rule of law. As the chairman of a listed company, Li Ruijie used violent behavior to assault the lawyer of the opposing party and did not repent after the reconciliation. Instead, he continued to use derogatory language to attack the entire legal profession, damaging the image of the legal profession and constituting an infringement.

After being attacked, Ms. Liao Aimin had six stitches on her head. Her family, worried about harassment, transferred her early the next morning from Nanshan Hospital in Shenzhen to Guangdong Public Security Frontier Corps Hospital for treatment. The attending physician wrote in the diagnosis certificate five days after the attack: concussion; scalp laceration; multiple soft tissue injuries throughout the body.

Guangdong lawyer Pan Xiang believed that Chairman Li Ruijie's words and actions were "one mistake piled on another."

On November 23, staff members of the Shenzhen Intermediate People's Court delivered the complaint filed by Adobe and Autodesk against ZQBO for computer software copyright infringement. The complaint claimed that ZQBO engaged in infringing their computer software copyrights and demanded compensation of RMB 2 million each, totaling RMB 4 million. On the afternoon of November 25, at the Starbucks in Nanshan Science and Technology Park in Shenzhen, Chairman Li Ruijie of ZQBO met with Ms. Liao Aimin to discuss the matter. During the communication, due to differences in opinion and words, physical conflict occurred, resulting in Ms. Liao Aimin being hospitalized.

However, the aftermath of this incident has not subsided.

Huang Yaying indicated that, "Once the reconciliation agreement is signed, it takes immediate effect and has legal binding force. His comments on voluntary actions between both parties essentially show disrespect for the law. His words and actions have caused significant damage to the public image of the listed company, and he should conduct a profound self-examination rather than making things worse and creating contradictions that are hard to reconcile."

As a lawyer and member of the Shenzhen Municipal Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, Cui Jun said that he himself was deeply shocked by the extremely weak legal awareness of the CEO of a listed company. "This experience could not help him make up for the lesson on the rule of law, which should be worth deep reflection by the whole society."

The incident involving the CEO of a listed company assaulting a lawyer and making inappropriate remarks has sparked heated discussions among local legal professionals.

It was learned that on December 17, the International Trade Software Alliance issued a statement condemning the behavior of ZQBO, stating that it was both illegal and immoral. Chairman Li Ruijie's remarks distorted the essence of ZQBO's software infringement case and the fact that he violently attacked the lawyer representing the Commercial Software Alliance, Ms. Liao Aimin.

In an exclusive interview with a Shenzhen media outlet on December 15, when asked about the "female lawyer beating incident," Li Ruijie made remarks such as "Lawyers often use the law as a weapon, extorting businesses everywhere, causing them great distress" and "In my eyes, lawyers' behavior resembles that of compradors in the past, coming to 'collect debts.'"

"He actually overturned all legal facts with witnesses and evidence, showing disdain for the law." Ms. Liao Aimin's younger sister said, "Li Ruijie fabricated facts and defamed my sister's 20-year legal career. We do not rule out suing Li Ruijie for defamation."

Recently, the Shenzhen Bar Association urgently convened an expanded meeting of the president, condemning inaccurate statements harmful to the image of the legal profession, and deemed that the chairman had committed an infringement, reserving the right to take legal action. The family of the beaten lawyer also expressed that they might sue the chairman for defamation.

Recently, Ms. Liao Aimin's younger sister broke her silence and spoke out for the first time, expressing the family's stance: "If the attacker continues to distort facts and maliciously attacks the victim, we will consider holding the other party legally accountable."

Since "not discussing" was one of the terms of the reconciliation agreement, Li Ruijie's reneging on the deal made Ms. Liao Aimin's family feel extremely indignant.

President of the Law School of Shenzhen University, Huang Yaying, believed that any defense by Li Ruijie would be detrimental to him. "His random talk is accelerating his own disadvantage, which is fundamentally not a sign of remorse."

Cui Jun believed that Shenzhen has always emphasized technological innovation, ranking first nationwide in the number of invention patent applications, and also topping the list in PCT international patent applications. Much work has been done in protecting intellectual property rights, but from the series of incidents triggered by the intellectual property disputes involving the CEO of ZQBO, it is evident that Shenzhen still has a long way to go in promoting intellectual property awareness and legal education. "We need to protect our own intellectual property rights while respecting others' achievements; otherwise, it will affect Shenzhen's overall investment environment."

Since "not talking" was one of the clauses in the reconciliation agreement, Li Ruijie's reversal of his promise enraged the family of Ms. Liao Aimin.

It was learned that the cause and process of the incident were as follows: Lawyer Liao Aimin from Beijing Jingwei (Shenzhen) Law Firm, acting on behalf of the International Trade Software Alliance, represented the American company Adobe (Autodesk) in suing the listed company Shenzhen Zhongqingbao Interactive Network Co., Ltd. for infringing on its computer software copyright. The Shenzhen Intermediate People's Court accepted the case on November 1.

On December 8, Zhongqingbao announced that it had reached a settlement with injured lawyer Liao Aimin. On December 9, Li Ruijie posted online expressing regret for his previous emotional outburst, claiming he harbored no ill intentions toward Ms. Liao Aimin and wished her a speedy recovery.

It was learned that this incident stemmed from Li Ruijie using a chair to injure Ms. Liao Aimin's head during the event, drawing attention from the legal community in Shenzhen.

The family of the beaten female lawyer does not exclude suing the attacker for defamation.

On December 6, the investigating officer brought the "minor injury" appraisal report to Ms. Liao Aimin and her family. The family believed the appraisal did not match the actual injuries and requested a re-appraisal. That evening, under the strong persuasion of multiple departments and relevant leaders, Ms. Liao Aimin's family accepted the settlement proposal. Both parties signed the settlement agreement, promising not to publicly discuss the matter in the media again.
