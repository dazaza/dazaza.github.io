Who broke the wings of the "mountain eagle"

by hunanshida on 2012-02-15 13:58:07

The teachers of Hunan Normal University's self-study program have found that the spirit of adventure is an excellent quality that is most lacking in Chinese children today. In fact, not only do Chinese children today lack this quality, but even Chinese children in the past have been extremely deficient in it. I believe that you, who are reading this article, may also be the same. This might be determined by the national character of the Chinese people, but the formation of this national character is closely related to the influence of national culture.

The teachers at Hunan Normal University Self-Study Examination Network have found that relatively speaking, children in Western countries, including our close neighbor Japan, especially revere adventure. Extreme sports that are now quite popular, such as rock climbing, bungee jumping, and rafting, originated in these countries and then gradually spread around the world. Moreover, from some classic children's literature, such as Mark Twain's novel "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer," we can also see this point.

So, what should we do? As parents and teachers, what basic knowledge of exploration should you teach your children? The teachers of Hunan Normal University's self-study program believe that the first thing is to make sure to prepare adequately before embarking on an adventure and act within one's capabilities — at least, stop at the threshold of the limit.

For example, when it comes to mountain climbing, blindly moving forward with just enthusiasm is absolutely unacceptable; nor should one aimlessly pursue height and danger as the ultimate goal. Because the purpose of climbing mountains is to cultivate a spirit of daring to challenge oneself, not necessarily to achieve a certain altitude. One must know that under conditions where professional equipment and experience are relatively lacking, the consequences of doing so can be easily imagined.

The reason why Peking University's young "mountain eagles" lost their lives in the snow-capped mountains was due to obvious lessons. And in a certain sense, these lessons were also because they lacked the habit of scientific exploration. Since habits should be cultivated from a young age, parents need to consciously foster such habits in their children under possible conditions, such as teaching them more exploration knowledge and engaging in some low-risk training together with their children. When the correct concept of exploration matures in their hearts, naturally, the road ahead will become much smoother for them.
