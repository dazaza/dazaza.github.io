"BoGuang Cup" 2009 International Year of Astronomy Total Solar Eclipse Thematic Photography Exhibition

by a9107857 on 2009-11-22 22:34:37

On the morning of July 22, 2009, a total solar eclipse that will be the most spectacular of this century will occur in the mid-lower reaches of the Yangtze River region of our country. In many areas, the totality will last as long as five to six minutes. To comprehensively reflect and showcase the observation activities of this total solar eclipse, further promote the development of popular photography in our country, and provide a chance for all photography workers and enthusiasts to display their photographic talent, depict the customs and folkways of southern China, and present its history and culture, the National Astronomical Observatory of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, the Astronomy Popularization Network Committee of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, the Chinese Youth Palace Association, the China Ethnic Photography Art Publishing House, Sina.com, and relevant local governments have decided to jointly host the "Boguan Cup" 2009 International Year of Astronomy Total Solar Eclipse Theme Photography Exhibition (hereinafter referred to as the "Theme Photography Exhibition").

The Theme Photography Exhibition sincerely invites domestic and foreign photography groups, photography workers, and a wide range of photography enthusiasts to submit works for exhibition.

I. Organizational Structure

Organizers: National Astronomical Observatory of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, Astronomy Popularization Network Committee of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, Chinese Youth Palace Association, China Ethnic Photography Art Publishing House, Sina.com, Tongling Municipal People's Government, Yi County People's Government, Tongcheng Municipal People's Government, Wuhan Science Association, Suzhou Municipal People's Government, Jiaxing Municipal People's Government, Gaochun Municipal People's Government.

Undertaker: "China National Astronomy" Magazine

Supporting Unit: Guangzhou Boguan Enterprise Co., Ltd.

This event will set up an organizing committee and an evaluation committee for submitted works (list to be issued separately) responsible for the specific organization and evaluation work of the Theme Photography Exhibition.

II. Content of Works

Photographic works mainly include the following topics:

1. Astronomy Section: Professional photography of the total solar eclipse, recording the process of the total solar eclipse and its magnificent and exciting moments.

2. Activity Section: Reflecting the grandeur of the total solar eclipse observation, capturing snippets and close-ups from the observation and related activities.

3. Art Section: Mainly combining celestial and terrestrial landscapes during the occurrence of the total solar eclipse, emphasizing the artistic nature of the works.

4. Customs Section: Representing the historical culture, natural scenery, and customs of the local area.

III. Submission Rules

1. All photography workers, photography enthusiasts, and the public can submit entries for exhibition.

2. The shooting time of the submitted works is from July 1 to July 31, 2009.

3. There are no restrictions on the form and style of the submitted works; black and white or color photos are acceptable (but slides are excluded). The submitted works should highlight artistry and the scientific popularization and cultural connotations, and require the content of the works to be true and not processed by computer in any way.

4. Submitted works need to be printed as ordinary 10-inch photographs (please do not frame them), while also submitting the original electronic version of the work. Each participant may submit no more than 10 pieces of work, with each group photo not exceeding six frames (please stick the photos in order with transparent tape on the back, and name the original electronic version in order).

5. On the back of the submitted works, please note: title of the work, shooting time, location, as well as the author's name, address, telephone number, postal code, email address, etc. The original electronic version of the work needs to be recorded on a CD for submission.

6. This Theme Photography Exhibition does not charge entry fees and does not return manuscripts. For all submitted works, the organizers have the right to use them in relevant venues, websites, newspapers, magazines, albums, video works for non-commercial purposes without additional remuneration.

7. Authors who submit works to participate in this Theme Photography Exhibition are deemed to agree and abide by the above submission rules. Submitted works must not infringe upon others' copyright, portrait rights, reputation rights, fabricate facts, or cause legal issues. All disputes arising therefrom shall be borne by the author.

IV. Award Settings and Evaluation

1. Awards settings for each topic:

a. Astronomy Section: 1 first prize, 3 second prizes, 5 third prizes;

b. Activity Section: 2 first prizes, 5 second prizes, 8 third prizes;

c. Art Section: 3 first prizes, 6 second prizes, 9 third prizes;

d. Customs Section: 2 first prizes, 5 second prizes, 8 third prizes;

e. Each section will also set several commemorative awards.

2. Submitted works will be evaluated by the Evaluation Committee according to the principles of fairness, justice, and openness. Works that win first, second, and third prizes will be awarded plaques and certificates by the Organizing Committee.

3. All winning works will be included in "China National Astronomy" (Supplement - "Award-winning Works Collection of the 2009 International Year of Astronomy Total Solar Eclipse Theme Photography Exhibition (Tentative Name)") and published and displayed on relevant websites such as Sina.com without additional remuneration.

4. Submitted works uploaded to Sina.com will go through a preliminary selection to participate in the evaluation of this Theme Photography Exhibition and compete for awards in each section. At the same time, netizens can vote online, selecting 30 network popularity awards for this Theme Photography Exhibition.

V. Submission Methods

Method One: Send by mail to the Customer Service Department of "China National Astronomy": Room 605, Building A, National Astronomical Observatory, No. 20 Datun Road, Chaoyang District, Beijing, Postal Code 100012.

Contact person: Hao Yuhua, Tel: 010-64807696, 15801316608

Method Two: Sina.com will open an online collection entry for total solar eclipse photography works. Users need to fill out complete personal information to participate in the exhibition, including name, contact phone number, and e-mail. The submitted works should be JPG or GIF format files, with a size controlled within 300K. Users should retain the original photo files (for details, see the Sina.com total solar eclipse special feature). Click here to enter the online submission.

VI. Evaluation Schedule

Submission deadline: August 9, 2009 (based on the postmark date of the mailed works).

Evaluation date: Mid-August 2009.
