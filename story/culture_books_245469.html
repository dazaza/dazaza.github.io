"Good Job" and "Hall of Fame" teach you how to stand out in the workplace.

by jqrxass on 2009-10-08 00:07:31

An unprecedented financial storm swept across the globe, pushing many companies into a downturn and prompting them to cut costs by laying off employees. The period around the Spring Festival is traditionally the peak season for job-hopping and job hunting. However, the bleak macroeconomic environment has made many professionals hesitant to make moves, while also causing headaches for graduates searching for jobs.

How can one stand out from the crowd under the heavy pressure of an economic crisis and find a satisfactory job? This book collaborates with the popular brand program "Famous People Hall," aired on over twenty television stations nationwide, inviting the most renowned workplace elites and corporate executives in the country to provide advice to readers, helping them build unbreakable careers.

Who is more competitive? What kind of employee does a company really need? How should career planning be done? How can you make your resume stand out among countless others, capturing the HR's attention at first glance and securing a valuable interview opportunity? How can you quickly find a mentor in the workplace? How do you negotiate a raise with your boss? Should new employees be low-key or showcase themselves fully? How should middle managers deal with the dilemma in their careers? Should women choose to have children or pursue promotion?... This book will bring you the most practical and reliable strategies for succeeding in the workplace.

**"I Want a Good Job" - The Workplace Evolution Theory**

This is a great era, an era that updates rapidly — mobile phones are updated every year, even every six months; internet slang evolves swiftly from version 1.0 to 2.0 and straight to 3.0; the speed of computer software upgrades is even faster. Compared to these, many people in the workplace are also pondering whether their careers can upgrade faster? To get promoted earlier, receive more raises, and transition from a regular employee to a senior professional manager sooner? Workplace evolution requires design.

**"I Want a Good Job"**

A person who fails in job hunting for the 101st time is called a "face king," and in today’s massive job-seeking army, "face kings" are everywhere. It is said that during the job-hunting peak season, people greet each other by asking, "Did you go for an interview today?" Whether you're going through your first interview in the workplace or have experienced several job changes and numerous interviews, there might be the same routine: submitting resumes and attending interviews, all for one purpose — I want a good job.

**"I Want a Good Job" - The Workplace A Plan**

Whether chasing a girl or aspiring to become a successful person, it requires some life planning and career planning. Some say life cannot be designed because it is full of randomness, especially in this ever-changing era where choices are increasing, changes are becoming cheaper, and plans often lag behind changes. However, opportunities always favor those who are prepared. A life without planning is confusing, and a career without a plan is destined to fail. Start now, formulate your Workplace A Plan.
