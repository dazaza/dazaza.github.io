How to play

The number of times a player can be the banker is configurable in the backend.

The basic way to play Pai Gow is to compare the size of the domino points. Each person gets two tiles, and the winner is immediately determined.

Game Process

Mainly compare sizes with the banker. Interestingly, players can also take turns being the banker and play against other players.

After everyone has placed their bets, the banker will place all the tiles face down and start stacking them. Then the players can place their bets. After betting is complete, the banker opens the tiles to determine which player starts dealing the tiles.

Each player gets 2 tiles.

Tile ranking order

First, check if there are any pairs. If there are no pairs, then the sum of the two tiles' unit digits (see decimal system) determines the winner.

Game rules

1. Basic rules

Number of players: 4 people

Team formation: One person is the banker, the others are the players

Number of tiles: 32 tiles, excluding big and small kings,

Composed of 2 red Qs, 2 black Js, 4 10s, 2 red 9s, 4 8s, 4 7s, 4 6s, 2 red 5s, 4 4s, 2 2s, spade A, spade 3

2. Order of dealing

The banker deals the cards first, giving each person two tiles. Each idle player compares sizes with the banker.

3. Tile rankings: Pairs > Special tile combinations > Points

If there are no pairs, then the unit digit of the sum of the two tiles determines the winner.

If there are no pairs mentioned above, then the unit digit of the sum of the two tiles determines the winner. The highest is 9, the lowest is 0. This is where the name 'Pai Gow' comes from, meaning arranging nine numbers.

If the tile points are the same, the banker is considered the winner.
