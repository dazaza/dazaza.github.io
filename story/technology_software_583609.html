BMW hit a person and sped off in the midnight, and the victim was still in coma

by supercusa on 2011-11-24 14:38:49

The police pointed out the collision marks on the BMW involved in the accident. Reporter Yan Zhiguo took the photo at the intersection of Changjiang Road and Zhufeng Street.

The police screened nearly a hundred BMWs to catch the perpetrator; the injured victim remains unconscious.

On the afternoon of October 12, outside the ICU ward on the 6th floor of City No.3 Hospital, Han Yinshui, a villager from Dahu Town, Lüquan City, sat quietly. He had waited here for nine days and received mixed news: the good news was that after relentless efforts by the traffic police of the Development Zone, the hit-and-run driver Ma had been caught two days ago; the bad news was that his 24-year-old youngest daughter remained unconscious.

The surveillance camera recorded the entire process of the BMW's hit-and-run incident.

BMW Hits Pedestrian, Accelerates to Flee

At around 10:42 PM on October 3, a serious traffic accident occurred at the intersection of Changjiang Avenue and Zhufeng Street in the Development Zone. A BMW hit a young woman riding an electric bike and fled the scene.

The video surveillance installed by the public security authorities at the intersection clearly recorded the entire accident. On the night of the incident, Changjiang Avenue was brightly lit. At 10:42:23 PM, a man and a woman riding electric bikes appeared on the screen, traveling east along Changjiang Avenue. When they reached about halfway through the intersection, a car suddenly rushed from south to north along Zhufeng Street, throwing the female rider into the air. After rolling several times, she landed in the middle of the intersection, more than ten meters away. The male companion immediately abandoned his electric bike, waving his hands as he chased after the fleeing vehicle, but the vehicle showed no sign of slowing down. Instead, it sped up and disappeared northward along Zhufeng Street.

A witness at the scene immediately called 110 to report the incident and provided information that the offending vehicle was a BMW with the license plate number "Ji A32XXX," where the last three digits included the letter "F."

Upon receiving the alarm, the accident team of the Development Zone Traffic Police arrived at the scene. By this time, the injured person had already been sent to the nearby Great Wall Hospital for treatment and later transferred to City No.3 Hospital. There was a patch of blood on the road surface. After carefully examining the scene, the police collected a "fog light lower strip" and several silver-gray paint chips that had fallen off due to the impact.

Wang Li, an officer handling the case from the Accident Team of the Development Zone Traffic Police, introduced that the police conducted accident investigation work overnight. They first retrieved surveillance footage from near the incident location and checked each one individually. Meanwhile, they also conducted investigations into vehicles registered in nearby residential areas but failed to find any valuable clues.

Given the seriousness of the case, on the morning of October 4, the Development Zone Traffic Police established an escape pursuit team led by a deputy captain, dividing their forces into three groups to strengthen the case investigation:

One group went to the vehicle management department to retrieve all vehicle information related to the license plate number of the offending vehicle, finding nearly 100 BMWs and conducting individual checks. After identification, eight BMWs were similar to the offending vehicle. The police contacted them one by one via phone. One of the cars was in Xijing. Considering the high likelihood of involvement, the officers rushed to Xijing on the afternoon of the 4th and found that particular BMW, only to rule out its involvement in the hit-and-run immediately.

Another group visited citizens near the incident location, asking witnesses for information. Some citizens provided leads, indicating that the offending BMW might be an older model of the BMW 3 Series.

Yet another group continued retrieving surveillance footage, discovering that after the offending vehicle passed over Changjiang Avenue along Zhufeng Street heading north, it appeared once in a surveillance camera near "No.8 Mansion" before disappearing completely. The police determined that the offending vehicle had continued fleeing northward.

Case Investigation Involved Screening Nearly a Hundred BMWs Across the City

Wang Li introduced that the investigating officers gave up their National Day holidays. After a day of intensive investigation on October 4, they gathered full data on eight suspicious vehicles. One of the vehicles was registered in Dongdu Village, Xizhaotong Town, Chang'an District. To avoid alarming the suspect prematurely, the investigating officers secretly investigated Ma for several days. On October 10, they finally managed to contact Ma by phone, notifying him to drive his BMW to the Development Zone Accident Team for cooperation in the investigation. Around 5 PM on the afternoon of October 10, Ma drove his BMW to the Accident Team of the Development Zone Traffic Police.

"As soon as I saw this BMW downstairs, I noticed that part of the plastic strip below its front left fog light was missing," Wang Li said. Based on this observation, the suspicion of this BMW being involved in the accident became very high. He then walked to the front of the car and examined the paint closely, finding that some parts of the paint were newly sprayed and obviously different from the original paint.

"Has your car been repaired recently?" Wang Li asked Ma. "There was a scrape in my hometown Henan a few months ago, so I fixed it," Ma replied.

Upon hearing this, Wang Li knew that Ma was lying — driving from Henan to Shijiazhuang would not leave the paint looking so new. Subsequently, the investigating officers called in technical personnel and relevant experts to dismantle and compare this BMW. It turned out that the "fog light lower strip" found at the scene could fit perfectly under the front left fog light of this BMW. Thus, the police decided to seize this BMW on the spot.

"The Fog Light Lower Strip" Locked Down the Offending Vehicle

Subsequently, the investigating officers summoned Ma inside and asked him to provide a detailed account of his activities during the National Day holiday. After a few rounds of questioning, Ma finally lowered his head and began confessing, "I admit it, I got into an accident in the Development Zone a few days ago."

"Where exactly did it happen?"

"Near 'Eight Horses.'"

When Ma said this, Investigating Officer Wang Li's tense mood of the past week finally relaxed — the driver of the hit-and-run BMW was him.

It turns out that Ma is from Lan考, Henan Province, doing business in the provincial capital. After the accident, he fled the scene and subsequently painted over the damaged parts of his car in his small workshop, attempting to destroy evidence of the hit-and-run.

That evening, Ma was handed over by the Development Zone Traffic Police to the Criminal Investigation Team for detention.

Confession of the Hit-and-Run Driver

Father of the Victim Expresses Gratitude to the Officers, Hopes for Severe Punishment of the Hit-and-Run Driver

At around 5 PM on the afternoon of October 12, I met Han Yinshui, father of the victim Xiao Han, at City No.3 Hospital. Han Yinshui told reporters that his younger daughter was extremely unfortunate. On September 3, 2008, she had experienced a severe accident at the entrance of the Shita Expressway near Tianzhuang Bridge on Shi Qing Road. The offending vehicle also fled the scene. After the traffic management department's search, it was confirmed to be from Shuozhou, Shanxi, but the offending driver has not been found in the three years since.

Han Yinshui said that before the incident, his younger daughter worked as a salesperson in a supermarket. On the evening of the incident, she went to visit a former colleague after work and ended up in this accident while riding her bike. Fortunately, this time the offending driver was found. "Thank you to the investigating officers, thank everyone." Han Yinshui expressed his hope that the offending driver would receive severe punishment.

The on-duty doctor at the ICU ward of City No.3 Hospital introduced that Xiao Han has remained unconscious since being admitted to the hospital, with very serious injuries. After several days of treatment, her vital signs have gradually stabilized.

Hit-and-Run Harms Both Others and Oneself

"The net of justice may be wide, but it has no loopholes," yet there are always some individuals who, harboring侥幸psychology, flee after committing crimes hoping to evade legal punishment. What is the result? Not only can they not escape, but it actually worsens their situation.

With the increase in private cars, traffic accidents have also remained consistently high. It is understood that currently, the city experiences an average of over 300 various types of traffic accidents daily. Most drivers involved in traffic accidents voluntarily stop to treat the injured and handle the accident. However, there are still some who flee the scene to evade responsibility.

So, can fleeing after causing an accident help evade responsibility? Let's consider two aspects. First, the economic aspect. Quickly treating the injured after causing an accident may cost a large sum of money, but fleeing temporarily avoids paying this fee, seemingly advantageous. In reality, once caught, these fees still need to be paid, and because of delayed treatment, additional costs may be incurred. Second, the legal aspect. The investigating officer explained that after a traffic accident, depending on the severity of the accident, the criminal responsibility of the offending driver may or may not be pursued. However, fleeing undoubtedly exacerbates the nature of the accident, significantly increasing the likelihood of pursuing the offending driver's criminal responsibility.

With advancements in technology, the possibility of catching those who flee after causing a traffic accident is increasing. Here, we advise those few drivers harboring侥幸psychology that while having an accident is indeed terrifying, what is even more terrifying is choosing to escape and showing disregard for life.
