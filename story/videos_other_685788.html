Mother wants to donate kidney to save her sick son, 400,000 yuan for medical treatment fees overwhelms the whole family

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 17:01:46

Wang Yunlong, 22 years old, from Huining, Gansu. In 2010, he was admitted to the Communication Engineering major at Lanzhou Jiaotong University with a high score of 540 points. One month after school started, he was diagnosed with uremia. To give him dialysis, the family borrowed money everywhere and has already spent 150,000 yuan on treatment fees. The attending physician suggested: it would be best to undergo a kidney transplant surgery. After hearing this, his mother decided to donate her kidney to save her son. After hospital examinations, it was found that the mother's kidney compatibility rate with her son was the highest, making it possible for a successful match. However, a kidney transplant surgery would cost nearly 400,000 yuan, where would they get so much money?

Admitted one month, diagnosed with uremia

Ten days before the 2010 college entrance exam, Wang Yunlong felt a headache. After taking some cold medicine, he participated in the exam. One month later, Wang Yunlong was admitted to Lanzhou Jiaotong University. Amidst the joy, his headache suddenly worsened, and the Huining County Hospital diagnosed it as high blood pressure. On September 8, 2010, Wang Yunlong went to Lanzhou Jiaotong University on time to register for enrollment. Just one month after enrollment, during a basketball game, Wang Yunlong, who had not fully recovered from his cold, suddenly fainted. Subsequently, after examination by the Second Hospital of Lanzhou University, he was found to have uremia.

Wang Yunlong first returned home for treatment for nearly half a month, then came to Lanzhou and applied for a leave of absence. On December 29, 2010, Wang Yunlong was admitted to the First City Hospital, beginning a long "dialysis" journey. In March this year, Wang Yunlong transferred to the Provincial First Hospital. During this period, the prolonged dialysis of their son caused great distress and frustration for father Wang Yaowen and mother Shao Ruiqin. They took their son to two hospitals in Nanjing and Beijing, seeking Chinese herbal medicine and medical equipment to replace dialysis treatment, but without any results.

Wang Yunlong, who had taken a year-long leave of absence, desperately wanted to study. On September 8 this year, Wang Yunlong attended class with the freshmen of the 2011 cohort. For convenience, on October 29, Wang Yunlong transferred from the Provincial First Hospital to the City First Hospital, which is closer to Jiaotong University. Currently, Wang Yunlong goes to the hospital twice a week for dialysis, each session lasting four hours, and he normally attends classes at school.

Yesterday, when the reporter arrived at the City First Hospital, Wang Yunlong had just finished dialysis and was standing on the electronic scale to weigh himself. "Yunlong used to have a very good constitution and rarely got sick. Before he fell ill, his weight was over 70 kilograms, now it's only 60 kilograms," said father Wang Yaowen.

The reporter noticed that due to prolonged care and mental anguish, mother Shao Ruiqin's eyes were slightly swollen, she rarely spoke, and only sat quietly shedding tears.

"I want to study, I want to live well"

"It's very painful! Very desperate." Upon learning that he had uremia, the usually strong Wang Yunlong could hardly bear it. Wang Yunlong is of medium height, and from his handsome face, one can see the shadow of a former basketball star.

"I still want to study, I want to live well." This is Wang Yunlong's only request. He really wants to learn. Father Wang Yaowen said that during the treatment period, Wang Yunlong often read books, which made everyone feel very uncomfortable.

What surprised the reporter was that Wang Yunlong never told the school about his uremia condition. "Yunlong is too proud, he is worried that if he tells others, he will be looked down upon by classmates," his uncle guessed.

Doctor's suggestion: Best to undergo a kidney transplant

Dr. Cao, Wang Yunlong's attending doctor, introduced that the treatment methods for chronic uremia generally only have two options: either lifelong dialysis or a kidney transplant surgery. "The child is still very young, the best option is a kidney transplant; dialysis is also an option, but it must be maintained for life," Dr. Cao said that Wang Yunlong's uremia has already triggered multiple organ pains such as repeated heart failure, anemia, and renal hypertension.

Wang Yaowen, Wang Yunlong's father, is 50 years old this year, a genuine farmer with no special skills, mainly relying on potato farming to support the family. "The best income was nearly 10,000 yuan, last year's income was 5,000 yuan, and this year, due to drought, only sold for 1,000 yuan." The Wang family has four children, the eldest daughter is already married. Due to his younger brother's illness, the second daughter and youngest daughter working outside had to return home to take care of household chores.

To treat his son, Wang Yaowen sought help everywhere, borrowing 80,000 yuan from relatives and taking out a bank loan of 30,000 yuan. So far, treating Wang Yunlong has already cost 150,000 yuan.

"The compatibility rate of the child's mother's kidney is very high, there is a high possibility of a successful match. If surgery is performed, the surgical fee and anti-rejection rehabilitation fee will cost nearly 400,000 yuan." The doctor's words gave Wang Yaowen a glimmer of hope, but heavily indebted, he was at a loss. If you wish to show your love, please contact our newspaper or the patient's family.
