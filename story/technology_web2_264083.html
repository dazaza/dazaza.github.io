Posing as a philanthropist and committing outrages, the former national wrestling champion was arrested for involvement with organized crime

by 5lda682e4g on 2009-11-20 16:26:41

Zhang Jiaqiang, the "philanthropic businessman" who won two consecutive national wrestling championships in the 57 kg category in the 1980s, was placed under police control for his involvement with a black society organization and was formally approved to be arrested the day before yesterday. The Beijing Times reported on November 20 that Zhang Jiaqiang, who retired from sports and went into business, got involved in real estate, serving as Vice Chairman of the Jilin Boxing Association and Chairman of the Songyuan Saint Water Lake Agricultural Development Co., Ltd. This "philanthropic businessman," who had won two consecutive national wrestling championships in the 57 kg category in the 1980s, was placed under police control due to his involvement with a black society organization and was formally approved to be arrested the day before yesterday. Suspect Zhang Jiaqiang.

After retiring, he established a company and engaged in charity work. In the 1980s, Zhang Jiaqiang became famous for winning two consecutive national wrestling championships in the 57 kg category and was nicknamed the "Little Northeast Wrestling Tiger." After retiring from the provincial sports school with great prestige, he started his own company, mainly focusing on agricultural development and real estate according to local media reports. After becoming wealthy, he donated to charity multiple times, presenting himself as a "philanthropist" in public.

He fell into being the top black society figure in Songyuan. Since 2000, Zhang Jiaqiang has long gathered together labor education (reform) released personnel and idle social individuals to form a black society gang, engaging in numerous criminal activities such as extortion, bid rigging, and theft. On October 15th this year, the local police captured Zhang Jiaqiang. Zhang Jiaqiang is 42 years old this year. During the flood resistance in 1998, he drove to comfort the flood-fighting troops and donated more than 100,000 yuan in money and goods. Every festival, Zhang Jiaqiang would go to the impoverished areas of Songyuan City to deliver rice, flour, oil, and consolation funds. Just one day before his arrest, Zhang Jiaqiang, who devoutly believed in Buddhism, attended the consecration ceremony of the Great Hero Hall at Longhua Temple in Songyuan City, where it was rumored that he donated 200,000 yuan. But merely a day later, Zhang Jiaqiang was apprehended by the special case team.

At the end of last month, after Zhang Jiaqiang "got into trouble," the real estate housing developments developed by his company were sealed off by the police. Sales staff told reporters: "He treated employees quite well; he had a kind of 'gangster air,' which might be related to his previous experience in sports." The investigators revealed yesterday that Zhang Jiaqiang was very influential locally in Songyuan and belonged to the "black society boss": "Wang Gui Min, the head of the Wang family crime syndicate, who was once very famous in Songyuan and whose nickname was 'Er Min Zi,' felt intimidated upon hearing Zhang Jiaqiang's name. Although they both were involved with black societies, they were not on the same level."

The villa covers an area of 6000 square meters. In 2007, Zhang Jiaqiang began constructing a villa in Nonglin Village, Xincheng Township, Ningjiang District, Songyuan City. The entire villa covers nearly 6000 square meters, but only 2800 square meters have proper documentation. Two-story "watchtowers" were built at each corner of the wall. According to the special case team, the total investment in the villa exceeded 4 million yuan, and most of the workers who built the villa did not receive their wages. He privately connected to the Jilin Oilfield power grid, stealing oilfield electricity for his construction, living, and entertainment needs.

The investigators told the reporter that the villa has a swimming pool for diving, a boxing arena, a tennis court, a stage, etc. Because Zhang Jiaqiang enjoyed sports, he often invited some friends to hold boxing matches in his home boxing arena.

(Source: Beijing Times)
