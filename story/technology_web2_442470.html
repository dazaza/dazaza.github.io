A deep sentiment for Tsinghua developed. The red brick constructions that are ubiquitous in UVa's campus made her feel as though she had returned to Tsinghua, feeling an exceptional warmth; perhaps this was fate.

(Comparison picture of Tsinghua's auditorium and Virginia's Rotunda - how many categories are similar?)

When applying to UVa, we expressed development trends for two majors: bioengineering and economics. But even up until now, Gaga still cannot clearly determine what she wants to do in the future. Today, UVA forwarded us a letter from an alumnus. After reading it, we gained some new insights into the mission of our four years at university, and I would like to share it with you (personal information has been omitted):

When I left the country back then, I only had one thought in mind: go out and see the world. Before that, I had never been to the US, let alone personally visited the University of Virginia (UVa). Giving up on being accepted by a prestigious domestic university, I only had a bit of information about UVa that I had gathered from their website, along with a simple longing for the unknown. At 18, I never thought about what I wanted to achieve here or considered what kind of difficult moments I might face. Looking back now, it was a somewhat casual decision, but precisely because of this randomness, it added an unexpected touch of romance to my brilliant four years.

I am a senior student at UVa, double majoring in biomedical engineering and economics. After graduation, I will be working for a well-known consulting company on the west coast of the United States. I have also been accepted into Harvard Business School and plan to enroll in the fall, two years later, to pursue a master's degree in business administration. If I were to summarize my four years at university with two words, I would say exploration: exploring interests and self.

One, Exploration of Interests

What subjects do I enjoy? What career should I pursue? How should I live my life? These questions guided me throughout my university life. UVa's abundant resources gave me the opportunity to experiment with various possibilities that I wanted to try. During my first year, I thought about pursuing scientific research, so I worked in the school laboratory during the summer break after my first year, learning basic research techniques. In my second year, I was fortunate enough to apply for one of the school's awards and received $3000 in research funding to study prostate cancer. A year of independent research not only allowed me to explore the topics I was interested in but also gave me a rough understanding of what the life of a researcher is like. I realized that my personality may not be suited for scientific research. I am very grateful for such experiences, as they taught me that unless you try things yourself, you won't discover if they are suitable for you.

Although I was in the engineering college, I was never confined to the fields of science and technology. During the summer break of my second year, I participated in an internship organized by the school in the field of public policy. That experience gave me the chance to apply the medical knowledge I learned in class to the design of government policies. I understood that what I was studying wasn't just about MRI machines or heart transplants or other medical technologies; I gained a broader understanding of healthcare and health itself. I cherish those days spent moving between various government departments, understanding how public policies affect the general public—possibilities I had never imagined but achieved at UVa.

UVa has one of the top undergraduate business schools in the nation and thus a strong network of alumni working in finance. During the summer break of my third year, I used the connections provided by the teaching staff and entered an internship at a hedge fund company. Before that, my understanding of finance was limited to what I had learned in my economics classes. Still, I wanted to try out this field that many students flock towards, to understand what makes it interesting and whether it suits me. Over the ten-week internship, I constantly asked myself if the daily work made me passionate, if the lifestyle of my colleagues was something I aspired to. I absorbed financial knowledge and the significance of this industry like a sponge. I miss those busy days, but I also peacefully chose that financial investment wasn't my interest. I understood that others' dream jobs may not necessarily be the best fit for me. Thus, through each exploration and rejection, I gradually discovered my own interests and the kind of life I want to lead. I am lucky that UVa provided so many opportunities for me to experience various possibilities and make choices. These choices also gave me the confidence to complete my preliminary planning for my career.

Two, Exploration of Self

Who am I? What kind of person can I become? This is another question I tried to answer during my time at university. The diverse student groups and individuals at the school gave me deeper insight into these questions. As a public university with nearly 15,000 undergraduates, UVa has a highly diversified student body. Everyone has their own interests and specialties, and everyone adheres to their own life principles and pursuits. To be honest, high school's exam-oriented education once narrowed my perspective significantly, to the point where I never even thought about what kind of person I wanted to become. It was through constant exchanges with friends around me that I realized how different I was from them. Precisely because I witnessed the differences between others and myself, I had the opportunity to reflect on who I truly am.

During my four years, I participated in various activities—from performing as a pirate in a musical to using spring break to volunteer in South Africa, from working on a project for a car brand in Germany to representing the school in interviewing applicants from the Chinese region. These activities documented my growth bit by bit. I remember when I first arrived in the US, I didn't know how to confidently speak up in a group of five people, but later, I could lead more than 30 American students in creating an organization for scientific research pairing. UVa imposes a permanent expulsion penalty on students who commit acts of plagiarism or other dishonest behaviors. When I first arrived in the US, I was shocked by this seemingly overly strict punishment method, but now, I genuinely respect honesty and believe that integrity is the most important virtue of a person.

As a senior student, every time I witness the vitality and enthusiasm of freshmen towards any activity, I can't help but marvel at how the University of Virginia is a place full of possibilities and imagination. Looking back on my four years, I have reaped a full harvest of growth. The omnipresent opportunities and relentless attempts have helped me find myself and the direction of my future. Recalling the impulsive boy who boarded a plane and crossed the ocean all those years ago, I can't help but want to tell him: You are incredibly lucky. (End)

University life is not only a process of accumulating knowledge but also a process of self-exploration. This alumnus opened another window in his life due to a seemingly random decision and found himself and the direction of his future through relentless exploration. He thereby gained brilliance and romance, which is enviable. I look forward to seeing Gaga's lively figure among UVa's red brick walls and green lawns. At the same time, I would like to remind her that success does not come easily; difficulties and setbacks will always accompany brilliance and romance. Success is a momentary concept, while exploration knows no end.
