S.K-Nike Cortez 1990 OG vintage

by dunk9321 on 2010-04-11 14:09:07

This is a pair of Nike running shoes I cherish as a treasure - the 1990 version of Nike Cortez - all red edition. In the past, when I wore DC and VANS, everyone was hyping Nikes. When I wore Air Force 1, people started playing with old basketball shoes. When I wore Dunk lo Sb and air wovan, people started wearing them again... Chinese people have no proper understanding of fashion trends. They follow others blindly, buying what others buy or what the media promotes. Sigh, some people asked me how to keep up with shoe trends. Speechless, I thought for a moment and told him that shoes are like anything else, you can never buy enough. You need to see what suits you. Sometimes, you can consider the advice from magazines and friends. If you really like something and can afford it, just buy it. Don't regret it later! After I finished speaking, he nodded thoughtfully and then asked another question that made me speechless: "What will be popular next year?" What... Since when did I become the trendsetter for sneakers? Isn't this just the cycle of fate? The Cortez sitting at home has now truly become a treasure.

Nike has been aggressively promoting the Cortez series since last year. This year, they haven't given up on using the Fusion technique that was used on AF and Jordan last year, and applied it to this year's Cortez. The latest Fly and the previous Free added to Cortez makes most people feel quite uncomfortable. I wonder if time can dilute all this. For many years, I have loved sneakers more than 11 years. I've worn and owned over 150 pairs. Now that I'm older, my passion for sneakers has gradually faded. Sometimes, I don't even understand why I like this kind of thing, spending 1000-2000 yuan easily. Fortunately, I only like shoes and have no interest in jeans or Jordans. China's counterfeiting technology and "appropriation" are world-renowned. To buy clothes, browsing Taobao can get everything at a very low price. It's good.

This pair of shoes was sent to me by an American friend. I love the look of retro running shoes, with their slightly raised heels, cute yet somewhat arrogant. What's rare and precious is that this pair is the 1990 version, now a one-of-a-kind item. Its 19-year history hasn't caused any damage to the quality of the shoe, which remains as good as new. Thinking carefully, I actually had this color Cortez four times before. The first time was in 1996 when I bought a black and white checkered Vans Silp-on. I accidentally found this Nylon version of Cortez. At that time, I bought it for $43 because of the small size, but later sold it. Later, in 2007, I collected a pair of full red/white hook (all leather) Cortez made in South Africa in 1994. It was still second-hand goods that I let a friend sell (for $350). Last year, I once bought a brand-new Cortez 97 Leather version from eBay in Australia, but due to financial difficulties, I transferred it to another friend. I always feel that I have a connection with Cortez but no fate. Every time, we just missed each other, or I only possessed it temporarily. Well, a little regret makes things perfect, doesn't it?

Another type of fashionista forms differently. There's a kind of fashionista who wears shoes unlike others. They wear what others don't, they go against the flow. They wear brands that are out of print or rarely seen on the market. If others don't wear it and you do, you're a trendsetter. If others wear it and you do too, you're common.
