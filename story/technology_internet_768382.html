Samsung Electronics with $50 billion in hand will actively make acquisitions

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 17:46:10

Samsung Electronics, with $50 billion in cash on hand, will seek more mergers and acquisitions as the largest Asian tech company is concerned that its core consumer electronics business is approaching saturation and is seeking growth beyond this field.

Lee Sang-hoon, the Chief Financial Officer of Samsung Electronics, stated at an analyst meeting held in Seoul on Wednesday that after investing $1 billion into 14 companies since 2010, Samsung Electronics will become more aggressive in its M&A operations. Additionally, Samsung Electronics is considering adjusting its dividend payout to 1% of the average stock price, a move that would mean doubling the dividends paid out to shareholders.

As the world's largest manufacturer of smartphones, TVs, and memory chips, amid concerns that low-cost Chinese manufacturers might lower their product prices, causing these businesses to gradually decline, Samsung Electronics held its first analyst conference since 2005 on Wednesday. Due to increasing reliance on the smartphone business, Samsung Electronics' stock price will experience its first decline in five years this year.

Even though Samsung Electronics currently holds nearly one-quarter of the global mobile phone market share, analysts still worry that the company's growth rate may slow down.

Lee Sang-hoon said, "We will extend our M&A strategy in several areas, seeking broader opportunities." He said that the company hopes to "strengthen the competitive advantages of current businesses and capture new opportunities for future growth." After investing in companies such as Sharp, Novaled, and Medison, Samsung Electronics is now seeking better integration of hardware and software businesses.

Stock Price Decline

Samsung Electronics stated last month that as of September 30, the company held a total cash reserve of 52.68 trillion won (approximately $50 billion). Excluding debt, Samsung Electronics' net cash was 40.93 trillion won, higher than 33.16 trillion won as of June 30.

Samsung Electronics' stock price fell by 2.3% on Wednesday in the Seoul Stock Exchange, closing at 1.451 million won. Since the beginning of the year, the cumulative decline in the company's stock price has reached 4.7%. In June, due to JPMorgan Chase and Morgan Stanley successively lowering Samsung Electronics' revenue and net profit forecasts, citing that the flagship smartphone Galaxy S4 shipments did not meet expectations, the company lost $26 billion in market value. Bloomberg data shows that Samsung Electronics' current dividend yield is 0.55%.

Lee Sang-hoon stated that by the end of this year, Samsung Electronics' R&D investment will reach $14 billion, far exceeding the $8 billion in 2010.

About 400 analysts and technology experts attended the meeting held by Samsung Electronics on Wednesday. The heads of various departments including displays, mobile, chips, and consumer electronics all participated in this meeting.

Capital Expenditure

Marcello Ahn, an analyst at Quad Investment Management in Seoul, said, "Samsung Electronics has grown into a global tech giant, and the company needs to be more open to the outside world. From the current situation, it at least indicates that Samsung Electronics is beginning to consider shareholder returns."

Samsung Electronics' capital expenditure this year will reach a record-breaking 24 trillion won for the company. As of September 30, 63% of the budget has been used. Samsung Electronics' capital expenditure this year will exceed Sony's current market value.

Coo Hyun-Woo, co-CEO of Samsung Electronics, stated that the company's goal is to achieve $400 billion in revenue by 2020. Bloomberg data shows that Samsung Electronics' net profit for the entire year is expected to reach 31.4 trillion won; revenue may increase to 232.1 trillion won.

Samsung Electronics' mobile business accounts for about two-thirds of the company's net profit. In the third quarter of this year, driven by increased demand for mid-range smartphones in China and India, the business achieved an operating profit of a record-breaking 6.7 trillion won. Shin Jong-kyun, head of Samsung Electronics' mobile business, stated that the company will sell over 100 million units of Galaxy S and Note series phones this year.
