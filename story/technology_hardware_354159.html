South Korea Issues School Ban: Cutting Off Online Game Connections at Midnight

April 14, Beijing Time — Currently in South Korea, about 40% of male students are addicted to video games. South Korea is trying to control this issue so that it doesn't worsen. To achieve this, South Korea plans to cut off online game connections after midnight.

According to the Korea Herald on April 12, the Ministry of Culture and Sports announced the cutting-off ban yesterday. The ban requires domestic junior high schools and high schools to choose one of three time periods during which online game connections will be cut off: from midnight to 6 a.m., or from 1 a.m. to 7 a.m., or from 2 a.m. to 8 a.m.

The policy states that once the designated time is reached, a dialog box will pop up in the game, followed by an automatic logout. Additionally, the policy includes a "slowdown" system where internet speed will be throttled after the designated time, causing significant delays for users attempting to log in. Currently, the slowdown system is being tested on four role-playing games, including "Dungeon & Fighter" and "Dragon's Dogma."

Domestic Korean games such as "MapleStory" and "Dragon's Dogma," along with 17 other role-playing games, have been listed as prohibited targets. Notably, "Dragon's Dogma" was developed by South Korea's Eyedentity Games and is operated in China by Shanda. According to statistics, the 19 banned games account for 79% of South Korea's online gaming market.

South Korea is one of the countries most severely affected by online gaming addiction. A recent report cited by the Huffington Post indicates that 30% of students in South Korean public schools are addicted, with male students having a higher addiction rate of 40%.

Not long ago, there were reports of a couple who became so engrossed in online games that their baby tragically died at home.

According to the Ministry of Culture and Sports, the government is also considering new measures to prevent minors from using their parents' identities to register game accounts. New policies are expected to be introduced within six months to a year.

Interestingly, the popular Korean game "Lineage" has not been included on the banned list.
