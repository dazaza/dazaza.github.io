This is an ordinary place, so common that throughout history, not a single somewhat famous person has ever emerged from here.

In the earliest period after my birth, I didn't leave much of an impression. I only remember that at first, we were always staying in a pile of cardboard boxes, with two figures surrounding us. It was only gradually afterward that the concept of "parents" began to form.

After some unknown amount of time had passed, one day, my father forcibly pulled me away from that pile of cardboard boxes and took me to a place outside the city.

When I first arrived here, I noticed that mother was no longer by father's side as she used to be. For a long time, father never mentioned anything about mother. He only taught me how to hunt, including catching mice hiding in holes and fish from a nearby lake. In the beginning, I often tripped over narrow mouse holes or got water in my nose because I couldn’t swim. The worst part wasn’t the pain but the fact that every day, father made me repeat these monotonous actions over and over again.

Out of boredom and frustration, I finally asked father on another painful training day: "Why do I have to do these boring actions every day?"

"Do you remember where the mice and fish you ate when you were young came from? They are living beings too, they don't just stay still, and they certainly don't jump directly into your mouth!" Father said this with a cold expression, showing no emotion.

"Isn't there an easier way?"

"We don't have brains like humans that can endlessly expand and differentiate. We can only see those human settlements built with stones, their height far beyond our imagination, and those various machines made of metal moving around... All these things are created by them using natural resources. They can achieve any purpose for survival, and not just survival, they can fulfill all sorts of desires with these things. But we, we only have the most primitive method — using our own strength to capture the food we need. There are no shortcuts. Once, your mother and I were picking up leftover fish meat from humans, and a human saw us. We had no grudge against him, yet he poured boiling water on us. Luckily, we ran back in time and weren't injured."

Mother… Yes, where is Mother now after all this time?

"Dad, why haven't you mentioned Mother since we've been here? Where is she now?"

"..."

"Dad, tell me!"

"…It seems you're not so small anymore. Fine, you'll find out sooner or later anyway, so I'll tell you everything," Father still showed no expression, but his facial muscles twitched slightly. "Do you know why we are here instead of staying in the old cardboard box area? That day, it was just dawn, and I had just woken up. Suddenly, I felt the trash pile around the box violently shaking. When I went out to check, I found human machines clearing this garbage pile. I didn't have time to think more, so I dragged you, who were still half asleep, out. But when I went back, the cardboard boxes where we lived were already buried under the garbage... Not long after, they built another settlement there. Humans are so greedy. We have no great power, so we cannot take revenge. We can only continue hunting for food to survive."

I felt like crying, but I ultimately held back.

We continued repeating the routine of hunting for food. My skills improved gradually, and the probability of getting hurt dropped to almost negligible levels. Every day, when the warm and dazzling sunlight spread across the earth, we felt tired instead of choosing to lie lazily on the grass and nap. Surrounding shiny objects seemed unclear to us. But when the sun set and the moon adorned the land in another beautiful form, it was the moment we felt most energetic. With practiced techniques, we hunted the prey we discovered. This life, although monotonous, allowed us to stop worrying about survival.

No matter what kind of life goes on for too long, eventually everyone will seek novelty, even if it's the most comfortable life. Father said that if I stayed here forever, I would never grow up.

Gradually, we finally set out to find a new place to live. However, this time, we weren't so lucky. Even after walking for days, we still hadn't encountered an ideal location — mainly due to the lack of water sources. We wanted to go back, but we had already forgotten which direction we came from, so how could we return? There was no other choice but to keep moving forward — either find water or die of thirst.

After another two days and nights, just as we were about to lose hope, a city appeared on the horizon, and below that line, shimmering in the sunlight — it was a river! Oh, let's drink! We had no reason to suppress our joy. After drinking and celebrating, suddenly, a voice came from the side: "You... are you new here? I don't seem to have seen you before."

Only then did we realize there was another cat drinking water next to us. Unlike our red-black mottled appearance, she had silver-white fur — something I had never seen before. Still, Father spoke first: "Yes, we can't go back to our hometown, so we've come out to explore."

That was a good excuse... at least that's what I thought, though I didn't say it aloud.

"Oh, then come settle in our community later. We have many members here, maybe because there are many humans around. This large human settlement, last time I accidentally heard humans talking, it seems to be called 'Chongqing' or something. I don't have the ability to understand human language. We have many members, so it's convenient to work together. We can also connect with some compatriots in other human settlements nearby, and hold regular gatherings. By the way, your place should also be a human settlement, right? Why don't you have a community?"

"Well, perhaps the economy in our place isn't developed enough yet. We agree, we'll register at your community later."

"Alright, then come to a garbage pile closer to the center of this human city later. There are several trash bins that can serve as reference points. I'm going back now."

"Okay, see you later."

Watching her retreating figure, I was still hesitant inside.

"Dad, are we really going there?"

"Otherwise, we can only keep wandering. Living in this world is not easy for us. Having one more companion to support each other gives us more courage to survive."

"Alright, but it seems we need to ask for directions..."

"We can't possibly ask humans; our languages are different. As for compatriots, we probably won't encounter them easily."

"So troublesome, if only humans spoke the same language as us."

"If what you said happened, the world would be in chaos. Alright, let's set off. If necessary, we'll detour a few more times."

We were like refugees fleeing war, worse than refugees actually. Refugees in such a densely populated human area still have opportunities to ask for directions, but we could only rely on our instincts to predict and slowly advance towards the city center — unable to cross the wide roads directly, knowing well what would happen if we encountered those fast-moving human machines.

"We must find the target location before nightfall," Father said, "or we will surely starve to death."

Easier said than done. This human city was like a maze. We turned left and right, and when we finally saw the trash bins she mentioned — we had even forgotten to ask her name — we immediately knew we had arrived.

But we couldn't quite reconcile what we saw with her description — it looked like there was not a single compatriot around, devoid of any vitality. How could this be a community?

Just as we were pondering, suddenly dozens of cats emerged from various piles of debris: "Haha, welcome new members, surprise is our traditional way of greeting."

"As far as I know, we never had the habit of living in large groups. Why are you all living together?" Father was still confused.

They didn't answer. Only an elder, who probably served as the leader, covered in signs of old age, spoke: "We originally didn't live here." He coughed a few times. "We moved here from other cities near this human city. The humans in those cities, in order to continuously expand their construction areas, kept reducing the size of waste disposal zones. Naturally, our living places suffered as well. Many of our clan members were captured and killed by them. We had no choice but to move here. We heard that the mayor here doesn't implement that policy, so it should be safer."

I don't know what Father was thinking, but at this moment, I felt secretly pleased — I could finally feed myself properly, maybe even gain weight, haha.

As expected, the following days turned out to be the best of my life so far — no longer just two individuals catching mice and fish, but coordinated group operations led by the leader. The spoils were distributed according to effort, and the meals were many times better than before. As for entertainment, the occasional gatherings she mentioned gathered all the talented singers and dancers from all directions. Various colored troupes competed brilliantly, making it hard even for professional judges to decide. Oh, wait, her name... during a toast, the leader addressed her — I was sure she was the one from that day — saying, "Fuyuan, please bring some more fish soup." Yes, remembering names comes naturally to me, haha.

Good times always pass quickly. One day, while we were still immersed in the joy of celebration, Fuyuan suddenly raised her voice: "Uh-oh, I hear a sound, very similar to the machines that once cleared our home." As soon as she finished speaking, a wall beside our community suddenly collapsed! We barely had time to gather our belongings! Everyone scattered! Father initially called for me to run with him, but at that moment, a trash bin fell...

I have no memory of what happened after that. Only after an indeterminate amount of time, when the heavy object pressing on me was suddenly removed, followed by a soft, light thing slowly covering me. I struggled to open my eyes. Father, and the others who were previously with us, were nowhere to be seen. Only... a human girl stood in front of me? She opened her mouth, but I paid no attention to what she said. Father had told me before that each species has its unique language, and we fundamentally cannot understand each other. Yet, what followed left me incredulous. "Are you okay?" I widened my eyes — I clearly understood what she was saying! I pinched myself — it wasn't an illusion. "I'm fine." Still a simple sentence, but she was also surprised. I continued: "It seems you can understand our language." "Too flattering, how could I have the ability of the little boy in the movie 'Atmosphere Disappearance'?" "But the reality is that you can understand. Also, what is 'Atmosphere Disappearance'?" "I'll tell you when there's a chance. Right now, your body is very weak. Let me take you to my house to recover." "Wait, where is my father? And my other friends?" "My father is the mayor, he originally didn't plan to do this, but the developers convinced him with profit. Then, they started cleaning this area," she sighed, "Let me take you back. Staying here now is no different from waiting to die. As for whether the adults in my family will allow it, they usually indulge me, so it shouldn't be a problem. Even if not, I can hide you and secretly raise you."

She put me in her schoolbag, leaving a bit of the zipper open. "Bear with it for a bit, I'll wake you up when we get home."

Completely unfamiliar environment, I could only obediently follow.

After an unknown amount of time, I was awakened by her voice. "We're here. My parents aren't home temporarily. Great, I'll go to the fridge and fetch some milk for you to drink."

I didn't refuse, not even a word — any resistance here would be meaningless.

Any beverage I've never tasted always seems delicious. Feeling energized, I couldn't help but lick my lips.

Are girls inherently observant? Whether it's Fuyuan or this currently still unfamiliar human girl in front of me, they both gave me the same feeling. "My name is Dai Wei Ni, what about you?" "...Father never gave me a name." "...Ah, that makes sense. The animals in 'Atmosphere Disappearance' didn't have names either." "By the way, what exactly is 'Atmosphere Disappearance'?" "It's a story movie made by humans. The plot revolves around a train hijacking case, causing three yellow tank cars of toxic substances to leak, burning through the ozone layer above a certain region, putting global life at risk. But that's not the main point. The protagonist is a little boy who can understand the language of a stuffed cat that often stays in his schoolbag, which is quite similar to our situation now. As for the name, I'll give you one." She drank a glass of water and continued, "To be honest, my family background in this city should be considered pretty good. However, living on such a high floor, I've rarely had the opportunity to play with peers since I was young. Every day, I can only study what the elders require me to. No matter how much they buy me in terms of food and toys, it can't change my feeling of boredom. A lot of homework, even after finishing it, I can only stare blankly at the blurred buildings outside the window. By the way, I'll name you 'Mirage'... wait, that's too complicated. Simply put, I'll call you 'Reflection'."

Reflection... Reflection... I silently contemplated this new name she gave me, still not quite accustomed to it.

"Of course, that movie is just similar in this plot point. I'm nothing like the protagonist who bears the responsibility of saving the Earth, let alone being able to bear it alone... Anyway, from now on, you'll sleep in the upper layer of my schoolbag. I'm not quite sure about the adults' attitude yet."

Since then, Wei Ni took me to school every day. She told me to sleep well during class and play after school. The human school was a strange place to me, but despite that, I didn't like being stared at by many pairs of eyes simultaneously.

About a year passed like this without much incident. However, I might have forgotten to ask her about the adults' stance toward me. One day, as usual, she was doing her homework and playing with me. Then, rhythmic and urgent knocking came at the door. Wei Ni hid me and went to open it. She stayed out of the room for a long time, until evening, when she brought out a plate of fish and called me out, indicating I should eat slowly. "A classmate wants to borrow you for a few days. I refused, and then he told my father. My father doesn't approve of you staying here," her tone was still gentle, but her eyes, filled with tears, were fixed on the ground, "Previously, the order to clean up the garbage area also came from my father. The developers told my father that cats and dogs are carriers of various bacteria, and my father believed it." "It's alright, I know how to take care of myself." "Haha. Don't fool me. This is the city center, not undeveloped suburbs. There's basically no source of food for you, and you're not fully recovered anyway. You won't make it to the suburbs." "If I have the will, I can make it." "Even if you have the will, what can you do without the strength? I don't know what you're thinking, but I have a fiancée already. Strangely, regarding you, I somehow feel a kind of love at first sight — a feeling that should only exist between the same species. Love is inexplicable, but the truth is, it exists here. Even if you're not human, just a cat, but someone with emotions, how could I let you go so easily? Moreover, how could you survive outside..." She sobbed slightly. "Eat slowly. They only allow me to keep you for one night. Tomorrow, I have to let you go." I felt like the fish in front of me was as hard as a shell, impossible to chew, and even if I tried, it would take a lifetime to finish. Of course, that was just an illusion. The lights went out, and I lay quietly, the smell in her room probably came from some artificial perfume — but it didn't affect my memory.

The next night finally came. I slowly opened — more accurately, forced open — my single eye. Wei Ni stood at their doorstep, bloodshot eyes clearly showing she hadn't slept well. I walked outside with a poker face, then slowly descended the stairs along the railing. Her gaze remained fixed on me the entire time, as did mine on her. When our gazes lost their targets, I heard a sudden slam of the door, mixed with five parts anger and five parts helplessness. And I, the railing in front of me seemed to lead to a bottomless pit.

I finally reached the bottom of this human residential building. If the journey to that garbage pile near the Chongqing city center had nearly starved us to death, now being in the true center of the city, wouldn't reaching the outskirts be just as fatal as Wei Ni said?

But, where could I go now? There's no place in this human city where I can find food, and Father and the others aren't around to help. ...I hope they're doing well now. Now, I can only gamble with death — either reach the outskirts before collapsing or perish. Any pursuit or goal is discarded. The only thought in my mind now is: survival.

The roads were still filled with various speeding machines, and I still had to take detours. Wei Ni was right, at least theoretically. Just crossing the area cleared last time had already exhausted me. But I kept reminding myself not to lie down, otherwise, I would never have the chance to stand up again. My four legs felt as if they were tied to heavy fish, and eventually, I could only drag myself slowly.

Until I could hardly open my eyes, I finally saw a hint of blue — yes, it was the river where Father and I first came and drank water.

With great difficulty, I crawled to the riverside and pressed my mouth to the water. Unexpectedly, my legs gave out, and my whole body fell into the water...

In a daze, I seemed to hear a voice from Death: "Sleep well..."
