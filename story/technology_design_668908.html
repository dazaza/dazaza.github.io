in just one month, the gang managed to conduct over 20 raids

by 2l7b86g2p on 2012-02-18 16:31:31

Zhang Xiaoming, who is not yet 20 years old, is a freshman in the investigation department of a university in Lanzhou. Both his parents are farmers from Wu Mountain in Tianshuo. During one chance encounter while playing at an animation town near the university with his friends, Zhang Xiaoming became addicted to a slot machine that had gambling characteristics. Before long, he had fed all the living expenses and tuition fees provided by his frugal parents into the slot machine. At this point, having lost everything, Zhang Xiaoming conceived the idea of theft. He noticed that every time he entered the school's office building, all he needed to say was that he was there to see a teacher, and no registration was required. In Zhang Xiaoming's eyes, these teachers who usually taught him were all well-paid, and they had little vigilance against students, making them the easiest targets.

After being arrested, Zhang Xiaoming confessed that between November and December of 2010, he not only repeatedly stole from his alma mater but also extended his black hand to other universities. All his thefts targeted on-duty teachers of various major universities, including some of his own teachers. It was learned that Zhang Xiaoming had devoted all the money he stole to the slot machines. During the trial, Zhang Xiaoming admitted without reservation to the charges brought by the prosecution. The Peace District Court made the above judgment based on Zhang Xiaoming's attitude towards confession.

According to reports (from correspondent Qi Yang and journalist Li Jing), Zhang Xiaoming, originally a top student in the investigation department of a certain university, became so engrossed in gambling machines that he could not extricate himself. Using the investigative skills he had learned, he repeatedly scouted and stole, committing over 20 crimes in just one month, involving an amount of more than 30,000 yuan. All the ill-gotten gains were fed into "slot machines." Just as he was preparing to commit another crime, he was caught red-handed by the police. On September 29th, it was reported that the Peace District Court sentenced Zhang Xiaoming to three and a half years in prison for theft and fined him 5,000 yuan. After the verdict was announced, Zhang Xiaoming expressed his acceptance of the sentence in court.

In November 2010, the student of the investigation department, Zhang Xiaoming, began to apply what he had learned by entering office buildings to steal teachers' belongings. He stole a total of over 20,000 yuan in cash, a laptop, and two mobile phones. Just as Zhang Xiaoming was preparing to steal again, he was caught by the police on the spot.

Currently, journalist Liu Qingxia continues to report on this case.
