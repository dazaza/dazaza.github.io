BEIJING, February 25th - The 2012 Asian Table Tennis Championships continued in Macao. The Chinese women's team defeated the Indian and South Korean teams with two 3-0 victories to advance to the final, where they will face their old rivals from Singapore for the championship. The Taipei and Japanese teams secured the last two spots in the men's team semifinals, with the Taipei team set to meet the Chinese team in the semifinals.

The women's team competition for the championship saw two rounds of matches. In the quarterfinals, China sent out Ding Ning, Guo Yan, and Guo Yue, who easily swept their Indian opponents with three 3-0 wins, advancing to the semifinals with a 3-0 victory over India.

The world champion Singaporean team faced North Korea in the quarterfinals. Wang Yuegu defeated Kim Jong 3-0, Feng Tianwei triumphed over Kim Hoesung 3-0 in the second match, and Li Jiawei sealed the deal with a 3-1 win over Ri Myong Sun, ensuring a 3-0 sweep for Singapore and their advancement.

The South Korean team encountered some difficulties against the Taipei team. Tang Na gave South Korea an early lead, but Shi Lei and Liang Xiayin lost to Li Yizhen and Chen Siyu respectively, leaving South Korea trailing 1-2. Facing elimination, Shi Lei and Tang Na won their next two matches, allowing South Korea to reverse the situation and eliminate Taipei 3-2.

In the evening semifinal, China met South Korea. China made some lineup adjustments, sending out Guo Yan, Ding Ning, and Liu Shiwen. Guo Yan opened with a straight sets 3-0 (11-3, 11-4, 11-9) win over Shi Lei. Ding Ning followed with another 3-0 (11-3, 11-9, 11-8) victory over Tang Na. Liu Shiwen then completed the sweep with a 3-0 win over Liang Xiayin, helping China advance to the final with a 3-0 win.

In the other semifinal, Singapore faced Japan. Feng Tianwei gave her team an early lead with a 3-1 (9-11, 11-8, 11-5, 11-7) victory over Kasumi Ishikawa. In the second match, Ai Fukuhara avenged her earlier loss to Yu Mengyu at the Kuwait Open, narrowly winning 3-2 (8-11, 11-4, 9-11, 11-2, 11-7), leveling the score at 1-1 for Japan. Saori Hirano and Li Jiawei battled it out in the third match, with Hirano prevailing 3-2 (13-11, 7-11, 11-9, 4-11, 11-3), giving Japan a 2-1 lead.

Feng Tianwei steadied the ship in the fourth match, defeating Ai Fukuhara 3-1 (11-9, 7-11, 11-6, 11-9). In the decisive fifth match, Yu Mengyu narrowly edged out Kasumi Ishikawa 3-2 (11-7, 4-11, 8-11, 11-2, 11-9), allowing Singapore to overturn the result and secure a 3-2 victory to enter the final, where they will face China for the women's team championship at the Asian Championships.

The men's team competition for the championship saw the last two quarterfinals. Huang Shengsheng, Wu Zhiji, and Chen Jian'an each contributed a victory, allowing the Taipei team to narrowly defeat India 3-2. Japan's Dan羽 Xiaoxi (Dan羽孝希), Mizutani Jun, and Matsuda Kenta also each secured a victory, helping their team sweep Singapore 3-0. In the semifinals, China will face Taipei, while Japan will play against South Korea.
