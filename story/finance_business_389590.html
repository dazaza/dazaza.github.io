[1] Don't wait for me to tell you to act

Following his previous statement "It's the people who support you, so figure it out yourselves!", Premier Wen's recent remark during a government work report symposium, "Don't wait for me to tell you to act," has once again been warmly embraced by netizens.

Because the urban registered unemployment rate reported in the government work report was 4.6%, while the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences (CASS) released a social blue book reporting an urban surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4%. Why is there a 5.2% difference between the statistics of the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security (MHRSS) and CASS? The public has many questions about this. In response to this "dispute," Premier Wen stated:

"You, the Ministry of Social Labor Security, should hurry up and go online, or quickly hold a press conference, explain the differences between the 4.6% and CASS's 9.4%, don't wait for me to tell you to act."

Why are these two figures different? Director Li Peilin of the Institute of Sociology at the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences explained it like this:

"For nearly two decades, there has been a difference between the urban registered unemployment rate and the urban surveyed unemployment rate, usually the latter being higher than the former. When the job market is relatively stable, the two numbers are close; when the employment situation is bad, there will be a larger gap between the two data points. Because our unemployment insurance does not cover all urban workers, such as migrant workers who have not participated in unemployment insurance, they cannot be counted after becoming unemployed."

However, the MHRSS had another explanation:

"The surveyed unemployment rate of 9.4% is the result of a sample survey of seven thousand people. Among the seven thousand people, there were 2,288 economically active urban residents, of which more than 200 were unemployed, and this number is derived from the comparison of these two."

In fact, for these two sets of data, we ordinary people all have our own opinions:

"Like me, graduates who have worked in the city for only a few years, our hukou (household registration) is still left at the school. Where can we register as unemployed? After becoming unemployed, we can only quickly look for jobs ourselves. How dare we tarnish the national unemployment rate?"

The method of calculation that excludes those who are neither employed nor registered has long been questioned by domestic and international scholars. Premier Wen should make them explain not just the severely underestimated urban registered unemployment rate, but also give us a number that can withstand scrutiny.

Some departments indeed acted without waiting for the premier to speak. Should the total number of working days in a year be unified? In fact, I just want to ask, why did various places call out to each other without reporting to the General Office of the State Council?

[3] Saving money that could be saved by every province

The case of a Yunnan police officer shooting and killing someone, which has already attracted much attention, has recently caused controversy due to an agreement. Mengzi County Public Security Bureau reached a reconciliation agreement with the victim's family, paying a huge compensation of 550,000 yuan to the victim's family.

Regarding the case of Police Officer Ji Zhongchun shooting and killing someone, the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau originally classified it as a personal act, unrelated to his identity and position as a police officer. If it was a personal act, why did the Public Security Bureau pay compensation? Deputy Director Zhao Xiangrong of the Mengzi County Public Security Bureau explained it like this:

"The reason the County Public Security Bureau paid this amount is considering Ji Zhongchun's specific circumstances. Ji Zhongchun is an old police officer who has worked at the County Public Security Bureau for over 20 years, with poor economic conditions at home. Out of humanitarianism, the County Public Security Bureau first gave humanitarian compensation to the victim's family. And these 550,000 yuan come from the office expenses saved by the County Public Security Bureau."

It is well known that the incident occurred because Ji Zhongchun got into a dispute while reversing his car. How can a police officer with a private car be classified as having poor economic conditions at home? This can't help but raise the question: what exactly constitutes good economic conditions?

According to Article 14 of the Regulations on the Use of Police Equipment and Weapons by the People's Police: if a police officer illegally uses weapons, causing unnecessary casualties and property damage, the relevant organ to which the police officer belongs shall compensate according to the relevant provisions of the State Compensation Law. Why doesn't Mengzi Public Security Bureau admit that this compensation is state compensation? If it is not, why does the Public Security Bureau use taxpayers' money to benefit individuals?

[6] Overhyped network entrepreneurship

Suddenly, as if overnight, millions of online stores sprang up everywhere. In the current economic crisis, the internet seems to have become an important way to solve employment problems. Not only do media outlets repeatedly emphasize that students finding jobs online is a great way, but even during the Two Sessions, some representatives suggested that the state should subsidize college students starting businesses on Taobao.

Low cost, low investment, low risk, network entrepreneurship became an instant darling overnight, and many domestic colleges and universities have opened online store training classes to help students start businesses. Yes, if everyone now follows the trend and opens online stores, employment problems can be solved immediately, but what about one year later, ten years later? How many people can truly succeed? Let alone issues like medical insurance and social security. Network security and legal standardization have always been the most neglected difficulties in the information economy tide. Are our experts and professors considering this point while advocating for college students to choose network entrepreneurship?
