A Detailed Explanation of the Factors That八字 (Ba Zi) Destiny Calculation Cannot Determine

by dd26huiuygui on 2011-10-11 14:47:00

Nowadays, more and more friends are paying attention to the art of BaZi fortune-telling. However, many friends often resort to BaZi for every matter. It is important to know that BaZi fortune-telling is not omnipotent, and there are various factors that influence one's destiny. Before seeking a fortune-teller, one should have a rough understanding of BaZi knowledge. This way, when consulting a fortune-teller, you can be more targeted and selective in your calculations, or choose other methods instead of being rejected by the master.

The determining factors of destiny by BaZi are approximately 50% to 80% (BaZi is essentially a time factor), spatial factors (Feng Shui) account for about 30% to 50%, human affairs factors account for about 20% to 30%, and names, as part of human affairs factors, account for about 10% to 20%.

Here is a summary of what BaZi cannot determine:

1. **BaZi cannot determine Feng Shui.** While we can deduce the general situation of Feng Shui from BaZi, it cannot be said that people with such BaZi will necessarily be born in a specific Feng Shui environment. For instance, those with the same BaZi as Mao Zedong were definitely born in different Feng Shui conditions. Mao was born in Shaoshan, while others might have been born in deserts. Conversely, Feng Shui can affect BaZi; an average BaZi under good Feng Shui will certainly bring more blessings, but if the Feng Shui is damaged, blessings will decrease.

2. **BaZi cannot determine bone structure.** Although we can deduce someone’s approximate appearance from BaZi, it cannot predict their bone structure. We cannot deduce from BaZi that Lin Biao’s nobility lies in "the lion looking back," Chiang Kai-shek’s in "the divine turtle emerging," or Mao Zedong’s in having a feminine appearance as a male. There are many people in the world who look identical, yet there are also many people with the same appearance and the same BaZi but different fates, mostly due to differences in bone structure and Feng Shui. In twins, the influence of bone structure and heart structure is most significant.

3. **BaZi cannot determine heart structure.** When the heart structure changes, so does one’s fate, facial features, and even bone structure. The concept in Yuan Jushi's book *Destiny Is Self-established* that destiny cannot constrain extremely evil or virtuous people is based on the theory of heart structure.

4. **BaZi cannot determine names.** If people with the same BaZi should be identical in every aspect, then logically they should not only look alike but also have the same name. This shows that BaZi cannot decide names. Instead, it is correct to say that names can influence BaZi. Names can have certain effects on BaZi through symbolic and numerical principles, but this effect absolutely cannot surpass Feng Shui, bone structure, or heart structure.

5. **BaZi cannot determine genes.** For example, when predicting the appearance of people with the same BaZi, Asians and Europeans will definitely have significant differences. Furthermore, predicting the skin color of people with the same BaZi, Europeans would be white and Africans black, not yellow or white. This is a genetic issue, not something BaZi can determine.

6. **BaZi cannot determine social environment.** If Mao had been born in the twentieth century, he would have been just an official, not an emperor or king. Moreover, over thousands of years, how many times has Mao's BaZi appeared? But why did only the person born in Shaoshan in 1893 rule the country? This is because only such a social environment suits this BaZi, just as seedlings grow well in suitable environments. Without this environment, it would be like a hero having no place to display his prowess. Zhang Zuolin was also shaped by the times; if placed in the present, he would merely be the head of a criminal organization.

7. **BaZi cannot determine nature.** BaZi is a small-scale thing, while nature is vast. When BaZi conflicts with nature, nature decides everything. For example, can we say that all those who died in earthquakes had reached the end of their lives at the same time? Humans are insignificant before nature, and in front of nature, BaZi becomes irrelevant.

There are many things that BaZi cannot determine, but the above are the most fundamental. In fact, Master Huítōng, these points are sufficient to illustrate the issue.
