The roof of the second floor of the kindergarten has always been like this.

by sujie28l63 on 2010-04-10 12:26:02

A kindergarten child climbs on a shabby railing and throws things downstairs. Photographed by Zhang Han. The children often play near the railing after class. Photographed by Zhang Han. On the roof, there is no railing installed, but instead a circle of 1-meter high wire mesh "acting" as one; inside the net on the roof, every day many children play and frolic. This is the playground of a kindergarten named "Little Red Riding Hood Kindergarten" near Daying Village Farmers' Market. Watching the children crouching on this wire mesh every day, the surrounding residents are very worried that an accident might happen one day. This kindergarten is called "Little Red Riding Hood Kindergarten". From the outside, it looks like a two-story building, with the first floor used for commercial purposes and the second floor as the kindergarten teaching area. Around 3:30 in the afternoon, after the bell rings, a group of children run out of the classroom on the second floor. Then, the children appear on the roof of the second floor. Some climb, some lean against, some kick... The children mess around close to the wire mesh at the edge of the roof, without any adult in sight. "Every morning at 8:30, all 180 plus students have to go up to the roof for exercise," said Ms. Wang, who claimed to be just a worker doing odd jobs at the kindergarten. After visiting the surrounding merchants and villagers, it was learned that most of the kindergarten's students are children of migrant workers. Ms. Li, the operator of a nearby cake shop, said she has been doing business around Daying Village since she came to Kunming three years ago. Her child graduated from this kindergarten last year. During the three years her child attended this school, the roof of the second floor of the kindergarten has always been like this. "I often worry, just in case something happens to the child, but fortunately, nothing has happened so far." Looking at the thin row of wire mesh on the roof, Ms. Li said she indeed had concerns. "If a group of children all crowd towards one direction of the wire mesh, it's hard to guarantee that nothing will happen!" Mr. Zhao, Ms. Li's fellow townsman, worriedly said. However, in Ms. Wang's view, the concerns of Ms. Li and Mr. Zhao are unnecessary. "The wire mesh on the roof has been installed since the school moved here seven or eight years ago, and nothing has ever happened so far." As for whether this kindergarten, which has set up a playground on the roof, has legal approval procedures, since the person in charge of the kindergarten was not in the garden yesterday, the reporter was unable to conduct an interview inside the garden. Relevant staff members of the Xishan District Education Bureau stated that they will quickly investigate and provide a response. (Reported by Liu Zhao)
