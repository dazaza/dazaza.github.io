Safe use of hoist construction

by dang000 on 2008-11-27 14:31:13

To ensure the safe use of suspended baskets throughout the process, control should be exercised in five aspects: market research and procurement, installation and commissioning, acceptance and rectification, education and training, and operation.

1. Market Research and Procurement

Conducting surveys on suppliers and purchasing high-quality suspended baskets with structural features and performance parameters that meet the needs of the project is the first step to ensure their safe use. As a fixed asset of an enterprise, this step is also an important part of the early-stage equipment management.

When researching and examining suspended basket manufacturers, one should first check if they have a production license and what their qualification level is. Among many manufacturers with production capabilities, serious evaluations should be conducted to select the best supplier. From the product series recommended by various manufacturers, choose a specific model based on the parameters determined by the construction plan for the project. When selecting the model, compare the main mechanisms of the suspended baskets, including the lifting (climbing) mechanism, safety lock, working platform (suspended basket itself), suspension mechanism, electrical control system, and safety devices in terms of quality and reliability.

The price is an important factor that cannot be ignored when purchasing a suspended basket, but it is not the decisive factor; the decisive factor is quality. High-quality suspended baskets, especially well-known brands, are bound to be more expensive, which is a general rule of the market economy. If one prioritizes price over quality, we will eventually suffer significant losses regarding the normal and safe use of the suspended basket.

Specific operational items when examining the suspended basket manufacturer include: checking the steel test report, the manufacturing process of the lifting mechanism, and the welding process of the basket body; at the same time, examining the operation of the factory's quality assurance system and its after-sales service. Secondly, it is necessary to check the production certificates of major components such as steel wire ropes, safety locks, and electrical parts; if necessary, further examination of the product quality control status of the accompanying production plant is required.

Upon arrival of the ordered suspended basket, the incoming inspection must be carefully conducted. The main inspection items include:

① The lifting mechanism, safety lock, electrical parts, and steel wire ropes must be brand new and come with product certificates; the steel and other auxiliary materials must also be brand new, conforming to the design requirements; the safety lock must also have a calibration certificate issued by a national designated institution; even if unused upon arrival, the safety lock must be recalibrated upon reaching the valid calibration period.

② The suspended basket itself must not have twists, deformations, or cracks.

③ Welds, especially those under stress, must meet the requirements, with smooth surfaces free of defects (such as slag inclusion, unevenness, and burn damage to the base material).

④ Complete random documentation, including product certificates (including certificates for accompanying parts), installation, use, and maintenance manuals (the manual should include detailed installation methods and requirements, installation diagrams, electrical principle diagrams and wiring diagrams, hydraulic system diagrams, lists of easily damaged parts and bearings, etc.).

④ Safety precautions during the installation process, etc.

Before installation work, the person who prepared the plan should conduct technical and safety briefings to all installation personnel, enabling all operators to familiarize themselves with the installation process and work environment, understand their tasks, clarify responsibilities, and know the requirements. During the briefing, a safety officer should participate and sign for record-keeping purposes.

Installation personnel should adhere to a high sense of responsibility towards installation quality, strictly follow the procedures and requirements of the plan during installation, and conduct self-inspections and debugging. During trial operation, the lifting mechanism should run normally; after stopping the machine, whether ascending or descending, reliable braking should occur, meaning the suspended basket itself should not slide down along the hoisting (wire) rope. Pulling the safety (wire) rope at a speed greater than 2.5 meters per minute should cause the safety lock to engage the safety rope. Safety protection devices should be sensitive and reliable.

2. Installation and Commissioning

Relevant data indicates that an important and direct cause of multiple suspended basket accidents is the presence of safety hazards in certain aspects of the suspended basket installation. Therefore, correctly installing the suspended basket is a key process to ensure its safe use.

Suspended basket installation must be carried out by trained professional personnel who have obtained the appropriate certification. Within construction enterprises, suspended basket installation is generally undertaken by professional teams qualified for tower crane installation and dismantling.

Preparing a good suspended basket installation plan is a prerequisite for correct installation. To this end, the plan preparers should develop a targeted and feasible suspended basket installation plan after carefully reading the installation and operation manual, referring to relevant materials, and conducting on-site inspections. According to the enterprise management process, the plan preparation should also be reviewed by relevant departments within the enterprise and approved by the chief engineer before implementation. The installation plan for the suspended basket should include the following contents:

① Project overview. Describe the height, width, and area of the working wall surface, the work content; specify the product model, mechanism principle, operating characteristics, and loading capacity of the configured suspended basket; also draw the layout diagram of the suspended basket on the wall surface and the layout diagram of the suspension mechanism on the roof.

② The width and length of the suspension mechanism extending beyond the wall surface, the width and length laid out on the roof, the weight, number, and type of counterweights should be calculated. Generally, the following formula should be met:

① Steel wire rope: Including the lifting rope and safety rope, these must comply with the relevant regulations of GB5972—86; there should be no damage (tangled wires, burrs, broken wires, indentations, dead bends, looseness, or bulges) or rust; since the suspended basket ascends and descends along the lifting rope and safety rope, the steel wire rope must not be contaminated with grease, mortar, or dirt.

② Suspension mechanism: a) The number and weight of counterweights must be correct and securely placed on the counterweight frame (ensuring no dropping during operation); b) The width, extension length, and supporting length must meet the dimensions specified in the plan; c) The positioning of the suspension mechanism must be reliable (ensuring no displacement during operation).

③ Safety lock: Within the effective calibration period, it must operate sensitively and reliably.

④ Lifting device: The connection between the lifting device and the cantilever beam of the suspension mechanism must be reliable, and the climbing mechanism must operate normally without unusual noise.

⑤ Electrical system: All control buttons and emergency stop switches must be sensitive and reliable; overload limiters and height limiters must be sensitive and reliable; the power cable must include a grounding wire, and the grounding wire must be reliably connected to the metal structure of the suspended basket itself; measure the grounding resistance of the suspended basket, which should be ≤4Ω.

⑥ Operation test: Raise and lower the suspended platform three to four times within a range of 3 meters from the ground; the wall-following rollers should rotate freely, and the climbing mechanism should operate normally. During the load test, when exceeding the rated load, the overload limiter should cut off the power supply; finally, when the suspended platform reaches the highest point of the wall, the height limiter should cut off the power supply.

Any non-conformities found during the acceptance process should be seriously rectified until all items meet the requirements.
