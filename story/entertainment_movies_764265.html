Synopsis:

As a woman approaches her thirties, every choice she makes regarding the other half feels like being at a three-way crossroads—should she go left or right? The men in front of her never seem to satisfy Nie Bing. Perhaps around the next corner lies someone worth staying for. So she keeps turning and moving forward. Reality often doesn't align with wishes—has Nie Bing simply never met the right man, or does she herself not want to settle down? As the movie title suggests, signaling left but going right.

Nie Bing, a single beautiful hostess of a well-known emotional program, wakes up every day from nightmares of growing old alone and dying in solitude. The black cat in her dreams becomes a recurring nightmare in her reality. After breaking up with her previous boyfriend and turning 29, Nie Bing remains alone.

In her own emotional program, when the studio spotlight is turned on, Nie Bing transforms into an emotional expert, helping many troubled couples achieve happy marriages. In real life, however, Nie Bing can only hide in the vast emptiness of her apartment, crying alone in the corner of the sofa.

After getting drunk at a bar, a man claiming to be an angel gives her a magical car, telling her that driving this car will give her ten chances, each turn bringing her a different type of man. Thus, Nie Bing embarks on this magical journey of love.

The first encounter is with a failed pop idol, crazily seeking fame and fortune to the point of madness. Of course, this is just a minor malfunction. After the angel repairs the car, Nie Bing continues her journey. Then she meets a handsome traffic cop with his own fan base, who always warns Nie Bing when she drives irregularly—they meet often but never connect. There's also a wealthy boss who wants a wife to manage him and his money, using every trick in the book to win her favor but failing to capture Nie Bing's heart. There's a fitness coach with a reassuring appearance—a muscle-bound man—but essentially a timid weakling. There's a high-IQ, low-EQ lawyer who champions life's finer tastes yet shifts all the blame for his failed marriage onto helpless women. And there's a Japanese man named Taro with great love for small animals, but he’s an extreme animal protectionist, crossing from romance into fantasy.

There's also her childhood neighbor and secret admirer, Yutong, who returns from abroad to pursue Nie Bing. Though moved by Yutong's sincerity, the age gap of ten years proves too much for Nie Bing to handle...

Who exactly is her Mr. Right? How will Nie Bing make her choice?
