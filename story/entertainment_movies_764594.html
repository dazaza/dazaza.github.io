Jimmy Gator has been hosting the hit TV show "Children's World" for 33 years. Guest Stanley Spack is just three days away from breaking a record set by child prodigy Donny Smith 30 years ago. Spack knows he's merely a tool for public entertainment, while Donny, once a star, now works in an electrical store.

Jimmy also has his own troubles; he’s diagnosed with cancer and has only two months to live. His daughter Claudia, who's just over 20, has a fixation on a neighbor that disrupts family harmony. Mother Rose is worried about her daughter and husband. Lonely police officer Colin arrives at Jimmy's house due to a noise complaint dispute. From the moment Colin sees Claudia, he falls into a painful one-sided love. Claudia harbors deep hatred for her father, becoming Jimmy's biggest source of worry. One day, Jimmy prepares to tell his beloved daughter about his impending death…

In another home located in Southern California, Sir Partridge lies in bed, knowing his time is limited. Standing by his bedside are his young wife Linda and nurse Phil Bema, each with different feelings. Linda regrets marrying the old man for his money but realizes she has fallen in love with him as he approaches death. Bema carries the heavy responsibility of bringing Frank Mackey, the son who ran away from home many years ago, back to the sickbed. A father-son conflict led Mackey to never step foot inside this home again. Seeking his son's forgiveness is the old man's last wish.

Mackey has become a famous TV host, his appearance giving off a sense of decadence, and his prejudice against his stepmother leads him to hate all women…

[Behind the Scenes]

During the editing of "Boogie Nights," Paul Thomas Anderson conceived the idea for "Magnolia." As the editing neared completion, Anderson began to outline the script. With the overwhelming success of "Boogie Nights," New Line Cinema informed Anderson that he could shoot his next film freely without any constraints. Realizing this opportunity might not come again, Anderson quickly signed with New Line's production supervisor Mike De Luca to produce "Magnolia," even though De Luca knew nothing about the film's content at the time.

Anderson initially wanted to make a small, intimate movie with a shooting schedule of only 30 days. Before starting the script, he had already thought of naming it "Magnolia." As the script developed, Anderson kept expanding its length to create roles for many actors, aiming to craft an unprecedentedly brilliant San Fernando Valley movie. The story is pieced together from many fragments, and Anderson started from images, texts, scene ideas, lists of actors, and music. He first envisioned Melora Walters' smiling face, then chose Philip Baker Hall as her father, imagining their intense confrontation. Anderson also researched magnolia trees, discovering that eating magnolia bark can help treat cancer.

As a loyal fan of "Boogie Nights," Tom Cruise contacted Anderson during the filming of "Eyes Wide Shut." Anderson met Cruise on set, where Cruise expressed interest in starring in Anderson's next movie. Soon after, Cruise read the script and, although intrigued by the story, had reservations about the character Frank. Anderson based Frank on recordings of a friend's engineering class, including bold statements about women that left Cruise uncertain. However, Anderson believed Cruise was perfect for this strange and exaggerated new role since he had portrayed a repressed character in "Eyes Wide Shut." Eventually, after much persuasion from the film's creators and Mike De Luca, Cruise agreed to take on the role.
