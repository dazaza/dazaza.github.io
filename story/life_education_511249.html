The Youngest Sixteen-Year-Old Doctor, Zhang Xinxiang, Criticizes White-Collars on Weibo: Don't Want to Buy a House and Become a "Beipiao"

by edivista on 2011-10-19 08:47:50

Click to enter Zhang Xinxiang's Weibo

Zhang Xinxiang, who once used his parents' house purchase in Beijing as the content of his master's thesis defense, has recently received much media attention due to his remarks. The other night, he sparked heated discussions among netizens again after claiming on Weibo that white-collar workers were useless for Beijing's development. Last night, this 16-year-old doctoral student told our reporter that he wanted his parents to buy a house so that he wouldn't become a "Beijing drifter," and hoped to live with dignity.

Since entering Beihang University, Zhang Xinxiang, the youngest doctoral student, has always been the focus of media attention. Recently, CCTV's "See" program conducted an exclusive interview with him, and afterward, this video was widely shared and commented on Sina Weibo. In the interview, this 16-year-old doctoral student defined the standard of success as having a Beijing hukou (household registration), owning a house, and finding a good job, which sparked heated discussions among netizens. His demand that his parents pay in full for a house for him in Beijing also pushed him into the spotlight. (Click to enter Zhang Xinxiang's Weibo)

The other night, Zhang Xinxiang engaged in a dialogue with netizens on Sohu Weibo. When discussing the construction of Beijing's urban image, he proposed that white-collar workers contributed nothing to Beijing's development but caused severe damage, consuming Beijing's precious resources and job opportunities. He believed that Beijing should restrict outsiders, and some of his statements about outsiders were direct and even offensive. These remarks also caused dissatisfaction among some netizens.

When the reporter reviewed Zhang Xinxiang's Weibo last night, these remarks had already been deleted. The reporter contacted Zhang Xinxiang, who was polite and cautious on the other end of the phone. He first stated: "If you want to interview me, please contact my school mentor and my parents beforehand; they don't want me to give any more interviews." Regarding his remarks about white-collar workers, he said: "White-collar jobs require neither physical nor mental effort; anyone can do them. If anyone can do them, why not let Beijing residents do them instead of outsiders?" Regarding the controversial issue of asking his parents to buy a house in Beijing, Zhang Xinxiang explained: "If I don't buy a house and continue working in Beijing, wouldn't I just be another 'Beijing drifter'? He hopes to live with dignity. Finally, Zhang Xinxiang expressed again his wish to keep a low profile.

The evening before, CCTV broadcast an exclusive interview with Zhang Xinxiang, China's youngest doctorate. Facing the camera, the 16-year-old Zhang Xinxiang frankly said that before his master's thesis defense, he had demanded that his parents fully fund the purchase of a house for him in Beijing, otherwise, he would not defend his thesis or take the doctorate exam. "They most hoped I would stay in Beijing; they should make efforts for it." His remarks quickly sparked heated discussions among netizens. Many comments questioned whether he had a healthy mindset despite having a high academic degree.

Through several contacts and interviews, the reporter learned that Zhang Xinxiang's relationship with his parents was very average, even cold. As he put it, the values of two generations were completely different, making communication impossible.

The housing issue seems to have been a knot in Zhang Xinxiang's heart for quite some time. He is familiar with the activity areas of Beijing's "ant tribe." Watching shows like "Snail House" and "Naked Marriage" wasn't just for entertainment for him; the pressure of buying a house, which others face, he also faces. This has gradually made him feel insecure. In fact, seven years ago, even before coming to Beijing to study for his master's degree, Zhang Xinxiang had discussed with his parents the idea of buying a house in Beijing as soon as possible. At that time, buying a house in Daxing could secure a Beijing hukou, and the housing prices were not expensive. "I told them, but they didn't listen."

Regarding online opinions, Zhang Xinxiang calmly told the reporter yesterday that he had already gone online to check, and he would rationally handle them. Reasonable suggestions he would consider, but it's impossible to make everyone understand me, so I'll still maintain a normal attitude. Zhang Xinxiang did not expect that his remarks would cause such a strong public reaction. Now, I just want to get out of all the disturbances as soon as possible and pursue my studies quietly without being disturbed.

His mentor, Professor Zhou Meng, also expressed the same idea when interviewed by reporters, hoping that the media and the public concerned about Zhang Xinxiang would not overly focus on him, allowing him to settle down and conduct professional research. In Professor Zhou Meng's view, it is not easy for Zhang Xinxiang to complete his doctoral studies, and he even speculated that Zhang Xinxiang would need at least one more year to graduate.

Currently, apart from conducting research with his mentor, Zhang Xinxiang also works as a teaching assistant every Thursday evening and corrects 60 assignments each week. He said that although it is tiring, it is also fulfilling and joyful.
