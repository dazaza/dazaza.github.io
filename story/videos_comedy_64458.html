Plot introduction:

The male protagonist Ji Cunxi (played by Johnny Yan) has the power to decide whether the island will be developed or not. However, the owner of the island only wants to protect this piece of pure land and does not want it to be used as a money-making tool that destroys the environment. The owner came up with an outrageous plan to "design Cunxi" in order to stop the development of the island...

Chen Xinyi (played by Ruby Lin), an ordinary office lady, longs to tie down her handsome and upright boyfriend. By chance, her boyfriend invites her on the Love Boat. Secretly rejoicing, Xinyi starts planning how to use the old trick of "losing her virginity to bind him". However, things don't go as planned. Due to seasickness and excessive drinking, she ends up vomiting uncontrollably and mistakenly enters the wrong room...

Cunxi is planning to propose to his girlfriend on this day. He sets up a proposal banquet on the Love Boat, but unexpectedly, the proposal fails. His girlfriend doesn't even board the ship. The island owner drugs Cunxi's drink. After consuming it, Cunxi's mind becomes unclear and he returns to his room. Meanwhile, Xinyi, who is vomiting all over the place, sees her boyfriend enter the room and decides to take advantage of this opportunity...

However, things don't go as planned. Not only does Xinyi enter the wrong room, but she also ends up with the wrong person. Even worse, she... gets pregnant...

This story begins with a type of girl that exists throughout Asia: the "sticky note girl." She is simple, unassuming, content with small wishes, and has never been anyone special nor expects to become one. Her function may be small, but she is indispensable, just like a sticky note that can be torn off at any time - inconspicuous and ordinary, but you will definitely find her around! A trip on a 'luxury cruise' brings such an ordinary girl into the most extraordinary situation. Since childhood, Xinyi has always been afraid of others disliking her, so she works hard to be nice to others. Whenever someone gives her a little response, she easily develops feelings for them. Everyone calls her a "sticky note girl" because when someone needs her, they tear her off and use her, and when they don't need her, they throw her away carelessly. But she is actually a sentimental fool. Her lack of strength makes her often hesitate when she should make a decision. One day, in order to win back her playboy boyfriend Gucci, she spends a lot of travel expenses to go on a love boat tour. Unexpectedly, due to a series of mishaps, she wakes up to find herself having spent a night with a stranger, only to discover that her boyfriend Gucci has been unfaithful, hooked up with a wealthy widow, and left her behind. This comes as a shocking blow.

A month later, when Xinyi sees the result of the pregnancy test, she regrets not jumping off the ship. At 25 years old, working as a clerk at a law firm, she is now in debt for 200,000 yuan due to a lousy boyfriend and is carrying a child from an unknown man. It's the most ironic joke in the world. She takes a week off from work and prepares to return to her hometown, "Ginger Island," to figure out what to do.

The stranger in bed that day was named Ji Cunxi. He is the ideal prince charming in every girl's dream - wealthy, handsome, young, and talented. When he encounters the ordinary office lady, Chen Xinyi, it marks either the beginning of trouble or the start of happiness.

Due to the hidden genetic problem in the Ji family, each generation has had fewer male descendants, continuing through nine generations. This makes Pearl Grandma restless every day, fearing that the family line might accidentally break! Grandma hopes that her grandson Ji Cunxi can be like other rich heirs and sow wild oats everywhere. However, this grandson remains clean and self-respecting. One day, Grandma accidentally discovers that Xinyi is carrying Cunxi's child. Perhaps it is the blessing of the Ji ancestors; unexpectedly, heaven has truly bestowed upon her a "little golden grandson." Grandma insists that Cunxi marry Xinyi at all costs. This way, when she passes away, she can face the ancestors of the Ji family with dignity.

Grandma uses the threat of exposing Cunxi's three-year girlfriend to force him to marry Xinyi. Being "tricked into marriage," Cunxi reluctantly marries Xinyi to fulfill Grandma's last wish while struggling to face the reality of marriage. Unable to decide whether to run away or bravely take responsibility, they suddenly realize they must start dating under Grandma's request.

In fact, Cunxi has a girlfriend named ANNA, a ballet dancer with whom he has been in a relationship for three years. During this period, he romantically proposed multiple times, but ANNA kept giving him the cold shoulder. Still, Cunxi silently waited, hoping that one day ANNA would let go of everything and agree to marry him. ANNA chose not to marry for her career, while Cunxi unintentionally got Xinyi pregnant. Fortunately, ANNA is on an overseas performance tour, allowing Cunxi to hide this awkward incident. However, without Grandma's knowledge, Cunxi signs a prenuptial agreement with Xinyi, stating that they will divorce after the child is born. Cunxi promises to give Xinyi a sum of money and then part ways without owing anything. Xinyi, considering the future of her unborn child, agrees after careful thought. Cunxi plans that once Xinyi gives birth, ANNA will finish her overseas performance and return home, marking the expiration of his contract with Xinyi, allowing him to reunite with ANNA.

Unaware Xinyi moves into Cunxi's home and starts learning to get along with him. The evolution from discomfort to habit between two strangers is very subtle. Originally thinking they were just living under the same roof without interfering with each other, their lives gradually intertwine due to many daily trivial matters. Under Grandma's request, Xinyi moves into Cunxi's bedroom. Cunxi finds excuses to sleep in the guest room but eventually realizes he dislikes sleeping alone. In the middle of the night, Cunxi always returns to the original room, and over time, having someone beside him while sleeping becomes a habit. Cunxi, who used to dislike coming home for dinner, starts returning home for meals. Cunxi, who vowed never to help with anything, begins selecting wallpapers for the nursery and toys for the children. Just as they think this is happiness, the previously ignored and concealed bombshell, ANNA, appears. Cunxi never expected that his unclear relationship with ANNA would hurt Xinyi. He leaves behind a divorce agreement and disappears. Thinking this ending is the best conclusion, Xinyi unknowingly occupies his heart. He swears that if he ever finds Xinyi again, he will chase her back, no longer as the father of the child to the mother of the child, but as a man pursuing a woman...
