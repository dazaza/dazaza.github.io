Yu Zhengsheng replies to cancer patients' relatives via Weibo

by abcurtbt on 2012-03-01 15:55:25

Yu Zhengsheng replied through the [Guangzhou Announce (Weibo)] Weibo platform and made a phone call: The problems caused by the shortcomings of the medical system may be difficult to solve thoroughly in the short term, but I believe that your pain is also the pain of everyone, and the resonance of everyone will promote our progress.

Chief reporter Sun Gang

[Unexpectedly, the article I posted online out of temporary emotion has drawn so much attention. Around 5 p.m. in the evening, Secretary Yu personally called me to inquire about the situation and arranged for my father's treatment.] Last night, high school Chinese teacher Qinling (Weibo) emotionally informed .www.baode.cc

Qinling's father was recently diagnosed with lung cancer and was sent to the Pulmonary Hospital for treatment. Due to the severity of his condition, he could not undergo surgery or chemotherapy and could only return home for rest. On February 4th, the elderly man suddenly suffered from a stroke and was rushed to the emergency department of Changhai Hospital, where his condition improved. However, due to the tight availability of wards, three days later, the hospital requested that the patient be transferred. After rushing around, Qinling managed to contact the Dongshi Hospital, but after 18 days of his father's discharge, the hospital cited reasons such as the need for bed turnover and giving priority to acute patients, requiring him to be discharged again. Helplessly, Qinling transferred his father to Kongjiang Hospital, where he temporarily found a place to settle down.

However, Qinling still felt uneasy in his heart: Would the hospital ask us to transfer again in one or two weeks? Would my old father have to go through another round of trouble? He wrote down his experiences over the past few days into an article, which he addressed to Comrade Yu Zhengsheng, the Secretary of the Guangzhou Municipal Committee, and posted it on his blog yesterday afternoon. In the blog post, he recounted the difficulties he had experienced over the past month while accompanying his father through multiple hospitals. He also proposed demands and suggestions such as [providing a dignified, stable, and safe medical environment for early-stage cancer patients], [supporting and encouraging social forces to provide psychological counseling, companionship, and other charitable services or volunteer actions for early-stage cancer patients and their families]. This blog post was reprinted by many websites. www.tingfeier.com

After seeing this blog post, Secretary Yu Zhengsheng replied to Xiao Qin through the [Guangzhou Announce] Weibo platform yesterday.

Upon being informed, the Health Bureau immediately contacted Xiao Qin through relevant departments to understand and confirm the situation, indicating that they would definitely make reasonable arrangements to address his urgent needs and allow his father to focus on his treatment.

At 5 p.m., just as Qinling was heading to the hospital, he received a call from Secretary Yu Zhengsheng. In the phone call, Yu Zhengsheng expressed concern for Xiao Qin's father's condition and inquired whether the health management department had already made arrangements. After receiving confirmation, Yu Zhengsheng stated that the problem of early-stage cancer patients having difficulty accessing hospital beds indeed exists, caused by the current flaws in the medical system. Although it may be difficult to resolve completely in the short term, the voices of patients and their families deserve attention, and the Party and government have the responsibility to accelerate reforms and strive for breakthroughs as soon as possible. www.syzc020.com

After listening to Yu Zhengsheng's words, Qinling said his "mind became settled." In the phone call, he informed that the purpose of writing this article online was not only to reflect the suffering of his own family but also to hope to attract social attention to the difficulties others are also encountering. However, he did not have any "high expectations" for what kind of impact it might have. But Yu Zhengsheng's Weibo reply and return call made him very pleased: [A simple appeal from an ordinary citizen has attracted personal attention from important leaders of the Municipal Committee—this was truly unexpected!]

At around 8 p.m., Qinling returned the call, saying, [The leadership of the Yangpu District Health Bureau (Weibo) and the leadership of Kongjiang Hospital have come to visit, indicating that they will arrange for my father's continued treatment at Kongjiang Hospital based on his medical needs or transfer him to another hospital if necessary. My mother and I are both very satisfied!]

Qinling also mentioned that after publishing the blog post, more than a dozen patients and their families with similar experiences had called to express condolences and care. [While expressing gratitude, I also conveyed Secretary Yu's words to them: This is a systemic issue that cannot be resolved all at once, but the timely response of the Party and government to our voices has made me feel warmth and care. I believe that in the near future, this problem will be perfectly resolved, allowing early-stage cancer patients like my father to have a good treatment environment and fully demonstrating the dignity of everyone's life.]

Your pain is also everyone's pain

Comrade Qinling:

After reading your open letter, I feel very heavy-hearted. Everyone has parents, everyone has relatives. When you see a beloved relative who raised you fall critically ill and find yourself unable to help, further compounded by some institutional flaws, the pain in your heart is self-evident.

We will all work hard to help you, and more importantly, comrades support your four points of demand, especially concerning the care for early-stage cancer patients, striving for progress in the system. I cannot guarantee that all issues can be resolved quickly, but I believe that your pain is also everyone's pain, and the consensus of everyone (including the hospital staff) will drive us forward.
