Taobao Draws a National 'Insomnia Map': Shanghai Has the Most Sleepless People

by anonymous on 2013-07-24 16:00:15

"Every day, I go to bed around 11 o'clock, then look at videos and browse online stores on my phone. I don't feel sleepy until around 1 o'clock." Mr. Ding, a 30-year-old white-collar worker who works in a commercial building on Moganshan Road in Hangzhou, complained. In fact, more and more people are beginning to have trouble sleeping. "People who sleep well are all similar, but those who can't sleep each have their own reasons," the Taobao store owners take advantage of this trend to sell "sleep aid" products.

Yesterday, a set of data published by Taobao was also quite interesting. The group of people who can't sleep has very obvious regional characteristics. In the top 5 cities with the highest per capita purchase volume of sleep aids such as eye masks and earplugs over the past 5 months, all four first-tier cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen - made the list collectively. Hangzhou is the only non-first-tier city among the top five, ranking third.

The most active "insomniacs":

23 million people shop online at midnight

"At this time, is there anyone still awake?" Nowadays, such late-night insomnia tweets have become ubiquitous. "Insomniacs" instantly transform into "night shoppers", shopping on mobile Taobao while lying in bed. Their passion for shopping does not wane due to the quietness of the night; instead, late night becomes one of the times when mobile online shopping orders are placed frequently.

"In such hot weather, I definitely won't go shopping during the day. When I can't sleep at night, I use that time to shop," said Ms. Li from Hangzhou, a typical night owl. She said she often doesn't feel sleepy after 12 o'clock, so she opens her mobile phone and browses Taobao. "Yesterday, I bought two pairs of sandals and one dress within an hour." Finally, with the satisfaction of shopping, she finally fell asleep. However, money just flows out every day.

There are many such "night shoppers". According to the data published by Taobao, in 2012, a total of 22.832 million people shopped on Taobao between 11 PM and 5 AM. Among them, men who stay up late to shop on Taobao are not few, accounting for 48% of the total, while women account for 52%.

The most insomniac provinces:

Jiangsu, Guangdong, Zhejiang

What to do if you really can't sleep? More and more people start using external tools like earplugs and eye masks to fall asleep. Clever online store owners have obviously already spotted the business opportunity. I searched for earplugs on Taobao and found more than 200,000 items for sale, and nearly 90,000 eye masks.

The "sleep aid market" is quite promising. Just the product of eye masks comes in various types: 3D, cartoon patterns, summer-appropriate ice packs, silk eye masks, bamboo charcoal eye masks, etc. Since the start of summer, ice pack eye masks have become very popular. I saw on one Taobao store that the monthly transaction volume of a single item had exceeded ten thousand pieces.

There are also many sellers making big moves with pillows, launching calming lavender pillows, buckwheat pillows, etc., and even foods like millet and dates are labeled as promoting sleep.

The "insomnia map" shows that according to Taobao's transaction data over the past 5 months, from the proportion of people buying earplugs and eye masks in relation to the total population of the city, all the first-tier cities - Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen - made the top five. Hangzhou, which ranks third, is the only non-first-tier city among them.

Top 10 cities for purchasing earplugs: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chongqing, Ningbo.

Top 10 cities for purchasing eye masks: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chongqing, Nanjing, Suzhou, Wuhan, Chengdu.

Shanghai can be considered the most severely insomniac city. Jiangsu, Guangdong, and Zhejiang are the three provinces with the worst sleep.

"The cycle of 'can't sleep at night, can't wake up in the morning' has formed a vicious circle. The cities where people sleep less at night are also the cities where people wake up latest in the morning. In the past 5 months, the top five cities in terms of total population purchasing alarm clocks are the same: Shanghai, Beijing, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou."

"I have to wear earplugs every night to fall asleep, but then I can't hear my phone's alarm in the morning. After setting my phone alarm to vibrate, I'm afraid it won't be powerful enough to wake me up, so I bought another vibrating alarm clock." Xiao Mao's "sleep aid" and "wake-up" equipment has become essential for many insomniacs.

The most helpless reason for buying earplugs:

Husband snores

So, who are the insomniacs? Online store owners know best. "The number of people who need earplugs to sleep increases every year, and there are more customers consulting, and the age range is getting wider. Basically, students and urban white-collar workers account for 90% of the total sales," said the owner of an online store selling earplugs.

Indeed, the store's flagship product - a 19 yuan earplug - sold 19,000 pieces in a month.

Why can't everyone sleep? There are actually many reasons. It is understood that students who buy eye masks and earplugs may live in school dormitories. Mobile devices are very popular among students. After the bedtime, some classmates in the same dormitory may still be playing games. To avoid being disturbed, they might as well "seal themselves off".

White-collar workers, on the other hand, experience greater work pressure during the day, leading to sleep problems at night. Some buyers amusingly state their reason for buying earplugs is "my husband snores too loudly."

According to Taobao data, most of the buyers of earplugs and eye masks are from the "post-90s" and "post-80s" generations. Taking the eye mask sales over the past 5 months as an example, the "post-90s" is the largest demand group, with 36% of the eye masks purchased by people aged 18-24. Additionally, people aged 25-29 also account for more than 30% of the total.

From the perspective of the purchase ratio of various products, men are more likely to suffer from insomnia than women, with about 20% more male insomniacs than female.
