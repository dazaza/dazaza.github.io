Yellow movies: A type of feature film, named for its predominantly yellow tones. The Japanese productions in this category are the most outstanding. These films generally have low budgets; sometimes they can't even provide costumes for the actors. Everyone says Japan is wealthy, but their yellow movies aren't as grand in production as Zhang Yimou's "Hero."

Mao movies: A kind of pirated disc, called Mao movies due to their rough production quality and sometimes having burrs, which infuriates the common people. However, some lawless individuals associate Mao movies with tribute films honoring the great leader Chairman Mao, also abbreviated as Mao movies, which is extremely infuriating and shameless!

A movies: Those who have passed English level four know it refers to American movies - American movies - A movies. It is reported that recently popular films like "The Lord of the Rings" and "Cold Mountain" are all A movies. Our country imports a certain number of A movies every year, but to avoid impacting domestic films, the China Film Group Corporation must strictly select A movies and there are quantity restrictions. Only excellent A movies that conform to traditional Chinese values are imported. Although protecting wild animals is good, A movies that excessively "pamper" wild animals will not be introduced.

AV idols: This term is not yet widely known among teenagers because it requires a high level of English. A, as mentioned earlier, represents America, and V represents Vermont (VERMONT). The people of this state are very talented, so they are called "AV idols, AV male idols." Since the women of this state are especially talented, AV idols are more commonly seen.

Pole dance: A folk dance originating from the United States, created and performed by the working class. Initially, it spread among some construction workers who danced and sang holding building steel pipes, showcasing the cheerful and optimistic national character of Americans. Pole dance is one of the world's top ten folk dances.
