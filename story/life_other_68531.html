I am in Haidian District, Beijing. My office is on the 8th floor, and I felt a slight sway. At first, I thought it was just me feeling dizzy.

Later, I realized everyone in the office felt it. It lasted for a few minutes. Just now, my computer desk kept shaking and made a faint "woo woo" sound. At first, I thought it was the optical drive working, so I didn’t pay attention (PS. If the optical drive can shake the whole desk, that’s not normal either, haha). But soon after, the windows and doors started making soft sounds. Initially, I thought it was the wind playing tricks, but then I clearly felt myself and my chair swaying slightly and continuously. The whole process lasted two or three minutes. That's when I realized, ha, it was an earthquake. I ran to the balcony and indeed felt our building swaying continuously. I wasn't scared at all; I was more excited than fearful. After all, in this small town, earthquakes are very rare.

I was thinking, somewhere on this planet, there must have been a big earthquake today. Otherwise, why would it affect us here?

Earthquakes can actually be quite fun... I'm looking forward to the next one lasting longer. Of course, the intensity and magnitude could be a bit higher, HOHO, as long as buildings don't collapse, I can accept it~ HOHO~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
