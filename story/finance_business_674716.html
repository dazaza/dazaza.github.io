Is it good to use iPad in education? Will introducing iPad into classroom teaching become a trend?

by anonymous on 2012-02-24 15:17:55

Modern technology is highly developed, and mobile devices are becoming increasingly popular. So would it be a good idea to use some tech devices in the classroom? This has become an issue of concern for many parents and teachers. A recent teacher exchange conference held in Hangzhou also considered and discussed this issue. More than 400 teachers participated in the listening and exchange session on that day. Most were in favor, some had doubts, and none opposed it. The teachers who had doubts all cited the reason that iPads have not been recognized by authoritative experts. A veteran teacher expressed that it is unclear what the long-term impact of electronic and flat teaching methods will be on children's future, but at least so far, the observed situation is still quite encouraging, because students really like using iPads for teaching demonstrations.

However, after conducting surveys and interviews at some schools, it was found that there are currently no schools in China that completely use iPads as teaching tools; they only incorporate iPads into their teaching. Principal Wang from a certain experimental elementary school believes that as long as time is well controlled and content is carefully selected, it is safe to let children use iPads. It can be seen that one of the major issues facing the decision of whether or not to use mobile devices such as iPads in the classroom is content. This sets a relatively high threshold for developers aiming to create children's app products. Of course, high risk often brings high returns, so it can be foreseen that competition in the children's app market will become increasingly fierce, and competition for product inclusion and publishing platforms will also intensify. There may even be a need for specialized media dedicated to children's applications to meet market demands. Moreover, such sites have already appeared domestically, such as 7kiwi and Engineer Dad. Perhaps in the future, traditional app markets will also specifically open sections targeting the children's market to join this battle.

We cannot predict the future, but education concerns the long-term plan of a century. Using mobile devices in the classroom is a unique and innovative approach. Although we do not know how far this path will go, it is worth our anticipation.
