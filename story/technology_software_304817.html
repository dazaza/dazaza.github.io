Record of the 16th round of Serie A matches: AC Milan lost only one game at home, Inter Milan has a small advantage.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-13 12:49:40

Round 16 of Serie A: AC Milan only lost once at home, Inter Milan has a slight advantage. According to Sina Sports, at 12:00 AM Beijing time on December 13, the 16th round of matches in the 2009-2010 Serie A season will be held. This weekend, the defending champion Inter Milan will play away against Atalanta. After securing group qualification in the Champions League earlier in the week, the focus of the Nerazzurri has shifted back to the domestic league. Although they lost in the Derby d'Italia and their point gap was narrowed, the advantage of the Nerazzurri is hard to surpass. AC Milan will face Palermo, an inconsistent team, at home. If the Rossoneri can start with a dream beginning again, they should be able to win consecutively. Additionally, Juventus will play away against mid-table team Bari, Fiorentina will play away against relegation-threatened Chievo, and Roma will also play away against Sampdoria, who had a very sharp attack at the start of the season.

Below are the specific historical head-to-head records (home teams listed first):

Atalanta vs Inter Milan:

The two teams have faced each other 98 times in Serie A history, with Atalanta winning 19, drawing 23, and losing 56, scoring 89 goals and conceding 176; at home, Atalanta has won 12, drawn 16, and lost 21, scoring 52 goals and conceding 81.

AC Milan vs Palermo:

The two teams have only faced each other 44 times in Serie A history, with AC Milan winning 27, drawing 9, and losing 8, scoring 77 goals and conceding 29; at home, AC Milan has completely dominated, winning 19, drawing 2, and losing 1, scoring 56 goals and conceding 13.

Bari vs Juventus:

The two teams have only faced each other 56 times in Serie A history, with Bari winning 5, drawing 16, and losing 35, scoring 97 goals and conceding 122; at home, Bari has been at a disadvantage, winning 5, drawing 11, and losing 12, scoring 25 goals and conceding 43.

Chievo vs Fiorentina:

The two teams have only faced each other 10 times in Serie A history, with Chievo winning 1, drawing 1, and losing 8, scoring 7 goals and conceding 16; at home, Chievo has not won any games, drawing 1 and losing 4, scoring 3 goals and conceding 9.

Sampdoria vs Roma:

The two teams have faced each other 104 times in Serie A history, with Sampdoria winning 32, drawing 30, and losing 42, scoring 107 goals and conceding 136; at home, Sampdoria has dominated, winning 25, drawing 19, and losing 8, scoring 67 goals and conceding 40.

Lazio vs Genoa:

The two teams have faced each other 78 times in Serie A history, with Lazio winning 28, drawing 23, and losing 27, scoring 117 goals and conceding 110; at home, Lazio has dominated, winning 20, drawing 16, and losing 3, scoring 70 goals and conceding 29.

Parma vs Bologna:

The two teams have only faced each other 20 times in Serie A history, with Parma winning 6, drawing 7, and losing 7, scoring 21 goals and conceding 21; at home, Parma has slightly dominated, winning 3, drawing 5, and losing 2, scoring 10 goals and conceding 8.

Cagliari vs Napoli:

The two teams have only faced each other 48 times in Serie A history, with Cagliari winning 13, drawing 19, and losing 16, scoring 37 goals and conceding 46; at home, Cagliari has dominated, winning 9, drawing 12, and losing 3, scoring 22 goals and conceding 14.

Siena vs Udinese:

The two teams have only faced each other 12 times in Serie A history, with Siena winning 2, drawing 4, and losing 6, scoring 13 goals and conceding 20; at home, Siena has been slightly at a disadvantage, winning 1, drawing 3, and losing 2, scoring 9 goals and conceding 10.

Catania vs Livorno:

The two teams have only faced each other 4 times in Serie A history, with Catania winning 2 and losing 2, evenly matched, scoring 5 goals and conceding 7; at home, Catania has maintained a perfect record in 2 games, scoring 4 goals and conceding 2.
