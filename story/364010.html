11 Most Sophisticated Methods to Deal with Problems

by diglogtest on 2010-09-08 12:45:37

 

(1) Learn to be silent

Sometimes, when you are misunderstood by others and don't want to argue, you choose silence. Not everyone needs to understand you, so you think it's unnecessary to shout at the world. However, there are times when you are misunderstood by the person you love most. You feel too sad to argue and can only choose silence. The whole world may not understand you, but he should. If he cannot understand you, what else is there to say? Life often has moments where even Schubert would be speechless, as not all rights and wrongs can be clearly listed. Sometimes, there may not even be a real right or wrong. So, if you don't want to talk, then don't. In situations where talking more won't help, perhaps silence is the best explanation.

(2) At least remain calm

When you fall into the lowest point of your life, everyone around you tells you: stay strong, and be happy. Strength is absolutely necessary, but happiness? In such a situation, that might be too much to ask of you. After all, who can still feel happy after falling and getting hurt? But at least you can stay calm. Calmly view this matter, calmly handle other things that need to be dealt with. Calmness, without happiness, also without unhappiness.

(3) Learn to bend over

When you have differences of opinion with others, or even verbal conflicts, you become upset because you think it's all due to their ill intentions. Don't dwell on it anymore; go home and mop the floor instead. Grab a cloth, bend down, kneel on both knees, and thoroughly clean every corner of the floor in front of you. Then reconsider every word you said during that conflict. Now, you realize that you were also wrong in some ways, don't you? Aren't you gradually becoming more peaceful? Sometimes you must learn to bend over, because this action can make you humble. While working physically, you also brighten your own mind. And, you end up with a spotless floor as well. That's your second gain.

(4) Don't dwell on "if only"

You say that life is a long road with infinite forks, always making choices. Choosing between fried noodles or fried rice seems to have little impact, but choosing a major, a job, marriage or no marriage, whether to have children or not—each choice has far-reaching consequences, and different choices will certainly create completely different lives. You also say that the unbearable weight in life lies in the fact that life offers no chance to start over. If only... then now wouldn't be like this... This kind of wistful muttering had better stop. Actually, each choice at the fork in the road isn't truly good or bad. As long as you see life as your unique creation, you won't keep looking back on what could have been if you had made different choices.

(5) Strive, regardless of success or failure

While walking through the forest, you see a vine clinging to a tree trunk, softness and strength intertwined. You are moved by this serene beauty, and let happiness and belonging linger here. You wonder, what storms will come in the future? Perhaps the vine will break, the tree will fall, or heaven will waste away and earth grow old. So, let time pause at this moment. Pausing is eternal, and if there is this serene moment within eternity, then any calamities that may come in the future have already received comfort and recompense.

(6) Stay simple

Because of excessive thinking, you often complicate your life. Even though you are living in the present, you are always reminiscing about the past and worrying about the future. Insisting on carrying the past, future, and present together makes your life muddy and messy. Simplicity is a state of grace. Simply feel the changes in the weather with your skin, simply savor the fresh grass scent after the rain with your nose, simply take in the distant and near scenery as a painting with your eyes, simply live in the moment, and the moment actually has nothing to do with right or wrong. Since there is no right or wrong, there is no need for excessive thought; no truth or falsehood, so no need to worry constantly. Without right or wrong, isn't it like dreaming? Yes, just treat your life as a dream to execute.

(7) Occasionally be worldly

After eating too much healthy food, occasionally you also want to nibble on duck tongues and salted chicken wings. After watching too many masterpieces, occasionally you also want to glance at tear-jerking soap operas. After listening to too much classical music, occasionally you also want to sing popular songs that express loving someone for a hundred years and hating them for ten thousand. You know that healthy food is beneficial for digestion, masterpieces are meaningful for cultivating temperament, and classical music is significant for uplifting the soul, but sometimes you actually don't want to live beneficially all the time. Life doesn't need to bind yourself so tightly. Occasional small indulgences are moral. Being full of spirit might approach maturity, but occasional worldliness makes one more approachable.

(8) Control emotions

Today, you are unhappy because someone hurt you with words. You dislike arguing, so you leave. But you only leave the place, not the context of being hurt by that person, so the more you think about it, the angrier you get. The angrier you get, the less energy you have to deal with other things, many events that require more attention and thought are neglected, ignored, or omitted in your overwhelming anger. Because you are solely focused on being angry. Dwelling on emotions is a waste of oneself, and a very bad waste at that. After all, anger requires effort, and anger definitely harms one's vital energy. So, intelligent as you are, don't let emotions control you. When you are about to get angry again, gently remind yourself: "Don't waste it."

(9) Seize the best opportunity, never miss it

You once bought a dress you liked but were reluctant to wear, solemnly enshrining it in your wardrobe. After a long while, when you saw it again, you found it was out of style. So, you missed it. You also once bought a beautiful cake but were reluctant to eat it, solemnly enshrining it in the refrigerator. After a long while, when you saw it again, you found it had expired. So, you also missed it. Clothes not worn at their favorite time, cakes not tasted at their best flavor, like things not done when you most wanted to, are all regrets. Life also has an expiration date. Things you want to do should be done as soon as possible. If you only solemnly enshrine your wishes in your heart but never act on them, the only result will be missing them, just like that out-of-style dress, just like that expired cake.

(10) Occasionally deviate from the track

Once you took a train intending to go to destination A, but halfway through, you suddenly decided to get off at destination B. Maybe the unique name of the place attracted you, or maybe the fleeting scenery touched you. In short, you changed your originally planned journey and experienced a surprising unexpected trip. Destination A was your original goal, but destination B gave you a small adventure. Looking back, you say it was an unforgettable experience of going off track. Many times in life are like this too. Focusing solely on a single purpose is merely fulfilling the original journey; leaving the predetermined track gives you the chance to discover other landscapes.

(11) Quietly return to calmness

There was a time when you felt low, even too lazy to pull the curtains and look at the sunshine outside the window. Therefore, you naturally forgot to check the Marguerite on the windowsill that needed water every day. For how long did this go unnoticed until one day, you got through your low mood and also remembered your Marguerite. Oh my goodness, poor flower, is she still alive? Trembling, you pulled the curtain and saw her waving in the wind, her flowers smiling at you. It turns out that during this period, although you forgot to water her, heaven didn't forget to nourish her with rain. Many things quietly happen beyond your sight and quietly arrange themselves. Heaven creates all things, and Heaven nurtures all things. Everything actually need not be worried about. All you need to do is be the best version of yourself, leave no regrets, and that’s enough.
