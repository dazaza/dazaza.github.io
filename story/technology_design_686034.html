Recently, reporters saw this short video on many video websites. In the video, an emotionally charged man argued with a female passenger sitting in a seat for seven minutes. The reason was that the woman occupied three seats and lay down on them. The man's father, who had bought a standing ticket, wanted to borrow a seat but was coldly rejected by the woman. Seeing his father humiliated, the man became angry and approached the woman to try and reason with her, but the woman claimed, "I bought three tickets, these three seats are all mine, I have the right not to let you sit!" According to reports, cases of one person buying several seats at once also occasionally occur in the aviation industry, mostly out of necessity. For example, Air France introduced a new rule last year where overweight passengers would be weighed at the check-in counter, and if they were too large to fit into a single seat, they would be required to purchase two adjacent seats. However, this rule only applied in full-capacity situations, with the second ticket being sold at 75% of the original price, excluding taxes and fuel surcharges. If there were empty seats, the airline would refund the cost of the second ticket to the passenger.
