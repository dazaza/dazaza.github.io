(I) Establishing a Good Corporate Culture

A good corporate culture builds an intangible channel that makes all employees operate within it, making them more efficient and cohesive, which is what all company leaders hope to achieve.

Today’s business operations place greater emphasis on talent cultivation and retention, making a good corporate culture more important than ever. Sincere and hardworking employees expect the company to have a clear and good culture.

For employees, the company's mission and value declarations are beautiful, but if they remain at the level of slogans, it's difficult to implement them, and employees easily become disappointed and lose trust in the company. NLP provides effective methods to make the company's mission a daily goal for employees.

(II) Solving Problems by Asking Questions – Super Language Patterns

Language reveals a person's mindset and thoughts at a very deep level, and we can use language to improve our own and others' mindsets and thoughts, thereby changing behavior and outcomes. Successful political figures show us the immense power of language. In fact, for ourselves and those around us, we often use language to control our psychological states.

The Super Language Pattern (The Meta Model Of Language) is one of the main skills in NLP.

"Meta" originates from Greek, meaning beyond (Beyond, over, on a different level). The Super Language Pattern teaches us how to use language to clarify language, giving us the ability to master language.

Using this language pattern on oneself greatly enhances the clarity of goals and thinking, making it a tool that can both help others and oneself. This is an extremely effective questioning technique and an important tool in coaching techniques (Coaching), allowing the coached person to reorganize their inner world, thereby improving in terms of thought, mindset, and behavior.

(III) Goal Management

The responsibility of a manager is to enable subordinates to fully utilize their capabilities and make the greatest contribution to the company. The success of subordinate personnel determines the success of their managers. Therefore, how to make each subordinate improve and continue to progress is the most important work task for managers.

"Goal Management" helps superiors achieve the greatest motivation for subordinates with minimal time, and the process is enjoyable, maintaining good relationships. Moreover, "Goal Management" has the following advantages:

1. Making subordinates clearly understand how to improve themselves and where to focus for the fastest results. Thus, they can more effectively use their time to make improvements.

2. Promoting good and effective communication and relationships between two people.

3. Making the criticism and follow-up from superiors become positive support and care for subordinates.

4. Providing a fair, positive, and measurable mechanism to present the performance levels among a group of employees.

5. Traditionally, many managers fear discussing the evaluation results of employees' performances. This technique eliminates the possibility of arguments and conflicts while promoting the best outcomes from such discussions.
