The sorrow of passing, the unforgettable sentiment

The desktop background is a sea of green grass, full of vitality.

This kind of grass, when I was in high school, covered the whole campus, emerald green and brimming with life. But this wasn't why we liked it.

I remember during our idle times, we would squat there searching, looking for one that didn't have four leaves, something unique, to make a wish.

Back then, we called this grass "wishing grass." We would make small wishes to the ones with five leaves.

I stubbornly called it wishing grass until just now when I looked it up on Baidu and found out its real name is clover.

A very nice sounding name, but I don't have much heart to appreciate it because I feel very sad today.

Sadness can come from many reasons, but there's only one kind of sadness: gloominess.

I remembered a book I read, about a story of a third party, which I suddenly thought of because the third party had a beautiful name: Little Fish.

Little Fish, not exceptionally pretty but very pleasant to look at, with clear eyes.

That clarity is something hard to find in people who've been in society for too long, very charming.

In the story, the male protagonist was Little Fish's colleague. Little Fish had just started working, always speaking timidly, but this was definitely not weakness.

At the company, Little Fish was a happy girl, her faint smile always on the tip of her lips.

The male protagonist was a man named Jun, neither fat nor thin, with a very magnetic voice. Jun and Little Fish were from different departments. Six months after Little Fish started working, she once called Jun for business purposes. Jun's voice was very kind, but this did not lead to their acquaintance.

About a month later, one day, while Little Fish was revisiting a certain business matter, she unexpectedly chatted a lot with Jun.

Jun was a good conversationalist; he said to Little Fish, "You're a very understanding girl."

Others wouldn't understand the sorrow, but Little Fish was surprised that this man could read her fatigue in such brief communication.

Although they worked in the same company, by this time, Little Fish and Jun had still never met face-to-face.

But an inexplicable warmth allowed them some extra exchanges beyond work, as work began and life ended.

Jun said to Little Fish, "Girls with cold hands are loved by someone;" Jun said to Little Fish, "We really have a connection;" Jun said to Little Fish, "I'll lend you my shoulder to cry on!"

Little Fish was very happy, that someone could understand her; Little Fish felt sweet, that someone could talk to her endlessly; Little Fish was flustered, because everything happened too fast.

Things easily obtained are easily lost.

Jun said to Little Fish, "Let me take you out to eat." Little Fish was excited but still refused.

The next day, Jun again said to Little Fish, "Wait for me after work, alright? I'll take you out to eat."

Little Fish really didn't know how to refuse, so she waited for Jun after work. Little Fish was very excited; she didn't know what Jun's face would look like, if he'd be like the prince on a white horse in her dreams, with a warm smile at the corner of his mouth, and sunlight dancing in his eyes.

When Little Fish saw Jun, her whole heart was already in disarray. She couldn't have imagined that Jun would possess all the beauty she imagined—handsome, sunny, elegant, most importantly, Jun's mouth had the warm smile Little Fish hoped for, and between his brows, there was leaping sunlight.

At that moment, Little Fish was dumbfounded, at a loss. Jun smiled warmly and said, "It's freezing outside, get in the car!" Little Fish got into Jun's car absentmindedly, because at the moment she saw Jun, she lost all thoughts. Little Fish awkwardly tried twice to close the car door but failed. Jun's mouth curved into a perfect smile as he said, "Little Fish is so clumsy."

Hearing Jun say this, Little Fish forgot her usual sharp tongue and retort, instead feeling deeply moved inside. Little Fish thought, "How wonderful, finally I can be clumsy and silly in front of someone, not pretending to be strong, being simple and true to myself. I can also hear his gentle voice whispering in my ear, filled with affection: 'Clumsy!'"

For the first time in front of someone, Little Fish was at a loss, accurately speaking, in front of a man.

Jun asked, "What do you like to eat? I'll treat you;" Jun asked, "Are you cold? I'll turn up the heater;" Jun asked, "Why haven't I seen you before at the company?" In Jun's warm questions, Little Fish was like a child who had made a mistake, not daring to look at Jun sideways. Her heart was in turmoil, like two little rabbits drumming.

Little Fish didn't know when Jun placed his thick palm over hers. At that moment, Little Fish felt her heart skip a beat. That big hand was warm, making Little Fish feel secure. But she still pulled away from Jun's hand because everything happened too fast, making her half-dreamlike.

Jun reached out again, and Little Fish spent a great deal of effort pulling away, yet softly said, "Drive carefully, please."

That dinner that night must have been the most unforgettable one in Little Fish's life. A food lover, Little Fish suddenly didn't know how to hold her chopsticks, every way felt awkward. She didn't know how to chew or how to drink the delicious soup...

Suddenly, Jun gripped Little Fish's right hand, his face inching closer to hers until it touched her cheek. He whispered gently in her ear, "Be a good girl, do you like me?"

At that moment, Little Fish was petrified.

"Jun, Jun, Jun," Little Fish repeatedly thought of Jun's name in her mind, her face burning red.

That night must have been Little Fish's most beautiful memory. She stumbled upon her prince charming, being lovingly scolded by him, "So clumsy!" Being warmly called "good girl," a term of endearment only her parents used; Jun gently embraced Little Fish and said, "Good girl, seeing your hands frozen like that almost made me cry;" "Good girl, let's have a child after we get married;" "Good girl, do you prefer boys or girls? I'm the only heir in my family, so having a girl definitely won't work!" "Good girl..."

Little Fish had never spoken so softly and intimately with anyone. She didn't know how Jun could read the fatigue in her heart; she didn't understand how Jun could see the answer in her eyes. Jun said, "Little Fish, do you like me?" Little Fish didn't answer, but Jun understood.

That night lying in bed, Little Fish couldn't sleep, for a man, a prince-like man, a man who made her fall in love at first sight.

I think Little Fish must have been a self-conscious girl, otherwise she wouldn't have thought of a fairy tale: Cinderella.

In front of Jun, Little Fish considered herself that Cinderella. The prince's favor made Cinderella happy, yet she was at a loss. How would a proud princess think of such a fairy tale in the midst of happiness? Like a child, Little Fish asked Jun, "Will Cinderella really live happily ever after with the prince? Does reality really have fairy tales?"

Jun seriously said, "Of course yes." Just like when Little Fish hadn't met Jun yet and complained to him about how cold it was and her hands were swollen, Jun said, "Women with cold hands are loved by someone." Little Fish naively asked, "Really?" Jun's response was the same: "Absolutely!"

Little Fish fell under the spell, in Jun's irresistible handsomeness, in the perfectly curved smile when he smiled, in his soft calling of that warm word "good girl"...

Or perhaps she fell in love the moment they first met!

Little Fish thought it must be God seeing her too tired, sending such a perfect prince into her life. What a blessing from God! I must seize this gift without hesitation!

Little Fish thought they would be like all couples, pouring out their overwhelming longing for each other in every second apart. Little Fish thought they could walk hand in hand after work every day, share noodles from the same large bowl, just like in fairy tales where the prince and Cinderella lived happily ever after!

Reality is cruel. Jun seemed very busy every day. Jun said to Little Fish, "Lately, I will be very busy. Dad has high blood pressure and is hospitalized, I need to take care of Dad; It's the end of the year, there are a lot of things to handle at the company, but you should believe me, I'll contact you when I have time;" Jun said to Little Fish, "After I finish, I will definitely spend time with you properly!"

Little Fish tried her best to hide all her longing, spending all her time thinking about Jun after work. Smiling foolishly, spacing out, every glance, every expression was full of love for Jun. Love, it must be love, that makes a girl lose herself.

Jun rarely met with Little Fish, or rather, though they were in the same company, they had no chance to meet. Every night, Little Fish thought Jun would at least send a text message to say goodnight, so she foolishly held her phone waiting for the beep of a text message, waking up multiple times at 2:30 AM, 4:20 AM, 5:30 AM... Waking up each time, disappointed each time. Little Fish would only truly fall asleep after 5:30 AM because it was getting cooler, and Little Fish was sure Jun wouldn't send a text anymore!

If Little Fish wasn't sad, that would be a lie. But Little Fish wanted to consider more for Jun, even more. Jun was exhausted taking care of Dad, Jun was very busy at work, Jun must be thinking of her... All these reasons, Little Fish sought for Jun. Sometimes the reasons were far-fetched, but perhaps only reasons could soothe the pain in her heart.

Little Fish couldn't help herself and sent a text message to Jun telling him about her sadness. She didn't understand why lovers in the same city had only met once. Little Fish said to Jun, "I've forgotten your face. Little Fish wants to see you every day. Little Fish misses you so much!"

Jun's reply was this: "I understand, Little Fish. I'm sorry, I'll be more careful in the future. Don't overthink it, listen to me!"

Just a simple text message, but Little Fish was incredibly happy. Originally, her prince understood. He wanted her to listen, so Little Fish would behave well, not causing trouble for her prince, right? The next day after work, Little Fish began a long and excited wait. Having a cold didn't affect her excited mood. 8 PM, 9 PM, 10 PM... As time approached 11 PM, Little Fish finally called Jun. Jun said, "Good girl, I'm drunk. Come pick me up, I won't drive."

When Little Fish took a taxi and arrived, the streets were already sparsely populated. Little Fish stood downstairs calling Jun, saying, "I'm downstairs." Jun told Little Fish to come up, but the flashy place made Little Fish unsure how to step inside. Little Fish said, "I'll wait for you downstairs."

Jun came down. This was the second time Little Fish saw Jun. It was a very cold night, and Jun wasn't wearing a jacket, a cigarette burning between his fingers. Little Fish rushed into Jun's arms, feeling like a lost child returning home, so warm, so beautiful, and so sweet.

Jun said, "Good girl, accompany me upstairs, we'll go home together later." Little Fish refused. Jun let Little Fish wait in the lobby, saying, "Wait for me, okay? I'll be down soon." At that moment, Little Fish suddenly became afraid, or rather, suddenly wanted to escape. Her prince wasn't drunk, and she knew what was about to happen.

After Jun went upstairs, Little Fish ran away. She thought coming to find him at midnight was just to ensure he was fine. Since he wasn't drunk and she had seen him, wasn't that enough?

As she fled in a taxi, Little Fish turned off her phone. She didn't turn it back on until just before sleeping. She thought there would be texts or calls from Jun, but the silence after turning it on made her very disappointed. She set the phone to silent mode and went to sleep. The next morning, there were two missed calls from Jun on her phone.

Little Fish couldn't resist calling back. Jun said he was busy and would call back later, then hung up.

By noon, Jun's Feixin came online. Little Fish said, "Sorry, I wasn't feeling well last night." Jun got angry, "Little Fish, did you ignore me because I was drunk?"

Little Fish apologized, but Jun seemed really upset. Then Little Fish blurted out, "Were you really drunk last night?"

Jun logged off. Little Fish knew Jun was really mad. Jun, why don't you understand? Little Fish cares about you so much!

During her break, Little Fish chatted with one of Jun's employees, subtly bringing up Jun. She said, "That boy is so handsome, are a lot of girls chasing him?" Then she saw a sentence that almost suffocated her: "Married man."

Little Fish doesn't know how she felt at that moment or how she ended the conversation with Jun's employee. She only felt her heart breaking, like shattered crystal scattered all over the floor. She thought she could act as if nothing happened, but the pain was so clear.

Little Fish felt like she was going crazy. Falling in love at first sight, the prince of sweet words already had a princess marking him as exclusively hers. And herself, what did she mean to the person she loved most? How could the man who whispered sweet nothings in her ear suddenly throw her into the depths of hell?

Little Fish was furious, heartbroken, and overwhelmed with grief. Like all clichéd scenarios, she questioned in a text message: "Jun, why didn't you tell me you were married?"

Waiting bitterly without a reply, Little Fish called Jun. When the call connected, she didn't know what to say, her heart aching. Jun said, "Hello, I'm busy. What do you want to say quickly?" Hearing Jun's voice, Little Fish felt so wronged, tears threatening to spill. But she forced herself to hold them back and said, "Jun, why didn't you tell me you were married?" After a brief silence on the other end, Jun replied with a surprising question: "Does it matter?"

Does it matter? Of course it does! It matters to every girl, extremely important. Little Fish was almost desperate at that moment. She thought she could forget this man, this man she had only met twice, but Little Fish overestimated herself.

Heartbroken, Little Fish decided to end it here. See this man one last time, and then cut ties forever. This decision was made a few days after knowing Jun was married. Little Fish deliberately waited for Jun after work. She thought, "I'll only take five minutes of his time, ask him what I mean to him, and why he played with my feelings?"

Little Fish sent a text message to Jun, saying she was waiting for him at the mall across from the company for one last meeting, taking only five minutes of his time.

Jun's call came 30 minutes after Little Fish sent the text. Jun said, "I'm off work. My sister-in-law and mom are waiting for me downstairs. I have something at uncle's house today. Let's meet another day!" At that moment, Little Fish felt like a child who had done something wrong and said, "Can I just take five minutes of your time?" Immediately, Jun's rebuke came through the phone: "How can you be like this? I said my sister-in-law is waiting for me downstairs..." Little Fish couldn't hear anything else Jun said, tears streaming down her face...

At that moment, Little Fish suddenly realized there was never any love. Jun was just lonely, seeking excitement, and Little Fish inadvertently became prey. What love, what fairy tale, what longing, were all legends, myths, and dreams.

But Little Fish really fell in love. She didn't understand why Jun had to deceive her feelings. Those sweet words, those intimate whispers, those former tender moments were all lies Jun casually fabricated. The dream woke up, but the pain was so deep, so bone-deep, so real, so unbelievable.

Little Fish was ultimately too naive, too weak, and cared too much about this man who deceived her.

Little Fish said, "After the New Year, I will send you my wedding invitation."

Saying this was just using the most extreme method to prove that this man still cared about her, maybe this text message would make his conscience feel guilty, maybe only a brand new life could make her forget this man she both loved and hated.

Little Fish, like a clumsy girl, thought this way, punishing herself to retaliate against life. At that moment, Little Fish's heart was evil, dark, and radical... But Little Fish really got engaged. The boy looked quite good, shy-faced, lacking the manliness of Jun, but still a decent young man.

Manliness was something Little Fish couldn't quite explain, a feeling, standing beside him gave a sense of security. It was very reassuring, as if his tall shoulders could bear everything about to happen, staying by his side, you didn't need to think about anything, just put your hand in his large palm and let him guide you forward.

When work resumed after the holiday, due to work reasons, Little Fish still had contact with Jun. She said, "I'm about to get married." Jun said, "Don't joke around." Little Fish said, "Really, my husband is very handsome. Will you come to my wedding?" Jun said, "What do you think? I definitely won't come." Little Fish said, "Is your sister-in-law pretty?" Jun said, "Average." Little Fish said, "Jun, you should cherish your sister-in-law, right? A woman willing to give her entire life to you must be the person who loves you the most in this world besides your parents. She bet her whole life on you, don't let her lose." Jun said, "Don't say these things."

Little Fish envied the woman who could stay by Jun's side. Occasionally chatting, Little Fish couldn't help but advise Jun, "You should treat your sister-in-law well, right?" Little Fish asked Jun, "What's it like to be married?" Little Fish said, "I will treat my husband well in the future and cherish him." Jun always got angry and said, "Can we talk about something else?"

Little Fish still didn't understand this man. Perhaps the regret of not being able to be together made Little Fish miss him, feel pain, and shed tears.

Jun acted like a self-righteous gentleman, prying into Little Fish's words: "What exactly do you mean by that?" It seemed that even an unintentional word from Little Fish could lead Jun to guess many ambiguities. Little Fish didn't hate Jun because she thought Jun should be decent. Jun might have just made a mistake once, disturbing his life. Foolishly, Little Fish thought maybe they could still become good friends, confiding their troubles to each other, encouraging and consoling each other, without crossing boundaries.

Every time Jun typed "What do you mean?" on the keyboard, Little Fish's heart ached painfully. Little Fish was originally a pure girl, just wanting to quietly accompany him as a friend since they couldn't be lovers, watching him live well, making herself happy. But that phrase "What do you mean?" was so caustic. Little Fish felt so degraded, each time her heart ached as if bleeding, yet she still humbly apologized to Jun, saying, "I'm sorry, I really meant nothing else." Jun, sitting at the other end of the computer, could never see the tears dripping onto the keyboard as Little Fish said "I'm sorry."

In March, with bright sunshine and warm weather, when Jun asked again, "What do you mean?", Little Fish had no more tears left. She typed on the keyboard, "Whatever you interpret it as, that's what it means."

Little Fish was tired. She thought, not only could they not be lovers, but they couldn't even be friends. They must be people from two different worlds! She preferred to remain simple, pretending to be strong, waiting for that shy man to marry her, then living harmoniously and growing old together. All topics about love at first sight vanished in the March sunshine.

But one day, while making a PPT presentation and choosing a background, she saw a picture of clover on Baidu, downloaded it into her folder, and looking at the vast green fields, made a wish, tears falling as well.

I think that Little Fish must have entrusted all her weariness and love to that vibrant green. Green represents hope, prayer, peace, and progress.

The story is as cliché as all stories, seemingly casual words, but sorrow permeates between the lines. People with stories should understand, right!

Suddenly, I want to return to my high school years, searching for a clover with five leaves on the campus, closing my eyes devoutly, making a small wish to it, letting it carry my wish, blooming little white flowers, commemorating the lost love and years!
