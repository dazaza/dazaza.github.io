(I) The Parable of Growth: Be an Apple Tree That Keeps Growing Forever

An apple tree finally bears fruit.

In the first year, it bore 10 apples, 9 of which were taken away, leaving only 1 for itself. The apple tree was indignant about this and decided to cut off its own lifeline, refusing to grow any further. In the second year, it bore 5 apples, 4 of which were taken away, leaving 1 for itself. "Ha ha, last year I got 10%, but this year I got 20%! It's doubled." The apple tree felt that things were now fair.

However, it could have done this instead: continue to grow. For instance, in the second year, it could have borne 100 fruits, 90 of which were taken away, leaving 10 for itself.

It’s also very possible that 99 would be taken away, leaving only 1 for itself. But no matter, it can still keep growing, bearing 1000 fruits in the third year...

In fact, how many fruits it gets is not the most important thing. What's most important is that the apple tree is growing! When the apple tree grows into a towering tree, those forces that once hindered its growth will become so weak as to be negligible. Truly, don't worry too much about the fruits; growth is the most important thing.
