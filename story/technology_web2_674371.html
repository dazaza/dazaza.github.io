Walnut, pine nut and corn porridge: beautifies and firms breasts

by anonymous on 2012-02-24 13:01:31

Walnuts and pine nuts are classic nourishing foods for women. They are rich in Vitamin E and zinc, which help to moisturize the skin and delay skin aging. Moreover, they also contain a large amount of protein, minerals, and B vitamins, making them excellent choices for beauty and hair care. Both walnuts and pine nuts are good sources of linoleic acid, a popular breast health ingredient that stimulates the synthesis of estrogen. Corn itself is rich in Vitamin E and is recommended by experts as a food that promotes healthy skin.

The preparation method is very simple: take an appropriate amount of broth, add rock sugar and corn, stew over a low heat until cooked, then sprinkle with walnut kernels and pine nuts before serving.

It's best to buy raw walnut kernels and pine nuts and process them yourself, ideally using sunflower oil rich in linoleic acid. When consuming walnut and pine nut corn porridge, it’s important not to overeat; it's suggested to have a small bowl daily. Eating too much may lead to excessive calorie absorption, causing obesity.

Peanut and sesame paste: Anti-aging

Peanuts and black sesame seeds are the top anti-aging foods for women in their 30s, mainly due to their richness in Vitamin E. They also prevent pigmentation on the skin, avoiding the formation of spots and butterfly spots. Sesame contains a powerful anti-aging substance called sesamol, which is an important nourishing food for preventing female aging. It also has a high content of B vitamins, which can promote human metabolism and are beneficial for the synthesis of estrogen and progesterone, providing effective benefits.

To prepare peanut and sesame paste, you need peanuts, black sesame seeds, and salad oil. First, fry the peanuts until they're cooked, roast the black sesame seeds until fragrant, then grind them together in a blender into a fine powder, and store it in a sealed glass jar. To eat, use a clean spoon to scoop some into a bowl, add boiling water, and stir. If you prefer sweetness, you can add honey appropriately.

Homemade peanut and sesame paste is more natural and healthier than store-bought versions, making it ideal for breakfast or as an afternoon snack.

Yam is highly regarded in traditional Chinese medicine as a supplement for deficiency, having the functions of strengthening the spleen and benefiting the kidneys, replenishing essence and energy. Chicken liver is rich in iron, zinc, copper, Vitamin A, and B vitamins, which not only facilitate the synthesis of estrogen but are also preferred for blood replenishment. Green bamboo shoots are beauty vegetables rich in dietary fiber. Together, these ingredients help regulate qi and blood, improving the moisture and color of the skin.

Ingredients needed include yam, green bamboo shoots, chicken liver, salt, monosodium glutamate, broth, and starch. First, peel and wash the yam and green bamboo shoots, then cut them into strips. Wash the chicken liver with clear water and slice it. Then blanch the yam, green bamboo shoots, and chicken liver separately in boiling water. Finally, add some cooking oil to a pot, along with an appropriate amount of broth. After seasoning, add all the ingredients, stir-fry for a few times, thicken with starch, and serve.

When eating yam and green bamboo shoots stir-fried with chicken liver, remember not to drink coffee or strong tea before or after meals to avoid affecting the absorption of nutrients in the food. Furthermore, isoflavones also have the function of preventing osteoporosis.

In addition, many people know that pig trotters are excellent beauty products, but few realize that chicken wings also contain a large amount of collagen protein. The protein content in chicken wings is higher than in pig trottes, and when consumed with soybeans and green beans, it greatly benefits increasing skin elasticity and moisturizing the skin.

Materials needed: soybeans, green beans, chicken wings, salt, monosodium glutamate, cooking wine, and broth. First, place the soybeans, green beans, and chicken wings in a casserole, add an appropriate amount of broth, and simmer over low heat until cooked. Finally, season with salt, monosodium glutamate, and cooking wine before serving.

When soaking soybeans and green beans in water, be sure not to remove the outer skins. For chicken wings, it's better to choose the mid-section and tips rather than the drumettes, which have lower collagen protein content.
