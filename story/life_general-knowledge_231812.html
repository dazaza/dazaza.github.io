Some relatively useful experience in choosing sanitary ware

by longersing on 2009-09-01 18:15:41

The bathroom is the highlight of decoration, and ceramic sanitary ware is the most critical product in the bathroom. Therefore, its selection and matching are particularly important.

Selection of Ceramic Sanitary Ware Types

The main types of ceramic sanitary ware include toilets, bidets, washbasins, sinks, mop pools, toilet paper holders, soap dishes, etc. There are many brands of ceramic sanitary ware available in the market currently, with significant differences in quality and price ranges. When selecting, one should first have a sense of coordination, determining the standard they expect to achieve. Each component or accessory in the set should be at the same level of quality.

Determine the Drainage Method and Installation Dimensions of the Toilet

Before purchasing a toilet, it's essential to measure the distance from the center of the drain outlet to the wall (for bottom drainage) or the distance to the ground (for rear drainage). This will help determine whether the toilet you select has a bottom drainage or rear drainage structure. Choosing a toilet with a consistent drainage method and appropriate installation dimensions is crucial for proper installation. The wall distance of the toilet's drainage outlet should equal or be slightly less than the wall distance of the bathroom's drainage outlet (bottom drainage); the distance of the toilet's drainage outlet from the ground should equal or be slightly higher than the height of the bathroom's drainage outlet (rear drainage).

Matching Style and Tone of Accompanying Products

The number of ceramic pieces in the bathroom is not limited to just one. For multiple ceramic products such as toilets, washbasins, soap dishes, toilet paper holders, and mop pools, consistency or proximity in design and color is necessary for harmony and aesthetic appeal.

Carefully Select Visible Surfaces

When choosing ceramic sanitary ware, it’s important to understand that visible surfaces and non-visible surfaces have different requirements. Visible surfaces refer to the areas of the porcelain piece that people can easily see after installation. The quality of these visible surfaces should be strictly controlled, especially in areas where water splashes during use. After installation, these surfaces should maintain their quality and appearance.
