21-Year-Old Woman Was Murdered by a Former Colleague While on Duty

by uelu6c6b on 2012-03-01 09:02:46

Our staff reporter (named Wang Qiushi) - A 21-year-old girl, originally named Li Yu, used to work for a real estate intermediary company. While on duty, she was robbed and murdered by her former colleague Deng Changlin. Yesterday, it was reported that after Deng Changlin was sentenced to death, the mother of the victim, Li Yu, sued both Deng Changlin and the company located at Xindi Market, claiming compensation of over 890,000 yuan. The People's Court of Changping District has accepted this civil case.

Deng Changlin had previously worked as a maintenance worker in the customer service engineering department of a Beijing real estate brokerage company. In July 2010, he resigned from the company. Last January, close to the Spring Festival, he was heavily in debt and thus conceived the idea of robbery.

His former frail colleague Li Yu became his target. Deng Changlin learned about the company’s duty roster and salary distribution time. He knew that Li Yu was on duty every Thursday and Saturday, and that January 15th was a Saturday when salaries were distributed. Hence, he decided to commit the crime on that day.

At around 8 PM on January 15th last year, Deng Changlin arrived at Li Yu's office at the Xindi Market in Huilongguan Town, Changping District, where the company was located. He found that the office light was still on, so he picked up half a brick and waited at the door. After a while, Li Yu came out of the office with her bag, holding her mobile phone, ready to lock the door and leave. Deng Changlin rushed forward, pushing Li Yu back into the room, one hand covering her mouth and the other using the brick to hit her head. Li Yu fought back fiercely and crawled towards the door, but Deng Changlin did not stop. He continued hitting Li Yu's head with the brick. During her struggle, the brick fell to the ground. Seeing a claw hammer on the west side of the desk nearby, Deng Changlin picked it up and struck Li Yu's head, causing severe cranial injuries which led to her death at the young age of 21.

After killing her, Deng Changlin threw down the hammer, took Li Yu's bag, locked the office door, and fled the scene. Inside the bag were more than 7000 yuan, along with a wallet, ID card, bank cards, and other items. On January 20th last year, Deng Changlin was arrested and later sentenced to death by the court for robbery, with all political rights revoked for life, and his personal property confiscated entirely.

Recently, Ms. Wang, the mother of the victim Li Yu, filed a lawsuit against Deng Changlin and Beijing Northern Xindi Building Materials and Department Store Market Co., Ltd. at the Changping Court, demanding that the two defendants compensate for funeral expenses, living expenses of dependents, death compensation, mental damages, and other costs totaling over 890,000 yuan. The court will hold a hearing on this case on a selected date.
