Symptoms of the 4th Month of Pregnancy

Author: Skin Care Products for Pregnant Women Source: Date: February 28, 2012, 9:50:30 AM Popularity: 2 Tags: Precautions for the Fourth Month of Pregnancy

Now that your abdomen has grown larger, your old clothes are beginning to become ill-fitting, and you need to start wearing maternity wear. The dream of having your own baby once seemed so distant, but now it feels within reach because this month you will truly begin to feel fetal movement.

Symptoms of the Fourth Month of Pregnancy

1. Specialized skincare products for pregnant women. Generally, when mothers are about four months pregnant, their abdomen begins to noticeably show due to the uterus growing to the size of a child's head. Expectant mothers will be surprised by the gradual fullness in their lower abdomen. Despite the significant expansion of the uterus, when at rest, the internal abdominal pressure remains completely normal.

2. Expectant mothers will clearly notice an increase in breast size. The areola around the nipples becomes darker and more distinct.

3. The likelihood of miscarriage significantly decreases, but increased vaginal discharge, heaviness in the abdomen, and frequent urination persist. Pregnancy spots also become more noticeable, and direct sunlight on the face should be avoided.

4. Morning sickness starts to gradually subside, and appetite improves.

Condition of the Baby in the Fourth Month of Pregnancy

At this stage, the fetus is approximately 10 centimeters long and weighs about 120 grams.

1. The formation of the baby’s lungs is basically complete during this period, and respiratory movements become more developed, allowing rhythmic and regular contraction activities.

2. The gastrointestinal tract develops fully, capable of absorbing water and transporting unabsorbed substances to the large intestine. The immune system also gradually matures, with certain antibody concentrations in the baby's blood reaching a certain level.

3. The joints of the arms and legs have formed, and the fingers can grip tightly. The limbs move powerfully, and the baby can perform actions in the amniotic cavity, such as kicking the uterine wall with small feet or moving hands across different parts of the body. The placenta has fully developed, connecting the baby to the mother through the umbilical cord, forming a system that sustains fetal development.

Precautions for the Fourth Month of Pregnancy

Since expectant mothers tend to sweat easily and secretions increase, they are particularly susceptible to bacterial infections. Therefore, daily showers or cleaning of the genital area are necessary, along with frequently changing underwear.

Some expectant mothers may start prenatal exercises to make childbirth easier, but it is essential to do so within their capabilities and not overexert themselves.

Key Points of Prenatal Education in the Fourth Month

Visual Prenatal Education:

Starting from the fourth month of pregnancy, the fetus becomes highly sensitive to light. Scientific researchers have observed fetal reflexes using B-mode ultrasound when directly shining light on the mother's abdominal wall; the fetus turns its face away while exhibiting eye-opening and closing activities.

Therefore, some suggest that visual function training can be conducted during fetal development.

Method: Use a flashlight, flashing intermittently, and place it directly on the mother's abdomen to shine light, three times a day for 30 seconds each time, while recording the fetus's response. Note: When using light, avoid strong light and ensure the exposure time is not too long.
