The Rockets Welcome Their First Nationwide Broadcast Game, Battier Willing to Be Slapped to Stay Alert

TOM Sports News - On December 13, Beijing time, according to the Houston Chronicle, the Rockets are about to face the Raptors and the Pistons, two teams with a winning percentage of less than 50%. This brief period is considered the "happy time" for the Rockets. This season, the Rockets have almost always come out on top when playing weaker teams (teams with a winning percentage under 50%), boasting an impressive record of 8 wins and 1 loss. After facing the Raptors and the Pistons, the Rockets will go head-to-head with the Nuggets, Mavericks, and Thunder, all tough Western Conference teams.

This season's Rockets team is vastly different compared to last season. Last year, they lost to many weaker teams but managed to beat stronger opponents. Coach Rick Adelman prefers the current situation and stated, "I believe that by the end of the regular season, you'll find that the teams capable of making the playoffs are those who rarely lose to weaker teams. If you start losing to weaker teams, it signifies the beginning of your decline."

Tomorrow's game between the Rockets and the Raptors will be broadcast nationwide, though not across the entire United States but rather throughout Canada. Regardless, this is excellent news for the Rockets, as none of their games this season have been eligible for national U.S. broadcasts. To accommodate the Canadian broadcast, the game's start time has been adjusted to 11:30 AM (local time). Rockets captain Shane Battier said, "If you ask NBA players what time they'd prefer to play, I’d say 85% would choose 12:30 PM, yet Rockets players usually peak at 7 PM every evening, which is our best physical condition. For most days, 12:30 PM should be nap time. We might need to drink a lot of coffee or ask someone to slap us a few times to stay more alert tomorrow."

In the past two games, there was one where rookie Chase Budinger went 0-for-5 in shooting, and another where he hit 6 out of 8 shots. Budinger remarked, "I don't dwell on my own shooting problems. When it comes to shooting, you just need to maintain confidence. I always forget the previous game and confidently participate in warm-up drills and shooting practice, then look to find my rhythm during the game. If you can make your first shot, it helps a lot."
