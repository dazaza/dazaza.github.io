Nanjing's real estate market sees a price drop trend, with the largest decrease approaching 13% (Part I)

by cscj010181 on 2010-05-12 18:13:30

Nanjing's Jiangbei real estate market suddenly hit by price cuts yesterday

With super-large scale developments standing tall and consistently leading in both development volume and sales in Nanjing, the Jiangbei real estate market suddenly turned aggressive yesterday! Following the Xuri Shangcheng development offering a fixed price of 4500 yuan/sq m for over 300 units, Suning Properties then launched a fixed-price strategy for its three developments in North Bridge: the Tianrun City development set a fixed price of 4088 yuan/sq m for 350 units, which is about 600 yuan/sq m cheaper than before.

The continuous discounting and promotions by North Bridge developments—does their collective price drop signify that Jiangbei housing prices can no longer hold up? Is this a harbinger of an overall decline in Nanjing's housing prices?

[Price Drop] Three more developments offer fixed-price units

The super-large Tianrun City development, which shockingly offered a fixed price of 4088 yuan/sq m, has a total of 350 special-priced units distributed across the development. The 550 special-priced units account for 16% of the total available units.

Interestingly, two other developments in the same Jiangbei section also implemented fixed pricing: Venice Water City set a uniform price of 4480 yuan/sq m for 150 units, and Tianhua Lvgu set a uniform price of 4188 yuan/sq m for 50 units. However, it was reported that these developments imposed strict restrictions on floor levels, such as Development B targeting only the first and top floors, and Development C targeting floors 1-6 of high-rise buildings, the first floor and sixth floor of multi-story buildings.

In total, the 550 special-priced units represent what proportion of the available units? According to Nanjing Online Real Estate data, these three developments currently have a total of 3360 available units, meaning the 550 special-priced units account for approximately 16%.

An industry insider noted that for a major player like Suning in North Bridge, the 550 units spread across three developments are just a drop in the bucket, but considering the current total available units, 16% is still a significant proportion.

Sold to specific groups? Sales office says anyone can buy

Interestingly, Suning Properties' official statement yesterday was that the 550 units across the three developments were specifically set aside to give back to society and demonstrate social and cultural care. These units are intended to reward provincial and municipal labor models, advanced workers, soldiers, and others who have contributed significantly to society.

However, inquiries made to these three developments revealed that there were no such restrictions, and buyers could purchase normally.

Why does this developer seem so evasive? An industry insider speculated that the developer might be concerned that previous buyers might feel they bought at a loss and demand refunds or returns.

[Citizen Reactions] Both those wanting to buy and not wanting to buy have their reasons

Citizen Mr. Shi stated that if the fixed-price discounts were only for the first and top floors, it wouldn't mean much. But he noticed that some fixed-price units weren't exclusively for the first and top floors; for example, Development C offers fixed pricing for floors 1-6 of high-rise buildings, with units on the 4th and 5th floors priced at 4480 yuan/sq m, making them worth considering.

Nevertheless, Mr. Shi also believed that compared to the prices he had been tracking, these fixed-price units were several hundred yuan/sq m cheaper, and with hundreds of units available, the scope of the price cut was quite extensive. He decided to visit the developments over the weekend.

But some citizens were unfazed by the price drops in Jiangbei developments. Citizen Ms. Lu said that recently, while taking a bus to look at properties in Jiangbei, she was stuck in traffic for over two hours on the bridge. By the time she arrived at the development, she had lost all enthusiasm due to the poor transportation environment, which would surely make living there inconvenient. Despite the significant price cuts, she remains hesitant and currently has no plans to buy property in Jiangbei.

[Industry Analysis] North Bridge housing prices reaching five thousand is now a mirage

Developments in Jiangbei last year aimed to break through five thousand yuan/sq m, but now it seems they've been brought back down to earth. Vice General Manager Shi Dong of Nanjing Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution analyzed that from a transaction average price of less than 3500 yuan/sq m in February last year to struggling to maintain around 4500 yuan/sq m now, breaking through 5000 yuan/sq m in Jiangbei has clearly failed.

This can also be seen from the comparison of sales volumes during the same period: in February this year, the sales volume of developments in Pukou decreased by 74% month-on-month, putting pressure on developers.

According to predictions by Wangshang Real Estate Research Institution, this year, Jiangbei will see the launch of 3.313 million square meters of new residential properties (slightly higher than the 3.21 million square meters in 2007), with new approvals amounting to 2.865 million square meters, leading all sectors in Nanjing. This year, the 45 developments in Jiangbei will add 2.8 million square meters of residential supply, and the concentrated, large-scale supply of commercial housing will inevitably intensify competition in the North Bridge area of Jiangbei.
