The secondary school teaching model of ocean thinking: Why does the Yangsi Secondary School create such a miracle?

(-) Having firm educational beliefs. Believing that every student can be taught well, people at Yangsi hold an unshakable conviction which is written in eight firm characters upstairs in the ocean thinking teaching area, also deeply embedded in the hearts of everyone from the principal to the teachers and students. They are convinced of these eight characters, and when people from Yangsi speak these words, they do so with full confidence and a sense of responsibility. Over ten years as if it were one day, they have strived to practice this philosophy of education, turning what seemed impossible into reality for Yangsi Secondary School, ultimately succeeding.

This belief was established by Principal Cailin Sen at Yangsi Secondary School. This insight came from his own experience of educating his three children and was unwaveringly implemented during the process of developing ocean thinking in secondary schools, making it what it is today. Principal Cailin Sen has two sons and a daughter; all three children initially had unsatisfactory results in elementary school. The eldest son didn't know numbers and struggled to keep up with classes; the second son, in fourth grade, once scored 68 on an arithmetic test and was the last among 26 students in junior high school. He did not excel in town primary school either, causing teacher Cai Linsen to sigh deeply: "Middle school to middle school."

Duty-bound, Cai Linsen quietly observed the children, patiently waiting. Waiting and waiting, he finally saw them surprise him. Cailin Sen said with a smile: Before going to bed, take time to ask some similar questions, see if the children really understand and grasp them. Every night, he let the children correct their wrong English words themselves, writing them down again, then repeating dictation and checking. On weekends, while riding a bike six miles home, Cai Linsen took the kids, reviewing the week's incorrect subjects along the way and checking again. If wrong, they corrected it. This twenty-mile country road became a classroom where children summarized what they learned each week.

The youngest daughter graduated from Jiaotong University, the second daughter from East China Normal University, and she is now studying in the U.S., while the eldest son received his Ph.D.; the second son graduated from college, joined the party, and became the Chief of Finance at the China Petrochemical Company. A fact of life caused Cailin Sen to reflect: If you treat your own children like this, why not teach each student the same way? What's wrong with this teaching method? This simple idea led Cailin Sen to guide the teachers of ocean thinking in starting an extraordinary great educational experiment, achieving success that seems divine.
