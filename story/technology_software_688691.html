The development of Internet of Things home appliances will surely enter the fast lane.

by gdshstyle on 2012-03-03 12:57:57

Smart homes begin to enter the fast lane and will accelerate in formation; smart shoe cabinets have recently been included in the "12th Five-Year Plan for the Development of the Internet of Things" issued by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (hereinafter referred to as "the Plan"). Smart home systems, including intelligent control of home appliances, have been listed as key application demonstration projects. The Plan aims to speed up the establishment of a standard system as one of its main objectives. In response, industry experts believe that the release of the Plan will not only promote the perfection of the IoT home appliance industry chain but also expedite the formation of smart homes.

The Plan proposes that by 2015, China should achieve significant results in core technology research and industrialization, key standard research and formulation, establishment and improvement of industrial chains, major application demonstrations and promotion, etc., initially forming an innovative-driven, application-led, collaborative development, secure and controllable Internet of Things development pattern. At the same time, it will carry out application demonstration projects in key areas, explore application models, accumulate experience and methods for application deployment and promotion, form a series of mature, replicable, and promotable application templates, and prepare for large-scale promotion of IoT applications across society and industries. Among these, the content of smart homes includes home networks, home security, intelligent control of household appliances, intelligent energy measurement, energy-saving and low-carbon, remote education, etc.

It is understood that currently, 28 provinces and cities across the country have identified the Internet of Things as a key area of industrial development, listing the Internet of Things as a leading industry. Almost all first- and second-tier cities are building or planning to build IoT industrial parks. The "2010-2011 Annual Report on the Development of China's Internet of Things" mentioned that it is expected that in the next five years, the global IoT industry market will show a rapid growth trend, reaching close to $350 billion by 2015, with an average annual growth rate approaching 25%. A conservative estimate suggests that by 2015, China's IoT industry will reach a scale of more than 500 billion yuan, with an average annual growth rate of around 11%.

As an important part of smart homes, the development of IoT home appliances will inevitably enter the fast lane. Since 2010, companies with strength have successively launched IoT home appliances to seize the market. Haier launched IoT refrigerators, Little Swan launched IoT washing machines, Changhong launched IoT TVs, and Gree cooperated with the three major operators in the field of IoT air conditioners. However, experts pointed out that IoT home appliances are not single products but require collaboration throughout the entire industry chain. Therefore, standards play an indispensable coordinating role. For this reason, accelerating the construction of a standard system has become one of the main goals during the "12th Five-Year Plan" period. According to the principles of overall planning, division of labor, ensuring priorities, and urgent needs first, an efficient standard coordination mechanism will be established. Disinfectant shoe cabinets will actively promote the internationalization of autonomous technical standards and gradually improve the IoT standard system.

Journalists learned that in 2011, the National Standardization Administration Committee and the National Development and Reform Commission issued documents agreeing to set up three groups under the National Basic Standards Working Group for the Internet of Things: the General Project Department, the Identification Project Group, and the Security Project Group, to conduct research on general standards, identification standards, and information security standards. At the same time, after the government work report in the same year clearly proposed "accelerating IoT R&D and application," the Ministry of Finance issued the "Management Measures for IoT Special Funds," hoping to guide the acceleration of industry cultivation and development through special funds. The special funds will mainly support projects such as IoT technology R&D and industrialization, standard research and formulation, application demonstration and promotion, public service platforms, etc.

In June 2011, the Ministry of Finance revised the "Management Measures for Central Fiscal Interest Subsidy Funds for Basic Construction Loans," adding interest subsidies for providing venue services to service outsourcing and IoT enterprises. The National Home Appliance Standardization Technical Committee also stated that in the next five years, it will propose plans for the formulation and revision of multiple specific technical standards, including technical requirements for IoT washing machines.
