"Dragon Ball GT" is an anime series produced by Toei Animation, based on the popular work "Dragon Ball" by manga artist Akira Toriyama. It is the third part of the Dragon Ball series. This work is not originally created by Akira Toriyama, but it has been authorized by Shueisha, and Toei Animation broadcast the third part of the "Dragon Ball" series, "Dragon Ball GT," after "Dragon Ball" and "Dragon Ball Z." Toriyama received the story outline from Toei Animation, made some modification suggestions, drew some character designs and illustrations, and highly endorsed this series.

"Dragon Ball GT" premiered on Fuji TV in 1996. Later, it was also aired on Hong Kong's RTHK. In 2004, it was re-broadcast, and a special television version was released that same year. The viewership ratings and influence of this animation were not as high as those of "Dragon Ball Z," but it was still well-received by most Dragon Ball fans.

Plot Summary:

The content is divided into three parts:

The first part involves Goku being turned into a child by the Black Star Dragon Balls, and he embarks on a journey with Trunks, Pan (Goku's granddaughter, also translated as Pepe or Abo), and the robot Giru to find the Black Star Dragon Balls scattered across the universe. During their adventures, they encounter an enemy named Baby who takes over Earth while Goku is searching for the Dragon Balls. Goku returns to find himself betrayed by everyone... Goku transforms into Super Saiyan 4 in this segment, and it concludes with the destruction and restoration of Earth, with Piccolo sacrificing himself.

After a relatively light-hearted transition episode comes the second part, where Dr. Myu and another mad scientist create Hell Seventeen in Hell and open a connection between the human world and Hell, luring Goku into Hell. They then fuse Hell Seventeen with Seventeen to create Super Seventeen. Krillin is killed, others are no match, and even Vegeta is defeated. Piccolo sacrifices himself to save Goku, but even Super Saiyan 4 cannot handle Super Seventeen, who can absorb energy. Just when all hope seems lost, Eighteen arrives, enraged by her husband's death...

Finally, in the last part, the battle against Super Seventeen causes severe damage to Earth, and Goku and his friends gather the Dragon Balls to restore Earth. However, instead of Shenron, evil dragons appear (due to excessive use of the Dragon Balls). These seven evil dragons wreak havoc on Earth, and Goku and his friends try their best to stop them... The final foe is Super One-Star Dragon. The ending here is particularly touching.

Akira Toriyama Talks About "Dragon Ball GT"

Q: What is your relationship with the latest animated series "Dragon Ball GT"?

A: It's nothing serious; I just reviewed Toei Animation's story outline, gave a few minor suggestions, and drew some character designs and illustrations. But it's really interesting! There's a feeling of "Ah, there's actually a way to write it like this." The staff at Toei Animation must have found it quite challenging. They've worked hard! If Goku had been turned into a small child, it might have continued as a comic series... But I no longer have any interest in continuing (laughs).

Q: So, GT is a recommended work by Mr. Toriyama?

A: Yes, it's definitely worth recommending. As a viewer, I am truly looking forward to it.
