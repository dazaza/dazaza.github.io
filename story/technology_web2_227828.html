Sister-in-law is nineteen years old.

by u82491650 on 2009-08-24 09:26:24

Young Hye-young had a happy childhood. Her father, Jung Joon-seok, was the son of a big business conglomerate boss. He married a female worker, Park Yeong-zi, against his father's wishes and they had a daughter, Hye-young. As a result, he almost severed ties with his family. Living with them were also widows Song and Kim, as well as Song's daughter, Soo-jee. Joon-seok loved Hye-young and Soo-jee dearly, often taking them out to play. On his way home to visit his mother, Joon-seok got into a car accident and lost his memory. Joon-seok's father lied to Yeong-zi, telling her that Joon-seok decided to return home to inherit the family business, and gave them a large sum of money. From then on, Yeong-zi and her daughter lost contact with Joon-seok. Unable to bear the blow, Yeong-zi fell ill. The two women living with them, Song and Kim, in order to seize her property, threw the four-year-old Hye-young onto a departing train.

More than ten years passed. The abandoned Hye-young was adopted by a fisherman family and renamed Han Jeong-min. Her adoptive father died in a sea accident, and her adoptive mother, unable to endure the hardships of life, took the family savings and left. Hye-young became the only family of her adoptive parents' son, Han Kang-pyo, who suffered from severe kidney disease since childhood. Hye-young had to regularly take him to the hospital for dialysis. To cover her brother's medical expenses, the nineteen-year-old Hye-young had to drop out of school and work various jobs. At the hospital where her brother was treated, Hye-young met the kind and handsome doctor, Kang Min-jae. Hye-young developed feelings for Min-jae and often followed him around, making Min-jae feel helpless. One day, Min-jae's younger brother, Seung-jae, ran away from home and accidentally knocked over Hye-young's fish stall. With no money, Seung-jae had to work for Hye-young to compensate for the loss. Initially, Hye-young's wild and aggressive nature made it almost unbearable for Seung-jae, but gradually he developed feelings for her.

Unexpectedly, Min-jae and Seung-jae's mother turned out to be none other than Kim, the woman who abandoned Hye-young. Another woman, Song, who also tried to seize Hye-young's family property, went bankrupt several times due to her bad habits, causing her daughter Soo-jee to live a troubled life. After going bankrupt, Song sought help from Kim multiple times and even threatened to expose the past incident of abandoning Hye-young. Having become a company boss, Kim had no choice but to comply. Before dying, Joon-seok's father entrusted him to find Hye-young. With no memory of the past, Joon-seok sent people to investigate and found Soo-jie, who lived with Hye-young during their childhood. In order to enjoy Joon-seok's fatherly love and for her own benefit, Soo-jie falsely claimed that her mother raised Hye-young until she accidentally drowned.

Kim forced Min-jae to get engaged, but Min-jae was waiting for his lost lover who had no contact. Helpless under his mother's pressure, Min-jae sought Hye-young's help to pretend to get engaged to placate his mother. They agreed on a contractual marriage, thus making the nineteen-year-old Hye-young the future sister-in-law of Seung-jae, leaving him conflicted in his feelings for her.

The exact translation may vary depending on the context in which it is used, but it generally conveys the idea of returning to a single principle or outcome after many variations or complexities.
