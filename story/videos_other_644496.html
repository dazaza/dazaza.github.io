From the sides to the center - The professional makeup artist of Yazzi said that many people have the habit of "starting from the core and moving outward" when applying makeup, which results in a thicker makeup on the nostrils and T-zone, making it more prone to smudging. She recommends applying foundation from the perimeter of the face towards the inner side, so by the time you reach the nose, the foundation is thinner and looks more natural.

Powder puff mixed with moisturizing lotion - This method involves applying it to the T-zone, which is the oiliest part of the face, making it less likely to smudge while enhancing the glow of the face for a hydrated look. The method involves wetting a sponge, squeezing out the excess water, then adding a bit of moisturizer, squeezing it again to evenly distribute it on the sponge, and finally pressing the powder onto the T-zone.

Upper and lower eyelids - Blinking can easily leave an embarrassing "fine line" on the upper eyelid. Once eyeliner and mascara smudge, they can easily stain the lower eyelid, resulting in an unattractive "panda eye."

5. Moisturizing spray - Makeup artists suggest spraying a moisturizing mist 20 centimeters away from your face after finishing your makeup. This not only effectively cools down the skin but also reduces the heavy feeling, making the makeup adhere better.

2. Damp powder puff with pure water - Kane, a Shiseido makeup artist, also recommends a good method: first dampen the powder puff with pure water, then squeeze it until it's about 70-80% dry before applying foundation. This makes the foundation especially adherent.

■TIPS

Many people are skeptical about "waterproof makeup" because various waterproof cosmetics on the market in the past could not completely resist sweat intrusion. However, this summer, makeup has entered a super-waterproof stage. To maintain a long-lasting makeup look, you need to purchase a super-waterproof cosmetic set. Meanwhile, makeup artists also particularly remind that removing super-waterproof makeup is harder than regular non-waterproof makeup. Therefore, during the removal process, allow the makeup remover to stay longer on the waterproof makeup to fully interact with it before gently removing it.

To keep your makeup lasting longer and make touch-ups easier, maintaining well-hydrated skin is very important. Hong Kong skincare expert MONICA pointed out that the key to creating a good "pre-makeup skin base" is using effective methods to apply efficient hydrating products, controlling oil in areas prone to oiliness, and ensuring the skin is hydrated without excessive oil. The key to choosing hydrating products is good spreadability, freshness without greasiness, and preferably thin gel-like or liquid textures. After washing your face, evenly spray toner all over the face, then gently cover with a warm towel for 5 minutes to lock in moisture. Apply oil control products on areas prone to oiliness like the T-zone, cheeks, lips, and chin. For example, to ensure eye makeup stays hydrated and adheres well, use an eye shadow primer that inhibits eyelid oil production before makeup or during touch-ups to prolong the effect.

Stylist Li Rui also mentioned that a crucial step in creating long-lasting base makeup is using a sponge to gently pat the skin. After applying foundation, lightly pat the face with a sponge to ensure the foundation blends well with the skin.

Hydrating serum mixed with foundation - First mix two or three drops of your daily moisturizer or hydrating serum with the foundation, then apply it to your face. This allows both hydration and base makeup to be done simultaneously, providing excellent moisturization. Afterward, proceed with other base makeup steps such as applying powder.

■Expert Secrets

Eyeshadow brush dipped in water - Dip the eyeshadow brush in water, then dab it on a tissue before picking up eyeshadow powder for longer-lasting makeup. Additionally, for sharper brows, first outline the brows with an eyebrow pencil, then dip a fine eyeshadow brush in water, wait until it's almost dry, then pick up brow powder and gently brush along the shape of the eyebrows. Use mascara to set the brows - For long-lasting eyebrow makeup, using brow powder works best. Another secret is setting the brows with mascara; transparent brow gel or slightly dried brown mascara works best.

Clever use of frozen mineral water - In hot weather, skin sebum can easily melt, so it's essential to cool the skin first. Before makeup, roll a mineral water bottle kept in the fridge overnight on your face to achieve the desired effect.
