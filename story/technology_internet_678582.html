The curtain fabric industry should try online marketing.

by bery2012045 on 2012-02-27 15:29:28

Online marketing is no longer a novel term. In recent years, its development speed has been astonishing. In 2009, under the pressure of the economic crisis, facing the saturated first and second-tier markets and suddenly fiercely competitive third and fourth-tier markets, all the executives of curtain fabric companies [http://www.beryhome.com/] were frowning with worry. At this moment, the online market with over 300 million internet users became the sweet big cake in everyone's eyes. Moreover, so far this cake still had more onlookers than takers. It depended on whether you could move fast enough and use the right methods to get more of the cake.

Event Replay: A Few Stones Stir Up Big Waves Online

All things must have a beginning. China having a huge number of internet users did not happen overnight. This blue ocean of the online market had been waiting for a long time. But until the past couple of years, online marketing really started to boom. Who was the first to eat the crab? Who opened the precedent for online marketing? To find this first one in the vast sea of the internet would be like looking for a needle in a haystack. But there were indeed a few events that turned red all over the network, not only hotly discussed among netizens but also became excellent cases for later online marketing discussions, allowing everyone to witness the powerful influence of online marketing.

As early as 2005, a post appeared on the Tianya Forum where a rich girl looked down on migrant workers and mocked the poor. At the time, it did not attract much attention, but a reply from another netizen claiming to be from China's upper class ignited an unprecedented war of words on the Tianya Forum. The reply described the extravagant life of China's secret aristocracy and expounded their values of respecting the poor, reducing the rich girl to nothing. A grand drama unfolded on [Tianya Community], attracting over 223,000 viewers and nearly 4,000 participants. This was not the most followed conversation on the network forum, but it was the rarest, most dramatic, confrontational, unprecedentedly brutal, and lasted through the night. The Southern Weekly even commented that "[this is a post that takes over seven hours to finish reading, a post that is said to be popular until the end of the year, a historically unprecedented super strong post]."

It was just such a post that made people realize the explosive power and influence of online marketing. Another commercial case echoed this, after the 5·12 Great Earthquake, again on Tianya, a post titled [Boycott Wanglaoji] instantly went viral on Tianya. Why boycott a patriotic company that donated 100 million yuan during the earthquake period? The popularity of this post was no less than the aforementioned noble post. Confusion and anger attracted millions of views and replies, but it turned out to be a [fake boycott], the real intention was to call on everyone to support patriotic enterprises, [buying out] Wanglaoji. Undoubtedly, this was a very successful online marketing campaign.

If Wanglaoji's online marketing had a strong flavor of hype, Red Bull's "Energy Journey" was even more worth imitating. This event held by Red Bull attracted over 10 million internet users to participate. They not only discussed enthusiastically but directly participated in the activity. They posted their feelings about the journey on forums and shared their photos with netizens. They talked about this event with great interest, and Red Bull ran through their entire journey. Experience replaced "credibility", "word-of-mouth communication" added points for Red Bull. In fact, online marketing here already had the triple function of "word-of-mouth communication", "online hype", and "experience marketing".

Curtain [http://www.beryhome.com/] Trial: Soft Online Marketing [Flowing Gold]

If you think all the above-mentioned are irrelevant to curtains, it can only mean that you have not yet entered the state of online marketing. The most different thing about online marketing compared to traditional media marketing lies in the interactivity between brands and netizens. The cases mentioned above are all classic examples of cleverly initiating the initiative of netizens, making netizens become a member of brand promotion and publicity. However, the scope of online marketing is very broad, and not all enterprises need to adopt the methods in the cases. In the curtain fabric industry, merchants also adopted different online marketing methods for promotion.

First, soft news release and hard advertising placement for brands. This is the most basic way of online marketing. It can be seen that brands adopting this marketing strategy are mostly national-scale enterprises with certain scale, strength, and fame, or curtain enterprises that are currently fully constructing national marketing networks. For most small and medium-sized regional curtain enterprises that are relatively safe in one corner, they have not yet taken the step of online news marketing.

Second, brand homepage. Used for brand news release and enterprise information provision. Netizens can view products, technologies, and store distribution maps through different links on the webpage.

Third, online event planning. This is already considered a relatively advanced level in online marketing, and the human and financial resources consumed are incomparable to the first two.

Fourth, word-of-mouth marketing through forums and blogs, community marketing through QQ groups and SNS. Forums, blogs, and SNS platforms have a relatively powerful function, which is that some relatively young netizens with similar preferences are active in certain specific online communities. On the sales level, group buying can already begin to be organized through the network, directly driving sales. For example, organizing discussions on decoration and curtains in online communities or establishing dedicated curtain QQ groups can not only directly sell and promote products but also solve issues within the industry such as recruitment and cooperation with upstream and downstream industries.

In fact, whether it is the construction and improvement of brand homepages, the placement of hard brand advertisements, online news dissemination, special reports, or forum community promotion, etc., they are gradually forming trends. From the results of the reporter's interviews and investigations, the online marketing of the curtain fabric industry has expanded from simple advertising placement to soft news release, event planning and organization, forum promotion, SNS platform marketing, blog word-of-mouth transmission, and even started practicing integrated marketing communication.
