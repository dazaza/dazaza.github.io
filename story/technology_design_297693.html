If someone else wins

by cq3118wpeq on 2009-12-07 12:54:53

"Me too..." Yi Shui said softly. Zhao Feng smiled faintly, with a sense of relief and fatigue. The hand that was hugging Yi Shui stroked her smooth long hair upward. "What are you going to do?" Yi Shui asked. Zhao Feng spoke, his eyes closed at the moment. Yi Shui didn't know whether he didn't want to say or didn't know how to say. Just as she wanted to change the topic, Zhao Feng began to speak. "Hua Zi opened a place without telling me. He didn't find the right people. It's a local bully from Hebei. He ran off with all three of our money. Only then did Hua Zi come to find me. There was no way, so I went to Hebei. This Hua Zi has too much courage." After finishing, Zhao Feng sighed wearily.

Yi Shui had heard something about these places, which meant opening a casino. But Zhao Feng didn't run a long-term casino. He only opened it occasionally when there was an opportunity, and each time the location was different, very hidden. Generally, they were some relatively wealthy people who didn't care about money. Their biggest hobby was gambling. They found people like Zhao Feng who had "resources", paid them to gather a gambling session. And the people Zhao Feng found were also rich and loved gambling. These people generally had cooperated with Zhao Feng before. Each session lasted for a minimum of three days and a maximum not exceeding two weeks. During this period, the gamblers were not allowed to leave the specified range by Zhao Feng. After the session ended, if the person who gathered the session won, they would split the profit 40-60 with Zhao Feng. If others won, the big winner would split the profit 30-70 with Zhao Feng, and the small winner would split the profit 20-80, depending on the actual situation. This kind of business made the most money, but the risk was also the greatest. Zhao Feng never opened a small session. Each time the amount involved was large, and the relative danger was also great.

"Are all problems solved?" Yi Shui asked.

"Hmm! Hua Zi almost lost his life..." Zhao Feng said slowly. He was really tired, leaning back against the sofa and sighing deeply.

"You should take a nap," Yi Shui said gently.

"No, let me stay with you like this for a while..." Zhao Feng said.

Yi Shui stopped talking, leaning on Zhao Feng's chest, listening to his weary yet strong heartbeat. Zhao Feng didn't say a word, his chest rising and falling with his breathing. This was the first time Zhao Feng had spoken so directly to Yi Shui about his own affairs. Except for being a little nervous when she heard that Hua Zi almost lost his life, Yi Shui didn't think these things were inappropriate. Somehow, these things happening to Zhao Feng seemed normal to her, just like workers having to go to work and doctors having to perform surgery. She was just a little worried about Zhao Feng's safety and whether her parents could accept him. Perhaps, Yi Shui still didn't fully understand the path Zhao Feng had chosen...

"Like this... girl... forever like this..." Zhao Feng's weary voice sounded.

Today, Zhao Feng woke up very late. He was too tired. Yi Shui received the interview notice, scheduled for 2 PM. When she pushed open the bedroom door and saw Zhao Feng still sleeping, she quietly retreated. She called for takeaway and placed it on the coffee table in the living room, then got dressed and left. It wasn't cold outside anymore. After the Spring Festival, the temperature rose. Yi Shui took a deep breath, sensing the arrival of spring.

The company for the interview was located in Si Hui. Yi Shui planned to take the bus and then transfer to the subway. Before the bus arrived, Zhao Feng called and asked where Yi Shui was. Yi Shui said she was out doing something and would be back in the afternoon. Yi Shui hadn't told Zhao Feng that she was looking for a job. Zhao Feng had once said that he didn't want Yi Shui to work so hard.
