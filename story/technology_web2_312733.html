Barca's Alleged 'Champion Escort' Sparks Outrage: Rivals Claim "We'll Give You 70 Points, We'll Compete for Second"

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 17:06:16

Barcelona Accused of Being Favorited for Championship, Rivals Joke: "Give You 70 Points, We'll Compete for Second"

According to Sina Sports, last weekend's Barcelona derby left Espanyol feeling aggrieved over their loss, while Barcelona felt their win was not dignified enough. The blame fell on referee Iturralde. He inexplicably awarded Barcelona a penalty, helping the home team defeat their fierce local rivals Espanyol 1-0. Then, in the player tunnel after the match, he red-carded Spanish player Baena, who had been questioning his penalty decision. In all honesty, this penalty seemed intentionally aimed at harming Espanyol. Equally intriguing is how two different truths emerged from the accounts of both Barcelona and Espanyol regarding this one penalty.

If Brualla is considered part of the "Real Madrid clique" among referees, then this game's official—Iturralde, one of Spain's famous referees—is considered part of the "Barcelona faction." Previously, whenever he refereed at Camp Nou, Barcelona never lost. This season, he only officiated one Barcelona game, where they won 2-0 away against Getafe. Another telling fact is that in the past six matches Iturralde refereed involving Barcelona, five penalties were awarded, all benefiting Barcelona. According to Marca, four of these penalties were questionable and suspected of being "gifted."

The controversial moment occurred in the 39th minute of the match against Espanyol when Ibrahimović crossed from the left edge of the box, and Xavi suddenly fell while challenging for the ball. Iturralde awarded a penalty, citing Baena pulling Xavi's shirt during the challenge, which immediately sparked strong dissatisfaction from the Espanyol side. Espanyol coach Pochettino subtly criticized after the match, saying, "I didn't see clearly what happened in the box, but maybe the referee did. Even after watching the replay, I can't be sure it was a penalty. My players told me at halftime that nothing happened, so in my view, there was no penalty."

Espanyol player Luis Garcia expressed frustration, complaining that Iturralde's unfair decision harmed them, saying, "If we were judged by such standards, we could also get a penalty in Barcelona's box. Yes, Barcelona is very strong, but they shouldn't score points through such penalties."

In contrast, the reaction within the Barcelona camp was quite different. Xavi, the player involved, testified, "This was an obvious penalty, didn't you see? I was grabbed by the jersey inside the box; Baena pulled my shirt, preventing me from jumping. There's no doubt, it was a penalty. He pulled me, and because I couldn't use my legs properly, I fell." After Xavi fell, Guardiola innocently spread his hands from the sidelines, indicating support for his player's claim of a penalty. However, post-match, Guardiola admitted he wasn't entirely sure if it was a penalty, stating, "Xavi told me Baena pulled him, and I believe Xavi."

Clearly, Espanyol manager Planes didn't trust Xavi, saying, "How can Xavi say that? By Iturralde's standard, there could have been 12 penalties in this Barcelona derby! I insist that there was no physical contact between Baena and Xavi. Even if there was, it wasn't enough to make him fall." CEO Collet added, "Why do we always get hurt in derbies? Last time, it was a penalty in the 93rd minute, and there was Messi's handball. This time again... If the opponent was Real Madrid, someone would have surely called for Franco's overthrow already. The federation should consider this issue. If it keeps happening like this, why not just give Barcelona 70 points and let the rest of us fight for second place?"

No matter what others said, it made little difference. Regarding the penalty controversy, Baena, the involved player, remained tight-lipped, refusing to comment until two days after the match, when the Barcelona-trained Espanyol player appeared on Catalan Radio to recount the incident. "Yes, I did pull Xavi," confirming the possibility of the penalty existing. But either Xavi's fall was exaggerated, or Iturralde made an incorrect call, as Baena continued, "But it definitely wasn't a penalty. My pull on Xavi wasn't enough to make him lose balance and fall. Xavi was in a very advanced position, and I reached back to sense his location. I did block Xavi momentarily, but the force wasn't enough to make him fall. You can check the TV replay. Honestly, I found it hard to believe it was called a penalty since I was still thinking about Xavi possibly shooting before turning around."

Reports also mentioned Baena confronting Iturralde in the players' tunnel after the match, resulting in a red card. What exactly did Baena say to irritate Iturralde? "I simply told him, 'Go check the TV replay!' Definitely not in an aggressive tone, that’s all." Baena was also dissatisfied with Guardiola, who stated in the post-match press conference, "Xavi told me that the Espanyol player admitted it was a penalty." "I really don’t know how they can say that. I never told Xavi it was a penalty. If we had any conversation, I would have surely told him it wasn't a penalty."

Madrid saw an opportunity to stir up controversy in this situation. Media opinions on this contentious call were significant, with Marca and AS both targeting Iturralde, accusing him of protecting Barcelona and awarding them three points. Otherwise, Real Madrid, who played one fewer game, would only be trailing Barcelona by two points. In contrast, Catalan media, though Espanyol is also a Barcelona-based team, wouldn’t speak up for a club with the Spanish royal crest. To counter capital media, the Sport newspaper specifically analyzed the match footage and included screenshots showing Baena indeed pulling Xavi. Deputy editor Carazo said, "Clearly, this was a penalty." More interestingly, the paper also presented a screenshot from Real Madrid's game against Valencia, suggesting Benzema was offside when scoring Valencia's first goal, with a provocative headline: "Real Madrid's first goal was offside. So, what's there to argue about?"

Fortunately, the controversy surrounding the penalty has moved Barcelona, Xavi, and Guardiola away from the center of the storm in Spain to Abu Dhabi for the Club World Cup. In their eyes, winning this championship is much more meaningful than debating whether it was a penalty. (Liu Hui)
