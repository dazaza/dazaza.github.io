If an adult's testicle is smaller than 8 milliliters, it could indicate testicular atrophy. For boys around age 10, the testicle volume generally should reach 4 milliliters, with anything less considered abnormal.

Next, use the palpation method to examine the testicles. The normal state of the testicles should be a smooth surface, full texture, no tenderness, and a clear boundary from the epididymis. Typical varicocele can be seen as twisted and expanded veins under the scrotal skin or felt as a worm-like mass of dilated vessels. Primary varicocele may reduce or disappear when lying down. If the symptoms persist even when lying down, it could be due to compression from tumors or other lesions. Once these symptoms appear, it's best to seek medical treatment as soon as possible.
