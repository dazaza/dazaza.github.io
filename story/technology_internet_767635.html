Google's Stock Breaks US$1000, Becoming the 3rd Largest Company in the US by Market Value

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:49:23

Google's stock price rose above $1,000 on Friday, as investors believed that Google would continue to dominate in mobile and video advertising despite fierce competition from companies like Facebook and Yahoo.

In Friday's trading on the Nasdaq, Google's stock increased by 13.80%, reaching $1,011.41, a new record high, adding approximately $40 billion to Google's market value.

This increase pushed Google’s market value past Microsoft and Berkshire Hathaway, making it the third-largest U.S. publicly traded company by market capitalization, only behind Apple and ExxonMobil.

Currently, Google's Android system is the world's most widely used mobile software, while its YouTube website is the world's most popular video streaming service. Google reported on Thursday that its net internet revenues for the third quarter grew by 23% year-over-year; the number of advertisements increased by 26% year-over-year, the highest growth rate in the past year, which more than offset an 8% decline in advertisement prices.

Analysts stated that due to uncertain global economic conditions, there was concern over whether U.S. companies could achieve revenue growth in the third quarter. However, Google's data released on Thursday showed good performance across all its businesses, except for Motorola Mobility, which has consistently been operating at a loss.

Analyst Jordan Rohan from Stifel Nicolaus said: "Google has the Android ecosystem, giving it a significant advantage. The success of Android, with user numbers growing daily, has begun to yield substantial returns for Google."

Rohan noted that Google saw accelerated revenue growth outside the U.S. and UK, particularly in Korea and Japan, during the third quarter, which was impressive. This situation might persist for some time."

At least 16 brokerage firms raised their target price for Google between $880 and $1,220.

Analysts from JPMorgan Chase & Co. said: "We believe the strong growth in paid clicks indicates that Google's ongoing transition to mobile is boosting demand for its advertisements." Previously, they expected Google's ad volume to grow by 21.5% year-over-year in the third quarter.

Negative Factors

Although many Wall Street analysts have raised their target price for Google above $1,000, Brian Wieser, an analyst at Pivotal Research, maintained his target price at $880 and gave Google's stock a "Hold" rating.

Some analysts warned that some of Google's ambitious projects, including driverless cars and Google Glass, have yet to prove their commercial potential, and expenditures on these projects may negatively impact Google's profit margins. Wieser estimated that excluding traffic acquisition costs, Google's gross profit margin in the third quarter was 34.9%, down from 37.2% in the previous quarter.

Moreover, Facebook and Twitter might attract an increasing number of small clients away from Google. In what is anticipated to be one of the most awaited initial public offerings (IPOs) of the year, Twitter plans to raise $1 billion or more.

Additionally, Google's Motorola Mobility division recently launched its customizable Moto X smartphone, hoping to reverse years of losses and eventually achieve growth. Although sales data for the Moto X smartphone have not yet been disclosed, analysts generally believe that the device faces a challenging competitive landscape against products like the iPhone.

Google acquired Motorola Mobility in 2012, and excluding certain items, this division incurred a loss of $218 million in the third quarter, four times the $49 million loss recorded in the same period last year.

Wieser explained his below-average target price for Google by saying: "While we acknowledge that most investors have gained more confidence in Google following its third-quarter earnings report, we believe that issues such as eroding profit margins, intensifying competition, and high capital intensity will ultimately affect its stock price over time."

Room for Further Growth

The significant rebound in Google's stock price on Friday was hard-earned, with investor focus primarily shifting to Facebook. So far this year, Google's stock has risen by 26%, but Facebook's stock has nearly doubled.

However, both Google and Facebook are performing better than Yahoo!. Yahoo! released a lackluster third-quarter earnings report this week, showing declines in its market share for display and search advertisements.

Facebook's stock rose by 3.6% on Friday to $54.08, while Yahoo!'s stock increased by 2% to $33.40. Baidu, often referred to as "China's Google," saw its stock rise by 7.1% to $164.78.

Some analysts pointed out that Google's stock still has room for further growth. Analysts from JPMorgan Chase & Co. indicated that some measures Google has continuously introduced to address declining ad rates could result in better performance during the upcoming holiday season.

This year, Google launched a service to help advertisers promote their products across various devices, including smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers. This move is expected to mitigate the negative impact of mobile advertising on ad rates, thereby boosting Google's overall ad pricing.

Other analysts mentioned that the potential of YouTube remains underutilized. In the third quarter, the number of ads on YouTube increased by over 75% year-over-year, and now 40% of the site's traffic comes from mobile devices.

Analysts from RBC Capital Markets said: "We estimate that YouTube generated about $4 billion in revenue for Google in 2012, positioning Google very favorably in the rapidly growing video advertising sector."
