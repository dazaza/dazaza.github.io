Don't Mourn for a Whole Life Over a Lost Love

A girl was involved in a car accident and called me from a shabby clinic where she lay injured. She cried, saying that the worst pain wasn’t her physical wounds but the immense sense of loss within her heart—right at this moment when she needed comfort and help the most, her boyfriend of six years coldly and ruthlessly chose to evade the situation. For this man, she had unconditionally loved him for six years without asking for any return, tolerating him, and using all she had to help him achieve his dream—buying a taxi. Now, she couldn’t even get a single word of concern or greeting from him.

Another girl fell in love with a man despite her family’s strong opposition—a man whose conditions were far inferior to hers in every aspect. This wasn’t necessarily a problem; as long as it was true love, they could be together. The problem was that this man clearly stated that he would never choose her. He wanted a wealthy woman and didn’t care about age or appearance as long as she had money. His shamelessness was laid bare.

Yet, the girl remained infatuated,纠缠ing desperately, even coming up with an incredibly foolish plan: no matter what means necessary, if she could have a child with this man, he would be forced by the pressure from both sets of parents to marry her.

There was another girl who, after dating a man for half a year, was suddenly informed by another girl that this man had been juggling relationships between the two of them. Even more absurdly, this man who professed deep love for her was already the father of a child—he also had a third woman who was persistently clinging to him.

After much painful thought, this girl decided to let go, though she still regretted it: “He is a very good man with great ambition.”

This is a story that happened last week in my life. I have always encouraged young girls to bravely pursue their own love because, after all, love is one of the beautiful things in life worth celebrating. However, if it is one-sided love, if loving someone means losing your dignity and self-respect, if you know clearly that the person is not worth entrusting your feelings to yet you insist on pursuing it regardless, I absolutely do not support, agree with, or encourage such so-called love.

Many things can only be truly understood after experiencing them. Just like emotions, after enduring pain, one learns how to protect oneself; after being foolish, one learns the importance of timely persistence and abandonment, and through gaining and losing, we gradually come to know ourselves.

In fact, life doesn't require such meaningless attachment. There's nothing that truly cannot be let go of. Moreover, a love that leaves one battered and bruised isn't necessarily unforgettable. To sacrifice one's brilliant youth and positive mindset for an unworthy relationship makes me feel sorry every time I hear such stories.

Let go. What you've missed out on is just a man who doesn't love you. It isn't until you meet someone who treasures you as a rare gem that you will feel the clouds parting and the moon shining brightly again—this is the true love that truly belongs to you.
