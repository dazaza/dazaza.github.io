**Case Analysis of Samsung TV Repair: Inspection of Open-Circuit Start Resistor R828**

**Symptoms:** A Samsung TV, after being turned on, shows no indicator light, no raster, no audio, and no image.

---

**Analysis and Inspection:** Preliminary judgment indicates a fault in the switch-mode power supply (SMPS) circuit. After disassembling the casing for visual inspection, it was found that both the power fuse F801 (T4A) and the rectified 300V DC voltage output fuse F802 were blown, indicating a short circuit in the primary circuit of the switch-mode power supply. Measuring D801's resistance to ground in-circuit showed normal values. Further tracing along the circuit revealed that the filter capacitor C809 (560uF/400V) and the power switch transistor Q823 (2SC4706) were both damaged. Other components checked out as normal.

After replacing C809, F801, F802, and substituting Q823 with C5148, the unit was tested again but still exhibited no light or sound. At this point, the +B (120V) output terminal voltage was measured at 0V. During the less than 2-minute test period after turning on the device, F802 (T315A) briefly emitted a green flash, confirming an ongoing issue in the power circuit. The unit was immediately powered off, and upon checking, C5148 was found to be blown again, while C809 and F801 remained intact.

C5148 was removed, and the secondary output circuit of the switch-mode power supply was inspected. Removing the load fuse F803 (TIA) from the +B output revealed that the filtering capacitor C831 (330uF/200V) had a ground resistance of 0Ω. Upon removing C831 for further examination, it was found to be normal. It was then discovered that the high-frequency rectifier diode D832 (RU-4AM) at the 120V output terminal was also blown.

After replacing Q823, F802, and D832, and restoring C831, a dummy load was connected across C832, and the unit was tested again. The bulb did not light up, and measuring the voltages at the b and c terminals of the switch transistor Q823 showed 0V and 300V, respectively. This clearly indicated an abnormality in the b terminal voltage. Further investigation revealed that the start resistor R828 (220kΩ) was open-circuited.

---

**Resolution Method:** Replacing R828 with a same-specification component resolved the fault.
