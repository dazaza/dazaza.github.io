Currently, competition is fierce across all industries. To secure a place in the market, various forms of promotion are inevitable. But as a laundry store, what kind of promotional method is more suitable? 

   

   Laundry stores are generally small in scale and are mostly located near residential areas. This determines that our promotions for laundry stores must follow two principles: 1. The scale of promotion is relatively small; 2. Promotions mainly target consumers in nearby residential areas. 

       The following are several promotional methods we often use: 

   One, Basic promotional methods 

   1. Self-advertising 

   This type of advertising has low investment, quick results, and a higher chance of reaching target customers. It is a widely adopted sales model for service industries to attract customers. Specific operations include making POP advertisements or lightboxes, signboards, etc., placed at the store entrance. This way, passers-by can see it, resulting in good effects. 

   2. Outside-store promotion 

   Utilize staff to distribute flyers, brochures; set up a promotional booth, hire promotional assistants during the opening period for on-site promotion, or establish a customer consultation desk for discount explanations and processing. 

   3. Trial washing activities 

   This is a fundamental method of brand marketing, especially suitable for newly opened or initially opened laundries, which can quickly establish a brand image. Specific methods include discounts, trial washes, free services, such as offering free services to the first customer who arrives at the store every day. Dry cleaning franchise

   4. Gift giving 

   Gift giving should be targeted and not conditionally offered. Otherwise, gift giving will lose its value, and pursuing a temporary sensational effect is not worthwhile. Gift giving should not only increase brand awareness but also consider enhancing brand reputation, closely linking with the brand's image. 

   5. Group services 

   Targeted promotions for groups other than individual customers can expand the business scope and enhance brand recognition. It can also transform sales into interactive sales, expanding the business district range and increasing revenue. 

   · Conducting business contacts in residential communities, neighborhood committees, etc. 

   · Expanding business in office buildings, public institutions, etc. 

   · Providing targeted services to hotels, restaurants, wedding photography studios, kindergartens, elite private schools, etc. 

Two, Comprehensive promotional methods 

   1. Discount promotions 

   The advantage of discount promotions lies in stimulating consumers to try the brand, reversing consumption preferences, quickly showing promotional effects, increasing existing customers' desire to consume, and encouraging customer spending. 

   a. Prize promotions 

   Forms: On-site lottery, prize-winning quizzes, competitions, giving away lottery tickets (with good social benefits) 

   Prize selection: Based on small amounts but high stimulation, prizes should be novel and unique. 

   Prize combinations can adopt a pyramid form. 

   Event principles: Including event time, lottery methods, etc. 

   Note: Laundry stores generally use on-site lotteries, with prizes consisting of gifts and discount coupons. 

   b. Coupon promotions 

   Usage time: Dry cleaning franchise chain

   * Store turnover shows a downward trend 

   * Facing competitors' low-price strategies, retaining old customers 

   * Store opening, distributing gift certificates in surrounding areas to expand influence 

   * Increasing customer loyalty by providing appropriate discounts to old customers. 

How to distribute: 

   * Select distribution targets based on different promotional goals, such as considering education, occupation, lifestyle preferences, levels, living conditions, etc. 

   * Choose distribution locations, either directly distributing to customers at the store, like giving a 5 yuan discount for every 50 yuan spent, or distributing to crowds in areas with high foot traffic or neighborhoods. 

   * Avoid repetition and waste, ensuring the recipients are potential customers, avoiding those outside the store’s service area and those with no obvious consumption potential, while preventing excessive concentration of coupon distribution to individuals. 

   c. Points-based discounts 

   Points-based consumption involves accumulating points for a certain amount of money, allowing customers to enjoy discounts or prizes when they reach a certain number of points. Due to its special nature, this is usually a long-term activity but should not exceed one year. Loyalty cards are typically printed uniformly by the headquarters to maintain a consistent brand image. 

   Points-based consumption can increase customer loyalty, stimulate repeat purchases, and overcome seasonal changes. 

   d. Membership promotions 

   Goal: Establish a long-term stable market, build consumer brand awareness, obtain a substantial number of membership fees, and understand market dynamics and customer needs. 

   Establishment: During the early stages of store opening, free provisional membership cards can be given (these cards have restricted usage times, usually less than 3 months, with discounts lower than or equal to formal members). After the opening period, as service quality gradually gains recognition, encourage customers to purchase cards and become formal members. The headquarters will provide gold and silver cards for premium members to meet different consumption needs. 

   2. Gift promotions 

   a. Free gifts 

   Timing: A. Encouraging competitors’ customers to switch to our brand B. Store opening celebrations C. Holiday promotions 

   Gift selection: Attractive, aligning with target customers' preferences, such as practical items, commemorative items, or decorative items. Gifts generally need to be branded with service logos and slogans, designed and produced by the headquarters. 

   b. Paid gifts 

   Timing: A. Enhancing brand image B. Increasing advertisement exposure C. Boosting store sales D. When the price of the gift itself is high 

   Note: When paid gifts are distributed, the gifts must be rare and personalized. The price is generally 30~50% of the gift's price, such as windmills, which are highly decorative symbols of Holland. 

   3. Price promotions (discounts, price reductions, special offers) 

   Timing: A. Facing threats from industry competitors B. Attracting large numbers of customers C. Off-season D. Major holidays 

   Discounts: Typically around 20~30% is appropriate. Too low prices not only harm the store's image and interests but also invite malicious competition from peers. 

   Scope: Depending on the situation, comprehensive price reductions can be implemented. If specific products are chosen for discounts, it can attract a particular group of people, such as professional attire in summer or down jackets in winter. 

   4. Service promotions 

   Experience in the laundry industry shows that the success of dry cleaning stores largely depends on the successful selling efforts of counter staff. 

   a. Stores with the capacity can offer some free consulting services, educating customers about laundry and clothing care knowledge. 

   b. Based on customer information, send greeting cards during holidays, along with coupons (mainly targeting members). 

   c. Provide humanized services, promoting company philosophy, allowing customers to quickly understand the services provided by Dutch Golden Windmill Laundry, including specialty services. 

   d. Smile service, engaging in friendly conversations with customers to learn basic information, expressing concern, and demonstrating service awareness. 

   5. Joint promotions 

   Collaborate with related industries such as clothing stores, beauty salons, gyms, etc., for joint promotions. Since both parties' product market positions differ, they can complement each other, creating a win-win platform. Although the Golden Windmill brand has strong market appeal, it is still a new face in the Chinese market. Establishing connections with these industries can help leverage resources to build product images and increase brand awareness. 

   Specific operations: 

   For example, establish a contract with a local well-known suit brand, offering a discount coupon for the Dutch Golden Windmill Laundry Chain with the purchase of a suit; or offering a discount coupon for the purchase of clothing from that brand to customers consuming at the Dutch Golden Windmill Laundry Chain. 

   6. Competitive promotions 

   Competitive contests are common motivational tools for employees, which can also enhance the operational capabilities of enterprises. According to surveys, 60% of employee potential can be unleashed through corporate incentive policies. 

   Specific operations: 

   For employees engaged in business liaison, formulate laundry contracting agreements to expand the business scope, such as nearby hotels, guesthouses, wedding studios, kindergartens, restaurants, etc. 

   Improve service levels, conduct periodic evaluations of business activities, and offer multiple types of rewards, such as training opportunities, job upgrades, material rewards, etc.
