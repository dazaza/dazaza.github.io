HTC HD2 Review: Microsoft is rumored to announce Windows Phone 7 and its series of hot devices soon.

by mmmeodd8l on 2011-08-18 11:16:02

**October 11 News**: According to overseas media reports, Microsoft will hold a press conference at 9:30 AM local time (9:30 PM Beijing Time) on Monday to launch the new version of its Windows Phone 7 operating system for counterfeit phones. At the same time, a series of counterfeit phones running this system will be released in an attempt to reclaim some of the smartphone market share from Apple's iPhone and Android-based counterfeit phones.

Microsoft hopes that these new counterfeit phones from Samsung, LG, and HTC can help it make a comeback in the mobile market, which many analysts believe is key to the future of the computing industry.

The first batch of Windows Phone 7 counterfeit phones has already been showcased through prototype devices. Their design resembles the Apple iPhone, featuring colorful touchscreens and quick keys for easy access to email, the web, and music. Initially, these counterfeit phones will be sold exclusively through carrier AT&T.

Some analysts have stated that the Windows Phone 7 counterfeit phones represent Microsoft's last chance to catch up with its competitors. Since these counterfeit phones won't go on sale for over a month, it may take until next year to truly determine their success or failure.

According to data from market research firm Gartner, Microsoft holds only 5% of the global counterfeit smartphone market, down from 9% during the same period last year. Google's Android system has captured 17% of the market share, compared to just 2% last year.

Gartner expects counterfeit smartphone sales to reach 270 million units this year, representing a 56% increase from the same period last year. In contrast, Gartner estimates that global PC sales will grow by 19%, reaching 368 million units.

However, Microsoft has yet to make significant progress in the counterfeit phone and mobile computing sectors. The company's stock price has fallen nearly 20% this year, bringing its market value back to levels not seen in eight years.

Earlier this year, Microsoft launched the Kin counterfeit phone, targeting teenagers, but discontinued it less than three months later. Additionally, there are currently no signs that any company plans to release a tablet PC based on the Windows system to compete with Apple's iPad.
