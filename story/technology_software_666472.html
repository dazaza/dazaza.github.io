About the NBA New Season

Question: CCTV broadcasts fewer NBA games this year. What do you think the reason is? Is it because of the lockout?

Stern: Everything takes time. The Oklahoma City Thunder is a young team that performs well both on the court and financially. The Utah Jazz suddenly became energetic this season and performed well. The Indiana Pacers are also a very young team and may make it into the playoffs based on their current ranking. After the new system is formed, teams like the Pacers could earn $16 million annually within two years. These teams can find suitable young players through the draft and sign free agents. This largely depends on the management of the team. If the team performs better in the draft, player exchanges, training, signing free agents, and using the Development League, they are likely to succeed. The Knicks decisively signed Jeremy Lin, trained him in the Development League, and recalled him, cultivating him better. At this point, other teams might ask themselves why the Knicks chose Lin while they didn't. He was available to any team at that time. Good team operations are not determined by the size of the market, but largely depend on how the team is managed. Of course, sometimes luck is needed.

Stern: Over time, people will see the unified relationship between a player's performance on the court and their income. Now, if you want to sign a free agent, you can only sign them for up to four years. Many years ago, we had ten-year contracts, eight-year contracts, and six-year contracts, but now the longest contract is four years. After four years, players who perform well can get higher-paying new contracts, while those who don't perform well won't get such contracts. Starting from the year after next, we will begin implementing a high luxury tax. When your player salaries exceed the cap, for example, if you sign a player for $20 million, you will have to pay an additional $45 million in luxury tax, making the total cost of the player $65 million. We believe this will constrain teams with substantial financial resources, as they would rather save money. This way, the strength between teams will become more balanced. Although the new luxury tax regulations haven't been implemented yet, we can already see their impact. Now, when teams sign new players, for example, when the Pacers signed David West, he is an excellent player, but the team only signed him for two years, this year and next year, because starting from the third year, the tax amount will be very high, so the team must carefully consider. The Dallas Mavericks did not sign J.J. Barea, Caron Butler, and Tyson Chandler because if they had signed long contracts with them, the luxury tax would have been very high, leaving no flexibility or room for change in the Mavericks' payroll. We have already seen some short-term effects, and I believe the new labor agreement will make our league more competitive.

Stern: I don't think I ever said players were greedy. I may have criticized the union, but I never said that about the players, because they work hard to earn their deserved pay. Our players have shown the fans how diligently they prepare for each game, with veteran players like Kobe and Garnett always maintaining a proper attitude towards the game, and young players like Paul and LeBron training hard. These players are not only good players who perform well on the court, but they are also highly focused on basketball and understand their influence on the public. At the same time, they have shown how basketball helps the community, such as our NBA Cares initiative. In the upcoming All-Star Weekend in two weeks, we will not only see exciting dunks and three-point shots, NBA and WNBA All-Star games, but we will also see contributions made by players and legendary stars to the community. Players will showcase their talents through a series of activities.

About the Future Development of the NBA in China

Stern: If the playoffs started tomorrow, according to the current rankings, the Lakers and Celtics would both make the playoffs. Once in the playoffs, the question becomes how well you can perform, whether you can reach the finals and win the championship. About the Current NBA Landscape

Stern: This is not due to the lockout, but the result of the development of the entire media industry. We have two games every weekend, which is ideal for us and fits the viewing habits of Chinese fans. Moreover, Sina broadcasts games live every day. We have already begun to see changes in the viewing habits of fans, especially young fans. I read an article in The New York Times stating that our fans are increasingly watching NBA games and video clips through tablets or smartphones. I may be old and unable to fully understand this trend (joke). Of course, I actually do understand it. This is a global trend. While fans watch games on TV, they simultaneously watch games online, and the number of fans using two to three viewing tools at the same time is continuously increasing. We know this is the future trend. We highly value our relationship with TV partners, and we also believe that cooperation with digital partners is equally important.

Stern: I can't answer this question for you right now. According to our current system, a team can have three players with high salaries simultaneously, but then they won't have the salary cap space to sign other players. Other players who want to join this team must accept the reality of having to take a pay cut. For example, the New York Knicks are a good example. They signed Tyson Chandler, Carmelo Anthony, and Stoudemire, then began looking for players who are not superstars but have the potential to become stars, like Jeremy Lin, whose annual salary is less than one million. Teams can still sign several superstar players, but strong teams place more emphasis on the overall lineup. The purpose of the salary cap and luxury tax is to balance the financial strength of each team. In the new labor agreement, there is also a revenue-sharing clause aimed at enabling less wealthy teams to have the ability to sign good players, and constraining wealthy teams when signing players. I believe this will make our league more competitive.

Stern: I'll answer this question from two aspects. For us, the foundation for the long-term development of the NBA in China is for fans to truly love and understand our games and follow our stars, regardless of the nationality of the stars or the country of origin of the fans. As Yao Ming recently said, the NBA is more popular in China than ever before because fans focus on James, Kobe, and other players, such as Wade. Having local players is certainly a good thing, as it brings more fans. But I think to achieve this, we need to work together with the CBA, such as establishing a basketball training center in Dongguan. I've been to our training center, where the coach proudly told me about a player who is 7 feet 3 inches tall and has a wingspan longer than Yao Ming's. I don't know how well he plays basketball, but we know his wingspan is long. I think these two points are important: training and developing young players. Of course, we also need to give back to our fans. We will have an exhibition game, which I prefer to call a friendly match, similar to soccer. We have already started planning to host at least two friendly matches in China this year, and we will hold two preseason games before the 2012-13 season. I hope other sports can come to China, but we are confident in basketball and believe it will remain the favorite sport of Chinese fans.

Stern: I think you're flattering me, the NBA cannot create stars, the true creators of stars are the players themselves. In the Atlanta Hawks game I watched last night, Josh Smith proved that he is an outstanding young power forward. Similarly, Cousins of the Sacramento Kings would say the same, and Favors of the Utah Jazz would also say "I'm young, I have potential." Judging the current power forwards solely by their performance in a particular season or moment is not comprehensive. We indeed have many excellent guards, but I believe that in the future, the NBA will produce many outstanding power forwards; they just need more time to show their abilities.

Question: How can Jeremy Lin help expand the Chinese market? Will he surpass Yao Ming?

Question: During the labor negotiations, you once criticized players for being too greedy. With the regular season now underway, how do you hope these players improve their public image?

Stern: I don't think it's appropriate to say this season is better than last season. Last season was the most successful season in our history. Indeed, this season was shortened due to the labor negotiations, with low shooting percentages for players and teams, and only two preseason games before the start of the season instead of eight in previous seasons. Therefore, teams and players need more games to adapt and get back on track. It's not just about shooting percentages; whether players shoot 46% or 44%, our players are still the best in the world, and our games remain very exciting. You might think Boston Celtics are too old, but that's not necessarily true, and you need to continue observing by watching the games; you might think the Lakers are too old, but Kobe might disagree, and you need to continue observing by watching the games; you might think the Los Angeles Clippers with Chris Paul will be the better team in Los Angeles, but that's not necessarily true, and you need to continue observing by watching the games; if the regular season ended today, the Milwaukee Bucks would make the playoffs, but that's not certain either, and you need to continue observing. I also need to continue observing Jeremy Lin. We already have highlights throughout the season, with high-quality games. Compared to last year, some numbers may be slightly lower, the season is compressed, and the time coaches allow players to rest due to injury is different from last season, but this doesn't prevent us from having a successful season. By the playoffs, you will have a new understanding of this season.
