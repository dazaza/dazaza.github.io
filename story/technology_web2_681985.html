Follow-up on the public's indifference to the girl who was knocked down: an aunt who collects scrap says that she is not afraid of being wronged

by fd12yt9hgyi on 2012-02-28 16:39:50

Yueyue's parents knelt before their illiterate and waist-pained benefactor, sobbing incoherently. Nearby, Chen Xianmei covered her face and wept. This photo was taken by reporter Kunlun Chen of the Express Newspaper. Chen Xianmei witnessed the first vehicle running over Yueyue.

At 5:30 PM on October 13th, the sky gradually darkened over Guangfo Hardware City in Huangqi Town, Nanhai, Foshan, Guangdong, as a drizzle began to fall.

Two-year-old Yueyue (alias) had just returned from kindergarten when her mother went to collect laundry. As usual, Yueyue played alone in the alley near her home when misfortune suddenly struck. A bread van driving towards her suddenly accelerated, knocking Yueyue down and dragging her under the car. The right wheel ran over Yueyue’s hips. The driver stopped for a moment, then stepped on the gas and drove away, with the rear wheels running over Yueyue again.

The suffering Yueyue was unable to call out loudly for her mother or struggle. A witness walked by her side without even glancing at her. Two more passersby walked past Yueyue, equally indifferent. At this point, a small cargo truck drove up, seemingly not noticing Yueyue on the ground, and ran over her again. Yueyue was now motionless. In the following five minutes, it seemed like a nightmare as more than ten passersby walked past Yueyue. Each one merely glanced at her, with no one offering help, not even making a phone call for assistance. It seemed as if the shops nearby were too busy to spare a glance. Thus, eighteen people passed Yueyue.

In this critical moment, a short garbage collector aunt noticed Yueyue. She immediately put down her jute bag and tried to lift Yueyue, but Yueyue was already limp. The aunt then moved Yueyue from the middle of the road to the roadside and called for help. Still, no one came forward. The aunt ran into the alley, and several seconds later, Yueyue's mother rushed over, panic-stricken. She picked up Yueyue, who was drenched in blood, and ran madly to the hospital.

Currently, Yueyue lies unconscious in the intensive care unit of the Guangzhou Military Region General Hospital of the Army. Her eyeballs show no signs of movement, her face is swollen, her pupils are dilated, and she is in a deep coma.

Dr. Peng from the ICU said that Yueyue suffered severe traumatic brain injury, almost reaching brain death, with a poor prognosis. The most optimistic estimate is that she will remain in a vegetative state, currently relying on machines and medication to maintain her breathing and heartbeat, "always in danger of losing her life." According to Dr. Peng, the most fatal injury was caused by the first violent collision, which resulted in a deceleration brain injury upon hitting her head.

Yesterday, the Foshan police announced that at 9 PM on the day of the incident, the officer captured the second hit-and-run driver Jiang. In the afternoon, the driver Hu of the first vehicle turned himself in. Currently, both have been criminally detained.

"No fear of being wrongfully accused—saving lives is the most important."

■ Dialogue with the garbage collector aunt

The place where the little girl was tragically run over, Guangfo Hardware City, is located in Huangqi. Transport vehicles constantly weave through the area, making the already narrow roads between shops even more crowded. On this street lined with shops, no one helped the little Yueyue struggling painfully in a pool of blood until the garbage collector aunt appeared. This aunt is named Chen Xianmei, 58 years old, only over 1.4 meters tall, and weighs less than 40 kilograms.

Reporter: Please tell us about your personal situation?

Chen Xianmei: I am from Yangshan, Qingyuan, Guangdong, living in Foshan with my children. During the day, I cook for a small company, and in the afternoon, I go out to collect garbage to make some money.

Reporter: Can you describe the situation when you saved Yueyue?

Chen Xianmei: That afternoon, I was picking up garbage in the hardware city when I saw a child lying on the road from afar, so I hurried over. When I got next to the girl, I heard her "ah ah" cries. I picked her up and saw one eye open, the other closed, tears streaming down her face, with blood flowing from her mouth and nose, and also bleeding from the back of her head. I wanted to pick her up, but the child was limp and collapsed immediately. I didn't dare to use force, so I dragged the child to the side of the road and shouted for help from passersby, but no one paid attention.

Reporter: In the video, I see you shouting continuously. What were you shouting?

Chen Xianmei: I asked the nearby shops if this was their child. They said they "didn't know." Later, I saw Yueyue's mother.

Reporter: What were you thinking when you saved Yueyue? Did you think about possibly being falsely accused?

Chen Xianmei: I wasn't afraid. I usually help elderly people who fall down. Someone has to help. Saving lives is the most important thing. After all, she was still a child. Even though it was dark, it was impossible not to see the child lying on the road. I saw her from far away.

Yesterday, the garbage collector aunt became widely known in Foshan, attracting a large number of journalists to interview Chen Xianmei, which "startled" her, as she had spent her entire life farming. "There are some rumors, and hearing them makes us feel very bad," said Chen Xianmei's daughter-in-law. She heard people say her mother-in-law was "seeking fame, hyping herself up." Hearing these words made her heart heavy, "Is it really so hard to be a good person?"

According to reports by our correspondent Shuling Zhang and the Evening News among others.
