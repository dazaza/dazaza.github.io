Speculative enthusiasm in the diethylene glycol industry wanes, market oversold

by jdhgsa078 on 2011-08-29 16:44:09

Recently, the market of diethylene glycol has entered an over-fall state. Due to the increasing uncertainties in the economic situation, the negative factors have been magnified. For instance, the European debt crisis remains unresolved and the prospect for the US economy is not optimistic. The world economy may experience a second dip. Therefore, one should not expect too much from the commodities market. Currently, the rebound is still weak. Apart from being affected by financial aspects, the weakness in the fundamentals of diethylene glycol cannot be overlooked. The port inventory is as high as 110,000 tons, basically all stored in bonded warehouses. Confronted with cost pressure and heavy losses, mainstream cargo holders are reluctant to clear customs and are waiting for a rebound opportunity. Moreover, the operation rate of downstream unsaturated resin industry is insufficient. Purchasing of raw materials is need-based. The demand is very weak and the mentality of players in the industry has dropped to freezing point. Hence, market analysts take a cautious wait-and-see stance on the future of diethylene glycol.

From an economic perspective, the economic data from the United States and Europe is not optimistic. The entire economy is contracting. Although commodities have financial attributes, they are mainly commodity attributes. Only after the overall world economy improves will crude oil and other leading products truly see a rise. Up to now, it can be said that the financial market in Europe is in turmoil. Instability in peripheral economies has caused noticeable fluctuations in the mentality of the diethylene glycol market, which has somewhat restrained the delivery situation.

Currently, although sellers are trying hard to maintain prices, the cost of sources in the hands of cargo holders is relatively high. Unless the seller has problems with cash flow or due to warehousing expiration and other factors sells at low prices, mainstream cargo holders will hold out if possible under normal circumstances; being in a loss-making position, the mentality of players in the industry is relatively sensitive. Intermediaries have reduced their attention due to the limited profit margin of this product; another part of people is waiting to bottom fish. However, because the market shows no signs of bottoming out, there is no strong intention to enter the market. Given that the fundamentals of diethylene glycol remain relatively weak, before, Zhuochuang suggests that a short-term bearish and long-term bullish strategy would be more appropriate. Short selling should be mainly within the day, while a sharp retracement suggests light-position buying would be better. Long positions should be held cautiously according to the funds and risk capacity of investors with stop-loss set properly.

In summary, the current diethylene glycol market is rather complicated. The main factor is still constrained by the high inventory and low demand situation. Although the market occasionally shows signs of rebound, in the long run, the market may still have the possibility of repeated fluctuations; the contradiction between buyers and sellers is becoming increasingly prominent, choosing the best entry point is a rather headache-inducing topic for insiders recently; the downstream demand side still lacks certain highlights to boost market sentiment. Zhuochuang believes that in the short term, the possibility of weak oscillation in diethylene glycol is relatively high.
