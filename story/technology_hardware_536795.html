(1) Precautions for repairing inverter air conditioners. The DC power supply of an inverter air conditioner differs from that of a regular air conditioner. Its main circuit rectifies high voltage and has a large filter capacitor capacity. During repair, it is essential to discharge the capacitor to prevent electric shock. Since inverter air conditioners have a wide range of power supply voltages, some manufacturers use switch-mode power supplies for the control circuits. Care must be taken during repairs to avoid live board issues.

(2) Differences in inverter air conditioner circuit repairs. Inverter modules are manufactured with varying internal circuits based on manufacturer requirements. Some modules contain protective circuits that provide current power to the main control board. When using fault codes for diagnostics, it's important to understand the overall circuitry of the machine; otherwise, it’s easy to take wrong steps. For example, if the air conditioner displays a communication fault, the issue may not necessarily lie in the communication circuit. Issues such as missing DC280V or internal protection within the inverter module can cause similar fault symptoms.

(3) Inverter Module Testing. An inverter module (power module) has five separate plugs labeled P, N, U, V, W. P and N connect to the positive and negative terminals of the DC power source, while U, V, W connect to the three-phase windings of the compressor. When the five plugs of the inverter module are disconnected from external circuits, the resistance between U, V, W should be infinite. If the measured resistance is very small, it indicates an internal breakdown. The resistance between P and U, V, W should have forward and reverse values of 40k and infinite respectively. The measurement results between N and U, V, W should be opposite. If the measurement patterns differ, it indicates that the inverter module is damaged.

(4) Main Circuit Repair of Inverter Air Conditioners. Common faults in the main circuit often involve blown fuses, varistors, rectifier bridges, main filter capacitors, inverter modules, and compressors. Repairs can be conducted step-by-step. First, measure whether there is 280V voltage between the P and N connectors of the inverter module. If there is voltage, the preceding circuit is normal; otherwise, it is faulty.

To distinguish between inverter module and compressor faults, measure the three-phase voltage on the compressor coils. If there is voltage but the compressor does not start, the fault lies in the compressor. Alternatively, measure the resistance of the compressor coils. Normally, the resistance of the three-phase coils should be identical. If conditions permit, three same-power light bulbs can be connected in star formation and then connected to the U, V, W terminals of the inverter module. After turning on the machine, the bulbs should gradually brighten from dim. If the bulbs do not light up, it indicates a fault in the inverter module or control circuit; otherwise, the fault lies in the compressor.

(5) Too Low Frequency of Inverter Air Conditioner Compressor. A low compressor frequency is often due to: too low power supply voltage, too small difference between set and actual temperature, failure of indoor ambient temperature thermistor, incorrect position of the air conditioner debugging switch, failure of outdoor ambient temperature thermistor, excessively high outdoor environmental temperature, failure of compressor exhaust pipe thermistor, poor electromagnetic interference resistance of the outdoor main control board, power grid pollution or poor contact, excessive refrigerant inside the system.

(6) Too High Frequency of Inverter Air Conditioner Compressor. A high compressor frequency that cannot be reduced is often due to: too large difference between set and actual environmental temperature, failure of indoor ambient temperature thermistor, insufficient refrigerant inside the system, poor interference resistance of the outdoor main control board, power grid pollution or system freeze.
