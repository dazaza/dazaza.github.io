1. Blockage of pores by dirt: Many people have oily skin with long-term excessive sebum secretion. If they don't take proper care of their skin and fail to clean the dust and dirt off daily, over time, the skin becomes unclean, leading to blocked pores. Sebum cannot be expelled, naturally enlarging the pores and resulting in large pores.

2. Dryness and dehydration: Once the keratin absorbs enough water, it swells like a sponge soaked in water. The cells around the pores swell with water, making the pores less noticeable. Conversely, when the skin surface lacks moisture, the stratum corneum appears dry and rough, making the pores more prominent. Many young people, relying on their youthful skin, often overlook this point. Poor skin hydration not only makes the skin look rough and the pores larger but also causes dull complexion.

3. Improper care: Over-stimulation caused by improper squeezing of pimples can break the epidermis. Once the dermis is damaged and the skin lacks regenerative function, new cells cannot be produced, leaving uneven scars and making the pores larger. Improper makeup application and overuse of masks can also lead to enlarged pores.

4. Skin laxity and aging: With age, blood circulation gradually becomes less smooth, and the subcutaneous fat layer of the skin tends to loosen and lose elasticity. If appropriate maintenance and care are not provided, aging accelerates, and the pores naturally enlarge further.

5. Smoking is also one of the causes of enlarged pores. While enjoying the euphoric sensation of smoking, cigarettes cause vasoconstriction and reduced blood circulation, preventing nutrients from being delivered smoothly to the skin cells. As a result, dryness and aging occur earlier, facial lines sag, and pores expand.
