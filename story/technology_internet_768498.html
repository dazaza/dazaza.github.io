3D-printed car Urbee2 can drive from Shanghai to Urumqi with only 38 liters of gasoline

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:56:30

Urbee 2 is a super fuel-efficient car made through 3D printing, and it is a collaborative effort by the design company KOR Ecologic, direct digital manufacturer RedEye On Demand, and 3D printing manufacturer Stratsys. This is a three-wheeled, 7 horsepower (5 kW), rear-wheel drive eco-friendly hybrid vehicle. It is expected to hit the road in 2015, and with just 10 gallons of gasoline (about 38 liters), you can drive it across the United States (approximately 4500 kilometers from east to west, similar to the distance from Shanghai to Urumqi).

The first Urbee was released in 2010 as a 5 horsepower (3.8 kW) hybrid car with a 3D-printed body. However, due to design and safety issues, this car remained only at the conceptual stage.

Urbee 2 has an overall similar design to its predecessor: two seats, three wheels, and powered solely by the rear wheel. It weighs about 550 kg, which is 30% lighter than its predecessor. The body design has been slightly adjusted to enhance aerodynamic performance. Its drag coefficient is 0.149, compared to Toyota Prius and Tesla Model S's drag systems which are 0.25 and 0.24 respectively.

Urbee 2 is a hybrid vehicle that runs on electricity in the city. It stores approximately 7.6 kWh of electricity internally, and the two front wheels are driven by a pair of 36-volt electric motors, providing together 8 horsepower (6 kW) cruising power, with a maximum power of 16 horsepower (12 kW).

Under electric drive, it can travel about 64 km. If it’s running low on power during your commute, an internal combustion engine will drive a generator to charge the battery.

On highways, it uses the internal combustion engine for power. At 60% of its maximum power, the engine operates most efficiently, with a cruising speed of approximately 110 km/h. When climbing hills, both the electric and diesel engines will be used simultaneously to gain more power.

Urbee 2 includes over 50 3D-printed components, taking 2500 hours using the Stratsys Fortus 900mc 3D production system. "Over 50% of Urbee 2 will be 3D printed," said Jim Kor, owner and chief designer of KOR Ecologic. "The parts you see and touch on the car are basically all 3D printed. Most 3D-printed components use ABS plastic."

In most regions, Urbee 2 is expected to be registered as a motorcycle because its size and weight do not fit the majority of automobile definitions. This car is currently raising funds on Kickstarter with a target of 30,000 Canadian dollars.
