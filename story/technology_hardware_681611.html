What do you eat for lunch? Food not to eat.

by hdghu19056 on 2012-02-28 14:27:54

What to eat for lunch? Unhealthy food to avoid:

1. Fried food: Major hazards include: 1) fried starch leading to cardiovascular diseases; 2) containing carcinogenic substances; 3) destroying vitamins and causing protein denaturation.

2. Pickled food: Major hazards include: 1) leading to high blood pressure, overburdened kidney, and nasopharyngeal cancer; 2) affecting the mucosal system (harmful to the gastrointestinal tract); 3) prone to cause ulcers and inflammation.

3. Processed meat products (such as dried meat, meat floss, sausages, etc.): Major hazards include: 1) containing one of the three major carcinogens: sodium nitrite (preservative and coloring agent); 2) containing a large amount of preservatives, increasing the burden on the liver.

4. Biscuit-type foods (excluding low-temperature baked biscuits and whole wheat biscuits): Major hazards include: 1) excessive use of flavoring and coloring agents which burdens liver function; 2) severely destroys vitamins; 3) too many calories, low in nutrients.

5. Carbonated drinks and cola-type beverages: Major hazards include: 1) containing phosphoric acid and carbonic acid, taking away a large amount of calcium from the body; 2) excessively high sugar content, causing a feeling of fullness after drinking and affecting regular meals.

6. Convenient-type foods (mainly referring to instant noodles and puffed snacks): Major hazards include: 1) excessively high salt content, containing preservatives and flavoring agents, harmful to the liver; 2) only providing calories, no nutrition.

7. Canned foods (including fish, meat, and fruit types): Major hazards include: 1) destroying vitamins and causing protein denaturation; 2) too many calories, low in nutrients.

8. Dried plum and preserved fruit products (fruit preserves): Major hazards include: 1) containing one of the three major carcinogens: sodium nitrite; 2) excessively high salt content, containing preservatives and flavoring agents, harmful to the liver.

9. Frozen sweet food (ice cream, popsicles, and various ice creams): Major hazards include: 1) containing cream that easily causes obesity; 2) excessively high sugar content affects regular meals.

10. Barbecue-type foods: Major hazards include: 1) containing a large amount of "benzo[a]pyrene" (the top of the three major carcinogens); 2) one roasted chicken leg = toxicity of 60 cigarettes; 3) causing protein carbonization and denaturation, increasing the burden on the kidneys and liver.
