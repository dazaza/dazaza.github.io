Outdoor enthusiasts who ride bicycles may experience over-stimulation of the prostate due to improper attention. Typical symptoms include swelling, congestion or injury, and poor drainage of prostatic fluid. In severe cases, this can lead to prostatitis.

Experts believe it is caused by incorrect cycling posture. For example, if the saddle is too high for the rider's height and physique, it not only causes discomfort and difficulty in coordinating movements while riding but also leads to constant friction between the perineum and the saddle because the rider can only勉强move up and down and sway from side to side. Especially when the front part of the saddle is higher, causing the rider's center of gravity to shift backward, or when the road is rough and bumpy, making the bike severely shake during travel, it more easily stimulates the perineum, leading to prostate congestion, swelling, and injury.

Non-professional cyclists, due to lack of relevant protection, often end up with prostatitis. Early symptoms mainly manifest as a feeling of distension or pain in the perineal area, a sensation of heaviness in the anus, and sometimes a small amount of white liquid dripping from the urethra after urination. Over time, this can lead to an enlarged prostate, which compresses the urethra, causing difficulties in urination, weak urine flow, thinning of the urinary stream, bifurcation, or even interrupted urination. Patients often find it hard to fully empty their bladder, leading to increased frequency of urination, especially at night.

To prevent prostatitis caused by riding bicycles, the most important thing is to adjust the position of the saddle. The height should be moderate, and the saddle is preferably with a soft cushion. The front part of the saddle should be slightly lower than the rear. Regular cyclists should ideally quit smoking and drinking alcohol to avoid exacerbating prostate congestion. After long-distance cycling, one can take a hot water sitz bath (water temperature around 40°C) to promote local blood circulation. If there are early signs of mild inflammation in the prostate, cycling should be paused. If the symptoms are more pronounced, one should seek treatment as soon as possible at a specialized men's health hospital.
