A layer of red mist over the pastry

by anonymous on 2012-03-09 08:57:48

My body, out of control due to fear, instinctively retreated a few steps. I turned around and closed the door. Outside, a scream was heard. Everyone has gone mad; that thing outside wasn't Miao Wei at all, but more like a walking corpse. His expression, his eyes were like those of a wolf that hadn't eaten for a long time. Huang Hua Dong, who had been picking his toenails earlier, also heard the sound outside and looked at me with suspicion. I didn’t want to explain anything, so I returned to my seat, looking at the dark sky outside, feeling as if the world had fallen into chaos.

The commotion outside soon subsided. Huang Hua Dong looked at me as if I were a monster, then curiously opened the dormitory door. I wanted to stop him, but found myself drenched in cold sweat from behind, my body once again overwhelmed by sorrow. The door opened to reveal many people gathered outside, bloodstains on the ground spoke volumes. I struggled to move towards the door step by step. Several people ran out covering their wounds, and lying on the ground between the crowd was someone whose clothes were completely soaked in blood—Miao Wei.

Not far away, Yang Jian Xiang stood there, holding a stool covered in blood. Blood flowed incessantly from his ankle, yet he seemed to feel no pain, his gaze fixed on Miao Wei. Huang Hua Dong pulled Tang Fei, one of the onlookers, over and asked what happened. Tang Fei, only just recovering from shock, said, "Miao Wei... he just started biting people for no reason. Several people got bitten. In panic, Yang Jian Xiang grabbed the stool to knock him unconscious, but he lost control of his strength, and this is what happened."

"Move aside," said the dorm supervisor and several security guards as they parted the crowd. None of them could believe the scene before them. In such a short period, two bloody incidents occurred.

"Hey, there's been a violent incident at Linjiang Garden, come quickly to help."

"At Dexing Garden, some students have gone crazy, come quickly."

The walkie-talkies of the security guards kept ringing. "... This..." The security guards stood helplessly in place, and the dorm supervisor, in a rush, dialed 110, but the line was busy!!! If even 110 was this busy, had the whole world gone mad? We students stood dumbfounded, vaguely sensing that something major had occurred.

After several failed attempts, the dorm supervisor finally managed to get through to 110 and reported the situation at the school. Police support should arrive shortly. For now, we could only treat the wounded and send them to the hospital.

My phone buzzed incessantly in my pocket. It took me a while to notice the vibration, realizing how much my nerves had frayed. But why hadn't I seen any blood soaking through my clothes? Could these black bugs be sucking blood? I didn't want to take off my clothes to check my skin. Let it be, live day by day.

"Mom, what's wrong?" I answered the call.

"Child, are you okay? Your dad and I saw the news. There are people biting others everywhere now. Something happened at your dad's school yesterday too. Is everything okay at your place?"

"Mom, I'm fine. I'm in a very remote area, nothing's happening here. I feel like something bad is about to happen. You and Dad should go down to the city," I replied. I've always felt unfilial, never calling home, always waiting for my parents to call. And now, with my half-crippled body, I probably won't be able to fulfill my filial duties anymore. The only thing I can do is not worry my parents.

"Child, come back. Buy a ticket and fly back now. We'll feel better when you're back with us."

"Mom, really, it's fine. As you've seen on the news, tickets are almost impossible to buy. Besides, I've been an adult for so long, I'll be fine. If anything happens, wouldn't I tell you? That's all, I have something to do, I'll hang up now," I quickly ended the call, my eyes moist. I feared that if I continued, I would reveal my own situation. Better to let myself perish alone than burden them with worry.

In the distance, police cars sped towards us. Many people stuck their heads out to watch. This wasn't the first time we'd seen police cars, and we hadn't expected that after just a day, we'd need police intervention again. I thought a large number of police officers would come to handle the situation, but only two arrived—one taking notes with a notebook, the other explaining to the dorm supervisor and security guards, "Crimes are happening everywhere. All police officers have been deployed. The police station has already detained a large number of people and can no longer arrest suspects. We hope the school can allocate a space to separately detain student suspects." The dorm supervisor understood and immediately contacted the school administration. Students dispersed, many contacting their families, but more packing their luggage, hoping to return home as soon as possible.

I sat alone on the balcony, gazing at the night sky. It was still pitch black, and I had grown accustomed to living without sunlight. The frequency of my body's pain gradually decreased. I still lacked the courage to face my own body. Now, the only pain was in my abdomen, occasionally excruciating, as if it were splitting apart. Fewer people attended classes today, even teachers requested leave to leave the school. Fear enveloped everyone, and no one knew when this darkness would end.

I swallowed a mouthful of saliva and realized everyone's mouths were dry, stomachs growling incessantly. I had eaten dinner just half an hour ago, and surprisingly, I ate only meat. Yet, I was hungry again. I had a strong urge to bite someone, but I knew I couldn't do it. Miao Wei’s face kept appearing in my mind—he was more like a thousand-year-old zombie, feeding on humans. I didn't want to become like him because... I felt it wouldn't be cool.

I aimlessly walked out of the dormitory. Looking back, I saw Cheng Wei and Huang Hua Dong talking on the phone with their families. They didn't notice the abnormality in my eyes at that moment. This was the best time for me to act—just one bite, to taste human flesh. Just one bite would suffice. Saliva uncontrollably surged out.

I slapped myself, hating what I was thinking, and turned to leave. On the way out, I encountered Tang Fei, the foolhardy one, who assumed I was going to buy food. He encouraged me to treat guests and kept talking as I ignored him and kept walking. I passed Jin Di Yuan, the supermarket, missed the cafeteria, went past Building Four, and finally stopped at Building Six. Along the way, I kept thinking about my future, only seeing bleakness.

When I reached Building Six, I suddenly felt an ominous chill. The gate was closed and locked with a chain. Through the glass, I could see people wandering inside, but something felt off.

Tang Fei, following my gaze, explained, "Building Six is now used to detain those who bite others. But apparently, people who are delirious are also being thrown in."

"What about the original residents of Building Six?" I asked.

"They’ve been relocated to the school hotel," Tang Fei replied.

I've always been curious, and this time was no exception. I approached the glass door of Building Six to see clearly what was inside, but due to the warm air, a red mist covered the interior, allowing only vague silhouettes moving about. I pressed closer to the glass for a better view.

Suddenly, a face appeared right in front of me, scaring me to the ground. My heart raced. The face was so flushed, the eyes bulging, the mouth opening and closing, saliva dripping down to the glass door.

I took a deep breath, calming myself. I thought it was a ghost, but its appearance was almost ghostly enough. I intended to turn and leave, but the face remained plastered against the glass, staring straight at me without moving. It began pounding on the glass.

By this time, my heartbeat had returned to normal. I leaned half against the glass door and said to the person inside, "I know you want to get out, but what you did before was wrong, why bite people? If you hadn't bitten anyone, you wouldn't be locked up here. If you weren't locked up here, you wouldn't have the idea of wanting to get out. If you didn't have the idea of getting out, you wouldn't try to break the glass. Wukong, I know you miss your master, but doing this is wrong." I wanted to continue lecturing him, but suddenly more figures moved towards the door. More faces appeared on the glass, their appearances nearly identical, all staring straight at me, pounding on the glass. I felt uneasy under their gazes.

Everyone inside had gone mad. It was best for me to leave quickly. Tang Fei, who had been chatting nonstop, was clearly scared too, and followed me away from Building Six.

After walking a few steps, I stopped and asked Tang Fei, "Are you sure all those who are delirious and have a tendency to bite people are detained inside?"

Tang Fei wasn't entirely sure, saying, "They should be."

"Then look at the people passing by us."

Earlier, I hadn't noticed anything unusual while pondering my future, but now I noticed something amiss. Among the people passing by, a couple always looked lifeless, stepping forward like empty shells. Some walked with exaggerated sways, drawing my attention. The surrounding pedestrians seemed numb, paying no attention to the odd ones.

Tang Fei noticed too, unease evident on his face. I told Tang Fei, "I think we should head back to the dormitory—it's safer there." Tang Fei immediately agreed, and the two of us sprinted back like wild horses.

The dormitory was like a utopia, untouched by the chaos outside. Cheng Wei and Huang Hua Dong were still on the phone. Breathing heavily, I adjusted myself and fell asleep immediately. My restless mind replayed the scenes from Building Six—their eyes were unsettling, devoid of human characteristics. Moreover, they didn't exhale any visible breath when opening their mouths. How come the glass didn't fog up? Could it be... No, it must be my imagination. They were clearly still moving, they must be alive. Whatever, I'll sleep first.

A scream pierced the quiet night sky, accompanied by beast-like growls. Awoken by these sudden sounds, I saw the dorm lights were on, and Huang Hua Dong and Cheng Wei were getting dressed, clearly wanting to investigate what had happened.

Judging by the time, it should have been morning near midnight, but outside it was still dark. We had completely lost track of time. The three of us rushed to the bottom of the dormitory building, where chaos reigned. More specifically, three pale-faced individuals were chasing a large group of people, and at the entrance, the dorm supervisor lay in a pool of blood. Two figures were tearing at her, chunks of flesh being bitten off, blood spurting from the wounds, instantly staining the glass doors red.

Seeing this scene, I was shocked. Were they cannibals? Or perhaps, were they zombies from the movies? Tang Fei and the others were also caught in the chaos. A zombie relentlessly chased Tang Fei, cornering him. In a burst of inspiration, Tang Fei smashed a nearby fire window, grabbing a fire extinguisher and hitting the zombie hard from behind. The zombie's head deformed and it collapsed, further confirming my suspicions—it didn't bleed at all.

Not far away, two other zombies tackled two students, the result predictable. Those two zombies mercilessly bit down like wild animals. Surrounding classmates merely avoided them from afar, completely unwilling to intervene.

An idea flashed through my mind—if zombies attacked me, I would likely become their prey. I needed to find a strong person nearby, someone capable of handling these zombies. Of the people I knew, Xiao Qin was the best choice. She should be at Linjiang Garden, a bit far from here. Given the situation here, it was likely similar elsewhere. Alone, I couldn't make it there, and besides, two zombies were eating the deceased dorm supervisor at the entrance, blocking the way. I needed a plan to get out.

I shouted to Tang Fei and Wan Fa Xian nearby, "Quick, take out the fire hydrant and use the powerful water pressure to destroy these zombies. Otherwise, none of us will survive."

Fang Xiao Chun also came to help. The two held the fire hydrant while another spun the switch, and a powerful jet of water shot out. However, the recoil force was immense, and the two couldn't control it at all. The hose twisted uncontrollably, making it impossible to aim accurately.

Eventually, classmates hiding far away came to help. With a large group effort, the hose was finally controlled, and the powerful water stream sprayed like a dragon towards the surrounding zombies. Upon contact with the water, the zombies were pushed out of the entrance. The obstruction was cleared momentarily, but soon chaos erupted again. Everyone fled for their lives. Without anyone controlling the hose, the powerful recoil threw Wan Fa Xian and Fang Xiao Chun to the side. Tang Fei had no choice but to shut off the switch and stop the water flow.

Originally, there were five zombies at the entrance. Two were pushed outside by the water pressure, one fell to the ground, and two rushed into the crowd, killing two more people. Wan Fa Xian and Fang Xiao Chun appeared seriously injured. Huang Hua Dong and Cheng Wei escaped far away. The two zombies blocked our path, leaving only Tang Fei relatively close to me. I grabbed Tang Fei to run outside, but he pulled me back, asking fearfully, "It doesn't seem safe outside. Aren't we just heading out to die?"

With firm resolve, I replied, "Staying here is certain death. Follow me, it's the right choice." Without further argument, I dragged him and ran. Initially reluctant, he eventually followed me.

Outside, the lighting was dim. We could see two zombies pushed by the water pressure pinned against the wall of the opposite dormitory building. The streets were chaotic, with zombies chasing and biting people everywhere. However, a few zombies still wandered aimlessly without actively attacking the fleeing humans. With several loud explosions, some cars on the roadside blew up. Thick black smoke slowly rose, and the massive flames illuminated everything around. Bloodstains and corpses were everywhere. We avoided places with large crowds or where many people were fleeing, avoiding being chased by zombies. We passed Teaching Building Four, with only one destination in mind: Linjiang Garden.

Just as we exited Teaching Building Four, we felt a gust of wind, and something fell from above, colliding strongly with the ground. Turning my head, I saw a scene I would never forget—a corpse had turned into a pile of minced meat, limbs completely twisted, blood flowing incessantly.

Looking up, I saw a classroom window wide open, a student lying by the window, another, seemingly a teacher-turned-zombie, ripping apart the corpse with large bites. Glass shards kept falling. I felt something warm on my face, wiping it with my hand, I realized the dark red liquid could only be blood.

I knew my time was running out. This place would soon become a land of death. Linjiang Garden was about a street away, and we would have to pass Building Six, likely facing a group of zombies. Regardless, I decided to check it out since it was the nearest route.

What happened to the world? How did such a drastic change occur so suddenly? This didn't seem like a biochemical infection. What was the cause? Humanity's world seemed to be ending, but everything was inexplicable. I urgently needed someone to answer all my questions, but who could possibly know?

Sure enough, the street leading to Building Six was occupied by zombies. At the other end of the street was Linjiang Garden. I had a strong premonition that if I found Xiao Qin, I wouldn't have to fear these zombies anymore. Thinking about it made me laugh at myself—a grown man needing protection from a little girl. Never mind, the world had turned upside down. Preserving my life was paramount.

Just as I was at a loss, an unexpected event occurred. A sound resembling a wolf's howl echoed from somewhere near Buildings Five and Six. Strangely, upon hearing this sound, the zombies all moved towards it. Only a few remained, wandering aimlessly like headless flies. The difficulty had significantly decreased.

Tang Fei patted my shoulder, pointing in a direction. I saw a tricycle. This was our chance to break through the street. No need for words—let's go!
