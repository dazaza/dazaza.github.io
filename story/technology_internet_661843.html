This surprised and delighted his parents and grandparents, who praised him, saying, "What a smart and lively child!"

Therefore, under the approving eyes of his parents and grandparents, the little boy could freely wear his emotional clothing and experience different feelings every day.

The little boy grew quickly. The clothes his mother bought for him would soon become too small, but the little boy's "emotional clothes" were magical —— they always fit perfectly, never deforming and always standing out prominently!

You're a boy, and in the future, you should become a man like your father! So, starting now, throw away your fear. That way, you'll be a real man!"

"To be a man like my father!" The little boy repeated it in his heart.

"Yes! I want to be a man like my father!" The little boy firmly nodded to his mother and everyone else. As to why becoming "a man like my father" required him to get rid of his fear, the little boy didn't think much about it.

What the little boy thought about was changing himself according to others' approval standards.

The little boy began trying to take off his fear, but strangely, no matter how hard he tried, he couldn't get it off. What should he do? The little boy blinked his eyes pitifully and looked at his mother, who was also at a loss.

At this moment, the family's most authoritative and wisest figure, his father, came over. He looked down at the little boy and said, "Son! Dad will give you some strength. Wear it over your fear, and then others won't see your fear!"

So, the little boy struggled to put the strength his father gave him over his fear, but it still seemed very difficult, and he couldn't manage to put it on. To help the child wear the strength, the parents and grandparents all came together to help him. After spending a lot of time, the little boy finally managed to wear the strength over his fear.

l EQ: Mastering the Critical 6-Second Time Lag

Emotions are active biological-physiological-chemical reactions of the brain to external stimuli.

Emotions are chemical substances produced by the limbic system in our brain. The limbic system continuously generates various emotions based on the external stimuli we receive, rapidly and automatically. Thus, emotions are natural biological-physiological-chemical reactions, and their occurrence is unavoidable. Using functional identification experiments and MRI scans, scientists can show that emotions are constantly working in the brain.

More crucially, the reaction speed of the limbic system is astonishingly fast, 80,000 times faster than the reaction speed of our "logical thinking center" (cerebral cortex). Therefore, at the moment emotions arise, they are completely beyond the control of the cerebral cortex responsible for logical thinking. This helps us understand why we cannot fully discard emotions and why the little boy couldn't completely take off his fear.

Although the generation of emotions is beyond our control, the information and value they possess can become powerful assistants in helping us think and act. However, this requires patience after emotions arise, waiting approximately six seconds —— because only after about six seconds can the limbic system complete the process of transmitting emotional information —— passing the generated emotional information to the cerebral cortex. At this point, these two important parts of the brain truly connect.

In other words, before six seconds, our emotions cannot be influenced by "reason." Any reaction during this time is due to innate instincts —— purely "emotional" reactions. After six seconds, our emotions and thoughts can communicate and integrate information to complete "high EQ" decision-making and actions.

Six seconds —— this time point is determined by our human physiological system.

Now, we finally understand why experienced people often advise: "Slow down! Don't rush into abandoning actions. Spend some time considering whether it's appropriate to act."

l EQ: Grasping the Influence of Emotions

The speed at which emotions arise, we have already understood, and this characteristic forms the first major feature of emotions —— primacy. The primacy of emotions means: emotions not only arise quickly but also last the longest, affecting us fastest and strongest. We can understand this with simple examples:

A company interviews an unfamiliar job applicant. We notice that even before he opens his mouth, our emotions have already been triggered by his appearance and demeanor, which is what we commonly refer to as "first impression." "Impression" instantly records a lot of information —— his physique, looks, tone of speech, behavior, etc.

If the emotions this person brings us are intense, his information will be tightly recorded due to the influence of emotions and more quickly retained in the brain. After interviewing many applicants, when we review the files, we can recall the feeling we had during this interview —— he made me feel very happy, so we say: "I have a deep impression of this person. His behavior was very proper. His speech was logical. His responses to challenging questions were clever..." All the information recorded by emotions vividly reappears.

Conversely, if the emotions brought by the applicant are mild and weak, we may forget about him almost immediately after he turns around. When reviewing the files, we can hardly remember interviewing this person.

Regarding the longevity of emotions, there is another example. Try recalling someone you interacted with many years ago, such as one of your elementary school teachers. You might have forgotten their name, the specific words they said, or the specific things they did, but you can easily recall the "feeling" you had towards them —— they were a teacher you disliked: likely because they offended you frequently, making you angry; they were a kind teacher: likely because they were willing to help you, making you feel pleasant and trustworthy.

"They may forget what you said, but they will never forget how you made them feel."

— Carl W. Buechner

The Story of the Man

Why do suppressed fear and loneliness expand?

What can different emotions bring us?

On a summer night, a bright white moonlight flooded the bedside, sweet floral scents wafted in the air, and cicadas chirped melodiously, creating a serene ambiance. Mom and Dad were probably already asleep, and under normal circumstances, the little boy would have been wandering in dreamland by now.

However, this summer night, the little boy was wide awake.

Ever since the little boy put on those "good" feelings given by his father, he often stayed awake in such nights. He would quietly sit on the bed, gazing at the moon, enjoying the symphony composed by nocturnal insects. The night was filled with so many rich elements, evoking many subtle feelings —— those rich feelings.

At this moment, the little boy would secretly lift the layers of strength and anger covering his fear and loneliness, sneakily checking on the hidden feelings, touching them with his hands, and then covering them again with strength and anger. However, each time after uncovering, the little boy had to spend longer covering his fear and loneliness because they were quietly growing. With one hand pulling here and the other there, it took him a long time to cover them completely without any trace. Mom and Dad knew nothing about it.

Have you ever, in solitude, secretly revisited the emotions you've concealed?

Now, we must specifically discuss this summer night's story.

That night, the little boy wanted to sneakily lift the "good" feelings again to check on the fear and loneliness beneath, and touch them. As usual, he lifted layer after layer of strength and anger. When he lifted the last layer, the little boy was suddenly shocked by what he saw!

What happened? How did it turn into this?!

The little boy saw two inflated "balloons"! Rubbing his eyes and examining closely, he realized these were the two feelings —— fear and loneliness! Though they weren't balloons, they looked like balloons filled with air, round and puffed up. Moreover, the little boy could hear the rumbling sounds inside the "balloons," and fear and loneliness continued to swell outward with these sounds! Originally, after lifting the strength and anger, the "balloons" expanded due to losing their constraints during this relaxation opportunity. It seemed they were very reluctant to be covered, and now they were ready to burst forth!

Suddenly, the little boy panicked and hurriedly rushed out of the small bedroom straight to his parents' room.

Mom woke up first, startled by the sight of two round objects that resembled balloons but weren't quite balloons. She immediately sat up, frightened, but upon closer inspection, she realized these were the greatly swollen fear and loneliness behind her son's terrified face. Her son was frantically calling out: "Mom! Dad! Help me quick!"

Dad woke up too. Calm as he usually was, he was also stunned by the "big balloons" in front of him. However, he quickly regained composure and immediately took out more strength and anger to give to his son. Together with Mom, they helped the son cover these feelings with the "big balloons." It was a tough process. Finally, they managed to cover the enormous fear and loneliness! The thick layers of strength and anger covering the fear and loneliness were stretched taut, making the little boy look especially robust!

"Great! Now you look even stronger!" Dad happily looked at his "strong" son who was slightly wobbling. The little boy struggled to maintain his balance and asked, "Dad, do I look like a strong man now?"

"Yes! You are already a strong man!" Dad affirmed, "My strong son, from now on, you need to learn to seek out strength and anger yourself because you are an adult now and should not rely on Mom and Dad anymore. Starting now, you need to learn to isolate your emotions and not be disturbed by them!"

The little boy, indeed, was no longer a little boy. He had been defined as an adult by his dad, so we need to change our mindset. We should call him a man! Alright, friends, we all remember, our protagonist is now called a man.

The nearly comprehending man listened to his dad's words and nodded. At that moment, the swelling fear quietly slipped out a little, causing the man to hastily pull the layers of strength to cover the escaping fear and then repeatedly nodded to his dad.

The little boy learned that if he wanted to grow up —— we all must become well-trained emotional actors.

The man truly stopped asking his dad for help. In fact, the concealed feelings kept expanding, but our "strong" man had learned to control and isolate them! The more they expanded, the stronger the man appeared.
