A delicate move, harvest a lot of praise - is he the most loving traffic controller in Hangzhou?

by malayo on 2012-02-25 11:18:48

A few days ago, a student's parent posted on the 19th Floor Forum with a set of pictures, praising a traffic assistant at the school gate. The pictures were very simple, just like the protagonist in the pictures - a traffic assistant who braved the rain to open the car door for each child and patiently escorted them.

These pictures moved many netizens. In this drizzly early spring, these scenes in the rain were very warm.

Parents said that this traffic assistant uncle had been helping children open the car doors for three or five years, regardless of wind and rain. Could he be considered the most loving traffic assistant in Hangzhou?

With him, we feel reassured.

Let's first take a look at how the thoughtful parent "Xishan Qingxue" described this uncle.

"There was a sense of warmth when I sent my son to school in the morning. He always smiled and opened the car door for my child, escorting him into the campus. One day, because I had to carry the interest class equipment, I got out of the car with my child and paid special attention. I found that the auxiliary police (the correct title should be 'traffic assistant', as noted by the reporter, same below) did not only help my child but all the students. Finally, let me say one thing, thank you, the 'awesome' auxiliary police of Changjiang, you have brought safety to the children and peace of mind to us."

Once the post was made, it received a lot of replies. Parent "Murong Xianxian" said that her daughter studied at Changjiang Experimental School for six years and really missed that auxiliary police officer. "My daughter is now in the first year of junior high school, no longer at Changjiang, but she still often mentions this auxiliary police uncle. When she was in the sixth grade, she didn't let me send her to the school gate anymore, saying that the auxiliary police uncle was too tired and didn't want to trouble him to open the car door for her, so she got off the car twenty or thirty meters away from the school."

Another parent said: "I see this auxiliary police every day when I send my child to school, regardless of wind and rain. Once, my son forgot his schoolbag when he got out of the car, but luckily this auxiliary police reminded him. Hats off to him!"

Parent "2011zq" said: "Since my daughter started the first grade, it has been my responsibility to send her to school. The road at the school gate is very narrow, a one-way street. Every time I reach the school intersection, there are crowds of people and cars sending their children to school, but even during peak hours, the vehicles going in and out of the school gate are still orderly. This is because there is an auxiliary police uncle maintaining traffic at the school gate. I almost see him every day; he smiles every day and maintains order at the school gate together with the duty teacher. Sometimes he asks the crowd sending their children to school to stop, sometimes he asks the passing vehicles to stop, sometimes he helps parents open the car door, sometimes he directs students to cross the road. Everything is very orderly, both people and vehicles pass quickly. And he, busy as ever, takes great pleasure in it. Although I see him almost every day picking up and dropping off my child, occasionally we greet each other. Although I only know his surname Mao, his kind and lovable image is deeply etched in our hearts. My daughter always asks me to park at the school gate. She says that Uncle Mao opens the car door for her every day, giving her a great sense of security! Every day, he helps so many children, opens so many car doors. On rainy days, it is him who holds the umbrella for the children. Whenever I see this scene, I am always moved, I can't explain why, but it is just a feeling of being touched. From the first grade of elementary school to the fifth grade now, I have seen such scenes often, but unfortunately, I haven't recorded any of them with a camera. Every day, I pass by in a hurry, we rarely talk, but I always express my gratitude to him and say thank you. My mood for the whole day is also good. Actually, what he does are ordinary and common things for his position, without any earth-shattering achievements, even thinking these are insignificant little things. But with him here, I feel reassured."

One parent's reply was the most interesting, but it exactly shows how "awesome" this traffic assistant is: This auxiliary police uncle is the best at recognizing every vehicle and knowing how many people are in each vehicle. The absolute best is, he knows which child has entered the school gate, and then the school can close the gate.

Helping children open the car door is for their safety

With a sense of emotion and curiosity, at 1:30 PM yesterday afternoon, the reporter went to the main gate of Changjiang Experimental Primary School on Zhi Jietan Temple Alley.

From afar, I saw a figure in deep blue uniform with a fluorescent vest directing vehicles to stop at the school gate. At this time, there weren't many cars, most driving into the Radisson Hotel entrance.

"That's Auxiliary Police Officer Mao!" said the school security guard.

Mao's real name is Mao Dongjie, a local Hangzhou person, 48 years old, a traffic assistant for the Third Detachment of the Xiacheng District Traffic Police Brigade. He is not tall, cool and a bit serious when directing traffic, but when chatting, his voice is loud, very cheerful and enthusiastic, very talkative and loves to laugh.

Yesterday was Friday, the school would dismiss earlier than usual, and the first batch of children could go home around two o'clock.

"I'll be here at 1:30 PM. Parents will start coming to pick up their children around two o'clock, until about four thirty," said Mao Dongjie. Once the work here slows down a bit, he needs to go to the booth on Yan'an Road to direct traffic and assist in maintaining order during the evening rush hour.

Zhi Jietan Temple Alley is a one-way street running from south to north. There are two schools nearby, Anji Road Experimental School and Changjiang Experimental Primary School, and two hotels, Radisson Plaza Hotel and Zhejiang Grand Hotel. Vehicles picking up and dropping off children, vehicles entering and exiting the hotel, plus nearby metro construction, make this complex alley the focus of Mao Dongjie's work.

Changjiang Experimental Primary School stipulates that students must arrive at school before 8:10 AM every day. Each Monday to Friday morning from 7:15 AM to 8:30 AM is Mao Dongjie's busiest time.

Why did he think of opening the car doors for the children?

Mao Dongjie said: "I remember it was in 2008, one morning, I was on duty at the school gate. Nearby, there was a vegetable market where someone was riding an electric bike delivering eggs to a nearby restaurant. A car stopped at the school gate, the child didn't notice, opened the car door and hit the electric bike next to it, scattering eggs everywhere. After that incident, I started opening the car door for the children. There are a lot of cars here, and a lot of electric bikes, helping them open the door can keep the children safer and allow the cars to pass faster."

Mr. Mao said that he started working as an auxiliary police officer in 2007. He used to work in a factory, but later the factory closed down.

When asked if he had calculated how many car doors he had opened for children? Is it tiring?

"Never counted. Not tiring, very easy." He smiled.
