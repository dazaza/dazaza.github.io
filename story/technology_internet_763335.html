Amazon Posts $7M Loss in Q2, Shares Hit a Record High

by anonymous on 2013-07-30 11:49:21

The quarterly report issued by Amazon, the world’s largest online retailer on July 25th, showed that the company unexpectedly incurred a loss of 7 million US dollars in the second quarter, mainly affected by its aggressive investment and expansion plan. However, the underwhelming financial results did not affect investors' optimistic sentiment. On Friday last week (July 26), Amazon's stock price rose to a record high.

Performance was outstanding in the North American market

Based in Seattle, Amazon's total revenue increased from 12.8 billion US dollars last year to 15.7 billion US dollars in the second quarter, up 22%, in line with external expectations. However, surprisingly, the company recorded a loss of 7 million US dollars this quarter, while the market had expected it to make a profit of 28.8 million US dollars. The reporter for the Economic Information Daily noted that Amazon achieved a net profit of 82 million US dollars in the first quarter of this year.

By region, although Amazon's international business performance was weak, its U.S. market grew rapidly with good earnings. Sales in the North American region rose 30% to 9.5 billion US dollars, while operating income increased from 344 million US dollars last year to 409 million US dollars, up 18.9%.

"They look very solid in their biggest market," said Wells Fargo analyst Matt Nemer.

Amazon CFO Tom Szkutak said that strong sales of clothing and consumer goods have attracted more shoppers to the site. At the same time, Szkutak also stated that Amazon's membership program, Amazon Prime, is growing rapidly, with members typically spending 3-4 times more than non-members. It is understood that members of this project pay an annual fee of 79 US dollars per year to enjoy free shipping within two days across the United States.

On Friday last week (July 26), Amazon's stock price rose 2.8% to 312.01 US dollars, hitting an intraday high of 313.62 US dollars, the highest historical price, with a cumulative increase of 24% this year. Currently, the company's stock valuation is already one of the highest among peers, showing that investors still have confidence in Amazon.

Cloud services become the fastest-growing business

Despite excellent performance in the U.S. market, surging expenses still caused Amazon to post a loss in the second quarter.

The financial report showed that Amazon's capital expenditures increased to 1.526 billion US dollars in the second quarter, up 127.8% quarter-on-quarter, while operating expenses also rose by 23%. Amazon explained that the expenses were used for investment and construction of digital media businesses complementary to Kindle devices.

In order to transform from an online retail company into a technology company with a wider range of services, the Economic Information Daily reporter noted that Amazon has been active recently and expanding aggressively.

The company continues to invest in cloud infrastructure technology to add to "Amazon Web Services" (AWS). This year, Amazon is actively seeking a contract with the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) of the United States. It is understood that the CIA will collaborate with an internet giant to invest in cloud computing to collect intelligence data from around the world.

This quarterly report shows that Amazon's cloud service AWS is one of the company's fastest-growing businesses. Cloud services are classified in the "Other" section of the report, where revenue surged 61% to 892 million US dollars. Although the specific revenue situation of AWS is unknown, Wall Street analysts generally believe that this part of the business has a higher gross margin than Amazon's main retail business.

Michael Graham, an analyst at Canadian investment bank Canaccord Genuity, said that Amazon's gross margin in the second quarter reached 28.6%, up 250 basis points year-on-year, the highest value in the company's history.

In addition to cloud computing, Amazon has also heavily invested in its cargo transportation, warehousing, and delivery services this year. In order to keep up with skyrocketing sales and to complement Amazon Prime's two-day express service, Amazon is "crazily" adding warehouses in the U.S. Earlier this year, Amazon announced that it would invest 300 million US dollars in Florida over the next three years, bringing more than 3,000 jobs.

On Monday this week (July 29), Amazon again announced that it would add 5,000 jobs in 17 states across the U.S., to expand the capacity of its cargo transportation network and improve delivery efficiency.
