Tourists Win Big Prize in Lijiang Scenic Spot Tickets but Hard to Claim 200,000 Yuan Bonus Without Ticket Stubs

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-04 17:55:12

"Mr. Hui, your real-name ticket from a trip to Lijiang Ancient Town in November last year has won a grand prize of 200,000 yuan! Please bring the stub of the ticket to Lijiang before March 6th to claim your prize!" On February 16th, Mr. Hui Jinbie, who works in Chen仓 District of Baoji City, received such a call. After multiple verifications, it turned out to be true. However, "the tour guide didn't even give me the ticket," as the deadline for claiming the prize approached, Mr. Hui became extremely anxious.

The Tourist: Learned About the Prize Only After Receiving the Call

"At first, I didn't believe it, but after contacting the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau, I found out that it was indeed true! But they told me that I must have the stub of the ancient city maintenance fee ticket to claim the prize, which left me stunned: I had never known about the maintenance fee tickets or the lottery, and the tour guide hadn't given us the stubs."

According to Mr. Hui's introduction, on November 3rd of last year, he participated in a tour organized by a travel agency in Baoji to visit Kunming, Dali, Lijiang, and other places. The contract clearly stated: the tour fee included the entrance fees for scenic spots, ancient city maintenance fees, etc. On November 6th, after arriving in Lijiang, the local "Sunshine Holiday Travel Agency" took over and was fully responsible for the Lijiang itinerary.

"I was part of a tour group, so I didn't pay much attention to the ticket matters, all organized by the tour guide. Since we didn't know there was a lottery, we didn't ask the guide for the tickets either," said Mr. Hui Jinbie.

When contacted, several other tourists from the same tour group also said they hadn't seen the ticket stubs.

Voucher: Both Ticket and ID Are Necessary

On February 27th, Mr. Hui Jinbie once again set off for Lijiang. Liu Zheng'an, the guide who was responsible for the tour in Lijiang, claimed that all tickets had been handed over to the tourists. The travel agency he worked for also stated that they would never withhold tourists' tickets. Mr. Hui came to the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau and found his winning ticket stub in their records, but the staff informed him that both the purchase ticket and personal ID were required to claim the prize, neither of which could be missing.

The person in charge of the travel agency in Baoji said, "After arriving in Lijiang, the tour group was handed over to the local travel agency, and it no longer had anything to do with us. Since Mr. Hui signed up with our agency, out of consideration, I personally accompanied him to Lijiang to verify this matter and hope to resolve it as soon as possible."

Mr. Hui Jinbie said, "Winning the prize should have been a happy thing, but now I feel extremely disheartened. I can't understand why, since the tickets are under a real-name system and the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau called to notify me about claiming the prize, I hope relevant departments can understand my actual situation."

Prize Collection: Out of 10 Winners, Only 3 Have Claimed Their Prizes

On March 2nd, when contacted, tour guide Liu Zheng'an also expressed his grievances, "The tickets are under a real-name system. After verifying, I let the tourists take back their tickets. Keeping those tickets myself wouldn't serve any purpose."

He Shiyong, director of the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau, said, "The event of paying the Lijiang Ancient Town maintenance fee giving you a chance to win a big prize has been reported on the official website and major media outlets in Yunnan. The rules for claiming prizes are also very clear. Since Mr. Hui does not meet the conditions for claiming the prize, we cannot process his request. Currently, both the travel agency and the tourists insist they did not take possession of this winning ticket, which is an issue between the travel agency and the tourists, something we cannot intervene in."

It has been reported that Lijiang was the first in China to conduct regular lotteries using ancient city maintenance fee receipts, a move aimed at minimizing tourists evading the maintenance fee. It is understood that 10 lucky tourists were drawn nationwide, and currently only 3 have claimed their prizes, with several others also saying they couldn't find their ticket stubs.

Lawyer: Can Seek Legal Assistance

Fang Ligang, a lawyer from Shaanxi Ligang Law Firm, said that first, after paying the corresponding travel fees, tourists should receive the corresponding tourism services, including the right to obtain tickets, and the travel company should provide evidence of delivering the tickets; secondly, since the tickets are under a real-name system, and the staff also called to notify the winner to claim the prize, this proves that Mr. Hui's winning information is genuine. "Within the valid redemption period, if legal procedures are followed, the possibility of obtaining the prize money is still quite high."

Note for Lijiang Tours: In January 2012, Lijiang decided that henceforth, lottery events for ancient city maintenance fee receipts will be held every January 6th and July 6th. On January 6th, the organizers of the Lijiang Ancient Town Maintenance Fee Receipt Lottery drew 10 winners from 1,991,584 receipt numbers. Each winner receives 200,000 yuan in cash, with the deadline for claiming the prize being March 6th.
