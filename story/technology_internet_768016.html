iPhone Accounts for 34% of Japan's September Mobile Phone Sales

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 16:50:04

According to estimates from market research company Counterpoint Research, of the 2.8 million phones sold in Japan in September this year, Apple's iPhone accounted for 34%, significantly higher than the 14% in July and August.

Counterpoint executive Neil Shah stated that Japan sold nearly 500,000 iPhone 5s units in just a few days, while Apple's total sales of the iPhone 5s that month were less than one million units. The reasons include the wide acceptance of the iPhone 5 in Japan, as well as contributions from NTT DoCoMo, the mobile communications arm of NTT with its 60 million users in Japan.

In discussing the market performance of the iPhone, Counterpoint executive Tom Kang pointed out, "In the past decade, no mobile phone brand in Japan has crossed the 30% market share threshold. Apple has set a record."

Tom Kang further added, "Japan has always been a fiercely competitive market, with over 10 mobile phone brands and three major mobile operators. It is almost impossible for any single brand to capture one-third of the market share. Even Sharp, which dominated the Japanese mobile phone market for nearly five years, only held 26% of the Japanese market at its peak."

In the last seven quarters, Apple's revenue growth in the Japanese market has been rapid, sometimes much faster than other companies. In the third fiscal quarter ended June this year, Apple's revenue in Japan was $2.5 billion (7% of its total revenue), an increase of 26.6% compared to the same period last year, while other companies' revenue in Japan declined by 1%.

According to Localytics estimates, Japan is the most popular market for the iPhone 5c and 5s, with nearly 10% of active iPhones being the iPhone 5c and 5s. The second most popular market for the iPhone 5c and 5s is the United States, where 6.4% of active iPhones are the iPhone 5c and 5s.

Counterpoint also estimated that in September this year, Apple's share of the U.S. smartphone market rose from 17% to 39%, more than doubling.
