Ready to Challenge the Previous High, Two Sectors Are Favored ＿ Stock

by fj0953210 on 2009-12-05 21:57:44

[Hotspot Focus and Market Opportunities] After three consecutive trading days of rebound, the Shenzhen and Shanghai stock indices adjusted with volatility on Thursday due to the divergence in individual stock performance. The Shenzhen and Shanghai indices fell slightly by 0.29% and 0.16%, respectively, both closing with negative cross stars and long lower shadows. Despite the index correction, the overall market operation remained relatively stable, and filling the previous day's gap would help resolve the market's bullish and bearish differences. After a short-term adjustment, it is not impossible that the market may accumulate strength again to challenge the previous high resistance.

From the perspective of Thursday's market hotspots, two highlights are worth noting. First, the GEM (Growth Enterprise Market) continued to perform strongly collectively as it had been active recently. In fact, the recent strength of the GEM has played a significant role in maintaining market sentiment. The reason why the GEM is favored by the market lies first in its alignment with the current market enthusiasm for small and medium-sized market capitalization stocks. Among the currently listed 28 GEM companies, most have a circulating market value below 1 billion yuan, with only a few exceeding this threshold, such as Jinya Technology (300028, 1.028 billion), Huayi Brothers (300027, 2.332 billion), Jifeng Agricultural Machinery (300022, 1.611 billion), Lepu Medical (300003, 2.053 billion), Hongri Pharmaceutical (300026, 1.121 billion), Robot (300024, 1.040 billion), Aier Ophthalmology (300015, 1.597 billion), Teradyne (300001, 1.348 billion), and ShenZhou TaiYue (300002, 3.433 billion).

Secondly, GEM companies generally possess high growth characteristics. According to past experience, there is often a year-end trend in the market to explore annual report topics, and stocks with high dividend and high distribution themes tend to receive significant attention from major funds. Many GEM companies have such expectations.

Thirdly, GEM companies have certain scarcity characteristics. As a new phenomenon domestically, the GEM lacks good valuation references. In the current market environment where small-cap stocks are popular, they will inevitably become the focus of major fund operations, especially speculative attacks by short-term investors. Statistics show that the average dynamic P/E ratio of the 28 GEM companies is 92.57 times. Although the market was previously concerned about the high issuance price of the GEM giving its high growth feature a high premium, the significant increase in performance in the first three quarters of this year should lead to a balanced view of its current high P/E ratio. Additionally, the second batch of GEM companies includes 23 companies going public. Among them, Harbin Jiuzhou Electric, Hubei Huitian Adhesive Industry, Beijing Maitaino Communication Technology, Shenzhen Xinzhuobang Technology, Guangzhou Yangpu Medical Technology, Beijing SuperMap Software, Hunan Zhongke Electric, Jiangsu HuaSheng Tianlong Photoelectric, Beijing Gangyan High-Na Technology, Jinlong Micro-Electronics, Zhejiang Hexin Tonghuashun, Wuxi Baotong Belt Industry, Julit Sling Tools, etc., have successfully passed their initial public offerings. The market expects that the second batch of GEM companies will start listing this month, which has drawn market attention to the GEM.

However, it is worth noting that after a sustained rise in the recent stage, the GEM has seen a large cumulative short-term increase. Moreover, the high growth of the GEM also hides high risks, so investors need to avoid blindly chasing highs and over-positioning during the operation process.

In addition to the GEM, the agricultural sector became another highlight of the market on Thursday, with XinNong Development, DunHuang Seed Industry, DengHai Seed Industry, and FengLe Seed Industry all strongly closing at the limit up. I believe that the strength of the agricultural sector mainly comes down to the following three factors: First, the agricultural sector has been a significantly lagging sector in this year's market, with catch-up potential. Second, under the background of gradually rising inflation, there will be further room for future agricultural product prices to rise. Third, the impact of earlier disasters in the north led to reduced seed production and inventory decline, improving the supply-demand relationship while some quality varieties began to see price increases. Therefore, in terms of operations, investors are advised to pay close attention to two types of individual stocks in the agricultural sector: one being sub-sector listed companies with price elasticity and sensitivity, such as Nanning Sugar Industry, Gui Sugar Shares, XinNong Development, XinSai Shares; the other being seed industry companies indirectly benefiting from the rise in agricultural product prices, such as DengHai Seed Industry, DunHuang Seed Industry, LongPing Hi-Tech, etc.

It should be noted that in the process of the market accumulating strength to break through, investors should closely monitor the direction of market hotspots, such as whether the low-carbon sector can successfully transition and rotate orderly, as well as the activity of blue-chip sectors.

[Risk Warning and Strategy]

According to foreign media reports, Vice Governor Hu Xiaolian of the People's Bank of China said on Wednesday that the current gold price is already at a historical high, and attention must be paid to the price bubble of gold assets. Currently, U.S. gold futures prices have hit a record high. Market analysts pointed out that apart from the weakening dollar, rumors of China increasing its gold reserves again and substantial ETF buying have also contributed to the surge in gold prices. Data shows that due to China's continuous trade surplus and the government's foreign exchange control system, the Chinese government has accumulated huge foreign exchange reserves. By the end of September 2009, China's foreign exchange reserve balance had reached $2.27 trillion, of which approximately 70%, or $1.6 trillion, were dollar reserves.

[Today's News Highlights]

The following information deserves investor attention on Thursday:

1. Vice Chairman Yao Gang of the China Securities Regulatory Commission stated on December 2nd that China's stock market has gradually recovered with the stabilization of the economy, enhancing investor confidence. Institutional professional investors currently hold about 2.25 trillion yuan worth of A-shares, accounting for 17.2% of total circulating market value, an increase. Institutional investors are facing excellent opportunities for further development. A new technological revolution could bring more space for the capital market, and the capital market will play a more active role in "adjusting structure."

2. A report released by the National Information Center on December 3rd suggested that next year China's GDP growth rate is expected to reach around 8.5%, roughly equal to 2009, achieving "dual stability" in economic growth and prices. The possibility of obvious inflation in the short term is unlikely, but the report advises against further intensifying expansionary policies. It suggests that if the global economy continues to recover slowly, China's macroeconomic policy orientation next year should remain basically unchanged, continuing to implement an active fiscal policy and moderately loose monetary policy. The proportion of fiscal deficit to GDP should be maintained at around 3%, the year-end growth rate of broad money supply (M2) should be gradually controlled to around 18%, the scale of new loans should be controlled within 8 trillion yuan, and the scale of corporate financing from the securities market should exceed that of 2009, ensuring a relatively relaxed overall social capital situation.

3. An announcement on Wednesday showed that to maintain steady growth of base money and basic stability of money market interest rates, the central bank will issue the 63rd central bank bill of 2009 on December 3rd (Thursday). This central bank bill has a term of 3 months, with a scale of 700 billion yuan. The amount of expiring funds in the open market this week is 135 billion yuan, and 12 billion yuan worth of 1-year bills and 45 billion yuan worth of 28-day positive repurchase agreements have already been issued on Tuesday this week. It is expected that the open market will continue to see net withdrawals for the eighth consecutive week.

[Today's Market Situation]

After three consecutive trading days of volatile rebound, the Shenzhen and Shanghai stock indices adjusted with volatility on Thursday due to the divergence in individual stock performance. The Shenzhen and Shanghai indices fell slightly by 0.29% and 0.16%, respectively, both closing with negative cross stars and long lower shadows, with a combined turnover of approximately 319.301 billion yuan. From the trend of the entire day's index movement, the Shanghai Composite Index opened at 3268.00 points and then declined due to the drag of gold, non-ferrous metals, banking, and insurance sectors, falling to a low of 3227.77 points near the 20-day moving average, filling the upward gap from the previous day. It slightly rebounded at the close, eventually closing at 3264.63 points. The Shenzhen Component Index correspondingly underwent a slight adjustment, but the overall operation remained relatively stable.

From the perspective of the stock market, the performance of individual stocks showed clear divergence, with the Shenzhen market showing a ratio of rising to falling stocks of 518:348, and the Shanghai market showing a ratio of 508:377. In the list of gainers, 24 stocks including Rainbow Shares, Sinoma Automobile, Chongqing Road & Bridge, Wuhan Water Supply,烽Fire Communication, XinNong Development, HaiMa Shares, and Lemei Pharmaceuticals closed at the limit up. From the sector style, the GEM continued to perform strongly collectively, with an overall increase of 5.99%. Among them, Jinya Technology, Dayu Water Conservation, Beilu Pharmaceutical, Huace Testing, Lemei Pharmaceuticals, and XinNing Logistics closed at the limit up. Secondly, agriculture, automobile, and chemical fiber stocks also performed well.
