A Plain and Romantic Love

This is a story between a boy and a girl. There is no stormy love-hate relationship, only the plain and simple passage of time.

He used to be a boy who didn't understand love, self-centered and not considering others' feelings. One might say he didn't know what true love was. She was a girl who knew how to cherish but was afraid of losing because she had been hurt before and was now afraid to love wholeheartedly.

They met, and they began to discover that their schools were separated by only a wall. They both had scars on the same spot of their left hands. They both enjoyed eating different kinds of delicious food. They both disliked taking taxis. They both liked going to the cinema to watch movies. They would both listen to sad songs at night and suffer from insomnia. Even their physical flaws were the same, much to the boy's surprise.

They started eating together, playing basketball together, singing karaoke together, strolling together, having typing competitions, going to the library to read books together, watching movies together, going home and to school together, eating big lollipops together, taking photos together, and riding a tandem bicycle together.

When it got cold, the boy gave his clothes to the girl. On her birthday, the boy gave her a huge cake. One evening, the boy gave the girl three big surprises and romantic moments, yet they were still not quite lovers.

Finally, one evening while playing shadow stepping games, they confirmed their relationship.

The girl finally waited for the four words the boy said to her.

From that day on, the boy sent a text message to the girl every morning. The content was different every day, always bringing new excitement.

On an ordinary evening, the boy gave the girl 10 roses. The girl felt that with the boy around, even when she looked up at the moon, it seemed full.

The boy updated his social media space every day. When the girl said she didn't fully trust love, he stayed up all night.

The boy and the girl agreed to write daily diaries in their respective notebooks, but the girl didn't stick to it.

During the New Year celebrations, the boy and the girl went out to play. However, the girl's wallet was stolen, and she sat crying on the street corner.

The next day, the boy ran to near the girl's house, setting off beautiful fireworks for her and presenting her with a beautiful gift box containing a fashionable ladies' wallet. At this moment, instead of being moved, the girl appeared numb. The boy returned home, and the girl told him on QQ not to be too good to her. The boy was heartbroken, not understanding why after doing so much, he didn't receive a happy response from the girl. In fact, it was just because the girl was deeply touched by this moment of happiness and worried that such happiness would slip away.

In the days that followed, the boy slowly opened the girl's heart and told her that love is not war; it doesn't need to learn how to win. Love can give everything sincerely.

The girl gradually returned to her most innocent self, returning to the realm of giving 100% to love.

The boy no longer acted as selfishly as he once did. The boy learned how to give and understood that loving someone should be more than loving oneself.

Half a year passed.

The boy no longer showed romance towards the girl like he used to. Their dates became boring.

And as they came to know each other's personalities better, they also argued over small things. The girl thought they weren't as good as they used to be. She felt the boy no longer changed his signature for her or updated his space for her, nor did he give her surprise after surprise.

The girl thought it was another lost luster of love and slowly accepted this kind of plainness.

Suddenly one day, the girl had a big argument with the boy. While she was angry, the boy didn't comfort her, and the girl burst into tears.

"Why don't you give me romance anymore? Those romances of yours were fake, right?"

The boy calmly answered the girl: "In the past, I wasn't a romantic person. I was even a selfish ghost who wouldn't do anything for others, which is why my girlfriends left me. After meeting you, all the romantic things I did were voluntary, not to please you, but because I thought of doing them without extra consideration. Seeing you laugh happily at my romance made me feel satisfied. That's all. Now we understand each other. We are not dating but living life."

"If what you mean by romance is roses and chocolates, then let me ask you:

How many boys can rush from far away to accompany their girlfriend when she says she's hungry, have dinner with her, and then go home?

How many boys, when they have no money left, will empty their wallets to ensure the girl doesn't spend a cent, inviting her to watch a movie and eat Western cuisine, while eating bread themselves when they return home?

How many boys can travel from one end of the city to the other, just to see their girlfriend at her school for a short moment, spending several times more on the journey than the actual meeting?

How many boys can refrain from disturbing their girlfriend when she goes out with friends, even if they have something to do, but will cancel their own plans to accompany her when she wants to go shopping?

How many boys can absolutely ensure that they send their girlfriend home, no matter how late or tired, personally seeing her enter the door before leaving alone...?"

A confident expression appeared on the boy's face, while the girl had already cried herself into tears.
