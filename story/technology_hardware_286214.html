Floating World Edges 2

by fkeiigtd on 2009-11-30 09:31:49

When Jasmine was a young girl, she had a plain face with a delicate nose and glasses that accentuated her refined and elegant demeanor. She exuded the air of a well-bred young lady, which earned her many admirers. However, after her first romance ended in failure, she became more cautious and hesitant to commit to anyone for life. This caution inadvertently caused her to waste several years of her youth. In smaller cities, people marry early, and by the age of twenty-four or twenty-five, those who are still unmarried start to become fewer. It is said that twenty-five is the turning point. Although Jasmine herself didn't feel it, she remained carefree, much to the distress of her aunts and matchmakers. Her mother, on the other hand, constantly reminded her of the urgency of marriage. In moments of frustration, her mother would scold her, saying things like "Why do you keep your daughter at home? At this age, she should already be married." But Jasmine would retort, "Every day I hope you get married soon so I can have peace of mind. Besides, with your appearance and past experiences, how can you still be so picky?"

Despite these words, when Jasmine finally found someone, her mother initially criticized him harshly. The reason being, Jasmine had always been known for her talent and intelligence. Although her looks were not extraordinary, her writing and handling of affairs surpassed even her brother, who studied journalism, and her sister who worked at a newspaper. From a young age, Jasmine showed great leadership skills. She managed all work-related matters independently. Year after year, the factory relied on her to handle various issues since they couldn't depend on their elder son. At the age of twenty-one, when her father was falsely accused and imprisoned, Jasmine and her sick mother silently sought his release. Even when Jasmine herself was detained by the prosecutor's office, she handled the situation calmly and composedly. Afterward, she went out of town to collect payments without any hesitation. Payments that her uncle had failed to recover multiple times were easily collected by Jasmine with just a few words, and she even managed to collect an additional 30,000 yuan. When her uncle suggested finding connections to help them through tough times, Jasmine gently reminded her mother that he had ill intentions. A single encounter revealed his sycophantic nature and exaggerated tales of past glories. Despite her mother's doubts, her uncle used his influence to pressure them into compliance, even going as far as claiming that Jasmine and her mother were ungrateful. Three years later, the truth came out from the prosecutor's office, revealing that her uncle had orchestrated a scheme to take advantage of them, exactly as Jasmine had predicted.

Although Jasmine was adept at handling external affairs, when it came to matters of the heart, she seemed particularly unclear. Her mother noticed that while she appeared to be pursued and frequently went on dates, she never seriously brought anyone home, which infuriated her mother. Her mother accused her of deliberately using these men to evade responsibility. Realizing that she could no longer deceive or avoid the situation, Jasmine resolved to take matchmaking seriously.

Her matchmaker was Aunt Ding, an old colleague from her workplace whom Jasmine respected and liked as if she were her own mother. Although Aunt Ding was disabled, she spoke with great gentleness and always listened with a smile, like an older sister. Additionally, Aunt Ding sang beautifully, often performing duets with Jasmine at karaoke bars, which were quite impressive. Aunt Ding's male voice was powerful and deep, earning Jasmine's admiration. During a meeting break, Jasmine received a call from Aunt Ding introducing her to a friend named Xiao Dong. They planned to meet in the evening and sing songs together.

Jasmine enjoyed such casual introductions where music provided a natural backdrop for conversation. It allowed people to mingle easily without awkwardness. Moreover, Jasmine loved music and singing was one of her strengths. She thought that perhaps her talents and gentle personality were what made her appealing to others. Regardless, being special in others' eyes gave her the power to make choices freely.

This was how Jasmine met Xiao Dong. Their introduction lacked drama but ended with a dramatic and melancholic conclusion.

Upon entering the room, Jasmine found it dimly lit with shadowy figures sitting in the corners. As Aunt Ding and Jasmine entered, most people stood up except for one man who sat motionlessly in the corner near the door. Aunt Ding introduced Jasmine to everyone, stopping at the man who didn't stand and said, "This is Xiao Dong, he works at the police station." Xiao Dong corrected her quietly, "I run my own freight station, godex." Aunt Ding's face momentarily flashed with discomfort, a detail Jasmine would recall much later.

Xiao Dong's cold demeanor surprised and annoyed Jasmine. She thought he must be very successful and arrogant, otherwise why would a man around thirty years old act so haughtily without a girlfriend. In this small county, these excellent men ruled supreme, like emperors selecting concubines, with an unparalleled sense of superiority. Many outstanding women around Jasmine had to settle for less remarkable men and became the supportive wives behind them. Seeing Xiao Dong's attitude, Jasmine felt a silent sorrow for herself. She shook hands with him lightly, feeling no warmth. Xiao Dong barely glanced at her, an attitude that irked Jasmine, who prided herself on her pride, stirring within her a faint desire to conquer him, though she hadn't yet realized it herself.

After seating arrangements, Aunt Ding made sure Jasmine sat next to Xiao Dong. However, Jasmine deliberately distanced herself, sitting at the far end of the sofa. Later, she used the opportunity to select songs and sat beside Aunt Ding. Meanwhile, a man in his forties named Qiang Ge danced with a woman around forty. Jasmine thought it was rare for someone to bring their spouse to a karaoke bar, showing their affection. She watched them intently.

Jasmine didn't choose any songs and sat quietly. Aunt Ding's performance had already won applause. Jasmine noticed that Xiao Dong only politely clapped, seemingly disinterested in the occasion. Thus, Jasmine lost interest in singing too. However, Aunt Ding wouldn't let her off the hook and chose many songs that Jasmine excelled at. When the cheerful prelude of "Eighteen Bends in the Mountain Road" played, everyone looked around in surprise. Qiang Ge said it was a mistake, and the next song should play. Jasmine stood up calmly, smiled at Qiang Ge, and said, "Let me give it a try, throwing a brick to attract jade, hoping not to disturb your listening pleasure." Then she began with a high-pitched note, "Ya... children of the big mountain... love the sun..." After the song ended, there was silence. Jasmine worried about her choice of folk songs in such a setting, thinking it was self-inflicted embarrassment. Just then, thunderous applause erupted. People stared at Jasmine in disbelief, amazed that a star singer was among them. Jasmine glanced around and saw Xiao Dong's eyes sincerely fixed on her. She blushed slightly and returned to her seat.

Another song began, and Xiao Dong approached Jasmine to invite her to dance. She hesitated for a moment before standing up.

Without question, Xiao Dong danced better than Jasmine. While Jasmine could only manage basic steps without stepping on her partner's feet, Xiao Dong skillfully led her through simple moves without showing off unnecessarily. Jasmine appreciated his consideration. Xiao Dong complimented her singing, expressing genuine admiration, which erased Jasmine's previous displeasure. He explained that he had undergone vocal cord surgery, so he avoided talking and singing. Jasmine responded, "It's fine; you don't have to talk. I don't enjoy making small talk either."

Later, they parted ways without exchanging contact information. That night, Jasmine's mother asked about the matchmaking result. Jasmine replied, "His job is great, surely to your liking. He runs a freight station outside, quite accomplished. He's a capable young man and quiet and steady. But we only exchanged two sentences today, so it probably won't go further." Her mother teased her, "You've always been unbeatable in love. What kind of man has defeated my daughter? I'd like to see him. You've always liked mature and responsible men, why didn't you give it your best shot this time? I still have confidence in my daughter. If you're so capable, reel him back in." Jasmine laughed and said, "Alright, I'll use myself as bait and slowly catch a wealthy husband for you."
