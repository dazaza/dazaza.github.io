Guangdong will experience rain and temperature drop this weekend, ending the returning south weather. Weather Forecast News Center Sina.com

by psyzems3 on 2012-03-08 23:12:54

On the 7th, counties in the eastern part of the province will experience moderate to heavy (thunder) rain, with localized extremely heavy rain. The rest of the counties will have light rain and localized heavy rain. Experts remind that due to frequent recent rainfall, spring planting has already begun across the province, and it is necessary to strengthen field management in order to prevent continuous rainy weather from causing seeds to rot. Meanwhile, Guangdong has entered a season with frequent severe convection weather; thus, precautions must be taken against lightning, heavy precipitation, short-term strong winds, and their derivative disasters.

Guangzhou: Light rain from the 7th to the 8th, temperature ranging from 17 to 22°C; on the 9th there will be light to moderate rain, temperature ranging from 15 to 21°C; on the 10th, light rain will turn into overcast with partly cloudy skies, temperature ranging from 10 to 16°C.

Under the joint influence of a high-altitude trough and warm moist air currents, after significant precipitation occurred in Guangdong from the 4th to the 5th, stronger precipitation reappeared in the central and northern regions on the 6th. According to monitoring by the provincial meteorological station network, from 8 am on the 6th to 8 am on the 7th, 117 meteorological stations in Qingyuan, Shaoguan, Zhaoqing, Yunfu, and Heyuan recorded downpours exceeding 50 millimeters. The average rainfall in these areas was 31.4 millimeters, with Xinja Town in Wengyuan County recording the highest rainfall in the province at 115.4 millimeters.

In the urban area of Guangzhou, moderate to heavy rain fell on the 6th, with visibility maintained between 9 and 15 kilometers. Luogang Meteorological Observatory in Guangzhou recorded a cumulative rainfall of 37.9 millimeters in 24 hours, and the lowest morning temperature today was 17.1°C.

The Guangdong Provincial Meteorological Bureau forecasts that most areas will still have light rain today, with more noticeable rain in the eastern regions. From the night of the 8th to the 9th, a relatively strong cold air mass will affect the province from north to south, followed by another cold air supplement on the 10th. Affected by this, the temperatures across the province will gradually drop from the 9th to the 11th, with the lowest temperature expected on the morning of the 11th. Specific forecasts are as follows:

On the 8th, the northern part of Guangdong will gradually be affected by the cold air, transitioning from light rain to moderate to heavy rain, while the rest of the counties will have light rain. The lowest temperatures in Shaoguan and northern parts of Qingyuan will be between 7 to 10°C, other parts of the central and northern regions will be between 11 to 16°C, and southern areas will be between 17 to 21°C.

According to China Weather Network, some parts of Guangdong experienced heavy to extreme heavy rain yesterday (the 6th). It is forecasted that starting from the night of the 8th, a relatively strong cold air mass will affect Guangdong from north to south. From the 9th to the 11th, the average temperature will decrease by 6 to 8°C, accompanied by light to moderate rain and localized heavy rain, bringing an end to the "hui nan tian" phenomenon.

From the 9th to the 10th, under the influence of the relatively strong cold air, the temperatures across the province will gradually drop, with light to moderate rain and localized heavy rain. The lowest temperatures in northern Guangdong will be between 6 to 10°C, central parts between 9 to 11°C, and southern parts between 12 to 15°C.
