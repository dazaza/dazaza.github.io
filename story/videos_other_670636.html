A catering mix-up, bad weather, or an embarrassing best man’s speech can be quickly forgotten or relegated to the "funny tales for the future" category in your memory, but issues with your dress might stay with you forever, filed under 'abject humiliation.'

Going custom-made

Of course, a custom bridal gown can't guarantee these things won't happen to you - but it will certainly reduce the likelihood of such calamities occurring. If you get a custom-made wedding dress, you're not just picking something that will be special for you and your style, but you'll also be very closely and personally involved with the dressmaker. That means you'll have a much greater level of involvement and quality control than if you buy an off-the-peg gown.

That, of course, also includes precision measuring and making adjustments during the fitting process. You'll end up with a far better-fitting final product, which is also likely to reduce the probability of disasters on the day itself.

The nature of your relationship with your dressmaker is such that you can avoid last-minute glitches and compromises, like needing to make urgent changes only to find that "nobody is available." They will also personally know you, and personal relationships are known to have a positive impact on the quality and completeness of the final product.

Ultimately, a custom bridal gown may cost a little more than a broadly equivalent one bought off the peg, though the price differences may be smaller than you imagine. In the end, it may be a small price to pay for a combination of reduced risk and increased reassurance regarding your wedding dress detail!
