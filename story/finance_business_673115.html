Linshuhao.com is for sale for a six-digit figure.

by xwkj0983 on 2012-02-23 13:54:34

In the past two weeks, Jeremy Lin, a basketball player graduated from Harvard University, not only swept up a "Linsanity" in NBA courts but also promoted unlimited business opportunities including domain name registration. Mr. Liu, a software engineer working in Guangzhou, had a sharp eye for beauty and registered the Chinese pinyin domain linshuhao.com as early as May 2011. With the increasingly strong "Jeremy Lin storm" recently, hundreds of people contacted him every day, among which there were no lack of domain farmers (domain name investors) and companies who intended to buy it. Yesterday, he told reporters that he was very optimistic about "Ahao's" next performance, and his current psychological price for this domain name was six digits. For the "Lin comes crazy" triggered domain name registration heat, senior domain name experts reminded that new entrants should not pursue short-term effects, invest more time and patience, find the way before investing more funds. Netizens heatedly discussed the craze of registering Jeremy Lin's domain name. At one o'clock in the afternoon on February 19, Jeremy Lin (Jeremy Shu-How Lin), born in 1988, updated his Sina Weibo: "9 mistakes cannot complete the mission, must learn from mistakes, then enter the next station...." In less than 30 hours, this Weibo was forwarded nearly 9,000 times, and comments reached more than 12,000. This Weibo account, which currently only released 59 posts, has nearly 1.9 million fans. The 80s-born Jeremy Lin, who currently plays for the New York Knicks in the NBA, has been praised by the media for his outstanding performance, saying that he has driven a "Linsanity". Business opportunities followed suit. It was reported that Jeremy Lin applied for the right to use the trademark "Linsanity" on all types of clothing on February 14, including underwear, toy figures, drinks, and luggage, etc. Meanwhile, 22 Chinese and English domain names related to Jeremy Lin were also registered, becoming a hot topic of discussion within the domain circle and on Weibo. Many people praised the commercial insight of the registrants. A netizen named "Jian Dian Gan" claimed: "Register the names of all players in the youth training camp in the future, there will always be one who stands out." However, some domain farmers held reservations about registering Jeremy Lin's Chinese domain name, believing that Lin himself uses an English name, so registering his Chinese pinyin domain name is not reliable. The domain linshuhao.com was registered last May. Among the 22 registered Jeremy Lin homonymous domains, the pinyin domain linshuhao.com has drawn certain attention from the media and netizens. There are claims that the holder of this domain quoted 260,000 yuan. Yesterday, I logged into the whois website to query the owner information of the linshuhao.com domain. The website data showed that the domain was registered on May 29, 2011, and renewed until May 29, 2013. The registrant surnamed Liu. The traffic of this domain has been quite good recently, with over 500,000 IP visits in the past three months. According to relevant information, I contacted Mr. Liu in Guangzhou yesterday afternoon. He said that recently, hundreds of people call him or send him emails every day. "Some people may just want to join the fun, but there are still many people seriously asking for prices, including domain farmers from all over the country, and intermediary platforms doing domain transactions." Mr. Liu has loved sports since childhood, especially basketball. Last May, he read a report about Jeremy Lin by chance. As a software engineer, he has long insisted on amateur domain investment, so "I had a sudden idea," and registered the domain linshuhao.com. "I originally planned to set up a blog introducing him. He was not famous at that time, and no one in China knew him." On February 6 this year, in the game between the Knicks and the Nets, Jeremy Lin came off the bench but contributed the highest 25 points of the whole game. Jeremy Lin became popular instantly! After that, Mr. Liu found that the page views of linshuhao.com increased sharply, "not less than 50,000 IPs per day." And the number of domain farmers who voluntarily asked for prices also rose to hundreds per day. Regarding this pinyin domain that cost less than a hundred yuan to register, Mr. Liu said, "I am an ordinary person, 'a normal heart,' hoping to sell it at a reasonable price, allowing this domain name to get better application." When asked if the previously reported 260,000 yuan was his ideal market value, he paused for a few seconds and said: "Although I am not a fan of Ahao, I am very optimistic about his performance. Isn't Yi Jianlian's international domain transaction price several tens of thousands of US dollars? You can refer to it. My current psychological price is six digits." "Jeremy Lin Storm" domain fever "short-term effect, meaningless" Wang Senli, a native of Shanghai, started investing in domains in 1999 and is a well-known domain farmer in the circle. The domains he has transacted range from tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands, and the domains he holds include those worth millions. In early June last year, after Chinese player Li Na won the women's singles championship at the French Open, domain farmers rushed to register domains related to her, with quotes exceeding five digits. For this wave of market sentiment, Wang Senli said that it was possible to quote tens of thousands of yuan. But when consulted yesterday about the domain fever caused by the "Jeremy Lin Storm", he frankly replied: "Short-term effect, meaningless." In his view, it is difficult to evaluate personal name domains, which generally have low value. Moreover, although Jeremy Lin is of Chinese descent, he uses English names, so Chinese pinyin is useless to him. Similarly, senior domain farmer Jacky pointed out that Jeremy Lin's name is in English, so pinyin domains like linshuhao.com may only be visited by Chinese netizens. "Jeremy Lin currently has no commercial application or development domestically, so the possibility of using this pinyin domain is small. Therefore, the value of this domain is not high at present. Domains themselves are like futures. Now whoever buys it at a high price will bear great risks, but if there is commercial development, it can also be considered to buy." Both domain experts reminded the new domain farmers paying attention to this wave of domain registration fever to look, ask, and think more, invest more time and patience, and wait until they understand some tricks and feelings before investing more funds. "The domain investment industry develops relatively fast, and the cost is now relatively high. Investment always carries risks. If the risks are controlled well, half of the success is achieved." http://www.xwkj.com/yumingzhuce.asp
