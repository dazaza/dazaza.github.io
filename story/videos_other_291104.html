ECNS, December 3 -- According to the American Chinese-language newspaper "Chicago Chinese Forum", after more than three years of hard work and with the support and help of many Chinese associations, businessmen and overseas Chinese compatriots, the first chronicle-style material collection for Chinese in the United States edited by the "Chicago Chinese Forum" -- the "Chicago Chinese Yearbook 2009-2010" has been officially printed and published in Beijing. About 10,000 copies will be shipped to the United States within a month.

According to the editorial committee of the yearbook, the "Chicago Chinese Yearbook 2009-2010" integrates historical data, practicality, and knowledge. It will become an essential reference manual for Chinese groups, merchants, and relevant institutions in the Midwest region of the United States and even across the country. The content includes a brief history of Chinese immigrants in the Greater Chicago area, introductions to the purposes and histories of over sixty Chinese associations in the region, lists of staff members, photos of key personnel, major news events and important comments from the Chinese community over the past two years, a special introduction to twenty community and business figures of the year, as well as encyclopedic information and commonly used telephone numbers related to immigration, education, catering, real estate, finance, transportation, tourism, and entertainment for Chinese life and careers. The entire book is divided into sections on history, personalities, associations, reviews, news, abstracts, and telephone directories, totaling about six hundred thousand words, mainly in Chinese with some accompanied by English summaries. It also contains many historically valuable pictures.

Mayor Richard Daley of Chicago wrote a congratulatory letter specifically for the publication of the "Yearbook". In his letter, he said that this yearbook provides information on the activities and services of Chinese people and businesses throughout our region, reflecting the many contributions made by the Chinese community to the growth and diversification of our city. Daley said that the city of Chicago has close ties with China. Our cities are sister cities with Shanghai and Shenyang, and at the same time, commercial and other bilateral relations that closely connect our citizens with China are deepening continuously. I look forward to further exchange opportunities between the United States and China in terms of culture, economy, and other aspects.

Mr. Xu Qingsong, researcher at the National Museum of China and vice president of the Chinese Collectors Association, wrote a preface titled "The Spring Water Turns Green Like Blue" for the yearbook.

David Zhang, publisher of the "Yearbook" and editor-in-chief of the "Chicago Chinese Forum," said in the foreword that the "Yearbook" aims to systematically record the footprints of the struggles of Chinese people in the United States. Its purpose is to honor our predecessors, inspire future generations, commemorate Chinese history, and exchange Chinese information. This relates to the great cause of our descendants, and can be considered an unprecedented initiative. We sincerely hope that this pioneering yearbook will help enhance the status and image of Chinese people and promote their better integration into mainstream society, putting down roots and bearing fruit.

Recently, a small number of samples of this "Chicago Chinese Yearbook" have been gifted in the Greater Chicago area and domestically in China, receiving positive evaluations and affirmation from overseas Chinese and relevant experts. It is understood that these "Yearbooks" will be distributed free of charge to restaurants, shops, schools, community centers, libraries, various overseas Chinese organizations, and relevant institutions where there are relatively concentrated Chinese populations, mainly issued in the Midwest region of the United States, but also sent as gifts in small quantities across the United States and domestically in China. The editorial committee also plans to launch different versions for major cities with concentrated Chinese populations in the United States at an appropriate time, which will also be published annually, with 10,000 copies issued each time.
