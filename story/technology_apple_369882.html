Is there an official version of Jobs' suit? Named iWear...

by shootero on 2010-10-30 21:10:17

Official version of Jobs suit? Named iWear... On October 30, according to foreign media reports, the annual Halloween is approaching, and Apple has brought a surprise again. It plans to launch a fashion set called iWear in Apple specialty stores during Halloween. The peculiarity of iWear lies in its complete cloning of Jobs' attire. It is reported that the price of iWear is about $150. The set includes one of Jobs' signature high-necked black sweaters, a pair of blue jeans, a pair of sneakers, and a pair of wire-frame glasses, all styles are exactly the same as Jobs'.

The slogan for this campaign is "Think different, dress alike." Why not dress up like Jobs during Halloween celebrations? For Apple product enthusiasts, Jobs is like a landmark, and Apple represents fashion. Apple fans can't resist the temptation of Macs and iPhones, and they will also be unable to resist the "Steve-Jobs style" clothing.

The manufacturer of Mac computers and iPhones seems to be entering the market dominated by Prada and Gucci. Apple said, "This is the next logical step. Since Jobs has such a great impact on our lives, let him influence our dressing too."

Imagine the scenario where Apple fans flock to buy old-fashioned high-necked black sweaters, blue jeans, and geeky glasses. This is no joke, given the fanatical worship of Jobs and Apple by Apple fans, all of this will soon happen.
