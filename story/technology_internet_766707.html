Apple updates Apple TV set-top box system, adding multiple functions

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 20:25:39

Apple TV Interface

Who says Apple doesn't prioritize innovation? Today, Apple has once again updated the Apple TV. With the launch of iOS 7 and the new iPhone series, Apple today also upgraded the Apple TV set-top box software to version 6.0. The newly updated Apple TV will integrate features such as iTunes Radio support, direct access to the iTunes Music Store, and the ability to use AirPlay directly from iCloud.

The latter function may be very similar to Google's recently launched Chromecast—uploading content directly to the user’s Apple TV for playback, without first uploading it to the user’s iPhone or iPad, and then using AirPlay to transfer it to the Apple TV for playback. This function can help users make more efficient use of bandwidth. At the same time, the user’s iOS device won’t be blocked during content transmission, meaning that users can still perform more advanced tasks on their iOS devices while transmitting content, such as playing games.

Industry insiders expect that Apple will announce a completely new Apple TV in the coming weeks. The Apple TV currently available on the market is still Apple's third-generation device. The Apple TV initially appeared in May 2012, and aside from security updates, only minor updates have been made this year.

After today's update, Apple TV will also be able to access the user’s iCloud photos and videos, as well as synchronize subscription content across various iOS devices.
