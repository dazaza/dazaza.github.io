The voice recognition alarm clock that communicates with you in English - a creative product from Kupin.com.

by koodj on 2009-11-28 09:59:17

Oh, actually I don't need to tell you, once you see Nobby, you will know he is an alien. His nose is not on his face, ears are not on the head, the mouth is under the ass, there is only one eye but it is quite big, but no eyelashes... Well, just treat him as from the "strange and weird star".

His name is Nobby, which is a voice recognition alarm clock. If you ask it for time, it will tell you in English; if you say some simple sentences to it, Nobby will also respond differently. For example, if you ask it "How do I look?" (Am I handsome (pretty)?), it might answer "Well, to be honest, Err" (Well, to be honest, hmm...). Don't mind, it just wants to joke with you like a friend.

Nobby doesn't always stay quietly in the corner. Maybe when you are chatting with friends about everything under the sun, he can't help but interject a few words like "What's up?" "It's me!" This feeling, really, is like having one more real partner at home.

There were a few times when I was in a daze thinking that Nobby was not an electronic product. He is real!

When the alarm goes off, you can say "Alarm off", or you can hold its neck and shake it a bit, and the alarm will stop. This is such an interesting little monster. Unfortunately, Nobby can only understand a few sentences in English and only responds in English. But for those who are familiar with English conversations and are tired of waking up every day from the noisy alarm sound, Nobby is a pretty good choice.

Nobby was designed by the designer Tom Lawton, who has created a lot of interesting products, and was produced by the British professional watch manufacturer ZEON company.

Me: "Nobby~"

Nobby: "What's up?"

Me: "What’s the time?"

Nobby: "8:59 AM"

Oh, I am late again ^^

In addition, you can try more interesting dialogues. For example, if you tell it "Go to sleep", it will say "No, I'm not sleepy yet" or answer "Okay, zzz Z~". When you let it "Make a decision", it may say "Why ask me about your own life?". Nobby's voice recognition function consumes a lot of power. Fortunately, it will also tell you with voice when the battery is almost dead, but you'd better plug it into the power adapter that comes with it. Nobby may give different answers each time for the same question. If it didn't hear clearly or didn't understand what you mean, it will tell you with voice, which makes you feel like it is alive, and not just an alarm clock. It's worth learning a few more English sentences for this;)
