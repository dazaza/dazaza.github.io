Some employees defraud housing fund at year end, the center claims that it will be harder to extract again

by asljw9004 on 2012-02-26 16:02:48

White-collar workers deceive to withdraw housing provident fund at year-end due to lack of money; some intermediaries can operate with a fee of thousands of yuan for invoices and remuneration. Housing Provident Fund Center: once discovered, it will be very difficult to withdraw again.

At the end of the year, some young office workers who are short of cash have started eyeing the money in their housing provident fund accounts. When reporters visited multiple real estate intermediaries in Chaoyang and Fengtai districts, some staff promised that as long as a fee of about one thousand yuan is paid for invoices and remuneration, they can extract the housing provident fund through means such as renting.

However, the Housing Provident Fund Center reminds that once fraudulently obtaining the housing provident fund using fake invoices is discovered, there will be a permanent "bad record" left at the Housing Provident Fund Center, and strict reviews will be conducted when attempting to withdraw again.

● Who is withdrawing? White-collar workers succeed or fail in deceiving to withdraw

"We have four colleagues in our company who all processed it through this company, and after submitting the materials, the housing provident fund arrived within three days." Ms. Wang (a pseudonym) said.

As year-end spending increases, Ms. Wang, who works in Dawang Road, needed funds for bank repayment and thus contacted a company online that claimed to provide relevant materials for those needing to withdraw the housing provident fund.

After agreeing on price and other issues, the company sent an application form with a blank rental contract and a printed invoice for 10,000 yuan in rent to Ms. Wang's home via courier. After successfully receiving the housing provident fund, Ms. Wang paid 800 yuan to the company via Alipay, where 500 yuan was for the tax required to issue the invoice, and the remaining 300 yuan was the company's remuneration.

But Mr. Zhai (a pseudonym), who also wanted to withdraw the housing provident fund through fake renting, was not so lucky. Since the invoice he bought was fake, it was detected by the staff of the Housing Provident Fund Management Center. As a result, Mr. Zhai failed to obtain the housing provident fund, and the money spent on buying the invoice was wasted.

What kind of organization can both issue rental invoices and provide complete rental contracts? From Ms. Wang, reporters learned that the company is a newly registered intermediary mainly engaged in house leasing and sales.

● Who is doing it? Mainly property intermediaries, with involvement from invoice scalpers

Yesterday afternoon, reporters went to a certain intermediary company in Wannian Huacheng. When reporters mentioned wanting to withdraw the housing provident fund, the intermediary’s staff readily agreed to help. He confidently guaranteed that the invoice provided by their company could smoothly withdraw the housing provident fund.

Subsequently, reporters also visited areas such as Baijiazhuang and Nan Sanlitun in Chaoyang District, where both well-known intermediary companies and smaller startups all indicated that they could provide services to withdraw the housing provident fund. In addition, compared to the relatively professional "service" provided by property intermediaries, some invoice scalpers also got involved in this business.

Through small advertisements sprayed on the roadside claiming to process housing provident fund withdrawals, reporters contacted the "advertiser." "Invoices of any amount are fine, you just need to prepare the rental contract yourself," an invoice scalper told reporters.

It is understood that issuing regular house rental invoices requires paying 5% tax on the rental price. For example, for 10,000 yuan, 500 yuan in tax would be required. During the investigation, reporters learned that regular intermediary companies charge around 1,000 yuan as their remuneration, subtracting the 500 yuan in taxes, leaving them with about 500 yuan per transaction. However, invoice scalpers only charge three or four hundred yuan as remuneration, which clearly does not even cover the tax cost.

● Is there a problem with the invoice? "One number multi-use" fake invoices hard to distinguish for ordinary people

Why do invoices of the same amount have different selling prices? Reporters learned from tax staff that there is indeed a phenomenon of repeated use of a single invoice number. Simply put, the invoice number and password are genuine, but the invoice itself is fake.

Such invoices can appear as authentic as real ones when queried on the local taxation bureau's website, showing as true tickets. Only through observation and touch by professionals with years of experience can the authenticity finally be determined, making it hard for ordinary people to tell the difference. In this way, invoice scalpers make money through "one number multi-use." Therefore, it is not surprising why the price of invoices sold by scalpers is less than the tax cost.

Tax staff remind that one should not easily trust the network query results provided by invoice scalpers to avoid damage to personal interests.

● How to manage?

Applications with a history of fraudulent withdrawal will be strictly investigated

Staff from Beijing Housing Provident Fund Management Center hotline 96155 also stated that currently, there are indeed some applicants using false rental contracts and invoices to withdraw the housing provident fund.

To prevent such behavior, the Beijing Housing Provident Fund Management Center, Beijing Local Taxation Bureau, and Beijing Municipal Commission of Urban Planning and Construction jointly issued the "Opinion on Solving Problems Related to Fraudulent Withdrawal of Housing Provident Fund" on August 23rd this year.

The "Opinion" clarified the steps for strictly reviewing the authenticity of materials provided by applicants. If it is found that the applicant's invoice or rental contract is false, the processing of the housing provident fund withdrawal will be immediately stopped, and the relevant departments will conduct a review. Only after passing the review will the processing continue.

Staff also noted that if the applicant has a record of fraudulent withdrawal, future applications will undergo stricter reviews, making it more difficult to withdraw the housing provident fund.
