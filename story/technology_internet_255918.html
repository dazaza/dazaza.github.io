The Biggest Change in 40 Years of Internet, Chinese Domain Names Receive Much Attention

by now on 2009-11-04 15:29:28

The greatest technical change on the Internet in 40 years has been launched this week. According to reports, at a meeting held last week in Seoul by the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN), it was most noted that the approval of international domain names or addresses written in non-English characters will be approved. Peter Thangaraj Trushie, Chairman of the ICANN Board, said that this is the biggest technical change that has occurred since the invention of the Internet 40 years ago. In order to create a system that can translate multi-language scripts into correct addresses, they have been testing for several years, and are determined to successfully implement this major change.

According to the decision made at this meeting, many languages will be able to be used as network addresses, including simplified Chinese, traditional Chinese, Russian, Korean, Japanese, Chosŏn'gŭl, etc. Among all these languages, Chinese is the language with the largest number of users. The application and popularization of Chinese domain names will also attract worldwide attention. Currently, there are 1.6 billion Internet users globally, and our country accounts for 300 million of them. Adding the Chinese population scattered around the world, facing such a huge group of people, the application of Chinese domain names will play an important role in this Internet transformation. For enterprises, after this meeting, Chinese domain names will provide more help and improvement in terms of enterprise brand protection and recognition than before.

In fact, before this meeting was held, Chinese domain names had already gained their status on the global Internet and successfully promoted their popularization and application. Now, many government agencies, enterprises, and individuals in our country have already activated Chinese domain names. For example, if netizens input www.时代互联.cn in the address bar, they will successfully access the main site of Era Interconnection, www.now.cn; and some foreign enterprises have also activated Chinese domain names to cater to the Chinese market. For example, if netizens input www.谷歌.cn in the address bar, they can also access Google's main site. It seems that the popularization and application of Chinese domain names have taken the lead and are now ready to be widely adopted.

It is understood that in order to further promote the application and popularization of Chinese domain names, Era Interconnection, a five-star domestic domain name registrar, has enthusiastically launched a promotion campaign for Chinese domain names. Now, any user who registers a Chinese domain name with Era Interconnection can receive a gift worth 50 yuan. During the time when ICANN approves the use of non-English domain names, this event has attracted considerable attention, thus prompting many users to decide to register Chinese domain names.

As ICANN promotes the use of non-English domain names, it will gradually eliminate some netizens' doubts about the feasibility of Chinese domain name applications. If other countries' languages are approved for use, how could Chinese, with so many users, not be recognized internationally? Moreover, the application of Chinese domain names has already taken a big step forward. It has won a large market domestically and has sparked waves of enthusiasm for protecting Chinese domain name brands. We believe that in the near future, Chinese-speaking netizens will get used to entering Chinese domain names in the address bar to easily reach the websites they want to visit.
