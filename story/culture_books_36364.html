Ancient civilizations, lost treasures, and mysterious ancient tombs - nothing captures the audience's attention more than these elements. Currently, a "tomb economy" is emerging around the world. American commercial blockbusters like *The Mummy* and *Raiders of the Lost Ark*, as well as classic video games like *Tomb Raider*, have all achieved tremendous commercial success. These fictional "tombs" allow people to embark on thrilling and grand adventures from the comfort of movie theaters or their homes, following the protagonists in films or games and experiencing unprecedented excitement.

*Ghost Blows Out the Light* (*The Adventures of Grave Robbers*) is a series of text-based adventure stories in this vein. Three modern treasure hunters (摸金校尉) use secret feng shui techniques to interpret the pulses of the great mountains and rivers, seeking out the lost palaces and treasures scattered across the land. Deserts, snow-capped mountains, forests, canyons, rapids, grasslands, rare and mysterious flora and fauna, perilous traps—every step is filled with suspense and tightly woven plots that challenge the limits of imagination. In this bizarre and eerie underground world, layer after layer of ancient mysteries are unveiled. The story begins with a mysterious incomplete scroll passed down through the protagonist’s family, leading them on a journey spanning thousands of miles in search of hidden dragon-shaped formations. They face countless hardships and dangers, as their actions—capable of moving heaven and earth, overturning seas and rivers—are far from ordinary. From the nine-story demon tower beneath the great glacier of the Kunlun Mountains, the secret stronghold of the Kwantung Army in the Wild Man Gully on the China-Mongolia border, the Jingjue Ancient City lost in the black desert of Taklamakan, the Corpse Fragrance Devil Yam Flower in the bottomless cave of the Sacred Mountain, the Demon Coffin in the Insect Valley of the Yunnan jungle, the Headless Cave of the ancient Guge Kingdom in the Karakoram Range of Tibet, to the Dragon Ridge Mystery Caves in Shaanxi...
