Self-Study Examination Expert Guidance: How to Quickly Improve Self-Study Efficiency

February 16, 2012

Zhiseng Education Network [http://www.zsjykj.com/zikao/content.aspx?table=zkdtnews&id=7#]

Self-study examination is a form of continuing education that mainly relies on candidates using their spare time to complete their studies. In other words, self-study is the primary form through which candidates participate in learning. Therefore, how to improve self-study efficiency is a problem every self-study candidate faces during the learning process. Especially under conditions where work and study conflicts are prominent and various interferences have significant impacts, exploring and summarizing effective self-study methods suitable for oneself to enhance self-study efficiency, thereby achieving better results in as short a time as possible, is the greatest wish of each self-study candidate during the self-study process. So, how can self-study candidates improve their self-study efficiency during the self-study process? For this reason, experts from Hefei Guosheng Education have summarized methods to improve self-study efficiency, hoping to be helpful to all self-study candidates.

1. Clarify the System, Grasp the Overall Picture

Experts from Guosheng Education point out that when self-studying any course, candidates should grasp the overall system of the textbook to accurately understand its content. To achieve this, candidates can master the structure of the textbook by reading the table of contents, thereby identifying the main thread running through the textbook, understanding and mastering the relationships between chapters, thus providing a relatively clear path for further in-depth study.

2. Distinguish Between Primary and Secondary Points, Highlight Key Areas

The key to self-study candidates studying textbooks is to focus on the "outline" of the textbook. By focusing on the "outline," one can effectively cover the "details." During the self-study process, attention should be paid to finding key areas, focusing on them, grasping them, and mastering them. While finding key points, it's not necessarily about understanding them solely from an exam perspective. Generally speaking, the key points of a textbook are multi-layered, including key points of the entire book, each unit, every chapter, and even every issue. Additionally, basic concepts, theories, and even key words and sentences of every issue should not be overlooked. Therefore, experts from Guosheng Education advise candidates to learn how to grasp key points at every level, focusing on studying, deeply understanding, accurately remembering, and continuously consolidating, thereby achieving the effect of covering the whole with a single point and fully mastering the material.

3. Find More Questions, Deepen Understanding

Experts from Guosheng Education tell candidates that when facing some difficult points, doubts, and key issues in the textbook, they should ask more "what" and "why" questions, then think more, write more, consider more, and find answers. Learning with questions makes the purpose strong, thoughts focused, beneficial for self-study, deepening understanding, and improving memory efficiency, achieving twice the result with half the effort. Candidates should promptly resolve any questions they don't understand, without delaying or procrastinating, otherwise, it will affect further learning later.

4. Connect Vertically and Horizontally, Integrate Knowledge

Different chapters within the same textbook, and even different courses offered annually, have certain connections. Experts from Guosheng Education suggest that no matter what content is being studied, the method of connection should be used to make previous and subsequent content mutually comprehensible. Doing so helps digest new knowledge, expand one’s thinking, deepen and elevate one’s understanding, achieving the effect of reviewing old knowledge to gain new insights.

5. Connect Theory with Practice, Learn Actively and Apply Knowledge

Connecting theory with practice, history with reality, is the most fundamental method of learning. Experts from Guosheng Education tell candidates that during the process of studying textbooks, to master newly learned theoretical knowledge, one cannot rely on rote memorization or existing "standard answers." The key lies in analysis and understanding, learning actively rather than passively, thoroughly analyzing and deeply understanding basic theories, and firmly mastering them. On the other hand, one must also learn to apply knowledge. Experts from Guosheng Education also point out that during the self-study process, attention should be paid to practical work and ideological realities, consciously applying learned knowledge gradually and progressively. Doing so not only enlightens deeper understanding of theoretical essence but also enhances the ability to use theoretical knowledge to analyze and solve problems. Moreover, applying theoretical knowledge in work itself is a process of re-learning, deepening, and elevating, representing a higher-level learning.

6. Use Diagrams, Eliminate Difficulties

When the content of the textbook is complex or difficult to remember, it is necessary to draw a clearly structured diagram of the textbook to assist in self-study and memory. This allows for a deeper understanding of the textbook during the drawing process and saves time for comprehensive review and focused mastery. Experts from Guosheng Education remind candidates to carefully analyze and synthesize while drawing diagrams following the order from left to right, top to bottom, and from large to small, layering down and gradually deepening. Each level's key points should be connected according to causal and logical relationships to form a knowledge network diagram. Experts from Guosheng Education tell candidates that the size and detail of the knowledge network diagram depend entirely on individual self-study and review situations, requiring flexible handling. Using diagrams for self-study provides an outline, facilitating both in-depth focus and comprehensive understanding, playing a very important role in successfully completing the self-study task.

7. Simulate Training, Grasp Question Types

During the review and preparation stage, based on comprehensive self-study and focused review, simulation training should also be conducted. The purpose is not to "guess questions or speculate on answers" but to grasp question types to ensure correct and standardized answering. Experts from Guosheng Education mention that the main question types for simulation training include past exam questions, self-test questions, and assignment questions from the textbook. During simulation training, one should pay attention to the answering methods of different question types, mastering the answering patterns; compare the answer content against the questions to make them more accurate and reliable; and pay attention to changes in the revised content of the textbook, grasping the key points.

Experts from Guosheng Education also mentioned that a considerable portion of self-study candidates are working learners, with prominent conflicts between work and study, limited study time, and greater learning difficulty. This requires candidates to explore learning methods suitable for their own learning characteristics during the self-study process, leveraging their advantages as much as possible, reducing the blindness of learning, and persistently summarizing to improve efficiency and achieve better academic results.
