The day before, we came out from Tangbala Grassland and returned along the original route, passing through XinYuan County all the way, arriving at Nalati Town at about 10 p.m., where we found an inn to stay for the night. I've mentioned before that the most impressive thing on the road to Nalati was the continuous and vast wheat fields. I felt like those wheat fields extended for several miles, with a vast plain in sight, only mountains in the distance, and no obstructions nearby. The wheat fields were indeed spectacular. If it hadn't been for the pressing time and the impending darkness, I would have definitely asked the driver to stop so we could admire it for a while. Since we couldn't indulge ourselves, let's keep that fleeting glimpse forever in our memories. Nalati Town is not big, but due to its famous Nalati Grassland, there are mainly hotels and restaurants in the town. Perhaps because the season we went wasn't very busy, finding a hotel at 11 p.m. was still quite smooth, with prices and environments much better than the previous night in Gongliu County. However, the hotel we stayed at that night had a power outage, while the ones across the street had electricity. We thought the power would come back in the middle of the night, but it didn't, and there was no hot water for showers. Luckily, the cool and dry weather at night in the grasslands meant that even if we didn't shower for a few days, it wouldn't be too bad. In mainland China, especially Guangzhou, we might have already been unable to endure it.

In the morning, after having breakfast at a restaurant opposite the hotel, we set off. I've always said that the cost of living in Xinjiang isn't low, feeling similar to Shanghai. This can also be felt from that breakfast; eggs were either 1.5 yuan or 2 yuan each, which was quite expensive. The plan for this day's journey was to go from Nalati Town through Nalati Grassland, over the Gongnaisi Mountain Pass, and then proceed all the way to Bayinbrooke. The distance was far, and the road conditions after Gongnaisi Mountain Pass to Bayin weren't good. Passing by Nalati Grassland, we made a small detour at the entrance, considering the repetition with the previous grasslands and the commercialization of Nalati, we did not buy tickets to enter. But the early morning Nalati was still very beautiful, if it weren't for the limited budget, we would have liked to see this so-called "sky grassland." Leaving Nalati and continuing forward, the car climbed continuously, reaching the top of Gongnaisi Mountain Pass, where large areas of snow were visible. The scenery along this road was extremely beautiful, Gongnaisi Mountain Pass lived up to its reputation, and the Gongnaisi forest farm was as enchanting as the legends suggest. Miu classmate had never seen snow before, so she was excitedly ecstatic when she saw some snow at the top of the pass. L and Quan classmates were both speechless at her behavior. Hmm, we suddenly went from summer to winter. At the top of the pass, the sky seemed closer, with mist swirling around, giving a feeling of entering a fairyland.

After descending from the pass, the scenery gradually changed from the lush, rolling hills to a grand western landscape. The endless straight road stretched like a celestial path, with vast lands on both sides, resembling a Gobi desert but not quite. In the distance, there were still undulating mountains. Such emptiness, such blue skies and white clouds, such desolation, the greatest joy was to gallop freely in the car.

With great reluctance, we left Jiuxu, driving for a long time on the dark roads, finally returning to Bayin Town. After a simple dinner, we washed up and slept again until midnight. That's how life is in Xinjiang, sunset at 10 p.m., dinner at 11 p.m., and sleeping at 12 p.m. I wonder, in the quiet and distant depths of Bayin, whether the swans have already fallen asleep peacefully, will they dance gracefully at some moment in the night?

After finishing the highway and turning onto the small road leading to Bayin, the journey became quite arduous due to the poor road conditions and severe bumps. But fortunately, the roadside scenery comforted us. The crystal-clear springs we occasionally encountered, the shadows cast by the sunlight on the mountain ranges, the adorable cows and sheep we frequently saw along with the sheepdogs, and even the patches of purple flowers we discovered while singing mountain songs were all enough to alleviate the hardships of our journey. Before entering Bayin, all the grasslands we saw were alpine meadows. Bayin, however, was the true meaning of a steppe grassland, the kind you'd instantly think of as "the sky vast and boundless, the wilderness endless, the wind blowing the grass low to reveal cattle and sheep." Compared to Kalaqun and Tangbala, it was open and far-reaching.

【The Serenity of Swan Lake, No Trace of Swans】

【The Misty Nine Bends Without a Sunset... But Still Quite Beautiful】

However, there were no swans at Swan Lake, the swans were hiding far away from us. Even with the lens zoomed in, we couldn't see them clearly; we could only use binoculars. Actually, while driving along the edge of Sayram Lake, we did see swans, several of them, much clearer and more intimate than those at Bayin. But at that time, the master said they weren't swans, and Quan and others also said they weren't. Afterward, I read several other people's travel diaries and confirmed that they were indeed swans. To be honest, the swans at Sayram Lake seemed more beautiful, why? Because Sayram Lake was beautiful. Although we didn't see the swans clearly, we did see plenty of other flying birds, after all, it was a wetland area, so there were many birds. And Swan Lake was still very beautiful. After lingering for a while at Swan Lake, we saw lightning storms in the distance and retreated toward the Nine Bends. Unfortunately, on the way to the Nine Bends, the clouds became thicker, the sky darker, and it eventually started raining. Once it rained, it didn't stop. We sat in the car and waited in the parking lot at the foot of the Nine Bends mountain for one or two hours. The rain was utterly disheartening, and at one point, we thought that once the rain stopped, the sun would shine brightly on the earth. But alas, things didn't go as planned. Around eight o'clock, when the rain stopped and we walked up the mountain, after walking for a very long time and reaching the viewing platform of the Nine Bends, the sky didn't clear up, and the sun didn't show its face. Sigh, it was sunny when we went to Swan Lake. It was freezing cold on the mountain peak, after waiting for a while, I unwillingly let L drag me down the mountain, looking back constantly hoping for a ray of sunshine, filled with such feelings, such melancholy. I blamed all the rain I encountered on Quan, who always encounters rain when he goes out. How could I bring him out? Alas, alas. In fact, Quan was even more melancholy. Because everyone called him Rain God. Alright, the legendary Nine Bends with nine suns, I am still destined not to meet you. But I know, one day I will see it. Because on the land of Northwest China, there are even more magnificent Nine Bends. We still have opportunities.

Compared to all the grasslands and lakes mentioned in the previous few articles, Bayinbrooke is undoubtedly the most famous. Even if you've never been to Xinjiang and know little about it, I believe you've heard of Bayinbrooke from somewhere. Bayinbrooke Grassland, the second largest grassland in China, means "abundant springs" in Mongolian.

【I really like this tone, I love the tranquility of Swan Lake】

【Birds flying over Swan Lake】

We arrived at Bayinbrooke Town around two in the afternoon. Miu and I went to look for rooms and negotiate prices. We used our silver-tongued skills to reduce the price from 180 to 120, successfully securing a good hotel. The master said that even if they came, the cheapest would be 150. Bayin Town is far from the outside world, so the consumption in the town is also not cheap. We had a nice Sichuan meal, rested for a while in the hotel, and then set out for Bayinbrooke Grassland. Note, Bayin Town and the grassland are not adjacent, but dozens of kilometers apart, requiring quite some driving time. In fact, coming to Bayin was mainly to see the swans at Swan Lake and the sunset at the Nine Bends. The ticket price for Bayin has increased to 40 yuan, without student tickets, only recognizing military officer certificates and senior citizen certificates. Alright, I again demonstrated my bargaining skills, trying to fool them with four expired student IDs at the window, but failed, so I directly showed the student ID to their leader standing not far from the window, and the leader exempted me from one ticket, so four people bought three tickets. Master Xu looked at me and said, Qi is indeed Qi. I sweated. Then I played another round on horseback riding fees, alright, I saved everyone a little money, haha. You cannot drive into Swan Lake, after the car reached a certain place, it could no longer enter, you could only choose to ride a horse or walk to enter. We decided to ride horses as we were lazy and hadn't ridden horses during this trip. Facts told us our choice was correct, because walking would take a long time, and there were many swamps on the way, making it painful to cross. Riding horses on such a vast and open grassland was simply too enjoyable.
