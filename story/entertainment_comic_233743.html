The story begins with a young boy named Carl Fredricksen attentively watching a newsreel about the famous explorer Charles Muntz and his beloved dogs who have just returned from an expedition in South America via hot air balloon. Muntz announces the discovery of a new species, but scientists call him a fraud and his reputation is ruined. However, young Carl believes every word Muntz says, especially his motto "Adventure is out there," which becomes Carl's lifelong motto. He dreams of becoming a great adventurer like Muntz.

The scene shifts to young Carl walking home with a balloon when he hears another child’s voice near an abandoned house. It's a tomboyish girl named Ellie, who is also fascinated by Muntz's adventures. Chatty Ellie uses the wind vane on the roof as a control stick, imagining the house as a big spaceship and persuading the bewildered Carl to join her plan for a "flying house" adventure. Although the two kids fail to make the house fly and end up falling, they become friends. Subsequent scenes show a montage of their lives as they grow up, get married, and live together. Ellie works as a zookeeper while Carl runs a business selling helium-filled balloons. They both dream of exploring the "Paradise Falls" in South America that Muntz mentioned, but life keeps them busy until Ellie passes away, leaving this dream unfulfilled.

After Ellie's death, Carl (voiced by Ed Asner) becomes reclusive and unwilling to interact with others. Worse still, his house is located on land planned for development, where a skyscraper is set to be built. Carl becomes a "nail household," refusing to move, and the government sends people to prepare to send him to a nursing home (John Ratzenberger voices a construction worker arguing with Carl). Before they arrive, Carl makes the decision to leave with his house, heading to South America to realize the dream he shared with his wife!

Thus, we see the classic scene in the trailer: colorful balloons pulling an entire house, including its foundation, into the sky. The aerial view of the house overlooking the earth is breathtaking, which was the "initial inspiration" that sparked the director's creation of this film.

However, old Carl didn't expect that his "flying house" had a "stowaway"—a nine-year-old Asian boy named Russell (voiced by Jordan Nagai), an overly optimistic chatterbox who participates in the Wilderness Explorers association. To become a senior member, he needs to collect a badge, which can be obtained by helping elderly people. Unfortunately, he picks the grumpy Carl as his target, who dislikes the little chubby boy following him around.

Carl devises many ways to throw this annoying kid off the "flying house" (this part is very funny), but before he can implement them, the "flying house" flies into a storm cloud cluster, and many of Ellie's belongings fall down. Carl struggles to protect these items as the screen gradually darkens. When Carl wakes up, the "stowaway" Russell tells Carl that his GPS can help them fly to "Paradise Falls." However, Carl cuts several balloons, preparing to land and send Russell back home in a car. At this moment, an accident occurs; in the thick fog, the "flying house" collides with a large rock as it approaches the ground. Although the house escapes damage, both Carl and Russell are thrown off. Seeing the "flying house" about to drift away, Carl secures it with a water pipe. Then the "flying house" drags him all the way to the cliff before stopping, and Carl realizes that "Paradise Falls" is right in front of him. From here, the two begin to pull the "flying house" forward on foot (as seen in the trailer, they enter the jungle).

In South America, Carl and Russell meet a new friend—a large, colorful bird with a long red beak (the new species discovered by explorer Muntz). This bird is really big, and Russell names it Kevin. The bird likes Russell but dislikes Carl. Then they meet their second friend—talking dog "Doug." More accurately, the collar around the dog's neck translates the dog's thoughts into human language. Later, they come across a canyon where several dogs appear, including two bulldogs and one Doberman (these dogs may be related to Muntz).

Subsequently, their flying house continues moving forward, encountering a saboteur driving an airship. The airship releases many planes that interfere with the flying house... Finally, the man on the airship turns out to be the explorer Charles Muntz, whose goal is to capture the bird. In the end, Carl and Russell fight Muntz, rescue the bird, and Russell earns his badge. He and the old man become inseparable friends with the talking dog, and our flying house eventually lands at the edge of Paradise Falls on Table Mountain, fulfilling old Carl's lifelong dream.
