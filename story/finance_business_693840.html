Two women disguised as matchmakers defraud rural men of marriageable age

by zuowenhl3 on 2012-03-06 19:08:57

The suspects, surnamed Zhang and Li respectively, are receiving police interrogation. (video screenshot) Weifang News Network (Microblog) reported on November 15 that two women pretended to sell cotton as they went from street to alley, cooperating with each other while targeting older single men in rural areas under the pretense of introducing them to potential partners for financial fraud. During their operations, whoever discovered a "client" first would act as the "matchmaker," while the other would play the role of the "potential bride." In half a year, they committed over 30 such crimes, swindling more than 80,000 yuan in cash. On November 8, when the two were committing another crime, it happened that a relative of the victim came to visit, witnessing the scene. Thus, the relative quietly dialed 110 to report the incident. It turned out that a few days ago, someone in the relative's village was defrauded of 3,000 yuan by these two women under the guise of selling cotton and introducing partners. On November 14, it was learned from the Shouguang police that the suspects had been legally placed under criminal detention. A strange occurrence indeed - a "potential bride" falling from the sky. Mr. Lu from Hualong Town in Shouguang City, who is already in his forties, has not married due to his family's poverty and slight stuttering. His marriage has become a major concern for his family. In May 2011, someone came to make a match for Mr. Lu. The "matchmaker" claimed her surname was Zhang and she was from Peiling Village in Hualong Town. Zhang told Mr. Lu's family that she and her aunt's younger sister were constantly going door-to-door selling cotton. Upon arriving at this village and hearing that Mr. Lu was still unmarried, she wanted to introduce him to a partner. Zhang said her aunt had a younger sister surnamed Li, aged 40, who got divorced two years ago. If Mr. Lu agreed, she could arrange a meeting. Soon after, Zhang made a phone call to bring Li, and Mr. Lu's family, finding Li suitable, agreed to the marriage proposal. Zhang then said, "Since today is a good day, let's finalize the engagement," and proceeded to have a middle-aged man deliver several large bags of cotton. Zhang told Mr. Lu that he did not need to prepare any dowry; just a few thousand yuan would suffice to make a few quilts and some clothes for Li. Consequently, Mr. Lu's mother took out three thousand yuan, wrapped it in red cloth, and handed it to the "future daughter-in-law" Li. Before leaving, Zhang also instructed Mr. Lu to tidy up his house, saying Li would come for dinner that evening. The scam was exposed when they attempted another fraud. After walking away, Mr. Lu never saw the face of his "future bride" again. The "good cotton" left by Zhang was merely some thread ends made from scraps of fabric mixed with some cotton linters. Selling cotton was a ruse; the real intent was matrimonial fraud. Zhang and Li staged their cotton-selling act to deceive older single men or elderly singles in rural areas, quickly driving away once they succeeded. On November 7, 2011, Zhang and Li returned to a certain village in Hualong Town, where they found a 40-year-old bachelor. They repeated their old tricks, swiftly gaining the man's trust. This man even handed over 5,000 yuan to Li on the spot. Just as the two thought they had succeeded again, things took a turn. It so happened that a relative of the man came to visit, seeing him give money to his "future bride." After understanding the situation, the relative had a clear idea and quietly dialed 110 to report the matter. Originally, a few days ago, someone in the relative's village was defrauded of 3,000 yuan in cash by two women selling cotton under the pretense of introducing partners. Upon receiving the report, officers from the Hualong Police Station of the Shouguang Public Security Bureau quickly arrived at the scene, controlling the two suspects and their driver. After being captured, Zhang and Li confessed to their crimes without reservation. All three are from Hualong Town in Shouguang City. According to Li's confession, since selling cotton did not yield much profit, she and Zhang decided to use matrimonial fraud to earn more money. Thus, Zhang hired a minivan, ostensibly to sell cotton but actually to commit matrimonial fraud everywhere. After succeeding, the two would take out a few hundred yuan from the obtained gifts to give to the driver. At every location, Zhang and Li would split up to find "clients." Whoever found a suitable person first would act as the "matchmaker," while the other would play the "single" role. If there was a suitable person but they were temporarily not at home, Zhang would note down the individual's name and address on paper, planning to return on another day. "Based on the confessions of the suspects, within half a year, they committed over 30 crimes, yet only four or five cases were reported to the public security bureau. The reason these criminals are so rampant is because they have clear targets, exploiting the victims' reluctance to publicize the matter. These two suspects are very cunning, specifically targeting older rural men who have been widowed for many years or have never married, taking advantage of the difficulty these men face in finding partners by actively approaching them, making it hard for the victims to guard against them. Each time they ask for a few hundred to a few thousand yuan, and once the victims become alert, they immediately vanish. Feeling old and fearing loss of face, the victims often choose to endure the losses rather than report to the police, inadvertently fueling the audacity of these criminals," explained Officer Wang Xiguang from the Criminal Investigation Team of the Shouguang Public Security Bureau. (Reported by Zhang Shaohua, Wu Hongxia, Wang Xiguang, Li Hongguo) @Police Reminder Officers remind relevant parties: First, do not easily believe matrimonial advertisements published in newspapers. For any newly acquainted opposite-sex individuals, always verify their ID cards. Second, before marriage registration, it is best to avoid financial transactions, especially large ones. Third, be cautious and vigilant with newly met, unfamiliar people, carefully identifying the promises and self-introductions given by the other party. If defrauded, preserve all related evidence materials and immediately report to the public security authorities to ensure legal punishment for the wrongdoers.
