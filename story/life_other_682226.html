What to do if the baby doesn't drink formula when mom goes to work?

All mothers want their babies to grow up healthily. However, some babies who are exclusively breastfed refuse to accept formula when their moms are about to go back to work, which makes the mothers very anxious. If this situation occurs, it is necessary to first identify the reasons and then develop specific solutions.

Increasing the baby's interest in formula

* Choose the right nipple. A nipple that resembles the mother's nipple can be selected, and a nipple with a slightly larger hole can be chosen to increase the flow of milk, making it easier for the baby to drink and naturally adapt to the bottle.

* Preserve breast milk in a bottle. During the baby's normal feeding time, the mother should step away and have someone else familiar to the baby feed him. The feeder can communicate with the baby using affectionate language, making it easier for the baby to accept.

* Proceed gradually. First, divide the baby's feeding times into three segments: morning, noon, and evening. Try during the middle segment, as the baby is more likely to accept new things at this time. Be prepared mentally for the baby's crying or even refusal to eat.

* You can try adding some rice soup to the formula or mixing the formula with porridge, which can change the taste of the formula. Feed the baby less but more frequently, gradually letting the baby adapt.

* Add complementary foods in a timely manner. By 4 months, the baby can start eating complementary foods, starting from rice powder, egg yolk, liver powder, fish powder, fruit puree, gradually moving on to vegetable and meat porridge, soft noodles, etc. Once the baby starts eating complementary foods, it gets better, as at least nutrients can be absorbed from other sources.

* Appropriately extend the feeding time, feeding the baby formula when the baby is slightly hungrier, to achieve the purpose of gradually accepting it.

Expert advice: Pay attention to the baby's emotions.

During the period of switching to formula feeding, mothers should often hug and caress the baby to reduce the anxiety caused by weaning.

Complementary food cannot replace milk. Do not over-supplement because the baby does not drink formula, as eating too much complementary food can affect milk intake.

Do not resort to random methods due to urgency. Babies have individual differences and innate temperaments. Methods that work for other babies may not necessarily work for your baby, so do not try all methods at once, nor rush to find a new method after one failure. Patience and guidance are key.
