Sina's Premier League Round Best XI: No Manchester United Players, Korean Wing Included  

In a tough away game against the highly potent Tottenham Hotspur, Rui Patricio made as many as seven saves, ultimately keeping a clean sheet. Notably, this was only the third league appearance of the season for the American goalkeeper. However, in these three matches, Wolves conceded only two goals, securing an impressive record of two wins and one loss. Who would have thought that Patricio is already 37 years old!

Defenders: Branislav Ivanovic (Chelsea), Sol Campbell (Portsmouth), Jody Craddock (Wolves), Maynor Figueroa (Wigan)

Despite being held to a draw by Everton at home, Chelsea's manager did not leave the game without some gains, particularly from right-back Branislav Ivanovic. Ivanovic’s two assists helped Didier Drogba and Nicolas Anelka score each a goal. With two assists, five key passes, and three successful tackles, Ivanovic has performed satisfactorily on both offense and defense after the injury of first-choice right-back Jose Bosingwa. In the 93rd minute, it seemed like another defeat awaited Portsmouth playing away until Campbell stepped up, scoring the equalizer that secured a crucial point for their relegation battle. Besides, Campbell contributed ten clearances as a central defender, earning him a perfect 9 out of 10 rating from Sky Sports. Regrettably, due to removing his shirt in celebration, Campbell received his second yellow card and was sent off just before the final whistle. The promotion team Wolverhampton Wanderers could secure three points at White Hart Lane largely thanks to captain Jody Craddock, who earned the highest match rating from both Sky Sports experts and netizens. This season, Craddock has scored four goals in the league, showing excellent abilities in both attack and defense. A stunning 60-meter direct free-kick shot by Maynor Figueroa resulted in a world-class goal that secured a point for Wigan Athletic in Stoke City’s dreaded home ground. Moreover, the 26-year-old Honduran left-back delivered one key pass during the game. He was awarded a 9 out of 10 rating from Sky Sports.

Midfielders: Lee Chung-yong (Bolton), Kevin Davies (Birmingham), Ashley Young (Aston Villa)

One assist and several brilliant passes later, Lee Chung-yong made his debut in the Sky Sports weekly best XI since joining the Premier League. The Sky expert gave him an 8 out of 10 rating, while fans rated him even higher at 9.1. Lee’s performance is critical for Bolton's survival in the Premier League. Once again, thanks to Kevin Davies’ last-minute winner, Birmingham City achieved their fourth consecutive victory in the Premier League. During this period, Davies has thrice provided the winning goal. Since Chinese owner Carson Yeung took over the club, Birmingham has won five and drawn two in their last seven rounds. The two draws were against strong teams like Liverpool and Manchester City. Now, Birmingham has climbed to eighth place in the Premier League standings, level with Liverpool. Aston Villa's away win over Manchester United for the first time in 26 years owes much to Ashley Young. His assist to Darren Bent was hailed by Sky Sports as the best pass of the week. With Steven Gerrard occupying the starting left midfield position for England, Ashley Young can compete for the substitute role.

Forwards: Carlos Tevez (Manchester City), Didier Drogba (Chelsea), Andrei Arshavin (Arsenal)

Following his last-gasp free-kick winner against Chelsea last round, Carlos Tevez once again shone in the away game against Bolton. In times of adversity, Tevez’s brace twice drew Manchester City level, marking his fourth goal in the last three matches across all competitions. Apart from the two goals, Tevez also created two key chances, made two successful tackles, and won two free-kicks. The Argentine's form has been improving recently. Despite Chelsea failing to secure all three points, Didier Drogba still managed a brace. His stellar performance might leave even the harshest critics speechless. This season, Drogba has scored 13 goals in the Premier League and 18 across all competitions. We must admit that Chelsea with or without Drogba is essentially two different teams. Although Arsenal's Andrei Arshavin wasn't very active throughout the game against Liverpool, stars are stars. With just one precise shot, Arshavin deserves a spot in our weekly best XI. At Anfield, the Tsar always delivers jaw-dropping performances. Remember the epic 4-4 clash back in April? Arshavin scored a hat-trick, silencing all doubters.
