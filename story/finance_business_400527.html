The popularity of purchasing glasses online has been growing rapidly, showing a significant price advantage and convenience that has attracted the masses. As is well known, buying contact lenses online is straightforward; one only needs to input their prescription details to purchase easily. However, buying eyeglasses can seem more complicated. This article will provide an in-depth introduction to the detailed steps involved in purchasing glasses online, allowing consumers to understand the process, appreciate the meticulous craftsmanship involved in assembling glasses, and highlight the recent "high-end" online try-on feature that builds trust with customers. For this article, we'll use Kede.com as an example (Kede.com is China's largest eyewear mall and has been the top seller for the last three years).

Online Eyewear Purchase Manual

The Emergence of Eyewear B2C Ends the Era of Excessive Profits

With the rapid development of the internet industry, consumer attitudes are undergoing tremendous changes. A sales model that seems capable of altering the excessive profit margins of eyewear has quietly emerged - eyewear B2C (Business to Customer) websites. This is a model where eyewear merchants bypass intermediaries to sell directly to end consumers. By facing the audience directly, this simplification of the sales channel allows merchants to offer glasses at very low prices, which are reasonable production costs plus a certain profit margin. In essence, it returns the excessive profits of middle distributors directly to the consumers.

Under this model, a pair of glasses that is priced exorbitantly in physical stores can be purchased by consumers at a fraction of the market price, making quality eyewear truly a daily necessity rather than a product of excessive profit.

Therefore, during this process, utilizing the internet, this fast and convenient new media, to purchase satisfactory and reasonably priced glasses becomes particularly important.

Eight Steps to Purchasing Glasses Online

1. Registration.

When shopping on online eyewear malls, you first need to register as a member to accumulate points and enjoy more member discount benefits. For instance, on Kede.com, the largest online eyewear mall, different levels of members receive different discount offers.

2. Upload Prescription. (Skip for sunglasses and contact lenses.)

Glasses are standardized products; all information from the frame, lenses to your prescription and pupil distance can be represented digitally. Therefore, it is entirely possible to purchase glasses online that fit your size and prescription accurately.

Generally, you can get free eye exams at physical eyewear stores. When ordering glasses, request a copy of your prescription from the store, note down the appropriate size of your glasses, and know the basic parameters of the glasses you are ordering. Then log into the online mall and upload your prescription.

*Appendix: Maintenance of Eyeglass Frames

Different types of eyeglass frames require different maintenance methods mainly because the materials of the frames vary, so they cannot be treated uniformly with one cleaning or maintenance method.

・Natural material frames, such as tortoiseshell, should not be cleaned with ultrasonic cleaners commonly found in eyewear stores, as it can cause the tortoiseshell to heat up, deform, and break.

・Plastic or acetate frames should not be exposed to direct sunlight for long periods, as they may deform or break. Do not soak them in hot water, as heat can cause deformation.

・Metal frames can cause allergies or be corroded by sweat secreted from the skin, so attention must be paid to this point in daily use.

Common Sense for Purchasing Contact Lenses

Choosing a good pair of contact lenses requires thorough checks and proper fitting, along with personal judgment. Here are some tips for those who wear contact lenses:

1. Although contact lenses are now quite common, they are not suitable for everyone. People with conjunctivitis or keratitis, for example, cannot use them. Therefore, a doctor's examination and treatment are necessary before deciding to adopt contact lenses.

2. Buying a pair of contact lenses and related care products involves considerable expenses. While saving money is desirable, vision correction is paramount, so do not compromise on quality for small savings.

3. Contact lenses are not everyday items but medical devices, so regular check-ups are necessary. If any abnormal symptoms occur, consult an ophthalmologist immediately.

4. During the initial wearing period, there may be a slight sliding sensation, so it takes time to adapt.

5. Small Knowledge About Contact Lenses: Water Content - The percentage of water in the total weight of the contact lens material after it has absorbed water to saturation.

Note: People whose eyes do not dry out are more suited to high water content contact lenses, while people with dry eyes should use low water content ones. High water content contact lenses do not produce water themselves; they are sponge-like polymer structures that absorb water from the eyeball.
