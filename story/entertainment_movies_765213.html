1. Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone (2001)

Harry Potter has spent his entire life living under the stairs at his aunt and uncle’s house, enduring years of neglect and cruelty. However, on his eleventh birthday, Harry learns the truth about his past and his life changes forever. His parents were kind wizards who were killed in a battle with an evil force. Determined to follow in their footsteps, Harry arrives at Hogwarts, a British boarding school dedicated to teaching magic and witchcraft.

Upon entering Hogwarts, Harry becomes a first-year student in Gryffindor House, quickly forming close friendships with Ron and Hermione. He faces many magical courses waiting for him to study, including flying lessons, defense against the dark arts, potions, and transfiguration. Of course, there is also Quidditch, the sport that drives all wizards wild. In a heated argument with classmates, Harry displays an unexpected flying skill, surprising even himself. Professor McGonagall, who teaches flying techniques, recommends him to join the Gryffindor Quidditch team. On the other hand, Professor Snape, who teaches potions, seems unfriendly towards Harry, not only making things difficult for him in class but also constantly finding trouble for Harry. However, Harry discovers something alarming during a chance encounter: Professor Snape sternly threatens the timid Professor Quirrell, and the bite marks from a three-headed dog on Snape’s leg seem to confirm Harry's suspicions: an evil conspiracy is quietly growing within the peaceful Hogwarts, and Snape appears to be the key figure behind it all.

Determined to uncover the truth and prevent the evil plot, Harry, Ron, and Hermione decide to investigate together. Will these three first-year Gryffindor students be able to stop this mysterious evil event? Who is the mastermind behind it all? Can Harry survive his first year of schooling safely...?

2. Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (2002)

In the blink of an eye, Harry’s first year at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry has come to an end. For Harry, leaving this newly familiar magical world and returning to the Dursleys' home on Privet Drive is the most painful thing. He must endure another summer filled with the constant nagging and supervision of his mean Aunt Petunia and overbearing Uncle Vernon.

Perhaps out of fear of Harry's magical abilities, the Dursleys hardly troubled him throughout the summer, except forbidding him from doing his magical homework. But Harry still feels distressed because it seems as though his two best friends at Hogwarts—Hermione and Ron—have forgotten about him. The three friends had agreed to keep in touch by writing letters during the summer, but despite Harry sending countless letters to them, he hasn't received a single reply yet.

Just as the holiday was about to end, a small house-elf named Dobby suddenly appeared in Harry's bedroom. It warned Harry that terrible things would happen if he returned to Hogwarts—he might even face life-threatening danger! To protect Harry's life, Dobby intercepted all the letters from Hermione and Ron—thinking that this way, Harry would become disappointed with his friends and choose not to return to Hogwarts. However, Harry insists on going back to Hogwarts. To stop him, Dobby even used magic in the Muggle world, causing chaos in the Dursley household. As a result, Harry missed the Hogwarts Express. Just when he was feeling anxious, his friend Ron arrived in a magically enchanted flying car to rescue him. After much effort, they finally managed to make it back to the school just in time for the start-of-term feast.

By now, Harry's heroic act of defeating Voldemort last semester has spread widely throughout Hogwarts and the entire magical world. Suddenly, Harry becomes the center of attention. Every little move he makes can become a topic of discussion among people. Unfortunately, the new Defense Against the Dark Arts professor, Gilderoy Lockhart (Kenneth Branagh), is also someone who loves to be in the spotlight. He enjoys subtly challenging Harry and then portraying himself as the true hero of Hogwarts.

Sure enough, just as the house-elf Dobby predicted, not long after the start of the new term, terrifying and mysterious incidents began happening frequently at Hogwarts—several students and the caretaker's cat were attacked by some terrifying power and turned to stone. Moreover, Harry kept hearing a ghostly voice telling him: "The Chamber of Secrets has been opened, and the heir of the evil force will take revenge on the mudbloods (students whose parents are not wizards) in the school..."

For a while, Hogwarts was filled with panic. Since Harry could understand the language of snakes, he was suspected of being the heir of the legendary evil force. Even his friends around him began to question Harry. Only Hermione and Ron continued to trust him completely. One day, however, Hermione herself became a victim of the mysterious attack. The always boastful Professor Lockhart revealed his cowardly nature and retreated. Only Ron stood firmly by Harry's side against the evil force. Harry knew that to protect his beloved Hogwarts, save his dear friend who shares life and death with him, and clear the various doubts placed upon him, he must take action. But who exactly is the enemy he faces? Where is he hiding? And what is his purpose? Harry finds himself surrounded by a thick fog of mystery...

3. Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (2004)

Harry encounters the real culprit who killed his parents…

Spending the summer at his aunt's house is truly a terrible experience. Not only is Harry Potter separated from his friends, but he also has to endure daily torment from the Muggles. Until his thirteenth birthday, everything changed completely. Early that morning, Harry received birthday gifts from his friends Hermione and Ron, which made him very happy. Coincidentally, starting that day, Aunt Marge was staying for a week, and she angered Harry so much that he accidentally turned her into a balloon and sent her flying into the sky.

Harry fled from his aunt's house and found himself walking alone on the midnight street when he saw a terrifying black dog. Fortunately, a Knight Bus from the magical world arrived just in time, rescuing Harry and bringing him to London. In London, Harry enjoyed a period of carefree happiness. He not only encountered many fascinating magical items but also completed his essay. Strangely, he also came across a book titled "Death Omens," whose cover featured the black dog he had seen the night he left his aunt's house. A sense of foreboding made Harry feel that something terrible might happen.
