Why Kahn Lost to a Woman

by ereia0354 on 2011-05-25 17:16:59

Hailed as France's most successful economic minister and also known as Mr. Euro, Dominique Strauss-Kahn is leading the reform of the International Monetary Fund (IMF), helping more emerging market economies integrate into world affairs. His talents and achievements are difficult to summarize in a few sentences.

Kahn graduated from the Paris Institute of Political Studies and the French Higher Business School, known as the cradle of French social elites, obtaining degrees in law and economics. However, this individual who is well-versed in legal knowledge and enjoys prestigious status in both the economic and political circles became, within just a few minutes on the afternoon of May 14, a rapist in the eyes of the US police.

The trial for attempted rape has just begun, yet Kahn's political future and reputation can never be restored. On May 17 local time, Kahn attended his first hearing at the Manhattan Criminal Court in New York. The first round of questioning did not go smoothly; Leder believed that Kahn had a risk of fleeing and rejected the acceptance of a $1 million bail, prohibiting him from being bailed out. It was announced that there will be a second questioning on May 20 local time, during which he will continue to be detained in prison.

Kahn has been charged with multiple offenses including criminal sexual behavior, attempted rape, and illegal confinement. On the evening of May 15, Kahn underwent medical examination at the request of investigators. The court searched Kahn's clothing to check for scratches and other evidence, while extracting DNA samples to compare with those of the female housekeeper.

Since ancient times, emotions have always brought harm. Regardless of whether this sexual harassment scandal is true or not, Kahn's past records indicate that between his career and beautiful women, he finds it hard to control himself. On May 14, after being intercepted by the New York police on the plane, Kahn's previous romantic affairs were exposed one by one. In 2008, Kahn was investigated for having an extramarital affair with a Hungarian female economist subordinate. Yesterday, another revelation emerged: 31-year-old French novelist Tristane Banon claimed that she was sexually assaulted by Kahn nine years ago and may file an appeal.

However, regarding the accusation that Kahn attempted to rape a 32-year-old black housemaid at the Sofitel Hotel where he stayed, his wife Anne Sinclair said, "I have no doubt that he will be proven innocent."

According to RMC radio in France on May 16, the lawyers clarified Kahn's activities on the day in question, discovering that he left the hotel at noon, having already checked out and returned the key, then dined with his daughter, and subsequently took a taxi to the airport. This timeline indicates that when the housekeeper claimed that Kahn chased her along the corridor, pushed her into a room, and assaulted her, he had already left the hotel. The radio station reported that the lawyers had substantial evidence and witnesses.

New-style political assassination?

After the exposure of the Kahn sex scandal, people naturally linked the incident to a political trap. Kahn studied law in university, is a successful economist, and even has the potential to become a future leader of France. How could such a renowned high-IQ strategist chase a woman under surveillance cameras and attempt to rape her? The French media generally believes that Kahn is a victim of a conspiracy.

Who might have set this trap? It could come from within the IMF, or from the French right-wing or left-wing, or perhaps it's a new trick by the Americans. Michele Sabban, who is close to Kahn and serves as the Vice President of the Regional Council of Île-de-France, believes this is an international conspiracy. She is convinced that someone wants to get rid of the head of the IMF. In her view, Kahn is the most capable person after Obama. As proof, Mrs. Sabban complained that it is well-known that his weakness is temptation and women, and they want to use this against him.

Even if Kahn is found innocent, he cannot become a candidate in the Socialist Party presidential primary and cannot continue serving in the IMF, because he has become deeply entangled with a sex scandal. This is a major taboo for political candidates and an indelible piece of evidence. Some French officials stated that it is not impossible that Kahn's arrest on charges of sexual assault is a politically motivated trap. Kahn may be forced to withdraw from next year's French presidential election, greatly increasing Sarkozy's chances of re-election.

Prominent figures embroiled in sex scandals

The Kahn incident raises questions: does power make him feel invincible, above the law? Georgetown University Associate Professor Michelle Swers believes that the intertwining of politics, power, and sexual harassment has a long history. The severity of this attempted rape case differs from previous ones. On May 16, German Finance Minister Wolfgang Schäuble expressed that unless there is new evidence proving Kahn's guilt, his charges should be presumed not guilty.

In fact, examples abound of individuals standing at the pinnacle of power falling due to sex scandals. However, leaders like Kahn accused of violent rape are extremely rare in world history. What we are accustomed to accepting is consensual relationships.

The prostitution scandal involving Italian Prime Minister Berlusconi has never ceased, former U.S. President Clinton, former New York Governor Spitzer, and the top contender for the 2012 U.S. Presidential Election, Newt Gingrich, all struggled with their attraction to beautiful women. The most infamous scandal was the Profumo Affair in Britain in 1963, where the Army Minister had an affair with a prostitute who also had ties with a Soviet spy, eventually forcing him to resign.

From time to time, these personal affairs of prominent figures are prominently displayed on the front pages of major media outlets worldwide. While people discuss them over tea and snacks, they also ponder the privileges granted by power to certain individuals.
