Not your good child_4014

by valw92 on 2012-02-23 11:13:12

http://www.niugu8.com I won't be your good child anymore. Please believe, I am really not a good child. -- Preface

I often seriously tell Heihei on my way home: "I am really a good child," then laugh out loud with a sunny smile, and listen to her rhythmic reply: "Yes, I am the bad child. When someone wants to eat ice cream, I will accompany them; when someone bullies me, I don't fight back; someone..." Listening to her words, I learned to smile quietly because the person she referred to was me. Xiao Xi once seriously frowned at me and said: "You are fundamentally and forever not a truly good child. A good child wouldn't live in loneliness and sorrow. Good children always live in a bright, sunny world, living simply and happily. Jie, give up this kind of life now and integrate into a brighter world." I thought about how I had sincerely written in my reading notes: it's time to put an end to this pain. Then I silently thought in my heart: it's also time to put an end to endless loneliness and start a new journey. I heard the sound of my heart breaking and the echoes repeatedly reverberating in my heart.

At home, I am the good child for my parents and elders; at school, I am a good friend to my classmates and a good child to my teachers. But secretly, I wipe my tears all over my face. I often feel like a clown, painting heavy and strong oil paints, bringing happiness to others in this false happiness, becoming numb and feeling happy myself, then stepping off the stage, seeing my sad and lonely face after my tears and sweat have washed away the oil paint.

I really like Si Wei's article "Overcast" in "Left Hand Reflection, Right Hand Youth." His opening almost burned my eyes. He wrote: I am a good child who looks up at the sky on an overcast day, I am really a good child. Si Wei said there is a kind of weather called overcast. In the overcast weather, there is a feeling called loneliness. In the lonely overcast weather, there is always a obedient good child looking up at the sky foolishly, at that lead-gray sky full of lonely clouds. I know the good child looking up at the sky is him, but I am a child completely different from everyone else.

I often think Heihei should be a standard good child, bullied by me, listening attentively in class, accompanying me when I want to eat ice cream, not coming home late, calling her parents when something happens, drinking very mild green tea, laughing without making much noise, not getting angry even when teased multiple times for being dark-skinned, while I am completely opposite to her. I am very noisy, very mischievous, love to laugh loudly, drink very bitter coffee or very stimulating cola, read very lonely texts, write melancholy essays, wear pure black coats, come home late without calling, complain frequently, very unreasonable, dislike studying and shopping, only like being spoiled and loved. In fact, I am really not a good child, but in the eyes of my parents, elders, teachers, classmates, and those who meet me briefly, I really am, a good child.

I try hard to wear a false exterior, then learn to be a well-behaved and obedient good child. I write very formulaic, sentimental essays, wear very neat red and white school uniforms, button up the top button of my shirt, also learn to drink mild green tea or jasmine tea, learn to stand with my legs together, say hello, sorry, goodbye, thank you anytime, wear black-framed glasses to listen to lectures, frown and study problems, browse the Internet for IELTS or TOEFL exams. I really did it, as well as any good child.

But in the deep, dark night, I write very insignificant, humble words, deeply engraved in my heart. Then sleep, and tomorrow still be my good child. I often become a better child in the process of repeated shaking and retreating. Actually, I should have realized long ago that I have fallen into a pattern, a painful pattern that entangles me.

Xiao Ai once said something to me, similar to what H said years ago: "Jie, just be yourself! You are getting less and less happy. I see unprecedented pain and resilience on your fake face." People say that those with nasolabial folds represent resilient pain. My nasolabial folds aren't deep, but I am still in pain, still resilient. I remember how H looked at me years ago, saying seriously: "Jie, you need to be yourself. No matter what you do, even if you cause trouble every day or feel sad every day, I will be by your side." I remember how I cried my eyes out, messily, and later they described the scene as magnificent. But years later, when Xiao Ai said similar things to me, I didn't feel even a bit of sadness.

I remember a passage from "Chinese Paladin": roughly speaking, at her mother's funeral, she didn't shed a single tear. Others said she was heartless, but I knew she was too deeply hurt. I think I am the same, too deeply numb to even squeeze out tears. I have a large group of friends, but I am still lonely. Xiao Chen said: "Lonely people are all good children." I am lonely and alone, so I am a good child. Because lonely people are alone, no one indulges their mischief, coquetry, tears, or tantrums, so they can only hug their knees and curl up in a corner, living quietly. If it's cold, hug your legs tighter. I like this posture, so I pretend to be a well-behaved child.

"I am a reserved, quiet, and somewhat shy child," when I wrote this sentence, I remember everyone laughed at my impracticality, and I remember my pale face and painful smile. At that moment, I suddenly remembered what Luo Zhi Xiang said in an interview: "Although I seem funny on the surface, I am actually a very shy person." My heart ached painfully.
