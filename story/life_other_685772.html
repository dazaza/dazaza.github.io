Rescue operation for a tanker truck carrying 12 tons of liquid oxygen that rolled over, contact number 18671079366.

by xfjndf on 2012-03-01 16:56:06

On February 29, at the scene of the tanker rollover accident (photographed by Wang Chuanyuan), Sichuan News Network reported on March 1 from Panzhihua (written and photographed by Wang Chuanyuan and Sun Xiaolong).

At 20:27 on February 29, a tanker carrying 12 tons of liquid oxygen overturned at a turning point at the Wanli Bridge intersection in Zaozi Ping, Dong District, Panzhihua City. One driver was unhurt, while one escort officer was injured with a dislocated right hand but no life-threatening injuries. After the incident, it took more than seven hours of emergency rescue efforts by the Panzhihua fire department, public security bureau, safety supervision departments, and others. By 3:30 AM on March 1, the danger was successfully eliminated. Due to proper handling of the accident, there was no leakage or explosion, preventing a serious incident.

At around 9 PM, when the reporter arrived at the scene, at the turning point of the Wanli Bridge intersection in Zaozi Ping, a tanker with license plate Sichuan D36000 was overturned by the roadside. Several meters of iron guardrails were broken, and the corner of the adjacent Unity Community residential building was severely damaged. The seats inside the liquid oxygen tanker cab had shifted, and the front windshield was completely shattered and thrown outside the vehicle. The tires on the right side of the tanker had burst, and the road surface showed several cracks.

The reporter observed that the middle part of the tank body had been deformed, and the fuel tank had leaked, filling the scene with a strong smell of diesel. Firefighters had already set up warning lines around the area, and traffic control was implemented at the accident site. The dispatch command center of Panzhihua's 119 sent seven fire trucks and over forty personnel from the First, Sixth, and Special Service Detachments, along with the public security and safety supervision departments for on-site rescue operations.

A firefighter said that the accident occurred around 8:20 PM, when they arrived at the scene, the driver and escort officer had already crawled out of the cab, both sustaining minor injuries. It was reported that the tanker contained 12 tons of liquid oxygen.

Firefighting Chief Counselor Dai Zuguang stated that although liquid oxygen leakage would not cause pollution, liquid oxygen can strongly assist combustion, classified as a Class B fire hazard. Under normal pressure, when the oxygen concentration exceeds 40%, it may cause oxygen poisoning, and static electricity could lead to burning or even explosions. Additionally, liquid oxygen has a temperature of -180℃, which can cause frostbite upon contact with human skin. To prevent further escalation of the situation, firefighters immediately established a restricted area, prohibiting irrelevant individuals from entering, banning all fire sources, evacuating over 100 residents and surrounding people, and wearing personal protective equipment while using water spray guns to protect the scene.

Meanwhile, technical engineers from the oxygen factory inspected the tank body, using oxygen concentration detectors to monitor the entire process. They also mobilized an empty tank truck of approximately 13 tons from the factory to perform the transfer operation. Workers connected the two tankers with pipes. By 3:30 AM on March 1, all the liquid oxygen from the accident tanker was transferred into another tanker. Under the protection of the firefighters, two cranes successfully lifted the overturned tanker back onto the road, successfully eliminating the danger.

As of March 1, under the cover of firefighters, the overturned tanker was successfully lifted.

On February 29, engineering technicians tested the oxygen concentration (photographed by Wang Chuanyuan).

Retired worker Aunt Xiang Rongxian, who lives on the third floor, described the scene during the incident, still shaken.

She said that she heard a "thunderous roar," causing the entire building to shake. The sound of flying iron railings lasted over ten seconds. She and her family thought it was an earthquake and rushed downstairs, hearing shouts of "the car flipped, the car flipped." Upon reaching the ground, they saw the overturned liquid oxygen tanker at their doorstep, frightening Aunt Xiang so much that she trembled and dared not enter her home for a long time.

The cause of the accident: brake failure led to the tanker overturning.

At the scene, the reporter found Mr. Hu, the driver of the accident tanker. Mr. Hu said his tanker was registered under the Aoda Logistics Company and carried 12 tons of liquid oxygen. He departed from the Messer company around 8 PM that evening. "The incident happened too suddenly! The car seemed to lose control all of a sudden!" Mr. Hu recounted that before the accident, his vehicle was functioning normally. While driving downhill on the Zaozi Ping Wanli Bridge section, he felt the speed was getting too fast and losing control. He quickly slammed on the brakes, but they failed. In a panic, he turned the steering wheel sharply to the right at the intersection. Unexpectedly, the vehicle skidded sideways for over ten meters, lost balance, and overturned to the left, breaking the iron guardrails beside the road with a "thunderous crash" and overturning by the roadside. He and the other escort officer climbed out through the shattered front windshield of the cab. The escort officer was injured with a dislocated right hand and was sent to the hospital for treatment. Fortunately, the tanker did not collide with any other vehicles or pedestrians.

Currently, relevant departments are conducting further investigations into the cause of the accident.
