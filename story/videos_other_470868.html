USD/JPY:

This morning's intervention was carried out by Japan's Ministry of Finance, not the Bank of Japan, which began its meeting at 10:00. Therefore, the market should maintain some patience regarding this matter. The USD/JPY pair may push towards 80, so investors who went long during the early session can continue to hold their positions. However, in the overall trend, the Bank of Japan might not be able to counteract the market, so holding for the long term is not advisable.

EUR/USD:

The euro performed strongly yesterday, with better-than-expected June retail sales figures both monthly and annually, along with an overall positive July services purchasing managers' index (PMI). Improved economic conditions were favorable for the euro, driving it to a V-shaped rebound yesterday. Today, the European Central Bank will hold its interest rate meeting, and the market expects no interest rate hike, so a significant rise in the euro today is unlikely.

Regarding European debt, although Cyprus has requested assistance, its small economy limits its impact on the market. Additionally, Italian Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi stated that Italy possesses solid economic and financial system fundamentals, pushing the euro higher again. Due to the upcoming U.S. non-farm payroll data on Friday, the market may not immediately break through the key level of 1.4400 effectively. It is suggested to take profits at high levels and wait for the non-farm payroll data.

GBP/USD:

The UK's Services Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) for July rose to 55.4, the highest since March, exceeding the forecast of 53.2; June's figure was 53.9. Better-than-expected data supported a significant strengthening of the pound. However, a single good data point is insufficient to sustain the overall trend of the pound. Furthermore, today's Bank of England interest rate decision needs attention. An interest rate hike is unlikely today, but the critical factor is whether the quantitative easing scale will be expanded, which will be a turning point for whether the pound moves higher.

Today, there is a possibility of testing the previous high of 1.6475. If it cannot break through effectively, there is a tendency to go short on the pound. However, caution is needed before the release of the non-farm payroll data, and today's Bank of England interest rate decision must also be watched. The expected trading range for today is 1.6200-1.6500.
