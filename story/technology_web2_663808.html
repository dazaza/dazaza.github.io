New Policies for Applying for Affordable Housing in Zhengzhou: Stricter Checks on "Family Net Assets"

On February 8, 2012, Dàyù Network reported that new policies have been introduced for applying for affordable housing in Zhengzhou, focusing on stricter checks of "family net assets." The report garnered significant attention and comments from the public.

#### Key Points:

- **Interconnected Systems:** The Housing Administration Bureau has networked with departments such as the Vehicle Management Office and banks to review applicants' vehicles, deposits, stocks, and other forms of "family net assets."

- **Economic Housing (Jingji Fang):** Applicants must have per capita family assets not exceeding 100,000 yuan.

- **Low-Rent Housing (Lianzu Fang):** Applicants must have per capita family assets not exceeding 60,000 yuan.

#### Background

To prevent situations where individuals own luxury cars like BMWs yet still qualify for subsidized housing, Zhengzhou has implemented a new policy requiring reviews of applicants' family net assets. This measure aims to ensure fair distribution of affordable housing resources.

#### New Standards

The new standards primarily verify non-residential assets of applying families to more comprehensively reflect their economic situation. According to the new policy:

- For Economic Housing: Per capita family assets should not exceed 100,000 yuan.

- For Low-Rent Housing: Per capita family assets should not exceed 60,000 yuan.

#### Policy A: Verification of "Family Net Assets"

"The most notable point of the new policy is the addition of a standard to check the net assets of families applying for affordable housing," said Feng Rongzhi, Director of Planning and Housing Security at Zhengzhou's Housing Bureau. Family net assets refer to income sources such as deposits, vehicles, and securities outside of residential properties.

The upper limit of family net assets is generally determined by six times the annual household income corresponding to different types of subsidies. For example, for low-rent housing, the per capita family asset is calculated as 340 yuan (urban resident minimum living standard) × 2.5 × 12 months × 6 = 60,000 yuan. For economic housing, the per capita family asset is capped at 100,000 yuan.

#### Policy B: Introduction of Price-Capped Commodities Housing

Feng Rongzhi mentioned that to address the housing needs of the urban "sandwich class" (middle-to-low-income earners), Zhengzhou plans to introduce price-capped commodities housing this year. These houses will mainly be encouraged in remote industrial parks, urban clusters, and counties or cities where housing prices are rising too fast.

#### Policy C: Public Rental Housing - "For Rent Only, Not for Sale"

This year, Zhengzhou plans to build 22,000 units of public rental housing, surpassing the number of economic housing units for the first time. The government encourages large and medium-sized enterprises, hospitals, schools, and other public welfare institutions to construct public rental housing. These entities can use their own land to solve employee housing difficulties under a registration and filing system.

Public rental housing constructed by enterprises, schools, and hospitals cannot be sold but can only be rented out. This measure aims to prevent some units from covertly developing and selling ordinary commercial housing under the guise of public rental housing.

#### Verification Methods for "Family Net Assets"

Zhengzhou aims to establish an effective method for verifying family net assets by June. The verification includes checking bank deposits, land and property, vehicles, investment assets, and collectibles. Families found owning expensive vehicles through installment payments may be disqualified from applying for affordable housing.

In summary, these new policies aim to ensure fairness in the allocation of affordable housing by thoroughly reviewing applicants' financial statuses, including their net assets beyond just income and housing conditions. This comprehensive approach seeks to address the housing challenges faced by different income groups in Zhengzhou effectively.
