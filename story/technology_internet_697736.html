So-called communism simply put is distribution according to need. Distribution according to need can be giving 100% of the money based on individual needs, a situation that cannot yet be realized now, but subsidies according to a certain ratio are still possible.

Subsidies are a form of government behavior, not only including subsidies from central and local governments, but also including subsidies from private institutions intervened by the government. In addition, subsidies are a fiscal action: that is, there are expenditures in the government's public account. Subsidies are not easy for ordinary people to obtain, with complicated procedures and difficulties in implementation, such as affordable housing. So, does non-governmental commercial subsidies exist? The answer is yes.
