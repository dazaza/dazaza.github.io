Thromboangiitis obliterans (Buerger's disease) is an allergic inflammatory condition that occurs in blood vessels, causing segmental narrowing and occlusion of medium and small arteries. This leads to a loss of nutrition in the extremities, resulting in ulcers and necrosis. It is a rather stubborn vascular disease, representing an inflammatory manifestation of the vascular wall itself, and it is unrelated to bacterial infections.

Research has found that smoking is one of the important initiating factors for thromboangiitis obliterans. Nicotine in tobacco is a vasoconstrictor, and after smoking, it can cause skin vessel constriction, slow blood flow, and a significant drop in finger or toe skin temperature.
