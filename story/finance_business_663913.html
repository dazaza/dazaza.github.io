In front of the old school building of Chongwen School

by xunning1350 on 2012-02-12 21:18:53

The founder of Yantai Chongwen School, Song Renping, was originally a private teacher. Due to a dream he had while visiting relatives, he eventually spent all his life savings and half of his lifetime's energy to establish the Yantai Chongwen School.

For others, inheriting a family business usually means acquiring substantial wealth. However, for Song Shaoguang, it meant inheriting a large debt, a heavy responsibility, and his father's deep regret.

Chongwen School does not evoke pleasant memories in Song Shaoguang's childhood. After his father ventured into business, their family life became very affluent. Song Shaoguang said that as a child, he never ate candy. But after his father started the school, all the family's money was invested into it, and they had to borrow from everywhere. In 1999, after graduating from high school, Song Shaoguang could have attended university, but his father, Song Renping, asked him to work first to earn money. Thus, he and his sister earned money by working to support the family, and any surplus went to Chongwen School. Two years later, when the school's finances improved slightly, Song Shaoguang took the college entrance exam and was admitted to Ludong University's Chinese Department.

Seeing the school buses, Song Shaoguang's eyes were filled with sorrow. He told the reporter that these three vehicles had been in operation for 10 years. According to relevant regulations, as school buses, they must "retire." Currently, the school no longer provides school buses, and hundreds of students can only squeeze onto public buses or wait for their parents to pick them up. Worrisome is that some younger grade students are too young, and waiting for buses alone is unsafe. But their grandparents are migrant workers from other places who usually work late, making it inconvenient for them to pick up students after school.

"Several years ago, the Price Bureau set our fee standard at 600 yuan per semester. Considering the financial situation of the students' families, we finally set it at 460 yuan per year. Families with better conditions still receive a 50% reduction, and particularly poor students are completely exempted," said Vice Principal Zhao in charge of schooling. Due to reductions and exemptions, the school's finances are always extremely difficult at the end of each year. "Many of our teachers request temporary non-payment of salaries at the end of the year because we know the school lacks funds. The school has the most money when tuition fees are collected, so we all wait until then to get paid," Vice Principal Zhao said with a smile.

What comforts Song Shaoguang is that, as the school's reputation grows, the number of students increases every year. In previous years, there was an increase of one or two hundred students, and in recent years, even an increase of three or four hundred students annually. With more students, the school's financial situation gradually improves. He hopes for more investment so that the students can enjoy better education.

Song Shaoguang: Male, Han nationality, from Qixia, Shandong Province. Originally a science major, he graduated from the Chinese Department of Ludong University (formerly Yantai Normal College) in 2000. "I will strive to follow in my father's footsteps and dedicate my entire life to providing a blue sky for the children of migrant workers!"

Through Principal Song's narration, the reporter gradually learned about the 15-year history of founding Chongwen School. One could say that today's dream of thousands of migrant worker children attending school is the result of a retired private teacher's dream.

In 2004, with the increase in migrant workers coming to Yantai, the number of students at Chongwen School also increased significantly, and the original campus could no longer meet the needs of the students. Having a teaching building of their own became Lao Song Renping's greatest wish.

On the afternoon of January 4th, the students of Yantai Chongwen School were in class, and the reporter visited this school. The melodious reading sounds came from the beautiful teaching building, and the school's 15th anniversary photos hung on the bulletin board on the first floor. In front of the teaching building, there were three already "retired" school buses lined up.

"Now that the winter vacation is approaching, we have explained the situation to the parents, and they understand. But after the New Year, it will be the start of the new semester. If we can't raise the funds, hundreds of children will have to挤公交车for a whole year, which is too dangerous," said Principal Zhao, who has worked at Chongwen School for over ten years, with a worried look on his face. "We want to buy three school buses, but each costs over 500,000 yuan. It's impossible to gather over a million yuan in such a short time." Principal Song said that he hopes that a company can sponsor or lend the school part of the money, or that the bank can provide a loan for the school to buy the buses, which they will repay in installments later.

The former principal once gave up going to university for the school; now he wants to give the students better education.

"In 1994, it was a turning point in my father's life. He went to visit relatives near Xingfu Jiu Village and saw many children playing on the street. Later, he found out that these children's parents were migrant workers from other places who couldn't afford the additional tuition fees, and suburban schools were unwilling to accept these children," Song Shaoguang said. His father returned home in silence and had a dream that night, dreaming of building a school in Xingfu Jiu Village where children happily studied.

As time passed, and especially feeling the children's thirst for knowledge at Chongwen School, Song Shaoguang gradually understood his father and became his biggest supporter.

"The hardest times were when they had no rice, flour, or vegetables for the New Year, and the whole family just sat on the kang crying," Vice Principal Zhao said. The elder principal devoted everything to the school. In 2004, due to excessive fatigue, the old principal passed away, and his son Song Shaoguang became the new principal of Yantai Chongwen School.

The school operates as a social force-run institution, offering primary education and nine years of compulsory education from grades 1 to 9. Its main focus is on the children of migrant workers. It is the only special private school in Yantai that unconditionally accepts the children of migrant workers. The school's motto is "Facing the entire society, facing ordinary people, facing future generations, facing the country's future." The school implements the principle of "low tuition fees, strict management" and has the school motto of "Jointly supporting the growth of children under the blue sky." It has received multiple honors such as "Municipal Class One School" in Yantai City, "Class One Private Education School" in Zhifu District, "Advanced Position in Private Education Work," "Advanced Unit in Social Force Education Work," and "Demonstration School of Honest Education."

It is understood that in 15 years, Yantai Chongwen School has cumulatively reduced tuition fees by over 1.3 million yuan. The school unconditionally accepts the children of local migrant workers, and currently, thousands of students have graduated from Chongwen School.

"Coming here to teach, the salary is a bit low, but I feel very fulfilled inside, doing a good deed!" A teacher smiled and said she liked it here. Even with a low salary, she loves the children here.

Yantai Daily reported on January 17th that this January, Principal Song of Yantai Chongwen School sought help from the newspaper. This migrant worker children's school, which has "started from scratch" in Yantai for 15 years, is now facing a dilemma. Because the school buses have been in operation for 10 years and cannot continue according to regulations, and the school cannot afford to purchase new buses, Principal Song hopes to receive societal assistance, even borrowing money to help them through this difficulty. They plan to repay the loans later so that students can ride the school bus in the new academic year.

"Let every migrant worker child enjoy equal education like city children." This is the ideal of both Song Renping and Song Shaoguang.

Chongwen School relies on societal forces for its operations. The school's income comes from two parts: student tuition and societal aid and donations. Over the years, moved by the Song family, many enterprises and citizens have extended a helping hand to Chongwen School. However, the school's most important source of income remains the students' tuition fees, and the school often reduces these fees.

Borrowing money to buy school buses might seem unrealistic to many. However, Principal Song told reporters that since his father used their family savings to establish the school, the school's development to today mostly relied on loans taken by his father and himself. In recent years, due to an increase in students, the school has seen profits, and he originally planned to save for a year or two before updating the school buses. Unfortunately, the current buses have reached their service life.

After his father's passing, Song Shaoguang became the new principal of Chongwen School. As a graduate of a normal university, he hopes to provide students with better education. In 2008, Song Shaoguang founded the Yantai Chongwen Kindergarten, forming a unique characteristic of continuous education from kindergarten, elementary school, to junior high school.

The school unconditionally accepts the children of migrant workers.

Song Shaoguang said that thus, his father decided to use all their savings to build the school. In 1995, his father rented a schoolhouse in Xingfu for 60,000 yuan. Initially, there was only one elementary school class named Hope Elementary School, with fewer than 10 students. Including his parents as teachers, there were eight people in total, and all miscellaneous tasks were handled by his mother. In 1997, the school received formal approval from the educational department and was renamed Yantai Chongwen School. At this time, every year during the Spring Festival, his father's main task was to go out and find people to repay debts and ensure the teachers received their salaries. Such days continued for over 11 years.

However, due to excessive fatigue, Song Renping's health deteriorated, and he never recovered after falling ill. In 2005, through much persuasion, the Happiness Family Construction Company, moved by the father-son duo's efforts in founding the school, agreed to fund the construction of the teaching building. In November of that year, the new teaching building was fully put into use. Speaking of these matters, Song Shaoguang feels deep regret. His father was so excited upon hearing about the construction of the teaching building that he cried, but unfortunately, he didn't live to see the day the teaching building was completed. If only his father could have seen the brand-new teaching building, how happy he would have been.

Song Renping: Female, founder of the school, from Qixia, Shandong Province, was formerly a private teacher at Machuan Primary School, Xijia Middle School, and Dongsanquan Middle School in Tangjiapo Town, Qixia City. She spent 12 years on the three-foot platform. In 1995, she founded a school in Xingfu, Yantai, at her own expense. In 1997, the school obtained approval, and Yantai Chongwen School was established. She passed away in 2004 due to overwork.

Song Shaoguang informed the reporter that when he was young, both his parents were private teachers, and at that time, private teachers' salaries were very low. As he and his sisters grew older, their family always struggled financially. His father, who had been a teacher for 12 years, quit his job to support the family, but he knew his father always cherished the teaching profession. After quitting, his father "went into business" and opened a photography training school to teach people photography, which was quite successful.
