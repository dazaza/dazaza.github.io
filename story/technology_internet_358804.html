Shao Ge: Urban waste puts us on high alert

by ditan360 on 2010-06-23 10:47:35

Mr. Shaogo

Exclusive manuscript of Low Carbon Network: How about science, religion, art and human destiny? This is a problem faced by both the East and the West. In contemporary society, this is a problem that needs to be reinterpreted by morality, faith, ethics, and culture - what are humans? Humans forget more existences in the process of paying attention to their own existence. This is human arrogance and conceit, and this fearlessness and expansion will throw oneself into the abyss. Humans are like naughty children who cannot control their own behavior. While trying to change themselves, art acts as the guardian of culture and attempts to change everything. This kind of change can't be touched by popular art, and the pain of elite artists begins from here. Whether they bear responsibility and mission determines the survival of contemporary art. The reason why we are different from yesterday today is because everything around us is different from before. Our contribution today is tomorrow's archive.

——Shaogo

One of Shaogo's series of paintings "Urban Waste"

There is no doubt that low carbon is a hot topic nowadays, and all industries are talking about low carbon. Whether it is the decoration field related to people's daily life or the art field that enriches people's thoughts, they all raise low carbon and environmental protection to a specific height and give them full attention. Some well-known artists even take low-carbon environmental protection as their creative material to express their love for the environment and their concern for environmental protection. Shaogo is one of them. At the end of the last century, Shaogo created a sensational work, the "Urban Waste" series. This series of works aims to express the joy and pain of industrial civilization. The theme is that environmental destruction has made modern humans become deformed, and the author is concerned with the fate of life and the country. Once this series of paintings was exhibited, it caused a sensation in the industry, and Shaogo became a symbolic figure of environmental and eco-friendly paintings.

About Shaogo's "Urban Waste" series of paintings, someone commented: The "Urban Waste" series of paintings is a new non-traditional modern art opened up by Mr. Shaogo under the premise of adapting to the needs of the changing times after clearly observing the facts. This series of paintings recombines traditional ink, symbols, language, and color with modern creative ideas and methods to reflect the subjective intention of the individual on the canvas, reflecting the creative style of a generation of masters. "There is no lofty theory, the master is forged by system and cognition issues, making major breakthroughs in aesthetics, realizing his ambition and ideal for humanity through his own efforts and practice. 'Know where the ancients have been, and go where the ancients have not been.'"

With a bit of admiration for Shaogo and moved by his artistic works, recently, a reporter from Low Carbon Network interviewed him on topics such as environmental protection and low carbon. From an artistic perspective, Mr. Shaogo explained to us the urgent need for low carbon and environmental protection and the way to artistically practice them.

The Pursuit of Low Carbon and Environmental Protection is the Responsibility of Contemporary Artists in the Era

When the spiritual environment deteriorates day by day, environmental protection becomes a high-end pursuit. Whether it is environmental protection in ideology or environmental protection in living space, it has become an urgent need. The implementation of environmental protection involves national policies at the upper level and civilian attitudes at the lower level, both of which are crucial. Regarding the attitude towards environmental protection, Shaogo said that environmental protection is an action, a concept and attitude towards life. Art is an ideology for artists to express their hearts and cultural thoughts. Of course, art can pay attention to environmental protection and express content related to environmental protection. In this era, such works and authors bear more responsibilities. Art is something at the spiritual level of humans, and artists must first be good at thinking and grasping the current human survival problems and sustainable development issues. This is the responsibility of every person with a conscience, and also the primary concern of artists. Besides expressing through painting language, at the life level, one should perceive everything happening around with heart.

In a sense, artists are the "capitalized" people who explore the progress of human spirit as their mission, which requires artists to stand at a specific height, lead people forward. Through depicting the masses with brush and ink, leading the collective upward movement of human thought.

Through paintings, showing both the beauty and ugliness of China, using the conscience of a Chinese person, using the language of painting, recording them down, and expressing the joy and pain brought by modern social civilization with a great sense of responsibility. Caring about environmental protection, caring about the next generation, attracting the attention of healers through painting garbage. This is our greatest wish.

Low-carbon living is the big trend of people's lives

Low-carbon living is the big trend of people's lives, Shaogo said so.

Nowadays, natural disasters occur frequently, and the Earth we live on is punishing our ineffective protection in its unique way. If we could have realized earlier the consequences of excessive development and un-environmental practices, then these disasters might not have occurred or would have occurred less. History teaches us: low-carbon living is the big trend of people's lives.

Low-carbon living is not just a lifestyle, but also a life attitude. In low-carbon living, people are not simply saving electricity and water, but taking responsibility for certain social responsibilities through achievable frugality. Without accumulating small steps, one cannot reach a thousand miles; without accumulating small streams, one cannot form rivers and seas. Without starting from little things and each individual, global low-carbon living would be impossible. Everyone on Earth should realize this and strive to start from themselves and achieve low-carbon living.

"Low-carbon living" is a current concept, referring to the survival crisis caused by excessive exploitation and use of natural resources during human industrialization. It requires each person to reduce living costs, consumption, produce less waste, and achieve a comprehensive balance between humans and nature, allowing humans to sustainably develop. Just like the restrictions on vehicle tail numbers in Beijing.

It's impossible for modern people to completely achieve "low-carbon living" all at once, but we must have the awareness of "low carbon" and try our best. Artists are no exception. They need to advocate this new concept beneficial to the environment and the earth, starting from every small thing in life, such as saving electricity, water, and reducing exhaust emissions. My work, the "Urban Waste" series, has always been paying attention to these issues, making many people reflect on life in front of my works.

We need to influence people from the heart, making them realize the importance of low-carbon and environmental protection, and join the ranks of promoting low-carbon and environmental protection.

Urban waste is an inevitable result of reform and opening-up, minimizing this inevitability is our contribution to environmental protection

Reform and opening-up have continuously improved our lives, rapidly developed our economy, and transformed China from a large agricultural country into urbanization. Cities have become the main living space for people today. Urbanization brings great economic prosperity to society, but it also brings many real-world confusions, such as intensified competition and environmental pollution. The formation and accumulation speed of urban waste almost coincides with the pace of reform and opening-up. Regrettably, it is only until today that the huge impact of these wastes has become apparent. Wherever there are human activities, there will be garbage, which is an objective fact beyond our control. Facing this objective fact, the only thing we can do as civilians is to minimize the garbage we produce as much as possible. This is our contribution to environmental protection in the current era. As painters, creating urban-themed works and awakening people with our works is our responsibility.

The original intention of creating the "Urban Waste" series is to pay attention to existing problems in reality, including concerns for people and environmental protection, etc. Anyone who sees the "Urban Waste" can think divergently. You can intuitively think that this is garbage in the natural environment, or you can think that it is garbage in the fields of thought and spirit, etc. In short, everything that is not conducive to environmental protection, everything that pollutes us, can be called garbage. If every person who sees this series of paintings can realize the objective existence of garbage and its terrible impact through the paintings, then the "Urban Waste" can be considered a perfect work.

While Mr. Shaogo pays attention to the fate of mankind, he pays more attention to the struggle against urban pollution. After the creation of the "Urban Waste" series, it undoubtedly caused a sensation in the industry. Shaogo said, I paint garbage, actually using garbage to awaken the world. The polluted air, water, and substances are "materialized" by Shaogo as "images" (garbage). People seem to live in trash cans (hell cages), where the surrounding environment is completely polluted, emitting a stench, and people have all become deformed. Moreover, spiritual and material garbage are everywhere, seriously affecting human survival and health. Shaogo said: "If Chinese continue to develop like this, they will be covered by garbage, and it will be difficult for humans to survive." "Show the beauty and ugliness of China, with the conscience of a Chinese person, use the language of painting, record them, and express the joy and pain brought by modern social civilization with a great sense of responsibility."
