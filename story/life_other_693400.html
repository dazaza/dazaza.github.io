8. After a car is equipped with an ABS system, it is to shorten the braking distance; Incorrect. The biggest advantage of ABS is not to shorten the braking distance, but to prevent the tires from locking up. Therefore, even if you slam on the brakes in an emergency situation, the steering wheel can still control the direction of the car. Learning how to buffer the braking distance after an emergency brake is key to understanding ABS.

10. When turning or changing lanes, you should look at the rearview mirror first; Incorrect. You should first turn your head to check behind the car and then look at the rearview mirror because sometimes the rearview mirror adjustment may result in blind spots. Moreover, turning on the turn signal may increase the likelihood of nearby vehicles speeding up or slowing down. Relying solely on the rearview mirror cannot fully ensure accurate judgment.
