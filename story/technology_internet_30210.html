The enterprise email market will reach 680 million, and standardized services are inevitable.

by now on 2007-09-19 18:10:36

According to authoritative statistics, this year the average daily email traffic for global individual users will reach 73 billion messages, and that of corporate users will reach an average of 124 billion messages per day. It is estimated that by 2011, both individual users and corporate users will maintain steady growth respectively. As an important component of electronic mailboxes, the corporate email market is rapidly expanding with the acceleration of corporate informatization. It is estimated that this year, the domestic corporate email market size will reach 680 million yuan. At the same time, experts also pointed out that the service capabilities of operators in the current market are uneven, and standardizing the market is imperative.

As the flow of company information increases, the corporate email system has become an indispensable channel for information exchange in the office environment. A dedicated email server can achieve fast and accurate file transfer, and an email address with the company domain name as the suffix greatly enhances the corporate image. However, there are various sizes of email service providers in the market, with significant differences in technical levels and service capabilities, causing a lot of trouble for enterprises. If they encounter a poor service provider, it is highly likely that important commercial information will be lost, resulting in huge losses.

To address this issue, the Ministry of Information Industry officially issued the "Administrative Measures for Internet Email Services" in 2006. The regulations stipulate that operators without an operating license or without filing shall not conduct email services arbitrarily. This measure means that those email service providers with non-standard operations will be excluded from the market. This has, to some extent, purified the corporate email market and provided good protection for corporate customers. However, many small service providers still try to pass themselves off as legitimate, disrupting market order.

Regarding this issue, a journalist interviewed the head of professional email service provider Now Communications (www.now.cn). According to their introduction, "With the rapid development of network technology, the use of email has become an indispensable means of communication. For enterprises to choose a good service provider, first, they need to check whether the service provider is professional and whether they have obtained an operating license and filed; secondly, they need to test the performance of the email system."

It is understood that Now Communications' email system offers free trials. The fourth-generation corporate email system adopts SPF and SBL systems, with super large capacity and super large attachments. The capacity ranges from 100M to tens of Gs, meeting the needs of different enterprises for email. In addition, their R&D personnel introduced that similar products in the market often have sufficient space but support a limited number of users, leading to a waste of resources. Considering this, Now Corporate Email supports more users compared to similar products, saving resources. Moreover, Now Email uniquely features multi-server forwarding and backup functions, ensuring fast and secure email transmission.

Experts stated that as the Chinese network market continues to grow, the vast email market will undoubtedly attract many service providers to join. With the deepening of national informatization, standardizing the email market and improving relevant laws and regulations are crucial and inevitable.
