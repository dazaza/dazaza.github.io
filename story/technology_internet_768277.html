China Mobile's 4G mobile phones go on sale on November 6: 10 business halls in Beijing are accepting pre-orders.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:59:14

Starting official sales of 4G phones on November 6th

Tencent Technology learned from China Mobile Beijing that it will start the official sale of 4G phones on November 6th. Ten business halls in Beijing will simultaneously accept pre-orders.

It is understood that the launch models include multiple smartphones such as Sony M35t and Samsung N7108D, all supporting China Mobile's TD-LTE network and capable of switching between 2G/3G/4G modes. Customers using 4G phones do not need to change their original mobile phone numbers; they just need to replace their regular SIM cards with USIM cards, truly achieving "no number change, no registration".

This time, Beijing Mobile has specially launched a "First-to-Taste 0 Yuan Purchase" event for users. Taking Samsung GT-N7108D (NOTE 2 Mobile 4G Edition) as an example, early adopters only need to pre-deposit 3936 yuan in phone fees and commit to a minimum monthly consumption of 328 yuan for 24 months to easily obtain one.

Compared to the current 3G models on the market, 4G phones run faster. According to TD-LTE network test results, the single-user downlink peak rate can reach 80 megabits per second, more than four times the speed of mainstream 3G networks. For example, downloading a 2GB movie using a 4G network can be completed in just a few minutes.

Currently, the 4G network in Beijing has covered areas within the eastern, western, and northern third ring roads, south to Liangguang Road, as well as Tsinghua University, Peking University, Guomao, CBD, and Yaboyuan areas.

Ten business halls in Beijing are accepting pre-orders for 4G phones

Just as Beijing Mobile is pre-selling 4G phones, relevant Guangdong Mobile officials also told Tencent Technology yesterday that starting November 3rd, Guangzhou Mobile will offer free upgrades for Hong Kong versions of the iPhone 5s/5c to mobile 3G and 4G networks in ten service halls.

Prior to this, the domestic version of the iPhone 5s/5c by China Mobile had already received trial network access approval from the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT).

Since the 4G license has not yet been officially issued, the network that users can currently experience is still in the 4G trial commercialization phase, and terminals have not yet been fully released.

In fact, in order to expand 4G terminals, especially TD-LTE terminals, China Mobile is not only strongly promoting its own branded phones but also widely cooperating with domestic and international manufacturers. To date, more than 18 chip manufacturers, over 10 testing equipment manufacturers, and more than 50 terminal manufacturers have participated.

In early September, MIIT issued the first batch of domestic 4G phone network trial access approvals. The qualified models are four phones from Samsung, Sony, Huawei, and ZTE, with model numbers Samsung GT-N7108D, Sony M35T, Huawei D2-6070, and ZTE U9815.

According to China Mobile's "Dual Hundred Plan", China Mobile will build TD-LTE networks in over 100 cities nationwide and procure one million TD-LTE terminals. In the second quarter of this year, China Mobile has already conducted centralized procurement of 4G terminals, purchasing eight phone models, ten MiFis, four data cards, and three CPEs from over 200 4G terminal models, totaling 206,500 units.

It is not difficult to see that the true large-scale launch of 4G terminals will be after the issuance of licenses, and it is expected that there will be a market explosion period in the first half of next year, with more diverse choices for 4G terminals.

Regarding when the 4G license will be issued, there is currently no definite timetable, but Minister Miao Wei of MIIT previously told Tencent Technology that it will definitely be issued by the end of the year.
