Splendid: The Catalyst for Hunan Radio and Television Reform

by b48560406 on 2009-11-22 22:25:51

Wang Jia: The intervention of Shengda has become a catalyst for the reform of Hunan Broadcasting System. The "balanced" product of Hunan Broadcasting and Shengda is the establishment of a new entertainment company with an investment of 600 million yuan - Prosperous Pictures Co., Ltd. "Tutor of Selection Competition" Long Danni was appointed as the president of the company.

For Ouyang Changlin, the leader of Hunan Broadcasting System, this step was not easy for the broadcasting system within the system. In cooperation with Shengda, the broadcasting system carefully made a bold decision: to directly introduce capital but give up the controlling equity.

"This is only the first step." Ouyang Changlin said. In his second year as the director of the Hunan Provincial Administration of Radio, Film and Television, he finally had a clear timetable for the third reform of the radio and television system that he advocated to further promote the property rights reform.

Joint venture story: making incentives possible

The joint venture story between Hunan Broadcasting and Shengda has been tortuous. The broadcasting system has its own practical intentions.

Ouyang Changlin spoke frankly, although users have contributed a high rating to Hunan Satellite TV, Hunan Satellite TV has not established a good product service relationship with customers, but this problem can be solved by new media. Ouyang further explained, "For example, we have made many people famous, but there is a lack of business model and industrial chain to bring returns, so it is necessary to connect the upstream, midstream and downstream platforms and integrate a richer and more forward-looking industrial chain."

In fact, there has been a consensus within the broadcasting system. Nie Mei, director of the office of the Hunan Provincial Radio and Television Bureau and director of the Reform Office, told the China Business News reporter that one of the important problems faced by Hunan Broadcasting at present is the unreasonable industrial structure: including single revenue model, incomplete industrial chain, and lagging development of new media.

For Hunan Broadcasting, which urgently needs to unblock the industrial chain and open up new media resources, cooperation with Shengda is an opportunity they have been looking for. As one of the promoters of this matter, Nie Mei told the reporter that Shengda Literature controls part of the market share of online novels, which is very attractive to the broadcasting system. If there is an explosive development in the field of entertainment content in the future, novels are the source of all original movies and TV series. This market is broad, which is to convert what young people pay attention to into visual media.

Although Prosperous Pictures disclosed little information externally, there are two points worth noting: one is that the broadcasting system introduced funds but gave up shares, which is unprecedented in external cooperation of state-owned broadcasting institutions; the other is that equity incentives and other issues are not involved in this joint venture company. For example, as president, Long Danni cannot hold shares in the joint venture due to systemic reasons.

Hunan Satellite TV's chief editor Li Hao revealed that this decision also drew lessons from previous experiences and lessons. "If you talk about cooperation with some private enterprises before, if you control the shares yourself, it will be difficult for the other party to play their advantages in personnel mechanism and distribution mechanism."

As Li Hao said, the broadcasting system values the substantial connection between the joint venture and the third round of reforms in the broadcasting system: the possibility of legitimizing the incentive mechanism.

The third round of reforms of Hunan Broadcasting started in 2006. Zhang Huali, who was then the director of the Reform Office, said, "We must reform the people and push them to the market." "Pushing talents to the market means that their original level and treatment in the station, even the 'three guarantees' are gone. Without the incentive mechanism that connects with the market as a guarantee, it is impossible to retain talents."

Apparently, Hunan Broadcasting hopes to break through with Prosperous Pictures, incubate companies outside the system, and use market-oriented means to incentivize broadcasting talents.

Reform of the broadcasting system: telling the story of capital

The reform of the national broadcasting system has already begun. When Wei Wenbin, the former director of Hunan Broadcasting, was in office, he said, "Billions of assets, and none of them are related to anyone. The system is still an administrative and public system, which is public, loose, vague, and backward. Such a mechanism can work at a certain stage, but it is very difficult to make a bigger breakthrough." He bluntly said, "The more public it is, the less it belongs to everyone."

In fact, the dual nature of the broadcasting system determines its embarrassing situation. "Media itself has the attribute of publicity, and also has the attribute of industrialization. After industrialization develops to a certain extent, research, after all, no matter how big your scale is, you will find that property rights is the most fundamental issue." Nie Mei said.

In fact, the reform of Hunan Broadcasting in recent years has also been promoted in a small range, such as taking Tianyu, Mango Pictures and other separable businesses to the market to tell stories. But the results were not significant. Zhang Huali, one of the promoters and the first deputy director of Hunan Satellite TV, believed that this was still a systemic issue, still a power-oriented structure, and essentially did not form a free pricing ability.

In 2008, Hunan Broadcasting's efforts to inject some operational assets into its listed company, Hunan Electric Broadcasting, also failed. The reform once fell into a stalemate. But the turning point came in 2009.

In 2009, the State Administration of Radio, Film and Television and other ministries issued policies successively, clearly encouraging local radio and television systems to explore "separation of production and broadcasting". Hunan Broadcasting, as the "leader" of local radio and television, together with Shanghai Media Group, CCTV Channel 2, Channel 5, Beijing Television Station, etc., was officially included in the list of pilot units for the separation of production and broadcasting. This finally allowed local radio and television reform to find a macro policy path.

In October, Shanghai Media Group broke the ice first, and officially reformed by splitting Shanghai Media Group into Shanghai Radio and Television Station and Oriental Media. The reform method was recognized by the State Administration of Radio, Film and Television. "Shanghai Media Group's reform is bold," Nie Mei thought.

So what is the approved reform plan announced by Hunan Broadcasting?

Nie Mei, who personally formulated the plan, told the China Business News reporter that the first step Hunan Broadcasting would take is to separate management and operation. Nie explained that this is actually to relatively separate administrative functions and market functions.

According to exclusive information obtained by the reporter, an important step is that Hunan Radio and Television Station will be officially established at the end of 2009, fully controlled by Hunan Broadcasting. Ouyang Changlin once explained that the reason for setting up a general station is that the Radio and Television Bureau is a government functional department, and needs the general station to manage the market subject.

Next is to take gradual actions on the huge amount of operational assets under Hunan Broadcasting. Establish market entities, separate production and broadcasting, clarify property rights relations and incentive mechanisms. However, Nie Mei did not disclose the specific content.

Ouyang Changlin said: "We will not carry out formalistic separation of production and broadcasting, and will not design everything well at once." Nie Mei said that no matter how the steps go, the core of the third round of reforms will not change, that is, to further promote the property rights reform of Hunan Broadcasting and operate like an enterprise.

As mentioned earlier by Ouyang Changlin, the next step of Hunan Broadcasting is to cooperate with Qinghai Satellite TV. According to Li Hao, the cooperation will be announced in the next two weeks. Although the specific details of the cooperation have not been disclosed, industry insiders analyzed that after the effective implementation of the separation of production and broadcasting, a market-oriented content company cannot provide products for only one channel, and cross-regional development is the only way.

At the same time, Ouyang Changlin also said that Hunan Broadcasting is negotiating with some venture capitalists to achieve diversified equity of market entities as soon as possible. It is worth noting that at the end of June, Shanghai Media Group signed a "strategic cooperation agreement" with the well-known investment institution SoftBank of Japan.

It can be seen that the emergence of the policy of separating production and broadcasting has enabled many radio and television institutions within the system to see the possibility of reform and listing in the capital market.
