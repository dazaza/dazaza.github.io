Leadership Test: Are You Skilled in Military-Style Management?

Parsen once said a famous quote: "The best management in the world is in the military." In our country, companies like Huawei, Changhong, Lenovo, Yuanda, and Chunlan were once renowned for their military-style management. The United States' West Point Military Academy is also one of the cradles of business leaders. The reason behind this is that soldiers under military-style management develop many excellent qualities, such as a sense of mission, honor, responsibility, dedication, professionalism, and expertise. As we celebrate the "August 1st" Army Day, let's test whether you are skilled in military-style management!

1. When executing a task, as a leader:

A. I will lead by example because leading is key; all members participating is the foundation.

B. I delegate to subordinates and provide guidance.

C. I fully delegate to subordinates.

2. In terms of hiring, I tend to:

A. Use people with no way out, as they usually go all out.

B. Use people who meet the job requirements.

C. Use highly capable people.

3. Regarding discipline, I believe:

A. Company members should consciously follow and execute discipline, showing the team's alignment and standards.

B. Freedom should be exercised within the framework of discipline.

C. Discipline only needs to be emphasized appropriately.

4. Regarding employees' responsibilities and rights, I think:

A. Work should be assigned to each employee, giving them a sense of mission.

B. Power should be delegated fully.

C. Responsibilities and rights do not need to be very clear.

5. About the company's system, I think:

A. The team relies on systems, which should clearly define division of labor and cooperation.

B. There should be both systems and democracy.

C. It's more important to give employees democratic rights.

6. Regarding management and assessment:

A. We have strict management systems and assessment methods.

B. Our management and assessment are not very strict.

C. Our management and assessment are extremely unstrict.

7. In the entire production process and daily management, I would:

A. Organically incorporate the unique organizational structure and behavioral standards of military management.

B. Not deliberately arrange military-like organization and standards but have similar practices.

C. Not use military-like organization and behavior standards.

8. Under the premise of not affecting the normal work of the enterprise, I would train employees gradually:

A. Yes, hoping the company can achieve military-like actions, standardized behaviors, and standardized work through training.

B. Sometimes targeted training is conducted.

C. Rarely conduct training.

9. I would require employees to wear uniform clothing, showing a unique spirit:

A. Yes, using uniform clothing to show neatness and teamwork.

B. There are certain requirements, but not very strict.

C. No requirement for uniform clothing.

10. Regarding promotion systems and penalties for violations:

A. There are strict promotion systems and strict penalties.

B. There is a general reward and punishment mechanism.

C. Rewards and punishments are not very strict.

Test Results:

Choose A for 3 points, B for 2 points, C for 1 point.

24-30 points: In your company, you have great authority and control, and you're skilled in military-style management. Military-style management can fully mobilize the collective's fighting spirit and has immediate effects. However, it's important to strengthen the humanization of the company to adapt to long-term development.

17-23 points: You don't have a strong military-style management style, possibly due to the special nature of the industry you're in, which may not suit military-style management. In management, some companies admire military-style, while others believe in humanization. They each have their advantages and disadvantages.

10-16 points: You're not very good at using military-style management methods, but the operation of a company indeed requires perfect systems and certain behavioral norms. Many commercial elites from the U.S. West Point Military Academy owe their success to the cultivation of their military style, which is worth learning from for enterprises.
