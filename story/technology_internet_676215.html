The most blatant adultery draws onlookers.

by edivista on 2012-02-25 21:21:22

http://www.7weibo.com/blog/post/712.html

It's not uncommon for couples to express their affection. When love is strong, it’s natural to want to announce one's happiness to the world. However, there was a man who shamelessly flirted with his mistress on Weibo and even @-mentioned his own wife, which immediately drew fierce attention from netizens.

The Most Public Display of Infidelity Draws Attention

On February 18th, a post appeared on the entertainment and gossip section of Tianya Community titled "A Man Publicly Displays Affection with His Mistress on Weibo, and Even @-Mentions His Wife! How Heartless Can This Despicable Man Be?!" The explosive headline quickly drew strong attention from netizens. By 8 PM last night, the post had nearly 300,000 views.

The male protagonist's Weibo nickname is "Water-like Peach," with the signature: "A man who only loves Wen." This account frequently interacts with the real-name certified account "Vinne_Xu Jiawen." At 19:44 on January 8th, "Water-like Peach" posted a Weibo saying, "Without Xu Jiawen, I can't go on living," while also @-mentioning "Naimomo's Love." "Naimomo's Love" does not have a Weibo profile picture set, but her update frequency is quite high.

On February 12th, "Water-like Peach" posted a close-up photo of a couple and wrote on Weibo: "The feeling of being together is so good, let's love each other for a lifetime."

The poster revealed that the person posting the Weibo is the male lead, Ms. Xu is the mistress, and the one @-mentioned, "Naimomo's Love," is the wife of the male lead. This open and brazen act of infidelity incited righteous indignation among netizens, and the famous Tianya Sightseeing Group immediately swarmed to the Weibo accounts of the three parties involved.

Wife Regrets Harming the Child

As netizens continued to observe and gossip, the details of the incident gradually came to light.

"Naimomo's Love" had already posted a Weibo on December 20th of last year saying, "It's easy to avoid overt seduction, but hard to guard against covert betrayal."

From her continuous updates on Valentine's Day (February 14th), it seems her husband met a female gamer while playing games. After I gave birth, your mother asked me why I suddenly didn’t have breast milk anymore. You don't know, one day I accidentally saw a call record at 1 AM lasting over an hour. A woman answered when I called. As a woman, you can imagine what happened. I argued, you tried to justify yourself, I smashed the phone, and the next day I didn't have breast milk anymore. Then we agreed that no matter how much you played games, just don't bring it into reality. You said okay. Did I really not notice your abnormal behavior in September last year? I thought you wouldn't be stupid enough to this extent. I was wrong. But I don't regret these things. What I regret more is marrying you, having a child, and harming the child. The child is 18 months old, and you've held him for a total of 48 hours. Are you even a father? (Screenshots of Weibo posts by "Water-like Peach," "Vinne_Xu Jiawen," and "Naimomo's Love")

On February 20th, "Naimomo's Love" posted eight consecutive Weibo messages, one of which read: "Back then, the mistress told me this: to empathize with her being a mistress, fine, once we divorce, you two can live happily ever after." Subsequently, she exposed numerous text messages and QQ chat records on Weibo, one of which was sent by the mistress: "We're both girls, we should understand each other."

Pressure Leads to Nickname Deletion

The male lead's high-profile actions sparked netizen outrage. In the comments, netizen "Won't Name 007" said: "The mistress actually got verified (i.e., passed the real-name certification on Weibo)!" Netizen "Super Potato Lover" replied: "Just went to check, found out the female lead is also Miss Yangzhou, speechless." Some netizens pointed out that the Vinne_Xu Jiawen suspected of being the mistress had participated in the Yangzhou Beauty Contest selection and won the title of Best Advertising Model in the 2011 China Yangzhou Beauty Contest.

Under the pressure of netizen attention, by the evening of the 20th, the Weibo account of user Vinne_Xu Jiawen already showed "Nickname Does Not Exist," and the Weibo account of "Water-like Peach" similarly displayed "Nickname Does Not Exist."

Subsequently, reporters attempted to contact the parties involved but did not receive any response. Friend LuckyJuana of "Naimomo's Love" told reporters that the situation reflected online is not fabricated.
