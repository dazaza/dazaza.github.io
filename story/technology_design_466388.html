"Wong Kar-wai's Kung Fu Epic 'The Grandmaster', Prepared for 8 Years"

by tvbing21 on 2011-07-26 22:35:29

"The Grandmaster" is the first kung fu epic directed by Wong Kar-wai with a preparation period of as long as 8 years. It stars famous actors such as Tony Leung, Zhang Ziyi, Song Hye-kyo, Zhang Zhen, Zhao Benshan, etc., and tells the legendary life of Master Yip Man, a generation's martial arts master played by Tony Leung.

Personally, I have always been a fan of Wong Kar-wai, Tony Leung, and Yuen Woo-ping. I have been following Tony Leung on Weibo because in this world filled with bad movies, he still seems to be a sincere filmmaker. At least, he can shoot a movie year after year and refuse all sorts of vulgarities and favors. "The Grandmaster", from its preparation ten years ago to its filming three years ago, features a dare-to-refuse and dare-to-silence emperor of film and director. If they are not just putting on a show, then they may really be very confident about the inherent quality of the movie itself. But no matter how confident they are, the release date of "The Grandmaster" has made movie fans wait anxiously. They even boldly announced that they would directly compete with "The Flowers of War". Martial, he was towering and majestic, wise and invincible, accumulating strength and bursting forth, stopping war with martial arts, stunning many male fans! Therefore, watching Wong Kar-wai polish "The Grandmaster" year after year, I was afraid that Mr. Tony Leung might ruin his own reputation due to being more literary than martial. The preview that appeared this time exclusively featured Tony Leung's version of Yip Man, without seeing other main actors, fully demonstrating the confidence of all the people involved in "The Grandmaster" in Tony Leung's charisma.

"The Grandmaster"'s 80-second trailer, with simply 22 words, shows Tony Leung wearing black clothes and a white hat, standing alone in the vast rainy night fighting against numerous villains. His moves are pure, his eyes sharp, and his demeanor profound. Besides actions, I saw Tony Leung's aura, gentle yet humble. Every move, every glance and smile, radiates brilliance. In the trailer, Yip Man's voice is deep, his tone slow, making listeners feel very comfortable. It can be described as lightly sketched but resonant.

Tony Leung's eyes can act, which is world-recognized. One look from him represents a different character. Pay attention to the trailer from 24 to 28 seconds. Not only does Tony Leung reveal his dazzling eyes, but he also displays his sensitive soul. Regardless of whether it's a combination of offense and defense or hitting diagonally, every action of his seems to be an emotional release. Such actors who amplify certain traits of themselves repeatedly are rare in the Chinese film industry, with only a few people like Chow Yun-fat and Louis Koo. Speaking solely of acting, based solely on this 80-second trailer, I am sure that Tony's version of Yip Man is enough to surpass Donnie's version.

Before every movie is released, my habit is to watch the trailer first. And the trailer of "The Grandmaster" really made me couldn't help but shout out loud: release it quickly! I will definitely go to the cinema to support it! (By Qian Du Movie Network qdfilm)
