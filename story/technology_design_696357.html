The referee's conspiracy to send off Pique is exposed on Crown Football www.rz5888.com, as Barcelona frequently suffers from wrong judgments, fueling their indignation.

by hdukafkk on 2012-03-08 01:08:03

The Spanish Football Association denies it. Barcelona often receives special treatment in international competitions. However, in La Liga matches, Barcelona frequently suffers disadvantages without the "godfather's" care. For instance, let's take the referee of this match, Carballo, as an example. In this season's La Liga, he has only refereed two Barcelona games, both of which have caused significant controversy. Besides giving a red card to Pique, Carballo also overlooked three clear fouls by Sporting Gijon in the penalty area. During the 2-2 draw between Barcelona and Valencia, he twice ignored clear fouls on Messi by Miguel and Rami in the penalty area. In these two refereeing performances, five penalties were wrongly denied to Barcelona.

Despite having solid evidence that Pique was wronged, Almeyna, the chairman of the La Liga Referees Committee, still refused to back down: "Barcelona indeed had three missed penalty calls, I admit that point because referees are allowed to make mistakes. But there was indeed a personal conflict between Pique and the referee. His conspiracy theory is completely wrong, even for the smallest mistake, there must be consequences."

News background: The team suffered a great loss. Barcelona has long been called the "Galaxy Team" because it has a famous "godfather," Platini. Under Platini's care, Barcelona often receives favorable treatment from referees in European competitions. However, back in La Liga, Barcelona becomes the one being bullied. Last weekend, after Barcelona's 3-1 victory over Sporting Gijon, Pique criticized the main referee Carballo for intentionally sending him off and claimed that he was threatened by the referee in the tunnel. Yesterday, Barcelona officially announced that they would appeal Pique's red card and strongly criticized the domestic referees' performance.

Barcelona and Real Madrid have double standards when it comes to refereeing issues. This time, Barcelona plans to take real action. According to the plan, the senior management of Barcelona Athletic will hold another meeting at 12 o'clock local time on the 7th to discuss the time and venue of the Copa del Rey final. Freixa then clearly stated that Barcelona would not send anyone to participate in this meeting as a protest against the La Liga Referees Committee. Barcelona obviously intended to escalate the matter and put pressure on Villar, the president of the Spanish Football Association.

Barcelona legends express dissatisfaction. Among all La Liga teams, Barcelona is undoubtedly UEFA President Platini's favorite, known as Barcelona's "godfather." And with the help of their "godfather," Barcelona always sails smoothly in the Champions League, winning the trophy twice in five years. In the 2006 and 2009 Champions League finals, Barcelona faced much criticism regarding refereeing decisions.

Evidence of the referee threatening Pique has been exposed. Regarding refereeing issues in La Liga, Catalan media believe that referees apply different standards when dealing with Barcelona and Real Madrid. After being eliminated from the Copa del Rey, Casillas openly cursed the main referee Texeira, saying "Go celebrate with Barcelona," while Mourinho even exploded with profanity when Texeira arrived at the parking lot. However, these incidents were ignored by the La Liga Referees Committee. When the media asked about this, Gimenez, the spokesperson for the Spanish Football Association, responded: "I don't know if Mourinho was waiting to curse the referee; we only saw this in the media, there was no official report, but we did see Pique insult the referee." This statement avoided the question of why Casillas was not penalized since the Real Madrid captain, like Pique, directly cursed after the game.

In domestic competitions, Barcelona is always "bullied."

Regarding the opinion of the Referees Committee, Barcelona is very dissatisfied. Yesterday, the club officially decided to appeal Pique's red card. Freixa, the official spokesperson for Barcelona, stated that the Blaugrana will fight the referees to the end. Freixa requested that Almeyna clarify in writing the reasons for suing Pique, bluntly pointing out the huge loopholes in the management of the La Liga Referees Committee: "We demand that the La Liga Referees Committee does not just target individuals but treats everyone fairly. We want to know what rules they have set for the games." Clearly, Freixa sarcastically pointed out that the La Liga Referees Committee does not equally treat every team in terms of rules and standards.

After being sent off by Carballo, Pique immediately expressed his view that this red card was "premeditated." Because of this statement, Pique was reported to the La Liga Disciplinary Committee by the La Liga Referees Committee, and the referees requested additional punishment for Pique. However, the fact is that during halftime, the Spaniards had already received threats from the referee that they would get a red card in the second half. A Spanish radio station disclosed that after the first half of the match between Barcelona and Sporting Gijon, Pique found Carballo in the player's tunnel. Pique told Carballo that he misjudged the penalty created by Keita. Carballo immediately retorted fiercely: "Shut up, you're going to get a red card!" As a result, at the start of the second half, Pique was directly shown a red card for a not-so-obvious foul, causing Barcelona No. 3 to explode in anger. According to the Daily Sports also revealed, Pique was very surprised to learn that he was complained about by the Referees Committee, as he didn't expect that after being disadvantaged, he would still be taken to court.

Former Barcelona star Lefeu, who served the club for 13 years, frankly stated that the Blaugrana has suffered greatly due to the referees. "Sometimes the referees' performances harm the team. This season, Barcelona has suffered too many losses, with referees making mistakes repeatedly," said Lefeu. "The match against Sporting Gijon is very obvious; Pique was not only incorrectly sent off but also prosecuted. I can't understand it because the referees themselves admitted that Barcelona was denied three penalties. Someone from Barcelona needs to stand up. Guardiola and his players usually don't talk much about referees, so someone else needs to come out and defend rights." Another La Liga referee, Albert, also expressed: "I can't agree with Carballo. When sending off Pique, he made a wrong call. He indeed did not make the best choice, but he wasn't trying to deliberately send off Pique."
