The global oil resources are becoming increasingly tight and the price keeps rising. In order to ease the tension in energy supply and address environmental pollution, the Energy Department and Environmental General Administration jointly issued a document stating that within the next few years there will be a complete ban on diesel fuel for kitchen use; this has already been implemented in some cities. The tense situation of fuel supply, the huge market demand, and strong support from national environmental policies all indicate that the new energy era has arrived.

Traditional alcohol-based fuels have low calorific values, high consumption rates, and significant waste—only about 5300 kcal/kg—which fails to meet the requirements for cooking and does not offer significant economic benefits. Additionally, alcohol-based fuels have poor stability and are prone to evaporation. Therefore, it is necessary to solve the problems of heat and stability in alcohol-based fuels. Our team of chemical and energy experts has successfully developed emulsified bio-alcohol oil after dedicated research, which effectively addresses the issues of insufficient calorific value and high usage rate in alcohol-based fuels.

At the same time, Chuan Island Heavy Industry Machinery Equipment Manufacturing Co., Ltd. has also solved the problems of instability, volatility, and safety concerns with alcohol-based fuels. The product has passed inspections by national quality inspection departments and has been piloted for promotion and use. Both its technical performance and safety indicators meet the requirements for civilian fuels, making it an ideal green and environmentally friendly fuel.

This is the result of painstaking research by dozens of energy experts from our unit, representing a new type of environmental biofuel. It can be stored, transported, and used under normal temperature and pressure conditions without the need for high-pressure steel cylinders. Ordinary metal or plastic containers can be used for storage. Its calorific value reaches as high as 8600 kcal/kg, fully capable of replacing diesel and liquefied gas used in kitchens. Bio-alcohol oil stands out in the energy field—it's the call of the times!
