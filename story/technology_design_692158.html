1. [03106522] - A Method for Manufacturing Artificial Stone Imitating Jade

A method for manufacturing artificial stone imitating jade. It involves mixing quartz sand, feldspar powder, dolomite, barite, quartz stone, sodium fluosilicate, sodium nitrate, arsenic trioxide, borax, soda ash, zinc oxide, and bone powder in a certain proportion, then melting the mixture into a paste in a glass furnace, casting it into molds, and finally annealing the formed material to cool it down to ambient temperature. The artificial stone produced by this method has high strength, excellent flexural and impact resistance, uniform texture, and good gloss. It can rival natural jade and is available in various colors such as pure white, moon white, and beige. It can be used to produce various architectural decorative materials like tiles, stone columns, and stone lions according to need. The process is simple and low-cost.
