A new perspective from an old internet enthusiast!

by seo17519 on 2012-03-06 11:11:32

My name is Long Lin, I am a super veteran netizen with 13 years of internet experience, and I have over 10 years of experience building web pages (sites). I remember when NetEase first started, we used to go there to learn HTML tutorials. Back then, websites were basically independent; if they weren't introduced by magazines or newspapers, you wouldn't even know about the existence of other websites. Nowadays, websites mainly rely on search engines for traffic. Compared to back then, this is a huge change, or even unimaginable. Perhaps this is also why Google is so respected. I think the emergence of search engines is just as great as the appearance of Windows.

I studied nuclear geology in college. Whether it was lucky or unfortunate, at the end of my second year, the state approved the establishment of an undergraduate software engineering program. Due to my personal interest, I applied to "switch" to the first batch of national undergraduate software engineering programs (specially approved by the department head). To this day, many friends still ask me why I suddenly switched majors so drastically. Maybe this is fate.

After graduation, my first job was being dispatched to IBM by a subsidiary company of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and it was for their official website. Later, due to outstanding performance, I was transferred to become a regular employee of IBM. However, this was not the kind of work I had dreamed of, at least not the kind of work I should be doing at my age right after graduating from university, so I resolutely quit and went to another job that I liked a little more as a manager, which gave me more time to study things I like.

In 2007, I spent over $2000 buying www.conghua.com from overseas for use as a local portal for Conghua. Gradually, I began to come into contact with SEO. Unfortunately, during the operation process, due to several domestic filing issues and server problems, my site was shut down for half a month to two months multiple times, resulting in penalties where I lost my top ranking for the keyword "Conghua". Now, I consistently rank on the second page, which has led me to want to learn SEO knowledge and hope to return to the first page. During this period, it's common for Baidu weight to increase, and I also engaged in the page design of another now fairly large-scale website. However, due to some unpleasant matters later, I don't want to mention the name of this website here. Because of these reasons, through my learning, I realized that external links are very important for a website.

One day, while chatting casually with a friend, I learned that A5 was conducting a group-buying activity for ChongChong (a marketing tool). After understanding the functions of ChongChong, I felt that its features would be suitable for my target operations, so I quickly paid and bought ChongChong. Of course, in the first two weeks after purchasing ChongChong, my mood changed from very excited before buying to somewhat disappointed, mainly because of ChongChong's powerful functions but complex operations, not knowing where to start. However, after two weeks of learning, I finally understood ChongChong, and at this point, I started to crazily collect resources.

However, possibly because the things I collected were mostly what ChongChong users had already collected and used, I found that there were very few resources that could be successfully published without being deleted. Moreover, I personally felt that publishing large amounts of information on other people's websites was not ethical, so I considered using some of my own domains to operate a network of sites. I built seven websites, installed programs with interfaces supported by ChongChong for posting, set permissions so only my account could post, and others who registered couldn't post information. This was mainly to prevent other ChongChong users from posting information on my sites. Indeed, although my new sites have only been online for over a month, there have already been many applications for new accounts, so when we build websites, we should also consider how to prevent others from bombarding our websites with information when we implement information bombing on others' websites.

In operating the network of sites, I paid attention to the inclusion status of the websites every day. Initially, within about a week, Baidu enthusiastically included hundreds of pages. However, within less than half a month, it suddenly dropped my inclusion to just over twenty articles, and even two sites had no pages included. At this point, I thought it might be because I set ChongChong to post too frequently, basically releasing thousands of articles at fixed points every day. So I adjusted, using ChongChong's posting interval settings to randomly publish each article between 30 seconds to 10 minutes. This should make it seem more like human operation to search engines. Whether it's because of my correct operation or Baidu was just playing around with me, now all seven websites' inclusions have recovered, and the inclusions have reached several hundred. I estimate that because these are all low-weight websites, the inclusion is temporarily not very good, but I feel that for new websites, this result is already quite good. I believe it will gradually improve. However, because the content is all collected, the quality varies greatly and is difficult to categorize, so my next step will be to slowly adjust, making the content better serve the websites for which I'm trying to build external links.

When these seven websites have gained certain weight, I plan to create full-site links for the websites I want to build external links for, which should give them decent external link resources, and it won't interfere with others' websites. Currently, because I was eager for the websites to go live and didn't plan out a particularly good website structure, I haven't installed Baidu Statistics either, because I heard that it analyzes your website experience. My content is all collected, so naturally the experience isn't good, and because I set up one account to post information, this created a situation where all the information came from one user. But with my subsequent improvements, this should change. When the websites are well-included and some keywords rank higher, further SEM operations can be carried out, directing traffic to valuable websites or conducting targeted marketing to convert traffic into money.

Of course, the portal blog function of ChongChong is also good. I copied another instance of ChongChong specifically for portal blogs, updating these blogs daily. If I persistently post these blogs, the future weight will be good, because there are too many stations to post, SEO optimization is not a mechanical repetitive task, so actually I haven't monitored the inclusion status of these stations either. But I just did a specific keyword search for inclusion, and found that there are still quite a few articles indexed by search engines, proving it effective, and not wasting electricity.

In fact, I've used at most 20% of ChongChong's functions. Recently, I've started using ChongChong to properly optimize a structured website http://www.smf.cn, learning from previous shortcomings, and reusing ChongChong's software experiences more effectively for my own website services. Therefore, I continuously research and share experiences interacted among friends in the QQ group. If you know me, even though I'm also a user, I help answer others' questions in the group. This is thanks to the PMP course I took before. The technology or experience you master, if you just keep it to yourself, you'll forget it soon in the future. But if you organize it and share it with others, you'll retain more long-term memories. I forgot the exact theory, but I remember this experience.

For a software, I think utilizing 40% of its functions is already quite good. For example, Word, in reality, we probably don't even use 5% of its functions, so using tools in the most suitable places for oneself is the right way. Of course, if you need to widely release a lot of content, I continuously use the built-in functions of ChongChong to collect resources. Resources aren't something you can pull down in a day or two, neither Google nor Baidu rose in a day or two. In the beginning, everyone knows, even if you get tired of typing verification codes, the success rate is less than 1%. But as you gradually collect new resources and accumulate your resources, you can improve the success rate. My current experience is to delete all places that require entering verification codes. If after a period of posting on a website, the password login is incorrect, discard it. This indicates that the administrator has noticed you, continuing to post will just waste everyone's time. Slowly accumulate your resources, don't be afraid of failure. Every time I collect hundreds of resources, only 2-3 dozen can eventually be used for resource posting, and I don't collect every day either. If I collected every day, the accumulated resources would be quite astonishing.

After buying ChongChong, my favorite part is the customer service QQ group. Here, experts often share various experiences, weekly regular shares, not only about the software, but also other special uses of ChongChong, or other experiences related to SEO. This is a super value-added place, and I especially thank Le Xiaoyao and Chen Mo. In summary, it's about using the software in the places where you need it most and suits your actual situation best.
