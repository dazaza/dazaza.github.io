A 9-Year-Old Boy Disappears While Playing downstairs

Further police interrogation revealed that the two individuals kidnapped Xiaolong to get some money. They chose their target randomly. On the day of the incident, the two suspects drove to the victim's neighborhood and noticed several children playing around. When committing the crime, Zhang first approached a boy and upon learning that the boy's father was a taxi driver, he abandoned his plan and then targeted Xiaolong. Zhang tricked Xiaolong by telling him that he had a letter from the kindergarten in the community along with many toys for Xiaolong to see. Out of curiosity, Xiaolong followed Zhang to the car and was subsequently forced inside.

The Qiaoxi Public Security Bureau established a special investigation team. After reviewing the surveillance footage from the neighborhood, the police discovered that a black Santana sedan had stayed in the area, and a young man from the car had been seen with Xiaolong. Starting with this black Santana, the police quickly identified the suspects.

Yesterday afternoon, the two criminal suspects were placed under criminal detention.

Less than five hours after the report, the kidnapped child was rescued.

The investigating officers reminded the public to warn their children to be cautious when encountering strangers. Additionally, residential areas should strengthen security management and register vehicles entering and exiting the community.

At 14:00 on November 29, the police found the suspect vehicle in a residential area of Nanwei Village, Yuhua District. Half an hour later, the young man suspected of kidnapping was arrested. Using the door key found on the man, the police located Xiaolong in a unit apartment. At that time, Xiaolong was tied to the head of the bed, with a pair of gloves stuffed in his mouth and wrapped with tape, his hands bound behind his back, and his feet tied with rope. The officers consoled the child while untying the ropes around him.

The kidnappers selected their "target" randomly to get some money.

According to Ms. Wei, the mother of the kidnapped boy, at dusk on November 28, her 9-year-old son Xiaolong (a pseudonym) finished his homework and went downstairs to play with other children. At 18:30, when she called Xiaolong for dinner, she realized he was missing. She learned from other children that Xiaolong had left with a strange man. Despite searching all night, Ms. Wei's family did not find him.

The suspect put a pair of underwear over the child's head to cover his eyes. Photo by Dong Shijie.

On the morning of November 29, Ms. Wei received two text messages, roughly saying, "We have your child, prepare the money." At 9:40 AM, Ms. Wei's family rushed to the Xinshi Criminal Investigation Team to report the case.

Two young men, wanting some money, kidnapped a 9-year-old boy in a residential area on Huai'an West Road in the provincial capital. After receiving the report, the police rescued the kidnapped boy in less than five hours.

As the boy kept struggling, the suspect stuffed a glove into the child's mouth, then sealed it with tape, and used ropes to tie Xiaolong's hands and feet. One of the suspects took off his underwear and covered the child's head with it, blinding him.

From the on-site interrogation, it was learned that the suspect Zhang (male, 28 years old, from Nangong, Hebei) ran an internet cafe in Shijiazhuang and owed a lot of debt. Li (male, 24 years old, from Jiangxi), who committed the kidnapping with Zhang, had worked as a network administrator in his internet cafe but was now unemployed. Around 7 PM that day, the police captured Li near a supermarket in Qiaoxi.
