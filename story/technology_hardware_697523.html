[Repost] Warning to myself, never ever beat your children

by koucai4472 on 2012-03-08 16:05:26

According to a survey by the Public Speaking Training Network, more than half of parents have physically disciplined their children. This can be understood to some extent, as there is an ancient Chinese saying: "A filial child comes from under the rod; without beating, one cannot become a useful person." However, today, the Public Speaking Training Network wants to tell everyone that this is not actually true. Here, I want to emphasize that hitting children is foolish, and the consequences of doing so are serious. Some parents might scoff at this idea.

Indeed, I must admit that every form of education has its own reasons for existence and successful cases; otherwise, we wouldn't regard them as educational textbooks. So, let's discuss whether hitting children is right or wrong.

First, let's analyze the two most common outcomes for children who are frequently beaten:

One outcome is that when you hit them, they take out their frustration on others outside the home. You've created a little tyrant. Such examples are not uncommon. This happens when the child has relatively strong psychological resilience and isn't completely intimidated by you. They vent their dissatisfaction outside by projecting it onto others.

The second outcome is that when you hit them, you create a timid child. Because your child is small and you are big, they find your angry expression terrifying. As a result, the child forms a conditioned reflex: they start trembling whenever an adult raises their voice. Such children are afraid of everything when they go out and fear everyone.

Which parent doesn't want the best for their child? I believe that parents who hit their children also share this desire; it's just that their methods of education differ. Every punch or kick you give your child results in more harm than just physical pain. The child feels humiliated and suffers emotionally, which breeds resentment. Therefore, scolding and hitting are extremely detrimental to a child's development. Parents should remember this: hitting children is foolish and leads to no good outcome.

So today, the Public Speaking Training Network will provide some practical suggestions:

1. Respect the rights of the child

Each child has their own independent personality. Therefore, what parents need to do is respect the child's personality. Parents should learn to treat their children with civility, using love to call forth love and sincerity to evoke sincerity. Children, as independent individuals, have the right to be respected. Resorting to violence is an uncivilized and immoral act. Although parents have the right to criticize and punish their children, this does not mean they can disrespect them. There are plenty of ways to help children understand principles and correct their mistakes. Therefore, we should abandon and change the outdated habit of hitting children. Hitting children is not only illegal but also unwise and may exacerbate problems. There are almost no children in this world who benefit from being hit. When children are beaten too often, their thinking becomes rigid, and learning turns into mere compliance. Moreover, hitting children could damage the parent-child relationship, making education difficult. Therefore, it's crucial to treat children kindly. Empathy is the simplest way to conduct good education.

2. Count before hitting

Through conversations with many parents, most parents admit that hitting their children is often an impulsive act. In the heat of the moment, they lash out, but after calming down, they deeply regret it. Yet each time, they can't control themselves. They regret it, and if the child doesn't understand, it leaves a lasting impression that negatively impacts parent-child communication. Therefore, I suggest that when you feel like hitting your child or getting angry, establish a rule for yourself: count from 1 to 100 before hitting. The process of counting helps calm you down, and only then can you rationally educate your child.

3. Let the child find their own mistakes

Some parents ask, if hitting isn't the answer, what is a reasonable way to criticize or punish a child? Actually, you can let the child find their own mistakes. Parents should calmly and patiently guide the child to identify their own errors. Additionally, parents can collaborate with the child to make rules about how to correct these mistakes or ensure they don't happen again, helping the child develop the habit of taking responsibility for their actions.
