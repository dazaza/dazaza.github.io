Is "crossing boundaries" a blessing or a curse? 2012 Home Appliances Development Forecast

by anonymous on 2012-03-03 11:46:01

In the first half of 2011, China's home appliance market sales scale exceeded 600 billion yuan; for the whole year of 2011, some institutions predicted that the home appliance market sales scale would reach 1.2 trillion yuan, an increase of 20% compared to the 104 billion yuan home appliance sales scale in 2010.

In 2011, with the wave of home appliance intelligence rising one after another, frequent mergers by Chinese home appliance enterprises, and the gradual expiration of policies like "home appliances to rural areas" and "old-for-new exchanges," made the home appliance market exceptionally lively in 2011, further promoting the rapid development of the home appliance market.

In 2012, China's home appliance market still faces opportunities and challenges coexisting, market competition will become more intense, issues such as overcapacity, increased saturation of home appliances, sluggish real estate markets, etc., will all become focal points of attention for many home appliance enterprises. So, what development trends will China's home appliance industry present in 2012? Wantai Home Appliance Network timely launched predictions for the development of home appliances in 2012, hoping it can provide inspiration and assistance to everyone.

・Is the boundary-crossing development of home appliances and IT a blessing or a curse?

Boundary-crossing simply explained means "crossing boundaries or demarcations."

In the home appliance industry, with home appliances gradually becoming smarter, "boundary-crossing" development has become the norm. Apple, Lenovo, Google, and other traditional IT giants have all targeted the smart home appliance sector and have plans to launch smart TVs; at the same time, IT products like tablets are becoming increasingly popular, leading home appliance manufacturers to also enter this field. Haier, Skyworth, TCL, Changhong, and other traditional home appliance enterprises have all launched their own branded tablets, joining the battle; thus, the confrontation between home appliances and IT camps will be even more evident in 2012.

Can the "boundary-crossing" development of these two camps achieve anything?

Firstly, regarding IT companies like Apple and Lenovo entering the smart home appliance market, traditional home appliance companies believe that the entry of IT giants into the smart home appliance market indicates great potential, but for them to dominate the world will be difficult, not as easy as in the IT domain like smartphones. The specific reasons include three points: First, although IT products are close to home appliances, consumer demand points are entirely different, and home appliance companies understand the needs of Chinese consumers better; second, the distribution channels for home appliances differ from those of IT products, especially in second- and third-tier cities where differences are greater, and IT companies need a certain familiarization process to integrate into home appliance channels; third, policy avoidance, the development of domestic televisions differs from IT and will encounter various policy restrictions, and if IT companies rashly enter, they will face many limitations.

For home appliance companies to make significant achievements in the tablet computer field is not easy either, such as lacking sales platforms for flat-screen TV products, insufficient understanding of consumer demands, lack of professional technical talents, no price advantage, etc., all of which require an adaptation process.

With traditional IT groups represented by Apple, Google, and Lenovo entering the home appliance sector and home appliance companies moving into IT, who will go further? In 2012, we will wait and see...

・Prosperity & Crisis - Home Appliance E-commerce May See a Wave of Bankruptcies

In 2011, home appliance e-commerce developed rapidly, especially after Gome and Suning aggressively entered the e-commerce sector, making competition even more fierce, changing the sales landscape repeatedly, and gradually cultivating the habit of online shopping for large home appliances among consumers. Will home appliance e-commerce channels capture half of the traditional channel market share in 2012? Can they develop a business model truly suitable for the development of online home appliance purchases in China? These are worth looking forward to.

Currently, the most obvious feature of home appliance online shopping is price competition, which offers significant advantages compared to traditional channels. Price wars during holidays are also powerful tools used by e-commerce players, but overall, this method of sacrificing profits to gain high turnover is not conducive to the long-term development of the entire industry. The apparent prosperity may just be fleeting.

In 2012, besides price, logistics delivery and after-sales service will receive more attention from large home appliance buyers. For home appliance online shopping enterprises, only through a complete product procurement and sales system, a consumer demand collection system, and a service system, etc., and by transforming their strategic development models to achieve efficient supply-demand chain connections, thereby achieving seamless and rapid responses in consumer demand, product manufacturing, and product circulation links, ultimately enhancing the efficiency of the industrial chain, can they achieve continuous and stable development.

・Content & Olympics - 3D TV Market Share Breaks Through 50%

Many friends who purchased 3D TVs in 2011 complained that their 3D TVs were just "showpieces," and the 3D function was just for show. However, this problem might be resolved in 2012.

Firstly, China Central Television's first 3D channel will begin broadcasting on New Year's Day 2012 and will be shared nationwide. Previously, many provinces such as Tianjin and Shanghai had trial runs of 3D TV channels, but due to regional and content scarcity limitations, their appeal to consumers was weak. Entering 2012, CCTV's 3D channel will start broadcasting, and Beijing TV, Shanghai TV, Tianjin TV, Shenzhen TV, Jiangsu TV, and six other units will all provide content sources, greatly increasing 3D content, which will significantly promote the development of the entire 3D industry chain.

Additionally, it can be expected that 3D sports events will become the main content broadcasted on CCTV's 3D channel. Currently, CCTV has acquired the rights to broadcast the 2012 London Olympics in 3D, providing 300 hours of 3D live broadcasts including the opening and closing ceremonies, athletics, gymnastics, etc., which will further satisfy users' desire to watch 3D content.

Under the impetus of numerous favorable news, industry insiders predict that in 2012, 3D functionality will gradually become a standard feature for large-sized flat-panel TVs, and the sales share of 3D TVs could very well account for 50% of flat-panel TVs.

・Personalization & Intelligence - High-end Home Appliances Break Growth Bottlenecks

There is a saying in the home appliance sector that many people deeply feel — "price wars have exhausted the profits of home appliance manufacturers." In the Chinese market, many home appliance companies, due to excessive reliance on "price wars" forming a culture of thin profit margins and high volume sales, must also endure problems such as gradually decreasing corporate profit margins, severe lack of core competitive products, and frequent anti-dumping cases in overseas markets. To effectively solve this issue and break through this profit growth bottleneck, personalized and high-end strategies are the most recognized approaches.

Nowadays, individuality and style have become synonymous with many young people. They don't follow the beaten path and always have various strange ideas, seemingly ignoring traditional home appliances and conservative styles.

Many companies have started to notice and value this point, gradually shifting towards personalized home appliance marketing by understanding what consumers want to buy, and even offering globally unique TVs. Of course, this custom service is currently not widespread, but it is believed that in 2012, with the rapid development of smart home appliances, customized services for users will become more perfect and better meet personalized user needs.

Furthermore, in 2012, after the application of trinetwork convergence and Internet of Things technology, refrigerators, lights, air conditioners, TVs, DVDs, speakers, microwave ovens, washing machines, and all electrical appliances will enter the smart era. All home appliances can be conveniently controlled via mobile phones or other integrated devices, thereby creating development space for interconnectivity and product upgrades of home appliances. The intelligent development of home appliances may even comprehensively rewrite the current status of the home appliance market and industry structure.

・Replacement & Rural Markets - Second and Third-Tier Cities Become Competition Hotspots

Since the introduction of policies such as "Home Appliances to Rural Areas" and "Old-for-New Exchanges," they have not only added vitality to the home appliance market but also greatly stimulated the rural consumption market, playing a significant role in increasing the sales of home appliances. However, with the gradual conclusion of policies like "Home Appliances to Rural Areas" and "Old-for-New Exchanges," their impact on the home appliance market cannot be underestimated, especially for enterprises with weak market competitiveness and large inventories of "Home Appliances to Rural Areas" products, which will be a considerable blow, and this will also accelerate the transformation of the home appliance market.

Since the introduction of policies like "Home Appliances to Rural Areas" and "Old-for-New Exchanges," the consumption markets in second and third-tier cities have been quickly activated. Industry insiders predict that this consumer demand will continue in 2012, and the consumption market in second and third-tier cities will become a hotspot for competition among home appliance merchants.

Through the guidance of national preferential subsidy policies over the past two years, the level of home appliance consumption in second and third-tier cities has been improved to a certain extent, and it has also made some astute home appliance manufacturers realize the consumption potential of rural markets. In 2012, while ensuring stable market share in urban areas, manufacturers will certainly focus on continuing to develop the rural consumption market. Even without policy subsidies, manufacturers will use promotional tactics to mitigate the effects after the policy period ends.

2012 is a transition period after the end of the policies. For home appliance companies, how to quickly摆脱 dependency on policies, expedite the research and development of new products suitable for market demands, win the market with excellent product quality and comprehensive after-sales service, is also an important topic.
