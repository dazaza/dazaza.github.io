Job Hunting Skills - Secret Tips for Successful Interviews

by longersing on 2009-05-18 23:45:16

The financial crisis has caused a large number of unemployed people, and also made it more difficult for college students to find jobs after graduation. How to perform excellently in the interview can, to some extent, make up for the previous shortcomings in written tests or other conditions such as academic qualifications and majors. Many graduates fail in job hunting because they fail in the interview. We don't want to see college students selling their youth just to find a job.

Under the current situation of workplace exams and the financial crisis, here are three key techniques for successful interviews:

Technique One: Self-introduction should not exceed 2 minutes

"Please introduce yourself" is a question that over 90% of employers will ask. Interviewees should prepare in advance by writing down and memorizing their self-introduction. Actually, the employer already knows the basic information about the interviewee. The purpose of this question is to test the interviewee's language expression ability, logical thinking, and honesty. Therefore, the content of the self-introduction should be consistent with the personal resume, and the way of expression should be as oral as possible. Pay attention to being concise, focusing on the key points, and avoiding irrelevant or useless content. The logic should be clear, and the levels should be distinct. The self-introduction should not exceed 2 minutes, and it's best to control it within 1 minute.

Technique Two: Emphasize a warm and harmonious family atmosphere

"Talk about your family situation" is a question that 70% of employers will ask. Interviewees should briefly introduce their family members, usually only introducing their parents. If relatives are related to the industry being applied for, they can also be introduced. When answering, emphasize the warm and harmonious family atmosphere, the importance parents place on education, the good condition of each family member, and the support from family members for work and the interviewee's sense of responsibility towards the family.

Technique Three: Use sociable hobbies to embellish your image

"Talk about your hobbies" is a question that joint ventures and private enterprises like to ask, because companies mainly want to use this question to understand whether the interviewee has an outgoing personality and team spirit. So, the interviewee must not say they have no hobbies, nor should they say they have vulgar or unpleasant hobbies. When talking about hobbies, it's best not to say that they are limited to solitary activities such as reading, listening to music, or surfing the internet. This might make the interviewer suspect that the applicant is introverted. It's better to have some outdoor group activities like basketball or badminton to "embellish" one's image, highlighting the interviewee's sociability and teamwork abilities.
