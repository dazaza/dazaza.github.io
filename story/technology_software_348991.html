More than 80 MBAs from Tsinghua University Volunteer for the International Automobile City

Report by Wu Xiaoqing

"This year, for the enrollment of master's degrees in engineering, we have already signed employment internship base agreements with MBAs from the top 50 universities nationwide. These include outstanding graduates from prestigious universities such as Tsinghua, Jiaotong, and Tongji," said Zhu Wen, head of the Talent Service Center at Jiading District’s Anting International Automobile City, yesterday. He also mentioned that more than 80 outstanding MBA graduates from Tsinghua University's School of Economics and Management have actively volunteered for positions within the Shanghai International Automobile City this year.

According to my investigation, these more than 80 graduates from renowned universities have an average age of less than 28 years old. Despite having an average of about seven years of work experience, the vast majority do not have a solid background in automotive specialties. Mr. Zhu's first statement during the interview was: "The International Automobile City is currently at a crucial moment of accumulating strength before a significant breakthrough. We still need at least 30,000 professionals of various types, especially those who can contribute comprehensively in areas such as product development and project planning. For instance, in international business settings, appearance and attire are very important. In just this aspect, mainland graduates slightly lag behind 'returnees'."

MBA stands for Master of Business Administration in English, which translates to Master of Business Administration in Chinese.

The MBA originated in the United States. After nearly a century of exploration and effort, it has trained a large number of outstanding business management talents, creating economic miracles and legends for the United States. MBAs are hailed as "heaven's pride" and "management elites," becoming special figures respected and envied by the business world and even society at large. They are even seen as "business heroes" in the public mind. Statistics show that the general managers, chairmen, and other senior executives of the largest 500 companies in the U.S. are predominantly MBAs. This astonishing fact best illustrates the success of MBA education. An MBA signifies exceptional ability, courage, and character; it represents wealth, status, power, and honor; and it foretells hope, success, and brilliance.
