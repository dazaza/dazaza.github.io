Synopsis:

On a future July 2nd, as Americans prepare to celebrate the Fourth of July, monitoring stations across the U.S. detect an enormous spaceship hundreds of miles in diameter approaching Earth. This ship emits radio signals that interfere with Earth's communication satellite signals. The alien fleet surrounds Earth and releases 36 smaller spacecraft, each 15 miles in diameter, hovering over major cities worldwide. People are confused and frightened, while some await the arrival of friendly extraterrestrial envoys.

Computer engineer David discovers that the signal emitted by the aliens is actually a countdown to an attack. David manages to relay this information to his estranged wife Connie, who is an assistant to the President.

The government attempts to contact the aliens but receives no response, causing the President great concern. Upon learning of David's message, he urgently orders an evacuation, but it’s too late. On July 3rd, the alien ships launch devastating attacks on several major American cities. Humanity suffers immense destruction and disaster almost instantly.

The resourceful President, Bill Pullman, calls for global unity against the alien threat, transforming July 4th into Earth's Independence Day. With assistance from computer expert Jeff Goldblum and African-American Air Force pilot Will Smith, Earth launches a full-scale counterattack. The President even personally joins the front lines in a fighter jet. The U.S. Air Force assaults the alien ships, but their efforts prove futile except for one instance where pilot Steven lures an alien combat vessel into crashing.

Survivors begin fleeing after the attack. The President escapes at the last moment with David and others, arriving at the Roswell Base, codenamed Area 51. There, scientists have been studying a crashed alien spacecraft from over 50 years ago. Due to the Defense Secretary's political ambitions, the President was unaware of this research, leading to the failure to prevent the tragedy earlier.

Steven captures an alien prisoner and brings it to the Roswell base. Through brainwave communication, the President learns of the aliens' intentions and decides to conduct a nuclear strike, which fails due to the alien ship's protective force field. Humanity's last hope seems lost. Steven returns survivors to the base, including his girlfriend and her son, as well as the critically injured First Lady, who later passes away, leaving the President deeply saddened.

David devises a plan to disable the aliens' protective force field using a computer virus. After repairing the 50-year-old alien spacecraft, Steven prepares to infiltrate the alien mothership with David aboard the repaired craft. Simultaneously, the U.S. issues a signal for a global counterattack to surviving armies worldwide.

On July 4th, Steven and David successfully infiltrate the alien mothership, upload the virus, and disable the protective force field. The President personally pilots a plane to battle the aliens. Steven and David launch a nuclear bomb into the center of the alien mothership and narrowly escape just in time.

The aliens are defeated, and David and Steven return safely. Humanity survives the ordeal, defeating the alien invaders at the last moment. Amidst the ruins, humanity begins rebuilding their homes.
