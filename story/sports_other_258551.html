Bolt sets a record to win the 100m gold medal.

by qq1035886355 on 2009-11-11 16:46:00

The 22-year-old Jamaican recorded a time of 9.58 seconds, reducing the mark he set last year when winning gold at the Beijing Olympics by 0.11 seconds.

American Tyson Gay finished second with a time of 9.71 seconds, while Jamaica's Asafa Powell claimed bronze in 9.84 seconds.

Britain’s Dwain Chambers came sixth in a season's best time of 10.00 seconds.

Bolt, who set three world records when securing his Olympic golds in Beijing last summer, delivered another extraordinary performance to further enhance his reputation as the greatest sprinter of all time.

In the final, he powered out of the blocks on the first attempt and took control of the race within the first 30 meters, the crowd going wild as he streaked across the finish line.

Bolt's time represents the largest improvement in the record since electronic timing was introduced in 1968.

"I was ready, I was feeling good after the semi-finals," Bolt told BBC Sport.

"I knew it was going to be a great race and I came out and executed it. It's a great time. I did well and I feel good about myself."

Former world record holder Powell paid tribute to his compatriot, saying: "When I saw the time I had to try and catch him, but I couldn't."

Gay, who entered the final as the reigning world champion, has been troubled by a lingering groin injury that cut into his start practice.

"I ran the best I could but it was not enough," he said. "I believe I put in a championship performance and I am very pleased with the national record.

"I'm happy he ran 9.5 because I knew he could do it. I'm happy for him."

Chambers, back competing at the top level after serving a two-year ban for taking the designer steroid THG in 2003, described the final as a "great experience."

"It is hard to explain what it is like to go out there and stand on the line to compete with the best in the world," said the 31-year-old. "It doesn't get easier as you get older but it is worth it."

Earlier, there was controversy as Britain’s Tyrone Edgar was disqualified from the semi-finals.

After Bolt made the opening false start, Edgar was ruled to have transgressed on the second occasion, although initial reaction times seemed to suggest that decision was harsh.

"I don’t think it was a false start," said the 27-year-old Edgar, who was also disqualified at the London Grand Prix last month.

"To me it looked pretty good but there is nothing I can do. I am not going to argue the point. I am disappointed right now because I reckon I would have made the final."
