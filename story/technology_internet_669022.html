March flies with spring snow. The Kingdom of Thailand, abbreviated as Thailand, borders Laos and Cambodia to the east, faces the Gulf of Thailand and Malaysia to the south, and connects with Myanmar and the Andaman Sea to the west. Before May 11, 1949, Thailand was named Siam. Thailand is one of the important tourist countries in Asia, and its charming tropical scenery and unique Buddhist culture are important factors that attract tourists. Thailand is a historical Buddhist country. This beautiful country known as the "White Elephant Kingdom" has unique cultural traditions and national customs, such as various colorful festivals, fresh lives of people living on water, world-famous classical dances and national dances, interesting mime and Loh Kun theater, unique Thai boxing, cockfighting, playing fish, and beautiful transgenders, all make people "forget to return". Here, everywhere are dazzlingly magnificent temples and pagodas with pointed corners reaching high into the sky, and there are exquisite and beautiful Buddha statues, stone carvings, and paintings everywhere. These ancient sites under the perennial green coconut groves add infinite brilliant colors to Thailand's bewitchingly attractive tropical scenery.

Buddhism is the state religion of Thailand, and in this sacred land, most beautiful and luxurious buildings are related to Buddhism.

The four-faced Buddha

Although this four-faced Buddha is not as famous as the one at Erawan Shrine, it is also a deity from Indian Brahmanism, showing compassion, wisdom, limitless joy, and answering all prayers. The four hands of the Four-faced Buddha hold a staff, two golden keys, a copper object, a scripture, and a string of prayer beads around the neck. It is not only revered by ordinary Thai believers but also attracts many Southeast Asian tourists who come specifically to worship and offer blessings and repayments.

The shrine is also a tourist shopping area.

Pattaya

Pattaya City is located 147 kilometers southeast of Bangkok. Originally a small fishing village in the Gulf of Siam, it has now been replaced by various luxury hotels, holiday homes, and simple guest rooms, becoming a famous tourist resort in Thailand, known as the "Oriental Hawaii."

Nong Nooch Tropical Garden

Nong Nooch Tropical Garden is nestled against mountains and beside the sea, about a 15-minute drive from the Pattaya city center, covering an area of five hundred acres. There is an artificial lake inside the garden, where the water shimmers, and rivers wind through pavilions and terraces. Trees are lush, birds sing, flowers bloom, and the scenery is picturesque. The garden has a cultural village that showcases Thai national culture. The programs in the garden consist of three main parts: Thai folk performances, elephant shows, and a botanical garden.

Nong Nooch Tropical Garden is a private garden created by Mr. Wang Liang, a Thai-Chinese, who is one of Thailand's ten richest tycoons.

Pattaya Botanical Garden

Inside the botanical garden of Nong Nooch Tropical Garden, there are multiple sections including coconut groves, orchid gardens, European-style garden areas, and Thai-style garden areas. The garden is beautifully trimmed and maintained by specialists, making it a great place for photography. There are also viewing platforms built within the garden, allowing visitors to walk on them and enjoy the entire garden.

Driving along the seaside in Pattaya (seaside view captured from inside the car window)

Taking a boat ride out to sea in Pattaya, wearing shorts, elephant shirts, and flip-flops. I spent 350 baht on the boat to play parasailing pulled by a speedboat, freely soaring like a bird in the air.

Kwai River

Kwai River became famous due to the movie "The Bridge Over the River Kwai." The Kwai River Bridge is an inconspicuous railway bridge on the Kwai River. It is located next to a town called Kanchanaburi in the Kanchanaburi Province of Thailand, with the other end of the bridge leading to Myanmar. The Kwai River Bridge is a world-famous bridge. During World War II, it was the most famous symbol on the "Death Railway" built by Japanese imperialism for invading Myanmar. (Due to camera battery issues, no photos were taken of the Kwai River Bridge.)

The Kwai River is more than 100 kilometers away from Bangkok. There are floating restaurants and floating markets here, which are shopping paradises for tourists visiting Thailand. Shopping only requires saying "Sawadee Ka" (Hello).

The Kwai River before sunset is tranquil and peaceful - we took a boat ride while having dinner and enjoying the scenic views along the shore.
