Elop changes Nokia's bad habits and attracts users with Lumia 1020.

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 16:19:48

After taking office, Nokia CEO Stephen Elop corrected Nokia's "arrogance" problem and accelerated the product launch cycle. With its latest high-end flagship product, the Lumia 1020, it is believed that consumer attention will be drawn back to Nokia. Elop recalled a meeting in August 2011 where the company leadership was discussing the name of its first Windows Phone.

Elop said: "We almost fell into the same trap again, spending more time deciding on the name of this phone because we couldn't agree." But ultimately, Elop demanded an answer by the end of the day. Elop said: "Why wait until tomorrow or next week? We need to make the decision today. Eventually, we chose Lumia." Some long-time Nokia employees stated that Elop forced them to make quick decisions on almost everything, from restructuring to the development of new phones.

Nokia indeed had no time to waste. Currently, Nokia's competitiveness in the global smartphone market is increasingly being questioned. Its market share is only about 3%, far behind Samsung and Apple. In the second fiscal quarter, Lumia phone sales did not meet analyst expectations. Due to a sharp decline in cash reserves, some analysts believe that Elop's time is running out. This year, Nokia has accelerated the pace of bringing products to market. On the 11th of this month, Nokia launched the latest Lumia 1020 smartphone, equipped with a 41 million pixel camera.

Elop noted that the N8, which was based on the Symbian system, took approximately 22 months from development to market, whereas now many Windows Phones take only 6 to 8 months.

Veteran Nokia employee Samuli Hanninen said: "I like this rhythm; it feels like Nokia 10 years ago." Elop stated that one issue facing Nokia is: having been in the industry lead for many years, it is not easy to face challenges. Therefore, Elop encourages employees to have a mindset ready to accept challenges.

He said: "My real intention is to avoid complacency. Over the past 6 to 7 years, there have been many arrogant behaviors within the company, unable to courageously confront new trends, which affected the company for many years." Alf Noto, head of Nokia's customer care department, said that this attitude also reflects in interactions with customers. Noto indicated that Elop now replies to 10 to 20 user emails every day.

An insider also mentioned that Nokia treats its partners humbly, including carriers, retailers, and developers. Christof Hellmis, an executive at Nokia's Here Maps service, said: "Elop can put aside his pride, something Nokia wasn't able to do before."

So far, Elop has not proven that switching to the Windows Phone platform was the right choice, as Google's Android and Apple's iOS hold about 90% of the market share. Chris Weber, Nokia's Executive Vice President of Global Sales and Marketing, said that through an expanded product line, joint promotion of the Lumia 1020 with Microsoft and AT&T, it is believed that Nokia's smartphone sales will increase.

Gartner analyst Carolina Milanesi is also optimistic, saying: "Their current approach is correct. Success doesn't happen overnight, but I believe the Lumia 1020 will attract consumers to re-focus on Nokia."
