Zou Ji selected as lead author coordinator of IPCC's Fifth Assessment Report

by ditan360 on 2010-06-17 14:40:02

Low Carbon Network - Recently, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) announced the list of lead authors for the Fifth Assessment Report (AR5). Professor JI Zou from the School of Environment, after a rigorous global selection process, was ultimately selected as one of the Coordinating Lead Authors (CLA) for the third working group. The letter from the Department of Science and Technology and Climate Change of the China Meteorological Administration pointed out: "Being selected as an author for the IPCC AR5 is of great significance to our country's efforts in addressing climate change, and it will help enhance our influence in the scientific field of climate change response."

The IPCC is an intergovernmental organization jointly established by the World Meteorological Organization (WMO) and the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) in 1988. Its main task is to conduct scientific evaluations on the current state of climate change science knowledge, the potential impacts of climate change on society and the economy, and possible strategies for adapting to and mitigating climate change. The IPCC has successively released four assessment reports in 1990, 1995, 2001, and 2007, which represent the most important scientific basis for the international community to establish cooperative mechanisms and take action in response to climate change. It holds a crucial position in the international climate change process.

According to the decision made at the 28th session of the IPCC plenary meeting, the IPCC Secretariat will organize the preparation of the Fifth Scientific Assessment Report (AR5), which will be completed in 2014. The 41st session of the IPCC Bureau Meeting held in Geneva from May 17-20, 2010, reviewed and approved the list of lead authors for the fifth assessment report. From the 3,260 candidates recommended by governments around the world, 835 lead authors were selected. Among the 190 candidates recommended by the Chinese government, 48 were ultimately selected.

The IPCC has three working groups, each consisting of co-chairs of the working group, coordinating lead authors of each chapter, lead authors, and reviewers. The primary responsibility of the third working group is to evaluate options for limiting greenhouse gas emissions and mitigating climate change. Globally, 1,081 people were recommended for the third working group, and 270 authors were ultimately selected, including 3 co-chairs and 32 coordinating lead authors. Eighteen Chinese experts were selected to be part of the writing team for the third working group of the Fifth Assessment Report "Climate Change 2014: Mitigation of Climate Change," serving in roles such as coordinating lead authors, lead authors, and reviewers. Professor JI Zou is one of the two Chinese experts who have been appointed as coordinating lead authors for the third working group this time.

This author team for the third working group includes renowned economists worldwide, such as Nobel laureate Kenneth Arrow, Amartya Sen, Parta Dasgupta, head of the Economics Department at Cambridge University, Robert Stavins, professor at the Harvard Kennedy School, and Warwick McKibbin, economic advisor to the Australian Prime Minister.

Professor JI Zou will work together with Robert Stavins, professor at the Harvard Kennedy School, as the coordinating lead authors for Chapter 13 "International Cooperation: Agreements and Policy Instruments." As a coordinating lead author, in addition to fulfilling the responsibilities of a lead author, Professor JI Zou will also play a leadership role in ensuring that any cross-cutting scientific or technical issues that may involve several chapters of the report are handled in a complete and coherent manner, reflecting the latest information. He will be fully responsible for coordinating the writing of related sections of the report.

It is understood that Professor JI Zou has long been engaged in teaching and research in the fields of energy and climate change, and served as a member of the Chinese delegation to the United Nations climate change negotiations from 2000 to 2009. Professor JI Zou also served as a lead author for the third working group of the Fourth Assessment Report of the IPCC, and collectively won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2007. The completion time of the Fifth Assessment Report of the IPCC will be a critical period for international negotiations on the post-2020 international mechanism for responding to climate change. The conclusions on key issues in the assessment report will have a significant impact on the international negotiation process. Therefore, Professor JI Zou, as a representative of Chinese scientists, taking up the position of coordinating lead author for the third working group of the IPCC, and being responsible for writing the highly sensitive and attention-grabbing hot chapter "International Cooperation: Agreements and Policy Instruments," will help enhance the international voice of Chinese academia in the construction of the international management system for climate change, and have a significant impact on international climate change policy decisions. This indicates that the "Energy and Climate Economics Project (PECE)" of Renmin University of China has already gained significant influence in domestic and international policy circles and academic circles.
