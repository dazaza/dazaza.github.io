Customer's voice: Frequent prepayment for group buying scams

by beetgk2958 on 2012-02-08 09:57:09

Group buying, online food ordering, online ticket purchasing, and online apartment renting... As the O2O market continues to expand, third-party payment services are attracting customers by introducing an escrow payment model to ensure secure consumption. Reporters learned yesterday that Huifu has taken the lead in launching group-buying escrow payments, allowing consumers to complete their payments only after they are satisfied with the service. Meanwhile, Alipay is also considering introducing a travel escrow transaction, adopting a "pay after you play" new model.

Customer Voice: Group Buying Often Leads to Disappointment

Currently, group buying has become a habitual way of consuming for the younger generation. However, there are frequent instances where customers pay upfront but later face disappointments during consumption, as the actual services provided by merchants significantly differ from the advertisements on group-buying websites.

"The introduction of escrow payments in group buying is good news for customers, as it can prevent merchants from providing services that don't match what was advertised," said customer Mr. Li to reporters.

Another customer, Miss Zhang, believes that merchants on group-buying sites vary greatly in quality. Introducing the escrow payment model into group buying, similar to shopping on Taobao, allows customers to be more proactive. They can decide whether to pay based on their satisfaction with the merchant's service, which could help improve the service quality of group-buying merchants.

Innovative Model: Introducing Escrow Payments to Group Buying

When reporters experienced well-known group-buying sites such as Gaopeng, Nuomi, and Manzuo, they found that consumers must first pay the consumption amount to merchants through the group-buying site. Afterward, when enjoying discounted services, everything is entirely at the discretion of the merchant. Even if consumers are dissatisfied with the service quality, they have no control over the situation.

However, Huifu, a third-party payment company, has introduced an innovative escrow payment model as part of its O2O industry chain payment solution. In this model, consumers first deposit their payment amounts with a third-party payment platform, which then provides guarantees to group-buying merchants. Once the consumer is satisfied with the service, the payment is completed. This helps increase users' trust in merchants.

Actually, the O2O market includes not only group buying but also other lifestyle consumption methods like online food ordering, online ticket purchasing, and online apartment renting. The large potential young customer base is one of the key reasons why third-party payment platforms are eager to enter the O2O market.

"The O2O platform has evolved from the stage of competing for users to the stage where service is paramount," Huifu President Zhou Ye told reporters. Therefore, a trustworthy payment platform is a crucial factor in expanding O2O business.
