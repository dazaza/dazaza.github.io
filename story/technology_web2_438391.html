But I don't know why I have 3000 yuan by the end of the year.

by opde36418 on 2011-05-28 09:29:45

Now I find myself to be a materialistic person.

When I first started university, my mom gave me 300 yuan per month as living expenses, and I was pretty satisfied with that. I remember our dorm senior sister often being criticized by us because she frequently went shopping for clothes. But all of that came from her saving 200 yuan per month for living expenses; she was the type who would buy clothes as soon as she had money. Others didn't even have that much for living expenses, so it's clear that I was relatively content.

During winter and summer vacations, I found out that people like Jingjing in the local area had monthly living expenses of five or six hundred yuan, so I strongly expressed my dissatisfaction and protested to my mom. As a result, during my second year, my living expenses increased to 400 yuan RMB per month, and I would also ask my mom for money under various pretexts. So at that time, my material life was very satisfying. I even saved up money to buy a CD player on my own, feeling particularly accomplished, and began pursuing so-called brand names. I lived comfortably in that environment at He Yi (Henan Medical University).

In my third year, it rose to 500 yuan, how disgusting! The food I made wasn’t edible, and in my fourth year, it increased to 600 yuan. At that time, I felt incredibly happy, thinking of myself as a rich person, a rich person who had an income of 600 yuan per month! Haha~

In my fifth year, I was preparing for postgraduate exams, so I wasn't very focused on money. However, by the end of the year, I surprisingly discovered that I had saved 3000 yuan. It seemed that every time dad visited me, he would give me money, and when I stayed up late studying, he still gave me money to enroll in various postgraduate exam tutoring classes. Anyway, I was quite clear about attending all the sessions of Yunpeng’s political and English classes. In the past, whenever I went home every week, I only had 2 yuan left because I had to take bus number 301 home.

Now that I'm working, I know that my monthly salary is over a thousand yuan, and my bonus is also over a thousand yuan, but I strangely feel much poorer. When I go shopping with Jingjing, I see that woman buying piles of clothes and cosmetics, and I wonder how she has so much money. A single cosmetic item costs 259 yuan! My money seems to disappear quickly, and I barely dare to buy anything. Let alone any brand name stuff; in two months of working, I've only bought clothes once, and when it comes to cosmetics, I only dare to buy the practical ones. Social engagements are minimized as much as possible, and I don't dare to go out. I still feel incredibly poor.

Why do I feel poorer the more money I have? Autumn health tips!
