Understanding the role of collagen, and easily become a beautiful woman.

Nowadays, the most popular skin care products are none other than collagen. As a beauty-loving woman, the more you understand about collagen, the closer you get to health and beauty. Below, the editor will take you to understand the relevant knowledge about collagen:

1. Collagen and Skin: Collagen is the main component of skin collagen, accounting for 70-80% of the total protein in the skin. The collagen molecule has a triple helix structure that other proteins do not have, and is linked with a large number of anionic side chains. The triple helix structure and anionic side chain can firmly lock water molecules, thereby giving the skin high elasticity and tension, which can not only stretch outward, but also quickly shrink back. A decrease in the amount and quality of collagen leads to a decrease in elasticity. The loose skin on an elderly person's neck cannot quickly shrink back after being stretched.

2. Functions of collagen:

1) Locking water molecules to maintain skin moisture and elasticity, blocking the formation of brown pigments, and keeping the skin healthy (preventing spots, peeling, red blood vessels, etc.)

2) Nourishing hair to make it black and shiny

3) Nourishing capillary walls: Capillary walls are also composed of collagen. A decrease in collagen content in capillary walls makes the vessels thinner and transparent, leading to the appearance of red blood vessels on the face. Supplementing enough collagen can prevent the formation of red blood vessels.

4) Cell proliferation, growth, metabolism, etc.: Collagen is the nutrition and environment for cell growth, promoting cell proliferation, growth, and metabolism, thereby enhancing the body's immunity and energy.

5) Promoting bone formation and preventing osteoporosis.

3. Collagen is the young molecule that keeps the skin beautiful:

As age increases, the content and quality of collagen in the skin gradually decrease, making collagen fibers dry, coarse, and stiff. This also causes water loss in the skin, and aging features appear on the face. The process of collagen content decreasing and collagen fiber aging begins at the age of 20.

4. Taking collagen has a significant tightening effect on the skin, making loose skin tight and strong:

This tightening effect includes correcting facial skin sagging, leg muscle relaxation, and abdominal bulging.

5. Oral collagen can prevent and treat UV radiation damage:

UV radiation severely damages collagen fibers, making them age and reducing their content. Only by taking collagen orally and promoting the synthesis of new collagen in the body can UV damage to the skin be prevented and treated.

6. Collagen fibers are the functional molecules that keep the breasts upright and beautiful:

1) Collagen has a unique triple helix structure, forming suspensory ligaments composed of collagen fibers, which act as "steel cables", firmly fixing the breasts on the skin and body wall.

2) Collagen fibers are widely distributed in the breasts. Young collagen fibers have a dense structure, like an elastic net that pulls and supports breast tissue.

3) The collagen fibers in the breast surface skin must be youthful and strong to tighten the skin of the breast surface, making the breasts stand upright and regain youthful charm.

7. Weight loss and collagen:

Weight loss is achieved through different methods to excrete or aspirate excess fat accumulated under the skin. The relationship between fat and skin collagen is similar to the relationship between "grain" and "bag". If some "grain" is poured out, the "bag" will deflate. After weight loss, wrinkles appear on the face and the abdominal skin sags due to the removal of fat. Taking collagen orally can timely deliver highly elastic triple helix collagen fibers to the skin, tighten the skin, increase skin tension, and prevent the skin from sagging and developing wrinkles after weight loss.

8. Taking collagen has a regulatory effect on many gynecological and obstetric diseases:

Because the endometrium is composed of collagen fibers. In a normal menstrual cycle, the endometrium matures (rich in collagen fibers) and sheds (loss of collagen fibers) according to hormonal changes. After the endometrium falls off, the uterus needs to quickly synthesize new collagen to repair the endometrium. However, due to the lack of hydroxyproline, a raw material for synthesizing collagen in food, or excessive stress from study or work, the endometrium cannot reach sufficient thickness, leading to irregular bleeding and causing menstrual disorders. Many girls and women who suffer from menstrual disorders see their menstruation become regular after taking collagen for 1-2 months. Similarly, collagen plays a nurturing role in postpartum and post-abortion uterine recovery, and taking collagen can quickly repair the damaged endometrium during childbirth and abortion, allowing women to receive timely care.
