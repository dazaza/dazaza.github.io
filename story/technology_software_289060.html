The new version of the national medical insurance medicine catalogue is released, with 260 new medicines added

by 0deq589k6s on 2009-12-02 14:40:37

Core Tip: The Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security officially announced the new version of the National Medical Insurance Drug Directory on November 30. In the new edition of the "Drug Directory", 260 drugs were added, an increase of 13.7%. Class A are security drugs which can be fully reimbursed, while the reimbursement ratio for Class B drugs is determined by each locality.

Video Description: All therapeutic drugs in the "National Essential Drug List" have been included and can be fully reimbursed. Song Song made the chart. People's Daily reported on December 1 that the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security officially released the "National Basic Medical Insurance, Work-related Injury Insurance and Maternity Insurance Drug Directory (2009 Edition)" (hereinafter referred to as the "Drug Directory"). This adjustment mainly targets Western medicine and traditional Chinese medicine. After the adjustment, there are a total of 2151 varieties of Western medicine and traditional Chinese medicine in the new edition of the "Drug Directory". There are 1164 kinds of Western medicine, including 349 Class A drugs, 791 Class B drugs, 20 only for work-related injury insurance, and 4 only for maternity insurance; there are 987 kinds of traditional Chinese medicine, including 154 Class A drugs and 833 Class B drugs.

What are the characteristics of the new edition of the directory? What impact will it have on insured persons? Yao Hong, director of the Medical Insurance Department of the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security, gave an interpretation.

What changes are there in the types of drugs in the "Drug Directory"? Will the drugs that could be reported originally suddenly be removed from the directory and not be reimbursable?

A total of 260 drugs have been added; chronic disease drugs will only be removed if no one uses them.

Compared with the 2004 edition of the drug directory, the new edition of the "Drug Directory" has added a total of 260 drugs, an increase of 13.7%, of which Class A drugs have increased by 53, an increase of 11.8%. The added drugs are all new drugs that have been clinically verified to be effective and highly safe in recent years. Ethnic medicines and Chinese herbal pieces will continue to be implemented according to existing policies.

The use of Class A drugs will begin in December this year. However, adjustments are still being made to Class B drugs in various places. According to regulations, provinces (regions, municipalities) should release their local basic medical insurance, work-related injury insurance, and maternity insurance drug directories before March 31, 2010. By then, insured persons will be able to fully use the new edition of the drug directory. Yao Hong said: "The update of the computer information management system drug database may not be completed until June 30, 2010. However, localities must not refuse to pay insured persons' fees on the grounds that the drug database has not been updated."

At the same time, some drugs in the original directory that can be better replaced or are not used by anyone have also been removed. "We considered that it might not be feasible to suddenly say that the drugs that were previously reimbursable are no longer reimbursable. Therefore, we collected a medication database. For chronic disease drugs, as long as someone uses them, they will generally be retained."

Why does the "Drug Directory" need to add medication restrictions?

Medication restrictions have increased by 12 percentage points, and the principle of antibiotic classification has been proposed to avoid drug abuse.

Yao Hong introduced that there are a total of 2151 varieties of Western medicine and traditional Chinese medicine in the new edition of the "Drug Directory", of which 843 restrictions have been made, including restrictions on insurance types, hospital levels, and indications, which is an increase of 12 percentage points compared to the 2004 edition of the "Drug Directory".

The Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security explicitly stated that localities should strengthen the management of designated medical institutions and retail pharmacies using the "Drug Directory". In principle, similar drugs under each smallest classification should not be superimposed. Penalties should be clearly defined and incorporated into designated agreements for unreasonable medication use, such as prescribing traditional Chinese medicine based on Western medicine diagnosis, prescribing Western medicine based on traditional Chinese medicine diagnosis, repeated medication use, and drug abuse. Measures should be taken to encourage doctors to choose drugs according to the principles of first Class A then Class B, first oral preparations then injections, and first immediate-release preparations then sustained (controlled)-release preparations. Pharmacists should be encouraged to first choose cheaper drugs among the same variety and formulation when dispensing drugs.

"We found during a survey of 150,000 people that 'three elements and one soup' were used very improperly," Yao Hong said. "'Three elements' refer to the abuse of antibiotics, vitamins, and hormones, and 'one soup' refers to the situation in some places where everyone is given intravenous drips. Therefore, this time we followed the principle of antibiotic classification management, proposed a classification principle, clarified which antibiotics can only be used when other antibiotics are ineffective; which antibiotics can only be used when there is bacteriological support; and which antibiotics can only be used in cases of severe illness or under the guidance of senior doctors or hospitals."

Yao Hong said that the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security will work together with relevant ministries to make full use of basic data on drug usage, analyze the amount of various drugs used by insured persons and the expenditure on various insurance costs, strengthen key monitoring of drugs with high usage and high cost expenditures, and take targeted regulatory measures to strengthen control over adverse behaviors such as drug abuse during the medical process.

Can all the drugs in the "Drug Directory" be reimbursed?

Class A are security drugs which can be fully reimbursed; the reimbursement ratio for Class B drugs is determined by each locality.

In the medical insurance drug directory, Class A directory is a security directory, and the drugs in it are fully reimbursed. Yao Hong introduced: "The principle of determining the Class A drug directory is that even the poorest county in the country can use it. We ensure that there are drugs in every smallest classification directory, but we select relatively cheaper ones." It is understood that all therapeutic drugs in the "National Essential Drug Directory" have been included in the new edition of the "Drug Directory" as Class A drugs.

For drugs in the Class B directory, localities have a 15% adjustment authority. Yao Hong said: "Mainly considering different medication habits in different places, for example, wind-cold colds are common in the north, while wind-heat colds are more common in the south." In addition, regions can set a certain personal co-payment ratio for Class B drugs based on the fund's ability to bear the cost, and then reimburse according to the basic medical insurance regulations. That is to say, Class B drugs may not necessarily be fully reimbursed.

It is worth noting that the work-related injury insurance and maternity insurance drug directories are not divided into Class A and Class B, and all can be reimbursed. Chinese herbal pieces use the exclusion method, specifying drugs for which the fund does not pay. For national free antiretroviral drugs provided by the state and drugs involved in national basic public health projects such as anti-tuberculosis drugs, antimalarial drugs, and anti-schistosomiasis drugs, if insured persons use them and they fall within the scope of public health payments, the basic medical insurance, work-related injury insurance, and maternity insurance funds will not pay; if they do not fall within the scope of public health payments, the basic medical insurance, work-related injury insurance, and maternity insurance funds will pay according to regulations.

Are drugs outside the "Drug Directory" not reimbursed at all?

Special diseases and emergency rescue drugs can also be reimbursed after application; negotiation access drug categories will also be determined.

"Orphan disease" refers to special diseases with a chance of occurrence of only a few out of millions or tens of millions. According to regulations, for drugs outside the directory that are necessary for clinical emergency rescue and treatment of special diseases, a designated medical institution application system will be established with corresponding review and management methods. That is to say, if the application is made and passes the review, these drugs can also be reimbursed.

In addition, the "Drug Directory" published this time does not include negotiation access drugs. Yao Hong introduced: "Our department will study and formulate relevant rules for the drug negotiation mechanism together with relevant departments, establish a corresponding working organization system, determine the drug categories for negotiation access, and negotiate with social insurance agencies and drug suppliers regarding the payment methods and standards for some drug varieties with confirmed clinical efficacy and major innovative value but high prices that may pose risks to the fund."

(Source: People's Daily)
