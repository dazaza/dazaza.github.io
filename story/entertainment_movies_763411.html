Synopsis:

In 1958, during a primary school celebration, a group of students were asked to draw pictures that would be placed into a "time capsule" for preservation. All the students took their task seriously except for one mysterious and peculiar girl who didn't draw anything on her paper but instead carelessly scribbled some numbers.

Fifty years later, out of curiosity, a group of students opened the time capsule to check its seal and examine its contents. The mysterious girl's paper with those strange numbers eventually found its way into the hands of a boy named Caleb Kestler, who then handed it over to his father, Professor John Kestler. Carefully analyzing the seemingly ordinary numbers, Professor Kestler uncovered a hidden secret — these numbers weren't randomly written down by the girl; each number was closely linked to major disasters that had occurred over the past 50 years. These numbers functioned like precise coordinates, marking the exact times and dates of when the disasters happened and when people perished without any deviation.

Professor Kestler realized that these unusual numbers were actually prophecies. After further studying the numbers, he discovered another prophecy about to come true — the Earth would be destroyed. Terrified, John tried to get the authorities' attention to prevent this disaster in advance, but they showed no interest in what seemed like mere numerical games. Determined, John managed to track down the mysterious girl’s daughter and granddaughter from 50 years ago, inviting them to join his cause to stop the impending catastrophe. Although reluctant at first, they eventually got involved. Naturally, Professor Kestler became the leader of this team, initiating the battle to save the Earth.
