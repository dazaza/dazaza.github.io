Taxi driver runs red lights to send passenger who was vomiting blood to the hospital

by xiaoyou234l on 2011-06-10 11:38:00

Our news (reported by Li Jun, correspondent Liu Xuejing): "Please help me find this good taxi driver." Yesterday morning, Qu Hanchen, who lives in the Houhu district, called Huachang Taxi Company to look for the driver who drove him and his son through red lights at high speed on the previous evening to send his son, who was spitting blood, to the hospital.

At around 10 p.m. on the 18th, Qu Hanchen's 22-year-old son, who had undergone a throat operation, suddenly had his stitches rupture, causing non-stop bleeding. Qu Hanchen quickly went downstairs to the Tongxin Garden where he lived and flagged down a blue taxi with the license plate number E AXG817.

After getting into the car, Qu Hanchen informed the driver of his son's condition and hoped to reach the hospital as soon as possible. Upon learning that there was a critically ill patient in the car holding a plastic bucket and continuously spitting blood, the blue taxi driver immediately stepped on the gas pedal and sped off. Due to it being late at night, there were not many vehicles on the road, so when passing through several red-light intersections, the driver ran the red lights.

Qu Hanchen worried that the well-meaning taxi driver might receive traffic violation tickets for running red lights, so he urgently called 122 to plead on behalf of the taxi driver to the traffic management department. After understanding the entire situation, the 122 operator asked Qu Hanchen to call an internal phone number of the traffic management department, detailing the incident and the route taken, and assured that after verification, the driver's violations during this time would be exempt from punishment.

At 11:30 p.m., the life-saving taxi finally arrived at the provincial People's Hospital where Qu Hanchen's son was undergoing surgery. In the rush to get his son treated, Qu Hanchen didn't have time to inquire about the taxi driver's name, only remembering the Huachang Blue Taxi emblem.

At 3 a.m. yesterday, Qu Hanchen's son finally escaped danger. It was only then that he remembered he hadn't properly thanked this taxi driver. As soon as the office opened, he called Huachang Company. "I must personally tell this good taxi driver that my son is out of danger."
