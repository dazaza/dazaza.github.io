The most excellent and efficient pre-production and post-production shooting setup

by pex9wew8n on 2011-08-12 16:32:00

Since 2007, when Sony launched the first handheld memory card camcorder PMW-EX1, followed by the semi-shoulder design EX3 in 2008 and then the successor model of EX1, EX1R in 2009, several generations of EX series models have been widely favored by users for their ultra-high image quality and cost performance. The PMW-EX1R added SD DVCAM recording function on the basis of HD recording format, which can meet the needs of TV stations, industry users, and commercial users in their work. It is the only handheld camcorder in the industry that uses a 1/2-inch imager, and each imager reaches an effective pixel of 1920 x 1080, highly recommended by many TV station users, with a market share far ahead of similar products.

In 2010, in order to further enrich the XDCAM EX product line and meet the needs of more users, Sony introduced the third product in this series - PMW-EX330. The Sony PMW-EX330 is a full-size shoulder-mounted HD camcorder designed in the same style as broadcast-grade equipment. Not only is it a full HD ENG camcorder, but through additional accessories, it can also be a fully functional studio camcorder. The launch of the PMW-EX330, a shoulder-mounted HD camcorder using new medium storage method, will inevitably break the traditional concept of previous HD models, attracting continuous attention from many users. To understand the advantages of this model compared to earlier models and similar products, the [DVCLUB] Product Evaluation Lab conducted a comprehensive test on this model, aiming to let more users understand this model. Professional body, reasonable layout. First, in terms of the body, as the first shoulder-mounted HD camcorder in Sony's EX series, its ingenious body design conforms to ergonomics, while having excellent mobile flexibility, suitable for comfortable shooting operations in various occasions. Due to the low center of gravity of this model, photographers can maintain full stability during long-term shooting. Compared to the semi-shoulder design of the EX3, the PMW-EX330 has a wider operating space between the body and the lens handle, allowing photographers to operate more comfortably and flexibly. (The position of the shoulder pad at the bottom of the body can be adjusted).

In terms of the lens, the Sony PMW-EX330K is equipped with a high-quality Fujinon 1/2-inch 16x HD zoom lens. This lens was specially customized by Fujinon for the Sony PMW-330, using advanced lens production technology, achieving a 16x zoom, small in size and light in weight. Its wide-angle end can reach 5.8mm (equivalent to 35mm), fully meeting the requirements for working in tight indoor spaces. In addition, this lens allows unique focusing operation, with two independent focus and zoom rings at the lens end. The focus ring can slide back and forth to switch between automatic focus and full manual focus modes. And under full manual focus mode, the special "manual focus assist" function can also help photographers focus. In this mode, if the user stops adjusting the focus ring, the auto-focus function will automatically activate. Thus, ensuring that the subject in the foreground of the frame is always clear. If you feel that a 16x zoom is not enough, you can choose other 1/2-inch HD television lenses, such as the newly released XS20s6.3BRM by Fujinon, which is a 20x telephoto lens that can well cooperate with the PMW-EX330. Adding a wide-angle attachment WCV-X85 to the XS20sx6.3BRM can achieve a wide-angle effect, providing a high-cost-performance wide-angle solution.

In terms of the LCD screen and viewfinder, it adopts the same integrated design as the previous generation product, with a viewfinder eyepiece added to the LCD screen. If you want to directly watch the LCD screen, you can flip up or remove the eyepiece. You can also rotate the viewfinder up to 180 degrees towards the subject, allowing videographers to observe the effect of the shot whether shooting from above or at a low angle. People other than the photographer can also conveniently observe the effect of the shot. Its 3.5-inch, 1920x480-pixel color LCD screen and viewfinder present very detailed images, with accurate color reproduction, allowing photographers to completely rely on them for exposure and focusing. They can even judge whether the white balance is correct based on it (as shown in the figure).

In terms of batteries, the Sony EX330 uses widely applied BP-type batteries (also known as V-interface batteries). Although the Sony PMW-EX330 looks almost identical to broadcast-grade equipment and is indeed a "big guy," users might think it consumes a lot of power and worry about carrying a bunch of batteries and power supplies when working outdoors. In this regard, [DVCLUB] learned from Sony technicians that due to Sony's experience in designing energy-efficient products, the PMW-EX330 features an environmentally friendly design that reduces power consumption by about 45% compared to traditional Sony HD camcorders, consuming only 18W. Plus, with a fully tapeless design, there are no traditional drum or tape compartments and mechanical structures. Therefore, the Sony PMW-EX330 is truly a low-consumption, energy-saving, and environmentally friendly HD camcorder. Through actual outdoor testing by the [DVCLUB] product room, it was found that a fully charged BP battery could provide uninterrupted operation for over 310 minutes.

In terms of audio recording, compared to the dual-channel recording of the previous generation products PMW-EX1/EX3, the Sony PMW-EX330 has four-channel 16-bit, 48-kHz linear uncompressed audio recording capability. The audio level of each channel can be controlled separately, providing higher-quality audio recording for producing complex types of programs.

HD picture presentation, multi-format recording. In terms of image quality performance, since the Sony PMW-EX330 uses three 1/2-inch full HD "Exmor CMOS sensors" with a maximum bitrate of 35Mbps, its 19801080 full HD pixels can capture excellent image quality. Combined with the outstanding F11 sensitivity, excellent 54db signal-to-noise ratio, and high-level resolution of over 1000 TV lines of the Sony PMW-EX330, this model in the Sony EX series has become one of the representative king models in the era of HD tapeless recording. In practical tests, whether shooting in low-light environments or complex lighting environments, the Sony PMW-EX330 can capture high-quality, excellent images. Additionally, due to the large imaging device used in the Sony PMW-EX330, photographers can capture ideal shallow depth-of-field image effects in complex environments. For filmmakers, this undoubtedly provides more free creative shooting space.

When Sony launched the PMW-EX330, the market positioning for this high-cost-performance model was mainly for industry users. However, during the current transition phase of mixed HD and SD recording, whether some industry users or part of the medium and small TV stations still use SD recording workflows, it is difficult to quickly transition to HD production workflows. To accommodate these old users and achieve a transition from current SD production to future HD production, the Sony PMW-EX330 not only has HD recording formats but also standard SD DVCAM recording and playback.

Tapeless storage, efficient work. The Sony PMW-EX330 uses the MPEG-2 MP@HL compression standard "MPEG-2 Long GOP" to record 1920x1080 full HD images. With this highly efficient codec and large-capacity SXS memory cards, up to 140 minutes of high-quality HD images can be recorded on a 32GB memory card. There are dual slots on the body, allowing seamless dual-card recording without any multi-frame or lost frame situations. Additionally, hot-swapping of memory cards can be done during shooting without interrupting the recording process. This brings great convenience to workers who need long-duration recording. Sony's 64GB SxS card will also be available in November, with the model number SBS-64G1A. Large-capacity memory cards bring great convenience to long-duration shooting.

The Sony PMW-E X 3 3 0 can use all the media currently available in the XDCAM EX family. Besides professional ExpressCard/34 standard SxS Pro and SxS-1 memory cards, high-speed memory sticks can be used through MEAD-MS01 or high-speed SD cards through MEAD-SD01. Besides built-in memory cards, external PHU-120R hard disk recording units can be used for long-duration shooting. The new PXU-MS240 portable storage unit can transfer data from SxS memory cards to the storage unit at extremely fast speeds without using a computer to quickly back up the materials on the SxS memory cards. The .MP4 file format for HD recording and the original packaging format for SD recording support mainstream non-linear editing software applications. Compared to traditional broadcast-grade equipment using tape recording at a 1:1 ratio, the efficiency of the workflow is unimaginable. Various new types of storage media and highly efficient post-production workflows are essential for today's HD tapeless video production processes, bringing substantial benefits to users.

Comprehensive functions, humanized design. The Sony PMW-EX330 is also equipped with a variety of interfaces, including HD/SDI output used in the broadcast-grade field, HDMI output interface popular in the consumer-grade field, IEEE1394, and audio/video output interfaces. It also supports down-conversion in SD mode through the IEEE1394 interface. Since the PMW-EX330, like the PMW-EX1/EX3, has an HD-SDI interface capable of outputting 1920x1080 resolution 4:2:2, 10-bit uncompressed signals, and can be used as an EFP studio camera through accessories such as CBK-CE01, XDCA-55, and XDCU-50, these studio accessories will also be launched by Sony in November.

Besides rich interfaces and more accessory options, the built-in functions of the Sony PMW-EX330 are also very powerful, such as strong variable frame rate shooting capabilities, true progressive recording ability of 23.98P suitable for filmmaking, interval recording, frame recording, super gain, six standard gamma curves, and four super gamma curves settings. Through these special function settings, photographers can easily shoot perfect cinematic works. For journalists, they often encounter sudden events. Using the "image buffer recording" function, up to 15 seconds of pre-recorded footage can be captured before pressing the record button, which is crucial for capturing sudden news events.

Another commendable feature is the five ASSIGN customizable switches on the body. Through the ASSIGN.0, ASSIGN.1, ASSIGN.1 ASSIGN.4, ASSIGN.5 buttons on the body, shortcuts such as ATW auto white balance, EZ mode, record preview, record, image buffer, zebra pattern display, histogram, depth of field, manual focus correction, etc., can be set, making it convenient for photographers to perform quick operations using these shortcuts.

In summary, through the brief testing by the [DVCLUB] evaluation room and communication with users, the Sony PMW-EX330 is a highly cost-effective HD tapeless camcorder that integrates the advantages of Sony's XDCAM EX series models, with stronger functionality. The [DVCLUB] product evaluation room believes that whether for industry users or commercial users, using the Sony PMW-EX330 can meet the current production needs.

Continuing the brilliance of the PMW-EX series

At the end of 2007, Sony launched its first handheld memory card camcorder PMW-EX1, praised for its high image quality and friendly price. In November 2009, Sony released the PMW-EX1R, which added SD DVCAM recording capability to the PMW-EX1. It remains the only handheld camcorder in the industry that uses a 1/2-inch imager, with each imager reaching an effective pixel resolution of 1920 x 1080, highly recommended by many TV station users, with a market share far ahead of similar products. The introduction of the semi-shoulder memory card camcorder PMW-EX3 and recorder PMW-EX30 further expanded the application range of XDCAM EX cameras.

The XDCAM EX series products, with their superior high image quality, have been favored in the high-end program production field, especially by large production companies and film companies. In film institutions such as China Film Group, Central New Image, Beijing Science and Technology Film, XDCAM EX series products have become the standard auxiliary shooting cameras for HDCAM or film cameras, widely used in the shooting of advertisements, MVs, HD documentaries and specials, movies, and TV dramas.

Director Zhang Yimou's films "Three Guns Three Cases" and the ongoing "Love Under the Hawthorn Tree" both adopted PMW-EX1/R as auxiliary cameras for the F35, and used PMW-EX30 to synchronously record with the F35. Since the PMW-EX1R handheld camera has DVCAM SD shooting capability, major television media institutions such as CCTV, Shenzhen TV Station, Hebei TV Station, Shenyang TV Station, and Xinhua News Agency have started using it for news shooting. The PMW-EX3, due to its interchangeable lens and multiple interfaces required for multi-camera operation, has become a favorite in the film and television production market, even the 3D production market and rental market, successfully used in the 3D shooting of the Shanghai World Expo. According to a survey by the UK's "Televisual" magazine on the British rental market, the popularity of PMW-EX3 ranked first.

With great success, industry customers also put forward new demands for the XDCAM EX series products. They hope to own a reasonably priced shoulder-mounted XDCAM EX camera, leading to the birth of the shoulder-mounted memory card camcorder PMW-EX330.

New media, high efficiency, multiple applications

PMW-EX330 comes in two models: the lens-equipped PMW-EX330K and the lens-less PMW-EX330L.

The main features of PMW-EX330 are as follows:

◆ 3-piece 1/2-inch Exmor CMOS sensor

◆ Each sensor has 1920 x 1080 effective pixels, horizontal resolution of 1000 TV lines

◆ F11 sensitivity (50i mode), minimum illumination requirement: 0.05lx

◆ PMW-EX330K is equipped with a standard Fujinon 16x5.8 HD lens

◆ Supports 1920x2080 full HD format, compatible with DVCAM SD format

◆ Equipped with HD/SD-SDI, HDMI digital HD interface

◆ 15-second pre-shooting function

◆ Four-channel audio

◆ Low power consumption: 18W, BP-GL95 can operate for 310 minutes

In terms of recording media, the PMW-EX330 can use all the media currently available in the XDCAM EX family. Professional SxS Pro and SxS-1 memory cards can be used, as well as high-speed memory sticks through MEAD-MS01 or high-speed SD cards through MEAD-SD01. Besides memory cards, the PHU-120R hard disk recording unit can be used for long-duration shooting. The PHU-120R hard disk recording unit has a recording capacity of 120 GB. The new PXU-MS240 portable storage unit can transfer data from SxS memory cards to the storage unit at extremely fast speeds without using a computer to quickly back up the materials on the SxS memory cards.

The PMW-EX330 can not only be used as an ENG single-unit application but also as a full-function studio camera by combining studio accessories. Sony will launch related studio accessories at the end of October 2010: XDCA-55 camera adapter, XDCU-55 camera expansion unit, CBK-CE01 50-pin interface.

The studio system built in this way has the following features:

◆ Digital signal transmission, no degradation of image quality during transmission

◆ Maximum cable length of 100m between XDCA-53 and XDCU-50

◆ XDCU-50 can be installed in a 1U high 19-inch standard system rack

◆ Can use various Sony RM/RCP studio remote control units

◆ XDCU-50 can down-convert camera output signals and is compatible with SD systems

◆ XDCU-50 can accept black field signals and three-level synchronization signals for locking

◆ XDCU-50 can accept composite or HD/SD-SDI return signals

◆ Has two Tally functions in red and green, which can be displayed on the camera screen

Sony will also simultaneously launch the XDCA-53 camera adapter, which can be paired with the XDCU-50 to form a full-function studio with the PMW-EX3. XDCA-53 can be converted from XDCA-55.
