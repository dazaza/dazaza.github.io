How to prevent antral gastritis? Experts from the Gastroenterology Department of Zhengzhou Xiehe Hospital introduced that antral gastritis is a chronic inflammation that occurs in the antrum of the stomach, which can generally be divided into two categories: superficial and atrophic. Through years of observation and research, it has been found that there is a close relationship between gastric cancer and atrophic antral gastritis.

So, how to prevent antral gastritis? Let's listen to the experts' explanation:

1. Hard, spicy, salty, hot, overly rough, and strongly stimulating foods should be avoided. Eating should be moderate and regular, with fixed times and quantities. It is recommended to eat small meals frequently, chew slowly, mix food thoroughly with saliva, and avoid binge eating. Food should mainly consist of nutritious, easily digestible soft foods. Eat more foods rich in plant protein and vitamins.

2. Avoid smoking and drinking alcohol. Smoking stimulates the gastric mucosa, increases gastric acid secretion, affects blood supply to the gastric mucosa, and hinders the repair and regeneration of gastric mucosal cells. Alcohol can directly destroy the gastric mucosal barrier.

3. Aspirin, acetaminophen, butazolidin, indole drugs, tetracycline, erythromycin, prednisone, and other medications should be avoided, especially during the active phase of chronic gastritis.

4. People with excessive gastric acid should avoid foods that stimulate gastric acid secretion, such as strong spices, alcohol, and sour agents.

5. People with insufficient gastric acid should not dilute gastric juice. Vinegar, lemon juice, and acidic seasonings can be added to the diet. Avoid eating hard-to-digest, gas-producing foods, and try to drink less before and after meals.

6. Mental stress is a promoting factor for chronic gastritis and should be avoided. Anxiety and irritability can easily cause gastric mucosal disorders and gastric functional disorders. Therefore, emotional stress responses should be avoided as much as possible, and tense emotions should be relieved.

7. Avoid irregular lifestyles and excessive fatigue. Pay attention to appropriate rest and exercise. Physical exercise promotes gastrointestinal peristalsis and emptying, enhances gastrointestinal secretory function, improves digestive power, and helps in the recovery of gastritis.

How does Zhengzhou Hospital treat best? Many gastritis patients are concerned about this question. Superficial gastritis is a chronic superficial inflammation of the gastric mucosa and is the most common type of chronic gastritis. Patients should seek timely treatment at regular hospitals. How does Zhengzhou's hospital treating superficial gastritis perform the best treatment? Next, we will specifically look at the detailed introduction by the experts.

The occurrence of superficial gastritis is mostly related to dietary factors, such as long-term consumption of strong liquor, strong tea, coffee, excessive chili condiments, and repeated stimulation of the gastric mucosa by overly salty, overly acidic, and overly coarse foods. More importantly, unreasonable dietary habits, irregular eating patterns, binge eating, or long-term lack of protein and B vitamins can lead to gastric mucosal degeneration.

Experts from Xiehe Hospital point out that dietary therapy is the best treatment for superficial gastritis. The general principle of dietary care includes non-irritating, low-fiber, easily digestible foods with sufficient nutrition; eating small meals frequently; relaxing during meals and maintaining a pleasant mood. The treatment of superficial gastritis should pay attention to the following points:

1. Avoid eating various stimulating foods such as strong liquor, concentrated coffee, raw garlic, mustard, etc., which damage the gastric mucosa, and also avoid eating overly hard, overly acidic, overly spicy, overly cold, overly hot, and excessively rough foods. Easily digestible foods can be selected, and attention should be paid to avoiding fried or grilled cooking methods. Foods should be light and soft.

2. Increase nutrition and pay attention to choosing high-protein foods and vitamin-rich soft foods, such as milk, tofu, carrots, and some fermented foods. Chew food slowly.

3. Follow a regular diet, eat at fixed times and quantities, avoid binge eating, develop good eating habits, and reduce the burden on the stomach. If the three main meals have smaller portions, snacks can be added between meals at fixed times. Pay attention to food combinations, ideally having both dry and liquid foods, protein-rich foods, and a small amount of staple food.
