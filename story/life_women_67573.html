Beijing's black bars that deceive male Internet users to consume with beauties

by niuerwa on 2008-05-08 14:58:46

Recently, I came across a lot of content online about Beijing's black bars using "drink-touting" to deceive netizens into spending money. With nothing better to do over the three-day Labor Day holiday, I decided on a whim to experience it firsthand, thus leading to the following story:

First, I applied for a new account and named myself "Bored." Then, I began searching for women in the Beijing area on various dating websites, adding them as friends. When asked for identity verification, I would send a message saying, "Are you interested in playing together on May 1st?" (Note: This was to avoid sincere women looking for genuine relationships ^_^). After trying with more than 10 people, two immediately passed the verification, their numbers being 34374XXXX and 87752XXXX.

34374 directly replied, "Come find me," knowing there was a chance, I started chatting with her right away. Not long after, I gave her my phone number, agreeing to meet the next day (May 1st) when she would call me.

87752 was initially vague, chatting for half a day before we only exchanged phone numbers and agreed to sing karaoke the next day.

On May 1st, since I wasn't sure if 87752 was one of the so-called drink touts, I made an excuse about waking up late and didn't show up, instead waiting for 34374's call.

However, after the agreed time, 34374 didn't call (probably too busy^_^), leaving me no choice but to chat again with 87752 who was still online. During our conversation, the woman claimed to be 25 years old and from Chengde. Since I am familiar with Chengde, I asked a few questions, and her answers were full of loopholes. Understanding her true nature, I pretended not to know and still invited her to sing the next day. She called herself Lin Qiao (considering it must be a fake name, therefore not abbreviated^_^), claiming to be the assistant to the general manager of a foreign company, living in Guozhan Inter International Apartment, asking me to pick her up at 2 PM the next day.

On May 2nd, I intentionally arrived an hour early at the Inter Apartment building and called the woman. The person who answered the phone (note: this woman had a coarse voice with a noticeable southern accent) seemed confused: "You're here already? Aren't you earlier than the agreed time?" Then she claimed she was busy now and would contact me in an hour. I became even more certain, thus offering to wait nearby for an hour.

An hour later, I finally met the woman at the T-junction west of the Inter Apartments (the opposite side of the road is Dazhong Electronics). Her voice was softer, without any accent, confirming that she was not the same person who answered the phone earlier. She had an average appearance and a very unfriendly attitude (lacking professionalism indeed^_^). I tentatively suggested taking a taxi to Money Box Karaoke (if she had agreed at this point, I would have found an excuse to leave^_^), but as expected, she claimed she didn't have the habit of singing during the day, preferring to walk around nearby, asking me to lead the way.

I then led the way in the direction she came from (south), but she stood still. I guessed I had walked in the wrong direction, so I turned back and asked why she wasn't moving. She said she wanted to walk along the Third Ring Road, so I followed her instructions and walked west along the Third Ring Road.

After walking about 2-300 meters, she silently entered a small, dimly lit bar by the roadside. Realizing the main act was about to begin, I followed her inside without a word.

The space inside the bar was not large, the decoration was simple, though it appeared to have two floors. Due to the darkness and lack of lights, it was difficult to see clearly. All the waiters were young men in their twenties, and I saw around 5-6 of them.

After sitting down, a waiter immediately brought a drinks menu. A quick glance revealed that the prices were generally much higher than normal, though they did not reach the ridiculous thousands mentioned by some netizens. I wondered if they used different menus as some netizens had suggested.

I feigned surprise and asked, "Why are your items so expensive?" The waiter claimed this was the common price for bars in Houhai.

Thus, I let the woman order first. Familiar with the process, she ordered a drink for 80 yuan, while I ordered a regular green tea for 30 yuan. She then asked if there was a fruit platter; the waiter said a medium-sized one for 80 yuan or a large one for 120 yuan, asking which one she preferred. She said, "Just bring whatever," the waiter replied, "Then I'll definitely bring the large one," also asking if I was okay with it. I smiled and did not refuse.

Soon, the items arrived, and the waiter immediately demanded payment (perhaps fearing that I might finish drinking and not have enough money?). I asked if I could pay by card, and the waiter said yes (they were well-equipped, most people probably wouldn't have enough cash and would have to pay by card... laugh), then took me to the bar counter at the back to swipe my card.

I deliberately pulled out a credit card that required a password. When entering the password, I noticed the amount displayed was 237 yuan, so I asked what the extra 7 yuan was for. The cashier man claimed it was a cross-bank swiping fee (first time hearing about it -_-!). So I said I'd pay in cash, and the cashier man said that was even better.

When I asked for my credit card back, the man insisted I pay first before returning the card. Seeing the situation about to get awkward, another man nearby said, "Give the card back first." Only then did the cashier return my card.

When I pulled out my wallet to take out the money, I claimed I couldn't see clearly and asked him to turn on the light. The man glanced at me but did not turn on the light, leaving me no choice but to pay and return to my seat.

Not long after sitting down, 2-3 pairs of men and women entered the bar one after another. They didn't look like couples, and mostly the women were leading the men inside, leading me to infer they were in similar situations.

At this moment, seeing that I remained silent and didn't touch the tea water (to be honest, I didn't dare to drink it, haha), just observing the surroundings attentively, the woman might have become suspicious and pretended to get up to leave. Knowing the show wasn't over yet, I insisted on finishing the tea before leaving.

To prevent her from becoming overly suspicious, I pretended to drink a sip of tea and started chatting with her. After a few sentences, I suddenly asked, "Do you often come to this place?" Her expression slightly changed, replying, "This is my first time, what's wrong?" Seeing my goal achieved, I stopped pressing further.

A short while later, the woman began calling the waiter to order alcohol. The waiter introduced a wine for 600 yuan per bottle (didn't hear clearly, either counterfeit or low-quality red wine). She said, "Alright, I'll take this," but I refused. She then falsely claimed she wouldn't make me pay, insisting on ordering anyway. I took the opportunity to argue with her while sending the waiter away.

Our voices weren't loud, others definitely couldn't hear us. I just stood up, but at that moment, several men in the bar immediately gathered around the aisle near my seat, asking a waiter standing nearby what happened. (One of the biggest flaws: they showed too much concern for this woman, in a normal operating place, seeing a couple arguing wouldn't elicit such a reaction unless they already knew the relationship between me and the woman.)

Realizing I shouldn't stay too long, I asked the woman, "Are you going or not? If not, I'm leaving." She said, "Then go ahead," giving me the chance to leave.

On the way home by taxi, I sent a text message to the number I previously contacted: "Little sister, find a proper job, being a tout has no future." But it was like a stone sinking into the sea, with no reply.

Final thoughts: As the internet becomes increasingly widespread, the methods of fraud and deception through the internet are also increasing. The method described above is far from something new anymore. I hope that when netizens encounter similar situations, they won't be blinded by certain desires and will think carefully. Because this kind of situation falls under voluntary consumption, even if you report it afterward, it won't be accepted, at most they will suggest you file a complaint with the Price Bureau.

While sitting in the black bar, I kept thinking, if there was a way to report it without the scammers noticing, and also record the evidence on-site, it would be great. Before writing this article, I searched online and found that there is indeed a company in Beijing researching a security mobile phone that can quickly and discreetly report emergencies with a single button press, transmitting the on-site sounds and other evidence in real-time to the police center. I hope this type of phone will be available soon, providing a response method when encountering such deception and forced consumption, ha.

Summarizing their common characteristics:

1. All claim to be company employees, but how many company employees don't sleep until past midnight?

2. All meeting places are relatively secluded, but there must be a small, unknown bar nearby.

3. All meetings start with the reason of making friends.

4. In the bar, no need to look at the menu, the names of the drinks are very familiar.

5. Their appearances are generally acceptable.

6. The bartenders seem polite, but upon closer inspection, either at the entrance or in other quiet seats, there are a few idle people who are likely enforcers.

7. There aren't many customers in the bar, sometimes there may only be you.

Therefore, dear netizens, please be careful in the future and don't fall into their traps.
