To deal with the college entrance examination, you need to adjust your mentality.

by aiourstudy on 2010-05-29 06:40:14

Confidence is the spiritual pillar of a test-taker's success. Without the confidence to succeed in the college entrance examination (CEE), it is unlikely that one will succeed in it. How can we strengthen our confidence?

(1) Appropriate Expectations for CEE Goals

Test-takers should determine their CEE goals based on their usual academic strength and mental state, being realistic about them. Generally speaking, they should set expectations for their CEE scores based on their results from mock exams. If the goal is set too high, it may increase test anxiety due to its difficulty to achieve; if set too low, it could affect the potential for performance.

(2) Avoid Comparisons

Each candidate’s learning strength and mental condition vary, so their expected CEE scores should also differ. Some candidates blindly compare themselves with classmates who are academically stronger, which can damage their confidence. For candidates, achieving their normal level during the CEE constitutes success.

(3) Strengthen Capability

Exam confidence is built on exam capability. Therefore, enhancing review and improving ability is an important measure to reinforce confidence. Instead of engaging in prolonged study sessions or late-night cramming, focus on improving review efficiency, establishing knowledge networks and systems, and learning to solve problems using existing knowledge. This helps to strengthen confidence in the CEE.

(4) Avoid Superstition

In early July 2000, a good student told me: "Professor Wang, I have no hope of getting into a key university." He said he had seen crows recently, which he believed were ominous signs, meaning he would likely not get into a key university. Whether one succeeds in an exam is determined by their usual academic strength and mental state during the exam, not by supernatural forces. Candidates should believe in their own power and avoid seeking divine help, worshiping Buddha, or fortune-telling.

(5) Positive Self-Suggestion

Positive self-suggestions can enhance confidence, while negative ones can reduce it. Before the exam, students often receive many negative suggestions, so it is crucial to eliminate their influence. Each candidate has different mental states under different circumstances, so they should use positive self-suggestions according to their situation to adjust their psychology and strengthen their confidence. They can place notes with positive self-suggestions on their desks, such as "I have the ability to learn, and I will definitely succeed!"

(6) Walk with Chest Out, Head Up, and Quick Steps

A person's inner experience aligns with their behavior. When feeling happy and confident, people walk with their chest out and head up, taking quick and powerful steps; when feeling disheartened and lacking confidence, they walk slowly and weakly. Candidates can use this principle by walking with their chest out and head up every day, taking slightly quicker steps. Doing this regularly can enhance their confidence.

Positive emotions can improve CEE results, while negative ones can lower them. Adjusting emotions is an essential part of CEE mental adjustment.

What are the good emotions for successful CEE?

- Calm and composed

- Full of energy

- Good control over emotions

Before and during the CEE, candidates will inevitably encounter difficulties and setbacks, but they must have good emotional control.

How can candidates optimize their emotions?

(1) Treat the CEE with a Normal Heart

Whether before or during the exam, treat the CEE with a normal heart, and your mood will naturally stabilize.

(2) Don't Overestimate Test Anxiety

Candidates may experience test anxiety before and during the exam, characterized by some tension, uneasiness, and urgency. This is inevitable for candidates. Moderate anxiety has a significant negative impact on performance, while mild test anxiety can, in a sense, promote the potential of candidates.

(3) Positive Self-Imagination

Positive self-imagination can evoke good emotions. During the review process and entering the examination room, candidates can use positive self-imagination to invigorate and reinforce good emotions, preventing the generation of negative emotions.

(4) Listen to Music

Listening to music, especially light music, effectively adjusts tense emotions. Many top scorers and successful candidates have told me that they relax their emotions by listening to their favorite light music when they feel stressed.

(5) Positive Self-Suggestion

During the CEE review process and in the examination room, candidates can give themselves positive self-suggestions based on their current mental state. Depending on their situation, they can use different self-suggestions. When overly nervous, they can silently repeat "Relax, relax, relax"; when feeling irritable, "Calm, calm, calm"; when feeling discouraged, "I can do it, I can do it, I can do it."

Deep breathing is a simple and effective method to adjust emotions. Regularly practice deep breathing techniques so that you can use them skillfully when needed.

Sit in a chair with your eyes closed, inhale slowly and rhythmically, hold for a few seconds, then exhale slowly and rhythmically.

(7) Massage the Neiguan Acupoint

Candidates can lightly massage the Neiguan acupoint (located three transverse fingers above the wrist crease on the median line toward the elbow) with their right thumb. The massage should be gentle and rhythmic in a clockwise direction, repeated 36 times.

(8) Relaxation Training Method

This is a relaxation method using verbal suggestion. Sit in a chair with your whole body relaxed, feet shoulder-width apart, and eyes slightly closed. Mentally repeat: "Head loose, face loose, neck loose, chest loose, back loose, abdomen loose, waist loose, front thigh loose, back thigh loose, front calf loose, back calf loose, foot loose, sole loose." Repeat this sequence repeatedly to relax both the body and mind.

(9) Imagery Troubleshooting Method

Candidates can stand or take a shower in the evening for training. Begin by imagining a drizzle falling from the sky, flowing down the face to the chest and abdomen, simultaneously flowing down the back of the head to the back and waist. The water continues to flow down the thighs, calves, and onto the foot and sole. While imagining the water flowing down the body, imagine that it washes away all troubles, worries, tensions, and anxieties. Repeated training following this order can help eliminate bad moods and relieve troubles and anxieties.

Candidates can choose one or several methods according to their situation to regulate their emotions frequently. Using these methods regularly will certainly benefit having a good mindset before and during the exam. With regular practice, applying these methods in the examination room will become smooth.

As everyone knows, whether athletes enter the competitive state on the field is closely related to their competition results. Exams are the same. What does it mean to enter the state?

- Calm and composed

- Full of energy

- Focused attention

- Vigorous spirit

- Confidence to perform normally

- Normal intellectual activity

I particularly emphasize that entering the state refers not only to the CEE in July but also includes entering the state during regular studies, exams, and self-study. How can one enter the state?

(1) Self-Decompression

The attitude towards exams greatly affects entering the state.

Approaching exams positively helps enter the state. Viewing exams as a challenge can stimulate oneself to enter the state.

Seeing exams as an opportunity leads to cherishing the moment.

Viewing exams as a great threat causes tension and uneasiness.

Seeing exams as unfortunate events leads to discouragement.

Some CEE champions have told me that July is a memorable month because the hard work of daily life will bear fruitful results in the CEE examination room in July.

(2) Correctly Handle External Pressure

Excessive attention from families and society can create psychological pressure on candidates. Properly handling external pressure helps lighten the burden.

Exams are personal matters; accept correct opinions and ideas from others. Despite good intentions, unnecessary opinions shouldn’t become a psychological burden.

(3) Practice Through Testing

Athletes undergo training before competitions to help them enter the state. Similarly, candidates need to train in small tests and simulated exams to enter the exam state.

In the month before the exam, some schools conduct daily tests for students, treating them as formal exams. Improving the ability to enter the state through small tests and simulated exams significantly enhances skills.

Candidates can train their ability to enter the state through school tests or simulate exams during self-study.

(4) Adjust Biological Clock

Each student has peak energy periods within 24 hours. The CEE takes place from 9 am to 11:30 am and 3 pm to 5 pm, requiring candidates to adjust their peak energy times accordingly. It is generally better to start adjusting the biological clock one month before the exam.

(5) Regular Practice Before the Exam

Some candidates think that since they’ve done plenty of tests in the second half of senior three, they don’t need to continue practicing ten days before the exam. However, without doing tests, they may feel unprepared in the CEE examination room, unable to find their rhythm and enter the state.

Xing Ziqiang, the national CEE champion in Anhui Province in 2000, said: “A few days before the CEE, I reviewed my notes and did some medium-difficulty questions, which gave me confidence.” Avoid difficult and confusing questions, as they can affect one's mood.

I suggest candidates keep doing some tests before the exam, though not for long periods to avoid fatigue and stress. Doing some tests keeps one sharp.

(6) Don't Go to Bed Too Early on the Night of July 6th

Many candidates think they should sleep early on the night of July 6th since the exam starts on July 7th. However, sleeping early disrupts their usual schedule, leading to tossing and turning and anxiety.

Parents might urge their children to go to bed early, turning off the lights, but if the child isn’t asleep, neither are the parents. Some parents get anxious if the child hasn’t fallen asleep by midnight and even give them sleeping pills, resulting in difficulty waking them up in the morning. A groggy brain doesn’t help entering the exam state.

I recommend that students who haven’t taken sleeping pills before avoid them on the night of July 6th. Don’t go to bed too early, as it often leads to insomnia and anxiety.

If a candidate doesn’t sleep well on the night of July 6th, they should treat it with a normal heart, let it be, and not dwell on it.

Some candidates get busier as the exam approaches and rarely think about how to enter the best state for excellent results. This lacks strategic vision. I suggest candidates spend more time thinking about how to better enter the state and improve efficiency and quality during the exam.

After years of preparation, how can one fully perform in the CEE?

(1) Fully Understand the CEE Situation

Many candidates focus solely on reviewing without understanding CEE content reforms, question-setting reforms, or the 3+X exam model. They believe mastering the lessons will suffice.

I suggest each candidate repeatedly read and understand the "Exam Instructions" to grasp the CEE direction and prepare adequately.

(2) Strategically Disdainful, Tactically Respectful

Strategically disdainful means believing in oneself after extensive preparation and previous mock exams.

Tactically respectful means carefully answering every question, earning points step by step, and completing each question cautiously and responsibly.

(3) Response Methods

Candidates should summarize their experiences in responding to subjective and objective questions across subjects to answer effectively during the exam.

(4) Use Exam Strategies and Techniques

Using strategies and techniques significantly improves performance. Candidates should systematically summarize their strategies and techniques before the CEE, including those specific to each subject.

(5) Timely Summarize Exam Experience

After starting the CEE, candidates should summarize their experience from each subject to improve subsequent performances.

(6) Timely Adjust Mindset

Candidates’ mindsets change with the difficulty of the questions and their answers. Key is timely adjustment to maintain full energy, focused attention, and confidence. When encountering difficulties, candidates should remind themselves promptly and use simple psychological adjustment methods like self-suggestions to eliminate unfavorable mindsets.

(7) Fully Utilize Observational Skills

Observational skills require sensitivity, accuracy, and comprehensiveness.

Sensitivity involves capturing subtle information.

Accuracy involves correctly obtaining question information. Misreading or missing questions result from inaccurate observation.

Comprehensiveness requires analyzing questions from various aspects to make correct judgments.

(8) Fully Utilize Memory

Memory retrieval plays a significant role in exam success. In exams, memory retrieval manifests as quickly and accurately searching for required knowledge in one’s mind. To fully utilize memory retrieval, categorize and connect various pieces of knowledge during regular study, organizing learned knowledge systematically in the brain.

Recalling under confident conditions yields good results, whereas recalling under stressful, anxious, or uncertain conditions produces poor results. Negative emotions and lack of confidence significantly affect memory recall.

(9) Fully Utilize Thinking Ability

With CEE reform, thinking ability, especially analytical and comprehensive abilities, becomes increasingly prominent.

① Utilize Analytical Thinking Ability

Analytical thinking involves recognizing various aspects and parts of the exam paper. The more fully analytical thinking is utilized, the more precise the analysis of the questions' requirements, enabling accurate judgment.

② Utilize Comprehensive Thinking Ability

CEE assesses comprehensive ability, including intra-disciplinary knowledge integration and inter-disciplinary integration.

Liberal arts integration evaluates the ability to use historical, geographical, and political knowledge to solve problems.

Science integration evaluates the ability to use physical, chemical, and biological knowledge to solve problems.

Grand integration evaluates the ability to use physical, chemical, biological, historical, geographical, and political knowledge to solve practical problems.

③ Utilize Comparative Thinking Ability

Determining multiple-choice questions relies on both analytical and comparative thinking abilities.

④ Utilize Abstract Thinking Ability

Fully utilizing abstract thinking allows for correct judgment of the essence of the questions.

⑤ Utilize Generalizing Thinking Ability

Fully utilizing generalizing thinking enhances the quality of exam answers.

In the CEE, it's important to utilize divergent thinking, breaking free from habitual thinking constraints. Divergent thinking requires considering problems from various angles and perspectives. Fully utilizing divergent thinking plays a crucial role in solving difficult CEE questions. In a certain sense, solving difficult questions in each subject is mainly constrained by the level of divergent thinking.
