Is it really awesome to work at Google? Check out what employees have to say.

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 19:50:34

For many IT professionals, Google is their career heaven. Not only that, but Google often ranks in the top three when online media and magazines review the world's best employers. However, even at such a company, employees have exposed some of the terrible things they encountered while working at Google in Quora posts. Here we excerpt some parts from Quora to see what those terrible things are.

Terrible Thing One: Everyone is too excellent, so the most outstanding talents end up doing very ordinary jobs

Perhaps for many people, the worst thing is that they shouldn't work at Google because their qualifications far exceed the requirements of their roles. Why does this happen? This is mainly due to Google's large reputation, high salary, numerous benefits, and an extremely positive work culture, which makes many people eager to join. As a result, Google can even select the most capable individuals from a pool of excellent talents to take on lower-level positions.

The work content of these graduates from top universities across the U.S. might just involve providing support for Google's advertising products, manually removing illegal content from YouTube, or writing the most basic AB test code for colored buttons on websites.

Terrible Thing Two: This is a big company, but there’s no startup vibe

A former Google employee said that he left this company, considered by others to be the best employer, when it lost its startup atmosphere. There are office politics here too; you get promoted easily if you're on the right project and your work is appropriately noticed.

Terrible Thing Three: Many engineers are arrogant

A Google employee mentioned that although everyone shares common beliefs, unfortunately, the average level of Google engineers is mediocre. They are too arrogant, making each one feel superior to those around them, thus making it really hard to discuss any issue unless the other person is your friend. Yes, objective discussions in respective fields are quite rare, and they aren't interested in others' opinions unless the person is a "god."

Terrible Thing Four: Google is too big, so you have no influence within it

Someone answered that after working at Google for three years, he found it difficult to decide whether to leave Google. But later, one main reason helped him make the decision to leave – his individual business impact was minimal. As many Google employees have responded, Google benefits from its advertising business, giving it an incredible money-printing machine. Therefore, unless you're a talented engineer who can create something new, you're just the lubricant on a gear in the machine.

Terrible Thing Five: Mediocre middle management

Google's mid-level management is entirely focused on continuous daily mediocrity thinking, excluding all other factors. They don't want to cause trouble and don't know how to motivate their employees. They overly rely on the names and reputations of certain people at Google to do what should be their own tasks.

Terrible Thing Six: Google may understand engineering, but not design

Google doesn't focus enough on product and visual design, leading to many products being discontinued or semi-successful, like Wave, GoogleVideo, Buzz, and Orkut, etc. The reason could be that they overly concentrate on pure engineering.

Terrible Thing Seven: Your office might be too small

If you work in one of the four main campus buildings, you are likely to be accompanied by overcrowding. It's not uncommon to see three or four employees in a single room, or several managers sharing an office. With open areas for food, games, TV, and technical lectures, it becomes difficult to find a quiet, private place for reflection.

Terrible Thing Eight: Some things must be expressed in written form

When negotiating with Google during the onboarding process, as you strive to negotiate and raise your demands, ensure that there is a written contract, because Google has many vague promises that ultimately aren't fulfilled for employees.

Terrible Thing Nine: Immature Google culture

It's like imagining a remote area where people never grow up because they constantly drink, socialize endlessly, play games, and do things that don’t help much with work.

Terrible Thing Ten: No remote work allowed

What frustrates some employees is that remote work isn't possible at Google, whereas this has been a possibility for some employees over the past five to six years.

Terrible Thing Eleven: Google employees disrespect temporary workers

As someone who has worked as a temp at Google, the worst part about working at Google is the smug attitude of the Googlers. They seem to think that if you're not a regular Google employee, then you're somehow inferior in spirit and morality.
