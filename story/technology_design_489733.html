After powering on and during the operation of the refrigerator, the temperature in the refrigerator's chilling compartment was measured to be approximately 15℃. From this, it can be concluded that the reason for the continuous operation of the refrigerator compressor is due to the inability of the internal temperature to reach its set value.

Causes of the fault: too thick a layer of frost on the evaporator, too much food inside the refrigerator, frequent opening of the door, excessively high ambient temperature, loss of control of the heater in refrigerators with heaters, the light inside the fridge staying on (long-burning light), poor sealing of the door, partial blockage of the drier filter or capillary tube, minor leaks in the refrigeration system, or decreased efficiency of the compressor.

Based on the fault analysis, the possible causes might be partial blockage of the capillary tube, minor leaks in the refrigeration system, or reduced efficiency of the compressor. The process pipe of the compressor was cut off, and the refrigerant was released. Then, at the process pipe location, a repair valve and nitrogen cylinder were connected to check for leaks in the refrigeration system. After confirming through leak detection that the seal of the refrigeration system was intact, F12 was added into the refrigeration system until the pressure reached about 0.2MPa, and then the compressor was started. The vacuum pressure gauge on the repair valve showed a pressure of 0.05MPa, which was not under vacuum conditions, indicating that the capillary tube was not blocked. Therefore, it could basically be judged that the inability to lower the box temperature was due to the reduced efficiency of the compressor.

The casing of the compressor was opened, the top shell was removed, and a thick piece of paper was used to shield the back of the compressor. When the compressor was started, it was found that a large amount of freezing oil sprayed out from the gap between the piston and the cylinder, indicating that the clearance between the piston and the cylinder was too large; upon disassembly and measurement, the gap between the piston and the cylinder exceeded the allowable value. Due to the excessive clearance between the compressor's piston and the cylinder, the working efficiency of the compressor was reduced, leading to a decline in the cooling effect of the refrigerator, causing the continuous operation of the compressor, thus resulting in the aforementioned fault. After selecting a new piston and reassembling it, the refrigerator returned to normal, and the fault was eliminated.
