Once the sun rises

by fgffeifeiw on 2011-09-18 20:41:45

Those stories with ulterior motives have become the dust that disperses in this fleeting world, losing vitality. By now, sorrow has tasted disappointment.

When you, fireworks sublimate and red makeup covers the ground, it is I, who pours out love and suffers from widespread injuries. I have used up a lifetime of tenderness and in return received heart-wrenching pain.

The brilliance of youth fades as time leaves its mark; half your appearance changes into another form. You are the shore I cannot pass in this life. For you, I am willing to wander between human worlds, just to find the smile in my heart that will never fade.

I forgot when it started, but after darkness covered the light, I lay quietly in memories. The surrounding mist created an ambiguous sadness. Thinking of fake smiles, my heart returned to struggle once again.

A brief meeting, a journey of acquaintance, life ultimately isn't about first impressions. Love came and went, leaving behind only the excruciating scars exposed to wind and rain.

The sunrise deceived the mortal world; everything was familiar. Sometimes low, sometimes melancholic, the corners of my eyes remained crystalline with your warmth. At sunset, the pretense of strength was removed. Crouching by the door of memories, calmness slowly turned into gut-wrenching sorrow.

Time flows on, never to return. Sunrise and sunset are no longer the old sun. Past stories explained the meaning of fleeting clouds and smoke. Meeting strangers again felt like unfamiliarity from another era.

At the moment your wedding dress fell off, did you recall the former me? Did you miss the warmth of my palm?

Through the years, the smiling faces beside me, the hands that organized my messy papers, and the people who comforted my weary body have all disappeared in this lifetime.

Sunrise brings affection, sunset brings injury. I only wish to guard the sorrow in my heart, waiting for the next cycle to meet again.

Love, so light, it leaves only pain in the heart; love, so heavy, it fills the heart with you. Light and heavy, in an instant, all became dreamlike illusions. Ultimately, the mulberry fields cannot moisten the mulberry fields.

In the past, at sunrise, your smiling face surpassed the sun, illuminating my heart for the day. In the past, at sunset, your affectionate eyes dispersed the cold, warming my dreams for the night.

I forgot when it started, but with the first ray of dawn, my hand gently caressed my cheek, piling up joy. With endless singing, I disguised countless heartaches, letting everyone know how carefree I was.

Always dreaming of a bright future, holding you in my arms, but you couldn't wait for my prosperity. You left so hastily, cutting off all my hopes, leaving me to guard sorrow and desolation.

I have never stopped missing you, but you hold hands with others in my thoughts. Missing you until the end leads to emptiness; ultimately, it's not me accompanying you forever.

Losing you, sunrise and sunset mean nothing. My empty soul has nowhere to return. The world is left with monochrome colors, and my heart resonates with a single sentiment.

Under the sunlight, the robust figure turns back and forth, glancing around, yet still unsure of where they stand. The melody echoing in the horizon ignites fleeting illusions in the mortal world. Lost in confusion, step after step.

Thinking upon thinking, the moment we met, I gave everything. Regret upon regret, the moment we parted, there was no looking back. If, heaven allows me to start over, I would certainly let go of your hand and not use pretended indifference to harm deep love.

By/ Yi Chen

You left, so thoroughly, using the fresh red all over the ground to extinguish my lifelong hope. Sunrise and evening, I lost direction.

Gentle feelings overflow from my eyes, falling into the bustling mortal world. It touched the birds to sing for me, touched the flowers to bloom for me, touched the heavens to shine for me. Ancient vows descended into the world, and the mighty river of time paused its eastward flow. I embraced countless splendors but could not hold onto your body carried away by the wind.

With one writing brush of the mortal world, picking up sorrow fills the garden. Writing passionately about you, who knew the worldly bond was shallow. Now you belong to someone else, ink becomes obsession and falls full of sorrow.

When you stroll by the roadside, do you remember the former me tightly leaning on your left side? When you whisper under the moonlight, do you think of the former me, willing to be your star for a lifetime? When you dance in dreams, does the former me appear, mesmerized by your side?

Under the night curtain, pouring out countless thoughts in my heart, touching emotions through the pen, those lost corners awaken mournful chants in the dark, lighting up bridges of remembrance. Vaguely, scene after scene is painted.
