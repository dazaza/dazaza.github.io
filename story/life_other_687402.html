The School Beauty

On Saturday morning, Liping drove his car downstairs and called me to get going. The weather seemed to understand our feelings, giving us special care. After days of continuous drizzle, the rain finally stopped, and the long-awaited sun came out, warming us with its rays. This time we decided to go to Diding Town, where a classmate named Guangming lived. At the reunion, he mentioned that there were several classmates there whom we hadn't seen since graduation.

In this cold winter season, there was no scenery along the way worth viewing. Liping said, "It's been nearly thirty years, so this meeting will surely be very joyful."

We didn’t tell Guangming, wanting to surprise the classmates. After lunch, we arrived at Diding Town, had a quick bite, and made the necessary arrangements according to the original plan before calling Guangming. He immediately rushed to the inn, and we informed him of the purpose of our trip. He then made phone calls to notify everyone. Within an hour, six classmates from Diding had gathered in our room, where we sat down to drink tea and chat.

There were two male and four female classmates. Except for Guangming, the other five were unfamiliar to both Liping and me. We met for the first time after being classmates for several years and not seeing each other for twenty-eight years since graduation. Naturally, we were extremely close. We talked about our work and life situations after parting, as well as our spouses, children, and family circumstances. Later, the conversation turned to other classmates. A female classmate named Rongrong said that Wang Qiufeng got married in Chensheng Township, not far from here. We were all very surprised. Wang Qiufeng was the Miao ethnic girl from Wuwangzhai who we often talked about during our reunions, but none of us knew where she was now. She was universally recognized as the most beautiful girl in the entire school, or what we would call today, the "school beauty." It was unexpected that she was here. I hurriedly asked who could contact her and urged them to notify her quickly.

Rongrong said, "It's more than twenty miles from here to her village, mostly mountain roads, and there is no telephone, so it's hard to notify her. I can only go downtown and see if there are any acquaintances from her village who are going back, and ask them to deliver a message for her to come quickly."

When Rongrong returned from downtown, she said she had found someone to deliver the message, but we didn't know if she would come. The topic shifted to Wang Qiufeng. It seemed that Rongrong knew the most about her. She told us: After finishing junior high school, Qiufeng did not attend senior high school but returned to her village. Later, she became a militia member and attended training in the county, where she met Yang Mingqi, also a Miao person from Chensheng. They corresponded and fell in love, eventually getting married. Qiufeng was an only daughter, and her parents wanted her husband to live with them, so Yang Mingqi moved to Wangwuzhai. Three years after their marriage, when their son was just over one year old, a natural disaster struck the Yang family—a landslide took the lives of Yang Mingqi’s mother, older brother, and a sister. His father lost both legs. As the second son, the survival of the whole family depended on him. Therefore, Qiufeng resolutely decided to move back with her husband and child to Chensheng. Her father-in-law was bedridden, her sister-in-law had returned to her maternal home with her child, and there was still a twelve-year-old younger sister at home. One could imagine the situation of this family. Qiufeng was a very capable woman, taking care of everything both inside and outside the house. Her husband was honest and simple, and everything was decided by Qiufeng. Ten years later, the younger sister got married, and Qiufeng provided her with all the dowry and held a wedding banquet, which was considered decent in our area. She even invited me. Qiufeng gave birth to a daughter who was almost finished with elementary school. Her son was already eighteen and seemed to have signed up for military service this year, having passed the physical examination, though it was uncertain whether he had made it through the final round. Over the years, Qiufeng endured much hardship, frequently buying things on credit from my dry goods store. My financial condition was not good either, so I couldn’t help her much. However, I covered the cost of salt for her family without charging her, and sometimes I gave her some old clothes for her children to wear. One day in late December, she approached me asking to borrow 30 yuan, saying she wanted to buy some food for the children. I gave her 50 yuan and told her she didn't need to pay it back.

Rongrong's words dampened the joyous atmosphere of reuniting with old classmates. Both Liping and I were eager to meet Qiufeng and waited for her to arrive before starting dinner. By 7:30, she still hadn't shown up, so we assumed she wouldn't come and started eating. Just as Liping proposed a toast, a waitress came in and said someone outside was looking for Rongrong. It must be Qiufeng, so I called Liping, and we went out together to greet her.

At the restaurant entrance, there was only one peasant woman, who looked over fifty. She wore a checkered woolen jacket and a pair of rubber boots, splattered with mud halfway up her legs. Her short hair, reaching her ears, was half gray and somewhat disheveled. Her face was dark yellow with many spots, making her appear very old and emaciated. We didn't acknowledge her. When Rongrong came out and called her name, "Qiufeng," we couldn't believe this was the former "school beauty." But she quickly recognized us, saying that if we had met on the road, we might not have recognized each other, but once reminded that we were junior high school classmates, she remembered.

Even though it was a gathering of old classmates, Qiufeng was very reserved at the dinner table, barely touching her chopsticks. I arranged for her to sit between Liping and me. To liven up the atmosphere, I joked, "Today is the happiest day for Liping and me. We've seen our 'school beauty' again and relived dreams long lost."

She laughed and said, "Back then, we were so young, just over fourteen. It's been so many years since we parted. Now we're all over forty. Time flies!"

Liping and I kept serving her dishes. She didn't drink alcohol, so we poured her a glass of red wine. Each time we clinked glasses, she only touched hers lightly. In the end, the wine in her glass was divided between Liping and me.

After dinner, it was already ten o'clock in the evening. Qiufeng insisted on going home, saying she had many things to do. Her son's military physical examination had passed, and the results would be announced soon, so she needed to make preparations. Moreover, when they left, her family wasn't aware. When the messenger delivered the message, she was working in the field, and no one was at home because everyone had gone to work in the mountains. She feared they would worry and wanted to find them.

Liping persuaded her to stay and talk for another half hour. I understood his intention and called Rongrong outside, giving her 500 yuan to buy two pieces of clothing for Qiufeng according to her size and some snacks for her daughter, but not telling her beforehand. Rongrong then called three female classmates to go out.

These female classmates were very efficient. They bought two sweaters, one dark green and one bright red, and a pair of pants. They also bought cookies and milk candy for the children. When Qiufeng realized these items were for her, she quickly stood up, repeatedly saying she didn't want them, that they were too expensive, and that she wasn't used to wearing such fancy clothes.

Seeing her flushed face and unsure hands, Liping said, "Qiufeng, please accept them. These are small tokens of appreciation from me and Zhihong. Don't feel bad. They aren't very expensive, just costing a hundred or so yuan each. You're only in your forties; you should dress up and look pretty."

"I'm a farmer; I don't need to dress so well," she replied.

I shot her a glance, "So what if you're a farmer? Farmers shouldn't enjoy life and look beautiful?"

At that moment, she remained silent. When we saw her off, the meticulous Liping also bought her a flashlight. Watching the light disappear into the misty mountain road, we returned to our room and sipped tea, sighing.

The next day was the market day in Diding. I woke up at dawn, rarely coming to the countryside, and wanted to wander around to see the winter scenery of the rural area. It was another sunny day, with fog forming. A thin layer of mist flowed along the mountain and the river and gradually spread onto the highway. Standing on the second-floor balcony, I saw people at the inn entrance, a man and a woman. The man was standing, and the woman was sitting on a carrying basket.

"What diligent farmers, rushing to the market so early," I thought to myself and walked downstairs. The woman immediately stood up, smiling and saying, "Zhihong, why are you up so early?" I looked closely and realized it was Qiufeng.

"Qiufeng, didn't you go home last night? Why are you here so early?"

"After I went home last night, I thought about it. Since you specifically came to see me, I wanted to invite you to my house, but the road is difficult, and your foot has problems, so you couldn't come. Also, we don't know when we'll meet again. I discussed it with Mingqi. There's nothing good in the mountains, but these walnuts are from our own trees. I asked my son to bring them to you. Afraid you might leave and we wouldn't see each other, we came early and waited here." She pointed to the man and introduced him as Yang Mingqi, a straightforward person who didn't say much. Indeed, her husband was very honest, standing quietly beside her and smiling kindly. I nodded slightly and greeted him with a "hello," and he smiled back.

I asked how long she had waited and where her son was. She said they had waited for more than two hours, and her son, fearing the cold, had gone to a friend's house. At this point, Liping also came downstairs. He had already heard our conversation and asked about her daughter. She said she originally intended to bring her daughter along for us to see, but she was too young and couldn't walk at night. Liping said:

"You should send your daughter to school in town. Our generation may be set, but we cannot delay the next generation."

"Yes, I've already discussed it with her father. Next year, when she starts junior high school, she will study in town, and her brother strongly requests it too," she smiled.

Liping told her, "If you encounter any economic difficulties, please let us know. We will help you. Since we are old classmates, there's no need to be polite."

She shook her head quickly: "No, no, we won't have difficulties. Grandpa's illness has lasted for nearly twenty years, and it won't require much more money. Her father will start working at Sun Erwa's brick factory after the New Year, earning at least two or three hundred yuan a month. After autumn, our family will have chestnuts and walnuts to sell, and I can raise pigs and poultry, which will also bring income."

Seeing her stubbornness, I got a little angry and said firmly, "Qiufeng, you must promise us that if you encounter any difficulties, you will definitely tell us. I've already given you my phone number, so just call us."

Finally, she nodded slightly, lowering her head and looking at the ground. I clearly saw tears welling up in her eyes. She said, "I know, you sincerely care about me. Your economic conditions are better, but earning money isn't easy either. Zhihong is an official, and officials' salaries are very low now. Liping does business, but that also requires a lot of effort. Besides, you also have families to support! Actually, I think about you too. Especially in recent years, every night when I go to sleep, I count on my fingers, remembering the names and appearances of all fifty-seven classmates from our junior high school class."

We had nothing more to say. Looking at the former "school beauty" in front of us and her husband standing beside her, smiling honestly, I suddenly felt my eyes brighten. Qiufeng still retained the charm of the "school beauty" from those days, despite wearing rustic clothes, despite her gray hair and aged appearance, looking ten years older than her actual age. But in my eyes, she was still beautiful, a beauty blended with kindness and simplicity, blended with dignity and magnificence... She was our forever radiant and dazzling "school beauty."

I was about to invite them to have breakfast together when I turned around and noticed Liping's red eyes. He opened the trunk of the car, walked over, picked up the carrying basket, and dumped the walnuts inside.
