Strategic Trade-offs in the Translation of Long Sentences in Scientific and Technical English

Abstract: This paper discusses the translation of long sentences characteristic of British legal and technical documents. Through an analysis of complex long sentence translation strategies, it illustrates the approach to translating long sentences in scientific and technical English, analyzing the syntactic structure and practical meaning of these sentences.

Keywords: Scientific and Technical English; Syntactic Function; Long Sentence Translation

In contrast to Chinese, which emphasizes re-negotiation for coherence, English values formality, often resulting in longer sentences. This is because English sentences frequently contain more parallel structures and various clauses modifying phrases or sentence components, leading to a tree-like branching structure. To express multiple closely related concepts, scientific and technical English particularly emphasizes strict reasoning and precise descriptive styles. Therefore, judgments are often accompanied by modifiers, restrictive elements, and appositive components, inevitably leading to longer sentences. In the translation of long English sentences in technical contexts, the first step involves syntactic analysis to determine the grammatical relationships between parts, following chronological and logical orders. Based on accurate analysis and understanding of the original text, one can employ different methods, using appropriate language expression techniques to ensure that the translation is both meaningful and fluent.
