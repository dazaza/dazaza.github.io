Will not include housing prices directly into the CPI calculation - synthetic fiber

by rescal4614 on 2010-03-31 09:45:47

Relevant persons in charge of the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) said in an interview with a reporter from our newspaper on the 30th that, in the CPI statistics for 2011, the weight of housing prices will increase while that of food prices will decrease. However, this does not mean that house prices will be directly included in the calculation of the CPI. Wei Guixiang, Director of the Department of Urban Affairs of the NBS, told reporters that the CPI is generally adjusted once every five years. Currently, the NBS is working on these adjustments and it is expected that the weight of housing prices in 2011 will increase. "With the improvement of people's living standards, the weight of food among consumer goods is constantly decreasing, while the weight of housing, education, and medical care is constantly increasing," said Wei Guixiang. Previously, there were frequent calls within the industry to change the weight of the CPI. Many experts have proposed including house prices in the calculation of the CPI. The proposal submitted by the Central Committee of the Zhigong Party to the two sessions pointed out that the proportion of housing consumption in the current CPI index system in China is too low, while the proportion of food consumption is too high (it accounted for as high as 32.8% in 2009). The current proportion of housing consumption in the CPI index system in China is about 13%, while in the United States it is 42.1% (in 2006). Relevant personnel from the Statistical Bureau analyzed that the increase in China's CPI in March might slow down. The rapid increase in the CPI in February was mainly due to the Spring Festival factor, and the weather factors had a significant impact in February. Heavy rainstorms caused difficulties in transporting vegetables and fruits, and it is expected that the weather impact will weaken in March and thereafter, and vegetable prices will remain stable in May and June. Relevant personnel from the Statistical Bureau believed that the drought disaster in Southwest China would not have a significant impact on China's CPI, "On the one hand, Southwest China is not a major grain-producing region in China, and on the other hand, the government has already taken a series of measures to stabilize the situation." Despite this, Wei Guixiang pointed out that house prices could not be directly included in the CPI accounting system because houses are still considered investment products. Within their property rights period, they can be passed down to descendants. Moreover, if we take an ordinary consumer as an example, spending 1 million yuan in a month to buy a house involves a huge value compared to other item consumptions.
