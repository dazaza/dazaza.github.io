Actually, the title I want to express is "Fighting a group of brain-dead people dancing in the Internet bar", but when it comes to J5, this popular intellectual game among the young people of our nation, I can't help but associate it with children who are mentally and physically disabled. So, I specially found an online Martian text converter to turn it into symbols they are accustomed to accept, so as to serve as a warning for the children to learn not to be deceived in the Internet bar anymore...

One day, I was playing CS in an Internet bar called Huangting or something like that. It's quite big, and since it wasn't peak hours, there weren't many people, so it was relatively quiet.

I was bored, entered several public servers, but couldn't find suitable opponents. Later, my buddy on QQ called me to join a competitive server. After playing a few rounds, I found that there was an expert on the other side who kept defeating me. Many times, I almost beat him down to just a few HP points, but he always managed to kill me at the end. I was extremely frustrated.

At this moment, a young person came over from the other side, I didn't know if it was a boy or a girl. Anyway, his hair was dyed orange like a rocket pineapple head, with earrings, rings, and necklaces, all shining brightly. Soon after, he started dancing hard, and while he danced, he also banged on the keyboard loudly, which made me really angry, but I ignored him. Who would have thought that he became even more aggressive, and the whole Internet bar was filled with the sound of his keyboard.

I got a little mad, so I slapped my keyboard hard to vent my dissatisfaction. He tilted his head and looked at me, and only then did I realize he was a guy, dressed like a woman. Sensing my annoyance and challenge, he increased the intensity of his keyboard pounding. Oh no, it seemed like he was deliberately going against me. I lifted my keyboard and hit the table hard, and after I finished, I noticed his eyes widened, probably because he didn't expect me to be so fierce. At this point, many people in the Internet bar were paying attention to us. Unexpectedly, he also lifted his keyboard and slammed it on the table hard, and after that, he even tapped his monitor a few times. If he hadn't realized it was an LCD screen, he might have continued hitting it.

Seeing that he was beyond saving, I threw my keyboard onto the ground, breaking it into two large pieces, scattering key caps all over the floor. My action actually earned applause from other netizens in the Internet bar, probably because they had been annoyed by such brain-dead dancers for a long time.

At this moment, the network administrator and security guards came over. That guy pulled off his keyboard and threw it on the ground, stepping on it hard while looking smugly at me.

I ignored him, packed up my bag and prepared to leave, but he was surrounded by the security guards and got beaten up well. I felt so relieved, and when I walked out of the door, I heard him saying, "That person also threw his keyboard, why don't you beat him?"

The network administrator told him, "That's the keyboard brought by the CS player..."

Children, if you go to the Internet bar to play J5 in the future, remember to bring your own keyboard, or at least bring a dedicated "space bar".
