There are many reasons for bleeding during bowel movements. One reason could be anal fissures; another is hemorrhoids, mainly characterized by bloody stools due to hemorrhoids; the third possibility includes rectal cancer. The most common causes of blood in the stool can be categorized into six types:

1. Hemorrhoids: The bleeding occurs during defecation or after, with bright red blood that does not mix with feces.

2. Anal Fissure: The blood is bright red, either dripping out or visible on toilet paper. People with fresh anal fissures may experience severe pain after defecation.

3. Digestive Tract Diseases: Stools may appear tarry or dark red, indicating bleeding in the upper digestive tract. If the blood is purely red, it is more likely due to bleeding in the lower digestive tract.

4. Advanced Rectal Disease: The blood in the stool is bright red and drips onto the surface of the stool. In advanced stages, there may be a sensation of heaviness in the anus and rectum, along with weight loss, increased frequency of bowel movements, alternating constipation and diarrhea.

5. Rectal Bleeding, Colonic Polyps: The blood is bright red, painless, and does not mix with the stool. Ulcerative colitis and dysentery usually involve mucus or pus-blood stools, accompanied by lower abdominal pain, fever, and frequent bowel movements.

6. Systemic Diseases: Such as leukemia, hemophilia, uremia, and some rare infectious diseases. When there is blood in the stool, there may also be bleeding in other parts of the body.
