Nearly 2000 couples in Qingdao, Shandong Province registered their marriage on Valentine's Day (Photo Attached)

by zxyasdw90 on 2011-07-04 13:56:47

Got the marriage certificate, let's pose for a picture!

February 14th, Valentine's Day, the twelfth day of the first lunar month, and also the second anniversary of NetEase Blog. In the eyes of many newlyweds, this can be considered the first good day of 2011, being both Valentine's Day and a wedding anniversary. This double blessing led to a surge in couples registering their marriages on this day in the island city, setting a record for the highest number of registrations on Valentine's Day in the history of Qingdao. By 4 PM on February 14th, I learned from the Civil Affairs Bureau that nearly two thousand couples had registered across the 13 marriage registration offices in all districts and cities, including 241 in Shibei District, over 230 in Shinan District, 162 in Sifang District, and 101 in Licang District.

"They say there was no Start of Spring in the lunar calendar of 2010, making it a 'widow year' unsuitable for marriage. Now that the year without spring has passed, we've come!" Fang Zhigang and Zhao Guangjuan, who had been in love for over two years, arrived at the Shinan District Marriage Registration Office before 7 AM, eager to complete their life event on the first auspicious day of 2011. At 9:30 AM on February 14th, when I arrived at the Shinan District Marriage Registration Office, it was already packed with couples waiting to register, and more than 200 registration numbers had been issued in just over an hour since opening.

At 10 AM, I saw long queues at every window of the Shinan District Marriage Registration Office, with staff continuously distributing forms and reviewing documents but still unable to keep up. Some brides-to-be fell asleep resting their heads on the tables after waiting for a long time. Meanwhile, Xue Wei and Jiang Shasha were much calmer while waiting for their turn. This young couple even took out prepared playing cards and started playing Landlord in the waiting hall.

"The entire month of January saw only 293 registrations, but there have already been 174 this morning alone!" At 11:30 AM, I arrived at the Shibei District Civil Affairs Bureau Marriage Registration Office, where the head, Chen Mei, told me that today's registration count set a record for the most registrations on Valentine's Day in the past eight years in Shibei District. "We didn't expect so many people! We opened the doors at 7:30 AM, and we even converted the meeting room into additional windows, but we're still overwhelmed!" Chen Mei informed me that there were over seventy couples who had pre-booked their registration for February 14th before the New Year, whereas there were only fifteen couples who registered on Valentine's Day in 2003. "In the past, people avoided getting married on the 13th or 14th days, but now young people's thinking has changed, and Valentine's Day has completely overshadowed the Qixi Festival."

"Choosing a date is indeed important, but the essence of marriage lies in how both parties nurture their feelings post-marriage, respect their parents, and live well together." Director Chen Mei reminded all new couples that marriage registration offices now accept advance reservations. If relevant materials are submitted early for review, one can quickly obtain the certificate on an auspicious day through a dedicated window without needing to queue.

Written and photographed by reporter Huang Mo
