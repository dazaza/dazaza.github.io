Sina Weibo is testing the "Fan Headlines" feature, which is being opened to users with less than 1000 fans in the first batch.

by anonymous on 2013-08-29 16:59:22

On August 28, Sina Weibo quietly launched the internal testing of its self-service promotion feature for individuals called "Fan Headlines." With this feature, personal users can pin a single Weibo post to the top of their fans' Weibo homepages once. The first batch of internal test permissions is only available to non-verified Weibo personal users with fewer than 1000 followers.

Highlighting personal value and ensuring important content isn't lost.

Some Weibo users noticed that starting from that day, on the bottom of a single posted Weibo, to the left of the "like" function, when you hover over it with your mouse, the word "Promote" appears (previously it was the "VIP Pin" function). The promotion menu includes two functions: "Pin" and "Fan Headlines."

Sina Weibo's internal testing of "Fan Headlines"

Different from the "Pin" function available to Weibo VIPs, a single Weibo post promoted using the "Fan Headlines" function can be pinned to the top of fans' Weibo homepages, appearing once within 24 hours. Users can repeatedly promote the same content, and if multiple friends use this function at the same time, another friend's promoted content will appear after refreshing the page.

During the 24-hour promotion period, the user's promoted content can achieve the highest priority in the fans' reading lists, thereby increasing the exposure of that content and promoting information interaction among Weibo friends. For fans who see the "Fan Headlines" information, they won't miss important information shares such as weddings or births of people they follow.

The foundation of social platforms lies in the stability of the platform's social relationships, and by continuously providing content creation and consumption to achieve platform development. People, content, and relationships are the necessary conditions for the survival of social platforms. From last year's "Weibo Read Count" to the launch of "Fan Headlines," Sina Weibo is gradually highlighting its social platform genes and enhancing the value of personal users.

Future of social websites: great potential for personal growth

"Fan Headlines" is a function launched by Sina Weibo targeting the content promotion needs of personal users. To prevent the possible abuse of the "Fan Headlines" function for commercial purposes, Sina Weibo will impose restrictions from two dimensions.

Firstly, restrictions on the qualifications of users using this function. Currently, "Fan Headlines" is only open to non-verified users with a certain number of fans or less, to prevent large V users and other accounts from obtaining commercial benefits through this function, affecting user experience.

Secondly, controlling the price. The usage price of the "Fan Headlines" function is set based on the number of fans, divided into different levels. According to Sina, most users in the first batch have fewer than 1000 Weibo fans, and a single promotion costs only 10 yuan.

The launch of the "Fan Headlines" function by Sina Weibo is not accidental. In recent years, most websites at home and abroad have successively tried and launched similar message pinning features.

As early as May last year, Facebook released the message pinning feature "Promote Message Pinning" in New Zealand for the first time, followed by tests in 20 other countries. The price Facebook quoted users was a fixed $7, equivalent to about 45 RMB.

According to foreign media reports, microblogging site Tumblr also launched a new advertising model in July last year: "Pinned Posts." Users just need to spend $5 to keep one of their posts pinned on their fans' pages for 24 hours.

"Fan Headlines" is closer to Pinned Posts but much cheaper. As one of the first domestic social networking sites to try paid personal message promotion, Sina Weibo is actively exploring its commercialization.

According to the latest financial report data released by Sina, the revenue from Weibo value-added services in the second quarter of 2013 increased significantly, growing by 186% compared to the same period last year, reaching 7.7 million US dollars. The value-added service business mainly realizes revenue through Weibo VIP and game businesses, and personal value-added services are playing an important role in social websites.
