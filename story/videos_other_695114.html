5-Year-Old Girl Survived on Leftover Rice for 7 Days with Her Dead Grandmother's Body

by aiipii12987 on 2012-03-07 14:20:00

Tianshan Net (Xinjiang Metropolis Daily reporter Sulupa, correspondent Zhang Danhui report) - Recently, the Xinjiang Metropolis Daily learned from the Yining police that an elderly person died at home from carbon monoxide poisoning and was only discovered 7 days later. Shockingly, her 5-year-old granddaughter survived for 7 days alongside the body, subsisting on leftover rice, biscuits, and other food.

On February 3rd, in a bungalow within a courtyard on Airport Road in Yining City, a 5-year-old girl was found playing alone by the bed. Beside her was an uncovered spittoon filled with burnt-out coal ash. Not far away, her grandmother lay stiffly on the ground, having already passed away silently. At around 7 PM that day, after receiving the alarm, the Yining Public Security Bureau arrived at the scene and preliminarily determined that the deceased had been dead for 7 days, succumbing to carbon monoxide poisoning.

It was understood that the deceased, Li Mou, was from Anhui Province. Due to their busy work schedules, her son and daughter-in-law were unable to care for their young daughter, so Li Mou came to Yining City just before the Spring Festival. On January 17th, due to an urgent matter in their hometown, her son, Zhang Mou, sent his mother Li Mou and his 5-year-old daughter to the rented courtyard near Airport Road. After settling the elderly woman and child, Zhang returned to his Anhui hometown. In his haste to leave, Zhang did not inform his wife Zhao, who was doing business in Yining, of the exact address of his mother.

In the following dozen or so days, Zhao had also called her mother-in-law's phone, but it was always off. Busy with her business, Zhao did not give much thought to this matter. It wasn't until February 3rd, when she hadn't heard any news from her mother-in-law or child, that she became anxious. After contacting her husband, Zhao, accompanied by neighbors, went to the rental house located on Airport Road in Yining City, where she discovered that her mother-in-law Li Mou had already passed away. Fortunately, during the week-plus time after Li Mou's death, the over-5-year-old child miraculously survived by eating leftover rice, biscuits, and other snacks at home.

The police reminded the public, especially those using fireplaces, to check if the fire is safe before going to sleep every night. Especially during winter overcast or snowy weather, when coal does not burn fully, and outdoor air pressure is low, making it difficult for indoor carbon monoxide to be discharged, it is important to keep the indoor area ventilated to prevent accidents.

Reporter's Notes:

This accident is both regrettable and heart-wrenching. The elderly woman was dead for a full 7 days before being discovered. A child, who has no concept of life and death, had to endure the loss of her grandmother all alone. It is unimaginable how this 5-year-old child endured these 7 days. Alone facing day and night, fear and hunger, she must have called out "Mom" and "Dad" countless times through the door cracks.

The reporter believes that if Li Mou's busy son and daughter-in-law could have shown more care towards the elderly and children who were not present with them, making daily calls to inquire about their situation, being more cautious, sensitive, and serious, perhaps such a tragedy would not have occurred.
