"Sex Photo Scandal" stirs again, as Qi Na announces more explicit photos to be released on the 24th (Pictures are interesting)

by bugbear on 2008-04-28 12:31:55

The sensational "sex photo scandal" has now gradually calmed down, and the "Qina" who initially released the sex photos of celebrities on the Internet has also disappeared for a long time, and is still whereabouts unknown. Just as everyone was gradually forgetting about this incident, it was recently rumored online that "Qina" will soon return. It was reported that "Qina" had been waiting for the right moment all along, and he would once again release new "sex photos" on the Internet, and the date would be April 24th - the day of the pre-trial hearing of the "sex photo scandal".

New "flash cards" mysteriously reappear

On April 1st, in the "Gordon Discussion Area", which was rumored to be where "Qina" initially released the "sex photos", a mysterious netizen posted a thread titled "Successfully downloaded Qina's new flash cards", boldly claiming that they had downloaded over a hundred "flash cards" (hinting at "sex photos"). This news immediately drew a lot of attention from netizens, who were vying to ask how to obtain the new "flash cards". However, many netizens also questioned the credibility of the new "flash cards", because that day was April Fools' Day in the West.

Is Qina about to "revive"?

Although the mysterious netizen never posted any new "sex photos" or related link addresses in the discussion area, many netizens still responded enthusiastically. Until April 10th, a netizen in the Hong Kong discussion area suddenly claimed, "I just heard from my colleague... Qina will appear next week." Some media even received anonymous emails, but the attached "new sex photos" were eventually confirmed to be old photos that "Qina" had previously released.

Recently, in the "Gordon Discussion Area", another netizen posted a suspected new "flash card" link address. Many netizens who entered the address claimed that the female protagonist was an actress named "Haiqi", but no netizen posted the "sex photos" to verify it. It was discovered that the link led to an adult discussion forum, which had a "Chen Guanxi Sex Photo Special Zone", but there were no new "sex photos".

High alert by the police to prevent leaks

After more than a month of low-key investigation by the Hong Kong police, the sensational "sex photo scandal" case was scheduled for a pre-trial hearing on the 24th of this month. At that time, both the prosecution and defense lawyers would submit relevant evidence, witness lists, etc., to confirm everything clearly and decide on the official trial date. As the pre-trial date approached, rumors of Qina's return spread online, once again causing panic in the entertainment industry. The nightmare for the actresses who initially rejoiced that their sex photos were not published seems far from over.

It was reported that Qina indeed remains at large until now, and he still holds some "sex photos" that are already in the hands of the police but have not yet been released. He plans to officially "revive" on April 24th, the day of the pre-trial hearing of the "sex photo scandal", challenging the Hong Kong police once again. It was learned that the Hong Kong police have already gone on high alert to prevent any further leakage of "sex photos".

The "sex photo scandal" has lasted nearly three months. And April 24th, Thursday, is exactly the day of the pre-trial hearing of the Hong Kong sex photo case.

The person who released the sex photos remains at large

In fact, after Qina stopped releasing new sex photos in February, Hong Kong and mainland websites occasionally had people claiming to release new sex photos or even videos for netizens to download, but all the sex photos turned out to be computer-synthesized photos, and the links led to ordinary adult websites. Some netizens even maliciously spread computer viruses.

It was learned that the person who released the sex photos, Qina, indeed remains at large, and he still holds some sex photos that are already in the hands of the police but have not yet been released. He is preparing to officially "revive" on April 24th, the day of the pre-trial hearing of the sex photo case, once again challenging the Hong Kong police to show their helplessness when the sex photo case goes to trial.
