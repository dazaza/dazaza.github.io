The 10 Latest Secrets to Running a Successful Online Store in 2011

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:28:23

Many people want to start a business by opening a store, and quite a few have already done so. However, those who truly understand how to manage their stores are relatively few. How to make your store recognized and become the first choice for customers when they shop is the most concerning issue for all sellers. Below is a brief analysis of this question.

1. Think of a good store name. A great name is your first step to grabbing attention, even if it's quirky. Ideally, it should be unforgettable, but at least it should remind customers of your store when they see similar products. If you're using an independent mall system, make sure to choose a simple and memorable domain (like XpShop.cn). Remember, having buyers remember your website is more sustainable than them simply adding you to their favorites.

2. Design a personalized logo. A good store name alone isn't enough; customers may prefer something simple, which is where the logo comes in. If you're running a platform-based online store without a dedicated spot for a logo, you can incorporate it into your banner ad and watermark it onto product images. For independent mall users, this is usually straightforward as there’s typically a designated space for the logo. Even if you're not a professional designer, basic skills in PS or CorelDRAW can yield good results. Alternatively, you can ask a friend for help. A personalized logo greatly aids visitors in remembering your store. If you're an independent online store, you can leave the website development and logo design to your e-commerce system provider.

3. Do well in SEO for your online store. Many people know about SEO (Search Engine Optimization), but few know how to do it. The biggest advantage of SEO is that it allows search engines to better index your site. In fact, SEO optimization applies mainly to mall systems. Since platform-based stores belong to the platform, they cannot be indexed by Baidu and other search engines, meaning specific store information won't appear in search results. However, SEO for independent online stores is simpler than most people think. Once the system's SEO optimization is complete, you just need to fill in some store keywords to be easily indexed by search engines. Don't underestimate search engine indexing; a good ranking can bring significant traffic. Of course, you can also leave the optimization work to your e-commerce system provider.

4. Place appropriate product information and suitable pictures. Note the emphasis on "appropriate" rather than "detailed" or "beautiful." Complete product information serves as the window through which buyers understand the product. Detailed descriptions indeed help sales, but more detail isn't always better. Concise and convincing explanations might be more effective for picky buyers. The same goes for pictures; elaborate processing doesn’t necessarily attract buyers. Pictures need to be practical, with each one clearly conveying benefits of purchasing the product. It's important to be appropriate because exaggerated images and text can leave a bad impression unless you don't care if customers return or refer friends. Independent malls have a slight edge here since these elements often come pre-framed in the system, saving much mental effort.

5. Offer a variety of payment options. Platform sellers don't need to worry much as platforms set restrictions, allowing only certain choices like credit card support. For independent online stores, due to payment integration issues, special attention is needed. The XpShop independent online store system has integrated payment gateways, making it user-friendly. If your system hasn't integrated all mainstream payment gateways, you can sign up with these payment institutions yourself. Providing sufficient payment options is crucial for store operation. You've likely encountered losing a sale because a buyer used Tenpay while your store didn't support it. Thus, preparing accordingly is essential—better safe than sorry.

6. Provide customers with a pleasant shopping experience. This point applies whether you run an independent store or a platform-based store. Customers care deeply about their shopping experience. A new store owner in a chat group kept complaining about why no one was buying from his store. He ran a top-up store, and I checked it out—it seemed okay, so I decided to support him. But after paying, he took a long time to ship and didn’t communicate (others might have thought they were scammed). After a long wait, he finally showed up, apologizing profusely. Turns out, he didn't know how to operate the system and was sweating it out alone. You can imagine why his store had no customers. If you buy something and ask how to use it and the seller can't answer, would you still buy? If a transaction involves repeated hassle, wouldn’t you opt for a refund? Clearly, customers won't demand perfection, but if your store fails to provide ease, dissatisfaction will follow. At best, they'll buy once and never return, possibly spreading negative word-of-mouth. Your first order could be a trial for a friend, or even for yourself. Without experiencing it, no one knows what it feels like as a buyer, nor how to improve sales. I believe no customer wants to deal with such a seller.

7. Reasonable promotion. Promotion is the most headache-inducing problem for online store operators. Many sellers associate promotion with flooding forums and posting ads everywhere. Indeed, many do this. For non-large stores, large-scale advertising investments aren't feasible, so many choose to spread information via posts. But how effective are these actions? Those who haven't tried can test it—watered-down posts in Baidu Taobao bar can vanish from the first page within seconds, and other popular bars are similar. In reality, both forums and comment sections are hostile to ads. Given general aversion to advertisements, these posts rarely reach viewers, yielding minimal effects.

A superior promotional method is soft articles and blog promotion. Everyone knows soft articles, but a good one must subtly convey your message without appearing promotional, allowing readers to unconsciously accept it. This requires practice and summarization. Regardless of the type of soft article, it needs to be posted in the right place—forums require the correct section. This increases the article's survival chance. Initially, I took shortcuts with soft article promotion services included in the mall system, spending some money but seeing decent results.

Blog promotion is a patience-testing endeavor requiring substantial time and energy. Fortunately, a more convenient way now exists—Weibo. Cleverly utilizing this emerging medium might yield unexpected results.

8. Timely promotional activities. Promotions, yes, but not just discounts. Irrational consumer behavior is particularly prominent in online shopping, and promotions are the best way to attract their attention. However, constantly promoting like those merchants shouting “last three days, clearance” all year round merely draws onlookers without much effect. By timely, I mean grasping the timing of promotions, like during the World Cup, where many made a fortune through promotions and bundled sales. Promotion content can be innovative or adopt proven methods, but try not to stick to one way. Platform stores face operational limitations, while independent online store systems come with built-in promotional schemes.

9. Detailed sales statistics. Many only focus on selling but never care about their sales situation, a huge misconception. Sales reflect your current development stage and offer the best guidance for your sales efforts. Regularly analyze your sales data to derive income, profit, and sales time information, creating tables for reference. From these statistics, you can intuitively understand your store's problems and necessary adjustments. The longer the statistical period, the more reliable the information.

Remember, don't dismiss these details as unimportant. Details determine success or failure—a true reflection. Independent online store system users can directly achieve this through backend statistical functions, saving effort; platform users need to spend time manually. However, the investment yields visible returns.

10. Obtain customer contact information and stay in touch as much as possible. This is easier for independent online store sellers since users must register before shopping, making retrieval straightforward. However, platform store users may need to actively seek contact information due to platform intervention. Having just Wangwang isn't enough, as not everyone stays logged in all day. If unable to get phone numbers, at least obtain QQ numbers to help with future targeted promotions and increase repeat purchases.

After obtaining contact information, regularly send SMS messages, ideally once a week to avoid being seen as overly eager. This reminds the recipient of your store's existence and builds rapport. When they get accustomed to your greetings, even if a promotional message doesn't interest them, they might check it out. This wins valuable revisit opportunities and potential consumption for your store.

These ten points need to be used together. Remember, any method will be systematic; relying on a single advantage to stand out isn't realistic. Hopefully, this helps sellers in confusion. Store management strategies vary among individuals, with no universal solution. Even for the same aspect, opinions may differ, and identical methods may yield different results across stores. However, it's certain that with dedication, focusing on these details can lead to success.
