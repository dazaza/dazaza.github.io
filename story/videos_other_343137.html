The Rule of Accessory Points in Fashion Coordination - March 31, 2010, 10:22 "Points", meaning the quantification of beauty. A rule is a formula, a universal tool. The great mathematician Pythagoras said: "Where there are numbers, there is beauty." Without numerical proportions or the proper handling of numerical relationships, beauty can turn into ugliness. For women, the most frustrating issue may not just be how to wear accessories but also the question of how many to wear. Here, I propose the concept of "points". This is a relevant rule that addresses the issue of how many accessories should be worn for different occasions.

A "point" refers to any part of the body where visual focus converges, including all kinds of jewelry and even nail polish on hands and feet. The "number" is determined based on the visual attractiveness synthesized from each "focus point". Of course, these are all relative concepts. First, let's see what qualifies as a "point".

From head to toe, earrings, necklaces, rings, watches, bracelets, anklets, glasses, hairpins, conspicuous buttons on clothing, prominent belt buckles, metallic decorations on shoes and bags, and non-natural colored nail polishes on fingers and toes all count as decorative "points" on the body.

Next, let's discuss how to calculate "points". Among the items listed above, those that are small in size and inconspicuous in color do not count. Items that serve as accents and attract attention count as one point per location. Larger and more prominent items should be counted more heavily. For example, regular-sized earrings on both ears count as two points; however, if they are large and very noticeable, they might count as four points.

The general standard for determining whether the quantity is appropriate is as follows: up to three points indicates minimal decoration; between three and five points indicates moderate decoration; around six to eight points indicates obvious decoration; between nine and twelve points indicates formal decoration; and over thirteen points indicates excessive decoration.

We can adjust the number of accessories we wear according to our occasion needs by referring to the point system. Additionally, we can adjust the relationship between accessories and clothing based on the elegance of the attire, such as that from Tunai Trendy Women's Clothing Discount Store.

It is important to note that at high-class social events, women have more freedom to expose skin. Since skin is considered the largest accessory for women, it should also be converted into points and factored into the calculation to adjust the evening's accessories and avoid being overly formal.
