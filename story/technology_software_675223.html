The 80-mu fishpond contracted by the man was poisoned, and he refused to sell the poisoned fish

by xue94fwsh on 2012-02-24 21:46:20

(Informant: Mr. Liu; Bonus: 50 yuan) "I've been busy since early morning scooping up dead fish from the pond. More than twenty thousand fish have already died, and according to market price, I've lost more than 150,000 yuan." According to Boss He, he had just successfully bid for the contract of an 80-mu fishpond in the village two days ago. Unexpectedly, someone put parathion-methyl into the pond yesterday. "Now this 10-mu fishpond has concentrated all the fish from other ponds covering more than 70 mu. Dead fish are still appearing continuously. This morning, I found pesticide bottles on the shore, and I have already reported it to the police." Boss He told the reporter that yesterday morning, he and his partner were patrolling the fishpond when they smelled a strong smell of parathion-methyl. They then found discarded pesticide bottles in the grass with about 50 milliliters of residual liquid inside. There were also large numbers of floating dead carp and grass carp on the water surface. His heart sank instantly. By afternoon, there were more and more dead fish, which drew quite a few villagers to watch. He quickly hired people to scoop up the dead fish from the pond to bury them deep underground.

This report comes from Qingyuan (reported by Cao Jing with photos). Two people from Jiangxi province who had just won the bid for an 80-mu fishpond encountered poison retaliation the next day. Yesterday morning, the two people faced a pond full of floating dead fish in Yuangang Village, Shijiao Town, Qingyuan City, with worried expressions. The incident occurred in Shijiao Town, Qingyuan yesterday, resulting in losses of tens of thousands of yuan for the victims. The police have already intervened in the investigation. Boss He said that he had been contracting fishponds in Qingyuan for more than a year. This was the first time he encountered such poisoning causing economic losses. The incident also drew attention from the village officials of Yuangang Village, who specially came to the scene for inspection. Despite occasional inquiries about whether the dead fish were for sale, Old He refused them all out of concern that if the dead fish entered the market, the consequences would be severe.
