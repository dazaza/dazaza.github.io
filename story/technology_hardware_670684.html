Four obstetricians, five midwives, five neonatal doctors, two surgical instrument nurses, at least five anesthesiologists, five maternity nurses, one internal medicine director... Yesterday morning, a large-scale cesarean section surgery was performed in Guangdong Maternity and Child Healthcare Hospital. Under the joint efforts of dozens of medical workers, the five babies in the belly of Ms. Zheng, a 24-year-old female worker from Henan who had been pregnant for 28 weeks, were born within five minutes. These three boys and two girls are also the first quintuplets to be successfully born in Guangzhou.

By reporter Rao Zhen

Correspondents Peng Wenbin, Lin Huifang

Photographer Qiao Junwei

A probability of one in more than 60 million: A frail female worker became pregnant with quintuplets

In July this year, our newspaper reported the story of Ms. Zheng Xiaoni, a 24-year-old female worker from Henan, who unexpectedly found out that she was carrying quintuplets during prenatal check-ups in Guangzhou, which attracted much attention from readers (see A22 of our newspaper on July 6, "24-Year-Old Female Worker Pregnant with Quintuplets"). Ms. Zheng's grandmother, uncle, and aunt were all twins, indicating a clear family genetic tendency in her situation. However, quintuplets are indeed rare, with a probability of only one in more than 60 million. Due to poor basic nutritional status, although Ms. Zheng was 1.65 meters tall, she weighed only 114 pounds at 22 weeks of pregnancy. With the help of volunteers, she was sent to the provincial maternal and child healthcare hospital for monitoring.

Multiple pregnancies can bring many risks to the mother's health and the growth and development of the children. In Ms. Zheng's case, if it was early in the pregnancy, doctors usually would suggest reducing the number of fetuses. However, since Ms. Zheng discovered that she was carrying quintuplets relatively late, after organizing relevant experts for multidisciplinary consultations, the provincial maternal and child healthcare hospital believed that reducing the number of fetuses would pose significant risks.

In the following month or so, while preventing Ms. Zheng from having a miscarriage or premature birth, the doctors were also trying to extend her gestation period as much as possible, hoping that the five babies could grow inside their mother's womb for as long as possible.

Large-scale cesarean section surgery: Dozens of medical workers participated together

"Ms. Zheng started experiencing frequent contractions around 1:30 am today, and after we evaluated, we thought she had entered labor," said Professor Niu Jianmin, Director of Obstetrics at Guangdong Maternal and Child Healthcare Hospital. Since Ms. Zheng's cervix had already dilated to 3 centimeters and the amniotic sac was protruding, it was impossible to avoid premature birth. The doctor decided to perform a cesarean section to terminate the pregnancy. He explained, "For Ms. Zheng, a cesarean section is more beneficial than natural childbirth, because a cesarean section can promptly handle the situation of the baby during delivery, avoiding the prolongation of labor and asphyxia caused by natural childbirth."

At 8:20 am, under the watchful eyes of her husband, Ms. Zheng was pushed into the operating room. At the same time, dozens of medical workers from different departments were also preparing tensely for this special cesarean section surgery. Four obstetricians, five midwives, five neonatal doctors, two surgical instrument nurses, at least five anesthesiologists, five maternity nurses, one internal medicine director... Such a large-scale cesarean section surgery was also the first in the nearly 70-year history of Guangdong Maternal and Child Healthcare Hospital.

Joy: Five babies were born within five minutes

At exactly 9:00 am, under the expectant gazes of all medical workers, the first baby was born! The neonatal doctor waiting nearby immediately took over this male baby weighing 0.83 kg for rescue. At 9:05 am, the fifth baby came to meet everyone, and his cry was the loudest. "To prevent a too fast drop in body temperature, the small bodies of the five babies were quickly wrapped with cling film, and then placed into incubators."

"Fortunately, both Ms. Zheng's own condition and the condition of the babies at birth were better than we expected," said Professor Niu Jianmin. Ms. Zheng's vital signs remained stable throughout the surgery, without any serious surgical complications, and the amount of blood loss during the surgery was also very little, about the same as that of a single fetus mother; "After birth, it was found that the heaviest baby weighed 940 grams, while a normal single fetus pregnancy at 28 weeks is about 1000 grams, which is really very precious."

Worry: How will income from working support five children

At around 10:10 am, Ms. Zheng, who had just become a mother, was wheeled out of the operating room. Facing relatives, doctors, and the media, the weak Ms. Zheng struggled to say a sentence with a faint voice: "Are the children okay?" When she learned that all five babies were fine, she closed her tear-filled eyes and did not speak again.

On the side, Mr. Zhang Zhongtang, Ms. Zheng's 25-year-old husband, did not show the joy of becoming a father for the first time. His face seemed filled with worry. He did not know whether the five babies, each weighing less than 1 kilogram, could survive smoothly, where to raise money to support them, or even whether the five babies could fit on the bed in the rented house.

Since the five babies would have to stay in the incubator for at least another one or two months, raising medical fees was an urgent matter. Mr. Zhang Zhongtang said that his mother previously worked as a cleaner and earned 1300 yuan per month, but had resigned a few days ago to prepare to help take care of the children. His father, who relied on odd jobs, also had no savings. He himself used to work as a security guard with a monthly income of about 2000 yuan, but later got injured when moving glass, cutting the nerves in his wrist, making him unable to lift heavy objects and unable to find new work.

Heartwarming moment:

Father visits babies in the incubator

At around 11:00 am yesterday, wearing visiting clothes, mask, and hat, Mr. Zhang Zhongtang was specially allowed to enter the neonatal intensive care unit to visit the five babies. Five incubators were lined up, and the babies lying inside were connected to ventilators, looking like fragile kittens. Only the differently colored small hats on each of their heads could distinguish them.

Mr. Zhang Zhongtang walked in front of each incubator in turn, affectionately gazing at these unfamiliar yet familiar children. The children's small chests rose and fell as they struggled to breathe, as if saying to their father, "We will try to live."

"I am really worried about them, they are so small," said Mr. Zhang Zhongtang. He had too many worries and did not know how to express his feelings, "My heart is all over the place." However, he did not tell his wife about his worries, "I don't want her to worry, so I told her the children are fine."
