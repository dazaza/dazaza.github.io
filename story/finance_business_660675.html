In 2011, the pressure of falling consumer prices became evident, with the largest declines seen in home renovation and building materials costs – Domestic News - Industry News - Building Materials Network. Investigations into living consumption costs, which are most concerning to the public, revealed that among various industries over the past year, renovation and building material prices fell the most. Following this were declines in domestic services, furniture and appliance repairs, travel, car rentals with drivers, entertainment and leisure, domestic logistics, and more.

Among the 2,368 consumers surveyed, 17% had plans for home renovations or purchasing building materials. Among these, over 90% reported that their renovation budgets had exceeded expectations, while only 10% said they stayed within budget. More than 50% exceeded their budget by more than 30%.

Journalists learned that with the arrival of spring and summer, the renovation market entered its peak season. People starting renovations noticed that the majority of building materials had quietly increased in price, causing renovation budgets to continuously rise. Through random phone interviews with merchants specializing in renovations and building materials on Ganji.com, it was found that the prices of renovations and building materials indeed began to rise in the second half of 2010. Besides increases in raw materials, labor, and logistics costs, advertising expenses also rose, leading to a general increase in the cost of renovations and building materials. While seasonal price increases due to rising consumer demand during the peak season is one reason for higher consumption costs, the overall increase in both hard and soft costs within the industry cannot be overlooked.

The survey also found that among the 2,368 consumers polled, 58% said they would consider comparing prices through multiple channels before making purchases, choosing the most cost-effective options that suit their needs. Through internal information from Ganji.com, it was learned that in addition to renovations and building materials, there has been a significant increase in demand for domestic services, furniture and appliance repairs, travel, car rentals with drivers, entertainment and leisure, and domestic logistics over the past year. An increasing number of "smart" consumers are using their own judgment to save expenses in response to the issue of budget overruns caused by rising prices.
