Apple's Fourth Quarter Financial Report Interpretation: Signs of Rebound Emergence

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 16:50:24

Apple today released its financial results for the fourth fiscal quarter of 2013, ending September 28. The company saw a 4% increase in revenue but a 9% drop in net profit compared to the same period last year. These figures, while not outstanding when compared to Apple's long-term historical data, do indicate signs of recovery relative to the performance over the past three quarters.

The latest figures show that Apple's revenue growth has started to rebound from the bottom, temporarily reversing the previous downward trend. In the previous seven quarters, Apple’s revenue growth had dropped from 73% to just 1% in the third quarter.

The most recent net profit figures also reveal a significant slowing down of the decline. Apple experienced its first year-on-year net profit drop in a decade during the second fiscal quarter this year, and this trend continued into the fourth quarter of 2013. Although there was a 9% year-on-year decline in the fourth quarter, this represents a substantial improvement compared to the 18% and 22% declines seen in the two preceding quarters.

Last week, Apple significantly refreshed its product lineup with the launch of the new iPad Air, iPad mini, and MacBook computers, as well as the earlier release of the iPhone 5S and iPhone 5C. With the approach of the Christmas season, Apple is expected to see more growth in the first quarter of the 2014 fiscal year.

However, Apple did not make overly optimistic forecasts. In its latest earnings report, Apple projected revenues for the next quarter to be between $55 billion and $58 billion, which would represent only a 6.4% growth at the upper end of the forecast.

Analysts generally believe that Apple needs to expand into new product categories to regain rapid growth. Tim Cook, Apple’s CEO, confirmed this during the earnings call with analysts. Cook acknowledged that Apple will introduce new product categories in the 2014 fiscal year but did not disclose further specific details.

Speculation persists that Apple will release new smartphone and television products. If successful in expanding into these new categories, Apple could return to a high-growth trajectory.

In terms of core product performance, the iPhone remains Apple’s most important product, critical to its financial health. According to the latest earnings report, Apple sold 33.8 million iPhones in the quarter, up from 26.9 million in the same period last year and exceeding the average analyst estimate of about 32 million units.

Apple's iPhone sales over the past two quarters have demonstrated that the theory of saturation in the high-end smartphone market does not hold water. When Apple announced its third-quarter results, concerns about the saturation of the high-end smartphone market were widespread due to Samsung and HTC failing to meet expectations. However, the consistent iPhone sales figures over two quarters suggest that the high-end smartphone market is far from saturated, and consumers continue to favor premium products.

On the iPad front, Apple’s performance was less impressive. During the fourth quarter, Apple sold 14.1 million iPads, slightly higher than the 14 million units sold in the same period last year but below the average analyst expectation of 14.5 million units. This latest figure indicates that the iPad's weak performance continued from the third quarter.

In the third quarter, Apple sold 14.6 million iPads, down from 17 million in the same period last year, marking a 14% decline, which was the first time iPad shipments showed a year-over-year drop.

The lackluster performance of the iPad over two consecutive quarters may be related to Apple's failure to timely launch new models. Additionally, low-cost tablets from competitors like Samsung, Amazon, and Microsoft may have impacted iPad sales.

However, with the introduction of the new iPad Air and iPad mini, iPad shipments are expected to see renewed growth.

The Chinese market continues to be sluggish. The latest earnings report shows that Apple’s revenue in Greater China was $5.733 billion, an increase of 6% compared to $5.427 billion in the same period last year. While Apple reversed the declining trend seen in the previous quarter, overall performance in the Chinese market remains lackluster.

In the first fiscal quarter of this year, Apple reported Greater China revenue separately for the first time, with revenue reaching $6.83 billion, representing a 67% increase from the same period last year. Previously, revenue growth in Greater China had consistently exceeded 40%.

However, starting from the second quarter, Apple's performance in Greater China began to slow significantly. In the second quarter, revenue in Greater China was $8.213 billion, up 8% from $7.637 billion in the same period last year.

In the third quarter, revenue in Greater China was $4.641 billion, down 14% from $5.389 billion in the same period last year. In the fourth quarter, Apple's revenue growth was only 6%.

For the entire fiscal year 2013, revenue in Greater China was $27 billion, up 14% year-over-year. For Apple, the Chinese market remains a huge opportunity. To achieve greater growth in China, Apple needs to increase investment and open more retail stores.
