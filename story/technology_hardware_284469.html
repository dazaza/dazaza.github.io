+ Maintenance and Care of Carpets    

It is necessary to ensure regular cleaning, washing once every one to two months. If there are stubborn stains on the carpet, professional personnel should use specialized equipment for cleaning and maintenance.

+ Maintenance and Care of Marble    

In daily life, substances like soy sauce and vinegar may fall onto the surface of marble. If not cleaned in time, they will seep into the marble, causing discoloration and leaving permanent marks.

+ Maintenance and Care of Wooden Furniture    

Do not place hot cups or high-temperature objects on the furniture surfaces. Applying a layer of wax on the furniture surface can make it look shiny and prevent dust accumulation, making cleaning easier.

+ Maintenance and Care of Floors    

The floor should be cleaned daily to prevent hard objects from scratching the floor or small sand particles entering the gaps between floorboards. The best maintenance method is applying a protective layer of wax to increase the brightness of the floor while effectively protecting it.

+ Cleaning of Redwood Furniture    

If redwood furniture has dust, it can be lightly swept with a duster or wiped with a soft cloth. It is recommended to use pure cotton fabric as a wiping cloth, gently removing surface dust. Do not rinse directly with water or use excessively wet cloths, and avoid using alcohol or gasoline solvents for cleaning. For difficult stains on redwood furniture, vinegar can be used for cleaning. Mix white vinegar with hot water and use a pure cotton cloth to wipe the furniture surface, then scrub vigorously with a soft cloth to remove the stains. This method can also clean ink stains on harder furniture.

    To maintain the luster of lacquer coatings, you can grind walnuts, remove the skin, and wrap them in three layers of gauze to create an oil cloth. After applying oil to the redwood surface, polish it with gauze. Avoid using chemical brightening agents to prevent damage to the lacquer coating.

+ How to Restore Furniture Shine    

After prolonged use, furniture surfaces may lose their shine. Below are two simple methods to restore its original brightness:

1. Strong Tea Washing: Soak a soft cloth in strong tea and wash the furniture surface, which will make the lacquer shine.

2. Sewing Machine Oil Wiping: Dab a little sewing machine oil on a soft cloth, then repeatedly wipe the furniture, finally cleaning it with a clean soft cloth to restore its shine.

+ How to Clean Wicker Products with Oxalic Acid    

After long-term use, wicker products may gradually turn yellow. Although this color has its own charm, if you want to restore its previous white appearance, you can use oxalic acid to bleach it. Oxalic acid is a colorless crystalline substance. Dissolve it in hot water (about one spoon of oxalic acid per cup of water), then dip a brush in it and scrub. You will achieve unexpected results. When autumn arrives, to change the indoor atmosphere, you can apply water-based paint, which adds a touch of elegance. After removing dust or dirt, use a small brush to coat the mesh deeply, then along the mesh, and the process can be completed smoothly. The white color appears very striking.

    When the wicker becomes loose, soak it in water first, then fix it with pliers, and apply strong adhesive to make it stick together.

+ Maintenance of Artificial Stone Countertops    

Maintenance Tips:

1. Most Stains Pose No Threat to Artificial Stone    

Artificial stone is a solid, pore-free dense material that resists erosion by various stains. Food, cooking oil, soy sauce, vinegar, alcohol, iodine, lemon oil, lipstick, shoe polish, ink, etc., do not pose a threat to artificial stone. Its durability is already excellent, and it is easy to maintain without waxing, saving costs and making it widely applicable in both homes and commercial spaces.

2. Daily Cleaning    

Water stains or small amounts of dirt can be wiped with a damp cloth and a small amount of household cleaner or soapy water to remove them immediately. For severe dirt or cuts, use household stain remover and steel wool to wipe, maintaining the surface gloss.

3. Stronger Stains, Cigarette Burns, or Minor Scratches    

Use sandpaper with water to remove stains or scratches, then polish with polishing wax. For more serious damage, contact an artificial stone processing plant, as they have the skills and equipment to repair scars.

4. Renewed Appearance    

Since artificial stone is a solid material, inside and out, after several years, the way to keep the artificial stone looking brand new is to re-grind and polish the surface, immediately restoring its original appearance.

+ Waxing and Polishing of Wooden Floors    

Floor waxing is a measure to protect floors, extending their lifespan and achieving aesthetic results. Professional operators use waxing machines, polishing machines, combined with different types of wax, water-based wax, and hard wax for wooden floors, composite wooden floors, marble, and tiles to achieve a bright and wear-resistant effect.

Floor Waxing Operation Procedure:

1. Dust Removal: Remove attachments and some adhesives, glue, cement, and paint spots on the floor surface.

2. Cleaning: Use electric mops, saunas, disinfection, cleaning, without damaging the floor.

3. Waxing: Use different waxes for different floors, evenly applying eco-friendly wax.

4. Polishing: Completed by professional operators + polishing machine.

+ Refurbishment of Solid Wood Floors    

Solid wood floor refurbishment has become increasingly popular as more families start using wooden floors. Unlike other types of floors, wooden floors require periodic refurbishment and maintenance. Generally, depending on usage frequency, they need refurbishment every 5 years or so.

Many wooden floors, after years of use, appear worn, prompting many people to consider replacing them. However, this is not a wise decision. Because wooden floors are only superficially worn, the wood itself is still good. Especially after years of use, wooden floors have become very stable, whereas new wooden floors are less stable in this regard. Refurbishment involves grinding off only 0.5 to 2 millimeters of the floor surface. Moreover, refurbishing wooden floors is cheaper than buying new ones, making it economically advantageous.

Refurbishment Procedures:

Grinding: Use professional equipment to grind the surface and some scratches. Gradually grind from 40 grit, 80 grit, to 120 grit sandpaper to ensure the floor's smoothness and fineness.

Filling: After grinding, it is essential to apply filler to ensure the floor's natural color and prevent scratches.

Floor Varnish: Use eco-friendly water-based floor varnish, which won't cause unpleasant odors in the room. The varnish must be dedicated floor varnish, whose wear resistance meets floor requirements.

Maintenance: For refurbished wooden floors, we use dedicated wooden floor wax and equipment.

+ Methods for Handling Stone Diseases    

In recent years, due to the deterioration of the natural environment, dust, industrial exhaust, automobile exhaust, acid rain, and other pollutants easily contaminate stones used in buildings. Once building stones are contaminated, they are very difficult to clean. Using inappropriate cleaning agents to forcibly remove stains can easily damage the stones, accelerating their deterioration. In addition to a perfect structural design, the overall appearance maintenance of a modern building cannot be overlooked. Domestic stone applications for interior and exterior wall decorations are becoming increasingly common, and stone maintenance projects are gaining attention from architects, construction companies, and owners alike.

Common issues encountered include: water seepage through stone walls, efflorescence, rust stains, dirt spots, water marks, oil spots, contamination from construction materials, weathering, aging, and loss of luster due to foot traffic. Regarding these problems, our company provides the following treatment and preventive maintenance methods for your reference.

1. Handling Water Seepage and Pollution on Stone Walls:

Due to the strategic location of the building, pollution is especially severe. Stones naturally have capillaries and absorb water, and air pollution, dust, plus acidic gases and vehicle exhaust, accumulate over time on the stone surface. When it rains, acid rain accelerates the erosion of these contaminants and adsorbs them within the stone's capillaries, forming hard-to-clean stains. This process occurs faster on fire-treated stone surfaces and is harder to clean. Regular cleaning agents simply cannot remove these contaminants; glossy surfaces on polished stones quickly lose their sheen. Therefore, to remove these contaminants, SCI stone-specific cleaning agents can be used, such as SCI strong cleaning agent or SCI multi-functional cleaning agent. These stone-specific cleaning agents can completely remove contaminants that have penetrated into the capillaries without harming the stone. To prevent re-contamination, after thoroughly cleaning the stone, apply a protective treatment when the stone surface is dry. Commonly used penetrating protective agents include SCI multi-functional protective agent, SCI 15 stone protective agent, and HANO penetrating protective agent. These protective agents penetrate deep into the stone, forming a protective layer that offers water resistance, dirt resistance, rust prevention, oil stain resistance, anti-weathering, anti-aging, acid-base resistance, and prevents tea stains, cola, soy sauce, etc. They also effectively control the formation of efflorescence while maintaining the stone's natural breathability. Routine cleaning requires only water wiping to achieve effective results without the need for additional cleaning agents.

Preventative Measures for Exterior Wall Pollution:

Generally, stone exterior walls reach considerable heights, making cleaning difficult once polluted. If preventative measures are taken beforehand, there will be no pollution issues after completion, and daily cleaning will only require water wiping. Pre-construction protective treatments are cost-effective, easier to implement, and more efficient compared to traditional methods. Revolutionary breakthroughs in stone protection have improved the labor-intensive nature of past protective treatments, and this method is now widely applied in international stone projects.

2. Handling and Preventing Water Stains:

After stone installation, if the surface remains wet for a long time and never dries, it is commonly referred to as "water stain." Removing water stains is extremely challenging because their formation involves complex factors, including cement, acid rain, efflorescence, poor-quality sand, and underground pollution absorbed by the stone. These contaminants cause the stone to deteriorate, making it very difficult to clean. Therefore, the only method for dealing with water stains is prevention. To prevent water stains from forming, SCI multi-functional protective agent or SCI 15 stone protective agent can be used before construction. Applying protective treatment before construction effectively avoids the formation of water stains while achieving good stain-resistant effects.

3. Handling Efflorescence:

We often see white crystalline substances flowing out of the seams of stones, known as "efflorescence" (alkali leaching). Efflorescence can be divided into two types: "primary efflorescence" and "secondary efflorescence." Primary efflorescence occurs when cement mortar undergoes chemical changes, forming calcium hydroxide, which emerges from the seams of the stones or through the natural veins and capillaries of the stone, combining with carbon dioxide in the air to form white crystals. Secondary efflorescence mainly occurs when rainwater penetrates into the stone and comes into contact with cement mortar, causing chemical reactions and forming efflorescence. Both types can be removed using strong cleaning agents. Generally, post-event remediation and preventing regrowth of efflorescence require multiple approaches. First, prevent external contamination, i.e., rainwater penetration, by applying protective treatment to the stone surface and enhancing waterproofing in the joints. As long as outside moisture does not penetrate the stone and there is no internal moisture, efflorescence will not regenerate.

4. Handling Rust Stains:

Rust stains generally have two causes: one is from the iron content inherent in the stone. Typically, all stones contain some iron components. When rainwater comes into contact with the iron in the stone, rust stains slowly form, which are difficult to remove and require longer treatment times. Using rust stain removers, SCI strong cleaning agents can effectively eliminate rust stains without harming the stone. Avoid using industrial hydrogen peroxide or other corrosive strong acids, as this could backfire. The most effective method to prevent rust stains from recurring is to apply protective treatment immediately after rust removal to prevent water from penetrating the stone again and eliminating the possibility of rust regeneration. Another type of rust stain comes from external sources, such as residual steel sand during processing or iron tools during construction, metal fittings, etc. This type of rust stain is easier to remove, and the cleaning agents used are the same as those mentioned earlier. Generally, once stones develop rust stains, it is quite difficult to completely remove them. Therefore, applying protective treatment before installation is better than doing so after completion, as finished exterior walls can be quite high, making construction difficult. Additionally, during the installation process, the stones may come into contact with acid rain, so pre-installation protection is the best option, offering both rust prevention and stain resistance.

5. Handling Stains:

Stone surfaces can develop stains from various sources. Some come from environmental pollution, while others result from improper use of construction materials or poor workmanship during construction. Common scenarios include yellowing, discoloration, residual cement residue, quick-drying glue, adhesives, tape, wood pigments, etc. Another type of pollution is caused by human activity, such as tea stains, cola, coffee, soy sauce, etc. Different types of stains can be effectively removed using appropriate "Magic No. 2" cleaning agents or SCI stone-specific cleaning agents. The most effective way to prevent stone from being stained is to apply SCI stone protection treatment, especially before installation, which can effectively prevent pollution caused by improper construction or decoration or at least apply protection treatment upon completion. This can avoid the inconvenience of maintenance after the building is put into use.

6. Handling Wax Residue:

The correct method for stone maintenance is not to apply wax because wax is oily and easily penetrates into the stone's capillaries. Additionally, wax is sticky, attracting airborne dust, leading to hard-to-clean wax residue that damages the stone's aesthetics. Another type of hard wax seals the stone surface entirely after application, harming the stone's natural breathability. Moreover, this hard wax is difficult to remove after application, and in cases where the stone cannot breathe, any moisture will easily cause the stone to deteriorate. To remove wax residue, SCI stain and wax remover, "Magic No. 2" cleaning agent, or SCI super wax remover can be used. They effectively remove wax residue from the stone surface and capillaries without harming the stone's luster. After removing the wax, apply SCI multi-functional protective agent or SCI 15 stone protective agent for protection. Stones treated with these protective agents have water resistance, dirt resistance, rust prevention, oil stain resistance, anti-weathering, anti-aging, acid-base resistance, and prevent tea stains, cola, hand sweat, soy sauce, etc., achieving multiple benefits. Daily cleaning and maintenance only require water wiping, eliminating the need for any cleaning agents or waxing, extending the life of the stone. This method is much cheaper than traditional waxing and can handle both new and old stones. Currently, this protective treatment abroad is gradually replacing wax due to its lack of wax's drawbacks and multiple protective effects.

7. Handling Wear:

Granite is generally twice as hard as marble, and marble tends to lose its luster more easily. Regardless of whether it's granite or marble, once the luster is worn off, restoring it is quite challenging. Restoration typically requires re-polishing, so the best way to prevent wear and extend the life of the stone is to apply protective treatment first, then use SCI stone protector for surface treatment. In areas with high foot traffic, such as banks, restaurants, and building lobbies, regular surface protective treatment can effectively provide water resistance, dirt resistance, and wear resistance, enhancing the stone's brightness.

8. Handling Yellowing (Straw Rope Yellow):

Stones may suffer from unreasonable packaging, stacking, and transportation. If they encounter rainy days or come into contact with moisture, the straw ropes used for wrapping can easily stain the stones, causing straw rope yellow pollution. Additionally, after installation, moisture can cause chemical reactions within the stone, leading to yellowing. In such cases, using SCI multi-functional remover can effectively solve these problems. Subsequently, applying SCI protective series products can effectively prevent yellowing from reoccurring.
