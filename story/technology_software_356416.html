The main focus of the last adjustment was to address the issue of too much movement and repetitive actions, which left players feeling overwhelmed and unable to enjoy the game freely. Many users were spending less time online, which is not healthy for the game. Over the past few days, everyone should have gradually adapted to the new rhythm, making the game feel a bit more intense.

However, the adjustments in the last update left a big gap on weekends, which wasn't right. Many friends still want to play longer on weekends, so the next version will bring back some activities on weekends until new weekend activities are introduced to replace them.

Regarding some students' feedback that after adjusting the activities, leveling up became slower, there is indeed some impact, but it's not the goal. After adjusting the Friend and Town Attack events, we originally planned to double the experience points, but we felt that doubling the experience would make the rewards too significant, causing players to feel a great loss if they missed a day. Therefore, we didn't implement the doubling, but now I think we overthought it. In the next version, we will also double the experience for these two activities.

In terms of experience, we have never intended to withhold from players. In fact, Unrivaled has designed many time-consuming activities that offer large amounts of experience, including automatic storage for many high-experience activities. The goal is to ensure that players with more time can also have a good leveling experience, allowing them to do other enjoyable things in their spare time. Unrivaled has been in closed beta testing for over a month now, and already there are some level 60 players and a group of level 50 players. Reaching higher levels is a fast process; there’s no need to rush.

Now, talking about the next adjustments, the originally planned next step was to adjust the PVP system, including city battles, guilds, faction towers, leagues, and foreign armies, all of which have many flaws and issues that need addressing. However, their priority has decreased. Currently, what most troubles users is actually the non-payable resource issue. What are non-payable resources? These are resources that cannot be directly purchased with yuanbao, such as token equipment, gold gear, skills, civil officers, civil officer skills, real generals, accessories, etc. On these items that can be purchased with yuanbao, the development team and the game company cannot make any profit.

It’s been over a month, and everyone should now understand how powerful these non-payable resources are. One could say that without the assistance of these non-payable resources, even a full set of +15 gear is meaningless. As the game progresses, this situation becomes more evident because Unrivaled’s numerical system is built on the foundation of defense and attack. Without proper defense and attack, even high-level enhancements are useless.

The purpose of designing these numerous non-payable resources is to ensure that players can have a good gaming experience. Personally, I don’t mind a game that charges everywhere. In an era of rampant materialism, I hope that at least Unrivaled, while ensuring basic profits, can allow everyone to relive the original joy of gaming that we experienced in our pure gaming youth.

Unrivaled cannot remain as a completely free-to-play game; this is a trend that cannot be resisted. Therefore, Unrivaled will always maintain its payable items because a game needs to generate profit to sustain itself. Otherwise, the company would reduce investment, merge servers, and stop developing new content, leading to a lose-lose situation. Purely free games only exist in low-investment experimental works. Mature commercial products must have their profit sources. This is not only the reality of the gaming industry's development in the future but also the reality of any industry's development.

Now, talking about these non-payable resources, if using a standard set of city token gear, a person with +15 gear cannot possibly defeat two players with +9 gear. But in reality, a single +15 player often defeats four or five, sometimes even seven or eight other players. This is due to the fact that these players lack cooperation—since Unrivaled’s control skills cannot be immunized against through enhancement, theoretically, any three well-coordinated players can control a single player to the point where they cannot retaliate.

As developers, we cannot demand that all players have excellent cooperation awareness and operational skills. There is a huge issue here, one that the development team has consistently overlooked: a paying player gains non-payable resources far more efficiently than a non-paying player because they can buy gold, purchasing large quantities of non-payable resources, thus forming a capability suppression. A player with a real general, complete suffixes, three-attribute base gold gear, full set of accessories, and civil officers with full skills and growth, their power has far exceeded the initial design.

In obtaining non-payable resources, non-paying players and small-amount paying players are at a significant disadvantage. To catch up with paying players, they must heavily invest their gold into non-payable resources, thereby falling far behind paying players in enhancement. They might then feel that this game requires a lot of money, whereas in reality, the manufacturers make no profit from these items.

This is the crux of the problem, confusing both non-paying and paying players alike. They will find that the money spent on non-payable resources exceeds the money spent on enhancements. Thus, the game begins to feel like it burns money, not because people need to make money—we don’t earn money from it—but because these mechanisms are poorly designed. When a mechanism makes everyone feel it’s unreasonable, it must change. This is the key area we will focus on adjusting in the upcoming version.
