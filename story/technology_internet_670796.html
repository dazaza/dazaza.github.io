The purple "membrane structure" cocoon-shaped building. During the later stages of Expo preparation, which coincided with the global financial crisis, no country chose to withdraw. Among the many reasons for this, the most fundamental was that nothing could stop humanity from showcasing their pursuit of a better future. Countries around the world used the Expo Garden as a stage, presenting a variety of interpretations through unique designs of their national pavilions, reflecting on their thoughts about ideal cities and the future of humanity.

The Japan Pavilion, costing $133 million, is a purple "membrane structure" cocoon-shaped building. This "breathing architecture," which coexists harmoniously with the environment, uses environmental protection and recycling technologies, embodying the concept of "harmony between heaven and humanity." The UK Pavilion resembles a dandelion in bloom, with over 60,000 "tentacles" dancing in the wind. Each "tentacle" conceals a living seed, expressing the theme of "inheriting classics, casting the future." The bronze statue of "The Little Mermaid," a fairy tale character from Danish writer Andersen's pen, traveled thousands of miles to attend the Shanghai Expo, interpreting the theme of "happy life, fairy tale paradise." In the Russia Pavilion, which resembles an ancient Slavic round village, the seemingly unfinished tower tops seem to tell people: the development of an ideal city is endless...
