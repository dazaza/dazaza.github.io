Pato Wins 2009 Golden Boy Award, Serie A Duo Claims Top Two Spots

According to reports from Sina Sports on December 13th, Italian time, the Turin-based newspaper *Tuttosport* announced the winner of the 2009 "Golden Boy Award," with AC Milan forward Alexandre Pato emerging victorious. He scored 67 points more than second-place finisher Miralem Jovetić, securing this prestigious title.

The "Golden Boy Award" was established in 2003 by *Tuttosport*, with European media outlets voting for the best young player of the year. Nominees must be under 21 years old. Back in late October, *Tuttosport* released a shortlist of 40 candidates. Ultimately, Pato stood out due to widespread recognition from Europe's mainstream press. The inaugural award in 2003 went to Rafael van der Vaart, followed in subsequent years by Wayne Rooney, Lionel Messi, Cesc Fàbregas, Sergio Agüero, and last year's recipient, Manchester United midfielder Anderson. This marks Pato’s victory as the seventh edition of the Golden Boy Award.

Upon receiving the award, Pato expressed his delight: "I am truly very happy; it is an honor that fills me with pride. Last year, I also hoped to win this award, but I finished fifth. My compatriot Anderson won it then, leading Manchester United to claim the UEFA Champions League title. Next year, I will turn 21, so I won't be eligible for this award anymore. Still, I hope another fellow Brazilian can win it—Manchester United's Rafael."

"In the list of Golden Boy nominees, I saw many familiar faces from Serie A. I admire Jovetić, who scored twice against Liverpool in the Champions League. I also respect Balotelli," said Pato. "And Adjei, he's an extremely fast striker who made Brazil's youth team cry in the FIFA U-20 World Cup final. Moreover, I must mention that there are quite a few of my compatriots and friends among the Golden Boy nominees. First is Sidnei, the Benfica defender. We both joined Internacional's football academy at age 11 and were teammates for six years. Then there's Bebé, now on loan to Braga from Manchester United, also from Internacional's academy. And of course, Rafael, whom I believe will be the next Golden Boy. Regardless, winning this award is no easy feat."

With the Golden Boy Award secured, does Pato aspire to one day win the Ballon d'Or? This would signify his evolution from a rising star to a superstar. "Of course, I hope to win the Ballon d'Or someday," Pato responded without hesitation. "Messi won this prestigious accolade from *France Football* this year. I truly hope that the Golden Boy selected by *Tuttosport* becomes the best in the future. Winning the Ballon d'Or is the dream of every professional footballer."
