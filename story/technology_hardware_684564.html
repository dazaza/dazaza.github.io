The image shows: Little Su's "stocking" photo. The image shows: the exterior of the "Yihong Courtyard" for rent with multiple damages. A shocking post on a university forum about shared renting has surfaced.

"Those with remarkable appearance and temperament, scoring 8/10, can reside for free year-round. I will even cover water, electricity, and broadband fees and offer an additional 200 yuan monthly for room maintenance. Those with average looks have a monthly rent of 200 yuan." For those scoring below 5/10, "please don't bother coming..." Recently, a fourth-year student from a Wuhan university posted an advertisement seeking female tenants on a BBS.

This post titled "Small Nest for Rent" was published on the online forum of a university in Wuhan on the morning of February 14th. The poster referred to himself as "Yihong Gentleman". According to the post, the place available for female co-renters is a single room within the university campus, equipped with basic appliances like a water dispenser and air conditioner. "Yihong Gentleman" requires that the tenant must be a female undergraduate or graduate who has not previously known each other.

Yesterday, the reporter pretended to be a potential renter and went to apply. The male poster refers to himself as a modern Jia Baoyu. Yesterday at noon, the reporter made contact with "Yihong Gentleman" and agreed to meet at his school. "Yihong Gentleman," whose real name is Xiao Su (a pseudonym), is a fourth-year student at the school, short in stature, and fair-skinned. Under Xiao Su's guidance, the reporter saw the room for rent. Half of the room had bedding laid out, with light-colored wallpaper, purple incense, and a screen dividing the kitchen area, making it seem like entering a girl's room.

"You decide the rent based on the female tenant's appearance, so how much do you think I should pay?" the reporter asked. Xiao Su quickly waved his hand and said, "That's not something I should say."

The reporter then asked why he insisted on finding a female tenant and determined the rent by looks. Xiao Su explained, "I've always liked playing with girls and disliked being around boys. I enjoy reading 'Dream of the Red Chamber.' I consider myself a modern Jia Baoyu; I just feel ugly, so I want to find someone attractive to complement me."

Xiao Su took out a photo he had posted online before, taken during his third year of university. In the photo, Xiao Su is wearing thigh-high stockings and a rabbit outfit. He mentioned that he enjoys imitating female anime characters. When the reporter joked about his sexual orientation, Xiao Su laughed and claimed that he was a very manly guy with a beautiful girlfriend. He added that his girlfriend knew about his post looking for female renters.

Xiao Su stated that he has another dormitory at school, and this room is intended for rent, but it must be rented by a female. "I might come over sometimes, but if you're mentally strong enough, sharing the same room shouldn't be a problem."

After hearing this, the reporter said they needed more time to consider and left accordingly.

Netizens question his motives

Xiao Su's rental post sparked heated discussions among many netizens. Netizen "Guli Guli" replied: "I still don't understand. Is it the same room? Isn't this just a disguised way of looking for a girlfriend?"

Netizen "Fenfen Buping" said, "Renting a female roommate and determining the rent based on looks divides women into different classes. Isn't this openly discriminating against women with less appealing appearances?"

A fourth-year student from the same school posted that Xiao Su's behavior was merely to attract attention from others. Some female netizens also questioned his intentions.

Expert: Poster needs self-psychological guidance

Ye Xian, deputy dean of the School of Education at Hubei University and an expert in education, expressed disapproval of the practice of opposite-sex co-renting. He believes that there are safety risks involved in opposite-sex co-renting, as the moral quality of college students varies greatly, and there are many traps associated with such arrangements. Therefore, female college students must remain vigilant and protect themselves. Additionally, even when co-renting with acquaintances of the opposite sex, one must be cautious about possible safety issues.

Jia Hongwu, a senior psychological advisor at Wuhan Dacheng Psychological Counseling Center, believes that Xiao Su's behavior indicates flaws in his social interactions and obstacles in communicating with people of the same sex. Subconsciously believing that one dislikes men signifies a psychological deviation. Generally speaking, when such individuals need to interact deeply with their peers due to work requirements, they may have the potential for self-repair if they actively guide and correct themselves.
