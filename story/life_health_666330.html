PID is a relatively common type of gynecological disease, which has a significant impact on the normal life of patients. According to the gynecological experts of Xi'an Sanqin Hospital, simple PID does not affect pregnancy. However, PID can easily cause inflammation to spread to both fallopian tubes, leading to salpingitis, fallopian tube blockage, etc. Therefore, PID is a gynecological inflammation that can easily lead to infertility. Many women still cannot conceive after curing PID, which is likely due to salpingitis.

What are the harms of PID? The gynecological experts of Xi'an Sanqin Hospital point out that PID, due to its stubborn condition, often affects the uterus and fallopian tubes, thus posing significant harm. If left untreated for a long time, it may cause the following harms:

1. Infertility: The greatest harm of PID is causing infertility. When any part of the uterus, fallopian tubes, ovaries, or surrounding tissues in the pelvis, including the peritoneum, becomes inflamed, it can result in female infertility. When acute inflammation is not thoroughly treated and turns chronic, or when PID is not promptly treated and becomes chronic, it often leads to female infertility.

2. Ectopic Pregnancy: Chronic PID often causes bilateral salpingitis. Over time, it can lead to tubal adhesion and blockage, narrowing or closing of the tubal cavity, preventing the fertilized egg from implanting in the uterine cavity and resulting in ectopic pregnancy.

3. Impact on Marital Life: PID often recurs and lingers, causing anxiety, irritability, depression, and other negative emotions in patients, leading to secondary sexual aversion, affecting marital life.

4. Damage to Ovarian Function: Chronic inflammation can lead to pelvic congestion, excessive menstruation, ovarian dysfunction causing menstrual irregularities, and fallopian tube adhesion and blockage leading to infertility. Acute inflammation can cause diffuse peritonitis, sepsis, and even infectious shock, while chronic inflammation, due to prolonged treatment, recurrent episodes, affects women's normal work, life, and mental health.

5. Kidney Disease: Since pelvic connective tissue is connected to retroperitoneal connective tissue and can reach around the kidneys, if acute pelvic connective tissue inflammation is left untreated, the inflammation can not only spread to the fallopian tubes, pelvic peritoneum, and other tissues, causing pelvic abscesses, but also upward, leading to perinephric abscesses.
