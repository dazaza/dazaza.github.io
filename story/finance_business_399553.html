Baidu Entertainment News - The annual audition season is about to begin. Every year there are always some persistent "re-heated meat" who are addicted to auditions and "can't get out". Tonight at 22:00 on Qinghai TV's "Next Stop Happiness", they encountered such a "star mania patient" who spends 365 days of the year either in an audition or on the way to one.

Aldous Huxley said: We will be destroyed by what we are obsessed with. This year, Maun Shuai, 25 years old, seems to be "destroyed" by the auditions he has been obsessed with for five years. Without work, relying on family support, living in a basement in Beijing, running around nightclubs, occasionally entering a film set as an extra, in the five years since graduating from art school, Maun Shuai has participated in various auditions from "Super Boy" to "Star Avenue" to "Dream of Red Mansions". He has lost count of how many times he has participated.

The previously supportive mother Chang Fangyuan finally could no longer tolerate it. When Maun Shuai proposed needing 50,000 yuan for self-packaging, when she discovered that her son's girlfriend Wang Lingling had a new boyfriend, she hid Maun Shuai's ID card. The tension between mother and son escalated, leading to a major conflict.

It was then that Maun Shuai found out that his beloved Lingling couldn't endure him anymore and had a boy who was rich, caring, and had time to accompany her. But Lingling claimed that Maun Shuai changed his heart first, saying "he has already been supported by a wealthy woman." What happened? What special identity does this "wealthy woman" who gives money to Maun Shuai have?

Jay Chou, Jolin Tsai, Eason Chan, Stefanie Sun, a host of kings and queens of pop have gone from the stage to stardom. "Loving auditions" isn't wrong, but for many people who are madly rushing down the star path, "auditioning" can become addictive, and "being addicted to auditioning" is already a disease. Can Maun Shuai, facing the tests of family and love in tonight's 22:00 Qinghai TV "Next Stop Happiness: My 'Star' Son," cure this disease?
