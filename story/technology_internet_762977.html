Technology has become fashion.

by anonymous on 2013-07-29 11:58:10

Tesla and SpaceX, these two companies are increasingly gaining attention. The founder of both companies, Elon Musk, has been shaped into a new generation digital hero - when smartphones are no longer so exciting, electric cars and space rockets become the next tech totems, receiving admiration.

This year, another topic that maintained its heat was Google with its mysterious laboratory. Autonomous cars and Google Glass have people eagerly anticipating. In February this year, Google's stock price climbed to $806.85, and just a few days ago, the company's stock price hit a record high at $924, reflecting public sentiment.

The comic xkcd reflects the public's perception of Geeks and Nerds - in the eyes of outsiders, these two groups are one and the same: unkempt, socially inept, and behaving abnormally. These two words were originally derogatory terms; the former means "freak" or "deformity," and the latter refers to "bookworm." However, these two words have gradually lost their original negative connotations and have become more positive.

People who love learning English must not miss the classic American comedy "Friends." The main male character, Ross Geller, exhibits strong Nerd characteristics. As for "The Big Bang Theory," which has aired six seasons and inherited the mantle from "Friends," it is deeply ingrained in the hearts of the public and has even won an Emmy Award. Sheldon from the show, due to his quirky behavior, has gained immense popularity. However, just a few years ago, in VOA's slow-speed English textbook, Nerd was still defined as "weird person."

Nowadays, the public's attitude towards tech entrepreneurship has changed significantly, influenced by Jobs, Page & Brin, Mark Zuckerberg, and now Elon Musk - the tech industry is no longer just about Geeks and Nerds typing on computers all night. It is gradually evolving into a new fashion trend. The public's perception of technology is no longer one of awe and distance but rather one of understanding and appreciation.

Why does fashion exist? Why do trends emerge? Neither being too sophisticated nor too common defines fashion standards. For something to become popular, the primary condition is that it can be understood, followed by it meeting the psychological needs of people at the time, and having a refined taste. Whether it's a popular song or trendy clothing, it's the same. Technology is getting closer and closer to the masses.

In the past, literature was once fashionable in America. Due to the promotion of Protestantism, the United States, initially a British colony, allowed cities to use printing presses to print books, making reading no longer a privilege of cultural aristocrats. Daniel Boorstin wrote in "The Americans: The Colonial Experience," "Reading became a widespread trend. There were reading centers everywhere because there was no center. Everyone could directly understand the content of printed materials, and everyone could speak the same language. Reading is the inevitable product of this busy, mobile, open society."

As for literary giants from Britain, their status was comparable to that of sports stars and pop idols today. In 1842, Dickens visited the United States and received a warm welcome. His letters to friends reveal his excitement, "I cannot describe to you the welcome I received. Crowds chased after me, cheering, with lavish balls and parties, surrounded by various public figures. Probably no king or emperor on this earth has ever had such treatment... If I rode in a carriage, the crowd would surround it and escort me home; if I entered a theater, everyone would stand up, and the music would start all over again."

Then how does the status of today's tech heroes compare to that of national leaders? Let's take a look at the comparison between Google searches for Obama and Jobs: 6.86 billion vs. 8.21 billion.
