jpapendick@aberdeennews.com Sioux Falls arrives in Aberdeen on a high note for the girls' state hockey tournament. The young Flyers won the state girls' junior varsity hockey tournament last weekend in Yankton, finishing with a 19-1-0 record. "Girls who are 13 and 14 years old make up the majority of our team," said Sioux Falls head coach Brian Wrightsman. "Two years ago, we were the eighth seed coming into the varsity state tournament, last year we were the seventh seed and this year we are the sixth seed. It is good to see our girls are maturing, becoming more developed and getting better each year. But we are the underdog this weekend. We have a pretty good young team, but nobody is expecting anything from us." However, Sioux Falls has proven that it can be a dangerous team on the varsity level as well. "We lost 5-3 to league champion Brookings at Brookings this season," Wrightsman said. "We are a younger and smaller team, but we can move the puck," he added. "We have two strong lines that take a lot of shots, but that is OK. I always tell the girls whatever it takes to get the job done. If we have to take 30 shots, 40 shots or 50 shots, so be it." Wrightsman pointed to the recent 2-0 JV championship game win against Pierre when the Flyers faced Oahe goalie Tessa Singleton, "one of the best goalies in the state," the Sioux Falls coach said. "She made some great saves, and it took us 42 shots to get two by her. I just told the girls to keep shooting." While Sioux Falls has had a solid year offensively, its defense has broken down at times. Wrightsman said his team needs to get back on defense and stay aggressive. "We have a young and underdeveloped defense." Sioux Falls has two seniors on its 20-player squad, Maggie Drexler and Cassie Nack, and both share time in the net with eighth-grader Brittany Corcoran. Nack is coming off a great weekend as the goalie pitched back-to-back shutouts in the state JV tournament, saving all 21 shots that came her way. Offensively in that state tourney, Jennika Wrightsman had three goals and one assist in Sioux Falls’ two games. The seventh-grader is the daughter of the coach. In the championship game against Pierre, Sioux Falls got its goals from Maggie Drexler (assists from Mollie Drexler and Madison Elkins) and Wrightsman (Alex Bettmeng). Coach Wrightsman said the tourney’s top three seeds, Brookings, Rushmore and Mitchell, are the teams to beat. "Anyone of those teams can win. This is my third year as a coach in South Dakota, and those are the most evenly matched top-three teams I have ever seen coming into a state tournament. But if those teams are off their games and seeds four, five or six play a strong tournament, you could see one of those teams in the championship game as well."
