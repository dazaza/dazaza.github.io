Bus gearbox failure splashes and injures 4 passengers

In Panyu, a bus gearbox malfunction caused harm to passengers, including a 23-year-old female passenger who suffered severe head injuries and was in critical condition.

■ Report by Xin Kuai Daily reporter Chen Haisheng and intern Zhu Shuoran

At around 9 a.m. yesterday, on Shilian Road in Shijing Village, Panyu District, a bus traveling suddenly emitted three loud bangs from its rear engine area. Flying parts injured four passengers inside the bus. One white-collar woman suffered serious head injuries and underwent emergency craniotomy but remained in critical condition. The other three injured individuals sustained minor injuries. Preliminary investigations revealed that the involved bus had both its engine and gearbox placed at the rear. During operation, a screw from the gearbox fell off, rebounded against the road surface, generating significant force which led to the accident.

Mr. Shi, aged 60, was sitting on the left side near the rear door. Before he could react to the explosion sounds, he was struck in the left cheek and head by a part.

Three Explosions While Driving

The incident involved a bus traveling from Lotus Mountain to Shibridge with over 30 passengers onboard. Witness Mr. Gou stated that the bus was moving fast when it approached Shijing Archway. Suddenly, three loud "bangs" came from the rear of the bus, followed by smoke and some engine components falling onto the road. Mr. Gou said the bus driver immediately applied the brakes, but the vehicle slid for more than 20 meters before stopping and opening the doors.

"The people on the bus were in chaos, scrambling to get out. Several passengers sitting in the back rows were hit by flying parts," neighbors said. One female passenger was seriously injured and was carried off the bus by the driver and other passengers. "Her left eye and forehead were bleeding, and she soon lost consciousness." Everyone quickly dialed 120 for emergency medical assistance, and the ambulance transported the injured to a nearby hospital for treatment.

Four Passengers Injured and Hospitalized

At around 11 a.m., reporters arrived at the Shijing Town People's Hospital. According to the emergency department doctor, they treated four injured passengers. Diagnoses revealed one female passenger with severe injuries requiring surgery, while the other three male passengers had only minor injuries and were treated without further complications.

Mr. Shi, aged 60, was one of the injured. After boarding the bus, he sat on the left side near the rear door. Before he could react to the explosions, he was struck in the left cheek and head by a part. He was bandaged upon arrival at the hospital. According to his son, Mr. Shi, after the incident, the bus company covered the day’s treatment costs and would reimburse subsequent expenses based on invoices. Mr. Shi expressed hope that the bus company would fully cover his father's medical expenses and provide additional compensation for nutritional support.

Special Feature: Female White-Collar Worker Suffered Severe Head Injuries

It was learned that the injured female passenger surnamed Liang is 23 years old. At around noon, her family rushed to the hospital waiting anxiously outside the operating room. "She was fine this morning, but less than 20 minutes later, an accident happened," said Mr. Liang, her brother. During the incident, Ms. Liang was sitting in the last row, resulting in the most severe injuries. "Doctors said she experienced intracranial hypertension, and there was organ displacement requiring craniotomy to relieve pressure," Mr. Liang explained.

After the incident, personnel from Ms. Liang's company's HR department visited the hospital to inquire about her condition and offer condolences. Colleagues described Ms. Liang as having worked in fashion design for over a year and being well-liked by her peers. After the surgery concluded yesterday afternoon, Ms. Liang was transferred to the ICU for monitoring and treatment, but her condition remained unstable. "Doctors said she hasn't passed the danger period yet and has no spontaneous consciousness," Mr. Liang noted. It was reported that Ms. Liang's family had average financial conditions, but they temporarily weren't overly concerned about medical expenses, prioritizing saving her life first.

Cause: Gearbox Screw Detached, Rebounded Off Road Surface Causing Impact

Yesterday, Mr. Xu, the station manager of Panyu District Traffic Management Station, introduced that the involved bus was operated by Panyu Jinxin Automobile Transportation Co., Ltd. After the incident, they initiated an investigation, confirming it as a mechanical failure rather than the "explosion" claimed by citizens.

Mr. Xu stated that preliminary investigations found that the involved bus had both its engine and gearbox located at the rear. During travel, a screw from the gearbox detached, rebounded against the road surface, generating immense force. "There was originally a cover plate inside the bus that was blown open by the force, causing parts to fly out and injure passengers in the back rows," Mr. Xu explained. It is suspected that the flying gearbox cover struck Ms. Liang's head, leading to her severe injuries. Additionally, the sound of parts colliding with the vehicle's chassis and smoke emanating from the rear of the bus misled citizens into believing an explosion had occurred.
