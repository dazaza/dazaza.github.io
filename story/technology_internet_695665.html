Family who watched TV day and night disturbing neighbors has TV confiscated by government

by o5vxvt74625 on 2012-03-07 16:43:33

The Cregan couple is upset about the government confiscating their TV

The Craziest Family

According to British media reports on December 12, Paul Cregan and Amanda Cregan particularly enjoy watching television. They watch cartoons with their children whenever they have free time. A few years ago, in order to get better TV quality, the couple bought a luxurious 62-inch plasma TV. However, because the Cregan family watched TV excessively without regard for the time of day or night, and turned up the volume very high, their neighbors complained multiple times to the local government. Eventually, the local government confiscated the Cregan family's TV. Nevertheless, none of this dampened the Cregan family's enthusiasm for watching TV as they borrowed a TV temporarily to continue watching.

Saving Every Penny to Buy a TV

Paul, aged 27, and Amanda, aged 30, live in Rochdale, Greater Manchester, UK, with their three children. The couple often watches cartoons with their kids. A few years ago, to allow their children to better enjoy the fun of animated shows, Paul, who was unemployed at home, discussed with his wife, and they saved up £2500 by cutting back on expenses to buy a 62-inch plasma TV.

Waking Neighbors at 4 AM

Every day at 4 AM, the Cregan family's TV would turn on because the children wanted to watch the cartoons broadcast before school. Not only that, but when the children came home from school in the evening, they would watch cartoons until late at night. The strong electronic music, transmitted through the stereo sound system, reached the neighbors' homes, making their walls seem like they were shaking.

Government Confiscates TV

The neighbors, unable to endure it any longer, complained to the government multiple times. In 2006, the government required the Cregan couple to control the noise from their TV. However, things didn't last long. Last year, the problem reappeared. Environmental health officers found that the sound level from the Cregan family's TV exceeded the government's control standards eight consecutive times. The neighbors strongly urged the local government to take firm action. Finally, after obtaining court permission, government staff forcibly entered the Cregan residence and carried away their luxurious large TV. They also demanded that the Cregan couple pay £397 within 28 days to redeem it.

Borrowing a TV Temporarily

However, the situation worsened when, just the day after the government confiscated their TV, the Cregan family temporarily borrowed a 50-inch flat-screen TV. Despite one of their children experiencing hearing loss due to the loud TV volume, they clearly showed no regret. Mrs. Cregan said, "After getting the TV back, we will watch it the same way as before; otherwise, I can hardly hear anything."
