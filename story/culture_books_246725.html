Under a twist of fate, Si Xiaoyin, a female college student of the Wa ethnic group in Western Yunnan, met Zhan Xiaofei, the Deputy Secretary of the Municipal Committee of Liaoan City and Secretary of the Political and Legal Affairs Commission, and recognized him as her godfather. After graduation, Si Xiaoyin came to Liaoan and met the young playwright Qiu Weihan at a dinner party arranged by Zhan Xiaofei. Moreover, another identity of Qiu Weihan is the accomplished disciple of Gu Mingfan, the Secretary of the Liaoan Municipal Committee.

Qiu Weihan indeed fell in love with Si Xiaoyin at first sight.

With the promotion of Secretary of the Municipal Committee Gu Mingfan, Zhan Xiaofei and Lin Zhixia, the Standing Deputy Secretary of the Municipal Committee, launched an intense competition for the vacancy. Seeing that Qiu Weihan had taken the bait (Si Xiaoyin) but never blew the wind according to his intention in front of Gu Mingfan, Zhan Xiaofei designed to push Si Xiaoyin to Lin Zhixia, who was lustful. Then one day after a dinner party, Si Xiaoyin was deceived into the room of drunk Lin Zhixia...

Part One: Dinner Party

Zhan Xiaofei is a man who loves liveliness, and a dinner party is a lively place. Therefore, attending dinner parties is something he enjoys. In his own words, half of his life's career has basically been spent at dinner parties. He also has a set of theories about dinner parties, which I have heard him expound on more than once at different dinner parties. According to him, the term "dinner party" did not originate today but from the Song Dynasty. "Party" originally comes from chess terminology, extended to mean "gambling, gathering, trap." The combination of "meal" and "party" into one word is a great contribution of ancient literati to the Chinese language and culture. ——Because there are simply too many traps at dinner parties.

Part Two: Dinner Party

I got up early Saturday morning. For this outing, I specially took my "Sara Picasso" to the 4S store yesterday afternoon for a thorough check-up, filled it up with fuel, and went to the supermarket to prepare all the food and supplies we would need. Although Miss Ge said there was no need for me to spend money, when traveling with a beautiful woman, one should always appear generous. Experience tells me that good steel should be used on the cutting edge, and spending money on women often brings unexpected returns. Haven't some people said that men earn money just for women! Being overly calculating sometimes saves a few coins, but can only make you watch helplessly as the beauty leaves you with disdain, such embarrassing things must not be done.

Part Three: Dinner Party

I secretly rejoiced in my heart but showed no emotion on the surface, saying, "Then there's no need, I don't have any urgent matters, waiting for him a bit is fine!"

"That Uncle Seven, come with me to the backyard and sit for a while, let's have a cup of tea!" I followed Si Xiaoyin along the path paved with pebbles towards He Dongpu's small quadrangle courtyard. Walking shoulder to shoulder, I realized how tall she really was; I was barely more than half a head taller than her. I quietly glanced at her sideways and felt that she seemed to have hardly applied any makeup, appearing completely natural, even the faint fuzz on her face could be seen through the sunlight, making one instantly think of the poem "Lotus emerging from clear water."

Part Four: Dinner Party

I bent down to observe this bright pink chrysanthemum. It was clearly of the peony type, with outer petals spread flat and inner petals curled inward, glossy veins, and charming appearance. What made it unique was that its petals slightly tilted to one side, resembling a shy girl dressed modestly. I became interested in the name of this flower and asked He Dongpu, "Big Brother San, its name 'Taizhen Smiling' is related to Consort Yang, right?"

"You're absolutely correct. When Yang Yuhuan was ordained as a Taoist nun, she lived in Taizhen Palace. Later, she was favored by Emperor Xuanzong of Tang and was given the name Taizhen. Because this flower has a peculiar beauty and refuses to show its face easily, it thus received this name." "Wow, so romantic!" I casually praised, and when I looked up, I saw Si Xiaoyin standing behind He Dongpu.
