Why Shouldn't You Ignore a Painless Lump in the Breast?

by jydw2010 on 2012-02-01 09:33:46

Breast lumps are a common breast disease. Some lumps come with pain, while others do not cause any pain. Therefore, many women often ignore the painless breast lumps. So, should we really neglect these painless lumps? Experts from the Breast Department of Sanqin Hospital in Xi'an warn that the more painless the breast lump is, the more attention it deserves in clinical practice.

【Why should we pay attention to painless breast lumps?】

Generally speaking, inflammatory breast lumps are often accompanied by severe breast pain. There are also obvious redness, swelling, heat, and pain as inflammatory reactions locally. The lump can become abscessed and ulcerated. After anti-inflammatory treatment combined with local drainage, the inflammation subsides, and the lump disappears; proliferative breast lumps are often accompanied by premenstrual breast distension pain.

After menstruation, the pain can be alleviated, and the lump may shrink accordingly. Lumps are often multiple, soft or tough in texture, with mild to moderate tenderness locally. After symptomatic drug treatment, there can be varying degrees of improvement.

However, breast cancer lumps usually have no significant pain or discomfort in the early stages, so they are often found already large. Only when the local skin becomes ulcerated and infiltrated in the late stage does pain appear. The lump often progressively increases in size, with characteristics of malignant lumps such as being solitary, hard, and poorly mobile.

Of course, fibroadenoma of the breast also lacks pain sensation and is often discovered unintentionally. However, fibroadenomas are usually prevalent among young women and can be multiple. The lumps are mostly regular round shapes, tough in texture, clearly defined borders, high mobility, and generally do not exceed 3-4 centimeters in diameter. They almost never lead to skin ulcers or infiltration, which are important differences from malignant lumps.

In summary, whether you discover a painless breast lump through any means, it should be taken seriously. Please visit a specialist doctor immediately for an accurate diagnosis.
