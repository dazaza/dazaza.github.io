Venice Case Pavilion officially opens to showcase the protection and utilization of historical heritage

by fksief360 on 2010-05-26 22:14:32

Italian Consul General in Shanghai Massimo Roscigno said in his speech that more people will get to know about the beautiful city of Venice through such a platform as the Expo, and will also allow more people in the economic sector to understand the importance of Venice's economic industry. Showcasing the protection and utilization of historical heritage, the Venice Best Practice Area officially opened. May 26th news: This evening, the Venice Best Practice Area, themed around "the best practices for the protection and utilization of Venice's historical heritage," officially opened. Italian Consul General in Shanghai Massimo Roscigno, Mayor of Venice Giorgio Orsoni, Italian General Commissioner for the Shanghai Expo Beniamino Quintieri, and relevant responsible persons from the Shanghai Expo Bureau Best Practice Area Department attended the ceremony. The Venice case is located within the "Case Joint Hall One" in the E zone, covering an area of about 500 square meters. In the eyes of Venice's Mayor Giorgio Orsoni, this exhibition area will show everyone how Venice creates a livable environment within limited space, presenting a brand-new Venice to the Chinese and people all over the world. Entering the hall, exquisite ponds, giant maps of Venice, and picturesque videos make visitors feel as if they are standing in this famous water city. Through five touch screens and wall monitors, the exhibition hall will demonstrate successful cases of Venice in the "protection and utilization of historical heritage," showcasing Venice's unique characteristics in balancing urban life with a fragile environment.
