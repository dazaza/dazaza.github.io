Career re- enhance the performance of high-voltage LED launch

by ledlamp8 on 2012-02-27 20:30:23

Cree has launched a new high-voltage XLamp® XT-E HVW and XM-L HVW LED, offering unprecedented performance and the lowest system cost.

On October 31, 2011, Beijing - Cree, a market leader in LED lighting, announced the launch of the high-pressure XT-E XLamp® HVW and XM-L HVW LED. These new LEDs enable the use of more efficient, smaller drivers for energy-saving lamps, thereby reducing the cost of compact lighting applications such as candle lamps and alternative light source lamps.

Ken Chakravarti, CTO of Ledzworld, said: "More efficient, better-performing, lower-cost, and smaller LED lamps, such as the B10-type replacement light source, can now be achieved. In the past, LEDs could not provide the desired system-level performance within the customer's package size."

Peter Vaughan, director of applications engineering at Power Integrations, pointed out that using these LEDs can effectively reduce driver losses and heat dissipation, improving the overall system luminous efficiency by several percentage points. To take full advantage of all the benefits of such high-performance, high-voltage LEDs, achievements that were not possible in the past, we will continue to develop appropriate LED products for different applications, thus accelerating the popularity of energy-efficient LED lighting products.

Under conditions of >6W and 85°C, cool white (6000K) and warm white (3000K) XLamp® XM-L HVW LEDs deliver a luminous flux of up to 600 lm and 462 lm respectively; cool white (6000K) and warm white (3000K) XLamp® XT-E HVW LEDs deliver a luminous flux of up to 300 lm and 228 lm respectively. Under specified conditions, the typical voltage values for both LEDs are 46V.
