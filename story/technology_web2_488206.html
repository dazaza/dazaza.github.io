A cat is aware of a mouse.

by haitaohnf on 2011-09-12 12:45:04

There was a cat who had become aware of a mouse, and began to talk to it at great length about how much she loved mice, and wanted to be friends. Eventually, the mouse agreed to live with the cat. "We need to prepare something for the winter, otherwise we will starve," said the cat. "As for you, my little mouse, don't go anywhere. I'm afraid you might get caught in a mousetrap." The mouse accepted the cat's good advice, and they bought a jar of lard. But neither of them knew where to put the lard. They thought and thought, and finally the cat said: "I think this lard should be placed in the church, because no one would dare to steal from the church. We'll hide the lard under the altar, and only use it in emergencies." Thus, the jar of lard was placed in a safe place.

But soon, the cat started craving the lard, and said to the mouse: "Little mouse, I want to tell you something. My cousin just had a baby, and asked me to be the godmother. The baby is all silver white, with some brown spots. I have to hold it during the baptism, so I need to go out today. Can you stay home alone?" "Sure, sure," said the mouse, "just go ahead. If there's any good food, remember me. I'd like to taste some of the red wine used during the baptism." Of course, none of this was true, because the cat didn't have a cousin, nor was she invited to be a godmother. She went straight to the church, sneaked up to the lard jar, and started licking. She licked off the top layer of the lard. Then, she took a walk on the rooftops of the city, hoping for other luck; then she lay down to bask in the sun. Every time she thought of the lard jar, she couldn't help but lick her lips. She didn't return home until nightfall. "Ah, you're finally back," said the mouse, "you must have had a great day?" "Everything went smoothly," replied the cat. "What name did you give the child?" "No more top!" said the cat indifferently. "No more top!" cried the mouse, "that's a strange name indeed. Do you often give names like that?" "What's wrong with that?" said the cat, "it's no worse than calling your godchildren 'Steal Bread Crumbs'!"

Not long after, the cat wanted to eat the lard again. She said to the mouse: "You have to do me a favor and stay home alone again. Someone else has asked me to be a godmother, and this child has a white circle around its neck. I can't refuse." The kind-hearted mouse agreed. The cat slipped into the church from behind the city wall and ate half the jar of lard. "Nothing is better than eating it yourself," she said, feeling quite satisfied with her day's harvest. When she got home, the mouse asked: "What name did you give this child?" "Eaten half," answered the cat. "Eaten half! What are you talking about? I've never heard such a name in my life. I bet there wouldn't be such a name even in the calendar!"

Soon, the cat's mouth started watering again, wanting to lick the lard once more.
