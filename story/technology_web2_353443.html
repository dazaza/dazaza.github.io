I have been lurking in the community for a long time, but today I couldn't resist posting. The reason is that I just came back from beautiful Sanya, with so many feelings and beautiful pictures. If you're planning to go, make sure to read carefully!

Shanghai budget hotel; In 2010, I finally saw the real sea! Why do I say that? Because although I've seen the sea in Shanghai, it's not very beautiful, no different from the Yangtze River or the Yellow River. The beach environment is terrible. Although I've also seen the sea in Shenzhen, it was only from afar at the top of the Window of the World, so it doesn't count. Therefore, I was extremely excited before going to Sanya this time!

After booking the round-trip tickets online, along with the apartment and hotel, my companion and I set off! Here, I can tell everyone that the most economical season to go to Sanya should be March, April, and November. Why? As everyone knows, the Spring Festival is the peak season for Sanya. Immediately after the Spring Festival, March becomes the off-season. At this time, most cities are still winter, while November follows the same logic. The National Day holiday is the second busiest season after the Spring Festival, and right after the peak season comes the small off-season in November. Generally, during these months, you can get tickets for around 30% off or even lower. But remember, if you can determine your travel dates, make sure to book early! I once missed out by an hour and the discount went up, which was really frustrating!

Alright, now let's talk about hotels. There are many great hotels in Sanya, but they come at a price. I'm not a rich woman, so I opted for an apartment plus hotel. There are many family-style apartments available for reservation on Taobao, which are also very comfortable! Generally, they range from 280-400 yuan per night, with both single rooms and suites available. This time, I stayed in a family apartment for two days and then in a hotel for the next two days. Also, here's a tip: if you're brave enough and want to save money, you can find accommodation after arriving in Sanya. Ha ha, there's a small town near the Sanya airport called Mango Village or Apple Village. The local houses are quite clean, costing only 40 yuan per night, absolutely true!

No more chatting, let's get straight to the pictures. When the temperature in Shanghai was only eight degrees and we were still wearing thick coats, we boarded the plane to Sanya, which would take three hours. It was the longest flight I had ever taken, and it was truly tough. The outside light was too glaring, so I pulled down the window shade to rest for a while. After about two and a half hours, the announcement said we would land at Sanya Phoenix Airport in half an hour. Everyone on the plane perked up. I immediately opened the window, wow! The sky was so blue, and I took a few photos as evidence. Not bad, right?

This is the blue sky of Sanya I saw from the plane, such a beautiful color, never seen in Shanghai! The plane was about to land soon. Before landing, I managed to take another photo. By this time, the plane was already very close to the ground. The deep blue in the distance was the sea, and the light blue was the sky. My eyes were relaxed, and the scenery was very pleasant.

As soon as we got off the plane, wow, it was so hot, 32 degrees. All the tourists on the plane were from cold Shanghai, dressed in overcoats and down jackets. We started to undress regardless of our appearance while walking, ha ha. Looking at the distant green mountains and coconut trees, I deeply breathed in. What a beautiful coastal city!

Then we looked for bus No. 8. Let me tell you, taking taxis in suburban areas of Sanya are not metered, meaning whatever the driver says goes. Moreover, Sanya is a small city, so buses are sufficient if you know how to find them. Don't pay attention to the drivers pulling customers outside the airport, just walk straight ahead. The Sanya airport is small, and it takes only two minutes to walk out. Then you can see bus No. 8. Many places in Sanya can be reached by this bus for only two yuan. How economical! The key is saving money and being safe. The Lanhai Garden where I stayed is about half an hour away from the airport by bus No. 8. The staff of the apartment picked us up at the station, good service indeed. Once we arrived at the room, the first thing I did was to change into summer clothes immediately. The apartment was only five minutes' walk from the beach. From the balcony, you could see the front-line sea view, which felt great!

We could have breakfast here in the morning, how romantic! Below is the view from the apartment balcony, a front-line sea view. This picture was taken with a card camera, so the effect is not very good, but you can still see it clearly! There is nothing blocking the view ahead!
