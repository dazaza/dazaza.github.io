Experts from Guangzhou Chang'an Hospital on Infertility: Inside a woman's body, an egg generally survives for one to two days. Within 24 hours after ovulation, it is the best time for fertilization.

Sperm can survive in the female reproductive tract for about 24 to 72 hours, but the optimal time for fertilization only lasts from 24 to 36 hours post-ovulation. For women with regular menstrual cycles, calculating when the next period will come allows you to determine the day of your next menstrual cycle and subtract 14 days; that day is the ovulation day.

For example, if a woman has a 28-day menstrual cycle, her ovulation day should be around the 14th day of her menstrual cycle. This period is considered dangerous because it is easy to get pregnant. If the woman’s menstrual cycle is 40 days, then her ovulation day should be around the 26th day of her menstrual cycle. The four days before and five days after the ovulation day are the danger period (ovulation period), during which pregnancy is highly likely. Experts from Guangzhou Chang'an Hospital on Infertility indicate that conception is a complex physiological process that requires the following conditions:

1. Ovaries release normal eggs.

2. Semen is normal and contains normal sperm.

3. Fertilized eggs are successfully transported into the uterine cavity.

4. The uterine lining is adequately prepared for the implantation of the fertilized egg.

5. Eggs and sperm meet and combine into a fertilized egg inside the fallopian tube.

If any of these steps go wrong, both male and female infertility tests may affect fertility. It is recommended that women who wish to conceive undergo pre-pregnancy examinations at a hospital to ensure they have a healthy body ready for successful conception.
