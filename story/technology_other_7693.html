The fraudulent process of the fake company National Life Insurance Co., Ltd. (short for National Shares). Those who are recruiting and job hunting should take a look.

by chang9260 on 2006-10-20 09:50:16

Yesterday, I received an interview invitation from National Shares. The caller was a woman who said in the phone: "Your qualifications are excellent, and we hope you can attend our company's interview at 3 PM tomorrow afternoon, at No. 3 Suzhou Street, Haidian District, Dahan Technology Building, 5th floor, find Miss Hu Yanli, telephone 86198007" etc.

But I didn't send my resume to this company. The next day, I went to Daheng according to the specified time. After going up to the 5th floor, I saw PICC Life Insurance, so I felt something was wrong. However, since I've come, I wanted to see what's going on. After entering, I was required to register at the front desk, leaving my phone number and name, etc. There were many people for the interview, and I believe they were all deceived. After asking clearly which room Miss Hu Yanli was in, I entered. The scene inside was even more exaggerated. The room was not big, with many desks and chairs. The walls were full of PICC Life Insurance information. There were about 50-60 people, half of them were interview supervisors, and the other half were those who were deceived. Now I'm very sure that I've been deceived. Because while I was waiting for Miss Hu Yanli, I chatted with a person beside me, she also didn't know what position she was applying for when she came.

After waiting for about 5 minutes, a supervisor came over and said he temporarily switched shifts with Miss Hu Yanli, and he would interview me. Originally, I still wanted to chat with Miss Hu, but unexpectedly it turned into a man, so the speed of being interviewed accelerated. He asked me what work I did before? Why didn't I stay in the original company? What industry do you like? Why do you like it? What is your current income level? Ideal salary level? Work plan? ..., I casually thought of some answers to deal with his questions. Then it's my turn to ask him, what positions are you recruiting? I haven't sent my resume to the company, how did you get my information? Salary待遇? Why recruit so many people? etc. It says recruitment, but it actually seems like market research. Afterwards, he started recommending sales and life insurance to me, comforting me by saying that since I've come, I should understand our insurance.......

It made me waste 3-4 hours and more than 10 yuan. I just don't understand why insurance companies don't do things properly. What benefits does such things bring to their company? It can only increase others' hatred towards it.
