Two Sessions Focus on E-commerce, Promoting the Benign Development of Employment Situation

by now on 2009-03-20 10:41:52

Under the impact of the international financial crisis, China’s employment situation is facing unprecedented pressure. During the national two sessions, many representatives have been brainstorming for solutions, and e-commerce has become everyone's first choice. Regarding this, CPPCC member Xie Chaohua pointed out that from the central government to local governments at all levels are paying attention to the employment issue, and more entrepreneurial space should be created for the unemployed. "Promoting employment through entrepreneurship" is an effective method. The financial crisis has led to a decrease in purchasing power, with most consumers trying to find cheaper goods as substitutes, or even spending more time searching for products with higher cost-performance ratios. E-commerce provides them with such channels.

According to relevant statistical data, the total online transaction volume in 2008 approached 270 billion yuan, exceeding the expected 200 billion yuan transaction scale by more than 30%. In the next one or two years, China's e-commerce transaction volume will continue to grow at a rate of about 20%. The development of e-commerce not only became a lifeline for many small and medium-sized enterprises facing the financial crisis but also opened another window for the unemployed - e-commerce provided society with a large number of "invisible positions." As a result, a large number of unemployed people do not need to crowd into "visible positions" in enterprises and institutions; intermediary industries like sales and "invisible positions" can also provide them with a new and broad stage.

Looking around, there are no shortage of e-commerce service providers domestically. However, in the face of increasingly fierce competition, what customers need more are integrated services combining hardware and software. The close cooperation between IDC and e-commerce further meets customer needs while promoting the continuous development of e-commerce, providing more "invisible positions," and alleviating current employment pressure to a certain extent.

According to relevant persons in charge of Times Interconnect, from the current development status, China's IDC industry is in the transition period from the first-generation data center to the second-generation data center, centered on value-added services and e-commerce, gradually integrating value-added services with basic hosting services. But despite this, as early as 2007, companies in China's IDC industry with 500 servers already accounted for 48% of the total number of companies, among which 5% had more than 5000 servers. IDC has become the backbone of the Internet.

Therefore, in the face of the increasingly close ties between the IDC industry and e-commerce, the first problem to be solved is choosing a good service provider to provide large-scale, high-quality, safe, and reliable professional server hosting, space leasing, and other businesses for Internet content providers (ICP), enterprises, media, and various websites. It is understood that as a well-known domestic hosting service provider, Times Interconnect (www.todayidc.com) owns a high-quality Guangdong Telecom Internet Data Center. With nine years of industry experience, it has not only entered the era of high server stability in advance but also won the favor of a wide range of users with its excellent service. Currently, it has launched the E Eagle Server 3180 and S Eagle Server 6721 with annual payment and ownership transfer. The E Eagle Server 3180 economic business server has expanded its memory from 1G to 2G, enhancing performance without price increase. The S Eagle Server 6721 server adds Hong Kong data centers for user selection, offering fast and stable advantages, solving the interconnection problems between domestic telecom and network communications, and further facilitating overseas business.

In the face of urgent employment pressure relief, we need to make full use of the rapidly developing e-commerce to open up more employment channels. At the same time, leveraging the momentum of the IDC industry to achieve twice the result with half the effort. During the two sessions, some members suggested that in the 4 trillion yuan investment, special funds could be allocated to support e-commerce enterprises with innovative models and development potential, thus better solving the "employment problem" and minimizing the impact of the financial crisis on us.
