Amazon is recruiting aggressively and will expand its private label business on a large scale.

by anonymous on 2013-11-21 14:04:03

Amazon, which operates on the principle of low profit but high sales volume, has businesses that cover everything from cloud computing, tablet hardware to book publishing. At present, Amazon also plans to expand greatly in a new area - self-brand daily commodities. In the future, global consumers will be able to buy more and more toilet paper, vegetables, health products, etc. with the "Amazon" brand.

Self-brand commodities have already become a profitable way for many supermarkets, and Amazon has also tried it out earlier, such as launching self-brand batteries, computer keyboards, bedding supplies, etc. However, Amazon recently released job advertisements, showing plans to significantly expand its self-brand program in the daily necessities sector.

Amazon has posted several job vacancies, including one for a financial analyst responsible for the "launch of consumer goods under its own brand", and another position is a "senior product manager responsible for consumer's own brands".

Amazon said that the purpose of recruiting these positions is to launch high-quality daily consumer goods under the Amazon brand for global consumers.

In Amazon's product categories, consumer daily necessities mainly include fruits and vegetables, health products, personal care, maternal and infant products, etc., and Amazon's Fresh service is also included in the consumer goods category.

It is currently unclear whether Amazon will launch self-brands for specific products in certain categories (such as baby diapers) or simultaneously launch self-brands for numerous products in all categories.

Generally speaking, in the retail industry, through self-branding, retailers can directly order goods from manufacturers, reducing the links of middle wholesalers and agents, thus obtaining higher profits. Meanwhile, they can also lower the final retail price, which aligns with Jeff Bezos' business philosophy of low profit but high sales volume.

Many years ago, Amazon once tried self-branding, such as launching a "Pinzon" brand involving bed sheets and towels. Another product brand is "AmazonBasics", including batteries, keyboards, USB cables, etc.

However, according to reports, Amazon has never made big-budget advertising campaigns for its self-brand products and businesses. Additionally, in earnings analysts meetings, self-brand product businesses are only mentioned in secondary positions, generally not brought up.

Analyst Ben Schachter of Macquarie Capital said that when Amazon launched its small appliance brand, some people thought that traditional product brands like "Monster" would be overwhelmed, but this did not happen.

Many large American retailers have already implemented self-branding, but the promotion efforts vary. For example, Target once launched a baby diaper called "up＆up" and a fresh food brand called "Archer Farms".
