To a hunter who left the game for real life

by lk0gamec on 2009-07-08 09:35:35

Since stepping into the Warcraft, I have experienced curiosity in Zone 1 and testing in Zone 3. When Zone 4 opened, I finally settled down there. For the sake of my friends' choices, especially the choice of MM, I eventually gave up on my favorite Tauren Warrior and chose a very ordinary Human Warrior instead. Playing Warcraft is one of my greatest pleasures in my spare time from work.

I got to know this Hunter when he was level 46. At that time, I had already established my own guild and often organized dungeon runs as the initiator. With a Priest, Paladin, Rogue, and Mage by my side, I rarely grouped with anyone outside the guild, let alone go into a dungeon with a Hunter.

That day seemed to be a weekend, around 3 PM, I was going to Rujenor with a few friends for the Stonecutter that I longed for at that time. Just as we were about to enter the instance, the rogue suddenly disconnected, leaving us waiting idly at the entrance. Twenty minutes passed, and he still hadn't come online.

A level 48 Hunter walked past us, and after a while, he came back and stood silently beside us. My friends were already getting impatient and started complaining. This Hunter suddenly whispered me: "Are you guys going into a dungeon?"

"Yes."

"Why haven't you gone in yet? Are you waiting for someone?"

"A friend disconnected, and he hasn't come back online for over 20 minutes."

After waiting a few more minutes, the Hunter suddenly whispered me again: "If you're short of one person, can you group me?"

To be honest, leading my friends from Deadmines all the way to Rujenor, I haven't grouped with a Hunter before. I asked my friends in the group, they said it's up to you. I casually grouped the Hunter in and shouted in the team: Let's go!

The Hunter was very quiet, just closely following us. Whatever we fought, his pet would immediately rush up to bite, and he would keep shooting from behind. I noticed that his pet was a wolf named "Take you out for a breeze."

Although we grouped with a stranger, our habit of looting things didn't change. Whoever wanted something would roll, and if no one wanted it, they would pass (there was no such thing as greed back then). As we were fighting, I realized that the Hunter never rolled for anything.

"You don't want anything?" I whispered him.

"Being able to come in and see is already so exciting!!!"
