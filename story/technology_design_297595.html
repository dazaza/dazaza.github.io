And they say the little grass can repay the favor of the sun in spring.

by bjseeprof on 2009-12-07 11:25:02

Last Friday night at 3:30, after singing, I returned home and was full of smoke and alcohol smell. While taking a shower, I heard my wife shouting that our son had a fever. It's normal for children to have a slightly higher temperature, but I didn't expect that it would be 39 degrees when measured. We immediately got dressed and went to the hospital. Because the foreign doctors at和睦家were not very good several times, we asked which doctor it was on the way. After confirming, we went directly there. Our son is most afraid of this place. Every time he goes there, it's the same room with the logo wall. I think he must have memories of it, but that night, his behavior was surprisingly good. He cooperated well with the thermometer, blood pressure measurement, and stethoscope examination. He was just held in my arms, with his head against mine, hot. I feel sorry for this little guy. Since he started eating at nine months old, he has been eating with us. His strong body actually got so sick. All I could think of was that he shouldn't get burned, otherwise, all the hard work would be wasted, ha! After the medicine was prescribed, we returned home to take the medicine, and the sky was already bright. On Saturday, the spirit of the feverish child was surprisingly good, constantly walking around, playing with one thing and then another. But the temperature remained high, and we used everything available like bathing and alcohol. However, there was no effect. He began vomiting in the evening, and we were worried that he might suffocate himself, so we took turns on duty overnight. The situation was similar on Sunday, except that he started coughing at night, and we continued to take turns watching over him. My mother was the most attentive, constantly wiping his body with alcohol. Finally, on Monday afternoon, his temperature came down. However, he started coughing non-stop at night, and we took turns on duty again. Watching the child reminded me of the scene from "Saint Seiya" where the father holds his son in a heavy snowstorm, using a large cloak to shield him while braving the wind, all because the child had a fever. That scene is something I've always wanted to paint myself, but I don't know how to start since I can't draw. When I was small, my own mother carried me on her back to see a doctor and get medicine. Who says that the love of parents can be repaid? A friend said that raising a child is always impossible to calculate costs and benefits, because no matter what, you are constantly giving until death, still thinking about what you can leave for him. Life is always like this. Even if there are unpleasantness and disharmony with family members, it's like the annual rings carved by time. No matter how much unpleasantness or unhappiness there is, the hearts of children and parents deeply love each other forever!
