It looks very similar to a friend named "Wang Zhicheng" from the past.

by lieencq09 on 2011-09-19 07:19:35

At the beginning of the gambling session, "Boss Ou" still won some money, but later he lost money continuously. In no time at all, the 700,000 yuan was completely lost.

One day in June, Uncle Tang from Heshan, Jiangmen, reported to the Xinhhu police station that someone had defrauded him of more than 700,000 yuan using toothpicks. Defrauding money with toothpicks? This sounds like a joke. Even funnier, Uncle Tang said that the person who deceived him was an old friend. Uncle Tang said that on that morning, after finishing his tea and preparing to go home, as soon as he went downstairs, he met someone who looked very much like an old acquaintance named "Wang Zhicheng." Uncle Tang approached him for confirmation, and unexpectedly, it was indeed Wang Zhicheng. Upon meeting the old friend, they both returned to the teahouse to continue drinking tea and chatting, exchanging phone numbers.

Later, "Boss Ou" asked Uncle Tang, "Do you have any money? Can you lend it to me?" Seeing the sure-win situation, Uncle Tang readily agreed and even drove with "Boss Ou" to withdraw money from the bank. Uncle Tang's account had 700,000 yuan, which was cash he had just liquidated from stocks. "Boss Ou" told Uncle Tang, "If we win, I will give you 40%."

In Zhaoqing, the police successfully apprehended four individuals: Yang Yan, Wang Qiang, Peng Liang, and Ou Wen. After trial, the four confessed to their crime of jointly deceiving Uncle Tang.

The scam involved staged performances where the four worked together to defraud money.

It turned out that the person pretending to be Wang Zhicheng was actually Yang Yan, while Wang Qiang played the role of "Boss Ou," Peng Liang acted as the Hong Kong guest, and Ou Wen was responsible for lookout. When Uncle Tang mistakenly identified Yang Yan as his old friend Wang Zhicheng, Yang Yan knew that a "big fish" had been hooked. Uncle Tang was originally the factory manager of a paper mill where Yang Yan once worked. Taking advantage of the situation, Yang Yan led Uncle Tang astray multiple times, luring him into a toothpick gambling game. Using this method, they defrauded Uncle Tang of 700,000 yuan, which was then split among the four.

Two days later, Wang Zhicheng called Uncle Tang, inviting him to a hotel in Heshan city for morning tea. During the meal, "Boss Ou" appeared. Wang Zhicheng introduced "Boss Ou" as a Foshan local who owned a large hotel, saying, "If you go to any hotel in Foshan looking for me, you don't need to pay for sleeping or eating!"

"Boss Ou" did not talk about business but instead picked up the toothpicks on the table and started talking about toothpick gambling with Uncle Tang and Wang Zhicheng. He mentioned that a good friend of his had been scammed out of tens of thousands of yuan by toothpick gambling. Uncle Tang didn't believe it, thinking it seemed simple and certain to win. At this moment, another "Hong Kong boss" arrived, and Wang Zhicheng introduced him as the factory owner from his Shenzhen workplace. "Boss Ou" then started gambling with the "Hong Kong boss" using toothpicks and kept winning.

Under the persuasion of Wang Zhicheng and "Boss Ou," Uncle Tang lent all 700,000 yuan in cash to "Boss Ou" as gambling capital. Uncle Tang planned that as long as they won, he would get his money back.

Uncle Tang had no suspicion. Subsequently, "Boss Ou" proposed going to the pharmacy to buy some medicine powder for the gambling. As soon as they left the hotel, "Boss Ou" disappeared on a motorcycle. Uncle Tang went to buy the medicine himself and later tried calling "Boss Ou," but the call couldn't get through. Only then did Uncle Tang realize he had been scammed!

Based on Uncle Tang's description, the Xinhhu police officers quickly located Wang Zhicheng. When the officers brought him in front of Uncle Tang, Uncle Tang shook his head repeatedly, saying that this "Wang Zhicheng" was not the "Wang Zhicheng" he had met. He finally realized that he had mistaken the person.

Police conducted investigations at nearby hotels and motels. A hotel attendant provided information that two days before the incident, four middle-aged men had checked into the hotel. These men matched the descriptions given by Uncle Tang. Relevant video footage identified one of the men as "Boss Ou."

Southern Daily reporter Hong Yizhi, communication officer Jiang Xueyuan, Mo Wenliang, concurrently Rong Mingchang

Investigation revealed that "Boss Ou's" real name was Wang Qiang, nicknamed "Big Eyes," from Luoding, Yunfu, unemployed. Officers went to Luoding for investigation, discovering that Wang Qiang often hung out with fellow unemployed locals. They had been to Heshan together and stayed at a hotel in early June.
