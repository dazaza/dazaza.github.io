Interesting forum jargon:

- **Little knife**: Though the discount may not be substantial, there is still some room for price reduction.

- **Knife**: As the name suggests, this means a significant discount.

- **Big knife**: This represents the limit of price negotiation, rarely seen because after such discounts, the actual selling price would be significantly reduced.

- **Face-to-face transaction**: This term refers to meeting in person to complete a transaction. Buyers agree on a time and place to inspect the goods and pay in person. A related term is "face-to-face party," referring to buyers who insist on face-to-face transactions.

- **Flaking**: This refers to failing to complete a transaction after agreeing to do so, often due to unforeseen circumstances preventing the buyer from showing up.

- **Alternate accounts**: Refers to a user having multiple forum accounts.

Dominant King with Unwavering Popularity:

**HTC HD2**

![HTC HD2](front image)

While the HTC HD2 offers better performance compared to its predecessor, unfortunately, it was released at an inopportune time. In today's competitive smartphone market, it coincided with the rise of Android systems, causing this once-deserving "King of Phones" to quietly decline. Currently, the new price of this phone is less than 3000 RMB, so the second-hand market hasn't pushed the price too low. However, the grand design of the phone still appeals to many "large-screen enthusiasts." Speaking of large screens, the HD2 features a colossal 4.3-inch screen surpassing the HTC HD, exuding a regal aura. Additionally, the HD2 employs a virtual full keyboard with five physical function keys capable of handling various states effortlessly.

![HTC HD2](back image)

As a smart gray market phone using the Windows Mobile 6.5 operating system, the HTC HD2 is equipped with a Snapdragon 1GHz processor, allowing users to experience smooth and rapid performance. Paired with 448MB RAM and 512MB ROM memory, it adds considerable power to the device. However, as a flagship gray market phone, the 5-megapixel camera seems unworthy of the title, though its clear and natural shooting results fully satisfy everyday shooting needs.
