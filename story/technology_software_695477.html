No 24 Outside the police station. Sister Qiangcai looked at me and smiled, "Look at the two of you, do you still look like students?" Tang Lei and I glanced at each other, both seeing the disheveled state we were in, and couldn't help but laugh through our tears. "Sister Qiangcai, how did you get here?" I said, rubbing my sore cheek. "It was Kiki who called me," she replied, "As soon as I picked up the phone, all I could hear was her crying. I was terrified, thinking that the two of you had gotten into some serious trouble." She rolled her eyes, "But when I arrived, there you two were, lounging around like you were on vacation." I opened my mouth to respond, feeling a bit ashamed, and scratched my head. Brother Long stood off to the side, smoking a cigarette, silent. "Oh, Sister Qiangcai," I suddenly remembered something, "my guitar!" "Don't worry, it'll be brought out in a moment," she said, glancing inside the police station. Kiki clung tightly to Tang Lei's arm, refusing to let go, her eyes still glistening with tears. Tang Lei looked at her and said, "What's with all the crying? See, nothing happened." The policeman who had escorted us in came out holding my and Tang Lei's guitars. I immediately stepped forward to take mine, opening the case to inspect it. But when I saw the damage, my heart sank. Seeing that I hadn't moved, Qiangcai approached me, "What's wrong?" "I'm about to cry," I said, "Sister Qiangcai, if Shan Shan sees what's happened to the guitar, I'm dead." "Serves you right," she huffed, "I told you not to go to the bar, but you insisted. Now look what's happened. You've only got yourself to blame." "It's my fault, it's my fault," I quickly stood up and grabbed her arm, "Sister, please, don't tell Shan Shan. If she finds out, I'll be in so much trouble." "Even if I don't say anything, you need to deal with this yourself," she said dismissively. "Let's go," Brother Long finally spoke, waving his hand in farewell to the policeman, "Thanks for your trouble, we're leaving now." "Not at all," the policeman responded politely, "If you have time, come for a drink sometime." "We'll see," Brother Long coolly put on his sunglasses, though I wanted to ask him how he could see properly at night with them on. At the school gate, Tang Lei, Kiki, and I got out of the car, thanking Brother Long and Qiangcai before getting ready to leave. Qiangcai suddenly called me back. Tang Lei paused, thought for a moment, then pulled Kiki away to give us some privacy. "What is it, Sister Qiangcai?" I asked, lowering my head. She hesitated. Brother Long broke the silence, "Just say it, I don't want him following me around either." This threw me a little. Qiangcai sighed, "Ma Lin has been trying to follow Brother Long around, saying he wants to join his crew. Brother Long hasn't allowed it, but apparently Ma Lin has already hooked up with Lao San." "Lao San?" I thought hard, "You mean the bald guy?" I blurted out. Hearing this, Qiangcai couldn't help but laugh, "Bald guy? He's just Brother Long's lackey, completely brainless." "But how did Ma Lin hook up with him?" I wondered aloud. Qiangcai sighed again, "Do you remember that incident where those students caused trouble for you at the bar?" I thought back, "Yes, I remember." "During that incident, Ma Lin's actions surprised me," Brother Long continued, "He was quick to offer me a light when I pulled out a cigarette." "I remember that," I said, still puzzled, "What does that mean?" "You're dense," Qiangcai looked at me, "That means Ma Lin enjoys that kind of life, or rather, he sees Brother Long as the person he wants to become. Even if it's buried deep inside, given the right opportunity, it will surface." "So, what was the opportunity?" I asked, beginning to understand. "Yes," Qiangcai nodded, "After that incident, he kept pestering me for Brother Long's contact information. When I refused, he would hang around the bar waiting for Brother Long to show up. Eventually, they met once when I wasn't there. Brother Long recognized him, but still refused his request to join his crew. But Ma Lin didn't give up, and somehow ended up with Lao San." "Why didn't you nip this in the bud?" I asked anxiously. "I did," Qiangcai shot me a glare, "Brother Long told me, so I went to talk to Ma Lin. He promised he wouldn't do it anymore, but secretly, he was still hanging out with Lao San. I went back to Brother Long, who questioned Lao San. Lao San said he saw potential in Ma Lin and was determined to befriend him. Brother Long has been trying to focus on legitimate business ventures lately, so his interactions with Lao San have decreased. But Lao San doesn't seem to listen very well." "Talk some sense into Ma Lin," Qiangcai looked at me seriously, "This path he's chosen is difficult, and once you're in, it's hard to get out." I nodded. But I wasn't sure how to approach Ma Lin. His behavior had indeed been strange recently. He was often nowhere to be found, had stopped organizing his English club, and had become quite arrogant. There was even an incident where he threatened to kill someone from another class over a conflict. Ma Lin returned to school three days before the semester started. During dinner, I asked him, "What did you do during the summer break?" Ma Lin's mouth was full of rice as he replied, "Nothing much, just wandered around. You don't know until you go out and experience things." "Oh?" Lu Fei became interested, "Where did you go? Tell me, maybe I'll visit too." "Nowhere special," Ma Lin swallowed, "Bars, large bathhouses, discos, nightclubs, red-light districts..." "Haha, stop bragging," Lu Fei laughed, "Could you really go to places like that?" "Why would I lie?" Ma Lin wiped his mouth, "And the girls there are beautiful, many of them college students. It was mesmerizing, and I felt quite aroused inside." "Did you get any action?" Lu Fei grinned at Ma Lin. "At first, they were all pretending not to care, unwilling to drink or sing with me," Ma Lin said, looking quite pleased, "But as soon as I took out some cash, they swarmed around me, their big chests and big butts pressing against me, impossible to shake off." "Cash?" Lu Fei mocked Ma Lin, "Do you even have enough money?" "Don't believe me?" Ma Lin looked at Lu Fei, "Watch this." He pulled out a wad of hundred-yuan bills from his wallet. I took a glance—it was almost 2000 yuan. "Are you using your tuition money?" Lu Fei still wore a mocking smile. "Why would I do that?" Ma Lin became a bit angry, "I have enough money for this." Seeing Ma Lin's reaction, Lu Fei chuckled, "Alright, I get it. Put it away, careful someone tries to rob you." "Who dares!" Ma Lin looked completely unconcerned. Lu Fei gave me a meaningful look, but I just pursed my lips and stayed silent. I understood what Lu Fei meant, but I chose not to explain. Lu Fei was merely guessing, whereas I knew the truth. Watching Ma Lin spout nonsense, I felt deeply worried. In the scorching end of August, my third year of university began. Unfortunately, Xiaoshan returned before my facial injuries healed, and everything about the fight and the stint in the police station was exposed. As a result, my ears were swollen for another three days. Secretly, I was relieved that the guitar incident hadn't been discovered yet. It had been sent for repairs, and the damage was no longer obvious. Then came the task of checking grades and adding elective courses. But when I saw my grades, I was utterly enraged. A glaring 55 in Deng Xiaoping Theory nearly blinded my metal-alloy eyes. I shouted in anger, "Do I have to bribe someone to pass?" Xiaoshan looked at me helplessly beside me. Thus, my sole failed course during my university years was born—a disgraceful one. Tang Lei, Ma Lin, Lu Fei, and Adai also flunked Deng Xiaoping Theory, which made Sun Xin extremely smug. He taunted us, prompting Tang Lei to want to hit him again—indeed, I felt the same way. Zheng Yu failed Modern Chinese; when he saw his grade, he looked bewildered. It was understandable—Zheng Yu was always proactive in erasing the blackboard, handing in homework, and participating in classroom activities, yet he scored 58, failing the course. Not only did this affect his passing, but it also ruined all chances of being considered for any excellence awards that year. So, at the start of our third year, we trudged to the study room with our backpacks to prepare for the make-up exams. During the make-up exams, I realized that every undergraduate class in the English department had 3-5 people failing, all males. Before the exam, everyone looked at each other with somber expressions. Fortunately, we all passed without major issues, and I breathed a sigh of relief. However, another problem arose—I had to double-major in Japanese and Korean. By the third year, English majors had to choose a second foreign language. We could only choose between Japanese and Korean and had to decide by the end of the second year. However, I hadn’t realized that the system had automatically enrolled me in both Japanese and Korean. Of course, others unfortunate like me included Adai, Lu Fei, and Ma Lin. When the schedule came out, I was dumbfounded to see both Japanese and Korean on it. This meant that in my third year, I would have six more classes per week than other students, plus an extra professional course exam. For someone new to both languages, it was practically a disaster. All I knew about Japanese was “baka” and “yamete” (learned from watching Japanese adult films in the dorm), and about Korean, I only remembered “annyeonghaseyo.” I yelled at Xiaoshan, “Why didn’t you remind me?” Xiaoshan looked at me innocently, “I did tell you, but you were too focused on preparing for the Deng Xiaoping Theory exam. I assumed you had already decided, so I didn’t mention it further. How was I supposed to know you hadn’t chosen?” With no choice, I grudgingly carried my bag back and forth between different classrooms. Despite being mentally prepared, when I actually started studying, I was completely overwhelmed. Japanese has 50 hiragana, 50 corresponding katakana, plus ん and ヌ (pronounced 'n'). Staring at them made me dizzy. Just as I began to differentiate them, it was time to rush to the Korean classroom. Korean has 40 letters, including 21 vowels and 19 consonants. After one class, my head was spinning. There was no other option; at the start of the semester, I abnormally carried my bag and dragged Xiaoshan to the study room. Xiaoshan looked at me as if I were a rare animal, her face filled with disbelief. Thus, Room 101 in Area F became my revolutionary base. I consistently occupied a corner, tirelessly writing Japanese and Korean characters. After memorizing Japanese, I proceeded to learn Korean, then had Xiaoshan quiz me. If I made mistakes, I went back to memorize again. During class meetings, Zhengyu and Gaofeifei discussed matters above, while I stubbornly held onto my textbook. During breaks, Gaofeifei walked over and picked up my book. Seeing it was a Japanese textbook, she smirked, "Little brother, you’re obsessed." "Almost there," Xiaoshan interjected unhappily, "Learning Japanese and Korean all day, it’s all in his mind, I can barely keep up." I glared at both of them, "Do you think I want to? I’m notorious for being lazy in studies. To be this diligent, it’s not my style. This is all forced." "Looks like you need someone with a whip behind you to move fast," Gaofeifei laughed. "That's my main job in the future," Xiaoshan nodded solemnly. Looking at them, I felt like a lost cause. "What did you say?" Xiaoshan stretched out her criminal hand again. I slapped it away, "Don't interfere with my studies. My bad habits are real this time. Watch closely." Xiaoshan looked at me expectantly, while Gaofeifei wore a skeptical expression. Thus, I loudly announced my grand learning campaign. And within this short span of less than a month, Zhengyu fell in love. We were already juniors, considered upperclassmen. Each class assigned two representatives to mentor the freshmen. Naturally, Zhengyu and Gaofeifei became the mentors for Class 06-1. However, Gaofeifei was deeply engrossed in her own romance and paid little attention, leaving most of the responsibilities to Zhengyu, who introduced the freshmen to their environment and handled various tasks like issuing cards. Love blossomed amidst these small, everyday tasks. The girl’s name was Wu Xiaoli, originally from Yunnan, making her first trip to the north. Unfamiliar with many things and a bit uncomfortable, Zhengyu naturally became her guide, caring for her daily life and studies, eventually leading to personal matters. Such occurrences are common throughout university, where senior students taking care of juniors often lead to romantic relationships. This method, although effective, irked the female students of the same year, who complained, "Are we not good enough compared to these young sisters?" Initially, Zhengyu tried to hide his feelings, but we soon discovered his secret. I was still crouching in the corner of F101, while Xiaoshan stayed in the dormitory due to a friend’s visit. Zhengyu burst in, and I glanced up briefly before continuing to memorize Japanese words. Apparently unaware of my presence, Zhengyu sat down in the first row on the left, followed by a girl who sat behind him. Initially, they hardly exchanged words, and I didn’t notice anything unusual. After a while of memorizing, feeling bored, I closed my book and took out my phone to text Xiaoshan. However, a soft laugh interrupted my texting. Zhengyu was leaning sideways, and the girl behind him was stretching her neck, chatting and laughing happily, completely ignoring the other seven or eight people in the study room. Intrigued, I put my phone down and propped my book upright to hide my face. I couldn’t hear what Zhengyu said, but the girl behind him playfully punched him, and Zhengyu laughed happily, clearly enjoying it. A girl stood up, tidied herself, and left, giving Zhengyu a glare before exiting. But Zhengyu paid no attention, putting his book aside and sitting together with the girl. As the saying goes, the beginning of love is sweet and passionate. Zhengyu suddenly leaned in and kissed the girl on the cheek. The girl blushed furiously, punching him with both fists as Zhengyu laughed arrogantly. Hiding behind my book, I snickered, thinking, "Good kid, you’ve achieved so much without even telling us. Wait till we get back to the dormitory, and I’ll deal with you." But my phone suddenly rang, startling me, and Zhengyu turned to look. Having no choice, I walked out, giving Zhengyu a mischievous grin before leaving. Zhengyu was dumbfounded. Later, Zhengyu couldn't escape our dormitory's old rules: treating us to a meal and revealing all details of their relationship. During the drinking session, Zhengyu asked me, "Why am I always involved whenever something happens?" Xiaoshan sneered, "You're just noticing now? He's a troublemaker, stirring up trouble everywhere." I chuckled evilly. Soon, it was the National Day holiday. Gaofeifei planned to visit Yang Feng, and since Xiaoshan and I had no plans, we tagged along, only to leave after a meal. Initially, Yang Feng was told we’d arrive around noon for lunch, but Gaofeifei had a sudden idea to catch him off guard and woke Xiaoshan and me up at 5 in the morning. After a blurry two-hour ride, we arrived at Qingdao University of Science and Technology Fang Campus. Excitedly, Gaofeifei wanted to raid Yang Feng's dormitory, but Xiaoshan and I showed no interest. We greeted her and decided to stroll around the area to grab some breakfast instead. Gaofeifei thought it over and agreed to call later. My stomach growled hungrily, and Xiaoshan looked at me disapprovingly. "I never see you skipping breakfast and getting hungry like this," she remarked. "There's no choice," I scratched my head, "I've never woken up this early before. Let's find something to eat quickly; I'm starving." So, we walked along the road heading north unknowingly reaching a residential area. I snapped my fingers, "There must be small restaurants in the residential area. Let's find one; we might even buy some buns." "Eat, eat, all you ever think about is eating," Xiaoshan teased, "You're going to turn into a bun." I looked at myself, noticing a slight bulge in my belly, but hunger quickly drove me to walk faster. "What?" Xiaoshan pointed to a small square nearby, "Dancing?" Turning around, I indeed saw a group of elderly people dancing energetically to rhythmic music. "Don't look, let's go," I urged Xiaoshan, "I'm starving." Xiaoshan laughed, "Yes, Bad Brother." Sure enough, there was a steamed bun shop. I bought a whole tray of buns, gave two to Xiaoshan, and bought two cups of milk tea from a nearby store, pouring hot water to complete our breakfast. "Let's eat near the dancing place," Xiaoshan suggested, pulling me towards it. "Enjoy the performance too." We found a spot to sit, eating our breakfast while watching the elderly men and women dance gracefully. Suddenly, I noticed someone and froze. Xiaoshan noticed my odd behavior, "Hmm? What's wrong? Choking? Drink some milk tea quickly." But I remained motionless, silent. Xiaoshan grew anxious and patted my back hard. I sprayed the bun out, glaring at Xiaoshan, "Are you trying to murder me?" "You were choking," Xiaoshan retorted unhappily. "Choking? Look over there," I pointed toward a young man among a group of elders. "Ah! Yang Feng!" Xiaoshan exclaimed, her mouth wide open. Among the dancing uncles and aunts, there was a slightly plump figure, holding a embroidered fan, dancing enthusiastically to the music.
