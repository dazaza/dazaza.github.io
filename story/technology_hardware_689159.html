In the past, if a boy grew up to be handsome, or was good at basketball, or excelled in math, or even if he wasn't particularly outstanding in anything, there would still be someone who liked him. But now, for two people to be together, there are too many conditions and obstacles to overcome. The fantasy of going home together after school gradually turns into waking up in the morning with the sunlight and you by my side. If I must say what I regret or have missed, it is the time when I could fall in love while wearing a school uniform. I missed the days when someone held my hand at the gate and we went home together. I missed the times when we used the excuse of extra lessons to go out and eat cotton candy. These words about first love only belong to that age. Which young man does not know passion? Which young woman does not dream of springtime? When Jiu Ba Dao's "Those Years, Together We Chased After Girls" became popular on the Internet, it made me unconsciously recall those years when we were girls no one chased. From childhood to adulthood, our lives were never short of either excellent or beautiful girls. Some were even as talented and beautiful as tea. Under the shadow of these girls, most of us were famously like ugly ducklings. Ordinary looks, ordinary dressing, ordinary achievements. Our lives were always filled with bland tastes. We were someone's best friend, watching others' romances and being pursued by others. Then we would silently tell the person we secretly liked, if only you knew how I felt. Dear, where in this world are there so many Shen Jiayi and Ke Jingtengs? More often than not, we were Shen Jiayi's best friends or Ke Jingteng's good brothers. In those years, there were always some girls no one chased, and some boys who didn't chase anyone. Did we miss our youth? Perhaps not. In other people's stories, we were beautiful backdrops. We envied, hoped, and as children thought that falling in love meant holding hands on the way home from school and then going home together. Now, grown up, we realize that romance can become very complicated. Remember when we were kids, our favorite topic was who liked who and who was dating who. True or false, we teased each other endlessly, and the girls often blushed with anger while the boys stood up to joke around. During class, when two people everyone thought were related were called by the teacher consecutively, it always caused a burst of laughter. The teachers, either confused or seeing something in our ambiguous smiles, would raise their voices and say: stop laughing, let's look at this problem. But do you still know where the person you silently liked back then is now, whether they're doing well, what they've become? That boy you stole a glance at during physical exercises, that boy whose name made your heart race and cheeks flush. Did he, in the end, know your feelings? Did you ever daydream and nightdream that he just happened to fall in love with the unremarkable you? How many words did you write for him? Did you deny it with a red face when your close friend pointed out that you liked him? Do you remember how much of an ugly duckling you were back then? Dear you, does the current you ever wonder why you fell in love with him back then? Perhaps, that was youth, that was the story of girls no one chased. No beginning, no end. In those years, we were girls no one chased. In those years, we all grew up between understanding and not understanding love. We girls no one chased will eventually become girls someone loves. It's always lonely when you're alone, loving or being loved, having been foolish for many years, suddenly realizing that everything is empty. For oneself, it's like a story, impossible to narrate clearly. Listening to Eason Chan's "Because of Love," liking the lines: because of love, it doesn't easily sadden, so everything appears happy. Someone left me a message saying, there will always be two people, one who amazes time, and one who gentles the years. I think the first is the person deeply loved but couldn't be together with, the second is the person holding your hand until old age. There used to be a hundred reasons for despair that led to my downfall, but now there's a clear goal guiding me forward. Parents don't allow early romance because they know this relationship is destined to bear no fruit. I understand, I comprehend, I get it, so I must strive. The only thing one can do for oneself and not regret for life is to study hard. University, I once only imagined university, but never understood the meaning of these two words. University is not only a new beginning, but another society, idealistic yet realistic. Many people already in society haven't realized what cruel reality is; they just monotonously shuttle between home and workplace. They never closely see the reality of society, the cruelty of reality. I think my sixteenth year should be different, returning to the wonderful times of the past. It's said that the long journey of old times can become a beautiful memory. So let me forget all those decadent times.
