A regular sex life can regulate menstruation. This is because sexual activity allows women to obtain beneficial pheromones from their partners, which in turn affects their own endocrine condition. The more regular the sexual activity, the more stable the level of androgen secretion in their bodies, and the more stable the related physiological reactions, such as the menstrual cycle.

When it comes to the monthly "visit" of menstruation, the vast majority of women associate it with feelings of depression, lower back pain, and that sudden worry that catches them off guard. Many people secretly hope: if only they could take control of their periods without taking medicine! In fact, achieving this isn't difficult.

Have sex at least once a week

The concern about menstruation often stems from its irregularity, which can leave women embarrassed about soiling their clothes.

According to a report on the American "Today Physiology" website, Dr. Winnifred Cutler, an expert in sexology, found that a regular sex life can regulate menstruation. This is because sexual activity allows women to obtain beneficial pheromones from their partners, thereby influencing their own endocrine status. The more regular the sexual activity, the more stable the level of androgen secretion in their bodies, and the more stable the related physiological responses, such as the menstrual cycle.

Specifically, having sex once a week can adjust the menstrual cycle to 29.5 days, which is the optimal cycle for maintaining female fertility and endocrine health.

If the frequency of sex is less than once a week, the effect is less ideal, and may even be worse than not having sex at all; while a higher frequency, as long as it's moderate, also benefits women's physical health.

Control period pain through diet

The next issue troubling women is dysmenorrhea. It can make women feel cold in their hands and feet, break out in cold sweats, and even vomit or faint. Therefore, many women avoid cold water and fruit during their period, consuming large amounts of warming foods instead.

Many people mistakenly believe that dysmenorrhea is caused by "cold constitution and a chilled uterus," but in fact, there are many causes.

If dysmenorrhea is caused by diseases such as uterine fibroids or endometriosis, these women need to seek medical attention promptly. If they blindly consume warming supplements, it could result in "the faster you supplement, the faster the endometrium swells."

If dysmenorrhea is caused by a cold uterus, these women often have problems such as reduced menstrual flow and delayed periods, and experience severe contraction pain when menstruating, making the process less smooth. Such women can eat some chocolate, drink ginger soup, or red sugar water to alleviate discomfort when menstruating.

If dysmenorrhea is caused by a hot uterus and excessive pelvic congestion, a safer method is to eat some cooler foods, such as light fruits and vegetables.

Irregular menstruation should prompt timely pregnancy testing

Women fear menstruation because they often encounter non-menstrual bleeding. Women with sexual experience who experience irregular menstruation should promptly test for pregnancy.

In outpatient settings, vaginal bleeding that occurs during early pregnancy due to miscarriage is often mistaken for prolonged menstruation; and occasional amenorrhea in women of childbearing age is often mistaken for delayed menstruation. "If this phenomenon is ignored, the harm can be very significant."

After ruling out the possibility of pregnancy, if there is still a problem with too short a menstrual cycle, such as occurring every two weeks, it may cause anemia and weakness in women, and it's best to take Chinese medicine to help control it; if the cycle is too long, such as exceeding 1.5-2 months, or simply absent, it may lead to infertility in women, and hormone secretion issues should be checked.

Abnormal bleeding may also be related to having sex during menstruation or overly intense sexual activity. Having sex during menstruation can easily lead to infections of the female reproductive organs and should be avoided.
