The Most Suitable Time for an Abortion

Gynecological experts introduce: Having an abortion within 10 weeks of pregnancy is the most suitable. The earlier the abortion surgery, the simpler and safer it is; the later it is, the more complex the surgery becomes, and the longer the post-surgery recovery time.

Gynecological experts also state that within 10 weeks of pregnancy, the uterus isn't too large, and the fetus and placenta have not yet formed. Generally, there's no need to dilate the cervix; suction curettage (negative pressure aspiration) can be used, making it easy to remove fetal tissue. The reaction during surgery is mild, with less bleeding, faster recovery, and minimal impact on the body. Between 10-14 weeks of pregnancy, as the embryo grows larger, forceps evacuation abortion is required. This surgery has a slower recovery and a certain impact on the body. If the pregnancy exceeds 14 weeks, neither of these two types of abortions can be performed, and instead, hospitalization for labor induction surgery is necessary. Therefore, pregnant women who need an abortion should try to have the negative pressure aspiration surgery within 10 weeks of pregnancy to reduce the pain associated with the procedure.
