Female postdoctoral fellow runs up huge debts from charity work, owes nearly 500,000 yuan

by xue94fwsh on 2012-03-01 16:58:31

Tsinghua postdoctoral student Cao Mingxiu borrowed money to fund education. (Photo from the Internet) Influenced by the Guo Meimei incident, the social donations for Riyin Wangwei Fund have almost been zero. The couple has no car, no house, no deposit, but they are still doubted. Recently, a microblog named "Tsinghua postdoctoral students Cao Mingxiu couple borrow money to run education" has been widely spread on the Internet. Yesterday, the parties involved, Cao Mingxiu, a postdoctoral student in finance at Northern Jiaotong University, and her husband Huang He, were interviewed by our reporter. In order to support the long-term construction of the housekeeping female worker training project and the support for teachers in migrant workers' children schools, the two have accumulated a huge debt, with a total debt of nearly 500,000 yuan. Last week, their office was closed because they could not pay the rent. Written by our reporter Li Ying. The one in trouble this time is Riyin Wangwei Fund managed by Huang He. This fund was established last April under the support of the China Women's Development Foundation, with an initial donation of 1 million yuan from Wang Wei, then chairman of Beijing Dao Heng Company, to set up a special fund. The fund is committed to the education of migrant workers' children and the employment support of rural housekeeping female workers. However, just after the establishment of the fund, the founder Wang Wei passed away due to illness. The fund relied solely on the initial investment of 1 million yuan to carry out public welfare activities. In one year, they sponsored a total of 120 teachers from five migrant workers' children schools in Beijing: Yuzhu, Mingxin, Dongfanghong, Huilei, and Jinbang. "Each person is subsidized between 300 yuan and 600 yuan per month, so when added to the money given by the school, the teacher's salary is between 1200 yuan and 1600 yuan. In order to ensure that the children receive quality education, four schools are equipped with executive principals. They are experienced famous principals from all over the country, some of whom are retired second-line personnel. Each person receives a monthly subsidy of 2000 yuan," Huang He told reporters that in this way, the monthly expenditure on the aspect of migrant workers' children schools alone amounts to nearly 80,000 yuan. Meanwhile, Cao Mingxiu is also running a social enterprise called "Green Heart Light", providing housekeeping service training for rural female workers to help them find employment. The couple's plan is to establish a sustainable social enterprise, including a public welfare fund, a training school, and a housekeeping social enterprise, using the profit-making income to further assist the education of migrant workers' children. "We currently only have two stores open in two neighborhoods. According to our model design, if we open five stores, this model can be sustainably developed. But unexpectedly, it was suddenly interrupted," said Huang He. Facing difficulties: repeatedly surrounded by creditors Since the beginning of this year in April or May, the funds for the foundation have dried up. "One reason is that the founder Mr. Wang Wei has already passed away, and the later-stage funds cannot be in place, and secondly, this year coincides with the Guo Meimei incident. Originally, we were negotiating some social donations, but they all disappeared." Huang He said that they owe four months of subsidies to the teachers. Besides the money from parents, relatives, and friends, due to the lack of car or house as collateral, in order to maintain the operation of the fund, Cao Mingxiu and Huang He had no choice but to borrow more than 300,000 yuan from companies and individuals in society. The interest rate is as high as 10%~20%. "One person lent 60,000 yuan, and after one month, 72,000 yuan needs to be repaid." Huang He said that with the accumulation of interest, they already owe more than 400,000 yuan. They have not thought about seeking help from public welfare organizations in society. But "they do not care about the teachers in migrant workers' schools and rural housekeeping female workers." Cao Mingxiu wrote online: "For one and a half years, since I finished my postdoctoral studies, every day several groups of creditors have besieged my home, even at 2 o'clock in the morning, the police came. I am taking care of my child and elders, and I can't hold on anymore, I have symptoms of rhinitis." Last week, their office was also closed because they couldn't pay the rent, and next week, two places where rural female workers live will also be closed. And they still have additional debts. In 2008, Huang He donated the Xingzhi School for migrant workers' children that he founded himself, "leaving 420,000 yuan in cash for the school as operating expenses, bearing two months of teacher salaries amounting to 160,000 yuan, plus 400,000 yuan in engineering costs", accumulating a total debt of more than 900,000 yuan. Cao Mingxiu told reporters that she had to use her parents' pensions and repay nearly 600,000 yuan from relatives and friends, but there are still more than 300,000 yuan that cannot be repaid. "Public welfare requires the attention and support of the whole society." Responding to doubts: They are objectively existing From a certain point of view, both Huang He and Cao Mingxiu are "celebrities". Huang He was one of the earliest people to establish schools for migrant workers' children and once appeared on CCTV's "Face to Face". And Cao Mingxiu is well-known to the public as a female postdoctoral student engaged in the nanny business. But they are not understood by everyone. "If I stop now and give up, go out and find a job, I would earn at least 500,000 yuan a year." "Borrowing money for public welfare" has led to doubts. Some netizens said, why borrow money for public welfare? Can't you make money first and then do charity? Huang He faced multiple school closures when he was running the Xingzhi School for migrant workers' children. Regarding the doubts raised by this incident, he told reporters that the number of teachers in migrant workers' children schools and rural housekeeping female workers is huge, and they are objectively existing. "The two of us can stop immediately, and we can live very well, but they are still there."
