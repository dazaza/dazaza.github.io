Investigation on sales of new iPhone in China: Not that bad

by anonymous on 2013-10-01 21:00:22

Amidst a wave of skepticism about the new iPhone being "all dressed up with nowhere new to go," the iPhone 5s/5c hit the market. Although it was proudly announced that China would participate in the global launch, the lukewarm sales scene at Apple's flagship store in Beijing raised doubts about how much longer Apple could continue innovating. The introduction of the iPhone 5c as a new product with just a colorful shell change left many speechless. Some industry insiders commented that perhaps only when things get truly dire will Apple wake up, though that moment is still far from here.

A Huge Contrast to Its Historical Success

In Xidan, Beijing, stretching from Joy City northward all the way to Lingjing Hutong, what can be called a high-end smartphone showcase street exists. Here, Apple's flagship store, China Unicom's Xidan branch, and China Telecom's branch are all located—some of the largest stores for these three companies in Beijing. Each year, the new iPhones are launched here.

On the morning of September 20th, Sina Tech observed the iPhone sales situation at these three stores along Xidan Street in Beijing. At Apple’s Xidan flagship store, there were dozens of people queuing up, with plenty of security personnel standing outside. Although this scene wasn't exactly embarrassing, compared to the grand spectacle of the iPhone 4S sales over a year ago, it was rather desolate.

At that time, a foreign media outlet described the scene like this: "On January 13, 2012, hundreds of customers, including migrant workers hired by scalpers, queued up outside the Apple flagship store in Beijing, braving the cold for an entire night. When the store failed to open on schedule at 7 am, the crowd erupted. Some people began throwing eggs and shouting angrily at the employees through the windows. A staff member announced the cancellation of the sale via a loudspeaker. The police ordered the gathered crowd to disperse and sealed off the area with yellow tape. Employees posted notices stating that the iPhone 4S was out of stock. Due to the unexpectedly large number of people gathered outside, Beijing's Apple flagship store suspended the sale of the iPhone 4S in mainland China, leading angry customers and scalpers to throw eggs in retaliation."

Now, China has become one of the first countries to launch the iPhone 5s/5c, but the scalpers have disappeared. It's said they've moved to Apple's online mall, but in reality, their profits have thinned out.

A merchant in Zhongguancun told Sina Tech that when the iPhone 4S first came out, it was nearly impossible to get one, with prices marked up by thousands. Now, except for the gold version of the iPhone 5s, which still holds some value, the silver version of the iPhone 5s only sees a markup of a few hundred yuan. The grey version of the iPhone 5s is rarely stocked because its markup potential is minimal. As for the iPhone 5c, very few people even inquire about it.

Huge Regional Differences

Similarly, at the Beijing Unicom branch just a few hundred meters away from Joy City, the official sales of the iPhone 5s/5c began at 8 am. Perhaps due to opening earlier, the queue visible outside wasn’t too long. However, interestingly, the Xidan Telecom branch was bustling with activity. Famous TV host Chun Ni and several students from "The Voice of China" were present, hanging huge banners promoting the launch of the iPhone 5s/5c, attracting many people to form long queues.

In fact, the sales situation of the iPhone 5s/5c varies greatly across different regions.

Guangdong Province can be said to be one of the hottest regions for the sales of the iPhone 5s/5c this year. In some local branches of Guangdong Unicom, you can still see the fervent crowds lining up, with the queue already circling the business hall multiple times. From 8 pm the previous evening, numerous Apple fans had already started queuing up.

Likewise, before 7 am, a large number of "Apple fans" had gathered in front of Guangdong Telecom’s business hall, completely surrounding it.

At Ningbo Yili Road Telecom business hall, according to the manager, "We opened two hours early, but at 5 am, there were already people queuing up to grab the new iPhone! One couple stands out in my memory; the man wanted the 5s, while the woman wanted the 5c."

However, according to a consultation conducted by Sina Tech with the manager of a cooperative business hall in a provincial capital city in the west, the business hall sold only 15 units of the iPhone 5s/5c in one day, a stark contrast to the sales of the iPhone 4s.

Clearly, the enthusiastic queuing for purchases in the eastern regions versus the desolation in the western regions reflects differing economic levels, determining that the sales of the iPhone 5s/5c in China will increasingly concentrate in first- and second-tier cities.

Promotional Efforts by Unicom and Telecom

It should be said that what kept some "dignity" for the iPhone 5s/5c was due to the vigorous promotional efforts of the telecom operators. China Telecom frequently leverages its relationship with "The Voice of China" for nationwide tours, inviting famous artists and "Voice of China" trainees to promote the iPhone 5s/5c. For example, Beijing Telecom invited "The Voice of China" host Hua Shao to sign books and give autographs at the sales site, and during the launch ceremony...

Zhejiang Telecom, on the launch day, invited well-known actor Zhang Duo to specially come from the filming site to Ningbo, along with his good friend Hu Ling, known as the "Queen of Healing Ballads".

In Guangdong, Guangzhou Evergrande FC players and coach Marcello Lippi became the first users of the Guangdong Telecom version of the iPhone 5S, with Lippi becoming the first user of the iPhone 5S for Guangdong Telecom.

China Unicom also prepared attractive discount measures. For instance, Guangdong Unicom not only offered an online reservation gift worth up to 600 yuan but also provided 12G of data within Guangdong province and 6G of WeChat data, and even gifted a family card, allowing family members to share a package, significantly reducing expenses for users.

These substantial support costs were aimed at boosting the popularity of the iPhone 5s/5c. The two operators managed to help Apple save face through purchase subsidies and marketing expenses, turning the iPhone 5s/5c into performances for the two operators.

The "Best" Plastic Version of the iPhone 5c

Some analysts pointed out that the once common scenes of hundreds or even thousands of people queuing up enthusiastically for the first day of Apple iPhone launches have long since disappeared. This is mainly due to Apple strengthening its control over scalpers, requiring the phone number and identity information used during the reservation to be provided when picking up the phone on-site, and limiting each person to purchasing only two iPhone 5S units, resulting in a relatively quiet scene.

But the truth may not be so simple. From a psychological perspective, "surprise" comes more from internal "change" rather than color. This time, the iPhone 5s/5c combined offer five colors and eight new models, but no matter how many colors there are, it does not equate to innovation. The iPhone 5c is merely referred to as a plastic version of the previous generation product, yet its price is anything but cheap, being only about 800 yuan less than the iPhone 5S, but with configurations identical to the iPhone 5.

Although the colorful plastic version of the iPhone 5c is considered the "best" plastic version of the iPhone 5c, actual sales figures have proven that consumers do not accept it.

Back in the day, Nokia phones came in many colors, but the advent of the smartphone era meant that no matter how many colors Nokia phones changed, they continued to lose ground. Now, Apple's new iPhone products seem to be repeating the story once played by the fallen aristocracy, raising questions about whether this is really Apple's innovation?
