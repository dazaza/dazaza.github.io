by anonymous on 2012-02-17 10:34:13

Against the backdrop of the junk food consumed in a year, British children appear so small.

According to a comprehensive report by Information Times, amidst towering piles of food, a British child appears diminutive. However, these are the "junk foods" he consumes in a year. Due to the UK Food Standards Agency's intention to significantly reduce supervision over food companies, the public wishes to express their discontent in this manner. Nowadays, children are referred to as the "generation raised on junk food," and British children are no exception. Statistics show that one-third of British children aged 5 to 13 suffer from varying degrees of obesity. Investigations into food companies by the UK Food Standards Agency began a decade ago because several victims died due to food-related issues, sparking controversy. However, these investigations remain unresolved to this day. Now, the UK government intends to drastically cut back on monitoring food companies, a decision that has left the British public quite dissatisfied. They prefer that the country impose a "fat tax" on unhealthy foods.

In 2009, the UK Food Standards Agency conducted a survey on diet and nutrition, revealing the alarming total amount of junk food consumed by British children in a year.
