Beijing Shunyi No.5 Middle School "trousers-down" video shocks the Internet

by kaxiya on 2009-07-06 13:01:56

The "Pants-Down Scandal" Video of Shunyi No.5 Middle School in Beijing Shockingly Appears Online

- All participants are students from the second year of junior high school at this school.

-The District Education Commission responds: Do not harm the victims any further.

Recently, a video involving students from Shunyi No. 5 Middle School in Beijing was exposed on the internet. On the morning of May 5th, Jiang Huayang, the spokesperson for the Shunyi District Education Commission, gave an interview to journalists, revealing the preliminary investigation results for the first time. He expressed the hope that the media and the general public would refrain from excessively disclosing relevant personal information about those involved, considering the protection of the physical and mental health of teenagers.

It is reported that on the night of July 3rd, a large number of videos featuring students from Shunyi No. 5 Middle School appeared online. In these videos, several male students were seen removing the clothes and pants of a female student inside a classroom.

Upon discovering these videos, the District Education Commission and the District Public Security Bureau immediately launched an investigation. Currently, after preliminary investigations, it has been confirmed that all individuals involved are students from the second year of junior high school at this school. The police are still conducting further investigations into the situation.

Not long ago, the internet revealed the "Pants-Down Scandal" incident involving Shunyi No. 5 Middle School in Beijing: Several male students removed the clothing and underwear of a female student inside a classroom. This video angered a large number of netizens, with some even conducting "human flesh searches" and publishing the personal information of three male students featured in the video. Subsequently, the police and the education commission intervened in the investigation, and the police controlled seven or eight students. By the evening of the third day, most of these students had admitted to filming the video.

The reporter observed that the screenshots and video footage on websites were blurry in quality, but desks and chairs could be seen within the frames. Both males and the female in the video were wearing long-sleeved uniforms of Shunyi No. 5 Middle School. At the start of the video, two male students were seen touching the female student's chest over her clothes while speaking; the female student struggled and resisted, shouting "Don't!" However, her resistance proved ineffective as the male students eventually pinned her down and stripped her of her clothes and underwear.

At 4 p.m. on the third day, all links to the video were deleted from the internet, but comments from netizens regarding the incident remained. Most netizens believed that it was "a blatant case of forced molestation of a female student."

[Netizens] Conducted "Human Flesh Searches" on Male Students in the Video

Not long after the video appeared online, the several male students in the video became the focus of many netizens' attention. Around 6 a.m. on the third day, netizens began conducting "human flesh searches" on these male students. At 7:38 a.m., Baidu Tieba posted photos and related information of several individuals from the "Pants-Down Scandal" video. One netizen provided three personal photos of three teenagers, along with one group photo. The background of the photos showed they were mostly taken in a bathroom tiled with white tiles.

In the photos, these students wore sunglasses, some with cigars in their mouths, others holding wooden sticks. They were all wearing school uniforms with the inscription "Shunyi No. 5 Middle School" on the left chest area. The last photo provided by the netizen was a group photo of eight people, five of whom wore the Shunyi No. 5 Middle School uniform. The reporter discovered that the background of this group photo was within Shunyi No. 5 Middle School.

Some netizens pointed out the names of the three individuals and even published one person's QQ number. Subsequently, this information was censored.

Yu Qi (pseudonym), who was "human flesh searched" and had his QQ number disclosed, claimed he had no connection to the incident. The exposure of his QQ number led him to be attacked multiple times by netizens, prompting him to consider suing the person who released his information.

At 5 p.m. on the second day, the reporter entered Yu Qi's QQ space and found a blog post titled "Attention Netizens," where the author stated, "I am a senior high school student. Yes, my name is Yu Qi (pseudonym) and I didn't make that video. Those kids are two grades younger than me!"

[Students] Saw This Video Last Year

At 4:20 p.m. on the third day, it was dismissal time at Shunyi No. 5 Middle School. A large number of students wearing the blue uniforms from the video exited the school gates. The blackboard at the school entrance announced that all teachers needed to attend a meeting after school. Most students said that previously, there were no regular meetings scheduled for Fridays.

When asked about the "Pants-Down Scandal," most students claimed they were unaware of it. A first-year female student said that classmates in her class started discussing it privately a few days ago, saying it was "several boys gang-raping a girl." However, most classmates believed the content of the video was filmed by someone pretending to be a student from Shunyi No. 5 Middle School. She mentioned that Shunyi No. 5 Middle School uniforms were frequently stolen and that there were cases of third-year students selling them.

Among the three students identified through "human flesh searches," only Li Dazhong (pseudonym) was confirmed by multiple students from Shunyi No. 5 Middle School as being a student in Class 3, Grade 3. A second-year male student familiar with him said, "He used to study quite well in elementary school." This student described Li's appearance: "He is 1.7 meters tall, neither fat nor thin, with fairly dark skin." Since the third-year students had already finished their middle school exams and were on vacation, the reporter could not gather more information about Li Dazhong.

In a nearby billiard hall, many students from Shunyi No. 5 Middle School claimed to know Li Dazhong or had heard of his name. A second-year male student said that Li Dazhong often hung out with people outside of school. He mentioned seeing this video last year when it was passed on to him, "I watched it, found it very boring, and then deleted it." These students all indicated that they had not heard of the names of the other two students in the video.

[Progress] Multiple Students Admit Involvement

On the afternoon of the third day, the reporter called the principal's office at Shunyi No. 5 Middle School, attempting to verify the identities of the three students and the "Pants-Down Scandal" incident. A teacher answering the phone said that the school was aware of the matter but only knew details through the internet. The school was currently investigating and verifying the incident, with the police also involved.

However, five minutes later, a call from the school office of Shunyi No. 5 Middle School came to the reporter. The teacher who made the call stated that everything previously told to the reporter represented personal opinions and not those of the school. When asked if the three students were indeed students of Shunyi No. 5 Middle School, the teacher replied that they had checked the registration records from Grade 1 to Grade 3 and found no record of these three students. The District Education Commission had already intervened.

At 5 p.m. on the third day, the reporter attempted to enter the school to interview relevant responsible persons but was refused by security guards citing "the responsible person is not available and cannot be contacted." The reporter saw a person carrying a cloth bag containing materials like lesson preparation books, but when approached for inquiry, this individual denied being a teacher.

The reporter verified the matter with the Shunyi police. The police stated that they had held meetings to discuss and were conducting investigations. According to informed sources, the police had set up a temporary investigation point at an educational institution under the District Education Commission. Seven or eight students, accompanied by some parents, had been brought here for questioning.

At 6 p.m. on the third day, two police cars were parked in the courtyard of the temporary investigation site. When the reporter approached for inquiries, the gatekeeper denied that Shunyi No. 5 Middle School students were being investigated inside and refused the reporter entry.

At 10 p.m. on the third day, relevant officials from the Shunyi District Government stated that the case was still under investigation and they would inform the media of the results as soon as possible. Informed sources revealed that most of the students under control had admitted to participating in the filming of the video where a female student's clothes were forcibly removed. The police were conducting further investigations.

● Early Morning Comment

Excessive "Gates,"

Moral Impotence

□Li Yingchun

In recent times, issues surrounding teenage students have been "endless storms." A series of "gates" have captured the eyes of many while simultaneously provoking deep thought. From the "Grope Gate," "Cucumber Gate," "Swing Gate" to the current "Pants-Down Gate" incident at Shunyi No. 5 Middle School in Beijing, behaviors inappropriate for their age have surfaced in ways unimaginable, causing great shock.

These "gates" have formed around the theme of "sexuality," yet the people involved are merely students. Such combinations are what all people with conscience do not wish to see. Nevertheless, these incidents have repeatedly occurred in our reality. Whether it's "gropping," "cucumber masturbation," or even now "removing a girl's pants," all involve curiosity about sex and the issue of correct sexual psychology and concepts.

However, this series of sexual matters, magnified infinitely by the internet and exaggerated by some media, lead many to only see the "sex" aspect without understanding the underlying factors. At first glance, these "gates" seem to arise due to "problems in sex education," but upon deeper analysis, they reflect the ineffectiveness of moral education for teenagers.

There is no denying that today, the topic of sex has transitioned from initial "concealment" to becoming "commonplace." Various advertisements for sexual products are everywhere, and ambiguous scenes fill television and the internet... Ambiguity and desire are both expanding. Originally, if morality had strong constraints, such expansions wouldn't easily manifest, at least not so abruptly and harmfully to others.

This tide of ambiguity accumulates in these children's hearts, needing only a slight touch to trigger reactions beyond mere "blushing and palpitations." With so much sexual information available, the moral dams in these young minds are not yet solid enough, let alone effectively blocking the various ambiguities that stir infinite imagination. In these children's hearts, sex is no longer a taboo, and shame diminishes gradually. How can shocking actions be avoided during the surging spring tides?
