Sister Lotus angrily enters the mobile phone circle

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:10:29

Like before a movie is released, stars tour around to boost popularity. Nowadays, the mobile phone industry is becoming increasingly entertaining with endless topics. These days, it's not about avoiding trouble, but about being forgotten when there's nothing happening.

Recently, there have been many events in the IT circle. There are serious big events, such as Ballmer, who has led Microsoft for ten years, leaving (the news of which caused Microsoft's stock price to rise immediately; poor Ballmer). Baidu "ate" Nuomi, and group-buying websites entered the era of comparing fathers. There have also been many topic-worthy events recently. I won't mention that event involving a famous venture capitalist. This time, I want to talk about how Sister Lotus is entering the mobile phone industry and promoting her Goddess Phone. As the saying goes, "Wu Sangui was enraged for the beauty, and Sister Lotus angrily made a divine machine." But was Sister Lotus really angry or pretending to be?

The incident started from Lei Jun's Redmi phone. After a long buildup, a month ago, Xiaomi launched the Redmi phone targeting the low-end market. This made J.Wong, CEO of Meizu who has always disliked Xiaomi and Lei Jun, very upset. Seeing many people discussing the Redmi phone on his Meizu forum, J.Wong exploded and publicly posted a message titled "To the students in the forum who like to discuss Sister Feng, Sister Lotus, Redmi, and Xiaomi." The post was short, with the following content: "Perhaps setting lower limits will attract more attention, perhaps having no bottom line at all will make one stand out. Success often requires any means necessary. But this is not my pursuit. If you like heavy taste, please note that this is the Meizu forum. Please don't buzz around here like flies, I want to crush you."

J.Wong compared Xiaomi and Redmi to Sister Feng and Sister Lotus, sarcastically criticizing them as "lacking integrity and having no bottom line." J.Wong's actions were truly shocking. Lei Wanqiang, vice president of Xiaomi, retaliated, calling the "no bottom line" Redmi phone making Huang Zhang "bare-bottomed." However, this post did not anger Lei Jun, but instead infuriated Sister Lotus.

It's understandable that Sister Lotus was angry. She was truly caught in the crossfire this time. Honestly, J.Wong was too harsh. Consequently, Sister Lotus retaliated fiercely: "I've never heard of Meizu, nor do I know this dog called J.Wong!" and demanded an apology from Meizu.

After Sister Lotus retaliated, everyone thought there would be a showdown, but the event did not develop as outsiders expected. It ended abruptly at its climax. After posting that Weibo post at the beginning of August, Sister Lotus did not comment further on the matter on her Weibo. Whether Meizu apologized or not remains unknown.

That's how nearly a month passed quietly. Just as people were about to forget this farce, the ever-surprising Sister Lotus stirred up another wave, announcing on Weibo her intention to enter the mobile phone industry: "Making phones is like being a person; one must have conscience and integrity. Some become godfathers by making phones, some turn them into futures, and others make them into big mouths! Sister will tell you what industry conscience is. Wait for me to customize a Goddess Phone. A quad-core top configuration will only cost 1949!" Sister Lotus indirectly referred to a group of people, including J.Wong.

Is this the ruthless move Sister Lotus has been holding back for a month? According to my understanding, the actual situation is more complicated than what the public imagines, because a third party joined in, causing the event to take twists and turns. The participant was an internet phone company. They approached Sister Lotus, hoping to collaborate and launch a customized phone for her. The company's motivation is easy to understand - event marketing, and Sister Lotus could also benefit, possibly through endorsement fees or phone sales revenue sharing. I also learned about the specifications of this phone beforehand: the hardware configuration is indeed high - 5.0-inch FHD screen, dual cameras of 8 million + 13 million pixels, 6.98mm ultra-thin body, 1.5GHz quad-core CPU.

Initially a conflict, it later evolved into a commercial interest cooperation. This is the truth behind "Sister Lotus angrily creating a divine machine."

I won't evaluate this matter itself, neither belittling nor praising it. I just want to say that the mobile phone industry is becoming increasingly entertaining. Just like before a movie is released, stars go around boosting their popularity and even create irrelevant emotional entanglements related to the film, providing fans with gossip to increase attention to the movie. These are common tactics. In this day and age, it's not about avoiding incidents, but about being forgotten when there's nothing happening. Nothingness means being forgotten.

For mobile phone manufacturers, this approach is also acceptable as long as they don't cross the line. Everyone can understand and accept it. Who says mobile phone products cannot engage in cross-industry entertainment marketing? However, that old saying still holds true - one must hone internal skills. Making phones isn't a one-time deal, nor is it like a movie where you just need to lure the audience into the theater. Even if you capture high-attention marketing points to make users aware of your product, if the quality and experience of the product itself aren't solid, it will ultimately damage your brand and harm yourself.

The competition in the mobile phone industry is becoming increasingly fierce. Traditional methods may not work effectively anymore. Therefore, I don't oppose the entertainment marketing of mobile phone products, but I do oppose excessive hype without boundaries and detached from reality.
