Author: Lian Kuoru. The author is a famous storytelling artist, and listeners once praised him, saying: "Thousands of households listen to storytelling, and the streets are cleared for Lian Kuoru." Writing is really an unintentional act that brings unexpected results. Many professional writers find writing very tedious. On the contrary, many people who don't make a living by pen or have not received much education can write beautiful articles. For example, Mei Lanfang's stage self-narrative, Gai Jiaotian's autobiography, and this book "Jianghu Congtan" by Lian Kuoru. More recently, there are also some nostalgic talks by Mr. Yu Shizhi.

This book is not a memoir but should be classified as documentary literature because the articles in it were serialized columns published in the 1930s under the pen name "Yun Youke" in the Beiping "Shiyan Newspaper". Therefore, its narrative attitude is quite immediate.

Many people who read this book praise the author's mature writing style, his engaging storytelling, and the profound charm hidden within. In my view, there is a certain degree of youthful vigor beneath the surface. From the original intention of writing, it is so - being a person from the Jianghu (underworld), he rebelled against the rules of the underworld by revealing all the "Chun Dian" (secret jargon). "Chun Dian" is the secret language of those who live in the underworld. As the old saying goes, "spare no gold, but do not let go of a word of Chun," according to the old rules of the underworld, if these "Chun Dian" were known by outsiders, business would be ruined. The author explained his "great rebellion" in this way: "Always with the aim of protecting the majority, exposing the dark deeds of the minority, eliminating harm for the masses, and showing my loyalty to society!" Such forward-thinking and rebelliousness is understandable, as the author was only about 30 years old at the time.

The reissue of this book today clarifies the concept of "Jianghu". In the eyes of modern people, the so-called Jianghu started from the movie "Shaolin Temple", filled with countless martial arts films and TV series featuring fights, discussions on Mount Hua, and claims of kingship and supremacy. At the very least, it must involve seizing mountain strongholds and having concubines... It seems that without bloodshed, it does not deserve to be called Jianghu. Fortune-tellers, face readers, medicine sellers, acrobats, magicians, bodyguards, performers, storytellers, crosstalk artists, drum singers, etc., seem too trivial and mundane, unworthy of mention. However, unknown to many, each has their own secrets, ultimately summed up in two words: "human relationships." And the hardest thing to learn in this society is worldly wisdom and human relations. Writer A Cheng once said: Wang Xifeng in "Dream of the Red Chamber" is adept in human relations; that's what real Jianghu is. Thinking that fighting and killing is Jianghu? That's called being a bandit. That's exactly the point.

With social development, the trend towards global unity becomes increasingly evident, and cultural people naturally develop a sense of fear, hoping to find the most unique things in their own culture, so as to distinguish themselves from the rest. For instance, the Olympics will be held in Beijing, and friends come from afar. What should we show them? What should they listen to? Rock music? Movies? Obviously, these are like showing off one's skills before an expert. Then where lies the characteristic? Many people turn their eyes to the once glorious folk art, such as opera, crosstalk, and folk songs, etc. Is this book currently favored by many sensitive cultural people due to this factor?

Recently, every weekend, some cultural people, who are always known for not following the crowd, will punctually head to the Tianqiao Le Teahouse to listen to the crosstalk organized by Guo Degang's "Deyun She". This vulgar crosstalk was originally the favorite of taxi drivers (I once saw a taxi driver who was fined 200 yuan for making a phone call while driving to invite a buddy to listen to crosstalk, enough to supply ten people to listen to a crosstalk performance), now it has become the new pursuit of cultural people, which subtly proves another significance of the reissue of the old book "Jianghu Congtan".
