Hunan Yiyang frequently occurs karst collapse, emerging 693 sinkholes

by abcfofas on 2012-02-27 17:17:36

Yiyang News - Since the beginning of this year, frequent karst collapse geological disasters have occurred in 9 administrative villages in Yuezhaqiao Town, Heshan District, Yiyang City, Hunan Province: cracked residential houses, river channel cut-offs, dried-up ponds and dams, damaged farmland. These issues have drawn high attention from society and local governments. The Yiyang municipal government held a press conference yesterday to report on the situation of the karst collapse geological disaster in Yuezhaqiao Town (www.baode.cc[http://www.baode.cc]). From January last year to February 24 this year, within less than two months, large-scale karst collapse geological disasters occurred in villages such as Yuezhaqiao, Huangbanqiao, and Xizhaoping in Yuezhaqiao Town. Subsequently, more than 200 new cases of farmland collapse, 56 households with cracked houses (five severely affected becoming dangerous structures), and over 20 river collapses were reported (www.premierclean.com.cn[http://www.premierclean.com.cn]). As of now, a total of 693 "sky holes" have appeared across the nine administrative villages in the town [Car rental prices in Guangzhou http://www.syzc020.com], including 537 farmland collapses, 150 river and stream collapses, and 6 reservoir collapses. Throughout the town, 167 households have experienced house cracking due to ground karst collapse, with 34 households experiencing severe damage affecting over 1200 people; five river and stream barriers and two reservoirs have been damaged, impacting irrigation for over 10,000 acres of farmland, causing the main flow of the Quanjiao River to dry up, and putting the daily lives of some residents along the riverbanks into difficulty (www.tingfeier.com[http://www.tingfeier.com]).

Currently, warning signs have been set up at the collapsed areas in Yuezhaqiao Town, Heshan District, and guardrails have been placed around the collapsed river channels and caves. Town and village cadres have formed teams [Self-driving car rentals in Guangzhou http://www.syzc020.com] responsible for specific regions and households, conducting 24-hour patrols and monitoring, keeping track of changes in the disaster situation, and meticulously recording fixed-point monitoring data. For severely damaged houses, compulsory evacuation measures have been taken, with five households already evacuated from their dangerous homes. Water conservancy and geological departments will formulate plans to restore water conservancy infrastructure based on geological survey results within a week. The housing construction department has suspended construction and economic project development in the affected areas, while the land resources department has initiated geological disaster surveys in Yuezhaqiao Town. Technical personnel from the No. 414 Geological Survey Team have entered the villages to conduct investigations, with preliminary investigation reports expected within four days.

Restoration of water facilities within one week to ensure spring plowing

According to Jingshi News, after four days of investigation, it was confirmed that the number of "sky holes" in Yuezhaqiao Town, Yiyang, reached nearly 700. On the afternoon of the previous day, Mayor Hu Zhongxiong of Yiyang rushed to Yuezhaqiao Town for an inspection, demanding zero casualties and the restoration of water facilities within a week to ensure spring plowing (www.syzc020.com[http://www.syzc020.com]).

As an engineer dispatched by the Provincial Department of Land and Resources, Yi Xinmin spent four days traveling through Yuezhaqiao Town. According to the requirements, the team of 25 members responsible for geological disaster investigations must complete the engineering inspection report within a week. Yi Xinmin revealed that the field investigation is temporarily concluding, and further investigations will be conducted targeting the entire collapsed area. Over just four days, the inspection team identified problems at over 110 investigation points, confirming 693 instances of issues in Yuezhaqiao Town. The causes of the disasters require comprehensive analysis.

The "sky holes" have drawn significant attention from the Yiyang Municipal Party Committee and Government. On the afternoon of the previous day, Mayor Hu Zhongxiong rushed to Yuezhaqiao Town, establishing the Yuezhaqiao Geological Disaster Emergency Response Steering Group and serving as its leader. Local housing construction departments have sent staff to eliminate hazardous housing risks (www.nanyun.cc[http://www.nanyun.cc]).

Large-scale karst collapses have occurred multiple times since 1979.

Yuezhaqiao Town in Yiyang City is located at the intersection of Heshan, Ningxiang, and Taojiang counties (districts). It features mountainous and hilly terrain, abundant limestone deposits, and widespread underground caves with frequent activity. Large-scale karst collapse geological disasters have occurred multiple times in the town since 1979 (www.itsen.net[http://www.itsen.net]). In October 1979, dozens of surface cracks appeared in Gaolian and Tongguang villages near the Ningxiang Meitanba mining area, resulting in serious cracks in over 40 residential buildings. In August 1985, karst collapse caused the Songtang Reservoir, with a capacity of over 1 million cubic meters, to suddenly dry up overnight; in December of the same year, the Liujia Dam Reservoir also collapsed due to karst subsidence, creating a dam base gap. From October 2002 to November 2011, large-scale karst collapses occurred multiple times in Dadaquan and Yuezhaqiao villages, with some collapsed sinkholes being bottomless and the largest having an area exceeding 200 square meters. This resulted in surface river cutoffs, dried fish ponds, damaged farmland, and over 110 residential buildings severely cracked to the point of becoming dangerous structures. Drinking water for humans and livestock in Gaolian village had to be transported by fire trucks at one point.
