Abstract: The company is a leading enterprise in the modified plastics industry, benefiting from the rapid development of downstream industries. Its performance continues to improve. Moreover, the major shareholder, Yuan Zhimin, is a prominent figure among the Forbes billionaires and has strong capital operation capabilities. The company has a broad future growth potential.

With the continued improvement of domestic and international macroeconomic conditions, enterprises with internal demand-driven effects, high-cost-performance products, and certain brand advantages will achieve rapid development during the economic recovery phase. As a new material industry that the state's "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" focuses on encouraging for development, the modified plastics industry benefits from the rapid development of downstream home appliance and automobile industries, showing a significant trend of profit recovery. For instance, Jinfa Technology (600143), as a leader in the modified plastics industry, relies on its scale and technical advantages to maintain continuous high-speed growth in sales. The major shareholder, Yuan Zhimin, is a notable figure among the Forbes billionaires and possesses strong capital operation capabilities, indicating a vast future growth space for the company.

Modified Plastics Leader; Broad Prospects for New Materials

The company is China's largest producer of modified plastics and one of the global enterprises with the most comprehensive range of modified plastic varieties. Relying on a top-tier domestic R&D platform, it has developed seven series of proprietary intellectual property products, including flame-retardant resins and reinforced toughened resins, achieving an annual production capacity of 8 million tons of modified plastics. As a new material industry that the state's "Eleventh Five-Year Plan" emphasizes encouraging for development, the domestic modified plastics market is growing at an annual rate of 10%. Leveraging its scale and technical advantages, the company has maintained continuous high-speed growth in sales. By September 2009, Jinfa Technology's total assets reached 7.027 billion yuan. With the rapid development of the global economy, "plastic instead of steel" and "plastic instead of wood" have become new trends in the global new materials industry. As the vanguard of China's modified plastics, Jinfa Technology adheres to modified plastics as its main business, vigorously implementing standardization, informatization, and internationalization of the enterprise, composing a grand chapter of "creating world brands and building a century-old Jinfa."

Benefiting from Improved Demand; Promising Performance Growth

With the support of national policies for downstream industries, the home appliance and automobile industries are experiencing rapid growth, and the company's fourth-quarter performance is expected to continue improving. Approximately 40% of the company's sales revenue comes from the home appliance sector, and about 20% from the automobile sector. From quarterly data, the company achieved operating revenue of 1.982 billion yuan in the third quarter, a year-on-year decrease of 10.7%, but a sequential increase of 18.3%. The fourth quarter is traditionally the peak season for home appliance sales, and the country also plans to further increase the intensity of the "home appliances to the countryside" initiative. China's automobile industry continues to maintain robust production and sales, with automobile sector demand possibly exceeding expectations. The sustained rapid growth of downstream industries drives the demand for the company's products, and performance is expected to continuously improve. Meanwhile, the company actively enters the high-end product market, possessing long-term investment value. Currently, the company has developed high-end products such as high-temperature nylon and fully biodegradable plastics, with good trial results downstream and currently being promoted; carbon fiber is still in the early stages of development. These advanced material products align with the future development direction of China's petrochemical industry and will all become strong future growth points for the company's performance. Additionally, it is worth noting that the major shareholder, Yuan Zhimin, is a prominent figure among the Forbes billionaires. Besides controlling this company, he also holds stakes in Zhiguang Electric and Lucky Film through his subsidiary Chengxin Entrepreneurship, showcasing strong capital market operation capabilities. As a relatively low-concentration modified plastics industry, the major shareholder's efforts to expand the main business could potentially lead to increased investment or mergers and acquisitions to raise market share, offering a large future growth space for the company.

In the secondary market, the stock has recently been steadily rising, with the stock price bottom gradually increasing, maintaining a sound upward trend. Today, the 5-day, 10-day, and 20-day moving averages converge, with trading volume significantly amplifying, indicating active trading and demonstrating strong intentions by major players to increase positions. Future accelerated gains can be anticipated, making this stock worthy of investor attention.
