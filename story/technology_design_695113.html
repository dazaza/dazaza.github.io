Because the early symptoms of brain atrophy are mild, develop slowly, and are not very obvious, they can last for several years and are not easily noticed by patients and their families. They are often considered common phenomena in the elderly and are easily overlooked. Symptoms include:

1. Increased uncoordinated sensations in limbs and muscles, with significant movement coordination problems. This is one of the symptoms of mid-stage brain atrophy.

2. Tongue-tied, unclear speech, and difficulty writing are also symptoms of mid-stage brain atrophy.

3. Inability to control posture and gait, walking like a penguin; wobbling, legs slightly apart or scissoring gait are also symptoms of mid-stage brain atrophy.

4. Symptoms of mid-stage brain atrophy also include choking while eating or drinking water.

5. Inability to maintain balance and difficulty walking long distances are also symptoms of mid-stage brain atrophy.

6. Inability to run, difficulty going up and down stairs, inability to adjust body flexibly while walking, and frequent falls.
