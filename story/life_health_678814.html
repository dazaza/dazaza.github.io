Experts talk about chronic myelomonocytic leukemia

by laomao741 on 2012-02-27 16:44:01

The incidence of chronic myelomonocytic leukemia (CMML) is approximately 1-2 per 100,000 people per year, with a median age of onset between 65 and 75 years. The male-to-female ratio is about 1.5 to 3:1. The specific cause remains unclear, but ionizing radiation, occupational factors, environmental carcinogens, and toxins may be associated with the disease. CMML is a clonal disorder of bone marrow hematopoietic stem cells. Common chromosomal abnormalities in CMML patients include +8, -7/del(7q), and structural abnormalities of 12p. About 40% of patients have point mutations in the Ras gene at diagnosis or during the course of the disease.

Common clinical symptoms include fever, infection, bleeding, fatigue, weight loss, night sweats, and varying degrees of hepatosplenomegaly. Lymphadenopathy is uncommon; however, if it occurs, it indicates progression to an acute phase. The median survival for CMML patients is 20-40 months (1-100 months), with approximately 15-30% of cases progressing to acute leukemia. Factors such as splenomegaly, severity of anemia, and leukocytosis are important prognostic indicators. However, the proportion of blast cells in peripheral blood and bone marrow is the most critical factor determining survival.

### Diagnostic Criteria for Chronic Myelomonocytic Leukemia:

1. Persistent peripheral blood monocytosis > 1 × 10^9/L.

2. Ph(-), BCR/ABL(-).

3. Peripheral blood or bone marrow blasts (including myeloblasts, monoblasts, and immature monocytes) < 20%.

4. Dysplasia in one or more myeloid cell lineages. If dysplasia is absent or minimal, but other criteria are met, and any of the following are present, a diagnosis of CMML can still be made:

- Bone marrow cells exhibit acquired cytogenetic clonal abnormalities.

- Monocytosis has persisted for more than 3 months.

- All causes of reactive monocytosis have been excluded.

5. Peripheral blood blasts < 5% and bone marrow blasts < 10%: Diagnosis is CMML-1.

Peripheral blood blasts 5%-19% or bone marrow blasts 10%-19%: Diagnosis is CMML-2.

6. Patients meeting the above diagnostic criteria with eosinophils ≥ 1.5 × 10^9/L in peripheral blood are diagnosed with CMML-1 or CMML-2 with eosinophilia.
