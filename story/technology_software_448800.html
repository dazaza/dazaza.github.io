Grade 10 students, unhappy with the disbandment of their class, attack the campus network

by zzf0zxyh00 on 2011-06-20 10:43:41

Clicking on Xinghua 400 Lifestyle Network displays a blog with "Plants vs Zombies" as its background.

The founder of the famous social networking site Facebook, Zuckerberg, once played the role of a hacker when he was in his second year of college. He infiltrated Harvard University's student records database and used students' photos on his own website for public comments. Now, there is also an "unruly" student in Xinghua, Jiangsu, who is even younger than Zuckerberg when he got into trouble.

Here's what happened: At the end of April, the second-year classes at Xinghua No.1 High School were reorganized, and Class 23 was to be split up. A male student then used hacking techniques to successively attack the "Xinghua No.1 High School Campus Network" and the "Xinghua 400 Lifestyle Network" to express dissatisfaction with the school's decision. Afterwards, this student restored the campus network and expressed apologies. Regarding this incident, the school stated that they did not impose any punishment on the involved student but hoped that teachers would give him more care. The person in charge of the attacked Xinghua 400 Lifestyle Network also said that they would not pursue legal responsibility against this post-90s hacker.

By Reporter Zhang Yu

News Event

Xinghua local website was "hijacked" for two hours

At around 11 PM on May 12th, Xinghua's largest local lifestyle service website - Xinghua 400 Lifestyle Network was suddenly hijacked by a hacker. When netizens clicked on the website, what appeared was a personal blog with "Plants vs Zombies" as its background, titled "Xinghua No.1 High School". The blogger wrote in a blog post: "Firstly, I deeply apologize to the website administrator. Hijacking the Xinghua Lifestyle Network, we had no ill intentions. There are just too few places where we can speak out. As ordinary high school students, we want everyone to hear our voices..."

The blogger claimed to be a student from Class 23, Grade 2 of Xinghua No.1 High School. Due to the school's newly established art class, his Class 23 was dissolved on April 27th. Many classmates expressed dissatisfaction, and this hacker was particularly emotional. He hijacked the Xinghua Lifestyle Network and used it as a platform to vent his dissatisfaction.

"Our staff noticed immediately after the attack occurred that night," Mr. Liu, the head of Xinghua Lifestyle Network, told reporters. They subsequently received reports from netizens that the hacker redirected the domain name to his personal blog. "In fact, the hacker is a sophomore student, and he has apologized. He said that because our website has the highest traffic in Xinghua, hijacking this website is to make everyone pay attention to the school affairs." Mr. Liu said that after the website was hijacked, he immediately organized technical personnel to handle the fault, and the website returned to normal after two hours.

The Hacker

Live streaming via Weibo

The entire "hijacking" process

Yesterday, while searching online, the reporter unexpectedly discovered the personal Weibo and blog address of this post-90s hacker. He used his blog to record the process of hijacking the website. From the records, it appears that he not only hijacked the Xinghua 400 Lifestyle Network but also the Xinghua No.1 High School campus network. The young hacker said: "In fact, I had no malicious intent, and I hope not to be held accountable. I just wanted to express my feelings."

April 27th: Class 23 was disbanded.

April 28th: We cannot allow a class without a single group photo to be disbanded.

April 29th: A confrontation is brewing among the students of the forever Class 23.

May 1st: Only after leaving do you realize how harmonious the original class was; I did nothing wrong.

May 5th: Last night, I successfully infiltrated the email account of the school website administrator and hijacked the school website domain.

May 9th: (Re-hijacked Xinghua No.1 High School website) Decided to jointly hijack the school domain with Class 23, Grade 2 of Xinghua No.1 High School.

May 12th: Members of CLASS23 will fight to the end! No compromise, no fear, no giving up, no regret.

May 14th: Hijacked the Xinghua Lifestyle Network www.400.com.cn domain.

May 14th: Didn't expect such a big reaction, so decided to stop.

All parties' attitudes

Website does not pursue, school says child was not punished

Back then, Zuckerberg faced severe criticism from Harvard authorities after causing trouble. Nowadays, this young hacker from Xinghua is much luckier.

Yesterday afternoon, the reporter contacted this post-90s hacker online. He said he hopes this matter will pass. "Our current homeroom teacher is very nice to us."

Mr. Liu, the head of Xinghua 400 Lifestyle Network, said, "This hacker later added me on QQ and even called my mobile phone via network phone, saying that even if we hadn't noticed, he would have quickly changed the domain back. Considering that he is still a student and hasn't caused significant impact on the website, we didn't pursue legal responsibility."

Yesterday afternoon, Vice Principal Cao of Xinghua No.1 High School told reporters that this post-90s hacker is a very talented student. "This student is already attending classes normally in his new class. Although he attacked the school website, the school did not impose any punishment on him."

Vice Principal Cao said, "After he hijacked the school domain, his classmates advised him, telling him that what he did was wrong, and this student quickly restored the school website." Vice Principal Cao said that disbanding Class 23, Grade 2 wasn't because the class was bad. Previously, the school reorganized the second-year classes, and since there were many students in this class choosing other subjects, leaving too few people behind, the school decided to dissolve their class and reorganize an art class. "Now all the students have been assigned to three other classes and are attending classes normally."

When the reporter contacted the local police in Xinghua yesterday, an officer told reporters that they had already contacted this student: "We hope he can voluntarily come and explain the situation, recognize his mistakes, and we will only criticize and educate him."
