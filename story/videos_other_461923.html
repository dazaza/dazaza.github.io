Vegetable base sells vegetables at low prices in the market, and dozens of peers jointly strike

by zxyhszzfz on 2011-07-13 13:10:42

Dozens of vegetable stalls in the market that went on strike were covered with white cloth.

Changzhou Ju Hong Comprehensive Market is located in the Shifu Neighborhood Community in Xinbei District, meeting the "vegetable basket" needs of tens of thousands of residents in nearby neighborhoods. At 8 o'clock yesterday morning, dozens of vegetable stalls in the market jointly "went on strike", because a vegetable sales point from a Liyang vegetable base entered the market, and the vegetable prices were extremely low, affecting the business of other vegetable stalls. Relevant persons from government departments said that under the background of high vegetable prices, the appearance of low-priced vegetables was a good thing.

□Fast reporter Liu JinSong / text and photos

Strange: Vegetable stalls have vegetables but don't sell them

"How much does bok choy cost per pound?" "Not selling, you can check at other stalls," an old grandmother received the same reply at several stalls. "Having vegetables but not selling them is really strange." The old grandmother muttered.

The reporter observed on-site that except for a few fish and meat stalls still operating, dozens of vegetable stalls were covered with white cloth. The stall owners were either chatting or reading newspapers.

In sharp contrast to the cold atmosphere of the entire market, there was a lively scene at a stall in the center of the market. The reporter noticed that all the staff at this stall wore green uniforms with the name of a certain vegetable base written on their chest.

"All the other vendors have gone on strike," Aunt Wang, who often shopped here, told the reporter. Because this vegetable stall sold vegetables at a lower price, it caused the "collective anger" of other vendors. Since the morning of the 24th, except for this one, the rest of the vegetable stalls in the market had vegetables but didn't sell them.

Reason: Low-priced vegetables in the market

The reporter learned that the stall named "Green Zhou Hundred Vegetable Garden" started operating vegetable business in the market on Tuesday. Zhang Mou, the person in charge of the stall, told the reporter that "Green Zhou Hundred Vegetable Garden" was a large vegetable base in Liyang, planting thousands of acres of vegetables. Recently, the base has been expanding its Changzhou market and began setting up stalls in various markets to sell various vegetables produced by the base.

"Since Tuesday, we have started selling in the market. In order to give benefits to the residents, we adopted some promotional measures, such as giving away soy sauce when buying vegetables, buy one get one free, etc., besides, our vegetable prices are relatively low. We originally thought this was a good thing, but unexpectedly it brought a big problem." The reporter learned that the current vegetable prices at this stall are much lower than those at other stalls in the market. For example, leeks are sold for 2.5 yuan per pound at this stall, while other stalls sell them for 4 yuan per pound; bok choy is sold for 1 yuan per pound at this stall, while other stalls sell them for 1.5-2 yuan per pound. Additionally, vegetables like water bamboo shoots, lettuce, celery, and lettuce are all priced 0.5-1 yuan lower than other stalls.

Zhang Mou believed that due to the low price, they faced opposition from other stall owners in the market, "They demanded that we withdraw from the market. On Tuesday afternoon, our stall was attacked by vegetable monopolists, the stall was smashed, and the vegetables were destroyed."

According to Zhang Mou, those involved in the smashing were a few old operators in the market, and the strike originated from their instructions.

Stall owners: It's unfair competition

An Anhui-based stall owner explained the reason for the "strike" to the reporter. "Our reason for striking is very simple, because the new stall disrupted the rules. We all came to Changzhou to sell vegetables with our families, and our entire family relies on this stall for a living. Once it arrived, everyone went to buy vegetables from them, what should we do?"

Another Shandong-based stall owner believed that the strike was not instigated by anyone, but rather everyone had opinions about the "Green Zhou Hundred Vegetable Garden," and their actions affected everyone's business. He also believed that the purpose of the new stall entering the market was not pure. For instance, the wholesale price of bok choy is around 0.8-0.9 yuan per pound, and with transportation costs and stall rent, it must be sold for 1.5 yuan to break even. How could they sell it for only 1 yuan per pound? "They just want to use low-price promotions to put other stalls out of business and eventually achieve monopoly operations, because they have enough financial resources to do so, while we are small individuals, how can we compete with them?" This stall owner expressed that since it was unfair competition, everyone's common idea was "either him or us, if he doesn't withdraw, then we will."

Residents: We support this stall

However, the residents buying vegetables did not agree with these stall owners' claims. A resident said, "Nowadays, vegetable prices are so high, being able to buy cheap vegetables is a good thing for us, so we all support the new stall." To show support, some residents even engaged in verbal arguments with the striking stall owners.

The staff of the "Green Zhou Hundred Vegetable Garden" believed that there was no such thing as unfair competition.
