Baidu Gate incident

by langmzz on 2008-11-17 20:24:51

In the past two days, CCTV's "30 Minutes News" has consecutively reported for two days on the dark side of Baidu's bidding rank system. It is pointed out that Baidu's bidding rank system interferes too much with search results manually, leading to spam information and malicious blocking, which has been referred to as "extortion marketing". This has sparked public doubts about its information fairness and business ethics.

In the program broadcast on the 15th, when a reporter from CCTV entered the keyword "tumor" for a search, the website ranked first was named "China Anti-Cancer Network". On its homepage, it recommended Professor Bai Xihé, who had multiple titles including Chief Expert in Oncology at the China Academy of Chinese Medical Sciences, Senior Professor, and Invited Professor of the Oncology Committee of the Chinese Medical Association. After verification, Tian Yi, the secretary of the president of the China Academy of Chinese Medical Sciences, stated that the academy never had any Chief Expert in Oncology; Cui Xinsheng from the Organizational Management Department of the Chinese Medical Association also said that the association did not have this invited professor.

In the program aired on the 16th, the CCTV reporter entered Baidu's bidding query webpage and inputted "treating sexually transmitted diseases" in the bidding query bar, resulting in over 100 enterprises bidding on the keyword "treating sexually transmitted diseases". The fifth-ranked one was a fake military hospital: the website of the "Hospital of the General Department of the General Staff", whose bid was to pay 16.56 yuan per click.

What does this mean? Simply put, no matter what product you make or service you provide, even if it is harmful or toxic, as long as you give Baidu money, people will see your product or service among the top few when they search for similar products or services on Baidu. Frankly speaking, it's a kind of vicious deception!

This year can be called a season of troubles for Chinese national enterprises. First came the shocking domestic and international Sanlu "toxic milk powder" incident, followed by a large number of dairy industry national enterprises such as "Yili" and "Mengniu" being pushed to the forefront. Now, the dark side of Baidu's bidding rank system has been exposed, truly one wave after another. Baidu has always made netizens proud because it successfully prevented GOOGLE from gaining market share in China's Internet industry. However, after CCTV exposed the "dark side of the bidding rank system," it became a target for criticism overnight.

In fact, insiders would not be unaware of the underhanded operations of Baidu's bidding rank system, especially Baidu's fierce rival GOOGLE, which also does bidding rank, wouldn't be unaware of the tricks involved. It's just that due to some reasons, perhaps the unspoken rules, they themselves are not clean, so they haven't taken action against their opponents.

Premier Wen Jiabao has repeatedly emphasized that "enterprises should have moral blood flowing through them," but many of China's national enterprises suffer from overall moral deficiencies.

As a part of the network industry, we call on those network giants to take on national responsibilities and restore China's Internet to a pure land.
