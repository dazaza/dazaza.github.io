Six Longevity Secrets from Qiumed Network

by lokes350 on 2012-03-08 15:49:54

1. No hourly worker at home

A survey study of 302 adults showed: spending just one hour a day cleaning the room, mopping the floor, and wiping windows allows an average person to burn 285 calories, reducing the likelihood of death by 30%.

2. Brave enough to accept challenges

Scientific research shows: people with strong self-discipline who are organized and methodical tend to live longer. They are 89% less likely to develop dementia compared to those who are less responsible. If you're good at focusing, your intelligence will be more developed and utilized to a deeper extent. Therefore, individuals need to set personal or career goals and then face various difficulties and challenges to achieve these goals. Continuously trying new things to stimulate the brain is also important: if you always read science fiction novels, try choosing a biography to read.

3. Having close friends

Good interpersonal relationships can help alleviate stress. A man with three helpers is stronger; if someone has support behind them, they will be healthier both mentally and physically. Studies show that long-term chronic stress weakens the immune system, accelerates cell aging, and ultimately shortens life by 4-8 years.

4. Attending college may also lead to a longer life

Recent research from Harvard Medical School shows: people who have received more than 12 years of formal education (even if they only attended college for a year) live on average 18 months longer than those with less education. The reason lies in the fact that the higher the level of education, the lower the likelihood of smoking. According to statistics from the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, only 10% of people with a bachelor's degree or higher smoke, while 35% of those with only a high school diploma or less smoke.

5. Keep up with the times

Familiarizing yourself with new technologies helps keep brain cells young and healthy. Many American seniors use email for daily communication, search online for old friends they haven't contacted for years, and even date through online platforms. More interaction with new technologies, as well as with family, friends, and society, keeps the elderly mentally sharp and energetic.

6. Avoid traffic jams

The latest study in Germany shows: encountering traffic jams increases the likelihood of a heart attack by three times. Although the relationship between the two is still uncertain, scientists speculate that air pollution caused by continuous car traffic and the stress brought on by traffic jams increase the likelihood of heart attacks.
