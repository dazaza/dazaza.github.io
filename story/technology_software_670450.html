College students fabricating official seals to impersonate the president to send weibo were detained for 5 days

Faking the school seal to impersonate the principal and send Weibo posts: Fan and others have confessed without reservation to their prostitution activities carried out through online solicitation. Currently, three people including Fan have been criminally detained according to law, while four people including Wen have been administratively detained and fined according to law.

A network solicitation gang was dismantled. The Zengcheng police formed a special task force to crack down on online pornography, comprehensively investigating clues and cases involving solicitation information posted under the guise of "massage, leisure center" on the internet, digging deep into them. After continuous investigation, they discovered the situation of a network solicitation gang located in Xintang Town, Zengcheng City, which was conducting illegal operations from Room 603 of a building in Yatai New City. In the early morning of July 25th, the special task force conducted a surprise operation, destroying the hideout of the network solicitation gang, catching three members including Fan at Room 603 of a building in Yatai New City. At the same time, another team of the special task force quickly caught Wen and Zeng who were engaged in prostitution at a hotel in Wushan, Tianhe District, and Li and Yang who were also engaged in prostitution at a motel on Chisha Road in Haizhu District.

Since the establishment of a national cultural city this year, Guangzhou police have greatly strengthened the information security patrol of websites, forums, blogs and other online public places, clearing all kinds of illegal, harmful and vulgar information online locally, clearing more than 3 million pieces of bad information such as obscenity and gambling, and investigating six lawbreakers (corrected to investigate 34 units/persons for illegal internet use).

Among these, the most notable case is that the Guangzhou police investigated the first instance of impersonating an official Weibo account to release false information. According to relevant officials of the Guangzhou Public Security Bureau, at the end of June this year, the police received a report from a university stating that someone had registered an official Weibo account in the name of the university on a famous website, and released false information under the name of the school's leadership, disrupting the normal teaching order of the school and causing adverse social effects.

Starting from the beginning of July, Guangzhou police organized a special operation across the city to severely crack down on obscene and pornographic criminal activities on the internet. During the operation, the Zengcheng police dismantled a suspected online solicitation gang and arrested seven suspects.

The Guangzhou police have installed "virtual police" and "virtual booths" alarm icons or network links in various important online venues locally, assigning dedicated personnel to handle online reports, answer netizens' consultations and requests for help, and deal with illegal and criminal information according to law, ensuring expanded police presence online, public duties by net police, and increased visibility and efficiency of police online. It is understood that since the installation of "virtual police", they have answered over 8,600 inquiries from netizens and handled over 3,000 pieces of illegal and criminal information.

According to Information Times (reporter He Huagao, correspondent Xing Zengxi, Zhang Yitao), Guangzhou police have investigated for the first time a case where someone impersonated an official Weibo account and released false information under someone else's name. A first-year student at a Guangzhou university, Ruan, was given a warning and five days of administrative detention. The reporter learned that during the creation of a national civilized city, Guangzhou police strictly implemented network supervision measures, standardized the operating order of internet cafes, severely cracked down on "black internet cafes", and intensified efforts to clean up harmful information, strengthening the network security barrier.

After investigation by the Guangzhou police, it was found that this was done by Ruan, a 2010-level student of the university. Ruan admitted that due to dissatisfaction with some management systems of his university, he wanted to attract the attention of the school, so in November 2010, he registered a Weibo account for his university, and in May this year, he bought a seal with the university's name online, forged an official certification application letter, obtained the Weibo certification of the website, then released 16 fabricated messages under the name of the university leadership, and uploaded multiple obscene and pornographic pictures to the Weibo account. Ruan's behavior violated relevant provisions of the "Law of the People's Republic of China on Public Security Administration Punishments" and the "Regulations on the Protection of Computer Information System Security of Guangdong Province". On July 8th, the Guangzhou police gave Ruan a warning, five days of administrative detention, and a fine of 500 yuan.
