What diseases should be differentiated from vulvar leukoplakia?

by bailingdanyanshu on 2012-02-22 14:34:30

● Vulvar Leukoplakia

It mostly occurs in women around middle age or peri-menopause. The lesions often involve the inner half of the labia minora, labia majora, clitoris, clitoral prepuce, and perineum, but do not occur on the vaginal wall, vestibule of vagina, or around the anus. At the onset of the disease, there is an itching sensation in the vulva, followed by local swelling. Occasionally, there are a few flakes on the surface, and the color gradually turns white, but atrophy is not obvious. In the late stage of the lesion, the skin appears pearl-white and has a parchment-like surface. Sometimes, the labia minora and majora shrink, making the vaginal opening narrower and causing dyspareunia. This condition usually occurs bilaterally and symmetrically.

● Lichen Sclerosis (abbreviated as LS)

It can occur at any age, including in young girls. It commonly affects the vulva, the vestibule of the vagina, and the area around the anus. Therefore, the white areas sometimes appear in a figure-eight shape or hourglass shape, with dilated capillaries and scattered purpura on the surface. Initially, the lesions are light pink, then gradually turn white accompanied by itching. As the disease progresses, the skin becomes atrophic and thin. Severe cases may have partial adhesion of the labia minora on both sides, making the vaginal opening smaller, leading to urinary and sexual intercourse difficulties. This condition can also occur in other parts of the body, such as the neck, trunk, and forearms.

● Chronic Dermatitis

Before chronic dermatitis occurs, there is often an acute inflammatory process, which causes itching, pain, and swelling in the vulva, then it turns into a chronic condition. There may be no acute inflammation process, and it happens unconsciously. This condition is common in middle-aged and elderly women. The lesions often involve the labia minora, the inner half of the labia majora, clitoris, and clitoral prepuce. The discoloration is not very severe. The surface skin is often grayish-white or yellowish-white, with thickened epidermis. The atrophy is not serious, and it usually occurs bilaterally and symmetrically. If left untreated, the skin will gradually become thicker and rougher, eventually developing into lichenification.

● Neurodermatitis

Besides the vulva, this condition can also occur on the neck and limbs, with similar lesions. When it occurs on the vulva, the affected area may extend to the outer side of the labia majora, mons pubis, and around the anus. Patients often feel itchy inside the pubic hair. The lesions are mostly grayish-white, with thickened and hypertrophic epidermis. Over time, they develop into lichenification, with severe itching. Sometimes, fine flakes can be seen on the surface. The lesions usually occur asymmetrically on both sides.

● Lichen Planus

The lesions are formed by the fusion of flat papules that are white or pale pink. Besides the vulva, it can also occur in other parts of the body. When it occurs on the vulva, it often involves the labia minora, clitoral prepuce, and vestibule of the vagina. It usually occurs asymmetrically on both sides, with irregular white areas and unclear boundaries.

● Vitiligo

It can occur on any part of the body. When it only occurs on the vulva, it is easily confused with vulvar leukoplakia. The lesions are white with irregular borders. Sometimes, they are scattered and exceed the boundaries of the vulva. They occur asymmetrically on both sides. The epidermis does not thicken or atrophy, and the tissue elasticity is normal. The skin pigment around the white area is slightly darker, and the pubic hair growing on the white skin is also white. Vitiligo usually has no symptoms. If there is a secondary infection, itching occurs, and the color changes from white to pink. Under the microscope after slicing, except for the complete disappearance of pigmentation in the basal layer, all other parts are completely the same as normal skin, without any abnormalities.

● Diabetic Vulvitis

The lesions of diabetic vulvitis involve the entire vulva and the area around the anus. The texture is pinkish, and sometimes covered with fine white curd-like substances. If the curd-like substance is gently scraped off with a spatula and smeared, add 1 drop of 10% potassium hydroxide. Under the microscope, fungal hyphae and spores are often seen. Due to intense itching, scratches and ulcers are often seen on the surface. Patients often have a history of diabetes, which can be confirmed by blood and urine tests. In the late stage of the lesion, the local changes are the same as chronic dermatitis.
