In the group buying business, the traditional model of "one cooperation per deal" with merchants is beginning to change: In October, Wowotuo launched the 55 Life Mall first, leading the way for group buying to advance into electronic commerce for living services and also bringing new imaginative space to China’s group-buying industry. Wowotuo's "55" strategy, starting with one-stop group-buying and striving to create a "Walmart for living services," has given Wowotuo a "traditional group-buying + living service mall" dual engine. Jumei Youpin recently launched the sale of non-cosmetic products such as women's clothing, handbags, and health supplements, increasing the proportion of physical online shopping, clearly showing an intention to transform into a B2C mall. Lashou.com will launch the "Lashou Mall" on December 8th, directly charging merchants rent; Tuangou.com's previously launched "Caimi Laodao" uses a countdown-style bidding trading mechanism that feels familiarly similar to Taobao Mall, Tencent Paipai Shopping, etc.

Many group-buying websites are leveraging transformation to attract long-term offline physical store merchants to reside permanently, aiming to position themselves as platforms, making their cooperation with merchants routine, thus entering the O2O model (i.e., Online to Offline, online ordering, offline consumption). Relevant personnel from Mansuo.com also stated that they are currently exploring how to transform their business.

"Mansuo.com", "24 Coupon", "Tuanhao.com", "Geji Tuan", "Zhima Tuan", "Meihao Shenghuo Wang" and other group-buying websites have recently opened official stores on Taobao, hoping to leverage Taobao's enormous daily traffic to bring in customers for themselves through group-buying. Relevant personnel from Mansuo.com's marketing department said that this is for multi-channel promotion.

Abroad, Groupon, the pioneer of the group-buying industry, although already listed, has shown average stock price performance, revealing that even in the birthplace of the group-buying boom, the United States, group-buying websites are not performing satisfactorily, and all group-buying websites are seeking new strategies. To encourage more frequent purchases of group-buying items by customers, LivingSocial, the second-largest group-buying website in the United States, recently launched a new subscription service called "LivingSocial Plus." Members pay $20 per month to receive $25 worth of "Deal Bucks" points that can be used to buy group-buying items. However, the $5 bonus expires at the end of each monthly billing cycle, creating a sense of urgency and prompting members to purchase products quickly to avoid wasting the extra $5 discount.

Currently, China's group-buying industry has entered "winter," with the top ten group-buying websites collectively suffering losses. In order to survive, multiple group-buying websites have begun to seek transformation.
