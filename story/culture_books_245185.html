"Mayor's Driver" - A small car driver's perspective on the power struggles in the political arena.

by jqrxass on 2009-10-04 20:56:35

The driver of a government official is a special profession. Compared with the leader's secretary, they are equally close to the daily work and life of the leader and are highly valued by the leader. The difference is that the leader's driver is always outside the power circle, and sometimes does not even have a formal position. It is precisely because of this psychological imbalance that it induces the leader's driver to often transcend their own role and act beyond their authority.

The mayor's driver opens the car window for the first time to tell you the true face of the leaders, revealing the root cause of "wheels" corruption, as well as the unknown side of those agency personnel inside and outside the car...

This article differs from traditional officialdom literature. From the perspective of a small car driver, it presents in a documentary style the corruption and problems existing in today's official vehicle use, as well as the intricacies of power struggles in the officialdom.

Mayor's Driver Part One

I am just a driver. Because I come from a poor background and look honest, I was fortunate enough to be chosen by the chief to drive when I was in the army. After being discharged and returning to the place, thanks to the care of the old chief, I entered the government agency and first drove for the director. A few years later, the director became the vice mayor, and I also followed him into the government building. As the leader's position rose, so did the status of the ride. Although I am still a driver, my status has risen accordingly. At least the steering wheel is new and feels smooth to the touch. When the vice mayor was promoted, I got an unofficial nickname, "Secretary." Those bureau-level cadres put aside their official airs and began calling me "Secretary" behind my back, which was an honor I had never experienced in the army.

Mayor's Driver Part Two

To be honest, when Vice Mayor Wu sat down, I started feeling nauseous. Her perfume was too strong for my nose. At that moment, I couldn't light a cigarette to dilute the smell, how could I not feel sick? All the way, I used drinks to fill my mouth and throat. Without smoke on the road, the vision of this old driver became blurry instead, and I felt uneasy. Driving for a female leader for the first time made me think of the old man's wife.

Mayor's Driver Part Three

I deliberately broke the rules this time, running every red light, but was spotted by a motorcycle police car, which chased after us all the way, wailing its siren. Damn it, today is really unlucky, even drinking water gets stuck in the teeth. Are the traffic police on the wrong medication or something, trying to ticket me? That would be great; I'm bored anyway. Let's go to your detachment and catch up, maybe I can get a good meal, haven't had one in ages. When we were almost at the traffic police building, the guy behind me finally shut up. He must be newly hired and only looks at the lights, not recognizing the license plates. I saw him take off his helmet and wipe sweat in the mirror, making me want to laugh. I intentionally reversed the car back to him and smiled, asking: Shall we go in together? The young guy reacted quickly, knowing who I was. He saluted and smiled back: Sending Buddha home. Pretty witty, promising future!

Mayor's Driver Part Four

I simply replied: Have you ever seen a city-level leader directly go to a rural credit cooperative? Director Fat's small eyes moved, starting to show fear. He hasn't seen it either, and neither have I. The size of the problem has nothing to do with me, but I know Mayor Xiao Wu's behavior often violates the rules of officialdom. She handles everything herself, like her perfume and cheongsam, which are her characteristics. But when the fat director hears this, he naturally follows the routine. Can a small problem stir up the big mayor to personally inquire about it? Thinking this way, the small problem becomes a big one. The fat director absentmindedly chatted with me for a few sentences, then hurried away, dialing the phone while walking, looking very nervous.
