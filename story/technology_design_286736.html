Football recommendations, but if Klasic doesn't leave in the winter.

by hartyqekb on 2009-11-30 18:35:22

Last week, Mourinho said "Inter Milan is not as strong as the Barcelona on the field," which upset Moratti. Upon careful analysis, the Eto'o-Milito combination may not be as good as Ibrahimovic-Messi, but they are certainly not inferior to the aging Henry and young Pedro of that day. The Inter Milan defense with Lucio, Samuel, Maicon, and Julio Cesar is an enviable combination across all of Europe. However, it's true that Inter Milan was too passive on the field that day. Aside from Mourinho's insufficient tactical preparation, Inter Milan's midfield was indeed not as good as their opponents who had Xavi and Iniesta.

Against Fiorentina, Sneijder continued to be absent, and Inter Milan's midfield organization remained far from smooth. This season, the midfield has seen the most reinforcements among Inter Milan's three lines, with the signings of Motta and Sneijder, and the promotion of youngster Korcin. But Inter Milan's midfield still has the most room for improvement. If there's one position Inter Milan needs to continue recruiting for next season, it's the midfield.

Inter Milan has already taken action. On Saturday local time, it was reported that Inter Milan has already signed the young Ghanaian midfielder Obi Olaeh. The deal is completed and just awaits official announcement when the winter transfer window opens. This young player, who recently participated in the U-20 World Cup in Egypt and helped his team win the championship, holds dual nationality of Ghana and France. He will be available for Mourinho's call-up starting in January. Olaeh plays as a central midfielder and stands at 185 cm, making him quite promising.

Olaeh is just the beginning. There are more reports indicating that Inter Milan has joined the competition for Clarence Seedorf's Serbian international right winger, Krasic, from CSKA Moscow. His agent revealed that Manchester United, Manchester City, Arsenal, Chelsea, and AC Milan are all interested in him. However, if a winter transfer happens, in his agent's own words, it would be "90% to the Premier League." If Krasic doesn't leave in the winter, he will likely go to Italy in the summer, with everyone having equal opportunities.

Transfer news aside, before the winter break, Inter Milan still has four rounds of league matches and a crucial Champions League game. The top priority now is Sneijder's return. Unfortunately, the Dutchman's statement isn't optimistic: "It's definitely much better than at first, but I can't say exactly when I'll be back." Fortunately, there's still ten days until the Champions League match. Due to the points gap being widened to eight with Juventus, the Italian Derby actually doesn't matter as much anymore. The key is to get Sneijder back as soon as possible before the match against Kazan Rubin. From the current situation, the difference between having and not having Sneijder for Inter Milan is still significant.
