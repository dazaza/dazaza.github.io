The level of "psychological age" is closely related to factors such as genetics, personality, experiences, environment, and even recent mood. There is no absolute good or bad distinction in the highs and lows of "psychological age". For example, a person whose "psychological age" is lower than their chronological age may appear immature, which is not conducive to personal socialization, survival, and growth. However, their mindset is usually simple and happy, they enjoy participating in activities, have abundant energy, and are not conservative. On the other hand, a person whose "psychological age" is much higher than their actual age might seem mature and steady. However, due to being overly calculating, it is difficult for them to have mutual understanding and common language with people of the same age.
