Whose fault is it for illegal parking? Should we pay for the parking fee and towage fee? Is it law enforcement or a way to increase revenue?

by lesile7hl0 on 2010-04-12 13:38:09

While browsing the forum just now, I found a post like this: http://cs.rednet.cn/a/a.asp?B=3&ID=1421514. The title is "How to Park on Renmin Road in Changsha? Be Careful of Being Taken Away by the City Management". Friends who have seen it may feel very angry. But two hours ago, I also encountered something very annoying. My three friends and I decided to find a place to sit down and have a good chat because we had something to do. There is a nice leisure place on the second floor of Apollo near Changsha Railway Station, so we went there! We parked the car on the side of Jiaruomo, which was across from Apollo and where many cars were already parked (I must emphasize that there was no sign prohibiting parking nearby, and no one came to prohibit us from parking). Then we left. However, a few hours later, when we returned, the car was gone, and we were all very anxious. I believe my friend who drove the car would be even more anxious, and I deeply understand this! But we saw the words and phone number left by the traffic police who towed the car away at the parking place. At this point, our hearts were finally put at ease.

We found the traffic booth in front of KFC across the street according to the phone number. Through him, we learned that the car had been taken to the traffic police parking lot in front of the East Station on the Liuyang River Bridge. So we took a taxi over there. Sure enough, our car was parked there. After paying the towing fee of 280 yuan, the parking fee (for two days) of 30 yuan, and receiving a fine notice to pay at the bank, they let us drive the car away.

Some people might ask, if you're so dissatisfied, why did you pay the fine then?

Answer: In order to get the car back, we cooperated and paid the fine! You little citizen, could you say no at that time? As long as they can solve the problem, we can only comply with whatever they demand, and I believe most people would do the same!

After the car was towed away, I kept an eye out and took pictures of the scene at that time. Everyone can see that our car was parked next to the trash can at that time, but there were still many cars parked on site. After I finished taking pictures, a black Hyundai sedan parked in that spot. (It's really ironic.)

After writing so much, I have a few questions, and I want to ask everyone who has read the post!

1. Why was there not a single sign prohibiting parking where we parked? From the picture, when we parked, we definitely noticed that others had parked their cars, so we parked too! This shows that the place has already been recognized as a parking lot, and it hasn't been for just one or two days. Why didn't anyone manage it or put up a warning sign to remind the car owners?

2. I haven't taken the driving test, and I don't know how to drive, so I don't know! Under what circumstances does the traffic police have the authority to tow the car away? Let's cut to the chase, who should bear the cost of the towing and parking fees? I hope an authoritative leader will come out and explain to the vast number of car owners and citizens.

According to the information I checked afterwards, everyone take a look:

According to Article 93 of the "Road Traffic Safety Law": "If the motor vehicle driver is not present or is present but refuses to immediately drive away, obstructing the passage of other vehicles and pedestrians, a fine of more than twenty yuan but less than two hundred yuan shall be imposed, and the motor vehicle can be towed to a location that does not obstruct traffic or to a location designated by the public security traffic management department. The public security traffic management department shall not charge the parties for towing the vehicle, and shall promptly inform the parties of the parking location."

3. Who gave the right for the vehicle custodian to collect fees, and not even issue a fine slip afterward, but only give a fine notice to pay at the bank! Even if they can, they said it's Sunday today, and the finance staff is not around, so they collected on behalf.

After saying so much, I believe everyone will have the same question!

Of course, I must clarify one point. Today, I just felt uncomfortable after venting some unnecessary frustration. I'm not doing this for the hundreds of yuan. He can afford a car, do we care about this amount of money? It's just for a breath of air!

Similarly, I happen to work in the network field, so I have accumulated a lot of resources. This article will appear on various forums, and I only seek for someone to give us justice! Give justice to those car drivers who have been harmed by the "under-the-table rules" of these government units!
