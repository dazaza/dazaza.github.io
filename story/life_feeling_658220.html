Those Eternal Memories

This is a silent night, and I cannot fall asleep as peacefully as usual. I can't explain the reason, I can only say that I always feel this way for no apparent reason. These days have been very ordinary, and I like this kind of faint, personal life.

The temperature drops quickly at night. The wind sneaks in through the slightly open door of the bathroom, making me feel even more the chill of winter. Instinctively, I pull the quilt tighter and curl up my body to feel a moment of warmth. But I don't know if it's the meditation in my mind or real warmth? Because it's still cold outside the door, and the cold air hasn't stopped sneaking in. Also, because at this moment, I think of my mother who is far away in another place.

I remember one day. Once again, I stood on the square at night. The neon lights flashed with dazzling light, and people rushed past my line of sight. I looked up and gazed into the distance, where it seemed there was the moon of my hometown. For a long time, I thought of my family under its glow.

Our family is scattered all over the world, for life.

The north is vast and even colder. Perhaps at this moment, you are still worried and tired for the life of our family? If the moonlight shines on your face thinking of me now, please look at the twinkling star beside you, that's my heart missing you.

So many times I have walked myself into disappearance. At a dark alley corner, a figure flashes by, resembling my father's back. The heavy steps are like my father's mark in the passage of time, step by step reminding me to keep going. Watching him walking further and further away, I deeply miss my father.

The south is winding, that's where my home is, where my respected father lives. By now, he has probably collapsed in sleep after a tiring day.

Like many people's parents, they are simple, hardworking... They lead busy lives so their children can live better. Thinking about them, they are so selfless and great in their whole lives. But why does time steal my father's black hair and my mother's beauty? I am lost in deep self-reproach.

In my memory, there are often too many images of my parents, such as at this very moment. Although tears keep welling up in my eyes, I have always believed that this is a great happiness.

Similarly, every time I travel far away, I remember it clearly, and I will remember it forever. My mother's repeated instructions, my father's infinite expectations, the deep gazes time after time. The longing in their eyes weaves into threads of thoughts. We've watched each other's backs for twenty years, and what we call moving, in countless journeys and gazes, it is explained perfectly with tears.

Finally, in countless times of his departure and mine, I understand: Being father and son or mother and daughter is just watching each other's backs disappear at the end of the small road over and over in this life. And also using our backs to tell each other, don't chase...
