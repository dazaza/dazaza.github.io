From 1935 to 1937, Zhuang Xueben, an early Chinese photographer, was hired as a photographer by the special envoy office of the Nationalist Government which escorted the Panchen Lama back to Tibet. He conducted physical measurements of ethnic minorities on behalf of the Central Research Institute and collected ethnic minority cultural relics on behalf of the Sun Yat-sen Cultural Education Hall. He traveled from Nanjing to Xi'an, through Lanzhou, Xining, Guoluo, and finally to Yushu with the special envoy office. The earthquake occurred in Yushu County, located in the southern part of Qinghai Province and the eastern part of the state, bordering Sichuan Province and the Tibet Autonomous Region to the east. The altitude of Yushu County is 3700 meters, and it is predominantly Tibetan.

In 1937, photography took place in Yushu, Qinghai. Yushu, Qinghai is a semi-pastoral area for Tibetans, producing barley. At that time, most of the land was owned by local chieftains, headmen, and monasteries. Tibetan farmers had little grain left after paying rent after a year's hard work, living in poverty.

In 1937, photography took place in Yushu, Qinghai. It was customary for young Tibetan women in Yushu to apply butter oil and soot on their faces. According to legend, in ancient times, the Tibetan king, because of the beauty of the women, ordered them to apply the mixture to protect the Buddhist law, ensuring monks could adhere to their vows. Another meaning of applying the mixture is said to be: the strong winds of the plateau can dry out the skin, applying it helps to moisturize the skin.

Old photos - taken in Yushu, Qinghai in 1937. In Tibetan pastoral areas of Sichuan, Gansu, Qinghai, and Tibet, people follow ancient customs for sky burials, feeding vultures with the body. If the body is completely eaten, it is considered auspicious. Sky burial sites are specific locations. After carrying the corpse into the burial site, "burning incense" (smoke) is done, gathering flocks of vultures. When the lamas finish chanting scriptures for the deceased's soul crossing over, the corpse is revealed, and the flock of vultures rush forward to eat. After eating, they fly up into the sky, symbolizing the deceased ascending to heaven.

Yushu is located in the southwestern part of Qinghai, at an altitude of about three to four thousand meters. After autumn rain, a rainbow spans the sky, and the plateau scenery is very magnificent. The building on the mountain is Jiegu Monastery.

Jiegu Monastery, known in Tibetan as "Jiegu Dun Zhu Luo", is located on the Mutameima Mountain to the north of Jiegu Town in the Yushu Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture of Qinghai Province. It is a Sakya Sect monastery of the original Zawu tribe. Due to its great reputation and location in Jiegu Town, many famous people have visited. On the first day of the twelfth month of the Tibetan calendar in 1937, the ninth Panchen Lama, Quxi Niema, passed away at Jiegu Monastery while returning to Tibet.
