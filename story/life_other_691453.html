Survey data from Roland Berger (RolandBerger) showed that as of December 31, 2011, the market share of the top five companies in China's car rental industry was only 11%, while the market share of the top five companies in the U.S. market was as high as 95%. Many Chinese consumers still do not understand car rental. However, the car rental industry is a scale economy industry, without scale there is no economic benefit. Especially in 2011, China's car rental industry experienced several rounds of significant price cuts, leading to lower industry profit margins. The major players in the car rental industry were more eager to increase the concentration of China's car rental market.

According to the prospectus disclosed by Zipcar China, its current fleet size is 26,000 vehicles, nearly four times that of China's second-largest car rental company, and the total sum of the fleet sizes of the second to ninth largest car rental companies in the industry. This gives it a considerable scale advantage. The launch of Zipcar China's dual spokesperson plan will further enhance its influence and market share.

The IPO in the U.S. is the "driving force" behind this move.

On January 19, Zipcar China filed its IPO application with the SEC, planning to list on the New York Stock Exchange with the stock code "CARH", with JPMorgan Chase as the underwriter. The IPO proceeds will be used to repay debt and purchase vehicles. This is the first Chinese company to file an IPO application with the SEC since Tudou.com listed in the U.S. last August.

The current scale and development momentum of Zipcar Beijing undoubtedly make it the leading enterprise in China's car rental industry.

Zipcar has performed exceptionally well in the market in recent years —— with a 6 billion yuan car purchase order, not only are the models available for rent very diverse, but the condition of the vehicles also makes other car rental companies envious. Many consumers choose Zipcar not only because of reasonable rental prices, but also because there are plenty of new cars to choose from, giving them ample options.

Guangdong ZhiZun Car Rental, although claimed to be the first nationwide chain car rental company in China, has been slower than other giants in the car rental industry in terms of financing and expansion speed in recent years. Apart from being an old-established company, compared to Zipcar, ZhiZun Car Rental does not have the scale effect or cost advantage, resulting in a lack of obvious attractiveness in terms of model quantity and price structure. Under the impact of several rounds of price wars in recent years, ZhiZun Car Rental could only passively accept price reductions.
