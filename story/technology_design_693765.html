Nokia 6760S Review: Inventory of Five New Nokia Grey Market Phones, with Flagship N8 Standing Out the Most

by mxnc595 on 2012-03-06 17:59:47

In the past month, both iPhone and Android have been continuously launching new products. Since it's a Google-based system, Samsung's i9000, which came out after the Desire, is enough to rival the iPhone 4. However, Nokia, once the market leader, seems unable to resist. The few new products they've launched are mainly mid-to-low-end and basically insufficient to challenge flagship-grade gray-market phones like the iPhone 4. Now, Nokia's painstakingly crafted N8 has finally come before us, and with its launch conference held, we believe the day of its official release is not far off. Below, let's review some of the recent gray-market phone releases from Nokia.

Product Model: Nokia C5

Reference Price: 1190 RMB (Revised Edition)

Suitable for: Users accustomed to Nokia's nine-grid keyboard layout and students with limited budgets.

The Nokia C5 was announced at the beginning of the year and finally reached the Beijing market this week. This model focuses on affordable 3G smart features and excels in community networking functions, catering well to the entertainment needs of mid-to-low-end consumer groups. Currently priced at 1190 RMB, its sales volume keeps breaking records! The Nokia C5 uses a straight bar design, equipped with a 2.2-inch screen with QVGA resolution, running on the Symbian S60 third edition operating platform. It also supports GPS and FM radio functions. The phone comes with a built-in 50MB storage device and supports microSD card expansion. The Nokia C5 is a smart terminal optimized for sharing functions, supporting various services from Nokia Ovi, allowing users to quickly access and experience music through Ovi Music applications.

Product Model: Nokia C3

Reference Price: 1350 RMB (Revised Edition)

Suitable for: Users who prefer texting and chatting using a full keyboard.

Currently, there are more and more full-keyboard gray-market phones available. Models like Nokia's E71, E72, and E63 have received positive feedback. However, this newly released C3-00 is a model that uses the Symbian S40 platform. Recently, this gray-market phone is quoted at 1350 RMB and has quickly gained recognition from many consumers upon its release. The Nokia C3 adopts a straight bar + Qwerty keyboard design similar to the classic E-series models like the E71. However, if you look closely, you'll notice that the iconic menu key found on S60 phones is missing. Yes, the Nokia C3 is a non-smartphone using the S40 operating platform, making it the first S40 model to feature a full keyboard design.

Product Model: Nokia E5

Reference Price: 1990 RMB (Revised Edition)

Suitable for: Regular internet chatters.

Recently, Nokia has been releasing new models one after another, and the Nokia E5 stands out among them. As an E-series gray-market phone, the E5 further improves on the classic designs of the E71 and E72, with a more rounded appearance and a reasonable color scheme, making it appear more fashionable. Moreover, this E5 also boasts impressive performance, and its entry into the market will once again sweep the full-keyboard smart gray-market phone sector. This week, the price of this gray-market phone dropped below 2000 RMB, offering excellent value for money.

Product Model: Nokia C6

Reference Price: 2050 RMB (Revised Edition)

Suitable for: Users accustomed to full-keyboard texting and QQ chatting.

As a new product in Nokia's C series, the Nokia C6 features a side-sliding full keyboard design, which is quite stylish. Being an upgrade of the 5230, its enhanced performance has also satisfied many consumers. Currently, this gray-market phone is priced at 2050 RMB, and if it can drop below 2000 RMB, it would likely become another popular street phone. The Nokia C6 uses a side-sliding full keyboard design, with a fashionable appearance. Its front features a 16 million color 3.2-inch TFT screen with a resolution of 360x640 pixels, offering good display quality. On the back, it is equipped with a 5-megapixel camera with LED flash, providing excellent imaging results.

Product Model: Nokia N8

Reference Price: Not yet on sale

Suitable for: Loyal "Nokia fans" with a comfortable budget.

Since Nokia started equipping their phones with Carl Zeiss lenses, there has been no stopping them. In terms of camera performance, Nokia continues to excel, and the flagship touch-screen N8 is also equipped with a 12-megapixel Carl Zeiss lens, delivering outstanding shooting results. As a touch-screen smartphone, the N8 performs even better in terms of entertainment functions, being more comprehensive and professional.

Equipped with the Symbian 3 operating system, the N8 is a multi-touch capacitive gray-market phone. Compared to previous generations of touch-screen gray-market phones, the N8 is bolder and more avant-garde in overall design and color usage. This straight bar phone is equipped with a 12-megapixel Carl Zeiss certified lens, delivering exceptional shooting results; paired with Dolby sound technology, enhancing the entertainment capabilities of the entire gray-market phone. With professional audio-visual technologies, the N8 promises an outstanding performance that won't disappoint.

Editorial Comment: The N8 features three main screens—one for work, one for entertainment, and another for using your favorite photos as wallpapers, enriching the user interface of your gray-market phone. Currently, this model has not yet been released domestically.
