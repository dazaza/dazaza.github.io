Revealing Ten Secrets of Women's Periods

by surds21p5 on 2012-02-13 21:52:08

Core Tip: Menstruation is both familiar and mysterious for women. The pain is familiar to everyone, but how much do you know about the secrets hidden behind menstruation? The following ten menstrual secrets will reveal the unknown aspects of women's periods.

Secret 1: Emotional Contagion

The emotional instability caused by menstruation can also be contagious. Men may become irritable when their wives are on their periods. Some scholars believe this is due to fluctuating estrogen levels in women affecting male hormone secretion in a "roller-coaster" manner.

Secret 2: Decreased Appetite

Research from Tufts University in the U.S. shows that women consume 12% less food during their menstrual week compared to normal times, and the hormone-induced appetite also decreases.

Secret 3: Quitting Smoking More Successfully Before Menstruation

A study from Minnesota State University in the U.S. points out that in the few days before menstruation, estrogen levels drop to their lowest, significantly increasing the chances of successfully quitting smoking.

Secret 4: Slurred Speech

During menstruation, due to the influence of progesterone, women may experience slurred speech and incoherent thinking, which is a normal phenomenon.

Secret 5: Constipation and Abdominal Bloating

Many women experience constipation and bloating. It is important to drink more water and consume high-fiber foods to maintain regular bowel movements.

Secret 6: Women Living or Working Together Have Closer Periods

Women who live or work together tend to have their periods get closer over time. This is partly due to psychological tension regarding the arrival of the menstrual cycle being influenced by others, and partly because stress-related chemicals released by the body can be contagious.

Secret 7: Some Women Experience Diarrhea

During menstruation, certain chemicals are secreted in the body to stimulate uterine contractions for smooth menstrual blood discharge. These chemicals are the same as those that stimulate intestinal contractions and cause diarrhea. Therefore, when abdominal cramps occur, women can take some anti-inflammatory painkillers to suppress contractions.

Secret 8: Menstrual Blood Has Its Own Color

The longer menstrual blood stays inside the body, the darker its color becomes. Some women may experience yellow or green menstrual blood, which could be an early sign of vaginitis.

Secret 9: Never Experienced Dysmenorrhea

If after menarche, menstrual cycles are irregular, there may be no dysmenorrhea within two years. Unfortunately, as one ages, the body regulates the menstrual cycle, and dysmenorrhea may then appear.

Secret 10: Highest Libido

At this time, the secretion of progesterone, which suppresses libido, is at its lowest. Combined with the fact that pregnancy is not possible, women may feel more relaxed about sexual activity.
