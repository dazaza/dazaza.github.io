Second, Reproduction of Centipedes

Centipedes are oviparous animals. [Agri-science] Generally, they mate on sunny days after rain from March to June. The eggs gradually mature at the end of spring and beginning of summer each year. Egg-laying starts in mid-June, with the peak egg-laying period being in the first half of June to mid-July, continuing until early August. Before laying eggs, the female centipede's body becomes swollen, with its abdomen close to the ground, moving slowly. Female centipedes showing signs of egg-laying should be isolated 3-4 days before using a bottomless canister about 10 cm in diameter. The opening should be covered with fiberboard or glass. This effectively utilizes the breeding area, avoids mutual interference and predation, facilitates smooth egg-laying in a quiet environment, and makes it easier to separate young centipedes from the mother centipedes into different tanks (or pools) for rearing.
