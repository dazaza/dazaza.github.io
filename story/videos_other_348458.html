Liverpool's players and coaches become the must-dig targets for Inter Milan and Juventus this summer, Chinese buyers reveal plans to purchase the Reds.

by ztt0288868 on 2010-04-19 18:27:06

Juventus is testing Benitez's devotion. According to reports on Sina Sports, Liverpool's troubled season is finally coming to an end, but this summer, another battle to defend Anfield will take place for the red half of Merseyside. Moreover, this invincible force that once established a red dynasty in Europe might collapse instantly. This is not an exaggeration; if Liverpool does not have Gerrard, Torres, and a top-class famous coach, it is unknown when the struggling Reds will see the light at the end of the tunnel.

As the storm approaches, the captain of the Reds first falls into transfer rumors. According to the Daily Mail, Mourinho, who failed twice to acquire Gerrard while coaching Chelsea, has listed the England international as his top target this summer and has sent "instructions" to Inter Milan chairman Moratti, sparing no expense to secure the soul of the Reds. Gerrard had just renewed his contract with the club last summer until 2013, but this season's poor performance by the Reds has made even next season's Champions League qualification seem unattainable. Additionally, the deteriorating financial situation at Anfield makes it almost impossible to make significant reinforcements in the summer transfer market.

Italian media believe that Inter Milan believes spending 25 to 30 million pounds could sign Gerrard, with the only hesitation being whether to include "bad boy" Balotelli as part of the transfer deal. In Mourinho's reconstruction plan this summer, he hopes to pair Gerrard with Sporting Lisbon midfielder Varejo in midfield, while cleaning out fringe players like Montari and Quaresma. Italian media speculate that if Spanish master and apprentice Benitez and Torres choose to leave this summer, Gerrard will no longer linger with this team that he both loves and hates.

The status of the captain remains uncertain, while the top scorer continues to be plagued by injuries. Torres mysteriously disappeared during last weekend's league match against Fulham, and after the game, Coach Benitez revealed that he went to Barcelona to see a "specialist" due to a knee injury. The Spanish "miracle doctor" Cugat cautiously expressed that under normal circumstances, the Golden Boy could return in Monday's match between Liverpool and West Ham United. However, if the swelling on Torres' body does not subside after five days of rest, the 26-year-old Spanish striker may face surgery again.

Torres' injury also worries the Spanish national team, with Spain's sports director Hierro maintaining daily contact with Torres, but the true cause of the fluid accumulation in the Golden Boy's knee remains unclear. Hierro revealed: "The doctor wants Torres to rest for five days, and we will know how serious his injury is after five days. Torres is optimistic, and so am I. Hopefully, the problem is caused by external impact, which would be easier to handle."

Although such an injury, even if requiring surgery, will not affect Torres playing for Spain in the World Cup, it will let him miss more Liverpool games. Torres himself is eager to return to the Calderon Stadium in the Europa League semifinal against his old club Atletico Madrid. He optimistically believes that he will recover soon.

Since joining Liverpool, Torres has always been the coveted target of many major clubs, including Barcelona, Chelsea, Juventus, and Manchester City. Despite the Golden Boy repeatedly emphasizing his loyalty and love for the Reds, everyone knows that his future at Anfield is closely tied to Spanish coach Benitez. According to the Times, Juventus, hoping to change coaches and rebuild this summer, has issued a final ultimatum to Coach Benitez, giving him ten days to consider whether to take over "the Old Lady." The Mirror believes that whether Juventus can get Benitez depends on whether they can enter next season's Champions League. Coincidentally, like Liverpool, Juventus is currently sixth in Serie A and may also miss the Champions League.

According to the Daily Mail, Juventus offers a four-year contract worth 4 million pounds annually to Benitez. Although Benitez's current contract is worth 4 million pounds annually, due to the UK's new tax rate of 50%, the Spanish coach would earn more if he leaves Liverpool for Juventus. Despite this, Benitez's agent Quilon still denied rumors of contact with Juventus executives: "I repeat, I haven't met anyone from Juventus (The Daily Mail claims that Benitez's agent and two Juventus management personnel secretly met in Milan last weekend). Benitez still has four years left on his contract with Liverpool, and in principle, he will stay here, but Liverpool's poor financial condition is also a fact, which will affect the club and Benitez's future. Next month, I don't know what will happen."

Quilon also revealed that a Chinese investor told him that if Benitez continues as Liverpool's manager, they will buy all shares held by Hicks and Gillett. "Chinese investors are very interested in buying Liverpool because they called Benitez, stating that their condition for buying Liverpool is that Benitez remains Liverpool's manager. For now, Real Madrid doesn't have this (recruiting Benitez) plan, but Juventus will never wait for Benitez."

Benitez's five-year contract with Liverpool has only been in effect for 12 months, but Juventus hopes to benefit from the recent turmoil at Anfield. The Times points out that because Benitez has successively locked down key players like Torres, Reina, and Gerrard with long-term contracts over the past year, he hopes to stay at Liverpool. However, Juventus has identified the Reds' weakness and will promise to provide Benitez with 70 million pounds in summer transfer funds, which is the most scarce resource at Anfield. Besides Juventus, Benitez's options also include Real Madrid. Although Real Madrid's official has not yet taken action, a recent poll by Marca showed that Liverpool's coach is the person Real Madrid fans want most to replace Pellegrini, even surpassing Mourinho and Capello.
