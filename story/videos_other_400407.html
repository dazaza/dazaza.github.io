3.3 Guide Shopping Websites

Inland online stores are abundant and product categories are rich, but consumers find it difficult to locate the specific products they have in mind. In this situation, the search engine within guide shopping websites can help consumers find suitable product sites. The search engine is a selection tool where consumers can set filtering conditions such as price, efficacy, brand, and specifications, allowing the website to screen and narrow down products that meet customer requirements. For example, Aibei.com and Congmingdian in inland China collect products from different regional retailers and provide functional matching services, enabling consumers to shop easily.

Guide shopping websites can offer the same product's price at different merchants or regions. Consumers can also set their requirements for products (such as price, brand, merchant, region), and the website will match out online store data, user reviews of products and merchants; consumers can then compare before linking to the selected merchant's online store to purchase.

Guide shopping websites provide consumers with extensive shopping information through offering product information, prices, shopping searches, and comparison functions. They also match high-quality potential customers for merchants. To ensure that the enterprise’s products are successfully picked out by the search engine, product suppliers need to describe the product's function and characteristics in detail, accurately grasp consumer search terms, and establish appropriate keywords.

4. Comparison between Independent Online Stores and Third-Party Trading Platforms

4.1 Autonomous Space

Third-party trading platforms already have a fixed customer base and browsing rate, allowing businesses just starting online sales to quickly reach potential consumers. However, at the same time, there are more products and suppliers on third-party trading platforms, making it hard to highlight individual suppliers or brands.

Some companies hoping to highlight their own brand image, especially those that have established a fixed customer base, choose to set up independent online stores. Some Hong Kong brands that are famous in inland China, such as Giordano, SaSa, and Chow Sang Sang, use the independent website model to open online sales channels in inland China. SaSa opened its first cosmetics specialty store in Shanghai in 2005, but SaSa's online store was established as early as 2000, allowing customers in inland China and around the world to select beauty products at any time. In 2008-09, sasa's sales accounted for about 4.8% of total revenue, an increase of 66% compared to 2007-08.

Some brands have only used the independent online store method since the beginning, such as the inland clothing brand Vancl. Established in October 2007, Vancl adopted the concept of an "Internet fashion lifestyle brand" and sold its own VANCL men's and women's clothing on an independent online store, gradually expanding its product range to include children's clothing, shoes, and home goods. Although it does not have physical stores or other operational methods on third-party platform stores, VANCL's revenue increased from RMB 1.12 million in 2007 to RMB 6 billion in 2009, accounting for approximately 28.4% of the B2C clothing market share in inland China, ranking first.

To meet the needs of some sellers who want to enhance their brand image and have an independent store, some third-party trading platforms like Taobao Mall allow individual brands such as Uniqlo, Levi’s, Belle, etc., to open exclusive zones/flagship stores on their websites. However, third-party platform websites are systematized; even if they have their own sales zones, there are still designated layouts, and they will standardize the terms of sale between sellers and consumers, affecting the autonomous space and development strategies of enterprises. For example, sellers on Taobao Mall must provide a "Taobao Mall 7-day no-reason return and exchange" after-sales service.

4.2 Registration and Management

The procedures for setting up an online store on a third-party platform are simple, requiring only a business license and tax registration certificate in mainland China. Additionally, if the enterprise is a brand owner or manufacturer, it needs to apply for a Chinese trademark certificate (trademark acceptance notice); if the enterprise is an agent, it needs a brand authorization letter. However, enterprises that set up independent online stores as sales channels need to apply to national telecommunications management agencies or the State Council Information Industry Department.

Besides providing basic web page layout, payment systems, integrity and security systems, web management, etc., third-party platform websites are also responsible for deleting suspected plagiarized, counterfeit products or companies from the website to protect the patents of registered merchants and maintain copyright interests. Generally speaking, the threshold for opening an online store on a third-party platform is lower, requiring less manpower, technology, and capital. However, independent online stores require enterprises to be responsible for daily operations and system maintenance, as well as electronic payment security issues, which require certain human resources and make it heavier for SMEs.

Most third-party platforms have designated independent electronic payment providers, and enterprises need to separately apply for accounts and pay deposits to the electronic payment provider. For example, Taobao Mall uses "Alipay" as the payment platform, and Alipay charges enterprises RMB 10,000 as a deposit. Afterward, transactions between enterprises and consumers will be handled by the independent electronic payment provider.

5. Electronic Payment Security Issues

Network transaction security and confidentiality are one of the obstacles to the development of internet sales, because consumers cannot directly pay sellers (except for direct transfers to the seller's bank account), and over 70% of consumers choose electronic payments when shopping online. The Ministry of Commerce's "Opinions on Promoting the Standard Development of E-commerce" aims to standardize electronic payment behavior and ensure the safety of fund flows; it also guides enterprises to take effective technical and management measures to ensure the security of payment passwords and financial data.

5.1 Third-Party Payment Platform

To solve the safety issue of customer cash flow, most online commodity trading platforms adopt third-party payment methods. For example, Taobao uses the Alipay platform, and EachNet uses the Anfutong platform. Both websites require consumers to deposit cash into Alipay or Anfutong accounts when purchasing goods; Alipay/Anfutong then notifies the seller to ship the goods. When the transaction ends and the consumer confirms that the received goods match what was purchased online, they notify Alipay/Anfutong to pay the seller. However, third-party payment methods also have some problems, as a cash retention platform is formed during the transaction process. How to ensure the safety of the cash requires supervision from regulatory authorities.

5.2 Fraud Prevention System

Currently, most consumers use bank-issued credit cards or debit cards for online electronic payments. However, due to the short duration of online transactions, banks find it difficult to detect problematic credit cards (stolen, lost, fake cards), resulting in losses. Some third-party payment platforms provide "fraud prevention systems" to protect sellers and banks. Before issuing transaction instructions to the bank, the third-party payment platform compares credit card data with the bank's customer database, assesses risks, and confirms transactions with the bank. For example, if the same credit card number is used for online consumption in different regions and IP addresses within minutes, it likely involves a fake card. If the risk rating is high, the third-party payment platform will terminate the transaction to protect the interests of the bank and seller.

5.3 Guofubao Electronic Payment Platform

In response to corporate needs and the development of e-commerce, in 2007, the International E-commerce Center of the Ministry of Commerce and Guofutong Company launched the state-owned "Guofubao" electronic payment platform. It provides B2B (e.g., China Apparel Network and China Machinery & Electronics Trade Network) and B2C (e.g., Zhengjia Network and Joyo.com) payment platforms, as well as G2C government fee collection and public utility fee payment applications. Guofubao is the only state-level guaranteed trading platform, possessing a state-level security certification system, ensuring payment and fund safety, reducing consumer concerns, and protecting sellers.

6. Methods of Product Delivery

The biggest difficulty in product delivery is the dispersion of customers and varying distances, increasing shipping costs and requiring additional manpower to individually package and send goods. On the other hand, time is also an important factor because consumers are accustomed to the traditional "buy and have immediately" shopping mode, so goods must be delivered to consumers as soon as possible. Currently, the product delivery models for B2C online transaction websites mainly include the following three types:

6.1 National Postal Service

Sellers can use China Post Group Corporation and its provincial postal subsidiaries to send goods to the address provided by consumers. Regular mail is slower, so sellers can choose express delivery, though the cost is higher. Sellers need to be mindful of the inconsistent postal service levels in different regions, where consumers may have difficulty receiving goods within the expected time, leading to damaged packaging or damaged goods, affecting reputation.

6.2 Website Self-built Delivery Model

Some large-scale enterprises and large B2C websites (such as Dangdang, Joyo.com, and JD.com) have self-built logistics models, setting up warehouses and delivery points in areas with dense internet users, and delivering goods according to the personal information on the consumer's shopping list. This delivery model can quickly deliver goods to consumers, but increases logistics costs, and sellers find it difficult to pass these costs onto consumers, reducing profits. It is also challenging for B2C websites to accurately determine the location of delivery points, the number of staff, and the inventory of goods. Some websites with many outlets allow consumers to pick up goods in person; for example, JD.com's "door-to-door self-pickup" allows consumers to pay in cash, check, or POS machine swipe after online shopping.

6.3 Third-Party Logistics Delivery Model

Since B2C deliveries are smaller in volume, even though courier fees are higher (they can exceed 20% of gross profit), many sellers are willing to adopt them because delivery times are fast (same-city deliveries can be made within 24 hours). However, sellers still face risks such as lost goods, product damage during delivery, and leakage of customer data. If goods are lost, even if the seller shows receipts, the courier company will only compensate with 3 to 5 times the shipping cost. Regardless of which delivery model is chosen, the existing delivery system in mainland China has yet to catch up with the development of B2C commerce, and shipping and courier fees often constitute a significant proportion of online store operating costs.

7. Online Store Promotion and Competition

7.1 Establishing Online Store Reputation

Online marketing has low barriers to entry, leading to more competitors, and some online stores may remain unnoticed for months. How can customers find Hong Kong merchants' online stores amidst the vast sea of the internet? Mainland consumers value word-of-mouth, with nearly half of netizens hearing about or being introduced to a shopping website by friends and family, while 30% learn about shopping websites from the internet.

Most websites have buyer ratings where consumers who have purchased the product can leave feedback on their transaction experience, evaluating both the product and the seller, helping to build business credibility. For example, EachNet has a Seller Service Rating System (DSR), where after completing a transaction, buyers can rate the seller's service quality in four categories: differences between item descriptions and actual items, communication quality and response speed, reasonableness of item delivery time, and reasonableness of shipping and handling fees. This allows enterprises to directly understand their service performance, and other consumers can choose sellers with better service quality based on scores, obtaining a better shopping experience.

7.2 Using Third-Party Platform "Recommendation Spots"

Recommendation spots are like online ads, where sellers purchase them. General recommendation spots are located in prominent positions on the website, displaying products in text or photo form. Of course, recommendation spot fees vary across different websites and positions, allowing merchants to choose according to budget and need. Since recommendation spots incur fees, online stores cannot place all products in recommendation spots, but instead strategically place their most distinctive products there to attract buyers to check out other product information in the store.

The category of products also affects the effectiveness of recommendation spots. Products with standard specifications and functionality (such as computer accessories, vitamins) tend to use recommendation spots less frequently, as other product factors (such as price, after-sales service) may more significantly influence consumer purchasing desires. Conversely, consumer products (such as jewelry, watches) differ in style, material, and design, and need to keep up with seasons and trends, making recommendation spots effective in highlighting the merchant's products.

7.3 Promotion of Independent Online Stores

Independent online stores can register their company URLs on major search engines (such as www.google.cn; www.yahoo.cn; www.baidu.com, etc.). If you want your customers to quickly find your company website, you can buy "keyword advertisements," "friend links," "promotion links," etc., from search engine service providers, appearing in a better position in search results to attract consumer attention.

Another method is through "Optimization Ranking" (Search Engine Optimization—SEO), using search rules to improve the ranking of your company's website in relevant search engines. When searchers input keywords into search engines, the engines compare the content of websites for relevance, and browsers present these contents as quickly and completely as possible to searchers. Websites processed through SEO are more easily accepted by search engines, thus ranking higher.

7.4 Enhancing Product Competitiveness

Although data shows that clothing is the most purchased item by mainland internet users, the quality, style, and price of products are also important factors for success. One example is a Hong Kong trading company engaged in import and export, which uses a third-party platform to open an online store selling Korean trendy clothes, targeting working women in Shanghai. Due to the large number of similar online stores, products imported from Korea are expensive and lack competitiveness. Moreover, clothing trends change rapidly, and styles quickly go out of fashion. Also, selling clothing requires providing various sizes and colors, necessitating substantial capital for inventory. Due to intense competition, the online store closed after a year. Therefore, Hong Kong merchants should thoroughly understand the actual situation of online sales, the characteristics, and selling points of their products before opening an online store.

8. Government Regulation of Internet Sales

As online shopping is still a relatively new channel, mainland China has not yet formulated a complete set of laws and regulations specifically for online sales. In 2008, the Ministry of Commerce issued the "E-commerce Mode Standards" and the "Network Shopping Service Standards" draft for comments, hoping to standardize online commodity sales and service behaviors. According to the Ministry of Commerce's 2007 "Guidance Opinions on Online Transactions (Interim)," selling goods or services online extends traditional business practices to the internet and should be detailed according to existing laws such as the "General Principles of Civil Law," the "Company Law," the "Electronic Signature Law," and other commercial operation laws. Many regional governments have also formulated regional e-commerce regulations, such as the "Shanghai Regulations for Promoting the Development of E-commerce" and Beijing's "Opinions on Promoting the Development of Online Retail."

8.1 Business Registration

According to the "Guidance Opinions on Online Transactions (Interim)," opening an online store is like running a physical store, and enterprises need to register with the State Administration for Market Regulation. Enterprises already registered in mainland China for physical stores do not need to re-register if the online store's business scope is consistent with the physical store. If the enterprise does not have a physical store in mainland China, it needs to submit the required application documents to the provincial commerce department where the foreign-invested commercial enterprise is registered.

If an enterprise only sells on third-party platforms on the internet or is represented by online distributors, it only needs to register for commercial registration in mainland China. However, if an enterprise sets up an independent online sales platform (i.e., directly providing internet information services - ICP), according to the 2000 State Council Order "Regulations on the Administration of Internet Information Services," it should apply to the provincial/autonomous region/municipality telecommunications management agency or the State Council Information Industry Department for an "Internet Information Services Value-Added Telecommunications Business Operating License." The license number should be displayed in the middle of the bottom of the homepage. Additionally, according to the 2005 "Internet IP Address Filing Management Measures," the website's independent IP address (whether or not it has a domain name) should also be registered with the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology. Websites engaged in news, publishing, education, medical health care, pharmaceuticals and medical devices, culture, radio, film, and television programs should obtain prior approval procedures.

8.2 Taxation Issues

According to the 2002 "Detailed Rules for the Implementation of the Tax Collection and Administration Law of the People's Republic of China": “Taxpayers engaged in production and business operations shall, within 30 days from the date of obtaining the business license, declare and register for tax purposes with the competent tax authority at the place of production and business operations or the place where the tax obligation arises, truthfully filling out the tax registration form and providing relevant certificates and materials as required by the tax authority.” Foreign-invested enterprises are subject to main taxes such as corporate income tax, value-added tax, consumption tax [9], and business tax for agency services when selling goods in China. If the taxpayer belongs to manufacturers or business merchants, the taxation includes value-added tax, corporate income tax, and business tax. However, internet sales also include some digitizable products, such as books and magazines, audiovisual products, computer software, etc. These transactions do not need to pass through traditional customs borders and national borders, so customs duties and domestic taxes cannot be levied. In fact, no country can currently fully levy all taxes on online trade.

9. Summary

Undoubtedly, online sales will be an important growth pole for the Chinese consumer market, a new channel for Hong Kong merchants to expand domestic sales. Online sales can break geographical and storefront size restrictions, offering goods 24/7 to consumers nationwide. The development of different types of third-party sales platforms provides small and medium-sized enterprises with a low-threshold, simple procedure, and relatively low-cost opportunity to develop domestic sales.

There are many market opportunities, but also much competition. Facing fierce competition, Hong Kong merchants need to stand out among numerous suppliers and products. They must understand the actual situation of online sales in mainland China, find market space, and use service platforms effectively to highlight product features and advantages, attracting buyers. Considering the characteristics of current mainland online shoppers, Hong Kong merchants can consider the following when conducting online sales:

Before purchasing products online, consumers, especially for personalized goods like clothing and home decor, prefer to feel the size and quality of the product personally, confirming it suits them. Therefore, to effectively develop the market, consider combining physical stores with online stores, making corresponding arrangements regarding product structure and pricing.

Mainland internet users habitually browse different websites and use search tools within websites to look for products before purchasing online. To increase click-through rates and transaction opportunities, try to utilize titles and concisely explain product features and list component information, honestly noting in product photos/pictures to facilitate buyer selection. At the same time, to increase the chance of search engines selecting products, Hong Kong merchants should clearly describe the product's function and characteristics and accurately grasp consumer search terms, establishing appropriate keywords.

Mainland consumers love fashion. Hong Kong merchants have mainly focused on foreign trade production, offering unique product styles and guaranteed quality, which should appeal to consumers, but they should also adapt appropriately to the tastes of the mainland market. Moreover, the mainland market offers diverse product choices, and consumers focus on cost-performance ratios. To increase competitiveness for online sales, Hong Kong merchants must pay attention to factors such as rapid product updates, multiple design options, complete sizes and colors, and competitive pricing.

Some internet users leave credit ratings on sellers' websites after online purchases, becoming an important reference for other consumers when shopping. Therefore, Hong Kong merchants should pay attention to building credibility online with consumers. Besides the products themselves, the process of selling goods and after-sales service are also crucial. To maintain good communication with customers, most online stores have online customer service. If real-time interaction with customers is not possible, generally responding within 24 hours is necessary, otherwise potential customers may be lost.

Additionally, goods must be delivered within the stated timeframe, without excuses such as out-of-stock, postal delays, or other reasons. Especially since the biggest difference between online stores and physical stores is that consumers cannot enjoy the product immediately after payment, receiving the product later than expected greatly reduces interest in the product and impression of the seller, making it difficult to attract repeat purchases. Simultaneously, sellers should list product after-sales service explanations on their online store, clearly explaining under what circumstances consumers can return or exchange goods, who bears the transportation costs, etc., to avoid disputes later.

Currently, there are many options for third-party online shopping platforms in mainland China. Some large comprehensive malls offer warehouse storage, logistics distribution, electronic payment services, and some website distributors even offer customer management, information feedback value-added services. Hong Kong merchants can choose suitable websites as partners based on their own needs and development strategies to explore online sales in mainland China.
