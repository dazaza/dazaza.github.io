Netizens call her "Superwoman" and affectionately refer to her as Sister Hehe. Not relying on beautiful pictures but gaining popularity online through her writing style

No one would have expected that in such a short time, from last Friday until now, her followers have increased by 1.1 million! From her Weibo content, we can understand that Superwoman Doctor is Yu Ying, the attending physician of the emergency department at Peking Union Medical College Hospital. She came from Shanghai to Beijing in 1993 to attend university, and it has been 18 years since then.

Rarely accompanied by pictures in her Weibo posts, often within a hundred words or less, yet she can gain over 500 forwards in no time, leaving people feeling incredulous.

Her explanation of Starbucks has become a classic joke on Weibo, spaducks has already sparked endless imagination among people. Waiting for someone at Starbucks, pondering the English name starbucks, actually I prefer spaducks. Countless strong ducks enjoying spa treatments, just thinking about it makes me feel exhilarated! The cold humor created by reverse thinking leaves a deep impression once seen. The mischievous and humorous nature of Superwoman Doctor, unrestrained expression, leaves many netizens momentarily stunned.

Hiding her care for patients behind her carefree demeanor

As a doctor, Superwoman Sister Hehe is very busy every day. In Yu Ying's eyes, there are countless fun stories in the hospital. Bored male patients during night shifts, nurses crying and being teased into laughter, Yu Ying finds motivation in her work through her heart. She always manages to place herself in the role within her Weibo posts, summarizing medical experiences or expressing philosophical reflections with just a few simple words. Sometimes, she moves small incidents from her life onto Weibo, bringing laughter and thoughtful reflection afterward.

Patients come for consultation, coughing, prescribed compound ammonium bromide solution. Patients pay and collect their medicine, asking the pharmacy how to take it. The pharmacy replies: drink one line (the bottle has lines marked for drinking one unit). As a result, the patient drinks one line on the way home, and by the time they get home, the whole bottle is gone! Some say Sister Hehe is carefree, but she pays attention to these small details concerning patients.

If someone asks for medical advice via Weibo, Yu Ying will not easily respond. Sometimes I don't answer because seeing patients involves clinical judgment, and without seeing the person, jumping to conclusions would be extremely irresponsible to the patient.

There was once a junior doctor who kept a notebook recording her famous quotes

Some people ask, isn't the emergency department very busy? How does she have time to post on Weibo? Observing her Weibo, it is generally text-based without pictures, taking only two minutes to post a Weibo update, so it doesn't take up too much time.

Frequently posting on Weibo, how do family members feel about it? My family is now annoyed with me, checking Weibo every now and then takes up time spent with them, so I try hard to please them, hehe! Yu Ying's voice is just like her Weibo language, full of wit and humor, giving a sense of warmth. From coming to Beijing Union Medical College in 1993 to staying and working in Beijing now, 37-year-old Yu Ying is married with a daughter. Her old classmates describe her as still the same, unchanged.

Yu Ying frankly admits that before, there were junior doctors carrying notebooks recording her famous quotes, planning to publish a collection of Yu Ying's sayings one day. When Yu Ying became popular, her colleagues in the emergency department joked: After becoming famous, if there are more patients in the emergency department, remember to treat everyone to dinner.

If every doctor could let patients' illnesses vanish with a smile and a word, wouldn't that make both parties feel refreshed and joyful? Netizen Shebin Xiaoxiong said.

Now, Yu Ying continues her usual life, starting her mornings from Beijing's eastern sixth ring road, commuting back and forth between home and the hospital.

Highlights from Superwoman Yu Ying's Weibo

◎A patient with thrombocytopenia tried all local therapies without success and came to our emergency department. Hematology came for consultation and recommended 40mg dexamethasone intravenous injection. The pharmacy thought the dosage was too high and dared not dispense the medication. I had to guarantee it before the medication was retrieved. The patient started hesitating and requested halving the dosage. I said: This is the specialist doctor's consultation opinion, you either use it or don't use it, go see another expert. It's not like buying a trotter, one weighs four catties, and you only want two!

◎At around 11 pm, a consultation in the department, a pregnant woman with familial hyperlipidemia combined with pancreatitis and pancreatic pseudocyst formation. A heated discussion ensued regarding whether to keep the baby or not. I advocated keeping it! First, it's not severe pancreatitis, not a matter of life and death yet. Second, no eating means no pain, and all laboratory indicators show no signs of deterioration. Third, any future pregnancies would face the same problem. The final discussion results were as I had anticipated, multi-department collaboration, continue keeping the baby! Mother and child, keep going!

◎Night shift, everything peaceful, at one o'clock in the morning, the emergency room door opens, an old man runs in shouting: I'm dying, I'm dying! Then collapses on the rescue bed, breathing and heartbeat stop, resuscitated successfully after three minutes. The ECG suggests a massive myocardial infarction. Summary: First, self-rescue awareness is crucial. Second, familiarity with the hospital layout is important. Third, concise and effective communication is extraordinarily important, especially in life-and-death situations!

◎Last National Day, starting from September 30th, I had a persistent fever above 39 degrees Celsius. On the third day, I refused visitors and stuck to my decision. By the sixth day, the head of the infectious diseases department sent condolences: Considering possible bacterial infection, you should take medicine! Still refused! On the eighth day, it was time to return to work, damn it, the fever was gone! Ruined my entire holiday!

One day, while receiving radiation therapy, the patient met with a car accident and underwent craniotomy to save their life. With decreased immunity, the patient contracted tuberculous pericarditis and underwent pericardial window surgery. A year later, the original tumor extensively metastasized to the lungs. During this visit, the patient suffered from severe edema in the lower body, pulmonary infection accompanied by respiratory failure. Only the son was taking care of the patient throughout. Despite all this, mother and son remained cheerful, teasing each other. Heart-wrenching yet warm!
