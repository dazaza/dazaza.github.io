Gave Her First Kiss to Farewell

Looking up at the still brightly lit internet cafe sign, it couldn't help but remind Xue'er of everything that had happened back then.

Memories were like yesterday, and everything that had happened was vividly remembered. At the time, she was just bored and went to the internet cafe to surf the web, killing time. However, sitting in the internet cafe, she didn't know what to do, so aimlessly played games, listened to music, and with headphones on, fully immersed herself in the virtual world. Indeed, the internet cafe was really a good place to kill time. After playing for not long, three hours had already passed. Seeing that her time was almost up, Xue'er stopped the game and started chatting with online friends. Just five minutes before she had to log off, a strange number applied to add Xue'er as a friend.

The note read: "I am a lonely soldier boy, can you chat with me?" Thinking it was fun, Xue'er added him, and the strange avatar started flashing.

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: Are you at Global Peninsula?

Lonely Queen (Xue'er's): What is that place? I'm not there!

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: Are you not in the internet cafe?

Lonely Queen: I am, are you? Are you also in an internet cafe? Is Global Peninsula an internet cafe?

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: Yes, you're also in one, right? What's the name of your internet cafe? I'll come find you, I'm so bored, okay?

Lonely Queen: Sunshine Web Garden, come if you want. (Xue'er thought to herself, all talk, let's see how this lady comes, humph, boasting.)

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: ?? Where is it? Is it not far from me? Tell me the address, I'll definitely get there.

Seeing the prompt on the screen that said [You have one minute left, please log off! We look forward to seeing you next time], Xue'er hesitated a bit. She thought about it, got up from her seat, went to the counter, paid again, and continued surfing. When she returned to her seat and saw the message sent by the stranger, she replied,

Lonely Queen: Even if I told you, you wouldn't know. By the way, I don't even know what you do yet. Are you really a soldier? Where are you? (Xue'er was a very curious girl; anything could spark her curiosity.)

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: Of course, I'm not lying to you. You can check out my space; they're all military items!

Lonely Queen: Who knows if it's really you? These days, people lie without even thinking twice, especially soldiers who are all smooth talkers.

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: Do you have video? Let me show you the real person, genuine, absolutely not fake. I'm just bored and want someone to chat with me. Since I came here to serve, I don't know anyone, I'm about to suffocate! Today, I finally managed to get out, so I want to find a local person to chat with me and learn about the local customs.

Lonely Queen: It seems your lying skills are pretty good. If what you say is true, then come find me. I'm a local, I can tell you all about our local customs, haha! (Xue'er thought to herself, this person is quite absurd. Still, she entered his space and in the photo album, she saw many things related to the army, making her feel that he might not be lying. But these days, no one is reliable.) As Xue'er looked around and saw the time was getting late, she chose to log off. Before going offline, she sent the stranger a last message,

Lonely Queen: Big liar, my time is up, I won't waste my time with you anymore, 88, we'll talk next time!

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: So you're leaving already? At least leave your name?

Lonely Queen: You're so annoying. My name is Wind, what about you?

Listening to Rain Under the Tree: My name is Rain.

Lonely Queen: Bah, boring. Logging off, hopefully I won't see you again!

After logging off, Xue'er walked out of the internet cafe. It was already quite late outside, so she hurried home because she didn't want her family to worry.

Xue'er never would have guessed that this accidental chat would turn out to be the beginning of her first love. When Xue'er logged onto QQ again at the internet cafe, she saw the familiar strange number, which had left her a bunch of messages asking why she wasn't online or replying. Xue'er found it quite boring and ignored them. It seemed fate was playing tricks on her. Just after Xue'er went online, the strange number's avatar lit up, and it left her another message. Because Xue'er was invisible, she didn't like deception, so she replied to the stranger. The stranger was very happy, and this chat was much better than the previous one. Xue'er chatted a lot with the stranger and eventually realized that the stranger wasn't as she had imagined. Thus, they began contacting each other frequently. Since Xue'er had always liked soldiers, whenever she had time, she asked the boy about the army. Over time, Xue'er learned that the boy's name was Wei Yun, that he was from the south, and that he was an officer. However, Xue'er did not reveal her true identity to the boy, keeping everything false until one day when Xue'er felt that constantly deceiving Wei Yun was unfair and unbearable. So, she sent him a message (because they had been in contact for a long time and already exchanged phone numbers).

The content was: [Everything I've told you about myself has been false. Today, I want to tell you that I've deceived you. Don't contact me anymore!] There was no plea for forgiveness in the text message. The boy, upon reading the message, felt how foolish he was, how foolish he had been, giving his heart sincerely only to receive such a result. The boy did not reply to the text message but fell silent instead. A month passed, and Xue'er had not received any messages from Wei Yun. She thought Wei Yun must be heartbroken and unwilling to speak to her anymore. She regretted why she had deceived him in the first place. She inexplicably felt her heart ache deeply, feeling that the past month without Wei Yun had been very bleak.

Unable to resist, she picked up her phone and sent Wei Yun a text message. The content was: [Are you mad? Why haven't you spoken to me for so long? Are you even a man? Are you even a soldier? Over something so trivial, you ignore me. Grumpy!]

During the month of training, the boy tried to forget those painful memories, but when he saw Xue'er's text message, he remembered everything about her. So, he picked up the phone he hadn't touched for a month and replied to Xue'er with a text message: [It's not that I'm angry and ignoring you, but that I've been busy with training recently, and there hasn't been time. Every time the training ends, it's too late to send you a text message. Do you know how sad your deceit made me? How can I accept it?] Upon seeing the text message, Xue'er didn't know why, but tears streamed down her face—whether from excitement or sorrow.

They resumed contact quickly. Soon, Wei Yun no longer needed daily training. On a sunny weekend, Wei Yun invited Xue'er out. They agreed to meet at the internet cafe since that was where they had met. Wei Yun had never seen Xue'er, not once throughout their entire relationship. This was their first meeting, and he was both excited and nervous. He was worried he wouldn't recognize Xue'er, leading to an awkward situation. However, fate seemed to favor Wei Yun. Relying solely on his intuition, he looked toward the entrance of the internet cafe. After a while, a young woman dressed in camouflage caught his eye.

Without calling to confirm, he walked straight toward the girl. Xue'er had seen his picture and knew the boy in military uniform walking toward her was Wei Yun. Yet, she wanted to test Wei Yun's recognition skills and deliberately pretended not to know him, continuing to walk forward. Just as they were about to pass each other, Wei Yun called out the girl's name. Xue'er stopped and made a funny face at Wei Yun, asking him, "Are you sure I'm Xue'er? What if I'm not?"

"Little missy, your actions have already told me that you are. You're Xue'er, 100% correct." Xue'er curiously asked, "How do you know? How did you figure it out?" "Your clothes. You said you like the military, naturally, camouflage would be your favorite, right?" "So smart! I give up. No wonder you're a soldier. How do you plan to keep me standing in the hallway forever? Aren't you going to find me a place to sit?" Xue'er's mood shifted from happiness to dissatisfaction.

"Let me think about it. We can't just hang out at the internet cafe for the first time, right? I need to figure out where to go." "Forget it. You're new here, let this local take you around!" Saying this, she pulled Wei Yun and headed outside.

Xue'er took Wei Yun to the Culture Square, which was perfect for chatting. The wide square was bustling with playful people. The two found an empty bench and sat down to chat. When Wei Yun learned Xue'er's true identity, he was surprised, feeling that her age didn't match her thoughts. Simultaneously, he believed he and Xue'er wouldn't have a future, but he couldn't bear to hurt such a pure and adorable girl. He didn't want to leave her with any bad memories, so he tried his best to preserve their budding feelings. Time passed like this.

As their meetings became more frequent, they held hands while strolling through malls, eating snacks. Xue'er never allowed Wei Yun to wear civilian clothes because she loved seeing him in his military uniform, finding it handsome and making her proud. Whenever they shopped together or ate snacks, people around them would cast envious glances. On the street, Xue'er always hopped joyfully ahead of Wei Yun, who watched her happily from behind but couldn't feel the same joy, knowing he didn't know how much longer he could stay by her side. His family had called him, urging him to return home next month for matchmaking, saying he was old enough and it was time to marry. Despite Wei Yun's efforts to delay repeatedly, his family directly contacted his superior and applied for leave on his behalf, forcing him to go back.

Wei Yun decided to return home first to deal with the situation and informed his parents that he already had a girlfriend. Time spent with the person he loved was always fleeting. Wei Yun saw Xue'er off onto the bus. As the bus drove away, he took out his phone and sent her a text message. On the bus, Xue'er, reluctant to part, heard her phone ring. [Dear Xue'er, did you have fun today? I know you didn't want to leave. Don't be sad. When I come back from home, I'll definitely make up for it and play with you enough. I didn't tell you earlier because I have to go home tomorrow to handle some things, but don't worry, I'll be back soon to be with you. Wait for me, dear.]

Xue'er, reading the message, had an uneasy premonition, feeling that Wei Yun might leave her after returning home. She told herself it was impossible, that she was just overthinking. With unease, she went home. Back home, Wei Yun followed his parents' arrangements and met the girl they had arranged for him. Her family was a military household, and she was the daughter of high-ranking officials. Wei Yun's parents hoped their son could rise to prominence, live a life of ease, and bring honor to the family.

Wei Yun was utterly unwilling. When he told his parents about Xue'er, whom he had met at his post, they gave him a resounding 100% opposition. Wei Yun emphasized repeatedly how good Xue'er was and how much he loved her, but his parents paid no heed to his words. Out of filial piety, Wei Yun could only painfully abandon his love for the girl he adored. Wei Yun returned, and he called Xue'er, telling her many things about how much he missed her and asking her to take care of herself in the future. He also said he wouldn't have time to be with her anymore.

Xue'er detected the underlying meaning in Wei Yun's words: he was leaving her and couldn't continue loving her. Xue'er cried, unable to utter a single word, not knowing what to do. Without Wei Yun, what would become of her?

The next day, Wei Yun's transfer order came down, transferring him back to his hometown's military unit. Xue'er rushed from home to the military base, seeing Wei Yun by the gate, luggage in hand, ready to leave. Xue'er ran to Wei Yun's side without crying or causing a scene but instead gave him her first kiss. Tears streaming down her face, Xue'er told Wei Yun, "No matter whether you will love me in the future or not, I want to use this action to tell you that I love you, from the bottom of my heart. I hope you remember that I once appeared in your life, even if I'm just a passerby."

After saying this, Xue'er turned and ran away. She didn't want to see the back of the person she loved as he left, so she chose to leave first. She didn't know what Wei Yun was like behind her. Returning to reality, Xue'er cried again, looking at the QQ account Wei Yun had never logged into since their breakup, crying so sadly and sorrowfully. She only hoped that one day in the future, she could see her beloved man again.
