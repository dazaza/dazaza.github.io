Netizens post online to sell human placenta; experts say eating it may cause diseases

by anonymous on 2012-02-16 10:33:41

Northeast Network, Qiqihar, March 23rd - On March 11th, in the online chat tool group "Hecheng Living Network Mommy Baby", nearly 500 netizens all received an email from a netizen named "Baby Mom" asking "Is there anyone who wants to buy a placenta?"

After the email was sent out, netizens responded enthusiastically; some inquired about the price, others asked whether the placenta was from a male or female baby, and some asked for contact information... More netizens, however, expressed that openly selling placentas was hard to understand. On March 17th, a netizen who had posted a request to purchase informed the reporter that this netizen had already spent 200 yuan to buy a human placenta from "Baby Mom," but this netizen was unwilling to discuss the specifics.

Regarding the phenomenon of selling placentas online, the reporter consulted with Xue Feng, deputy lawyer at Heilongjiang Sifang Law Firm. He said that currently, there is no explicit legal provision stating whether buying and selling placentas is legal. He personally believes that placentas should not be bought and sold like commodities. Lawyer Xue Feng also looked up relevant materials and found that in a reply issued by the Ministry of Health in 2005, it was clearly recorded: After childbirth, the placenta belongs to the mother. If the mother abandons or donates the placenta, it can be handled by medical institutions. No unit or individual may buy or sell placentas. If the placenta could cause the spread of infectious diseases, medical institutions should promptly inform the mother and handle it according to the "Law on the Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases," the "Regulations on Medical Waste Management," and other relevant regulations.

Does eating human placenta really have the effect described in folklore as being able to "cure all diseases"? Regarding this, the reporter interviewed Guan Yu, the director of the First Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology at the Third Affiliated Hospital of Qiqihar Medical University. According to her introduction, the placenta has a certain tonic effect on various deficiency syndromes, but its functions are not as described in folklore as being able to "cure all diseases." Its functions are limited. Eating placenta is suitable for those with TCM deficiency syndromes. Some people themselves do not have deficiency syndromes, so they should not blindly take tonics. Additionally, the placenta contains maternal blood, and if the pregnant woman's blood carries infectious disease viruses such as hepatitis B virus or HIV, these viruses will enter the placenta. If these viruses are not completely eliminated, eating them afterward could lead to contracting hepatitis B or AIDS.
