Website Optimization

Website optimization includes two parts:

1. Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

2. User Experience Optimization (UEO)

SEO

By adjusting the structure of a website and changing its content, it becomes easier for search engines to index, thereby achieving the purpose of higher rankings.

Recently, many novice webmasters have been asking me what SEO is, and I've also noticed that there are many friends on Baidu and Google searching for what SEO is. Since there is so much confusion about the definition of SEO, I will explain here what SEO is. Below is an official definition and other websites' definitions of SEO that I have compiled:

SEO is the abbreviation of Search Engine Optimization. In English, it is described as "using some techniques to make your website rank at the top of the search engine when someone uses a search engine to find something." Translated into Chinese, it means "search engine optimization," which can generally be abbreviated as website optimization. Related search knowledge includes Search Engine Positioning (SEP) and Search Engine Ranking (SER).

The main work of SEO involves understanding how various search engines crawl internet pages, how they index them, and how they determine the search result rankings for specific keywords, in order to optimize web pages accordingly. This improves their ranking in search engines, increases website traffic, and ultimately enhances the website's sales or promotional capabilities.

Searching is one of the most commonly used network behaviors besides email. Searching for information through search engines is the primary method by which today's internet users look for online information and resources. Search engine marketing has become the most important part of online marketing. How to get one's website indexed by major search engines and then obtain a high ranking has become a topic that website builders rack their brains over.

Abroad, SEO started earlier, and those who specialize in SEO are called "Search Engine Optimizers" by Google, abbreviated as SEOs. Since Google is currently the largest search engine provider in the world, Google has become the main research object for SEOs worldwide. For this reason, the official Google website has a dedicated page introducing SEO and expressing Google's attitude towards SEO.

Search engine optimization is a technology that follows the scientific and comprehensive theoretical mechanism of search engines. It reasonably plans and deploys the website structure, webpage text language, and interactive diplomatic strategies among sites to uncover the maximum potential of a website, giving it a strong natural ranking competitive advantage in search engines, thereby promoting corporate online sales and strengthening online branding.

SEO relies on an understanding of search engines, accumulating many effective methods and resources. This makes your website comply with search engine rules (not exploiting search engine vulnerabilities or defects). When users input specific keywords to look up information, your page appears in a prominent position on the "search engine results page," thus guiding a large number of users to browse your website. These users all found your website through the same keywords, showing interest in your website's content, making it easier to convert them into customers. Outdoor signs, posters, newspapers, television, and other media have a large audience, but the majority are not your potential customers. This is one of the advantages of search engine marketing.

What is SEO? Different search engines provide different answers. Optimization methods for different search engines also vary. There are significant differences between Baidu and Google, and as search engines continue to develop, many methods become ineffective. Therefore, SEO is not something that can be done casually; continuous understanding of different engines and the accumulation of extensive experience are required to be a qualified SEO.

Website optimization refers to improving the ranking of a user's website in search engines as the basic starting point and fundamental goal. It involves professional analysis and detailed improvement suggestions from three aspects: the user's website, search engine optimization (such as GOOGLE, Baidu, Yahoo, Sogou), and website operation and maintenance. Abroad, SEO is already a relatively mature industry, while domestically it is still in the initial development stage. Before starting this series, you need to understand the following terms: Directory (Directory) - A directory is a search result edited manually. Most directories rely on manual submissions rather than crawlers (spiders).

Keywords, keyterms, and keyphrases (Keyword, keyterm, and keyphrase)

Keywords, keyterms, and keyphrases are the words that websites are ranked by on search engine results pages (also known as SERPs). Depending on the site audience, you can choose a single word, a combination of multiple words, or an entire phrase. For simplicity, this article will use the term keyword to represent all three types.

Link farm (Link farm)

In SEO terminology, a link farm refers to a page full of links that have no practical function. They exist solely as links without any actual context. Those who use black hat SEO methods take advantage of link farms by adding a large number of links on a page, hoping to trick Google™ and other search engines into believing the page is valuable.

The goal of SEO

The goal of SEO is to improve the ranking of web pages for target keywords.

Ranking algorithm (Ranking algorithm)

A ranking algorithm is the rule used by search engines to evaluate and rank lists of web pages in their index. The ranking algorithm determines which results are relevant to a particular query.

Search engine marketing (Search engine marketing, SEM)

The term SEM can be used interchangeably with SEO, but SEM often refers to promoting websites to search engines through paid advertising while applying SEO techniques.

Search engine optimization (Search engine optimization, SEO)

SEO optimizes content based on its appeal and visibility to search engines, allowing web pages to be selected by search engines. SEO is mainly used to improve the ranking of organic listings.

Search engine results page (Search engine results page, SERP)

SERP is the list or result displayed for a specific search. Sometimes SERP is defined as the arrangement (placement) of search engine results. For the purposes of this series, we refer to it as a page rather than an arrangement. In the field of SEO, achieving good performance in SERP is everything.
