Lei Jun sells Xiaomi products like selling health products, Mi fans participate actively

by anonymous on 2013-09-11 14:06:13

What is the most lacking in 21st century enterprises? It's the satisfaction of user needs. This may sound arbitrary, but it just goes to show how important user needs are. Let’s first look at an example.

Health supplements are useless? The elderly are willing!

Recently, the media has repeatedly exposed some marketing groups that sell expensive health supplements to the elderly. They first organize large events, inviting the elderly with small gifts, then showing concern and warmth, followed by a lecture on life philosophy and health, then promoting products, and finally the elderly go home excitedly after purchasing them. Of course, these health supplements have little effect; they can be used as something similar to malted milk. But must we say that the money spent by the elderly was wasted? Not necessarily. The elderly gain much, actually:

1. They gain attention from others; if there are regular activities, they will regularly receive attention and care;

2. Many elderly people gather together, creating resonance and a lively atmosphere;

3. The miraculous effects introduced by the master give the elderly a positive mindset and hope (a positive attitude is very important for health);

4. They are taught many principles, gaining a set of theories and beliefs, increasing their spiritual wealth;

5. They gain someone to admire, which is also a kind of belief, strengthening their psychology.

Looking back, let us see what Lei Jun's Xiaomi brings to its fans:

1. They gain attention; Xiaomi uses forums and other channels to actively involve fans, giving them a sense of belonging;

2. Many fans gather together, creating resonance;

3. The various powerful features of Xiaomi phones make the fans extremely excited;

4. Xiaomi's low price and high performance have become a model and theory, with strong guidance and reference;

5. Lei Jun gives everyone a sense of trust and faith through each new product release and shipment.

In short, I do not think selling health supplements is inappropriate, nor do I think selling Xiaomi phones is inappropriate. Both genuinely meet user needs and have certain therapeutic effects, although the efficacy of health supplements is weak, while Xiaomi is still a solid IT product. Xiaomi creates not only products but also fans, and fans mean not only sales volume but also user enthusiasm, and human emotions actually have enormous energy.

Mi Fans Emphasize Participation

"Fan" is a phonetic translation of the English word "Fans" (meaning fanaticism or passion, later extended to mean movie fans or star chasers), and fan marketing (Fans marketing) refers to the commercial concept where companies use excellent products or corporate fame to attract a large consumer base as fans, using the mutual transmission among fans to achieve marketing purposes. Users become fans because the company and product bring them joy; remember, money is easy to obtain, but joy is hard to buy. Products that bring users joy will inevitably gain fans. Let's take a look at some joyful moments of Xiaomi fans:

1. Every product launch, with ultra-high cost-performance, makes fans jump with joy, proving that their choice is correct, bringing happiness;

2. Every debate and comparison with competitors, gaining advantages through software and hardware characteristics, brings joy to fans;

3. Through forums and other forms, maintaining everyone's feelings and interests, creating resonance, bringing joy;

4. Through Lei Jun's repeated display of personal charm, confidence, and determination, deepening trust and recognition, bringing joy;

5. Each instance of scarcity marketing, making those who manage to buy feel overjoyed, bringing great satisfaction (getting F codes similarly satisfies).

So, can't Xiaomi's competitors bring joy to users? For example, Lenovo's Kobe phone, OPPO's selfie phone, Huawei's ultra-thin phone, Meizu's conscientious phone, aren't they as effective as Xiaomi? There is one weakness: price. For users who lack money (those who don’t lack money go for Apple and Samsung), the joy brought by functions, design, and marketing does not outweigh the joy of getting a good deal.

Create Fans First, Let Fans Drive Everything

Xiaomi, through its products and marketing, has created Mi Fans, including brain-dead level fans and rational fans, which is Xiaomi's proudest achievement, far surpassing just a phone or a TV. A friend of mine, married with children, owning two houses and two cars, still remains enthusiastic about Xiaomi, showing that Xiaomi not only conquers the underprivileged but also gains the affection of the poor and even low-income populations. With these fans, it is not difficult for Xiaomi to fully develop smartphones, TVs, laptops, tablets, smartwatches, and more software creations simultaneously are also reliable. Therefore, in the short term, Xiaomi's rivals are domestic brands like ZTE, Huawei, Coolpad, and Lenova, or some home appliance manufacturers, but in the future, Xiaomi will likely face many more competitors across many fields.

Passionate fans, or calm and independent-minded fans, are invaluable treasures for any enterprise. Giants like Intel and Microsoft spend huge amounts on marketing every year, yet they may not cultivate many fans willing to cheer for them, but Xiaomi has succeeded. In the past, the few times I encountered fan explosions were for Sony's PS, BenQ's MP3, ThinkPad's laptops, Nokia's phones, nVidia's graphics cards, Apple's phones and tablets, etc. Now, let's see how long Xiaomi can lead the trend.
