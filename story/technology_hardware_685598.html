Submitting your site (registering web pages) to any search engine is quite simple. On the home page of the search engine, find the "Add Your Site" or "Register URL" link. Just follow the instructions to add your website. Some search engines will let you submit all the pages you want indexed, like Altavista. Others require you to submit the URL for just one page of your entire site, and the rest of the pages will be searched automatically. This means that the search engine will automatically add the remaining pages. Google is an example of such a case. Submitting to directories like Yahoo! can be somewhat tedious because they keep web pages in separate categories (directories), so you need to find the best category under which your site will fit. Remember, these are managed by humans. If you submit to irrelevant categories, it may be considered spam, causing your site to be ignored. Getting Indexed Now you know that submitting your site isn't a big issue, but how confident are you that your site will be indexed? Indexing simply means getting a place for your site in the search engine's database. How do you find out if your site has been added to the search engine? It's easy. If you search using your URL or domain name as keywords and the results show up, then you are indexed by the search engine. Each search engine has its own policy (secret!) for accepting submitted URLs. Some search engines will take less time than others to list you. Be patient. Directories take more time because they require human intervention. It's a good practice to check after 30 minutes or 40 days whether the site has been listed here. META tags announce all the details of your site to search engines. Hidden in the site's code, they can provide different information to search engines such as keywords, description, author, and more. Although these can be included on any webpage, they are essential for promoting your site through search engines. Each search engine assigns its own weightage to META tags. Therefore, it's important to know some interesting things when dealing with these search engines. You need to put real effort into finding the best keywords. Search engines and directories focus on website promotion through search engines. There are usually two types. A search engine is a software-compiled index (such as Google, Hotbot, etc.), or a human-compiled directory (such as Yahoo, dmoz, etc.). In other words, it's a huge database containing links to all websites submitted to them, including details of each site. The main difference between these two is that one is handled by a person while the other by a spider. In some cases, someone reviewing your site might reject it because it's not interesting enough in terms of type or quality. But in the case of a computerized search engine, a program called a robot visits the submitted site, collects information, and then includes it in the search engine's website.
