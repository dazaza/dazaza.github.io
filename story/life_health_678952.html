[What is the cause of breast redness and swelling? It could be a symptom of acute mastitis.]

1. In the initial stage of acute simple mastitis, the main symptoms are breast distension and pain, local high skin temperature, tenderness, hard lumps with unclear boundaries, and touch-induced pain.

2. Acute suppurative mastitis presents with local skin redness, swelling, heat, and pain, more obvious hard lumps, increased touch-induced pain, along with chills, high fever, headache, weakness, fast pulse, and other systemic symptoms. Swollen lymph nodes may appear under the armpit with tenderness, elevated white blood cell count in blood tests, and severe cases can lead to sepsis.

3. Abscess formation: due to ineffective treatment measures or worsening of the condition, local tissues undergo necrosis and liquefaction. Various sizes of infection foci merge to form abscesses. Abscesses can be single-chambered or multi-chambered. Superficial abscesses are easily detected, while deeper abscesses have less obvious fluctuation and are harder to detect. If the patient has significant systemic symptoms and local and systemic drug treatments are not effective, it is necessary to perform puncture at the painful area. Diagnosis of abscesses can be confirmed by drawing pus or finding white blood cells in smears.
