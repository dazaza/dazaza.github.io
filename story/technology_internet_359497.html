Sanya Leisure Self-guided Tour (Seven Maps and N Photos)

 

As autumn departs and winter arrives, it's the perfect time for a southern coastal getaway. Last winter, my husband and I spent 7 days exploring Sanya in Hainan and Wuzhizhou Island on a completely self-guided trip. Below is our detailed itinerary, hoping to provide some help to those planning a trip to Sanya.

D1: From Capital Airport to Sanya Phoenix Airport - Experience Flying

We took China Airlines flight CA1345 from Capital Airport at 16:00 PM directly to Sanya Phoenix Airport. Our air tickets were booked in advance on the Air China website. As it was our first time flying, we were both nervous and excited. To capture the "cotton clouds" (my affectionate term for the clouds), we filled out our boarding passes online, choosing window seats which also saved us time (I must commend my husband's foresight). Flying was quite comfortable, not as intense as others had said, perhaps due to individual differences. I really enjoyed the captain's voice, his fluent English was pleasant and magnetic.

T3 Terminal and Airport Express ABC

Above the Clouds

The plane was like a mighty eagle, gracefully soaring between the blue sky and white clouds. Looking out the window at the fluffy clouds, I truly felt the essence of 'fish leap in the vast ocean, birds fly freely in the high sky'. Due to a slight delay, we arrived at Sanya Phoenix Airport around eight-thirty PM. Since it was our first time, we didn't pay attention to the corresponding baggage carousel when waiting for our luggage, causing some delay.

We had booked a house at Lanhai Garden in Sanya Bay through the Sanya homestay forum. At the airport exit, we took the shuttle bus to the city area, costing 15 yuan with a journey of about twenty minutes (actually, there are buses available right outside the airport that can get you there for just a few yuan, but we hadn't done enough research!). Upon reaching the neighborhood entrance, we called Lanhai Garden, and their staff came to pick us up with good attitudes. We had reserved apartments No. 38 and No. 39 on the fifth floor (since we went to Wuzhizhou Island in between, we couldn't book one room throughout, but they were adjacent with the same price of 198 yuan per night). The rooms were exactly as shown in the online photos, giving a homely feeling. After finishing washing up around ten-thirty PM, we still had no sleepiness. Sitting on the wicker chairs on the balcony, we felt the sea breeze and listened to the returning fishing boats' whistles and the waves crashing against the shore, slowly getting drunk in the heart.

 

Small Tips:1. Prepare supplies:◆ Important items: ID card, credit card, cash, air ticket, hotel reservation confirmation, Sanya travel information, home keys and access cards.◆ Sun protection: Umbrella (as Sanya has frequent rain, a dual-purpose umbrella is best), sunglasses, sun hat.◆ Hygiene products: Toothbrush, toothpaste, facial cleanser, shampoo, sunscreen (SPF30++ recommended), whitening masks, soap, mirror, comb.◆ Clothing: Summer wear, swimming gear (swimsuit, towel, goggles, earplugs), sandals, jacket (due to temperature difference, long sleeves are essential, especially on Wuzhizhou Island).◆ Medicines: Antihistamines, cold medicine, antidiarrheal, anti-inflammatory drugs, bandages, mosquito repellent, seasickness medicine, Coptisine, Buffalo Horn Detox Pills (for eating seafood).◆ Food: Gum, chocolate and other snacks; no need to bring much, you can buy them in Sanya supermarkets.◆ Digital equipment: Camera, mobile phone (don't forget to recharge before departure), various chargers, laptop, if needed, bring a tripod for sunrise/sunset or night scenery.◆ Others: Wet wipes (for eating seafood), napkins, toilet paper, compass, flashlight, pen and notebook. Bring a small bag to carry your wallet and phone, keeping them close to prevent theft.

2. If your itinerary is confirmed, it's best to book your air tickets in advance for significant discounts. We booked ours about two weeks ahead, and got nearly half off.

3. It's best to arrive at the airport an hour early, as airlines stop check-in procedures 30 minutes before departure. You can handle your boarding pass online (if you have a printer), choose your seat, and save time; when checking in luggage, it's best to mark it clearly (tie a colored rope or stick a big label), similar bags are many, making it hard to find. After arriving at your destination, confirm your flight number and collect your checked luggage at the corresponding carousel. (Remember not to make mistakes.)

4. How to reach Beijing airport? Taxis aside, if taking the airport express, note: Fare: 25 yuan, total length: 28.5 kilometers, airport~Dongzhimen approximately 18 minutes. Airport Express operating hours: Dongzhimen Station first train at 6 am, last train at 11 pm; Terminal 3 station first train at 6:21, last train at 10:51 pm; Terminal 2 first train at 6:35, last train at 11:15 pm.

5. Regulations regarding carry-on and checked luggage

Restrictions on carry-on luggage

⑴ For domestic flights: Total weight of hand luggage should not exceed 5 kg, each piece of luggage should not exceed 20×40×55 cm (varies by airline). There are standard boxes at security checkpoints for testing the size of carry-on luggage. If the luggage fits inside the box, it can be carried on board, otherwise, it needs to be checked. ⑵ For international flights: Typically, total weight of hand luggage should not exceed 7 kg, each piece should not exceed 20×40×55 cm (sum of three sides not exceeding 115 cm). Passengers traveling to North America can only carry one piece of hand luggage. (Some airlines have special weight restrictions, please refer to the prompt on your air ticket or consult the airline.)

Regulations on checked luggage

⑴ For domestic flights: Adult or child ticket holders in first class have a free checked luggage allowance of 40 kg, business class 30 kg, economy class 20 kg. Infant ticket holders do not have a free luggage allowance.⑵ For international flights: Economy class passengers have a free checked luggage allowance of 20 kg, students with student passports have a free allowance of 30 kg; business class free allowance is 30 kg; first class free allowance is 40 kg. When the destination is America, the checked luggage can be two pieces, each not exceeding 23 kg, sum of three sides not exceeding 158 cm. When exceeded, passengers need to pay overweight fees. (Some airlines have special weight restrictions, please refer to the prompt on your air ticket or consult the airline.)★ Please refer to the Capital Airport terminal passenger service guide upon arrival and proceed to the specific check-in counter to check in your luggage.★ Items that cannot be checked as luggage include: important documents and materials, securities, money, drafts, jewelry, precious metals and their products, antiques, fragile and easily damaged items, perishable goods, samples, travel documents, valuable items, etc. (Please consult the airline for details.)★ Please remove old luggage tags and barcoded labels from your luggage/bag before checking in to ensure correct delivery to your destination.
