Mobile QQ: Falling on the stage of WeChat Cosplay

by geekzhang on 2013-05-13 11:46:51

By Gu XiaoboAt the recently concluded 2013 Mobile Internet Conference, Pony Ma stated that the mobile QQ product line had been thoroughly reviewed and would now compete differentially against WeChat.Immediately after this statement, the latest version of Mobile QQ 4.0 was officially launched on the App Store. This major update included a group assistant feature that allows users to create free QQ groups of up to 50 members, as well as support for adding friends and groups via QR codes.However, these significant improvements were not well-received by users. Among nearly 30,000 user reviews, 90% gave the lowest possible rating of one star.Similar to how the innovations in Windows 8 alienated users accustomed to Windows 7, the changes in Mobile QQ 4.0 would not immediately take effect. However, this marked an important milestone: under the direct leadership of Pony Ma, Mobile QQ, with its 500 million monthly active users, began challenging WeChat on mobile devices. Regardless of who wins or loses in this challenge, the biggest winner will still be Pony Ma.Missing a Good Opportunity Amidst UncertaintyIn May last year, Tencent reorganized its structure, dividing it into six business groups. QQ fell under SNG (Social Network Group), while Mobile QQ was assigned to MIG (Mobile Internet Group).Half a year later, Senior Vice President of Tencent and President of MIG, Liu Chengmin, suddenly announced his retirement. As a result, Mobile QQ was transferred back to the instant messaging team under SNG, personally supervised by Pony Ma. Other products and teams under MIG were also redistributed across various business groups.Since then, QQ, Mobile QQ, and QQ Zone have been grouped under one division. After several months of adjustments, the organizational relationships among these three products have been clarified, and the timing for Mobile QQ to exert its strength has matured.During this year-long period, however, WeChat expanded its user base to 300 million, nearing 400 million, and successfully developed mobile social networking through public accounts and Moments. Meanwhile, Mobile QQ remained just a mobile version of QQ; WeChat became the QQ of the mobile internet era.Cosplaying WeChat Faces ResistanceWeChat's business model is yet to fully form, but QQ’s decades of accumulation have already established a very mature business model. Pony Ma would not sit idly by and let Mobile QQ remain ineffective in the tumultuous mobile internet era. One ticket does not guarantee safe passage, and Pony Ma needs another ticket.Under the leadership of Allen Zhang, WeChat laid a solid foundation for mobile products, allowing Mobile QQ to naturally emulate WeChat's strategies. An interaction designer from Tencent's CDC (User Research and Experience Design Department) expressed on Zhihu: "As a product already validated by the market, why shouldn't Mobile QQ emulate WeChat? Revolutionary innovation in the interface only increases learning costs."Mere imitation in the interface cannot satisfy Mobile QQ's pursuit of catching up with WeChat. Thus, in version 4.0, Mobile QQ adopted new strategies: canceling online status display so users cannot see if their QQ friends are online, nor distinguish between being online via WeChat or PC. Users can only see online friends by clicking on 'Dynamics - Online Friends.'This adjustment indicates that Mobile QQ is beginning to follow WeChat's always-online route. This is a significant difference between Mobile QQ and PC QQ, and an attempt to break away from being merely a mobile version of QQ.Unexpectedly, users did not accept such improvements at all. Comments on the App Store show that by noon on May 12th, Mobile QQ received a total of 256,746 evaluations, with 29,358 for version 4.0, accounting for 11.4% of the total comments. Alarmingly, over 90% of these nearly 30,000 evaluations rated the app with the lowest score of one star.User complaints about this version mainly focused on the adjustment of online status. Users could no longer see if their contacts were online, leading many to express discomfort. Numerous one-star raters mentioned this was their first review due to dissatisfaction with the change.User Xie Jinfu commented on Zhihu: "Displaying different online statuses describes a user's usage scenarios. When two people chat, whether they are online via mobile or computer affects the conversation content. If the other party is online via mobile, we generally do not send urgent files, large images, or links." Meanwhile, some users noted that without knowing the other party's login status, considerations such as data usage, scenario, and input methods lead to inconvenience, necessitating asking how the other person is connected.Seeking Differentiation, Risking Becoming a Supporting RolePerhaps even Tencent itself hasn't clearly defined the positioning of Mobile QQ. Derived from PC QQ, Mobile QQ is moving further away from its parent body and closer to WeChat. Stuck between WeChat and PC QQ, Mobile QQ risks becoming a secondary role in both areas.Founder of iLoveiTech Luo Chao indicated that although there are indeed overlapping areas in user demographics, scenarios, and functions between Mobile QQ and WeChat, each can encroach upon the other's territory. WeChat's current focus is on commercialization, whereas Mobile QQ must consider how to differentiate itself from its rapidly rising younger sibling.However, WeChat has already achieved some success with public accounts and Moments, with features like Shake and漂流瓶 once being highly popular. The upcoming game platform is highly anticipated, and WeChat spans communication, social networking, and media attributes. How much room does Mobile QQ have left for differentiation?On the other hand, Mobile QQ is gradually developing some functionalities that are difficult to synchronize with PC QQ, such as online status and high-definition avatars. Former Tencent product experience designer Chen Xi noted that Mobile QQ hopes to achieve greater development on mobile devices, but making these decisions often conflicts with maintaining consistency with PC QQ. Tencent frequently attempts bold self-disruptions, but such disruptions often come with risks. These difficult choices have led to some seemingly disharmonious functional characteristics in the current Mobile QQ.The real first step towards mobilization for Mobile QQ was met with fierce criticism. The deeply ingrained user habits formed by PC QQ make it hard to pivot. Currently, Mobile QQ faces tough decisions regarding direction and positioning.However, one thing is certain: regardless of whether Mobile QQ or WeChat prevails, Pony Ma will be the biggest winner. QQ's position on PCs is unshakable, and WeChat has already helped Tencent firmly establish its footing in the mobile internet era. Both hands are flesh, whether one side gains or loses slightly, it may be trivial to Pony Ma.
