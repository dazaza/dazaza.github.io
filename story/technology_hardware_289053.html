Disease enters through the mouth: be careful not to swallow harmful substances unwittingly.

by bbeij62s on 2009-12-02 14:31:50

"The saying 'disease enters through the mouth' tells us that we shouldn't eat indiscriminately. However, in many cases, it's not that we intentionally consume unhygienic or unhealthy food, but rather that we unknowingly ingest harmful substances. Below are some unexpected situations that might lead to consuming the wrong things.

Food wrapped in newspapers:

Newspaper ink contains a toxic substance called polychlorinated biphenyl (PCB), which has a chemical structure similar to pesticides. If food is wrapped in newspapers, the PCBs can seep into the food and then enter the body. When the amount of PCBs stored in the human body reaches 0.5-2 grams, it can cause poisoning. Mild cases may result in swollen eyelids, sweaty palms, and red bumps all over the body; severe cases may lead to nausea, vomiting, abnormal liver function, muscle pain, persistent coughing, and even death.

Moldy rice, peanuts, and corn:

These contain aflatoxins, which are currently recognized worldwide as potent carcinogens and can easily cause liver and esophageal cancer. Some people believe that washing several times or disinfecting at high temperatures can remove the toxins, but once aflatoxins contaminate food, they are very difficult to completely eliminate.

Tea scale:

Some people think that tea scale is formed by long-term deposition of tea water and is harmless to the body, so they rarely clean it. In fact, this is incorrect. Tea scale contains various harmful metals such as cadmium, lead, mercury, arsenic, and certain carcinogenic substances like nitrosamines, which can lead to diseases in the kidneys, liver, intestines, and stomach.

The rotten parts of fruit:

After fruits rot, microorganisms produce various harmful substances during their metabolic processes, especially accelerating the reproduction of fungi. Some fungi have carcinogenic effects and can spread from the rotten part through the juice to the non-rotten part. Therefore, even if the rotten part is removed, the remaining fruit should still not be eaten.

Monosodium glutamate (MSG) in alkaline foods:

When MSG meets alkaline foods, it turns into disodium glutamate, losing its flavor-enhancing properties. When heated to 120°C, it turns into the carcinogenic substance sodium pyroglutamate. Therefore, MSG should not be added to foods containing baking soda or alkali; when making soup or dishes, MSG should be added just before removing from heat to avoid prolonged cooking.

Burnt fish and meat:

The incomplete combustion of fat in fish and meat produces a large amount of V-amino methyl derivatives, which are carcinogens stronger than aflatoxins. Therefore, when cooking fish and meat, attention should be paid to the heat control, and once burnt, it should not be eaten.

Rotten cabbage:

Both rotten and insufficiently pickled cabbage contain carcinogenic nitrites.

Grilled food:

All grilled foods are prone to produce a highly carcinogenic substance—benzopyrene, which is the same substance produced by repeatedly used oil in fried foods.

Toxic substances on tablecloths:

Many restaurants use thin plastic tablecloths made of polyvinyl chloride (PVC) on their tables, which contain toxic free radicals that can enter the body through utensils via food, leading to chronic poisoning. Therefore, when dining out, chopsticks should not be placed directly on tables covered with plastic film.

Paint on chopsticks:

Painted chopsticks are still very common, but many people don't know that these paints contain lead, benzene, and other chemical substances, which often enter our bodies along with peeling paint, causing certain health hazards."
