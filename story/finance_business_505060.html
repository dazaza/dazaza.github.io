On May 27th, the Criminal Investigation Team of the Jinwan Branch of the Municipal Public Security Bureau received a report that Zhang, a retired worker of a certain unit in Xigu, was defrauded of 13,000 yuan around 9 o'clock on the morning of the same day outside a large pharmacy opposite the Xigu District Hospital by someone who claimed to be looking for the old Chinese doctor Wang Yutang for treatment.

(Reported by Evening News reporter Wang Jin, special correspondent Wei Liwu, text and photos) Recently, after more than four months of arduous investigation, the Jinwan Branch of the Municipal Public Security Bureau eradicated a major criminal gang that mainly targeted elderly women. They used excuses like "miracle doctor" treatment and rituals to ward off disasters, committing more than 40 fraud cases in the urban areas of our city and other places such as Chengdu in Sichuan, Yinchuan in Ningxia, Xining in Qinghai, and Baiyin City in our province, involving more than 1 million yuan in total. They arrested 11 suspects including Wang, seized two vehicles and nine mobile phones involved in the case, and recovered more than 150,000 yuan in cash related to the case.

Through interviews and investigations, the police determined that the perpetrators were three men and one woman, and the female scammer was addicted to drugs. On June 24th, the police locked onto the whereabouts of this female scammer, who often appeared in the Xigu city area and indulged in drug use with her ex-husband. On July 7th, the police captured the ex-husband of the female scammer and lured the female scammer into being captured, identifying her as Zhang. According to Zhang's confession, this fraud gang was led by Wang and Sun, with nine accomplices including Sun, Ma, Cao, and Gao. From July 8th to August 23rd, the police set up a net and control system, capturing all nine members of the gang. Upon interrogation, it was found that Wang and his group were all drug addicts and gamblers, with two having been punished for fraud before. Since January 2009, Wang's group had committed more than 40 fraud cases in the Chengguan, Qilihe, Xigu, Anning, and Baiyin areas, as well as in Xining, Yinchuan, Taiyuan, Xi'an, and Chengdu, involving more than 1 million yuan. The smallest amount of fraud was more than 3,800 yuan, and the largest was 160,000 yuan. All the proceeds from the fraud, including gold and silver jewelry, were sold and used entirely for drug addiction and gambling. Currently, the police have verified and confirmed 30 fraud cases in the four urban districts of our city and Baiyin City, involving more than 1.01 million yuan.

On September 8th, the police tracked down and captured the fence Cui and Hu from a certain mall in Lanzhou, seizing two cars, nine mobile phones used in the crime, and recovering more than 150,000 yuan in stolen funds.
