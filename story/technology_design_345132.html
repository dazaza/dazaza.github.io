Be an Imperfect Parent

by gfrw0738 on 2010-04-13 23:21:43

A complete hatred for children is a cage; perfect parents deprive their children of solitude; children have the right to choose, even if it means choosing misfortune. These are some of the latest educational aphorisms from certain psychological educators. "Being a parent" as a "profession" is destined to end in failure.

★ Every advancement comes with side effects. For two centuries, the progress of Eastern societies has been the collective prioritization of survival over individual freedom. Within families—be it between spouses or among family members—the mutual understanding and tolerance have been completely restructured. And the side effect of individual freedom and full development is anxiety.

★ In the past, raising children was entirely left to chance—"extensive operation." Now, people spend too much time and energy learning how to be parents, and they are often dissatisfied with themselves for not doing well enough. In the past, children feared not meeting their parents' expectations; now, the situation is reversed, with parents fearing not meeting their children's needs.

★ In modern society, each family lives in its own closed space, with almost no mutual support. If one of the parents gets seriously ill or dies, the child’s world collapses.

★ In modern families, the level of education is higher than ever before, but the effectiveness of education is worse than ever before because books can't fully compensate for the lack of leading by example.

★ In the past, all young women who became mothers instinctively knew how to raise children. Grandmothers were like "clinical doctors," diagnosing issues such as diarrhea or dehydration... Today, mothers can only take their children to see pediatricians or look up information in books. The relationship between parents and children has become technical and professionalized. Being a parent has become a profession.

★ The more children become rare treasures, the more parents fear making mistakes. But there is no such thing as a perfect person, so no child can reach the height that parents hope for. In this sense, the "profession" of "being a parent" is destined to end in failure.

Scientific knowledge makes children equal to, or even superior to, their parents.

★ In the past, women would say: "I will have a child for my husband, he will be happy"; now they say, "I want to give my child a father"—children have replaced fathers and become the center of the family.

★ In the late stages of pregnancy, ultrasound examinations can even detect the behavioral tendencies of the fetus. Future mothers then begin to plan: "He will be a soccer star," "She will be a dancer"... Science and technology have participated in the creation of myths. In early human societies, an individual's development was entirely determined by the tribe; today, a child can be any one of a thousand different futures. In the past, children served the plans of the family; now, families serve the future of the child.

★ The authority of fathers is diminishing—a fact. When the level of science and technology was relatively low, and the functioning of society still depended on male muscle strength, men, as symbols of strength, obtained authority. Fathers were the pillars of the family, and their wives were in subordinate positions. However, after two generations, the value of muscle strength has lost its original significance—except in sports like rugby and boxing matches. Scientific knowledge has become the new symbol of strength. In this artificial ecological environment, women's abilities are in no way inferior to men's. Thus, the authority of fathers was dismantled by feminists in the 1970s, but mothers cannot yet share or replace the father's position within the family.

★ Scientific knowledge makes children equal to, or even superior to, their parents. What parents can pass on to their children is becoming less and less. In the past, fathers trained sons in occupational skills, and mothers taught daughters how to raise children. Today, all that knowledge is useless, and children are becoming the teachers.

★ In the past, people clearly knew what was passed down from generation to generation: a surname, wealth, land, a profession, a religion, a culture... Families said: you will believe in this religion, you will marry at this age, you will have children... Today, we pass down less tangible things to the next generation. The most important inheritance—happiness, anxiety, indifference—is accomplished unconsciously. Many things are passed down to the next generation without being subject to people's will.

★ Faced with children who have gone astray into drugs and violence, defeated parents anxiously examine themselves: Do I fully understand my child's pain? Am I not doing enough? What did I do wrong? In fact, quite the opposite, these parents are overly permissive and dedicated. They are successful in their professional lives as doctors, lawyers, etc., but behind their children, they lack authority and willingly abandon it.

Children do not need perfect parents.

★ Relax, parents! Don’t forget that a child with perfect parents may suffer from a lack of imperfection: this is equivalent to forcing the child to be happy, forcing them to be happy—because it is the predetermined goal of the parents! "You know, you should have your own choice, even if it means choosing misfortune." A mother with a good sense of humor often says this to her children.

★ Since the late 1960s, psychologists have become educational celebrities through various psychological consultations and children's magazines. One kind of doctrine has gained prominence: there are no inherently bad children, only those who haven't received enough love. This generation's mistake lies in refusing conflict. Do they really believe that children can grow up in a conflict-free environment? Can they not speak about it? Remember, uncontrolled desires are a form of violence, and controlled conflicts are beneficial to children's growth.

★ Children are at the stage of striving for independence. When parents surround them with love, it's like locking them in a cage of love. So if children want to break out of the cage, the first thing they will strongly resist is their parents. Parents often say about their children: "They are angels at home and devils outside." They are angels at home because they fear the outside world; they are devils because they know they can get away with it.

★ Through research on the personalities of many terrifying children, it was found that most of them received very good education and their parents loved them very much. Their family environments were too good. Out of kindness, the parents took care of everything for them, solved all problems, and eliminated all difficulties. This made it impossible for the children to discover themselves—they didn't know who they were, what they wanted, the consequences of their actions, what tests they could endure, or their own worth. The girls then turned to drugs and terror to try dangerous things; boys might turn to sexual games.

★ Some parents hide their own pain and cannot bear their children to endure even a bit of suffering. They don't realize that admitting their own imperfections—weaknesses, cowardice, and setbacks—to their children is beneficial. Children need complete parents, not perfect ones without flaws. It is only through interacting with imperfect parents that children can grow.
