**Muted Cyan**

& nbsp; & nbsp; The commonly used method often results in an inaccurate color, and the color fades quickly. There’s a better method: first bleach the hair to level 8, then apply the base color. A 4/0 base color will result in deep green, while a 5/0 will yield a slightly lighter green. This method produces accurate colors that fade more slowly.

**Smoke Gray**

First, bleach the hair to above level 9. Depending on the condition of the hair, the number of bleaching sessions required may vary from 1 to 3 times. Do not assess the color based on the number of bleaching sessions. Wuhu Hairdressing Training [http://www.whainisi.org]. You must carefully observe whether the base color has reached the required level and the correct tone.

1. A: Use a same-level [blue-purple] to neutralize the yellow (can be made by mixing blue + red + large amounts of 0/00 to form a light blue-purple).

B: You can also use gray tones mixed with some purple-red to neutralize the yellow. (If you only use gray tones, the percentage of blue particles is usually higher, which can result in a gray-green tone rather than pure gray. Adding some purple-red can cancel out the green, making the gray cleaner.)

2. When neutralizing, use low-volume hydrogen peroxide (10vol). Time should be judged visually. Once the yellow is completely neutralized, rinse immediately. If left too long, it may turn light gray-purple (gray with a hint of pale purple).

**Bleached White**

Use bleach powder to lighten the natural hair to around level 10, achieving a transparent color. Finally, rinse with 3% or 6% hydrogen peroxide alone. This helps remove any residual bleach powder from the hair, allowing for even coloring.

Mixing Method 1: Use blue + purple + serum + water in a ratio of 1:1:3:5 to wash the hair you want to whiten. It will first appear gray and then turn white after about 5 minutes. Use hydrogen peroxide below 30 degrees! You can bleach multiple times! Never heat it, as it will cause breakage! Wuhu Hairdressing Training [http://www.whainisi.org]. You must use hydrogen peroxide below 30 degrees. Add more serum and do not heat it. Repeat the process several times until the hair reaches around level 10.

Mixing Method 2: Use 20-degree hydrogen peroxide + serum + 0/00 + pure purple. Ratio: 5:1:3:1. This can produce a snow-white effect.

**Blue**

Step 1: Use 40% hydrogen peroxide cream + bleach powder. Lighten to levels 9-10. Ratio: Normal (1.5:1).

PS: Since the concentration of hydrogen peroxide cream varies, the most important thing during mixing is to check the consistency of the bleach powder and hydrogen peroxide cream. It shouldn’t be too dry or too wet. Make sure it’s evenly mixed.

Step 2: Use 20% hydrogen peroxide cream + blue paste + a little purple. Ratio: (1.5:1:0.01).

Note: In general, the mixed color paste will show results on the hair within a few minutes, but for better results, it’s recommended to leave it on for 20 minutes.
