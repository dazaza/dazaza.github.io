Chinese textile and apparel enterprises are thirsty

by wuhuishen4w on 2012-02-16 15:51:01

According to the latest statistical data, in the first half of this year, the talent demand in Beijing's clothing industry increased by 17.9% compared with the same period last year, showing a steady growth trend; the talent demand in Shanghai's clothing industry increased by 20.3% year-on-year, slightly higher than that in Beijing; while Shenzhen's women's clothing industry, which has a place in the domestic fashion women's wear field, saw a year-on-year increase of 77.1% in talent demand, far higher than the talent demand growth rate in Beijing and Shanghai. From the changes in the recruitment direction of enterprises and the dynamics of new positions, we can see the development trend of the clothing industry. For this reason, our newspaper conducted an investigation, and from the data, three key points can be seen, which are worthy of attention.

Design talents become the mainstream demand

Enterprises pay more attention to originality and image

Recently, Shen Yongfang, president of Shenzhen Garment Industry Association, said that because design, originality, and independent innovation are the driving force for the development of brands and industries, more than 5% of Shenzhen's garment enterprises have allocated 5%-15% of their sales as design, innovation, and R&D funds. Therefore, in the first half of this year, the recruitment demand for only the chief designer position in Shenzhen increased by 105% compared to the same period last year, doubling the increase; the recruitment demand for Shenzhen pattern designers increased by 150% compared to the same period last year, increasing by 1.5 times; the demand for Shenzhen men's wear designers also showed strong growth, increasing by 144% compared to the same period last year, closely following the growth rate of pattern designers.

In a joint survey targeting 308 clothing enterprises, it was found that among the designer categories, the top four positions in terms of enterprise demand were clothing designers, window display designers, visual merchandisers, graphic designers, and store designers, accounting for 65.23%, 13.04%, 13.04%, and 8.69%, respectively. It is not difficult to see from the data that the demand for graphic designers has ranked third, heralding the arrival of the electronic age in the clothing industry. In recent years, e-commerce has continuously impacted the traditional marketing model of the clothing industry. In competition, clothing enterprises gradually realized the benefits brought by online marketing. Many responsible persons of clothing enterprises believed that no one knows how big the future space of e-commerce will be, but its prospects are certainly promising. Therefore, the reserve of talents such as graphic designers and website designers is indispensable. However, because the website design and management of clothing enterprises require a higher level of aesthetics and fashion taste than other industries, many clothing enterprises have higher requirements for graphic designers than other industries.

Clothing quality inspection is valued

Enterprise quality management awareness enhanced

Besides designers taking up a large proportion of enterprise recruitment, there has also been an increase in the demand for production management talents compared to previous years. The survey found that the top five positions in terms of demand in this category were pattern makers accounting for 29.03%, order followers accounting for 20.97%, sample garment makers accounting for 19.35%, QC (Quality Control) accounting for 17.74%, and fabric procurement accounting for 12.91%. The proportions of these five positions are relatively balanced, but the demand for QC is significantly higher than in previous years, indicating that enterprises are paying more attention to clothing quality inspection. In recent years, in clothing quality inspections, problems such as excessive formaldehyde content, pH value, color fastness, odor, and decomposable carcinogenic aromatic amine dyes have frequently occurred. Among the clothing brands exposed each year due to failing quality inspections, there are also well-known brands. The insufficient quality supervision of enterprises has also caused great concern among consumers.

In the recently reported H&M brand clothing fading incident, the parties involved even consulted a lawyer regarding related quality issues. The lawyer's response was that once it is determined that the women's wear on Taobao has quality issues, consumers can request the manufacturer to return or exchange goods and claim corresponding losses. Such incidents, although the economic loss to the enterprise is not a major issue, the damage to the brand image would destroy the good reputation built over many years, making it unprofitable. Therefore, many responsible persons of the surveyed enterprises indicated that recruiting QC personnel is aimed at avoiding the recurrence of quality issues and is also a significant measure for clothing enterprises to maintain their brand image.
