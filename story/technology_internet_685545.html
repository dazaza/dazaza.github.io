Weishan says that Apple infringes on the right of reputation, Yang Rongshan claims that the sale of trademark is unknown

by sgba1jm1nf on 2012-03-01 15:42:28

Today, Wintek Corporation officially issued a statement claiming that the King & Wood Mallesons law firm sent out a lawyer's letter filled with numerous false statements and released it online before the Hong Kong High Court had made an official ruling on the iPad trademark rights. This has seriously infringed upon Wintek’s and its related parties’ rights to reputation and privacy. Wintek also stated that it reserves the right to pursue relevant legal responsibilities through legal means against the King & Wood Mallesons law firm and its client, Apple Inc.

Shenzhen Wintek claims that the Hong Kong High Court did not formally and effectively deliver the subpoena and pre-trial injunction in the iPad trademark case to Shenzhen Wintek. Chinese lawyers believe that the injunction from the Hong Kong court does not automatically have legal effect in mainland China and cannot be used to oppose Shenzhen Wintek’s rights under Chinese law.

Wintek expressed that under the condition where the Hong Kong High Court has not yet made an official ruling on the iPad trademark rights, King & Wood Mallesons prematurely drew conclusions, violating the professional ethics that Chinese lawyers should uphold. Furthermore, they published online the lawyer's letter sent on behalf of Apple to Wintek and its related parties, which contained numerous false statements. This has severely infringed upon Wintek’s and its related parties' rights to reputation and privacy. Such behavior is illegal.

Wintek Technology also stated that it will reserve the right to pursue relevant legal responsibilities through legal channels against King & Wood Mallesons and its client, Apple.

Moreover, Yang Rongshan sent a clarification to Tencent Technology explaining the matter concerning [Shenzhen Wintek's lack of awareness regarding the sale of the iPad trademark]. Yang Rongshan claimed that he never had any knowledge of selling the trademark for mainland China; the Shenzhen Wintek board never received any report, request, or requirement to sell the iPad trademark registered under its name; starting from the second quarter of 2009, Shenzhen Wintek's assets were under the supervision of eight banks, and handling any assets required their agreement.

It was reported that on the afternoon of February 22, the first trial of the case filed by Shenzhen Wintek against Apple Trading (Shanghai) Co., Ltd. was held at the Pudong Court. The trial did not result in a verdict, and according to relevant sources, the court requested both Wintek and Apple to stop disclosing related information to the media. The Pudong Court did not announce any time for the next hearing or verdict.

At today's trial scene, the evidence exchange for Wintek's lawsuit against Apple for trademark infringement was conducted first. Both sides' lawyers presented corresponding evidence involved in the case and conducted cross-examinations. Subsequently, the court conducted hearings on two aspects: Wintek's application to halt the sale of iPads in Shanghai, and Apple's application to terminate the proceedings.

Event Recap:

In 2000, Wintek International's Taipei branch registered the iPad trademark in multiple countries and regions. In 2001, Shenzhen Wintek registered the iPad trademark in mainland China.

In 2009, Apple purchased the global iPad trademark from Taipei Wintek through IP Company for 35,000 pounds. However, since the ownership of the iPad trademark in mainland China was not with Taipei Wintek but with Shenzhen Wintek, disputes arose as Apple's iPad entered the mainland market.

Additionally, at the end of 2009, a creditors' meeting represented by eight major banks decided to restructure Wintek's debts. Hexun Chuangye Company served as the debt restructuring advisor for Wintek Technology. In 2010, Hexun Chuangye pushed the eight creditor banks of Wintek to jointly sue Apple for compensation of 10 billion RMB.

On December 5, 2011, the first-instance judgment of the case where Apple sued Wintek Technology (Shenzhen) Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Shenzhen Wintek) for the iPad trademark rights in mainland China resulted in the Shenzhen Intermediate People's Court rejecting all of Apple's litigation requests.

On January 5, 2012, Apple appealed to the Guangdong Higher People's Court.

On February 14, 2012, Apple announced support from Hong Kong law.

On February 17, 2012, Wintek denied Apple's announcement during a press conference and stated that it would initiate related lawsuits against Apple overseas. On the same day, Shun Dian Huizhou Jiahua Store became the first Apple distributor judged by the court as losing the case.

On February 20, 2012, Apple's new law firm sent a lawyer's letter to Yang Rongshan, the founder of Wintek.

On February 22, 2012, the case of Shenzhen Wintek suing Apple Trading (Shanghai) Co., Ltd. was officially brought to trial.
