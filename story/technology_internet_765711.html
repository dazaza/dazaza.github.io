WeChat games are cracked, "Everyday Love Elimination" can score 1 million points for 10 yuan on Taobao

by anonymous on 2013-08-19 15:18:43

On August 19th, it was reported that after the WeChat game "Classic Airplane Battle" (commonly known as "打飞机"), Tencent's first social mobile game, "Toon Blast," also got hacked. On Taobao, a service to artificially boost scores became available for 10 yuan for a million points.

After the release of WeChat 5.0, its built-in game "Classic Airplane Battle" quickly gained popularity. Within a few days, the leaderboard showed multiple players with over a million points and even some exceeding two million. At the same time, score-boosting services started appearing on Taobao. Currently, searching for "打飞机刷分" (airplane battle score boosting) on Taobao yields numerous results, with many sellers having monthly sales exceeding ten thousand transactions.

A Taobao shop listed the price for boosting airplane battle scores at 10 yuan for two million points, which could be completed within five minutes. For four million points, it would take only ten minutes. The shop also sold cheating tools for both iOS and Android platforms, including features like "invincible mode," "unlimited dual bullets," and "unlimited bombs."

Recently, "Toon Blast" also got cracked. Jailbroken iOS devices and rooted Android devices can use plugins to directly modify scores or enable infinite time cheats. However, the process is somewhat complicated and does not support non-jailbroken or non-rooted devices. A seller told NetEase Tech that many users do not understand the tutorials or know where to download the plugins, so they opt for Taobao’s proxy boosting service. Similar to the airplane battle game, "Toon Blast" score boosting is achieved through proxy boosting. Users need to provide their WeChat ID and password to the seller, who then logs in and boosts the score on their own device.

The price for boosting scores in "Toon Blast" is 10 yuan for a million points. NetEase Tech registered a new WeChat account and purchased a one-million-point boosting service. Less than five minutes after placing the order, the seller confirmed completion. The leaderboard showed the account's score as 1.39 million points. The seller mentioned that many users are hesitant to give out their accounts, but due to the reporter’s swift decision-making, the seller added an extra 300,000 points.

Searching for "Toon Blast" score boosting on Baidu yields several versions of tutorials and tools, mostly using methods such as slowing down gameplay or modifying scores.

Recently, Tencent hinted through QQ's startup screen that they will release the next WeChat game on August 20th. An insider revealed it might be the sister game of "Toon Blast," titled "Toon Link." When asked, the seller confirmed receiving the news and was researching boosting methods, claiming similar techniques could likely be used.

Since both "Classic Airplane Battle" and "Toon Blast" reset scores weekly, maintaining high rankings requires repeated boosting. The seller noted that many users choose monthly boosting packages.

It is understood that WeChat has begun directly resetting the scores of some users caught boosting. Some users have even faced account bans. However, judging from Taobao's transaction records, many users continue to choose this method to improve their rankings. NetEase Tech reminds players that providing their WeChat accounts to third parties carries risks such as privacy breaches.
