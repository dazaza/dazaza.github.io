March 30, 2011, Sunny

Tian Tian absolutely loves going out to play. Fortunately, outside Grandma's house, there's a square and a supermarket. Now that the weather is getting warmer, we go to the supermarket every day. As soon as we put on his hat before heading out, he gets excited, showing off his little teeth and laughing while bouncing around in my arms. Once inside the supermarket, he greets everyone, strangers or acquaintances alike, with a smile and even gives a hearty pat on the shoulder of the salesperson. Grandma says, "You're small now, but when you grow up and still pat people on the shoulder like that, they'll call you a hooligan..."

Some things are becoming easier to teach.

We often wash Tian Tian's hands. After washing, we apply lotion, then place his little hands on my nose to smell them. "Ah! So fragrant! Tian Tian, smell this!" Then he imitates by putting his chubby hands to his tiny nose and taking a whiff.

When dressing, he knows to stretch out his sleeves actively now.

Walking while holding one hand is no problem; on his own, he can walk at most three steps.

He's learned to mimic the sound of slurping noodles and smacking lips.

He recognizes his toy box, which holds all his toys from infancy to now, including some from Uncle's childhood.

Whenever he picks up the TV remote, he pretends to press buttons seriously and looks up to see if the TV reacts.

Like a bandit, he grabs toys from Xiao Pang and Zhuang Zhuang, always winning. At first, Xiao Pang and Zhuang would cry loudly, but now they seem to have accepted reality: "As soon as you grab, I'll let go."

He likes pinching people, but I have a trick—pretending to cry. Ha ha, once Tian Tian sees me crying, he immediately lets go and kisses my face. But pinching is an issue; when he grows up and pinches me, I'll pinch him back.

He enjoys doing relaxation exercises after gymnastics and laughs contentedly.

It seems like he's growing more and more like his dad in some ways?

Next month on the 24th, Tian Tian will be one year old, and we plan to take him for a photo session.

PS: The voice-hanging chart sent by Second Auntie is something Tian Tian really likes and learns from every day. My sister Cong is helping Tian Tian shop online for little shoes, and we’re eagerly waiting...

March 16, 2011, Wednesday, Sunny

Yesterday was the eleventh day of the second lunar month, and Tian Tian celebrated his first birthday a month early. For various reasons, it's been over two months since I last recorded anything, so I haven't been doing a good job as a recorder.

The birthday was relatively calm, with close family members present and many little friends. Grandpa ordered a birthday cake for Tian Tian, who ate a small piece. It said, "Happy Birthday Little Tian Tian," but unfortunately, I forgot to help Tian Tian make a wish—I was busy filming at the time.

He received many gifts: from his great-grandfather, grandpa, grandma, godmother, aunt, etc., etc.

Last Saturday, Pumpkin brought us to Tian Tian's grandparents' house, and tomorrow we'll return to Grandma and Grandpa's house.

Tian Tian has made so many advancements that I don't know where to start.

Let's just write about whatever comes to mind.

The most obvious advancement is that he can understand many things adults say now. When we say, "Let's go play," Tian Tian gets very excited. Although he dislikes wearing hats, once we mention playing outside, he obediently wears one and even searches for it himself. If someone says they'll carry him to play, he stretches out his arms wanting to be carried... Previously, he enjoyed having adults hold his little fingers to turn lights on and off, but now he can do it independently... He couldn't sit up by himself before, but now he manages to fall to one side and use his little arms to prop himself up... He's started mimicking. When Uncle does B-Boxing, Tian Tian stares intently and tries to drum, though it's not quite right yet... He stands pretty steadily now and can take a couple of steps when things go well, still being as proud as ever...

He now has four and a half baby teeth.

He recognizes ears, melons, red lanterns, phone toys, and what else? Can't think of it right now.

The weather has been excellent lately, so I took Tian Tian out to the supermarket. He was so happy, his eyes couldn't keep up with all the excitement. He held onto finger cookies tightly and didn't drop them.

He likes Grandpa and Papa. In critical moments, he only calls for me. After Pumpkin returned, she cherished her son for two days, but the quality of cherishing has clearly declined because sometimes high-quality baby care is hard to manage.

Pumpkin says, "You're really patient. Motherhood is truly great."

February 2, 2011, New Year's Eve, Sunny Weather

Today is New Year's Eve, and tomorrow Gao Tian Tian will be two years old in Chinese age terms, but not yet one in Western terms. After the New Year, it will be ten months exactly.

On January 25th, Daddy picked us up and brought us to Grandma's house, where Tian Tian has made some progress.

Finally, he's catching on with small movements and can follow verbal commands:

Progress One: Scratch, scratch, little hands scratching.

Progress Two: Shake your head, little head moving left and right.

Progress Three: Tough guy, clenching teeth and looking fierce.

Progress Four: Goodbye, waving the right hand up and down, sometimes the left hand spinning around.

Progress Five: When called by name, he responds when in a good mood.

Progress Six: When adults cough, he imitates them.

Additionally, he's learned "Grandma, Grandpa, Uncle, Fan, Dog" and catches on quickly.

It seems like Tian Tian can understand some words now. One morning after getting dressed in the bedroom, I told him, "Tian Tian, wait on the bed, Mommy will go get your jacket." Unexpectedly, Tian Tian turned around and hugged me while humming, as if saying, "Oh, you understood?"
