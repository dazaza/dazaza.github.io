The theoretical system of knowledge economy promotes sustainable development

by ditan360 on 2010-07-09 16:34:23

Dr. Wu Jisong

On July 3, in an academic speech titled "The Essence of Low-Carbon Economic Development is Technological Innovation" held in Shijiazhuang, Hebei Province, Dr. Wu Jisong emphasized multiple times that it is very dangerous and alarming for academics not to pursue innovation or fail to keep up with the times. He pointed out that since the 1990s, new economic terms represented by the "knowledge economy" have emerged one after another. However, most of these academic terms require further examination. For instance, some people have proposed the concept of a "zero-carbon lifestyle," but human survival requires the intake of carbohydrates. In comparison, the concept of "zero-carbon" lacks rigor.

Currently, the development of economics academia is entering a special phase characterized by multi-layered aspects, information explosion, methodological innovation, and institutional innovation. How to scientifically, objectively, and fairly evaluate, establish, and improve the evaluation mechanism, standards, and system of China's new economic theory has become a research direction for many scholars.

Wu Jisong summarized by saying that if there is no spirit of keeping up with the times in research, the feasibility of the research goals will be lost. Similarly, without an appropriate and scientific theoretical system, it will be impossible to play a guiding role in social practice.

As one of China's earliest visiting scholars, Wu Jisong's research on economics is rigorous yet innovative. As early as 1992, based on the "knowledge economy" theory, he creatively proposed the concept of "intellectual economy." As a synthesizer of Western traditional learning and new economics, he summarized the 3R theory of the transition from Western economics to new economics in circular economy and established the "new circular economics."

"The knowledge economy," as a systematic theory, falls within the scope of general scientific methodology. In contrast, the "new circular economics" has specific guiding significance in transforming industries to reduce energy consumption and emissions, enhancing the capacity of ecosystems, and promoting the absorption, storage, and utilization of greenhouse gases.

Especially after the Copenhagen Conference, China has made tremendous efforts to achieve its goal of reducing carbon dioxide emissions per unit of GDP by 40%-45% by 2020 compared to 2005. The practical guiding significance of the new circular economics theory has become even more evident.

In the late 1980s, Wu Jisong conducted in-depth research on the systematic "knowledge economy" theory. He summarized the "5R Theory" (Rethink, Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Repair), which provided certain theoretical guidance for China's sustainable development. (It is worth noting that the term "sustainable development" was first introduced by Wu Jisong.)

Nowadays, sustainable development (Sustainable Development) has become a universally recognized economic concept, playing a crucial role in socio-economic development.

According to Wu Jisong's research, in 1962, American economist Fritz Machlup proposed the concept of the "knowledge industry," from which the "knowledge economy" developed horizontally. Between 1984 and 1986, the UNESCO project led by Wu Jisong, "Application of Multidisciplinary Integrated Research to Economic Development," conducted the first systematic study of the "knowledge economy" theory. Four years later, in 1990, UNESCO officially proposed the term "knowledge economy," clarifying the nature of this new type of economy.

In the article "The New Economics Theory System and Its Practical System," Wu Jisong emphasized that the purpose of developing the economy is to create wealth. However, we need to redefine wealth. When studying economics, we should divide wealth into two parts: one is social wealth (first wealth), and the other is natural wealth (second wealth). Production must not only create social wealth but also repair and maintain natural wealth. Continuously maintaining the balance between the two allows us to use natural wealth to create more and better material wealth.

However, during human development, the ratio of these two types of wealth has been unbalanced, even reaching a state of imbalance. Consequently, more and more social contradictions have arisen. From this, he derived the three major problems facing humanity today: the increasing scarcity of non-renewable resources; the growing environmental pollution; and the continuous deterioration of the ecosystem.

These issues go beyond merely rebalancing the two types of wealth; they permeate various fields of socio-economics and may even hinder the progress of China's sustainable development. Wu Jisong expressed deep concern, using the example of insufficient oil supply to emphasize that resources face shortages, making it difficult to discuss the sustainability of development. He criticized the market-dominated views in traditional economics. He stated that these views are all based on theoretical concepts. When human resources are exhausted, what will the "invisible hand" of the market economy control?

As an economist, the "knowledge economy" theory and "new circular economy" theory proposed by Wu Jisong provide directional choices for solving the three major problems currently faced by humanity and lay the theoretical foundation for China to maintain sustainable development.

Therefore, we have reason to believe that in the future, many basic assumptions in economic activities may be overturned. However, the social chaos brought about by the imbalance of "two types of wealth" in the 20th century cannot be concealed. In the past 200 years, industrialization and science have brought unprecedented transformations to human society. Humans have created unprecedented social wealth but left behind social philosophy and second wealth.

This is like he once said: "social wealth" and "natural wealth" complement each other and neither can be missing. This is the inevitable choice to promote harmony between humans and nature and build ecological civilization, and it is also an important way for China to transform its economic development model. Only by maintaining the proportion relationship between these two types of wealth can we better and faster implement the sustainable development strategy and better and faster promote the development of socialist economy in China.

Brief Introduction to Wu Jisong

Wu Jisong was born in August 1944, Manchu ethnicity, Beijing native. Former Director of the Water Resources Department of the Ministry of Water Resources, former Deputy Director of the National Water Conservation Office, former Special Assistant to the Chairman of the Beijing Olympic Bid Committee, the only Asian member of the Advisory Committee of the International Science Park Association, Advisor Professor and doctoral supervisor at Beihang University; doctoral supervisor at Hohai University. Dean of the School of Economics and Management at Beihang University, Director of the China Circular Economy Research Center, Advisor to the Overall Impact Project of the Organizing Committee of the Olympics, President of the Beijing Circular Economy Promotion Association.
