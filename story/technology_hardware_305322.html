Xu Hongtao's reputation ruined by match-fixing: calmly optimistic about the future of Chinese football

The anti-gambling and fake match storm in Chinese football has uncovered another case of promotion-related match-fixing. Yesterday, public security authorities confirmed through a CCTV program that the 2007 away game between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng was a fixed match. Former Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei confessed to police that in order to ensure Chengdu Sheffield United’s promotion to the Chinese Super League (CSL), Xu Hongtao, chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United, and vice president You Kewei bribed the Hailifeng team with 500,000 yuan.

For promotion, Chengdu Sheffield United paid 500,000 yuan for a fixed match

This match between Chengdu Sheffield United and Hailifeng is the second domestic league match confirmed as fixed after the Guangyao vs Shanxi Lutiger match. Unlike the gambling-related match involving Wang Bo and others in the Guangyao vs Shanxi Lutiger case, this match involved Xu Hongtao and You Kewei taking a huge risk by bribing the Hailifeng team to ensure Chengdu's promotion to the CSL.

According to police details, on September 22, 2007, during Chengdu's crucial promotion phase, to win this match, You Kewei and Xu Hongtao conspired to buy the match. After arriving in Qingdao, You Kewei met secretly with then Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei to plan the fake match. Ultimately, Chengdu offered 300,000 yuan in cash and free winter training at their training base for one month (both parties agreed this condition was worth 200,000 yuan) in exchange for Hailifeng "letting up" in the match. In the end, Chengdu Sheffield United easily won 2-0.

Police spent 6 days and 6 nights capturing Liu Hongwei

Police revealed that this case involved deep collusion among suspects, occurred a long time ago, and presented significant challenges in arrests and evidence collection. One of the suspects, Liu Hongwei, fled after hearing rumors, and it took the police 6 days and 6 nights of surveillance and stakeout before they finally captured him.

After detaining You Kewei, Xu Hongtao, and Liu Hongwei, the police stated that their manipulation of match results through commercial bribery violated Articles 163 and 164 of the Criminal Law and constituted suspected criminal activity. They would continue to interrogate them.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Suspect Confessions

Three parties, three expressions

In yesterday's CCTV Focus Talk program, the three main individuals involved in the fixed match between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng displayed three different expressions when facing the camera. All expressed regret for their roles in the fixed match but had differing views on the future of Chinese football.

A Sigh

Liu Hongwei: Low level, can't even fix matches properly

"Chinese football's level is too low; we couldn't even fix the match properly." In yesterday's program, as the recipient of the bribe, former Hailifeng team leader Liu Hongwei revealed the details of the fixed match. Although Hailifeng fielded several young players in this match, Chengdu Sheffield United still failed to score in the first half. At halftime, Chengdu Sheffield United's vice president You Kewei had to call Liu Hongwei, who reassured him over the phone, "Don't worry, we'll let you win."

Regarding this fixed match, Liu Hongwei sighed deeply, believing that Chinese football's level was so low that they couldn't even fix matches well. When asked by the TV reporter, "Can insiders not tell?" Liu Hongwei replied dismissively, "Insiders might be able to tell, but such things happen too often in Chinese football."

A Cry

You Kewei: Fixing matches wasn't my original intention

As the first exposed main character in this fixed match case, former Chengdu Sheffield United vice president You Kewei tearfully expressed his regret on television, "(Fixing matches) wasn't my intention, I'm sorry!"

You Kewei was already notorious in the Chinese football circle for gambling and fixing matches, but yesterday, he shed bitter tears in front of the camera, "I myself am an athlete... but the club requested..." In You Kewei's view, he somewhat felt forced into fixing matches, "It really wasn't my original intention!"

Calm Expression

Xu Hongtao: Optimistic about the future of Chinese football

Undoubtedly, Xu Hongtao is the most important figure in this fixed match case and also the heaviest-weight individual since the anti-gambling storm began in Chinese football. Facing the camera, Xu Hongtao reflected on his journey from initially "upholding principles" to later "going along with the corruption." Xu appeared calm and shared his thoughts on the current anti-gambling storm in Chinese football: "Chinese football is like a critically ill patient who needs supplements. This anti-gambling storm is like surgery to make the patient better!" For this reason, Xu Hongtao expressed optimism about the future of Chinese football at present.

"During that 2007 match, it was a critical moment for our promotion. To be safe, we entertained the idea of buying the match," Xu Hongtao said. He admitted bearing undeniable responsibility for the "buying matches" incident, being overly eager for quick success, and feeling very regretful.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Key Figures

From legendary agent to Chengdu Football Club Chairman

Xu Hongtao's reputation destroyed by match-fixing

In the latest match-fixing case disclosed by public security authorities, Xu Hongtao, chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United, is not only the key figure in this case but also the most significant person since the anti-gambling storm began in Chinese football.

Previously, Xu Hongtao was considered the most legendary agent in Chinese football. He facilitated the overseas careers of many Chinese players, including notable figures like Hao Haidong, Li Tie, and Sun Jihai, who played in England due to his efforts. In late 2005, Xu Hongtao, representing the English Championship team Sheffield United, arrived in Chengdu to negotiate the acquisition of Chengdu Wuniu. In 2006, Xu Hongtao officially took over the Chengdu club and participated in that year's China League Two (now known as the Chinese League One). In 2007, the team, renamed Chengdu Sheffield United, aggressively competed in the league and ultimately promoted to the CSL.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Arrest Details

Xu Hongtao hidden in Shenzhen but still arrested

On the 29th of last month, news spread that Xu Hongtao had been taken away by the police. At that time, his phone was already unreachable. Previously, Chengdu explained that Xu Hongtao and head coach Wang Baoshan had gone to Brazil to recruit foreign players, but after Wang Baoshan returned from Brazil, Xu Hongtao remained missing.

Although everyone wanted to know Xu Hongtao's whereabouts, Chengdu kept silent about it, even the club staff, coaches, and players were unaware. Last weekend, Chengdu Sheffield United held a special internal meeting where vice president Luo Xiaowei instructed everyone not to contact Xu Hongtao in any form. Nevertheless, despite hiding in Shenzhen, Xu Hongtao was eventually taken away by the police.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Reactions from All Sides

Chengdu Football Association Chairman temporarily assumes duties of Chengdu Football Club Chairman

Will travel to the UK to consult with Sheffield United

The arrest of chairman Xu Hongtao has dimmed the prospects of Chengdu Sheffield United. As the only club in the CSL with shares owned by a local football association, Chengdu Football Association formulated corresponding measures yesterday. It was reported that Chengdu Football Association Chairman Gu Jianming will temporarily take over as chairman of Chengdu Sheffield United and will travel to Hong Kong and consult with representatives from Sheffield United in the UK.

Speaking about the attitude of the joint venture partner, the English Premier League Sheffield United Club, towards Xu Hongtao's involvement, Gu Jianming said, "They also strongly support the anti-gambling campaign, and our cooperation will definitely continue."

Chengdu Football could face deductions or relegation

Following Guangzhou Pharmaceutical Club, Chengdu Sheffield United became the second team embroiled in the "gambling scandal." According to relevant regulations of the FA, both clubs that successfully promoted from the 2007 China League Two are highly likely to face the harshest punishment of relegation ordered by the Chinese Football Association. Referring to the Guangzhou Pharmaceutical Club incident, Chengdu Sheffield United is more likely to face point deductions.

■ Second Anti-Gambling Case: Voices

Nanyong: It's these people who corrupted the kids

Yesterday, while CCTV News Channel broadcasted the latest anti-gambling news, Vice President of the Chinese Football Association Nanyong watched the entire news report at the FA office building. According to CCTV reporters, Nanyong attentively followed the latest developments in the anti-gambling campaign. In the TV news, a suspect incited players to gamble by saying, "Don't listen to the club," which angered Nanyong, who scolded, "It's these people who corrupted the kids."

Chengdu Fans: Chinese football ruined Xu Hongtao

After the exposure of the fixed match case between Chengdu Sheffield United and Qingdao Hailifeng, fans from both cities were shocked. Chengdu fans were particularly astonished by the capture of club chairman Xu Hongtao. The head of the Chengdu fan organization "Red Blades," Chen Tao, angrily stated, "It's Chinese football that ruined Xu Hongtao!" According to Chengdu colleagues, Chengdu fans generally have a good impression of Xu Hongtao, especially his previous promise not to engage in match-fixing, which Chengdu fans greatly admired.
