Citizens applying for overseas travel on official business are defined according to the "Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Entry and Exit Administration" as follows: "official business" refers to activities including residence, visiting relatives and friends, studying (including self-funded and government-funded study), tourism (including individual and group tours), employment, inheriting property, and other official activities. Specifically:

1. Spouses residing abroad or invited by foreign residents for residence, inheritance of property abroad, or obtaining a residence permit from the destination country can apply for overseas residence.

2. Citizens invited by relatives with permanent residency in the destination country or valid long-term residency permits can apply for visiting relatives and friends abroad.

3. Those sent abroad for study by the state or their work units, or choosing self-funded study, can apply for overseas study.

4. Participants in state-approved overseas tour groups or individuals traveling abroad can apply for tourist visas.

5. Individuals employed under contracts with foreign employers or labor service institutions, or dispatched by domestic labor companies, can apply for overseas employment.

6. Business personnel sent abroad for company needs can apply for business trips.

7. Other official activities requiring overseas travel.

Conditions and Procedures for Government-Funded Overseas Study

Eligibility:

1. Government-funded study is not restricted by age or educational level; all eligible individuals may apply to study at overseas universities (undergraduate, graduate) or conduct research.

2. University graduates and government-funded students can directly apply for self-funded study abroad with relevant documentation at their local public security bureau.

3. Self-funded students below the undergraduate level, after serving a certain number of years domestically or repaying their education fees, can apply for self-funded study abroad. (Service period: five years for bachelor’s degree holders, three years for master’s degree holders, two years for associate degree and adult university graduates; Repayment fee: 1,500 yuan per year for associate degrees, 2,500 yuan per year for bachelor’s degrees, 4,000 yuan per year for master’s degrees, 6,000 yuan per year for doctoral degrees.)

4. Doctoral graduates going abroad for postdoctoral research do not need to repay their education fees.

5. In addition to the above conditions, self-funded students must also have sufficient legal financial support or scholarships from abroad, along with proof of economic guarantees from sponsors (recognized by the destination country), or scholarship letters from foreign institutions. The financial support should be enough to cover living and study expenses abroad. Additionally, they should have an adequate level of foreign language proficiency.

Procedures and Relevant Regulations:

Upon receiving acceptance letters and financial sponsorship proofs, if the applicant is a current student or employee, they must first submit an application to their institution or workplace for approval. Then, with the necessary documents and approvals, they can apply for a passport at the local public security bureau, which will review and issue passports according to regulations. Applicants should then proceed to the embassy or consulate of the destination country for a visa.

University students (undergraduate and graduate) who go abroad can retain their student status for one year. Self-funded students pursuing graduate studies abroad can opt to be classified as government-sponsored but self-funded, receiving a "Certificate for State-Sponsored Overseas Students" from Chinese embassies or consulates without changing their passports or visas.

Self-funded overseas study applications are handled by local public security departments. Students or employees must obtain approval from their schools or workplaces before applying.
