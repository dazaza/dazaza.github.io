On the Phenomenon of "Er Nai" in "Snail"

by 125eggdgf on 2009-11-30 16:35:48

It took me three days to finish watching "Snail House". It can be said that "Snail House" is a very realistic and profound TV series reflecting the society. It reflects the suffering of contemporary common people as house slaves, while delving into the officialdom and business circles, analyzing the governance experience of today's officials and the business philosophy of real estate developers, with profound social implications, worthy of our reflection after watching. One of the most eye-catching highlights in the whole series is the detailed description of how a college student becomes a "mistress", and how a corrupt official keeps a "mistress". What's more fatal is that this series subverts our usual views on corrupt officials and mistresses. For example, for Song Siming, the corrupt official who keeps a "mistress" in the series, we don't hate him until the end of the series. And for Hai Zao, the post-80s college student who becomes a "mistress", we actually feel some sympathy. On the contrary, we are quite repulsed by Hai Ping, who has been striving to pay off her mortgage, Song's legal wife, and even the city party secretary who fights against corruption. Why do we like what we should oppose, and hate what we should admire? Why does "Snail House" subvert people's understanding? After a deep analysis and thought of the whole series, I found that the reason is very simple. The author uses the culture of righteousness throughout the series to measure human relationships, creating an effect of subversion where the righteous is right, and the unrighteous is wrong no matter how right they seem. This proves that the encompassing role of China's culture of righteousness is quite extensive and deeply rooted in people's hearts. Its subversive power is irresistible by ordinary people.

Firstly, why don't we dislike the corrupt official Song Siming? Some say he is very capable. There are many capable officials, but they usually handle official matters in an official way. But Song Siming is different. He is very righteous. He doesn't just take care of his own official duties; he has a wide range of connections because he helps others with righteousness everywhere. Song Siming helped Hai Zao when she first borrowed money from him, partly because Hai Zao's "righteousness" towards her sister moved him, and partly because he must help with righteousness - it's his character. It should be noted that at that time, Hai Zao had no mistress relationship with him, not even mentioned, but righteousness and trust are connected. Where there is righteousness, there is trust. So, Hai Zao later returned the favor physically, which also involved gratitude in it, thus determining the uniqueness of their mistress relationship. Of course, Hai Ping didn't approve of her sister becoming a mistress, as it violated the principle, but later when her husband was also saved by Song Siming's righteousness, she gave in. It should be noted that Song Siming secretly saved Su Chun, and at that time, her sister had already cut off the relationship with him. Song Siming's saving them was an act of righteousness, and Hai Zao was moved by righteousness, so she voluntarily returned to Song Siming.

The change from opposition to non-repulsion regarding Song Siming's mistress affair still occurs in the second half of the drama, i.e., after Song Siming's father-in-law suffered a stroke, we learned such inside stories: his father-in-law was his old superior who promoted him in the past. When the old superior suffered a stroke 20 years ago, he betrothed his daughter to Song Siming. The father-in-law was willing to betroth his daughter to Song Siming because Song Siming was righteous, and he trusted him. Therefore, when the father-in-law suffered another stroke and was about to die, he still placed his daughter's hand in Song Siming's hands. I think the father-in-law could not possibly not know about Song Siming having a mistress, but he still believed that Song Siming would not abandon his daughter. Righteousness is more reliable than love. At the same time, we may infer that Song Siming's marriage to the old superior's daughter might have been more of a repayment of gratitude for the old superior's promotion, i.e., although he might not necessarily like her, he must marry her and never abandon her. Why did Hai Zao's appearance move Song Siming? According to Song Siming's wife's evaluation, Hai Zao lacks both beauty and figure, not up to the standard of a mistress, but Song Siming fell for Hai Zao. Only during their classmates' reunion did we understand that Hai Zao looked too much like Song Siming's first love in university, who unfortunately died young. Song Siming and Hai Zao's romance, though a kind of transfer of affection, seemed to possess more true love connotations than his relationship with his wife. However, Song Siming also cherished the emotional bond of going through thick and thin with his wife. He couldn't abandon the old superior's deathbed entrustment, and his firm refusal to divorce was also a matter of keeping righteousness. Therefore, when the corrupt official Song Siming enjoyed two wives, we were not very angry because there was true love and sentiment here. What's wrong with such a man having a little more love?

Since Hai Zao had no title and was pregnant, bearing the burden of raising a son in the future, so when Song Siming felt his end was approaching, he concentrated more love and righteousness on Hai Zao - that is, he almost gave all his money to Hai Zao. Later, he also felt a bit guilty towards his original wife and tried to entrust Hai Zao to his wife's care, because then both could share that wealth. However, his wife met Hai Zao privately in the end, kicked Hai Zao's unborn child out of her womb, and also destroyed Hai Zao's uterus. Upon hearing this news, Song Siming resolutely drove into a large truck and perished, showing that his love and righteousness were so deep that he was willing to sacrifice his life. China has had coexisting wives and concubines for thousands of years, always with contradictions and quarrels, but to cripple another woman of one's husband and destroy the only son of the husband is rare. A woman's lack of benevolence and righteousness means she is not virtuous, and an unvirtuous woman can cost a man's life. This might be the reason why people in this drama prefer the mistress over the wife!

Let's talk about the pitiful Xiao Bei next. Xiao Bei is from the post-80s generation, very pure and kind-hearted. His tragedy doesn't lie in his inability to match Song Siming, but in his ignorance of the culture of righteousness. Xiao Bei said, why should we care about your sister's affairs? Why can't we own our own happiness? This immediately widened the distance between him and Hai Zao. Because Hai Ping and Hai Zao are a very righteous pair of sisters, Hai Zao was raised by Hai Ping alone. When Hai Zao herself had not yet stabilized in the big city, she brought her sister up to share the joy, showing this righteousness. Without this sisterly righteousness, Xiao Bei would not have met and fallen in love with Hai Zao in this city. But Xiao Bei didn't understand righteousness. He grew up under the influence of Western culture. In China, whether a man has righteousness or not makes a qualitative difference, which made him unable to compete with Song Siming. Therefore, his love was deprived in the competition, which was inevitable. Xiao Bei is pitiful and tragic. Some say that the post-80s and post-90s generations cannot accomplish great things, not because they are not smart or kind, but because they are too selfish and do not understand righteousness. People without righteousness are small-minded and cannot achieve great things. Of course, one Xiao Bei cannot represent the entire post-80s generation, but it also shows that Chinese young people still need to inherit their traditional culture and follow the model of their traditional culture to be a person. The highly selfish worldview and values of the West will not make people too successful.

Why don't we like Hai Ping? Mainly because Hai Ping is the biggest beneficiary of Hai Zao becoming a mistress, but later she preached to Hai Zao, which was too unrighteous! In fact, Hai Zao became a mistress behind Hai Ping's back, and Hai Ping disagreed when she knew. When Hai Ping's husband was also saved by Song Siming, Hai Ping was very conflicted. As the Chinese saying goes, if someone gives you a drop of water, you should return a spring of water. This is China's moral culture, which every Chinese knows, but she couldn't let her younger sister do something that would destroy her life. Later, when her sister got pregnant, it was entirely a private behavior between her sister and Song Siming, unrelated to Hai Ping. Hai Ping's preaching to Hai Zao at the end was actually the author using Hai Ping's mouth to explain the main idea of the whole series, that is, in order to live in a good house, Chinese people have to borrow money and abide by the contract to repay the loan monthly for 20 years. But speculation can erase 20 years of labor in an instant, and the risk of speculation is that one misstep can lead to total loss. Therefore, the conclusion is still that people should work hard honestly. Hai Ping's preaching annoyed the audience, which was somewhat unjust, because it was a special arrangement of the plot.

Why don't we like the anti-corruption Party Secretary of the city? It's not because he opposes corruption, but because of his mediocre official theory. His official theory is for his own safety and convenience, he recognizes neither reason nor facts. But Song Siming, despite his blemishes, is generally doing practical things, adhering to sentiments, responsibilities, and loves. If he could successfully introduce Hong Kong tycoons to the city's real estate market, it would greatly strengthen the city's new housing construction funds, create wealth while allowing more people to live in good houses. Therefore, self-centered "clean officials" who only enjoy benefits in the eyes of the masses are not as valuable as "corrupt officials" who can bring them certain practical benefits. One major distinction is: the former lacks righteousness, while the latter has righteousness.

Foreigners value contracts more than righteousness, while Chinese value righteousness more than contracts. House slaves are slaves to the contract - must repay the loan monthly, no negotiation. In China, keeping righteousness is done in one's heart, guarding it with one's personality. Righteousness can restore humanity and morality, while contracts make people lose dignity and distort human nature. Therefore, the culture of righteousness is higher than the culture of contracts. This is another prominent feeling I have after watching "Snail House", and this topic will be discussed later.
