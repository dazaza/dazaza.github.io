Boy suffers from food phobia, hasn't eaten by mouth for four years (Picture)

by anonymous on 2012-02-25 10:48:03

Four-year-old British boy Daniel Harrison appears to be a healthy little boy, but in fact, Daniel suffers from a rare condition known as solid food phobia. He is afraid to try any solid food. Since he was born, Daniel has never eaten a single bite of food through his mouth.

According to Daniel's 41-year-old father Kevin Harrison and 37-year-old mother Catherine, Daniel developed the rare "solid food phobia" after experiencing an episode of acid reflux during infancy. At that time, Daniel's stomach acid flowed back into his throat, causing him immense pain.

Since then, Daniel has refused to eat any solid food and even rejects some drinks. In order to provide Daniel with sufficient nutrition, Mr. and Mrs. Harrison have no choice but to deliver essential nutrients to him daily through a nutrient delivery machine connected to a tube attached to Daniel's abdomen.

It is reported that in order to cure Daniel's "solid food phobia," the Harrisons have taken their son multiple times to the famous Great Ormond Street Hospital for Children in London for treatment. Despite numerous medical surgeries performed by British medical experts on Daniel, there has been no effect; Daniel still refuses to eat anything through his mouth. British medical experts stated that they could not find any way to make Daniel eat, as Daniel also suffers from a certain degree of autism, and the pain from the acid reflux during his infancy left a deep impression on him.

Currently, Daniel can only absorb some powdered nutrients mixed with water through the nutrient delivery tube. These contain vitamins and other essential nutrients required by the human body. Father Kevin told reporters: "We must help Daniel eat through the machine and tubes every day. We need to carefully observe each time, just like inflating a balloon. If Daniel absorbs nutrients too quickly through the delivery tube, it would be very dangerous. We hope that Daniel can eat by himself. There is nothing physically wrong with Daniel that prevents him from eating with his mouth; it is simply his brain refusing to eat."

Now, the only hope for the Harrisons is to take their son to the University Hospital in Graz, Austria, for treatment, as it is reportedly the only hospital in the world that has successfully cured this strange illness before.

Kevin said: "Our only hope now is to take Daniel to the University Hospital in Graz, Austria, for treatment because the experts there claim they have successfully treated this kind of disease before. However, even in the past 22 years, the University Hospital in Graz has only cured 12 children with this condition."
