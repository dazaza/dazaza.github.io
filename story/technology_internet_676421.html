[Working Principle]

Firstly, understand the working principle of the weighbridge/vehicle scale: A vehicle scale mainly consists of components like the load cell, weighing display instrument, weighing sensor (resistive strain gauge), connectors, limit device, and junction box, as well as optional external equipment like printers, large screen displays, computers, and voltage stabilizers. The object or loaded vehicle is placed on the load platform, and under the force of gravity, the load is transmitted to the weighing sensor through the load cell, causing deformation in the elastic body of the sensor. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance, outputting an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. The signal is then converted into a digital signal via an A/D converter, processed by the microprocessor (CPU) of the weighing instrument, and finally displayed as weight data. With a printer configured, the weight data can be printed and recorded. If a computer is configured, the measurement data can be entered into a computer management system for comprehensive management. Whether it's a scale or a weighbridge, the basic principle is to use sensors (force sensors) to convert the weight of objects into electrical signals, which are then sent to conditioning circuits, A/D converters, and processing circuits (MCU), before being converted into digital displays.
