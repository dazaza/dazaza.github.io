"Paris Has No Ferris Wheel" - The Confused Love of a Single Female White-Collar Worker

by jqrxass on 2009-10-17 14:22:28

There is a kind of life called "Ferris wheel": you always stand in the observation cabin and look at the scenery through the glass. Even when you reach the highest point, even if you are infinitely close, the scenery does not belong to you. After completing a full circle, you still leave the Ferris wheel alone.

"Paris Has No Ferris Wheel" tells the story of Ning Mo, an urban OL whose life is stuck in one loop of the Ferris wheel. She lives a monotonous 9-to-5 life like all the young white-collar workers drifting in Beijing, only her job sounds glamorous: fashion editor. Crawling on the fringes of the fashion world, she wears the Queen's new clothes, takes the commoner's bus, works as busy and trivial as an ant, and is caught between old relationships and new life like a walnut squeezed between two hard shells.

Regarding work, she is right in the middle of all kinds of rules in this dazzling and restless circle; regarding feelings, she has experienced the betrayal of her ex-boyfriend Lin Fei and is currently maintaining an ambiguous relationship with Bai Yan; regarding life, her best friend Gu Xin is about to go far away to Paris to start a new life; her roommate Yu Zheng earns her living as a model while looking for a way out.

Everything is happening, yet no end is in sight. Many people believe that if happiness is not on the road, it must be at the end of the road. In the revolving Ferris wheel, who can see what kind of scenery awaits at the end of the road? Between the horizon and the sky, who can sit by happiness? To which unknown destination will their fates be spun?

Paris Has No Ferris Wheel Chapter One

But, hypocrisy is the passport of hypocrites, and innocence is the epitaph of the innocent. If I hadn't been so innocent back then, who could have predicted my ending with Lin Fei?

I digress. When I realized this, I started concentrating on the road ahead, automatically blocking out memories. If we could choose, amnesia would undoubtedly be the best solution - but once memory is lost, can six years of time be forcibly taken away, forever becoming a blank? The absence of a corner of memory might be more terrifying than sadness itself.

Paris Has No Ferris Wheel Chapter Two

Back then, Lin Fei sat here translating each line of the lyrics for me: "Even if he calls her name on his deathbed, he sings, he laughs, he cries, he dies from a fatal passion. The sorrowful dove rises early to sing, flying to the lonely little house. Dove, do not cry, stones never understand, they do not know what love is..."

At that moment, I looked up at his wheat-colored cheeks, his bright black eyes, and the short hair on his forehead. Watching his soft lips pronounce the sorrowful Spanish words, I thought this scene would last a lifetime.

"Didn't expect your first love to be so beautiful," said Bai Yan, this heartless guy picking up a cherry from the half-transparent lotus leaf-shaped porcelain plate, absentmindedly commenting.

Paris Has No Ferris Wheel Chapter Three

That's right, I, Ning Mo, was stood up for the first time in my life. And the person who stood me up was a seemingly pure-hearted man named Bai Yan, who once had countless possibilities to formally hook up with me and solve our single problems. But many things require fate - just like how you've walked under an apple tree countless times, but the apple fell on Newton's head instead of yours. This is fate: it can make you crazy with joy or angry enough to spit blood. But whether you go crazy or spit blood, fate is set, impossible to change with human effort.

Paris Has No Ferris Wheel Chapter Four

To find out who you rely on most is actually quite obvious: whoever you feel comfortable bothering is the one. My relationship with Lin Fei lacked the sensitivity and suspicion common in lovers because we'd known each other too long and always got along rationally like old friends. I thought I had found the ideal state of love - turns out, love itself is blind. If someone is too rational and calculating in love, the other party may appreciate you but seek the blindness characteristic of love in another person. That blindness means your understanding of the other can only reach 80%, leaving the remaining 20% to torment you, making you restless, guessing, testing, irritable, sensitive, emotional, and unreasonable.

In love, rationality is a sufficient condition, but blindness is a necessary condition.
