Nokia is set to hold its annual conference, Nokia World 2013, tomorrow afternoon in Abu Dhabi. According to current information, the event will feature Nokia's first Windows RT tablet, the first 1080p quad-core phone, the Lumia 1520, and the Asha 500... At least six new mobile terminals will be showcased along with a variety of accessories.

Lumia 1520 - Nokia's first 6-inch WP flagship, also its first 1080p, quad-core phone

The Lumia 1520 will feature a 6-inch 1080p super-sensitive touch screen, powered by Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 quad-core processor, equipped with a 20 million pixel PureView camera, 2GB of RAM, optional 16/32GB storage space, and a 3400mAh battery.

The 1520 briefly appeared on Nokia’s Tmall flagship store this morning, showing a pre-sale price of 4999 yuan.

Lumia 929 (International version named Lumia 1320)

The Lumia 929 will be launched next month by the American carrier Verizon, and it is said that the international version will be named Lumia 1320. The unlocked version is priced at $599, featuring a 5-inch 1080p screen, with dimensions of 137x71x9.5mm, weighing approximately 166g.

This model is equipped with a Snapdragon 800 processor, offering a 20.7 million pixel Carl Zeiss certified camera with optical image stabilization and dual LED flash, 2GB of RAM, and optional 16/32GB storage space.

Lumia 720 Dual SIM Version - Nokia's first Lumia phone supporting dual SIM cards

The Lumia 720 Dual SIM Version comes with an 800 x 480 resolution screen, likely still 4.3 inches in size. It features Qualcomm dual-core Snapdragon processor and Adreno 305 GPU, with core configurations basically consistent with the previous single SIM version of the Lumia 720.

Lumia 2520 - Nokia's first Windows RT tablet

The Lumia 2520 features a 10.1-inch 1920x1080 IPS screen, powered by Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 MSM8974 processor, equipped with 2GB of RAM and 32GB storage space (expandable), providing cameras with 2 million pixel front-facing + 6.7 million pixel rear-facing, with a built-in battery offering around 10 hours of endurance, supporting LTE 4G network.

As currently understood, the tablet is 8.9mm thick, weighs about 589g, available in red, lake blue, black, white, and green.

Asha 500/502 (variant version 503)

The Asha 502 will feature a 3.2-inch 240x320 touchscreen, equipped with a 5-megapixel camera and LED flash, supporting Wi-Fi wireless, with single SIM and dual SIM dual standby versions. The main difference between Asha 503 and Asha 502 is that the former supports 3G network.

The device is available in six colors: yellow, white, blue, green, red, and black, with a transparent shell covering the surface. According to leaks from Nokia’s Tmall flagship store in China, the Asha 502 pre-sale price is 749 yuan, and the pre-sale will start at midnight this Friday.

In addition to the above six mobile terminals, Nokia is also expected to launch a special accessory tomorrow - the blue "soap box" Treasure Tag. This is actually an NFC accessory for Lumia phones, supporting low-power Bluetooth 4.0, mainly used for tracking items connected to the accessory via the phone, helping forgetful users find things, reportedly lasting up to half a year.
