How do highly educated full-time mothers raise their daughters?

Wang Qun makes handicrafts with her 3-year-old daughter.

Are stay-at-home moms necessarily of low education and without income? Are they necessarily always around the pot, looking disheveled every day? No. With the development of urbanization, many originally highly educated and high-income career women, facing the situation of no one to take care of their babies after childbirth, resolutely choose the profession of "stay-at-home mom," which appears to have no income but requires great dedication.

Wang Qun, 31 years old, holds a master's degree from Shanghai University of Finance and Economics, and was originally employed by a real estate company in Central Hong Kong. However, in order to stay home and accompany her daughter's growth, she gave up her superior salary.

Every day, she personally bakes cakes, chocolates, and cookies for her daughter, takes her to early education classes; she neatly arranges the lives of her husband and daughter, and even specializes in researching edible playdough exclusively for her 3-year-old daughter. Because her husband's job requires him to travel all over the country, Wang Qun lived in Chongqing for a year, and at the beginning of this year, she moved to Tianjin with her daughter to follow her husband.

Children grow up following their parents

Which is more beneficial for emotional intelligence development

Two years after getting married, Wang Qun gave birth to her daughter Tian Lan. [My husband's job is to run around all over the country.] If she wanted to balance work, Wang Qun would have to give up the days of being together with her daughter and husband every day.

Wang Qun believes that growing up with parents is more helpful for children's character and emotional intelligence. [There are also such families around me where young couples work outside while leaving their children with grandparents.] Wang Qun calls these children "urban left-behind children."

After careful consideration, Wang Qun voluntarily proposed resignation and became a full-time mother at home: [Since my child was born, I have been taking care of her alone.]

Telling stories to her daughter in both languages

Baking pastries for her personally

After quitting her job, Wang Qun became a food expert. She learned Western pastry baking and took care of her daughter's diet. Three-year-old Tian Lan eats cakes, chocolates, cookies, even yogurt and bread that are all handmade by her mother every day; she also became an early education expert, reading a large number of early education books, telling stories to her daughter in two languages every day; because her daughter who loves playing with playdough often puts it into her mouth, she even invented edible playdough.

[At the end of last year, I still lived in Chongqing, and I purchased all the items needed at Metro.] With the experience of baking Western pastries, she prepared flour, salt, water, tartar powder, oil, and food coloring.

The way to make it is very simple: mix all the materials except the food coloring, stir evenly, and boil them in a pot on medium heat. The process went smoothly, and after cooling down, knead it evenly, divide the dough into the required number of pieces, add food coloring, and knead evenly to complete.

[Of course, this playdough is not specifically made for children to eat, as too much food coloring is not good.] Wang Qun said that the handmade playdough quickly gained the affection of her beloved Tian Lan. She and her mother pinched trucks, dolls, sofas, and many other things together, [I pinch things while she cooperates by pinching small balls, tires, etc.]

Making handicrafts, telling stories, taking walks

Daily schedule is very rich

In addition to playdough, Wang Qun also bought colored drawing paper and made lanterns, cut paper, puzzles with her daughter, [From 3 PM to 4:30 PM every afternoon is our fixed time to make handicrafts together.]

As her child grows, Wang Qun has more and more free time. She also opened an online store, [posting some homemade recipes.] Because Wang Qun has good English skills, she usually browses foreign shopping websites, purchases some branded items for resale in her own online store.

[It can also be considered as a subsidy for household expenses.] Wang Qun showed the reporter her daily schedule: wake up at 7:30 AM, have breakfast with the baby at 8:10 AM, do housework at 8:30 AM, take the baby out to play at 9:40 AM, return home to prepare lunch at 11:30 AM, handle online store matters while the baby sleeps at 1 PM, have snacks and make handicrafts with the baby after the baby wakes up at 3 PM, tell stories, go out to buy groceries and prepare dinner with Blue at 4:30 PM, tell stories to her daughter before 9 PM, and finally sleep.

Not only is her daughter particularly sweet-mouthed

But she also helps her mother with housework

Under the care of a stay-at-home mom, three-year-old Tian Lan is clearly more sensible than children of the same age. After waking up in the morning, she will neatly fold her clothes, help her mother sweep the floor, shell beans, pick vegetables, sometimes wash her own socks, though not well done, but very seriously.

Wang Qun says that children are curious and will imitate whatever adults do, as long as it's not dangerous, she will let her child do things with her. Usually, Wang Qun often teaches her child to be loving and polite. When her parents wash their feet, Blue will actively ask to wipe their feet, and when she sees her mother tired, she will help her bring a stool for her to sit on.

[The little girl is particularly good at complimenting others, she will say kind mother, beautiful mother, Mommy, I like you the most, I want to be with you forever... This is probably the joy of raising children.] These three years as a stay-at-home mom have been hard for Wang Qun, but also very happy.
