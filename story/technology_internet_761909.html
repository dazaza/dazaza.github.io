Alibaba Plots Move on TV Screen: Reports Say Alibaba System Will Launch a Set-top Box Product

by geekzhang on 2013-05-15 10:35:20

Alibaba, which is very aggressive in the mobile internet sector, is expanding its layout: According to insiders, Alibaba Cloud plans to launch an Internet set-top box product to seize the TV screen. However, unlike LeTV and PPTV that focus on content, Alibaba's box will emphasize the system, hoping to connect multiple screens and promote TV screen shopping.QualificationMaking a box product requires an Internet TV license. Previously, Xiaomi's box was halted for this reason. Although the insider did not disclose who Alibaba will cooperate with to obtain the license, analysts believe it is likely to be Wasu Media or Hunan TV, both old friends of Alibaba.These two companies established joint ventures with Alibaba in 2010: The former partnered with Taobao to create Wasu Taobao, offering 12 categories of goods on digital TV. Users can interact and purchase through the remote control and set-top box, choosing cash-on-delivery or Alipay TV version. The latter partnered with Taobao to establish the Hitao.com celebrity e-commerce platform, embedding products into variety shows.In 2010, Wasu Media and Hunan TV both obtained Internet TV licenses.After two years, Wasu and Hunan TV have completed their layouts in the Internet TV field. Wasu recently collaborated with PPTV to launch the 299 yuan PPBox, while Hunan TV released the Mango Pi box product manufactured by Huawei in April.Currently, Alibaba has not commented on this matter.LayoutWhether it's LeTV or PPTV, the stakes in laying out Internet set-top boxes are more about content. iQIYI, which just acquired PPS, also plans to accelerate its layout in this area. They value the next screen of the mobile internet wave - TV.For Alibaba, content is undoubtedly its disadvantage, but the good news is that although it does not have video content itself, both Wasu Media and Hunan TV have accumulated strong content resources:Wasu's star business is VOD and value-added services, with powerful movie, drama, and variety content. Hunan TV combines its own resources with Phoenix TV, Huayu TV, and South Korean SBS programs for content support.However, insiders revealed that what Alibaba values more in making the box is the integration of the Ali OS.The Ali OS was officially launched in July 2011. Although it has been questioned throughout its development, it has launched more than ten smartphones with Tianyu and Haier, with a total shipment volume exceeding 3 million units. Since last year, the Ali OS has been negotiating operating system issues with TV manufacturers.This plan to make a box aims to achieve connectivity across multiple screens on devices running the Ali OS, such as using a smartphone to control the TV screen, thus forming a closed loop among several screens within the Ali OS ecosystem. Further, it aims to provide strong growth momentum for TV screen shopping.ChallengesLike the challenges faced by Ali OS smartphones, there are already many box products in the market. How can users be persuaded to choose Alibaba's box? Especially since Alibaba's potential partners, Wasu and Hunan TV, have already laid out their strategies in the box market. How to allocate resources and promotion efforts are issues that Alibaba needs to address or solve.On the other hand, the previous attempt at TV shopping by Wasu and Taobao did not yield significant results. Although the concept was ahead of its time and lacked a suitable environment for development, how many users today, in the era of mobile internet penetration, would abandon the convenience of PC and mobile shopping and turn to the TV end?Some analysts pointed out that the answers to the above questions may not be important. Just like Alibaba's frequent investment moves in mobile internet recently, the main purpose is to secure a favorable position in the fierce market competition.Previously, when PPTV launched the PPBox product, Tao Chuang from PPTV told Sina Tech that although there will be competition between Internet companies entering the set-top box field, it is not as intense as outsiders imagine. The main target is still the unlicensed counterfeit set-top box market.However, it seems that various Internet companies are now starting to take differentiated routes with their boxes. For example, LeTV started making TVs after making boxes, which is rumored to be Xiaomi Box's original plan. PPTV, on the other hand, uses the box system to let more TV manufacturers understand its content APPs, thus promoting more content.It is worth noting that with the accelerating update frequency and declining prices of smart TVs, set-top boxes as transitional products carry significant risks. Moreover, the current pricing around 300 yuan cannot generate profit, and whether there will be sufficient market space in the future remains questionable.
