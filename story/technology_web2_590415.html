Poetry Pairing - Mutual Pairs

Eight, Mutual Pairs: This refers to a format where two similar terms are used consecutively within one sentence, and then paired with another set of two. These can be divided into three categories: concrete word mutual pairs, abstract word mutual pairs, and mixed concrete-abstract mutual pairs.

One temple bell makes the mountain courtyard quiet, a thousand lanterns make the stream path bright. (Tang/ Yu He/ Master Wang's Hermitage) ○ The fifth regulated verse's third couplet. Mountain courtyard, stream path - concrete word mutual pair.

Ten years of tears for family and country, a life full of danger amidst water and clouds. (Ming/ Zeng Can/ Meeting Monk Sanlun at Lingyan Temple with Joy) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Family and country, water and clouds - concrete word mutual pair.

People's hearts are new in the changing times, spring is present in the old world. (Song/ Zhen Shanmin/ New Spring) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Times, world - concrete word mutual pair.

In life there is half sorrow and joy, in heaven and earth there is order and chaos. (Tang/ Du Fu/ Thirty Verses on the High Hall of County Clerk Cui Shao) ○ Ancient style poem sentence. Sorrow and joy, order and chaos - abstract word mutual pair.

There is little joy in the human world, many partings on the village roads. (Song/ Pan Lang/ Writing Affairs on Spring Day with Wine) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Joy, parting - abstract word mutual pair.

Entering the Dao has no coming or going, clear words see ancient and modern times. (Tang/ Chu Guangxi/ Inscription on Monk Qiu’s Chamber) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Coming and going, ancient and modern - mixed concrete-abstract mutual pair.

Nine heavens are rich with rain and dew, one river connects Suzhou and Hangzhou. (Song/ Zheng Sixiao/ Sending to a Friend) ○ The fifth regulated verse's third couplet. Rain and dew, Suzhou and Hangzhou - concrete word mutual pair.

Three watches of wind and rain heard, thoughts of friends from all over the sea. (Song/ Zhao Fan/ Night Assignment) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Wind and rain, friends - concrete word mutual pair.

Fertile soil grows mulberry and hemp, ruined city turns grass and trees green. (Song/ Xiao Yuanzhi/ Sending Governor Zhang Zongcheng) ○ The fifth regulated verse's third couplet. Mulberry and hemp, grass and trees - concrete word mutual pair.

Ten thousand miles of dreams across rivers and lakes, thousands of mountains of snow and rain journey. (Tang/ Qingjiang/ Early Departure from Shan Prefecture Gifting Secretary Yan) ○ Ancient style poem sentence. Rivers and lakes, snow and rain - concrete word mutual pair.

Mountains and rivers are southern and northern roads, winds and snows are parting skies. (Tang/ Yao He/ Sending Official Lu Chang back to Yangzhou) ○ The fifth regulated verse's third couplet. Mountains and rivers, winds and snows - concrete word mutual pair. Southern and northern, parting - abstract word mutual pair.

Mountains and rivers have existed for a thousand generations, city walls have changed temporarily. (Song/ Wen Tianxiang/ Nan'an Army) ○ The fifth regulated verse's third couplet. Mountains and rivers, city walls - concrete word mutual pair.

Mountains' brows divide Wu and Chu, river mouths bend west and east. (Song/ Gao Zhu/ Collecting Stones) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Wu and Chu, west and east - mixed concrete-abstract mutual pair.

Households differ, but the interest in streams and mountains is the same. (Song/ Chen Zhu/ Using Previous Rhymes Answering Age Old Man's Letter) ○ The fifth regulated verse's second couplet. Households, streams and mountains - concrete word mutual pair.

And so forth...

This pattern continues through various poetic lines demonstrating different types of mutual pairs as outlined above. Each line showcases either concrete-concrete, abstract-abstract, or mixed concrete-abstract pairings based on the nature of the words used.
