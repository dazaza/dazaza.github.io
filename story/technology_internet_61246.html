**Article 1: Optimize Your Blog**

A typical WordPress theme already has good design and structure, but you need to take some steps to make your blog more optimized for search engines.

**Use the Best URLs**

Setting up website permalinks is simple, and most people probably have done it. You can go to Settings - Permalinks in the backend to set them up. But which structure of links is the best? Keeping only the `/%postname%/` structure is the best because your articles will never change. If one day you change the category of an article or write a similar article, it doesn't matter; it will still be the same link. Or if you have two articles with the same title, that's fine too because WordPress will automatically add "-2" at the end.

Are you using the wrong format? Well, if your blog isn't brand new, you don't have to change it. Just keep this in mind for next time when you create a new blog.

**URL Handling**

There is one issue common to every WordPress blog: an article may have four different types of links, and these are fixed to prevent changes. Here’s some guidance on that.
