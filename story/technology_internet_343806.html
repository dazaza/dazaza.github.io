Cheap dunk shoes, baby items ready before the park.

by jir6uu3sme on 2010-04-12 11:14:13

Baby will join in, and what parents should be prepared is plenty of money. Besides training the baby in advance to take care of themselves, an essential preparation is to get the items needed for admission ready.

1. Daily Necessities: There are certain requirements for purchasing nursery bedding and toiletries, while others require personal preparations. Before admission, parents should inquire about relevant matters well to avoid wasteful duplication of preparation. If you already have daily necessities, attentive mothers can write the baby's name on items like towels and quilts to prevent the baby from not being able to find their own things.

2. Baby Clothes: You should prepare 3-4 sets of clean clothes, as it's easy for pants to get wet, so prepare two more sets for children. The best quality clothing is cotton, not too thick, and not too thin, preferably with pockets, open-fronted, and easy to wear and take off. Girl’s clothes should not have too many ornaments; if the girl has braids, use a simple hair comb, and do not wear too much jewelry. Boys should not wear pants with zippers. Babies must wear underwear to avoid catching a stomach cold and form good health habits. The temperature difference between indoors and outdoors in autumn and winter is significant, so preparing one thin and one thick small vest is necessary.

3. Shoes: Generally, nurseries will require the preparation of a pair of indoor shoes and a pair of outdoor shoes. Indoor shoes should be lightweight, non-slip soles to prevent children from slipping when going to the toilet. Indoor slippers cannot be used to prevent wrestling. Outdoor shoes should be sneakers or sports shoes suitable for running and jumping activities. Do not give children high boots, as they may cause ankle strain and make it easy to fall. Summer sandals should have closed fronts to prevent children from tripping over things during games and injuring their toenails.

4. Comfort Items: If your baby has strong dependence, to reduce the baby’s separation anxiety, you can let the baby take one or two toys they usually like. If your baby finds it difficult to nap without family members, you can prepare his usual small quilt with a small pillow or other comfort items.

Tips: Drink plenty of water. It's easy for babies to cry in kindergarten due to anxiety. Therefore, parents should ensure the baby drinks enough water in the morning before leaving and upon returning home in the evening. You can also take a bottle of boiled water or green bean soup for the child. Balanced Diet: Some babies always eat first, eating too much, and because of the change in flavors, their appetite significantly increases, resulting in water retention, which can easily cause fever once they catch a cold. Parents and teachers should communicate in a timely manner to control the baby’s intake. After returning home, do not let your baby eat too much, instead add some fruit.

Modest Clothing: Their parents might be afraid of the baby getting cold, so they tend to dress them too warmly, leading to sweating during outdoor activities and vulnerability to cold winds. In fact, children with strong body heat should wear less than adults, and clothing should be modest.
