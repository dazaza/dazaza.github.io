Love for a Lifetime --- The Dialogue between Buddha and the Stone

by saomao3381 on 2010-04-12 18:55:48

The stone asked: Should I find a person whom I love to be my wife? Or should I find a person who loves me to be my wife? Buddha smiled and said: The answer to this question is actually in your own heart. Over the years, those who have made you fall in love with them so deeply, those who have made you feel that life is full, those who have made you stand up and keep moving forward, are they the ones you love? Or are they the ones who love you? The stone also smiled: But all my friends advised me to find a girl who loves me to be my wife. Buddha said: If that were really the case, your whole life would be doomed to mediocrity! You are used to improving yourself continuously in the process of chasing love. If you no longer chase someone you love, your steps towards self-improvement will stop as well. The stone interrupted Buddha's words: What if I chased and got the one I love? Would it... Buddha said: Because she is the one you love most, making her live happily and joyfully is regarded by you as the greatest happiness in life. Therefore, you will continue to work hard for her to live even happier and more joyfully. Happiness and joy have no limits, so your efforts will have no limits either, and will never stop. The stone said: Then wouldn't I live a very hard life? Buddha said: All these years, do you feel that you have worked hard? The stone shook his head and smiled again. The stone asked: If that's the case, then shouldn't I treat the people who love me nicely? Buddha shook his head and said: Do you need the people you love to treat you nicely? The stone bitterly laughed: I guess I don't need it. Buddha said: Tell me your reason. The stone said: My requirements for love are quite strict, which is that I don't need sympathy or pity mixed in it. I require her to love me from the bottom of her heart. Although sympathy, pity, tolerance, and endurance are also kinds of love, although they can bring some kind of happiness, they are what I deeply hate. If her love for me contains these, then I would rather she ignore me, or directly reject my affection when I still have time to withdraw, because feelings can only get deeper and deeper. Despair is much more real than hope, because the pain of despair is temporary, while the pain of hope is indefinite. Buddha smiled: Very good, you have already said the answer! The stone asked: Why was the girl I loved before the most beautiful in my eyes? And now I love a girl, but I often find girls who are prettier than her? Buddha asked: Are you sure you really love her, and you are the one who loves her the most in this world? The stone answered without hesitation: Of course! Buddha said: Congratulations. Your love for her is mature, rational, sincere, and deep. The stone was somewhat surprised: Oh? Buddha continued: She is not the most beautiful in the world, and even when you love her so much, you clearly know this fact. But you still love her so much, because you don't just love her youth and beauty. After all, youth fades easily, and beauty doesn't last long, but your love for her has surpassed these surface things, thus surpassing time. You love her entire being, mainly her unique inner self. The stone couldn't help saying: Yes, I indeed love her purity and kindness, and cherish her childishness. Buddha smiled: Any test of time means nothing to your love. The stone asked: Why did the two of us lose the passion we had before when we were together later on, and instead became more dependent on each other? Buddha said: That's because your love has unconsciously turned into family affection... The stone scratched his head: Family affection? Buddha continued: When love reaches a certain level, it will unconsciously turn into family affection. You will gradually see her as a part of your life. In this way, you will have more tolerance and understanding. Only family affection is something that heaven has arranged for you since your birth, and it's something you have no choice about. So what you can do later is to adapt to your family affection. No matter how noble your birth is, you have to unconditionally accept them and take responsibility for them and be good to them. The stone thought for a moment and nodded: Family affection is indeed like that. Buddha smiled: Love begins because of mutual appreciation, falls in love because of a heartbeat, marries because of mutual dependence, but more importantly, it needs tolerance, understanding, habit, and adaptation to spend a lifetime together. The stone fell silent: It turns out that love is also a destiny. The stone asked: In college, I once met a girl. Back then, I loved her very much, but she didn't love me at that time; but now she loves me, and I seem to have lost that feeling I used to have, or I might say I don't love her anymore. Why does this happen? Buddha asked: Can you make yourself stop thinking about her from now on? The stone pondered for a while: I think I can't, because for many years I always consciously or unconsciously thought about her, or when classmates gather and talk about her news, I pay extraordinary attention; my heart gets inexplicably excited and nervous when I receive her letters or calls; the reason I've been single for so many years is because I haven't forgotten her all along, or I am looking for my future girlfriend based on her standards; but now I really don't like her anymore. Buddha sighed deeply: You now, compared to the past you, may not have changed much outwardly, but your heart has gone through a long journey, or you have tied a knot of reality and rationality to your love. You don't like her because of this knot, and knots need to be untied by yourself. Remember, five hundred glances in the previous life are exchanged for a fleeting encounter in this life. People always have to make choices, and how to choose is still up to you, no one can help you. The stone didn't say anything anymore, he just gazed quietly into the distance, it turns out that Buddha isn't omnipotent... The stone asked: In such an era, in such a society, is it worth it for someone like me to work so hard to love someone? Buddha said: What do you think? The stone thought for a moment, and was speechless. Buddha also fell silent for a while, finally he spoke again: Since the road is chosen by yourself, you cannot blame heaven or others, you can only be without regrets. The stone sighed deeply, the stone knew he understood. He looked at Buddha with a determined gaze, and didn't say anything else.
