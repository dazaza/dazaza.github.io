Online stores vs physical stores: Who will dominate the future of channel dynamics?

Online shopping is a fashionable and convenient way to shop these days. According to data from some survey companies, in recent years, the hot-selling products for online shopping have mainly been cosmetics, digital products, women's clothing, and accessories. The low prices of these items sold by online shops have made them popular. Some insiders in the industry stated that with the increasing demand for convenient and personalized consumption, online shops will have a significant impact on physical stores. According to Taobao's latest data for 2010, the annual transaction volume of the website in 2009 exceeded 200 billion yuan.

Physical Store: Online Shop Takes Business

As the saying goes, "three people love something cheap," female consumers have long been highly sensitive to price. The rapid popularization of online shopping has given them a powerful tool for price comparison. The free and quick method of online shopping has also made the public less inclined towards physical stores. Physical store owners are constantly figuring out how to retain valuable customers, while online shop owners are doing their best to increase browsing click rates. An invisible but intense battle is being waged around us.

"Yesterday, a girl came to my store and spent half a day choosing things, only to conclude that 'your stuff is too expensive,' which really angered me." Yesterday, a friend who operates a cosmetics store complained bitterly to me. In this friend's store, I saw the Meifubao whitening isolation sunscreen that was deemed too expensive by the customer, priced at 89 yuan. However, searching for the same product on Taobao showed that the cheapest one was only 25 yuan, and even the most expensive one was just 47.5 yuan. "At this price, you can't even sell it without making a profit!" My friend exclaimed. During my subsequent investigation, I also discovered that it is quite common for the prices of the same cosmetics to differ by half between online and offline.

Due to the low price, online cosmetic shops have already had a certain impact on the operation of physical stores. Many operators of cosmetic stores interviewed expressed complaints, stating that the cost of physical stores is too high. If they lower the price, there will be no profit, but if they don't lower the price, they will lose customers, making business increasingly difficult.

In addition to cosmetic stores, the emergence of "copy number tribes" due to online shopping has brought increasing impact and pressure to brand clothing physical stores. Faced with the high prices of hundreds or even thousands of yuan for various brands of clothing and shoes, savvy consumers often choose to try clothes and shoes in specialty stores, secretly note down the model numbers, then compare prices online, and purchase cheaper branded goods. This makes physical stores become fitting rooms, leaving specialty store merchants feeling helpless. These consumers are humorously referred to as the "copy number tribe."

Online Shop: No Rent, Naturally Lower Prices

Online shops can break through the limitations of time and space, with their consumer base potentially spread across the world. This is something physical stores can only envy. Compared to physical stores, the biggest advantage of online shops is that operating costs can be almost negligible. Opening a physical store requires a large amount of rent. A friend who operates a clothing store at Hatton Square revealed that the rent for a store of less than 10 square meters is about 1500 yuan per month. If water fees, electricity fees, and management fees are added, the monthly cost would not be less than 2000 yuan. In other words, regardless of whether you can sell goods, as long as you open a physical store, a considerable expense must be paid first.

With high costs for physical stores, the price of goods naturally cannot come down. However, online shops do not require rent, and their costs are so low they can be almost ignored, thus giving them an astonishing price competitiveness. Regular online shoppers know that for the same item, buying online is much cheaper than buying from a physical store. A housewife calculated that last year, thanks to online shopping, she saved nearly 5000 yuan. For an ordinary family, this amount is quite meaningful.

Currently, many C2C websites in China do not charge rent for store owners. China's largest shopping website, Taobao, does not require renters to pay rent when opening a store. However, in May 2006, Taobao launched "Zhaocai Jinbao," which essentially aimed to introduce a paid bidding ranking "value-added service." As a result, sellers who were accustomed to a free environment strongly opposed this fee-based service, leading to its eventual abandonment and stoppage. In 2007, Taobao also introduced "Wangpu," but this plugin only charges a few dozen yuan per month for usage.

Unlike Taobao, eBay's profit model involves charging users for shop rent, product listing fees, and transaction service fees. Although the monthly rental fee for ordinary shops keeps rising, it remains within the range of dozens of yuan per month.

Although some websites charge rent while others do not, one thing is clear: compared to physical shops, the rent for online shops is almost negligible.

Business Administration: Not a Single Online Shop Registered

Online commerce naturally constitutes a market. With markets and transactions, a question arises: what kind of shop is an online shop? Do they need to register for a business license like traditional shops? How should taxes be collected from these shops? In recent years, many industry experts believe that issues such as licenses and taxation brought about by online shops are facing an awkward situation of regulatory absence.

On April 2nd, the State Administration for Industry and Commerce announced the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Related Service Behaviors (Draft for Soliciting Opinions)" on its official website, and officially solicited opinions from the public. In the "Interim Measures," I saw that online shop operators in the future should register under their real names, and those who meet the conditions should also register for industrial and commercial registration; existing business entities with business licenses should make their electronic versions public.

However, according to the Registration Division of the Municipal Administration for Industry and Commerce, currently, none of the online shop operators in our city have registered for industrial and commercial registration at this bureau. Deputy Director Tian Hong stated that the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Related Service Behaviors (Draft for Soliciting Opinions)" is just a draft and has not yet been formally implemented. Only after the formal introduction of the "Interim Measures for the Administration of Online Commodity Transactions and Relevant Service Behaviors" will Huangshi's specific management measures be introduced.

"Without a business license and office premises, all you need is a network cable, a few computers, and a few people, and you can start operations." The industrial and commercial department frankly admitted that the lack of a business license makes it difficult to regulate online shopping.

The "softness" of registering and obtaining licenses for online shops has created a tax vacuum. Among the many online shop owners surveyed by me, the number of people who admitted to paying taxes was zero. Although some relatively "cautious" websites posted reminders, for example, eBay's terms of service state: "Users should pay taxes according to national tax regulations to relevant departments." And on Taobao's terms of service, there are related prompts: "Users should pay all applicable taxes due to transactions, obtaining paid services, or accessing Taobao's servers, as well as all hardware, software, service, and other related expenses." However, all online shop owners admitted that such clauses "are often overlooked, and even if seen, there is no intention to pay."

Expert: Future Coexistence of Online Shops and Physical Stores

"The rapid rise of online shops and other e-commerce platforms meets the requirements of low-carbon economic development. They don't require physical storefronts or resource waste, which is a good way to develop the tertiary industry and significantly enhances the traditional sales model. Online shops have low initial investment costs and small risks, making them suitable for laid-off workers and college students to start businesses."

Associate Professor Li Tao, the Party Secretary of the School of Economics and Management at Hubei Normal University, believes that online shops have no time or space restrictions, with global customers targeting all groups. They break the information asymmetry of previous physical stores, reduce many intermediate links such as wholesalers and retailers, and fully utilize modern logistics to support direct sales models. Products are inexpensive, and consumers have strong initiative in consumption. At the same time, this e-commerce model caters to the fast-paced lifestyle of modern times, offering convenience and speed, aligning with modern consumption trends. This is an important reason for the rapid development of online shops.

Li Tao analyzed that although online shops are developing rapidly and impacting physical stores, they cannot replace them, and their mutual integration is inevitable. Shopping in physical stores offers experiences like browsing, trying out products on-site, and touching textures, which online shopping cannot match. When shopping online, consumers initially see only attractive pictures, sometimes even processed artistically, with insufficient demonstrations. Once consumers receive the goods and find them not meeting expectations, they may experience significant psychological gaps, along with wear and tear during transportation, which could lead to returns. Moreover, consumers' habit of believing what they see rather than hear is hard to change, and physical stores offer higher security, making their advantages more apparent.

Li Tao indicated that individual online shops in our city are developing rapidly but are still in their early stages. The regional development of online shops is uneven, and consumers' purchasing habits need guidance and improvement. Consumers are mostly young people with higher education levels, and the scale and momentum have not yet formed. Online shops have great development potential but require a supporting stage. Before reaching a certain scale, the government can delay taxes and fees. Of course, during development, online shops may encounter fraudulent behaviors like false online information. While the country protects consumers' legitimate rights, relevant departments should jointly standardize the e-commerce market, such as implementing real-name registration for online shops, allowing operators peace of mind and consumers to shop confidently.

Traditional Retail Channels Launch Counteroffensives

Recently, Zhang Jindong, chairman of Suning Appliance, directly pointed out the "drawbacks" of e-commerce. Zhang Jindong believed that the circulation industry must have added value, but most online malls sell goods at product cost prices, unable to provide added value, which does not conform to the laws of commercial development.

Zhang Jindong stated that although the 21st century is the era of the Internet and information, the Internet is merely an information platform and cannot serve as the foundation of commercial circulation. He believed that "anything without added value will not last long."

The view that e-commerce enterprises will not become mainstream has received support from some executives of traditional enterprises. Besides losing money to attract customers, "inability to experience on-site" is the defect most frequently discussed by leaders of traditional commercial enterprises, determining that online sales can only play a "supplementary" role.

A senior executive of a supermarket did not directly say that online shopping could not shake the mainstream status of traditional channels but answered this question based on the proportion of online sales and traditional channel sales of foreign retail giants like Walmart and Tesco. Public data shows that the online sales of foreign retail giants are far from comparable to the sales generated by physical stores.

Although traditional enterprises do not believe that online shopping will replace in-store sales, they have already launched counterattacks by opening online malls to divert online shopping customers.

Survey data from the China Chain Store Association in January showed that large chain enterprises in Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and other places generally mentioned starting to pay attention to online shopping businesses, among which chain enterprises in Shanghai showed the highest level of attention to online shopping businesses. Shanghai Nonggongshang Supermarket launched online shopping services at the end of last year and is currently improving the system to support in-store pickup services. It is understood that COFCO Group has already opened Womai.com, Xidan Mall has increased publicity for its online mall, and domestic home appliance retail giants have also entered the online sales market.

There are reports that the Ministry of Commerce is drafting a document titled "Supporting Traditional Commercial Enterprises Entering E-commerce", which will provide special fund subsidies for traditional commercial enterprises entering e-commerce to promote commercial enterprises using online sales to drive in-store sales.

China E-commerce Research Center analyzed that as e-commerce becomes an important tool for cross-border trade in our country, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., e-commerce is extending from B2B to B2C. Relevant departments of the state will introduce preferential measures to encourage traditional commercial enterprises to enter e-commerce. Traditional commercial enterprises have good credibility and after-sales service. Although more traditional retail giants are getting involved, traditional retail online malls mainly bear the function of brand effect at present.

Whether it is a traditional physical store or e-commerce, both need to integrate and control suppliers to become the main consumption channel. Currently, a fact is undeniable: traditional physical store sales remain the primary consumption channel for manufacturers.

Take home appliance manufacturers as an example. A responsible person from a domestic color TV enterprise talked about the mainstream issue of online and traditional channels, stating that currently, home appliance malls are still the core sales channels, and the significance of online sales channels lies more in "product display and information release".

Mr. Wang, who is both a supplier of small appliances and kitchenware in the three major malls in Beijing and has his company's online mall, said regarding the status of physical channels and online malls, that the online mall is merely in a "survival maintenance" state, and the company's main profits and business still come from traditional mall sales.

According to a diamond merchant, his diamond products are not only sold on mainstream online channels like dangdang, but also have physical counters. In his view, online shopping and physical stores are two different consumption channels, both of which cannot be ignored, and the debate over which is the main and which is the secondary seems unnecessary.
