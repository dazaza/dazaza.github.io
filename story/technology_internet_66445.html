MonoTracer enclosed motorcycle

by hamz on 2008-05-02 09:31:38

MonoTracer was a concept motorcycle at last year's Geneva Motor Show, where it caught many eyes with its beautiful design, enclosed shape, and powerful performance. Now, the MonoTracer has been produced, albeit as a limited edition product sold at a high price ($82,350), yet it still has a considerable number of customers. Below are some super-cool real-life shots of the MonoTracer. Normally, enclosed motorcycles always seem very bulky and fail to reflect the high-speed characteristics of motorcycles. However, the MonoTracer performs exceptionally well in this regard, with a highly futuristic design that is extremely visually striking. The MonoTracer's body is made from Kevlar fiber, carbon fiber, and epoxy resin, while the engine compartment, front fork, and wheel supports are made from high-strength aerospace-grade alloy steel. The gull-wing doors come with a transparent roof made from high-grade aerospace polymethyl methacrylate material. The designer also included a 200-liter capacity luggage compartment at the rear of the vehicle. The power for the Monotracer comes from BMW K1200 motorcycle's 1.2-liter 16-valve four-cylinder engine, delivering a peak output of 130 hp and a maximum torque of 117 Nm. It features a multi-plate dry clutch and a four-speed sequential PG transmission. Owners can opt for an automatic clutch, but it comes at an additional cost. For braking, the new model is equipped with 320mm diameter ventilated discs and four-piston hydraulic brake calipers, combined with an ABS anti-lock braking system, allowing it to stop from 100 km/h in just 43 meters. The vehicle's curb weight is 485 kilograms, and it accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in 5.7 seconds, with a top speed of 250 km/h. The new form of the Monotracer’s body naturally requires a different control method; the gearshift control and parking control buttons are located on the left handlebar, the left side is the clutch pedal, and the right side is the integrated brake pedal. The new model is equipped with two Recaro racing seats with three-point seat belts. Optional configurations include a sound system with GPS, a cruise control system, a front airbag, dual xenon headlights, and a CD player.
