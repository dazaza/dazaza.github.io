As China entered a new era of reform and opening up, the development of the commodity economy brought about population mobility and a wave of people going to cities to work. This quietly swept across the rural areas depicted in "Women's Village". In a small village named Zhangling in Northeast China, the men went to the city in groups to work, leaving the village and their homes to the women. This forms the backdrop of the entire series, narrating the arduous lives of these left-behind women. However, due to their optimism and creativity, life remains vibrant. Especially their small actions, strategies, and means during emergencies add many comedic elements to their otherwise mundane lives. Renowned actress Yan Xuejing brings Northeast dialect into the show this time, adding more color to it.

Yan Xuejing plays Li Erbai in the show. Although she isn't someone viewers would hate outright, her many actions are quite repelling. For example: when the whole village starts raising pigs to get rich under the leadership of Zhang Xifeng (played by Wang Qianhua), Li Erbai, because of her narrow-mindedness, refuses to follow suit and builds a greenhouse to grow vegetables instead. She doesn’t get along with other women in the village and throws stones into their yards at midnight while their husbands are away to scare them. When Zhang Xifeng's little piglet runs into her house, she dyes the piglet and claims it as her own. Despite all these wrongdoings, from another perspective, her refusal to blindly follow trends and her courage to innovate align well with the inspiring spirit the show wants to convey. Each show has good and bad characters; each role has its own unique traits. Nonetheless, her intelligence and capability eventually create wealth for her.

From "Old Root Liu" to "Auntie Duohe" and "Full House Parents," every character with different expressions has been vividly portrayed by Yan Xuejing. Regarding Li Erbai in "Women's Village," Yan Xuejing made her debut as an antagonist, marking a significant breakthrough that earned great praise from director Wu Zhaoshan. Yan Xuejing expressed that over the years, she has stayed in Northeast China and shot many rural-themed dramas, getting familiar with various rural characters. For her transformation into Li Erbai, Yan Xuejing hopes that if audiences watching the series dislike Li Erbai, it would signify her success.

Plot summary of "Women's Village":

Zhang Xifeng, a discharged female soldier, serves as the village head and head of the Women's Federation in Zhangling Village. She is attending a "Double Study, Double Comparison" field conference in town. Meanwhile, Ping Tai Ming, the son of former village chief Ping Wu, returns to the village to recruit workers for his newly contracted construction project. Consequently, the usually peaceful Zhangling Village becomes bustling. Young and able-bodied men like Jing Cai, Wang Xuebu, Si Ku, and Wang Xiaoqing sign up one after another to work in the city with Ping Tai Ming. When Zhang Xifeng, carrying an ecological pig farming project obtained from the higher-level Women's Federation and ready to make big moves, returns to the village, the men preparing to leave have already packed their bags. Thus, this place truly becomes a "Women's Village." These left-behind women and children exemplify the spirit of entrepreneurship in the new era, showcasing the demeanor of discharged soldiers in building a harmonious society.
