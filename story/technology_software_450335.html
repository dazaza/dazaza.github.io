— This is another profound reflection by Ren Zhengfei, president of Huawei Company, following "Huawei's Winter".

I have listened to "The Spring of the Northern Land" hundreds of times, and each time I am moved to tears by its simple and unadorned lyrics. The original intention of the creator of "The Spring of the Northern Land" was to sing about the pioneers and fighters, not the love song that today's youth mistakenly think it is.

In the season when cherry blossoms bloom and the spring scenery is bright, we set foot on Japanese soil. Our trip to the Orient this time is not for the purpose of feeling the smell of spring in a foreign land or admiring the widespread cherry blossoms, but rather to learn from the experience of getting through the winter.

The spring of the northern land will always come.

Upon stepping onto Japanese soil, my first impression was still the same as ten years ago: peaceful, harmonious, clean, prosperous, and comfortable. From remote rural areas to bustling big cities, the streets were still so neat, every place we went was still orderly; people were still so kind, gentle, courteous, their pace was still brisk; from the waiters at ramen shops, to the old ladies at small town hotels, from office workers to... everyone was so calm, optimistic, and dedicated to their work. They cherish their jobs so much, they value the opportunity to serve others so much, work seems to be their highest enjoyment, without any restlessness, dissatisfaction, or resentment. In my view, Japan is still the Japan of ten years ago, the Japanese are still the Japanese of ten years ago.

But who would have thought that during these ten years, Japan endured the coldest and longest winter after the war. Precisely because what we see and hear now is built on such a long period of low growth, this makes me feel especially deeply. The vast majority of Japanese companies have not increased wages for nearly eight years, yet social security remains better than in Northern Europe, truly amazing. Once Japan takes off again, such a foundation will surely make it soar. If Huawei continues to endure two winters, I don't know if Huawei employees can remain calm, face challenges calmly, overcome difficulties, and look forward to spring.

Since the early 1990s, Japan has experienced continuous ten years of low growth, zero growth, negative growth... This winter has been too long. How did Japanese companies get through it? What difficulties did they encounter? What experiences do they have? And what inspiration can they give us?

This is the purpose of our visit to Japan.

Huawei has experienced ten years of rapid growth. Can it sustain continuous growth? Will it encounter low growth, or even a long period of low growth? What problems exist in the structure and management of the company? Can employees who have rapidly advanced during peacetime withstand the cold of winter? Will the cash flow in rapid growth be interrupted, like a trickle in frozen rivers and streams, preventing the company from coming to a complete standstill... These are all issues that corporate leaders should study in advance.

Huawei will always have its winters. Being prepared with warm clothing is better than not being prepared. How should we deal with Huawei's winter? This is the topic we constantly consider and discuss while in Japan.

Struggle is the source of power for the rise of a nation.
