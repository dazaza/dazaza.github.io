Website keyword research, when keywords meet visitors

by soft0604 on 2009-01-20 14:17:22

The foundation of effective search engine marketing, including search engine optimization and bidding ads, is selecting the right keywords and phrases that your potential customers are likely to search for. However, how to choose a good keyword is worth studying.

Assume there is someone who has never heard of your company. A person visiting your website might be searching for a product you happen to produce in a search engine. This is the starting point of keyword research. Please note, what you need to find are the words your customers would search for, not the words you think should be entered into the search engine.

By applying these keywords in your web pages and links between them, visitors will be attracted to visit your site because the keywords in their minds overlap with the ones used on your webpage. But, choosing the wrong keywords means you'll pass by these visitors or may never encounter them at all.

What constitutes a keyword isn't determined by the website owner, but rather by the search results from internet users. If people are searching for certain terms related to your business, then your website needs to use those terms. If you use keywords that seem cool or overly technical to you, first consider how many people are actually using them.
