When the roses have bloomed and withered, the green shade is cool.

by zhouzhou56 on 2012-02-02 12:58:32

The roses have bloomed and the green shade is cool.

[One]

I noticed you at the crossroads near the school. Like most boys, you had a Nike bag on your back, Nike sneakers, G-STAR T-shirt, and jeans. Your hair was cut short, neat and tidy, with a sharp gaze full of vigor. I'm not infatuated, but happened to be standing next to you waiting for the red light to turn green, so out of boredom, I took a look at you.

You are quite handsome, reminding me of ancient descriptions of beautiful youths - eyebrows like ink splashes, hair like knife-cut.

Thirty seconds later, we walked together from one side of the road to the other. The green light turned red again. At this time, a sickly dog with almost no fur passed by your feet, unknowingly stepping onto the road. You gently whistled, and surprisingly, the dog actually stopped. You squatted down and tore the bread in your hand into strips to feed it.

Thirty seconds later, the red light turned green again. You patted its head and smiled, saying, "There were too many cars just now, but now you can go, be a good boy."

It looked at you, its small body trembling slightly, but with a gentle and obedient expression, then happily ran to the other side of the road.

You brushed off the crumbs of bread in your hands and walked past me with a calm demeanor. You kept looking straight ahead, naturally ignoring me, an unremarkable girl. But you didn't know that your unintentional action gave me such a shock under the warm afternoon sun.

Since childhood, when others asked me what kind of boys I liked, my first reaction was kindness.

He doesn't have to be tall, or handsome, he doesn't have to squat on the street to tie my shoelaces for me, or wait downstairs at my house holding melting ice cream in the hot summer, but he must have a kind heart, which is more important than anything else.

Actually, I know a little about you. An upperclassman in the club told me that you entered the school as the top student in science during your freshman year. Most boys in this school only focus on studying, but you are different. You also play basketball, your paintings have won awards in national competitions, and you have excellent calligraphy. I heard that when you concentrate on writing, even if someone suddenly grabs your pen from behind, you remain still. What really made you famous was the art festival in your freshman year, when the band you formed with your friends caused countless girls to scream. When the last note stopped, you threw the bass forcefully onto the stage.

When the senior described the grandeur of the scene to me, her whole face was flushed like a rose. Jia Yan, you didn't see the scene that day. Someone took his photo and posted it on the school BBS. For two years, that post has always been on the front page.

You are the legend of this school.

That night, I logged onto the campus BBS for the first time and found the post mentioned by the senior. The you from a year ago looks no different from now. Those photos were taken very well, whether in terms of angle or lighting, they were perfect. I stared at the computer screen blankly for a long time, and after a while, I realized that my face was red.

I clicked on those photos with the mouse, right-clicked and saved them in a folder, which has the same name as you, Su Shan Yu.

[Two]

During the school basketball game, I was dragged by my friends to watch. Since we arrived late, there was no place left, so I stood outside the court blowing a small horn hard.

The finance department and international communication department, which are not particularly popular majors in the school, attracted countless spectators. It's simple, a large part of the girls came for you. The screams filled the entire venue, drowning out the referee's whistle and the commentator on site. The October sunlight shone brightly. Every face was so passionate and vivid. I watched you among the crowd stealing the ball, dribbling past people, and making three-step layups, your movements smooth and seamless. Halfway through the game, you were knocked down by the opposing center, the game paused, your teammates gathered around, but you just smiled faintly, got up, and continued playing.

During the halftime break, I was forced by some jerks to buy drinks. I turned around holding a bunch of chilled Coca-Cola and orange juice, and bumped into you coming towards me. I stumbled, and the drinks in my arms spilled all over the ground. At that moment, I shamefully blushed. You stared at me wide-eyed, and I almost wanted to cry. This was our first direct encounter, but I was in such an awkward state.

You helped me pick up the drinks, then suddenly said, "What's your name? You look familiar."

My face turned even redder, and I stammered, "You must have mistaken me for someone else... I... I... I'm leaving." Then I turned and ran away, ignoring your shouts of "Hey! Hey!" behind me. I thought I should run fast, hoping you would bump into a lamppost on your way back and forget everything that happened this afternoon. Next time when I appear in front of you, I will definitely be elegant and composed.

But a few minutes after I finally sat in the seat my friend squeezed out for me, you limped to me with a playful smile, "Hey, why did you run so fast? I couldn't even call you back."

Even though I didn't look at the expressions of the people around us, I knew their mouths could fit an egg. I was speechless looking at you, and you still smiled, "I hurt my foot, will you accompany me to the infirmary?"

At this point, a clear sound of people gasping could be heard around us. My head was already bowed as low as possible. You paused for a moment, then directly reached out to drag me, taking my hand without hesitation and walking away. I don't remember how we left the gymnasium. I didn't dare to look back, fearing that if I did, I would be pierced to death by the arrows in their eyes.

After the school doctor finished bandaging your injured foot, you grinned at me like a little thug, your mouth crooked with mischief. You said, "Hey, shouldn't you pay me back?"

I stared at you dumbfounded, "What money?" You raised your eyebrows, "Pretending to be stupid? Two bottles of Coca-Cola, three bottles of orange juice, that's 15 yuan."

I quickly opened my wallet, and before my eyes went dark, there were two neatly folded hundred-yuan bills inside.

Isn't it fate that wants me dead?

[Three]

Soon, I became a celebrity in the school. Someone uploaded the pictures taken with their phone that day to the BBS. The replies below were mostly from girls, asking who this girl was? Quickly, someone who recognized me answered everyone's questions, she is a freshman in the journalism department, Zhou Jiayan.

You wouldn't know how much trouble you've caused me during this period. Everyday, people come to my classroom pretending to glance at me casually, and from their eyes, I know things are far from simple. Even the professor wonders why the number of students attending class has suddenly increased recently.

I will never forget that afternoon, after the last class of Chinese and foreign news history in the lecture hall, I was packing up my textbooks ready to leave, when a girl walked straight to me with deep resentment in her eyes. She asked me, "Are you Zhou Jiayan?"

I nodded dumbly, completely unaware of the impending danger.

When I was unprepared, her slap came like a sudden storm, giving me no chance to breathe. I only heard the thunderous explosion on my left cheek, countless roaring sounds in my ears, and everything went black.

Her tears soon burst forth, "Zhou Jiayan, please respect yourself."

The onlookers took a long time to disperse. I stood dazed, unable to move. My friends came to hold my hand, and I turned my face to look at them, tears streaming down my face. What exactly happened?

Su Shanyu, why did you let me fall into such disgrace? What exactly did I do wrong?

For a whole week, I stayed in the dormitory bed without going downstairs. Everyone bought my favorite chocolate puff pastry, but I had no interest. Various snack wrappers piled up on the table like my shattered self-respect. I hate you, and I also hate that psycho. I can't imagine what terrible words my classmates who witnessed the beating incident will use to talk about me.

On the weekend, when everyone was gone, the entire female dormitory was very quiet. I was starving, and reason and pride were struggling fiercely when I heard you calling me downstairs. With the last bit of strength, I crawled to the window and looked down. Your smile was like the summer sun, painfully bright, and you said, "Zhou Jiayan, come down. I'll treat you to something delicious."

Your girlfriend hit me, and I foolishly didn't fight back. Now that you're willing to apologize, I have no reason to let you go. So that afternoon, I ordered the most expensive dishes at Jiangnan Restaurant, including abalone for revenge. But you weren't angry at all, your sparkling eyes still full of apology.

When you sent me home, it was already completely dark. The yellow streetlights stretched our shadows long. An old beggar woman stopped us and kept saying, "Sir and miss, may you live happily ever after." You gave her all the remaining 20 yuan you had. I tugged at your sleeve, "There are a lot of scammers nowadays." Your eyes were so clear, "She's so old and still has to beg, even if she's a scammer, she can't cheat me out of much money, it's fine."

At that moment, I suddenly felt that if I were your girlfriend, I would also be afraid of losing you.

It was also at that moment that I suddenly felt that you meet all my fantasies about love. I like you, very much, extremely much. Although many people like you, I am different from them.

But does my insignificant admiration reach you?
