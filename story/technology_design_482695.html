Sun Wukong's Letter to White Bone Demon VS White Bone Demon's Reply to Sun Wukong

by aqzyanshengi on 2011-08-29 08:56:51

Dear Ms. Bai,

First of all, I sincerely apologize to you and say sorry. Yesterday, my master wrote me a letter which I read over and over again. It truly hit the sore spot in my heart, like digging into my soul, making me suddenly realize my faults. I reflected on them all night, lying awake, and now everything is clear. Having lived for hundreds of years, I've been reckless and unruly, and today I have nothing to show for it, as if I've lived in vain. When I think about it, I feel deeply pained. Although the master may be somewhat hypocritical, he has always been sincere with me, saying some heartfelt words that I am truly grateful for.

The journey to the West was actually a trap set by the Jade Emperor and the Bodhisattvas. The journey wasn't as difficult as we imagined. They could have sent a more skilled immortal who would have completed the task in no time. However, the master was an object of promotion, a standard candidate, but lacked grassroots work experience, so he was sent down for training, yet they couldn't let him suffer alone. Therefore, they assigned me, along with Bajie, Shaseng, and Bai Long Ma, who had made mistakes, to accompany and serve him.

The eighty-one tribulations along the way were all calculated by the Bodhisattva. Tang Monk was never truly in danger. Wherever we went, Guanyin Bodhisattva knew exactly where we were. At crucial moments, if I didn’t seek her out, she or her disciples would certainly come personally. A wave of the lotus flower, and all evil forces would disappear.

As for those so-called demons, aside from ones like you without any backing trying to make a living on your own, most were either celestial beings' mounts or Bodhisattvas’ pet goldfish. They all possessed exclusive divine weapons. How could I possibly defeat them? After exerting all my strength, their masters would arrive at the critical moment, saying, "Wukong, let me take them away." What could I say? The only ones who truly suffered were those like you, without any connections, ultimately revealing your true form.

I always thought that with my unwavering loyalty and demon-slaying efforts, I would gain recognition from both my leaders and comrades. But when we returned and held the summary meeting, all honors went to the master, and we only received golden bodies. Thankfully, the master removed the Tightening Headband from my head, allowing me to breathe a sigh of relief.

Later, they were all appointed to official positions, while no one cared about me. Helpless, I returned to my hometown of Huaguo Mountain, subsisting on wild fruits and spring water with no benefits. Yet whenever there was something, such as studying documents, someone would come to check my learning situation, asking me to write self-examination materials, saying that my mistake from five hundred years ago was still serious and needed deep reflection. This made me feel as if the Tightening Headband, though removed, was still invisibly present on my head.

If only I hadn't hidden in Laozi's furnace back then, resulting in these broken fiery eyes that can see through others' tricks and ugliness that others cannot see. Seeing these things, I couldn't help but speak out, and speaking offended people. During the journey, I also beat many subordinates or relatives of leaders, causing everyone to sneer at me, saying, "How capable are you when today you're even worse off than me?"

I naively thought that only Bajie was lustful, and the master was a man of integrity. But after reading his letter, I finally realized the truth. Along the journey, he was just putting on a show. While we worried to death, he secretly romanced the Queen of the Western Liang Kingdom and the Spider Demons behind our backs, seriously violating discipline without anyone questioning him. In America, this would have led to resignation due to responsibility, yet wine grapes...

However, they managed to get fake diplomas, evaluations of competence, and promotions to director-level positions. Today, I remain with nothing. Housing is too expensive to buy, so I still sleep in the Water Curtain Cave. Now, the age for promotion has passed, and I don't care much anymore. I just want to find someone, settle down, and live a good life.

We became acquainted through conflict. Among the women I know, you are the one I admire the most. You possess beauty and capability, but you don't rely on officials or rich men, instead creating your world through your own abilities. Moreover, you are hardworking, brave, kind, and virtuous.

Looking back, I still like you very much. We are both passionate individuals, romantic, upright, and hating evil. You're not young anymore, soon becoming a leftover woman. Why don't we live together? Either going to your White Bone Cave or coming to my Huaguo Mountain would be fine. We can develop tourism based on Huaguo Mountain, and I can start a martial arts school. Life will surely go on. Whatever hobbies you have, I support. Let’s raise our children well, send them to Tsinghua or Peking University when they grow up, let them become officials, and avoid walking our old paths.

I await your response... Wishing you happiness.

Wukong

July 16, 2011, Huaguo Mountain

White Bone Demoness's Reply to Sun Wukong

Mr. Sun,

Your letter has been received.

Thank you for remembering me, but I cannot marry you. I apologize.

I am not someone who holds grudges; in fact, I once harbored a hero complex. Back then, when you revealed my true form, I thought that once you returned from the Western Heavens, I would find you, marry you, bear your children, wash clothes for you in the Water Curtain Cave, and till the soil for you on Flower Fruit Mountain. At the time, your master, along with Zhu Bajie and Sha Wujing, already saw that I was a demon. Your master pretended to be unaware, Zhu Bajie had ulterior motives due to my beauty, and Sha Wujing, being timid, preferred to avoid trouble. Only you had some spirit and a sense of justice, chasing and attacking me under everyone's watchful eyes. At that time, I deserved to be beaten. Being naive, I was lost in dreams and hurt some people. You woke me up, gave me a lesson, and I thank you for that.

However, later events changed, and my thoughts evolved. After your pilgrimage, your master was praised for leadership and decisiveness, promoted to a higher leadership position. Zhu Bajie, despite his penchant for womanizing, was assigned to oversee women's affairs because such behavior isn't considered problematic these days. Sha Wujing was recognized for his hard work and logistics support, promoted to the head of transportation management. Only you were deemed impetuous, lacking in discipline, and arrogant, and thus left to fend for yourself. Since then, I've realized that idolizing heroes serves no purpose; one must be more pragmatic. Life is full of twists and turns, and one should not lose direction.

I'm doing well now. I have my own villa, my own car, and my own child.

You might not know, but after you returned to Flower Fruit Mountain to fend for yourself, I worked at a high-class establishment called Tian Ren Jian (Heavenly Realm). Later, I met your master. He is no longer the bookish fool calling out for his disciples to save him. With his background in chanting scriptures, he sings exceptionally well and frequently comes to our place, spending generously. Moreover, he now travels with an entourage, wielding considerable power. Your master also has a good sense of humor; he says I have fair skin and a bony beauty, hence calling me the White Bone Demoness, making me feel flattered inside.

Because of past encounters, every time your master came, he asked for me to accompany him. He told me that after returning, he married the princess of the Western Liang Kingdom. He also said they had marital discord, as that woman had turned into a tiger, constantly nagging and checking phone calls and QQ chat records. More importantly, after giving birth, that woman suddenly gained weight, becoming unrecognizably fat, offending audiences. Your master also said he had noticed me back then, but due to organizational scrutiny, he didn't dare approach me. After returning, he intended to look for me, but the father of that woman was a close associate of his superior, so he couldn't offend him. In short, your master wanted me to be with him, offering everything except for formal recognition. Later, I moved into the villa he provided for me. He also replaced the white horse with a BMW, gifting it to me. Although I know your master has other women, including Spider Demons whose files he altered and reassigned to another unit for receptions, leaving traces everywhere, I have no choice. Last year, I bore him a son, not daring to give him the surname Tang, and eventually followed my own surname, naming him Bai Tang.

Mr. Sun, please don't be angry or look down on me. If I were with you, I would have status, but no money. Perhaps you don't know, but I fear poverty. Nowadays, we can't afford housing and can't live in caves forever. Peaches don't fetch much money these days, and one season's harvest doesn't compare to what I earn in one night at Tian Ren Jian. How will we live, raising children and sending them to school? It's too difficult. In this world, as long as you have money, people will look at you differently, regardless of its source or cleanliness. You mentioned starting a martial arts school, but I think you should reconsider. You only know some stick techniques, but you can't match Zhou Jielun's nunchucks, which are far more profitable. All I can say is, take care of yourself. Take care of your health, peel peaches before eating them, as modern peaches are covered in pesticides that can't be washed off.

By the way, I heard Niuhuang is dead. Go visit Princess Iron Fan, who is now a widow. There might still be hope.

Farewell, Mr. Sun!

YOURS,

White Bone Demoness
