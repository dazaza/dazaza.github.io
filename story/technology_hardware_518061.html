Li Ning launches the "Winter Defense Battle"_6271

by mlocka07 on 2011-10-24 16:07:04

Li Ning (China) Sports Goods Co., Ltd. is currently caught in a "Winter Defense Battle."

Following the announcement of its Q2 2011 order situation last Friday, Li Ning's stock price plummeted significantly over the first two trading days of this week. On December 20, Li Ning's stock price fell nearly 16%, and on December 21, it continued to decline by another 5.26%, resulting in a loss of HK$4.5 billion in market value over two days.

On December 22, Li Ning's stock price rebounded by 2.48% to HK$17.36, but this recovery was insufficient to compensate for the substantial losses, with investor anxiety continuing to spread.

"Excessive reliance on distributors opening stores to drive growth," was the explanation provided by relevant personnel from Li Ning regarding this sudden event. Guo Jianxin, Chief Operating Officer of Li Ning Company, believed that "the approach of sports goods companies relying on new store openings to boost performance has hit a ceiling."

As of the evening of December 22, this reporter was unable to ascertain whether there were any underlying malicious sudden events behind the aforementioned analytical explanations. However, just before Li Ning's stock price plummeted, Extraordinary China, through which Li Ning intended to conduct a reverse takeover, disclosed that the Growth Enterprise Market (GEM) Listing Committee of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange had ruled that Extraordinary China's acquisition of approximately 30% of Li Ning's equity constituted a reverse acquisition.

According to the Listing Rules of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, a "reverse acquisition action" refers to transactions that may affect the company's control or indirectly list the assets to be acquired.

A spokesperson for Extraordinary China stated, "The company has appealed against the ruling by the Hong Kong Stock Exchange that Extraordinary China's acquisition of Li Ning constitutes a reverse acquisition. The company remains confident in ultimately completing the acquisition, with the timeline depending on the hearing date set by the Stock Exchange."

**Institutional Short Selling**

According to information released by Li Ning, the second-quarter 2011 Li Ning brand product ordering session has concluded. Based on retail tag prices, the total value of orders is roughly equivalent to the same period in 2010. The average retail price of clothing and footwear products increased by more than 8%, but the quantity of orders decreased by more than 7% and 8%, respectively. Due to adjustments in wholesale discounts given to dealers, the total value of orders calculated based on wholesale shipments declined by approximately 6% year-on-year, and the order volume also decreased.

After Li Ning released the above news, multiple institutions expressed bearish sentiments towards Li Ning, subsequently triggering a continuous decline in Li Ning's stock price, erasing a total of HK$4.5 billion in market value over two trading days.

CCB International's research report stated that Li Ning recorded the worst order results among sports apparel enterprises for the second quarter of 2011, with the decline in order growth exceeding market expectations. It maintained an underperform rating and sharply reduced its target price from HK$21.9 to HK$15.2.

CCB International believed that Li Ning's second-quarter order session saw a year-on-year decline of about 6% in order amounts, representing the weakest performance among sports goods stocks. It expected Li Ning's market share to decrease under market competition, with weak outlooks for the next two quarters' orders. As such, CCB International downgraded its earnings per share forecasts for Li Ning for the 2011-2012 fiscal years by 16%-19%.

Additionally, institutions like Deutsche Bank also issued research reports expressing disappointment with Li Ning's performance.

In response to the capital market's reaction, a spokesperson for Li Ning stated, "Stock price movements are actions of the market; we will continue to focus on improving our business."

**Channel Challenges**

However, the market's disappointment has prompted Li Ning to re-examine its traditional distribution channels.

A spokesperson for Li Ning stated that the company currently has 129 distributors and over 2,000 sub-distributors. A large portion of these sub-distributors are relatively small, operating an average of 2 stores each. In fact, over 1,700 sub-distributors operate only one store.

Behind this "small and scattered" distribution team lies uneven management capabilities. "We have noticed that most sub-distributors lack strong retail management skills, purchasing abilities, and product lifecycle management, leading to their poor single-store efficiency and operating profit margins. They also lack economies of scale brought by a larger number of stores," the spokesperson said.

Moreover, as the sports goods market rapidly changes and national urbanization continues, sports goods are experiencing consumption upgrades, with higher demands for products and brands. Meanwhile, both distributors and sub-distributors face rapid cost increases, especially in labor costs and rent.

Regarding the decline in order quantities, industry veteran Wang Yiming commented, "Unlike competitors like Anta Sports, Li Ning has made little progress in channel integration over the past few years, leaving Li Ning, as a relatively older brand, with limited room to increase market sales through multi-brand agents."

Compounding issues, Li Ning's efforts to venture into sports real estate have suffered a severe blow. Extraordinary China recently announced that the GEM Listing Committee of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange had ruled its acquisition of Li Ning's equity as a reverse acquisition.

Extraordinary China was previously known as Comfort Energy Savings, an HK GEM-listed company, which announced in late August the acquisition of 30.9% of Li Ning's equity and renamed itself "Extraordinary China Holdings Limited." Subsequently, Extraordinary China acquired investment holding companies Bright Equal and Union Wealth for a total of HK$700 million, granting Extraordinary China the rights to develop and manage industrial parks and land development projects in designated areas, allowing the Li Ning group to enter the sports real estate sector.

Despite the setback, a spokesperson for Extraordinary China stated, "Our direction remains to continue the acquisition under compliance with the GEM Listing Rules of the Stock Exchange, establish a strategic partnership with Li Ning, enhance sports-related businesses, and build a comprehensive enterprise including sports talent and competitions, event production and management, as well as sports and green community businesses, thereby growing Extraordinary China into a leading sports and environmental protection integrated enterprise."

**"Winter Defense Battle"**

Facing the current situation, Li Ning is waging a "Winter Defense Battle."

"The key to future growth lies in improving the operational efficiency of existing stores," a Li Ning spokesperson said. "We identified the problems early and proactively proposed reforms to its distribution system, including consolidating inefficient distributors, improving store structures, enhancing product lifecycle management, increasing discount rates for dealers, and other measures aimed at addressing challenges faced in the retail segment."

In fact, Li Ning has already implemented a series of measures aimed at improving channel efficiency. One major initiative involves encouraging more efficient, larger distributors or those seeking further growth to acquire smaller stores, while also promoting the expansion of direct-to-dealer store numbers among large distributors.

This reporter learned that Li Ning plans to consolidate over 2,000 of its sub-distributors, eliminating 500-600 sub-distributors from its distribution system. As of June 30 this year, Li Ning had a total of 7,478 stores.
