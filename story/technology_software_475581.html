Introduction to the Finalist Entries of the Evernote Global Developer Competition

by xdash on 2011-08-12 18:22:28

The Evernote Global Developer Competition is an official contest organized by Evernote for third-party developers. Nearly 1,000 developers from around the world demonstrated and introduced their creations, with the official selection of 6 works to compete for a $50,000 prize. Here is the event introduction, where you can now vote for your favorite works. (From Sync Control)

Official judging criteria:

- Whether it can seamlessly integrate with Evernote;

- Whether it showcases a novel aspect of Evernote;

- Whether it meets needs reflected in the user community;

- Whether it can inspire other types of applications;

- Whether it is truly cool.

Here are brief introductions to the six finalists:

1. **Colorstache**

Colorstache provides a new concept for browsing and searching notes through color. It analyzes the colors in your notes and identifies the five main hues in any image. After clicking on a color in the color wheel, it will list images that meet the criteria.

Official Website: [http://www.colorstache.com/](http://www.colorstache.com/)

Voting Page: [http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/colorstache.php](http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/colorstache.php)

YouTube Demo: [http://www.youtube.com/embed/9vxrgZnkjaE](http://www.youtube.com/embed/9vxrgZnkjaE)

2. **MyWorld**

MyWorld allows you to save places of interest in Evernote and then view them on Facebook as a map. You can also share saved locations with friends or view their collections. When used, create a note about the current location via a mobile device client, and MyWorld will obtain relevant information through GPS and create a map on Facebook.

Official Website: [http://www.wikitude.com/](http://www.wikitude.com/)

Voting Page: [http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/wikitude.php](http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/wikitude.php)

YouTube Demo: [http://www.youtube.com/embed/McmDs4AotV4](http://www.youtube.com/embed/McmDs4AotV4)

3. **Notablemeals**

Notablemeals is an iPhone app designed to record a dining experience. Why record meals? Perhaps due to an unusual menu, a great restaurant, a strange address, someone you dined with, or just another moment worth preserving in everyday life. Notablemeals supports rating, taking photos, adding sound effects, and viewing past history.

Official Website: [http://www.notablemeals.com/](http://www.notablemeals.com/)

Voting Page: [http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/notablemeals.php](http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/notablemeals.php)

YouTube Demo: [http://www.youtube.com/embed/fiXqGrqcWq4](http://www.youtube.com/embed/fiXqGrqcWq4)

4. **Sniptastic!**

Sniptastic! is a tool for sharing and organizing code snippets. All code is saved in Evernote like regular notes. Sniptastic! offers line numbering and color annotation support. Evernote's Tag function is used to label the code language. In addition to the web tool view, there is also a Visual Studio plugin editor.

Official Website: [http://www.sniptastic.com/](http://www.sniptastic.com/)

Voting Page: [http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/sniptastic.php](http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/sniptastic.php)

YouTube Demo: [http://www.youtube.com/embed/iQ9fNu19lis](http://www.youtube.com/embed/iQ9fNu19lis)

5. **Touchanote**

Touchanote connects Evernote with real-world NFC (Near Field Communication) tags, becoming a bridge between the virtual and physical worlds (requires phone hardware support). There are many uses, such as early education for children or check-ins based on items.

Official Website: [http://www.touchanote.com/](http://www.touchanote.com/)

Voting Page: [http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/touchanote.php](http://www.evernote.com/about/etc/touchanote.php)

YouTube Demo: [http://www.youtube.com/embed/mpqpGQMzR7s](http://www.youtube.com/embed/mpqpGQMzR7s)

6. **Zendone**

Zendone is a productivity tool that strictly follows David Allen’s GTD (Getting Things Done) method.
