How should teachers dress so that students don't have sexual fantasies?

by gege on 2011-12-22 18:55:04

No fish-mouth shoes, no flat shoes, the length of hair tied up to the position of the bra clasp should be coiled up, the length of pants must be over the knee, toilet paper must be placed in a place where students can't see... When seeing these sweating terms, please don't calmly take them as jokes, because these are not scolding from a jealous husband to his beautiful wife, but possibly a regulation that may affect many people: it was said this is a detailed regulation for teachers' dressing and behavior in a school in Haizhu District - netizens seriously called it "The Ten Commandments for Teachers".

"The Ten Commandments for Teachers" were written by the teaching director of a school in Haizhu District. The credibility of the online post is relatively high. According to experience, we just wait for the "rumor denial statement" from relevant authorities to confirm it.

Anyway, middle school and primary school students are still minors, so teachers should avoid some things in their dressing, and schools can negotiate to introduce some regulations. But carefully looking at the content of these "Ten Commandments", they all reveal an unpleasant atmosphere, especially the neurotic regulations like "toilet paper must be placed in a place where students can't see", which makes people realize the malicious guess of putting oneself in others' shoes, thinking everyone is as sensitive as him and rich in sexual imagination: seeing toilet paper and associating it with genitals, then associating it with XXXXOOOO...

I'm not interested in knowing whether the designer of this "Ten Commandments for Teachers" is really psychologically dark, but I believe he/she is full of good intentions. After all, it's necessary to free middle school and primary school students from the interference of sexual fantasies, or say, even if sexual fantasies are not bad things, they at least can't happen in the classroom of middle school and primary school students.

Of course, there are Western countries that take sexual hints such as sexy dressing as incentives for students to listen actively in class, and have achieved good results, but this may not be suitable for us. We have the responsibility and obligation to believe that we need a conservative view on dressing, and also believe that only conservative dressing is the most suitable for students' growth.

Well, let's assume that we temporarily reached a consensus on the issue of teachers' dressing, such as "conservative" and "eliminating sexual fantasies". But specifically, what clothes to wear is "conservative", and exposing to where will lead to "sexual fantasies" etc., these details vary from person to person, and it's hard to reach a consensus casually. If you say that female teachers' hair length to the bra clasp should stop, because it will cause sexual fantasies, but I would say that sexual fantasies only occur when the hair length reaches the hip, what to do? Do we need to negotiate, I hear your opinion, you hear mine, and then each step back, unify the hair length to "waist-length"?

I believe that as long as there is a "negotiation" process first, after the issuance of the regulation of "female teacher's waist-length hair", even if it sounds absurd, the regulated object is still willing to abide by it.

Everything is negotiable, this "negotiation" may be more important than what kind of values you hold. Because as long as there is "negotiation", there is no need for schools and students to unify values on the issue of teachers' dressing. Different schools will differentiate into schools with different values according to different "negotiation results". Parents who take sexual hints as incentives can send their children to schools with "open" dress codes, and parents who take sexual hints as interference can naturally send their children to schools with "conservative" dress codes.

A post circulating on Guangzhou Mommy Network stipulates: the length of teachers' hair tied up to the position of the bra clasp should be coiled up, otherwise cut off. The position of coiling hair should be in the middle of the back of the head, not too high or too low; teachers under 30 years old are not allowed to perm their hair, while those over 40 are not allowed to tie their hair in a ponytail; teachers are not allowed to wear fish-mouth shoes or flat shoes, but the heel should be less than 3 centimeters.

In addition, the regulations have strict provisions for toilet paper and the pace of morning reading patrol: teachers should greet the leaders; breakfast is not allowed to be eaten at school; non-office supplies cannot be brought into the school; toilet paper must be placed in a place where students can't see; the pace of morning reading patrol must be neither too fast nor too slow; during meetings, the teaching director is not allowed to sleep, use mobile phones, or go on microblogs, and is required to keep meeting records, which will be checked at any time.

This teacher's "ten commandments" labeled as "perverse" by netizens were written by the teaching director of a school in Haizhu District.

The poster "a big fat monkey" is a teacher at that school. She self-mockingly said: "We summarized today that we should patrol the morning reading at a speed of 20 kilometers per hour, cut our hair to the shoulder, wear school uniforms and Huili shoes, greet the leaders when we see them, there should be no toilet paper in the office, and except for bringing personal items, nothing else should be brought back to school."

"The regulations that teachers wear work uniforms from Monday to Thursday and casual clothes on Friday, the color of casual clothes can't be too bright or revealing, skirts and pants must reach the knee, etc., can be understood and implemented," said "a big fat monkey." Some regulations are very puzzling, "not focusing on teaching but focusing on teachers. Now in class, we only pay attention to our feet. I haven't worn pretty skirts or shoes or done a pretty hairstyle for a whole week."

Some netizens worried: Will the "Ten Commandments" regulations be promoted in all schools in the city?

Peng Suyan, principal of Sanyuanli Primary School, believed that, "Such regulations may indicate problems in that school. But teachers are adults, setting such rigid regulations shows disrespect for teachers."

"Ten Commandments" for Teachers

● The length of hair tied up to the position of the bra clasp should be coiled up, otherwise cut off. The position of coiling hair should be in the middle of the back of the head, not too high or too low.

● Teachers under 30 years old are not allowed to perm their hair. Teachers over 40 are not allowed to tie their hair in a ponytail.

● For the dignity of teachers, wear work uniforms from Monday to Thursday, and casual clothes on Friday. Skirts can't be shorter than the knee. Colors can't be too bright, and there can't be too little fabric. Shoulders can't be exposed, and tight-fitting clothes are not allowed.

● Teachers are not allowed to wear shoes with heels higher than 3 centimeters, fish-mouth shoes, or flat shoes. The length of pants must be over the knee.

● All teachers should greet the leaders when they see them.

● Breakfast is not allowed to be eaten at school.

● Non-office supplies cannot be brought into the school.

● Toilet paper must be placed in a place where students can't see.

● The pace of morning reading patrol must be neither too fast nor too slow.

● Sleeping, using mobile phones, or going on microblogs during meetings is not allowed. Meeting records must be kept well.
