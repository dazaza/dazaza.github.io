Report from China News Service, Taiyuan, February 20th: Title: Strange Traffic Accident in Shanxi: Victim's body found at construction site, driver lost on way to hospital.

On February 13th, a traffic accident occurred in a village surrounded by urban areas in Taiyuan, Shanxi Province. The driver claimed that he was taking the injured person to the hospital but then mysteriously disappeared. Several hours later, the police found the victim at a remote construction site. Despite efforts to save him, the victim was pronounced dead.

The troublemaker claimed he was taking the injured person to the hospital, but the police found the deceased at a secluded construction site.

On February 20th, the reporter visited the home of the deceased, Li Zhiyuan. His wife, Wang Guohua, looked haggard and sobbed uncontrollably, unable to express herself fully. His family members recounted the details of the incident to the reporter.

In the afternoon of February 13th, Li Zhiyuan was helping his son run a kindergarten located on Nan Street in Beizhang Village when he discovered a leaking pipe and proceeded to repair it. At 6:15 PM, as he was about to climb out of a manhole on the street outside the kindergarten, a car with the license plate number Jin AHM274, a green Chery QQ, passed by and one of its wheels pushed Li Zhiyuan back into the well.

Li Zhiyuan's niece, Li Huifang, said, "At the time, bystanders heard my uncle say from below the well, 'I'm being crushed,' 'I'm being crushed.'"

When passersby and the driver lifted him out, he was already unconscious.

It was reported that after the driver borrowed a phone from a bystander to call 120, he and others helped lift Li Zhiyuan onto the offending vehicle. A concerned citizen recorded the license plate number. Li Huifang said, "According to the bystanders, two minutes after the call, the owner said they couldn't wait any longer and decided to take the unconscious victim to the nearby hospital themselves."

At this point, Li Zhiyuan's family had no idea what had happened.

At 7:00 PM that evening, Li Zhiyuan's daughter-in-law, Liu Lingling, noticed her father-in-law had been out for a long time and went to look for him. She was informed by a previous bystander that her father-in-law had already been taken to a nearby hospital by the offending driver, who had also helpfully recorded the license plate number. Subsequently, her family called the 110 and 122 emergency numbers.

Between 9:00 PM and 10:00 PM that night, the family and the police officers who answered the call searched all major hospitals in Taiyuan but could not find the victim. Meanwhile, relatives were frantically calling Li Zhiyuan's mobile phone, which remained unanswered.

Finally, at 10:50 PM that night, Li Zhiyuan's phone was answered by a police officer, informing the family that Li had been found at a construction site on Yari Street in the urban-rural fringe area of Taiyuan. The offending vehicle and driver had vanished without a trace.

At 11:30 PM that night, when the family arrived at the construction site, both the hospital and police were present. After unsuccessful attempts to revive him, it was confirmed that the individual had died.

By this time, four hours had passed since the incident.

The Li family believed that this was no longer just a case of hit-and-run; the driver failed to deliver the injured person to the hospital, delaying the golden treatment period, resulting in death. On the afternoon of February 14th, they reported the incident again to the criminal investigation team of Xiaodian District in Taiyuan.

The hidden portrait

On the morning of February 20th, at the home of the victim, Li Zhiyuan, his wife Wang Guohua held his portrait and wept incessantly, refusing to let go.

The portrait, about a foot tall, was not very clear. According to the family, Li Zhiyuan had always been in good health, rarely falling ill, and seldom took pictures. This time, due to the sudden nature of the incident, they had to hastily enlarge the image from other daily life photos of Li Zhiyuan.

This only enlarged portrait was hidden by the family. Li Huifang explained to the reporter that their 88-year-old grandmother would soon be arriving home, and they did not want her to see the arranged altar. It would be too much for the elderly to bear, losing her child before her.

Li Zhiyuan's mother had five children, three sons and two daughters. The eldest brother is already in his seventies, the second son is disabled, and the two sisters live far away. Only Li Zhiyuan was able to stay by the old woman’s side, taking care of her every day.

The house, simply decorated, still had four jars of honey on the TV cabinet, unused. According to Li Zhiyuan's sister, the responsibility of caring for their sick mother fell solely on her younger brother. Every morning, a glass of honey water and a blood pressure pill would be delivered to her bedside on time, like brushing teeth and washing face upon waking up.

Currently, the criminal investigation team of Xiaodian District in Taiyuan has launched an investigation, and the family anxiously awaits the results of the autopsy, hoping to quickly locate the offending driver. (End)
