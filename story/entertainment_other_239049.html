Emotion Log: There is a feeling called "no缘" (no destiny)

by lwf225 on 2009-09-16 23:04:52

Many things only make sense after we have experienced them. Just like feelings, it is only after we have been hurt that we know how to protect ourselves; it is only after having done something foolish that we learn to persist or give up at the right time. We gradually come to know ourselves through gains and losses. In fact, life does not require so much meaningless attachment. Nothing is so precious that we cannot part with it. Learn to let go, and life will be easier.

Learn to let go, turn away before tears fall, leaving a simple back view; learn to let go, bury yesterday in the bottom of your heart, leaving behind the most beautiful memories; learn to let go, so both sides can start more easily. A battered love is not necessarily unforgettable. This journey of deep affection but shallow fate has not been easy to reach today. Gently withdraw your hand, say goodbye, and thank you very much. Thank you for being there on this journey. I once said I loved you, and today, I still love you. But loving you doesn't mean I can be with you. It's like loving the wild sunflowers, loving them but unable to take them home.

Every kind of feeling is beautiful, and every phase of companionship is intoxicating. It is the regret of not being able to possess that makes us cherish more; it is the sleepless thoughts at midnight that make us feel more attached. Feelings are a questionnaire without answers, and arduous pursuit cannot make life more perfect. Perhaps a little regret, a touch of sadness, makes this answer sheet more lasting and further-reaching.

Pick up your mood and continue walking. Missing the flowers, you'll harvest the rain; missing him, I met you. Keep going, and you will eventually reap your own beauty.

A person who never wants to lose you is not necessarily the one who loves you or is loyal to you. Sometimes, they are just people with an unclear mind and a strong sense of possession, who might do all sorts of "lose-lose" things, and consider them justified.

If there is a "once possessed, forever not lost" obsession and desire for possession in your heart, the more you want a permanent guarantee of love, the more you deviate from the path.

Who says that liking something means you must get it? Sometimes, some people, in order to get what they like, exhaust their energy, use all kinds of tricks, and may even resort to any means necessary, leading to extremes. Maybe they get what they like, but during their pursuit, they lose things beyond count, and the price they pay is irreplaceable by what they gain. Perhaps the cost is heavy, only to be discovered by them in the end. Actually, liking something doesn't mean you have to get it.

Sometimes, forcing yourself to get something leaves your body and mind utterly exhausted, which is not worthwhile. Moreover, some things are "only to be appreciated from afar and not to be closely examined." Once you possess them, over time, you may find that they're not as good as you imagined. If you then realize that what you've lost and given up is more precious, I believe you will regret it immensely. Hence, there is often such a saying: "The things we can't get are always the best." Therefore, when you like something, getting it isn't necessarily the wisest choice.

Who says that liking someone means you must be together? Sometimes, some people, in order to be with the one they like, are willing to use primitive methods like "crying, making a scene, threatening suicide," hoping to retain their lover. Perhaps this keeps the person physically, but it can't hold their heart. Worse yet, some even sacrifice their young and vibrant lives for this, which might awaken the lover's response, but also brings them more guilt, self-reproach, and unease, bidding farewell to happiness forever. Actually, liking someone doesn't mean you must be together. Although some people often say, "I don't care about lasting forever, I only care about having had it," not everyone finds happiness in this. Liking someone most importantly means making them happy because their joys and sorrows affect your emotions. So there is also a saying, "You're happy, so I'm happy."

Liking something means learning to appreciate it, cherish it, and make it more precious.

Liking someone means making them happy and content, deepening the sincerity of the feelings. If you can't do this, then let go! Therefore, sometimes, some people need to learn to let go, because letting go is also a kind of beauty!
