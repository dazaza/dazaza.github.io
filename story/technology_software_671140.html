The two criminal suspects have been criminally detained by Chaoyang police on suspicion of extortion.

by yesebaby5s5j on 2012-02-21 16:29:49

Secret overseas returnee marriage and childbirth lead to blackmail: two netizens threaten to inform her parents. 16 instances of extortion totaling 135,000 yuan.

On October 24, 2011, the Chaoyang Criminal Investigation Team received a report from Ms. Tong, who claimed she had been repeatedly blackmailed by female netizen Zhang and Zhang's boyfriend, Mr. Xu.

It turned out that in 2007, while studying in Canada, Ms. Tong met her current husband, Mr. Zhao, online. Their relationship deepened over time, but the long-distance became the biggest obstacle between them. After much consideration, Ms. Tong decided to sacrifice her studies for love and secretly flew to Beijing to meet Mr. Zhao without informing her parents.

Upon meeting, both felt an instant connection. Ms. Tong then concealed her plans from her parents and began living together with Mr. Zhao in Beijing. In 2008, they had a boy and subsequently registered their marriage.

Ms. Zhang and her boyfriend, Mr. Xu, were also friends Ms. Tong met online. After returning to Beijing in 2007, Ms. Tong often met with them and regarded Ms. Zhang as a close confidante. Over time, Ms. Zhang and Mr. Xu learned about Ms. Tong's parents' phone number, her wealthy family background, and the fact that she had secretly married and had a child without her parents' knowledge.

In September 2009, Ms. Tong suddenly received a call from Ms. Zhang, who claimed that she and Mr. Xu were facing financial difficulties and hoped Ms. Tong could provide some money. Otherwise, they would reveal Ms. Tong's secret to her parents. Reluctantly, Ms. Tong gave them 10,000 yuan from the money her parents had provided for her studies.

Tasting the sweetness of success, Zhang and Xu became increasingly brazen, asking for more money repeatedly. From September 2009 to July 2010, Zhang and Xu extorted a total of 135,000 yuan from Ms. Tong through face-to-face payments and online banking transfers, totaling 16 instances.

In October 2011, despite having promised Ms. Tong they would no longer ask for money, Zhang and Xu, wanting to buy a car, succumbed to their greed and demanded more money, which Ms. Tong refused. Enraged, they sent a text message to Ms. Tong's mother revealing her secrets. Finally, Ms. Tong confessed to her parents, who were living elsewhere, about her marriage and childbirth, gaining their forgiveness. On October 24, Ms. Tong reported the case to the police.

On January 1, officers apprehended the suspects, Zhang and her boyfriend Xu. Both were unemployed individuals from the city. After police questioning, Zhang and Xu admitted to the crime of extorting Ms. Tong. Currently, the two suspects have been criminally detained by the Chaoyang police on charges of extortion.
