According to the 2005 annual survey data from iResearch, online media has become the main channel for users to learn about online education companies or schools. Among these channels, online advertising accounts for 48.1%, ranking first, followed by online forums at 43.9% and search engines at 41.5%.

However, the institutions responsible for nurturing this main force—schools—are still predominantly enthusiastic about traditional promotional methods. The image and promotional means of their websites are out of step with the times, unable to attract the attention of the youth group, and even create a gap between students and schools, failing to integrate with the open, equal, and free spirit of the Web 2.0 era.

It can be seen that in today's increasingly market-oriented education industry, if one wants to stand out in competition and gain the favor of students and parents, visionary principals should consider the internet as the most important marketing tool for school promotion.

Combining several core aspects of educational industry operations, we believe that online marketing in the education sector can be summarized into the following four parts:

1. Online Enrollment — Expanding the student base of the school.

With the traditional unified enrollment model being broken, schools have started to be incorporated into the track of market competition. How to find students has become the most headache-inducing issue for various schools. Opening newspapers reveals a dazzling array of advertisements with different titles and honors. Schools are anxious, and so are students—one is worried about not finding students, the other is worried about not finding a suitable school. It’s difficult for schools to recruit students, and it's hard for students to find schools, with information asymmetry making it difficult for schools and students to find common ground, thus failing to attract students to enroll.

The internet is the most comprehensive and richest carrier of information display. To gain the favor of students, schools must present as much detailed and rich information as possible on their websites to enhance students' understanding of the school. The following points are what Shanghengtong suggests should be displayed on school-type websites:

1) School Overview: Detailed introductions about the history of the school's establishment, its educational culture, leadership figures, campus environment, etc., allowing students to understand the school more deeply and comprehensively.

2) Faculty Team: Introductions to the excellent teaching staff at the school, which is an important part in enhancing the confidence of students and parents.

3) Majors and Course Settings: This part of the introduction is also a core concern for students. A simple search function can be set up to help students quickly find courses they like.

4) Teaching Advantages: Besides the school's own introduction, graduates or parents of students can recommend the school, thereby enhancing its credibility.

5) Online Consultation: If students have any questions about the school, they can raise them online, and timely responses from the school will help build trust.

6) Online Registration: Online registration serves as a crucial channel for understanding student intentions, allowing timely contact with students to answer their questions.

2. Online Campus — Creating a New Space for Communication Between Schools and Parents.

The internet has become a tool that students cannot live without every day. Utilizing the interactivity of the internet to establish communication with students and parents not only strengthens mutual feelings but also helps understand the thoughts of students and parents. Additionally, it showcases students' rich creativity and imagination, enlivening the school atmosphere and enhancing campus culture.

1) Online Communication: Establish a communication platform among students, parents, and teachers to discuss issues equally.

2) Campus News: Timely reporting of the latest news and activities in the school, arranging student correspondents to report topics of interest to students, attracting their attention.

3) Online Bulletin Board: Moving the traditional blackboard bulletin board online, fully utilizing students' intelligence and talents, providing a space for them to showcase their skills.

4) Online Survey: Conduct research on issues of concern to students on the website, listening to the opinions of students and parents to enhance the school's credibility.

3. Employment (Further Studies) Guidance — Paving the Way for Students’ Future

Further studies (employment), as a topic of common concern for students and parents, is also an important focus for schools. On the website, a special column for further studies and employment guidance can be established to provide beneficial support for students' futures.

1) Job Recommendations: For some outstanding students, recommendations can be made on the school website, reflecting the school's people-oriented educational philosophy.

2) Employment/Further Studies Information: Extensively searching for the latest employment and further studies information to attract browsing by students and parents, demonstrating the school's concern for students.

3) Employment/Further Studies Guidance: Answering the difficulties encountered by students in further studies/employment, helping them find their direction in employment/further studies.

4. Alumni Home — Accumulating Long-term School Brand Value

Accumulating alumni resources can bring the best word-of-mouth promotion for the school. By forming an online alumni association, not only can alumni recognize and communicate with each other, accumulating social resources, but they can also become the "living banners" of school promotion, attracting students to join.

1) Alumni Directory: Recording the contact information of each graduating class for easy retrieval by alumni.

2) Alumni Testimonials: Let successful alumni express their sentiments towards the school, enhancing alumni affection for their alma mater and giving full confidence to students and parents.

3) Alumni Exchange: Establishing a space for alumni exchange, maintaining regular contact among alumni, and bringing more social resources to the school.
