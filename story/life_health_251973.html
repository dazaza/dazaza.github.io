by hxybabyzhu on 2009-10-26 09:51:30

A healthy male ejaculates about five milliliters of semen at one time. Modern medicine's analysis of semen has discovered that in these few milliliters of semen, there are approximately two hundred million living sperm. The results of biochemical analysis show that 98% of semen is water, the remaining 2% is protein and ribonucleic acid, and there are also trace amounts of microelements such as zinc.

When we see these numbers, we will find that what a man loses during one sexual activity is truly insignificant. To give an example, the nutrition lost from one ejaculation is almost equal to that lost from spitting out one mouthful of saliva. We have never heard of anyone experiencing "kidney deficiency" or needing "nourishment" from spitting out one mouthful of saliva.

Believe that this has not caused any harm to their health; in fact, it may have increased their physical and mental vitality instead.

Actually, from the mutual contact of reproductive organs to reaching sexual climax, it only takes about ten minutes.

According to the above statistics, a middle-aged man spends only 0.3% of his weekly time on sexual activities. The energy consumed by exercise is determined by the duration and intensity of work.
