CSR Denies Allegations of Excessive Promotion Costs for Bullet Trains; Reporting Party Says It Will Release Names If Necessary

by king3h0a on 2012-03-07 10:36:44

Yesterday, China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation responded to media reports regarding the [luxurious] high-speed train procurement, stating that the reported prices for the high-speed train components were significantly inconsistent with reality.

Caixin Media, which first reported on the [luxurious high-speed trains], responded yesterday by saying they strictly adhered to journalistic ethics and professionalism. When necessary, they would release the [List].

On February 5th, Sheng Guangzu stated in an interview that China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation is not a subordinate enterprise of the Ministry of Railways. Specific issues regarding corruption during bidding should be addressed to China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation.

South Carriage: Media Exposed Prices Inconsistent with Reality

China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation responded yesterday on its official website to media reports [ladies' underwear brands][http://www.tingfeier.com].

The response stated that [CSR Sifang Co., Ltd., a subsidiary of China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation, is an important R&D and manufacturing base for China's high-speed trains. It has always paid attention to the construction of corporate control systems. We have noticed recent media reports on the procurement of high-speed train components and have conducted careful investigations and verifications. The reported procurement prices are significantly inconsistent with reality. Since last year, the Ministry of Railways has taken a series of measures to standardize tender management. As suppliers, we fully support and actively cooperate. We thank the public and the media for their attention and welcome everyone's supervision.]

Relevant personnel from China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation said that the relevant situation had been verified and reported upwards, but due to commercial secrets, it was not convenient to disclose. Such a big issue cannot be ignored. If something is wrong, it won't be right. As a company, there are many aspects to consider, one cannot speak hastily or just for the sake of argument.

South Carriage is an independently accounted state-owned enterprise

It is understood that the predecessor of China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation was the original China Railway Locomotive and Rolling Stock Industrial Corporation under the Ministry of Railways. In September 2000, China Railway Locomotive and Rolling Stock Industrial Corporation decoupled from the Ministry of Railways, split into two, and restructured into North Carriage Group and South Carriage Group, both large companies [Guangzhou car rental company][http://www.syzc020.com]. Both are centrally managed state-owned sole proprietorship groups. After decoupling, these two groups became independently accounted enterprises. [www.tingfeier.com]

According to railway insiders, the high-speed trains produced by North and South Carriage Groups have no exports, and their largest and only customer is the Ministry of Railways.

Ministry of Railways: South Carriage Needs to Investigate Component Prices

On February 20th this year, media exposed that the procurement prices for multiple equipment of CRH2 type high-speed trains were much higher than market prices.

A source close to the Ministry of Railways told the media that senior officials of the Ministry of Railways had already met with executives such as Wang Jun, General Manager of CSR Sifang Locomotive & Rolling Stock Co., Ltd., requesting South Carriage to investigate component prices, strictly manage self-bidding procurement, ensure transparency, avoid similar incidents, effectively reduce costs, and reflect this in future bids.

South Carriage denied this information.

■ Response

Caixin Media

Strictly Adheres to Journalistic Ethics

Last night, Caixin published a statement saying that the price information in Caixin's report came from an obtained list titled “CRH2 Type High-Speed Train Components Supplier List” (hereinafter referred to as the “List”).

Through interviews and verification with multiple independent sources, the editorial department of Caixin confirmed the authenticity of this list and conducted responsible investigations, ultimately disclosing the fact that the procurement prices of some components were much higher than the market prices for similar components.

As described in the report “Luxurious High-Speed Trains” and learned through Caixin interviews, the component prices shown in the “List” are the procurement prices after CSR reduced them twice, differing by less than 10% from the procurement prices at the time of publication.

During the process of interviewing, writing, and editing for this report, the editorial department of Caixin strictly followed journalistic ethics and professional standards, especially paying attention to the possible impact of related facts on the public and relevant units, maintaining considerable caution.

In response to China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation’s “Explanation,” Caixin Media maintains the stance of the “Luxurious High-Speed Trains” report and is willing to publish the “List” if necessary.

■ Retrospective

Media Exposes High-Speed Train Equipment Procurement List

On February 20th this year, Caixin Media's New Century Weekly exposed a procurement list for CRH2 type high-speed train equipment, where the procurement prices for multiple internal train equipment items were much higher than market prices.

An automatic washbasin priced at 72,395 yuan, a colored marble washbasin at 26,000 yuan, an induction water valve at 12,800 yuan, a restroom tissue holder at 1,125 yuan, forming a total bathroom unit worth hundreds of thousands of yuan; a 15-inch LCD monitor costing over ten thousand yuan, a single-person seat at 22,000 yuan, and a refrigerated display cabinet at 68,000 yuan.

It is understood that the CRH2 type high-speed train set is a high-speed train produced by China South Locomotive and Rolling Stock Corporation based on technology introduced from Japan's Shinkansen.

Through investigation, Caixin Media's New Century Weekly found that many suppliers of high-speed train components lack qualifications but have become monopolists in the high-speed rail supply chain through joint ventures with foreign companies, squeezing out other long-established suppliers. Some companies entered solely based on connections, lacking research and production capabilities, profiting merely from reselling products and technologies. However, due to high-level connections, the main manufacturers could not replace them, and even companies with real technology had to rely on these firms to enter the high-speed rail industry.
