The reason why the Three Gorges Project was blocked

by 3yll869c5t on 2009-11-23 13:12:14

The operation and management of the Three Gorges Project is not only a technical problem, but also an issue involving the decision-making mechanism. At present, there are many departments that can influence the scheduling of the Three Gorges Project, making coordination very difficult. Even if the Three Gorges Project has new dispatching regulations, its operation and management cannot be carried out rigidly according to the book, but may need to be optimized and adjusted in time according to special circumstances of different years. "The old set of water resources dispatching concepts is already hard to adapt to the new situation," wrote Jiang Shiqiang, Wu Zhi, and Tang Yaoguo for the Outlook News Weekly.

After 17 years of construction, the final storage level of the Three Gorges Reservoir is 175 meters, after which it will transition from the initial operation phase to the normal operation stage, comprehensively playing its integrated benefits of flood control, power generation, navigation, and water replenishment. The trial storage water level target of 175 meters by the end of October or early November this year may not be achieved due to drought conditions downstream. This marks the second consecutive year that the project's schedule has been disrupted. Last year, frequent geological disasters and water extraction difficulties upstream prevented the first trial storage to 175 meters, stopping at 172.8 meters.

This year, although the geological disaster risks upstream remain concerning, the more direct cause is the drought in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River. With the middle and lower reaches "crying out for water," the discharge flow had to be increased beyond plan, while the inflow from upstream continued to decrease. For nearly a week, the water level upstream of the Three Gorges has hovered around 170-171 meters, making the 175-meter goal temporarily out of reach.

Faced with this awkward situation, interviewed experts such as Academician Zheng Shouren, Chief Engineer of the Yangtze River Water Resources Commission, suggested considering earlier water storage next year, "otherwise, the Three Gorges Reservoir may not be fully filled in most years."

However, if water storage begins earlier, it might face risks such as upstream flood prevention and reservoirs "competing for water." Experts like Nie Fangrong, former vice chief engineer of the Hunan Provincial Department of Water Resources, believe that the traditional water resources dispatching concept is no longer suitable for the new situation. The dispatching method of the Three Gorges Project needs improvement, and the water resources of the main and tributaries of the Yangtze River need comprehensive planning and scientific dispatching.

Drought Hinders Water Storage

According to relevant persons in charge of the Three Gorges Group, after 17 years of construction, the final storage level of the Three Gorges Reservoir is 175 meters, after which it will enter the normal operation phase, comprehensively playing its integrated benefits of flood control, power generation, navigation, and water replenishment.

Since September 28, 2008, the first trial storage was initiated. Despite issues such as geological disasters and water extraction difficulties, the assessment by the expert group on the quality inspection of the Three Gorges Hub Project considered that all aspects including hub operation, safety monitoring, geology and earthquakes, ecological water quality, sediment, and relocation were normal, and the Three Gorges Reservoir had the conditions for experimental storage to 175 meters in 2009.

On September 15 this year, the new round of trial storage was initiated with a starting storage level of 145 meters and an inflow volume of 23,500 cubic meters per second. This storage plan was more detailed than last year, stipulating that by the end of September, the water level of the Three Gorges Reservoir should not exceed 158 meters in principle, and by the end of October, it should reach 175 meters. If the water level does not reach 175 meters by the end of October, it would continue to store water until 175 meters in November.

By the end of September, the Three Gorges Reservoir successfully completed the first stage of the storage task, with the water level rising to about 158 meters and a storage volume of over 7 billion cubic meters. Just as success seemed within reach, an unexpected drought emerged.

Since mid-September, rare droughts have occurred in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River, especially in Dongting Lake, Poyang Lake, Xiangjiang River, and Ganjiang River, causing difficulties in urban and rural residents' production and living water use, fishermen being out of work, and shipping being obstructed.

In response to this situation, since October 19, the Yangtze River Flood Control and Drought Relief General Command has three times required the Three Gorges Reservoir, which is in the trial storage period, to increase the discharge flow. By late October, the discharge flow of the Three Gorges Reservoir remained no less than 9,500 cubic meters per second, 3,000 cubic meters per second more than the original plan, affecting the storage process.

As the dry season of the Yangtze River approaches, the inflow into the Three Gorges continues to decrease. At 14:00 on November 4, it fell below 9,000 cubic meters per second, reaching 8,800 cubic meters per second. Although the water level of the Three Gorges Reservoir struggled to rise to 171 meters at 9:00 on November 1, it slightly retreated afterward and currently remains near 171 meters.

According to the dispatch order of the Yangtze River Flood Control General Command, starting from 14:00 on October 31, if the inflow into the Three Gorges exceeds 9,500 cubic meters per second, the discharge flow should be controlled at no less than 9,500 cubic meters per second; if the inflow is less than 9,500 cubic meters per second, the discharge flow should be balanced with the inflow.

Relevant persons in charge of the China Three Gorges Corporation indicated that if there is no significant inflow in the upper reaches of the Yangtze River in November and the water demand downstream remains unchanged, the Three Gorges Reservoir will not be able to store water up to 175 meters this year. If the inflow drastically decreases, the Three Gorges Reservoir may even experience an inflow shortfall.

Water and Drought Entanglement

At 8:00 am on October 22, the Chenglingji water level of Dongting Lake rose from 21.62 meters, maintained for two days, to 21.67 meters. Chen Weili, deputy director of the Yiyang City Flood Control Office in Hunan Province, said, "This is the first rise since the Chenglingji water level fell from 30.93 meters on August 10, indicating that increasing the discharge flow of the Three Gorges Reservoir has had a noticeable effect."

If releasing water can alleviate drought, what is the relationship between drought and water storage? Chen Weili told our reporter a set of data: after the Three Gorges Reservoir started storing water, its discharge flow gradually decreased. For example, between September 15 and 17, the discharge flow was between 16,000 and 17,000 cubic meters per second, reduced to 11,000 cubic meters per second by the end of the month, and further dropped to 6,770 cubic meters per second on October 19. In normal years, the discharge flow ranges between 13,000 and 20,000 cubic meters per second, averaging 18,500 cubic meters per second in October.

"People easily associate droughts with the water retention of the Three Gorges Dam," but Academician Zheng Shouren believes that water storage is not the main cause of drought. The main reason lies in the drought conditions of these regions themselves, i.e., continuous hot and dry weather since September with little rainfall, unable to form runoff.

Our reporter learned from the China Meteorological Administration that the main reason for the drought in southern regions this time is continuous high temperature and little rain.

Chen Weili also admitted that the main cause of the drought in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River this year is still the insufficient rainfall. "The water storage of the Three Gorges only exacerbates the situation."

Following the same logic that water storage is not the main cause of drought, Zheng Shouren pointed out that the role of water replenishment in alleviating drought is also limited. Increasing the discharge flow of the Three Gorges Reservoir helps alleviate the drought in Dongting Lake and Poyang Lake and their surrounding areas, but it may not help much in maintaining the water levels of the Xiangjiang River and Ganjiang River.

Chen Weili also believed that although the discharge flow has increased, it is just a drop in the bucket for the "eight hundred li Dongting." It is understood that although the water levels at the mouths of Dongting Lake and Poyang Lake on the Yangtze River mainstream have stabilized, the water level of the Changsha section of the Xiangjiang River is still falling.

According to the interviewed experts, the supply and demand contradiction of Yangtze River water resources has existed for a long time. Since 1877 when actual hydrological data began, the average inflow volume of the upper reaches of the Yangtze River in October was 19,700 cubic meters per second, but due to the reduction in precipitation in the upper reaches and the construction of large reservoirs, the average inflow volume in the same period in the past 20 years has decreased by about 3,000 cubic meters per second. And this October, the inflow volume of the upper reaches was only above 14,000 cubic meters per second for six days, far below the normal level.

On the other hand, due to the recent development of industrial and agricultural economy in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River, and the migration of many water intake outlets from polluted lakes to the Yangtze River, the water supply burden of the Yangtze River mainstream has greatly increased. Zheng Shouren said that when the preliminary design of the Three Gorges Project was made in 1993, it was thought that a discharge flow of 5,000 cubic meters per second in autumn and winter could meet the water needs of the middle and lower reaches, but now, a discharge flow of 7,000 cubic meters per second in mid-October may not be enough.

Advance Water Storage?

In the view of the interviewed experts, the current inflow volume is continuously decreasing, and the probability of the reservoir reaching 175 meters is already very small. For the Three Gorges Project, the experimental storage to 175 meters is a necessary comprehensive test. Only when the water level reaches 175 meters can many problems be verified, and the overall benefits of the project can be fully realized.

More importantly, if the Three Gorges Reservoir is not stored up to a high water level, during January and February of the following year when the inflow volume from the upper reaches is the smallest, it will not be able to replenish water downstream, and the drought downstream will be more severe then. Historical data shows that in normal years, the inflow volume from the upper reaches of the Yangtze River in January and February is between 3,000 and 4,000 cubic meters per second, with the minimum value being only 2,700 cubic meters per second. Only after the Three Gorges stores water can a discharge flow of 5,000 to 6,000 cubic meters per second be guaranteed every January and February.

The interviewed experts believe that an effective way to solve the contradiction between water storage and water use is to advance the water storage time and make full use of flood resources. Zheng Shouren said that compared to the situation when the Three Gorges Project was initially designed, the inflow volume of the upper reaches of the Yangtze River in October has decreased significantly. Therefore, it can be considered to start water storage on September 10 and raise the water level to 160 meters or even 162 meters by the end of September.

It is understood that the Three Gorges Reservoir started the first trial storage on September 28 last year. Although it did not increase the discharge flow repeatedly like this year, if it had not encountered a once-in-588-years flood with a peak flow of 33,000 cubic meters per second in early November last year, the reservoir water level would not have reached 172.8 meters. Starting water storage on September 15 this year aims to take advantage of the autumn floods in September to raise the water level as much as possible, so that a certain discharge flow can be maintained in October when the inflow from the upper reaches is relatively low. However, this lead time may still be insufficient.

In the past, the dispatching regulations of the Three Gorges Project were anti-seasonal regulation, discharging water during the flood season and storing water during the dry season. Nie Fangrong believes that in the future, we can advocate "storing floodwaters to supplement dry periods" under the premise of controllable flood prevention risks, transforming "floodwater into resources," otherwise, the water storage of the Three Gorges may occupy the ecological water resources of the middle and lower reaches in October and November, leading to water shortages in the middle and lower reaches.

However, advancing water storage may also lead to upstream flood prevention risks. Yuan Jie, Director of the Three Gorges Hub Cascade Dispatching Center of the Three Gorges Group, believes that retaining the "tail" of the flood can achieve risk control. The meteorological and hydrological forecasting level in our country has improved. Even if a major flood may brew upstream after the start of water storage, the Three Gorges Reservoir can still manage timely dispatching.

He said that from mid-July to mid-August each year is the main flood season of the Yangtze River, and the probability and magnitude of floods in September are much smaller. After entering September, the water level in the middle and lower reaches has decreased significantly compared to the flood season, with a strong flood-carrying capacity, and large floods can be discharged completely. Moreover, the silt content of the upstream inflow water in recent years has decreased by about 50% compared to the initial design of the project. Therefore, the Three Gorges Reservoir has the conditions to start water storage earlier.

Not Just a Technical Problem

Advancing water storage also brings an unknown risk.

Currently, the total volume of reservoirs built in the main and tributaries of the upper reaches of the Yangtze River has reached 28 billion cubic meters, and it is expected to exceed 60 billion cubic meters by 2020. Interviewed experts indicated that under the current trend of decreasing inflow in the upper reaches of the Yangtze River, if a large number of reservoirs store water simultaneously at the end of the flood season or after the flood season, a "mutual water grabbing" situation will inevitably occur, affecting the play of individual benefits and maximizing comprehensive benefits.

In August this year, at the acceptance meeting for the normal water storage level of 175 meters in the third phase of the Yangtze River Three Gorges Hub Project, Pan Jiazhen, deputy leader of the acceptance team and head of the expert group, pointed out that in the future, the Three Gorges Project and the hydropower stations already built, under construction, and planned in the upper reaches of the Yangtze River will form the world's largest reservoir cluster, requiring joint dispatching. This means that there are still a large number of complex issues to be studied and resolved, and at the same time, the Three Gorges Project may play a greater benefit and impact than specified in the preliminary design.

"The reservoirs and lakes in the lower reaches of the Yangtze River should also be included in this unified dispatching," Nie Fangrong said. Taking this year as an example, relying solely on the Three Gorges Reservoir to increase the discharge flow is difficult to raise the water level of the Changsha section of the Xiangjiang River. The largest reservoir upstream of the Xiangjiang River - the Dongjiang Reservoir, since it did not bear the task of water replenishment, had released the accumulated 2.5 billion cubic meters of water before September for power generation, resulting in the reservoir now being unable to effectively replenish water to the Xiangjiang River at a low water level.

Zheng Shouren also mentioned that the Three Gorges Project can currently withstand a flood that occurs once every 100 years, but in the face of a flood that occurs once every 1,000 years, it still needs to cooperate with the Jingjiang diversion and flood storage project. However, the implementation of joint dispatching of the reservoir cluster may hope to cancel many flood diversion areas in the middle and lower reaches in the future, allowing them to develop the economy with peace of mind.

Many interviewed experts believe that running and managing the Three Gorges Project well is not only a technical problem but also involves a decision-making mechanism problem. Currently, there are many departments that can influence the scheduling of the Three Gorges Project, making coordination very difficult. Even if the Three Gorges Project has new dispatching regulations, its operation and management cannot be carried out rigidly according to the book but may need to be optimized and adjusted in time according to special circumstances of different years. Therefore, the comprehensive planning and scientific dispatching of water resources in the Yangtze River Basin need to be conducted at the national level. □ (Source: Xinhua News Agency)
