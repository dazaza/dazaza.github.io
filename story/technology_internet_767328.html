Apple Design Chief Ive: The Most Important Part of Design Is Full Commitment

by anonymous on 2013-10-11 19:49:28

Apple's chief designer, Jony Ive, in a media interview, stated that the most important aspect during the product design process is genuinely valuing the work and giving it one’s best effort. Moreover, designers should enhance their physical interaction with various materials rather than overly relying on computer modeling.

"In dealing with things people won't immediately notice, we are incredibly passionate," Ive said in an interview alongside another designer, Marc Newson. "It's like making the back panel of a cabinet. No one sees it, but you still have to do it. A product is a form of communication — it shows your value system, what you care about."

These two designers will collaborate for the first time at a Sotheby's auction in November, where they will auction over 40 items to benefit Bono's Product (Red) anti-AIDS project. Among these items is a limited edition Leica camera co-designed by them, a metal table they jointly designed, and a pair of 18k gold Apple Earpod headphones.

This camera received widespread praise upon its debut, with observers believing it embodies Ive's minimalist aesthetic philosophy. However, Ive pointed out that designing such products typically starts with the material, not initially envisioning the final appearance. "We rarely talk about shape; our discussions revolve around processes, materials, and working methods," he mentioned when referring to his exchanges with Newson.

Newson also agreed: "Indeed, it's not about shape, but it relates to many other things."

Ive also expressed dissatisfaction with certain trends in today's design field, such as how many designers have gradually distanced themselves from hands-on contact with materials, turning instead to entirely utilizing computer modeling programs for conceptual designs. He said, "Many people graduating now don't know how to make things by hand. In this way, you don't understand the properties of a material or how to fully shape it. Unless you do it yourself, it's impossible to comprehend these aspects."

Ive emphasized that the key to shaping devices like the iPhone lies in focus and effort. He pointed out, "The most important thing is that you truly care about the work and give it your best effort."

Ive anticipates that the camera he designed in collaboration with Newson will fetch $6 million at auction due to the significant amount of labor hours it consumed and because it is unique. The design and production process of this camera took nine months, involving the creation of 947 prototype machines and 561 models. Apple indicated that 55 engineers contributed to this process, accumulating a total of 2,149 hours of work. One engineer spent 50 hours assembling the final product.

As Apple's senior vice president overseeing design, Ive is responsible not only for hardware but also software. The latest iOS 7 mobile operating system was crafted under his direction.

Due to his outstanding contributions to design and the business world, Ive was knighted in 2011. In 2012, Apple's 16-member design team, led by Ive, was rated as the best design studio in the past 50 years. Earlier this year, Ive also received the "Blue Peter" badge from BBC Children's programming.
