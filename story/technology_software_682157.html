Generally speaking, when taking portraits, being farther away is always better than being closer. Therefore, when taking photos, choose a suitable focal length lens and maintain a certain distance between the lens and the subject. Based on practical knowledge, if using a standard lens to shoot a portrait headshot, the optimal distance should be between 6-8 feet; for a bust shot, it should be between 8-9 feet; for a full-body shot, a distance of 13-22 feet is ideal. 6. When showing the subject in a seated position, avoid letting them sit back fully into the chair like usual.
