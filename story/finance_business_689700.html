Exclusive Interview with Dell Executive: Focusing on PC Sales Figures Isn't Very Meaningful

by anonymous on 2012-03-04 14:10:17

Sina Technology News, morning message on November 14th, Steve Felice, President of Dell's Consumer and Small Medium Enterprises Department, recently stated that PC product sales are no longer Dell's primary goal. The company will pay more attention to the market share of solutions among customers.

Felice recently attended a business conference held by the US-China Chamber of Commerce, visited Chinese customers, and took time to exclusively accept an interview with Sina Technology. Felice is responsible for Dell's small and medium enterprise customer solutions business as well as the entire line of Dell consumer products business, including desktops, laptops, software, peripherals, and product design and sales.

PC sales are no longer Dell's top priority

Market research company Gartner recently announced in a report that Lenovo Group's global PC shipments reached 12.35 million units in the third quarter, surpassing Dell to become the world's second largest PC manufacturer with a global market share of 13.5%. Dell's PC shipments for the quarter were 10.67 million units, with a global market share of 11.6%.

Although Dell's ranking has dropped, Felice takes it relatively lightly. "Many people like to use sales volume to measure rankings. A few years ago, Dell began to convey a view that sales volume is no longer as important to total revenue growth as before, and we started to transform into a comprehensive solutions company," Felice said.

He added that sales volume is no longer Dell's top priority. "If you look at the changes happening in the industry, you will find that there are products priced at $200 in the market, as well as products priced at $2000. There are servers priced at $500, as well as servers priced at $50,000. Focusing only on sales volume actually doesn't make much sense."

He emphasized that Dell is more concerned about the market share of solutions among customers. "Our revenue and profits continue to grow. We are measuring our success from a wider range of aspects."

In the past year or so, Dell has acquired several technical solution vendors and launched solutions for multiple industries. Michael Dell, founder of Dell, bluntly told Wall Street in June this year that Dell is currently an enterprise solutions company.

Felice said in an interview that Dell will focus on mergers and acquisitions in the future. "You will see that Dell's acquisition work will be more focused on the solution field of the technology market."

The PC industry will continue to grow

Regarding the future development of the PC industry, Felice believes that computing technology has always been an important source of productivity, and the industry is expected to continue growing, but sometimes it will fluctuate with the economic situation.

He pointed out that "the growth rate of the PC industry is directly related to global economic factors. In some regions with stronger economies, PC growth remains strong. China is a typical example. Dell's business in China also maintains good growth momentum. However, in some developed areas where the financial crisis is more serious, you can observe that the growth rate is indeed slowing down."

Gartner's recently published data shows that global PC shipments reached 91.8 million units in the third quarter, growing by 3.2% compared to the same period last year, which is lower than the previously expected growth rate. In the second quarter this year, China surpassed the United States to become the world's largest PC market.

Regarding Lenovo Group, which surpassed Dell to become the world's second largest PC manufacturer, Felice admitted that Lenovo is indeed a strong competitor, but he said that the two companies actually have completely different strategies. "Our strategy and focus is to become a more comprehensive solution provider, while they seem to focus more on selling more laptops and desktops."

Felice said, "Our focuses are different. Dell will continue to promote its independent development strategy. Lenovo has indeed achieved great success in the Chinese and global markets, but Dell's standard for measuring success is not sales volume."

More smart phones and tablet products will be launched

Regarding the much-anticipated smart phones and tablet computers, Felice said that Dell is just starting in this field and has very little influence in this area. However, he said that Dell has been working hard to understand the concept of IT consumerization.

Currently, Dell has launched several tablet computer products. Not long ago, they also launched a 10-inch tablet computer in China. Felice revealed that Dell's 5-inch Streak product has been selling well, but he did not give specific numbers. He pointed out that Dell will achieve something in the tablet computer and smart phone fields in the next few years.

He also said that Microsoft's upcoming Windows 8 system is an excellent product that will help change the prospects and trends of tablet computers, even smart phones. He emphasized that Dell will maintain a very open strategy, so it will continue to use Android and Windows platforms. (Liang Luo, Jing Quan)

Below is the full transcript of the interview:

Sina Technology: What is the purpose of your visit to China?

Steve: For various reasons, I often come to China, and each time I try to do something different. The purpose of this trip is to attend the meeting held yesterday by the American Chamber of Commerce in China with the Chinese Premier and several Chinese ministers. I met with many customers and have been evaluating business.

Sina Technology: According to the latest report by ABC, although global PC sales are increasing, the growth in this market is slowing. How do you see this report?

Steve: The growth rate of this industry is directly linked to global economic factors. In some regions with stronger economies, PC growth remains strong. China is a typical example. Dell's business in this region maintains good growth momentum. In some developed regions where the financial crisis is more serious, you can observe that the growth rate is indeed slowing down. Computing technology has always been an important source of productivity, and the industry is expected to continue growing, but sometimes it fluctuates with the economic situation.

Sina Technology: Are there any other reasons? Will smart phones and tablet computers hinder the development of this business market?

Steve: We haven't seen any actual threats from similar products such as smart phones or tablet computers yet. Research shows that they are only additional devices in the market because many people cannot actually afford to buy three different devices. Tablet computers are good platforms for browsing content, but their efficiency in creating content is still low. I think most of the changes in this industry are more about responding to the economic environment rather than market changes.

Sina Technology: Still about the PC market, Dell is currently the world's third largest PC manufacturer in this field, losing the second position. What is your response to this change?

Steve: This rank is the entire criterion you use to measure first, second, or third place. Many people like to use sales volume to measure rankings. A few years ago, we began to convey a view that sales volume is no longer as important to total revenue growth as before, and we started to transform into a comprehensive solutions company. Sales volume is really not our top priority. If you look at the changes happening in the industry, you will find that there are products priced at $200 in the market, as well as products priced at $2000. There are servers priced at $500, as well as servers priced at $50,000. Focusing only on sales volume actually doesn't make much sense. We are more concerned about the market share of our solutions among customers. Our revenue and profits continue to grow. We are measuring our success from a wider range of aspects.
