People who are used to being watched will always wear masks unconsciously.

People who are used to observing others with a calm head will always unconsciously look for the faint cracks on that mask.

Anyone who loves light and shadow, and cares about rumors, is closest to the stars. Close enough to touch the pictures in newspapers and magazines, allowing for an intimate inspection.

So photographer Xie Manglang never expected to have more contact with Yan Cai. The other party was too dazzling, and thus not an appropriate intersection point.

However, it's always like this: when someone exists under the spotlight, another must press the flash for them. Whether you, or him, or the faceless masses, just for that one person to shine the brightest light at the moment of eye contact. He unfortunately had to cast a beam of light for him.

Unfortunately, he was pulled closer, allowing him to glimpse behind that light, revealing the present day and past.

The beauty of 24 frames may not be real, the people who weave dreams may not linger in those dreams. Good actors who get too into their roles probably always have truly good lives.

Fortunately, when the fleeting light fades away, the glamorous figures who long existed in brilliance will eventually settle down one day.

What remains for them is not merely love.

the Ge Huai they spoke of was precisely Yan Cai's agent who was being interviewed. Yan Cai is well-known in the entertainment industry for being kind, polite to anyone to the point where it seems unlike a rising star idol. A famous rumor in the industry is that no matter how difficult or sharp the journalist is, after interviewing Yan Cai, they will always praise and defend him without exception. But despite Yan Cai's good reputation inside and outside the circle, mentioning his agent absolutely makes all journalists grit their teeth.

In the second part of "Floating Light,"

Xie Manglang did not have the habit of reading entertainment magazines. In fact, every month he only bought one copy of National Geographic, specializing in portrait photography magazines which he didn't buy. However, that day when he went out to lunch with a few colleagues, one of them, an intern art editor around his age, was full from eating and drinking, flipping through her favorite fashion magazine while sighing, "Nowadays, there are fewer and fewer pretty faces in print modeling who aren't stiff. They all have deadpan faces, it's really boring."

before he even took a step, he felt a force pulling him from behind. Unprepared, he was pulled backward. Initially, Xie Manglang's mind went blank for a moment, but when he came to, he saw that both he and Yan Cai were already standing in the shadows below the steps. Xie Manglang could only see Yan Cai's eyes, filled with playful laughter. He couldn't help but feel startled and angry, his hands cold, but his face burning. "What are you doing..."
