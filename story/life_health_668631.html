What are the causes of menstrual irregularities in women? There are many reasons for menstrual irregularities in women. Gynecological diseases such as acute and chronic pelvic inflammatory disease and uterine fibroids can cause menstrual irregularities in women. In addition to gynecological diseases, some bad living habits can also cause menstrual irregularities in women. Causes of menstrual irregularities in women:

1. Abnormal emotions and excessive stress.

Women who suffer from long-term excessive pressure, mental suppression, holding in anger, or major mental stimulation and psychological trauma, etc., may experience menstrual disorders, dysmenorrhea, or amenorrhea.

2. Cold stimulation

Some women prioritize style over warmth and do not pay attention to keeping warm. They dress lightly for beauty's sake and do not take care during their period. This can easily lead to menstrual irregularities in women. When women are exposed to cold during their period, it can cause excessive contraction of blood vessels in the pelvis, leading to reduced menstruation or even amenorrhea.

3. Smoking and alcohol can also cause menstrual irregularities in women.

Certain components in tobacco and alcohol and alcohol itself can interfere with the physiological process of menstruation in women, causing menstrual irregularities. Therefore, women should quit smoking and drinking for the sake of their health. Experts point out that 25% to 32% of women seeking treatment for menstrual irregularities at hospitals have this problem due to frequent smoking and drinking. Smoking and drinking not only cause menstrual irregularities but also harm the physical health of women.

4. Excessive dieting can also cause menstrual irregularities.

A woman's fat must account for at least 17% of her body weight for menarche to occur, and at least 22% of her body weight to maintain a normal menstrual cycle. Excessive dieting, due to insufficient energy intake by the body, leads to the consumption of a large amount of fat and protein in the body, causing obstacles in the synthesis of estrogen and significant deficiency, affecting menstruation, and even leading to oligomenorrhea or amenorrhea.

5. Electromagnetic waves can also cause menstrual irregularities in women.

With social progress, mobile phones, computers, DV cameras, vacuum cleaners, refrigerators, etc., have become an integral part of people's lives. In fact, the electromagnetic waves generated by these appliances can affect the endocrine and reproductive functions of women when acting on the human body for a long time, leading to endocrine disorders and menstrual irregularities in women.
