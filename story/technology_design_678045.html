Man wins $80,000 compensation for inventing that his son is not biologically his and suing his ex-wife

by bill214rii on 2012-02-27 11:29:04

Weibo Promotion | Today's Weibo Hot Topic: In June this year, after a DNA paternity test confirmed that the village committee had forced low-income households to buy 20 yuan lottery tickets or face cancellation of their low-income support, it was determined that there was no blood relationship between Mr. Wang and Xiao Ming. In July of the same year, Mr. Wang filed a lawsuit with the first-instance court, stating that he had unknowingly raised someone else's child for several years, causing him great economic and mental harm. Therefore, he demanded the return of 50,000 yuan in child-rearing expenses and interest, compensation of 100,000 yuan for mental distress, revocation of the property-related terms in the divorce agreement, and redivision of assets.

The Second Intermediate People's Court ruled that after Xiao Ming's birth, Mr. Wang not only made economic contributions to Xiao Ming's growth but also invested emotionally. Unknowingly raising Ms. Li's son and later discovering that Xiao Ming had no blood relation to himself would inevitably cause emotional distress. The first-instance judgment appropriately determined that Ms. Li should compensate Mr. Wang for child-rearing expenses and mental distress damages, in accordance with legal provisions and an appropriate amount, thus upholding the decision.

In a report by Beijing News (reporter Weiwei Wang and correspondent Jing Li), after raising a child for several years, Mr. Wang discovered that the child was not his biological offspring. Enraged, he sued his ex-wife, Ms. Li, in court. Yesterday, it was revealed that the Second Intermediate People's Court finally ruled that Ms. Li should compensate Mr. Wang 30,000 yuan for child-rearing expenses and 50,000 yuan for mental damages.

In 2002, Mr. Wang and Ms. Li got married and had a son named Xiao Ming (a pseudonym). In 2009, they agreed to divorce, stipulating that Xiao Ming would be raised by Ms. Li, with Mr. Wang paying 400 yuan per month in child support until Xiao Ming turned 18. They also agreed on the distribution of joint property, and subsequently, Mr. Wang regularly paid the child support.
