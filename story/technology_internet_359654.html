Mothers with vitiligo don't need to be overly fussy or worried.

by king0r8k on 2010-07-01 14:17:13

I have had vitiligo for over ten years, and I am now preparing to get pregnant. I wonder if it will affect the fetus and what I should be mindful of? Can I get pregnant while having vitiligo? I am a vitiligo patient; can I have a healthy baby? I am forty years old, and since I am within the childbearing age, I am considering pregnancy. However, I am afraid it might be passed on to my child, which makes me very worried. Additionally, being close to forty, not having a child is no longer an option. What should I do?... On authoritative websites such as the official website of Beijing Vitiligo Specialty Hospital www.jkykkfw.com, Health Network, Brandtong, and iAsk Knowledgeable People, many fellow patients are extremely concerned about the relationship between vitiligo and pregnancy.

Through telephone reservation at 010—63620988, I specially visited the chief expert of Beijing Vitiligo Specialty Hospital. The expert's insightful answers enlightened the patients greatly.

The expert pointed out that vitiligo often occurs on the face, drawing people's attention, especially in areas where science and culture are underdeveloped, where sufferers may face some criticism or even discrimination in society. Moreover, most patients with this condition are young people, so whether vitiligo patients can marry and bear children, and whether it will be passed on to the next generation, are questions that every vitiligo patient and their family urgently want to understand. We often encounter such situations where passionate young couples, once they discover that one partner has vitiligo, their love quickly fades away. This certainly involves personal reasons, but there are also many who succumb to pressure from family and friends. They believe that the offspring of vitiligo patients will definitely also suffer from vitiligo. In fact, this is a misunderstanding caused by incomplete understanding of the causes of vitiligo.

    The mechanism of vitiligo is complex. Although research suggests that vitiligo has a certain genetic background, and there are reports of identical twins both developing the condition, leading to the proposal that vitiligo might be caused by an abnormality in a dominant gene on a chromosome. From a genetic perspective, genetics is only one factor in the onset of vitiligo. Furthermore, environmental factors (including lifestyle, work and study environment, dietary habits, mental state, and air and water quality) also play important roles. Generally, the disease will only occur when both genetic and environmental factors are present. Therefore, even if genetic factors exist, as long as the influence of environmental factors is eliminated, the disease may not develop, especially since genetic factors vary from person to person. According to relevant reports, 3% to 17.2% of patients have family members with vitiligo, and it is even rarer for direct lineal relatives across generations to develop the condition. It is evident that the chances of passing vitiligo to the next generation are less than those of other certain hereditary diseases, thus vitiligo patients can reproduce.

Finally, the expert emphasized that as long as the following four points are followed, vitiligo patients absolutely can have a healthy baby.

    One, Correct Perspective: Vitiligo has a certain hereditary nature, but patients and their families need not worry that it will definitely be passed on to the next generation, as the probability is low.

    Two, Maintain a Balanced Mindset: Vitiligo itself does not directly impact reproductive ability, so vitiligo patients should not be tense during pregnancy. Relaxing can prevent possible adverse effects on fetal development.

    Three, Timing of Pregnancy: Vitiligo patients should avoid medications that could affect the fetus during pre-pregnancy or pregnancy periods and cautiously use or avoid drugs whose safety to the fetus is unclear.

     Four, Balanced Nutrition: Due to the immune and metabolic abnormalities in vitiligo patients, especially those with prolonged conditions who overly restrict their diet, it leads to relative deficiencies or insufficiencies in some vitamins, trace elements, and proteins. Since pregnancy increases the body's demand for nutrients, it is necessary to timely supplement the nutrients required for fetal development.
