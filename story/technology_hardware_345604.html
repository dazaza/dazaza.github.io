Experience of a Yacht Engineer by Thomas Sabo

by euirpqc665 on 2010-04-14 17:06:03

Depending on the vessel and whether you are a Junior or Chief Engineer, responsibilities can include: Maintaining the smooth running of mechanical and electrical systems from AV to sewage systems and everything in between! Ensuring that the vessel is in good mechanical order and is safe to take guests and crew out to sea. Polishing/cleaning, correct maintenance of engine room & all technical equipment, computer/electronics/network administration skills. Safe fuelling/fuel handling. Air conditioning. Electrical. General maintenance of interior technical equipment such as lights, vacuums, galley & laundry equipment, etc. In more senior positions this can include budgeting, accounting/bookkeeping, purchasing, scheduling, human resources/crew management, business relations (with management, accounting, owners' office, etc.) As superyachts get bigger and more complex, there are increasing numbers of dedicated computer system and networking engineers. On some smaller yachts, a single person may be both Engineer and Mate. In more senior positions, Engineer responsibilities can easily and often escalate into refit/build management, requiring many specialist skills such as planning for tankage, engine rooms, electronics/computer, security requirements, as well as Project management/Owners Representative.

### Responsibilities of Yacht Engineer:

- **Maintain** mechanical and electrical systems.

- **Ensure** the vessel is in good mechanical order and safe for guests and crew.

- **Clean/maintain** the engine room and all technical equipment.

- **Administer** computer/electronics/networks.

- **Handle** safe fuelling processes.

- **Manage** air conditioning and electrical systems.

- **Maintain** interior technical equipment like lights, vacuums, galley, and laundry equipment.

- Senior roles may involve **budgeting**, **accounting**, **purchasing**, **scheduling**, **human resources**, and **business relations**.

### Qualifications and Courses Needed for Yacht Engineer:

All Engineers going to sea will need the STCW95 Basic Crew Training (Standards of Training, Certification & Watchkeeping). This course is split into four modules, including Fire, Sea Survival, First Aid, and Personal Safety. The MCA (Maritime and Coastguard Association) has an established Engineer Path from the basic Approved Engine Course (AEC) up to the Chief Engineer Certificate of Competency Y1 Large Yacht Endorsement.

### Yacht Engineer Jobs on Superyachts:

Nowadays, yacht jobs have become one of the best and most lucrative choices among other jobs. Working on a yacht is not as easy as it looks but it is exciting and adventurous. This article will inform you about the job of yacht engineers, their responsibilities, required experience, and qualifications on superyachts, which will be helpful for potential job seekers.

### Experience of a Yacht Engineer:

You will need to be highly practical and have a good understanding of how the systems on the yacht work — most of all, be ready to get your hands dirty and work in noisy, sometimes confined spaces. Skills are built up over time and may mean you start off as a Junior Engineer. Practical experience with repairs of mechanics, such as watersports equipment, air conditioning, refrigerators, electrical wiring, diesel engines, generators, and plumbing is useful. Almost all vessels will require the Engineer to have some formal training, certification, and licensure — particularly the larger superyachts.
