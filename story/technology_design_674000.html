Myths and Truths About Bridal Gowns

There are several factors that well-meaning men and women inform brides about wedding ceremony gowns. However, not all of these options are accurate! Get the serious scoop with these myths and truths about bridal gowns.

**Myth:** If you're dropping weight, you should hold off on purchasing your wedding gown until you reach your goal weight.

**Truth:** Many brides continue to lose weight until the last week before they get married. If you wait too long, you will be limited in your options and have to pay rush delivery fees. The best plan of action is to purchase your wedding dress within the standard timeframe recommended by a bridal salon (usually at least 6 months prior to when you need it, sometimes longer). The bridal salon can inform the clothing designer that you've been losing weight and plan to send updated measurements closer to the time they start cutting your gown. This way, your dress purchase remains in the queue, and you'll achieve the best fit possible. Of course, you should also plan on getting alterations, just as every bride does.

**Myth:** Heavier brides cannot wear strapless bridal gowns.

**Truth:** Strapless gowns often look incredible on plus-sized brides! A well-built strapless gown includes a lot of structure built into the bodice, which serves to keep everything lifted and smoothed out nicely. Additionally, gowns with straps often draw attention to the upper arms, rather than away from them. Definitely give strapless bridal gowns a try, whether you're a size 2 or a size 22.

**Myth:** It's bad luck to let your fiancé see your wedding dress before the wedding.

**Truth:** It's not bad luck, and in fact, some brides even bring their fiancés with them to shop for their gowns. If you choose to do this, wait until later to shop for bridal jewelry and your veil so that your fiancé will still be surprised by your final look. While it's not bad luck to discuss your gown, do remember that it could detract from the specialness of the moment as he first lays eyes upon you walking down the aisle.

**Myth:** You should purchase your wedding accessories from the same place as your wedding dress.

**Truth:** High-end bridal designers have suggested retail price ranges for their gowns, so the prices are fairly consistent from one salon to the next, as long as they are an authorized retailer (and for your safety, you should only order a designer gown from a store that is authorized to carry the line). The same is not true of wedding accessories, and the prices can vary widely. Bridal salons tend to charge more for sets of bridal jewelry, veils, and headpieces than online merchants, so it really pays to do your research and shop around.
