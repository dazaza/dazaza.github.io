There are tricks to quickly becoming proficient in buying and selling on Paitao.

by anonymous on 2011-03-28 09:32:57

Indeed, starting a business on Paipai is a very good entrepreneurial project because of its low threshold, simple operation, small start-up capital. With the development of the Internet and the improvement of the platform, the number of online shoppers is gradually increasing, and the future development prospect is bright. Precisely for this reason, many people have chosen to take this narrow path. Just this year alone, the number of sellers on Paipai has doubled compared to last year. This无形ly adds a lot of pressure to each seller. Moreover, the goods on Paipai can be described as dazzling, almost everything. There are countless similar products from the same industry. And due to the special environment of the network, compared with traditional industries, the price of products is very transparent. Who is high and who is low can be seen at a glance with just one click. In such a big environment, under such fierce competition, many people come to Paipai with great interest, but eventually "come with high hopes and leave in disappointment". So I want to say to the sellers who stay: "You are doing a great job!" Let's all work hard together. Believe that our tomorrow will be better. The competition among many heroes, XXX, has not yet been decided, right! Of course, it is not that easy to make a difference on Paipai and to carve out a career with your own abilities. First of all, you must have good psychological quality and a certain business philosophy.

Many new sellers on Paipai come with full confidence, wanting to manage their small shops well. But after a month, the business is still cold and deserted. Where did the customers go? I think they all queued up at the diamond crown sellers' stores, hehe~ Although some new sellers also made a lot of efforts, often reading the essence posts of their predecessors, learning from their experiences, busy reading posts and replying, and many sellers worked hard to promote their stores and items. It is definitely worth affirming and encouraging to learn from the essence posts of predecessors and avoid detours. Promotion is also indispensable. But if these two aspects of effort are still cold and deserted, you may need to think carefully whether there are still things you haven't realized or haven't done.

As the saying goes: "To repel external enemies, one must first stabilize internally." If your own store is not ready and you rush to promote it, no matter how much promotion you do, although the store's popularity is good and the browsing volume has increased, ultimately, there are only more lookers and very few actual transactions. This might excite you for a while, but it seems like wasted effort. What's the reason? For example, something that once happened to me personally - I asked my friend on Paipai to check out my store and find out why my business was still cold after a month. After she looked, she frankly told me, "Honestly, standing from a consumer's point of view, I didn't see any attractive places in your store. I believe I wouldn't stay in your store for more than a minute." This is the truth. No matter how much promotion you do, if the customers entering feel this way, then what you're doing is useless. So before promoting, everyone must first take care of their own store.

One, Have Your Own Characteristics

If you are from Shaoxing, you can sell preserved vegetables; if you are from Fuzhou, you can sell Fuzhou's three treasures; if you are from Xinjiang, you can sell jade; if you are from Northeast China, you can sell chestnuts, ginseng, etc. Of course, these are just examples. Mainly, I want to tell everyone to make the products they operate distinctive, advantageous, expressive, and become a more eye-catching landscape on Paipai. This way, having something others don't is great, hehe~ If you can't achieve distinctive operations in terms of products, you can still start from the above points, work hard to do them well, and I believe it will be of great help to everyone.

Two, Products Should Be Fashionable and Trendy

Aim at the market hotspot, Guangzhou VIPshop Information Technology Co., Ltd., grasp the products that general consumers like, and purchase accordingly, allowing the products to speak for themselves, saving money and effort.

Three, Pictures Should Be More Realistic and Clear

Using the official website's advertisement pictures may be attractive and effective, but when buyers receive the goods, the actual product may differ greatly from their expectations, which inevitably leads to a significant psychological gap. I think negative reviews might easily visit your store. This is probably why many new sellers complain that even though their items are so good, they still get complaints, and the diamonds-level sellers' items aren't necessarily better than theirs. This might be related to this aspect! Clearer pictures require sellers to be more patient and spend more time studying and researching. My brother's photos aren't presentable either, and I share this thought with all the sellers, hehe.

Four, Higher Quality, Lower Price

Quality products are your best promotional tool and the most effective and fundamental guarantee to retain old customers. Imagine which buyer would remember buying a pair of shoes from your store that turned into crocodile skin after a week! And they certainly won't recommend to friends that they bought a ten-yuan pair of shoes from a certain store that were cheap and not bad, lasting a week. On one hand, people don't want others to know they're greedy for small bargains, even though your prices might already be very low, almost unprofitable. But they won't consider these things, so it's better to choose better quality items, even if the price is a bit higher. Procurement is also crucial~ Lower prices: Not making money or losing money for publicity might not be unacceptable, depending on whether you treat it as a long-term career. If you only want to make a little money or gain short-term benefits, I don't recommend it. As Jack Ma said, "Making money is actually very easy, as long as you grow big and strong. Perhaps you don't even know now where you'll make money and what projects you'll have." Everything comes slowly. As long as your store has enough popularity and loyal customers, will you worry about not making money? By then, just signing contracts with courier companies to secure some discounts on courier fees; just negotiating lower prices with manufacturers by a few yuan, I think it will be enough. You can also apply for some big brands' agency... Isn't that pretty good, hehe? Personal opinion, it all depends on how everyone views their Paipai. Do you want to make a little money first or expand your business first? It's up to you.

Five, More Promotion, Wider Mindset

Promotion is an indispensable and crucial part of online stores. Regarding methods of promotion, the posts by predecessors have explained them in great detail, so I won't elaborate here. However, I found that many new sellers seem a bit impatient. Although it's not entirely commendable, it's understandable. Who doesn't want their hard work to pay off quickly? But what I want to say is that promotion should be treated as a long-term investment, whether in terms of manpower or financial resources. Perhaps the short-term benefits are not obvious, but in the long term, they will definitely be very significant. Think about the most familiar and funny ad around us: Brain White Gold "This year, don't accept gifts, only accept Brain White Gold" When I first saw the ad, I thought, how could an ad with grammatical errors even dare to appear on TV, hehe, but surprisingly, this is the brilliance of Shi Yuzhu, the chairman of the Giant Group. Such advertising makes people remember it more easily. Due to such advertising influence, now when I go to large supermarkets to buy supplements for my parents, I always choose Brain White Gold over Brain Gold because it's more familiar, and the ad airs every day, indicating high standards, hehe...

Six, More Diligence, Better Attitude

Here we talk about service. Everyone knows that service is important, but implementing it can be said to vary from person to person. Since I used to work as a customer service specialist in a listed company, I feel that service is really important. Perhaps your product is not the best or the most cost-effective, but if you provide excellent service, I believe customers will return. When I first joined this company, it was still an unknown small company, but it had 400-500 customer service personnel, and even more staff handling problems in the back office. Eventually, within less than a year, it went public in Hong Kong. I think what attracts customers is not just the product itself, but more importantly, the subsequent service. Why is Haier famous? Because its after-sales service is famous.

On Paipai, many new sellers don't seem to pay enough attention to it or implement it well enough. In our competition with diamond crown big sellers, our biggest advantage and where we can fully leverage our own strengths is service. We have more time and energy than diamond crown sellers. In this era and social environment, I personally think that service is our core competitiveness. Because in this era of rapid information network development, no product can be monopolized by one person. Hoarding and profiteering have become outdated, equivalent to suicide. Your smart opponents may discover and replicate your products and business models, but your thoughtful service and the deep bond built with buyers cannot be replicated. As Chen Anzhi, the greatest motivational master among Chinese people, said, "Selling products is not as good as selling yourself," which means exactly this. Often, what buyers buy is not your product, but the satisfying service. Such emotional attacks are everywhere. China Mobile also sends festive greetings to its customers during holidays, and even government agencies occasionally send messages of concern, such as "As the Spring Festival approaches, please take precautions against theft and fire. Wish you a happy Spring Festival." Even these large groups and national agencies understand the importance of heartfelt service. Shouldn't we learn from them? Anyway, just keep the concept of "customer first" in mind. Whether you want to do it or not, you must believe one thing: customers are your bread and butter. In economics, there is a very famous 20/80 rule, which I believe everyone knows: 80% of your total income comes from 20% of your customers, the big clients and old customers. How to maintain these 20% of old customers and let them continuously bring you new customers depends on everyone's efforts!
