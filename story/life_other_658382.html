The Loneliness of the Sea

Spring with rain, brimming with affection.

But, there's too much sorrow. But, there's too much loneliness. Because even the sea in its wanderings cannot escape solitude.

The lush bay. I forgot to say goodbye, and now I can't see its shadow. Alone, I journey forward in the season of crossing the ocean, slowly sailing into a foreign land. But before I see the other shore, I catch your distant gaze. Is it my heart? Perhaps, it's just the weary musings of a tired mind.

Thus, the seawater soaks my trouser cuffs. My cool ankles awaken me once again. Change course, raise the sail. The horn for departure hasn't sounded; I leave quietly, leaving behind a desolate silhouette.

Occasionally, I quiet both the morning and early spring. But there are always different pasts. Because one person’s journey can be so far that it makes one's heart ache, bending this spine.

In moments of clarity, I think of you. In dreams, I also try to forget. In these desolate tales, without my solitary sail moving forward or turning back, O sea, wipe away the sadness of my homeland. Alone, I walk forward.

With such feelings, I resist losing the meaning of life. Yet, I still can't help but think of you. Occasionally, I forget you. How should I practice? How should I make the decision to let love go?

Alone, I quietly turn my back on you, that harbor I haven't seen again, step by step, walking further, so far that perhaps one day you'll wait for me there day by day. But should I change course and head towards you? No, because in the rainy sky, without the sun, I can't find my way back. Maybe it's not the right time yet, not the right place. So I'd rather wait until the sky is square and the earth is round, then I will occasionally come back to see you.

I sigh lightly and talk quietly. Always smiling faintly, tossing my hair with a sense of desolation, I am willing to spend my entire life burying you deep in my heart. Do you know, my love? Perhaps our fleeting past has little to remember, but from you, I have learned something unforgettable. We once struggled to forget which direction to take. When tears overflowed, I understood the answer you gave me.

Alright, I'll leave. Alone, from where I came, I'll retreat. Occasionally, I have moods, occasionally I get angry, occasionally I think of you, occasionally I resist. I don’t know if my occasional thoughts are inspired by the vast blue sea or the boundless sky filled with longing. Simply put, I still love you.

How can I blame myself for wanting to go back? Wait, because the sky isn't square, but the earth is definitely round. And I, can slowly return following the compass's direction.

We've all lost something in that realm where there are no more secrets. Yet we can't help but remember the decision we made: never to see each other again. But can I really do it? It rains when I'm happy, I think of you when I forget. Can such a me be acceptable?

No one accompanies me, no one stops me, thus letting me cry alone in the embrace of the sea. Lonely to the point of unbearable fear. Finally, I think of you again. Thinking of your smile, thinking of what you look like, only to find it getting blurrier the more I think. Feeling so powerless, how can I express this profound feeling?

It's just me, the lonely shadow of a single sail. Can you feel it? That's a desperate cry at night, the howling sea wind like my cries. Perhaps, it's real because I'm in the middle of the sea, unable to sever my love for you, even though you've never acknowledged it. But what does it matter? I've stepped back, giving you the most beautiful space. So, that thank you, is it mocking me?

Alright. Being alone for too long drives one a bit mad. Mad with longing, mad with thought. Afraid to sleep, fearing to wake up without you. Not having gained, there's nothing to lose. But why am I still afraid? Because the sea, vast and lonely, fills me with fear.

Clearly knowing you've already forgotten, yet I still fear losing you. Therefore, this last time, I tightly embrace you, pain making it hard to breathe. Only then do I realize that leaving you requires immense courage, like warding off the arrival of flying fish.

So, can I slowly gather this courage alone? Don't push me, don't let me see you again. Thus, I left when the sky wasn't bright, and spring hadn't arrived. I'm gone. Until now, early spring has passed, midsummer has passed, autumn has passed, and it's another winter. But this winter, there's no you. There's no you.

This way, I repeatedly think of you, recalling another self. I don't know whether I'm keeping the sea company or you're keeping mine. Either way, we've walked through a year together. My skin has tanned, my eyes have sunken, but my thoughts of you remain unchanged.

Soon I'll return, slowly raising the five-starred red flag. I smiled because in a few months, I can face you again. Face that beautiful you, isn't it?

But, if I knew upon returning that you were getting married, I'd rather circumnavigate the globe again, unwilling to indulge in such anticipation.

Resisting the world's prophecies, yet unable to resist your alluring presence. Your white simplicity, your long hair brushing against your ears, leaning into someone else's arms, I once again lack the courage to face you.

Forget it, the loneliness of the sea, a journey of one, only I truly understand. Whether fear or despair, I haven't forgotten the support you gave me. So, this time, as long as you're happy, isn't that enough? In a year, alone with just a sail, I've traveled farther than all of you. Isn't that enough?

When alone, perhaps I'll think of you, but then you belong only to our shared memories. What I can't forget is the image of the sea. That's it. Gradually distancing myself from our waltz, I'll grab a new sheet of paper, squeeze some watercolor, and paint the most beautiful thing in my heart—perhaps it's you, perhaps it's not. Anyway, I must forget your indelible smile. Gently, I lay down on the studio table and fall asleep. Very comforting, very comforting.

The wind howls, the sea smells cool, waking me from deep slumber. Rubbing my still-sleepy eyes,

Where am I? Oh, still at sea, still without you. Bitterly laughing at myself, it's alright. Dreaming of genuine heartache, maybe it's better this way.

The loneliness of the sea gives me a beautiful longing. Maybe being apart from you is possible, maybe I can never part from this kind of solitude.

Quietly hugging my legs, I sit at the boat's stern waiting for sunrise...
