Diodes Incorporated Introduces AL8807 Buck Switch-Mode LED Driver

Its switching frequency can reach up to 1MHz, with strict control of the switch rise and fall times, specifically designed to reduce the cost of MR16 LED lamps and electromagnetic interference (EMI). In order to meet high output power requirements, the driver can operate from an input voltage of 6V to 30V, suitable for LED spotlights, providing a constant current for up to eight LEDs in series with a current of up to 1A. The drive current accuracy is ±5%, which can meet the requirements of most low-voltage lighting applications, including energy-saving lamps, and can improve the brightness match between 3W LED lighting systems.

The driver achieves up to 96% efficiency. Setting the output current is simple, requiring just an external resistor. By controlling the input DC voltage or pulse width modulation (PWM) signal to the driver, users will be able to adjust the LED brightness while avoiding LED overcurrent. The AL8807 offers a PWM dimming ratio of 1000:1 and a DC signal dimming range of 16-100%, providing users with a flexible and broad lighting brightness control range.

The AL8807 LED driver uses a smaller footprint SOT25 package, and an MSOP-8EP package will also be introduced.
