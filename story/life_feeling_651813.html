Recall again in the silence

by lili1 on 2012-01-03 00:37:21

In the silence, memories come flooding back again.

Not long ago, I read Tong Hua's "Those Unforgettable Youthful Years", and I realized that everyone's youth is filled with countless moments of happiness, not just mine. Initially, I wasn't particularly interested in campus stories, but as I delved deeper into the book, I discovered its true value. Perhaps because of reading this novel, I often find myself reminiscing about my own past as the story unfolds. Maybe it's because we were around the same age, so it feels like we've been transported back to those days.

It's another evening like this, at this familiar time, and I've grown accustomed to this way of life. Sitting on the bus home, out of habit, I choose the seat by the window, occasionally resting my head against the glass, which makes me feel more relaxed. Watching the passersby, I feel how weary and oppressive life can be, yet we continue to challenge it. Looking at the children coming home from school, chattering and laughing in groups, they seem so happy. Sometimes I wonder, wouldn't it be great if we never had to grow up? Then we wouldn't have so many worries. But we've all drifted apart, and I don't know how you're doing now. I've gotten used to recalling these stories in the quiet moments. Time has made us lose touch with each other. Yet, on countless sleepless nights, I've imagined scenarios of our reunion. How would our conversation start? With a simple greeting or would we silently walk away? I can't predict any of this. Maybe I'll only know the answers when we meet again. But when and where will we meet?

Do you remember those years we spent together? Occasionally, I visit the school we once attended. Seeing the flowers blooming in the campus, how many years has it been since we last came here? Walking along this tree-lined path, gazing at the blue sky and white clouds, listening to campus songs, where are you? They say women are emotional, but behind that emotion lie stories worth remembering. For women, these are treasures worth cherishing.

It's graduation season again, a time for parting, and a time to set off with dreams. I wonder how many people will look back on their youthful years like we do after so many years.

The bus has arrived at the station, and I move forward with the crowd. Someday, somewhere, we will meet again.
