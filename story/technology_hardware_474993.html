Abstract: Inventory management, as the main factor affecting corporate capital flow, has been increasingly valued by enterprises. This article mainly takes the inventory control model reform of Guangzhou Second Light Industry Research Institute as the research object. Through analyzing the achievements obtained from its inventory control model reform, it finds out the impact of optimizing inventory management on the turnover of corporate working capital and proposes several improvement suggestions.

Keywords: Working capital; Inventory control; ABC inventory classification management method; Periodic observation inventory control model

I. Research objectives, significance, and current status

1. Research objectives and significance. The flow of capital plays an increasingly important role in enterprise operations. In today's increasingly fierce market competition, more and more enterprises pay attention to the management of working capital. Inventory management is a major influencing factor affecting the flow of corporate funds, so studying the impact of inventory management on the turnover of corporate working capital has extremely important practical significance.

"Inventory" refers to resources temporarily idle for future purposes. Generally, people set up inventories to prevent shortages. Additionally, it also serves to maintain the continuity of the production process, allocate ordering costs, and quickly meet customer order needs. In business production, although inventories exist due to various economic considerations, they are also an inevitable result because people cannot predict future demand changes, making it necessary to adopt measures to cope with external changes. It is also because people cannot make all work perfect, resulting in some unwanted redundancies and stockpiles.

The importance of working capital lies in the fact that each turnover can generate operating revenue and create profit. Therefore, the turnover of working capital is the direct creator of corporate profits. The significance of optimizing inventory management lies in ensuring smooth material flow, facilitating capital turnover, promoting more rational production management, and allowing companies to gain more profits.

Below, taking the inventory control model of Guangzhou Second Light Industry Research Institute as an example, we analyze the effects of its new inventory control model combining "ABC inventory classification management method" and "periodic observation inventory control model" by comparing the inventory levels and capital turnover before and after the reform. Several improvement suggestions are also proposed.

II. Two excellent inventory control models

1. ABC inventory classification management method.

(1) Basic concept of ABC inventory classification management method. Inventory goods come in a wide variety and large quantities. Some goods have few varieties but high market value, while others have many varieties but low market value. Due to limited resources in various aspects of the enterprise, it is not possible to equally focus on all inventory goods. Therefore, resources should be allocated where they matter most, focusing on key inventory areas. Classify and manage inventory based on the different levels of importance of inventory goods.

(2) Definition of ABC inventory classification management method. Divide inventory items into three categories based on their types and the amount of funds occupied: A class (very important), B class (moderately important), and C class (less important). Then manage and control them accordingly. Identify the critical few and the trivial many.

(3) Basis for ABC classification. The basis for ABC classification is the proportion of annual consumption amounts (the product of annual consumption quantity and average unit price) of each item in the total annual consumption amount.

A class items account for 5-15% of the variety ratio, averaging 10%, with a very small variety weight. Their annual consumption amount accounts for 60-80%, averaging 70%, consuming the majority of the annual consumption amount. They are the critical few and require focused management.

B class items account for 15-25% of the variety ratio, averaging 20%. Their annual consumption amount accounts for 15-25%, averaging 20%. Their variety ratio and amount ratio are roughly similar, requiring routine management.

C class items account for 60-80% of the variety ratio, averaging 70%, with a very large variety weight. Their annual consumption amount accounts for 5-15%, averaging 10%. Although they only consume a very small portion of the annual consumption amount, due to their large quantity, they actually consume a lot of management costs. They need to be streamlined and managed conventionally.

2. Periodic observation inventory control model. The periodic observation inventory control model, also known as periodic control or reorder interval method, is an inventory control method based on fixed inspection and reorder intervals. In this control model, orders are placed at fixed reorder intervals T. The periodic control model does not have a fixed reorder point but has a fixed reorder interval. The quantity ordered each time is not fixed and needs to be replenished to the target inventory level S according to certain rules. The target inventory S and reorder interval R are the key parameters determined at the time, with S primarily considering setting a control limit for inventory.
