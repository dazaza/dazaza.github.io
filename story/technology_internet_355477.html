Real estate tycoon met his end due to gambling

by oeerryy05 on 2010-05-18 13:42:57

He is from Fuqing, Fujian. He accumulated a wealth of hundreds of millions by the age of 30 through construction projects and real estate development; but due to his gambling addiction, he squandered all his fortune and became penniless. On the evening of August 6 last year, he was stabbed to death, leaving behind many mysteries... Recently, the mystery of the death of the billionaire in Xiamen has finally been revealed.

The Armed Billionaire Was Stabbed to Death

At 11:34 PM on August 6, 2007, on the streets of Xiamen under the cover of night,

An urgent call was made to the Huli Criminal Investigation Brigade: "A man was stabbed and died on the way to the hospital due to excessive blood loss." The police immediately arrived at the scene.

The middle-aged victim had knife wounds on his thigh, and there were no identification documents on him. However, what surprised the police was that at the scene, there was a dropped waist bag containing a handgun. After reviewing a large amount of surveillance footage, the police discovered that the waist bag had always been carried by the deceased. "At the time, there was a guess that the murderer and the victim were probably acquaintances; otherwise, how could one stand still with a gun and let someone stab their thigh," said an informed person.

The police soon identified the victim's identity: he was a real estate developer with status and position in Xiamen named Weng. This real estate developer was already a famous billionaire in the 1990s, with very complex interpersonal relationships.

Who Exactly Pulled the Trigger?

During extensive investigations afterward, the police learned that at around 6 PM on the day of the incident, a mysterious black-clad man was waiting for someone at the Luhui Building teahouse. At the same time, Weng received a phone call and was invited to discuss something at the teahouse an hour later.

More than three hours later, Weng arrived. He walked closely with the black-clad man, exchanging intimate conversation and laughter. They talked for about 20 minutes. At 10:28 PM, they went together to a sauna; at around 11 PM, they left the Luhui Building.

Surveillance footage showed that an argument broke out between them at the entrance. A second black-clad man suddenly appeared from across the street, and the two jointly attacked the victim. Soon after, one of them stabbed the victim, then both fled. "The entire process took less than a minute," a police official believed the murderers' methods were highly professional.

Two people who walked out of the building together as if they were good friends, why did they suddenly turn knives against each other?

The Two Suspects Were Arrested

According to related personnel's recollections, the man who spoke to Weng on the phone that night had an accent from another region.

Based on the suspects' escape direction after committing the crime, the police officers narrowed down the investigation scope to nearby hotels and apartments. Three days after the case occurred, the police obtained an important clue: the black-clad man was temporarily staying at a hotel-style apartment on Hubin North Road. His name on the check-out form was "Ah Peng."

The investigation started from Ah Peng. His real name was Huang, from Youyang County, Chongqing. They also discovered that Huang had fled to the Shanghai area.

The police found that Huang only played the role of a henchman, while the mastermind behind the scenes was Liu, known as "Li Ge," who had fled to Suzhou. On the morning of August 11, 2007, Liu and Huang were almost simultaneously arrested.

Failure to Repay Debts Led to Death

It was introduced that Liu had been engaged in high-interest loans in Macau for a long time. In 2006, Weng went to Macau to gamble extravagantly and borrowed 1 million yuan in high-interest loans from him in an attempt to recover his losses, but ended up losing everything. Not long after returning to Xiamen, Weng began to be chased for debt by "Li Ge" through his henchman Ah Peng. Weng managed to scrape together 400,000 yuan to repay, but the remaining 600,000 yuan remained unpaid.

As time passed and it seemed Weng had no intention of repaying the remaining balance, "Li Ge" flew to mainland China to collect the debt.

Huang introduced that on the day of the incident, Huang and Weng met at the teahouse for negotiations. Huang even brought along two brothers to intimidate Weng. Unexpectedly, Weng suddenly pulled out a handgun from his bag and waved it around. Huang and his companions were momentarily stunned. Subsequently, the two went to have a sauna, but when they came out, an argument ensued again. At this point, Huang's companion rushed out and started beating Weng. Fearing that Weng would fire the gun, Huang hastily pulled out a knife and stabbed Weng fiercely on his thigh.

The Decline of a Once-Billionaire

As the police unraveled the case details thread by thread, the decline trajectory of a former billionaire emerged.

People within the circle told reporters that Weng from Fuqing used to be incredibly successful in Xiamen: "Engaged in construction projects, building skyscrapers, he was extremely decisive, and by the 1990s, his assets were already worth hundreds of millions."

The Xiamen police also confirmed Weng's past status as a billionaire.

It was introduced that after achieving success in his career, Weng seemed to become more ostentatious, even frequenting teahouses and karaoke lounges. "His true downfall began in 2000 when he became addicted to gambling," recalled a friend of Weng's before his death.

In just three years, this once-glorious billionaire turned into a penniless pauper, not only losing all his cash in gambling but also causing his company to go bankrupt and selling off his house. With nowhere to live, he could only rely on the charity of his friends to survive.
