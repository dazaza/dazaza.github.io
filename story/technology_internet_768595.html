Facebook's biggest acquisition ever... rejected by Snapchat again

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 19:57:31

Faced with the increasingly difficult challenge of retaining young users, Facebook made a $3 billion acquisition offer for the popular mobile "view-and-delete" app Snapchat. This is Facebook's highest acquisition offer to date.

Unfortunately, the offer was rejected once again.

Previously, Mark Zuckerberg intended to acquire Snapchat at a price higher than Instagram, but was turned down by Snapchat CEO Evan Spiegel.

According to sources cited by The Wall Street Journal, while Facebook was making its acquisition offer to Snapchat, some other investors also showed interest in this mobile internet company, including Tencent. It was reported earlier that Tencent led an investment round in Snapchat, valuing the company at $4 billion.

Reports indicate that before the beginning of next year, Evan Spiegel is unlikely to consider selling the company or accepting any investments. He hopes to see continued growth in Snapchat's user base, thereby achieving a higher valuation.

Just last week at a financial forum, Tencent Chairman and CEO Pony Ma admitted that he "doesn't understand" Snapchat:

"The things that young people like on the internet now are increasingly incomprehensible to me, and this is my biggest concern. For example, there's a very popular photo-sharing platform in the U.S., but I find it boring when I use it. I'm in this industry, yet I still find it uninteresting. However, girls aged 12 to 18 absolutely love it; it’s extremely popular."

In September this year, the number of messages sent daily on Snapchat had already reached 350 million.

Why is Facebook willing to spend so much money to acquire this mobile application?

Business Insider analyzed the reasons from three aspects: Sharing photos is Facebook's core function, and Snapchat has become the largest image-sharing app on mobile platforms. Snapchat is building a unique "social graph." Facebook aims to create an all-encompassing "social graph" to better understand the patterns of social relationships, and Snapchat already represents a set of critical data that cannot be ignored. An increasing number of Facebook users are moving to Snapchat. It was previously reported that more and more teenagers are getting tired of Facebook, partly because it is not considered cool enough.
