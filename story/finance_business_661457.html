Steel futures are getting further and further away from traders.

by lkfgskkgfei4 on 2012-02-09 08:58:07

Steel futures are moving further away from traders - Steel Information - Industry News - Building Materials Network

Last week, the main contract of rebar on the Shanghai Futures Exchange gradually shifted from May to October. By March 5th, in terms of trading volume, October was about four times that of May. In terms of open interest, October and May were roughly equal, while the combined open interest of the months in between was less than 150,000 lots.

As an important pricing reference for the steel market, what does such a large jump in the main contract mean? Many industry insiders point out that this is a specific manifestation of the further enhancement of the financial attributes of futures.

Gexin Ge, a researcher at the Lange Steel Information Research Center, pointed out that although there are industry-specific reasons for the large jump in the main contract, the main reason is to avoid the impact of the spot market on futures. "Futures are different from electronic trading. The main participants in electronic trading are traders, reflecting market conditions closer to the spot market, truly reflecting the spot market situation. However, futures are different. Their main participants are financial capital. Currently, the main participants in the futures market are financial capital, and the steel industry itself is increasingly being squeezed by speculative capital. The futures market cannot truly reflect the current status of the steel market, and futures are gradually moving away from the steel spot market and traders."

Lange Steel Electronic Trading Market analyst Liu Yu told reporters: "The steel market has always had the saying of 'Red May' and 'Golden September and Silver October', but it's just the beginning of March now, and the main contract has already jumped to October, who can predict the market trend seven months later? This also shows that the current futures market has a certain degree of blindness and gambling. The reason for such a large jump is also to get rid of the pressure of the actual disk in the middle months. This is determined by the speculative nature of futures. The main contract jumps directly from May to October, which mainly reflects the further enhancement of the financial attributes of the futures market. The main purpose is still to exclude spot traders from the futures system, reducing the market risk brought by the spot market to futures."

"Jumping and shifting of the main contract is not only due to changes in fundamentals and technical aspects, but capital forces play an important role," said Guanda Chen, a futures analyst at the Lange Steel Information Research Center. He believed that since the demand in the middle months is strong, the space for financial capital to exert its influence is relatively small. Shifting to October, on one hand, can avoid the peak period of spot transactions, giving speculative capital more room to maneuver. And October is also the month of 'Golden September and Silver October', suitable for speculation. On the other hand, it can effectively avoid the interference of traders and steel market conditions on speculative capital."

Shenhua Futures analyst Zhang Yongli told reporters: "The fact that futures shift the main contract to October is an embodiment of its speculative nature. This can avoid the impact of the current massive inventory on trends, making its connection with the current market supply and demand smaller, thus not being affected by fluctuations in current spot prices. Furthermore, with less impact from market supply and demand, its connection with policies and finance becomes tighter, making it easier for speculative capital to control the market. This is the outcome that speculative capital is most willing to see. In this way, the risk of being affected by fluctuations in the spot market will be greatly reduced."

Regarding the direct jump of the rebar main contract to October, there are naturally also "normal" voices in the industry. Yongan Futures analyst Wang Yi believed: "This is a normal shift, there is no special reason, it is just for the needs of delivery. On the other hand, October is generally a month when the market trend goes up, which is beneficial for operations."

Although opinions vary on the reasons for the multi-level jump of the steel futures main contract, there is remarkable consistency among steel enterprises and traders returning to the electronic board.

"In the future, those steel enterprises and traders who do futures will still return to the electronic board because their participation in the futures market has lost its practical significance," Wang Yi advised traders: "Because the electronic board is relatively more closely linked to the spot market. According to my understanding, the majority of participants in domestic steel electronic trading are mainly traders, which more accurately reflects the current state of the market and facilitates traders in operating spot goods, forming a situation where the spot market and the electronic board influence each other and complement each other."

Since the launch of steel futures in March 2009, many industry insiders originally thought that the spot market would have a certain pricing basis. However, according to the recent trend, the hope of industry insiders may be becoming increasingly dim, and the three-level jump or even more jumps in the rebar main contract have greatly surprised many traders.

It seems that traders returning to the electronic board and spot trading is not a bad choice.
