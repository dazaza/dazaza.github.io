Futures - Domestic economic slowdown, Shanghai zinc continues the tough road to bottom building

by zhanladaim on 2012-03-06 09:53:26

Hua'an Futures - Jie Shuangqing

Prospects for March Zinc Futures

I. Market Review

The metal market in February did not have the same good fortune as January, ending the single-sided upward trend and entering a period of wide fluctuations. In early February, after the failure of domestic easing policy expectations, zinc futures fell back after an initial rise and then oscillated narrowly around 16,000 yuan. In mid-February, with unresolved issues regarding Greece's second bailout and the failure of domestic easing expectations, zinc futures fell from their consolidation platform and gave back nearly half of previous gains. In late February, with progress on the Greek issue and a reduction in reserve requirements to ease liquidity tensions, market risk appetite improved somewhat, allowing zinc futures to stabilize and consolidate.

Overall, zinc prices in both Shanghai and London maintained wide fluctuations in February. However, the performance of metals in the Shanghai market was significantly weaker than in London, mainly due to continued economic slowdown in China with limited improvement in liquidity, leading to cautious trading in the domestic market.

In the spot market, metal prices were high in early February. Upstream miners, smelters, and traders had large profits and were relatively active in selling. However, after the holiday, most downstream companies were still on break, resulting in general trading conditions. In mid-to-late February, as metal prices gradually declined and downstream companies returned to the market, trading improved slightly compared to earlier periods. Overall, there was no significant restocking phenomenon in the post-holiday spot market as previously expected, with trading conditions remaining less optimistic.

II. Macroeconomic Analysis

1. Domestic Economy Still Slowing Down

The slowdown in European and American demand continues to impact China's real economy, potentially accumulating overall macroeconomic risks. Customs data shows that China's imports and exports both fell into negative growth in January, reaching a near two-year low. The decline in external demand due to the European debt crisis is prominent, with the foreign trade situation remaining severe. Continuous three-month negative growth in foreign exchange reserves can indirectly reflect the unoptimistic state of domestic foreign trade. Without significant improvement in domestic demand and with severe external demand slowdown, the weakness in domestic manufacturing continues. The preliminary HSBC PMI value rose to 49.7 in February, continuing to increase for the third month, but still below the dividing line between expansion and contraction, indicating that China's manufacturing has not yet left the slowing track. The increase in the PMI preliminary value is mainly contributed by the output index, possibly due to inventory replenishment after the Spring Festival holiday. Additionally, the new export orders index dropped sharply from 50.4 last month to 47.4, reaching the lowest level in eight months, consistent with the unexpected "double decline" in China's January imports and exports, showing a still severe foreign trade situation. Fixed asset investment represented by real estate and infrastructure investment is unlikely to warm up in the short term. Real estate regulation will continue in the short term without relaxation; large-scale infrastructure, high-speed rail, urban construction, etc., are mostly on hold due to tight funds and national regulation, with investment enthusiasm much lower than before. In summary, under the continued stable monetary policy, the persistence of real estate regulation and the worsening export situation will inevitably continue to drag down the economy. The slowing economic situation in the next one or two months is estimated to remain unchanged.

2. US Economic Data Continues to Improve, Extending Rebound

Recent economic data has continued the improving trend from the fourth quarter of last year, indicating that the US economy is unlikely to fall back into recession in the short term and is expected to return to normal and continue its rebound. First, looking at the US labor market, significantly improved employment data shows that the job market is improving, and the continuous improvement in the job market further indicates potential improvements in the US manufacturing and real estate markets. Only with economic improvement can businesses hire more people for production activities. However, from a historical perspective, the US unemployment rate is still at a high level, and only continuous improvement can provide strong evidence for the recovery of the US economy. In January 2012, non-farm employment increased by 243,000 people, the highest since last April. The unemployment rate fell by 0.2% to 8.3%, the lowest since February 2009. Meanwhile, in the week ended February 18, initial jobless claims were 351,000, the lowest since March 2008, and the four-week moving average dropped to the lowest level in four years.

Furthermore, data from the Federal Reserve shows that the housing market is slowly stabilizing. New home starts rose 1.5% month-over-month in January, reaching 699,000 units annually; new home sales totaled 321,000 units annually; building permits reached 676,000 units; NAR seasonally adjusted existing home sales reached 4.57 million units annually, rising 4.3% month-over-month, and the seasonally adjusted existing home sales index hit a two-year high. The latest data from the National Association of Home Builders shows that US home builder confidence rose to the highest level in over four years in February, reaching 29. Comparing this data with previous figures, all aspects of the housing market have improved. The collective improvement in housing market data may indicate that it is likely to bottom out and stabilize soon. This could be described as giving the US economy a strong boost. If it continues to improve, it will provide more momentum for the US economic rebound. However, from a historical data perspective, the housing market is still relatively in a difficult situation, and potential threats to medium- and long-term economic growth still exist.

In summary, the sharp rise in consumer confidence index in February, the rebound in manufacturing index, and the unexpected growth in industrial new orders and durable goods orders... these strong economic data all strongly support the rebound of the US economy. The recovery in automobile manufacturing and the stabilization of the housing market will ensure the demand for industrial metals. It is expected that economic data will continue to improve in the future, providing some support for metal prices.

3. Eurozone Debt Issues Continue, Economic Outlook Remains Unpromising

After nearly a month of deliberation, the second round of Greek bailout finally came to fruition. On February 20, the "Troika" signed the second bailout agreement with Greece. A few days later, Greece issued a bond swap proposal to private sector creditors, initiating a debt restructuring plan worth 100 billion euros, which further strengthened market confidence in Greece's debt restructuring plan and temporarily avoided the risk of default. However, the temporary deferral of Greece's default risk did not eliminate market concerns about the eurozone debt crisis. The bond haircut for Greece's private creditors might impact the eurozone banking sector, and investors are skeptical whether the eurozone can come up with more funds when the debt problem spreads to Italy and Spain... In short, the Greek issue has been temporarily resolved, but concerns over the eurozone debt will continue to worry investors in the long term.

The European Central Bank's (ECB) second Long-Term Refinancing Operation (LTRO) at the end of February is expected to inject more liquidity into the market, enabling European banks to maintain higher capital adequacy ratios to avoid a banking crisis, and hoping that they purchase high-yield sovereign bonds through risk-free arbitrage activities to mitigate the debt impact on Greece, Italy, Spain, and other countries when their debts mature. However, whether this quasi-quantitative easing will be effective remains to be seen. Based on current trends, the EU summit in March is unlikely to reach constructive consensus on expanding the size of the rescue fund, and the G20 will similarly not increase funding for the IMF in the foreseeable March. The firewall for the eurozone will not strengthen in the short term. Given the limited available funds of the EFSF and the ineffectiveness of the ESF in the short term, whether the second round of LTRO can play an effective role remains uncertain. With large amounts of debt maturing for major debtor countries in the region, investors may become concerned again.

During the brief reprieve from eurozone debt worries, what concerns investors more is the weakness of the eurozone economy. The PMI indices of the eurozone and Germany have fallen again. Combined with the recent weak economic indicators of the eurozone, the recessionary trend of the eurozone economy has not changed. Subsequently, the EU's mid-term economic report predicts that the GDP of the 17 eurozone countries will contract by 0.3% this year, falling into mild recession, while the economies of the 27 EU member states will stagnate. Although the ECB may tolerate higher inflation and cut interest rates again to support economic growth, the eurozone, ravaged by the debt crisis, has not emerged from the shadow of the economic downturn in the fourth quarter of last year, and the economic outlook for the first quarter of this year is not optimistic.

In summary, eurozone debt issues will continue to trouble the market in March, and the continued weakness of the eurozone economy will further prevent the release of investor risk sentiment, directly hindering the rise in metal prices.

III. Zinc Fundamental Supply and Demand Analysis

1. Zinc Processing Fees Decline in 2012, Global Zinc Concentrate Supply Relatively Shortage

The International Zinc Association (IZA) meeting recently announced that zinc concentrate processing fees for 2012 would be around $191 per ton, with a benchmark price of $2000 per ton, up 5% and down 2%. Compared to last year, international zinc concentrate processing fees have slightly declined, indicating a relatively insufficient supply of global zinc concentrates. According to predictions by the International Lead and Zinc Study Group, zinc mine production will increase by 4.8% to 13.42 million tons in 2012, while zinc smelting output will increase by 2.4% to 13.49 million tons. There will still be a gap between mines and smelting, but the gap will be significantly reduced compared to 2010-2011.

From the domestic processing fee perspective, given the relative shortfall in domestic zinc ore production versus zinc smelting demand, zinc processing fees have significantly declined since 2011. However, with the expansion of domestic mining capacity, further integration of mines, and the expansion of smelting capacity, domestic zinc concentrate production remained steadily increasing last year. Coupled with large imports of zinc ores, the overall shortfall is not significant.

In summary, the decline in global zinc processing fees indicates a relative shortage of zinc concentrates, but compared to the severely oversupplied refined zinc market globally, it offers insufficient support for zinc prices.

2. Global Refined Zinc Surplus in 2011, Inventories Recently Reached Historical Peaks Again

International Supply and Demand: The latest report from the International Lead and Zinc Study Group (ILZSG) shows that global refined zinc production exceeded demand by 353,000 tons in 2011, higher than the surplus of 258,000 tons in 2010. Global refined zinc production increased by 1.8% to 13.062 million tons in 2011, while refined zinc consumption increased by 1.1% to 12.709 million tons during the same period. China's refined zinc production increased by 1.1% to 5.222 million tons in 2011, while China's refined zinc demand increased by 2.1% to 5.468 million tons compared to 2010. Refined zinc demand in the EU increased slightly to 2.211 million tons in 2011, higher than 2.203 million tons in 2010. Refined zinc production in the EU27 also increased slightly by 1.1% to 2.014 million tons in 2011, compared to 1.992 million tons in 2010.

Inventory Aspects: By February 24, London inventories increased by 23,525 tons to 867,825 tons compared to the end of the previous month. Since December last year, LME inventories have been continuously increasing, with the main inventory increases coming from Asia, possibly indicating poor circulation in the local spot market. Shanghai inventories increased by 9,015 tons to 378,713 tons compared to February 3. The weekly inventory of zinc on the Shanghai Futures Exchange has been on an upward trend for three consecutive months, indirectly reflecting the poor situation in the domestic spot market. The total inventory of the two markets has reached a high point of 1.2 million tons, approaching the historical peak in July 2011.

From the above data, it can be seen that the global refined zinc market still maintains a significantly oversupplied pattern, with little improvement compared to the previous period. The long-term increase in inventories in both London and Shanghai indicates that, with the recent rise in zinc prices, the downstream consumption in the spot market has not been smooth, causing continuous inflow of goods into warehouses. Insufficient demand and the oversupply pattern are expected to improve only marginally in March, combined with high inventories, which will directly suppress the strengthening of zinc prices.

3. Downstream Terminal Consumption Situation

Real Estate Market: In January this year, the property market in many places recorded the coldest transactions in the past three years. A recent monitoring report stated that in January, among the 40 key cities including first-tier cities like Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen and major second-tier cities, more than 80% of the cities saw a month-on-month decline in transactions, with 13 cities experiencing a decline of over 50%. Meanwhile, according to data released by the National Bureau of Statistics recently on "Changes in Residential Sales Prices in 70 Large and Medium-sized Cities in January 2012," none of the 70 large and medium-sized cities saw a price increase, with prices halting across the board. Although recently top-level officials have repeatedly emphasized supporting rigid demand and increasing market expectations for fine-tuning of the property market, the overall direction of property regulation is unlikely to change in the short term. The continued decline in property volume and prices will undoubtedly increase inventory pressure and financial pressure on real estate companies. In the short term, investment in the real estate market remains pessimistic.

Automobile Market: According to the auto sales data for January recently published by the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers, auto production and sales in January completed 1.2994 million units and 1.3898 million units respectively, decreasing by 23.2% and 17.75% month-on-month, and by 27.47% and 26.39% year-on-year respectively. Affected by multiple factors such as national macro-control, the withdrawal of incentive policies, high base numbers from the previous year, and purchase restrictions in cities like Beijing, auto production and sales in 2011 grew only slightly compared to 2010. Currently, in 2012, the industry has yet to escape the困境 of last year. The tight liquidity domestically and the continued poor macroeconomic environment mean that the domestic automotive industry situation will remain unfavorable in the short term without consumption stimulus policies.

Appliance, Transportation and Other Industries: With the sluggishness in real estate sales and the expiration of domestic preferential policies such as rural appliance subsidies, the growth rate of the appliance market showed no significant improvement in January of the new year. Air conditioner exports in the fourth quarter of last year decreased by approximately 37.1% compared to the same period in previous years; washing machine exports in November and December of last year saw year-on-year growth rates drop below 10%; refrigerator and TV exports also began to decline in the fourth quarter. With weak domestic demand and poor external demand, the growth rate of the appliance market in the first quarter may continue to decline, with unclear prospects. Although there has been speculation about the introduction of a new round of appliance preferential policies, it is unlikely to materialize in the short term. Even if it does, it will not have a significant stimulating effect on the appliance industry in the short term. After a significant slowdown in growth in the transportation industry last year, current national policies suggest that investment growth may recover to ensure stable economic growth, but its share in zinc consumption is small, so its pulling effect is limited.

Fundamentally, whether in the automotive industry or real estate, although with the resumption of normal operations by downstream enterprises in March, the demand for raw materials has improved somewhat compared to the previous period, the overall weak consumption situation is expected to remain difficult to significantly improve in March. There is even a possibility of repeating the "weak peak season, even weaker off-season" pattern seen last year, which is undoubtedly the most unfavorable factor for the strength of metals.

IV. Conclusion and Trading Strategy

In March, downstream enterprises in China will resume normal operations. Due to the lack of significant restocking behavior before the holiday, raw material demand is expected to further increase, and the downstream consumption market should improve somewhat compared to the previous period if everything goes as expected. Combined with the recovery of the U.S. automobile manufacturing and the revival of the housing market, these two factors may basically maintain the demand for zinc, limiting the downside space for zinc prices. However, the domestic economy is still in a slowdown channel, struggling to build a solid bottom, compounded by uncertainties surrounding the Eurozone debt crisis and even geopolitical crises, all of which will exert significant pressure on the upward movement of zinc prices, making it unlikely for zinc prices to rise unilaterally. In summary, SHFE zinc may maintain a wide range of fluctuations in March, continuing its arduous bottom-building process.

In March, investors should primarily adopt a strategy of expecting volatility with a slight upward bias, operating within a range of 15,500-17,000 yuan. Aggressive investors may consider medium- to long-term buying opportunities when zinc prices retreat to around 15,500 yuan.
