Smart Grid Has Broad Development Prospects, International Market May See Disputes

by ditan360 on 2010-07-05 11:58:21

Low Carbon Network Exclusive - Recently, the State Grid Corporation officially released the smart grid technology standards system and the R&D plan for key smart grid equipment, listing the development of smart grids as one of the main future directions. Simultaneously, for the first time in China, a systematic proposal was put forward regarding the smart grid technology standards system, which includes 8 professional branches, 26 technical fields, and 92 standard series. It is understood that this plan will serve as a guiding document and technical guideline for compiling enterprise standards for the smart grid within the State Grid Corporation. In the future, the State Grid will accelerate the compilation of smart grid enterprise standards based on this guidance, deploying a grand blueprint for the future development of the smart grid.

An industry expert who has long been involved in the development of smart grids estimated that the smart grid involves many related industries, including power equipment, information and communication, new energy sources, and more. Among these, the business opportunities in just the power grid technology sector alone could exceed trillions of yuan. Faced with such enormous business opportunities, industry giants are all eyeing the market closely. How to better share the smart grid 'cake' while vigorously promoting the development of the smart grid has become a focal point of attention for related industries.

Strong-Strong Cooperation, Joint Development

Relevant personnel from the State Grid Corporation recently stated that they will not monopolize the smart grid but instead will actively seek relevant cooperation, using strong-strong alliances and joint development to promote the overall progress of the smart grid.

On June 30th, the State Grid Corporation signed a strategic cooperation agreement with the Chinese Academy of Sciences in Beijing. According to the agreement, in the future, both parties will conduct in-depth cooperation in eight key areas: electric vehicles, Internet of Things and sensors, high-temperature superconductors, power fiber-to-the-home, large-capacity energy storage systems, semiconductor lighting, etc., targeting the construction of the robust smart grid that the State Grid Corporation is fully advancing. The signing of this agreement laid a technical foundation for the diversified development of the smart grid.

On July 2nd, the State Grid Corporation signed a strategic cooperation framework agreement with China Telecom Group Corporation. In the future, both parties will carry out extensive cooperation in infrastructure, market, resources, technology, management, and other aspects within the fields of power grids and communications. Both parties expressed their commitment to setting an example for cooperation among central enterprises, striving to make this cooperation a benchmark for the "integration of informatization and industrialization."

As the formulator of relevant smart grid standards, the State Grid Corporation has already occupied a position of discourse power. Its leading scale in the smart grid is beginning to emerge. However, the smart grid involves numerous industries, and monopolistic development would undoubtedly become a shackle. Only through strong-strong alliances can the overall development of the industry be promoted.

Gradually Expanding Investment Proportion, Promoting Systematic Development of Smart Grids

In light of the broad prospects for development versus the current sluggish development situation, investments by the state and enterprises in the smart grid's future development have become increasingly clear.

It is learned that the proportion of investment in intelligentization in our country’s power grid is relatively low. However, with the advancement of the smart grid, the proportion of intelligentization investment in the power grid investment will significantly increase. In the first phase (2009-2010), the total power grid investment was 551 billion yuan, with an intelligentization investment of 34.1 billion yuan, averaging an annual intelligentization investment of 17 billion yuan, accounting for 6.2% of the total power grid investment; in the second phase, the total power grid investment is expected to be 1.5 trillion yuan, with an intelligentization investment of 175 billion yuan, averaging an annual power grid investment of 35 billion yuan, accounting for 11.7% of the total investment; in the third phase, the total power grid investment will be 1.4 trillion yuan, with an intelligentization investment of 175 billion yuan, averaging an annual intelligentization investment of 35 billion yuan, accounting for 12.5% of the total investment.

For the State Grid Corporation, the total investment in grid equipment this year is around 250 billion yuan. Since the smart grid work is still in the pilot stage, the main task this year is the construction of 75 electric vehicle charging stations, 6,200 charging piles, and other smart grid demonstration projects, with this aspect's investment proportion being less than 10%. The next step will gradually expand the investment.

Gradual development, expanding the investment proportion, and promoting the systematic development of smart grids may be an effective way to currently promote the development of China's smart grid, said relevant experts.

International Disputes Emerge, Market May Become Turbulent

There is no doubt that smart grids have attracted widespread international attention. Not only China, but also countries like the United States, Japan, South Korea, and others have listed the development of smart grids as a focus for their new economic growth.

Not long after the State Grid Corporation announced its plans for the vigorous development of smart grids, relevant U.S. government officials revealed that the United States plans to establish an international organization concerning smart grids to promote the formulation of relevant technical standards and the research and development of new technologies. This international organization will be named the "International Smart Grid Action Network (ISGAN)." It is understood that the U.S. aims to advance the standardization of smart grid technology specifications to expand the market scale, thereby gaining control over the dominant position in the new energy market. South Korea, which focuses on nuclear energy to vigorously cultivate the clean energy industry, and Italy, which has successfully popularized smart meters, announced that they will soon become core members of this organization. They will jointly cooperate with the International Energy Agency (IEA) and the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) to formulate specific action plans.

Besides the United States, the Japanese government also proposed in its new economic growth strategy to expand environmental businesses, including overseas markets, and positioned smart grids as a top priority.

In the face of the enormous profits offered by smart grids, the international market may become turbulent.
