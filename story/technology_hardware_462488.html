There are hundreds of detailed implementation methods for collecting video PR.

by nairzupe on 2011-07-14 11:33:37

The release of the annual meeting video by Ling Si and Orient Rende is a landmark move that provides an excellent example for developing public relations services through the use of collected video media. Public relations plays a crucial role in maintaining relationships between relevant stakeholders, with customer experience being a key component.

Public relations service content aimed at providing tangible experiences includes test drives for automotive PR, product launch events for IT products, press conferences, and journalist receptions. These projects are traditionally conducted because traditional media channels such as newspapers, television, magazines, and radio can only publish information after review by professionals like journalists and editors. As a result, many companies invite media personnel to experience their products or services to gain exposure, which in turn leads target consumer groups to purchase products or services.

Television media has been an important channel for public relations due to its immediacy, broad reach, and ability to convey information without cultural barriers through visual and audio elements, giving audiences a sense of tangibility.

However, using television media for public relations comes with two major drawbacks:

1. High costs associated with information placement on TV, making advertisements the mainstream method.

2. Lack of interactivity in one-way communication, along with strict and complex program reviews, making it unsuitable for interactive public relations projects. Consequently, television media is rarely used in corporate public relations efforts, while TV ads remain the primary method of information dissemination due to their inherent characteristics aligning with TV media features.

With the rise of internet media and the utilization of its information creation and self-retrieval functions, online videos have broken these limitations. This has provided a new platform for public relations services. As the internet continues to grow alongside video websites, online video PR is increasingly valued by many brand enterprises, becoming a powerful tool in digital public relations.

Currently, there are several methods of online video PR:

1. Companies invest money while netizens contribute effort, fostering nationwide interaction and mutual benefits through a strategy of harnessing citizen creativity.

Companies upload advertisements, brand elements, and new product information onto video platforms to engage netizens, such as soliciting short video ads or evaluations for new products. This not only offers netizens earning opportunities but also serves as excellent promotion. Chinese netizens' creativity is boundless, and they actively produce and share short clips rather than passively receiving information. Besides browsing and uploading, netizens can comment on videos and rate them, making the activation of their creativity a good PR choice.

2. When happy memories contain brand information, happiness becomes the carrier, and the brand acts as the "virus."

Companies create valuable and engaging videos according to their needs and then seek target consumers or opinion leaders to help spread them. Netizens seeing these classic, funny, and relaxing videos willingly share them, allowing the video to carry the company's message and spread virally across the internet.

3. Instead of hyping events, create stories with loyal customers.

Public relations events have always been a focus of offline PR activities, with many domestic brands achieving success through such events. Planning influential PR events, crafting interesting stories, and filming these events into videos is an excellent approach. Videos with event content are more likely to be shared by netizens. Incorporating PR event thinking into online video PR will uncover new PR values. Collaborating with consumers to create PR events and uploading them in video form to the internet will enhance credibility and dissemination effects.

4. Integration as an amplifier for online video PR dissemination.

Due to differences in user habits and internet contact behaviors, single video dissemination is often ineffective. Therefore, videos should first be featured in a dedicated section on the company’s website to attract target customer attention. Additionally, collaborations with major portals and video sites should enhance video influence. For internet users, offline activities and participation are also important. Thus, integrating online video marketing with offline activities and media for brand promotion will be more effective.

There are hundreds of specific ways to implement online video PR, but all must adhere to the principles of public relations: education, guidance, fairness, transparency, honesty, integrity, professionalism, and independence. Actions such as bias, ulterior motives, excessive embellishment, over-youthfulness, and forgetting the brand can also cause online video PR to lose credibility, leading to its exit from the public relations service domain.
