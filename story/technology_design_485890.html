12 Tips for a Prosperous 2008

Feng Shui is a profound study in home design and layout. Many people enjoy Feng Shui, but often lack the time and energy to understand and learn it. Here, we introduce 12 currently popular Feng Shui methods as a reference for your home life.

Some people don't have the time or energy to study Feng Shui, but they are very interested in it. Studying Feng Shui doesn't necessarily mean starting with ancient diagrams like the Ho Tu and Lo Shu, celestial stems, and earthly branches. People who talk about door-to-door alignments without understanding these concepts haven’t truly studied Feng Shui—they’ve just read some books, probably modern ones. Feng Shui emphasizes the integration of external environment, internal layout, and personal destiny. Our team at Zhongchuan Yilin can only discuss based on diagrams since we cannot see the entire external environment nor combine it with personal destiny due to the time-consuming nature of analyzing one's destiny (which takes over an hour). Online analysis can only provide general insights. Below, we will introduce 12 popular Feng Shui methods for your reference.

Tip 1: Stand at your front door and look outward. Check if there are any large buildings or obstructions directly facing you that could create what Feng Shui refers to as "hidden arrow sha" (negative energy). A neighbor’s satellite dish, streetlight poles, church spires, or a long road leading straight to your front door can all form this "hidden arrow sha." If anything aligns with your front door that creates this effect, hang a Bagua mirror above the door with the mirror facing outward to deflect the harmful influence.

Tip 2: Examine your back door and each main window using the same method.

Tip 3: Stand at your front door again and use a compass to determine the direction your house faces. You can use a simple Boy Scout compass or an intricate Chinese Luo Pan (compass), but ensure the needle points north. (If you don't have a compass, you might use a street map to find the direction your house faces.) Record this result to later compare it with your personal best direction.

Tip 4: Inspect your home entrance to ensure nothing blocks the flow of Qi entering through the front door. Ensure the Qi can quickly gather inside. The area inside and outside the front door should be open and spacious, so other obstacles should be removed immediately.

Tip 5: Sketch a rough floor plan of your house. Place the Bagua chart over your floor plan, aligning it correctly with the compass directions. This way, the blue section representing career will fall in the North, while the red section representing reputation will fall in the South.

Tip 6: The eight sectors of the Bagua represent the "Eight Great Human Desires"—family, wealth, reputation, marriage, children, benefactors, work, and knowledge. Determine which aspect you want to improve or enhance among these eight. For example, if you wish to increase your wealth, its corresponding direction is Southeast. Check your Bagua chart and find that the Southeast corresponds to the Wood element. To stimulate this sector, place items related to the Wood element (such as potted plants) in this direction. (You can also enhance this with the Water element, as Water nourishes Wood.) Refer to the Bagua chart to find the meaning of each direction. However, we advise not trying to enhance too many sectors at once, as it might overwhelm you with too many opportunities.

Tip 7: If your home is not square or rectangular, check if your floor plan lacks certain corners. Missing corners mean certain Bagua sectors do not correspond to rooms. For instance, if your house is L-shaped, this situation occurs. If you feel the missing sector is important, recreate it using mirrors.

Tip 8: Inspect your kitchen. When standing in the food preparation area, ensure you are not facing away from the door. If so, rearrange your kitchen so you can see the door while cooking. Ensure water elements in your kitchen do not conflict with fire elements. In other words, make sure your stove is not directly next to or facing your sink or refrigerator. If so, relocate either the water (sink, fridge) or fire (stove) element. Finally, check if the stove direction aligns with your personal best direction.

Tip 9: Inspect your living room or dining room. Try to position large mirrors in these rooms so they reflect food on the table, symbolizing "doubling your wealth."

Tip 10: Check your bedroom. Ensure there is nothing hanging above your bed that could cause pressure, such as built-in wardrobes or heavy paintings. When sitting on the bed, ensure you do not appear in any mirror. If so, move the mirror, cover it, or place it far from the bed.

Tip 11: Check the location of your bathroom and toilet. These rooms are crucial because they are where water leaves your home. Confirm if these rooms are in important sectors of your Bagua. For example, if the toilet is in the Southwest, it may affect your relationships. This happens because these rooms drain water out of the house, taking good Qi and energy with it. For instance, if the toilet or bathroom is in the western half of your home, it may deplete your children's luck. If your children have had bad years, this might be a key reason. However, if you don't have children, it might not matter. If these "wet areas" are in important sectors, try keeping the bathroom door closed, the toilet lid down, or covering the door with a full-length mirror to "make the room disappear."

Tip 12: If you have a garden, inspect it and apply the Bagua and compass to its layout. Ensure no paths lead straight to your front door, as this resembles an arrow shooting directly at your home. As you become more familiar with Feng Shui, consider building a pond or water feature in the correct position.

Now that you know the orientation of your home and garden, carefully determine your personal best direction, which benefits you personally and affects the proper placement of furniture indoors.
