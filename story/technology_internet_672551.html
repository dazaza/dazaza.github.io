Someone might complain that QQ groups found on [http://zhaoqq.cn] are now full of advertisements. Have you ever thought about why people dislike ads so much? Is there a way to make people like our advertisements?

The original intention of netizens joining QQ groups is to chat with like-minded individuals. When they're chatting enthusiastically, suddenly an ad pops up saying "[come here for clothing purchases]." Would you bother to look at it? Or in a home decoration group, an ad for "English training" appears. Would you check it out? Therefore, please remember the first secret of posting in QQ groups: when joining a QQ group, find places where potential target customers gather. Don't waste everyone's time in unrelated groups and avoid directly sending out advertisements.

Chinese people like to join in lively situations; the more people there are, the more they enjoy it. In a market setting, the stalls that have no inquiries tend to attract even fewer inquiries. So, when joining QQ groups, bring along a few of your acquaintances. If you all properly discuss a certain company, product, or event within the group, it will naturally attract others to participate in the QQ group search [http://zhaoqq.cn]. Note, don't make it too obvious. Even if someone acts as a shill, they can criticize the main subject, creating some drama—people like that more.

Sales personnel know that to make clients accept your product, you must give them reasons to like you. Otherwise, clients have no reason to like you. When doing advertisements, you need to grasp the pulse of the customer.

Timing is also crucial for posting ads. If everyone is chatting enthusiastically, your advertisement won't be noticed. Similarly, if you post an ad just because someone else did, it won't get attention either [http://zhaoqq.cn]. It's recommended to post ads around 11:30 AM to 12 PM, and 4:30 PM to 5 PM. During these two periods, many people are tired from work and are looking for entertainment. If your ad is good, it will attract readers. Also, check how many people are online in the group when you post your ad. Try to post when there are more people online. Additionally, if you think your ad skills aren't great, see if the group admin is present. Post the ad when the admin isn't around.

I often receive private messages with small ads from some groups. The advantage of these private ads is that if there's interest, the other party can directly reply via QQ. However, if you first engage in casual conversation with the netizens in the group and then guide them, the effect would be better. Moreover, besides sending messages in the group or through private chats, you can also send group emails. After the email is sent, QQ Mail will notify users of new mail, and this notification will appear in the lower right corner of the screen. The content of this notification is key, including the title of the email and a brief description. It needs to be attractive enough to entice readers to click on the group email prompt to view.

Of course, we can create our own QQ groups, which eliminates the risk of being kicked out. However, even if you're the group admin, you still can't control whether users will click to view or pay attention to your advertisements. Therefore, mastering some techniques is still necessary.
