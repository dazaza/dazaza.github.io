Chelsea's 6-0 Triumph Over Three Major Powerhouses: A Strong Case for Championship Reservation

In the first round of the Premier League, Chelsea has utterly dominated three major powerhouses. On November 30th, as the main referee Mr. Marriner blew his whistle, Chelsea easily defeated Arsenal 3-0 away. After 14 rounds in the Premier League, Chelsea had previously won 1-0 against Manchester United and 2-0 against Liverpool. In the matches among the top four teams, Chelsea achieved a remarkable 6-0 victory over the three major powerhouses. What makes Chelsea so terrifying? And why do the other three Premier League powerhouses seem so powerless against Chelsea? The author believes there are three main reasons behind Chelsea's dominance this season.

1. Diversified Attack Points Leave the Three Powerhouses Defenseless

Drogba, Rooney, Torres, and Van Persie are the core strikers who support Chelsea, Manchester United, Liverpool, and Arsenal respectively. Their goals almost account for one-fourth of their respective team’s total goals. However, Chelsea doesn't solely rely on Drogba's scoring ability, especially when facing the other three powerhouses. The魔兽 (Drogba) only scored twice in the match against Arsenal, while the remaining four goals were scored by another forward, midfielders, defenders, and even the opponent. The variety of attacking points leaves opponents defenseless.

Even with six goals, each goal was scored differently. Four goals came from left and right wing breaks passing and shooting, one goal resulted from a central rush causing a foul leading to a direct free-kick goal, and another was an indirect free-kick due to a foul on the left flank. The diversity and complexity of these scoring methods make it difficult for the three powerhouses to defend effectively. This rich offensive strategy undoubtedly owes much to the tactical genius of Carlo Ancelotti's mesmerizing command and deployment.

2. Stable Defensive Lineup Renders the Three Powerhouses Powerless

This season, Chelsea's main defensive lineup has been very stable. The defensive lineup consisting of Cech, Ivanovic, Carvalho, Terry, and Ashley Cole hasn't changed much. Among them, except for Ivanovic who became a regular player this season, the other four have been playing together for more than three years, which shows their默契 (perfect coordination). Additionally, Ancelotti's diamond-shaped positioning set this season makes the midfield protection of the defense even more complete.

Of course, injuries are unavoidable, but fortunately, in the battles against the three powerhouses, except for the game against Liverpool where Hilario replaced Cech, the other two games featured a full defensive lineup. Breaking through this defense line proved extremely challenging for the opponents. Manchester United tried to retreat and increase the number of midfield players to exert pressure, but it was ineffective, with only two accurate shots throughout the game. Liverpool, despite having Gerrard and Torres on the field, managed only three accurate shots, lacking the two key shooters Van Persie and Bendtner, they hardly challenged Chelsea's defense with only two accurate shots and the lowest number of total shots among the three powerhouses, at just 12. In comparison, Chelsea had a total of 18 accurate shots and an astonishing 40 total shots.

Such data sufficiently proves the chilling stability of the Blues' defense. If one can't even threaten Chelsea's goalposts, how can one talk about scoring or winning?

3. Chelsea's Deeper Squad Outweighs the Three Powerhouses' Financial Constraints

The matchups between Chelsea and the Premier League's three major powerhouses coincidentally occurred after three Champions League matches. The arrangement of three high-intensity matches within eight days sufficiently tested the depth of the rosters among the four major clubs.

During the game week against Liverpool, Chelsea rotated seven players across three matches; during the game week against Manchester United, Chelsea rotated eleven players; during the Arsenal week, nine players were rotated. In comparison, apart from the experienced Ferguson who meticulously rotated an astonishing fourteen players surpassing Chelsea, Arsenal's seven rotations and Liverpool's meager four rotations were both less than Chelsea's. Physical exhaustion already tilted the scales in Chelsea's favor before the opening whistle.

Certainly, more rotations naturally come with the cost of reduced team strength. But in this aspect, Chelsea undoubtedly has the deepest bench among the top four. Especially after October, with the return of strong reinforcements such as Joe Cole, Zhirkov, Alex, and Ferreira, Ancelotti's tactical arrangements became even more flexible. Therefore, in the six games across the three cycles, Chelsea achieved a record of four wins, one draw, and one loss. Manchester United struggled to deal with Chelsea, barely drawing against CSKA Moscow in the Champions League; whereas the clearly thinner squad of Arsenal lost to Sunderland in the league, and Liverpool, losing to Fiorentina in a crucial Champions League group stage match, ultimately led to an embarrassing elimination.

The inadequacy of rotation also stems from the failed policy of the Premier League's three powerhouses in the summer transfer market of 2009, who only sold and didn’t buy, being tempted by money. Manchester United transferred out two key offensive players, Cristiano Ronaldo and Tevez, and although Owen was signed, it wasn't enough to compensate for the loss of key players; Arsenal sold strong players like Adebayor and Toure for pounds; Liverpool also lost Alonso. In contrast, although Chelsea had heated transfer news, they only bought and didn't sell, even retaining popular figures like Deco and Drogba. Although these players are relatively older, compared to having no one to use, they are more than sufficient.

In conclusion, Chelsea's dominance this season is not unexpected. With a deep roster and the magical touch of Ancelotti, this group of players who seemed to be nearing the end of their careers have once again reached their peak form. If they can overcome the test of the Africa Cup in January next year, the Blues will undoubtedly become one of the most successful teams in the 2009-2010 season.
