Reform of Experimental Teaching in Computer Network Courses

Summary: Experimental teaching is an important component of the entire teaching process in higher vocational education. Regarding the specific characteristics and current situation of computer network experimental teaching in vocational schools, this paper closely revolves around the objectives of experimental teaching, exploring the experimental teaching system, content, and methods to propose a reform plan for computer network experimental teaching.

Keywords: Vocational school, experimental teaching, experimental teaching methods, computer network professional teaching. Experimental teaching plays a very important role in the teaching of computer network courses. Not only does it reinforce classroom content related to the course, but it also further develops students' professional skills, enabling them to apply what they've learned in their future work.

The demand for advanced network laboratory teaching has been raised in schools. However, due to insufficient funding in most vocational schools, laboratory equipment often fails to meet teaching requirements. Funding and equipment directly affect the quality of experimental teaching conditions. In the context of computer network experimental teaching, hardware is essential for achieving good experimental teaching outcomes. Similarly indispensable are the discoveries made through experimental teaching evaluations.

To achieve the teaching goal of developing students' professional abilities in computer networks, carefully designed experimental content is necessary. Basic capabilities can be developed through designing experimental content that ensures students not only understand how to perform experiments but also possess the professional technical skills required for their future jobs. Here are some key fundamental functionalities and corresponding experiments that students should master:

1) Network Management and Maintenance:

Daily network management and maintenance tasks, including configuration and change management, performance management, fault management, network management software, network verification, accounting management, router configuration experiments, OSPF dynamic routing experiments, VRRP configuration experiments, switch control interface/remote login experiments, three-layer switch VLAN interoperability tests, etc. The purpose of this type of experiment is mainly to cultivate students' enhanced network management and maintenance abilities, helping them overcome common faults encountered in practical work. To improve students' network protection and attack resistance capabilities, allowing them to handle certain levels of network attacks and understand current network security devices and debugging configurations, the following experiments have been set up: Operating system security experiments, network security management experiments, bandwidth and traffic monitoring and maintenance experiments, network surveillance experiments, secure ACL configuration experiments, MAC address binding experiments, VLAN configuration experiments, firewall configuration experiments, data backup and disaster recovery classification experiments.

2) Network Composition Implementation Ability:

For elementary students composing networks, which involves network devices and applications for planning and designing complete network schemes of varying sizes. This has largely led to the establishment of the following experiments: twisted pair cable connector production experiments, simple local area network experiments, L2 switch management IP address setting experiments, switch cascading configuration experiments, network cabling ability experiments.

This article discusses the importance of experimental teaching in computer network courses at vocational schools. It focuses on the specific features and current status of experimental teaching, including its objectives, teaching systems, content, and methods. Additionally, it proposes some reform solutions for computer network experimental teaching.
