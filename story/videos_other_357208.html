Knowledge of clothing washing

by scai56896 on 2010-05-31 19:59:46

1. When washing, please avoid hot water and prolonged soaking. For garments with a lot of decorations, it is recommended to turn them inside out and wash by hand.

2. Color fading in pure cotton fabrics is an international issue that cannot be resolved. Therefore, special attention should be paid to the method of washing black and other dark-colored all-cotton fabrics. Dark clothes should preferably be washed separately in cold water for the first time!!

3. Small tips to prevent clothing color fading.

First method: Acid wash. Required material: edible vinegar. This method is mainly targeted at red or purple and other brightly colored pure cotton clothes and knitted products. The method involves adding some common vinegar to the water before washing these clothes and soaking them for a while! However, the amount of vinegar should not be too much, otherwise it may easily dye light-colored clothes. If you can often clean your clothes this way, you can ensure that the colors of your clothes remain bright and new!

Second method: Flower Water cleaning method. Required material: flower water. This method is more suitable for cotton textiles and woolen knitted products. The method is to wash the clothes according to the regular method, after the clothes are rinsed clean, drop a few drops of flower water into clear water, then soak the cleaned clothes in such water for ten minutes. Clothes cleaned with this method can also serve to disinfect, kill bacteria, and remove sweat odor.

Third method: Saltwater soaking method. Required material: table salt. This method is best suited to deal with the fading problem of denim wear and patterned clothes, with extremely effective results. For newly purchased clothes, to prevent fading, soak them in concentrated salt water for half an hour before the first wash, and then wash according to the regular method. If there is still slight fading, you can soak them in diluted salt water for ten minutes before each wash. Persisting in doing so will ensure that the clothes will absolutely not fade anymore!

Fourth method: Reverse drying method. This method is the most commonly used, especially effective for some dark-colored clothes. The method is very simple, just dry the clothes inside out. Here we would like to particularly remind you: Most fabric clothes should not be directly exposed to sunlight, because ultraviolet rays are the main cause of clothes fading. Therefore, not only should you dry them inside out, but if possible, try to place them in a shaded and well-ventilated area to dry.
