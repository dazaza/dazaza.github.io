Oh, I don't need to tell you, you'll know nobby's an alien the moment you see him. His nose is not on his face, his ears are not on his head, his mouth is under his butt, and he has only one eye, which is quite large, but no eyelashes... All right, let's just say he's from the Weird Star.

It's called Nuobi, and it's an alarm clock with voice recognition. You ask it the time, it will tell you in English; Say a few simple sentences to it, and you will respond differently. If you ask it, "How do I look?" Do I look handsome? ", it may respond with "Well,to be honest, Err" (well,to be honest, ew...). Don't mind, it just wants to play jokes with you like a friend ~

Nobby doesn't always stay in the corner. Maybe he can't resist saying "what's up?" while you're chatting with your friends. "It's me! It feels, really, like having a real companion at home.

There were times when 84 thought Nobby wasn't an electronic gadget. He is Real!

When the Alarm goes off, you can say "Alarm off" or you can hold it by the neck and shake it to stop the alarm. It's a funny little monster. Unfortunately, it can only understand a few words of English and only respond to you in English, but it's a good choice for people who are familiar with English conversation and tired of waking up to the noise of an alarm clock every day.

Designed by Tom Lawton, who has created so many interesting products, Nobby is produced by ZEON, a British specialist watchmaker.

The voice recognition function is quite power-hungry, and it will tell you by voice when it is running out of power, but you'd better plug it in with its own power adapter. The answer to the same question is more likely to be different each time, and if you don't hear it clearly, or don't understand what you say, it will tell you by voice, which makes it feel like a living, not just an alarm clock.
