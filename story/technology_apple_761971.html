iOS shipment share drops to 17% in Q1, WP beats BlackBerry for first time

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 23:16:19

May 16th, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, market research company IDC released the latest research report today pointing out that due to the挤压of Google's Android platform, Apple's iOS platform's share of smartphone shipments in the first quarter of this year dropped to 17%. Meanwhile, Microsoft's Windows Phone platform surpassed BlackBerry to become the third-ranked mobile platform in terms of smartphone shipment share.The latest market data published by IDC on Thursday showed that Apple's iOS platform's share of smartphone shipments in the first quarter fell from 23% for the same period a year ago to 17.3%. However, according to the sales performance Apple released last month, its iPhone shipments still set a record high for the first quarter, reaching 37.4 million units.Since Apple's iOS platform is only applied to the iPhone, its growth rate cannot compare with Android. Currently, many smartphone manufacturers in the industry are continuously developing new Android smartphones, driving the continuous growth of Android platform shipment shares.According to IDC statistics, the share of Android smartphone shipments in the first quarter reached 75%, significantly increasing from 59.1% for the same period in 2012. IDC estimates that the total number of Android smartphone shipments in the first quarter reached 162.1 million units.Google announced at the Google I/O Developers Conference on Wednesday that the total activation volume of Android devices recently broke through the 900 million mark. A year ago, the activation volume of Android devices was only 400 million. The activation volume of Android devices reached 100 million in 2011, and judging from the growth rate of this data, the Android platform has maintained an accelerating growth trend in recent years.Since Apple and Google's mobile operating system platforms together take up 92% of the smartphone shipment share, there is very little left for other competitors. However, it is worth noting that Microsoft's Windows Phone platform surpassed BlackBerry in the first quarter of this year, becoming the third-ranked mobile platform in terms of smartphone shipment share. The smartphone shipment share of the Microsoft Windows Phone platform increased from 2% for the same period last year to 3.2%, while BlackBerry's share fell from 6.3% for the same period last year to 2.9%.In terms of smartphone shipments, the shipments of Windows Phone devices increased from 3 million units in the first quarter of 2012 to 7 million units. This is also the first time that the Windows Phone platform has taken the third position in terms of shipment share.BlackBerry's smartphone shipments in the first quarter were only 6.3 million units, but it is very likely to see a turnaround in the second quarter because BlackBerry just launched the BlackBerry 10 platform in the first quarter, and products based on this platform will welcome their first full sales quarter in the second quarter. The shipments of products based on the BlackBerry 10 platform exceeded 1 million units in the first quarter.Ramon Llamas, research manager of IDC's Mobile Phone Team, said: The landscape of the global smartphone market continues to change. Android and iOS still occupied the largest shipment share in the smartphone market in the first quarter, but from the development situation of other platforms, the market has shown certain signs of transformation and demand for products from other platforms. Windows Phone has already begun to benefit from Nokia's participation, and BlackBerry's latest BB10 device achieved excellent sales of 1 million units in the first quarter.
