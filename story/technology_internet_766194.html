Amazon China's return policy is accused of "consumer discrimination"

by anonymous on 2013-09-08 19:43:15

Recently, Amazon (China), the leader in online retail, has been frequently complained about by consumers due to issues such as substandard product quality and private order cancellations.

A customer surnamed Zhou from Changsha, Hunan Province, claimed that she bought a Sony mobile phone on Amazon not long ago but later discovered it was a refurbished unit. She faced numerous difficulties when attempting to return the item. The reporter's investigation revealed that for products like mobile phones and communication devices, Amazon's return policies in China and the United States differ significantly.

Amazon Frequently Complained About by Consumers

"A company as big as Amazon also sells refurbished units, which is extremely infuriating. In the future, how are consumers supposed to trust them? In the US, you can return items unconditionally, but in China, they make all sorts of excuses," Ms. Zhou from Changsha, Hunan Province, complained to the Economic Reference Newspaper.

Zhou told the reporter that on July 25th, she purchased a Sony LT29i phone online through Amazon. However, after receiving it, she found out it was a refurbished unit. "I had my eyes on the white version of the Sony LT29i for quite some time. After purchasing it, I used the AnTuTu software to test it, and the results showed it was a refurbished black unit. Additionally, the color bar under the detection results of the phone's built-in browser also displayed black."

Similarly, on July 19th, a Weibo user named "Paser Bai" complained about Amazon selling refurbished units: "My classmate bought a Samsung I9500 phone on Amazon, which broke down within a month. The Samsung after-sales service determined it was likely a refurbished unit."

On August 22nd, another Weibo user named "robin_lv39" complained about Amazon: "I spent 3,199 yuan on Amazon to buy an HTC One X phone, only to find out it was a refurbished unit that had undergone repairs. HTC after-sales service refused to warranty it. Contacting Amazon after-sales service resulted in their response of 'no returns or exchanges,' leaving the device defunct at home."

In addition to refurbished units, Amazon has also faced numerous complaints for selling counterfeit goods.

Mrs. Wang from Langfang placed an order on May 14th for a Fannia silk quilt on Amazon. Upon receiving it, she noticed something unusual about the texture and sent it to the National Textile Products Quality Supervision and Inspection Center for component testing. The results showed that the quilt consisted of 70.6% regenerated cellulose fiber and 29.4% polyester fiber. However, the label clearly stated that the filling material was 100% mulberry silk. After inspection by Fannia staff, it was confirmed to be a counterfeit product.

On August 19th, a Weibo user named "Lori Nian Nian Bu Wang Bi You Hui Xiang" complained about Amazon selling counterfeit goods: "The Johnson & Johnson ONE TOUCH Select Simple blood glucose meter (with a free gift of 40 test strips + 40 needles)! After verification by Johnson & Johnson's official hotline 8008201800, the gifted test strips were counterfeit! The instrument number I purchased was SNSA BFB0N M, and the test strip number was 34215242014-06. The Johnson & Johnson official hotline customer service stated that the last batch number ending with '06' did not exist; they were uniformly '09'!"

Difficulties in Returning Goods and Rights Protection

Online shopping is quick and convenient, but once it involves returns, it becomes rather difficult.

After self-inspecting her purchased phone as a refurbished unit, Ms. Zhou immediately called Amazon's after-sales customer service hotline. The response she received was: "AnTuTu might have made an error in its detection, and the results of the consumer's own detection are insufficient evidence. Within 7 days of receipt, the phone must be sent to Sony's after-sales service for inspection to issue a report. If the after-sales inspection result confirms it as a refurbished unit, then a return will be allowed." Ms. Zhou said, "The customer service representative repeatedly emphasized that only Sony's after-sales service issuing a detection report proving it to be a refurbished unit would qualify for a return, and this must be done within 7 days."

Ms. Zhou complained, "It took me several days to discover it was a refurbished unit, and there's only one Sony after-sales service point in Changsha, far from where I live. How could I possibly get the detection report within 7 days? This is clearly setting up obstacles for returns. I've been very busy recently, and if the after-sales service refuses to issue the detection report, not only will I not be able to return the item, but it will also waste my time."

Subsequently, Ms. Zhou complained about Amazon on Weibo, hoping to attract attention. Not long after posting the complaint, Ms. Zhou contacted Amazon's customer service multiple times. The customer service attitude slightly softened, stating that if going to the after-sales service for inspection was inconvenient, the item could be mailed to Amazon's headquarters in Beijing, where they could handle the inspection, and further actions would depend on the results.

"I don't feel secure sending it to Beijing for inspection. They act as both the athlete and the referee. I'm not in Beijing, so how can I ensure they will inspect it seriously? By then, they might come up with some excuse to shirk responsibility. Anyway, merchants always set various obstacles, making consumers lack the time and energy to deal with them, ultimately leading to unresolved issues. I myself found it troublesome and didn't want to argue with them anymore." After multiple rounds of communication and negotiation without results, Ms. Zhou reluctantly gave up.

Facing the various conditions set by merchants, many consumers, like Ms. Zhou, know their purchased goods are substandard, but considering the various troubles involved in returning them, they can only back away.

Additionally, e-commerce platforms and manufacturers mutually "passing the buck" makes it even more difficult for consumers to return items or protect their rights. Weibo user "Paser Bai" complained about Amazon selling refurbished units: "Samsung after-sales personnel said the phone had been disassembled, and the special sticker had been replaced, likely indicating it was a refurbished unit. Amazon requires a certification report, but Samsung doesn't provide one. This is the situation consumers face."

Significant Reduction in After-Sales Service

Amazon's American official website stipulates that Amazon commits to providing unconditional full refunds for most products within 30 days of delivery, provided the product and packaging remain in the original condition and all accessories are intact. Qi, who has lived in the U.S. for over two years, told the Economic Reference Newspaper, "In the U.S., Amazon provides unconditional returns. For the vast majority of products, as long as the product and packaging are well-preserved, they can be returned unconditionally within 30 days. As an extreme example, when I first came to the U.S., I didn't have a computer. To video chat with my parents, I temporarily bought one on Amazon. After using it for ten days, I decided I didn't like it, so I returned it, and it was successfully refunded. It felt magical. Actually, many large-scale malls and shopping websites in the U.S. allow unconditional returns within a specified period as long as the price tag or receipt is still present, without needing any reason."

The reporter's investigation found that for products like mobile phones and communication devices, Amazon's return policies in China and the U.S. differ significantly. On Amazon's official U.S. website, it is stipulated that mobile phones purchased on Amazon can be returned for a full refund within 30 days of purchase if the product and packaging are intact. In China, however, the policy changes: For mobile phones and communication devices (excluding accessories), once signed for, within the return and exchange period, if quality issues are discovered, customers need to contact the customer service center via self-service to apply for returns or exchanges; depending on the product category, customers need to contact the manufacturer or supplier's after-sales department according to the instructions to process a detection report according to the national three guarantees regulations; after successfully processing the detection report and confirming the product has performance faults, Amazon will handle the returns or exchanges.

"In terms of return policies, Amazon China exhibits serious 'consumer discrimination.' Adding the various obstacles and conditions set during specific operations further deprives domestic consumers of the rights they should enjoy," a relevant person in charge of the China Consumer Association told the Economic Reference Newspaper.

A reporter from the Economic Reference Newspaper interviewed the public relations department head of Amazon (China) regarding the inconsistency between domestic and international return standards. The responsible party replied, "There's absolutely no need for comparison. Amazon (China) formulates related return and exchange policies based on domestic laws and regulations, and they cannot be completely identical to foreign standards. Moreover, domestically, consumers also enjoy special discounts like free shipping for orders over 24 yuan, which are unavailable abroad."
