Why has Alibaba invested in these companies over the years?

by geekzhang on 2013-05-04 11:08:09

In the past 10 years, Alibaba has carried out some representative cases in terms of investment and acquisition for its overall strategy. Many signs indicate that Alibaba's layout has extended from initially focusing on e-commerce to social networking and mobile terminals.In August 2005, Alibaba acquired Yahoo! ChinaEvent: Alibaba acquired all assets of Yahoo! China and received a $1 billion investment from Yahoo!. They aimed to create the largest internet search platform in China. The acquired Yahoo! China assets included the Yahoo! China portal website, search technology, communication and advertising services, as well as the 3721 network real-name service. Both parties also planned to integrate Yahoo!'s part in the online auction business into Alibaba. Alibaba gained the indefinite exclusive right to use the globally leading internet brand Yahoo! in China.Acquisition Reason: Jack Ma once said that the true reason for acquiring Yahoo! China was because Alibaba saw that future e-commerce would be inseparable from search engines. He hoped that cooperation with Yahoo! could inject new concepts and vitality into e-commerce. We will create a company that is truly great worldwide.In October 2006, Alibaba acquired Koubei.comEvent: In October 2006, Alibaba Group made a strategic investment in Koubei.com. In June 2008, Koubei.com merged with China Yahoo! to form Yahoo! Koubei. In August, as part of the Big Taobao strategy, Koubei.com was integrated into Taobao, making Taobao a one-stop e-commerce service provider offering more services to e-commerce users.Acquisition Reason: Tian Jian, former vice president of the Investment Department of Alibaba Group, stated that the development direction of Koubei.com aligned with Alibaba Group's overall direction centered on e-commerce, using search and community tools. He said that the strategic investment in Koubei.com not only provided strong momentum and robust protection for its further development but also effectively connected and supplemented Alibaba Group's e-commerce and search businesses, ultimately becoming an indispensable component of online work, consumption, and life for a large number of netizens. Tian Jian believed that after obtaining the strategic investment from Alibaba Group, Koubei.com would focus more closely on local development directions, prioritizing dining and entertainment, real estate transactions, and covering dozens of major and medium-sized cities nationwide. With a reputation for excellent user experience, it aimed to develop into the largest localized lifestyle community platform domestically and ultimately bring better user stickiness and reputation to various services under Alibaba Group.Status: Koubei.com is a website under Taobao.com, dedicated to building the first brand in the field of life services e-commerce. The site provides consumers with review sharing and consumer guides, and serves as a platform for merchants to release promotional information, conduct word-of-mouth marketing, and implement e-commerce.In September 2009, Alibaba acquired China WanwangEvent: Alibaba acquired China Wanwang's equity in two phases for RMB 540 million. This was the largest single investment project since Alibaba's IPO in Hong Kong in 2007. China Wanwang holds a very prominent position in the internet infrastructure service industry. As the entry point for small enterprise e-commerce, its services such as domain name services, hosting services, corporate email, and website construction have leading advantages in multiple aspects like industrial layout, customer base, and technical status.Acquisition Reason: Market analysis suggests that China Wanwang will become an important layout and beneficial extension for Alibaba in the SME e-commerce industry chain. Investing in China Wanwang aligns with Alibaba's overall strategic layout of solving SME IT issues, thereby addressing the problems of survival difficulty, development difficulty, and financing difficulty for SMEs.Status: On January 1, 2013, Alibaba Group announced that its Aliyun and Wanwang would merge into a new Aliyun company. After the merger, the Wanwang brand would continue to be retained, becoming a domain service brand under Aliyun.In January 2010, Alibaba made a strategic investment in Shanghai Baozun CompanyEvent: Founded in early 2007, Shanghai Baozun Company currently operates flagship stores for multiple brands on Taobao and provides comprehensive e-commerce services including marketing services, IT services, customer services, and logistics warehousing services for these brands. In January 2010, Alibaba announced the completion of its strategic investment in Shanghai Baozun Company.Investment Reason: Zhang Wei, head of the Strategic Investment Department of Alibaba Group, expressed that with the rapid development of e-commerce, more and more traditional enterprises are starting B2C businesses based on the Taobao platform. The value provided by e-commerce service providers to enterprises looking to vigorously develop e-commerce is increasingly evident. Specialized division of labor in the e-commerce field is a prevailing trend. The market potential of the e-commerce service provider sector is enormous, and Alibaba Group will continue to invest in this area to further enhance Taobao's ability to serve consumers and brand enterprises.In June 2010, Alibaba's first acquisition in the U.S. marketEvent: In June 2010, Alibaba acquired Vendio Services (Vendio), an American e-commerce SaaS (Software as a Service) provider. This was Alibaba's first acquisition in the U.S. market.Acquisition Reason: Albert Ho, former Executive Director and CEO of Alibaba Network Limited, once indicated that through the acquisition of Vendio, Alibaba would directly attract over 80,000 high-quality buyers and potential procurement customers in the U.S., bringing tens of billions of dollars in procurement opportunities to domestic small enterprises. This represents a global reverse buyer acquisition model. Moreover, after the integration of Global速卖通and Vendio platforms, Alibaba can also leverage this platform to provide a one-stop e-commerce solution for domestic and international users.In July 2011, Meituan raised $50 million in Series B funding led by AlibabaEvent: Meituan's Series B funding round was led by Alibaba Group, with three institutions—Northern Light Venture Capital, Walden International, and Sequoia Capital—following up.Investment Reason: For this lead investment in Meituan, Zhang Wei, head of Ali Capital, officially stated that group buying provides new marketing channels for small and medium-sized local life service merchants, helping them understand consumer feedback and improve their products and services. At the same time, group buying offers consumers discounted life service experiences. Alibaba hopes to support group-buying companies with good brands, execution capabilities, and entrepreneurial passion with capital, providing better services for consumers and local small and medium-sized merchants.In November 2012, Alibaba strategically invested in MomoEvent: Ali Capital, under Alibaba Group, invested in the social application Momo. The financing was exclusively advised by Huaxing Capital, with investors being DST and Alibaba, representing a strategic investment.Investment Reason: At the time, Momo version 2.0 added location-based group social features. CEO Tang Yan gave Momo a clearer positioning: a social application based on LBS. This aligned perfectly with Alibaba's strategy. Not long after Taobao launched map services, which used map positioning provided by Ali Cloud, Taobao could locate, find nearby discounts, and merchants. Its O2O strategy increasingly focused on combining with local services. From this event, it can be seen that Alibaba once again strengthened its efforts in mobile social networks.In November 2012, Alibaba strategically invested in Dingding to layout O2OEvent: Following the low-key launch of Taobao Map service in October 2012, Alibaba again exerted effort in the local life service sector. Alibaba joined Citi Bank to invest in the life information service platform Dingding.Investment Reason: Zhang Hongping, managing director of Ali Capital, introduced that the investment in Dingding mainly looked forward to the development prospects of its O2O business. Dingding Youhui is one of the best channels for local life areas to transition to O2O e-commerce platforms and would synergize with multiple business departments of Alibaba. Additionally, Dingding has seven years of experience in the local life sector. In the view of Dingding CEO Xu Longjiang, Alibaba Group has many good resources and payment channels that can help Dingding grow faster. Accepting Alibaba's investment is mainly to leverage its strength to accelerate the e-commerce transformation of Dingding's mobile O2O platform.In April 2013, Alibaba acknowledged acquiring Xiami and jointly promoted new productsEvent: In late April, Taobao will collaborate with Xiami to launch a service where users can shop while listening to music. By then, Taobao users can directly access embedded Xiami music services when shopping. Alibaba Group simultaneously confirmed that they had completed the acquisition of Xiami.Acquisition Reason: In the future, Taobao and Xiami will introduce a dedicated digital music category, including mobile cooperation. Taobao's search function not only includes simple product and seller searches but also collaborates with Xiami to search for songs or artists for playback and audition. Taobao expressed that with the deepening cooperation between Taobao and Xiami, Taobao is no longer just a shopping website but a multimedia lifestyle platform expanding into more fields, enhancing user shopping experiences, and meeting users' diverse and personalized needs.Additionally, there were rumors about Alibaba's intentions to acquire Moji Weather and Umeng. These undoubtedly stemmed from Alibaba's repeated actions in the mobile sector. For instance, Moji Weather's vast user base establishes its entrance status and social attributes, which are also Alibaba's shortcomings in the mobile field. As for Umeng, given Alibaba's recent push for big data strategies, if they acquire Umeng, they would gain the latter's most valuable asset—accumulated massive mobile application data.Currently, Umeng controls data from over 100,000 mobile applications, considered crucial for analyzing user behavior and precise mobile e-commerce advertising. An investment professional stated that the data accumulated by Umeng in the mobile internet field could potentially combine with Alibaba's existing advertisers and allow for deeper understanding of Alibaba's users through analysis of Umeng's data. Virtual goods dominate sales on the mobile Taobao app, yet Taobao as a platform cannot benefit much due to the limited ad space available in mobile apps. Each acquisition by Alibaba shows careful planning and purposeful progress, and the acquired companies generally develop well. Whether they can achieve the overall goals set by Jack Ma remains to be seen.In April 2013, Alibaba's strategic investment in Sina benefits its overall layoutIf we say that Alibaba's investment brings much-needed funds to Weibo, then investing in Weibo fills a significant gap in Alibaba's strategic layout. From the perspective of the overall mobile strategy, Weibo is Alibaba's most important strategic investment. On desktop platforms, Alibaba's Taobao and Tmall already hold an unshakable leading advantage, with market share and revenue scale far exceeding competitors. Alibaba has a loyal and vast user base, with sufficient traffic so they don't need to rely on search engines, allowing them to confidently refuse Baidu advertisements and resist Baidu spider web crawling.However, the originally closed traffic chain of Taobao faced challenges from social commerce websites like Meilishuo and Mogu Street due to the lack of social elements. Alibaba doesn't lack the ability to monetize traffic; what they need is a more effective traffic entry point. According to market research firm Hitwise, 8.6% of Weibo's external traffic flows to e-commerce and local life websites, with Taobao and Tmall accounting for 3.7 percentage points. Investing in Sina Weibo undoubtedly provides Alibaba's mobile layout with the most urgently needed social genes and traffic entry point.Besides, investing in Weibo allows Alibaba to complete its mobile layout. Among the BAT trio, whoever takes the lead in mobile opportunities dominates the future. Tencent, with its WeChat, Mobile QQ, QQ Browser, QQ Address Book, and other applications with hundreds of millions of users, has taken the lead in completing its mobile layout. While Alibaba owns Mobile Taobao and Alipay, two top-tier applications, it lacks Tencent-like products in other mobile applications; thus, investment and acquisition become Alibaba's most effective remedy. From UC Browser, Momo Technology, Changba, Kuaidi Dache, to the latest Sina Weibo, Alibaba quickly completes its mobile internet entry layout through a series of acquisitions and strategic investments. Weibo is clearly the most critical social and mobile deployment in Alibaba's strategic layout.This collaboration prospect includes several aspects:Account Interoperability. Sina Weibo, Taobao, and Alipay each have hundreds of millions of users, with highly overlapping internet users. Once account interoperability is achieved, future Weibo users can click links to directly enter Taobao and Tmall to purchase goods and complete payments via Alipay, finally sharing their shopping insights and recommendations on Weibo, integrating social and shopping behaviors.Complete Data. Account interoperability also means the completeness of user data. Sina Weibo and Alibaba Group both have huge user bases, mastering massive and detailed user data. Sina Weibo understands users' friends, personalities, interests, and living habits, while Taobao and Tmall understand users' shopping, delivery, and payment data. Combining these two sets of data, Sina Weibo and Alibaba will possess detailed information on nearly real-name users, enabling them to provide precise advertisements and services.Precise Advertising. User data is the most important wealth of an internet company, and data analysis and processing require higher technical capabilities. Twitter achieves $500 million annual revenue through promoted tweets and trending topics, relying on data analysis and applied advertising capabilities. This is what Sina Weibo currently needs to improve, while Alibaba has stronger data analysis techniques developed through operating Taobao and Tmall. After the integration of the two ecosystems, big data analysis will become the most important enhancement capability for both sides, providing advertisements and recommendations that users want to see. Meanwhile, Sina Weibo will gain more advertising revenue, expected to earn $380 million in the next three years.We can imagine that when a user participates in discussions about Apple iPads on Weibo, the right side of their Weibo webpage will display links to buy iPads with the highest credit ratings on Taobao and Tmall. When a user frequently expresses interest in fitness on Weibo, their mobile Weibo client will show promotional tweets for Nike sports shoes. When viewing products, users can see purchases and reviews from their Weibo friends, allowing them to make more accurate judgments. Ads become more precise, more in line with user needs, and more socially interactive—only the cooperation between Sina Weibo and Alibaba can achieve this.Convenient Payment. Alipay is the crown jewel of Alibaba and the largest third-party payment system in China, having made significant progress in exploring offline payments. Given that Weibo and Alipay both require binding to mobile phones, after the integration of these two accounts, Weibo users can enjoy various convenient online and offline payment services from Alipay simply by using their usernames and passwords. Alipay will make Weibo payments safer and more popular, allowing users to pay for taxi fares, small merchants, phone bills, household expenses, etc., while browsing Weibo.Social Marketing. After cooperating with Weibo, Alibaba's millions of Taobao small sellers and thousands of Tmall sellers will gradually move onto Weibo to explore e-commerce marketing in the era of social networks. A realistic expectation is that these sellers will have certified e-commerce accounts, displaying store information, credit evaluations, and user feedback on their Weibo pages. These sellers will interact with their fans (consumers or potential customers) and market through social platforms, achieving cyclic interaction between marketers and audiences, and between audiences themselves. In the field of social e-commerce, Weibo and Alibaba have greater space for cooperation and exploration.Premium Listing. Besides business cooperation, this deal has special significance for both parties. Alibaba Group and Sina Weibo may list independently, and how to tell a better story to impress investors is key to gaining high valuations. With the strategic investment in Sina Weibo, Alibaba's complete ecosystem fills the most critical social link, giving it stronger leverage in capital markets to demand higher valuation premiums. And Sina Weibo, with the support of the e-commerce giant, greatly enhances its financial resources and monetization capabilities, enabling it to better persuade investors in possible future listings.Spanning mobile and social domains, Weibo and WeChat have established unshakable advantages in China. Compared to WeChat's point-to-point transmission method, Weibo's point-to-surface and cyclic interaction characteristics are more suitable for e-commerce marketing. Furthermore, since WeChat belongs to Tencent, a direct competitor, Sina Weibo becomes Alibaba's ideal cooperative choice. The natural synergy between social media and e-commerce platforms makes the marriage of Sina Weibo and Alibaba widely praised, but specific cooperation methods still need to be explored in the future.Amazon or eBay missed the best opportunity to invest in Twitter, and now Twitter has grown significantly. Alibaba is undoubtedly astute, obtaining the chance for strategic investment at the moment when Sina Weibo already possesses immense influence but is just embarking on commercialization. This deal might set the tone for Alibaba e-commerce's development in the next decade. Meanwhile, Sina Weibo not only gains capital but also acquires substantial growth space for future commercial operations.The best timing meets the most suitable partner, which is fortunate for both. Great cooperation benefits everyone except competitors.
