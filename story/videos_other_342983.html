Sanyuan milk subscription, the freshness speed of Sanyuan milk

by dingnai310 on 2010-04-10 13:18:16

    San Yuan Milk Subscription;     Wang Dan is the manager of San Yuan's operation center. Since April this year, she can see the latest distribution report of the previous day on her desk every early morning. After correctly understanding the sales situation of each warehouse area yesterday, she adjusts the production orders according to these information and customers' ordering conditions of the day.

Although it seems that shipping at 9 p.m. every night now is no different from before, in fact, many changes are taking place imperceptibly. After the new system was launched, the company's staff can easily query order tracking, and analyzing customer needs through the estimated delivery table is much more convenient than before. The brand-new information system has already enabled San Yuan's managers to accurately grasp the enterprise's pulse, and it has only been over a month since the new system was launched.

The road has been bumpy. San Yuan is an old-established milk production enterprise in Beijing, with products including milk, yogurt, cheese, etc., up to 150 varieties. On March 28, 2007, San Yuan announced its annual report for 2006, reporting nearly 1 billion RMB in main business revenue and profits reaching 2.09 billion, indicating the company had overcome the development difficulties of the previous few years.

In the late 1990s, San Yuan began its informatization construction process. Starting from the perspective of information data management, it chose Limas software which had certain advantages in this direction. "For a long period of time, the Limas system indeed made contributions to our sales business in terms of information data processing," introduced Yang Qinggui, the financial director and secretary of the board of directors of Beijing San Yuan Food Co., Ltd.

For enterprises like San Yuan that require daily distribution, saving time and ensuring sales quality are the top priorities. From receiving orders to production, and finally to delivery to users, all must be completed on the same day. Customer orders flood into the operation center, then customer managers place orders in the distribution system. Due to the lack of system information sharing, producers could only estimate production. When the products were delivered to the warehouse areas, problems would arise: the products produced on that day might not meet the order requirements, while customers were waiting outside the gate to pick up goods. At such times, supplies could only be provided based on the importance of the customer and personal relationships. Chen Yan Yong, the manager of San Yuan's information department, depicted the typical operational scene at the time. In response to the issues, constructing a smooth and efficient logistics system became one of the urgent requirements of San Yuan's management for the informatization system.

Wang Dan is the manager of San Yuan's operation center. How to predict and control the real demand volume of customers every day, place orders with manufacturers, and achieve both 100% guarantee of customer needs and near-zero corporate inventory have always troubled her.

San Yuan has four warehouse areas, each with a server. Even slight differences in various types of information may increase the complexity of subsequent data processing. Moreover, these production and sales data were not integrated with finance, requiring dedicated personnel to unify the data from the four warehouse areas, followed by joint statistics. By the time such reports came out, they were often outdated, and analyses based on such lagging reports made San Yuan's response ability in the changing market clearly sluggish.

The ideal product distribution model in Wang Chichen's mind includes accepting customer orders, combining terminal user demand analysis to place orders with the production factory, placing orders with logistics, picking up goods for the production factory, and finally reaching users in each link. All links should have precise reflections and traceability through data analysis in the system. Meanwhile, Wang Chichen also realized that for daily distribution enterprises like San Yuan, achieving such an ideal model is not easy. Compared with other similar enterprises, San Yuan has almost covered all large malls and supermarkets in Beijing with thousands of terminal customers, plus a wide variety of products, all of which impose high requirements on system calculations and delivery speed.

Facing market opportunities, any small mistake may come at a high cost. In recent years, the dairy market has quickly shifted from a fragmented era to a consolidation era. The rise of emerging dairy companies like Mengniu and Yili has changed the market landscape. "San Yuan is making strategic adjustments in production, sales, and management, and the old Limas system can no longer meet the new business needs," Yang Qinggui commented.

A Brighter Path

In October 2005, the Digital China ETOP ERP system was officially launched at San Yuan Huaguan Production Plant, simultaneously establishing a data information center of about 10 people, specifically responsible for various links including procurement, workshop consumption, finished goods warehousing, etc., from orders to product shipment, timely entering and summarizing various information, ensuring order response time within one day, and directly summarizing reports to the finance department.

"It was difficult to coordinate and complete before the new system was launched," Chen Yan Yong told reporters while leading them to visit the San Yuan Huaguan production plant and the Huaguan data information center. "Now the system can directly allocate goods according to data, and the communication between production and sales information can be seamlessly connected. The production plant can instantly understand the quantity and type of orders, and logistics can know when to pick up goods and what kind of goods to take, all of which can be shared. Both front-end production and back-end logistics can proceed orderly."

Currently, the daily output of San Yuan Huaguan production plant has reached 16 tons, doubling compared to before.

Finally, it's up to consumers who have the power to vote with their feet, which product is the most cost-effective - this is a promotional management issue. Promotions for dairy consumer goods happen every day, and companies must always understand the flow and quantity of gifts to spend the least money for the best results. Sales managers need to quickly understand the promotional report analysis of the previous day, understand what competitors are doing, and adjust their own promotional strategies through analysis.

San Yuan has achieved multiple models in promotional management, such as A giving A, A giving B+C, weight promotion, gifts accompanying goods, and post-gifts, etc. San Yuan staff only need to input corresponding condition requirements according to different promotional models, so that the quantity of gifts can be accurately reflected in the system. With the support of the information system, promotional items can be accurately delivered to the terminal outlets of various channels such as wholesalers and supermarkets, allowing the channel to conduct promotional activities more realistically based on the market conditions of regions and main products.

A Sunny Future?

"Everyone hopes that the product that reaches the customer is their most proud product." Talking about the IT technical support needed by the San Yuan operation center in the future, Wang Dan told reporters, "We are considering managing products with barcodes in the future, so that all information from production to warehousing and then to shipment is reflected in the barcode, making our management more refined."

At the same time, San Yuan also hopes that the future information system can help San Yuan effectively avoid risks, such as formulating new ordering rules, making customers order according to the product's production cycle. For dairy products with a two-day production cycle, customers can be allowed to order two days in advance. "For customers, pre-ordering can guarantee 100% supply; for San Yuan, it means more perfect inventory management," Wang Dan said.

As the financial director of San Yuan, Yang Qinggui has another concern: will the implementation of the ERP system cause some degree of management rigidity due to excessive dependence on the system? He revealed to reporters that he has suggested setting up a temporary institution within San Yuan, designating specific personnel to constantly monitor changes in assets and business, while maintaining a smooth contact with the consultants of Digital China ERP to respond to possible changes at any time.

Attitude determines everything. San Yuan's investment in informatization has also become a symbol of San Yuan breaking away from the image of an old state-owned enterprise. Since the end of 2004, the open Beijing market has accepted various similar products from different regions, giving consumers more choices, making competition in the market even more intense. Market expansion has become extremely difficult. In 2006, Yili's main business revenue had reached 16.339 billion yuan, followed closely by Mengniu Dairy, which also reached 16.246 billion yuan. Compared with the young talents of the past, now opponents in the dairy world, San Yuan obviously has a certain gap with them. How to stand firm and lay the foundation for future counterattacks? The senior management of San Yuan is pondering this question. Using informatization to improve efficiency and reduce costs is the most important step among many things to do. In the modern market, competition also means progress! We believe that San Yuan, with firm determination, will develop further under the support of a modern management system!
