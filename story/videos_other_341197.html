Journalists help the veteran of War to Resist US Aggression and Aid Korea to safeguard rights, were reviled and run over by the feet by the party secretary

by ztt0288868 on 2010-04-07 14:15:49

On the Wuchang campus of Huazhong University of Science and Technology, an employee wearing a light red outfit pointed at our female reporter and loudly insulted her. Photo by our reporter Sun Chen. On the Wuchang campus of Huazhong University of Science and Technology, an elderly farmer picked up and raised a cerebral palsy baby girl for 30 years. The university's discipline inspection committee secretary ran over our female reporter's ankle with his car. After getting out of the car, he mocked our reporter and called the security department staff. Photo by our reporter Sun Chen. The injured female reporter was unable to walk. Photo by our reporter Sun Chen. Xiaochang County. Veteran Li Zhensheng showed his retirement certificate.

Introduction: To protect the rights of an 80-year-old veteran who once participated in the Korean War, Li Zhensheng, reporters went to investigate at the Wuchang branch of Huazhong University of Science and Technology. During the interview, a person known as "Secretary" named Chen drove over the right foot of the female reporter, and someone at the scene threatened to "beat" the reporter.

Online news: "I am an old worker from the original 7011 factory, with 40 years of service. Why did the factory allocate my single dormitory room to someone else without my knowledge, causing me to lose the economic compensation of 83,000 yuan and also losing the qualification to purchase affordable housing?" said Li Zhensheng, an 80-year-old veteran who once participated in the Korean War, in a post online yesterday.

Yesterday afternoon, reporters went to investigate this matter at the Wuchang branch of Huazhong University of Science and Technology. During the interview, a person known as "Secretary" named Chen drove over the right foot of the female reporter, and someone at the scene threatened to "beat" the reporter.

Returning to Wuhan to collect consolation money, only to find that the house is gone.

"After retiring, my grandfather transferred to work at the 7011 factory of the Nanhu Auto School in Wuchang. He retired in 1990. In 2003, the 7011 factory was acquired by the Wuchang branch of Huazhong University of Science and Technology and became a school-run enterprise," said Li Min, grandson of Li Zhensheng. Li Zhensheng is a veteran of the Korean War who participated in the Battle of Shangganling. He successfully blew up the enemy's fortress, playing a decisive role in the success of the battle. According to relevant policies, the Wuhan Civil Affairs Bureau gives Li Zhensheng a consolatory payment of 1,000 yuan every year. Half a month ago, he accompanied his grandfather to Wuhan to collect the money from his former unit, only to find that his original single dormitory had been demolished and a building over 20 stories high was being constructed.

"We asked the old neighbors and found out that Grandpa's original single dormitory was demolished and rebuilt into affordable housing," said Li Min. Each of the more than 700 workers living in the single dormitory building received an 83,000 yuan demolition compensation. Workers who did not receive compensation could buy an affordable housing unit at a low price of 2,000 yuan per square meter.

"Why didn't I get any compensation?"

"With a Wuhan hukou (household registration), 40 years of service, and the original single dormitory, why didn't I get any compensation?" Li Zhensheng could not understand. He asked his former colleague Peng Tieqing, who told him that he had already received over 80,000 yuan in economic compensation but did not know why Li did not.

"When the houses were being demolished, was there your house? You came too late!" When Li Zhensheng and Li Min approached the community committee director Pan Jianling of the factory, she said that after Li Zhensheng retired, he lived long-term in his hometown, which was equivalent to voluntarily giving up this dormitory. Without the house, there would be no economic compensation.

According to Li Min, in 2006, he still lived in this dormitory, but at some point, the factory tore it down without his knowledge, "Even if others wanted to live there, they should have notified me beforehand."

Yesterday morning, reporters found Li Zhensheng in Xiaochang County. He said that after retiring, he lived in Wuhan where his family felt uneasy, so most of the time, he lived in Xiaochang and sometimes returned to the single dormitory for a few days. Since there were still gas stoves, beds, tables, and other daily necessities in the dormitory, each time he returned to his hometown, he would lock the door.

Li Zhensheng recalled that when he left in 2008, there were still over 200 yuan in the room. "After 2008, I never went back to the factory, I didn't know the house was going to be demolished, and no one notified me to vacate. Now I don't even know where the things in the room have gone."

Huazhong University of Science and Technology Wuchang Branch obstructed the reporter's investigation

Secretary Chen: "Get out of here!"

Director Peng: "What the hell are you doing?"

Yesterday, reporters came to the Wuchang branch of Huazhong University of Science and Technology to investigate this matter. Secretary Chen said to the reporter: "What are you supervising? Who are you supervising? Who asked you to supervise? Get out of here!" Then, this secretary and Pan Jianling got into a Santana and prepared to leave. Facing the reporter's interview, the secretary's attitude was extremely rude, and while preparing to leave, he ran over the female reporter's foot.

"You supervise who? Get out!"

During the interview, Secretary Chen and Pan Jianling kept blaming Li Min: "What does your business have to do with them? Can they help you solve the problem?" After scolding Li Min, Secretary Chen shouted loudly at the reporter: "You're talking nonsense! Who are you supervising? Get out!" Then, Secretary Chen and Pan Jianling got into the Santana and prepared to leave.

Li Min opened the car door and sat in the back seat. When the female reporter stepped her left foot on the car to prepare to sit in, Secretary Chen suddenly started the car after looking back once. The Santana's wheels rolled over the female reporter's right Achilles tendon. The female reporter shouted and asked the car to stop, but Secretary Chen continued driving forward. After the reporter repeatedly asked the car to stop, Secretary Chen finally stopped the car.

The reporter then called 110 to report the incident. Secretary Chen, who had been sitting in the car all along, finally got out of the car. While waiting for the police, Secretary Chen made a phone call: "Old Cao, something happened here. Bring a few security guards over." Secretary Chen narrated the entire incident to the people who arrived. When talking about running over the female reporter's foot, Secretary Chen laughed continuously, "Laughing is my freedom. Can't I laugh while talking to others?"

At this moment, a person claiming to be a police officer from the school's police station rushed over. However, there was no registration done on-site. Secretary Chen narrated the entire incident process to them. When the reporter tried to correct the incorrect parts of what Secretary Chen said, the police officer said, "Let him finish speaking first."

Then, the 110 operator called the reporter and said that the traffic police would arrive soon. "Why would the traffic police come? Let them handle it. We don't care anymore." The police officer wrote down the reporter's name and ID number on a piece of scrap paper before leaving.

Threatening to "beat" the reporter

Later, Secretary Chen called a group of people. A middle-aged man wearing light yellow yelled and cursed at the reporter, using foul language. While waiting for the traffic police, these people said, "What nonsense! You're not important. Why do you need to interview? It's none of your business," and claimed they would "beat" the reporter.

At this moment, a white car pulled up, and a man in a windbreaker got out. After investigation, it turned out to be Director Peng from the school's labor and personnel department. Without asking any questions, he said, "This doesn't need to be reported. I will directly contact your boss. You're still a reporter, what the hell are you doing?"

When the traffic police arrived, they asked Secretary Chen to hand over the keys and take photos as evidence. Someone questioned the traffic police, "Are you handling this like this? Why are you taking the keys? For such a small matter, do you need to seize the car? We didn't even call you, why are you seizing the car?"

After the traffic police dealt with the situation, Secretary Chen asked the reporter to go apologize alone, refusing to accompany the reporter to apologize.

(Source: Changjiang Business News, Reporter Minsheng)
