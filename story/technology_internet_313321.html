Barca being accused of handing over the championship ignites a bombshell; Opponent: We'll give you 70 points, we'll compete for the second place.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-18 08:15:46

Barcelona Accused of Being Given Preferential Treatment in Title Race: Rivals Claim "Give Them 70 Points, We'll Compete for Second"

Sina Sports - Last weekend's Barcelona derby left both Espanyol feeling aggrieved over their loss and Barcelona feeling the win wasn't entirely honorable, all thanks to referee Iturralde. He "mysteriously" awarded Barcelona a penalty that helped them secure a 1-0 victory over their local rivals Espanyol. Subsequently, he also red-carded Espanyol's Baena in the player tunnel after the match for continuously questioning his penalty decision.

In all honesty, this penalty seemed intentionally detrimental to Espanyol. Interestingly, from this one penalty call, two different versions of the truth emerged from the Barcelona and Espanyol camps.

If Brualla is considered a "Real Madrid affiliated" referee, then Iturralde — one of Spain's most renowned referees — is clearly "Barcelona aligned." In previous matches he officiated at Camp Nou, Barcelona has never lost. This season, he refereed only one Barcelona game where they triumphed 2-0 away against Getafe. Another telling fact is that in Iturralde’s past six matches officiating Barcelona, five penalties were awarded, all benefiting Barcelona. According to Marca, four of these penalties are questionable, suggesting an ulterior motive of "gifting penalties."

The controversial moment occurred in the 39th minute of the Espanyol match when Ibrahimovic crossed from the left edge of the box, and Xavi suddenly fell while contesting for the ball. Iturralde ruled that Baena had pulled Xavi’s shirt during his backtracking defense, awarding a penalty. This decision immediately sparked strong dissatisfaction from the Espanyol side. Espanyol's young coach Pochettino cryptically said post-match: “I didn’t see clearly what happened in the box; maybe the referee saw it. Even after watching the replay, I cannot confirm it was a penalty. My players told me at halftime that nothing happened, so in my view, this penalty does not exist.”

Espanyol's Luis Garcia expressed his grievances, complaining that Iturralde's unfair decision hurt Espanyol: “If we follow this standard, we could get a penalty inside Barcelona’s box too. Sure, Barcelona is very strong, but they shouldn’t score points with such penalties.”

The reaction within the Barcelona camp was exactly opposite. The involved party Xavi testified: “This is an obvious penalty, didn’t you see? I was grabbed by the jersey inside the box; Baena pulled my jersey preventing me from jumping. There’s no doubt, it’s a penalty. He pulled me, and because I couldn’t use my legs properly, I fell.” After Xavi fell, Guardiola helplessly spread his hands on the sideline, indicating support for his player demanding a penalty. However, discussing this ruling post-match, Guardiola admitted that he himself couldn’t be sure if it was a penalty, saying, “Xavi told me Baena pulled him, and I believe Xavi.”

Clearly, Espanyol's manager Planes did not trust Xavi: “How can Xavi say that? By Iturralde’s standard, there could have been 12 penalties in this Barcelona derby! I insist that there was no physical contact between Baena and Xavi. If there was any, it certainly wasn’t enough to cause him to fall.” CEO Collet added, “Why do we always get penalized in every derby? Last time, it was a penalty in the 93rd minute, and there was Messi's handball. This time again... If our opponent was Real Madrid, someone would have surely called for Franco’s overthrow already. The FA should consider this issue. If it continues like this, why not just give Barcelona 70 points and let the rest of us fight for second place?”

No matter what others said, it was futile. Regarding the penalty controversy, Baena, the involved party, remained tight-lipped, refusing to comment, until two days after the event when the Barcelona-trained Espanyol player appeared on Catalan Radio to start explaining the situation. “Yes, I did pull Xavi,” confirming the possibility of the penalty, though either Xavi’s fall was exaggerated or Iturralde made a wrong call. He continued, “But it definitely wasn’t a penalty. The force of my pull on Xavi was not enough to make him lose balance and fall. Xavi’s position was very forward; I turned around and reached out to feel his position, that’s all. I obstructed Xavi momentarily, but the force was insufficient to make him fall. You can check the TV replay. Honestly, I find it hard to believe that it was called a penalty because I was thinking about Xavi possibly taking a shot before I turned around.”

There were also reports that Baena confronted Iturralde in the player tunnel after the game seeking justice and was subsequently shown a red card. What did Baena say that angered Iturralde? “I simply told him, go watch the replay! But I definitely didn’t use aggressive language, that’s all.”

What further annoyed Baena was Guardiola, as Barcelona’s coach mentioned in the post-game press conference, “Xavi told me, Espanyol's players admitted it was a penalty.” “I really don’t know how they can say that. I never told Xavi it was a penalty. If we ever had any conversation, I would have definitely told him it wasn’t a penalty.”

This controversy gave Madrid media a chance to stir up the topic. Both Marca and As strongly criticized this contentious decision, pointing fingers at Iturralde, believing he protected Barcelona, allowing them to take three points. Otherwise, Real Madrid, who played fewer games, would only trail Barcelona by two points.

In contrast, Catalan media, despite Espanyol being a Barcelona team, wouldn’t speak for a club bearing the Spanish royal crest. To counter the capital city media, Sport newspaper specifically analyzed the match footage and included screenshots showing Baena indeed pulling Xavi, with deputy editor Carazo stating: “Obviously, it’s a penalty.” More interestingly, the paper also provided a screenshot of Real Madrid's play against Valencia, highlighting Benzema's offside suspicion during the first goal scored against Valencia, provocatively titled: “Real Madrid’s first goal was offside, okay, what’s there to argue about?”

Fortunately, the involved parties Barcelona, Xavi, and Guardiola have left the center of the storm in Spain and started their FIFA Club World Cup journey in Abu Dhabi. In their eyes, winning this championship is much more meaningful than debating whether it was a penalty or not. (Liu Hui)
