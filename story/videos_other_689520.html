In the prime of youth - Li Fei attended the premiere of "In the Prime of Youth" and sang the theme song, receiving praise - www.d1k

by mkomko8f1h on 2012-03-04 10:44:23

Yesterday, Li Fei, a young female singer of the Chinese People's Liberation Army Opera Group was invited to attend the premiere of China's first "cruel youth film" "Innocence Lost", and performed the theme song of the movie "A Fated Encounter" on the scene. Due to the involvement of sensitive issues in the realistic theme, "Innocence Lost" has also been labeled as a "banned film" by the media. Li Fei's passionate and grand performance fully expressed the profound meaning that the "banned film" wanted to convey with musical notes, receiving unanimous praise on the scene.

"Innocence Lost": A Cruel Tale of Youth

Youth, like love, has always been an eternal topic for artists' creation. However, each artist's Hamlet is different, and each filmmaker's view of youth varies greatly. In director Zhao Yanguozhang's eyes, youth is undoubtedly a cruel tale of youth, a classic film. Everyone is like a creature burdened with social pressures, gradually losing their original innocence and beauty in the process of being polished by society. The unbearable pressure and hardships from all sides are like an unbreakable curse. However, as long as one holds a positive spirit, they will not retreat in the face of challenges or get lost in the forest of stones, and their life coordinates will be adjusted accordingly.

Due to winning the most prestigious Outstanding Director Award at the 6th Hollywood Chinese Film Festival, "Innocence Lost" has made countless domestic audiences look forward to its release, expecting this highly appreciative film to hit the screens soon for a visual feast. The broadcast of this film will inevitably spark a great discussion among many people who still have dreams of youth, igniting a wave of crazy fantasies about youth.

The Eccentric Director Chooses a Talented Singer: "A Fated Encounter" Becomes the Theme Song of "Innocence Lost"

As a young female singer of the Chinese People's Liberation Army Opera Group, Li Fei is deeply loved by the industry and the public alike. She was not only invited as the only female actor from Asia to perform in the famous American musical "Cats" but also hailed by her fans as "China's Sarah Brightman". Her explosive power when singing, her graceful and natural presence on stage, and her comprehensive skills in interpreting songs have left many people inside and outside the circle captivated. Especially her singing style that blends opera bel canto, musical theater, and pop, has attracted a world-renowned tenor, Jose Carreras, to form a mentor-student relationship with her.

Li Fei, who has sung theme songs for several TV dramas and films, collaborated with Zhao Yanguozhang in a natural progression. Since both are from Hebei province, Zhao Yanguozhang had already been deeply impressed by Li Fei's musical talent and singing skills during their previous encounters, while Li Fei was extremely admiring of Zhao Yanguozhang's ingenious conception in filmmaking, which earned him the title of "eccentric genius." After listening to "A Fated Encounter," written by Yang Yang and Xiao Zhuang and passionately sung by Li Fei, Zhao Yanguozhang was deeply moved. It seemed as if this grand and passionate song was tailor-made for "Innocence Lost," and he promptly decided to adopt it as the theme song of the film. Li Fei reinterpreted the song to make it more in line with the theme of the film, blending it seamlessly with the movie, making people marvel at the artistic convergence between "A Fated Encounter" and "Innocence Lost."

Li Fei Performs "A Fated Encounter" Live, Fully Promoting the Film

Since the film will hit major theaters on the 23rd of this month, this premiere served as the final warm-up before its official release. At the premiere of "Innocence Lost," Li Fei's invited performance of the theme song "A Fated Encounter" became one of the highlights of the day. Dressed elegantly, Li Fei appeared poised and composed, captivating the entire audience with her first note, immersing them in her musical world. Her voice was like a silent yet vivid movie, taking people into the world of hope and despair, helplessness and powerlessness, loss and confusion depicted in "Innocence Lost." As soon as the song ended, she received unanimous praise from everyone present. It seems that Li Fei's full-hearted efforts to promote the film have already opened a promising start for its successful release.
