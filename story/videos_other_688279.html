Man Establishes 4,000-People Rare Blood Type Alliance to Provide Rescue

by r46dt3ifhj on 2012-03-03 09:26:49

Xiangtan Correspondence (Reported by Zhu Yanhuang) - In the movie "Spiderman," high school student Peter gains spider-like special abilities after being bitten by a spider, allowing him to scale walls and save people as Spiderman. Xu Guodong, 34, is an ordinary employee at Xianggang Steel Mill. By chance, he discovered that he has a rare blood type known as "Panda Blood," transforming himself into "Panda Hero" and saving lives with his friends. Yesterday, the reporter listened to his story.

Empathy leads to the creation of the "Hug Group"

In 2002, when Xu Guodong was 25 years old, he was still living a routine life of clocking in and out at Xianggang Steel Mill, not feeling any different from others. It wasn't until a blood test that he realized how unique he was; the results showed that his blood type was Rh-negative B. This blood type is extremely rare in the Asian population, accounting for only about 0.3%, commonly referred to as "Panda Blood."

Xu Guodong couldn't help but feel anxious, "What if I urgently need blood in the future? Can I get timely blood supply?" He began consciously befriending people around him with rare blood types, exchanging phone numbers, and adding them as QQ friends.

As his QQ friends increased, Xu Guodong had the idea of forming a team of rare blood type individuals. He tried gathering these friends into three QQ groups covering Changsha, Zhuzhou, and Xiangtan, naming it the "Rare Blood Type Alliance," with a membership reaching 200.

Xu Guodong's father was also a rare blood type individual, with Rh-negative O blood type. At the end of 2007, his father underwent multiple chemotherapy sessions due to leukemia, losing his hematopoietic function and urgently needing fresh blood. However, because there were limited members of Rh-negative O type in the alliance and those few were scattered across different regions of Hunan Province, far apart from each other. By the time several donors finally arrived, his father was almost gone.

This incident made Xu Guodong realize that the team he established needed to grow stronger. He organized members to go onto the streets and into communities to widely gather more like-minded individuals' information. With everyone's efforts, QQ groups named "Rare Blood Type Home" based on various cities and prefectures within Hunan Province were successively established, and some provincial groups like Qinghai and Hebei also sprang up like mushrooms after rain.

The first cross-provincial rescue draws significant attention

On October 22 this year, an urgent assistance request from Ms. Zhang in Jiangsu appeared multiple times in the QQ group of the Chinese Rare Blood Type Alliance. The content roughly stated that her younger brother urgently needed Rh-negative O type blood for support, and the situation was quite critical.

After verification, Ms. Zhang's younger brother was named Zhang Chuanfu, who was 21 years old. On the morning of October 16, while working at a construction site in Suqian, Jiangsu, Zhang Chuanfu was suddenly electrocuted by 380 volts of high voltage electricity. After being rushed to the Ren Ci Trauma Hospital in Xuzhou, Jiangsu Province, it was confirmed that his burn area reached seventy percent, and he urgently needed a second escharotomy and skin graft surgery. However, due to his rare blood type, the blood supply could not keep up, with a shortage of over 20,000 milliliters, requiring at least 70 people to donate blood.

The Rare Blood Type Alliance quickly spread this urgent message among its sub-groups and allied groups, receiving responses from nearly 20 members. Xu Guodong decided to assemble 10 people nearby to self-fund their trip to Xuzhou for the rescue mission, while other members would travel separately.

After a 16-hour journey, the team arrived in Xuzhou at noon on October 29. As soon as they got off the train, they were warmly welcomed by local media, blood stations, and volunteers. At the train station exit, they even encountered rare blood type individuals who had rushed from all over the country. That day, they collectively donated 2,400 milliliters of blood to Zhang Chuanfu.

This loving rescue operation drew attention from many media outlets, including CCTV. After returning to Hunan the next day, some alliance members continued to rush to Xuzhou to donate blood. Xu Guodong said that this large-scale cross-provincial rescue was the first for the alliance.

Building a website to benefit more people

Nowadays, every time Xu Guodong opens QQ, he receives hundreds of applications to join the group. So far, the alliance has 19 provincial groups, 11 other provincial domain groups, and 11 mixed groups, with over 4,000 members.

At the beginning of this year, the "Rare Blood Type Alliance" was officially renamed the "Chinese Rare Blood Type Public Welfare Alliance," expanding its services to include emergency backup and rescue for rare blood types, publicizing knowledge about rare blood types and voluntary blood donation, promoting bone marrow stem cell donation services, assisting socially disadvantaged groups, public welfare scholarships, and other volunteer activities.

Xu Guodong revealed that they are currently building a website for the Chinese Rare Blood Type Public Welfare Alliance (http://www.zxgdl.org). "In the future, everyone can pass on information more quickly and conveniently through the website. Social assistance will be more timely."
