A Heartfelt Plea from the Founder of Syncplicity: Why is Dropbox More Popular than Other Similar Products?

by xdash on 2011-02-22 19:42:58

# Translator's Preface: This article was originally a Q&A post on Quora. The original title of the post was "Why is Dropbox more popular than other tools with similar functionality?" Interestingly, one of the respondents was none other than the co-founder of Syncplicity, a competitor to Dropbox. His response contains years of thoughtful reflection and some insider anecdotes about the industry. Below is a complete translation.

As a co-founder of Syncplicity (a file-syncing tool similar to Dropbox), the question "Why is Dropbox more popular than other similar products?" has haunted me for many years.

We launched our product just weeks after Dropbox released theirs. We had multi-folder synchronization and read-only sync features, and our team was older and more experienced than Dropbox's. I was proud to see our service mature and begin monetizing, but clearly, Dropbox was still more successful than us. Why?

Here are some reasons I’ve thought about:

Before officially launching their product, Dropbox created a geeky video that showcased an incomplete, flawed prototype of their product.

The video demonstrated a binary difference synchronization (binary diff sync) of an image. Binary diff sync is impressive, but it only works with uncompressed file formats—so it was limited to BMP bitmap files. Despite this seemingly absurd focus, the video spread incredibly fast, and Dropbox quickly overshadowed us in terms of reputation.

At the time, we didn’t even think of doing something similar and were baffled by it: Users wouldn’t actually use this feature—they would mostly be syncing compressed files like JPEGs. Who the hell would sync Windows bitmaps?

Next, let’s consider how they created such a buzz-inducing launch.
