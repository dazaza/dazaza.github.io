Please stay away from the "danger zone" - 12 wrong car maintenance methods

In recent years, with the development of the economy, family incomes have been increasing continuously. Whether we are walking in crowded cities or country roads, cars are always within our line of sight. They are forever the brightest teams on the road. Are you also one of those enviable members? I believe that people who own cars should have some methods for maintaining their vehicles to make them last longer and reduce repair costs. However, car maintenance is a deep subject, and if not careful, it can have counterproductive effects, turning cleverness into folly. Let's take a look at these 12 wrong car maintenance methods. Are they what you consider to be your exclusive maintenance secrets? Do any of these apply to you? Let's review them together...

First, washing the car like taking a bath - harms the air conditioning

As summer temperatures rise, dust increases, and the body of the car gets dirty more easily. The frequency of car washes also increases. Many car owners hope to clean their cars as thoroughly as they clean themselves when taking a bath. But here's something to note: it's important to keep the exterior of the car air conditioning dry. If the car air conditioning accidentally gets wet, it will affect its lifespan.

Actually, washing the car requires careful protection. Otherwise, water might get inside certain parts, especially the car air conditioning, which could affect the working life of the car.

Second, over-tightening bolts - permanent deformation

There are many bolts and nuts used to connect fasteners on a car, which should have sufficient pre-tension. However, they shouldn't be tightened too much. If tightened excessively, on one hand, it will cause the connected parts to permanently deform under external force; on the other hand, it will cause the bolt to stretch permanently, reducing the pre-tension, and may even result in slippage or breakage.

Third, fan belt too tight - high bearing load

Summer temperatures are too high, and some drivers think that increasing the tightness of the fan belt can improve the engine cooling effect, so they increase the tightness of the fan belt, causing it to be too tight. This practice is incorrect. The fan belt should be kept moderately tight because if it's too tight, it will increase the bearing load, accelerate wear, increase power consumption, and at the same time, it will bend the water pump shaft, elongate and deform the belt, shortening its lifespan.

Fourth, imported tires - unsuitable

Some people particularly emphasize "imported" when buying tires. A fatal flaw of newly launched foreign tires for domestic users is that they are unsuitable. The smooth roads in Europe differ greatly from the conditions of Beijing's second and third ring roads. Tires suitable for European roads perform poorly in Beijing, especially in terms of side impact resistance, showing clear signs of incompatibility, just like wearing Italian brand shoes on gravel roads. Sides of the tires inevitably bulge. After some foreign brands produce tires jointly in China, they add an extra layer of fabric to the tire sides according to domestic road conditions, greatly enhancing the side impact resistance. Jointly produced tires perform well enough to replace imported ones.

Fifth, too much oil - causes problems

When there is insufficient oil in the engine sump, the bearings and journal will experience poor lubrication due to lack of oil, accelerating wear and possibly leading to burnt bearings and shafts accidents. However, if there is too much oil, the crankshaft handle and large end of the connecting rod will create violent stirring while the engine is running, not only increasing internal power loss in the engine but also increasing the amount of oil splashing onto the cylinder walls, causing oil burning and exhaust problems. Therefore, the oil in the engine sump should be controlled between the upper and lower marks on the dipstick.

Sixth, circular waxing - poor results

Many people habitually wax their cars in a circular motion, which is an incorrect method. The correct way to wax is in straight lines, alternating horizontal and vertical lines, and finally applying in the direction of water flow. This can reduce the halo effect on the car paint surface.

Seventh, washing the car under the scorching sun - damages the paint?

Many private car owners like to wash their cars under the scorching sun, thinking that this will quickly dry the water on the body. In fact, this is wrong. The convex lens effect formed by water droplets under the scorching sun creates localized high-temperature areas on the top layer of the car paint, and over time, the paint will lose its luster. Waxing at this time can also cause uneven body color. Therefore, washing and waxing the car should be done in a shaded area. If this cannot be guaranteed, it is best to do it on cloudy days or in the early morning or evening on sunny days.

Ninth, new batteries not charged - shortens lifespan

The first charge of a battery is called the initial charge, which has a great impact on the battery's lifespan. If it is not charged, adding "water" and using it directly will result in low battery capacity and shortened lifespan; direct charging will also shorten the lifespan. Usually, the initial charge of a battery is done after adding electrolyte, using a small current to charge for about an hour before installation and use.

Tenth, coolant temperature too low - wears out the engine

During hot weather, some drivers try to prevent the engine temperature from getting too high by keeping the coolant temperature as low as possible. Some drivers even remove the thermostat to achieve cooling purposes, but these practices are wrong. Car engines are afraid of both heat and cold. If the coolant temperature is too low, it will cause poor fuel combustion, increased fuel consumption, increased wear, increased viscosity of the oil, and reduced engine power. Studies show that when the car is driving and the coolant temperature is between 40-50 degrees Celsius, the engine wear increases by 60%-80%, power decreases by 25%, and fuel consumption increases by 8%-10%. Therefore, the temperature of the engine coolant is not necessarily better when lower, and it should generally be controlled between 80-90 degrees Celsius.

Eleventh, quickly adding water when the radiator "boils" - cracks the cylinder

Some drivers, upon seeing the radiator "boiling", worry that the engine temperature will rise further and immediately turn off the engine to add water. This practice is wrong, as it can cause the cylinder head to crack due to sudden cooling. If the radiator "boils", the general correct method is: immediately stop the car and keep the engine running. After the coolant temperature drops, then turn off the engine. At this point, if the coolant quantity is insufficient, water should be added slowly to prevent the cylinder head from cracking due to sudden cooling.

Twelfth, long-term non-use or short-distance driving - damages components

Some people, although part of the car-owning crowd, "don't dare" to drive regularly and only drive during holidays. Actually, this kind of car usage is very harmful to the car. The surfaces of the engine and transmission components will rust due to constant exposure to air, and the battery will lose its lifespan due to continuous natural discharge. The best method is to drive once every few days for thirty to forty minutes. Additionally, always driving short distances also harms the car. The car moves frequently but doesn't go far, which is an important reason for damaging the car.
