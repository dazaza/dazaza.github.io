When the object to be weighed or the loaded vehicle is placed on the load platform, under the force of gravity, the load cell transmits the force to the weight sensor, causing deformation in the elastic body of the sensor. The strain gauge bridge attached to the elastic body loses balance and outputs an electrical signal proportional to the weight value, which is amplified by a linear amplifier. The signal is then converted to a digital signal by an A/D converter. The microprocessor (CPU) of the instrument processes the weight signal and directly displays the weight data. Whether it's a scale or a weighbridge, the basic principle is to use a sensor (force sensor) to convert the weight of the object into an electrical signal, send it to the conditioning circuit, A/D converter, and computational circuit (MCU), and then convert it into a digital display.
