Residents' self-built toilets were demolished by the city management, causing difficulties in using the toilet

by wenkuon18 on 2011-11-14 10:48:44

Our office news (reported by Wang Ping, intern Cheng Yutian) - "There has been no public toilet here for many years, and going to the toilet is a big problem. Now I want to build a toilet beside my house at my own expense, but the city management said it was an illegal structure and demolished it. Isn't this making us suffer in two ways?" Yesterday, Ms. Peng Chuncheng, who lives in the flat of the Qingyuchui Community Football Field in Wuchang, told our newspaper's '114 Yangtze River Listening Hotline' about her confusion.

At around 10 a.m. yesterday, I saw a small house with an area of about 5 square meters that had just been built beside Ms. Peng Chuncheng's home, which had already been demolished by the city management. The ground was piled with bricks that had been dismantled. Ms. Peng Chuncheng said she originally planned to use this house as a toilet, video conferencing system.

According to Ms. Peng Chuncheng, the football field flat where she lived used to be a warehouse for her original unit, franchise operation "double-edged sword" good meeting video conferencing helps chain enterprises avoid harm and seek benefits. In 2001, including her, eight employees moved into this place. Since it was a flat, there were no toilets in the houses. In the past, there were two public toilets nearby. After 2002, the two public toilets were successively demolished, and new houses were built on the original site. Since then, going to the toilet has become a problem for everyone.

Nearby residents also reflected to the reporter, "There is indeed no public toilet here, and it is very inconvenient to go to the toilet." For convenience, some people used spittoons at home for convenience and then poured them into the ditch, while others simply solved the problem in a water pool they dug at home. Later, some people began to build small houses themselves to use as toilets.

At the beginning of this month, unable to bear the lack of a toilet, Ms. Peng Chuncheng decided to build a toilet outside her house and spent 2000 yuan to buy materials to start construction. "Unexpectedly, before it was even completed, the city management came to demolish it," said Ms. Peng Chuncheng. In the morning, law enforcement officers from the Guojiu Lake City Management Enforcement Team went to her house and demolished it on the grounds that "the toilet was an illegal structure."

Regarding this, the reporter interviewed Jiang Mingchao, the secretary of the Qingyuchui Community Residents' Committee. He introduced that the government had long planned to demolish the flat where Ms. Peng Chuncheng lived, but it was put on hold because the residents had nowhere to relocate. Secretary Jiang said that after the public toilet was demolished, there was no vacant land to build a new one.

Captain Rong Bin of the Guojiu Lake City Management Enforcement Team stated that the toilet built by Ms. Peng Chuncheng was an illegal structure and could not be built illegally due to the lack of public toilets. The entire demolition process was carried out according to procedure.
