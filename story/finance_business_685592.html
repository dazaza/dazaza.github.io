Submitting your site (registering webpages) submitting a location to any search engine is quite simple. On the homepage of the search engine, find the "add your site" or "submit URL" link. Just follow the instructions to add your website. Some search engines will let you submit all the pages you want indexed, such as Altavista. Some require you to submit the URL of just one page of your entire website, and all the remaining pages will be searched. This means that the search engine will automatically add the remaining pages. Google is an example of this. Submitting to directories like Yahoo! can be somewhat tedious. Since they keep webpages in separate categories (directories), you need to find the best category under which your site will fit. Remember, these are human-operated. If you submit to an irrelevant category, it may be considered spam, and your site might be ignored. Getting indexed: now you know that submitting your site is not a big problem, but how sure are you that your site will be indexed? Indexing simply means getting a place for your site in the search engine's database. How do you find out if your site has been added to the search engine? It's easy. If you search with your URL or domain name as the keyword and the search results show up, then you are indexed by the search engine. Each search engine has its own policy (secret!) for accepting submitted URLs. Some search engines will take less time than others to list you. Be patient. Directories take more time because they require human intervention. It's a good practice to check after 30 minutes or 40 days if your site has been listed here.

Search Engine and Directory Website Promotion focusing on search engines. There are usually two types. A search engine is a software-compiled index (such as Google, Hotbot, etc.), or a human-compiled directory (such as Yahoo!, dmoz, etc.). In other words, it is a huge database containing links to all websites submitted to them, including details about each website. The main difference between these two is that one is operated by humans while the other by spiders.

META Tags Announce all the details of your website to search engines. Hidden in the code of the website, they can provide different information to search engines such as keywords, descriptions, author, and more. Although these are available on any webpage, they are essential for promoting your site through search engines. Every search engine assigns different weightage to META tags. Therefore, optimizing website rankings requires knowing some interesting things when dealing with these search engines.

Preparing your website for submission before submitting your website to any search engine, you need to ensure it is ready. You may need to do some work on the webpage title, META tags, and some of the opening paragraphs based on that.

These robots are faster and distinguish differently compared to humans.
