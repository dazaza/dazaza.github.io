How to Properly Select a Gas Turbine Flow Meter

With the advancement of the "West-to-East Gas Transmission" project, the use of natural gas has become increasingly widespread. In the process of using natural gas, the gas turbine flow meter has become an indispensable measuring instrument. Below, the technical personnel of Jiangsu Aike Instrument Co., Ltd. will introduce the issues that should be considered in selecting, using, and maintaining gas turbine flow meters:

I. Selection

The selection of gas turbine flow meters should consider the following aspects:

1. Accuracy Class: Generally, the reason for choosing a turbine flow meter is its high accuracy. However, the higher the accuracy of the flow meter, the more sensitive it becomes to changes in on-site usage conditions. Therefore, careful consideration should be given to the choice of instrument accuracy from an economic perspective. For large-caliber gas pipelines used for trade settlement, it is cost-effective to invest more in the instruments. For occasions with smaller conveying volumes, medium-accuracy level flow meters are sufficient.

2. Flow Range: The selection of the flow range of a turbine flow meter has a significant impact on its accuracy and service life. Each caliber of the flow meter has a certain measurement range, and the selection of the flow meter caliber is also determined by the flow range. The principle of selecting the flow range is: the minimum flow during use should not be lower than the minimum measurable flow allowed by the instrument, and the maximum flow during use should not exceed the maximum measurable flow allowed by the instrument. For intermittent working conditions where the actual daily operating time of the instrument does not exceed 8 hours, choose 1.3 times the actual maximum flow as the upper limit of the flow range. For continuous working conditions where the actual daily operating time of the instrument is not less than 8 hours, choose 1.4 times the actual maximum flow as the upper limit of the flow range. The lower limit flow of the instrument should be 0.8 times the actual minimum flow.

3. Gas Density: For gas turbine flow meters, the influence of fluid properties mainly lies in gas density, which has a significant effect on the instrument coefficient, especially in the low-flow region. If the gas density changes frequently, corrective measures should be taken for the flow coefficient of the flow meter.

4. Pressure Loss: Try to select a turbine flow meter with minimal pressure loss. Because the smaller the pressure loss when the fluid passes through the turbine flow meter, the less energy consumed by the fluid from input to output pipelines, thus reducing the required total power, conserving energy, lowering transportation costs, and improving utilization rates. Someone once conducted an experiment showing that the main component affecting pressure loss is the pre-guide of the turbine flow meter. Choosing a semi-ellipsoidal pre-guide instead of a conical one can significantly reduce the pressure loss of the turbine flow meter.

5. Structural Form: (1) Preferably select a reverse-thrust type turbine flow meter for internal structure. Because within a certain flow range, the impeller of the reverse-thrust structure can float, eliminating axial contact points, avoiding end face friction and wear, thereby extending the service life of the bearing. (2) Select according to the pipeline connection method. There are two installation methods for flow meters: horizontal and vertical. Horizontal installation includes flange connection, threaded connection, and clamp connection. Medium calibers prefer flange connections; small calibers and high-pressure pipelines prefer threaded connections; clamp connections are only suitable for low-pressure small-medium pipe diameters; vertical installation only uses threaded connections. (3) Select according to environmental conditions, considering the effects of temperature and humidity. For natural gas measurement, choose an intrinsically safe explosion-proof turbine flow meter.

6. Bearings: The bearings of turbine flow meters generally have three types of materials: tungsten carbide, polytetrafluoroethylene, and carbon graphite. For natural gas measurement instruments, tungsten carbide material should be selected for the bearings.

These are the main aspects to consider during selection. Due to the wide variety of turbine flow meters and differences in product quality between manufacturers, try to gather as much information as possible about manufacturers and products, compare thoroughly before making a decision.

II. Use and Maintenance

To ensure the long-term normal operation of the gas turbine flow meter, it is necessary to regularly check the operating status of the flow meter, perform maintenance work, and promptly address any issues discovered.

1. Before putting the turbine flow meter into operation, set the instrument coefficient first. Carefully check to confirm that the flow meter wiring is correct and properly grounded before powering on.

2. Regularly clean, inspect, and recalibrate the flow meter. For flow meters equipped with lubricant or cleaning liquid injection ports, follow the instructions in the manual to inject lubricants or cleaning liquids regularly to maintain the good operation of the impeller.

3. Monitor the status of the display instrument and evaluate the readings. Address any abnormalities immediately.

4. Keep the filter unobstructed. If the filter is clogged with impurities, this can be judged by an increase in the difference in pressure readings at the inlet and outlet. Remove blockages promptly; otherwise, the flow will be severely reduced.

5. For large-volume trade settlement measurements, to ensure the accuracy of the flow meter, it must be calibrated regularly. On-site online calibration equipment or portable calibration devices should be provided. Although the initial investment is substantial, considering long-term economic benefits, it is worthwhile.
