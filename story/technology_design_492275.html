Among the ball

by lieencq09 on 2011-09-20 21:15:40

Taboo one: Being an unapproachable beauty

At the ball, everyone is happy and you look like you're there to collect a debt with a cold face, ignoring everyone and just eating and drinking on your own; or because of poor social skills, you retreat to the wall like a wallflower, which goes against the etiquette expected at a ball. Attending a ball is about meeting new friends and expanding your social circle. A responsible host should take new guests around the room to introduce them to others; if you can't think of any topics, it's fine to start by talking about yourself.

Taboo two: Talking non-stop to someone

The host invites more than just one or two guests. While there's no need to flit around like a butterfly, you shouldn't stick to one person either. After greeting a guest upon their arrival, the host should introduce them to others in a timely manner to avoid making other guests feel neglected. Any deep personal conversations can be left for a more appropriate time.

Taboo three: Lack of self-control in drinking

While alcohol can make people cheerful and enliven the atmosphere of the ball, drinking too much, whether it leads to nonsense or vomiting, turns a joyful occasion into a farce. As a host, it’s a good idea to have some snacks and milk next to the drinks to help protect the guests' stomachs. As a guest, you should know your limits. Remember, being slightly tipsy can be charming, but embarrassing yourself when drunk is utterly shameful.

Taboo four: Ignoring table manners

For a home ball, it's best to prepare some self-service snacks to satisfy hunger. If attendees go hungry and then stand in front of the table eating indiscriminately like refugees fearing the food will run out, you might as well go to the supermarket and buy a cartload of food to eat at home instead. Whether you want to wear pajamas or raincoats, standing or lying down while eating is up to you and your comfort, so why ruin your dining appearance in front of everyone at the ball?

Attending a ball is about meeting new friends and expanding your social circle. A responsible host should take new friends around the room to introduce them to others; if you can't think of any topics, it's fine to start by talking about yourself.
