Weight loss exercises suitable for women friends

by apple6m19 on 2012-03-02 15:41:33

As our material life improves, health preservation has become a kind of lifestyle. Weight loss should also be about health preservation; we cannot just diet or take too many medicines. We need to regulate ourselves, do more exercise, lose weight through exercise, and only then can we lose weight healthily. Do you know which health-preserving weight-loss exercises are suitable for you? The 39 Weight Loss website introduces a few types of weight-loss exercises suitable for women.

Skating: Whether it's roller skating in summer or ice skating in winter, skating rinks are everywhere now, and I'm sure you can find one near your home. This sport has no age limit; it not only trains our coordination but also strengthens our leg muscles, giving the whole body a workout. According to measurements, an hour of skating burns 420 calories, so go give it a try.

Cycling: Cycling can be said to be a daily companion, closely related to our lives. You cycle every day when buying groceries or walking short distances. This sport is very practical and close to life, without wasting too much of your time. Cycling can strengthen your thighs, relax your knee joints, normalize blood circulation, thereby achieving weight loss effects.

Jogging/Walking: No sport is more popular than jogging and walking. It doesn't require a big investment but can bring great benefits. If you love sports or love losing weight, it's best to choose running. If you don't have time, I suggest you place your morning exercise on the way to work, preferably walking instead of taking transportation if possible.

Golf: Golf is a noble sport, but it is also very suitable for women. First, the venue is spacious and beautiful, and appropriate exercise can relax your mood.

Horse Riding: For people in inland areas, horse riding might seem distant, often seen on TV with scenes of galloping horses, feeling extremely relaxed and graceful. This sport is suitable for women under 40 because it carries some risks. It can train your agility and coordination, allowing your whole body muscles to get exercised, especially the leg muscles.

Volleyball: With the success of our women's volleyball team at the Olympics, more and more women have started liking volleyball, gradually turning it into an art form, though it requires a team. This sport demands high jumping ability, and its effects on exercising arm and abdominal muscles are particularly noticeable. At the same time, it greatly helps improve your agility.
