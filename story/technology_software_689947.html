Starry Sky Change: A tourist wins a big prize in Lijiang's scenic spot ticket lottery, but without the ticket stub, it's difficult to claim the 200,000 yuan bonus.

by sznstejx3q3 on 2012-03-04 17:53:42

"Mr. Hui, your real-name ticket for the trip to Lijiang Ancient Town in November last year has won a grand prize of 200,000 yuan! Please bring your ticket stub to Lijiang before March 6th to claim your prize!" On February 16th, Mr. Hui Jinbie, who works in Chen仓 District of Baoji City, received such a phone call. After verification from multiple sources, it was confirmed that this was true. However, "the tour guide didn't give me the ticket at all," as the redemption deadline approaches, Mr. Hui is extremely anxious.

The tourist only learned about the prize after receiving the call. "I didn't believe it at first, but after contacting the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau by phone, I found out that it was indeed true! But they told me that I must have the ancient city maintenance ticket stub to claim the prize. I was immediately confused: I never knew there were maintenance fee tickets and a lottery, and the tour guide didn't give us the stubs."

According to Mr. Hui's introduction, on November 3rd last year, he participated in a tour group organized by a travel agency in Baoji to visit Kunming, Dali, Lijiang and other places. The contract clearly stated: the group fee includes the entrance fees for scenic spots, ancient city maintenance fees, etc. On November 6th, after arriving in Lijiang, the local "Sunshine Holiday Travel Agency" took over and was fully responsible for the Lijiang itinerary.

"I joined a tour group, so I didn't pay much attention to ticket-related matters, which were all arranged by the tour guide. Not knowing about the lottery, we didn't ask the tour guide for the tickets." Mr. Hui Jinbie said. Other tourists in the tour group also said they did not see the ticket stubs.

The voucher indicates that both the ticket and ID card are essential. On February 27th, Mr. Hui Jinbie once again set off for Lijiang. Liu Zheng'an, the tour guide who was responsible for the group in Lijiang, claimed that all the tickets had been handed over to the tourists. The travel agency where he worked also stated that they would never withhold tourists' tickets. Mr. Hui Jinbie went to the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau and found his winning ticket stub. However, the staff informed him that the claimant must provide the purchased ticket and personal ID to claim the prize; neither could be missing.

The head of the travel agency in Baoji said, "After the tour group arrived in Lijiang, it was handed over to the local travel agency, and it no longer had anything to do with us. Since Mr. Hui registered for the tour with us, considering this, I personally accompanied him to Lijiang to verify the matter, hoping to resolve it as soon as possible."

Mr. Hui Jinbie said, "Winning should be a happy thing, but now I am extremely depressed. I can't understand, since the tickets are under a real-name system and the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau also called to notify me to claim the prize, I hope the relevant departments can understand my actual situation."

In terms of claiming the prize, among the 10 winners, only 3 people have claimed their prizes. On March 2nd, when contacted, tour guide Liu Zheng'an also expressed his grievances, "The tickets are under a real-name system. After verifying, I let the tourists take back their tickets. Keeping those tickets myself would be useless anyway."

Director He Shiyong of the Lijiang Ancient Town Protection Bureau stated: "The opportunity to win a big prize by paying the Lijiang Ancient Town maintenance fee has been reported on our official website and major media outlets in Yunnan, and the rules for claiming the prize are very clear. Since Mr. Hui does not meet the redemption conditions, we cannot process the prize collection for him. Currently, both the travel agency and the tourists insist that they did not take the winning ticket, which is an issue between the travel agency and the tourists, and we cannot intervene."

Some media reports mentioned that regular lotteries for ancient city maintenance fee tickets were a precedent set by Lijiang domestically, aimed at minimizing tourists evading the ancient city maintenance fee. It is understood that among the 10 lucky tourists drawn nationwide, only 3 people have claimed their prizes so far, while others have also indicated that they cannot find their ticket stubs.

Legal counsel suggests pursuing legal procedures. Attorney Fang Ligang from Shaanxi Ligang Law Firm said that first, after paying the corresponding tourism fees, tourists should receive the corresponding tourism services, including the right to obtain tickets, and the travel company should also present evidence of delivering the tickets; secondly, since the tickets are under a real-name system and the staff also called to notify the winner to claim the prize, it can be proven that Mr. Hui's winning information is genuine. "If Mr. Hui pursues legal action within the valid redemption period, the possibility of obtaining the prize is still quite high."

Lijiang tourism notice: In January 2012, Lijiang City decided to hold a lottery event for the Lijiang Ancient Town Maintenance Fee Tickets every January 6th and July 6th. On January 6th, the organizer of the Lijiang Ancient Town Maintenance Fee Ticket Lottery drew 10 winners from 1,991,584 ticket numbers, each winning 200,000 yuan in cash, with the redemption deadline being March 6th.
