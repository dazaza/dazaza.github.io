My husband and I got married out of love. Two years ago, he had a lover. From the time I found out to now, he has vowed to break up with her for no less than ten times. I'm at a loss!

by lk0cswu on 2009-08-18 14:05:03

In the past two years, I have experienced emotional encounters that I have never encountered in my life. I am a woman full of feelings and sensitivity, delicate. My husband is smart and down-to-earth. In my view, his involvement in an extramarital affair is almost like a fairy tale, but I also commented that once he was involved, it would not be a casual fling, he would be very committed and persistent.

They have been in their extramarital love affair for two years now. I found out last March, and I felt extremely desolate inside. I would involuntarily shed tears while walking on the street, always revolving around this question: how could he betray me? Did he think of me when he betrayed me?

When I first learned of it, he did not apologize to me. He just gave me two options: one, forgive him, and we start over. Two, if I can't get over this hump, then seek new happiness. In our communication, I clearly felt that he hoped I wouldn't leave him. To be honest, I couldn't let go of him either. Not to mention the affection of more than ten years of marriage, even those details given to me made it impossible for me to break up with him. It was precisely these touching details that created something eternal.

However, his promise was not fulfilled, they are still in touch, just more secretly than before. I suddenly felt that despite more than ten years of marital affection, it might not be enough to match his current second spring. I was so distressed that only physical pain could relieve my inner pain. When he saw me like this, he decisively decided to end things with her. That woman was also in great pain, threatening to die. He clearly said to that woman: "Seeing her like this, my heart is broken. Seeing you like this, I am just sad."

This time, as "Xun Brother" would say, "my heart feels indescribably big."

However, after a few days, when that woman, who was receiving intravenous drips, very weakly told him she couldn't live without him, his determination instantly collapsed.

Afterwards, I found that whenever he was determined to end things, when we were calm, they continued their relationship again. This happened repeatedly no less than ten times.

My mentality has been fluctuating all the time. The journey of the mind I went through during this period cannot be expressed in words. I don't know how to face him, face the family, face emotions, face marriage, face life.

What should I do?

I think your husband will come back sooner or later. You shouldn't push too hard. Naturally, he will increasingly feel that he doesn't deserve you, and then return to you. I hope you two can be happy. Nowadays, men either never cheat or it's hard for them not to! If he knows to turn back, that's good.

Let go. Don't torment yourself anymore. Didn't you give him a chance? But matters of the heart, outsiders really find it hard to comment on anything. Regardless of who's right or wrong. I'm just speaking from your perspective, based on my own thoughts. Letting go might be the best for you. Being alone can be wonderful too! Life without him may seem unbearable, but obviously, he is no longer someone you can rely on. Tell yourself, I have no retreat, I can only survive alone. Be brave, be strong. Without him, you still have your parents, friends. You still have yourself.

Blessings to you! Believe that there are still many people who love you. You are a good woman worth loving.
