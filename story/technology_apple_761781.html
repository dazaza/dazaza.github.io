How Amazon, Google's Biggest Customer, Is Stealing the Search Business

by geekzhang on 2013-05-10 11:10:26

May 9, Beijing time, according to foreign media reports, when you want to search for something, you will definitely think of Google first. Just like when shopping online, you would think of Amazon first. They seem to provide completely different services, but is that really the case?Jeff Jordan doesn't think so. He is now one of the partners at Andreessen Horowitz, and previously served as the chairman of the board of PayPal.Recently, Jordan commented on an article on Fortune's website, stating that online shopping will become increasingly important for Google's revenue. Similarly, search is becoming more crucial for Amazon. Jordan said that Amazon uses vertical search methods, focusing on helping users find specific products. Currently, the most important search form on the site is entering keywords in the search toolbar. After users enter keywords and click search, they can immediately find the products they need. Moreover, these search results are ranked according to product relevance and conversion rates.Jordan said that the biggest difference in shopping on these two sites is that Amazon offers a much better user experience, not to mention its low prices, wide range of product choices, and fast delivery speeds. He said that if you buy things on Google, it might take more than an hour, whereas on Amazon, it only takes a minute.Although Google does not sell any physical goods, it has made a lot of money by selling online ads. So why should Google still pay enough attention to the shopping experience?Ironic as it may sound, the companies that have placed a large number of advertisements on Google actually sell their products on Amazon. If shoppers gradually transition to using Amazon's search function to purchase products, more and more merchants will likely withdraw their advertisements from Google because, in such a scenario, Google would become irrelevant.However, that said, Amazon currently does not have enough strength to snatch advertisers away from Google. A market report released earlier this year showed that in order to secure the best position for their ads on Google's search result pages, advertisers' spending in this area increased by 600%, and the click-through rates of these ads also increased significantly by three times.In addition, Google has increased its investment in physical commerce, such as offering same-day delivery services like Amazon, but only in the San Francisco Bay Area. For Google, building large warehouses and purchasing freight trucks like Amazon might not be the best way. However, there is no doubt that Google must improve the shopping experience for users on its own site.And now, what can offer some solace to Google, albeit with mixed feelings, is that the world's largest online retail store (Amazon) is also one of Google's largest advertisers.
