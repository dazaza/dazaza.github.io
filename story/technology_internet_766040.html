Meizu MX3 launch event: making a more user-friendly large-screen phone.

by anonymous on 2013-09-04 14:38:46

Last year, it was the bleak early winter with cold winds, this year, it's the transition between summer and autumn. Meizu once again headed north to release the MX3 smartphone at the Water Cube, marking the first move in the fierce domestic and international competition among smartphone manufacturers in September. Recalling the MX released at the end of December two years ago and the MX2 launched at the end of November last year, Meizu’s pace is accelerating.

This acceleration in rhythm and the shift in promotional marketing styles indicate that Meizu no longer wants to be a niche manufacturer.

Before the launch event, Meizu teased with "Think.BIG" copywriting and "a better large-screen phone," hinting that the focus of the MX3 would be on how to handle screens 5 inches and above.

Meizu MX3

Let us first summarize the configuration parameters of the MX3:

5.1-inch (resolution of 1800x1080) IPS screen supplied by Sharp and JDI, 415 PPI, color correction technology added Exynos Octa 1.6 GHz octa-core processor (4 A15 cores + 4 A7 cores) + PowerVR SGX 544MP3 GPU 2 GB RAM, operating frequency 800 MHz Internal storage options: 16/32/64/128 GB 8-megapixel rear camera with an F2.0 aperture, 2-megapixel front camera 2400 mAh Sony battery with new energy-saving technology NFC 3 microphones Flyme 3.0

As with the spy photos we received earlier, the Meizu MX3 continues the black front and white back panda design style of the MX and MX2, reducing the bezel width from 3.15 mm on the MX2 to 2.9 mm. To enhance the body strength, the metal frame has been retained. Remarkably, despite the increased size, the weight has been kept at the same level as the MX2, and it is slightly thinner. However, the thickness of 9.1 mm still doesn't stand out.

The MX3 inherits the design style of its predecessor with some changes, looking overall more rounded. In general, the MX3 remains a beautifully designed smartphone, standing out in the crowd of domestically produced phones which generally lack distinctive design language.

Moreover, for the reading and browsing experience that Meizu emphasizes, even with a larger screen, they have persisted with non-mainstream resolutions and aspect ratios.

As for specifications, Meizu previously said to forget about specs. Indeed, the MX3's configuration isn't surprising, falling within expectations. After all, which smartphone manufacturer today can produce a device with jaw-dropping specs? Anyway, configurations that match flagship smartphones like the Samsung Galaxy S4 are absolutely sufficient.

Additionally, the MX3 claims to use a new dual-path heat dissipation system and "extraordinarily good" antenna design. Can this solve the heating and signal issues that Meizu has been criticized for?

An 8-megapixel rear camera, compared to current flagship models, seems rather modest. However, on Meizu's forum, Li Nan cited the iPhone 4 and Atrix 4G, which use the same CMOS, explaining that high pixel count does not necessarily equate to high image quality—sensor, lens, ISP, software optimization, and screen all influence user evaluation of a phone's camera capabilities.

According to sample images presented at the event, performance in low-light and macro environments is impressive, offering a continuous shooting capability of 20 frames per second.

Sound quality is difficult to measure with parameters. Meizu uses an independent audio chip, reportedly achieving "almost" Hi-Fi levels. In collaboration with Swedish company Dirac, transient and phase correction technology has been applied to the MX3 and Meizu headphones, purportedly enhancing the listening experience, making the sound more authentic and improving bass performance. Sound quality could become an advantage for Meizu, which started as a music player manufacturer.

Flyme 3.0

Based on Android 4.2, the customized Flyme 3.0 improves the operating system, cloud services, and online application experiences. With the trend towards larger screen phones being irreversible, Flyme 3.0 considers the issue of "how to solve large screen interaction," incorporating more gesture operations. Keeping up with modern design trends, Flyme 3.0 also adopts more flat design elements.

At the launch event, Li Nan compared the performance of the MX3 with other large-screen phones, summarizing that other large-screen phones are difficult to operate single-handedly.

For this reason, Flyme 3.0 has made improvements in interaction and visuals. The main interaction improvements include:

smartbar 2.0, easier large-screen interaction, extending finger reach More side-swipe menus supported, sub-items concentrated in the middle for easy clicking, more card-like Lower pull-to-pause, pulling down lists to hover in the center of the screen Gesture operations, swipe up to return, direct access to the home screen from lock screen Swipe to switch tabs, third-party apps adapt to the screen

The main improvements in new feature additions include:

Desktop apps can be dragged down, breaking the 20-item limit, finally allowing browser dragging Contact search and recent calls integrated together Calculator can float over other apps, notes support voice input, added drawing board function Camera adds 13 real-time filters Documents can be directly dragged

This year, Meizu’s new thoughts and practices regarding devices have been to make phones more social.

Through system-level integration, users can log in with one click to synchronize with multiple social services. "Ultimately, smartphones should become a key. They are convenient enough and secure enough."

For Meizu, the phone is a transparent medium for online services, enabling users to "simply know the latest information and respond without thinking about which app to open, without starting many background services, and without repeatedly downloading information."

This year, after a dispute with China Mobile due to custom phone reasons, Meizu's corporate character became a focal point of discussion. Flyme's closed nature was similar to the previous Meizu, but there has been a significant change this time. Besides deep cooperation with Sina Weibo, Flyme integrates Alipay and LAKALA scenario payment functions, enabling in-message payments and contact transfers. Apart from the Meizu App Center, Meizu will collaborate with豌豆荚to bring 600,000 apps. In music, it chose to partner with Duomi Music, and in video, it partnered with Letv. It can be seen that a more open Meizu is coming.

Price-wise, the 16 GB version starts at 2499 yuan, 2699 yuan for 32 GB, 3099 yuan for 64GB, and 3999 yuan for 128 GB.

Here, I revisited an article written by He Cai last year after the MX2 launch event. He mentioned that Meizu's simple Southern spirit seemed to have to take a step back in this noisy era. There are too many orators in this era and too few good craftsmen.

Meizu's craftsmanship spirit stems from Huang Zhang, but such stubbornness struggles to align with the times. Therefore, we see Meizu learning to become an orator.
