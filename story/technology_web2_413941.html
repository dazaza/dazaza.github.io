**Sharing Experience of Outsourcing in C#.NET Software Projects [From a Contractor to an Employer]**

In my daily life, I have about 300-500 programmer friends on QQ. Sometimes, when I want to outsource a task, it can be quite headache-inducing due to several reasons:

1. It's difficult to clearly describe which functional parts need to be outsourced. For example, downloading free .NET video tutorials.

2. It's hard to clearly define the level of completion required for satisfaction.

3. It's challenging to specify how deep or detailed the work needs to be before payment is made.

4. It’s difficult to find developers who are efficient, communicate well, have time available, and possess high skill levels.

If someone were willing to pay 1 million yuan for completing a single page, outsourcing would be incredibly easy. However, the key issue is that budgets are often limited. Within this limited budget, one wants to achieve the best possible results while finding a developer who can extrapolate from examples, understand instructions quickly, and doesn’t require repeated explanations. Such developers are hard to come by.

Timely acceptance and confirmation of completed work are also difficult because people who outsource tasks are often very busy themselves. As a result, even after the work is done, timely testing and acceptance aren't always possible. This often leads to delays in true acceptance, and some errors may not be discovered or corrected in time.

The quality of outsourced tasks is often poor. Many contractors adopt an attitude of just getting by, treating the task as something they merely need to complete rather than taking ownership of it. They don't test the work thoroughly, leading to awkward situations where we, as employers, not only pay for their efforts but also end up providing free guidance and training. Ha ha!

Since I am not a robot, a god, or someone with six arms, when work gets too overwhelming or I’m not skilled in certain areas, I have no choice but to outsource these tasks to suitable individuals. Sometimes, I also think: Is life all about making money? If I've earned enough, why not let others make some money from me? It's also a chance for me to rest and enjoy the services provided by others. Following the principle that money cannot be taken to the grave, I've started outsourcing various daily tasks to netizens for compensation, reducing my workload while allowing other programmers to earn from us older coders. After all, if there's extra money in the wallet, why not use it to purchase the fruits of our younger programmer brothers' labor?

In the past month, I outsourced the creation of a homepage effect and paid 1,000 yuan as a fee. I feel quite satisfied with the outcome; it was worth the expense. The overall software effect has improved significantly, and I'm pleased with it. I’d like to share this ultimate version of the C/S configuration module for general permission management. I don't plan to make any major changes now since the program is already stable enough.

Although 1,000 yuan isn't much, considering the workload, it was only for one page. Moreover, most of the functions were already developed; it was mainly about refining the effects. So, the compensation wasn't insignificant.

Next, I will focus on improving the B/S workflow and permission configuration management sections. Once these are done, it will fulfill a long-held software dream of mine—creating a well-known software design and development auxiliary component for the Chinese public that can be reused by many people.

Friends who are energetic and have specific skills are welcome to add my QQ: 252056973. I frequently outsource tasks to share with everyone.

I aim to take permission management and workflow management to the limits of my ability. A person can only excel at a few things.
