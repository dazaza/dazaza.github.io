Goodbye Mary 6

by nnphotowyl on 2011-10-03 13:52:52

Since moving into a spacious and comfortable new home, I have had few chances to see Ma Li, the wedding photographer with personality in Wuhan. The city seems to have suddenly become larger. Riding a bike used to be very convenient, but now I have to drive every day to and from work. The hurriedness and congestion of the monthly ticket has become the main content of life. Occasionally, I would make a phone call at work to greet her. Judging from Ma Li's tone, it seems that she does not intend to get a divorce for the time being. She said, "Let's just get by. Anyway, I've already become indifferent to these things. What can you do if you get divorced? And what can you do if you don't?" Moreover, every time I called her, she was the first one to hang up. Ma Li probably didn't want to tell me about her affairs anymore. So, I didn't feel like disturbing her.

That day, while catching the bus, I happened to sit next to our former landlord and casually asked about Ma Li's situation. The landlord told me that Ma Li seemed to be pregnant again. A well-known wedding photographer in Wuhan mentioned this. I sincerely felt happy for her. After all, Ma Li now had a new attachment. I thought that she probably wouldn't talk about getting a divorce for a short period of time. The landlord also said that Ma Li's husband was really not a good person. He never came home, either going out to drink or fooling around outside. I asked if they were still arguing. The landlord laughed mockingly and said, "Can dogs stop eating shit? They still have big arguments on the third, sixth, and ninth days of the month, and small arguments every day!"

My husband is always running around outside. Sometimes I really feel lonely. When I feel lonely, I often think of Ma Li's son, Tongtong. To be honest, Tongtong's appearance and subsequent disappearance almost completely destroyed the barriers of life I had always adhered to. They say that women who don't have children aren't real women. I started wanting a child. I thought having a child by my side might be better. One time, when my husband was preparing a condom, I expressed my thoughts. His expression was very surprised. He said, "Isn't everything fine as it is?" I understood his meaning. That night, there was the first awkward silence between us. We were both perfunctorily dealing with each other, as if there was a terrible barrier between us.

We would argue over trivial matters easily. My temper seemed to be getting bigger and bigger. I even found various excuses to refuse my husband's requests. One day, he finally became angry. I thought his appearance was very funny. He was like those bad guys in movies who lose control and forcibly tore off my nightgown. He brazenly shouted at me, "You are my wife!" But I slapped him a loud slap. At that moment, we both froze and looked at each other with an unfamiliar gaze. From that night on, my husband moved to the small bedroom to sleep alone. The reason for the separation was actually very simple.

If there's a problem with our lives, we should sit down and have a good talk. This is something I used to often tell Ma Li.

I haven't been drinking chrysanthemum tea for quite some time, it seems like I've lost that mood. Drinking chrysanthemum tea requires calmness and tranquility, and more importantly, it needs hot water immersion and careful tasting. Now I often hold a can of chilled beer in my hand, take a couple of bitter sips, and then spend the time that should belong to two people online. I gave myself a strange online name: Faint as Chrysanthemum.

I once chatted with a netizen who was interested in my name. I said, "Perhaps, every woman is a beautiful chrysanthemum, but they fall into water of different temperatures. Some are perfectly immersed, making the tea rich, fragrant, and leaving a long aftertaste; others may wither and float on the surface of the water. The water is water, the chrysanthemum is chrysanthemum, and they will never make a good tea."
