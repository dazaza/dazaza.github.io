From the user's perspective - Usability research on vertical search engines across industries

Source: http://hi.baidu.com/search2006 Author: Xiao Kenge

Do not make users spend a lot of time learning how to use your product.

Quickly help users find what they need, then leave. This is the responsibility of a search engine.

The quality of product usability design directly affects user satisfaction with the product.

So how are the existing vertical search engines doing?

Overall page effect analysis

As a search engine, the most important job is to search, and the service provided is search. Do not confuse the user. The homepage design should be intuitive and highlight the key points. Too much information not only gives users a sense of fullness, but also makes it difficult for them to distinguish the key points.

Why register? Why log in? Confusion arises.

The search function should be highlighted, and irrelevant things should not occupy too much space.

Function usability analysis

City switching function.

From the priority of the user's line of sight and the priority of the function, it is obviously reasonable to place this function in the upper left corner.

Search keyword guidance function.

For vertical search engines, unlike general search engines, inappropriate input of keywords can easily lead to search failure.

Users who cannot find enough results will easily lose confidence in this search engine and choose to leave. Therefore, how to guide users is a problem that user experience engineers need to study.

Qiku and Kuxun both use direct language prompts to guide users.

However, the author believes that using simple popular keywords instead of direct prompts may subconsciously guide users better.

Clearly indicating the range of keywords that should be entered effectively eliminates user confusion. But at the same time, it increases the number of operations.

How to balance the resulting contradictions is a problem that user experience engineers always need to solve.

Place the city in a prominent position so that users can see it at a glance.

Make guesses about user intent. This feature should be strengthened.

Search result filtering function

How do users find the information suitable for them among a large amount of data? Is the result filtering function simple and easy to use? According to the differences in each industry, there will be different design strategies. What are the filtering conditions that users care about the most? Arrange them according to priority and place them in the most prominent position. Use the simplest method to achieve the most complex functions.
