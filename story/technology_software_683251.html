China encourages media groups to publish newspapers overseas and supports their cooperation with large e-commerce platforms

by tongdma2 on 2012-02-29 13:24:23

By Cao Hong, Early Edition Reporter - Under the large framework of cultural system reform, the General Administration of Press and Publication (GAPP) has issued new regulations to strongly promote deep reforms within publishing and media groups.

Xinhua News Agency reported yesterday that GAPP recently released the "Guiding Opinions on Accelerating the Reform and Development of Publishing Media Groups" (hereinafter referred to as the Opinions), which consists of 32 articles divided into eight sections. The Opinions explicitly encourage publishing media groups to merge with or restructure central and local publishing enterprises whose businesses are similar and resources interconnected, achieving cross-regional development. It also encourages publishing media groups to merge with or restructure other state-owned enterprises outside the news publishing sector, realizing cross-industry development.

Support for cooperation with major e-commerce platforms

A senior publishing insider told the Early Edition yesterday that the General Administration is encouraging mergers and acquisitions in the scope of "capital markets." With the promotion of enterprise restructuring and reform in the publishing and media sectors, many publishing and media enterprises have been confirmed as market entities, implying more possibilities for buying and selling in the capital market.

In addition, the Opinions clearly propose for the first time "encouraging and supporting news publishing units that have completed enterprise restructuring to voluntarily join various publishing media groups." The aforementioned publishing person said this is about loosening restrictions on the industry, encouraging mergers and acquisitions in the publishing and media market through the capital market. Voluntary joining means that cross-media operations in the field of news publishing are encouraged.

Regarding how to conduct mergers and acquisitions, the Opinions further specify the targets: Encourage publishing media groups to integrate newspaper, periodical, book, audiovisual product, electronic publication, digital publishing businesses, and publishing, printing duplication, distribution resources to achieve multimedia and full industrial chain development. Support publishing media groups in establishing branches in different regions, engaging in chain operations, and cooperating with similar enterprises in terms of products, projects, and capital to realize cross-regional operations. Support publishing media groups in collaborating with large enterprises in the broadcasting, television, telecommunications, and other industries.

Moreover, the Opinions mention encouraging publishing media groups to develop new forms of publishing represented by online publishing, mobile phone publishing, cloud publishing, etc.; support publishing media groups in conducting strategic cooperation and resource integration with large e-commerce enterprises, building a modern publishing logistics system combining online and offline circulation.

However, a securities analyst told the Early Edition yesterday that the policy is "encouraged," not mandatory, and there are no specific measures on how to "encourage," so its operability is relatively low.

Allow holding stakes in non-public enterprises

The Opinions mention that by the end of the "12th Five-Year Plan," efforts will be made to strengthen and optimize three national-level publishing media groups in humanities, education, and science and technology. Multiple large backbone publishing media groups with annual sales exceeding 20 billion yuan will be cultivated, promoting cross-regional mergers and acquisitions among Xinhua Bookstores, and forming nationwide large state-owned distribution groups.

According to data from the GAPP website in February, so far, China has established more than 120 news publishing enterprise groups including newspapers, publishing, distribution, and printing. Among them, 48 enterprise groups involve news publishing businesses and have gone public. In 2010, these over 120 groups owned total assets of 323.42 billion yuan and achieved main business revenue of 178.58 billion yuan.

In the path to strengthening and expanding publishing media groups, there is also a signal of further opening up to non-public cultural enterprises.

The Opinions point out that it is necessary to guide and standardize the product cooperation, project cooperation, and capital cooperation between state-owned publishing media groups and non-public cultural enterprises; allow state-owned publishing media groups to introduce qualified non-public cultural enterprises as a department of the state-owned publishing media group to participate in publishing activities; allow publishing media groups to hold controlling or participating stakes in non-public cultural enterprises with good growth prospects; achieve cross-ownership development; support publishing media groups in achieving overall listing of their main businesses; recommend high-tech R&D publishing media enterprises to list on the Growth Enterprise Market.
