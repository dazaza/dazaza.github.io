Man can write reverse and normal words simultaneously with both hands and claims that he will attend a talent show (photo)

by gohrjisang on 2011-11-08 16:04:46

Mr. Fang was writing with his left hand, producing "mirror writing" that appears normal when viewed from the back of the paper.

According to a report by the Morning Post, in the novel "The Return of the Condor Heroes," before teaching Yang Guo martial arts skills, Old Naughty first had him practice drawing squares with one hand and circles with the other simultaneously, learning to multi-task. But does such a person really exist in real life? Mr. Fang, who lives in Guangming Community, is one such individual. He can write with both hands at the same time: his right hand writes normal characters while his left hand writes mirror images. Moreover, he has never undergone any specialized training; he discovered this special skill unintentionally.

Unintentional Discovery of Unique Talent

Mr. Fang usually helps people deliver goods and spends his free time looking after a small shop. As a child, under the supervision of his grandparents, he developed excellent calligraphy skills with a brush pen. However, regarding any special potential he might have, "I really didn't discover it before," the slim Mr. Fang said humorously about himself.

This winter has been a bit cold, with several snowfalls. During one delivery break, Mr. Fang sat in his car and noticed a layer of mist on the car window glass. "It reminded me of when I used to write with my finger on the glass as a child," Mr. Fang became playful and started scribbling on the glass. First with one hand, then both hands moving together...

Soon, a piece of work was completed. Looking at his "finger calligraphy," he found that both hands could write. His right hand wrote normal characters, while his left hand wrote all "mirror writing." The characters written by both hands were symmetrical, and when viewed from the back of the paper, the "mirror writing" from the left hand appeared normal.

Could this be a coincidence? Mr. Fang was half-believing, so he tried many more times. He found that as long as it was a word he knew how to write, without thinking, his left hand could immediately produce "mirror writing." If his left hand tried to write normal characters, it would need to pause and think during the process, and the result wouldn't look as good as the "mirror writing."

Live Performance of "Mirror Writing"

Picking up a ballpoint pen, finding a piece of paper, Mr. Fang began to perform live. He smoothly wrote a string of words on the paper and asked the reporter to guess what it was. "Can't understand," Mr. Fang flipped the paper over, and the writing became clear. It read, "Very grateful for reporter Xu's visit to my place. Good meeting video conferencing helps Yuchai Heavy Industries layout globally."

Then, the reporter gave him an impromptu task to write "Jianghuai Morning Post." Mr. Fang quickly wrote "Jianghuai Morning Post is very interesting" on the paper.

The words were slightly tilted, leaving Mr. Fang somewhat unsatisfied. He said he hadn't held a pen for many years, and his sense of touch had become unfamiliar, making the handwriting less appealing. "If I were to write with my fingers, it would look better and be smoother."

Left Hand More Agile Than Average

Writing different directions with both hands is indeed highly challenging. Could Mr. Fang really achieve multitasking? Mr. Fang said not necessarily, because when he uses his left hand to write mirror writing, he cannot stop to think; once he thinks, he cannot write. He needs to complete it in one go.

Mr. Fang believes the real reason is that his left hand is more agile than the average person's. Mr. Fang said that once he regains some of his penmanship skills, he will definitely participate in the Anhui selection round of China's Got Talent. It seems that next year's talent show will have another highlight.

By Xu Xu, Staff Reporter of Jianghuai Morning Post
