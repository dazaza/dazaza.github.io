Five survivors of the Nanjing Massacre 74 years ago open weibos to mourn for the victims

by edivista on 2011-12-14 20:39:50

According to China National Voice's News纵横, after 74 years, the Qinhuai River dyed red by blood has long washed away its bloody color, and the Yanzijin with corpses everywhere has been newly built. However, the pain of the humiliating death of 300,000 compatriots cannot be erased. If it were not for this painful national memory, perhaps "The Flowers of War" would not have so strongly occupied the top headlines of movie news.

Nanjing holds a series of activities to commemorate the dead.

Today is the 74th anniversary of the massacre of 300,000 compatriots by the Japanese invading army in Nanjing. Nanjing will hold a series of activities to commemorate the dead. At half past seven last night, mourning candles were lit in Nanjing. In the sky, there was a full moon; on the ground, there were 3000 red candles swaying in the cold wind. In the sacrificial venue of the Memorial Hall for Compatriots Killed in the Nanjing Massacre by Japanese Invading Army, people from all walks of life held a vigil activity for the victims of the Nanjing Massacre last night. The abbot of Nanjing Pilu Temple, Master Chuan Yi, first led the monks to chant scriptures and pray for the victims, then the monks from the Nishi Honganji Temple and the Yunxiang Temple in Aomori Prefecture, Japan, chanted their prayer songs. Zhu Chengshan, director of the Memorial Hall for Compatriots Killed in the Nanjing Massacre by Japanese Invading Army, said that the activity of guarding the souls of the victims had been continuously held for three years.

Zhu Chengshan: I think there are two meanings. One is to express our remembrance of history, the victims, and the war tragedy. At the same time, we hope for peace and safety.

Although the right-wing elements active in Japan are trying hard to cover up the truth of history, more and more Japanese people have realized the blemish of this period of history. Secretary General of the Ninth Friendly Visit to China Mission of the True Pure Land Sect of Japan, Sayoko Yamaji, said that the light of the candle is both a mourning for the dead by the living and a symbol of the power of peace.

Sayoko Yamaji: We must not only face history but also remember history, with the aim of looking to the future.

Xiaoyu, a student from Jiangdongmen Primary School in Nanjing, said that participating in the memorial activities every year makes him realize how precious peace is.

Xiaoyu: I must remember history, study hard, and prevent this part of history from happening again now.

Five survivors open microblogs to mourn the victims.

Every year on this day, not only the people in Nanjing, but every descendant of the Chinese nation relives this painful memory. Letting more people understand and remember this period of history is the wish of many. In addition to big-budget movies and best-selling books, more efforts come from the elderly who survived the massacre. They repeatedly expose their scars and recall the nightmare-like year of 1937, in order to let more people know the truth of history.

In recent days, five special microblogs have appeared online, recalling the tragic details of 74 years ago. Their bloggers share a common identity as survivors of the Nanjing Massacre. These microblogs are maintained by their children. Last night, Zhou Jilin, son of survivor Zhou Xiuhua, said that they hope to help young people who like to use microblogs perceive this history through microblogs in the limited remaining years of their parents' lives.

Zhou Jilin: Opening microblogs is also a learning process for us, so I am slowly studying and posting. There are fewer and fewer survivors of the massacre now, from initially over a thousand to several hundred, and now less than 200. Their ages are all relatively high, over 80 years old, even my father is already over 90. When their minds were very clear, truly seeing my father made me sob uncontrollably. Now, they can only remember the deeper parts of that history. Once this generation is completely gone, this history may be forgotten. I call on everyone to pay attention to this matter and remember this history.

In addition to the efforts from the families of eyewitnesses, a professional oral history interview team is being formed. With the passage of time, more and more eyewitnesses have passed away successively, and the witnesses to history are disappearing. Zhu Chengshan, director of the Memorial Hall for Compatriots Killed in the Nanjing Massacre by Japanese Invading Army, said that the work of rescuing historical testimonies is urgent despite the great difficulty.

Zhu Chengshan: Although the materials of these survivors have been collected in the past, we still need to further collect them. In the past, we mainly talked about their experiences as victims. We want the complete data chain of the elderly, including the psychological trauma after their victimization, and how to view history decades later. But there is a difficulty: since the elderly generally have low educational levels and are quite old, their memories often become blurred, so this task is quite difficult. We may need to interview multiple times, if once is not enough, then three or five times, or even ten times, to rescue as much as possible.
