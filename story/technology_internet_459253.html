Establishing the International Cybersecurity Protection Alliance to severely crack down on cybercrime gangs

by nuo01 on 2011-07-08 09:49:42

The Internet, our network paradise, brings business opportunities to enterprises while also opening up channels for profit for criminals. The ever-emerging cybercrime incidents make our "paradise" seem always clouded over. The "public security" on the network has been a concern of the public. Cybercriminal activities, network pyramid selling, network infringement and network hackers have been affecting a healthy "online gaming life". It has become an urgent problem across the globe to crack down on cybercrimes and arrest the rampant thieves.

In order to combat cybercrimes, on July 5, under the strong support of the British government, a grand press conference of the International Cyber Security Protection Alliance (ICSPA) was held in London to officially announce its establishment. It is reported that this global non-profit organization received a hefty investment of 650 million pounds from the British government. It consists of many world-renowned enterprises such as McAfee and Trend Micro and will cooperate with Europol to jointly combat cybercrimes and protect enterprises and users from being infringed through international law enforcement capabilities.

As long as the cyber security is not improved, it will continue to hinder the rapid development of the network. There has been a saying that "cyber security may cause the country to collapse and the system to break down." Everyone has the responsibility to ensure cyber security. The Internet domain knows no boundaries or restrictions and is a fully open free space, which provides favorable conditions for some criminal organizations. The network thieves are always playing a game of "cat and mouse" with the network police. Moreover, due to the low level of popularization of cyber security awareness among the public, they cannot effectively resist network attacks, making the "thieves" more reckless and do as they please. Security issues are not just targeted at one area but are on a global scale. The emergence of the International Cyber Security Protection Alliance is like a oasis in the desert.

It is well known that the cybercrime industry once affected the network information security and directly impacted the national economy. Relevant data shows that "cybercrime causes more than 4 billion dollars loss to the UK every year", "the US online fraud in 2009 caused a loss increase of 559 million dollars", and "in 2010, China spent 15.3 billion yuan on handling security events services." Behind these shocking numbers, the criminals are "elated" while the enterprises are "bewildered".

In recent years, with the increasing prosperity of e-commerce, the network has become an important lifeline for enterprise development. The tempting profits attract the desires of the "black hands" who aim at the booming enterprises, stealing data, leaking secrets, maliciously attacking, sending spam emails, and using website forgery techniques to harm enterprises. Even in this seemingly harmonious Internet world, there is a clear distinction between the "white" and "black" sides behind the scenes. The black side has even developed a black network path, recruiting soldiers and conducting a series of hacker trainings and technical methods to build a "powerful" black gang. So-called "black party", "black organization", "black gang", "network water army" treat the Internet as a "big pot", turning right and wrong, creating chaos and invasions, increasing the concerns of netizens about network security.

Recently, the 3Q war continued again, spitting out insults until they were deadlocked. 360 claimed that QQ infringed and mimicked its security program and secretly assassinated QQ Computer Security Manager. The situation was unpredictable, leaving the network restless; on June 6, Baidu's search engine inexplicably encountered a network security issue again, with large-scale inability to access, web pages unable to open, affecting nearly 13 provinces and cities nationwide. Baidu expressed that it was due to a fault in individual server rooms, but the truth remains mysterious. Some netizens guessed it might be the work of hackers. Security issues are everywhere and can burst out at any time.

Regarding network security issues, global e-commerce expert China Nuowang www.nuo.cn stated that not only should safety awareness and monitoring measures be strengthened, but more importantly, technical updates and breakthroughs should be made, such as domains, servers, and server rooms. Servers are the face that ensures the circulation of data and materials. If hacked, it will result in losses. When enterprises engage in overseas trade, they need to choose high-end, stable, and secure servers and corporate email accounts as backup to ensure economic security and avoid being controlled by others.
