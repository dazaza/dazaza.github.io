Manchester United's flying wing is more stylish than the "Stylish Brother" and becomes the Devil's second brother; Real Madrid finds it hard to buy him for 20 million.

by qinglai888 on 2009-12-17 17:07:42

Manchester United's Flying Wing is more stylish than "Showboat" and has become the Devil's No. 2; Real Madrid's 20 million offer can't get him. Since November, a new firepower point for Manchester United has emerged: Antonio Valencia. The Ecuadorian player has scored 3 goals in 6 league matches, averaging one goal every two games. The situation where he only provided assists without scoring goals himself has been improved. Currently, in Manchester United, he has played in 16 league matches and scored 4 goals. Scoring one goal every four matches might not be extraordinary, but judging from his recent performance, Valencia has become the second most important attacking player for Manchester United after Rooney.

It's somewhat embarrassing to say that although Manchester United has had 14 players score goals in league and cup matches this season, Rooney’s 13 goals stand far above the rest. The second-highest scorer, Owen, has only 7 goals, and Owen has only scored twice in the crucial league matches. Berbatov’s four league goals were spaced out over time. Only Valencia has continuously scored recently with impressive goals. Although on the team's league goal-scorers list he ranks second alongside Berbatov, fans clearly recognize Valencia's recent form as surpassing Berbatov.

Today's goal thoroughly demonstrated how strong teams emphasize individual creativity and opportunity-grasping abilities while maintaining high-speed and efficient counterattacks. Any team can execute quick attacks, but just pursuing speed without precise passing or final touches will make fast attacks nothing more than empty talk. We see in this goal, Scholes' perfectly judged lofted pass, Berbatov's backheel cross after a flick-up - a perfect combination of technique and imagination, and finally Berbatov's right-footed volley after adjusting his center of gravity. This classic finish after a brilliant team play reminds us of Teddy Sheringham and Ole Gunnar Solskjær, completely replicating Valencia's current role today.

After the match, Ferguson praised Valencia highly: “This finishing was very spectacular. Antonio can increase his number of goals significantly because we all know during training that he is actually an excellent shooter. Today's goal is a great example. Antonio's improvement has been significant; he plays particularly expansively in matches and interprets his role well. He keeps improving, and we are very satisfied.” What does playing expansively mean? It refers to Valencia's style of play where his activity in the midfield and forward positions covers more ground, allowing him to move and switch positions comfortably rather than being confined to the right wing as he was when he first joined the team. His one-touch passes throughout the game were highly accurate, requiring high levels of understanding between teammates running along different routes while maintaining overall high-speed progress. Valencia’s willingness to perform such actions indicates that he has now fully integrated into Manchester United. Thus, the future number of goals and assists for this 17 million star should, as emphasized by Ferguson, increase substantially.

Manchester United should feel fortunate to have secured Valencia. In the summer, Bayern Munich offered 18 million pounds, and Real Madrid went up to 20 million, both higher than Manchester United's offer. Fortunately, Wigan Athletic didn't care about these few million differences and stressed that Valencia's destination would depend solely on himself. Valencia, who was already accustomed to playing in the Premier League, ultimately chose Manchester United. Looking back six months later, this deal has begun to pay off significantly for Manchester United. Although Valencia hasn't yet reached the destructive level of Cristiano Ronaldo, his performance of 4 goals and 3 assists in 16 games, especially his heroic displays since November, makes people believe that despite his somewhat fixed position, the 24-year-old Valencia is moving towards becoming a legendary absolute starting right winger spanning an era for Manchester United's illustrious history.
