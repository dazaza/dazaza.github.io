Mother accidentally smothers daughter gets lenient sentence and probation

In addition, based on Aqiao's repentance, the opinions of her workplace and community where she resides, as well as considerations for family relations, it was determined that announcing a suspended sentence would have no adverse impact on her community, leading to the decision of a suspended sentence.

Excessive indulgence, improper allowance of daughter's reckless spending.

■ Written/Photographed by Southern Metropolis Daily reporter Huang Qiong, Correspondent Su Fa Xuan, Li Fa Xuan

At around 9 a.m. yesterday morning, Aqiao was escorted to the courtroom by the bailiff, immediately becoming emotionally overwhelmed and beginning to cry, even fainting momentarily. It was understood that Aqiao, who had been highly emotional since the incident, had not eaten properly for 11 consecutive days in the detention center, leaving her extremely weak. To calm her emotions, the court specially invited a psychologist to provide her with over 40 minutes of pre-trial therapy.

Mother's Statement

The Southern Metropolis Daily reported that the presiding judge of this case is the chief judge of the juvenile court of Liwan District Court, with over 20 years of experience in criminal cases. Even so, he still sighed deeply at the family tragedy in this case. The chief judge had multiple conversations with Aqiao, visited the workplaces, communities, neighbors of Aqiao and her husband, as well as the school where their daughter Xiaowei once studied. All these external testimonies confirmed that Aqiao made many contributions to her family, loving her home, daughter, and having a very good relationship with her husband.

Neighbors' Statements

She looked down on her daughter's father because he had no money or power.

Some neighbors commented that the couple was too indulgent with their child, allowing her to spend money freely while they themselves lived frugally. After the incident, the father found five bank cards belonging to their daughter. Xiaowei's first debit card was opened in 2006 when she was only 11 years old, and the spending limit at that time was generally around a hundred yuan. However, after 2010, the spending limit began to increase significantly, often reaching thousands of yuan, as Xiaowei became obsessed with online shopping.

Liu Yubin believed that Aqiao's biggest mistake was being overly indulgent with her child. Appreciative education should not be abandoned, but excessive indulgence harms the child.

Within ten minutes of the announcement, Aqiao suddenly collapsed, "plop" falling to the ground. Several bailiffs quickly rushed forward to support her, but her tightly closed eyes and tear-streaked cheeks remained evident. Her husband, Mr. Zhong, sitting in the audience, also panicked and rushed forward to offer her medicated oil. The bailiffs applied the oil to various acupoints such as the temples and philtrum. After a while, Aqiao slowly regained consciousness.

Judge's Explanation

After the incident, Aqiao's husband, relatives, and workplace submitted six letters of plea to the court, requesting leniency. In his letter, her husband described Aqiao as a virtuous wife and mother, loving her family more than herself, and being devoted and satisfactory towards her in-laws; while their daughter, although a cherished treasure, was stubborn and capricious, often verbally abusing her parents. Heartbroken and unable to blame his beloved wife anymore, he petitioned the court for leniency, hoping they could overcome this difficult period together.

The court recognized: no intent to harm.

It was introduced that the night before the incident, during an argument, Zhong Xuexeng said, "Daughter, you need to reason with your mother," and subsequently slapped Xiaowei several times. Additionally, the fiery-tempered Xiaowei had set fire to her parents' bedroom three times, burning her mother's clothes. Two weeks before the incident, all three door locks in the house were damaged by her...

After falling, Xiaowei continued to curse Aqiao. Aqiao then stood on Xiaowei's back, pressing her hands on her head to stop the cursing until she stopped making any sound. Later, realizing Xiaowei was unresponsive, Aqiao immediately called 110 and 120 for help, but upon rescue, it was discovered that Xiaowei had no vital signs. Subsequently, Aqiao voluntarily turned herself in and confessed to her crime.

Her excessive capriciousness led to demands for both a computer and apology fees.

A 16-year-old daughter demanded an Apple computer and thousands of yuan in apology fees from her mother, resulting in a violent altercation where she was accidentally suffocated by her mother. This recent report by the Southern Metropolis Daily has drawn significant public attention. Yesterday morning, the Liwan Court made a first-instance verdict, believing that the defendant mother Aqiao had no intention to harm her daughter. Considering her voluntary confession, her husband's plea, and the situation of family reconciliation, she was sentenced to three years in prison, suspended for five years. Although lightly sentenced and able to return home that day, Aqiao still wept bitterly with deep sorrow, fainting twice at the scene.

The most reluctant Aqiao still sobbed uncontrollably, and out of concern for her physical condition, the presiding judge expedited the reading of the verdict. Upon hearing the touching words about her son, Aqiao cried even harder. Finally, after being sentenced to a suspended term, facing the judge's inquiry, Aqiao took a deep breath and stated, "No objection, will not appeal."

Liwan Court ruled it as negligent homicide with a voluntary confession scenario, Aqiao sobbed and fainted twice during the hearing.

Before yesterday's trial, senior psychological therapist Liu Yubin was entrusted by the court to provide psychological intervention therapy to Aqiao. She indicated that losing her beloved daughter, with the culprit being herself, was the biggest hurdle for Aqiao. Despite her constant state of grief and crying throughout, Aqiao had realized that her methods of educating her child were unsuccessful.

Why was it ultimately ruled as involuntary manslaughter instead of intentional injury? After yesterday's trial, Chief Justice Luo Weicheng of the Liwan Court's criminal division explained to reporters that through on-site investigation and injury appraisal, the injuries on Xiaowei were abrasions, not caused by deliberate beating, nor did they result in serious or fatal injuries due to intentional harm. Therefore, it can be proven that Aqiao had no intention to harm her daughter Xiaowei, and her death was a result of negligence.

Both the workplace certificate and testimony from Xiaowei's teachers, along with her own bank cards, demonstrated that Aqiao and her husband excessively indulged Xiaowei, paying extreme attention to her growth regardless of their own financial situation. Hence, it was concluded that Aqiao's educational methods towards her daughter Xiaowei were inappropriate, involving excessive indulgence without malicious intent.

Previously, the prosecution believed that Aqiao intentionally harmed others, resulting in one death, constituting the crime of intentional injury.

The court finally ruled that during the argument between Aqiao and her daughter Xiaowei, Aqiao lacked proper attention to Xiaowei's life safety, causing her death. Her actions constituted involuntary manslaughter. Given that Aqiao voluntarily surrendered and truthfully confessed to her crimes, which constitutes a voluntary confession, she could be given a lighter punishment. Simultaneously, her behavior was also understood by her husband, warranting a mitigated penalty.

The victim had a bank card since the age of 11, and extensive questioning revealed: parents had consistently spoiled their daughter.

Later, Xiaowei started cursing her and physically attacking her in the living room, wielding a kitchen knife...

Aqiao claimed that on the evening of the incident, Xiaowei requested to buy an Apple computer, which she had already agreed to, but Xiaowei insisted on demanding 5900 yuan for apology fees after discovering that her bank card balance of over 6300 yuan was nearly depleted, which she refused.

Combining evidence from Aqiao's workplace, Xiaowei's teachers, and her own bank cards, it was evident that Aqiao and her husband excessively indulged Xiaowei, showing great attention to her upbringing, regardless of their own financial situation, allowing her to spend money freely. Thus, it was concluded that Aqiao's educational methods towards her daughter Xiaowei were inappropriate, involving excessive indulgence without malicious intent.

Previously, the prosecution believed that Aqiao intentionally harmed others, resulting in one death, constituting the crime of intentional injury.

A middle school teacher who once taught Xiaowei provided testimony stating that Xiaowei's performance from 2007 to 2010 was acceptable, but her academic results were poor. During a conversation, the teacher praised Xiaowei's father for being highly capable and a graduate of a prestigious university, but Xiaowei remarked, "What's so great about university? He has no power, no money, many people are richer than him." It showed that Xiaowei looked down on her father.

Xiaowei frequently went to her parents' room to look for money. She also prohibited them from using the kitchen toilet unless paid 500 yuan...

Liu Yubin believed that the top priority was to help Aqiao re-establish life goals. Aqiao must also face reality, accept lessons, and improve herself. She mentioned that the 40-minute session before yesterday's trial was limited, and she would continue to follow up on this case.

Upon discovering her daughter was "unresponsive," she promptly called 110 and 120 for help.

The court reviewed the case and found that the 41-year-old defendant Aqiao and the 16-year-old victim Xiaowei (both pseudonyms) were mother and daughter. On the evening of August 18th this year, due to Xiaowei's request to purchase an Apple computer and demand for 5900 yuan in apology fees, an argument erupted in their home's living room. Xiaowei then physically attacked Aqiao, leading to mutual grappling. During the struggle, Aqiao tripped Xiaowei, causing her to fall and land face-first on a round cushion on the ground.

Why was it judged as a negligent crime?

The day before the incident, on August 17th this year, Xiaowei's card still had 6336 yuan, but by noon of the incident day, over 6000 yuan had been withdrawn, leaving only 8 yuan.
