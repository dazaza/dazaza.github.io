"Tiger-head shoes" conquer the north and south

by elandbin14 on 2010-04-18 10:44:29

【Putian Shoe Network】The custom of sewing and wearing tiger-head shoes is widely spread in our country, especially in the North China Plain. The俗语 "The first pair blue (a homophone for 'lan', meaning to prevent early death), the second pair red (red wards off evil and avoids disasters), the third pair purple completion (meaning the child grows up in his own home)." talks about the custom of aunts giving tiger-head shoes to their nephews to ward off disasters and evil.

Zhu Weiwei, a college graduate from Guoyuan Village in Longyao County, and Kong Qianqian, with the help of their mother and sister, explored, designed, and made children's tiger-head shoes. These shoes have novel patterns, exquisite materials, and delicate craftsmanship, making them very popular. They have already applied for the "袼褙虎" trademark at the industrial and commercial department. The small "tiger-head shoe" has now become a thriving industry with seven series, more than 50 types of patterns, an annual output value of over 500,000 yuan, and providing employment for more than 100 people.

Maternal love inspires entrepreneurial passion

Zhu Weiwei and Kong Qianqian both graduated from university in 2006. After graduation, they worked in private enterprises in Guangzhou and Yantai. During this period, they saw that many urban stores sold "tiger-head shoes" at high prices but with average quality, which sparked a desire to start a business back home. In Zhu Weiwei's memory, his mother would spend entire nights embroidering "tiger-head shoes" for the children, stitch by stitch. When he woke up, his mother was still embroidering. Those intricate stitches filled with maternal love, those tiger-shaped patterns filled with blessings, once accompanied him taking his first steps in life. This formed his "tiger-head shoe" sentiment.

After much contemplation, they decisively ended their working lives and returned home to start a business.

Research and development form a large-scale industry

Traditional "tiger-head shoes" had simple patterns and single styles, so they came up with more attractive designs. The flowers and grasses in the countryside inspired them. They embroidered the eyes into pomegranate shapes, changed the eyebrows into little roosters, added a circle of fluffy rabbit hair on the toe, and sewed two realistic little ears. Various "little tigers" came to life. As a result, whenever a baby was born in the surrounding villages, people would come one after another asking them to make shoes, leading to a high demand for their products.

In the following days, they not only mobilized their entire family to work on "tiger-head shoes," but also invited relatives from neighboring villages such as Lincun, Nanguan, Xihou, and Pengcun to participate, forming a large-scale "tiger-head shoe" production team.

Self-entrepreneurship brings abundant fruits

Hard work pays off. Ordinary "tiger-head shoes" in their hands have achieved great success and produced abundant results. Now, apart from tiger-head shoes, they have designed seven series and more than 50 varieties including dragon shoes, phoenix shoes, fish shoes, pig shoes, and confused shoes. Among these, there are over 50 flower patterns just for the "tiger-head shoe" toes alone. Due to their solid quality and diverse patterns, merchants from Longyao, Neiqiu, Boxiang, and Shijiazhuang all come for their reputation, and all products are order-based. This spring, a Hungarian businessman placed an order for ten thousand pairs of "tiger-head shoes."

Nowadays, the "袼褙虎" brand "tiger-head shoes" produce more than 2000 pairs per month, with monthly sales reaching four to five hundred thousand yuan. The small "tiger-head shoe" creates over 500,000 yuan in value each year for them, often in high demand.

The Legend of "Tiger-head Shoes"

In Northern China, especially in rural areas, there is a custom of dressing one or two-year-old children in "tiger-head shoes." Tiger-head shoes have wide soles, making them comfortable to wear and convenient for children to practice walking. The shoe face is shaped like a tiger, symbolizing power and vitality. It is believed that children wearing them can drive away illness and evil spirits and grow up successfully. Therefore, most children in this area take their first steps in life wearing them.

After making tiger-head shoes, remember that there are many rules when it comes to gifting them. You should give in pairs, not singles—six, eight, or twelve pairs, the more the better. The grandmother should not sew the laces; the laces should be sewn by the grandfather. It is said that this will make the child wealthy and complete.

The history of wearing "tiger-head shoes" cannot be accurately traced, with different regions having different stories. In the production place of "袼褙虎," there is one such version: A long time ago, in a village, there was a clever woman who excelled in embroidery. It was said that her embroidered fish could swim in water, and her embroidered flowers could attract bees and butterflies. With such a talented mother, this family's children dressed differently, almost never repeating outfits.

One night, a monster suddenly appeared, capturing all the children in the village except for one child from this family. Why? Because this child was sleeping in a pair of newly embroidered "tiger-head shoes" by the clever woman. The child liked them so much that he didn't even take them off while sleeping. Thus, the villagers believed that this pair of "tiger-head shoes" saved the child. Since then, the custom of wearing tiger-head shoes has been passed down to today.
