Google Reports Third-Quarter Earnings: Net Profit Up 36% Year-over-Year

by anonymous on 2013-11-16 11:19:43

At 0:00 Beijing time on October 18th, Google released its financial report for the third quarter of the 2013 fiscal year ending September 30. The report showed that Google's total revenue for the third quarter was $14.89 billion, up 12% from the same period last year; according to U.S. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), Google's net profit for the third quarter was $2.97 billion, up 36% from $2.18 billion in the same period last year.

Key Performance Indicators:

Google's total revenue for the third quarter was $14.89 billion, an increase of 12% year-over-year, including traffic acquisition costs. Google's traffic acquisition cost for the third quarter was $2.97 billion, representing 24% of advertising revenue.

According to GAAP, Google's operating profit for the third quarter was $3.44 billion, accounting for 23% of revenue. According to GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $2.74 billion, accounting for 21% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the third quarter was $4.34 billion, accounting for 29% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $3.76 billion, accounting for 28% of revenue.

According to GAAP, Google's net profit for the third quarter was $2.97 billion, compared to $2.18 billion for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's net profit for the third quarter was $3.64 billion, compared to $2.96 billion for the same period last year.

According to GAAP, Google's earnings per share (EPS) for the third quarter were $8.75, compared to $6.53 for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's EPS for the third quarter were $10.74, compared to $8.87 for the same period last year.

Financial Analysis:

Google's total revenue for the third quarter was $14.89 billion, up 12% year-over-year, including traffic acquisition costs.

Google's third-quarter revenue (advertising and other businesses) was $13.77 billion, accounting for 92% of total revenue, up 19% from $11.53 billion for the same period last year.

Google's third-quarter website revenue, or revenue generated by its own websites, was $9.39 billion, accounting for 68% of total revenue, up 22% from $7.73 billion for the same period last year.

Google's third-quarter network revenue, or revenue generated by partner websites through the AdSense program, was $3.15 billion, accounting for 23% of total revenue, slightly higher than $3.13 billion for the same period last year.

Google's third-quarter other revenue was $1.23 billion, accounting for 9% of total revenue, up 85% from $666 million for the same period last year.

Google's third-quarter international revenue, or revenue generated outside the United States, was $7.67 billion, accounting for 56% of total revenue, higher than 55% in the previous quarter and 53% for the same period last year. If exchange rates had remained flat with the previous quarter (not counting the relevant gains from foreign exchange risk management plans), Google's third-quarter revenue would have increased by $41 million; if exchange rates had remained flat with the same period last year (not counting the relevant gains from foreign exchange risk management plans), Google's revenue would have increased by $135 million.

Google's third-quarter revenue from the UK was $1.39 billion, accounting for 10% of total revenue, down from 11% for the same period last year.

Google's third-quarter revenue benefit from foreign exchange risk management plans was $22 million, compared to $62 million for the same period last year.

Google's total paid click-throughs (including Google websites and AdSense partner websites) for the third quarter increased by approximately 26% year-over-year and 8% quarter-over-quarter.

Google's average cost-per-click (including Google websites and AdSense partner websites) for the third quarter decreased by approximately 8% year-over-year and 4% quarter-over-quarter.

Google's third-quarter Traffic Acquisition Costs (TAC), or shared revenue with partners, was $2.97 billion, compared to $2.77 billion for the same period last year. Google's TAC as a percentage of advertising revenue was approximately 24%, down from 26% for the same period last year.

In Google's third-quarter TAC expenses, $2.22 billion will ultimately be paid to AdSense partners, and another $755 million will eventually be paid to specific distribution partners.

Google's third-quarter revenue from the Motorola Mobility division was $1.18 billion (of which $1.78 billion came from the mobile department and $797 million from the home department), accounting for 8% of Google's total revenue; last year's revenue from the Motorola Mobility division was $1.78 billion, accounting for 13% of Google's total revenue.

Starting from the third quarter of 2013, Google's and Motorola's divisional revenues began to be affected by inter-division transactions, which will be eliminated during the integration process. Additionally, certain product-related divisional revenues in the third quarter were recorded in divisional performance but will be deferred to future quarterly reports. In the third quarter, this inter-divisional revenue and deferred revenue amounted to $63 million.

Google's third-quarter other revenue costs were $3.44 billion, accounting for 23% of revenue. Last year's other revenue costs were $3.19 billion, accounting for 24% of revenue. Google's other revenue costs mainly include data center operating expenses, amortization of intangible assets, and content acquisition costs.

Google's third-quarter operating expenses were $5.04 billion, accounting for 34% of revenue. Last year's operating expenses were $4.61 billion, accounting for 35% of revenue.

Google's third-quarter amortization expense related to acquisition-related intangible assets was $281 million, compared to $287 million for the same period last year. Of this amount, $153 million was due to the acquisition of Motorola. Of this $153 million, $116 million was included in Google's divisional performance, and $37 million was included in Motorola's divisional performance.

Google's third-quarter equity-based compensation expense was $886 million, compared to $750 million for the same period last year.

Google expects the expenditure associated with options granted to employees prior to September 30, 2013, to be approximately $3.29 billion in 2013, excluding employee stock award-related expenditures after September 30 or non-employee stock award-related expenditures already granted or potentially granted.

According to GAAP, Google's third-quarter operating profit was $3.44 billion, accounting for 23% of revenue. According to GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $2.74 billion, accounting for 21% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's third-quarter operating profit was $4.34 billion, accounting for 29% of revenue. Excluding GAAP, Google's operating profit for the same period last year was $3.76 billion, accounting for 28% of revenue.

According to GAAP, the third-quarter operating profit attributable to Google's segment was $4.64 billion, accounting for 34% of Google's segment revenue. According to GAAP, the operating profit attributable to Google's segment for the same period last year was $3.95 billion, accounting for 34% of Google's revenue.

According to GAAP, the third-quarter operating loss attributable to Motorola Mobility's segment was $248 million, accounting for -21% of Motorola Mobility's segment revenue. According to GAAP, the operating loss attributable to Motorola Mobility's segment for the same period last year was $192 million, accounting for -11% of Motorola Mobility's segment revenue.

Google's third-quarter interest and other income was $24 million, compared to $65 million in interest and other expenses for the same period last year.

Google's effective tax rate for the third quarter was 15%.

According to GAAP, Google's net profit for the third quarter was $2.97 billion, compared to $2.18 billion for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's net profit for the third quarter was $3.64 billion, compared to $2.96 billion for the same period last year. According to GAAP, Google's EPS for the third quarter was $8.75, compared to $6.53 for the same period last year. Excluding GAAP, Google's EPS for the third quarter was $10.74, compared to $8.87 for the same period last year.

Google's net cash provided by operating activities for the third quarter was $5.08 billion, compared to $4 billion for the same period last year; capital expenditures for the third quarter were $2.29 billion, primarily consisting of production equipment, data center construction, and real estate purchases; free cash flow (excluding GAAP) for the third quarter was $2.79 billion, defined as net cash provided by operating activities minus capital expenditures.

Google anticipates continuing significant capital expenditure activities in the future.

As of September 30, 2013, Google held a total of $56.52 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.

As of September 30, 2013, Google employed a total of 46,421 full-time employees globally (42,162 in Google's segment and 4,259 in Motorola Mobility's segment). As of June 30, 2013, Google employed a total of 44,777 full-time employees globally (40,178 in Google's segment and 4,599 in Motorola Mobility's segment).
