Coming out of the Civil Affairs Bureau

by jzpxiaoyanr on 2011-09-13 22:10:47

(Old Matters Touching the Heart)

Upon leaving the Civil Affairs Bureau, our hands were still clasped together, but both of us felt a bit lost in our hearts. Was this it, we were now married? I quietly asked with a smile: Any feelings? Sigh! He sighed contentedly, "I have a wife now!" Laughing heartily, he grabbed my hand. "Wife! Wife..." All the way, he kept calling out nonstop, laughing all the while as we walked toward home.

Even though I had dreamed of a beautiful wedding dress since I was a little girl, even though I had imagined countless times the figure of my prince on a white horse and fictionalized countless wedding scenes, as time went by, these dreams seemed to drift further and further away. Those beautiful fantasies were sealed away in my innocent childhood and diary books.

Among thousands of people, I met him; he neither came riding on a cloud of five colors nor on a white horse, yet we quickly fell deeply in love. Thus, we created our own simple romance, having our own spring in winter, experiencing restless nights, feeling as if one day lasted three autumns, finding excuses for quarrels that would turn into joy, along with many small traps and inexplicable pranks.

Marriage seemed like a natural progression.

Parents urged, friends urged. But should we just get married like this? Exchange the freedom of being single for the constraints of daily necessities like firewood, rice, oil, salt, soy sauce, and vinegar?

But when a man grows up, he must marry, and so must a woman! We could only encourage each other, singing songs as we boldly plunged into the city of marriage. What song? If the wolves come, they will be greeted with hunting rifles! They say marriage is the grave of love, and that grave is the wolf we must compromise with. I moved my things into his dormitory. His belongings were truly pitifully few. After I arranged my things one by one, his pre-marriage assets almost disappeared from sight. He couldn't find his socks, then his razor, shouting: "Isn't this like the Japanese invading the village?" But the "Japanese" worked hard to decorate the bridal chamber beautifully, and he happily slept with the "Japanese." Habitually, I said "my basin" and "your fan," but he suggested: "Can we change that to ours?" I said, "Okay, okay." But it took several months before this habit was corrected.

The house was still too empty. We brought 4000 yuan to buy some furniture, ambitiously wanting to arrange a warm home. But the cheapest set of sofas cost over five thousand yuan, and a coffee table alone cost more than a thousand. After wandering around the furniture mall all day, we returned home empty-handed, a bit gloomy. But this unhappiness didn't last long; soon, we were holding hands and joking around again.

We were always sticky together, happy yes, but there was always a sense of guilt for not striving for progress. I shouted, "I haven't written anything for months!" He seriously made a resolution: "I won't disturb you tonight." In the evening, I sat at the desk, but I couldn't write a single word. Because I knew someone was in the room, I couldn't concentrate fully. I threw down my pen and crawled into his arms. He repeatedly asked, "What's wrong?" I could only say, "I'm already so happy that I have nothing to say." Isn't it said that anger inspires poets? I don't feel any anger right now!

He rarely went out to eat or drink with his friends anymore, fearing I would be lonely at home. What did we do then? We didn't watch TV; it was too boring. So, we played chess. After playing a few rounds, he absolutely refused to play with me again because I always regretted my moves. We had no choice but to play Go. Rarely was he my match, and I was very interested, enjoying it endlessly, while he smiled and accompanied me. But he was indeed smart, improving quickly. My old habits resurfaced, and I wanted to cheat. He had no choice but to surrender and announced that he would quit chess forever.

In the morning, we woke up simultaneously. By the time I finished dressing, combing my hair, and brushing my teeth, a steaming breakfast was already served. In the evening, as I rushed to meet deadlines on the computer, I listened to the sound of water from the bathroom; he was washing clothes. As he whistled while hanging the clothes to dry, I gently embraced him, pressing my face against his smooth back. He asked, "What's wrong?" Seeing my tears, he was surprised. "Silly girl!" He teased me.

We lived our days, certainly these days were humble, but our spiritual life was rich! We consoled ourselves this way. He doesn't belong to any surname in the Hundred Family Names except for Qian (Money). Once, while planning our future, he said seriously, "Our child will surely have a good name, right? Let's call him Qian Buduo (Not Few Money)." I laughed and said, "If it's twins, let's call the other Qian Duoshao (Much Money)!" When I told this joke to my friends, they laughed and said, "Why not just call him Qian Hao (Good Money)?"

As soon as I finished work, I rushed straight home, heading towards my place of happiness. From afar, seeing the light shining brightly, my heart filled with warmth. It feels so good to have a home. Sometimes he wouldn't be home, but he always left the lights on. I scolded him for wasting electricity, but he said, "It's too dark without the light on; I'm afraid you'll be scared coming back." How meticulous my husband is!

Being together for a long time, we developed a feeling of mutual dependence. One joy became double joy, and one worry became half worry. I kept urging my single friends: If you love each other, get married!
