1. Shoulder straps always slip down

This phenomenon may be caused by two reasons: either the bra is too small or too loose, so when it moves up, the shoulder straps are prone to slipping off. Another reason could be choosing the wrong shoulder strap style. Everyone has different shoulder shapes - wide shoulders, narrow shoulders, flat shoulders, sloping shoulders, etc. Different shoulder types suit different styles. For example, people with sloping shoulders should pay special attention to the design of the bra's shoulder straps, making sure they aren't too wide on the outside. They should choose those with an inward design, which makes them less likely to slip.

Another common mistake regarding shoulder straps is that they are too narrow or too loose. You need to know that most of the lifting for the chest comes from the shoulder straps, so the wider and stronger the pull, the better. Too thin straps are purely decorative and provide almost no lift. If this continues over time, your firm and beautiful chest will definitely become more and more saggy. I'm not exaggerating here.

2. Cups frequently move up

This problem is very common. You often see people in public places unconsciously pulling their bras down. This is usually because the bottom edge of the bra is too loose, or there was a sizing error when choosing the bra. The cups may be too shallow, just floating on your chest like a plate. So when you raise your hands, the bra naturally goes up as well.

3. Breast indentation

After taking off the bra, there are obvious marks left on the breast from the underwire. This is even more obvious, directly indicating that the cup size of the bra is too small. You need to know that the underwire is like a frame, it should fit the contour of the breast and give it support. However, if it's too small and presses against the breast, it will cut out the fat that belongs to the breast, making the breast smaller over time. Remember, 70% of the breast composition is fat. When the fat decreases, the breast naturally shrinks.

4. Excess space or pressure at the top of the cup

Excess space indicates that the cup is too large, while pressure indicates that the cup is too small. This is clear at a glance.

5. Indentation on the back

This is also a very direct issue, usually indicating that the bottom edge is too tight, requiring a larger size. Or the bottom edge may be too narrow, easily pressing into the skin and causing indentations. You should try wearing bras with wider back straps. This increases support and stability, and also helps to distribute and balance the fat.

These are roughly the five points. Ladies, if you find yourself having these issues, then you should immediately go to some truly professional lingerie stores to reselect. At the very least, you should figure out what size bra suits you best.

Determining whether a bra fits properly actually has a direct relationship with the shape of your chest. Different chest shapes require different styles. For example, severe sagging requires choosing full-cup styles with strong lifting power, while excessive outward expansion requires choosing three-quarter cup styles with strong side-pushing power. There are many variations depending on individual needs.
