The difficulty of finding a job has led to a sharp reduction in the number of people taking the college entrance examination.

by longersing on 2009-05-31 21:18:22

In recent years, the number of people registering for the college entrance examination has continued to grow. Relevant data from the Ministry of Education shows that between 2002 and 2008, the number of people registering for the national college entrance examination increased from 5.27 million to 10.5 million. At a recent national television and telephone conference on university admissions, Zhou Ji, Minister of Education, predicted that the number of people taking the national college entrance examination this year would still exceed 10 million.

At the same time, according to the latest data, apart from provinces such as Guangdong, Jiangsu, and Chongqing where the number of people registering for the college entrance examination has slightly increased compared to last year, the number of people taking the exam in most provinces has decreased, with some provinces experiencing a decline of more than 10%.

In some places, this has become a topic of discussion among people.

The majority of provinces have seen a decrease in the number of people registering for the college entrance examination.

As the college entrance examination approaches, various regions have successively announced the statistics on the number of people registering for this year's exam. The data shows that the number of people taking the exam in the majority of provinces across the country is decreasing, with many places experiencing their first drop in the number of examinees after years of increase:

The Shandong Provincial Education Recruitment and Examination Institute stated that the number of candidates registered for this year's college entrance examination in Shandong is about 700,000, a decrease of approximately 80,000 from 2008, with a reduction exceeding 10%.

The Henan Provincial Education Department stated that the total number of candidates for this year's college entrance examination in the province is expected to be 960,000, a decrease of 29,000 from last year, marking the first drop after years of continuous increase. Among them, the number of people registering for the college entrance examination in Luoyang City has decreased for the first time since the restoration of the college entrance examination in 1977.

The number of people registering for the college entrance examination in Shanghai this year is about 83,000, a decrease of about 20,000 from last year.

The number of people registering for the college entrance examination in Hebei Province this year is about 559,000, a decrease of 16,300 from last year. This is the first time since 1995 that the number of people registering for the college entrance examination in the province has decreased.

Apart from individual provinces such as Guangdong where the number of people registering for the college entrance examination has slightly increased or remained basically the same as last year, more than half of the provinces across the country have experienced a decrease in the number of people taking the exam. Regarding this, a recruitment staff member from Beijing Institute of Technology analyzed to the reporter that there are many deep-seated reasons for the general decline in the number of people taking the college entrance examination in various places. One reason is that most of the current candidates are from the post-1990s generation, who were born after China's third baby boom (1985-1990), so their absolute numbers are fewer; another reason is that in many places, curriculum reform is being promoted, which makes some non-current-year candidates worry about significant changes in the college entrance examination after the reform, leading them to abandon retaking the exam, thus resulting in fewer candidates compared to previous years.

"The third and most important point is that since the outbreak of the financial crisis last year, the severe employment situation has shattered the myth of college students' employment, shaking the idea of high school graduates finding good jobs through attending university, causing some students to change their mindset and even not participate in the college entrance examination."

The difficulty of college students finding jobs has cooled down the college entrance examination.

In 1977, the national college entrance examination was restored. "At that time, the college entrance examination was the best opportunity for us to change our fate," said Li Dajun, who is now retired from a large state-owned enterprise in Beijing. For quite a long time afterward, participating in the college entrance examination meant having an opportunity to completely change one's destiny.

"Entering university means becoming a public servant," the concept of "guaranteed job placement" still lingers in the understanding of universities for many middle-aged and elderly people. Mr. Li's son graduated from Beijing Geely University this year, "I never imagined how difficult it is for college graduates to find jobs now. When I graduated, my job was already assigned, and I didn't have to worry about it." Mr. Li lamented.

The shattering of the employment myth has led many parents to begin questioning the value of "university" and "college entrance examination" in terms of employment in the new era. An Feng, Secretary of the Party Branch of the Political and Legal College of Luoyang Normal University, believes: "The college entrance examination is facing a crisis of public trust, which should attract attention from the industry. Currently, the reform of higher education lags behind the overall social reform, and it's time for reform."

Some students and parents are beginning to change their mindset, seeking breakthroughs outside of the college entrance examination.

Chen Wei is a graduating student from a high school in Fujian Province. Previously, his decision surprised many classmates as both fresh and unimaginable. Although his grades were consistently average in class, he decided not to take the college entrance examination but instead set his sights on a vocational secondary school outside the province. "Going to university doesn't necessarily mean finding a good job; learning a skill is more important," he said.

Not long ago, in some places, there even appeared phenomena of college students returning to technical schools for retraining. "The employment situation of vocational education is even better than that of some ordinary colleges, making vocational education gradually become an option for some high school graduates," said Ms. Chen, a teacher at Beijing Information Vocational and Technical School.

Moreover, an increasing number of graduating high school students are joining the ranks of studying abroad, which is also one of the reasons for the decrease in the number of people taking the college entrance examination in many places. It is understood that some candidates give up the domestic college entrance examination and only participate in the American College Standard Admission Test, known as the "American college entrance examination." Taking Beijing as an example, in recent years, more than 3,000 high school students in Beijing participate in the "American college entrance examination" each year, and many receive admission notices from American universities before the domestic college entrance examination.

The decrease in the number of people taking the college entrance examination is not a bad thing.

"The decrease in the number of people taking the college entrance examination is not a bad thing," several teachers from Beijing middle schools interviewed by the reporter expressed. On one hand, the decrease in the number of people taking the college entrance examination helps improve the admission rate; on the other hand, the decrease in the number of people taking the exam reflects, to a certain extent, the transformation of students' and parents' mindsets, which contributes to the rationalization of society's talent view.

After reviewing relevant materials, it was found that the national college entrance examination admission rate decreased from the original 60.91% in 2002 to 56.14% in 2008. This means that the number of candidates failing the exam increases every year.

This year, while the number of people taking the college entrance examination has decreased in most provinces, the enrollment plan has slightly increased compared to last year, and accordingly, the admission rate will be higher than last year. Taking Jiangxi as an example, this year, 349,015 candidates registered for the regular college entrance examination in Jiangxi, a decrease of 35,478 from last year, while more than 1,500 regular colleges have a total enrollment plan of 226,000 people in Jiangxi, an increase of 7,000 from last year, and the admission rate will be higher than last year. Similar situations will occur in Liaoning, Qinghai, etc. "This will alleviate the accumulated pressure of college entrance examination admissions to a certain extent," relevant experts indicated.

"The decrease in the number of people taking the college entrance examination will also lead to a gradual shift in society's talent view from 'the single-mindedness of the college entrance examination' to 'coexistence of multiple paths,'" Ms. Chen, a teacher at Beijing Information Vocational and Technical School, believed. After the concept that the college entrance examination determines one's fate fades, people can consider more from the child's own characteristics and choose suitable paths for success without blindly following the college entrance examination or universities.
