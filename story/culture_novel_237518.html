Willing to be the long wind that wipes away the dust of war, and refreshes the weary faces of my kin. - Preface

Even much later, those involved still felt that everything that happened on that day was like a dream: the sky was blue, the clouds were white, the temperature was neither too high nor too low, the air was neither too dry nor too sticky, there was peace in the four seas and tranquility under heaven. It didn't seem like a day for accidents, so when the white crane flapped past his head and flew into the Yu Xu Palace, someone had no idea that their life of quiet cultivation was about to end. They were even drawn in by the strange conversation that followed.

But then again, how familiar did the place "Yu Mountain" sound?

Before they even reached the entrance of the Yu Xu Palace, a huge shadow whizzed past their ear and straight into the hall. Judging by its shape, it should have been a Yellow Turban Warrior, but what about the two big characters "Yuan Guang"? Was there such a person on Kunlun Mountain?

"It seems Master is in high spirits; Lady will surely be pleased to hear this." A clear voice carried laughter that couldn't be concealed. The old man's taut nerves visibly relaxed after recognizing the person who jumped out from the Yellow Turban Warrior: "Are you the only one here... cough, does Yuan Guang have any message for me?"

After that, he couldn't hear anything else. The clear voice of the visitor was like the fine spring rain, lightly wrapping around the heart, tempting countless buried memories to stir, yet leaving no trace when carefully identified.

Fluttering cinnabar skirts, calamus swords, pomegranate flowers ablaze.

Jade-like morning mist, clear springs, pastoral songs.

Suddenly, he felt an indescribable fear and turned to hide, but it was too late.

The two people, having finished their conversation, slowly walked out of the palace. The peanut old man's dissatisfied scolding echoed behind him: "Li Jing, what are you secretly plotting!"

"Ah... I'm just passing by, pass..." He froze in place as soon as he saw the red-clothed girl standing next to the Primordial Heavenly Venerable, struck as if by lightning.

Memories came flooding back, surging ceaselessly in his mind.

Whose young girl's red skirt trails on the ground? Who broke the calamus to practice the sword techniques taught by the Degree-granting Immortal? And who pointed at the fiery pomegranate blossoms under the moonlight and said, "They're all watching"?

Her black hair was slightly damp in the jade-like morning mist. He said by the clear spring, "Wait for me to come back," while she smiled silently, plucked a willow leaf, and blew a pastoral song.

"Who is this weird uncle?" The red-clothed girl whispered to the Primordial Heavenly Venerable, feeling a chill from Li Jing's unblinking gaze. The old man cursed his unworthy grand-disciple inwardly for disgracing Kunlun, and chuckled dryly: "Li Jing from the Degree-granting Sect - don't mind him, his brain isn't quite right."

Weird uncle? Li Jing suddenly snapped back to reality. The girl in front looked almost identical to the one in his memory, but, after all, it wasn't her - twenty years had passed, even if she could remain youthful forever, there was no way she could become younger - besides, such spirited demeanor didn't resemble the gentle and affectionate figure in his memory.

Just as he was about to sigh in relief, his eyes caught the jade bracelet on the girl's wrist, and in an instant, the world seemed to flip upside down.

Its lustrous green color, with white threads swirling within, and a maple-leaf shaped red spot adorning it - it was the heirloom of the Li family, the one he personally put on her hand that year.

"Yun Chang?!"

"Indeed, it's you..." The red-clothed girl slightly narrowed her eyes, a certain indescribable expression flashing across them, "But I must point out that you've mistaken me for someone else. My name is Yun Rong."

She slightly curled her lips and softly uttered two words that instantly petrified Li Jing: "- Old Dad."
