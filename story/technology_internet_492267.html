by lieencq09 on 2011-09-20 21:10:40

What is reading for? My answer is for living well, for making the people we love and who love us live wonderfully, and for being able to do many interesting things. So how can we achieve our ultimate goal? Combining the advantages and disadvantages of doctors and farmers, I suggest that farmers should read more books and doctors should practice more. Any statement that abandons either side is wrong.

Secondly, the explosive power of a doctor's entrepreneurship is inferior to that of a farmer. For highly educated people to start a business, keeping their company alive is not a difficult task. However, once the company reaches a certain level, it begins to stagnate. The reason is that highly educated people generally lack "wolf nature". Here, "wolf nature" refers to the belief of counterattacking in a desperate situation, and if not successful, then die trying. Highly educated people rarely have such determination because even if a doctor fails in his entrepreneurship, he still has a large choice space. Living as a middle-class person without difficulty. However, for a farmer, once his entrepreneurship fails, it will mean that he himself, along with his parents, wife and children, will go bankrupt, and it is very possible that the whole family will wander the streets with him. For a farmer, the cost of entrepreneurial failure is something he cannot afford. This is a dangerous game. On the path of entrepreneurship, for a farmer, there is only life and death, while for a doctor, besides life and death, there is also living; all this directly determines that the explosive power of a farmer's entrepreneurship will far exceed that of a doctor.

Some say this is an era of "farmer entrepreneurs", before I didn't believe it, now I begin to feel some truth. Then what makes a doctor worse than a farmer? Is a senior intellectual who has read for decades actually failing himself? We need to deeply examine what makes the doctor of this era worse than the farmer?

I wish every aspiring youth rationally understands and clearly sees the relationship between theory and practice, and does not easily give up either one.

Firstly, the starting point of a doctor's entrepreneurship is much higher than that of a farmer. Doctors are highly educated intellectuals, they have strong social cognitive abilities. Relative to farmers, they can adapt faster to this rapidly changing social environment. Similarly, highly educated people usually have more interpersonal relationships, for example, many university or master’s classmates work in various industries. Once a doctor starts a business, he can use these existing interpersonal resources. Compared to a farmer, a doctor undoubtedly has all the advantages, and the starting point of a doctor's entrepreneurship is much higher than that of a farmer.

Chinese people value the harmony of the Tao, but in fact, Chinese people may be one of the easiest groups in the world to go to extremes. Sometimes when we see farmers succeed, we deny the value of doctors, and when we see doctors succeed, we deny the advantages of farmers. In fact, the essence of all things is the same, interconnected, usable together, and should learn from and complement each other.

Fourthly, doctors' interpersonal communication skills are generally worse than those of farmers. During the spring festival this year, I went back to my hometown for the new year. On the fifth day of the first lunar month, I had to return to Beijing to teach postgraduate students at the accommodation department of New Oriental. But I couldn't buy a train ticket back to Beijing on that day, so I barely managed to get a standing ticket through someone's help. And the next day, as soon as I arrived in Beijing, I had to give a class, so if I stood all the way to Beijing, I might not have enough physical strength. My father (he is a relatively pure farmer) gave me a pack of Zhonghua cigarettes just before I got on the train. I thought it was for me to smoke on the road, so I said I had quit smoking. Father smiled and said it was for the train conductor. Once you get on the train, find a train conductor, take him to a place where no one is, give him the cigarette, and ask him to arrange a berth for you. To be honest, I extremely despised this kind of corrupt behavior of my father, and I firmly believed in the principled stubbornness of the train conductors. Doubtfully, I got on the train like this. After getting on the train, I found a train conductor. When there was no one around in the carriage connection area, I followed my father's instructions. To be honest, my face turned red, thinking others would look down on me. However, the result was that the train conductor took the cigarette without hesitation, asked me to follow him, and then arranged a lower berth for me (of course, I still had to pay the fare). In the process of entrepreneurship, one of the biggest challenges is dealing with complex interpersonal relationships with various people. For a highly educated doctor, the books he touches every day are cold, even a bit outdated. Obviously, in actual combat, he must lose to those experienced old hands.

I have watched many episodes of CCTV's "Win in China". It is surprising that there are very few contestants with doctoral degrees participating in this program, and it is even more surprising that the contestants who are often admired by the judges tend to have lower educational levels. Someone says this is an era of farmer entrepreneurs. Before, I didn't believe it, but now I start to think it makes sense. Then what makes a doctor worse than a farmer? Is a senior intellectual who has read for decades actually failing himself? We need to deeply reflect on what makes the doctor of this era worse than the farmer?

Thirdly, the practical ability of a doctor is far inferior to that of a farmer. A master's degree in China is very necessary, because undergraduate education has become rampant. Now for all undergraduates, reading a master's degree may not necessarily be useful, but not reading it is definitely useless. However, within the foreseeable 5 years, a doctorate is not necessarily a required academic qualification. If you don't want to do academic research, then it's better to spend these three years practicing in society and accumulating the law of social survival. Entrepreneurship is real-life experience, absolutely not theoretical exposition. A theoretically simple matter may be extremely complex at the operational level. Chinese doctors are basically grown up in papers. These doctors are not only grown up in papers, but also swollen in them, making them feel omnipotent. It is a miracle if such people succeed in entrepreneurship. Looking at farmers, which farmer doesn't start practicing from the first day? Although their knowledge base is not as broad as that of a doctor, every piece of knowledge they learn in practice can be applied flexibly. On the contrary, a doctor may have 100 theories but can hardly apply any of them flexibly in life.
