Riding Diary: The clear water is greener than the sky, and the smoke from a few houses can be seen clearly.

by ditan360 on 2010-06-28 09:56:24

Low Carbon Network exclusive report: At this time, a rhythmic melody drifted from the distance, ethnic songs penetrating the vastness of the grasslands and the emptiness of the lake surface. We cycled towards the sound and discovered that the singing was emanating from a herdsman's tent. The powerful speakers played a toasting song that lingered in our ears. Here, the altitude is 3155 meters.

After riding for a while longer, I saw the three words "Dao Tang River" on a blue sign by the roadside and felt very puzzled. So, I asked the boss of a Halal restaurant by the road. The boss wore a small white felt hat and stood at the door, pointing to the distant Riyue Mountain as he said: Originally, Qinghai Lake water flowed through the Dao Tang River into the Yellow River. Later, due to tectonic movements, the Riyue Mountain rose, blocking the outflow of Qinghai Lake water. As a result, the river now flows back into Qinghai Lake, hence its name. He adjusted his hat with his hand, smiling, and said he had also heard it from someone else.

At the crossroads of the Dao Tang River, we turned right onto National Highway 109, bypassing the Riyue Mountain. As we continued cycling, more road signs appeared along the way, with kilometer numbers painted in white paint every ten meters. When I mentally counted up to 151, I passed through a small town. This time, the sign read: "Welcome to Erlang Mountain Scenic Area."

Under the slanting sunlight, we drove swiftly forward and arrived at Hatu Inn at 151 kilometers at 5:00 PM. The inn was extremely cheap, offering a large dormitory bed.

Since it was still early, under the guidance of the locals, we hired a minivan to head to Mount Erlang to view the panoramic view of Qinghai Lake. Climbing to an altitude of 3630 meters, we looked down upon Qinghai Lake, where clouds swirled above the lake surface under the blue sky, making it look like a beautiful mirror. The flowers and grass nearby seemed like decorations on the frame of the mirror. Under the setting sun, the mountains and rivers were bathed in golden light.

The driver said that this height wasn't enough and the scenery was somewhat diminished. We then rushed all the way to Luhe Pass at an altitude of 3740 meters, where a cluster of prayer flags fluttered in the wind at the pass. Standing on the cliff edge, the most beautiful scenery in the world came into view; lush distant mountains encircled around; birds soared in the sky. The vast grassland stretched endlessly, green like a carpet, dotted with cattle and sheep moving like clouds. The clear lake water shimmered with ripples, connecting with the sky. Fish leapt joyfully. Herdsmen's tents were scattered like stars. Everything was dreamlike and surreal, leaving one feeling refreshed and serene.

Being high up, the cold became unbearable. By the time we returned to Hatu Inn, the sky had gradually darkened. My heart was filled with uncontrollable excitement. I thought, heaven has bestowed upon us such breathtaking beauty, and if we remain stubbornly blind to it, our wasteful actions will surely be punished by nature. Humanity should lend an ear to listen - our beautiful Qinghai Lake is crying.
