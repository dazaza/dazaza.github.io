Mimosa-like Love

The wind was warm, the sun high. Birdsong filled the air, and flower shadows overlapped. I still remember a long, long time ago when I lived alone in that enchanted secluded valley. That afternoon, I lay among the large patches of mimosa in front of the wooden house, lost in thought about the man who had haunted my dreams.

How long has it been since I last saw you? Are you well?

"Ajiao..." Suddenly, that clear voice like a spring gushed forth. It's you...

I crouched among the pink-purple flowers, hiding my figure with the vast field of mimosa. Through the gaps, I finally saw that familiar face. It really is you...

By your side is another woman, her black hair flowing, her eyebrows delicate, her bright eyes glancing around, her red lips and white teeth shining, her jade fingers and fair arms exposed. You gently embraced the woman called "Ajiao" by you, smiling broadly.

"Ajiao, from now on, you will be my concubine. Believe me, if I have Ajiao, I will keep her in a golden house forever. For life and eternity, never to part." You said with unwavering determination.

She, lowered her head, smiled silently.

At this moment, my eyes were wet...

Night fell, and among the pink-purple blooms, there were shimmering green dots flickering. You held her as she drifted off to sleep in the carriage. Outside the carriage, I stood far away, gazing intently.

It was the first time I had missed out, for the so-called freedom, for the supposed prohibition between humans and spirits, I let you slip away. The unbearable heartache of losing you, I have tasted enough. So this time, I will not let you escape again. I tightly pursed my cherry lips, making a firm decision. Closing my eyes, the mimosa mark on my forehead lit up, transforming into specks of pink-purple entering her heart, from then on, she would no longer be herself...

Dawn broke, the carriage finally drove out of the secluded valley, slowly heading towards the palace you spoke of. From Ajiao's memories, I finally understood, the elder princess, also my current "mother," that ambitious schemer. She sought to continue her royal favor. She treated me as merchandise to help her control power. She said, your beauty could conquer any man.

From this moment on, I truly grasped the deep resentment and sorrow in Ajiao's heart. I think only when she faced you did that sadness turn into happiness. As a woman, I decided to help this woman whose body I borrowed.

When I arrived at the palace, I learned that today was the execution day of the former crown prince Liu Rong.

Liu Rong died a terrible death. Suffering humiliation, he met his end unjustly. He only wanted some water. But no one in the palace dared to bring him any. When I approached, his eyes shone brightly, and he whispered something shyly to me.

No one knew that Liu Rong confessed his love for me in his final moments. A love that couldn't be realized, more accurately, for the original Ajiao. His mother refused simply because she wouldn't allow her son to have love.

He accepted death calmly after finally speaking these words.

I cried loudly. The whole room was filled with sorrow. I didn't know why I was crying, for the man I met for the first time and would meet for the last time. Perhaps, it was Ajiao's soul crying deeply.

That was the first time I faced death so vividly. I watched Prince Liu Rong's body slowly, slowly come to a stop.

Until silence reigned.

That night, we hid under an old locust tree, bathed in moonlight. Neither wanting to return to the palace. You said, if not born into the imperial family, how wonderful it would be, rising with the sun and resting with its setting, without strife, without death, peacefully and plainly.

We stayed from dusk till dawn until discovered by the palace maids.

Not long after, I married you. I became the Crown Princess. Finally, completing my dream, or perhaps also Ajiao's dream. I remember, that day in Chang'an, countless commoners stood by the roadside blessing us. I sat in the felt carriage, dreaming a long and splendid dream.

I dreamed of myself, gray-haired, the hand of the man holding mine was still yours. Then, you too had aged significantly, your face full of wrinkles. You smiled warmly at me. You called me, Ajiao, Ajiao.

Awakening, you stood by the felt carriage. You softly and affectionately told me, I want you to fulfill my vow.

You took me to the Changmen Palace.

A dazzling sea of gold greeted my eyes. You said, I have finally fulfilled my promise to you, if I have Ajiao, I wish to keep her in a golden house. Look, isn't this a golden house?

Tears welled up in my eyes on the spot. So, the words you spoke amidst the mimosa were true?

You promised each thing you said, keeping them all in mind. Never forgetting. You said, you are the only woman I, Liu Che, have ever loved. You are my only concubine, and will be the sole empress of Han. I will only dote on you.

The promises made back then were sincere. I believe, and Ajiao believes, they were indeed sincere.

Sincerity often leads to betrayal. Not meant to be taken seriously. However, women always believe everything will remain unchanged forever. Thus, they easily trust promises, easily believe lies, and ultimately place their faith entirely in love.

You really, as you said, exclusively favored me. When you became emperor, you crowned me as empress. Everything was perfect.

Changle Palace. The residence of successive empresses. But compared to the golden house you built for me, I preferred to live in the Changmen Palace. Therefore, during my years as empress, Changle Palace remained empty.

I don't know how grand or luxurious Changle Palace is. I don't care. Just as I don't care about the empress title, but only care about you.

I stayed in Changmen Palace, just because it was the fairy tale you created for me. I thought, staying in the castle, the fairy tale would last a lifetime.

Until the fairy tale disappeared, other women moved into Changle Palace, then I believed, love is so fragile and fleeting. Nightmares eventually came, that woman, the one who cruelly snatched you away from me, finally appeared.

I knew, in that Pingyang Princess's mansion, when you saw the singer named Wei Zifu, your heart was already captured by her. At that moment, I began to understand that in love, there is also a word called jealousy.

That day, I lost my temper with you. Saying sharp and cutting words, I burst into tears at the end. If at that moment, you patted my shoulder, or didn't need to do anything, say nothing, just stayed by my side, perhaps I would still believe you loved me.

You walked away without looking back. You said, you are becoming increasingly unreasonable. Don't forget, I am the Emperor.

I heard that night, you went to the Pingyang Princess's mansion. You favored a singer named Wei Zifu. So easily, you betrayed that eternal love.

Not long after, you announced like an edict that you would take Wei Zifu as a concubine. Your resolve was unshakable. You wouldn't allow me to oppose. All my accumulated resentment surged up. Thus, I expressed my dissatisfaction through what you considered irrational tantrums. I shouted loudly at you, aren't you just interested in watching people dance? I can too, and I dance better than her. With a face full of disgust, you left, leaving my soul and Ajiao's lonely heart dancing sadly indoors.

Soon, Wei Zifu became your new favorite. Changmen Palace rarely saw your shadow anymore. The palace maidens would gather in groups, whispering and mocking the final outcome of the 'golden house' story. They all speculated when the woman named Wei Zifu would replace the empress.

They all say Wei Zifu is humble, compassionate, and tolerant. You said the same to me.

So, I didn't tell you about the witchcraft calamity being framed upon me by her.

With a hundred mouths, I couldn't defend myself; you had already determined it was my doing.

The massive search and capture operation commenced. Your face was cold like ice. Wei Zifu looked pitiful and weak. She leaned beside you.

Finally, you came to Changmen Palace, bringing the entire cold night of Chang'an towards me. You said, you have a venomous heart and narrow-mindedness. Unsuitable to be the empress. Hence, you specially issued an edict to depose the empress. At that moment, my soul was crying, Ajiao's heart was bleeding with tears. But you couldn't see, you would never see...

That day, I finally danced the first dance in my life that someone appreciated. Yet it was during my abandonment.

That day, the entire city of Chang'an cried. Hundreds of people involved in the witchcraft incident were decapitated and displayed publicly. It was said that even the execution supervisor was moved.

That day, Wei Zifu was crowned as empress.

Later events, I cannot recount anymore, yet the reality of their existence pierced Ajiao's heart needle by needle, piercing me too. What saddened me wasn't Wei Zifu's deep plotting, nor her poisonous schemes, nor the palace maidens' hidden sarcasm, but the deep distrust and disgust in your eyes...

Later, a great fire occurred at Changmen Palace. It burned for an entire day. Once, the magnificent pillars, halls, and pearl curtains turned into ruins overnight.

I carried this beautiful and pure body, taking one last deep look at you outside the flames, laughing amidst tears and fire, laughing so tragically, so sorrowfully...

The body, carrying the soul, carrying Ajiao's heart, carrying those wind-like beautiful memories, transformed once more into specks of pink-purple, scattering, scattering...

Still, I couldn't bear to leave. The mimosa plant with two souls, two loves, deeply rooted itself in the corner of the garden wall of the golden house, swaying gracefully...
