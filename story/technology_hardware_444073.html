The magical fruits and vegetables that make you more charming

by fkflr938 on 2011-06-10 14:53:18

Although autumn has arrived, the weather remains scorching with a blazing sun and stifling air, while the wind has disappeared. This has led to frequent health crises for many women, including heatstroke, summer colds, insomnia, fatigue, lack of appetite, and more. Are there effective measures to resolve these health issues? Recently, Dr. Lü Li, head of the Nutrition Department at the Armed Police General Hospital, pointed out in her newly published book "Health Alkaline Method" that the secret to maintaining good health is keeping the body in a slightly alkaline state, and natural fruit and vegetable juices are a shortcut to adding alkalinity to the body. Especially during hot weather, adding some alkalinity to the body is undoubtedly the best way to stay healthy and beautiful.

Studies have shown: Among various balances in the human body, the most important and fundamental balance is acid-alkaline equilibrium. Only when the body fluids are slightly alkaline will all bodily functions be optimal. Once the acid-alkaline balance of body fluids is disrupted, the body begins to "rob Peter to pay Paul," leading to chaos in various systems and the onset of various diseases.

An acidic constitution is closely related to obesity and an aged appearance. This is because the state of acid-alkaline balance indicates normal metabolic function in the human body. The powerful self-regulating function of the human body constantly adjusts and corrects all imbalances in the body. However, in an acidic environment, fat cells cannot metabolize normally and gradually accumulate. When body fluids are too acidic, normal blood circulation is affected, metabolism slows down, internal organ functions decline, and the secretion of sebum and sweat glands is also impacted. The skin then begins to show symptoms such as allergies, and various skin diseases follow, such as rough skin, pigment deposition, dull complexion, and easy formation of wrinkles.

The acidity or alkalinity of human body fluids is also related to intelligence quotient (IQ). Within the allowable range of body fluid acidity or alkalinity, when body fluids are acidic, IQ is lower; when they are alkaline, IQ is higher.

Therefore, adding some alkalinity to the body has been increasingly confirmed by researchers, and fresh fruits and fruit and vegetable juices from nature are delicious ways to add alkalinity to the body.

Summer is the season when fruits are most abundant. Faced with various kinds of fruits, how should you choose? Why not start with the king of fruits! According to a report from Rutgers University in New Brunswick, New Jersey, USA, among 27 of the most popular fruits, kiwifruit, with an index of 16, is the fruit with the highest nutritional density. Next come papaya (14), honeydew melon (13), strawberry (12), mango, orange, lemon (11). The report shows: Kiwifruit contains 1.7 times more vitamin C than oranges; 1.7 times more dietary fiber than pineapples; twice as much vitamin E as cherries; 3.3 times more antioxidants than tomatoes; and 2.5 times more calcium than bananas. It also has the characteristic of being "low in sodium but high in potassium" and is a rich source of magnesium and zinc trace elements. The title of "King of Fruits" is well-deserved!

In short, kiwifruit is No.1 in terms of plant-based physiological activating substances!

1. Highest nutrition: DV/100g (the percentage of the daily recommended intake provided by each 100 grams of fruit) is the highest among 27 types of fruits.

2. Lowest calories: Measured as having the lowest CPN (Calories Per Nutrient, the number of calories per unit of nutrients).

3. Best source of vitamin C: The DV value of vitamin C in kiwifruit is 230%, 1.7 times that of oranges, making it one of the fruits sufficient to supply the daily recommended amount of vitamin C, measured as the best source of vitamin C.

Additionally, according to a report in the "Lianhe Zaobao," eating kiwifruit before bed can make sleep sweeter. This is the result of a survey conducted by Taipei Medical University. Professor Liu Zhenfang of the university's Health and Nutrition Department revealed at a press conference in Singapore that there have always been claims online that eating kiwifruit benefits sleep, so she decided to confirm this claim scientifically.

She surveyed 29 people with sleep disorders. The results showed that if sleep-disordered individuals consistently eat two kiwifruits one hour before bedtime for a month, the time from getting into bed to falling asleep can be shortened by 39%. The time patients wake up deeply at night is reduced by 43%, and total sleep time is extended by 14%.

This is because kiwifruit is rich in calcium, magnesium, and vitamin C, which help soothe the central nervous system. In particular, calcium has a calming effect on the nerves. Although these nutrients can be obtained from different foods, kiwifruit simultaneously possesses these three substances with low calories, making it very suitable for consumption before bed.

To achieve the effect of improving sleep, patients must consume kiwifruit regularly over a long period. Studies also show that when the same group of patients only eats kiwifruit for four days, their sleep does not improve significantly. However, if too many kiwifruits are eaten before bed, it may lead to indigestion, affecting sleep.

Of course, it's not just the king of fruits, kiwifruit; any fruits and vegetables originating from nature are the best methods to add alkalinity for better health. To address the health crises of extreme heat, insomnia, summer colds, fatigue, loss of appetite, and weight gain, please use the magical fruit and vegetable method recommended by Director Lü Li.

A refreshing breeze in the heat: Melon mustard greens lemon juice

Ingredients: 1/2 melon, 1 bunch of mustard greens, 2 slices of lemon

Method: Wash the melon, remove the seeds and pulp, and cut it into 2 cm pieces; wash the mustard greens and chop them finely. Remove the peel and seeds from the lemon. Add one cup of water to the juicer, then add the above ingredients and juice.

Heatwave countermeasure: Melon is rich in vitamins A and C, as well as potassium and fiber, aiding digestion, diuresis, and preventing bacterial infections; mustard greens provide calcium, and this juice calms irritability.

Turn into a sleeping beauty: Grapefruit mint lemon juice

Ingredients: 1 grapefruit, 4 mint leaves, 1/4 lemon

Method: Peel and seed the grapefruit and lemon, cut into small pieces, and juice together with the mint leaves.

Heatwave countermeasure: The sweet smell of lemon can calm the mind, showing noticeable sedative effects when taken at night.

Weight loss: Grapefruit waxberry juice

Ingredients: 1/2 grapefruit, 15 waxberries

Method: Peel and seed the grapefruit, tear the pulp into small pieces, add an appropriate amount of pure water, and juice with the waxberries.

Heatwave countermeasure: Waxberries contain components similar to capsaicin, and the sugar in grapefruit can be immediately burned as energy without allowing fat accumulation.

Destroy summer colds: Apple lotus root juice

Ingredients: 1/2 apple, 1/2 sweet pepper, 50 grams of lotus root

Method: Wash and seed the apple, cut into 2 cm pieces; wash the sweet pepper, remove the stem and seeds, cut into small pieces, add half a cup of pure water, and juice all three ingredients together.

Heatwave countermeasure: When the body is fatigued, immunity decreases, making it easy to catch a summer cold. Fruit and vegetable juice replenishes vitamins and sugars, naturally strengthening the body's immunity.

For night owls: Kiwifruit orange juice

Ingredients: 1 kiwifruit, 1 orange, 1 tomato

Method: Wash and peel the kiwifruit, wash and peel the orange, remove the seeds, blanch the tomato and peel, cut each into small pieces; juice separately, finally adding lemon juice and honey for flavoring.

Heatwave countermeasure: Kiwifruit can block the synthesis of carcinogens in the body, providing some preventive effect against various cancers. It also aids in treating hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and coronary heart disease. The three fruits comprehensively replenish vitamins, rescuing the weary bodies and skins of night owls.
