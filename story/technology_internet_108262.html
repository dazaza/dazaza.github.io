Zhou Chengyu, the agent of Huang Jing, the protagonist of the Asustek rights protection case, revealed on the afternoon of the 2nd that he would formally submit a complaint to the Beijing First Intermediate People's Court at 10 am on the 3rd, claiming a total of 5 million yuan to Asustek, and at the same time require Asustek to close for rectification and revoke its business license.

Zhou Chengyu told Sina Technology on the afternoon of the 2nd that he, Huang Jing and his lawyer will go to the Beijing First Intermediate People's Court on the morning of the 3rd and formally submit a civil complaint to the court at 10 am to Sue Asustek. The complaint Zhou Chengyu presented to Sina Technology shows that Huang Jing has a total of six lawsuit requests, they are:

First, the ruling ordered Asustek to return 20,900 yuan, while increasing the compensation of twice the purchase price, a total of 41,800 yuan.

Second, Asustek was ordered to pay the plaintiff a total of 259,840 yuan in appraisal fees, notary fees, bank storage fees, transportation expenses, loss of time and other expenses related to the case.

Third, ordered Asustek to stop the infringement and apologize to the plaintiff, eliminate the impact, and restore its reputation.

Fourth, Asustek was ordered to pay compensation of 469,360 yuan for mental loss and personal loss to the plaintiff.

5. Ordered Asustek to suspend business for rectification and revoke its business license.

6. Asustek was ordered to bear all legal costs of the case

Previously, Zhou Chengyu has been claiming that "Intel that hides Asus" is not among the defendants in this prosecution. From Huang Jing's indictment, it can be calculated that the total amount of the claim is 5 million yuan, after he claimed the amount of $500 from Asus, according to the exchange rate calculation, the middle difference is nearly seven times.

Asustek company officials said that they have learned that Zhou Chengyu will Sue Asustek on the morning of the 3rd, but the other party has not commented on this.

The case against Asustek began in 2006 when Huang Jing, then a junior at the Capital University of Economics and Business, bought an Asus laptop. According to Huang Jing, there was a problem with the use of the computer, and it was later tested to use a beta version of the processor, and Huang Jing's agent Zhou Chengyu claimed $5 million from Asus. Asustek later filed a complaint for extortion, and Huang was held in a detention center for nearly 10 months. Huang Jing received nearly 30,000 yuan in state compensation on November 27, an average of less than 100 yuan per day.
