Look forward to the turn of the next millennium.

by malayo on 2012-02-15 12:54:43

In the winter month, the cold wind whistles desolately. It seems that everything has turned into an untouchable chill.

My thoughts linger in the freezing night. The wind sings repeatedly a sorrowful melody, melancholic and plaintive. I pick up my pen and sketch a few light strokes. What slips away is an endless sadness and deep longing.

Who is it that, in the most beautiful moment of this life, unlocked the yearning I had locked away? Gazing out the window on this cold night, the moon appears so full.

Meeting you in this life has brightened my entire lifetime's scenery. For me, it surpasses a thousand years. I gather the longings from my previous life into sentences, and compile the waitings of this life into a collection, only hoping that you can read, within those words formed with heartfelt tears, the anticipation of a yearning heart.

Dear, let me write for you some unwritten words. Between the lines, they hide your lifelong brilliance and my present-life attachment.

Dear, let me paint for you a faint sketch. In every stroke, there lies your warm and adorable smile.

Dear, let me recite to you a poem called "That Day, That Moon, That Year." If I could gain the heart of one person, I would surely not betray this person, this affection, this rare love in this life.

We missed each other thousands of years ago. Yet after thousands of years, we meet again in this world, where you reveal your face.

You wield your brush and ink extravagantly in the picturesque Jiangnan, your talent as poetic as ever. Meeting you in this life is our destined connection.

Dear, how did you encounter me at my most beautiful moment?

Was it because in our past lives, we ceaselessly prayed before Buddha, asking him to grant us a touching relationship in this life?

Therefore, you came riding your horse without pause, traveling along the flower-lined path; I danced with the wind, crossing thousands of waters and mountains. In an instant, the icy memories of the past life were melted by your warm gaze. In this cold winter night, I suddenly felt a gentle breeze blowing past, butterflies fluttering gracefully, as if it were April in this earthly realm.

On many sleepless nights, sitting alone in front of the computer, even though the keyboard heats up my fingertips, I still cannot convey the warmth I want to give you.

This is an unfinished mortal bond, distant yet close, enduring through countless changes. Dear, I do not care about the remoteness of distance, looking forward to the next thousand-year cycle.
