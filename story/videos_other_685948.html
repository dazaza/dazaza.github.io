Experts from the Canadian Quebec Study Abroad Network point out that, from the perspective of technical immigration domestically, the scope of industries is becoming narrower and the standards are becoming higher. The amount of assets required for investment immigration has doubled, the investment amount has doubled, and the quota is even "hard to come by". Studying in Canada first to obtain a degree and then an identity might be a more powerful tool for young people to immigrate to Canada today, a highly cost-effective path.

Firstly, after graduating from a university in Quebec, Canada, one can obtain a three-year open work visa. This means that students can stay in Canada for three years after graduation from a Quebec university, which provides more favorable conditions for gradually adapting to the local environment and smoothly finding a job after leaving school.

Secondly, having one year of working experience in Quebec, Canada, and holding a long-term stable work contract, one can apply for immigration. The review and approval cycle is about one year.

Finally, the above-mentioned Chinese students, regardless of whether they study at a college, undergraduate, or master's level in Quebec, Canada, are all applicable. This reflects the Canadian government's full recognition of the academic level and work ability of students who can successfully graduate from a university in Quebec, Canada.

"Experience-based Immigration" mainly targets international students and foreign workers with local working experience. The approval time for this type of immigration application is usually faster, and the rejection rate is lower.

Compared with Australian international students who are in tears due to the tightening of immigration policies, overseas graduates from Quebec, Canada, have received many benefits when applying for immigration recently.

Facing the new immigration project "Experience-based Immigration", international students have mixed reactions. Some take a proactive attitude as the "first to try" and apply immediately; others are unwilling to take risks and choose to apply for the relatively stable Federal Skilled Immigration. Experts believe that overseas graduates choosing "Experience-based Immigration" will have more advantages than choosing "Skilled Immigration".

Firstly, the average application time for Canadian "Experience-based Immigration" in 2009 was only 5-6 months, and the rejection rate was only around 4%, the lowest among all immigration categories; while the average application time for Canadian Skilled Immigration in 2009 was approximately 12-14 months, with those belonging to the "38 shortage occupations" requiring about 6-12 months and having a higher approval rate; those not belonging to the "shortage occupations" may drag on for 5 years, with a high rejection rate.

Secondly, "Experience-based Immigration" is not restricted by the "38 shortage occupations" for skilled immigration, greatly increasing the immigration opportunities for graduates in other majors. There are fewer restrictions on "Experience-based Immigration" compared to skilled immigration. As long as the work meets the NOC (National Occupational Classification of Canada) O, A, B categories, it can be used to apply for "Experience-based Immigration", and as long as the applicant obtains 12 months of working experience within the previous 24 months, part-time work with sufficient hours also qualifies.

Finally, "Experience-based Immigration" does not have a hard requirement that the applicant's work must be related to their major, whereas skilled immigration does have such requirements.
