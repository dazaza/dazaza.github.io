Yesterday, the younger students of grades one and two at Xiangyang No.1 School received special care. In the first lesson of the school year, everyone got a big "red envelope." However, this "red envelope" did not contain lucky money but rather the teachers' wishes for the new semester.

Xiangyang No.1 School teachers gave out "red envelopes" to students.

Huang Jiaxin is a student in grade six, class one at Lotus Elementary School. A few years ago, her father passed away due to illness, and just after this year's Spring Festival, her mother collapsed from exhaustion after working two jobs and left her forever. Now, she lives with her 89-year-old grandmother who collects recyclables daily to supplement their income. Helping the struggling Jiaxin became the top priority for the school in the new semester. Guo Yixuan, a student in grade four, class three, donated 500 yuan, which was meant for buying new glasses. She said the old pair still worked fine, so she didn't need to buy new ones yet.

"Students, there is a sudden fire in the southeast corner of the third floor library. Please do not panic, leave the classroom in order..." Yesterday morning, the campus broadcast of Unity Road Primary School suddenly sounded the alarm. Students quickly put down their books, picked up towels or cloths from their desks, covered their mouths, and orderly jogged out of the classroom.

Searching for smiles is part of the "Angel of Love One-Ten-Hundred-Thousand" project initiated by Hunan No.2 School. It includes "One Letter" - every student writes a letter to their parents, relatives, friends, neighbors, community staff, or even the mayor, expressing their understanding and opinions about the National Games held in Hunan; "Ten Proposals" - every elementary school student discovers problems and disharmonious phenomena in the city as a little owner, proposes suggestions, and the school ultimately selects the ten fairest proposals to report to relevant departments; "Hundred Sentences, Hundred Faces" - every student learns a hundred common English phrases and simultaneously participates in the "Smiling Shenyang" activity organized by the city, collecting and photographing a hundred of the most beautiful smiles inside and outside the school, welcoming guests from all over the country with the purest smiles; "Thousand Deeds, Thousand Hearts" - all 1022 Young Pioneers in the school do practical things for the National Games, and each personally makes a sincere gift for the National Games.

Every student looked around attentively at the blackboard, watching Teacher Wang write while reciting the strokes of the characters. Some students even stretched out their little hands, following along on their desks, looking incredibly focused!

That day, the entire school raised a total of 20,000 yuan for Jiaxin. The school plans to use this money to establish a "Jiaxin Love Fund," paying whenever she needs it.

Erjing No.2 School determined the time from 1:00 PM to 2:30 PM every Wednesday as "Class-Based Course" time. "Class-Based Courses" are divided into three categories: brain-training mini-games, classic reading, and skill enhancement. Specific "Class-Based Courses" are freely chosen by each class based on student interests and other conditions. Vice Principal Han Xuedan of Erjing No.2 School stated that the school strictly controls homework volume, with no written homework for grades one and two. The school leaves ample time for students to participate in sports activities. Each student masters fancy rope skipping and basketball skills.
