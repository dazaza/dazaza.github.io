When the weather is dry, indoor air needs to be humidified to ensure comfort and health, which is a well-known fact, thus creating a booming market. However, when encountering prolonged rainy weather, the air becomes damp, leading to issues such as clothes and bedding getting moist, water seeping through floors and walls, paint peeling off, and mold forming on furniture and books. The answer, of course, is to use a dehumidifier to drive out the indoor moisture. This creates a need. According to the development situation of the humidifier market, the dehumidifier market should also become popular. But in reality, China's dehumidifier market has always been lukewarm.

Because of the inconvenience caused by humid air, most people only passively deal with it, waiting for the sun to come out and quickly moving things out of the room to dry them under the sun.

Firstly, consumers' awareness of consumption is not enough. Humans have a wide tolerance range for air humidity, and generally, only 2-3 months of the year are considered the humid season. Most consumers usually adopt a method of enduring it.

Additionally, the dehumidifying function of air conditioners can easily make consumers overlook the necessity of dehumidifiers, viewing them merely as luxury home appliances.

For this reason, experts point out that although China's dehumidifier market is currently small, with the development of the economy and the improvement of people's living standards, its future prospects are worth paying attention to. In the coming years, the普及 (popularization) of dehumidifiers in Chinese households will be similar to the普及 (popularization) of home air conditioners, and commercial applications will inevitably achieve significant progress.

Regarding the current state of dehumidifiers, experts point out that although the performance of the dehumidifier market is not entirely satisfactory, its market potential cannot be ignored. Especially with the improvement in people's living standards and increased attention to health, the potential of the dehumidifier market is beginning to emerge.

According to reports, the development of domestic dehumidifiers in China has a history of more than ten years but remains at an early stage. The overall capacity is very limited, far from reaching scale. According to statistics from the Ministry of Commerce, exports are currently the main sales direction for China's home dehumidifiers. In 2005, the total sales volume of China's dehumidifier industry was 2.5 million units, of which nearly 96% were for export. Although exports have increased rapidly, domestic market demand has not yet opened up. In 2005, the entire year's domestic sales amounted to only 72,000 units, accounting for only 3% of total sales. On one hand, consumers have widespread potential demand for dehumidifiers, while on the other hand, market performance is not entirely satisfactory. The primary reason for this conflicting status quo is the low level of public awareness about dehumidifiers. Experts point out that the reasons for this phenomenon mainly include the following three aspects:

Thirdly, insufficient promotion. Currently, home dehumidifiers are generally in a natural sales state, and manufacturers usually do not assign sales promoters. There is also a serious lack of promotional efforts for dehumidifiers, resulting in a significant information gap between producers and consumers.

Secondly, limitations due to consumption levels. The material cost of dehumidifiers is close to that of air conditioners (the cost of a 12-liter dehumidifier is equivalent to a 1-horsepower air conditioner). Additionally, due to the small market demand, production is difficult to form a scale advantage, leading to prices similar to those of air conditioners, making it hard for general consumers to accept.

China’s vast territory experiences significant climate variations between the north and south. The north has less rainfall and drier weather, so humidifiers are essential for home life; the south has more precipitation and humid air, so dehumidifiers should be busy. Especially during the plum rain season in the Yangtze River Basin, with continuous drizzle and exceptionally high humidity, theoretically, dehumidifiers should be as favored by consumers as humidifiers are in the northern regions during winter and spring. However, investigations reveal that dehumidifiers have remained lukewarm in the southern and even nationwide markets.

Medical research reports indicate that the relative humidity of the human survival environment should ideally be between 60%-70%. High humidity not only facilitates the generation of influenza but also easily causes diseases like rheumatism and arthritis, endangering human health. The role of dehumidifiers lies in maintaining the air at a relative humidity of 50%-60% through the operation of internal compressors and utilizing the circulation of air to liquefy moisture into droplets, thereby creating a healthy living environment for consumers.
