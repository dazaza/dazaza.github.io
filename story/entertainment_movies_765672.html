Synopsis:

A smart and kind-hearted five-year-old girl named Lilo (voiced by Daveigh Chase) loves listening to Elvis Presley's old songs and has always dreamed of owning her own pet dog. One day, with the help of her older sister, she takes in a stray dog and names him Stitch (voiced by Chris Sanders), hoping that Stitch will add some fun to her life. However, what Lilo doesn't know is that Stitch isn't just a poor stray dog; he's actually a dangerous alien creature with six legs who sneaked away to Earth during his exile.

To hide his identity on Earth, Stitch has no choice but to "endure humiliation," hiding two of his legs to make himself look like an ordinary dog. As Lilo and Stitch spend time together, the little girl's kindness and innocence turn them into inseparable best friends. Meanwhile, the aliens who created Stitch discover his escape and immediately launch a full-scale search across the universe to capture him and bring him back.

When the aliens finally find Stitch disguised on Earth, they kidnap Lilo to force Stitch into compliance. To rescue his good friend and prove that he's not beyond redemption, Stitch must use all his skills and fight with everything he's got...including showing off all six of his legs.

Behind-the-scenes Production:

This animated film reflects Disney's innovative approach, incorporating many sci-fi elements. While it may not leave a stunning impression due to some abrupt transitions, the visuals diverge from the popular 3D animation style of the time and instead return to a classic 2D watercolor aesthetic, restoring the original authenticity of animation. The story's visual features are very distinct—beaches, surfing, parties, dances—all brimming with the vibrant Hawaiian atmosphere. With Disney’s signature clever humor sprinkled throughout, the film becomes even more entertaining. Though not Disney's most iconic work, it carries modern sensibilities, provides educational value without being preachy, making it relatable for parents as well.
