Mobile skull virus robs millions of users, the culprit is Guosheng Mingdao Company- Nanjing SEO吧

by maomaochaoren on 2010-03-26 08:56:51

Since the advent of computer networks, viruses have appeared, and now the proliferation of mobile smart systems has given criminals another way to make money. The virus called "Mobile Skull" has caused losses for nearly a million users. Currently, Kingsoft Antivirus claims that the main culprit behind this scheme is Beijing Guosheng Mingdao Technology Co., Ltd.

Recently, a Trojan virus named "Mobile Skull" has been consuming large amounts of phone credit from victimized mobile users through mass text messages and multimedia messages (MMS), while also stealing personal information from phones, causing panic among smartphone users.

On March 25th, Li Xiaoguang from the marketing department of Kingsoft Antivirus Security Lab told *The Daily Economic News* that they have identified the creator of the virus as Beijing Guosheng Mingdao Technology Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Guosheng Mingdao Company). Due to the malicious behavior of the virus actively sending infected text messages and aggressively making money, Kingsoft has reported the company to the public security authorities, who have already opened an investigation.

The mastermind behind "Mobile Skull" has been identified.

According to reports by *The Daily Economic News*, all victimized mobile users would receive text messages with the following content: "My phone has been controlled by Mobile Skull, the contact list has been uploaded to http:/tran****.com". If the recipient uses a NOKIA smartphone with Nokia's Symbian operating system Symbian60 third edition, once the user clicks on the link in the message and starts browsing the web, a program file named LanPackage.sisx will be downloaded. If the user accidentally opens it, the program will falsely claim to be the system's Chinese language package. If the user clicks "Yes", the virus will be installed on the phone.

Li Xiaoguang pointed out that if users of NOKIA S60 third edition smartphones unfortunately download and run the "Mobile Skull" virus program, it will wildly subscribe to various SP services. "The virus" will automatically send large amounts of MMS containing virus download links to the contacts in the phone's address book until the phone credit or battery is exhausted, bringing endless harassment and financial loss to the phone user.

Currently, Kingsoft Antivirus has conducted a detailed identification and reverse analysis of the virus, confirming that the creator of the "Skull" virus is indeed Guosheng Mingdao Company. Additionally, Kingsoft Antivirus has also reported Guosheng Mingdao Company to the Symbian Certification Center. Once confirmed, the company's Symbian certificate will be revoked.

When reporters from *The Daily Economic News* searched using the company name provided by Kingsoft Antivirus, no information about the company was found on the internet. It was also discovered that the company had not registered with the Beijing Administration for Industry and Commerce system.

"We estimate that Guosheng Mingdao Company is a small SP company; however, whether the company profited from this mobile virus still requires further investigation," said Li Xiaoguang.

It is introduced that the virus mainly targets smartphones with the widely used Symbian S603 series version operating system, including most Nokia phones and some Samsung phones.

The number of affected users could reach the hundred-thousand level.

"This is, so far, China's first true mobile virus," said Wang Yingqi, Technical Director of the Wireless Application Department at Kingsoft Antivirus Security Lab. He pointed out that previously, there were very few mobile malicious software that met the "virus" standard, and their spread was also very limited. This time, the "Mobile Skull" virus is a mobile malicious software that integrates both transmission and destruction, meeting the "virus" standard.

According to Wang Yingqi, "Mobile Skull" can only "commit crimes" after being clicked, downloaded, and installed, so it mainly targets smartphones with operating systems, because non-smartphones cannot install it. The reason why most of the victims use the Symbian system is that currently, 70% of smartphone users are using this system.

"The virus spreads very quickly. In the worst consumer complaints we received, within a few hours, the phone overheated, and the operator sent a text message reminder exceeding the maximum daily limit for sending MMS." Wang Yingqi estimated that the number of poisoned phones has reached the hundred-thousand level.

As for how "Mobile Skull" entered the operator's system to become the carrier of the virus, Wang Yingqi said that it might be due to the company randomly sending the virus body. "Randomly sending to 100 people, 10% get infected, then another 10%, it can become the source of the virus."

Security expert Shi Xiaohong from 360 Security pointed out that "Mobile Skull" can also connect to the internet to download other malicious plug-ins, used to steal the user's contact list, text messages, photos, videos, and other private files, even eavesdrop on user calls and locate the user's whereabouts. At the same time, this Trojan also has the ability to force automatic startup when the phone is turned on and block the system program management menu, making it difficult for ordinary users to uninstall normally. Users who fall victim will turn their phones into "zombies" with no secrets left.
