Baidu Bang ushers in a new era of pharmaceutical e-marketing

by memoryst on 2010-09-15 14:02:16

According to data from Nielsen, a third-party market research agency: From the perspective of advertisers, the investment in event sponsorship advertising has gradually increased since 2009, showing a steady upward trend; from the media revenue perspective, 42% of respondents believe that event sponsorship advertising has become an important part of increasing media income; and from the internet user perspective, 64% of internet users trust event sponsorship advertisements. 81% of internet users agree that sponsorship advertisements play a crucial role in promoting major events such as sports, politics, and culture. By leveraging major events, Betadine has ushered in a new era of pharmaceutical company relationship chain online marketing.

"The focus of the world in June and July is the World Cup. Fully utilizing the influence of the World Cup to maximize the brand communication of Betadine was our initial planning intention." On one hand, the World Cup is a single sports event with influence second only to the Olympics, bound to ignite nationwide attention, and marketing through the World Cup will inevitably amplify marketing effects geometrically. On the other hand, Tencent's unprecedented investment in the 2010 World Cup ensured the media reporting heat, providing a solid traffic foundation for World Cup marketing activities. However, how to connect Betadine and the World Cup, two seemingly parallel lines that can never intersect, and cleverly graft the value of the Betadine brand onto the football concept of the World Cup, became the first obstacle that Betadine's World Cup online marketing needed to break through.

Tencent provided Betadine with a clever entry point, namely "No fear of falling, play beautifully." "No fear of falling" well showcases the product feature of Betadine specializing in treating external wound infections, while "play beautifully" closely connects with the football matches of the World Cup. Additionally, "falling" and "fake falls" are themselves key points of focus for fans. This creative idea tightly combines the Betadine brand with the World Cup, effectively conveying the brand proposition of Betadine.

The OTC drug market manager of Smith & Nephew attributed the success of the Betadine World Cup community to three points: the right time, the right creativity, and the right promotion. The right time, as the name suggests, refers to the period of the World Cup. The right creativity refers to deeply mining the content of controversial topics to find the connection points between activities, brands, and products, such as using "fall" as the core of overall activity planning to interpret the World Cup from a new angle. The long-standing and controversial topic of "fake falls" attracts user attention and forms discussion topics. Using "no fear of falling" as positive guidance highlights the brand spirit and product function of Betadine. The right promotion refers to allocating promotional resources according to the World Cup schedule, directly converting the heat of the World Cup into marketing heat, achieving the maximum utilization of promotional resource values. For example, multiple interactive columns were set up within the community. Netizens could earn activity points after participating in activities and gain more points by inviting friends to participate or forwarding activity videos, enabling the active spread of activity information within the relationship network.

According to statistics provided by Tencent, the total number of registered users in the Betadine World Cup community reached over 1.19 million. Online marketing in the pharmaceutical industry can be said to be just starting, presenting both challenges and rare opportunities.
