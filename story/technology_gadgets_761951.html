Nurse dies after getting head trapped in elevator due to phone use

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 17:23:20

According to the Guangzhou Daily, at around 11:30 a.m. yesterday, in the No. 1 elevator of the Changhong Building on Songgang East Road in Shenzhen, an intern nurse from Pengcheng Hospital had her head caught in the elevator while riding it. However, the elevator did not stop running but instead directly dropped to the negative first floor, causing the woman to die on the spot due to an arterial rupture. The management office stated that the initial suspicion is that the accident was caused by an elevator malfunction. Currently, the safety supervision departments of the district and street have intervened in the investigation.Young Woman Dies After Being Caught in ElevatorYesterday afternoon, reporters arrived at the basement level one of the Changhong Building on Songgang East Road, where the police had already set up a cordon. The four elevators of the Changhong Building had all stopped operating, and the incident occurred in the No. 1 elevator.A deliveryman from a fast-food store, Mr. Chen, was inside the elevator when the incident happened. He told reporters that there were more than ten people in the No. 1 elevator, and the elevator had not correctly stopped at its position, being stuck between the third and second floors. However, the elevator door opened anyway. This young woman stepped out with a mobile phone in her hand.Possibly too engrossed in playing with her mobile phone, she didn't notice that the elevator was stopped in the interlayer. Mr. Chen said that she first extended her head and foot outside, and suddenly the elevator door closed, trapping her head inside. Thirty seconds later, the elevator suddenly fell, directly reaching the basement level one, where the elevator door then opened.A nurse from Pengcheng Hospital said that the deceased was her colleague, who was playing with her mobile phone and stepped out before the elevator was fully open, resulting in the elevator door closing immediately, trapping her arm. With the downward pull force of the elevator, her upper body was sucked into the elevator door gap. During the fall, the girl's neck was torn off, and blood continuously spilled out. When the elevator door opened, the girl collapsed.Subsequently, reporters learned from the 120 Emergency Center that medical staff arrived at the scene upon receiving the report, and the woman had already died, with no other injured individuals found at the scene.The Incident Elevator Was Just Maintained Last WeekFloors 1 to 4 of the Changhong Building are occupied by Pengcheng Hospital. Although the No. 1 elevator has signs indicating it is for medical use, residents also frequently use this elevator. Some residents mentioned that these elevators should be nearly 20 years old, and malfunctions are not uncommon.Mr. Ye, who works on the 15th floor, claimed that last June, the elevator also malfunctioned, falling from upstairs, fortunately without causing any casualties. A security guard who wished to remain anonymous also mentioned that he once experienced an abrupt drop in the elevator while traveling from the 22nd floor, but shortly after, the elevator returned to normal, and he was unharmed.Reporters subsequently asked Director Fan of the Changhong Building Management Office, who stated that he had never heard of frequent elevator malfunctions. The elevator maintenance was entrusted to a professional company, with weekly inspections. The most recent maintenance was last Sunday, during which no issues were detected. Reporters' requests to view the surveillance footage were denied by Director Fan.The Luohu District Work Safety Supervision Bureau reported that according to preliminary investigations, when the involved elevator descended to the third floor, passenger Ms. Wang (female, 24 years old, from Shanxi, intern at Shenzhen Pengcheng Hospital) attempted to exit the elevator when the elevator door suddenly closed and descended, causing her to be trapped and killed. Currently, relevant departments are conducting investigations.
