This year, the total volume of car exports by independent automakers is expected to exceed 1 million vehicles.

by jintaimqr on 2012-02-22 14:46:22

According to recent statistics from the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM), in January this year, Chinese automakers exported a total of 56,300 vehicles, representing a month-on-month increase of 0.49% and a year-on-year increase of 8.32%, achieving a strong start.

In 2011, Chinese automakers exported a total of 814,300 vehicles, an increase of 49.45% compared to the previous year, setting a new historical record. This marks the second consecutive year of rapid growth. CAAM optimistically predicts that the total vehicle exports by Chinese automakers could exceed one million units this year.

Against the backdrop of a sluggish domestic market, Chinese automakers are increasingly focusing on developing international markets. So, what strategies are Chinese automakers employing as they venture overseas?

**SAIC Motor: Returning to the UK**

Among domestic automakers, SAIC Motor's export volume accounts for a relatively small proportion. Starting this year, SAIC has chosen a "return" path for its exports—originating in the UK, reborn in China, and now returning to the UK.

In 2005, the century-old British MG Rover company went bankrupt. In 2007, SAIC Motor acquired the MG brand from the UK.

In April 2011, the European version of the MG6 car rolled off the assembly line at SAIC MG's UK facility. In June, the MG6 was officially launched in the UK market. This marked the first new MG model sold in the UK since 1995 and also the first large-scale production at the factory in Birmingham, England, since the bankruptcy of MG Rover in 2005. The launch of the MG6 garnered significant attention from the British media and consumers. As a local British automotive brand, MG had a glorious history spanning nearly 90 years, with fans ranging from royalty to celebrities and common people. For this "return," the media lavished praise, and the international professional car rental group Avis Europe pre-ordered 500 units before the official launch of the MG6.

The "return to the UK" of the MG brand benefited from SAIC's creation of a new model: "UK design, Chinese production, UK assembly." Since 2007, SAIC has established a European R&D center despite opposition. Subsequently, all models under the Roewe and MG brands have been primarily designed by European teams.

How can MG regain vitality and achieve greater success? To this end, SAIC has renovated the assembly line at its UK factory to accommodate the production and assembly of MG models and future new models. Over the next few years, SAIC plans to introduce multiple models into the UK market, starting with the MG3. Starting this year, the MG6 GT, which will be launched in the UK, will participate in the 2012 BTCC British Touring Car Championship and make its debut at the Brands Hatch circuit on April 1.

**Chery Automobile: Three-pronged Approach**

In January alone, Chery Automobile continued its rapid growth momentum from 2011 in the overseas market, exporting 11,823 vehicles, representing a year-on-year increase of 21.2% and a month-on-month increase of 25.1%. It broke the ten-thousand-unit mark in the first month, achieving a strong start.

Following last year's record-breaking 160,000 vehicle exports, Chery continues to prioritize cultivating overseas markets this year. In addition to adhering to the overseas development strategy of "going deep and taking root," Chery is adopting a three-pronged approach to comprehensively enhance its overseas business expansion capabilities.

Firstly, it has formed a joint venture with Israel Group to establish Qoros Automotive Company and create the new "Qoros" brand. It is reported that Qoros Automotive has a professional technical team of over 3,000 members from China, Europe, America, and the Asia-Pacific region. This team is currently collaborating with the renowned independent automobile R&D institution Magna Steyr of Austria to fully develop new models. It is claimed that these new models are designed according to European automobile quality, safety, and emission standards and are planned to be launched in China and the EU in 2013. Within two years after production begins, three new products will be introduced, followed by the release of another electric vehicle. Industry professionals believe that the international background of the Qoros brand makes its R&D capabilities worth looking forward to.

Secondly, Chery has formed a joint venture with the China-Africa Development Fund Co., Ltd. to establish Chery Overseas Industrial Investment Co., Ltd., aiming to comprehensively expand the African market. Currently, Chery has built 15 factories in 15 countries and regions, including Russia, Ukraine, Iran, Egypt, Malaysia, Indonesia, and Uruguay, partnering with local large dealership groups to open up the market. This year, Chery will focus on strengthening channel construction in the African market through its overseas industrial investment company.

Thirdly, Chery plans to build 500 new overseas sales networks this year, increasing the total number of overseas sales networks to more than 1,500. Currently, Chery's sales network covers more than 80 countries and regions, with nearly 1,000 dealers, covering the five major automobile markets of Asia, Europe, Africa, South America, and North America. This year, Chery will change its past practices, no longer sending personnel from within China but instead focusing on training local employees to adapt to local market development needs.

**Great Wall Motor: Brand First**

At the 2012 Dakar Rally, the silhouette of a Chinese-made vehicle always stood out prominently—it was the Great Wall Wingle pickup truck.

On January 9, after a day of rest following the seventh stage of the Dakar Rally in Chile, local dealers of Great Wall Motor organized a large gathering at the race site to cheer on the Great Wall team. Ten Wingle pickups were displayed at the event. It is reported that local dealers often independently organize such activities without receiving instructions from Great Wall Motor because Great Wall is one of the most popular automotive brands in the area.

Over the years, Great Wall Motor has consistently adhered to the overseas market development strategy of "self-reliance and brand-first," achieving good results with steady increases in brand awareness and market sales. This year, Great Wall Motor will fully leverage the advantages and initiative of local dealers to achieve new market expansion goals.

Placing top priority on enhancing the brand's reputation in overseas markets, besides participating in large events like the Dakar Rally, Great Wall Motor will continue to host various family days and customer experience days in overseas markets to bridge the gap with consumers and prepare for the future export of SUVs, A-class sedans, and other models.

Additionally, Great Wall Motor has organized the "One World, One Great Wall" domestic and overseas dealer exchange program to allow local dealers to better understand the Great Wall brand. This activity will further enhance overseas dealers' understanding of Great Wall culture and their recognition of Great Wall's production and R&D capabilities, as well as their understanding of Great Wall's brand building and business philosophy.

Expanding overseas production capacity comprehensively. By the end of the year, Great Wall Motor will work with local partners in the Philippines, Senegal, Bulgaria, Venezuela, and Malaysia to build five assembly lines, with an annual total production capacity exceeding 100,000 units.

After years of development, Great Wall Motor has established sales networks in over 100 countries and regions worldwide, including Russia, South Africa, Egypt, Mauritius, Malaysia, and the Philippines. Leveraging the outstanding performance of its pickup trucks, both sales and reputation have rapidly increased.
