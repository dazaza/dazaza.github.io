The Past is Already Blurred

Last night, I had a strange dream. I dreamed that he had a new girlfriend, and I cried my eyes out. The feeling after waking up was indescribable, and memories flooded back like a tidal wave.

It has been three years since our breakup, and during my time in university, I haven't found the right person. Although I don't admit it, I know it has something to do with him. This morning, when I logged onto the campus network, I saw that he had updated his status: "The day after tomorrow, I will start working." Instantly, I felt heartache and wanted to cry.

For the past three years, we have been in the same city. I knew about his relationships and breakups. Even though we haven't seen each other, I roughly understood his life. Suddenly, knowing that he was going to work and wouldn't return to this city left me unsure of how I felt. I don't understand why I am sad, as it has been a long time since then, and I no longer think about him frequently. I'm confused; I don't understand myself, and I don't know why I still can't let go of a relationship that he has already moved on from. Even though I have let go, I am still troubled by recurring nightmares. Three years have passed, and I still can't start anew. Why do I keep meeting the wrong people? Is it because of my bad luck, or is it true, as most people console me, that the best always comes last? I don't want to be pessimistic, but I often find myself trapped in such thoughts.

When I was feeling disoriented, I came across a review of "Gossip Girl" on Douban, along with the picture above. This was the first American TV series I watched when I started university. After following two seasons and finding it too chaotic, I stopped watching new episodes. After reading the review, I learned that B and C, my favorite characters, had broken up. So, I opened PPS and watched the first four updated episodes.

In the second episode, there was a beautiful scene. B, wearing a red evening gown, walked towards C. She couldn't forgive his betrayal but wanted to hold onto him. I thought she would forgive him again and get back together with him, but instead, she said, "I can't love you, but my life can't be without you." It wasn't that she didn't love him; it was that she couldn't. They realized that being together would only hurt each other and make them dislike themselves. So they chose to separate but not leave each other's lives.

Suddenly, I realized that I also had this kind of feeling. I don't love you, but I don't want you to leave my life or leave the 'us' in my memory. In my memory, we were so young and innocent. I don't want the next time I see you for you to have been worn down by life, losing your edge, becoming an average and mundane office worker, or even getting married and having children, becoming overweight...

"Leung Yiu-fai, let's start over."

At the beginning of the movie "Happy Together," in black and white lighting, inside a small hotel room in Argentina, Ho Po-wing lying slanted on the bed said this to Leung Yiu-fai. "Starting over," what a warm and alluring phrase! These simple words seem to have a magical power. Whether for lovers who have already broken up or are about to break up, as long as there is still affection for each other, hearing these words can't help but stir some inexplicable emotion... It seems that as long as one nods their head, all past grievances, loves, and hatreds can be erased, returning to the emotional starting point, and life could feel like the first meeting again, allowing another chance to hold hands and reconcile.

He said it to me, and I said it to him. But the outcome is that nothing can start over.

Because the past is already blurred, simply because it has become the past.

What has passed cannot be reclaimed, and fate cannot be changed. We can only go with the flow and wait.

Maybe I will still have dreams like this in the future, which might only indicate that I still dwell on the past, but it cannot change my life. I cannot be held hostage by memories anymore. Memories are just memories.

If one day, after a long time, we meet again, I would say,

"Hey, look at us, we're both old now."

He probably won't see this post, but I still want to say, may your work go smoothly.
