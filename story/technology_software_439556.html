Cadillac short films: Five directors analyze the development of Chinese films, only with individuality and contention can it last-

by letaoeoe on 2011-05-30 18:57:37

The film market environment is great, but films with individuality are rare.

Regarding the issues of film investment and director's individuality, Director Wang Xiaoshuai, who is often said to love criticizing, also has his own insights: "If I say a few lies, people will say I'm criticizing. The film market is good, and everyone is happy. So I have nothing to criticize. Let me clarify something before this: there is also a segment of backshot in my 'Sunshine in Chongqing' that lasts for a few minutes, and fortunately, it didn't win an award (laughs). I think it's not just about whether directors should have their own individuality, but also whether the whole society has reached a stage where it can accept and respect the individuality of directors. If it hasn't reached that stage, then directors still have to surrender when they encounter capital. Today, it's quite rare that none of the directors on stage are boasting about how much box office their films have made. This is actually a wake-up call for film industry professionals. We should maintain our own characteristics and appeal to investors to respect the individuality of artists. Only when different types of films can all get investments will it be the true springtime for Chinese-language films."

For young directors, Wu Yusen said: "Young directors need encouragement, but it's also easy to fall into the trap of imitation. For example, if a movie with a five or six minute backshot wins an award, many people will think that shooting a five or six minute backshot will win an award, and they will all follow this trend. In this way, movies will lose their individuality, and the film industry will quickly decline. Therefore, I hope that film festivals, such as the Shanghai International Film Festival, Golden Horse Awards, Hong Kong Film Awards, and Hundred Flowers Awards, as well as the judges of these festivals, do not classify films by type. Each type of film has its own value. Do not exclude commercial films; there should be broad inclusiveness. Encourage new talents, let them feel a sense of achievement, and make them feel that devoting themselves to the film industry is worth it. Don't let them get discouraged. Give them guidance: only films with individuality can win awards. Let them maintain their own individuality. Only when young directors maintain their own style will Chinese cinema have a more promising and sustainable future."

In the热烈 applause at the opening, guest host Director He Ping briefly introduced the topic of this Chairman Forum: "We discussed it, and we thought the original topic 'What kind of films does China need?' was not broad enough. Because besides blockbusters, other types of films have always been produced, but how to make good films is a big issue. Now, there are many investors willing to invest in films, but the problem is that the creators are not adequately prepared. The market environment is good, but there are fewer quality products. With the investment environment in place, the quality of the creators' work cannot keep up. How to better combine creativity and capital to promote the better development of Chinese cinema."

Regarding how to solve the problem of film uniformity, Wu Yusen believed it had a lot to do with current film investors: "The phenomenon of so many copycat films indicates that we need more young directors to get opportunities to showcase their talents, and investors should have long-term investment plans. Just like 'Avatar' was very successful and shocking, people thought it was impossible to surpass, but it was developed over many years and required a lot of money. We haven't done enough in this aspect. Young directors have their own ideas and creativity, and investors should support and assist directors' new technologies and creativity to create an innovative situation for Chinese cinema. Many young people lack practical opportunities, and no one cares."

For film investors, Director Wu Yusen said: "Film investors are not all short-sighted. Back in the day, I collaborated with嘉禾on 10 films, and only one of them made money, the other nine all lost money, but the boss still let me shoot without complaining. When I first signed the contract, the boss decided to sign me after seeing one of my films. I asked why, and he said it was because I used different techniques to express emotions in action films, which were actually ideas I came up with while shooting experimental films. Later, when I shot 'A Better Tomorrow,' I lost tens of millions of Hong Kong dollars, and the boss said: 'Money doesn't matter, this is your best film, Wu Yusen.' Later in Hollywood, I also met great bosses. In fact, directors and investors are in a cooperative relationship. Investors' support for the individuality of directors, respect for originality, and respect for the film industry will make Chinese cinema better and better."

On June 17, 2010, the brand unit Chairman Forum of the 13th Shanghai International Film Festival was held in Shanghai. Originally, the topic for directors He Ping, Wu Yusen, Wang Xiaoshuai, Pang Haoxiang, and Niu Chengze was 'What kind of films does China need?' But after discussion among the five directors, they believed the thinking should be broadened to explore the calls for creativity and diversity in Chinese cinema. The foresight and free expression of the directors at the forum benefited the audience greatly, and they gave enthusiastic applause for the directors' brilliant remarks.

Guest host Director He Ping also expressed his views: "I can see many examples around me. Some good directors maintain their own style unchanged, but the investors change; some directors change their style when the investor changes, and eventually lose their style, forcing them to exit the industry."

Director Wu Yusen agreed with Wang Xiaoshuai's speech, saying: "Young directors should make films with individuality and see if their works' individuality can align with the demands of the times. Directors' styles can actually guide the audience. For example, a film like 'Mang Ke' with sentiment can resonate with the audience."

Calling for investors to have a long-term vision and providing opportunities for young directors.
