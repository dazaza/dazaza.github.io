BEIJING, February 23rd - The 17th round of the National Men's Volleyball League has concluded, with the Bayi Men's Volleyball Team defeating the Beijing Men's Volleyball Team in a grueling five-set match at Beijing’s home ground. Despite having an additional win in hand, Bayi Team only managed to secure 2 points in this round, falling behind Shanghai Men's Volleyball Team who claimed a full 3 points this round in terms of overall points. Consequently, Bayi ceded the top position in the regular season standings. Coach Li Haiyun of the Bayi Men's Volleyball Team stated that his team experienced significant fluctuations during certain phases of today’s game. However, he believes that going through such a rare intense match will help his team better prepare for potential challenges in the elimination stage.

In their first-round clash in the second phase, Bayi Men's Volleyball Team had easily defeated Beijing Team with a 3-0 victory. This time around, returning to their home ground, Beijing Team presented a completely renewed appearance. After losing the first set 22-25, Beijing Team fought back fiercely, winning the next two sets 26-24 and 25-21 respectively. Following the third set, Coach Li Haiyun took his Bayi team off the court for a break, after which they regained their offensive rhythm, leveling the score at 25-17, and eventually securing the match in the decisive set with a 15-11 win. Trailing by one point behind Shanghai Men's Volleyball Team, Bayi Team is highly likely to maintain this disadvantage in the final round. If both Bayi and Shanghai make it to the finals, Bayi will have one fewer home advantage in the championship battle.

Coach Li Haiyun said: "Both teams delivered an exciting match for the audience. Bayi Team demonstrated its level intermittently but also experienced some ups and downs. This is beneficial for the upcoming elimination matches. We rarely encounter such intense games this season, and this experience will help us better prepare for the difficulties in the latter half of the competition." Taking the team off the court between sets became a crucial turning point for Bayi Team to reverse the unfavorable situation. Li Haiyun explained: "I told the players not to overthink the outcome, as excessive focus on results can distract from the game itself. They were too fixated on rankings, leading to hesitance and reluctance to fully engage in the match. They were overly eager to score quickly, lacking sufficient patience."

The core player of Bayi Men's Volleyball Team, main attacker Zhong Weijun wearing No. 7 jersey, was a key target of attention for the fans throughout the match. Whenever it was Bayi's turn to receive serve, the fans continuously shouted encouragements, from "Serve No. 7" to "Serve No. 7 and block No. 7". Zhong Weijun had more serving errors in the early stages, but after easing his mental burden, he performed well in serving and attacking in the later part of the game. Li Haiyun said: "It benefits Zhong Weijun's growth to endure such pressure. As the core player of Bayi Team and also a key player in the national team, his ability to withstand the atmosphere and pressure on-site needs to improve. Without being able to handle such pressure, it would be hard to shoulder important responsibilities."

Facing the fervent atmosphere of the Beijing venue, Li Haiyun remarked: "We had 2,000 fans at our home ground cheering for the visiting team, equivalent to playing two away games. Having more difficulties isn't necessarily a bad thing; how can we claim championships without such resilience? At the same time, I believe that more fans, regardless of whom they support, are beneficial for the volleyball market. This is why we invited Beijing fans to enter for free during our home games."
