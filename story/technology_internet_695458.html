"Peek" at the Difficulty of Various Types of On-the-job Graduate Student Exams Decrypted

by qindingdangx on 2012-03-07 15:45:26

According to the Zhishe Education Network [http://www.zsjykj.com/], beginners focus on the application conditions for postgraduate studies while working. Of course, this is the primary condition. After meeting the requirements, they then pay attention to the difficulty of the postgraduate entrance examination and information about obtaining certificates. Is the postgraduate examination difficult or not? Is there a standard to measure it? The editor of Zhishe Education will analyze it comprehensively for everyone:

Postgraduate studies while working are generally divided into six major categories. Among them, May's "Same Force Application for Master's Degree" and October's "In-service Postgraduate Entrance Examination" are more common. In addition, January's Legal Master and MBA exams are conducted together with the January unified exam, which has relatively high difficulty. Independent exams are independently set questions and lines by recruiting institutions, and are also conducted together with the January unified exam. The difficulty of the exam depends on the key points examined by each institution. For in-service postgraduate study classes, after graduation, there is no academic degree certificate or master's degree certificate. Admission without examination mainly aims at improving one’s comprehensive business level. Below is a detailed introduction to the difficulty of the May Same Force Application for Master's Degree and the October In-service Postgraduate Entrance Examination:

Analysis of the difficulty of the Same Force In-service Postgraduate Examination:

The threshold for Same Force is relatively low, allowing admission without examination. After entering and accumulating enough credits, one can participate in the master's degree application exam. Same Force adopts the "[study and exam simultaneously]" method, which facilitates passing and is the easiest way to obtain a master's degree.

Passing the course exams organized by the degree-granting unit, completing the required credits for each course and passing the exams. Generally speaking, as long as you review according to the teacher's schedule and listen carefully, passing should be no problem. The key is the foreign language proficiency and subject comprehensive proficiency exams in May. For candidates with weak English foundations, this is a fatal blow. The difficulty of English is between CET-4 and CET-6, and the difficulty of the comprehensive subject exam is not fixed, varying depending on the institution and major. Therefore, the comprehensive subject and English exams at the end of May are crucial. Based on your understanding of the knowledge structure, focus your review accordingly.

However, within four years from the date of passing the qualification review, applicants can pass all the course exams of the degree-granting unit and the national-level exams. Even if they fail the first time, they can start over again as long as they don't scare themselves.

Analysis of the Difficulty of the October Joint Exam for In-service Postgraduate Studies:

The professional master's categories for the October Joint Exam include Legal Master, Engineering Master, Sports Master, Military Master, Education Master, Accounting Master, Veterinary Master, Art Master, Agricultural Extension Master, Business Management Master (MBA), Public Management Master, Public Health Master, Landscape Architecture Master, and Masters for Teachers in Secondary Vocational Schools studying while working.

The difficulty of admission varies for different majors, with popular majors being relatively more difficult. Some majors in the October In-service Joint Exam require GCT subjects, which are a combination of Chinese, mathematics, English, and logic. There are 175 questions in 180 minutes, with each subject worth 100 points. Mathematics has 25 questions, and the overall difficulty coefficient is not high, but the volume of questions is large, often leaving candidates feeling short of time. Collecting past experiences, it is concluded that mathematics and logic consume more time and are relatively more difficult. Therefore, candidates who are weaker in logical thinking ability should focus on training. Avoid disappointment in the exam. Although the In-service Joint Exam is relatively easy, admissions place more emphasis on the applicant's work background and experience.

In summary, regardless of the type of in-service postgraduate chosen, the difficulty coefficient varies depending on the major category. You can combine past real exam questions to feel the difficulty yourself. After all, everyone's knowledge structure is different. Those who know find it not difficult, those who don't find it hard, so the perception of difficulty varies. Trust your own feelings, make up for deficiencies, and achieve good results.
