Oriental Fashion Driving School: Tips for Using the Clutch in a Manual Transmission Car

by ganyao3816 on 2009-11-30 17:50:37

For manual transmission vehicles, the importance of the clutch for smooth driving is paramount. But how should the clutch be used correctly?

Do not press the clutch unnecessarily. During normal driving, the clutch in a car is in a tightly engaged state, and the clutch should not slip. When driving, apart from starting the car, changing gears, and braking at low speeds, there's no need to press the clutch pedal unnecessarily or rest your foot on it. Keeping your foot on the clutch pedal for extended periods while driving can easily cause the clutch to slip, leading to the friction plates burning out. In severe cases, it may even result in damage such as burn marks on the pressure plate and flywheel face, or the compression springs losing their temper. Simultaneously, this habit can lead to increased fuel consumption, vehicle wear, and higher driving costs.

The correct operation during start-up. The key to operating the clutch pedal during start-up is "one fast, two slow, three联动 (coordinated)." That means lifting the pedal quickly at the beginning; when the clutch reaches semi-engagement (at which point the engine sound changes), lift the pedal more slowly; and finally, as it moves from semi-engagement to full engagement, gradually lift the pedal. As the clutch pedal is lifted, adjust the accelerator pedal according to the engine resistance, ensuring a smooth start.

The correct operation during gear shifting. When shifting gears while driving, the clutch pedal should be pressed down and released quickly without any semi-engagement, as this will accelerate clutch wear. Additionally, coordination with the throttle is essential.

To ensure smooth gear shifts and reduce wear on the transmission shift mechanism and clutch, it is recommended to use the "two-pedal clutch shifting method." Although this method is more complex, it is an excellent way to save on both vehicle maintenance and money.

The correct use of the clutch during braking. During regular driving, apart from situations where low-speed braking requires pressing the clutch pedal, other braking scenarios should avoid pressing the clutch pedal if possible.

The method for braking and stopping at low speeds involves first stepping on the brake pedal, followed by the clutch pedal, allowing the car to come to a smooth stop.
