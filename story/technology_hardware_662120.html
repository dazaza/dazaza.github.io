by a01ua87xp on 2012-02-09 18:08:38

Male infertility is a common and high-incidence disease among men, which is a disease with complex causes. To cure infertility, the key is to find out its cause, and this requires patients to go to the hospital for certain examinations. So, how should male infertility be examined? The authoritative experts of the Urology Diagnosis and Treatment Center of the Third Hospital of North General have made the following explanation on the examination of male infertility:

Examination methods for male infertility:

1. Semen routine examination: This is the most basic and important clinical indicator to measure male fertility. Parameters related to semen are the first thing that doctors need to understand. Male infertility patients should note that they should not have sex for 3-5 days before the examination, and it is best to collect samples by masturbation. Ensure that all semen is obtained, and recheck 1-2 times within 2 weeks.

2. Microbial examination: Infections in the male urinary and reproductive systems often lead to male infertility. Pathogenic microorganisms related to infections in the male urinary and reproductive systems include bacteria, viruses, spirochetes, mycoplasma, chlamydia, etc., commonly seen are gonococcus, papillomavirus, adenovirus, Chlamydia trachomatis, etc.

3. Genetic examination: Chromosome and gene examinations for individuals with abnormal gender differentiation or other genetic defects, azoospermia and severe oligospermia, a family history of genetic diseases, and habitual abortion couples have very important significance.

4. Endocrine examination: Reproductive endocrine dysfunction affects male sexual function and reproductive function, which is an important reason for male infertility. Endocrine examination mainly involves the measurement of sex hormones T, FSH, LH, PRL, E2 and various stimulation tests, such as HCG stimulation test, which are items for the examination of male infertility!

5. Prostate fluid examination: Prostatitis can lead to male infertility. Examination of prostate fluid can provide evidence for the diagnosis of prostatitis.
