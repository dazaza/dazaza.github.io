"The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology's Pilot Plan for Further Implementing the Verification of the Authenticity of Website Filing Information" - Nanjing SEO Bar

by maomaochaoren on 2010-02-23 09:07:58

The process of website registration is becoming increasingly troublesome. Not only does it require the submission of detailed information, but now it also requires the submission of a color, front-facing, uncapped photo of the person in charge. Moreover, according to the regulation description, the individual must personally go to the registration management center to take the photo.

On the morning of February 23rd, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) issued a notice to relevant units including the communication administration bureaus of various regions, further implementing the verification of the authenticity of website registration information. The trial plan for registration verification clearly stipulates that individuals are qualified as website hosts; at the same time, website registration will require the person in charge to submit a color, front-facing, uncapped photo.

Below is the full text of the notice issued by MIIT:

Notice on Issuing the "Pilot Plan for Further Implementation of Website Registration Information Authenticity Verification Work by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology"

Ministry of Industry and Information Technology Document No. 64 [2010]

Communication Administration Bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government, National Computer Network Emergency Technical Handling Coordination Center, China Internet Network Information Center, China Internet Association, China Telecom Corporation Limited, China Mobile Group Corporation, China United Network Communications Group Corporation Limited, related access service units:

In order to implement the requirements of the "Special Action Plan for Further Rectification of Mobile Phone Pornography and Obscenity" issued by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (Document No. 672 [2009] of MIIT), deepen the legal crackdown on mobile phone pornography and obscenity, purify the internet environment, strengthen the verification of website registration information, further improve the accuracy of website registration information, and give full play to the role of website registration management in tracing the origin of website hosts and access locations, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology has formulated the "Pilot Plan for Further Implementation of Website Registration Information Authenticity Verification Work" based on extensive solicitation of opinions from all parties. This plan is hereby issued to you. Please earnestly implement it in accordance with actual work conditions.

February 8, 2010

Pilot Plan for Further Implementation of Website Registration Information Authenticity Verification Work by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology

To effectively ensure the authenticity of website registration information and implement the requirements of the "Special Action Plan for Further Rectification of Mobile Phone Pornography and Obscenity" issued by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology: "Basic telecommunications enterprises and all access service providers must verify the identity information of website hosts face-to-face and retain copies of valid documents before submitting website applications to the communication administration bureau. They must also review the subject information, contact information, and access information of the website." Based on the relevant provisions of the "Measures for the Administration of Internet Information Services" (State Council Order No. 292) and the "Measures for the Administration of Non-commercial Internet Information Service Registration" (Order No. 33 of the Ministry of Information Industry), a pilot plan for website registration information authenticity verification work is formulated.

1. Verification Content

When an access service unit fulfills registration or registration change procedures on behalf of related websites, it should verify the authenticity of the subject information, contact information, website information submitted by the website host, as well as the access information submitted by the unit itself.

(1) Website Host (Subject) Information

Website hosts refer to internet information service providers, including both organizations and individuals.

If the website host is an organization, the verified documents include:

- If the website host is a government agency, public institution, or social organization, the original organizational code certificate is the primary document for verifying its organizational qualifications. In the absence of an organizational code certificate, the original public institution legal person certificate or social organization legal person certificate can be verified.

- If the website host is an enterprise, the original business license or organizational code certificate is verified.

- If the website host is the military, the original military code certificate is verified.

Personal documents of the website responsible person: If the website responsible person is a Chinese citizen, the original ID card is the primary document for verifying their identity. In the absence of an ID card, the original household registration book, officer's certificate, Taiwan compatriot certificate, etc., can be verified. If the website responsible person is not a Chinese citizen, their original passport is verified.

If the website host is an individual, the verified documents include:

- If the website host is a Chinese citizen, the original ID card is the primary document for verifying their identity. In the absence of an ID card, the original household registration book, officer's certificate, Taiwan compatriot certificate, etc., can be verified.

- If the website host is not a Chinese citizen, their original passport is verified.

Definition of the website responsible person: If the website host is an organization, the website responsible person refers to the legal representative or the department head appointed by the organization. If the website host is an individual, the website responsible person is the individual themselves.

(2) Contact Information

If the website host is an organization, the contact information includes the mobile phone number, office telephone, email address, and mailing address of the website responsible person. If the website host is an individual, the contact information includes the mobile phone number, office telephone or residential telephone, email address, and mailing address of the website responsible person.

(3) Website Information

Website name, website domain, content requiring preliminary approval or special approval, website service content/projects.

(4) Access Information

Name of the access service provider, access method, server placement location, website IP address.

2. Verification, Review, and Inspection Procedures

(1) Responsibility Subject for the Authenticity of Registration Information

When an access service unit fulfills registration or registration change procedures on behalf of related websites, the verification subject is the access service unit, including: provincial branches of basic telecommunications enterprises (including city and county-level branches), internet access service units (IDC, ISP), public welfare interconnection units, etc.

The review subject is the communication administration bureaus of each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government.

The inspection subject is the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (entrusting the National Internet Registration Management Support Center, hereinafter referred to as the "registration center").

(2) Verification, Review, and Inspection Procedures for the Authenticity of Registration Information

The flowchart for the verification and review of the authenticity of website registration information is attached.

1. Verification Procedures for Website Registration by Access Service Units

(1) The website host logs into the access service unit's registration system to input website registration information.

(2) After accepting the information submitted by the website host, the access service unit pre-verifies the subject information and contact information: preliminarily determines whether the subject information is true, verifies through telephone, email, etc., whether the contact information is correct. After pre-verification, the access service unit sends a text message notification to the website host for on-site verification or information return: "The website registration information you submitted has passed the pre-verification. Please have the person in charge of the website bring relevant original documents to our unit within one month to handle the verification procedures on-site" or "The website registration information you submitted did not pass the pre-verification. Please modify and resubmit." Pre-verification must be completed within five working days after the host submits the registration information.

(3) After receiving the notification, the website host sends the person in charge of the website to carry the required original documents and materials to the access service unit's registration site for on-site verification procedures.

(4) The access service unit collects and retains a color, front-facing, uncapped photo of the website responsible person at its own registration site (electronic photo specifications: 800x600 pixels). The registration center uniformly produces and provides a banner with identification as the background for taking photos, and the photo should display the shooting time and background identification.

(5) The registration personnel of the access service unit use information sources provided by departments such as public security, quality inspection, and industry and commerce, such as resident identity cards, organization code certificates, and business licenses, to verify the authenticity of the original documents provided by the website host. At the same time, they verify whether the identity card matches the party through the original identity card of the website responsible person. After verifying without errors, retain copies of identity proof and valid organizational documents.

The registration personnel of the access service unit log into their unit's registration system and verify the subject information submitted online by the website host based on the original documents: verify whether the holder of the document, the type of document, the content of the document, and the website registration information entered in the registration system are completely consistent, and if inconsistent, do not accept it; verify the consistency between the website domain owner and the website host's identity based on the domain certificate or domain registration information queried publicly on the domain registration institution's website, and if inconsistent, do not accept it; accurately enter all contents of the website registration access information based on the actual access situation of the unit.

(6) If the verification is correct, the registration personnel of the access service unit fill out a unified format "Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information" (the registration center is responsible for formulating the format of the "Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information"); if errors in the registration information are found, verify and modify on-site, and record in the "Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information." The "Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information" is in duplicate: one copy retained by the access service unit, and one copy submitted to the communication administration bureau where the website subject is located, stamped with the seal of the access service unit. The website host and the access service unit simultaneously sign the information security management agreement.

(7) After confirming that all registration information has been verified correctly, the access service unit submits the website registration information to the registration system of the communication administration bureau where the subject is located through its own registration system, and submits the original paper, fax, or electronic scanned copy of the "Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information."

2. Review Procedures for Provincial Communication Administration Bureaus

The communication administration bureaus of each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government log into the provincial bureau registration system and review the registration information submitted by the access service unit within twenty working days. If the review is qualified, issue the website registration number, and the access service unit implements website access; if unqualified, return the registration information to the system of the access service unit for re-verification.

3. Inspection Procedures for the Registration Center

(1) The registration center, commissioned by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, constructs a website registration information verification platform to conduct full automatic verification of the identity information in the registration information submitted by each communication administration bureau. Combining manual telephone spot checks, monthly accuracy verification and sampling evaluation of the website registration information submitted by the registration system and feedback on the situation.

(2) The registration center conducts quarterly verification and sampling evaluation of the accuracy rate and registration rate of existing website registration information in the ministry's registration system by province and access provider and feeds back the situation.

3. Work Requirements

(1) Work Requirements for Access Service Units' Verification

1. Organizational Guarantee and System Improvement Requirements

Each access service unit should establish a department for on-site verification of website registration information by the end of February 2010, specifically responsible for the authenticity verification work of website registration information, and implement the unit leadership responsibility system. Should establish the unit's registration business standards and registration work assessment system. Clearly define the work responsibilities of registration personnel, implement annual assessments of the business capabilities of registration personnel, and use the quality of daily registration work as an important indicator for assessment.

Each access service unit should officially implement face-to-face verification of website registration information by the end of March 2010 and report the work situation to the licensing authority and the communication administration bureau where the unit is registered.

Basic telecommunications enterprises should inspect by April 2010 whether access service units leasing the unit's telecommunications resources to engage in access services have established face-to-face verification personnel and are fulfilling face-to-face verification of registration information, and shall not provide telecommunications resources to those who fail to fulfill face-to-face verification responsibilities.

2. Business Disclosure Requirements

Access service units should disclose to website hosts during business operations that setting up a website requires legally processing registration procedures, the website registration process, a list of required documents and materials, and relevant precautions for website registration.

3. Business Contract Content Requirements

Access service units should clearly state in the business contract signed with the website host: "According to Article 23 of the 'Measures for the Administration of Non-commercial Internet Registration', if the registration information is not true, the website will be closed and the registration canceled. Please commit and confirm: all registration information you submit is true and effective, and when your registration information changes, please promptly submit updated information to the registration system. If you fail to update in time resulting in inaccurate registration information, our company has the right to legally close the accessed website." Both parties sign and stamp the unit to confirm.

4. Information Security Management Responsibility Requirements

Access service units should establish archives of website host materials, properly preserve the documents and photos obtained during the verification process, the 'Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information,' and various agreements signed with the website host, ensuring that the information is not leaked and assuming responsibility for the confidentiality of the information security of the materials submitted by the website host. These materials should be retained for at least five years to prepare for inspections by the competent authorities of the communications industry.

5. Website Registration Information Update Requirements

Access service units should do a good job in routine revisit and verification work to ensure that after changes in website registration information, the website host promptly updates the main information, contact information, and website information. At the same time, access service units should update access information in a timely manner based on changes in website access and cooperate with the competent authorities to do a good job in verifying the authenticity of website registration information. For access service units that fail to conduct routine revisit and verification work leading to inaccurate website registration information, the competent authorities will impose penalties according to law.

6. Verification Requirements for Existing Website Registration Information

For websites accessed before the official launch of face-to-face verification of the authenticity of registration information, each access service unit should formulate detailed plans for the verification of the authenticity of registration information by the end of February 2010, report to the licensing authority and the communication administration bureau where the unit is registered, and complete the authenticity verification of registration information for all websites by the end of September 2010.

(2) Review Work Requirements for Provincial Communication Administration Bureaus

1. Do a Good Job in Routine Review Work

Each communication administration bureau should do a good job in reviewing the authenticity of daily website registration information, ensuring detailed reviews of key information (business licenses, identity cards, etc.). After the upgrade and transformation of the second phase registration system, review personnel of each communication administration bureau can log into the access service unit system using passwords to randomly check and review original documents, while also checking the 'Verification Form for the Authenticity of Website Registration Information' submitted by the access service unit.

2. Check the Conduct of 'Face-to-Face Verification' Work

Each communication administration bureau should guide and inspect the establishment of departments and improvement of work systems by access service units for face-to-face verification of website registration information. Based on the authenticity verification proof materials preserved by the access service unit, regularly inspect or irregularly spot-check the conduct of 'face-to-face verification' work by the access service unit and the accuracy of the registration information submitted by the access service unit. For those found not executing 'face-to-face verification' or providing false information, they should be seriously dealt with. At the same time, the results of daily inspections and evaluations should be used as important references for the annual inspection of licenses.

(3) Inspection Work Requirements for the Registration Center

1. New Website Registration Information Inspection

The registration center conducts monthly accuracy spot checks on newly submitted website registration information. Focuses on spot-checking and verifying key information such as identity cards and organization code certificates in the registration subject information.

2. Inspection of Existing Website Registration Information

The registration center conducts quarterly accuracy inspections of the existing website registration information in the system, and inspects and spot-checks the conduct, completion progress, and work quality of the authenticity verification work of website registration information by each access service unit.

3. Develop Inspection Standards and Supervision Evaluation Methods

Based on the commission of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, the registration center appropriately develops, refines, and improves website registration information inspection standards and supervision evaluation methods, and releases them to each communication administration bureau and access service unit.

4. Strengthen Guidance, Statistics, and Supervision of Work Implementation

Based on the commission of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, the registration center guides, assists, and supervises the implementation of website registration information authenticity verification work by each unit. Strengthens classified statistics on the implementation of website registration information authenticity verification work by each unit, and conducts summaries of administrative penalty situations in various local communication administration bureaus.

5. Conduct Website Registration Business Training

Based on the commission of the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, the registration center compiles and develops website registration training materials, organizes nationwide website registration training for each access service unit, and provides unified explanations of relevant policies, laws, and business knowledge that need to be mastered.

4. Accountability

Based on the principle of territorial management, the communication administration bureaus of each province, autonomous region, and municipality directly under the Central Government impose legal penalties on access service units that fail to conduct work according to the requirements of the "Special Action Plan for Further Rectification of Mobile Phone Pornography and Obscenity" issued by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology regarding 'face-to-face verification of website host identity information and retention of copies of valid documents,' fail to seriously conduct website registration information authenticity verification work, and submit untrue website registration information, in accordance with Articles 10 and 24 of the 'Measures for the Administration of Non-commercial Internet Information Service Registration,' ordering rectification within a time limit.
