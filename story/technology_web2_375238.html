The previous article discussed the mainstream mobile advertising platforms at home and abroad, and this article will delve into some technical aspects. Once developers understand these, they can choose an advertising platform that suits them.

To understand their revenue situation, developers only need to grasp the income formula:

Advertising Revenue (priced in CPC) = Number of Ad Requests (request) * Fill Rate (fill rate) * Click-Through Rate (CTR) * CPC (cost per click)

Number of Ad Requests: This is set by the programmer, and it is generally reasonable to set the request frequency to once every 30 seconds. Whether or not to request ads on each page should be reasonably arranged by the developer based on statistical conditions. A suggestion is to reduce the impact on user experience, for example, having ads pop up while playing games can be annoying, but placing them on a game navigation page would be better.

Fill Rate: The fill rate is determined by the ad platform, which depends on what kind of delivery method the ad platform chooses. For instance, if a certain ad platform chooses to deliver ads to entertainment applications, your app, if it happens to be a news application, may not get ad fills. Generally, the fill rate of ad platforms might be between 30-50%. Sometimes, due to factors such as app quality, there can be significant differences in fill rates. You can check out a post on JavaEye. In the previous article, we mentioned a type of ad aggregation platform: AdWhirl, Guohe. They increase the fill rate (usually between 80-95%) by aggregating multiple ad platforms, meaning that when one ad platform does not have inventory, it automatically requests inventory from another ad platform.

Click-Through Rate: The click-through rate is related to many factors. Some developers have found that the placement of ads, the quality of the application, the display time, regional differences (foreign users are more likely to donate to developers by clicking on ads), ad relevance (the correlation between ads and user needs), whether the ads are location-related, etc., all influence the click-through rate.

CPC (Cost Per Click): This is determined by the advertiser and the ad platform. Typically, advertisers are willing to pay a premium for high-end ads (such as brand ads). In domestic ad platforms, the CPC price is around 0.1-0.3 yuan. When mobile ads are more closely integrated with local merchants, these merchants will become an important long tail in mobile advertising, willing to pay 1-5% of the cost to facilitate a transaction. For example, if a customer gets a coupon through your ad and spends 200 yuan at a restaurant, you will earn 2-10 yuan in CPC.

There is another less commonly used pricing method: CPM pricing (cost per thousand impressions).

Advertising Revenue (priced in CPM) = Number of Ad Requests (request) * Fill Rate (fill rate) * CPM (cost per thousand impressions)

It's clear that it mainly depends on two factors: fill rate and CPM.

Some suggestions to increase revenue:

Increase the installation volume of the app itself (improve quality, promotions, etc.);

You can choose platforms like Guohe to increase the fill rate;

If the click-through rate is within a reasonable range, seek ad platforms with higher click prices;

There are many discussions about ad platforms like AdMob, iAd, etc., on Cocoa and EOE developer communities. You can take a look.

* You may notice that AdMob does not show the CPC price. Actually, it also charges by CPC, but presents the results in another way - eCPM (effective cost per mille, suggested to be remembered as "estimated cost per mille").
