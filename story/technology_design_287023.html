Tips for Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

by tuoqing05 on 2009-12-01 01:56:02

1) Preparation and Content Creation. Before you register a domain, you should document your ideas and prepare enough content for at least 100 webpages. Please note the word "at least." These 100 pages should contain genuine content, not link pages, "about us" pages, "contact us" pages, or copyright notices, etc.

2) Domain Name. You should choose a domain that is easy to brand, like Google.com, not Keywords.com. Domains stuffed with keywords should be discarded; domains that are easy to brand and recognize are the best. The role of keywords within a domain name is now very minimal. Why did goto.com change its name to Overture.com? That renaming was one of the boldest strategies I've ever seen. This rebranding strategy destroyed years of brand building.

3) Website Design - Simpler is Better. A good measure is that the proportion of text content should outweigh HTML formatting. The entire webpage should be standardized and display correctly on all browsers. For example, it should comply with the HTML3.2 standard. Search engine spiders don't favor the HTML4.0 standard much yet.

You should avoid heavy elements such as Flash, DOM, JavaScript, etc. If you must use these scripts, use them as external files. In my view, there's no need to use these things. They hardly enhance a website but can harm its performance in various ways (search engine friendliness being just one issue).

Logically organize your website. You can use keywords in directory names. Alternatively, you could place all webpages in the root directory (this method is rare but has proven to be very effective).

Don't clutter your site with useless elements, such as recommending specific browsers or adding counters, etc. Keep it as simple as possible, making it look professional. Learn from Google itself; take a look at their homepage—it's as simple as it gets. That's exactly what people want.

Speed isn't one of the most important factors—it's the only important factor. Your webpages should load extremely quickly. If your webpage takes 3-4 seconds to load, you're done for. Of course, if your host and visitors are in different countries, 3-4 seconds might be understandable, but for local visitors, 3-4 seconds is the limit. Beyond this time, every extra second will cost you 10% of your traffic. And that 10% could be the difference between success and failure.

4) Webpage Size

The smaller, the better. Ideally, keep it under 15K. Even better, keep it under 12K. Best of all, keep it under 10K. Do you get the idea? Between 5K and 10K is ideal. It's hard to achieve, but it's the most effective. It works well for both search engines and visitors.

5) Content

Create one page per day with 200-500 words. If you don't know what to write, do some keyword research and write articles based on popular keywords.

6) Keyword Density, Position, Etc.

Simple, old-school SEO: Use your keyword once in these places: the webpage title, meta description, main heading, URL, bold text, italicized text, and at the very beginning of the webpage. Aim for a keyword density between 5% and 20% (but don't worry too much about it). Write good articles and check for typos. Spell-checking (for Chinese, checking for wrong characters) is becoming increasingly important because search engines are starting to use automatic correction features, so there's no excuse for misspelling anymore.

7) Outbound Links

Link to one or two high-ranking websites on each webpage, and include keywords in the link text (this will be important in the future).

8) Internal Cross-Linking

This refers to linking within the same website. Link high-quality content pages together within your site. If a page is about food, ensure it links to pages about apples and vegetables. Especially for Google, mutual linking based on common content is crucial for distributing PR within your site.

What you want is not one superstar page that overshadows others, but 50 pages that attract one visitor each day instead of one page that attracts 50 visitors daily. If you find a page on your site that attracts most of the traffic, you should distribute that page’s PR value through cross-linking to other pages. This is somewhat like the old saying about sharing wealth.

9) Launching the Website

It's best not to use shared hosting. It's better to have your own dedicated IP address. Ensure your website can be indexed by search engine spiders. All webpages should link to other pages within your website. No webpage should be more than two clicks away from the homepage. All content pages should also link back to the homepage. A menu system present on all pages should link to the main sections of your website.

Do not launch your website until you’ve completed a high-quality site. Launching a bad website is worse than not launching at all. You want an excellent website from the start.

Submit your site to open directories, and if you have the budget, also submit it to Yahoo! and other paid directories. If you don’t have the budget, try Yahoo!'s free submission, but don't expect too much.

10) Website Submission

Submit your homepage to search engines like Google, then forget about it for the next six months. Yes, submit, and then forget about it.

11) Logging and Tracking

Use a high-quality log analysis software that records visitor sources. If your hosting provider doesn’t offer visitor source tracking, you should switch to another hosting provider.
