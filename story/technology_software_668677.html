The CPI price should be in a falling channel.

by 8j6q24l1p on 2012-02-18 12:12:24

Netease Finance, February 9th - The economic data for January was released today. Among it, the overall level of national residents' consumption prices increased by 4.5% year-on-year and 1.5% month-on-month, hitting a three-month high; the price of industrial producers nationwide increased by 0.7% year-on-year but dropped by 0.1% month-on-month, reaching the lowest point in 26 months. In response to the economic data, Netease Finance connected with multiple market participants for interpretation. Experts believe that the over-expected increase in January's CPI is mainly due to the Spring Festival factor, but overall, the upward trend of CPI will not continue. Since the market expected the central bank to cut the reserve requirement ratio recently, experts believe that the rebound in January's CPI will delay the timing of the RRR cut, which is unfavorable for the stock market.

The Spring Festival factor led to an over-expected increase in CPI. Renmin University of China's Vice Dean of Economics, Liu Yuanchun, told Netease Finance that the rebound in January's CPI was consistent with market expectations, but the extent of the rebound exceeded expectations. He pointed out that the rise in January's CPI was related to the double festival factor and seasonal factors, "because cold weather affects vegetable prices, and the return of migrant workers also caused a significant rise in service prices." Excluding seasonal and festival factors, the CPI should be on a downward trend.

Financial expert Zhao Qingming also believed that the over-expected increase in January's CPI was mainly due to the Spring Festival consumption season driving up prices, predicting that the inflationary pressure this year would be relatively small.

Professor Wang Jianmao from CEIBS said in an interview with Netease Finance that the continuous decline in PPI would indicate a cooling trend in the economy, but the inflation trend would be somewhat relieved.

The postponement of the RRR cut is unfavorable for the stock market. Liu Yuanchun indicated that because the CPI still appears to be at a high level, the relaxation of monetary policy and the adjustment of the reserve requirement ratio might be delayed, "in fact, there are already signs of relaxation in the total credit volume and the central bank's recent series of monetary policies, but a clear easing mode has not yet appeared."

Wang Jianmao also stated that although the call for a cut in the reserve requirement ratio is loud, it makes little sense in the near term.

Southwest Securities Research Institute Director Wang Jianhui said that although the CPI rebound was beyond expectations, seasonal factors were evident and did not represent a resurgence of inflation. From the continued decline in PPI, it could be seen that the overall downward trend in price increases persists. However, this data delays the time for the central bank to implement the next RRR cut measure, which is unfavorable for the sustained improvement of the stock market.

Yingda Securities Research Institute Director Li Daxiao believed that the rebound in January's CPI data would not have a significant impact on the stock market, "it can only be said that the data is not very ideal, but it does not constitute a bearish signal for the stock market." Li Daxiao emphasized, "the data cannot kill the bull." Regarding the future market, Li Daxiao maintained his judgment that "2132 points is the diamond bottom."

The sustainability of the rebound needs to be tested, and Shenwan Hongyuan warns of three factors. Yesterday, both markets soared, and the Shanghai Index broke through the 60-day line. How will the A-share market perform in the future?

Analysts believe that technically, recently, bullish and bearish factors have gathered in the A-share market, and the index movement around 2300 points has become more "ghostly," making it harder for ordinary investors to operate. We still maintain our previous judgment that the timing for large-scale entry has not yet arrived, and the authenticity of the market bottom at 2132 points still needs to be verified. In the short term, today's new high with increased volume suggests the possibility of a continued short-term uptrend, but above 2350 points lies the area of previous concentrated transactions. The amplification of trading volume will determine the short-term upward space of the index. If the trading volume cannot keep up, even if the downtrend channel since 3067 points is broken, investors should pay attention to locking in profits in the short term to avoid falling into the trap of a false rally set by major players.

Shen Yin Wan Guo noted that many people have been paying attention to the 60-day moving average recently, and this line had fully demonstrated its reference role in 2008. On February 8, 2012, the Shenzhen Component Index closed above this line, marking the first recovery since its collapse in early August 2011. But currently, it cannot be determined whether the market has reversed, as the多方 (bulls) ultimately failed in July and November 2011 when they approached and recovered the 60-day moving average. Currently, the 60-day moving average of the Shanghai Composite Index is around 2310 points, and the Shenzhen Component Index's 60-day moving average is about 9465 points, both of which are dynamic and gently declining each trading day. There may still be a struggle between bulls and bears near this line.

Shen Yin Wan Guo mentioned that when challenging 2350 points, there are three things to note. First, the pressure from the upper rail of the downtrend channel since 3067 points, where no clear signal of fundamentally reversing the downtrend is visible yet. Second, the short-term overbought condition of the daily stochastic indicator. Over the past ten months, when this indicator enters the overbought state, generally speaking, the upward momentum weakens, and without good fundamental cooperation, the power for the index to continue rising is limited. Third, the leading roles in Wednesday's index increase were non-ferrous metals and mining, which had briefly performed well in the previous two weeks. For the non-ferrous metal industry index, it fell from 6205 points in November 2010 to 2821 points on January 6, 2012, a significant drop, so a restorative rebound can be understood, but the difficulty of sustaining a sharp rise in the short term is considerable.

Shen Yin Wan Guo believes that although the index may still have some opportunities for a short-term inertial rebound, chasing gains above 2350 points is not advisable, and one can appropriately adjust their position to wait for better participation opportunities. However, observing from the medium term, the monthly stochastic indicator gradually falls below 20, indicating that medium-term risks may be gradually releasing, and the hope for the market to gradually emerge from difficulties is increasing.
