Ah Fanti 2.0 is now online, dedicated to creating a Chinese version of Quora.

by ybcz0519 on 2011-01-06 17:11:07

This article is simultaneously published on Mobinode. Business media, please do not reprint! Previously, the website "A Fan Ti" was introduced; at that time, A Fan Ti was a real-time Q&A platform based on social networks. Users could conduct real-time Q&A with their user network on A Fan Ti through various channels such as MSN, GTalk, email, and text messages (Via). The previous version of A Fan Ti could be said to have developed according to the model of vark.com. Recently, A Fan Ti sent an email informing that they had made a shift in direction and recently launched version 2.0. Version 2.0 of A Fan Ti has undergone significant changes, mainly in that its model has become more similar to Quora's.

Quora was founded in 2008 by former Facebook employees Charlie Cheever and Adam D'Angelo and is a question-and-answer SNS product (Via). The advantages of the Quora model lie in:

1. Establishing a user-following system based on Q&A, where users can follow other users who interest them.

2. Creating a topic-based following mechanism, allowing users to follow topics of interest.

3. A screening mechanism for answers to questions, where users can write answers and use systems like Digg or summaries to determine the best answers.

4. A community-based form, establishing user groups based on content of interest.

A Fan Ti 2.0 provides some new features, including:

Support for direct login via multiple accounts, including: Google accounts, Sina Weibo, NetEase Passport, MSN, Sina Weibo, Baidu Zhidao, Douban, and mobile binding.

The entirely new A Fan Ti 2.0 integrates instant notifications (IM, mobile phones);

It allows sharing questions on Sina Weibo, Tencent Weibo, Kaixin001, etc.;

Includes wiki (topic data can be modified), topic trees, and other new functions;

Provides question-targeting functionality, which is akin to the "Add Follow-Up Question" function on Quora.

Watsonxu's comment: A Fan Ti 2.0 should be the first officially launched community-style Q&A service website similar to Quora domestically. We also hope to have a service like Quora within China. Of course, A Fan Ti 2.0 still has many details that need improvement in terms of user experience, and the question recommendation feature also requires refinement. After years of development in Q&A websites, the emergence of Quora indeed gives a new vitality to such sites. We also hope that while domestic services draw inspiration from it, they will also consider localization and create a truly suitable Chinese 2.0 Q&A platform.
