G5 Review: Aiming for the Prize in Jingzhou - Unofficial Phones Hit the Market in July

by anonymous on 2012-02-29 14:43:06

During the Three Kingdoms period, Jingzhou was known as the "belly of the land". It had a four-way transportation network and extremely fertile land. Its strategic position was extremely important, hence the saying "one who seeks to rule the world must first capture Jingzhou." Let us rewind to the present day, in July, the midpoint of the year, which is the most crucial time for the digital product market. It's also the best time for gray-market smartphone manufacturers to offer discounts and launch new products. Best-selling products from the first half of the year would boost their sales in July during the "summer promotion," while major new models from various brands would be released one after another to seize the second-half market. Therefore, July is undoubtedly the "Jingzhou" in the eyes of gray-market smartphone manufacturers. Although capturing July doesn't necessarily mean capturing the whole market in the gray-market smartphone sector, its importance can significantly influence the final results and future development direction of these manufacturers this year. During the month of July this year, multiple brands announced dozens of new models, including many highly anticipated ones. These models are expected to capture a significant share of the market in the competitive landscape of the second half of the year. Below, let's take a look at some of the major new releases that came out in July to vie for the "Jingzhou" territory.

**Nokia C6**

This month, Nokia launched several new models, with the Nokia C6 being the hottest among them. It not only continued the successful trend of the 5230 but also added many fashionable and personalized elements. The side-sliding keyboard design won over many consumers, generating a lot of attention upon release.

The Nokia C6 features a side-sliding full QWERTY keyboard design, making it stylish and unique. It has a 3.2-inch 16 million-color TFT resistive touchscreen with a resolution of 360x640, providing sharp visuals and good touch responsiveness. It runs on the Symbian 9.4 S60 5.0 intelligent operating system, offering excellent expandability due to the vast array of software resources available. The device is powered by an ARM11 processor with a clock speed of 434 MHz, with internal memory of 512MB ROM + 128MB RAM. While the overall operation speed could still be improved, it includes a 5-megapixel camera with decent image quality. Bluetooth, WIFI, and GPS functions are all well-rounded, ensuring comprehensive performance.

Editor's comment: The Nokia C6 can be considered an "upgraded slide version" of the Nokia 5230. After this upgrade, the Nokia C6 directly competes head-to-head with the Nokia N97mini. Both in terms of price and overall performance, they are very similar. In the second half of the year, the Nokia C6 is expected to secure a significant market share, leaving fans scratching their heads over which model to choose. Since its release, the price of the Nokia C6 has dropped significantly, making its current cost-performance ratio quite attractive, worthy of further observation.

**Apple iPhone 3GS 8GB**

At the end of June, Apple launched the iPhone 4, with its entirely new system and upgraded features drawing global attention. At the same time, Apple also introduced the new version of the iPhone 3GS 8GB. What differences are there between this model and other versions of the iPhone 3GS besides capacity? Let’s find out together.

The iPhone 3GS 8GB features a 3.5-inch 16 million-color TFT screen with a resolution of 320x480, supporting multi-touch gestures for an immersive gaming experience. The camera is equipped with a 3-megapixel CMOS sensor, supporting autofocus, digital zoom, and photo effects, delivering commendable picture quality. The biggest difference lies in its operating system. This model runs the latest iOS 4 smart operating system, the same as the iPhone 4. With abundant software resources and newly added features, combined with the ARM Cortex A8 processor running at 660 MHz, the experience becomes smooth and effortless.

Editor's comment: At first glance, the iPhone 3GS 8GB looks no different from other versions of the iPhone 3GS, except for the storage capacity. However, its inclusion of the new iOS 4 system is indeed exciting. Currently, the newly released iPhone 4 is priced higher and comes with some negative news. On the other hand, the iPhone 3GS 8GB, with the same system as the iPhone 4, has gained recognition for its more stable performance. With the launch of the Hong Kong version of the iPhone 4, the price of the iPhone 3GS 8GB is expected to drop further.

**Motorola ME511**

Motorola's design has always stood out in the industry. Personally, I believe its aesthetic style is much more comfortable compared to other brands. Motorola can design body appearances that fit the styles of different target consumer groups. The cheerful Ming series and the fashionable RAZR series both showcase Motorola's sophisticated design and craftsmanship. Now, the brand-new Motorola ME511 makes a spinning appearance.

The Motorola ME511 adopts a design featuring a 45-key full keyboard and a rotatable touch screen. It is equipped with a 2.8-inch 260,000-color TFT capacitive touch screen that supports multi-touch gestures, offering a very comfortable touch experience, although the color reproduction remains mid-range. Inside, it has a 3-megapixel camera with average imaging quality, sufficient for daily casual shooting. The system runs Android OS v2.1 with a user interface of MOTO BLUR, offering commendable software expandability and entertainment usability. It is powered by a Qualcomm MSM7200 processor running at 528 MHz, delivering satisfactory overall operation speed. Additionally, it includes Bluetooth, WIFI, and GPS functions.

Editor's comment: The arrival of the Motorola ME511 brings excitement to young consumer groups who are individualistic and energetic, further validating Motorola's design style that aligns well with its target audience. With its unique rotating touch screen and a variety of color shell options, plus the integration of a smart operating system and other complete functions, it truly deserves love.

**Conclusion**

In ancient times, Cao Cao said, "If I were to have a son, I wish he would be like Sun Zhongmou." Nowadays, we say, "If I were to make a phone, I wish it would be like Apple." The release of the Apple iPhone 4 was like a powerful bombshell, not only stunning numerous consumers but also greatly shaking up various gray-market smartphone manufacturers. Subsequently, these manufacturers launched their own major new models this year to counter Apple's sweeping global trend. Models like the Nokia C6, Motorola ME511, Sharp SH-07B, etc., have garnered great market attention upon release and are expected to soon occupy a significant market share. Today, the gray-market smartphone brands and intelligent systems are divided into three parts, just like the battle for Jingzhou will continue. This way, consumers can purchase gray-market smartphones that are both affordable and practical.
