Inter Milan's new signing has been deemed a flop by Mourinho - the new Ibra can't even make it onto the bench for the Italian Cup.

Sina Sports - Inter Milan's transfer activities this summer have been widely regarded as successful. The performances of Sneijder, Eto'o, Lucio, and Motta have been mixed but generally positive. However, not all of Inter Milan's summer signings have been perfect. For example, Arnautovic has already been labeled as a flop by Mourinho.

In last night's Italian Cup match, Eto'o and Milito were rested, and Balotelli and Suazo became the main players in attack. Arnautovic didn't even make the squad for the Italian Cup. In the eyes of the Italian media, the only future for this Austrian forward is to leave the club.

Arnautovic was signed on loan by Inter Milan this summer, but it was Blanc's decision rather than Mourinho's. As a result, Mourinho hasn't given him a single second of game time, nor has he registered him for the Champions League. Arnautovic's agent initially claimed that "Arnautovic had just recovered from injury and needed time to adapt." After Arnautovic scored in the Milan youth derby, some Italian media began to say that "the Austrian would be Mourinho's winter signing," but Mourinho crushed these rumors with his actions. It's normal not to be registered for the Champions League, but if one can't even make the squad for the Italian Cup, Arnautovic should really consider whether he has a future under Mourinho. One of his agents, Groner, said: "If he can't even make the squad for the Italian Cup, it's clear that the coach doesn't trust him."

According to previous media reports, Inter Milan signed Arnautovic on a six-month loan deal. According to the contract, if he plays fewer than six games in half a season, he will be returned automatically. However, yesterday, Arnautovic's former club Twente's chairman Musterman debunked this rumor. Currently, Twente is facing a striker shortage. Besides Arnautovic, another Twente forward, Denneboom, is also on loan at Rotterdam Sparta. Musterman said: "Both Arnautovic and Denneboom are on one-year loans, so there is no possibility of returning in the winter. We'll have to figure it out ourselves."

Arnautovic was once hailed as the "new Ibra" and had an outstanding performance in the Eredivisie last season. When Inter Milan signed Arnautovic on loan this summer, they also secured the right of first refusal. However, the stipulated purchase amount for this right is 9 million euros. Now, it seems that this Austrian forward is worth far less than that number in Mourinho's mind.

In fact, as Arnautovic's agent, Groner still holds out a glimmer of hope. He stated: "By January, Arnautovic will get his chance." What he means is when Eto'o goes to the Africa Cup, but Mourinho has already made it clear: "If possible, I hope to bring in another striker in the winter." This means that Arnautovic isn't even considered as a "reserve." Perhaps Mourinho, who watches him every day, knows better about his training condition. Like Robiati, Binotto, and Giotos before him, Arnautovic may end up being just a fleeting guest at Inter Milan.
