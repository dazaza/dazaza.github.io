Will the aging industry really usher in a new dawn?

by xixigu20108 on 2012-03-06 16:08:42

Nowadays, as the parents of the first generation of single children enter the ranks of the elderly, major cities have suddenly encountered great difficulties related to the elderly industry, such as the supporting facilities for old-age homes. It is difficult to enter public-run old-age homes, privately-run old-age homes are expensive, and whether medical insurance coverage has been included. Even so, the vast majority of nursing homes are still in a loss-making state. Why does an industry that should be booming end up in such a predicament? The reason is very simple, that is, the relevant supporting facilities lag behind, such as medical insurance and social welfare. The cost of opening is too high, especially in big cities, the funds invested in purchasing or leasing land may not be recovered. The shortage of talent is also a very serious problem. Personnel with relevant medical qualifications and accompanying skills do not want to work in nursing homes. Not only is it tiring here, but the salary is also low. Moreover, related disputes are more likely to arise. Some irresponsible children even abandon the elderly in nursing institutions without asking or caring, which adds a lot of trouble to the operation and management of nursing homes. The situation of Shanghai's old-age homes is the most serious nationwide. One is that the relevant operating costs are the highest nationwide, and the degree of aging is the highest nationwide. There will soon be 4 million registered elderly people, excluding migrants.
