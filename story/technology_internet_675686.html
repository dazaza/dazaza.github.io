At 3 o'clock in the morning on the 17th, Yuan Yuan donated a pair of corneas, a pair of kidneys and a liver. The doctor said that these organs would save at least five patients in need. Other organs had to be abandoned because there were no suitable recipients. It is understood that this 20-year-old female college student became the first volunteer to donate both organs and remains since Shenzhen launched the organ donation program in 1999. After donating her daughter's body and organs free of charge, Honglin Huang received a stack of medical expense invoices worth more than 80,000 yuan from the visiting hospital, the Second People's Hospital of Shenzhen. According to Honglin Huang, he is a villager from Daming Village, Maojiagang Town, Gong'an County, Jingzhou City, Hubei Province. The village secretary Li Wenxiang called on the villagers to donate 10,000 yuan, and then guaranteed a loan of 70,000 yuan from the credit union with the villagers. After settling the accounts, Honglin Huang went to his nephew's place in Huizhou to rest and prepare to return home in the next few days. Both Honglin Huang and his wife are farmers. They have 10 mu of farmland and an annual income of about 10,000 yuan. Even without eating or drinking, it will take eight years to pay off the medical debt. "Let my daughter live on in others" Hong YuanYuan, 20 years old, a student in the class of Business Japanese 0901 at Xiangfan Vocational and Technical College. Last December, she interned in Shenzhen with her classmates. Because she didn't buy a train ticket to go home, she stayed temporarily in a rented apartment of a high school classmate in Guangming New District, Shenzhen. On the fourth day of the first lunar month, a gas poisoning incident occurred in the rented apartment, and Yuan Yuan was hospitalized. On the fifth day of the first lunar month (January 27), Hong Lin and his wife rushed to Shenzhen and transferred their daughter to the ICU (Intensive Care Unit) of the Second People's Hospital of Shenzhen for treatment. "On the ninth day of the first lunar month, the attending doctor told me that Yuan Yuan had already lost autonomous breathing, and the brain edema had already pressed on the nerves, making the chances of waking up very small." Since early February, Hong Lin and his wife haven't rested peacefully, spending day and night on the stainless-steel chairs outside the intensive care unit. Over the phone, Hong Lin told the reporter that his wife once strongly opposed the donation, and he consoled her like this: "As parents, seeing our child unable to move, who wouldn't feel heartbroken? I don't want her to turn into ashes and disappear, but rather hope that she lives on in others." Yuan Yuan's younger brother is in the third year of junior high school at home, and Hong Lin hasn't yet told his son about Yuan Yuan's accident. "I haven't told him yet, planning to guide him slowly when I get home." Deng Fei initiated a love donation for the Huang family.
