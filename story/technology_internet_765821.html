Ballmer to Employees Memo: This is a Critical Time for Microsoft's Transition

by anonymous on 2013-08-26 13:59:17

Microsoft announced on Friday that its CEO, Steve Ballmer, has decided to retire within the next 12 months. Ballmer has been serving as Microsoft's CEO since he took over from company founder Bill Gates in 2000.

Driven by this news, Microsoft's stock surged nearly 9% in pre-market trading on the NASDAQ Stock Market on Friday, reaching $35.28 per share. Below is the memo sent by Ballmer to Microsoft employees, providing a detailed explanation of his decision to retire. In the memo, Ballmer mentioned that he originally planned to "retire during the company's transformation into a device and service company," which he has been striving to execute. He said, "We need a CEO who can lead the company in a new direction for the long term."

Below is the original memo sent by Ballmer to employees:

Title: Moving Forward

Date: August 23, 2013

I am writing this letter to inform you that I will retire from my position as CEO of Microsoft within the next 12 months after a successor is selected. There is never a perfect time for such a transition, but now is the right time. My initial retirement plan was to retire during the company's transformation into a device and service company. We need a CEO who can lead the company in a new direction for the long term. For more information about my retirement, please refer to the press release published on the Microsoft News Center.

This is a critical period of transformation for Microsoft. Our new executive team is excellent. The strategy we have developed is top-notch. Our new corporate structure centered on functions and technology is well-suited for the opportunities and challenges ahead.

Microsoft is an incredible company. I love this company. I love how we helped invent and popularize computing and personal computers. I love our bold and ambitious ventures. I love our employees and their talents, and our willingness to accept and embrace their diverse capabilities, including their quirks. I love how we collaborate with other companies to change the world and achieve success together. I love the breadth and diversity of our customers, ranging from consumers to enterprises across various industries, countries, and people from all backgrounds and age groups.

I am proud of what we have accomplished. Since I joined Microsoft, our annual revenue has grown from $7.5 million to nearly $78 billion, and our workforce has expanded from over 30 employees to nearly 100,000. I feel great about playing a part in achieving such success. We have over a billion users and have generated substantial profits for our shareholders. In fact, we have delivered more profit and cash returns to shareholders than almost any other company in history.

I am excited about our mission to empower the world and believe in our future success. I value my ownership in Microsoft and look forward to continuing as one of Microsoft's largest shareholders.

Emotionally, this is a difficult decision for me. Taking this step is in the best interest of the company I love; it is the thing outside of my family and closest friends that matters most to me.

Microsoft’s best days are still ahead. I know you are part of the best team in the industry and possess the right technological assets. We cannot and will not falter in this transformation. Let’s be proud of ourselves.

Steve

Below is the English original version:

I am writing to let you know that I will retire as CEO of Microsoft within the next 12 months, after a successor is chosen. There is never a perfect time for this type of transition, but now is the right time. My original thoughts on timing would have had my retirement happen in the middle of our transformation to a devices and services company focused on empowering customers in the activities they value most. We need a CEO who will be here longer term for this new direction. You can read the press release on Microsoft News Center.

This is a time of important transformation for Microsoft. Our new Senior Leadership team is amazing. The strategy we have generated is first class. Our new organization, which is centered on functions and engineering areas, is right for the opportunities and challenges ahead.

Microsoft is an amazing place. I love this company. I love the way we helped invent and popularize computing and the PC. I love the bigness and boldness of our bets. I love our people and their talent and our willingness to accept and embrace their range of capabilities, including their quirks. I love the way we embrace and work with other companies to change the world and succeed together. I love the breadth and diversity of our customers, from consumer to enterprise, across industries, countries, and people of all backgrounds and age groups.

I am proud of what we have achieved. We have grown from $7.5 million to nearly $78 billion since I joined Microsoft, and we have grown from employing just over 30 people to almost 100,000. I feel good about playing a role in that success and having committed 100 percent emotionally all the way. We have more than 1 billion users and earn a great profit for our shareholders. We have delivered more profit and cash return to shareholders than virtually any other company in history.

I am excited by our mission of empowering the world and believe in our future success. I cherish my Microsoft ownership, and look forward to continuing as one of Microsoft’s largest owners.

This is an emotional and difficult thing for me to do. I take this step in the best interests of the company I love; it is the thing outside of my family and closest friends that matters to me most.

Microsoft has all its best days ahead. Know you are part of the best team in the industry and have the right technology assets. We cannot and will not miss a beat in these transitions. I am focused and driving hard and know I can count on all of you to do the same. Let’s do ourselves proud.

Steve
