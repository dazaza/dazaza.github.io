Google launches a new service to help Android developers track user locations

by geekzhang on 2013-05-16 19:56:08

May 16, Beijing time -- According to foreign media reports, at the Google I/O Developer Conference, Google has brought a series of updates and upgrades for its software product line. Now, Android app developers can use these newly launched features to better track user locations without severely draining the battery.Now, Google is offering mobile app developers more ways to understand user activities and locations, and even more wonderfully, it won't significantly impact battery life.Using personal location information has become a trend for many apps, closely aligning services, advertisements, and discounts with user behavior, helping users track their activities. Leading this wave is Google's own Google Now service, which uses sensors or other data (such as emails) to predict people's needs before they need to open an app or perform a search. When such services are handed over to Android developers, there will be more and more third-party software adding similar in-the-moment data.Today, at the annual Google I/O Developer Conference in San Francisco, USA, Google launched a new service that can identify whether the user is driving, walking, or biking, and all this can be done without the high-data-consumption GPS sensor reporting data in real-time in the background. Activity recognition can access the device's accelerometer and use Google's algorithms for learning. Over time, the service can determine confusing situations, such as whether a person is stuck in traffic or just enjoying a pleasant walk after dinner.Another service allows developers to create Geofences. Developers can define an area on the map, such as an office area or shopping center, and once the user enters this area, it will trigger notifications or other automatic behaviors, such as popping up a discount message or setting an alarm.Using this service, Google Maps becomes more convenient, and developers can create up to 100 map-based triggers. Gradually, some applications may allow users to draw their own exclusive geofences, marking home positions or work locations.Currently, there are quite a few applications that use location information to achieve certain functions, but a common defect is that these functions can cause battery life loss. If people realize that an application consumes too much power, they might immediately close it, or not even choose to download it in the first place. To address this issue, Google also announced a feature called FusedLocationProvider, rewriting the positioning algorithm to use all the phone's sensors—not just GPS—to more accurately and quickly determine the location.Hugo Barra, Vice President of Android at Google, pointed out that the number of applications in the Google Play store has achieved rapid growth, with total installations reaching 48 billion times. Despite this, in terms of monetary returns, applications on Apple's iOS platform still lead, mainly because many applications often target Apple devices first and launch on the iOS platform. Helping Android developers better track user activities and locations may help Google reverse this situation.
