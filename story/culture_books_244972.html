**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" - Content Related to Sending 84 Martyrs Home**

Wang Aifu is an ordinary retired cadre. During one of his amateur collecting sessions, he accidentally came across four large volumes of registration records for fallen soldiers from the Taiyuan Campaign during the Liberation War, as well as 84 undelivered death notifications hidden within them. Over the course of ten years, braving all kinds of weather, he traveled across more than a dozen provinces and regions, spending all his savings, mobilizing widespread participation from all sectors of society. From having streaks of gray hair to becoming completely white-haired, he successfully found the families of over 60 fallen soldiers, helping their spirits return home or restoring their status as martyrs. He made the names of those who sacrificed their lives for the country shine brightly and provided long-awaited solace and eternal glory to their grieving families.

**Chapter One: The Bond Between a Veteran and Another Group of Veterans**

That day, the occurrence was almost serendipitous, as if it were fate. While rummaging through a vendor's sack filled with old books and papers, he almost absentmindedly picked up something—a yellowed notebook that left him stunned.

“Taiyuan Campaign,” “Fallen” – these words danced before his eyes. He thought he might be seeing things but upon closer inspection, there was no mistake. It clearly read “Registration of Fallen Soldiers from the 68th Army Taiyuan Campaign.”

How could this be? What kind of joke is this?

How did such a thing end up here?

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Two: Turning Point**

Wang Aifu never truly let go of the mission to find the families of these martyrs. An illness made him realize how urgent this matter was. However, all attempts over the past decade had disappeared without a trace, making him reluctant to even mention it anymore.

He couldn’t express it, feeling quite frustrated inside.

Not saying it didn’t mean he wasn’t acting. He tried every method possible to publicize his search for the families, hoping society would know, hoping someone might see it, or perhaps that someone was the family of the martyr listed on the death notification, leading them to come forward.

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Three: Twists and Turns Along the Fen River Basin**

Even before going to Wuhan, while confirming the burial site of martyr Hao Zaihu, the hotline number published in the newspaper for finding the families of martyrs had already received over 300 calls. These calls included inquiries about finding relatives, as well as greetings and praise, providing many valuable pieces of information.

Facing so much information, Wang Aifu and his colleagues from the collectors' association discussed starting from the counties around Taiyuan before expanding further.

“This doesn’t wait for time. Nearly 60 years have passed. When the martyrs fell, the youngest were in their teens and the oldest in their thirties. Their relatives are also nearing old age. If the families don't know the whereabouts of their loved ones within their lifetimes, it will forever be a regret.”

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Five: Relay, Comforting the Souls of Martyrs**

The winter vacation social practice confirmed the whereabouts of five martyrs and located the families of four. This result greatly exceeded the expectations of the event organizers, and even the 172 students participating in the activity didn’t expect such a smooth outcome. Although some groups didn’t complete their tasks, the uncertain clues they obtained during their visits occasionally surfaced, and they eventually had to give up. However, this process cannot be considered fruitless.

As Huazhong University of Science and Technology’s Vice Secretary of the Youth League Committee, Chen Gang said, the groups that succeeded in finding had gains, and those that didn’t find results can’t be called unsuccessful. In this process, our students were not only searching for the families of martyrs but also found a belief, a responsibility, and a historical memory.

Indeed, for these young students who are just 20 years old, what’s more important is a rare life experience.

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Six: Returning Souls**

Starting from November 2005 when the first family of a martyr was found, good news kept coming in from Hubei, Hunan, Anhui, Hebei, Sichuan, Guizhou, and other places, gathering in the cold, damp office near the Shanxi Provincial Museum. However, the atmosphere in this narrow, dark office remained lively, enthusiastic, and even busy, somewhat chaotic. After all, that was a room less than 10 square meters in size.

More and more media focused on the activity initiated by Wang Aifu to find the families of martyrs, and more and more people voluntarily joined the ranks to help find these families. Wang Aifu seemed to become a "celebrity" overnight.

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Seven: Tragic Song of Yanzhao**

On May 22, 2006, Wang Aifu was invited by the Yanzhao Evening News to participate in a reader meeting held in Shijiazhuang, Hebei Province, organized by the newspaper for its special action titled "Great Search for Families of Hebei Martyrs."

Upon arriving in Shijiazhuang, Wang Aifu was deeply moved by the attention and support given by all sectors of society to the activity of finding the families of martyrs. At this time, Shijiazhuang welcomed a fine spring rain, but it did not affect the enthusiasm of the meeting. Many readers braved the rain to rush to the newspaper office to converse and exchange ideas face-to-face with the elderly man from Shanxi.

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Eight: Restoring Honor, Retrieving Memories**

The stories are endless. Under each name of the martyrs are written countless twists and tragic events, tears and blood.

Every family of the martyrs found has one wish: to personally see the land where their loved ones shed their blood, to personally offer flowers at the graves of their long-lost relatives after 57 years, to mourn and express remembrance.

On November 22, 2005, Wang Aifu welcomed the first batch of relatives of the martyrs mentioned in the death notifications. The martyrs shed their blood on the land before their eyes, and although their families arrived late, this belated tribute seemed not too late. Wang Aifu read a sense of solemnity, dignity, and nobility on the face of each relative.

**"Sending 84 Martyrs Home" Chapter Nine: Finding Relatives in 2008**

Since 2005, when the first location of a martyr was found until today, Wang Aifu has never left the help of media friends. In 2005 and 2006, Wang Aifu was a regular guest on TV stations. Invitations poured in from Shanghai, Hong Kong, Hebei, Shandong, Beijing, and even CCTV. Taiyuan Television was always close at hand, ready to act whenever a clue appeared. He didn’t care much about radio or television. Going on TV and being featured in newspapers to expand influence has been an important way to obtain clues for finding relatives over the years. He is grateful to modern media. Looking back now, the media and media workers themselves actually directly participated in the activity of finding the families of martyrs, which is an important part of this news event.

But this time, the situation got bigger.
