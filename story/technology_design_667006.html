The editor will first introduce what collagen is! There are 27 types of collagen in the human body, and the types of collagen contained in different parts vary. The skin collagen is mainly Type I and Type III, formed by three left-handed peptide chains twisted into a right-handed structure. Collagen is the main component of the dermis layer of human skin, accounting for more than 80%. In the skin, collagen forms a dense elastic net that can lock in moisture and support the skin like a scaffold.

In the skin, collagen acts as a "spring," determining the elasticity and firmness of the skin; it is also a "reservoir," determining the water content and storage capacity of the skin. Collagen directly determines the hydration, smoothness, tightness, and "skin age" of the skin.

Collagen is an extracellular protein consisting of three peptide chains twisted into a helical fiber protein. Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body, accounting for over 30% of total body proteins. Collagen is rich in amino acids such as glycine, proline, and hydroxyproline required by the human body. It is the most important component of the extracellular matrix. An adult's body contains about 3 kilograms of collagen, mainly found in the skin, bones, eyes, teeth, tendons, internal organs (including heart, stomach, intestines, blood vessels), etc. Its function is to maintain the shape and structure of the skin and tissues and is also an important raw material for repairing damaged tissues. In the components of human skin, 70% is composed of collagen.
