Bathroom and home decoration are important parts of a family. Every year, new decoration styles appear in the home decoration market. According to industry insiders, the concept of home decoration will change significantly in the future. This year, home decoration is beginning to return to its essence, decorating for "people" and for "home." Future spaces should consider more personal spiritual and emotional factors within limited space. Decoration serves people; it's not about decorating for the sake of decoration. In terms of decoration, we should guide consumers to be people-oriented, considering the needs of the family, pursuing what suits their own characteristics, and not abandoning the essence for the sake of form.

Diverse Styles

The eternal theme of room design reveals itself when the drawbacks of pseudo-minimalist styles become increasingly apparent. More and more designers start re-examining home designs that meet the homeowners' lifestyle requirements based on their specific needs. Adding modern elements to classical styles makes the entire space more vibrant or adding European elements to Chinese-style environments creates a more comfortable life, gradually being accepted by people. The fusion of styles not only demonstrates design diversity but also emphasizes people's understanding of different levels of life from another aspect. With the increase in property ownership frequency, people's broadened horizons, and full understanding of life and lifestyle, they emphasize nature, health, comfort, and the atmosphere of gathering with family even more. In the future, people will stress the healthy psychological need of bringing nature into home design, such as connecting indoor and outdoor spaces through green plants indoors or using high-tech means to create the effect of a stream in the bathtub, which are currently popular.

Elegant Simplicity

Classical simplicity blends seamlessly with elegance. Elegance can be simple without the dullness and monotony of pseudo-minimalism; elegance can be classical without the complexity and seriousness of classical styles. Elegance originates from materials and decorative details. Coated and polished wood, rich and warm colors, luxurious fabrics, refined black accents, smooth hardwood floors, or polished tiles combined together can make the entire living atmosphere filled with warmth and comfort. It combines classical shapes with modern lines, ergonomics with decorative art, fully highlighting the natural and rustic characteristics.

Natural Texture

Bringing nature back into the home, emphasizing the natural texture in design, and expressing the quality of life through material details have gradually been recognized by people. Natural decorations, environmentally friendly materials, and even an insignificant corner can reflect the homeowner's attitude towards life and pursuit. Displaying natural textures has two ways: one is using industrial methods to fully express the natural texture hidden in decorative materials, paired with non-metallic lighting; the other is selecting more pine, cedar, and other natural solid wood furniture, which best shows its natural texture. Currently, some Cantonese-style furniture excels in showcasing natural textures, such as coffee tables or desks being cross-sections sawed from thick tree stumps, with rough bark around the edges, concentric growth rings in the middle of the table, and visible cracks, giving a startling yet satisfying experience. The essence of the original wood is fully released.

Pursuit of Individuality

Young people prefer open kitchens and large countertop cabinets, while middle-aged and elderly people have higher requirements for the reasonable placement of dining and kitchen items. The demand for dining and kitchen styles and spatial division varies greatly across different social classes. Pursuing individuality will be the mainstream of dining, kitchen, and bathroom decoration in 2011. Due to the relatively small bathroom space in Chinese residential homes, style positioning should be determined by space, scale, or personal economic strength and construction technical conditions. Currently, changes in Chinese interior design are rapid, making it difficult to fixate on one trend. For example, the "new generation" likes placing beds in bathrooms, so designers must guide consumers to explore their inner desires, focusing on what they truly like, even if it's no longer trendy but still favored by the owner, requiring us to empathize with consumers heart-to-heart. Trends must suit the owner themselves.
