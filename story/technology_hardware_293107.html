The Thai boxing champion said he wanted to challenge Chinese Kung Fu. The Emei school will respond to the challenge.

Before the exciting competition, a bold "challenge letter" was revealed first. Representing the strength of Muay Thai are the five boxing kings - Shenmu Sha, Guijian Xi, Magic Zhuī, Quanmie Feng, and Tulong Zhou who challenged Shaolin Kung Fu.

However, as the "challenged" party, the Shaolin Temple recently stated that "Shaolin monks mainly focus on cultivation and will never challenge others actively, nor accept challenges from any side." Recently, Wang Jian, the leading figure of the Emei Sect, and his accomplished disciple Huang Lin submitted an application to participate in the competition to the Chinese Wushu Association, aiming to meet the arrogant and overbearing Muay Thai Kings with Chinese Kung Fu.

Replacing Shaolin to face Muay Thai

Since the five Muay Thai Kings - Shenmu Sha, Guijian Xi, Magic Zhuī, Quanmie Feng, and Tulong Zhou arrogantly claimed they would "instantly kill Chinese Kung Fu," Wang Jian, president of the Emei Martial Arts Research Association, has been paying close attention to related reports. As a martial arts expert, the leading figure of the Emei Sect, and the intangible cultural heritage inheritor of Emei Martial Arts, his first reaction was anger. When interviewed by journalists, one could still feel the indignation in this martial arts master's heart. He said, "Chinese martial arts have been passed down for thousands of years. If it were 'a piece of cake,' wouldn't that be a joke? In fact, Chinese Sanshou has faced off multiple times with Muay Thai and Karate, and is not afraid of Muay Thai at all. This time, the words of these Muay Thai Kings have a bit of bragging. What about Shenmu Sha, Guijian Xi, Magic Zhuī, Quanmie Feng, and Tulong Zhou? They sound terrifying, but actually very funny. We want to participate and sweep away the Muay Thai Kings."

After the arrogant statements of the five Muay Thai Kings were published in the media, many Sanda team disciples from Wang Jian's Leshan Giant Buddha Martial Arts School came to "pester" him every day, each full of passion and requesting to join the battle. Disciple "Meng Long Guo Jiang's" words represented the sentiments of many disciples. He said, "The Muay Thai King boasts about 'instantly killing' Chinese martial arts, and many netizens hope that those who practice martial arts bravely step forward to accept the challenge. Since the Shaolin Temple refuses to come forward, many netizens have expressed disappointment or even resentment towards martial arts. As Emei disciples who have practiced martial arts for many years, it is simply unacceptable not to take action at this moment."

Superb disciples fight alongside their master

Wang Jian told reporters that he had been waiting, waiting for the Shaolin Temple to come forward and accept the challenge since the Muay Thai Kings directly pointed to the Shaolin Temple. However, when the news that the Shaolin Temple recently announced that they would not accept the challenge reached him, he could no longer sit still.

Despite not undergoing large amounts of training daily like he used to when he was both the head coach and athlete of the Sichuan Sanda Training Team, he must now stand up and represent the prestige of Emei Sect, one of the three major Chinese martial arts schools.

Meanwhile, Wang Jian's former accomplished disciple, Huang Lin, who participated in the National Sanda Championships and International Sanda Arena Competitions twice consecutively in 1990 and 1991 and achieved remarkable results, joining the Armed Police Headquarters Sanda Team, also thought along the same lines as his master. This iron man, who has now become the deputy captain of the court police, once defeated the world Sanda champion at the national Sanda championship held in Jiaozuo, Henan in 1991.

After retiring from the professional team, Huang Lin still loved martial arts and often persisted in Sanda training during his free time, keeping a close eye on major events in the martial arts world. Upon learning about the Muay Thai King's contemptuous remarks towards Chinese martial arts, especially hearing that the Shaolin Temple refused to accept the challenge, he became extremely agitated. Although martial artists emphasize the word "endurance," there comes a point where endurance is no longer possible, and it becomes unnecessary to endure further, especially since this is not a matter of personal honor but rather about restoring the name of Chinese martial arts. Thus, Huang Lin found his master Wang Jian, and the two passionate men immediately decided to submit an application to the Chinese Wushu Association, strongly requesting participation in the competition to face the Muay Thai Kings. Currently, the master and disciple have already started training, preparing positively, and recently submitted a participation application to the Chinese Wushu Association. Wang Jian also prepared to call Secretary Kang Gewu of the Chinese Wushu Association to request participation.

(According to the report by "Three Rivers Evening News")
