Time of yesterday

by wenuzolove on 2011-09-13 21:47:45

Yesterday's time, I missed a segment; today's time, I wrote a chapter; tomorrow's time, I forgot a scene. Along this journey of time, I heard that the blessing of love is to carve a person's name.

Written beforehand:

In winter, the icy seawater constantly surged onto the beach. I repeatedly drew on the sand with my fingers, but every time it was covered by the seawater, not leaving even a trace. If only everything could be drawn on the sand and disappear when the tide comes, wouldn't that be great? In reality, there are too many things we can never possess, we can only helplessly watch their beauty pass before our eyes again and again. Reality is like a beautiful race track, and at the end, all that’s left for us is desolation and loneliness. The initial splendor has long turned to dust, becoming intangible and disappearing in all directions.

The sea breeze blowing towards me carried no songs but cold words. At this moment, I don't feel overly sad because I've already seen through all of this. For those insignificant contents, I simply ignore them. In the past, did we ever love passionately? But this love wasn't as distant as imagined, and the ending was ultimately a breakup. After the breakup, how many people can still be friends? How many become strangers? And how many aren't even strangers anymore? Unfortunately, there are always people who can't help but look back, checking if anyone turns around and runs back or smiles looking back, but what often appears most frequently is tears wetting the street corners.

In Qinyin Seven's "Distant Town," there is such a passage: Sometimes, we realize that deep longing is the bond that connects us with memories. It ties together all the past events, joys and sorrows, and also guides us through the vast journey of life. This is our destined burden. But I have always willingly borne its heavy weight, using it to balance the frivolousness of life. People always like to engrave in their own exclusive memories those thoughts that have already ended. These continuous longings always entangle the joys and sorrows of the past. Recalling once, being tangled for a while, surrounding for a day, spreading for a year, bearing for a lifetime.

Sometimes, we are too stubborn, blindly pursuing the blessings of love. Unfortunately, it is so rare, and the process is always so profound. The music box of memories keeps spinning. I want to stop it, yet I'm reluctant to do so because the melody is a song we once both liked. I still remember the happy lyrics, but I can't see the shadow of happiness. It turns out that the melody of happiness reflects an illusory joy that dissipates when within reach. I don't know why reality always likes to make people cry and laugh, but what can we do? Those excessive reasons and assumptions only make us feel the pain of parting and the difficulty of letting go, turning even smiles bitter.

The love buried by the sand stirred up by the sea breeze during quiet times, I don't want to dig it up. I'd rather gently cherish the faintly sorrowful posture on each day of remembrance. Time won't rewind for anyone, years won't stop waiting for anyone, and love won't return because of longing. Gazing at the sea ahead, I put my hand into the surging seawater, touching the sand where your name was carved. I see the blessing of love slowly eroding every second and minute of life, from one moment to the next, from one year to a lifetime.
