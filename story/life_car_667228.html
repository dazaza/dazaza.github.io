The price of oil has risen again! In recent days, car owners have started to complain once more. The continuously rising oil prices have almost become an unbearable burden for them. Saving fuel has become their top priority. How to save fuel? "Improving driving skills", "driving as little as possible", "switching to electric vehicles", consumers are brainstorming ideas. Similarly, the automotive industry is also racking its brains. Thus, the call for automobile lightweighting has sounded again...

At the end of 2011, the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology released the "Investment Guidelines for Industrial Transformation and Upgrading". In this important reference for government and financial institutions conducting industrial investment projects, the automobile lightweighting industry was prominently listed. Automobile lightweighting presents a new industrial opportunity.

Lightweighting to "pull" the automobile industry

In the above-mentioned "Investment Guidelines", in the second chapter "Energy conservation and emission reduction - automobile industry", people found the proposition of automobile lightweighting, and at the same time discovered that there was no mention of "electric vehicles". Data shows that the government plans to invest 100 billion yuan to develop electric vehicles within the automobile industry, establish a new energy mechanism, and aims to achieve a sales target of 5 million new energy vehicle models by 2020. However, in reality, the production and sales of electric vehicles have encountered significant bottlenecks. Industry insiders analyze that compared to the "beautiful on the surface" electric vehicle industry, lightweighting is more practical.

"As two different technical means for skill-based emissions reduction, whether it's traditional automobiles or new energy vehicles, lightweighting is a common technology," said Wang Zhiwen, Deputy Secretary-General of the Automotive Lightweighting Technology Innovation Strategic Alliance. The energy-saving effect of new energy is reflected in energy substitution, but this technology, both domestically and internationally, has not yet reached a mature standard, so there is still no good solution to the offsetting of energy savings caused by battery weight issues. According to international authoritative research results, every 100 kilograms reduction in the overall weight of a car can reduce fuel consumption per 100 kilometers by 0.3-0.6 liters. When the car weight decreases from 2500 kilograms to 750 kilograms, the mileage per liter of gasoline increases from about 5 kilometers to about 25 kilometers, and the corresponding carbon dioxide emissions decrease from about 400 grams per kilometer to about 100 grams per kilometer.

Regarding the "Investment Guidelines", Wang Zhiwen believes that "the major significance of placing lightweighting first is not just in terms of investment, but from the policy-making department to the relevant government departments, they have recognized the need to equally focus on traditional energy vehicles and new energy vehicles. And how to improve the energy-saving level of traditional energy vehicles is a more important issue - after all, in the present and for a period in the future, traditional energy vehicles will still be the mainstream product in the market." As the foundation for energy conservation and emissions reduction in traditional energy vehicles, "lightweighting is a visible and tangible, more pragmatic choice, and in terms of promotion, a pragmatic attitude must be adopted."

Who will seize the competitive advantage?

After talking for so long, what exactly is automobile lightweighting? Actually, it refers to reducing the overall weight of a car as much as possible while ensuring the strength and safety performance of the car, thereby improving the car's power performance, reducing fuel consumption, and lowering exhaust pollution. Experiments have proven that if the total weight of a car is reduced by 10%, fuel efficiency can be improved by 6%-8%; for every 100 kilograms reduction in the car's weight, fuel consumption per 100 kilometers can be reduced by 0.3-0.6 liters; for every 1% reduction in car weight, fuel consumption can be reduced by 0.7%. Currently, due to the needs of environmental protection and energy saving, automobile lightweighting has become a global trend in the development of the automotive industry.

Lightweighting is not cutting corners, nor is it simply the lightweighting of the car body or certain parts of the car. A report issued by the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers titled "The Significance of Automotive Lightweighting" pointed out: analyzing and optimizing the overall structure of the car, achieving simplification, integration, and lightness of car parts; engine lightweighting; transmission lightweighting; suspension lightweighting; body lightweighting, and accessory lightweighting are the six current aspects for achieving whole vehicle lightweighting. Guo Shuowen, a researcher of automotive technology and industrial development at Beijing University of Technology, said in an interview that China's automotive lightweighting technology faces two major difficulties. On one hand, breaking down the barriers between various disciplines involved in lightweighting technology to form a comprehensive and systematic knowledge system, and on the other hand, the state should play a role in making strategic and forward-looking deployments.

In fact, although as early as 2008, the automotive lightweighting industry innovation alliance had been established, companies were basically operating independently in this field. Guo Shuowen said that automobile companies' attention to automotive lightweighting is clearly insufficient, especially for domestic brands. If this continues, automobile companies will lose a great opportunity for technological leapfrogging, "Lightweight cars are absolutely one of the key points of future automobile product competition." Guo Shuowen said.

Relevant industries are eager to try

Compared with automobile manufacturers' neglect of automotive lightweighting, the automotive materials industry has sharper eyesight, and many have begun to enhance their technology, hoping to gain a head start in the process of automotive lightweighting. First is the steel industry. In 2011, domestic steel enterprises were nearing comprehensive losses. Raw material costs, labor costs, and capital costs were all high, downstream enterprise orders were significantly declining, and agents generally requested reductions or withdrawal. Domestic steel mills were having a tough time. To find new profit points, many steel enterprises began to enter the relatively close-to-steel automobile industry. The most typical example is Baosteel. The State Key Laboratory of Development and Application Technology for Automotive Steel is located under its wing. In the technical field, Baosteel has gained a certain amount of discourse power. In action, Baosteel has already begun cooperation with SAIC Motors, achieving substantial progress in strategic cooperation on automotive lightweighting technology. Through the application of comprehensive lightweighting technology, the body weight was reduced by approximately 13%, creating conditions for the energy conservation and emissions reduction of automobiles.

There is also the aluminum-magnesium industry.

Due to the low cost-performance ratio of aluminum-magnesium alloys and the lack of obvious advantages in key mechanical properties compared to steel, the use of hot stamping technology with high-strength steel plates is showing a flourishing trend, becoming the best way to simultaneously achieve lightweighting and improve collision safety. This provides new opportunities for industrial development. China Aluminum Corporation, Yunnan Aluminum, etc., are already eager to try. Encouraging news is that according to research analysts from the American consulting company Frost & Sullivan, the revenue of the lightweight automotive materials market reached $38 billion in 2010, and it is estimated that by 2017, this figure could reach $95.34 billion driven by the demand for compliance with environmental regulations.
