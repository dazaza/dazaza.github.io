The father's request for a paternity test was rejected by the woman, and her demand for child support from the man was also not supported by the court. The day before yesterday, Zhenxiong County People's Court held a trial for this divorce dispute case.

The plaintiff, Hu, claimed that he and the defendant, Shen, cohabited at the beginning of 2003 and later completed their marriage registration on April 9, 2007. Currently, their marital relationship has deteriorated to the point of no return, and he is seeking a divorce. Hu stated that the daughter born during their cohabitation is not his biological child. He requests that the daughter be placed under the custody of the defendant, with no obligation on his part to pay child support.

Defendant Shen argued that she agrees to divorce Hu and also agrees to take care of Xiaohua (the daughter), but insists that Xiaohua is the plaintiff's biological daughter and demands that the plaintiff pays 500 yuan per month as child support.

During the trial, Hu applied for a paternity test for Xiaohua, but Shen explicitly refused it.

The court ruled: Hu’s request for divorce was agreed upon by Shen, and thus the court supports Hu’s petition. Due to Shen's refusal to conduct a paternity test on the daughter, it cannot be confirmed whether the daughter is indeed the plaintiff's biological child. Furthermore, since Hu insists that Xiaohua is not his biological child, the court does not support Shen's request for Hu to pay 500 yuan monthly in child support.
