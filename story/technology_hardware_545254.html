Mark is heading naked to the swimming pool at his home located in the "Oasis" neighborhood of Florida. Residents of Miami gather for champagne parties in their own neighborhoods, and of course, they are all naked. This is the Pasco County Nude Beach in Miami.

Although the free-spirited hippie generation is slowly aging, the back-to-nature movement promoted by anti-materialists has begun to rapidly spread again in developed countries. Nudism has not only become a significant lifestyle but also driven a boom in real estate.

Written by Catherine Moye for the Financial Times, translated by Zhuang Qingmei.

Today, nudism is no longer just about casual talk; the real estate market targeting nudists has also flourished, with "optional clothing" communities becoming another emerging market. The related industrial chain of nudism is gradually taking shape, leading to annual evaluations of various sizes of "optional clothing" vacation spots.

Nudists are another major client for real estate agents. Mark Srigman, who lives in the small nudist community called "Oasis" in Pasco County, Florida, said: "They are a group of older people with some savings. They may not need a mortgage to buy a house, allowing them to purchase and move in immediately." Pasco County is known as the "capital of American nudism," featuring many "no clothes" and "optional clothing" neighborhoods.

The contemporary nudism trend originated in Germany in the 1920s and spread to Pasco County, Florida, in the 1940s. A person named Avery Weaver Brubaker bought 2125 acres of land in Lutz and built the first nudist residential area. Today, the largest nudist destinations in Pasco County are Paradise Lakes, Caliente, and Lake Como, along with several smaller communities. This has established Pasco County's reputation in the nudist community.

Nudist neighborhood housing prices remain stable. Even though both are in Pasco County, the degree of nudity varies across different neighborhoods. Residents of Oasis strive to avoid making mail carriers blush. Mark Srigman, who runs a website helping nudists buy and sell houses online, said: "I think it's a mixed neighborhood. In the front yard, we are 'clothed'; but in the backyard, each household is surrounded by a fence, and we remain naked."

Srigman's five-bedroom villa is one of 28 fenced villas, complete with a swimming pool, an outdoor shower, and a community club exclusively for nudists, featuring hot tubs, tennis courts, and a swimming pool. Srigman purchased this house for $750,000 in 2004, which was also the peak time for housing prices in Florida. He guesses that even though housing prices in Florida's nudist communities have dropped, they certainly haven't fallen as much as other areas.

This view is supported by Toby Carolyn, the owner of Sun&Fun Real Estate, located in Paradise Lakes, which gathers over 700 nudist families. It features studios worth $65,000 for 400 square meters and family homes valued at $1 million. About 60% of the residents in Paradise Lakes are permanent residents, while the remaining 40% are temporary vacationers.

Carolyn said: "The nudist community is a market everyone in the real estate sector is vying for. The external market fluctuates, but more and more people are embracing this lifestyle. Factors like unemployment definitely affect the 'clothed' real estate market, which in turn impacts the 'nude' real estate market. However, in Paradise Lakes, only two houses have been foreclosed, significantly lower compared to Tampa in western Florida."

In areas near Tampa, such as Caliente and Dominica, nudists can be completely naked anytime and anywhere, whether in restaurants, bars, clubs, or nightclubs. In 2006, a project called Caliente Tampa launched a batch of residences on 789 acres, including 700 villas, apartments, and cottages, which were almost sold out upon release. Property manager Stephen Dorsey said: "This shows how hot the real estate market in this area is. Prices started soaring in the first year after its launch, and currently, prices have dropped by 20%. Moreover, property prices in Caliente are 20% to 30% higher than those in nearby areas."

The rental market is also stable, with limited options available for nudists. Dorsey said: "About 40% of people in Caliente Tampa are permanent residents, while the other 60% are vacationers. Most villas rent for between $1,500 and $2,000 per month."

Clicking on the Caliente Tampa website, you will find that developers have come up with the most enticing slogans to attract clients who enjoy nudity, such as: "Here you can experience the most luxurious 'optional clothing' experience imaginable. Whether you're a pure nudist or just want to explore your potential, the perfect adventure awaits you here."

Following the success of Caliente Tampa, the real estate company acquired another nudist community called Cabrea in the Dominican Republic, which is popular among European nudists, especially from the UK, Russia, France, and Spain. After renovations and upgrades, these properties will be released in 2010, increasing the number of houses to 2,000, including standalone homes and villas priced between $200,000 and $500,000. "Whenever you discover a good business model, you should take advantage of it," Dorsey said.

A similar growth trend is also evident in Europe. Andrew Welch, the commercial manager of the British Naturism Society, said: "Many people live according to the nudist lifestyle. There is no doubt that public acceptance of nudism is much higher than it was ten years ago."

In nudist neighborhoods, the criteria for evaluating a person also change, at least eliminating questions like "Is she wearing Prada or fake Versace?" Without nosy neighbors, nudist neighborhoods are more suitable for living. Similarly, beaches frequented by nudists are quieter and cleaner than others.

The Mediterranean town of Cap d'Agde in southern France is one of the world's largest nudist gathering places. This town is called the "naked town" because nudity is legal and very common everywhere, including restaurants and shops, day and night. The town has a 2-kilometer-long beach, a marina, a hotel, and 2,000 residences, ranging from standalone villas, townhouses to wooden cabins and stylish apartments. Two-bedroom houses near the sea can sell for $200,000. The peak season lasts 17 weeks from May to September, with up to 4,000 tourists during the busiest times, and the best apartment rents can reach €140 per night.

David James, who handles property rentals and sales in Cap d'Agde, said: "We have also been affected by this year's economic crisis, and house prices here are lower than before. But the price of houses in the center of the nudist community is still 20% higher than in the surrounding non-nudist communities."

To showcase France's liberal lifestyle, there are nudist neighborhoods throughout the country. To cater to the nudists' concept of "returning to nature," these neighborhoods are mostly concentrated in forests, including La Jenny near the Atlantic Ocean in the Bordeaux region.

La Jenny is a self-sufficient resort where there are numerous wooden cabins close to the beach. A two-bedroom cabin costs around $80,000, with kitchens and living rooms; houses with extra living rooms and large balconies can cost up to $300,000. During the peak season, the rental price for two months is approximately $4,000 to $5,000.

Similar phenomena exist in other Eurozone countries. Although Spain is not very tolerant towards nudists, they are still active on three large coasts: Narura coast has a nudist resort and an officially recognized nude beach, where vacation apartments sell for $90,000; EI Portus, 10 kilometers from the historical city of Cartagena, is a semi-camping and semi-enclosed resort; Vera Playa on the southeast coast of Spain includes a beach, several restaurants, bars, stores, and 250 residences. A modernly decorated studio apartment costs $100,000, while a luxury home with 5 rooms and a swimming pool costs $1 million. In the hot summer, there can be up to two to three thousand nudist tourists.

Local resident and real estate agent Peter Mason said: "Vera Playa is more suitable for family vacations. Although people tend to wear clothes at night to avoid mosquito bites, all bar and restaurant staff work unclothed."
