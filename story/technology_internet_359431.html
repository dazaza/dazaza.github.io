Yingli Green Energy Powers "Football for Hope"

by ditan360 on 2010-06-29 14:03:58

Low Carbon Network exclusive - Yingli Green Energy Holding Company Limited (referred to as "Yingli Green Energy" or "the company"), is a leading solar energy company and one of the world's largest vertically integrated photovoltaic product manufacturers. The company announced today that it will provide solar power systems for 20 "Football for Hope Centers." The "Football for Hope Centers" (20 Centres for 2010) is one of the official events of the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa, and an important component of the "Football for Hope" campaign. The "Football for Hope" campaign is jointly organized by the international governing body of football, the Federation Internationale de Football Association (referred to as "FIFA") and the social welfare organization Grassroots Football Alliance (Street Foodball World), aiming to promote social reform through football.

Yingli Green Energy will actively support this event by providing solar power systems for the 20 Football for Hope Centers distributed across Africa. This sponsorship activity has been named: "Football for Hope, Energy for Hope." The sponsored solar power systems will provide the necessary electricity for lighting, computers, and classrooms at the Football for Hope Centers. These 20 centers have five located in South Africa and the remaining fifteen in other African countries. The first six are located in South Africa, Kenya, Rwanda, Mali, Namibia, and Ghana.

Yingli Green Energy will co-host with FIFA a press conference titled "Football for Hope, Energy for Hope" on June 30th in Johannesburg, South Africa. At that time, FIFA's Corporate Social Responsibility Director Mr. Federico Addiechi and senior executives from Yingli Green Energy will attend and be available for one-on-one interviews. Additionally, the company will display various solar application systems customized according to the different needs of each venue.

Mr. Miao Liansheng, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Yingli Green Energy, said: "This World Cup provides Yingli Green Energy with a great opportunity to participate in the 'Football for Hope' initiative, linking solar energy with social public welfare activities. Corporate social responsibility is one of the core values of Yingli Green Energy, and we widely participate in social public welfare activities around the globe. We are committed to making solar power a low-carbon green energy source that is affordable for ordinary people."

Mr. Federico Addiechi stated: "We are very pleased that Yingli Green Energy, as a sponsor of the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa, also actively supports the 'Football for Hope' campaign. We commend Yingli's vision of building a better future, as this aligns with one of FIFA's core values."

The press conference will be held on June 30th in Johannesburg, South Africa, and sincerely invites all friends from the press to attend.

Introduction to Yingli Green Energy

Yingli Green Energy Holding Company Limited is a globally leading solar energy company and one of the world's largest vertically integrated photovoltaic product manufacturers. Yingli Green Energy's production line covers the entire photovoltaic industry chain, including polysilicon production, silicon ingot casting, wafer cutting, photovoltaic cell production, and photovoltaic module packaging. To date, Yingli Green Energy's annual production capacity exceeds 600 megawatts for all production stages from silicon ingot casting to photovoltaic module packaging. The Baoding 300-megawatt and Hainan 100-megawatt expansion projects are currently underway, and Yingli Green Energy's total capacity is expected to reach 1 gigawatt by the end of 2010. In addition, the self-built silicon material factory, Sixiu Silicon Industry, with a designed annual production capacity of 3,000 tons, entered the segmented trial operation phase in December 2009 and is expected to achieve full-line linkage in the middle of 2010. Yingli Green Energy has extensive sales markets for its photovoltaic modules, including Germany, Spain, Italy, Greece, France, South Korea, China, and the United States. Yingli Green Energy's headquarters is located in Baoding, China, with more than 7,000 employees and over 10 branches and offices worldwide. Yingli Green Energy is a publicly listed company on the New York Stock Exchange in the United States.

Introduction to FIFA

The International Federation of Association Football (referred to as "FIFA") is headquartered in Zurich, Switzerland, and is the governing body of world football, responsible for all aspects of work from setting match rules to organizing major international events (such as the World Cup). In 1904, representatives from Belgium, Denmark, France, Netherlands, Spain, Sweden, and Switzerland jointly signed relevant documents, officially establishing FIFA. FIFA now has more than 200 member organizations and has established regional organizations on six continents: Asian Football Confederation, Confederation of African Football, Union of European Football Associations, Oceania Football Confederation, Confederation of North, Central America and Caribbean Association Football, and South American Football Confederation. As the highest authority of world football, FIFA aims to embody the core values of football matches and ensure the highest quality of football worldwide. For this purpose, FIFA has formulated a strict testing system for global football manufacturers, namely the "FIFA Quality Concept for Footballs." The globally recognized "FIFA Quality Marks" (i.e., approved by FIFA and inspected by FIFA) ensures the high quality and uniformity of footballs.
