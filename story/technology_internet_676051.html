The Supreme People's Court requires that railway courts be transferred to local authorities before the end of June.

by king2z9o on 2012-02-25 16:55:46

Our press (Song Shirad) reported yesterday that the Supreme People's Court issued a notice on the 23rd, requesting the rapid completion of the reform of railway transport courts and the transfer to the central government by the end of June this year.

On January 12th, Taiyuan Railway Procuratorate underwent restructuring, with personnel, finances, and materials transferred from the railway system to the central government, achieving local governance. However, the Taiyuan Railway Court system did not undergo synchronous restructuring.

This newspaper recently published a series of reports titled "The Great Reform of Railway Justice." Informed sources analyzed that the lack of synchronized transfer of railway transport courts to the central government was related to the absence of unified and clear standards for court establishment, especially regarding grassroots courts not located in provincial capitals, where no consensus existed between the courts and the Ministry of Railways. It was learned at the time that the Supreme People's Court would issue a document to address the above issues.

Yesterday, it was learned that the Supreme People's Court had already issued a notice to relevant provincial high courts on the 23rd, requesting the rapid completion of the reform of railway transport courts. It is understood that the notice clarified the transfer standards for personnel, finances, and materials of railway transport courts, made specific regulations on office space, court construction, informatization construction, and vehicle configuration, and requested that provinces quickly sign transfer agreements and complete the transfer process.

Previously, the Ministry of Railways (Weibo) had indicated that financial support for the two judicial institutions of railways would continue until the end of June this year.
