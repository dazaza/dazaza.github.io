Mr. He, with "little girl," collects trash at a university in Wuhan (photo by reporter Xiao Hao). Jingchu.com News (Chutian Metropolis Daily) (reporter Cai Qing, special reporter Liu Chunbin, correspondent Wei Wentao): For 31 years, I have successively adopted one elderly person and four children. Aside from the "girl" who is over three years old by my side, the other three children, just like my biological son, have all entered university. And in order to take care of and support the "second girl" who is currently pursuing her master's degree, I live in a small rented room in Donghu Mianzixiang, making a living by collecting trash near a university in Wuhan and shining shoes.

This year, at the age of 62, Mr. He was accidentally discovered by reporters due to an occasional family search action by our newspaper. Out of respect for Mr. He's wishes, our newspaper did not disclose his name. But his actions will surely move many people.

Unexpectedly,

Encountering a legendary figure on the way to search for relatives

Four days ago, Ms. Xiao, working in Dongguan, entrusted our newspaper to look for her sister who had been separated for 25 years in Tianmen. After several days of searching in the urban area of Tianmen, the reporter learned that Mr. He from Tianmen had once adopted a girl. However, Mr. He's family was no longer in Tianmen and had lost contact with their hometown for several years. The day before yesterday, after our newspaper published "On the Way to Search for Relatives, Harvesting a Series of Warm Memories," early in the morning, Mr. Ke from Daye called our newspaper: I had seen this master at a certain university in Wuhan, where he picked up trash to support his adopted daughter who was studying for her master's degree.

After several inquiries, the reporter finally found Mr. He, 62 years old, in a rental house of four or five square meters in Donghu Mianzixiang. He sheepishly said: It's too messy here, let's talk outside.

A little girl of three or four years old was riding on Mr. He's neck. Looking up, she saw a kite flying and asked, "Dad, what is that?" Mr. He said, "That is an eye watching the ground, it can see everything bad that happens." The little girl seemed to understand and said, "Then she will listen to you and not bother you." Over the course of a few hours, the little girl remained leaning against Mr. He's chest, very adorable.

The conversation began with the little girl. Mr. He recalled that in January 2007, while taking a bus from Tianmen to Wuhan, when passing through a gas station in Hanchuan, a woman less than 30 years old claimed she was going to the restroom and left the baby girl on the bus, never showing up again. Later, passengers found a note inside the cloth wrapping the baby, which read, "January 28th, Xiang."

As the bus moved on, all the passengers were discussing which heartless parents abandoned the child. Mr. He could not bear it, so he took the girl home and gave her a beautiful name: Xiang Zhuolin. "The child grows up on your shoulders, you hope I grow strong and healthy."

Legendary,

Adopting one mother and four children

From the Civil Affairs Bureau of Tianmen, the reporter learned that Mr. He has five children, only the eldest son born in 1980 is his biological child. The other four children are all adopted.

Mr. He recalled that in March 1984, he saw an abandoned baby girl at the gate of the People's Hospital in Tianmen and immediately felt compassion. After bringing her home, he told his wife: She is also a life, let's keep her. Unexpectedly, in December of the same year, he found another baby girl wrapped in swaddling clothes near Tianmen Bridge. His wife suggested he go to the Civil Affairs Bureau. At that time, the Civil Affairs Bureau was somewhat embarrassed and told Mr. He: Since you do not yet have arrangements for these children, let me take care of them for a while.

In March 1986, while sending off his father for burial, Mr. He saw a boy in a basket on the road leading from the Tianmen Funeral Home to Zhaoshi Town, wearing a coarse cloth coat, emaciated, covered in red spots, and his lips quivering. This time, Mr. He did not "move" initially. But after leaving, he felt uneasy and returned to find the boy still there. He quickly scooped the boy into his arms and brought him home.

The previous day, the reporter found several neighbors of Mr. He in the Jinglecheng district of Tianmen. They said that at the time, Mr. He's home already had a six-year-old son, several two-year-old "girls," and a newly adopted "son." Moreover, there was also a seventy-year-old grandmother adopted from Jing Mountain County.

It was 1979, when Mr. He went to Yangji People's Commune in Jingshan County to dig a well. In the temporary village where he lived, he saw a sixty-two-year-old widow named Zuo Chuangxiang, who due to mobility issues, had to use an earthenware jar to fetch water every day. Mr. He then said to the elderly woman, "I lost my mother at nine, I really want a mom, come back to Tianmen with me, and I'll take care of you until the end!" The elderly woman did not believe Mr. He, thinking he was just saying it. Later, Mr. He gradually completed the adoption procedures and brought the elderly woman home. She lived to be eighty-six before passing away.

Hardship,

Encountering cheap vegetables and carrying them back in a sack

Mr. He's family conditions were not good. With so many children, even eating became a problem.

Mr. He said that his household couldn't afford milk, so according to the advice of a village Chinese medicine practitioner, he "discovered" a local food: using ten pounds of rice, one pound of sesame seeds, one pound of red beans, one pound of green beans, and one pound of soybeans, soak them, steam them, spread them out to dry, then grind them into fine powder to feed the children. "Who doesn't want their child to drink good milk, but I really didn't have the money, so I had to think of such local methods."

In 1986, when the third child was picked up, the eldest girl fell ill with a persistent fever for half a month. Mr. He sent the child to the People's Hospital in Tianmen, diagnosed with congenital biliary obstruction. At the time, Dean Li contacted Wuhan Union Hospital, deciding to transfer the patient, but it would cost tens of thousands of yuan in medical expenses.

Mr. He sold everything he owned and borrowed money everywhere, and Grandma Zuo contributed her savings of 200 yuan accumulated over many years, eventually raising only 4,000 yuan. Mr. He then sought help from Long Wangyou, then Director of the Tianmen Municipal Party Committee.

This experience, recalled yesterday by Deng, who worked in the Tianmen Municipal Party Committee at the time: At the municipal government compound, Director Long gathered the heads of the Women's Federation and the Civil Affairs Bureau, asking them to subsidize as much money as possible. For the shortfall, they launched a city-wide donation appeal. However, Mr. He refused, saying: "I cannot trouble society like this, otherwise others might think I am incapable of raising these children." They helped her secure a loan of 5,000 yuan, part of which was used for the child's treatment, and the rest to rent a taxi.

Director Long agreed and secured a 5,000 yuan interest-free loan for Mr. He. Director Long also helped solve the household registration issue for the children.

The child was transferred to Union Hospital and saved. Mr. He rented a taxi and drove tirelessly around the city of Tianmen. When grain was scarce at home, he sometimes encountered cheap vegetables elsewhere and bought a whole sack to bring home, enough to last for quite some time.

Satisfaction,

Three adopted children entered university

Around 2000, the girls and boys were about to enter high school. In 2001, the second girl missed the entrance score for Tianmen High School by just a few points, each point requiring an additional 2,000 yuan.

Mr. He brought the child to the principal and pleaded for a reduction in fees, but the principal did not agree. Mr. He suddenly knelt down in front of the principal. The principal said: "Don't resort to this kind of behavior." The understanding girl pulled him up and said, "Father, I can study well at another school, I will work hard."

Later, a leader in Tianmen heard about this incident and helped Mr. He contact the city's experimental high school. Meanwhile, his biological son attended a remote fishing middle school, bringing a jar of pickled vegetables home every week.

Mr. He's eldest son graduated from Huazhong University of Science and Technology's computer science department and now works in Beijing; the eldest daughter graduated from Hubei University and found work in Hangzhou; the second daughter is currently pursuing her master's degree at a key university in Wuhan; the second son graduated from Huazhong University of Science and Technology and works in Wuhan.

Two important things in life—

Respect parents above, raise children below

"I lost my mother at nine, and my father was labeled a rightist, enduring much hardship. Kind neighbors and villagers took me in. Later, when I saw other children suffering, I would think of my own childhood. My father was a veteran revolutionary, having served under Li Xiannian. After rehabilitation, he often reminded me to do two important things in life: Respect parents above, and raise children below." Old Mr. He said quietly.

After several children successively entered university, Mr. He moved to Wuhan in 2004, working and earning money while taking care of the children's lives.

Every month, he gives each child 400 yuan. The children are all very sensible, participating in part-time work-study programs, and some even received scholarships.

When the second daughter was admitted to graduate school, her stepmother went to Beijing to take care of her grandson. Once, while picking up recyclables, she met her daughter at school and quickly dodged. The sensible daughter, however, grabbed her hand and said, "Dad, you haven't done anything wrong, why be afraid? She supports you!" At that moment, tears streamed down her face as she hugged her daughter and said, "Child, Dad's efforts were worth it."

Last night, Mr. He's eldest son called the reporter from Beijing, saying he had already seen reports about his father online. "I understand everything my father has done. Dad is ordinary, modest, but in our children's hearts, he is extraordinary."
