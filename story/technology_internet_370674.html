by sharevt on 2010-11-04 15:53:30

[Techwom.com] Recently, the conflict between 360 and Tencent has been hyped up all over the Internet. However, the general public may not realize that this is not just a dispute between 360 and Tencent; it's actually a battle involving the entire Chinese IT industry against Tencent in an anti-monopoly, anti-counterfeiting, and anti-piracy war. Both 360, many other websites, and even Tencent itself, operate on a free value-added service or advertising fee profit model. The difference lies in the fact that most websites specialize in specific areas: those who do antivirus focus on antivirus (360, Kingsoft...), those who do video focus on video (PPLIVE, PPS, Xunlei...), and those who do card games focus on card games (Zha You Xi...). Tencent, however, is different. It leverages its monopolistic position with the high installation rate of its QQ client to do everything. What's worse is that it lacks innovation and has always been about copying others' ideas. When others have done all the work from conception to implementation and cultivating user habits, Tencent steps in to copy, appropriating the fruits of others' labor, rendering all their hard work and investment futile. This is the worst kind of behavior and is what IT professionals criticize the most. Think about it: aren't most people playing card games on Tencent coming from Zha You Xi? Aren't those watching QQ Live mostly coming from PPS and PPLIVE? Aren't those playing farm games coming from Renren and Kaixin? Looking back, you'll see that these sites that once brought you surprises and joy either no longer exist or are barely surviving due to lack of further investment. This is Tencent's culture of counterfeiting. While it copies others and indeed brings convenience to Q friends by allowing them to directly launch corresponding services on the QQ panel without having to type in URLs or log in, etc., it also stifles others' innovation, creativity, and entrepreneurial spirit while bringing convenience to the vast number of QQ users.
