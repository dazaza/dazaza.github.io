[Repost] Teach You How to Build Good Interpersonal Relationships

by koucai4472 on 2012-03-08 16:02:38

How to build good interpersonal relationships? I think this is also an answer that many people are looking for, because we live in this society and always have to interact with others. In fact, interacting with others is what we call interpersonal relationships. The quality of your interpersonal relationships has a direct relationship with how enjoyable your life is. Imagine, a person with bad interpersonal relationships, who can't fit in anywhere and frequently argues with others. Do you think such a person would be happy with their life? On the other hand, a person with excellent interpersonal skills can make a lot of good friends wherever they go, and people around them won't cause them trouble. When they need help, there will always be someone by their side. Would you like to have such a life? In fact, it's not difficult to have such a life. As long as we learn how to handle interpersonal relationships, this kind of life will be attracted to us.

Handling interpersonal relationships, from one perspective, is a reflection of eloquence. This may seem relatively thin and weak when said like this. In fact, interpersonal relationships involve many aspects, not just eloquence. It also relates to the way you conduct yourself, etc. There are many ways that interpersonal relationships are manifested, and the simplest one should be an important part of our daily lives - the workplace. In the workplace, our interpersonal relationships are mainly reflected in team communication, team building, and leadership eloquence, among other things. Have you noticed that those with excellent public speaking skills, those who are articulate, usually have many people surrounding them? This is just one aspect of the manifestation of eloquence. Interpersonal relationships have many forms of manifestation. So next, the Oral Talent Training Network will show you how to attract such a life to come into ours.

1. To gain others' recognition and evaluation, first you must have conditions that can be recognized and evaluated by others.

Interpersonal relationships are actually a kind of interaction based on mutual respect. Therefore, during the interaction process, you must pay attention to etiquette, which is a sign of respecting others. We all want to be acknowledged and affirmed by others. This is a natural human thought. If you want to gain others' affirmation, you must have some conditions to exchange for this affirmation. If you don't have such conditions, how can others affirm you? You leave others no way in, so naturally they won't take this path. Do you admit this point? Therefore, we must strive to create such conditions and give ourselves this capital.

2. Don't be stingy about satisfying others' desire for importance.

This second point that the author wants to say is mutual benefit; doing business emphasizes win-win, and interpersonal relationships are naturally also this kind of transaction. In this world, it's not just you who wants to be recognized and affirmed by others. The people around you are also the same. So, don't be stingy with your words of praise and your articulate mouth. Say more affirming words, and you'll find that handling interpersonal relationships is actually quite easy. It's not that hard to deal with. So, don't be stingy about satisfying others' desire for importance. Try to fulfill their wishes.

3. During conversations, avoid joking with others or mocking them.

When you've achieved the previous two points, it can be said that you've used your speech art to drive them, and they will naturally become your good friends. This way, your interpersonal relationships will naturally be established. Then, what comes next? We've built the house, and now the next thing is to maintain it well. Interpersonal relationships are maintained by people. If you don't maintain it or maintain it poorly, you'll lose it. Isn't there a saying that it's easy to conquer but hard to hold onto power? So, the latter is truly what requires full effort. For example, during conversations, avoid joking with others or mocking them. Even if you're the best of friends, try not to do this.
