Work more efficiently with vertical search engines

by woyaodiglink on 2008-08-14 18:17:05

Search engine marketing, search engine optimization, and website enhancement can all help you achieve better returns on your B2B marketing budget. The reason is that search engines are the primary source of information for your potential and existing customers when they are looking for products, services, and companies.

Although this trend has become increasingly evident in recent years, internet searches continue to grow rapidly. Everyone is familiar with Google, Yahoo, and MSN, but for specific industries and markets, vertical search engines are more applicable and more profitable for target users and advertisers within their target groups.

Lawyers prefer Westlaw, IT buyers go to Knowledge Storm, and engineers and experts in the engineering and technical community favor GlobalSpec. There are also vertical search engines for finance and healthcare, as well as image, blog, and specialized content search engines. Vertical search engines continue to attract more attention due to their many benefits.

Why are there more and more vertical search engines?

Several reasons contribute to the growth of vertical search:

Website visitors are categorized according to different types of information needs. This does not only happen on the internet; any form of media develops and matures in such a way. Remember, television was once dominated by the three major American networks. Today, cable channels focus more on categorizing programs – from golf, gardening, food to finance. As vertical search engines grow, the same thing will happen to the internet.

General search engines often provide search results without knowing what users are searching for, which means that irrelevant results are likely to appear. A classic example is when an engineer searches for the keyword "pump" on a general search engine and ends up finding women's shoes. On the other hand, searching for "pump" on GlobalSpec will only yield results related to technology and engineering.

Vertical search provides deep, highly filtered, and intelligent access to more specialized markets. Companies that offer vertical search engines have the expertise to meet customer information needs and content, which other search engines cannot do, such as properly filtering content and presenting the best and most relevant search results to users. This is why users like vertical search engines.

Vertical search aggregates target audiences

For a manufacturer or supplier, appearing on a vertical search engine like GlobalSpec helps you aggregate your target audience. You don't need to use general search engines to extract your target population from a large group, which is both costly and prone to errors.

The 2006 GlobalSpec Engineering Trends Report shows that 79% of survey participants use two or more search engines for work-related purposes. 51% consider Globalspec one of the top three search engines they use most frequently.

As a marketer, aggregating your target audience simplifies your job. Your ads will only be seen by purchasing engineers and other technical experts. There’s no need to waste resources on unqualified browsers.

Vertical search offers more than just clicks

Another significant advantage of vertical search engines is that they offer more than just clicks, like Globalspec does. How many advertisers spend a lot of money on click-throughs from general search engines to their own websites, only to get very few actual inquiries? Such a situation is pitiable.

GlobalSpec provides a different approach because it is more than just a search engine. Many suppliers advertise on GlobalSpec and use "Supplier Center Pages" to launch their marketing campaigns, including company descriptions, comprehensive searchable product and service directories, white paper links, technical articles, etc. Single-click ads, banner ads, product bulletins, e-newsletters, and other strategies around online theme promotions directly reach your technical community.

Since GlobalSpec users register online, when they browse supplier information, the supplier receives complete contact information, company name, and categories of interest. This model not only generates quality inquiries but also saves costs and burdens for suppliers and manufacturers.

Vertical search is part of the marketing mix

Marketing work is often compared to a game rule: "Where there are fish, where to fish" — and use the right bait. This analogy perfectly applies to search engines. General search engines are like vast oceans, where every imaginable type of fish swims. Vertical search engines are like small ponds, where only one type of fish may swim.

The type of fish you want to catch exists in both the sea and the pond, but the easiest way to catch them is in the pond. Why? Because only this type of fish exists in the pond, and the bait you use — technical information prepared for the engineering community — is exactly what the fish wants to eat.

This simple analogy is closely related to your marketing mix. You might include general search engines as part of your marketing mix, catching some of the fish you want — and then throwing back those that aren’t really what you want. Therefore, you would rather perform well on GlobalSpec: GlobalSpec is the home of engineers and technical experts who really want technical information.
