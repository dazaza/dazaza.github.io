10 Must-Do Plans for Website Construction

by dang000 on 2009-04-29 00:40:25

I. Market Analysis Before Website Construction

1. What is the market like for related industries? What are its characteristics? Is it possible to conduct company business on the internet?

2. Main market competitor analysis, including their online presence and website planning, functions and roles.

3. Company self-condition analysis, company overview, market advantages. In what ways can a website enhance competitiveness? Capability of constructing a website (costs, technology, human resources, etc.).

II. Purpose and Function Positioning of Website Construction

1. Why build a website? Is it for product promotion, e-commerce, or building an industry-specific website? Is it a necessity for the company or an extension of market expansion?

2. Integrate company resources to define website functions. Based on the company's needs and plans, determine the website's function: product promotional type, online marketing type, customer service type, e-commerce type, etc.

3. Based on the website's function, define the purpose and role that the website should achieve.

4. The construction status of the company’s internal network (Intranet) and the scalability of the website.

III. Website Technology Solution

Based on the website's function, determine the technical solution for the website.

1. Should we use a self-built server or rent a virtual host?

2. Choose an operating system: Unix, Linux, or Windows 2000/NT. Analyze input costs, functionality, development, stability, and security.

3. Adopt systematic solutions (such as those provided by IBM, HP) for corporate internet access plans, e-commerce solutions, or develop our own?

4. Website security measures, anti-hacking, and anti-virus solutions.

5. Development of relevant programs such as web page programs (ASP, JSP, CGI), database programs, etc.

IV. Website Content Planning

1. Plan website content based on the website's purpose and function. A general enterprise website should include basic content such as company profiles, product introductions, service descriptions, price information, contact details, and online orders.

2. E-commerce websites should provide member registration, detailed product/service information, search queries, order confirmation, payment options, personal information confidentiality measures, and relevant help.

3. If the website has many sections, consider assigning specific personnel to manage the relevant content. Note: Website content is the most important factor in attracting visitors. Uninformative or impractical information will not attract hurried visitors. You can conduct surveys on the types of information people want to read before launching the site, and after launch, survey visitor satisfaction with the website content to adjust accordingly.

V. Webpage Design

1. Artistic design requirements for webpages. Generally, webpage artistic design should be consistent with the overall image of the enterprise and comply with CI standards. Pay attention to the use of webpage colors, images, and layout planning to maintain the overall consistency of the webpage.

2. When adopting new technologies, consider factors such as the geographic distribution of the target audience, age groups, network speed, and reading habits.

3. Develop a webpage revision plan, such as making large-scale revisions every six months to one year.

VI. Website Maintenance

1. Maintenance of servers and related software/hardware. Evaluate potential issues and establish response times.

2. Database maintenance. Effectively utilizing data is an important aspect of website maintenance, so database maintenance should be given due importance.

3. Content updates and adjustments.

4. Establish relevant website maintenance regulations to institutionalize and standardize website maintenance.

VII. Website Testing

Before publishing the website, conduct thorough testing to ensure normal browsing and usage. Main test contents:

1. Server stability and security.

2. Program and database testing.

3. Webpage compatibility testing (e.g., browsers, displays).

4. Other tests based on needs.

VIII. Website Release and Promotion

1. Public relations and advertising activities after website testing before release.

2. Search engine registration, etc.

IX. Website Construction Schedule

Start and completion times for various planning tasks, responsible persons, etc.

X. Cost Details

The above ten points may not be comprehensive or perfect, but since I've spent quite some time writing them, everyone should take a good look at them.
