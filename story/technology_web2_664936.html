How to check baby's vision

by anonymous on 2012-02-14 09:57:39

Check method 1: Observe eye movement. Check if the baby's eyes tremble when moving, and if they do, such as rapid side-to-side shaking, it is very likely that there is a visual impairment.

Check method 2: Observe the ability of eyes to track objects. Place a red wool ball with a diameter of 10 centimeters 15 centimeters away from the baby's eyes. Babies at one and a half months can follow the red wool ball from right to left or from left to right to the middle line; four-month-old babies can follow the red wool ball from right to left or from left to right for 180 degrees.

Check method 3: Observe if responsive smiles appear. For two-month-old babies, when someone faces them and plays with them (without making sounds or touching the baby), they will show responsive smiles.

Check method 4: Observe the ability of eyes to focus on objects. When the baby is four and a half months old, both eyes can focus on colored small pills placed on the table, such as sugar pills or small balls.

Warm reminder: If the baby does not meet the requirements of the above checks by four and a half months, it may have a visual impairment and needs to go to the ophthalmology department for further examination as soon as possible.
