Her name is Meng Lingjie, from Luohe, Henan Province. She is 22 years old and is a clever, obedient, considerate, and filial girl who loves life even though her family is not wealthy. However, misfortune quietly befell this girl in full bloom in August 2011. She gradually felt a persistent low-grade fever, her body became increasingly swollen day by day, she was completely drained of energy, and normal activities became more and more difficult. After visiting multiple hospitals, she was finally diagnosed with lupus nephritis (a severe lifelong disease ranked among the world's most serious illnesses, where patients endure long-term suffering due to appearance changes, economic burdens, and symptoms).

In just six months, she was transferred between several hospitals including Luohe City First People's Hospital, Zhumadian Emergency Center, and twice admitted and discharged from Zhengzhou University First Affiliated Hospital. Escaping death once, she now finds herself hospitalized again. All of her family’s savings have been spent, further worsening their already indebted situation. She says it feels like there's a deep pit ahead; despite knowing it might not help, she has no choice but to move forward. In truth, she wishes to find a quiet place to lie down, and thus refuses treatment. Thinking about her hardworking and silent parents and her younger brother still in elementary school, she says she feels like a burden dragging everyone down financially, so while she is reluctant to let go, she also considers giving up...

Her family is already struggling. Her father suffered a third-degree disability after an accident in April 1999, causing a comminuted fracture in his right leg. He fell from a building in June 2005, fracturing again, and since then, he can only do odd jobs at home. Her mother is an honest farmer with no education, too old to work in factories or as a migrant worker. Her younger brother struggles to pay for schooling fees and is only eleven years old, facing the risk of dropping out. Seeing her 81-year-old grandmother and 89-year-old grandfather, she laments disrupting their peaceful twilight years...

This good-hearted and understanding girl, so young, has endured nearly half a year under the pressures of her family's financial difficulties, fluctuating health conditions, and uncertain future. Yet, she says she wants to live and still has many things left undone...
