The pre-opening of Shanghai's second Apple store —— the Hong Kong Plaza store

by diglog on 2010-09-23 11:38:34

The second Apple store in Shanghai — the Hong Kong Plaza store on Huaihai Road in Puxi — will officially open this Saturday, once again located near luxury brands like LV, COACH, and TIFFANY. Coinciding with the China launch of the iPhone 4, the store opening has been moved up to 8 a.m. — so those planning to line up should start early.

Apple4.us will bring you the latest news from the new store's media day, giving you a first glimpse inside the Hong Kong Plaza store.

First, here are some photos of the inside and outside of the store. Although the total area of the Hong Kong Plaza store is 800 square meters, smaller than the 1000 square meters of the IFC Mall store, it may be due to better lighting and higher ceilings that the entire store appears larger than the IFC Mall location.

There’s still no checkout counter; payments are handled via an iPod Touch equipped with a credit card reader and barcode scanner, with electronic receipts sent directly to your email.

In addition to consultations, the "Genius Bar" also offers free services to transfer data from your old Apple devices to your new ones.

The spherical stools are comfortable to sit on but aren’t for sale.

An Apple store is undoubtedly a great place for free Wi-Fi access; strong Wi-Fi signals can be picked up even outside the store, and the in-store wireless network speed is quite fast. We tried downloading a 15MB app, which only took three minutes — hopefully, there won't be any repeat of the tragic Wi-Fi connectivity issues experienced at the IFC Mall opening.

Ron Johnson, Apple's Senior Vice President of Retail, gave a speech — one of the likely recruitment requirements for Apple executives is excellent public speaking skills. He said that the IFC Mall store is the busiest Apple store in the world (considering the overwhelming foot traffic in Lujiazui, this is undeniable), but when asked about the sales performance of the IFC Mall store, he only said it was “very good” and refused to disclose specific numbers — what we all know is that the free phone calls placed from the store have put a significant strain on Apple's phone bill, leading to the closure of free international long-distance calling.

Regarding everyone's most anticipated product, the iPhone 4, Ron Johnson said, “On the opening day, there will be ample supplies of both the iPhone 4 and iPads.” The price is well-known: ¥4999 (unlocked model) — only about ¥600 more expensive than in Hong Kong, but without the long wait after placing an order, allowing immediate possession. We predict that this price will not only attract a large number of Apple fans eager to purchase, but also a fair share of scalpers who might resell unlocked units to Japan or even the U.S., where only locked models are available. Apple did not reveal how many units each person or credit card will be limited to purchasing on opening day, presumably depending on the enthusiasm of the crowd.

This is the exclusive PRODUCT (RED) edition of the sixth-generation iPod Nano, available only at Apple stores.

As for the iPod lineup, the Shuffle doesn't offer much innovation, but the new Nano boasts excellent build quality. Its screen is very detailed, with a resolution that, while not reaching "retina" levels, is approximately double that of the iPhone 3GS. It can also function as a pedometer and calorie counter without needing the NIKE+ sports kit. The clip on the back is non-removable, just like on the Shuffle.
