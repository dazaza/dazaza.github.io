Google Blackboard - The official release of Google Toolbar 3.0 for Firefox is available

by kunshou on 2007-02-17 02:45:02

Recently, Google brought some good news to Chinese users of Firefox. The Google Toolbar has been upgraded! The official Chinese version 3.0 has been released! Now, in addition to the functions of the Google IE Toolbar, the Firefox toolbar has a new feature - it integrates the newly released Chinese version of Google Docs. Users do not need to open the Google Docs interface specifically to use this service directly.

Below, I will not elaborate on the search function and will mainly introduce other functions beyond the search.

Custom Buttons

Custom buttons allow users to add their favorite websites to the toolbar. They can then access, search for, and obtain the latest RSS/Atom feeds from these sites directly through the toolbar. For example, people who are enthusiastic about news can customize news website links and browse the latest headlines directly from the toolbar.

Online Bookmarks

On the new version of the Google Toolbar, users can create bookmarks for their favorite websites and categorize them with simple operations. This is designed for users' convenience when using different computers, as the bookmarks are saved in the user's Google account. A user who customizes an interesting article at work can also read it on their home computer.

In addition to Simplified Chinese, the official version 3.0 of the Google Toolbar for Firefox supports another 25 languages. The latest version of the Google Toolbar supports Windows XP/2000 SP3+, Mac OS X 10.2+ or Linux. In the coming weeks, existing users' versions will be automatically upgraded. New users can download it from the following address:

http://www.google.com/tools/firefox/toolbar/FT3/intl/zh-CN/install.html
