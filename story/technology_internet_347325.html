Virtual reality technology is a technical system that uses computers and other technologies to simulate the real world. With the application of virtual reality technology, people can conveniently carry out research work that is difficult to perform in real life due to reasons of safety or economy. In civil aviation safety management, virtual reality technology has also been widely applied. This article summarizes the existing applications of virtual reality technology in civil aviation, analyzes the gaps in civil aviation operations where virtual reality technology has not yet been applied, and proposes the concept and application of a digitalized civil aviation operation system focusing on system operation research.

Overview of Virtual Technology

Virtual reality technology is the technique of mapping the real world into a virtual one. As humanity's understanding of the objective world deepens and new technologies continue to emerge, the connotation of virtual reality technology continues to expand. Currently, commonly used developments include the latest results of various technologies such as computer graphics (CG: Computer Graphic), computer simulation technology, artificial intelligence, sensor technology, display technology, network parallel processing, etc., like Daikin compressors. In fact, virtual reality technology is an integration of a series of new technologies centered around computer technology.

Although there is no unified understanding of what virtual reality is, many scholars agree that virtual reality has the characteristics of immersion (Immersion), interactivity (Interactivity), and imagination (Imagination), abbreviated as so-called immersion, also known as presence. This refers to the degree to which users feel they exist as the main character in the simulated environment. An ideal simulated environment allows users to fully immerse themselves in the three-dimensional virtual environment created by the computer, feeling as if they are in the real world. Interactivity refers to the level of operability users have over objects within the simulated environment and the naturalness of feedback received from the environment (including real-time response). Imagination emphasizes that virtual reality technology should possess a broad imaginative space, capable of expanding human cognitive scope. It can not only reproduce environments that truly exist but also imagine scenarios that do not objectively exist or are even impossible.

Due to the above characteristics of virtual reality and the fact that virtual reality technology uses computer technology to simulate known objective laws in the real world, it has been widely applied in various fields. The most common application is undoubtedly in games with realistic scenes, where players can live lives in the virtual environment that they yearn for but cannot achieve in real life, providing a physical and mental experience that is both close to reality and transcends it. Besides entertainment, virtual reality technology also performs outstandingly in planning, design, and personnel training, and there are also many applications in civil aviation safety management.

Application of Virtual Reality Technology in Civil Aviation Safety Management

Safety is the lifeline of the civil aviation transportation industry. The application of virtual reality technology in the civil aviation industry, from aircraft design, airport emergency rescue drills, accident simulation reproduction, accident precursor investigation simulation, to the education, training, and exercises of various operational personnel (pilots, maintenance personnel, and air traffic controllers), not only significantly improves technical levels but also saves funds and greatly enhances operational safety levels. From domestic and international references, the application of virtual reality technology in the civil aviation industry mainly includes the following aspects:

(I) Personnel Training

Virtual reality technology is widely used in the training of air traffic controllers, pilots, and maintenance personnel. Taking the CDZS airport tower view simulator, DRS air traffic control radar simulator, and DPS procedural control simulator developed by a software company in China as examples, these systems are large-scale virtual reality systems designed for training airport tower controllers. They can realistically recreate the tower control working environment with a 360-degree open airport and nearby airspace views, realistic aircraft taxiing, takeoff, landing, various weather conditions such as rain, snow, fog, and night flights. Through pre-designed busy and dangerous situations, they provide systematic training for tower controllers and evaluate training results while supporting multiple training combination methods. These systems are important tools and standard equipment for training air traffic controllers. The application of virtual reality technology more realistically simulates the complex, high-traffic operating environment of airports, providing high-quality, high-definition visuals with good immersion, thus achieving better training results.

Simulators are comprehensive applications of various virtual reality technologies. Supported by high-performance computer-based virtual environment processors, two-dimensional or three-dimensional display technologies create realistic virtual environments. Various sensors sense user control actions or body positions as inputs, calculate corresponding responses, and output feedback to users. Using simulators can simulate rare hazardous situations in real life, allowing trainees to repeatedly operate until proficient without facing any actual danger, avoiding training accidents, improving training levels, and occupying an extremely important position in the training of pilots, maintenance personnel, and controllers. Since 2006, with the appearance of domestically produced flight trainers in China, the original blind spots and situations dominated by Airbus and Boeing have been overcome.

For the training of maintenance personnel, simulators can also be used. For example, engine testing is a high-tech, high-risk task. Using a flight simulator instead of an actual aircraft for engine testing training, maintenance personnel can familiarize themselves with relevant procedures and steps of engine testing on one hand, and effectively reduce aircraft costs on the other hand. More importantly, it avoids all risks caused by misoperation.

(II) Computer-Aided Design

Aircraft design is costly and time-consuming. By adopting virtual reality technology, design drawings can be transformed into three-dimensional models and assembled like physical objects. Designers can observe the shapes, internal structures, and layout effects of aircraft and their components as if they were present in person. They can also conduct computer-simulated tests for strength, stiffness, vibration, wind tunnels, etc., promptly identifying problems and making timely design modifications. This avoids the waste of substantial time and funds required for producing test pieces and improves safety.

(III) Safety Management

The U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA, National Aviation Space agency) launched the Aviation Safety Program (ASP: Aviation Safety Program), which extensively utilizes virtual reality technology. This program models and monitors the aviation system, providing real-time detailed data to managers to support decision-making, issuing accident warnings, thereby achieving the goal of preventing accidents. Specific measures include enhancing training through virtual reality training equipment to avoid accidents caused by human factors; detecting adverse weather and providing pilots with timely, accurate weather information to strengthen situational awareness under low visibility and avoid accidents; providing pilots with synthetic views displaying ground terrain and targets, air traffic control status, landing approach patterns, and runway surface conditions, improving landing safety.

Using virtual reality technology to build a digital civil aviation operation system

With the rapid development of China's civil aviation industry, various advanced technologies have been introduced successively. The various training equipment mentioned above has been applied in China with good results. However, from the perspective of application scope, they are all single-item partial applications that fail to reflect the highly systematic nature of civil aviation operations. The application of virtual reality technology in China's civil aviation operation safety management is still insufficient, leaving considerable room for expansion. Starting from the systematic nature of civil aviation operations, this article proposes the concept of using virtual reality technology to build a digital civil aviation operation system.
