Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI (I recommend this version, which I like very much)

by youxiruanjian88 on 2009-03-04 09:25:46

[Basic Introduction]

The "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI", released in 1998, is already a classic for today's players. However, many domestic players started to get in touch with the real "Romance of the Three Kingdoms" series from this version. The game transitioned from the DOS platform to the Windows platform, and the graphics reached a height of 16 colors. The concepts of field battles and city battles were restored in the game, and functions such as the chronology of heroes and hero memories were added. Its unique "Heaven (cooperate with the Son of Heaven to command the feudal lords), Earth (three-day plan combat mode), People (idealistic and distinctive generals)" system laid an important foundation for the subsequent changes in the series. In early 1999, the enhanced version of this generation was released, adding a short scenario mode. The game has no outstanding performance in terms of graphics, maps, character drawings, and music, but it has great significance for this series. It can be said that it is a milestone exploration of the Three Kingdoms theme games by Koei.

Since the release of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms V" in 1996, after more than two years of waiting, Koei's new work "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" finally unveiled its veil and appeared before our eyes. This "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" transitioned from the DOS platform to the Windows platform, so the characters and portraits in the game were redrawn. However, I feel that the character portraits and maps in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" are not particularly commendable, except for the graphics reaching a height of 16-bit color, there is nothing else worth mentioning. On the contrary, the music in this game is very wonderful, reflecting Koei's consistent exquisite and perfect production style, and can be considered the best background music in the history of Romance of the Three Kingdoms series. I still vividly remember some beautiful pieces in it, such as "Waves Wash Away Heroes" and "General's Triumph".

In "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI", the concepts of field battles and city battles were restored. Moreover, in order to reflect the historical atmosphere of this work, in addition to the traditional general biographies of the fourth and fifth generations, functions such as the chronology of heroes and hero memories were added. Through the chronology of heroes, players can review the orders they have given and the events they have experienced. And the function of hero nostalgia allows you to reminisce about past events and feel sentimental after completing the game. The more historically accurate chronology function of the sixth generation has been adopted in subsequent generations, just with different names and forms. In terms of internal politics, "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" is even simpler than the fifth generation. The ruler does not have to do everything personally; just delegate the trivial matters to capable subordinates. It can be considered the generation in this series that pays the least attention to internal politics.

The biggest difference between "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" and previous generations lies in its unique "Heaven, Earth, People" system. This system originates from the ancient Chinese saying "heavenly timing, geographical advantage, and harmonious people," and the grasp of the entire game also depends on the effective coordination and operation among these three elements. This system enabled the sixth generation to completely break away from the framework of previous generations, thus laying the foundation for the comprehensive changes in the seventh and eighth generations. Below is a brief description of these three elements in the sixth generation:

Heaven - "Cooperate with the Son of Heaven to command the feudal lords"

In the sixth generation, Emperor Xian is no longer just a dispensable decoration. Only the emperor can grant players higher official positions or noble titles. Only by getting closer to the emperor can players obtain more honors, thereby indirectly increasing the number of troops they can lead and enhancing their prestige. For example, the highest-ranking general can lead 20,000 troops, while if the ruler himself has no title, his subordinates can only lead up to 5,000 troops at most. The key to expanding power in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" lies in how to control Emperor Xian as soon as possible, so Emperor Xian naturally became the "money-making Bodhisattva" that everyone wants to grab, which is what is called "cooperating with the Son of Heaven to command the feudal lords."

Earth - "Three-day planning combat mode"

The sixth generation abolished the army formation mode of the fifth generation, retaining only the characteristics of the troop types. Of course, due to the high defensive ability of soldiers in this game, both city siege battles and defense battles take a long time. The semi-real-time combat mode was introduced for the first time in the city siege battles. Players issue orders every turn, which last for three days. Once the orders are issued, the computer will automatically carry out the player's orders within the next three days. Perhaps Koei did this to save players time and effort, but the downside is that sometimes players can only helplessly watch the computer-controlled armies fall into ambushes or traps within three days, unable to do anything about it. Personally, I am not very interested in this pre-deployment and post-execution combat mode. The troop design in the sixth generation is very distinctive, featuring ethnic minority armies like the "Southern Barbarian Troops" and "Mountain Cavalry" and six ethnic minority rulers. The various troop types have mutual advantages and disadvantages, combined with terrain restrictions, making the battles changeable in many ways. Just like the fifth generation, the importance of military provisions in this generation cannot be overstated. Once the military provisions are seized in the war, it will cause a drop in morale, soldiers deserting, and inevitable defeat. Notably, the duels in the sixth generation feature sketched outlines of generals, where every move in the duel animation shines brilliantly, appearing realistic and powerful. The duels in the sixth generation can be fully controlled by the players, and I believe that the general duels in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" are unparalleled in the series.

People - "Generals with ideals and personalities"

The generals in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" have personalities and ideals, and loyalty is not easily improved by simple rewards. Instead, it depends on the ruler's actions and compatibility, as well as the differences in ideals. If a cruel ruler has some generals who highly value righteousness under him, they will leave him soon; conversely, if an extremely ambitious general encounters a weak ruler, then internal strife in that country is inevitable. Besides generals, soldiers no longer have training values but are measured by morale, and armies with zero morale will automatically disintegrate. The humanization of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" is also reflected in the following aspects: when there is activity of enemy generals around the territory of a country with a strategist, a military meeting will automatically be convened to discuss countermeasures. Moreover, anyone can be a strategist in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI". If the enemy plans to attack our territory, intelligent strategists will obtain intelligence before the enemy attacks and notify the ruler to take measures; while less intelligent ones may only wake up when the enemy is already at the city gates. This "foresight" setting is unique to the sixth generation in the Romance of the Three Kingdoms series, and it is very interesting.

The general orders in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" are simultaneously issued to all generals, so when executing them, the synchronized pace of friendly and enemy generals leads to many new changes. For example, the situation where the enemy occupies an empty city earlier than us can occur, prompting a meeting to decide whether the attacking force should continue advancing; when our forces are on expedition, it is possible that the rear is attacked, at which point we can order the expeditionary forces to return for defense. Sometimes generals may disobey the ruler's orders due to impulsive or cowardly personalities. These humanized features have been adopted and improved upon in the subsequent ninth generation.

At the beginning of 1999, the enhanced version of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" was released. However, I vaguely remember that the enhanced version only added a short scenario mode, allowing players to choose famous figures from the Three Kingdoms period to experience many classic battles. This mode that bypasses strategy and directly enters combat also led to the "Sharpening History Tales Chapter" in the enhanced version of "Romance of the Three Kingdoms IX".

The successful application of the "Heaven, Earth, People" system in "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" shows Koei's continuous exploration and pursuit of change in the traditional style of the Romance of the Three Kingdoms series. However, no matter how good something is, after trying it six times, it will eventually become tasteless. Thus, Koei continues to strive for new breakthroughs, and this "Romance of the Three Kingdoms VI" is their first attempt at breaking through.
