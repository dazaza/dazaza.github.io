Synopsis:

Wolverine's real name is James Howlett, born into a wealthy family in Canada at the end of the 19th century. Though frail since birth, he possessed an extraordinary physique and came from a dysfunctional family, making him introverted and solitary. He lived a tranquil but dull life until one evening when the gardener Logan killed his father. This tragedy awakened James' peculiar abilities, causing bone claws to emerge from his body. Young James used these "bone claws" to kill Logan. Unexpectedly, Logan was actually James' biological father, revealing Wolverine's origins just before his death.

James and his brother Victor fled afterward. They participated in multiple wars, from World War I all the way to the Vietnam War. Later, they were recruited by Colonel Striker for special operations. Tired of killing, Wolverine left the organization and worked as a lumberjack in a Canadian forest, renaming himself Logan. During his escape, Logan both feared and despised his innate superpowers, leading to self-abandonment. Until one day, he met and fell in love with Kayla, an indigenous woman known as Silver Fox. After Kayla was killed by his brother Victor (Saber-toothed Tiger), an enraged Wolverine agreed to undergo mutant experiments under Colonel Striker's conditions.

Logan renamed himself "Wolverine," and his bones were transformed into indestructible material, turning his natural bone claws into adamantium claws. Wolverine was brainwashed by the "X Weapon" organization but broke free after killing its members. Saber-toothed Tiger then pursued him, leading to a fierce battle where Wolverine narrowly triumphed. Wolverine later met Professor X, who took him in, beginning his X-Men career.

In 1845, in a Canadian estate, young Wolverine was a six or seven-year-old boy named James, weak and sickly. His loving father called him Jimmy. Tragedy struck one evening when the gardener Logan killed his father, awakening James' strange powers. Bone claws emerged from his body, and he used them to kill Logan. Before dying, Logan told James, "You are my son." James' mother viewed him as a monster, and that night, he ran away from home with his half-brother Victor, who would later become Saber-toothed Tiger.

He changed his name to Logan and together with Victor experienced countless wars, including the Civil War, World War I, World War II, and the Vietnam War. Two immortal beings eventually encountered Colonel Striker and joined his secret military organization composed of mutants, including Wolverine Logan, Saber-toothed Tiger Victor, Deadpool Wade—a highly skilled swordsman mercenary—Agent Zero—a deadly sniper—Shadow—a teleporter—Meatball—a being capable of creating gravitational fields—Brady—who could freely manipulate electricity. In various missions, Victor's beastly nature became evident, while Logan refused to be a killing machine and chose to leave.

He became a regular lumberjack, living quietly with his girlfriend Kayla in the Rocky Mountains. Kayla told Logan a story about the moon goddess: "Why is the moon lonely? Because she once had a lover but can never touch him again." In legend, there was a moon goddess who had a boyfriend named Krclraz. Every night, they walked together under the sky gazing at the horizon. However, their happiness didn't last long. Another sprite envied them and wanted to take the moon goddess away from Krclraz. One day, the sprite told Krclraz that the moon goddess wanted flowers, teaching him to descend from heaven to pick wild roses, symbolizing love and fidelity—"loyal love, till death do us part." Believing the sprite's words, Krclraz tried to secretly descend from heaven to find wild roses without realizing that once he left, he could never return. Thus, every night when you look up at the stars, you see the moon crying alone, calling out his name. But the gates to heaven have closed forever, separating them eternally. He is Krclraz, meaning Wolverine.

Peaceful life was shattered when Colonel Striker implemented the "X Weapon Plan." To make Logan voluntarily accept physical transformation, he staged Kayla's murder by Victor. For revenge, Logan underwent risky Adamantium infusion surgery, naming himself "Wolverine" before the procedure. After learning he was exploited and designated as weapon number ten with plans to erase his memory, Wolverine escaped in rage. Unable to control Wolverine, Colonel Striker decided to eliminate him. A naked Logan sought refuge in a kind elderly couple's barn, receiving warmth, dinner, and shelter for the night, only to have them cruelly murdered by Agent Zero sent by Striker. An enraged Wolverine destroyed two military Hummers and an armed helicopter, leaving Agent Zero to perish in the explosion.

Now Wolverine possessed superior healing abilities, indestructible steel claws, and immense combat strength. Foremost on his mind was avenging Kayla by killing Saber-toothed Tiger. After twists and turns, he arrived at Three Mile Island, finding Kayla alive. It turned out everything was Colonel Striker's conspiracy. He captured various functional mutants, including Kayla's sister, imprisoning them on Three Mile Island to extract their powers and create the powerful weapon eleven, which he aimed to control. Kayla, also a mutant who could control thoughts at close range, saved her sister under Striker's control. Wolverine and Saber-toothed Tiger clashed again, with Saber-toothed Tiger defeated. As Wolverine raised his steel claws to pierce his throat, Kayla's voice echoed, "Logan, you're not a beast." Indeed, like the kind old man in the barn who told revenge-driven Logan, "You have other choices," and like Kayla comforting nightmare-plagued Logan in the Rocky Mountains saying, "Remember, you're not a beast," Wolverine retracted his steel claws and his murderous intent.

Logan and Kayla freed the mutants imprisoned by Striker. Striker resorted to his ultimate weapon—weapon eleven, but his haste led to using it before completion. Deadpool Wade, after Striker's modifications, fused fire eyes, instant teleportation, and automatic healing powers of multiple mutants, nearly defeating Wolverine in a nuclear power station tower. At a critical moment, Saber-toothed Tiger intervened, extending a helping hand to Wolverine, saying, "No one but me can kill you." Victor, who always refused to lose to his younger brother, was also lonely. Together, the brothers defeated weapon eleven.

Meanwhile, Kayla leading the children to escape was fatally shot. Logan, holding the cold corpse of Kayla, ultimately couldn't leave with his love as Colonel Striker hit him in the head with an Adamantium bullet, causing him to lose his memory. Faced with Kayla's icy remains, Logan forgot his love. But his emotions were sealed along with Kayla's death, burying a sorrowful, lost heart beneath his violent exterior. Perhaps he no longer remembered Kayla, but her words—"Logan, you're not a beast"—were forever etched in his heart.

Behind-the-scenes production:

Unveiling Wolverine's origin story

As the first prequel to the "X-Men" series, this is not only the fourth film in the "Wolverine" series but also possibly the last in the X-Men franchise. "Wolverine" maintains the balance between the supernatural and reality seen in previous X-Men films while highlighting emotional relationships among characters. The entire film reveals Wolverine's mysterious past, including the darkest and most romantically tragic elements. It details Wolverine's birth, how he gained astonishing healing abilities and unbreakable steel claws, becoming the most formidable fighting machine. Notably, the film also unveils Wolverine's tragic love story with Silver Fox, enriching the entire "Wolverine" series.

Besides Wolverine, the movie features a group of mutants, some eagerly anticipated by global fans, making their first appearance on the big screen. Audiences will witness for the first time this secretive military organization entirely composed of mutants, the X-Force. Members include Wolverine; his brother Victor Creed, nicknamed Saber-toothed Tiger, ferocious and possessing unimaginable strength; Wade Wilson, later known as Deadpool, a highly skilled swordsman mercenary; Agent Zero, a deadly sniper; Shadow, a ghost-like teleporter; Blob, resembling an exceptionally large and strong beast; and Brady, who can freely manipulate electricity. Their leader, Colonel William Striker, appeared in "X-Men 2," but this installment fully explains his background and motivations. The complex relationship between Striker and Wolverine allows audiences to better understand Logan's past and future.

A fresh and powerful cast

Hugh Jackman, the lead actor, has recently soared in popularity, ranked as the world's sexiest man, and hosted the 2009 Academy Awards. Hugh Jackman's portrayal of Wolverine, a formidable fighter with unbreakable steel claws and a fierce temper, is perfectly suited. The film not only continues his signature ruggedly handsome appeal but also solidifies his status as a Hollywood superstar.

Hugh Jackman feels that reprising this role allows fans to gain deeper insights into Wolverine from the first three films. Jackman believes playing Wolverine in this film is more interesting than ever. This isn't just due to his love for the character and the entire X-Men universe but because he also served as a producer on the film. Collaborating closely with director Gavin Hood, Jackman paid meticulous attention to every detail of the production process, thoroughly enjoying this new role. "We'll get a glimpse into Logan's past and his internal war with himself, seeing his journey along the way," Jackman said. Lauren Shuler Donner, producer of the first three X-Men films, added, "Wolverine has some unique qualities. He has a bit of a bad-boy attitude. Logan is very individualistic, with his own style and sense of humor. His nonchalant attitude towards things is loved by fans."

Simultaneously, the rest of the cast enhances the film's appeal. Among them is Dominic Monaghan, well-known for his outstanding performances in the three "Lord of the Rings" films. Additionally, Korean actor Daniel Henney, familiar to domestic audiences for his excellent performance in "My Name is Kim Sam Soon," was boldly cast. Originally, in the comic, "Agent Zero" was a special agent born in Germany, but the director chose a Korean actor, proving that his handsome image indeed adds color to the film.

First-class audiovisual effects

"Wolverine" not only features gripping action adventures but also retains the complex and intense visual impact characteristic of the X-Men series. Director Gavin Hood demanded that the film be visually stunning and the action sequences breathtaking.

Beyond cooler action scenes and more new characters, the production team decided to shoot this new X-Men film in new locations. The first three X-Men films were shot in Toronto, the second and third mostly in Vancouver, whereas "Wolverine" was largely filmed in New Zealand and Australia, with only a few continuous shots completed in Vancouver. "Finding a new location for the entire movie was one of my challenges," Jackman said. "Despite the great styles of the first three works, I wanted this episode to look different, grander. Nowhere resembles a utopia more than New Zealand, full of magic, making the movie appear more realistic." Producer John Palermo added, "Shooting in Australia and New Zealand expands the X-Men world, and audiences will surely enjoy it. We were lucky to shoot in such beautiful places."

Jackman, director Gavin Hood, and cinematographer Donald McAlpine utilized various locations and scenes in New Zealand and Australia to replicate five battles: New Orleans City, American high schools, and remote Canadian wilderness. Donald believed the scenic beauty of New Zealand is priceless, especially for continuous scenes like the fictional Canadian scene Lake Arcadia and the South Island of New Zealand, the utopia where Logan and Silver Fox fell in love. The South Island was also the location for one of the film's major action scenes, where the helicopter chase and crash against a magnificent backdrop created a very realistic effect.

Trivia:

• "Wolverine" is set to premiere in mainland China on May 3, meaning that after "Spider-Man 3" in 2007 and "Iron Man" in 2008, it will be the third consecutive year importing Hollywood superhero blockbusters during the "May Day" holiday season.

• Gavin Hood explained why he directed "Wolverine": "Part of the reason is to prepare for my daughter's tuition... Actually, I'm not really cut out to direct 'Wolverine'..."

• The script for the film was completed in October 2006 but waited a year due to Jackman's schedule.

• Zack Snyder and Bryan Singer both declined to direct the film. Snyder was busy preparing his "Watchmen," and Singer was occupied with other projects. Moreover, Snyder was interested in making an R-rated film, while Hugh Jackman preferred a PG-13 rating.
