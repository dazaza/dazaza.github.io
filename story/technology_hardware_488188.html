China has nearly 300 million mobile internet users

by lieencq09 on 2011-09-12 11:26:43

There are nearly 1 billion users over the age of 15 worldwide who use the internet.

There are more than 150 million personal blogs globally.

China has over 400 million internet users.

China has close to 300 million mobile internet users.

These numbers symbolize how our lives have already been changed by the internet, and how our business operations are being influenced and transformed. The internet is making the world smaller, shortening spatial distances, and also changing our perception of time - you can go shopping at midnight, play games with friends on the other side of the planet, an email can reach anywhere in the world with internet access in just a few seconds, and within a second you can gather information from all over the world... When the internet integrates with the portable terminal of a mobile phone, its scope becomes truly limitless. Today, the internet has fully integrated into our lives, completely overturning our way of life. The technology of the "Internet of Things" has also matured, which will enable the network to permeate every aspect of our lives, making each of us, whether actively or passively, participate in this magical web.

As a result, business opportunities continue to emerge. Global online commerce transactions amount to several trillion dollars per year, and China's annual online transaction volume has already exceeded hundreds of billions. In comparison, Chinese enterprises' emphasis on the internet is still far from enough. Among the hundreds of billions of yuan in online transactions generated, most are facilitated through intermediary platforms of online traders, while direct transactions between businesses and consumers are very few.

Currently, the involvement of Chinese enterprises in internet-related activities is still quite limited, remaining at a relatively basic level of application, such as publishing advertisements on the internet and building company websites. Less than 20% of companies nationwide have their own websites, fewer than 5% have run online advertisements, and other advanced applications of the internet are not even considered.

Most Chinese enterprises believe that the internet is merely a publicity method with unclear results, and its value is extremely limited. In reality, the scope of internet application for business operations goes far beyond this. As we delve deeper and apply it more broadly, the value that the internet can bring will become increasingly significant. In other words, the reason enterprises perceive the internet as lacking value is simply because they have barely tapped into its potential. I believe that the emergence of the internet has had an impact on human society no less significant than the industrial revolution. With the increasing maturity of network technology, it will not only change human lifestyles but also disrupt business operating models. Therefore, today's enterprises should take the lead in fully utilizing internet resources. If handled appropriately, enterprises can even leverage the internet to build core competitiveness.

First, enterprises' use of the internet should rise above the current basic level of building a corporate website merely for appearances or running some online advertisements for product promotion. It should elevate to the height of integrating internet usage into the enterprise's operational strategy. Only by treating internet application as one of the key strategic resources in business operations and incorporating it into strategic planning can the internet demonstrate its astonishing power.

The application of the internet in business operations can be divided into two aspects:

1. Management;

2. Marketing.

Below, we will discuss the application of the internet in these two areas respectively.

Application of the Internet in Enterprise Management Activities

Currently, Chinese enterprises mainly use the internet for basic functions such as teleconferencing, instant communication, and emails in management. This is just the most basic use of the internet, and the true value of the internet has only been developed to about 10%. We can fully utilize the internet to achieve functions such as dispatch centers, virtual offices, and information databases. The realization of these functions will significantly reduce management costs and improve operational efficiency.
