[Treatment of Prostatitis]

Prostatitis, a common and frequently occurring disease in men, is not yet fully understood in terms of its causes. Combined with its relatively special anatomical structure and the fact that it often occurs in people with frequent sexual activity, treatment is not easy. In daily life, it is common to see patients with prostatitis who have undergone multiple treatments without complete cure. In fact, with the continuous development of medicine, many methods have achieved significant therapeutic effects in treating prostatitis. As long as patients build confidence to overcome the disease and closely cooperate with doctors, prostatitis can be cured and a new life can begin.

◇ Antibiotic Therapy:

This is a common method for treating prostatitis. Antibiotics diffuse from the plasma into the prostate fluid and are effective against most Gram-positive bacilli that cause urinary tract infections. However, since they cannot cross the lipid membrane of the prostate epithelium to enter the prostate acini for therapeutic effect, the treatment outcome is not ideal. Therefore, when selecting antibiotics for treating prostatitis, the following principles should be followed:

(1) The drug has high sensitivity to bacteria.

(2) The selected drug should have high lipophilicity, high permeability, low plasma protein binding rate, and high dissociation degree.

(3) Combination therapy using two or more drugs with synergistic effects.

(4) To achieve effective drug concentration in the prostate interstitium and prevent urethral infection, it is recommended to use ultra-high doses and ultra-long duration (4-12 weeks).

Recently, some scholars have classified antibiotics based on their different levels of diffusion into the prostate. High-level ones include trimethoprim, clindamycin, erythromycin, etc.; medium-level ones include chloramphenicol, lincomycin; low-level ones include ampicillin, cefalexin I, cefalexin IV, doxycycline, kanamycin, nitrofurantoin, oxytetracycline, penicillin G, polymyxin B, rifampin, and most sulfonamide drugs. Patients can choose the type of drug, usage, and dosage under the guidance of a doctor.

◇ Injection Therapy:

Since antibiotics administered systemically are difficult to reach effective concentrations in the prostate, affecting the efficacy, direct injection of antibiotics into the prostate has been developed. The specific method is to select gentamicin, kanamycin, cefalotin, used alone or in combination, injected directly into the prostate through the perineum or under ultrasound guidance directly into the lesion within the prostate, once or twice a week, ten times as one course.

The main advantage of this treatment method is that the drug is directly injected into the prostate, easily diffusing and reaching an effective antibacterial concentration. It can not only kill bacteria at the injection site but also eliminate bacteria in surrounding tissues. Part of the drug absorbed into the bloodstream can also help eliminate infections in the urethra and other areas. This is a valuable treatment method for refractory and stubborn chronic prostatitis. However, it also has some disadvantages that are difficult to overcome, mainly:

(1) The prostate location is deep, direct injection is not easy, puncture can cause damage to surrounding tissues and cause hematuria.

(2) Puncture can cause pain and discomfort, repeated punctures are difficult for patients to accept.

(3) Skin or rectal puncture may carry bacteria into the prostate causing re-infection.

(4) Repeated punctures may cause fibrous tissue proliferation in the prostate, prostate sclerosis, palpable hard nodules or hardened prostate fibrous tissue during digital rectal examination, separation and encapsulation of lesions making it harder for antibiotics to penetrate and making it difficult for prostate fluid to drain.

Therefore, when choosing this treatment method, caution must be exercised. It should only be considered when other methods fail, and punctures should not be too frequent, once or twice a week, with the course lasting no longer than 1-2 months.

◇ Sitz Bath Therapy:

Sitz bath therapy is actually also a form of physical therapy. Since it does not require any medical equipment and can be operated by the patient themselves at home, it is a worth-promoting and most effective family treatment method. The specific operation is as follows:

Pour water around 40% temperature (not scalding when hand is placed in) into a basin, half full is sufficient. Sit for 10~30 minutes each time. When the water temperature drops, add appropriate hot water to maintain an effective temperature. Perform 1~2 times daily, with 10 days as one course. Aromatic Chinese herbs like Atractylodes, Saussurea costus, and Cardamom can be added to the hot water. If prostate suppositories are inserted before sitting in the bath, it can promote drug absorption and improve efficacy.

It should be noted that for those diagnosed with infertility due to prostatitis, the sitz bath method should not be adopted because sperm are high-level cells that require high survival conditions. Taking temperature as an example, the normal temperature inside the scrotum should be 32~33°C. When the temperature inside the scrotum rises for some reason, it can cause obstacles in sperm production, leading to the cessation of sperm production, thus further reducing the possibility of conception.

The method is as follows:

(1) After defecation, clean the anus and the lower segment of the rectum, then proceed with massage therapy. The patient takes a chest-knee prone position or a lateral recumbent position. The doctor uses the index finger to feel the prostate on the anterior wall of the rectum and gently presses in the order of outside up, inside down. Simultaneously, instruct the patient to perform anal lifting exercises to expel prostate fluid from the urethral orifice and immediately urinate.

(2) Self-massage: The patient takes a squatting position or a lateral flexion recumbent position. After defecation, clean the anus and the lower segment of the rectum, then use the middle finger or index finger to press the prostate body. The method is the same as before. Each massage lasts 3~5 minutes, ideally until prostate fluid is discharged from the urethra each time. The force used during massage must be gentle. Lubricate the finger sleeve with soap before massaging to reduce discomfort. There should be at least a 3-day interval between each massage treatment. If significant tenderness or increased cystic sensation is found during self-massage, consult a specialist clinic promptly to avoid chronic prostatitis turning into an acute episode while undergoing prostate massage.

[Acute Bacterial Prostatitis Treatment]

1. General Treatment: Bed rest for 3-4 days, drink plenty of water, abstain from alcohol and spicy foods, hot water sitz baths or perineal heat application can be performed, and maintain smooth bowel movements. Abstain from sexual activity.

2. Antibiotic Treatment:

(1) When the patient has obvious systemic symptoms, high fever, and significantly elevated white blood cells in the blood, intravenous medication should be used for 1 week before switching to oral medication until 1 month.

(2) Select antibiotics that can diffuse into the prostate and quickly take effect intravenously to rapidly control symptoms and prevent conversion to chronic prostatitis. Choose sensitive antibiotics after bacterial culture results are available.

Commonly used broad-spectrum antibiotics include cephalosporins and fluoroquinolones.

Commonly used intravenous medications include: Suprax 2.0g intravenous drip daily 2-3 times; Rocephin 3.0g intravenous drip daily 2 times; Levaquin 2.0g intravenous drip daily 2 times; Avelox (Baxdela) 400mg intravenous drip daily 1 time.

Commonly used oral medications include: Ceftin 0.5g twice daily; Ofloxacin 0.2g twice daily; Baxdela 0.4g once daily.

3. Symptomatic Treatment:

(1) If high fever occurs, antipyretics should be given accordingly, such as Indocin suppository 1/2 PRN rectally; Tylenol 1 tablet PRN.

(2) If bladder irritation symptoms are obvious, options include: Ditropan 0.2g orally three times daily; Flomax 0.2mg orally once daily.

(3) If difficulty in urination or urinary retention occurs, temporary suprapubic bladder puncture drainage should be performed to drain urine or fine soft silicone catheter indwelling catheterization should be used.

4. Surgical Treatment: If acute prostatitis has formed a prostate abscess, incision and drainage should be performed through the rectum or perineum. If the abscess is limited to the prostate, urethroscopy can be used for prostate puncture and pus drainage followed by injection of broad-spectrum antibiotics.

[Chronic Prostatitis Treatment]

General Treatment: Enhance confidence, eliminate worries, moderate sexual desire but avoid forced abstinence. Avoid alcohol and stimulating foods, take hot water sitz baths once every evening, undergo local physical therapy, and change obvious triggering factors in life, such as avoiding prolonged cycling.

Prostate Massage: Regular prostate massage can promote the discharge of inflammatory secretions from the prostate, once a week. Simultaneously, routine examinations of prostate fluid can be conducted to evaluate treatment effectiveness.

Drug Infusion: Through a specially designed balloon catheter inserted into the urethra, sterile physiological saline is injected into the prostatic urethra and aspirated several times to remove purulent secretions, then antibiotics are injected, once a week.

Urethral Dilation: For those with urethral stricture or obstruction, regular urethral dilation facilitates excretion. When the probe passes through the urethra, it stretches the prostate opening, which is beneficial for gland drainage.

Periprostatic Blockade:

Antibiotics: General antibiotics find it difficult to enter prostate tissue, which is one of the reasons why clinical treatment is relatively difficult. They can be used in combination of 2~3 kinds or selectively based on the results of bacterial culture and drug sensitivity tests of prostate fluid.

[Difficulties in Treating Prostatitis]

Many patients with prostatitis find that their condition often recurs and is difficult to completely cure during actual treatment. This is quite normal, and the reasons are mainly the following:

The first and most fundamental reason is the anatomical and physiological characteristics of the prostate itself. The deep location of the prostate, especially the special structure of the prostate lipid capsule, makes drug treatment very difficult;

The second reason is the unclear diagnosis of the causes of prostatitis, which poses great difficulties for clinical treatment. Often, due to unclear treatment goals and lack of specificity, prostatitis persists and is difficult to cure.

Finally, neglecting partner treatment leads to many patients with prostatitis caused by sexually transmitted diseases only seeking treatment themselves while ignoring the examination and treatment of their partners, resulting in a vicious cycle of infection - treatment - reinfection - retreatment. Some patients' incorrect understanding of prostatitis, non-standard treatment, and improper use of drugs cause bacteria and other pathogenic microorganisms to develop drug resistance, making it quite difficult for doctors to treat. Therefore, clinical doctors must fully consider these factors during the treatment process. With the active cooperation of patients, after identifying the cause, targeted treatment should be carried out, and preventive health care work should be done after treatment to ensure that prostatitis no longer recurs.
