Man sexually assaults 15-year-old boy, can't be convicted, detained for 15 days

by zsh0827098 on 2012-02-08 09:13:52

Xiaqiang always lies in bed and doesn't want to get up, and the food by the bedside hasn't been touched. The act of violating a woman's will and forcibly having sexual intercourse with her through violence, coercion or other means is called rape. But if a boy is molested by a man, how should it be defined?

Yang Xiaqiang (a pseudonym), a 15-year-old boy from Xinmi City, has stayed at home for more than 20 days since being molested by a man on the night of June 8. When he hears someone entering the room, he wraps his whole body in a quilt.

Xiaqiang's grandmother said that the child had been seriously harmed, and the public security organs should arrest the man and let the court impose a heavy sentence. However, the police handling the case from the Qingping Street Police Station said that according to the existing laws, the man could only be detained for 15 days. As for the harm suffered by the child, civil litigation can be initiated in court.

According to reports by Henan Business Daily reporter Wenkai Wang with text and photos:

Incident:

A man threatened a 15-year-old boy twice to take off all his clothes

Yang Xiaqiang, 15 years old, dropped out of school. In late March this year, Xiaqiang's uncle found him a job at a car repair shop in the county town. During the day, Xiaqiang went to the repair shop, and rested at his uncle's house at night.

On the evening of June 8, after work, Xiaqiang and two classmates were playing at X Square in Xinmi City. At 11 pm, they decided to go home.

At this time, Xiaqiang wanted to relieve himself and ran to a secluded area of X Square. However, the two classmates waited for a long time but did not see him return, and when they called his mobile phone, it was turned off. They thought Xiaqiang might have left first, so they went home.

According to Mr. Jin, Xiaqiang's uncle, at that time, while Xiaqiang was relieving himself near a passage in the square, he was forced into a red Mazda car by a man with a dagger. "After getting into the car, his phone was confiscated and turned off. The man held a dagger to threaten Xiaqiang and drove north towards the back mountain of Snowflake Mountain." Mr. Jin said that after reaching the back mountain, the man used a knife to threaten his nephew to take off all his clothes.

About an hour later, the man was still not satisfied and asked Xiaqiang to call a classmate over. Under duress, Xiaqiang had no choice but to call his classmate Wang Chao (a pseudonym). The man and Xiaqiang picked up Wang Chao in the city and then came to a secluded place on the Qingping Mountain. The man again forced Wang Chao to take off all his clothes, feeling that Wang Chao was a bit older, so he drove Wang Chao back to the city and let him go home.

It was already around 1 am at this point. The man took Xiaqiang to a secluded place near a gas station in the northern part of the city and ordered Xiaqiang to take off all his clothes. After also taking off his own clothes, the man tortured Xiaqiang in the car for nearly 4 hours.

Consequences:

The child lies in bed every day, unwilling to speak or eat

When Xiaqiang returned to the repair shop, it was already 5:30 in the morning. According to Mr. Jin, Xiaqiang told him that when he got out of the car, the man threatened him not to look back and not to check the license plate. He walked all the way to the door of the repair shop before the man quickly left.

On the morning of June 9, after learning about the incident, the boss of the repair shop reported it to the police and contacted Mr. Jin. Fortunately, Xiaqiang remembered the license plate number.

After receiving the report, the Qingping Street Police Station responded. On June 15, Xiaqiang's family and Wang Chao spotted the red Mazda car at the intersection of Kaiyang Road and Qingping Street in Xinmi City. The police who arrived immediately controlled the 29-year-old owner Li Weixing (a pseudonym).

Although the person was under control, the next step was how to handle it. Mr. Jin went to the police station to inquire, and the police said that this matter was quite complicated and needed to consult the legal department of the municipal bureau. "In the afternoon, I asked again, and they said it was difficult to define. The legal department replied that according to Article 44 of the Public Security Administration Punishment Law, the maximum detention period is 15 days, and that’s all the police station can do."

Mr. Jin said that the child suffered such great physical and mental harm, and according to what the police station said, detaining Li Weixing for 15 days would be enough. "Even though my nephew is a little boy, he suffered harm similar to rape, and the public security organs should not just detain Li Weixing for 15 days according to Article 44 of the Public Security Administration Punishment Law."

At around 10 am yesterday, in Xiaqiang's home in a suburban rural area of Xinmi City, the boy lying on the bed in the cave wrapped his entire body tightly in a sheet. On the bedside table were sausages, instant noodles, ice green tea, eggs, and other foods. No matter how the reporter tried to talk to him, the boy lying on the bed remained silent.

"From the 9th to today, it has been more than 20 days. Look at this child, lying in bed every day and not getting up, not eating," said the 86-year-old grandmother, crying.

Police:

According to the law, only a 15-day detention penalty can be given

Xiaqiang's 69-year-old grandmother also rushed over. She said, isn't there any provision in the law? Can't we really punish Li Weixing?

Xiaqiang's father said that after the child experienced such an event, he feared he couldn't bear it. "If this thing spreads in the countryside, it would be even more embarrassing than a young girl being raped. Now we only want the perpetrator to get the punishment he deserves."

Yang Chaojun, the officer responsible for handling this case at the Qingping Street Police Station, said that after investigation, Li Weixing's and Yang Xiaqiang's accounts basically matched. From the statements, all the actions that night were carried out against Xiaqiang's will.

"From accepting this case to filing it on the 15th, we encountered great difficulties; we've never seen anything like this before. Detaining Li Weixing for 15 days was a carefully considered decision. Current laws do not allow us to make any mistakes. If we apply the wrong law, Li Weixing might sue us," said Shang Wenzhou, the instructor of the Qingping Street Police Station. As for the psychological harm suffered by the child, everyone can imagine it, and the family can file a civil lawsuit in court.

According to the investigating officer, because this case involves the privacy of both parties, they are extremely cautious. Li Weixing is married and has children as well. On the evening of the 8th, he drank a lot of alcohol. When Li Weixing's wife came to the police station asking why he was detained, "we could only say it was a fight. If we told the truth, she would definitely divorce him in the future."

Shang Wenzhou said, everyone is equal before the law, and the public security organs treat everyone the same. "This does not fall under rape, nor illegal detention, nor intentional injury. We enforce the law according to the law, but if there is no law for us to rely on, it is really difficult."

Opinions:

What crime does the man's behavior involve?

Opinion one: Intentional Injury Crime

Definition: The act of intentionally and illegally harming another person's body

Wei Guangyang, Deputy Director of Henan Yinji Law Firm: Intentional Injury Crime. First, Li Weixing's actions constitute harm to another person's body, and it is not only physical harm but also damage to personality. Second, from the perspective of the consequences, Yang Xiaqiang not only endured physical pain but also immense mental suffering. Finally, according to the relevant provisions of China's current Criminal Law, although Li Weixing's degree of intentional injury does not meet the standard for criminal conviction, considering the harmful results, criminal penalties are sufficient.

Investigating Officer: For intentional injury, there must be a forensic medical appraisal. Xiaqiang is the victim, but there are no injuries on his body, making it impossible to conduct a forensic medical appraisal.

Opinion two: Defamation Crime

Definition: Using violence or other methods to openly degrade another person's dignity and destroy their reputation, under serious circumstances

Liu Hui, Attorney of Henan Yinji Law Firm: Defamation Crime. Holding a knife and threatening the victim severely damaged the victim's dignity and reputation, constituting defamation crime.

Investigating Officer: Li Weixing's behavior was not conducted in front of others, thus not constituting defamation crime.

Opinion three: Illegal Detention Crime

Definition: The act of illegally depriving others of personal freedom through confinement, imprisonment, or other coercive methods

Yang Lei, Deputy Director of Henan Yinji Law Firm and Attorney Wu Leisong: Illegal Detention Crime. Li Weixing coerced Yang Xiaqiang to deep mountains and inside the car using a dagger, forcing them to engage in sexual acts for several hours. The victim's personal freedom was severely restricted, causing high levels of tension, and afterward, they became mentally confused and secluded themselves.

Professor Liu Defa, Doctor of Zhengzhou University Law School: Illegal Detention Crime. In 2006, the Supreme Procuratorate's judicial interpretation clearly stated that during illegal detention, violent, coercive, or humiliating means can be used without the 24-hour restriction. Li Weixing's behavior severely degraded the victim's dignity, and the act of holding a knife constitutes a serious coercive act, which should result in a heavier punishment for illegal detention.

Professor Ruan Qilin, Doctoral Supervisor of the Criminal Justice College of China University of Political Science and Law: Illegal Detention Crime. Subjectively, Li Weixing aimed to satisfy his abnormal sexual desires, and objectively, he restricted the victim's personal freedom, degraded their dignity, and used violence and coercion, sufficiently qualifying as illegal detention crime.

Investigating Officer: Illegal detention refers to specific places, such as small rooms or basements, but Li Weixing took Xiaqiang to the back mountain of Snowflake Mountain and near a gas station. Although these areas are remote, they belong to public places, especially Snowflake Mountain, where citizens often go for cooling off and walking at night.

Continuation of News:

According to the provisions of China's Criminal Law, rape is defined as the act of violating a woman's will and forcibly having sexual intercourse with her through violence, coercion, or other means.

According to a report by Legal Evening News on January 4 this year, a 42-year-old male security guard 'raped' an 18-year-old male colleague, and eventually, the security guard was sentenced to one year in prison by the Chaoyang District Court of Beijing for intentional injury. It was reported that this was the first time a domestic court pursued criminal responsibility for raping a male.

In this case, Lu Zheng, the director of Beijing Zhongguangweitian Legal Service Office, said that the current provisions of the Criminal Law regarding rape crimes are not conducive to protecting citizens' rights. In many countries, it has already been stipulated that the subject of rape crimes can also be males.
