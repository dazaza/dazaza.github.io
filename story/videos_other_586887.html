Peking Union Medical College Hospital was sentenced to infringement for demolishing civilian houses at night without signing agreements

by hejundcai on 2011-11-26 08:39:25

The outpatient and emergency building of the under-construction Xiehe Hospital. Photographed by reporter Tianxiao Yang. On the evening of March 20, 2008, Duan Yun's house was forcibly entered and demolished overnight by the Peking Union Medical College Hospital (referred to as Xiehe Hospital). At that time, the two parties had not yet reached an agreement on demolition compensation, and Duan Yun, over 80 years old, had fainted on her way to see a doctor and was helped by many passers-by and taken to the hospital. Duan Yun did not agree to the demolition.

Duan Yun reported the incident to the police, filed administrative and civil lawsuits, and used these as means of rights protection, accusing Xiehe Hospital of illegal demolition. Xiehe Hospital, however, claimed that the demolition project was a key municipal government project, and that their demolition actions were legal and compliant.

Recently, the Dongcheng Court made a first-instance judgment in the civil lawsuit, determining that Xiehe Hospital's actions violated Duan Yun's property rights, ordering compensation of more than 1.46 million yuan.

Currently, due to Xiehe Hospital's appeal, Duan Yun has not yet received her demolition compensation. However, on the original site of her house, the key Beijing project - the Xiehe Hospital outpatient and emergency building - is already under construction.

It took Duan Yun six days to learn about the demolition of her house.

In 1994, after obtaining her medical doctorate, Duan Yun started working at Xiehe Hospital as a physician. Five years later, she was allocated a public housing unit by the hospital, located in Building No. 41 on Dongdan North Street, adjacent to Xiehe Hospital, with an area of 52.65 square meters.

After the implementation of the housing reform policy, Duan Yun paid a sum of money to purchase this public housing unit and obtained the property certificate. In 2004, she resigned and went abroad, entrusting her sister Duan Fang (a pseudonym) to manage the house. Because relatives often came to Beijing to stay, this house was never rented out and was mostly vacant.

In 2007, Xiehe Hospital began negotiating the demolition with Duan Fang, offering a compensation of 15,000 yuan per square meter, which Duan Yun did not agree to, stating, "At this price, it would be impossible to buy a house in this area." Subsequently, Duan Yun proposed buying an equivalent-sized second-hand house in the same area, but this was also rejected by the hospital.

Negotiations over demolition compensation reached an impasse. By then, most residents in the building had negotiated their compensation and moved out. By early 2008, only three households, including Duan Yun's, remained steadfast but no longer lived there.

On the evening of March 20, 2008, a group of men broke into the houses of Duan Yun and two other households, moving all their belongings away before demolishing the dormitory building overnight.

Six days later, when Duan Fang came to check the house, she found that the entire building had been demolished, prompting her to report the incident to the police immediately. After receiving the report, the police found a situation statement issued by Xiehe Hospital to the Jianguomen Police Station in Dongcheng District on March 21. The statement indicated that on March 20, heavy rain caused a danger in Building No. 41, and to protect the belongings inside the houses of Duan Yun and the other two households from damage, Xiehe Hospital urgently relocated their items to a safe place and properly stored them. The statement, however, did not mention anything about the overnight demolition of the building.

"Demolition compensation was still being negotiated, we never agreed to the demolition, these people are engaging in violent demolition!" Duan Fang said, expressing strong dissatisfaction with Xiehe Hospital's actions and reporting the case to the police for intentional destruction of public and private property.

The Housing Bureau stated "basically true."

On March 28, 2008, the Dongcheng Public Security Bureau issued a "Notice of Non-Filing," stating that after review, the case did not meet the criteria for filing. Ms. Duan initiated an administrative review, and on April 15 of the same year, the Municipal Public Security Bureau upheld the decision not to file the case, determining that Ms. Duan's reported case lacked criminal facts.

With the criminal case not filed, Duan Yun hired a lawyer to sue the Dongcheng Housing Bureau. Duan Yun believed that the Dongcheng Housing Bureau failed to act administratively and demanded punishment for Xiehe Hospital's illegal actions during the demolition process. Two other families with similar experiences also reported Xiehe Hospital's issues during the demolition to the Dongcheng Housing Bureau.

In its defense statement, the Dongcheng Housing Bureau stated that regarding the demolition dispute between Ms. Duan and Xiehe Hospital, Xiehe Hospital had applied for administrative arbitration, which the bureau had accepted, but because Ms. Duan initiated an administrative lawsuit, the arbitration process was suspended.

Regarding Xiehe Hospital's "demolition in the rain," the Housing Bureau issued a "Handling Opinion on Some Residents' Reports Regarding Issues in Your Hospital's Demolition Work" on April 3, 2008. The opinion stated that upon receiving reports reflecting Xiehe Hospital's "illegal actions during the demolition of the employee dormitory building," the Housing Bureau investigated the scene and determined that the residents' reported issues were "basically true." In the "Opinion Letter," the Housing Bureau proposed handling opinions: Xiehe Hospital should immediately stop the demolition work of the building and rectify the situation; actively cooperate with the police investigation and evidence collection; and do a good job with the residents, properly resolving the residents' demolition compensation and property loss compensation issues.

On August 12, 2008, the administrative litigation concluded. The Dongcheng Court ruled that Duan Yun was a Chinese citizen residing overseas, and her authorization letter to lawyer Wang Liangbin was not certified by the Chinese embassy in Australia, thus her lawyer's representation did not meet the legal requirements, leading to the dismissal of the case.

House value becomes the key controversy

Failing in the administrative litigation, on July 28, 2009, Duan Yun filed a civil lawsuit with the Dongcheng Court, directly targeting Xiehe Hospital.

The complaint stated that Xiehe Hospital's "illegal demolition behavior" caused significant economic losses and mental harm to Duan Yun, therefore demanding compensation for the house value of 1.57 million yuan, indoor item losses of 20,000 yuan, lawyer fees of 40,000 yuan, totaling 1.82 million yuan, and simultaneously demanding a written apology from Xiehe Hospital.

This case lasted nearly two years, with the Dongcheng Court holding nearly ten hearings, attempting mediation from various angles but without success.

Xiehe Hospital repeatedly stated in court that as the construction party of a key municipal project, its construction and demolition actions were legal and compliant. Duan Yun's refusal to relocate seriously affected the progress of the key municipal project. On March 20, 2008, Xiehe Hospital transferred Duan Yun's property within the house to avoid emergencies, which was considered gratuitous management rather than infringement. Duan Yun's claim of property loss had no basis, and Xiehe Hospital currently only agreed to compensate Duan Yun according to the building area of the involved house, at a price of 12,000 yuan per square meter.

As for the value of the house, Xiehe Hospital presented an appraisal report commissioned in April 2007, valuing the involved house at 458,637 yuan.

In the correspondence sent by Xiehe Hospital to Duan Yun, it particularly pointed out that the National Development and Reform Commission approved a demolition compensation price of 10,571 yuan per square meter, which was eventually raised to 15,000 yuan based on Xiehe Hospital's proposal. Lawyer Wang Liangbin requested to see the approval documents from the National Development and Reform Commission and submitted an application. In July 2008, the National Development and Reform Commission replied that these documents involved third-party rights, and the Ministry of Health and other third parties disagreed with disclosure, so they could not disclose relevant files and materials for this project.

After the case was filed, Duan Yun's side also submitted an application, requesting the court to commission Beijing Antai Xiang Land and Real Estate Appraisal Co., Ltd., to evaluate the market price of the involved house. On November 18, 2010, Antai Xiang Company issued an appraisal report, estimating that on July 28, 2009, when Duan Yun filed the lawsuit, the value of her house was 1.4637 million yuan. Duan Yun's side stated that they recognized this appraisal report, and the price of 15,000 yuan per square meter previously proposed by Xiehe Hospital was too low.

Xiehe Hospital judged infringing and appealed

On May 20 this year, the Dongcheng Court made a first-instance verdict in this case. The court relied on the "Property Law" to rule, ordering Xiehe Hospital to pay Duan Yun 1.4637 million yuan for house loss and 1,800 yuan for indoor property loss, dismissing Duan Yun's other claims.

The judgment stated that although Xiehe Hospital had obtained a demolition permit, it moved Duan Yun's property out of the house and demolished it without reaching an agreement with Duan Yun on demolition resettlement compensation and without Duan Yun's consent, infringing on Duan Yun's ownership of the house and should bear corresponding compensation responsibilities.

The Dongcheng Court believed that since Duan Yun and Xiehe Hospital had not signed a demolition resettlement compensation agreement, and Xiehe Hospital had not provided sufficient evidence proving that Duan Yun had received the appraisal report in April 2007, the compensation standard for Duan Yun's house value should refer to the appraisal report issued after the lawsuit.

Due to Duan Yun's residence overseas, the Dongcheng Court's judgment specified an appeal period of 30 days. After receiving the express delivery, Duan Yun did not file an appeal application.

On June 2, Xiehe Hospital, dissatisfied with the judgment, submitted an appeal to the Second Intermediate People's Court of Beijing, requesting a retrial of the Dongcheng Court's civil judgment and ordering Duan Yun to bear the litigation costs.

The appeal document showed that Xiehe Hospital's reasons for appeal were as follows. First, the court overlooked major facts, as Xiehe Hospital's outpatient and emergency building was a key municipal project, a public welfare project rather than a commercial project. Duan Yun's insistence on excessively high demolition compensation affected the progress of the project and disrupted the overall demolition order. Second, at the time of appraisal, the involved house had already been demolished, losing the tangible subject for appraisal. Moreover, the appraisal reference date of July 28, 2009, could not reflect the value of the involved house at the time of demolition. As early as April 26, 2007, the appraisal institution commissioned by Xiehe Hospital estimated the value of the involved house at 458,637 yuan, which was the true value of Duan Yun's house. Third, Xiehe Hospital did not infringe on Duan Yun's indoor property.

Currently, the Second Intermediate People's Court of Beijing has accepted the appeal and will schedule a hearing for this case.

Is the demolition action illegal?

In demolition cases, situations where the expropriated party and the expropriator cannot reach an agreement on demolition compensation occur frequently. In this case, without reaching a demolition agreement, Duan Yun's house was demolished on a rainy night, and Duan Yun believed that Xiehe Hospital's actions constituted "illegal demolition," which Xiehe Hospital found difficult to accept, emphasizing that Duan Yun's behavior disrupted the overall demolition order of the key municipal project. Was Xiehe Hospital's demolition action illegal? The Dongcheng Court's civil judgment did not explicitly state this. According to an unnamed scholar, based on the previous version of the demolition regulations, the demolisher must first have a demolition permit, secondly, have a signed demolition resettlement compensation agreement with the expropriated party, or an administrative ruling issued by the relevant department. Therefore, Xiehe Hospital's demolition without reaching an agreement with Duan Yun and without obtaining an administrative ruling from the Housing Bureau was clearly inappropriate.

To resolve this issue, the newly enacted demolition regulations stipulate that if both parties cannot reach an agreement, and the expropriated party does not apply for administrative reconsideration or file an administrative lawsuit within the statutory period, and does not relocate within the period specified in the compensation decision, the municipal or county-level people's government making the house expropriation decision shall lawfully apply to the people's court for compulsory execution.
