Determination of litigation subject qualification for affiliated operation in the construction field

by udolaw45 on 2010-04-14 15:51:49

Currently, China is in an economic transition period. Due to the different qualifications and treatments for various forms of ownership in economic activities, a large number of individuals hang their operations under other entities to engage in economic activities. This phenomenon is particularly common in the construction sector.

In civil trials, a considerable number of construction contract disputes involve contracts signed by construction companies but executed by individual construction teams under the name of these companies. These teams are self-organized, self-financed, bear risks individually, and operate on a profit-and-loss basis while only paying management fees to the construction company. The latter is responsible for signing contracts, handling project settlements, and collecting management fees without investing in or managing the actual construction work. This is known as "hanging" operation in the construction field. The nature of such hanging operations has not been clearly defined by Chinese laws and regulations. To address issues arising in trials, the Supreme People's Court has made judicial interpretations regarding the litigation parties involved in hanging operations. According to Article 43 of the Supreme People's Court's "Opinions on Several Issues Concerning the Application of the Civil Procedure Law of the People’s Republic of China" (hereinafter referred to as the Civil Procedure Law Opinion), individual industrial and commercial households, personal partnerships, or private enterprises that hang under collective enterprises and conduct business operations under the umbrella of these enterprises shall be considered co-litigants with the collective enterprise they are hanging under. This is currently the sole legal basis for handling hanging operations. Due to the lag in legal provisions, there is significant controversy both theoretically and practically regarding how to determine the litigation subject qualification in hanging operations, especially in the construction sector where such operations are widespread. This article attempts to present some personal opinions for discussion.

Civil disputes related to hanging operations in the construction sector mainly fall into three categories: First, contractual debt disputes caused by construction teams owing material costs or labor fees; Second, breach of contract disputes due to construction units failing to pay project fees; Third, hanging contract disputes caused by construction enterprises owing project fees to hanging construction teams or construction teams owing management fees or advanced material costs to construction enterprises.

Regarding the first type of case, according to the Civil Procedure Law Opinion, the construction unit and the head of the construction team should be co-defendants. Creditors can sue both the hanging party and the hung entity simultaneously. However, based on the voluntary principle of lawsuits, creditors may also sue either the hanging party or the hung entity alone. The court cannot add defendants at its discretion.

In hanging economies, the hanging entity and the hung person are two different types of civil subjects. Debts incurred by individual construction personnel should originally be borne by them personally, making the construction team the main debtor. However, allowing personal hanging operations violates relevant administrative and economic management regulations. Construction units permitting personal hanging operations have subjective faults, leading creditors to believe that the construction team is part of the hanging unit. When creditors demand that the hanging unit assume civil liability, the hanging unit should indeed assume civil liability. This is a kind of compensatory civil liability based on fault. After the hanging unit assumes responsibility, it can seek compensation from the head of the construction team.

Regarding the second type of case, the hanging unit and the hanging construction team are creditors, but the hanging unit is the nominal creditor, while the construction team is the actual creditor. Both parties to the construction contract are the construction company and the construction unit. It is natural for the construction company to claim debts from the construction unit based on the contract. However, whether the head of the hanging construction team can assert claims in their personal name remains controversial in practice. Some argue that since individuals are not parties to the contract, they should not be allowed to assert claims personally. If individuals are allowed to assert claims under the name of the construction party, given that individuals do not have construction qualifications, this would lead to confusion in legal relationships. Therefore, the construction team can only assert rights under the name of the construction company or directly against the construction company. The author believes that as long as it is confirmed that the construction team indeed belongs to the hanging nature, the head of the construction team can assert claims based on the construction contract because the head of the construction team personally invests, organizes the construction, and has the right to request payment from the construction unit. The outcome of the case has a direct causal relationship with them, qualifying them as plaintiffs. If the head of the construction team cannot sue as a plaintiff, the construction company has no investment and thus no actual loss. If the construction company does not file a lawsuit either, the rights of the head of the construction team will not be protected, leading to non-payment of migrant workers' wages and material costs, which would inevitably trigger many social contradictions. This contradicts the function of the people's courts to resolve disputes. Regarding the validity of the construction contract, if after the construction company signs the contract with the construction unit, the construction team personally organizes the construction, and the construction team fulfills its contractual obligations without any other illegal acts, the contract should be deemed valid. If serious quality issues arise during the construction process, it should be recognized that the construction company subcontracts the project to individuals without corresponding qualification conditions, violating Article 29 of the Construction Law, rendering the contract invalid. In practice, due to the strict implementation of construction supervision systems, construction quality issues are relatively few, and most construction contract disputes belong to the category of construction units withholding project payments. If the construction contract is deemed invalid, the principle of mutual return cannot be applied, so the people's courts should carefully determine the invalidity of the construction contract.

For the third type of case, there are differing opinions in practice. One opinion holds that the dispute between the construction company and the construction team falls within the internal controversy of a single legal entity, and regardless of who sues, the people's court should not accept the case. Another opinion argues that although the construction team nominally belongs to the subordinate institution of the construction company, it is actually a hanging relationship. The construction company and the head of the construction team are different subjects, one being a legal entity and the other a citizen. Disputes over debts and credits arising from hanging should be regarded as civil rights and obligations controversies, and the people's court should accept the lawsuit from either side. During the trial, if there is a written hanging contract, the rights and obligations of both parties should be determined according to the contract. If there is no written contract, the rights and obligations of both parties should be determined according to the general understanding of hanging relationships. That is, the hanging party should bear responsibility for the debts and credits generated during the construction process. The unpaid project fees owed by the construction company should be enjoyed by the head of the construction team, and the debts owed during the construction process should be repaid by the head of the construction team. The head of the construction team has the obligation to pay the management fee to the construction company as agreed. If the construction company possesses the project fees, it should return them to the head of the construction team. If the construction company advances labor fees, material costs, etc., on behalf of the construction team, it can seek compensation from the construction team.

In summary, civil disputes caused by hanging in the construction sector are numerous and complex. The people's courts should start from reality, consider the special form of hanging operations, and accurately identify the subject qualifications of the hanging party and the hung entity. They should fully play the role of civil trial functions in fair adjudication and resolving disputes, promptly handle such civil disputes, protect the legitimate rights and interests of citizens and legal entities, maintain integrity, and sanction breaches of contract.
