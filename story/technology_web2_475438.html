Centipede, centipede farming, centipede breeding base, centipede market price. The centipede is commonly known as "heavenly dragon" and also called "hundred-foot insect." It belongs to the arthropod family. In Xindu, the prospect of centipede farming involves chilopods (multi-legged), under the Scolopendromorpha order and Geophilidae family. There are about 30-40 species. The most common one is the Lesser Spiny-headed Centipede, along with the Flat-eared Hole Centipede.

The centipede is one of the common medicinal animals. It primarily treats conditions such as stroke, tetanus, umbilical wind, hemorrhoids, snake bites, and many other diseases.

Centipedes do not need to eat every day; after a full meal, they can go without food for 2 to 3 days. Therefore, under artificial breeding conditions, feeding can be done every 1 to 2 days. However, the food must be fresh. Feeding should take place in the evening, and any leftover food can be cleared by the next morning. The feeding location should be chosen on a relatively flat surface or within a vessel.
