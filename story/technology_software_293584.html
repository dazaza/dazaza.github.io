I. Information Affecting the Behavior of Major Players and Interpretation

1. The National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) once again strictly controls approvals for overcapacity industries such as steel, cement, and flat glass;

2. The State Council approves the development plan for the Yellow River Delta Efficient Ecological Economic Zone;

3. The "one-to-many" special account business of funds enters another intensive issuance period.

Interpretation: The upcoming Central Economic Work Conference at the weekend is currently receiving significant market attention, with one of the key topics being structural adjustment. Recently, measures such as the NDRC's rectification of overcapacity industries, preparation to support emerging industry policies, and the State Council's consecutive approval of regional revitalization plans are actually practical actions in the aspect of structural adjustment. This will lay a foundation for China's long-term sustainable and healthy economic development, which is a major long-term positive factor. After issuing a large number of new funds recently, the "one-to-many" business has entered another intensive issuance period, further replenishing market liquidity and providing conditions for future market growth.

II. Analysis of Major Player Behavior

1. Funds: Resonance sentiment index 55, main attack sectors: consumption, machinery, finance, electricity

Short-term behavior: Balance between bullish and bearish forces, engaging in a game with retail investors;

Medium to long-term behavior: Defensive strategy.

According to the position measurement data from DeSheng Fund Research Institution on November 26th, stock fund positions began to diverge last week, although the overall position was basically unchanged compared to the previous week. However, the proportion of heavily weighted funds decreased, while lightly weighted funds increased their positions.

This week's dragon-tiger list data showed that institutional seats purchased more stocks, and these seats are generally dominated by funds. It seems that with the fading negative effects of the Dubai incident and the introduction of domestic favorable news, funds have started increasing their holdings. These funds should mainly be newly established funds. Since the fourth quarter, many new funds have been issued, and recently the "one-to-many" business of funds has also welcomed an intensive issuance period. Under the circumstances of a favorable economy and trend, these newly entering funds have a very high possibility of accelerating their portfolio construction.

On the other hand, it is close to the end of the year, and the ranking competition among funds seems about to escalate. In the case of fund battles, the market is prone to significant fluctuations due to mutual pressure and individual support among them. Especially those heavily weighted stocks deserve investor attention because they significantly contribute to their performance. If there is good news stimulation, these stocks are very likely to be lifted by funds to improve overall performance.

2. Insurance, Social Security: Resonance sentiment index 50, main attack sectors: real estate, chemicals, low carbon

Short-term behavior: Swing trading;

Medium to long-term behavior: Long-term layout.

By October, the total assets of China Social Security Fund reached RMB 700 billion, with an investment return rate of 12.98% for the National Social Security Fund, where 32% of assets were used for stock investment. Previously, the upper limit for social security fund investment in stocks was 20%, but due to the transfer of state-owned shares, the proportion of social security fund investments in stocks has greatly increased, further enhancing the influence of the social security fund in the stock market. Therefore, every move by the social security fund deserves more attention from investors.

According to the latest disclosed data from the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, the social security fund increased its holdings in real estate in October. In October, the buying ratio of the social security fund was 17.82%, while the selling ratio was 4.63%, resulting in a difference of over 10 percentage points. The social security fund is a typical value investor. They bought heavily into real estate when most people thought it was overvalued, indicating there may be factors unseen by ordinary investors, such as inflation expectations, RMB appreciation, or other positive factors affecting the real estate sector.

Contrary to the behavior of the social security fund are insurance funds, which reduced their holdings significantly in October. Since the beginning of this year, insurance funds have been relatively cautious, adopting a "slow accumulation, lock in profits" strategy throughout the year. This is one reason why insurance funds trade frequently. Therefore, the behavior of insurance funds affects the short-term market more significantly, while in the long term, their fluctuating operation strategy only increases market volatility and does not have a significant impact on the direction of trends.

III. Investment Opportunities and Risks

Summary of Behavioral Directions: Today’s news is neutral, the stock market opened with a volatile downward trend, accelerated a decline in the afternoon, but rebounded in the last hour, largely recovering the drop gap. Eventually, the Shanghai Composite Index fell slightly by 5.12 points. From the K-line chart, today seemed to form what is known as a "hanging man line". Technically speaking, the appearance of a "hanging man line" often suggests a market peak, but from the trading floor, it looks more like a shakeout. These are two completely different scenarios, worthy of high attention. Our Wanyong Growth Investment Advisory Group believes that the shakeout possibility is greater, which was also our judgment in yesterday's column. The reasons are: first, the current level does not allow major players to exit entirely; either they continue to go long-term bullish or quickly push up prices. Second, negative news has just been digested, and the long-term positive factors of structural adjustment, economic recovery, and inflation cannot be absorbed so quickly. There is still much room for these positives to be utilized by major funds.

Short-term (within 1 month) opportunities and risks: The short-term market resonance sentiment index is 60. The Wanyong Stock Hotspot Tracking System indicates that the short-term hotspot sectors are seed industry, energy conservation and environmental protection, and low-carbon economy.

Medium-term (1~3 months) opportunities and risks: The medium-term market resonance sentiment index shows 60. The Wanyong Stock Hotspot Tracking System indicates that the medium-term hotspot sectors are finance, machinery, agriculture, low-carbon economy, and stocks with asset injection and restructuring expectations.

The process of buying and selling stocks is as follows: When buying and selling stocks, you must specify whether to buy or sell, the name (or code), quantity, and price of the stock. Moreover, this order is only valid on the day it is placed. The content of the order includes the abbreviation (code) of the stock you want to buy or sell, the quantity, and the price of the stock. The abbreviation of the stock usually consists of three to four Chinese characters, and the code is six digits. When placing an order, the code and abbreviation of the stock must be consistent. At the same time, there are certain regulations regarding the quantity of stocks traded: the quantity of stocks to be purchased must be an integer multiple of 100, but the quantity of stocks to be sold does not have to be an integer multiple of 100.

There are four ways to place an order: counter submission, automatic telephone orders, automatic computer orders, and remote terminal orders.
