Guangzhou Gas Explosion Interview续: Beating Continues, Factory Apologizes

by king2z9o on 2012-02-24 11:39:43

At around 11:50 AM yesterday, during the technical renovation and major overhaul of the gas triple pressure station (80,000 cubic meter gas holder) at Guangzhou Meishan Steel Company by Baosteel Construction Guangzhou Branch, converter gas backflowed into the gas holder, causing 13 workers to be poisoned. Among them, three people died after unsuccessful rescue attempts, three were lightly injured and are currently still under emergency care, and seven have left the hyperbaric oxygen chamber and are out of life-threatening conditions. After the incident, Jiangsu Provincial Party Secretary Luo Zhijun and Governor Li Xueyong paid high attention and immediately requested full efforts to rescue the poisoned individuals, investigate the cause, and handle the aftermath properly.

Unexpectedly, a worker mistakenly cut the bolts of the gas blocking blind flange due to operational errors, resulting in sudden leakage of converter gas that backflowed into the gas holder. The 13 workers inside the cabinet were caught off guard and fainted one after another from poisoning. After the accident, company leaders quickly organized personnel to send the affected to Meishan Hospital for rescue.

According to the hospital's president Ma Zhaolong, the first batch of nine poisoned individuals arrived at 12:20 PM that day. Two of them died despite rescue attempts, while seven entered the hyperbaric oxygen chamber for treatment, with their vital signs stabilizing by the afternoon. The second batch of three injured individuals arrived at around 2:20 PM with minor injuries and are currently still in intensive care. Another poisoned worker was sent to a suburban hospital in Guangzhou but also died after unsuccessful rescue attempts. In total, this accident resulted in 13 workers being poisoned, with three deaths, three lightly injured, and seven out of danger.

A lucky escape: A 20-year-old Henan youth avoided death because he was feeling drowsy after drinking the previous night

After the gas leak incident at Meishan Steel Plant, the gas concentration within the factory premises was high, and some workers woke up from poisoning. By 2:00 PM, the Guangzhou fire department had rescued nine people, with four still trapped. After all rescues were completed, a contractor responsible for construction mentioned that one worker who came to work in the morning was missing. When the fire personnel failed to find him after multiple searches, a colleague found this 20-year-old Henan youth sleeping in the dormitory. It was said that he drank a little alcohol the previous night and felt sleepy while working with his colleagues in the morning, so he left halfway through. Unexpectedly, he escaped with his life. It was reported that dozens of operators dispatched by the construction party Baosteel Corporation mostly came from areas such as Liyang and Xuzhou, gathered by several contractors. Due to poor visibility in the working area, the construction worker responsible for cutting the bolts made a mistake, leading to the gas leak incident. After the incident, more than 100 firefighters in 12 fire trucks rushed to the scene for rescue. At the time, many people were still trapped inside the tank. Firefighters mechanically ventilated the outside of the tank to eliminate residual gas while actively rescuing the trapped people. By 3:00 PM, all 13 trapped workers were rescued and sent to the hospital.

An unexpected twist: Two reporters were injured after being stopped by security guards; the vice president of Meigang publicly apologized.

At 2:30 PM yesterday, our reporter and Jinling Evening News reporter were obstructed by factory security when taking photos during an interview. Subsequently, physical conflicts occurred. One reporter from Jinling Evening News had their lower lip hit, causing it to swell and bleed, while our reporter suffered vomiting after being punched in the head. After the incident, the two injured reporters were sent to Mingji Hospital Emergency Center for treatment.

In the afternoon, while interviewing at Meishan Police Station in Yuhuatai District Public Security Bureau, two security guards involved had already arrived at the police station to accept inquiries. Last night, after receiving treatment at the hospital, the injured Jinling Evening News reporter also went to the police station for questioning, while our reporter could not go temporarily due to discomfort in the head. A relevant person in charge of the security office handling the conflict said that upon arriving at the scene after the incident, he saw both sides pushing each other and immediately criticized the security guards, telling them to talk things out instead of resorting to violence. According to the injured reporters, their vehicle followed a private car into the factory premises, and there was no obvious notice posted at the gate prohibiting vehicles from entering. During the photo-taking interview, they were outside the cordon line at the accident site.

Yesterday afternoon, during the media briefing held by Meishan Steel Company, Vice President Gong Suoguo of Meigang Company stated that the reason the security guards stopped reporters from taking pictures was because there was still a gas odor at the accident site to prevent further accidents. Secondly, regarding the injury of reporters in the conflict with security guards, he represented the company in apologizing.

Active aftermath: High attention from provincial and municipal leaders requesting full efforts to rescue poisoned individuals.

After the incident, Jiangsu Provincial Party Secretary Luo Zhijun and Governor Li Xueyong paid high attention and immediately requested full efforts to rescue poisoned individuals, investigate the cause, and handle the aftermath properly. Standing Committee Member of the Provincial Party Committee and Party Secretary of Guangzhou City Yang Weize instructed full efforts to rescue and treat poisoned individuals, while Vice Governor Shi Heping and others organized leaders from the provincial and municipal safety supervision, public security, fire, and health departments to arrive on the scene as soon as possible. They clearly required that provincial and municipal relevant departments cooperate with Meigang Company to make every effort to rescue the injured and comfort the families of the deceased and handle the aftermath well. Mayor Ji Jianye of Guangzhou City promptly organized the Guangzhou Emergency Command Center and other departments to fully rescue the poisoned employees, requiring investigation of the cause and control of the hazard.

According to Vice President Gong Suoguo of Guangzhou Meishan Steel Company, the preliminary cause of the accident was that during the maintenance process, the construction personnel cut the bolts of the gas blocking blind flange, causing the blind flange to fall off, allowing gas to backflow into the cabinet, resulting in 13 people inside the cabinet being poisoned. Three of them died despite rescue attempts, three were lightly injured and are currently still under emergency care, and seven have left the hyperbaric oxygen chamber and are out of life-threatening conditions.

News review: Multiple reporters were beaten while covering the Guangzhou Meishan Steel gas backflow incident.

At noon on February 23rd, a gas leak occurred at the steel plant of Meishan Steel Factory in Yuhuatai District, Guangzhou. Several reporters went to cover the story and were subsequently beaten and injured by factory security.
