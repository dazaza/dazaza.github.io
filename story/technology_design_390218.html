The fates of express delivery and online shops are intertwined - loving each other is not that simple.

by anonymous on 2011-03-25 14:39:48

Currently online shopping is prevailing. It's reported that Taobao sells on average 48,000 items per minute, and most of these items are delivered to customers through express delivery. Online stores have an overwhelming demand for logistics like wolves for lambs. Christmas, New Year’s Day, Spring Festival... one wave after another of online shopping craze has caused some private express companies to experience situations such as "warehouse explosion" and "slow delivery". On the one hand, online stores complain bitterly about private express companies; on the other hand, private express companies are the "breadwinners" of online stores. The complicated relationship between the two, which is "inextricably linked", makes people feel "a special kind of taste in their hearts".

IT Times, by You Xinfeng

Online Shopping Packages Take Over Half of the Express Market

"With the development of the IT industry, express companies have been making corresponding adjustments," Ma Youqiang, manager of Zhongtong Express Xuhui store, told the IT Times reporter. He has been working in this industry for seven years, and the development history of the express industry reflects the history of the development of e-commerce. "At first, most of our business came from companies, mainly documents and papers. Since around 2004, the volume of online shopping suddenly skyrocketed. Currently, my store sends out more than 2,000 packages every day, of which about 1,000 come from online stores." Ma Youqiang introduced that company business and online business each have their own characteristics. Generally speaking, company business does not care about price, but requires a rigorous transportation process and needs formal invoices; while online stores need volume, focus on low prices and fast speed, and do not care much about ticket issues.

"The types of express packages from online stores are diverse, electronics, clothing, books... we do them all," said Ge Xiaoyan, market manager of YTO Express Hongkou (upper section) branch. She said that about 60% to 70% of the express packages from their company are sent by Taobao sellers. Many Taobao sellers have long-term cooperation with private express companies, sending dozens or hundreds of packages every day, and can get corresponding price discounts.

Finding Online Stores Just to Save Face

Despite the increasing number of express packages from e-commerce, many managers of private express companies are indifferent. "Actually, the profit from delivering online shopping packages is not high," a manager of a private express company who wished to remain anonymous revealed that taking Taobao as an example, many online stores have private agreements with express companies and can get lower express fees. The bottom price for Shanghai area packages is 4 yuan, and the company's profit is very thin. Although the volume of goods from online stores is relatively large, they are not sent to the same place, which instead increases the transportation cost. "Considering car fuel costs, driver salaries, and other labor costs, it's already good if we make a little profit."

The manager stated that private express companies doing online shopping packages is just for saving face and maintaining appearances. "When there are more online shopping packages, the vehicles are fully loaded, giving a better impression. Moreover, the customer source is relatively stable, which is conducive to long-term development." He said that in order to attract online store customers, sometimes they would send 100 orders for free, saving customers hundreds of yuan. If they encounter long-term customers, they will "show appreciation" during holidays. When delivering express packages, the company will also specifically investigate where more orders are being sent, and proactively negotiate with store owners for contracting.

Territory Grabbing: There Are "Unspoken Rules" in the Express Industry

Old Wang started working as a courier two years ago, riding his electric scooter all over the streets. "Delivering from morning to night is not an easy job!" Old Wang told the IT Times reporter that couriers only get a 10% commission on each package delivered, usually only 5-6 jiao. "The phenomenon of couriers grabbing territories is very serious. Some experienced couriers have monopolized neighborhoods with high volumes of business. Moreover, regional branches cannot poach each other's business, they can only 'stay within their own territory'. If poaching business is discovered by the headquarters, there will be a fine of 1000 yuan per package."

With the increasing number of IT products, Old Wang is especially careful when transporting express packages. "Some electronic products can easily be damaged if not handled carefully. Extra attention must be paid when moving them up and down. After we deliver the goods, we first let the buyer sign in the 'intact packaging' column, because the packaging is our responsibility, if there is any problem with the internal goods, it is the seller's responsibility."

Making Price Differences: Both Sides Have Their Own Calculations

Courier agent Xiao Chen from Shentong Express introduced that income disparities in the courier industry are quite significant. Smart couriers will negotiate prices with customers. "For instance, a local courier with a base price of 4 yuan, the courier fee paid by the customer often ranges from 5-6 yuan to over ten yuan. The difference in price is pocketed by the courier. If the courier can persuade the customer to pay more for the courier fee, he earns more money."

Ma Youqiang also indicated that the rumors of couriers earning "ten thousand yuan a month" are not unfounded. If frequently engaged in high-end business and having relatively fixed customers, it can be achieved. "Some experienced couriers are very close with the online stores in their area. Sellers will bypass the courier company and directly contact the courier. Some couriers even pocket all the courier fees." Ma Youqiang said that although the company has some explicit regulations, due to the high turnover of agents, some employees "make a quick buck" and leave, making it difficult for the company to manage.

As for online store owners, they can also make money through the price difference in courier fees. Online stores charge individual customers in Shanghai 6-10 yuan per package for courier fees, while private express companies give the store owners internal prices of 4-5 yuan. Owners earn a few yuan for each delivery, and some small store owners don't make money from selling goods themselves, but can earn one or two thousand yuan every month from these price differences.

On Couriers: Online Store Owners Love and Hate Them

Millions of online store owners have complex feelings towards private express companies. On one hand, logistics is an indispensable part of the marketing process, and express delivery is the foundation they rely on for survival and development; on the other hand, the lagging service and quality of private express companies cannot keep up with the development of e-commerce, causing them frustration.

Online store owner Xiao Zhou told the IT Times reporter that since 2006, she and her friends have been running an online store on Taobao selling clothes. From originally just a few pieces, to now shipping nearly a hundred pieces daily, their demand for express delivery has been increasing daily. "Running an online store, besides sourcing goods, the most important thing is the logistics link. If customers have demands and the goods cannot be delivered in time, there will be big trouble. A courier company has been handling my business long-term, as long as I guarantee a certain volume of shipments, each express delivery is 1.5 yuan cheaper than the market price." Originally, the smooth logistics channel encountered problems during this year's Spring Festival. Her clothes were delayed on the road, and buyers couldn't receive them on time, which worried Xiao Zhou greatly. She kept calling the courier company for help, and it wasn't until a week after the Spring Festival that the goods were delivered.

Similarly, online store owner Xiao Li had the same feeling. Once, a journalist bought a set of interview microphones from him, saying they would be urgently needed in three days. He estimated that intra-city express generally ships on the same day and will definitely arrive the next day, but the item was delayed for a whole week without arriving, leading to the buyer rejecting the package later, causing economic and reputation losses to the online store. "Such experiences are fatal blows to everyone who runs an online store."

Regardless of how valuable the goods are, if they are not insured, compensation is generally limited to several times the courier fee after damage or loss. Incomplete laws and inability to hold responsible parties accountable often places consumers at a disadvantaged position. Mr. Wang reflected that he transported 4 boxes of health supplements to Mianyang, Sichuan via a courier company. Since the transported goods were bottled liquids, Mr. Wang specially labeled the boxes with "fragile goods, handle with care." Unexpectedly, when the goods arrived at the destination, almost all of them were damaged. After contacting the courier company, they responded that as long as the outer packaging was intact, whether the internal goods were damaged or not was unrelated to them, fragile items inherently carry risks, and consumers should take their own protective measures. Helplessly, Mr. Wang could only accept the loss himself.

Delay of courier packages, damage or loss during transportation... various problems in the courier industry have made online sellers heartbroken. On Taobao's merchant forum, they have called for large e-commerce websites to establish their own courier companies, unify management, prevent savage operations, and reduce cargo damage.

Analysys International: Courier Services Restrict the Development of E-commerce

Data shows that in 2009, the online shopping business was 258.6 billion yuan, in 2010 it exceeded 500 billion yuan, growing nearly double. According to statistics from the China Express Association, due to the promotion of online shopping, in 2010, domestic express business revenue reached 57.3 billion yuan, three times what it was five years ago. The volume of express packages received last year increased from 1.86 billion pieces in 2009 to 2.4 billion pieces. Our country has become the third country in the world where daily express processing exceeds 10 million pieces.

Logistics is an important component of the three flows in e-commerce (information flow, capital flow, and logistics), and is the last mile for the completion of e-commerce transactions, always a real-world issue that cannot be avoided. During the Spring Festival, many online stores bent over backwards for logistics, along with the previously exposed phenomena of "express flying everywhere", logistics has become a focal point.

Analysys International analyst Chen Shousong believes that from the current status of China's logistics industry, logistics cannot keep up with the pace of e-commerce, and its maturity is far from meeting the rapid development needs of e-commerce. Analysys believes that the future development of e-commerce will continue to drive the development of China's express industry, and the impact of express on the development of e-commerce will last for a long time, both sides will be in a complementary relationship for a long time.

Reporter's Notes

Same Grassroots Brothers, Why So Hurried to Attack Each Other

This year, e-commerce encountered a "boom" during the Spring Festival, and online stores vented all their anger on private express companies, with public opinion fanning the flames, pointing out various flaws, leaving private express companies looking rather disheveled. This practice of shifting responsibility is really inappropriate. If you know that the service of private express companies has flaws, why not choose international large express companies like Federal Express or UPS? If you understand the principle of "you get what you pay for", online stores shouldn't complain after getting a bargain.

In fact, online stores and private express companies have many similarities in their operating methods, both filled with "copycat" elements. If international logistics is like the advertisement says "airplanes flying in the sky... that's logistics"; then private express can be seen as "trucks running on highways, couriers dashing under the sun." Clearly, the "rustic" private express model is more suitable for the needs of online stores to transport goods, while the advantage of international logistics companies lies in overall international logistics service outsourcing for enterprises.

Similarly, many online stores are full of "original sins" in their operations, such as not paying taxes, high imitation, uneven product quality, etc., which still hinder the growth and development of online stores. The products they sell are "cheap but not necessarily of good quality." Therefore, online stores and private express companies have similar grassroots growth experiences, they are "head-to-head, toe-to-toe" brothers, and there is no reason for anyone to look down on the other.

Even more so, private express companies have taken steps towards standardization and regulation before online stores. Currently, some well-reputed and excellent private express companies have introduced many technological means to improve service quality. According to insiders, their increasingly prosperous business has even drawn the dissatisfaction of international giants, who once wanted to sue them for trademark infringement. It can be seen that the "crazy growth" of private express has already made international giants restless. In comparison, online stores should think more about their own path to standardized development.
