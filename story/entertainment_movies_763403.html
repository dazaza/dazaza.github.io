Introduction:

Dylan Thomas was born in 1914 in the UK. His poetry is acclaimed for its high literary quality, earning him the title of a genius poet. However, his private life has also been controversial. This film depicts his emotional entanglements with the two most important women in his life. Dylan and Vera were once childhood sweethearts. Years later, Vera married and became a singer, while Dylan wed the bold Caitlin. When they meet again, their love rekindles. After Vera's husband William is deployed abroad to fight, Vera and Dylan become entangled again. Upon William's return and discovery of this affair, he angrily grabs a submachine gun to seek revenge on his rival. Fortunately, there are no casualties. However, due to William's retaliation, Dylan and Vera's affair becomes the talk of the town, with locals even believing that Dylan's wife Caitlin and Vera have a homosexual relationship, maintaining a mysterious triangular relationship with these two women...

Behind-the-scenes Production:

Sealed Old Affairs:

In the Cardigan Bay area near New Quay in South Wales, UK, William Curreck climbed onto the roof of a bungalow. Inside, British genius poet Dylan Thomas was partying with friends, while his two-year-old daughter slept soundly in the next room. Curreck fired a shot through the window, then went inside and fired another shot, but before he could reveal the grenade that could blow up the entire house, he was arrested on charges of attempted murder.

Curreck had every reason to complain about the injustice of fate. He was sent to Greece to carry out special operations, fighting bravely on the front lines for 18 months. Just when he returned home, he heard rumors that his wife Vera and his childhood sweetheart Thomas were having a passionate affair. To make matters worse, all the money in Curreck's bank account had been emptied to pay for Thomas's extravagant lifestyle of wine and women. In order to protect veterans, the British Veterans Committee stood up for William, resulting in his acquittal due to insufficient evidence after his failed attempt to retaliate against Dylan.

"When you return from the front line and suddenly find yourself with nothing, and you're still bleeding for your country, this is clear enough," said Rebecca Gilbert. You should also know one thing: William and Vera are her grandfather and grandmother. The whole family rarely mentioned this scandal. Rebecca said, "This matter always seemed somewhat mysterious. My elders once reached a consensus never to mention it again. My grandfather felt that since he had five daughters, he didn't want them to think of him as someone who tried to kill Dylan Thomas." Now, the long silence is finally about to explode. Gilbert has just produced her first film, "The Edge of Love," which tells precisely this story.

Revised Story:

"The Edge of Love" is directed by British director John Maybury and stars Keira Knightley, Sienna Miller, Cillian Murphy, and Matthew Rhys. The script is written by Knightley's mother, renowned screenwriter Sharon Macdonald, originally titled "The Best Time of Our Lives." The film premiered at the Edinburgh International Film Festival in Scotland. As for the costumes, scripts, and props used during filming, they are currently on display at the Dylan Thomas Center in Swansea, Wales, UK, and the exhibition will continue until September 2008.

After a brief stint in Hollywood where he only made one supernatural thriller, "The Jacket," director John Maybury returned to his hometown of London, UK, to shoot this new work. The plan for the film was set in early 2007, including three popular British actors returning from Hollywood to shoot in the UK. Additionally, American actress Lindsay Lohan was confirmed to join, but just before the official start of shooting at the end of April, she was replaced by Sienna Miller. Despite this, Lohan couldn't help but reveal the plot to MTV: "Keira is older than me, but she has a special relationship with my husband, and there are some lesbian tendencies in the story." Screenwriter Macdonald denied this, insisting that the plot would not include any lesbian elements.

Set against the backdrop of 1940s London, this is a love story between wartime and intimacy under blackout conditions. The obese poet Thomas occasionally meets his long-lost lover Vera Philip in a bar. This movie doesn't focus too much on Thomas, but rather on the two important women in his life. The film aims to express not about the artist, but the timeless great friendship between these two women.

Initially, the plan was to make "The Edge of Love" into a biographical film about Dylan Thomas, but producer Gilbert believed that focusing on the most unforgettable love of someone's life would move audiences more. Gilbert introduced: "My interest lies not in making a biographical or documentary film, but in placing these four characters against the backdrop of World War II to explore the emotions that drive and bind them." As for the characters William and Vera in the film, they are no longer her grandparents, but two roles jointly created by everyone. Screenwriter Macdonald agreed with her idea.

Matthew, who plays Dylan, also agrees: "It will be wonderful to shoot like this, and this period happens to be the best time in Dylan Thomas' life. If the film is to tell this improper love well, then the script must make this controversial artist appear more human."

About Knightley:

More than twenty years ago, Knightley's mother Macdonald was also an actress, but due to her innate stage fright, she eventually chose to retreat behind the scenes and successfully transitioned into a well-known screenwriter. She began writing the script for this film six years ago when she first met Rebecca, while Knightley was involved in the shooting plans for "Pirates of the Caribbean: The Curse of the Black Pearl" (2003). Before writing, her husband Will Knightley did meticulous research, although Macdonald initially wasn't very willing to write this script due to multiple tasks at hand, she reluctantly continued to avoid disappointing her husband. During the writing of "The Edge of Love" script, she traveled many places: from Los Angeles, to the Bahamas, to New York, and France.

There are rumors that it was Keira Knightley who worked hard to convince John Maybury to direct the film. Their first collaboration was completed in Hollywood on "The Jacket." Prior to this, Knightley's mother Macdonald recommended that she watch another of Maybury's films, "Love Is the Devil: Study for a Portrait of Francis Bacon" (1998), leading her to greatly admire this director. During the shooting of "The Jacket," Knightley was already reading the script for "The Edge of Love," firmly believing that Maybury was the perfect choice to direct the film. Mother and daughter first praised him with poems, then presented him with champagne and fine food, and after four years, Maybury finally took up the script.

Regarding the character Vera she portrays, Knightley believes she didn't perform based on a real person, but entirely according to the description in her mother's script. On set, there was another person present every day - producer Rebecca Gilbert. Getting the recognition and support of this granddaughter of Curreck was a great encouragement. Since this is a fictional story, there's no need to stick to history and real people, thus giving the character image more distinct characteristics. Regarding her ability to speak fluent Welsh dialect in the film, Knightley proudly attributes it to her mother. Half of her family comes from Wales, and when she was little, her mother read fairy tales to her, where the wizards and witches in the books spoke with a Welsh accent. Living in Swansea, Wales, for 18 months and receiving guidance from a language teacher made it easy for Knightley to master this accent.

Fun Facts:

- While Sharon Macdonald envisioned her daughter Keira Knightley playing the role of the poet's wife Caitlin when creating the script, Knightley ended up becoming the lead but not as Caitlin Thomas, but as Thomas's lover Vera Philip.

- Poet Dylan Thomas loved movies immensely and wrote a poem about cinema. During WWII, he produced numerous documentaries and wrote several movie scripts, though none were filmed during his lifetime.

- British writer Kingsley Amis wrote in the preface of a Dylan Thomas collection published in 1991: "Thomas was an extremely unpleasant man, deceiving and stealing from his friends, and urinating on their carpets."

- Keira Knightley made her singing debut onscreen for the movie "The Edge of Love" by performing the song "Blue Tahitian Moon." She later stated, "Since I can't sing, I specially studied with a vocal teacher. The singing was performed live on set, with 100 extras present, and director John Maybury simply said to start singing now. I've never been so nervous in my life."

- The original music for the film was composed by Italian maestro Angelo Badalamenti. To date, he has composed themes and background music for 58 films, including multiple collaborations with David Lynch such as "Blue Velvet," "Wild at Heart," the "Twin Peaks" series, and "Mulholland Drive," as well as Jane Campion's "Holy Smoke" and Jean-Pierre Jeunet's "A Very Long Engagement."

- After watching the film, Dylan Thomas's daughter Aeronwy found the overly glamorous costumes of the characters amusing, despite acknowledging the aesthetic demands of commercial cinema. She recalled that her childhood home was a small, dilapidated wooden house by the sea, where everyone wore rough and shabby clothes, and her father spent almost his entire life in poverty.
