What are the reasons for blood streaks in leucorrhea?

Many women become very nervous when they notice blood streaks in their leucorrhea, suspecting that there might be a problem with their health, which makes them worried. Professional gynecological experts remind us: blood streaks in leucorrhea are not necessarily always caused by physical illnesses. Under normal circumstances, some women may experience normal ovulation bleeding during the ovulation period, also known as mid-cycle bleeding, which refers to slight bleeding between two regular menstrual cycles. These women may also experience varying degrees of lower abdominal pain. Generally speaking, these symptoms are mild and last for less than one or two days, so such women do not need treatment. However, if the bleeding is heavy or the lower abdominal pain is significant, attention should be paid because this could be caused by pathological gynecological diseases, and timely treatment should be sought.

Gynecological hospital experts introduce that the pathological reasons for blood streaks in leucorrhea could be due to cervical polyps, acute vaginitis, severe cervical erosion, submucosal uterine fibroids, cervical cancer, etc. Additionally, sometimes women with intrauterine contraceptive devices may also have some blood streaks in their leucorrhea. Once blood streaks are noticed in the leucorrhea, especially after sexual intercourse when blood is mixed in the leucorrhea, one should be alert to the possibility of malignant tumors in the reproductive organs. It is recommended that women promptly visit a hospital for examination and then take targeted treatment measures.

Gynecological experts explain that when women are pregnant, the vaginal pH balance may be disrupted, and immunity decreases, making it easy to develop gynecological inflammations such as vaginitis, cervicitis, and cervical erosion. The uterus, where the fetus develops, is connected to the vagina through the cervical canal. If necessary examinations and treatments are not conducted before an abortion surgery, pathogenic bacteria may be directly introduced into the uterine cavity, leading to various inflammations such as endometritis, pelvic inflammatory disease, adnexal inflammation, and salpingitis. Patients may experience high fever and abdominal pain, and severe cases can even be life-threatening. Moreover, these conditions can easily cause fallopian tube blockage, hindering the meeting and fertilization of eggs and sperm, leading to infertility.

Gynecological experts remind us: blood streaks in leucorrhea can be divided into two situations. If women experience this during their ovulation period, they can first observe carefully. If it's just bloody leucorrhea with mild lower abdominal discomfort lasting only one or two days, there’s no need to worry. However, if the symptoms are severe, last too long, or have an unusual odor, please seek examination and treatment at a professional gynecological hospital promptly because this could likely be caused by gynecological inflammation.
