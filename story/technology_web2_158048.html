How to check the content of mobile text messages has become the biggest obstacle in front of us. When you need to use the content of mobile text messages as evidence, it is important to confirm the identity of the sender and retain the original content of the text message. The principle of sending a text message by mobile or Unicom is that the sender first sends the text message content upstream to the SMS gateway of the telecommunications operator (mobile, Unicom, Netcom, Telecom, etc.), and after instant processing, it is sent downstream to the receiver's mobile phone. Therefore, theoretically speaking, the content of the text message can be checked at the telecommunications operator, but for the purpose of "protecting user privacy", they all claim that the content cannot be queried. Just like Netcom claims that it can only query the long-distance list within 3 months and cannot query the local call list, while in fact, the continuous 3-year local call list can also be queried, but it is not open to users. Once it is opened, it will not only cause a loss of economic interests for the telecommunications operators, but also the user's privacy cannot be guaranteed, which will lead to legal litigation and other issues. If your messages are stored on the SIM card, then if you go to the service hall, the service personnel there might have a way, but you can only query your own mobile text message content. What about when facing the need to check someone else's mobile text message content?
