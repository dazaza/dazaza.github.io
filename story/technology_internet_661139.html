Methods for measuring the equivalent salt density of insulators

by zhihao2033 on 2012-02-08 16:34:04

Methods for measuring insulator equivalent salt density:

Definition of insulator equivalent salt density: The distribution density of soluble salts on the surface of the insulator, equivalent to the mass of NaCl, with units of mg/cm² (milligrams per square centimeter).

Complete testing steps (using YM3200 direct-reading salinity meter as an example):

1. Sampling:

Use a dedicated moistened gauze or cotton to wipe the surface of the insulator. Each insulator is generally wiped 2-3 times until the surface is clean. Place the sampled gauze or cotton into deionized water.

Tips:

(a) If the insulator string has not been removed but must be handled during power-off, place the wiped insulator in a self-sealing bag to ensure no solution loss. Dissolve the sample after returning to the laboratory.

(b) When the insulator string has been removed, avoid sampling the topmost and bottommost insulators, as their surfaces can easily be damaged during removal from the high-voltage tower, potentially affecting measurement results.

2. Dissolution:

Solvent: It is recommended to use deionized water or distilled water. If unavailable, ordinary tap water may be used, preferably with a conductivity less than 100 µS/cm.

Amount: For standard ordinary insulators, each piece requires about 300 ml of water. However, if the surface area of the measured insulator differs from the standard type, adjust the water amount proportionally according to the insulator's surface area, as shown in the table below.

3. Measurement:

First measurement: Measure the current solvent's conductivity and set this value as the baseline (see function description). Subsequent measurements will automatically subtract this baseline value, improving accuracy and reducing solvent requirements. This step must be performed before placing the sampled gauze in the water.

Second measurement: Use the above moistened gauze or filter paper to clean the dirt off the insulator surface, then immerse it in the water. Immerse the electrode in the test solution, gently stir the solution with the electrode, and wait for a stable period (about 2 minutes) before reading the conductivity, salt density, and temperature measurement results. After testing, you can print and save the results.
