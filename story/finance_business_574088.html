What kind of man can be considered truly healthy? Recently, after a long period of effort, more than a dozen health experts in the United States published a list. The experts pointed out that if a man could meet 7 out of these 11 criteria, he would qualify as a "truly healthy" man.

1. He has high levels of HDL

Globally, about 356,000 men die from heart disease each year, which is twice the number for cancer. "If there is sufficient HDL (high-density lipoprotein) in the blood, then you basically don't need to worry," said cardiovascular expert Dr. Eckert. "HDL has the function of cleaning arteries, and if it reaches or exceeds 90 mg/dL, serious clots rarely occur." A 21-year follow-up survey of 8,586 men in Israel confirmed this view: men with the highest HDL levels have the lowest risk of dying from strokes. To increase his HDL level, he needs at least four moderate-intensity exercises per week, each lasting 20 minutes. The normal HDL level in human blood is around 40 mg/dL, and ideally, it should reach twice that number.

2. He has two close friends

These two friends should not be his current colleagues, neighbors, fitness coaches, etc., in short, they should not be anyone who regularly appears in his life. Psychologist Dr. Sherod believes that if a man over 35 meets this standard, it indicates that he has healthy social connections and will live longer than two-thirds of men. This means he builds friendships based on common interests rather than geographical proximity, work benefits, or life conveniences—these fleeting and changeable factors that make most men become utilitarian and worldly after the age of 40.

3. He answers "yes" to the following two questions

The two questions are: "Do you look forward to going to work every morning?" and "Do you look forward to coming home every afternoon?" After a 30-year practical study, Dr. Grayburn found that people who answered affirmatively had a very low risk of dying from heart disease.

4. PSA value below 2.5 ng/mL

PSA stands for prostate-specific antigen. Prostate cancer expert Dr. Hetrona pointed out that this indicator shows that his risk of prostate cancer is relatively low.

5. Ejaculation ability

After an exciting session in bed, his ejaculation volume should reach 5 milliliters. Dr. Hetrona believed that this is the most direct manifestation of a healthy prostate. A simple measurement method is to pour 5 milliliters of water into a clean condom, remember the position of the water, and next time you can estimate the ejaculation volume.

6. Walk 3 kilometers within 28 minutes

Being able to maintain a brisk and powerful pace for a considerable amount of time is a truly good way to measure physical health. According to the conclusion of Swedish researchers, a healthy 40-year-old man should be able to walk 3 kilometers within 28 minutes, equivalent to walking 134 steps per minute with a normal stride.

7. Balanced muscle strength

Most men are enthusiastic about excessive external pushing exercises for their muscles, such as push-ups, but fewer men have balanced muscle strength. Ideally, the strength ratio of his biceps to triceps should be 1:1, meaning if he can pull a 25 kg resistance machine 10 times, he should also be able to push a 25 kg thrust machine 10 times; the strength ratio of his quadriceps to hamstring should be 3:2, meaning if he can lift his legs 10 times with a weight of 30 kg, he can press down a weight of 20 kg with his legs exactly 10 times. If this test reveals that his muscle strength is unbalanced, then he should strengthen the weaker muscles.

8. Symmetrical skull proportions

Clinically, the optimal facial width is 60% of its length. Men with such standard skulls not only have handsome appearances but also have good immunity against respiratory, jaw, and dental diseases. Moreover, they tend to have excellent interpersonal relationships, and even children will approach them as "good uncles." Take out a tape measure and measure him (length); the second step is to measure the distance between his ears (width), then divide the width by the length, and subtract this number from 100. For example, if his face is 23 cm long and 14 cm wide, then 14 ÷ 23 = 0.61, which is 60%, and 100% - 60% = 40%.

9. Quick uphill climb

Dr. Grayburn said: "'Bruce protocol' is a method to test heart strength, the most accurate, and simultaneously the cruelest." It's very simple to do: let him walk on a treadmill, gradually increasing the tilt of the treadmill until he can no longer take another step. "Most men aged 30-40 can last 9 minutes, and reaching 12 minutes is quite remarkable," summarized Dr. Grayburn. However, our standard is higher: 15 minutes, which means excellent cardiovascular health. You can replicate this test at home, but if his heart has had problems before, you must get the doctor's approval, as the intensity of this test is enough to trigger a heart attack. The method is: speed at 5 km/h; then gradually increase the incline, adding 5 cm every 3 minutes (if your treadmill cannot adjust the incline, you can also increase the speed by 1.5 km/h every 3 minutes instead). Most well-built men start sweating after walking for 7 minutes.

10. Successfully threading a needle in one attempt

This means he has very good depth perception and three-dimensional acuity. If he can't thread a needle or always misses catching a ball when playing, he should consider going to an ophthalmologist for a Titmus test — measuring the three-dimensional resolution of the eyes, which can diagnose amblyopia. Additionally, glaucoma testing should be done specifically during annual eye examinations. Glaucoma is a disease that causes increased intraocular pressure and damage to ocular tissues; 100 million people in the world are gradually losing their vision due to undiagnosed glaucoma, so this examination is very necessary.

11. Perfect left-right symmetry

Take a soft tape measure and measure the circumference of his wrists, ears, and nostrils. If these places are symmetrical left and right, or differ by less than 1 mm, he is a born athlete. A study conducted by Liverpool scientists on 50 men found that the most physically symmetrical men ran the fastest. The more balanced the body, the better the self-control during intense exercise.
