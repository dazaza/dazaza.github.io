Three junior high school students forced their classmate to jump from a bridge and got disabled

by wdrt4yse on 2012-03-02 09:40:10

By Wang Le of Jinjian Morning Post

Hearing representatives approve the decision not to prosecute

At 9:20 AM, the three children involved in the incident - Wang Ming, Zhang Xin, and Lin Hui - were brought into the hearing venue. "Since June this year, after the prosecution initiated the conditional non-prosecution procedure, Wang Ming and the other two have shown good behavior during the probation period and actively participated in school public welfare activities," the prosecutor introduced. The parents of Wang Ming and others have actively compensated, gaining the forgiveness of Xiao Lin and his family.

Last December, after a verbal altercation with Xiao Lin, Wang Ming and the other two extorted 400 yuan from Xiao Lin at the school gate and used a pocketknife they carried to rob him of another 50 yuan. Fearing for his life, Xiao Lin fled and jumped off the Wukuai Stone Overpass, resulting in a grade 9 disability. Afterwards, the parents of Wang Ming and the others provided 100,000 yuan in economic compensation to Xiao Lin.

Xiao Lin's father also spoke at the scene, acknowledging the performance of the three children: "Their attitude towards admitting guilt is good, and their families have actively compensated. As long as they can take this as a lesson and refrain from committing any more illegal acts, I am willing to forgive them."

The decision on whether to prosecute was heard yesterday

At 9:00 AM yesterday, the hearing began. In addition to the hearing delegation composed of representatives from all sectors of society, there was also a group of "special representatives" present - they were the victims or the parents of the victims from six criminal cases.

After careful deliberation, the hearing representatives reached a unified opinion: they agree with the Jin Niu Procuratorate's decision not to prosecute Wang Ming and the other two.

It is understood that cases applicable for conditional non-prosecution are clearly divided into two categories: one category involves minor criminal cases where the statutory penalty is less than three years imprisonment; the other category involves cases where the statutory penalty is more than three years imprisonment, but the suspects possess multiple legal circumstances for leniency or mitigation of punishment, allowing for a possible sentence of less than three years imprisonment, making them eligible for conditional non-prosecution.

(All individuals mentioned in the article are pseudonyms)

A 14-year-old boy named Wang Ming colluded with two other young companions, using a pocketknife to extort 400 yuan from his classmate Xiao Lin. Out of fear, Xiao Lin fled and jumped off the Wukuai Stone Overpass, resulting in a grade 9 disability... After taking over the case, the Jin Niu Procuratorate learned that the parents of Wang Ming and the other two had already actively compensated. Considering the young age of the three and their continued schooling, the procuratorate initiated the conditional non-prosecution procedure and set a four-month probation period.
