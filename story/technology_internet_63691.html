Google joins hands with CCTV to release the global torch route map_I want IP traffic network

by 51ipw on 2008-04-17 21:15:01

After the completion of the lighting ceremony in Athens, Greece and the handover ceremony in Beijing at the end of March this year, the 2008 global torch relay has already started its four-month relay journey through 134 cities around the world to fulfill its mission of spreading messages of friendship and peace globally. For this purpose, Google has teamed up with CCTV.com, the official Internet broadcasting agency of this event, to launch a simplified Chinese version of the "2008 Global Torch Relay Coverage Route Map" (test version). Simplified Chinese, traditional Chinese, as well as English, French, Spanish, Italian, Dutch and other five language versions will also be launched globally soon, so that people from all over the world can track the torch relay route, tour all the cities along the relay route, and watch the relay videos from each location online provided by CCTV.com via Google Maps products and technology platforms.

Chinese users can access this route map through http://ditu.google.cn/torchrelay/. They can also download the complete set of landmarks for the 134 relay cities on this page, and experience the amazing feeling of viewing the relay route and cities from an altitude of 6,000 kilometers in space using the Google Earth software.

Highlights of this route map:

1. Full route tracking: You will be able to clearly see the entire relay route and arrival dates, with the red route representing the part that the relay has already passed.

2. City details: Click on each city point on the map or the city list on the right side, a small window will open where you can learn about the basic situation of this city on the relay route, browse city landscape pictures, and see detailed maps of this city layer by layer. By clicking on the relevant link such as "more city pictures", you can also directly see the results of searching for images of this city on Google.

3. Local relay video: The most exciting part is, by clicking on the video tab in the small window, you can watch the relay report video of this city provided by CCTV.com online. Note that the video will only be updated after the relay takes place; before that, what you see will be related promotional videos or historical video materials of the city.

4. Embed the route map into your own blog: You can also embed this interactive route map that updates in real-time into your own website or blog by copying and pasting a piece of code to share with more people.

5. Save the route map to Google's "My Maps" collection: The same map can also be browsed directly on Google Maps, divided into the relay route segment in mainland China and the global route segment. After opening the previous link, click on the "Save to My Maps" link on the page. Next time you visit the Google Maps website, you will still see this collection.
