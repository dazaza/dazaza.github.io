Methods for Sobering Up - Ten Secrets to Help You Handle Situations Gracefully

The year-end social obligations can be truly headache-inducing. Especially the Chinese custom of "no alcohol, no banquet" causes even more anxiety. How can one drink to build relationships without harming their health?

As one gathering follows another before the New Year, especially for many business professionals who cannot avoid these year-end social obligations, you might have to grit your teeth and down a few glasses even if you really don’t want to. How can you prevent getting drunk? Here are a few tricks that might help.

Highly Effective Methods for Sobering Up:

1. Before drinking, have a glass of milk or eat a few slices of bread. Avoid drinking on an empty stomach to prevent irritation of the stomach lining.

2. If you estimate that you will be drinking heavily, take B vitamins beforehand until the social engagements taper off to protect your liver. You can also consciously consume more foods rich in B vitamins, such as animal livers, beef, lamb, egg yolks, vegetables, oats, and other whole grains, to increase the level of B vitamins in your body.

3. When drinking white liquor, drink more water to help the alcohol leave your body quickly through urination. When drinking beer, go to the restroom frequently. When drinking strong spirits, it’s best to add ice cubes.

4. Don’t drink too quickly or forcefully; sip slowly to give your body time to break down the ethanol. Drinking penalty shots or gulping drinks at the table can easily lead to intoxication.

5. Eat more animal livers like pork liver while drinking to enhance your body's ability to detoxify ethanol.

6. Eat more leafy greens while drinking, as their antioxidants and vitamins can protect the liver.

7. Eat more soy products while drinking, as the lecithin they contain has a protective effect on the liver.

8. Avoid drinking when you're tired.

9. Do not drink after taking medication, especially after taking sleeping pills, sedatives, or cold medicine.

10. Do not drink carbonated beverages like cola or soda while drinking alcohol, as this can accelerate the body's absorption of alcohol.

Common Misconceptions About Sobering Up:

1. Drinking Strong Tea to Sober Up

The popular belief that drinking strong tea can sober you up lacks scientific basis. While the tea polyphenols in tea leaves do have some liver-protective effects, the caffeine in strong tea can cause blood vessels to constrict and raise blood pressure, which can exacerbate headaches. After getting drunk, you can drink some light tea but should avoid strong tea.

2. Taking a Shower After Drinking

Taking a hot shower after drinking can speed up blood circulation, causing increased energy consumption within the body, which can easily lead to low blood sugar. Hypertensive and cardiovascular disease patients may experience strokes after drinking and taking a shower, so it’s better to rest a bit first.

Sobering-Up Tricks:

1. Eating Fruit

If someone drinks too much involuntarily, they can eat some fruit afterward or drink some fruit juice, as the acidic components in fruits and juices can neutralize alcohol.

2. Eating Steamed Bread

Many people skip meals after drinking, which is even more harmful. It's better to eat some easily digestible starchy foods, like steamed bread or noodles. Steamed bread can convert into glucose, aiding in supplying blood to the body and increasing stamina. Since it undergoes a fermentation process, it also has a neutralizing effect on stomach acid, making the body feel better immediately after eating.
