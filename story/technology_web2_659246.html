Migrant Couple Trapped in Small Workshop, Working Over 12 Hours a Day (Photo Gallery)

by anonymous on 2012-02-05 22:40:13

The "agreement" signed by Zhang and Lu when they started working at the small workshop. The guarantee letter written by Zhang and Lu when they were redeemed by their families.

By our reporter in Baoding, Guo Zhikun (text and photos)

On April 27 this year, two young people around 20 years old were introduced by a labor intermediary company to a family-style luggage workshop in Songxin Village, Sizhuang Town, Gaobeidian City. On July 10, nearly two and a half months later, when their families tried to take them home through the local police station, the workshop owner demanded 2500 yuan as so-called "breach of contract fee."

After leaving Gaobeidian with immense indignation, the two young people and their families came to our newspaper and told the reporter about their inhuman experiences.

The Working Couple Trapped in the Small Workshop

Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting are a young couple. Their hometowns are Tang County and Quyang County respectively.

On April 27 this year, the two lost their mobile phones in Shijiazhuang, and all the money they had was gone. To make a living, these inexperienced young people walked into a nameless small labor intermediary office near the train station. The intermediary staff said they could introduce work for the two, working 6 to 10 hours a day, with a monthly salary of 1000 to 1500 yuan. They felt this treatment was acceptable and agreed. That afternoon, a 30-year-old boss from Gaobeidian named Wang Hua drove to Shijiazhuang and took them to a family-style luggage workshop inside Songxin Village, Sizhuang Town, Gaobeidian City. The boss said they would be processing schoolbags for students and informed them not to leave the gate unless accompanied by the boss's family or someone else.

Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting said that the next day they began working. In more than two months, the two did not have a single day off. Every day, they had to sew 200 to 300 backpacks, working from 8 am to 8 pm. Lu Tingting could just finish her quota, while Zhang Hao, being slower, had to work until 9 pm, 10 pm, or even later. During this time, they borrowed only 268 yuan from the boss to buy clothes, toiletries, and medicine. They wanted to contact their families but were not allowed, and the door of this house was often locked even during the day, making it impossible for them to go out.

Family Spends Money to "Redeem" the Two

On July 9, Lu Tingting asked a kind aunt to help her buy a phone card and secretly charged an old mobile phone. Then at night on July 9, she called Zhang Hao's family and asked them to come quickly to Gaobeidian to rescue them.

On the afternoon of July 10, after much trouble, Zhang Hao's father Zhang Hongyu and his mother and brother finally found the Sizhuang Police Station of the Gaobeidian Public Security Bureau. After hearing the situation, two police officers drove them to Songxin Village and found the luggage workshop where Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting worked. Inside this family workshop, the police let Zhang Hao's mother negotiate with the boss, Wang Hua. Wang Hua proposed that Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting had agreed to work there for a year, but now they had only worked for two months. According to the employment agreement, the two had to pay 6000 yuan as a breach of contract fee. After much negotiation, ultimately, after Zhang Hao's mother paid 2500 yuan as a so-called breach of contract fee, the workshop boss Wang Hua agreed to let Zhang Hongyu take Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting away.

The Result of the Police Station's Handling Was "Satisfied"

In the morning of July 12, I followed Zhang Hao, Lu Tingting, and Zhang Hongyu to Gaobeidian to report the situation to relevant departments. Under the guidance of the staff from the Publicity Department of the Gaobeidian Municipal Committee, Director Liu Huixiang and Captain Gao Jinhua of the Labor Supervision Team of the Gaobeidian Human Resources and Social Security Bureau received us.

Liu Huixiang and Gao Jinhua said that the bureau had already dispatched labor supervision law enforcement personnel to inspect the involved luggage workshop. Subsequently, the two led the reporter to the Sizhuang Police Station and met Deputy Chief Dong who handled the matter at the time.

Deputy Chief Dong introduced that he took Zhang Hongyu et al. to that luggage workshop and let him negotiate with the workshop boss Wang Hua. He also let Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting sign separate guarantees: "Introduced by the Shijiazhuang intermediary, we came to work at Wang Hua's home in Songxin Village, Sizhuang Town, Gaobeidian. Now we voluntarily return home with our parents. From today onwards, any incident is unrelated to Wang Hua. We are very satisfied with the handling result of the police station." The police did this because both sides of the employment had a one-year employment agreement, and the two workers voluntarily paid the breach of contract fee to the boss.

Chief Ma of the Sizhuang Police Station said that if local workers want to leave the company and the company does not want to let them go, the police station should first ensure the personal safety of the workers and ensure their safe departure. If there are other labor disputes, then both sides need to negotiate.

Regarding this, Zhang Hongyu expressed that they went to the Sizhuang Police Station for help, but the police station let them negotiate with the boss. As outsiders, they only wished to leave this place as soon as possible. And during the negotiation process hosted by the police station, his son Zhang Hao almost got beaten. Later, when they arrived at the police station, the police took out the pre-written "guarantee" and made the two young people sign it. Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting also told the reporter that they did not agree with the content of the guarantee.

The Boss and His Family Loudly Insulted the Working Couple

While the reporter was interviewing at the police station, the labor supervision law enforcement personnel who went to conduct an employment inspection at the luggage workshop in Songxin Village also arrived at the Sizhuang Police Station. They claimed that the workshop's door was tightly closed, and after knocking for a long time, no one opened the door, so they left. The reporter insisted on going together with the labor supervision law enforcement personnel and the police officers to the workplace site to understand the situation. Upon arrival, it was seen that the courtyard gate of the involved workshop was wide open, and several people were eating inside.

Boss Wang Hua and his father introduced to the reporter and the law enforcement personnel that all their employees were legally employed. They provided food and accommodation, implemented piecework wages, with worker salaries ranging from 800 to 1500 yuan, and working hours generally being 10 hours. If the workers were slow, their working hours would be longer. They implemented an annual wage system, meaning wages would only be settled after working for a full year, and within a year, only basic personal expenses could be given to the workers.

When the reporter proposed to inquire about the situation from the workers, Wang Hua and his father claimed that there were only 10 workers in their home, and seven of them had run away due to hearing that people from the town had come, leaving only three behind.

Just as the reporter and the law enforcement personnel were asking the remaining workers about the situation, sudden loud quarrels were heard outside the courtyard. Fearing unexpected incidents, the reporter quickly ran out with the law enforcement personnel. It turned out that boss Wang Hua and his parents were loudly cursing Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting hiding in the car, scaring them pale and unable to move. To ensure the safety of the two, the reporter proposed to immediately leave Wang Hua's home. In the car, the reporter learned that Zhang Hao and Lu Tingting had learned at Wang Hua's doorstep that Wang Hua's family had taken the other seven workers away by car, not because "the workers themselves were scared away." Wang Hua's family saw the two investigating these matters and cursed them loudly.

The reporter also contacted a Shanxi-born worker named Ye Ningning who had just left Wang Hua's home. Ye Ningning said on the phone that he had worked for Wang Hua for eight months. A few days ago, when he proposed that he did not want to continue working, Wang Hua grabbed his hair and slapped him a few times before sending him away. In those eight months, he only received 1800 yuan for personal living expenses, and besides that, he did not receive a single cent of his wages.

Feeling the arrogance of the box workshop boss again and thinking about the experience of his own children, Zhang Hongyu angrily said, "I didn't expect that after liberation for over 60 years, how could 'indentured laborers' appear again?!"
