Structural design method of corrugated steel web composite box girder

1 Introduction

With the increasingly mature extrados prestressed technology and the development of new building materials, engineers in many countries are studying the lightweight problem of main girders for long-span bridges. In the 1980s, France first designed and built a new type of composite structure bridge - Cognac Bridge, which replaced the concrete web of the box girder with a corrugated steel web marketing software [http://www.xinge001.com]. Subsequently, several other composite structure bridges with corrugated steel webs were built, including Maupre Viaduct, Asterix Bridge, and Dole Bridge. Since the 1990s, Japan has also studied this type of bridge, and based on the reference to similar French bridges, successively constructed a series of bridges such as Shinaka Bridge, Motoya Bridge, and Matsugi No.7 Bridge, which include continuous beam bridges and continuous rigid frame bridges, expanding their application scope marketing software [http://www.xinge001.com], and developing design and construction technologies.

Corrugated steel plate, or folded steel plate, has high shear buckling strength. Using it as the web of a concrete box girder not only fully satisfies the mechanical performance requirements of the web but also significantly reduces the self-weight of the main girder, thereby reducing the upper dead load borne by the lower structures, including the foundation. It also eliminates the complicated work of arranging reinforcement bars and setting forms inside the web during construction. Additionally, the longitudinal free expansion and contraction characteristics of the corrugated steel plate make it almost resistant to axial forces, allowing more effective prestressing of the concrete deck slab marketing software [http://www.xinge001.com], thus improving the efficiency of prestressing. This composite structure can reduce the amount of work, shorten the construction period, lower costs, and is very attractive in terms of construction performance and economic performance.

2 Design Method

When the superstructure of a bridge adopts the structural form of a prestressed concrete composite box girder with a corrugated steel web, like an ordinary reinforced concrete box girder bridge, its design needs to address different requirements at the construction and service stages. The calculations at the construction stage should be combined with specific construction methods. For example, continuous beam bridges can adopt cantilever construction, push-launching construction, or other methods. The main calculation loads include self-weight, prestress, shrinkage and creep of concrete at different ages, construction loads, etc. During the service stage, vehicle loads, wind loads, temperature loads, etc., need to be considered. Prestressing is usually provided both internally and externally within the box girder. Internal prestressing resists construction loads and dead loads, while external prestressing resists live loads. One reason for this consideration is to meet the force requirements of the structure when replacing external prestressing strands.

2.1 Longitudinal Bending Calculation

Under axial force, the axial deformation of the corrugated steel web is large, showing a small equivalent elastic modulus. The longitudinal equivalent elastic modulus of the corrugated steel plate is related to the plate thickness and the corrugation shape, and can be calculated by the following formula:

Ex = αE(t/h)^2 (1)

where Ex is the equivalent axial elastic modulus; E is the elastic modulus of the steel; t is the thickness of the steel plate; α is the shape factor of the corrugation. According to this formula, for the newly opened bridge in Japan, Ex = E/617. Model experiments and finite element calculations have further confirmed that the longitudinal normal stress and strain of the corrugated steel web under bending are small and can be neglected. Thus, in performing section bending resistance design, only the effects of the concrete top slab and bottom slab are considered, and it is approximately assumed that the longitudinal normal strain in the concrete top slab and bottom slab follows a linear distribution law, still calculating stress and arranging prestressing strands according to the flat section assumption.

2.2 Torsional Resistance Calculation

Under eccentric loads, the cross-section of the box girder will undergo torsional deformation. 2.4 Buckling Stability Calculation of Corrugated Steel Web

There are mainly three modes of buckling failure for corrugated steel webs.

(1) Local Buckling Mode

The phenomenon where a certain segment of the corrugated steel web experiences buckling failure.

(2) Overall Buckling Mode

The phenomenon where the entire corrugated steel web experiences buckling failure.

(3) Composite Buckling Mode

The phenomenon where the corrugated steel web simultaneously experiences local buckling failure and overall buckling failure, representing a buckling mode intermediate between local and overall buckling.

2.5 Connection of Corrugated Steel Web with Concrete Top Slab and Bottom Slab

Model tests show that in the later stages of loading, besides cracking of the bottom slab in the transverse direction, cracks appear longitudinally at the intersection of the corrugated steel web and the bottom slab. As the cracks develop, the stiffness of the structure rapidly decreases, ultimately leading to failure, characterized by the fragmentation of the connection between the web and the bottom slab. The connection between the corrugated steel web and the concrete top slab and bottom slab directly affects the load-bearing capacity of the structure and is a very critical aspect in designing this type of bridge.

For the design of the connection, a common practice is to weld steel flange plates at the upper and lower ends of the corrugated steel web. Shear studs are welded onto the flange plates to connect them with the concrete slab. Another method involves drilling holes in the steel web, passing reinforcing bars through them, and then welding longitudinal constraint bars at the upper and lower ends of the steel plate before embedding them into the concrete slab. On this basis, other connection methods can also be derived.

3 Engineering Example

Since 1993, Japan has introduced the technology of composite structures with corrugated steel webs from France. Currently, Japan strongly encourages designers to adopt this structural form in major highways.

The ongoing construction of the Nakano Viaduct is part of the Hanshin Expressway section in the Kansai region of Japan. It is a four-span continuous beam bridge adopting a prestressed concrete composite box girder with a corrugated steel web. The overall elevation layout of the bridge is shown in Figure 5. The main girder is a single-cell variable-height box girder, with both internal and external prestressing systems. The pier support girder height is 4.0-4.6 m, and the mid-span girder height is 2.0-2.2 m, varying according to a second-order parabolic curve. The corrugated steel web uses corrosion-resistant steel plates with a tensile strength of 490 MPa and a shear strength of 205 MPa. The wavelength is 1.2 m, wave height 200 mm, and the steel plate thickness ranges from 9 to 19 mm. To enhance the lateral deformation resistance of the main girder, transverse diaphragms are installed at the supports and at the turning points of the external prestressing system. Transverse diaphragms are also added at different longitudinal positions. The main girder cross-section and general construction of the corrugated steel web are shown in Figure 6.

The superstructure of this bridge is constructed using the cantilever casting method. The 0th segment at the pier cap is 12 m long and cast in situ on the pier scaffold. The remaining segments are 3.6 m and 4.8 m long respectively, and are cast in place on the hanging basket along with the assembly of the steel web.

4 Conclusion

The design and construction of steel-concrete composite structure bridges started relatively late in China, especially the prestressed concrete composite box girder bridge with a corrugated steel web introduced in this article, for which there are no actual bridges in China yet. Meanwhile, France, Germany, and especially Japan have successively constructed several bridges of this type, and their design and construction technologies are becoming increasingly mature. The prestressed concrete composite box girder with a corrugated steel web is particularly suitable for medium and long-span continuous beam bridges. With the development of research and analysis of this structure in China, prestressed concrete composite box girder bridges with corrugated steel webs will be applied in China's bridge construction.
