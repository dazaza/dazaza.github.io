Users need a three-dimensional online shopping credit system.

by anonymous on 2011-04-01 13:37:31

I believe many readers are familiar with the "three-good" student, and many have been awarded this title. The "three-good" refers to students excelling in their studies, possessing good moral character, and having positive thoughts. When evaluating a "three-good" student, students are assessed from multiple aspects such as academic performance, morality, and thoughtfulness. This also reminds me of the existing online shopping credit system.

As we all know, the current online shopping credit evaluation system is relatively simple, with only three levels: good, medium, and poor, making it difficult to truly reflect a store owner's real creditworthiness. Let me give you a simple example; many store owners boost their credit through virtual transactions like selling point cards and game coins. Since these virtual items don't require logistics transportation for delivery, multiple store owners can collude to quickly enhance one store owner's credit, which undoubtedly makes the credit evaluation unfair. If the future online shopping credit evaluation system could be as three-dimensional as the "three-good" student evaluation system, it would more objectively reflect the store owner's credit, which is what users need in an online shopping credit evaluation system.

In fact, all platforms have been striving in constructing an online shopping credit evaluation system. Not long ago, Taobao launched a self-check credit activity, aiming to eliminate credit cheaters. In reality, this activity has proven difficult to fundamentally eradicate credit cheaters. Meanwhile, Youa separates the credit evaluations of virtual item transactions from physical goods, which to some extent makes the store owner's credit evaluation more objective, but it still isn't perfect.

How to evaluate a store owner's credit from multiple aspects becomes an urgent need for improvement in the online shopping credit evaluation system. Recently, according to media reports, Baidu’s “Youa” new credit review system, the Bretton Woods System, will introduce a "scoring system" including parameters such as "product matches description," "seller service attitude," and "product delivery speed." It will completely separate virtual credit from physical credit. That is to say, in the future on the Youa platform, credit evaluations will no longer be just based on the "good, medium, or poor reviews" model, but instead use multiple indicators to score sellers.

It is clear that the Bretton Woods System has overturned the traditional credit evaluation system by using multiple parameters to assess a store owner's credit. Can it really more objectively reflect a store owner's credit?

Actually, shopping online is the same as shopping in a physical store, the only difference being the shopping platform. When shopping in a physical store, one looks at whether the product is fake, the seller's service attitude, and the price. When shopping on an online platform, users not only look at whether the product matches the description provided by the store owner in the shop but also consider the delivery speed and the store owner's service attitude. In other words, to objectively evaluate a store owner's strengths and weaknesses, they must be assessed from the aforementioned several aspects, thereby reflecting the store owner's true integrity. Compared to the traditional credit evaluation system, the Bretton Woods System is clearly more objective.

However, the Bretton Woods System is not the end of the credit system for online shopping platforms. I believe that with the continuous development of the online shopping market, the credit system must continue to improve. The development process of the domestic online shopping market over the past decade has confirmed my view. For many years, most domestic online shopping platforms have followed eBay's good-medium-poor evaluation system. To date, the flaws in the good-medium-poor evaluation system have become apparent, leading to the introduction of the relatively comprehensive and relatively objective Bretton Woods Credit System. In the future, when the Bretton Woods System reveals its own flaws, a more complete online shopping credit system will also emerge.

For people like me who frequently shop online, they need to find a store owner with good credit to shop from. Given the current state of the domestic online shopping market, users need a three-dimensional online shopping credit system rather than a single credit evaluation system. As the online shopping market continues to develop, users also expect this three-dimensional online shopping credit system to improve accordingly.
