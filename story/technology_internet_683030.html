[Qingdao Auto] Pay attention to your health while driving.

by qssqwe215 on 2012-02-29 11:20:10

In the increasingly prosperous cities, cars have become one of the indispensable elements. People are becoming more and more reliant on cars, and with the growing influence of cars, a series of problems caused by them cannot be ignored. Cars bring driving pleasure to their owners but also pose dangers, particularly health risks. It is very necessary to pay attention to health issues related to driving because even a small ailment might prevent car owners from enjoying the pleasure of driving.

Vision Fatigue

When driving, eyes are constantly focused on the road ahead, paying attention to pedestrians and other situations. This keeps the eye muscles tense, which may cause soreness. In summer, sunlight and road reflection can easily lead to visual fatigue, causing symptoms such as dizziness, blurred vision, and eye pain.

Suggestion: It's best to stop in a safe place after a certain period of time to rest your eyes. For blocking out sunlight, you can consider using heat-insulating sun films, sunshades, or sun-blocking nets. During intense sunlight, drivers should wear sunglasses.

Body Shape Changes

With the increase in private cars, many private car owners prefer to drive regardless of the distance. While this brings convenience, neglecting health issues can lead to various ailments. Especially for beauty-conscious women, maintaining body shape requires extra attention.

Suggestion: Fitness coaches introduce that some private car owners originally had no major health problems, but due to long-term driving and lack of necessary exercise, their health conditions were severely affected. Therefore, it is recommended that car owners ensure at least half an hour of activity daily. When traveling short distances, it's better to walk instead of driving.

Lower Back Pain

Drivers often maintain a sitting posture for extended periods, leading to frequent lower back pain. Muscles remain in a static tension state, easily causing fatigue in the lower back muscles. When these muscles are fatigued for a long time without relaxation, it can develop into chronic muscle strain. Under the influence of external forces like car bumps, it might even trigger herniated discs.

Suggestion: Continuous driving time should not be too long, especially when road conditions are poor. Speed should not be too high to avoid severe jolts that could harm the spine. Using cushions is practical; appropriate cushions can be placed on the seat and backrest. Additionally, drivers should participate more in sports, preferably full-body exercises involving large muscle groups, such as running or brisk walking.

Frozen Shoulder and Cervical Spondylosis

Similarly, the neck is also affected. Drivers keep their gaze fixed in one direction while driving. When neck muscles spasm and cervical vertebrae shift slightly, nerves are compressed or stimulated, leading to headaches, shoulder pain, and upper limb numbness.

Suggestion: Drivers should adjust their sitting posture and regulate the seat position so that the entire spine's four physiological curves can fully support against the backrest. You can use the waiting time at red lights to perform some health-promoting activities. The simplest method is to rotate your head in the order of forward, backward, left, and right. First, rotate your head once in this order, then in the opposite direction. Perform 10 repetitions each side.

Hearing Damage, Dizziness, and Lack of Focus

The space in general sedans is relatively small. Drivers spending too much time inside can be affected by the confined space, various noises within the car, and even prolonged air conditioning use. Non-ventilation causes some odors from the interior (often mildly toxic) to affect the body. Long-term exposure to noise, vibration, carbon monoxide, and other harmful factors can easily disrupt autonomic nerve function, further affecting other organs such as hearing loss and dizziness. This greatly reduces the driver's ability to respond to emergencies and make judgments, posing a serious threat to driving safety.

Suggestion: If drivers experience nausea or dizziness, they must pay attention. Do not stay in a closed cabin for too long. Although air conditioning provides temporary comfort, natural ventilation is always better. It's best to open the windows every so often for five minutes to allow fresh air circulation and refresh the mind. Additionally, if driving long distances for extended periods, it’s recommended to stop and rest every one to two hours.

Use low-volume horns while driving, and keep music volume moderate. Furthermore, wearing anti-noise earplugs is a simple and effective way to prevent noise-induced hearing loss. Additionally, check your vehicle to see if there are any fault-related noises. If so, repair them as soon as possible.
