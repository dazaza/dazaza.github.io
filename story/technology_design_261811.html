It is not an easy task to rank these 10 highly concerned phones. However, the criteria listed by PCWORLD can help you find the most suitable touchscreen smartphone for you. Due to the ten popular phones available for selection, it is difficult to keep track of comparisons. However, our created charts will help you find the most ideal touchscreen phone. Nokia 5530 HTC Touch Pro2     Keeping close tabs on the latest multiple phones in the market is a time-consuming task, especially considering that Apple, Palm, Nokia, and HTC have all released multiple phones in the recent months (including the recently announced T-Mobile Dash similar to "BlackBerry"). But fortunately, with PC World, you do not need to personally follow the overwhelming number of touchscreen smartphones. We have comprehensively compared the 10 recently released touchscreen smartphones to see how they stack up against each other. Based on the comparison results, we have compiled three tables which can help you decide whether the HTC equipped with a 5-megapixel camera or the Nokia N97 with stereo sound frequency speaker is more suitable for you. These tables can quickly answer questions such as: which smartphones have screen keyboards? Which ones have software keyboards? Which smartphones have the best multimedia functions? And what are the prices of each one? The first table lists some basic specifications: manufacturers, operators, platforms, dimensions, weight, keyboard types, colors, prices, availability, etc. The second table lists the multimedia capabilities of these phones (screen resolution, camera image resolution, auto-focus, flash, video recording, auxiliary camera, audio jack, and wireless) and navigation functions (GPS and text tags). The third table focuses on storage capabilities (external storage and expansion), connectivity capabilities (3G, Bluetooth, USB, and Wi-Fi), and battery features (removable capability, standby time, and talk time).
