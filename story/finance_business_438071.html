Do you have wooden integrated cabinets? Will tile cabinets get damaged?

by f34ttr4t5ye on 2011-05-27 15:20:56

The myriad of materials involved in renovation can already be a headache, and choosing kitchen cabinets can be even more perplexing. Should you choose modular or tile cabinets? Is there a compromise between the two? Are modular cabinets worth it? Are tile cabinets harmful?

1. Is there a difference in base materials?

Tile-built cabinets primarily consist of a brick skeleton with custom-made cabinet doors (moisture-proof boards, baked lacquer, solid wood, etc.). The characteristics are definitely hardness and a high environmental protection coefficient, with no worry about penetration occurring. However, the downside is that cleaning isn't easy, as moss can grow, and bacteria can easily breed in the gaps between tiles. Other countertops also need to be purchased separately.

The cabinet body base material for modular kitchens is moisture-proof board, which is commonly known as particleboard. It has stable physical properties, strong nail grip, and is waterproof, moisture-resistant, and heat-resistant to a certain extent. However, it cannot withstand the same level of impact as tiles. Even if the quality reaches E1 or EO grade, formaldehyde is still present, and if the edges aren't properly sealed, pollutants within the panels will slowly release. Inferior panels are prone to moisture damage. All materials for modular cabinets, from handles to appliances, can be purchased one-stop from merchants.

2. Does the craftsmanship make a difference?

For tile cabinets, it mainly depends on the skills of the craftsman. The craftsmanship determines the aesthetics, from the initial skeleton construction to the later addition of functional components, all heavily depend on the craftsman's skill. A well-made cabinet can withstand heavy chopping, has strong impact resistance, and once properly built, won't fear water immersion and has good moisture resistance.

Modular cabinets focus on the cutting, edge sealing, and installation quality of the panels. As long as the brand is good and the machinery used in the process is reliable, the production process doesn't need to be worried about, as it follows standard procedures. Connection keys also play an important role during assembly.

3. Is there a price difference?

Brick-built cabinets seem economically practical on the surface? Maybe, maybe not. The base material of tile cabinets, such as bricks and tiles, compared to panel cabinets, is dozens or even hundreds of times cheaper. However, individually purchased functional parts, such as drawers, baskets, and bowls, are more expensive than those offered by cabinet merchants. Plus, the cost of labor for the craftsman isn’t cheap. Cabinet doors also pose a problem, as many cabinet merchants don't sell them separately. After detailed calculation, the overall cost isn't low either.

Modular cabinets are priced per meter or square meter. The entire set’s cost is quoted upfront, including appliances and decorations, which might be intimidating at first. However, modular cabinets won't incur additional costs during transportation and installation, like installing a basket, which would be handled by the merchant.

4. Is there a difference in space utilization?

Tile cabinets waste more space because there isn't a standardized size specification. Once built, they are fixed, and the space cannot be altered afterward.

Through reasonable design by designers, modular cabinets integrate functionality and artistry to perfectly utilize kitchen space, avoiding unnecessary wastage. If any dissatisfaction arises later, adjustments can be made freely, such as adjusting shelves or replacing functional parts.

5. Does after-sales service matter?

Once tile cabinets are formed, they're hard to modify, especially when repairing items like baskets or plumbing, which may require more effort. A major concern with brick-cut cabinets is whether the metal fasteners connecting the cabinet body and the cabinet door are sturdy enough. Otherwise, after 2-3 years of use, if the door loosens or falls off, who should be held responsible — the craftsman who did the brick cutting or the manufacturer of the cabinet doors?

Panel cabinets, being custom-made through machine production, have standardized processes and dimensions, ensuring reliable after-sales service. They are currently more popular but require consideration of their environmental compliance and durability. However, they mainly offer a sense of spiritual enjoyment. After installation, they have strong adjustability, easier repairability, and longer-lasting after-sales service.
