The fitness enthusiasts in the city have fallen into five major misconceptions.

by sonicli1983 on 2007-12-30 12:27:27

Treadmills put less pressure on the knees

Some people believe that exercising on a treadmill puts less pressure on the knees compared to running on asphalt roads and sidewalks. Todd, a clinical medical expert at New York University Medical Center, said: "The weight of the body pressing on the joints creates pressure. Regardless of where you run, this pressure is the same."

The best way to reduce knee pressure is to change the form of exercise. If running is combined with other cardiovascular exercises, such as using an elliptical machine or a stationary bike, the impact on the knees will be reduced, and the longevity of exercise will be extended.

Swimming is the best way to lose weight

Swimming can increase lung capacity, make muscles more evenly shaped, and even reduce stress. However, experts say that unless you swim for several hours every day, it won't help you lose much weight. The buoyancy in water supports the body, so exercisers don't need to exert too much force. Moreover, after getting out of the water, fitness enthusiasts are often very hungry, making it difficult to stick to the originally planned weight-loss diet.

Yoga relieves all back pain

Practicing yoga helps relieve back pain, but it doesn't work for all types of pain. If the back pain is related to muscles, the stretching movements and certain poses in yoga can be helpful. It also strengthens the core muscle group, thereby reducing back pain.

However, if the back pain is caused by a herniated lumbar disc, yoga won't have any effect and may even cause more pain. If your back hurts, be sure to consult your doctor before starting any exercise.

No sweating means no calories burned

Sweating is not an indicator of exhaustive exercise; it's just the body's way of cooling itself. Even without sweating, a significant amount of calories can still be burned, which can be achieved through brisk walking or some light strength training.

Feeling good means you're not over-exercising

This is a common mistake people make during fitness activities. The consequences of over-exercising become apparent only after some time, and they cannot be felt immediately.

After taking a break from exercise for a period and then resuming, avoid quickly returning to your previous training level. Even if you don't feel uncomfortable at the moment, it can still lead to serious consequences that may even prevent you from participating in this sport again.
