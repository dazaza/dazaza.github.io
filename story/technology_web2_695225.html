Getting more sun exposure can promote the secretion of melatonin.

Autumn and winter are peak seasons for depression. People can strengthen self-care, exercise more, get more sun exposure, ensure adequate sleep, and stay away from seasonal depression.

In the human brain, there is a gland called the pineal gland, which secretes a hormone known as "melatonin." This hormone has the ability to regulate the levels of other hormones in the body. When it is secreted in large amounts, the secretion of thyroid hormones and adrenaline decreases, and their concentration in cells reduces. With the decrease of these two hormones that stimulate cellular excitement, the cells become less active, leading to a state of inhibition, low mood, and feelings of fatigue. In summer, with ample sunlight, the secretion of this "melatonin" is relatively lower, and melatonin capsules are used accordingly. After autumn, due to the reduction in daylight hours and weaker intensity, more of this hormone is secreted, making people prone to feeling low, negative, lethargic, and even causing symptoms such as endocrine disorders, lack of concentration, palpitations, and insomnia.

Typically, the number of female patients with depression is four times that of males. People who work indoors all year round, especially those with weaker constitutions or who rarely participate in physical exercise, as well as those who are sensitive to cold, are more likely to experience depression compared to others.

Increasing sun exposure and light exposure is the first step in staying away from depression. Citizens should try to reduce their "indoor working" time, make more time to go out for walks, and increase physical exercise. Additionally, continuous work throughout the day is not recommended, and daily overtime should not exceed two hours. If a series of self-regulation measures fail to improve the depressive condition, it is necessary to seek help from a specialist doctor promptly for early diagnosis and treatment to prevent the condition from worsening.
