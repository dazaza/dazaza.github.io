Twitter IPO is very popular, let's take a look at those key figures.

by anonymous on 2013-11-17 18:07:51

Twitter has finally made its debut on the NYSE. Since submitting its IPO documents in September, Twitter's public offering has been a focal point of public attention. If Alibaba did not go public this year, Twitter would be the largest IPO in the tech industry this year.

Trading of Twitter shares began at midnight yesterday, opening at $45.10, which was a 73% increase from the issue price of $26. It continued to rise afterwards, reaching as high as $48 at one point. Based on a total share capital of 545 million shares, Twitter’s market value exceeded $25 billion, raising $1.82 billion.

As the largest IPO in the American tech industry this year, there are some figures worth noting:

- Raised Twice: Due to strong demand for the stock, Twitter raised the issue price twice. Initially set between $17 and $20, it was then adjusted to $23-$25, and finally set at $26 on the eve of the IPO.

- $1.82 billion: This is the amount of funding that Twitter obtained this time, surpassing Google's $1.7 billion, but still far behind Facebook's $16 billion.

- 240 million monthly active users: According to the IPO documents, Twitter currently has 240 million monthly active users, averaging 100 million daily active users. The proportion of bot accounts is approximately 5%, with 77% coming from overseas. Currently, 500 million tweets are sent daily, with a cumulative total of 300 billion tweets.

- 76% mobile users: Thanks to its inherent mobile nature, the degree of mobile use by Twitter users reaches as high as 76%. In terms of revenue, 71% of advertisements also come from mobile devices.

- $534 million in revenue: According to the documents submitted by Twitter to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, over the past year, Twitter’s revenue was $534 million, with a loss of $143 million. Its revenue grew by about 100% year-over-year, but losses have also expanded. In the most recent quarter, the loss was $64.61 million, an increase of about 200% year-over-year.

- Seven rounds of financing: Starting with a $5 million Series A round in 2007, Twitter underwent seven rounds of financing, accumulating a total of $1.16 billion.

- 31 acquisitions: Over its 8 years of existence, Twitter has acquired 31 companies in total. It started in 2008 by acquiring real-time search service Summize, and since then has continued non-stop. The most recent acquisition was spending $350 million to acquire mobile advertising company MoPub. The short video application Vine was also acquired, and Jack Dorsey almost closed the deal to acquire Instagram.

The strong performance of Twitter's stock has led many to suspect that it might be an internet bubble. At the opening price of $45.10, Twitter's price-to-sales ratio reached as high as 50 times, with both stock prices and market values exceeding previous expectations.

Moreover, from a business perspective, Twitter still has some issues that cannot be ignored. One is the slowing user growth rate, which is a rather fatal point; the other is the expanding loss, without having found a good profit model yet. After the initial frenzy, the stock price after some time will better reflect its true level.
