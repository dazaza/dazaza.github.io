According to the country's subsidy policy for private purchase of new energy vehicles

by qaswr949 on 2011-05-30 13:40:15

The latest news shows that, on November 2, the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Science and Technology, MIIT, and NDRC combined to organize an expert group to argue about Beijing's pilot plan for private purchase of new energy vehicles. According to the planning of Beijing Municipal Government, it is expected to achieve the goal of 30,000 privately purchased new energy vehicles by the end of 2012, including 23,000 pure electric vehicles and 7,000 plug-in hybrid vehicles. For this purpose, the local government of Beijing plans to raise 2.0586 billion yuan by itself, while applying for a central fiscal subsidy of 1.73 billion yuan, totaling 3.7886 billion yuan, Google Maps exchange, which will be used to subsidize the private purchase of new energy vehicles and the construction of related charging facilities. According to the preliminary draft of the subsidy plan, the Beijing Municipal Government will provide subsidies according to the standard of 3000 yuan/kilowatt hour based on the central fiscal subsidy for new energy vehicles that meet the support conditions. The maximum subsidy for plug-in hybrid vehicles is 50,000 yuan per vehicle, and the maximum subsidy for pure electric vehicles is 60,000 yuan per vehicle. In addition, in order to encourage social capital to enter the supporting facilities construction field, Beijing also plans to provide subsidies for the construction of charging piles, with the subsidy amount up to 30% of the construction investment.

It is understood that, among the 30,000 new energy vehicles in the pilot scope, the Beijing Municipal Government plans to promote 1,000 vehicles this year, 5,000 vehicles in 2011, and 24,000 vehicles in 2012. And plans to select new energy vehicles with independent intellectual property rights nationwide for promotion.

Previously, cities such as Shenzhen and Shanghai have also made clear plans for the development of local new energy vehicles and issued relevant subsidy standards for private purchases of new energy vehicles.

In the Shanghai municipal government's plan for new energy vehicles, it is expected to form an annual production capacity of about 1 million new energy vehicles by 2012, including about 60,000 new energy passenger vehicles, and equip with 25,000 charging piles and 50 charging and swapping stations. However, since the issuance of relevant subsidies for national new energy subsidies in June, so far, the sales volume of new energy vehicles in Shanghai has basically remained stagnant, with almost zero transactions. In order to promote the personal sales of the new energy vehicle market, the Shanghai municipal government submitted the local new energy vehicle subsidy plan to relevant national ministries in August. It may add a subsidy of 20,000 RMB for private purchases of plug-in hybrid vehicles on the basis of national subsidies, and add 40,000-50,000 RMB for pure electric vehicles. As a result, the private purchase of new energy vehicles in Shanghai could receive a maximum subsidy of 110,000 RMB. However, whether this plan has been approved by relevant national ministries has not yet been announced.

Compared with Shanghai, Shenzhen municipal government's plan for new energy vehicles in 2012 is relatively conservative. According to the "Shenzhen New Energy Industry Revitalization Development Plan", by 2012, Shenzhen will promote various types of new energy vehicles citywide, reaching 34,000 vehicles, including 4,000 buses, 2,500 taxis, 2,500 official cars, and only 25,000 private cars.

According to the national subsidy policy for private purchases of new energy vehicles, plug-in hybrid vehicles can receive a maximum subsidy of 50,000 RMB per vehicle, and pure electric vehicles can receive a maximum subsidy of 60,000 RMB per vehicle. On this basis, Shenzhen's local finance will add a maximum subsidy of 30,000 RMB and 60,000 RMB respectively for the two types of vehicles. It is reported that these subsidy funds will be directly given to automobile enterprises, and the enterprises will return the discount to consumers when selling. Shenzhen also stipulates that consumers using electric vehicles will receive a 9,000 RMB electricity subsidy.

Although the subsidy plans for private purchases of new energy vehicles issued by the state and local governments have successively come out, the private consumption market for new energy vehicles has not been driven. Although the infrastructure construction of new energy vehicles in some areas is being vigorously promoted, the actual market entry time is still relatively late. In addition, consumers still have concerns about the performance of electric vehicle batteries in terms of range and safety. Therefore, it will take some time for new energy vehicles to truly break through in the automotive consumption market.
