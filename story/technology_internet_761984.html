Lu Zhaoxi is directly in charge of "Ali Box" and cooperates with third-party hardware manufacturers.

by geekzhang on 2013-05-17 09:56:10

Original title: The Imminent Family Internet War: Alibaba Box Plans in Secret

On May 15, this reporter confirmed through insiders that the Alibaba Group will launch a box (set-top box) product, with the project directly overseen by Alibaba Group CEO Jonathan Lu. Currently, the Alibaba operating system is also still directly managed by Lu.

After personal computers (study and office desks), tablet computers + smartphones (mobile devices), where will the next battlefield of the internet be? The battlefield in the home (smart TVs or set-top boxes) has become a consensus among internet companies.

The clarion call for the mobile internet war has already sounded, and the family internet war is about to begin.

Launching such hardware as a box is not the goal; the aim is to push existing internet applications and services through new hardware to netizens, which can include video, games, news, local search, and even e-commerce.

The goose formation model of the industrial age is now taking effect in the information industry, with computing technology first covering PCs, tablets, smartphones, TVs (set-top boxes), and other hardware, followed by various internet applications and services.

Lu's direct responsibility for the box plan represents that Alibaba Group places family internet, mobile internet, and PC internet at the same level. The rapid march into living rooms sees Lu opening up new battlefields for Alibaba.

In addition to Alibaba Group, PPTV, Letv, and Xiaomi have all launched boxes to join the family internet war.

Lu's Secret Box

An insider close to the Alibaba operating system revealed that the Alibaba box will focus on the system and the ecosystem surrounding it: the Alibaba box will come pre-installed with the Alibaba OS (operating system) box version. Previously, the Alibaba OS mobile version was used for smartphones. On April 15 this year, five smartphone manufacturers - Little Pepper, Konka, Amoi, Zopo, and GFive - cooperated with Alibaba OS to launch smartphones pre-installed with Alibaba OS.

The upcoming Alibaba OS box version will be similar to the Alibaba OS mobile version, cooperating with third-party hardware manufacturers who will be responsible for producing and selling the boxes, while Alibaba Group will provide the operating system for free. Alibaba Group's approach differs slightly from other internet companies entering the box market, as those companies are involved in the manufacturing and sales of hardware.

Duan Xiaolei, Vice President of Polymedia and General Manager of the Multi-terminal Division, told this reporter in an interview that the PPBOX (the box launched by PPTV) uses an operating system independently developed and designed by PPTV based on Android, with the aim of ensuring seamless integration of PPTV’s video applications and services across multiple screens. This model is similar to Xiaomi Box and Letv Box but differs from Alibaba Box.

Besides differences in operational methods, there are also differences in purpose. Unlike Letv and PPTV, whose primary focus is content, Alibaba Group aims more to connect multiple screens and enable TV screen shopping.

Compared to traditional broadcasting companies (mainly home appliance manufacturers), the above-mentioned internet companies emphasize two points when launching their boxes. One is cloud computing + big data (PPcloud); the second is multi-screen interaction, Duan Xiaolei said.

Duan Xiaolei explained that through PPcloud, whether watching videos on a PC, tablet, smartphone, or PPBOX, different terminals can synchronize. For example, if you watch halfway through a video at the office on your PC, you can continue watching at home via PPBOX. Multi-screen interaction allows content being watched on a phone (or tablet or other smart terminal) to be pushed to a TV connected to PPBOX, provided they are within the same domain network.

Alibaba Box will also make efforts in these two areas, especially in big data. Through Alibaba Box, netizens can use all services provided by Alibaba Group's companies, including Alipay, Taobao, and Tmall.

Regarding licenses, Alibaba Group may cooperate with Wasu Media or Hunan Television.

Both of these companies established joint ventures with Alibaba in 2010. The former partnered with Taobao to create Wasu Taobao, offering 12 categories of goods on digital TV, allowing users to interactively purchase items via remote control and set-top box, choosing either cash-on-delivery or payment through Alipay TV Edition. The latter jointly established the Hitao.com celebrity platform with Taobao, embedding products into variety shows.

Aiming to Create an Ironman Triathlon

The goose formation model of the industrial age is now operating in the information age, continuously igniting new battles.

The goose formation model of the industrial age works like this: mature industries or those with potential comparative disadvantages shift to new regions; the goose formation model of the information age, however, operates differently: mature internet services and applications shift from old, mature terminal devices to new ones.

Each wave of migration is a collective celebration of the information industry. Investments flood in, entrepreneurs swarm in, and a batch of star companies emerge. Meanwhile, companies that fail to keep up with trends decline or eventually disappear.

For Alibaba Box, it carries the dream of Alibaba Group moving from PC internet and mobile internet to family internet, similar to the cloud phones and cloud operating systems previously launched by Alibaba Group.

It is well-known that in PC internet, e-commerce, entertainment (games as representative), and information are three main types of applications and services, corresponding to three major entry points: e-commerce, instant messaging, and search, corresponding to three major companies: Alibaba Group, Tencent, and Baidu, forming the three giants (BAT) of China's PC internet landscape.

In mobile internet or family internet, e-commerce, entertainment, and information will still be three mainstream types of applications and services, albeit with some differences. Duan Xiaolei said: "For instance, regarding information, many people believe that video-based information will replace text-based information to become the mainstream. In entertainment, internet films will rank alongside games as the most mainstream applications."

Currently, internet companies entering the box market fall into three categories: one provides video services, including LetvTV, PPTV, and rumored entrant PPS; another category includes Xiaomi, which is not just a smartphone company but aims to provide internet applications and services; the final category is Alibaba Group, mainly providing e-commerce services with the goal of bringing e-commerce into the living room.

In the new era, the game might differ. In the PC internet era, the internet, software, and hardware were provided by numerous companies within a specialized division of labor system; in the mobile internet era, Apple's ironman triathlon (software + hardware + internet) proved to be the most effective, and achieving the heights of BAT solely through a single application seems impossible.

In other words, a single application cannot serve as an entry point; the ironman triathlon is the entry point.

What about family internet? Is it specialized division of labor or the ironman triathlon? Regardless of Alibaba Group, Xiaomi, LetvTV, or PPTV, the chosen path is the ironman triathlon, not specialized division of labor.
