I. Causes of Stretch Marks

Medical research tells us:

The "main director" causing changes in a pregnant woman's appearance is the hormones within her body. In early pregnancy, these hormones are mainly produced by the corpus luteum of the ovary. As the demand for hormones increases, the placenta takes over as the "main character" in hormone secretion. Simultaneously, the workload of glands such as the thyroid, pancreas, and adrenal glands also increases.

Among the various hormones, estrogen, progesterone, oxytocin, and prolactin play decisive roles in major metabolic activities during pregnancy. They effectively regulate the metabolic processes of the mother during pregnancy, promoting the development of uterine tissue in its vigorous growth stage. They are responsible for utilizing the mother's reserves to meet the needs of fetal growth and development and promote breast development.

However, increased hormone secretion can lead to pigmentation on the skin surface, with the "culprit" being the cortisol in the pregnant woman's body. The mechanism behind this is that after pregnancy, the secretion function of the adrenal gland is enhanced, resulting in an increase in cortisol, which then leads to the formation of stretch marks on the skin surface and the appearance of dark brown spots on the face.

6. Constant Itching in the Abdomen

Pregnant women often complain about itchy bellies, which is caused by skin expansion. Applying some moisturizing lotion or massage cream can alleviate and soothe symptoms.

However, if the itchiness prevents sleep and rashes or large patches appear from the belly to the thighs, consult a doctor immediately. It might be prurigo. The exact cause of prurigo remains unclear, but according to statistics, it usually occurs in the last three months of pregnancy. Although it does not endanger the health of the pregnant woman or fetus, it causes significant discomfort, sometimes even preventing sleep. In such cases, doctors may prescribe antipruritic drugs or oral medications as treatment methods.
