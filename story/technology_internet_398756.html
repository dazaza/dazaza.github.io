Weibo replaces search - the crisis facing Baidu

by edivista on 2011-03-31 09:21:04

(Weibo News Network) Click to log in to Sina Weibo, Sohu Weibo, Tencent Weibo.

What is the role of Weibo? Its biggest role is clearly reducing people's dependence on search engines.

However, as Zhou Hongyi, chairman of 360 Company (enter Zhou Hongyi's Weibo), said, what disrupts IM is definitely not called IM. Therefore, what disrupts search is not necessarily search. What can disrupt search must be social media like Weibo. Internet commentator Liu Xingliang said.

Overseas, social networking sites like Facebook even block the search function of search engine Google. This makes search engines, which heavily depend on content, feel powerless when faced with increasingly new content. However, in China, the battle between search engines and social media has not become so obvious yet. Sina is currently the most hardworking in terms of Weibo investment. David W. C. Kang, CEO of Sina, publicly stated at the Shenzhen IT Leaders Summit that Sina will invest a large amount of funds into the Weibo product, even at the cost of sacrificing performance and stock prices. In internet competition, what ultimately determines victory is user usage time and user access numbers. This also means that Sina, which was once replaced by Baidu and Google search engines as the gateway for netizens to go online, will seek to regain control over the internet gateway.

Although Sina is referred to as China's first news portal, it has been continuously pursued and impacted by other portals in recent years and has been actively seeking new changes. Cao Guowei believes that social media or Weibo is the future development trend of the internet. Similarly, Sohu, Tencent, and NetEase are also continuously investing in Weibo. Zhang Chaoyang, founder of Sohu, and Ma Huateng, founder of Tencent, have personally taken charge, providing support for Weibo, which has yet to discover an obvious profit-making method.

The diversion of internet users by social media is inevitable because search is no longer the core gateway of the internet. Qiu Bowang, president of Zhengwang Consulting, told reporters.

In fact, search engines will not only face competition from Weibo; netizens have already shifted their attention from initial news searches and music searches to richer internet content and applications, and more entry point competitions have already begun frequently.

The listing of 360 Company has already shown the ability of browsers to monetize traffic. Taking browsers as an example, apart from Microsoft's IE9 browser, Tencent, Sohu, Google, Baidu, and 360 all consider browsers as a direction to exert force, and various browsers are becoming increasingly client-based.

Wang Xiaochuan, CTO of Sohu, told reporters that if Sogou Browser can achieve a market share of more than 10% domestically in China, then they would consider going public. Liang Nianjian, president of Microsoft Greater China Region, told this reporter that Microsoft's IE9 will open more interfaces, allowing videos, Weibo, and even games to be completed through the browser.

Even Sogou Input Method and security products such as those from 360 and Kingsoft networks are becoming new gateways for netizens and have started providing news pop-up windows or various reminder services and value-added services. Additionally, various large games, video players, Tencent QQ instant messaging clients, etc., are competing for netizens' limited online time. Even NetEase and Tencent email services have integrated more internet functions including search, news, shopping, and music, providing gateway platform functions for netizens.

It can be seen that Baidu, as a news search engine, has no advantage whatsoever in the various newly emerging internet content and business models, especially in capturing netizens' online time and stickiness. When netizens open any internet product, it can meet personal online needs, so why bother opening a search engine? If Baidu's traffic decreases, its income will naturally drop significantly.

Regarding these changes in China's internet, Hu Yanping, director of the DCCI Internet Data Center, expressed that it is mainly due to the current shift in internet centers. The time, place, and scenarios where users acquire information have all changed, and these changes mean that users' dependence on search has changed.

However, search, as a basic need for netizens, is still difficult to replace. Internet commentator Hongbo believes that the ability of users to answer and integrate questions within social networks will partially replace the function of search engines, especially for various newly generated content, which search engines find difficult to immediately cover. But for some data accumulated historically, search engines are better suited.
