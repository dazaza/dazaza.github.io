How to Cook Delicious Broccoli

The green broccoli has a naturally endearing appearance, complemented by its elegant name, and it surely has no shortage of admirers. However, aside from garlic-infused or plain stir-fried recipes, people seem unsure how to engage more intimately with this vegetable.

Referred to as the "Crown of Vegetables," broccoli is a high-value natural ingredient known not only for its excellent cancer-fighting properties but also for its superior taste. Whether served cold, hot stir-fried, or in soup, its fragrant flavor can be fully realized, captivating even the pickiest eaters.

Flowers are meant to be cherished, and broccoli is no exception. Yet, you can express your love through your taste buds and gastric juices, giving it a more thorough, genuine, and unrestrained affection.

Nutritional Components of Broccoli (per 100g):

- Protein: 1.9g

- Fat: 0.2g

- Dietary Fiber: 1.2g

- Carbohydrates: 3.6g

- Beta-Carotene: 10μg

- Vitamin B1: 0.01mg

- Vitamin B2: 0.05mg

- Vitamin C: 66mg

- Vitamin E: 0.08mg

Broccoli is also rich in various minerals.

Jade Rabbit Broccoli:

Ingredients: Broccoli, pigeon eggs

Seasonings: Salt, chicken essence, sesame oil, pepper powder, honey

Instructions:

1. Wash the broccoli, cut into small florets, blanch in boiling water; hard-boil the pigeon eggs, peel them, and set aside.

2. Carve the pigeon eggs into rabbit shapes.

3. Mix the broccoli with salt, chicken essence, sesame oil, pepper powder, and honey. Arrange together with pigeon eggs on a plate.

Broccoli Soup:

Ingredients: Broccoli, quail eggs, black-bone chicken, ginseng, goji berries

Seasonings: Salt, chicken essence, salad oil

Instructions:

1. Clean the chicken, cut into pieces, and simmer with ginseng and goji berries for two hours over low heat.

2. Wash the broccoli, cut into small florets, and fry the quail eggs in oil until cooked.

3. Take an appropriate amount of chicken broth, add broccoli, cook until done, season with salt and chicken essence, then add the fried quail eggs.

Broccoli with Mushrooms:

Ingredients: Broccoli, white mushrooms, shiitake mushrooms, carrots

Seasonings: Salt, mushroom essence, oyster sauce, sugar, starch, original chicken juice

Instructions:

1. Cut broccoli into small florets, slice white mushrooms and shiitake mushrooms, dice carrots, and set aside.

2. In a pot, add oyster sauce and original chicken juice, add all ingredients, simmer for five minutes over low heat, season with salt, mushroom essence, and sugar, then thicken with starch. (Using mushroom essence enhances the aroma and freshness of the mushrooms.)

Shrimp Balls with Broccoli:

Ingredients: Broccoli, fresh shrimp

Seasonings: Salt, chicken essence, starch, salad oil

Instructions:

1. Wash the broccoli, cut into small florets, blanch in boiling water, and set aside.

2. Heat oil in a pan, stir-fry broccoli and shrimp, season with salt and chicken essence, then thicken with starch.

Tai Chi Broccoli:

Ingredients: Broccoli, cauliflower

Seasonings: Salt, chicken essence, starch, salad oil

Instructions:

1. Wash broccoli and cauliflower, cut into small florets, blanch separately in boiling water, and set aside.

2. Heat oil in a pan, stir-fry broccoli, season with salt and chicken essence, thicken with starch, and place on a plate.

3. Stir-fry cauliflower using the same method and arrange on the other side of the plate.

Mixed Vegetables with Broccoli:

Ingredients: Broccoli, cauliflower, carrot, red pepper

Seasonings: Salt, chicken essence, starch, salad oil

Instructions:

1. Cut broccoli and cauliflower into small florets, peel and slice carrots, remove seeds and cut red peppers into chunks, and set aside.

2. Blanch all vegetables briefly in warm oil.

3. Leave a little oil in the pan, add all ingredients, stir-fry, season with salt and chicken essence, then thicken with starch.

Key Benefits of Broccoli:

1. Rich in vitamin C, enhancing liver detoxification and boosting immunity.

2. Studies show that broccoli effectively combats breast and colon cancers. Regular consumption by healthy individuals can prevent cancer, earning it the title of "Anti-Cancer New Star."

3. Regulates and prevents hypertension and heart disease.

4. High fiber content effectively reduces intestinal glucose absorption, lowering blood sugar and controlling diabetes. Thus, broccoli is considered a blessing for diabetic patients.

Five Criteria for Quality Broccoli:

1. Bright green color indicates better quality; yellowing suggests over-maturity or prolonged storage.

2. Smooth surface with tightly packed flower buds signifies better quality.

3. Heavier feel denotes better quality, but overly hard flower heads and thick stems indicate older plants.

4. If leaves are present, tender and moist leaves suggest freshness.

5. Check if the stem's cut is moist; dryness indicates longer storage time and less freshness.

Two Methods to Preserve Broccoli:

1. Wrap broccoli in paper or breathable film (lightly spray water on the paper), store upright in the refrigerator's chill compartment, and it can last about a week.

2. Tear broccoli into small florets, soak in saltwater for about five minutes to remove dust and pests, rinse, drain, blanch in boiling saltwater, cool, and either cook directly or freeze in a preservation bag.

Even Broccoli Waste is Valuable:

1. Fresh broccoli leaves can be eaten. Wash, tear into small pieces, and stir-fry with seafood for a refreshing dish.

2. The main edible part of broccoli is the flower head, while the stem is often discarded. However, peeling off the tough outer layer reveals tender stem meat that can be pickled with salt, making a great appetizer.
