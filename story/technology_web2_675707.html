They originally had a younger sister as well. After their father's death, the family lost its only pillar. Due to financial difficulties, they had no choice but to give their daughter away. The mother was always weak and sickly, and during a major illness two years later, she eventually passed away due to long-term hunger and malnutrition. What was originally a family of five was now left with just the two brothers. Many years later, Han Mingliang recalled his mother, and the only impression he had was "her whole body was swollen before she died."

At that time, everyone in the village were relatives. Seeing the pitiful state of the two brothers, the relatives took turns giving them food. Although the younger brother didn't understand, the older brother, Han Mingxing, knew he had to work to support his younger brother. He started helping others with work. "My brother would leave home before dawn and sometimes wouldn't return until the middle of the night. Those years, I rarely saw him. Whatever food my brother had, he would save it for me. If I woke up in the morning and saw a piece of rice cake on the table, it must have been brought back by my brother last night," Han Mingliang recalled those days.

The older brother helped the younger brother find a disabled wife. When Han Mingliang was around fourteen or fifteen, he also started working on other people's land, and the opportunities for the brothers to see each other became even fewer. During the busy farming season, they couldn't spare the time, so the leisure time became the most anticipated part of Han Mingliang's year: he could finally visit his brother. "Actually, there wasn't much to talk about when we met, just asking if we were eating enough." Han Mingliang said. For them at the time, "eating enough" was the greatest happiness.

Because there was no money at home, Han Mingxing couldn't afford to marry, and as his younger brother also grew up, the younger brother's lifelong matter became his heart knot. Han Mingxing asked people everywhere until 1976 when a cousin of the same clan found him, saying that a woman begging had come to the village, "We're all unfortunate people, let's live together." This woman was Fei Guangyin, who had a disability in her left leg due to polio. That year, Han Mingliang was already 35 years old.

Fei Guangyin was also an unfortunate person. She was the eldest at home, and because of poverty, she had to drag her lame leg out to beg. Before meeting Han Mingliang, she had been begging for two years. When they got married, she was just 20 years old.

After getting married, the two brothers did not separate households, and Fei Guangyin gave birth to a daughter and a son for Han Mingliang. Having children meant hope, but unfortunately, Han Mingliang suddenly fell ill at this time.

▲ More than 20 years ago

After the younger brother fell ill, the older brother supported the family. It was the summer of 1990, Han Mingliang was working in the fields when he suddenly fainted. When the neighbors sent him to the hospital, his face was yellowish, and pus was flowing out of his left ear. The doctor initially diagnosed him with colon cancer. Upon hearing the bad news, the older brother, on his way to the hospital, cried uncontrollably.

Han Mingliang's daughter, Han Ju, recalled the moment when Uncle Han Mingxing walked into the ward: "Uncle was crying and shouting dad's name, dad was unconscious, and Uncle completely lost his composure." Han Ju also said that she had never seen Uncle so sad, and it was the only time she saw Uncle cry.

Fortunately, Han Mingliang did not have colon cancer, but rather what is locally called "fecal poison." This illness left Han Mingliang's health in ruins. His left ear suffered severe hearing loss, and his right ear became completely deaf. All his teeth fell out within one or two years after being discharged from the hospital. The burden of taking care of him fell on his brother and wife.

So Han Mingxing found a job at a nearby rice processing factory. The monthly salary of more than twenty yuan was pitifully small, but at least it gave him time to take care of his younger brother. When autumn came, Fei Guangyin limped to the fields to work, "My legs are different lengths, standing in the field doesn't give me any strength, I knelt down to finish plowing the land."

▲ Now

The younger brother takes care of his paralyzed older brother. The next year, Han Mingliang left the sickbed, but his complexion was far worse than before. He also developed hearing problems, and more of the household burdens fell on his older brother, Han Mingxing. Another twelve years passed, during which Han Mingliang's daughter, Han Ju, and son, Han You, successively got married, leaving only the original three people under the eaves. The difference was that these three people had grown old.

When people grow old, they get sick. In 2003, Han Mingxing suffered a stroke, unable to take care of himself and lost his ability to move. Han Mingliang served his brother every day by bringing water and food, taking care of his brother's daily life. He specially bought a bedpan and urinal to take care of his brother's toileting needs. Because he worried that lying still might cause sores on his brother's body, turning him over and massaging him became his daily routine. Of course, he started working outside again because the family needed an economic source.

Under the care of his younger brother and sister-in-law, Han Mingxing's condition improved, and he could walk with a cane. Unfortunately, last winter, Han Mingxing fell while going out to use the toilet at night, hit by a car reversing at the door. This collision not only broke one of his legs but also left him permanently paralyzed, unable to move.

Now, it is even harder for Han Mingliang to take care of his brother. Neighbors suggested sending Han Mingxing to a nursing home, but neither of the brothers agreed. "My brother can't leave me, and I can't leave my brother. My brother never married and has no children, so I, as his younger brother, will take care of him until the end," Han Mingliang said.
