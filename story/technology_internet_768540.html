Sina Weibo Open Platform Launches "Micro Services" System

November 12th News - Today, the Sina Weibo Open Platform held a developer salon and announced the launch of the Weibo Open Platform's "Micro Services" system. Co-President and CTO of Sina, Charles Xu, Vice President of Strategic Development and General Manager of Weibo Open Platform at Sina, David Chu, and key product managers from the Weibo Open Platform shared insights on the future direction of Weibo and the open platform, explaining the definition and value of "Micro Services."

Co-President and CTO of Sina, Charles Xu, stated at the event: "Sina Weibo leverages its big data system, which includes hundreds of millions of users, over a million organizations, and vast object relationships, to construct an efficient interest graph and information network. Sina hopes to structure unstructured data, collaborating with partners and third-party developers to transform Weibo into an efficient socialized information and service platform."

Micro Services Launched for Corporate Weibo Accounts

It is understood that in the three years since its establishment, the Sina Weibo Open Platform has opened up more than 200 interfaces covering multiple dimensions such as users, relationships, topics, content search, messages, recommendations, and more. Currently, over 12 million original pieces of content from third parties enter Weibo daily, bringing back more than 80 million views to these third parties.

Based on these interfaces and services, the Weibo ecosystem continues to evolve. The total number of enterprises and institutions on Weibo has exceeded 390,000, with their fan base surpassing 325 million. To meet the increasing marketing needs of businesses, Sina Weibo launched the "Micro Services" system. Micro Services are services provided by the Sina Weibo Open Platform for third-party enterprise partners serving the Weibo ecosystem. These services allow marketing and service systems to depend on each other and develop together, including message channels, Page application frameworks, commercial data APIs, and more.

Weibo emphasized that to promote healthy development of the platform, the Weibo Open Platform focuses exclusively on foundational services and key product constructions related to core Weibo functionalities such as accounts, relationships, search, feeds, advertising systems, etc. All other product areas are open to third-party development, including marketing services and tool management applications for enterprise developers, as well as entertainment, lifestyle, tools, and news applications for all developers. In this system, the Weibo Open Platform primarily serves as a strategy maker, builder of core Weibo functions and foundational services, leaving more imaginative space and product domains to third-party developers. This clearly defines the division of labor between the platform and developers, enriching the Weibo ecosystem.

Creating a New Weibo "Marketing + Service" Ecosystem

In the Micro Services system, the message channel builds a bridge for communication and interaction between enterprises and users. It includes three main functions: "Custom Replies," "Subscription Sending," and "Private Message Alerts." Enterprises can use these features to push relevant content or services based on keywords from user interactions, regularly send information to subscribed users, provide price drop alerts for products, traffic condition warnings, and more. For example, companies often receive a large number of repetitive @questions from users, making it difficult to respond promptly. After using the message service interface, whether it’s an @mention, comment, or private message, the system can automatically reply according to pre-set strategies, enhancing the company's online service capabilities.

The Page Application Framework allows integrators to embed lightweight applications within Weibo homepages, enabling third-party applications to function as natively as Weibo's own features. This is suitable for meeting enterprise needs such as product display, marketing interaction, and data management. The Commercial Data API features characteristics such as data analysis, public sentiment monitoring, and obtaining full fan lists. Additionally, it provides real-time data pushing, historical data retrieval, detecting fan change trends, active and event-participating fan analysis, and more, assisting and guiding enterprises in conducting social marketing.

Providing Cross-Platform One-Stop Services for Developers

According to relevant data, the current Sina Weibo Open Platform has 570,000 registered developers (over 120,000 certified developers), 48,000 live applications, over 18,000 mobile applications, covering 60,000 websites, 850,000 secondary domain names, with a large volume of content spreading on Weibo every day.

Vice President of Strategic Development and General Manager of Weibo Open Platform at Sina, David Chu, said: "The Weibo Open Platform continues to strengthen its open mechanism for interconnection with external web pages and apps. With the launch of the 'Micro Services' system, Weibo has transitioned from a 'marketing' platform to a 'marketing + service' platform, providing cross-platform, one-stop services for partners, jointly building Weibo's large ecosystem of 'users + business,' 'PC + mobile,' and 'marketing + service.'"
