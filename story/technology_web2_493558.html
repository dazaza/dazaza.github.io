Viewing the Development Trend of Elastic Social Network Applications from Xinghui

by ybcz0519 on 2011-09-22 18:52:44

I. Introduction to Xinghui

Xinghui is a mobile social network based on events and gatherings, helping you meet more friends through offline activities and discover valuable people around you. Each Xinghui user has a social electronic business card. By exchanging and managing these cards, users can establish strong connections based on shared scenarios and mutual friends, making it easy to maintain real-life networks.

Main Features:

1. Social Electronic Business Card - Personal homepage for events and gatherings - Integrates elements like business cards/contact lists/Weibo/personal updates - Convenient for memorizing, querying, and managing

2. Event Creation and Interaction - People in the same time/place/activity automatically form groups - Group members can exchange and view electronic business cards - Members who have exchanged cards can interact in real-time

3. Dynamic Sharing of Events and Gatherings - View contacts' event and gathering dynamics - Discover interesting people and those you'd like to know by sharing activity dynamics

4. Recommendations Based on Mutual Friends - Unable to exchange business cards with group members not in the same time/place/theme, but can find common friends - Based on recommendations from mutual friends, it's easy to introduce friends to each other and establish connections

Link: http://xinghui.me/

II. About Elastic Social Networks

Previously, Minghao wrote an article about elastic social networks, introducing the concept and some products currently being developed domestically. Elastic social network products can also be considered a typical application of SOLOMO (Social-Local-Mobile). Elastic social networks are understood as mobile social networks based on context (scenes), so there are a few important points here: one is what the scene is, second is the social need, third is what basis is used to establish social relationships; I summarize these as three necessary conditions:

1. Based on a certain scene, such as an event;

2. The event scene has a strong social demand;

3. Based on a certain function point (demand point) to trigger social interaction;

Currently, there are already many elastic social applications based on context, although their entry points vary. For example:

- Stranger dating as an entry point, including apps like Momo and Moguo;

- Events as an entry point, including apps like Xinghui, +1, and Zhe'er IM;

- Photo sharing as an entry point, such as Color;

- Buying and selling as an entry point, building buyer-seller relationships, such as Yihu;

III. Some Thoughts on Elastic Social Networks

1. Social networking itself is not the goal; it's important to clarify why social networking is needed, and the demands after social networking are even more critical;

2. What value can be provided after social networking? For instance, QQ provides a channel for online contact once both parties become friends, so similarly, elastic social networks established based on context must consider the value of the relationship after its establishment;

3. The concept of elastic social networks sounds good, but I believe only a few fields with clear needs can successfully implement this concept. Other elastic social needs can actually be met through extensions of existing services, such as WeChat;

4. There are many directions for mobile applications, and it's not necessarily required to enter the circle of elastic social networks. Among the three necessary conditions mentioned above, triggering social interaction based on a certain function point might mean that for some mobile applications, users' demand for the function itself outweighs their demand for social interaction, so the product may be better suited to develop into a tool-type mobile application with social attributes;

IV. Issues and Challenges

Elastic social network applications seem perfect in theory, but there are indeed some problems that need to be overcome in actual products:

1. User density issue: Most mobile social networks use geographical location as the bond for social interaction. If based on geographical location, if there are too few users in a single area, it would be difficult to generate effective social behavior;

2. Extension of elastic social needs: As mentioned earlier, elastic social networks can be considered situational social networks based on scenes, which means the probability of such situations occurring is relatively low. How to meet subsequent extension needs after situational social interactions is a question worth considering;

3. Dilemma between specialized and comprehensive functions: If only the social function mentioned in point two is implemented, the user churn rate might be relatively high because no additional value is provided after social interaction is completed. However, if the aim is to satisfy post-social needs, it implies that the product's development cycle will be extended, which also means greater challenges. From this perspective, the choice of entry point for elastic social applications is crucial—it should be highly sticky and ideally have a relatively high frequency of triggers.

V. Conclusion

From the perspective of Xinghui: choosing the entry point well, using events as the entry point and application scenario (I think this is a great entry point), participating in events has relatively clear social needs. Through the function of exchanging business cards for social activities, based on event-based business card exchanges, recommending friends through friends are all strong demands within the event scenario. Of course, for Xinghui, it might also need to consider how to transform from a tool for exchanging business cards at events into a tool for maintaining and expanding networks, since the frequency of attending events is not very high for many users. Currently, the official events launched by Xinghui allow users to expand their networks according to different fields, which I think is a good attempt. This transformation should also be a problem that all elastic social network applications need to consider—how to transition from a situational social application into an application that meets more essential user needs. This requires a deeper understanding of the more fundamental human needs for social interaction.

For elastic social networks, I personally feel there's no need to dwell too much on the concept. It's more important to focus on the needs, perceive offline needs more, and solidify solutions to these needs into products through mobile products. Besides considering the driving needs of users based on scenarios when using the application, it's also necessary to consider broader social needs in this context.
