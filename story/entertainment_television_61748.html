The TV series "The Legend of Bruce Lee," filmed by China International Corporation, held a press conference. In the drama, the legendary life of the martial arts master Bruce Lee will be showcased. Directed by Wu Ziniu, filming is expected to start in December.

Bruce Lee may be a household name, but Wu Ziniu believes that today's young people in China have very limited knowledge about Bruce Lee, much of which comes from the movies of Stephen Chow and Jet Li. "In fact, Bruce Lee's life was very short, but what he left behind goes beyond just martial arts. He studied philosophy and was among the few who could derive joy from the spirit. His tough character also exudes masculinity, all of which are attractive qualities of Bruce Lee."

Regarding the much-discussed casting for Bruce Lee's role, Wu Ziniu stated that the actor does not necessarily need to look exactly like Bruce Lee; the key is resemblance in spirit. Producer Yu Shengli told reporters that Nie Yuan and Jay Chou were both among the candidates. "We will further contact Jay Chou in the future. Actually, our requirements for the actor playing Bruce Lee are quite simple: suitable image, performance, and martial arts skills. In terms of martial arts, moves like Jeet Kune Do and nunchucks will also be featured in the TV series."

When discussing Bruce Lee's mysterious death, screenwriter Qian Linsen told reporters that he would downplay Bruce Lee's death in the TV series. "There are many controversies surrounding Bruce Lee's death. The TV series will avoid focusing on his death. The conclusion of the entire series will be Bruce Lee's most glorious movie 'Enter the Dragon,' and it will still end tragically."
