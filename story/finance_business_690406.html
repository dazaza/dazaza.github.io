"Wild Foolish Thieves" is the opening film of the 24th Harbin Film Festival

by anonymous on 2012-03-05 10:09:32

"Crazy Fools" is a film crafted by the team behind the "Crazy" series, starring Louis Koo, Annie Yi, and Simon Yam.

"Crazy Fools" was the opening film of the 24th Harbin Snow and Ice Film Festival. The 24th Harbin Film Festival grandly opened on February 12 in Harbin, the Ice City, where "Crazy Fools" held its first national trial screening. After officially hitting major theaters nationwide on March 1st, the movie had an impressive attendance rate. However, box office success does not necessarily equate to critical acclaim, as audience reviews for this "crazier" "Crazy Fools" were polarized: some viewers said "there were more laughs than expected, feeling very relaxed and relieved after watching," others "laughed until their stomachs hurt," while some commented "comedy should be this pure, don't overcomplicate things; making the audience laugh is king." However, many middle-aged and elderly viewers found "Crazy Fools" "too chaotic, with some segments being too extreme," and some lamented that the film had too many plotlines, with character relationships intertwined, making it "a bit dizzying." Regarding the contrasting reception of "Crazy Fools," industry insiders analyzed, "this isn't a bad thing; differentiation is crucial in today's film market. Being different from others ensures people notice you at least have something worth talking about."

The premiere location of "Crazy Fools," Harbin, offers picturesque scenery. Harbin, known as the "Oriental Paris," features a blend of Eastern and Western architectural styles with distinct characteristics. With a rich history and culture, it not only showcases the history and culture of northern minority ethnic groups but also serves as a renowned city where Chinese and Western cultures converge. The solemn and magnificent St. Sophia Cathedral, the mysterious atmosphere of the Nicholas Church, the ingeniously designed Russian log cabins, the elegant Gothic buildings, the European-style Central Street, the bright and clean architectural tones, the bustling urban landscape, the annual "Harbin Summer Music Concert," the international ice sculpture and snow sculpture competition during the "Ice and Snow Festival," and the "International Trade Fair" all reflect the unique charm of "Oriental Moscow."

The Flood Control Monument in Harbin, the premiere site of "Crazy Fools," was built in October 1958 to commemorate the victory of the Harbin people over the "1957 Great Flood." It was jointly designed by Soviet designer Bajis Zierlev and the second-generation architect Li Guangyao from Harbin Institute of Technology, with the site chosen by then Mayor Lu Qien of Harbin.

The International Ice and Snow Festival in Harbin, the premiere site of "Crazy Fools," opens annually on January 5th. It is China's first international festival themed around ice and snow. Every year, a large winter swimming competition is held, presenting a spectacular scene. Tourists can enjoy the beauty of ice lanterns and snow sculptures, appreciate ice and snow cultural performances, and participate in numerous ice and snow entertainment activities in Harbin. Harbin's ice lanterns and snow sculptures have become one of the three wonders of world ice and snow art.

The Ice and Snow World in Harbin, the premiere site of "Crazy Fools," was initially created in 1999. To celebrate the millennium anniversary, Harbin Municipality fully utilized its ice and snow resources and launched a super-large-scale ice and snow art project. The Ice and Snow World of Harbin displays the unique charm of Harbin's ice and snow culture and tourism. Each winter, domestic and international tourists flock here continuously. It is the world's largest outdoor ice and snow-themed super-large entertainment project. Channels such as National Geographic from the U.S., NHK from Japan, and SBS from South Korea have all featured special reports.

The Summer Music Concert in Harbin, the premiere site of "Crazy Fools," is a national-level music extravaganza held every other year from August 6th to 15th, lasting 10 days. Created by music enthusiasts in Harbin, it is grand in scale and features a constellation of stars, becoming a cradle for musical artists. Now elevated to a local traditional music festival, it is also one of China's traditional music festivals, known as one of the "Three Major Concerts," solidifying Harbin's status as a city of music. In 2010, Harbin was recognized by UNESCO's Global Creative Cities Network as the "Capital of Music."

Sun Island in Harbin, the premiere site of "Crazy Fools," is located on the north bank of the Songhua River in Harbin City, Heilongjiang Province, China. As a wetland meadow grassland scenic area, it is a well-known national summer resort covering 38 square kilometers with a protected area of 88 square kilometers. It is a famous scenic spot in Harbin and a national AAAAA-level tourist attraction.

There are two folk legends regarding the origin of Sun Island in Harbin. One says the island resembles a sun, hence the name Sun Island. Another suggests that the slopes are covered with clean fine sand, which becomes particularly hot under the sunlight, thus named Sun Island. Surrounded by rivers, the scenery is exceptionally beautiful. In spring, the wildflowers bloom, leaves sprout, and birds chirp harmoniously. The gentle breeze intoxicates visitors who often linger unwillingly. In summer, green shade, blue water, white sand, vibrant flowers, butterflies, and frogs create an ideal summer retreat. In autumn, the clear sky contrasts beautifully with golden leaves covering the paths and red maple leaves in the vast wilderness. In winter, thousands of miles are covered in ice and snow, featuring horse-drawn sleighs, ice sports, and ice and snow sculptures, bringing vitality to this snowy world. The white tree rime appears as if "the forest flowers bloomed overnight," and the flying snow transports people into the magnificent realm of "ten thousand pear trees in bloom," forming a uniquely beautiful landscape. These stunning scenes across the four seasons constitute an ecologically advantageous natural scenic area, earning Sun Island the reputation of "Northern Scenery Rivaling Jiangnan."
