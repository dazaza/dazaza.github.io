Inter Milan's Terrifying Star-Making Efficiency Faces First Obstacle in 3 Years: Mourinho's Obsession with One Aspect Could Stifle Many Talents

by yinding999 on 2009-12-18 06:46:58

Inter Milan's frightening star-making rate encounters its first obstacle in 3 years; Mourinho's obsessive preference for one type of player may stifle many prodigies.

Sina Sports - "He is not a phenomenon, not the next Pele or the next Maradona chosen by God, but God clearly said this child is a footballer."

Donati, the "child" referred to in Mourinho's above statement, made his debut for Inter Milan's first team in the 1-0 victory over Livorno in the Italian Cup round of 16 early this morning. Mourinho said: "I have a soft spot for Donati. I've spoken with him because he is passionate about football, and it's not just verbal passion, as anyone can say they love football. Passion is demonstrated through training and how much football occupies in life. He enjoys training with the first team, even if it's only for a minute. With such enthusiasm, he can absorb any knowledge gained."

Regardless of how things were in England, at least since coming to Italy, Mourinho's standard for judging young players has always been whether they are humble and pragmatic. This is the reason why he strictly manages Balotelli and Santon, and also why he favors Klisins and Donati. Therefore, it was unsurprising to see a hardworking and diligent Donati, who tries his best not to make mistakes, on the field at the Meazza early this morning.

It wasn't until the 29th minute that Donati took the ball down the flank and delivered a cross to Suazo, who missed with an overhead kick. This was his first real assist of the match. For most of the game prior to this, Donati rarely crossed the halfway line. Sneijder, when receiving the ball near the penalty area, usually chose to combine with Balotelli or pass to Montari on the left wing. Inter Milan's right side frequently appeared vacant, a rare occurrence this season. Of course, Donati doesn't need to be anyone's clone; he doesn't need to imitate Maicon. However, during the 2-0 away win against Livorno in the league this season, Mourinho also started Klisins, another player he liked for his "diligence," who was substituted at halftime for not providing enough support and focusing only on defense. Fortunately, Livorno didn't put too much pressure on Inter Milan's defense today, otherwise Donati might not have played until the 82nd minute.

After winning four consecutive league titles, the Italian Cup is no longer as important to Inter Milan as it was in the 2004-05 season. Interestingly, in the past two seasons of the Italian Cup, two young players launched by Inter Milan have garnered significant attention. In the 2007-08 season, Balotelli made his first start for Inter Milan in a 4-1 Italian Cup win over Reggina, scoring twice. In the 2008-09 season, Santon made his first start in the Italian Cup against Roma, revealing his talent from thereon. Now, Donati becomes the third player in three years to be unveiled in Inter Milan's Italian Cup journey.

"This is a beautiful Christmas gift for him and all the young players," Mourinho did not admit that Donati's performance was mediocre, "This is a message to all the kids. If they are honest, professional, and give their best effort, then they can participate in top-level matches."

Unlike Mancini, Mourinho does not simply field a completely substitute lineup in the Italian Cup when nurturing young players. He prefers to make opportunities precious to motivate the youngsters. "Honesty, professionalism, and giving your best effort," this is the standard he sets for Inter Milan's youth players, deliberately excluding "talent," which happens to be the recognized quality of Balotelli and Santon. Mourinho's decision to promote young players in this way may bury many exceptionally talented individuals with unique personalities in the future. Playing honestly and defensively in the back is undoubtedly easier than making bold moves forward. A good kid without a desire to perform or lacking sufficient confidence will likely end up closer to being a craftsman than a master. There's no need to provide more examples—right beside Donati are two current ones, Materazzi and Lucio.

In the 82nd minute, Mourinho replaced Donati with Maicon. The 19-year-old boy had a smile on his face, and there was scattered applause within the stadium. He should feel satisfied. Having joined Inter Milan from Luccese in the 2007-08 season, he received an opportunity to play with the first team just 18 months later. "Excited? Yes, but I started getting excited when I knew I was included in the list. When I stepped onto the pitch at Meazza, I realized it was a completely different feeling, which made me incredibly excited." (Giotto)
